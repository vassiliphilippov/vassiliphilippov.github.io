maieutic method

noun
Socratic teaching by question and answer
6.3
Pronunciation: / maɪˈjuːtɪk ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / maɪˈjuːtɪk ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
A method of teaching through questions and answers, often to help students discover truths for themselves; famously used by Socrates.

Examples

The maieutic method of teaching
Socrates is famous for employing the maieutic method in his dialogues with pupils.
The maieutic method relies on thoughtful questioning to help learners uncover underlying assumptions.
The philosophy professor encouraged debate, using the maieutic method to guide students to their own conclusions.
