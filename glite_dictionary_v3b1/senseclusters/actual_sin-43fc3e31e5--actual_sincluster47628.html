actual sin

noun
sin committed by one's own actions
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tʃu.əl sɪn / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃu.əl sɪn / (BrE)
a sin committed knowingly and voluntarily, as opposed to original sin, in Christian theology.

Examples

Actual sin committed knowingly
He distinguished between original sin and actual sin in his sermon.
In some Christian teachings, actual sin must be confessed for forgiveness.
Many theologians debate the consequences of actual sin on moral responsibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original sin - the inherited sinful state from Adam and Eve
