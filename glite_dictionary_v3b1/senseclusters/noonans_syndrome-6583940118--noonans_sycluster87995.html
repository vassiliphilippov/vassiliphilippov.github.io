Noonan's syndrome

noun
genetic disorder with facial and growth features
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːnənz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈnuːnənz ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a genetic disorder characterized by short stature, distinctive facial features such as low-set ears, and often reduced fertility; affects both males and females

Examples

Genetic counseling is often recommended for families affected by Noonan's syndrome.
