sarcoma

noun
malignant tumor from connective tissue
4.4
Pronunciation: / sɑːrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / sɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a usually malignant tumor arising from connective or supportive tissue, such as bone, muscle, or fat

Examples

A sarcoma tumor
Her research focuses on understanding genetic mutations involved in sarcoma development.
The patient was diagnosed with a rare sarcoma in his thigh muscle.
Treatment options for sarcoma often include surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carcinoma - a type of cancer that begins in the epithelial tissue
