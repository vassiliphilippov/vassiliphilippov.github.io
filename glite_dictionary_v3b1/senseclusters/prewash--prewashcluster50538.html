prewash

noun
initial cleaning step
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈpriːwɒʃ / (BrE)
a cleaning step or cycle performed before the main washing process to help remove dirt or stains

Examples

Prewash cycle
She always uses a prewash to help remove stubborn stains from her laundry.
The dishwasher has a prewash option for heavily soiled dishes.
Adding a prewash can improve the results when cleaning muddy sports uniforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main wash - the primary washing cycle in a cleaning process
  • synonym: rinse - to wash lightly, especially to remove soap or dirt
