aplasia

noun
failure of organ or tissue to develop
8.6
Pronunciation: / əˈpleɪʒə / (AmE), / əˈpleɪzɪə / (BrE)
failure of an organ or tissue to develop normally or to be formed in the body

Examples

Bone marrow aplasia
Bone marrow aplasia can lead to a dangerous drop in blood cell counts.
Researchers are studying genetic causes of aplasia in various organs.
The infant was diagnosed with aplasia of the thymus, requiring special medical care.
