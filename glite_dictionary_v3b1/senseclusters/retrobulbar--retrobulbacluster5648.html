retrobulbar

noun
eye anesthesia injection
9.9
Pronunciation: /re.trə.ˈbʌl.bər/ /; / /re.troʊ.ˈbʌl.bər/ (AmE), /re.trə.ˈbʌl.bɑː/ /; / /re.əʊ.ˈbʌl.bɑː/ (BrE)
an injection or nerve block given behind the eyeball to numb the eye, especially before eye surgery

Examples

Retrobulbar injection
The doctor performed a retrobulbar before starting the cataract surgery.
A retrobulbar is often used to ensure the patient feels no pain during eye operations.
Some patients may experience mild discomfort after receiving a retrobulbar injection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peribulbar - an injection around the eyeball to numb the eye
  • antonym: topical anesthesia - anesthesia applied to the surface of the eye
