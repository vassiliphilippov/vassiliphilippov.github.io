platyrrhinic

adjective
having broad, widely spaced nostrils
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌplætɪˈrɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌplatɪˈrɪnɪk / (BrE)
having broad, widely spaced nostrils; relating to New World monkeys with nostrils set far apart, or to people with broad noses

Examples

A platyrrhinic nose
Anthropologists sometimes use the term platyrrhinic to describe populations with characteristically broad noses.
Certain species of New World monkeys are classified as platyrrhinic because their nostrils are set far apart.
The platyrrhinic structure of the monkey's nose helps distinguish it from Old World species.
