treeline

noun
limit of tree growth
Pronunciation: / ˈtriːˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtriːˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the highest elevation or northernmost latitude at which trees are able to grow

Examples

Mountain treeline
We camped just below the treeline to avoid the cold winds.
Above the treeline, only low shrubs and grasses can survive the harsh conditions.
Hikers noticed the sudden change in vegetation as they crossed the treeline on the mountain.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: summit - the highest point of a mountain
  • synonym: timberline - the upper limit of tree growth in mountains or cold regions
