French polish shellac

noun
wood varnish made of shellac in alcohol
4.2
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ ˈpɑlɪʃ ʃəˈlæk / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈpɒlɪʃ ʃəˈlak / (BrE)
a varnish for wood made by dissolving shellac in alcohol, used for creating a high-gloss finish

Examples

French polish shellac finish
Restorers often choose French polish shellac when working on antique furniture for an authentic look.
The cabinetmaker applied several coats of French polish shellac to achieve a perfect glossy finish.
You should always use French polish shellac in a well-ventilated area because of the alcohol fumes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: varnish - a liquid for coating wood to give a hard, glossy finish
