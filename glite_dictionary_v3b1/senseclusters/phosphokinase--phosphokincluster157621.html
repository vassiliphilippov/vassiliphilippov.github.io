phosphokinase

noun
phosphate-transferring enzyme
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑsfoʊˈkaɪneɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəʊˈkaɪneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that transfers a phosphate group to another molecule, usually playing a role in cell metabolism

Examples

Phosphokinase enzyme activity
Doctors measured the patient's phosphokinase levels to assess muscle damage after the injury.
A rise in phosphokinase activity can indicate certain metabolic disorders.
Researchers study phosphokinase to better understand how cells use and store energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kinase - an enzyme that transfers phosphate groups to other molecules
  • antonym: phosphatase - an enzyme that removes phosphate groups from molecules
