syncytial

adjective
shared cytoplasm
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈsɪʃiəl / (AmE), / sɪnˈsɪʃiəl /; / sɪnˈsɪtɪəl / (BrE)
relating to a mass of cells with multiple nuclei sharing a common cytoplasm, usually formed by the fusion of individual cells

Examples

Syncytial cell mass
The syncytial layer in the placenta helps nutrients pass from mother to fetus.
Some viruses cause cells to fuse and form a syncytial structure.
Muscle fibers are often described as syncytial because they contain many nuclei within a single cell membrane.
The syncytial nature of the tissue allows for rapid communication between cells.
Certain viruses have a syncytial effect, causing cells to merge together.
Researchers observed syncytial structures under the microscope after the infection.
