counteroffer

noun
an offer made in reply to another offer
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌɔfər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntərˌɒfə / (BrE)
An offer made in response to a previous offer, rejecting the original offer and presenting new terms.

Examples

A counteroffer made
After rejecting the initial bid, the company submitted a counteroffer with more favorable terms.
She declined his proposal but was open to negotiating if he made a reasonable counteroffer.
The landlord refused the first price and waited to see if the tenants would make a counteroffer.

  • synonym: offer - a proposal to give or do something
