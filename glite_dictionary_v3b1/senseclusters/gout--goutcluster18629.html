gout

noun
painful joint inflammation (medical condition)
6.5
Pronunciation: / ɡaʊt / (AmE), / ɡaʊt / (BrE)
A painful medical condition characterized by inflammation of the joints, especially the big toe, caused by excess uric acid in the blood.

Examples

Gout attack
A diet high in purines can increase the risk of developing gout.
She suffered from gout, which made it difficult for her to walk at times.
The doctor diagnosed his swollen toe as a symptom of gout.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arthritis - inflammation of the joints
  • synonym: rheumatism - chronic pain and inflammation of joints or muscles
