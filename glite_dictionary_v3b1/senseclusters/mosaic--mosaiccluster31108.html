mosaic

noun
plant viral disease causing leaf mottling
4.4 6.2
Pronunciation: / moʊˈzeɪɪk / (AmE), / məʊˈzeɪɪk / (BrE)
A viral disease affecting certain plants, such as tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco, characterized by mottled and often shriveled leaves.

Examples

Mosaic virus infection
Cucumber plants infected with mosaic often show yellow and green patches on their leaves.
Experts recommend using virus-resistant seeds to prevent mosaic in potato plants.
The tomato crop suffered severe losses due to mosaic spreading rapidly through the field.
