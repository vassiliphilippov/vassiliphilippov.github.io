pretense

noun
imaginative pretending or make-believe
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛns /; / priˈtɛns / (AmE), / ˈpriː.təns /; / prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
the act of pretending, especially using imagination in play or in intellectual exercise

Examples

Childhood pretense
Children often engage in pretense when they invent stories with their toys.
Her pretense of being a detective helped spark the group's imagination.
Through pretense, young students learn important social and problem-solving skills.
Actors must often rely on their sense of pretence to fully become their characters.
As children, much of our afternoon was spent in pretence, building castles out of cushions and ruling imaginary kingdoms.
The teacher encouraged pretence in the classroom to help students develop their creativity.
