blench

verb
turn pale or flinch from fear
Pronunciation: / blɛntʃ / (AmE), / blɛntʃ / (BrE)
to turn pale or flinch, especially from fear or surprise

Examples

To blench in fear
Despite the harsh accusations, she refused to blench or show any sign of weakness.
He might blench at the sight of blood, but he never backs down from a challenge.
She did not blench when she heard the sudden thunderclap outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cower - crouch down in fear
  • synonym: flinch - draw back suddenly, as from pain or fear
