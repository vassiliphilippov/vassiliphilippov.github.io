rehypothecate

verb
to reuse pledged collateral
9.9
Pronunciation: /riː.haɪ.ˈpɑːθ.ə.keɪt/ /; / /rɪ.haɪ.ˈpɑːθ.ə.keɪt/ (AmE), /riː.haɪ.ˈpɒθ.ɪ.keɪt/ /; / /rɪ.haɪ.ˈpɒθ.ɪ.keɪt/ (BrE)
to use collateral pledged by a client as security for another loan, typically in financial markets

Examples

To rehypothecate client assets
The investment bank rehypothecated the client's assets to secure additional funding.
Some brokers routinely rehypothecate securities to maximize their available capital.
Regulations may limit how much collateral a firm can rehypothecate on behalf of clients.
