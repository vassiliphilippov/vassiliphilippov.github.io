predate

verb
hunt or prey on (animals)
7.2 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / priËˆdeÉªt / (AmE), / priËˆdeÉªt / (BrE)
(of an animal) to hunt, kill, and eat another animal; to prey on.

Examples

Lions predate zebras
Larger fish often predate on smaller species in the reef.
The birds predate insects that hide beneath the leaves.
Wolves typically predate deer during the harsh winter months.

Synonyms

  • prey
    3.7 1.54/m
    prey is when an animal catches and eats another animal, while predate is a formal term for that hunting action.
  • hunt
    1.4 19/m
    hunt means chasing and killing animals for food or sport, while predate usually means animals hunt and eat other animals.
  • hunt down
    4.0 0.19/m
    hunt down means track and capture or kill an animal after a chase, while predate focuses on eating other animals.
  • depredate
    9.9 0.02/m
    depredate means to raid or damage crops or livestock, while predate refers to animals hunting and eating other animals.
