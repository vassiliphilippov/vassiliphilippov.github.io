embroider

verb
add extra (often exaggerated) details
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbrɔɪdər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɔɪdə / (BrE)
to add extra details or embellishments to a story or account, often exaggerating beyond the truth

Examples

To embroider a story
He tends to embroider the facts when telling stories about his childhood.
She couldn't help but embroider her tale of how she met the celebrity.
The journalist was accused of embroidering the details to make the article more sensational.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem more impressive, important, or worse than it really is
