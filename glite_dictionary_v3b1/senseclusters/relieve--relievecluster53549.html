relieve

verb
save from ruin or harm
Pronunciation: / rɪˈliv / (AmE), / rɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to save someone or something from ruin, destruction, or harm; to rescue or bring relief from danger or threat

Examples

To relieve a city
The arrival of reinforcements helped to relieve the besieged city.
The firefighters managed to relieve several families from the burning building.
Timely intervention by medics can relieve victims from life-threatening situations.
