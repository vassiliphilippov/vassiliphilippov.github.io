unblooded

adjective
inexperienced; untested
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
not yet experienced or tested in a particular job, activity, or role.

Examples

Unblooded players
The team relied on several unblooded players during the championship game.
As an unblooded recruit, she was eager to prove herself to her colleagues.
The company took a risk by promoting an unblooded manager to lead the new project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: novice - a person new to a job or activity
