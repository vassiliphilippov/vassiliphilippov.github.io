alveus

noun
anatomical cavity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈælviəs / (AmE), / ˈalvɪəs / (BrE)
a channel, cavity, or hollow, especially in anatomy, such as a groove or space in an organ or tissue

Examples

Brain alveus
The alveus of the hippocampus plays a role in transmitting nerve signals in the brain.
In some animals, the alveus refers to a cavity that holds teeth or other structures.
Researchers studied the structure of the alveus to better understand its function in the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface
