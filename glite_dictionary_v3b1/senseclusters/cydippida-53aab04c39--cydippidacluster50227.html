Cydippida

noun
order of ctenophores with two long tentacles
Pronunciation: / sɪˈdɪpɪdə / (AmE), / sɪˈdɪpɪdə / (BrE)
An order of ctenophores distinguished by having two long, branched tentacles.

Examples

Order Cydippida ctenophores
Scientists observed several species of Cydippida during their deep-sea expedition.
The Cydippida are easily recognized by their two slender, retractile tentacles.
Unlike other ctenophores, Cydippida use their tentacles to capture prey.
