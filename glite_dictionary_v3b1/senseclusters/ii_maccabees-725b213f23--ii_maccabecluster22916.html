II Maccabees

proper noun
Apocryphal biblical book about Judas Maccabeus
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkənd ˈmækəˌbis / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈmækəˌbiːz / (BrE)
an Apocryphal book of the Bible describing the Jewish revolt led by Judas Maccabeus against Antiochus IV Epiphanes, concluding with the defeat of Nicanor in 161 BC

Examples

II Maccabees book
Many historians refer to II Maccabees when discussing the Jewish resistance against Hellenistic rule.
Some Christian denominations include II Maccabees in their biblical canon, while others do not.
The story of the martyrdom of seven brothers is vividly recounted in II Maccabees.
