loudspeaker

noun
device that produces sound from electrical signals
5.7 1.9
Pronunciation: /ˈlaʊd.spiː.kər/ /; / /laʊd.ˈspiː.kər/ (AmE), /ˈlaʊd.spiː.kə/ /; / /laʊd.ˈspiː.kə/ (BrE)
An electronic device that converts electrical signals into audible sound, often used to amplify music, speech, or other audio signals.

Examples

A loudspeaker system
He spoke into the microphone, and his voice echoed through the loudspeaker.
Please do not stand directly in front of the loudspeaker during the performance.
The concert hall installed a new loudspeaker system to improve sound quality.
