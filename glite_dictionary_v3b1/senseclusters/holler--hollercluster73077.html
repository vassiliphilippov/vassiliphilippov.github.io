holler

verb
to complain loudly; to protest
8.3 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑː.lər/ (AmE), /ˈhɒ.lə/ (BrE)
(informal) To complain loudly or protest, often in an emotional or noisy way.

Examples

To holler about rules
She hollered that it wasn't fair when she lost the game.
Stop hollering about your chores and just do them.
Whenever the rules change, someone is sure to holler.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • synonym: grumble - to complain in a quiet or bad-tempered way
