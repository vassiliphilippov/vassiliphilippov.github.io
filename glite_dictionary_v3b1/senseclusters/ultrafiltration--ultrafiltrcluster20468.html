ultrafiltration

noun
kidney filtration process
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing fluid and small molecules from blood through a membrane, especially in the kidneys or during dialysis

Examples

Kidney ultrafiltration
During ultrafiltration, waste products and excess water are removed from the blood in the kidneys.
The doctor described how ultrafiltration is essential for forming urine and maintaining fluid balance.
Modern dialysis machines use ultrafiltration to help patients with kidney failure remove toxins from their blood.
The doctor explained that ultrafiltration would help remove excess fluid from the patient's body during treatment.
Modern dialysis machines use ultrafiltration to carefully control the amount of fluid taken from the blood.
Nurses monitor the rate of ultrafiltration to ensure patient safety during each dialysis session.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialysis - a medical process for removing waste and excess fluid from the blood
  • synonym: filtration - the process of separating solids from liquids or gases using a filter
  • antonym: reabsorption - the process in the kidneys where substances are taken back into the blood from the filtrate
