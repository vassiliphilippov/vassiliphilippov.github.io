shortchange

verb
to give too little
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrtˌtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtˌtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to give someone less than they deserve or are owed, especially in money or treatment

Examples

To shortchange a customer
The cashier accidentally shortchanged me at the register, so I asked for the correct amount.
Many workers feel they are shortchanged when their efforts are not recognized by management.
If you rush through your homework, you might shortchange yourself on learning.
