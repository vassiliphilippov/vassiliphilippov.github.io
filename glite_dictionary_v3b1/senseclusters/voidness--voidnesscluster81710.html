voidness

noun
state of emptiness
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being empty or lacking inherent, independent existence, especially in philosophical or religious contexts

Examples

Philosophical voidness
The philosopher discussed the concept of voidness in relation to the nature of reality.
Meditation can help one understand the voidness of thoughts and emotions.
In Buddhist teachings, voidness is seen as a fundamental aspect of existence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emptiness - the state of containing nothing or lacking meaning
  • antonym: fullness - the state of being full or complete
