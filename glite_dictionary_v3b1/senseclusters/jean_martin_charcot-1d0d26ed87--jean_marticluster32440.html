Jean Martin Charcot

proper noun
French neurologist (1825–1893)
Pronunciation: / ʒɑ̃ mɑʁˈtɛ̃ ʃɑrˈkoʊ / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ mɑːˈtɛ̃ ʃɑːˈkoʊ / (BrE)
(1825–1893) A French neurologist renowned for his pioneering work on neurological diseases and for using hypnotism in the study and treatment of hysteria.

Examples

Jean Martin Charcot neurologist
Jean Martin Charcot is often credited as the founder of modern neurology for his groundbreaking research.
Many neurological terms are named after Jean Martin Charcot due to his important discoveries.
Sigmund Freud studied with Jean Martin Charcot in Paris, which greatly influenced Freud’s later theories.
