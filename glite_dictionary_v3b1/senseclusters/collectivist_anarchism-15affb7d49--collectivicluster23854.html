collectivist anarchism

noun
anarchist philosophy favoring collective ownership
6.9
Pronunciation: / kəˌlɛktɪvɪst ˈænərkɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˌlɛktɪvɪst ˈanəkɪzəm / (BrE)
a revolutionary doctrine advocating the abolition of the state and private ownership of the means of production, favoring collective ownership instead

Examples

Collectivist anarchism theory
He wrote his thesis on the theoretical differences between collectivist anarchism and anarchist communism.
Many 19th-century radicals supported collectivist anarchism as an alternative to both capitalism and state socialism.
The principles of collectivist anarchism influenced various labor movements across Europe.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anarchist communism - an anarchist philosophy advocating the abolition of ownership in favor of communal use or possession
  • antonym: individualist anarchism - anarchist philosophy emphasizing individual autonomy and private property
