latigo

noun
saddlery leather
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlætɪˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlætɪˌɡəʊ / (BrE)
a strong, heavy leather commonly used for making saddles, belts, or harnesses

Examples

Latigo leather belt
The saddle maker chose latigo for its durability and strength.
Belts made from latigo leather are known to last for many years.
He repaired the harness using a strip of thick latigo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rawhide - untanned animal skin used for making leather goods
  • antonym: suede - a type of soft leather with a napped finish
