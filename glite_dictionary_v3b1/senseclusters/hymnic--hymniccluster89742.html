hymnic

adjective
praising or exalted
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪmnɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɪmnɪk / (BrE)
having the qualities of a hymn, such as being lofty, exalted, or expressing enthusiastic praise; relating to hymns or hymn-like poetry

Examples

A hymnic poem
The author's hymnic language made the tribute feel especially moving and grand.
Her speech took on a hymnic quality as she praised the team's achievements.
The review was so hymnic that it seemed more like a song of praise than a critique.
The choir sang a hymnic melody that filled the church with a sense of reverence.
Her writing style is often described as hymnic because of its solemn and uplifting tone.
The ceremony opened with a hymnic poem that set a spiritual mood for the event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: critical - expressing disapproval or finding fault
  • synonym: hymnal - relating to hymns or a collection of hymns
  • synonym: laudatory - expressing praise or admiration
  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
