artificial stupidity

noun
ineffective or flawed decision-making by AI systems
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəl stuːˈpɪdəti / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈfɪʃəl stjuːˈpɪdəti / (BrE)
incompetence or error in decision-making by computer systems or artificial intelligence, especially resulting in poor or absurd outcomes

Examples

Artificial stupidity in computers
Critics often use the term artificial stupidity to describe the unintended consequences of poorly designed algorithms.
Many users have encountered artificial stupidity when chatbots provide nonsensical answers to simple questions.
The researchers highlighted instances of artificial stupidity in the self-driving car's navigation decisions.
