grey-leaf pine

noun
pine tree native to southwestern California
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪ lif paɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ liːf paɪn / (BrE)
a medium-sized pine tree of southwestern California with five needles per bundle and long cylindrical cones

Examples

The grey-leaf pine tree
Botanists studied the cones of the rare grey-leaf pine for their unique shape.
The grey-leaf pine is well adapted to the dry climate of southern California.
You can find several grey-leaf pines growing along the remote hillsides of the region.
