inheritability

noun
ability to be inherited
7.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ə.rə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ə.rə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to be passed on to heirs by succession or under a will

Examples

Inheritability of property
The inheritability of certain assets depends on the laws of the country.
Lawyers discussed the inheritability of intellectual property rights in the meeting.
Questions about the inheritability of the family business arose after the owner's passing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inheritance - property or rights received from someone after their death
  • antonym: nontransferability - the quality of not being able to be transferred or inherited
  • synonym: transferability - the quality of being able to be transferred to another person
