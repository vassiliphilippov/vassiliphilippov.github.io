putting words in someone's mouth

verb
falsely attributing statements to someone
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊtɪŋ wɜrdz ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / ˈpʊtɪŋ wɜːdz ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
to claim that someone said something that they did not actually say; to falsely attribute statements to someone

Examples

To put words in someone's mouth
During the interview, the journalist was accused of putting words in the politician's mouth.
It's unfair to put words in someone's mouth just to win an argument.
Please stop putting words in my mouth; I never said anything like that.

Related Terms

  • synonym: misquote - to repeat someone's words incorrectly or with changes
  • synonym: misrepresent - to give a false or misleading account of the words or actions of someone
