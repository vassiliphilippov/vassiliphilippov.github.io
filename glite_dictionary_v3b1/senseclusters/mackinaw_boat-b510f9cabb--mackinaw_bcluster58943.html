Mackinaw boat

noun
flat-bottomed boat from Great Lakes
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmækɪˌnɔ boʊt / (AmE), / ˈmakɪnɔː bəʊt / (BrE)
A flat-bottomed, double-ended boat traditionally used on the upper Great Lakes, especially for fishing and transport.

Examples

A Mackinaw boat on lakes
A restored Mackinaw boat is displayed at the maritime museum, illustrating early Great Lakes travel.
The fishermen set out at dawn in their sturdy Mackinaw boat to work on Lake Superior.
With its broad beam, the Mackinaw boat handled the choppy waters with ease.
