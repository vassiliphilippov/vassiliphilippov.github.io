vulture

noun
ruthlessly exploitative person
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a person who preys on others, especially in a ruthless or exploitative way, often seeking to profit from their misfortune

Examples

A business vulture
After the company went bankrupt, financial vultures swooped in to buy its assets at a bargain.
Some people become vultures in a crisis, looking for ways to take advantage of others' misfortune.
The paparazzi acted like vultures, crowding around the grieving family for photographs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or support
  • synonym: predator - a person or animal that preys on others
