entrainment

noun
matching speech or behavior
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈtreɪnmənt / (AmE), / ɛnˈtreɪnmənt / (BrE)
the process by which people unconsciously match each other's speech patterns, tone, or behavior during interaction

Examples

Speech entrainment
During the meeting, there was clear entrainment as team members began to mirror each other's gestures.
Researchers study entrainment to understand how people build rapport in conversations.
Her rapid entrainment with new colleagues helped her fit in quickly at the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mimicry - the act of imitating someone’s actions or speech
  • synonym: synchronization - the process of occurring at the same time or rate
