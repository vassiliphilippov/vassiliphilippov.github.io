forfend

verb
to prevent or ward off
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈfɛnd / (AmE), / fəˈfɛnd / (BrE)
To prevent something undesirable from happening; to ward off or protect against danger or harm.

Examples

To forfend danger
He wore a charm believed to forfend evil spirits.
Nothing could forfend the storm from sweeping across the town that night.
The villagers built a tall fence to forfend attacks from wild animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avert - to turn away or prevent (especially danger or harm)
  • antonym: invite - to cause or make something (undesirable) likely to happen
  • synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening
