psychotic depression

noun
severe depression with psychosis
1.0 7.0
Pronunciation: /saɪk.ˈɑː.tɪk dɪ.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (AmE), /saɪk.ˈɒ.tɪk dɪ.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (BrE)
A severe form of depression in which a person loses touch with reality and may experience delusions or hallucinations, often with significant functional impairments.

Examples

Severe psychotic depression
Family members noticed that his psychotic depression was impairing his ability to perform daily tasks.
The patient was diagnosed with psychotic depression after she began experiencing hallucinations during her depressive episode.
Treatment for psychotic depression often involves a combination of antidepressant and antipsychotic medications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: major depressive disorder - a mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest
