set

adjective
hardened; become firm or solid
5.0 2.43/m
Pronunciation: / sÉ›t / (AmE), / sÉ›t / (BrE)
(of substances such as concrete, glue, or jelly) having become solid or firm by chemical or physical process, or (of a person's expression or features) fixed and unmoving, not changing in response to emotion or situation

Examples

Set jelly
Before serving, make sure the jelly has set properly in the refrigerator.
Once the glue has set, the pieces will stay together firmly.
The concrete had fully set by the next morning, allowing the workers to continue building.
He walked into the room with a set expression, unwilling to show any emotion.
Her lips remained set and unyielding during the entire conversation.
The old man's jaw was set in determination as he faced the challenge.

Synonyms

  • solid
    1.0 2.19/m
    solid is firm and keeps its shape like a block or jelly, while set describes something that has become firm or fixed.
  • hardened
    4.1 0.00/m
    hardened is made strong or hard by heating or treatment, while set means to become firm by cooling or a chemical process.
  • fixed
    1.0 0.03/m
    fixed is held in one position and cannot move or change easily, while set often means having become firm or unchanging.
  • rigid
    4.0 5.55/m
    rigid cannot bend or change shape and feels stiff, while set describes becoming firm or an expression that stays the same.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardened - made hard or solid
  • antonym: relaxed - free from tension and anxiety; opposite of set when describing facial features or expressions
  • synonym: rigid - unable to bend or be changed; stiff; especially of a facial expression
