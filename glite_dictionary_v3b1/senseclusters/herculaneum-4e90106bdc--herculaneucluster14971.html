Herculaneum

proper noun
ancient Roman city destroyed by Vesuvius
Pronunciation: / hɜrkjʊˈleɪniəm / (AmE), / hɜːkjʊˈleɪniəm / (BrE)
an ancient Roman city near Naples that was destroyed and buried by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD

Examples

Herculaneum ancient city
Archaeologists have uncovered stunning mosaics in Herculaneum.
The ruins of Herculaneum attract thousands of tourists each year.
Unlike Pompeii, much of Herculaneum was preserved under a deep layer of volcanic mud.
