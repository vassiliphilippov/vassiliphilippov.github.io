reattribution

noun
assigning new source
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌætrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of assigning something, such as a work or cause, to a new source or origin

Examples

Reattribution of a painting
The reattribution of the ancient sculpture to a different artist surprised many historians.
After new evidence emerged, the reattribution of the quote was widely accepted by scholars.
The museum announced the reattribution of several paintings after a detailed investigation.
