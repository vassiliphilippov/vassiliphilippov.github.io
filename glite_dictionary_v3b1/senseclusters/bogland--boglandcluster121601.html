bogland

noun
wet, spongy land
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑɡˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈbɒɡˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of wet, spongy land, often covered with moss or peat, sometimes used for turf and home to wildlife

Examples

Remote bogland areas
The rare bird nests only in remote bogland regions.
Hikers wore waterproof boots to cross the muddy bogland safely.
Scientists study the unique plants that thrive in the bogland ecosystem.
The village was surrounded by miles of bogland rich in wildlife and rare plants.
Farmers avoided building on the bogland because the ground was too soft and wet.
In the past, people cut peat from the bogland to heat their homes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - an area of wet, spongy ground
  • antonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land with little rainfall
  • synonym: marsh - an area of low, wet land with grasses and reeds
  • synonym: swamp - a wetland area with standing water and trees
