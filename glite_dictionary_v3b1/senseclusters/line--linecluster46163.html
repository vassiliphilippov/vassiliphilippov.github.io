line

noun
a row of people or things, one behind another
Pronunciation: / laɪn / (AmE), / laɪn / (BrE)
a row or formation of people or things arranged in a straight or organized way

Examples

A line of people
A long line of cars stretched down the highway during rush hour.
Please form a line so everyone can get their tickets easily.
We waited in a line outside the theater for over an hour.
Each tree was planted in a perfectly straight line along the road.
The children formed a line before entering the classroom.
The students stood in a line waiting for the bus to arrive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: column - a vertical arrangement as opposed to one side by side
  • antonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together
  • synonym: queue - a line or sequence of people or vehicles awaiting their turn
  • synonym: row - a linear arrangement of things or people
