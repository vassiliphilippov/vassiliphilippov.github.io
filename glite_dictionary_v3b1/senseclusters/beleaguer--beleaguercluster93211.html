beleaguer

verb
annoy or beset persistently
7.2 6.8
Pronunciation: /bə.ˈliː.gər/ /; / /bɪ.ˈliː.gər/ (AmE), /bə.ˈliː.gə/ /; / /bɪ.ˈliː.gə/ (BrE)
to cause repeated trouble or harassment to someone; to annoy or beset persistently

Examples

To beleaguer someone constantly
If you beleaguer your friend with too many requests, he might stop answering your calls.
The department was beleaguered by constant complaints from users.
The press continued to beleaguer the politician with difficult questions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bother - to take the trouble to do something or annoy; less formal synonym
  • synonym: harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation; synonyms for persistent annoyance
  • antonym: soothe - to gently calm or relieve; an opposite of annoying persistently
