replevin

noun
property recovery lawsuit
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈplɛvɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈplɛvɪn / (BrE)
a legal process or action to recover personal property that has been wrongfully taken or held by someone else

Examples

File a replevin case
The company filed a replevin to retrieve its equipment from the former employee.
After the landlord seized her furniture, she initiated a replevin to get it back.
A court may grant a replevin order if property is wrongfully withheld.
The company filed for replevin to recover the equipment taken by the contractor.
A judge issued a replevin order to return the disputed artwork to its owner.
Without a replevin, the property could not be legally reclaimed before the trial ended.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forfeiture - the loss of property or rights as a penalty
  • synonym: repossession - the act of taking back property, especially for nonpayment
  • antonym: seizure - the act of taking possession of property by legal right or process
