posterior synechia

noun
iris-lens adhesion (eye, medical)
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɔː.ˈstɪr.iː.ər sɪ.ˈniː.kiː.ə/ /; / /oʊ.ˈstɪr.iː.ər sɪ.ˈniː.kiː.ə/ /; / /pɑː.ˈstɪr.iː.ər sɪ.ˈniː.kiː.ə/ (AmE), /pɒ.ˈstɪə.riː.ə sɪ.ˈniː.kiː.ə/ /; / /əʊ.ˈstɪə.riː.ə sɪ.ˈniː.kiː.ə/ (BrE)
An adhesion between the posterior surface of the iris and the lens of the eye, often as a result of inflammation.

Examples

Posterior synechia adhesion
If untreated, posterior synechia can lead to increased intraocular pressure and further complications.

Treatment was started immediately to prevent the formation of posterior synechiae after the episode of uveitis.
