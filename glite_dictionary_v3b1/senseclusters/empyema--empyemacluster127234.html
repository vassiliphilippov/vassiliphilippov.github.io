empyema

noun
accumulation of pus in a body cavity
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…õmpa…™Ààim…ô / (AmE), / Àå…õmpa…™ÀàiÀêm…ô / (BrE)
A collection of pus within a body cavity, especially in the pleural cavity of the lungs, usually as a complication of infection.

Examples

A empyema in lung
A chest X-ray confirmed the presence of an empyema in his left lung.
The patient developed an empyema following a severe case of pneumonia.
Treatment of empyema generally requires antibiotics and sometimes surgical drainage.

Synonyms

  • pyothorax
    9.9 0.00/m
    pyothorax is pus in the chest space between the lungs and chest wall, while empyema is pus collected in a body cavity, usually the pleura.
  • purulent pleurisy
    7.9 0.00/m
    purulent pleurisy is pleural inflammation with pus and pain, while empyema is a pus collection that fills the pleural space.
