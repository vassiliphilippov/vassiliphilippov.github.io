Middle Ages

noun
period in European history (5th–15th centuries)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdəl ˈeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ˈeɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
the period of European history between the end of classical antiquity and the beginning of the Renaissance, roughly from the 5th to the late 15th century

Examples

The Middle Ages period
Many famous works of literature, such as 'Beowulf,' were written in the Middle Ages.
Scholars often study art and architecture from the Middle Ages to understand medieval culture.
The castle was constructed during the Middle Ages to protect the inhabitants from invaders.
