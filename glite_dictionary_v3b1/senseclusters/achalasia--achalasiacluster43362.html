achalasia

noun
esophageal muscle disorder
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.kə.ˈleɪ.ʒə/ /; / /eɪ.kə.ˈleɪ.ʒə/ (AmE), /ə.kə.ˈleɪ.ʒə/ /; / /eɪ.kə.ˈleɪ.ʒə/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which the esophagus is unable to move food properly into the stomach due to muscle failure

Examples

The doctor explained that achalasia was causing her difficulty swallowing solid foods.
Treatment for achalasia may include procedures to help relax the esophageal muscles.
After months of discomfort, he was finally diagnosed with achalasia by a specialist.
