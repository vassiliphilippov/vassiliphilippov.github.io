radialis

noun
taxonomic epithet
9.8
Pronunciation: / reɪˈdiːəlɪs / (AmE), / reɪˈdɪːəlɪs / (BrE)
a species name or epithet in scientific classification indicating something is radial or radiating in form

Examples

Radialis species
The botanist labeled the specimen radialis, noting its distinctive radial arrangement of leaves and petals.
Several animal species bear the epithet radialis to describe their radiating body structures.
In taxonomy, the term radialis often refers to organisms with parts arranged like rays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radiatus - a species epithet meaning 'radiated' or 'having rays'
