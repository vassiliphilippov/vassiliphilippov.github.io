a requirement, assumption, or clause that must be met for something to be valid or effective

Examples

A condition of the contract
His offer included a strict condition that no changes be made.
Payment is subject to the condition that the work is completed on time.
The contract will only be valid under this condition.
Before signing the agreement, she carefully reviewed all the conditions.
Most rental contracts include several conditions to protect both the tenant and the landlord.
The sale will only proceed if the conditions outlined are met by both parties.

Synonyms

  • stipulation
    6.6 1.57/m
    a stipulation is a specific clause written into an agreement, while a condition is any requirement that must be met.
  • provision
    5.6 43/m
    a provision is a clause in a legal document that sets rules, while a condition is a requirement for something to be valid.
  • requirement
    2.0 93/m
    a requirement is something needed or demanded for a purpose, while a condition is a clause that must be met.
  • term
    3.3 154/m
    a term often appears as one item in a contract's terms, whereas a condition is a required clause or assumption.
