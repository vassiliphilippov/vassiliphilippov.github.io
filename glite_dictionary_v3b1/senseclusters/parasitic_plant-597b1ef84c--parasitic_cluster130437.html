parasitic plant

noun
plant that obtains nutrients from another plant
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk plænt / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that lives on another plant and obtains its organic nutrients from the host

Examples

A parasitic plant species
Dodder is a well-known example of a parasitic plant found in many gardens.
Some parasitic plants lack chlorophyll and rely entirely on other plants for food.
The parasitic plant wrapped around the tree, drawing nutrients from its host.

Related Terms

  • antonym: host plant - a plant that supports a parasite or symbiont
  • synonym: parasite - an organism living on or in another, benefiting at the host's expense
