visible balance

noun
difference in a nation's exports and imports of goods
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪzəbl ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈvɪzəbəl ˈbæləns / (BrE)
the difference between the value of a country's exports and imports of physical goods over a specified period

Examples

The visible balance of trade
A negative visible balance can place pressure on a nation's currency value.
Analysts are closely watching the visible balance as imports of electronics continue to rise.
The country's visible balance improved after it increased its exports of manufactured goods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: invisible balance - the difference in value of services and other non-goods components in trade
