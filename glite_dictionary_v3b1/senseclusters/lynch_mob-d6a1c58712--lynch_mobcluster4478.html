lynch mob

noun
mob executing punishment without legal authority
Pronunciation: / lɪntʃ mɑb / (AmE), / lɪntʃ mɒb / (BrE)
A group of people who kill or threaten to kill someone, typically by hanging, for an alleged offense without legal authority, often motivated by mob justice or prejudice.

Examples

A lynch mob forms
A lynch mob formed outside the jail, demanding immediate punishment for the accused.
History remembers the grave injustice carried out by the lynch mob in the small Southern town.
The film depicted a lynch mob chasing an innocent man through the streets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mob - a large disorderly crowd, especially one intent on causing trouble
  • synonym: vigilante - a person who undertakes law enforcement without legal authority
