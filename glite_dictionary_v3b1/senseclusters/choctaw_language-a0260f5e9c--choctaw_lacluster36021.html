Choctaw language

noun
language of the Choctaw people
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑːktɔː ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒktɔː ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language belonging to the Choctaw people, part of the Muskhogean language family, traditionally spoken in the southeastern United States

Examples

The Choctaw language
He grew up speaking both English and the Choctaw language at home.
Linguists study the grammar and structure of the Choctaw language to preserve its heritage.
The Choctaw language is taught in some schools in Oklahoma and Mississippi.

  • degree higher: Muskogean language - a language family including Choctaw and related southeastern U.S. languages
