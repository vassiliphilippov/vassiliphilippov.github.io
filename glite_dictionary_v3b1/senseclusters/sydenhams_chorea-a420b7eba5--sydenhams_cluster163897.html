Sydenham's chorea

noun
involuntary movement disorder linked to rheumatic fever
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪd(ə)nəmz kɔˈriə / (AmE), / ˈsɪd(ə)nəmz kɔˈriːə / (BrE)
A form of chorea, primarily affecting children, that is associated with rheumatic fever, characterized by rapid, involuntary movements of the face, hands, and feet.

Examples

Sydenham's chorea in children
Sydenham's chorea is a serious complication that can occur during rheumatic fever in children.
The doctor diagnosed the child with Sydenham's chorea after noting her sudden, involuntary movements.
Treatment for Sydenham's chorea often involves medications to manage movement and prevent further heart damage.
