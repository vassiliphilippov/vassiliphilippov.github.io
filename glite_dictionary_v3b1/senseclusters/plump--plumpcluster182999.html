plump

verb
choose or support (often with 'for')
7.4 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / plÊŒmp / (AmE), / plÊŒmp / (BrE)
to support or choose one out of a group; to express a clear preference (often used with 'for')

Examples

Plump for the winner
After much debate, she plumped for the red dress over the blue one.
Many voters plumped for the new candidate in the recent election.
When offered dessert, he automatically plumped for the chocolate cake.

Synonyms

  • plump for
    7.3 0.07/m
    plump for expresses a clear choice between options in speech, while plump alone is rarely used without the particle 'for'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choose - to pick or select from a number of alternatives
  • synonym: prefer - to like one thing better than another and choose it if possible
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or consider
