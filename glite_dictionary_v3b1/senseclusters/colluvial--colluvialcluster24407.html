colluvial

adjective
gravity-deposited soil
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈluːviəl / (AmE), / kəˈluːvɪəl / (BrE)
describing soil or material that has been moved and deposited at the base of a slope by gravity

Examples

Colluvial soil deposits
The geologist identified the colluvial deposits at the base of the steep hillside.
After heavy rains, a thick layer of colluvial soil accumulated at the foot of the slope.
Farmers avoid planting crops on colluvial ground because it is unstable after landslides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alluvial - relating to soil or material deposited by water
