cosmic background radiation

noun
radiation left from the Big Bang
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑzmɪk ˌbækˌɡraʊnd reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɪk ˌbækˌɡraʊnd reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the faint thermal radiation left over from the Big Bang, filling the universe and observed at a nearly uniform temperature of about 2.7 kelvin

Examples

Cosmic background radiation temperature
Scientists study the cosmic background radiation to understand the origins and structure of the universe.
Sensitive satellites have been launched to measure slight variations in the cosmic background radiation.
The discovery of cosmic background radiation was a major piece of evidence supporting the Big Bang theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cosmic microwave background - the electromagnetic radiation remnant from the early universe, also called CMB
