heteropterous insect

noun
true bug with leathery-tipped forewings
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˈrɑptərəs ɪnˈsɛkt / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒptərəs ɪnˈsɛkt / (BrE)
a member of a group of insects (true bugs) whose forewings are partly leathery at the tips and partly membranous

Examples

Heteropterous insect species
Many farmers are familiar with the damage that certain heteropterous insects can cause to crops.
The scientist observed a heteropterous insect resting on the leaf.
Unlike beetles, a heteropterous insect has forewings that are only partly hardened.

Related Terms

  • synonym: true bug - an insect of the order Hemiptera, having forewings with both leathery and membranous parts
