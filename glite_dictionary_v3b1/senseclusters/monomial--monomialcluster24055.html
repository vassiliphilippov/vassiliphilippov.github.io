monomial

noun
single-term expression
Pronunciation: / məˈnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / məˈnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
an algebraic expression that consists of only one term, such as a number, a variable, or their product

Examples

A monomial term
The teacher asked the students to identify the monomial in the equation.
A monomial like 7y can be multiplied by another to form a polynomial.
In algebra, 5x^2 is an example of a monomial.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: binomial - an algebraic expression with exactly two terms
  • antonym: polynomial - an algebraic expression with two or more terms
