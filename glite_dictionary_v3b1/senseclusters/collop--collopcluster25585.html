collop

verb
to slice thinly
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.ləp/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.ləp/ (BrE)
to cut or slice meat or other food into thin pieces or portions

Examples

To collop the meat
The chef began to collop the beef for the stew, making sure each piece was even.
Before adding the chicken to the pan, she colloped it into thin strips.
You should collop the vegetables so they cook more quickly and evenly.
