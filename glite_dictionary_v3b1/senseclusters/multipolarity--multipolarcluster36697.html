multipolarity

noun
many centers of power
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltipoʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪpəˈlærəti / (BrE)
a situation in which power or influence is shared among several countries or groups rather than dominated by one

Examples

Global multipolarity
The emergence of multipolarity has led to new alliances in international relations.
Experts debate whether multipolarity makes the world more stable or more unpredictable.
Economic multipolarity is evident as several countries now drive global growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bipolarity - a situation where two countries or groups dominate power
  • antonym: unipolarity - a situation where one country or group holds most of the power
