hastatus

noun
Roman infantryman
9.9
Pronunciation: / hæsˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / hæsˈtɑːtəs / (BrE)
a member of the lowest class of infantry in the ancient Roman army, typically armed with a spear and shield

Examples

Roman hastatus soldier
The hastatus stood at the front of the formation, ready to face the enemy.
Each hastatus carried a long spear and a small shield into battle.
In Roman military tactics, the hastatus played a crucial role in the first line of attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legionary - a soldier in the Roman army
  • degree higher: principes - a member of the second line of Roman infantry
  • degree higher: triarii - a member of the veteran third line of Roman infantry
