escrow

noun
money or property held by a third party until a condition is met
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛs.kroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛs.krəʊ / (BrE)
money, property, or a written agreement held by a third party to be delivered upon fulfillment of a condition in a contract.

Examples

Escrow account
The funds were placed in escrow until the construction was complete.
They agreed to use an escrow service for the online transaction to protect both parties.
Upon closing the deal, the escrow was released to the seller.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trust - financial arrangement in which a third party holds property for the benefit of another
