carotid body

noun
chemoreceptor near carotid artery regulating respiration
8.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑːt̬.ɪd ˈbɑː.di / (AmE), / kəˈrɒt.ɪd ˈbɒd.i / (BrE)
a small mass of tissue located near the bifurcation of the carotid artery that acts as a chemoreceptor, monitoring the oxygen content of the blood and helping regulate respiration

Examples

The carotid body tissue
Damage to the carotid body can impair the regulation of respiration.
Researchers studied the function of the carotid body in patients with sleep apnea.
The carotid body detects low oxygen levels in the blood and sends signals to adjust breathing rate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aortic body - a similar chemoreceptor located near the aortic arch
