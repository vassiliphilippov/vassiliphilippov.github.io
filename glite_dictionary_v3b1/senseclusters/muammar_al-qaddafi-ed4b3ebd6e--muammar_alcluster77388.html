Muammar al-Qaddafi

proper noun
Libyan leader (1969–2011)
8.8
Pronunciation: /muː.ˈæm.ər əl kə.ˈdɑː.fiː/ /; / /muː.ˈæm.ər æl kə.ˈdæ.fiː/ (AmE), /muː.ˈæm.ə əl kə.ˈdɑː.fiː/ /; / /muː.ˈæm.ə æl kə.ˈdæ.fiː/ (BrE)
(1942–2011) The Libyan political leader who ruled Libya from 1969, after deposing the monarchy in a military coup, until 2011; known for imposing socialist policies and promoting Islamic orthodoxy.

Examples

The military coup led by Muammar al-Qaddafi in 1969 dramatically changed Libya's political landscape.
