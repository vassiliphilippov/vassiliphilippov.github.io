tumble

verb
fall suddenly and sharply
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəl /; / ˈtʌmbl̩ / (BrE)
to suffer a sudden defeat, failure, collapse, or loss of value or status

Examples

Prices tumble fast
Housing prices began to tumble after the economic crisis.
Shares may tumble if the company announces lower profits next quarter.
The statue lost its balance and started to tumble down the stairs.
The ancient wall looked like it might tumble at any moment.
The company began to tumble after years of financial mismanagement.
When trust is lost, relationships often tumble beyond repair.
After years of dominance, the champion team finally tumbled in the semifinals.
Many leaders have tumbled from power after losing public support.
Stocks tumbled when the unexpected announcement shook investors' confidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break down - to cease to function or to fail
  • synonym: collapse - (of a structure or organization) to fall down or give way suddenly
  • synonym: crumble - to break or fall apart into small fragments
  • synonym: fall - to descend suddenly and freely; also to suffer defeat or loss of status
  • synonym: plunge - to fall or drop suddenly in amount or value
  • antonym: rise - to move upwards or increase in value
