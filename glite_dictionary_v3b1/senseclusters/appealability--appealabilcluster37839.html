appealability

noun
ability to be appealed
Pronunciation: / əˌpiːləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / əˌpiːləˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being able to be appealed to a higher authority or court

Examples

The appealability of a ruling
The appealability of the court's ruling was discussed at length by the legal team.
Questions about the appealability of the verdict delayed the start of the next trial.
Lawyers debated whether the decision had any appealability under current regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appealable - able to be taken to a higher court for review
  • antonym: finality - the quality of being final and not subject to appeal
