portentous

adjective
ominously significant; foreboding
8.6 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / p…îrÀàt…õnt…ôs / (AmE), / p…îÀêÀàt…õnt…ôs / (BrE)
giving a sign or warning that something important or bad is going to happen; ominous or prophetic

Examples

Dark portentous clouds
Dark, portentous clouds gathered above the city, warning of the approaching storm.
His voice took on a portentous tone as he predicted the company's downfall.
The old castle had a portentous atmosphere that made visitors feel uneasy.
A portentous silence filled the room as the announcement approached.
His portentous tone suggested that something important and troubling was about to be revealed.

Synonyms

  • ominous
    6.4 7.21/m
    ominous gives a threatening, uneasy sense that something bad will happen, while portentous also suggests an important, prophetic sign.
  • premonitory
    9.8 0.08/m
    premonitory serves as a clear warning sign of danger to come, while portentous adds a weighty, often prophetic sense.
  • precursory
    8.1 0.04/m
    precursory appears before an event and hints something will happen, while portentous stresses a warning that the event matters.
  • foreboding
    7.3 0.65/m
    foreboding creates a strong, fearful feeling that trouble is near, while portentous focuses on a sign or warning of importance.
  • prefigurative
    9.9 0.02/m
    prefigurative suggests a vague or symbolic hint of what may come, while portentous gives a clearer, weighty warning of future events.
  • prophetic
    7.4 4.15/m
    prophetic predicts future events often with accuracy or vision, while portentous adds an ominous, noticeable sense to that prediction.
  • fateful
    6.5 2.87/m
    fateful means causing a decisive, often disastrous outcome, while portentous warns of something important or ominous before it happens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ominous - suggesting that something bad will happen
