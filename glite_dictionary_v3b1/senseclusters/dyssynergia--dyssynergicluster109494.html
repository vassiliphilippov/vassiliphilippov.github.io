dyssynergia

noun
loss of coordination of voluntary muscles
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪˈnɜrdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪˈnɜːdʒɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an inability to coordinate voluntary muscle movements, often resulting in unsteady movements and a staggering gait.

Examples

Cerebellar dyssynergia
Cerebellar dyssynergia can cause difficulties in performing precise tasks such as writing or buttoning a shirt.
Patients with dyssynergia often present with an unsteady gait and frequent stumbling.
The neurologist diagnosed him with dyssynergia after observing his uncoordinated movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ataxia - impaired balance or coordination, often due to neurological problems
