welfarism

noun
focus on individual welfare
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛlˌfɛrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌfɛərɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that the value of actions, policies, or states should be judged solely by their effects on individual welfare or well-being

Examples

Ethical welfarism theory
The government's policies were influenced by welfarism, aiming to maximize the well-being of all citizens.
Critics argue that welfarism overlooks important values like justice and freedom.
Many debates in economics center around the principles of welfarism and how to measure individual welfare.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarianism - the belief in equal rights and opportunities for all people
  • synonym: utilitarianism - the ethical theory that actions are right if they benefit the majority
