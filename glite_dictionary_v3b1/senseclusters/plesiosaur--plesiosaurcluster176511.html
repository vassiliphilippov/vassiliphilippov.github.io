plesiosaur

noun
extinct marine reptile of Mesozoic era
Pronunciation: / ˈpliːsiəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈpliːsiəˌsɔː / (BrE)
an extinct marine reptile of the Mesozoic era, with a small head, long neck, short tail, and four paddle-shaped limbs

Examples

The plesiosaur fossil
Many fossils of the plesiosaur have been found in ancient seabeds.
Scientists believe the plesiosaur became extinct at the end of the Cretaceous period.
The plesiosaur had a distinctive long neck and small head, unlike most reptiles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dinosaur - a group of extinct terrestrial reptiles of the Mesozoic era
  • synonym: pterosaur - extinct flying reptile of the Mesozoic era
