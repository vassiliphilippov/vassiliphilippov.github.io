enfranchiser

noun
grantor of rights
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzər / (AmE), / ɪnˈfræntʃaɪzə / (BrE)
a person or organization that gives someone important rights or freedoms, such as the right to vote or freedom from slavery

Examples

The enfranchiser grants rights
The government acted as the main enfranchiser by extending voting rights to all adults.
As an enfranchiser, the organization helped former prisoners regain their right to vote.
The new policy made the local council an important enfranchiser in the community.
The enfranchiser played a key role in the movement to abolish slavery.
As an enfranchiser, she worked tirelessly to secure voting rights for women.
History remembers him as an enfranchiser who fought for the freedom of others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disenfranchiser - a person or entity that takes away the right to vote or privileges
  • synonym: emancipator - a person who frees others from bondage or oppression
  • synonym: grantor - a person who gives or grants something
  • antonym: oppressor - a person who keeps others in subjugation or hardship
