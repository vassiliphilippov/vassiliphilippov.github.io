gavel to order

verb
open a meeting using a gavel
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡævəl tə ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈɡævəl tʊ ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
to officially start a meeting or session by striking a gavel, typically to call for attention or commence proceedings

Examples

To gavel to order
As soon as the mayor gavels to order, all discussion must cease and the agenda starts.
The chairman gaveled to order at precisely 9 a.m. to begin the annual conference.
The judge gaveled to order the hearing, signaling that the proceedings were officially underway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: call to order - to officially begin a meeting, often by speaking or using a gavel
