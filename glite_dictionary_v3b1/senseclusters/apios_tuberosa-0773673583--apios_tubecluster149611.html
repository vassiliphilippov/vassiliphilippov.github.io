Apios tuberosa

noun
North American vine with edible tubers
Pronunciation: / əˈpaɪ.əs tuˈbɜr.oʊ.sə / (AmE), / əˈpaɪ.ɒs tjuːˈbɜː.rə.sə / (BrE)
A North American vine, also known as groundnut, with fragrant blossoms and edible tubers, historically an important food crop for Native Americans.

Examples

Apios tuberosa plant
Botanists studied Apios tuberosa to learn more about its agricultural potential.
For centuries, Native Americans relied on Apios tuberosa as a staple source of nutrition.
The edible tubers of Apios tuberosa can be cooked much like potatoes.
