favor

noun
feeling of approval or preference
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪvər /; / ˈfeɪvɚ /; / ˈfeɪ.vər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvə /; / ˈfeɪ.və / (BrE)
approval, support, or preference shown toward someone or something; an advantage or benefit given

Examples

The favor of the king
He clearly enjoyed the favor of the king, which gave him special privileges at court.
Her new ideas quickly found favor among other committee members.
The company lost the favor of its customers after raising prices.
The judge ruled in the company’s favor, granting them compensation.
The new policy operates to the favor of local businesses.
The unexpected vote swung the decision in her favor.
His proposal quickly gained favor among the committee members.
That style is currently in favor with young professionals.
The new policy fell out of favor after several complaints were raised.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approval - the belief that something or someone is good or acceptable
  • antonym: disfavor - a state or feeling of being disliked or having lost approval
  • antonym: disfavour - disapproval or lack of support
  • synonym: favor - American spelling of 'favour' (approval or support)
  • synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over another or others
