Enki

proper noun
Sumerian god of water and wisdom
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛŋ.ki / (AmE), / ˈɛŋ.ki / (BrE)
A god of water, knowledge, crafts, and creation in ancient Sumerian mythology, considered the counterpart of the Akkadian god Ea.

Examples

Enki god of water
In ancient Mesopotamian texts, Enki is often invoked as a god of wisdom and creation.
Scholars believe that Enki played a central role in Sumerian cosmology and mythology.
The temple dedicated to Enki in Eridu was among the earliest religious sites in Mesopotamia.
