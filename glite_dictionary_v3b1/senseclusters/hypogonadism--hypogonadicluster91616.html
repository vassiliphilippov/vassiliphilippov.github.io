hypogonadism

noun
deficiency of sex hormones
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈɡoʊnəˌdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈɡəʊnədɪzəm / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the gonads produce insufficient sex hormones, resulting in reduced development of secondary sex characteristics.

Examples

Hypogonadism treatment
Hormone replacement therapy is often used to treat hypogonadism in both men and women.
Long-term hypogonadism in men can lead to decreased muscle mass and infertility.
The doctor diagnosed her with hypogonadism after noticing delayed puberty.
