nonrelative

noun
person not family
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈrɛlətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈrɛlətɪv / (BrE)
a person who is not related to you by blood or marriage

Examples

A helpful nonrelative
A nonrelative offered to help with the move when no family was available.
The scholarship is open to any student, whether a nonrelative or a family member of the staff.
She trusted her friend, even though he was a nonrelative, to handle her affairs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relative - a person connected to you by blood or marriage
  • synonym: stranger - a person you do not know
