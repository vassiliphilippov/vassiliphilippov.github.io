interplant

verb
to plant mixed crops
5.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈplænt/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈplɑːnt/ (BrE)
to plant different types of plants or crops together in the same area

Examples

To interplant crops
Farmers often interplant beans and corn to improve soil fertility and reduce pests.
Gardeners may interplant flowers with vegetables to attract pollinators and deter insects.
By interplanting lettuce with taller crops, you can make better use of garden space.
Many gardeners interplant herbs and vegetables to make better use of space.
Farmers sometimes interplant beans with corn to improve soil quality.
You can interplant flowers among your vegetables to attract pollinators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: companion planting - growing different plants together for mutual benefit
  • antonym: crop rotation - the practice of growing different crops in succession on the same land
  • synonym: intercrop - to grow two or more crops together in the same field
  • antonym: monocrop - to grow only one type of crop in an area
  • antonym: monoculture - the cultivation of a single crop in a given area
