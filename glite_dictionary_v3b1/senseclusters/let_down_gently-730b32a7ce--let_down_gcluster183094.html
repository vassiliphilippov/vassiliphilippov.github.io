let down gently

verb
tell bad news or reject kindly
Pronunciation: / lɛt daʊn ˈdʒɛntli / (AmE), / lɛt daʊn ˈdʒɛntli / (BrE)
to inform someone of disappointment, rejection, or bad news in a kind and considerate way so as to minimize their distress

Examples

To let down gently someone
He tried to let her down gently after she interviewed for the position, praising her skills first.
The company decided to let down gently those applicants who were not selected by sending personalized letters.
When ending the relationship, Maria made sure to let him down gently to spare his feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: let down - to disappoint someone; to fail to do what is expected
