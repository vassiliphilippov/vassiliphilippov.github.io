ominous

adjective
suggesting something bad will happen
6.4 7.21/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀê.m…ô.n…ôs / (AmE), / Àà…ím.…™.n…ôs / (BrE)
giving the impression or warning that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen

Examples

An ominous tone
Her ominous tone made everyone in the room uneasy.
The sky was covered with ominous clouds, warning of an approaching storm.
There was an ominous silence in the hallway after the principal walked past.
She received an ominous phone call that made her uneasy all day.
The silence in the abandoned building felt particularly ominous at night.
The sky was filled with ominous dark clouds before the storm began.

Synonyms

  • foreboding
    7.3 0.65/m
    foreboding gives a strong, uneasy sense that something bad is coming, while ominous warns more quietly of trouble.
  • portentous
    8.6 0.58/m
    portentous signals an important, often serious event ahead, while ominous simply warns that something bad may happen.
  • premonitory
    9.8 0.08/m
    premonitory shows a clear early sign before danger or harm appears, while ominous creates a general warning feeling.
  • threatening
    3.6 4.68/m
    threatening is a direct sign of danger from someone or something, while ominous is more atmospheric and indirect.
  • menacing
    5.0 0.02/m
    menacing looks or actions clearly suggest harm or attack, while ominous gives a quieter sense that trouble may come.
  • sinister
    6.1 5.89/m
    sinister feels evil and secret, suggesting hidden harm or bad plans, while ominous warns of trouble without saying why.
  • baleful
    8.4 0.55/m
    baleful looks or words promise harm in a dark, angry way, while ominous is a more general warning of bad events.
  • inauspicious
    8.1 0.36/m
    inauspicious suggests bad luck or a poor chance of success, while ominous warns of a coming harmful event.
  • forbidding
    6.6 0.00/m
    forbidding looks rough, unfriendly, or hard to approach, while ominous hints that something bad may occur soon.
  • minatory
    9.9 0.01/m
    minatory states a clear threat in formal or legal tones, while ominous is a broader warning used in everyday language.
  • minacious
    9.9 0.00/m
    minacious sounds rare and literary when it threatens harm or danger, while ominous is common and widely used to warn.
