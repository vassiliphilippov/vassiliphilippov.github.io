ominous

adjective
suggesting something bad will happen
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑː.mə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɒm.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
giving the impression or warning that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen

Examples

An ominous tone
Her ominous tone made everyone in the room uneasy.
The sky was covered with ominous clouds, warning of an approaching storm.
There was an ominous silence in the hallway after the principal walked past.
She received an ominous phone call that made her uneasy all day.
The silence in the abandoned building felt particularly ominous at night.
The sky was filled with ominous dark clouds before the storm began.

Related Terms

  • antonym: auspicious - suggesting a positive or successful outcome
  • synonym: inauspicious - not conducive to success; unpromising
  • synonym: menacing - threatening harm or danger; suggesting menace
  • antonym: promising - giving hope or indication of success or of a positive outcome
  • synonym: sinister - giving the impression that something harmful is happening or will happen
  • synonym: threatening - suggesting danger or harm
