tone

noun
pitch quality that distinguishes words (linguistics)
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /toʊn/ (AmE), /təʊn/ (BrE)
the pitch, quality, or character of a sound, especially in music or language

Examples

A high tone
In Vietnamese, a single syllable can have multiple meanings depending on its tone.
Learning to differentiate the four tones in Cantonese can be challenging for beginners.
Mandarin is a language in which each syllable has a specific tone that changes the meaning of a word.
In Western music, a major scale is made up of five tones and two semitones.
She practiced singing scales, paying attention to each tone and semitone along the way.
The interval from C to D is a tone, while from C to C-sharp is a semitone.
Each tone on the sheet music corresponds to a specific sound on the instrument.
He struggled to read the tone at the beginning of the passage.
The composer carefully wrote every tone to match the lyrics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of the voice in speaking, not lexical in most languages
  • antonym: noise - a sound, especially one that is loud or unpleasant or that causes disturbance
  • synonym: note - a symbol denoting the pitch and duration of a musical sound
  • degree lower: semitone - a musical interval of half a tone
  • synonym: timbre - the character or quality of a musical sound or voice
