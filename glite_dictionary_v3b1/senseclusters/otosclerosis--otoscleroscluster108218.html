otosclerosis

noun
hereditary inner ear bone disorder
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊtoʊskləˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊtəʊskləˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A hereditary disorder in which abnormal bone growth in the inner ear (labyrinth) causes hearing loss, tinnitus, and sometimes deafness.

Examples

Hereditary otosclerosis disorder
Because otosclerosis runs in families, genetic counseling was recommended.
The doctor diagnosed her with otosclerosis after noting her gradual hearing loss.
Treatment options for otosclerosis may include hearing aids or surgery.
