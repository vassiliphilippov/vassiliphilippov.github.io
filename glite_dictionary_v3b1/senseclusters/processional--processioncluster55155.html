processional

noun
music for a religious procession
Pronunciation: / prəˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / prəˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a piece of music or performance played or sung during a formal or religious procession

Examples

A processional hymn
A solemn processional marked the beginning of the ceremony.
The choir rehearsed the processional for the upcoming Easter service.
The organist played a beautiful processional as the bride entered the church.
The organist played a solemn processional as the wedding party entered the church.
Graduates walked in time to the processional at the start of the ceremony.
The choir rehearsed the processional for the upcoming religious festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: march - a piece of music with a strong regular rhythm, often for processions
  • antonym: recessional - music played at the end of a religious service or ceremony
