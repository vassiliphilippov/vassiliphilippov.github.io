murky

adjective
cloudy or dark (liquid, with sediment)
3.7 2/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ùÀêki / (AmE), / Ààm…úÀêki / (BrE)
(of a liquid) dark or cloudy, especially because of suspended dirt, sediment, or other particles.

Examples

Murky water
After stirring the sediment, the wine became murky and unappetizing.
The murky water in the pond made it impossible to see the fish.
We couldn't drink from the river because the water was too murky.

Synonyms

  • turbid
    8.1 0.3/m
    turbid is a technical word for cloudy water with stirred up particles, while murky suggests darker, dirtier liquid seen in nature.
  • muddy
    1.1 7/m
    muddy describes water or ground mixed with mud and feels thick and dirty, while murky focuses on darkness or cloudiness.
  • cloudy
    2.3 0.9/m
    cloudy is used for water that is not clear but still light, while murky suggests darker, dirtier liquid hard to see through.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - (of a liquid) transparent, with nothing suspended in it
  • synonym: cloudy - (of a liquid) not clear; containing small particles that make it hard to see through
