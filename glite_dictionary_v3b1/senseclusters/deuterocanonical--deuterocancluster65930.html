deuterocanonical

adjective
of secondary canon
Pronunciation: / ˌduːtərəkoʊˈnɑːnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdjuːtərəˌkænɪˈkəʊnɪkəl / (BrE)
regarded as belonging to a second canon of Scripture, accepted as canonical by some Christian traditions but not by others

Examples

Deuterocanonical books
The Book of Judith is considered deuterocanonical by the Catholic Church but not by most Protestant denominations.
Scholars often debate the status of deuterocanonical books in the history of the Bible.
Some Christian traditions include deuterocanonical texts in their Old Testament readings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apocryphal - of doubtful authenticity or not accepted as part of the official canon
  • degree higher: canonical - accepted as part of the official canon of Scripture
