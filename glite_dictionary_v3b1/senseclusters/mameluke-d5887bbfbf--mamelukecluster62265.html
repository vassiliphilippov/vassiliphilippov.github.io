Mameluke

noun
ceremonial curved sword
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmæməˌluk / (AmE), / ˈmamɪluːk / (BrE)
a short, curved ceremonial sword modeled after those used by the historical Mamelukes, often carried by military officers as a dress sword

Examples

Mameluke sword
The officer marched with a polished Mameluke at his side during the ceremonial review.
He admired the ornate handle of the Mameluke displayed in the museum's military collection.
Many Marine Corps officers carry a Mameluke as part of their formal dress uniform.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sabre - a curved, single-edged sword used in cavalry
  • synonym: scimitar - a curved, single-edged sword of Eastern origin
