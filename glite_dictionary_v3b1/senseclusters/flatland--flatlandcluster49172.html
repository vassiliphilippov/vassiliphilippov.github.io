flatland

noun
dull or unvaried place
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈflætˌlænd / (BrE)
a place or situation that is dull, unvaried, or lacking in excitement, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Cultural flatland
After years in the suburbs, she described her town as a cultural flatland of few surprises.
He longed to escape the flatland of his routine office job for something more adventurous.
The novel portrays the city as a flatland where nothing ever changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excitement - a feeling of great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • synonym: monotony - lack of variety and interest; tedious repetition
