butcherbird

noun
a shrike that impales its prey
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊtʃɚˌbɜrd /; / ˈbʊtʃɚˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈbʊtʃəbɜːd / (BrE)
a predatory songbird (such as a shrike or an Australian butcherbird) known for impaling its prey on thorns or barbed wire

Examples

Butcherbirds hunt prey
Butcherbirds use their hooked bills to hunt small animals and store them for later.
The butcherbird is notorious for catching insects and impaling them on sharp twigs.
We spotted a butcherbird perched on a fence post with a grasshopper in its beak.
Several butcherbirds have built their nests in the eucalyptus trees along the trail.
The butcherbird is known for its eerie song and its habit of impaling insects on thorns.
We saw a butcherbird catch a lizard and secure it on a sharp branch.
