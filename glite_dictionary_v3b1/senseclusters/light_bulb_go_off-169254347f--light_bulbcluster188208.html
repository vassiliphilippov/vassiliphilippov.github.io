light bulb go off

verb
sudden realization or inspiration
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌbʌlb ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌbʌlb ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
used to describe a moment when someone suddenly realizes or understands something, often represented as a flash of inspiration

Examples

As soon as he saw the missing piece, the light bulb went off and he solved the puzzle.
During the meeting, a light bulb went off in her mind and she proposed a new strategy.
It took a while, but when the light bulb finally went off, everything made perfect sense.
