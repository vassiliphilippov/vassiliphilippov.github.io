solicit

verb
request earnestly or persistently
7.5
Pronunciation: / səˈlɪsɪt /; / səˈlɪs.ɪt / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪt /; / səˈlɪs.ɪt / (BrE)
to ask for something from someone, especially in a formal, earnest, or persistent way

Examples

To solicit donations
He has solicited signatures for the petition all week.
Teachers often solicit feedback from students to improve their lessons.
The charity will solicit donations from local businesses to support its cause.
It is not legal to solicit money on this property without a permit.
Nonprofit organizations often solicit donations from the public to fund their activities.
The politician decided to solicit feedback from her constituents before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • antonym: offer - present or propose something for acceptance or rejection
  • synonym: petition - make a formal request for something
  • synonym: request - to ask for something politely or formally
