heterolytic

adjective
bond splits unevenly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
describing a chemical bond breaking in which both electrons from the bond go to one atom, forming ions

Examples

Heterolytic bond cleavage
In a heterolytic cleavage, the bond breaks so that both electrons go to the same atom.
A heterolytic reaction often results in the formation of a cation and an anion.
Chemists study heterolytic processes to understand how ions are produced in chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homolytic - describing a bond breaking where each atom takes one electron
