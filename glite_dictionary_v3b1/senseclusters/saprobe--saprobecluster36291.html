saprobe

noun
organism feeding on decaying matter
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈproʊb / (AmE), / səˈprəʊb / (BrE)
An organism that obtains its nourishment from decaying organic matter, especially in stagnant or foul water.

Examples

A saprobe organism
A saprobe plays an important role in recycling nutrients within aquatic ecosystems.
Fungi are classic examples of saprobes, breaking down organic matter in the soil.
The pond was filled with saprobes thriving on decaying leaves and dead insects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: saprophyte - organism that feeds on decaying organic matter, especially a plant or fungus
