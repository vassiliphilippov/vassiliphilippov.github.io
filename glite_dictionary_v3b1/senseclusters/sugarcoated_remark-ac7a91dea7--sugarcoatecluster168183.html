sugarcoated remark

noun
a softened or flattering critical comment
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʊɡɚˌkoʊtɪd rɪˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡəkəʊtɪd rɪˈmɑːk / (BrE)
a comment that makes criticism sound less harsh by expressing it in a pleasant or flattering way

Examples

A sugarcoated remark
He preferred honesty over a sugarcoated remark that hid the truth.
The manager delivered a sugarcoated remark to soften the blow of the negative feedback.
Though her sugarcoated remark sounded kind, it masked an underlying critique of his work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backhanded compliment - an insult disguised as a compliment
  • antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or judgment
