coccyx

noun
small bone at base of spine (tailbone)
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑk.sɪks / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.sɪks / (BrE)
the small, triangular bone at the base of the human spine, formed by the fusion of usually four rudimentary vertebrae; commonly called the tailbone

Examples

The coccyx bone
Pain in the coccyx can make sitting uncomfortable for prolonged periods.
She fractured her coccyx when she slipped on the ice.
The doctor examined his coccyx to check for injury after the fall.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: sacrum - the large, triangular bone above the coccyx at the base of the spine
  • synonym: tailbone - common term for the coccyx
