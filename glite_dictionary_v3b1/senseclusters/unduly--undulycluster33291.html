unduly

adverb
to an excessive or improper degree
6.9 2.35/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàduÀêli / (AmE), /  ånÀàdjuÀêli / (BrE)
to an excessive, unreasonable, or unnecessary degree; more than is appropriate or justified.

Examples

An unduly harsh comment
His remarks were criticized as being unduly negative during the meeting.
She was unduly worried about the possibility of failure.
The judge decided the punishment was unduly harsh for such a minor offense.
