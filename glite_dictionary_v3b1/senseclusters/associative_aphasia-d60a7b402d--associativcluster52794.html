associative aphasia

noun
difficulty repeating speech due to brain lesion
9.1
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsoʊ.si.ə.tɪv ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /ə.ˈsoʊ.ʃi.ə.tɪv ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsəʊ.si.ə.tɪv ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /ə.ˈsəʊ.ʃi.ə.tɪv ə.ˈfeɪ.zɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A type of aphasia in which the affected person has difficulty repeating spoken sentences, typically due to a lesion in the brain's association tracts that connect language centers.

Examples

Associative aphasia repetition difficulty
Neurologists suspect associative aphasia when repetition is specifically impaired, but comprehension remains intact.
The patient diagnosed with associative aphasia was unable to repeat simple sentences spoken to him.
Treatment for associative aphasia focuses on improving the brain's ability to process and relay spoken language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conduction aphasia - A similar type of aphasia characterized by an inability to repeat words or phrases
