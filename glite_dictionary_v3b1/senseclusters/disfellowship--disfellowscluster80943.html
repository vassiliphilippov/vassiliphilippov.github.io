disfellowship

noun
formal exclusion
9.5
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈfel.oʊ.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈfel.əʊ.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
the formal act of excluding someone from membership in a group or community, especially for disciplinary reasons

Examples

Church disfellowship
The church elders decided on disfellowship after a lengthy investigation into his actions.
Her disfellowship from the organization was a difficult but necessary decision.
Members are warned that repeated violations could result in disfellowship from the community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admission - the act of allowing someone to join a group or organization
  • synonym: excommunication - the act of officially excluding someone from participation in a religious community
  • synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave an organization or place
