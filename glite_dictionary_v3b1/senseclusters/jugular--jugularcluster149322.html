jugular

noun
vulnerable or crucial part
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡjʊlə / (BrE)
A vital or vulnerable part of something, especially one that is open to attack.

Examples

Go for the jugular
The candidate went straight for his opponent's jugular during the debate.
Their strategy was to attack the company's jugular by targeting its most profitable product line.
Whenever she feels threatened, she instinctively goes for the jugular in arguments.
