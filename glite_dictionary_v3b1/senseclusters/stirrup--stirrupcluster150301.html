stirrup

noun
ossicle bone of the middle ear
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈstɪrəp / (BrE)
A small stirrup-shaped bone (the stapes) in the middle ear that transmits sound vibrations from the incus to the cochlea.

Examples

Ear stirrup bone
Damage to the stirrup can result in significant hearing loss or deafness.
Doctors examined the patient's middle ear and found that the stirrup was malformed.
The stirrup is the smallest bone in the human body and plays a vital role in hearing.
