reorder

verb
place an order for more goods or services
2.5 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / riÀà…îrd…ö / (AmE), / riÀà…îÀêd…ô / (BrE)
to place an order again for goods or services, especially when the original supply has run out

Examples

To reorder the product
After selling out of the product, we decided to reorder it from our supplier.
If you finish your prescription, you can easily reorder your medication online.
The store had to reorder the popular CD several times to keep up with demand.

Synonyms

  • restock
    3.2 0.82/m
    restock means filling a shop or shelf with goods again, while reorder means placing a new purchase order.
  • resupply
    3.5 0.42/m
    resupply is sending supplies again to people or places in need, while reorder is making a purchase order to replace stock.
  • replenish
    4.3 3.23/m
    replenish is refilling something until it is full again, while reorder is placing an order to get more goods.
