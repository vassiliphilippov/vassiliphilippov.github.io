farmer's cheese

noun
mild white cheese from soured skim milk
4.0 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfÉ‘rmÉ™rz ËŒtÊƒiz / (AmE), / ËˆfÉ‘ËmÉ™z ËŒtÊƒiËz / (BrE)
A mild white cheese made from the curds of soured skim milk, usually pressed to remove most of the whey.

Examples

Spread farmer's cheese
She spread farmer's cheese on her toast for a light breakfast.
The recipe calls for a cup of farmer's cheese to be mixed into the batter.
Traditionally, farmer's cheese is used in Eastern European dumplings.

Synonyms

  • farm cheese
    3.0 0.00/m
    farm cheese is a mild white cheese made on small farms and pressed lightly, while farmer's cheese is firmer and pressed to remove most whey.
  • pot cheese
    7.0 0.00/m
    pot cheese is a mild, soft, slightly crumbly cheese used for spreads and cooking, while farmer's cheese is firmer and pressed.
