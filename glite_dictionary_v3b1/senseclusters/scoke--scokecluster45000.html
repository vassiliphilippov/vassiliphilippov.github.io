scoke

noun
American herb with poisonous berries
Pronunciation: / skoʊk / (AmE), / skəʊk / (BrE)
A tall, coarse perennial American herb with small white flowers followed by blackish-red berries on long drooping clusters; its young stems are edible, but its berries and root are poisonous.

Examples

Young scoke shoots
After careful preparation, the young shoots of scoke can be eaten like asparagus.
Native Americans once used parts of the scoke for traditional remedies, despite its toxicity.
The berries of the scoke plant are beautiful but highly poisonous if ingested.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pokeweed - Common name for Phytolacca americana, same species as scoke
