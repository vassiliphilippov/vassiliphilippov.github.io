sabicu

noun
West Indian tree with mahogany-like wood
Pronunciation: / səˈbiːkuː / (AmE), / səˈbiːkuː / (BrE)
A West Indian tree (Lysiloma sabicu) that produces a hard, dark brown wood similar to mahogany in texture and value.

Examples

Sabicu tree Caribbean
Many old Caribbean buildings feature beams made of sturdy sabicu wood.
The botanist explained that sabicu rivals mahogany in both appearance and durability.
The furniture was crafted from rare sabicu, giving it a deep, rich hue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mahogany - a reddish-brown hard wood used for furniture, from various tropical trees
