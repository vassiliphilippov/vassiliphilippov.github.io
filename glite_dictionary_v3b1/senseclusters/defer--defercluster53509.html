defer

verb
postpone to a later time
5.5 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / d…ôÀàf…ùÀê / (AmE), / d…™Ààf…úÀê / (BrE)
to postpone something or delay it until a later time

Examples

To defer a decision
Due to heavy rain, the match was deferred to the following day.
The committee decided to defer the meeting until next week.
We had to defer payment because of unforeseen expenses.

Synonyms

  • postpone
    3.4 9.91/m
    postpone means arranging something to happen later for a clear reason, while defer means to delay something until a later time.
  • put off
    2.9 4.77/m
    put off is informal and means delaying a task until later, while defer is more formal and neutral.
  • delay
    1.4 33/m
    delay means making something happen later or slowing its start, while defer usually suggests choosing to postpone it.
  • hold off
    3.8 2.37/m
    hold off means wait before acting or decide later, while defer often refers to formally postponing a plan.
  • reschedule
    3.0 2.84/m
    reschedule means change a meeting or event to a new time, while defer means delay it without always setting a new date.
  • table
    7.3 0.30/m
    table means put a proposal aside during a meeting, while defer means postpone something to a later time in general.
  • put on ice
    5.8 0.17/m
    put on ice means stop or delay a plan for a while, while defer simply means to postpone to a later time.
  • shelve
    5.0 0.83/m
    shelve means decide not to continue or set a plan aside, while defer means merely to delay it for later.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward in time or make something happen sooner
  • synonym: postpone - to arrange for something to take place at a later time
