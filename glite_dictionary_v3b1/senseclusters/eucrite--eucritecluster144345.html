eucrite

noun
basaltic meteorite
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːkraɪt / (AmE), / ˈjuːkraɪt / (BrE)
a type of stony meteorite made mostly of basaltic rock, often originating from the asteroid Vesta

Examples

A eucrite meteorite
The geologist examined a eucrite under the microscope to study its mineral structure.
Many eucrites are believed to have come from the surface of the asteroid Vesta.
A rare eucrite was discovered in the desert, exciting researchers interested in planetary science.

Related Terms

  • synonym: achondrite - a stony meteorite that does not contain chondrules
  • antonym: chondrite - a common type of stony meteorite containing small mineral grains
