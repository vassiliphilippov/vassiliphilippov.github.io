compensability

noun
ability to be offset
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpənsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpənsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the extent to which something can be counteracted, corrected, or offset by other means or interventions

Examples

Compensability of errors
The compensability of the system was tested under various failure scenarios.
Researchers studied the compensability of the organism's response to environmental stress.
Low compensability means that errors in the process are difficult to correct.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensate - to counteract or make up for something
  • antonym: irreparable - impossible to repair or compensate for
