PFC

noun
peripheral awareness
Pronunciation: / pi ɛf si / (AmE), / piː ɛf siː / (BrE)
the peripheral field of consciousness; the part of awareness that is present but not in direct focus

Examples

Meditation PFC
During meditation, you may notice your PFC expanding to include sounds and sensations around you.
Athletes often train to improve their PFC so they can react to events outside their main focus.
Becoming aware of your PFC can help you stay present in busy environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attention - the act of focusing awareness on something
  • synonym: consciousness - the state of being aware of and able to think
