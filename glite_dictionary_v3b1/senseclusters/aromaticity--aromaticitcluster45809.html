aromaticity

noun
chemical ring stability
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɛroʊməˈtɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌærəməˈtɪsəti / (BrE)
the property of certain molecules, especially rings, to be unusually stable due to a delocalized ring of electrons

Examples

Benzene aromaticity
Benzene is a classic example of a molecule with high aromaticity in organic chemistry.
The aromaticity of the compound contributes to its chemical stability and unique reactivity.
Scientists study aromaticity to understand why some ring structures are more stable than others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antiaromaticity - the property of certain molecules to be unusually unstable due to a ring of electrons
  • synonym: aromatic - a compound with a stable ring of delocalized electrons
