limnetic

adjective
open lake waters
9.9
Pronunciation: /lɪm.ˈnet.ɪk/ (AmE), /lɪm.ˈnet.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to the open, well-lit surface waters of a lake or pond, away from the shore

Examples

The limnetic zone
The limnetic zone supports many species of plankton and fish in large freshwater lakes.
Researchers collected water samples from the limnetic area to study its unique ecosystem.
Sunlight penetrates deeply into the limnetic region, allowing photosynthesis to occur.
The limnetic zone of the lake supports many species of fish and plankton.
Scientists collected water samples from the limnetic region to measure oxygen levels.
Algae blooms often occur in the limnetic areas during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benthic - relating to the bottom of a body of water
  • antonym: littoral - relating to the shore region of a lake or sea
  • synonym: pelagic - relating to the open sea, away from the shore
