marketization

noun
introduction of market principles
7.4
Pronunciation: /mɑːr.kə.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mɑː.kə.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of introducing or increasing market principles, such as competition and private ownership, or making something available for sale in a commercial market

Examples

The marketization of education
The marketization of education has led to increased competition among schools.
Many economists debate the effects of marketization on public services.
Rapid marketization can sometimes result in social inequality and reduced access to essential services.
The marketization of healthcare has led to increased competition among providers.
Many countries have undergone marketization of their energy sectors in recent decades.
Critics argue that the marketization of education can reduce equal access for all students.
The marketization of the healthcare system led to increased competition among hospitals.
Rapid marketization in the 1990s changed the structure of many Eastern European economies.
Critics argue that marketization can sometimes reduce access to essential public services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercialization - the process of bringing something to the market for profit
  • synonym: liberalization - the removal of restrictions to allow for more competition and free-market activity
  • antonym: nationalization - the transfer of private assets into public ownership
  • synonym: privatization - the transfer of ownership from public to private hands
