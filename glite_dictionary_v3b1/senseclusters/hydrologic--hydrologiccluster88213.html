hydrologic

adjective
about water movement
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdrəˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the movement, properties, or scientific study of water in the environment

Examples

Hydrologic cycle
Scientists study hydrologic processes to understand how rainfall affects river systems.
The hydrologic balance of a region can be disrupted by deforestation and urbanization.
A hydrologic model helps predict flooding after heavy storms.
The hydrologic cycle describes how water moves through the environment.
Engineers used hydrologic data to design the new flood control system.
A hydrologic study was required before building the dam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aquatic - relating to water
  • synonym: hydrological - relating to the science of water movement and distribution
  • antonym: meteorological - relating to the science of weather and atmosphere
