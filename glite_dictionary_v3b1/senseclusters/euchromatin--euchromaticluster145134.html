euchromatin

noun
active DNA structure
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / juÀêÀàkro äm…ôt…™n / (AmE), / juÀêÀàkr…ô äm…ôt…™n / (BrE)
the part of chromatin in a cell nucleus that is loosely packed and usually active in gene expression

Examples

Active euchromatin regions
Most genes that are actively transcribed are found in euchromatin regions of the nucleus.
Scientists study euchromatin to understand how gene expression is regulated in different cell types.
The transition from heterochromatin to euchromatin can make certain genes accessible for transcription.
Scientists observed that euchromatin regions are more likely to contain actively transcribed genes.
The presence of euchromatin allows for easier access to DNA during cell processes.
Under the microscope, euchromatin appears lighter compared to the darker heterochromatin.
