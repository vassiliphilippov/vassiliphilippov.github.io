rugosa

adjective
wrinkled or rough (botany)
Pronunciation: / ruˈɡoʊsə / (AmE), / ruˈɡəʊsə / (BrE)
used in scientific names to describe a species or variety with a wrinkled or rough surface, especially of leaves or bark

Examples

Rugosa leaves
The species name rugosa refers to the plant's rough, wrinkled leaves.
In botanical Latin, rugosa is often used to describe textured bark.
The rose variety Rosa rugosa is known for its deeply veined foliage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rugose - having a wrinkled or rough surface
  • antonym: smooth - having an even, unwrinkled surface
