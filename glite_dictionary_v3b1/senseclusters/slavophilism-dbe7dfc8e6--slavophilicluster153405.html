Slavophilism

noun
19th-century Russian intellectual movement
7.6 9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈslɑːvəˌfɪlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈslævəˌfɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
An intellectual movement that originated in 19th-century Russia, advocating the development of Russian society based on traditional Slavic values and institutions.

Examples

Slavophilism movement
Many writers influenced by Slavophilism promoted a return to traditional Slavic customs and Orthodox faith.
The 19th century saw the rise of Slavophilism as Russian thinkers debated the future of their country.
While Westernization was popular among some elites, others turned to Slavophilism as an alternative path.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pan-Slavism - political and cultural movement advocating Slavic unity
