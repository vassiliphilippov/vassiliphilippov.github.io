Mesozoic

adjective
relating to the Mesozoic geological era
6.3 8.0
Pronunciation: /mes.ə.ˈzoʊ.ɪk/ (AmE), /mes.ə.ˈzəʊ.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or denoting the Mesozoic era, a geological era between the Paleozoic and Cenozoic eras, characterized by the rise of dinosaurs and the development of flowering plants.

Examples

Mesozoic rocks
Geologists studied rock formations from the Mesozoic era to learn about ancient climates.
Many dinosaur fossils date back to the Mesozoic period.
The Mesozoic landscape was dominated by large reptiles and early mammals.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Paleozoic - relating to the geological era before the Mesozoic
