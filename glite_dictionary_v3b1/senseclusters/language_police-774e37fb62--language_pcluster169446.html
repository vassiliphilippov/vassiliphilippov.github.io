language police

noun
people enforcing or criticizing language usage
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ pəˈlis / (AmE), / ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ pəˈliːs / (BrE)
people who enforce or are excessively critical of others' language usage, especially in a pedantic or prescriptive way

Examples

Language police criticize speech
Don’t let the language police stop you from expressing yourself however you want.
He laughed and said the language police were out in full force after his typo.
Whenever I use casual expressions at work, the language police always seem to notice.
