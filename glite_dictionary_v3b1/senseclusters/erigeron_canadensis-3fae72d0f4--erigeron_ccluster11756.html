Erigeron canadensis

noun
a species of weed, also called horseweed
Pronunciation: / əˌraɪdʒəˌrɒn kænəˈdɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɛˌrɪdʒərɒn kanəˈdɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A tall North American annual weed with narrow leaves and small yellowish flower heads, naturalized in many temperate regions; also known as horseweed.

Examples

Erigeron canadensis weed
Erigeron canadensis can quickly take over disturbed ground and agricultural fields.
Gardeners often struggle to control Erigeron canadensis because of its prolific seed production.
Native to North America, Erigeron canadensis has spread to many other temperate regions worldwide.
