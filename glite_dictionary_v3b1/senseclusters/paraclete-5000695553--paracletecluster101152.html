Paraclete

noun
the Holy Spirit as advocate in Christianity
9.4 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpæ.rə.kliːt/ (AmE), /ˈpæ.rə.kliːt/ (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the Holy Spirit, regarded as an advocate or helper whom Jesus promised to send to his followers after his death and resurrection.

Examples

The Paraclete Spirit
Many Christians refer to the Holy Spirit as the Paraclete in their prayers.
The apostles believed that the Paraclete came to them on the day of Pentecost.
The concept of the Paraclete is central to understanding the doctrine of the Trinity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Holy Spirit - the third person of the Christian Trinity
