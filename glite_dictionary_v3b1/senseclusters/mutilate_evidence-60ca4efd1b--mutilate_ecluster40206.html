mutilate evidence

verb
destroy or alter evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuː.t̬ə.leɪt ˈɛv.ə.dəns / (AmE), / ˈmjuː.tɪ.leɪt ˈɛv.ɪ.dəns / (BrE)
to intentionally destroy or alter evidence, especially to hinder an investigation or legal process.

Examples

To mutilate evidence illegally
It is a crime to mutilate evidence connected to a court case.
Security cameras captured someone trying to mutilate evidence in the laboratory.
The suspect attempted to mutilate evidence before the police searched his house.
