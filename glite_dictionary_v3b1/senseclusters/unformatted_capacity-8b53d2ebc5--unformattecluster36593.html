unformatted capacity

noun
total data space on an unformatted disk
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈfɔːrmətɪd kəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈfɔːmætɪd kəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
(computing) the total amount of data a disk can hold, including space needed for formatting and overhead, measured before the disk is formatted.

Examples

The unformatted capacity size
Most manufacturers list the unformatted capacity, but users get less space after formatting.
The salesperson explained that the hard drive's unformatted capacity is slightly higher than its usable storage.
Understanding the difference between unformatted capacity and formatted capacity is important when buying storage devices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: formatted capacity - data storage space available after disk formatting
