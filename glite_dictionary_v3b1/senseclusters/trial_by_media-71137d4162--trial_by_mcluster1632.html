trial by media

noun
judgment influenced by media coverage
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪəl baɪ ˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / ˈtraɪəl baɪ ˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
a situation in which the media heavily influences or prejudges the outcome of a public controversy, often swaying public opinion before any legal process is complete

Examples

Trial by media coverage
Many people believe that celebrities regularly undergo a trial by media whenever scandal arises.
The accused businessman found himself subject to a trial by media long before his court appearance.
The lawyer argued that the negative news coverage amounted to a trial by media.
