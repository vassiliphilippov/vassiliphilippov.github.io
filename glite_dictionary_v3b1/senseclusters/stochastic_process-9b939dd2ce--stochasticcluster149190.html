stochastic process

noun
process involving random variables over time
Pronunciation: / stoʊˈkæstɪk ˈproʊsɛs / (AmE), / stəʊˈkæstɪk ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
A mathematical process in which a sequence of random variables evolves over time or another parameter, typically used to model systems that are subject to random changes.

Examples

A stochastic process model
Brownian motion is an example of a physical phenomenon described by a stochastic process.
Researchers used a stochastic process to analyze the spread of an infectious disease over time.
The stock market can be modeled as a stochastic process to capture the unpredictable changes in prices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: random process - A process whose outcomes are determined by chance; general term for processes involving randomness
