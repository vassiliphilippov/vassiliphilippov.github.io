deictic word

noun
word relating to speaker's context (linguistics)
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: / daɪk.tɪk wɜrd / (AmE), / daɪk.tɪk wɜːd / (BrE)
a word that indicates identity, place, or time in relation to the speaker or hearer (such as 'this', 'that', 'here', 'now')

Examples

Deictic word examples
In the sentence 'I will go there tomorrow', both 'there' and 'tomorrow' are deictic words.
Pronouns like 'I', 'you', and 'they' are examples of deictic words in English.
The meaning of a deictic word often depends on the specific context of the conversation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deixis - reference to identity, location, or time relative to speaker or hearer
