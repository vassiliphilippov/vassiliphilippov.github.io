correlation

noun
statistical relationship between variables
4.9 17/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…îÀêr…ôÀàle…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåk…ír.…ôÀàle…™. É…ôn / (BrE)
a statistical measure that indicates the extent to which two or more variables fluctuate together, usually expressed as a value between -1 and 1

Examples

Statistical correlation value
A negative correlation was observed between age and physical activity levels.
Researchers found little correlation between diet and the risk of the disease.
The study showed a strong correlation between hours studied and exam scores.

Synonyms

  • correlation coefficient
    6.1 2.19/m
    correlation coefficient is a single numerical value showing strength and direction between two variables, while correlation names the general relationship.
  • coefficient of correlation
    6.8 0.06/m
    coefficient of correlation is the measured number between -1 and +1 showing how two variables move together, while correlation names the broader link.

Related Terms

  • synonym: association - a connection or relationship between things
  • antonym: causation - the action of causing something; not the same as correlation
