regulationist

noun
supporter of regulation
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports, advocates for, or develops systems of regulation or regulatory policy

Examples

A regulationist in government
The regulationist argued that stricter environmental laws would benefit public health.
As a known regulationist, she often debates for more oversight in the financial sector.
Critics of the regulationist approach claim it can slow economic growth.
The regulationist argued for stricter environmental policies at the international conference.
As a leading regulationist, she advises governments on financial oversight.
Many regulationists believe that clear rules help protect consumers and the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deregulationist - a person who supports reducing or removing regulations
  • synonym: interventionist - a person who favors government intervention in the economy
  • antonym: laissez-faire - an economic philosophy opposing government regulation
  • synonym: regulator - a person or body that supervises or enforces rules and regulations
