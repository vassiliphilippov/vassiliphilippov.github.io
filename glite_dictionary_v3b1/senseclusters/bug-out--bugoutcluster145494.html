bug-out

verb
to panic suddenly
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌɡ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to react with sudden fear or panic, often losing self-control or composure

Examples

To bug-out in panic
When the fire alarm went off, several students started to bug-out and ran for the exits.
He tends to bug-out whenever he has to speak in public.
Try not to bug-out if things don't go exactly as planned.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm down - to become relaxed and less anxious
  • synonym: freak out - to become very anxious or upset suddenly
  • synonym: panic - to feel sudden, uncontrollable fear
