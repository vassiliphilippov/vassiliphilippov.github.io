yellow journalism

noun
sensationalist, exaggerated journalism
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ ˈdʒɜrnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈdʒɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
journalism that uses sensationalism, exaggeration, or scandal to attract attention, often ignoring accuracy or ethics

Examples

Yellow journalism style
Many historians blame yellow journalism for inflaming public opinion before the Spanish-American War.
Some news outlets continue to rely on yellow journalism to draw in more readers.
The tabloid was accused of yellow journalism for exaggerating the facts of the scandal.
Many critics argue that yellow journalism undermines public trust in the media.
She studied the history of yellow journalism in her journalism ethics class.
The newspaper was accused of yellow journalism after publishing exaggerated reports about the incident.

Related Terms
