arpeggiate

verb
to play chord notes separately
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈpɛdʒiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɑːˈpɛdʒieɪt / (BrE)
to play the notes of a chord one after another in quick succession rather than all at once

Examples

To arpeggiate chords
The guitarist decided to arpeggiate the chords for a softer sound in the song.
In classical music, pianists often arpeggiate chords to add texture and movement.
She learned how to arpeggiate smoothly during her piano lessons.
