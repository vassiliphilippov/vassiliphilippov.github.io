serratus muscles

noun
group of trunk muscles with a saw-toothed edge
5.8 5.9
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈreɪ.təs ˈmʌs.əlz/ (AmE), /sə.ˈreɪ.təs ˈmʌs.əlz/ (BrE)
a group of muscles of the trunk, characterized by a saw-toothed edge, especially the serratus anterior and serratus posterior muscles

Examples

Serratus muscles pain
Injury to the serratus muscles can cause pain during breathing and upper body movement.
Strengthening the serratus muscles is important for athletes involved in throwing sports.
The serratus muscles play a key role in the movement of the shoulder blade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: serratus posterior - either of two muscles on the back of the trunk (superior and inferior)
