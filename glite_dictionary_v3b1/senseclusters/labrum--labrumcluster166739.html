labrum

noun
insect mouthpart
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbrəm / (AmE), / ˈleɪbrəm / (BrE)
a flap-like structure forming the upper lip of the mouthparts in insects and other arthropods, used in feeding

Examples

Insect labrum
The entomologist examined the beetle's labrum under a microscope to study its feeding habits.
Damage to the labrum can affect how an insect eats and processes food.
In many insects, the labrum works together with other mouthparts to handle different types of food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mandible - the jaw or jawbone, especially in insects and arthropods
  • synonym: maxilla - a paired mouthpart in insects used for manipulating food
