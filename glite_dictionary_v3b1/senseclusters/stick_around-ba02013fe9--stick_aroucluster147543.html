stick around

verb
remain in a place; not leave
Pronunciation: / stɪk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / stɪk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to stay in a place and not leave, often waiting for something to happen

Examples

To stick around after
I'm not sure what's happening next, but I'll stick around to find out.
If you stick around after the show, you can meet the performers.
Many students stuck around after class to ask the professor questions.
I can't stick around much longer because I have another appointment.
If you stick around for a few more minutes, the manager will see you.
She decided to stick around after the show to meet the actors.
She decided to stick around after the concert in case there was an encore.
They stuck around even though the event was running late.
Why don't you stick around for a while and have some coffee with us?
