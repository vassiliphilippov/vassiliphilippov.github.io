Radiigera fuscogleba

noun
species of fungus similar to earthstar
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈɡɛrə ˌfʌskoʊˈɡliːbə / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈdʒɛərə ˌfʌskəʊˈɡliːbə / (BrE)
A species of fungus resembling an earthstar, but unlike earthstars, it does not open up; at maturity, its spore mass is brown with a column of sterile tissue extending into it.

Examples

Radiigera fuscogleba fungus species

The unique structure of Radiigera fuscogleba helps mycologists distinguish it from similar fungi.
Unlike the common earthstar, Radiigera fuscogleba remains closed throughout its life cycle.
