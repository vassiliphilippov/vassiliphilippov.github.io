leaf lettuce

noun
lettuce with loose, curly leaves
Pronunciation: / lif ˈlɛt̬.ɪs /; / lif ˈlɛt.ɪs / (AmE), / liːf ˈlɛt.ɪs / (BrE)
a type of lettuce with loose, curled leaves that do not form a compact head, often used in salads

Examples

Fresh leaf lettuce
I prefer leaf lettuce to iceberg because it's more flavorful and tender.
The salad was made with fresh leaf lettuce, cherry tomatoes, and cucumbers.
We planted several types of leaf lettuce in our garden this spring.
I added freshly washed leaf lettuce to the salad for extra crunch.
She prefers making sandwiches with leaf lettuce instead of head lettuce.
Unlike iceberg, leaf lettuce has open, loose leaves and a milder flavor.
