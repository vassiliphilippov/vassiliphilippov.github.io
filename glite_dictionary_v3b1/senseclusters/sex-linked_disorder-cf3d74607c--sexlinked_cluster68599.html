sex-linked disorder

noun
genetic disorder linked to sex chromosomes
6.7 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛks lɪŋkt dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈseks lɪŋkt dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a genetic condition caused by a gene located on a sex chromosome, typically the X chromosome, resulting in diseases or abnormalities that are inherited in a sex-dependent manner

Examples

A sex-linked disorder
A mutation on the X chromosome can result in a sex-linked disorder.
Doctors often screen for sex-linked disorders during genetic counseling sessions.
Hemophilia is a well-known sex-linked disorder that primarily affects males.
