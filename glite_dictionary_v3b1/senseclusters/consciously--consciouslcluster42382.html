consciously

adverb
deliberately; with awareness
3.7 9/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên. É…ôs.li / (AmE), / Ààk…ín. É…ôs.li / (BrE)
In a deliberate and intentional way; with full awareness of what one is doing.

Examples

To consciously choose
He consciously tried to keep his voice calm during the conversation.
Many people consciously choose healthier foods when they learn more about nutrition.
She consciously avoided making eye contact with her former boss at the event.

Synonyms

  • intentionally
    3.1 11/m
    intentionally means doing something on purpose, while consciously means acting with full awareness of what one is doing.
  • deliberately
    3.7 20/m
    deliberately is done on purpose often after thought, while consciously stresses clear awareness of one's action and choice.
  • mindfully
    3.9 0.3/m
    mindfully describes paying calm attention to experience, while consciously highlights general awareness of what you do.
  • volitionally
    8.4 0.03/m
    volitionally means acting by an explicit act of will, while consciously means doing something with awareness rather than automatically.
  • purposefully
    3.9 3/m
    purposefully shows action done to reach a clear goal, while consciously focuses on being aware of the action itself.
  • calculatedly
    6.8 0.07/m
    calculatedly describes a cold, carefully planned move, while consciously means acting with awareness but not always with cold planning.
