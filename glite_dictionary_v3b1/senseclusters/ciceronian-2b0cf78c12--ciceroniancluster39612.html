Ciceronian

adjective
eloquent, Cicero-like
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪsəˈroʊniən / (AmE), / ˌsɪsəˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the eloquent style or rhetorical skill of the Roman orator Cicero

Examples

Ciceronian style of speaking
The professor admired her Ciceronian command of language during the debate.
His essay was praised for its Ciceronian structure and persuasive arguments.
They studied the Ciceronian techniques of public speaking in their rhetoric class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eloquent - fluent or persuasive in speaking or writing
  • synonym: rhetorical - relating to effective or persuasive speaking or writing
