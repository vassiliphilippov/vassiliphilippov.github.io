cliched

adjective
lacking originality; overused
Pronunciation: / klɪˈʃeɪd / (AmE), / klɪˈʃeɪd / (BrE)
(of an idea, phrase, or artistic work) lacking originality or freshness; overused and unoriginal.

Examples

A cliched phrase
Critics complained that the movie relied on cliched characters and predictable scenarios.
Her speech was filled with cliched expressions that failed to inspire the audience.
The novel's plot was so cliched that I could predict every twist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - not derived from something else; new or inventive
  • synonym: trite - (of a remark or idea) overused and consequently of little import
