Ailanthus altissima

noun
fast-growing deciduous tree, tree of heaven
Pronunciation: / aɪˈlænθəs ælˈtɪsɪmə / (AmE), / aɪˈlænθəs ælˈtɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A species of fast-growing, deciduous tree native to China, known for its large, sumac-like leaves and clusters of sweet, foul-smelling flowers; often used as a street tree because of its tolerance to urban pollution.

Examples

Ailanthus altissima tree
Despite its rapid growth, Ailanthus altissima is considered invasive in several parts of North America.
The city planted Ailanthus altissima along many busy streets because of its resistance to pollution.
The leaves of Ailanthus altissima are large and resemble those of a sumac.
