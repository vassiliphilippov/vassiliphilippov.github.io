ascus

noun
spore sac in ascomycete fungi
Pronunciation: / ˈæskəs / (AmE), / ˈaskəs / (BrE)
A saclike structure in which ascospores are formed during the sexual reproduction of ascomycete fungi.

Examples

An ascus sac
Each ascus typically contains eight ascospores when mature.
Microscopic examination revealed several asci containing newly formed spores.
The ascus is essential for the sexual reproduction of many fungi.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascospore - spore formed inside an ascus
