Dionysiac

adjective
wildly celebratory
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈnɪziæk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈnɪziæk / (BrE)
relating to Dionysus or to wild, ecstatic, uninhibited, and emotional qualities or celebrations (especially in art or behavior)

Examples

Dionysiac festival
The parade had a Dionysiac energy, with dancers and musicians filling the streets.
Her paintings are known for their Dionysiac colors and sense of movement.
The party took on a Dionysiac atmosphere as the night went on.
The artist's Dionysiac style shocked the traditional art community with its raw energy.
Her performance was praised for its Dionysiac passion and emotional intensity.
The festival had a Dionysiac atmosphere, filled with music, dance, and spontaneous celebration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Apollonian - relating to order, reason, and rationality, often contrasted with Dionysian
  • synonym: Dionysian - relating to the sensual, spontaneous, and emotional aspects of human nature
  • synonym: bacchanalian - characterized by noisy, drunken revelry
  • antonym: restrained - controlled and not excessive
