sidecut

noun
ski or board curve
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.kʌt/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.kʌt/ (BrE)
the inward curve along the sides of a ski, snowboard, or skate blade that helps it turn more easily

Examples

Ski sidecut curve
The sidecut of these skis makes them perfect for quick, sharp turns on the slopes.
A snowboard with a deeper sidecut allows for easier carving at lower speeds.
Modern skate blades feature a pronounced sidecut to improve maneuverability on the ice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camber - the slight upward curve in the middle of a ski or snowboard
  • synonym: radius - the measure of the curve that affects turning in skis and snowboards
