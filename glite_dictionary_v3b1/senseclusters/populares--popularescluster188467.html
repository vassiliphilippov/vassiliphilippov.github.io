populares

noun
Roman political faction
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːpjəˈlɛəriːz / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊˈlɑːriːz / (BrE)
a political group in ancient Rome that represented and supported the interests of the common people

Examples

The populares party
The populares sought reforms to help the lower classes in Roman society.
Julius Caesar was often associated with the populares during his rise to power.
Conflicts between the populares and the optimates shaped much of Roman political history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: optimates - the aristocratic political group in ancient Rome
