canniness

noun
clever carefulness
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkænɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈkænɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being clever and careful, especially in making decisions or dealing with business matters

Examples

Business canniness
Her canniness allowed her to avoid risky investments and grow her savings steadily.
The manager's canniness was evident in every negotiation he led for the company.
With remarkable canniness, he spotted opportunities that others missed in the market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • synonym: shrewdness - the ability to make good judgments and decisions
