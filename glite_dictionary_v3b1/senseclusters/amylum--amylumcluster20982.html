amylum

noun
complex carbohydrate; starch
8.6
Pronunciation: / əˈmaɪləm / (AmE), / əˈmɪləm / (BrE)
A complex carbohydrate, chiefly found in seeds, fruits, tubers, and roots, used as a foodstuff and in various industrial applications.

Examples

Plant amylum starch
Chemically, amylum is classified as a polysaccharide, consisting of many glucose units.
Corn and potatoes are both rich in amylum, making them important sources of energy worldwide.
The textile industry sometimes uses amylum as a stiffening agent for fabrics.
