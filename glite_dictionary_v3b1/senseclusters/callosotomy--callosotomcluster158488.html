callosotomy

noun
surgical severing of the corpus callosum
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæl.əˈsɑː.t̬ə.mi / (AmE), / ˌkæl.əˈsɒ.tə.mi / (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which the corpus callosum is severed to interrupt communication between the cerebral hemispheres, used to treat severe, intractable epilepsy.

Examples

A callosotomy surgery
A callosotomy can sometimes result in split-brain symptoms due to the loss of communication between hemispheres.
Following the callosotomy, the patient reported a reduction in seizure frequency.
The neurologist recommended a callosotomy as a last resort for the patient's severe epilepsy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corpus callosum - the large bundle of nerve fibers connecting the two hemispheres of the brain
