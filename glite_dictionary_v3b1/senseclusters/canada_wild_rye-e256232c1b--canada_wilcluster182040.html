Canada wild rye

noun
a North American wild grass species
8.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈnædə waɪld raɪ / (AmE), / kəˈnædə waɪld raɪ / (BrE)
A tall, coarse grass (Elymus canadensis) native to North America, with bristly flower heads, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Examples

Canada wild rye grass
Canada wild rye is often used in prairie restoration projects due to its hardiness.
Farmers sometimes plant Canada wild rye to prevent soil erosion on their land.
The bristly flower heads of Canada wild rye make it easy to identify in the field.
