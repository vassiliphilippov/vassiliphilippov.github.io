unschool

verb
to educate informally
8.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈskuːl / (AmE), / ʌnˈskuːl / (BrE)
to educate someone or oneself outside formal schools, using learner-directed and informal methods instead of a set curriculum

Examples

To unschool a child
Many parents choose to unschool their children to encourage creativity and independence.
After leaving traditional education, she decided to unschool herself in art and science.
Some families unschool by letting their children follow their interests instead of a fixed curriculum.
Some parents choose to unschool their children to encourage more creativity and independence.
After years in the public system, they decided to unschool their son for a more flexible education.
Families who unschool often use everyday experiences as learning opportunities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeschool - to educate children at home instead of in a traditional school
  • antonym: school - to educate in a formal school setting
