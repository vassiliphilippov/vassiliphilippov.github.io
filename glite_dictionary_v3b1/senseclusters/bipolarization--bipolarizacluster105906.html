bipolarization

noun
division into two sides
9.9
Pronunciation: /baɪ.poʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /baɪ.pəʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of dividing or being divided into two opposing groups or poles

Examples

Political bipolarization
The bipolarization of the country led to increased political tension and conflict.
During the debate, there was a clear bipolarization of opinions among the participants.
Historians often discuss the bipolarization of world powers during the twentieth century.
