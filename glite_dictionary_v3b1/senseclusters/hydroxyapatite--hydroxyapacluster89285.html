hydroxyapatite

noun
biomaterial for bone repair
Pronunciation: / haɪˌdrɑksiˈæpəˌtaɪt / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksiˈæpətaɪt / (BrE)
a mineral form of calcium phosphate that is the main component of bone and tooth enamel, and is also used in medicine and dentistry for bone repair and implants

Researchers are studying how hydroxyapatite can help repair damaged bones.
Toothpastes containing hydroxyapatite may help strengthen enamel and reduce sensitivity.
