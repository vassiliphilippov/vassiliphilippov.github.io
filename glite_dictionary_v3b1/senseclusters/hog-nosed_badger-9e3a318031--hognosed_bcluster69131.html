hog-nosed badger

noun
Southeast Asian badger with pig-like snout
Pronunciation: / hɑɡ noʊzd ˈbædʒər / (AmE), / hɒɡ nəʊzd ˈbædʒə / (BrE)
A badger native to Southeast Asia, distinguished by its pig-like snout.

Examples

The hog-nosed badger species
Researchers observed a hog-nosed badger displaying unique foraging behavior near the riverbank.
The hog-nosed badger uses its snout to dig for insects and roots in the forest.
Unlike European badgers, the hog-nosed badger is found primarily in Southeast Asia.
