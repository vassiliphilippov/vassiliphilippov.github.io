argue

verb
to have a verbal disagreement
1.1 26/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ër…°ju / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀê…°juÀê / (BrE)
to speak angrily with someone because you disagree; to have an argument about something

Examples

To argue with friends
I didn't want to argue, so I just let her have her way.
The children began to argue about whose turn it was to play with the toy.
They often argue over politics at family dinners.

Synonyms

  • quarrel
    2.6 2.47/m
    quarrel is an angry, often short disagreement about small things, while argue covers any angry discussion of a point.
  • have a quarrel
    2.8 0.12/m
    have a quarrel often leaves bad feelings after an angry argument, while argue is the general verb for disagreeing loudly.
  • have words
    6.9 0.22/m
    have words suggests a brief angry exchange between people, while argue can mean a longer, full angry discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion; the opposite of to argue
  • degree lower: discuss - to talk about something in detail (less confrontational)
  • synonym: quarrel - to have an angry argument or disagreement
