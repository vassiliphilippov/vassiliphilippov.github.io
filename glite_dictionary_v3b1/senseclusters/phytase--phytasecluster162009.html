phytase

noun
enzyme for phytic acid
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪteɪs / (AmE), / ˈfaɪteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down phytic acid in plants, often added to animal feed to help animals absorb more nutrients

Examples

Phytase enzyme added
Scientists add phytase to animal feed to help animals absorb more nutrients from grains.
The presence of phytase in seeds allows plants to release stored phosphorus during germination.
Supplementing diets with phytase can reduce the need for additional phosphorus in agriculture.
Adding phytase to animal feed helps livestock absorb more phosphorus from their diet.
Researchers developed a new form of phytase that works efficiently at higher temperatures.
The use of phytase in agriculture can reduce environmental pollution from animal waste.
