Caucasia

proper noun
region between Black and Caspian Seas
Pronunciation: / kɔˈkeɪʒə / (AmE), / kɔˈkeɪziə / (BrE)
a region between the Black Sea and Caspian Sea that includes the Caucasus Mountains, noted for its cultural diversity and natural resources, especially oil

Examples

Caucasia region
Centuries of migration have shaped the diverse cultures found in Caucasia today.
Oil remains a major economic resource for the countries of Caucasia.
The mountains of Caucasia are famous for their breathtaking scenery and unique wildlife.
