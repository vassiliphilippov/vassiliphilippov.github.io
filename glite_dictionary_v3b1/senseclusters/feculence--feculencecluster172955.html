feculence

noun
the quality of being foul or muddy
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…õkj…ôl…ôns / (AmE), / ÀàfiÀêkj äl…ôns / (BrE)
The state or quality of being foul, muddy, or containing sediment; dregs or filth.

Examples

The feculence of water
The river's feculence made the water unfit for drinking or bathing.
They found the well clogged with a dark feculence that gave off a foul odor.
Years of neglect allowed a thick feculence to accumulate at the bottom of the reservoir.

Synonyms

  • filth
    2.0 1.67/m
    filth is the dirty, messy material people see, while feculence points more to muddy sediment or fine dregs.

Related Terms
