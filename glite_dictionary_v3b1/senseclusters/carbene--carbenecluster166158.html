carbene

noun
reactive carbon molecule
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrˌbiːn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːˌbiːn / (BrE)
a highly reactive molecule containing a carbon atom with two bonds and two unshared electrons

Examples

A carbene molecule
A carbene can act as an intermediate in many organic reactions.
Chemists observed the formation of a carbene during the experiment.
Because a carbene is so reactive, it usually exists only for a short time.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: alkene - a hydrocarbon containing a carbon-carbon double bond
  • synonym: radical - a molecule with an unpaired electron, often highly reactive
