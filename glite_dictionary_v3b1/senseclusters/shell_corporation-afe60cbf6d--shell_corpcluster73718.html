shell corporation

noun
inactive company with no assets or operations
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl ˌkɔɹpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ʃɛl ˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A company that exists only on paper and has no active business operations or significant assets, often used to conceal ownership or for financial maneuvers.

Examples

A shell corporation
He set up a shell corporation to hide his ownership of the properties.
Investigators found that the funds were routed through several shell corporations before disappearing.
The tax authorities suspected the offshore account belonged to a shell corporation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: front company - a business that serves as a cover for another, often secret, activity
