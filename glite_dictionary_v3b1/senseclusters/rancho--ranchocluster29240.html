rancho

noun
makeshift dwelling
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑntʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈræn.tʃəʊ / (BrE)
a poor or makeshift dwelling or shack, especially in a Latin American shantytown

Examples

Poor rancho home
After the storm, several families built a rancho from scrap wood and metal sheets.
The children played outside their small rancho at the edge of the city.
Volunteers helped repair the roof of a rancho damaged by heavy rains.
