microdata

noun
individual-level data
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌdeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌdeɪtə / (BrE)
individual-level data, often used in research or web development to provide detailed information about people or items

Examples

Microdata from census
The researchers used microdata from the census to study income differences across regions.
Web developers add microdata to HTML to help search engines understand page content.
Access to detailed microdata allows analysts to identify trends at the individual level.
