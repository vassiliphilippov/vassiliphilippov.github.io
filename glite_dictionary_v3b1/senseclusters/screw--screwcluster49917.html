screw

noun
propeller with angled blades (esp. ship/aircraft)
5.1 0.6/m
Pronunciation: / skruÀê / (AmE), / skruÀê / (BrE)
A propeller with several angled blades that rotates to push against water or air, especially on a ship or aircraft.

Examples

Ship screw
A broken screw makes it impossible for the submarine to maneuver properly.
Early steamships often used a single screw for propulsion.
The ship's screw was damaged during the storm, slowing their journey.

Synonyms

  • screw propeller
    5.3 0.01/m
    screw propeller is the formal name for a rotating set of angled blades on a ship or plane, while screw is the shorter everyday name.
  • propeller
    3.9 3/m
    propeller is a general machine of rotating blades that moves boats or planes, while screw is a word often used for ship or aircraft propellers.
  • prop
    4.6 2/m
    prop is an informal short word used by pilots and sailors for a rotating blade, while screw is the full technical term often used on ships.
