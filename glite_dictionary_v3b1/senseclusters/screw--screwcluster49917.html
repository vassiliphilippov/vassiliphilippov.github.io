screw

noun
propeller with angled blades (esp. ship/aircraft)
9.5 4.2
Pronunciation: /skruː/ (AmE), /skruː/ (BrE)
A propeller with several angled blades that rotates to push against water or air, especially on a ship or aircraft.

Examples

Ship screw
A broken screw makes it impossible for the submarine to maneuver properly.
Early steamships often used a single screw for propulsion.
The ship's screw was damaged during the storm, slowing their journey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propeller - a device with revolving blades used to propel a vehicle through water or air
