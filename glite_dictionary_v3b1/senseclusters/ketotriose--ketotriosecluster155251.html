ketotriose

noun
a ketose sugar with three carbon atoms
Pronunciation: / kɛtoʊˈtraɪoʊs / (AmE), / kɛtəʊˈtrʌɪəʊz / (BrE)
A ketose sugar molecule containing three carbon atoms; specifically, dihydroxyacetone.

Examples

A ketotriose sugar
Dihydroxyacetone is the only naturally occurring ketotriose found in biological systems.
Researchers analyzed the crystalline structure of the ketotriose to understand its properties.
The metabolism of the ketotriose dihydroxyacetone plays a key role in glycolysis.
