fizzle out

verb
end weakly or fail gradually
6.4 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪz.əl aʊt/ (AmE), /ˈfɪz.əl aʊt/ (BrE)
to gradually lose strength or energy and come to a disappointing or weak end after starting off strongly

Examples

To fizzle out slowly
After a promising start, the project began to fizzle out due to lack of interest.
The concert seemed exciting at first, but it eventually fizzled out with no real conclusion.
Their enthusiasm for the new club quickly fizzled out over the following months.
Despite high hopes, the protest movement quickly fizzled out by the end of the year.
Interest in the new product began to fizzle out after a few months.
Their relationship slowly fizzled out when they realized they had nothing in common.

  • synonym: fade away - to slowly disappear or become weaker
  • synonym: peter out - to gradually decrease and stop
