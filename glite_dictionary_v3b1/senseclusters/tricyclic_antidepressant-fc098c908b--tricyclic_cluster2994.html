tricyclic antidepressant

noun
type of antidepressant drug
1.7 4.7
Pronunciation: / traɪˈsaɪklɪk ˌæn.ti.dɪˈprɛ.sənt / (AmE), / traɪˈsɪklɪk ˌæn.ti.dɪˈprɛs.ənt / (BrE)
a type of antidepressant drug that works by blocking the reuptake of norepinephrine and serotonin, increasing their levels in the brain

Examples

Tricyclic antidepressants increase serotonin
Doctors sometimes prescribe tricyclic antidepressants when other treatments for depression have failed.
Imipramine is an example of a tricyclic antidepressant widely used in the past.
Side effects are common with tricyclic antidepressants, so their use has declined in recent years.
