Prosopis juliflora

noun
a drought-resistant mesquite shrub or tree
Pronunciation: / proʊˈsoʊpɪs dʒuːləˈflɔrə / (AmE), / prəˈsəʊpɪs dʒuːlɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of mesquite, a thorny shrub or small tree native to the Gulf Coast and Caribbean Islands from Mexico to Venezuela, known for its drought resistance and use in erosion control.

Examples

Prosopis juliflora tree
Prosopis juliflora is often planted in arid regions to combat desertification.
Local farmers use wood from Prosopis juliflora for fuel and fencing.
The spread of Prosopis juliflora has significantly altered the landscape in some African countries.
