silicone

noun
synthetic polymer material used in industry
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlɪˌkoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪˌkəʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic material consisting of polymers with repeating siloxane units, known for its stability across temperature extremes and used in lubricants, adhesives, coatings, synthetic rubber, and electrical insulation

Examples

Silicone rubber
Medical equipment often makes use of silicone for its flexibility and resistance to heat.
She applied a layer of silicone sealant around the bathtub to prevent leaks.
The chef preferred baking molds made from silicone because they were easy to clean and heat resistant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polymer - a substance made up of large molecules with repeating units
