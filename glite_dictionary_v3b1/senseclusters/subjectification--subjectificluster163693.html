subjectification

noun
making subjective
9.7
Pronunciation: / səbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more subjective or relating it to personal perspective or experience

Examples

Subjectification of facts
The subjectification of historical events can lead to different interpretations based on personal experience.
In literature, the subjectification of a narrator's voice can make the story feel more intimate.
Some critics argue that the subjectification of facts undermines objective reporting in journalism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectification - the process of making something objective or impersonal
  • synonym: personalization - the act of making something personal or tailored to an individual
