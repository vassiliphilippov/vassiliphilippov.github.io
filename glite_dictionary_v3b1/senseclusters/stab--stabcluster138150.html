stab

verb
thrust with a knife or sharp object
Pronunciation: / stæb / (AmE), / stæb / (BrE)
to push or thrust a sharp or pointed object into someone or something, often suddenly or forcefully

Examples

To stab with a knife
He tried to stab the intruder with a kitchen knife.
Police arrested the suspect for stabbing a man outside the bar.
She was stabbed in the arm during a robbery last night.
He stabbed at the elevator button impatiently.
She stabbed her finger at the map to show the location.
The nurse stabbed the needle into his arm quickly.
He stabbed the potato with a fork to see if it was cooked.
She tried to stab the attacker with her keys in self-defense.
The villain threatened to stab anyone who came closer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jab - to poke quickly or abruptly
  • synonym: pierce - to penetrate or make a hole with a sharp instrument
  • synonym: poke - to push with a finger or other object
  • antonym: shoot - to fire a gun at someone or something
  • synonym: slash - to cut with a sweeping or forceful stroke
