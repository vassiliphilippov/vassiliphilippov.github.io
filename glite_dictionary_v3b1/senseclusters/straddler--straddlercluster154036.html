straddler

noun
options strategy holder
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstrædlər / (AmE), / ˈstrædlə / (BrE)
an investor or trader who holds both call and put options on the same asset as part of a straddle strategy

Examples

Options straddler
The experienced straddler profited from the market's sharp movement in either direction.
As a straddler, she managed risk by holding both call and put options on the stock.
Many investors become a straddler during periods of high market volatility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: speculator - someone who takes financial risks in hopes of profit
  • synonym: trader - a person who buys and sells financial assets
