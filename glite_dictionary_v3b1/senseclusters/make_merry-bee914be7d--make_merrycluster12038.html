make merry

verb
celebrate festively and noisily
6.0
Pronunciation: / meɪk ˈmɛri / (AmE), / meɪk ˈmɛri / (BrE)
to celebrate in a lively, cheerful, and noisy way, often with drinking, eating, and fun activities

Examples

To make merry together
As the final exams ended, students took to the streets to make merry late into the night.
Every holiday season, the family gathers to make merry with food, music, and laughter.
In the medieval hall, people would dance and make merry after the feast.
After the successful project, the team decided to make merry at their favorite bar.
The townspeople gathered in the square to make merry during the annual festival.
They stayed up late, singing songs and making merry with friends.

  • synonym: celebrate - mark a special day or event with festivities or activities
  • synonym: revel - enjoy oneself in a lively and noisy way, especially with drinking and dancing
