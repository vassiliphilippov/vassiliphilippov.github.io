infection

noun
illness caused by harmful microorganisms
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃən/ (BrE)
a disease or condition caused by harmful microorganisms (such as bacteria or viruses), or the process of these microorganisms entering the body and causing illness

Examples

A serious infection
After the surgery, he developed a serious infection that delayed his recovery.
Hand washing is important to prevent the spread of infection.
The doctor prescribed antibiotics to treat the infection in her lungs.
Doctors prescribed antibiotics to help fight the bacterial infection in his lungs.
Good hand hygiene can prevent the spread of infection in hospitals and schools.
The child developed a serious infection after scraping his knee on the playground.
After the infection, the child was kept at home to avoid passing it to classmates.
Doctors traced the source of the infection to contaminated water supplies.
The hospital took strict measures to prevent another infection from spreading among patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagion - the process of disease transmission by direct or indirect contact
  • antonym: immunity - resistance to a particular infection or toxin
