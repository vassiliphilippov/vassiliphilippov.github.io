unsnap

verb
to release from fixed position
4.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈsnæp/ /; / /ə.ˈsnæp/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈsnæp/ /; / /ə.ˈsnæp/ (BrE)
to release something from a fixed or snapped position, especially in a computer interface, so it can move or resize freely

Examples

To unsnap a window
You can unsnap the window from the side of the screen to move it anywhere you like.
After editing the document, she unsnapped the toolbar to customize her workspace.
If you want to resize the panel, first unsnap it from its fixed position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detach - to separate something from what it is attached to
  • antonym: snap - to fix or attach something into a position with a snapping action
