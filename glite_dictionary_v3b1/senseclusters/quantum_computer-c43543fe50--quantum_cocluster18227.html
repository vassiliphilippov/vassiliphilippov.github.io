quantum computer

noun
computer using quantum phenomena
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːntəm kəmˈpjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
A computer that uses quantum-mechanical phenomena, such as superposition and entanglement, to perform operations on data.

Examples

A quantum computer system
A fully operational quantum computer could revolutionize fields such as cryptography and materials science.
Researchers are working on building a quantum computer that can solve certain problems much faster than classical computers.
She wrote her thesis on error correction algorithms for a quantum computer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: classical computer - a conventional computer based on binary logic and classical physics
