in delicto

preposition
at fault legally
Pronunciation: / ɪn dɛˈlɪktoʊ / (AmE), / ɪn dɛˈlɪktəʊ / (BrE)
legally or morally at fault, especially as a participant in wrongdoing that affects legal rights or remedies

Examples

Both parties in delicto
The court found both parties in delicto and denied their claims for damages.
If a person is in delicto, they may not be entitled to legal relief.
Because the company was in delicto, it could not recover its losses in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culpable - deserving blame or responsibility for a wrongdoing
  • antonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or wrongdoing
