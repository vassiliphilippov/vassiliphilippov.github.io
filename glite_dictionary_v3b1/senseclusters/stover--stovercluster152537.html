stover

noun
dried crop residue used for animal feed
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈstəʊvə / (BrE)
the dried stalks and leaves of field crops, especially maize, left after harvesting the grain and used as animal fodder

Examples

Dried stover stalks
After the corn was harvested, the farmers collected the stover to feed their cattle during winter.
Baling the stover makes storage and transport to the barn much easier.
Some farmers choose to plow the stover back into the soil to improve fertility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hay - grass that has been cut and dried for use as fodder
  • synonym: silage - fermented, high-moisture stored fodder
