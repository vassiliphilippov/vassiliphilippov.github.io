genus Calendula

noun
genus of flowering plants (marigolds)
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈlɛn.dʒʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs kəˈlɛn.djʊ.lə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, commonly known as marigolds, native to southwestern Asia, western Europe, and the Mediterranean.

Examples

Genus Calendula flowers
Gardeners often plant members of the genus Calendula as ornamental borders in sunny spots.
Medicinal preparations sometimes use extracts from the genus Calendula due to its traditional healing properties.
The genus Calendula includes several species widely grown for their bright and cheerful flowers.
