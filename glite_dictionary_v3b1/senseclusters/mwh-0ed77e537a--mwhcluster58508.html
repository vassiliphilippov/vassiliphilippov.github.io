MWh

noun
unit of energy (megawatt-hour)
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɛm dʌbəl.juː eɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɛm dʌbəl.juː eɪtʃ / (BrE)
A unit of energy equal to one million watt-hours, often used to measure electricity generation or consumption.

Examples

One MWh unit
Our consumption last month was nearly 3 MWh due to increased heating.
The solar farm is expected to generate 500 MWh of electricity per year.
The wind turbines supply around 1,200 MWh annually to the local grid.
