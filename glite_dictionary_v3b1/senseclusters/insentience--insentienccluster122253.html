insentience

noun
lack of consciousness or sensation
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɛnʃəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛnʃɪəns / (BrE)
the quality or state of lacking consciousness or the ability to perceive sensations

Examples

Lack of insentience
Debates about artificial intelligence often address the question of insentience versus sentience.
Some philosophers argue that true insentience is impossible for any living creature.
The insentience of rocks distinguishes them from animals and plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sentience - the capacity to feel, perceive, or experience subjectively
