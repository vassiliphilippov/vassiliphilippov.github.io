antiseptic

noun
substance preventing microbial growth
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tɪˈsɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsept.ɪk / (BrE)
A substance that prevents the growth of disease-causing microorganisms without harming living tissue.

Examples

Antiseptic cream
Applying an antiseptic cream can help protect cuts and scrapes from bacteria.
He cleaned the wound with an antiseptic to prevent infection.
Hospitals use various antiseptics to reduce the risk of disease transmission.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disinfectant - a chemical substance that destroys bacteria, typically used on non-living objects
