search warrant

noun
legal permit for searching property
Pronunciation: / sɝtʃ ˈwɔɹənt /; / ˈsɜrtʃ ˌwɔrənt / (AmE), / sɜːtʃ ˈwɒrənt /; / ˈsɜːtʃ ˌwɒrənt / (BrE)
an official legal document that gives police or authorities permission to search a place for evidence or people related to a crime

Examples

A search warrant
A judge must approve a search warrant before it can be used to conduct a search.
The police obtained a search warrant to look for stolen goods in the suspect's house.
Without a search warrant, officers are generally not allowed to enter private residences.
A judge signed the search warrant to allow the investigation to proceed.
The police needed a search warrant before entering the suspect's house.
Without a search warrant, authorities cannot legally search your property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: warrant - a document issued by a legal or government official authorizing the police to make an arrest, search premises, or carry out some other action
