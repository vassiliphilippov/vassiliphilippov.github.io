non-canonicity

noun
state of not being canonical
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑn kəˌnɑnɪˈsɪɾi / (AmE), / nɒn kəˌnɒnɪˈsɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being canonical; not conforming to established rules, standards, or the accepted canon.

Examples

The non-canonicity of texts
Academic debates often revolve around the non-canonicity of certain texts.
Its non-canonicity set the work apart from mainstream scientific theories.
The non-canonicity of the novel invites readers to question literary traditions.
