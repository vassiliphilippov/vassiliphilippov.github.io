sound hole

noun
opening in a musical instrument's soundboard
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊnd hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd həʊl / (BrE)
An opening in the soundboard of a stringed musical instrument, such as a violin or guitar, which enhances and projects the instrument's sound.

Examples

The sound hole of guitar
Luthiers carefully design the shape and position of the sound hole to influence the instrument's resonance.
She decorated the edge of her violin's sound hole with an intricate pattern.
The guitar's sound hole amplifies the vibrations of the strings, giving it a rich tone.
