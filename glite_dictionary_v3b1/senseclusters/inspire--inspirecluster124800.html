inspire

verb
fill with revolutionary ideas
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to make someone feel motivated, enthusiastic, creative, or to cause a particular feeling or action

Examples

To inspire others
Her words inspired young activists to challenge the status quo.
History teachers often inspire students to think critically about social change.
The leader's speech inspired the crowd with visions of a better future.
A good teacher can inspire students to achieve great things.
Her dedication inspired me to work harder than ever before.
The film inspired him to travel the world and explore new cultures.
A good teacher can inspire a lifelong love of learning in their students.
Her courage and determination inspired everyone in the community.
The novel inspired him to travel the world and pursue his dreams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diminish - to make less or cause to appear less
  • antonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
  • synonym: encourage - to give someone confidence or support to do something
  • synonym: exalt - to elevate in rank, power, or character; to intensify
  • synonym: motivate - to provide someone with a reason or incentive to act
