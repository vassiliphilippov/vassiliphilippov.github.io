congenitally

adverb
from birth
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəli / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəli / (BrE)
in a way that exists from birth or is present at birth

Examples

Congenitally blind
She was congenitally deaf and learned sign language at an early age.
Some people are congenitally predisposed to certain medical conditions.
He was congenitally missing two of his front teeth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired - developed after birth, not present from birth
  • synonym: innately - in a way that is natural or inborn
