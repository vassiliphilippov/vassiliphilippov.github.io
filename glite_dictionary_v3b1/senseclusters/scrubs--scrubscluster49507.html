scrubs

noun
inexperienced or unskilled people (slang)
Pronunciation: / skrʌbz / (AmE), / skrʌbz / (BrE)
slang (informal) people who are inexperienced, unskilled, or performing poorly at a game or activity; often used derogatorily in online gaming and sports contexts.

Examples

Online scrubs
The streamer dismissed his opponents as scrubs after a string of losses.
We practiced all week, but the new players still played like scrubs in the tournament.
Stop arguing and focus on the match; you sound like a team of scrubs right now.
