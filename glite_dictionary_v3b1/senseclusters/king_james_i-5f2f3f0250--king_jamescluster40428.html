King James I

proper noun
Stuart king of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1566–1625)
Pronunciation: / kɪŋ dʒeɪmz ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / kɪŋ dʒeɪmz ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
the first Stuart king of England and Ireland (1603–1625) and king of Scotland (1567–1625), known for his union of the crowns and the authorized King James Bible; son of Mary, Queen of Scots.

Examples

King James I of England
King James I ascended the English throne in 1603, marking the start of the Stuart dynasty.
Parliament frequently clashed with King James I over the extent of royal authority.
The King James Bible, commissioned by King James I, remains influential in English-speaking culture.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Charles I - King James I's son and successor
  • antonym: Elizabeth I - queen of England before King James I
  • synonym: James VI and I - alternative style for the same monarch, also emphasizing his kingship of Scotland as James VI
