gannet

noun
large seabird known for diving for fish
Pronunciation: / ˈɡænət / (AmE), / ˈɡænɪt / (BrE)
a large, heavily built seabird with a long, strong bill, known for its spectacular headlong dives to catch fish

Examples

A gannet colony
On the rocky cliffs, a colony of gannets built their nests for the breeding season.
The gannet plunges into the ocean from great heights to catch its prey.
We watched a gannet dive with astonishing speed and accuracy into the water.
