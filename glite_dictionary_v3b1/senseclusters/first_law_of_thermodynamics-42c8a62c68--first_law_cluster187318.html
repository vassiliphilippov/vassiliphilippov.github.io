first law of thermodynamics

noun
law stating total energy is constant in a system
1.0 3.7
Pronunciation: /fɜːrst lɔː əv θɜːr.moʊ.daɪ.ˈnæm.ɪks/ /; / /fɜːrst lɑː əv θɜːr.moʊ.daɪ.ˈnæm.ɪks/ (AmE), /fɜːst lɔː əv θɜː.məʊ.daɪ.ˈnæm.ɪks/ (BrE)
the principle stating that the total energy of an isolated system remains constant; energy can be transformed but not created or destroyed

Examples

The first law of thermodynamics
According to the first law of thermodynamics, the total energy of the system remains constant during a chemical reaction.
The first law of thermodynamics ensures that energy is neither lost nor gained in any isolated physical process.
The engineer explained how refrigeration systems operate based on the first law of thermodynamics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservation of energy - principle that energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another
