ribbon development

noun
development in a long row along a road
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪbən dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˈrɪbən dɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
Building of houses or other structures in a long, narrow line along a road, particularly on the outskirts of towns or cities.

Examples

Ribbon development along roads
Many planners are concerned that ribbon development leads to inefficient land use and environmental problems.
The old village has been overwhelmed by ribbon development stretching for miles outside the town.
The unchecked ribbon development along the highway caused traffic congestion and loss of green space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: urban sprawl - the uncontrolled expansion of urban areas
