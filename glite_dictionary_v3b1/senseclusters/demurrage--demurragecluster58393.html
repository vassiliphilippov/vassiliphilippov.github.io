demurrage

noun
charge for delayed unloading/loading of cargo
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɜrɪdʒ / (AmE), / dɪˈmʌrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a charge you must pay for keeping a ship, freight car, or other cargo transport longer than the agreed time for loading or unloading

Examples

Demurrage charges
Due to port congestion, several vessels incurred significant demurrage charges this month.
If the containers are not returned promptly, additional demurrage fees will be applied.
The shipping company was forced to pay demurrage after the cargo was not unloaded on schedule.
If the freight car is delayed, the customer is responsible for any demurrage incurred.
The shipping company charged demurrage for every day the containers remained at the port.
Their legal dispute centered on who should pay the demurrage fees after the vessel was held up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detention - the act of detaining a ship or cargo beyond the agreed time
