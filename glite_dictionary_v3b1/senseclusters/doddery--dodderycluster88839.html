doddery

adjective
physically or mentally frail with age
8.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…ëÀêd…ôri / (AmE), / Ààd…íd…ôri / (BrE)
physically weak or mentally impaired, especially because of old age

Examples

A doddery old man
As he became more doddery, his children visited him more often to assist with daily tasks.
Her movements were slow and doddery, but her mind remained sharp.
The elderly man grew increasingly doddery and needed help to cross the street.

  • feeble
    3.9 2/m
    feeble describes a person who lacks strength or energy from age or illness, while doddery suggests also a weak, unsteady mind.
  • frail
    6.5 0.00/m
    frail describes a thin, delicate older person with little physical strength, while doddery can mean both weak body and confused mind.
  • decrepit
    7.0 0.6/m
    decrepit describes something or someone very worn out and weak from age, while doddery is milder and often means shaky or confused.
  • rickety
    7.0 0.2/m
    rickety usually describes a shaky body or thin person and things that are unsteady, while doddery focuses on old age weakness and confusion.
