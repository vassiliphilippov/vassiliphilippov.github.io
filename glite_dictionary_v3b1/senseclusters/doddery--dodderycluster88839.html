doddery

adjective
physically or mentally frail with age
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːdəri / (AmE), / ˈdɒdəri / (BrE)
physically weak or mentally impaired, especially because of old age

Examples

A doddery old man
As he became more doddery, his children visited him more often to assist with daily tasks.
Her movements were slow and doddery, but her mind remained sharp.
The elderly man grew increasingly doddery and needed help to cross the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially as a result of age or illness
  • antonym: spry - active and lively, especially for one's age
