multihull

adjective
with multiple hulls
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltiˌhʌl / (AmE), / ˈmʌltiˌhʌl / (BrE)
having two or more hulls, especially referring to boats or ships designed with multiple hulls for stability or speed

Examples

Multihull yacht design
The multihull design made the yacht much more stable in rough waters.
Engineers compared multihull and monohull vessels to determine which performed better in high winds.
Many modern ferries use a multihull structure to increase passenger comfort and safety.
