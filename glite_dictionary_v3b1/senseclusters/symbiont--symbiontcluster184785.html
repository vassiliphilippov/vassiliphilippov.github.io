symbiont

noun
organism in symbiosis
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmbaɪˌɑnt / (AmE), / ˈsɪmbɪɒnt /; / ˈsɪmbiɒnt / (BrE)
an organism (or sometimes a person or group) that lives in a close, long-term relationship with another, often with mutual benefit

Examples

A symbiont organism
The tiny symbiont helps the plant absorb nutrients from the soil.
Scientists discovered a new symbiont living inside the insect's gut.
Some corals rely on a symbiont to survive in warm ocean waters.
The tiny symbiont helps its host digest tough plant material.
Coral reefs depend on a symbiont called zooxanthellae for their vibrant colors.
Scientists discovered a new symbiont living inside deep-sea worms.
A symbiont can help its host digest food that would otherwise be indigestible.
Coral reefs depend on the relationship between the coral and its symbiont algae.
Scientists discovered a new symbiont living inside the roots of certain plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: host - an organism that supports a symbiont
  • degree higher: mutualist - an organism in a relationship where both partners benefit
  • degree lower: parasite - an organism that lives on or in another and benefits at the host's expense
  • synonym: partner - a person or organism that takes part in a partnership
