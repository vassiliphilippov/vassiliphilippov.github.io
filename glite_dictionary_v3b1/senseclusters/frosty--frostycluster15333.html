frosty

adjective
pleasantly cold and invigorating (weather)
1.0 1.02/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfrÉ‘sti / (AmE), / ËˆfrÉ’sti / (BrE)
cold in temperature or covered with frost; having a thin layer of ice crystals from cold weather

Examples

Frosty morning

The mountains were especially beautiful in the frosty autumn air.
We enjoyed a frosty evening stroll under a clear, starry sky.
A frosty layer had formed on the car windshield overnight.

After their argument, there was a frosty silence between them at dinner.

Synonyms

  • rimy
    9.9 0.01/m
    rimy is covered with a thin white frost on surfaces, while frosty often means general cold or light frost on things.
  • icy
    1.0 7.35/m
    icy feels or looks very cold and often has hard ice, while frosty means milder cold with a thin, soft layer of frost.
