feather

noun
row: turning oar parallel between pulls
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛðɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
The act of turning an oar parallel to the surface of the water between strokes to reduce wind resistance.

Examples

Feather of the oar
Learning to feather properly helps reduce air resistance when rowing against the wind.
Rowers must feather their oars after each stroke for maximum efficiency.
She struggled at first to master the feather of the oar, but soon got the hang of it.
