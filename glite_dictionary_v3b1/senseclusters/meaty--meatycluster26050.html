meaty

adjective
substantial or thought-provoking
Pronunciation: / ˈmiti / (AmE), / ˈmiːti / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) substantial and thought-provoking; containing a lot of meaningful content.

Examples

Meaty discussion
She prefers to read meaty articles that challenge her perspective rather than light entertainment.
The professor's lecture was especially meaty, sparking lively debate among the students.
We had a meaty discussion about the implications of artificial intelligence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: substantial - important, meaningful, or considerable in amount or value
  • antonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • synonym: weighty - serious, important, or thought-provoking
