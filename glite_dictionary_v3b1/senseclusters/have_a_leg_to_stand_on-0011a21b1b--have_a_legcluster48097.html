have a leg to stand on

verb
have justification or support for one's case
2.3 5.3
Pronunciation: /hæv ə leɡ tə stænd ɑːn/ /; / /həv ə leɡ tə stænd ɑːn/ (AmE), /hæv ə leɡ tə stænd ɒn/ /; / /həv ə leɡ tə stænd ɒn/ (BrE)
to have a valid reason, argument, or justification that supports one's case or position

Examples

To have a leg to stand on in argument
He realized he didn't have a leg to stand on after his story fell apart.
If she can't find any witnesses, she won't have a leg to stand on in the case.
Without proof, the company doesn't have a leg to stand on in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: have grounds - to have basis or justification for something
  • antonym: have no leg to stand on - to lack any justification or support for one's argument
