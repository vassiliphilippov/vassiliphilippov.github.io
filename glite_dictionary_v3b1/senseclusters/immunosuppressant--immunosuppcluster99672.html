immunosuppressant

noun
drug that suppresses the immune response
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəsoʊˈprɛsənt / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊsəˈprɛsənt / (BrE)
A drug that reduces the strength of the body's immune response, often to prevent rejection of transplanted organs or to treat autoimmune diseases.

Examples

An immunosuppressant drug
Following her organ transplant, the patient began taking an immunosuppressant to prevent rejection.
Some immunosuppressants can make patients more susceptible to infections.
The doctor explained that an immunosuppressant is necessary for people with autoimmune disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunosuppressive - pertaining to the suppression of the immune response
