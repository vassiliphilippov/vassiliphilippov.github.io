ignition

noun
the act of setting on fire
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪgˈnɪʃən /; / ɪɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪgˈnɪʃ(ə)n /; / ɪɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of starting something burning or causing it to catch fire

Examples

Accidental ignition
Careless ignition of flammable materials can have disastrous consequences.
The ignition of the dry grass resulted in a rapidly spreading wildfire.
The engineer explained how precise timing of ignition is critical in car engines.
A single spark can lead to the ignition of flammable materials.
Scientists studied the ignition point of various chemicals in the lab.
The ignition of the engine occurred as soon as she turned the key.
