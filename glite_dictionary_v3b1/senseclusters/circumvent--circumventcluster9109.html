circumvent

verb
surround or besiege (military)
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝː.kəmˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈvent / (BrE)
to surround or enclose (an enemy) so as to force surrender; to besiege

Examples

To circumvent an army
By using superior numbers, they managed to circumvent the enemy stronghold.
The army planned to circumvent the city and cut off all supplies.
The rebels attempted to circumvent the fortress by laying siege for weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besiege - to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force surrender
