Cronbach's alpha

noun
reliability statistic
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑːnbɑkz ˈælfə / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnbækz ˈælfə / (BrE)
a statistic that measures the internal consistency or reliability of a set of survey or test items

Examples

Cronbach's alpha value
The researchers calculated Cronbach's alpha to assess the reliability of their new questionnaire.
A high Cronbach's alpha value suggests that the survey items are measuring the same concept.
Before publishing the results, the team checked Cronbach's alpha to ensure internal consistency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reliability coefficient - a measure of the consistency of a test or scale
  • synonym: test-retest reliability - a measure of reliability based on repeated testing
