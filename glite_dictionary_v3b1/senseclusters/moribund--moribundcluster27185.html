moribund

adjective
in decline or lacking vitality
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrɪˌbʌnd /; / ˈmɔr.ɪˌbʌnd / (AmE), / ˈmɒr.ɪ.bʌnd / (BrE)
in a state of being nearly dead, inactive, or about to end; showing no signs of life or growth

Examples

A moribund industry
Interest in that traditional art form has become moribund over the past decade.
Many experts agree that the company’s moribund product line urgently needs innovation to survive.
The once bustling town center is now moribund, with empty shops and little foot traffic.
After years of neglect, the moribund tradition finally disappeared from the community.
Economic reforms were introduced to revive the moribund industry.
The moribund patient was surrounded by his family in his final hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deceased - no longer living; dead
  • synonym: dying - being in the process of dying
  • antonym: flourishing - growing or developing in a healthy or vigorous way
  • synonym: stagnant - not flowing or changing, lacking movement or development
