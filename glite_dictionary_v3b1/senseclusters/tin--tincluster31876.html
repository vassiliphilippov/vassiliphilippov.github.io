tin

noun
a silvery-white metallic element (Sn)
3.9
Pronunciation: / tɪn / (AmE), / tɪn / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white metal, chemical element number 50, known for its resistance to corrosion and use in making alloys and coating other metals.

Examples

Pure tin metal
Tin is often combined with copper to make bronze.
Large deposits of tin can be found in Southeast Asia.
The cans are coated with tin to prevent them from rusting.
