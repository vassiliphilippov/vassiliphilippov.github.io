course credit

noun
recognition for completing an academic course
Pronunciation: / kɔrs ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / kɔːs ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
an official recognition by a college or university that a student has successfully completed a course, usually measured in semester hours or units

Examples

Earn course credits
Each student must earn at least 120 course credits to graduate with a bachelor's degree.
One course credit typically represents three hours of classroom work per week.
She transferred her course credits from her previous university to her new program.
