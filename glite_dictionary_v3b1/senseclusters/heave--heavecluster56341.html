heave

noun
horizontal displacement in geology
5.8
Pronunciation: / hiv / (AmE), / hiːv / (BrE)
(Geology) A horizontal displacement or dislocation along a fault plane.

Examples

Fault heave
Geologists measured the horizontal heave to better understand the fault's movement.
The earthquake caused a significant heave along the fault, shifting the land to one side.
This part of the valley shows clear evidence of a major heave in the underlying rock layers.
