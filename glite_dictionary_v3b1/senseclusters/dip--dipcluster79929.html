dip

noun
a small hollow or depression
Pronunciation: / dɪp / (AmE), / dɪp / (BrE)
A small depression or hollow in an otherwise level surface.

Examples

A dip in the ground
A small dip in the pavement caused her to stumble.
After the heavy rain, a shallow dip formed in the field and filled with water.
Drivers often slow down before a sudden dip in the road to avoid damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bump - a raised area on a surface, the opposite of a dip
  • synonym: depression - a sunken area or hollow in a surface
