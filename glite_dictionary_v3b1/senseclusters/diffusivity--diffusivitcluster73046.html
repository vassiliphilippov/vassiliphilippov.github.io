diffusivity

noun
rate of mixing
Pronunciation: / dɪˌfjuːˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / dɪˌfjuːˈsɪvɪti /; / dɪˌfjuːˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
the rate or measure of how quickly something (such as heat, mass, or particles) spreads or moves through another substance or material

Examples

Diffusivity of heat
Scientists measured the diffusivity of salt in seawater to better understand ocean mixing.
Higher diffusivity in the atmosphere can lead to faster temperature changes.
Engineers adjusted the model's diffusivity to simulate the effects of turbulence in the pipe.
The diffusivity of gases increases with temperature, making them spread faster.
Scientists measured the diffusivity of the new material to determine how quickly water could pass through it.
High diffusivity allows nutrients to move rapidly within the cell membrane.

The diffusivity of gases increases with temperature, allowing them to spread more quickly.
Understanding the diffusivity of soil helps engineers design better drainage systems.

  • synonym: permeability - the ability of a material to allow substances to pass through it
