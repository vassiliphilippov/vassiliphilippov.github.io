honeycombing

noun
pattern of cavities
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌnikəʊmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌnikəʊmɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of forming a pattern of holes or cavities resembling a honeycomb, especially in materials like concrete or tissue

Examples

Concrete honeycombing
The engineer noticed honeycombing in the concrete wall, which could weaken the structure.
Lung scans revealed areas of honeycombing typical of certain chronic diseases.
After removing the old insulation, they found extensive honeycombing in the wooden beams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: porosity - the quality of having tiny holes that allow air or liquid to pass through
  • antonym: solidification - the process of becoming solid without forming cavities
