Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution

noun
radical political movement in China (1966-1976) led by Mao Zedong
9.9
Pronunciation: /greɪt proʊ.lə.ˈter.i.ən ˈkʌl.tʃər.əl rev.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (AmE), /greɪt prəʊ.lə.ˈteə.ri.ən ˈkʌl.tʃər.əl rev.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (BrE)
a radical sociopolitical movement in China launched by Mao Zedong from 1966 to 1976, aiming to enforce communism by removing capitalist, traditional, and cultural elements from Chinese society; it resulted in widespread purges, social upheaval, and persecution of intellectuals

Examples

The Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution
During the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution, many intellectuals were targeted and sent to rural areas for re-education.
Historians continue to debate the impact of the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution on China's development.
The Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution profoundly changed the social and political landscape of modern China.
