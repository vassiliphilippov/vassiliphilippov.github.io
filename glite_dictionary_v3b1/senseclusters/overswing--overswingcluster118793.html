overswing

noun
excessive shift
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌswɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌswɪŋ / (BrE)
a large or excessive change in direction, opinion, policy, mood, or market values, often beyond what is reasonable or expected

Examples

Market overswing
The stock market experienced an overswing after the unexpected economic news.
Analysts warned that an overswing in public opinion could lead to unstable policies.
The team's strategy change resulted in an overswing that surprised their competitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcorrection - an excessive adjustment in response to a problem
  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and not changing
  • degree lower: swing - a change or shift in direction, opinion, or value
