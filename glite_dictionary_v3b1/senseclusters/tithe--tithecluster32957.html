tithe

noun
one-tenth tax, especially for the church
6.6 9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪð /; / taɪð / (AmE), / ˈtaɪð /; / taɪð / (BrE)
a tenth part of income or produce given as a tax or offering, especially for religious or charitable purposes

Examples

Church tithe payment
Many medieval farmers were required to pay a tithe to the local church each year.
She donated a tithe of her earnings to charity every month.
The law required landowners to give a tithe of their harvest as part of their religious obligations.
Historically, farmers would pay a tithe to support the local parish.
In many churches, members are encouraged to give a tithe of their income.
She calculated her tithe based on her annual earnings and donated it willingly.

  • synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue
