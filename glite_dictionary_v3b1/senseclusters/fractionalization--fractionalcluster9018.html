fractionalization

noun
dividing ownership
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌfrækʃənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfrækʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of dividing ownership of an asset or property into smaller parts so that multiple people can own shares

Examples

Fractionalization of ownership
The fractionalization of the property allowed several families to invest together.
Modern investment platforms make fractionalization of expensive assets much easier for small investors.
After fractionalization, each shareholder owned a portion of the building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consolidation - the act of combining things into a single whole
  • synonym: partition - the act of dividing something into parts
