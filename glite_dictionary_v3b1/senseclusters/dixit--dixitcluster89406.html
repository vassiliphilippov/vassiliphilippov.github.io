dixit

noun
unsupported assertion
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪksɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪksɪt / (BrE)
an assertion or statement made without supporting evidence, often presented as authoritative or dogmatic

Examples

A bold dixit
The expert's opinion was dismissed as mere dixit without any data to support it.
Journalists criticized the report for relying on dixit rather than verifiable facts.
In academic writing, a dixit is not enough; you must provide evidence for your claims.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assertion - a confident and forceful statement of fact or belief
  • synonym: claim - a statement that something is true, often without proof
  • antonym: evidence - facts or information supporting a belief or claim
