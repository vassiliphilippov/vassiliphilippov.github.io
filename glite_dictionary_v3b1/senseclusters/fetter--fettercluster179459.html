fetter

verb
to restrain with chains or shackles
8.7 0.22/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfÉ›tÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆfÉ›tÉ™ / (BrE)
to restrain or shackle someone with chains around the ankles; to limit someone's freedom or movement

Examples

To fetter a prisoner
Heavy chains fettered the prisoners, making escape impossible.
She felt fettered by the strict rules of her household.
The prison guard fettered the inmate's feet before escorting him down the hall.

Synonyms

  • shackle
    6.2 1.53/m
    shackle attaches heavy metal restraints to hands or feet, while fetter often means chains around the ankles or limits movement.
  • tether
    4.4 1.54/m
    tether ties an animal or object to a fixed point with a rope or chain, while fetter means to bind ankles and limit movement.
  • restrain
    4.6 5.37/m
    restrain stops someone from acting or moving by force or rules, while fetter suggests binding with chains and limiting freedom.
  • tie down
    4.7 0.55/m
    tie down makes someone dependent or responsible and reduces freedom, while fetter usually means physical chains or clear movement limits.
