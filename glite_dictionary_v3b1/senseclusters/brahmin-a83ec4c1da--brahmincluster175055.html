Brahmin

noun
a breed of heat-resistant humped cattle
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːmɪn / (BrE)
a breed of large, greyish humped cattle originally from India, developed in the United States for resistance to heat and ticks, and commonly used for crossbreeding

Examples

Brahmin cattle breed
Brahmin cattle are noted for their distinctive humps and loose skin.
Farmers often use Brahmin bulls for crossbreeding with local cows to improve their stock.
The Texas rancher raised a herd of Brahmin cattle for their resilience in hot weather.
