payout

noun
sum of money paid
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪˌaʊt / (BrE)
a sum of money paid to someone, for example as a reward, compensation, winnings, or return on investment

Examples

A payout to shareholders
The company announced a record payout to its shareholders this year.
After the accident, he received a large insurance payout to cover his medical expenses.
Lottery winners often choose between a lump-sum payout and annual payments.
The lottery winner received a huge payout after matching all the numbers.
After the accident, the insurance company processed his payout quickly.
The company announced a special payout to shareholders at the end of the year.
The insurance company made a large payout after the accident was confirmed.
Slot machines advertise their payout rates to attract more players to the casino.
Shareholders were pleased with the annual payout from the company's profits.

  • antonym: penalty - a sum of money paid as punishment
  • antonym: receipt - the act of receiving money
