sacculate

adjective
having saclike expansions
Pronunciation: / ˈsæk.jəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsak.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(chiefly of biological or anatomical structures) formed with or having saclike expansions or pouches.

Examples

A sacculate stomach
In certain species, the sacculate stomach allows for more efficient digestion.
Some segments of the large intestine are notably sacculate in appearance.
The sacculate structure of the respiratory system can trap air for buoyancy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: saccular - having the form of a sac or pouch
