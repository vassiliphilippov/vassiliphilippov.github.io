best evidence rule

noun
legal rule requiring original documents as evidence
Pronunciation: / bɛst ˈɛvədəns ruːl / (AmE), / bɛst ˈɛvɪdəns ruːl / (BrE)
a legal rule requiring that, to prove the content of a document, recording, or photograph, the original must be produced in court if available

Examples

The best evidence rule applies
Law students often study the best evidence rule when learning about rules of admissibility.
The court rejected the photocopy as evidence because it violated the best evidence rule.
Under the best evidence rule, the original contract must be presented to prove its terms in court.
