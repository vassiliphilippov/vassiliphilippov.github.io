unrecoverably

adverb
irreversibly
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈkʌvərəbli / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈkʌvərəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be restored, regained, or brought back to a previous state

Examples

Documents unrecoverably destroyed
After the fire, the documents were unrecoverably destroyed and could not be replaced.
The files were unrecoverably deleted from the computer after the virus attack.
Once trust is broken unrecoverably, it is very difficult to rebuild a relationship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irretrievably - in a way that cannot be recovered or undone
  • synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever or cannot be changed
  • antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time
