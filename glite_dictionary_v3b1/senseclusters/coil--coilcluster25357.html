coil

noun
loops or rings wound together
9.1 3.1
Pronunciation: /kɔɪl/ (AmE), /kɔɪl/ (BrE)
a length or series of something (such as wire, rope, or petals) wound into a spiral or loops

Examples

A coil of rope
A coil of wire was needed to repair the old radio.
She wrapped the coil of rope tightly and placed it on the shelf.
The snake rested in a loose coil beneath the rock.
She admired the delicate coils of the rose petals.
The old rope lay on the dock in a loose coil.
The snake rested in a tight coil in the shade of the tree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loop - a shape produced by a curve that bends around and crosses itself
  • synonym: spiral - a curve that winds around a central point, getting progressively closer or farther away
