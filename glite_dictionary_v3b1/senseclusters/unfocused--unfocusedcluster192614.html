unfocused

adjective
not concentrated; lacking clear focus
6.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
not concentrated on a particular point or objective; lacking clear direction or focus

Examples

Unfocused attention
Her unfocused mind drifted from one thought to another during the lecture.
His photos are often unfocused, making it hard to see the subject clearly.
The company's unfocused strategy made it difficult to achieve consistent results.
His unfocussed energy made it difficult for him to finish any of his projects.
The photograph was unfocussed, so the details were blurry and indistinct.
The team's strategy was unfocussed, leading to confusion among the players.

Related Terms

  • antonym: focused - concentrated on one point or goal
  • synonym: scattered - spread out in different directions, lacking order or unity
