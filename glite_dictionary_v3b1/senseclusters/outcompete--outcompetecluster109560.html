outcompete

verb
to perform better
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtkəmˈpiːt / (AmE), / ˌaʊtkəmˈpiːt / (BrE)
to perform better than someone or something else and win as a result

Examples

To outcompete rivals
The new smartphone is expected to outcompete all other models this year.
Invasive species often outcompete native plants for sunlight and nutrients.
Small businesses must innovate to outcompete larger corporations in the market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to fail to win against someone or something
  • synonym: outperform - to achieve better results than someone or something
  • synonym: surpass - to do better than someone or something
