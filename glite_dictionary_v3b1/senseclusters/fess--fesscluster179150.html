fess

noun
heraldic horizontal band on a shield
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɛs / (AmE), / fɛs / (BrE)
(in heraldry) a broad horizontal band across the middle of a shield.

Examples

Fess on a shield
A fess is often used in heraldry to symbolize a military belt or girdle of honor.
The family crest displays a red fess across a silver background.
Three golden lions are shown above the fess on the ancient shield.
