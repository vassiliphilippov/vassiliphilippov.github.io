head sea

noun
waves opposing a ship's course
7.8 8.0
Pronunciation: /hed siː/ (AmE), /hed siː/ (BrE)
A sea in which the waves are running directly against the direction of a ship's movement.

Examples

A head sea wave
Engineers must account for the impact of a head sea when designing the bow of an ocean-going vessel.
Sailing into a head sea can make for a rough and uncomfortable journey for everyone aboard.
The ship slowed considerably as it encountered a strong head sea on its way to the harbor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: following sea - a sea in which the waves move in the same direction as the vessel
