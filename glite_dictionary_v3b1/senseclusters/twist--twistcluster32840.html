twist

noun
a sudden pulling or wrenching movement
2.3 2.1
Pronunciation: /twɪst/ (AmE), /twɪst/ (BrE)
a movement, shape, or bend that is curved, wound, or twisted; or the act of turning or winding something

Examples

A sharp twist
He gave the handle a sharp twist to open the door.
The mechanic used a firm twist to loosen the stubborn bolt.
With a sudden twist of her body, she freed herself from his grip.
A sudden twist in the river created a visible swirl near the bank.
Dust rose in a tiny twist as the warm wind met the cold air.
The boat spun unexpectedly after hitting a twist in the shallow water.
The river takes another twist before flowing into the valley.
There was a sharp twist in the mountain road just before the old bridge.
With each twist in the path, the scenery changed dramatically.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bend - a curve in a road or river
  • synonym: eddy - a circular movement of water, forming a small whirlpool
  • synonym: knot - an interlacing, looping, or fastening of rope or cord
  • synonym: rotation - the movement of turning around a central point
  • synonym: spin - the act of turning around quickly
  • antonym: straight - having no curves or bends
  • degree lower: turn - the act of moving something in a circular direction
  • synonym: wind - the act of turning or winding something
  • synonym: wrench - a sudden violent twist or pull
