twist

noun
a sudden pulling or wrenching movement
2.4 3.62/m
Pronunciation: / twÉªst / (AmE), / twÉªst / (BrE)
a movement, shape, or bend that is curved, wound, or twisted; or the act of turning or winding something

Examples

A sharp twist
He gave the handle a sharp twist to open the door.
The mechanic used a firm twist to loosen the stubborn bolt.
With a sudden twist of her body, she freed herself from his grip.
A sudden twist in the river created a visible swirl near the bank.
Dust rose in a tiny twist as the warm wind met the cold air.
The boat spun unexpectedly after hitting a twist in the shallow water.
The river takes another twist before flowing into the valley.
There was a sharp twist in the mountain road just before the old bridge.
With each twist in the path, the scenery changed dramatically.

Synonyms

  • winding
    4.9 0.12/m
    winding is the act of coiling or turning something around to make a coil, while twist can be a single turn.
  • turn
    1.0 41/m
    turn is changing direction or rotating around a point, while twist often adds bending or winding into a more complex shape.
  • winding
    3.1 0.15/m
    winding is a long curve or spiral like a river or path, while twist can be a short or tighter bend.
  • kink
    5.9 0.23/m
    kink is a sharp, unwanted twist or bend in a rope or wire, while twist can be a normal or planned turn.
  • crook
    4.4 1.32/m
    crook is a sharp bend where something turns suddenly, while twist often means twisting or winding along a length.
  • bend
    1.2 6.56/m
    bend is a smooth curved part of something like a road or pipe, while twist implies turning plus winding.
