without

preposition
outside; beyond
Pronunciation: / wɪˈðaʊt / (AmE), / wɪˈðaʊt / (BrE)
outside or beyond the limits of a place, especially in a literary or formal context.

Examples

Without the city
The travelers camped without the city walls for the night.
A crowd gathered without the palace, waiting for news from inside.
Soldiers stood guard without the fortress until morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: within - inside or inside the limits of something
