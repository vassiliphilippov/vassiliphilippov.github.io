Manichaean

adjective
of or relating to Manichaeism
Pronunciation: / ˌmænəˈkiːən /; / ˌmænəˈkiən / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkiːən / (BrE)
relating to Manichaeism, an ancient religion that taught the struggle between good and evil

Examples

Manichaean beliefs
Her thesis analyzes the Manichaean worldview in medieval literature.
Some historians debate the influence of Manichaean beliefs on early Christianity.
The philosopher wrote extensively on the Manichaean concept of dualism.
His rhetoric often takes on a strongly Manichaean tone, dividing the world into clear good and evil.
Some philosophers criticize Manichaean thinking as an oversimplification of complex issues.
The novel explores Manichaean themes of light versus darkness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dualistic - involving two opposing forces or principles
  • antonym: monistic - relating to the doctrine that reality is a single principle or entity
