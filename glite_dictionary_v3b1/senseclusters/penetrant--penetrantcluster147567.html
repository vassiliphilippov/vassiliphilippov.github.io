penetrant

noun
substance that soaks in
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpe.nə.trənt/ /; / /ˈpe.nɪ.trənt/ (AmE), /ˈpe.nə.trənt/ /; / /ˈpe.nɪ.trənt/ (BrE)
a substance that is used to enter or soak into materials, often to reveal defects, loosen parts, or help other chemicals penetrate

Examples

A penetrant spray
The engineer sprayed a penetrant on the metal to check for hidden cracks.
A special penetrant is used in non-destructive testing to find flaws in materials.
Before loosening the rusty bolt, he applied a penetrant to help break the corrosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lubricant - a substance used to reduce friction between surfaces
  • synonym: solvent - a liquid that dissolves other substances
