marine turtle

noun
large sea-dwelling turtle with flippers
Pronunciation: / məˈrin ˈtɝː.tl̩ / (AmE), / məˈriːn ˈtɜː.tl̩ / (BrE)
any of various large turtles living in the sea, with limbs adapted into flippers, found in warm oceans worldwide

Examples

A marine turtle swims
A marine turtle can often be seen surfacing for air near coral reefs.
Conservation programs aim to protect marine turtles during their nesting season.
The marine turtle population has declined significantly due to habitat loss and poaching.
