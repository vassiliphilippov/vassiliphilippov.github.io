setup

noun
act of framing someone with false charges
2.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈse.tʌp/ /; / /ˈse.təp/ (AmE), /ˈse.təp/ /; / /ˈse.tʌp/ (BrE)
an act of tricking someone into being accused or convicted of a crime they did not commit, often by arranging false evidence

Examples

A police setup
After the investigation, it became clear that the entire case was a setup to ruin his reputation.
Many believed the drug charges were simply a setup orchestrated by political rivals.
She claimed she was the victim of a setup and had nothing to do with the crime.
