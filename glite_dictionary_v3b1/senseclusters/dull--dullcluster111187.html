dull

adjective
lacking liveliness or excitement
Pronunciation: / dʌl / (AmE), / dʌl / (BrE)
not interesting, exciting, or lively; boring or causing boredom (used for things, events, business, or weather)

Examples

Dull movie
After the thrilling summer, winter seemed long and dull.
She found the class dull and wished for something more exciting.
The performance was so dull that several people fell asleep in the audience.
After a week of dull weather, everyone was desperate for some excitement.
His voice was so dull that nobody listened to the presentation.
The dull lecture put half the class to sleep within minutes.
After the holidays, the store's dull sales reflected the seasonal slowdown.
It's been a dull week in the office, with very few new clients.
The market has remained dull despite hopes for a quick recovery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active - engaged or busy; lively
  • synonym: boring - not interesting; tedious
  • antonym: interesting - arousing curiosity or engaging attention
  • antonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • synonym: slow - not quick or lively in activity
  • synonym: tedious - too long, slow, or dull; tiresome or monotonous
