re-address

verb
put a new address on (an envelope or package)
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.əˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to put a new address on something, especially an envelope or package, usually for forwarding to a different location

Examples

To re-address an envelope
After moving to a new apartment, he had to re-address all the invitations he had sent.
She carefully re-addressed the package so it would reach its correct destination.
The post office will re-address your mail if you submit a change of address form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forward - to send mail to a new address
