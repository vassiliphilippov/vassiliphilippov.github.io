stapedotomy

noun
ear surgery for hearing
9.9
Pronunciation: /steɪ.pə.ˈdɑː.tə.miː/ /; / /steɪ.pə.ˈdɔː.tə.miː/ (AmE), /steɪ.pə.ˈdɒ.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a small hole is made in the stapes bone to improve hearing

Examples

A stapedotomy surgery
The doctor recommended a stapedotomy to help restore her hearing loss.
After undergoing stapedotomy, he noticed a significant improvement in his ability to hear conversations.
A stapedotomy is often performed to treat hearing problems caused by otosclerosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: otosclerosis - a condition causing abnormal bone growth in the ear, often treated by stapedotomy
  • synonym: stapedectomy - surgical removal of the stapes bone to improve hearing
