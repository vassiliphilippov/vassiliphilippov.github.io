tonnage

noun
a tax on ships based on their size
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʌnɪdʒ / (BrE)
a tax levied on ships when they enter a country, calculated according to the size or tonnage of the ship

Examples

Tonnage tax
Many countries imposed tonnage on foreign vessels entering their ports in the 19th century.
Merchants often factored tonnage charges into the total cost of their shipping operations.
The ship's owner paid the tonnage before docking at the New York harbor.
