embryogenesis

noun
embryo development process
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmbrioʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛmbrɪəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which an embryo forms and develops from a fertilized egg or single cell

Examples

Embryogenesis process
Researchers are investigating how genes control embryogenesis in different animal species.
Abnormalities during embryogenesis can lead to developmental disorders.
The study of embryogenesis helps scientists understand the earliest stages of life.
Scientists study embryogenesis to better understand how complex organisms develop from a single cell.
In plants, embryogenesis can be induced in the laboratory to produce new seedlings.
Errors during embryogenesis may lead to developmental abnormalities in animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embryology - the scientific study of embryos and their development
  • degree lower: fertilization - the union of male and female gametes to form a zygote
  • synonym: morphogenesis - the process that causes an organism to develop its shape
  • synonym: organogenesis - the formation of organs during development
