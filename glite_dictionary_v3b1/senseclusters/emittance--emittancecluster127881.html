emittance

noun
beam spread measure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɪtəns / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪtəns / (BrE)
a measure of how much a particle beam spreads in position and momentum, indicating its focus or quality

Examples

Beam emittance
The emittance of the particle beam must be minimized for precise experiments.
Scientists monitor the emittance to ensure the accelerator produces a high-quality beam.
Lower emittance allows for more accurate targeting in advanced physics research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brightness - the intensity or quality of a particle beam
  • synonym: focus - the degree to which a beam is concentrated
