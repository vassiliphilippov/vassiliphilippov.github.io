cachet

noun
prestige; mark of high status
Pronunciation: / kæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / kæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
a quality that marks someone or something as prestigious or having approved or superior status

Examples

Social cachet
Owning a home in that neighborhood carries a certain cachet among the city's elite.
The designer's name adds extra cachet to the clothing line.
Winning the award gave him considerable cachet within his profession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinction - excellence or unique quality that sets someone apart
  • antonym: obscurity - lack of fame, importance, or recognition
  • synonym: prestige - widespread respect and admiration; high status
