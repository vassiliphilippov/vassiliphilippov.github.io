lean

verb
to incline or bend from vertical
1.5 39/m
Pronunciation: / lin / (AmE), / liÀên / (BrE)
to incline, bend, or rest at an angle; to place something so it is sloping or supported

Examples

To lean against a wall
He leaned against the wall, exhausted from the long run.
She leaned forward to listen more closely.
The tree leans slightly to the left after the storm.
He accidentally leaned the ladder against the window at an angle.
Heavy winds can lean a fence if it's not properly secured.
The sculpture was leaned to one side to create a dramatic effect.
He leaned the ladder on the side of the house before climbing up.
She carefully leaned the painting against the wall to avoid damaging it.
They leaned their bikes against the fence while they played in the park.

Synonyms

  • tilt
    2.0 16/m
    tilt moves or makes something slope, while lean also means resting at an angle or being supported.
  • slant
    3.7 3.40/m
    slant slopes or tilts in one direction, while lean covers both a physical rest and placing something at an angle.
  • tip
    1.8 10/m
    tip tilts or moves an object and can suggest overturning, while lean mainly means to rest or slope without falling.
  • angle
    3.2 3.65/m
    angle moves or sets something at a specific sloping position for use, while lean is more general about resting at an angle.
  • list
    8.8 3.51/m
    list tilts unintentionally to one side, often of ships, while lean covers any incline or placing something to rest at an angle.
  • skew
    4.8 1.02/m
    skew turns or places something so it is not straight or parallel, while lean simply means incline or rest at an angle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prop - to place something so that it supports or holds up another object
  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight or upright
  • synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
  • synonym: tip - to tilt or cause to lean to one side
