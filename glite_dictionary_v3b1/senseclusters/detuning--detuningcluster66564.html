detuning

noun
frequency offset
8.6
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈtuː.ɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈtuː.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈtjuː.ɪŋ/ /; / /diː.ˈtjuː.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the amount by which a signal, oscillator, or system differs from a reference frequency or resonance

Examples

Radio detuning
The detuning of the radio receiver caused it to miss the correct station frequency.
Scientists adjusted the detuning to optimize the performance of the laser system.
A small detuning can significantly affect the outcome of sensitive experiments.
