tettigoniid

noun
insect family including katydids or bush crickets
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɛˈtɪɡəˌniːɪd / (AmE), / tɛˈtɪɡəniɪd / (BrE)
A member of a family of grasshopper-like insects with long, threadlike antennae and, in males, well-developed sound-producing organs on the forewings; commonly known as katydids or bush crickets.

Examples

A tettigoniid insect
Many gardeners appreciate the presence of tettigoniids because they help control pest populations.
The male tettigoniid produces its distinctive song by rubbing its wings together.
Unlike true grasshoppers, a tettigoniid has much longer antennae and a more delicate body.
