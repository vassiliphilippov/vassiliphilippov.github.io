rose bay

noun
poisonous ornamental shrub (oleander)
6.0 6.6
Pronunciation: /roʊz beɪ/ (AmE), /rəʊz beɪ/ (BrE)
An ornamental but poisonous evergreen shrub with narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant white, pink, or red flowers; native to the East Indies and widely cultivated in warm regions.

Examples

The rose bay shrub
Be careful not to let pets or children near the rose bay as all parts of the plant are toxic.
In Mediterranean climates, rose bay thrives as an attractive but potentially hazardous ornamental shrub.
The garden was lined with tall rose bay, their blossoms perfuming the warm evening air.
