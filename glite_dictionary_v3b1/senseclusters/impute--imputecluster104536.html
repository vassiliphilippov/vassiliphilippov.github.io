impute

verb
attribute something, especially negative, to someone
8.7 1.04/m
Pronunciation: / …™mÀàpjut / (AmE), / …™mÀàpjuÀêt / (BrE)
to attribute responsibility, blame, or cause to someone or something (often unfairly or without proof)

Examples

To impute blame
He was careful not to impute blame to any particular person for the mistake.
It would be unfair to impute any wrongdoing to her without evidence.
Some critics impute ulterior motives to the company's charitable actions.
It was unfair to impute blame to the new employee without evidence.
She resented that her motives were imputed to selfishness when she was truly trying to help.
Some historians impute the country's rapid progress to innovative government policies.

Synonyms

  • attribute
    3.8 28/m
    attribute is to say something is caused or owned by someone, while impute often suggests blaming without strong proof.
  • blame
    1.6 97/m
    blame means saying someone is at fault and often sounds direct or angry, while impute often implies an unfair or unproven charge.
  • ascribe
    6.8 5.20/m
    ascribe means to say something is caused or made by a person or source in formal use, while impute often suggests blaming without proof.
  • assign
    3.2 5.65/m
    assign often means to give a task or credit in formal settings, while impute usually means blaming or attributing cause, often without proof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascribe - to regard a quality as belonging to someone
  • synonym: attribute - to regard something as being caused by someone or something
  • antonym: exonerate - to absolve someone from blame for a fault or wrongdoing
