suturing

noun
joining tissues together surgically
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːtʃɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsjuːtʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
The surgical act of joining two surfaces, such as tissues, with stitches.

Examples

Surgical suturing technique
Advances in materials have made suturing safer and more effective in modern medicine.
Proper suturing is crucial to prevent infection and promote healing in surgical wounds.
The surgeon was skilled at suturing delicate tissues after the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suture - a stitch or row of stitches holding together the edges of a wound
