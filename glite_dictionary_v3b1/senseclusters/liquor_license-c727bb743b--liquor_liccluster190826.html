liquor license

noun
permit to sell alcoholic drinks
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkər ˌlaɪsəns /; / ˈlɪkər ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈlɪkə ˌlaɪsəns /; / ˈlɪkə ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
an official permit allowing the sale of alcoholic beverages

Examples

Apply for a liquor license
She applied for a liquor license to open her new wine shop.
The restaurant finally received its liquor license after months of waiting.
Without a liquor license, the bar was unable to serve alcoholic drinks.
Obtaining a liquor licence can be a long and expensive process.
The restaurant lost its liquor licence after repeated violations of the law.
You need a liquor licence to serve alcohol at your event.
