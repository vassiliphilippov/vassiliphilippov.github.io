jole

verb
to jerk or shake
Pronunciation: / dʒoʊl / (AmE), / dʒəʊl / (BrE)
to move or cause to move with a sudden jerk or shake, especially in a rough or uneven manner (dialectal)

Examples

To jole over bumps
The wagon would jole over every bump in the old country road.
As the carriage joled along the rocky path, passengers held tightly to their seats.
He warned that the rough trail might jole the supplies loose from the cart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jolt - to move with a sudden, rough movement
  • synonym: shake - to move quickly back and forth or up and down
  • antonym: steady - to make or become stable and not likely to move suddenly
