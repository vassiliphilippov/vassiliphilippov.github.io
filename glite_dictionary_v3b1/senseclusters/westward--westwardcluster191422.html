westward

adverb
toward the west
1.0 2.05/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwÉ›stwÉšd /; / ËˆwÉ›stwÉšdz / (AmE), / ËˆwÉ›stwÉ™d /; / ËˆwÉ›stwÉ™dz / (BrE)
toward the west; in the direction of the west.

Examples

Travel westward
Many birds migrate westward during the autumn season.
The clouds drifted westward as the wind picked up.
The pioneers moved westward in search of new land.
At sunset, the birds began flying westwards in a perfect V formation.
The river flows westwards before emptying into the bay.
We decided to drive westwards to explore the coastal towns.

Synonyms

  • west
    1.0 28/m
    west names the direction or point on a map and can mean toward the west, while westward emphasizes movement toward the west.
  • westbound
    2.8 0.31/m
    westbound describes things moving or scheduled to move toward the west, while westward simply means in that west direction.
  • westerly
    3.1 0.03/m
    westerly often means coming from or toward the west, especially for winds, while westward focuses only on movement toward the west.
