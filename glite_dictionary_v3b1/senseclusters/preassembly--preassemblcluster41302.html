preassembly

noun
initial assembly process
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːəˈsɛmbli / (AmE), / ˌpriːəˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
the process of putting parts together before the final assembly of a product or structure

Examples

Preassembly of parts
The preassembly of the frame made the final construction much faster.
Workers completed the preassembly of parts before moving them to the main assembly line.
By organizing a preassembly stage, the company reduced errors during final production.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: assembly - the process of putting parts together to make a finished product
  • synonym: preparation - the act of getting something ready for use or action
