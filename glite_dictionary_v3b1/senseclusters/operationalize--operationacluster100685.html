operationalize

verb
to define for measurement
7.7
Pronunciation: /ɑː.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ɒ.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to make an idea, plan, or concept practical, usable, or ready to be put into action, especially by defining it in clear, measurable terms or by implementing it

Examples

To operationalize a plan
Researchers must operationalize abstract ideas like happiness to collect reliable data.
The team decided to operationalize success by tracking customer satisfaction scores.
It is important to clearly operationalize each variable before starting the experiment.
The research team needed to operationalize their theory before starting the experiment.
It can be challenging to operationalize broad concepts in a real-world setting.
The manager explained how to operationalize the new procedures across all departments.
The research team will operationalize the new procedures in all departments next year.
It can be challenging to operationalize theoretical concepts in real-world situations.
After months of planning, they finally operationalized the project across the company.
