push

verb
press against forcefully without moving
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ / (AmE), / pʊʃ / (BrE)
to move something or someone away from yourself or in a particular direction by using force, especially with your hands or body

Examples

To push a door
Despite her efforts, Alice pushed on the boulder without moving it an inch.
He pushes against the wall every morning as part of his stretching routine.
She pushed against the heavy door, but it didn't budge.
He pushed with all his strength, but the door wouldn't budge.
She had to push her way through the busy hallway.
The crowd began to push forward as the doors opened.
He pushed the box under the bed to keep it out of sight.
She had to push the heavy door open with all her strength.
The children love to push each other on the swings at the playground.

  • antonym: pull - to apply force to move something towards oneself
  • synonym: shove - to push someone or something roughly
