use

verb
exploit for personal advantage
6.3 176/m
Pronunciation: / juÀêz / (AmE), / juÀêz / (BrE)
to exploit a person, relationship, or resource as a means to achieve one's own goal or advantage, sometimes unfairly

Examples

To use a friend
He tried to use his friendship with the manager to get a promotion.
She felt betrayed when she realized she was being used for her connections.
Some people will use others for personal gain without any guilt.

Synonyms

  • exploit
    5.6 13/m
    exploit treats someone or something unfairly to gain, while use can be neutral and simply means employing someone or something.
  • take advantage of
    2.5 22/m
    take advantage of means unfairly using someone's weakness or a situation to benefit yourself, while use can be neutral or general.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: abuse - to use something or someone improperly or unfairly, especially to one's own advantage
  • synonym: exploit - to make full use of and derive benefit from a resource or person
