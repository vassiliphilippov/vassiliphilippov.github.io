crosscutting

noun
editing technique
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɑːs.kʌt.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈkrɔːs.kʌt.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒs.kʌt.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
an editing technique in film, television, or literature that alternates between two or more scenes to show simultaneous action or create contrast

Examples

Crosscutting in film
The director used crosscutting to build suspense between the two storylines.
Effective crosscutting can make viewers feel like events are happening at the same time.
In the film, crosscutting between the hero and the villain increased the tension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: montage - a sequence of short shots edited together to condense space, time, or information
  • synonym: parallel editing - an editing technique showing two or more actions happening at the same time
