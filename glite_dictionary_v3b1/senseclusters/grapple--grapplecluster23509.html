grapple

verb
to struggle with difficulty
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈgræp.əl/ (AmE), /ˈgræp.əl/ (BrE)
to try hard to deal with or understand a difficult problem or challenge

Examples

To grapple with a problem
The government must grapple with rising unemployment and economic challenges.
As a new manager, he had to grapple with unexpected problems in the team.
Many students grapple with balancing their studies and part-time jobs.
She grappled with the complex instructions before finally understanding the process.
Many students grapple with balancing schoolwork and personal life.
The government has grappled with the issue of climate change for years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: avoid - to keep away from or not deal with something
  • synonym: cope - to deal successfully with a difficult situation
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
  • synonym: wrestle - to struggle or fight with something difficult
