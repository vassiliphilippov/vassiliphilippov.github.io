upland cotton

noun
species of cotton plant, widely cultivated
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌp.lənd ˈkɑː.tən / (AmE), / ˈʌp.lənd ˈkɒt.ən / (BrE)
A type of cotton plant native to tropical America, now widely grown in the United States, that produces short-staple cotton.

Examples

Upland cotton plant
Most American-grown cotton is upland cotton, valued for its versatility and high yield.
Scientists are developing new varieties of upland cotton that can resist drought conditions.
The field was planted with upland cotton to meet the demand from textile manufacturers.
