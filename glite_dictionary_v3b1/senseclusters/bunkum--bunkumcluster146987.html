bunkum

noun
nonsense or insincere talk
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌŋkəm / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋkəm / (BrE)
Language or statements that are nonsense, insincere, or empty, often meant to deceive or impress.

Examples

Empty bunkum talk
Don’t listen to that salesman—most of what he says is just bunkum.
His explanation turned out to be pure bunkum and convinced no one.
The politician promised change, but all we heard was the usual bunkum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drivel - silly or senseless talk
  • synonym: nonsense - language or ideas that are foolish or senseless
  • antonym: sense - language or reasoning that is logical or meaningful
