tump

verb
to pile roughly
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʌmp / (AmE), / tʌmp / (BrE)
to heap or dump something in a pile, especially in a rough or careless way (dialectal or regional use)

Examples

To tump leaves
They tumped the garden waste into a corner to rot over the summer.
After cleaning the attic, we tumped all the old boxes in the garage.
He tumps his dirty laundry in a heap by the door every evening.
