ultrasonics

noun
high-frequency sound study
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəˈsɑnɪks / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəˈsɒnɪks / (BrE)
the study or use of sound waves with frequencies higher than humans can hear, often used in medicine and technology

Examples

Ultrasonics in medicine
Many hospitals use ultrasonics to create images of internal organs for diagnosis.
The engineer specialized in ultrasonics to develop better cleaning devices for delicate equipment.
Research in ultrasonics has led to advances in both medicine and industry.
Engineers use ultrasonics to inspect metal parts for hidden flaws without damaging them.
Medical professionals rely on ultrasonics for non-invasive imaging techniques like ultrasound scans.
The laboratory specializes in research on ultrasonics and its industrial applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acoustics - the science of sound, including its production, transmission, and effects
  • antonym: infrasound - sound waves with frequencies below the range of human hearing
  • synonym: ultrasound - sound waves with frequencies above human hearing, often used in medical imaging
