prescriber

noun
person who writes prescriptions
Pronunciation: / prɪˈskraɪbər / (AmE), / prɪˈskraɪbə / (BrE)
a medical professional, such as a doctor or nurse, who is authorized to write prescriptions for medicine

Examples

Medical prescriber
The prescriber must review your medical history before giving you a new medication.
If you have any side effects, contact your prescriber immediately.
Only a licensed prescriber can authorize this type of drug.
Only a licensed prescriber can issue this medication to patients.
The hospital requires all prescribers to complete annual training on safe medication practices.
Pharmacists must verify the credentials of the prescriber before filling a prescription.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nurse practitioner - a nurse with advanced training who can prescribe medication
  • antonym: patient - a person receiving medical care
  • synonym: physician - a medical doctor who treats patients
