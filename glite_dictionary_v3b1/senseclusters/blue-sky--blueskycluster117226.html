blue-sky

adjective
speculative; not of immediate practical value
5.6
Pronunciation: / bluː skaɪ / (AmE), / bluː skaɪ / (BrE)
(of ideas, research, etc.) concerned with theoretical possibilities and not with immediate practical or commercial value.

Examples

Blue-sky ideas
Many great inventions began as blue-sky ideas that seemed impractical at first.
Some investors are reluctant to fund blue-sky ventures because of their high risk and uncertainty.
The lab focuses on blue-sky projects that might lead to breakthroughs years from now.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice as opposed to theory
  • synonym: speculative - based on conjecture rather than knowledge
