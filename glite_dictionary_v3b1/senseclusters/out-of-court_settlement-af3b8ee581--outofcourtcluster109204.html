out-of-court settlement

noun
settlement before a court decision
4.5 3.3
Pronunciation: /aʊt əv kɔːrt ˈset.əl.mənt/ /; / /aʊt əv kɑːrt ˈset.əl.mənt/ (AmE), /aʊt əv kɔːt ˈset.əl.mənt/ /; / /aʊt ə kɔːt ˈset.əl.mənt/ (BrE)
an agreement reached by parties to a dispute that resolves the matter before the court renders a final decision

Examples

An out-of-court settlement

An out-of-court settlement is often preferred to reduce legal expenses and public attention.
The company and the former employee reached an out-of-court settlement to avoid a lengthy trial.
