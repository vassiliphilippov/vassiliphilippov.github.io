digest

verb

Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈdʒest/ /; / /dɪ.ˈdʒest/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈdʒest/ /; / /dɪ.ˈdʒest/ (BrE)
to break down food or other substances in the body or by chemical means so they can be absorbed or used

Examples

To digest food
Certain foods digest more quickly than others in the body.
Protein usually digests within a few hours after consumption.
This medicine helps fats digest more efficiently in the intestine.
Acids are often used in laboratories to digest samples before analysis.

The compost heap digests organic waste through a combination of heat and moisture.

Snakes can digest animals much larger than their own heads.
Some people have trouble digesting dairy products.
