lobola

noun
bride price tradition
Pronunciation: / loʊˈboʊlə / (AmE), / ləʊˈbəʊlə / (BrE)
a traditional payment or practice in some African cultures where the groom's family pays a bride price to the bride's family as part of marriage customs

Examples

A lobola payment
He worked for several years to afford the lobola required by his fiancée's family.
The payment of lobola is an important part of many traditional wedding ceremonies.
Her family discussed the terms of lobola before agreeing to the marriage proposal.
The families gathered to negotiate the lobola before the wedding ceremony could take place.
Paying lobola is an important tradition in many Southern African communities.
He saved for years to afford the lobola required to marry his fiancée.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bride price - money or goods given to a bride's family by the groom's family
  • synonym: dowry - property or money brought by a bride to her husband on marriage
