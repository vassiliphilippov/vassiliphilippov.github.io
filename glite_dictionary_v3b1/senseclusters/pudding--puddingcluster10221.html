pudding

noun
soft, sweet dessert
4.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpʊ.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpʊ.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
a sweet, soft dessert, often thickened with flour or milk, and baked, boiled, steamed, or chilled

Examples

Chocolate pudding
British pudding is traditionally steamed rather than baked.
For dessert, she served chocolate pudding with whipped cream on top.
He made banana pudding from an old family recipe.
She made a bowl of chocolate pudding for the family to enjoy after dinner.
Vanilla pudding is a popular treat at birthday parties and school lunches.
He topped his homemade pudding with fresh strawberries and whipped cream.
