kick in the teeth

noun
severe disappointment or setback (idiom)
Pronunciation: / kɪk ɪn ðə tiθ / (AmE), / kɪk ɪn ðə tiːθ / (BrE)
an event or action that is a severe disappointment or setback

Examples

A kick in the teeth
Getting rejected after months of preparation was a real kick in the teeth.
Losing his scholarship was a kick in the teeth he hadn't expected.
When the company announced layoffs just before the holidays, it felt like a kick in the teeth for the staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disappointment - sadness or displeasure caused by the non-fulfillment of one's hopes or expectations
  • synonym: setback - an unanticipated or sudden check to progress or reversal of fortune
