Arminian Church

noun
Protestant denomination following Arminian theology
8.6
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ˈmɪn.ɪ.ən tʃɜːrtʃ/ (AmE), /ɑː.ˈmɪn.ɪ.ən tʃɜːtʃ/ (BrE)
A Protestant denomination that adheres to the theological views of Jacobus Arminius, emphasizing free will in salvation.

Examples

The Arminian Church beliefs
Debates between the Arminian Church and the Calvinist Church were common in seventeenth-century Europe.
He attended services at his local Arminian Church every Sunday morning.
The Arminian Church places great emphasis on human free will in matters of salvation.
