archerfish

noun
freshwater fish that shoots water at insects
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrtʃɚˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɑːtʃəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of Southeast Asia and Australia that catches insects by shooting jets of water to knock them into the water

Examples

The archerfish shoots water
At the aquarium, we watched a archerfish demonstrate its hunting skills by targeting a fly above the tank.
Scientists have studied how the archerfish judges distance before firing at its prey.
The archerfish is known for its remarkable ability to shoot down insects with streams of water.
