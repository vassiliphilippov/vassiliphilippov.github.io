slick

verb
make smooth and glossy
Pronunciation: / slɪk / (AmE), / slɪk / (BrE)
to make something smooth, shiny, or slippery, often by applying a substance

Examples

To slick hair
He carefully slicked his hair back before the meeting.
She slicks her ponytail down to keep flyaway hairs in place.
The chef slicked the dessert plate with a layer of chocolate sauce for a glossy finish.
He used gel to slick his hair back for the party.
She carefully slicked the countertop with oil to give it a shine.
The workers slicked the ice to make it perfect for skating.
