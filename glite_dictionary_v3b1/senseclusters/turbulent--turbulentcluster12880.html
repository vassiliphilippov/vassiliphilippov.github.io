turbulent

adjective
agitated or disturbed (of liquid/gas)
6.6 1.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…úrbj…ôl…ônt / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀêbj äl…ônt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or gas) moving in a violently irregular or agitated way; characterized by disturbance or turbulence.

Examples

Turbulent water
During the storm, the usually still lake became noticeably turbulent.
Strong winds created turbulent currents that made swimming dangerous.
The boat struggled to stay afloat in the turbulent waters of the river.

Synonyms

  • roiling
    7.6 0.02/m
    roiling is a violently agitated, bubbling motion in liquids, while turbulent is a more general term for irregular, disturbed flow.
  • churning
    4.9 0.00/m
    churning is a strong, swirling agitation that often looks like waves or foam, while turbulent is a broader chaotic, irregular motion.
  • agitated
    4.4 0.07/m
    agitated means physically shaken or set in motion by stirring or force, while turbulent implies violent, irregular disturbance in flow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not agitated; free from disturbance (of liquid/gas)
