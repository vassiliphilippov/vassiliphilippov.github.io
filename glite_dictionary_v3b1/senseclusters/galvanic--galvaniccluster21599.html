galvanic

adjective
producing electricity by chemical action
Pronunciation: / ɡælˈvænɪk / (AmE), / ɡælˈvanɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or producing electricity by chemical action, especially through the use of a galvanic cell.

Examples

Galvanic cell reaction
A galvanic cell converts chemical energy into electrical energy.
Engineers studied the galvanic corrosion that occurs when different metals are connected underwater.
The battery relies on a galvanic reaction to generate power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: voltaic - producing electric current by chemical means; used interchangeably with 'galvanic' in technical contexts
