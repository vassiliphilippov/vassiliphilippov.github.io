dibble

verb
make holes in soil with a tool
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪb.əl /; / ˈdɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈdɪb.əl /; / ˈdɪbəl / (BrE)
to make holes in the ground with a pointed tool, especially for planting seeds or bulbs

Examples

To dibble holes
Farmers often dibble rows to ensure seeds are evenly spaced in their fields.
In spring, the gardener would dibble small holes for the young seedlings.
She dibbled into the wet earth, preparing for this year’s tulip bulbs.
Gardeners often dibble small holes in the soil before setting the seeds.
It is best to dibble tulip bulbs several inches apart for optimal growth.
She carefully dibbled the young plants into the freshly prepared beds.
