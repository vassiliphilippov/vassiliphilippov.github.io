striped dogwood

noun
maple with striped bark and yellow autumn leaves
8.0 6.3
Pronunciation: / straɪpt ˈdɔɡˌwʊd / (AmE), / straɪpt ˈdɒɡˌwʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to eastern North America, noted for its striped bark and large, two-lobed leaves that turn bright yellow in autumn.

Examples

The striped dogwood tree
Gardeners often plant striped dogwood for its attractive appearance throughout the year.
In the fall, the leaves of the striped dogwood turn a brilliant yellow.
The striped dogwood is easily recognized by its distinctive green-and-white bark.
