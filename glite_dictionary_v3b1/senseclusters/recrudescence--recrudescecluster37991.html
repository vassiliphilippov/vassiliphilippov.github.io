recrudescence

noun
return of something after abatement
9.9 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀåkruÀêÀàd…õs…ôns / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêkruÀêÀàd…õs…ôns / (BrE)
the recurrence of an undesirable condition, disease, or problem after a period of improvement or inactivity

Examples

A recrudescence of infection
Doctors fear a recrudescence of the infection if the medication is stopped too soon.
The athlete's injury showed sudden recrudescence after weeks of apparent recovery.
There has been a worrying recrudescence of violence in the region.

Synonyms

  • reoccurrence
    6.2 0.03/m
    reoccurrence is the return of a disease or symptom after remission, while recrudescence describes a problem coming back after a quiet period.
  • relapse
    6.5 2.13/m
    relapse is a return to a worse state often used for health or behavior, while recrudescence means the problem returns after improvement.
  • recurrence
    5.0 3.49/m
    recurrence is something happening again, often at regular times, while recrudescence implies an unwanted condition returning after inactivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relapse - a return of a disease or condition after improvement
  • antonym: remission - a period during which symptoms of a disease are reduced
  • synonym: resurgence - an increase or revival after a period of little activity
