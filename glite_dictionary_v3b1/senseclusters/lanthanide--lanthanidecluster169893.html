lanthanide

noun
element of the lanthanide series
Pronunciation: / ˈlænθəˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlanθənʌɪd / (BrE)
any of the series of fifteen metallic elements from lanthanum (atomic number 57) to lutetium (atomic number 71) in the periodic table, known for similar properties.

Examples

A lanthanide element
Cerium is one example of a lanthanide used in catalytic converters.
Scientists are studying the properties of each lanthanide to develop new materials.
The lanthanide series is essential for many modern technologies, including magnets and lasers.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: actinide - any element in the actinide series (atomic numbers 89 through 103)
  • synonym: rare earth element - any of a group of metallic elements including the lanthanides
