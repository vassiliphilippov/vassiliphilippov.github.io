custody

noun
detention by police or authorities

the state of being kept in detention or confinement by the police or authorities

Examples

Police custody
After his arrest, he was taken into police custody.
She was released from custody after posting bail.
The suspect remains in custody while the investigation continues.
After several hours in custody, she was released without charge.
The police held him in custody while they conducted their investigation.
The suspect was taken into custody shortly after the incident.

Synonyms

  • detention
    3.9 10/m
    detention is being held by police, often briefly while officers decide what to do, while custody is the state of such confinement.
  • detainment
    6.7 0.17/m
    detainment is the act of holding someone for a short time by authorities, while custody is the formal state of being kept by police.
  • confinement
    5.9 3.97/m
    confinement is being kept in a limited space, often physical or private, while custody is official detention by police or authorities.
  • incarceration
    8.0 4.56/m
    incarceration is being put in prison as punishment, while custody is being held by police and may be shorter or before trial.
  • imprisonment
    4.0 6.19/m
    imprisonment is serving time in prison as a legal punishment, while custody is being kept in police detention or short confinement.
  • captivity
    3.7 4.84/m
    captivity is being held against your will, often by enemies or animals, while custody is formal detention by authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrest - the act of seizing someone by legal authority
  • synonym: detention - the act of detaining someone, especially by the authorities
  • antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from custody or detention
