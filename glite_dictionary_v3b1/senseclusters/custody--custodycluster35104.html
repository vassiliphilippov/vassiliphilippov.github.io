custody

noun
detention by police or authorities
4.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌ.stə.diː/ /; / /ˈkʌ.stə.dɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkʌ.stə.diː/ /; / /ˈkʌ.stə.dɪ/ (BrE)
the state of being kept in detention or confinement by the police or authorities

Examples

Police custody
After his arrest, he was taken into police custody.
She was released from custody after posting bail.
The suspect remains in custody while the investigation continues.
After several hours in custody, she was released without charge.
The police held him in custody while they conducted their investigation.
The suspect was taken into custody shortly after the incident.
