bilinguality

noun
use of two languages
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪlɪŋˈɡwæləti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪlɪŋˈɡwælɪti / (BrE)
the condition of regularly using two languages within a community, institution, or system

Examples

Bilinguality in schools
The country's bilinguality is evident in its schools, where both languages are taught equally.
Many government services reflect the bilinguality of the region by offering information in two languages.
The museum's exhibits highlight the bilinguality of the local population throughout history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bilingualism - the ability to speak two languages fluently
  • antonym: monolingualism - the use or knowledge of only one language
