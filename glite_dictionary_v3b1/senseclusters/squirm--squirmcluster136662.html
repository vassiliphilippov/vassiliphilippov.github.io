squirm

noun
the act of wriggling or twisting
5.2
Pronunciation: / skwɝm / (AmE), / skwɜːm / (BrE)
a twisting or wriggling movement, especially as a result of discomfort or nervousness

Examples

A nervous squirm
A visible squirm of discomfort crossed her face as she awaited the results.
The child's squirm in his seat made it difficult for the teacher to focus.
With every squirm, the trapped fish tried desperately to escape the net.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stillness - absence of movement
  • synonym: wriggle - to twist and turn with quick movements
