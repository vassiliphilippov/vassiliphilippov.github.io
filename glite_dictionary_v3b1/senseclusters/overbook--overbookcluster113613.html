overbook

verb
to exceed booking limit
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbʊk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbʊk / (BrE)
to accept or sell more reservations, bookings, or tickets than there is space or capacity available

Examples

To overbook a hotel
The hotel accidentally overbooked and had to find rooms for guests elsewhere.
Airlines sometimes overbook flights to ensure all seats are filled.
We were bumped from our reservation because the restaurant had overbooked for the evening.
The airline overbooked the flight, so several passengers were left without seats.
If you overbook your calendar, you may end up missing important meetings.
Hotels sometimes overbook rooms, expecting that some guests will not show up.
The hotel had to turn away guests because they had overbooked the rooms for the weekend.
Airlines sometimes overbook flights to make up for no-show passengers.
If you overbook your schedule, you might not have time to rest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: double-book - to schedule two reservations for the same time or space
  • degree lower: reserve - to book or arrange in advance
  • antonym: underbook - to accept fewer reservations than capacity
