(Physics) The symmetry of physical laws under spatial inversion, where the laws remain unchanged if spatial coordinates are reversed, as in right-handed and left-handed systems.

Examples

Space-reflection symmetry in physics
Many particle interactions display space-reflection symmetry, meaning the process looks identical in a mirror.
Physicists once believed that space-reflection symmetry was always preserved in nature, until its violation was discovered in weak interactions.
The concept of space-reflection symmetry is fundamental in understanding certain conservation laws in physics.
