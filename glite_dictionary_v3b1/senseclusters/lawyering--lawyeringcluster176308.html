lawyering

noun
use of legal tactics
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːjərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɔːjərɪŋ / (BrE)
the work, practice, or skill of being a lawyer, including using legal tactics and arguments

Examples

Expert lawyering
Her expert lawyering helped the company avoid a costly lawsuit.
The negotiations involved a lot of strategic lawyering on both sides.
He was known for his aggressive lawyering in high-profile court cases.
She found lawyering to be both challenging and rewarding.
After years of lawyering, he decided to teach at a law school.
Modern lawyering often involves both courtroom work and advising clients outside of court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legal practice - the professional work of lawyers
  • synonym: litigation - the process of taking legal action
