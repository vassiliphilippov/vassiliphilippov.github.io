French Indochina

proper noun
former French colonial territories in Southeast Asia
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ ˌɪndoʊˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˌɪndəʊˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
the group of French colonial territories in Southeast Asia, comprising present-day Cambodia, Laos, and Vietnam, existing from the late 19th century until 1954

Examples

French Indochina colonial territory
During the first half of the 20th century, French Indochina was a key outpost of French colonialism in Asia.
Ho Chi Minh was a prominent leader in the movement for independence from French Indochina.
Many of the modern borders in Southeast Asia have their origins in the administration of French Indochina.
