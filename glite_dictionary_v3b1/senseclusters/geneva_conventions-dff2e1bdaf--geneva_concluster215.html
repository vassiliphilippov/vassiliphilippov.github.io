Geneva Conventions

noun
international treaties on wartime humanitarian conduct
4.2
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnivə kənˈvɛnʃənz / (AmE), / dʒəˈniːvə kənˈvɛnʃənz / (BrE)
a series of international treaties that establish standards for humanitarian treatment during war, especially relating to the protection of wounded soldiers, prisoners of war, and civilians

Examples

The Geneva Conventions treaties
Many countries are signatories to the Geneva Conventions, which regulate the treatment of prisoners of war.
Medical personnel are protected under the Geneva Conventions during armed conflicts.
The court ruled that the actions constituted a violation of the Geneva Conventions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hague Conventions - series of international treaties focusing on laws of war and war crimes
  • synonym: international law - the set of rules generally accepted as binding in relations between states
