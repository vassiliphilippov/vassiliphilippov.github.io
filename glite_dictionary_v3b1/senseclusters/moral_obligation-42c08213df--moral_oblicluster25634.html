moral obligation

noun
a duty based on ethical principles
2.4 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔːr.əl ə.blɪ.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈmɑːr.əl ɑː.blə.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈmɒr.əl ɒ.blɪ.ˈɡeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a duty or responsibility that arises from considerations of what is right and wrong, rather than law or custom

Examples

A moral obligation to help
He questioned whether he had a moral obligation to help his neighbor in need.
Many people donate to charity out of a sense of moral obligation.
She felt a moral obligation to speak out against injustice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duty - something that one is required to do by moral or legal obligation
  • antonym: legal obligation - a duty imposed by law
