benzoic acid

noun
white crystalline chemical compound
Pronunciation: / bɛnˈzoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / bɛnˈzəʊɪk ˈasid / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound found in various resins and used as a food preservative and in the manufacture of other chemicals

Examples

Benzoic acid crystals
Certain fruits naturally contain small amounts of benzoic acid.
Sodium benzoic acid is commonly used as a food preservative.
The laboratory synthesized pure benzoic acid from toluene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: salicylic acid - a related aromatic acid used in food and pharmaceuticals
