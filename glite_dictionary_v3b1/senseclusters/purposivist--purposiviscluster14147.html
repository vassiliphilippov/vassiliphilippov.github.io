purposivist

adjective
focused on purpose
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɜːr.pə.ˈsɪ.vɪst/ /; / /pɜːr.pə.ˈsaɪ.vɪst/ (AmE), /pɜː.pə.ˈsɪ.vɪst/ /; / /pɜː.pə.ˈsaɪ.vɪst/ (BrE)
relating to or supporting the interpretation of actions, laws, or texts based on their intended purpose or goals

Examples

Purposivist approach
The judge used a purposivist method to interpret the new law.
A purposivist reading of the constitution considers the goals behind each article.
Many scholars debate whether a purposivist or literal approach is better for legal analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literalist - interpreting texts or laws strictly by their exact wording
  • synonym: teleological - explaining things by their purpose or goal
