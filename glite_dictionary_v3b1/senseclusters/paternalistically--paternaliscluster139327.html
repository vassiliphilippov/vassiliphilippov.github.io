paternalistically

adverb
in a fatherly way
Pronunciation: / pəˌtɝːnəˈlɪstɪkli / (AmE), / pəˌtɜːnəˈlɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that shows care or concern like a parent, but also with authority or by making decisions for others, sometimes limiting their freedom

Examples

To speak paternalistically
The manager spoke paternalistically to his team, offering advice as if they were his children.
She acted paternalistically toward her younger colleagues, always checking if they needed help.
The government sometimes intervenes paternalistically in citizens' lives, claiming it is for their own good.
The teacher spoke paternalistically to the students, making decisions without asking for their opinions.
He managed the team paternalistically, believing he always knew what was best for everyone.
Policies were implemented paternalistically, limiting choices while claiming to protect the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritatively - in a commanding or controlling manner
  • synonym: condescendingly - in a way that shows a feeling of superiority
  • synonym: protectively - in a way that protects or shields someone
