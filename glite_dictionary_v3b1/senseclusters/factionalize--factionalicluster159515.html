factionalize

verb
to create factions
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfæk.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈfæk.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to cause a group or organization to split into smaller, often opposing, groups or factions

Examples

To factionalize a group
The controversial policy began to factionalize the organization, making cooperation much more difficult.
Leaders worried that the heated debate would factionalize the community and weaken their shared goals.
Attempts to factionalize the team failed, and they remained united despite the challenges.
The organization began to factionalize after disagreements over its future direction.
If the team continues to factionalize, it will be difficult to achieve any common goals.
Leaders tried to prevent the group from becoming factionalized by encouraging open communication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • antonym: unify - to bring together into a single group
