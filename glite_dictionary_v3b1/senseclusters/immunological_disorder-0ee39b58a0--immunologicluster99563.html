immunological disorder

noun
disorder affecting the immune system
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˈlɑdʒɪkəl dɪsˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˈlɒdʒɪkəl dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A disease or condition that affects the functioning of the immune system.

Examples

An immunological disorder disease
Advances in research have improved our understanding of how certain viruses can trigger an immunological disorder.
Rheumatoid arthritis is considered an immunological disorder because it involves the body's immune system attacking its own tissues.
The patient was diagnosed with an immunological disorder after experiencing recurring infections and unexplained symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autoimmune disease - a condition in which the immune system attacks the body's own tissues
