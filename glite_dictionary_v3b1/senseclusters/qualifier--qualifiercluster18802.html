qualifier

noun
word or phrase that limits meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑl.əˌfaɪ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒl.ɪ.faɪ.ə / (BrE)
a word or phrase, especially an adjective or adverb, that restricts or modifies the meaning of another word.

Examples

A qualifier word
Adjectives often serve as qualifiers for nouns in English grammar.
In the phrase "reasonably fast car", the word "reasonably" is a qualifier of the adjective.
The report contained several qualifiers that limited the findings' applicability.
