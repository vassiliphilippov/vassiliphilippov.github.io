subordinationist

noun
believer in hierarchy
9.9
Pronunciation: /sə.bɔːr.də.ˈneɪ.ɪst/ /; / /sʌb.ɔːr.də.ˈneɪ.ɪst/ (AmE), /sə.bɔː.də.ˈneɪ.ɪst/ /; / /sʌb.ɔː.də.ˈneɪ.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes in or supports the idea that one group or thing should be ranked below or made subordinate to another (in religion, society, or politics)

Examples

A subordinationist thinker
The subordinationist argued that certain roles should always be ranked below others in the organization.
In the theological debate, a subordinationist view was presented regarding the relationship within the Trinity.
Some critics challenged the subordinationist perspective as outdated and incompatible with modern values.
The politician was criticized as a subordinationist for opposing equal rights legislation.
Many activists work to challenge the views of subordinationists in society.
She refused to support any subordinationist policies that limited opportunities for women.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarian - a person who believes in equality for all people
  • synonym: hierarchist - a person who supports a system of ranking or hierarchy
  • synonym: supremacist - a person who believes one group is superior to others
