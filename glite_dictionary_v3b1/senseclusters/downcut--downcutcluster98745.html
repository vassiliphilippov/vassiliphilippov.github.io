downcut

verb
to cut downward
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (BrE)
to cut downward into a material using a tool, often to create a clean top surface or precise edge

Examples

To downcut wood
The machine was programmed to downcut the metal sheet for a smooth finish.
Carefully, he downcut the plastic panel to fit the new component.
To install the lock, you need to downcut a groove along the door edge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incise - to cut into a surface with a sharp tool
