polyol

noun
sugar alcohol compound
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈpɒliˌɒl / (BrE)
a chemical compound with multiple hydroxyl groups, used in food as a sugar substitute and in industry to make products like foams and adhesives

Examples

Many sugar substitutes are made from a type of polyol to provide sweetness without extra calories.
