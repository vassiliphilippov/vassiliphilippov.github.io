astute

adjective
shrewd; clever at assessing situations
6.9 3.11/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàstuÀêt / (AmE), / …ôÀàstjuÀêt / (BrE)
having or showing a keen ability to accurately assess situations and turn this to one's advantage; shrewd and perceptive.

Examples

An astute analysis
Her astute analysis of the situation impressed her colleagues.
Only an astute investor would spot such an opportunity in the market.
The manager made an astute observation that helped save the company money.

Synonyms

  • shrewd
    4.7 3.39/m
    shrewd is practical and clever at judging people or deals, while astute quickly sees chances and uses them to advantage.
  • perspicacious
    9.8 0.18/m
    perspicacious notices hidden facts and patterns quickly, while astute also acts on those insights to gain advantage.
  • perceptive
    5.5 2.25/m
    perceptive reads small signs of feeling or danger, while astute uses those signs to judge and profit.
  • percipient
    9.2 0.04/m
    percipient understands things quickly from small clues, while astute turns such quick understanding into clear practical advantage.
  • discerning
    6.7 1.61/m
    discerning spots subtle quality or truth in people or art, while astute focuses more on judging situations for gain.
  • sagacious
    8.6 0.20/m
    sagacious offers wise long-term judgment and calm advice, while astute often acts quickly to exploit a situation.
  • keen
    2.8 3.69/m
    keen notices fine details and small differences, while astute goes beyond noticing to judge and use situations cleverly.
  • sharp
    3.3 7.50/m
    sharp thinks very quickly and notices small details, but astute combines quick noticing with clever practical judgment to profit.
