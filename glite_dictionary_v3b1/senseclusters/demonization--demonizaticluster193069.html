demonization

noun
portraying as evil or wicked
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːmənəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌdiːməˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌdiːmənaɪˈzeɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
the act of portraying someone or something as wicked or evil, often unjustly or for propaganda purposes

The demonisation of rival politicians is a common feature of election campaigns.
The historian examined the demonisation of witches in seventeenth-century Europe.
