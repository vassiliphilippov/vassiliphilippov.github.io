senile psychosis

noun
dementia in the aged due to brain degeneration
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː.naɪl saɪˈkoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈsiː.naɪl saɪˈkəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A type of dementia occurring in old age, caused by degeneration of the brain that is not linked to cerebrovascular disease.

Examples

Senile psychosis symptoms
Family members were concerned about the behavioral changes linked to his senile psychosis.
Research on senile psychosis has led to better understanding of age-related cognitive decline.
The patient was diagnosed with senile psychosis after showing progressive memory loss and confusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dementia - chronic or persistent disorder of mental processes marked by memory disorders, personality changes, and impaired reasoning
