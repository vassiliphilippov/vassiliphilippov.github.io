charisma

noun
compelling charm or influence
3.1 4.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈɹɪzmə / (AmE), / kəˈrɪzmə / (BrE)
A compelling attractiveness or charm that can inspire devotion in others or enable someone to influence people effectively.

Examples

Strong charisma
Despite lacking experience, his charisma won over everyone at the event.
Her charisma made her an effective leader who inspired confidence in her team.
The politician's charisma was evident as the crowd cheered loudly during his speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charm - quality that attracts or delights others
  • synonym: magnetism - power to attract people, charm
