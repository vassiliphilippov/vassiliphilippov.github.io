jip the line

verb
to unfairly cut ahead in a line
Pronunciation: / dʒɪp ðə laɪn / (AmE), / dʒɪp ðə laɪn / (BrE)
to unfairly move ahead of others waiting in line; to cut in line or cheat by not waiting your turn

Examples

To jip the line unfairly
Anyone who tries to jip the line will be asked to move to the back.
Please don't jip the line; everyone here has been waiting patiently.
Some kids attempted to jip the line at the ticket counter, but they were caught.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut the line - to move ahead of others waiting in line; to skip ahead
  • antonym: wait your turn - to remain in place in a queue until it is your turn
