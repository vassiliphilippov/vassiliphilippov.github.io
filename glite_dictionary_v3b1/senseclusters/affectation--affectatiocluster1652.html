affectation

noun
artificial or pretentious behavior
Pronunciation: / ˌæfɛkˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌafɛkˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
behavior, speech, or manner that is artificial and designed to impress others

Examples

An affectation of speech
His British accent was merely an affectation he put on to impress his friends.
She spoke with an affectation that made her sound insincere.
The lavish decorations were more of an affectation than a true reflection of her taste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
