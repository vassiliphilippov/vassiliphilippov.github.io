proximally

adverb
closer in position
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑksɪməli / (AmE), / ˈprɒksɪməli / (BrE)
in or toward a position that is closer to a specified place, object, or point in time.

Examples

Proximally to the road
The school is located proximally to the main road, making it easy to find.
The new apartment building was constructed proximally to the city center for convenience.
The event was scheduled proximally to the holiday to allow more people to attend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distally - in or toward a position farther from a specified point
  • synonym: nearby - close in distance; not far away
