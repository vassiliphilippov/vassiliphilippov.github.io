foreflipper

noun
front swimming limb
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌflɪpər / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌflɪpə / (BrE)
the front limb of a marine animal, such as a seal or whale, adapted for swimming

Examples

The foreflipper of seal
The sea lion used its foreflipper to push itself onto the rock.
Researchers measured the length of the whale's foreflipper for their study.
A seal's foreflipper helps it steer quickly through the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flipper - a broad, flat limb used for swimming by marine animals
  • antonym: hindflipper - the rear limb of a marine animal adapted for swimming
