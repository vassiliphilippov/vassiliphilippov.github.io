tranquil

adjective
calm, undisturbed (used of water)
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtræŋ.kwəl /; / ˈtræŋ.kwɪl / (AmE), / ˈtræŋ.kwɪl / (BrE)
calm and peaceful; free from disturbance or agitation (can describe people, places, situations, or water)

Examples

A tranquil place
In the early morning, the river looked tranquil, like a mirror reflecting the pale pink sky.
The lake remained tranquil despite the storm clouds gathering above.
They enjoyed a boat ride across the tranquil waters of the bay at sunset.
After meditation, he felt completely tranquil and refreshed.
She spoke in a tranquil tone, soothing everyone in the room.
The lake looked particularly tranquil in the early morning light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous
  • synonym: calm - (of water) free from roughness or agitation
  • antonym: rough - (of water) agitated or disturbed by waves
  • synonym: serene - calm, peaceful, and untroubled
