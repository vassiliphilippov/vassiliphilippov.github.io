respirable

adjective
suitable for breathing
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspaɪrəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪərəbl / (BrE)
able to be breathed in, especially referring to air or particles small enough to enter the lungs

Examples

Respirable dust particles
The factory installed filters to reduce respirable dust in the air.
Miners are at risk of lung disease from exposure to respirable particles.
Proper ventilation ensures that only respirable air circulates in the laboratory.
The factory installed filters to reduce respirable dust in the air for worker safety.
Scientists measured the concentration of respirable particles in the urban environment.
Wearing a mask can help protect against respirable fibers released during construction work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inhalable - able to be breathed in through the nose or mouth
  • antonym: nonrespirable - too large to be breathed into the lungs
  • antonym: toxic - harmful or poisonous when breathed in
