prim

verb
contract or purse the lips
Pronunciation: / prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
to act, dress, or appear in a very formal, proper, or affected way

Examples

To prim your lips
He primmed his mouth in disapproval at the suggestion.
She primmed her lips before giving her answer.
The child primmed her lips, trying not to giggle during the ceremony.
He likes to prim whenever important guests arrive at dinner.
She couldn't help but prim in front of the strict headmistress.
The children would prim and giggle, imitating their teachers' manners.
He primed for the ceremony, fussing over every detail of his outfit.
She always prims herself before an important meeting, straightening her dress and smoothing her hair.
They spent an hour priming before joining their distinguished guests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: posture - to assume a particular pose or stance, often to impress others
  • synonym: preen - to tidy and arrange one's appearance in a self-satisfied manner
  • antonym: relax - to become less formal or strict in manner or dress
