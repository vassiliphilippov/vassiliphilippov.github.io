conciliate

verb
bring to agreement; make peace
6.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɪliˌeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪlieɪt / (BrE)
to stop people from being angry or hostile, or to bring people or groups to agreement

Examples

To conciliate angry people
Efforts to conciliate the two rival teams proved successful in the end.
Her sincere apology helped to conciliate the offended parties.
The company tried to conciliate the workers after the strike by offering better benefits.
He hoped to conciliate the opposing sides in the negotiation.
Her efforts to conciliate the board members were ultimately successful.
The manager tried to conciliate staff concerns by offering flexible work hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravate - to make someone more angry or hostile
  • synonym: appease - to calm or pacify someone, especially by giving in to their demands
  • synonym: mollify - to calm or soothe someone, especially someone who is angry
  • antonym: provoke - to make someone angry or hostile
  • synonym: reconcile - to restore friendly relations between
