Carolina buckthorn

noun
deciduous shrub with black fruit, native to North America
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˌraɪlɪnə ˈbʌkˌθɔrn / (AmE), / kəˌraɪlɪnə ˈbʌkˌθɔːn / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of eastern and central North America, with black berrylike fruit and golden-yellow leaves in autumn.

Examples

The Carolina buckthorn shrub
Many birds feed on the fruit of the Carolina buckthorn during late summer.
The Carolina buckthorn is prized for its golden-yellow autumn foliage and attractive berries.
We planted a Carolina buckthorn in our backyard to attract wildlife.
