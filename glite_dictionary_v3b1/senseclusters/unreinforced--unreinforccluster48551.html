unreinforced

adjective
without extra support
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌriːɪnˈfɔrst / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌriːɪnˈfɔːst / (BrE)
not made stronger or more secure by adding extra support or material

Examples

An unreinforced bridge
The old bridge was unreinforced and could not withstand the heavy trucks.
Engineers warned that unreinforced concrete walls are at risk during earthquakes.
Many historic buildings remain unreinforced, making them vulnerable to damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reinforced - made stronger by adding extra support or material
  • synonym: unsupported - not having support or reinforcement
