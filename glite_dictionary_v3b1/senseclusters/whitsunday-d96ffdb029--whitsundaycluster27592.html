Whitsunday

noun
seventh Sunday after Easter (Pentecost); Scottish quarter day
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtˌsʌn.deɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.sʌn.deɪ / (BrE)
The seventh Sunday after Easter, also known as Pentecost, commemorating the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the Apostles; in Scotland, a traditional quarter day for hiring and rent payments.

Examples

Whitsunday after Easter
Whitsunday is often associated with processions and the wearing of white garments in some Christian communities.
Every year, the church holds a special service on Whitsunday to celebrate Pentecost.
Traditionally, Scottish leases and contracts were renewed on Whitsunday each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pentecost - Christian festival commemorating the Holy Spirit's descent on the Apostles
