activation energy

noun
minimum energy needed for a process to occur
7.7 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.təˈveɪ.ʃən ˌɛn.ɚ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˌæk.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən ˌɛn.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
the minimum amount of energy that particles must have in order to undergo a specific chemical or physical process, such as a reaction or emission

Examples

Activation energy of a reaction
Enzymes lower the activation energy required for many biochemical reactions.
Heating the mixture provided enough activation energy for the reaction to take place.
The activation energy of the process determines how rapidly the reaction occurs at a given temperature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction by reducing the activation energy
