impulsive

adjective
acting without forethought
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpʌlsɪv / (BrE)
acting or done suddenly, without careful thought or planning, usually because of a sudden desire or emotion

Examples

An impulsive action
An impulsive remark during the meeting caught everyone by surprise.
His impulsive buying habits often led to regret after shopping trips.
She made an impulsive decision to quit her job and travel the world.
His impulsive actions sometimes get him into trouble.
It was an impulsive decision to buy the tickets at the last minute.
She gave him an impulsive hug when she heard the good news.
Buying that expensive dress was quite impulsive of you.
His impulsive behavior often gets him into trouble.
She made an impulsive decision to quit her job and travel the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capricious - given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior
  • antonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • synonym: impetuous - acting quickly and without thought; rash
  • synonym: spontaneous - performed without premeditation or external stimulus
  • antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration or attention
  • synonym: whimsical - playfully quaint or fanciful, especially in an appealing and amusing way
