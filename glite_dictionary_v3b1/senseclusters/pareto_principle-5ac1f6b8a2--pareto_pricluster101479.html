Pareto principle

noun
80/20 rule concept
5.2
Pronunciation: / pəˈreɪtoʊ ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / pəˈreɪtəʊ ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
the idea that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of the causes, often used to prioritize efforts

Examples

The Pareto principle rule
The Pareto principle suggests that focusing on key tasks can lead to most of your productivity gains.
Many businesses use the Pareto principle to identify which customers generate the most revenue.
By applying the Pareto principle, she realized that a few habits were responsible for most of her progress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: 80/20 rule - the rule that 80% of results come from 20% of causes
  • synonym: law of the vital few - the principle that a small number of causes have a large impact
