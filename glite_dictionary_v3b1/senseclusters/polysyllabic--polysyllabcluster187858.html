polysyllabic

adjective
having many syllables
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːlɪsɪˈlæbɪk /; / ˌpɑːliˌsɪˈlæbɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪsɪˈlæbɪk / (BrE)
(of a word) having more than one syllable; also used to describe speech or writing that uses long or complex words

Examples

Her writing style is dense and polysyllabic, making it difficult to read quickly.
Many scientific articles are filled with polysyllabic jargon.

The teacher asked the students to find a polysyllabic word in the paragraph.
Children often struggle with spelling polysyllabic words correctly.
In poetry, using polysyllabic words can affect the rhythm of a line.
