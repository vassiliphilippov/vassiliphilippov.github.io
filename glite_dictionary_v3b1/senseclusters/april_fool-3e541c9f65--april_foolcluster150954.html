April fool

noun
person tricked on April Fool's Day
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪprəl fuːl / (AmE), / ˈeɪprəl fuːl /; / ˈeɪprɪl fuːl / (BrE)
a person who is the victim of a joke or trick played on April 1st (April Fool's Day)

Examples

An April fool victim
Don't believe everything you hear today, or you might be the next April fool.
Every year, someone at the office ends up being the April fool.
He put salt in my coffee and called me an April fool.
Every year, someone ends up as the April fool thanks to my brother’s clever jokes.
He felt embarrassed when he realized he was the April fool in their office prank.
She wore a silly hat all morning before discovering she was the April fool.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - a person who is easily deceived or tricked
  • antonym: trickster - a person who cheats or deceives people
