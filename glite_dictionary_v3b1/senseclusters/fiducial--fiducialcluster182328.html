fiducial

adjective
relating to a trust; held in trust
Pronunciation: / fɪˈduːʃəl / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəl / (BrE)
relating to or of the nature of a trust, especially in a legal or financial context, where something is held or managed for the benefit of another person.

Examples

Fiducial duty
All contracts involving fiducial duties must be carefully supervised by a lawyer.
He acted in a fiducial capacity while managing the estate.
The court examined whether the trustee fulfilled their fiducial responsibilities properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiduciary - relating to trust, especially in a legal or financial relationship
