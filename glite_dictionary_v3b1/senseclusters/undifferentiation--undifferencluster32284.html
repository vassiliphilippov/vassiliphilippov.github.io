undifferentiation

noun
lack of distinction
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪˌfɛrənʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˌfɛrənʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of not being divided into different parts or types; lack of distinction or specialization

Examples

Complete undifferentiation of cells
In the earliest stages of development, the embryo shows complete undifferentiation of its cells.
The undifferentiation of roles in the team led to confusion about responsibilities.
Some diseases are marked by the undifferentiation of tissues that should be specialized.

Related Terms

  • antonym: differentiation - the process of becoming different or specialized
  • synonym: uniformity - the quality of being the same or consistent throughout
