psychodrama

noun
psychological conflict drama
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkoʊˌdrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəʊˌdrɑːmə / (BrE)
a play, film, or situation that explores psychological conflicts or emotional struggles of the characters

Examples

Psychodrama movie
The director's latest film is a powerful psychodrama that delves into the main character's childhood trauma.
She enjoys reading psychodramas that focus on complex family relationships and emotional struggles.
The play was a gripping psychodrama about betrayal and forgiveness within a close-knit community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melodrama - a dramatic work that exaggerates emotion and emphasizes plot over character
  • synonym: tragedy - a serious play or story with a sad or disastrous ending
