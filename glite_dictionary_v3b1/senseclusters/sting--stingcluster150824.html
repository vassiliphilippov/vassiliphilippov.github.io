sting

verb
cause a sharp, burning pain
4.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /stɪŋ/ (AmE), /stɪŋ/ (BrE)
a sharp, sudden pain or injury, especially from something like an insect, plant, or irritant; or to cause such pain

Examples

To sting the skin
Her eyes stung from the smoke filling the room.
The salt water began to sting the cut on my finger.
When I accidentally touched the nettles, my hand started to sting.
Her criticism really stung him, even though she didn't mean to hurt his feelings.
The cold wind seemed to sting his cheeks as he walked outside.
The soap can sting if it gets into your eyes.
Be careful, the jellyfish can sting if you get too close.
He was stung on the hand by a wasp while gardening.
Several mosquitoes stung me last night while I slept.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bite - to cut or press with the teeth (as by an animal or insect)
  • synonym: burn - to cause injury or pain with heat or similar sensation
  • synonym: prick - to puncture or cause a small sharp pain
  • antonym: soothe - to ease pain or discomfort
