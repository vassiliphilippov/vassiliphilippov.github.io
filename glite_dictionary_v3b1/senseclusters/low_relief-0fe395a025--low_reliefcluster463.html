low relief

noun
sculptural relief with slight projection
5.7 6.2
Pronunciation: /loʊ rɪ.ˈliːf/ (AmE), /ləʊ rɪ.ˈliːf/ (BrE)
A style of sculptural relief in which the forms project only slightly from the background and no parts are fully detached.

Examples

A low relief sculpture
A low relief sculpture requires careful control of depth to keep the figures close to the background.
She prefers working in low relief because of its subtle, understated effect.
The ancient temple walls are decorated with low relief carvings of animals and gods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bas-relief - sculptural relief with slightly raised forms; synonymous term
  • antonym: high relief - sculptural relief where forms are deeply cut and project far from the background
