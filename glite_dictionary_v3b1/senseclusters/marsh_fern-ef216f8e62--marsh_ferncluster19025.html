marsh fern

noun
fern found in wetlands
Pronunciation: / mɑrʃ fɝn / (AmE), / mɑːʃ fɜːn / (BrE)
a fern with deeply divided fronds, commonly found in wet habitats in temperate regions

Examples

A marsh fern plant
Gardeners often plant marsh fern in shaded, moist gardens to mimic its natural habitat.
In damp woodland areas, the marsh fern thrives alongside mosses and sedges.
We found several marsh ferns growing at the edge of the pond during our nature walk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bracken - coarse fern with large triangular fronds
