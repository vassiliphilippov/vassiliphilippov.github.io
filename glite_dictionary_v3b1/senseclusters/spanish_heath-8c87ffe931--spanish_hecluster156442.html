Spanish heath

noun
a flowering shrub native to western Iberia
Pronunciation: / ˈspænɪʃ hiːθ / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ hiːθ / (BrE)
a dense, erect shrub native to the western Iberian Peninsula, with profuse white or pink flowers, naturalized in southwestern England

Examples

Spanish heath shrub
Spanish heath can become invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth.
The garden is famous for its collection of Spanish heath, which blooms profusely in spring.
We planted Spanish heath along the hedge to add color during the summer months.
