thoracodorsal

noun
nerve or artery to back muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌθɔːrəkəʊˈdɔːrsəl / (AmE), / ˌθɒrəkəʊˈdɔːsəl / (BrE)
an anatomical structure, such as the thoracodorsal nerve or artery, that supplies the latissimus dorsi muscle in the back

Examples

Thoracodorsal nerve
The thoracodorsal is often preserved during breast surgery to maintain arm movement.
Damage to the thoracodorsal can affect the function of the latissimus dorsi muscle.
The surgeon identified the thoracodorsal before proceeding with the procedure.
