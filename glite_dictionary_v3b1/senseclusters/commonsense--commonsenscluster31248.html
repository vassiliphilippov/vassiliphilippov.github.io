commonsense

adjective
showing sound practical judgment
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.mən.sens/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.ən.sens/ (BrE)
Having or showing sound, practical judgment that is independent of specialized knowledge or training; based on good sense and reason.

Examples

Commonsense judgment
Her commonsense approach to problem-solving made her a valuable team member.
It requires commonsense solutions rather than complex theories.
The book is full of commonsense advice on everyday living.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • synonym: prudence - careful good judgment that allows someone to avoid danger or risks
  • synonym: sensible - having or showing good sense or judgment
