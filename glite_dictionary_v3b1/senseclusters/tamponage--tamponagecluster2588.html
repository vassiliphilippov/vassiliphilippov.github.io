tamponage

noun
blockage of wound or cavity by tampon
Pronunciation: / ˌtæmpəˈneɪʒ / (AmE), / ˌtæmpəˈnɑːʒ / (BrE)
The blockage or closure of a wound or body cavity, especially to stop bleeding, by means of a tampon or similar material.

Examples

Effective tamponage method
Effective tamponage can prevent excessive blood loss during certain surgical procedures.
In some cases, tamponage may be required to control internal hemorrhage.
The surgeon performed tamponage of the nasal cavity to stop the persistent bleeding.
