overtax

verb
to overload demands
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtæks / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtæks / (BrE)
to put too much demand, strain, or stress on someone or something, making it difficult to cope

Examples

To overtax your body
If you overtax your body with too much exercise, you may risk injury.
The teacher worried that too much homework would overtax her students.
Trying to do everything at once can easily overtax your mind.
If you overtax your muscles at the gym, you risk injury and fatigue.
The constant demands of the project began to overtax the entire team.
Trying to do too many things at once can overtax your concentration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overburden - to give too much work or responsibility
  • synonym: overwhelm - to give too much to handle
  • synonym: overwork - to make someone work too hard or too much
  • antonym: relieve - to reduce strain or pressure on someone or something
  • antonym: underwork - to give too little work or demand
