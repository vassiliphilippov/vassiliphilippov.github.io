tangent

noun
line touching a curve at one point
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / ˈtan.dʒənt / (BrE)
a straight line or plane that touches a curve or curved surface at a single point, but does not cross it at that point

Examples

Tangent to a curve
In calculus, finding the tangent to a curve at a given point is a fundamental concept.
The tangent to the circle at point A is drawn in red.
The architect explained how the tangent line affected the building's design.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secant - a straight line that cuts a curve at two or more points
