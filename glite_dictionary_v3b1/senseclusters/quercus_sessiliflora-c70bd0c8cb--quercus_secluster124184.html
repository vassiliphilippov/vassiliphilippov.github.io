Quercus sessiliflora

noun
a European oak tree species
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkwərkəs sɛˌsɪlɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˈkwɛːkəs sɛˌsɪlɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous European oak known for its tough, elastic wood, commonly called sessile oak.

Examples

Quercus sessiliflora wood
Furniture made from Quercus sessiliflora wood is prized for its strength and durability.
Many forests in France and Germany consist largely of Quercus sessiliflora trees.
The Quercus sessiliflora is widespread across central and western Europe.
