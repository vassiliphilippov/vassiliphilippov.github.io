greystone

noun
gray building stone
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪstəʊn / (BrE)
a building stone of gray color, commonly used in construction, especially for walls and buildings

Examples

Greystone blocks
The historic library was constructed entirely from greystone quarried nearby.
Many old houses in the city feature elegant greystone facades.
Workers carefully restored the damaged greystone on the church tower.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brick - a rectangular block used in building, usually made of clay
  • synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock often used as a building material
  • synonym: sandstone - a type of stone used in construction, typically tan or reddish in color
