regurgitate

verb
bring up swallowed food to feed young
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡɝdʒəˌteɪt /; / rɪˈɡɜrdʒɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɜːdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
to bring food or liquid back up from the stomach into the mouth, as in vomiting or when animals feed their young

Examples

Birds regurgitate food
Parent penguins often regurgitate partially digested fish for their young.
Some bird species regurgitate food to feed their chicks during the early stages of life.
When food is scarce, flamingos regurgitate a nutrient-rich liquid for their offspring.
Many birds regurgitate food to feed their young.
She felt nauseous and had to regurgitate after eating spoiled seafood.
The patient began to regurgitate his lunch unexpectedly during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retch - to make the sound and action of vomiting
  • antonym: swallow - to cause or allow (something) to pass down the throat
  • synonym: vomit - to eject stomach contents through the mouth
