globalization

noun
expansion to worldwide scale
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /gloʊ.bəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /gloʊ.bə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /gloʊ.bəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /gləʊ.bə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /gləʊ.bəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process by which businesses, cultures, or institutions develop international influence or start operating on a global scale.

Examples

The globalization of culture
Advances in technology have accelerated the globalization of culture and information.
Some critics argue that globalization can lead to the loss of local traditions.
The globalization of markets has changed the way companies do business worldwide.
Many critics argue that globalisation can threaten local cultures and traditions.
Technological advances have accelerated the pace of globalisation in recent decades.
The globalisation of markets has created new opportunities and challenges for businesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internationalization - the process of making something international
  • antonym: localisation - the process of making something fit local conditions
