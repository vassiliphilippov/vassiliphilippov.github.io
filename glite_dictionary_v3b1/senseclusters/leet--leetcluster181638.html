leet

noun
medieval local court
9.9
Pronunciation: /liːt/ (AmE), /liːt/ (BrE)
a historical local court in medieval England that dealt with minor offenses and local administration within a manor or district

Examples

A medieval leet
The villagers gathered at the leet to resolve disputes and discuss local matters.
Records from the old leet provide insight into medieval community life.
Minor crimes were often judged by the leet rather than higher courts.
