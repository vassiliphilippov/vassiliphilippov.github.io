deliquescent

adjective
absorbing moisture and becoming liquid
9.0 9.1
Pronunciation: /de.lɪ.ˈkwes.ənt/ /; / /dɪ.lɪ.ˈkwes.ənt/ (AmE), /de.lɪ.ˈkwes.ənt/ /; / /dɪ.lɪ.ˈkwes.ənt/ (BrE)
(of a substance) tending to become liquid by absorbing moisture from the air.

Examples

Deliquescent salt
Some salts are deliquescent and will turn into a solution if left exposed to humid air.
Sugar can sometimes behave as a deliquescent substance under very humid conditions.
The deliquescent nature of calcium chloride makes it useful for drying gases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hygroscopic - readily absorbing moisture from the air
