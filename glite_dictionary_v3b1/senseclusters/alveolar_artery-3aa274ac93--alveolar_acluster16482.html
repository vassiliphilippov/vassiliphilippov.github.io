alveolar artery

noun
artery supplying the jaw's alveolar process
7.3
Pronunciation: / ælˈviːələr ˈɑːrtəri / (AmE), / alˈviːələ ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery, branching from the maxillary artery, that supplies blood to the alveolar process of the jaw

Examples

The alveolar artery
An X-ray showed that the alveolar artery was intact following the procedure.
Injury to the alveolar artery during dental surgery can lead to significant bleeding.
The alveolar artery supplies blood to the teeth and gums in the upper jaw.
