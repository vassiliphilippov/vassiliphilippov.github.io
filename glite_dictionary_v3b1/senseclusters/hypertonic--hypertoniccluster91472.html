hypertonic

adjective
having abnormally high tension (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈtɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of living tissue) having or involving abnormally high tension or tone in the muscles or tissues.

Examples

Hypertonic muscles
Certain neurological disorders can result in hypertonic muscles, making movement difficult.
Patients with hypertonic muscle tissue often experience stiffness and limited mobility.
The therapist noted a hypertonic response in the patient's arm during the examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypotonic - (of living tissue) having abnormally low tension
  • degree lower: tonic - having normal muscle tension; relating to muscle tone
