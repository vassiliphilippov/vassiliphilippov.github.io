genealogy

noun
study of family ancestry
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːniˈælədʒi / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːniˈælədʒi / (BrE)
the study or record of family ancestry and the history of family lines

Examples

Family genealogy
Many people become interested in genealogy as they try to trace their roots.
She devoted years to the genealogy of her mother's family.
The library has a special section dedicated to genealogy resources.
Modern technology has made tracing your genealogy much easier than before.
She spent years researching her family's genealogy and created an elaborate family tree.
The genealogy of royal families is often documented in great detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancestry - one's family or ethnic descent
  • antonym: descendant - a person, plant, or animal that is descended from a particular ancestor
  • synonym: family tree - a diagram showing the descendants of a common ancestor
