monocentric

adjective
single centromere
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
having a single, localized centromere on a chromosome, as in most eukaryotic cells

Examples

Monocentric chromosome structure
Most plants have monocentric chromosomes, each with a single centromere visible during cell division.
Researchers observed that the species' monocentric chromosomes behaved differently from those with multiple centromeres.
The textbook explains the difference between monocentric and holocentric chromosomes in detail.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holocentric - having centromeric activity distributed along the entire chromosome
