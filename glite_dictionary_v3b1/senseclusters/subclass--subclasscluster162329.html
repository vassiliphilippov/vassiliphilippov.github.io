subclass

noun
smaller group category
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌklæs / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌklɑːs / (BrE)
a smaller group within a larger group, especially one sharing specific characteristics in a classification system

Examples

A subclass of objects
In computer programming, a subclass can inherit properties from its parent class.
The teacher divided the students into a subclass based on their reading levels.
Birds of prey form a subclass within the larger class of birds.
