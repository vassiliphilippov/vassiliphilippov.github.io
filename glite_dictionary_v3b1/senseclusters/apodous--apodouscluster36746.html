apodous

adjective
lacking feet or limbs (zoology)
7.1 9.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈpoʊ.dəs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈpəʊ.dəs/ (BrE)
(of animals) having no feet or lacking limbs, especially used of snakes and some fishes such as eels.

Examples

Apodous animals
Because they are apodous, eels move using undulating movements rather than walking.
Some species of amphibians have evolved to become apodous as an adaptation to burrowing.
The glass snake is an apodous reptile that is often mistaken for a real snake.
