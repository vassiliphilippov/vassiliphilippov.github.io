bang

noun
exclamation mark (computing, informal)
8.1
Pronunciation: / bæŋ / (AmE), / bæŋ / (BrE)
(informal, computing/typography) the punctuation mark '!' (exclamation point), especially when referred to in programming or technical contexts.

Examples

A bang in code
In the code, a leading bang means logical NOT and negates the following expression.
Unix scripts often start with #! called a bang line that tells the system which interpreter to use.
When writing documentation, she warned not to confuse a programmer's bang with ordinary punctuation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclamation mark - the punctuation mark '!' used to express strong feeling or, in computing, logical negation
