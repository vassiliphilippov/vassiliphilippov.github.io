tarry

verb
to linger or stay somewhere
9.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.rɪ/ /; / /ˈtæ.rɪ/ (AmE), /ˈtɑː.rɪ/ /; / /ˈtæ.rɪ/ (BrE)
to stay or remain in a place longer than expected, often delaying departure

Examples

To tarry too long
Please do not tarry on your way; we are already late for the appointment.

Do not tarry on your way home, as it will soon get dark.
He tarried outside the door, uncertain whether to enter.
She chose to tarry at the party, even after most of the guests had left.
