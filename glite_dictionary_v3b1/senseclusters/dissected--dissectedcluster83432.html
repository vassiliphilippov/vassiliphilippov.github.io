dissected

adjective
leaf with deep indentations or lobes
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛktɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf or other plant part) having one or more deep indentations or lobes reaching almost to the midrib.

Examples

A dissected leaf
Many maple leaves are deeply dissected, giving them a distinctive shape.
The botanist noted that the dissected margin was a key identifying feature of the specimen.
The fern has dissected fronds, making its appearance delicate and feathery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: entire - (of a leaf) having a continuous, unbroken margin
  • synonym: lobed - (of a leaf) having rounded or pointed projections
