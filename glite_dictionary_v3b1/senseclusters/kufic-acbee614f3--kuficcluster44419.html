Kufic

adjective
early Arabic script
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkuːfɪk / (AmE), / ˈkuːfɪk / (BrE)
relating to an early angular style of Arabic script used in ancient manuscripts and inscriptions

Examples

Kufic script style
The museum displayed a Quran written in beautiful Kufic script from the 8th century.
Scholars studied the Kufic inscriptions carved on the ancient mosque walls.
The artist incorporated Kufic calligraphy into her modern designs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Naskh - a later, more cursive style of Arabic script
