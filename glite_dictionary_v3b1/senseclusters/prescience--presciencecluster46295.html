prescience

noun
the ability to foresee the future
9.9 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpriÀê.si.…ôns / (AmE), / Ààpr…õs.i.…ôns / (BrE)
the ability to know or predict what will happen in the future; foresight

Examples

Financial prescience
Many admired his financial prescience during the turbulent markets of the 2000s.
The scientist's prescience in predicting the pandemic's impact impressed her colleagues.
With remarkable prescience, the author described technologies that would not exist for decades.

Synonyms

    9.6 0.01/m

  • foresight
    5.6 2.79/m
    foresight is the ability to see and prepare for future problems or chances, while prescience focuses on predicting what will happen.
  • foreknowledge
    8.9 0.64/m
    foreknowledge is knowing an event before it occurs, while prescience is an active ability to predict or sense future events.
