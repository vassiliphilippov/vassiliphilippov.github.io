preshape

verb
to shape in advance
5.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈʃeɪp/ /; / /prɪ.ˈʃeɪp/ (AmE), /priː.ˈʃeɪp/ /; / /prɪ.ˈʃeɪp/ (BrE)
to shape or form something in advance, before its final processing, fitting, or use

Examples

To preshape metal
The technician preshaped the metal sheet to fit the car's bumper before welding.
Bakers often preshape dough before letting it rise for the final time.
Engineers preshape plastic parts to speed up the assembly process.
