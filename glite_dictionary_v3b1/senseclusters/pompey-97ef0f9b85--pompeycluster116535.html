Pompey

proper noun
Roman general and statesman (106–48 BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑm.pi / (AmE), / ˈpɒm.pi / (BrE)
(106–48 BC) A prominent Roman general and statesman who was an ally and later rival of Julius Caesar, eventually murdered in Egypt.

Examples

General Pompey
Pompey was once an ally of Julius Caesar before their bitter rivalry led to civil war.
After his defeat at the Battle of Pharsalus, Pompey sought refuge in Egypt, where he was ultimately killed.
Many historians regard Pompey as one of the most influential figures of the late Roman Republic.
