vitrification

noun
rapid freezing preservation of biological material
9.8 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ccv6atr6af6ac8ke6a8359n / (AmE), / ccv6atr6af6ac8ke6a8359n / (BrE)
The process of preserving biological materials (for example eggs, embryos, sperm, or tissues) by rapid cooling into an amorphous, glass-like solid to prevent ice crystal formation.

Examples

Vitrification of embryos
The clinic recommended vitrification of embryos to improve survival rates after thawing for future transfers.
Studies show that oocyte vitrification preserves fertility better than some traditional slow-freezing methods in certain patients.
After the lab procedure, the surgeon noted that tissue samples survived vitrification without damaging ice crystals forming.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cryopreservation - the process of cooling and storing cells, tissues, or organs at very low temperatures to maintain viability
  • antonym: slow freezing - a freezing method that allows ice crystals to form, contrasted with vitrification which prevents crystallization
