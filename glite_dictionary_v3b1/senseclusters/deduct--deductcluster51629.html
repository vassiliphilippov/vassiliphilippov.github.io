To reach a conclusion by reasoning from known facts; to infer or establish by deduction.

Examples

To deduct the answer
After examining the clues, the detective was able to deduct who committed the crime.
From her tone of voice, I could deduct that she was upset about the decision.
Scientists deduct patterns in nature by carefully analyzing large sets of data.

Synonyms

  • deduce
    4.6 4/m
    deduce draws a conclusion directly from available facts, while deduct is presented as inferring by logical reasoning.
  • infer
    5.5 7/m
    infer means forming a conclusion from evidence and hints, while deduct focuses on reaching a conclusion by deduction.
  • derive
    4.3 3/m
    derive means get a result from a source or previous facts, while deduct here means infer a conclusion by reasoning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: induce - to draw a general conclusion from specific cases (opposite process of deduction)
  • synonym: infer - to reach a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning
