overseasoned

adjective
excessively embellished
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsiːzənd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsiːzənd / (BrE)
excessively decorated, embellished, or filled with unnecessary elements, especially in language, writing, or performance

Examples

Overseasoned writing
The director's style was criticized as overseasoned, with too many dramatic flourishes.
Her speech felt overseasoned because of the excessive use of flowery language.
Some readers found the novel overseasoned and preferred a simpler narrative style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overdone - excessively elaborate or exaggerated
  • antonym: understated - presented in a subtle or restrained way
