easement

noun
legal right to use another's land
6.1 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈiːz.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈiːz.mənt/ (BrE)
(law) The legal right to use another person's land for a specific purpose, such as a right of way.

Examples

A land easement
A dispute arose after the city tried to remove a historic easement for a pedestrian walkway.
The neighbor granted us an easement so we can access the main road through their property.
When buying land, always check if any easements are attached to the property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: right of way - the legal right to pass through land owned by another
