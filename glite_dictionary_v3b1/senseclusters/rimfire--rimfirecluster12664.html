rimfire

noun
cartridge ignited at rim
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪmˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪmˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a type of gun cartridge that is fired by striking the rim of its base to ignite the primer

Examples

Rimfire cartridge
The .22 caliber rifle uses rimfire ammunition, which is popular for target shooting.
Many beginners start with rimfire cartridges because they are affordable and have low recoil.
He collected several types of rimfire rounds from different manufacturers for his display.
