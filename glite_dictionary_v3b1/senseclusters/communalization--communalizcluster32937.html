communalization

noun
making communal
8.3
Pronunciation: /kə.mjuː.nə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.mjuː.nə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making living arrangements, facilities, or resources shared and used collectively by a community

Examples

Communalization of housing
The communalization of farmland allowed villagers to share equipment and labor more efficiently.
After the disaster, there was a push for communalization of resources to help everyone recover.
Some cities encourage the communalization of public spaces to foster a sense of community.
The communalization of the park allowed all residents to use the space for events and gatherings.
After communalization, the kitchen became a shared area for everyone in the building.
Local leaders promoted the communalization of water resources to ensure fair access for all villagers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collectivization - the organization of people or resources into a collective group
  • antonym: privatization - the process of transferring ownership from public or communal to private control
