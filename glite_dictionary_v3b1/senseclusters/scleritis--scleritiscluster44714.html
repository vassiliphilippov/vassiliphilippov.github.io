scleritis

noun
inflammation of the eye's sclera
8.6
Pronunciation: /sklɪ.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ /; / /skə.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ (AmE), /sklɪ.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ /; / /skə.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ (BrE)
Inflammation of the sclera, the white outer layer of the eyeball, often causing pain and redness.

Examples

Scleritis eye inflammation
If left untreated, scleritis can lead to serious complications affecting vision.
The ophthalmologist diagnosed her with scleritis after noticing severe redness and pain in her eye.
Treatment for scleritis usually involves anti-inflammatory medications prescribed by an eye specialist.
