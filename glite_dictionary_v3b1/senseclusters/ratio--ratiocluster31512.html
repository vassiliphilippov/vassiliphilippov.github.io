ratio

noun
social-media replies-to-likes measure
7.0 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8re\u026a\u0283io\u028a / (AmE), / \u02c8re\u026a\u0283\u026a\u0259\u028a / (BrE)
(informal, on social media) the relationship between the number of replies or comments and the number of likes or reactions on a post; often used to indicate negative reception when replies exceed likes.

Examples

A tweet's ratio
The journalist noted the tweet's ratio of replies to likes suggested widespread criticism among readers.
After the announcement, the post's ratio of comments to likes showed strong online backlash.
Companies monitor the ratio of positive comments to likes to measure whether a campaign is well received.
