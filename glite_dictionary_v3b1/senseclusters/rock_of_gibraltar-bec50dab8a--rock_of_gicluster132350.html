Rock of Gibraltar

proper noun
strategic limestone promontory at Spain's southern tip
5.9
Pronunciation: /rɑːk əv dʒɪ.ˈbrɔːl.tər/ (AmE), /rɒk əv dʒɪ.ˈbrɒl.tə/ /; / /rɒk əv dʒɪ.ˈbrɔːl.tə/ (BrE)
A limestone promontory at the southern tip of Spain, belonging to the United Kingdom, of strategic importance as it controls the entrance to the Mediterranean Sea.

Examples

The Rock of Gibraltar promontory
Control of the Rock of Gibraltar has long been a point of contention between Britain and Spain.

Tourists often visit the Rock of Gibraltar to see its famous Barbary macaques.
