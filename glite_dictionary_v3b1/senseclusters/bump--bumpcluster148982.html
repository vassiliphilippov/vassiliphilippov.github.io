bump

verb
collide with; knock against
1.0 7.58/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒmp / (AmE), / bÊŒmp / (BrE)
to collide with something or someone, usually unintentionally, often causing a jolt or shock

Examples

To bump your head
Be careful not to bump your head on the low doorway.
He accidentally bumped into a stranger while walking down the hall.
The bus bumped against the curb as it turned the corner.

Synonyms

  • bump into
    2.1 1.19/m
    bump into means to hit someone unexpectedly while moving, while bump often suggests a smaller accidental jolt.
  • run into
    2.3 2.73/m
    run into means to accidentally hit something while moving faster, while bump usually implies a slower, lighter collision.
  • knock against
    2.7 0.39/m
    knock against means to strike a surface by accident with some force, while bump is a more general light collision.
  • collide with
    3.7 2.82/m
    collide with means to hit with strong force during movement, while bump usually describes a smaller, often accidental jolt.
  • knock
    1.0 32/m
    knock means to hit or strike, often deliberately, while bump usually describes an accidental collision that causes a brief jolt.
