polonaise

noun
Polish dance or music
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊləˈneɪz / (AmE), / ˌpɒləˈneɪz / (BrE)
a slow, stately Polish dance in triple time, or the music composed for this dance

Examples

Chopin polonaise

Chopin's most famous polonaise is often played at piano competitions.
Dancers in traditional costumes moved gracefully to the rhythm of the polonaise.
The orchestra played a beautiful polonaise to open the evening's concert.
She learned the steps of the polonaise for the cultural festival.
Chopin composed several famous polonaises that are still performed today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waltz - a ballroom dance in triple time with a strong accent on the first beat
