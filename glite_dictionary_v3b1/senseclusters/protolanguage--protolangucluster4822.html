protolanguage

noun
early form of language
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊtoʊˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəʊˌlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a basic or early form of language with simple sounds, words, or gestures, used before full language develops

Examples

Child protolanguage use
Researchers study how children use protolanguage before they learn to form full sentences.
Some animals communicate with a kind of protolanguage made up of simple sounds and gestures.
The development of protolanguage is an important step in early childhood communication.
Researchers believe that early humans may have used a protolanguage before developing complex speech.
The concept of protolanguage helps explain how communication evolved in human history.
Some animals use signals that resemble a protolanguage but lack true grammar.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: language - a system of communication using words and grammar
  • synonym: primitive language - a basic or undeveloped form of language
