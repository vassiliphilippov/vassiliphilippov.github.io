flowstone

noun
cave mineral deposit
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfləʊˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a sheet-like layer of minerals deposited by flowing water on the walls or floors of caves

Examples

Smooth flowstone sheets
The explorers admired the smooth sheets of flowstone covering the cave walls.
Over thousands of years, water created impressive flowstone formations in the underground cavern.
Scientists studied the layers of flowstone to learn about the cave's history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stalactite - a mineral formation hanging from the ceiling of a cave
  • synonym: stalagmite - a mineral formation rising from the floor of a cave
