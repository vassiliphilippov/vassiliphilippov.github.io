decay

verb
lose stored charge or current
4.2 5.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
(of electrical charge, magnetic flux, or current) to diminish gradually, especially as a result of natural or physical processes.

Examples

To decay in circuits
If not maintained, the stored current may rapidly decay and affect performance.
Magnetic fields in the core can decay due to electrical resistance.
The capacitor will decay to zero voltage over time if left in the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissipate - (of energy or charge) to spread out and gradually disappear
