oil future

noun
contract for future delivery of oil
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl ˈfjuːtʃɚ / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈfjuːtʃə / (BrE)
a contract to buy or sell petroleum at a predetermined price for delivery at a specified future date

Examples

Oil futures contract
Investors often use oil futures to hedge against unpredictable price changes in the energy market.
The price of oil futures rose sharply after news of the supply disruption.
Trading oil futures can be risky without a solid understanding of the global oil industry.
