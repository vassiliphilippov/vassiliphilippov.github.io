condonement

noun
overlooking wrongdoing
9.6
Pronunciation: / kənˈdoʊnmənt / (AmE), / kənˈdəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the act of overlooking, forgiving, or accepting wrongdoing without punishment or criticism

Examples

Condonement of bad behavior
The manager's condonement of lateness led to a decline in team discipline.
Some people saw his silence as a form of condonement for the unethical behavior.
Repeated condonement of minor offenses can encourage more serious problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condemnation - the act of expressing strong disapproval or punishment
  • synonym: forgiveness - the act of pardoning someone for a mistake or wrongdoing
