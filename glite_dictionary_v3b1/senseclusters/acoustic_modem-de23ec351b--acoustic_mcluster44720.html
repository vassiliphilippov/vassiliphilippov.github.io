acoustic modem

noun
device for sending digital data as telephone tones
6.7 5.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkuː.stɪk ˈmoʊ.dəm/ /; / /ə.ˈkuː.stɪk ˈmoʊ.dem/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkuː.stɪk ˈməʊ.dəm/ /; / /ə.ˈkuː.stɪk ˈməʊ.dem/ (BrE)
a device that converts digital electrical signals to audible tones for transmission over a telephone line, and vice versa

Examples

An acoustic modem device
Before digital lines, an acoustic modem was essential for remote data access.
In the early days of computing, many people connected to networks using an acoustic modem.
The acoustic modem translates digital information into audible sounds for telephone transmission.
