labourism

noun
support for workers' rights
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.bər.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈleɪ.bə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.bə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
support for or advocacy of the political ideology and policies that favor labor unions, workers' rights, and social welfare, especially as associated with the Labour Party or labour movement

Examples

Labourism and workers' rights
The rise of labourism influenced many political parties in the early twentieth century.
Her strong belief in labourism led her to join several workers' rights organizations.
Debates about labourism often focus on the balance between business interests and fair wages.

Many social policies in Europe were influenced by the principles of labourism.
Debates about labourism often focus on the balance between public and private ownership.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets
  • antonym: conservatism - a political ideology favoring traditional values and limited government intervention
  • synonym: social democracy - a political ideology supporting social justice within a capitalist framework
  • synonym: socialism - a political system advocating collective or governmental ownership of production
  • synonym: unionism - support for labor unions and their activities
