hominin

noun
human lineage member
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːmɪnɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɒmɪnɪn / (BrE)
a member of the group that includes modern humans and their extinct ancestors after the split from chimpanzees

Examples

An ancient hominin
Scientists discovered new fossils of an ancient hominin in Africa.
The study of hominins helps us understand the evolution of modern humans.
Researchers debated whether the bones belonged to a new species of hominin.
Scientists discovered a new hominin fossil that sheds light on human evolution.
The museum's exhibit features skulls from several extinct hominin species.
Researchers debate how closely related certain hominin fossils are to modern humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ape - a primate such as a gorilla, chimpanzee, or orangutan
  • synonym: hominid - a member of the family that includes all great apes and humans
