voluntary

adjective
controlled by conscious will
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːlənˌteri / (AmE), / ˈvɒlənˌtri / (BrE)
(of an action or movement) controlled by a person's conscious will; done by choice rather than automatically or involuntarily.

Examples

Voluntary movement
His recovery included regaining voluntary control over his leg muscles.
Learning to play piano requires precise voluntary finger movements.
The doctor explained that voluntary muscles are those we can control consciously.

Related Terms

  • antonym: involuntary - not under conscious control; automatic
