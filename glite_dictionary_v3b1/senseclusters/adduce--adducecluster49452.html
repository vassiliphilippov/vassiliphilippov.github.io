adduce

verb
present evidence or reasons
9.1 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàdus / (AmE), / …ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to present or cite (evidence, reasons, or examples) as proof in discussion or argument

Examples

To adduce evidence
Additional data were adduced at the conference to bolster the argument.
Scientists adduce recent studies as evidence for their new theory.
The lawyer adduced several precedents to support her case.

Synonyms

  • cite
    3.6 45/m
    cite is mentioning a source or example to support a point in speech or writing, while adduce is formally presenting evidence in an argument.
  • abduce
    9.9 0.00/m
    abduce is a rare technical term used when offering evidence to support an inference, while adduce is the common formal verb for presenting evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cite - to quote as evidence for or against an argument
