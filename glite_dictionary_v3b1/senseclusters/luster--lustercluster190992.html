luster

noun
special brilliance or distinction
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌstɚ /; / ˈlʌstər / (AmE), / ˈlʌstə / (BrE)
A quality that gives someone or something special distinction, brilliance, or glory beyond the ordinary.

Examples

Luster of fame
A Nobel Prize brings international luster to a scientist's career.
Her performance added luster to an already impressive résumé.
The fresh paint gave the old building a new luster.
After years of neglect, the silver lost its lustre and looked faded.
The polished marble surface had a deep lustre that caught everyone's eye.
Winning the award added lustre to her already impressive reputation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dullness - lack of brightness or interest
  • synonym: glory - great honor or praise
  • synonym: shine - to be bright or give off light; brilliance
