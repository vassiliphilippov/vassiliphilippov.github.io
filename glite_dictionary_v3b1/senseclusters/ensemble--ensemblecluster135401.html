ensemble

noun
chorus group in ballet or musical
Pronunciation: / ɑnˈsɑmbəl /; / ɑnˈsɑm.bəl / (AmE), / ɒnˈsɒmbəl /; / ɒnˈsɒm.bəl / (BrE)
a group of musicians, singers, dancers, or actors who perform together as a unit, often supporting the main performers

Examples

Although not in the spotlight, the ensemble plays a vital role in every performance.
She joined the ensemble of the ballet company to gain more stage experience.

The jazz ensemble gave a breathtaking performance last night at the concert hall.
Their vocal ensemble won first prize in the national competition.
She was proud to be part of the ensemble, contributing to the show's success alongside her colleagues.
The theater company had a talented ensemble that brought energy to every scene.
Unlike traditional musicals, the play featured a strong ensemble without a single star.
