hematopoietic stem cell

noun
blood-forming stem cell in bone marrow
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmətoʊpoʊˈɪtɪk stɛm sɛl / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəʊpɔɪˈɛtɪk stɛm sɛl / (BrE)
a stem cell found in bone marrow that gives rise to all types of blood cells, including red cells, white cells, and platelets

Examples

Healthy hematopoietic stem cells
A bone marrow transplant provides healthy hematopoietic stem cells to replace damaged or diseased bone marrow.
Researchers are studying how hematopoietic stem cells can be used to treat blood disorders like leukemia.
The ability of hematopoietic stem cells to self-renew makes them vital for lifelong blood production.
