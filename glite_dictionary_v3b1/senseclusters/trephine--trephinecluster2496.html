trephine

verb
operate with a trephine (medical)
8.4
Pronunciation: / trəˈfaɪn / (AmE), / trɪˈfiːn /; / trɪˈfaɪn / (BrE)
to use a trephine to cut out a circular piece of bone, especially from the skull, as a surgical procedure

Examples

To trephine the skull
Modern techniques have made trephining much safer than in ancient times.
The surgeon decided to trephine the patient to relieve intracranial pressure.
They trephined the skull to access the affected brain tissue.
The surgeon had to trephine the patient's skull to relieve the pressure caused by the injury.
In ancient medicine, doctors would trephine to treat head trauma or neurological symptoms.
Modern neurosurgeons rarely need to trephine thanks to advances in less invasive techniques.
