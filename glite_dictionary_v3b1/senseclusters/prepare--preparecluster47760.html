prepare

verb
educate or train for a future role
1.0
Pronunciation: / prɪˈpɛr /; / prəˈpɛr / (AmE), / prɪˈpeə / (BrE)
to train or educate someone (or yourself) so they are ready for a job, activity, or future role

Examples

To prepare students
He spent years preparing his team for the championship match.
Her parents did everything they could to prepare her for college life.
The academy was designed to prepare students for careers in leadership.
As a mentor, she helped prepare the young artists for their future careers.
My job is to prepare new employees for customer service through extensive training.
The program is designed to prepare students for the challenges of engineering.
He spent two years preparing to join the national ballet company.
Many students prepare to become teachers through graduate programs and internships.
She decided to prepare for a career in medicine by enrolling in specialized courses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: educate - to give someone knowledge or skills, especially formally
  • synonym: groom - to prepare someone for a particular position or job
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to train or educate someone for something
  • synonym: train - to teach skills for a particular job or activity
