resolution

noun
number of pixels on a display
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the clarity or level of detail in an image or the ability of an instrument to distinguish fine detail

Examples

High resolution screen
Before buying a TV, check the resolution to ensure you get the picture quality you want.
Printing at low resolution can result in blurry photos and unclear text.
The new monitor has a higher resolution, making the images look much sharper.
A higher resolution means the instrument can distinguish objects that are very close together.
Astronomers improved the telescope’s resolution to observe distant galaxies more clearly.
The microscope’s resolution allows scientists to see individual cells in remarkable detail.
