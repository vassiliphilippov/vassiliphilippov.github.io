zincate

noun
zinc coating layer
Pronunciation: / ˈzɪŋkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈzɪŋkeɪt / (BrE)
a thin layer or coating of zinc formed on a metal surface by a chemical zincating process

Examples

Zincate coating
The technician inspected the zincate to ensure the metal was properly protected from corrosion.
A uniform zincate formed on the aluminum part after the chemical treatment.
Without a good zincate, the metal surface would quickly begin to rust.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrosion - the gradual destruction of metals by chemical reaction
  • synonym: galvanization - the process of applying a protective zinc coating to steel or iron
