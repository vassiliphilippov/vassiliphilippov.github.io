lag

verb
fall behind in movement or progress
Pronunciation: / læɡ / (AmE), / læɡ / (BrE)
to move, develop, or progress more slowly than others; to fall behind

Examples

To lag behind
After a mile, she began to lag behind the rest of the group.
As the day wore on, his energy started to lag and he moved more slowly.
The company's new product continues to lag in sales compared to its competitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall behind - to fail to keep up with others in movement or progress
  • antonym: keep up - to move at the same speed as others
  • synonym: trail - to move or lag behind someone or something
