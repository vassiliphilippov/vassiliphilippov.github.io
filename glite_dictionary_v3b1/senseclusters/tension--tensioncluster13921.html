tension

noun
force stretching an object
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛn.ʃən /; / ˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.ʃən /; / ˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
the state of being stretched tight or the force that makes something tight

Examples

Rope tension
As you pull the rope, the tension in it increases.
The bridge cables are under enormous tension to support the roadway.
The material can withstand high tension without snapping.
High tension is needed for the cable to support the weight of the bridge.
If you reduce the tension, the tent fabric will become loose and flap in the wind.
The tension in the guitar strings determines the pitch of the notes.
After running the marathon, he felt a lot of tension in his calf muscles.
The bridge cables are kept under high tension to support the heavy load.
Yoga helps to relieve physical tension in the back and shoulders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compression - a force that shortens or squeezes something
  • antonym: relaxation - the state of being free from tension or strain
  • antonym: slack - looseness; the state of not being taut or stretched
