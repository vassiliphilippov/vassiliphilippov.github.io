involve

verb
include as a part
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to include, require, or have someone or something as a necessary part or participant; to engage someone in an activity or situation

Examples

Involve extra work
Any renovation project is likely to involve additional costs.
Her research involves the study of rare plant species.
The new policy will involve changes in how employees are trained.
Achieving this goal involves both time and dedication from everyone on the team.
Adopting a pet often involves long-term commitment and responsibility.
The renovation will involve several major changes to the layout.
Her presentation involved the audience from the very first minute.
It was a complex story that involved him emotionally throughout the film.
The novel involved me so much that I lost track of time.

  • synonym: demand - to ask for authoritatively or forcefully; require as necessary
  • synonym: engage - to occupy or attract someone's involvement or attention
  • synonym: engross - to completely occupy someone's attention
  • synonym: entail - to involve as a necessary consequence
  • antonym: exclude - to deliberately leave out; to not include as a part
  • synonym: include - to comprise or contain as part of a whole
  • synonym: require - to make something necessary; demand as essential
