contest

verb
to compete for
2.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛst / (AmE), / kənˈtɛst / (BrE)
to compete for something or try to win against others, especially in a competition or election

Examples

To contest an election
Three teams will contest the championship title this year.
She decided to contest the results of the competition after a scoring error was found.
Several candidates are expected to contest the mayoral election next month.

Related Terms

  • synonym: challenge - to invite someone to compete or take part in a contest
  • synonym: compete - to try to win or gain something by defeating others
  • antonym: surrender - to give up or stop competing for something
