unnaturalness

noun
the state of being unnatural
5.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈnætʃrəlnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈnatʃrəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unnatural or not in accordance with natural principles or appearance

Examples

The unnaturalness of trees
She noticed a certain unnaturalness in the way the trees stood perfectly straight.
Some critics objected to the unnaturalness of the dialogue in the play.
The unnaturalness of his smile made everyone at the table uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artificiality - the quality of being made or contrived rather than natural
  • antonym: naturalness - the quality of being natural or free from artificiality
