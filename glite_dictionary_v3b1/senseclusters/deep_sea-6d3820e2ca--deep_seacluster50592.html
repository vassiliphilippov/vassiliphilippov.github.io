deep sea

noun
very deep part of the ocean
3.6 0.31/m
Pronunciation: / Àådip Ààsi / (AmE), / ÀådiÀêp ÀàsiÀê / (BrE)
The deeper parts of the ocean, typically far below the surface, where little or no sunlight penetrates.

Examples

The deep sea
Many strange and undiscovered species inhabit the deep sea.
Scientists use special submarines to study creatures living in the deep sea.
The Titanic sank to the bottom of the deep sea after hitting an iceberg.

Synonyms

  • depth of the ocean
    4.7 0.07/m
    depth of the ocean describes the ocean's deepest regions far below the surface, while deep sea names zones with almost no sunlight.
  • deep
    7.4 0.63/m
    deep means the very lowest parts of the ocean or a trench, while deep sea stresses broad dark ocean areas.
  • deepwater
    4.0 0.24/m
    deepwater refers to deep parts of any large body of water, while deep sea specifically means the ocean's dark, sunless zones.
  • depths
    3.9 0.05/m
    depths name the furthest, often hidden parts of something, while deep sea refers to the ocean's very deep, sunless regions.
