flash

noun
a brief intense experience
Pronunciation: / flæʃ / (AmE), / flæʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, brief burst of feeling, sensation, inspiration, or moment of understanding

Examples

A sudden flash of inspiration led her to solve the problem.
During the meeting, a flash of panic crossed her face.
He felt a flash of anger when he heard the news.
In a flash, he understood exactly what needed to be done.
She had a sudden flash of inspiration while working on the problem.
That idea came to me in a flash during the meeting.
