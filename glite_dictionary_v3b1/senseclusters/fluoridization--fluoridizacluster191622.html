fluoridization

noun
addition of fluoride to water
5.8
Pronunciation: / flʊˌɔrɪdəˈzeɪʃən /; / flɔːrɪdəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / flʊˌɒrɪdaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / flʊəˌrɪd.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding a fluoride compound to a water supply to help prevent dental decay

Examples

Fluoridization of water
Opponents of fluoridization expressed concerns about the safety of adding chemicals to drinking water.
Studies show that fluoridization can significantly reduce cavities in children.
The city council voted in favor of fluoridization to improve public dental health.
Several studies have shown that fluoridisation significantly reduces the rate of cavities in children.
Some communities oppose fluoridisation due to concerns about potential health risks.
The government launched a campaign to promote fluoridisation in rural areas.
