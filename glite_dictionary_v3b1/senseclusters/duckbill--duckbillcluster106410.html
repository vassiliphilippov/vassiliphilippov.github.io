duckbill

noun
aquatic mammal of Australia, platypus
9.9 7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌkˌbɪl / (BrE)
A small, aquatic, egg-laying mammal native to Australia and Tasmania, with dense fur, a broad bill, webbed feet, and a flat tail; also called a platypus.

Examples

A duckbill mammal
Children at the zoo were excited to see the duckbill swimming in its enclosure.
Researchers studied the unique features of the duckbill, particularly its bill and webbed feet.
The duckbill is one of the few mammals that lay eggs.
