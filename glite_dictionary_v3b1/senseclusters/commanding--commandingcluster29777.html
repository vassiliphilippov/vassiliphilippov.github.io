commanding

adjective
having a wide or strategic view
3.7 4.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈmændɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a height or viewpoint) having a position that provides a wide, impressive, or strategic view over an area.

Examples

Commanding view
From the tower’s commanding position, one could see for miles in every direction.
The hilltop hotel offers a commanding view of the valley below.
They built their house on a commanding ridge to take advantage of the scenic landscape.
