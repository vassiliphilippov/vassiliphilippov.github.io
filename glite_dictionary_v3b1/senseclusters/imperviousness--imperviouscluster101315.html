imperviousness

noun
impenetrability; resistance to penetration
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɜrviəsnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːviəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of not allowing something, such as water, light, or people, to pass through or penetrate

Examples

The imperviousness to water
His imperviousness to criticism surprised his colleagues.
Modern raincoats are praised for their imperviousness to water and wind.
The imperviousness of the fortress walls made them a daunting obstacle for any attacker.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impenetrability - the state of being impossible to pass through or enter
  • antonym: porousness - the quality of allowing liquid or air to pass through easily
