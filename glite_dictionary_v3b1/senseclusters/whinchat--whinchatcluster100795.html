whinchat

noun
European songbird of grassy meadows
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪn.tʃæt / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.tʃæt / (BrE)
A small brown-and-buff European songbird found in grassy meadows, known for its distinctive call.

Examples

A whinchat bird
During migration, several whinchats stopped to rest in the fields.
The whinchat is less common in cities than in rural grasslands.
We spotted a whinchat perched on a fence post near the meadow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stonechat - another small European chat species, similar in appearance
