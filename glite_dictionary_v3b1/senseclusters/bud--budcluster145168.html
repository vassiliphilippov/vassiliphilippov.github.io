bud

verb
(of a plant) develop buds
1.0 0.37/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒd / (AmE), / bÊŒd / (BrE)
to begin to grow or develop, especially as a new shoot on a plant or the start of something new

Examples

Plants bud in spring
Every spring, the rose bush begins to bud with new life.
The trees have started to bud after the long winter.
When conditions are right, certain cacti will bud rapidly.
A new idea started to bud in her mind as she listened to the lecture.
After the long winter, the trees finally began to bud in early spring.
Their friendship continued to bud throughout the school year.

Synonyms

  • shoot
    2.8 0.95/m
    a shoot is a young plant stem or new growth, while a bud describes the very early stage of that growth.
  • germinate
    4.8 1.44/m
    germinate is to begin growth from a seed or spore, while bud means to start growing as a new shoot or idea.
  • grow
    2.2 71/m
    grow means to increase or develop over time, while bud refers to the first small shoot or the very start of development.
  • burgeon
    7.6 4.16/m
    burgeon means to grow quickly and become large, while bud means a first small stage of growth or an idea.
