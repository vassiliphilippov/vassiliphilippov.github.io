assassinate

verb
to deliberately ruin someone's reputation
1.0
Pronunciation: / əˈsæsəˌneɪt / (AmE), / əˈsasɪneɪt / (BrE)
To deliberately damage or destroy someone's reputation or character, especially in a calculated and malicious way.

Examples

To assassinate a reputation
He was furious when his opponent tried to assassinate his good name in public.
Rumors can quickly assassinate someone's reputation, often without basis in truth.
The media attempted to assassinate the politician's character during the scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defame - to damage the good reputation of someone
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something
