Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa

proper noun
Roman general and statesman (63–12 BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkəs vɪpˈsæniəs əˈɡrɪpə / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs vɪpˈsæniəs əˈɡrɪpə / (BrE)
(63–12 BC) Roman general and statesman who commanded the fleet that defeated Antony and Cleopatra at the Battle of Actium.

Examples

Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa Roman general
Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa played a crucial role in Octavian's rise to power during the final war of the Roman Republic.
Historians often regard Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa as one of the most talented Roman generals and engineers.
The decisive victory at Actium was largely credited to Marcus Vipsanius Agrippa's naval strategies.
