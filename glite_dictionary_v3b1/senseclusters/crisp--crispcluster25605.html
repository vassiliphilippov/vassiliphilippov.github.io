crisp

adjective
concise, to-the-point (speech or writing)
3.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /krɪsp/ (AmE), /krɪsp/ (BrE)
(of speech or writing) brief and clear, with no unnecessary words; concise and to the point.

Examples

Crisp reply
He gave a crisp answer that left no room for doubt.
Her report was praised for its crisp prose and straightforward conclusions.
The manager's crisp instructions made the task easy to understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: curt - rudely brief in speech or manner
  • synonym: terse - using few words, often in a way that seems unfriendly
  • antonym: verbose - using more words than needed; wordy
