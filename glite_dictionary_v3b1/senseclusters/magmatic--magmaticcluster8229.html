magmatic

adjective
from molten rock
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / mæɡˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to magma (molten rock beneath the Earth's surface), or things formed from it

Examples

Magmatic rocks
The region is known for its magmatic rocks, which formed millions of years ago.
Scientists study magmatic processes to understand how volcanoes develop and erupt.
The magmatic intrusion altered the surrounding layers of sedimentary rock.
The magmatic activity beneath the volcano increased before the eruption.
Scientists analyze magmatic rocks to learn about the Earth's interior.
A magmatic intrusion can create new mineral deposits over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: igneous - formed by the cooling and solidification of magma or lava
  • antonym: metamorphic - relating to rocks changed by heat and pressure
  • antonym: sedimentary - formed by the accumulation of mineral or organic particles
