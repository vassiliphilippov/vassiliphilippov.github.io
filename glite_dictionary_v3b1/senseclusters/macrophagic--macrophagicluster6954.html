macrophagic

adjective
feeding on large particles
9.1
Pronunciation: /mæ.krə.ˈfæg.ɪk/ /; / /mæ.krə.ˈfeɪ.dʒɪk/ (AmE), /mæ.krə.ˈfæg.ɪk/ /; / /mæ.krə.ˈfeɪ.dʒɪk/ (BrE)
relating to organisms or cells that feed on or ingest large particles or prey.

Examples

Macrophagic fish
Some fish species are considered macrophagic because they consume large pieces of food.
The macrophagic behavior of these organisms allows them to process bigger prey than others in their environment.
Researchers observed that the macrophagic cells quickly engulfed the large particles introduced into the sample.
