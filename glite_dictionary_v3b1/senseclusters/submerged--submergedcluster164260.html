submerged

adjective
growing or remaining under water
1.0
Pronunciation: / səbˈmɝdʒd / (AmE), / səbˈmɜːdʒd / (BrE)
completely under the surface of water

Examples

Submerged leaves
Researchers studied the adaptations of submerged leaves in the river.
Some insects lay their eggs on the submerged stems of aquatic plants.
The pond is filled with submerged plants that help maintain the ecosystem.
After the storm, several streets were submerged by floodwaters.
Fish darted between the submerged branches of the fallen tree.
The divers carefully avoided the submerged rocks near the shore.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emerged - risen out of water; no longer submerged
  • antonym: emergent - (of a plant) rising above the surface of water
  • synonym: immersed - completely covered or dipped in a liquid
  • synonym: subaquatic - living or occurring under water
