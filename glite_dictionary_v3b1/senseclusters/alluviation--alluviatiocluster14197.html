alluviation

noun
accumulation of sediment by water
8.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌluːviˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌluːvɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or phenomenon of sediments, such as sand or gravel, accumulating in a particular area, typically as a result of the action of water.

Examples

Alluviation of sediments
Geologists study alluviation to understand how landscapes evolve in response to water movement.
Recent flooding led to significant alluviation in the low-lying fields near the river.
The alluviation of gravel along the streambed can alter its flow over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - sediment deposited by flowing water
