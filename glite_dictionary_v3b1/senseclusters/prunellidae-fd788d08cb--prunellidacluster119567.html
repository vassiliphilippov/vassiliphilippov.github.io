Prunellidae

noun
family of birds including hedge sparrow
Pronunciation: / pruːˈnɛlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / pruːˈnɛlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, sparrow-like birds known as accentors, commonly found in Eurasia and noted for species like the hedge sparrow.

Examples

Prunellidae family birds
Many birdwatchers admire the subtle beauty of the Prunellidae family.
The Prunellidae are known for their unobtrusive habits and drab plumage.
The hedge sparrow, or dunnock, belongs to the bird family Prunellidae.
