clergy

noun
official religious leaders (Christianity)
Pronunciation: / ˈklɝːdʒi / (AmE), / ˈklɜːdʒi / (BrE)
The official leaders of religious services in Christianity, such as priests, ministers, or pastors, collectively, as distinct from regular church members.

Examples

The clergy members
Many reforms in the church have been initiated by members of the clergy.
The clergy led the procession through the cathedral on Easter Sunday.
The distinction between the clergy and the laity has been debated for centuries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: laity - members of a religious community who are not clergy
  • synonym: minister - an individual member of the clergy
  • synonym: priest - an ordained member of the clergy
