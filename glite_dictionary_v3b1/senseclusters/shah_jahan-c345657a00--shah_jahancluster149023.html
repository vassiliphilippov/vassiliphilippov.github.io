Shah Jahan

proper noun
Mughal emperor of India, builder of the Taj Mahal
7.0
Pronunciation: / ʃɑ dʒəˈhɑn / (AmE), / ʃɑː dʒəˈhɑːn / (BrE)
(1592–1666) The Mughal emperor of India, renowned for commissioning the Taj Mahal and other exemplary monuments of Mughal architecture.

Examples

Shah Jahan emperor
Shah Jahan commissioned the Taj Mahal as a tomb for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.
Historians often highlight Shah Jahan's contributions to India's cultural heritage.
The reign of Shah Jahan is considered a golden age of Mughal architecture.
