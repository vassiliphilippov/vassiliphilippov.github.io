Phycomycetes

noun
outdated group of fungi
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪkoʊmaɪˈsiːtiːz / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪkə(ʊ)mɪˈsiːtiːz / (BrE)
an outdated grouping of fungi and fungus-like organisms that included the former subdivisions Mastigomycota and Zygomycota, now recognized as an unnatural classification

Examples

Old Phycomycetes group
Many older textbooks classify water molds as Phycomycetes, though modern taxonomy no longer supports this group.
Researchers found that organisms once called Phycomycetes are actually quite diverse and distantly related.
The term Phycomycetes is now rarely used due to advancements in fungal classification.
