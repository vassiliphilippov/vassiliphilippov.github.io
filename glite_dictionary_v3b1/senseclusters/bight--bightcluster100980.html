bight

noun
a widespread curve in a coastline or river
Pronunciation: / baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
a wide curve or bend in a coastline or river, forming a large open bay

Examples

A river bight
A broad bight in the river created a calm harbor for fishing boats.
Several small villages are nestled along the bight of the coast, sheltered from the wind.
The sailors navigated their way around the bight to avoid the rocky shoreline.
Many fishing boats gather in the bight during the summer season.
The Great Australian Bight is famous for its dramatic cliffs and marine life.
They sailed along the bight, admiring the sweeping curve of the coastline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bay - a broad inlet of the sea where the land curves inward
  • degree lower: cove - a small indentation or recess in the shoreline
  • synonym: gulf - a large area of sea partly enclosed by land
