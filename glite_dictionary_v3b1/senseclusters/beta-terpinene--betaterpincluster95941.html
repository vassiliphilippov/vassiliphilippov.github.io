beta-terpinene

noun
an isomeric terpene hydrocarbon
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪtə tərˈpɪniːn / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə tɜːˈpɪniːn / (BrE)
an isomeric hydrocarbon classified as a terpene, with the molecular formula C10H16, not known to occur naturally but synthesized in the laboratory.

Examples

Beta-terpinene compound
Researchers studied the physical properties of beta-terpinene to compare with other terpenes.
Synthesis of beta-terpinene is achieved through chemical modification of sabinene.
When analyzed in the laboratory, beta-terpinene showed no signs of natural occurrence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: terpene - any of a large group of volatile unsaturated hydrocarbons found in plants
