emotionality

noun
quality of being emotional
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmoʊʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌməʊʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having or expressing strong emotions; emotional nature.

Examples

Strong emotionality
Her emotionality was evident in the heartfelt speech she delivered at the ceremony.
In therapy, expressing one's emotionality can be an important step towards healing.
The novel explores the emotionality of its characters, revealing their deepest fears and desires.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emotion - a strong feeling such as love, fear, or anger
  • antonym: stoicism - the endurance of pain or hardship without display of feelings
