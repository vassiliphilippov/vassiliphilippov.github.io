palpable excitement

noun
intense or obvious excitement
Pronunciation: / ˈpælpəbəl ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˈpælpəbl ɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
A feeling of excitement that is so intense or obvious that it can almost be felt physically.

Examples

A palpable excitement swept
During the announcement, a palpable excitement swept through the crowd.
The palpable excitement in the stadium was contagious as the final whistle approached.
There was palpable excitement in the air before the concert began.
