price war

noun
intense competition with heavy price cuts
3.2 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / pra…™s w…îr / (AmE), / pra…™s w…îÀê / (BrE)
a situation in which companies compete intensely by lowering their prices to attract customers, often resulting in lower profits for all involved.

Examples

Many small retailers struggled to survive during the electronics price war.
The supermarket price war has led to lower grocery bills for customers.
