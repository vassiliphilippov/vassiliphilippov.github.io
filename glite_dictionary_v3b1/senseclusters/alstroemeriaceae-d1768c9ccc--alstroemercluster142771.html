Alstroemeriaceae

noun
family of flowering plants (botany)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ælˌstroʊməriˈeɪsi.i /; / ˌælstroʊˌmɛriˈeɪsiˌi / (AmE), / ælˌstrəʊmɛriˈeɪsiː.iː /; / ˌalstrəʊˌmɛriˈeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, sometimes included in Liliaceae or Amaryllidaceae, known for species such as Alstroemeria (Peruvian lily)

Examples

Alstroemeriaceae family plants
Botanists sometimes include Alstroemeriaceae within the larger family Amaryllidaceae.
Members of the Alstroemeriaceae are mostly native to South America.
The Alstroemeriaceae family is known for its showy, colorful flowers often used in bouquets.
Many species in the Alstroemeriaceae are native to South America.
Some botanists classify Alstroemeriaceae as a separate group from the Liliaceae.
The Alstroemeriaceae family is known for its ornamental plants such as the Peruvian lily.
