crush

verb
make ineffective or suppress completely
3.8 3.8
Pronunciation: / krʌʃ / (AmE), / krʌʃ / (BrE)
to defeat, suppress, or overwhelm someone or something completely, often by force or pressure (including emotionally or in competition)

Examples

To crush the competition
Rapid technological change can crush industries that fail to adapt.
The government acted quickly to crush the rebellion before it could spread.
This new law is designed to crush any attempts at political dissent.
Our basketball team crushed their rivals in the championship game.
The incumbent senator crushed his opponent in the election, winning by a landslide.
They crushed the competition with their innovative new product.
He was crushed when he heard that he didn't get the job.
The harsh criticism from her teacher completely crushed her spirits.
The team's defeat in the finals crushed their hopes of winning the championship.
