Psilophytaceae

noun
extinct family of Paleozoic vascular plants
Pronunciation: / saɪˌlɑːfaɪˈteɪsi.iː / (AmE), / sɪˌlɒfaɪˈtiːsi.iː / (BrE)
an extinct family of simple, leafless vascular plants that lived during the Paleozoic era, especially the Silurian and Devonian periods

Examples

The Psilophytaceae family
Fossil records suggest that Psilophytaceae were among the earliest vascular plants to colonize land.
Many genera within the Psilophytaceae family are known only from Devonian-aged rock formations.
The simple structure of Psilophytaceae makes them important for understanding plant evolution.
