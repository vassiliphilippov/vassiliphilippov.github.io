tableau

noun
a striking or dramatic scene
Pronunciation: / tæbˈloʊ /; / tæˈbloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtæbləʊ / (BrE)
a visually striking or dramatic scene, especially with people or objects arranged in a deliberate way, often as in art, theater, or a photograph

Examples

A tableau scene
Each Christmas, the town square becomes a living tableau depicting the nativity scene.
The curtains opened to reveal a beautiful tableau of dancers frozen in mid-leap.
The photographer captured the solemn tableau created by the mourners at the memorial.
At the end of the play, the actors froze in a striking tableau that captured the final scene.
Each festival year, volunteers recreate a famous painting as a living tableau for the delighted crowd.
The wedding party formed a beautiful tableau on the steps of the church for photographs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scene - a part of a play or a situation viewed as if in a play
  • synonym: tableau vivant - a living representation of a scene by silent, motionless people
