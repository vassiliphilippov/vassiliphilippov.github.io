infusion

noun
slow introduction of liquid into the body (medical)
4.7 4.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ /; / /ɪn.ˈfuː.ʒən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ /; / /ɪn.ˈfuː.ʒən/ (BrE)
(medicine) the slow introduction of a fluid, drug, or electrolyte into a vein or between tissues, often using gravity.

Examples

IV infusion
Complications can occur if the infusion is too rapid or not properly monitored.
Doctors started a saline infusion to treat her dehydration.
The patient received an infusion of antibiotics over several hours.
