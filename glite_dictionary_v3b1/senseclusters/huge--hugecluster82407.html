huge

adjective
extremely large in size or amount
1.0 164/m
Pronunciation: / hjud í / (AmE), / hjuÀêd í / (BrE)
Extremely large in size, amount, or degree.

Examples

A huge increase
The company announced a huge increase in profits this year.
The concert attracted a huge crowd of enthusiastic fans.
They live in a huge house on the outskirts of the city.

Synonyms

  • enormous
    2.1 58/m
    enormous describes very large size or amount in neutral tone, while huge is the common everyday word for great size.
  • immense
    3.7 14/m
    immense suggests very great size or amount and sounds a bit more formal, while huge is more everyday and casual.
  • vast
    2.1 65/m
    vast describes very large area or amount over wide space, while huge applies to many things of great size.
  • gigantic
    3.2 8.27/m
    gigantic paints a picture of something enormously large like a giant object, while huge is a simple, common way to say big.
  • giant
    1.0 49/m
    giant means very large and can be used as a noun or adjective, while huge is mainly an adjective for great size.
  • massive
    2.9 54/m
    massive suggests heavy, solid, or powerful size, while huge simply names great size without implying weight or force.
  • tremendous
    3.0 36/m
    tremendous often shows great size or strong feeling and has an emotional tone, while huge is a plain size word.
  • colossal
    4.7 3.43/m
    colossal feels dramatic and causes awe for very great size, while huge is more neutral and less dramatic in tone.
  • gargantuan
    7.3 1.30/m
    gargantuan means extremely large in a grand, often literary way, while huge is simple everyday speech for something big.
  • mammoth
    3.9 0.78/m
    mammoth suggests great size or scale often used for problems or tasks, while huge mainly describes physical size.
  • humongous
    3.6 1.04/m
    humongous is informal and playful for very large things, while huge is more neutral and fits formal and casual speech.
  • astronomical
    5.6 3.13/m
    astronomical often refers to very large numbers or costs, while huge refers to general size or amount in everyday speech.
  • outsized
    4.6 1.07/m
    outsized means larger than usual for its kind or role, while huge simply labels something as very large without comparison.
