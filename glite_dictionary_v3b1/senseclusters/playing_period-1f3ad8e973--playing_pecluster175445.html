playing period

noun
segment of active play time in games
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪɪŋ ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪɪŋ ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
A segment of time in a game or performance during which play actively proceeds.

Examples

A playing period in basketball
Each playing period in basketball consists of twelve minutes of action.
Heavy rain caused the suspension of the third playing period in the tournament.
The referee indicated that the first playing period would be twenty minutes long.

Related Terms

  • antonym: halftime - The interval between the first and second halves of a game
  • antonym: interval - A pause or break between periods of play
