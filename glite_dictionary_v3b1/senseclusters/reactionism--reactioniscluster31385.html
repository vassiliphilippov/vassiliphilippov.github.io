reactionism

noun
reactionary political ideology
9.3 8.8
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈæk.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /riː.ˈæk.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the political philosophy or ideology characterized by opposition to social or political progress and a desire to return to a previous state or system.

Examples

Reactionism in politics
His writings reveal a deep commitment to reactionism and the restoration of monarchy.
Many scholars have analyzed the rise of reactionism in 20th-century Europe.
The party's policies were rooted in a distinct form of reactionism that rejected modern reforms.
