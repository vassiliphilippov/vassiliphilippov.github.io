pleurocarpous moss

noun
moss with side-branch reproductive organs
9.9
Pronunciation: /plʊə.rə.ˈkɑːr.pəs mɔːs/ /; / /plʊə.rə.ˈkɑːr.pəs mɑːs/ (AmE), /plʊə.rə.ˈkɑː.pəs mɒs/ /; / /plɪə.rə.ˈkɑː.pəs mɒs/ (BrE)
A type of moss in which the reproductive organs (archegonium or antheridium) are borne on short side branches rather than on the main stem.

Examples

Dense pleurocarpous mosses
Many pleurocarpous mosses spread to form dense carpets on logs and rocks in damp forests.
The pleurocarpous moss is easily identified by the way its reproductive structures cluster on side branches.
Unlike acrocarpous types, a pleurocarpous moss typically has branching stems and a sprawling growth habit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acrocarpous moss - a moss with reproductive organs at the tips of the main stem
