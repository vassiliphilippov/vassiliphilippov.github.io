St. Vitus

proper noun
Christian martyr and patron saint of epilepsy
9.7
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈvaɪtəs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈvaɪtəs / (BrE)
A Christian martyr and saint who is venerated as the patron of those suffering from epilepsy and Sydenham's chorea (St. Vitus' dance).

Examples

St. Vitus church
Many medieval churches were dedicated to St. Vitus across Europe.
She prayed to St. Vitus for relief from her illness.
The festival of St. Vitus is celebrated on June 15th in some countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: St. Vitus' dance - Sydenham's chorea; a disorder involving involuntary movements, historically associated with St. Vitus
