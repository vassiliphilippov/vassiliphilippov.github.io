emerger

noun
insect in transition stage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɜrdʒər / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒə / (BrE)
an insect, especially a mayfly, in the stage of emerging from its aquatic larval form to adulthood

Examples

Mayfly emerger
The fly fisher tied a emerger pattern to mimic insects rising to the water's surface.
During the hatch, trout feed eagerly on emergers struggling to break through the surface film.
A mayfly emerger is especially vulnerable to predators as it transitions to adulthood.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: adult - a fully developed insect after metamorphosis
  • degree lower: nymph - an immature form of an insect that does not change greatly as it grows
