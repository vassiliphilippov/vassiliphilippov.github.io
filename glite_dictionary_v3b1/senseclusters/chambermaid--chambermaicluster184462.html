chambermaid

noun
woman employed to clean bedrooms, esp. in hotels
3.6 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃeɪm.bər.meɪd/ (AmE), /ˈtʃeɪm.bə.meɪd/ (BrE)
a woman employed to clean and care for bedrooms, especially in hotels or large houses; now chiefly used in reference to hotel staff

Examples

The chambermaid cleans rooms
In Victorian novels, a chambermaid was often responsible for looking after the guest rooms in grand houses.
The chambermaid knocked before entering to clean the suite for the next guests.
The hotel chambermaid came by every morning to tidy the room and replace the towels.
