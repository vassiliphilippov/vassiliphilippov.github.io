anthracosis

noun
lung disease from coal dust
9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.θrə.ˈkoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /æn.θrə.ˈkəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A lung disease caused by the accumulation of coal dust in the lungs, typically due to long-term inhalation.

Examples

Anthracosis lung disease
Doctors diagnosed the patient with anthracosis after reviewing his X-rays and occupational history.
Long-term exposure to coal dust can lead to anthracosis in miners.
Preventing anthracosis requires strict safety measures in coal mining operations.
