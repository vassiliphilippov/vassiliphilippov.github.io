imitation

noun
something made as a copy of an original
5.1 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a thing that is made to look, feel, or seem like something else, especially in order to deceive or as a substitute for the original

Examples

Imitation leather
He bought an imitation of the famous painting for his office wall.
The jewelry was just an imitation of the designer brand, but it looked very convincing.
This cheese is actually an imitation made from soy, not real dairy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - something made to be like another
  • antonym: original - something serving as a model or source for copies
