swoop

verb
move in a sweeping, curving motion
3.7
Pronunciation: / swuːp / (AmE), / swuːp / (BrE)
to move quickly and smoothly in a sweeping or curving motion, often from a higher to a lower position

Examples

To swoop around a corner
Suddenly, a cyclist swooped around the corner at high speed.
The children swooped across the lawn, pretending to be airplanes.
The eagle swooped down and caught its prey in the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dive - to descend quickly in a steep path
  • degree lower: glide - to move smoothly and continuously along, as if without effort
