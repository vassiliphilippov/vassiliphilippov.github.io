catechu

noun
brownish extract from Acacia catechu tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkætɪˌtʃu / (AmE), / ˈkatɪˌtʃu / (BrE)
A brownish resinous extract obtained from the heartwood of the Acacia catechu tree, used for dyeing, tanning, preserving nets and sails, and formerly in medicine.

Examples

Catechu extract dye
In the past, catechu was prescribed by herbalists for its supposed medicinal properties.
The fisherman treated his nets with catechu to protect them from rotting in the water.
Traditional textiles in India are sometimes dyed using catechu to produce a rich brown color.
