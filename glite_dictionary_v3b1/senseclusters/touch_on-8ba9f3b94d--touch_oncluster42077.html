touch on

verb
have an effect or influence (slightly)
4.0 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / tÊŒtÊƒ É‘n / (AmE), / tÊŒtÊƒ É’n / (BrE)
to have an effect upon or influence something or someone, even if only slightly

Examples

Touch on other areas
Her decisions as a manager often touch on the work of several different departments.
Rising fuel prices touch on all sectors of the economy, not just transportation.
The new law will touch on every aspect of our daily lives.

Synonyms

  • affect
    3.0 167/m
    affect changes or influences something directly and often clearly, while touch on suggests a small or slight influence.

    5.8 0.27/m

  • bear on
    5.1 3.65/m
