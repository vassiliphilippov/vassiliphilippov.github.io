catch

verb
become accidentally ensnared or entangled
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / kætʃ /; / katʃ / (BrE)
to become accidentally or suddenly entangled, hooked, or stuck on something

Examples

Catch on a nail
Be careful not to catch your scarf in the subway door.
Her sweater caught on a nail as she passed by.
I was walking through the woods when I caught my sleeve on a thorn bush.
Be careful, or your necklace might catch in your hair.
My sleeve caught on a nail as I walked past the door.
The kite caught in the branches and was difficult to remove.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - to release from being caught or entangled
  • synonym: snag - to catch and tear or become stuck on something
