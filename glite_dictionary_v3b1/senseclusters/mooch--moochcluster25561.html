mooch

verb
ask for and get things for free
8.5 0.36/m
Pronunciation: / muÀêt É / (AmE), / muÀêt É / (BrE)
to ask for and obtain something, especially food, money, or help, without paying or giving anything in return; to act as a parasite

Examples

To mooch a meal
He always tries to mooch free meals from his friends.
She managed to mooch a ride home after the concert.
Stop trying to mooch off my hard work and do something yourself.

Synonyms

  • cadge
    9.8 0.11/m
    cadge is to ask or persuade someone for small items or money, while mooch implies taking without offering anything in return.
  • bum
    7.5 0.01/m
    bum is to ask casually for food or money from others, while mooch often suggests a parasitic, repeated habit.
  • sponge
    7.9 0.06/m
    sponge is to live off someone and take benefits regularly, while mooch can mean asking for small things without paying.
  • leech
    7.5 0.33/m
    leech is to take advantage of another without giving back and sounds very negative, while mooch is softer and casual.
  • grub
    8.5 0.02/m
    grub is to ask or scrounge for food or money in an informal way, while mooch covers general free-taking behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeload - to take advantage of others for free food, drink or entertainment
  • antonym: pay - to give money in exchange for something received
  • synonym: scrounge - to obtain something, usually food or money, by seeking it from others for free
