peel

verb
come off in thin flakes or pieces
3.0 4.86/m
Pronunciation: / pil / (AmE), / piÀêl / (BrE)
(of a surface or covering) to come off in thin flakes or small pieces, often as a result of age or damage.

Examples

Paint peels off
After a week at the beach, my sunburned skin began to peel.
The label will peel if you leave the bottle out in the sun.
The old posters started to peel from the walls after the rain.

Synonyms

  • peel off
    3.9 0.63/m
    peel off refers to a surface coming away in strips or flakes, while peel is the general verb for a covering coming off.
  • flake off
    3.9 0.30/m
    flake off describes small pieces breaking away from a surface, while peel often means larger strips or whole layers coming off.
  • flake
    2.6 1.08/m
    flake means pieces break away in thin, flat bits, while peel describes a surface coming off in strips or flakes.
  • exfoliate
    6.2 0.98/m
    exfoliate is a formal or technical term for shedding layers like skin or bark, while peel is everyday talk for surfaces coming off.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chip - to break or come off in small fragments
  • synonym: flake - to come off in thin small pieces
