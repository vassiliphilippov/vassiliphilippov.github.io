wheel

verb
to turn or change direction suddenly
4.1 3/m
Pronunciation: / wiÀêl / (AmE), / wiÀêl / (BrE)
to turn or move around in a circle or to change direction suddenly, like revolving on a pivot

Examples

To wheel around
She wheeled around to face the person who had called her name.
The cyclist wheeled left at the intersection, avoiding the traffic.
The soldiers wheeled sharply at their captain's command.
The birds wheeled above the lake in wide circles.
The skater wheeled gracefully across the ice, drawing patterns with her blades.
Clouds of dust rose as the horses wheeled around the field at full speed.

Synonyms

  • wheel around
    4.2 0.4/m
    wheel around means to turn quickly and change direction, often for vehicles, while wheel means to turn or pivot.
  • swivel
    4.7 3/m
    swivel is to turn on a fixed pivot or axis, while wheel describes turning or changing direction more generally.
  • swing around
    3.2 1/m
    swing around is to turn abruptly to face another direction, while wheel can mean turning or changing direction more slowly.
  • spin around
    2.1 3/m
    spin around is to revolve quickly and repeatedly on one's axis, while wheel can mean turning or changing direction less rapidly.
  • spin
    1.0 12/m
    spin means to turn very fast, often repeatedly, while wheel can mean turning or changing direction without great speed.
  • whirl around
    3.7 1/m
    whirl around means to move rapidly in a circular way through the air, while wheel often means to turn on a pivot.
  • whirl
    2.8 6/m
    whirl means to spin quickly in a circular motion, often without control, while wheel often means to turn with a purpose.
  • rotate
    3.0 11/m
    rotate means to turn around a fixed center or axis, usually in machines, while wheel often implies a sudden change of direction.
  • revolve
    3.2 4/m
    revolve means to move in a circular path around a central point, often steadily, while wheel can mean turning suddenly on a pivot.
