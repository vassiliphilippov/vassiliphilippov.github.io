wheel

verb
to turn or change direction suddenly
5.5
Pronunciation: / wiːl / (AmE), / wiːl / (BrE)
to turn or move around in a circle or to change direction suddenly, like revolving on a pivot

Examples

To wheel around
She wheeled around to face the person who had called her name.
The cyclist wheeled left at the intersection, avoiding the traffic.
The soldiers wheeled sharply at their captain's command.
The birds wheeled above the lake in wide circles.
The skater wheeled gracefully across the ice, drawing patterns with her blades.
Clouds of dust rose as the horses wheeled around the field at full speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circle - to move in a circular path around something
  • antonym: reverse - to turn completely in the opposite direction
  • synonym: turn - to move in a circular direction wholly or partly around an axis or point
  • synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly
