hovel

noun
small crude or dirty dwelling
8.3 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhʌ.vəl/ (AmE), /ˈhɒ.vəl/ /; / /ˈhʌ.vəl/ (BrE)
A small, simply built, often dirty or poorly constructed dwelling, typically considered unpleasant or unfit for habitation.

Examples

A hovel of sticks

Many poor families lived in a hovel on the edge of the village.
The abandoned hovel was falling apart and barely provided any protection from the weather.
