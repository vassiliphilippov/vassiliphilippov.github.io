sea slug

noun
shell-less, brightly colored marine gastropod
Pronunciation: / ˈsi ˌslʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌslʌɡ / (BrE)
any of various marine mollusks of the suborder Nudibranchia, typically without a shell and often brightly colored.

Examples

A bright sea slug
Divers were delighted to spot a vivid sea slug gliding over the coral reef.
Some sea slugs use their bright colors as a warning to predators.
The biology textbook included a chapter on the anatomy of sea slugs.
