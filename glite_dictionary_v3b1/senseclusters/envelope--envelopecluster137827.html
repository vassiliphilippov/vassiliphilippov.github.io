envelope

noun
a surrounding layer, especially gas or fluid
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.vəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛn.və.ləʊp /; / ˈenvələʊp /; / ˈɛnvələʊp / (BrE)
a layer, covering, or wrapper that surrounds or encloses something (such as a layer of gas, plastic, or the casing of a balloon)

Examples

Protective envelope layer
A protective envelope of atmosphere shields the planet from harmful radiation.
Scientists studied the envelope of plasma surrounding the star.
The comet's envelope was visible through the telescope as a faint halo of gas.
A plastic envelope covered the delicate machine to keep it safe from dust.
A thick fog formed an envelope around the entire city.
The nuclear envelope protects the genetic material in a cell.
After a tear in the envelope, the balloon quickly lost altitude.
Technicians inspected the envelope for leaks before the test flight.
The hot air envelope determines how high the balloon can ascend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balloon - a large bag filled with gas or heated air that rises and floats in the air
  • synonym: shell - a hard, protective outer layer, especially in animals or objects
