merchandise

verb
to trade or market goods
5.0 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…úrt É…ônÀåda…™z / (AmE), / Ààm…úÀêt É…ônÀåda…™z / (BrE)
to engage in the buying and selling of goods; to promote or market a product for sale.

Examples

To merchandise products
He has spent years learning how to effectively merchandise various consumer electronics.
Retailers often merchandise goods differently during the holiday season to attract more customers.
The company plans to merchandise its new line of eco-friendly products worldwide.

Synonyms

  • market
    2.8 14/m
    market means to promote and sell products to customers in a business setting, while merchandise covers selling and promoting goods more generally.
  • retail
    2.9 1.26/m
    retail is selling goods in small quantities directly to the public, while merchandise can mean buying, selling, or promoting products in general.
  • trade
    2.3 19/m
    trade is the act of buying and selling, often between businesses or markets, while merchandise adds the idea of promoting products for sale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buy - to acquire in exchange for payment
  • synonym: sell - to exchange something for money
