homology

noun
similarity in structure or position
5.5 7.7
Pronunciation: /hə.ˈmɑː.lə.dʒiː/ /; / /hoʊ.ˈmɑː.lə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /hə.ˈmɒl.ə.dʒiː/ /; / /hɒ.ˈmɒl.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
the state of having the same or a similar relation, position, structure, or function as something else, especially due to shared ancestry.

Examples

Homology in biology
The forelimbs of whales and bats display remarkable homology despite their different functions.
Their research compares the homology of various protein structures across multiple organisms.
Understanding genetic homology helps scientists trace the evolutionary relationships between species.
