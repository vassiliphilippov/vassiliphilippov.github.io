air gas

noun
manufactured fuel gas from air and coke
6.9 8.4
Pronunciation: /er gæs/ /; / /eər gæs/ (AmE), /eə gæs/ (BrE)
a manufactured gas composed mainly of carbon monoxide, hydrogen, and nitrogen, produced by passing air over heated coke, formerly used as a fuel

Examples

Air gas fuel
Because of its high carbon monoxide content, air gas requires careful handling.
In the late 19th century, air gas was widely used for lighting and heating.
The plant generated air gas by passing a stream of air over hot coke.
