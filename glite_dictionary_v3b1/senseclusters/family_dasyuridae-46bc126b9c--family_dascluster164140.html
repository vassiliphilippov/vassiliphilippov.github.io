family Dasyuridae

noun
a family of carnivorous marsupials
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli dæˌsjuːˈrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli dæsjʊˈrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of carnivorous marsupials native to Australia and New Guinea, including dasyures, native cats, pouched mice, banded anteaters, and Tasmanian devils.

Examples

The family Dasyuridae
Many of the most iconic Australian mammals belong to the family Dasyuridae.
Scientists studied the decline in several species within the family Dasyuridae due to habitat loss.
The Tasmanian devil is perhaps the most famous member of the family Dasyuridae.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marsupial - mammal of the order Marsupialia, typically carrying young in a pouch
