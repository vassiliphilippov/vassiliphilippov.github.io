waddle

verb
walk with short, swaying steps
7.5 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwɑː.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈwɒ.dəl/ (BrE)
to walk with short steps and a clumsy, swaying motion, like that of a duck

Examples

To waddle like a duck
After spraining his ankle, he could only waddle across the room.
She waddled around the house in her oversized boots.
The ducklings waddle after their mother along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps
  • synonym: toddle - to walk with short, unsteady steps, especially as a small child does
