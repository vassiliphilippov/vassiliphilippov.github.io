glide

noun
phonetic term for a vowellike consonant sound
4.6 6.2
Pronunciation: /glaɪd/ (AmE), /glaɪd/ (BrE)
a speech sound that is articulated like a vowel but functions as a consonant, such as the sounds represented by 'w' or 'y' in English

Examples

A glide sound
In the word 'yes,' the initial glide is the sound represented by 'y.'
Linguists classify 'w' and 'y' as glide sounds in English.
The glide in 'wait' connects the initial consonant to the vowel.
