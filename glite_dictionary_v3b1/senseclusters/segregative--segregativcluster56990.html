segregative

adjective
relating to genetic separation
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛɡrəˌɡeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡrɪɡətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or causing the separation of genetic material or alleles during inheritance or cell division

Examples

Segregative cell division
The scientist studied the segregative patterns of chromosomes during meiosis.
Certain genetic disorders are linked to segregative errors in cell division.
The segregative nature of allele distribution was evident in the experiment's results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assortative - relating to the pairing or grouping of similar or dissimilar genetic types
  • synonym: segregation - the process of separating or being separated
