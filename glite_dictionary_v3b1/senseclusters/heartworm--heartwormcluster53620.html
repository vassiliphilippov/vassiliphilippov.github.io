heartworm

noun
parasitic animal disease
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːr.twɜːrm/ (AmE), /ˈhɑː.twɜːm/ (BrE)
a parasitic worm that lives in the heart and blood vessels of animals, especially dogs, and causes serious disease

Examples

Heartworm disease
The veterinarian explained that heartworm can be fatal if left untreated in dogs.

If left untreated, heartworm can cause severe damage to a pet's heart and lungs.
Preventive medicine is the best way to protect your dog from heartworm.
