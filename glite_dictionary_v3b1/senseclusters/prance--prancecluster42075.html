prance

verb
move with springy, high steps (horse or person)
7.5
Pronunciation: / præns / (AmE), / prɑːns / (BrE)
(of a horse, or a person riding or making a horse) to move with high, springy steps in a lively or spirited manner

Examples

A horse prances high
She pranced onto the stage, full of energy and confidence.
The children pranced in their costumes during the parade.
The young colt loves to prance around the paddock every morning.
He taught his steed to prance on command, delighting the audience.
The trainer skillfully made the horse prance across the parade ground.
With a gentle tug on the reins, she urged the horse to prance forward.
As she pranced her horse around the ring, the crowd cheered her on.
The stallion began to prance as the parade passed by, drawing everyone's attention.
Young colts often prance excitedly when let out into the field.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: amble - to move at a relaxed, easy pace (of a horse)
  • degree higher: gallop - to move very fast, especially of a horse running
  • synonym: strut - to walk with a stiff, erect, and apparently arrogant or conceited gait
