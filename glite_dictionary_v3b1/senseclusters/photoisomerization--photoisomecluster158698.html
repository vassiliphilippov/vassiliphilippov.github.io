photoisomerization

noun
light-induced molecular change
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊaɪˌsɑːmərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊaɪˌsɒmərəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which light causes a molecule to change its structure by rearranging its atoms

In some plants, photoisomerization helps regulate growth in response to sunlight.
