butte

noun
steep, flat-topped hill
Pronunciation: / bjuːt / (AmE), / bjuːt / (BrE)
A steep, isolated hill with a flat top and sloping sides, rising abruptly from the surrounding landscape.

Examples

A butte hill
A dramatic butte dominates the skyline of Monument Valley.
Photographers are often drawn to the striking silhouette of a butte at sunset.
The hikers could see a lone butte rising above the prairie in the distance.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: mesa - an isolated, flat-topped elevation, larger than a butte
