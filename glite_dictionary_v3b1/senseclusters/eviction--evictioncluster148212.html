eviction

noun
legal removal from property
3.0 4.0
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈvɪk.ʃən/ /; / /ə.ˈvɪk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈvɪk.ʃən/ /; / /ə.ˈvɪk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of forcing someone to leave a property they are renting or occupying, usually by the owner or landlord

Examples

Eviction notice
After missing several rent payments, John faced eviction from his apartment.
Rising housing costs have led to an increase in evictions across major cities.
The family fought the eviction notice in court, hoping to stay in their home.
Following the landlord's intimidation tactics, the family faced an eviction without ever going to court.
She described the eviction as unofficial, since the apartment became unlivable after basic utilities were shut off.
The city is investigating cases of eviction where tenants were forced to leave due to neglected repairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone out
  • antonym: possession - having ownership or control of something
