Indian plantain

noun
North American plant of genus Cacalia
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈplænˌteɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈplænˌteɪn / (BrE)
Any of several plants of the genus Cacalia, having leaves that resemble those of the plantain.

Examples

Broad Indian plantain leaves
Gardeners appreciate the broad, veined leaves of the Indian plantain for their ornamental value.
The Indian plantain grows in moist meadows across parts of North America.
While hiking, she was able to identify an Indian plantain by its tall stalk and distinctive foliage.
