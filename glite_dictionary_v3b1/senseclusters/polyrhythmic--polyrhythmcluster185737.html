polyrhythmic

adjective
multiple rhythms
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈrɪðmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈrɪðmɪk / (BrE)
having or using two or more different rhythms played or sung at the same time

Examples

A polyrhythmic pattern
The drummer created a complex polyrhythmic pattern that challenged the other musicians.
African music often features polyrhythmic structures that make the songs lively and engaging.
The composer is known for his polyrhythmic style, blending several rhythms in one piece.
