minor fast day

noun
Jewish religious fast day with partial restrictions
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnɚ fæst deɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə fɑːst deɪ / (BrE)
a day of fasting observed in Judaism on five specific dates in the Jewish calendar, observed with partial restrictions compared to major fasts.

Examples

A minor fast day observed
During a minor fast day, work is permitted as usual unlike on Yom Kippur.
On a minor fast day, healthy adults abstain from food and drink from dawn until nightfall.
The Jewish calendar includes several minor fast days commemorating historical tragedies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: major fast day - a Jewish fast day with stricter observance, such as Yom Kippur or Tisha B'Av
