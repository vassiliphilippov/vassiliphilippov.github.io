stratocumulus

noun
low lumpy cloud
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌstrætoʊˈkjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˌstrætəˈkjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
a type of low, thick, lumpy cloud that often covers the entire sky in large patches

Examples

Thick stratocumulus clouds
A thick layer of stratocumulus clouds blocked the sun for most of the afternoon.
Pilots often report turbulence when flying through stratocumulus formations near the coast.
The weather forecast predicted widespread stratocumulus covering the region by evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cumulus - a type of fluffy, white cloud with a flat base
  • synonym: stratus - a type of low, gray, uniform cloud layer
