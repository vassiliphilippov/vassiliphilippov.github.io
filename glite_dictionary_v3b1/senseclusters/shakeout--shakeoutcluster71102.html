shakeout

noun
elimination of weaker market participants
7.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʃeɪ.kaʊt/ (AmE), /ˈʃeɪ.kaʊt/ (BrE)
an economic or business event in which weaker companies or participants are forced out of a market, typically due to increased competition or unfavorable conditions.

Examples

Market shakeout
A price war can trigger a shakeout among smaller competitors.
Investors predicted a shakeout in the tech sector following the rapid rise of startups.
The industry experienced a major shakeout after the financial crisis, with only the strongest companies surviving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consolidation - merging of smaller companies into larger ones, often after a shakeout
