eugenist

noun
supporter of eugenics
Pronunciation: / juˈdʒɛnɪst / (AmE), / juˈdʒɛnɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or studies the improvement of human genetic qualities, often through selective breeding

Examples

Human eugenist
In the early 1900s, a prominent eugenist promoted policies aimed at improving the population's genetic health.
The controversial views of the eugenist sparked debates among scientists and the public.
Many modern scientists reject the ideas once supported by eugenists due to ethical concerns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eugenicist - a person who advocates for or studies eugenics
  • synonym: geneticist - a scientist who studies genetics
