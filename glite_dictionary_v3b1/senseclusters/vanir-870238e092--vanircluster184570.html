Vanir

proper noun
ancient Norse gods (fertility, wisdom)
Pronunciation: / ˈvænɪr / (AmE), / ˈvænɪə / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, a group of ancient gods associated with fertility, wisdom, and nature, sometimes in conflict or alliance with the Aesir.

Examples

The Vanir gods
Freyr and Freyja are famous members of the Vanir in Norse legends.
In Norse myth, the Vanir were known for their powers over fertility and the earth.
The war between the Vanir and the Aesir ended with a truce and the exchange of hostages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Aesir - the principal group of Norse gods, often in conflict with the Vanir
