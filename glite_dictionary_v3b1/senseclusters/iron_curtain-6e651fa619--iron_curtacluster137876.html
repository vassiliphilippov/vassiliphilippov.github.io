iron curtain

noun
Cold War boundary of secrecy and censorship
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪərn ˈkɜrtən /; / ˈaɪɚn ˈkɝːtn̩ / (AmE), / ˌaɪən ˈkɜːtn̩ /; / ˈaɪən ˈkɜːtn̩ / (BrE)
a barrier of secrecy and censorship, especially the division between Eastern and Western Europe during the Cold War, or any impenetrable barrier between groups or ideologies

Examples

The iron curtain fell
During the Cold War, the iron curtain separated families and friends living on opposite sides of Europe.
Information could rarely pass through the iron curtain, due to strict government control and censorship.
Winston Churchill's speech popularized the term iron curtain to describe Soviet domination over Eastern Europe.
After the iron curtain fell, Eastern European countries began to embrace democratic reforms.
Many families were separated by the iron curtain for decades until new policies allowed for reunions.
The iron curtain was a powerful symbol of political and ideological division during the twentieth century.
