chirographic

adjective
about chirographs
Pronunciation: / ˌkaɪrəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌkaɪrəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to a chirograph, especially a historical legal document written in duplicate and divided into matching parts.

Examples

Chirographic document
The museum displayed a rare chirographic agreement from the twelfth century.
Scholars studied the chirographic records to understand medieval property laws.
A chirographic contract was often split so each party kept a matching piece.
