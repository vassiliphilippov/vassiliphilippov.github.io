swash

verb
move noisily or violently, splash
Pronunciation: / swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
to move or splash with force and noise, especially of water or objects

Examples

To swash water around
She swashing her broom angrily across the floor, making a loud racket.
The boat swashed through the rough waves, sending spray high into the air.
Water swashes against the rocks during the coming tide.
As the tide came in, the sea swashes against the rocky shoreline.
The gardener swashed water onto the hot pavement to cool it down.
Waves would often swash the deck during heavy storms.

  • synonym: splash - to strike and scatter liquid about noisily
