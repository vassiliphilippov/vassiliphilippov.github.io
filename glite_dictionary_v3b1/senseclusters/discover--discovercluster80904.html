discover

verb
become aware, often by accident
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskʌvɚ / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌvə / (BrE)
to find, learn about, or become aware of something for the first time, often unexpectedly or by chance

Examples

To discover a new place
He discovers something new about his family history every year.
I was surprised to discover that my neighbor speaks five languages.
She discovered her mistake only after submitting the assignment.
Historians often discover forgotten stories through old letters and photographs.
Scientists hope to discover new planets in other solar systems.
She was excited to discover an old diary in the attic.
In 1869, Dmitri Mendeleev helped discover several elements later added to the periodic table.
Researchers discover bacteria in environments previously thought to be sterile.
Scientists were thrilled to discover a new species of orchid in the rainforest.
