pseudorandom

adjective
seemingly random
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˈrændəm / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈrændəm / (BrE)
appearing to be random but actually generated by a specific, deterministic process

Examples

Pseudorandom sequences
The encryption algorithm relies on pseudorandom sequences to secure data.
A pseudorandom number generator is often used in computer simulations.
Although the results look unpredictable, they are actually pseudorandom and repeatable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deterministic - having outcomes that are completely determined by initial conditions
  • antonym: random - happening without a specific pattern or plan
