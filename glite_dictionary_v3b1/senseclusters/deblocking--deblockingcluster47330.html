deblocking

noun
removal of supports
6.8
Pronunciation: / diˈblɑkɪŋ / (AmE), / diˈblɒkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing blocks, supports, or packing materials used to stabilize goods or structures, especially after transport or construction

Examples

Deblocking the cargo
After the shipment arrived, the team began the deblocking of the cargo to prepare it for unloading.
Proper deblocking is essential to ensure that goods are not damaged when removing them from containers.
The construction crew handled the deblocking of the temporary supports before dismantling the scaffolding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blocking - the act of placing blocks or supports to stabilize goods or structures
  • synonym: unpacking - the act of removing items from a container or packaging
