retrofit

noun
adding a feature after original manufacture
9.7 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈre.troʊ.fɪt/ (AmE), /ˈre.trəʊ.fɪt/ (BrE)
the act of adding new parts, equipment, or features to something after it was built, or the new part or feature itself

Examples

A retrofit project
A new ventilation retrofit improved the air quality in the office building.
Government incentives often help fund the retrofit of inefficient heating systems.
The city completed a retrofit of older bridges to withstand earthquakes.
Adding a new filtration system as a retrofit can extend the life of industrial equipment.
Many homes have had a retrofit to meet updated safety standards.
The old factory received a retrofit to improve its energy efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: upgrade - an improvement to existing equipment or systems
