scramble

verb
mix or stir vigorously (esp. eggs)
2.1 2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈskræmbəl /; / ˈskræm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈskræmbl /; / ˈskræm.bəl / (BrE)
to mix or arrange things in a disordered or vigorous way, especially by stirring (such as eggs) or making things messy

Examples

To scramble eggs
First, scramble the eggs until they are light and fluffy.
He likes to scramble eggs every morning for breakfast.
You should scramble the batter thoroughly before pouring it into the pan.
He accidentally scrambled the papers, so they were out of order.
She likes to scramble the letters before starting the word puzzle.
The teacher asked the students to scramble their desks for the group activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat - to stir vigorously, especially in cooking
  • synonym: shuffle - to mix up or arrange in a random order, especially cards
