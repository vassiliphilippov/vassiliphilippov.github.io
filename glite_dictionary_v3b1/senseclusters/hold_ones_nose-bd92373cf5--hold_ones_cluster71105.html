hold one's nose

verb
endure something unpleasant
3.6
Pronunciation: / hoʊld wʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / həʊld wʌnz nəʊz / (BrE)
to do or accept something unpleasant or distasteful because it is necessary

Examples

To hold one's nose and accept
He decided to hold his nose and accept the job promotion, despite the extra stress.
Many voters felt they had to hold their noses and choose the lesser of two evils.
Sometimes you just have to hold your nose and do what needs to be done.
He didn't want to lie, but he decided to hold his nose and go along with the plan.
Sometimes you just have to hold your nose and finish the task, no matter how much you dislike it.
We may have to hold our noses and accept the proposal for the greater good.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bite the bullet - to do something difficult or unpleasant that one has been putting off or hesitating over
  • synonym: grin and bear it - to accept something unpleasant without complaining
  • synonym: put up with - to tolerate or endure
  • synonym: swallow the bitter pill - to accept or do something unpleasant or difficult
