thin

adjective
narrow in width
Pronunciation: / θɪn / (AmE), / θɪn / (BrE)
having little width or thickness; not thick or dense (used for objects, layers, lines, or substances)

Examples

A thin line
Please draw a thin line with your pencil.
She sliced the carrots into thin strips for the salad.
The thin streets made driving difficult in the old town.
A thin layer of fog hovered over the field in the early morning.
He grew a thin patchy mustache during the winter.
The forest was so thin that sunlight reached the ground easily.
A thin layer of snow covered the grass this morning.
Be careful with the thin glass; it can break easily.
She wore a thin sweater even though it was chilly outside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dense - closely packed together; opposite of thin (not dense)
  • synonym: narrow - of small width
  • synonym: slender - gracefully thin in shape or form
  • synonym: sparse - thinly dispersed or scattered; synonym for thin (not dense)
  • antonym: thick - having large width or being wide from side to side
