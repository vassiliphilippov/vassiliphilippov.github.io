monosymptomatic

adjective
one symptom only
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊsɪmpˈtædɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊsɪmpˈtætɪk / (BrE)
having or showing only one symptom of a disease or condition

Examples

Monosymptomatic patient
The doctor noted that the child was monosymptomatic, displaying only a high fever.
A monosymptomatic presentation can make diagnosis more challenging for physicians.
Some diseases may appear monosymptomatic in their early stages before other symptoms develop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polysymptomatic - having or showing multiple symptoms
