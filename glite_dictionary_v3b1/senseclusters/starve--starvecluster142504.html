starve

verb
to suffer or die from lack of food
Pronunciation: / stɑrv /; / stɑɹv / (AmE), / stɑːv / (BrE)
to suffer or die from not having enough food; to be extremely hungry

Examples

To starve all day
After hiking all day without lunch, I was beginning to starve.
If we do not get help soon, the villagers will starve.
Many animals starve during harsh winters when food is scarce.
If we don't get help soon, many could starve in the disaster zone.
Some animals starve when their natural food sources are destroyed.
Thousands of people began to starve during the prolonged famine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: famine - extreme scarcity of food
  • synonym: famish - to suffer from extreme hunger
  • antonym: feed - to give food to; to eat in order not to be hungry
