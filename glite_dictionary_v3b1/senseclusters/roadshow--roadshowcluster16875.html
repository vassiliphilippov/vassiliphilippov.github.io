roadshow

noun
promotional event series
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊdˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
a series of events or presentations held in different places to promote products, services, or a company, often to attract customers or investors

Examples

Investor roadshow
The marketing team planned a roadshow across five cities to launch the new product.
Investors attended the roadshow to learn more about the company's upcoming projects.
The roadshow attracted large crowds interested in the latest technology trends.
The company organized a roadshow to attract investors before launching its shares on the stock market.
During the roadshow, executives answered questions from potential investors in several major cities.
A successful roadshow can help generate interest and confidence in a company's upcoming public offering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conference - a formal meeting for discussion, often with presentations
  • synonym: presentation - an event where information is shown to an audience
  • synonym: tour - a journey to several places for a particular purpose
