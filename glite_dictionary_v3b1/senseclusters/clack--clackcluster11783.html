clack

noun
sharp, repeated striking noise
Pronunciation: / klæk / (AmE), / klæk / (BrE)
a sharp, abrupt noise, often caused by two objects striking together; can be repeated

Examples

The clack of heels
The clack of her high heels echoed down the marble hallway.
The clack of typewriter keys filled the otherwise silent office.
We heard the clack of billiard balls from the next room.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: clatter - a continuous rattling sound as of hard objects falling or striking each other
  • synonym: click - a short, sharp sound as made by a switch or latch
