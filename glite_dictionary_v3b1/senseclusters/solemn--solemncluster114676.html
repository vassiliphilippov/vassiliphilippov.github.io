solemn

adjective
serious and sincere; without humor
2.6 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑləm /; / ˈsɑː.ləm / (AmE), / ˈsɒləm /; / ˈsɒl.əm / (BrE)
serious, sincere, and formal in manner or appearance; showing no humor, especially in important situations or ceremonies

Examples

A solemn expression
His solemn expression told me that he was absolutely convinced of his decision.
She made a solemn vow to complete the project, no matter the obstacles.
The judge delivered the verdict in a solemn tone.
He made a solemn vow to always tell the truth in court.
The president delivered a solemn speech addressing the nation after the tragedy.
There was a solemn atmosphere in the church during the memorial service.
