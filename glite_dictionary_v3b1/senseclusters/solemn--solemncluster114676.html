solemn

adjective
serious and sincere; without humor
4.7 7.12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ël…ôm /; / Ààs…ëÀê.l…ôm / (AmE), / Ààs…íl…ôm /; / Ààs…íl.…ôm / (BrE)
serious, sincere, and formal in manner or appearance; showing no humor, especially in important situations or ceremonies

Examples

A solemn expression
His solemn expression told me that he was absolutely convinced of his decision.
She made a solemn vow to complete the project, no matter the obstacles.
The judge delivered the verdict in a solemn tone.
He made a solemn vow to always tell the truth in court.
The president delivered a solemn speech addressing the nation after the tragedy.
There was a solemn atmosphere in the church during the memorial service.

Synonyms

    1.4 18/m
    serious is not playful and focuses on an earnest attitude, while solemn is more formal and tied to ceremonies or important moments.
  • grave
    4.2 9.82/m
    grave is weighty and signals great importance, while solemn is formal and shows serious respect in ceremonies or important moments.
  • dignified
    4.6 4.30/m
    dignified has calm self-respect and wins respect from others, while solemn is more strictly formal and often without any humor.
  • sober
    4.6 3.61/m
    sober is sensible, calm, and without joking, while solemn adds formality and ceremony to that serious, sincere manner.
  • serious-minded
    4.7 0.08/m
    serious-minded is thoughtful and habitually earnest, while solemn describes a formal, sincere manner often used at ceremonies or official events.
  • sedate
    5.9 0.79/m
    sedate is calm, slow, and unhurried in behavior, while solemn is more formal and used for serious, important occasions.
