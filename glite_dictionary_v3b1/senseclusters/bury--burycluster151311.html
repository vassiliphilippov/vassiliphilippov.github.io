bury

verb
put deeply into or under something
2.5 1.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbe.riː/ /; / /ˈbʊ.riː/ /; / /ˈbʌ.riː/ (AmE), /ˈbe.riː/ /; / /ˈbʊ.riː/ /; / /ˈbʌ.riː/ (BrE)
to cover or hide something completely so that it cannot be seen

Examples

To bury treasure
He buried himself under a pile of blankets to keep warm.
She buried her face in her hands and wept silently.
The dog buried its bone deep in the garden.
After the avalanche, the entire village was buried under tons of snow.
Fog buried the city, obscuring all the buildings from view.
The paperwork on his desk buried him, making it impossible to find anything.
A thick layer of fog buried the city, making the skyline invisible.
Fresh snow quickly buried the footprints, erasing all trace of the hikers.
The little boy tried to bury his toy truck under a pile of leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embed - to fix firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
  • synonym: engulf - to sweep over and surround completely
  • antonym: expose - to make something visible by uncovering it
  • antonym: uncover - to remove a covering so something becomes visible
