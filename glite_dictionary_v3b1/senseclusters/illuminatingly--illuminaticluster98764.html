illuminatingly

adverb
decoratively, with embellishment
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːməˌneɪtɪŋli / (AmE), / ɪˈluːmɪneɪtɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that decorates or embellishes a text or object with illustrations, gold, or color, especially as in illuminated manuscripts

Examples

Illuminatingly decorated pages
The artist worked illuminatingly to add gold leaf and vibrant colors to the ancient manuscript.
Each page was illuminatingly adorned with intricate borders and miniature paintings.
The scribe wrote illuminatingly, turning the religious text into a work of art.

  • antonym: plainly - in a simple or undecorated manner
