sphincterotomy

noun
surgical muscle cutting
9.9
Pronunciation: / sfɪŋktəˈrɑːtəmi / (AmE), / sfɪŋktəˈrɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a sphincter muscle is cut to relieve constriction or improve function

Examples

A sphincterotomy surgery
After several failed treatments, the surgeon performed a sphincterotomy to relieve the patient's symptoms.
A sphincterotomy is often recommended for chronic anal fissures that do not heal with medication.
The doctor explained that a sphincterotomy could help improve muscle function and reduce pain.
