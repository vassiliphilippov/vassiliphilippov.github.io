infallibilism

noun
belief in certainty
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfæləbɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfælɪbɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical belief that some knowledge or beliefs are absolutely certain and cannot be mistaken

Examples

Philosophical infallibilism
The theory of infallibilism claims that some beliefs are immune to error.
Debates about infallibilism are common in epistemology courses at university.
Critics of infallibilism argue that certainty is impossible in human knowledge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fallibilism - the belief that all knowledge could be mistaken
