tow

verb
pull or drag behind (often a vehicle)
2.6
Pronunciation: / toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ / (BrE)
to pull or drag something, typically a vehicle, behind another vehicle or animal using a rope, chain, or similar device.

Examples

To tow a trailer
A small boat was towing a barge along the river early this morning.
If you park here, your vehicle may be towed away by the city authorities.
The mechanic had to tow the broken car to the nearest garage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drag - to pull something along the ground with effort or difficulty
  • antonym: push - to move something away from oneself by exerting force
