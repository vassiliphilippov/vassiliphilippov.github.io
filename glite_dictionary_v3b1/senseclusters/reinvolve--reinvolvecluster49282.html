reinvolve

verb
to become involved again
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈvɑːlv /; / ˌriːɪnˈvɑlv / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to become involved again, or to involve someone or something again, in an activity, situation, or group after a period away

Examples

To reinvolve yourself again
After several years away, she chose to reinvolve herself in local politics.
He wanted to reinvolve his team in the project after their initial withdrawal.
Many retired professionals reinvolve themselves in their fields through volunteer work.
After his suspension ended, the manager decided to reinvolve him in the project.
The organization worked hard to reinvolve former members in community events.
It took time to reinvolve the student in classroom activities after her long absence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exclude - to prevent someone from taking part in an activity or group
  • synonym: reengage - to participate again in something
  • antonym: withdraw - to stop being involved in something
