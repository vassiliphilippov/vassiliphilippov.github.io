connate

adjective
closely joined or united organs (esp. in biology)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒneɪt / (BrE)
(of similar parts or organs) closely joined or united, especially at the base, as in certain botanical or anatomical contexts.

Examples

Connate petals
In certain plant species, connate sepals provide extra protection for developing buds.
The leaves are connate at the base, making them look like a single leaf.
The petals of some flowers are connate, forming a tubular structure.
