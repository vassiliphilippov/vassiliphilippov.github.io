shy

adjective
wary; inclined to avoid
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʃaɪ / (AmE), / ʃaɪ / (BrE)
nervous, lacking self-confidence, or inclined to avoid people or situations

Examples

A shy child
Children can sometimes appear shy of new experiences until they feel comfortable.
He has always been shy of making investments in volatile markets.
Many animals remain shy of humans and quickly disappear into the woods.
As a shy child, he often avoided speaking in front of the class.
Even though she was shy, she managed to introduce herself at the party.
He became less shy once he got to know everyone in the group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bold - not afraid to take risks or face danger
  • synonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • antonym: confident - feeling or showing certainty about something
  • synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
