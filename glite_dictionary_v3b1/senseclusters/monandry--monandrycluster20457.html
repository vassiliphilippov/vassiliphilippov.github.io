monandry

noun
state of having one husband at a time
Pronunciation: / moʊˈnændri / (AmE), / məʊˈnændri / (BrE)
The practice or condition of having only one husband at a time.

Examples

Practice of monandry
Biologists studying insect mating habits often observe cases of monandry in various species.
Her research focused on the cultural implications of monandry in rural communities.
In certain societies, monandry is the only socially accepted form of marriage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polyandry - the state of having more than one husband at the same time
