meunière

noun
butter-fried dish
Pronunciation: / mɜːnˈjɛr / (AmE), / mɜːnˈjɛə / (BrE)
a dish, especially fish, prepared by dredging in flour and frying in butter, often served with lemon and parsley

Examples

A meuniere meal
The chef recommended the sole meunière for its delicate, buttery flavor.
She learned to make meunière after visiting a French cooking class.
At the restaurant, he ordered trout meunière with a side of vegetables.

Related Terms

  • synonym: almondine - a dish prepared with almonds, often similar in style to meunière
  • antonym: grilled - cooked over direct heat, not fried in butter
