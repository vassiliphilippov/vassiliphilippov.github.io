pluvial

noun
period of heavy rain
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpluːviəl / (AmE), / ˈpluːvɪəl / (BrE)
a period of increased rainfall, especially one that occurred in the geological past and affected climate and landscapes

Examples

An ancient pluvial
During the African pluvial, many now-dry lakes supported diverse wildlife.
Scientists study sediment layers to learn about each ancient pluvial and its impact on the environment.
The Sahara was much greener during the last pluvial thousands of years ago.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drought - a long period of abnormally low rainfall
  • synonym: interglacial - a period of warmer climate between ice ages
