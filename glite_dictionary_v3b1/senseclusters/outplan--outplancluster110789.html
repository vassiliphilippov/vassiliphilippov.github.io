outplan

verb
to plan better
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈplæn / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈplæn / (BrE)
to plan something better or more effectively than someone else

Examples

To outplan rivals
The team managed to outplan their rivals and secure the top spot in the competition.
If we want to win this project, we must outplan every other company bidding for it.
She always tries to outplan her opponents by thinking several steps ahead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outmaneuver - to gain an advantage by using clever planning
  • synonym: outsmart - to be more clever than someone else
  • antonym: underprepare - to prepare less thoroughly than needed
