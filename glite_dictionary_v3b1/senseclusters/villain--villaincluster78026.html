villain

noun
wicked or evil person
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪlən / (AmE), / ˈvɪlən / (BrE)
a person who is evil or does bad things, especially the main bad character in a story, play, or film

Examples

The villain in story
In the novel, the villain is eventually caught and punished for his crimes.
She played the villain in the school play with great enthusiasm.
The movie's villain plotted to take over the world.
Children at the party dressed up as their favorite heroes and villains from comic books.
She was cast as the villain in the television series, a role she relished playing.
The movie's villain plotted to destroy the city, but the hero managed to stop him in time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antagonist - a character who actively opposes the protagonist
  • synonym: criminal - a person who has committed a crime
  • antonym: hero - a person admired for their courage or noble qualities; the opposite of villain
  • synonym: scoundrel - a dishonest or unscrupulous person
