pterothorax

noun
wing-bearing thorax segments
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrəˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
the middle and rear segments of an insect's thorax, to which the wings are attached

Examples

Insect pterothorax wings
The wings of the dragonfly are attached to its pterothorax.
Scientists examined the pterothorax to understand how the beetle's wings function.
Damage to the pterothorax can prevent an insect from flying properly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prothorax - the front segment of an insect's thorax
