devilfish

noun
type of marine cephalopod (octopus or ray)
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛvəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛv.əl.fɪʃ / (BrE)
A cephalopod with a soft, oval body and eight long tentacles, typically living on the sea bottom; used especially for certain octopuses or rays.

Examples

Octopus-like devilfish
Fishermen were surprised to catch a large devilfish near the rocky shore.
The devilfish glided silently along the ocean floor, blending into its surroundings.
The documentary showed how the devilfish uses its tentacles to capture prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied, eight-armed marine cephalopod mollusk
