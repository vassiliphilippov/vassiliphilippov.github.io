Musa textilis

noun
Philippine banana species; source of Manila hemp
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmjuː.sə/ /; / /ˈmuː.sə/ (AmE), /ˈmjuː.sə/ /; / /ˈmuː.sə/ (BrE)
A species of banana native to the Philippines, known for its leafstalks which yield Manila hemp used in making rope and paper.

Examples

Musa textilis plant
Many farmers in the Philippines cultivate Musa textilis for its strong, durable fibers.
Ropes made from Musa textilis are valued for their resistance to saltwater.
The fiber extracted from Musa textilis plants is commonly referred to as Manila hemp.
