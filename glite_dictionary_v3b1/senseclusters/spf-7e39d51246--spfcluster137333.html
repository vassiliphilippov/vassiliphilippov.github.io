SPF

noun
measure of sunscreen protection level
4.3
Pronunciation: / ɛs pi ˈɛf / (AmE), / ɛs pi ˈɛf / (BrE)
a measure of how well a sunscreen protects the skin from ultraviolet (UV) rays; expressed as a number indicating the level of protection

Examples

SPF 50 sunscreen
Always choose a sunscreen with a high SPF for maximum protection during summer.
Dermatologists recommend reapplying SPF after swimming or sweating.
The bottle is labeled with an SPF of 30, which should be sufficient for everyday use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sunblock - a cosmetic preparation that protects the skin from UV rays
