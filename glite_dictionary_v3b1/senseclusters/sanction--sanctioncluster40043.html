sanction

noun
mechanism for enforcing societal standards
5.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsæŋk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈsæŋk.ʃən/ (BrE)
A measure or mechanism used to enforce rules, standards, or norms within a society, often through approval or disapproval.

Examples

Social sanction
In some cultures, gossip serves as a powerful sanction against unacceptable conduct.
Laws are a formal type of sanction that societies use to regulate behavior.
The threat of social sanction prevents many people from breaking community norms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punishment - Penalty given for breaking rules or laws
  • antonym: reward - A benefit given for following rules or meeting standards
