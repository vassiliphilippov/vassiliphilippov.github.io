water chinquapin

noun
North American water lily with edible seeds
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər tʃɪŋˌkwɑːpɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə tʃɪŋˌkwɒpɪn / (BrE)
A water lily native to eastern North America, known for its pale yellow flowers and edible, globular nutlike seeds.

Examples

Water chinquapin plant
Native Americans historically used the seeds of the water chinquapin as a food source.

We spotted several water chinquapins during our canoe trip through the marsh.
