lymphadenoma

noun
diseased, enlarged lymph node
Pronunciation: / lɪmˌfædəˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / lɪmfˌadɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
an abnormally enlarged lymph node, typically as a result of disease

Examples

A lymphadenoma swelling
A biopsy confirmed that the swelling was due to a lymphadenoma in his neck.
Doctors noted a lymphadenoma in the mediastinal area during the CT scan.
The patient's lymphadenoma was treated with targeted radiation therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lymphadenopathy - disease of the lymph nodes, often causing swelling
