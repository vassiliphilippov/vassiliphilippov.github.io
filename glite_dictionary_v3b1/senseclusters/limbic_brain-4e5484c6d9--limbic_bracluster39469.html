limbic brain

noun
part of brain involved in emotions and memory
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmbɪk breɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbɪk breɪn / (BrE)
The group of interconnected structures in the brain associated with emotions, memory, and behavior.

Examples

The limbic brain system
Damage to the limbic brain can result in changes to a person's emotional responses.
Many psychiatric disorders are believed to be linked to dysfunctions within the limbic brain.
The limbic brain plays a crucial role in processing emotions such as fear and pleasure.
