quayside

noun
area beside quay
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkiˌsaɪd /; / ˈkiːsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkiːsaɪd / (BrE)
the area or walkway next to a quay, often used for loading ships, walking, shopping, or relaxing by the water

Examples

The quayside area
The fishermen waited on the quayside for the boats to return with their catch.
Tourists strolled along the quayside, watching the ships being loaded with cargo.
Vendors set up their stalls on the quayside to sell fresh seafood to locals.
We enjoyed a long walk along the quayside after dinner.
Many new cafes have opened on the quayside in recent years.
The festival brought music and food stalls to the lively quayside.
Tourists enjoyed the lively atmosphere along the quayside on a sunny afternoon.
Several restaurants line the quayside, offering views of the boats in the harbor.
Artists often set up their easels on the quayside to paint the waterfront scene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waterfront - the land or district along the edge of a body of water
