foramen

noun
natural opening in bone or tissue
Pronunciation: / fəˈreɪmən / (AmE), / fəˈreɪmən / (BrE)
an opening or hole, especially one that occurs naturally in a bone or membranous structure, allowing the passage of nerves or vessels

Examples

A foramen in bone
A herniated disc can sometimes put pressure on a foramen and cause pain.
During surgery, the doctor identified a small foramen in the bone as the source of the issue.
The optic nerve passes through the foramen in the skull to reach the eye.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canal - a tubular passage or channel in the body
  • synonym: fossa - a depressed or hollow area, usually in a bone
