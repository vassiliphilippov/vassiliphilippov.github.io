easy

adjective
(finance) readily obtainable; not in high demand
Pronunciation: / ˈizi /; / ˈiːzi / (AmE), / ˈiːzi / (BrE)
not difficult to do, understand, or achieve; requiring little effort or skill

Examples

An easy task
Analysts noted that trading remained easy for copper all month.
Because gold was easy on the market, prices remained stable.
Commodities are easy this quarter due to decreased demand.
Compared to the mountain's north face, the southern route is much more easy.
The easy slope makes this hill ideal for sledding.
The trail offers an easy climb suitable for beginners.
She found the recipe easy to follow, even as a beginner.
The exam was so easy that most students finished early.
Winning the match was easy thanks to their excellent teamwork.
