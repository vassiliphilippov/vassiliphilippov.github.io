American arborvitae

noun
a small North American evergreen tree
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˌɑr.bərˈviː.tiː / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˌɑː.bəˈviː.tiː / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to eastern North America, having tiny scalelike leaves on flattened branchlets, commonly used for hedges and ornamentation.

Examples

The American arborvitae tree
The American arborvitae is popular for forming dense, green hedges in suburban gardens.
The flat, scale-like leaves make the American arborvitae easy to identify among other evergreens.
We planted several American arborvitae along the backyard fence for privacy.
