knotted

adjective
having knobs or knots

having a rough, twisted appearance with knobs or knots, especially when describing old trees or hands.

Examples

Knotted tree trunk
A knotted walking stick lay beside the fireplace, its surface worn and uneven.
The ancient oak tree had knotted branches stretching skyward, gnarled by centuries of storms.
The old man's knotted hands showed years of hard work in the fields.

Synonyms

  • gnarled
    7.1 1.72/m
    gnarled describes very old, twisted trees or hands with deep knobs and bumps, while knotted is a more general rough, knotted look.
  • gnarly
    8.4 0.43/m
    gnarly often describes rough, twisted trees or old skin with an informal tone, while knotted is a neutral description.
  • knotty
    7.0 0.40/m
    knotty means full of knots, lumps, or tangles in wood, hair, or skin, while knotted focuses on twisted shapes.
  • knobbed
    7.6 0.02/m
    knobbed means a surface covered with small rounded bumps you can feel, while knotted suggests visible twists and knots.
