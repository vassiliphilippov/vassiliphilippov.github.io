snap

verb
to seize quickly and eagerly
Pronunciation: / snæp / (AmE), / snæp / (BrE)
to quickly catch, grab, or seize something with a sudden motion (with hands or jaws)

Examples

To snap up tickets
She managed to snap the last ticket before they sold out.
The child reached out to snap a cookie from the plate.
The crocodile tried to snap at the bird sitting nearby.
Feeling threatened, the snake snapped its jaws shut.
He snapped his teeth together in frustration.
The dog snapped at the fly as it buzzed past its nose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: snatch - to quickly seize something in a sudden motion
