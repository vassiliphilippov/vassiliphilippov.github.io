rum

adjective
strange, odd, or peculiar (archaic)
Pronunciation: / rʌm / (AmE), / rʌm / (BrE)
(archaic, chiefly British) Strange, odd, or peculiar; deviating from what is expected or normal.

Examples

A rum character
He was always considered a rum fellow by the villagers.
It was a rum old house with creaking stairs and mysterious noises.
There's something rather rum about the way she looks at strangers.

  • synonym: peculiar - not usual or normal
