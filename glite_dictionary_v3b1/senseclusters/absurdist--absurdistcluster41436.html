absurdist

noun
creator of absurd works
8.1
Pronunciation: / əbˈsɝːdɪst / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːdɪst / (BrE)
a writer, artist, or performer who creates works that emphasize the absurd or meaningless aspects of life, often using illogical situations or dialogue

Examples

Absurdist playwright
The absurdist challenged the audience with a play full of strange and illogical events.
As an absurdist, she often used humor to highlight the confusion of modern life.
Many critics praised the absurdist for his unique approach to storytelling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realist - an artist or writer who represents things as they actually are
  • synonym: surrealist - an artist or writer who creates works with dreamlike or illogical qualities
