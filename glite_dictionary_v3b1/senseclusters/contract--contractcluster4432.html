contract

noun
self-executing blockchain program
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.trækt/ /; / /kən.ˈtrækt/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.trækt/ /; / /kən.ˈtrækt/ (BrE)
(in computing, especially blockchain) a program or piece of code deployed on a blockchain that automatically executes and enforces predefined rules or transactions without a central intermediary.

Examples

Blockchain contract
Developers deployed a contract on Ethereum to automate token transfers for the app.
Before sending funds, the startup audited the contract code to avoid security vulnerabilities.
A contract can automatically release payment when predefined conditions are met.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smart contract - a program on a blockchain that executes predefined rules automatically; often used interchangeably with 'contract' in developer contexts.
