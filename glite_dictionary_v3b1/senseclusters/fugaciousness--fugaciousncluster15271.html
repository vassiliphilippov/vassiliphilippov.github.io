fugaciousness

noun
short-lived quality, especially in plants
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /fjuː.ˈɡeɪ.ʃəs.nəs/ /; / /fuː.ˈɡeɪ.ʃəs.nəs/ (AmE), /fjuː.ˈɡeɪ.ʃəs.nəs/ /; / /fjʊ.ˈɡeɪ.ʃəs.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being transient or fleeting, especially referring to plant parts that do not last long

Examples

The fugaciousness of petals
Botanists note the fugaciousness of petals that drop quickly after blossoming.
The fugaciousness of certain flowers means they bloom and fade within a single day.
The fugaciousness of those blossoms makes their brief appearance all the more cherished.

  • synonym: transience - the state or fact of lasting only for a short time
