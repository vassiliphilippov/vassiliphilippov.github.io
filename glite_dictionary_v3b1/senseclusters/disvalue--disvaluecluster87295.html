disvalue

verb
to consider unimportant
8.4
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈvæljuː / (AmE), / dɪsˈvæljuː /; / dɪsˈvaljuː / (BrE)
to consider something as having little or no value, or to reduce its value or importance

Examples

To disvalue old ideas
Some people disvalue traditional skills in favor of modern technology.
It is unfair to disvalue someone's opinion just because you disagree.
The new policy may unintentionally disvalue the contributions of older employees.
Neglecting regular maintenance can disvalue your car and make it harder to sell.
Unfounded rumors may disvalue a company's reputation in the market.
New regulations could disvalue certain types of property in the city.
The government decided to disvalue the national currency to make exports more competitive.
After the scandal, investors began to disvalue the company's shares.
Economic instability can disvalue assets that were once considered safe investments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciate - to recognize the value or importance of something
  • synonym: depreciate - to decrease in value over time
  • synonym: devalue - to reduce or underestimate the worth of something
