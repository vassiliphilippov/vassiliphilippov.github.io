Advaita

noun
non-dual Hindu philosophy
Pronunciation: / ədˈvaɪtə / (AmE), / ədˈvaɪtə / (BrE)
a Hindu philosophy that teaches that the self and ultimate reality are one and not separate (non-dualism)

Examples

Advaita philosophy
Many spiritual seekers are drawn to Advaita for its emphasis on unity and self-realization.
The teachings of Advaita have influenced many modern philosophers and thinkers.
He attended a lecture on Advaita to learn about the concept of non-duality in Hinduism.
Many spiritual seekers study Advaita to understand the concept of oneness in Hindu philosophy.
The teachings of Advaita emphasize that the self and the absolute are not separate.
Adi Shankara is known for establishing Advaita as a central tradition in Indian thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Vedanta - a major school of Hindu philosophy based on the Upanishads
  • antonym: dualism - the belief that reality consists of two fundamental parts
  • synonym: non-dualism - the belief that all things are ultimately one
