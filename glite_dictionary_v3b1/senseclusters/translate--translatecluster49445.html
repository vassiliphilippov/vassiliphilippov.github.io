translate

verb
change from one form or medium into another
1.0
Pronunciation: / trænˈsleɪt, ˈtrænzleɪt /; / trænˈsleɪt, ˈtrænsleɪt / (AmE), / trɑːnzˈleɪt, ˈtrænzleɪt /; / trænsˈleɪt, ˈtrænsleɪt / (BrE)
to change something from one form, medium, style, or level of complexity into another

Examples

To translate ideas
Modern technology can translate digital images into physical objects with a 3D printer.
She translates her emotions into beautiful paintings.
The director translated the novel into a powerful stage performance.
Could you translate this technical report into language I can actually understand?
Teachers often need to translate complicated theories for their students.
The doctor translated the diagnosis for the patient in simple terms.
