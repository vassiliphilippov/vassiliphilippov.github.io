Creek Confederacy

noun
historical Native American tribal alliance
9.9
Pronunciation: / kriːk kənˈfɛd(ə)rəsi / (AmE), / kriːk kənˈfɛd(ə)rəsi / (BrE)
a historical confederation of Native American tribes, led by the Muskogee (Creek) people, that dominated the southeastern United States before their relocation to Oklahoma

Examples

The Creek Confederacy tribes
After the treaties, members of the Creek Confederacy were forced to relocate to Oklahoma.
Many descendants of the Creek Confederacy still maintain their cultural traditions today.
The Creek Confederacy played a significant role in shaping the history of the southeastern United States.
