nonattachment

noun
lack of attachment
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnəˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnəˈtætʃmənt / (BrE)
the state or practice of not being emotionally or physically attached to people, things, desires, or outcomes, often for spiritual growth or inner peace

Examples

Practice of nonattachment
Many spiritual traditions encourage nonattachment to reduce suffering and find inner peace.
Her practice of nonattachment allowed her to handle disappointment calmly.
Learning nonattachment can help people cope with changes in life.
Many spiritual traditions teach nonattachment as a way to achieve lasting happiness.
Through meditation, she learned the value of nonattachment to material things.
Practicing nonattachment helped him remain calm during difficult situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attachment - a strong emotional or physical connection to someone or something
  • synonym: detachment - a state of being objective or uninvolved
