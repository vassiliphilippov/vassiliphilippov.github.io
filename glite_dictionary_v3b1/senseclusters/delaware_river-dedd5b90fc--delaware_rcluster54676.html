Delaware River

proper noun
major river in the eastern United States
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛləˌwɛr ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdɛləwɛə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A major river in the eastern United States, rising in southeastern New York and flowing south along the borders of Pennsylvania, New York, and New Jersey before emptying into Delaware Bay.

Examples

The Delaware River valley
Boaters enjoyed a sunny afternoon paddling down the Delaware River.
Many historic events during the American Revolution took place along the Delaware River.
The Delaware River serves as a natural boundary between Pennsylvania and New Jersey.
