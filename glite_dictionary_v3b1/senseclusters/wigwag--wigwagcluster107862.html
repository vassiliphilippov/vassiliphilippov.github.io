wigwag

verb
signal with a flag, light, or similar object
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪɡˌwæɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡˌwæɡ / (BrE)
to signal by waving a flag, light, or other object according to a code

Examples

To wigwag a flag
During the blackout, the guard wigwagged a lantern to send a message across the river.
Engineers wigwagged flags to direct the troop movements.
To alert the rescue team, he wigwagged a flashlight from the hillside.
During the demonstration, she wigwagged a lantern to alert the approaching ship.
The soldier began to wigwag instructions to his comrades across the field.
They wigwagged with their flags to communicate the message across the valley.
