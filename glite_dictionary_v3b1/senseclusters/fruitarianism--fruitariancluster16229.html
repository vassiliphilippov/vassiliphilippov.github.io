fruitarianism

noun
fruit-based diet
Pronunciation: / fruːˈtɛriənɪzəm /; / fruːˌtɛriˈænɪzəm / (AmE), / fruːˈtɛəriənɪzəm /; / fruːˌtɛəriˈænɪzəm / (BrE)
a diet or lifestyle in which a person eats only or mainly fruit (sometimes with nuts and seeds), sometimes for ethical or spiritual reasons

Examples

Fruitarianism diet
After learning about fruitarianism, he decided to try eating only raw fruits for a month.
Some people choose fruitarianism because they believe it is healthier and more natural.
The documentary explored the challenges and benefits of fruitarianism as a lifestyle.
She adopted fruitarianism after learning about its environmental benefits.
Some people choose fruitarianism for spiritual or ethical reasons rather than health concerns.
Debates about the health effects of fruitarianism are common among nutritionists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: omnivorism - a diet including both plant and animal foods
  • synonym: veganism - a diet and lifestyle avoiding all animal products
  • synonym: vegetarianism - a diet that excludes meat but may include dairy and eggs
