noun
a freshwater fish, the burbot
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêt / (AmE), / Ààl…ít / (BrE)
A freshwater fish, also called the burbot, with a large mouth and a wormlike filament used to lure prey; found in cold rivers and lakes in northern regions.

Examples

A large lotte fish

Scientists studied the lotte to understand how its wormlike filament attracts prey.
The lotte is prized by anglers in northern Europe for its delicate flavor.
