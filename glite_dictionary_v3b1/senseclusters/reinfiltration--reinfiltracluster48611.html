reinfiltration

noun
water re-entering soil
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɪnfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of water soaking back into the ground after having previously moved away

Examples

Soil reinfiltration process
After the initial storm, reinfiltration helped restore moisture to the dry soil.
Engineers studied reinfiltration rates to improve groundwater management in the region.
Proper landscaping can encourage reinfiltration and reduce surface water runoff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infiltration - the process of water entering the soil
  • antonym: runoff - water that flows over the ground surface instead of soaking in
