remaster

verb
to improve recording quality
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈmæstər /; / ˌriːˈmæstər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmɑːstə / (BrE)
to create a new version of a recording, film, or game by improving its sound or image quality using new technology

Examples

To remaster a movie
The band decided to remaster their debut album for its twentieth anniversary release.
Many classic films are remastered to enhance their picture and sound for modern audiences.
She worked on remastering old recordings to preserve them for future generations.
The studio decided to remaster the classic movie for its 30th anniversary release.
They plan to remaster the old video game with updated graphics and sound.
After many requests, the band will remaster their debut album for digital platforms.
The studio decided to remaster the album to celebrate its twentieth anniversary.
Many classic films have been remastered for high-definition television and streaming services.
Fans were excited when the video game company announced plans to remaster their favorite title.

Related Terms

  • antonym: degrade - to make the quality of something worse
  • synonym: remake - to make a new version of something, especially a film or song
  • synonym: remix - to create a new version of a recording by altering or adding to the original
  • synonym: restore - to bring something back to its original condition
