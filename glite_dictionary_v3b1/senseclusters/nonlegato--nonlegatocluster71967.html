nonlegato

adjective, adverb
separated musical notes
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnleɪˈɡɑːtoʊ /; / nɑn lɛˈɡɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnleɪˈɡɑːtəʊ /; / nɒn lɛˈɡɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
in music, played or to be played with slight separation between notes, not smoothly connected

Examples

Nonlegato playing style
The pianist performed the passage in a nonlegato style, making each note distinct.
When the conductor asked for nonlegato articulation, the strings played with gentle separation.
Some pieces require a nonlegato approach to highlight the rhythm and phrasing.
The violinist performed the melody nonlegato to create a clear separation between each note.
In this section, the composer instructs the orchestra to play nonlegato for a distinct effect.
She practiced the scale nonlegato as required for her music exam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legato - played smoothly and connected, with no breaks between notes
  • synonym: staccato - played with each note sharply separated from the others
