mise en place

noun
preparation and organization
6.9
Pronunciation: /miːz ən pleɪs/ /; / /maɪz ən pleɪs/ (AmE), /miːz ən pleɪs/ /; / /maɪz ən pleɪs/ (BrE)
the preparation and arrangement of ingredients and tools before cooking to ensure everything is ready and organized

Examples

mise en place preparation
Chefs rely on mise en place to ensure every ingredient is ready before cooking begins.
Her mise en place included organizing all the tools and materials needed for the art class.
Good mise en place can make any project run more smoothly and efficiently.
Professional chefs rely on mise en place to keep their kitchens running smoothly during busy dinner services.
Before starting the recipe, she completed her mise en place by measuring and arranging all the ingredients.
Learning the importance of mise en place helped him become a more organized and efficient home cook.
