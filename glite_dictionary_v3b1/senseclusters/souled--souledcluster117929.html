souled

adjective
having a soul
Pronunciation: / soʊld / (AmE), / səʊld / (BrE)
having a soul or spiritual essence; possessing an immaterial or non-physical nature.

Examples

Souled being
Many faiths teach that every person is a souled being with inherent worth.
The story describes a world where only a few souled creatures can communicate with the gods.
He believed that all living things, even animals, are souled and deserve respect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soulless - lacking a soul or spiritual essence
  • synonym: spiritual - relating to the soul or spirit
