Marcuse

proper noun
German-American political philosopher
9.1
Pronunciation: / mɑrˈkuːzə / (AmE), / mɑːˈkuːzə / (BrE)
Herbert Marcuse (1898–1979), a German-born American political philosopher known for his critiques of capitalism and modern technology.

Examples

Marcuse political philosopher
Marcuse argued that modern technology could contribute to social control and alienation.
In his writings, Marcuse called for a revolutionary change in society.
Many students in the 1960s were influenced by the radical ideas of Marcuse.
