have the nerve

verb
to have courage or audacity
3.6 5.0
Pronunciation: /hæv ðə nɜːrv/ /; / /həv ðiː nɜːrv/ (AmE), /hæv ðə nɜːv/ /; / /həv ðiː nɜːv/ (BrE)
to have enough courage or audacity to do something considered bold, difficult, or presumptuous

Examples

To have the nerve to speak
He didn't have the nerve to speak in front of the entire audience.
I can't believe you have the nerve to ask for another favor after all I did for you.
If you have the nerve to tell her the truth, she might respect you more.
