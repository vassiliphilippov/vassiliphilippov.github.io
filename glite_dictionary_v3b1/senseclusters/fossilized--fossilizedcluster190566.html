fossilized

adjective
rigidly fixed or outdated (figurative)
7.0 0.28/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ëÀês…ôÀåla…™zd / (AmE), / Ààf…ís…ôÀåla…™zd /; / Ààf…ís…™la…™zd / (BrE)
(of ideas, habits, or systems) rigidly fixed and unable to change; having become unchangeable or outdated through lack of development.

Examples

Fossilized ideas
His fossilized beliefs prevented him from accepting new ideas.
Some educational systems are so fossilized that they resist any form of innovation.
The company was slow to adapt due to its fossilized management structure.
His views on education are considered fossilised by younger teachers.
Some traditions have become so fossilised that people follow them without question.
The company's fossilised culture makes it difficult to accept any new ideas.

Synonyms

    9.3 0.16/m
    ossified describes ideas stuck in old ways and unwilling to change, while fossilized implies they became rigid through lack of development.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: obsolete - no longer produced or used; out of date
  • synonym: ossified - (of systems, attitudes, or institutions) having become rigid and unchangeable
