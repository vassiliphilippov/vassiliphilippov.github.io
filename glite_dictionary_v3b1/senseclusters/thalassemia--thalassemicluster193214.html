thalassemia

noun
inherited blood disorder causing anemia
4.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌθæl.əˈsiː.mi.ə /; / θəˌlæsəˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˌθæl.əˈsiː.mi.ə /; / θəˌlasɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
A hereditary blood disorder characterized by reduced, absent, or otherwise abnormal hemoglobin production, leading to anemia.

Examples

Thalassemia blood disorder
Children with thalassemia may require regular blood transfusions throughout their lives.
Researchers are developing new treatments for thalassemia to improve patients' quality of life.
The doctor diagnosed her with thalassemia after a series of blood tests.
Children born with thalassaemia often require regular blood transfusions to manage their condition.
Genetic counseling is recommended for couples who may carry the thalassaemia gene.
The doctor explained that thalassaemia is most prevalent in people of Mediterranean descent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anemia - a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells or hemoglobin
