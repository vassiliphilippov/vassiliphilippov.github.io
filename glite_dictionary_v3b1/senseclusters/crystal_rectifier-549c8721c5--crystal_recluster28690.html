crystal rectifier

noun
semiconductor device (p-n junction diode)
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstl ˈrɛktəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl ˈrɛktɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A semiconductor device comprising a p-n junction, used to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC).

Examples

A crystal rectifier device
A crystal rectifier is commonly used in power supply circuits to convert AC to DC.
Early radios often relied on the crystal rectifier to detect radio signals.
The efficiency of a crystal rectifier makes it ideal for electronic applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diode - an electronic device that allows current to flow in one direction only
