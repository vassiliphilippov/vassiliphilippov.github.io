excerpt

verb
take or cite part from a work
9.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /ek.ˈsɜːrpt/ /; / /ɪk.ˈsɜːrpt/ (AmE), /ek.ˈsɜːpt/ /; / /ɪk.ˈsɜːpt/ (BrE)
to take a short extract from a text, film, or piece of music and use it elsewhere, especially for citation or reference

Examples

To excerpt a passage
She excerpted a few lines from the poem for her essay.
The article excerpts key sections from the original report for analysis.
The teacher excerpted several passages from the novel to discuss in class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quote - to repeat or copy a group of words from a text, typically with an indication of the original author
