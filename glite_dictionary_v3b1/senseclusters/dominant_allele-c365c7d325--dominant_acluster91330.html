dominant allele

noun
an allele that determines phenotype regardless of pair
7.7 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.mə.nənt ə.ˈliːl/ /; / /ˈdɑː.mɪ.nənt ə.ˈliːl/ (AmE), /ˈdɒm.ɪ.nənt ə.ˈliːl/ /; / /ˈdɒm.ɪ.nənt ˈæl.iːl/ (BrE)
an allele that expresses its trait in the phenotype whether paired with an identical or a different allele

Examples

A dominant allele trait
If a child inherits a dominant allele for brown eyes, their eyes will typically be brown.
Scientists studied how the dominant allele affected the plant's flower color.
The presence of a dominant allele masks the expression of a recessive one.
