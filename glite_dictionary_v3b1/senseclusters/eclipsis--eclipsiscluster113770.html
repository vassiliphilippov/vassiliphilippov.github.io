eclipsis

noun
omission of word or sentence parts
Pronunciation: / ɪˈklɪp.sɪs / (AmE), / ɪˈklɪp.sɪs / (BrE)
the omission or suppression of parts of words or sentences, especially in speech or writing

Examples

Use of eclipsis in speech
Her use of eclipsis in the letter made some passages hard to interpret.
In some dialects, eclipsis is a common feature in casual speech, omitting unstressed syllables.
The poet used eclipsis to create a sense of mystery and brevity in the verse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ellipsis - the omission of words that are understood in context
