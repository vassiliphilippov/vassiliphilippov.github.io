Levi-Lorrain dwarf

noun
person with short stature from hormone deficiency
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåliÀêva…™ l…îÀêÀàre…™n dw…îrf / (AmE), / ÀåliÀêvi l…íÀàre…™n dw…îÀêf / (BrE)
A person of unusually short stature whose condition is caused by a deficiency of growth hormones, rather than by genetic factors such as achondroplasia.

Examples

A Levi-Lorrain dwarf child
The child was diagnosed as a Levi-Lorrain dwarf after tests showed a lack of growth hormone.
The doctor explained the difference between a Levi-Lorrain dwarf and other forms of dwarfism to the family.
Unlike an achondroplastic dwarf, a Levi-Lorrain dwarf may benefit from hormone therapy.

Synonyms

  • pituitary dwarf
    8.7 0.00/m
