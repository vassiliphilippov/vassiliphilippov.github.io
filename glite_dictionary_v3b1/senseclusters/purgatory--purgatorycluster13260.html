purgatory

noun
state or place for posthumous purification
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜːrɡəˌtɔːri / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(in Roman Catholic theology) a place or state after death where souls are purified to achieve the holiness necessary to enter heaven.

Examples

Souls in purgatory
Catholic doctrine teaches that some souls enter purgatory before reaching heaven.
He described the experience as a kind of emotional purgatory between hope and despair.
The church holds special masses to pray for those suffering in purgatory.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: heaven - the place regarded in various religions as the abode of God and the angels
  • degree lower: hell - a place regarded in religions as a realm of eternal suffering for the wicked after death
