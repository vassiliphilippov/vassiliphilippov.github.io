cost-pull inflation

noun
inflation caused by higher production costs
Pronunciation: / kɑst pʊl ɪnˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒst pʊl ɪnˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
A rise in the general price level caused by increases in the costs of production, such as wages or raw materials.

Examples

Cost-pull inflation rise
A surge in wages and raw material prices can trigger cost-pull inflation in an economy.
Many economists attribute current price increases to cost-pull inflation rather than excessive consumer demand.
The central bank raised interest rates to counter cost-pull inflation driven by rising oil prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demand-pull inflation - inflation resulting from increased demand rather than increased costs
