commoner

noun
person without a noble title
4.2 1.69/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀê.m…ôn.…ö / (AmE), / Ààk…ím.…ôn.…ô / (BrE)
a person who does not have a title of nobility; an ordinary member of society.

Examples

A commoner in society
Despite being a commoner, she married into a royal family.
In medieval Europe, a commoner was rarely able to rise above his social status.
The law was applied differently to nobles and commoners alike.

Synonyms

  • common person
    1.4 0.09/m
    common person is an ordinary member of society used in everyday speech, while commoner names someone without a noble title.
  • nonnoble
    7.4 0.01/m
    nonnoble describes someone without noble birth or rank and sounds more formal or technical, while commoner is the everyday word.
  • plebeian
    7.9 0.31/m
    plebeian is a more formal or slightly negative word for ordinary people, whereas commoner is a neutral term for those without titles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: noble - a person of noble rank or birth
