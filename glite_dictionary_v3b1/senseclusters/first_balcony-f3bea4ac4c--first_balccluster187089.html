first balcony

noun
lowest balcony in a theater
2.7 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst ˈbæl.kə.ni / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈbæl.kə.ni / (BrE)
the first or lowest tier of seats projecting from the wall of a theater above the main floor (orchestra)

Examples

Seats in the first balcony
She reserved two seats in the first balcony for the opening night performance.
The theater's first balcony is accessible by stairs near the main entrance.
Tickets for the first balcony often offer a good view at a lower price than orchestra seats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mezzanine - a low-ceilinged story between two main stories, often refers to a seating area in a theater above the orchestra and below the balcony
  • antonym: orchestra - the main floor of a theater, closest to the stage
  • degree higher: upper balcony - a tier of seats above the first balcony in a theater
