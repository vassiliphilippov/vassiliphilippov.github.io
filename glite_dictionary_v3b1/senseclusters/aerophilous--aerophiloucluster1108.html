aerophilous

adjective
dependent on air or oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˌɛr.əˈfɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˌɛərəʊˈfɪl.əs / (BrE)
(of an organism or process) requiring or depending on the presence of free oxygen or air.

Examples

Aerophilous bacteria
Certain bacteria are aerophilous and thrive only in oxygen-rich environments.
Scientists studied the aerophilous aspects of this microorganism to understand its respiration.
The aerophilous properties of the fungus make it suitable for laboratory cultivation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anaerobic - not requiring oxygen; able to occur without air
