solitariness

noun
preference for being alone
9.3 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsɑː.lɪ.tə.rɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈsɒl.ɪ.tə.rɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the state or quality of being alone or preferring to be alone; solitude

Examples

The solitariness of a person
His solitariness often puzzled those who could not understand his preference for quiet moments alone.
Some people embrace solitariness as a way to recharge and reflect on their thoughts.
Throughout her childhood, solitariness was her companion, shaping her love of books and nature.
Long stretches of solitariness can be both peaceful and challenging to endure.
She sometimes craved solitariness after spending hours surrounded by crowds.
The writer cherished the solitariness of his mountain cabin, finding inspiration in its silence.

  • antonym: gregariousness - the quality of enjoying the company of others
  • synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, especially when it is peaceful or pleasant
