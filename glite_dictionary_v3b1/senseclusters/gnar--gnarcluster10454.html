gnar

noun
tree knot or lump
Pronunciation: / nɑr / (AmE), / nɑː / (BrE)
a knot, lump, or rough swelling on the trunk or branch of a tree, often caused by injury or growth irregularity

Examples

Tree gnar
The old oak tree had a large gnar near its base that made it unique.
Woodworkers sometimes use a gnar from a tree to create decorative objects.
A bird built its nest in the shelter of a gnar on the branch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burl - a rounded outgrowth on a tree trunk or branch
  • synonym: knot - a hard, cross-grained mass of wood where a branch was
