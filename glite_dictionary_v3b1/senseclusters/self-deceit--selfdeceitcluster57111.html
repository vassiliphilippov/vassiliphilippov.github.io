self-deceit

noun
deceiving oneself; self-delusion
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf.dɪˈsit / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.dɪˈsiːt / (BrE)
The act of deceiving oneself by refusing to acknowledge or accept the truth about something unpleasant or undesirable.

Examples

His self-deceit
His self-deceit prevented him from seeing his own faults.
It can be hard to overcome self-deceit when facing uncomfortable truths.
The novel explores the dangers of self-deceit in personal relationships.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: deceit - the act of deceiving others
  • antonym: self-awareness - conscious knowledge of one's own character, feelings, motives, and desires
  • synonym: self-delusion - the action of deceiving oneself or being deluded about one's own abilities or situation
