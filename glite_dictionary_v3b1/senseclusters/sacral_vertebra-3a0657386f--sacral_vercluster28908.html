sacral vertebra

noun
one of five fused vertebrae forming the sacrum
3.4 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈseɪ.krəl ˈvɜːr.tə.brə/ /; / /ˈsæ.krəl ˈvɜːr.tə.brə/ (AmE), /ˈseɪ.krəl ˈvɜː.tɪ.brə/ /; / /ˈsæ.krəl ˈvɜː.tɪ.brə/ (BrE)
any of the five vertebrae in the lower back that fuse in adulthood to form the sacrum in humans and many other vertebrates.

Examples

A sacral vertebra bone
An injury to a sacral vertebra can affect nerve function in the lower body.
During embryonic development, each sacral vertebra is distinct before forming the sacrum.
The sacral vertebra fuse together during adulthood to create a strong base for the spine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cervical vertebra - any of the seven vertebrae in the neck region
  • synonym: lumbar vertebra - any of the five vertebrae between the rib cage and the pelvis
  • synonym: sacrum - a triangular bone at the base of the spine formed by the fusion of sacral vertebrae
