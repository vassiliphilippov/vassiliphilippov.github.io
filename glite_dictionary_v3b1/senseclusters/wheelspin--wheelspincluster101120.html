wheelspin

verb
to spin wheels without grip
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːlˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌspɪn / (BrE)
to cause a vehicle's wheels to spin rapidly without moving forward, usually due to lack of traction.

Examples

To wheelspin the car
He tried to wheelspin the car, but the slippery surface prevented any gains.
If you wheelspin on ice, you might lose control of the vehicle.
The driver accidentally wheelspun when starting too quickly at the traffic light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accelerate - to increase speed
  • synonym: skid - to slide uncontrollably on a slippery surface
