estop

noun
legal bar to claims
Pronunciation: / ɪˈstɑp / (AmE), / ɪˈstɒp / (BrE)
a legal bar that prevents a person from making claims or arguments that contradict their previous actions or statements

Examples

Legal estop order
The court issued an estop to prevent the defendant from denying the agreement.
Without an estop, the company could have changed its position in the dispute.
The lawyer referenced the estop as a key reason for dismissing the claim.

Related Terms

  • synonym: estoppel - a legal principle preventing contradictory claims
  • synonym: waiver - the voluntary relinquishment of a right
