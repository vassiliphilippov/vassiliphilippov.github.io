caisson disease

noun

A medical condition caused by rapid reduction in air pressure, leading to pain and other symptoms such as joint pain and dizziness; also known as decompression sickness.

Examples

Caisson disease symptoms
Divers must ascend slowly to avoid caisson disease after spending time underwater.
Symptoms of caisson disease can include joint pain, dizziness, and fatigue.
The development of safer pressurization techniques greatly reduced the risk of caisson disease among workers.
