focal ratio

noun
ratio of focal length to lens diameter
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊ.kəl ˈreɪ.ʃi.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.kəl ˈreɪ.ʃi.əʊ / (BrE)
The ratio of the focal length of a lens or mirror to its diameter, used to describe the speed and brightness of optical systems.

Examples

The focal ratio of lens
A lower focal ratio results in a brighter image, making it suitable for astrophotography.
Modern cameras often display the focal ratio on the lens barrel for easy reference.
The telescope’s focal ratio determines its field of view and image brightness.
