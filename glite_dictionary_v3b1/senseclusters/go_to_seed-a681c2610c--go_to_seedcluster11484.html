go to seed

verb
become neglected or deteriorate
7.3 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / …°o ä t…ô sid / (AmE), / …°…ô ä t…ô siÀêd / (BrE)
to become neglected, overgrown, or to deteriorate in quality or appearance

Examples

To go to seed quickly
After her retirement, the immaculate garden began to go to seed.
If left unattended, the old building will go to seed in just a few years.
Without regular maintenance, the machinery quickly started to go to seed.
