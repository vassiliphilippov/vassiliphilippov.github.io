diurnal parallax

noun
parallax observed due to Earth's rotation
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɝːnəl ˈpærəˌlæks / (AmE), / daɪˈɜːnəl ˈpærəlæks / (BrE)
the apparent displacement of a celestial body when observed from two different points on the Earth's surface due to the Earth's rotation

Examples

Diurnal parallax measurement
Astronomers use diurnal parallax to measure the distances to nearby stars and planets.
The calculation of the Moon's diurnal parallax requires careful timing of observations from different locations.
Without considering diurnal parallax, the position of a celestial body could be miscalculated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parallax - the apparent displacement or difference in position of an object due to a change in the observer's point of view
