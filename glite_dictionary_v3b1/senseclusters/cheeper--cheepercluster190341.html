cheeper

noun
young chirping bird
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃiːpər / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːpə / (BrE)
a young bird, especially one that makes high-pitched chirping sounds

Examples

A baby cheeper
The mother bird returned to the nest to feed the hungry cheepers.
We could hear the tiny cheeper calling for food from inside the bush.
Several cheepers hopped around the yard, following their parent closely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - a fully grown bird
  • synonym: chick - a young bird, especially a baby chicken
  • synonym: nestling - a young bird that has not yet left the nest
