Bachelor of Medicine

noun
British undergraduate degree in medicine
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbætʃələr əv ˈmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələ əv ˈmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
A first university degree in medicine, awarded chiefly in Britain and some Commonwealth countries.

Examples

A Bachelor of Medicine degree
After six years of study, she received her Bachelor of Medicine from the University of Oxford.
Applicants for the hospital position must have at least a Bachelor of Medicine.
Many British doctors hold the combined degree of Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bachelor of Surgery - A companion undergraduate degree in surgery, often awarded together with Bachelor of Medicine
  • antonym: Doctor of Medicine - American and some other countries' equivalent professional degree in medicine
