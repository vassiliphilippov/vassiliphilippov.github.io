ruinous

adjective
causing great damage or destruction
7.0 1.18/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀên…ôs /; / ÀàruÀê.n…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàruÀê…™n…ôs /; / ÀàruÀê.…™.n…ôs / (BrE)
causing serious damage, destruction, or ruin (physically, financially, or otherwise)

Examples

A ruinous fire
A ruinous fire swept through the old mansion, leaving nothing but ashes.
Industry experts warned that such a policy could prove ruinous for small businesses.
The hurricane had a ruinous effect on the small coastal town.
His ruinous gambling debts forced him to declare bankruptcy.
Taking on so much debt proved to be a ruinous decision for the company.
The hurricane left a ruinous path through the coastal town.

Synonyms

  • devastating
    5.1 13/m
    devastating brings very severe, often sudden damage to places or people, while ruinous means causing serious lasting destruction.
  • catastrophic
    7.0 9.73/m
    catastrophic causes sudden complete failure or loss for whole systems, while ruinous means causing serious long-term damage or ruin.
  • cataclysmic
    9.1 1.58/m
    cataclysmic brings violent, wide destruction in huge events, while ruinous describes serious damage that can be physical or financial.
  • cataclysmal
    9.9 0.00/m
    cataclysmal describes huge, violent change in nature or society, while ruinous is broader and includes slow financial or physical ruin.
  • disastrous
    3.5 9.73/m
    disastrous causes great harm, loss, or failure in events or plans, while ruinous often stresses severe or lasting destruction.
  • destructive
    4.2 17/m
    destructive tends to mean causing physical breaking or ruin, while ruinous often implies severe loss, including money or reputation.
  • ravaging
    7.4 0.00/m
    ravaging attacks or storms sweep through and destroy areas quickly, while ruinous can mean causing slow or sudden lasting ruin.
  • calamitous
    7.9 0.61/m
    calamitous describes events that bring great misfortune or loss to people, while ruinous focuses on serious damage or financial ruin.
  • baneful
    9.1 0.15/m
    baneful is a literary word for causing harm or trouble, while ruinous usually refers to heavy damage, loss, or collapse.
  • damaging
    3.1 5.45/m
    damaging means causing harm or a negative effect but is milder, while ruinous implies very serious or complete destruction or loss.
