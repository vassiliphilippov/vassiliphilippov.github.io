scalping

noun
quick resale for profit
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈskælpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskælpɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of buying assets or tickets and quickly reselling them at a higher price for profit

Examples

Ticket scalping
Many people try scalping concert tickets online to make extra money.
Authorities are cracking down on scalping outside major sports stadiums.
Some investors use scalping as a strategy to profit from small price changes in stocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arbitrage - the practice of buying and selling assets to profit from price differences
  • synonym: reselling - the act of selling something that was previously bought
