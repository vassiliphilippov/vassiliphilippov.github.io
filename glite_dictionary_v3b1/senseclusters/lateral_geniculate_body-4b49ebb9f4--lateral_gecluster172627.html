lateral geniculate body

noun
part of the brain processing visual signals
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl dʒəˌnɪk.jə.lət ˈbɑː.di / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl dʒəˌnɪk.jʊ.lət ˈbɒd.i / (BrE)
a structure in the thalamus of the brain that processes visual information received from the retina before transmitting it to the occipital lobe of the cerebral cortex

Examples

The lateral geniculate body in brain
Damage to the lateral geniculate body can lead to impaired vision.
Researchers studied neuronal responses in the lateral geniculate body of primates.
The lateral geniculate body acts as a relay center in the visual pathway.
