board

verb
get onto a vehicle
1.1 10/m
Pronunciation: / b…îrd / (AmE), / b…îÀêd / (BrE)
to get onto or enter a vehicle such as a train, bus, ship, or aircraft

Examples

To board a train
Passengers are asked to board the train five minutes before departure.
She boarded the bus and found a seat near the back.
We will board the plane as soon as the gate opens.

Synonyms

  • get on
    1.0 15/m
    get on is a common everyday phrase for entering buses, trains, or planes, while board is more neutral and formal.
  • embark
    4.4 1.38/m
    embark is a more formal word often used for starting a sea or air journey, while board means simply to get onto a vehicle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disembark - to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle
  • synonym: embark - to go on board a ship or aircraft
