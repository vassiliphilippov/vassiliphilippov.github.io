staggered board of directors

noun
board with members elected in rotating groups
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡɚd bɔrd əv dəˈrɛktɚz / (AmE), / ˈstæɡəd bɔːd əv dɪˈrɛktəz / (BrE)
A board of directors in which only a portion of members are elected in a given year, with terms expiring at different times, rather than all directors being elected at once.

Examples

A staggered board of directors
Many companies adopt a staggered board of directors to increase stability in corporate governance.
Shareholders debated the advantages and disadvantages of switching to a staggered board of directors.
The hostile takeover was more difficult because the firm had a staggered board of directors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: board of directors - a group of people elected to represent shareholders and oversee company management
