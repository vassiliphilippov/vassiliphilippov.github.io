flush out

verb
remove or expel something unwanted
1.9
Pronunciation: / flʌʃ aʊt / (AmE), / flʌʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to force something undesirable out from a place, especially by using liquid or other means; to remove or expel completely

Examples

To flush out suspects
After hours of searching, the police managed to flush out the suspects hiding in the building.
Doctors recommend drinking plenty of water to flush out toxins from the body.
The special solution was used to flush out dirt from the engine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expel - to force something out
  • synonym: purge - to remove something unwanted thoroughly
