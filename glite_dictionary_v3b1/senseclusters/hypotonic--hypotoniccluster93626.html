hypotonic

adjective
having lower osmotic pressure
5.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.ˈtɑːn.ɪk/ /; / /haɪ.poʊ.ˈtɑːn.ɪk/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈtɒn.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a solution) having a lower osmotic pressure than another solution, usually referring to lower solute concentration compared to another fluid.

Examples

Hypotonic solution
A hypotonic fluid is often used to treat dehydration when cells need to absorb more water.
The laboratory compared the effects of hypotonic and hypertonic solutions on plant cells.
When placed in a hypotonic solution, red blood cells can swell and burst due to water intake.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypertonic - (of a solution) having a higher osmotic pressure than another solution
  • antonym: isotonic - (of a solution) having the same osmotic pressure as another solution
