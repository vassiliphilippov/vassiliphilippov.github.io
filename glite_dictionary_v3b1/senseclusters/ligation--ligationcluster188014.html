ligation

noun
surgical tying of a vessel or duct
Pronunciation: / laɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
The surgical procedure of tying a duct or blood vessel with a ligature to prevent bleeding or the flow of fluids.

Examples

Surgical ligation procedure
After the ligation procedure, the patient's recovery was closely monitored.
The surgeon performed ligation of the artery to stop the bleeding.
Tubal ligation is a common method of permanent birth control for women.
