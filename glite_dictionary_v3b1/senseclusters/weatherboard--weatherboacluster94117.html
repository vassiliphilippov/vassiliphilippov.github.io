weatherboard

noun
overlapping exterior siding board
7.8 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈweð.ər.bɔːrd/ (AmE), /ˈweð.ə.bɔːd/ (BrE)
A long, thin board with one edge thicker than the other, used as siding by overlapping each board over the one below to protect exterior walls from weather.

Examples

A weatherboard siding
Many Australian homes feature weatherboard siding for protection against rain and strong winds.
Painters had to replace several rotten weatherboards before they could repaint the old cottage.
The house was rebuilt using traditional weatherboard to maintain its classic appearance.
