Synonyms

  • plentiful
    2.5 2/m
    plentiful appears in everyday speech to mean more than enough supply, while abundant is a general formal word for large amounts.
  • ample
    3.7 5/m
    ample suggests enough space or room and often size, while abundant focuses on large available quantities.
  • rich
    1.0 14/m
    rich often describes soil, food, or resources and can be figurative, while abundant names quantity itself.
  • copious
    6.8 0.9/m
    copious describes large amounts of writing, notes, or speech and sounds formal, while abundant fits many general supplies.
  • profuse
    6.5 0.2/m
    profuse suggests something pours out in excess, like sweat or thanks, while abundant simply means very many in supply.
  • bountiful
    4.9 0.9/m
    bountiful often shows a generous harvest or giving, while abundant describes plain large amounts without that gift feeling.
