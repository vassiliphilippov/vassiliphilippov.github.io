contractive

adjective
causing reduction
7.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / kənˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
tending to make something smaller, shorter, or reduced in size or extent

Examples

Contractive property
The muscle has a contractive property that allows it to shorten during movement.
A contractive force acts to pull the two ends of the spring together.
In mathematics, a contractive function brings points closer together in a space.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expansive - tending to increase in size or extent
  • synonym: shrinking - becoming smaller in size
