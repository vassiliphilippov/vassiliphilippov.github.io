family Tuberaceae

noun
family of fungi including truffles
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli tuːbəˈreɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tjuːbəˈreɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi whose fruiting bodies (ascocarps) resemble tubers, varying in size from an acorn to a large apple and including truffles.

Examples

family Tuberaceae fungi
Scientists are studying the genetic diversity within the family Tuberaceae to better understand its evolution.
The family Tuberaceae contains fungi that are highly prized as culinary delicacies.
Truffles, which belong to the family Tuberaceae, are found underground by trained dogs or pigs.
