whew

verb
to exhale in relief
Pronunciation: / hjuː / (AmE), / hjuː / (BrE)
to exhale sharply or make a quick exclamatory sound, often to express relief, surprise, or exhaustion

Examples

To whew loudly
After running up the stairs, she paused to whew and catch her breath.
He couldn't help but whew when he saw the test results were better than expected.
The team whewed in relief when the final whistle blew and they had won the game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gasp - to inhale suddenly with surprise or shock
  • synonym: sigh - to exhale audibly to express emotion
