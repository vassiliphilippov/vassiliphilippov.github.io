regularization

noun
granting legal residency to undocumented migrants
7.0
Pronunciation: /reɡ.jə.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɡ.jʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /reɡ.jʊ.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɡ.jə.lə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(law, immigration) the process by which a government or authority grants legal residency or work rights to people previously living in a country without authorization.

Examples

Immigration regularization
The government announced a regularization program allowing undocumented workers to apply for temporary work permits.
After years of campaigning, migrants welcomed the city's regularization policy that prevented mass deportations.
Legal aid clinics taught applicants how to complete forms required for regularization under the new law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amnesty - a government decision to forgive or grant legal status to people who have committed certain offences, often used for undocumented migrants
  • antonym: deportation - the official removal of a person from a country, often the opposite outcome to regularization
