conscious

adjective
aware; noticing or recognizing something
3.5 8.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên. É…ôs / (AmE), / Ààk…ín. É…ôs / (BrE)
aware of and able to perceive or notice something; having realization or recognition (especially followed by 'of').

Examples

Conscious of danger
As a new driver, Mia was always conscious of the rules of the road.
He seemed barely conscious of the people around him at the party.
She was fully conscious of her responsibilities at work.

Synonyms

  • aware
    2.7 112/m
    aware is having knowledge or perception of facts and situations, while conscious often means noticing or recognizing things in the moment.
  • cognizant
    7.5 1.80/m
    cognizant is a formal word for being aware of a fact or situation, while conscious often appears in general speech.
  • mindful
    4.0 4.64/m
    mindful is giving careful attention to something on purpose, while conscious simply means you notice or are aware.
