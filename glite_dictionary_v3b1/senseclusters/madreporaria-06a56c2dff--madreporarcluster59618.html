Madreporaria

noun
order of stony corals
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmædrɪpoʊˈrɛriə / (AmE), / ˌmædrɪpɒˈrɑːrɪə / (BrE)
An order of marine animals comprising the stony corals, known for forming calcium carbonate skeletons that build coral reefs.

Examples

Madreporaria coral reefs
Climate change poses a significant threat to the survival of Madreporaria around the world.
Scientists study Madreporaria to better understand how coral reefs develop and support marine biodiversity.
The Great Barrier Reef is largely composed of various species within the Madreporaria order.
