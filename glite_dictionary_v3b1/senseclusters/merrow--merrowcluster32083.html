merrow

noun
Irish sea spirit
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛrəʊ / (BrE)
a mermaid or merman in Irish and Scottish folklore, often depicted as living in the sea and sometimes coming ashore

Examples

A merrow in the sea
The old tale describes a merrow who left the sea to live among humans.
Fishermen believed that seeing a merrow was a sign of coming storms.
In Irish legends, a merrow could remove its magical cap to walk on land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mermaid - a mythical sea creature with a woman's head and body and a fish's tail
  • synonym: selkie - a mythical creature that can change from seal to human form in Scottish folklore
