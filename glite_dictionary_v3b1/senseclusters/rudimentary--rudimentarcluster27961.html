rudimentary

adjective
basic or elementary; undeveloped
6.4 4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌruː.dəˈmɛn.tə.ri /; / ˌruːdəˈmɛn.tə.ri / (AmE), / ˌruː.dɪˈmen.tər.i / (BrE)
basic, simple, or at an early stage; not fully developed (used for knowledge, skills, or things in general)

Examples

Rudimentary skills
Children learn rudimentary math concepts before moving on to more complex problems.
Her knowledge of French is rudimentary, limited to a few basic phrases.
The museum displayed rudimentary tools made by early humans.
His drawing skills were rudimentary, but he enjoyed the creative process.
The species still possesses a rudimentary tail from its evolutionary ancestors.
We had only a rudimentary understanding of the problem at the time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basic - forming an essential foundation or starting point; fundamental
  • antonym: developed - fully grown or advanced; matured
  • synonym: elementary - simple or basic in form, dealing with the simplest facts or principles
