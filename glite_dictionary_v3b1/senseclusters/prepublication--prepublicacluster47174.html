prepublication

noun
advance copy
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌpʌblɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a version of a work or the period before it is officially published

Examples

A prepublication copy
The author sent out prepublication copies to several critics for early feedback.
Receiving a prepublication version allowed the journalist to prepare her article in advance.
Many reviewers appreciate getting prepublication materials to read before the official release date.
The research team distributed a prepublication version of their findings to gather feedback.
She was excited to read the prepublication manuscript before it appeared in the journal.
Many errors were corrected during the prepublication review process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galley proof - a preliminary version of a publication for proofreading
  • synonym: manuscript - an author's original text before publication
  • antonym: publication - the official release of a book, article, or other work
