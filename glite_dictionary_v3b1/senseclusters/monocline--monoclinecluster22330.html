monocline

noun
geological formation with uniformly inclined strata
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnəˌklaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌklaɪn / (BrE)
A geological formation where all rock layers are inclined in the same direction, creating a step-like fold in otherwise horizontal strata.

Examples

A monocline formation
Geologists discovered a prominent monocline stretching across the valley.
In their report, they described the unusual monocline found in the sedimentary basin.
The presence of a monocline can indicate ancient tectonic movements in the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticline - an upward-arching fold of rock layers
  • antonym: syncline - a downward-curving fold of rock layers
