eyestalk

noun
eye-bearing stalk
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌstɔk / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌstɔːk / (BrE)
a stalk or projection on some animals, such as crabs or snails, with an eye at the end

Examples

The crab raised its eyestalk above the sand to scan for predators.
Snails can retract their eyestalks when they sense danger nearby.

Crabs can move each eyestalk independently to scan their surroundings for danger.
A shrimp's eyestalk helps it see above rocks and sand on the ocean floor.
