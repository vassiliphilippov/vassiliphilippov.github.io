outbox

verb
to win by skill in boxing
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈbɑks / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈbɒks / (BrE)
to defeat an opponent in boxing by using superior skill, speed, or technique

Examples

To outbox a rival
The champion managed to outbox his opponent with quick footwork and sharp jabs.
Despite being smaller, she outboxed her rival by staying light on her feet.
He hopes to outbox his next challenger by focusing on defense and speed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to be defeated in a competition or contest
  • synonym: outfight - to defeat an opponent in a fight by being stronger or more aggressive
