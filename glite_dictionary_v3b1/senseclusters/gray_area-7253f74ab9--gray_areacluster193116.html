gray area

noun
an unclear or undefined situation
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈeəriə / (BrE)
an unclear or undefined situation or topic that does not fit neatly into a particular category

Examples

A gray area in ethics
Many ethical issues in science occupy a gray area between right and wrong.
The question of privacy on social media remains a gray area for both users and lawmakers.
There's a gray area in the rules that makes it hard to know what's allowed.
Ethical issues in artificial intelligence are a grey area for many researchers.
Many of the company's policies fall into a grey area and are open to interpretation.
The law regarding online privacy is still a grey area in many countries.
Ethical decisions often fall into a grey area where right and wrong aren't easily distinguished.
Navigating the grey area between personal freedom and public safety can be challenging for lawmakers.
The contract has some grey areas that led to confusion between the parties.
