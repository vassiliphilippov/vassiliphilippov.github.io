rebaptism

noun
baptism again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbæptɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbæptɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of baptizing someone again, especially after a previous baptism

Examples

Religious rebaptism
The issue of rebaptism caused disagreement among members of the congregation.
Some denominations do not recognize rebaptism if the original ceremony was performed correctly.
After joining the new church, he underwent rebaptism as a sign of renewed faith.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baptism - the Christian religious rite of sprinkling water on a person's forehead or immersing them in water
