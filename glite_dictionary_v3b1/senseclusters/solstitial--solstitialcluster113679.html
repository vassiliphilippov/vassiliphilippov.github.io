solstitial

adjective
of the solstice
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɑːl.ˈstɪ.ʃəl/ (AmE), /sɒl.ˈstɪ.ʃəl/ (BrE)
relating to or occurring at the time of a solstice, especially the summer or winter solstice

Examples

The solstitial night
The festival is held on the solstitial night to celebrate the longest day of the year.
Ancient monuments were often aligned with solstitial sunrise or sunset points.
Many cultures have traditions that mark the solstitial changes in daylight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solstice - either of the two times in the year when the sun is at its greatest distance from the celestial equator
