accomplice

noun
person who helps commit a crime
6.7 4.39/m

a person who helps another to commit a crime or do something wrong

Examples

An accomplice in crime
He was found guilty as an accomplice in the smuggling operation.
She claimed she had no idea she was acting as an accomplice to fraud.
The police arrested the mastermind and his accomplice after the bank robbery.

Synonyms

  • abettor
    9.2 0.08/m
    an abettor encourages or helps someone to commit a crime, while an accomplice is the partner who takes part in the illegal act.
  • aider
    9.3 0.04/m
    an aider assists or supports another person in committing a crime, while an accomplice often shares blame and acts with them.
  • confederate
    7.6 0.49/m
    a confederate works in secret with others for a shared purpose, while an accomplice specifically helps commit a crime.
