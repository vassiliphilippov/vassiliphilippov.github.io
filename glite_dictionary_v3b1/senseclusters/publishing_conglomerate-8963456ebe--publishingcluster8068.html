publishing conglomerate

noun
large corporation of merged publishing companies
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪʃɪŋ kənˈɡlɑmɚət / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪʃɪŋ kənˈɡlɒmərət / (BrE)
a large corporation formed by the merger or acquisition of several publishing companies, operating as a single entity

Examples

A publishing conglomerate company
After years of acquisitions, the company became a leading publishing conglomerate in the industry.
She landed a job at a major publishing conglomerate headquartered in New York.
The rise of the publishing conglomerate has greatly affected independent authors and small presses.
