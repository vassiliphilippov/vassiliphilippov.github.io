swale

noun
a low, marshy area between ridges
9.2
Pronunciation: / sweɪl / (AmE), / sweɪl / (BrE)
a shallow, low-lying area or channel in the ground, often moist or grassy, that collects or manages water (can be natural or designed for drainage)

Examples

A swale between hills
After heavy rain, water collected in the swale between the two hills.
The trail followed a swale that separated the grassy ridges.
Wildflowers flourished in the moist swale at the edge of the forest.
The landscape architect designed a swale to help control stormwater in the park.
After heavy rain, water collects in the swale before slowly soaking into the ground.
Planting native grasses in the swale helps filter pollutants from runoff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depression - a sunken or lower area of land
  • synonym: ditch - a narrow channel dug in the ground for drainage
  • antonym: embankment - a raised structure to hold back water or support a road
  • antonym: ridge - an elevated landform often bordering a swale
