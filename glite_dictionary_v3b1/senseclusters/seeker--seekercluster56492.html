seeker

noun
device in a missile that tracks target emissions
8.3 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːkər / (AmE), / ˈsiːkə / (BrE)
a missile guidance device that detects and follows a target by being attracted to its emissions of heat, light, sound, or radio waves

Examples

Missile seeker
Modern infrared seekers enable missiles to accurately track moving targets.
Radar-guided seeker systems are used in many anti-aircraft missiles.
The missile's seeker locked onto the enemy aircraft's heat signature.
