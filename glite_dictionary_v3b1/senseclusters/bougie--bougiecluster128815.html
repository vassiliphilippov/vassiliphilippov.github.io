bougie

noun
medical dilating instrument
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbuː.ʒiː/ /; / /ˈbuː.dʒiː/ (AmE), /ˈbuː.ʒiː/ /; / /ˈbuː.dʒiː/ (BrE)
a thin, flexible medical instrument used to widen or examine narrow passages in the body, such as the esophagus or urethra

Examples

A bougie tube
The doctor inserted a bougie to help open the patient's narrowed esophagus.
A bougie is often used in procedures to treat strictures in the urethra.
Before surgery, the nurse prepared the bougie for the physician's use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catheter - a tube inserted into the body to remove or deliver fluids
  • synonym: stent - a small tube used to keep a passage in the body open
