tonic

noun
sweet, carbonated drink (New England regional)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(New England) A sweet, carbonated soft drink, similar to what is commonly called 'soda' or 'pop' elsewhere.

Examples

A cold tonic
In some Boston neighborhoods, you'll hear people order a tonic instead of a soda.
My grandparents always served grape tonic at family gatherings in Massachusetts.
The store stocks every kind of tonic you can imagine, from root beer to orange flavor.
