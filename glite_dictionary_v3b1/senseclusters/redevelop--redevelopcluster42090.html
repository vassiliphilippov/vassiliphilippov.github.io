redevelop

verb
develop again, often in an improved form
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.dɪˈvɛ.ləp / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪˈvɛl.əp / (BrE)
to formulate or develop something again, especially in an improved or revised form, such as a theory or hypothesis

Examples

To redevelop a theory
After reviewing new data, the researchers decided to redevelop their initial hypothesis.
Scientists often have to redevelop their theories as new evidence comes to light.
The team worked to redevelop the software architecture, addressing several core issues and inefficiencies.
