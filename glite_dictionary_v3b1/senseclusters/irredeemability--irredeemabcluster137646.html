irredeemability

noun
impossible to redeem
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˌdiːməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˌdiːməˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to redeem, correct, or recover

Examples

The irredeemability of mistakes
The irredeemability of the damaged reputation made recovery impossible for the company.
Some critics argued about the irredeemability of the failed policy after repeated setbacks.
He regretted the irredeemability of his mistakes, knowing he could not undo them.
