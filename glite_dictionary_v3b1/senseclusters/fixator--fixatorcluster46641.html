fixator

noun
stabilizing muscle
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪkˌseɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfɪkseɪtə / (BrE)
a muscle that stabilizes a joint or body part by holding it steady during movement

Examples

Fixator muscle support
The physical therapist explained how the fixator muscles help keep the shoulder joint stable during exercise.
In weightlifting, a strong fixator is essential to prevent injury while performing overhead presses.
The surgeon noted that the patient's weak fixator contributed to his recurring joint instability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agonist - a muscle that causes movement by contracting
  • antonym: antagonist - a muscle that opposes the action of another muscle
  • synonym: stabilizer - a muscle or device that keeps something steady
