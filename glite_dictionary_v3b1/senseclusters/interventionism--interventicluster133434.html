interventionism

noun
government economic policy
8.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈven.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈven.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a policy or practice of a government actively intervening in the economy or other affairs to influence outcomes

Examples

Economic interventionism
The government's policy of interventionism included subsidies for struggling industries.
Debates about interventionism often arise during times of economic crisis.
Some economists argue that too much interventionism can limit free market growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: laissez-faire - an economic policy of minimal government intervention
  • synonym: regulation - the act of controlling or governing by rules or restrictions
