by way of

preposition
via or through something
Pronunciation: / baɪ weɪ əv / (AmE), / baɪ weɪ əv / (BrE)
using, via, or through a particular place, process, or means to reach something or achieve a result.

Examples

News by way of a friend
I heard the news by way of a mutual friend who called me yesterday.
She sent the message by way of her assistant to ensure it arrived quickly.
We drove to Paris by way of Lyon, enjoying the scenic route.
