buggered

adjective
hopeless or ruined
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌɡɚd / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡəd / (BrE)
in a hopeless, ruined, or seriously damaged state, often used informally or as a vulgar expression

Examples

The plan is buggered
If the car breaks down here, we're completely buggered with no way to get home.
After the storm, the old roof was so buggered it needed to be replaced entirely.
He realized he was buggered when he lost his wallet and phone on the same day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doomed - certain to fail or be destroyed
  • synonym: finished - no longer able to continue; ruined
  • antonym: safe - not in danger or at risk
