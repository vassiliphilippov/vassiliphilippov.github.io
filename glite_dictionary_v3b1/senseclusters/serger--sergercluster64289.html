serger

noun
machine for finishing fabric edges
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒə / (BrE)
A sewing machine that trims and overcasts the raw edges of fabric with a V-shaped stitch, used to prevent fraying.

Examples

A serger machine
After buying a new serger, he practiced making professional seams on scraps of material.
She finished the dress hem with her serger to prevent it from fraying.
The serger makes sewing projects faster by trimming and stitching the fabric edges at the same time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overlocker - a British term for a serger, a sewing machine for overcasting fabric edges
