showboating

noun
flashy attention-seeking
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊˌboʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌbəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of behaving in a flashy or attention-seeking way to impress others

Examples

No showboating allowed
The coach warned the players that showboating would not be tolerated during the game.
Her showboating on stage drew cheers from the audience but annoyed her bandmates.
Instead of focusing on teamwork, he was more interested in showboating for the cameras.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grandstanding - acting in a way intended to attract attention or applause
  • antonym: modesty - the quality of not drawing attention to oneself
