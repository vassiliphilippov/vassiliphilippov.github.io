strike out

verb
get a batter out in baseball by three strikes
4.2 3.3
Pronunciation: /straɪk aʊt/ (AmE), /straɪk aʊt/ (BrE)
(in baseball) to be dismissed as a batter after missing three pitches, or to cause a batter to be dismissed in this way

Examples

The batter strikes out
If you strike out three times in a row, it can be very discouraging.
She was determined not to strike out during her first at-bat.
The pitcher managed to strike out the final batter and win the game.
He has struck out twelve batters so far in this game.
She was disappointed when she struck out with the bases loaded.
The rookie pitcher managed to strike out the league's top hitter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retire - to cause a batter or player to be out in baseball or softball
  • antonym: walk - to reach first base on four balls (baseball)
