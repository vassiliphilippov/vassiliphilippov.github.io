originalism

noun
constitutional interpretation theory
4.7 8.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.nəl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈrɪdʒ.ən.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ə.ˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.nəl.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈrɪdʒ.ən.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The belief that the United States Constitution should be interpreted according to the intentions and understanding of its original authors.

Examples

Originalism in law
Critics argue that originalism can be too rigid for a modern, evolving society.
Debates about originalism often arise during Supreme Court confirmation hearings.
The judge is known for his commitment to originalism in constitutional interpretation.
