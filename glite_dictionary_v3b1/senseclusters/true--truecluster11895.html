true

adjective
accurately aimed or placed
Pronunciation: / truː / (AmE), / truː / (BrE)
accurate, precise, or correctly aligned; exactly aimed, level, or in tune

Examples

A true aim
Only a true shot can hit such a small mark from this distance.
She let the arrow fly, and her aim was true, striking the center of the target.
The quarterback’s throw was true and landed perfectly in the receiver’s hands.
After hours of adjustment, the carpenter finally made sure the door was perfectly true.
If the wheel isn't true, it will wobble when you spin it.
The builder checked to ensure the wall was true before installing the shelving.
Maps usually indicate both magnetic and true north for orientation purposes.
The pilot adjusted his course to follow true north.
To navigate accurately, you must use the true bearing rather than the magnetic one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accurate - free from error; precise or exact
  • synonym: aligned - in correct or appropriate relative position
  • antonym: errant - straying from the proper course; not accurate
  • synonym: in tune - having the correct pitch; accurate musically
  • synonym: level - having a flat or even surface
  • antonym: magnetic - of direction: relating to the Earth's magnetic poles rather than geographic axis
  • antonym: off-key - not in tune; inaccurate in pitch
