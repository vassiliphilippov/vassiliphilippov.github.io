Inupiat

proper noun
Indigenous Alaskan group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnuːpiˌæt / (AmE), / ɪˈnuːpiˌæt / (BrE)
the Indigenous people of northern Alaska, especially those living along the Arctic coast, considered collectively

Examples

The Inupiat people
The Inupiat have a rich cultural heritage rooted in the Arctic environment.
Many Inupiat communities rely on traditional hunting and fishing for their livelihood.
The language spoken by the Inupiat is an important part of their identity.
