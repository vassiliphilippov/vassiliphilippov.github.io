leaseholding

noun
property under lease
Pronunciation: / ˈliːshoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈliːshəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
property or land held or the condition of holding it under a lease agreement, rather than owning it outright

Examples

A leaseholding property
The company expanded its operations by acquiring a leaseholding in the city center.
Many apartments in the area are available only as leaseholdings, not for full ownership.
He sold his leaseholding after the lease term ended and moved to a new location.
The family's leaseholding of the farmland was renewed for another decade.
Many urban apartments are available only through leaseholding rather than outright purchase.
Changes in law affected the rights of those with long-term leaseholding arrangements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freehold - property owned outright, not leased
  • synonym: leasehold - a property held by lease
  • antonym: ownership - the state of owning something, especially property
  • synonym: tenancy - the possession or occupancy of land or property as a tenant
