feme

noun
archaic legal term for woman
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / fiÀêm / (AmE), / fiÀêm / (BrE)
an archaic term for a woman, especially used in legal contexts to refer to a married or unmarried woman

Examples

A feme sole
In medieval law, a feme sole had the right to own property independently.
The contract specified the rights of a feme covert in marriage.
The old statute used the term feme to distinguish between married and unmarried women.
