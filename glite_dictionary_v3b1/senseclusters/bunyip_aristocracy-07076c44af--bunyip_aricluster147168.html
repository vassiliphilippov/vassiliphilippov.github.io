bunyip aristocracy

noun
pretentious or self-styled elite in Australia (satirical)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌn.jɪp ˌɛr.ɪˈstɑː.krə.si / (AmE), / ˈbʌn.jɪp ˌær.ɪsˈtɒk.rə.si / (BrE)
an Australian satirical term for people who claim aristocratic status or act as though they are part of an elite, often implying they are pretentious or self-important

Examples

The bunyip aristocracy debate
Historians often reference the debate over establishing a bunyip aristocracy in colonial Australia.
Many reject the idea of a bunyip aristocracy in a country known for its egalitarian values.
The satirical speech ridiculed politicians who saw themselves as part of a bunyip aristocracy.
