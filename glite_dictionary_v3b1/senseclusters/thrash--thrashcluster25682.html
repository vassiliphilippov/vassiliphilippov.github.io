thrash

verb
beat hard or severely
Pronunciation: / θræʃ / (AmE), / θræʃ / (BrE)
to beat someone or something hard, especially as a punishment or in a fight

Examples

To thrash an enemy
He thrashed his opponent in the boxing ring last night.
The angry farmer thrashed the thief with a stick after catching him in the act.
The coach threatened to thrash any player who disobeyed the rules.
