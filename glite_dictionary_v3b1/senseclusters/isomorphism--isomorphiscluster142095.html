isomorphism

noun
structure-preserving mapping
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈmɔːrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
a one-to-one correspondence between two structures that preserves their properties or relationships

Examples

Mathematical isomorphism
The isomorphism between the two algebraic structures allowed the mathematician to transfer results from one to the other.
In biology, researchers sometimes look for isomorphism between different species' developmental processes.
Understanding isomorphism is essential in abstract algebra and many areas of mathematics.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: automorphism - an isomorphism from a structure to itself
  • degree lower: homomorphism - a mapping between structures that preserves operations but may not be one-to-one
