philosophical

adjective
calmly accepting trouble; detached composure
4.8 5.4
Pronunciation: /fɪl.ə.ˈsɑː.fɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /fɪl.ə.ˈsɒf.ɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
Calmly accepting difficulties or trouble without getting upset; showing reasoned detachment or composure, especially in challenging situations.

Examples

Philosophical attitude
Despite the unexpected loss, she remained philosophical and focused on what could be learned from the experience.
Even when things went wrong, Mark managed to stay philosophical and kept a positive outlook.
He responded to the criticism with a philosophical shrug, refusing to let it disturb his peace of mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emotional - showing strong feelings or reactions
  • synonym: resigned - accepting something unpleasant calmly
  • synonym: stoic - enduring pain or hardship without complaint
