come forth

verb
emerge; appear from somewhere
Pronunciation: / kʌm fɔrθ / (AmE), / kʌm fɔːθ / (BrE)
to appear or emerge from a place or source, especially suddenly or formally

Examples

The sun comes forth
As the mist cleared, the mountain began to come forth in the morning light.
Many volunteers came forth to help after the disaster.
New ideas can come forth during lively discussions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emerge - to become visible or come out into view
  • antonym: withdraw - to move back or away, especially from a place or a position
