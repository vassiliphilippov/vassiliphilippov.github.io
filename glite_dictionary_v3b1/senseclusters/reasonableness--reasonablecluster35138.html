reasonableness

noun
quality of being sensible or fair
5.6 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.zən.ə.bəl.nəs/ /; / /ˈriːz.nə.bəl.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈriː.zən.ə.bəl.nəs/ /; / /ˈriːz.nə.bəl.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being fair, sensible, moderate, and showing good judgment

Examples

The reasonableness of actions
Courts are expected to assess the reasonableness of actions taken by officials.
The reasonableness of her proposal convinced the whole committee to support the project.
We questioned the reasonableness of the new company policy during the staff meeting.
He spoke with reasonableness, never letting his hopes run too high or too low.
The judge admired the reasonableness of her demands in the settlement discussion.
Without greater reasonableness on both sides, the negotiation could not move forward.
Many customers appreciate the reasonableness of the restaurant's lunch menu prices.
The reasonableness of the rent makes this apartment complex very popular among students.
We questioned the reasonableness of the charges on our final bill.
