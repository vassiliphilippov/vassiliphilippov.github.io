Bloodless Revolution

noun
the 1688–1689 overthrow of James II with little violence
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌd.ləs ˌrɛv.əˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈblʌd.ləs ˌrɛv.əˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
the overthrow of King James II of England in 1688–1689, during which William III and Mary II assumed the throne with minimal violent resistance in England; also called the Glorious Revolution.

Examples

The Bloodless Revolution in England
Historians often refer to the Glorious Revolution as the Bloodless Revolution because of the lack of large-scale violence in England.
The Bloodless Revolution brought significant political changes to England without widespread conflict.
The Bloodless Revolution established parliamentary supremacy over the monarchy in England.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Glorious Revolution - another common name for the same historical event in 1688–1689 England
