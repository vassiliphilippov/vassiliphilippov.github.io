luminous intensity unit

noun
unit measuring luminous intensity
Pronunciation: / ˌluːmɪnəs ɪnˈtɛnsəti ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˌluːmɪnəs ɪnˈtensəti ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to quantify luminous intensity, the amount of visible light emitted by a source in a particular direction

Examples

The luminous intensity unit candela
The luminous intensity unit commonly used in science is the candela.
The engineer recorded the light output in luminous intensity units for the experiment.
When measuring the brightness of a light source, scientists refer to a standard luminous intensity unit.
