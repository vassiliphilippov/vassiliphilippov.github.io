chemoautotrophic

adjective
energy from chemicals
9.9
Pronunciation: /kiː.moʊ.ɔː.toʊ.ˈtroʊ.fɪk/ /; / /kiː.moʊ.ɑː.toʊ.ˈtroʊ.fɪk/ (AmE), /kiː.məʊ.ɔː.təʊ.ˈtrɒ.fɪk/ (BrE)
describing organisms that obtain energy by oxidizing inorganic substances and use this energy to produce their own food

Examples

Chemoautotrophic bacteria
Some bacteria living near hydrothermal vents are chemoautotrophic and survive without sunlight.
Scientists study chemoautotrophic organisms to understand life in extreme environments.
The chemoautotrophic process allows certain microbes to thrive deep underground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterotrophic - obtaining food by consuming other organisms
  • antonym: photoautotrophic - obtaining energy from sunlight to produce food
