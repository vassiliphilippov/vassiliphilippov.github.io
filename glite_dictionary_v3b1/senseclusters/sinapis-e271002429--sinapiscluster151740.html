Sinapis

noun
a genus of Old World mustard herbs
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈneɪpɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈneɪpɪs / (BrE)
A small genus of Old World herbs in the mustard family, often included in the genus Brassica.

Examples

Sinapis plants
Botanists often debate whether Sinapis should be a separate genus or part of Brassica.
Some species of Sinapis are cultivated for their seeds and leaves.
