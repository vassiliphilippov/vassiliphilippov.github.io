middle cerebral vein

noun
vein draining lateral brain surface
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl sɪˈriː.brəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl sɪˈriː.brəl veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins in the brain that drain blood from the lateral surfaces of the cerebral hemispheres

Examples

Blockage of middle cerebral vein
A blockage in the middle cerebral vein can lead to serious neurological complications.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully identified the middle cerebral vein to avoid damaging it.
The anatomy class included a detailed discussion of the middle cerebral vein and its function in the brain.
