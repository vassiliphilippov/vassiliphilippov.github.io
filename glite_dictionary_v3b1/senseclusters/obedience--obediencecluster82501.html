obedience

noun
following rules or wishes to please authority
2.6 8.88/m
Pronunciation: / o äÀàbiÀêdi…ôns /; / …ôÀàbiÀêdi…ôns / (AmE), / …ô äÀàbiÀêdi…ôns /; / …ôÀàbiÀêdi…ôns / (BrE)
the act or quality of doing what someone in authority tells you to do; willingness to obey

Examples

Obedience to rules
Her obedience to her parents' wishes was evident in every decision she made.
The teacher praised the boy's obedience in always following the classroom rules.
They expected complete obedience from their children, especially regarding family traditions.
Military training emphasizes strict obedience to superiors' commands.
Religious leaders often speak about the importance of obedience to moral principles.
The child's obedience to her parents was noticed by everyone at the party.
His obedience to his parents was admired by everyone in the community.
Religious orders often require vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience.
The teacher praised the students' obedience during the fire drill.

Synonyms

  • submissiveness
    6.0 0.29/m

  • compliance
    5.0 22/m

  • docility
    7.1 0.31/m
    docility is being easy to teach or manage and calm in training, while obedience is doing what someone in authority tells you to do.
