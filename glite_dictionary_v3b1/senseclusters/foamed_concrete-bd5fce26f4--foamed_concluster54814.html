foamed concrete

noun
lightweight, aerated concrete with air bubbles
5.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / fo ämd k…ënÀàkrit / (AmE), / f…ô ämd k…ínÀàkriÀêt / (BrE)
a lightweight type of concrete made by mixing cement, water, and an air-entraining agent, resulting in a porous, aerated structure

Examples

Foamed concrete mix
Engineers chose foamed concrete because it is easy to pour and shapes well for unusual designs.
The new building uses foamed concrete to improve insulation and reduce structural weight.
With the addition of air bubbles, foamed concrete offers excellent thermal and acoustic properties.

Synonyms

  • cellular concrete
    6.1 0.00/m
    cellular concrete contains many small air cells formed during mixing, while foamed concrete is made by injecting foam to create pores.
  • lightweight aerated concrete
    5.8 0.00/m
    lightweight aerated concrete uses air-entraining agents or lightweight aggregates to reduce weight, while foamed concrete is made by mixing foam into cement.
