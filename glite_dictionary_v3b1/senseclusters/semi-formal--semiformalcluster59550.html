semi-formal

adjective
moderately formal in style or attire
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈfɔrməl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈfɔːməl / (BrE)
Moderately formal in style or attire; suitable for occasions requiring dress that is less formal than black tie but more formal than casual.

Examples

Semi-formal dress code
Guests were advised that the wedding reception would be semi-formal, so jackets and ties were encouraged.
Her outfit was perfect for a semi-formal dinner—elegant but not overly dressy.
The school dance was a semi-formal event, so students wore dresses and suits but not tuxedos.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: casual - relaxed in style, not formal
  • degree higher: formal - very proper or elegant in style or manner
