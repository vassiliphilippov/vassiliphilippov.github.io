radiation pressure

noun
pressure from electromagnetic waves
4.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən ˈpreʃ.ər/ /; / /reɪ.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən ˈpreʃ.ər/ (AmE), /reɪ.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən ˈpreʃ.ə/ /; / /reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən ˈpreʃ.ə/ (BrE)
the tiny pressure exerted by electromagnetic radiation on a surface perpendicular to its direction of propagation

Examples

Radiation pressure on surface
In certain experiments, radiation pressure is strong enough to influence the motion of particles.
Laser beams can generate radiation pressure that pushes small mirrors in physics laboratories.
The radiation pressure from sunlight can cause tiny objects in space to move over time.
