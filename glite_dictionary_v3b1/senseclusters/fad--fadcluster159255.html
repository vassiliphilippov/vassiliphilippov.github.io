fad

noun
short-lived popular trend
3.5 3.61/m
Pronunciation: / fÃ¦d / (AmE), / fÃ¦d / (BrE)
a style, activity, or interest that is very popular for a short period of time

Examples

A fad in fashion
He tends to jump from one fad to another without any real commitment.
Many fashion fads from the 1990s are making a comeback today.
The low-carb diet was a fad that faded as quickly as it rose in popularity.

Synonyms

  • craze
    4.2 2.27/m
    craze is an intense, short-lived enthusiasm, while fad is any style or activity that becomes briefly popular.
  • rage
    3.7 2.79/m
    rage is a current fashion or trend people follow widely, while fad usually means something popular only for a short time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: classic - a work, style, or item of lasting value or timeless quality
  • synonym: craze - an enthusiasm for a particular activity or object that appears suddenly and achieves widespread but short-lived popularity
  • synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
