divining rod

noun
forked stick used to find water or oil
5.3 6.8
Pronunciation: /də.ˈvaɪ.nɪŋ rɑːd/ /; / /dɪ.ˈvaɪ.nɪŋ rɑːd/ (AmE), /də.ˈvaɪ.nɪŋ rɒd/ /; / /dɪ.ˈvaɪ.nɪŋ rɒd/ (BrE)
a forked stick or rod, traditionally used in the search for underground water, minerals, or oil, believed to dip or twitch when those are found

Examples

A divining rod tool
Despite skepticism, he walked across the field with his divining rod hoping to find oil.
Some people believe a divining rod can detect underground water sources through subtle movements.
The farmer used a divining rod to search for a new well on his property.
