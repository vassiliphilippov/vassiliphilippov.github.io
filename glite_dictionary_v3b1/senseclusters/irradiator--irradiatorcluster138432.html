irradiator

noun
radiation device
Pronunciation: / ɪˈreɪdieɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪdieɪtə / (BrE)
a machine or person that exposes objects or people to radiation, usually for sterilization or treatment

Examples

Medical irradiator
The laboratory uses an irradiator to sterilize medical equipment before use.
Food products are sometimes treated with an irradiator to eliminate harmful bacteria.
Researchers placed the samples inside the irradiator for controlled exposure to gamma rays.
The hospital hired an experienced irradiator to oversee the sterilization of surgical instruments.
A modern irradiator can treat large batches of food to eliminate harmful bacteria.
The technician operated the irradiator carefully to ensure the safety of the laboratory staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radiation - the process of emitting energy as waves or particles
  • synonym: radiographer - a person trained to take medical images using radiation
  • synonym: sterilizer - a device used to make objects free from bacteria or other living microorganisms
