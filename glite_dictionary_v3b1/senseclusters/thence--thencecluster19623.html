thence

adverb
from that place or circumstance
Pronunciation: / ðɛns / (AmE), / ðɛns / (BrE)
as a result of something; therefore; thus

Examples

Thence follows the rule
He made a decision, and thence all his actions were guided by it.
The committee drafted a plan and thence submitted it to the council for approval.
The new evidence emerged, and thence the judge reconsidered the verdict.
He was the eldest son, thence inheriting the estate by right.
The evidence was clear; thence, the jury returned a quick verdict.
There was no food left, thence we decided to leave early.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hence - for this reason; as a consequence
  • synonym: therefore - for that reason; consequently
  • synonym: therefrom - from that (used especially in legal contexts)
  • synonym: thus - as a result; consequently
