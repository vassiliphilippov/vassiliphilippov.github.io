hyperpolarize

verb
to increase membrane charge
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈpoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈpəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to increase the difference in electrical charge across a cell membrane, making the inside more negative

Examples

To hyperpolarize a cell
Certain drugs can hyperpolarize nerve cells, reducing their activity.
When potassium channels open, they hyperpolarize the cell membrane and inhibit firing.
Researchers observed that the treatment would hyperpolarize the neurons, making them less likely to send signals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depolarize - to reduce the difference in electrical charge across a cell membrane
