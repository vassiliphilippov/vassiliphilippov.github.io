autocratic

adjective
with absolute ruling power
6.5 6.4
Pronunciation: /ɔː.tə.ˈkræt.ɪk/ (AmE), /ɔː.tə.ˈkræt.ɪk/ (BrE)
having or showing complete power and control over others, often in a domineering or dictatorial way

Examples

Autocratic leadership style
For years, the company maintained an autocratic management style that discouraged feedback from employees.
Her autocratic approach to decision-making alienated many team members.
The country was ruled by an autocratic leader who tolerated no opposition.
Her autocratic attitude made it difficult for colleagues to express their own views.
Parents should avoid being autocratic when dealing with their children's choices.
The new manager has adopted an autocratic style, rarely consulting his team before making decisions.
