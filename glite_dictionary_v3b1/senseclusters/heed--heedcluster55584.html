heed

noun
careful attention or consideration
5.3 1.91/m
Pronunciation: / hid / (AmE), / hiÀêd / (BrE)
careful attention or consideration to something, especially to advice or warnings.

Examples

Take heed of advice
She gave little heed to the doctor's advice and continued her normal routine.
The company failed to pay heed to the complaints of its customers.
Without proper heed, important details can easily be overlooked.

Synonyms

  • regard
    2.9 14/m
    regard shows steady consideration for others' needs and feelings, while heed focuses on attention to advice or warnings.
  • attentiveness
    4.2 0.62/m
    attentiveness means paying close, watchful attention to people's needs, while heed means attention to advice and warnings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attention - notice taken of someone or something
  • antonym: disregard - lack of attention or consideration
