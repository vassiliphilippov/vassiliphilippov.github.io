counterpose

verb
to set in contrast
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌpoʊz / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpəʊz / (BrE)
to place or set something in contrast or opposition to something else

Examples

Counterpose ideas
The artist decided to counterpose light and dark colors in her painting.
In his speech, he counterposed the benefits of tradition with the need for innovation.
The novel skillfully counterposes hope and despair throughout its chapters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: combine - to join or bring together
  • synonym: contrast - to compare in order to show differences
  • synonym: oppose - to place against or in opposition
