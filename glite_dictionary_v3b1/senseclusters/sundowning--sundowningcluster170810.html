sundowning

noun
evening confusion in dementia
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌnˌdaʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌdaʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
a state of increased confusion or agitation that occurs in the late afternoon or evening, especially in people with dementia

Examples

Evening sundowning symptoms
The nurse noticed increased sundowning symptoms in her patient as evening approached.
Families often find sundowning challenging when caring for loved ones with dementia at home.
Doctors recommend creating a calm environment to help reduce sundowning episodes in elderly patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or restlessness
  • synonym: delirium - a sudden state of severe confusion and rapid changes in brain function
