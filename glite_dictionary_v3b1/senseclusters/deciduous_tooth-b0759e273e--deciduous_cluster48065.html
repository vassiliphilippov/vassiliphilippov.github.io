deciduous tooth

noun
temporary tooth in young mammals
4.6 3.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪdʒuəs tuθ / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪdjuːəs tuːθ / (BrE)
a temporary tooth in young mammals, such as a child's baby tooth, which is later replaced by a permanent tooth

Examples

A deciduous tooth
A deciduous tooth will eventually fall out to make room for its permanent successor.
Most children start to lose their deciduous teeth around the age of six.
The dentist explained that the pain was due to a loose deciduous tooth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: milk tooth - a temporary tooth in young children; another term for deciduous tooth
  • antonym: permanent tooth - a tooth that replaces a deciduous tooth and remains for adult life
