bring down

verb
to reduce spirits; cause trouble; sometimes enliven
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ daʊn / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn / (BrE)
to cause something or someone to be in a worse or lower state, especially to reduce someone's spirits or cause negative results; (occasionally) to enliven a space or situation.

Examples

To bring down the mood
The cheerful decorations brought down the formality of the office, making it feel more inviting.
The negative comments from her coworkers really brought down her confidence.
The new policy has brought down profits for many small businesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheer up - make someone feel happier or more cheerful
  • synonym: depress - make someone feel less cheerful or hopeful
  • antonym: lift - raise spirits or mood
