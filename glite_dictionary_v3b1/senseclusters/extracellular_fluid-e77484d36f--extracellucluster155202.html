extracellular fluid

noun
fluid outside cells in the body
4.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…õkstr…ôÀàs…õl.j…ôl…ôr ÀàfluÀê.…™d / (AmE), / Àå…õkstr…ôÀàs…õl.j ä.l…ô ÀàfluÀê.…™d / (BrE)
the body fluid situated outside cells, including blood plasma and interstitial fluid, that contains proteins and electrolytes

Examples

Loss of extracellular fluid
A sudden loss of extracellular fluid can lead to dehydration and shock.
Doctors monitor the levels of extracellular fluid to assess a patient's hydration status.
Electrolytes in the extracellular fluid are vital for nerve function.
