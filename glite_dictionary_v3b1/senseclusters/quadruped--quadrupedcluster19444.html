quadruped

adjective
having four feet or legs
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑː.drəˌpɛd / (AmE), / ˈkwɒ.drʊˌpɛd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having four feet or legs.

Examples

Quadruped dinosaur
Ancient reptiles included both biped and quadruped forms.
Most mammals in the savanna are quadruped creatures, adapted for running.
The horse is a classic example of a quadruped mammal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biped - an animal having two feet
