nontherapeutic

adjective
not effective medically
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌθɛrəˈpjutɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌθɛrəˈpjuːtɪk / (BrE)
not related to or effective for treating or healing disease or injury

Examples

Nontherapeutic treatment
The doctor explained that the treatment was considered nontherapeutic and would not help the patient's condition.
Researchers discontinued the nontherapeutic drug after it failed to show any improvement in clinical trials.
Some procedures are labeled nontherapeutic because they do not provide any medical benefit to the patient.
The study focused on the nontherapeutic use of certain medications in healthy volunteers.
Some procedures are considered nontherapeutic because they do not aim to cure or treat illness.
The hospital does not allow nontherapeutic cosmetic surgeries in its facilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: therapeutic - having a healing or beneficial effect
