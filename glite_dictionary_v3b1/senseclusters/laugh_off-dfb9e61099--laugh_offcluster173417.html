laugh off

verb
dismiss a problem by joking or laughing
1.0
Pronunciation: / læf ɔf / (AmE), / lɑːf ɒf / (BrE)
to try to make a problem, criticism, or embarrassing situation seem less important by making a joke or pretending to be amused by it

Examples

To laugh off criticism
He always laughs off criticism and rarely takes offense.
She tried to laugh off the awkward question during the interview.
They laughed off the mistake as if it were nothing serious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shrug off - to dismiss something as unimportant or not serious
