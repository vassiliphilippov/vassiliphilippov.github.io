villeinage

noun
feudal land tenure by a villein
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪlɪnɪdʒ /; / ˈvɪlənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈvɪlɪnɪdʒ / (BrE)
the condition or legal status of a villein (a feudal serf), including the land they held and the services or obligations owed to a lord in medieval times

Examples

Medieval villeinage system
In medieval England, most peasants lived under villeinage and worked the land for their lord.
Legal documents from the era describe disputes over the obligations of villeinage.
The historian explained how villeinage shaped rural economies before its decline in the fifteenth century.
Documents from the 13th century provide detailed accounts of the obligations tied to villeinage.
During the Middle Ages, peasants living under villeinage owed labor and allegiance to their local lord.
The abolition of villeinage ushered in significant social change across England.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the state of being free rather than in servitude or under constraint
  • synonym: serfdom - the state of being a serf or laborer bound under the feudal system
