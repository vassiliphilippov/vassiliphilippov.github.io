anticlassical

noun
opponent of classicism
9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈklæs.ɪ.kəl/ /; / /æn.tə.ˈklæs.ɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈklɑː.sɪ.kəl/ /; / /æn.tə.ˈklɑː.sɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
a person who opposes or rejects classical traditions, styles, or principles, especially in art or literature

Examples

An anticlassical thinker
The young artist was considered an anticlassical for rejecting traditional painting techniques.
Many anticlassicals in the early 20th century sought to redefine the boundaries of literature.
Her views as an anticlassical often sparked debates at academic conferences.
