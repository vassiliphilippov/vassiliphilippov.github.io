unperfected

adjective
not legally completed
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnpərˈfɛktɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnpəˈfɛktɪd / (BrE)
not having completed the legal steps required to make a security interest or right legally effective or enforceable

Examples

Unperfected security interest
The lender lost priority because the collateral remained unperfected when the debtor filed for bankruptcy.
An unperfected security interest may not be enforceable against third parties.
The lawyer explained that the claim was still unperfected due to missing documentation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: perfected - having completed all legal steps to make a right effective
