landslide

noun
mass of earth/rock sliding down a slope
Pronunciation: / ˈlændˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlan(d)ˌslaɪd / (BrE)
a mass of earth, rock, and debris rapidly moving down a slope or cliff

Examples

A landslide destroys homes
A landslide destroyed several homes at the base of the mountain.
Heavy rains increased the risk of a landslide in the valley.
The highway was closed due to a massive landslide blocking both lanes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avalanche - a mass of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside
  • synonym: mudslide - a mass of mud sliding down a hill or slope
