rebound

noun
movement back after impact
3.3 1.13/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀåba änd / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêba änd / (BrE)
a sudden movement back after hitting something; a reaction when force causes something to return to its original position or direction

Examples

A rebound off the wall
After striking the wall, the ball took a sharp rebound toward the players.
The basketball made a loud rebound off the backboard before dropping into the net.
The car's tire suffered a rebound after hitting the curb, causing a slight jolt.

Synonyms

  • bounce
    2.2 2.92/m
    bounce is a light, springy motion after hitting a surface, while rebound is any sudden movement back caused by force.
  • recoil
    6.3 3.28/m
    recoil is a sharp backward jerk often from an impact or gun, while rebound is a more general return after impact.
  • bounce-back
    3.0 0.11/m
    bounce-back is the act of quickly returning to a previous position or state, while rebound describes the physical movement after a hit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bounce - the movement of something springing back after hitting a surface
