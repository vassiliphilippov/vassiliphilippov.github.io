Myxiniformes

noun
order of jawless fishes (hagfishes)
Pronunciation: / maɪˌksɪnəˈfɔrmiːz /; / maɪˌksɪnɪˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / mɪkˌsɪnɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of jawless fishes known as hagfishes, distinct from lampreys, used in zoological classification

Examples

Myxiniformes jawless fishes
Some biologists study Myxiniformes to understand early vertebrate evolution.
The Myxiniformes are known for their ability to secrete slime as a defense mechanism.
Unlike lampreys, Myxiniformes lack a true jaw and have distinctive tentacles around their mouths.
Scientists study Myxiniformes to understand the evolution of vertebrates.
The Myxiniformes are known for producing large quantities of slime as a defense mechanism.
Unlike other jawless fish, Myxiniformes lack vertebral elements and have unique feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Petromyzontiformes - Suborder of lampreys, related jawless fishes
  • synonym: hagfish - marine jawless fish in the order Myxiniformes
  • antonym: lamprey - jawless fish often confused with hagfishes but in a different order
