base-forming

adjective
yielding a base in water (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪs ˌfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪs ˌfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a substance) yielding a base when dissolved in water, especially in chemical reactions or tests.

Examples

Base-forming compound
Sodium oxide is a base-forming compound that reacts with water to produce sodium hydroxide.
Their research classified the minerals as either base-forming or acid-forming depending on the solution produced.
When dissolved in aqueous solution, ammonia acts as a base-forming agent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acid-forming - yielding an acid in aqueous solution
