Browning machine gun

noun
a powerful belt-fed military machine gun
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊnɪŋ məˈʃin ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnɪŋ məˈʃiːn ɡʌn / (BrE)
a belt-fed, rapid-fire machine gun designed by John Browning and used by United States forces during World War II and the Korean War

Examples

A Browning machine gun
A restored Browning machine gun is on display at the military history museum.
During World War II, the Browning machine gun was mounted on tanks and aircraft.
The soldiers were trained to operate the Browning machine gun under combat conditions.
