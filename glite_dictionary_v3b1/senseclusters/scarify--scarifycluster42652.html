scarify

verb
to make shallow cuts in skin for ritual or identification
Pronunciation: / ˈskærəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈskærɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make shallow cuts or incisions in the skin, especially as part of a ritual, often leaving permanent scars for identification or cultural reasons

Examples

To scarify the skin
He chose to scarify his arm as part of the sacred ceremony.
In some cultures, people scarify their bodies as a rite of passage.
The community elder explained why certain tribes scarify the cheeks of young members.
