epiphysis

noun
end part of a long bone
6.0 8.7
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈpɪ.fɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪ.fɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈpɪ.fɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪ.fɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
the rounded end part of a long bone, initially separated from the main bone by cartilage, which later fuses with the shaft as the bone matures

Examples

Bone epiphysis
A fracture involving the epiphysis may affect bone growth in young people.
Ossification of the epiphysis usually completes by late adolescence.
The growth of the epiphysis is crucial for the lengthening of long bones during childhood.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diaphysis - the shaft or central part of a long bone
