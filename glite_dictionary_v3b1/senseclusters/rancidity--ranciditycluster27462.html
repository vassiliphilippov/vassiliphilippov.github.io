rancidity

noun
state of being rancid
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: / rænˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / ranˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being rancid, especially having an unpleasant odor or taste due to decomposition of fats or oils

Examples

The rancidity of oils
Prolonged exposure to heat can accelerate the rancidity of vegetable oils.
Proper storage helps prevent the rancidity of nuts and seeds.
The rancidity of the butter was noticeable as soon as I opened the container.
