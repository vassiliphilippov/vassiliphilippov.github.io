varmint

noun
undesirable or nuisance wild animal
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːrmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvɑːmɪnt / (BrE)
a wild animal considered undesirable, especially a predatory or nuisance species such as a coyote or fox

Examples

A wild varmint
Coyotes are often considered varmints by ranchers because they prey on livestock.
He spotted a varmint sneaking around the trash cans late at night.
The farmer set traps to catch any varmint that might raid his chicken coop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vermin - animals or insects regarded as pests
