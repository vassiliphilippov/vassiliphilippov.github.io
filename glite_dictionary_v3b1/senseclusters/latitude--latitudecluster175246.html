latitude

noun
freedom to act or think without restriction
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlætɪˌtud /; / ˈlæ.t̬ɪ.tud /; / ˈlæt̬.ɪ.tuːd / (AmE), / ˈlætɪˌtjuːd /; / ˈlæt.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
freedom or flexibility to act, think, or make decisions within certain limits

Examples

Latitude for decision-making
He appreciated the latitude his new job offered in terms of creativity.
Teachers have some latitude in designing their lesson plans.
The manager was given considerable latitude to make decisions for the team.
Parents should give their children some latitude to make mistakes and learn from them.
The manager was given wide latitude in hiring new staff for the project.
We have considerable latitude in deciding how to approach the assignment.
Employees with more latitude in their schedules often report higher job satisfaction.
My manager allows me considerable latitude in how I organize my workday.
The new policy gives workers greater latitude to work remotely when needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt easily
  • synonym: freedom - the power or right to act without constraint
  • synonym: leeway - extra freedom or flexibility in action or decision
  • antonym: restraint - limitation or restriction of freedom
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being strict or inflexible
