hall pass

noun
written permission to be out of class at school
Pronunciation: / hɔl pæs / (AmE), / hɔːl pɑːs / (BrE)
a written permission given by a teacher, allowing a student to leave the classroom and be in the hallways of the school during class time

Examples

A hall pass from class
She showed the principal her hall pass when he stopped her in the hallway.
The teacher gave me a hall pass so I could use the restroom during class.
Without a hall pass, students are not allowed to wander the corridors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pass - official permission to go somewhere
