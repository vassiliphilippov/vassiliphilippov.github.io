tessellation

noun
subdivision of polygons in graphics

(in computer graphics) the process of subdividing polygons or mesh patches into smaller polygons to increase geometric detail or enable displacement during rendering.

Examples

3D tessellation process
The game's engine applies real-time tessellation to add geometric detail to distant terrain.
Modern GPUs support hardware tessellation stages that subdivide patches for smoother surfaces.
Artists use displacement maps with tessellation to create fine surface wrinkles without extra geometry.
