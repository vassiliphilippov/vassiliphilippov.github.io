fadeout

noun
temporary reduction in signal strength
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdˌaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary gradual reduction in the strength of a transmitted signal, often caused by electrical interference

Examples

Radio fadeout
During the storm, the radio experienced several sudden fadeouts that made the broadcast unintelligible.
Satellite communication can be interrupted by a fadeout caused by atmospheric conditions.
The technician diagnosed the recurring fadeout as interference from nearby electrical equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fading - the variation or loss of signal strength, especially in communications
