rollback

noun
return to lower price or rate
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlbæk / (BrE)
a reduction of prices, wages, or rates to a previous or earlier level, especially as an official measure

Examples

Rollback of prices
After much public pressure, the government implemented a rollback of the recent tax increases.
The company is offering a temporary rollback of fuel prices to attract more drivers.
The grocery store announced a rollback on several essential items to help customers save money.

Related Terms

  • antonym: increase - an instance of growing or making greater
  • synonym: reduction - the act of making something less or smaller
