Karelian

noun
a member of the Finnic people from Karelia
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛliən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛːlɪən / (BrE)
A member of a Finnic ethnic group native to Karelia, a region in northwestern Russia and parts of Finland.

Examples

A Karelian person
Her grandmother was a proud Karelian who spoke both Finnish and Russian fluently.
Many Karelians emigrated to Finland during the 20th century due to political changes in the region.
The traditions of the Karelian people are reflected in their unique folk music and crafts.
