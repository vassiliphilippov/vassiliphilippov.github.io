unsortable

adjective
cannot be ordered
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɔːrtəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɔːtəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be arranged in a particular order or sequence

Examples

An unsortable list
The files were so disorganized that they were completely unsortable by any method.
After the system crash, the database became unsortable and required manual review.
Some types of mixed data are simply unsortable without additional information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sortable - able to be arranged in order
