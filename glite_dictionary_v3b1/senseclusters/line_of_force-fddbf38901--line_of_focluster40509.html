line of force

noun
imaginary line showing force direction in a field
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪn əv ˈfɔrs / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn əv ˈfɔːs / (BrE)
an imaginary line in a field of force, such as an electric or magnetic field, where the tangent at any point indicates the direction of the force at that location

Examples

A line of force in physics
In physics class, we learned to draw each line of force emerging from the positive charge.
Many diagrams illustrate the line of force patterns in electric fields.
The line of force around a magnet shows the direction in which a north pole would move.

Related Terms

  • synonym: field line - another term for an imaginary line representing direction of a force field
