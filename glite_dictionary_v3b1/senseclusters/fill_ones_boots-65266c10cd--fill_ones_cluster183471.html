fill one's boots

verb
indulge oneself excessively; enjoy fully
Pronunciation: / fɪl wʌnz buːts / (AmE), / fɪl wʌnz buːts / (BrE)
to take as much as you want of something or enjoy something as much as you can (especially when given the chance)

Examples

To fill his boots with luxuries
After the promotion, he decided to fill his boots with the luxuries he'd always dreamed of.
She really filled her boots with excitement after hearing the good news.
Whenever there's free food at a party, people tend to fill their boots.
Go ahead at the buffet and fill your boots—there's plenty of food for everyone.
When given unlimited overtime, the workers really filled their boots with extra pay.
Whenever there's free pizza in the office, he makes sure to fill his boots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enjoy oneself - to take pleasure in an activity or experience
  • synonym: overindulge - to indulge oneself to excess
