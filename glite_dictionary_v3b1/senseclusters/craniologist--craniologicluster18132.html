craniologist

noun
one who reads character from skull shape
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkreɪniˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌkreɪniˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who claims to be able to determine character or mental ability from the shape of the skull

Examples

A craniologist studies skulls
Although now discredited, some once believed a craniologist could reveal their true nature.
In the 19th century, a craniologist might measure skulls to deduce personality traits.
The museum exhibit included the odd tools once used by a craniologist.
