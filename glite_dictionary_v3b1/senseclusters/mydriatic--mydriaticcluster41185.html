mydriatic

noun
drug that dilates the pupil
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪdriˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪdrɪˈatɪk / (BrE)
A drug or agent that causes the pupil of the eye to dilate, commonly used during eye examinations.

Examples

A mydriatic drug
After receiving a mydriatic, her pupils remained dilated for several hours.
Doctors often apply a mydriatic to improve visibility during an eye examination.
The ophthalmologist used a mydriatic before examining the patient's retina.
