ridge

noun
long narrow elevation on ocean floor
3.9
Pronunciation: / rɪdʒ / (AmE), / rɪdʒ / (BrE)
a long, narrow, raised area or line, such as on land, a roof, a bone, or a surface

Examples

Mountain ridge
Scientists mapped a previously unknown ridge on the Atlantic Ocean floor.
Sediments collect at the base of the ridge, creating diverse habitats.
The submarine followed the ridge for several miles, observing unique marine life.
A dental ridge can be felt along the top of the jawbone.
The surgeon carefully avoided the ridge of tissue during the procedure.
The tibia has a sharp ridge along its anterior surface.
A ridge of snow formed on the roof after the storm.
The fingerprints left a ridge pattern on the glass.
We hiked along the narrow ridge that separated the two valleys.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crest - a prominent ridge or raised part, especially on a bone
  • antonym: eaves - the part of a roof that meets or overhangs the walls of a building
  • degree lower: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain
  • degree higher: mountain - a large natural elevation of the earth's surface
  • synonym: seamount - an underwater mountain rising from the ocean floor
  • antonym: trough - a long, narrow depression on the ocean floor
  • antonym: valley - a low area of land between hills or mountains
