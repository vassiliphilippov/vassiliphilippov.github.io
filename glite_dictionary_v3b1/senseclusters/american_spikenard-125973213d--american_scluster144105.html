American spikenard

noun
North American perennial plant with aromatic roots
9.5
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈspaɪkˌnɑrd / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈspaɪkˌnɑːd / (BrE)
a North American perennial plant (Aralia racemosa) with aromatic roots and broad clusters of small greenish flowers, found from southeastern North America to Mexico

Examples

American spikenard plant
Botanists distinguish American spikenard from its relatives by its panicled flower clusters and strong aroma.
Native Americans valued the roots of American spikenard for medicinal purposes.
The woodland path was lined with the tall, leafy stems of American spikenard.
