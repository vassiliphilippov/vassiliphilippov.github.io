reemit

verb
to send out again
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈmɪt / (BrE)
to transmit, broadcast, or send out something again, such as a signal or message, often to extend its reach

Examples

To reemit a signal
The satellite will reemit the television signal to areas that cannot receive the original broadcast.
After processing the data, the device can reemit the information to other connected systems.
To improve coverage, the station decided to reemit the emergency alert across multiple frequencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broadcast - to transmit a program or signal by radio or television
  • synonym: relay - to receive and then transmit again, especially a signal or message
