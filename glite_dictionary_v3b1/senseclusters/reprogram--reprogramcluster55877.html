reprogram

verb
to change cell type
7.0
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈproʊ.græm/ /; / /riː.prə.ˈgræm/ (AmE), /riː.ˈprəʊ.græm/ /; / /riː.prə.ˈgræm/ (BrE)
to change the gene expression of a cell so that it transforms into a different cell type

Examples

To reprogram a cell
Researchers have learned how to reprogram adult cells to act like stem cells in the laboratory.
The team was able to reprogram blood cells into neurons using a special technique.
By introducing certain genes, scientists can reprogram skin cells to become heart cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dedifferentiate - to revert from a specialized cell type to a more primitive one
  • synonym: differentiate - to develop into a more specialized cell type
