lose

verb
be unable to find; misplace
Pronunciation: / luːz / (AmE), / luːz / (BrE)
to be unable to find, keep, or maintain something or someone; to no longer have something (physically or abstractly)

Examples

To lose your keys
Don't lose your ticket, or you can't get back in.
I always lose my keys when I'm in a hurry.

She often gets so absorbed in thought that she can lose track of what's happening around her.
The detective managed not to lose sight of the suspect for most of the chase.
If you lose your keys, you might have trouble entering your house.
It is easy to lose motivation during difficult times.
She didn’t want to lose touch with her childhood friends.

  • synonym: misplace - to put something in the wrong place and lose track of it
