discomfort

verb
to cause unease
4.3
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkʌmfərt / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌmfət / (BrE)
to make someone feel uneasy, anxious, or uncomfortable

Examples

To discomfort a guest

He was clearly discomforted by the unexpected personal question.
Loud noises in the hospital often discomfort patients trying to rest.
The news may discomfort some viewers who are sensitive to violence.
Her direct questions seemed to discomfort the panel of experts.
He tried not to discomfort his guests with personal topics.
