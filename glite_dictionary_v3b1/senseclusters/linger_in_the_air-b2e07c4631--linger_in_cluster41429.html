linger in the air

verb
remain perceptible in the air
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋɚ ɪn ði ɛr / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡə ɪn ði ɛə / (BrE)
(of a smell, feeling, or other sensation) to remain noticeable or perceptible in a place for some time after its source has gone.

Examples

Silence lingered in the air
A tense silence lingered in the air following the heated argument.
Even after the rain stopped, the earthy smell lingered in the air for hours.
The sweet aroma of fresh bread seemed to linger in the air long after the bakery had closed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than expected or to be slow to leave
