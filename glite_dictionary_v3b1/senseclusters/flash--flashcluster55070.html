flash

noun
a sudden burst of light or energy
Pronunciation: / flæʃ / (AmE), / flæʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, brief burst of light; a device that produces such a burst of light (like a camera flash)

Examples

Camera flash
A camera flash momentarily lit up the dark room.
The explosion was accompanied by a brilliant flash visible for miles.
There was a sudden flash of lightning during the storm.
A strong flash illuminated the subject as the photo was taken.
She used the camera's flash to take pictures at the party.
The photographer forgot to turn on the flash in the dark room.
A sudden flash of lightning lit up the night sky.
He saw a flash of light out of the corner of his eye.
The camera blinded me with its bright flash.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burst - a sudden outbreak or explosion
  • degree higher: flare - a sudden, intense blaze or burst of light
  • degree higher: flashlight - a portable hand-held electric light
  • synonym: flicker - a brief or unsteady light
  • degree higher: glare - a strong, harsh burst of light
  • degree lower: glimmer - a faint or brief light
  • synonym: strobe - a device producing regular flashes of light
