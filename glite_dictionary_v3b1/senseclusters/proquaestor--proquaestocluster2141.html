proquaestor

noun
Roman substitute official
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈkwɛstər / (AmE), / prəʊˈkwiːstə / (BrE)
a Roman official who acted in place of a quaestor, often managing finances or administration in a province

Examples

The proquaestor official
The proquaestor was appointed to oversee the province's accounts during the governor's absence.
In ancient Rome, a proquaestor could exercise the same powers as a regular quaestor.
Historians found records of a proquaestor managing military supplies in the eastern provinces.
