concave lens

noun
lens that spreads light rays outward
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.keɪv lɛnz / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.keɪv lɛnz / (BrE)
A lens that is thinner at the center than at the edges, causing parallel rays of light passing through it to diverge.

Examples

A concave lens
A concave lens is often used in eyeglasses to correct nearsightedness.
The telescope design required both a convex and a concave lens to focus the image properly.
When light passes through a concave lens, the rays spread out instead of coming together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convex lens - A lens that is thicker in the center than at the edges and converges light rays
