glider

noun
aircraft without an engine
5.1 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈɡlaɪdə / (BrE)
an aircraft without an engine that is supported in flight by the lift created as air moves over its wings

Examples

A glider aircraft
A glider requires excellent weather conditions to travel long distances without an engine.
Many enthusiasts enjoy flying a glider because of the challenge of using only air currents to stay aloft.
The pilot launched the glider from the top of the hill and soared gracefully above the valley.

Related Terms

  • antonym: powered aircraft - an aircraft equipped with an engine
  • synonym: sailplane - a type of glider designed for sustained flight
