pass on

verb
decline an offer or relay information
Pronunciation: / pæs ɑn / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒn / (BrE)
1. To decline or forgo something, especially an opportunity or offer. 2. To convey or relay information or a message to another person.

Examples

To pass on dessert
He decided to pass on dessert after having such a large meal.
I think I'll pass on the invitation to the party tonight.
Please pass on this message to your supervisor as soon as possible.
