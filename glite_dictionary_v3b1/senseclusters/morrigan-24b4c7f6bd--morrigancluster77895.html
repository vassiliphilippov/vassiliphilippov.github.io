Morrigan

proper noun
Celtic war and fate goddess
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈmɒrɪɡən / (BrE)
In Irish mythology, a goddess associated with war, fate, and death, often appearing as a crow or in multiple forms.

Examples

The Morrigan as a crow
Legends say that warriors sometimes saw the Morrigan as a crow on the battlefield.
Stories of the Morrigan have inspired many modern works of literature and art.
The Morrigan is often depicted as a shapeshifter who predicts the outcome of battles.
