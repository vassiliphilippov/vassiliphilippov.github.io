ouster

noun
someone who ousts another
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊstər / (AmE), / ˈaʊstə / (BrE)
a person who removes or supplants someone from a position or place

Examples

A political ouster
History often paints the revolutionary as both a hero and an ouster of the old regime.
Some saw the deputy as an ambitious ouster waiting for his chance to take control.
The board considered the new CEO an ouster after he replaced his predecessor so abruptly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: usurper - a person who takes a position of power illegally or by force
