disease

noun
illness or abnormal health condition
2.3 194/m
Pronunciation: / d…™Ààziz / (AmE), / d…™ÀàziÀêz / (BrE)
A disorder or condition that impairs normal physical or mental functioning, especially one that produces specific symptoms or affects a specific part of the body.

Examples

A disease condition
Her immune system fights off disease more effectively than most people's.
Scientists are working to find a cure for the rare disease that affects the nervous system.
The spread of disease in the crowded city posed a serious public health concern.

Synonyms

  • illness
    1.1 58/m
    illness is the personal experience of being unwell with symptoms, while disease is the clinical condition defined by doctors.
  • disorder
    4.3 46/m
    disorder describes a disturbance in body or mind function, while disease is a specific medical condition with clear causes or signs.
  • sickness
    1.0 5.38/m
    sickness means the state of feeling ill or nauseous, often in everyday speech, while disease is a diagnosed medical condition.
  • malady
    7.9 1.33/m
    malady sounds old-fashioned or literary for any illness, while disease is the modern medical term used by doctors.
