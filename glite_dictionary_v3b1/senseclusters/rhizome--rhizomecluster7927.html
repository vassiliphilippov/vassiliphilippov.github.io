rhizome

noun
horizontal underground plant stem (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪˌzoʊm / (AmE), / ˈraɪzəʊm / (BrE)
a horizontal, usually underground plant stem that produces shoots above and roots below, serving as a means of reproduction

Examples

A rhizome stem
Ginger is an example of a plant whose edible part is a rhizome.
Some weeds spread rapidly due to their underground rhizomes.
The fern's rhizome allows it to colonize new areas and survive harsh conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rootstock - a horizontal underground stem; another term for rhizome
