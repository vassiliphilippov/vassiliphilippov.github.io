mustard seed

noun
seed used for making mustard
1.6 2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌstɚd siːd / (AmE), / ˈmʌstəd siːd / (BrE)
A small seed, usually black or white, from the mustard plant, used whole or ground to make mustard condiments or powders.

Examples

Whole mustard seed in pickles
A single mustard seed can add a sharp flavor to pickled vegetables.
Chefs often grind mustard seed to prepare their own spicy condiments.
She scattered mustard seed on top of the salad for extra crunch.
