toe

verb
drive a nail at an angle
6.5 6.7
Pronunciation: /toʊ/ /; / /təʊ/ (AmE), /təʊ/ /; / /toʊ/ (BrE)
to drive (a nail or similar object) into wood at an oblique angle, rather than perpendicular to the surface, usually for added strength or when access is limited.

Examples

To toe a nail
Always toe the nails when working in tight corners for a stronger hold.
If you can't reach straight in, you may need to toe the screws to hold the bracket.
To secure the frame, the carpenter had to toe each nail into the beam at an angle.
