Podocarpus elatus

noun
Australian tree (brown pine, Illawarra plum)
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊdəˈkɑːrpəs ɪˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌpəʊdəˈkɑːpəs ɪˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A large Australian tree with straight-grained yellow wood that turns brown when exposed to air, commonly known as the brown pine or Illawarra plum.

Examples

Podocarpus elatus tree
Gardeners often plant Podocarpus elatus as an ornamental tree due to its attractive foliage.
The Podocarpus elatus is valued both for its timber and edible fruit in eastern Australia.
The wood of Podocarpus elatus darkens to a rich brown as it ages.
