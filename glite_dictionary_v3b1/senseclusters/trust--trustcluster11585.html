trust

noun
firm belief in reliability or truth
1.0 39/m
Pronunciation: / trÊŒst / (AmE), / trÊŒst / (BrE)
firm belief or confidence in the reliability, honesty, or ability of someone or something

Examples

Mutual trust
After working together for years, there was a deep trust between the partners.
He placed his trust in the guide, believing she knew the safest path.
It's difficult to rebuild trust once it has been broken.
Her trust in her friends grew stronger with each shared secret.
The scandal severely damaged public trust in the institution.
Years of honesty earned him the trust of his colleagues.
It's important to have trust between team members in order to work effectively.
She placed her trust in the lawyer, believing he would represent her honestly.
Years of lying destroyed the trust in their relationship.

Synonyms

  • confidence
    1.9 24/m
    confidence is a personal feeling that someone can do something, while trust is a firmer belief in their honesty or ability.
  • faith
    1.4 49/m
    faith is complete belief often without proof and used in religion, while trust is a tested confidence in honesty or ability.
  • reliance
    3.2 14/m
    reliance is dependence on someone or something for help or support, while trust is belief in their honesty or ability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distrust - lack of trust or confidence
  • synonym: reliance - dependence on or trust in someone or something
