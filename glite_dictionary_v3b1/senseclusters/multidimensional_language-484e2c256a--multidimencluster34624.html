multidimensional language

noun
programming language using multiple dimensions for expressions
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌl.ti.dɪˈmɛn.ʃə.nəl ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmʌl.tɪ.dɪˈmen.ʃən.əl ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A programming language in which expressions are structured in more than one dimension, rather than in a single sequence.

Examples

A multidimensional language design
Researchers are developing a new multidimensional language to simplify complex data visualizations.
She wrote her thesis on the theoretical foundations of multidimensional language design.
The workshop focused on how a multidimensional language differs from traditional linear programming languages.
