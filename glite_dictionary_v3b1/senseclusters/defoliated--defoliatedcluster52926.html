defoliated

adjective
lacking leaves
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfoʊliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈfəʊlieɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant or tree) having been deprived of leaves, typically by disease, weather, or chemical agents.

Examples

A defoliated tree
After the storm, the old maple tree was completely defoliated, its branches bare against the sky.
During autumn, many trees become naturally defoliated as part of their life cycle.
The use of certain pesticides can leave crops defoliated and weakened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foliated - having or covered with leaves
  • synonym: leafless - having no leaves
