deliquesce

verb
become liquid by absorbing moisture
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs / (AmE), / ˌdɛlɪˈkwɛs / (BrE)
to become liquid by absorbing moisture from the air, especially used for certain salts or decaying substances

Examples

To deliquesce in air
Certain salts will deliquesce if stored in a humid environment for too long.
Some chemicals deliquesce so rapidly that they must be kept in airtight containers.
The fruit began to deliquesce after being left out in the open air.
After several days in the damp cellar, the old mushrooms began to deliquesce.
Certain salts will deliquesce if left exposed to humid air.
The corpse of the small animal seemed to deliquesce rapidly in the hot weather.

  • synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid by heat
