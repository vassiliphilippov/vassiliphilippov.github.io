quinquereme

noun
ancient warship
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪŋkwəˌriːm / (AmE), / ˈkwɪŋkwɪriːm / (BrE)
an ancient warship with five rows of oars on each side, used especially by the Greeks and Romans

Examples

Ancient quinquereme warship
The Roman fleet launched a powerful quinquereme against their enemies during the naval battle.
Historians believe that building a quinquereme required advanced shipbuilding skills and many resources.
A quinquereme could carry hundreds of rowers and soldiers across the Mediterranean Sea.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bireme - an ancient warship with two rows of oars on each side
  • degree lower: trireme - an ancient warship with three rows of oars on each side
