leash

noun
figurative restraint or control
4.9 3.7
Pronunciation: /liːʃ/ (AmE), /liːʃ/ (BrE)
control or restraint imposed on someone or something, often used figuratively to describe the extent of freedom allowed.

Examples

A short leash
As the children grew older, their parents gave them a longer leash to make their own decisions.
The new regulations put a leash on risky financial practices in the industry.
The project manager kept a tight leash on the team's spending to avoid overruns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants
  • synonym: restraint - the action of keeping someone or something under control
