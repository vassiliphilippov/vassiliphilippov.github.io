flick

verb
throw with a quick sudden motion
4.1
Pronunciation: / flɪk / (AmE), / flɪk / (BrE)
to make something move, strike, or remove with a quick, light, or sudden movement, especially using your hand or finger

Examples

To flick a switch
He flicked the ball into the air and caught it skillfully.
She flicked the crumbs off the table with her finger.
The cat flicked its tail in annoyance.
He flicked his fingers to get her attention.
She flicked the switch, making it snap loudly.
You need to flick the tab to open the can.
He flicked the switch and the lights came on.
She absentmindedly flicked the crumbs off the table with her finger.
With a practiced motion, he flicked the playing card across the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brush - to remove something lightly or quickly, usually from a surface
  • synonym: flip - to toss or move something suddenly, often by turning it over
  • synonym: flutter - move with quick, light movements
  • synonym: jerk - move with a quick, sudden motion
  • synonym: snap - to make a sudden, sharp cracking or clicking sound
  • degree higher: strike - to hit someone or something firmly or forcefully
  • synonym: tap - to hit something gently, often making a soft sound
