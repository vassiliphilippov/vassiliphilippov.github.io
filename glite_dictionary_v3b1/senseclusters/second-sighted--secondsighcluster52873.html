second-sighted

adjective
able to foresee the future
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkənd ˈsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkənd ˈsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
Having the supposed ability to foresee future events; possessing second sight.

Examples

A second-sighted person
His second-sighted visions were both a blessing and a curse to his family.
Many believed the second-sighted woman could predict the outcome of village events.
The legend spoke of a second-sighted child born every generation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clairvoyant - having or claiming to have the power of seeing objects or actions beyond natural vision
