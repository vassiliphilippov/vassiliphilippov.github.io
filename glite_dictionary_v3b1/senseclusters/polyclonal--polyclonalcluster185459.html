polyclonal

noun
mixed antibodies
8.2
Pronunciation: /pɑː.li.ˈkloʊ.nəl/ (AmE), /pɒl.ɪ.ˈkləʊ.nəl/ (BrE)
a preparation of antibodies produced by different B-cell clones, containing a mixture that recognizes multiple parts of an antigen

Examples

A polyclonal mixture
The researchers used a polyclonal to detect several types of viral proteins in the sample.
A polyclonal can recognize multiple regions of an antigen, making it useful for certain laboratory tests.
Unlike a monoclonal, a polyclonal contains antibodies from many different B-cell clones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monoclonal - an antibody preparation from a single B-cell clone, recognizing one epitope
