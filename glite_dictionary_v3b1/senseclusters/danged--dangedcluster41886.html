danged

adjective, adverb
mild annoyance
8.6
Pronunciation: / dæŋd / (AmE), / dæŋd / (BrE)
used informally to add emphasis or express mild annoyance, often instead of 'damned' (before adjectives, adverbs, or nouns)

Examples

Those danged keys
I can't find those danged keys anywhere, no matter how hard I look.
That danged alarm clock woke me up an hour too early again.
He tripped over the danged step for the third time this week.
I was so danged hungry that I ate two sandwiches in five minutes.
That was a danged good movie, and I would watch it again.
He was danged lucky to catch the last bus home last night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damned - used to express annoyance or emphasis, often considered stronger
  • synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely
