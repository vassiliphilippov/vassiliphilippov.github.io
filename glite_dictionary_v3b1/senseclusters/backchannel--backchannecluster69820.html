backchannel

noun
private communication
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌtʃænəl / (BrE)
a private or secondary means of communication used alongside the main conversation or event

Examples

Secret backchannel talks
During the meeting, they kept a backchannel open to discuss their strategy privately.
The students used a backchannel chat to share notes while the lecture was ongoing.
Journalists often rely on a backchannel to verify sensitive information discreetly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main channel - the primary or official means of communication
  • synonym: side channel - an alternative or secondary route for communication
