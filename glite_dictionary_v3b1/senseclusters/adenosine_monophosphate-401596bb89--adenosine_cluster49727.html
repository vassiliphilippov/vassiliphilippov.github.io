adenosine monophosphate

noun
a metabolic nucleotide in muscle cells
6.6
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈden.ə.siːn/ /; / /ə.ˈden.ə.sɪn/ /; / /æd.ə.ˈnoʊ.saɪn/ (AmE), /ə.ˈden.əʊ.siːn/ (BrE)
A nucleotide present in muscle cells and important in metabolism, which can be reversibly converted to ADP and ATP.

Examples

adenosine monophosphate in muscles
Muscles synthesize adenosine monophosphate during intense exercise to help restore energy balance.
Scientists analyzed the concentration of adenosine monophosphate in the tissue samples from laboratory animals.
When energy is required, adenosine monophosphate is converted into ADP and ATP in living cells.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: adenosine diphosphate - a nucleotide with two phosphate groups, product of AMP phosphorylation
  • degree higher: adenosine triphosphate - a nucleotide with three phosphate groups, key energy carrier in cells
  • synonym: nucleotide - basic building block of nucleic acids like DNA and RNA
