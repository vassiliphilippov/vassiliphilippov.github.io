argus pheasant

noun
large, patterned Southeast Asian pheasant
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrɡəs ˈfiːzənt / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡəs ˈfiːzənt / (BrE)
a large, brilliantly patterned pheasant native to Southeast Asia, known for the eye-like spots on its wings and tail

Examples

The argus pheasant bird
During the tour, we spotted an argus pheasant walking gracefully through the rainforest undergrowth.
The argus pheasant is admired for its spectacular plumage and elaborate courtship displays.
The feathers of the argus pheasant are covered in intricate patterns that resemble eyes.
