eldest

noun
the oldest sibling or child
Pronunciation: / ˈɛldɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛl.dɪst / (BrE)
the oldest child or offspring among a group of siblings

Examples

The eldest in the family
As the eldest, he set a good example for his brothers and sisters.
She is the eldest of three daughters in her family.
The eldest often takes on more responsibility for younger siblings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: youngest - the child or offspring who is the last born
