disinhibition

noun
loss of restraint
8.2
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ɪn.hɪ.ˈbɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪs.ɪn.ɪ.ˈbɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪs.ɪn.hɪ.ˈbɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪs.ɪn.ɪ.ˈbɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a loss or reduction of restraint, resulting in more open, impulsive, or uninhibited behavior

Examples

Alcohol disinhibition
Alcohol can cause disinhibition and make people act more freely.
Some medications may lead to disinhibition in elderly patients, resulting in unusual behavior.
The comedian's disinhibition on stage made the audience laugh at his bold jokes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uninhibitedness - the quality of being free from restraint or self-control
