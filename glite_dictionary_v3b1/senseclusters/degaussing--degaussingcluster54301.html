degaussing

noun
erasing data on magnetic media
8.3
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈɡaʊ.sɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈɡaʊ.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /diː.ˈɡaʊ.sɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈɡaʊ.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in computing and data security) the process of erasing or sanitizing information on magnetic storage media by applying a strong magnetic field to remove recorded magnetic patterns.

Examples

Hard drive degaussing
Before disposal, the company required degaussing of all magnetic hard drives to meet security standards.
The technician performed degaussing on the tape backup to ensure no recoverable data remained afterward.
Military protocols mandate degaussing for classified disks before they are transported or destroyed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data sanitization - methods for securely erasing or destroying data so it cannot be recovered
  • synonym: demagnetization - the process of removing magnetic properties from an object
