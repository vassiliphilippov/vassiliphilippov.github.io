therian

noun
marsupial or placental mammal
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪəriən / (AmE), / ˈθɪəriən / (BrE)
a mammal belonging to the group that includes marsupials and placental mammals, but not monotremes

Examples

A therian animal
The kangaroo is a well-known therian found in Australia.
Unlike monotremes, every therian gives birth to live young.
Scientists study the evolution of therians to understand mammalian diversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mammal - a warm-blooded animal with hair or fur that feeds its young with milk
  • antonym: monotreme - an egg-laying mammal such as the platypus or echidna
