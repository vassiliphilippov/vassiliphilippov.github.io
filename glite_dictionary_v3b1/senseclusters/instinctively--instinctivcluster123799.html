instinctively

adverb
in a manner driven by instinct
4.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈstɪŋk.tɪv.liː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈstɪŋk.tɪv.lɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈstɪŋk.tɪv.liː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈstɪŋk.tɪv.lɪ/ (BrE)
In a way that is driven by instinct rather than conscious thought or reasoning.

Examples

To act instinctively
He instinctively knew something was wrong as soon as he entered the room.
She instinctively reached out to catch the falling glass before it hit the ground.
The dog instinctively protected its owner from danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automatically - without conscious thought; by reflex
  • antonym: deliberately - done consciously and intentionally
  • synonym: impulsively - acting on impulse rather than thought
