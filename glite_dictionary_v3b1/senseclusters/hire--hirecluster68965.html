hire

verb
obtain use by paying rent
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪə / (BrE)
to pay to use something or someone's services for a short period of time

Examples

To hire a car
It is common to hire extra equipment for big events rather than buying it.
We decided to hire a car for our holiday in Spain.
You can hire bicycles from the shop next to the station.
They are hiring a boat for the afternoon to explore the nearby islands.
We decided to hire a car for our trip around the countryside.
She hired a guide to show her the historic parts of the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buy - to obtain something by paying money for it permanently
  • synonym: lease - to make a legal agreement to use something for a set time
  • synonym: rent - to pay for the use of something (especially property or equipment)
