thrall

noun
person held in bondage or slavery
Pronunciation: / θrɔl / (AmE), / θrɑːl / (BrE)
a person who is held in bondage or slavery; a captive or serf

Examples

A thrall in chains
He spent years as a thrall in a distant land, longing for freedom.
Legends spoke of heroes breaking the chains of thrall and reclaiming their freedom.
The conquerors reduced the villagers to thralls and forced them to labor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: master - a person who has control or ownership over someone in bondage
  • synonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to obey them
