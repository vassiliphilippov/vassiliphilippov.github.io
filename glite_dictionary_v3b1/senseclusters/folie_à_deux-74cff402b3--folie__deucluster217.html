folie à deux

noun
shared mental disorder between two people
Pronunciation: / foʊˈli ɑː ˈduː / (AmE), / fɒˈliː a dɜː / (BrE)
a rare psychiatric syndrome in which two closely associated people share the same delusional beliefs or hallucinations

Examples

Folie à deux syndrome
Cases of folie à deux are extremely rare in modern psychiatric practice.
Investigators found evidence that the siblings’ bizarre behavior was due to folie à deux.
The psychiatrist diagnosed the couple with folie à deux after discovering their identical paranoid delusions.
