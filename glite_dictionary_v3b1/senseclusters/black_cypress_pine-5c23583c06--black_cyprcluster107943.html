black cypress pine

noun
Australian tree valued for timber and resin
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈsaɪprəs paɪn / (AmE), / blæk ˈsaɪprəs paɪn / (BrE)
An Australian evergreen tree (Callitris endlicheri) with small, flattened scale-like leaves and dark brown seeds, valued for its timber and resin.

Examples

The black cypress pine tree
Carpenters prize the wood of the black cypress pine for its durability and fine grain.
The black cypress pine thrives in dry, rocky terrain across parts of Australia.
The park is dotted with black cypress pines, providing shelter for many native bird species.
