dephase

verb
to alter signal phase
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˈfeɪz /; / diːˈfeɪz / (AmE), / diˈfeɪz /; / diːˈfeɪz / (BrE)
to change the phase of a wave, signal, or oscillation relative to another, usually by delaying or advancing it

Examples

To dephase a signal
The engineer needed to dephase the signal to reduce interference in the circuit.
By carefully dephasing the two audio tracks, the technician eliminated the unwanted echo.
Scientists often dephase laser pulses to control the timing of experiments.
The technician needed to dephase the signals to prevent interference in the system.
Researchers dephased the laser beams to study their combined effects on the material.
You can dephase the audio channels to create a surround sound experience.
