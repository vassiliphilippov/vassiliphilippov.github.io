allusively

adverb
indirectly referring
Pronunciation: / əˈluːsɪvli / (AmE), / əˈluːsɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that refers to something indirectly or by suggestion

Examples

To speak allusively
The author allusively referenced historical events without naming them directly.
He allusively hinted at his disappointment during the meeting.
Her comments were allusively critical, never stating her opinion outright.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicitly - in a clear and direct manner
  • synonym: indirectly - in a way that is not direct or explicit
