nonspecifically

adverb
in a non-specific way
Pronunciation: / nɑnspəˈsɪfɪkli / (AmE), / nɒnspɪˈsɪfɪkli / (BrE)
In a way that is not specific or not limited to a particular kind or category.

Examples

To stain nonspecifically
Certain dyes stain tissues nonspecifically, making analysis more difficult.
The antibody reacted nonspecifically with several proteins in the sample.
This drug affects the immune system nonspecifically, rather than targeting a single pathway.

Related Terms

  • antonym: specifically - in a way that is precise or clearly defined
