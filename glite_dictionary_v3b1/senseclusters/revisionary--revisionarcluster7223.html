revisionary

adjective
relating to revision
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by making revisions, changes, or modifications to something established

Examples

Revisionary changes
The committee adopted a revisionary stance, suggesting several updates to the policy.
Her revisionary ideas challenged the accepted theories in the field.
A revisionary approach can lead to significant improvements in outdated systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and resisting change
  • synonym: reformist - favoring or promoting change and improvement
