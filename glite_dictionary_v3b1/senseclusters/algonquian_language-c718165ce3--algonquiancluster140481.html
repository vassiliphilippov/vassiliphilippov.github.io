Algonquian language

noun
North American Indigenous language family
Pronunciation: / ælˈɡɑŋkwiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ælˈɡɒŋkwɪən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of North American Indigenous languages spoken from eastern Canada to the Great Plains, including Cree, Ojibwa, and Blackfoot

Examples

Algonquian language family
Linguists have extensively studied the structure of each Algonquian language.
Many tribes speak an Algonquian language as part of their cultural heritage.
The Blackfoot people use an Algonquian language distinct from that of neighboring tribes.
