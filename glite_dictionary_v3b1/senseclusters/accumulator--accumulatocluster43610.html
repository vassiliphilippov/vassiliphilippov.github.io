accumulator

noun
device for storing electrical energy
3.6 5.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkjuː.mjə.leɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkjuː.mjə.leɪ.tə/ (BrE)
A device, especially a rechargeable battery, that stores electrical energy for later use.

Examples

Car accumulator
Hybrid cars often use an accumulator to store energy generated during braking.
Some early radios were powered by a lead-acid accumulator that needed regular recharging.
The electrician replaced the old accumulator with a new one to store power more efficiently.
