barbican

noun
defensive outpost or tower in fortifications
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrbɪkən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbɪkən / (BrE)
a tower or fortified outpost, typically forming part of the outer defenses of a castle or city wall

Examples

The barbican tower
During the siege, archers defended the barbican against the approaching enemy troops.
The ancient castle's barbican still stands, guarding the main entrance as it did centuries ago.
Tourists admired the restored barbican, which provided a glimpse into medieval military architecture.
