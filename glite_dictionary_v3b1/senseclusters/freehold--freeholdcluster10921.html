freehold

noun
ownership of property for an unlimited period
4.2 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːˌhoʊld /; / ˈfriˌhoʊld / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌhəʊld /; / ˈfriːhəʊld / (BrE)
legal ownership of land or property for an unlimited or indefinite period (not rented or leased)

Examples

The freehold of property
He bought the freehold of the house and became its outright owner.
Purchasing a freehold property means you own both the building and the land.
The freehold was transferred to the new landlord after the sale.
He purchased the freehold to avoid paying rent to a landlord.
Owning a freehold property gives you more security than a leasehold arrangement.
The freehold of the building was transferred to the new owner.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leasehold - ownership of property for a fixed period under a lease
