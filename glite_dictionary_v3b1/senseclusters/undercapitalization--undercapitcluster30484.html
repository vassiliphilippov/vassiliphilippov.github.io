undercapitalization

noun
insufficient capital
8.2
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.kæp.ɪ.tə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.kæp.ɪ.tə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a situation where a business or organization does not have enough money or investment to operate or grow properly

Examples

Company undercapitalization
The company faced undercapitalization after several years of declining profits and poor investment decisions.
Regulators warned the bank about its undercapitalization and the risks it posed to customers.
Persistent undercapitalization can lead to bankruptcy if not addressed promptly by management.
The startup failed due to chronic undercapitalization and could not expand its operations.
Many small businesses struggle with undercapitalization in their first few years.
Government programs aim to address undercapitalization in rural areas by encouraging investment.
The company struggled with undercapitalization and could not expand its operations as planned.
Many small businesses fail in their first year due to undercapitalization.
Investors were concerned about the risk of undercapitalization in the new venture.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: insolvency - the state of being unable to pay debts owed
  • antonym: overcapitalization - a situation where a business has more capital than it needs
  • synonym: underfunding - providing less money than is needed
