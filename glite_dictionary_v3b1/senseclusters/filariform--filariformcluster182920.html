filariform

noun
infective nematode larva
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪl.ə.rɪ.fɔːrm/ (AmE), /ˈfɪl.ə.rɪ.fɔːm/ (BrE)
the infective, third-stage larva of certain parasitic roundworms, adapted for penetrating a host

Examples

Filariform larva

Doctors explained that the filariform stage is responsible for infecting humans.
Researchers studied how the filariform penetrates the skin to begin infection.
