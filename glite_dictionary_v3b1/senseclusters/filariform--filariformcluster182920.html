filariform

noun
infective nematode larva
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɪˈlɛrɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / fɪˈlærɪfɔːm / (BrE)
the infective, third-stage larva of certain parasitic roundworms, adapted for penetrating a host

Examples

Filariform larva
The laboratory identified a filariform in the patient's stool sample.
Doctors explained that the filariform stage is responsible for infecting humans.
Researchers studied how the filariform penetrates the skin to begin infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: larva - an immature form of an animal that undergoes metamorphosis
  • antonym: rhabditiform - a non-infective, early-stage larva of certain nematodes
