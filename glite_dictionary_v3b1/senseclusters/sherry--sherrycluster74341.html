sherry

noun
fortified Spanish wine
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈʃɛri / (BrE)
a type of fortified wine, ranging from dry to sweet, originally from the Jerez region of southern Spain, typically served as an aperitif

Examples

A glass of sherry
Many recipes call for a splash of sherry to enhance the flavor of sauces.
She poured herself a glass of sherry before dinner.
They visited a vineyard famous for producing excellent sherry in southern Spain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: port - a sweet, fortified red wine from Portugal, often served as a dessert wine
  • synonym: vermouth - an aromatized, fortified wine flavored with various botanicals, often consumed as an aperitif
