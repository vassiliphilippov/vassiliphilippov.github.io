regional anesthesia

noun
anesthesia numbing a body region
3.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.dʒən.əl æn.əs.ˈθiː.ə/ /; / /ˈriː.dʒən.əl æn.əs.ˈθiː.ʒə/ (AmE), /ˈriː.dʒən.əl æn.əs.ˈθiː.zi.ə/ /; / /ˈriː.dʒən.əl æn.əs.ˈθiː.ə/ (BrE)
a type of anesthesia that numbs a specific area of the body by blocking nerve sensation in that region, often used for surgeries or procedures

Examples

Regional anesthesia for surgery
During childbirth, regional anesthesia such as an epidural is commonly administered for pain relief.
The patient received regional anesthesia for the knee surgery, so he remained awake throughout the procedure.
Unlike general anesthesia, regional anesthesia allows for faster recovery and fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general anesthesia - anesthesia affecting the whole body, leading to unconsciousness
  • degree lower: local anaesthesia - loss of sensation in a small, specific area of the body
  • degree lower: local anesthesia - anesthesia numbing a small, specific area
