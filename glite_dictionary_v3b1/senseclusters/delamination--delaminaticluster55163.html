delamination

noun
separation of layers
8.7
Pronunciation: /də.lə.mɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.lə.mɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /də.lə.mɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.læ.mɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or defect where layers of a material separate or split apart

Examples

Delamination in material
The engineer discovered delamination in the composite panel during the routine inspection.
If delamination occurs in the windshield, it can reduce visibility and safety.
Regular maintenance helps prevent delamination in laminated flooring materials.

Water damage can cause delamination in wooden flooring, leading to costly repairs.
Regular inspections help prevent delamination in composite materials used in construction.
