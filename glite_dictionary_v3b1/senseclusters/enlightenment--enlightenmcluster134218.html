enlightenment

noun

The act of gaining knowledge and understanding, especially resulting from education or exposure to new ideas.

Examples

Intellectual enlightenment
He viewed his years at university as a period of intellectual enlightenment.
Her trip abroad brought her a sense of enlightenment about different cultures and ways of thinking.
The spread of scientific knowledge led to an era of enlightenment in many parts of the world.

Synonyms

  • illumination
    5.3 0.49/m
    illumination gives a clear explanation that makes ideas easy to understand, while enlightenment is a broader and more lasting gain of knowledge.
  • realization
    3.6 13/m
    realization is a sudden moment of clear understanding, while enlightenment describes a longer process of learning and deep knowledge.
  • awakening
    4.4 4.89/m
    awakening is often an emotional or sudden awareness of something important, while enlightenment usually grows through study and exposure.
  • edification
    7.4 0.91/m
    edification means being taught or morally improved by instruction, while enlightenment focuses on gaining broad understanding through learning and new ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: education - the process of receiving or giving systematic instruction
  • antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or awareness
