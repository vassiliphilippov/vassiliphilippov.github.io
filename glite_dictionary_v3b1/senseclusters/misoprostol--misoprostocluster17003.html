misoprostol

noun
uterine medication
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪsəˈprɑːstəl / (AmE), / ˌmɪsəˈprɒstɒl / (BrE)
a medication used to prevent stomach ulcers and for reproductive health purposes, such as inducing labor, treating miscarriage, abortion, or preventing heavy bleeding after childbirth

Examples

Misoprostol medication
The doctor prescribed misoprostol to help complete the miscarriage safely.
In some countries, misoprostol is also used to induce labor when necessary.
After taking misoprostol, she experienced cramping as her body expelled the remaining tissue.
The doctor prescribed misoprostol to help prevent stomach ulcers caused by anti-inflammatory drugs.
In some countries, misoprostol is used with mifepristone for a safe medical abortion.
Before the procedure, the nurse explained how misoprostol would be administered to induce labor.
Doctors may prescribe misoprostol to help prevent excessive bleeding after delivery.
The nurse explained how misoprostol works to contract the uterus and reduce blood loss.
In some countries, misoprostol is an essential medicine for maternal health care.
