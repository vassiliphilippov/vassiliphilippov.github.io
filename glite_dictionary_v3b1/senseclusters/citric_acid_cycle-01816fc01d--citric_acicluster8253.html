citric acid cycle

noun
metabolic cycle in cells generating energy
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtrɪk ˈæsɪd ˈsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪtrɪk ˈæsɪd ˈsaɪkl̩ / (BrE)
a series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions in mitochondria involving the oxidative metabolism of acetyl groups, producing energy for cellular functions

Examples

The citric acid cycle process
Biology students often memorize the steps of the citric acid cycle for their exams.
Disruption of the citric acid cycle can lead to serious energy deficiencies in cells.
The citric acid cycle is an essential metabolic pathway found in nearly all living organisms.
