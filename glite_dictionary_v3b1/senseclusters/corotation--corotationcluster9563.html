corotation

noun
shared rotation
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊroʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊrəʊˈteɪʃən /; / ˌkɔːrəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or situation where two or more objects rotate together at the same speed, especially when an object's orbit matches the rotation of a central body

Examples

Corotation with orbit
The planet's rings are in corotation with its magnetic field.
Scientists observed corotation between the moon and the planet during the experiment.
In some galaxies, stars and gas clouds exhibit corotation at certain distances from the center.
The satellite entered the corotation zone, where its orbit matched the planet's rotation speed.
Scientists study corotation to understand how particles move in planetary rings.
At the corotation radius, the star's magnetic field and the disk rotate together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: differential rotation - a situation where different parts of a rotating body move at different speeds
  • synonym: rotation - the act of turning around a central point or axis
  • synonym: synchronization - the process of causing things to happen at the same time or rate
