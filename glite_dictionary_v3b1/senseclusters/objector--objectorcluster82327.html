a person who expresses opposition or disapproval, especially towards a policy or practice

Examples

A conscientious objector
As a conscientious objector, he refused to perform military service.
One vocal objector spoke out against the proposed law at the town meeting.
The court listened to each objector before making its final decision.

Synonyms

  • opposer
    4.5 0.09/m
    an opposer actively resists or opposes an action or person, while an objector mainly expresses disapproval or formal objection.
  • dissenter
    7.2 2.36/m
    a dissenter refuses to follow an established policy or belief, while an objector often simply voices disagreement or formal objection.
  • dissident
    7.5 4.47/m
    a dissident opposes official policy, often for political reasons, while an objector expresses general disapproval or complaint.
  • protester
    2.9 15/m
    a protester joins public actions or marches to show strong objection, while an objector may only state disapproval privately or formally.
  • contester
    6.2 0.00/m
    a contester formally challenges a result or decision in court or process, while an objector simply objects to a policy or practice.
