noncognate

adjective
not sharing origin
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkɑɡneɪt / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒɡneɪt / (BrE)
describing words in different languages that do not share a common origin

Examples

Noncognate words
The English word 'dog' and the French word 'chien' are noncognate terms.
Linguists often compare noncognate words to study language development and borrowing.
Spanish 'gato' and English 'cat' are not noncognate because they share a common root.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cognate - having the same linguistic origin as another word
