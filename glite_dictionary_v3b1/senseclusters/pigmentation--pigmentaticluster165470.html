pigmentation

noun
coloration of living tissues by pigment
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪɡmənˈteɪʃən /; / ˌpɪɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɪɡmɛnˈteɪʃən /; / ˌpɪɡ.mɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the natural color or coloring of living tissues (such as skin, hair, or plant tissue) caused by biological pigments

Examples

Skin pigmentation
Certain genetic conditions can lead to abnormal pigmentation of the skin.
Chameleons are known for their remarkable abilities to change their pigmentation.
The dermatologist examined the unusual pigmentation on her patient's arm.
Albinism is a condition characterized by a lack of pigmentation.
Changes in pigmentation can indicate underlying health problems.
The doctor examined the pigmentation on her skin to determine its cause.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coloration - appearance with regard to color
  • antonym: depigmentation - loss or removal of natural pigment from skin or tissue
  • synonym: melanin - a pigment giving color to skin, hair, and eyes in humans and animals
