splendidly

adverb
in an impressive or excellent manner
Pronunciation: / ˈsplɛn.dɪd.li / (AmE), / ˈsplɛn.dɪd.li / (BrE)
in a very impressive, excellent, or admirable way

Examples

Handled splendidly
He handled the challenging situation splendidly and impressed everyone.
She performed splendidly in the lead role, earning a standing ovation.
The garden looked splendidly colorful in the afternoon sun.
She performed splendidly on her final exams, earning top marks.
The garden looked splendidly colorful in the late afternoon sun.
The hall was splendidly decorated for the royal banquet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: badly - in a poor or inferior manner
  • synonym: magnificently - in a manner that is grand, impressive, or lavish
  • antonym: poorly - in a way that is not impressive or is of low quality
  • synonym: wonderfully - in a wonderful or excellent way
