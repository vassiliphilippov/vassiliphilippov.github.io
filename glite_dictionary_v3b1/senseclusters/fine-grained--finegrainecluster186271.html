fine-grained

adjective
having very small particles or grains
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪn ˌɡreɪnd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn ˌɡreɪnd / (BrE)
consisting of or having very small, closely packed particles or a smooth, fine texture (used for materials like sugar, wood, stone, etc.)

Examples

Fine-grained sugar
The chef prefers using fine-grained sugar to achieve a delicate texture in desserts.
The geologist described the soil as fine-grained, making it difficult for water to drain quickly.
This fine-grained sand is perfect for making smooth glass surfaces.
Because of its fine-grained structure, this wood is favored by carpenters for detailed work.
Scientific analysis revealed the fossils were embedded in a fine-grained sedimentary rock.
The countertop was made of fine-grained granite that felt smooth to the touch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coarse-grained - consisting of relatively large particles or grains
