haptics

noun
touch feedback technology
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhæp.tɪks/ (AmE), /ˈhæp.tɪks/ (BrE)
the science and technology of touch and tactile feedback, especially in electronic devices

Examples

Haptics technology
Many video game controllers use haptics to make the experience more immersive.
The new smartwatch features advanced haptics for more precise notifications.
Researchers are developing haptics that allow users to feel virtual objects.
Advances in haptics have made virtual reality experiences feel more realistic through simulated touch.
Researchers in haptics are developing gloves that let users feel virtual objects.
The field of haptics combines neuroscience, engineering, and psychology to understand how we sense touch.
