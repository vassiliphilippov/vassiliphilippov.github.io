predestinate

verb
to foreordain by divine will
Pronunciation: / prɪˈdɛstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɛstɪneɪt / (BrE)
to determine or ordain in advance, especially by divine will or fate

Examples

To predestinate a life
He argued that future events are predestinated and unchangeable.
Many people in the congregation believed that God had predestinated their fates from birth.
The doctrine taught that salvation was predestinated for the chosen few.
