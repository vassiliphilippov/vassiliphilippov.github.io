probably

adverb
likely but not certain
1.0 400/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêb…ôbli / (AmE), / Ààpr…íb…ôbli / (BrE)
used to say that something is likely to happen or to be true, but is not certain

Examples

Will probably happen
It's probably going to rain later, so take an umbrella just in case.
She will probably arrive before noon, but I'm not completely sure.
They probably forgot about the meeting, given how quiet it's been.
He probably forgot about the meeting since he didn't show up.
It will probably rain later this afternoon, so take an umbrella.
She will probably arrive at the station around six o'clock.

Synonyms

  • likely
    1.0 80/m
    likely is used to say something has a good chance of happening, while probably expresses general likelihood without certainty.
  • presumably
    4.3 20/m
    presumably is used when you assume something from facts or signs, while probably simply shows general likelihood without proof.
  • chances are
    3.2 4/m
    chances are is a casual phrase that says something is more likely to happen, while probably is neutral and fits many contexts.
  • in all probability
    3.8 0.6/m
    in all probability is a formal phrase suggesting a very high chance, while probably is more general and used in everyday speech.
  • in all likelihood
    3.2 2/m
    in all likelihood is a formal idiom that stresses near certainty, while probably is milder and more common in speech.
  • plausibly
    6.3 1/m
    plausibly means something seems reasonable or possible, while probably states it is likely to happen or to be true.
