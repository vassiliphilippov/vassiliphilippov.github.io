sonde

noun
medical tube or catheter
Pronunciation: / sɑnd / (AmE), / sɒnd / (BrE)
a thin tube or catheter inserted into the body for medical purposes such as feeding, draining, or measuring internal conditions

Examples

Medical sonde tube
The doctor carefully inserted a sonde to monitor the patient's internal pressure.
A feeding sonde was used to provide nutrition to the patient after surgery.
The nurse checked the placement of the sonde before starting the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catheter - a flexible tube inserted into the body to remove or deliver fluids
  • synonym: probe - a slender instrument used to explore or examine inside the body
