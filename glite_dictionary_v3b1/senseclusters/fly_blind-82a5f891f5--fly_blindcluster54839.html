fly blind

verb
operate an aircraft using only instruments
Pronunciation: / flaɪ blaɪnd / (AmE), / flaɪ blaɪnd / (BrE)
To operate an aircraft solely by relying on instruments rather than visual references.

Examples

To fly blind in fog
After losing radio contact, she continued to fly blind until the storm passed.
In severe weather, experienced aviators may be required to fly blind for safety.
The pilot had to fly blind through the thick fog when visibility dropped to zero.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instrument flying - aviating by instruments; not using visual cues
