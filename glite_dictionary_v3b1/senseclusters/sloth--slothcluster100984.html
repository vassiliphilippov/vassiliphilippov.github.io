sloth

noun
a slow-moving tree-dwelling mammal
Pronunciation: / sloʊθ / (AmE), / sləʊθ / (BrE)
a slow-moving, tree-dwelling mammal native to Central and South America, known for hanging upside down from branches and eating leaves and fruits

Examples

A sloth hangs
At the zoo, we watched a sloth slowly reach out to grab a leaf.
Scientists study the sloth to learn how it has adapted to a low-energy lifestyle.
The sloth spends most of its life hanging upside down from tree branches in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bradypus - three-toed sloth, a type of sloth
  • synonym: laziness - the quality of being unwilling to work or use energy (primary sense of sloth)
