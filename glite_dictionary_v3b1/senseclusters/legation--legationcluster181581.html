legation

noun
a diplomatic mission led by a minister
Pronunciation: / lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a permanent diplomatic mission in a foreign country, headed by a minister rather than an ambassador

Examples

French legation building
He worked at the British legation before it was upgraded to an embassy.
Many smaller states maintain a legation rather than an embassy in some capitals.
The country's legation in Paris was established in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embassy - a permanent diplomatic mission headed by an ambassador
