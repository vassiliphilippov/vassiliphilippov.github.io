depolymerization

noun
breaking down polymers
Pronunciation: / diˌpɑlɪmərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌpɒlɪməraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a polymer is broken down into its smaller units or monomers

Examples

Depolymerization process

The depolymerization of actin filaments is essential for cell movement and shape changes.
Scientists studied the depolymerization of plastics to develop better recycling methods.
During mitosis, rapid depolymerization of microtubules helps separate the chromosomes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degradation - the breakdown of complex substances into simpler ones
  • antonym: polymerization - a chemical process in which monomers are joined to form a polymer
