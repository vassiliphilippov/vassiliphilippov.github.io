anamorphism

noun
distorted image visible only from a specific viewpoint
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈmɔːrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌanəˈmɔːfɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a distorted projection or image that appears normal only when viewed from a particular angle or with a special device

Examples

Artistic anamorphism
Street paintings often rely on anamorphism to appear three-dimensional from certain perspectives.
The artist used anamorphism to create an image that looked abstract until viewed through a mirror.
Understanding anamorphism requires knowledge of both geometry and visual perception.
