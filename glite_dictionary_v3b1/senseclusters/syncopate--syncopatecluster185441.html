syncopate

verb
accent a weak beat in music
9.9 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.kə.peɪt/ /; / /ˈsɪn.kə.peɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.kə.peɪt/ /; / /ˈsɪn.kə.peɪt/ (BrE)
to modify the rhythm of music by emphasizing beats or accents that are normally weak or unstressed

Examples

Jazz musicians often syncopate the rhythm to create a lively and unexpected feel.
The composer decided to syncopate the melody, giving the song a unique groove.
