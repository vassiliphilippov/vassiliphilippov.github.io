cervical glands

noun

Small glands located in the mucous membrane of the uterine cervix that secrete mucus, playing a role in reproductive health.

Examples

Cervical glands secrete mucus
A blockage of the cervical glands may sometimes cause the formation of a nabothian cyst.

The cervical glands produce mucus that helps protect the uterus from infection.
