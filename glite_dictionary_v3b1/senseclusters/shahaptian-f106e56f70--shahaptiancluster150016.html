Shahaptian

noun
a Penutian language of the Pacific Northwest
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈhæptiən / (AmE), / ʃəˈhæptiən / (BrE)
A group of closely related Penutian languages spoken by members of the Sahaptin and Nez Percé peoples in the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

Examples

Shahaptian language group
Efforts are underway to revitalize the Shahaptian language among tribal communities.
Several texts in Shahaptian have survived from the early contact period.
The linguist specialized in documenting the Shahaptian languages of the Columbia Plateau.
