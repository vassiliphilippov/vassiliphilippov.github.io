hook

noun
a golf shot that curves left (right-handed player)
Pronunciation: / hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
in golf, a shot that curves sharply to the left for a right-handed player (or to the right for a left-handed player)

Examples

A golf hook
He tried to correct his hook with the help of a coach.
Many beginners struggle with a persistent hook in their swing.
That hook landed his ball deep in the rough on the left.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slice - a golf shot that curves to the right for a right-handed golfer
