core

noun
most important or essential part
Pronunciation: / kɔr / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
the central, most important, or essential part of something (such as an idea, group, or object)

Examples

The core idea
The core of his argument was about social justice and equal rights.
Trust and honesty are at the core of any strong relationship.
We need to focus on the core issues before moving forward with the project.
Radiologists examined the core of the tumor to determine its composition.
The Earth's core consists mainly of iron and nickel.
The apple's core remained after she finished eating the fruit.
Despite the complex language, the core message remained clear and powerful.
She summarized the core of the novel in just a few sentences.
The core of his argument focused on the importance of equal opportunity.
