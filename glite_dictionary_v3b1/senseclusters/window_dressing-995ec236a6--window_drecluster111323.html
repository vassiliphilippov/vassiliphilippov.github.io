window dressing

noun
action to make something look better than it is
5.4 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.doʊ ˈdres.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.dəʊ ˈdres.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
an action or arrangement that is intended to make something look better or more impressive than it really is, often to hide problems

Examples

Political window dressing
He accused the government of using reforms as window dressing rather than addressing the real issues.
Much of the new policy is little more than window dressing to improve the administration's image.
The company's impressive financial statements were just window dressing to conceal its mounting debts.
His apology was nothing but window dressing and didn't address the real issue.
Many reforms announced before the election turned out to be just window dressing.
The management's financial report was mostly window dressing to please investors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camouflage - something intended to hide or disguise
  • synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance
  • synonym: façade - a deceptive outward appearance
  • synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something appear true that is not
  • antonym: substance - the real content or value of something
  • antonym: transparency - the quality of being open and honest; nothing hidden
