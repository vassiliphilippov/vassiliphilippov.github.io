objective

adjective
(grammar) relating to object case
6.0
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒɛktɪv / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪv / (BrE)
(Grammar) Relating to or denoting the case of nouns, pronouns, or adjectives that are the object of a verb or preposition.

Examples

Objective case
In English, the objective case is used for direct and indirect objects of verbs.
Some languages have different word endings for the objective case.
The pronoun 'her' in 'I saw her' is in the objective case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accusative - grammatical case indicating the object of a verb
