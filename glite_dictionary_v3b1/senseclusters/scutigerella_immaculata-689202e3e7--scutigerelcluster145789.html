Scutigerella immaculata

noun
minute soil-dwelling arthropod pest
Pronunciation: / skuːˌtɪdʒəˈrɛlə ˌɪməˈkjuːlətə / (AmE), / skjuːˌtɪdʒəˈrelə ˌɪməˈkjuːlətə / (BrE)
A small, soil-dwelling arthropod (garden centipede) that often infests the roots and underground parts of greenhouse and garden crops.

Examples

Scutigerella immaculata root damage
Scutigerella immaculata can cause significant root damage to seedlings in commercial greenhouses.
Gardeners often mistake the presence of Scutigerella immaculata for a sign of poor soil health.
Preventing Scutigerella immaculata infestations is crucial for protecting young vegetable crops.
