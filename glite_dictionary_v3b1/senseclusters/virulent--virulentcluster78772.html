virulent

adjective
highly infectious or harmful (medical)
5.0 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪr.ə.lənt/ /; / /ˈvɪr.jə.lənt/ (AmE), /ˈvɪr.jʊ.lənt/ /; / /ˈvɪr.jə.lənt/ (BrE)
extremely harmful, infectious, or poisonous; able to cause disease or damage quickly

Examples

Virulent virus
Scientists are working to develop vaccines against this highly virulent virus.
The patient was infected by a virulent strain of bacteria that spread rapidly.
The sudden outbreak was traced back to a virulent pathogen introduced from abroad.
A virulent strain of the virus quickly spread throughout the community.
Some bacteria can become virulent and cause serious illness in vulnerable individuals.
The patient suffered a virulent reaction to the insect bite, requiring immediate treatment.
