hunker

verb
squat close to the ground
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌŋkə / (BrE)
to squat close to the ground, usually by bending the knees and balancing on the heels

Examples

To hunker down
During the storm, we had to hunker down behind the wall for safety.
She watched the children hunker beside the fire, warming their hands.
The hikers stopped to hunker on the trail and catch their breath.

Related Terms

  • synonym: squat - to crouch or sit with the knees bent and the heels close to or touching the buttocks or the back of the thighs
