cross-court

adjective, adverb
diagonal in sports
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌkɔrt /; / ˈkrɔs ˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌkɔːt /; / ˈkrɒs ˌkɔːt / (BrE)
going or moving diagonally from one side of a court to the other (used to describe shots or actions in sports like tennis or basketball)

Examples

Cross-court shot

A quick cross-court pass set up an easy basket for the team.
He hit the ball cross-court to catch his opponent off guard.
A powerful cross-court shot can be difficult to return in badminton.
She practiced serving cross-court to improve her accuracy and strategy.
