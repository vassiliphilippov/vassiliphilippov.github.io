interest group

noun
group seeking to influence policy
3.1 6.28/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…™ntr…ôst …°ruÀêp / (AmE), / Àà…™ntr…ôst …°ruÀêp / (BrE)
an organized group of people who share common aims and seek to influence public policy or decisions related to their field of activity

Examples

Teacher interest group
Education policy was heavily influenced by teacher interest groups during the debate.
Several interest groups submitted proposals to the government regarding healthcare reform.
The environmental interest group organized a rally to push for new climate legislation.

  • pressure group
    4.1 0.64/m

  • lobby group
    3.9 0.14/m

  • lobby
    3.9 5.76/m
    lobby seeks to influence politicians or officials through direct contact, while interest group is any organized group doing that.
  • advocacy group
    3.9 3.30/m
    advocacy group campaigns publicly and uses media or protests to change policy, while interest group may focus more on member benefits and lobbying.
  • special interest
    3.6 5.34/m

  • interest
