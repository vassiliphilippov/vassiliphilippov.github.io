get off one's chest

verb
express something worrying or troubling
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɔf wʌnz tʃɛst / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf wʌnz tʃɛst / (BrE)
to talk about something that has been worrying or bothering you so that you feel relieved

Examples

To get off his chest
After he got it off his chest, he felt a great sense of relief.
It's sometimes hard to get things off your chest, but it makes you feel much better.
She finally decided to get off her chest everything that had been bothering her at work.
He felt much better after getting the truth off his chest at the meeting.
I'm glad I finally got it off my chest—I've been worrying about it for weeks.
She needed to get something off her chest before she could relax.

  • synonym: confess - to admit something, especially a crime or fault, to someone
