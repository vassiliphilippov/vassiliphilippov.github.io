drive off into the sunset

verb
leave peacefully or contentedly, especially at an ending
2.6
Pronunciation: / draɪv ɔf ˌɪntu ðə ˈsʌnsɛt / (AmE), / draɪv ɒf ˌɪntə ðə ˈsʌnsɛt / (BrE)
(of a person or people) to leave a place or situation, often in a peaceful or contented way, typically at the end of an event or story.

Examples

To drive off into the sunset happily
After solving the case, the detectives drive off into the sunset, ready for their next adventure.
At the movie's end, the heroes drive off into the sunset as the credits roll.
They hoped their worries would end as they drive off into the sunset together.
