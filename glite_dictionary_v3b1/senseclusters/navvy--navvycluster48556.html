navvy

noun
manual laborer in construction
Pronunciation: / ˈnævi / (AmE), / ˈnavi / (BrE)
a manual laborer, especially one employed in digging or construction work such as building canals, railways, or roads

Examples

A navvy digging trenches
As a navvy, he spent long days digging trenches and transporting heavy materials.
During the 19th century, a large number of navvies were employed to construct the new railway lines.
The navvy worked tirelessly in the mud, helping to build the city's canal network.
