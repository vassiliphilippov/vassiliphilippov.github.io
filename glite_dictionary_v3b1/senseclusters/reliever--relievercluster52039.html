reliever

noun
person who takes over for another
5.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààliv…ôr / (AmE), / r…™ÀàliÀêv…ô / (BrE)
a person who takes over the duties or role of another, especially in demanding or hazardous situations

Examples

Night shift reliever
During the night shift, a reliever arrives to take over when workers need a break.
In emergencies, a trained reliever is always ready to step in for the main operator.
The pitcher called a reliever to finish the game in the final innings.

  • fill-in
