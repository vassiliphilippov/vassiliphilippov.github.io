parasitize

verb
to draw unwanted resources
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌsaɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to draw power, signals, or resources from a nearby device, circuit, or system, often causing interference or problems

Examples

Wires parasitize signals
If one wire is not properly shielded, it can parasitize signals from another, leading to data errors.
The old speaker system began to parasitize power from the adjacent amplifier, causing it to malfunction.
Engineers redesigned the circuit so that it would not parasitize energy from nearby components.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interfere - to cause disruption or disturbance in a system
  • antonym: support - to provide assistance or resources to something
