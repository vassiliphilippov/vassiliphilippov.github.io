abbé

noun
French Catholic priest
Pronunciation: / æˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ˈæbeɪ / (BrE)
a French Roman Catholic priest or clergyman, especially one not in charge of a parish; sometimes a scholar or companion to the aristocracy

Examples

The abbé preached
The abbé delivered a thoughtful sermon to the small congregation.
During the French Revolution, many an abbé faced difficult choices about his faith.
The villagers often sought advice from the wise abbé who lived nearby.
The young abbé was invited to tutor the children of the noble family.
In 18th-century France, an abbé often participated in intellectual salons.
The witty abbé impressed everyone with his knowledge of literature and philosophy.
