RTLT

noun
radio signal round-trip travel time
Pronunciation: / ɑːr tiː ɛl tiː / (AmE), / ɑː tiː ɛl tiː / (BrE)
the time required for a signal to travel from Earth to a spacecraft (or other body) and return to Earth

Examples

The RTLT time
Engineers calculated the RTLT before issuing remote commands to the lander.
The RTLT to Mars can exceed forty minutes, which complicates real-time control of spacecraft.
Understanding the RTLT is critical for planning communications with distant probes.
