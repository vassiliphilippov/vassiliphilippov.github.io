lake bottom

noun
bottom surface of a lake
Pronunciation: / leɪk ˈbɑtəm / (AmE), / leɪk ˈbɒtəm / (BrE)
the ground or surface that forms the lowest part of a lake, beneath the water

Examples

The lake bottom
Divers discovered an old shipwreck on the lake bottom.
Plants and animals living at the lake bottom contribute to its unique ecosystem.
Thick layers of mud can accumulate on the lake bottom over centuries.
