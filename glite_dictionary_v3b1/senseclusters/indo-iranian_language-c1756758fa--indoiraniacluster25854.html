Indo-Iranian language

noun
branch of Indo-European languages (Indic and Iranian)
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndoʊ ɪˈræniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪndəʊ ɪˈrɑːnɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-European language family comprising the Indic and Iranian language groups.

Examples

The Indo-Iranian language family
Linguists often compare the Indo-Iranian language family with other Indo-European branches.
Sanskrit and Persian are both members of the Indo-Iranian language group.
The evolution of the Indo-Iranian language group has been studied extensively.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Indic language - Languages of the northern Indian subcontinent, a subset of Indo-Iranian
  • degree higher: Indo-European language - The large language family including most languages of Europe and parts of Asia
  • degree lower: Iranian language - Languages spoken in Iran and parts of Central Asia, a subset of Indo-Iranian
