at the altar of the golden calf

sacrifice values for material wealth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ət ði ˈɔltɚ əv ðə ˈɡoʊldən kæf / (AmE), / ət ði ˈɔːltə əv ðə ˈɡəʊldən kɑːf / (BrE)
To sacrifice one's moral principles or values for the pursuit of material wealth or gain.

Examples

At the altar of the golden calf sacrifice
He chose fame and fortune at the altar of the golden calf, risking lifelong friendships.
In pursuit of profits, the company abandoned environmental responsibility at the altar of the golden calf.
Some politicians have betrayed public trust at the altar of the golden calf, prioritizing personal gain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having moral principles
  • synonym: mammon - wealth or material greed, often personified as an idol
  • synonym: materialism - belief that material wealth is more important than spiritual values
