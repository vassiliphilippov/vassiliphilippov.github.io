itinerancy

noun
period of traveling duties
8.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈtɪn.ər.ən.siː/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈtɪn.ə.rən.siː/ (BrE)
the practice or system of traveling from place to place, especially for work, duty, or official purposes

Examples

The itinerancy system
The judge's itinerancy required him to visit several towns each month.
During her itinerancy, the performer entertained audiences in many rural communities.
The minister's itinerancy lasted for a year, covering dozens of villages.
The church adopted itinerancy to ensure all congregations received experienced leadership.
Because of itinerancy, ministers rarely stayed in one community for more than a few years.
The system of itinerancy helped spread new ideas among different congregations.
The itinerancy of the sales team kept them on the road for weeks.
His job as a consultant required constant itinerancy across different cities.
Many artists in history lived lives of itinerancy, moving from one patron to another.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanence - the state of remaining in one place or position
  • synonym: rotation - the act of regularly changing positions or assignments
  • antonym: settled - living in one place and not moving around
  • antonym: stationary - not moving or traveling; staying in one place
  • synonym: travel - the act of going from one place to another
  • synonym: wandering - moving from place to place without a fixed home
