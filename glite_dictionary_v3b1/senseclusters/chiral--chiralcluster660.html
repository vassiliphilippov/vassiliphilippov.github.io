chiral

adjective
not mirror-identical
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkaɪ.rəl/ (AmE), /ˈkaɪ.rəl/ (BrE)
not identical to its mirror image, especially describing molecules or objects with handedness

Examples

Chiral molecule
A molecule is chiral if it cannot be superimposed on its mirror image.
Many drugs are chiral, and their effects can differ depending on their handedness.
The concept of chiral objects is important in chemistry and biology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: achiral - identical to its mirror image; lacking handedness
