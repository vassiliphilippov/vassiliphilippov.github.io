isolative

adjective
causing isolation
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈaɪsələtɪv / (BrE)
tending to cause or result in isolation from others

Examples

Isolative policy
The isolative policies made it difficult for new employees to feel welcome.
Some people find remote work to be an isolative experience if they lack social interaction.
The isolative effect of the new rules was noticed by everyone in the community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inclusive - encouraging the involvement or inclusion of all people
  • synonym: isolating - causing someone or something to be separated from others
