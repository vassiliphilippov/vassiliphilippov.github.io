extract

verb
to remove or obtain with effort
2.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈstrækt/ /; / /ˈek.strækt/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈstrækt/ /; / /ˈek.strækt/ (BrE)
to remove or take out something, especially by effort or process; also, to obtain information, meaning, or evidence from something

Examples

To extract information
Investigators managed to extract crucial evidence from the witness.
She used tweezers to extract a splinter from her finger.
The dentist had to extract the wisdom tooth because it was infected.
It was hard to extract the truth from him during the interview.
She managed to extract a confession after hours of persistent questioning.
The journalist finally extracted a statement from the reluctant official.
From her tone, I could extract a sense of disappointment.
It is difficult to extract the underlying principle from such limited data.
The researchers tried to extract a common theme from the interviews.
