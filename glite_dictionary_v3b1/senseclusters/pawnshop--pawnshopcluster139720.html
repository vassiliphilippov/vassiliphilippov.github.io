pawnshop

noun
shop lending money for pledged items
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔnˌʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈpɔːnʃɒp / (BrE)
a shop where people can leave valuable items as security for a loan or sell them for cash

Examples

The pawnshop on Main Street displays jewelry and electronics in its window.
She visited a pawnshop to buy a used camera at a lower price.
