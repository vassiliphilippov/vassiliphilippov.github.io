Lachesis

proper noun
Greek goddess of fate who measures life span
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlækəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈlakɪsɪs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the goddess of fate who determines the length of each person's life.

Examples

Lachesis measures fate
Ancient Greeks believed that Lachesis measured the thread of every life.
In the myth, Lachesis decides how long each mortal shall live.
Some poets refer to destiny as the hand of Lachesis weaving fate.

  • synonym: Clotho - Greek goddess of fate who spins the thread of life
