flatscreen

adjective
with a flat display
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌskriːn / (AmE), / ˈflætˌskriːn / (BrE)
having a flat, thin display, especially referring to modern electronic screens such as televisions or monitors

Examples

Flatscreen TV display
The hotel room featured a large flatscreen television mounted on the wall.
Many offices have replaced old monitors with modern flatscreen displays for better image quality.
She prefers a flatscreen computer monitor because it takes up less space on her desk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: curved - having a rounded or bent display surface
  • synonym: thin - having little thickness; slim in form
