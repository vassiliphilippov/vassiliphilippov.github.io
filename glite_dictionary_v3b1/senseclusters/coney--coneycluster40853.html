coney

noun
small, hoofed mammal (hyrax) of Africa and Asia
8.4 8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkoʊ.niː/ /; / /ˈkəʊ.niː/ (AmE), /ˈkəʊ.niː/ (BrE)
A small, herbivorous mammal found in Africa and Asia, with rodent-like incisors and hoof-like toes; also known as a hyrax.

Examples

African coney grazes
A coney uses its sharp incisors to graze on grasses and bark in its native habitat.
The rocky outcrops were home to dozens of coneys basking in the sun each morning.
Until recently, scientists believed the coney was closely related to rodents, but now they link it to elephants.
