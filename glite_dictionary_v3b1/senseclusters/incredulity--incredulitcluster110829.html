incredulity

noun
disbelief; unwillingness to believe

The state of being unable or unwilling to believe something; disbelief or doubt about the truth of something.

Examples

Look of incredulity
A look of incredulity crossed her face when she saw the surprising results.
His claim was met with widespread incredulity by the scientific community.
She stared at him in incredulity, unable to accept what she had just heard.

Synonyms

  • disbelief
    3.0 9/m
    disbelief is a plain lack of belief in something, while incredulity often includes surprise and an emotional refusal to accept it.
  • skepticism
    5.1 10/m
    skepticism is a careful, questioning attitude that doubts claims over time, while incredulity is a quick refusal to believe a single claim.
