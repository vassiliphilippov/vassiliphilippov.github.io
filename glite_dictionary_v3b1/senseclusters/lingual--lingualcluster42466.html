lingual

noun
consonant produced with the tongue
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋɡwəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡwəl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced with the tongue and other speech organs, as in many speech sounds in linguistics.

Examples

An alveolar lingual
Linguists often classify the English 't' and 'd' as alveolar linguals.
Proper pronunciation of some lingual consonants can be challenging for language learners.
The retroflex sound in Hindi is a type of lingual.

Related Terms

  • antonym: labial - consonant produced with the lips
