abstraction

noun
process of forming general concepts
6.8 6.4
Pronunciation: /æb.ˈstræk.ʃən/ /; / /ə.b.ˈstræk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.b.ˈstræk.ʃən/ /; / /æb.ˈstræk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or result of forming a general idea or concept by considering things independently of their specific details

Examples

Conceptual abstraction
Effective programming often requires a high level of abstraction to manage complex systems.
The mathematician relied on abstraction to identify patterns among the various equations.
Through abstraction, we can group different animals into categories based on shared characteristics.
Love, as an abstraction, is difficult to define but easy to feel.

The philosopher spoke about justice as an abstraction rather than a practical policy.

Mathematics is full of abstraction, where complex ideas are reduced to general principles.
The scientist used abstraction to develop a theory that applied to all cases, not just one.
