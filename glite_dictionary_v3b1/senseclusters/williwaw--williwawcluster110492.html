williwaw

noun
sudden disturbance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪliˌwɑ / (AmE), / ˈwɪliˌwɔː / (BrE)
a sudden, violent outburst or disturbance, especially a rapid and forceful upheaval in a situation or group.

Examples

A williwaw of anger
After the announcement, a williwaw of questions erupted from the audience.
The company faced a williwaw of complaints following the sudden policy change.
Social media was hit by a williwaw of reactions after the news broke.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of peace and quiet without disturbance
  • synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or excitement
