suborder Megachiroptera

noun
taxonomic group of fruit bats
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡəˌkaɪrəpˈtɪrə / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˌkaɪərəpˈtɪərə / (BrE)
A suborder of bats comprising the large fruit bats, commonly known as flying foxes, found mainly in tropical and subtropical regions.

Examples

The suborder Megachiroptera bats
Scientists study the suborder Megachiroptera to understand their role in pollination and seed dispersal.
The suborder Megachiroptera includes the flying foxes found across Asia and Australia.
Unlike the smaller microbats, members of the suborder Megachiroptera typically do not use echolocation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fruit bat - A bat that feeds mainly on fruit, belonging to Megachiroptera
  • antonym: suborder Microchiroptera - A suborder of bats comprising the smaller, echolocating bats
