duplexing

noun
two-way transmission
Pronunciation: / ˈduːplɛksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdjuːplɛksɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or technique that enables two-way communication on a channel, either at the same time or in turns

Examples

Wireless duplexing
Modern wireless networks use duplexing to allow users to talk and listen at the same time.
The technician explained that duplexing improves the efficiency of data transmission.
Some older radios do not support duplexing and can only send or receive, not both simultaneously.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: full duplex - simultaneous two-way communication
  • degree lower: half duplex - two-way communication, but only one direction at a time
