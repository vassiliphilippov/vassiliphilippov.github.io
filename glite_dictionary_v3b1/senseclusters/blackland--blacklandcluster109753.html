blackland

noun
fertile dark soil region
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈblækˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of land with dark, fertile soil, often used for farming

Examples

The rich blackland
The rich blackland of the region makes it ideal for growing crops.
Many settlers were attracted to the blackland because of its fertile soil.
Cotton fields stretch across the blackland as far as the eye can see.

Related Terms

  • antonym: badland - an area of dry, eroded land with little vegetation
  • synonym: prairie - a large area of flat, grassy land, especially in North America
