beset

verb
attack or trouble from all sides
9.5
Pronunciation: / bɪˈsɛt / (AmE), / bɪˈsɛt / (BrE)
to attack, trouble, or harass someone or something persistently from all sides

Examples

Beset by problems
The small village was beset by enemy forces for weeks.
Throughout her career, she was beset by rumors and criticism.
We found ourselves beset with challenges at every stage of the project.
He felt beset by doubts after receiving conflicting advice from his colleagues.
Journalists are often beset by deadlines and constant demands for new stories.
The project was beset with unexpected technical issues from the very beginning.
