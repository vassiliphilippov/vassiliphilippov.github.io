placenta previa

noun
placenta implanted low in uterus during pregnancy
7.1 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / pl…ôÀàs…õnt…ô ÀàpriÀê.vi.…ô / (AmE), / pl…ôÀås…õnt…ô ÀàpriÀê.vi.…ô / (BrE)
A condition in pregnancy where the placenta is implanted in the lower part of the uterus, potentially causing bleeding and requiring cesarean delivery.

Examples

Placenta previa diagnosis
Because of her diagnosis of placenta previa, the doctor recommended a scheduled cesarean section.
The ultrasound revealed that she had placenta previa, which required close monitoring.
Women with placenta previa are often advised to avoid strenuous activity to reduce the risk of bleeding.
