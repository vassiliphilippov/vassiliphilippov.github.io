notochordal

adjective
relating to notochord
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊtəˈkɔrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˌnəʊtəˈkɔːdl̩ / (BrE)
relating to or having a notochord, a flexible rod-like structure found in some animals during development

Examples

Notochordal cells
The notochordal cells play a key role in early vertebrate development.
Researchers studied the notochordal tissue to understand spinal formation in embryos.
Some rare tumors originate from notochordal remnants in the human body.

Related Terms
