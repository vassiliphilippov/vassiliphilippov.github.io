fusion

noun
joining of vertebrae in spinal surgery
8.1 0.5/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfjuÀê í…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàfjuÀê í…ôn / (BrE)
A surgical procedure to correct an unstable part of the spine by joining two or more vertebrae, sometimes done using traction or immobilization.

Examples

Spinal fusion surgery
Doctors recommended spinal fusion to treat her persistent back pain and weakness.
Recovery from a fusion can take several months and requires physical therapy.
The patient underwent a fusion to stabilize his lower spine after a car accident.
