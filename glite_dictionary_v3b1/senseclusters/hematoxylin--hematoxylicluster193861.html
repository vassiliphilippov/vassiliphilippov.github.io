hematoxylin

noun
biological stain
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈtɑksəlɪn /; / ˌhiːməˈtɑksɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪlɪn / (BrE)
a natural blue or purple dye obtained from the logwood tree, used especially in biology to stain cell nuclei for microscopic examination and more generally in laboratories to stain cell structures; it is also sometimes used in dyeing textiles.

Examples

hematoxylin stain
The technician added hematoxylin to the slide to highlight the cell nuclei.
In histology, hematoxylin is commonly paired with eosin for tissue staining.
The laboratory ordered more hematoxylin for their weekly staining procedures.
The laboratory technician stained the tissue sample with hematoxylin to highlight the cell nuclei.
Natural hematoxylin is extracted from the heartwood of the logwood tree.
Histologists often use hematoxylin and eosin together to examine tissue under a microscope.
The laboratory technician used haematoxylin to stain the tissue samples for microscopic examination.
Historically, haematoxylin was valued for its ability to produce rich blue and purple hues in fabrics.
Modern histology often relies on haematoxylin for highlighting cell nuclei in slides.
