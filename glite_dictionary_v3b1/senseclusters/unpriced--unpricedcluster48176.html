unpriced

adjective
without a price
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpraɪst / (AmE), / ʌnˈpraɪst / (BrE)
not assigned a monetary value or market price, especially in economic or accounting contexts

Examples

Unpriced costs
Many environmental costs remain unpriced in economic analyses, leading to incomplete assessments.
The antique vase was unpriced, so the buyer had to ask for an estimate.
Some services provided by nature are often unpriced in traditional markets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: priced - assigned a monetary value or market price
  • synonym: priceless - so valuable that its price cannot be determined
