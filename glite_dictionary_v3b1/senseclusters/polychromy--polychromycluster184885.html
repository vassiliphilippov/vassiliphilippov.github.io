polychromy

noun
multi-color decoration
9.4
Pronunciation: /pɑːl.ɪ.ˈkroʊ.miː/ /; / /pɑː.lə.ˈkroʊ.miː/ (AmE), /pɒl.ɪ.ˈkrə.miː/ /; / /pɒl.ə.ˈkrə.miː/ (BrE)
the use or practice of decorating objects, buildings, or artworks with several colors

Examples

Ancient polychromy art
The museum's exhibit highlighted the polychromy of ancient Greek statues, which were once brightly painted.

The ancient temple's polychromy was restored to reveal its original vibrant patterns.
Artists in the Renaissance often used polychromy to bring sculptures to life.
The museum exhibit showcased the polychromy of medieval religious statues.
