monozygotic twin

noun
twin from the same fertilized egg
2.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /mə.noʊ.zaɪ.ˈgɑː.tɪk twɪn/ (AmE), /mɒ.nə.zaɪ.ˈgɒt.ɪk twɪn/ (BrE)
either of a pair of twins that develop from a single fertilized egg and share the same genetic material

Examples

A monozygotic twin pair
Because they come from the same egg, monozygotic twin brothers are genetically identical.
The chance of having a monozygotic twin pregnancy is not affected by family history.
The researchers studied pairs of monozygotic twin sisters to understand genetic influences on health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dizygotic twin - one of a pair of twins developed from two separate eggs
