vigilance committee

noun

Pronunciation: /ˈvɪdʒ.ɪ.ləns kə.ˈmɪt.iː/ /; / /ˈvɪdʒ.ɪ.ləns kɑː.ˈmɪt.iː/ (AmE), /ˈvɪdʒ.ɪ.ləns kə.ˈmɪt.iː/ /; / /ˈvɪdʒ.ɪ.ləns kɒ.ˈmɪt.iː/ (BrE)
A group of volunteers organized to maintain order and enforce laws in a community where an effective legal system is not present or is considered inadequate.

Examples

A vigilance committee group

Without effective police, a vigilance committee became the main authority in the region.
