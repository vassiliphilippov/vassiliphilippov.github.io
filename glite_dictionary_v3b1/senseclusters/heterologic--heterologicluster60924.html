heterologic

adjective
structurally or evolutionarily different
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəroʊˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
not corresponding in structure or evolutionary origin; differing in form or development.

Examples

Heterologic features
Evolutionary biologists distinguish between homologous and heterologic features when comparing species.
The wings of a butterfly and the wings of a bird are considered heterologic structures because they evolved independently.
Their study focused on the heterologic tissues found in certain tumors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homologous - having the same relation, relative position, or structure
