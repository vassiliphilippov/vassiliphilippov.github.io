hypersurface

noun
surface in higher dimensions
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪpərˌsɜrfɪs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪpəsɜːfɪs / (BrE)
a geometric surface whose dimension is one less than that of the space it is in, especially in mathematics

Examples

A hypersurface in geometry
In advanced geometry, a hypersurface can exist in spaces with more than three dimensions.
The mathematician explained how a four-dimensional hypersurface is difficult to visualize.
A sphere is a simple example of a hypersurface in three-dimensional space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manifold - a mathematical space that locally resembles Euclidean space
  • degree lower: surface - the outside layer or boundary of an object
