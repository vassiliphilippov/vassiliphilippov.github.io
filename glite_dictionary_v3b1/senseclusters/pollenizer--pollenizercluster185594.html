pollenizer

noun
plant providing pollen
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑlɪˌnaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌnaɪzə / (BrE)
a plant that provides pollen to fertilize another plant of the same species

Examples

Pollenizer tree
A compatible pollenizer is necessary for the pear trees to produce fruit.
Gardeners often plant a pollenizer nearby to ensure successful cross-pollination.
Without a suitable pollenizer, the orchard's apple trees may not yield a good harvest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pollinator - an animal or agent that transfers pollen between flowers
