crosscurrent

noun
a current flowing across another
7.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr…îsÀåk…ùr…ônt / (AmE), / Ààkr…ísÀåk år…ônt / (BrE)
A current of water moving at an angle to or intersecting another current, often causing turbulence in a river or the sea.

Examples

Strong crosscurrent in water
Divers must always be cautious of unexpected crosscurrents while exploring coral reefs.
Strong crosscurrents made swimming across the channel extremely dangerous.
The small boat struggled against the crosscurrent near the river's mouth.
