maneuver

verb
act skillfully to achieve a goal
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvɚ / (AmE), / məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
to act or plan skillfully and strategically to achieve a goal, often by using clever or careful methods

Examples

To maneuver a deal
After months of planning, they finally maneuvered her into signing the agreement.
She skillfully maneuvered through the competition to win the promotion.
The politician maneuvered his way into the lead position within the party.
He maneuvered through the delicate negotiations with great diplomatic skill.
She had to maneuver her car into a tight parking spot on the busy street.
The officer instructed his team to maneuver into position before the operation began.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blunder - to move or act in a careless or clumsy way
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence cleverly or unfairly
  • synonym: navigate - to plan and direct the route or course of a ship, aircraft, or other form of transport
  • synonym: scheme - to make secret or underhanded plans to achieve a goal
