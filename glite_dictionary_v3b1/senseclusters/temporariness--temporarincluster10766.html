temporariness

noun
state of being temporary
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛmpəˈrɛrinəs / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpəˈrɛərɪnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of lasting for only a limited period; the condition of being temporary.

Examples

The temporariness of life
He struggled with the temporariness of his living situation after moving to the city.
Many people appreciate the temporariness of trends, as new styles are always emerging.
The temporariness of her contract made her anxious about her future prospects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanence - the state of lasting or remaining unchanged forever
