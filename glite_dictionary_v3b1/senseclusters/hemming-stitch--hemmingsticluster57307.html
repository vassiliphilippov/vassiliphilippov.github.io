hemming-stitch

noun
a stitch for hemming fabric edges
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛmɪŋ stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhɛmɪŋ stɪtʃ / (BrE)
A stitch used in sewing hems on garments, typically skirts and dresses, to secure the edge of the fabric neatly.

Examples

A hemming-stitch on fabric
A well-done hemming-stitch makes the hem almost invisible from the outside.
After turning up the hem, he secured it with a simple hemming-stitch.
She used a hemming-stitch to finish the edge of the dress neatly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blind stitch - a sewing stitch that is almost invisible from the outside of the garment
  • degree lower: overcast stitch - a stitch used to prevent the raw edge of fabric from fraying
