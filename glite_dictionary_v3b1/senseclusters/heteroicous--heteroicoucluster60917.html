heteroicous

adjective
having several types of reproductive organs on one plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˈroʊɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrəʊɪkəs / (BrE)
(of a plant) having several forms of gametoecia (reproductive organs) on the same individual.

Examples

A heteroicous plant
A heteroicous plant can increase its adaptability through multiple forms of reproduction.
Researchers identified the liverwort as heteroicous due to its varied reproductive structures.
Some moss species are described as heteroicous, producing different gametoecia types on the same plant.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: autoicous - having both male and female reproductive organs on the same plant but in separate structures
  • antonym: dioicous - having male and female reproductive organs on separate plants
