wood alcohol

noun
another name for methanol (methyl alcohol)
Pronunciation: / wʊd ˈælkəˌhɔl / (AmE), / wʊd ˈalkəhɒl / (BrE)
A poisonous, flammable liquid alcohol (methanol), formerly made by distilling wood, used as a solvent, antifreeze, fuel, and denaturant for ethyl alcohol.

Examples

Wood alcohol fuel
Early automobiles sometimes used wood alcohol as fuel before gasoline became widespread.
Small amounts of wood alcohol can cause blindness or even death if ingested.
The laboratory keeps wood alcohol in a secure cabinet because of its toxicity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: methanol - a light, volatile, flammable alcohol (CH3OH); also known as wood alcohol
