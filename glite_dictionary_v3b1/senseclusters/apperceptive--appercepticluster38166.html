apperceptive

adjective
relating new perceptions to prior experience
Pronunciation: / ˌæp.ərˈsɛp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌap.əˈsɛp.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or able to perceive new information in relation to past experiences or knowledge

Examples

An apperceptive mind
Her apperceptive skills helped her apply lessons from past mistakes to current problems.
The psychologist described the patient as highly apperceptive when processing new social situations.
Young children develop apperceptive abilities as they learn to make sense of their surroundings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight or understanding
