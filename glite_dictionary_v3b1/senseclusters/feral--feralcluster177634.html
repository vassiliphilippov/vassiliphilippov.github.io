feral

adjective
wild and menacing (especially animals)
6.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈfe.rəl/ /; / /ˈfɪə.rəl/ (AmE), /ˈfe.rəl/ /; / /ˈfɪə.rəl/ (BrE)
(of an animal) living in a wild state, especially after escape from captivity or domestication; wild and menacing.

Examples

Feral cat
After years in captivity, the released wolves soon became feral and wary of humans.
The abandoned house was overrun with feral animals searching for food.
The neighborhood has seen an increase in sightings of feral cats and raccoons.
