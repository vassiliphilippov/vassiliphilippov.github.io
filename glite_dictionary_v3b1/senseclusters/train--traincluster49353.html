train

noun
procession of wagons or animals traveling together
3.8
Pronunciation: / treɪn / (AmE), / treɪn / (BrE)
a line or procession of people, animals, vehicles, or a series of connected or related events or things

Examples

A camel train
A long camel train moved slowly across the desert.
The gold was transported by a train of mule-drawn wagons.
The settlers traveled across the plains in a train for mutual protection.
A long train of events led to the company's eventual success.
She lost her train of thought after being interrupted by a phone call.
There was a solemn train of mourners following the coffin.
His decision unleashed a train of problems that were hard to predict.
The invention sparked a train of changes throughout the industry.
The sudden resignation set off a train of unexpected political events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caravan - A group of travelers, especially in the desert, journeying together for safety
  • synonym: chain - a connected series of things or events
  • synonym: sequence - a set of things arranged in order
  • synonym: succession - the coming of one thing after another in order
