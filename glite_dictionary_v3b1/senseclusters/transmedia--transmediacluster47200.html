transmedia

noun
storytelling across multiple media platforms
Pronunciation: / trænzˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / tranzˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
involving storytelling or content that extends across multiple media platforms, such as film, television, books, and games, to create a unified and immersive experience

Examples

A transmedia story
Effective transmedia strategies can engage audiences by weaving a narrative across social media, podcasts, and movies.
The Marvel Cinematic Universe is an example of transmedia storytelling, spanning films, comics, and television shows.
The project's transmedia approach allowed fans to experience the story through games, books, and web series.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multimedia - the use of a combination of different content forms, such as text, audio, images, and video
