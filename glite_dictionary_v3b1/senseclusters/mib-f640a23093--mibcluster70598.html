MiB

noun
unit of digital information (1,048,576 bytes)
7.3
Pronunciation: / maɪ biː / (AmE), / maɪ biː / (BrE)
a unit of information used in computing, equal to 1,048,576 bytes or 1024 kibibytes

Examples

MiB of data
Modern software installers are often several hundred MiB or even a few GiB.
The memory limit was set to 512 MiB to conserve resources.
This file is just over one MiB in size, so it should transfer quickly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: GiB - a unit of information equal to 2^30 (1,073,741,824) bytes
  • degree lower: KiB - a unit of information equal to 2^10 (1,024) bytes
  • synonym: megabyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000,000 bytes
