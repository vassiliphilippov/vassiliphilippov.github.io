bloodborne

adjective
carried in blood
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌbɔːn / (BrE)
carried or transmitted through the blood, especially referring to diseases or pathogens

Examples

Bloodborne infections
Healthcare workers are trained to avoid bloodborne infections.
Hepatitis B is a bloodborne virus that can be transmitted through contaminated needles.
Proper safety measures help prevent the spread of bloodborne diseases in hospitals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: airborne - carried or transmitted through the air
  • antonym: waterborne - carried or transmitted through water
