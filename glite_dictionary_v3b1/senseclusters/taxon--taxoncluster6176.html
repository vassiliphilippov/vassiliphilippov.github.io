taxon

noun
biological classification group
6.2 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtæk.sən/ /; / /ˈtæk.sɑːn/ (AmE), /ˈtæk.sɒn/ /; / /ˈtæk.sən/ (BrE)
a group of one or more populations of organisms, such as species or family, that are classified as a unit in biological taxonomy

Examples

A taxon group
A taxon can include organisms at any taxonomic rank, such as orders, families, or species.
Scientists debated how to classify the newly discovered taxon of flowering plants.
The giant panda belongs to a unique taxon within the bear family.
