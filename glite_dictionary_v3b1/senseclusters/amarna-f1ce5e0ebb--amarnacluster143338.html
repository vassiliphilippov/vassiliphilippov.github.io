Amarna

proper noun
ancient Egyptian city site
Pronunciation: / əˈmɑrnə / (AmE), / əˈmɑːnə / (BrE)
an archaeological site in Egypt containing the remains of the ancient city built by Pharaoh Akhenaten

Examples

Amarna ruins
Archaeologists have uncovered many artifacts at Amarna that shed light on daily life in ancient Egypt.
The city of Amarna was founded by Pharaoh Akhenaten during the 14th century BCE.
Tourists visit Amarna to see the ruins and learn about the Amarna Period of Egyptian history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Akhetaten - the ancient city built by Akhenaten at the site of Amarna
  • synonym: Thebes - an ancient Egyptian city, once the capital of Egypt
