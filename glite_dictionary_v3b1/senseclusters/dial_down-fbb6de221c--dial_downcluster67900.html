dial down

verb
reduce intensity or amount
9.3
Pronunciation: / daɪəl daʊn / (AmE), / daɪəl daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the intensity, amount, or level of something; to make something less extreme or pronounced

Examples

To dial down volume
If you don't dial down your criticism, people will stop listening.
She had to dial down her expectations after hearing the team's feedback.
The manager asked everyone to dial down the excitement in the office before the client arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tone down - to make something less harsh or forceful
  • antonym: turn up - to increase the intensity or amount of something, especially sound or activity
