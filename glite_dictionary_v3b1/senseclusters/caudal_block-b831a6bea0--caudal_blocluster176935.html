caudal block

noun
regional anesthesia via injection into caudal spinal canal
7.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îd…ôl bl…ëk / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀêd…ôl bl…ík / (BrE)
A type of regional anesthesia produced by injecting an anesthetic into the lower (caudal) end of the spinal canal, primarily used for procedures below the umbilicus; now largely replaced by epidural anesthesia.

Examples

A caudal block injection
Advances in anesthesiology have led to epidural anesthesia largely replacing the caudal block in adults.
Because a caudal block numbs the lower half of the body, it is commonly used in pediatric operations.
The surgeon recommended a caudal block for the child's hernia repair surgery.

Synonyms

  • caudal anesthesia
    8.1 0.00/m
