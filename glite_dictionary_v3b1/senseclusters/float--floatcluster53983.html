float

verb
set something afloat on liquid
Pronunciation: / floʊt / (AmE), / fləʊt / (BrE)
to stay or move on the surface of water or in the air without sinking; to cause something to do this

Examples

To float on water
He floated a message in a bottle out to sea.
She floated the lily pads gently on the pond's surface.
The children floated their paper boats on the stream.
If the egg floats in water, it may be rotten.
Small leaves float gently down the stream every autumn.
The empty bottle will float on the surface of the lake.
After years of construction, they finally floated the vessel in the harbor.
Engineers carefully float the completed platform to begin testing in open water.
The workers will float the new ship at high tide tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drift - to move slowly on water or in air, usually by currents
  • synonym: glide - move smoothly and continuously along a surface
  • synonym: launch - to set a boat or vessel afloat for the first time
  • antonym: sink - to cause something to go below the surface of water; opposite of float
