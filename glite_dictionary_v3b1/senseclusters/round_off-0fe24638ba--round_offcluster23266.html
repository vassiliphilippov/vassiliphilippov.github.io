round off

verb
make a number simpler by rounding
Pronunciation: / raʊnd ɔf / (AmE), / raʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
to change a number to the nearest whole number or a number ending in zero, in order to make it simpler or easier to use

Examples

To round off a number
The accountant will round off the figures for simplicity.
We decided to round off the total bill to the nearest dollar.
You should round off 4.57 to 5 in your final answer.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: round down - decrease a number to the nearest whole or stated value
  • degree higher: round up - increase a number to the nearest whole or stated value
