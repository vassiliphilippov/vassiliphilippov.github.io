jerk

noun
sudden quick pull or movement
2.2 1.07/m
Pronunciation: / d í…ùk / (AmE), / d í…úÀêk / (BrE)
a sudden, quick, or sharp movement or pull

Examples

A sudden jerk
He felt a jerk on the fishing line and knew he had caught something.
The rope broke with a violent jerk as they tried to pull the crate up.
With a sudden jerk, the train started moving again.
He felt a sudden jerk as the car came to a stop.
The elevator gave a jerk before moving upward again.
With a quick jerk of her head, she signaled him to follow.

Synonyms

  • jolt
    3.8 3.40/m
    a jolt is a sudden hard shock that moves things sharply, while a jerk is a quick, sharp movement or pull.
  • tug
    1.1 2.98/m
    a tug is a short, forceful pull used to move things quickly, while a jerk is a sudden quick movement or pull.
  • yank
    4.1 13/m
    a yank is a very forceful, sudden pull often making objects move abruptly, while a jerk is a quicker, less violent movement or pull.
  • snap
    1.6 5.49/m
    a snap is a quick sharp sound or movement that often breaks things, while a jerk is a sudden movement or pull without breaking.
  • start
    1.0 2.82/m
    a start is a sudden involuntary move from surprise or fear, while a jerk is a quick movement or pull that can be deliberate or accidental.
