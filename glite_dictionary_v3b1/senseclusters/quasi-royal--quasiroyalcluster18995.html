quasi-royal

adjective
having king-like power without royal rank
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑːzaɪ ˈrɔɪəl / (AmE), / ˌkwɑːzɪ ˈrɔɪəl / (BrE)
having power or authority similar to that of a monarch, but without the official rank or title of royalty

Examples

Quasi-royal authority
Some spiritual leaders in history exercised quasi-royal authority over their followers.
The CEO's quasi-royal privileges set her apart from the rest of the executive team.
The family held a quasi-royal status in their home country due to their immense influence.
