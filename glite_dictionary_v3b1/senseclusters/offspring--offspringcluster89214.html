offspring

noun
a person's children or descendants
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsprɪŋ /; / ˈɑːfˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
a person's children, an animal's young, or the young of a plant; immediate descendants

Examples

Animal offspring
All three offspring attended their mother's birthday party together.
Genetic traits can be inherited by an organism's offspring.
The lioness fiercely protected her offspring from danger.
Farmers often keep the offspring of their best livestock to improve breeding stock.
Tadpoles are the aquatic offspring of frogs before they become adults.
The lioness carefully protected her offspring from predators.

  • synonym: descendant - a person descended from a particular ancestor
  • antonym: parent - an animal or person's father or mother
  • synonym: progeny - descendants or offspring, especially collectively
