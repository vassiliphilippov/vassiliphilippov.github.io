A force or influence that makes something happen or move; the energy or motivation behind an action.

Examples

Impetus for change
Rising fuel prices served as the impetus for developing alternative energy sources.
The discovery gave fresh impetus to the research project.
The new manager provided the impetus the team needed to reach its goals.

Synonyms

  • impulsion
    8.1 0.07/m
    impulsion is a rare, formal word for a force that prompts action, while impetus is the common term for driving energy.
  • driving force
    4.1 3.27/m
    a driving force is the power or agent that moves something forward, while impetus names the energy or motivation behind action.
  • motivation
    2.7 43/m
    motivation is the inner reason or desire to act, while impetus often means an external push or quick burst of energy that starts action.
  • push
    2.6 10/m
    a push is a short, often forceful effort to get something moving, while impetus means the energy or drive behind longer action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: momentum - the quantity of motion of a moving body
