Book of Ecclesiastes

proper noun
Old Testament book reflecting on life's meaning
9.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkliziˈæstiz / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈæstiːz / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament consisting of reflections on the meaning and vanity of human life, traditionally attributed to Solomon but likely written around 250 BC.

Examples

The Book of Ecclesiastes reflections
Many scholars debate the authorship and historical context of the Book of Ecclesiastes.
Our discussion in class focused on the theme of vanity central to the Book of Ecclesiastes.
The wisdom literature in the Book of Ecclesiastes offers a philosophical perspective on human existence.
