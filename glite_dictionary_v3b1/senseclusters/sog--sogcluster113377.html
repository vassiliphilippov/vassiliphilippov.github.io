sog

verb
to become soaked
7.2
Pronunciation: / sɑɡ / (AmE), / sɒɡ / (BrE)
to become very wet, soaked, or soggy from absorbing liquid

Examples

Shoes sog in rain
If you leave your shoes outside in the rain, they will sog overnight.
The picnic blanket began to sog after hours on the damp grass.
My socks sog quickly when I walk through wet grass.
The cardboard sogged on the doorstep after the summer storm had passed.
If you leave your shoes outside in the rain, they will quickly sog.
The picnic blanket sogged after hours on the damp grass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - to become free from moisture
  • synonym: soak - to become thoroughly wet by absorbing liquid
