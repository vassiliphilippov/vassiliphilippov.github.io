Lorenzo the Magnificent

proper noun
Italian Renaissance statesman and patron of the arts
9.9
Pronunciation: / loʊˈrɛntsoʊ ði məgˈnɪfɪsənt / (AmE), / ləˈrɛntsəʊ ði mægˈnɪfɪsənt / (BrE)
(1449–1492) Italian statesman and scholar, a leading figure of the Renaissance who supported artists such as Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci, and Botticelli.

Examples

Lorenzo the Magnificent ruler
As ruler of Florence, Lorenzo the Magnificent fostered an environment that allowed Renaissance art to flourish.
Many historians credit Lorenzo the Magnificent with supporting Michelangelo early in his career.
The Medici family's influence reached its peak under Lorenzo the Magnificent during the late 15th century.
