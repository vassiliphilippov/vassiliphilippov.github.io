violent

adjective
caused by force or injury
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪələnt /; / ˈvaɪə.lənt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪələnt /; / ˈvaɪə.lənt / (BrE)
using or showing great force, aggression, or strong emotions, often in a destructive or harmful way

Examples

Violent attack
He had suffered a violent injury in the accident.
The investigator determined that it had been a violent death rather than one from natural causes.
The police suspect foul play after a series of violent attacks in the area.
His violent hatred for dishonesty became obvious to everyone in the room.
She spoke with violent passion about the injustice she had witnessed.
There was a violent argument between the two men over the decision.
A violent storm damaged houses and uprooted trees last night.
He was overwhelmed by a violent rage when he heard the news.
The protesters staged a violent demonstration that resulted in several injuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront; characterized by aggression
  • synonym: fervent - showing strong and sincere feelings
  • antonym: gentle - having or showing a mild, kind, or tender temperament or character
  • antonym: mild - not severe, strong, or forceful in feelings or effect
  • antonym: natural - (of death, etc.) occurring due to natural causes, not by force
  • synonym: vehement - showing strong feeling; forceful, passionate, or intense
