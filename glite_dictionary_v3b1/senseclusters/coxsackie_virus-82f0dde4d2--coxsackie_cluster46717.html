Coxsackie virus

noun
an enterovirus causing mild polio-like diseases
Pronunciation: / kɑkˌsæki ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / kɒkˌsæki ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
an enterovirus that causes various diseases, often resembling mild polio but typically without paralysis

Examples

Coxsackie virus infection
Hand, foot, and mouth disease is commonly caused by a type of Coxsackie virus.
Outbreaks of Coxsackie virus often occur in childcare settings during the summer months.
The doctor suspected that the child had contracted a Coxsackie virus after reviewing the symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enterovirus - any virus of the genus Enterovirus, including poliovirus, coxsackievirus, and echovirus
