downtrend

noun
declining trend
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.trend/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.trend/ (BrE)
a period or pattern when prices, values, or activity are steadily decreasing, especially in financial markets

Examples

A downtrend in prices
Investors became cautious as the downtrend in the housing market continued for several months.
The company had to cut costs to survive the prolonged downtrend in demand for its products.
Analysts warned that the recent downtrend could signal a broader economic slowdown.
The stock market entered a downtrend after several months of steady growth.
Traders often look for signs that a downtrend is ending before buying shares.
A clear downtrend can signal investors to be cautious about making new investments.
Investors became cautious as the market entered a prolonged downtrend last quarter.
During a downtrend, many traders look for signs of recovery before buying stocks.
The company’s profits suffered due to the industry-wide downtrend in demand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, quality, or value
  • synonym: slump - a sudden severe or prolonged fall in price, value, or amount
  • antonym: uptrend - a general increase in value, activity, or price over time
