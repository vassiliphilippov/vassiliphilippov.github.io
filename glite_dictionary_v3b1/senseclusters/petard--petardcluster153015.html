petard

noun
explosive device for breaching gates or walls
Pronunciation: / pɪˈtɑrd / (AmE), / pɪˈtɑːd / (BrE)
a small bomb used in historical warfare to breach gates or walls

Examples

A petard bomb
In medieval times, a petard was often deployed during sieges.
The blast from the petard demolished a section of the fortress wall.
The soldiers placed a petard at the castle gate to force their way in.
