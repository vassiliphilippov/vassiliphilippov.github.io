flocculation

noun
formation of clumps in a liquid
Pronunciation: / ˌflɑːkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌflɒkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which individual particles in a liquid clump together to form fluffy or cloudlike masses (flocs), often as part of water treatment or in chemical processes

Examples

Flocculation process
Brewers monitor flocculation to ensure yeast settles properly after fermentation.
During water treatment, flocculation helps remove impurities by clumping particles together.
The visible flocculation of proteins signals a change in the chemical environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulation - the process of particles coming together to form a mass or clot, often used synonymously in chemistry
