philosophical

adjective
relating to philosophy or philosophers
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪləˈsɑːfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to philosophy or philosophers; concerned with the study of fundamental nature of knowledge, reality, and existence.

Examples

Philosophical ideas
His research is based on philosophical principles that have been debated for centuries.
Many students struggle to understand philosophical arguments in their first year at university.
She has a philosophical interest in the meaning of existence and reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theory or ideas
  • synonym: theoretical - concerned with theory rather than practical application
