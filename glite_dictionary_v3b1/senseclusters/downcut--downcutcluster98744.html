downcut

noun
downward-cutting tool
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌkʌt / (BrE)
a cutting tool, such as a router bit, designed to cut downward and pull chips down for a clean top surface.

Examples

A downcut bit
The woodworker used a downcut to achieve a smooth finish on the tabletop.
Switching to a downcut helped prevent splintering along the edges of the plywood.
For a clean top surface, the technician recommended a downcut over other types of bits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: router bit - a tool used in a router for cutting or shaping wood
  • antonym: upcut - a cutting tool that cuts upward and pulls chips up
