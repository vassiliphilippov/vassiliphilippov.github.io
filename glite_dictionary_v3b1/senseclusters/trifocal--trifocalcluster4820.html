trifocal

adjective
three-section lenses
Pronunciation: / traɪˈfoʊkəl / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊkəl / (BrE)
describing eyeglass lenses or optics that have three sections or focal points for near, intermediate, and distance vision

Examples

Trifocal lenses
Her new trifocal glasses allow her to read, use the computer, and see far away without switching pairs.
The optometrist recommended trifocal lenses for patients who need help with all three vision ranges.
He adjusted quickly to his trifocal spectacles and found them very convenient for daily activities.
The optometrist recommended trifocal lenses to help her see clearly at all distances.
A trifocal camera system can capture images at three different focal lengths simultaneously.
He adjusted the trifocal settings on the microscope for a more detailed examination.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bifocal - describing eyeglass lenses with two sections for near and distance vision
  • degree higher: multifocal - describing eyeglass lenses with multiple sections for different distances
