defalcation

noun
sum of money misappropriated
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːfælˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfælˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
an amount of money that has been misappropriated or embezzled, especially by someone trusted with its charge

Examples

A defalcation amount
Insurance covered the bank's losses due to defalcation by an employee.
The audit revealed a substantial defalcation in the company's accounts.
The treasurer was accused of defalcation after several thousand dollars went missing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embezzlement - the act of dishonestly taking money entrusted to one's care
  • antonym: restitution - the act of restoring something lost or stolen to its proper owner
