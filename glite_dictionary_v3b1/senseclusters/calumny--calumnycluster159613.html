calumny

noun
false, damaging statement; slander
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləmni / (AmE), / ˈkæləmni /; / ˈkaləmni / (BrE)
the act of making false and damaging statements about someone, or the false statement itself (slander, defamation)

Examples

Vicious calumny
His reputation was ruined by years of vicious calumny and false accusations.
She filed a lawsuit after the newspaper published a calumny against her.
The politician was the target of calumny from his opponents during the campaign.
Her reputation suffered from the constant calumny spread by her rivals.
It is difficult to recover from the effects of a vicious calumny once it circulates.
The politician sued the newspaper for calumny after the damaging article was published.

Related Terms

  • synonym: libel - a published false statement that is damaging to a person's reputation
  • antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
