quarterstaff

noun
long wooden weapon staff
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔrtɚˌstæf / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtəˌstɑːf / (BrE)
a long, stout wooden staff, traditionally used as a weapon, especially in medieval England

Examples

A wooden quarterstaff
He practiced martial arts using a wooden quarterstaff in the village square.
Historically, the quarterstaff was a common weapon among English peasants.
The knight defended himself skillfully with his quarterstaff against the bandits.
