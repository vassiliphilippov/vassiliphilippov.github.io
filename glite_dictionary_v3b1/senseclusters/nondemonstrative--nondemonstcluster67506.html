nondemonstrative

adjective
not specifying directly
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˈmɑnstrətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈmɒnstrətɪv / (BrE)
not indicating or pointing out a specific item, person, or location, especially in grammar.

Examples

Nondemonstrative pronoun
The pronoun in this sentence is nondemonstrative because it does not refer to a specific object.
In English, words like 'someone' and 'anything' are considered nondemonstrative pronouns.
Her explanation used only nondemonstrative language, avoiding direct references to particular people or places.
