alidade

noun
movable part of a surveying instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌdeɪd / (AmE), / ˈælɪdeɪd / (BrE)
a surveying instrument used to measure angles or directions, often as part of a theodolite or with a plane table

Examples

The alidade instrument
Before taking a reading, ensure the alidade is properly aligned with the reference point.
Older theodolites often feature a brass alidade mounted above the graduated circle.
The surveyor carefully adjusted the alidade to measure the angle of the distant hill.
Historical maps were often created with the help of a simple alidade and measuring tape.
The surveyor used an alidade to precisely determine the line of sight to the distant marker.
When mapping the field, she carefully positioned the alidade on the plane table.
