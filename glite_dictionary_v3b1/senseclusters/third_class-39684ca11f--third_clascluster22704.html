third class

noun
type of mail at reduced postal rate
Pronunciation: / ˌθɝd ˈklæs / (AmE), / ˌθɜːd ˈklɑːs / (BrE)
printed matter sent at a reduced postal rate, traditionally designated as a category of mail for items like advertising and circulars

Examples

Third class mail
Many businesses used third class mail to send out advertising flyers to customers.
Sending the newsletter as third class significantly reduced shipping costs for the publisher.
The post office no longer offers third class rates, as services have been renamed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: first class - highest category of mail service, prioritizing speed and reliability
  • degree higher: second class - category of mail service, often for periodicals and newspapers
