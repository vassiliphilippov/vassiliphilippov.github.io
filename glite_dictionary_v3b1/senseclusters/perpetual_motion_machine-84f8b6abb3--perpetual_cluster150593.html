perpetual motion machine

noun
impossible self-sustaining machine
Pronunciation: / pərˈpɛtʃuəl ˈmoʊʃən məˈʃin / (AmE), / pəˈpɛtʃuəl ˈməʊʃən məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
a hypothetical machine that can operate indefinitely without an external energy source, in violation of the law of conservation of energy

Examples

A perpetual motion machine
Every few years, someone claims to have invented a working perpetual motion machine.
Inventors have long dreamed of building a perpetual motion machine, but none have succeeded.
The laws of physics prove that a perpetual motion machine cannot exist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perpetual motion - the continuous movement of a body or system without external energy input
