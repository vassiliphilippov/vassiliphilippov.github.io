Spitfire

proper noun
WWII British aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪtˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈspɪtˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a British single-seat fighter aircraft used by the Royal Air Force during World War II, especially in the Battle of Britain

Examples

The Spitfire fighter
The Spitfire was famous for its speed and agility in aerial combat.
Pilots trained for months before flying a Spitfire in battle.
A restored Spitfire was displayed at the airshow, drawing large crowds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hurricane - a British fighter aircraft used alongside the Spitfire in World War II
  • antonym: Messerschmitt - a German fighter aircraft used during World War II
