full

adjective
satisfied (from food or drink)
1.0 47/m
Pronunciation: / fÊŠl / (AmE), / fÊŠl / (BrE)
Having eaten or drunk enough so that no more is wanted; satisfied by food or drink.

Examples

I feel full after dinner
After eating three slices of pizza, I felt completely full.
It's best not to go swimming right after a full meal.
She pushed her plate away, saying she was already full.

Synonyms

  • replete
    6.7 0.09/m
    replete is a formal word for having eaten so much you cannot eat more, while full is a common everyday feeling after eating.
  • satiated
    5.5 0.03/m
    satiated means having had enough or more food and feeling satisfied after a meal, while full means not wanting more to eat.
  • stuffed
    1.0 0.01/m
    stuffed is informal and describes feeling overly filled and often uncomfortable after heavy eating, while full means you have eaten enough and do not want more food.
  • satisfied
    1.0 2.82/m
    satisfied is used for many needs like hunger or curiosity, while full specifically means you no longer want more food.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hungry - feeling the need for food
  • degree higher: stuffed - feeling extremely full after eating a lot
