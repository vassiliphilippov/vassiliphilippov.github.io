nervous exhaustion

noun
mental and physical fatigue due to stress
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnɝːvəs ɪɡˈzɑːs.tʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ɪɡˈzɒs.tʃən / (BrE)
A mental and physical condition marked by extreme fatigue, inability to cope with normal activities, and emotional distress, often resulting from prolonged stress or anxiety.

Examples

Severe nervous exhaustion
After months of intense work and little rest, she suffered from nervous exhaustion.
Doctors diagnosed his inability to concentrate as a sign of nervous exhaustion.
Many nineteenth-century writers described nervous exhaustion as a common problem among urban professionals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burnout - a state of physical or mental collapse caused by overwork or stress
