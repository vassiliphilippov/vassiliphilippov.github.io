stupor

noun
a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
7.7 1/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàstuÀê.p…ö /; / ÀàstuÀêp…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàstjuÀê.p…ô /; / ÀàstjuÀêp…ô / (BrE)
a state in which a person is almost unconscious and unable to think or feel properly

Examples

In a stupor
His grief left him in a stupor from which he struggled to recover.
She sat in a stupor for hours after hearing the news of her dismissal.
The patient remained in a stupor after the accident, barely responding to questions.
Alcohol had left him in such a deep stupor that he did not remember the events of the night.
She remained in a stupor for hours after the accident, barely responding to anyone around her.
The patient was found in a stupor, unable to move or speak clearly.

Synonyms

  • sopor
    9.9 0.01/m
    sopor is a deep sleep caused by illness or drugs that is hard to wake from, while stupor is almost unconscious and unable to think.
  • semiconsciousness
    7.7 0.03/m
    semiconsciousness means being partly awake and aware, while stupor leaves someone nearly unconscious and unable to think or feel properly.
  • half-consciousness
    7.5 0.00/m
    half-consciousness is a state of only partial awareness of surroundings and feelings, while stupor is near-unconscious and unresponsive.
  • stupefaction
    9.5 0.1/m
    stupefaction is strong shock or astonishment that freezes your mind, while stupor is a near-unconscious state that blocks thought and feeling.
  • daze
    5.3 1/m
    daze is a confused, stunned feeling after shock or pain, while stupor is a deeper, near-unconscious state that dulls all senses.
  • grogginess
    4.8 0.1/m
    grogginess is a heavy, slow feeling after sleep or alcohol, while stupor is a much deeper near-unconsciousness that prevents thinking or feeling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alertness - the state of being fully aware and responsive
  • degree higher: coma - a state of deep unconsciousness from which a person cannot be awakened
  • synonym: daze - a state in which someone cannot think clearly
