crash barrier

noun
protective barrier on roads or racetracks
2.6
Pronunciation: / kræʃ ˈbæriər / (AmE), / kræʃ ˈbæriə / (BrE)
A strong protective barrier installed on roads or racetracks to prevent vehicles from leaving the roadway or crossing into oncoming traffic, reducing the risk of severe accidents.

Examples

A crash barrier on road
After the accident, engineers inspected the crash barrier for any signs of damage.
Highways are often divided by a central crash barrier to improve safety.
The car skidded on the wet road and struck the crash barrier at high speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guardrail - A rail serving as a barrier beside a road or track
