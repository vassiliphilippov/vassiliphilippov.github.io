represent

verb
be or constitute; amount to
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to be an example or typical instance of a group, quality, or style; to exemplify or constitute

Examples

To represent a group
For many, the flag represents their hopes for a better future.
His share represents 20 percent of the total amount.
This small sum represents all the money I have in the world.
Each team represents a different region of the country.
The painting represents the style of early Renaissance art.
This statue represents the ideals of freedom and unity.
Modern jazz represents a significant shift in the history of music.
The Renaissance period is often represented by artists like Leonardo da Vinci.
The bald eagle represents freedom in the United States.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comprise - to consist of; to be made up of
  • synonym: constitute - to make up or form; to be the parts that together form something
  • synonym: embody - to be an expression or give a tangible form to an idea, quality, or feeling
  • antonym: exclude - to leave out; to not include
  • synonym: exemplify - to be a typical example of something
  • antonym: misrepresent - to give a false or misleading account of
  • synonym: symbolize - to represent or stand for something
