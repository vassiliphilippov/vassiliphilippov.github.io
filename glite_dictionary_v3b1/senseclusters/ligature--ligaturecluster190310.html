ligature

noun
thing used for tying or binding
7.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪ.gə.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈlɪ.gə.tʃə/ (BrE)
a thread, wire, or other material used by surgeons to tie and bind blood vessels or tissues during a medical procedure

Examples

Surgical ligature
A silk ligature was used to secure the bundle of herbs together.
He carefully applied a ligature to prevent bleeding from the wound.
The surgeon tied off the artery with a ligature.
A ligature must be securely fastened to ensure that the vessel does not reopen.
Absorbable ligatures are often used in surgical procedures to avoid the need for later removal.
The surgeon tied a ligature tightly around the artery to prevent further bleeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suture - stitch or thread used to close a wound or incision in surgery
