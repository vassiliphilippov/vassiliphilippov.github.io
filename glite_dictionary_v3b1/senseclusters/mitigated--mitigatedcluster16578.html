mitigated

adjective
made less severe or intense
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪt̬.ɪ.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
made less severe, serious, or painful; lessened in intensity or harshness.

Examples

A mitigated punishment

Thanks to the new safety measures, the risks have been mitigated considerably.
The president offered a mitigated response to the crisis, seeking to ease public concerns.
