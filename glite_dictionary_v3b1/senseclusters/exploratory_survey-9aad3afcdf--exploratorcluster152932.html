exploratory survey

noun
preliminary investigation to gain information
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsplɔrəˌtɔri ˈsɜr.veɪ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsplɒr.əˌtɔː.ri ˈsɜː.veɪ / (BrE)
a preliminary investigation conducted to collect information and gain a general understanding of a subject, often before a more detailed or formal study

Examples

An exploratory survey of population
An exploratory survey of the local population revealed several concerns about environmental pollution.
Before launching the new product, the company carried out an exploratory survey to assess market demand.
The researchers conducted an exploratory survey to identify key issues before designing their main experiment.
