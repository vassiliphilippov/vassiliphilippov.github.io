parasitize

verb
to exploit for gain
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌsaɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpærəsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to exploit or take advantage of someone or something for personal gain while giving little or nothing in return

Examples

Companies parasitize trends
Some companies try to parasitize popular trends without adding real value to the market.
He was accused of attempting to parasitize the team's hard work for his own benefit.
Certain plants parasitize others by drawing nutrients from their roots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exploit - to use someone or something unfairly for personal benefit
  • antonym: support - to help or provide assistance to someone or something
