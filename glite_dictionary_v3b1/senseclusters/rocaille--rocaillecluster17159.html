rocaille

noun
decorative art style
Pronunciation: / roʊˈkaɪ / (AmE), / rɒˈkaɪ / (BrE)
an ornamental style featuring rock and shell motifs, especially used in 18th-century decorative art and architecture

Examples

Rocaille style in art
The ornate mirror was framed in the rocaille style, with delicate shell and rock patterns.
Many 18th-century French salons featured rocaille decorations on their walls and ceilings.
The museum's collection includes several pieces of furniture with intricate rocaille carvings.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: baroque - a grand and ornate style of art and architecture from the 17th and early 18th centuries
  • synonym: rococo - an elaborate decorative style of the 18th century with curves and ornate details
