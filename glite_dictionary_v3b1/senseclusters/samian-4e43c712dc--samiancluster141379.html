Samian

noun
Roman red pottery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪmiən / (AmE), / ˈseɪmiən / (BrE)
a type of fine red-gloss Roman pottery, also known as terra sigillata, commonly found at archaeological sites

Examples

Samian ware
The museum displayed a beautiful Samian bowl with intricate patterns from the Roman era.
Archaeologists often find fragments of Samian ware at ancient Roman settlements across Europe.
Collectors value Samian pottery for its distinctive red gloss and historical significance.
