born and bred

adjective
having been born and raised somewhere
6.3
Pronunciation: / bɔrn ənd brɛd / (AmE), / bɔːn ənd brɛd / (BrE)
(of a person) having been born and raised in a particular place or environment, especially with a strong connection to it.

Examples

Born and bred Londoner
Although he now lives abroad, Mark is a Londoner born and bred.
She was born and bred in Chicago and knows the city inside out.
The chef is born and bred in a family of restaurateurs.
