born and bred

adjective
having been born and raised somewhere
4.0 0.72/m
Pronunciation: / b…îrn …ônd br…õd / (AmE), / b…îÀên …ônd br…õd / (BrE)
(of a person) having been born and raised in a particular place or environment, especially with a strong connection to it.

Examples

Born and bred Londoner
Although he now lives abroad, Mark is a Londoner born and bred.
She was born and bred in Chicago and knows the city inside out.
The chef is born and bred in a family of restaurateurs.

Synonyms

  • native
    1.5 74/m
    native describes someone from a place by birth or origin, while born and bred stresses being both born and raised there.
  • native-born
    3.7 1.41/m
    native-born means born in a place but not always raised there, while born and bred means born and raised with strong local ties.
