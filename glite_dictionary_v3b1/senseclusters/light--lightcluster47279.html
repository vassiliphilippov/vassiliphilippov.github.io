light

verb
to illuminate
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
to make something bright or start a fire; to cause something to burn or shine

Examples

To light a candle
As dusk fell, the lantern lighted the garden path.
She lighted the room with a string of colorful fairy lights.
The old chandelier lights the grand hall magnificently each night.
Could you please light the fireplace before the guests arrive?
Every evening, she would light a candle at her window.
He struggled to light the campfire in the rain.
After dinner, he lit a cigar and sat on the balcony.
She lit her pipe, filling the air with a sweet smell.
You are not allowed to light cigarettes in this building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extinguish - to put out a fire or cause it to stop burning
  • synonym: ignite - to set something on fire; to start to burn
  • synonym: illuminate - to light up; to make bright with light
  • synonym: light up - to light a cigarette or similar item and start smoking it
