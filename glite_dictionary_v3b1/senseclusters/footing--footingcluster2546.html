footing

noun
foundation support base
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
the part of a building's foundation that spreads and supports the load of the structure above it

Examples

Concrete footing
The engineers inspected the footing before continuing with the rest of the construction.
A strong footing is essential to prevent the building from settling unevenly.
They poured concrete to create a solid footing for the new extension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building, typically below ground level
  • antonym: superstructure - the part of a building above its foundation
