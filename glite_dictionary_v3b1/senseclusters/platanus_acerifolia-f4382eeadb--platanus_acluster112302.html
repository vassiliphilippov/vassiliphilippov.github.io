Platanus acerifolia

noun
large deciduous street tree (London plane)
Pronunciation: / pləˈtænəs ˌæsərəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / pləˈtænəs ˌæsərəˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a large, fast-growing deciduous tree (the London plane) widely planted as a street or shade tree, especially in cities

Examples

The Platanus acerifolia tree
In autumn, the broad leaves of Platanus acerifolia turn yellow and fall, carpeting the pavement.
Rows of Platanus acerifolia line the boulevard, creating a pleasant, shady walk during summer.
The city chose Platanus acerifolia for new street plantings because of its hardiness in polluted environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: London plane - large deciduous tree cultivated in cities; hybrid of Platanus orientalis and Platanus occidentalis
