heave

verb
emit a sound or breath with effort
4.3 1.96/m
Pronunciation: / hiv / (AmE), / hiÀêv / (BrE)
to breathe, sigh, or make a sound with effort or exertion

Examples

To heave a sigh
As she read the letter, she heaved a heavy sigh of relief.
He heaved a groan after lifting the box.
When the meeting ended, several people heaved sighs of frustration.
After sprinting up the hill, she stood at the top and heaved for breath.
He collapsed onto the sofa, heaving with exhaustion after the long run.
The players were heaving by the time the final whistle blew.

Synonyms

  • sigh
    1.0 13/m
    a sigh is a long, soft breath that shows emotion, while a heave is a heavier, effortful breath or sound from exertion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pant - to breathe quickly and noisily from exertion
  • synonym: sigh - to emit a long, deep breath expressing emotion
