wear down

verb
make thinner or weaker by use
6.2 3.3
Pronunciation: /weər daʊn/ /; / /wɪər daʊn/ (AmE), /weə daʊn/ /; / /wɪə daʊn/ (BrE)
to gradually make someone or something weaker, less effective, or more tired through continuous use, pressure, or stress

Examples

To wear down resistance
Their constant criticism began to wear down her confidence.
Wind and rain can wear down even the hardest rocks over time.
Years of walking in the same shoes eventually wore down the soles.
After hours of negotiations, the constant arguments began to wear down even the toughest participants.
She was determined not to let criticism wear her down, but it became more difficult over time.
The repetitive tasks at his new job quickly wore down his enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erode - gradually wear away by natural agents
  • synonym: exhaust - to make someone extremely tired
  • antonym: revive - to restore to life or consciousness
  • synonym: wear out - use something until it is no longer usable
