inept

adjective
lacking skill or judgment
3.6 5.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɛpt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛpt / (BrE)
lacking skill or ability; clumsy, awkward, or incompetent

Examples

An inept person
Her inept handling of the situation only made things worse.
His inept remark during the meeting made everyone uncomfortable.
The new intern was inept at dealing with difficult customers.
He was known for his inept handling of difficult situations.
Her inept attempt to fix the computer only made matters worse.
The company's inept management led to its rapid decline.
Her inept handling of the subject led to many misunderstandings.
His inept attempt at humor left everyone in awkward silence.
The speech was so inept that it offended rather than inspired the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adept - very skilled or proficient at something
  • synonym: awkward - lacking social grace, skill, or ease
  • synonym: clumsy - lacking skill or grace in movement or action
  • antonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills
  • antonym: elegant - refined, graceful, and stylish
  • synonym: tactless - lacking tact; insensitive in dealing with others
