In a commanding, domineering, or overbearing manner that shows a sense of superiority or authority.

Examples

To speak imperiously
He brushed aside their concerns imperiously, showing little regard for their opinions.
She spoke imperiously, expecting everyone to obey her without question.
The manager waved his hand imperiously to dismiss the suggestion.

Synonyms

    8.1 0.00/m
    domineeringly means acting to control people openly and repeatedly, while imperiously adds a proud, superior, haughty tone.
  • overbearingly
    7.9 0.02/m
    overbearingly is domineering and pushy toward others, while imperiously adds a touch of proud superiority and firm command.
  • high-handedly
    8.2 0.01/m
    high-handedly treats people without care for their views, while imperiously shows a ruling, haughty sense of command.
  • dictatorially
    9.1 0.03/m
    dictatorially acts like an absolute ruler who forces others, while imperiously acts with proud superiority rather than brutal force.
  • magisterially
    7.7 0.03/m
    magisterially speaks with dignified authority, while imperiously adds haughty superiority and a colder, more commanding tone that feels distant.
  • commandingly
    5.8 0.07/m
    commandingly shows clear authority and control, while imperiously adds a proud, arrogant attitude often to that command.
  • peremptorily
    9.8 0.1/m
    peremptorily orders someone without allowing reply, while imperiously behaves as if superior and expects immediate, unquestioning obedience.
