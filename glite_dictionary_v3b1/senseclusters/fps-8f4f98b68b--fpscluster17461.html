FPS

noun
video frame rate
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɛf pi ɛs / (AmE), / ɛf piː ɛs / (BrE)
frames per second; a measure of how many images are displayed each second in video or games, indicating smoothness of motion

Examples

Video FPS
A higher FPS makes video games look smoother and more realistic.
The movie was filmed at 24 FPS, which is the standard for cinema.
If your computer cannot maintain a stable FPS, the gameplay may appear choppy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refresh rate - the number of times a display updates per second
