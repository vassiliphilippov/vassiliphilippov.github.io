bridge

noun
support on stringed instrument
7.5
Pronunciation: /brɪdʒ/ (AmE), /brɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a piece of wood or other material on a stringed instrument that supports the strings and transmits their vibrations to the body of the instrument

Examples

Guitar bridge
If the bridge is misplaced, the guitar's strings may not sound correctly.
Luthiers often carve the bridge by hand to suit each instrument's needs.
The violinist carefully adjusted the bridge to improve the instrument's tone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nut - small ridge that supports the strings at the end of the fingerboard
  • synonym: tailpiece - stringed instrument part that anchors the strings at the bottom
