aseity

noun
self-existence quality
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈsiːəti / (AmE), / əˈsiːɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being self-existent or existing independently, especially used in theology to describe God

Examples

God's aseity
In classical theology, God's aseity means that He depends on nothing outside Himself.
Philosophers have debated the concept of aseity for centuries.
Some religious traditions emphasize the aseity of the divine as a core belief.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contingency - dependence on something else for existence
  • synonym: self-sufficiency - the ability to exist or function independently
