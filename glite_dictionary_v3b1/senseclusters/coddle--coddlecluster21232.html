coddle

verb
cook food in water just below boiling
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.l̩ / (BrE)
to cook food, especially eggs, gently in water just below the boiling point

Examples

To coddle eggs
If you coddle tomatoes, their skins will slip off easily for sauce making.
Many chefs prefer to coddle eggs because the whites stay delicate and tender.
To make the perfect breakfast, she likes to coddle her eggs rather than boiling them.
