take time off

verb
temporarily stop working
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk taɪm ɔf /; / teɪk taɪm ɑf / (AmE), / teɪk taɪm ɒf / (BrE)
to stop working or take a break from duties for a period of time, usually to rest, recover, or handle personal matters

Examples

To take time off work
He plans to take time off next month to travel with his family.
Sarah decided to take time off after completing the big project.
Sometimes you need to take time off to focus on your mental health.
After working nonstop for months, he decided to take time off and travel abroad.
Many employees take time off during the summer to go on vacation.
She needs to take time off from work to care for her sick mother.

  • antonym: work - to be engaged in physical or mental activity for employment
