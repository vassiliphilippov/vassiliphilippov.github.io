create friction

verb
cause physical resistance between surfaces
3.9
Pronunciation: /kriː.ˈeɪt ˈfrɪk.ʃən/ (AmE), /kriː.ˈeɪt ˈfrɪk.ʃən/ (BrE)
to cause resistance when two surfaces move against each other; to make two surfaces rub together so they resist motion.

Examples

Create friction between surfaces
To test wear, rub the surfaces to create friction between them.
Designers sometimes add textured patterns so tires create friction that improves grip on wet roads.
When two metals slide without oil, they can create friction and heat that damages components.

Related Terms

  • synonym: increase friction - to make the resistance between surfaces greater, often by changing texture or material
  • antonym: lubricate - to apply a substance (like oil) to reduce friction between moving parts
