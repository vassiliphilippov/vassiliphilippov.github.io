jibe

verb
to shift a sail to the other side (nautical)
Pronunciation: / dʒaɪb / (AmE), / dʒaɪb / (BrE)
(of a sail or a sailing vessel) to shift suddenly from one side to the other when sailing with the wind, or to cause this to happen.

Examples

To jibe the sail
If you jibe too quickly, you risk losing control of the boom.
The captain ordered the crew to jibe the mainsail as the wind changed direction.
They practiced jibing in calm waters before attempting the open sea.
If you gybe too quickly in strong wind, the boom can be dangerous.
The skipper ordered the crew to gybe as we approached the mark.
We gybed several times while maneuvering through the narrow channel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gybe - to shift a sail from one side to the other, especially when running before the wind
  • synonym: jibe - to change course by shifting the sail across the wind; alternative spelling
