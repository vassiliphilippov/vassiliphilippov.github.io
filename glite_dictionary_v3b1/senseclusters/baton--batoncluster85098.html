baton

noun
conductor's rod for leading music
Pronunciation: / bəˈtɑn / (AmE), / ˈbæt.ɒn / (BrE)
A thin, tapered rod used by a conductor to direct an orchestra or choir.

Examples

Conductor's baton
She lost her baton just before the concert started, causing a brief panic.
The conductor raised his baton and the orchestra fell silent.
With a flick of his baton, the choir began singing in perfect harmony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baton - short stick carried by a police officer as a weapon
