legitimist

noun
supporter of rightful ruler
Pronunciation: / lɪˈdʒɪtəmɪst / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports the claim of a legitimate ruler or the Legitimist movement, especially in the context of royal succession

Examples

A legitimist leader
The legitimist movement gained strength after the king was deposed.
As a proud legitimist, she believed only the true heir should rule.
Debates between the legitimist and republican factions often became heated.
The legitimist argued passionately for the restoration of the Bourbon monarchy in France.
During the 19th century, many legitimists opposed the rise of republicanism.
He identified as a legitimist and attended royalist gatherings in Paris.

Related Terms

  • antonym: republican - a person who supports a republic instead of a monarchy
  • synonym: royalist - a supporter of a king or monarchy
