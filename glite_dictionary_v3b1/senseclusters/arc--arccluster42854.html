arc

noun
continuous electrical discharge through a gas
7.3 2/m
Pronunciation: / …ërk / (AmE), / …ëÀêk / (BrE)
A continuous electrical discharge through a gas, producing light and heat, caused by an applied electric field.

Examples

Electric arc
A lightning bolt is a natural form of an electric arc.
An arc lamp produces illumination by sustaining an electric discharge between two electrodes.
The welder created a bright arc to fuse the metal pieces together.

Synonyms

  • electric arc
    5.4 0.07/m
    an electric arc is a bright, steady discharge between two electrodes in gas, while an arc is a continuous electric discharge producing light and heat.
  • electric discharge
    5.2 0.02/m
    an electric discharge is a general flow of electricity through a gas that can produce light, while an arc is a continuous, bright discharge.
