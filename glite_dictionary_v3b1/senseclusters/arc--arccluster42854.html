arc

noun
continuous electrical discharge through a gas
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɑrk / (AmE), / ɑːk / (BrE)
A continuous electrical discharge through a gas, producing light and heat, caused by an applied electric field.

Examples

Electric arc
A lightning bolt is a natural form of an electric arc.
An arc lamp produces illumination by sustaining an electric discharge between two electrodes.
The welder created a bright arc to fuse the metal pieces together.
