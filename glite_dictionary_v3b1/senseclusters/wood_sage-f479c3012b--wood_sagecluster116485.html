wood sage

noun
North American shrubby sage with spiked flowers
9.7
Pronunciation: / wʊd seɪdʒ / (AmE), / wʊd seɪdʒ / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub with serrated leaves and cream to pink or purple flowers in spikes, native to North America.

Examples

Wild wood sage
Hikers often spot wood sage along forest trails in the northeastern United States.
She pressed the leaves of wood sage to make a fragrant herbal tea.
The wood sage in the garden attracted many pollinators during the summer.
