croon

verb
sing or hum softly and gently
8.4
Pronunciation: / kruːn / (AmE), / kruːn / (BrE)
to sing or hum in a soft, low, and gentle voice, often in an affectionate or sentimental manner

Examples

To croon a song
He sat by the fire, quietly crooning a familiar tune to himself.
She would croon lullabies to her baby every night before bed.
The singer crooned a romantic ballad to the delighted audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sing - to produce musical tones with the voice
