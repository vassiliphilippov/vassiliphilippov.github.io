secernment

noun
discriminating between stimuli
Pronunciation: / sɪˈsɜrn.mənt / (AmE), / sɪˈsɜːn.mənt / (BrE)
the cognitive process of distinguishing between two or more stimuli

Examples

Secernment of colors
Children develop secernment as they learn to tell colors and shapes apart.
Effective teaching often requires the secernment of students' individual learning needs.
His keen secernment allowed him to notice subtle differences in musical tones.
