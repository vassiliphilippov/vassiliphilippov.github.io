Kokka

noun
State Shinto of Japan
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ko äÀàk…ëÀê / (AmE), / k…ô äÀàk…ëÀê / (BrE)
the branch of Shintoism that was recognized and promoted as the official state religion of Japan, especially during the Meiji period and until the end of World War II

Examples

Kokka Shinto religion
During the Meiji era, Kokka was institutionalized to strengthen national identity.
Historians often study the influence of Kokka on Japanese society and politics.
The practices associated with Kokka were separated from government affairs after World War II.
