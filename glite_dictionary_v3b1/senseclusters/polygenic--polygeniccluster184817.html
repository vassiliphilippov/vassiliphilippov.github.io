polygenic

adjective
involving multiple genes
6.2 7.2
Pronunciation: /pɑː.lɪ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ /; / /pə.lɪ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (AmE), /pɒ.lɪ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ /; / /pə.lɪ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a trait or characteristic) controlled by or relating to several genes acting together rather than by a single gene.

Examples

Polygenic trait
Height is a polygenic trait influenced by the combined effects of many genes.
Many common diseases, such as diabetes, have a polygenic basis.
Research shows that intelligence is at least partly polygenic, involving numerous genetic factors.
