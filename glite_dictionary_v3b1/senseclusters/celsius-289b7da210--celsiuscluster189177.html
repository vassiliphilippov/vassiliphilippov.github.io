Celsius

noun
temperature scale
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛlsiəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛlsiəs / (BrE)
a scale for measuring temperature in which water freezes at 0 degrees and boils at 100 degrees

Examples

Degrees Celsius
The temperature outside reached 35 degrees Celsius during the heatwave.
Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius at sea level.
Doctors recommend keeping the fridge below 4 degrees Celsius to store food safely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Fahrenheit - a temperature scale where water freezes at 32 degrees and boils at 212 degrees
