concretistic

adjective
characterized by concrete interpretation
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːŋ.krɪˈtɪs.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒŋ.krɪˈtɪs.tɪk / (BrE)
Relating to, or characterized by, concrete representations or interpretations rather than abstract ones.

Examples

Concretistic approach
A concretistic approach to teaching mathematics might use plenty of visual aids and real-life examples.
Her concretistic explanations helped young students grasp complex philosophical concepts.
The artist's style was highly concretistic, focusing on tangible objects rather than abstract ideas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstract - existing in thought or as an idea but not having a physical or concrete existence
  • synonym: concrete - existing in a material or physical form; not abstract
