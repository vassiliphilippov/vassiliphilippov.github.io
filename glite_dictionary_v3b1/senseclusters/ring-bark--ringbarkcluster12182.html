ring-bark

verb
to remove bark ring
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪŋ bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋ bɑːk / (BrE)
to remove a strip of bark in a complete circle from around the trunk or branch of a tree, usually causing it to die

Examples

To ring-bark a tree
The farmer ring-barked several trees to clear space for new crops.
If you ring-bark a tree, it will usually die within a year.
They accidentally ring-barked the young sapling while removing vines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: girdle - to cut or remove a ring of bark from a tree
