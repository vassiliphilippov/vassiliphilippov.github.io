absorption coefficient

noun
rate of decrease in radiation intensity through a material
1.3
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɔrpʃən koʊˈɪfɪʃənt / (AmE), / əbˈzɔːpʃən kəʊˈɪfɪʃnt / (BrE)
a measure of how much the intensity of electromagnetic radiation, such as light, decreases as it passes through a material; the fraction of incident radiant energy absorbed per unit mass or thickness of an absorber

Examples

The absorption coefficient value
A higher absorption coefficient means more energy is absorbed by the material.
Scientists measured the absorption coefficient to determine how much radiation was absorbed.
The absorption coefficient of water varies depending on the wavelength of light.
