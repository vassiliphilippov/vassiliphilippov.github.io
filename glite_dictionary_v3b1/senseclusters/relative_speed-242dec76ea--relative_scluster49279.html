relative speed

noun
speed of one object measured relative to another
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪv spiːd / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪv spiːd / (BrE)
the speed of one object as measured in relation to another object, typically considering one as stationary

Examples

Relative speed between objects
Astronomers calculate the relative speed of galaxies to understand the expansion of the universe.
The relative speed between the two cars made the collision much more severe.
When measuring the relative speed of trains, it's important to consider the direction of travel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relative velocity - the velocity of one body with respect to another
