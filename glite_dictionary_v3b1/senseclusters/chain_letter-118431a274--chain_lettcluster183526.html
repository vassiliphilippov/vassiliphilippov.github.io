chain letter

noun
a letter asking recipients to send copies to others
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪn ˌlɛtər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn ˌlɛtə / (BrE)
a letter, often sent by mail or email, that instructs each recipient to send copies to several others, sometimes as part of a scam or to perpetuate a hoax.

Examples

Chain letters by email
Many people find chain letters annoying and delete them immediately.
Sending a chain letter is often considered a form of spam, especially online.
She received a chain letter telling her to forward it to ten friends or risk bad luck.
