inelastic supply

noun
economic term; supply not responsive to price changes
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.ɪˈlæst.ɪk səˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.ɪˈlæst.ɪk səˈplʌɪ / (BrE)
(in economics) a situation in which changes in the price of a good or service result in relatively small or no changes in the quantity supplied.

Examples

Inelastic supply in economics
Because agricultural products often take time to grow, many have inelastic supply in the short term.
Even if oil prices rise sharply, the inelastic supply means production cannot be quickly increased.
Some rare minerals have an inelastic supply due to limited extraction capabilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elastic supply - a situation in which the quantity supplied changes significantly in response to price changes
