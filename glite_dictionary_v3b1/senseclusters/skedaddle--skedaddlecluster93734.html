skedaddle

verb
run away hurriedly
Pronunciation: / skɪˈdæd.əl / (AmE), / skɪˈdæd.əl / (BrE)
to run away quickly, often because of fear or panic

Examples

To skedaddle away fast
As soon as the alarm sounded, everyone in the shop skedaddled out onto the street.
If you don't hurry up and skedaddle, you'll miss your train.
The children saw the angry dog and decided to skedaddle as fast as they could.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bolt - to move or run away suddenly, often out of fear
  • synonym: flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • antonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than necessary
