dyspnea

noun
difficult or labored breathing

difficult or labored breathing; shortness of breath, usually as a symptom of a medical condition.

Examples

Severe dyspnea attack
Asthma attacks are often characterized by severe dyspnea and wheezing.
People with heart failure may experience dyspnea even at rest.
The patient reported dyspnea after climbing a single flight of stairs.
Chronic heart failure can often present with symptoms like dyspnoea and fatigue.
The doctor noted the onset of dyspnoea during the physical examination.
The patient complained of severe dyspnoea following exertion.

Synonyms

  • shortness of breath
    4.0 1.15/m
    shortness of breath is the common phrase patients use to describe breathing difficulty, while dyspnea is the formal medical term.
  • breathlessness
    4.6 0.37/m
    breathlessness is a general, everyday word for struggling to breathe, while dyspnea is the clinical term used by doctors.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: apnea - temporary cessation of breathing
  • synonym: dyspnea - the American English spelling of 'dyspnoea'
  • degree higher: hyperpnea - increased depth and rate of breathing
  • synonym: shortness of breath - a common, less technical synonym for dyspnea
