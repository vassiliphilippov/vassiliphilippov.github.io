spermatic cord

noun
anatomical cord suspending the testis
9.7
Pronunciation: / spərˈmætɪk kɔrd / (AmE), / spəːˈmatɪk kɔːd / (BrE)
The cord-like structure that suspends the testis within the scrotum, containing the vas deferens, blood vessels, nerves, and lymphatics.

Examples

The spermatic cord anatomy
Each testis is suspended by its own spermatic cord, which contains important blood vessels and nerves.
Inflammation of the spermatic cord can cause severe pain and swelling in the scrotum.
The surgeon carefully examined the spermatic cord to ensure there was no damage after the procedure.
