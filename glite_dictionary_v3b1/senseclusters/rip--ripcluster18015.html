rip

noun
turbulent stretch of water due to crossing currents
8.4 0.36/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªp / (AmE), / rÉªp / (BrE)
a stretch of turbulent water in a river or the sea caused by opposing or crossing currents

Examples

A strong rip current
Fishermen warned us about the dangerous rip that forms at the river mouth.
Swimmers are advised to avoid entering the water near a visible rip.
The boat struggled to cross the rip as the currents clashed fiercely.

Synonyms

  • tide rip
    8.8 0.02/m
    a tide rip is a stretch of rough, turbulent water caused by meeting currents, while rip is a general name for that same phenomenon.
  • rip current
    6.0 0.08/m
    a rip current is a strong, narrow flow moving away from shore, while rip refers more broadly to turbulent crossing currents.
  • riptide
    8.2 0.31/m
    a riptide is a strong, dangerous current that can pull swimmers out to sea, while rip names turbulent opposing currents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rip current - a strong, narrow current of water flowing outward from the beach
