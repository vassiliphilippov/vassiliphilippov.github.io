incuriously

adverb
without curiosity
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a lack of interest or curiosity

Examples

Listen incuriously
She listened incuriously to the explanation, barely reacting to the news.
The students stared incuriously at the board, uninterested in the lesson.
He walked past the old building incuriously, not even glancing at its unique architecture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: curiously - in a way that shows eagerness to know or learn
  • synonym: indifferently - in a way that shows no interest or concern
