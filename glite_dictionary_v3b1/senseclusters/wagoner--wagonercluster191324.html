wagoner

noun
wagon driver; person operating a wagon
6.4 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwæɡənər / (AmE), / ˈwæɡənə /; / ˈwɒɡənə / (BrE)
a person who drives or manages a wagon, especially as an occupation

Examples

A wagoner drives a wagon
During the Civil War, a wagoner was responsible for transporting supplies to the front lines.
The wagoner guided his horses through the muddy lane with great skill.
The old wagoner told stories of his travels across the vast plains.
In the old days, a skilled waggoner was essential for transporting goods across the countryside.
The waggoner guided his team of horses steadily down the dusty road.
The waggoner stopped to rest and feed the horses at noon.
