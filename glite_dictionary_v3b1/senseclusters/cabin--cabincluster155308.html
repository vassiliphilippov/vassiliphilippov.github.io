cabin

noun
passenger area in aircraft or spacecraft
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkæb.ɪn /; / ˈkæbɪn / (AmE), / ˈkab.ɪn /; / ˈkabɪn / (BrE)
an enclosed space or room for people on a vehicle, such as an aircraft, ship, or boat

Examples

Ship cabin
Passengers were asked to return to their cabin and fasten their seat belts.
She walked down the aisle of the airplane, looking for her seat in the cabin.
The crew checked the pressure levels throughout the spacecraft cabin.
After a long day on deck, the sailors returned to their cabin to rest.
The stormy night made everyone grateful for the warmth of their cabin.
We booked a private cabin on the cruise ship for our vacation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: berth - a fixed bunk or bed on a ship, train, or other transport
  • antonym: cockpit - the area of an aircraft where the pilot sits and controls the plane
  • synonym: compartment - a separate section or part of a vehicle
  • degree higher: stateroom - a private room on a ship, usually more spacious and luxurious than a cabin
