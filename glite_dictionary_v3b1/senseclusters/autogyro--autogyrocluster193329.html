autogyro

noun
type of aircraft with unpowered rotating blades
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɔtoʊˌdʒaɪroʊ /; / ˌɔtoʊˈdʒaɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊˌdʒaɪərəʊ /; / ˌɔːtəʊˈdʒaɪrəʊ / (BrE)
an aircraft with unpowered rotating blades that provide lift, while forward thrust is supplied by a conventional engine-driven propeller

Examples

A autogyro aircraft
Several aviation enthusiasts flew their autogyros across the countryside in a daring expedition.
The inventor demonstrated his autogyro at the local airfield to an enthusiastic crowd.
Unlike helicopters, an autogyro's blades are not powered during flight.
He restored a vintage autogiro and took it to several airshows.
The autogiro was an important step in the development of rotary-wing aircraft.
Unlike a helicopter, an autogiro cannot hover in place.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gyrocopter - a similar type of aircraft with rotating blades for lift and a propeller for thrust
  • synonym: gyroplane - aircraft using lift from unpowered rotating blades
