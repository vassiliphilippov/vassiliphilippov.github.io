Clamatores

noun
historic bird taxon; passerines with simple vocal organs
9.9
Pronunciation: /klə.ˈmeɪ.tɔːrz/ /; / /klæ.ˈmeɪ.tɔːrz/ (AmE), /klə.ˈmeɪ.tɔːz/ /; / /klæ.ˈmeɪ.tɔːz/ (BrE)
a former suborder or superfamily of passerine birds characterized by simple vocal organs and limited singing ability, nearly coextensive with the suborder Tyranni in some classifications

Examples

Clamatores birds
Early taxonomists distinguished Clamatores from other songbirds by their simple vocal organs.
The classification of Clamatores is now rarely used in modern ornithology.
Unlike oscines, birds in the Clamatores group have limited singing ability.

  • synonym: Tyranni - suborder of passerine birds; suboscines
