potato disease

noun
disease affecting potato plants
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪɾoʊ dɪˈziz / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
Any disease or blight affecting potato plants, especially those caused by fungi, bacteria, or viruses.

Examples

Potato disease resistance
Scientists are working on new varieties resistant to potato disease.
Symptoms of potato disease include yellowing leaves and rotting tubers.
The farm lost most of its crop to a potato disease last summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blight - a plant disease typically causing rapid browning or death
