predetermined

adjective
decided or fixed in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɝː.mɪnd / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˈtɜː.mɪnd / (BrE)
decided, arranged, or established in advance; not left to chance or future decision

Examples

A predetermined plan
All participants arrived at the predetermined location as instructed.
At a predetermined signal, the team launched the experiment.
The schedule was predetermined and could not be changed at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prearranged - arranged or planned before it happens
  • antonym: spontaneous - not planned or predetermined; occurring without prearrangement
