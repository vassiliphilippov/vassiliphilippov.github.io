sanctimony

noun
hypocritical moral superiority or piety
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋk.təˌmoʊ.ni / (AmE), / ˈsaŋk.tɪ.mə.ni / (BrE)
the quality or attitude of pretending to be morally superior or pious, especially in a hypocritical way

Examples

Sanctimony in speech
He spoke with such sanctimony that it was hard to take him seriously.
Her public display of sanctimony masked her true intentions.
Many were put off by the sanctimony that permeated his speeches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypocrisy - the practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform
