orthodoxy

noun
traditional or established beliefs
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrθəˌdɑksi /; / ˈɔr.θəˌdɑk.si / (AmE), / ˈɔːθədɒksi /; / ˈɔː.θəˌdɒk.si / (BrE)
beliefs or practices that are traditional or generally accepted, especially in religion or other fields

Examples

Academic orthodoxy
Academic orthodoxy can sometimes stifle new ideas and approaches.
His views fall outside the political orthodoxy of his party.
The church continued to defend its orthodoxy despite growing criticism.
Her views gradually moved away from orthodoxy toward a more progressive stance.
Religious orthodoxy can sometimes discourage critical thinking and innovation.
The scientist challenged the prevailing orthodoxy with a radical new theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • antonym: heresy - belief or opinion that is opposed to orthodox doctrine
  • antonym: heterodoxy - Holding beliefs that differ from established or traditional doctrines
