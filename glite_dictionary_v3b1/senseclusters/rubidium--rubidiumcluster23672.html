rubidium

noun
soft, silvery alkali metal element
Pronunciation: / ruˈbɪdiəm / (AmE), / ruːˈbɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery metallic chemical element of the alkali metal group, symbol Rb, that burns in air and reacts violently with water

Examples

Rubidium metal
Rubidium is highly reactive and must be stored under oil to prevent contact with air or water.
Scientists discovered a new mineral containing traces of rubidium in the remote mine.
The unique properties of rubidium make it useful in atomic clocks and research applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: potassium - a soft, silvery-white alkali metal element
  • synonym: sodium - a soft, silver-white, highly reactive alkali metal element
