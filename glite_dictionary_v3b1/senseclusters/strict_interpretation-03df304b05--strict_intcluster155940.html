strict interpretation

noun
rigid, literal interpretation of texts or laws
1.0 5.6
Pronunciation: / strɪkt ˌɪntərprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / strɪkt ˌɪntəprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an interpretation of laws, rules, or texts that allows little to no flexibility and adheres closely to the literal meaning

Examples

A strict interpretation of rules
Her strict interpretation of the dress code left no room for exceptions.
Religious fundamentalists often rely on a strict interpretation of their sacred texts.
Some judges prefer a strict interpretation of the Constitution to avoid judicial activism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: literal interpretation - an interpretation based solely on the explicit meaning of the words
  • antonym: loose interpretation - an interpretation that allows flexibility and adaptation to context
