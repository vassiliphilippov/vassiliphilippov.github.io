incorporeal

adjective
without material form or substance
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.kɔːrˈpɔːr.i.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kɔːˈpɔː.ri.əl / (BrE)
not having a physical body or material form; immaterial or intangible.

Examples

Incorporeal beings
Many religions believe in incorporeal beings such as angels and spirits.
Philosophers have long debated whether the mind is a incorporeal entity.
The ghost was described as an incorporeal presence in the haunted house.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corporeal - having physical form; material, tangible
  • synonym: immaterial - not consisting of matter; intangible
