intercommunion

noun
joint participation in Holy Communion
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚkəˈmjunjən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkəˈmjuːnjən / (BrE)
the participation in Holy Communion by members of more than one church or denomination

Examples

Intercommunion among churches
Many theologians have debated the theological implications of intercommunion among various denominations.
The churches agreed to allow intercommunion between their members for Easter services.
The issue of intercommunion remains a significant topic in ecumenical discussions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communion - the Christian ceremony commemorating the Last Supper by consecrating bread and wine
