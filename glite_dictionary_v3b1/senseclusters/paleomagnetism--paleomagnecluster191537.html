paleomagnetism

noun
Earth's ancient magnetism
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˌoʊˈmæɡnəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˈmæɡnɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
the study of Earth's ancient magnetic field as recorded in rocks, sediments, or archaeological materials

Examples

Paleomagnetism study
Researchers analyze paleomagnetism to trace the historical movement of tectonic plates.
The discovery of reversed magnetic patterns in rocks provided key evidence for paleomagnetism.
By studying paleomagnetism, scientists can reconstruct the positions of continents millions of years ago.
Scientists use paleomagnetism to track the movement of continents over millions of years.
The paleomagnetism found in ancient lava flows helps researchers understand Earth's magnetic reversals.
By analyzing paleomagnetism, geologists can reconstruct the history of Earth's magnetic field.
Researchers use palaeomagnetism to trace the historical movement of continents.
The discovery of reversed magnetic patterns in rocks provided evidence for palaeomagnetism.
By analyzing palaeomagnetism, scientists can reconstruct the history of Earth's magnetic field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaeomagnetism - the study of past magnetic fields using archaeological materials
  • synonym: geology - the science of Earth's physical structure and history
  • synonym: geomagnetism - the study of Earth's present magnetic field
  • synonym: rock magnetism - the study of magnetic properties of rocks
