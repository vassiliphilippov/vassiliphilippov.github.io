nerve

noun
courage or boldness
3.9 9.17/m
Pronunciation: / n…ùÀêv / (AmE), / n…úÀêv / (BrE)
courage or boldness in difficulty or danger; the ability to continue despite fear or adversity.

Examples

Have the nerve to try
I wish I had the nerve to ask him out.
It took a lot of nerve to jump off the highest diving board.
She found the nerve to speak up for herself in the meeting.

Synonyms

  • mettle
    7.7 1.10/m
    mettle shows firm determination to keep going through hard times, while nerve means the courage to act despite fear.
  • nerves of steel
    5.8 0.15/m
    nerves of steel stay calm and steady under pressure, while nerve is general courage that can vary by situation.
  • spunk
    7.6 0.58/m
    spunk shows lively courage and spirit in hard times, while nerve is a broader, quieter readiness to face danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bravery - courageous behavior or character
  • synonym: courage - the ability to do something that frightens one
  • antonym: timidity - lack of courage or confidence
