lift

noun
British term for elevator
1.7 1.7
Pronunciation: /lɪft/ (AmE), /lɪft/ (BrE)
a device that carries people or goods vertically between floors in a building (elevator) or up a ski slope

Examples

A lift in a building
Please hold the lift for me while I get my bags.
The office building's lift is out of order, so you'll have to use the stairs.
They took the lift to the top floor to admire the view.
The chair lift was closed due to high winds.
The new lift takes skiers directly to the highest point of the resort.
We waited in line at the lift before starting our first run of the day.
