complementary color

noun
color that pairs to create white or grey
2.1
Pronunciation: / kəmˌplɛmənˈtɛri ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / kəmˌplɪmɛnˈtəri ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
either of two colors that, when combined, produce white light (in the case of light) or grey (in the case of pigments); pairs of colors directly opposite each other on the color wheel, such as red and green or blue and orange.

Examples

Complementary colors on wheel
Artists often use complementary colors to create vibrant contrasts in their paintings.
Red and green are complementary colors that, when mixed as lights, create white.
The color wheel shows which pairs make up complementary colors for mixing pigments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opposite color - color located directly opposite on the color wheel
