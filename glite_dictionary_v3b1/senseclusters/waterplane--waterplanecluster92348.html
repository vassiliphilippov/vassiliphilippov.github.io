waterplane

noun
reference water level
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌpleɪn / (BrE)
a horizontal plane representing the surface level of a body of water, used as a reference in surveying or design

Examples

Waterplane reference line
Engineers marked the waterplane to ensure the bridge would be above the highest expected water level.
The ship's stability was calculated based on its waterplane area at different drafts.
Surveyors used the waterplane as a reference when mapping the shoreline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseline - a starting point or line for measurements
  • synonym: datum - a reference point or level for measurements
