pantingly

adverb
breathing heavily
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpæntɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈpæntɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that shows quick, heavy breathing, or strong eagerness or excitement (literally or as if panting)

Examples

Arrive pantingly tired
After chasing the bus, she arrived pantingly at the stop, barely able to speak.
The dog waited pantingly by the door after running around the yard.
He finished the race pantingly, his chest rising and falling rapidly.
She waited pantingly for the phone to ring, hoping for news from the hospital.
The children ran pantingly to the ice cream truck on a hot summer day.
He listened pantingly for any sign that his name would be called next.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anxiously - in a way that shows worry or nervousness
  • synonym: breathlessly - in a way that shows difficulty in breathing
  • antonym: calmly - in a relaxed and untroubled manner
  • synonym: eagerly - in a way that shows strong desire or enthusiasm
