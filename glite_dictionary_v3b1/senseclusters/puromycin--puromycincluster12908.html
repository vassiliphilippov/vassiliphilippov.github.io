puromycin

noun
antibiotic for cell selection
Pronunciation: / ˌpjʊrəˈmaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌpjʊərəˈmaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used in scientific research, especially in cell culture, to kill certain cells or inhibit protein synthesis

Examples

Puromycin antibiotic
Researchers added puromycin to the culture to eliminate cells without the resistance gene.
Only genetically modified cells survived after treatment with puromycin.
The laboratory ordered more puromycin for their cell selection experiments.
Researchers added puromycin to the culture to selectively kill certain cells.
The laboratory ordered more puromycin for their protein synthesis experiments.
Using puromycin, scientists can study how cells respond to blocked protein production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chloramphenicol - an antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections and in research
