pseudoclassical

adjective
fake classical style
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈklæsɪkəl / (BrE)
imitating or resembling classical style, but not truly classical or authentic

Examples

Pseudoclassical building style
The museum's new wing has a pseudoclassical design that mimics ancient Greek temples.
Critics described the painting's pseudoclassical elements as lacking true historical accuracy.
Many modern buildings use pseudoclassical features to appear more traditional.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authentic - genuine; real; not false or copied
  • synonym: neoclassical - relating to a revival of classical style or spirit
