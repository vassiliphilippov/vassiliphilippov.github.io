tau cross

noun
T-shaped cross resembling Greek letter tau
Pronunciation: / taʊ krɔs / (AmE), / taʊ krɒs / (BrE)
A cross with a T-shaped form, resembling the Greek letter tau (Τ), used as a symbol in Christianity and art.

Examples

The tau cross symbol
In medieval art, the tau cross often symbolized St. Anthony.
The chapel's altar was decorated with a large wooden tau cross.
The monk wore a pendant shaped like a tau cross around his neck.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Greek cross - cross with all four arms of equal length
  • degree higher: Latin cross - cross with longer descending limb, traditional Christian cross shape
