grey

noun
Confederate soldier in US Civil War
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ɡreɪ / (BrE)
(historical) Used to refer to members or soldiers of the Confederate army in the American Civil War, who wore grey uniforms.

Examples

The grey army
In the heated debate, he was accused of siding with the grey cause.
The old veteran remained proud of his service as a grey during the war.
Visitors to the museum can see artifacts once belonging to the grey soldiers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blue - used to signify Union forces in the American Civil War (who wore blue uniforms)
