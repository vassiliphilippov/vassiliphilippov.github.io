stuck on

adjective
unable to move past something; fixated
5.1 0.90/m
Pronunciation: / stÊŒk É‘n / (AmE), / stÊŒk É’n / (BrE)
unable to move past or change something, often referring to being unable to stop thinking about something, or being unable to solve or let go of an issue.

Examples

To be stuck on details
He got stuck on the details and lost sight of the bigger picture.
I'm stuck on this problem in my assignment and need some help.
She has been stuck on that crossword puzzle all morning, unable to solve it.

Synonyms

  • get stuck on
    5.4 0.10/m

  • stuck
    3.5 11/m
    stuck usually means unable to proceed with a problem, while stuck on implies being unable to stop thinking or let go.
