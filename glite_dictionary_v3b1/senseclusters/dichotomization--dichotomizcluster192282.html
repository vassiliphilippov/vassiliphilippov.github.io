dichotomization

noun
division into two distinct groups
Pronunciation: / daɪˌkɑːtəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / daɪˌkɒtəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of dividing something into two distinct and often opposite groups or categories.

Examples

The dichotomization of groups
Her research criticizes the dichotomization of gender into only male and female.
Statistical analysis may be affected by the dichotomization of continuous variables into categories.
The dichotomization of social issues often ignores the nuances between opposing sides.
Medical research warns against the dichotomisation of continuous data, as it can oversimplify results.
The dichotomisation of opinions in the debate made compromise nearly impossible.
The social scientist criticised the dichotomisation of gender roles in the study.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dichotomy - a division into two parts or categories, often opposites
