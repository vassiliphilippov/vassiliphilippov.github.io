bioavailable

adjective
absorbable by organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊ.əˈveɪ.lə.bəl /; / ˌbaɪoʊəˈveɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊ.əˈveɪ.lə.bəl /; / ˌbaɪəʊəˈveɪləbəl / (BrE)
able to be absorbed and used by living organisms or the body, especially referring to nutrients, chemicals, or drugs

Examples

Bioavailable nutrients
The iron in spinach is not as bioavailable as the iron found in meat.
Scientists measured how much of the pollutant was bioavailable to aquatic plants and animals.
Taking vitamin C with iron can make the mineral more bioavailable to your body.
Iron in meat is more bioavailable than iron in plant sources.
Doctors recommend supplements that are highly bioavailable for better effectiveness.
The medication is not very bioavailable when taken on an empty stomach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorbable - able to be taken in or soaked up
  • antonym: toxic - harmful or poisonous to living organisms
  • antonym: unavailable - not able to be used or obtained
