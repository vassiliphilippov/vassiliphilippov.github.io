whuffle

verb
to breathe noisily
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈwʌfəl / (BrE)
to breathe noisily or snuffle softly, especially like an animal searching or sniffing

Examples

To whuffle in the grass
The horse began to whuffle softly as it searched for food in the grass.
You could hear the puppy whuffling under the table, hoping for a dropped snack.
As she entered the barn, the animals whuffled in greeting, their noses twitching.
