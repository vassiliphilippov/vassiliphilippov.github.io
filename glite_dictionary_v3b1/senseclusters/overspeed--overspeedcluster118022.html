overspeed

verb
to exceed safe speed
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈspiːd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspiːd / (BrE)
to operate or cause to operate at a speed higher than the safe or permitted limit, especially for a machine or vehicle

Examples

To overspeed an engine
If you overspeed the engine, it could suffer serious damage.
Pilots must avoid overspeeding the propeller during descent.
The technician warned not to overspeed the machinery to prevent overheating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overrev - to run an engine beyond its recommended speed
  • antonym: underdrive - to operate below the normal or recommended speed
