Shiite Muslim

noun
member of Shi'a branch of Islam
Pronunciation: / ˈʃiː.aɪt ˈmʌz.lɪm / (AmE), / ˈʃiː.aɪt ˈmʊz.lɪm / (BrE)
A member of the branch of Islam that regards Ali as the legitimate successor to Muhammad and rejects the first three caliphs.

Examples

Shiite Muslims community
After the revolution, Shiite Muslims gained significant influence in the country’s politics.
Many Shiite Muslims observe rituals commemorating the martyrdom of Imam Hussain.
The city has a large population of Shiite Muslims who celebrate important religious festivals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Sunni Muslim - a member of the largest branch of Islam, differing from Shiite Muslims in succession beliefs
