opacus

adjective
clouds obscuring sun
9.8
Pronunciation: / oʊˈpeɪkəs / (AmE), / əʊˈpeɪkəs / (BrE)
used in cloud classification to describe a cloud layer that is thick enough to obscure the sun or moon

Examples

Opacus cloud layer
The weather report mentioned stratocumulus opacus covering the sky, making the afternoon unusually dark.
When altostratus opacus forms, it can block sunlight and create a gloomy atmosphere.
Meteorologists observed a layer of nimbostratus opacus that completely hid the moon during the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: translucidus - clouds thin enough to let sunlight or moonlight through
