pachyderm

noun
large, thick-skinned mammal
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpækɪˌdɝm / (AmE), / ˈpækɪˌdɜːm / (BrE)
a large, thick-skinned mammal such as an elephant, rhinoceros, or hippopotamus

Examples

Large pachyderm animal
Ancient explorers were awestruck by the size and strength of African pachyderms.
The elephant is perhaps the best-known pachyderm in the world.
Unlike other mammals, a pachyderm has exceptionally thick skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elephant - a very large, herbivorous mammal with a trunk
  • synonym: hippopotamus - a large, thick-skinned herbivorous mammal living in or near water
  • synonym: rhinoceros - a large, thick-skinned herbivorous mammal with one or two horns on the nose
