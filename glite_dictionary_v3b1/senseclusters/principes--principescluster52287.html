principes

noun
Roman heavy infantry
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪnsɪˌpiːz / (AmE), / ˈprɪnsɪˌpeɪz / (BrE)
a class of heavy infantry soldiers in the Roman army during the Republic, forming the second line in battle formations

Examples

Roman principes soldiers
The principes were considered experienced soldiers and played a crucial role in Roman battles.
After the hastati engaged the enemy, the principes advanced to continue the fight.
Historians study how the principes contributed to the effectiveness of the Roman legion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hastati - the youngest class of Roman infantry, forming the first line
  • synonym: triarii - the veteran soldiers forming the third line in the Roman army
