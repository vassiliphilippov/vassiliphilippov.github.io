placoderm

noun
prehistoric armored jawed fish
Pronunciation: / ˈplæk.əˌdɜrm / (AmE), / ˈplak.əˌdɜːm / (BrE)
an extinct group of prehistoric, jawed fish characterized by bony plates covering the head and upper body, dominant in the Devonian period

Examples

Fossil placoderm
Fossils of placoderms provide crucial insight into the evolution of fish.
Many placoderm species thrived before going extinct by the end of the Devonian period.
The placoderm was among the earliest vertebrates to develop jaws.
