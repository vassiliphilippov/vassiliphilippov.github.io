cross-court

noun
diagonal court shot
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔs kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒs kɔːt / (BrE)
a shot in racket or court sports that is played diagonally from one side of the court to the other

Examples

A cross-court winner
She hit a powerful cross-court to win the point in the tennis match.
The coach praised his player's accurate cross-court during the training session.
A well-placed cross-court can catch your opponent off guard in badminton.

Related Terms

  • antonym: down-the-line - a shot hit straight along the sideline in court sports
