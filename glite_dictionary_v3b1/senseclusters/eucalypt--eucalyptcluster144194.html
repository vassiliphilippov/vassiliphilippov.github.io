eucalypt

noun
tree of the genus Eucalyptus
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːkəˌlɪpt / (AmE), / ˈjuːkəlɪpt / (BrE)
A tree belonging to the genus Eucalyptus, native to Australia and known for aromatic leaves and distinctive bark.

Examples

A eucalypt tree
A single eucalypt can release enough oil to scent an entire hillside.
Koalas spend most of their time eating the leaves of eucalypt trees.
The forest was dominated by towering eucalypts that filled the air with a fresh scent.
