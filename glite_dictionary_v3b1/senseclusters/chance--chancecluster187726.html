chance

noun
an unpredictable influence that affects outcomes
2.1 30/m
Pronunciation: / t É√¶ns / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêns / (BrE)
the occurrence of events in the absence of any obvious intention, cause, or predictable pattern; an unpredictable and unknown influence that brings about an event or outcome

Examples

Met by chance
She met her old friend purely by chance at the airport.
There was a chance that the meeting would be canceled due to the storm.
Winning the lottery depends largely on chance, not skill.

  • accident
    1.0 15/m
    accident is an unexpected event that happens without apparent cause, while chance refers to events caused by no clear intention.
  • fortuity
    9.1 0.04/m
    fortuity is a formal word for an event that happens unexpectedly, while chance is the everyday idea of an unpredictable occurrence.
  • happenstance
    8.5 1.02/m
    happenstance is a casual term for an accidental event that seems planned, while chance is a neutral idea of events without clear cause.
  • luck
    1.0 76/m
    luck is a force that brings good or bad results to people, while chance is the neutral fact of events without known cause.
  • fortune
    3.1 13/m
    fortune is the power or outcome people see as unexpectedly good or bad, while chance simply means events happening without predictable cause.
