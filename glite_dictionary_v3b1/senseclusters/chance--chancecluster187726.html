chance

noun
an unpredictable influence that affects outcomes
5.0 1.9
Pronunciation: /tʃæns/ (AmE), /tʃɑːns/ /; / /tʃeəns/ (BrE)
the occurrence of events in the absence of any obvious intention, cause, or predictable pattern; an unpredictable and unknown influence that brings about an event or outcome

Examples

Met by chance
She met her old friend purely by chance at the airport.
There was a chance that the meeting would be canceled due to the storm.
Winning the lottery depends largely on chance, not skill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - something that is known for sure; opposite of chance or unpredictability
  • synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance rather than one's own actions
