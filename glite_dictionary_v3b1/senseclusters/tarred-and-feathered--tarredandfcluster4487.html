tarred-and-feathered

adjective
covered with tar and feathers as punishment
8.2
Pronunciation: / tɑrd ənd ˈfɛðɚd / (AmE), / tɑːd ənd ˈfeðəd / (BrE)
smeared with tar and covered with feathers as a form of public punishment or humiliation, especially in the past.

Examples

Historical records describe several protesters being tarred-and-feathered for their defiance.
In the movie, a suspected thief is tarred-and-feathered by angry townspeople.
The mob left the tarred-and-feathered man in the town square as a warning to others.
