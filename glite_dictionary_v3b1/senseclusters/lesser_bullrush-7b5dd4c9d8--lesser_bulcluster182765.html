lesser bullrush

noun
a wetland plant; a reed mace species
9.9 7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛsər ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɛsə ˈbʊlˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
a species of reed mace found in America, Europe, North Africa, and Asia, typically growing in wetlands; characterized by long, narrow leaves and brown cylindrical flower spikes

The lesser bullrush thrives in shallow, slow-moving water near the edge of lakes.
Wetlands restoration projects often include planting native species such as the lesser bullrush.
