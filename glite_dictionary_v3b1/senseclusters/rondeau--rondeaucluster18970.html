rondeau

noun
musical form with recurring refrain
9.2
Pronunciation: / rɒnˈdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈrɒndəʊ / (BrE)
a musical form consisting of a recurring refrain, often used as the last movement of a sonata or other classical piece

Examples

Classical rondeau movement
Many Baroque composers favored the rondeau structure in their keyboard works.
The final movement of the sonata was a lively rondeau that delighted the audience.
The pianist skillfully interpreted the playful character of the rondeau.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rondo - a similar musical form with a recurring principal theme
