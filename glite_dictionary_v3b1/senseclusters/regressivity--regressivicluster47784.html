regressivity

noun
tax burden on poor
Pronunciation: / rɪˌɡrɛˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / rɪˌɡrɛˈsɪvɪti /; / rɪˌɡrɛˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality of a tax, fee, or policy that takes a larger percentage from low-income people or causes a return to a less fair or less advanced condition

Examples

Tax regressivity
Economists debated the regressivity of the proposed sales tax during the policy meeting.
The regressivity of certain public fees can increase inequality among different income groups.
Many argue that the regressivity of flat taxes makes them unfair to low-income families.
Many economists debate the regressivity of certain sales taxes and their impact on low-income families.
The policy's regressivity became a major concern during the election campaign.
Critics argue that the regressivity of the new law will widen the gap between rich and poor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressivity - the quality of a tax or fee that takes a larger percentage from high-income people
  • synonym: regression - a return to a previous or less developed state
  • synonym: regressive - describing a tax or fee that is more burdensome for low-income people
