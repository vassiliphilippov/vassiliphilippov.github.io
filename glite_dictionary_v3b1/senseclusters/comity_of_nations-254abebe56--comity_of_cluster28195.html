comity of nations

noun
mutual respect among nations' laws and customs
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmɪti əv ˈneɪʃənz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɪti əv ˈneɪʃənz / (BrE)
the principle by which nations show mutual respect for each other's laws and institutions, often facilitating cooperation and peaceful relations

Examples

Comity of nations principle
Courts sometimes apply the comity of nations to recognize foreign judgments.
Diplomats rely on the comity of nations to resolve sensitive cross-border disputes.
The principle of comity of nations underpins many international treaties and agreements.
