chinook salmon

noun
large Pacific salmon species
Pronunciation: / tʃɪˈnʊk ˈsæmən / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊk ˈsæmən / (BrE)
a large species of Pacific salmon valued as food, with adults dying after spawning

Examples

Wild chinook salmon
Grilled chinook salmon is considered a delicacy in many Pacific Northwest restaurants.
Thanks to conservation efforts, the chinook salmon population is beginning to recover in the river.
The spawning chinook salmon returned upstream to lay their eggs before dying.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: coho salmon - a smaller species of Pacific salmon
  • synonym: king salmon - another common name for the chinook salmon
  • degree lower: sockeye salmon - a different species of Pacific salmon
