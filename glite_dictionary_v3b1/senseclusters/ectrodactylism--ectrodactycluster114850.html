ectrodactylism

noun
missing or fused digits
Pronunciation: / ˌɛktroʊˈdæktɪlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɛktrəʊˈdæktɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
a congenital condition in which fingers or toes are missing or fused together

Examples

ectrodactylism condition
The genetic counselor discussed the risks of ectrodactylism with the family.
Children born with ectrodactylism may require specialized medical care.
Researchers are studying the causes of ectrodactylism in different populations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polydactyly - a condition of having extra fingers or toes
  • synonym: syndactyly - a condition where two or more fingers or toes are fused together
