psychodynamic

adjective
unconscious mental forces
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the influence of unconscious mental and emotional forces on behavior and personality

Examples

The psychodynamic approach
The psychodynamic approach focuses on how childhood experiences shape adult behavior.
Many therapists use psychodynamic theories to understand their clients' emotional struggles.
A psychodynamic perspective can reveal hidden motivations behind a person's actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behavioral - relating to observable actions rather than unconscious processes
  • synonym: psychoanalytic - relating to the analysis of the unconscious mind
