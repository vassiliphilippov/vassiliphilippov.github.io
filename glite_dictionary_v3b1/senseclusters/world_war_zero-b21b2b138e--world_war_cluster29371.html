World War Zero

noun
theoretical conflict representing new warfare
8.8
Pronunciation: / wɝːld wɔːr ˈzɪəɹoʊ / (AmE), / wɜːld wɔː ˈzɪərəʊ / (BrE)
a hypothetical or metaphorical conflict representing a new kind of large-scale warfare, often used to describe modern nontraditional conflicts.

Examples

World War Zero conflict
Debates about World War Zero highlight the changing nature of global threats.
Some analysts argue that climate change could trigger a World War Zero involving many nations.
The idea of World War Zero is used to describe the complexities of cyber conflict and hybrid warfare today.
