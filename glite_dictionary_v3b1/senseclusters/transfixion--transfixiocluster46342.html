transfixion

noun
being held motionless
9.3
Pronunciation: /træns.ˈfɪk.ʃən/ (AmE), /træns.ˈfɪk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of piercing something through, or the state of being held motionless by awe, fear, or amazement

Examples

State of transfixion
The snake's gaze held the mouse in a state of transfixion.
She watched the fireworks in complete transfixion, unable to look away.
The surgeon performed a transfixion of the artery to stop the bleeding.
