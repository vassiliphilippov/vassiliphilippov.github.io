ocean floor

noun
bottom of the sea or ocean
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊʃən flɔr / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən flɔː / (BrE)
the bottom surface of the sea or ocean, consisting of various geological features such as plains, ridges, and trenches

Examples

The ocean floor surface
Scientists use submarines to explore the ocean floor and study its unique ecosystems.
Sunken ships often rest undisturbed for centuries on the ocean floor.
Thick layers of sediment have built up on the ocean floor over millions of years.
