irritably

adverb
in an annoyed or impatient way
Pronunciation: / ˈɪr.ə.t̬ə.bli /; / ˈɪr.ɪ.təb.li / (AmE), / ˈɪr.ɪ.tə.bli /; / ˈɪr.ɪ.təb.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows annoyance or impatience

Examples

To answer irritably
He answered irritably after being asked the same question three times.
She irritably slammed the door when she didn't get her way.
The teacher irritably reminded the class to stop talking during the lesson.
He replied irritably when asked the same question for the third time.
She tapped her pen irritably on the table during the long meeting.
The baby cried irritably until she was comforted.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: angrily - in an angry manner
  • antonym: calmly - in a calm or composed manner
  • antonym: patiently - in a patient manner, showing tolerance
  • synonym: testily - in an irritable or impatient manner
