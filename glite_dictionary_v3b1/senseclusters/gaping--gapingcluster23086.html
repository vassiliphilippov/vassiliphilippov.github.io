gaping

adjective
wide open
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
very wide open, often referring to a hole, mouth, or space

Examples

Gaping hole
A gaping hole in the roof let the rain pour into the house.
The child stared in amazement, his mouth gaping at the magician's trick.
After the accident, there was a gaping gap in the fence that needed repair.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closed - shut or not open
  • degree lower: narrow - not wide; having a small width
  • synonym: open - not closed or covered
