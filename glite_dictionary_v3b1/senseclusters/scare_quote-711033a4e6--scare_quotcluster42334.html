scare quote

verb
put in quotation marks to show doubt
8.2
Pronunciation: /sker kwoʊt/ /; / /sker kwəʊt/ /; / /skeər kwoʊt/ (AmE), /skeə kwəʊt/ /; / /skeə kwoʊt/ (BrE)
(informal) to enclose a word or phrase in quotation marks to show that one doubts, dislikes, or distances oneself from the term; to signal irony or skepticism.

Examples

To scare quote a word
Many newspaper columnists scare quote words like 'expert' to show clear scepticism about claims.
She scare-quoted the minister's remark in her article, signalling doubt to readers.
Editors warned reporters not to scare quote sources unnecessarily, which can undermine credibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: put in quotation marks - to enclose words or phrases within quotation marks
  • related: quote - to repeat or copy words from someone else, often using quotation marks
