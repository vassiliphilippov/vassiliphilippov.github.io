unstick

verb
to resume progress
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɪk / (BrE)
to begin to move or make progress again after being stuck or unable to act, often in a figurative sense.

Examples

Unstick a process
After several failed attempts, the negotiations finally unstuck and both sides reached an agreement.
The project seemed stalled for weeks, but a new idea helped everything unstick quickly.
Sometimes, taking a short break can help your mind unstick and find a solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: free - to release from being stuck or held
  • antonym: stall - to stop making progress or become stuck
  • synonym: unstick - to become detached or able to move again
