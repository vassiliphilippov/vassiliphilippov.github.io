quotation mark

noun
punctuation mark indicating direct speech or citation
2.2 1.0
Pronunciation: /kwoʊ.ˈteɪ.ʃən mɑːrk/ (AmE), /kwəʊ.ˈteɪ.ʃən mɑːk/ (BrE)
either of a pair of punctuation marks (“ ” or ' ') used to show that the words between them are a direct quotation, the title of a short work, or speech; also used to highlight words or phrases.

Examples

A quotation mark pair
He accidentally left out the closing quotation mark at the end of the sentence.
The teacher explained how to use quotation marks for dialogue in stories.
When quoting someone in an essay, always put their words inside quotation marks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inverted comma - another term for quotation mark, primarily in British English
