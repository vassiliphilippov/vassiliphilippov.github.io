aneroid barometer

noun
barometer measuring pressure without fluids
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˌrɔɪd bəˈrɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌænəˌrɔɪd bəˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a type of barometer that measures atmospheric pressure without using any liquid, typically employing a small, flexible metal box

Examples

An aneroid barometer device
He checked the aneroid barometer before setting out on his hike.
The ship was equipped with an aneroid barometer to monitor weather changes at sea.
Unlike mercury barometers, an aneroid barometer uses no liquid to measure air pressure.
