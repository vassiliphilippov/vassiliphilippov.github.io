Augeas

proper noun
Greek king with unclean stables in mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.dʒiː.əs/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.dʒiː.əs/ (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a king whose stables, filled with an enormous herd of cattle, remained uncleaned for thirty years.

Examples

Augeas stables
Hercules was tasked with cleaning the Augeas stables as one of his twelve labours.
References to Augeas are common in discussions of seemingly impossible cleaning tasks.
The myth of Augeas inspired the phrase 'Augean stables' to describe an overwhelming mess.
