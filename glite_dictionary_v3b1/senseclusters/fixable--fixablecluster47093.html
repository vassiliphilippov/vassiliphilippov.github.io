fixable

adjective
able to be rigged
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪksəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪksəbl / (BrE)
able to be dishonestly arranged or manipulated to produce a desired outcome, especially in a competition or election.

Examples

Fixable match
The match was considered fixable because the referees could be easily bribed.
Some feared the lottery was fixable due to a lack of oversight.
Investigators looked into whether the bidding process was fixable by outside parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruptible - able to be influenced by bribery or dishonesty
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit or fraud
