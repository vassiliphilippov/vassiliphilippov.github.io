rehouse

verb
to move to new storage
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈhaʊz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhaʊz / (BrE)
to move an object, collection, or equipment into new protective storage or casing to preserve or protect it.

Examples

To rehouse old documents
The archivists are rehousing fragile documents in acid-free folders to ensure their preservation.
After the renovation, the museum staff rehoused the ancient artifacts in climate-controlled cases.
Properly rehousing photographs can prevent damage from light and humidity over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relocate - to move something to a new place
  • synonym: store - to put something away for future use or safekeeping
