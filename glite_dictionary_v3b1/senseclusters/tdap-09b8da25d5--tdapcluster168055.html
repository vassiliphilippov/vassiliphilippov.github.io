Tdap

noun
vaccine for three diseases
Pronunciation: / ti dæp / (AmE), / tiː dæp / (BrE)
a vaccine that protects against tetanus, diphtheria, and pertussis (whooping cough)

Examples

Tdap vaccine
Many schools require students to have a Tdap vaccination before enrolling.
Adults are advised to get a Tdap booster every ten years to maintain protection.
The doctor recommended a Tdap shot for anyone in close contact with newborns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: DTaP - a vaccine for diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis given to children
  • synonym: MMR - a vaccine that protects against measles, mumps, and rubella
  • synonym: tetanus vaccine - a vaccine that protects against tetanus
