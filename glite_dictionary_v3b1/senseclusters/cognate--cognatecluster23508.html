cognate

adjective
related by blood
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːɡˌneɪt /; / ˈkɑːɡ.neɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.neɪt / (BrE)
(of people, words, languages, or things) related by having the same origin or source

Examples

Cognate languages
In cases of succession, cognate kin can sometimes inherit when no direct heirs exist.
The document proved that the two rulers were cognate, sharing a common royal ancestor.
You may be entitled to the inheritance if you are a cognate relative of the deceased.
Linguists often study words that are cognate to find historical connections between languages.
The two cultures have cognate traditions that trace back to the same ancient civilization.
Their beliefs were cognate, stemming from a shared set of values and origins.
French and Spanish are considered cognate languages descended from Latin.
Scholars found that many words in Sanskrit and Greek are cognate, pointing to a shared origin.
The English word 'brother' and the German 'Bruder' are cognate terms.
