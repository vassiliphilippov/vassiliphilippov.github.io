Synonyms

  • dried-out
    2.8 0.49/m
    dried-out is very dry after losing moisture, while desiccated is a more formal word for the same complete drying.
  • dehydrated
    3.6 0.18/m
    dehydrated is often used for food preserved by removing moisture, while desiccated sounds more scientific and complete.
  • dried
    1.0 0.06/m
    dried is a general term for food with moisture removed, while desiccated implies a more complete, often technical drying.
  • bone-dry
    3.4 0.04/m
    bone-dry means completely dry in an informal way, while desiccated is a more formal, precise term for being dried out.
  • dried-up
    3.5 0.95/m
    dried-up has a sense of losing freshness or running out, while desiccated focuses on complete removal of moisture.
  • parched
    4.7 1.15/m
    parched describes being dried by heat or sun, while desiccated means thoroughly dry without naming the specific cause.
  • air-dried
    4.4 0.05/m
    air-dried is dried slowly by cool air without heat, while desiccated usually means fully dried, often by other methods.
