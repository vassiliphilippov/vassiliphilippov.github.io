desiccated

adjective
completely dried out
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛsɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
thoroughly dried out; having had all moisture removed (often used for food, plants, or substances)

Examples

Desiccated coconut
His lips were desiccated after walking for hours in the hot sun.
The desiccated coconut is often used in baking for its texture and flavor.
The museum displayed several desiccated plant specimens collected from the desert.
During the drought, many plants became desiccated and struggled to survive.
She bought a packet of desiccated fruit for her hiking trip.
The recipe calls for desiccated coconut, which gives the cake a unique texture.

  • synonym: dry - lacking moisture; not wet
