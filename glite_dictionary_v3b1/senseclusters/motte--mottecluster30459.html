motte

noun
small tree clump
Pronunciation: / mɒt / (AmE), / mɒt / (BrE)
a small group or clump of trees or bushes, especially one standing alone in open land

Examples

A motte of trees
The cattle rested in the shade of a motte in the middle of the field.
A lone motte provided shelter for birds on the otherwise open plain.
We set up our picnic under the trees of a motte to escape the sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grove - a small group of trees, often without underbrush
  • synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or small trees
