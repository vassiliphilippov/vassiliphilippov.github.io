microfracture

noun
cartilage repair surgery
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌfræktʃər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌfræktʃə / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to repair damaged joint cartilage by creating small holes in the underlying bone to stimulate new tissue growth

Examples

Microfracture surgery
After injuring his knee, the athlete underwent a microfracture to help restore the damaged cartilage.
The doctor explained that a microfracture could improve joint function and reduce pain.
Recovery from a microfracture procedure often requires several months of physical therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arthroscopy - a minimally invasive surgical procedure on a joint
  • synonym: cartilage repair - medical procedures to restore damaged cartilage
