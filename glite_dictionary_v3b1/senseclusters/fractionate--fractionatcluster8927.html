fractionate

verb
separate into components or fractions
3.8 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfræk.ʃə.neɪt/ (AmE), /ˈfræk.ʃə.neɪt/ (BrE)
to separate a mixture into its individual parts or components, especially by a scientific or industrial process

Examples

To fractionate a mixture
Chemical engineers fractionate crude oil to obtain useful fuels like gasoline and diesel.
Scientists can fractionate air by cooling it and separating the gases based on their boiling points.
To analyze the blood sample, the laboratory technician will fractionate it into its different components.
Industrial plants routinely fractionate crude oil to produce gasoline, diesel, and other valuable chemicals.
Scientists often fractionate blood to isolate plasma for medical use.
To obtain highly pure components, the chemist decided to fractionate the solution using column chromatography.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distill - to purify a liquid by vaporizing and then condensing it
  • synonym: separate - to set apart or divide into different parts
