ring

verb
to resound or echo with sound
2.2 1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
to make or produce a ringing sound, or to echo loudly

Examples

Laughter rings out
Her laughter rang through the empty corridors, making everyone smile.
The chamber rang with applause as the speaker concluded his address.
The stadium rang with the cheers of the fans after the final goal.
Sarah learned to ring the church bells as part of the traditional bell-ringing team.
The volunteers ring the bells every Sunday morning before the service.
They will ring the cathedral bells to celebrate the festival.
Church bells ring out every Sunday morning across the town.
The alarm rang loudly, waking everyone in the building.
The children listened as the school bell rang to signal the end of class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chime - to make a set of bells sound musically
  • synonym: clang - to make a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • synonym: echo - to repeat or reflect a sound
  • degree lower: murmur - to make a low, soft sound (degree lower in loudness)
  • synonym: resound - to fill a place with sound; to echo
  • synonym: toll - to ring a bell slowly, especially for death or mourning
