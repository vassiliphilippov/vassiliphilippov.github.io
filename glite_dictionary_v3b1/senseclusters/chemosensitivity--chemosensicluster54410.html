chemosensitivity

noun
response to chemicals
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˌsɛnsəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the ability of cells or receptors to respond to chemical stimuli such as odors, tastes, or changes in blood gases

Examples

Nerve cell chemosensitivity
Researchers studied the chemosensitivity of nerve cells to understand how the body detects changes in blood oxygen.
A decrease in chemosensitivity can affect how a person senses taste and smell.
Doctors monitor chemosensitivity in patients with respiratory disorders to assess their response to carbon dioxide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemoreception - the process by which organisms detect chemical stimuli
  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of response to stimuli
