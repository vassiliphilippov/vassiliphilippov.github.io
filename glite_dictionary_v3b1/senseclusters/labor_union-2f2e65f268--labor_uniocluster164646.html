labor union

noun
organization of workers for bargaining
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbɚ ˈjun.jən / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bə ˈjuː.njən / (BrE)
An organization of workers formed to protect their rights and interests by negotiating with employers.

Examples

A strong labor union
Many industries rely on a strong labor union to advocate for employee rights.
Negotiations between the labor union and management resulted in a new contract.
The workers voted to join the labor union to improve their working conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trade union - an organization of workers formed to protect and further their rights and interests; used mainly in British English
