labor union

noun
organization of workers for bargaining
2.7 3.79/m
Pronunciation: / Ààle…™b…ö Ààjun.j…ôn / (AmE), / Ààle…™.b…ô ÀàjuÀê.nj…ôn / (BrE)
An organization of workers formed to protect their rights and interests by negotiating with employers.

Examples

A strong labor union
Many industries rely on a strong labor union to advocate for employee rights.
Negotiations between the labor union and management resulted in a new contract.
The workers voted to join the labor union to improve their working conditions.

Synonyms

  • union
    2.3 71/m
    union is the general name for an organized group of workers that bargains collectively, while labor union is the full phrase for the same body.
  • trade union
    2.8 3.27/m
    trade union is a term for worker groups that negotiate specific employment conditions, while labor union is a broader name for worker organizations.
  • trades union
    2.7 0.01/m
    trades union is a phrase variant that also describes collective bargaining groups, while labor union is the standard more common term.
  • brotherhood
    3.5 15/m
    brotherhood often feels like a fraternal group of workers with close ties, while labor union focuses on negotiation and formal collective rights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trade union - an organization of workers formed to protect and further their rights and interests; used mainly in British English
