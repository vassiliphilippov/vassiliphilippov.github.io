true seal

noun
seal lacking ear flaps (Phocidae)
6.1 5.3
Pronunciation: /truː siːl/ (AmE), /truː siːl/ (BrE)
A type of seal lacking external ear flaps, with a hairy coat and hind limbs adapted as swimming flippers; belonging to the family Phocidae.

Examples

A true seal species
Scientists study the swimming techniques of the true seal to understand their aquatic adaptations.
The harbor true seal is commonly found along northern coastlines.
Unlike the sea lion, the true seal does not have external ears.
