suck in one's breath

verb
to inhale sharply or deeply
1.5 5.0
Pronunciation: /sʌk ɪn wʌnz brɛθ/ /; / /sʌk ən wʌnz brɛθ/ /; / /sʌk ɪn wʌns brɛθ/ (AmE), /sʌk ɪn wʌnz brɛθ/ /; / /sʌk ən wʌnz brɛθ/ /; / /sʌk ɪn wʌns brɛθ/ (BrE)
to inhale sharply, often suddenly, typically in response to surprise, fear, or preparation for exertion

Examples

To suck in my breath
Before lifting the heavy box, I sucked in my breath and prepared myself.
She sucked in her breath as the cold water touched her feet.
When he heard the news, he sucked in his breath in astonishment.
