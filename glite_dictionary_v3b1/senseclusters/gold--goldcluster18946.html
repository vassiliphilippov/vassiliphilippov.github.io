gold

noun
something precious or of great value (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊld / (AmE), / ɡəʊld / (BrE)
something regarded as precious, valuable, or superior, especially for its excellence, goodness, or noble quality.

Examples

Gold standard
Her kindness and honesty are worth their weight in gold.
The volunteers who helped after the storm had hearts of gold.
To his parents, he is as good as gold.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: diamond - something considered extremely precious or valuable (sometimes figuratively considered even greater than gold)
  • degree lower: silver - something considered valuable but less so than gold (in figurative expressions)
