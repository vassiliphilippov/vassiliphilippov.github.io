preconditioning

noun
advance preparation
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of preparing something in advance to improve its performance or outcome

Examples

Athlete preconditioning
Athletes use preconditioning to reduce the risk of injury before intense training.
Proper preconditioning of the equipment ensures better results during the experiment.
The doctor recommended preconditioning exercises to help the patient recover more quickly after surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conditioning - the process of training or accustoming someone or something to behave in a certain way
  • synonym: preparation - the act of getting ready for something
