fettered

adjective
bound by chains or shackles
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛtərd / (AmE), / ˈfɛtəd / (BrE)
bound by chains or shackles, especially around the ankles, to restrict movement

Examples

Fettered by chains
She gazed sadly at the fettered captives awaiting their fate.
The prisoner was fettered to prevent his escape from the dungeon.
Years of tradition have fettered progress in this ancient institution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unfettered - not bound or restrained; free
