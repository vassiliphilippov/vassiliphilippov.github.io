licitly

adverb
in a manner permitted by law or custom
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪsɪtli / (AmE), / ˈlɪsɪtli / (BrE)
In a manner permitted by law or by accepted standards; legitimately or lawfully.

Examples

To act licitly
All contributions to the fund must be acquired licitly and transparently.
The contract was drawn up licitly, following all the necessary legal procedures.
They acted licitly in accordance with the organization's ethical guidelines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illicitly - in a manner not permitted by law or custom
  • synonym: legitimately - in a way that conforms to the law or rules
