valorize

verb
to give value
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈvæləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈvæləˌraɪz /; / ˈvæləraɪz / (BrE)
to give value or importance to something, especially by officially recognizing or promoting it

Examples

To valorize local culture
The government hopes to valorize local agriculture by supporting small farmers.
Efforts to valorize cultural heritage can boost tourism and community pride.
The new curriculum seeks to valorize students' diverse backgrounds and experiences.
The museum's new exhibit seeks to valorize the contributions of local artists.
Many organizations are working to valorize traditional crafts in rural communities.
The documentary helped valorize the efforts of volunteers during the crisis.
The government hopes to valorize traditional crafts by including them in school programs.
New laws were introduced to valorize the work of healthcare professionals.
The museum's exhibition seeks to valorize the achievements of women in science.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appreciate - to recognize the value or significance of something
  • antonym: devalue - to reduce or underestimate the worth of something
  • synonym: validate - to confirm or recognize the value or truth of something
