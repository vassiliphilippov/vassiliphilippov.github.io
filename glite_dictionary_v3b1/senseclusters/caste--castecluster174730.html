caste

noun
social insect subgroup by function or form
Pronunciation: / kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
In social insects such as ants or bees, a physically distinct group of individuals specialized to perform certain functions within the colony.

Examples

Ant caste roles
In an ant colony, the worker caste is responsible for foraging and caring for the young.
Some bees belong to the queen caste, which is solely dedicated to reproduction.
The soldier caste in termites has powerful jaws for defending the colony against predators.
