subsidized

adjective
supported by public funds
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.sɪˌdaɪzd /; / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪzd / (BrE)
Partly paid for by public funds to reduce the cost for users or recipients.

Examples

A subsidized apartment
He moved into a subsidized apartment after losing his job.
Many families rely on subsidized child care to balance work and home responsibilities.
The museum offers subsidized tickets for students and senior citizens.
Many families rely on subsidised housing to afford a place to live.
She attended a subsidised preschool program designed to help low-income families.
The city offers subsidised public transport for students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subsidized - American spelling of 'subsidised'
  • antonym: unsubsidized - not receiving financial support from public funds
