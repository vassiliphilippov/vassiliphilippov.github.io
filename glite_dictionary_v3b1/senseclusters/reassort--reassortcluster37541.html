reassort

verb
to mix genetic material
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈsɔrt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsɔːt / (BrE)
to mix or rearrange genetic material, especially in viruses, resulting in new combinations

Examples

Viruses reassort genes
Influenza viruses can reassort their genetic material to produce new strains.
Scientists study how viruses reassort to better understand the emergence of pandemics.
When two different viruses infect the same cell, they may reassort their genes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutate - to change or alter genetic material
  • synonym: recombine - to combine genetic material in a new way
