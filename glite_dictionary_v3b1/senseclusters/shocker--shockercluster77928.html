shocker

noun
sensational or surprising message in media
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑːkər / (AmE), / ˈʃɒkə / (BrE)
an event, situation, or piece of news that is extremely surprising or sensational

Examples

A news shocker
She delivered a shocker during the interview that changed our perception of the case.
That twist in the story was a real shocker that no one saw coming.
The film's final shocker left the entire audience speechless.
Her decision to move abroad came as a total shocker to her family.
It was a shocker when the underdog team won the championship game.
The announcement of his resignation was a real shocker to everyone in the office.
