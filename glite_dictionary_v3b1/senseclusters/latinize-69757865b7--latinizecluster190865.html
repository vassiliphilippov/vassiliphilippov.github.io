Latinize

verb
write in the Latin alphabet
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt.əˌnaɪz /; / ˈlæt.ɪ.naɪz / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ɪˌnaɪz /; / ˈlæt.ɪ.naɪz / (BrE)
to write or represent (a word, text, or language) using the Latin alphabet instead of another script

Examples

To Latinize a name
Historians often Latinize names from ancient Greek manuscripts for easier study.
It is common to Latinize foreign brand names for international markets.
The Japanese word was Latinized so that English speakers could read it.
During the process of standardization, local signs were Latinised to accommodate tourists.
Many historical texts have been Latinised for easier reading by Western scholars.
The Chinese name was Latinised to assist with pronunciation in international documents.
