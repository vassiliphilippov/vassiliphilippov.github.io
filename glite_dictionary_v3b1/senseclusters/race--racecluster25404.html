race

noun
a division of a species (biology)
5.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /reɪs/ (AmE), /reɪs/ (BrE)
a group of people, animals, or plants with shared physical or genetic characteristics, often used as a category in society or biology

Examples

Human race
Biologists debate whether these birds represent distinct races or merely local variations.
Several races of the butterfly have developed due to their separation on different islands.
Within one plant species, there may be many races adapted to different environments.
Debates about race have played a major role in shaping social policy.
Many people prefer to focus on culture and ethnicity rather than assigning labels based on race.
The idea that intelligence varies by race has been widely discredited.
