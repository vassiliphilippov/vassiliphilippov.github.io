Archeozoic eon

noun
ancient geological eon when earliest life began
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːr.ki.oʊˈzoʊ.ɪk ˈiː.ɒn / (AmE), / ˌɑː.ki.əʊˈzəʊ.ɪk ˈiː.ɒn / (BrE)
the geological eon from about 3,800 to 2,500 million years ago, during which Earth's crust formed and the earliest unicellular life appeared

Examples

The Archeozoic eon period
During the Archeozoic eon, unicellular organisms were the only forms of life on the planet.
Major geological changes occurred throughout the Archeozoic eon, shaping Earth's early surface.
Scientists study rocks from the Archeozoic eon to learn about the formation of Earth's earliest crust.
