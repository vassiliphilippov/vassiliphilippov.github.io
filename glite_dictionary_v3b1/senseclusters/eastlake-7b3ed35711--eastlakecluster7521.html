Eastlake

proper noun
19th-century furniture style
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstleɪk / (AmE), / ˈiːstleɪk / (BrE)
a style of furniture and decoration from the late 19th century, characterized by simple, geometric designs and minimal ornamentation

Examples

Eastlake furniture style
The museum's collection includes several Eastlake chairs with distinctive geometric carvings.
She restored her living room with authentic Eastlake furniture to match the home's original style.
Collectors often seek out Eastlake pieces for their elegant simplicity and craftsmanship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Rococo - an elaborate and decorative style of art and furniture from the 18th century
  • synonym: Victorian - relating to the period of Queen Victoria's reign, often ornate in style
