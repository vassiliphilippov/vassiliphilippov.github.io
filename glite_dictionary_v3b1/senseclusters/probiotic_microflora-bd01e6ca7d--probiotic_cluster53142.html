probiotic microflora

noun
intestinal community of beneficial bacteria
Pronunciation: / proʊˈbaɪ.ɑtɪk ˌmaɪ.krəˈflɔrə / (AmE), / prəʊˈbʌɪ.ɒtɪk ˌmʌɪ.krəˈflɔː.rə / (BrE)
The community of beneficial bacteria living in the intestines, which contribute to digestive health and help prevent harmful microbial growth.

Examples

The probiotic microflora balance
Antibiotics may disrupt the balance of probiotic microflora in the gut, leading to digestive issues.
Regular consumption of yogurt can help maintain healthy probiotic microflora in the digestive tract.
Scientists are studying how probiotic microflora influence immune function and general well-being.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gut flora - the microorganisms, including bacteria, fungi, and viruses, that inhabit the digestive tract
  • synonym: intestinal microbiota - the microorganisms inhabiting the intestines, especially bacteria
