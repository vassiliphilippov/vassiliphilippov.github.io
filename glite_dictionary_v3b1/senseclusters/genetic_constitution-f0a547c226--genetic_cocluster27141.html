genetic constitution

noun
an organism's genetic makeup
5.7
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˌkɑnstɪˈtuʃən / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk ˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the complete set of genes or alleles present in an organism, determining its inherited characteristics

Examples

The genetic constitution of an organism
Identical twins share the same genetic constitution, but may still have differences in appearance or behavior.
Researchers analyzed the genetic constitution of several animal populations for variations.
The genetic constitution of the plant determines its resistance to certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genotype - an organism's genetic makeup, especially as distinct from its physical characteristics
