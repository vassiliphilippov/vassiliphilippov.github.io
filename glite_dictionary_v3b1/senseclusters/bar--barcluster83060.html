bar

noun
courtroom partition separating public from legal area
1.6
Pronunciation: / bɑr / (AmE), / bɑː / (BrE)
(law) The partition in a courtroom that separates the area for judges and lawyers from the public area.

Examples

Courtroom bar
Only attorneys and their clients were permitted beyond the bar.
Spectators watched the proceedings from their seats behind the bar.
The witness walked to the bar before testifying.
