polyphonic music

noun
music with multiple independent melodies
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈfɑːnɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈfɒnɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
music consisting of two or more independent melodic lines performed simultaneously

Examples

Polyphonic music style
Composers like Palestrina were renowned for their mastery of polyphonic music.
Her studies focused on the development of polyphonic music in medieval Europe.
The choir performed a challenging piece of polyphonic music from the Renaissance period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homophonic music - music with one clearly melodic line and accompanying harmony
  • antonym: monophonic music - music consisting of a single melodic line without harmony
