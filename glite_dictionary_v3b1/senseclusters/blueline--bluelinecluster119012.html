blueline

noun
ice hockey rink line
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbluː.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈbluː.laɪn/ (BrE)
a line on an ice hockey rink that marks the boundary between the defensive, neutral, and attacking zones and is used to judge offside

Examples

Hockey blueline
The defenseman skated backward across the blueline to keep the puck in the offensive zone.
Players must not cross the blueline before the puck to avoid being offside.
The coach explained the importance of holding the blueline during a power play.

Related Terms

  • synonym: goal line - the line at each end of an ice hockey rink marking the boundary of the goal area
  • synonym: red line - the center line on an ice hockey rink
