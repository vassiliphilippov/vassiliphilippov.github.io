bycatch

noun
unintended catch in fishing
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.kætʃ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.kætʃ / (BrE)
unwanted marine animals or other organisms caught unintentionally while fishing for a different species

Examples

Unwanted bycatch animals
Improved fishing gear can help lower the amount of bycatch caught each year.
Many fisheries are taking steps to reduce bycatch and protect endangered species.
Most turtles found in shrimp nets are considered bycatch and typically discarded.

Related Terms

  • antonym: target species - the species of fish or marine life intended to be caught
