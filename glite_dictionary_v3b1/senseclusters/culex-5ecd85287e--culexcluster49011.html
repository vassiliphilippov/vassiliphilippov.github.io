Culex

noun
genus of mosquitoes
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkjuː.leks/ (AmE), /ˈkjuː.leks/ (BrE)
A genus of mosquitoes widely distributed throughout the world, characterized by holding their bodies parallel to the surface when resting.

Examples

Culex mosquito species
Culex mosquitoes are known to be carriers of several diseases such as West Nile virus.
The scientist collected samples of Culex larvae from the marsh for research.
Unlike Anopheles, Culex rests with its body parallel to the surface.
