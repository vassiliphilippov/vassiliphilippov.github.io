paleopathologist

noun
ancient disease scientist
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˌoʊpæˈθɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊpəˈθɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies diseases and injuries in ancient human or animal remains

Examples

The paleopathologist studies bones
The paleopathologist discovered evidence of tuberculosis in the ancient skeleton.
A team led by a paleopathologist analyzed mummified remains for signs of old diseases.
As a paleopathologist, she specializes in identifying illnesses from prehistoric bones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaeologist - a scientist who studies human history through excavation of sites and analysis of artifacts
  • synonym: pathologist - a doctor who studies diseases by examining tissues and organs
