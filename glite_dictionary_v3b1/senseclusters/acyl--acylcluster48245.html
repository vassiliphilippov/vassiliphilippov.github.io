acyl

noun
chemical group RCO- (from carboxylic acid)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.sɪl/ /; / /ˈæ.sɪl/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.sɪl/ /; / /ˈæ.sɪl/ (BrE)
a chemical group formed by removing one hydrogen atom from a carboxylic acid, typically represented as RCO-, where R is an organic group

Examples

An acyl group
Fatty acids are converted into acyl derivatives in metabolic pathways.
One common acyl compound in biology is acetyl-CoA.
The acyl group plays a key role in many organic reactions.
