opposed

adjective
in opposition or disagreement
Pronunciation: / əˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / əˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
(of people, groups, or ideas) being in opposition or in disagreement with something or someone.

Examples

Opposed views
Her views are directly opposed to those of her colleagues.
Many people are opposed to the new development project in the city center.
The two politicians were opposed on most major issues during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hostile - actively opposed or unfriendly
  • antonym: supportive - giving help or encouragement
