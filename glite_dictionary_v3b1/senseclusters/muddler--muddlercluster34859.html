muddler

noun
fishing fly imitation
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdlər / (AmE), / ˈmʌdlə / (BrE)
an artificial fishing fly with a spun deer-hair head, designed to imitate small fish or sculpins

Examples

A fishing muddler
The angler selected a muddler from his fly box to attract larger trout.
She learned to tie a muddler using deer hair and sharp scissors.
Many fishermen believe a muddler works well in fast-moving streams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry fly - a fishing fly designed to float on the water's surface
  • synonym: streamer - a type of fishing fly that imitates small fish
