sight of

noun
the appearance or view of something
Pronunciation: / saɪt əv / (AmE), / saɪt əv / (BrE)
the appearance or view of someone or something, especially as perceived by the senses or by the mind

Examples

The sight of friends
He was overwhelmed with joy at the sight of his old friends at the reunion.
Many people faint at the sight of blood during medical procedures.
The child screamed at the sight of the large spider crawling toward her.
