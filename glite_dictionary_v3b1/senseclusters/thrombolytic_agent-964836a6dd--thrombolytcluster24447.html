thrombolytic agent

noun
drug that dissolves blood clots
6.1 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌθroʊm.boʊˈlɪt.ɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (AmE), / ˌθrɒm.bəʊˈlɪt.ɪk ˈeɪ.dʒənt / (BrE)
a drug that dissolves blood clots, especially those blocking blood flow to the heart or brain

Examples

A thrombolytic agent drug
Administering a thrombolytic agent can sometimes be risky due to the potential for excessive bleeding.
Doctors may use a thrombolytic agent in emergency situations like stroke or pulmonary embolism.
The patient was quickly given a thrombolytic agent to restore blood flow after the heart attack.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticoagulant - a drug that prevents blood clotting
  • synonym: fibrinolytic - a substance that breaks down fibrin in blood clots
