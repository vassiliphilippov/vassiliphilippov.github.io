Forssman antibody

noun
antibody linked with some diseases
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrs·mən ˌæn.tiˌbɒd.i / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsmən ˌæn.tɪˌbɒd.i / (BrE)
an antibody found in the blood of individuals with certain diseases, such as infectious mononucleosis, used as a diagnostic marker

Examples

A Forssman antibody test
A positive Forssman antibody test may indicate certain viral infections.
Researchers measured the patient's Forssman antibody levels as part of the diagnostic workup.
The presence of a Forssman antibody can aid in diagnosing infectious mononucleosis.
