enflesh

verb
to make tangible
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfleʃ/ /; / /ɛn.ˈfleʃ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfleʃ/ /; / /ɛn.ˈfleʃ/ (BrE)
to give physical or bodily form to something abstract or spiritual; to become corporeal or incarnate

Examples

To enflesh an idea
The writer tried to enflesh her dreams through vivid storytelling.
Modern architecture often seeks to enflesh abstract concepts in concrete structures.
He wanted to enflesh his vision of hope in the community project.
The ancient myth tells of a spirit that would enflesh and walk among humans.
Some believe that ideas can enflesh through art and become part of the real world.
In the story, the ghost enfleshed itself to communicate with the living.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstract - to consider something apart from physical reality
  • antonym: disembody - to free from a physical body; to exist without a body
  • synonym: embody - to give a tangible or visible form to an idea or quality
  • synonym: incarnate - to embody in flesh; to give a bodily form to
  • synonym: materialize - to become real or to appear in physical form
