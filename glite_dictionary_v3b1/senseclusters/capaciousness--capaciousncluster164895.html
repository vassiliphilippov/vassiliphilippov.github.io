capaciousness

noun
intellectual breadth or spaciousness
8.2 7.1
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈpeɪ.ʃəs.nəs/ (AmE), /kə.ˈpeɪ.ʃəs.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of having a wide intellectual scope; the ability to consider or understand a broad range of ideas and viewpoints

Examples

Capaciousness of mind
He admired the capaciousness of her intellectual interests, which ranged from philosophy to engineering.
Her capaciousness of mind allowed her to appreciate new scientific theories from a variety of disciplines.
The university values capaciousness in its faculty, encouraging open-mindedness and interdisciplinary study.

Related Terms

  • antonym: narrowness - the quality of being limited in extent, scope, or understanding
  • synonym: roominess - the quality of having a lot of space; spaciousness (used both literally and metaphorically)
