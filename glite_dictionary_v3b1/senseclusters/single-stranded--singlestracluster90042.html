single-stranded

adjective
having a single strand; not double-stranded
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋ.ɡəl ˈstræn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.ɡl̩ ˈstræn.dɪd / (BrE)
(of molecules, especially nucleic acids) consisting of only one strand rather than two intertwined strands.

Examples

Single-stranded RNA
Many viruses contain single-stranded RNA as their genetic material.
The researchers developed a method to rapidly amplify single-stranded DNA.
Unlike double-stranded molecules, single-stranded structures are more flexible and reactive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: double-stranded - (of molecules, especially nucleic acids) consisting of two complementary strands
