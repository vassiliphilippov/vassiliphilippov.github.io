tryptone

noun
peptide mixture for cultures
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪptoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪptəʊn / (BrE)
a mixture of peptides produced by digesting casein with enzymes, commonly used in bacterial culture media

Examples

Tryptone culture medium
The scientist added tryptone to the broth to support bacterial growth in the experiment.
Many standard laboratory media contain tryptone as a source of nutrients for microorganisms.
Without enough tryptone, the bacteria failed to grow properly in the culture dish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peptone - a water-soluble mixture of peptides and amino acids formed by protein digestion
