syne

conjunction
since; after that
9.9
Pronunciation: /saɪn/ (AmE), /saɪn/ (BrE)
used to introduce a clause meaning 'since' or 'after that', especially in archaic or Scots English

Examples

Syne he left
He left the village, and syne no one heard from him again.
They quarreled long ago, and syne have never spoken a word.
The old song tells of days gone by and all that's happened syne.

Related Terms
