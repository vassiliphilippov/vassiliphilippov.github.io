instrumentalization

noun
making a song instrumental
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstrəˌmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrʊˌmɛntəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of creating an instrumental version of a song by removing or omitting the vocal track

Examples

Instrumentalization of a song
The instrumentalization of the hit single allowed DJs to use it in dance mixes.
After the instrumentalization process, the song was ready for karaoke night.
Fans enjoyed the instrumentalization because it highlighted the band's musical skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remix - a new or different version of a song made by altering the original
  • antonym: vocalization - the act of adding or emphasizing vocals in a song
