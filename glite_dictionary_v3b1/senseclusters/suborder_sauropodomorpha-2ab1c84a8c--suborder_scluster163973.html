suborder Sauropodomorpha

noun
taxonomic group of large herbivorous dinosaurs
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àås…îÀêr…ôÀåpo äd…ôÀàm…îrf…ô / (AmE), / Àås…îÀêr…ôÀåp…íd…ôÀàm…îÀêf…ô / (BrE)
A taxonomic suborder of gigantic herbivorous dinosaurs characterized by large bodies, long necks, and small heads, including groups such as Prosauropoda and Sauropoda (e.g., apatosaurus, diplodocus, titanosaurs).

Examples

The suborder Sauropodomorpha dinosaurs
Apatosaurus and Diplodocus are well-known members of the suborder Sauropodomorpha.
Scientists have discovered new fossils belonging to the suborder Sauropodomorpha in South America.
The suborder Sauropodomorpha includes some of the largest land animals ever to have existed.
