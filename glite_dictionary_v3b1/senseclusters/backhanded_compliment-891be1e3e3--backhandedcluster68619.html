backhanded compliment

noun
insult disguised as praise
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌhændɪd ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (AmE), / ˈbakhændɪd ˈkɒmplɪmənt / (BrE)
A remark that appears to be praise but is actually insulting or critical in a subtle way.

Examples

A backhanded compliment given
His backhanded compliment made everyone at the table laugh awkwardly.
Receiving a backhanded compliment can sometimes be more hurtful than outright criticism.
She gave me a backhanded compliment when she said my presentation was impressive for a beginner.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: compliment - an expression of praise, commendation, or admiration
  • degree higher: insult - a rude expression intended to offend or hurt
