crosshair

noun
target of criticism
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌhɛr / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌheə / (BrE)
a person or thing that is the focus of strong criticism, attack, or close attention.

Examples

In the crosshair of critics
After the scandal, the CEO found himself in the crosshair of the media.
The new policy put the company directly in the crosshair of environmental activists.
She became the crosshair of criticism after her controversial statement.
