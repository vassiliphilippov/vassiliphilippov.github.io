checksummed

verb
to apply checksum
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkˌsʌmd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌsʌmd / (BrE)
to have had a checksum calculated and applied, usually to data, to verify its integrity

Examples

To checksum data
Before uploading, the document was checksummed to prevent errors during transfer.
All files must be checksummed to ensure they have not been altered.
The software automatically checksummed each download for security reasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrupt - to make data unreliable or incorrect
  • synonym: verify - to check that something is true or accurate
