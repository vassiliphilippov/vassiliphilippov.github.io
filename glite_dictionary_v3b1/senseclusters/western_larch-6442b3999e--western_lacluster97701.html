western larch

noun
a North American timber larch tree
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a tall larch tree (Larix occidentalis) native to western North America, noted for its pale green, sharply pointed leaves and oblong cones; valued for its timber

Examples

The western larch tree
Each autumn, the needles of the western larch turn golden yellow before falling.

The western larch is prized for its strong and durable wood, making it valuable in the timber industry.
