silkscreen

verb
to print using a silkscreen process
9.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪlk.skriːn/ /; / /ˈsɪlks.kriːn/ (AmE), /ˈsɪlk.skriːn/ /; / /ˈsɪlks.kriːn/ (BrE)
to print or produce an image or design by forcing ink through a mesh screen (the silkscreen process)

Examples

To silkscreen a poster
He spent all afternoon silkscreening limited edition prints for his loyal customers.
Posters for the event were silkscreened in bright, bold colors at the local print shop.
The artist decided to silkscreen her designs onto a series of T-shirts for the exhibition.
The artist spent hours silkscreening posters for the upcoming exhibition.
They learned how to silkscreen their own designs onto T-shirts in art class.
Many companies silkscreen their logos onto promotional bags for events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: etch - to cut or carve a design onto a surface
  • synonym: screen print - to print using a mesh screen and ink
  • synonym: screen-print - to print by forcing ink through a prepared screen of fine material
