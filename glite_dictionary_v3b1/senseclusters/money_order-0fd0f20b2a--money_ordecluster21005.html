money order

noun
written payment order to a named person
2.8 0.90/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm åni Àå…îrd…ôr / (AmE), / Ààm åni Àå…îÀêd…ô / (BrE)
a written order directing the payment of a specified sum of money to a named person, typically issued and paid at a post office or other authorized institution.

Examples

A money order payment
She sent the documents by mail along with a money order for the application fee.
The landlord requested a money order instead of a personal check for the rent payment.
You can purchase a money order at most banks or post offices if you don't have a checking account.
