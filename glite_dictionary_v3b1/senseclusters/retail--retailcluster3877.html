retail

verb
to be sold to the public
Pronunciation: / ˈriː.teɪl /; / ˈriːˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈriː.teɪl /; / ˈriːteɪl / (BrE)
to sell goods to the public, usually in small quantities for personal use (not wholesale)

Examples

To retail products
Organic produce often retails at higher prices than conventional alternatives.
The new phone models retail for less than $500 at most stores.
These limited-edition sneakers retailed quickly when they first launched.
Many farmers prefer to retail their produce directly at local markets.
The company plans to retail its new line of shoes in selected stores this fall.
The goods are imported from abroad and then retailed at competitive prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: wholesale - to be sold in large quantities, often to be retailed by others
