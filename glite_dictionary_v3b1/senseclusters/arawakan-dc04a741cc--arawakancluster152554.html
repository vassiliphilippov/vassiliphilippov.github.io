Arawakan

noun
family of South American Indigenous languages
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌærəˈwɑːkən / (AmE), / ˌærəˈwɑːkən / (BrE)
A member of a large family of South American Indigenous languages formerly spoken in a broad region of northeastern South America.

Examples

Arawakan languages
Linguists have studied the influence of Arawakan languages on modern South American dialects.
Several tribes in the Amazon basin spoke Arawakan languages before European contact.
The Arawakan language family includes a wide variety of dialects spread across different countries.
