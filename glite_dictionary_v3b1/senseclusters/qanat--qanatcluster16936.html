qanat

noun
underground water channel
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑːˈnɑːt / (AmE), / kəˈnæt / (BrE)
an underground tunnel or channel used to transport water from an aquifer, especially in dry regions

Examples

An underground qanat
The ancient city survived thanks to a network of qanats bringing water from distant mountains.
Farmers in the desert region depend on the qanat system for irrigation.
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved qanat beneath the old settlement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aqueduct - a structure for conveying water, often above ground
  • synonym: well - a deep hole or shaft dug to obtain water
