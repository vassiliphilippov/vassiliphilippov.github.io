scatter

verb
to spread things over an area
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈskæ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈskæ.tə/ (BrE)
to throw, spread, or distribute things over a wide area in a random or irregular way

Examples

To scatter seeds
Autumn leaves were scattered by the wind across the park.
She likes to scatter birdseed in the garden every morning.
The children scattered their toys all over the living room.
If you scatter grass seed evenly, the lawn will grow in thick and lush.
She scattered a handful of wildflower seeds across the garden bed.
The farmer likes to scatter seeds over the field in early spring.
Children laughed as they scattered confetti during the parade.
She scattered seeds in the garden to encourage wildflowers to grow.
The wind began to scatter the autumn leaves across the park.
