methinks

verb
archaic 'I think'
Pronunciation: / məˈθɪŋks / (AmE), / mɪˈθɪŋks / (BrE)
used to express the idea that it seems to me or I think, especially in archaic or literary English

Examples

Methinks the weather
Methinks the weather will turn stormy before nightfall.
In old plays, a character might say, 'methinks this plan will not succeed.'
Methinks you are too quick to judge his actions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: believe - to accept something as true or real
  • antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • synonym: seems - to give the impression of being or doing something
