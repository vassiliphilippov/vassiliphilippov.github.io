fable

noun
fabricated or improbable account
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈfeɪbəl / (BrE)
A deliberately false or improbable account; a fabricated story or myth.

Examples

A fable about riches
His entire story about winning the lottery was just a fable to impress his friends.
Many politicians' promises turn out to be nothing more than a fable once they are elected.
The rumor about the haunted house proved to be a fable after a thorough investigation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fact - something that is true or real, as opposed to fiction or fabrication
  • synonym: myth - a traditional or fictitious story, often explaining a phenomenon
