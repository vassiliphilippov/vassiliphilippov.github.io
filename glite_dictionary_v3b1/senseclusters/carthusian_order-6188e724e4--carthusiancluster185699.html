Carthusian order

noun
Catholic religious order known for austerity
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑːrˈθuːʃən ˈɔːrdər / (AmE), / kɑːˈθjuːzɪən ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a Roman Catholic religious order known for its strict asceticism and contemplative life, founded by St. Bruno in 1084
