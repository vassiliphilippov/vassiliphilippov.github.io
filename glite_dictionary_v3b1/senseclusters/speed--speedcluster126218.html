speed

noun
photographic lens: focal length to diameter ratio
Pronunciation: / spiːd / (AmE), / spiːd / (BrE)
The ratio of the focal length to the diameter of a camera lens system, determining its light-gathering ability.

Examples

Lens speed
A lens with a lower speed can capture better images in low-light conditions.
Photographers often choose lenses with a high speed for indoor shoots.
The speed of the lens affects both exposure and depth of field in photography.
