scrape

verb
to make or inscribe by scratching
3.2 4.12/m
Pronunciation: / skreÉªp / (AmE), / skreÉªp / (BrE)
to form or inscribe (something) by scratching or rubbing a surface, often with a sharp or hard object

Examples

To scrape a pattern
He carefully scraped a simple pattern into the wooden bench with his pocketknife.
The children scraped their initials into the wet cement sidewalk.
Years ago, people would scrape warnings onto cave walls to alert others of danger.

Synonyms

  • scratch
    1.1 7.86/m
    scratch makes shallow marks by dragging something sharp across a surface, while scrape often means rubbing or cutting to form or inscribe.
  • etch
    5.5 1.75/m
    etch cuts or bites a design into hard surfaces, often with acid or a tool, while scrape is more general rubbing or scratching.
  • engrave
    5.2 3.16/m
    engrave cuts deep, careful designs or words into metal or wood for lasting decoration, while scrape is rougher rubbing or marking.
  • scribe
    7.4 0.19/m
    scribe draws or marks a fine line or writes carefully by hand, while scrape implies scratching or rubbing to form or inscribe.

Related Terms
