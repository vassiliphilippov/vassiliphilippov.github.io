refute

verb
prove false by argument or evidence
5.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfjuːt /; / rɪˈfjut / (AmE), / rɪˈfjuːt / (BrE)
to prove that a statement, argument, or theory is false or incorrect, usually by using evidence or reasoning

Examples

To refute an argument
During the debate, he skillfully refuted his opponent’s main arguments.
She worked hard to refute the false accusations made against her.
The scientist was able to refute the hypothesis with new experimental data.
His theory was refuted by the results of the latest research.
She confidently refuted every allegation made against her during the interview.
The scientist was able to refute the claim with new experimental evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirm - to establish the truth or correctness of something
  • synonym: disprove - to show that something is not true
  • antonym: prove - to demonstrate the truth or existence of something
  • synonym: rebut - to claim or prove that evidence or an accusation is false
