overtip

verb
to tip too much
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtɪp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtɪp / (BrE)
to give a tip that is larger than what is usual or expected

Examples

To overtip a waiter
She did not want to overtip the taxi driver after a short ride.
If you overtip every time, it can become expensive over time.
He accidentally overtipped the server by adding an extra zero to the bill.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: tip - to give a small amount of money to someone for a service
  • antonym: undertip - to give a tip that is smaller than what is usual or expected
