credible

adjective
able to be believed
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛdəbəl /; / ˈkrɛd.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdəbl̩ /; / ˈkrɛd.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
able to be believed; convincing or trustworthy

Examples

A credible candidate
She is a credible candidate for the job, given her experience.
The witness gave a credible account of what happened that night.
Without credible evidence, the claims cannot be taken seriously.
For any rumor to be widely accepted, it must seem credible to the public.
Her story sounded credible, so the committee decided to investigate further.
The prosecutor presented a credible witness who supported the main argument.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incredible - impossible or very difficult to believe
  • synonym: plausible - seeming reasonable or probable
