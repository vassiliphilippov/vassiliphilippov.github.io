contempt of court

noun
disrespect for a court of law
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛmpt əv kɔrt / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpt əv kɔːt / (BrE)
the offense of showing disrespect or disobedience to a court of law, its officers, or its rules

Examples

Contempt of court penalty
Acts of contempt of court can result in jail time or other penalties.
She was fined for contempt of court after refusing to answer the judge's questions.
The lawyer warned his client that any further outbursts could be considered contempt of court.
Failing to follow the judge’s instructions can lead to contempt of court charges.
If a witness refuses to answer questions, they may be found in contempt of court.
The journalist was charged with contempt of court for publishing confidential details.
