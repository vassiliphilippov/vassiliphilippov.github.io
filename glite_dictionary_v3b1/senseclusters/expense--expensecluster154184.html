expense

verb
to record as expense
6.5
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspens/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspens/ (BrE)
to record or claim a cost as an expense in financial accounts or for reimbursement

Examples

To expense a purchase
The company expensed the new computers instead of treating them as long-term assets.
All travel costs were expensed in the current fiscal year.
She asked the accountant whether the repairs should be expensed or capitalized.
You must expense your hotel and meal costs after the business trip.
She forgot to expense the taxi fare from the airport to the office.
All employees are required to expense their work-related purchases by the end of the month.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capitalize - to record a cost as a capital asset rather than an expense
  • synonym: charge - to record a cost to an account
  • synonym: reimburse - to pay back money spent or lost
