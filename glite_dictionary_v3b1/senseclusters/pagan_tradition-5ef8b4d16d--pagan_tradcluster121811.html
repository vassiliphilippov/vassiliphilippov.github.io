pagan tradition

noun
non-mainstream spiritual customs or beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪɡən trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪɡən trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
A set of spiritual beliefs, practices, or customs that are not part of the major world religions, often associated with ancient or polytheistic faiths.

Examples

Old pagan tradition
Many old European festivals are rooted in pagan tradition and have been adapted over time.
She developed an interest in pagan tradition after studying ancient mythology and rituals.
Some modern spiritual groups incorporate elements of pagan tradition into their ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: folk religion - traditional, often local, religious beliefs and practices distinct from organized religions
  • antonym: mainstream religion - the most widely practiced religions, like Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, etc.
