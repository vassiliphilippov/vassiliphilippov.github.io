combat neurosis

noun
mental disorder caused by war stress
6.8 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.bæt njuː.ˈroʊ.sɪs/ /; / /kəm.ˈbæt njuː.ˈroʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.bæt njuː.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈkʌm.bæt njuː.ˈrəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A mental disorder resulting from the stress and trauma of active warfare, characterized by symptoms such as anxiety, nightmares, and emotional instability.

Examples

Combat neurosis symptoms
During the war, little was understood about combat neurosis and its long-term psychological effects.
Many soldiers returning from battle exhibited signs of combat neurosis, struggling to readjust to civilian life.
The doctor diagnosed him with combat neurosis after witnessing his anxiety attacks and recurring nightmares.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shell shock - psychological disturbance caused by prolonged exposure to active warfare
