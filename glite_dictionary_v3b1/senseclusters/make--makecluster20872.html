Synonyms

  • reach
    1.1 162/m
    reach means to arrive at or achieve a place or goal after effort, while make is a more general verb for this action.
  • get to
    2.4 93/m
    get to means reaching a place or goal, often in casual speech, while make is more neutral and appears in many contexts.
  • attain
    3.9 17/m
    attain means to reach a level or goal after effort and is formal, while make is less formal and used in everyday speech.
  • make it to
    3.7 14/m
    make it to means succeeding in arriving at or attending despite problems, while make is a simple verb meaning to reach or achieve.
  • arrive at
    2.1 25/m
    arrive at means to reach a place or conclusion after a journey or thought, while make is broader and can mean achieve.
  • hit
    2.5 38/m
    hit means to reach a target, amount, or level often suddenly or exactly, while make is more general for arriving or achieving.
