make

verb
arrive at a destination
1.1
Pronunciation: / meɪk / (AmE), / meɪk / (BrE)
to arrive at, reach, or achieve a place, goal, or standard

Examples

Make it on time
Despite the traffic, we made it to the meeting just in time.
She was so exhausted she barely made it up the stairs.
They drove all night to make the concert in Chicago.
If you make enough points in this round, you'll win a prize.
She made three goals in the championship game.
The team was thrilled when their forward made the winning basket.
If you hurry, you might still make the last train.
She ran all the way to make the 8 o'clock meeting.
We were stuck in traffic and almost didn't make our appointment.
