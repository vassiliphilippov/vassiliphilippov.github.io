underexposure

noun
insufficient light exposure (photography)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪkˈspəʊʒər / (BrE)
the act or result of exposing film, a sensor, or an image to too little light, resulting in a dark or poorly detailed picture

Examples

Photo underexposure
Accidental underexposure can ruin an otherwise perfect shot by making the image too dark.
Learning to avoid underexposure is essential for every aspiring photographer.
The photograph suffered from underexposure, making it difficult to see any details in the shadows.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overexposure - exposing film or a sensor to too much light
