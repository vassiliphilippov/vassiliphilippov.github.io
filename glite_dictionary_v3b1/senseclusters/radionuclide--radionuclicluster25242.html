radionuclide

noun
radioactive isotope
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈnuːklaɪd / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈnjuːklaɪd / (BrE)
a radioactive atom or form of an element that emits radiation, often used in medicine, industry, or scientific research

Examples

The laboratory detected traces of radionuclides in the soil after the nuclear accident.
Some radionuclides are used to treat cancer by targeting and destroying tumor cells.
