cajón

noun
box percussion instrument
Pronunciation: / kaˈxoʊn / (AmE), / kaˈxɒn /; / kaˈhɒn / (BrE)
a box-shaped percussion instrument, usually made of wood, played by slapping its front or sides with the hands, commonly used in Afro-Peruvian and flamenco music

Examples

Flamenco cajon
The musician sat on a cajón and kept the beat for the entire band.
She learned to play the cajón to add a unique sound to her group’s performances.
During the street festival, a performer played rhythms on his cajón for the crowd.
The percussionist sat on the cajón and kept the beat for the band.
She learned to play the cajón to add a unique sound to her music group.
During the street performance, the musician used a cajón instead of a traditional drum set.
