put to sleep

verb
euthanize (an animal) painlessly
5.8 0.63/m
Pronunciation: / p ät t…ô sliÀêp / (AmE), / p ät t…ô sliÀêp / (BrE)
to humanely kill (an animal), especially by giving it a drug, usually to relieve suffering

Examples

To put to sleep a pet
After a long illness, they made the difficult decision to put to sleep their beloved dog.
The veterinarian explained why it was kinder to put to sleep the suffering animal.
When her cat was no longer responding to treatment, she chose to put it to sleep to end its pain.

Synonyms

  • put down
    5.5 1.94/m
    put down is used to kill an animal humanely, often by vets, while put to sleep is a gentler, everyday phrase.
  • put away
    7.7 0.05/m
    put away ends an animal's life humanely, often by injection at a vet, while put to sleep is a softer, common phrase.
  • euthanize
    8.5 0.98/m
    euthanize is the formal medical term used by vets and doctors, while put to sleep is a softer everyday phrase.
  • mercy kill
    8.6 0.00/m
    mercy kill describes causing a painless death to relieve suffering, often of people too, while put to sleep focuses on animals.
