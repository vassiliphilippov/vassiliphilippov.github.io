autolysin

noun
bacterial cell wall enzyme
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtəˈlaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈlaɪsɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down the cell walls of bacteria, leading to cell lysis

Examples

Autolysin enzyme
Scientists discovered that autolysin plays a crucial role in bacterial cell division.
The presence of autolysin can cause bacteria to break down their own cell walls.
Researchers measured the activity of autolysin to understand how antibiotics affect bacteria.
