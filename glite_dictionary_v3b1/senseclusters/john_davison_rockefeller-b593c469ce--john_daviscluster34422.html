John Davison Rockefeller

proper noun
American oil magnate and philanthropist
6.2
Pronunciation: /dʒɑːn ˈdeɪ.vɪ.sən ˈrɑːk.ə.fel.ər/ (AmE), /dʒɒn ˈdeɪ.vɪ.sən ˈrɒk.ə.fel.ə/ (BrE)
(1839–1937) An American industrialist and philanthropist, founder of Standard Oil, noted for being one of the wealthiest individuals in history and for generous charitable donations.

Examples

John Davison Rockefeller founded Standard Oil
John Davison Rockefeller founded the Standard Oil Company, which dominated the oil industry for decades.
Many historians regard John Davison Rockefeller as one of the most influential figures in American business history.
Through his philanthropic efforts, John Davison Rockefeller established foundations that supported education and medical research.
