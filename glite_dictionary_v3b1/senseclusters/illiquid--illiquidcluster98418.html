illiquid

adjective
hard to sell quickly
8.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlɪ.kwɪd/ /; / /ɪl.ˈɪ.kwɪd/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlɪ.kwɪd/ /; / /ɪl.ˈɪ.kwɪd/ (BrE)
describing an asset or market that cannot be easily sold or converted into cash without losing value

Examples

Illiquid assets
The company's real estate holdings are considered illiquid because they cannot be sold quickly.
Investors should be cautious when buying illiquid assets, as they may be hard to sell in an emergency.
During the financial crisis, many markets became illiquid and trading nearly stopped.
Many collectibles are considered illiquid because they are difficult to sell quickly.
During a financial crisis, even some stocks can become illiquid and hard to trade.
Investors should be cautious about putting too much money into illiquid assets like real estate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illiquid asset - an asset that cannot be quickly sold or exchanged for cash
  • synonym: illiquidity - the state of being difficult to buy or sell quickly
  • antonym: liquid - easy to buy or sell quickly without affecting price
