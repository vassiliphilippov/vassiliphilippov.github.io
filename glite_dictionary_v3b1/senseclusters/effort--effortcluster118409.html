effort

noun
earnest attempt to accomplish something
1.0 101/m

the use of physical or mental energy to try to do something; an attempt

Examples

Make an effort
Even a small effort can make a big difference in someone's life.
She made a tremendous effort to finish the project on time.
Winning the championship was the result of years of effort and dedication.
Despite her best effort, she could not solve the complex puzzle.
The project was a group effort that demanded everyone's dedication.
Winning the championship took a lot of effort from the entire team.

Synonyms

  • exertion
    5.0 3.66/m
    exertion is strong physical or mental energy that tires the body, while effort means any attempt to do something.
  • attempt
    1.0 99/m
    attempt is a single act of trying to reach a goal, while effort can mean that act or ongoing work toward it.
  • try
    1.0 14/m
    try is an informal, often quick attempt to do something, while effort can mean more energy or planned work.
  • endeavor
    4.1 16/m
    endeavor is a determined, often long attempt at a difficult task, while effort can be any smaller or single attempt.
  • application
    3.4 2.52/m
    application is careful, steady attention given to a task, while effort is the general use of energy or a single attempt.
  • hard work
    1.6 18/m
    hard work is long, serious labor that wears you out, while effort can be a short or simple attempt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idleness - the state of being inactive or not making any effort
