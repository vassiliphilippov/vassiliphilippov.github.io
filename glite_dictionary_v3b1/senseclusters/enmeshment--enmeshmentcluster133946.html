enmeshment

noun
unclear personal boundaries
8.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ /; / /ɛn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ /; / /ɛn.ˈmeʃ.mənt/ (BrE)
a situation where personal boundaries are unclear because relationships are overly close or intertwined, especially in families

Examples

Family enmeshment
The therapist explained that enmeshment can make it difficult for family members to develop independence.
In some families, enmeshment leads to confusion about individual needs and boundaries.
She recognized signs of enmeshment in her relationship and decided to seek help.
Therapists often discuss enmeshment when talking about family boundaries and personal growth.
The enmeshment between the two departments led to confusion about responsibilities.
Overcoming enmeshment can help individuals develop a stronger sense of self.

Related Terms

  • synonym: codependency - a relationship where one person enables another's unhealthy behavior
  • antonym: detachment - the state of being emotionally separate or independent
  • synonym: entanglement - a complicated or difficult situation involving many connected parts
  • antonym: independence - the state of being self-reliant and not influenced by others
