orphaning

noun
loss of parent link
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrfənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːfənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of leaving items, such as files or records, without their parent or controlling link, often due to deletion or failure

Examples

Orphaning of files
A software bug led to the orphaning of several important files on the server.
Database administrators must regularly check for orphaning to maintain data integrity.
Improper shutdowns can result in the orphaning of system processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deletion - the act of removing or erasing something
  • antonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original state
