double-edged

adjective
having two contradictory meanings or effects
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌbəlˈɛdʒd / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of a remark, comment, etc.) having two possible meanings or effects, often with one being less favorable than the other; ambiguous or having both positive and negative aspects.

Examples

Double-edged remark
Be careful with double-edged remarks, as they can be easily misunderstood.
Her double-edged compliment left him wondering if she was being sincere.
The new policy has a double-edged impact, benefiting some while disadvantaging others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguous - having more than one possible meaning or interpretation
  • antonym: unambiguous - having only one possible meaning; clear
