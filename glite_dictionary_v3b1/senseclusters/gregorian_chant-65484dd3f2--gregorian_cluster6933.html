Gregorian chant

noun
style of unaccompanied sacred song
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪˈɡɔri.ən tʃænt / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔː.ri.ən tʃɑːnt / (BrE)
a style of unaccompanied, monophonic sacred song of the Roman Catholic Church, named after Pope Gregory I, used since the early Middle Ages

Examples

Gregorian chant music
Listening to a Gregorian chant can create a peaceful and meditative atmosphere.
Monks have preserved the tradition of Gregorian chant for centuries.
The choir performed a Gregorian chant during the solemn church service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plainsong - unaccompanied sacred song with a free rhythm
