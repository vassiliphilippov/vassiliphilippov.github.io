aerator

noun
device that exposes things to air
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈeəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
A device used to expose a substance, such as water or soil, to air, often to promote aeration or oxygenation.

Examples

Water aerator
Modern faucets often include an aerator to improve water flow and reduce splashing.
Please install an aerator in the pond to keep the fish healthy.
The sewage treatment plant uses an aerator to enhance oxygen levels in the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerate - to expose to the action or effect of air or to cause air to circulate through
