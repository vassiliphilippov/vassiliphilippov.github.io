neglect

noun
lack of care or attention
3.1 3.5
Pronunciation: / nɪˈɡlɛkt / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛkt / (BrE)
the act, state, or habit of not giving enough care or attention to someone or something

Examples

Child neglect
His plants died as a result of his constant neglect.
Historic buildings have fallen into disrepair due to governmental neglect.
The child's illness was caused by years of neglect at home.
After years of neglect, the garden was completely overgrown with weeds.
The building fell into neglect when the owners moved away.
Visible signs of neglect included peeling paint and broken windows.
Her chronic neglect of her duties led to serious problems at work.
Parental neglect can have lasting effects on a child's development.
The building had fallen into disrepair through years of neglect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attention - the action of dealing with or taking special care of someone or something
  • antonym: attentiveness - quality of paying close attention
  • antonym: care - the provision of what is necessary for the health, welfare, maintenance, and protection of someone or something
  • synonym: carelessness - lack of attention or concern
  • synonym: disrepair - the state of being in need of repair
