stream

verb
to flow or issue out profusely
4.4 1.5
Pronunciation: / striːm / (AmE), / striːm / (BrE)
to flow or move continuously in large amounts, like liquid, gas, people, or things

Examples

Sweat streams down
After the intense workout, her face was streaming with sweat.
Blood streamed from the cut on his arm as he tried to clean it.
Tears streamed down her cheeks during the emotional scene.
Sunlight streamed through the window, filling the room with warmth.
Tears began to stream down his cheeks as he listened to the news.
Water continued to stream from the broken pipe until it was finally shut off.
After the concert ended, people began to stream out of the venue.
Demonstrators were seen streaming down the main street during the rally.
Visitors continued to stream into the museum throughout the afternoon.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: drip - (of liquid) to fall in drops
  • synonym: pour - to flow rapidly in a steady stream; to come out plentifully
  • degree lower: trickle - to flow in a small, slow stream
