undersell

verb
to downplay value
4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.sel/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.sel/ (BrE)
to describe or present something as less important, valuable, or impressive than it really is

Examples

To undersell your talent
She tends to undersell her skills when talking to potential employers.
The review undersold the movie, but I thought it was excellent.
Do not undersell your achievements in the interview.
She tends to undersell her skills when applying for new jobs.
The company did not want to undersell the benefits of its latest product.
By underselling his experience, he missed out on several opportunities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
  • antonym: exaggerate - to make something seem more important or impressive than it is
