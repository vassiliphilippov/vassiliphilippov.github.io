polyphonic

adjective
having two or more pronunciations (linguistics)
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈfɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a letter or symbol) having two or more possible pronunciations or phonetic values.

Examples

Polyphonic letter sounds
In linguistics, a polyphonic letter requires context to determine its sound value.
Some alphabets contain polyphonic characters that change pronunciation based on surrounding letters.
The letter 'a' in English is often polyphonic, with different pronunciations in words like 'cat' and 'cake'.
