corepressor

noun
gene expression inhibitor
9.5
Pronunciation: /koʊ.rɪ.ˈpres.ər/ (AmE), /kəʊ.rɪ.ˈpres.ə/ (BrE)
a molecule or protein that binds to regulatory proteins to help turn off or decrease the expression of specific genes

Examples

A corepressor protein
A corepressor attaches to the repressor protein, preventing the gene from being transcribed.
Without a corepressor, the repressor cannot effectively block gene expression.
Researchers discovered a new corepressor involved in regulating metabolic genes.
A corepressor can silence specific genes by making the DNA less accessible to transcription machinery.
Researchers discovered a new corepressor that plays a key role in regulating hormone response.
The absence of a corepressor may lead to increased gene activity and abnormal cell growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activator - a molecule that increases gene expression
  • antonym: coactivator - a protein that increases gene transcription by binding to regulatory proteins
  • synonym: repressor - a protein that suppresses gene expression
