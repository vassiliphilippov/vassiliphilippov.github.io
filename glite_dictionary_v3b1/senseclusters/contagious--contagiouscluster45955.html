contagious

adjective
able to be spread by infection
4.5 2.6
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈteɪ.dʒəs/ (AmE), /kən.ˈteɪ.dʒəs/ (BrE)
able to be spread from one person or organism to another (such as a disease, feeling, or idea)

Examples

Contagious disease
Chickenpox is a contagious disease, especially among young children.
She stayed home because her illness was still contagious and she didn't want to infect others.
The flu is highly contagious and can spread quickly through a school.
He caught a contagious cold at school and had to stay home for a week.
Her contagious laughter soon had everyone in the room smiling.
The enthusiasm of the coach was truly contagious and inspired the whole team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infectious - (of disease) capable of causing infection or spreading easily
  • antonym: noncontagious - not able to be spread from one person or organism to another
