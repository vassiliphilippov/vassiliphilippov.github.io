scabbarded

adjective
in a scabbard
Pronunciation: / ˈskæbərdɪd / (AmE), / ˈskæbədɪd / (BrE)
having a scabbard or being placed inside a scabbard.

Examples

A scabbarded sword
The knight rode with his scabbarded sword at his side.
He carefully placed the scabbarded dagger on the table before him.
Soldiers marched past, each carrying a scabbarded weapon on their belt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unsheathed - not in a scabbard; drawn from its covering
