habeas corpus

noun
legal writ demanding a prisoner's appearance in court
Pronunciation: / ˌheɪbiəs ˈkɔrpəs / (AmE), / ˌheɪbɪəs ˈkɔːpəs / (BrE)
a legal order that requires a person under arrest to be brought before a judge or court, to protect against unlawful imprisonment

Examples

Habeas corpus order
After months in jail, he was finally released following a successful habeas corpus hearing.
The court granted habeas corpus and ordered the authorities to justify the imprisonment.
The lawyer filed a habeas corpus petition to challenge the detention of her client.
After months without trial, she was finally granted habeas corpus by the court.
Lawyers filed a habeas corpus petition to secure his immediate release.
The prisoner requested a habeas corpus to challenge the legality of his detention.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: detention - the act of keeping someone in custody
  • synonym: writ - a formal written order issued by a body with administrative or judicial jurisdiction
