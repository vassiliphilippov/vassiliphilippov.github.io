film advance

noun
mechanism for moving film to the next frame
3.6
Pronunciation: / fɪlm ədˈvæns / (AmE), / fɪlm ədˈvɑːns / (BrE)
a mechanism in a camera or projector that moves the film to the next frame after each exposure or projection

Examples

The film advance mechanism
If the film advance is broken, the camera cannot function properly.
Many vintage cameras use a manual film advance lever to prepare for the next shot.
The film advance jammed, preventing him from taking another photograph.
