editorial section

noun
part of a publication featuring opinions
Pronunciation: / ˌɛdɪˈtɔriəl ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛdɪˈtɔːriəl ˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the part of a newspaper or magazine where opinions or commentary on current events are published

Examples

The editorial section page
Debates about public health were especially prominent in last Sunday's editorial section.
She always turned to the editorial section for diverse perspectives on world events.
The newspaper's editorial section featured a powerful critique of government policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: news section - section of a publication presenting factual news reports
  • synonym: op-ed - page of a newspaper opposite the editorial page, typically for opinion pieces
