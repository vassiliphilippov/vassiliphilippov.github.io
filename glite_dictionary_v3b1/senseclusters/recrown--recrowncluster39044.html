recrown

verb
to replace dental crown
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkraʊn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkraʊn / (BrE)
to replace or fit a new crown on a tooth, especially after removing an old dental crown

Examples

To recrown a tooth
The dentist decided to recrown the damaged tooth to restore its function.
After the old crown broke, she needed to have the tooth recrowned as soon as possible.
It is common to recrown a tooth if the original crown becomes loose or worn out.
