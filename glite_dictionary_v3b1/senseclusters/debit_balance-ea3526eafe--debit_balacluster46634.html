debit balance

noun
account balance where debits exceed credits
4.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdeb.ɪt ˈbæl.əns/ (AmE), /ˈdeb.ɪt ˈbæl.əns/ (BrE)
A balance in an account where the total debits exceed the total credits, indicating that the account holder owes money.

Examples

A debit balance in account
After the recent transactions, his account showed a debit balance of $200.
If you have a debit balance, it means you owe money to the bank.
The financial statement revealed a significant debit balance at the end of the quarter.
