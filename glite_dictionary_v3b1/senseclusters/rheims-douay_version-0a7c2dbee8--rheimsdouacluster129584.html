Rheims-Douay Version

noun
English Bible translation from the Vulgate
Pronunciation: / riːmzˈ duːˌeɪ ˈvɜːrʒən / (AmE), / riːmzˈ duːˌeɪ ˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
an English translation of the Bible made from the Latin Vulgate by Roman Catholic scholars in the late 16th and early 17th centuries

Examples

The Rheims-Douay Version Bible
Many Catholics read passages from the Rheims-Douay Version during church services.
Scholars often compare the Rheims-Douay Version with other English translations to study doctrinal differences.
The language of the Rheims-Douay Version is notable for its faithfulness to the Latin Vulgate.
