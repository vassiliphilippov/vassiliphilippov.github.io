Atlantic cod

noun
a commercially important North Atlantic fish
1.6
Pronunciation: / əˈtlæn.tɪk kɑd / (AmE), / ətˌlæn.tɪk kɒd / (BrE)
a large marine fish (Gadus morhua) of the North Atlantic, valued as a major source of food and commercially important for its white, flaky flesh

Examples

The Atlantic cod fish
Fishing communities relied heavily on the Atlantic cod for their livelihoods.
Grilled Atlantic cod is a popular dish in many seafood restaurants.
The population of Atlantic cod declined sharply due to overfishing in the twentieth century.
