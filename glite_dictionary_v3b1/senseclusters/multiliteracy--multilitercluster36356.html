multiliteracy

noun
multiple communication skills
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈlɪt̬ɚəsi / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈlɪtərəsi / (BrE)
the ability or educational approach to understand and use multiple forms of communication and media, such as text, images, digital, and audio

Examples

Multiliteracy skills development
Modern classrooms often emphasize multiliteracy to prepare students for a variety of communication challenges.
The teacher designed lessons that promote multiliteracy by including videos, podcasts, and written texts.
Developing multiliteracy helps students understand and create messages in both digital and traditional formats.
Teachers are incorporating multiliteracy into their lessons to help students navigate digital information.
The concept of multiliteracy recognizes that communication goes beyond just reading and writing.
Her research in multiliteracy examines how social media shapes adolescents' writing practices.
Teachers are encouraged to foster multiliteracy so students can succeed in a digital world.
The concept of multiliteracy includes understanding images, videos, and online content as well as written text.
Modern workplaces value multiliteracy because employees must communicate across various platforms and media.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digital literacy - the ability to use digital technology and understand digital information
  • degree lower: literacy - the ability to read and write
  • synonym: media literacy - the ability to access, analyze, and create media in various forms
