bootjack

noun
device for removing boots
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌdʒæk / (BrE)
A device with a V-shaped notch used for pulling off boots.

Examples

A bootjack tool
He placed the heel of his boot in the bootjack and easily pulled it off.
The old farmhouse still had a wooden bootjack by the front door.
Using a bootjack saves effort when removing muddy boots after a long day outside.

Related Terms
