the line in the distance where the earth and sky seem to meet, or the imaginary circle in astronomy where the sky appears to touch the earth

Examples

The sea horizon
At sea, the apparent meeting of sky and water closely matches the astronomical horizon.
In astronomy, the horizon is defined as the circle on the celestial sphere that passes through the observer's zenith and nadir.
Navigators use the horizon as a reference when measuring the altitude of stars above the Earth's surface.
As the sun set, the orange glow spread across the horizon.
Clouds gathered on the horizon, signaling an approaching storm.
From the mountain peak, the horizon seemed endless and clear.

Synonyms

  • visible horizon
    4.1 0.00/m
    visible horizon is the clear line you see where the earth meets the sky from your viewpoint, while horizon is the general boundary.
  • apparent horizon
    6.7 0.01/m
    apparent horizon is the circle seen from one point in astronomy where the sky seems to meet the earth, while horizon is the general line.
  • sensible horizon
    7.6 0.00/m
    sensible horizon is the line you actually see where sea or land meets the sky, while horizon is the general or technical name.

Related Terms
