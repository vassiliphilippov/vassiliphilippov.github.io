go over someone's head

verb
be beyond someone's understanding
8.1
Pronunciation: /goʊ ˈoʊ.vər ˈsʌm.wʌnz hed/ (AmE), /gəʊ ˈəʊ.və ˈsʌm.wʌnz hed/ (BrE)
to be too difficult, technical, or abstract for someone to understand; to be beyond someone's comprehension.

Examples

The lesson went over my head
That technical memo will go over most staff's heads because it's full of legal jargon.
When he explained the algorithm, much of it went over his head and he couldn't apply it.
The professor kept going over the students' heads with complex theory, losing many from class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baffle - to confuse someone so that they cannot understand or explain something
  • antonym: understand - to grasp the meaning, significance, or nature of something
