electromagnetic interaction

noun
force between charged particles in physics
5.9 6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊmæɡˌnɛtɪk ˌɪntərˈækʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmæɡˌnɛtɪk ˌɪntərˈækʃən / (BrE)
The fundamental physical force that acts between charged particles, intermediate in strength between the strong and weak forces, and mediated by photons.

Examples

The electromagnetic interaction force
In quantum physics, the electromagnetic interaction is mediated by the exchange of photons.
The electromagnetic interaction is responsible for the forces between electric charges.
Without the electromagnetic interaction, atoms and molecules could not form stable structures.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gravitational interaction - the fundamental force of attraction between masses
  • degree higher: strong interaction - the fundamental force responsible for holding the nuclei of atoms together
  • degree lower: weak interaction - the fundamental force responsible for radioactive decay and neutrino interactions
