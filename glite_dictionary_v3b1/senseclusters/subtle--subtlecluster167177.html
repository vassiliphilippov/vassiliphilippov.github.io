subtle

adjective
able to make fine distinctions
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌt.əl / (BrE)
able to notice or understand fine differences or distinctions; characterized by keen or nuanced perceptiveness

Examples

Subtle mind
His subtle understanding of literature allows him to appreciate the deeper meanings in poems.
She has a subtle mind that catches the smallest nuances in arguments.
The detective's subtle approach to questioning revealed the truth without alarming anyone.
