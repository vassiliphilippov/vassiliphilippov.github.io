nonnormality

noun
not being normal (math)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌnɔrˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌnɔːˈmæləti / (BrE)
the property of a subgroup in group theory not being normal, meaning it is not invariant under conjugation by group elements

Examples

Nonnormality of a subgroup
The nonnormality of the subgroup prevented the construction of a simple quotient group.
Mathematicians often check for nonnormality when analyzing the structure of groups.
Because of its nonnormality, the subgroup did not have the expected symmetry properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normality - the property of a subgroup being invariant under conjugation in group theory
