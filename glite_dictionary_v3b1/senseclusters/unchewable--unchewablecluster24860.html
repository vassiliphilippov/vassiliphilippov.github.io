unchewable

adjective
impossible or very difficult to chew
4.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈtʃuː.ə.bəl/ /; / /ə.ˈtʃuː.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈtʃuː.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
too tough or difficult to chew; often referring to food, especially meat, that cannot be eaten because of its texture

Examples

An unchewable steak
After hours of cooking, the roast was still unchewable and stringy.
I had to spit out the unchewable piece of meat because it was impossible to swallow.
The steak they served me was practically unchewable due to all the gristle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tender - easy to cut or chew; soft (especially of meat)
  • synonym: tough - food that is difficult to cut or chew because it is hard or sinewy
