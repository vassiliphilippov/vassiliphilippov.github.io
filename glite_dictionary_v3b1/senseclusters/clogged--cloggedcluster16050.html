clogged

adjective
thickened or stuck together in lumps
3.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / klÉ‘gd / (AmE), / klÉ’gd / (BrE)
(of a substance) thickened or stuck together in soft, thick lumps; coalesced into a mass or clot.

Examples

Clogged gravy
After standing for hours, the gravy was clogged and unappetizing.
The drain became clogged with wet leaves after the storm.
The printer jammed because the ink nozzles were clogged with dried pigment.

Synonyms

  • clotted
    5.3 0.11/m
    clotted describes a liquid that has formed soft, thick lumps, while clogged refers to a substance stuck together as a mass.
  • coagulated
    7.0 0.00/m
    coagulated means a liquid has changed into a thicker, more solid mass, while clogged suggests matter is stuck together.
  • clotty
    8.2 0.01/m
    clotty describes something full of small clots or lumps, while clogged describes a substance that has coalesced into a mass.
  • clumpy
    4.7 0.18/m
    clumpy means uneven and lumpy in texture, often for solids, while clogged refers to thick masses or clots in a substance.
