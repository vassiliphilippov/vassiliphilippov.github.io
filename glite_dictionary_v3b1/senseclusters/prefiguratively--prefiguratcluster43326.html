prefiguratively

adverb
in a foreshadowing way
Pronunciation: / priːˈfɪɡjərətɪvli / (AmE), / priːˈfɪɡjʊrətɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that foreshadows or anticipates something before it happens

Examples

Used prefiguratively in art
The artist prefiguratively included symbols that hinted at the revolution to come.
Her speech prefiguratively addressed issues that would later become central to the debate.
The film's opening scene prefiguratively sets the tone for the tragic events that follow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prophetically - in a way that predicts or foretells the future
  • antonym: retrospectively - in a way that looks back on or reviews the past
