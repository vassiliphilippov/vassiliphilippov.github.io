galley

noun
ancient oar-powered warship
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæli / (AmE), / ˈɡali /; / ˈɡæli / (BrE)
an ancient or medieval ship propelled mainly by oars, sometimes with sails, used for war or trade

Examples

Ancient galley ship
A well-trained crew was essential for maneuvering a galley during battle in ancient times.
Historians found drawings of a large galley on the walls of the ruins.
The Roman fleet advanced with each galley cutting smoothly through the waves.
Artifacts from a sunken galley revealed much about ancient maritime warfare.
Several hundred men were needed to row a medieval galley into battle.
The Ottoman navy sailed a powerful galley across the Mediterranean during the 16th century.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: frigate - a fast, medium-sized warship
  • synonym: trireme - an ancient Greek or Roman war galley with three banks of oars
