deter

verb
discourage by persuasion or warning
5.6 8.09/m

to discourage or prevent someone from doing something by making them fear bad consequences

Examples

To deter bad actions
Negative reviews may deter customers from trying the new restaurant.
Strict laws can deter people from committing crimes.
The heavy rain did not deter us from going on our hike.
High fines are intended to deter illegal dumping in the park.
The government hopes the new law will deter people from texting while driving.
The presence of security cameras helped deter theft in the store.

Synonyms

  • discourage
    3.5 21/m
    discourage makes someone less confident or hopeful about doing something, while deter makes someone avoid it by fearing bad consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discourage - to try to prevent someone from doing something
  • synonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something
  • antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone (opposite of deter)
