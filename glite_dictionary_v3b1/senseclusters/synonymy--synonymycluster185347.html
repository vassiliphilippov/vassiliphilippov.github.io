synonymy

noun
the quality of being synonymous
5.2 7.2
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈnɑː.nə.miː/ /; / /sɪ.ˈnɑː.nɪ.miː/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈnɒn.ə.miː/ /; / /sɪ.ˈnɒn.ɪ.miː/ (BrE)
the semantic relationship between two words or expressions that have the same or nearly the same meaning in some or all contexts

Examples

Synonymy in language
Linguists study synonymy to better understand how language develops and changes.
Perfect synonymy is rare, as few words have exactly the same meaning in all contexts.
The synonymy between 'begin' and 'commence' can be seen in many dictionaries.
