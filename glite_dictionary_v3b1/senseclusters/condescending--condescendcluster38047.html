condescending

adjective
having a superior, patronizing attitude
7.5 5.9
Pronunciation: /kən.dɪ.ˈsend.ɪŋ/ /; / /kɑːn.dɪ.ˈsend.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /kɒn.dɪ.ˈsend.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
Displaying a superior attitude by behaving as if one is better or more important than others; showing condescension.

Examples

A condescending tone
He spoke condescendingly to the interns, assuming they knew nothing.
Her condescending smile made everyone feel uncomfortable.
It was difficult to ignore his condescending tone during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humble - showing a modest or low view of one's own importance
  • synonym: patronizing - acting with an apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
