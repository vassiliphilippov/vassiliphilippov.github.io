condescending

adjective
having a superior, patronizing attitude
7.4 3.19/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀên.d…™Ààsend.…™≈ã / (AmE), / Àåk…ín.d…™Ààsend.…™≈ã / (BrE)
Displaying a superior attitude by behaving as if one is better or more important than others; showing condescension.

Examples

A condescending tone
He spoke condescendingly to the interns, assuming they knew nothing.
Her condescending smile made everyone feel uncomfortable.
It was difficult to ignore his condescending tone during the meeting.

Synonyms

  • patronizing
    7.7 1.29/m
    patronizing sounds politely helpful but hides a feeling of superiority, while condescending openly acts as if one is better than others.
  • supercilious
    8.9 0.39/m
    supercilious shows a proud, disdainful look or tone and sounds formal, while condescending focuses on treating others as inferior.
  • haughty
    8.1 1.33/m
    haughty behaves with proud contempt toward others, often in social manners, while condescending treats people as less important.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humble - showing a modest or low view of one's own importance
  • synonym: patronizing - acting with an apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
