Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

proper noun
ancient monumental tomb in Halicarnassus
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔː.səˈliː.əm æt ˌhæl.ɪ.kɑːrˈnæs.əs / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.səˈliː.əm ət ˌhæl.ɪ.kɑːˈnæs.əs / (BrE)
An ancient white marble tomb built in 352 BC at Halicarnassus (present-day Bodrum, Turkey) as a memorial to King Mausolus; known as one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and destroyed in 1402.

Examples

Mausoleum at Halicarnassus in Turkey
Historians often include the Mausoleum at Halicarnassus in lists of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Many tourists visit Bodrum, Turkey, to see the site where the Mausoleum at Halicarnassus once stood.
The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus was regarded as an architectural marvel in the ancient world.
