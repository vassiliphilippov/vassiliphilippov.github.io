Boletus subvelutipes

noun
a species of bolete fungus with a velvety stalk
Pronunciation: / boʊˈliːtəs ˌsʌb.vɛˈluː.təˌpiːz / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs ˌsʌb.vɛˈluː.tɪ.piːz / (BrE)
A species of bolete fungus distinguished by a velvety stalk, usually a dingy brown cap, and rapidly blue-staining flesh when injured.

Examples

Boletus subvelutipes mushroom
Mushroom foragers are cautioned that Boletus subvelutipes can be confused with similar-looking toxic species.
The Boletus subvelutipes is easily identified by the blue stains that appear instantly on bruised flesh.
The velvety stem of Boletus subvelutipes distinguishes it from many other boletes in the forest.
