Senna alexandrina

noun
a shrub used for medicinal laxative purposes
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnə ˌælɪgˈzændrɪnə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnə ˌælɪgˈzændrɪnə / (BrE)
an erect shrub with racemes of tawny yellow flowers, whose dried leaves are used medicinally as a laxative; sometimes classified under the genus Cassia

Examples

Senna alexandrina plant
Many herbal remedies list Senna alexandrina as an active ingredient for its laxative effect.
Prior to brewing the tea, she checked that the Senna alexandrina leaves were prepared correctly.
The dried leaves of Senna alexandrina are traditionally used to relieve constipation.
