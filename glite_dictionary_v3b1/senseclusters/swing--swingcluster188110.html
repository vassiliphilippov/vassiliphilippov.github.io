swing

verb
influence decisively
Pronunciation: / swɪŋ / (AmE), / swɪŋ / (BrE)
to influence someone or something decisively, especially so as to change an opinion, result, or outcome

Examples

To swing the vote
A last-minute offer helped swing the negotiations to their side.
Her speech managed to swing several undecided voters in her favor.
Strong evidence can often swing a jury's verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convince - to cause someone to believe something or do something
  • synonym: sway - to persuade or influence someone
