Taíno

proper noun
Caribbean indigenous people
Pronunciation: / taɪˈiːnoʊ / (AmE), / taɪˈiːnəʊ / (BrE)
an indigenous people of the Caribbean, especially present in the Greater Antilles before European colonization

Examples

Taíno people
The Taíno developed advanced agricultural techniques in the Caribbean islands.
Artifacts from the Taíno culture can be found in museums throughout the region.
Many place names in the Caribbean come from the Taíno language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arawak - a group of indigenous peoples of South America and the Caribbean
