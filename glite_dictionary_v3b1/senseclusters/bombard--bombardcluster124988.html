bombard

verb
attack with persistent requests or questions
5.6 2.37/m
Pronunciation: / b…ëmÀàb…ërd / (AmE), / b…ímÀàb…ëÀêd / (BrE)
to direct a continuous stream of questions, requests, or other messages at someone, often in an overwhelming way

Examples

To bombard with questions
Journalists bombarded the politician with questions as she left the building.
My inbox was bombarded with promotional emails after I signed up for the newsletter.
The teacher was bombarded with complaints after announcing the new policy.

Synonyms

  • barrage
    7.8 0.41/m
    a barrage sends a heavy, continuous stream of questions or criticisms at someone, while bombard means nonstop messages that overwhelm.
  • besiege
    7.8 0.83/m
    besiege surrounds someone with many requests and attention, while bombard focuses on sending a nonstop flood of messages at them.
  • deluge
    7.4 0.49/m
    deluge floods someone with a large amount of tasks or information, while bombard means targeting them with continuous messages or questions.
  • badger
    4.7 1.19/m
    badger nags or pressures someone with repeated requests or questions, while bombard implies an intense, steady stream that overwhelms.
  • pester
    3.2 1.73/m
    pester bothers someone again and again with small requests or interruptions, while bombard means hitting them with a heavy, continuous stream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrage - to overwhelm with a large amount of something, especially questions or criticism
  • degree lower: pester - to trouble or annoy someone with frequent or persistent requests or interruptions
