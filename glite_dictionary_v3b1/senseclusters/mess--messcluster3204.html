mess

verb
to bother or provoke
Pronunciation: / mɛs / (AmE), / mɛs / (BrE)
to bother, harass, or provoke someone, often to intimidate or annoy them

Examples

To mess with him
If you keep messing with him, he might get really angry.
She warned her brother to stop messing with her things.
They were messing with the new student just to see his reaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harass - to repeatedly bother or trouble someone
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to someone
  • synonym: provoke - to deliberately try to make someone angry or upset
