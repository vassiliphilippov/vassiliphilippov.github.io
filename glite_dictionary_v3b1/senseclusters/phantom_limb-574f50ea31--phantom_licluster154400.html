phantom limb

noun
illusion of limb presence after amputation
Pronunciation: / ˈfæn.təm lɪm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm lɪm / (BrE)
the sensation that an amputated limb is still perceived as present by the person

Examples

A phantom limb feeling
Many amputees report feeling a phantom limb even years after surgery.
Researchers are studying how to treat the discomfort associated with a phantom limb.
The doctor explained the causes of phantom limb pain to her patient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amputation - removal of a limb by surgery
