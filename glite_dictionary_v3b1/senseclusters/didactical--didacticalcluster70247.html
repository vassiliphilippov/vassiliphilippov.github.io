didactical

adjective
excessively instructive, moralizing
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdæktɪkəl / (AmE), / dɪˈdaktɪkəl / (BrE)
Intended to instruct or teach, especially in a manner that is overly moralizing or patronizing.

Examples

A didactical tone
His didactical tone during the presentation annoyed several audience members.
She was praised for her clear, didactical approach to complex scientific topics.
The novel has a didactical bent that some readers find preachy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: didactic - intended to teach, especially with a moral lesson
