Amniota

noun
group of vertebrates with an amnion
Pronunciation: / ˌæm.niˈoʊ.tə / (AmE), / ˌam.niˈəʊ.tə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group of higher vertebrates—including reptiles, birds, and mammals—that possess an amnion during embryonic development.

Examples

Amniota group
All mammals, birds, and reptiles are classified within the Amniota.
The evolution of the Amniota enabled vertebrates to reproduce in terrestrial environments.
The presence of an amnion distinguishes the Amniota from amphibians and fish.
