ruach

noun
Hebrew spirit or breath
Pronunciation: / ˈruːɑx / (AmE), / ˈruːɑx / (BrE)
a Hebrew term meaning spirit, breath, or wind, often used in religious or biblical contexts

Examples

Biblical ruach
The word ruach is often used in the Hebrew Bible to refer to the spirit of God.
In some prayers, people ask for the ruach to guide and inspire them.
Scholars debate the different meanings of ruach in ancient religious texts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breath - the air taken into or expelled from the lungs
  • synonym: spirit - the non-physical part of a person or being
  • synonym: wind - moving air, especially as felt or heard outside
