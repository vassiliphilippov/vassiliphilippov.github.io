stereochemically

adverb
in terms of molecular structure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛrioʊkəˈmɪkli / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəʊkəˈmɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the three-dimensional arrangement of atoms in molecules

Examples

To be stereochemically different
The two drugs are stereochemically different, which affects how they interact with the body.
Although the formulas are identical, the compounds behave stereochemically in unique ways.
Researchers analyzed the molecules stereochemically to understand their biological activity.
