guided missile

noun
controllable rocket-propelled weapon
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪdɪd ˈmɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdɪd ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
A rocket-propelled weapon whose course can be controlled during its flight, typically by radio signals or internal guidance systems.

Examples

A guided missile system
A guided missile can adjust its trajectory to accurately strike moving targets.
Advancements in technology have made guided missiles more precise than ever before.
The military displayed its new guided missile system during the parade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ballistic missile - missile with a fixed trajectory after initial propulsion
