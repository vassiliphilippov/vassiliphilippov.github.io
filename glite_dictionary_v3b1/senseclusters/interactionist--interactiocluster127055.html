interactionist

noun
gene-environment theorist
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that traits or behaviors result from the interaction between genetic and environmental factors

Examples

A interactionist view
The interactionist argued that both nature and nurture shape human personality.
As an interactionist, she emphasized the importance of social and biological influences on behavior.
Many psychologists today take an interactionist approach to understanding mental health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinist - a person who believes events are determined by specific causes, often genes or environment alone
