prefixation

noun
adding a prefix to form a word
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːfɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːfɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation of a word by adding a prefix to the beginning of an existing word or root

Examples

Prefixation of words
In English, prefixation can change the meaning of words, as in 'unhappy' or 'redo'.
Many scientific terms are created through prefixation to indicate position or number.
The process of prefixation is common in both English and other languages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: suffixation - the formation of a word by adding a suffix
