secular

noun
layperson; not clergy
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
A person who is not a member of the clergy or a religious order; a layperson.

Examples

A secular helps
As a secular, he helped manage the parish's administrative affairs.
Only a secular could perform those tasks within the abbey grounds.
The community included both clergy and seculars who supported the church.

Related Terms

  • synonym: layperson - a person who is not a member of the clergy or a specialized profession
