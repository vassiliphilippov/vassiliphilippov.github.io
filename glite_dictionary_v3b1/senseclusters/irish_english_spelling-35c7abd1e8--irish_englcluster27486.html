Irish English spelling

noun
Irish variety of English spelling
7.4 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The system of English spelling conventions as used in Ireland, typically aligning with British spelling and featuring forms such as 'analyse', 'centre', and 'useable'.

Examples

Irish English spelling rules
Children in Irish schools are taught Irish English spelling from an early age.
Many official documents in Ireland follow Irish English spelling conventions.
The word 'colour' is an example of Irish English spelling rather than the American 'color'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: American English spelling - English orthography as used in the United States
  • synonym: British English spelling - English orthography as used in the United Kingdom and most of the Commonwealth
