detain

verb
keep in official custody
3.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈteɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈteɪn / (BrE)
to keep someone in official custody, especially in order to question them about a possible crime or to prevent their departure

Examples

Police detain a suspect
She was detained by customs officials due to a problem with her visa.
The police decided to detain him for further questioning after the incident.
Travelers can be detained at the border if their documents are not in order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imprison - to put or keep in prison or confinement
  • antonym: release - to set free from confinement or custody
