judgment

noun
the ability to make considered decisions
4.8 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt /; / ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt /; / ˈdʒʌdʒm(ə)nt /; / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
the ability to make good decisions or form sensible opinions

Examples

Sound judgment
Good judgment is essential when making important life decisions.
He regretted his poor judgment in trusting someone he barely knew.
Her judgment in choosing the best candidate was highly respected by her colleagues.
Her judgment in choosing investments has always been exceptional.
The company relies on the judgment of its experienced managers when making major decisions.
When safety is concerned, your judgment should never be compromised.
His judgment was respected by everyone in the company.
Making good judgment calls can be difficult in stressful situations.
She trusted her own judgment when choosing her career path.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decision - the process of deciding something, especially after some deliberation
  • synonym: discernment - the ability to judge well, especially in matters of taste or perception
  • antonym: foolishness - lack of good sense or judgment
  • degree lower: intuition - the ability to understand something immediately, without the need for conscious reasoning
  • synonym: wisdom - the quality of having experience, knowledge, and good judgment
