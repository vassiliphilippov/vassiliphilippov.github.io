dashiki

noun
loose, colorful West African shirt
Pronunciation: / dəˈʃiki / (AmE), / dəˈʃiːki / (BrE)
a loose, brightly colored shirt or tunic, originally from West Africa, worn especially by men

Examples

A dashiki shirt
At the wedding, many guests dressed in traditional African attire, including the dashiki.
He wore a brightly patterned dashiki to the cultural festival.
The dashiki has become a symbol of pride and heritage for many in the African diaspora.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kaftan - a long, loose garment with wide sleeves, worn in many Middle Eastern and North African countries
