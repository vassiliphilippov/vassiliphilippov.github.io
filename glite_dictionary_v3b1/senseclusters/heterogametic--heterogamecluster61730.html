heterogametic

adjective
different sex chromosomes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəɡəˈmitɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊɡəˈmiːtɪk / (BrE)
having two different types of sex chromosomes, such as XY in males of many species

Examples

Heterogametic male
In many animals, the male is heterogametic, possessing both X and Y chromosomes.
Birds are different from mammals because the female is heterogametic in their species.
Scientists studied how heterogametic individuals determine the sex of offspring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homogametic - having two of the same kind of sex chromosome
