march

noun
music genre with regular marching rhythm
8.1
Pronunciation: / mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
A piece of music with a strong regular rhythm, written to accompany marching.

Examples

A military march
John Philip Sousa is famous for composing the "Stars and Stripes Forever," a popular march.
Many high school bands learn at least one march for their performances.
The band played a lively march as the parade made its way down the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: military band - a group that often performs marches
  • antonym: waltz - dance music in triple time
