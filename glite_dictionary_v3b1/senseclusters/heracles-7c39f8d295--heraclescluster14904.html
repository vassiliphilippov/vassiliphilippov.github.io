Heracles

proper noun
Greek mythological hero known for great strength

In classical mythology, a hero famed for his strength, who performed twelve immense labors to gain immortality.

Examples

Heracles in mythology
According to Greek mythology, Heracles was the son of Zeus and Alcmene.
Many sculptures depict Heracles holding a club and wearing a lion's skin.
The legend of Heracles and his twelve labors has inspired countless works of art and literature.
