the process of reducing or suppressing a response, activity, or level, especially in emotional, physiological, or behavioral systems

Examples

Downregulation of anxiety
Cognitive behavioral therapy can help with the downregulation of anxiety in stressful situations.
Scientists studied the downregulation of certain genes in response to medication.
Effective downregulation of anger is important for maintaining healthy relationships.

Synonyms

  • inhibition
    6.1 2.16/m
    inhibition is holding back or restraining actions and feelings, while downregulation is a broader physiological or behavioral reduction process.
  • suppression
    6.3 0.69/m
    suppression is a conscious effort to push away unwanted thoughts or feelings, while downregulation can be an automatic physiological or behavioral reduction.
  • damping
    5.3 0.25/m
    damping is a general lowering of intensity in sound or motion, while downregulation refers to a specific biological or behavioral reduction.
