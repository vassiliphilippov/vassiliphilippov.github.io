downregulation

noun
reduction of response
7.5
Pronunciation: /daʊn.reɡ.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /daʊn.reɡ.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of reducing or suppressing a response, activity, or level, especially in emotional, physiological, or behavioral systems

Examples

Downregulation of anxiety
Cognitive behavioral therapy can help with the downregulation of anxiety in stressful situations.
Scientists studied the downregulation of certain genes in response to medication.
Effective downregulation of anger is important for maintaining healthy relationships.
