button-down

adjective
very conventional or conservative
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌtənˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ən.daʊn / (BrE)
(of a person or environment) unimaginatively conventional; very conservative or lacking in originality, especially in appearance or behavior.

Examples

Button-down attitude
Despite his young age, his button-down attitude fit right in with the firm’s culture.
He felt out of place in the button-down environment of corporate finance.
Her button-down approach to problem-solving often stifled creativity in the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - favoring traditional values and resistant to change
  • antonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to what is generally done or believed
