shadow of doubt

noun
feeling or suggestion of uncertainty
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ əv daʊt / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ əv daʊt / (BrE)
a feeling or slight suggestion of uncertainty or suspicion

Examples

A shadow of doubt
Despite the evidence, a shadow of doubt remained in the jury's minds.
He accepted the offer without a shadow of doubt or hesitation.
Years of trust meant she felt no shadow of doubt about his honesty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - the state of being completely confident or sure
  • synonym: doubt - uncertainty; lack of conviction
