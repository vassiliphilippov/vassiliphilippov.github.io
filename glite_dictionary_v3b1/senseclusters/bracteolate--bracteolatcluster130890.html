bracteolate

adjective
having small bracts (bracteoles)
Pronunciation: / brækˈtiːəˌleɪt / (AmE), / brækˈtɪələt / (BrE)
(of a plant or flower) having small bracts (bracteoles) beneath the blossom or inflorescence.

Examples

Bracteolate plants
In some grasses, the spikelets are bracteolate, distinguishing them from related types.
The bracteolate flowers were easily identified by the tiny leaf-like structures beneath the blossom.
This species is notable for its bracteolate inflorescence, which aids in species distinction.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bracteate - having bracts
  • antonym: ebracteolate - lacking bracteoles
