lead

noun
timing of ignition in an engine
Pronunciation: / lid / (AmE), / liːd / (BrE)
the timing of the ignition relative to the position of the piston in an internal-combustion engine, affecting engine performance

Examples

Ignition lead
Engineers measure the lead precisely during engine tuning sessions.
Incorrect lead can cause knocking and reduced performance in older cars.
The mechanic adjusted the lead to improve the engine's efficiency.
