piranha

noun
carnivorous South American freshwater fish
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɑnə / (AmE), / pɪˈrɑːnə / (BrE)
A small, carnivorous freshwater fish of South America, known for its sharp teeth and tendency to attack animals.

Examples

A piranha fish
A swarm of piranhas can strip the flesh from a large animal in minutes.
Fishermen in the Amazon are always wary of piranhas in the river.
She read an article about piranha attacks on local cattle near the water.
