Ockham's Razor

noun
principle preferring simplest explanations
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːkəmz ˈreɪzɚ / (AmE), / ˈɒkəmz ˈreɪzə / (BrE)
the principle that the simplest explanation or theory is preferred over more complex ones when all else is equal

Examples

Ockham's Razor principle
In philosophy, Ockham's Razor encourages avoiding unnecessary assumptions.
Many detective stories use Ockham's Razor to solve mysteries with the simplest solutions.
Scientists often apply Ockham's Razor when choosing between competing hypotheses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parsimony - preference for simplicity and minimal assumptions
