lobe

noun
rounded part of an organ
Pronunciation: / loʊb / (AmE), / ləʊb / (BrE)
a rounded or projecting part of something, such as a section of an organ (like the brain or ear) or a part of a leaf

Examples

Ear lobe
The doctor explained the injury was to the frontal lobe of the brain.
The human ear consists of an outer rim and a fleshy lobe.
The liver has several distinct lobes visible in an anatomical diagram.
She has a small diamond stud in her left earlobe, highlighting the rounded lobe of her ear.
The human brain consists of several lobes that have different functions.
The maple leaf has five distinct lobes separated by deep notches.
The lobe of the oak leaf is rounded rather than sharply angled.
The maple tree is known for its leaves, each with several pointed lobes.
You can distinguish the species by the shape and number of lobes on its leaves.
