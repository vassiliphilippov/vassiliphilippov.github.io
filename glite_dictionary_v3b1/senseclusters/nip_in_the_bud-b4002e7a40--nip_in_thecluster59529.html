nip in the bud

verb
stop something early, before it grows
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪp ɪn ðə bʌd / (AmE), / nɪp ɪn ðə bʌd / (BrE)
to stop something at an early stage before it develops or becomes a bigger problem

Examples

To nip in the bud early
If we nip this conflict in the bud, it won't turn into a crisis.
Parents should nip bad habits in the bud to prevent future problems.
The company tried to nip the rumor in the bud before it spread throughout the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone, often allowing growth or continuation
  • synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening or arising
