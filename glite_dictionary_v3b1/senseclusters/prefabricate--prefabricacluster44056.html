prefabricate

verb
produce or construct in advance, often artificially
Pronunciation: / priˈfræbrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈfræbrɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to produce or construct in advance, especially in a standardized, artificial, or unoriginal way

Examples

To prefabricate feelings
Critics argue that the movie prefabricates emotions, leading to a predictable and unoriginal plot.
Some journalists tend to prefabricate stories instead of relying on genuine research.
The company began to prefabricate its products to increase efficiency and consistency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fabricate - to make or construct, especially with skill
