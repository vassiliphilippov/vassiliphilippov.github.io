phonological system

noun
sound system of a language
Pronunciation: / fəˌnɑːləˈdʒɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / fəˌnɒlɒˈdʒɪkəl ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the set of speech sounds and their patterns used in a particular language

Examples

The phonological system of English
Every language has its own phonological system that governs how sounds are used and combined.
Researchers study the phonological system of a language to understand its unique sound patterns.
The differences in the phonological system explain why some words are difficult for non-native speakers to pronounce.
