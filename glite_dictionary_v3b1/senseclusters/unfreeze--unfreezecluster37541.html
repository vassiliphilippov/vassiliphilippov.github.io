unfreeze

verb
make or become liquid after freezing
3.5 0.10/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàfriÀêz / (AmE), /  ånÀàfriÀêz / (BrE)
to make or become soft or liquid after being frozen; to thaw

Examples

To unfreeze the meat
She left the meat out to unfreeze overnight.
The lake will unfreeze in the spring when the weather gets warmer.
You need to unfreeze the chicken before cooking it.

Synonyms

  • thaw
    1.1 5.58/m
    thaw becomes soft or liquid when warm, while unfreeze refers more broadly to reversing a frozen state.
  • thaw out
    2.3 0.16/m
    thaw out is used for food or things that warm up slowly, while unfreeze names the general act of removing frost.
  • defrost
    1.7 0.89/m
    defrost removes frost or ice from surfaces like windshields or freezers, while unfreeze means to become unfrozen or soft.
  • melt
    1.0 21/m
    melt changes a solid into a liquid, often by heat, while unfreeze means to stop being frozen or to thaw.
  • liquefy
    4.4 0.96/m
    liquefy makes something liquid, often by heat or pressure in technical settings, while unfreeze is about thawing frozen things.
