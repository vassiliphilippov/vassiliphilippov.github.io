retardant

noun
substance that slows or prevents an action/process
3.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɑrdənt / (AmE), / rɪˈtɑːdənt / (BrE)
a substance that slows down or prevents a process, especially burning or other unwanted reactions

Examples

A retardant chemical
Firefighters used a chemical retardant to slow down the spread of the forest fire.
Many fabrics contain a flame retardant to improve safety in the home.
The lab tested a new corrosion retardant to protect metal structures.
Firefighters use a retardant to help control wildfires.
They sprayed a chemical retardant on the crops to slow the ripening process.
This fabric has been treated with a flame retardant for extra safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accelerant - a substance that increases the speed of a process, especially combustion
  • synonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows or prevents a chemical reaction
