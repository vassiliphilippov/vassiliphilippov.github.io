parainfluenza virus

noun
virus causing respiratory infections in children
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˌɪnfluˈɛnzə ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˌparəˌɪnfluˈenzə ˈvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
A virus that causes upper respiratory infections, such as the common cold and bronchiolitis, especially in children.

Examples

The parainfluenza virus infection
Doctors often test for the parainfluenza virus when a patient presents with severe cold symptoms.
Outbreaks of parainfluenza virus infections frequently occur in childcare centers during the winter.
The parainfluenza virus is a common cause of croup in young children.
