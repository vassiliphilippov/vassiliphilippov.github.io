guardhouse

noun
military police building and detention facility
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrdˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a small building where guards are stationed, often at the entrance to a place, sometimes used for detaining people (especially in a military area)

Examples

The guardhouse entrance
Anyone caught breaking curfew was taken to the guardhouse by the military police.
The old guardhouse at the entrance of the base has been restored for historical tours.
The soldiers escorted the prisoner to the guardhouse for questioning.
The main entrance was watched from a small guardhouse beside the gate.
Visitors had to check in at the guardhouse before entering the military base.
A soldier stood outside the guardhouse during the night shift.
The security officer waited inside the guardhouse at the entrance to the factory.
Every visitor must sign in at the guardhouse before entering the military base.
A new guardhouse was built to improve security at the main gate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barracks - a building or group of buildings where soldiers live
  • synonym: checkpoint - a place where people or vehicles are inspected before being allowed to pass
  • synonym: gatehouse - a building at the entrance of a large property or castle, often for guards
  • synonym: guardroom - a room used by military guards while on duty
