charism

noun
spiritual gift
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkærɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkærɪzəm / (BrE)
a special spiritual gift or quality, believed to be given by God, that enables someone to help, lead, or influence others, especially in a religious context

Examples

Healing charism
The priest was known for his unique charism of healing within the community.
Many believe that leadership is a charism granted to certain individuals for the good of the church.
She discovered her charism for teaching and used it to help others grow in faith.
The nun was known for her charism of compassion and service to the poor.
Many followers were drawn to the leader's unique charism and vision.
Each member of the community is encouraged to discover their own charism and use it to help others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charisma - a personal quality that attracts or influences others
  • synonym: gift - a special ability or talent given to someone
  • synonym: talent - a natural ability to do something well
