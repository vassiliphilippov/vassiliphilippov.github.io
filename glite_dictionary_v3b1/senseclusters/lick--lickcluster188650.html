lick

noun
salt or mineral deposit licked by animals
Pronunciation: / lɪk / (AmE), / lɪk / (BrE)
a natural deposit of salt or other minerals that animals frequently visit to lick in order to obtain nutrients

Examples

A salt lick
Deer often gather at a lick in the forest to get minerals missing from their diet.
Many animals travel far distances to visit a lick during the dry season.
The wildlife biologists found a natural lick beside the mountain stream.
