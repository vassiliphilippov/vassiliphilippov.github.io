convocation

noun
formal gathering in response to a summons
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːnvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of people assembled in response to a summons, especially for a formal or official meeting

Examples

A convocation of leaders
A convocation of bishops was called to discuss church reforms.
Delegates from all regions attended the convocation at the capital.
The university held its annual convocation to welcome new students and faculty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together for a common purpose
  • antonym: dispersal - the action or process of distributing things or people over a wide area
