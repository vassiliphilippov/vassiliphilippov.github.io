grasp

verb
understand fully
3.1
Pronunciation: / ɡræsp / (AmE), / ɡrɑːsp / (BrE)
to understand something fully, especially something difficult or complex

Examples

To grasp an idea
After reading the article several times, she finally grasped its main ideas.
Children often grasp new concepts more quickly than adults expect.
It took him a while to grasp the complex theory explained by the professor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comprehend - to understand something completely
  • antonym: miss - to fail to understand or perceive
  • synonym: understand - to perceive the meaning of something
