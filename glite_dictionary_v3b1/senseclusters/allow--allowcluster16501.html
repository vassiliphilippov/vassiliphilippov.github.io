allow

verb
make provision for; concede the possibility

to make provision for the possibility of something; to admit or acknowledge as true or valid

Examples

To allow for mistakes
He allowed that her argument might have some merit.
The design allows for up to 20% expansion due to heat.
We must allow for unexpected expenses in our budget plan.

Synonyms

    3.2 11/m

  • admit
    3.6 6.15/m
    admit usually means accept that something is true, while allow can also mean make provision for a possibility.
  • acknowledge
    3.8 26/m
    acknowledge means openly accept that something is true or exists, while allow can mean make provision or admit.
  • grant
    3.8 20/m
    grant means concede a point or fact often reluctantly, while allow is a more general verb to admit or make provision.
  • accept
    1.3 83/m
    accept means agree that something is true or satisfactory, while allow can mean to make provision or admit a fact.
  • recognize
    1.4 27/m
    recognize means officially accept someone's status or something's validity, while allow is broader and can mean make provision or admit.
