antitrust case

noun
legal case involving competition law
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈtrʌst keɪs / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈtrʌst keɪs / (BrE)
a legal case brought against individuals or companies accused of violating laws that promote competition in the marketplace

Examples

Famous antitrust case
A famous antitrust case in history is the breakup of Standard Oil in the early 20th century.
Lawyers spent months preparing evidence for the antitrust case to prove violations of competition laws.
The government launched an antitrust case against the tech giant, alleging unfair business practices.
