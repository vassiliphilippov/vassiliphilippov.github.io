potato family

noun
family of plants including potato and tomato
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪdoʊ ˈfæmɪli / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants (Solanaceae) that includes herbs, shrubs, and trees, many of which are economically important and sometimes poisonous, such as potatoes, tomatoes, eggplants, peppers, and tobacco

Examples

The potato family plants
Many common vegetables such as tomatoes, peppers, and eggplants belong to the potato family.
Nicotine is found in tobacco plants, which are also part of the potato family.
The potato family includes both edible and toxic plants.
