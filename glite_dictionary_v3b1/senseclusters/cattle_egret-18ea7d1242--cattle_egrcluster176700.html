cattle egret

noun
a small white egret often near cattle
Pronunciation: / ˈkætl ˈiːɡrət / (AmE), / ˈkætl ˈiːɡrɪt / (BrE)
a small white egret (Bubulcus ibis) found in warm regions, often seen around grazing animals feeding on insects disturbed by livestock

Examples

A cattle egret bird
A cattle egret followed the herd, snapping up insects disturbed by the cows' movement.
During their migration, hundreds of cattle egrets were seen resting in the wetlands.
The cattle egret is easily recognized by its white feathers and habit of keeping close to grazing animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: egret - any of several herons with long plumes
