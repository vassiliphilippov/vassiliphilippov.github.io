rush

adjective
done quickly under pressure
3.0 4.1
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ / (AmE), / rʌʃ / (BrE)
(of a job, task, etc.) done quickly and under pressure, often with less care than usual.

Examples

A rush job
She handed in a rush report that was full of errors.
The repairs were only a rush fix until proper work could be done.
They had to do a rush job to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: careful - done with attention and caution, not rushed
  • synonym: hurried - done in a rush or with excessive speed
