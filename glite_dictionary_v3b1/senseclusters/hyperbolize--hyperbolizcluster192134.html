hyperbolize

verb
exaggerate or overstate
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɜrbəˌlaɪz /; / haɪˈpɝːbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜː.bə.laɪz /; / haɪˈpɜːbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to represent something as greater, more important, or more extreme than it really is; to exaggerate

Examples

To hyperbolize a story
Politicians often hyperbolize the effects of their policies to gain public support.
She hyperbolized her vacation experience, making it sound much more exciting than it was.
Try not to hyperbolize when telling the story; just stick to the facts.
He tends to hyperbolise his travel stories to make them more entertaining.
Politicians often hyperbolise the impact of their policies to win public approval.
The news report hyperbolised the severity of the storm, causing unnecessary panic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to magnify beyond the limits of truth; overstate
  • antonym: understate - to represent as less significant or important than is the case
