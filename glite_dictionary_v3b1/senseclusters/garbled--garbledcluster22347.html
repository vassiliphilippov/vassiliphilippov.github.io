garbled

adjective
lacking clear, logical order; jumbled
3.6 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈgɑːr.bəld/ (AmE), /ˈgɑː.bəld/ (BrE)
(of information, speech, or writing) lacking clear, logical order; confused or distorted so that it is hard to understand.

Examples

A garbled message
Because of the bad phone connection, her message was garbled and I couldn't understand what she said.
He tried to explain the process, but the instructions came out garbled, leaving everyone confused.
The recording was so poor that the entire conversation sounded garbled and impossible to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coherent - logical and consistent
  • synonym: confused - lacking clear order or understanding
