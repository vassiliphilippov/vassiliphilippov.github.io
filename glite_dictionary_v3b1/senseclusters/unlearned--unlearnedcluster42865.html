unlearned

adjective
not acquired by learning; innate
1.0 5.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɜrnɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɜːnɪd / (BrE)
(of a response or behavior) not acquired through experience, instruction, or conditioning; innate or instinctive.

Examples

Unlearned behavior
Many animal instincts are considered unlearned behaviors vital to their survival.
The baby's cry is an unlearned response to discomfort.
Yawning is often cited as an unlearned reflex in humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired - developed or learned; not innate
  • synonym: innate - existing naturally rather than acquired by learning
