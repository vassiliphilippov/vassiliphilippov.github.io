indefectible

adjective
incapable of failing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈfɛktəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈfɛktɪbəl / (BrE)
not able to fail, decay, or be destroyed; always remaining true or unchanged

Examples

An indefectible truth
The ancient philosophers spoke of an indefectible truth that would never change.
Many religions claim their core beliefs are indefectible and will endure forever.
She admired the indefectible loyalty of her closest friends.
Many theologians argue that the Church is indefectible and will always preserve its core teachings.
The constitution was designed to be indefectible against the pressures of political change.
Some traditions are considered indefectible, surviving for centuries without alteration.
