degermed

adjective
without germ (grain)
7.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈdʒɜːrmd/ /; / /diː.ˈdʒɜːrmd/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈdʒɜːmd/ /; / /diː.ˈdʒɜːmd/ (BrE)
having had the germ or embryo removed, especially from grain or seeds

Examples

Degermed corn cereal
Many breakfast cereals are made from degermed corn to create a smoother texture.
The flour is degermed to extend its shelf life and prevent spoilage.
Some people prefer degermed grains for baking because they produce lighter bread.
