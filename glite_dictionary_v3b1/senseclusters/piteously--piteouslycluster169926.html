piteously

adverb
in a manner evoking pity
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtiəsli / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪəsli / (BrE)
In a way that arouses pity or compassion; in a pitiful or sorrowful manner.

Examples

To piteously beg
He piteously begged for mercy, tears streaming down his cheeks.
She looked piteously at her teacher, silently pleading for a second chance.
The stray dog cried piteously outside the door, hoping someone would let it in.
