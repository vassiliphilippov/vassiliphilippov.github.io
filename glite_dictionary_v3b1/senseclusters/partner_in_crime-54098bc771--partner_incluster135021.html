partner in crime

noun
close companion involved in shared activities
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑɹtnɚ ɪn ˈkɹaɪm /; / ˈpɑːrtnər ɪn kraɪm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtnə ɪn ˈkɹaɪm /; / ˈpɑːtnə ɪn kraɪm / (BrE)
a close friend or companion with whom you do fun, mischievous, or adventurous things (often used humorously)

Examples

My partner in crime
I always go on adventures with my partner in crime Jenny.
My brother has been my partner in crime since childhood, getting into all sorts of mischief.
Who will be your partner in crime for the school project this year?
Ever since we met in college, Sarah has been my partner in crime for every crazy adventure.
He jokingly referred to his coworker as his partner in crime after they finished the project together.
On family holidays, my cousin and I were always partners in crime when it came to sneaking snacks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime or wrongdoing
  • synonym: collaborator - a person who works together with another, especially on something secret or illegal
  • synonym: conspirator - a person who takes part in a secret plan to do something unlawful or harmful
  • antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile
