osteoblastoma

noun
benign bone tumor
Pronunciation: / ˌɑsti.oʊˌblæˈstoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɒsti.əʊˌblæˈstəʊmə / (BrE)
A rare, benign tumor of bone and fibrous tissue, typically occurring in the spine or long bones, especially in young adults.

Examples

An osteoblastoma tumor
Osteoblastoma most often affects young adults and develops in the bones of the spine or legs.
The surgeon diagnosed the swelling as an osteoblastoma after reviewing the biopsy results.
Treatment for an osteoblastoma may involve surgical removal of the tumor.
