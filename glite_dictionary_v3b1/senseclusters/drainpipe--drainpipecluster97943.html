drainpipe

noun
pipe for carrying away liquid or waste
Pronunciation: / ˈdreɪnˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈdreɪnˌpʌɪp / (BrE)
a pipe that carries liquid, especially waste water, away from a building

Examples

A drainpipe on a house
He climbed up the drainpipe to reach the second-floor window.
The drainpipe was clogged with leaves after the storm.
Water poured out of the drainpipe and onto the driveway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downspout - a pipe for carrying rainwater from a gutter down the side of a building
