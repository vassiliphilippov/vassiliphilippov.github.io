nuclear physicist

noun
expert in nuclear physics
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliɚ fɪˈsɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliə fɪˈsɪsɪst / (BrE)
A scientist who studies or specializes in nuclear physics, the branch of physics concerned with atomic nuclei and their interactions.

Examples

nuclear physicist studies atoms
After obtaining his doctorate, he took a job as a nuclear physicist at a leading research laboratory.
Marie Curie is often regarded as a pioneering nuclear physicist for her work on radioactivity.
The nuclear physicist explained the process of nuclear fission to the students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nuclear engineer - an engineer who works with nuclear technology
  • degree lower: physicist - a scientist who is trained in physics
