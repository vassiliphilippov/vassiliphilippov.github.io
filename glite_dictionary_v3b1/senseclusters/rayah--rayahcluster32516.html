rayah

noun
Ottoman lower class
9.9
Pronunciation: / raɪˈɑː / (AmE), / raɪˈɑː / (BrE)
a non-privileged, tax-paying subject (especially a non-Muslim) in the Ottoman Empire

Examples

Ottoman rayah
In the Ottoman Empire, the rayah were required to pay taxes to the ruling class.
The sultan's policies often affected the daily lives of the rayah across the provinces.
Historical records describe the rayah as the backbone of the empire's agricultural economy.
Under Ottoman law, a rayah had to pay a special tax not required of Muslims.
The rayah communities often lived in separate quarters within the city.
Historical records show that many rayah worked as farmers and craftsmen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dhimmi - a non-Muslim living under Muslim rule with protected status
  • antonym: elite - a group of people with the highest status or privilege
  • synonym: peasant - a member of the lower social class, often a farmer or laborer
