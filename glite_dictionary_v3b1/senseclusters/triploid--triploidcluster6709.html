triploid

noun
organism with three chromosome sets
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtrɪplɔɪd / (BrE)
an organism or cell that has three complete sets of chromosomes instead of the usual two

Examples

A triploid organism
The scientist studied the genetic traits of the triploid plants.
Some fish species are bred as triploid to prevent them from reproducing in the wild.
A triploid cell contains three sets of chromosomes, which can affect its development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diploid - an organism or cell with two complete sets of chromosomes
  • synonym: polyploid - an organism or cell with more than two sets of chromosomes
