overbroad

adjective
too general
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbrɔd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbrɔːd / (BrE)
too general or not sufficiently specific, especially in rules, laws, or statements

Examples

An overbroad rule
The court found the regulation overbroad because it restricted more activities than necessary.
Critics argued that the policy was overbroad and could affect innocent people.
An overbroad statement can lead to misunderstandings and confusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: narrow - limited in scope or extent
  • antonym: specific - clearly defined or identified
  • synonym: vague - not clearly expressed or defined
