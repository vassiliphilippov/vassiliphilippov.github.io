reimplantation

noun
restoring body part
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪmˌplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˌplænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of putting something, especially a body part or tissue, back into its original place after removal or loss

Examples

Successful reimplantation surgery
The surgeon successfully completed the reimplantation of the patient's severed finger.
After the accident, immediate reimplantation of the tooth increased the chances of recovery.
Advances in microsurgery have improved the outcomes of reimplantation procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extraction - the act of removing something, especially a body part or tissue
  • synonym: transplantation - the process of moving tissue or an organ from one body to another
