homographic

adjective
same spelling, different meaning
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊməˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɒməˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
having the same written form as another word but a different meaning or pronunciation

Examples

Homographic words
The word 'tear' is homographic because it can mean to rip or a drop from the eye.
English contains many homographic words that can confuse learners.
In the sentence, 'He will lead the team with a bar of lead,' both uses are homographic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homograph - a word spelled the same as another but with a different meaning or pronunciation
  • antonym: homophonic - having the same pronunciation as another word but a different spelling or meaning
