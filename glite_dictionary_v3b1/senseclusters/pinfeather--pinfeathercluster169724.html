pinfeather

noun
immature feather
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪnˌfɛðər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌfɛðə / (BrE)
a small, new feather on a young bird that is still covered by a thin sheath

Examples

Young bird's pinfeathers
The young bird's wings were covered in soft pinfeathers as it started to grow.
Care must be taken when handling chicks with pinfeathers to avoid damaging their new feathers.
As the pinfeather matures, the sheath will fall away and reveal the full feather.
The young parrot's wings were covered in pinfeathers before its adult feathers appeared.
As the chick grew, its pinfeathers slowly broke through the protective sheaths.
Farmers check for pinfeathers when preparing poultry for market.
