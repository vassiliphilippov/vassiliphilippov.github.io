misplay

verb
play incorrectly or unskillfully
6.0 5.0
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈpleɪ/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈpleɪ/ (BrE)
to play or perform something incorrectly or in an unskillful way, especially in games, sports, or music

Examples

To misplay a move
During the chess match, she misplayed her queen and lost her advantage.
He misplayed the ball and gave the opposition a clear chance to score.
The team misplayed several crucial points in the final set.
If you misplay your cards, you can lose the entire hand.
She misplayed the ball, giving the other team an easy goal.
The pianist accidentally misplayed a note during the concert.

Related Terms

  • antonym: play correctly - to perform or play without error
  • antonym: play well - to play in a skilled or effective manner
