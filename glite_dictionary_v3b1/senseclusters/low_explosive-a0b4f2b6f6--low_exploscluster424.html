low explosive

noun
explosive that burns slowly
Pronunciation: / loʊ ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ləʊ ɪkˈspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
An explosive material that burns at a relatively slow rate rather than detonating, producing a subsonic explosion.

Examples

A low explosive material
A low explosive burns by deflagration rather than detonation, producing a slower energy release.
Because it cannot detonate, a low explosive is considered safer for certain industrial applications.
Black powder is an example of a low explosive commonly used in fireworks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high explosive - an explosive with a rapid detonation rate
