post office

noun
building or office for mail and postal services
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /poʊst ˈɔː.fəs/ /; / /poʊst ˈɑː.fɪs/ (AmE), /pəʊst ˈɒf.ɪs/ /; / /pəʊst ˈɒf.əs/ (BrE)
a public building or office where mail is received, sorted, and sent out, and where postal services such as selling stamps and handling packages are provided.

Examples

At the post office counter
I went to the post office to send a package overseas.
She collects stamps from every post office she visits during her travels.
The local post office closes at 5 p.m. every weekday.
