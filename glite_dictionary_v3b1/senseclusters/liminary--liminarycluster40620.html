liminary

adjective
introductory; preliminary
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪməˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɪmɪnəri / (BrE)
serving as an introduction or preface; preliminary (archaic)

Examples

Liminary note
The author included a liminary note to explain the purpose of the work.
A liminary chapter set the stage for the historical events described in the book.
In older texts, a liminary section often introduced the main arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concluding - ending or finishing something
  • synonym: prefatory - serving as a preface or introduction
  • synonym: preliminary - coming before the main part; introductory
