chain gang

noun
group of chained prisoners forced to work
3.9 8.8
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn ɡæŋ / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ɡaŋ / (BrE)
a group of prisoners chained together to perform physically demanding work as a form of punishment, especially in the past.

Examples

Chain gangs of prisoners
In the early twentieth century, chain gangs were common in the American South.
The film depicted the harsh conditions faced by members of a chain gang.
The prisoners were assigned to a chain gang to clear debris from the road.
