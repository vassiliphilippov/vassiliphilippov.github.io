Puck

noun
mischievous sprite in English folklore
Pronunciation: / pʌk / (AmE), / pʌk / (BrE)
A mischievous sprite or fairy in English folklore, known for playing pranks and tricks; famously portrayed in Shakespeare's 'A Midsummer Night's Dream'.

Examples

Mischievous Puck sprite
In folklore, Puck was notorious for leading travelers astray in the woods.
Shakespeare's rendition of Puck is both mischievous and charming in 'A Midsummer Night's Dream'.
Some believe that Puck is responsible for playful household tricks and minor accidents.
