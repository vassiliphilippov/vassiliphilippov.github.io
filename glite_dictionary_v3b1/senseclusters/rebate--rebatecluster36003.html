rebate

verb
cut a groove for joining material
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌbeɪt /; / ˈriˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈriːbeɪt / (BrE)
to cut a groove or recess in the edge of wood, stone, or other material so that another piece can fit into it

Examples

To rebate wood
Masons skillfully rebate stone blocks for a seamless joint.
The carpenter will rebate the edges of the door to ensure a snug fit.
You need to rebate the timber before assembling the window frame.
Before fitting the frame, be sure to rebate both sides of the timber.
Stonemasons often rebate the blocks to allow them to interlock securely.
The carpenter used a special tool to rebate the edge of the door for the glass panel.
