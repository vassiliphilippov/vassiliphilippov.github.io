nominative

adjective
appointed by nomination
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑm.ə.nə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈnɒm.ɪ.nə.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a person or position) appointed or designated by nomination rather than by election or another process.

Examples

Nominative member
She was appointed to the committee as a nominative member by the minister.
The governor has the power to fill certain offices through nominative appointments.
The position of chairperson was filled on a nominative basis, not by election.
