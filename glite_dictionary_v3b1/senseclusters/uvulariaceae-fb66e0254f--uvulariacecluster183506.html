Uvulariaceae

noun
former plant family, now split
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˌvjuːlɛriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / juːˌvjuːlɛriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a former family of flowering plants once included in some classification systems under the lily family, now split among several families including the autumn crocus and lily families

Examples

The Uvulariaceae family
Some botanists still refer to Uvulariaceae in historical contexts when discussing plant taxonomy.
The classification of certain woodland perennials has changed since the Uvulariaceae were split into different families.
The plants once grouped under Uvulariaceae share certain morphological traits such as bell-shaped flowers.
