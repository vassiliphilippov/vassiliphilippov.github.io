tell apart

verb
distinguish between; differentiate
Pronunciation: / tɛl əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / tɛl əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to recognize or show the difference between two or more people or things; to distinguish one from another

Examples

To tell apart twins

It's sometimes hard to tell apart identical twins.
Their voices are so similar that I can't tell them apart on the phone.
Can you tell apart Jane's handwriting from Emma's?
I can never tell apart those two species of birds from a distance.
It's hard to tell apart identical twins when they dress the same.
Can you tell apart these two shades of blue?
It's often hard to tell apart twins at a glance.
She couldn't tell apart the fake painting from the original.
