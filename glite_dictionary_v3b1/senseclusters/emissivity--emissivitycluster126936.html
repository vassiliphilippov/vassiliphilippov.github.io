emissivity

noun
thermal radiation property
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ /; / /ə.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɪ.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ /; / /ə.mɪ.ˈsɪ.vɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the ability or efficiency of a surface to emit energy as thermal radiation compared to a perfect emitter (blackbody)

Examples

Surface emissivity value
Black surfaces usually have a higher emissivity than shiny or metallic ones.
Engineers measure the emissivity of materials to design better heat shields.
A material with low emissivity will not radiate heat efficiently.
The emissivity of the metal surface affects how quickly it cools down after heating.
Scientists tested the emissivity of different fabrics to design better thermal clothing.
A high emissivity coating helps satellites release excess heat into space.
The emissivity of the metal surface was measured to determine how efficiently it radiated heat.
Materials with high emissivity are often used in thermal insulation to improve energy efficiency.
Scientists compared the emissivity values of different coatings to find the best one for the spacecraft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorbance - the ability of a material to absorb energy
  • synonym: absorptivity - the ability of a surface to absorb energy
  • antonym: reflectivity - the ability of a surface to reflect energy
