Indianism

noun
Brazilian literary movement
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.dɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.dɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a 19th-century Brazilian literary and artistic movement that idealized indigenous people as symbols of national identity

Examples

Brazilian Indianism movement
Many 19th-century poems openly celebrated Indianism, portraying indigenous heroes as symbols of national identity.
The influence of Indianism can be seen in Brazilian art and literature from that era.
Writers of the Indianism movement often depicted native people as noble and heroic figures.
