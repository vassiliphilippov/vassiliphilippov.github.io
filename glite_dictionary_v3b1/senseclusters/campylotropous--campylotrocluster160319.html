campylotropous

adjective
curved ovule with micropyle near stalk (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˌkæmpɪˈlɑːtrəpəs / (AmE), / ˌkampɪˈlɒtrəpəs / (BrE)
(of a plant ovule) having a curved form with the micropyle near the base, almost touching its stalk.

Examples

Campylotropous ovule shape
Many legumes produce campylotropous ovules, which are distinctively curved inside the ovary.
Researchers compared straight and campylotropous ovules to study their evolutionary advantages.
The campylotropous structure allows efficient nutrient flow between the ovule and its stalk.
