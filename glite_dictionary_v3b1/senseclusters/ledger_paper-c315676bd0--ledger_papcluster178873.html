ledger paper

noun
durable paper for record books
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒər ˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒə ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a strong, high-quality writing paper used for keeping records in account books and business ledgers

Examples

Strong ledger paper
The accountant always used ledger paper to record financial transactions in the company's books.
This notebook is made with high-quality ledger paper, perfect for archival records.
We need to order more ledger paper before we run out for our record-keeping.
