disfranchise

verb
deprive of the right to vote
6.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfræn.tʃaɪz /; / ˌdɪsfrænˈtʃaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈfræn.tʃaɪz /; / ˌdɪsfrænˈtʃaɪz / (BrE)
to take away someone's right to vote or other legal rights

Examples

To disfranchise voters
Campaigners protested against the plan to disfranchise prisoners.
The new law would disfranchise thousands of minority voters.
Women were disfranchised until the early twentieth century in many countries.
The new policy could disfranchise many citizens who lack proper identification.
Activists protested laws that would disfranchise minority voters in several states.
The court ruled that the measure would unfairly disfranchise thousands of people.

  • antonym: enfranchise - to give someone the right to vote
