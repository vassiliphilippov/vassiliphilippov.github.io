supervene upon

verb
to take the place of; to replace
7.1 9.9
Pronunciation: / suː.pɚˈviːn əˌpɒn / (AmE), / suː.pəˈviːn əˌpɒn / (BrE)
(formal) To take the place of something or someone; to replace or succeed in a position or role.

Examples

Supervene upon old customs
In time, new customs supervened upon the old ones, altering the culture drastically.
Technological advances may supervene upon traditional methods, making them obsolete.
When the vice president supervened upon the president's duties, policies began to change rapidly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replace - to take the place of something or someone
  • synonym: supplant - to take the place of something or someone, typically by force
