Mamluk

noun
military class in Egypt
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmæmluːk / (AmE), / ˈmæmluːk / (BrE)
a member of a military class of slave origin that ruled Egypt and parts of the Middle East from the 13th to 16th centuries

Examples

A Mamluk ruler
The Mamluk dynasty ruled Egypt until the Ottoman conquest in the 16th century.
Many Mamluks were originally enslaved boys who became powerful military leaders.
The influence of the Mamluks can still be seen in Cairo's architecture today.
The Mamluk s ultimately defeated the Mongols at the Battle of Ain Jalut.
Many historic buildings in Cairo were constructed during the Mamluk period.
A Mamluk could rise from slavery to become a powerful leader in Egypt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sultan - a Muslim sovereign or ruler
