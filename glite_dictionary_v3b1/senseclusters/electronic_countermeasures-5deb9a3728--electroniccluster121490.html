electronic countermeasures

noun
military actions to block enemy electronics
3.3 6.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.ˈtrɑː.nɪk ˈkaʊn.tər.meʒ.ərz/ /; / /ə.lek.ˈtrɑː.nɪk ˈkaʊn.tər.meʒ.ərz/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.ˈtrɒ.nɪk ˈkaʊn.tə.meʒ.əz/ /; / /e.lek.ˈtrɒ.nɪk ˈkaʊn.tə.meʒ.əz/ (BrE)
Actions involving the use of electronic devices or techniques to prevent or reduce an enemy's effective use of the electromagnetic spectrum, especially in military operations.

Examples

Effective electronic countermeasures
Effective electronic countermeasures can greatly reduce the threat posed by enemy electronic surveillance.
Modern aircraft are equipped with advanced electronic countermeasures to protect against guided missiles.
The military deployed electronic countermeasures to disrupt enemy radar systems during the attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electronic warfare - military activities involving use of electromagnetic energy
  • degree lower: jamming - deliberate interference with radio signals
