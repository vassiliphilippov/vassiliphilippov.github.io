snake

verb
move or extend in a winding pattern
6.6 3.5
Pronunciation: /sneɪk/ (AmE), /sneɪk/ (BrE)
to move or extend in a winding, curving, or twisting manner, like a snake

Examples

The road snakes around
A stream snaked its way around the large boulders in the meadow.
The long road snakes through the dense forest for miles.
The parade route snaked across the city, drawing crowds along every turn.
The river snakes through the valley, creating beautiful curves along the landscape.
The trail snaked through dense forest, making the hike both challenging and scenic.
We watched the long line of cars snake up the mountain road during the holiday rush.
The cable snaked along the floor, making it easy to trip over.
The river snakes through the valley, creating a stunning natural landscape.
We watched the procession snake its way down the crowded streets.
