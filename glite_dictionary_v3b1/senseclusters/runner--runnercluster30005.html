runner

noun
person who runs in races
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnɚ /; / ˈrʌnər / (AmE), / ˈrʌnə / (BrE)
a person who runs, especially for sport, exercise, or in races

Examples

Marathon runner
As a professional runner, she spent hours training every day for marathons.
Each runner wore a number pinned to their shirt during the race.
The runner crossed the finish line just a split second ahead of his rival.
Every morning, dozens of runners fill the park to train before work.
She is a talented runner who competes in marathons around the world.
The runner quickly outpaced the rest of the group during the race.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jogger - person who runs at a steady, moderate pace for exercise
  • antonym: jumper - an athlete who competes in jumping events (track and field)
  • synonym: sprinter - an athlete who runs short-distance races
  • antonym: walker - a person who travels on foot at a walking pace
