pressurization

noun
increasing air pressure
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛʃəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛʃəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of increasing or maintaining air pressure in a space, container, or system to a desired level

Examples

Cabin pressurization
The pressurization of the cabin is essential for passenger comfort during high-altitude flights.
Engineers checked the pressurization system before the submarine submerged.
Proper pressurization prevents damage to sensitive equipment in laboratory environments.
The pressurization of the airplane cabin ensures passengers can breathe comfortably at high altitudes.
Engineers monitor the pressurization of the storage tanks to prevent leaks or explosions.
A sudden loss of pressurization in a submarine can be extremely dangerous for the crew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compression - the act of pressing or squeezing something to reduce its volume
  • antonym: depressurization - the process of reducing air pressure in a space
