nonreplication

noun
failure to repeat results
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the failure or absence of repeating an experiment, study, or result, especially in scientific research

Examples

Nonreplication of results
The scientific community was concerned about the nonreplication of the original experiment's results.
A major issue in research is the nonreplication of studies that claim significant findings.
Journals are now more likely to publish reports on nonreplication to improve scientific transparency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: replication - the process of repeating an experiment or study to confirm results
  • antonym: reproducibility - the ability to achieve the same results when an experiment is repeated
