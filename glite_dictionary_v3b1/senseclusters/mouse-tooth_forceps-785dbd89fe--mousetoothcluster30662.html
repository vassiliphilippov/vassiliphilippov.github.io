mouse-tooth forceps

noun
surgical forceps with pointed, interlocking tips
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊs tuːθ ˈfɔrsɛps / (AmE), / ˈmaʊs tuːθ ˈfɔːsɛps / (BrE)
A type of surgical forceps that has one or more fine sharp points on the tip of each blade which mesh with teeth on the opposite blade, used for grasping tissue during medical procedures.

Examples

Mouse-tooth forceps for surgery
During the operation, she handed him the mouse-tooth forceps to hold the delicate tissue.
Nurses must ensure that mouse-tooth forceps are properly sterilized before any procedure.
The surgeon selected a pair of mouse-tooth forceps to gently grasp the thin membrane.
