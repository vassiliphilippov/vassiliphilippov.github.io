lift

verb
raise to a higher status or condition
2.1 3.1
Pronunciation: /lɪft/ (AmE), /lɪft/ (BrE)
to make someone feel happier, more confident, or to improve their situation or status

Examples

To lift spirits
Her dedication to education helped lift her family to a better life.
The economic reforms helped lift millions out of poverty.
The organization aims to lift disadvantaged communities through job training and support.
A sunny day can really lift your mood.
His kind words lifted my confidence before the interview.
The good news helped lift her spirits after a difficult week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boost - to improve or increase something; synonym for emotional or mental encouragement
  • antonym: demote - to reduce someone to a lower rank or position
  • synonym: elevate - to raise something or someone to a higher position or rank
  • antonym: lower - to reduce in position, amount, or mood; opposite of lifting in emotional sense
  • synonym: raise - to cause to rise to a higher level or position; can also refer to lifting moods
