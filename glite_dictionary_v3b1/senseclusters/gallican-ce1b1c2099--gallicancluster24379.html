Gallican

adjective
relating to Gallicanism or French Catholic traditions
Pronunciation: / ˈɡælɪkən / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪkən / (BrE)
Relating to Gallicanism, especially as pertaining to the independent traditions or policies of the Roman Catholic Church in France, distinct from those of the papacy.

Examples

Gallican principles
Many disputes between the French crown and the papacy involved Gallican principles.
The bishop's policies reflected a distinctly Gallican approach to church governance.
The historian wrote extensively on Gallican ideas in eighteenth-century France.
