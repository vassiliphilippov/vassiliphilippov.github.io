stay

noun
strip for stiffening a garment
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /steɪ/ (AmE), /steɪ/ (BrE)
a supporting device, such as a strip of material in clothing or a rope or wire on a ship

Examples

A dress stay
Old-fashioned dresses often contained stay inserts to keep their shape.
The corset was reinforced with several stays for better support.
The tailor replaced the broken stay in the bodice with a new one.
He repaired the stay using a length of new wire.
If a stay breaks in a storm, the mast can be damaged.
The forward stay must be checked before raising the sail.
