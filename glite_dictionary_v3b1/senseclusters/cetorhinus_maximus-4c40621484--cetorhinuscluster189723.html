Cetorhinus maximus

noun
scientific name for basking shark
Pronunciation: / siːtəˈraɪnəs ˈmæksɪməs / (AmE), / siːtəˈraɪnəs ˈmæksɪməs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the basking shark, a large, harmless shark that feeds on plankton and is often seen swimming slowly near the surface.

Examples

Cetorhinus maximus shark
Marine biologists observed a group of Cetorhinus maximus feeding near the Scottish coastline.
The Cetorhinus maximus can grow up to 10 meters in length, making it the second-largest living fish.
Unlike many sharks, the Cetorhinus maximus is harmless to humans and feeds mainly on plankton.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basking shark - large harmless plankton-eating shark, often seen at the surface
