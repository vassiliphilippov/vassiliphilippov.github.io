tough

adjective
difficult to comprehend or solve
Pronunciation: / tʌf / (AmE), / tʌf / (BrE)
difficult or hard to do, understand, or endure; requiring effort or strength

Examples

A tough problem
She found it tough to believe that the story was true.
Solving the riddle proved more tough than we expected.
That math assignment was really tough and took me hours to finish.
Climbing the mountain was a tough challenge, but they never gave up.
He went through a tough period after losing his job last year.
This exam is tough, so you should prepare as much as possible.
Her dismissal was a tough situation for the whole department.
It was a tough loss for the team, but they vowed to come back stronger.
Sometimes life hands you a tough break, but you just have to move on.
