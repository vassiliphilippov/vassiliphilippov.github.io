meadow foxtail

noun
a perennial grass species used for pasture and hay
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdoʊ ˈfɑːkstɛɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈfɒkstɛɪl / (BrE)
a stout, erect perennial grass of the northern Old World with silky flowering spikes, widely cultivated for pasture and hay, and naturalized in North America

Examples

The meadow foxtail grass
After the rainy season, meadow foxtail flourishes in the pastures across the countryside.
Farmers prefer meadow foxtail because it provides an early source of hay in the spring.
The meadow foxtail is often used as fodder for cattle in temperate climates.
