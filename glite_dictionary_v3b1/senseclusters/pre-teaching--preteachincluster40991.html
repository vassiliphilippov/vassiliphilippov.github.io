pre-teaching

adjective
before main instruction
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
happening or provided before the main teaching to prepare students for upcoming lessons or material

Examples

Pre-teaching activities
The teacher used pre-teaching activities to introduce new vocabulary before the main lesson.
Effective pre-teaching can help students feel more confident during challenging topics.
Many educators believe that pre-teaching improves students' understanding and participation in class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: post-teaching - happening after the main teaching
  • synonym: preparatory - serving as preparation for something
