extinction

noun
complete annihilation; dying out; disappearance
4.3
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
the complete dying out or end of a species, group, or phenomenon so that it no longer exists

Examples

Extinction of the dinosaurs
After the asteroid hit, the extinction of the dinosaurs was inevitable.
Scientists warn that climate change may lead to the extinction of several endangered animals.
The extinction of many species has been accelerated by human activity.
After the extinction of the dinosaurs, mammals became the dominant land animals.
Climate change is increasing the threat of extinction for polar bears and other Arctic wildlife.
Many scientists warn that rapid environmental change can lead to the extinction of numerous animal species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annihilation - complete destruction or obliteration
  • synonym: eradication - the complete destruction or removal of something
  • antonym: survival - continuation of existence
