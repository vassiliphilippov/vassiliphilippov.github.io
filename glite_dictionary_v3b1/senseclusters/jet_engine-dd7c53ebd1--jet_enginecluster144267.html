jet engine

noun
engine that propels aircraft using jet propulsion
Pronunciation: / dʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / dʒɛt ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
An engine that produces thrust for an aircraft by discharging a high-speed jet of gas, typically using a gas turbine.

Examples

A jet engine thrust
A jet engine works by sucking in air, mixing it with fuel, and igniting the mixture to create thrust.
After the bird strike, the pilot had to shut down one jet engine and make an emergency landing.
The Boeing 747 is powered by four jet engines for long-distance flights.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: rocket engine - an engine that produces thrust by expelling high-speed gases and operates without external air
  • degree lower: turboprop - an aircraft engine that drives a propeller using a gas turbine
