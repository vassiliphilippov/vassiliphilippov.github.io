patina

noun
sheen on wood from age or use
Pronunciation: / pəˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈpætɪnə /; / pəˈtiːnə / (BrE)
a thin layer or surface appearance that develops on something with age, use, or exposure, often giving it an attractive or distinctive look (can be literal or figurative)

Examples

Aged patina
The antique table had developed a rich patina over decades of careful use.
Years of polishing had given the dresser a beautiful patina that collectors adore.
You should avoid sanding the wood so as not to remove its natural patina.
A patina of dust covered the furniture after the long winter absence.
The old book possessed a patina of wisdom and charm that made it more appealing.
Years in the city had given him a patina of worldliness.
Collectors value old coins that show a natural patina from handling and time.
The antique table had developed a beautiful patina over the years.
The bronze statue's green patina made it look even more majestic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lustre - gentle sheen or soft glow, often on a polished surface
  • synonym: sheen - a soft glow on a surface
  • synonym: tarnish - a dullness that forms on metals due to oxidation
