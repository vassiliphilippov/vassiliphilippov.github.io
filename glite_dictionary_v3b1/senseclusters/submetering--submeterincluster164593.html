submetering

noun
individual utility measurement
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌmiːtərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌmiːtərɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice or system of measuring and recording individual utility (energy or water) usage within a larger property by installing separate meters

Examples

Submetering water use
Many landlords use submetering to bill tenants for their actual water consumption.
The new apartment complex installed submetering systems for both electricity and gas.
Submetering encourages residents to be more mindful of their energy usage.
The new apartment complex uses submetering to track each tenant's electricity consumption separately.
Thanks to submetering, residents only pay for the water they actually use in their units.
Property managers recommend submetering to encourage energy conservation among tenants.
