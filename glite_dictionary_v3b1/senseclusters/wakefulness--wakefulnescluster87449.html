wakefulness

noun
state of being awake and alert
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪkfəlnəs /; / ˈweɪk.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈweɪkfəlnəs /; / ˈweɪk.f(ə)l.nəs / (BrE)
the state of being awake (not sleeping), especially when alert or unable to sleep

Examples

Nighttime wakefulness
Caffeine can increase wakefulness and help you stay focused.
Meditation is sometimes practiced to enhance wakefulness and awareness.
Periods of wakefulness during the night can make you feel tired the next day.
After several nights of wakefulness, she finally fell asleep just before dawn.
Excessive caffeine can increase wakefulness and make it harder to fall asleep.
Periods of wakefulness are common as we age, often interrupting sleep throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alertness - the quality of being alert and attentive
  • antonym: sleep - a natural periodic state of rest for the mind and body
