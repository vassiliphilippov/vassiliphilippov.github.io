plain-woven

adjective
made using plain weave
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪn ˌwoʊ.vən / (AmE), / ˈpleɪn ˌwəʊ.vən / (BrE)
(of cloth) made using a simple weaving technique in which threads cross alternately over and under each other.

Examples

Plain-woven fabric
Most bed sheets are crafted from plain-woven fabric for softness and breathability.
The museum displayed a plain-woven linen fragment dating back to the medieval period.
This shirt is made of plain-woven cotton that feels both durable and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plain weave - a basic weaving pattern in which the weft passes over and under successive warp threads alternately
