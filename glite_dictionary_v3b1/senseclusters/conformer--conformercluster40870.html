conformer

noun
molecule with specific shape
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a molecule or organism that has a particular shape or structure, especially among possible alternatives

Examples

Molecular conformer
The protein can exist as more than one conformer depending on the environment.
Scientists studied how each conformer of the molecule reacted to changes in temperature.
A single molecule may switch between different conformers during a chemical reaction.
Each conformer of the molecule has different chemical properties.
Scientists studied how temperature affects the stability of each conformer in the sample.
The protein can exist as more than one conformer depending on its environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isomer - a molecule with the same formula but a different arrangement of atoms
