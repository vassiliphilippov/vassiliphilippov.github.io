unsympathetic

adjective
not likable; not evoking sympathy
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌnsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
Not evoking feelings of sympathy or approval; lacking in likable or appealing qualities, especially when referring to characters in literature or drama.

Examples

Unsympathetic character
Critics found the villain compelling but ultimately too unsympathetic to generate much interest.
Despite their hardships, the film portrayed the family in an unsympathetic light.
The novel's main character was deeply unsympathetic, making it hard for readers to relate to him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sympathetic - evoking sympathy or compassion; likable
