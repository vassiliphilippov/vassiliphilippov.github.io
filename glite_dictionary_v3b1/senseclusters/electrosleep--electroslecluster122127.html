electrosleep

noun
medically induced sleep using electrical current
7.9 8.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛk.troʊˌsliːp / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trəʊˌsliːp / (BrE)
A state of unconsciousness induced by passing a low-voltage electric current through the brain, typically for medical or therapeutic purposes.

Examples

Electrosleep therapy
Experimental studies are being conducted to assess the effectiveness of electrosleep in treating insomnia.
Researchers are exploring the safety of electrosleep as a non-pharmacological alternative for inducing sleep.
The patient underwent electrosleep therapy to help manage chronic pain.
