axoneme

noun
core of cilium or flagellum
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæksəˌniːm / (AmE), / ˈæksəˌniːm / (BrE)
the central structural core of a cilium or flagellum in a cell, responsible for its movement

Examples

The axoneme structure
The axoneme is essential for the beating motion of cilia in respiratory tract cells.
Scientists studied the axoneme to understand how flagella generate movement in single-celled organisms.
A defect in the axoneme can lead to problems with cell motility and cause certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cilia - hair-like structures on cells that move fluid or the cell itself
  • synonym: microtubule - a tubular structure in cells that forms part of the cytoskeleton
