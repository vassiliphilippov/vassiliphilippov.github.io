precursory

adjective
giving warning of something to come
8.1 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / pr…™Ààk…úrs…ôri / (AmE), / pr…™Ààk…úÀês…ôri / (BrE)
Giving warning or indication of something, especially something undesirable, that is going to happen.

Examples

A precursory tremor
Scientists are studying precursory tremors to predict major earthquakes.
Some experts saw the sudden drop in stock prices as a precursory sign of recession.
The dark clouds were a precursory warning of the approaching storm.

Synonyms

  • premonitory
    9.8 0.08/m
    premonitory gives a clear warning sign of future trouble, while precursory means a general early indication that something will happen.
  • portentous
    8.6 0.58/m
    portentous announces a heavy, important warning about bad events, while precursory simply signals a prior indication of something.
  • ominous
    6.4 7.21/m
    ominous feels threatening and creates a mood of danger, while precursory points to an earlier sign that something may happen.
