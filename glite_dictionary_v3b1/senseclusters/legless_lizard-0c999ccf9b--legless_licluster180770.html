legless lizard

noun
a snake-like lizard without legs
4.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈleg.ləs ˈlɪz.ərd/ (AmE), /ˈleg.ləs ˈlɪz.əd/ (BrE)
A lizard that has lost its legs through evolution, resembling a snake but differing anatomically, especially found in California and related to alligator lizards.

Examples

A legless lizard species
At first glance, a legless lizard can be mistaken for a snake, but it is not closely related.
Biologists study the legless lizard to understand evolutionary adaptation and limb loss.
The California legless lizard spends most of its life burrowing through sand and loose soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lizard - a reptile with a long body and tail, four legs, moveable eyelids, and rough, scaly, or spiny skin
  • antonym: snake - a legless, elongated reptile distinct from lizards in anatomy
