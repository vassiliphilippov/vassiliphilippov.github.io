shellack

verb
to defeat soundly
8.5
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈlæk / (AmE), / ʃəˈlæk / (BrE)
to defeat someone very thoroughly or by a large margin, especially in a competition or game

Examples

To shellack a team
Our team was shellacked in the championship game and lost by thirty points.
The candidate shellacked his opponent in the debate, leaving no doubt about the winner.
They shellacked the visiting team so badly that the crowd cheered for mercy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to be defeated in a competition or contest
  • synonym: thrash - to beat someone decisively in a contest
  • synonym: trounce - to defeat someone easily or completely
