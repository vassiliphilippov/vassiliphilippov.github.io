complicitous

adjective
involved in wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɪsɪtəs / (AmE), / kəmˈplɪsɪtəs / (BrE)
involved with others in an activity that is morally or legally wrong

Examples

Complicitous in crime
The manager was complicitous in the scheme to hide the company's losses.
Several officials were found to be complicitous in the illegal activities.
She denied being complicitous in any of the fraudulent transactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complicit - involved with others in wrongdoing
  • antonym: innocent - not involved in wrongdoing
