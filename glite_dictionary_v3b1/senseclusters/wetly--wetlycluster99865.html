wetly

adverb
weakly or sentimentally
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛtli / (AmE), / ˈwɛtli / (BrE)
in a weak, ineffectual, or overly sentimental manner, especially in an informal or disapproving way

Examples

Smiling wetly
He smiled wetly at the audience, hoping for sympathy rather than respect.
The team responded wetly to criticism, failing to defend their position.
She apologized wetly, her words lacking any real conviction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feebly - in a weak or ineffective way
  • antonym: firmly - in a strong or determined way
