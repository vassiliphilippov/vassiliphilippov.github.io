daze

noun
state of stunned confusion
Pronunciation: / deɪz / (AmE), / deɪz / (BrE)
a state of confusion or stunned disbelief, often caused by shock, distress, fatigue, or surprise

Examples

In a daze
After hearing the news of his job loss, Mark walked around in a daze for hours.
He stared at the broken vase, still in a daze from what had just happened.
The accident left her in such a daze that she could hardly speak.
After the accident, she wandered around in a daze for several minutes.
He sat staring at the television, lost in a daze of exhaustion.
The shocking news left everyone in a daze during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear, in contrast to confusion
  • synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting or surprising event or experience
  • synonym: stupefaction - a state of being stupefied or in stunned amazement
  • synonym: stupor - a state of near-unconsciousness or insensibility
