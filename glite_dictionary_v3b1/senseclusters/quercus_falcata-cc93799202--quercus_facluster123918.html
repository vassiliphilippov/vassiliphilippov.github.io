Quercus falcata

noun
a species of southern red oak tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs færˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs falˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A large deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, also known as southern red oak, with deeply lobed, narrow, falcate leaves and wood similar to northern red oaks.

Examples

A Quercus falcata tree
Along the roadside, several mature Quercus falcata provided much-needed shade each summer.
Botanists studied the unique leaves of the Quercus falcata to differentiate it from other red oaks.
The Quercus falcata is prized for its strong, reddish wood and broad, spreading crown.
