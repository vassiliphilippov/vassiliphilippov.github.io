catchy

adjective
attractive and memorable
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃi / (AmE), / ˈkætʃi / (BrE)
(of a tune, phrase, or title) instantly appealing and easy to remember; likely to attract attention.

Examples

A catchy slogan
She came up with a catchy slogan for the new campaign.
The commercial had a catchy jingle that everyone remembered for weeks.
They chose a catchy title to make the book stand out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; not likely to attract attention
  • synonym: memorable - easily remembered, especially because of being special or unusual
