sartorius muscle

noun
thigh muscle aiding leg rotation
9.4 5.7
Pronunciation: /sɑːr.ˈtɔː.ri.əs ˈmʌs.əl/ (AmE), /sɑː.ˈtɔː.ri.əs ˈmʌs.əl/ (BrE)
a long, narrow muscle in the thigh that helps rotate the leg and assists in crossing one leg over the other; the longest muscle in the human body

Examples

The sartorius muscle in thigh
Injury to the sartorius muscle can make leg rotation and crossing difficult.
The sartorius muscle is involved when sitting cross-legged on the floor.
The sartorius muscle stretches from the hip to the inner side of the knee.
