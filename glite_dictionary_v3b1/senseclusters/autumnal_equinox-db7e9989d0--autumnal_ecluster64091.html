autumnal equinox

noun
astronomical event marking start of autumn
Pronunciation: / əˈtʌmnəl ˈikwəˌnɑks /; / əˈtʌmnəl ˈekwɪˌnɑks / (AmE), / ɔːˈtʌmnəl ˈiːkwɪnɒks / (BrE)
the time in autumn when the sun crosses the celestial equator, making day and night about equal length (around September 22 or 23 in the northern hemisphere)

Examples

The autumnal equinox date
Astronomers use the autumnal equinox as a reference point for celestial coordinates.
Many cultures celebrate special festivals during the autumnal equinox to mark the change of season.
The autumnal equinox occurs each year in late September, bringing nearly equal hours of daylight and darkness.
Many cultures celebrate festivals around the time of the autumnal equinox.
On the autumnal equinox, day and night are nearly the same length.
The autumnal equinox marks the official start of fall in the northern hemisphere.
