salivate

verb
produce saliva, often in anticipation
Pronunciation: / ˈsæləˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈsælɪveɪt / (BrE)
to produce saliva, especially as a result of seeing or smelling food, or in eager anticipation

Examples

To salivate over food
He began to salivate as soon as he saw the juicy steak on the grill.
Pavlov's experiment showed that dogs would salivate at the sound of a bell.
The aroma of fresh bread made everyone salivate instantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drool - let saliva flow from the mouth; show eager anticipation
