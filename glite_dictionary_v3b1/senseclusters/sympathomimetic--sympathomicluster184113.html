sympathomimetic

adjective
mimicking effects of epinephrine or the sympathetic nervous system
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪm.pə.θoʊ.mɪˈmɛ.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪm.pə.θəʊ.mɪˈmɛ.tɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or substance) producing physiological effects similar to those caused by the stimulation of the sympathetic nervous system, especially relating to the release or action of epinephrine.

Examples

Sympathomimetic drug effects
Certain decongestants have sympathomimetic properties due to their action on the nervous system.
Many asthma medications are sympathomimetic, helping to relax airway muscles by mimicking adrenaline.
The doctor prescribed a sympathomimetic agent to increase the patient's heart rate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adrenergic - relating to or denoting nerve cells in which epinephrine, norepinephrine, or a similar substance acts as a neurotransmitter
  • antonym: parasympathomimetic - mimicking the effects of the parasympathetic nervous system
