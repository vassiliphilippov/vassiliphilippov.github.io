conformability

noun
willingness to conform
Pronunciation: / kənˌfɔrməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kənˌfɔːməˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or tendency of being willing or able to conform to rules, customs, or the behavior of others

Examples

Employee conformability
The manager valued conformability in employees who could easily adapt to new company policies.
High conformability can help individuals fit into different social groups more smoothly.
Some people see conformability as a strength, while others value independent thinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compliance - the act of obeying rules or requests
  • antonym: nonconformity - refusal to follow established customs or rules
