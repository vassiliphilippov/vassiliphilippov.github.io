fluxing lime

noun
calcium oxide used as an industrial flux
8.8 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈflʌk.sɪŋ laɪm/ (AmE), /ˈflʌk.sɪŋ laɪm/ (BrE)
a white crystalline compound, calcium oxide, used in industry as a fluxing agent and in the production of calcium hydroxide

Examples

Fluxing lime in steel
The addition of fluxing lime facilitates the removal of impurities during steel manufacturing.
The ceramic glaze formula calls for a precise amount of fluxing lime to improve melting.
To produce mortar, fluxing lime is first reacted with water to make slaked lime.
