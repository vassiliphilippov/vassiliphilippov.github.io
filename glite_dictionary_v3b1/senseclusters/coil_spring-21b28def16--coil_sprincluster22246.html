coil spring

noun
spring made of wound coil
Pronunciation: / kɔɪl sprɪŋ / (AmE), / kɔɪl sprɪŋ / (BrE)
A spring made from wire wound into a coil, used to absorb shock or maintain force between contacting surfaces.

Examples

A coil spring absorbs shock
After years of use, the coil spring in the mattress lost its bounce.
He replaced the old coil spring with a stronger one to improve the machine's performance.
The car's suspension uses a coil spring to ensure a smooth ride over bumps.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leaf spring - a spring made from one or more strips of metal, often used in vehicle suspension
  • synonym: torsion spring - a spring that works by twisting rather than compressing or stretching
