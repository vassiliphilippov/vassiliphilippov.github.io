cynipid gall wasp

noun
type of wasp inducing plant galls
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɪpɪd ɡɔl wɑsp / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪpɪd ɡɔːl wɒsp / (BrE)
A small solitary wasp of the family Cynipidae that induces the formation of galls on oaks and other plants.

Examples

The cynipid gall wasp insect

Some species of cynipid gall wasp are responsible for unusual plant growths on shrubs and trees.
The cynipid gall wasp lays its eggs in oak leaves, leading to the formation of distinctive galls.
