quasar

noun
distant, energetic celestial object
8.2 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkweɪ.zɑːr/ /; / /ˈkweɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈkweɪ.zɑː/ (BrE)
An extremely luminous and distant celestial object, appearing star-like, that gives off large amounts of energy, including radio waves and visible light; believed to be powered by a supermassive black hole at the center of a galaxy.

Examples

A quasar in space
Astronomers discovered a new quasar at the edge of the observable universe.
Some quasars are billions of light-years away, making them valuable for studying the early universe.
The intense radiation emitted by a quasar can outshine its entire host galaxy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pulsar - a highly magnetized, rotating neutron star emitting beams of electromagnetic radiation
