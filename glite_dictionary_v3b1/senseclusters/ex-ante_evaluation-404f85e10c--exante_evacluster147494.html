ex-ante evaluation

noun
assessment conducted before an initiative
9.8 7.0
Pronunciation: /eks ˈæn.tiː ɪ.væl.juː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /eks ˈæn.tiː iː.væl.juː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /eks ˈæn.tiː ɪ.væl.juː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /eks ˈæn.tiː ɪ.væl.jʊ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A forward-looking assessment of the expected effects of a proposed initiative, project, or policy, conducted before it is implemented.

Examples

An ex-ante evaluation report
An ex-ante evaluation helps policymakers predict potential outcomes before allocating resources to a program.
Comprehensive ex-ante evaluations are key to minimizing risks in large-scale development projects.
The funding agency required an ex-ante evaluation to ensure the project's feasibility and effectiveness.
