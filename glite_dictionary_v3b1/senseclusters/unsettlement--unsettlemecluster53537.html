unsettlement

noun
incomplete settlement
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɛtəlmənt / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛtəlmənt / (BrE)
the failure to complete, or the state of not having completed, the settlement of a financial transaction or account

Examples

Unsettlement of payments
The bank's system error led to unsettlement of several important transactions.
After the merger, there was significant unsettlement in the processing of client accounts.
Regulators are concerned about the risks posed by unsettlement in international payments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: default - failure to fulfill a financial obligation
  • antonym: settlement - the completion of a financial transaction or account
