savings account trust

noun
savings account held in trust for a beneficiary

A savings account in which the depositor acts as trustee, controlling the account during their lifetime, with the balance payable to a designated beneficiary upon their death.

Examples

A savings account trust
Many people set up a savings account trust to ensure their loved ones receive funds directly after their death.
She named her niece as the beneficiary of the savings account trust she opened last year.
The savings account trust allowed the depositor to retain control while designating a successor.

  • trustee account
