Occam's Razor

noun
principle favoring simpler explanations

the principle that the simplest solution or explanation is most likely to be correct, and that unnecessary assumptions should be avoided

Examples

Occam's Razor principle
In solving mysteries, detectives frequently invoke Occam's Razor to rule out unlikely scenarios.
Philosophers debate whether Occam's Razor truly leads to truth or just to convenient simplicity.
When faced with several explanations for the data, scientists often apply Occam's Razor to select the simplest one.

Synonyms

  • Ockham's Razor
    8.2 0.02/m
    Ockham's Razor is an alternate spelling used by some writers for the same simple-explanation rule, while Occam's Razor is the more common name.
  • principle of parsimony
    7.5 0.01/m
    principle of parsimony frames a formal rule for choosing simple theories in science, while Occam's Razor is the familiar name.
  • law of parsimony
    7.6 0.01/m
    law of parsimony is a phrase that treats simplicity almost like a rule in reasoning, while Occam's Razor names the guiding principle.
