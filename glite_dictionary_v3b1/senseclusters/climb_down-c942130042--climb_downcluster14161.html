climb down

verb
to withdraw a previous opinion or position
Pronunciation: / klaɪm daʊn / (AmE), / klaɪm daʊn / (BrE)
to retract or withdraw from a previously stated opinion, position, or claim, often under pressure or after reconsideration

Examples

To climb down from a statement
After facing criticism, the politician was forced to climb down from her earlier remarks.
Despite his initial refusal, he eventually decided to climb down and accept responsibility.
The company had to climb down after customers complained about the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: back down - to withdraw or concede in a dispute
