faint damn

noun
mild or subtle criticism
Pronunciation: / feɪnt dæm / (AmE), / feɪnt dæm / (BrE)
A mild or subtle criticism, often presented in the guise of praise or with little emphasis.

Examples

A faint damn given
He offered a faint damn by mentioning the food was 'acceptable enough.'
Her performance review included a faint damn for her attention to detail, suggesting it needed improvement.
The article's only complaint was a faint damn about the movie's length.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damning with faint praise - to criticize indirectly by giving weak or insincere praise
