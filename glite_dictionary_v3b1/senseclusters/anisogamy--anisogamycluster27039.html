anisogamy

noun
reproduction by fusion of different gametes
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ɪˈsɑː.ɡə.mi / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɪˈsɒ.ɡə.mi / (BrE)
(biology) reproduction by the fusion of two gametes that differ in size or form.

Examples

Anisogamy in biology
In certain algae, anisogamy leads to the production of male and female gametes that differ greatly in size.
Researchers study anisogamy to understand how sexual roles have arisen in various species.
The evolution of anisogamy marked a significant distinction from isogamous reproductive systems.
