jack

verb
to steal quickly
8.6
Pronunciation: / dʒæk / (AmE), / dʒæk / (BrE)
to steal something, especially quickly or in a sneaky way

Examples

To jack a bike
Someone tried to jack my bike from the garage last night.
He was caught trying to jack a wallet from a tourist in the crowd.
Teenagers sometimes jack snacks from the store when no one is looking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: return - to give something back to its owner
  • synonym: rob - to take property from a person or place illegally, often by force
  • synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you
