moralizer

noun
person giving moral advice
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who often gives moral advice or lectures others about what is right and wrong

Examples

A moralizer gives advice
Nobody liked being around the moralizer because he always criticized their choices.
She became known as a moralizer after constantly telling her friends how to behave.
The article accused the politician of acting like a moralizer instead of addressing real issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypocrite - a person who criticizes others' morals but does not follow them
  • synonym: lecturer - a person who gives formal talks or speeches, often with advice
  • antonym: listener - a person who listens rather than gives advice
  • synonym: preacher - a person who gives sermons or moral advice
