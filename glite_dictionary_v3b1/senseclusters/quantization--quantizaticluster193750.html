quantization

noun
division into discrete quantities; quantum theory process
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑːntəˈzeɪʃən /; / kwɑn.təˌzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒntɪˈzeɪʃən /; / kwɒn.tɪˌzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of dividing something into discrete quantities, especially in terms of quantum theory or digital representation.

Examples

Quantization process
Errors introduced during quantization can affect the accuracy of the results.
In digital signal processing, quantization refers to the process of mapping a large set of values to a smaller one.
The discovery of energy quantization revolutionized our understanding of atomic structure.
Digital audio requires quantisation to convert analog signals into digital form.
Einstein’s work led to the quantisation of light as discrete particles called photons.
The quantisation of energy levels explains many strange phenomena in atomic physics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quantization - the act of dividing into quanta or expressing in terms of quantum theory (American spelling)
  • synonym: quantum - a discrete quantity of energy or matter, fundamental in quantum theory
