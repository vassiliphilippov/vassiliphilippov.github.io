flashover

noun
sudden fire spread
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌəʊvə / (BrE)
a sudden and intense spread of fire throughout an area when all combustible materials ignite almost simultaneously

Examples

Fire flashover
The living room reached flashover within minutes, making it impossible for firefighters to enter safely.
Training includes recognizing the warning signs before a flashover can occur in a burning building.
After the curtains caught fire, the entire apartment experienced a rapid flashover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conflagration - a large and destructive fire
  • antonym: smolder - to burn slowly with smoke but no flame
