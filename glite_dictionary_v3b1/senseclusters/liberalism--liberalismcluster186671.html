liberalism

noun
economic theory advocating free markets
5.5 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪb.ər.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈlɪb.ər.əl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a political and economic philosophy that values individual freedom, equal rights, and limited government intervention, often supporting free markets and social progress

Examples

Liberalism philosophy
Critics of liberalism claim it can lead to greater inequality without appropriate regulation.
Many economists argue that liberalism leads to greater innovation through competition.
The country's shift towards liberalism in the late twentieth century significantly impacted its economic growth.
Her beliefs in liberalism encouraged her to advocate for new education policies.
Many voters support liberalism because it promotes equality and gradual social change.
The rise of liberalism in the nineteenth century led to significant political reforms.
The principles of liberalism have shaped many modern constitutions around the world.
Debates about liberalism often focus on the balance between freedom and government regulation.
She studied the history of liberalism in her political science class.

  • synonym: progressivism - political philosophy advocating progress and reform
