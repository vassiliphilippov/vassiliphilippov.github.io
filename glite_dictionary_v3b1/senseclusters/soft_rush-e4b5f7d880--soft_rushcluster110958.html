soft rush

noun
tall rush plant with soft stems
Pronunciation: / sɑft rʌʃ / (AmE), / sɒft rʌʃ / (BrE)
a tall plant of the rush family with soft, erect or arching stems, native to Eurasia, Australia, New Zealand, and widely found in North America

Examples

The soft rush plant
Biologists identified the plant as soft rush during the ecological survey.
Many gardeners use soft rush in water gardens because of its decorative stems.
The wetlands were thick with soft rush, providing shelter for birds and amphibians.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rush - a plant with grassy stems, often found in wetlands
