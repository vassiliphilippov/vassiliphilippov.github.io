enfoldment

noun
protective covering
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfoʊldmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfəʊldmənt / (BrE)
the act, process, or result of wrapping, enclosing, or surrounding something or someone, often with protection or affection

Examples

The enfoldment of cloth
The delicate sculpture was kept safe within the enfoldment of soft cloth.
During winter, the enfoldment of a warm blanket brings comfort.
The letter was hidden in the enfoldment of several layers of paper.
She felt safe in the gentle enfoldment of her grandmother's arms.
The enfoldment of the forest around the cabin made it feel secluded and peaceful.
After a long day, he found comfort in the warm enfoldment of his family.
The enfoldment of the child in a warm blanket brought comfort on the cold night.
In meditation, some describe a sense of enfoldment by peaceful thoughts.
The artist depicted the enfoldment of nature within the curves of the sculpture.

  • synonym: hug - a close, affectionate hold with the arms
  • antonym: unfolding - the process of opening or spreading out
  • synonym: wrapping - material used to cover or protect something
