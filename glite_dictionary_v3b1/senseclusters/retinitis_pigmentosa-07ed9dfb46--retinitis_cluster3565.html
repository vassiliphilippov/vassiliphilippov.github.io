retinitis pigmentosa

noun
genetic eye disorder
7.6
Pronunciation: /rɛ.tɪ.ˈnaɪ.tɪs pɪɡ.mən.ˈtoʊ.sə/ /; / /rɪ.tɪ.ˈnaɪ.tɪs pɪɡ.mən.ˈtoʊ.sə/ (AmE), /rɛ.tɪ.ˈnaɪ.tɪs pɪɡ.mən.ˈtəʊ.sə/ /; / /rɪ.tɪ.ˈnaɪ.tɪs pɪɡ.mən.ˈtəʊ.sə/ (BrE)
a genetic disorder causing progressive vision loss due to degeneration of the retina

Examples

Retinitis pigmentosa vision loss
People with retinitis pigmentosa often experience night blindness as an early symptom.
Genetic testing can help diagnose retinitis pigmentosa in families with a history of vision loss.
There is currently no cure for retinitis pigmentosa, but research is ongoing.
