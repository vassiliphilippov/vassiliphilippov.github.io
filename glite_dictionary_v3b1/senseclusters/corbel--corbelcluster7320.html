corbel

noun
projecting architectural bracket
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrbəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːbəl / (BrE)
an architectural bracket of brick or stone projecting from a wall to support a structure above it, often triangular in shape

Examples

Stone corbel support
Each of the old church's windows was accented by a decorative corbel beneath the arch.
Masons skillfully carved the corbel to help bear the weight of the upper floor.
The architect designed an elaborate balcony supported by a single stone corbel.
