tight

adjective
unwilling to spend money; mean (of a person)
4.1
Pronunciation: / taɪt / (AmE), / taɪt / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) unwilling to spend money; not generous; mean.

Examples

Tight with money
Everyone knows he's tight with his money, so don't expect a big present.
My uncle is so tight he reuses tea bags to save a few pennies.
She's far too tight to buy anyone a drink when she goes out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generous - willing to give money, help, or time freely
  • synonym: mean - unwilling to give or spend; not generous
  • synonym: miserly - showing a lack of generosity; stingy
