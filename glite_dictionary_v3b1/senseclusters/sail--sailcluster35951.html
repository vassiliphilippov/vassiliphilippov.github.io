sail

noun
fabric used to propel a vessel using wind
1.0 7.87/m
Pronunciation: / seÉªl / (AmE), / seÉªl / (BrE)
a large piece of strong fabric or a structure on a boat or ship that catches the wind to move it forward, or anything that resembles this in shape or function

Examples

Raise the sail
A sudden gust of wind filled the sail and pushed the boat forward.
The crew hoisted the sail before leaving the harbor.
The old sail was torn and needed to be replaced.
A solar sail was attached to the spacecraft to harness the force of sunlight.
Some ancient windmills used a cloth sail to catch the wind more efficiently.
The dinosaur had a large sail on its back, possibly for regulating body temperature.
