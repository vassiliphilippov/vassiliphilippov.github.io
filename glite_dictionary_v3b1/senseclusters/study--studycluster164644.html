study

verb
think carefully about; consider
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˈstʌdi / (BrE)
to examine, analyze, or think carefully about something in detail

Examples

To study a problem
Before investing in the company, he wants to study the financial reports.
She decided to study the proposal thoroughly before giving her approval.
They asked the board to study alternative solutions before making a final decision.
It is important to study the data thoroughly before making any conclusions.
She likes to study ancient poetry to understand its deeper meaning.
The scientist will study the samples in detail to identify their unique properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analyze - to examine methodically and in detail
  • synonym: consider - to think about carefully
  • synonym: scan - to look at something carefully, especially in order to find or detect something
  • synonym: scrutinize - to examine something very carefully
