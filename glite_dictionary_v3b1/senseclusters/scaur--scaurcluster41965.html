scaur

noun
steep riverbank
Pronunciation: / skɑr / (AmE), / skɑː / (BrE)
a steep bank, cliff, or rocky outcrop, especially beside a river

Examples

A steep scaur bank
The sheep grazed near the edge of the scaur overlooking the stream.
We climbed down the scaur to reach the riverbank below.
Erosion had made the scaur even steeper after the heavy rains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cliff - a high, steep face of rock or earth
