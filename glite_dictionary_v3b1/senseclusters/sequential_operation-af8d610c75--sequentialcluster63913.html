sequential operation

noun
execution of steps one after another
5.8
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkwɛn.tʃu.əl ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈkwen.tʃu.əl ˌɒp.ərˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an operation or process in which actions or steps are carried out one after another in a specific order

Examples

A sequential operation process
Careful planning is necessary when arranging tasks in a sequential operation.
In mathematics, some algorithms require sequential operations rather than parallel ones.
The computer program uses a sequential operation to process each record in order.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parallel operation - operations performed at the same time rather than sequentially
