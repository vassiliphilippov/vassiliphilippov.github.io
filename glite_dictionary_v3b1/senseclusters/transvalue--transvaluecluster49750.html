transvalue

verb
to assign new meaning
9.9
Pronunciation: /trænz.ˈvæl.juː/ /; / /træns.ˈvæl.juː/ (AmE), /trænz.ˈvæl.juː/ /; / /træns.ˈvæl.juː/ (BrE)
to reinterpret or assign a new value or meaning to something, especially in a philosophical context

Examples

To transvalue old ideas
The philosopher aimed to transvalue established ideas about morality and justice.
Artists often transvalue everyday objects by presenting them in new and surprising ways.
She tried to transvalue her failures as opportunities for personal growth.
Nietzsche aimed to transvalue traditional morals by questioning their underlying assumptions.
The artist tried to transvalue everyday objects by presenting them as works of art.
Social movements often seek to transvalue established cultural norms and beliefs.
