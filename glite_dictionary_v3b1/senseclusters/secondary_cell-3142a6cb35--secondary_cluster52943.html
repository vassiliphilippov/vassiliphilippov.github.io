secondary cell

noun
a rechargeable electrochemical cell
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkəndɛri sɛl / (AmE), / ˈsɛkəndəri sɛl / (BrE)
an electrochemical cell that can be recharged and used repeatedly by reversing the chemical reaction with an external electric current

Examples

A secondary cell battery
Compared to a primary battery, a secondary cell offers greater long-term value for portable electronics.
Modern smartphones use a secondary cell to allow users to recharge their devices efficiently.
The laboratory experiment demonstrated how a secondary cell could be recharged after each discharge cycle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: primary cell - an electrochemical cell that cannot be recharged
  • synonym: rechargeable battery - a battery that can be recharged multiple times
