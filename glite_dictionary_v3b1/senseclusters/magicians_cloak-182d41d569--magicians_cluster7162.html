magician's cloak

noun
cloak worn by magicians
9.4
Pronunciation: / məˈdʒɪʃənz kloʊk / (AmE), / məˈdʒɪʃənz kləʊk / (BrE)
a long outer garment, often dramatic or ornate, traditionally worn by magicians to create an air of mystery or perform illusions

Examples

A magician's cloak
She wore a magician's cloak as part of her Halloween costume, complete with a wand and hat.
The magician's cloak shimmered under the stage lights, capturing the audience's attention.
The old magician's cloak was lined with secret pockets for hidden props and tricks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloak - a loose outer garment, often sleeveless
