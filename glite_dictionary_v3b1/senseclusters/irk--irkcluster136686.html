irk

verb
annoy or irritate
6.3 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / …úrk / (AmE), / …úÀêk / (BrE)
to irritate or annoy someone; to make someone feel bothered or vexed

Examples

To irk a person
Her constant complaining began to irk everyone in the office.
It truly irks me when people are late for meetings.
The noise from the construction site irks the residents of the neighborhood.

Synonyms

  • annoy
    1.0 13/m
    annoy makes someone slightly angry or irritated by small actions, while irk makes someone feel bothered or vexed.
  • irritate
    2.9 5.10/m
    irritate means to make someone impatient or mildly angry through small, repeated actions, while irk means to bother them.
  • peeve
    6.8 0.19/m
    peeve means to annoy someone about small, personal things, while irk is a softer, more general feeling of being bothered.
  • rile
    6.9 2.20/m
    rile makes someone angry or upset, often over small things, while irk describes a less strong feeling of bother.
  • get on someone's nerves
    4.9 0.24/m
    get on someone's nerves annoys by being persistent or hard to bear, while irk means a brief, milder annoyance.
  • aggravate
    4.2 1.69/m
    aggravate often means to make someone more annoyed or exasperated, or to make a problem worse, while irk is simpler.
  • gall
    7.1 0.63/m
    gall strongly annoys or angers someone and often causes deep resentment, while irk means a lighter, less bitter bother.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone a little angry; to bother
  • antonym: please - to give someone satisfaction or happiness
  • synonym: vex - to make someone feel annoyed or worried
