in passing

adverb
incidentally; while doing something else
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈpæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪn ˈpɑːsɪŋ / (BrE)
while doing or mentioning something else, without giving it much emphasis; incidentally

Examples

Mention in passing
He apologized in passing, hardly stopping to explain.
I learned about the news in passing from a colleague at lunch.
She mentioned her new project in passing during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicitly - clearly and directly, with emphasis
  • synonym: incidentally - without intention; by chance or without focusing on it
