due process

noun
proper legal procedure; legal safeguards
4.0 7/m
Pronunciation: / du Ààpro ä.s…õs / (AmE), / djuÀê Ààpr…ô ä.ses / (BrE)
(Law) the administration of justice in accordance with established legal rules and principles, especially as a safeguard against arbitrary denial of life, liberty, or property.

Examples

Due process of law
The Constitution guarantees due process of law to all citizens.
The defendant argued that his right to due process had been violated during the trial.
Without due process, people could be punished without any fair legal procedure.

Synonyms

  • due process of law
    5.5 0.5/m

Related Terms

  • synonym: natural justice - the principle of fairness in legal proceedings
  • antonym: summary judgment - legal judgment without full legal procedure or trial
