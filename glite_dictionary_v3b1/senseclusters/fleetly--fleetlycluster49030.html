fleetly

adverb
in a swift manner
7.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfliÀêtli / (AmE), / ÀàfliÀêtli / (BrE)
In a swift or rapid manner; quickly.

Examples

To move fleetly
She moved fleetly through the crowd, barely brushing past anyone.
The gazelle ran fleetly across the open plain to evade the lions.
The news spread fleetly throughout the small village.

Synonyms

  • quickly
    1.0 208/m
    quickly is used in everyday speech for high speed or no delay, while fleetly sounds more literary or old-fashioned.
  • swiftly
    1.0 8.45/m
    swiftly often shows smooth, graceful motion at very high speed, while fleetly sounds more poetic and old-fashioned.
  • rapidly
    1.4 42/m
    rapidly stresses great speed or quick change, often in formal contexts, while fleetly focuses on swift movement.
  • speedily
    2.4 0.76/m
    speedily suggests promptness and little delay, often in legal or formal writing, while fleetly implies swift movement.
  • briskly
    3.5 3.68/m
    briskly means quick and energetic movement or action, often lively or businesslike, while fleetly only means moving swiftly.
