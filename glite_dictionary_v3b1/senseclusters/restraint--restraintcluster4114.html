restraint

noun
lack of ornamentation; simplicity in design
7.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
the quality of avoiding excessive decoration or embellishment; simplicity or moderation in style or appearance

Examples

Restraint in design
Designers often praise restraint as key to creating sophisticated interiors.
She admired the restraint in the painting's composition and subtle colors.
The architect's restraint gave the building a timeless elegance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: austerity - plainness and simplicity in appearance or style
  • antonym: ornateness - the quality of being elaborate and highly decorated
