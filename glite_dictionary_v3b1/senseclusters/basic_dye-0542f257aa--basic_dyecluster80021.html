basic dye

noun
positively charged synthetic dye
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.sɪk daɪ / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.sɪk daɪ / (BrE)
a dye in which the chromophore is part of a positively charged ion, used especially to stain acidic components in biological specimens

Examples

Basic dye in biology
Methylene blue is a well-known example of a basic dye used in biological staining.
The scientist used a basic dye to stain the cell nuclei for easier observation under the microscope.
Unlike acid dyes, basic dyes are attracted to the acidic structures within cells.
