bark

verb
to cover with bark
Pronunciation: / bɑrk / (AmE), / bɑːk / (BrE)
to remove, use, or cover with the bark of a tree

Examples

To bark a tree
Gardeners sometimes bark the base of young trees to protect them from pests.
The craftsman decided to bark the handle for a rustic appearance.
To retain moisture, they will bark the flower beds each spring.
Before modern chemicals, craftsmen barked hides using oak and chestnut trees.
The process to bark the skins was lengthy but produced high-quality material.
Traditional tanneries would bark animal hides to create durable leather.
The workers barked hundreds of saplings for the new fence.
They needed to bark the logs before processing them at the sawmill.
You should bark the trunk carefully to avoid damaging the wood underneath.
