present perfect tense

noun
a verb tense indicating completed action connected to the present
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈprez.ənt ˈpɜːr.fɪkt tens/ (AmE), /ˈprez.ənt ˈpɜː.fɪkt tens/ (BrE)
a verb tense used to express an action that has been completed at the present time or has relevance to the present

Examples

The present perfect tense
In English, we use the present perfect tense to talk about experiences up to now.
Students sometimes confuse the present perfect tense with the simple past.
The sentence 'She has gone home' is in the present perfect tense.
