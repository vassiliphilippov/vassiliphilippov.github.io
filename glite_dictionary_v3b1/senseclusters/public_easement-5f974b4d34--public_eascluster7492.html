public easement

noun
legal right for public land use
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk ˈiːsmənt / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˈiːsmənt / (BrE)
a legal right allowing the general public to use certain land for a specific purpose, such as streets or pathways

Examples

A public easement right
A dispute arose when a developer attempted to block a public easement used as a shortcut by locals.
Neighborhood residents rallied to preserve the public easement that connected two parks.
The city created a public easement so pedestrians could access the riverfront.
