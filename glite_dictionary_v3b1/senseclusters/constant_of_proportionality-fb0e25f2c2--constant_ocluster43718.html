constant of proportionality

noun
constant in a proportional relationship
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnstənt əv prəˌpɔrʃəˈnælɪti / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt əv prəˌpɔːʃəˈnalɪti / (BrE)
a constant value that relates two quantities that are directly proportional; typically symbolized as k in the equation y = kx

Examples

The constant of proportionality value
In the equation y = kx, the constant of proportionality is represented by k.
The value of the constant of proportionality determines how steep the line is in a graph of y versus x.
To solve the problem, first find the constant of proportionality between distance and time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proportional - having a constant ratio between two quantities
