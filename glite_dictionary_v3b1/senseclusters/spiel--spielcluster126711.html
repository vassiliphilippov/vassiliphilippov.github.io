spiel

noun
glib sales talk or persuasive speech
7.9 1.06/m
Pronunciation: / spiÀêl / (AmE), / spiÀêl / (BrE)
a lengthy or glib talk or speech, especially one intended to persuade or promote something

Examples

A sales spiel
After listening to her long spiel, I still wasn't convinced to buy anything.
I quickly recognized his pitch as just another marketing spiel.
The salesman launched into his usual spiel about the benefits of the product.

Synonyms

  • sales pitch
    3.2 1.19/m
    a sales pitch is a focused presentation to persuade buyers, while a spiel is often longer, glib, and used to promote many things.
  • sales talk
    3.0 0.06/m
    sales talk uses general persuasive phrases aimed at selling, while a spiel is a rehearsed or glib speech that can be longer and broader.
  • line of gab
    8.1 0.00/m
    a line of gab is smooth, plausible talk meant to charm or influence people, while a spiel is more saleslike and often longer.
  • patter
    7.4 0.44/m
    patter is quick, smooth talk used by sellers or entertainers to charm, while a spiel is often longer and more glib or rehearsed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pitch - a persuasive sales talk or argument
