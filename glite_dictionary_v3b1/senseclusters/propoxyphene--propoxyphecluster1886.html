propoxyphene

noun
a synthetic opioid painkiller
Pronunciation: / proʊˈpɑksiˌfin / (AmE), / prəˈpɒksɪˌfiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic opioid pain-relieving drug that is less addictive than methadone; formerly marketed under the trade name Darvon

Examples

Propoxyphene pain medicine
Due to safety concerns, propoxyphene was withdrawn from the market in many countries.
Patients taking propoxyphene should be closely monitored for side effects.
The doctor prescribed propoxyphene to help manage her post-surgical pain.
