Synonyms

    3.2 50/m
    reduction refers to the process of making something smaller or less, while decrease names the amount or act of getting smaller.
  • diminution
    7.9 1.0/m
    diminution means a formal reduction in size or importance, while decrease is more common for any reduction.
  • diminishment
    6.7 0.3/m
    diminishment describes a process of becoming smaller or less important, while decrease names the amount or drop that happens.
  • lessening
    3.8 0.5/m
    lessening is a gradual drop in amount or intensity over time, while decrease is a general reduction in size or number.
  • decrement
    7.7 0.5/m
    decrement is a specific, often technical amount by which something falls, while decrease is the general idea of reduction.
  • lowering
    2.8 0.00/m
    lowering is the act of making something less, often by someone, while decrease describes the result or amount of that change.
  • shrinkage
    5.1 0.6/m
    shrinkage is the loss in size or value often seen in goods or profits, while decrease covers many kinds of reductions.
