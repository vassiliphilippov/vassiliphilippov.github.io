firebrand

noun
person who deliberately causes unrest or trouble
1.0 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər.brænd/ /; / /ˈfaɪr.brænd/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə.brænd/ (BrE)
a person who deliberately tries to cause unrest or provoke trouble, especially one who incites others to protest or rebel.

Examples

Political firebrand
She gained a reputation as a firebrand in her university days, leading protests and challenging authority.
The company had to address concerns after a firebrand employee incited a strike.
The political firebrand urged the crowd to demand justice.
