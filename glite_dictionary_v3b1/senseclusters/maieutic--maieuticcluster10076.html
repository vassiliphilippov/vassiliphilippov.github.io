maieutic

noun
question-based teaching
Pronunciation: / maɪˈjuːtɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈjuːtɪk / (BrE)
a method of teaching or eliciting knowledge by asking questions to stimulate critical thinking and draw out ideas, especially as used in the Socratic method

Examples

The maieutic approach
The professor used a maieutic to help students discover the answers on their own.
Many educators value the maieutic for encouraging independent thinking in the classroom.
Through a maieutic approach, the teacher guided the discussion without giving direct answers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Socratic method - a teaching method using questions to stimulate critical thinking
  • antonym: didactic - intended to teach, often in a direct way
