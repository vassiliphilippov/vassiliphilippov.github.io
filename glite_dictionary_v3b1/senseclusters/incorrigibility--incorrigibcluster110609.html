incorrigibility

noun
impossible to reform
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkɔːrɪdʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒrɪdʒəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to correct, reform, or improve

Examples

His incorrigibility problem
Despite many warnings, his incorrigibility made it impossible for the school to help him change.
The judge commented on the incorrigibility of the repeat offender during the sentencing.
Her incorrigibility frustrated her friends, who tried to encourage better habits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incorrigible - impossible to correct or reform
  • antonym: reformability - the ability to be improved or reformed
