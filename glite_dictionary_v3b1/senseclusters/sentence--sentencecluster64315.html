sentence

noun
period of imprisonment as punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntəns /; / ˈsɛn.təns / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəns /; / ˈsɛn.təns / (BrE)
the punishment given by a court to someone found guilty of a crime (the judgment or the time to be spent in prison)

Examples

A prison sentence
She was released after serving half her sentence for theft.
The judge gave him a sentence of ten years in prison.
The lawyer argued for a reduced sentence based on mitigating circumstances.
After pleading guilty, he received a five-year sentence in prison.
Her sentence was reduced because she cooperated with the authorities.
The judge will announce the sentence at tomorrow's hearing.
