Protestant Episcopal Church

proper noun
Anglican Church branch in the United States
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊˈtɛstənt ɪˈpɪskəpəl tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌprɒtɪˈstɑːnt ɪˈpɪskəpəl tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The branch of the Anglican Communion in the United States, also known as the Episcopal Church, historically in communication with the see of Canterbury.

Examples

The Protestant Episcopal Church in America
Many leaders of the Protestant Episcopal Church have played significant roles in U.S. history.

The Protestant Episcopal Church traces its origins to the Church of England after the American Revolution.
