archesporium

noun
primitive reproductive cell in plants or fungi
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrkəˈspɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃɪˈspɔːrɪəm / (BrE)
a primitive cell or group of cells in plants or fungi from which a mother cell develops, eventually leading to spore formation

Examples

The archesporium cell
In mosses, the archesporium is located at the tip of the sporangium.
Researchers identified abnormal development of the archesporium under experimental conditions.
The archesporium gives rise to the mother cells that will eventually form spores.
