nonconductor

noun
material that does not conduct electricity or heat
7.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒnÉ‘n.kÉ™nËˆdÊŒk.tÉš / (AmE), / ËŒnÉ’n.kÉ™nËˆdÊŒk.tÉ™ / (BrE)
A material, such as glass or porcelain, that does not conduct electricity or heat to a significant degree.

Examples

A nonconductor material
Because rubber is a good nonconductor, it is used to coat the handles of electrical tools.
Glass is commonly used as a nonconductor in laboratories to prevent electrical accidents.
The technician replaced the metal rods with nonconductors to ensure safety in the experiment.

Synonyms

  • insulator
    4.7 0.83/m
    an insulator is a common material that blocks electricity or heat in everyday use, while a nonconductor is a technical term for the same property.
  • dielectric
    9.3 0.21/m
    a dielectric is a technical insulating material used in capacitors and electronics, while a nonconductor is a general term for any material that blocks conduction.

Related Terms
