melt

verb
change from solid to liquid by heat
1.0
Pronunciation: / mɛlt / (AmE), / mɛlt / (BrE)
to change or make something change from a solid to a liquid, usually by heating

Examples

To melt chocolate
If you leave butter near the stove, it will quickly melt.
She likes to melt chocolate before adding it to the cake batter.
The sun was so strong that it began to melt the ice on the sidewalk.
If you leave the snowman in the sun, he will quickly melt.
She watched the butter melt in the hot frying pan.
The summer heat made the chocolate melt in my hand.
