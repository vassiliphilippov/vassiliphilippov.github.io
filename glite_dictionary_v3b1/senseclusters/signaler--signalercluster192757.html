signaler

noun
person who communicates by signals
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡnələr /; / ˈsaɪnələr / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnələ / (BrE)
a person or thing that gives a signal or communicates using signals instead of spoken or written language; often used for a person whose job is to send or interpret signals, especially in the military or railways.

Examples

A signaler at work
A skilled signaler is essential in situations where radios cannot be used.
During the exercise, the signaler maintained communication between the teams using hand gestures.
The signaler used flags to convey urgent information across the field.
The flashing light was a clear signaler of danger ahead.
In the army, a signaler is responsible for sending coded messages.
Birds often act as a signaler of environmental changes in their habitat.
During the exercise, a signaller transmitted coded messages to the command post.
In the army, a skilled signaller ensures that vital information is communicated quickly and accurately.
The signaller raised a red flag to stop the approaching train.

Related Terms

  • synonym: messenger - a person who carries or delivers messages
