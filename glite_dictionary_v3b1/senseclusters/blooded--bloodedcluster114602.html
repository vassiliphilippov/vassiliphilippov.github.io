blooded

adjective
having pure ancestry; of recognized pedigree
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
(of an animal or, rarely, a person) having pure or unmixed ancestry or breeding; of a recognized pedigree.

Examples

A blooded horse
A blooded horse often commands a higher price at auction.
Only blooded animals are eligible for entry in the competition.
The farm prides itself on raising only blooded cattle with detailed pedigrees.
