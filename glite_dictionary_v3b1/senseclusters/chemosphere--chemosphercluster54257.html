chemosphere

noun
atmospheric chemical layer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmoʊˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkiːməʊˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the region of a planet's atmosphere where chemical reactions occur due to solar radiation

Examples

Atmospheric chemosphere
Researchers launched a balloon to collect data from the chemosphere above the Arctic Circle.
The chemosphere plays a crucial role in filtering harmful solar radiation before it reaches the surface.
Changes in the chemosphere can affect the composition of a planet's lower atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ozonosphere - the atmospheric layer containing a high concentration of ozone
  • synonym: stratosphere - the layer of the atmosphere above the troposphere
