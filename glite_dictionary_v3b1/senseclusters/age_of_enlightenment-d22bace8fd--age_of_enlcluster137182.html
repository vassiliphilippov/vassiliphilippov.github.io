Age of Enlightenment

noun
European intellectual movement (17th–18th c.)
5.8
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒ əv ɪnˈlaɪtənmənt / (AmE), / eɪdʒ əv ɪnˈlaɪtənmənt / (BrE)
A European intellectual movement from the mid-17th to the late 18th century that emphasized reason, individualism, and skepticism of traditional doctrines.

Examples

The Age of Enlightenment period
Historians often debate the impact of the Age of Enlightenment on modern scientific thinking.
Many philosophers during the Age of Enlightenment challenged the authority of monarchies and the church.
The Age of Enlightenment inspired political revolutions across Europe and the Americas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Enlightenment - the period in the 18th century marked by emphasis on reason and science
