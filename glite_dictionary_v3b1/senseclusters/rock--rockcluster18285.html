rock

verb
move back and forth or sideways
1.0 16/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ‘k / (AmE), / rÉ’k / (BrE)
to move gently or repeatedly back and forth or from side to side, often to comfort or calm

Examples

To rock a baby
He rocked back and forth nervously while waiting for his interview.
Strong winds rocked the boat from side to side all night.
The baby fell asleep while her mother gently rocked the cradle.
He likes to rock back and forth in his chair while thinking.
She gently rocked the cradle until the baby fell asleep.
Strong winds rocked the boat from side to side.
She was rocking the baby gently until he drifted off to sleep.
The old chair creaked as he sat down and started rocking slowly.
He found comfort in rocking back and forth while listening to music.

Synonyms

  • sway
    2.4 8.40/m
    sway moves slowly and rhythmically, often as a whole body or object, while rock is gentler and used to comfort or calm.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: shake - to move quickly up and down or side to side
  • antonym: still - to make or become motionless
  • synonym: sway - to move slowly or rhythmically backward and forward or from side to side
