Athapascan

noun
a group of Native American languages
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈpæskən / (BrE)
a family of Amerindian languages spoken in North America, especially in western Canada, Alaska, and the southwestern United States

Examples

The Athapascan language
Linguists have studied the migration patterns of Athapascan speakers for decades.
Many Native communities in Alaska speak a variety of Athapascan languages.
The Athapascan language family includes Navajo and Apache.
