preemptory

adjective
demanding obedience
Pronunciation: / priˈɛmptəri / (AmE), / priˈɛmptəri / (BrE)
insisting on immediate attention or obedience, often in a commanding or abrupt way

Examples

Preemptory command
The manager's preemptory instructions left the team with no chance to ask questions.
She gave a preemptory order to clear the room immediately.
His preemptory manner made it difficult for others to express their opinions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lenient - not strict or harsh; tolerant
  • synonym: peremptory - insisting on immediate attention or obedience
