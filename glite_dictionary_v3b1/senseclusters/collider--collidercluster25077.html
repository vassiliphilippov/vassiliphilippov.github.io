collider

noun
game-engine collision component
Pronunciation: / kəˈlaɪdər / (AmE), / kəˈlaɪdər / (BrE)
(in game development) a software component or shape attached to an object that defines its physical boundary used by the physics engine for collision detection and response.

Examples

Box collider
In Unity, attach a box collider to the player to enable physics collisions during gameplay.
The game's performance dropped because dozens of complex colliders were active on every frame.
To fix jitter, the developer replaced the mesh with a simpler collider shape for stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collision detection - the process or system that determines when two objects in a simulation intersect or contact
  • synonym: hitbox - an invisible shape used in games to determine when an object has been hit or intersected
