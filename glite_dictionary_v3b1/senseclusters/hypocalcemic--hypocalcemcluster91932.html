hypocalcemic

adjective
low blood calcium
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊkælˈsiːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəʊkælˈsiːmɪk / (BrE)
having an abnormally low level of calcium in the blood

Examples

Hypocalcemic symptoms
The doctor explained that a hypocalcemic condition can cause muscle cramps and weakness.
After surgery, the patient became hypocalcemic and required calcium supplements.
A hypocalcemic episode may be detected through routine blood tests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypercalcemic - having an abnormally high level of calcium in the blood
