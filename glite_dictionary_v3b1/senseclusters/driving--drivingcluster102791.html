driving

adjective
having strong motivating force
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
having strong force, energy, or motivation; providing the power behind something

Examples

Driving force
Her driving ambition led her to become the youngest CEO in the company's history.
His driving determination impressed everyone he worked with.
The driving force behind the project was a desire to help the local community.
He is recognized for his driving leadership style and relentless pursuit of success.
Her driving ambition inspired the whole team to work harder.
The company's driving force was its innovative approach to problem-solving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: energetic - having a lot of energy and enthusiasm
  • synonym: forceful - full of force; powerful
  • synonym: impelling - having the power to drive or urge forward
  • antonym: restraining - holding back or preventing forward movement
  • antonym: timid - lacking self-confidence, hesitant or fearful
