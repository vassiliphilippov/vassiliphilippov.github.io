redocument

verb
to issue documents again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈdɑːkjəmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdɒkjʊmənt / (BrE)
to provide or obtain official documents again, especially to replace lost or outdated paperwork or to re-register something.

Examples

To redocument company records
After the merger, the company had to redocument all of its assets under the new name.
She needed to redocument her vehicle after losing the original registration papers.
The agency will redocument your status if your previous documents have expired.

Related Terms

  • antonym: invalidate - to make a document or agreement no longer valid
  • synonym: reissue - to issue something again, especially a document
