snivel

verb
cry or whine with snuffling
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsnɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsnɪvəl / (BrE)
to cry, complain, or speak in a tearful, whining, or self-pitying way

Examples

To snivel about work
Don't snivel about your chores—just get them done.
He began to snivel when his request was denied.
The child continued to snivel long after the argument ended.
He began to snivel when told he couldn't have any more candy.
She sniveled about how unfair the decision was.
Stop sniveling and explain what happened calmly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • synonym: whimper - to make low, feeble sounds expressive of fear, pain, or unhappiness
  • synonym: whine - to complain in a feeble or petulant way
