jetsam

noun
goods thrown overboard from a ship
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛtsəm /; / ˈdʒɛt.səm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛtsəm /; / ˈdʒɛt.səm / (BrE)
objects or material thrown from a ship into the sea, especially to lighten the ship or as a result of a shipwreck, often found floating or washed ashore

Examples

Thrown jetsam at sea
During the storm, the crew threw jetsam into the sea to keep the ship afloat.
Much of the beach was scattered with jetsam after the hurricane had passed.
The coastguard identified the jetsam as spare parts from a cargo vessel.
After the storm, jetsam from the wrecked boat washed up along the shoreline.
Fishermen discovered pieces of jetsam floating among the seaweed near the harbor.
The coastline was littered with jetsam, evidence of the severe shipwreck.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flotsam - wreckage or cargo found floating on the sea after a shipwreck
  • synonym: lagan - goods thrown into the sea with a buoy attached, expected to be recovered
