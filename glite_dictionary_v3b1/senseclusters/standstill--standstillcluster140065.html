standstill

noun
complete halt of activity or movement
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstændˌstɪl / (AmE), / ˈstandˌstɪl /; / ˈstændˌstɪl / (BrE)
a situation where movement or progress stops completely

Examples

A standstill in traffic
After the accident, traffic came to a complete standstill on the highway.
Heavy snow brought the city to a standstill overnight.
Negotiations reached a standstill, and neither side was willing to compromise.
Negotiations between the two companies reached a standstill last week.
The project was at a standstill until new funding was secured.
Traffic came to a standstill after the accident on the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made; a standstill
  • synonym: halt - a temporary stop or pause
  • synonym: impasse - a situation in which no progress is possible
  • antonym: motion - the act of moving
