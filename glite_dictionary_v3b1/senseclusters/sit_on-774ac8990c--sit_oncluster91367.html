sit on

verb
postpone making a decision
2.6
Pronunciation: / sɪt ɑn / (AmE), / sɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to delay making a decision about something, often while considering it carefully

Examples

To sit on a decision
I'm going to sit on this job offer before giving my answer.
The board chose to sit on the recommendations until after the next meeting.
They prefer to sit on difficult issues rather than address them immediately.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - to make a decision or choice
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something to a later time
