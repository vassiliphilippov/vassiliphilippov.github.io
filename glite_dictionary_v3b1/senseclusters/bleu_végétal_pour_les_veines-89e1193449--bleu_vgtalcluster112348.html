bleu végétal pour les veines

noun
historical cosmetic for imitating veins
9.9
Pronunciation: /bluː ˈve.dʒə.təl pɔːr leɪ veɪnz/ /; / /bluː ve.ˈʒe.təl pɔːr le veɪnz/ (AmE), /bluː ˈve.dʒə.təl pɔː leɪ veɪnz/ /; / /bluː ve.ˈʒe.təl pɔː le veɪnz/ (BrE)
A blue-colored cosmetic preparation, traditionally in stick or pencil form, used from ancient times to the twentieth century to draw blue lines on the skin to imitate veins and accentuate a pale complexion.

Examples

Ancient bleu vegetal pour les veines
Historical portraits sometimes reveal faint traces of bleu végétal pour les veines along the temples and neck.
In Victorian England, aristocratic women often used bleu végétal pour les veines to enhance the illusion of delicately pale skin.
The museum collection includes several sticks of bleu végétal pour les veines among other 19th-century beauty products.
