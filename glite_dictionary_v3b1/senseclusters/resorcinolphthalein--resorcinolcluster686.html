resorcinolphthalein

noun
yellow dye and chemical indicator
9.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.zɔːr.sɪ.nəl.f.ˈθeɪ.lɪn/ /; / /rɪ.zɔːr.sɪ.nəl.f.ˈθeɪ.liːn/ (AmE), /rɪ.zɔː.sɪ.nɒl.f.ˈθeɪ.lɪn/ /; / /rɪ.zɔː.sɪ.nɒl.f.ˈθeɪ.liːn/ (BrE)
A yellow dye used as an absorption indicator, turning pink to signal the absence of chloride ions when used with silver nitrate and sodium chloride solutions.

Examples

Resorcinolphthalein indicator dye
As an indicator, resorcinolphthalein turns pink when chloride ions are no longer present in the solution.
Even at high dilution, resorcinolphthalein remains visible due to its intense yellow color.
The chemical laboratory stocked resorcinolphthalein for use in absorption indicator experiments.
