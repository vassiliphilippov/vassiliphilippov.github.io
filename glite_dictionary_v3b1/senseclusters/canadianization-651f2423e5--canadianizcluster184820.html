Canadianization

noun
adopting Canadian culture
8.0
Pronunciation: / kəˌneɪdiənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌneɪdiənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more Canadian in character, customs, ownership, or influence

Examples

The Canadianization of culture
The Canadianization of the curriculum included lessons on Canadian history and government.
After several years, his Canadianization was evident in his accent and daily habits.
Many immigrants experience Canadianization as they adapt to life in Canada.
The Canadianization of the curriculum included more local history and indigenous perspectives.
Many businesses underwent Canadianization to better appeal to local customers.
The film industry saw a wave of Canadianization in its storytelling and themes.
The government introduced new laws to encourage the Canadianization of the media industry.
Many saw the Canadianization of local businesses as a way to protect national identity.
Debates about Canadianization often arise when foreign companies invest in Canadian resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming part of a different culture or society
  • synonym: nationalization - the process of making something more characteristic of a particular nation
