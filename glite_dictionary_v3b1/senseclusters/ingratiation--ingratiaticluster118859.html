ingratiation

noun
the act of gaining favor by pleasing or flattering
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌɡreɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌɡreɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of trying to gain someone's favor or approval by deliberate efforts, often through flattery or pleasing behavior

Examples

Ingratiation with boss
His constant ingratiation with the boss annoyed his coworkers.
Politicians sometimes rely on ingratiation to win public support.
She viewed his compliments as mere ingratiation rather than genuine praise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flattery - excessive and insincere praise intended to gain favor
  • antonym: sincerity - genuine or honest expression, absence of deceit or pretense
