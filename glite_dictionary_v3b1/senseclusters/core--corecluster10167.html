core

noun
cylindrical soil or rock sample
Pronunciation: / kɔr / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
a cylindrical sample of soil or rock obtained with a hollow drill, often used for analysis or testing

Examples

A rock core
A core was extracted from the ice sheet to study climate history.
Engineers analyzed the rock core from the drilling site.
The geologist examined the core for signs of mineral deposits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sample - a small part or quantity intended to show what the whole is like
