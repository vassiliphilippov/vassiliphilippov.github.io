leucothoe

noun
shrub with white flowers; sometimes poisonous
Pronunciation: / luˈkɑːθoʊ.i / (AmE), / ljuːˈkɒθəʊ.i / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Leucothoe, valued for its attractive white bell-shaped flowers and glossy, often evergreen leaves, some species of which contain toxic compounds.

Examples

The leucothoe shrub
Gardeners should be aware that the leaves of leucothoe may be toxic if ingested by pets or children.
He chose leucothoe for the shady border because of its glossy foliage and graceful arching branches.
The leucothoe in her garden blossomed in early spring, filling the air with a delicate fragrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: andromeda - a related genus of flowering shrubs in the same family as Leucothoe
  • synonym: kalmia - a related genus containing similar poisonous compounds
