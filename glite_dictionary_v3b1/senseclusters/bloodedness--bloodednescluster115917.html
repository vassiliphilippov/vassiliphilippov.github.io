bloodedness

noun
state of blood type
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈblʌdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a particular type or condition of blood, often used in reference to animals or breeds

Examples

Horse bloodedness
The breeder emphasized the bloodedness of the horses to attract buyers.
Scientists study the bloodedness of reptiles to understand their evolution.
Some dog shows judge animals based on their bloodedness and lineage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pedigree - the recorded ancestry or lineage of an animal
