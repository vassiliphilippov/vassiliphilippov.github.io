relicense

verb
to grant new license
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
to grant a new license or official permission for something, especially after the original license has expired or changed

Examples

To relicense a business
The city decided to relicense all food vendors after updating health regulations.
After the merger, the company had to relicense its products in several countries.
You must relicense your software each year to continue receiving updates.
The city decided to relicense the restaurant after it passed all health inspections.
Software developers may need to relicense their code to comply with new regulations.
After the agreement expired, the company had to relicense the product to continue selling it.
The city decided to relicense all food vendors after updating health regulations.
After the merger, the company had to relicense its products under the new brand.
You must relicense your software every year to continue using it legally.

  • synonym: permit - to allow or give permission for something
  • antonym: prohibit - to officially forbid something
  • synonym: renew - to extend the period of validity of something
  • antonym: revoke - to officially cancel a license or permission
