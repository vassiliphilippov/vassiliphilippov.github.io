trillion

noun
one followed by 18 zeros (British, dated)
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪljən / (AmE), / ˈtrɪljən / (BrE)
(British, dated) The number represented as 1 followed by 18 zeros (1,000,000,000,000,000,000); a million million million.

Examples

Historic trillion
In historic British texts, a trillion referred to a million million million, not simply a thousand billion.
Scientific papers from the 19th century sometimes mention quantities as large as a trillion in this sense.
The national debt would have been estimated in trillions under the old British system.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: billion - the number represented as 1 followed by 12 zeros (in modern usage)
  • degree higher: quadrillion - the number represented as 1 followed by 24 zeros (British, dated)
  • antonym: trillion - the number represented as 1 followed by 12 zeros (American, now also standard British)
