Siberian elm

noun
fast-growing Asian ornamental tree
Pronunciation: / səˈbɪriən ɛlm / (AmE), / sɪˈbɪəriən ɛlm / (BrE)
a fast-growing deciduous tree (Ulmus pumila) native to Asia, often cultivated for shelter or ornament, especially in the United States

Examples

The Siberian elm tree
Many shelterbelts in the Midwest include the resilient Siberian elm.
The Siberian elm is valued for its ability to withstand harsh climates.
The rapid growth of the Siberian elm makes it a popular choice for urban planting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elm - a type of deciduous tree with serrated leaves
