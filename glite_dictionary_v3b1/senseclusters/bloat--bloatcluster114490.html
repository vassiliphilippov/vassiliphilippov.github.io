bloat

noun
gas swelling in animals
Pronunciation: / bloʊt / (AmE), / bləʊt / (BrE)
A swelling of the rumen or intestinal tract in domestic animals, caused by excessive gas accumulation.

Examples

Cattle bloat
If bloat is not treated quickly, it can be fatal for cattle.
Sheep can develop bloat if they graze too much on lush, wet pasture.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with bloat after noticing its distended abdomen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tympany - swelling or distention due to gas in a body cavity, especially in animals
