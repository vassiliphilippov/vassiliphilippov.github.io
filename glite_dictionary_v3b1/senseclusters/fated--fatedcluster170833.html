fated

adjective
destined or predetermined by fate
5.3 2.12/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfeÉªtÉªd / (AmE), / ËˆfeÉªtÉªd / (BrE)
Certain to happen or destined to be, especially as if by fate; often used as 'fated to' followed by a verb or outcome.

Examples

Fated to fail
He felt fated to repeat his father's mistakes, no matter how hard he tried.
The company seemed fated to struggle despite all their best efforts.
They believed their love was fated from the beginning and nothing could change it.

Synonyms

  • destined
    4.1 0.15/m
    destined often shows a planned or intended future path, while fated suggests an outside force deciding what happens.
  • predestined
    7.7 0.00/m
    predestined says an outcome is fixed beforehand by fate or divine will, while fated feels more general.
  • foreordained
    9.9 0.06/m
    foreordained implies a formally set, unchangeable outcome often with religious tone, while fated is more everyday destiny talk.
  • bound
    4.2 2.39/m
    bound is commonly used as 'bound to' for expected actions, while fated often stresses destiny or fate.
  • meant to be
    3.7 8.51/m
    meant to be sounds like a hopeful plan or purpose between people, while fated suggests impersonal destiny beyond choice.
  • inevitable
    5.3 0.01/m
    inevitable stresses that something cannot be stopped or avoided, while fated adds the idea it is caused by fate.
  • foregone
    4.6 1.09/m
    foregone often appears in 'foregone conclusion' to show an already expected result, while fated focuses on destiny deciding it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destined - certain to meet a particular fate
  • synonym: doomed - certain to fail or suffer; unable to avoid a bad outcome
