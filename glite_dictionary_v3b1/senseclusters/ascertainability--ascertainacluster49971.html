ascertainability

noun
ability to be determined
Pronunciation: / ˌæsərˌteɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌæsəˌteɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be found out, identified, or determined

Examples

The ascertainability of facts
The ascertainability of the facts was crucial to the outcome of the investigation.
Lawyers discussed the ascertainability of the group affected by the new regulation.
The judge questioned the ascertainability of the evidence presented in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: determinability - the quality of being able to be determined
  • antonym: uncertainty - the state of being unknown or not able to be determined
