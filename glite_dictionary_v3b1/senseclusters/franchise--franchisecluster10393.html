noun
a right or privilege granted by government

a statutory right or privilege granted by a government to a person or group, especially the right to vote in public elections

Examples

The franchise to vote
After years of protest, the government finally granted the franchise to all adult citizens.
Many countries extended the franchise to women in the early twentieth century.
The movement for civil rights was focused on expanding the franchise and ending discriminatory laws.

Synonyms

  • right to vote
    2.3 3.82/m
    right to vote is the legal entitlement to cast ballots in elections, while franchise is the formal statutory grant of that privilege.
  • suffrage
    7.6 2.50/m
    suffrage is the formal constitutional right to vote in public elections, while franchise is any statutory right or privilege granted by government.
  • voting right
    3.3 1.08/m
    voting right is the legal or official ability to take part in a vote, while franchise is a formal government-granted privilege.
  • vote
    1.0 20/m
    vote is the act of casting a ballot in a choice or election, while franchise is the legal privilege allowing people to vote.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disenfranchisement - being deprived of the right to vote
  • synonym: suffrage - the right to vote in political elections
