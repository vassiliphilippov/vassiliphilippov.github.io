vasoconstrictor

noun
agent that narrows blood vessels
7.3 0.09/m

a substance or agent that causes blood vessels to narrow, often increasing blood pressure

Examples

A vasoconstrictor substance
Cold temperatures are a natural vasoconstrictor, causing vessels to become narrower.
Doctors may use a vasoconstrictor to control bleeding during surgery.
Nicotine acts as a vasoconstrictor, which can raise your blood pressure.

Synonyms

  • vasopressor
    8.1 0.02/m
    vasopressor is a medical drug used to raise blood pressure by narrowing blood vessels, while vasoconstrictor is a broader agent that narrows vessels.
  • pressor
    9.9 0.10/m
    pressor is a substance that raises blood pressure by constricting blood vessels, while vasoconstrictor names any agent that narrows vessels.
