H

noun
thermodynamics: symbol for enthalpy
8.9
Pronunciation: /eɪtʃ/ (AmE), /eɪtʃ/ (BrE)
The symbol for enthalpy, a thermodynamic quantity equal to the internal energy of a system plus the product of its pressure and volume.

Examples

H in equations
In thermodynamic equations, H is often used to represent the heat content of a system.
Scientists use H to calculate the enthalpy change of chemical processes.
The change in H during the reaction determines whether heat is absorbed or released.
