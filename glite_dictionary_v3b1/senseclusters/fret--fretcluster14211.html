fret

noun
a worn or eroded spot
Pronunciation: / frɛt / (AmE), / frɛt / (BrE)
a spot or place that has been worn away by abrasion or erosion, often found on metals, stones, or other solid surfaces.

Examples

A fret on metal
Engineers studied the fret on the metal component to determine the extent of corrosion.
The statue's surface showed several frets caused by centuries of weathering.
Tiny frets appeared on the rock after years of exposure to wind and rain.
