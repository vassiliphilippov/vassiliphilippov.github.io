Gondwanaland

noun
prehistoric supercontinent including southern landmasses
Pronunciation: / ɡɑnˈdwɑnəˌlænd / (AmE), / ɡɒnˈdwɑːnəˌlænd / (BrE)
A prehistoric supercontinent that existed from the late Precambrian to the Jurassic period, comprising what are now South America, Africa, Antarctica, Australia, and the Indian subcontinent.

Examples

Gondwanaland supercontinent
During the Jurassic period, Gondwanaland began to fragment into several major continents.
Scientists have found fossils in Antarctica that provide evidence of its connection to Gondwanaland.
The break-up of Gondwanaland played a crucial role in shaping the continents as we know them today.
