grin and bear it

verb
endure without complaint
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪn ænd ˈbɛr ɪt / (AmE), / ɡrɪn ənd ˈbeə r ɪt / (BrE)
to accept an unpleasant situation without complaining or showing how upset you are

Examples

To grin and bear it patiently
Even though the service was slow, she chose to grin and bear it rather than make a fuss.
Sometimes we just have to grin and bear it when faced with unavoidable problems.
With a broken air conditioner on a hot day, he decided to grin and bear it until help arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym: put up with - to tolerate or endure something unpleasant
