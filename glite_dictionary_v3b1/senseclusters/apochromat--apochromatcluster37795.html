apochromat

noun
color-corrected lens
Pronunciation: / ˌæpoʊˈkroʊmæt / (AmE), / ˌapəˈkrəʊmat / (BrE)
a lens designed to bring three different wavelengths of light into focus at the same point, reducing color distortion

Examples

A microscope apochromat
The scientist used an apochromat to achieve sharper images in her microscope experiments.
Modern telescopes often include an apochromat to minimize color distortion in astronomical observations.
He replaced the standard lens with an apochromat for improved clarity in his photography.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: achromat - a lens that brings two wavelengths of light into focus at the same point
  • antonym: chromatic aberration - color distortion caused by a lens failing to focus all colors to the same point
