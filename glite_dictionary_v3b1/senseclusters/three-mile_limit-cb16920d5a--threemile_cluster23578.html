three-mile limit

noun
boundary of territorial waters (formerly three nautical miles)
5.0 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈθriː ˌmaɪl ˈlɪmɪt / (AmE), / ˈθriː ˌmaɪl ˈlɪmɪt / (BrE)
the boundary extending three nautical miles from a nation's coastline, formerly recognized as the extent of its territorial waters

Examples

Until the 20th century, the three-mile limit was commonly accepted by many countries.
