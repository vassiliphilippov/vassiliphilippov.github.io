crust

noun
outer layer of the Earth
1.0 4.0
Pronunciation: /krʌst/ (AmE), /krʌst/ (BrE)
the hard outer layer of the Earth, lying above the mantle and forming the continents and ocean floors

Examples

Earth's crust
Scientists study earthquakes to learn more about the movement of the crust.
The Earth's crust is much thinner under the oceans than under the continents.
Volcanoes form where the crust is especially weak or thin.
