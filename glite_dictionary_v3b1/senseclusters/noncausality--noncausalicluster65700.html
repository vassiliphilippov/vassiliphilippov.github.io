noncausality

noun
output depends on future
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkɔːˈzæləti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkɔːˈzælɪti / (BrE)
the property of a system whose output depends on future inputs, making it unable to operate in real time

Examples

Noncausality in signal processing
Because of its noncausality, the algorithm cannot be used for real-time signal processing.
The concept of noncausality is important when designing predictive filters in engineering.
Engineers must avoid noncausality if the system needs to respond instantly to inputs.
