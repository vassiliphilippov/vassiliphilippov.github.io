resew

verb
sew again
Pronunciation: / riˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsəʊ / (BrE)
to sew something again, especially after it has come undone or needs strengthening

Examples

To resew a button
After noticing the loose button, she decided to resew it properly.
If the patch falls off, just resew it instead of buying a new shirt.
The tailor had to resew the seam to ensure the jacket would not come apart again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sew - to join or fasten by stitches using a needle and thread
