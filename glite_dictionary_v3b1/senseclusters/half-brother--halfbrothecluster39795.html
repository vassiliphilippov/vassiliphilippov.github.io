half-brother

noun
brother with one parent in common
Pronunciation: / ˈhæfˌbrʌðɚ /; / ˈhæf ˌbrʌðɚ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌbrʌðə /; / ˈhɑːf ˌbrʌðə / (BrE)
a brother who shares only one parent with you (either the same mother or the same father)

Examples

My half-brother
Both boys share the same father but are half-brothers.
His half-brother visited from another city over the holidays.
She learned about her half-brother when she was an adult.
After her father remarried, Julia gained a half-brother from his second marriage.
He and his half-brother grew up in different cities but became close later in life.
My half-brother is five years younger than me and we share the same father.

Related Terms

  • synonym: half-sister - a sister who has only one parent in common with you
  • antonym: stepbrother - a brother who is the son of your stepparent by a previous marriage
