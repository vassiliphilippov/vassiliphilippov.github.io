linearizable

adjective
consistent operation order
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪniərˌaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪniəraɪzəbəl / (BrE)
having the property that operations appear to occur in a single, consistent order, as if each happens instantaneously between its start and finish

Examples

Linearizable database operations
The new database system is linearizable, ensuring that all transactions appear in a consistent order.
Engineers prefer linearizable algorithms for their strong guarantees in concurrent environments.
It is difficult to design a linearizable data structure that is also highly efficient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atomic - indivisible and uninterruptible in execution
  • antonym: nonlinearizable - not having a consistent, single order of operations
