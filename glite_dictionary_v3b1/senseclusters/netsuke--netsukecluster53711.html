netsuke

noun
Japanese carved toggle
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛt.suː.keɪ / (AmE), / ˈnɛt.suː.keɪ / (BrE)
a small, decorative Japanese carving used as a toggle to fasten items to a sash, especially on traditional clothing

Examples

A netsuke carving
The museum displayed a collection of intricately carved netsuke from the Edo period.
He attached his inro to his sash using a beautiful ivory netsuke.
Collectors often admire the craftsmanship and detail found in each netsuke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ojime - a bead used to fasten the cord of a Japanese inro
  • synonym: toggle - a small device used to fasten or secure something
