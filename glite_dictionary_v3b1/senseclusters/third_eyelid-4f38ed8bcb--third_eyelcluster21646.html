third eyelid

noun
protective membrane in animal eyes
Pronunciation: / θɜrd ˈaɪˌlɪd / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈaɪ.lɪd / (BrE)
a thin, protective membrane that can be drawn across the eye in reptiles, birds, and some mammals, acting as an additional eyelid.

Examples

A third eyelid in birds
Many birds have a third eyelid that keeps their eyes moist while flying.
Some reptiles can see through their partially transparent third eyelid while it covers their eye.
The veterinarian explained that a cat’s third eyelid helps protect its eyes from debris.
