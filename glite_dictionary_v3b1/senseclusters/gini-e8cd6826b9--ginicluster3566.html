Gini

noun
impurity measure
6.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íiÀêni / (AmE), / Ààd íiÀêni / (BrE)
a statistical measure used to show how evenly something (like income, wealth, or data) is distributed; used in economics and machine learning

Examples

Gini index value
The algorithm uses the Gini index to determine the best feature for splitting the data.
A lower Gini value indicates a purer node in the decision tree.
Many machine learning libraries allow you to choose between Gini and entropy as splitting criteria.
The country's Gini has increased, showing a widening gap between rich and poor.
Economists often use the Gini to compare inequality across different nations.
A lower Gini suggests a more equal distribution of income among the population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gini coefficient - a numerical value representing income or wealth inequality
  • synonym: Lorenz curve - a graphical representation of income or wealth distribution
  • synonym: entropy - a measure of uncertainty or impurity in information theory and machine learning
