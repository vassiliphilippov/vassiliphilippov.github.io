Carboniferous

proper noun
geological period, 359–299 million years ago
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrbəˈnɪfərəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbənˈɪfərəs / (BrE)
the geological period, lasting from about 359 to 299 million years ago, characterized by the formation of extensive coal beds

Examples

The Carboniferous period
Fossils from the Carboniferous provide insights into the evolution of early amphibians and plants.
Geologists study the Carboniferous period to understand the origins of major coal deposits.
The Carboniferous was marked by warm, humid climates and lush vegetation.
