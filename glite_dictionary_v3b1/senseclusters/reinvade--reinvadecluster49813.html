reinvade

verb
to invade again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈveɪd / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈveɪd / (BrE)
to enter a place or country again by force, especially with an army

Examples

To reinvade a country
After losing control, the general decided to reinvade the region with a larger force.
The country threatened to reinvade its neighbor if peace talks failed.
Historians debated whether the army should have reinvaded the territory so soon after retreating.
The military decided to reinvade the region after peace talks failed.
After losing control, the general ordered his troops to reinvade the city.
Historians debated whether the country would ever reinvade its former territory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invade - to enter a place or country by force with an army
  • antonym: withdraw - to leave a place or position, especially an occupied one
