reinvade

verb
to invade again
7.4
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪn.ˈveɪd/ /; / /rɪ.ɪn.ˈveɪd/ /; / /rə.ɪn.ˈveɪd/ (AmE), /riː.ɪn.ˈveɪd/ /; / /rɪ.ɪn.ˈveɪd/ /; / /rə.ɪn.ˈveɪd/ (BrE)
to enter a place or country again by force, especially with an army

Examples

To reinvade a country

Historians debated whether the army should have reinvaded the territory so soon after retreating.

After losing control, the general ordered his troops to reinvade the city.
Historians debated whether the country would ever reinvade its former territory.
