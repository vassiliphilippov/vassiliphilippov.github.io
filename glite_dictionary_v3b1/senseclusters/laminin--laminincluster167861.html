laminin

noun
cell-adhesion protein
Pronunciation: / ˈlæmɪnɪn / (AmE), / ˈlæmɪnɪn / (BrE)
a protein in the extracellular matrix that helps cells attach to each other and maintain tissue structure

Examples

laminin protein
Scientists discovered that laminin plays a crucial role in tissue repair and development.
The presence of laminin helps cells stay connected within the extracellular matrix.
Researchers are investigating how laminin influences the growth of nerve cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collagen - a structural protein in connective tissues
  • synonym: fibronectin - a protein that helps cells attach to the extracellular matrix
