interframe

noun
interval between frames
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌfreɪm / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌfreɪm / (BrE)
the period or time interval between two consecutive frames in film, video, or animation

Examples

Interframe interval
The software allows you to adjust the interframe to achieve smoother video playback.
A shorter interframe results in a higher frame rate and more fluid animation.
By increasing the interframe, the editor created a slow-motion effect in the video.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frame interval - the time between two frames in a sequence
  • antonym: frame rate - the number of frames displayed per second
