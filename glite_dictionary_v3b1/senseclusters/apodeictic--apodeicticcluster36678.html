apodeictic

adjective
necessarily true or logically certain
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.pə.ˈdaɪk.tɪk/ /; / /æ.pə.ˈdaɪk.tɪk/ (AmE), /ə.pə.ˈdaɪk.tɪk/ /; / /æ.pə.ˈdaɪk.tɪk/ (BrE)
(of a proposition or statement) necessarily true or logically certain; incontrovertible.

Examples

Apodeictic statement
Kant distinguishes between analytic, synthetic, and apodeictic judgments in his philosophy.
The mathematician offered an apodeictic proof that left no room for doubt.
The statement 'all bachelors are unmarried men' is considered apodeictic because it is logically certain.
