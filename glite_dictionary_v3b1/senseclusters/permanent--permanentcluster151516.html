permanent

adjective
lasting; enduring without change
3.2 41/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ùÀê.m…ô.n…ônt / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀê.m…ô.n…ônt / (BrE)
lasting or intended to last for a long time or forever; not likely to change

Examples

Permanent address
She listed her permanent address on the application form.
The effects of the new law could be permanent if not properly reviewed.
The museum seeks to create a permanent collection of modern art.
Many tattoos are considered permanent, though some removal techniques do exist.
Scientists warn about the permanent effects of certain environmental changes.
The accident resulted in permanent brain damage, altering his life forever.

  • long-lasting
    3.7 3.08/m
    long-lasting continues over a long period without quick change, while permanent refers to something meant to remain unchanged.
  • enduring
    4.2 0.13/m
    enduring survives through wear or difficulty for many years, while permanent is meant to stay the same long-term.
  • nontransient
    7.7 0.02/m
    nontransient lasts over time and is not temporary, while permanent often describes something intended to remain unchanged.
  • perpetual
    5.8 6.92/m
    perpetual goes on without stopping or pause, while permanent describes a lasting state that may not be continuous action.
  • everlasting
    4.5 1.84/m
    everlasting seems to last forever like a timeless object, while permanent usually means long-lasting but not always endless.
  • eternal
    4.3 21/m
    eternal lasts without an end, often in spiritual or cosmic talk, while permanent usually names long-lasting earthly things.
