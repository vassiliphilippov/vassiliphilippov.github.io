segregate

verb
to separate from a group
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛɡrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡrɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to separate or set apart from others or from the main group, often for a particular purpose or characteristic

Examples

To segregate a group
After the merger, the smaller department was segregated into a specialized unit.
During cell division, chromosomes segregate into two independent sets.
The waste material must be segregated from the recyclable items before processing.
Hospitals may segregate patients with contagious diseases to prevent the spread of infection.
In the 1950s, laws were created to segregate black and white students in some schools.
The company decided to segregate hazardous materials from regular waste for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrate - to combine or bring together into a whole
  • synonym: separate - to set or keep apart
