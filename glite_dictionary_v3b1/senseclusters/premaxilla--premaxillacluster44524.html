premaxilla

noun
front upper jaw bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːmækˈsɪlə / (AmE), / ˌpriːmækˈsɪlə / (BrE)
either of a pair of bones at the very front of the upper jaw in vertebrates, often supporting the front teeth

Examples

The premaxilla bone
In many fish, the premaxilla forms part of the mouth's edge.
The premaxilla is important for supporting the front teeth in mammals.
Scientists studied the fossil's premaxilla to learn about its feeding habits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mandible - the lower jaw bone in vertebrates
  • synonym: maxilla - the main upper jaw bone in vertebrates
