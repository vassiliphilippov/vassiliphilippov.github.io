false scorpion

noun
small arachnid resembling a tailless scorpion
6.3
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈskɔrpiən / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈskɔːpiən / (BrE)
a small, nonvenomous arachnid that resembles a scorpion but lacks a tail and stinger

Examples

A false scorpion species
Scientists discovered a rare species of false scorpion in the cave system.
The false scorpion hides under bark, feeding on tiny insects and mites.
Unlike true scorpions, a false scorpion does not have a venomous stinger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pseudoscorpion - another name for false scorpion, in zoology
  • antonym: scorpion - an arachnid with a tail and stinger
