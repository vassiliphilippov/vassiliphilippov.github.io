redistill

verb
to clarify again
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːdɪˈstɪl / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈstɪl / (BrE)
to express or present the essential meaning of something again, often in a clearer or more concise form

Examples

To redistill an idea
The teacher asked the student to redistill his argument into a single, clear sentence.
After several drafts, she managed to redistill her ideas into a powerful conclusion.
Can you redistill the main points of the meeting for the team?

Related Terms

  • antonym: complicate - to make something more difficult or complex
  • synonym: refine - to improve something by making small changes
  • synonym: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
