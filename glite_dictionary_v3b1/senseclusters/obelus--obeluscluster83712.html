obelus

noun
dagger symbol
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑbələs / (AmE), / ˈɒbələs /; / ˈɒbɪləs / (BrE)
a symbol (†) used in writing to indicate a footnote, reference, or that someone has died

Examples

Obelus for footnotes
The historian placed an obelus next to the king's name to show he had died.
In the document, an obelus indicated which authors were deceased.
The editor used an obelus to mark a special note at the bottom of the page.
The obelus at the end of the sentence directs readers to an important note below.
In older books, a obelus is often used instead of an asterisk for footnotes.
Please look for the obelus symbol to find additional information in the appendix.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asterisk - the symbol (*) used to mark footnotes or references
  • synonym: dagger - the typographical symbol (†) used for footnotes or to indicate death
