tempered

adjective
hardened or toughened by treatment
2.5 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtem.pərd/ (AmE), /ˈtem.pəd/ (BrE)
(of metal, glass, etc.) made hard, flexible, or resilient, especially by being subjected to heat or chemical treatment.

Examples

Tempered glass window
Many modern buildings use tempered glass for windows due to its safety qualities.
The chef's knife is made from tempered steel, ensuring both strength and sharpness.
The engineer recommended installing tempered bolts to withstand higher stress levels.
