mucilaginous

adjective
sticky, gluey or gelatinous in texture
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːsɪˈlӔdʒɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌmjuːsɪˈlӕdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
(of a substance) having a viscous, sticky, or gluey texture, similar to that of mucilage.

Examples

Mucilaginous texture
Some plants secrete a mucilaginous coating that helps conserve moisture.
The okra released a mucilaginous substance as it cooked, thickening the stew.
The surface of the seed became mucilaginous when soaked in water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slippery - difficult to hold firmly or stand on, especially because it is smooth, wet, or slimy
  • synonym: sticky - having the property of adhering or sticking to a surface
