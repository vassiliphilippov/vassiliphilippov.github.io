Tishah b'Av

noun
Jewish fast day mourning temple destruction
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪ.ʃə bɑːv/ /; / /tɪ.ˈʃɑː bɑːv/ (AmE), /ˈtɪ.ʃə bɑːv/ /; / /tɪ.ˈʃɑː bɑːv/ (BrE)
(in Judaism) A major fast day commemorating the destruction of the First and Second Temples in Jerusalem, observed with mourning and fasting.

Examples

Tishah b'Av fast day
Each year, many Jews observe Tishah b'Av by fasting and reading from the Book of Lamentations.
Jews around the world join together in mourning on Tishah b'Av, reflecting on the destruction of the ancient temples.
On Tishah b'Av, synagogues often hold special services to commemorate the tragedies of Jewish history.
