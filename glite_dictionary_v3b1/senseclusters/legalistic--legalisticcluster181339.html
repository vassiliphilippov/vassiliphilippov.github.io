legalistic

adjective
focused on legal rules
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌliːɡəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌliːɡəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
focusing on legal rules or procedures in a strict or excessive way, often making things complicated or rigid

Examples

A legalistic approach
The manager's legalistic approach made the process slow and complicated.
Some critics argue that the policy is too legalistic and ignores practical concerns.
Her legalistic interpretation of the rules left little room for flexibility.
Her legalistic approach to the contract left no room for negotiation.
The committee's legalistic interpretation of the rules frustrated many members.
A legalistic mindset can sometimes prevent practical solutions to problems.
The contract was written in such a legalistic way that few people could interpret it.
His legalistic approach to the problem ignored practical solutions.
The policy document became too legalistic for the average employee to follow.
