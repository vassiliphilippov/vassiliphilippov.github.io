decoct

verb
boil to reduce liquid; concentrate by boiling
8.3 8.8
Pronunciation: /də.ˈkɑːkt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈkɑːkt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈkɒkt/ /; / /də.ˈkɒkt/ (BrE)
to boil a substance (especially a plant or herb) in water to extract or concentrate its essence or active ingredients, often for medicinal or culinary purposes

Examples

To decoct herbs
After simmering for hours, he finally decocted the mixture to the desired strength.
Herbalists often decoct roots to extract beneficial compounds.
The chef will decoct the broth to intensify its flavor before serving.
She carefully decocted the bark to produce a concentrated remedy.
The herbalist would decoct the roots to make a medicinal tea.
To extract the flavor, you must decoct the leaves in hot water for an hour.
He carefully decocted a mixture of leaves and bark for the remedy.
In traditional medicine, practitioners often decoct roots for hours to obtain their medicinal properties.
The pharmacist will decoct the herbs to create a healing brew.
