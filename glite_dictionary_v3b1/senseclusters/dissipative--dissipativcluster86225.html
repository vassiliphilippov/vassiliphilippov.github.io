dissipative

adjective
losing energy as heat
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsəˌpeɪtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪpətɪv /; / ˈdɪsɪpətɪv / (BrE)
describing a process or system that loses energy, usually as heat, to its surroundings

Examples

Dissipative process
A dissipative system gradually loses energy to its surroundings.
Friction is a common cause of dissipative processes in mechanical systems.
In physics, dissipative forces convert useful energy into heat.
A dissipative system gradually loses energy until it reaches a steady state.
In physics, friction is a common example of a dissipative process.
The researchers studied how dissipative forces affected the motion of the particles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - not losing energy; energy is conserved within the system
