saxitoxin

noun
a neurotoxin from red tide organisms
Pronunciation: / ˈsæksɪˌtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈsæksɪˌtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a powerful neurotoxin produced by certain dinoflagellates, often accumulating in shellfish and potentially causing food poisoning in humans

Examples

Poisonous saxitoxin in shellfish
Eating contaminated mussels may expose people to dangerous amounts of saxitoxin.
Scientists monitor saxitoxin levels to prevent outbreaks of paralytic shellfish poisoning.
The presence of saxitoxin in shellfish led to a recall of seafood products along the coast.
