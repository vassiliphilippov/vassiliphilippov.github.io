fibrillation

noun
irregular muscle fiber contractions
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪbrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
rapid, irregular, and uncoordinated contraction of muscle fibers, especially in the heart or other muscles

Examples

Heart fibrillation
Fibrillation of the heart muscle can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.
Electrical impulses were used to stop the fibrillation and restore a normal heart rhythm.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with atrial fibrillation after noticing an irregular heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrhythmia - an abnormal heart rhythm
