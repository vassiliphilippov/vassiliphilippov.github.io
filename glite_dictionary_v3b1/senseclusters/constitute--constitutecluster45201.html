constitute

verb
formally establish or appoint
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.stəˌtut /; / ˈkɑːnstəˌtuːt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stɪ.tjuːt /; / ˈkɒnstɪtjuːt / (BrE)
to formally establish, set up, or create something such as a group, committee, organization, or system

Examples

To constitute a committee
A new committee was constituted to oversee the project.
The board will constitute a panel to investigate the matter.
The university plans to constitute a task force for climate research.
New policies were constituted to address emerging security concerns.
The board voted to constitute a new committee to handle the project.
They hope to constitute a scholarship program for underprivileged students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appoint - assign a role or task to someone officially
  • antonym: dissolve - to officially end or disband a group or body
  • synonym: establish - to set up or create something, such as an organization
  • synonym: found - to establish or originate an institution or organization
