budge

verb
move or cause slight movement
3.1 3.53/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒdÊ’ / (AmE), / bÊŒdÊ’ / (BrE)
to move or make something move slightly, often after resistance

Examples

She budged slightly
After hours of arguing, she finally budged a little on her position.
He tried to lift the box, but it wouldn't budge an inch.
The door was stuck and wouldn't budge no matter how hard we pushed.

Synonyms

  • stir
    1.0 5.81/m
    stir is a small, often sudden start of movement from stillness, while budge usually means move slightly after resistance.
  • shift
    2.0 65/m
    shift means moving or changing place or focus more generally, while budge implies a slight movement often caused by resistance.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: move - change position or go from one place to another
  • synonym: shift - to move or change position slightly
  • antonym: stay - to remain in the same position
