polarization

noun
restriction of wave vibrations to a direction
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or phenomenon by which waves of light or other radiation are restricted to vibrate in particular directions.

Examples

Polarization of light
Scientists studied the polarization of microwaves in their experiments.
The polarization of light can be observed using certain types of filters.
The sunglasses reduce glare by blocking the polarization of reflected light.
Certain sunglasses use polarisation to reduce glare from surfaces.
Scientists study the polarisation of radio waves to learn about space.
The polarisation of light can be observed using special filters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depolarization - loss or removal of polarization
  • synonym: polarization - the same phenomenon—restriction of wave vibrations, American spelling
