rain on someone's parade

verb
spoil someone's plans or enthusiasm
6.4 5.9
Pronunciation: /reɪn ən ˈsʌm.wənz pə.ˈreɪd/ (AmE), /reɪn ən ˈsʌm.wənz pə.ˈreɪd/ (BrE)
to spoil someone's plans, happiness, or enthusiasm, especially by criticizing or pointing out problems

Examples

To rain on someone's parade badly
Even though he tried to rain on my parade, I stayed positive and continued celebrating.
I don't want to rain on your parade, but I don't think your plan will work.
She was so excited about her promotion; I didn't have the heart to rain on her parade.
