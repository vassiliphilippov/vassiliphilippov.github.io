tripinnatifid

adjective
leaf divided three times pinnately
9.9
Pronunciation: / traɪˈpɪn.ə.tɪ.fɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈpɪn.ət.ɪ.fɪd / (BrE)
(of a leaf) divided three times pinnately, with the smallest divisions being themselves pinnatifid.

Examples

A tripinnatifid leaf
A tripinnatifid leaf is much more finely cut than a simple pinnatifid one.
Botanists identify some carrot family leaves as tripinnatifid due to their complex division.
The fern's tripinnatifid fronds give it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bipinnatifid - (of a leaf) divided twice pinnately, with the divisions being pinnatifid
