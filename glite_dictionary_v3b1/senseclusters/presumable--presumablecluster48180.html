presumable

adjective
likely to be assumed or inferred
7.2 3.9
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈzuː.mə.bəl/ /; / /prɪ.ˈzjuː.mə.bəl/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈzjuː.mə.bəl/ (BrE)
Capable of being reasonably assumed or inferred; likely based on available evidence.

Examples

A presumable reason
It is presumable that she will arrive on time, given her usual punctuality.
The cause of the power outage is presumable from the heavy storm last night.
There is no presumable reason for the data to have been manipulated.
