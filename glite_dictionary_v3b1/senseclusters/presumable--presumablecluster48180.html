Capable of being reasonably assumed or inferred; likely based on available evidence.

Examples

A presumable reason
It is presumable that she will arrive on time, given her usual punctuality.
The cause of the power outage is presumable from the heavy storm last night.
There is no presumable reason for the data to have been manipulated.

Synonyms

  • presumptive
    7.3 1.55/m
    presumptive is based on reasonable grounds and often used when evidence points that way, while presumable means likely to be assumed.
  • inferable
    6.1 0.02/m
    inferable is drawn by logical deduction from facts, while presumable is what one reasonably assumes from available evidence.
  • surmisable
    8.8 0.00/m
    surmisable is guessed from limited clues and feels less certain, while presumable is more reasonably supported by evidence.
  • supposable
    7.7 0.01/m
    supposable is imagined or lightly inferred on slight evidence, while presumable implies a firmer, reasonable likelihood.
  • putative
    7.9 2.19/m
    putative is commonly regarded as true though not proven, while presumable refers to what evidence makes likely.
