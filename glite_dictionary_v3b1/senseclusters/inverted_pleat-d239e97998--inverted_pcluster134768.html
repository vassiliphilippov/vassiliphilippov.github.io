inverted pleat

noun
pleat with folds meeting inward
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɝːtɪd pliːt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːtɪd pliːt / (BrE)
a type of pleat in fabric where two folds meet to form a crease on the outside, with the fullness turned inward

Examples

An inverted pleat in fabric
Sewing an inverted pleat can give your garment a tailored look.
The designer added a deep inverted pleat to the coat for a stylish detail.
The skirt features an inverted pleat at the back for added movement.
