sieve

noun
mesh device for separating particles
2.4 1.22/m
Pronunciation: / sÉªv / (AmE), / sÉªv / (BrE)
a device with a mesh or perforated bottom used for separating fine particles from coarser ones or for straining liquids from solids

Examples

A kitchen sieve
After washing the pasta, she shook it in a fine sieve to drain the water.
She used a sieve to remove lumps from the flour before baking.
The laboratory technician passed the sand through a sieve to grade the particles.

Synonyms

  • strainer
    1.7 1.36/m
    a strainer has a mesh for draining liquids and catching solids, while a sieve separates fine from coarser particles.
  • sifter
    2.8 0.20/m
    a sifter is a kitchen tool for aerating and breaking clumps in flour, while a sieve is more general in use.
  • riddle
    9.9 0.48/m
    a riddle is a coarse grid used to sift gravel or ashes, while a sieve is finer and used for many materials.
