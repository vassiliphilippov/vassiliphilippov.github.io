Vaudois

noun
member of a historical Christian sect (Waldenses)
7.0 9.9
Pronunciation: /voʊ.ˈdwɑː/ /; / /ˈvoʊ.dwɑː/ (AmE), /vəʊ.ˈdwɑː/ /; / /ˈvəʊ.dwɑː/ (BrE)
A member of a Christian sect, the Waldenses, that originated in southern France in the late 12th century and later adopted Calvinist doctrines in the 16th century.

Examples

Vaudois Christian sect
Historians have studied the migration routes of the Vaudois across Europe.
Many Vaudois communities later embraced Reformation teachings in the 16th century.
The Vaudois faced persecution for their beliefs throughout the Middle Ages.
