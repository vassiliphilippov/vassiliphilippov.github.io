fluoroboric acid

noun
a poisonous acid of boron and fluorine
Pronunciation: / flʊˌɔːroʊˈbɔːrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / flʊəˌrɒbɒˈrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, poisonous acid (HBF₄) formed by dissolving boron trifluoride in water, used mainly in electroplating and as a catalyst

Examples

Fluoroboric acid solution
Fluoroboric acid is commonly used in metal electroplating processes due to its unique chemical properties.
Always wear protective gloves and goggles when handling fluoroboric acid to prevent injuries.
The laboratory stored fluoroboric acid in a clearly labeled glass container for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boric acid - a weak acid of boron, often used as an antiseptic
