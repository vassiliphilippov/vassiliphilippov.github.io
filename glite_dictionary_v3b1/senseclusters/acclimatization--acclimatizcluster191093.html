acclimatization

noun
adaptation to new climate or environment
Pronunciation: / əˌklaɪmətəˈzeɪʃən /; / əˌklaɪmə.təˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌklaɪmətaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / əˌklaɪ.mɪ.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming accustomed to a new climate, environment, altitude, or conditions, especially through physiological or behavioral adjustment, allowing for better functioning in those conditions

Examples

To acclimatize to heat
After a week of acclimatization, the team began their expedition.
Proper acclimatization is essential before climbing to high altitudes.
The research studied the acclimatization of plants to urban environments.
Rapid acclimatisation helps travelers avoid the symptoms of altitude sickness.
Successful acclimatisation to a tropical climate often takes several weeks.
The team's acclimatisation to the high altitude was essential for their success on the mountain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - the process of changing to suit new conditions
  • antonym: maladaptation - inadequate or faulty adaptation to an environment or situation
