drift

noun
mine passageway (horizontal)
3.9 3.82/m
Pronunciation: / drÉªft / (AmE), / drÉªft / (BrE)
a mass or accumulation of something (such as sand, snow, or other material) moved and piled up by wind, water, or other force; or a passageway in a mine

Examples

A snow drift
A collapsed drift forced the operation to halt until it could be repaired.
Each drift was carefully mapped to ensure the safety of those underground.
The miners extended the drift to follow the new seam of coal.
A drift of sand blocked the path after the storm.
Heavy snow had formed a huge drift outside the front door.
The boat was caught against a drift of debris in the river.
A cross-current caused the sailboat's drift toward the rocky shore.
Due to strong winds, the plane experienced significant drift from its flight path.
The captain constantly monitored the drift of the ship to ensure it stayed on course.
