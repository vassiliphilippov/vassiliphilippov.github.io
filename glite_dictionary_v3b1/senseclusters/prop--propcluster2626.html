prop

noun
rotating device (short for propeller)
4.6 1.97/m
Pronunciation: / prÉ‘p / (AmE), / prÉ’p / (BrE)
a device with blades that rotates to push against air or water, especially in an engine-powered aircraft or boat; short for propeller

Examples

Plane prop
After the crash, fragments of the plane's prop were scattered on the runway.
He carefully removed the prop from the outboard motor for maintenance.
The pilot inspected the prop before taking off to ensure it was free of damage.

Synonyms

  • propeller
    3.9 3.91/m
    propeller is the full technical name used in manuals and reports, while prop is the shorter, informal term pilots often use.
