anesthetize

verb
administer an anesthetic
5.0
Pronunciation: / əˈnɛsθəˌtaɪz /; / əˈnɛsθətaɪz / (AmE), / əˈniːsθətaɪz / (BrE)
to administer an anesthetic to a person or animal so as to induce loss of sensation or consciousness, typically before a medical procedure

Examples

To anesthetize a patient
Dentists often anesthetize the area around a tooth before performing an extraction.
It took only minutes to anesthetize the injured animal before surgery.
The surgeon will anesthetize the patient before starting the operation.
It is important to anaesthetise patients properly to ensure they feel no pain during surgery.
The dentist will anaesthetise the area before starting the procedure.
Veterinarians often anaesthetise animals before performing operations.
Doctors must carefully anaesthetize patients to ensure they feel no pain during surgery.
She was anaesthetized for the knee operation to prevent discomfort.
The dentist will anaesthetize your jaw before beginning the tooth extraction.
