Fortunella japonica

noun
species of kumquat shrub
Pronunciation: / fɔr.tʃuˈnɛl.ə ʒæˈpɑ.nɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.tjuːˈnɛl.ə ˌdʒæp.əˈniː.kə / (BrE)
A species of evergreen shrub native to East Asia, bearing small, round, edible kumquats.

Examples

Fortunella japonica shrub
Botanists identified the shrub as Fortunella japonica due to its distinctive small fruits.
Many gardeners prefer to grow Fortunella japonica because of its attractive foliage and edible fruit.
The Fortunella japonica in our backyard produces sweet, round kumquats every spring.
