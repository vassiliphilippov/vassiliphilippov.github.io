rout

verb
defeat completely; cause to retreat
8.6 5.9
Pronunciation: /raʊt/ (AmE), /raʊt/ (BrE)
to defeat an opponent or group completely and force them to flee in disorder

Examples

To rout the enemy
Our army managed to rout the enemy forces before sunrise.
The defending champions were routed by a much stronger team in the finals.
The opposition party was routed in the recent elections, losing almost every seat.
The army managed to rout the enemy forces, sending them into a disorganized retreat.
The police used tear gas to rout the protesters from the square.
With a surprise attack, the defenders routed the invaders before dawn.
