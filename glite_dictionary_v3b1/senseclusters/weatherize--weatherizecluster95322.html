weatherize

verb
to protect from weather
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛðəˌraɪz /; / ˈwɛðəraɪz / (AmE), / ˈwɛðəraɪz / (BrE)
to make a building, object, or equipment more resistant to weather, usually by adding insulation, sealing, or other protective measures

Examples

To weatherize a home
The city offers grants to help residents weatherize their homes before winter arrives.
After moving in, they decided to weatherize the old windows to reduce drafts.
Many people weatherize their houses to save money on energy bills.
It is important to weatherize your outdoor furniture before the rainy season begins.
The engineers weatherized the equipment to ensure it would function in harsh climates.
You should weatherize your tools if you plan to store them outside all year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insulate - to cover or line with material to prevent heat loss
  • synonym: seal - to close tightly to prevent the passage of air or water
  • synonym: waterproof - to make something resistant to water
