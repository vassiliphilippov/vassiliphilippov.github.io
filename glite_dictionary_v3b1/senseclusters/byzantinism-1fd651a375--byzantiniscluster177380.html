Byzantinism

noun
state supremacy over church (Byzantine doctrine)
7.6
Pronunciation: / bɪˈzæn.tɪˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / bɪˈzæn.tɪ.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
The doctrine advocating that the state holds authority over the church in ecclesiastical matters, as practiced in the Byzantine Empire.

Examples

Byzantinism doctrine
Historians often debate the extent to which Byzantinism influenced Eastern Orthodox relations with secular authorities.
In contrast to Western Europe, Byzantinism gave the Byzantine emperor considerable control over church affairs.
The emperor's endorsement of Byzantinism led to decades of church-state tension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caesaropapism - the combining of the power of secular government with that of the church, especially in the Byzantine Empire
