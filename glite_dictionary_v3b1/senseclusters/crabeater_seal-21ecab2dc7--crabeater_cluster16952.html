crabeater seal

noun
Antarctic seal that eats crustaceans
Pronunciation: / ˈkræbˌiːtər siːl / (AmE), / ˈkræbˌiːtə siːl / (BrE)
a silvery-grey seal of the Antarctic that primarily feeds on crustaceans, especially krill

Examples

The crabeater seal
Despite its name, the crabeater seal rarely feeds on crabs, preferring krill instead.
Researchers observed a group of crabeater seals resting on a floating ice sheet.
The crabeater seal is one of the most numerous large mammals in Antarctica.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leopard seal - a large, predatory Antarctic seal known for its spotted coat
