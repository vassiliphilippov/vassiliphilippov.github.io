alluvial cone

noun
fan-shaped sediment deposit by a stream
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈlʌv.ɪ.əl koʊn/ /; / /æl.ˈuː.viː.əl koʊn/ (AmE), /ə.ˈlʌv.ɪ.əl kəʊn/ /; / /æl.ˈuː.viː.əl kəʊn/ (BrE)
A fan-shaped mass of sediment deposited by a fast-flowing stream as it slows down, typically at the base of a mountain.

Examples

An alluvial cone deposit
Many towns have been built on alluvial cones formed at the base of nearby hills.
Over time, the floodwaters created a broad alluvial cone of sand and gravel.
Sediments carried by the river settled into an alluvial cone at the valley entrance.
