searching fire

noun
gunfire covering an area in depth
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝtʃɪŋ ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtʃɪŋ ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
Gunfire delivered over a range of elevations so as to cover an area in depth, typically used to ensure targets at different distances are hit.

Examples

Searching fire coverage
By applying searching fire, the battery was able to suppress targets hiding at variable distances.
Effective searching fire requires precise adjustment of gun elevations to cover the entire depth of a defensive line.
The artillery commander ordered the use of searching fire to sweep the enemy's forward positions.
