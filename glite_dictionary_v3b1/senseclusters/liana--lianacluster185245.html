liana

noun
woody climbing tropical plant
Pronunciation: / liˈɑnə / (AmE), / lɪˈɑːnə / (BrE)
A long-stemmed, woody vine that climbs up trees, typically found in tropical forests.

Examples

A liana vine
Many liana species contribute to the dense undergrowth of tropical rainforests.
Researchers studied how a single liana could connect multiple trees in the jungle.
The monkey swung effortlessly from one liana to another high above the forest floor.
