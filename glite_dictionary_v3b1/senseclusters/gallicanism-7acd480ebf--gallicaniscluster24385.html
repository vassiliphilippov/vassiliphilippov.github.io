Gallicanism

noun
movement for autonomy of the French Catholic Church
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡælɪkəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪkənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A religious and political movement among French Roman Catholic clergy that advocated restricting papal authority and promoting national autonomy of the Church.

Examples

Gallicanism in France
Historians debate the impact Gallicanism had on the development of French national identity.
Many bishops during the seventeenth century were influenced by Gallicanism and resisted papal interference.
The rise of Gallicanism in France led to tensions between the monarchy and the papacy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Ultramontanism - belief in supreme papal authority in Catholicism
