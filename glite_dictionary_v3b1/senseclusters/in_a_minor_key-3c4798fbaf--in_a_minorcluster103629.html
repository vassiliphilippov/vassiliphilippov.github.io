in a minor key

preposition
having minor-key tonality (music)
Pronunciation: / ɪn ə ˈmaɪnɚ ki / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈmaɪnə kiː / (BrE)
(of music) having the tonality or character of a minor key, often producing a more subdued or somber mood.

Music in a minor key
Her improvisation shifted in a minor key, altering the entire mood of the piece.
That tune, in a minor key, always makes me feel nostalgic.
The composer wrote the symphony in a minor key to evoke a sense of melancholy.
