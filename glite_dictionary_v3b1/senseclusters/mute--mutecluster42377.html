mute

adjective
expressed without speech
4.1 2.15/m
Pronunciation: / mjut / (AmE), / mjuÀêt / (BrE)
expressed or conveyed without the use of speech; silent

Examples

Mute protest
His mute protest was more powerful than words.
She gave him a mute glance full of understanding.
There was a mute plea for help in her eyes.

Synonyms

  • silent
    1.0 31/m
    silent is not speaking or making noise in a situation, while mute describes something expressed without using speech.
  • wordless
    4.1 1.41/m
    wordless is expressed without words, often by gesture or look, while mute focuses on absence of speech or sound.
  • unspoken
    4.8 4.71/m
    unspoken refers to things left unsaid but understood between people, while mute describes something shown without using speech.
  • nonvocal
    5.7 0.00/m
    nonvocal is a technical term for not using the voice, while mute is more general and often describes silence or lack of speech.
  • nonspeaking
    6.0 0.04/m
    nonspeaking describes roles or situations where speech does not occur, while mute emphasizes expressing without using speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: silent - making no sound; completely quiet
  • antonym: vocal - expressed using the voice or speech
  • synonym: wordless - without words; expressed without speaking
