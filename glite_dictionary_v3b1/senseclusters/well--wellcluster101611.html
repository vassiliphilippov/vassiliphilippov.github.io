well

adjective, adverb
with high probability; very likely
Pronunciation: / wɛl / (AmE), / wɛl / (BrE)
used to indicate likelihood, advisability, or propriety; in all likelihood, wisely, or sensibly

Examples

He could well win
He could well become the next president if the polls are correct.
It might well snow before the end of the month.
You may well find it hard to finish the project on time.
He could not well decline the invitation without causing offense.
Students would do well to submit their assignments on time.
You would do well to check your facts before making accusations.
He thought it well to stay home during the storm.
It would be well to check your work before submitting it.
You would do well to listen to her advice in this situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advisable - that can be recommended; sensible and wise
  • antonym: imprudently - in an imprudent or unwise manner
  • degree lower: possibly - might happen or be true, but less certain than 'probably'
  • synonym: probably - in all likelihood; almost certainly
  • synonym: prudent - acting with care and thought for the future; wise
  • synonym: wisely - in a wise way; showing good judgment
