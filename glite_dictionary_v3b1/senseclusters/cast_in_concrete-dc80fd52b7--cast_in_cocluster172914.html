cast in concrete

adjective
fixed; unchangeable; made permanent
Pronunciation: / kæst ɪn kɑnˈkrit / (AmE), / kɑːst ɪn ˈkɒŋkriːt / (BrE)
(of a rule, plan, or decision) fixed and unchangeable; made permanent and not subject to alteration.

Examples

Cast in concrete rules
Remember, your schedule isn't cast in concrete and can be adjusted if necessary.
Their partnership agreement was cast in concrete by the end of the negotiations.
While the policy isn't cast in concrete, changes are unlikely in the near future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: set in stone - permanently fixed or established; not able to be changed
