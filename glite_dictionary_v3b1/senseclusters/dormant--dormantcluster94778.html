dormant

adjective
temporarily inactive, able to activate
5.2 2.25/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îrm…ônt / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀêm…ônt / (BrE)
temporarily inactive but able to become active or develop in the future

Examples

Dormant skills
Her artistic talent lay dormant until she enrolled in a painting class.
Some seeds remain dormant in the soil for years before they finally sprout.
The volcano has been dormant for centuries, but scientists believe it could erupt again.

Synonyms

  • inactive
    2.7 0.15/m
    inactive simply means not active now in general, while dormant means temporarily inactive but able to become active again.
