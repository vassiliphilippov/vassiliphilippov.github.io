sacramentary

noun
church sacrament assistant
Pronunciation: / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəri / (BrE)
an archaic term for a person who administers or assists in administering the sacraments in a church

Examples

A sacramentary assists
In medieval times, the sacramentary played a key role during important church ceremonies.
The duties of a sacramentary included preparing the altar and assisting with baptisms.
Few people today know the responsibilities once held by a sacramentary in the parish.
