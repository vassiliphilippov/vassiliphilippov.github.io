futurize

verb
to make futuristic
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːtʃəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈfjuːtʃəraɪz / (BrE)
to make something look, seem, or be suitable for the future, often by updating, modernizing, or giving it a futuristic style

Examples

To futurize a building
The architect wanted to futurize the city skyline with bold, innovative designs.
They plan to futurize the museum by adding interactive digital exhibits.
New lighting and sleek materials helped futurize the outdated office space.
The architect tried to futurize the city by designing buildings inspired by science fiction.
Our team will futurize the company's strategy to prepare for upcoming technological changes.
Writers often futurize their stories to explore how society might evolve.
The city council hopes to futurize public transportation by introducing electric buses.
Many companies are investing in technology to futurize their operations.
They hired consultants to help futurize the old manufacturing plant.
