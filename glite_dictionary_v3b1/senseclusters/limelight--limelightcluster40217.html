limelight

noun
historical stage lighting device
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪmˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪmlaɪt / (BrE)
a type of stage lighting, formerly used in theaters, in which a flame is directed at a cylinder of lime to produce a bright white light

Examples

Theater limelight
In the 19th century, theaters commonly used limelight for stage illumination.
The actors performed under the limelight throughout the entire play.
The invention of electric lights eventually replaced limelight in theaters.
