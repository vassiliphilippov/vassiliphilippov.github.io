copyism

noun
imitation in art
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːpiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒpiˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or doctrine of imitating or copying the style or work of others, especially in art

Examples

Artistic copyism
Many critics accused the painter of copyism instead of developing his own style.
The movement was dismissed as mere copyism by those who valued innovation in art.
Some believe that copyism can be a useful learning tool for young artists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitation - the act of copying someone or something
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being new and unique
