bushland

noun
wild wooded area
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌlænd / (BrE)
an area of wild or uncultivated land covered with bushes and trees, especially in Australia

Examples

Australian bushland
Kangaroos are often seen hopping through the bushland near the national park.
The fire spread quickly across the dry bushland during the summer heat.
Many rare birds make their nests deep within the bushland.
They hiked for hours through the dense bushland before reaching the river.
Many unique animals can be found only in the Australian bushland.
After moving from the city, she enjoyed the peace and quiet of the bushland.

Related Terms

  • antonym: city - a large and densely populated urban area
  • antonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land with little vegetation
  • synonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • synonym: outback - remote and sparsely populated inland regions of Australia
  • synonym: wilderness - a natural area that has not been significantly modified by humans
