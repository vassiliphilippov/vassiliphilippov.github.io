queenless

adjective
without a queen
6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkwiːn.ləs/ /; / /ˈkwiːn.les/ (AmE), /ˈkwiːn.ləs/ /; / /ˈkwiːn.les/ (BrE)
lacking a queen, especially referring to a colony of social insects such as bees or ants

Examples

Queenless hive
A queenless hive often struggles to survive without new eggs being laid.
The beekeeper noticed the colony was queenless and decided to introduce a new queen.
Ant colonies can become queenless if their queen dies or is removed.
