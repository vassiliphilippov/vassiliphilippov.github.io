hold-down

noun
limitation or restriction
6.0 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊld.daʊn/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊld.daʊn/ (BrE)
a restriction or control that limits something, especially the amount or rate of increase

Examples

A hold-down on prices
Many citizens demanded a hold-down on rent increases in the city.
The company implemented a strict hold-down on travel expenses to control costs.
Without a hold-down on emissions, pollution levels continued to rise.
