den of iniquity

noun
place of immoral or illegal activity
8.7
Pronunciation: / dɛn əv ɪˈnɪkwədi / (AmE), / dɛn əv ɪˈnɪkwɪti / (BrE)
a place where immoral or illegal activities happen, often secretly or discreetly

Examples

A den of iniquity
He feared that the city’s nightlife would turn the district into a den of iniquity after hours.
Some people considered the bar a harmless gathering spot, while others saw it as a den of iniquity.
The police raided the old warehouse, which had become a den of iniquity for illegal gambling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: haven - a safe or peaceful place, opposite of a place of wrongdoing
  • synonym: vice den - a place where vice or immoral behavior occurs
