harvest

noun
result or consequence gained from effort
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrvəst / (AmE), / ˈhɑːvɪst / (BrE)
the result or consequence of an effort or activity, especially used figuratively to refer to what is gained or produced

Examples

Harvest of success
After months of work, the team finally enjoyed the harvest of their dedication.
She hoped her years of kindness would bring her a harvest of friendship.
The scientist's research led to a rich harvest of discoveries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bounty - a large amount of something, especially a reward or result
  • antonym: loss - an instance of losing or failing to gain
  • synonym: yield - the total amount produced or supplied
