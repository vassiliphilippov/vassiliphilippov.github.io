Sadducee

noun
member of ancient Jewish sect
Pronunciation: / ˈsædʒəˌsi / (AmE), / ˈsædjuːˌsiː / (BrE)
a member of an ancient Jewish sect at the time of Jesus, known for its strict interpretation of the written law and opposition to the Pharisees

Examples

The Sadducees group
As a group, the Sadducees held significant influence over the Temple in Jerusalem.
Some Sadducees questioned Jesus about the resurrection to test his teachings.
The Sadducees rejected many of the oral traditions embraced by the Pharisees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Pharisee - member of a rival ancient Jewish sect advocating strict observance of traditions
