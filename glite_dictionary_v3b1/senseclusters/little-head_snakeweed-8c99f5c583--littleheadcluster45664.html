little-head snakeweed

noun
a species of snakeweed plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtəl hɛd ˈsneɪkˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl hɛd ˈsneɪkwiːd / (BrE)
a species of snakeweed plant similar to Gutierrezia sarothrae, but with flower heads that have fewer ray and disk flowers

Examples

Little-head snakeweed plant
Little-head snakeweed thrives in arid regions with sandy soils.
In the field, little-head snakeweed can often be confused with other kinds of snakeweed.
The botanist identified the plant as little-head snakeweed due to its distinctive small flower heads.
