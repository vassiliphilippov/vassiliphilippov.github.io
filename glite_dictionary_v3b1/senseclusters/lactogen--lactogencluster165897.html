lactogen

noun
agent that increases milk production
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlæk.toʊ.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈlæk.tə.dʒən / (BrE)
any agent that stimulates or enhances milk production, especially in mammals.

Examples

A lactogen agent
Scientists are studying a new lactogen found in certain plants to treat lactation disorders.
Some herbal supplements act as a lactogen, helping nursing mothers produce more milk.
The veterinarian prescribed a lactogen to improve the cow's milk yield after calving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galactagogue - substance that promotes lactation
