brecciation

noun
formation of breccia
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɛsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbrɛsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or condition in which rock is broken into angular fragments, often forming a rock called breccia

Examples

Brecciation of rock
The brecciation of the rock layer was caused by intense tectonic activity.
Geologists studied the patterns of brecciation to understand the region's seismic history.
Evidence of brecciation can be seen in the angular fragments within the sedimentary deposit.
Geologists studied the brecciation of the rock layers to understand the region's seismic history.
The brecciation visible in the outcrop indicated a violent geological event in the past.
After the earthquake, signs of brecciation were found in the exposed cliff face.

  • synonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into pieces
