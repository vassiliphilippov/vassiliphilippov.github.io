danaid

noun
a type of tropical butterfly with reduced forelegs
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪnəˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnɪˌɪd / (BrE)
a large tropical butterfly of a group whose forelegs are reduced and which often have an unpleasant taste, including the monarch butterfly.

Examples

A danaid butterfly
In Asia, the striped tiger is a common type of danaid butterfly.
Many bird species avoid eating the danaid due to its toxic chemicals.
The larvae of the danaid feed on milkweed, making them distasteful to predators.
