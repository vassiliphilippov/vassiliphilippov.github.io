fiscalism

noun
government financial control
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪskəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɪskəˌlɪzəm /; / ˈfɪskəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a policy or doctrine in which the government manages public finances, taxation, and spending, often with strict control or discipline

Related Terms

  • synonym: austerity - a policy of reducing government spending and deficits
  • antonym: expansionism - a policy of increasing government spending to stimulate the economy
  • synonym: fiscal conservatism - a belief in limiting government spending and debt
  • synonym: fiscal policy - government decisions about taxation and spending
  • antonym: laissez-faire - an economic system with minimal government intervention
  • antonym: monetarism - an economic theory emphasizing control of the money supply
