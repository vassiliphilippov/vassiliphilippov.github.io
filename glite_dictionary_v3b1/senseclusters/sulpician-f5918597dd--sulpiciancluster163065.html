Sulpician

noun
member of priestly order
Pronunciation: / sʌlˈpɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌlˈpɪsɪən / (BrE)
a member of the Society of Saint-Sulpice, a Roman Catholic order of priests dedicated to the education of clergy

Examples

A Sulpician priest
The Sulpician dedicated his life to training future priests at the seminary.
Many students admired the Sulpician for his deep knowledge of theology.
A Sulpician was invited to speak at the church's anniversary celebration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Dominican - a member of the Order of Preachers, a Roman Catholic religious order
  • synonym: Jesuit - a member of the Society of Jesus, a Roman Catholic religious order
