glucose tolerance test

noun
medical test for glucose metabolism
1.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈgluː.koʊs ˈtɑː.lə.rəns test/ /; / /ˈgluː.koʊz ˈtɑː.lə.rəns test/ (AmE), /ˈgluː.kəʊs ˈtɒl.ə.rəns test/ /; / /ˈgluː.kəʊz ˈtɒl.ə.rəns test/ (BrE)
a medical test that measures the body's ability to process glucose, used especially in diagnosing diabetes and hypoglycemia

Examples

A glucose tolerance test
A glucose tolerance test involves monitoring blood sugar after consuming a sugary drink.
Pregnant women may have a glucose tolerance test to screen for gestational diabetes.
The doctor ordered a glucose tolerance test to check for possible diabetes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: diabetes mellitus - chronic disease characterized by high blood glucose levels
  • degree lower: hypoglycemia - condition of abnormally low blood glucose
  • synonym: oral glucose tolerance test - a specific form where glucose is ingested by mouth
