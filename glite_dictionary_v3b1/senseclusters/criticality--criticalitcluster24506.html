criticality

noun
state of being critical; self-sustaining nuclear reaction
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪtɪˈkæləti /; / ˌkrɪtɪˈkælɪti / (AmE), / ˌkrɪtɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being critical, especially the point at which something becomes crucial, unstable, or (in technical contexts) self-sustaining, such as in a nuclear reaction

Examples

Nuclear criticality test
Engineers closely monitored the criticality tests during the experiment.
Reaching criticality is a crucial step in operating a nuclear power plant safely.
The reactor reached criticality and began to sustain a chain reaction.
Engineers evaluated the reactor's criticality during the safety inspection.
In times of crisis, recognizing the criticality of communication is essential to successful management.
The criticality of the situation demanded immediate action from the emergency services.

Related Terms

  • antonym: noncriticality - state of not being critical or crucial
  • degree lower: subcritical - having a level below that required for a nuclear reaction to be self-sustaining
  • degree higher: supercritical - having a level above that required for a nuclear reaction to be self-sustaining
  • synonym: urgency - immediacy requiring swift action
