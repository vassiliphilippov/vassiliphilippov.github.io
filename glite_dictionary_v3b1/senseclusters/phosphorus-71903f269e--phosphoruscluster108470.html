Phosphorus

noun
old term for morning star Venus
9.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ëÀêsf…ôr…ôs / (AmE), / Ààf…ísf…ôr…ôs / (BrE)
(Archaic) The morning star; the planet Venus when visible in the eastern sky before sunrise.

Examples

The Phosphorus star
As travelers set out before daylight, they often spotted Phosphorus shining brightly in the east.
In ancient texts, Phosphorus was described as the bringer of light before the sun rose.
The ancients called Venus Phosphorus when she appeared in the dawn sky.
