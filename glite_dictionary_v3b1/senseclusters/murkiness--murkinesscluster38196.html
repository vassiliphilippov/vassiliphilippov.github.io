murkiness

noun
cloudy or dark quality
7.1 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmɜːr.kɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈmɜː.kɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being cloudy, dark, or difficult to see through (in water, air, or a place)

Examples

Water murkiness
Scientists tested the lake's murkiness to determine the level of pollution.
The murkiness of the water made it impossible to see the bottom.
There was a murkiness in the air after the storm passed through the city.
Because of the murkiness in the room, she had to rely on her sense of touch to find the exit.
Fog added to the murkiness in the streets, making navigation challenging for drivers.
The murkiness of the water made it difficult to see the fish swimming below the surface.
