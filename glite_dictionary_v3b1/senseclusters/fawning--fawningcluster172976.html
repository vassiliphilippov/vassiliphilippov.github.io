fawning

adjective
excessively flattering to gain favor
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑnɪŋ /; / ˈfɔnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
showing too much praise or affection to someone, usually to get their approval or favor

Examples

Fawning behavior
He adopted a fawning attitude whenever the boss was around, hoping for a promotion.
Her fawning behavior made it obvious that she wanted to be in the manager's good graces.
The intern's fawning compliments quickly grew tiresome to her supervisor.
His fawning manner made many in the office uncomfortable.
She received a fawning letter from her assistant, filled with insincere praise.
The celebrity was surrounded by fawning fans hoping for an autograph.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assertive - confident and self-assured in behavior, not submissive
  • antonym: genuine - sincere, honest, not pretending or insincere
  • synonym: obsequious - excessively eager to please or obey
  • synonym: sycophantic - behaving in a way that flatters powerful people too much
