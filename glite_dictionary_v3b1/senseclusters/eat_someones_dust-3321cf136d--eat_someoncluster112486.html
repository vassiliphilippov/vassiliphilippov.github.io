eat someone's dust

verb
be decisively outperformed or left behind
Pronunciation: / it ˈsʌmwʌnz dʌst / (AmE), / iːt ˈsʌmwʌnz dʌst / (BrE)
to be decisively outperformed or left behind, especially in a competition or contest

Examples

To eat someone's dust
After the first lap, all the other runners could do was eat her dust.
Every time they play chess, he makes his brother eat his dust.
The new software is so advanced that competitors will eat its dust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lose - to be defeated; to not win
  • antonym: win - to be victorious over others
