crowd in on

verb
gather closely around
Pronunciation: / kraʊd ɪn ɑn / (AmE), / kraʊd ɪn ɒn / (BrE)
to move close together around someone or something, often leaving little space between people

Examples

Fans crowded in on singer
As the singer appeared, dozens of fans crowded in on the stage for a closer look.
Everyone crowded in on the table so they could hear the announcement.
The children crowded in on the magician, eager to see his next trick.

  • synonym: surround - to be all around someone or something
