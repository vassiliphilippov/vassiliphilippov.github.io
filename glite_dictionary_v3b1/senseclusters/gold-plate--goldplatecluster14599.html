gold-plate

verb
add unnecessary extras; over-embellish
8.2
Pronunciation: / c8 goldccplate / (AmE), / c8 goldccplate / (BrE)
(figurative) to add unnecessary features, embellishments, or regulatory requirements to something, making it more complex, costly, or difficult to implement.

Examples

To gold-plate new rules
Politicians warned that ministers might gold-plate the new safety rules, making them costly for small firms.
The contractor gold-plated the design with unnecessary features that delayed the project and increased costs.
To meet the EU directive some agencies started gold-plating standards, adding paperwork beyond what was required.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloat - to make a system or process larger or more complex than necessary
  • synonym: over-embellish - to add unnecessary decorative or nonessential elements
  • synonym: over-engineer - to design something with unnecessary complexity or features
  • antonym: streamline - to make a system or process simpler and more efficient
