idiographic approach

noun
approach focusing on uniqueness and subjectivity
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪdioʊˈɡræfɪk əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɪdiəˈɡræfɪk əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
An approach to knowledge that focuses on understanding unique, individual, and subjective phenomena, often through detailed case studies or qualitative methods.

Examples

The idiographic approach in study
In contrast to general theories, the idiographic approach values personal narratives and context.
Psychology often uses the idiographic approach when studying individual development.
The researcher chose an idiographic approach to explore the experiences of each participant in detail.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nomothetic approach - approach to knowledge concerned with general laws and patterns
