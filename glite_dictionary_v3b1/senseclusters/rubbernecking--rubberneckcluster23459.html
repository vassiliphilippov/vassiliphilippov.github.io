rubbernecking

noun
slowing to stare
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌbərˌnɛkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌbənɛkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of slowing down or stopping to stare at something, especially an accident or unusual event, often causing delays

Examples

Drivers rubbernecking at accident
The traffic jam was caused by drivers rubbernecking at the accident on the other side of the highway.
Police warned that rubbernecking can lead to additional crashes and slow emergency response.
He was late to work because of all the rubbernecking near the construction site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gawking - staring openly and often rudely
  • antonym: ignoring - not paying attention to something
