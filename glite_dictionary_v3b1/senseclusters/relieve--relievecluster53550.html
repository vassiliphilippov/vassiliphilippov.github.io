relieve

verb
to steal, to take by theft
Pronunciation: / rɪˈliv / (AmE), / rɪˈliːv / (BrE)
(humorous or formal) to steal something from someone; to take something away from someone, especially by theft.

Examples

To relieve someone of a wallet
A pickpocket relieved her of her wallet on the crowded bus.
The con artist relieved several tourists of their cash before disappearing into the crowd.
While she was distracted, someone relieved him of his suitcase at the station.

Related Terms

  • synonym: steal - to take (something) without permission or legal right and without intending to return it
