transformation

noun
linguistic rule for changing syntactic structures
Pronunciation: / ˌtræns.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræns.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a rule in linguistics describing how one syntactic structure can be converted into another related structure

Examples

Sentence transformation
A transformation may convert a declarative sentence into a question in English grammar.
Chomsky introduced the concept of a transformation to account for variations in sentence structure.
Passive sentences can be generated by applying a specific transformation to active sentences.
