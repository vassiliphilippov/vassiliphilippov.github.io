Earth's crust

noun
the outer layer of the Earth
2.0 2.9
Pronunciation: / ɝːθs krʌst / (AmE), / ɜːθs krʌst / (BrE)
the outermost solid layer of the Earth, lying above the mantle and forming the planet's surface

Examples

The Earth's crust layer
Scientists study earthquakes to learn more about the structure of the Earth's crust.
The Earth's crust is composed mostly of oxygen, silicon, aluminum, iron, and other elements.
Volcanic eruptions occur when magma from beneath the Earth's crust reaches the surface.
