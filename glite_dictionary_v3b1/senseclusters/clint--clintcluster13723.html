clint

noun
limestone block
9.9
Pronunciation: / klɪnt / (AmE), / klɪnt / (BrE)
a block of limestone separated by fissures in a limestone pavement, typically found in karst landscapes

Examples

A clint block
The geologist explained how each clint was formed by erosion over thousands of years.
Tourists admired the unique pattern of clints and grikes on the limestone pavement.
Careful footing was needed to cross the uneven surface of the clint field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grike - a deep fissure separating blocks of limestone in a pavement
