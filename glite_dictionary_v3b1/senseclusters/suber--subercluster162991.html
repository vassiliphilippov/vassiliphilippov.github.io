suber

noun
corky plant tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːbər / (AmE), / ˈsuːbə / (BrE)
a layer of corky tissue in the bark of some plants that protects against water loss and pests

Examples

Suber layer
The suber forms a protective barrier on the tree's surface.
Scientists studied the suber to understand how plants prevent water loss.
Damage to the suber can make trees more vulnerable to disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bark - the outer covering of the stems of trees and other plants
  • synonym: cork - the outer protective tissue of certain plants, especially the cork oak
