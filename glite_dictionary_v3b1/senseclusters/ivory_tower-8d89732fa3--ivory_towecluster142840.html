ivory tower

noun
detached or aloof intellectual state
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊər /; / ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈaɪvəri ˈtaʊə / (BrE)
a situation or place where someone is separated from practical or everyday concerns, especially in an academic or intellectual way

Examples

An ivory tower life
He left his ivory tower to work directly with disadvantaged communities.
Many critics claim that university professors often remain in their ivory tower, disconnected from society's problems.
Some politicians accuse experts of offering solutions from their ivory tower instead of practical experience.
As a writer, she tries not to live in an ivory tower but to stay in touch with the public.
His ivory tower attitude made collaboration with others difficult.
Many critics accused the policy makers of making decisions from their ivory tower without understanding ordinary people's lives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubble - a state of being isolated from real-world problems or experiences
  • antonym: real world - the practical, everyday world; opposite of 'ivory tower'
