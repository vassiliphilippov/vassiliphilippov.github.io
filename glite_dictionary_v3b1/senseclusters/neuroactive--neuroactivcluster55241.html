neuroactive

adjective
affecting nervous system
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈæktɪv / (BrE)
affecting or interacting with the nervous system or nerve cells

Examples

Neuroactive drugs
Researchers are studying how neuroactive substances can help treat neurological disorders.
Some plants produce neuroactive compounds that affect animal behavior.
The medication is considered neuroactive because it alters brain chemistry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neuroinhibitory - reducing or suppressing activity in the nervous system
  • synonym: neurotoxic - harmful or poisonous to nerve tissue
