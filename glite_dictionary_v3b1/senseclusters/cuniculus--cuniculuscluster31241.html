cuniculus

noun
underground tunnel
9.9
Pronunciation: / kjuːˈnɪkjələs / (AmE), / kjuːˈnɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
an underground tunnel or passage, especially one constructed for water supply or found in anatomy

Examples

An ancient cuniculus
The ancient engineers constructed a cuniculus to bring fresh water into the city.
Archaeologists discovered a hidden cuniculus beneath the ruins of the old settlement.
In anatomy, a cuniculus can refer to a small channel or duct within the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aqueduct - an artificial channel for conveying water
  • synonym: tunnel - an underground passage through or under an obstacle
