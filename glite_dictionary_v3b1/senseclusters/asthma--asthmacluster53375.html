asthma

noun
chronic respiratory disorder causing wheezing and shortness of breath
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈæz.mə/ /; / /ˈæs.mə/ (AmE), /ˈæs.mə/ /; / /ˈæz.mə/ (BrE)
A chronic medical condition in which a person’s airways become inflamed and narrowed, causing difficulty in breathing, wheezing, and coughing, often triggered by allergies or other factors.

Examples

Chronic asthma attack
During spring, his asthma often gets worse due to pollen.
Physical activity can sometimes trigger asthma attacks in children.
She uses an inhaler daily to manage her asthma symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bronchitis - inflammation of the bronchial tubes
  • synonym: emphysema - chronic respiratory condition involving damaged air sacs
