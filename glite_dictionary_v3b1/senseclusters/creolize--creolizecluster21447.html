creolize

verb
develop into a creole (linguistics)
9.6 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkriÀê…ôÀåla…™z / (AmE), / ÀàkriÀê…ô äÀåla…™z / (BrE)
(of a language) to develop into a creole, especially when a pidgin language becomes stable and is learned as a first language by a community.

Examples

To creolize a language
Linguists have documented how several pidgin languages creolize over successive generations.
Some scholars debate the conditions under which a language will creolize rather than remain a pidgin.
When a community adopts a pidgin as their native tongue, it may gradually creolize.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creole - a stable natural language developed from a mixture of different languages
  • degree lower: pidginize - to develop or simplify into a pidgin
