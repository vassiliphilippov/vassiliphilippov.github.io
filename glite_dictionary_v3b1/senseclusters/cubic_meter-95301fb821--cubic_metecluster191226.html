cubic meter

noun
metric unit of volume (1,000 liters)
5.6 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈmiː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkjuː.bɪk ˈmiː.tə/ (BrE)
a metric unit of volume equal to the volume of a cube with sides of one meter; equivalent to 1,000 liters; used for measuring large amounts of liquid or space

Examples

One cubic meter
A shipping container might have a capacity of thirty cubic meters.
One cubic meter of concrete weighs more than two tons.
The pool holds nearly fifty cubic meters of water when it is full.
A cubic metre of wood weighs much less than a cubic metre of steel.
Each shipment contained approximately five cubic metres of sand.
The swimming pool holds almost two thousand cubic metres of water.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: liter - metric unit of volume equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters
