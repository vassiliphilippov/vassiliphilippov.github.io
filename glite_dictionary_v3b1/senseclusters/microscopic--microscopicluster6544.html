microscopic

adjective
very small; seen with a microscope
1.0 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.krəˈskɑː.pɪk /; / ˌmaɪkrəˈskɑːpɪk / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.krəˈskɒ.pɪk /; / ˌmaɪkrəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
so small that it can only be seen with a microscope; extremely small

Examples

Microscopic dust particles
Dust particles are often microscopic and invisible to the naked eye.
The scientist discovered microscopic organisms in the water sample.
Using a microscopic examination, they identified the bacteria causing the infection.
The scientist used a microscopic lens to examine the specimen.
Their differences were so microscopic that no one noticed.
There were microscopic cracks in the metal that caused it to break.

  • synonym: minute - extremely small in size or amount
