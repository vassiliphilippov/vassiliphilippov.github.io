intrusive

adjective
geological: rock formed by magma forced into other rocks
5.8 8.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːsɪv / (BrE)
(of rock) formed by the forcible introduction of molten material into cracks within other rock layers.

Examples

Intrusive rock formation
Geologists found an intrusive layer cutting through the sedimentary strata.
Granite is often classified as an intrusive igneous rock.
The scientist explained how intrusive rocks differ from those formed on the surface.
