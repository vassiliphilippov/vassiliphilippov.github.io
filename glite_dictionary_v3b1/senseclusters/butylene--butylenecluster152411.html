butylene

noun
isomeric hydrocarbon, used in rubbers
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbjuː.tɪ.liːn/ /; / /ˈbjuː.tə.liːn/ (AmE), /ˈbjuː.tɪ.liːn/ /; / /ˈbjuː.tə.liːn/ (BrE)
any of three isomeric hydrocarbons (C4H8), used especially in making synthetic rubbers and other chemicals.

Examples

Butylene chemical compound
Butylene is a key ingredient in the production of synthetic rubber for tires.
Some industrial plastics are made using butylene as a starting material.
The different isomers of butylene exhibit slightly varied chemical properties.
