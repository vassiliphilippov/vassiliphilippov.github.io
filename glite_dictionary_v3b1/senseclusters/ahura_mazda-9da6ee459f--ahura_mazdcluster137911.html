Ahura Mazda

proper noun
supreme god of Zoroastrianism
Pronunciation: / əˌhʊrə ˈmɑzdə / (AmE), / əˌhʊərə ˈmæzdə / (BrE)
the supreme god in Zoroastrianism, regarded as the source of light, goodness, and truth

Examples

Ahura Mazda god
Ancient Persian kings often attributed their victories to the guidance of Ahura Mazda.
The teachings of Ahura Mazda emphasize the importance of truth and righteousness.
Zoroastrians worship Ahura Mazda as the creator and sustainer of the universe.
