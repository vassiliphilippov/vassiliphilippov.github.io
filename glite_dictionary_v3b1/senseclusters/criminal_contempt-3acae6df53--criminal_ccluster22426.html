criminal contempt

noun
disrespectful act that hinders justice (legal)
7.2 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɪm.ɪn.əl kən.ˈtempt/ /; / /ˈkrɪm.ə.nəl kən.ˈtempt/ (AmE), /ˈkrɪm.ɪn.əl kən.ˈtempt/ /; / /ˈkrɪm.ə.nəl kən.ˈtempt/ (BrE)
The offense of showing disrespect or defiance toward a court or its proceedings, which can impede the administration of justice and result in criminal penalties.

Examples

Criminal contempt of court
Any deliberate attempt to disrupt court proceedings can be punished as criminal contempt.
He was found guilty of criminal contempt for refusing to obey the court's direct order.
The judge charged the witness with criminal contempt after repeated outbursts during testimony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: civil contempt - failure to comply with a court order, usually to benefit another party; not a criminal act
