Remembrance Day

noun
commemoration day for war dead
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɛmbrəns deɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈmɛmbrəns deɪ / (BrE)
the Sunday nearest to November 11, observed especially in the UK and Commonwealth countries to commemorate those who died in World War I and World War II

Examples

Remembrance Day silence
A two-minute silence is observed across the country on Remembrance Day.
Many people wear poppies on Remembrance Day to honor fallen soldiers.
The Queen attended the Remembrance Day ceremony at the Cenotaph.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Memorial Day - a day for remembering those who died in military service, observed in the US in May
  • synonym: Veterans Day - a day honoring veterans in the US, observed on November 11
