Orwell

proper noun
British writer; author of 'Animal Farm', '1984'
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.wɛl / (AmE), / ˈɔː.wɛl / (BrE)
(1903–1950) The pen name of Eric Arthur Blair, an influential British writer and journalist, best known for his novels 'Animal Farm' and 'Nineteen Eighty-Four', and for his works on social injustice, totalitarianism, and language.

Examples

Orwell's novels
Orwell's novels have become classics of modern English literature.
Many political discussions today reference the dystopian visions described by Orwell.
The term 'Orwellian' is derived from the themes and ideas explored by Orwell in his works.
