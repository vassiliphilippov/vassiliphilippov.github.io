guar gum

noun
thickening agent from guar plant seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈɡwɑr ˌɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑː ˌɡʌm / (BrE)
a substance obtained from the seeds of the guar plant, used to thicken foods and as a sizing agent in paper and textiles

Examples

Guar gum thickener
Guar gum is also utilized in the production of paper to control ink absorbency.
Gluten-free recipes often use guar gum to improve texture.
The manufacturer added guar gum to increase the viscosity of the yogurt.
