unfaceted

adjective
lacking facets, especially of a gem
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfæsɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfæsɪtɪd / (BrE)
not having facets; lacking cut or polished flat surfaces, especially of a gem or mineral.

Examples

An unfaceted diamond
An unfaceted diamond reflects light differently than a traditional cut gemstone.
Collectors sometimes value unfaceted crystals for their natural beauty.
The jeweler examined the unfaceted stone before deciding how to cut it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faceted - having facets; cut with many flat surfaces
