dual-use

adjective
serving two purposes
7.1 0.48/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀê…ôl juÀês /; / Ààdu…ôl Àåjus / (AmE), / ÀàdjuÀê.…ôl juÀês /; / ÀàdjuÀê.…ôl ÀåjuÀês / (BrE)
designed or suitable for serving two different purposes (often used for things that can be used for both civilian and military purposes)

Examples

Dual-use goods
Many dual-use goods require special licensing before they can be exported.
The laboratory developed a dual-use technology that could be used for both civilian and military applications.
They installed dual-use containers that function as both recycling and trash bins.
Many chemicals are classified as dual-use because they have both industrial and military applications.
She outlined the risks involved in developing dual-use research in biotechnology.
The export of dual-use technology is strictly controlled by international law.

Synonyms

  • dual-purpose
    4.4 0.33/m
    dual-purpose describes something made to serve two functions at once, often everyday tasks, while dual-use highlights civilian and military roles.
  • bifunctional
    6.7 0.00/m
    bifunctional means having two distinct functions in design, often technical, while dual-use stresses possible civilian and military applications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-use - designed for one-time or single purpose
