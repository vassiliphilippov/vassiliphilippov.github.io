outwit

verb
defeat by cleverness or cunning
1.0
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈwɪt / (AmE), / aʊtˈwɪt / (BrE)
to defeat or get the better of someone by being more clever or cunning than they are

Examples

To outwit a rival
It takes quick thinking to outwit a skilled detective.
She tried to outwit her brother in every board game they played together.
The chess champion managed to outwit all his opponents in the tournament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outfox - to defeat someone through cunning or cleverness
  • synonym: outsmart - to defeat someone by being more intelligent or clever
