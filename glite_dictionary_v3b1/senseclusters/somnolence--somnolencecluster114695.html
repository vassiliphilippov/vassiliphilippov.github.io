somnolence

noun
state of drowsiness or sleepiness
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːmnələns / (AmE), / ˈsɒmnələns / (BrE)
A state of strong desire for sleep or unusual drowsiness; sleepiness.

Examples

Feeling somnolence after lunch
After a large meal, many people experience a feeling of somnolence in the afternoon.
The doctor noted the patient's somnolence during the examination.
The medication may cause somnolence as a side effect, so avoid driving while taking it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sleepiness - the state of feeling ready to fall asleep
  • antonym: wakefulness - the state of being awake and alert
