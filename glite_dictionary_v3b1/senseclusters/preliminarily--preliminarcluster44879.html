preliminarily

adverb
before main event
6.3
Pronunciation: / prɪˌlɪməˈnɛrəli /; / prɪˈlɪməˌnɛrəli / (AmE), / prɪˌlɪmɪˈnɛərəli /; / prɪˈlɪmɪnərɪli / (BrE)
in a way that happens before the main or final action, as an introduction, preparation, or first step

Examples

To preliminarily discuss results
The results were preliminarily discussed before the official announcement was made.
We preliminarily agreed on the terms, but the final contract will be signed next week.
The committee preliminarily approved the proposal, pending further review.
The results were preliminarily announced, but the official report will be released next week.
She was preliminarily selected for the team, pending a final decision after the trials.
The plan was preliminarily approved by the board, subject to further discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finally - at the end; after everything else
  • synonym: initially - at the beginning; at first
  • synonym: provisionally - for the time being; temporarily
