tensive

adjective
phonetic muscular tension
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ˈtɛnsɪv / (BrE)
in phonetics, produced with greater muscular tension, especially referring to vowels contrasted with lax vowels

Examples

Tensive vowel sound
The linguist explained that the tensive quality of the vowel made it sound distinct from its lax counterpart.
In some languages, a tensive vowel can completely change the meaning of a word.
Her careful articulation of tensive consonants impressed her phonetics professor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lax - produced with less muscular tension, especially in vowels
  • synonym: tense - produced with greater muscular effort, especially in vowels
