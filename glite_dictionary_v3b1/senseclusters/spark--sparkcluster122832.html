spark

verb
cause to begin; trigger
3.4 11/m
Pronunciation: / sp…ërk / (AmE), / sp…ëÀêk / (BrE)
to cause something to begin or happen, especially suddenly or as a result of a stimulus; to trigger or provoke

Examples

To spark a debate
Her sudden resignation sparked speculation about the company's future.
The news report sparked a heated debate among the viewers.
The teacher's passion for science sparked my interest in chemistry.
