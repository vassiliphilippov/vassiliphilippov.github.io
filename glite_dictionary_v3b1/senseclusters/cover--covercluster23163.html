cover

noun
military protective gunfire or bombardment
4.4 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈkʌ.və/ (BrE)
something that provides shelter, concealment, or protection, such as hiding places, false identities, or military protection

Examples

Take cover from danger
Helicopters gave cover to the convoy as it moved through hostile territory.
The platoon advanced while their comrades provided cover from behind sandbags.
They waited for cover before attempting to cross the open field.
He looked for cover as soon as the rain started pouring.
The soldiers took cover behind the rocks to avoid enemy fire.
The thick fog provided enough cover for the team to move undetected.
As a spy, he maintained a cover as an international businessman.
Her diplomatic role was only a cover for her intelligence work.
The police officer's cover was blown when a suspect recognized him.

  • synonym: front - a person or thing used to conceal someone's true identity or activities
