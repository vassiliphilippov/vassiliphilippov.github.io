pyranose

noun
six-membered sugar ring
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpaɪ.rə.noʊs/ /; / /paɪ.ˈreɪ.noʊs/ (AmE), /ˈpaɪ.rə.nəʊs/ /; / /paɪ.ˈreɪ.nəʊs/ (BrE)
a sugar molecule whose ring structure contains six atoms, including one oxygen atom

Examples

A pyranose ring
Glucose often forms a pyranose ring when dissolved in water.
The structure of a pyranose is important in many biological processes.
Chemists can distinguish between a pyranose and a furanose form of sugars.
