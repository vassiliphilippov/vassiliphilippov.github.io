silver sage

noun
a silvery-leaved shrub of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvɚ seɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə seɪdʒ / (BrE)
a low, much-branched perennial shrub native to the western United States with silvery leaves, important for providing browse and shelter to wildlife

Examples

The silver sage plant
Botanists studied how the silver sage adapts to harsh desert climates.
The silver sage thrives in dry, open terrain across much of the western United States.
Wildlife often take shelter under the dense branches of the silver sage.

Related Terms
