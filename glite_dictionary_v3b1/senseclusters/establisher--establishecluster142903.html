establisher

noun
pioneer species
8.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈstæb.lɪ.ʃər/ /; / /ə.ˈstæb.lɪ.ʃər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈstæb.lɪ.ʃə/ /; / /ə.ˈstæb.lɪ.ʃə/ (BrE)
an organism or species that first becomes established in a habitat, helping to form a new ecological community

Examples

Plant establisher
The invasive reed acted as an establisher, quickly dominating the marsh after the flood.
Lichens are often the first establishers on bare rock, paving the way for other species.
In ecological restoration, scientists look for effective establishers to help rebuild damaged habitats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pioneer - an organism that is among the first to colonize a new area
  • antonym: successor - an organism or species that follows after the first colonizers in a habitat
