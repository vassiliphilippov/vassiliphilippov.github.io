dodging

noun
avoidance by trickery or deceit
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of avoiding responsibility, questions, or something unpleasant by using trickery or cleverness

Examples

Dodging questions
His dodging of responsibility became obvious to everyone in the office.
Repeated dodging of the truth damaged his reputation beyond repair.
Tax dodging can result in heavy penalties if discovered by the authorities.
Her dodging during the interview frustrated both the host and the audience.
I'm tired of your constant dodging whenever I ask a direct question.
The politician's answer was mere dodging, skillfully avoiding the real issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoidance - the act of keeping away from something
  • synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding something you should do, often by cunning
  • antonym: fulfillment - the carrying out of a duty or obligation
  • synonym: prevarication - the act of avoiding giving a direct answer; evasion
