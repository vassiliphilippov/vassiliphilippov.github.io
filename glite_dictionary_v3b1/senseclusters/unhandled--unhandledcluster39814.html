unhandled

adjective
not processed (computing)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhændəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈhændəld / (BrE)
not caught, processed, or dealt with by a computer program or system.

Examples

Unhandled error message
The program crashed due to an unhandled error in the code.
If an unhandled exception occurs, the application may stop working unexpectedly.
Developers should ensure that no unhandled cases remain in the final version of the software.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignored - deliberately not noticed or dealt with
  • antonym: processed - dealt with or managed by a system
