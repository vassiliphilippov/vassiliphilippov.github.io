till

noun
glacial deposit of mixed sediments
6.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / tɪl / (AmE), / tɪl / (BrE)
Unsorted and unstratified soil or sediment deposited directly by a glacier, typically containing a mixture of clay, sand, gravel, and boulders.

Examples

Glacial till
Farmers often struggle to plant crops in till because of its stony composition.
Geologists examined the till to learn more about the region's glacial history.
The field was covered in a thick layer of till left by the retreating glacier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moraine - an accumulation of glacial debris (soil and rock) carried and deposited by a glacier
