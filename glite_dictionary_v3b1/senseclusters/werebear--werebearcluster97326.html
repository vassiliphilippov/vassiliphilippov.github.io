werebear

noun
mythical shapeshifter
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛrˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈwɪəˌbɛə / (BrE)
a mythical creature that can transform between human and bear form

Examples

The werebear legend
The villagers feared the werebear that was rumored to haunt the nearby woods.
In the legend, a werebear protected the mountain from intruders.
The story describes how a werebear could shift shape under the full moon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: human - a person, as opposed to an animal or mythical being
  • synonym: lycanthrope - a person who can transform into an animal, especially a wolf
  • synonym: werewolf - a mythical being that can change between human and wolf form
