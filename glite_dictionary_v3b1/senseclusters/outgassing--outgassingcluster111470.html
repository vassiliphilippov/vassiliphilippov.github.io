outgassing

noun
release of gases
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈaʊt.gæs.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈaʊt.gæs.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which gases are released from the interior of a planet, moon, or other body, often through volcanic activity

Examples

Planetary outgassing
Scientists study outgassing to understand how a planet's atmosphere develops over time.
Volcanic outgassing played a major role in shaping the early Earth's environment.
The spacecraft detected signs of outgassing on the surface of the distant moon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degassing - the removal or release of gas from within something
  • synonym: volcanism - the eruption of molten rock, ash, and gases from a planet's interior
