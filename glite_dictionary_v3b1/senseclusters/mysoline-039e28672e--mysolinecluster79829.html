Mysoline

noun
anticonvulsant drug (trade name)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪsəˌliːn / (AmE), / ˈmaɪsəliːn / (BrE)
A trademarked anticonvulsant drug used to treat grand mal seizures and essential tremor.

Examples

Mysoline medicine
After being diagnosed with essential tremor, he was prescribed Mysoline to help control his symptoms.
Some patients experience drowsiness as a side effect of Mysoline therapy.
The neurologist recommended switching from phenytoin to Mysoline for better seizure management.
