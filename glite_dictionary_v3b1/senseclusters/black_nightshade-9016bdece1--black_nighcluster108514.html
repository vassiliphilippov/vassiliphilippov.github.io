black nightshade

noun
a herbal plant with black berries, sometimes poisonous
6.8 7.4
Pronunciation: /blæk ˈnaɪt.ʃeɪd/ (AmE), /blæk ˈnaɪt.ʃeɪd/ (BrE)
A Eurasian plant (Solanum nigrum) with white flowers and black berries, some parts of which can be poisonous or, in some varieties, edible.

Examples

Black nightshade berries
Children should never eat black nightshade berries they find outdoors, since identification can be difficult.
Many gardeners are surprised to find black nightshade growing wild among their vegetables.
Some cultures use the ripe berries of black nightshade in traditional cooking, but caution is necessary because parts of the plant are toxic.
