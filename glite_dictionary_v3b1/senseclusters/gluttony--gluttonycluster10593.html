gluttony

noun
habitual excessive eating or drinking
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlʌtəni /; / ˈɡlʌt̬əni / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌtəni / (BrE)
the act or habit of eating or drinking too much; excessive greed for food (often considered a vice or sin)

Examples

Excessive gluttony
He blamed his weight gain on years of unchecked gluttony.
Her gluttony was evident as she filled her plate for the third time at the buffet.
Religious texts often warn against the sin of gluttony.
His gluttony during the holiday feast left him feeling uncomfortable for hours.
Many religions warn against the dangers of gluttony as a destructive vice.
The painting depicts gluttony as one of the seven deadly sins, showing a figure overindulging in food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: greed - intense and selfish desire, often for wealth or food
  • antonym: moderation - avoiding excess or extremes, especially in eating
  • antonym: temperance - moderation or self-restraint, especially regarding food or drink; opposite of gluttony
