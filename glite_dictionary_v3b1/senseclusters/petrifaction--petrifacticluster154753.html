petrifaction

noun
process of turning organic material into stone
4.7 8.0
Pronunciation: /e.trɪ.ˈfæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /e.trɪ.ˈfæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of turning organic material (like plants) into stone by mineralization, or the fossilized object created by this process

Examples

Petrifaction process
Scientists studied the petrifaction process to understand how ancient plants were preserved for millions of years.
The petrifaction of trees has resulted in beautiful stone logs in the Petrified Forest National Park.
The display included samples of petrifaction showing detailed patterns of the original wood.
Geologists study petrifaction to understand the mineralization process in fossil formation.
The desert walk revealed a rare petrifaction, once a piece of wood, now solid rock.
The museum displayed a petrifaction of an ancient tree trunk that turned to stone millions of years ago.

  • synonym: fossilization - the process of becoming a fossil
