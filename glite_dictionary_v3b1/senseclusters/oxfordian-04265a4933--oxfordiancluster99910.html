Oxfordian

adjective
Shakespeare authorship theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɑks.ˈfɔːr.dɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ɒks.ˈfɔː.dɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the theory that Edward de Vere, the Earl of Oxford, wrote the works attributed to Shakespeare.

Examples

Oxfordian theory
The Oxfordian argument challenges the traditional view of Shakespeare's authorship.
Many literary scholars debate the evidence presented by Oxfordian researchers.
Her essay took a distinctly Oxfordian perspective on the identity of Shakespeare.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Stratfordian - relating to the belief that William Shakespeare of Stratford-upon-Avon wrote the works attributed to him
