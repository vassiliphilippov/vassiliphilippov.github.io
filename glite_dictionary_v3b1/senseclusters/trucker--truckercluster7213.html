trucker

noun
person who drives a truck as a job
3.4 2.96/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtrÊŒkÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆtrÊŒkÉ™ / (BrE)
a person whose job is to drive a truck, especially for transporting goods over long distances

Examples

A trucker drives trucks
During winter storms, being a trucker can be especially challenging.
My uncle worked as a trucker for more than twenty years.
The trucker stopped at the diner to rest before his next delivery.

Synonyms

  • truck driver
    1.2 3.10/m
    truck driver is the standard, more formal job name used in writing and speech, while trucker is more informal and casual.
  • truckster
    7.4 0.00/m
    truckster sounds like a rare or informal term for someone who drives trucks, while trucker is the common everyday word.
  • teamster
    6.6 0.27/m
    teamster often means a truck driver who belongs to a union in North America, while trucker is the general job name.
  • truckie
    9.4 0.00/m
    truckie is an informal Australian or UK word for a truck driver, while trucker is the standard, mostly American term.
  • hauler
    4.1 1.56/m
    hauler can mean a company or business that moves goods by road, while trucker is an individual who drives the truck.
