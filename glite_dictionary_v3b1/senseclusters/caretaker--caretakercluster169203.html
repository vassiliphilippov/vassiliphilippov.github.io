caretaker

noun
person who looks after property or someone
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person employed to look after a building, property, or sometimes a person, especially when the owner is absent or unable to do so.

Examples

Building caretaker
As a caretaker, Tom was responsible for the elderly man's medication and daily needs.
The school caretaker opens the gates every morning and keeps the halls clean.
When her parents traveled abroad, they hired a caretaker to watch over their house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: custodian - a person responsible for cleaning and maintenance in a building
  • synonym: guardian - someone legally responsible for another person, especially a child
