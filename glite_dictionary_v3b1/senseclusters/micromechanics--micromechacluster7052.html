micromechanics

noun
mechanics at micro scale
8.6
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kroʊ.mə.ˈkæn.ɪks/ /; / /maɪ.kroʊ.mɪ.ˈkæn.ɪks/ (AmE), /maɪ.krəʊ.mə.ˈkæn.ɪks/ /; / /maɪ.krəʊ.mɪ.ˈkæn.ɪks/ (BrE)
the study and design of mechanical systems and materials at the microscopic or micrometer scale

Examples

Micromechanics study
Advances in micromechanics have enabled the creation of tiny sensors used in smartphones.
Researchers in micromechanics analyze how microscopic gears and levers behave under stress.
The field of micromechanics is essential for developing new medical devices at a miniature scale.
Researchers use micromechanics to analyze how tiny fibers in tissues respond to stress.
Advances in micromechanics have improved our understanding of bone strength at the cellular level.
The micromechanics of composite materials help engineers design stronger and lighter products.
Advances in micromechanics have helped engineers design stronger composite materials.
The course on micromechanics covers how microscopic structures influence material strength.
Researchers in micromechanics analyze how tiny flaws can lead to material failure.
