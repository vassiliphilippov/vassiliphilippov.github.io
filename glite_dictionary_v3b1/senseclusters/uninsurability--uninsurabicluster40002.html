uninsurability

noun
the state of being uninsurable
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.ɪnˌʃʊr.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌʌn.ɪnˌʃɔː.rəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The condition or quality of being uninsurable; circumstances in which an insurance company will not offer coverage to an applicant.

Examples

The uninsurability problem
Due to his numerous speeding tickets, his uninsurability became a major issue when trying to register his car.
Flood-prone areas often face uninsurability because insurance companies consider them too risky.
The company reviewed the applicant's medical history and determined her uninsurability for life insurance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insurability - the quality of being insurable
  • synonym: uninsurable - not able to be insured
