Theatine

noun
Catholic religious order
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθiːəˌtiːn / (AmE), / ˈθiːətiːn / (BrE)
a member of a Roman Catholic religious order founded in the 16th century dedicated to reform and education

Examples

A Theatine priest
The Theatine order played a significant role in the Catholic Reformation.
He studied the history of the Theatines and their influence on church education.
A group of Theatine priests established a new school in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Benedictine - a member of a Catholic monastic order following the Rule of Saint Benedict
  • synonym: Jesuit - a member of the Society of Jesus, a Catholic religious order
