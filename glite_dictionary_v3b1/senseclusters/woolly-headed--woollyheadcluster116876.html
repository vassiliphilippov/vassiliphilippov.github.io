woolly-headed

adjective
confused or vague in thinking
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊliˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊliˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person or their ideas) confused, vague, or lacking clear thought, especially in reasoning or planning.

Examples

A woolly-headed idea
Don't be so woolly-headed; we need a clear and practical plan.
His woolly-headed explanation left everyone more confused than before.
She became quite woolly-headed after sitting in the long, stuffy meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear-headed - able to think clearly and sensibly
  • synonym: muddleheaded - confused or lacking clear thought
