surrogate

adjective
acting as a substitute parent or caregiver
7.2 1.63/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…úÀêr.…ô.…°…ôt / (AmE), / Ààs år.…ô.…°…ôt / (BrE)
(of a person or role) providing care or fulfilling parental duties without being related by blood or legal ties; substitute.

Examples

Surrogate parent
After his mother died, his aunt stepped in as a surrogate parent.
He acted as a surrogate father to his friend's children during difficult times.
Many children in the shelter found a surrogate family among the caring staff.

Synonyms

  • foster parent
    3.2 1.67/m
    a foster parent cares for a child placed by authorities in their home, while a surrogate provides parental care without blood or legal ties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foster - providing parental care to a child not one's own by birth or legal adoption
