Y2K

noun
turn-of-millennium cultural aesthetic
Pronunciation: / waɪ tuː keɪ / (AmE), / waɪ tuː keɪ / (BrE)
A popular cultural style and aesthetic that references late-1990s and early-2000s fashion, design, and media, often featuring shiny materials, futuristic motifs, small sunglasses, and tech-inspired graphics.

Examples

Y2K fashion trend
The designer's new collection channels a Y2K vibe with metallic fabrics and tiny sunglasses.
They decorated the café in a Y2K style, using chrome surfaces and neon lights throughout.
Vintage shops now sell clothing labeled Y2K, attracting buyers nostalgic for early-2000s trends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: early-2000s fashion - clothing and style trends popular around the late 1990s and early 2000s
  • synonym: retro - a style that celebrates or imitates fashions and design from the past
