rictus

noun
a fixed, open-mouthed grimace or grin
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪk.təs / (AmE), / ˈrɪk.təs / (BrE)
A fixed grimace or grin, often showing the teeth, typically caused by pain, horror, or strong emotion.

Examples

A rictus grimace
Fear contorted his face into a wild rictus as the thunder crashed.
She greeted him with a nervous rictus that barely resembled a smile.
The corpse's mouth was twisted into a ghastly rictus of pain.
