septicemia

noun
serious bloodstream infection
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛptɪˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌsɛptɪˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which harmful microorganisms enter the bloodstream, potentially leading to severe infection throughout the body.

Examples

Septicemia infection
Prompt treatment is crucial because septicemia can quickly become life-threatening if left unchecked.
Signs of septicemia include high fever, chills, and rapid heartbeat.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with septicemia after bacteria were found in her blood culture.
Early signs of septicaemia include high fever, chills, and rapid breathing.
Prompt treatment is necessary to prevent septicaemia from causing organ failure.
The patient developed septicaemia after surgery due to a bacterial infection.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bacteremia - presence of bacteria in the blood, similar but less severe
  • synonym: blood poisoning - another term for septicemia
  • synonym: sepsis - a life-threatening condition caused by the body's response to infection
