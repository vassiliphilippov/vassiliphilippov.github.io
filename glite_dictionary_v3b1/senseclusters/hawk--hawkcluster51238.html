hawk

noun
advocate of aggressive foreign policy
Pronunciation: / hɔk / (AmE), / hɔːk / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports aggressive or military action in international affairs

Examples

A political hawk
During the debate, the hawks clashed with the doves over foreign intervention policies.
He has a reputation as a hawk due to his tough stance on national security.
Some politicians are known as hawks because they favor military solutions over diplomacy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dove - a person who prefers peaceful or conciliatory policies in international relations
