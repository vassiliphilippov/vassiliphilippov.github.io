Christianization

noun
conversion to Christianity
4.8 7.4
Pronunciation: /krɪs.tʃə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /krɪs.tʃə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The act or process of converting individuals or groups to Christianity, making something Christian in character, or the adoption of Christianity as a religion by a people or region.

Examples

The Christianization of regions
Early missionaries played a key role in the Christianization of remote regions.
Many ancient customs disappeared during the Christianization of the local population.
The Christianization of Europe had a profound impact on its culture and institutions.
Efforts at Christianisation sometimes included building churches and introducing new religious practices.
The Christianisation of Scandinavia took several centuries, beginning in the ninth century.
The region experienced rapid Christianisation after missionaries arrived from Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conversion - the act or process of changing religion or belief
  • antonym: secularisation - the process of making something less religious or more secular
