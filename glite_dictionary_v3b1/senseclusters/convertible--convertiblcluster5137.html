convertible

adjective
able to be converted or adapted
9.9 4.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝː.t̬ə.bəl /; / kənˈvɜrtəbəl / (AmE), / kənˈvɜː.tɪ.bəl /; / kənˈvɜːtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be changed into a different form, function, or exchanged for something else (including in finance)

Examples

Convertible car seat
Many parents choose a convertible car seat that grows with their child.
The guest room has a convertible sofa that doubles as a bed.
Their living room features a convertible table that expands for extra guests.
He invested in convertible shares, hoping their value would increase before conversion.
Many bonds are issued as convertible securities, allowing holders to exchange them for company stock.
The new vouchers are convertible to cash at any branch of the bank.

  • antonym: fixed - unable to be changed or adapted
  • antonym: nonconvertible - not able to be converted or exchanged
