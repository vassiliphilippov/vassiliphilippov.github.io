catch the wind

verb
use wind for movement or propulsion
2.9
Pronunciation: / kætʃ ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / kætʃ ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
to use the force of the wind for movement or propulsion, especially by adjusting sails or surfaces to make use of wind power

Examples

To catch the wind in sails
During the race, he struggled to catch the wind as the breeze kept changing direction.
The crew trimmed the sails to catch the wind and increase the boat's speed.
The windmill blades are designed to catch the wind efficiently.
