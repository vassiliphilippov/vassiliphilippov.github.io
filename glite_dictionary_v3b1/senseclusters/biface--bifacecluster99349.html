biface

noun
two-sided stone tool
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌfeɪs / (BrE)
a stone tool that has been flaked on both sides to produce a sharp edge

Examples

Stone biface tool
The archaeologist carefully examined the biface found at the excavation site.
Early humans used a biface for cutting meat and shaping wood.
A well-made biface can reveal much about ancient toolmaking techniques.

Related Terms

  • synonym: handaxe - a prehistoric stone tool shaped and flaked on both sides
  • antonym: uniface - a stone tool flaked on only one side
