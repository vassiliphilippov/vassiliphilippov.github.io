plodder

noun
person who walks laboriously
6.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpl…ëÀêd…ôr / (AmE), / Ààpl…íd…ô / (BrE)
A person who walks in a slow, heavy, and laborious way.

Examples

A slow plodder
The plodder made his way down the muddy lane, leaving deep footprints behind.
Though considered a plodder, she reached the finish line with steady determination.
Unlike the sprinters, he was a plodder, taking his time over every step.
