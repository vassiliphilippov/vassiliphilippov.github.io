underplant

noun
plant beneath others
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌplænt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌplɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant or group of plants grown beneath larger plants, often to provide ground cover or decoration

Examples

A shady underplant
The gardener selected a colorful underplant to brighten the shady area beneath the trees.
Hostas are a popular choice as an underplant in many landscaped gardens.
Using a dense underplant can help prevent weeds from growing around larger shrubs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groundcover - a low-growing plant that covers the ground under taller plants
