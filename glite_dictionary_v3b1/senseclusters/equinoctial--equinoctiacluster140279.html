equinoctial

noun
the celestial equator
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåiÀêkw…ôÀàn…ëÀêk É…ôl / (AmE), / ÀåiÀêkw…™Ààn…ík É…ôl / (BrE)
The great circle on the celestial sphere that is midway between the celestial poles; also known as the celestial equator.

Examples

The equinoctial line
Ancient astronomers mapped the equinoctial to chart the movement of the stars.
Many star charts prominently display the equinoctial as a reference point.
The sun crosses the equinoctial twice a year at the equinoxes.

Synonyms

  • celestial equator
    8.5 0.20/m
    celestial equator is the imaginary great circle that divides the sky into northern and southern halves, while equinoctial is its formal name.
  • equinoctial circle
    9.9 0.00/m
    equinoctial circle is the great circle that coincides with the celestial equator, while equinoctial is the same technical name used for it.
  • equinoctial line
    9.9 0.00/m
    equinoctial line is the imaginary great circle described as a line in some sources, while equinoctial is the standard name.
