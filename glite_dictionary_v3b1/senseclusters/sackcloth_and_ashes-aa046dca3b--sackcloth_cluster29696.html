sackcloth and ashes

noun
display of deep remorse or repentance
Pronunciation: / ˈsæklɔθ ənd ˈæʃɪz /; / ˈsækˌklɔθ ənd ˈæʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈsæklɒθ ənd ˈæʃɪz /; / ˈsækˌklɒθ ənd ˈæʃɪz / (BrE)
an expression or display of deep sorrow, remorse, or repentance, often as a sign of penance (usually used figuratively)

Examples

Sackcloth and ashes expression
After realizing the gravity of his mistake, he appeared before the board in sackcloth and ashes.
She expressed her apology, but he did not expect sackcloth and ashes in return.
The politician's public apology was sincere, but there were no sackcloth and ashes as some had demanded.
After her apology, she felt as if she should wear sackcloth and ashes for her actions.
Even though the mistake was minor, he acted as if he should don sackcloth and ashes.
In Shakespeare's play, the character appeared in sackcloth and ashes to show remorse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrition - the state of feeling sorry for bad behavior
  • synonym: mourning - expression of deep sorrow for a loss, especially someone's death
  • synonym: penitence - the action of feeling or showing sorrow and regret for having done wrong
  • synonym: remorse - deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
