go back

verb
to originate or date from a previous time
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˈbæk / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈbæk / (BrE)
to have existed or started at a particular time in the past; to belong to an earlier time

Examples

Go back many years
Some of these laws go back hundreds of years.
The origins of the festival go back to ancient times.
This tradition can go back several centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: date back - to have existed since a particular time in the past
