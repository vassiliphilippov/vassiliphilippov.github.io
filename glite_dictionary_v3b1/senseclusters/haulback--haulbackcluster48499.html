haulback

noun
retrieving line or net
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈhɔːlˌbæk / (BrE)
the act of pulling in a fishing line, net, or cable after it has been set out

Examples

The haulback of nets
After several hours of fishing, the crew started the haulback to see what they had caught.
The haulback of the net was difficult due to the strong current.
During the haulback, the team worked together to pull in the heavy cable.
