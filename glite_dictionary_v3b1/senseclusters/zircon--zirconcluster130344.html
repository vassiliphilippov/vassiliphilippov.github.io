zircon

noun
a mineral; zirconium silicate, gemstone
5.0 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈzɜr.kɑn / (AmE), / ˈzɜː.kɒn / (BrE)
a common mineral consisting of zirconium silicate, typically occurring as small crystals; used as a gemstone and as a refractory material

Examples

A zircon gemstone
Although it resembles a diamond, a zircon has different physical properties.
Industrial zircon is often used to line the inside of furnaces because of its heat resistance.
The museum displayed a rare blue zircon in its gemstone collection.
