gnomonic

adjective
map projection or sundials
9.9
Pronunciation: /nə.ˈmɑː.nɪk/ (AmE), /nə.ˈmɒ.nɪk/ (BrE)
relating to a type of map projection or to sundials, especially in geometry or astronomy

Examples

Gnomonic map projection
A gnomonic map projection shows all great circles as straight lines.
The ancient Greeks used gnomonic principles to design accurate sundials.
In cartography, gnomonic projections are useful for plotting the shortest path between two points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartographic - relating to mapmaking or maps
