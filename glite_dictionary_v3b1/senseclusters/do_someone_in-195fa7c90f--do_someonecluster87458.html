do (someone) in

verb
defeat or overwhelm someone
7.6
Pronunciation: / du ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn / (AmE), / duː ˈsʌmwʌn ɪn / (BrE)
to defeat, exhaust, or overwhelm someone; to cause someone to be unable to continue or to fail

Examples

To do you in completely
If you stay up all night again, it's going to do you in.
The heat was enough to do them in before the race even began.
The long hike really did me in, and I slept for hours when I got home.
