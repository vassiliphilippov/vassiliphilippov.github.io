vehement

adjective
forceful or passionate; intense
7.7 1.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàviÀê…ôm…ônt /; / Ààvi…ôm…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàviÀê…ôm…ônt /; / ÀàviÀê.…™.m…ônt / (BrE)
showing strong, forceful, or intense emotion or feeling

Examples

A vehement anger
His vehement anger startled everyone in the room.
She made a vehement protest against the unfair decision.
The senator spoke in a vehement tone during the debate.
His vehement objection to the plan surprised everyone in the room.
She gave a vehement denial when accused of the mistake.
The debate was marked by vehement disagreements between the two sides.

Synonyms

  • impassioned
    5.8 2.10/m
    impassioned shows strong heartfelt feeling in speech or writing, while vehement often feels more forceful and urgent.
  • passionate
    2.8 20/m
    passionate comes from deep love or strong interest in something, while vehement expresses strong, forceful emotion often in protests.
  • ardent
    6.3 3.70/m
    ardent shows warm, eager enthusiasm for people or ideas, while vehement shows stronger, harsher emotion and force.
  • intense
    3.7 41/m
    intense means very strong or deep feeling in any situation, while vehement adds forceful, often loud expression to that feeling.
  • emphatic
    5.2 2.21/m
    emphatic gives strong clear emphasis in speech or writing, while vehement shows strong feeling with power and heat.
  • forceful
    4.3 4.57/m
    forceful is strong in action or opinion and may lack emotion, while vehement carries strong, emotional intensity and force.
