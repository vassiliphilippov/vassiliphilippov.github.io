protrude

verb
stick out; extend beyond
5.7
Pronunciation: / proʊˈtruːd / (AmE), / prəˈtruːd / (BrE)
to stick out or extend beyond a surface or the usual limit

Examples

The jaw protrudes
His lower jaw protrudes slightly, giving him a distinctive look.
The corners of the metal sheet protrude dangerously from the wall.
You could see a bone protruding through the cut in his leg.
A broken branch began to protrude from the thick hedge.
His bottom lip would protrude whenever he was upset.
The mountain appeared to protrude sharply above the valley mist.
A nail was protruding dangerously from the wooden board.
His lower jaw seemed to protrude slightly when he spoke.
Sharp rocks protrude from the surface of the cliff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jut - to stick out, especially above or beyond the surface
  • synonym: project - to extend outward beyond a surface
  • antonym: recede - to move back from a previous position; to retreat
  • synonym: stick out - to be prominent or extend outward
