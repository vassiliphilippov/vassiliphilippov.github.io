quasiparticle

noun
quantum entity behaving like a particle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːziˌpɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkweɪzɪˌpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
In physics, a quantum entity in a material, such as a crystal lattice, that behaves in some respects like a particle, having properties such as position and momentum.

Examples

A quasiparticle in physics
Excitons are an example of a quasiparticle formed by an electron and a hole bound together.
In solid-state physics, the electron is sometimes treated as a quasiparticle within the crystal.
The study of quasiparticle interactions is essential for understanding superconductivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: particle - a minute portion of matter
