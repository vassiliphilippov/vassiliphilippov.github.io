piciform bird

noun
nonpasserine climbing bird group (woodpeckers, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪsɪˌfɔrm bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈpɪsɪˌfɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
Any of a group of nonpasserine insectivorous birds, usually with strong bills for boring wood, including woodpeckers and their relatives.

Examples

Piciform birds like woodpeckers
Many piciform birds, such as woodpeckers, have specialized feet for gripping bark.
Scientists studied several piciform birds to understand their adaptation to climbing.
The piciform bird drilled a hole in the old oak tree to find insects.
