crowd

verb
cause people or animals to move together
9.9 1.5
Pronunciation: / kraʊd / (AmE), / kraʊd / (BrE)
to gather together or fill a space in large numbers, often making it crowded

Examples

To crowd a room
Security tried to crowd the protesters into one corner while the event continued.
The organizers attempted to crowd the goats into a pen for the evening.
The teachers had to crowd all the students into the gym during the sudden storm.
Children crowded through the gate as soon as the playground opened.
Hundreds of fans crowded around the stage to catch a glimpse of the singer.
Shoppers crowded into stores on Black Friday looking for the best deals.
As the doors opened, commuters crowded into the subway car.
Hundreds of fans crowded the stadium during the championship game.
Tourists crowded the city square to watch the parade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assemble - to come together as a group, especially for a common purpose
  • antonym: disperse - to break up and leave a crowd or gathering
  • antonym: empty - to remove all contents from; make unoccupied
  • synonym: herd - to make people or animals move together as a group
  • synonym: jam - to fill tightly so that movement is difficult
  • synonym: pack - to fill tightly; cram
