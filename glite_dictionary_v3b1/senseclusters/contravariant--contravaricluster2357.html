contravariant

adjective
reversing direction
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntrəˈvɛriənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈveəriənt / (BrE)
describing a mathematical object or function that reverses the direction of mappings or transformations between structures

Examples

Contravariant functor
In mathematics, a contravariant functor reverses the direction of morphisms between categories.
The concept of contravariant vectors is important in differential geometry.
A contravariant transformation changes the direction of mapping compared to a covariant one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: covariant - changing in the same direction as another object or function
