Fighting French

noun
French resistance movement in WWII
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪtɪŋ ˌfrɛntʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtɪŋ ˌfrɛntʃ / (BrE)
the section of the French forces, led by Charles de Gaulle during World War II, who fought for the liberation of France from German occupation and for the restoration of the republic

Examples

Fighting French forces
Charles de Gaulle became the symbolic leader of the Fighting French throughout World War II.
Many members of the Fighting French risked their lives to undermine German control in occupied France.
The Fighting French played a crucial role in coordinating resistance efforts during World War II.
