emotivism

noun
emotional persuasion
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊtɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊtɪˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of using emotionally charged language or appeals to influence people's feelings or decisions instead of logical arguments

Examples

Political emotivism
The politician's speech was full of emotivism rather than clear explanations of his policies.
Critics accused the advertisement of relying on emotivism to manipulate consumers' feelings.
In academic writing, emotivism is generally discouraged in favor of logical reasoning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: logic - reasoning conducted according to strict principles of validity
  • synonym: rhetoric - the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing
