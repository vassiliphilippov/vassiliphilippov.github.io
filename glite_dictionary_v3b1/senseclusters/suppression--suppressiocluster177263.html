suppression

noun
act of forcefully preventing or stopping
7.5
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / səˈprɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of preventing something from being expressed, known, or practiced, especially by force or authority

Examples

Suppression of protests
Harsh suppression of protests often leads to further unrest among the population.
The suppression of cultural traditions can have lasting negative effects on a community.
The government ordered the suppression of the radical newspaper after it published dissenting opinions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encouragement - the act of giving support or confidence to something
  • synonym: repression - the action of subduing something or someone by force
