worse for wear

adjective
in poor condition or feeling tired
6.8 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / w…ùs f…ôr w…õr / (AmE), / w…úÀês f…ô w…õ…ô / (BrE)
in a poor condition or feeling tired and worn out, especially after use, effort, or an event

Examples

worse for wear after work
After staying up all night working, he looked worse for wear the next morning.
She returned from the marathon feeling worse for wear but proud of herself.
Their tent was a bit worse for wear after the thunderstorm, but still usable.

Synonyms

  • worn-out
    2.4 0.01/m
    worn-out is completely unusable or exhausted after long use, while worse for wear means in poor condition after use or effort.
  • worn
    2.8 0.16/m
    worn is visibly tired or damaged from use, while worse for wear implies being in poor shape or feeling tired after an event.
  • fatigued
    4.1 0.86/m
    fatigued is mentally or physically very tired after effort, while worse for wear often describes things or people left in poor condition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - feeling or looking healthy, energetic, and in good condition
  • synonym: run-down - in poor condition due to neglect or overuse
