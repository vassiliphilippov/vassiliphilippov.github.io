torus

noun
convex molding at base of a column
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔr.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A large, convex molding, typically semicircular in profile, that commonly forms the lowest part of the base of a classical column.

Examples

Column torus
Restoration work revealed the original torus at the foot of each marble column.
The ancient temple's columns were adorned with a distinctive torus at their bases.
The architect recommended a simple torus molding to enhance the column's appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: molding - decorative strip used in architecture
