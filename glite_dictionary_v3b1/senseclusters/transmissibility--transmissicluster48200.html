transmissibility

noun
degree of transmission
8.4
Pronunciation: / trænzˌmɪsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / trænzˌmɪsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the degree to which vibrations, sound, or energy are passed through a material or system

Examples

Transmissibility of vibrations
Engineers measured the transmissibility of the new material to determine how much vibration it would allow.
Reducing the transmissibility of sound is important in designing quiet office spaces.
The transmissibility of the bridge structure was tested to ensure safety during earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insulation - the property of preventing the passage of energy or sound
  • synonym: permeability - the ability of a material to allow substances to pass through it
