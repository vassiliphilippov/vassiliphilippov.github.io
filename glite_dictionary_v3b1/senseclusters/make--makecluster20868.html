make

verb
create or manufacture something
1.0
Pronunciation: / meɪk / (AmE), / meɪk / (BrE)
to create, build, or produce something by putting parts or materials together, or by artistic or mental effort

Examples

To make a cake
The new company hopes to make affordable solar panels for rural areas.
They make all their products by hand in Italy.
This factory makes thousands of shoes every day.
It takes time for a committee to make effective policies.
She was proud to have helped make a change in the law.
The government plans to make new regulations to improve air quality.
He used old bricks to make a garden path.
She likes to make bread from scratch on weekends.
The children worked together to make a model airplane for the competition.
