pteropod

noun
marine mollusk
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛrəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈtɛrəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a small, free-swimming marine mollusk with wing-like extensions used for movement

Examples

Small pteropods swimming
The scientist collected several pteropods to study their response to changing ocean conditions.
Tiny pteropods drift through the open sea, using their wing-like appendages to swim.
Many fish rely on pteropods as a vital part of their diet in the marine ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mollusk - an invertebrate animal with a soft body, often with a shell
  • synonym: sea butterfly - a common name for certain pteropods with delicate, wing-like bodies
