wound

verb
to injure by cutting or breaking skin
2.3 24/m
Pronunciation: / wund / (AmE), / wuÀênd / (BrE)
to injure someone physically, especially by cutting or breaking the skin

Examples

To wound a soldier
He was badly wounded in the accident but managed to survive.
The sharp edge of the metal wounded her arm during the fall.
Words can wound as deeply as physical blows.

Synonyms

  • lacerate
    7.5 0.34/m
    lacerate means to cut or tear flesh roughly, while wound means to injure someone, often by cutting or breaking the skin.
  • injure
    1.0 30/m
    injure means to harm or damage the body in many ways, while wound means to hurt someone specifically by cutting skin.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heal - to become healthy again; to recover from a wound or injury
  • synonym: injure - to harm or damage someone physically
