Carpinus betulus

noun
European hornbeam tree species
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈpaɪnəs bɪˈtjuːləs / (AmE), / kɑːˈpaɪnəs bɪˈtjuːləs / (BrE)
a medium-sized deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, with smooth grey bark and beech-like leaves that turn yellow-orange in autumn; also known as the European hornbeam

Examples

Carpinus betulus tree
Botanists identified the old tree as a mature specimen of Carpinus betulus.
In autumn, the leaves of Carpinus betulus turn brilliant shades of yellow and orange.
The Carpinus betulus is often planted in city parks for its attractive foliage and tolerance of pruning.
