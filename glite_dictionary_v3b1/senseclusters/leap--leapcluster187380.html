leap

verb
move quickly from one state or topic to another
3.2
Pronunciation: / liːp / (AmE), / liːp / (BrE)
to pass abruptly from one state or topic to another without a gradual transition

Examples

To leap into action
After years of hard work, she was able to leap into a leading role in the industry.
He tends to leap from one idea to another during conversations, making it hard to follow his point.
The company hopes to leap from obscurity into the market spotlight with its new product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jump - to make a sudden transition from one thing to another
