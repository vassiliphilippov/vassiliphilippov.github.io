recolonization

noun
taking control again
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkɑːlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkɒlənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking control of a place or territory again after it has become independent.

Examples

Political recolonization
After years of independence, the country feared recolonization by its former rulers.
Many historians debate whether economic influence can be considered a form of recolonization.
The movement was started to resist any attempts at recolonization by foreign powers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonization - the act of establishing control over a foreign territory
  • antonym: decolonization - the process of a colony gaining independence
