thermal emission

noun
release of electrons due to heat
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜrməl ɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməl ɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the release of electrons from a material as a result of high temperature

Examples

Thermal emission of electrons
In vacuum tubes, thermal emission provides a source of free electrons for conduction.
Scientists measured thermal emission to analyze the efficiency of the cathode material.
The thermal emission from the heated metal was detected using sensitive instruments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermionic emission - release of electrons from heated materials
