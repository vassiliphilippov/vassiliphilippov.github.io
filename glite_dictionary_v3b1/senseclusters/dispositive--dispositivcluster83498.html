dispositive

adjective
deciding or settling
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpɑːzətɪv / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
having the power to decide or settle an issue, especially in law

Examples

A dispositive order
The court issued a dispositive order that resolved all remaining disputes.
A dispositive motion can end a lawsuit before it goes to trial.
The lawyer argued that the evidence was dispositive and left no room for doubt.
The judge issued a dispositive ruling that settled the dispute between the two companies.
A dispositive provision in the contract determined who would receive the property after the sale.
Her lawyer explained that the document was not dispositive and would not affect the inheritance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decisive - having the power to decide something
  • antonym: nonbinding - not legally or formally binding or decisive
