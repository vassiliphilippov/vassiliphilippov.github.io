audacious

adjective
bold; unrestrained by convention
Pronunciation: / ɔˈdeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪʃəs / (BrE)
showing a willingness to take bold risks or act in a daring, unconventional way

Examples

An audacious move
He made an audacious move by questioning the established procedures during the meeting.
Her audacious performance challenged the norms of classical theatre.
The audacious plan surprised even the most seasoned executives at the company.
He presented an audacious plan to revolutionize public transportation.
The young entrepreneur made an audacious decision to launch her startup in a crowded market.
Their audacious climb of the remote mountain impressed even the most seasoned adventurers.
His audacious spirit inspired others to take risks and face their fears.
She made an audacious decision to travel alone through dangerous regions.
The audacious climber reached the summit despite the terrible weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brazen - bold and without shame
  • antonym: cautious - careful to avoid risk or danger
  • synonym: daring - willing to take risks; bold
  • synonym: fearless - not afraid; without fear
  • synonym: intrepid - very brave; fearless
  • antonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence
