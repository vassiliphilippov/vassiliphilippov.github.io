trijet

noun
three-engine jet aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌdʒɛt / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌdʒɛt / (BrE)
a jet aircraft that is powered by three jet engines

Examples

A trijet aircraft
The DC-10 is a famous trijet used for long-distance flights.
Engineers discussed the advantages of a trijet over a twin-engine design.
Many airlines replaced their old trijets with more fuel-efficient aircraft.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: quadjet - a jet aircraft powered by four jet engines
  • degree lower: twinjet - a jet aircraft powered by two jet engines
