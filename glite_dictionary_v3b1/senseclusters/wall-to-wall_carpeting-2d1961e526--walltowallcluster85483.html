wall-to-wall carpeting

noun
carpet covering an entire floor
Pronunciation: / ˌwɔl tə ˌwɔl ˈkɑr.pə.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌwɔːl tə ˌwɔːl ˈkɑː.pɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
A carpet designed to cover the entire floor of a room, fitting closely against the walls.

Examples

Wall-to-wall carpeting in rooms
Many apartments from the 1970s still feature wall-to-wall carpeting in every room.
Some people prefer hardwood floors instead of wall-to-wall carpeting for easier cleaning.
The living room looked cozier after we installed wall-to-wall carpeting.
