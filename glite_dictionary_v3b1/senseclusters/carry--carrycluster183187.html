carry

verb
hold or contain within
2.2 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.riː/ /; / /ˈkæ.rɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.rɪ/ (BrE)
to have, contain, or stock something; to have something available, in stock, or on board

Examples

Stores carry products
Many processed foods carry warnings about allergens within the packaging.
Their canteens carried just enough water to get through the day.
This jar carries a sweet-smelling oil that is used for rituals.
Do you carry this model in blue?
Many bookstores carry a wide selection of travel guides.
This store does not carry organic produce.
Many old fishing boats carried a single triangular sail.
The yacht can carry two large sails at once.
This dinghy does not carry a mast, making it less suited for racing.
