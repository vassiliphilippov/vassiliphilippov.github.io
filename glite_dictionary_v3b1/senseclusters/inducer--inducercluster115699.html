inducer

noun
agent activating specific genes
8.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdusər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a substance or agent that initiates or increases the expression of specific genes, especially in a biological system

Examples

A chemical inducer
Scientists added a chemical inducer to trigger gene expression in the experimental cells.
Some drugs act as an inducer of detoxifying enzymes in the liver.
The presence of an inducer in the medium caused the bacteria to produce the required enzyme.
