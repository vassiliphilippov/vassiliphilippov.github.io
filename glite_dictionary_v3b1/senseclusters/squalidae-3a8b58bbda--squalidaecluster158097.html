Squalidae

noun
a family of spiny dogfish sharks
Pronunciation: / ˈskwæləˌdi / (AmE), / ˈskwalɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of dogfish sharks characterized by a spine in each dorsal fin.

Examples

Squalidae shark family
Many species in the Squalidae family are found in temperate and subarctic waters.
Marine biologists study Squalidae to monitor changes in shark populations.
The Squalidae are often referred to as spiny dogfish due to the dorsal spines.
