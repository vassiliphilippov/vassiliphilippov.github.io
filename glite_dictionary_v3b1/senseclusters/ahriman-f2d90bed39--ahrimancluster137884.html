Ahriman

proper noun
evil spirit in Zoroastrianism
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrɪmæn / (AmE), / ˈɑːrɪmən / (BrE)
In Zoroastrianism, the spirit of evil and darkness, regarded as the chief adversary of Ormazd (Ahura Mazda).

Examples

Ahriman spirit of evil
In Zoroastrian mythology, Ahriman is depicted as the source of all evil and chaos.
Scholars have studied the symbolism associated with Ahriman in ancient Persian texts.
The struggle between Ahriman and Ormazd represents the eternal conflict between good and evil.

Related Terms
