impermanence

noun
the state of being temporary; not lasting

The state or quality of not lasting forever; the fact of being temporary or subject to change.

Examples

The impermanence of life
Buddhist philosophy teaches that impermanence is a fundamental characteristic of existence.
The impermanence of youth is a theme often explored in poetry and art.
The crumbling buildings reminded them of the impermanence of all human creations.

Synonyms

  • transience
    8.1 0.62/m
    transience describes things that last only a short time, while impermanence names the broader fact they do not last forever.
  • temporariness
    5.5 0.04/m
    temporariness means something exists for a limited period, while impermanence emphasizes the general state of not lasting forever.
  • transitoriness
    9.2 0.04/m
    transitoriness suggests a natural, often sad ending to things, while impermanence simply states they do not last forever.
  • ephemerality
    8.9 0.10/m
    ephemerality means lasting for a very short, fleeting time, while impermanence covers any temporary or changing state.
