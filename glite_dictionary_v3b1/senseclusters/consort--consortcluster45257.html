consort

verb
go together harmoniously
9.0
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɔrt / (AmE), / kənˈsɔːt / (BrE)
to be in harmony or agreement; to go together, especially in a way that is appropriate or harmonious

Examples

To consort well
Modern architecture often consorts uneasily with the historical landscape.
The flavors in this dish consort beautifully to create a balanced taste.
These colors do not consort well in a single outfit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clash - to be in direct opposition or conflict, especially unpleasantly
  • synonym: harmonize - to form a pleasing or consistent whole
