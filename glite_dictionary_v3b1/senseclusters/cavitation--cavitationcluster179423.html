cavitation

noun
bubble formation in liquid
Pronunciation: / ˌkævɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkævɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation of bubbles or empty spaces in a liquid, often causing damage to equipment (engineering, physics)

Examples

Cavitation in pumps
The engineer explained that cavitation can damage the blades of a water pump.
High-speed turbines are designed to minimize cavitation and extend their lifespan.
The ship's propeller suffered from cavitation at high speeds.
Engineers studied cavitation in the ship's propeller to prevent damage.
The loud noise from the pump was caused by cavitation in the water flow.
Repeated cavitation can wear down metal surfaces in machinery over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubble - a small pocket of air or gas in a liquid
  • synonym: erosion - the gradual destruction of material by physical forces
  • synonym: implosion - the sudden collapse of something inward, often used for bubbles
