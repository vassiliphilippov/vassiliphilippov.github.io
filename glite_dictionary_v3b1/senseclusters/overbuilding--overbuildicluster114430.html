overbuilding

noun
excessive construction
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of constructing more buildings or facilities than are needed in an area, often leading to surplus or unnecessary duplication

Examples

Urban overbuilding
Overbuilding in the city center has led to many empty office spaces.
The government warned that overbuilding could harm the local housing market.
After years of overbuilding, developers are now facing financial losses due to low demand.
The city warned that overbuilding of shopping centers could harm local businesses.
Telecommunications companies are often criticized for overbuilding networks in already connected neighborhoods.
Experts say overbuilding can lead to wasted resources and higher costs for everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overdevelopment - the process of developing land excessively or beyond what is needed
  • synonym: redundancy - the state of being unnecessary due to duplication
  • antonym: underbuilding - the act of constructing fewer buildings than are needed in an area
