interruption

noun
act of stopping or breaking continuity
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən / (BrE)
an act, event, or period when something is stopped or delayed, breaking its continuity

Examples

A interruption of work
He grew frustrated with the repeated interruption of his work by phone calls.
The unexpected interruption in the power supply lasted for over two hours.
We apologize for the interruption and will resume our program shortly.
A fire alarm caused an unexpected interruption during the final exam.
The movie was enjoyable except for the constant interruption by phone calls.
We apologized for the interruption and resumed the meeting as soon as possible.
The power outage caused a brief interruption in the meeting.
There was an interruption in the broadcast due to technical difficulties.
Without any interruption, the ceremony proceeded smoothly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on or resuming something without interruption
  • synonym: disruption - disturbance or interruption of a process or activity
  • synonym: pause - a temporary stop in an activity or speech
