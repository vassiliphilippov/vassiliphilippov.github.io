metallography

noun
study of metal structure
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈlɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈlɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the structure and properties of metals, often using microscopes

Examples

Metallography study
She specialized in metallography to analyze the grain structure of steel.
Modern metallography uses advanced microscopes to reveal tiny defects in metal samples.
A course in metallography is required for students studying materials science.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metallurgy - the science and technology of metals
  • synonym: petrography - the study of the composition and structure of rocks
