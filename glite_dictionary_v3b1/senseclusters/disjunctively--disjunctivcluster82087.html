disjunctively

adverb
using 'or' alternatives
8.7
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈdʒʌŋk.tɪv.li/ /; / /də.ˈdʒʌŋk.tɪv.li/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈdʒʌŋk.tɪv.li/ /; / /də.ˈdʒʌŋk.tɪv.li/ (BrE)
in a way that expresses alternatives or separates options, especially using 'or' in logic, grammar, or law

Examples

Options stated disjunctively
The options were presented disjunctively, so you could choose either one or the other.
In logic, statements can be combined disjunctively to show that at least one must be true.

The judge explained the law disjunctively, making it clear that only one condition needed to be met.
The survey questions were phrased disjunctively to ensure respondents chose only one answer.
You can interpret the instructions disjunctively, treating each step as a separate option.
