plantlike flagellate

noun
unicellular organism similar to algae
Pronunciation: / ˈplæntˌlaɪk ˈflædʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈplɑːntˌlaɪk ˈflædʒɪlət / (BrE)
A unicellular organism that possesses many features typical of algae, such as the ability to photosynthesize and the presence of flagella.

Examples

A plantlike flagellate cell
Certain plantlike flagellates can survive in both freshwater and marine environments.
Researchers observed that plantlike flagellates play an important role in aquatic ecosystems.
The study focused on the movement of plantlike flagellates in response to light.
