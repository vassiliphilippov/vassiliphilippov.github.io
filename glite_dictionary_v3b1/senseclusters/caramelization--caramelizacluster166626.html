caramelization

noun
browning of sugars
Pronunciation: / ˌkærəməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkærəməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of heating sugar or foods containing sugar until they turn brown and develop a rich flavor

Examples

Slow caramelization process
The onions developed a sweet flavor through slow caramelization in the pan.
Proper caramelization gives crème brûlée its signature golden crust.
The chef explained that caramelization enhances both the color and taste of roasted vegetables.
The caramelization of sugar gives crème brûlée its signature golden crust.
Slow cooking allows the caramelization of onions, making them sweet and flavorful.
Proper caramelization is essential for achieving the best taste in many desserts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: browning - the process of turning brown when heated
  • antonym: burning - to become blackened or charred from excessive heat
