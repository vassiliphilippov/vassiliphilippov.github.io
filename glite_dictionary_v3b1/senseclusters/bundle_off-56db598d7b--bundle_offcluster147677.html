bundle off

verb
send away hastily or abruptly
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌndl ɔf /; / ˈbʌndəl ɔf / (AmE), / ˈbʌndl ɒf / (BrE)
to send someone or something away quickly and without much ceremony

Examples

To bundle off quickly
After the meeting, the manager bundled off the interns without much ceremony.
His parents decided to bundle off the kids to their grandparents for the weekend.
They bundled off the guests as soon as the party was over.
She bundled off the kids to bed right after dinner.
The company bundled off the outdated equipment to a recycling center.
We decided to bundle off the donation boxes before the holiday rush.

Related Terms

  • synonym: send off - to cause someone to leave a place, typically abruptly
  • antonym: welcome - to receive someone warmly
