garnish with

verb
decorate food with another item
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrnɪʃ wɪθ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃ wɪð / (BrE)
to decorate or enhance (a dish) by adding a small amount of food on top or around it

Examples

To garnish with herbs
For a festive touch, you can garnish with orange zest and a cinnamon stick.
She likes to garnish with fresh herbs just before serving the soup.
The chef decided to garnish with edible flowers to make the plate more appealing.
