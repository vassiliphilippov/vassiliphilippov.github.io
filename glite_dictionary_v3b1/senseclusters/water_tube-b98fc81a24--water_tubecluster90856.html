water tube

noun
tube in a boiler carrying water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə tjuːb / (BrE)
a tube in a boiler through which water circulates to be heated and produce steam

Examples

A water tube in boiler
Modern boilers often use water tubes to improve efficiency and safety.
The engineer inspected each water tube for signs of corrosion.
The failure of a single water tube can cause a dangerous drop in pressure.
