Jesus Christ

proper noun
central figure of Christianity
3.0 11/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íiÀêz…ôs Ààkra…™st / (AmE), / Ààd íiÀêz…ôs Ààkra…™st / (BrE)
The central figure of Christianity, believed by Christians to be the Son of God and whose teachings form the foundation of the Christian faith.

Examples

Jesus Christ at Christmas
Many people around the world celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ at Christmas.
She grew up learning about Jesus Christ and the stories of the New Testament.
The teachings of Jesus Christ have influenced Western philosophy and culture for centuries.

Synonyms

  • Jesus
    1.0 103/m
    Jesus is the common name used in everyday speech, while Jesus Christ is the formal religious title stressing his divine role.
  • Christ
    3.5 86/m

  • Jesus of Nazareth
    5.1 0.36/m
    Jesus of Nazareth is the historical name used in life stories, while Jesus Christ is the faith title used by believers.
  • the Nazarene
    8.0 0.06/m
    the Nazarene is an older title pointing to his home town, while Jesus Christ emphasises his role as Son of God.
