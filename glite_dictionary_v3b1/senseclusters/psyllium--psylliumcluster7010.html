psyllium

noun
Mediterranean plant used as a laxative
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪliəm / (BrE)
A plantain native to Mediterranean regions, whose seeds swell and form a gel when moist, commonly used as a mild laxative.

Examples

psyllium seed husks
Adding psyllium to your diet may help improve digestive health.
Many fiber supplements contain psyllium because of its high soluble fiber content.
The doctor recommended psyllium husk as a natural way to relieve constipation.
