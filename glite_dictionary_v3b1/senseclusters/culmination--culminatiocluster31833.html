culmination

noun
highest celestial point above horizon (astronomy)

Pronunciation: /kʌl.mə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kʌl.mɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kʌl.mɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kʌl.mə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(astronomy) The moment when a celestial body reaches its highest point above the horizon as observed from a specific place.

Examples

Planet culmination
Astronomers often time their observations to coincide with the culmination of a planet.
Jupiter’s culmination occurs earlier each night as the seasons change.
The star reached its culmination just after midnight, appearing highest in the sky.
