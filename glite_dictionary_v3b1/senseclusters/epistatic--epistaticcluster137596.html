epistatic

adjective
gene-masking
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈstætɪk / (BrE)
describing a gene that affects or masks the expression of another gene

Examples

Epistatic gene effect
An epistatic gene can alter the expected traits in offspring.
Researchers discovered that the epistatic interaction between two genes changed the plant's color.
The study focused on how epistatic effects influence genetic diseases in humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominant - describing a gene that determines a trait even if only one copy is present
  • antonym: recessive - describing a gene whose effects are masked by a dominant gene
