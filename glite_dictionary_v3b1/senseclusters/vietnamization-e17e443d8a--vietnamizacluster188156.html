Vietnamization

noun
transfer of military control
9.3
Pronunciation: / viˌɛtnəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / viˌɛtnəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the policy during the Vietnam War of transferring military responsibility from U.S. forces to South Vietnamese forces, reducing foreign military involvement

Examples

Vietnamization of the war
The government announced a policy of Vietnamization to gradually withdraw its troops from the region.
Historians debate whether Vietnamization was effective in ending foreign involvement in the conflict.
Many commentators warned that the plan amounted to a broader Vietnamization of foreign wars in the region.
The policy of Vietnamization aimed to reduce American troop involvement in the Vietnam War.
Many historians debate whether Vietnamization was effective in achieving its goals.
As Vietnamization progressed, more South Vietnamese soldiers took over combat operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intervention - the act of becoming involved in a conflict or situation
  • synonym: localization - the process of transferring control to local authorities
