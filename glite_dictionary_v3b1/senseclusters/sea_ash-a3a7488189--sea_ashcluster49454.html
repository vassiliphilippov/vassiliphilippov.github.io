sea ash

noun
aromatic shrub or tree native to eastern North America
Pronunciation: / si ˈæʃ / (AmE), / siː ˈaʃ / (BrE)
A small deciduous aromatic shrub or tree with spiny branches and yellowish flowers, native to eastern North America.

Examples

The sea ash tree
Botanists studied the spiny branches of the sea ash during their field trip.
In spring, the sea ash produces clusters of yellowish flowers.
The sea ash thrives in sandy soils along the eastern coast of North America.
