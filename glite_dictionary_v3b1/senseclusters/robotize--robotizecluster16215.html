robotize

verb
to make mechanical
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊbəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrəʊbətaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or a group behave in a mechanical, emotionless, or automatic way, like a robot

Examples

To robotize the staff
The strict routine began to robotize the workers, leaving them feeling disconnected from their tasks.
Some fear that too much technology in schools could robotize the learning experience for children.
The company tried not to robotize its employees despite introducing new automated systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automatize - to make automatic or routine
  • synonym: dehumanize - to remove human qualities or individuality
  • antonym: humanize - to make more human or humane
