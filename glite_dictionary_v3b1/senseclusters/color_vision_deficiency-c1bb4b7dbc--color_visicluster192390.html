color vision deficiency

noun
inability to distinguish certain colors
7.6 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌl.ər ˈvɪʒ.ən dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː/ /; / /ˈkɔːl.ər ˈvɪʒ.ən dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː/ (AmE), /ˈkʌl.ə ˈvɪʒ.ən dɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː/ (BrE)
a genetic or acquired condition in which a person has difficulty distinguishing between certain colors or hues, typically due to deficiencies in the eye's color-detecting cells; commonly known as colour blindness

Examples

Color vision deficiency test
A routine eye exam can detect color vision deficiency in children before it affects their learning.
Many traffic signals are designed with shapes to help those with color vision deficiency.
People with color vision deficiency may find it difficult to distinguish between reds and greens.
He was unaware of his colour vision deficiency until he took a routine eye test.
People with colour vision deficiency may have difficulty distinguishing between red and green traffic lights.
Schools sometimes provide special resources for students diagnosed with colour vision deficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color blindness - the inability or decreased ability to see color or perceive color differences
