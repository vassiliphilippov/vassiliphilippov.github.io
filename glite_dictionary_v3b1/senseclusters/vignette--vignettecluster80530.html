vignette

verb
to darken image edges
Pronunciation: / vɪnˈjɛt / (AmE), / vɪnˈjɛt / (BrE)
to apply a visual effect to an image that darkens or softens its edges for artistic emphasis

Examples

To vignette a photo
The photographer used software for vignetting to draw attention to the subject in the center.
By vignetting the corners, she gave the old family photo a nostalgic feel.
Many apps offer easy tools for vignetting your pictures to create a dramatic effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - to make the edges or details less clear
  • antonym: highlight - to make part of an image stand out more clearly
