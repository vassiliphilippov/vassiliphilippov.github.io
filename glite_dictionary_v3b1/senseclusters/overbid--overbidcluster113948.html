overbid

verb
bid more than can be won or achieved
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbɪd /; / ˌoʊvɚˈbɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɪd / (BrE)
to offer a bid that is higher than previous bids or higher than is reasonable, especially in auctions or card games

Examples

To overbid at auction
At the auction, several bidders overbid, driving the price above the item's value.
He overbid during the bridge game and ended up losing more tricks than he could win.
She regretted overbidding after realizing her hand was too weak to win so many tricks.
If you overbid at the auction, you might regret your purchase later.
She realized too late that she had overbid for the painting and paid twice its value.
They tried not to overbid during the heated negotiation for the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outbid - to offer more than another person in a bidding process
  • antonym: underbid - to make a lower bid than an opponent or than expected
