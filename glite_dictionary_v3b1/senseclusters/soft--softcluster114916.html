soft

adjective
not based on strict experimental evidence
4.5 5.9
Pronunciation: /sɑːft/ /; / /sɔːft/ (AmE), /sɒft/ /; / /sɔːft/ (BrE)
(of data or a field of study) based on evidence or methods that are not easily tested or verified experimentally; lacking strict objectivity or experimental rigor.

Examples

Soft evidence
Critics argued that the policy was based on soft evidence rather than reliable experimental results.
Psychology is often considered a soft science compared to physics or chemistry.
The researchers relied heavily on soft data such as interviews and personal narratives.
