frenulum

noun
moth wing bristle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɛn.jə.ləm / (AmE), / ˈfrɛn.jʊ.ləm / (BrE)
a bristle or group of bristles on the hindwing of certain moths that connects to the forewing to couple the wings during flight

Examples

Moth wing frenulum
The scientist carefully observed the frenulum to understand how the moth's wings were connected.
Without a functioning frenulum, the moth struggled to fly properly.
The entomology textbook included a diagram showing the frenulum on the hindwing of a moth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retinaculum - a structure that holds an organ or part in place, especially in insects
