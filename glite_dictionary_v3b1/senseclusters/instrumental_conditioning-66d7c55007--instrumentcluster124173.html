instrumental conditioning

noun
learning via rewards or punishments after actions
3.0 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.strə.ˈmen.təl kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən.ɪŋ/ /; / /ɪn.strʊ.ˈmen.təl kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ɪn.strʊ.ˈmen.təl kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən.ɪŋ/ /; / /ɪn.strə.ˈmen.təl kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a type of learning in which a behavior is strengthened or weakened, depending on its consequences, especially through rewards or punishments occurring only after the behavior

Examples

Instrumental conditioning learning
In classrooms, teachers sometimes apply instrumental conditioning by rewarding good behavior with praise or stickers.
Many animal trainers rely on instrumental conditioning to shape desired actions in their trainees.
The psychologist used instrumental conditioning to teach the rats to press a lever for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: operant conditioning - learning in which behavior is controlled by consequences such as reinforcement or punishment
