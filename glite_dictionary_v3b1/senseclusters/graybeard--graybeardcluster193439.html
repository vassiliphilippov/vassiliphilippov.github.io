graybeard

noun
an old man (often with a gray beard)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪˌbɪrd / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌbɪəd / (BrE)
an old man, especially one with a gray beard, often used to imply wisdom or experience

Examples

A wise graybeard
At the meeting, a respected graybeard offered his opinion.
He was a wise graybeard who had seen many generations come and go.
The village graybeard told tales of times long past.
Many saw him as a wise greybeard who offered guidance to the community.
The novel's protagonist seeks advice from an old greybeard in the village.
The respected greybeard shared stories from his youth around the fire.
