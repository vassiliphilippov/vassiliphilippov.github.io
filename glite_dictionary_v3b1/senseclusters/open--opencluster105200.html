open

verb
begin a meeting or event
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən /; / ˈoʊ.pən / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən /; / ˈəʊ.pən / (BrE)
to begin or start an event, activity, or business

Examples

He was asked to open the discussion with a few remarks.

Kasparov opened with a standard King's pawn move.
She opened the debate by presenting the team's main arguments.
The host opened the ceremony with a brief introduction.
She plans to open her own restaurant after graduation.
The new library will officially open next week.
They decided to open a bakery downtown last year.
