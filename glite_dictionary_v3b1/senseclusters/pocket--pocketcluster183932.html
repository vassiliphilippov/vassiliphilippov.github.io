pocket

noun
isolated group or area
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkɪt /; / ˈpɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
a small, isolated group or area of people, activity, or condition, separate from what is around it

Examples

Pocket of resistance
Authorities discovered pockets of opposition scattered throughout the country.
The city still has pockets of poverty despite overall economic growth.
There remain several pockets of infection in remote regions.
Passengers felt a momentary jolt as the aircraft hit a pocket of low pressure.
Pilots are trained to handle sudden drops caused by an unexpected pocket in the atmosphere.
The plane dropped suddenly after entering a pocket of turbulent air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: air pocket - a localised region of turbulent or descending air affecting aircraft
