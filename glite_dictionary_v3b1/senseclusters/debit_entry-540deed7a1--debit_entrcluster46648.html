debit entry

noun
accounting record of an amount owed
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛbɪt ˈɛntri / (AmE), / ˈdɛbɪt ˈɛntri / (BrE)
an entry in a financial account that records an amount owed or a decrease in assets

Examples

A debit entry in accounts
A debit entry increases the balance of an asset account in double-entry bookkeeping.
Each payment made to the supplier was reflected by a debit entry in the account.
The accountant recorded the expense as a debit entry in the company ledger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: credit entry - an accounting entry acknowledging income or money received
