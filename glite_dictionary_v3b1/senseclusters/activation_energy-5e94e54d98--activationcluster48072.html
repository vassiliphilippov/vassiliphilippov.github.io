activation energy

noun

Pronunciation: /æk.tɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən ˈen.ər.dʒiː/ /; / /æk.tə.ˈveɪ.ʃən ˈen.ər.dʒiː/ (AmE), /æk.tɪ.ˈveɪ.ʃən ˈen.ə.dʒɪ/ /; / /æk.tə.ˈveɪ.ʃən ˈen.ə.dʒɪ/ (BrE)
(figurative) the initial effort, barrier, or cost that must be overcome to start a task, change, or process.

Examples

Activation energy for change
Reducing the activation energy for adopting electric cars can boost consumer uptake.
The training program lowered the activation energy for employees to try the new software.
Volunteer groups removed small barriers, reducing the activation energy needed to start community projects.
