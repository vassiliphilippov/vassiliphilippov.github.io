watch

noun
period of surveillance or guard duty
4.2 17/m
Pronunciation: / wÉ‘tÊƒ / (AmE), / wÉ’tÊƒ / (BrE)
a period of time or a group assigned to guard, observe, or stay alert, often as a duty or for a special purpose

Examples

Night watch duty
During the storm, the captain put the crew on watch around the clock.
She took her turn on watch after midnight, scanning the horizon for movement.
The night watch was responsible for keeping the village safe.
A watch was posted at the city gates throughout the night.
During the storm, a watch patrolled the coastline to alert the villagers of any danger.
The military assigned a watch to guard the supplies until morning.
During the night watch, the guards heard suspicious noises near the wall.
He stood his watch at the main gate until sunrise.
Her watch ends at midnight, when she will be relieved by another officer.

Synonyms

  • guard
    1.0 6.24/m
    a guard stands to protect a place by watching and acting, while a watch is a scheduled duty or team assigned to observe.
  • vigil
    6.1 3.99/m
    a vigil is staying awake, often at night to pray or support someone, while a watch is a duty-based period or team on alert.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guard - person who keeps watch; protector
  • antonym: neglect - failure to care for or guard appropriately
  • antonym: off-duty - not currently assigned to work or duty
  • synonym: patrol - an act of going around an area to guard or observe
  • synonym: sentinel - a person or thing that stands guard
  • synonym: shift - a scheduled period of work or duty
  • synonym: vigil - a period of keeping awake, often for watchful attention or prayer
