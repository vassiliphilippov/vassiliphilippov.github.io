cathead

noun
ship's anchor beam
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈkætˌhɛd / (BrE)
a strong horizontal beam on a ship used to support and secure the anchor when it is raised or lowered

Examples

Ship's cathead beam
The sailors carefully lifted the anchor onto the cathead after a long day at sea.
Before docking, the crew made sure the anchor was secure on the cathead.
A wooden cathead extended from the ship's bow to hold the heavy anchor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: windlass - a device for raising and lowering a ship's anchor
