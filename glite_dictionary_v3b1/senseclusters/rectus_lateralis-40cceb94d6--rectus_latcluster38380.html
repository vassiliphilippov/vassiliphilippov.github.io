rectus lateralis

noun
eye muscle moving eyeball outward
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈrek.təs lə.ˈte.rə.lɪs/ (AmE), /ˈrek.təs lə.ˈte.rə.lɪs/ (BrE)
the muscle on the outer side of the eyeball that moves the eye outward.

Examples

The rectus lateralis muscle
Damage to the rectus lateralis can cause the eye to turn inward abnormally.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully avoided the rectus lateralis to prevent double vision.
The ophthalmologist tested the function of my rectus lateralis during the eye exam.
