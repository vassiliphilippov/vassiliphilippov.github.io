capitation

noun
fixed payment per patient
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a payment system in which a provider receives a fixed amount of money per patient, regardless of services provided

Examples

Capitation payment system
Under capitation, doctors receive the same payment no matter how many times a patient visits.
The insurance company adopted capitation to control rising healthcare expenses.
Some clinics prefer capitation because it encourages preventive care and cost efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fee-for-service - a payment model where providers are paid for each service performed
