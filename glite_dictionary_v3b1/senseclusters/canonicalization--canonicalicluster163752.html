canonicalization

noun
making part of a canon
Pronunciation: / kəˌnɑːnɪkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌnænɪkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something part of an official or accepted canon, especially in religion, literature, or culture

Examples

Biblical canonicalization
The canonicalization of certain texts changed the way the literary tradition was taught in schools.
Debates about the canonicalization of new works often reflect broader cultural values.
Religious councils have historically overseen the canonicalization of sacred writings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canonization - the act of officially declaring something as part of a canon
  • antonym: exclusion - the act of leaving something out of a group or category
