retread one's ideas

verb
reuse previous ideas
6.1 6.5
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtriːd wʌnz aɪ.ˈdiː.əz/ /; / /riː.ˈtred wʌnz aɪ.ˈdiː.əz/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtred wʌnz aɪ.ˈdɪ.əz/ /; / /rɪ.ˈtriːd wʌnz aɪ.ˈdɪ.əz/ (BrE)

Examples

To retread one's ideas often
Every year, the politician would retread his ideas in campaign speeches without offering anything new.
Instead of proposing fresh solutions, she kept retreading her ideas from previous presentations.
Writers are often criticized when they retread their ideas instead of exploring new themes.
