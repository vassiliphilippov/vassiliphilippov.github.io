cru

noun
quality vineyard
8.7
Pronunciation: /kruː/ (AmE), /kruː/ (BrE)
a vineyard or wine from a vineyard recognized for quality, especially in French wine classification

Examples

Famous cru wine
The wine from this cru is known for its exceptional flavor and aroma.
He toured several famous crus during his trip to the Burgundy region.
A bottle from a top cru can be quite expensive and highly sought after.
The sommelier recommended a fine cru from Bordeaux to pair with the meal.
Collectors often seek out rare crus from renowned French vineyards.
He was proud to serve a prestigious cru at his wedding reception.

Related Terms

  • synonym: château - a wine estate, especially in France
  • antonym: table wine - ordinary wine not of special quality
  • synonym: vineyard - a plantation of grapevines for winemaking
  • synonym: vintage - wine from a particular year and vineyard
