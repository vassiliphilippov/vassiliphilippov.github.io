encyclopedia

noun
reference work with articles on many topics
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdiə / (AmE), / ɪnˌsʌɪkləˈpiːdɪə /; / ɪnˌsaɪklɪˈpiːdiə / (BrE)
A book or set of books arranged in alphabetical order, containing articles on a wide range of subjects or on one subject, intended for reference.

Examples

A encyclopedia set
Before the Internet, people often used an encyclopedia to find information.
She consulted the encyclopedia to learn more about the Civil War.
The school library has a full set of encyclopedias covering science and history.
He found the answer in a well-known encyclopaedia at the library.
Many families used to own a set of encyclopaedias for reference at home.
The online encyclopaedia covers everything from history to modern science.
