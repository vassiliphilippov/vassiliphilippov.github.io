philosophically

adverb
in a philosophical or calm manner
7.1
Pronunciation: / fɪˌlɑːsəˈfɪkli /; / ˌfɪləˈsɑfɪkli / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒsəˈfɪkəli /; / ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to philosophy or shows calmness, wisdom, or acceptance toward difficult situations

Examples

Think philosophically
Despite the setback, she handled the news philosophically and focused on her next steps.

When he lost the competition, he reacted philosophically rather than with frustration.
He remained philosophically detached during the debate, refusing to get emotional.
She approached the problem philosophically, considering various ethical theories.
The movement is philosophically indebted to Rousseau.
