midstream

adverb
in the middle
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪdˈstriːm / (AmE), / ˌmɪdˈstriːm / (BrE)
in the middle of an action, process, or event, before it is finished

Examples

Change midstream
She realized midstream that she had forgotten an important detail in her presentation.
The project was canceled midstream due to unexpected budget cuts.
He switched careers midstream, surprising everyone who knew him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beginning - the start of something
  • antonym: end - the final part of something
  • synonym: halfway - at or to the middle point
