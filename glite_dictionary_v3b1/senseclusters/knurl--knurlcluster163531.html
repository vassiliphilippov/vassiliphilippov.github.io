knurl

verb
to make ridges
9.7
Pronunciation: / nɜrl / (AmE), / nɜːl / (BrE)
to form or cut a pattern of small ridges or bumps on a surface, usually to improve grip or appearance

Examples

To knurl a handle
The technician knurled the handle to provide a better grip for users.
Before assembling the parts, she knurled the metal rod for added texture.
Many tools are knurled so they do not slip easily from your hand.
The technician spent hours knurling the handles to ensure a better grip.
Special machines are used for knurling metal parts in the factory.
He learned how to knurl the surface of the tool in his workshop.

  • antonym: smooth - to make a surface even and without bumps
