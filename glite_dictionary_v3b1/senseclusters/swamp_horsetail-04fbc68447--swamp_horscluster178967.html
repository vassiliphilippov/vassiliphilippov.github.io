swamp horsetail

noun
a wetland horsetail plant species
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ˈhɔrˌsteɪl / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈhɔːsteɪl / (BrE)
A species of horsetail (Equisetum fluviatile) commonly found in wetland areas of Eurasia and northern North America.

Examples

A swamp horsetail plant
Botanists surveyed a patch of swamp horsetail growing beside the riverbank.
The swamp horsetail thrives in marshes and shallow ponds across northern Europe.
You can identify a swamp horsetail by its tall, hollow stems and spongy texture.
