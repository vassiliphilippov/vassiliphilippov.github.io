hand-holding

noun
close support or guidance
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhænd.hoʊl.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈhænd.həʊl.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of giving close support, guidance, or reassurance to someone, especially to help them do something new or difficult

Examples

IT team hand-holding
The new software is easy to use and requires very little hand-holding from the IT team.
Some students need more hand-holding at the beginning of the course to feel confident.
The manager prefers to avoid hand-holding and encourages employees to solve problems on their own.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guidance - help or advice given to someone
  • antonym: independence - the state of not needing help or support from others
  • synonym: support - assistance or encouragement given to someone
