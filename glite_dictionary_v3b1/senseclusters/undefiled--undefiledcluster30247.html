undefiled

adjective
free from stain or impurity
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪˈfaɪld / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈfaɪld / (BrE)
not spoiled, stained, or made impure; pure and uncorrupted

Examples

An undefiled reputation
She prized her reputation as undefiled by scandal or gossip.
The mountain stream remained undefiled by human activity.
Their love for each other was undefiled by doubt or jealousy.
His poetry is celebrated for its undefiled simplicity and emotional clarity.
She valued an undefiled reputation above all else in her professional life.
The ancient manuscript was written in undefiled Latin, untouched by later influences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defiled - corrupted or made dirty or impure
  • synonym: pure - free from contamination, not mixed with anything else
