Mahican

proper noun
Native American people
Pronunciation: / məˈhiːkən / (AmE), / məˈhiːkən / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people who formerly lived in the Hudson River valley of New York State

Examples

The Mahican tribe
The Mahican established villages along the banks of the Hudson River.
Historians study the culture and traditions of the Mahican people.
Many descendants of the Mahican now live in other parts of the United States.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mohican - a Native American people of the northeastern United States, sometimes confused with Mahican
