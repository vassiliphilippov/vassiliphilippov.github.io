shigella

noun
genus of pathogenic bacteria
9.3
Pronunciation: / ʃɪˈɡɛlə / (AmE), / ʃɪˈɡɛlə / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria, some species of which cause intestinal disease in humans and other warm-blooded animals.

Examples

Shigella bacteria infection
Researchers are studying how shigella spreads in densely populated areas.
Some countries monitor outbreaks of shigella closely due to its impact on public health.
The infection was caused by shigella, a bacterium commonly found in contaminated food or water.
