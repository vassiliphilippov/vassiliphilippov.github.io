Heteroneura

proper noun
moth and butterfly suborder
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈnjʊrə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈnjʊərə / (BrE)
a suborder of moths and butterflies distinguished by having different vein patterns in their forewings and hindwings

Examples

Heteroneura moths and butterflies
Heteroneura includes many species of butterflies found in gardens and forests worldwide.
Scientists study Heteroneura to understand the evolution of wing patterns in insects.
The unique wing veins of Heteroneura help distinguish them from other moths and butterflies.
