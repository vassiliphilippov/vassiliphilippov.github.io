coinherence

noun
Trinitarian mutual indwelling
9.9
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ɪn.ˈhɪ.rəns/ /; / /koʊ.ɪn.ˈhɜː.rəns/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ɪn.ˈhɪə.rəns/ /; / /kəʊ.ɪn.ˈhɜː.rəns/ (BrE)
the mutual indwelling or interpenetration of the persons of the Christian Trinity, especially in theological doctrine

Examples

Trinitarian coinherence
The doctrine of coinherence is central to understanding the unity of the Trinity in Christian theology.
Scholars debated the meaning of coinherence in relation to the relationship between the Father and the Son.
Many medieval texts discuss coinherence as a way to explain divine unity and distinction.
