abrazo

noun
fighters' clinch
Pronunciation: / əˈbrɑːzoʊ / (AmE), / əˈbrɑːzəʊ / (BrE)
a close holding position between fighters, used to control an opponent or prevent strikes, also known as a clinch

Examples

Boxing abrazo
The referee separated the boxers after a long abrazo in the third round.
During the match, the fighters entered an abrazo to avoid further punches.
He used an abrazo to control his opponent and catch his breath.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clinch - a close grappling hold in fighting to prevent attacks
