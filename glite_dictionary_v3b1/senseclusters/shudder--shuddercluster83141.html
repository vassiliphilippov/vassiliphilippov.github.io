noun
brief feeling causing a shake

a sudden, involuntary shaking or tremor, usually caused by fear, cold, or strong emotion

Examples

A cold shudder
A shudder passed through the crowd when the lights suddenly went out.
He felt a shudder of anticipation as the curtain rose.
The memory of the accident sent a shudder down her spine.

She tried to suppress the shudder that crept up her spine at the memory.
The old house gave a shudder as the wind whipped around it.

Synonyms

  • tremble
    2.7 2.65/m
    tremble is a shaking that can be slow or long from fear, cold, or nerves, while shudder is sudden and sharp.
  • shiver
    1.4 4.33/m
    shiver is a repeated, small body shake often from cold, while shudder is a single sudden tremor linked to fear or strong feeling.
  • tremor
    5.3 2.42/m
    tremor is an involuntary shaking often seen in illness or nervousness, while shudder is a sudden fear- or cold-driven shake.
  • quiver
    3.9 0.70/m
    quiver is a slight, gentle trembling usually from emotion or nerves, while shudder is a stronger, sudden whole-body shake.
