shudder

noun
brief feeling causing a shake
3.4 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʃʌd.ər/ (AmE), /ˈʃʌd.ə/ (BrE)
a sudden, involuntary shaking or tremor, usually caused by fear, cold, or strong emotion

Examples

A cold shudder
A shudder passed through the crowd when the lights suddenly went out.
He felt a shudder of anticipation as the curtain rose.
The memory of the accident sent a shudder down her spine.
A sudden shudder ran through him as he stepped into the icy water.
She tried to suppress the shudder that crept up her spine at the memory.
The old house gave a shudder as the wind whipped around it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frisson - a sudden, strong feeling of excitement or fear
  • synonym: shiver - a sudden tremble or shake, often from cold or fear
  • synonym: tremor - an involuntary shaking or trembling movement
