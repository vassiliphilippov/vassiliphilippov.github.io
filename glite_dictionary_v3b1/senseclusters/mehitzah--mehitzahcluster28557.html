mehitzah

noun
synagogue partition
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈxit.sə /; / məˈxit.sɑ / (AmE), / məˈxiːtsə / (BrE)
a partition, especially one used in a synagogue to separate men and women during religious services

Examples

A mehitzah partition
The congregation debated whether to install a mehitzah in the new synagogue.
A wooden mehitzah separated the men's and women's sections during the service.
Some synagogues use a curtain as a mehitzah to divide the seating area.
A wooden mehitzah separated the men's and women's sections in the synagogue.
After the argument, an emotional mehitzah seemed to stand between the friends.
The new policy created a social mehitzah within the community.
