leeboard

noun
boat stabilizing board
Pronunciation: / ˈliːbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈliːbɔːd / (BrE)
a flat board attached to the side of a boat to reduce sideways drift and improve stability, especially when sailing upwind

Examples

Lower the leeboard
The sailor lowered the leeboard to help the boat sail upwind.
Traditional sailing boats often use a leeboard instead of a fixed keel.
By adjusting the leeboard, the crew reduced the boat's sideways movement in strong winds.
The old sailboat was equipped with a single leeboard on its starboard side.
Before heading upwind, the crew made sure the leeboard was properly lowered.
Without a leeboard, the small boat would drift sideways in strong winds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centerboard - a retractable board in the center of a boat to prevent sideways drift
  • synonym: keel - the main structural element running along the bottom of a boat, providing stability
