bushy aster

noun
North American perennial plant with tiny white flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃi ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃi ˈastə / (BrE)
a North American perennial plant with stiff stems, small linear leaves, and numerous tiny white flower heads, native to the eastern United States

Examples

The bushy aster plant
Gardeners often choose bushy aster for its hardy nature and decorative appeal.
The bushy aster blooms in late summer, brightening gardens with its small white flowers.
We spotted a cluster of bushy asters along the woodland trail.
