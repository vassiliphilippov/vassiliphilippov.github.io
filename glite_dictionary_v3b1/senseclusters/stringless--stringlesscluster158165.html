stringless

adjective
without fibrous string
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪŋləs / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋləs / (BrE)
lacking the tough fibrous string along the seam, especially of green beans, making them easier to eat or cook

Examples

Stringless beans
Farmers developed stringless varieties of beans to make preparation easier for cooks.
She prefers stringless snap peas because they are more tender and pleasant to eat.
The market was selling fresh stringless green beans perfect for salads and stir-fries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stringy - containing tough, fibrous strings
