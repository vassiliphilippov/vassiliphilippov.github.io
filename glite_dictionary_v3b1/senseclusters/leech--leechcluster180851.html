leech

verb
to exploit others
7.5 0.33/m
Pronunciation: / liÀêt É / (AmE), / liÀêt É / (BrE)
to take advantage of someone by continuously taking from them without giving anything in return

Examples

To leech money
He was leeching off his rich friends and never paid for anything himself.
Some people try to leech resources from the group without contributing.
She realized her roommate was leeching her food and utilities every month.
He would often leech off his friends by asking them to pay for his meals.
Some people try to leech resources from the community without contributing anything.
She realized her roommate was starting to leech her time and energy.
