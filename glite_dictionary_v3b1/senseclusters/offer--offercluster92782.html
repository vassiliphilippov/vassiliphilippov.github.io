offer

verb
present as worship or devotion
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfər /; / ˈɑfər / (AmE), / ˈɒfə / (BrE)
to present or express something formally, such as prayers, sacrifices, resistance, or a performance

Examples

To offer prayers
Devotees offer flowers at the temple as a sign of respect and faith.
During the ceremony, the priests offered incense to the ancient deity.
She offered a silent prayer for her family before beginning the day.
Despite the odds, the army managed to offer strong resistance to the invaders.
She tried to offer no opposition during the heated argument.
They will offer little defense if they are unprepared.
Our drama club is offering a series of one-act plays this spring.
The local theater will offer a new comedy next season.
They offered an exciting production of 'Hamlet' last year.
