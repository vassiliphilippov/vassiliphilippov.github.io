Tsaritsyn

proper noun
former name of Volgograd, Russia
9.9
Pronunciation: / tsəˈriːtsɪn / (AmE), / tsəˈriːtsɪn / (BrE)
the former name of the city now known as Volgograd, located on the Volga River in Russia; notable as the site of a major World War II battle, then known as Stalingrad.

Examples

City Tsaritsyn name
Few people today remember that Volgograd was once called Tsaritsyn.
Historians often refer to Tsaritsyn when discussing the early industrial growth along the Volga River.
The city of Tsaritsyn was renamed Stalingrad in 1925 and later Volgograd in 1961.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Stalingrad - name of the city after Tsaritsyn and before Volgograd, especially famous for the Battle of Stalingrad
  • synonym: Volgograd - modern name of the city formerly known as Tsaritsyn and Stalingrad
