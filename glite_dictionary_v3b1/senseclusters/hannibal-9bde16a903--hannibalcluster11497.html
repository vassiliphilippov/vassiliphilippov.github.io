Hannibal

proper noun
Carthaginian general of the Second Punic War
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhæn.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈhæn.ɪ.bəl / (BrE)
(c. 247–182 BC) A Carthaginian general celebrated for leading his army, including elephants, across the Alps to fight Rome during the Second Punic War.

Examples

Hannibal crossing Alps
Hannibal famously marched his army and war elephants across the Alps to confront Roman forces.
After years of victories, Hannibal was eventually defeated near Carthage at the Battle of Zama.
Many consider Hannibal one of the greatest military tacticians in history.
