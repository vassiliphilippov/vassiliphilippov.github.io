matzo

noun
unleavened bread eaten during Passover
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑtsoʊ /; / ˈmɑːtsə / (AmE), / ˈmatsəʊ /; / ˈmatsə /; / ˈmætsəʊ / (BrE)
A thin, brittle unleavened flatbread made from flour and water, traditionally eaten by Jews during Passover.
