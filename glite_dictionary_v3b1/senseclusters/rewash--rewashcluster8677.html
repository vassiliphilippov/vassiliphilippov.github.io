rewash

noun
second washing
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwɒʃ / (BrE)
a second or repeated washing of something, especially to remove remaining dirt or stains

Examples

A quick rewash
The dishes needed a rewash because some food was still stuck on them.
After the first cycle, the laundry required a rewash to get rid of the stains.
He gave his car a quick rewash after it rained and left spots.
