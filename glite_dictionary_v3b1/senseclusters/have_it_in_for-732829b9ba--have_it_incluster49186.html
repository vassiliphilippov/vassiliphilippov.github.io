have it in for

verb
to hold a grudge against someone
Pronunciation: / hæv ɪt ɪn fər / (AmE), / hæv ɪt ɪn fə / (BrE)
to be hostile toward someone or hold a grudge, often trying to harm, criticize, or cause trouble for them

Examples

To have it in for someone
Ever since the mistake, it feels like Mark has it in for his coworker.
I can't understand why my supervisor has it in for me these days.
She believes the principal has it in for her, no matter what she does.
Don't take it personally; she has it in for everyone who disagrees with her.
Ever since I reported his mistake, my boss has had it in for me.
It's obvious the teacher has it in for Jake because she never calls on him.
