ravine

verb
to form deep valleys
9.9
Pronunciation: / rəˈviːn / (AmE), / rəˈviːn / (BrE)
to form deep, narrow valleys in something, especially by the action of water or erosion

Examples

Water ravines the land
Over centuries, the river ravined the landscape, leaving behind dramatic cliffs and gorges.
Heavy rains have ravined the hillside, making it difficult for plants to grow.
The once flat plain was ravined by streams that cut through the soft earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carve - to cut or shape something by removing material
  • synonym: erode - to gradually wear away soil, rock, or land
