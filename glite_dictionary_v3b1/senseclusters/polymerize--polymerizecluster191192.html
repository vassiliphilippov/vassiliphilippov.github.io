polymerize

verb
cause to form a polymer (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪməˌraɪz /; / pəˈlɪm.ɚ.aɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪməraɪz /; / pəˈlɪm.ə.raɪz / (BrE)
to form or cause to form a polymer by joining small molecules (monomers) together in a chemical reaction

Examples

To polymerize molecules
Chemists can polymerize different compounds to produce new materials with unique properties.
Heat will polymerize the monomer solution into a solid plastic.
When exposed to light, the resin will polymerize quickly, forming a hard finish.
Certain plastic materials polymerize when exposed to ultraviolet light.
Chemists can polymerize monomers to create new synthetic fibers.
If the solution does not polymerize properly, the final product will be weak.
Some chemicals can polymerise rapidly if exposed to ultraviolet light.
The catalyst is added to polymerise the liquid mixture during manufacturing.
The heat will polymerise the monomers, resulting in a solid plastic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depolymerize - to break down a polymer into its monomers
  • synonym: polymerize - to cause (a compound) to undergo polymerization; American spelling
