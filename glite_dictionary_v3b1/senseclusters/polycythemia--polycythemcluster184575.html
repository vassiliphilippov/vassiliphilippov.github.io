polycythemia

noun
abnormally high red blood cell count (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˌsaɪˈθimiə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪsaɪˈθiːmiə / (BrE)
a disorder in which there is an abnormal increase in the number of red blood cells in the blood, which may cause thickened blood and lead to various health issues

Examples

High polycythemia levels
People with polycythemia may experience headaches, dizziness, or blurred vision.
Regular blood tests are required to monitor polycythemia and prevent complications.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with polycythemia after noticing high red blood cell counts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anemia - a condition marked by a deficiency of red blood cells
