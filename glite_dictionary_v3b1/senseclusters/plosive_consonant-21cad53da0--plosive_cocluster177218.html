plosive consonant

noun
consonant sound made by a sudden release of air
8.7 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈploʊ.sɪv ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt/ /; / /ˈploʊ.zɪv ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt/ (AmE), /ˈpləʊ.sɪv ˈkɒn.sə.nənt/ /; / /ˈpləʊ.zɪv ˈkɒn.sə.nənt/ (BrE)
A consonant sound produced by completely blocking and then suddenly releasing the airflow, such as 'p', 'b', 't', 'd', 'k', and 'g'.

Examples

Plosive consonants sounds
Children often struggle with plosive consonants when first learning to speak.
English has both voiced and voiceless plosive consonants in its sound system.
The teacher demonstrated how to pronounce plosive consonants by blocking air with her lips.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stop consonant - a consonant produced by stopping the airflow; synonym in linguistics
