rarefaction

noun
decrease in density, especially in a medium
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛrəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌreərəˈfækʃən / (BrE)
a decrease in the density of a material, especially in a physical medium such as air or gas

Examples

Rarefaction of air
During the experiment, scientists measured the rarefaction and compression of sound waves.
The rarefaction of air at high altitudes can make it hard to breathe.
The explosion caused a sudden rarefaction followed by a loud shock wave.
