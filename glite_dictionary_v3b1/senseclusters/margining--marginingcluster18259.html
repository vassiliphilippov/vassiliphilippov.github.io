margining

noun
financial risk management
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of managing financial risk by providing collateral to secure trading positions or obligations

Examples

Daily margining
Banks require margining to ensure that clients can cover potential trading losses.
Effective margining helps financial institutions manage their exposure to market risks.
The new regulations introduced stricter rules for margining in derivatives trading.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collateralization - the act of using assets as security for a loan or obligation
  • synonym: hedging - the practice of reducing financial risk by making offsetting investments
