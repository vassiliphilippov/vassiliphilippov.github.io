Crocus sativus

noun
plant yielding saffron spice
Pronunciation: / ˌkroʊ.kəs səˈtaɪ.vəs / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊ.kəs səˈtʌɪ.vəs / (BrE)
A species of crocus with purple or white flowers, whose orange stigmas are harvested as saffron, used as a spice and coloring in food.

Examples

Crocus sativus flower
Crocus sativus is cultivated widely for its costly saffron spice.
Farmers carefully harvest the orange stigmas from Crocus sativus by hand.
The unique flavor of saffron comes exclusively from the Crocus sativus plant.
