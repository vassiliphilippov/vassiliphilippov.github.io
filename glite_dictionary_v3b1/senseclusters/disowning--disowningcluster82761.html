disowning

noun
detaching a background process in a shell
8.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈzoʊ.nɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈsoʊ.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈzəʊ.nɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈsəʊ.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in computing, especially Unix-like shells) the act or operation of removing a background job from the shell's job table so the job will not be terminated when the user logs out.

Examples

Disowning a process
She used disowning to detach the job so it kept running after she logged out.
The tutorial explains disowning background tasks in the shell to prevent their termination.
After starting the process with &, he relied on disowning to avoid losing it when closing the terminal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disown - the shell built-in command that removes a job from the shell's job table so it won't be killed at logout
  • synonym: nohup - a command used to run another command immune to hangups, commonly used to keep processes running after logout
