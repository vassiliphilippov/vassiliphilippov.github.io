merger

noun
joining of companies into one
3.9 9.19/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…úrd í…ôr /; / Ààm…ùÀêd í…ö / (AmE), / Ààm…úÀêd í…ô / (BrE)
the act or process of joining two or more companies, organizations, or entities into one

Examples

Company merger
After the merger, many departments were consolidated to reduce costs.
Shareholders voted to approve the proposed merger between the companies.
The merger of the two banks created one of the largest financial institutions in the country.
Many employees were anxious about the changes brought by the merger.
The merger resulted in a larger, more competitive business.
The companies completed their merger after months of negotiation.

Synonyms

  • amalgamation
    7.5 0.75/m
    amalgamation is the process of joining two or more organizations into one, while merger is the formal combining of companies.
  • consolidation
    5.5 5.25/m
    consolidation often means combining parts to make a stronger, simpler whole, while merger is the act of companies joining.
  • unification
    4.3 4.51/m
    unification is bringing separate groups or states together to form one body, while merger usually refers to companies combining.
  • fusion
    4.1 4.05/m
    fusion describes blending two things into one style or form, often outside business, while merger is a legal joining of companies.
  • uniting
    3.5 0.30/m
    uniting means joining people or things together for any purpose, while merger specifically means companies legally joining into one.

  • antonym: division - the act of separating into parts or groups
