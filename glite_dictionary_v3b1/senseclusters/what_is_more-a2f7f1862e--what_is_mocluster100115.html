what is more

adverb
introduces additional, often stronger, information
4.4
Pronunciation: / wʌt ɪz mɔr / (AmE), / wɒt ɪz mɔː / (BrE)
used to introduce extra information, especially when it is more important, surprising, or impressive

Examples

What is more important
She managed to finish the project on time; what is more, she exceeded everyone's expectations.
The course is affordable and, what is more, it covers advanced topics rarely taught elsewhere.
The hotel provided free breakfast and, what is more, offered complimentary transport to the airport.
He passed the exam on his first try; what is more, he got the highest score in the class.
She received a promotion; what is more, she was offered a higher salary.
The weather was perfect, and what is more, everyone enjoyed themselves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furthermore - used to add additional point or idea
  • synonym: moreover - used to add information
