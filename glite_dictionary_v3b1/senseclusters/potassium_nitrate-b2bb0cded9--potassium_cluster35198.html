potassium nitrate

noun
chemical compound (KNO₃); used in fertilizers and explosives
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪsiəm ˈnaɪtreɪt / (AmE), / pəˈtæsɪəm ˈnaɪtreɪt / (BrE)
a white crystalline salt (KNO₃) used especially in fertilizers, explosives, and food preservation

Examples

White potassium nitrate salt
Gunpowder is made by combining charcoal, sulfur, and potassium nitrate.
Some foods use potassium nitrate as a preservative to prevent spoilage.
The farmer added potassium nitrate to the soil to improve crop growth.
