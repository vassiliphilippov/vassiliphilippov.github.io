multinuclear

adjective
with multiple nuclei
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈnuːkliɚ / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈnjuːkliə / (BrE)
having or involving more than one nucleus or central part, especially in a cell or structure

Examples

Multinuclear cell structure
The scientist observed a multinuclear cell under the microscope during the experiment.
Some muscle fibers are naturally multinuclear, containing several nuclei within a single cell membrane.
The disease is characterized by the presence of multinuclear giant cells in the tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mononuclear - having only one nucleus
