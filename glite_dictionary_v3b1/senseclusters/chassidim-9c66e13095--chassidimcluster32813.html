Chassidim

noun
members of a Jewish Orthodox sect

Members of a sect of Orthodox Jews that originated in 18th-century Eastern Europe, known for their strict observance of Mosaic law and religious mysticism.

Examples

The Chassidim community
Books about the Chassidim often explore their spiritual philosophy and traditions.
Many Chassidim gather at the synagogue every morning for prayer.
The Chassidim are known for their joyful religious celebrations and distinctive dress.
