forerunner

noun
one who precedes or announces another
7.1 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔː.rʌn.ər/ (AmE), /ˈfɔː.rʌn.ə/ (BrE)
a person or thing that comes before and signals, announces, or prepares for the arrival of someone or something else; a precursor

Examples

A forerunner of change
Many regarded John the Baptist as a forerunner to Jesus in the New Testament.
The colonial courier acted as a forerunner, delivering news ahead of the official delegation.
The high winds were a forerunner of the approaching storm.
Some consider the steam engine to be the forerunner of the internal combustion engine.
The telephone was a forerunner of modern communication technology.
This ancient festival was a forerunner of many customs we celebrate today.
Swallows are often viewed as a forerunner of spring.
The first compact discs were the forerunner of today's digital music formats.
The invention was seen as a forerunner to modern technology.
