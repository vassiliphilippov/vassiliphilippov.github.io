hushed-up

adjective
kept secret or concealed
3.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhʌʃtˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌhʌʃtˈʌp / (BrE)
(of information or news) deliberately kept secret or concealed, often by using influence or authority.

Examples

A hushed-up secret
Rumors about the CEO's sudden resignation were quickly hushed-up by the board.
The company took steps to keep the entire incident hushed-up.
There were many hushed-up scandals involving government officials.
