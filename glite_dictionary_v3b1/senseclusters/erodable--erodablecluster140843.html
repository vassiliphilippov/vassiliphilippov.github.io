erodable

adjective
can be worn away
Pronunciation: / ɪˈroʊdəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊdəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be worn away or destroyed by natural forces such as water or wind

Examples

Erodable soil
The erodable cliffs along the coast are slowly shrinking due to constant wave action.
Farmers planted grass to protect the erodable soil from being washed away by rain.
Engineers assessed which areas of the riverbank were most erodable before starting construction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erosive - causing erosion or wearing away
  • antonym: resistant - not easily affected or changed by something
