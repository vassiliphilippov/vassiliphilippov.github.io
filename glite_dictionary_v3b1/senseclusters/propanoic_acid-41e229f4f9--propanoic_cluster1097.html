propanoic acid

noun
a colorless organic acid, used as preservative
Pronunciation: / proʊˌpænoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / prəˌpænəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent organic acid (C3H6O2) found in milk, sweat, and some distillates, used as a preservative and in the manufacture of chemicals

Examples

Propanoic acid preservative use
Propanoic acid is commonly used as a food preservative due to its antifungal properties.
Scientists detected traces of propanoic acid in the sample taken from the dairy product.
The chemical smell was traced back to a bottle of propanoic acid in the laboratory.
