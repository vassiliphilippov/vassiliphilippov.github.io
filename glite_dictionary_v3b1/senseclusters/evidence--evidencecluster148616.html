evidence

noun
information used to prove facts in court
Pronunciation: / ˈɛvədəns /; / ˈɛvɪdəns /; / ˈɛv.ə.dəns / (AmE), / ˈɛvɪdəns /; / ˈɛv.ɪ.dəns / (BrE)
facts, information, or material that shows something is true or helps to prove something

Examples

New evidence in court
Her lawyer presented new evidence to support the appeal.
The judge ruled that the evidence was admissible in court.
There was insufficient evidence to convict the accused.
Her cheerful smile was evidence of her excitement about the trip.
The muddy footprints were clear evidence that someone had been in the house.
There is no evidence linking the two crimes together.
Scientific evidence suggests that climate change is accelerating.
The lawyer examined all the evidence before preparing her argument.
There was no evidence to support his claim of innocence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disproof - evidence that shows something is not true
  • synonym: indication - a sign that suggests the existence or truth of something
  • synonym: proof - evidence strong enough to establish something as true
  • synonym: testimony - a formal written or spoken statement given in a court of law
