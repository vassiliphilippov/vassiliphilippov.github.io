microphyllous

adjective
having small simple leaves
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈfɪləs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈfɪləs / (BrE)
having small or very small leaves (especially in certain plants)

Examples

Microphyllous leaves
Lycophytes are known for their microphyllous leaves, which differ from the larger leaves of most modern plants.
The microphyllous nature of the fossil suggests it is from an early group of vascular plants.
Scientists study microphyllous plants to understand the evolution of leaf structures in ancient times.
Many desert plants are microphyllous to reduce water loss through their leaves.
The botanist identified the shrub as microphyllous due to its tiny, needle-like leaves.
In dry climates, microphyllous species often have an advantage over broad-leaved plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macrophyllous - having large leaves
  • antonym: megaphyllous - having large, complex leaves with branched veins
  • synonym: microphyll - a small leaf, especially in certain plant groups
