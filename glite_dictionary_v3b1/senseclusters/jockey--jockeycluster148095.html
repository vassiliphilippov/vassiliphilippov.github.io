jockey

verb
compete for advantage or position
6.8 0.81/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊ’É‘ki / (AmE), / ËˆdÊ’É’ki / (BrE)
to compete or try to gain an advantage over others, often by using clever or strategic moves

Examples

To jockey for position
Several firms are jockeying to win the lucrative government contract.
She jockeyed herself into a key role on the committee through persistent effort.
The senators continued to jockey for position as the negotiations entered their final stages.
He tried to jockey his rivals into making a mistake during the negotiations.
She managed to jockey her way into a higher-paying job ahead of her colleagues.
The companies attempted to jockey for position by spreading misleading information.

Synonyms

  • jockey for position
    7.1 0.32/m
    jockey for position means to move or maneuver to gain a specific place before a decision, while jockey means to compete more generally using clever moves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • synonym: outmaneuver - to gain an advantage by skill or cleverness
  • synonym: struggle - to make forceful efforts to get free or achieve something
  • synonym: vie - to compete eagerly with others for something
