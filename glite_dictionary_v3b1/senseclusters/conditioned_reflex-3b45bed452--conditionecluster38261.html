conditioned reflex

noun
learned response to a stimulus
5.3 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàd…™ É…ônd ÀàriÀêÀåfl…õks / (AmE), / k…ônÀàd…™ É…ônd ÀàriÀêfl…õks / (BrE)
A learned response that occurs when a previously neutral stimulus comes to elicit a reaction after repeated association with a stimulus that naturally produces that reaction.

Examples

A conditioned reflex response
Over time, the child's response became a conditioned reflex triggered by the classroom bell.
The experiment demonstrated that a dog can develop a conditioned reflex to the sound of a bell.
Training techniques often rely on creating a conditioned reflex through repeated practice.
