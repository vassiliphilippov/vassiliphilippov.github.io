vinegar worm

noun
small nematode commonly found in vinegar
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪnəɡɚ wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈvɪnɪɡə wɜːm / (BrE)
a minute nematode worm (usually Turbatrix aceti) found in vinegar, feeding on microorganisms that cause fermentation

Examples

The vinegar worm moves
After opening the old bottle, she noticed a vinegar worm wriggling at the bottom.
Microbiologists often study the vinegar worm to observe fermentation-related ecosystems.
The presence of vinegar worms indicates natural fermentation is taking place in the liquid.
