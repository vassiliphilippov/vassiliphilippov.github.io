superannuate

verb
become obsolete or outdated
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈæn.ju.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈran.ju.eɪt /; / ˌsuːpərˈanjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to make something obsolete or out of date, or to become obsolete because it is no longer suitable

Examples

To superannuate a law
If a law fails to keep up with society, it may superannuate before it is repealed.
Many traditional teaching methods have been superannuated by digital learning tools.
Technological advances can quickly superannuate once-revolutionary products.
Many traditional teaching methods have been superannuated by advances in educational technology.
Policy makers may superannuate regulations that no longer serve the public interest.
The company decided to superannuate the old machinery in favor of modern equipment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernize - to make something more modern
  • synonym: obsolete - no longer produced or used; out of date
  • synonym: outdated - no longer current; old-fashioned
  • synonym: outmoded - no longer fashionable or useful
