overleveraged

adjective
excessively in debt
8.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈlev.ər.ɪdʒd/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈlev.ər.ɪdʒd/ (BrE)
having taken on more debt than can be managed or supported by assets or income

Examples

An overleveraged company
After several risky investments, the company became overleveraged and struggled to pay its debts.
Banks are cautious about lending to overleveraged businesses with high levels of existing debt.
The real estate market crash left many homeowners overleveraged and unable to refinance their loans.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: insolvent - unable to pay debts owed
  • degree lower: leveraged - having some debt relative to assets or income
  • antonym: solvent - able to meet financial obligations
