go along

verb
cooperate with or pretend to cooperate
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /goʊ ə.ˈlɔːŋ (AmE), /gəʊ ə.ˈlɒŋ (BrE)
to cooperate with or pretend to cooperate with someone or something, especially temporarily or to avoid conflict

Examples

To go along with a plan

She decided to go along with his story until she could find out the truth.
Sometimes you have to go along with the plan, even if you disagree with it.
