rimstone

noun
cave mineral dam
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪmˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈrɪmstəʊn / (BrE)
a mineral deposit that forms layered dams or barriers in caves where water flows over surfaces

Examples

Rimstone dams
The explorers admired the intricate rimstone dams that lined the cave floor.
Water flowing over the rocks gradually built up a series of rimstone barriers.
Photographers often visit caves to capture the beauty of rimstone formations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: travertine - a form of limestone deposited by mineral springs
