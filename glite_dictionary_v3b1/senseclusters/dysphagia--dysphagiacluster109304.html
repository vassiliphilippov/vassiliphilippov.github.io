dysphagia

noun
difficulty or pain swallowing (medical)
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfeɪdʒiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈfeɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
difficulty or pain in swallowing, often due to an underlying medical condition

Examples

Persistent dysphagia
If you experience persistent dysphagia, it is important to consult a doctor.
Patients with dysphagia may need a special diet to help them swallow more easily.
The doctor carried out a test to determine the severity of the patient's dysphagia.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: aphagia - inability to swallow
  • synonym: odynophagia - painful swallowing
