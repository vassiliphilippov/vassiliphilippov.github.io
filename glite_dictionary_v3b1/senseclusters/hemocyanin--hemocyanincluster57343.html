hemocyanin

noun
oxygen-carrying protein
9.6
Pronunciation: /hiː.mə.ˈsaɪ.nɪn/ (AmE), /hiː.mə.ˈsaɪ.nɪn/ (BrE)
a blue, copper-containing protein that transports oxygen in the blood of some invertebrates, such as mollusks and arthropods

Examples

Hemocyanin protein
Unlike humans, some invertebrates use hemocyanin to transport oxygen in their blood.
The blue color of the octopus's blood is due to the presence of hemocyanin.
Researchers studied how hemocyanin helps certain animals survive in low-oxygen environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hemoglobin - the iron-containing protein that carries oxygen in the blood of vertebrates
