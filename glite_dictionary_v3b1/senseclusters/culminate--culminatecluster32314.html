culminate

verb
reach the highest point or climax
5.2 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlməˌneɪt /; / ˈkʌlmɪˌneɪt /; / ˈkʌlmɪneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌlmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to reach the highest or most important point, or to arrive at a final or climactic stage (of an event, process, or achievement)

Examples

To culminate in success
Months of preparation will culminate in the opening ceremony next Friday.
The movie culminates with an unexpected twist in the final scene.
Their disagreement culminated in a heated argument.
Her efforts culminated in a stunning performance at the concert.
The festival culminates in a magnificent fireworks display.
The year-long investigation culminated with the arrest of several high-ranking officials.
Her years of study and research culminated in a groundbreaking discovery.
The festival culminates with a spectacular fireworks display on the final night.
The negotiations culminated in a historic agreement between the two countries.
