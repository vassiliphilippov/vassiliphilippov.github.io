resample

verb
to change sampling rate
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsæmpəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɑːmpəl / (BrE)
to change the sampling rate or format of a digital signal, such as audio, image, or video

Examples

To resample an image
The engineer needed to resample the audio files to ensure they matched the project's requirements.
When you resample an image, its quality can change depending on the new resolution.
The software automatically resamples video clips to fit the selected frame rate.
The engineer had to resample the audio file to match the new format.
If the image looks blurry, try to resample it at a higher resolution.
Researchers often resample data to test the reliability of their results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convert - to change something into a different form or format
  • degree lower: downsample - to reduce the sampling rate of a digital signal
  • synonym: sample - to take a part of something to analyze or use
  • degree higher: upsample - to increase the sampling rate of a digital signal
