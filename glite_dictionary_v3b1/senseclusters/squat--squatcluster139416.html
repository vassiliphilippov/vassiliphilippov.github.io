squat

noun
strength exercise involving crouching and standing
2.5 3.86/m
Pronunciation: / skwÉ‘t / (AmE), / skwÉ’t / (BrE)
a physical movement or exercise where you bend your knees and lower your body close to the ground, then stand up again

Examples

A set of squats
After a long set of squats, my legs felt exhausted.
He performed twenty squats as part of his daily workout routine.
Many athletes use the squat to build strength in their lower body.
He held a squat for thirty seconds to strengthen his legs.
She performed a deep squat as part of her exercise routine.
The child rested in a squat while playing in the sand.

Synonyms

  • squatting
    2.7 0.02/m
    squatting is doing repeated knee-bend movements as exercise to build leg strength, while squat names the exercise or single bending movement.
  • knee bend
    3.3 0.06/m
    knee bend is a simple movement of bending the knees for leg strength, while squat is a full exercise lowering and standing back up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crouch - the act of bending low with the limbs close to the body
  • synonym: lunge - a strength-training exercise involving stepping forward and bending the knees
  • antonym: stand - the act of being upright on the feet
