Pelham

noun
combined curb and snaffle horse bit
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛləm / (AmE), / ˈpɛləm / (BrE)
a type of horse bit with a bar mouthpiece, designed to combine the effects of both curb and snaffle bits

Examples

A Pelham bit
She cleaned the Pelham thoroughly before tacking up her horse for the show.
Some horses respond well to a Pelham because it provides both direct and leverage action.
The rider chose a Pelham to have greater control during the jumping course.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: curb bit - a type of horse bit that applies leverage to the horse's mouth
  • degree lower: snaffle bit - a simple horse bit without leverage
