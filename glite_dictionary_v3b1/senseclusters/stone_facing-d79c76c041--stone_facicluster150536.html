stone facing

noun
masonry covering on an embankment or structure
5.7
Pronunciation: / stoʊn ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / stəʊn ˈfeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
A covering or outer layer of stone, usually masonry, applied to support or protect an embankment or structure.

Examples

Thick stone facing
The ancient bridge was reinforced with a thick stone facing for extra durability.
The engineers installed a stone facing along the riverbank to prevent erosion.
Workers repaired the stone facing after a section was damaged by the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revetment - a retaining wall or facing of masonry to support an embankment
