illusionism

noun
belief world is illusory
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːʒəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʒənɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical or religious belief that the external world or ordinary appearances are ultimately an illusion.

Examples

Philosophical illusionism
Some philosophers argue that illusionism challenges our basic understanding of reality.
Buddhist teachings are sometimes interpreted as a form of illusionism regarding the material world.
The debate between illusionism and realism has continued for centuries in philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idealism - the belief that reality is fundamentally mental or spiritual
  • antonym: realism - the belief that the external world exists independently of perception
