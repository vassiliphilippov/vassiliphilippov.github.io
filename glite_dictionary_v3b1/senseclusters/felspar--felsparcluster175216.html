felspar

noun
group of aluminum-silicate minerals
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛlˌspɑr / (AmE), / ˈfɛlspɑː / (BrE)
any of a group of hard, crystalline minerals consisting of aluminum silicates of potassium, sodium, calcium, or barium, commonly found in rocks

Examples

A felspar mineral
Granite is mainly composed of quartz and felspar.
Some geologists study the properties of felspar to understand rock formation.
The shiny particles in the rock are actually bits of felspar.
