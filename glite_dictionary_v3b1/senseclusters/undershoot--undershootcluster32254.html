undershoot

verb
fail to reach a target by falling short
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈʃuːt /; / ˌʌndɚˈʃuːt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈʃuːt / (BrE)
to fail to reach or hit a target by falling short of it

Examples

To undershoot the target
He undershot the basketball hoop, and the ball bounced off the front rim.
If you undershoot the runway, you may land in the grass instead of on the pavement.
The company tends to undershoot its sales targets to manage investor expectations.
If the aircraft undershoots its target, it may land in unsafe terrain.
Investigators found that heavy fog contributed to the jet undershooting the airport runway.
The pilot had to abort the landing after the plane undershot the runway during bad weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overshoot - to go beyond or exceed a target or mark
