proboscidean

noun
large mammal with a trunk and tusks
7.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˌbɑːˈsɪdiən / (AmE), / prəˌbɒsɪˈdiːən / (BrE)
a large herbivorous mammal with tusks and a long trunk, such as an elephant or its extinct relatives

Examples

Large proboscideans like elephants
Scientists study proboscideans to learn more about mammalian evolution.
The proboscidean fossils found in the area indicate that ancient elephants once roamed here.
The woolly mammoth is a well-known extinct proboscidean.
