mercantile

adjective
profit-oriented; commercial
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝː.kən.taɪl /; / ˈmɜr.kənˌtaɪl /; / ˈmɜːrkənˌtaɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.kən.taɪl /; / ˈmɜːkəntaɪl / (BrE)
relating to trade, commerce, or merchants, especially with a focus on profit or commercial activity

Examples

Mercantile trade system

The city has a bustling, mercantile atmosphere, shaped by centuries of trade.
The company's mercantile strategies focused on maximizing profit at every opportunity.
Foreign investments have boosted the mercantile ties between the two countries.
Her family comes from a proud mercantile background.
The city's mercantile reputation grew quickly during the 18th century.
In the seventeenth century, European nations often adopted a mercantile approach to boost their economies.
Many mercantile theories emphasize the importance of accumulating precious metals.
The policy reforms were intended to dismantle the nation's mercantile system.

  • synonym: mercantilist - relating to a supporter or practitioner of mercantilism
  • synonym: mercenary - primarily motivated by profit; profit-oriented
  • antonym: noncommercial - not conducted for profit or commercial purposes
