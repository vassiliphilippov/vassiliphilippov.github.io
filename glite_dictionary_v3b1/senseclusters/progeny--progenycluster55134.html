progeny

noun
descendants; offspring
7.3 1.88/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêd í…ôni / (AmE), / Ààpr…íd í…ôni / (BrE)
A person's children or descendants; offspring.

Examples

Her progeny gathered
Her progeny gathered to celebrate her ninetieth birthday.
Many artists' progeny follow in their creative footsteps, continuing the family tradition.
The scientist was proud to see his progeny achieve great things in their respective fields.

Synonyms

  • offspring
    3.0 9.72/m
    offspring refers to the young of people, animals, or plants and is everyday and biological, while progeny is more formal for descendants.
  • issue
    7.9 0.67/m
    issue is a formal or legal word for someone's children or descendants, while progeny sounds more literary and biological.
  • descendant
    3.4 13/m
    descendant means a person born in a later generation, while progeny often refers to immediate offspring or collective descendants.

  • synonym: offspring - a person's child or animal's young
