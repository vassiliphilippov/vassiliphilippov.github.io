European rabbit

noun
a species of burrowing rabbit, widely domesticated
1.0
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
A common species of burrowing rabbit native to southern Europe and northern Africa, widely introduced elsewhere and domesticated in various colors; young are born naked and helpless.

Examples

The European rabbit burrows
Farmers often use fences to keep European rabbits out of their vegetable gardens.
The European rabbit has become an invasive species in Australia, causing significant ecological damage.
The soft fur of the European rabbit is prized in the textile industry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rabbit - a small mammal with long ears, soft fur, and a short tail that hops on its back legs
