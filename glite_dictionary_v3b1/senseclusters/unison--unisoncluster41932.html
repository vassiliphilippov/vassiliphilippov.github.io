unison

noun
musical notes sounding at the same pitch
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈjunəsən / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪsən / (BrE)
(music) the simultaneous sounding of two or more notes at the same pitch or in octaves.

Examples

Sing in unison
The choir sang in unison, creating a powerful and harmonious sound.
The violinists played the melody in unison during the performance.
When the band joined in unison, the audience was thrilled by the effect.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: octave - (music) the interval between one musical pitch and another with half or double its frequency
