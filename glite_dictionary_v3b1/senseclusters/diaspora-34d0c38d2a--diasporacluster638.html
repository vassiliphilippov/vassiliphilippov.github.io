Diaspora

noun
dispersion of Jews outside Israel
6.4 7.4
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæspərə / (AmE), / daɪˈæspərə / (BrE)
The dispersion of the Jews beyond Israel, especially after the destruction of the temple in Jerusalem in 586 BC and their exile to Babylonia, continuing up to the present day.

Examples

The Diaspora community
Jewish communities in the Diaspora maintained their traditions while adapting to new lands.
The Diaspora played a major role in shaping Jewish identity and culture.
The history of the Diaspora is marked by both hardship and resilience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • antonym: return - coming back to one's homeland, as in the context of the Jewish people
