demonstration

noun
logical proof or argument
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛmənˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
A logical proof or argument that establishes the truth of a proposition or conclusion.

Examples

Mathematical demonstration
Euclid's Elements contains a rigorous demonstration for each geometric proposition.
Her demonstration of the principle convinced even the skeptics in the audience.
The professor gave a clear demonstration of the theorem during the lecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proof - evidence or argument that establishes the truth of a statement
  • antonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong or false
