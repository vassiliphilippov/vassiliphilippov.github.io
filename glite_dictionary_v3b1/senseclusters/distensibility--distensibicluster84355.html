distensibility

noun
ability to stretch
8.8
Pronunciation: / dɪˌstɛnsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌstɛnsɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of something, especially a body part or tissue, to stretch or expand when pressure is applied

Examples

The distensibility of lungs
The distensibility of the lungs allows them to fill with air during breathing.
Doctors measure the distensibility of arteries to assess cardiovascular health.
Reduced distensibility in tissues can indicate certain medical conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elasticity - the ability of a material to return to its original shape after being stretched
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not easily bent or stretched
