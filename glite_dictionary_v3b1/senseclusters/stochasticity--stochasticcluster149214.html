stochasticity

noun
randomness; lack of predictability
Pronunciation: / stoʊˌkæˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / stəʊˌkæˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being random, lacking a predictable order or plan.

Examples

High stochasticity process
Natural processes like radioactive decay exhibit a high degree of stochasticity.
Researchers must account for stochasticity when modeling population dynamics.
The stochasticity of the weather makes forecasting very challenging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinism - the doctrine that all events are ultimately determined by causes external to the will
  • synonym: randomness - the quality of lacking any predictable outcome or pattern
