contraposition

noun
logical reversal
8.8
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.trə.pə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.trə.pə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒn.trə.pə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.trə.pə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of placing things in contrast or opposition, especially the logical process of reversing and negating terms in a statement

Examples

Contraposition in logic
In formal logic, contraposition allows you to infer a new statement by reversing and negating the original terms.
The teacher explained contraposition as a useful tool for understanding logical arguments.
By using contraposition, the student was able to prove the statement in a different way.
