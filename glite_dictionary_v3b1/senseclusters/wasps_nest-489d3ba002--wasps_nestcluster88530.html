wasp's nest

noun
structure built by wasps or hornets
Pronunciation: / wɑsps nɛst / (AmE), / wɒsps nɛst / (BrE)
a structure built by wasps or hornets as their habitation, typically made from chewed wood fibers formed into a papery substance

Examples

A wasp's nest in tree
A single wasp's nest can house thousands of insects during peak summer months.
Destroying a wasp's nest can be dangerous and should be done with caution.
We found a large wasp's nest hanging under the roof of the shed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beehive - structure built by bees to house their colony
