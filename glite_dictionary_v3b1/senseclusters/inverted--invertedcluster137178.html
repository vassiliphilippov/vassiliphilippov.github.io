inverted

adjective
having top and bottom reversed
1.8 3.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈvɜːr.tɪd/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈvɜː.tɪd/ (BrE)
placed or turned so that the top and bottom are reversed in position or direction

Examples

An inverted cake
Pour the batter over the fruit and bake an inverted cake for dessert.
Some scientists use an inverted microscope to view specimens from below.
The airplane performed an inverted loop during the airshow, thrilling the crowd.
In many flowering plants, the ovule becomes inverted during development.
Some species produce inverted ovules as a characteristic feature of their reproductive organs.
The botanist explained how the seed's inverted position aids in fertilization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anatropous - (of an ovule) completely inverted and turned back 180 degrees on its stalk
  • synonym: reversed - turned the other way around or upside down
  • antonym: upright - in a vertical position with the top above the base
