intervention

noun
involvement in foreign affairs or countries
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən /; / ˌɪn.tɚˈvɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən /; / ˌɪn.təˈvɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of becoming involved in a situation to influence or change what is happening, especially to help solve a problem or settle a dispute

Examples

Government intervention
Economic intervention by powerful nations often affects developing countries' sovereignty.
Many experts argue that foreign intervention can make local conflicts worse.
The government announced a policy of military intervention in the region.
Government intervention was needed to resolve the economic crisis.
Parental intervention in school matters is sometimes necessary for a child's well-being.
The negotiations proceeded without direct intervention from either party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interference - unwanted or inappropriate involvement in others' affairs
  • synonym: mediation - the process of intervening to resolve a dispute
  • antonym: nonintervention - the policy of not becoming involved in the affairs of other countries
