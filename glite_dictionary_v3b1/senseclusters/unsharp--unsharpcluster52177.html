unsharp

adjective
slightly flat (music)
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈʃɑrp / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈʃɑːp / (BrE)
in music, lower in pitch than intended; slightly flat or not accurately tuned

Examples

Unsharp singing
The singer's final note was unsharp, making the ending sound slightly off.
If your instrument is unsharp, you should tune it before the performance.
The choir director noticed an unsharp tone in the soprano section during rehearsal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flat - lower in pitch than the correct note
  • antonym: sharp - higher in pitch than the correct note
