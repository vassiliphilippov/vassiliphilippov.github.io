lubricant

noun
substance that reduces friction
3.7 2.90/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêbr…™k…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêbr…™k…ônt / (BrE)
A substance used to reduce friction between surfaces by making them smooth or slippery.

Examples

A lubricant oil
Applying a lubricant to the door hinge stopped it from squeaking.
Engine oil acts as a lubricant to protect moving parts from wear.
Some specialized lubricants are designed for use in high temperatures.

Synonyms

  • lube
    5.9 1.57/m
    lube is a short, informal word for oils or greases used on machines, while lubricant is the formal technical term used in manuals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grease - a thick oily substance used as a lubricant
  • synonym: oil - a viscous liquid used as a lubricant or fuel
