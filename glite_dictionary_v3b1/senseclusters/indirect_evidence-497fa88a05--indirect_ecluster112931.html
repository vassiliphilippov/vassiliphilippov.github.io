indirect evidence

noun
evidence providing a basis for inference
1.0 4.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.də.ˈrekt ˈev.ə.dəns/ /; / /ɪn.daɪ.ˈrekt ˈev.ə.dəns/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.ˈrekt ˈev.ɪ.dəns/ /; / /ɪn.daɪ.ˈrekt ˈev.ə.dəns/ (BrE)
Evidence that does not directly prove a fact but provides a basis for inferring its existence or truth.

Examples

Indirect evidence from records
Much of historical research is based on indirect evidence from written records and artifacts.
The prosecution relied heavily on indirect evidence to build their case.
While there was no eyewitness, the indirect evidence pointed towards the suspect's guilt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circumstantial evidence - evidence that relies on inference to connect it to a conclusion of fact
  • antonym: direct evidence - evidence that directly proves a fact
