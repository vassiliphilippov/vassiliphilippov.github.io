hexokinase

noun
glucose-processing enzyme
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛksoʊˈkaɪneɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛksəʊˈkaɪneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the first step in the breakdown of glucose in cells

Examples

Hexokinase enzyme activity
The presence of hexokinase in muscle cells is essential for efficient energy production.
Researchers measured hexokinase activity to study how cells metabolize glucose.
A deficiency in hexokinase can lead to problems with blood sugar regulation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glucokinase - an enzyme that also helps process glucose in the liver
