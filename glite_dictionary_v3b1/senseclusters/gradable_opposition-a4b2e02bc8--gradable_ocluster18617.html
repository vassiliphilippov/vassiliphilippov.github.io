gradable opposition

noun
opposites with intermediate degrees
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪ.dəb.əl ɑːˌpəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.də.bəl ɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a pair of words or expressions that represent opposite ends of a spectrum and can be graded between, such as 'hot' and 'cold'

Examples

Gradable opposition of words
Hot and cold form a classic gradable opposition because many temperatures fall between them.
In linguistics, gradable opposition refers to pairs like 'tall' and 'short' which allow for comparison.
The concept of gradable opposition helps explain how adjectives can be used in comparative ways.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antonym - a word opposite in meaning to another
  • antonym: complementary opposition - binary opposition with no in-between states, like 'alive' and 'dead'
