Pop Art

noun
art movement using imagery from popular culture
4.4 0.33/m
Pronunciation: / p…ëp …ërt / (AmE), / p…íp …ëÀêt / (BrE)
an art movement that emerged in the 1950s and 1960s, characterized by themes and techniques drawn from popular mass culture, such as advertising and comics

Examples

Pop Art movement
A classic example of Pop Art is Andy Warhol's brightly colored soup can series.
Roy Lichtenstein is known for his distinctive Pop Art paintings that mimic the style of printed cartoons.
The Pop Art movement challenged traditional fine art by incorporating imagery from advertising and comic books.
