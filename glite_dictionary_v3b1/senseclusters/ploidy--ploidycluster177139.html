ploidy

noun
chromosome set number
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplɔɪdi / (AmE), / ˈplɔɪdi / (BrE)
the number of complete sets of chromosomes in a cell or organism

Examples

Cell ploidy level
Scientists measured the ploidy of the plant cells to determine their genetic makeup.
Changes in ploidy can affect how an organism grows and develops.
The ploidy level is important in understanding certain genetic diseases.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: diploid - having two complete sets of chromosomes
  • degree lower: haploid - having a single set of chromosomes
  • degree higher: polyploid - having more than two sets of chromosomes
