vapor pressure

noun
pressure exerted by a vapor in equilibrium
2.1 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpər ˌprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈveɪpə ˌprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by a vapor in equilibrium with its liquid or solid form in a closed system

Examples

Liquid vapor pressure
At its boiling point, the vapor pressure of a liquid equals atmospheric pressure.
Proper storage requires monitoring the vapor pressure of chemicals.
The vapor pressure of water increases with temperature.
As the temperature increases, so does the vapour pressure of a liquid.
At a given temperature, the vapour pressure of water determines how quickly it evaporates.
The boiling point is reached when the vapour pressure equals the atmospheric pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boiling point - the temperature at which a liquid boils and turns to vapor
  • synonym: saturated vapour pressure - the vapour pressure when a vapour is in equilibrium with its liquid
