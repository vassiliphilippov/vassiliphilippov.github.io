calcar

noun
spur-shaped structure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkælkɑr / (AmE), / ˈkælkɑː / (BrE)
a spur-shaped projection or structure, especially in anatomy or zoology

Examples

A calcar in anatomy
The bat's foot has a calcar that helps support the wing membrane.
In some plants, a calcar forms at the base of the flower for nectar storage.
The radiologist noted a small calcar on the patient's femur in the X-ray.

Related Terms
