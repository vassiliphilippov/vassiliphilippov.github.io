meadow spittlebug

noun
North American insect that damages grasses
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdoʊ ˈspɪt̬əlˌbʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ ˈspɪtl̩ˌbʌɡ / (BrE)
a North American insect (Philaenus spumarius) known for producing frothy spittle masses on grasses and can cause significant damage to plants

Examples

Meadow spittlebugs on grass
A severe infestation of meadow spittlebugs can weaken a lawn and reduce crop yields.
Gardeners are advised to watch for frothy masses, signs that meadow spittlebug nymphs are feeding on their plants.
The meadow spittlebug is commonly seen on grass blades in late spring and summer.
