anechoic chamber

noun
room designed to absorb sound reflections
6.2
Pronunciation: / ænɪˈkoʊɪk ˈtʃeɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˌanɪˈkəʊɪk ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
A room designed to completely absorb sound reflections, used for acoustic testing or recording.

Examples

An anechoic chamber room
An anechoic chamber is often used in product development for microphones and loudspeakers.
Any sound made inside an anechoic chamber is absorbed, not echoed.
The scientists entered the anechoic chamber to test the new speaker's acoustic properties.
