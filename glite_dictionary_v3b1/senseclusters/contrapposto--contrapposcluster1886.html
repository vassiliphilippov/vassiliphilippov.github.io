contrapposto

adjective
artistic pose
9.9
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.trə.ˈpɑː.stoʊ/ (AmE), /kɒn.trə.ˈpɒ.stəʊ/ (BrE)
describing a pose in art where the body stands with weight on one leg, causing the shoulders and hips to tilt in opposite directions

Examples

Contrapposto pose
The statue's contrapposto stance gives it a sense of movement and naturalism.
Artists often use a contrapposto pose to make their figures appear more lifelike.
The model stood in a contrapposto position, shifting her weight onto one leg for the sketch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relaxed - not stiff or tense; at ease
  • antonym: rigid - stiff and unmoving
