myotonia atrophica

noun
a form of muscular dystrophy with weakness and wasting
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.əˈtoʊ.ni.ə æˈtrɑː.fɪ.kə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈtəʊ.nɪ.ə əˈtrɒ.fɪ.kə / (BrE)
a rare hereditary disorder characterized by progressive muscle weakness, wasting, and myotonia, often affecting the face, hands, feet, and neck, with onset ranging from birth to middle age and slow progression.

Examples

Myotonia atrophica muscle weakness
Families affected by myotonia atrophica often notice gradual muscle wasting over several years.
Speech difficulties and hand weakness are common early symptoms of myotonia atrophica.
The patient was diagnosed with myotonia atrophica after exhibiting prolonged muscle stiffness and weakness.
