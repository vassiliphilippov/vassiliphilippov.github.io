ticking bomb

noun
dangerous situation likely to worsen
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪkɪŋ bɑm / (AmE), / ˈtɪkɪŋ bɒm / (BrE)
A situation or problem that is likely to become dangerous or serious if not dealt with soon.

Examples

A ticking bomb situation
Experts warn that untreated sewage is a ticking bomb for public health.
Poverty in many urban areas is a ticking bomb that cannot be ignored.
The company's outdated software was a ticking bomb just waiting for a security breach.
