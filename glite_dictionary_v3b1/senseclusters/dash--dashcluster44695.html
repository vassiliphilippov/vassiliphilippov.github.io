dash

verb
break into pieces violently
6.6 1.6
Pronunciation: /dæʃ/ (AmE), /dæʃ/ (BrE)
to break, smash, or throw something violently so it breaks

Examples

To dash a glass
He accidentally dashed the vase against the floor, breaking it into pieces.
She dashed the glass in anger, sending shards flying everywhere.
The waves dashed the boat to bits on the rocks during the storm.
He dashed the letter into the fire without reading it.
Huge waves dashed against the rocks during the storm.
She dashed the glass to the floor in a moment of anger.
