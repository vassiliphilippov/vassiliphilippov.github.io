displacement

noun
psychiatric defense mechanism
5.0 6.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈspleɪs.mənt/ /; / /dɪz.ˈpleɪs.mənt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈspleɪs.mənt/ /; / /dɪz.ˈpleɪs.mənt/ (BrE)
In psychiatry, a defense mechanism by which an emotional response or impulse is redirected from its original object to a safer or more acceptable substitute.

Examples

Emotional displacement
In psychotherapy, displacement may be recognized as a way patients avoid confronting difficult emotions directly.
She used displacement to cope with her anger at her boss by becoming frustrated with her family instead.
The child's sudden aggression toward his toys was viewed as displacement of anxiety about starting school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: projection - A defense mechanism involving attributing one's own feelings to another person
