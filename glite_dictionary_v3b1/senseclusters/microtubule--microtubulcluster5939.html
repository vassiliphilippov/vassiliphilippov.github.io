microtubule

noun
microscopic tubular cell structure
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈtuːbjuːl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈtjuːbjuːl / (BrE)
a microscopic tubular structure present in the cytoplasm of cells, forming part of the cytoskeleton and involved in transport and cell division

Examples

A microtubule structure
Damage to the microtubule structure can lead to cellular malfunction.
Researchers used a fluorescent dye to visualize each microtubule within the neuron.
The microtubule network is essential for cell division in animal cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cytoskeleton - network of protein filaments in cells, providing structure and shape
