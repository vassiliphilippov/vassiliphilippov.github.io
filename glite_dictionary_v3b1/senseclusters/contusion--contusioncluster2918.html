contusion

noun
injury causing skin discoloration without breaking skin
Pronunciation: / kənˈtuːʒən / (AmE), / kənˈtjuːʒən / (BrE)
an injury where the skin is not broken but the tissue underneath is damaged, causing a bruise

Examples

A contusion injury
After falling off his bike, he developed a large contusion on his thigh.
Although the skin was not broken, a deep contusion caused significant pain and swelling.
The doctor examined the patient's leg and confirmed it was a contusion rather than a fracture.

The autopsy revealed several minor contusions but no broken bones.
