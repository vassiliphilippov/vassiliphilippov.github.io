monofocal IOL

noun
single-focus intraocular lens
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.nəˈfoʊ.kəl ˌaɪ.oʊˈɛl / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈfəʊ.kəl ˌaɪ.əʊˈɛl / (BrE)
a type of intraocular lens with a single focus point, implanted after cataract surgery to provide clear distance vision

Examples

A monofocal IOL implant
A monofocal IOL is often chosen for its ability to provide sharp distance vision.
After his cataract surgery, the doctor recommended a monofocal IOL to help him see distant objects clearly.
Most patients receiving a monofocal IOL still need reading glasses for close-up tasks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multifocal IOL - an intraocular lens with multiple focus points for near and distant vision
