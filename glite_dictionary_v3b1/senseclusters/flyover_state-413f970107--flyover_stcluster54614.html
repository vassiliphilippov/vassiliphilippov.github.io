flyover state

noun
U.S. state considered uninteresting or overlooked
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌoʊvɚ steɪt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌəʊvə steɪt / (BrE)
A U.S. state that is often regarded as uninteresting or overlooked, particularly by people traveling between more prominent coastal cities.

Examples

A flyover state
He grew up in what was often dismissed as a flyover state but loved the close-knit community.
Many politicians neglect the concerns of people living in a flyover state.
She discovered there was much more to the flyover state than people assumed.
