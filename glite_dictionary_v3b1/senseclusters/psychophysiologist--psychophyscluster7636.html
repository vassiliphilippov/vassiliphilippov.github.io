psychophysiologist

noun
mind-body scientist
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊfɪziˈɑlədʒɪst /; / ˌsaɪkoʊˌfɪziˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊfɪziˈɒlədʒɪst /; / ˌsaɪkəʊˌfɪziˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist or specialist who studies how psychological processes (like thoughts and emotions) are related to physical functions in the body

Examples

A psychophysiologist studies body
The psychophysiologist designed an experiment to examine how stress affects heart rate.
As a psychophysiologist, she often collaborates with both doctors and psychologists.
A psychophysiologist may use brain scans to study emotional responses.
The psychophysiologist monitored the patient's heart rate during the stress test.
A psychophysiologist may use biofeedback to help people manage anxiety.
She became a leading psychophysiologist after years of research on the mind-body connection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neuroscientist - a scientist who studies the nervous system
  • synonym: physiologist - a scientist who studies the functions of living organisms
  • synonym: psychologist - a scientist who studies the mind and behavior
