Artaxerxes II

proper noun
King of Persia, 404–359 BC

Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ˈtæk.sɜːr.siːz ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ɑːr.tə.ˈzɜːrk.siːz ðə ˈsek.ənd/ (AmE), /ɑː.ˈtæk.sə.siːz ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ɑː.tə.ˈzɜːk.siːz ðə ˈsek.ənd/ (BrE)
(circa 435–359 BC) King of Persia who ruled from 404 to 359 BC, known for subduing several revolts and making peace with Sparta.

Examples

Artaxerxes II king Persia
Artaxerxes II ruled Persia for over forty years, overcoming many internal rebellions.

History records that Artaxerxes II made treaties with Sparta during his reign.
