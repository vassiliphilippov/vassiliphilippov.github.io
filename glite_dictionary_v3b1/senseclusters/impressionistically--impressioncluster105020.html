impressionistically

adverb
in Impressionist style
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌprɛʃəˈnɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɛʃəˈnɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is characteristic of Impressionist art or music, focusing on general effects rather than precise details.

Examples

Impressionistically paint a scene
The landscape was painted impressionistically, with soft colors and blurred outlines.
She described the city impressionistically, focusing on the mood rather than specific landmarks.
The composer wrote the piece impressionistically, using sound to evoke feelings instead of clear melodies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artistically - in a manner relating to art or creativity
  • antonym: realistically - in a way that shows things as they really are, with attention to detail
