droopy

adjective
hanging down limply, weak or tired
3.9 0.65/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdruÀêpi / (AmE), / ÀàdruÀêpi / (BrE)
Hanging down limply, as from exhaustion or weakness; not firm or upright.

Examples

A droopy eyelid
His eyelids became droopy as he struggled to stay awake during the lecture.
She wore a droopy hat that shaded her eyes from the sun.
The flowers looked droopy after days without water.

Synonyms

  • sagging
    4.1 0.01/m
    sagging hangs down loosely, often from weakness or loss of strength, while droopy describes something that hangs down limply.
  • limp
    2.8 5.13/m
    limp is soft and weak in feel, often without energy, while droopy means hanging down or not standing firm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: perky - brisk, lively, or upright
  • synonym: sagging - hanging down loosely
