birthroot

noun
North American Trillium used in folk medicine
8.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrθˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːθˌruːt / (BrE)
A North American plant of the genus Trillium, especially Trillium erectum, with pink or purple flowers and an astringent root used traditionally in folk medicine, particularly to aid childbirth.

Examples

Birthroot plant
Early settlers used birthroot to prepare remedies for women in labor.
Herbalists sometimes recommend birthroot for its traditional medicinal properties.
The flowers of birthroot are known for their strong, unpleasant odor.
