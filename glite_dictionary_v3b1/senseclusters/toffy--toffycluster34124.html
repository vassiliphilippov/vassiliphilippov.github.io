toffy

adjective
pretentiously upper-class
Pronunciation: / ˈtɒfi / (AmE), / ˈtɒfi / (BrE)
affectedly upper-class in manner or style, often in a way that seems pretentious (chiefly British, informal)

Examples

Toffy accent
He put on a toffy accent to impress his new friends at the club.
Her toffy manner made her seem distant from the rest of the group.
People sometimes think he is toffy because of the way he dresses and speaks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: down-to-earth - practical and not pretentious
  • synonym: posh - having or showing an upper-class style or manner
  • synonym: snobbish - behaving as if you are better than others
