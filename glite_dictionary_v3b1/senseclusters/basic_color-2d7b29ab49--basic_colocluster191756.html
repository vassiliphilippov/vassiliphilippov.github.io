basic color

noun
a dye with a chromophore in a positive ion
5.8 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.sɪk ˈkʌ.lər/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.sɪk ˈkɔː.lər/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.sɪk ˈkʌ.lə/ (BrE)
A dye in which the chromophore is part of a positive ion, often used in textile dyeing and histology.

Examples

A basic color dye
Because it binds to acidic components, a basic color is ideal for staining cell nuclei.
Methylene blue is a commonly used basic color in histology.
Some basic colors are employed to dye wool and silk fabrics.
Many basic colours are used in the dyeing of cotton and wool fabrics.
The textile was stained using a basic colour for better visibility under the microscope.
This laboratory procedure requires both basic colours and acid dyes for differential staining.
