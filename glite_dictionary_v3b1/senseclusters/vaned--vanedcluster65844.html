vaned

adjective
fitted with feathers (arrow)
6.7
Pronunciation: / veɪnd / (AmE), / veɪnd / (BrE)
having vanes or fitted with vanes (such as feathers on an arrow or blades on a wheel) to guide movement or provide stability

Examples

A vaned arrow shaft
A properly vaned shaft helps an arrow maintain stability during flight.
Modern arrows are often vaned with plastic instead of real feathers.
The archer selected a vaned arrow for improved accuracy in the competition.
The engineer designed a vaned wheel to improve the efficiency of the pump.
Birds have vaned feathers that help them control their flight.
The vaned arrow flew straight toward the target.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bladeless - without blades or vanes
  • synonym: feathered - (of an arrow) having feathers
