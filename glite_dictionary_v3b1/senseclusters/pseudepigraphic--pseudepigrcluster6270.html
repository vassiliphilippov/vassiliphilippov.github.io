pseudepigraphic

adjective
falsely attributed authorship
9.9
Pronunciation: /suː.də.pɪ.ˈgræf.ɪk/ /; / /sjuː.də.pɪ.ˈgræf.ɪk/ (AmE), /sjuː.də.pɪ.ˈgræf.ɪk/ /; / /suː.də.pɪ.ˈgræf.ɪk/ (BrE)
falsely attributed to an author, especially describing a work claimed to be written by someone who did not actually write it

Examples

A pseudepigraphic work
Scholars debated whether the ancient text was truly pseudepigraphic or simply misattributed over time.

Many letters in the collection are considered pseudepigraphic by modern historians.
Scholars debated whether the letter was truly pseudepigraphic or written by the claimed author.
Many pseudepigraphic works were excluded from the official biblical canon.
The professor gave a lecture on pseudepigraphic literature in early Christianity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authentic - genuine; actually written by the claimed author
