exaggerated

adjective
enlarged or overstated
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzædʒəˌreɪtɪd /; / ɪɡˈzadʒəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
made to seem larger, greater, or more important than it really is

Examples

An exaggerated hat
She wore an exaggerated hat that was much too large for her head.
The comedian's exaggerated expressions made the audience laugh uncontrollably.
The report gave an exaggerated view of the problem, making it seem much worse than it actually was.
His exaggerated claims were quickly debunked by experts.
She gave an exaggerated sigh to show her annoyance.
The artist used exaggerated features to create a dramatic portrait.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overstated - expressed in a way that is too strong or exaggerated
  • antonym: understated - expressed or done in a restrained or subtle way
