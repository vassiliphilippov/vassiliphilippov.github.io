Synonyms

  • overstated
    4.6 0.00/m
    overstated sounds formal and measured in reports, while exaggerated is a more general and everyday way to say something is too large.
  • inflated
    4.9 0.00/m
    inflated describes claims or prices made to seem more important or valuable, while exaggerated simply means made larger or more extreme.
  • overinflated
    4.8 0.13/m
    overinflated suggests an extreme, often unreasonable increase in value or importance, while exaggerated covers general overstatement.
  • overblown
    5.7 2.05/m
    overblown sounds pompous and dramatic in speech, while exaggerated is a neutral word for something made larger than true.
  • overdone
    3.9 0.00/m
    overdone feels theatrical or too intense in style, while exaggerated is a general term for something shown as larger than it is.
  • magnified
    4.0 0.05/m
    magnified often means made to look physically larger through a lens or detail, while exaggerated means made seem greater or more important.
