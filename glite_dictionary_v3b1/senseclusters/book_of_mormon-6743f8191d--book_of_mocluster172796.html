Book of Mormon

proper noun
sacred text of the Latter-day Saint movement
Pronunciation: / ˌbʊk əv ˈmɔrmən / (AmE), / ˌbʊk əv ˈmɔːmən / (BrE)
the sacred text of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, said to have been revealed to Joseph Smith in 1830 and to record the history of ancient peoples of the Americas

Examples

The Book of Mormon scripture
Many missionaries of the LDS Church carry the Book of Mormon with them while traveling.
She spent the afternoon reading passages from the Book of Mormon.
The Book of Mormon was first published in the United States in 1830.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bible - Christian sacred text traditionally divided into Old and New Testaments
  • synonym: Qur'an - The Islamic sacred scripture
