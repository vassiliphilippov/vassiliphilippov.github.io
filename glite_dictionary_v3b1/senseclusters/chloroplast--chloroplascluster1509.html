chloroplast

noun
cell structure for photosynthesis
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːrəˌplæst / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəʊˌplɑːst / (BrE)
A cellular structure in plants and algae that contains chlorophyll and other pigments and is the site of photosynthesis.

Examples

Plant chloroplast
Researchers studied the structure of the chloroplast under a powerful microscope.
The chloroplast is responsible for converting sunlight into chemical energy in plant cells.
Without chloroplasts, plants would not be able to produce their own food through photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plastid - any of a class of small organelles in plants, including chloroplasts
