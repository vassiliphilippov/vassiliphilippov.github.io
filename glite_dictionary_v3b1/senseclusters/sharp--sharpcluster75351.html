sharp

adjective
ending in a fine or pointed edge
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrp / (AmE), / ʃɑːp / (BrE)
having an edge or point that can cut or pierce easily; ending in a fine point or edge

Examples

Sharp knife
Be careful with that sharp knife—it could easily cut you.
She picked up a sharp piece of glass from the floor.
The pencil has a sharp tip after being freshly sharpened.
Be careful with that sharp knife when you are slicing vegetables.
His tools are always kept sharp for precise woodworking.
The pencil had a sharp point, perfect for drawing fine lines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blunt - having a dull or rounded edge, not sharp
  • synonym: pointed - having a tip or end that tapers to a point
