residua

noun
statistical differences
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪdjuə / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪdjʊə / (BrE)
the differences between observed and predicted values in a statistical model, used to assess how well the model fits the data

Examples

Residua in regression
The statistician plotted the residua to check for any patterns in the regression analysis.
Large residua may indicate that the model does not fit the data well.
Researchers examined the residua to ensure the assumptions of the statistical model were met.
