sail

verb
travel on water by wind power
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /seɪl/ (AmE), /seɪl/ (BrE)
to travel on water in a boat or ship, especially one that uses sails, but also by other means

Examples

To sail a boat
The crew learned how to sail before embarking on their journey.
They decided to sail across the lake in their small boat.
We watched the boats sail into the harbor at sunset.
The ship will sail across the Atlantic next month.
They have sailed from France to Spain many times.
We plan to sail down the river at sunrise.
She sailed solo around the world in record time.
They plan to sail across the Mediterranean this summer.
We watched the tall ships sail into the harbor at dawn.
