keep down

verb
suppress or control by force
3.6 2.37/m
Pronunciation: / kip da än / (AmE), / kiÀêp da än / (BrE)
to prevent something or someone from increasing, rising, or becoming more successful; to limit or control

Examples

To keep down costs
Strict rules were imposed to keep down the workers' demands.
The military tried to keep down protests by deploying troops in the city.
The regime used violence to keep down any opposition.
Extra police were hired to keep down violence at the festival.
Strict laws were introduced to keep down the number of accidents on the road.
They are trying to keep down costs as much as possible this year.

Synonyms

  • hold down
    3.2 0.85/m
    hold down restrains something so it stays at a low or steady level, while keep down focuses on stopping increases.
  • hold the line
    5.1 0.16/m
    hold the line keeps prices or spending at the current level, while keep down has broader use to limit rises.
  • bring down
    3.0 4.71/m
    bring down lowers a level or rate actively, while keep down often means stopping or preventing something from rising.
  • reduce
    2.1 18/m
    reduce makes something smaller or weaker in amount or power, while keep down stresses preventing increases or success.
  • hold back
    3.2 7.12/m
    hold back stops progress or movement forward, while keep down is about limiting growth or preventing things from rising.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - give support, confidence, or hope to (opposite sense)
  • antonym: increase - to become larger in number or amount
  • synonym: limit - to restrict the size, amount, or number of something
  • synonym: reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount or degree
  • synonym: subdue - bring under control, especially by force
  • synonym: suppress - forcibly put an end to something
