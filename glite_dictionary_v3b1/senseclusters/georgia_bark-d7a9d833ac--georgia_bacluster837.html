Georgia bark

noun
shrub/tree with medicinal bark, southeastern US
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔrdʒə bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒə bɑːk / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the southeastern United States, especially found in swampy areas, with pink or white sepals, whose bark is used medicinally to treat fever

Examples

Georgia bark extract
Extracts from Georgia bark were historically important in traditional Southern medicine.
The Georgia bark has long been valued for its medicinal properties, particularly as a remedy for fevers.
We identified several Georgia bark trees thriving near the edge of the swamp.
