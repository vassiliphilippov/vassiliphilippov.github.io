lanista

noun
gladiator trainer
Pronunciation: / ləˈnistə / (AmE), / laˈniːstə / (BrE)
a person who trained and managed gladiators in ancient Rome

Examples

The lanista trained gladiators
The lanista was responsible for selecting and training new gladiators for the arena.
In ancient Rome, a successful lanista could become quite wealthy and influential.
The emperor visited the lanista to observe the fighters' progress before the games.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gladiator - a person trained to fight in public contests in ancient Rome
  • synonym: trainer - a person who teaches or prepares others for a sport or activity
