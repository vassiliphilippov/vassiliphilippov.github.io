underspin

verb
to apply backspin
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌspɪn / (BrE)
to hit or strike a ball so that it spins backward, making it bounce lower or drop quickly

Examples

To underspin a shot
The tennis player learned to underspin the ball to make it bounce lower on the opponent's side.
In table tennis, beginners often struggle to underspin their serves effectively.
He managed to underspin the shot, causing the ball to drop just over the net.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backspin - to hit a ball so it spins backward
  • antonym: topspin - to hit a ball so it spins forward
