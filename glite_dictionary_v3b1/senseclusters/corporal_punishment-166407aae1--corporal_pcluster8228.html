corporal punishment

noun
physical punishment as a penalty for a crime or discipline
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːr.pɚ.əl ˈpʌn.ɪʃ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pər.əl ˈpʌn.ɪʃ.mənt / (BrE)
the physical punishment of a person, especially by beating, as a penalty for a crime or as discipline

Examples

Corporal punishment in schools
Debates continue over whether corporal punishment should be allowed in schools or courts.
In some countries, corporal punishment remains a legal form of penalty.
The judge sentenced him to corporal punishment for his crime.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: capital punishment - the legally authorized killing of someone as punishment for a crime
  • degree lower: punishment - the act of penalizing someone for an offense or crime
