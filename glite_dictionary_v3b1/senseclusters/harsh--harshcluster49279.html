harsh

adjective
unpleasantly severe or demanding
2.3
Pronunciation: / hɑrʃ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ / (BrE)
Unpleasantly severe or demanding, especially of environments, conditions, or treatment.

Examples

Harsh winter
Many found the winter in the mountains too harsh to endure.
The desert is a harsh environment with little water or shade.
The judge's sentence was criticized as unusually harsh for such a minor offense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentle - mild or soft in effect; not harsh or severe
  • synonym: severe - very great; intense or serious in degree or harshness
