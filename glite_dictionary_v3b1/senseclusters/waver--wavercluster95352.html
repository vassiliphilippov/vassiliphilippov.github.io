waver

verb
to pause in uncertainty or be indecisive
6.1 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈweɪ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈweɪ.və/ (BrE)
to be indecisive or show uncertainty, especially when making a decision or maintaining a position

Examples

To waver between choices
His confidence seemed to waver as he listened to the arguments.
She began to waver when faced with such a difficult decision.
The committee may waver on its final recommendation until more evidence is presented.
Even under pressure, she did not waver from her beliefs.
His voice wavered as he tried to explain his reasoning.
She began to waver when asked to make a difficult decision.
Despite criticism from others, she did not waver in her beliefs.
He began to waver when faced with difficult choices.
The government must not waver in its commitment to the plan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - make a choice or come to a conclusion
  • synonym: hesitate - to pause before saying or doing something, often due to uncertainty
  • antonym: persist - continue firmly in an opinion or course of action
