Corallorhiza trifida

noun
species of orchid with pale stems and flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɔːˌræləˈraɪzə traɪˈfɪdə / (AmE), / kɒˌraləˈraɪzə traɪˈfɪdə / (BrE)
A species of orchid with nearly leafless, pale yellowish to greenish stems and similarly colored flowers, found from northern New Mexico to Alaska.

Examples

Corallorhiza trifida orchid
Researchers documented several clusters of Corallorhiza trifida during their botanical survey of the Alaskan forest.
The rare Corallorhiza trifida can often be found growing in damp woodlands at higher elevations.
Unlike most orchids, Corallorhiza trifida lacks true leaves and obtains nutrients from decaying matter.
