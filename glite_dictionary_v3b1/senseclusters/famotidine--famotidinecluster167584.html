famotidine

noun
drug for ulcers and acid reflux
6.6
Pronunciation: /fə.ˈmoʊ.tɪ.diːn/ /; / /fə.ˈmoʊ.tə.diːn/ (AmE), /fə.ˈməʊ.tɪ.diːn/ /; / /fə.ˈməʊ.tə.diːn/ (BrE)
A drug that reduces stomach acid production, used to treat peptic ulcers, gastritis, and acid reflux.

Examples

Famotidine tablets
If you experience heartburn frequently, your physician might recommend famotidine tablets.
Many patients take famotidine as part of their treatment for stomach ulcers.
The doctor prescribed famotidine to help manage her acid reflux symptoms.
