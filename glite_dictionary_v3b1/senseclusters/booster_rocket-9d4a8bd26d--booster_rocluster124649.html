booster rocket

noun
first stage of a multistage rocket
5.3 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbuÀêst…ôr Àår…ëÀêk…™t / (AmE), / ÀàbuÀêst…ô Àår…ík…™t / (BrE)
a rocket providing the initial thrust in a multistage launch vehicle, after which it is jettisoned when its fuel is spent

Examples

The booster rocket stage
After its fuel was spent, the booster rocket was jettisoned from the main spacecraft.
Every successful launch depends on reliable booster rockets to provide the initial thrust.
The space shuttle used a pair of solid-fuel booster rockets to gain enough speed for orbit.

Synonyms

  • booster
    5.8 1.57/m
    booster is the common short name for the first rocket stage, while booster rocket is the full technical name used in descriptions.
  • booster unit
    5.8 0.00/m
    booster unit sounds technical and names the stage as a component, while booster rocket names the rocket stage thrown away after use.
  • takeoff booster
    7.3 0.00/m
    takeoff booster highlights liftoff power for leaving the ground, while booster rocket refers to the stage later dropped when empty.
  • takeoff rocket
    6.7 0.00/m
    takeoff rocket suggests the rocket used at lift-off, while booster rocket specifically means the first stage that is jettisoned after burning fuel.
