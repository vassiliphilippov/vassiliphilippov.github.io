overdosage

noun
excessive amount
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.doʊ.sɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.dəʊ.sɪdʒ/ (BrE)
the act or result of taking or receiving too much of a substance, especially a drug or medicine, in a way that can cause harm

Examples

An overdosage of medicine
An overdosage of vitamin A can lead to serious health problems if taken for weeks.
The doctor warned that overdosage of the medication could result in dangerous side effects.
Careful measurement is important to avoid overdosage when giving medicine to children.
The doctor warned that overdosage of this medication could cause serious side effects.
Accidental overdosage is a common reason for emergency room visits involving prescription drugs.
Clear instructions help prevent overdosage when patients take their medicine at home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overdose - an amount of a drug or substance that is too large and may cause harm
  • antonym: underdosage - an insufficient amount of a substance, especially a drug
