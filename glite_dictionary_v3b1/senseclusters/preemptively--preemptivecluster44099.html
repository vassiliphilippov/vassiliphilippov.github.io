preemptively

adverb
to prevent in advance
6.3
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈempt.ɪv.liː/ /; / /prɪ.ˈempt.ɪv.liː/ (AmE), /priː.ˈempt.ɪv.liː/ /; / /prɪ.ˈempt.ɪv.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that acts before something happens, especially to prevent it or gain an advantage

Examples

Preemptively solve problems
The company acted preemptively to address customer complaints before they became widespread.
He apologized preemptively to avoid any misunderstanding about his intentions.
They closed the park preemptively because of the approaching storm.
The government acted preemptively to stop the spread of the disease.
She preemptively apologized before anyone could complain about her mistake.
The team preemptively addressed potential issues before launching the new product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proactively - in a way that takes action before problems arise
  • antonym: reactively - in a way that responds to events after they happen
