Caledonian

adjective
of Caledonian orogeny
Pronunciation: / ˌkælɪˈdoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈdəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the Caledonian orogeny or to geological features formed during that ancient mountain-building period in the Paleozoic era

Examples

Caledonian rocks
Geologists studied the Caledonian rocks to understand the region's ancient tectonic history.
The Caledonian mountains stretch across parts of Scotland, Norway, and Ireland.
Many mineral deposits in Europe are associated with Caledonian geological activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hercynian - relating to the Hercynian orogeny, another ancient mountain-building period
