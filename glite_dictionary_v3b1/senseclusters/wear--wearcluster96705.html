wear

verb
exhaust or tire out
Pronunciation: / wɛr / (AmE), / wɛə / (BrE)
to exhaust or tire (someone or oneself) through overuse, great strain, or stress

Examples

To wear yourself out
He didn't want to wear himself out before the big race.
She was worn down by years of hard work and little rest.
The constant noise from the construction site began to wear on the residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhaust - to make someone very tired
  • antonym: refresh - to make someone less tired or restore energy
