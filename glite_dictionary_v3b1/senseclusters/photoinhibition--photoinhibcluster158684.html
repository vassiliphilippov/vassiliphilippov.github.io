photoinhibition

noun
reduced photosynthesis
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊɪnɪˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊɪnɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
a reduction in a plant's ability to photosynthesize caused by exposure to excessive light

Examples

Photoinhibition in plants
Prolonged exposure to intense sunlight can lead to photoinhibition in many plant species.
Researchers study photoinhibition to understand how plants adapt to changing light conditions.
Greenhouse managers use shading techniques to prevent photoinhibition in sensitive crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photosynthesis - the process by which plants use sunlight to make food
