aged

adjective
worn by erosion (of rocks/land)
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒd / (AmE), / eɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of rock or land) at an advanced stage of erosion, having been worn down by natural processes over a long period.

Examples

Aged rock
An aged hillside reveals different layers of sediment deposited over millennia.
Geologists examined the aged limestone formations for signs of past river currents.
The aged rocks of the canyon have unique patterns from centuries of erosion.
