unearned run

noun
baseball: run due to defensive error
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɝnd rʌn / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːnd rʌn / (BrE)
(in baseball) a run that is scored because of a defensive error, rather than earned by the batter's skillful play.

Examples

An unearned run
Because of sloppy fielding, the team allowed two unearned runs in the fifth inning.
His statistics look worse than they are because most of the unearned runs weren't his fault.
The pitcher gave up four unearned runs after the shortstop made a costly error.

Related Terms

  • antonym: earned run - a run that scores without benefit of a defensive error or passed ball
