multicore

adjective
with multiple conductors
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltiˌkɔr / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌkɔː / (BrE)
having several separate conductors or fibers within a single outer covering, especially in a cable or cable assembly

Examples

Multicore cable
The technician installed a multicore cable to connect all the speakers in the auditorium.
A multicore wire is often used in professional audio setups for convenience and organization.
They replaced the old cables with a new multicore system to improve reliability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-core - having only one conductor or fiber inside the covering
