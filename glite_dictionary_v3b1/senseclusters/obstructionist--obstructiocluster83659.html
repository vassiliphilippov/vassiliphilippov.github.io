obstructionist

noun
someone who deliberately blocks progress
Pronunciation: / əbˈstrʌk.ʃə.nɪst / (AmE), / əbˈstrʌk.ʃə.nɪst / (BrE)
a person who deliberately delays or prevents progress, especially of legislative or organizational action

Examples

An obstructionist delays progress
Despite the urgent need for reform, one obstructionist managed to delay the vote for months.
She accused her colleagues of being obstructionists rather than offering constructive criticism.
The new policy was opposed by a group of obstructionists in the committee.

Related Terms

  • antonym: facilitator - a person who makes action or progress easier
  • synonym: obstructor - someone who obstructs; one who blocks progress
