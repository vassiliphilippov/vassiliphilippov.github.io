plasminogen activator

noun
enzyme that initiates fibrinolysis
Pronunciation: / plæzˈmɪnədʒən ˈæktɪˌveɪtər / (AmE), / plazˈmɪnədʒən ˈaktɪˌveɪtə / (BrE)
a protease enzyme, especially produced in the kidney, that converts plasminogen to plasmin and thus initiates fibrinolysis

Examples

A plasminogen activator enzyme
Administration of a plasminogen activator is crucial in the early treatment of some types of stroke.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of a new plasminogen activator in dissolving arterial blockages.
The doctor explained how a plasminogen activator works to break down blood clots in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: urokinase - a type of plasminogen activator produced in the kidneys
