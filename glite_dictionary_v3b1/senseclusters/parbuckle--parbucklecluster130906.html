parbuckle

verb
to right with ropes
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːrˌbʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːbʌkəl / (BrE)
to roll or right a heavy object, especially a ship, using ropes, cables, or chains.

Examples

To parbuckle a ship
The crew worked together, parbuckling the stranded vessel back to an upright position.
Engineers used strong cables for parbuckling the massive shipwreck during the salvage operation.
After hours of effort, the team succeeded in parbuckling the heavy container off the dock.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capsize - to overturn a boat or ship in the water
  • synonym: hoist - to lift or raise something, especially with ropes or machinery
