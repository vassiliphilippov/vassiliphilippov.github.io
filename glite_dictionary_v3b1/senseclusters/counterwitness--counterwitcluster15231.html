counterwitness

verb
to contradict testimony
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər ˈwɪt.nəs/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.tər ˈwɪt.nɪs/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə ˈwɪt.nəs/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.tə ˈwɪt.nɪs/ (BrE)
to give testimony or evidence that directly contradicts another person's testimony or statement

Examples

To counterwitness a claim
The defense attorney called a specialist to counterwitness the claims made by the prosecution's expert.
She was asked to counterwitness during the trial, challenging the accuracy of the previous testimony.
In some cases, a reliable person may counterwitness to ensure all sides of the story are heard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corroborate - to support or confirm a statement or testimony
  • synonym: refute - to prove a statement or theory to be wrong or false
