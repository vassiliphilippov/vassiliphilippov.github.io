paralyzing

adjective
causing immobility
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpærəlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone or something to be unable to move, act, or function (literally or because of strong emotion)

Examples

Paralyzing fear
The paralyzing cold made it impossible for the hikers to continue their journey.
A paralyzing fear of public speaking kept her from attending the conference.
The strike had a paralyzing effect on the city's transportation system.
The team was struck by a paralyzing sense of fear before the final match.
She felt a paralyzing anxiety that kept her from speaking in public.
A paralyzing cold swept through the city during the unexpected snowstorm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crippling - causing severe damage or making something unable to function
  • synonym: debilitating - making someone very weak or unable to act
  • synonym: disabling - making something unable to work or function
  • antonym: empowering - giving someone the power or confidence to act
  • antonym: enabling - making something able to function or act
