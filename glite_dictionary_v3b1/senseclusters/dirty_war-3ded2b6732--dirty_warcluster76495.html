dirty war

noun
state-led campaign against insurgents, often violent
Pronunciation: / ˈdɝːti wɔr / (AmE), / ˈdɜːti wɔː / (BrE)
a campaign of violence by a regime's military or secret police against suspected insurgents, often involving kidnapping, torture, and murder of civilians

Examples

The dirty war campaign
Journalists faced significant danger while reporting on the dirty war as the government tried to suppress information.
The history book covers several dirty wars around the world, highlighting tragedies suffered by civilians.
Thousands of people were kidnapped and killed during Argentina's dirty war in the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterinsurgency - military or political action against guerrillas or revolutionaries
