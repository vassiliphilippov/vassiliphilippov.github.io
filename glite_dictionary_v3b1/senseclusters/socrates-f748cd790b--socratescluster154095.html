Socrates

proper noun
ancient Greek philosopher (c. 470–399 BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːkrətiːz / (AmE), / ˈsɒkrətiːz / (BrE)
(c. 470–399 BC) An ancient Greek philosopher, renowned for his contribution to the development of Western philosophy and as the teacher of Plato and Xenophon.

Examples

Socrates the philosopher
Socrates is famous for his method of questioning and dialogue in philosophical discussions.
Many scholars study the teachings of Socrates to understand the origins of Western philosophy.
Plato's works often feature Socrates as a central character engaging others in debates.
