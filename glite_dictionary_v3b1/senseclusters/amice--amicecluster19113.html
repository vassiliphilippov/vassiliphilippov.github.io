amice

noun
clerical neck cloth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæmɪs / (AmE), / ˈæmɪs / (BrE)
a white cloth worn around the neck and shoulders by some Christian clergy during religious services

Examples

Priest's amice
The priest placed the amice carefully around his shoulders before the service.
During the ceremony, the bishop adjusted his amice to sit more comfortably.
The church's vestment room contained several freshly laundered amices for the clergy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alb - a long white robe worn by clergy in Christian churches
  • synonym: chasuble - a sleeveless outer vestment worn by clergy during Mass
