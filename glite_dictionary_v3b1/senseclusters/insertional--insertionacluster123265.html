insertional

adjective
at point of attachment
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɜrʃənl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːʃənl / (BrE)
relating to the place where a muscle, tendon, or ligament attaches to a bone in the body

Examples

Insertional tendon pain
The patient was diagnosed with insertional Achilles tendinopathy after months of heel pain.
Physical therapy focused on reducing insertional pain at the elbow joint.
Doctors often see insertional injuries in athletes who overuse certain muscle groups.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insertion - the point where a muscle, tendon, or ligament attaches to bone
  • antonym: origin - the fixed attachment point of a muscle
