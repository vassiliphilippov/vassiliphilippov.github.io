subjectify

verb
to make subjective
8.4
Pronunciation: /səb.ˈdʒek.tɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈsʌb.dʒek.tɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /səb.ˈdʒek.tɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈsʌb.dʒek.tɪ.faɪ/ (BrE)
to interpret, present, or treat something based on personal feelings, opinions, or individual perspective rather than objective facts

Examples

To subjectify information
It is important not to subjectify scientific data when presenting research findings.
The critic warned that the author tended to subjectify historical events in her writing.
When we subjectify our experiences, we may overlook important facts.
