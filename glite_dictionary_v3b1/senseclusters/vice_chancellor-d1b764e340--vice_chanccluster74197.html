vice chancellor

noun
deputy to a chancellor
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪs ˌtʃæn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪs ˌtʃɑːn.səl.ə / (BrE)
A deputy or assistant official who acts as the subordinate to the chancellor, often in universities or certain organizations.

Examples

The vice chancellor leads
After the chancellor resigned, the vice chancellor temporarily took charge of the university.
She was promoted from dean to vice chancellor within five years.
The vice chancellor addressed the faculty during the annual meeting.
