transient global amnesia

noun
brief episode of memory loss and confusion
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænziənt ɡloʊbəl æmˈniʒə / (AmE), / ˈtrænziənt ˈɡləʊbəl amˈniːzɪə / (BrE)
A sudden, temporary episode of memory loss and confusion, usually lasting several hours, in which the person remains alert and otherwise mentally functional.

Examples

Sudden transient global amnesia
After recovering from transient global amnesia, he had no recollection of the events from earlier that day.
During an episode of transient global amnesia, the patient repeatedly asked the same questions without realizing it.
The neurologist diagnosed her condition as transient global amnesia after she experienced several hours of memory loss.

  • synonym: amnesia - partial or total loss of memory
