foliot

noun
clock regulating bar
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊliət / (AmE), / ˈfəʊliət / (BrE)
a horizontal bar with adjustable weights used to regulate the movement of early mechanical clocks

Examples

A foliot bar
The ancient tower clock was controlled by a foliot instead of a pendulum.
Historians restored the old mechanism, carefully balancing the foliot to keep accurate time.
Before the invention of the pendulum, the foliot was essential in early European clocks.
