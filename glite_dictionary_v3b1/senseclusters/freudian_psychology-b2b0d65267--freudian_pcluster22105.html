Freudian psychology

noun
theories of Sigmund Freud in psychology
Pronunciation: / frɔɪdiən saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / frɔɪdiən saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the body of psychological theories developed by Sigmund Freud, focusing on the unconscious mind, psychoanalysis, and the role of childhood experiences in shaping behavior

Examples

Freudian psychology theories
He gave a lecture explaining the basic theories of Freudian psychology.
Her research explores the influence of Freudian psychology on twentieth-century art.
Many aspects of Freudian psychology have been challenged by modern psychologists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: psychoanalysis - a method of treating mental disorders by investigating the interaction of conscious and unconscious elements in the mind
