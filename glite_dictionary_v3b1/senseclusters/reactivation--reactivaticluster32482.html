reactivation

noun
making active again
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something active again after it has been inactive or paused

Examples

Reactivation of service
The reactivation of the old railway line brought new business to the town.
After a long pause, the reactivation of the project was announced by the manager.
Scientists studied the reactivation of dormant viruses in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deactivation - the act of making something inactive or stopping its operation
  • synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original state
