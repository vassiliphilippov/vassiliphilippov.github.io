withdrawal

noun
formal separation from an alliance or federation
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈdrɔəl / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːəl / (BrE)
the act of formally leaving, stopping participation, or taking back involvement in an organization, activity, or commitment

Examples

Withdrawal from a group
Britain's withdrawal from the European Union became official after years of negotiations.
Following months of heated debate, the parliament voted in favor of withdrawal from the alliance.
The country's withdrawal from the political union caused widespread concern among its neighbors.
After her withdrawal from the committee, the group struggled to make decisions.
His sudden withdrawal from the tournament surprised everyone.
The club announced the withdrawal of its team from the league.
Her sudden withdrawal from the competition surprised everyone involved.
The senator’s withdrawal of his earlier remarks came after public criticism.
There was an official withdrawal of support for the proposed policy.

  • synonym: resignation - the act of formally leaving a job or position

  • synonym: secession - the action of withdrawing formally from membership in a federation or body
