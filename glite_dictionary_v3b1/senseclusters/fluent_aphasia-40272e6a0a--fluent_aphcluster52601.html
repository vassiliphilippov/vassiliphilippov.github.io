fluent aphasia

noun
type of aphasia with fluent but meaningless speech
8.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààflu…ônt …ôÀàfe…™ í…ô / (AmE), / ÀàfluÀê…ônt …ôÀàfe…™zi…ô / (BrE)
A medical condition in which speech remains fluent but is often nonsensical, and the person's ability to understand spoken or written language is severely impaired.

Examples

Fluent aphasia speech
Despite his fluent aphasia, he could speak in long sentences that made little sense to listeners.
Her diagnosis of fluent aphasia explained why her speech was effortless but often confusing.
Patients with fluent aphasia often have difficulty understanding even simple questions.

Synonyms

  • receptive aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    receptive aphasia involves fluent but meaningless speech and poor understanding, while fluent aphasia names that general pattern of language loss.
  • Wernicke's aphasia
    9.5 0.01/m
    Wernicke's aphasia is the clinical name for a brain injury that causes fluent, meaningless speech, while fluent aphasia is the general description.
  • sensory aphasia
    9.2 0.01/m
    sensory aphasia is an older term used by doctors to mean fluent but unintelligible speech, while fluent aphasia is the modern descriptive label.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Wernicke's aphasia - A type of aphasia characterized by fluent but nonsensical speech and impaired comprehension
  • antonym: nonfluent aphasia - A type of aphasia with halting, effortful speech and relatively better comprehension
