refractory period

noun
period after neural or muscle activation when unresponsive
7.7 6.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktəri ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktəri ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
The period immediately following the firing of a neuron or the contraction of a muscle fiber during which it is unresponsive to further stimulation.

Examples

The refractory period after firing
After firing an action potential, the neuron enters a refractory period and cannot be stimulated again immediately.
During the refractory period, a second muscle contraction is impossible no matter how strong the stimulus.
The length of the refractory period can affect how quickly a nerve cell can fire again.
