well-appointed

adjective
having full and attractive furnishings or equipment
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛl əˈpɔɪntɪd / (AmE), / ˌwɛl əˈpɔɪntɪd / (BrE)
having all the necessary and attractive equipment, furniture, or features; well furnished and equipped

Examples

A well-appointed room

Her well-appointed study is perfect for working from home.
The hotel offers well-appointed suites with beautiful views of the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sparse - thinly dispersed or arranged, with few furnishings or features
