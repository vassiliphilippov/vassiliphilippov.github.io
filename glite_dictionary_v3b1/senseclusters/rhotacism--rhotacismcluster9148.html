rhotacism

noun
difficulty with 'r' sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊtəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəsɪzəm / (BrE)
a speech disorder in which a person has difficulty pronouncing the 'r' sound correctly

Examples

Childhood rhotacism problem
The speech therapist helped the boy overcome his rhotacism before he started school.
Children with rhotacism may substitute the 'r' sound with a 'w' sound.
After months of practice, her rhotacism became much less noticeable.
