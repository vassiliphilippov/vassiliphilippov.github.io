safety valve

noun
device that releases excess pressure automatically
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti ˌvælv / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ˌvɑːlv / (BrE)
A device on a container or system that automatically releases pressure when it becomes too high, to prevent accidents or explosions.

Examples

Safety valve on a boiler
Engineers check the safety valve regularly to maintain safe operating conditions.
The safety valve released steam automatically when the pressure in the boiler became too high.
Without a functioning safety valve, the system could explode under excess pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pressure relief valve - a valve that relieves pressure, especially in engineering contexts
