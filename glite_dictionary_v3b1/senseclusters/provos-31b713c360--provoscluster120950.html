Provos

noun
militant Irish nationalist group (the Provisional IRA)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊvoʊz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊvəʊz / (BrE)
A militant Irish nationalist organization, formally known as the Provisional Irish Republican Army, that used terrorism and guerrilla warfare to drive British forces from Northern Ireland and achieve Irish unification.

Examples

The Provos organization
Many considered the Provos to be the most formidable paramilitary group during the Troubles.
Negotiations often stalled due to actions taken by the Provos against British targets.
The British government increased security in Belfast in response to attacks by the Provos.
