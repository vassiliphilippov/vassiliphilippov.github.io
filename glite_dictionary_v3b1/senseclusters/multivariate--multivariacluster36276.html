multivariate

adjective
involving two or more variables
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈveriət / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈveəriət / (BrE)
involving or referring to two or more variables, especially in statistical or mathematical analysis

Examples

A multivariate analysis
A multivariate approach provides a more comprehensive view than a single-variable study.
The researchers used a multivariate analysis to examine the combined effects of several factors.
You need advanced software to perform multivariate data analysis effectively.

Related Terms

  • antonym: univariate - involving only one variable
