chop

noun
tennis return with downward motion and backspin
9.0 4.1
Pronunciation: /tʃɑːp/ (AmE), /tʃɒp/ (BrE)
a sports move made by striking the ball with a downward or sharp motion, producing spin or a high bounce

Examples

A tennis chop
He practiced the chop to improve his defensive shots on the court.
Her wicked backhand chop made the ball spin away from her rival.
The player surprised his opponent with a clever chop that barely cleared the net.
A well-placed chop can catch infielders off guard during a game.
The batter hit a chop toward third base, giving him enough time to reach first safely.
With a lucky chop, the runner made it to second base before the throw.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grounder - a ball that is hit and travels along the ground
  • synonym: slice - a tennis stroke that puts sidespin and a little backspin on the ball
