left ventricle

noun
heart chamber pumping blood into the aorta
Pronunciation: / lɛft ˈvɛntrɪkəl / (AmE), / lɛft ˈvɛntrɪkl̩ / (BrE)
the chamber on the left side of the heart that receives oxygenated blood from the left atrium and pumps it into the aorta

Examples

The left ventricle chamber
Doctors found that John had a thickened left ventricle due to long-term high blood pressure.
During heart surgery, the left ventricle must be closely monitored to prevent complications.
The left ventricle is responsible for pumping oxygen-rich blood to the entire body.
