metabasis

noun
rhetorical transition
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈtæbəsɪs / (AmE), / mɛˈtæbəsɪs / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which a speaker or writer transitions from one subject to another

Examples

Metabasis in a speech
The lecturer used metabasis to guide the audience from the introduction to the main argument.
In her essay, a well-placed metabasis helped connect two seemingly unrelated topics.
A skilled speaker employs metabasis to maintain the flow of a complex presentation.

  • synonym: segue - a smooth transition from one topic or section to another
