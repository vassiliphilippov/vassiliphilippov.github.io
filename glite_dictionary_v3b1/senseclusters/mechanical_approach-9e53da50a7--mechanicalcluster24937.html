mechanical approach

noun
rigid, formulaic method or thinking
3.1
Pronunciation: / məˈkænɪkəl əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəl əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a way of thinking or solving problems that is rigid, routine, and lacks creativity or flexibility

Examples

A mechanical approach to teaching
Her mechanical approach to teaching left little room for student input or discussion.
Relying on a mechanical approach can be efficient, but it may miss innovative solutions.
The team's mechanical approach to negotiations failed to address the nuances of the situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creative approach - an approach involving originality and imagination
  • synonym: rigid approach - an approach that is inflexible or unyielding
