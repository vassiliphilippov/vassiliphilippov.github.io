cancan

noun
lively French chorus line dance
Pronunciation: / ˈkænˌkæn / (AmE), / ˈkæn.kæn / (BrE)
a lively, high-kicking dance of French origin performed by a female chorus line, typically characterized by energetic movements and kicking up the legs

Examples

The cancan dance
Dancers trained for months to perfect the precise kicks of the cancan.
Many Parisian cabarets still feature the traditional cancan in their shows.
The famous Moulin Rouge is renowned for its energetic cancan performances.
