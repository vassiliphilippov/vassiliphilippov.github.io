changeableness

noun
quality of being likely to change
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪndʒəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒəblnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being likely or quick to change; tendency to change frequently or unpredictably

Examples

Economic changeableness
Economic changeableness can lead to uncertainty in the markets.
She admired the changeableness of his moods, finding spontaneity in his unpredictability.
The changeableness of the weather makes it hard to plan outdoor events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: changeability - the quality or state of being changeable
  • antonym: stability - the quality of being stable or unchanging
