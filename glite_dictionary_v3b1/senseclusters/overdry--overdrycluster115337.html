overdry

verb
to dry excessively
5.4
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈdraɪ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.draɪ/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈdraɪ/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.draɪ/ (BrE)
to make something too dry or to become too dry by removing too much moisture

Examples

To overdry bread
If you overdry the bread in the oven, it will become hard and tasteless.
Be careful not to overdry your skin by using harsh soaps every day.
The artist warned that the paint would crack if it was allowed to overdry on the canvas.
If you overdry the cake, it will become crumbly and lose its flavor.
Be careful not to overdry your clothes in the dryer or they may shrink.
The artist warned that too much heat would overdry the clay and cause it to crack.
If you overdry the laundry, the clothes may shrink or feel rough.
Be careful not to overdry the paint, or it could start to crack.
The recipe warns not to overdry the chicken in the oven.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dry out - to lose all moisture and become dry
  • antonym: moisten - to make or become slightly wet
