Niccolò Machiavelli

proper noun
Italian political philosopher and writer (1469–1527)
8.6
Pronunciation: / niˈkoʊloʊ ˌmɑːkiəˈvɛli / (AmE), / niˈkɒləʊ ˌmækiəˈvɛli / (BrE)
(1469–1527) An Italian political philosopher, writer, and statesman best known for his works on political theory, especially 'The Prince.'

Examples

Niccolò Machiavelli the philosopher
Niccolò Machiavelli is often cited as the 'father of modern political science.'

The term 'Machiavellian' is derived from the name Niccolò Machiavelli and his ideas about political strategy.
