thylakoid

noun
photosynthetic membrane sac
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈθaɪ.lə.kɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈθaɪ.lə.kɔɪd/ (BrE)
a membrane-bound sac inside chloroplasts (or photosynthetic bacteria) where the light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis happen

Examples

The thylakoid membrane
The thylakoid membranes contain chlorophyll, which captures light energy for photosynthesis.
Scientists studied the structure of the thylakoid to better understand how plants convert sunlight into energy.
In algae, the thylakoid system is arranged differently than in higher plants.
The thylakoid membranes contain chlorophyll, which absorbs sunlight for photosynthesis.
Stacks of thylakoids are called grana in plant cells.
Scientists study the structure of the thylakoid to understand how plants convert light into energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chloroplast - an organelle in plant cells where photosynthesis occurs
  • antonym: stroma - the fluid-filled space surrounding thylakoids in chloroplasts
