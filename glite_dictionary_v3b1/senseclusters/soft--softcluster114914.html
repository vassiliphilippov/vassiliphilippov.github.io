soft

adjective
voiced speech sound
7.4
Pronunciation: / sɑft / (AmE), / sɒft / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords; voiced.

Examples

Soft sound
In linguistics, the soft sound of 'b' distinguishes it from the hard, unvoiced 'p'.
Russian has both hard and soft consonants, depending on how the vocal cords are used.
The letter 'z' is a soft consonant because it is pronounced with the vocal cords vibrating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: voiced - (of a speech sound) produced with vibration of the vocal cords
  • antonym: voiceless - (of a speech sound) produced without vibration of the vocal cords
