realize

verb
become fully aware of
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈriːəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈrɪəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become aware of or understand something clearly, sometimes suddenly

Examples

Suddenly realize a mistake
As soon as I realized my mistake, I apologized.
He didn't realize how much work the project would require.
It took her a while to realize that her wallet was missing.
It took me a while to realize what he really meant.
She didn't realize how much the project meant to the team.
Suddenly, I realized that I had forgotten my keys.
He realised too late that he had left his wallet at home.
I didn't realise you were coming to the party as well.
It took her a moment to realise that everyone was looking at her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to stop remembering or fail to be aware of something
  • synonym: grasp - to comprehend or understand fully
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • antonym: misunderstand - fail to interpret or understand something correctly
  • synonym: realize - to become fully aware of or understand (US spelling)
  • synonym: recognize - to identify something as already known
  • synonym: understand - to grasp the meaning or significance of something
