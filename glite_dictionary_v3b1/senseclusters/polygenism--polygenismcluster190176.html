polygenism

noun
multiple independent origins
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪdʒəˌnɪzəm /; / pəˈlɪdʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdʒɛnɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that something (such as a species, language, or humans) originated independently in multiple places or from several sources

Examples

Polygenism theory
The debate between polygenism and monogenism shaped early studies of human origins.
Linguists sometimes use polygenism to explain the development of similar words in unrelated languages.
Some anthropologists rejected polygenism in favor of a single origin theory for modern humans.
In the nineteenth century, polygenism was debated by scientists studying human origins.
Some early anthropologists supported polygenism to explain differences among human populations.
The idea of polygenism has been largely rejected by modern genetic research.
