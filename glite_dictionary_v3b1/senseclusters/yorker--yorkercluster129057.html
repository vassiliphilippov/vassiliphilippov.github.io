yorker

noun
cricket delivery at feet
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈjɔrkər / (AmE), / ˈjɔːkə / (BrE)
a ball in cricket that is bowled so it lands at or near the batsman's feet, making it hard to hit

Examples

A fast yorker ball
The fast bowler delivered a perfect yorker that knocked over the stumps.
It is difficult for batsmen to score runs off a well-placed yorker.
He practiced bowling yorkers to improve his accuracy in the final overs.
The bowler surprised the batsman with a perfect yorker in the final over.
It is difficult to score runs off a well-executed yorker.
He practiced bowling yorkers to improve his accuracy at the end of the match.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bouncer - a cricket ball bowled short so it bounces high towards the batsman
  • antonym: full toss - a cricket ball bowled so it reaches the batsman without bouncing
