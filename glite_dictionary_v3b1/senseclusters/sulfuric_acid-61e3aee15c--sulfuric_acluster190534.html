sulfuric acid

noun
a strong, corrosive chemical acid
1.2 3.5
Pronunciation: / sʌlˈfjʊrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sʌlˈfjʊərɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a highly corrosive, strong acid (H₂SO₄) used extensively in industry and laboratory processes

Examples

Sulfuric acid in batteries
Sulfuric acid is used in car batteries to help generate electricity.
Industrial plants must handle sulfuric acid with care due to its corrosive properties.
The chemist accidentally spilled a few drops of sulfuric acid on the lab bench.

The laboratory keeps sulphuric acid stored in a special container due to its corrosive nature.
