call

noun

1.0 17/m

the sound made by a person or animal to attract attention or communicate

Examples

A bird's call
Some birds have a warning call to alert others of danger.
The owl's haunting call echoed through the forest at night.
We heard the distinctive call of a robin in the early morning.
A sudden call rang out from the back of the crowded hall, stopping the speaker mid-sentence.
Several angry calls erupted during the heated debate at the town meeting.
The politician ignored the call for resignation coming from the protestors outside.

Synonyms

  • shout
    1.0 9.62/m
    shout is a loud call used to grab attention or show strong feeling, while call is any sound to communicate.
  • cry
    1.0 12/m
    cry is a loud sound that often shows emotion or pain, while call is a neutral sound to attract attention.
  • yell
    1.0 2.46/m
    yell is a very loud, sharp shout usually from anger or pain, while call is a general sound to communicate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: silence - complete absence of sound
  • degree higher: song - the musical vocalization produced by birds, usually more complex than a call
