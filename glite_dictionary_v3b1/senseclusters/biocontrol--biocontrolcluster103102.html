biocontrol

noun
pest control with organisms
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊkənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊkənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the use of living organisms to control pests, diseases, or unwanted species, especially instead of using chemicals

Examples

Biocontrol using insects
Farmers are increasingly turning to biocontrol to reduce the use of chemical pesticides.
The introduction of ladybugs as a biocontrol helped manage the aphid population in the greenhouse.
Researchers developed a new biocontrol product using beneficial fungi to target crop diseases.
Farmers are increasingly turning to biocontrol to reduce their reliance on chemical pesticides.
The introduction of ladybugs for biocontrol helped manage the aphid population in the greenhouse.
Researchers are developing new biocontrol methods to combat invasive plant species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biological control - the control of pests using natural predators or agents
  • antonym: pesticide - a chemical used to kill pests
