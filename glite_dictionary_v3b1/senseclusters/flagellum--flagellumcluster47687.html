flagellum

noun
whip-like cellular appendage for movement
4.7 8.1
Pronunciation: /flə.ˈdʒe.ləm/ (AmE), /flə.ˈdʒe.ləm/ (BrE)
a long, whip-like projection from a cell used for movement, found in certain bacteria, protozoa, and sperm cells

Examples

Bacterial flagellum
Many bacteria move using a single flagellum that rotates like a propeller.
Microscopes revealed the flagellum extending from the protozoan, helping it to glide through water.
The flagellum of a sperm cell enables it to swim toward the egg.
