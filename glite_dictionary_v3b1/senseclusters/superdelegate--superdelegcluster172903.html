superdelegate

noun
unpledged party delegate
9.3
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.ˈdel.ə.gət/ (AmE), /suː.pə.ˈdel.ə.gət/ (BrE)
a party leader or official who can vote at a political convention for any candidate, without being chosen through the normal delegate selection process or being bound by primary or caucus results

Examples

A superdelegate vote
The superdelegate announced her support for the leading candidate.
Many people debated the influence of each superdelegate during the party's national convention.
Unlike regular delegates, a superdelegate is not bound by primary or caucus results.
The superdelegate announced her support for the frontrunner before the convention began.
Many voters were concerned about the influence of superdelegates in the nomination process.
Unlike regular delegates, a superdelegate is not bound by the results of the primary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delegate - a person chosen to represent others at a conference or meeting
  • antonym: pledged delegate - a delegate required to support a specific candidate based on primary or caucus results
