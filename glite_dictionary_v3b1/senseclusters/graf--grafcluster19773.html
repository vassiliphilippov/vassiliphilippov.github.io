graf

noun
German noble title
Pronunciation: / ɡrɑf / (AmE), / ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a German or Austrian noble title equivalent to a count; a nobleman of high rank

Examples

A German graf
The graf owned several large estates throughout the region.
At the royal banquet, the graf was seated near the emperor.
She married a wealthy graf and moved to Austria.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: baron - a member of the lowest rank of the nobility
  • synonym: count - a European nobleman ranking above a viscount and below a marquis
  • degree higher: duke - a nobleman of the highest hereditary rank
