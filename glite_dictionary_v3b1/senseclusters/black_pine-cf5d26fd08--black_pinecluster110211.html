black pine

noun
Japanese pine tree; ornamental, pollution-resistant
Pronunciation: / blæk paɪn / (AmE), / blak paɪn / (BrE)
A large Japanese pine tree (Pinus thunbergii) with long needles in pairs, often planted ornamentally for its resistance to salt and pollution.

Examples

Japanese black pine
In Japanese gardens, the black pine is carefully pruned to enhance its natural beauty.
Many landscapers recommend planting black pine near coastal areas because it withstands salty air.
The black pine is a popular choice for city parks due to its tolerance of urban pollution.
