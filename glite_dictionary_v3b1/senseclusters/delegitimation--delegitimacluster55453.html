delegitimation

noun
making less legitimate
9.3
Pronunciation: /də.lɪ.dʒɪ.tɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.lɪ.dʒɪ.tɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /də.lɪ.dʒɪ.tɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.lɪ.dʒɪ.tɪ.ˈmeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or state of losing legitimacy or making something or someone appear less legitimate or valid

Examples

The delegitimation of policy
The opposition's campaign focused on the delegitimation of the new policy.
Social media played a role in the delegitimation of the election results.
Critics accused the media of contributing to the delegitimation of public institutions.
The rapid delegitimation of the government led to widespread protests across the country.
Corruption scandals contributed to the delegitimation of the political party in the eyes of voters.
International observers noted the delegitimation of the election process after reports of fraud.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delegitimization - the act or process of making something seem not legitimate
  • synonym: discredit - to harm the reputation or credibility of someone or something
  • antonym: legitimation - the process of making something legitimate or accepted
