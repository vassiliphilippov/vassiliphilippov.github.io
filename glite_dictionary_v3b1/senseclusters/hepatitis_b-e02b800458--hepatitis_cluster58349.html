hepatitis B

noun
serious form of viral hepatitis
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs ˈbi / (AmE), / ˌhɛpəˈtaɪtɪs ˈbiː / (BrE)
A serious form of viral hepatitis caused by the hepatitis B virus, transmitted through blood, sexual contact, or contaminated bodily fluids; may be acute or chronic.

Examples

Hepatitis B virus
Chronic infection with hepatitis B can lead to liver cirrhosis or cancer.
Many countries recommend newborn vaccination against hepatitis B to prevent serious liver disease.
The doctor explained the risks of contracting hepatitis B through unprotected sex or sharing needles.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hepatitis A - a milder form of viral hepatitis, transmitted by ingesting contaminated food or water
  • degree higher: hepatitis C - a form of viral hepatitis that is often chronic and can lead to liver damage
