short-toed eagle

noun
large Old World bird of prey
Pronunciation: / ˌʃɔrt ˈtoʊd ˈiɡəl / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt ˈtəʊd ˈiːɡl / (BrE)
a large Old World bird of prey (Circaetus gallicus) with relatively short toes, intermediate in form between typical hawks and eagles

Examples

A short-toed eagle soaring
Birdwatchers were excited to spot a short-toed eagle soaring over the forest.
Due to habitat loss, the population of short-toed eagles has been steadily declining in some regions.
The short-toed eagle is commonly found in open habitats where it hunts snakes and other reptiles.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: eagle - large bird of prey with a massive hooked bill and long broad wings
  • degree lower: hawk - medium-sized bird of prey with broad rounded wings and a long tail
