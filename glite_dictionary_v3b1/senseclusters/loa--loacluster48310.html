loa

noun
Spanish prologue speech
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊə / (AmE), / ˈləʊə / (BrE)
a short prologue or introductory speech in a play, especially in Spanish drama, often praising someone or something

Examples

A loa before the play
Before the main performance, the actors delivered a loa to honor the king.
The tradition of including a loa at the start of Spanish plays dates back centuries.
In her research, she analyzed the themes presented in each loa of the festival.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epilogue - a concluding section at the end of a play or literary work
  • synonym: prologue - an introduction to a play or literary work
