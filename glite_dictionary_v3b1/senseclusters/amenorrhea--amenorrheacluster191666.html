amenorrhea

noun
absence of menstrual periods
9.3
Pronunciation: / əˌmɛnəˈriə /; / ˌeɪ.məˈnɔː.ri.ə / (AmE), / əˌmɛnəˈriːə /; / ˌæm.ɪ.nəˈriː.ə / (BrE)
the absence or abnormal cessation of menstrual periods in a woman of reproductive age

Examples

Primary amenorrhea
Athletes with very low body fat may experience amenorrhea as a result of intense training.
The doctor diagnosed her with amenorrhea after she missed three consecutive periods.
Treatment options for amenorrhea depend on the underlying cause of the condition.
Primary amenorrhoea can occur if a young woman has not started menstruating by age 16.
Stress and excessive exercise are known to cause amenorrhoea in some women.
The doctor diagnosed her with amenorrhoea after she missed several menstrual cycles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: menstruation - the process in a woman of discharging blood and other material from the lining of the uterus
