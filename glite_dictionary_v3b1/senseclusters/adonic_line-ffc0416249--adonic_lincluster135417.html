Adonic line

noun
verse line: dactyl + spondee/trochee

Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdɑː.nɪk laɪn/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdɒn.ɪk laɪn/ (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of a dactyl followed by a spondee or a trochee, traditionally associated with laments for Adonis in classical poetry.
