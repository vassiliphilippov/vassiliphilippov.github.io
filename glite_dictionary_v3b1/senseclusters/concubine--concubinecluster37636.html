concubine

noun
woman in an ongoing, non-marital partnership
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑŋ.kjəˌbaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋ.kjʊ.baɪn / (BrE)
a woman who lives with a man to whom she is not legally married, especially in a long-term relationship, often with lower status than a wife

Examples

The concubine lived
In ancient China, the emperor's favorite concubine wielded considerable influence at court.
She was treated as a concubine rather than a wife, never granted full status in the household.
The king had several concubines in addition to his queen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mistress - a woman in a long-term relationship with a man who is married to someone else
  • antonym: wife - a married woman joined to a man in marriage
