injuriously

adverb
in a harmful or damaging way

In a manner that causes or is likely to cause harm or injury; harmfully.

Examples

Used injuriously in speech
False statements made injuriously can damage someone's reputation.
The chemicals spread injuriously into the local water supply, endangering public health.
The company was sued for having acted injuriously toward its competitors.

Synonyms

  • harmfully
    4.3 0.06/m
    harmfully describes doing something that causes damage or pain, while injuriously sounds more formal and legal about harm.
  • deleteriously
    9.0 0.05/m
    deleteriously appears in scientific or formal texts about harm, while injuriously is a general formal adverb for causing injury.
  • detrimentally
    7.2 0.08/m
    detrimentally usually describes long-term damage or loss in results, while injuriously focuses on causing harm or injury.
  • damagingly
    4.4 0.01/m
    damagingly often describes things that leave harm or bad effects, while injuriously stresses causing injury or legal harm.
  • dangerously
    3.2 6.34/m
    dangerously means in a risky or unsafe way that can cause harm, while injuriously emphasizes actual damage or injury.
  • hazardously
    6.8 0.02/m
    hazardously refers to acting with clear risk or danger to people, while injuriously highlights causing real harm or injury.
