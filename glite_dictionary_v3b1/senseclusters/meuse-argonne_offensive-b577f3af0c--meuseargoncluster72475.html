Meuse-Argonne Offensive

proper noun
major American campaign in WWI, 1918
9.9
Pronunciation: / mjuz ɑrˈɡɑn əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / mjʊːz ˌɑːˈɡɒn əˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
a major American-led military campaign on the Western Front of World War I (1918) in which Allied forces pushed German armies back and contributed to the end of the war.

Examples

Meuse-Argonne Offensive ends the war
General Pershing commanded the Allied forces in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive, which ultimately led to the German retreat.
Many historians consider the Meuse-Argonne Offensive a turning point that hastened the end of the war.
The Meuse-Argonne Offensive was one of the largest operations involving American troops during World War I.
