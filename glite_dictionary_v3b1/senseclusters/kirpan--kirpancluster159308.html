kirpan

noun
ceremonial Sikh dagger
Pronunciation: / kɪrˈpɑn / (AmE), / kɪəˈpɑːn / (BrE)
A ceremonial curved dagger, about four inches long, that Sikh men and women are required to wear as a symbol of their faith.

Examples

A kirpan dagger
Airport security officers were unfamiliar with the significance of the kirpan.
As a Sikh, he carries his kirpan with him at all times.
The kirpan is one of the five articles of faith observed by baptized Sikhs.
