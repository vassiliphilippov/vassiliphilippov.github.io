candling

noun
inspection by light
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkændlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkændlɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of using a light to examine something, especially to check the quality or development of eggs

Examples

Candling eggs
The farmer used candling to check if the eggs were fertilized before selling them.
In the dim room, candling revealed hidden watermarks on the ancient manuscript.
Museum staff practiced candling to examine the authenticity of old documents.
The hatchery uses candling to check which eggs are developing properly.
During candling, a bright light is shone through the egg to reveal any defects.
Farmers rely on candling to ensure only healthy eggs are sold to customers.
