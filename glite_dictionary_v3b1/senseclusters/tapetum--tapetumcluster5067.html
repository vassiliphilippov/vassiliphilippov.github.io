tapetum

noun
brain fiber layer
Pronunciation: / təˈpiːtəm / (AmE), / təˈpiːtəm / (BrE)
a thin layer of fibers in the corpus callosum of the brain that covers the lateral ventricles and connects parts of the temporal lobes

Examples

Brain tapetum fibers
The tapetum is important for connecting regions of the brain involved in memory and language.
Damage to the tapetum can affect communication between the temporal lobes.
Neuroscientists studied the structure of the tapetum in patients with neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corpus callosum - the large bundle of nerve fibers connecting the two brain hemispheres
