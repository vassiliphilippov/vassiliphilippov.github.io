nonselective

adjective
not limited or selective
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.səˈlɛktɪv /; / ˌnɑnsəˈlɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.sɪˈlɛktɪv /; / ˌnɒnsɪˈlɛktɪv / (BrE)
not limited or restricted to a particular group, type, or target; affecting many kinds rather than being specific

Examples

A nonselective process
The university has a nonselective admissions process, accepting students from all backgrounds.
This medication is nonselective and affects several types of cells in the body.
A nonselective approach can sometimes lead to unexpected results.
The doctor prescribed a nonselective medication that affects several types of receptors.
Some pesticides are nonselective and can harm beneficial insects as well as pests.
Researchers warned that nonselective treatments may cause more side effects than targeted therapies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broad-spectrum - effective against a wide range of organisms or targets
  • synonym: inclusive - including all types or groups
  • antonym: selective - choosing only particular people or things
