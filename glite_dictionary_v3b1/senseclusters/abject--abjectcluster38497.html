abject

adjective
of the most contemptible or degrading kind
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈæbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˈabdʒɛkt / (BrE)
(of a situation or quality) of the most contemptible, miserable, or degrading kind; deserving strong disapproval or disgust.

Examples

Abject behavior
Many saw his abject apology as insincere and self-serving.
Such abject behavior was condemned by everyone who witnessed it.
The prisoners endured abject humiliation at the hands of their captors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admirable - deserving respect or approval
  • synonym: contemptible - deserving contempt; despicable
  • synonym: disgraceful - shameful or dishonorable
