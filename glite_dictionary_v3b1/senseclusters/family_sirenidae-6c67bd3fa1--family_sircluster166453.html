family Sirenidae

noun
family of aquatic salamanders (sirens)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli saɪˈrɛnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli saɪˈrɛnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of aquatic salamanders, known as sirens, characterized by elongated bodies, external gills, and the absence of hind limbs.

Examples

The family Sirenidae salamanders
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the family Sirenidae in a remote wetland.
The family Sirenidae includes salamanders that inhabit slow-moving or stagnant waters in North America.
Unlike most salamanders, members of the family Sirenidae retain their external gills throughout life.
