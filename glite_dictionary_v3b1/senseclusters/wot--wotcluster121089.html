wot

verb
to know (archaic)
Pronunciation: / wɒt / (AmE), / wɒt / (BrE)
to know or be aware of something; used chiefly in archaic or literary contexts.

Examples

I wot not
In the ancient manuscript, the hero declared, 'I wot not of this danger.'
Few today wot the meaning of such old-fashioned words.
He spoke as if he wot secrets no one else could guess.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • synonym: know - to be aware of or have knowledge of something
