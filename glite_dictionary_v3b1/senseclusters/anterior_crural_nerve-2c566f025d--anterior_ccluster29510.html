anterior crural nerve

noun
nerve supplying the front thigh (femoral nerve)
7.9
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪriər ˈkrʊrəl nɜrv / (AmE), / anˈtɪərɪə ˈkrʊərəl nɜːv / (BrE)
a large nerve that originates from lumbar nerves and supplies the muscles and skin on the front part of the thigh; also called the femoral nerve

Examples

The anterior crural nerve
Damage to the anterior crural nerve can result in weakness of the quadriceps muscle.
Symptoms included numbness in the area supplied by the anterior crural nerve.
The surgeon identified the anterior crural nerve during the procedure to avoid injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: femoral nerve - the main nerve supplying the anterior thigh
