chan

particle
Japanese affectionate suffix
Pronunciation: / tʃɑn / (AmE), / tʃæn / (BrE)
an informal Japanese honorific suffix added to a person's name to express affection, familiarity, or childlike endearment

Examples

Yuki-chan
Everyone in the class called her Yuki-chan because she was so cute and friendly.
His little sister giggled when their mother affectionately called her Emi-chan at breakfast.
In Japanese cartoons, characters often use chan to show closeness or endearment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kun - a Japanese honorific suffix used for boys or young men
  • antonym: san - a polite Japanese honorific suffix for addressing others
