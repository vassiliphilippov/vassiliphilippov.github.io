ionization

noun
process of forming ions
7.1 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.ə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌaɪə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.ə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which atoms or molecules become ions by gaining or losing electrons, usually caused by energy such as heat, radiation, or electric fields

Examples

Rapid ionization process
High temperatures in the plasma led to rapid ionization of the gas.
Radiation can cause the ionization of atoms in living cells, leading to damage.
The ionization of air allows electricity to flow through it more easily.
Solar flares can increase the ionization of Earth's upper atmosphere.
The scientist studied the ionization of gases in the laboratory.
X-rays cause ionization in the tissues they pass through, which is useful in medical imaging.
Radiation can cause the ionisation of molecules in living tissue.
The ionisation of water in the atmosphere affects electric conductivity.
The scientist measured the ionisation rate of the gas under different conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissociation - the process by which a molecule divides or splits into smaller particles, such as atoms or ions
  • synonym: ionization - alternate spelling (American English) of ionisation
  • synonym: ionize - to convert into ions, or to become ions
  • antonym: recombination - the process inverse to ionization, where ions and electrons combine
