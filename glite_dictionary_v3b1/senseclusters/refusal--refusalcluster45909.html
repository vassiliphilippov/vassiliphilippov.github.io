refusal

noun
an act or statement of rejecting something
1.4 11/m
Pronunciation: / r…™ÀàfjuÀêz…ôl / (AmE), / r…™ÀàfjuÀêz…ôl / (BrE)
the act of saying or showing that you will not accept, do, or allow something; a rejection

Examples

A firm refusal
Despite several offers, her refusal remained firm.
His refusal to join the team surprised everyone.
The company sent a formal refusal to the applicant.
Despite repeated offers, his refusal remained firm.
Her refusal to apologize only made the situation worse.
The manager expressed disappointment at the client's refusal to sign the contract.

Synonyms

  • rejection
    3.0 16/m
    rejection is the act of refusing something clearly and often officially, while refusal is a general act of saying no.
  • nonacceptance
    6.5 0.03/m
    nonacceptance is the formal act of not accepting an offer or proposal, while refusal is the everyday act of saying no.
  • turndown
    4.6 0.12/m
    turndown is a casual way to refuse an offer or request, while refusal covers both formal and informal say-no acts.
  • declination
    8.7 0.02/m
    declination is a polite, often formal refusal of an invitation or offer, while refusal is a broad act of saying no.
  • denial
    2.8 6.21/m
    denial is saying something is not true or refusing to agree, while refusal focuses on saying no to offers or requests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acceptance - the act of consenting to receive or undertake something offered
  • synonym: rejection - the act of dismissing or refusing an offer, proposal, or application
