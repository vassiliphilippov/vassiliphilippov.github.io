sentimental value

noun
emotional, not monetary, worth
4.6 2.7
Pronunciation: /sen.tɪ.ˈmen.təl ˈvæl.juː/ /; / /sən.tə.ˈmen.təl ˈvæl.juː/ (AmE), /sen.tɪ.ˈmen.təl ˈvæl.juː/ /; / /sən.tə.ˈmen.təl ˈvæl.juː/ (BrE)
emotional worth attached to something because of personal or emotional associations, rather than its monetary value

Examples

The sentimental value of gifts
Although the vase is cracked, its sentimental value makes it irreplaceable.
She couldn't bear to throw away the letter because of its sentimental value.
This watch has little monetary worth, but it holds great sentimental value for me.
