Garuda

noun
mythical eagle, mount of Vishnu
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈruːdə / (AmE), / ɡəˈruːdə / (BrE)
in Hindu and Buddhist mythology, a large, supernatural eagle-like bird that serves as the mount of the god Vishnu

Examples

The Garuda bird
According to legend, Garuda is the eternal enemy of serpents.
In many artworks, Garuda is depicted carrying Vishnu through the skies.
The statue of Garuda stands proudly outside the temple.
