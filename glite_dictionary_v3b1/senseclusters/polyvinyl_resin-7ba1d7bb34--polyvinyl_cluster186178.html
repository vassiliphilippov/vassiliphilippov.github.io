polyvinyl resin

noun
thermoplastic polymer made from vinyl compounds
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈvaɪnəl ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈvaɪnɪl ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
A synthetic thermoplastic material made by the polymerization of compounds containing the vinyl group, used in the production of plastics and coatings.

Examples

A polyvinyl resin product
Many household items, such as pipes and vinyl flooring, are made from polyvinyl resin.
Scientists are developing new biodegradable alternatives to polyvinyl resin for more sustainable packaging.
The manufacturer switched to polyvinyl resin for its improved durability and flexibility.
