muscle tone

noun
normal firmness and tension of resting muscles
2.6 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌs.əl toʊn/ (AmE), /ˈmʌs.əl təʊn/ (BrE)
the natural firmness and slight tension present in resting muscles, allowing them to be ready for action or movement

Examples

Muscle tone level
Babies are often assessed for reflexes and muscle tone at birth.
Regular exercise is essential for maintaining good muscle tone throughout the body.
The therapist evaluated his muscle tone after the injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flaccid - lacking normal muscle tone; limp
  • antonym: rigid - excessively stiff or inflexible, often from abnormal muscle tone
