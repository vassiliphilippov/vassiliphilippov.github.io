insemination

noun
the act of sowing or introducing seeds, germs, or ideas
9.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌsɛmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌsɛmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of sowing seeds in the ground, or, figuratively, of introducing something such as germs or ideas into a place or medium

Examples

Field insemination
In spring, the insemination of the fields by farmers marks the start of the cultivation cycle.
Some authors refer to the insemination of new ideas into society as a gradual process.
The insemination of bacteria into the nutrient solution is a key step in the experiment.
