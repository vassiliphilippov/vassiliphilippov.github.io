angina

noun
chest pain from reduced heart blood flow
5.7 5.1
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈdʒaɪ.nə/ /; / /æn.ˈdʒɪ.nə/ (AmE), /æn.ˈdʒaɪ.nə/ /; / /æn.ˈdʒɪ.nə/ (BrE)
a medical condition causing severe chest pain, usually because the heart is not getting enough blood

Examples

Severe angina pain
Her persistent angina required her to take medication and change her lifestyle.
Many people experience angina during physical exertion or times of stress.
The doctor warned that untreated angina could lead to serious heart problems.
Early treatments reduced the symptoms of angina and prevented further complications.
She had difficulty swallowing due to angina affecting her throat.
The patient was admitted to the hospital after complaining of severe angina attacks.
