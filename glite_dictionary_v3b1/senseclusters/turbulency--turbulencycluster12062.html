turbulency

noun
unstable flow in fluid dynamics
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrbjʊlənsi / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊlənsi / (BrE)
unstable or chaotic movement within a fluid, such as air or water, typically characterized by eddies or unpredictable currents.

Examples

Strong turbulency in water
Ocean currents sometimes display strong turbulency near the rocky coastline.
Sudden turbulency in the atmosphere can disrupt airplane flights and cause discomfort for passengers.
The engineer studied the turbulency in the river flow before designing the bridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turbulence - irregular, chaotic movement of fluid or air
