formalism

noun
emphasis on formal structure over content
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrməˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlɪzəm / (BrE)
The belief or doctrine that it is the formal structure, rather than the content, that should be emphasized or represented, especially in art, literature, or philosophy.

Examples

Artistic formalism
In art criticism, formalism values composition and technique above the message of the work.
Some mathematicians are drawn to formalism, focusing strictly on systematic processes and logical structure.
The school of Russian formalism argued that the structure of language is more important than its content.

Related Terms

  • antonym: content - the subject matter or substance, as opposed to form
