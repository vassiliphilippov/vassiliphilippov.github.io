organicity

noun
degree of organic matter
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːrɡəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality or degree of being organic, especially the amount of organic (carbon-based) matter in a substance or environment

Examples

Organicity of soil
Researchers analyzed the organicity of river sediments to assess environmental health.
The organicity of compost is important for improving soil fertility in agriculture.
High organicity in water samples can indicate pollution from decaying plant material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inorganicity - the quality or degree of being inorganic
  • synonym: organic content - the amount of organic matter present in a material
