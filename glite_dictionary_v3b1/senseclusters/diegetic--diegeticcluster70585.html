diegetic

adjective
within story world
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈdʒɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈdʒɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to sounds or elements that exist within the story world of a film, play, or narrative

Examples

Diegetic sound effects
The director chose diegetic music so the characters could interact with the song playing in the scene.
In the film, the sound of footsteps is diegetic because the characters can hear it too.
A narrator's voice is usually not diegetic, but the ringing phone in the story is.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-diegetic - not existing within the story world, such as background music only the audience hears
