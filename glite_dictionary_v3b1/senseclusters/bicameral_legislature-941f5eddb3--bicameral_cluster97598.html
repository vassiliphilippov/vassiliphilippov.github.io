bicameral legislature

noun
legislative body with two chambers
9.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /baɪˈkæm.ər.əl ˈledʒ.ɪ.slə.tʃər/ /; / /bɪˈkæm.ər.əl ˈledʒ.ɪ.slə.tʃər/ (AmE), /bɪˈkæm.ər.əl ˈledʒ.ɪ.slə.tʃə/ /; / /baɪˈkæm.ər.əl ˈledʒ.ɪ.slə.tʃə/ (BrE)
A legislative body consisting of two separate chambers or houses, typically an upper and a lower house.

Examples

A bicameral legislature system
Many countries have adopted a bicameral legislature to balance the interests of different regions or groups.
The United States Congress is a bicameral legislature, consisting of the Senate and the House of Representatives.
The proposal to switch from a unicameral to a bicameral legislature sparked intense political debate.
