abider

noun
person who obeys rules
Pronunciation: / əˈbaɪdər / (AmE), / əˈbaɪdə / (BrE)
a person who accepts, follows, or obeys rules, laws, or traditions

Examples

The school values every abider of its code of conduct.
He was respected as an abider of community traditions and customs.
As an abider of the law, she always drives within the speed limit.
The club values every abider who respects its long-standing traditions.
He is known as a strict abider of his family's customs.

  • antonym: rule-breaker - a person who does not follow rules or laws
