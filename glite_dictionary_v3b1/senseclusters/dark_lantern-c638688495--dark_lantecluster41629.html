dark lantern

noun
lantern with concealable light
Pronunciation: / dɑrk ˈlæntərn / (AmE), / dɑːk ˈlɑːntən / (BrE)
a lantern with a single opening and a sliding panel that can cover the light to keep it concealed

Examples

A dark lantern cover
He slid the cover of the dark lantern shut as the guards passed by in the corridor.
In Victorian novels, detectives often rely on a dark lantern during nighttime investigations.
The thief crept through the old mansion, guiding his way with a dark lantern to avoid detection.
