transeunt

adjective
producing effects beyond the mind
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænziˌʌnt / (AmE), / ˈtranzɪʌnt / (BrE)
(of a mental act) producing effects beyond the mind; having an effect on the external world.

Examples

A transeunt effect
Deliberate speech is considered a transeunt activity because it influences other people.
In certain philosophical systems, a transeunt act is one that brings about change in the physical world.
Not all mental actions are transeunt; some remain purely within the mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immanent - (of a mental act) remaining within the mind; not producing external effects
