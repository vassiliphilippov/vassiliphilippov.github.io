crack

verb
break suddenly under tension
3.5
Pronunciation: / kræk / (AmE), / kræk /; / krak / (BrE)
to break or cause to break suddenly, or to develop lines or splits on the surface without completely separating

Examples

To crack the glass
Be careful not to crack the glass when you tighten the lid.
During the earthquake, several walls in the building cracked.
The ice on the lake began to crack under our weight.
He cracked the bat hard against the ball, sending it flying over the fence.
She angrily cracked the newspaper on the table to get everyone's attention.
The teacher cracked him across the face with a ruler.
Extreme changes in temperature can cause glass to crack.
If the ice gets any thinner, it may crack under his weight.
The porcelain vase cracked after falling off the shelf.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to separate or cause to separate into pieces suddenly or violently
  • degree lower: chip - to break a small piece off something
  • synonym: fracture - to break or cause to break; usually refers to hard material
  • degree higher: shatter - to break suddenly into many small pieces
  • degree higher: smash - to shatter or break something with force
  • synonym: snap - to break suddenly with a sharp sound
  • synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
  • degree lower: tap - to hit lightly and quickly
