positive reinforcer

noun
stimulus increasing future behavior
2.8 3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzətɪv rɪnˈfɔrsər / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv rɪnˈfɔːsə / (BrE)
A stimulus that, when presented after a behavior, increases the likelihood that the behavior will occur again.

Examples

A positive reinforcer stimulus
Access to a favorite activity can function as a positive reinforcer for good behavior.
Food treats serve as a positive reinforcer during dog training sessions.
Praising a child for completing their homework is a classic example of a positive reinforcer in education.

Related Terms

  • antonym: negative reinforcer - a stimulus whose removal increases the likelihood of a behavior
  • synonym: reinforcement - the process of encouraging a behavior to occur again
