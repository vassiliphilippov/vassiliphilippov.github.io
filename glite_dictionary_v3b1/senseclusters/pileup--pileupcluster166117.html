pileup

noun
accumulation causing blockage
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪlˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlˌʌp / (BrE)
a situation in which a large number of things or vehicles accumulate in one place, often causing a blockage or delay

Examples

Traffic pileup
A massive pileup on the highway caused traffic to back up for miles.
There was a pileup of paperwork on her desk after the long weekend.
Winter storms often lead to a pileup of cars on icy roads.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearance - the removal of accumulated things or vehicles
  • synonym: congestion - the state of being overcrowded or blocked
  • synonym: jam - a situation where movement is blocked by a mass of things or vehicles
