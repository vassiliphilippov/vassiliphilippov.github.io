scrummage

noun
rugby method of restarting play
9.9 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈskrʌm.ɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈskrʌm.ɪdʒ/ (BrE)
(in rugby) a method of restarting play in which forwards from each team crouch and interlock arms, competing for possession of the ball as it is thrown between them.

Examples

A scrummage in rugby
A well-executed scrummage can determine control early in a rugby match.
The referee called for a scrummage after the knock-on.
The teams prepared for the scrummage, locking arms and crouching low.
