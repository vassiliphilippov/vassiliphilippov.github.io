receptivity

noun
willingness to receive ideas or influences
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌrisɛpˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌriːsɛpˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the willingness or ability to receive new ideas, suggestions, or impressions; openness to being influenced or affected

Examples

High receptivity to ideas
Children often have a great receptivity to learning new languages.
Successful leaders encourage receptivity among their team members.
The committee's receptivity to new proposals made it easier to implement change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: openness - willingness to consider new ideas; open-mindedness
  • antonym: resistance - refusal or unwillingness to accept something
