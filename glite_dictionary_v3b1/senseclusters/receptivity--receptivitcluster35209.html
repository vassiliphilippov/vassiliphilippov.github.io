receptivity

noun
willingness to receive ideas or influences
6.2 0.77/m
Pronunciation: / Àåris…õpÀàt…™v…ôti / (AmE), / ÀåriÀês…õpÀàt…™v…™ti / (BrE)
the willingness or ability to receive new ideas, suggestions, or impressions; openness to being influenced or affected

Examples

High receptivity to ideas
Children often have a great receptivity to learning new languages.
Successful leaders encourage receptivity among their team members.
The committee's receptivity to new proposals made it easier to implement change.

Synonyms

  • amenability
    6.8 0.04/m
    amenability means being ready to cooperate and accept influence, while receptivity stresses being open to receive new ideas or impressions.
  • acceptingness
    7.0 0.01/m
    acceptingness means being ready to take in new people or ideas, while receptivity highlights being open to influence or impressions.
  • openness
    3.9 5.08/m
    openness means a willingness to consider new ideas without bias, while receptivity more directly means being open to influence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: openness - willingness to consider new ideas; open-mindedness
  • antonym: resistance - refusal or unwillingness to accept something
