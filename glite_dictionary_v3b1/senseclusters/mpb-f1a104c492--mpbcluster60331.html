MPB

noun
male hair loss
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɛm piː biː / (AmE), / ɛm piː biː / (BrE)
a common form of hair loss in men, caused by genetic sensitivity to male hormones, resulting in a receding hairline and thinning crown

Examples

Male MPB pattern
Many men begin to notice MPB in their late twenties or early thirties.
He tried several shampoos to slow down the effects of MPB.
Doctors say that genetics play a major role in the development of MPB.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alopecia - medical term for hair loss
  • synonym: male pattern baldness - hair loss in men with a typical pattern of receding hairline and thinning crown
