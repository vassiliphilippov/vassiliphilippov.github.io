percept

noun
mental impression formed by perception
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrˌsɛpt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːsɛpt / (BrE)
a mental impression or image formed as a result of perception

Examples

A visual percept
Each percept is shaped by both sensory input and prior knowledge.
In philosophy, a percept is distinct from a concept or an idea.
The experiment measured how quickly a visual percept forms in the mind.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: concept - an abstract idea; a general notion
  • degree lower: sensation - a physical feeling or perception resulting from something that happens to or comes into contact with the body
