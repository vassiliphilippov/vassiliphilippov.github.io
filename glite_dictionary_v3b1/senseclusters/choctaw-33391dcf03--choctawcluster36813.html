Choctaw

noun
member of a Native American people
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑːktɔː / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒktɔː / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally inhabiting areas of present-day Alabama, Mississippi, and Louisiana

Examples

A Choctaw community
A group of Choctaw settled in what is now southeastern Oklahoma.
Several Choctaw served as code talkers during World War I.
The Choctaw have a rich cultural heritage that is still celebrated today.
