pentatonic scale

noun
musical scale with five notes
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛn.təˈtɑ.nɪk skeɪl / (AmE), / ˌpɛn.təˈtɒ.nɪk skeɪl / (BrE)
A musical scale consisting of five notes per octave, typically formed by omitting the fourth and seventh degrees of the diatonic scale.

Examples

A pentatonic scale
Learning to improvise with a pentatonic scale can help beginners create simple melodies.
Many folk songs are based on the pentatonic scale rather than the diatonic scale.
The melody uses notes from the pentatonic scale, giving it a distinctive sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diatonic scale - a musical scale of seven distinct notes per octave
