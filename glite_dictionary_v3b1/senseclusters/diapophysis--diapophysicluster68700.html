diapophysis

noun
vertebral lateral process
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈpɑfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈpɒfɪsɪs / (BrE)
a lateral projection on a vertebra to which a rib or muscle may attach

Examples

The diapophysis on vertebra
The paleontologist identified the diapophysis on the dinosaur vertebra.
Muscles attach to the diapophysis to help stabilize the spine.
In some mammals, the diapophysis is more prominent than in others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apophysis - a natural outgrowth or projection on a bone
  • synonym: parapophysis - a process on a vertebra for rib attachment, usually lower than the diapophysis
