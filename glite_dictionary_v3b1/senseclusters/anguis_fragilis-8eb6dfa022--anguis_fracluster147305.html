Anguis fragilis

noun
a legless European lizard (slowworm)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡwɪs frəˈdʒɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡwɪs frəˈdʒɪlɪs / (BrE)
A small, burrowing, legless European lizard with small eyes, commonly known as the slowworm, and often mistakenly thought to be blind.

Examples

The Anguis fragilis lizard
Children sometimes incorrectly believe the Anguis fragilis is blind because of its tiny eyes.
In damp British gardens, the Anguis fragilis is often mistaken for a snake due to its smooth, legless body.
Researchers discovered a population of Anguis fragilis thriving in the forest undergrowth.
