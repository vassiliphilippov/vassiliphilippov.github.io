performativity

noun
show for appearance
Pronunciation: / pərˌfɔrməˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / pəˌfɔːməˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality of an action or statement that is intended mainly to create an impression or appearance rather than to bring about real change

Examples

Political performativity
Critics accused the politician of performativity when his speech lacked any real policy proposals.
The company's public apology was seen as mere performativity without meaningful action.
Some activists warn against performativity in social justice movements, urging genuine commitment instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: posturing - behavior intended to impress or mislead others
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being genuine or honest
