draw and quarter

verb
execute by pulling apart with horses
6.8
Pronunciation: / drɔː ənd ˈkwɔrtər /; / drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːrtɚ / (AmE), / drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːtə / (BrE)
to execute someone by tying each limb to a different horse and pulling them apart; also used figuratively to mean punish someone extremely severely

Examples

Draw and quarter a traitor
In medieval England, traitors were often drawn and quartered as a gruesome punishment.
The historical drama depicted several prisoners being drawn and quartered for their crimes.
They threatened to draw and quarter anyone caught plotting against the king.
He exaggerated, saying his parents would draw and quarter him for breaking curfew.
In medieval times, people would draw and quarter traitors as a public warning.
Some critics threatened to draw and quarter the director for ruining the classic film.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dismember - to cut off the limbs of a person or animal
  • synonym: execute - to put someone to death as a legal penalty
  • antonym: spare - to refrain from inflicting punishment or harm
