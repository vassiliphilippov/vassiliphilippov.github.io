rootstock

noun
horizontal plant stem with shoots and roots
3.9 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrutˌstɑk / (AmE), / ˈruːt.stɒk / (BrE)
A horizontal plant stem, usually underground, with shoots growing above and roots growing below, serving as a reproductive structure; a rhizome.

Examples

Divided rootstock
Gardeners often divide the rootstock in spring to propagate new plants.
Many perennials survive winter by storing energy in their thick rootstock.
The bamboo spreads quickly thanks to its extensive rootstock underground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rhizome - an underground stem that sends out roots and shoots
