hesitate

verb
pause briefly before acting or speaking
1.0 2.1
Pronunciation: /ˈhez.ə.teɪt/ /; / /ˈhez.ɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /ˈhez.ɪ.teɪt/ /; / /ˈhez.ə.teɪt/ (BrE)
to pause before saying or doing something because you feel unsure or undecided

Examples

To hesitate before speaking
He didn’t hesitate for long before continuing his speech.
If you hesitate too much, you might miss your chance.
She began to hesitate before answering the difficult question.
He often hesitates at the door, unsure whether to enter.
If you hesitate too long, you might miss your opportunity.
She didn't hesitate before answering the question confidently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - to make a choice or come to a conclusion
  • synonym: pause - to stop briefly before continuing
  • antonym: proceed - to continue without pausing or hesitation
  • synonym: waver - to be undecided between two opinions or courses of action
