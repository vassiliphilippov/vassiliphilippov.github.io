Thomas

proper noun
the Apostle who doubted Jesus' resurrection
4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.məs/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.məs/ (BrE)
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus, known for doubting Jesus' resurrection until he had seen him

Examples

Thomas the Apostle
Many stories about Thomas appear in the New Testament, highlighting his doubts and eventual faith.
The phrase 'Doubting Thomas' originates from the Apostle's insistence on witnessing evidence before believing.
Tradition holds that Thomas traveled to India to spread Christianity after the resurrection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Doubting Thomas - a person who is habitually doubtful; referencing the Apostle Thomas
