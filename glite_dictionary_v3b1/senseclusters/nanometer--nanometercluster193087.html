nanometer

noun
unit of length, one billionth of a meter
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈnænəˌmitɚ /; / ˈnænoʊˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈnænəˌmiːtə /; / ˈnanəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter (1 × 10⁻⁹ meters), commonly used in scientific contexts.

Examples

A nanometer unit
Modern computer chips have transistors only a few nanometers wide.
Scientists use nanometer-scale measurements to study tiny particles and biological structures.
The wavelength of visible light ranges from about 400 to 700 nanometers.
A virus typically measures only a few hundred nanometres in diameter.
Modern computer chips contain features just a few nanometres wide.
The wavelength of visible light ranges from about 400 to 700 nanometres.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: micrometer - a metric unit equal to one millionth of a meter
  • synonym: nanometer - a metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter (US spelling)
