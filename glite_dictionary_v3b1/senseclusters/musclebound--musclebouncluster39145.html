musclebound

adjective
overly muscular; rigid
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌs.əl.baʊnd/ (AmE), /ˈmʌs.əl.baʊnd/ (BrE)
having large, stiff, or overdeveloped muscles that limit movement or flexibility (can also be used to describe someone who is not flexible in their thinking or behavior)

Examples

A musclebound athlete
The musclebound athlete struggled to touch his toes during the warm-up exercises.
Her ideas were anything but musclebound, showing creativity and flexibility in every project.
Some critics described the film's plot as musclebound and lacking subtlety.
The musclebound athlete found it difficult to reach behind his back.
Despite his musclebound appearance, he struggled with basic yoga poses.
Some people think being musclebound can actually reduce overall athletic performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brawny - physically strong and muscular
  • antonym: flexible - able to bend or move easily
  • antonym: nimble - quick and light in movement or thought
  • synonym: rigid - not flexible or adaptable
