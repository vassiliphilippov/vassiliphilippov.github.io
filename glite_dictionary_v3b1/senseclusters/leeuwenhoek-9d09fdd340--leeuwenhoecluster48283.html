Leeuwenhoek

proper noun
Dutch pioneer in microscopy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪvənhʊk / (AmE), / ˈleɪvənˌhʊk / (BrE)
Antonie van Leeuwenhoek (1632–1723), Dutch scientist regarded as the pioneer of microscopy, noted for his discovery of microorganisms and detailed observations of blood cells and spermatozoa.

Examples

Leeuwenhoek microscope discoveries
Leeuwenhoek is often credited as the first person to observe living microorganisms under a microscope.
Researchers still admire Leeuwenhoek's detailed illustrations of red blood cells.
The scientific community considers Leeuwenhoek's discoveries fundamental to the development of microbiology.
