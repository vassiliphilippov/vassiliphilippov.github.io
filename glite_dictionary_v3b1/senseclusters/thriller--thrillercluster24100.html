thriller

noun
suspenseful story, play, or movie
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɪlər / (AmE), / ˈθrɪlə / (BrE)
a book, play, or film with an exciting story, often involving crime or danger, intended to keep the audience in suspense

Examples

A thriller book
He prefers reading thrillers to comedies or science fiction.
She finished the thriller in one night because she couldn't put it down.
The movie is a psychological thriller that keeps viewers guessing until the end.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mystery - story involving a crime or puzzle to be solved
  • antonym: romance - story focusing on love and relationships, often light in tone
