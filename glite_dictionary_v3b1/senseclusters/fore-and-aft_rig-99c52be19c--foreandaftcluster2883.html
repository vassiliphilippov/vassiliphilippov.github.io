fore-and-aft rig

noun
type of sail arrangement on boats
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔr ənd ˈæft rɪg / (AmE), / ˌfɔːr ən ˈɑːft rɪg / (BrE)
A rig in which the principal sails are aligned along the length of a vessel, rather than across it, typically used in modern yachts and small sailing boats.

Examples

A fore-and-aft rig sailboat
Most modern sailboats use a fore-and-aft rig rather than a square rig.
Switching from a square rig to a fore-and-aft rig improved the ship's speed and handling.
The yacht was fitted with a fore-and-aft rig, making it easier to maneuver in changing winds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: square rig - rig with principal sails set square to the keel
