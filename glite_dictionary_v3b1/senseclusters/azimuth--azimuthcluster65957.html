azimuth

noun
angle from north along the horizon
Pronunciation: / ˈæz.əˌməθ / (AmE), / ˈæz.ɪməθ / (BrE)
The angle between a reference direction (typically north) and a line from the observer to a point of interest, usually measured clockwise around the observer’s horizon.

Examples

The azimuth angle
Astronomers measure a star’s azimuth to specify its position in the sky.
The navigator recorded the sun’s azimuth at noon to chart their course.
To find the North Star, you need to determine its azimuth relative to your position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bearing - the direction or path along which something moves or along which it lies
