blood knot

noun
a knot for joining fishing lines
Pronunciation: / blʌd nɑt / (AmE), / blʌd nɒt / (BrE)
a strong knot used to join two fishing lines, especially leaders, by wrapping the ends around each other several times before tightening

Examples

A blood knot for fishing
An experienced angler can tie a blood knot quickly and securely on the riverbank.
He used a blood knot to connect the two different weights of fishing line.
Learning the blood knot is essential for fly fishermen who frequently change leaders.
