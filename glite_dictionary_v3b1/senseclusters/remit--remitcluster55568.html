remit

verb
refer a case to another authority
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪt / (BrE)
to refer a matter or legal case to another committee, authority, or court for a decision

Examples

To remit a case
If the dispute cannot be resolved, the board must remit it to arbitration.
The judge decided to remit the case to the lower court for retrial.
The parliamentary committee may remit the matter to a specialist subcommittee for further consideration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refer - to direct someone or something to another place or person for assistance or information
