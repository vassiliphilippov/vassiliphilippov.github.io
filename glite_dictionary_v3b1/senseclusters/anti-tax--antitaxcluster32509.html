anti-tax

noun
tax-reducing measure
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈæntiˌtæks / (AmE), / ˈæntiˌtæks / (BrE)
a law, proposal, or political measure intended to reduce taxes or restrict the government's ability to increase them

Examples

Anti-tax law
Voters supported the anti-tax initiative to prevent new local taxes from being introduced.
The politician campaigned on an anti-tax platform, promising to lower property taxes.
Several states have passed anti-tax laws to limit government spending.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tax cut - a reduction in the amount of taxes
  • antonym: tax increase - an increase in the amount of taxes
