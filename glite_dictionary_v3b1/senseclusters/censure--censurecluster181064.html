censure

verb
formally express strong disapproval
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.ʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.ʃə / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval of someone or something, especially in a formal statement

Examples

To censure a leader
She was censured by her peers for failing to follow the agreed procedures.
The Board publicly censured the CEO over the financial irregularities.
The committee decided to censure the senator for his unethical behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of
  • synonym: reprimand - to criticize or rebuke someone, especially officially
