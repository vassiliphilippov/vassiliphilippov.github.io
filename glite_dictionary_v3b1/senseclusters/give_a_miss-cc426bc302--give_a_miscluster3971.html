give a miss

verb
choose not to attend or take part
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ə mɪs / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə mɪs / (BrE)
to decide not to attend or participate in something; to skip or avoid an event or activity

Examples

To give the pub a miss
I'm feeling tired tonight, so I think I'll give the pub a miss.
If you don't feel well, you can always give the class a miss.
She usually attends the seminar, but this week she decided to give it a miss.
