stimulus

something that provokes a response or arouses activity, especially in the body or mind; an event or information that encourages action or reaction.

Examples

A stimulus event
A sudden loud noise can serve as a stimulus for the startle reflex.
Exposure to sunlight is an important stimulus for the production of vitamin D in the skin.
The financial crisis acted as a stimulus for reform across the banking sector.

Synonyms

  • elicitor
    8.1 0.08/m
    elicitor is a technical prompt used in labs or therapy to get specific responses, while stimulus is a general trigger that causes reactions.
  • stimulant
    5.0 0.33/m
    stimulant is often a substance or agent that raises energy or excitement in body or mind, while stimulus is any trigger that causes a response.
  • stimulation
    4.1 3.02/m
    stimulation is the process or feeling of being aroused or encouraged, while stimulus is the thing that causes that reaction.
