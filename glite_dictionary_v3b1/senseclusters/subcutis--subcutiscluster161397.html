subcutis

noun
layer under skin
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈkjuːtɪs / (AmE), / sʌbˈkjuːtɪs / (BrE)
the layer of tissue beneath the skin, mainly made up of fat and connective tissue

Examples

The subcutis layer
The nurse injected the medication into the subcutis to ensure slow absorption.
Damage to the subcutis can affect the body's ability to regulate temperature.
Fat stored in the subcutis helps protect internal organs from injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dermis - the thick layer of living tissue below the epidermis
  • synonym: hypodermis - the layer of tissue directly beneath the skin
