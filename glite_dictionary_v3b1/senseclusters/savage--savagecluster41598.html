savage

verb
attack violently or fiercely
Pronunciation: / ˈsævɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsavɪdʒ / (BrE)
to attack someone or something violently or to criticize very harshly

Examples

To savage a car
An angry mob savaged the abandoned car, overturning and smashing it.
The critics savaged the new movie for its weak plot and poor acting.
The lion savaged its prey before dragging it away.
After the disappointing match, sports commentators savaged the team's performance on live television.
Her new book was savaged by critics for its lack of originality.
The media savaged the politician after the scandal became public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assault - to attack someone violently
  • synonym: attack - to strongly criticize or oppose
  • synonym: maul - to attack and injure someone or something severely
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
