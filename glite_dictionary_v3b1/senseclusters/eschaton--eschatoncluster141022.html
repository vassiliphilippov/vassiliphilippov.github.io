eschaton

noun
the end of the world; final event in Christian theology
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskəˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛskətɒn / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the final event in the divine plan; the end of the world or the ultimate destiny of humanity, especially as described in the New Testament.

Examples

The eschaton event
Believers often contemplate their actions in light of the coming eschaton.
Some theologians debate whether the eschaton will bring a literal transformation of the world.
The concept of the eschaton has inspired countless works of literature and art.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apocalypse - the complete final destruction of the world, as described in the biblical book of Revelation
