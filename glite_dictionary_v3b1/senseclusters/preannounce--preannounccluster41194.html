preannounce

verb
to announce in advance
6.4
Pronunciation: /priː.ə.ˈnaʊns/ /; / /prɪ.ə.ˈnaʊns/ /; / /priː.ˈnaʊns/ (AmE), /priː.ə.ˈnaʊns/ /; / /prɪ.ə.ˈnaʊns/ /; / /priː.ˈnaʊns/ (BrE)
to announce something before the official or main announcement or before it actually happens

Examples

To preannounce earnings
The company plans to preannounce its quarterly earnings to calm investors.
They decided to preannounce the new policy before the official launch.
Sometimes, organizations preannounce events to generate public interest early.
The company plans to preannounce its quarterly earnings to reassure investors.
They decided to preannounce the new policy changes before the official rollout.
The tech giant will preannounce its latest smartphone at the upcoming conference.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: announce - to make something known publicly
  • synonym: reveal - to make something previously secret known
  • antonym: withhold - to keep something back or not announce it
