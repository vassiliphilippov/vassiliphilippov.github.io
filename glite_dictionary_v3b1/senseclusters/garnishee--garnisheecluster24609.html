garnishee

noun
person whose wages are garnished
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɡɑːrnɪˈʃi / (AmE), / ˌɡɑːnɪˈʃi / (BrE)
a person or organization whose money, usually wages, is subject to a court-ordered garnishment, typically to satisfy a debt

Examples

The garnishee pays debt
A garnishee must comply with the instructions specified in the garnishment order.
As a garnishee, his employer was required by law to withhold part of his wages.
The court notified the garnishee to submit payment by the end of the month.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debtor - a person who owes a sum of money
  • antonym: garnisher - the party who obtains the garnishment order
