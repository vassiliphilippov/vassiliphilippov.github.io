root

verb
gain administrative (root) access (computing)
8.8
Pronunciation: /ruːt/ /; / /rʊt/ (AmE), /ruːt/ (BrE)
(computing) to gain root (superuser) access on a device or system, giving full administrative control, often by unlocking, modifying, or bypassing built-in restrictions.

Examples

To root a phone
Many users choose to root their Android phones to remove preinstalled apps and customize the interface.
After he rooted the tablet, he installed a firewall and recovered deleted system files.
Developers sometimes avoid rooting devices because it can void warranties and introduce security risks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jailbreak - to remove device restrictions (especially on phones) to gain greater control or install unauthorized software
  • synonym: superuser - an account with full administrative privileges on a computer system
