government-in-exile

noun
temporary government formed abroad by exiles

Pronunciation: /ˈgʌv.ərn.mənt ɪn ˈek.saɪl/ /; / /ˈgʌv.ən.mənt ɪn ˈek.saɪl/ /; / /ˈgʌv.ən.mənt ɪn ˈeg.zaɪl/ (AmE), /ˈgʌv.ən.mənt ɪn ˈek.saɪl/ /; / /ˈgʌv.ən.mənt ɪn ˈeg.zaɪl/ (BrE)
A temporary government established and operating outside its home country by exiles who intend to return and reclaim authority when their country is liberated.

Examples

A government-in-exile formed
After the military coup, the leaders formed a government-in-exile to coordinate resistance campaigns.
During World War II, several European countries established a government-in-exile after fleeing Nazi occupation.
The government-in-exile continued diplomatic relations and lobbied for international support.
