IMU

noun
inertial sensor unit
9.0
Pronunciation: / aɪ ɛm ju / (AmE), / aɪ ɛm ju / (BrE)
A compact sensor unit combining accelerometers, gyroscopes, and often magnetometers to measure motion, acceleration, and orientation.

Examples

IMU sensor unit
The drone's flight controller relies on its IMU to stabilize during flight.
Engineers calibrated the IMU before testing the robot's balance on uneven terrain.
Modern smartphones use a small IMU for screen rotation and motion-controlled games.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inertial measurement unit - a device combining accelerometers and gyroscopes to measure motion and orientation
