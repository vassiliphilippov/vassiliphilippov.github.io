parametric statistic

noun
statistic assuming data distribution
6.4 6.6
Pronunciation: /pə.rə.ˈmet.rɪk stə.ˈtɪs.tɪk/ /; / /pær.ə.ˈmet.rɪk stə.ˈtɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /pær.ə.ˈmet.rɪk stə.ˈtɪs.tɪk/ /; / /pær.ə.ˈmet.ʃrɪk stə.ˈtɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
a statistic that is calculated using methods based on the assumption that the data come from a specific probability distribution

Examples

A parametric statistic method
A parametric statistic like the mean is appropriate when your data set is normally distributed.
Researchers often use a parametric statistic when the underlying distribution of the sample is known.
When the required assumptions are met, a parametric statistic can provide more powerful results.
