basilica

noun
large important church, often with special status
4.3 5.6
Pronunciation: /bə.ˈzɪ.lɪ.kə/ /; / /bə.ˈzɪ.lə.kə/ /; / /bə.ˈsɪ.lɪ.kə/ (AmE), /bə.ˈzɪ.lɪ.kə/ /; / /bə.ˈsɪ.lɪ.kə/ (BrE)
A large, important church building, originally of early Christian or Roman design, or a Roman Catholic church given special ceremonial rights by the Pope.

Examples

Famous basilica church
Saint Peter's basilica is one of the most famous churches in Rome.
The ancient basilica attracted tourists from all over the world.
The church was granted the status of basilica by the Pope.
