weakness

noun
fondness for something, often unwise
3.3 1.58/m
Pronunciation: / Ààwikn…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàwiÀêkn…ôs / (BrE)
a strong liking for something, even if it may be unwise or harmful

Examples

A weakness for chocolate
Despite being on a diet, her weakness is still potato chips.
His weakness for gambling sometimes got him into trouble.
She always had a weakness for sweet pastries and could never say no to dessert.

Synonyms

  • predilection
    8.4 1.51/m
    predilection expresses a strong, steady liking for something, while weakness suggests a personal failing or temptation toward that thing.
  • penchant
    8.2 3.41/m
    penchant is a strong, often habitual liking people keep, while weakness suggests a personal fault or tempting pleasure they find hard to resist.
  • proclivity
    8.5 1.54/m
    proclivity is a natural tendency, often toward something bad or harmful, while weakness is a liking that shows poor self-control.
  • inclination
    4.2 6.09/m
    inclination is a general feeling or tendency to choose something, while weakness is a stronger liking that can be unwise or harmful.
