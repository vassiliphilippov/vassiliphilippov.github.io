police lineup

noun
police procedure for witness identification
3.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈlis ˈlaɪnʌp / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˈlaɪnʌp / (BrE)
a process in which a group of people, including a suspect, are lined up so that a witness can try to identify the person involved in a crime

Examples

A police lineup identification
After the robbery, the officers conducted a police lineup for the eyewitnesses.
She was nervous when she recognized someone she knew in the police lineup.
The witness studied the police lineup carefully to identify the suspect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: identity parade - procedure where witnesses identify suspects in a lineup (British term)
