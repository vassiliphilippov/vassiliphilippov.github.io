tilt

verb
move sideways or unsteadily
Pronunciation: / tɪlt / (AmE), / tɪlt / (BrE)
to move or cause to move into a sloping or leaning position; to tip or lean

Examples

To tilt the chair
She felt the world tilt when she lost her balance on the narrow beam.
The boat began to tilt dangerously as the wind picked up.
The truck suddenly tilted to one side after hitting the pothole.
As the boat filled with water, it started to tilt dangerously to one side.
She watched the painting tilt slowly before it finally fell off the wall.
The old bookshelf began to tilt under the weight of the books.
Please tilt the glass slightly when you pour the drink.
She tilted her head to listen more closely.
The painting on the wall started to tilt after the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lean - to incline or rest in a sloping position
  • synonym: slant - to slope or lean in a particular direction
  • synonym: tip - to move or cause to move to one side so as to lean or slope
  • antonym: upright - in a vertical or straight position
