New Wave

noun
a French film movement (1960s) with innovative techniques
Pronunciation: / nuː weɪv / (AmE), / njuː weɪv / (BrE)
A movement in French cinema of the late 1950s and 1960s characterized by innovative narrative techniques and visual styles, rejecting traditional filmmaking conventions.

Examples

The New Wave cinema
Her film studies thesis focused on the editing techniques developed by the French New Wave.
Several directors from the New Wave challenged established norms and influenced filmmakers worldwide.
The New Wave revolutionized international cinema with its bold storytelling and visual experimentation.
