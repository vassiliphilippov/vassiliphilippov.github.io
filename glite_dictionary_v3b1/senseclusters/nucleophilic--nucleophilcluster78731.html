nucleophilic

adjective
electron-donating (chemistry)
9.5
Pronunciation: /nuː.kliː.ə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ /; / /njuː.kliː.ə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ (AmE), /njuː.kliː.ə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ /; / /nuː.kliː.ə.ˈfɪl.ɪk/ (BrE)
describing a chemical species that tends to donate electrons to form bonds with other atoms or molecules

Examples

Nucleophilic reagent
A nucleophilic reagent donates electrons to an electrophile during a chemical reaction.
The reaction rate increases when a more nucleophilic compound is used.
Water can act as a nucleophilic agent in many organic reactions.
