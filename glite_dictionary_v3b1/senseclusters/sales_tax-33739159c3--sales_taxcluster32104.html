sales tax

noun
tax on goods/services at purchase
Pronunciation: / seɪlz tæks / (AmE), / seɪlz tæks / (BrE)
A tax imposed on the sale of goods and services, calculated as a percentage of the purchase price and collected from the buyer at the point of sale.

Examples

Sales tax rate
Clothing is exempt from sales tax in some states.
The city recently increased its sales tax to fund new schools.
When you buy electronics, the sales tax can make a big difference in the final price.

Related Terms

  • synonym: value-added tax - a tax on the amount by which the value of an article has been increased at each stage of its production or distribution
