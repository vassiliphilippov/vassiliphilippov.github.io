case-hardened

adjective
hardened outer layer
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsˌhɑrdənd / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsˌhɑːdənd / (BrE)
having a hard outer surface and a softer inner core, especially referring to metal objects treated for durability

Examples

Case-hardened steel
The engineer recommended using case-hardened bolts for greater strength in the machinery.
Many tools are case-hardened to withstand repeated use without wearing down quickly.
The case-hardened steel shaft lasted much longer than the untreated one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft - not hard or firm in texture
  • synonym: tempered - treated to increase hardness or toughness
