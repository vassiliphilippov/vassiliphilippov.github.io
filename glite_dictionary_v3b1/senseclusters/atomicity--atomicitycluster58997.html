atomicity

noun
property of having atoms
Pronunciation: / əˌtɑːˈmɪsəti / (AmE), / əˌtɒˈmɪsəti / (BrE)
the property of a mathematical structure, such as a lattice or Boolean algebra, of having atoms as minimal nonzero elements

Examples

Atomicity in a lattice
The atomicity of the Boolean algebra allows each element to be built from its atoms.
Researchers discussed the atomicity of the lattice in their latest mathematical paper.
Understanding atomicity is important when studying the structure of ordered sets.
