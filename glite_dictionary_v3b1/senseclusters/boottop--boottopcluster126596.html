boottop

noun
ship hull section
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌtɑp / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌtɒp / (BrE)
the part of a ship's hull between the waterline and the deck, often painted for protection

Examples

Ship's boottop
The crew repainted the boottop to prevent rust and damage from the sea.
Barnacles often collect on the boottop if the ship is not maintained regularly.
A red stripe along the boottop made the vessel easy to recognize from a distance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeboard - the distance from the waterline to the upper deck of a ship
