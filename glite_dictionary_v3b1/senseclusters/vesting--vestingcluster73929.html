vesting

noun
acquiring legal rights
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a person gains full rights or a legal interest in property, benefits, or assets, often after meeting certain conditions or time requirements

Examples

Full vesting rights
After five years of employment, full vesting in the retirement plan is achieved.
The vesting of stock options gives employees the right to purchase shares at a set price.
Legal vesting occurs when the property title is officially transferred to the new owner.
After ten years of employment, full vesting in the company pension plan was achieved.
The vesting schedule determines when employees gain ownership of their stock options.
She was excited to reach vesting and finally have full access to her retirement funds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forfeiture - the loss of a right or property
