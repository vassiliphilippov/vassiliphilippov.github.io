intransigently

adverb
refusing to compromise
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒəntli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrænsɪdʒəntli / (BrE)
in a manner that refuses to change one's views or agree to a compromise

Examples

To intransigently refuse
The committee members intransigently rejected every proposal for reform.
He intransigently held his position despite mounting pressure from his colleagues.
Negotiations stalled because both sides argued intransigently over minor details.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexibly - in a way that is willing to change or adapt
  • synonym: stubbornly - in a way that shows unwillingness to change or give in
