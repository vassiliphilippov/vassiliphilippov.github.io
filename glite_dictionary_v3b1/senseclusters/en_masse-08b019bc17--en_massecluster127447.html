Examples

Vote en masse

Synonyms

  • all together
    1.0 3.08/m
    all together is used in everyday speech to show everyone acting at once, while en masse sounds more formal and collective.
  • collectively
    3.6 10/m
    collectively is a neutral adverb for joint action in many contexts, while en masse feels slightly more formal and literary.
  • as a group
    2.3 6.09/m
    as a group is a plain phrase meaning members act together in one unit, while en masse sounds more formal and written.
  • en bloc
    8.3 0.03/m
    en bloc means taken or acting as a single unit without dividing parts, while en masse emphasizes people acting together collectively.
  • together
    1.0 215/m
    together means being with others or in the same place, while en masse stresses a group acting as one, often all at once.
