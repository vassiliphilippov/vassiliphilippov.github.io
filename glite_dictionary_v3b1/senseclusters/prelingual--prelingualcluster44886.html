prelingual

adjective
before speech develops
9.1
Pronunciation: / priːˈlɪŋɡwəl / (AmE), / priːˈlɪŋɡwəl / (BrE)
happening or existing before a person has learned to speak or before language is developed

Examples

Prelingual hearing loss
The child was diagnosed with prelingual hearing loss.
Early intervention is crucial for children with prelingual deafness.
Researchers studied the effects of prelingual exposure to sign language.
Some children are born with prelingual hearing loss, meaning it occurs before they learn to speak.
Researchers study prelingual communication to understand how early humans interacted before language.
The diagnosis of prelingual deafness can affect a child's language development and education.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postlingual - occurring after a person has learned to speak
