affrication

noun
conversion of a stop to an affricate (phonetics)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæfrəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæfrɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in speech in which a stop consonant changes into an affricate sound

Examples

Affrication of consonants
In some dialects, affrication of the /t/ sound can occur before certain vowels.
Linguists studied the affrication patterns in regional varieties of English.
The affrication of stops is a common process in Slavic languages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affricate - a consonant that begins as a stop and releases as a fricative
