cohosh

noun
a North American plant of the genus Actaea
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌhɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌhɒʃ / (BrE)
Any of several plants of the genus Actaea, native to North America, having acrid poisonous berries and sometimes used in herbal medicine.

Examples

Cohosh plant
Many species of cohosh are found in woodland areas across North America.
The berries of cohosh are attractive but toxic to humans.
The roots of cohosh have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
