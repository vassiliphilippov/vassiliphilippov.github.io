Johann Mendel

proper noun
founder of modern genetics; Augustinian monk
Pronunciation: / joʊˈhɑːn ˈmɛndl̩ / (AmE), / jəʊˈhan ˈmɛndl / (BrE)
(1822–1884) An Augustinian monk and botanist whose experiments with pea plants established the basic principles of genetics.

Examples

Johann Mendel genetics founder
Johann Mendel is often credited as the father of modern genetics.
Before his groundbreaking research, Johann Mendel was an Augustinian monk in Austria.
The experiments of Johann Mendel with pea plants changed the course of biology.
