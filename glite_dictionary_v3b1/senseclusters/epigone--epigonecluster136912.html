epigone

noun
inferior imitator of a distinguished person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpɪˌɡoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪɡəʊn / (BrE)
a person who imitates someone distinguished, especially in the arts, but is considered inferior

Examples

An epigone of Picasso
Many critics regarded him as merely an epigone of Picasso, lacking originality.
She refused to be called an epigone, insisting her paintings had their own distinct style.
The novel was dismissed as the work of an epigone rather than a true innovator.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitator - a person who copies the behavior or actions of another
