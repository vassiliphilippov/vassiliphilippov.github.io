infill

verb
to fill a gap
5.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfɪl/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfɪl/ (BrE)
to fill a space or gap with material, or to build on vacant land between existing structures, especially in urban areas

Examples

To infill empty lots
The city plans to infill empty lots with new apartment buildings to address the housing shortage.
Developers are infilling unused spaces between older homes to create more urban housing.
The construction crew will infill the trench with gravel before paving the road.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vacate - to leave a place empty
