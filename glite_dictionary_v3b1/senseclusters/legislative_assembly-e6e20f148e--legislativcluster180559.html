legislative assembly

noun
official law-making body
5.4 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlɛdʒ.ɪ.slə.tɪv ə.ˈsɛm.bliː/ /; / /ˈlɛdʒ.ɪ.sleɪ.tɪv ə.ˈsɛm.bliː/ (AmE), /ˈlɛdʒ.ɪ.slə.tɪv ə.ˈsɛm.blɪ/ /; / /ˈlɛdʒ.ɪ.sleɪ.tɪv ə.ˈsɛm.blɪ/ (BrE)
An official group of elected representatives who have the authority to make, amend, or repeal laws in a country or region.

Examples

The legislative assembly members
Members of the legislative assembly are elected every four years by the citizens.
The governor addressed the legislative assembly to outline her policy priorities for the year.
The new tax bill was debated extensively in the legislative assembly before being passed.
