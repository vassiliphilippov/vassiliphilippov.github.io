T-bar lift

noun
ski lift using a T-shaped bar
5.7 4.1
Pronunciation: /tiː bɑːr lɪft/ (AmE), /tiː bɑː lɪft/ (BrE)
A type of ski lift consisting of a T-shaped bar which skiers or snowboarders hold onto or sit against while being pulled uphill.

Examples

A T-bar lift ride
Learning to use the T-bar lift can be tricky for first-time skiers.
The old T-bar lift was replaced with a faster chairlift last season.
We took the T-bar lift to the top of the beginner slope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chairlift - A type of ski lift in which riders sit on chairs suspended from a moving cable
  • synonym: surface lift - A ski lift that pulls riders up the slope while they stand on their skis or snowboards
