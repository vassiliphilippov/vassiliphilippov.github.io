overclock

verb
run hardware above recommended speed
4.6 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.klɑːk/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ˈklɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.klɒk/ /; / /əʊ.və.ˈklɒk/ (BrE)
to make a computer processor or other component run faster than the speed recommended by the manufacturer to improve performance

Examples

To overclock a CPU
Enthusiasts often overclock their CPUs to achieve better performance for gaming or intensive tasks.
He spent the afternoon overclocking his computer to see if he could get higher benchmark scores.
I accidentally overclocked my graphics card and it started overheating.
Many gamers overclock their graphics cards to achieve higher frame rates in demanding games.
If you overclock your processor without proper cooling, it may overheat and become unstable.
