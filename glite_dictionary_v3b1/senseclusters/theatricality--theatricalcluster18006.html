theatricality

noun
exaggerated or artificial quality
Pronunciation: / θiˌætrɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / θɪˌætrɪˈkæləti / (BrE)
the quality of being exaggerated or artificial, especially in a way that resembles the theater

Examples

Sense of theatricality
Her speech was impressive, but it was marred by a sense of theatricality that made it feel less genuine.
The director criticized the theatricality of the actor's performance, calling it excessive and insincere.
The event's decorations and lighting added a layer of theatricality that delighted the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artificiality - the quality of being artificial rather than natural or genuine
