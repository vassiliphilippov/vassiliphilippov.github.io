major lobe

noun
main lobe in an antenna's radiation pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər loʊb / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə ləʊb / (BrE)
the main lobe in the radiation pattern of an antenna, typically aligned with the forward axis and representing the direction of strongest radiation or reception

Examples

The major lobe direction
Engineers examined the major lobe to ensure optimal signal strength along the targeted direction.
Signal interference was minimized by aligning the receiver with the transmitter's major lobe.
The antenna was carefully oriented so that the major lobe pointed toward the broadcast tower.

Related Terms

  • synonym: main lobe - the lobe in a radiation pattern with maximum amplitude, usually in the principal direction
  • antonym: side lobe - a secondary lobe in an antenna radiation pattern, not in the main direction of radiation
