brewer's grain

noun
residue from brewing process
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbruːɚz ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈbruːəz ɡreɪn / (BrE)
the solid residue left after malt and grain have been mashed and lautered in the brewing process, often used as animal feed

Examples

After the beer was brewed, the leftover brewer's grain was delivered to a local farm.

Pigs and chickens can both benefit from the protein in brewer's grain.
