duly

adverb
in a proper or appropriate way
4.6 3.82/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀêli / (AmE), / ÀàdjuÀêli / (BrE)
in a proper or expected manner; as is appropriate or suitable for the circumstances

Examples

Duly congratulated by all
After his achievements were announced, he was duly congratulated by all present.
She was duly warned about the risks involved in the project.
The form was duly signed and submitted before the deadline.

Synonyms

  • properly
    1.0 47/m
    properly is used for correct or normal action in everyday situations, while duly feels more formal or expected.
  • appropriately
    3.5 12/m
    appropriately fits a given situation in neutral speech, while duly is more formal and can mean 'as expected'.
  • suitably
    3.0 1.68/m
    suitably describes something made right for a purpose in everyday use, while duly carries a formal or timely sense.
  • aptly
    3.7 4.01/m
    aptly means something fits neatly or is named well, while duly focuses on proper, expected action or timing.
  • fittingly
    3.9 0.80/m
    fittingly describes an action that matches the situation in tone, while duly often implies formal correctness or being done at the right time.
  • befittingly
    6.8 0.01/m
    befittingly is a formal or old-fashioned way to say something suits the occasion, while duly is formal and also means 'as expected'.
  • appositely
    6.8 0.01/m
    appositely is a rare, literary word for saying something is suitable in context, while duly is common formal English meaning proper or expected.
  • fitly
    8.3 0.09/m
    fitly sounds old or rare and means suitably in form, while duly is standard modern formal English for proper or expected action.
