presumption

noun
legal inference based on established facts
5.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈzʌmp.ʃən/ /; / /priː.ˈzʌmp.ʃən/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈzʌmp.ʃən/ (BrE)
(Law) An inference that a fact is true based on other established or admitted facts, unless disproved.

Examples

Legal presumption
A presumption arises when certain facts are admitted without challenge in the proceedings.
In criminal cases, there is a presumption of innocence until proven guilty.
The court relied on the legal presumption that the missing person was deceased.
