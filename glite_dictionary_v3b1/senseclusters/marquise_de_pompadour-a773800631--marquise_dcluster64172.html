Marquise de Pompadour

proper noun
influential mistress of Louis XV of France
Pronunciation: / mɑːrˈkiːz də ˌpɒmpəˈdʊr / (AmE), / mɑːˈkiːz də ˌpɒmpəˈdʊə / (BrE)
(1721–1764) A French noblewoman and influential court figure who was the official chief mistress of King Louis XV and a patron of the arts.

Examples

Marquise de Pompadour patronage
Exhibitions often highlight the taste and patronage of the Marquise de Pompadour in rococo art.
Many artists of the 18th century sought favor with the Marquise de Pompadour for her significant influence at the French court.
The Marquise de Pompadour played a key role in shaping the cultural landscape of France during the reign of Louis XV.
