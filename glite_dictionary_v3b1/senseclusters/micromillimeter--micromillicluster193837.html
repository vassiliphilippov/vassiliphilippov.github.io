micromillimeter

noun
one billionth of a meter
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈmɪləˌmitər /; / ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈmɪl.əˌmiː.tər / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈmɪlɪˌmiːtə /; / ˌmʌɪ.krəʊˈmɪl.ɪˌmiː.tə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to one billionth of a meter (10^-9 meters).

Examples

One micromillimeter length
At the nanoscale, even a gap of one micromillimeter can be significant for precision engineering applications.
Technicians checked the part tolerances down to the level of several micromillimeters.
The microscope is capable of measuring distances as small as a single micromillimeter.
Scientists once suggested measuring extremely small wavelengths in micromillimetres.
Some conversion tables still list the micromillimetre, although it is now mostly obsolete.
The micromillimetre is not commonly used in modern scientific literature, with nanometre being preferred.
