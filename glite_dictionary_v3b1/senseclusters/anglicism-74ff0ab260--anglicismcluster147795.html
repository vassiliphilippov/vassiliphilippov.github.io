Anglicism

noun
expression peculiar to British English
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡlɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡlɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or idiom characteristic of English, especially British English, or borrowed from English into another language.

Examples

A borrowed Anglicism
Many French speakers use the Anglicism 'weekend' instead of their native term.
Some Anglicisms can be confusing for non-British English learners.
The word 'lorry' is an Anglicism not commonly used in American English.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gallicism - an expression borrowed from French
  • antonym: americanism - an expression characteristic of American English
