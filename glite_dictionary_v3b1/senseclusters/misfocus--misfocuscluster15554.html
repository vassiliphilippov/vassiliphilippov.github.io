misfocus

verb
to focus incorrectly
7.4
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈfoʊ.kəs/ /; / /mə.ˈfoʊ.kəs/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈfəʊ.kəs/ /; / /mə.ˈfəʊ.kəs/ (BrE)
to focus incorrectly, especially so that an image or object is not clear or sharp

Examples

To misfocus a camera
If you misfocus the camera, the image will be blurry.
The photographer accidentally misfocused the lens, ruining the perfect shot.
It is easy to misfocus when adjusting the microscope for the first time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defocus - to make an image or object less sharp or clear
  • antonym: focus - to adjust something so that it is clear or sharp
