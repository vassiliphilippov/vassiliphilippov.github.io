barnacle

noun
small marine crustacean with a hard shell
5.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːr.nə.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈbɑː.nə.kəl/ (BrE)
a small marine crustacean that attaches itself permanently to rocks, boat hulls, and other submerged surfaces, forming a hard shell as an adult

Examples

Boat barnacles
It takes special scrapers to remove barnacles from boats.
Marine biologists study the way barnacles attach themselves to underwater surfaces.
The hull of the old ship was covered in thick barnacle growth.
