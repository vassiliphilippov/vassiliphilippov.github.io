(in science and technology) a system or situation that is extremely unpredictable or sensitive to small changes, making it hard to control or predict

Examples

Chaos theory
In physics, chaos refers to systems where tiny changes in the starting state lead to unpredictable outcomes.
Scientists use mathematical models to study the chaos present in complex systems like the brain.
The weather is often cited as a classic example of chaos in nature.
The team ran chaos in production to verify the system's ability to recover from failures.
As part of onboarding, engineers learn how to design safe chaos experiments before causing outages.
Their SRE group schedules weekly chaos tests to discover hidden weaknesses in distributed services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaos engineering - the engineering discipline of running experiments that introduce failures to validate system resilience
  • synonym: chaos theory - The mathematical study of chaotic systems
  • synonym: fault injection - the technique of deliberately introducing errors or faults into a system to test behavior
  • synonym: resilience testing - testing to ensure systems can continue operating under adverse conditions
