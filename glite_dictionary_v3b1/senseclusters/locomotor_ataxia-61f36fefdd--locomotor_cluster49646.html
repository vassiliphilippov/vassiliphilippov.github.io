locomotor ataxia

noun
spinal cord disease causing unsteady movement
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊkəˈmoʊtər əˈtækiə / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəˈməʊtə əˈtæksiə / (BrE)
A medical condition, usually due to syphilis, in which degeneration of the spinal cord causes unsteady gait, stabbing pains, and loss of coordination of movement.

Examples

Locomotor ataxia symptoms
He gradually developed locomotor ataxia, which eventually left him unable to walk without assistance.
Symptoms of locomotor ataxia include stabbing pains and difficulty walking steadily.
The patient was diagnosed with locomotor ataxia after complaining of severe leg pain and balance problems.
