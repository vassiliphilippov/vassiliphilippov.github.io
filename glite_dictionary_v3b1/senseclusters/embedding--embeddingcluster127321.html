embedding

noun
nested sentence structure
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the placement of a clause or phrase within another clause, creating a subordinate or nested sentence structure

Examples

Clause embedding
The embedding of relative clauses can make sentences difficult to understand for language learners.
Teachers often highlight embedding to show how complex sentences are constructed in English.
Excessive embedding in writing may confuse readers and obscure the main point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coordination - the linking of clauses of equal importance
  • synonym: subordination - the use of subordinate clauses in a sentence
