occupy

verb
to be on someone's mind; preoccupy
3.5 6.27/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêkj…ôÀåpa…™ /; / Àà…ëÀêkj äÀåpa…™ / (AmE), / Àà…íkj äpa…™ /; / Àà…íkj äÀåpa…™ / (BrE)
to fill someone's mind, attention, or time; to keep someone busy or preoccupied

Examples

Thoughts occupy the mind
Her upcoming performance seemed to occupy her mind more than anything else.
The idea of failure continued to occupy his thoughts throughout the day.
Worries about the deadline occupy me every night before I fall asleep.
Her thoughts about the exam occupy her from morning till night.
The new project will occupy most of my evenings for the next month.
Worries about the future occupy his mind constantly.
I find that reading occupies my mind and calms me.
She likes to occupy herself with gardening on weekends.
The children were occupied with puzzles while I cooked dinner.

Synonyms

  • engross
    5.8 1.80/m
    engross draws a person completely into an activity or idea for a long time, while occupy keeps someone busy or preoccupied.
  • absorb
    3.1 10/m
    absorb leaves someone quietly lost in thought and focused for a while, while occupy keeps someone's mind or time filled.
  • engage
    3.5 10/m
    engage involves someone actively in an activity or task, often sharing attention, while occupy means to take up someone's time or mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take up all the attention or interest of someone
  • synonym: busy - keep occupied or engaged
  • antonym: distract - to prevent someone from giving full attention
  • synonym: engross - to occupy someone's complete attention
  • synonym: preoccupy - to dominate or engross the mind
