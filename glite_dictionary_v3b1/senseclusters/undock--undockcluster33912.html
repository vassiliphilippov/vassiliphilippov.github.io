undock

verb
move a vessel out of a dock
6.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈdɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈdɒk / (BrE)
to move a ship, spacecraft, or similar vehicle out of a dock or its secured position

Examples

To undock a ship
After essential repairs, the engineers supervised the process to undock the vessel.
Due to rising tides, we had to undock the ship earlier than planned.
The crew prepared to undock the spacecraft from the station the following morning.
Mission control watched as the spacecraft undocked from the space station.
Once all cargo was loaded, the boat prepared to undock.
The cruise ship undocked smoothly from the harbor this morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dock - to bring a ship or spacecraft into a dock
