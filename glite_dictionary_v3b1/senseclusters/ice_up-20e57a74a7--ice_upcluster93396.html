ice up

verb
to become covered with ice
Pronunciation: / aɪs ʌp / (AmE), / aɪs ʌp / (BrE)
(of a surface) to become covered with a layer of ice, often making it slippery or unusable.

Examples

To ice up a lake
As the lake started to ice up, it signaled the arrival of winter.
If the pipes ice up, the water flow will stop completely.
The car windows began to ice up during the cold night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze - to become covered with ice due to freezing
