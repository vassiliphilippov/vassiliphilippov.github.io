recapitalization

noun
restoring financial stability
7.6
Pronunciation: /riː.kæp.ɪ.təl.ɪz.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.kæp.ɪ.təl.ɪz.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.kæp.ɪ.təl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.kæp.ɪ.təl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of providing new capital or changing a company's financial structure (such as the mix of debt and equity), usually to improve its financial health

Examples

Company recapitalization plan
The central bank approved a major recapitalization to help stabilize the country's financial sector.
After the recapitalization, the bank was able to resume normal lending activities.
Shareholders voted in favor of a recapitalization plan to attract new investors.
The company announced a major recapitalization to attract new investors and reduce its debt.
After the economic downturn, several banks underwent recapitalization to stabilize their finances.
Shareholders voted in favor of the recapitalization plan proposed by the board of directors.
The board approved a recapitalization plan to help the company recover from its financial troubles.
After the recapitalization, new investors owned a majority of the business.
Banks often require recapitalization before agreeing to extend additional credit to struggling firms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bailout - financial help given to a failing business or economy
  • antonym: liquidation - the process of closing a business and selling its assets
  • synonym: restructuring - the act of reorganizing a company's structure or finances
