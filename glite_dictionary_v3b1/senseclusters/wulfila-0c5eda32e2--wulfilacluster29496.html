Wulfila

proper noun
Christian bishop, inventor of Gothic alphabet
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌlfɪlə / (AmE), / ˈwʊlfɪlə / (BrE)
A Christian bishop of the Visigoths (311–383), traditionally credited with inventing the Gothic alphabet and translating the Bible into Gothic.

Examples

Wulfila the bishop
Wulfila is best known for creating the first Gothic translation of the Bible.
Scholars debate whether Wulfila truly invented the Gothic alphabet or adapted earlier scripts.
The legacy of Wulfila influenced the spread of Christianity among the Germanic peoples.
