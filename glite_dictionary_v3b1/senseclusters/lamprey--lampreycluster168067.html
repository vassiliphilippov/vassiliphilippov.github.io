lamprey

noun
primitive jawless fish with sucking mouth
9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈlæm.priː/ /; / /ˈlæm.preɪ/ (AmE), /ˈlæm.preɪ/ /; / /ˈlæm.priː/ (BrE)
A primitive jawless fish with a long, eel-like body and a round, sucking mouth equipped with a rasping tongue, found in fresh or coastal waters.

Examples

A lamprey fish
A lamprey infestation can have a devastating impact on local fish populations.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of the sea lamprey in the Great Lakes.
The lamprey attaches itself to other fish with its sucking mouth to feed.
