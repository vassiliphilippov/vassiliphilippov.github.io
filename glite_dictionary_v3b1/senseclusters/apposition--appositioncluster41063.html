apposition

noun
biological process of wall thickening
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The increase in the thickness of a cell wall by the successive deposition of layers of material.

Examples

Cell wall apposition
Certain plant cell walls grow by apposition, increasing thickness layer by layer.
Some bacteria rely on apposition to reinforce their protective barriers.
The process of apposition allows for the strengthening of woody tissue in trees.
