sweep

noun
American football running play around end of line
6.6 0.28/m
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiÀêp / (BrE)
(in American football) an offensive play in which the ball carrier runs around the end of the line to advance the ball.

Examples

A sweep play
During the first quarter, the running back broke free on a sweep for a touchdown.
The team executed a sweep to the right, gaining fifteen yards on the play.
Their offense relies heavily on the sweep to exploit the defense's weak side.

Synonyms

  • end run
    6.5 0.01/m
