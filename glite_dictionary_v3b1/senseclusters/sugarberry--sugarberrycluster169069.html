sugarberry

noun
sweet edible berry of the southern US
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsʊɡɚˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡəbɛri / (BrE)
A small, sweet, edible dark purple to black berry with a large pit, found chiefly in the southern United States.

Examples

A ripe sugarberry
Birds love to feed on the ripe sugarberries that fall to the ground in late summer.
The sugarberry isn’t sold in most supermarkets because it is delicate and perishable.
We picked a bucket of sugarberries during our hike in Louisiana.
