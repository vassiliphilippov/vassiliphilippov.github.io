scarification

noun
skin-cutting procedure
9.0
Pronunciation: /skɑːr.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /skæ.rɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /skɑː.rɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /skæ.rɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a medical or minor surgical procedure in which small cuts are made in the skin to treat, drain, or stimulate healing of a lesion

Examples

Medical scarification procedure
The doctor recommended scarification to help the wound heal more quickly.
In some traditional practices, scarification is used for both medical and cultural reasons.
After the infection did not improve, the nurse performed scarification to drain the abscess.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cauterization - the process of burning a part of the body to remove or close off a part of it
  • synonym: incision - a surgical cut made in skin or flesh
