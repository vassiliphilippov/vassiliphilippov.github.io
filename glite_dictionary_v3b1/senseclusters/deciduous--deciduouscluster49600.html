deciduous

adjective
shed at the end of a growth period
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪdʒuəs / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪdjuːəs / (BrE)
(of teeth, antlers, etc.) falling off or shed at a particular stage of development or after a period of growth.

Examples

Deciduous teeth fall out
Most mammals have deciduous teeth that are replaced by permanent ones as they grow.
The deciduous antlers of deer are shed annually and regrown the next season.
Veterinarians check for retained deciduous teeth in young dogs as part of routine exams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanent - (of teeth) meant to last or remain throughout life, not shed
