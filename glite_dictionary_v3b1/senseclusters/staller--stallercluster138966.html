staller

noun
stable worker
7.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààst…îl…ôr / (AmE), / Ààst…îÀêl…ô / (BrE)
a person who puts animals into stalls or cares for animals kept in stalls, especially in a stable

Examples

Staller at the stable
The staller arrived early to prepare the horses for their morning exercise.
After the competition, the staller made sure each animal was comfortable in its stall.
She worked as a staller at the local riding school during the summer holidays.

Synonyms

  • stabler
    5.5 0.01/m
    a stabler cares for horses and animals in a stable, while a staller focuses on putting animals into individual stalls.
  • groom
    5.3 0.83/m
    a groom looks after horses in a stable, while a staller cares for animals kept in stalls of any kind.
  • hostler
    9.9 0.06/m
    a hostler tends horses at inns or stables, while a staller generally cares for animals kept in stalls at a stable.
