mercaptopurine

noun
chemotherapy drug for leukemia
Pronunciation: / mərˌkæptəˈpjʊriːn / (AmE), / məˌkæptəˈpjʊəriːn / (BrE)
a drug used in chemotherapy, especially to treat acute lymphocytic leukemia, by interfering with purine metabolism

Examples

mercaptopurine drug
Doctors monitor blood counts closely when patients are taking mercaptopurine.
Research has shown that mercaptopurine can be effective in maintenance therapy for acute lymphoblastic leukemia.
The patient started a course of mercaptopurine to treat her leukemia.
