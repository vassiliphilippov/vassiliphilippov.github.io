Giraffidae

noun
family of giraffes and relatives
9.6
Pronunciation: / dʒɪˈræfɪˌdi / (AmE), / dʒɪˈræfɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large African ruminant mammals that includes giraffes and their extinct relatives.

Examples

The Giraffidae family
Fossil records show that Giraffidae once included several extinct species.
Scientists study the evolution of Giraffidae to understand their adaptation to African environments.
The Giraffidae family is known for its long-necked members, especially the modern giraffe.

Related Terms
