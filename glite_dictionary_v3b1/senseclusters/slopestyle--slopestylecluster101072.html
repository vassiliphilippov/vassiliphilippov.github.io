slopestyle

noun
trick-based competition
Pronunciation: / ˈsloʊpstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsləʊpstaɪl / (BrE)
a sports competition (such as in snowboarding, skiing, or biking) where athletes perform tricks on a course with jumps and obstacles

Examples

A slopestyle competition
He trained for months to compete in the national slopestyle event at the winter sports festival.
The judges awarded high scores for creativity and difficulty in the slopestyle competition.
Many young athletes dream of winning a medal in Olympic slopestyle snowboarding.
He won a gold medal in slopestyle at the Winter Olympics.
The slopestyle course featured rails, jumps, and other creative obstacles.
Many young athletes dream of competing in slopestyle events around the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downhill - a race down a slope, usually focused on speed rather than tricks
  • synonym: freestyle - a style of competition where athletes perform tricks freely
  • synonym: halfpipe - a snowboarding or skiing event performed in a U-shaped ramp
  • synonym: moguls - a skiing event involving bumps and jumps on a slope
