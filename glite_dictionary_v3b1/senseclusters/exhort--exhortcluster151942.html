Examples

To exhort strongly
During the meeting, he exhorted everyone to support the new policy.
She exhorts her students to strive for academic excellence.
The coach exhorted the players to give their best effort in the final match.
At the rally, the leader kept exhorting the crowd to remain peaceful and united.

Synonyms

  • urge
    3.2 44/m
    urge is to strongly encourage, persuade, or advise someone to do something, while exhort is more formal and public in tone.
  • press
    3.9 15/m
    press is to insistently urge or demand action, often repeatedly, while exhort is more about strong moral or public encouragement.
  • encourage
    2.3 100/m
    encourage gives support and confidence to help someone try or continue, while exhort is stronger and more forceful urging.
  • spur
    4.1 7.08/m
    spur pushes or inspires action often by reward or need, while exhort is direct verbal urging to do something.
  • urge on
    3.4 0.50/m
    urge on encourages someone during action to go faster or try harder, while exhort is a strong general verbal urging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
  • synonym: encourage - to give someone support, confidence, or hope
  • synonym: urge - to encourage or try to persuade someone earnestly
