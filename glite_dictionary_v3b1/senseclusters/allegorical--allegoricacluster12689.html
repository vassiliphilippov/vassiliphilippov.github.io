allegorical

adjective
containing or using allegory
7.5 6.2
Pronunciation: /æ.lə.ˈgɔː.rɪ.kəl/ /; / /æl.ɪ.ˈgɔː.rɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ə.lɪ.ˈgɔː.rɪ.kəl/ /; / /æl.ɪ.ˈgɔː.rɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
(of a work of art, story, or idea) containing or using allegory; having a hidden moral or political meaning.

Examples

An allegorical story
Her novel takes an allegorical approach to the theme of freedom.
Many fables are allegorical, using animals to convey moral lessons.
The painting is highly allegorical, representing justice as a blindfolded woman.
