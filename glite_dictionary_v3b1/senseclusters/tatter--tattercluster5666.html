tatter

verb
to become ragged
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtæt.ər/ (AmE), /ˈtæt.ə/ (BrE)
to become torn, ragged, or worn out, especially from age or use

Examples

Old clothes tatter
After years of use, the old curtains began to tatter at the edges.
The child's favorite blanket started to tatter from being washed so often.
If you leave the flag outside in storms, it will quickly tatter in the wind.

  • synonym: shred - to tear or cut into thin strips
