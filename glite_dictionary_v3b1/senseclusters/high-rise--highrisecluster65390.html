high-rise

adjective
tall building with many stories
1.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌraɪz / (BrE)
(of a building) having many stories and usually equipped with elevators; tall.

Examples

High-rise office block
Many high-rise buildings were constructed during the housing boom.
The city center features several high-rise office blocks that dominate the skyline.
They moved into a high-rise apartment overlooking the river.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-rise - (of a building) having only a few stories
