rake

verb
sweep along, especially with fire or light
7.9
Pronunciation: / reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
to sweep or move across something, especially with gunfire or light, so as to cover its entire length

Examples

Sunlight rakes the valley
At sunrise, beams of sunlight raked the valley, illuminating the mist.
Enemy machine guns raked the advancing soldiers as they crossed the open field.
The warship raked the shoreline with a barrage of heavy artillery fire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombard - to attack continuously with bombs, shells, or missiles
  • synonym: sweep - to move swiftly and smoothly in a wide arc or over a surface
