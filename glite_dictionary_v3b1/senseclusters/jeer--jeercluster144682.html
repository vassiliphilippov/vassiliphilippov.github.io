jeer

noun
a mocking or scornful remark
7.5 0.47/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’Éªr / (AmE), / dÊ’ÉªÉ™ / (BrE)
a mocking or derisive remark or shout; an expression of scorn or contempt

Examples

A loud jeer
His speech was interrupted by a jeer from the back of the auditorium.
She ignored the jeer and continued her performance with confidence.
The crowd responded with a loud jeer when the referee made an unpopular call.

Synonyms

  • sneer
    5.6 0.36/m
    sneer is a quiet, contemptuous look or remark, while jeer is often a loud, public mocking shout.
  • mock
    5.5 2.02/m
    mock is an act of making fun to hurt or copy someone, while jeer is usually a short mocking shout.
  • fleer
    9.9 0.05/m
    fleer is a scornful look or remark often with a grin, while jeer is more often a loud, public insult.
  • mockery
    4.9 3.98/m
    mockery is the repeated act of making fun or ridicule, while jeer is usually a single teasing shout.
