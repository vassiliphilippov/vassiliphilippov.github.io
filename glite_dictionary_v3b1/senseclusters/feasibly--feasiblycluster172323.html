feasibly

adverb
in a practical or achievable way
1.7 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfiː.zə.bliː/ /; / /ˈfiː.zɪ.bliː/ (AmE), /ˈfiː.zə.bliː/ /; / /ˈfiː.zɪ.bliː/ (BrE)
In a way that is possible or practical to do; in a manner that can be achieved.

Examples

To feasibly complete
It is not feasibly possible to finish the project in two days without additional help.
The changes were implemented as feasibly as possible, given the limited resources.
We must consider whether the plan can feasibly be carried out with our current budget.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impossibly - in a way that cannot be done; not feasible
  • synonym: practicably - in a way that can be put into practice; feasibly
