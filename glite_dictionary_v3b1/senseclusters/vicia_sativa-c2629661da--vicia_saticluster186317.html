Vicia sativa

noun
annual fodder plant; common vetch
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʃiə səˈtaɪvə / (AmE), / ˈvɪsɪə səˈtiːvə / (BrE)
an annual climbing plant of the legume family, grown as fodder and for improving soil fertility; also known as common vetch

Examples

Vicia sativa plant
Farmers plant Vicia sativa to enrich the soil with nitrogen before sowing cereal crops.
Research shows that Vicia sativa can increase crop yields when used in crop rotation.
The livestock graze eagerly on Vicia sativa during the spring months.
