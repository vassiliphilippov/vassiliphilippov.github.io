thicky

adjective
dense or not watery
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪki / (AmE), / ˈθɪki / (BrE)
having a thick or dense consistency; not thin or watery

Examples

Thicky soup
The soup turned thicky after adding the cream and letting it simmer.
She prefers her milkshakes thicky rather than runny.
After cooling, the sauce became quite thicky and rich.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thick - having a relatively great distance between opposite sides; not thin
  • antonym: watery - thin or diluted with water; not thick
