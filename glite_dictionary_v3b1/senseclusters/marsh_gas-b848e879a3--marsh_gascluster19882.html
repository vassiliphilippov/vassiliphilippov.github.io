marsh gas

noun
methane from decaying plants in marshes
Pronunciation: / mɑrʃ ɡæs / (AmE), / mɑːʃ ɡas / (BrE)
a colorless, flammable gas (methane) produced in marshes or wetlands by the decomposition of organic matter

Examples

Marsh gas bubbles
Marsh gas is often seen bubbling up from the bottom of swamps and wetlands.
Some experts warn that releasing marsh gas contributes to climate change due to its potency as a greenhouse gas.
The scientists measured the amount of marsh gas produced by decaying plants.
Bubbles of marsh gas appeared on the surface of the swamp after heavy rainfall.
During the summer, a faint smell of marsh gas rose from the wetlands.
The scientist studied marsh gas emissions to understand their impact on climate change.
