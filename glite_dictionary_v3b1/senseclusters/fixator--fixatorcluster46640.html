fixator

noun
device for holding parts
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪkˌseɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfɪkseɪtə / (BrE)
a device or structure used to hold something (especially a bone or body part) firmly in place, especially during healing or repair

Examples

External fixator device
The surgeon applied an external fixator to stabilize the broken bone during healing.
A metal fixator was used to keep the machinery parts aligned during assembly.
Engineers designed a new fixator to hold the panels securely in place.
The doctor used an external fixator to keep the fractured bone stable during recovery.
After the accident, a fixator was attached to his arm to ensure proper alignment.
Modern surgical techniques often involve the use of a fixator for complex bone injuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cast - a hard protective shell applied to immobilize a broken bone
  • synonym: clamp - a tool used to hold objects tightly together
  • synonym: fastener - a device that attaches or secures objects together
  • synonym: splint - a rigid device used to support and immobilize a broken bone
