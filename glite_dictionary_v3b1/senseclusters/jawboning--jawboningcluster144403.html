jawboning

noun
official persuasion
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɔːr.boʊ.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɔː.bəʊ.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
the use of strong persuasion or pressure, especially by officials, to influence people's actions or decisions

Examples

Official jawboning effort
The government's jawboning convinced several companies to lower their prices.
Despite months of jawboning from officials, the industry was slow to change its practices.
Central bank jawboning can sometimes influence currency markets without any actual policy changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coercion - the act of forcing someone to do something by pressure or threats
  • antonym: negotiation - discussion aimed at reaching an agreement
