hornfels

noun
fine-grained metamorphic rock
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…îrnÀåf…õlz / (AmE), / Ààh…îÀênf…õlz / (BrE)
A compact, fine-grained metamorphic rock formed by the action of heat on clay-rich rocks, typically in contact with an igneous intrusion.

Examples

A hornfels rock
Because of its hardness and durability, hornfels is sometimes used in construction.
The formation of hornfels occurs when clay-rich rocks are baked by igneous intrusions.
The geologists discovered a layer of hornfels near the old quarry site.
