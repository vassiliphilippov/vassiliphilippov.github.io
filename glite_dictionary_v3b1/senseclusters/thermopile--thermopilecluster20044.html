thermopile

noun
instrument for measuring heat radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈθɝː.moʊˌpaɪl / (AmE), / ˈθɜː.məʊˌpaɪl / (BrE)
A scientific instrument consisting of several thermocouple junctions connected in series, used to measure heat radiation.

Examples

A thermopile instrument
A thermopile can be more sensitive than a single thermocouple for measuring thermal energy.
Modern remote sensors often include a thermopile array to map temperature differences across surfaces.
The laboratory used a thermopile to detect even faint sources of infrared radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermocouple - a device consisting of two different conductors forming electrical junctions at differing temperatures
