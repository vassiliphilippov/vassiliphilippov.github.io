underexpose

verb
expose to too little light (photography)
5.1 6.1
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ɪk.ˈspoʊz/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ɪk.ˈspəʊz/ (BrE)
to expose photographic film or a sensor to insufficient light, resulting in a darker image than intended

Examples

To underexpose a photo
If you underexpose your photos, the details in the shadows may be lost.
Make sure not to underexpose when taking pictures in low light conditions.
The photographer accidentally underexposed the film, resulting in darker than expected images.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overexpose - to expose photographic film or a sensor to too much light
