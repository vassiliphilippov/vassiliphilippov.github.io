PKD

noun
polycystic kidney disease (medical)
5.5
Pronunciation: / piː keɪ ˈdiː / (AmE), / piː keɪ ˈdiː / (BrE)
A kidney disease characterized by enlarged kidneys with many cysts, often leading to kidney failure; abbreviation for polycystic kidney disease.

Examples

PKD kidney disease
Early detection of PKD can improve a patient's long-term prognosis.
Genetic testing can help confirm a diagnosis of PKD in affected families.
People with PKD may require dialysis if their kidneys fail.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: kidney failure - loss of kidney function
  • synonym: polycystic kidney disease - a genetic disorder causing many fluid-filled cysts in the kidneys
