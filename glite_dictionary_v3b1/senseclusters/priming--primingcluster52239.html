priming

noun
cognitive effect from prior stimulus
6.8 0.16/m

(in psychology and cognitive science) the unconscious influence on a person's response to a stimulus caused by prior exposure to a related stimulus; often measured as faster or more accurate recognition or behaviour.

Examples

Word priming effect
The experiment demonstrated strong priming when participants responded faster to related words.
Advertising often uses subtle priming to make consumers prefer a product without noticing.
After the lesson on animals, students showed priming by recalling related words more quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: priming effect - the measurable change in responses (speed or accuracy) caused by prior exposure to related stimuli
  • synonym: semantic priming - a type of priming in which words or concepts related in meaning make recognition faster or easier
