recusal

noun
legal disqualification due to conflict of interest
9.5 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / r…™ÀàkjuÀêz…ôl / (AmE), / r…™ÀàkjuÀêz…ôl / (BrE)
(law) the act of removing oneself, or being removed, from participation in a legal case due to a potential conflict of interest or bias.

Examples

Judge recusal request
After filing for recusal, the lawyer argued that the judge's previous involvement might impact fairness.
Her recusal ensured that the case was heard by someone with no conflict of interest.
The judge's recusal was requested after concerns were raised about his impartiality.

  • antonym: impartiality - the quality of not being biased or prejudiced
