G

noun
gravitational constant in physics
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒi / (AmE), / dʒiː / (BrE)
the universal constant relating gravitational force to mass and distance in Newton's law of gravitation, equal to approximately 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·(m/kg)²

Examples

G constant in physics
Physicists use G to determine the strength of gravitational attraction in the universe.
Recent experiments have aimed to improve the precision of measurement for G.
The value of G is crucial for calculating the force between two massive objects.
