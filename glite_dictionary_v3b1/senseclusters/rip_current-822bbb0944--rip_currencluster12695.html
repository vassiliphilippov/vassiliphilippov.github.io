rip current

noun
strong water current flowing out from shore
Pronunciation: / rɪp ˈkɝː.ənt / (AmE), / rɪp ˈkʌr.ənt / (BrE)
a strong, narrow current of water flowing outward from the shore, often posing a hazard to swimmers

Examples

A rip current hazard
Beach signs often indicate areas where a rip current is likely to form.
Lifeguards warn swimmers about the dangers of a rip current near the pier.
Several people had to be rescued after being caught in a rip current yesterday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: undertow - a current of water below and in a different direction from the surface current
