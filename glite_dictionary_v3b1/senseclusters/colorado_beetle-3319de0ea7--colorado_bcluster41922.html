Colorado beetle

noun
potato leaf-eating beetle, pest species
7.3 6.0
Pronunciation: /kɑː.lə.ˈræd.oʊ ˈbiː.təl/ /; / /kɑː.lə.ˈrɑː.doʊ ˈbiː.dəl/ (AmE), /kɒ.lə.ˈrɑː.dəʊ ˈbiː.təl/ /; / /kɒ.lə.ˈræd.əʊ ˈbiː.təl/ (BrE)
a small black-and-yellow beetle that feeds on potato leaves in both its adult and larval stages; originally native to the eastern Rocky Mountains but now found worldwide

Examples

The Colorado beetle larvae
A single Colorado beetle can lay hundreds of eggs on the underside of potato leaves.
Farmers are concerned about controlling the spread of the Colorado beetle in their fields.
The Colorado beetle has devastated several potato crops this season.
