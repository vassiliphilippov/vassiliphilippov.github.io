keep pace

verb
go at the same speed or rate
3.6 3.0
Pronunciation: /kiːp peɪs/ (AmE), /kiːp peɪs/ (BrE)
to move, progress, or develop at the same rate as someone or something else

Examples

To keep pace with technology
As technology advances rapidly, it can be hard to keep pace with the latest developments.
She works hard to keep pace with her more experienced colleagues.
The runners struggled to keep pace with the leader in the final lap.
Governments must keep pace with public expectations for transparency.
It's difficult for small companies to keep pace with rapid technological changes.
She struggled to keep pace during the intense workout session.
