risk capital

noun
money for high-risk investments
7.2
Pronunciation: / rɪsk ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / rɪsk ˈkapɪtəl / (BrE)
money that is available to invest in ventures with a high chance of loss, especially new or speculative businesses

Examples

Risk capital investment
Banks are often reluctant to provide risk capital for projects without a proven track record.
He allocated a portion of his portfolio as risk capital for speculative investments.
Many entrepreneurs rely on risk capital from investors to launch innovative startups.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seed money - money used to start a new business or project
  • synonym: venture capital - money invested in a new business, especially one with risk
