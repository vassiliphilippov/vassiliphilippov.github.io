stifling

adjective
oppressively hot and suffocating
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪflɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstaɪflɪŋ / (BrE)
(of heat or air) very hot and causing difficulty in breathing; oppressive and suffocating.

Examples

The stifling heat
The stifling heat in the room made it hard to concentrate on the meeting.
The air was so stifling during the afternoon that everyone felt drowsy and exhausted.
We escaped the stifling atmosphere outside by stepping into the air-conditioned café.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oppressive - uncomfortably hot or close
  • antonym: refreshing - pleasantly cool and invigorating
  • synonym: sultry - hot and humid, oppressively so
