Nepenthaceae

noun
family of tropical pitcher plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪˌpɛnθəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / nɪˌpɛnθəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
a family of tropical carnivorous plants, coextensive with the genus Nepenthes, known for their pitcher-shaped leaves used to trap insects.

Examples

The Nepenthaceae family
Botanists have studied Nepenthaceae for insights into carnivorous plant evolution.
Several species within the Nepenthaceae possess remarkable adaptations for catching insects.
The Nepenthaceae are noted for their unique pitcher-shaped traps.
