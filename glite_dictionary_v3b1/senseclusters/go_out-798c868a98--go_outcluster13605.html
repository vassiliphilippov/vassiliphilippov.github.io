go out

verb
leave to participate in task or mission
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /goʊ aʊt/ (AmE), /gəʊ aʊt/ (BrE)
to leave a place or depart from an area, often to do a specific activity or task

Examples

The troops will go out at dawn for reconnaissance.

Please remember to lock the door when you go out.
She went out for a walk after dinner to get some fresh air.
