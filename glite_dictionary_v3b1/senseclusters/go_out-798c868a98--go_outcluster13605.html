Synonyms

  • leave
    1.0 507/m
    leave is to go away from a place or organization for any reason, while go out often means leaving to do something specific.
  • head out
    2.5 7.10/m
    head out is used when someone leaves to begin a trip or go somewhere, while go out is general leaving for an activity.
  • get out
    1.0 80/m
    get out often sounds informal or urgent, meaning leave quickly or escape, while go out is a neutral word for leaving.
  • exit
    1.0 8.88/m
    exit is a more formal or written verb for leaving a place, while go out is common spoken language for departing.
  • step out
    2.3 11/m
    step out means leaving a room briefly for a small purpose like a walk or call, while go out can be longer.
