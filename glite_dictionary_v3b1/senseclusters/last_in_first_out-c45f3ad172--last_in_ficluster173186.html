last in first out

noun
an inventory accounting method
8.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /læst ɪn fɜːrst aʊt/ (AmE), /lɑːst ɪn fɜːst aʊt/ (BrE)
An inventory accounting method in which the most recently acquired items are recorded as sold first.

Examples

Last in first out accounting
The accountant explained the pros and cons of last in first out versus FIFO.
The company switched to a last in first out approach to align better with rising material costs.
Using last in first out, the newest inventory is recorded as sold first, impacting tax calculations.
