judgment

noun
the act of forming an opinion or decision
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt /; / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt /; / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
an opinion or decision formed after careful thought

Examples

Personal judgment
He trusted his manager’s judgment when it came to choosing new team members.
Her judgment of the risks involved proved to be accurate.
You need to use your best judgment in emergencies.
His judgment was respected by all his colleagues.
In her judgment, the proposal would benefit the entire community.
We must not let personal feelings affect our judgment.
He trusted his friend's judgement when making important career decisions.
Her judgement of the situation proved to be accurate and insightful.
Many people questioned the coach's judgement after the team's unexpected loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assessment - the act of evaluating or appraising a person or situation
  • synonym: decision - a conclusion or resolution reached after consideration
  • antonym: indecision - inability to decide; hesitation
  • synonym: opinion - a belief or view about something
  • antonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion that is not based on reason or actual experience
  • synonym: verdict - a decision on an issue in a legal case or by a jury
