reframe

verb
to change perspective
4.8
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈfreɪm/ /; / /rɪ.ˈfreɪm/ /; / /rə.ˈfreɪm/ (AmE), /riː.ˈfreɪm/ /; / /rɪ.ˈfreɪm/ /; / /rə.ˈfreɪm/ (BrE)
to change the way you think about, interpret, or present a situation, idea, or problem, especially to see it in a more positive or useful way

Examples

To reframe a problem
She decided to reframe her failure as a valuable learning experience.
Therapists often help clients reframe negative thoughts into more positive ones.
By reframing the challenge, the team found a creative solution.
After talking to her mentor, she reframed her failure as a valuable learning experience.
The therapist encouraged him to reframe his negative thoughts about the situation.
By reframing the challenge, the team found a creative solution to the problem.
The therapist encouraged her to reframe her negative thoughts in a more positive light.
We need to reframe the discussion to focus on possible solutions instead of problems.
By reframing the challenge as a learning experience, he felt more motivated to continue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to agree to or receive something as it is
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
  • synonym: reconsider - to think about something again, especially to change a decision or opinion
  • synonym: reinterpret - to explain or understand something in a new or different way
