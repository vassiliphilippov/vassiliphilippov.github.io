Stymphalian

noun
mythical man-eating bird
9.9
Pronunciation: / stɪmˈfeɪliən / (AmE), / stɪmˈfeɪliən / (BrE)
a member of a flock of man-eating birds in Greek mythology, famously hunted by the hero Heracles

Examples

Stymphalian bird attacks
The hero Heracles faced the deadly Stymphalian birds as one of his twelve labors.
Legends tell of a wounded Stymphalian that flew from the marsh after the battle.
Ancient stories describe the metallic feathers of a Stymphalian as sharp enough to pierce armor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: griffin - a legendary creature with the body of a lion and the head and wings of an eagle
  • synonym: harpy - a mythical bird-woman creature in Greek mythology
