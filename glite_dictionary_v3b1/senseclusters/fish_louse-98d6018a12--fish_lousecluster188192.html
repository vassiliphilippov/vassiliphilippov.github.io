fish louse

noun
parasitic crustacean on fish
7.9
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ laʊs / (AmE), / fɪʃ laʊs / (BrE)
A small parasitic crustacean, especially a copepod, that attaches to fish and feeds on their blood or skin.

Examples

A fish louse parasite
A single fish louse can cause severe irritation and damage to its host.
Biologists studied the effects of the fish louse on different species of freshwater fish.
The salmon farm had to be shut down due to a fish louse infestation.
