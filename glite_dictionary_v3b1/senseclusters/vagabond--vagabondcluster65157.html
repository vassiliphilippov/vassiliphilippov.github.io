vagabond

adjective
wandering with no fixed home
6.2 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈvæɡəˌbɑnd /; / ˈvæɡ.əˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ˈvaɡəbɒnd /; / ˈvæɡ.əbɒnd / (BrE)
wandering from place to place with no fixed home; having a nomadic or unsettled lifestyle

Examples

Vagabond lifestyle
After years of living a vagabond existence, he finally settled down in a small town.
His vagabond lifestyle made it hard for him to maintain friendships.
She wrote a memoir about her vagabond travels across three continents.
During his vagabond years, he rarely stayed in one city for more than a month.
Her vagabond lifestyle made it difficult to maintain lasting friendships.
The vagabond musicians traveled from town to town, playing their songs wherever they could.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nomadic - living a wandering life, moving from place to place
  • antonym: settled - living in one place permanently, opposite of wandering
  • synonym: vagrant - tending to wander from place to place; homeless; a person with no settled home or job
