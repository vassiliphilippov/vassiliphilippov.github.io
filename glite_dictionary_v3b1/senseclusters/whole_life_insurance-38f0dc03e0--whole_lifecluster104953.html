whole life insurance

noun
life insurance with fixed premiums for life
Pronunciation: / hoʊl laɪf ɪnˈʃʊr.əns / (AmE), / həʊl laɪf ɪnˈʃɔː.rəns / (BrE)
A type of life insurance that covers the insured for their entire lifetime, with fixed annual premiums and a guaranteed payout upon death.

Examples

A whole life insurance policy
He compared several whole life insurance policies before deciding on the best option.
Many people choose whole life insurance to ensure lifelong financial protection for their families.
The premiums for whole life insurance remain the same throughout the policyholder's lifetime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: term life insurance - life insurance for a specified period, not for the insured's whole life
