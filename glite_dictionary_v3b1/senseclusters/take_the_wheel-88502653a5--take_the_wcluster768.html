Synonyms

  • take charge
    3.5 5/m
    take charge means accepting responsibility and leading people directly, while take the wheel often suggests stepping in or driving.
  • take the helm
    4.4 0.5/m
    take the helm means leading an organization from a top position, while take the wheel can mean driving or taking charge more casually.
  • take the lead
    3.2 4/m
    take the lead means starting or guiding an activity, while take the wheel focuses on assuming control or responsibility, sometimes literally driving.
  • hold the reins
    4.9 0.1/m
    hold the reins means managing or controlling a group quietly from behind the scenes, while take the wheel suggests actively stepping in to lead.
  • take control
    3.5 11/m
    take control means to gain power or authority, often strongly or suddenly, while take the wheel can be gentler and sometimes literal.
  • take over
    3.1 40/m
    take over means replace someone and run something, often formally or forcefully, while take the wheel can mean simply stepping in.
