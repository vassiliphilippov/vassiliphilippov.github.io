freeze

noun
period of freezing weather
Pronunciation: / friːz / (AmE), / friːz / (BrE)
a period or process when the temperature is cold enough for liquids to turn solid (like water freezing or a cold spell)

Examples

A winter freeze
A sudden freeze last night damaged many of the crops.
After the rain, a severe freeze turned the roads into ice.
The city warned residents to prepare for another hard freeze this weekend.
Scientists recorded the freeze in the laboratory as the water solidified.
The pond experienced its first freeze of the year last night.
We must harvest the crops before the first hard freeze arrives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frost - a thin layer of ice crystals formed on the ground or other surfaces during cold weather
  • antonym: thaw - the process of a solid (usually ice) becoming liquid by warming
