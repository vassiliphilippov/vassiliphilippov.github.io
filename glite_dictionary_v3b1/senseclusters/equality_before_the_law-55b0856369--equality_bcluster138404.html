equality before the law

noun
equal treatment by the law
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkwɑləti bɪˈfɔr ðə lɔ / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɒləti bɪˈfɔː ðə lɔː / (BrE)
The principle that all individuals are entitled to equal protection and treatment under the law, without discrimination.

Examples

Equality before the law principle
Activists fought for equality before the law regardless of race or religion.
Many countries enshrine equality before the law as a core value in their legal systems.
The constitution guarantees equality before the law for every citizen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discrimination - unfair treatment due to differences such as race or gender
  • synonym: equal protection - the right to equal treatment by the law
