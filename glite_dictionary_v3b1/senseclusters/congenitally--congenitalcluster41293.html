congenitally

adverb
innately or inherently
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəli / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəli / (BrE)
in a way that is innate or deeply characteristic of someone, often used figuratively to mean naturally or inherently

Examples

Congenitally cheerful
She is congenitally optimistic, always seeing the bright side of every situation.
Some people seem congenitally unable to tell a lie, no matter the circumstances.
He was congenitally curious, always asking questions about how things worked.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired - developed or learned, not present from birth
  • synonym: inherently - in a way that is a permanent or essential part of something
  • synonym: innately - in a way that exists from birth or is natural
