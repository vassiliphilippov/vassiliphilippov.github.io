hansel

verb
to give opening gift
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhæn.səl/ (AmE), /ˈhæn.səl/ (BrE)
to give a small gift or token to celebrate the beginning or first use of something, especially as a good luck gesture

Examples

To hansel a house
Traditionally, neighbors would hansel a new house with bread and salt for good luck.
They decided to hansel their new boat by sharing a meal on its first voyage.
It was customary to hansel the shop on opening day with a small token.
