off a cliff

adverb
into a dangerous or disastrous situation
3.3
Pronunciation: / ɔf ə klɪf / (AmE), / ɒf ə klɪf / (BrE)
in or into a situation that leads to danger, failure, or disaster, often suddenly or irreversibly

Examples

Go off a cliff
If we keep ignoring these problems, the company could go off a cliff financially.
Many investors panicked and sold their stocks off a cliff during the market crash.
She warned the team not to rush off a cliff with big decisions.
