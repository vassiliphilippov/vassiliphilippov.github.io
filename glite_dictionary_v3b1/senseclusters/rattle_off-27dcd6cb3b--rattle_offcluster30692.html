rattle off

verb
say or list quickly and easily
Pronunciation: / ˈræt̬l ɔf /; / ˈræɾl ɔf / (AmE), / ˈræt.əl ɒf / (BrE)
to say or recite something quickly and easily, often without pausing or much thought

Examples

To rattle off facts
During the interview, he rattled off his achievements without hesitation.
She could rattle off all the capitals of Europe in less than a minute.
The teacher rattled off the exam instructions in a hurry before class ended.
