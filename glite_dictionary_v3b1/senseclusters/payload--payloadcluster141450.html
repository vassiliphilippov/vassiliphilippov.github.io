payload

noun
explosive or cargo-carrying part of a missile or rocket
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ.ləʊd / (BrE)
the part of a missile, rocket, or similar vehicle that carries its explosive, chemical, biological, or other charge

Examples

Missile payload
Scientists designed a special payload to deliver chemical agents with precision.
The missile's payload can consist of either conventional explosives or nuclear warheads.
The rocket failed before releasing its payload over the intended target.

Related Terms

  • synonym: warhead - the explosive head of a missile, bomb, or torpedo
