railroad worm

noun
larva that bores into apples
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌroʊd wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈreɪlrəʊd wɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of certain beetles, especially the apple maggot, that bores into and feeds on apples

Examples

The railroad worm larva
Farmers monitor their trees closely for signs of railroad worm damage every harvest season.
If you slice a damaged apple, you may find a railroad worm inside the fruit.
The orchard lost much of its apple crop to the railroad worm infestation last year.
