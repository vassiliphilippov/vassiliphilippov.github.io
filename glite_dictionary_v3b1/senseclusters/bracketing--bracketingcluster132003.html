bracketing

noun
setting aside assumptions
Pronunciation: / ˈbrækətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrækətɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of setting aside personal beliefs or assumptions to examine experiences or data more objectively, especially in research or reflection

Examples

Bracketing personal beliefs
In qualitative research, bracketing helps ensure that the findings are not influenced by the researcher's own views.
The therapist practiced bracketing to better understand her client's unique perspective.
By using bracketing, the scientist aimed to analyze the data without personal bias.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bias - a tendency to favor or be influenced by personal opinions
  • synonym: suspension - the act of temporarily stopping or setting aside something
