braies

noun
medieval trousers
Pronunciation: / breɪz / (AmE), / breɪz / (BrE)
loose-fitting trousers worn by men in medieval Europe, often as an undergarment

Examples

Loose braies trousers
In medieval times, men commonly wore braies beneath their tunics for comfort and modesty.
The museum displayed a pair of linen braies found in a 13th-century grave.
Historical reenactors often make their own braies to wear at festivals.
