intercede

verb
act to help settle a dispute
6.7 1.02/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™nt…ôrÀàsid / (AmE), / Àå…™nt…ôÀàsiÀêd / (BrE)
to act on behalf of someone in a dispute, especially to help resolve a disagreement or reconcile differences

Examples

To intercede with committee
He promised to intercede with the committee to get their request approved.
She asked him to intercede on her behalf during the negotiations.
When tempers flared at the meeting, the manager quickly interceded and calmed everyone down.

Synonyms

  • mediate
    5.1 4.65/m
    mediate means acting as a neutral third party who helps both sides reach agreement, while intercede means acting for one person and pleading for them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intervene - to get involved in a situation to alter the outcome
  • synonym: mediate - to act as a go-between in resolving conflicts
