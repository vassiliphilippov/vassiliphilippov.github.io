crust

noun
hard outer covering or layer
2.9 1.1
Pronunciation: / krʌst / (AmE), / krʌst / (BrE)
a hard outer layer that forms on or covers something, such as bread, the earth, or other materials

Examples

Bread crust
After baking, a golden crust formed on top of the bread.
Scientists drilled through the Earth's crust to study the layers below.
She scraped the burnt crust off the casserole before serving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: core - the central part of something, especially as opposed to its outer layer
  • synonym: rind - the tough outer skin of some fruits, cheeses, or foods
