solar constant

noun
rate of solar energy at Earth's atmosphere
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊlər ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
the amount of solar radiation received per unit area at the outer layer of the Earth's atmosphere when the sun is at its mean distance from the Earth

Examples

The solar constant value
A change in the solar constant could significantly impact the planet's climate.
Scientists use the solar constant to estimate how much energy the Earth receives from the sun each day.
The value of the solar constant is approximately 1,361 watts per square meter.
