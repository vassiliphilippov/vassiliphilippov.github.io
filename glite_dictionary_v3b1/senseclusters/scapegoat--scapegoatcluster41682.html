scapegoat

noun
person blamed for others' faults
2.1 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈskeɪp.goʊt/ (AmE), /ˈskeɪp.gəʊt/ (BrE)
a person who is blamed or punished for the mistakes or wrongdoings of others

Examples

A scapegoat for failure
After the defeat, the coach became the scapegoat for the team's poor performance.
She refused to be the scapegoat for problems she did not cause.
They unfairly used him as a scapegoat to protect higher management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall guy - a person who is blamed for something to save others
