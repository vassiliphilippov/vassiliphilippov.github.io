grappling

noun
a sport of hand-to-hand wrestling
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡræplɪŋ /; / ˈɡraplɪŋ / (BrE)
a sport or act of close hand-to-hand combat where opponents try to seize, hold, or wrestle each other, usually without striking

Examples

Grappling sport
Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a popular form of grappling practiced worldwide.
He began training in grappling to improve his self-defense skills.
Many martial arts tournaments now include grappling events alongside striking disciplines.
After several minutes of grappling, the fighters were finally separated by the referee.
He has years of experience in various forms of grappling, including judo and Brazilian jiu-jitsu.
The two opponents engaged in intense grappling throughout the match.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boxing - a combat sport involving striking rather than grappling
  • synonym: wrestling - a sport or physical contest in which two unarmed individuals grapple and try to pin each other to the mat
