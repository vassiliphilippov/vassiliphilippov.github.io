textus

noun
integrating structure
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛkstəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstəs / (BrE)
the connective structure or fabric that links separate parts into an integrated whole, often used metaphorically.

Examples

Cultural textus links
The historian examined the cultural textus that unified the ancient city-states.
Understanding the textus of an organization can reveal how its departments collaborate.
The novel explores the social textus connecting individuals from different backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into separate parts
  • synonym: framework - a basic structure underlying a system or concept
  • synonym: network - an interconnected system of things or people
