undischargeable

adjective
cannot be forgiven
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪsˈtʃɑrdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪsˈtʃɑːdʒəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be released, forgiven, or eliminated, especially in a legal or financial context

Examples

Undischargeable debt
Student loans are often undischargeable in bankruptcy, making them difficult to eliminate.
The court ruled that the tax debt was undischargeable under current law.
Some legal obligations remain undischargeable regardless of a person's financial situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dischargeable - able to be released or forgiven, especially in a legal context
  • antonym: forgivable - able to be forgiven or excused
