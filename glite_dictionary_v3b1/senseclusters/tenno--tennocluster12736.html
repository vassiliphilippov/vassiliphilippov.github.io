tenno

noun
the emperor of Japan (esp. as religious leader)
Pronunciation: / ˈten.noʊ / (AmE), / ˈten.nəʊ / (BrE)
the emperor of Japan, especially as regarded as a religious or divine figure

Examples

Japanese tenno
After World War II, the role of the tenno became largely symbolic.
Many ceremonies in Japan still include references to the tenno as a spiritual leader.
The tenno was historically viewed as a divine figure by the Japanese people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emperor - a sovereign ruler of great power and rank, especially one ruling an empire
  • antonym: shogun - a hereditary military governor of Japan
