varactor

noun
voltage-controlled diode
Pronunciation: / vəˈræktər / (AmE), / vəˈræktə / (BrE)
an electronic diode whose capacitance changes with the applied voltage, used especially for tuning circuits

Examples

A varactor diode
The engineer replaced the old varactor to improve the radio's tuning accuracy.
A varactor is often used in voltage-controlled oscillators for frequency modulation.
By adjusting the voltage across the varactor, the circuit can select different channels.
