epoxide

noun
three-membered oxygen ring
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈpɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which an oxygen atom is bonded to two adjacent carbon atoms, forming a three-membered ring

Examples

An epoxide compound
The chemist isolated an epoxide during the synthesis of the new drug compound.
Many industrial processes use an epoxide as a reactive intermediate for making plastics.
Exposure to certain epoxides can be hazardous without proper safety equipment.
