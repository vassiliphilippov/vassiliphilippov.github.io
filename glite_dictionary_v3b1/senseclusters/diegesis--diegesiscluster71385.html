diegesis

noun
story's fictional world
9.9
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈdʒe.sɪs/ /; / /daɪ.ˈdʒə.sɪs/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈdʒe.sɪs/ /; / /daɪ.ˈdʒə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the fictional world and narrative framework in which the events of a story take place

Examples

The diegesis world
The film's soundtrack is part of the diegesis when the characters can hear the music.
Writers often create detailed rules for the diegesis to make their fictional worlds believable.
In some stories, the narrator exists outside the diegesis and knows more than the characters.
The novel's diegesis is shaped by the narrator's unique perspective and voice.
In film studies, scholars often discuss the difference between diegesis and direct visual storytelling.
The play uses diegesis to convey background information through a character's speech.
