hoke

verb
to exaggerate sentiment
Pronunciation: / hoʊk / (AmE), / həʊk / (BrE)
to make something seem more exaggerated, sentimental, or artificial than it really is

Examples

To hoke up a story
The movie hoked up the true story to make it more entertaining for audiences.
Critics said the author hoked the ending to appeal to readers' emotions.
He hoked his speech with dramatic pauses and exaggerated gestures.
The producer tried to hoke up the movie with flashy special effects.
Some politicians hoke their stories to appeal to voters.
He didn't want to hoke the facts just to make the article more exciting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or serious
  • synonym: embellish - to make a story or statement more interesting by adding details, often untrue
  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • antonym: understate - to describe something as less important or serious than it really is
