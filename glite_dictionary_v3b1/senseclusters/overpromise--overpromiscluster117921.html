overpromise

noun
unrealistic promise
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
a promise or claim that something will be better or more successful than is realistic or likely to happen

Examples

A big overpromise
The company's overpromise led to disappointment when the product failed to meet expectations.
Investors were wary after hearing yet another overpromise from the startup's CEO.
A single overpromise can damage a brand's reputation for years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggeration - a statement that makes something seem better or worse than it really is
  • antonym: guarantee - a formal assurance that something will happen
