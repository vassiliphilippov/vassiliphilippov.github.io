Pope Alexander VI

proper noun
pope (1431–1503), father of Cesare and Lucrezia Borgia
Pronunciation: / poʊp ˌælɪɡˈzændər sɪksθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˌælɪɡˈzændə sɪksθ / (BrE)
(1431–1503) Pope of the Roman Catholic Church, known for his controversial papacy and as the father of Cesare and Lucrezia Borgia.

Examples

Pope Alexander VI reign
Pope Alexander VI was notorious for allegations of corruption during his reign.
Many historians have debated the legacy of Pope Alexander VI in the Catholic Church.
The Borgia family rose to power partly due to the influence of Pope Alexander VI.
