Fresnel

proper noun
stepped optical lens
Pronunciation: / freɪˈnɛl / (AmE), / freɪˈnɛl / (BrE)
a type of lens or optical device with a stepped surface, designed to focus light efficiently

Examples

A lighthouse Fresnel
The lighthouse was equipped with a powerful Fresnel to guide ships safely at night.
Engineers chose a Fresnel for the stage lighting because it produces a strong, focused beam.
A Fresnel is often used in overhead projectors to direct light onto the screen.

Related Terms
