perchloride

noun
chloride with unusually high chlorine content
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɚˈklɔːr.aɪd / (AmE), / pɜːˈklɔːr.aɪd / (BrE)
a chloride compound in which the proportion of chlorine is unusually high, especially compared to other chlorides of the same element.

Examples

A perchloride compound
Excess chlorine can result in the formation of a perchloride instead of a normal chloride.
Mercury perchloride is also known as corrosive sublimate.
The laboratory stored several samples of perchloride for analytical experiments.
