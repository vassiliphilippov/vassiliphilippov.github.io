cyclophosphamide

noun
immunosuppressant drug
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkloʊˈfɑːsfəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkləʊˈfɒsfəmaɪd / (BrE)
a medicine used to suppress the immune system, often to treat severe autoimmune diseases or prevent organ transplant rejection

Examples

Cyclophosphamide immune therapy
The doctor prescribed cyclophosphamide to help control the patient's autoimmune disease.
After her kidney transplant, she was given cyclophosphamide to prevent rejection.
Some patients experience side effects when taking cyclophosphamide for long periods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: azathioprine - a medicine used to suppress the immune system
  • synonym: immunosuppressant - a drug that reduces the strength of the body's immune system
