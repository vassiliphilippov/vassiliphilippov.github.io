specialize

verb
focus on a specific field or activity
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz /; / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to focus on or become an expert in a particular area of study, work, or activity

Examples

To specialize in medicine
After medical school, she decided to specialize in cardiology.
As the tech industry grew, more companies began to specialize in cybersecurity solutions.
Many law firms specialize in intellectual property cases.
After several years, she specialized in French cuisine and became a renowned chef.
Dr. Lee specializes in pediatric medicine at the local hospital.
Our law firm specializes in environmental issues and litigation.
After law school, she decided to specialise in environmental law.
Many doctors specialise in a particular branch of medicine, such as cardiology.
This company specialises in the restoration of historic buildings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concentrate - to focus all one's attention or efforts on a particular task or subject
  • antonym: generalise - to make less specific, or broaden application rather than focus sharply
  • antonym: generalize - to make less specific or more general
  • synonym: specialize - to focus on a particular area (American spelling)
