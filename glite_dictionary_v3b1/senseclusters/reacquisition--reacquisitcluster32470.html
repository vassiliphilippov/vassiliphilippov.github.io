reacquisition

noun
rapid relearning
8.4
Pronunciation: /riː.æk.wɪ.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.æk.wɪ.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.æk.wɪ.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.æk.wɪ.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the rapid relearning of a conditioned response after it has been extinguished, especially in psychology experiments

Examples

Reacquisition of a response
After a short break, the animal demonstrated reacquisition of the behavior much faster than during initial training.
Researchers observed rapid reacquisition of the conditioned response after the extinction phase was completed.
The study focused on the factors that influence reacquisition in classical conditioning experiments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extinction - the disappearance of a learned response when it is no longer reinforced
  • synonym: relearning - the process of learning something again after forgetting it
