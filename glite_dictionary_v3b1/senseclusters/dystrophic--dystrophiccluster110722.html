dystrophic

adjective
impaired growth or nutrition
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtrɑːfɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by impaired growth, development, or nutrition, especially in tissues or organs

Examples

Dystrophic muscle tissue
The patient was diagnosed with a dystrophic condition affecting his muscle strength.
Researchers studied the effects of a dystrophic environment on plant growth.
The biopsy revealed dystrophic changes in the tissue structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atrophic - relating to the wasting away or decrease in size of a body part
  • antonym: healthy - in good physical condition; not affected by disease or impairment
