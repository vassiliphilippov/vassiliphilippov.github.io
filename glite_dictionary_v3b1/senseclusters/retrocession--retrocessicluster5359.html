retrocession

noun
return of ownership
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of returning something, such as land or rights, to its former owner or authority

Examples

Retrocession of land
The treaty required the retrocession of disputed territory to its original government.
After years of negotiation, the retrocession of the island was finally agreed upon.
Legal experts discussed the implications of retrocession in international law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cession - the act of giving up territory or rights, especially by treaty
  • synonym: restitution - the act of giving something back to its rightful owner
