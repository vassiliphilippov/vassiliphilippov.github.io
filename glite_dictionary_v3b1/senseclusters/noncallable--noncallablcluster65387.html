noncallable

adjective
not able to be recalled
7.8
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈkɔː.lə.bəl/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈkɔː.lə.bəl/ (BrE)
not able to be called back, redeemed, or terminated before a specified date, especially in finance or programming

Examples

Noncallable bond
The company issued a noncallable bond, guaranteeing investors it cannot be redeemed early.
Because the loan is noncallable, the lender must wait until the end of the term for repayment.
In programming, a noncallable function cannot be invoked directly by the user.

Related Terms

  • antonym: callable - able to be called back or redeemed before maturity
