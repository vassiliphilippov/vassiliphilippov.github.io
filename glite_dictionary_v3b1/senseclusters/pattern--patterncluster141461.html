pattern

verb
form or arrange in a pattern
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpæt.ɚn /; / ˈpætərn / (AmE), / ˈpæt.ən /; / ˈpætən / (BrE)
to arrange, form, or design something in a particular pattern or by copying a model

Examples

To pattern behavior
Her responses pattern exactly after her mentor’s teaching style.
The children were asked to pattern the tiles to create a mosaic.
The leaves pattern themselves in a spiral on the stem.
Designers often pattern their work on traditional motifs from their cultures.
The architect patterned the roof after an ancient temple to evoke a sense of history.
The new curriculum was patterned after the successful Finnish education system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to put in a particular order or position
  • synonym: imitate - to copy or follow as a model
  • synonym: model - to use as an example to imitate or follow
