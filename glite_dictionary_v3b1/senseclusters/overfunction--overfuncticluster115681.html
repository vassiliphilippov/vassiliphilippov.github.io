overfunction

noun
compensatory organ activity
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
an increased level of activity by an organ or system to compensate for reduced function elsewhere in the body

Examples

Gland overfunction
When one gland fails, overfunction of another may help maintain hormone balance.
Doctors monitored the patient's right lung for signs of overfunction after the surgery.
Chronic overfunction of the remaining kidney can lead to long-term health issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensation - the process of making up for a loss or deficiency
  • antonym: underfunction - reduced or insufficient activity of an organ or system
