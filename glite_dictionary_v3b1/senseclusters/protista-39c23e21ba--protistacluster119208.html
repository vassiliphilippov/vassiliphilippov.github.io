Protista

noun
kingdom of mostly single-celled eukaryotes
8.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈtɪstə / (AmE), / prəʊˈtɪstə / (BrE)
A major group (kingdom) of mostly single-celled eukaryotic organisms, including protozoa, slime molds, and some algae, that are distinct from plants, animals, and fungi.

Examples

The kingdom Protista
Many species in the kingdom Protista are found in freshwater ponds and lakes.
Scientists debated which organisms belonged to Protista and which should be classified elsewhere.
Some Protista cause diseases in humans and animals, while others play key ecological roles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: algae - simple non-flowering, typically aquatic plants including seaweeds and many single-celled forms
  • synonym: protozoa - single-celled microscopic organisms, formerly classified as animals
