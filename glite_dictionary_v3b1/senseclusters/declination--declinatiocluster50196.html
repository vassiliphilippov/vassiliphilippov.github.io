declination

noun
decline from a former condition
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a gradual decline from a better or earlier condition; deterioration or falling off from a previous state

Examples

Declination in quality
Following years of mismanagement, a noticeable declination in service quality occurred at the hotel.
Historians debated whether the empire's declination was inevitable or the result of poor leadership.
The declination of the town's economy was evident after the factory closures.
