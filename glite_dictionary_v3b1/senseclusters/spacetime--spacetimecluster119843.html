spacetime

noun
four-dimensional continuum (space and time)
Pronunciation: / ˈspeɪsˌtaɪm / (AmE), / ˈspeɪsˌtaɪm / (BrE)
The four-dimensional continuum in which all physical events occur, consisting of three dimensions of space and one of time.

Examples

The spacetime continuum
Einstein's theory of relativity revolutionized our understanding of spacetime and gravity.
Physicists study how matter and energy interact within the fabric of spacetime.
The curvature of spacetime is what causes the phenomenon we experience as gravity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continuum - A continuous extent, series, or whole, especially a fundamental concept in mathematics and physics
