ill-use

verb
treat badly or unfairly
8.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / …™lÀàjuÀêz / (AmE), / …™lÀàjuÀêz / (BrE)
to treat someone or something in a harsh, unfair, or cruel way; to abuse or mistreat.

Examples

To ill-use animals
It is wrong to ill-use animals, regardless of the situation.
She felt hurt after being ill-used by her closest friends.
The manager would often ill-use his staff to achieve his own goals.

Synonyms

  • mistreat
    2.9 2.91/m
    mistreat is a common, everyday verb for treating someone badly, while ill-use sounds older and is less used today.
  • ill-treat
    5.2 0.01/m
    ill-treat means treating a person cruelly in speech or action, while ill-use is an older term with same meaning.
  • maltreat
    7.4 0.26/m
    maltreat is a formal word for cruel or violent care of someone, while ill-use is more general and less strong.
  • abuse
    3.7 14/m
    abuse often shows serious, repeated harm or force and can be a noun, while ill-use focuses on treating badly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abuse - to treat (a person or animal) with cruelty or violence, especially regularly or repeatedly
  • synonym: mistreat - to treat (a person or animal) badly, cruelly, or unfairly
  • antonym: treat well - to act towards someone in a kind, fair, or pleasant way
