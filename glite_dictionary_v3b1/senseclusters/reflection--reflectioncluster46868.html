reflection

noun
ability to reflect beams or rays
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛk.ʃən /; / rɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛk.ʃən /; / rɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the process or ability of light, sound, or heat to bounce off a surface instead of passing through it

Examples

Light reflection
Surfaces with a high reflection are used in solar cookers to direct sunlight.
The mirror's reflection made the room seem brighter.
The scientist measured the reflection of the new material under different light conditions.
Mirrors rely on the reflection of light to produce an image.
The reflection of sunlight on the water created a dazzling effect.
You can hear the reflection of your voice as an echo in the canyon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: refraction - the bending of a wave as it passes through a medium
