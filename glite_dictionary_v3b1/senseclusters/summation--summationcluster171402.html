(physiology) The process by which repeated or simultaneous stimuli combine to produce a response in a muscle, nerve, or other tissue that one stimulus alone would not cause.

Examples

Neural summation response
Temporal summation occurs when several stimuli are delivered in quick succession to a neuron.
The summation of nerve impulses can lead to a stronger muscular contraction.
Without summation, a single stimulus would not generate an action potential in the muscle fiber.
