take legal action

verb
begin official legal proceedings
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈliːɡəl ˈækʃən / (AmE), / teɪk ˈliːɡəl ˈækʃən / (BrE)
to begin official legal proceedings against someone or something, typically by filing a lawsuit or complaint in court

Examples

To take legal action against
If the tenant refuses to pay, the landlord may take legal action to recover the money.
She decided not to take legal action after the parties reached a mutual settlement.
The company is prepared to take legal action against those who breach the agreement.
