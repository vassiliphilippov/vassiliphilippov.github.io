recountable

adjective
able to be counted again
4.6
Pronunciation: / riˈkaʊntəbl̩ / (AmE), / riˈkaʊntəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be counted again or capable of being told or narrated again

Examples

Votes recountable
The votes were recountable if the election results were challenged.
Only recountable stories can be shared with the group again.
The data must be recountable to ensure the accuracy of the results.
