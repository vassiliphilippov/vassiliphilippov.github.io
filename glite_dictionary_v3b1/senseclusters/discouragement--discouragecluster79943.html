discouragement

noun
loss of confidence or hope
3.6 0.36/m
Pronunciation: / d…™sÀàk…ùÀêr…™d ím…ônt / (AmE), / d…™sÀàk år…™d ím…ônt / (BrE)
A feeling of having lost confidence or enthusiasm, especially when faced with difficulties or setbacks.

Examples

A feeling of discouragement
After several job rejections, she felt a wave of discouragement wash over her.
Despite his discouragement, his friends urged him to keep trying.
Persistent discouragement can make it difficult to achieve personal goals.

Synonyms

  • disheartenment
    7.3 0.01/m
    disheartenment is the feeling of losing confidence and drive after setbacks, while discouragement means losing enthusiasm when things go wrong.
  • dispiritedness
    7.1 0.01/m
    dispiritedness is a low, quiet feeling of having no hope or energy, while discouragement often shows as reduced confidence and interest.
  • demoralization
    8.0 0.35/m
    demoralization is a loss of morale that can hit teams or people deeply, while discouragement is a more personal drop in confidence.
  • downheartedness
    6.8 0.00/m
    downheartedness is a sad, low mood you feel after bad news, while discouragement focuses more on lost confidence to act.
  • dejection
    6.3 0.32/m
    dejection is a heavy, sad state that makes people quiet and withdrawn, while discouragement is a loss of confidence or hope.
  • despondency
    8.3 0.46/m
    despondency is deep hopelessness that can stop daily life, while discouragement is a milder loss of confidence after setbacks.
