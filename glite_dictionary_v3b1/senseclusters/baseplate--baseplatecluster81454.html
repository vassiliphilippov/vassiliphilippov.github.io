baseplate

noun
virus attachment plate
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪs.pleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪs.pleɪt/ (BrE)
the structural plate at the end of a bacteriophage tail that enables the virus to attach to and penetrate a host bacterial cell

Examples

Virus baseplate
The baseplate of the bacteriophage is essential for attaching to the bacterial cell wall.
Scientists studied the structure of the baseplate to understand how viruses infect bacteria.
Mutations in the baseplate can prevent the virus from successfully injecting its DNA.
