woolly aphid

noun
insect that secretes white waxy threads
9.3 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwʊl.ɪ ˈæf.ɪd/ /; / /ˈwʊl.ɪ ˈeɪ.fɪd/ (AmE), /ˈwʊl.ɪ ˈeɪ.fɪd/ /; / /ˈwʊl.ɪ ˈæf.ɪd/ (BrE)
a small insect that secretes a white, waxy, cotton-like substance and is known for infesting plants

Examples

Woolly aphids on leaves
A thick coating produced by woolly aphids can make leaves look unusually white and fuzzy.
Gardeners often struggle to control woolly aphid infestations on their fruit trees.
The rose bushes were covered with woolly aphids that looked like tufts of cotton.
