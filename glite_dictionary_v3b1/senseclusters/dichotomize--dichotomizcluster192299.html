dichotomize

verb
divide into two groups
7.3
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑːtəˌmaɪz /; / daɪˈkɑtəmaɪz / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒtəmaɪz /; / daɪˈkɒtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to divide or separate into two distinct or opposing groups or kinds

Examples

To dichotomize groups
It's unhelpful to dichotomize people as successes or failures.
Scholars often dichotomize historical events as either progressive or regressive.
The debate tends to dichotomize issues into black and white, ignoring complexities.
It is unhelpful to dichotomise complex social issues into good and bad.
The debate has dichotomised the community into two opposing camps.
The media often dichotomises political opinions, ignoring the nuances in between.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • synonym: polarize - to cause to divide into two sharply contrasting groups or opinions
  • antonym: unify - to make or become united, uniform, or whole
