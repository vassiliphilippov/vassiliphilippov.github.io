reboot

noun
restart of a media franchise
4.1
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈbuːt/ /; / /rə.ˈbuːt/ (AmE), /riː.ˈbuːt/ /; / /rə.ˈbuːt/ (BrE)
a new film, television series, game, or other media that restarts a franchise or story universe, often reimagining characters, plot, or continuity.

Examples

A reboot movie
The 2005 Batman reboot relaunched the franchise with a darker, more realistic tone.
After weak ratings, the studio ordered a reboot of the series to attract a new audience.
Fans argued that the director's reboot ignored previous continuity and changed key character motivations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relaunch - to start a product, series, or franchise again, often with a new marketing push or changes to reach a wider audience.
  • synonym: remake - a new version of an earlier film or show that retells the same story; related but often preserves continuity differently from a reboot.
