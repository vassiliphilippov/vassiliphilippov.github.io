silhouette

verb
depict as dark outline against light
7.6 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪl.uˈɛt /; / ˌsɪluˈɛt / (AmE), / ˌsɪl.uˈɛt /; / ˌsɪluːˈɛt /; / ˌsɪluˈɛt / (BrE)
to show or represent someone or something as a dark shape or outline against a lighter background

Examples

To silhouette a figure
A bird was silhouetted in the window as the sun set behind it.
Photographers often silhouette their subjects to create dramatic effects.
The tree was silhouetted against the evening sky.
A lone figure was silhouetted by the headlights as he crossed the road.
Lightning briefly silhouetted the mountains on the horizon.
The tree was silhouetted against the orange glow of the sunset.
The mountain was silhouetted against the orange glow of the setting sun.
The artist skillfully silhouetted the dancer's graceful pose on the canvas.
The report silhouetted the main challenges facing the organization in the coming year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: highlight - to make something stand out or be easily noticed
  • synonym: outline - show the outer shape of something
