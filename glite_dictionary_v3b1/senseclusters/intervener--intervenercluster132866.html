intervener

noun
person who intervenes
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈviːnər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈviːnə / (BrE)
a person, group, or country who becomes involved in a situation, dispute, or conflict to help resolve it or influence the outcome

Examples

An intervener helps
The quick action of an intervener prevented the argument from turning violent.
In some situations, an intervener can help both sides reach an agreement.
A trained intervener stepped in to assist the injured cyclist after the accident.
The United Nations acted as an intervener to help negotiate peace between the two countries.
A third-party intervener joined the lawsuit to represent the interests of affected communities.
Sometimes an outside intervener can help resolve conflicts that seem impossible to settle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bystander - a person who is present but does not take part
  • synonym: mediator - a person who helps others resolve a conflict
