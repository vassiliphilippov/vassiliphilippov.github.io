snooker

noun
a cue sport played on a large table
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsnuk.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsnuː.kə / (BrE)
A game played on a large rectangular table with pockets, in which players use cues to pot colored balls in a set order, especially popular in Britain.

Examples

A game of snooker
He spent the afternoon playing snooker with his friends at the local club.
Learning the rules of snooker can be challenging for beginners.
The World Snooker Championship is watched by millions of fans each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: billiards - a cue sport played on a table without pockets or with different rules
  • synonym: pool - a popular cue sport with simpler rules
