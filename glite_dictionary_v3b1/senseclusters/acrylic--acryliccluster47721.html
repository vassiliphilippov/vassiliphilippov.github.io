acrylic

noun
transparent thermoplastic material
3.7 3.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkrɪ.lɪk/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkrɪ.lɪk/ (BrE)
a synthetic material, plastic, or paint made from acrylic compounds, commonly used in art, industry, and manufacturing

Examples

Acrylic paint
Many artists prefer acrylic paint because it dries quickly and is easy to layer.
She used clear acrylic sheets to build the model display case.
The window panes were replaced with acrylic to make them more shatter-resistant.
She bought several tubes of acrylic to complete her painting.
The artist preferred to use acrylic for its quick-drying properties.
Unlike oil paints, acrylic allows for easy clean-up with water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oil paint - a traditional paint made with oil, used by artists
  • synonym: polycarbonate - a durable, transparent thermoplastic used as a substitute for glass
  • antonym: watercolor - paint made with pigment suspended in water, used by artists
