disyllabic

adjective
having two syllables
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.sɪˈlæb.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪ.sɪˈlæb.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or part of speech) having or consisting of two syllables.

Examples

A disyllabic word
In poetry, a disyllabic foot contains exactly two syllables.
Many common words like 'table' and 'candle' are disyllabic.
The word disyllabic itself is actually polysyllabic, not consisting of two syllables.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monosyllabic - having only one syllable
  • antonym: polysyllabic - having more than two syllables
