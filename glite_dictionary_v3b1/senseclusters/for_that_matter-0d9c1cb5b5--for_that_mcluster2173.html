for that matter

adverb
to indicate an additional or related point
2.7
Pronunciation: / fər ðæt ˈmætər / (AmE), / fə ðæt ˈmætə / (BrE)
used to indicate that a statement applies equally to something just mentioned or is an extension of it.

Examples

Used for that matter
He hasn't visited Paris, or anywhere in Europe for that matter.
I don't like horror movies, and my brother doesn't either, for that matter.
She doesn't eat meat, and she doesn't like dairy products for that matter.
