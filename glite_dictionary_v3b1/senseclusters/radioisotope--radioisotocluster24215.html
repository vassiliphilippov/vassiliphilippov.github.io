radioisotope

noun
a radioactive isotope of an element
7.7 6.6
Pronunciation: /reɪ.di.oʊ.ˈaɪ.sə.toʊp/ /; / /reɪ.dɪ.oʊ.ˈaɪ.sə.toʊp/ (AmE), /reɪ.di.əʊ.ˈaɪ.sə.təʊp/ /; / /reɪ.dɪ.əʊ.ˈaɪ.sə.təʊp/ (BrE)
a radioactive form of a chemical element with an unstable nucleus, which decays by emitting radiation; produced naturally or artificially

Examples

A radioisotope decay
Scientists study the decay of a radioisotope to estimate the age of ancient artifacts.
Some industrial processes use a radioisotope to measure thickness and detect flaws in materials.
The patient was given a radioisotope to help doctors track the function of her thyroid gland.
