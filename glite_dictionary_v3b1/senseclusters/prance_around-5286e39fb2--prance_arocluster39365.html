prance around

verb
move about with lively steps
Pronunciation: / præns əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / prɑːns əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to move about with high, lively steps, often in a playful or energetic manner

Examples

To prance around happily
At the festival, people pranced around in colorful costumes and masks.
The actor pranced around the stage, delighting the audience with his energy.
The children pranced around the playground, laughing with joy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skip - to move along lightly, stepping from one foot to the other
  • antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps
