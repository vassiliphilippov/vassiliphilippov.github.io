agglutination

noun
clumping of particles in a liquid
3.6 7.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ɡluː.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.ɡlʌ.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.ɡlʌ.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.ɡluː.tɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which small particles, cells, or bacteria stick together and form larger clumps, especially in a liquid (such as blood)

Examples

Blood agglutination
Proper identification of blood type prevents unwanted agglutination during transfusions.
The scientist observed the agglutination of red blood cells under the microscope.
When the antibody is present, agglutination occurs, causing visible clumps in the solution.
Doctors observed agglutination in the blood sample, which helped confirm the diagnosis.
The laboratory test revealed the agglutination of red blood cells, indicating an immune response.
When exposed to the matching antibody, the bacteria underwent rapid agglutination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulation - the process by which a liquid, especially blood, changes to a thick or semi-solid state
  • antonym: dispersion - the process of distributing particles more widely in a liquid, the opposite of coalescence
