2-hitter

noun
baseball game with only two hits allowed by a pitcher
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː hɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtuː hɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a game in which a pitcher allows the opposing team only two hits.

Examples

A 2-hitter game
He celebrated his first career 2-hitter with his teammates after the final out.
Last night’s game was a tense 2-hitter that kept the crowd on the edge of their seats.
The rookie pitcher threw a remarkable 2-hitter against the league’s highest-scoring team.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: no-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows no hits
  • degree lower: one-hitter - a game in which only one hit is allowed by a pitcher
  • degree higher: three-hitter - a game in which only three hits are allowed by a pitcher
