foil

verb
cover or back with foil
5.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /fɔɪl/ (AmE), /fɔɪl/ (BrE)
to cover or back (a material or object) with a thin sheet of metal or foil, especially to enhance its appearance or function

Examples

To foil a frame
In restoration work, old picture frames are sometimes foiled to restore their original luster.
Some decorative lamps are foiled to give a warm, shimmering effect.
The craftsman carefully foiled the back of the glass to create a reflective surface.
