unbounded interval

noun
an interval without finite bounds
9.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbaʊndɪd ˈɪntərvəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈbaʊndɪd ˈɪntəvəl / (BrE)
a mathematical interval that extends indefinitely in at least one direction, not having upper or lower limits

Examples

An unbounded interval extends
An unbounded interval does not have a finite upper or lower endpoint.
Mathematicians often use an unbounded interval when discussing infinite sets.
The set of all real numbers greater than zero forms an unbounded interval.
