whirr

verb
make a soft, continuous sound
6.7 1.11/m
Pronunciation: / w…úr / (AmE), / w…úÀê / (BrE)
to make a continuous, low, vibrating or humming sound, usually from something moving quickly

Examples

To whirr loudly
As the drone took off, its blades started whirring overhead.
Dozens of insects whirred in the warm summer air at dusk.
The old fan began to whirr softly as soon as it was switched on.
As she spun the toy, its blades started to whirr loudly across the room.
I woke up to the sound of a fan whirring gently in the background.
The hummingbird's wings began to whirr as it hovered over the flower.

Synonyms

  • whir
    4.6 0.19/m
    whir is a soft rapid buzzing or swishing sound from fast machines, while whirr is a low vibrating hum.
  • purr
    3.5 0.49/m
    purr is a soft, smooth vibrating sound like a cat or engine, while whirr is a low, faster humming noise.
  • hum
    1.0 2.64/m
    hum is a steady, low background sound like bees or a room, while whirr is a quick vibrating noise from moving parts.
  • burr
    7.9 0.00/m
    burr is a buzzy, rough vibrating sound like an insect or voice, while whirr is a smoother low mechanical hum.
