slip carriage

noun
detachable railway car left behind without stopping
7.3 8.5
Pronunciation: /slɪp ˈkær.ɪdʒ/ (AmE), /slɪp ˈkær.ɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a railway carriage at the end of a train that can be detached while the train is still moving, allowing it to stop at a station without the whole train stopping

Examples

A slip carriage detaches
Few people today have seen a slip carriage in operation, as the practice has long since faded.
In the early 20th century, the express train often included a slip carriage for passengers bound for smaller stations.
The guard prepared to release the slip carriage as the train approached the junction.
