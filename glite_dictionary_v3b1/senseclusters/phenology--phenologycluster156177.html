phenology

noun
study of seasonal timing
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / fɪˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the timing of seasonal events in plants and animals, such as flowering or migration

Examples

Phenology of plants
Researchers in phenology observe when birds migrate and trees lose their leaves each year.
Changes in climate can affect the patterns studied in phenology.
The field of phenology helps scientists understand how plants respond to seasonal changes.
Scientists use phenology to track how climate change affects the timing of plant blooming.
The phenology of migratory birds can indicate shifts in seasonal weather patterns.
Gardeners often observe phenology to decide the best time for planting crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronobiology - the study of biological rhythms and cycles
  • synonym: climatology - the scientific study of climate and weather patterns
  • synonym: ecology - the study of relationships between living things and their environment
