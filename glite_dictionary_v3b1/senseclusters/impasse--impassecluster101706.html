A street with only one entrance and exit; a dead-end street or cul-de-sac.

Examples

A quiet impasse
Several elegant houses were built on the tree-lined impasse in the city center.

Synonyms

  • dead-end street
    2.1 0.21/m
    a dead-end street ends in a closed area you must turn around in, while impasse refers to a street with one entrance.
  • cul-de-sac
    4.7 1.17/m
    a cul-de-sac is a short curved road ending in a turning circle for cars, while impasse names a dead-end street.
  • dead end
    2.7 1.19/m
    a dead end is a road that stops with no through way, whereas impasse is a similar term for such a street.
  • blind alley
    4.1 0.01/m
    a blind alley is a passage closed at one end and often used metaphorically for no progress, while impasse means a literal dead-end street.
