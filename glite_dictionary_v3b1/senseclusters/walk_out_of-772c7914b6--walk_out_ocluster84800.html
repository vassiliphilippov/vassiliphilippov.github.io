walk out of

verb
leave suddenly, often in protest
Pronunciation: / wɔk ˈaʊt əv / (AmE), / wɔːk ˈaʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to leave a place, meeting, or event suddenly and often as a protest or expression of disapproval

Examples

To walk out of a meeting
He walked out of the performance halfway through because he disagreed with the message.
If you're unhappy, you can always walk out of the meeting.
Several delegates decided to walk out of the conference in protest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: storm out of - to leave angrily and abruptly
