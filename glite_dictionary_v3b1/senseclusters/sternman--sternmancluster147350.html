sternman

noun
boat's rear crew
Pronunciation: / ˈstɜːrnmæn / (AmE), / ˈstɜːnmən / (BrE)
a person who works at the back (stern) of a small or fishing boat, especially steering or helping with tasks like hauling traps

Examples

The sternman steers boat
The sternman kept the boat steady as the waves grew stronger.
During the fishing trip, the sternman managed the nets from the back of the boat.
In the race, the experienced sternman guided the crew through the narrow channel.
The sternman pulled up lobster traps while the boat drifted near the rocky shore.
After years as a sternman, he finally became captain of his own fishing boat.
Each morning, the sternman prepares bait and checks the equipment before heading out to sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bowman - the person who works at the front (bow) of a boat
  • antonym: captain - the person in command of a boat or ship
  • synonym: coxswain - the person in charge of steering and commanding a boat
  • synonym: deckhand - a worker who assists with general tasks on a boat
