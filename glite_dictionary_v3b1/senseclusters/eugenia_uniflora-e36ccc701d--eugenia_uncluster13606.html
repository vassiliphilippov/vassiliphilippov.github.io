Eugenia uniflora

noun
tropical tree with spicy red fruit
9.6
Pronunciation: / juːˈdʒiːni.ə juːˈnɪflɔːr.i / (AmE), / juːˈdʒiːni.ə juːˈnɪfləri / (BrE)
A tropical Brazilian tree or shrub of the myrtle family, cultivated for its aromatic red or orange fruit, commonly known as the Surinam cherry.

Examples

Eugenia uniflora tree
Eugenia uniflora is often used as a hedge in tropical and subtropical regions.
The gardener planted a Eugenia uniflora in the backyard for its attractive fruit.
The spicy flavor of Eugenia uniflora makes it popular for making jams and syrups.
