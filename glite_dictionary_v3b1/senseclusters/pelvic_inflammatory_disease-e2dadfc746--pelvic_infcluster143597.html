pelvic inflammatory disease

noun
infection of female pelvic organs
4.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpel.vɪk ɪn.ˈflæm.ə.tɔːr.iː dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /ˈpel.vɪk ɪn.ˈflæm.ə.tə.riː dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
a serious infection and inflammation of the female pelvic organs, most often caused by bacteria, that can lead to pain, fever, and abnormal vaginal discharge

Examples

Pelvic inflammatory disease symptoms
Early symptoms of pelvic inflammatory disease may include lower abdominal pain and unusual vaginal discharge.
Prompt treatment can help prevent the long-term complications of pelvic inflammatory disease.
