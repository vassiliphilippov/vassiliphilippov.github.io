give way

verb
collapse or yield under pressure
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv weɪ / (AmE), / ɡɪv weɪ / (BrE)
to collapse, yield, or break down under pressure or force; to fail or stop working

Examples

The bridge gives way
He pushed against the locked door until it gave way beneath his weight.
The old bridge finally gave way after years of neglect and heavy rain.
Their determination never gave way, even in the face of overwhelming opposition.
As the snow piled up, the roof gave way and collapsed.
Her patience gave way under the strain of constant criticism.
