leveraged buyout

noun
acquisition using borrowed funds
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvərɪdʒd ˈbaɪˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈliːvərɪdʒd ˈbaɪaʊt / (BrE)
the acquisition of a company using mainly borrowed funds, with the assets of the acquired company often used as collateral for the loan

Examples

A leveraged buyout deal
A leveraged buyout can increase a company’s debt significantly.
The firm announced a leveraged buyout of the struggling retailer yesterday.
Upper management used a leveraged buyout to maintain control during the takeover attempt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquisition - the act of acquiring another company
  • synonym: hostile takeover - an attempt to acquire a company against the wishes of its management
