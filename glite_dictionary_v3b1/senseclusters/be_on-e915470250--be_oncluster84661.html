be on

verb
be broadcast on TV or radio
1.0 38/m
Pronunciation: / bi É‘n / (AmE), / bi É’n / (BrE)
to be broadcast or shown on television or radio

Examples

To be on TV
Is the football game on now, or does it start later?
That show used to be on every Friday night when I was a child.
The movie will be on at 8 p.m. tonight.

Synonyms

  • air
    3.0 10/m
    air means to broadcast a program on radio or television, while be on means the program is being shown to the public.
  • televise
    3.8 6.31/m
    televise means to broadcast something on television specifically, while be on covers both radio and television broadcasts in general.
  • on air
    3.6 1.74/m
    on air describes a live broadcast happening now, while be on simply means a show is broadcast or shown.
