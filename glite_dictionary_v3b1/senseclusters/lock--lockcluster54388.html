lock

verb
become rigid or immoveable (body part)
3.7 2.2
Pronunciation: /lɑːk/ (AmE), /lɒk/ (BrE)
to become or make something rigid, tightly engaged, or firmly held in a position or state so it cannot move or change

Examples

Muscles lock tight
Doctors advised him to rest after his back muscles started to lock unexpectedly.
If you bend your elbow too far, it may suddenly lock and refuse to straighten.
Occasionally, her jaw would lock during long dental procedures.
Gears lock together inside the machine to keep it running smoothly.
Her arms locked around his neck in a warm embrace.
The two wrestlers locked in a fierce struggle, refusing to yield.
He locked his hands together behind his head and leaned back.
She locked her arms around the child's shoulders to comfort him.
The wrestler locked his opponent in a firm hold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engage - to cause something to become involved or fixed in position
  • synonym: fasten - to make secure or fixed
  • synonym: freeze - to become temporarily immobile or stuck
  • synonym: grip - to grasp tightly
  • synonym: interlock - to fit or be fitted together securely
  • antonym: release - to let go of or set free
  • antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to stop being engaged or mixed together
