pro forma

adjective, adverb
as a formality; for the sake of form
Pronunciation: / proʊ ˈfɔrmə / (AmE), / prəʊ ˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
done as a formality or just to satisfy requirements, not with real effort or meaning

Examples

Pro forma gesture
He sent a thank-you note pro forma, knowing it was expected.
The board voted pro forma on the proposal, having already made their decision in private.
The manager approved the request pro forma, without even reading the report.
His apology sounded pro forma rather than sincere.
It was a pro forma meeting, lasting only ten minutes with no decisions made.
The board approved the pro forma budget before reviewing the final figures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formally - according to established form or convention
  • synonym: perfunctorily - without real interest, care, or feeling; as a routine duty
