family Alcidae

noun
family of diving seabirds (auks, puffins, etc.)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli ælˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ælˈsɪdɪː / (BrE)
A family of web-footed diving seabirds of northern seas, including auks, puffins, guillemots, and murres.

Examples

family Alcidae seabirds
Members of the family Alcidae are excellent divers, adapted to cold, northern oceans.
Researchers observed several species from the family Alcidae nesting on rocky cliffs.
The family Alcidae includes well-known seabirds such as puffins and guillemots.
