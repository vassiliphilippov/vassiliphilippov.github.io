Tertiary period

noun
geological period 63–2 million years ago
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtɜːr.ʃɪə.riː ˈpɪr.i.əd/ /; / /ˈtɜːr.ʃə.riː ˈpɪr.i.əd/ (AmE), /ˈtɜː.ʃɪə.riː ˈpɪə.ri.əd/ /; / /ˈtɜː.ʃə.riː ˈpɪə.ri.əd/ (BrE)
the geological period spanning from about 63 million to 2 million years ago, following the Cretaceous and preceding the Quaternary period

Examples

The Tertiary period
Dinosaurs had already become extinct by the beginning of the Tertiary period.
Geological strata from the Tertiary period can be found throughout Europe.
Significant mammalian evolution occurred during the Tertiary period.
