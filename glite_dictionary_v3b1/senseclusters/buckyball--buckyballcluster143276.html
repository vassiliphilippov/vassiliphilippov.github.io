buckyball

noun
spherical carbon molecule (C60); fullerene
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkiˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈbʌkiˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a spherical molecule consisting of 60 carbon atoms arranged as a hollow cage; the first discovered and most common fullerene (C60)

Examples

A buckyball molecule
Research into the properties of buckyballs has led to advances in nanotechnology.
Scientists synthesized a buckyball in the laboratory in 1985.
The buckyball is famous for its stability and unique carbon structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fullerene - any of a class of carbon molecules with hollow structures
