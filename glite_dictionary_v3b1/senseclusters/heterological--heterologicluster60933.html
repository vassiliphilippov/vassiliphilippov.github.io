heterological

adjective
not corresponding in structure or origin
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /he.tə.rə.ˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /he.tə.rə.ˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
not corresponding in structure, meaning, or evolutionary origin; especially used to describe words or biological features that do not have the same origin or function.

Examples

A heterological word
In biology, two organs that serve similar functions but have different evolutionary origins are called heterological.
The concept of heterological words plays a key role in certain paradoxes of language.
The term 'heterological' is itself heterological, as it does not describe itself.
