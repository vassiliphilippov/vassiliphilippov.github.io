door latch

noun
spring-loaded door lock opened by key
3.7 3.2
Pronunciation: /dɔːr lætʃ/ (AmE), /dɔː lætʃ/ (BrE)
a spring-loaded locking mechanism for a door that can only be opened from the outside with a key

Examples

The door latch is locked
He couldn’t enter the house because the door latch was locked and he didn't have his key.
She replaced the broken door latch with a newer, more secure model.
The old apartment still uses a traditional door latch for security.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deadbolt - a locking mechanism requiring a key or knob to open and does not operate with a spring
