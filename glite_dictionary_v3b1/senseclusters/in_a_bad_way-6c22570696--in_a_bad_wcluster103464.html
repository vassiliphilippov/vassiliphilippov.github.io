in a bad way

adjective
in serious trouble or difficulty
Pronunciation: / ɪn ə bæd weɪ / (AmE), / ɪn ə bæd weɪ / (BrE)
in a serious or difficult condition, especially experiencing trouble or hardship, often referring to health or finances

Examples

In a bad way financially
After his business collapsed, he was in a bad way financially for months.
She knew her friend was in a bad way and tried to help as much as possible.
The hospital reported that several patients were in a bad way after the accident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in good shape - in a healthy or strong condition; not experiencing difficulty
  • synonym: in trouble - experiencing difficulty or problems
