syncretical

adjective
tending to reduce grammatical inflections
8.7 9.1
Pronunciation: / sɪŋˈkrɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪŋˈkrɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
Relating to a historical tendency in a language to reduce or merge inflectional forms, especially in grammar.

Examples

Syncretical language
Linguists describe modern English as a syncretical language due to its simplified inflectional system.
Old English had many more inflections than its syncretical descendant, modern English.
Some scholars debate how syncretical tendencies have shaped English grammar over the centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analytic - pertaining to languages that use few or no inflections
  • antonym: inflectional - pertaining to languages that make extensive use of inflections
