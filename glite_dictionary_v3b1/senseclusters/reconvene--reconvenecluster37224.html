reconvene

verb
to meet again after a break
6.1 0.54/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàviÀên / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàviÀên / (BrE)
to gather or assemble again, especially after a pause or interruption

Examples

To reconvene the meeting
Our study group decided to reconvene next week to review the material.
Parliament is set to reconvene in January following the winter recess.
The committee will reconvene after lunch to discuss the final report.

Synonyms

  • reconverge
    7.8 0.00/m
    reconverge describes people or groups coming together again at one place, while reconvene emphasizes formally assembling to meet.
  • resume
    3.1 17/m
    resume means starting an activity again after a pause, while reconvene means gathering specifically for a meeting.
  • reassemble
    3.6 1.90/m
    reassemble means putting parts or objects back together, while reconvene refers to people assembling again to meet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convene - to come together or assemble, usually for a public purpose
