Salvadora persica

noun
evergreen shrub used for chewing sticks (miswak)
9.9
Pronunciation: / sæl.vəˈdɔɹ.ə ˈpɝː.sɪ.kə / (AmE), / sal.vəˈdɔː.rə ˈpɜː.sɪ.kə / (BrE)
A small evergreen shrub or tree native to Africa and Asia, whose twigs are traditionally used as natural toothbrushes (miswak) and whose shoots serve as camel fodder.

Examples

Salvadora persica twigs
In many Middle Eastern countries, twigs of Salvadora persica are used as natural toothbrushes.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Salvadora persica extracts for oral health.
The desert nomads fed Salvadora persica shoots to their camels during long journeys.
