destabilization

noun
the act of making less stable
Pronunciation: / diˌsteɪbəlɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diːˌsteɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌsteɪbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diːˌsteɪbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act or process of making something less stable or secure, especially a government, country, or economy.

Examples

The destabilization of government
Environmental disasters can contribute to the destabilization of entire regions.
Political destabilization can occur quickly if leaders lose control of key institutions.
The sudden withdrawal of foreign aid led to the destabilization of the country's economy.
Heavy cargo shifting caused the destabilization of the ship during the storm.
Political unrest can result in the destabilization of government institutions.
The unexpected turbulence led to the destabilization of the aircraft.
Continued unrest brought about the destabilisation of the government and widespread uncertainty.
Economic sanctions are sometimes used as tools of destabilisation against rival countries.
The sudden political crisis led to the destabilisation of the entire region.
