obsequiousness

noun
excessive eagerness to please; servility
9.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàsi:kwi…ôsn…ôs / (AmE), / …ôbÀàsiÀêkw…™…ôsn…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being excessively eager to please or obey someone, often in a servile or ingratiating way

Examples

Obsequiousness toward boss
His obsequiousness toward his boss made his colleagues uncomfortable.
She used her obsequiousness as a strategy to gain favor with influential people.
The obsequiousness of the staff was obvious every time the celebrity visited the restaurant.

Synonyms

  • servility
    8.7 0.24/m
    servility is cringing, overly humble submission to others, while obsequiousness is eager pleasing that often flatters to gain approval.
  • subservience
    8.1 0.44/m
    subservience is a willing, obedient attitude that accepts lower status, while obsequiousness is eager pleasing often mixed with flattery.
  • toadyism
    9.9 0.01/m
    toadyism is active flattery to gain favor from someone important, while obsequiousness is a general eager desire to please or obey.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insolence - rude or disrespectful behavior (opposite attitude)
  • synonym: servility - excessive willingness to serve or please others
