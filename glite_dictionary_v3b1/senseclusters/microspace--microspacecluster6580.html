microspace

noun
tiny space
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌspeɪs / (BrE)
a very small or microscopic space, especially in scientific or technical contexts

Examples

Bacterial microspace
Scientists observed bacteria living in the microspace between grains of sand.
The engineer designed a sensor small enough to fit into a microspace within the device.
Moisture can accumulate in the microspace under a microscope slide, affecting the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microsite - a small, specialized area within a larger environment
  • synonym: void - an empty space or gap
