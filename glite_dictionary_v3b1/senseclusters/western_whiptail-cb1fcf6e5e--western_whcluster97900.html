western whiptail

noun
species of lizard found in western North America
9.4 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn ˈwɪpˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈwɪpˌteɪl / (BrE)
A species of active lizard, characterized by a network of dark markings, found in semiarid regions from Oregon and Idaho to Baja California.

Examples

The western whiptail lizard
Researchers observed a western whiptail darting among sagebrush in the Nevada desert.
The western whiptail is known for its speed as it scurries across sun-baked rocks.
Unlike some other lizards, the western whiptail is diurnal and highly active during the day.
