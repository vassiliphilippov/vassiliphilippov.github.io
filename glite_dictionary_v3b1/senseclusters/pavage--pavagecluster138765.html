pavage

noun
tax for street paving (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpævɪdʒ / (BrE)
A tax or toll levied for the paving of streets, especially in medieval times.

Examples

A pavage tax
During the Middle Ages, a pavage was collected from residents near major roads.
The city imposed a pavage on merchants to fund new cobblestones for the marketplace.
The historian discovered records of pavage being paid in thirteenth-century London.
