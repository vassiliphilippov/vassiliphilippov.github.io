The state or quality of being illogical or not valid, especially in an argument where the conclusion does not logically follow from the premises.

Examples

The invalidity of arguments
Law students are trained to recognize the invalidity of faulty syllogisms.
The invalidity of his argument was clear once we analyzed the logical steps.
They discussed the invalidity of the conclusion based on the given evidence.

Synonyms

  • unsoundness
    8.6 0.02/m
    unsoundness indicates an argument is faulty or has false premises, while invalidity means the conclusion does not logically follow.
  • illogicality
    7.8 0.02/m
    illogicality describes a general lack of logical thought or reasoning, while invalidity names a formal logical failure in an argument.
  • illogic
    8.0 0.41/m
    illogic feels like a vague absence of reason or pattern, while invalidity marks a precise logical failure in an argument.
