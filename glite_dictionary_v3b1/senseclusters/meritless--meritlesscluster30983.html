Having no merit, value, or worth; not deserving praise or approval.

Examples

A meritless claim

The judge dismissed the lawsuit because it was entirely meritless.

Synonyms

  • nonmeritorious
    7.8 0.00/m
    nonmeritorious sounds formal and means lacking merit or worth, while meritless is more natural and slightly broader in use.
  • valueless
    3.6 0.38/m
    valueless describes having no value or use in a thing, while meritless focuses on lacking praise or worth.
  • worthless
    3.0 11/m
    worthless feels stronger and often means useless or of no value, while meritless is more neutral about lacking merit.
  • undeserving
    5.5 0.68/m
    undeserving describes a person who does not deserve help or praise, while meritless labels something without merit.
  • unmerited
    7.6 0.17/m
    unmerited describes praise or reward given without reason, while meritless says the thing itself lacks merit or worth.
  • undeserved
    4.2 0.83/m
    undeserved refers to a result like praise that someone did not earn, while meritless describes lack of value or merit.
