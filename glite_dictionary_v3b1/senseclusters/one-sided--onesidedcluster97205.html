one-sided

adjective
involving only one side or part
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌnˌsaɪdɪd /; / wʌn ˈsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʌnˌsaɪdɪd /; / wʌn ˈsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
involving, supporting, or affecting only one side, person, or group; not balanced or fair

Examples

One-sided argument
She was frustrated by his one-sided argument, which ignored all other perspectives.
The competition was one-sided, with one team dominating from start to finish.
Their friendship felt one-sided because she was always the one making an effort.
His argument was one-sided and didn't give the opposition a fair chance to be heard.
The newspaper published a one-sided report that ignored important facts from the other perspective.
The referee's one-sided decisions frustrated the losing team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - showing unfair preference for or against someone or something
  • antonym: impartial - treating all sides equally; fair and just
  • antonym: mutual - experienced or done by each of two or more parties toward the other(s)
  • synonym: unilateral - involving or affecting only one side
