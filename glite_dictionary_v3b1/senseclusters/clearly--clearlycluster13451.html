Examples

Speak clearly
He spoke clearly, making it easy for the audience to follow his presentation.
She explained the process clearly so that everyone could understand.
The sign was clearly visible from the road.

Synonyms

  • intelligibly
    4.9 0.13/m
    intelligibly is expressed so listeners easily understand technical or difficult points, while clearly is general and used in many situations.
  • comprehensibly
    3.4 0.03/m
    comprehensibly explains ideas in a way learners or readers can follow, while clearly is more general about being easy to perceive.
  • plainly
    1.8 5.83/m
    plainly speaks or shows facts in a simple, direct way, while clearly covers being easy to see, hear, or understand.
  • distinctly
    3.2 7.47/m
    distinctly means something is easy to notice or separate from others, while clearly focuses on being easy to understand.
  • obviously
    1.0 143/m
    obviously is used when something seems true without doubt to the speaker, while clearly is a neutral word for being understandable.
  • transparently
    3.9 0.38/m
    transparently often shows motives or faults openly and simply, while clearly mainly means easy to see, hear, or understand.
  • discernibly
    5.7 0.10/m
    discernibly means something can be just noticed by senses or mind, while clearly means easily and plainly perceived or understood.
