bare

adjective
without a finish or covering
5.4 1.9
Pronunciation: /beər/ (AmE), /beə/ (BrE)
not covered, protected, or filled; exposed, empty, or plain

Examples

Bare hands
He admired the grain of the bare wood before applying any stain.
Leaving the table bare risks it getting stained from spills.
The walls were left bare, awaiting a final layer of paint.
She preferred a bare table setting, using only essential dishes and utensils.
The living room had bare walls, with not a single picture or decoration.
The new office design is intentionally bare, focusing on simplicity and function over decoration.
He placed his hand on the bare metal, feeling its coldness.
She walked across the lawn in her bare feet, enjoying the cool grass.
The trees stood bare after losing all their leaves in autumn.

  • synonym: desolate - (of a place) deserted and bleak
  • synonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied
  • antonym: fertile - (of land or soil) capable of producing abundant vegetation or crops
  • antonym: full - containing as much as possible; not empty
  • synonym: naked - without clothes or covering
  • antonym: ornate - elaborately decorated
  • synonym: plain - simple, undecorated, or unadorned; lacking embellishment
  • synonym: unfinished - not covered with paint or finish
