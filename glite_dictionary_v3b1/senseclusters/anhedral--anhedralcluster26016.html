anhedral

adjective
irregular crystal or wing angle
Pronunciation: / ænˈhiːdrəl / (AmE), / ænˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
lacking well-formed crystal faces, or having a downward angle of an aircraft wing relative to the horizontal

Examples

Anhedral crystal faces
The mineral was described as anhedral because it lacked distinct crystal faces.
Aircraft designers sometimes use anhedral wings to improve roll performance.
Most grains in the rock sample were anhedral, making them difficult to identify.

Related Terms

  • antonym: euhedral - having well-formed crystal faces
  • degree higher: subhedral - having partially developed crystal faces
