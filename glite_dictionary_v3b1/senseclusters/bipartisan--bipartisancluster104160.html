bipartisan

adjective
involving two political parties
Pronunciation: / baɪˈpɑːr.tɪ.zən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.pɑːˈtɪ.zæn / (BrE)
involving or supported by members of two political parties, especially those with opposing views

Examples

A bipartisan committee
A bipartisan committee drafted the new environmental legislation.
The law was passed with overwhelming bipartisan support in Congress.
The senator called for a bipartisan approach to solving the healthcare crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: partisan - strong supporter of a party or cause
