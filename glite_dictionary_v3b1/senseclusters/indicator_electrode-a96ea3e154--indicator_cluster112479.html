indicator electrode

noun
electrode used for detecting ion concentration
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndɪˌkeɪtɚ ɪˈlɛktroʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪkeɪtə ɪˈlɛktrəʊd / (BrE)
an electrode used in electrochemical measurements that responds to changes in ion concentration or excitation signal without altering the solution's composition

Examples

A indicator electrode in experiment
During the experiment, a silver chloride indicator electrode was immersed in the test sample.
Many electrochemical sensors rely on a well-calibrated indicator electrode for reliable results.
The indicator electrode is essential in measuring the pH of a solution accurately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reference electrode - an electrode with a stable, known potential used for comparison in electrochemical measurements
