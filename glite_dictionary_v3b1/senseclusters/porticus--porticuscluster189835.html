porticus

noun
classical covered walkway
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔrtɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtɪkəs / (BrE)
a covered walkway or porch, especially one found in classical architecture

Examples

A porticus walkway
The ancient temple was surrounded by a porticus that provided shelter from the sun.
Visitors admired the elegant porticus leading to the main hall of the museum.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a porticus at the entrance to the Roman villa.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonnade - a row of columns supporting a roof or entablature
  • synonym: portico - a structure consisting of a roof supported by columns at regular intervals, typically attached to a building as a porch
