phanerogam

noun
seed-producing plant
5.4 9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfæ.nə.rə.ɡæm/ (AmE), /ˈfæ.nə.rə.ɡæm/ (BrE)
a plant that reproduces by producing seeds rather than spores, including all seed-bearing plants such as gymnosperms and angiosperms

Examples

Seed-bearing phanerogams
Early botanists divided the plant kingdom into phanerogams and cryptogams.
Flowering plants and conifers are typical examples of phanerogams.
The phanerogam group includes all plants that develop true seeds.
