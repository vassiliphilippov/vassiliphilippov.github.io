serotinous

adjective
seed release after trigger
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈrɑːtənəs / (AmE), / sɪˈrɒtɪnəs / (BrE)
(of plants, especially cones or seeds) releasing seeds or opening only in response to an environmental trigger (such as fire), or occurring later than usual in the season or development

Examples

A serotinous pine cone
Some pine cones are serotinous and only open to release seeds after a wildfire.
The serotinous nature of these plants helps them survive in fire-prone environments.
Researchers studied how serotinous cones respond to different temperatures during forest fires.
Some pine trees have serotinous cones that only open after exposure to fire.
The serotinous flowers in this region bloom much later than those in neighboring areas.
Researchers studied the serotinous development of seeds in response to environmental changes.

  • synonym: late - happening or done after the expected time
  • antonym: non-serotinous - releasing seeds without an environmental trigger
