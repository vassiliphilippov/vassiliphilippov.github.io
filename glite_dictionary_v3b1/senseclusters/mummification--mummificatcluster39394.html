mummification

noun
drying or preservation like a mummy; becoming lifeless
5.9 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌm.ə.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmʌm.ɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a state in which something becomes dry, shriveled, and lifeless like a mummy, or the process of being preserved as a mummy; also used figuratively to describe something that has become rigid or lifeless

Examples

Natural mummification
After centuries in the hot, dry climate, the body underwent natural mummification.
Critics accused the institution of bureaucratic mummification and resistance to change.
The mummification of cultural traditions can prevent societies from adapting to new circumstances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desiccation - the process of drying out
  • antonym: vitalization - the act of making something more lively or vital
