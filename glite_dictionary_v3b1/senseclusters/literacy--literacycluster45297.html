literacy

noun
competence or skill in a particular domain
3.4 17/m

knowledge, skill, or competence in a particular area, especially the ability to find, understand, evaluate, create, and communicate information in that domain (for example digital, media, or data literacy).

Examples

Digital literacy
Schools now emphasize literacy in digital contexts to prepare students for online information.
Organizations increasingly value literacy in data to drive evidence-based decision making.
Policy makers promote literacy in media to help citizens evaluate news and advertising critically.

Related Terms

  • synonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • antonym: illiteracy - lack of necessary skills or knowledge (often used for inability to read and write)
  • synonym: proficiency - a high degree of skill or expertise in a particular area
