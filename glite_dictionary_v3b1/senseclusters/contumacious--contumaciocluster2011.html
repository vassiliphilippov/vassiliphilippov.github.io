contumacious

adjective
stubbornly disobedient to authority
9.2 9.6
Pronunciation: /kən.tʊ.ˈmeɪ.ʃəs/ /; / /kɑːn.tjə.ˈmeɪ.ʃəs/ (AmE), /kɒn.tjʊ.ˈmeɪ.ʃəs/ /; / /kɒn.tʊ.ˈmeɪ.ʃəs/ (BrE)
(formal, chiefly law) Wilfully and stubbornly disobedient to authority, especially in defiance of a court or official order.

Examples

A contumacious defendant
A contumacious child persistently ignores their parents' instructions, no matter the consequences.
Her contumacious attitude led to further disciplinary action at work.
The court held the contumacious defendant in contempt for repeatedly refusing to answer questions.
