flyby

noun
spacecraft close approach
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbaɪ / (BrE)
a close approach of a spacecraft to a planet or other object, usually for observation or data collection

Examples

Spacecraft flyby of Mars
The spacecraft performed a flyby of Jupiter to capture detailed images of its atmosphere.
Scientists analyzed the data collected during the flyby of the asteroid.
The mission included a flyby of Mars before heading deeper into the solar system.
The spacecraft performed a flyby of Mars to capture detailed images of its surface.
During the flyby, scientists collected important data about the planet's atmosphere.
The mission included several flybys of different moons in the outer solar system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encounter - a meeting or close approach, especially in space exploration
  • antonym: landing - the act of a spacecraft touching down on a surface
