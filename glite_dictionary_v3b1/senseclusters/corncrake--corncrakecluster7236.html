corncrake

noun
a Eurasian rail bird found in grain fields
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnˌkreɪk / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnˌkreɪk / (BrE)
A Eurasian bird of the rail family, noted for its rasping call and frequenting grain fields and meadows.

Examples

The corncrake bird
Efforts to protect the corncrake have led to changes in agricultural practices.
Farmers often encounter corncrakes nesting among their cereal crops.
The distinctive call of the corncrake can be heard in rural fields during spring.
