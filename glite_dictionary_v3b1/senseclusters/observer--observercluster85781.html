observer

noun
expert who comments on something
2.8 34/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàz…ùÀê.v…ö /; / …ôbÀàz…úrv…ö / (AmE), / …ôbÀàz…úÀê.v…ô /; / …ôbÀàz…úÀêv…ô / (BrE)
a person who watches, notices, or pays attention to something, sometimes as an expert

Examples

A careful observer
As a keen observer of politics, she was asked to comment on the new policy.
The conference invited an observer to provide insights during the panel discussions.
The match was attended by a respected observer who later wrote a detailed analysis.
An impartial observer might have seen things differently.
As a casual observer, she was amazed by the dancers' skill.
The observer carefully noted every detail of the experiment.

Synonyms

  • watcher
    3.9 5.30/m
    a watcher watches or monitors things closely and often for safety, while an observer may study or note things more formally.
  • viewer
    1.3 35/m
    a viewer watches films or shows or looks at images for pleasure, while an observer may watch to record or study details.
  • spectator
    2.6 11/m
    a spectator sits in an audience and watches a live event for fun, while an observer may watch to notice or analyze.
  • onlooker
    3.2 2.92/m
    an onlooker stands nearby and watches without joining the action, while an observer often pays attention to notice or record details.
  • looker-on
    4.4 0.01/m
    a looker-on watches an event from the side and does not join in, while an observer may study or judge what happens.
  • beholder
    5.8 2.02/m
    a beholder sees or notices beauty or scenes with personal feeling, while an observer often watches to record facts or study details.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commentator - person who discusses or analyzes events, especially for the public
  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something, as opposed to merely watching
  • synonym: witness - a person who sees an event, typically a crime or accident, take place
