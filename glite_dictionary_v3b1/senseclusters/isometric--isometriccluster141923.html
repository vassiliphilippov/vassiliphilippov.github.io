isometric

adjective
having equal dimensions or measurements
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.səˈmɛ.trɪk /; / ˌaɪ.səˈmɛt.rɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səʊˈmɛ.trɪk /; / ˌaɪ.səʊˈmɛt.rɪk / (BrE)
having equal measurements or dimensions; used for things that are the same size or preserve distances (including in geometry and crystallography)

Examples

Isometric drawing
In an isometric exercise, the muscles contract without changing length.
The artist used an isometric projection to create the illusion of depth in the drawing.
The crystals formed in an isometric shape, having all sides of equal length.
Fluorite displays an isometric crystal habit with symmetrical cubic faces.
Many gemstones, such as garnet, crystallize in the isometric system.
The isometric crystal system is also called the cubic system due to its symmetry.
An isometric mapping ensures that the original and the image have identical lengths between corresponding points.
In geometry, a function is isometric if it preserves the distance between all pairs of points.
The two triangles are isometric, as there is a transformation that preserves distances between them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congruent - (of figures) having the same shape and size
  • degree lower: similar - having the same shape but not necessarily the same size
