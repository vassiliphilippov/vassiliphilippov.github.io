squeamishness

noun
state of mild nausea or discomfort
Pronunciation: / ˈskwiːmɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈskwiːmɪʃnɪs /; / ˈskwiːmɪʃnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily made to feel sick, uncomfortable, or disgusted by unpleasant things (such as blood, dirt, bad smells, or improper behavior)

Examples

His squeamishness about blood
He apologized for his squeamishness at the sight of blood.
Her squeamishness prevented her from watching the surgery on TV.
The movie is not for those with any squeamishness about violence.
His squeamishness prevented him from watching horror movies with his friends.
Some critics accused the editor of squeamishness for refusing to publish controversial artwork.
The surgeon showed no trace of squeamishness during the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nausea - a feeling of sickness with an inclination to vomit
  • synonym: sensitivity - the quality of being sensitive to stimuli
  • antonym: stoicism - the quality of being able to endure pain or hardship without showing feelings or complaining
