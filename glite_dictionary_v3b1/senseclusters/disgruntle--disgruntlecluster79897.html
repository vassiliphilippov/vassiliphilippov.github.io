disgruntle

verb
make dissatisfied or annoyed
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɡrʌn.təl / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡrʌn.tl / (BrE)
to make someone dissatisfied or put them in a bad mood

Examples

To disgruntle employees
Changes in the company’s policies began to disgruntle even the most loyal employees.
Late deliveries can easily disgruntle customers and damage your reputation.
The unfair decision seemed to disgruntle several members of the committee.

Related Terms

  • antonym: satisfy - to make someone pleased or content
