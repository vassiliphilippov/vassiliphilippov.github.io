scleroprotein

noun
fibrous structural protein
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsklɛroʊˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˌsklɪərəʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a class of simple, fibrous proteins that are found in structural tissues such as hair, nails, cartilage, bone, and the lens of the eye

Examples

A scleroprotein fiber
Collagen is a type of scleroprotein abundant in connective tissues throughout the body.
Researchers are studying how scleroprotein structures contribute to tissue strength and flexibility.
The transparency of the eye's lens is partly due to an organized scleroprotein arrangement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: albumin - a type of simple, soluble protein
  • synonym: keratin - a scleroprotein forming hair, nails, feathers, and horns
