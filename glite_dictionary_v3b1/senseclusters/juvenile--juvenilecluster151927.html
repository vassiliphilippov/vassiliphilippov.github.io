Synonyms

  • immature
    3.5 5/m
    immature describes behavior that lacks emotional growth, while juvenile describes actions that seem young or not fully mature.
  • childish
    2.8 7/m
    childish means acting like a child with silly or rude ways, while juvenile is a more general lack of maturity.
  • infantile
    6.5 1.0/m
    infantile shows very childlike or extreme immaturity, while juvenile covers immature behavior without that strong young-child image.
  • puerile
    9.1 0.4/m
    puerile is silly or trivial in a negative way, while juvenile simply means immature without the same ridicule.
  • babyish
    3.0 0.1/m
    babyish feels like the actions of a baby, showing very early childishness, while juvenile means general immaturity.
