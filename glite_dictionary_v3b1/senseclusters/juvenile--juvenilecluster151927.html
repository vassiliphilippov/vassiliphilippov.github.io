juvenile

adjective
immature or childish
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːvəˌnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənaɪl / (BrE)
(of a person, action, or behavior) immature; displaying or suggesting a lack of maturity.

Examples

Juvenile behavior
He lost respect for his colleague after hearing his juvenile remarks at the meeting.
She found his sense of humor too juvenile for her taste.
Their constant arguing seemed rather juvenile considering their age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: childish - like a child; immature
  • antonym: mature - behaving in an adult, responsible way
