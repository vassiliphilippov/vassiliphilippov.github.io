kangaroo hare

noun
small Australian wallaby resembling a hare
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæŋɡəˌruː hɛr / (AmE), / ˌkaŋɡəˌruː heə / (BrE)
A small Australian wallaby that resembles a hare and is distinguished by its persistent teeth.

Examples

The kangaroo hare hops
Researchers observed the unique hopping pattern of the kangaroo hare in western Australia.
The kangaroo hare is difficult to spot in its natural grassy habitat.
Unlike many other marsupials, the kangaroo hare keeps its teeth for life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wallaby - a small or medium-sized marsupial native to Australia and New Guinea
