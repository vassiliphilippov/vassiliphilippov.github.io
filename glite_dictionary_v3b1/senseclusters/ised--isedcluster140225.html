x
verb-forming suffix
Pronunciation: / aɪzd / (AmE), / aɪzd / (BrE)
a suffix (written -ise or -ize) used to form verbs meaning 'to make' or 'to become', as in 'modernise' or 'legalise'

Examples

Words ending ised
The suffix ised is commonly found in British English spellings of certain verbs.
Words like 'modernised' and 'legalised' use the ised ending to show the verb form.
When you add ised to a noun or adjective, it often means 'to make' or 'to become' that thing.
