unbend

verb
relax; become less tense or formal
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbɛnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɛnd / (BrE)
to relax or become less tense, especially mentally or socially

Examples

To unbend and relax
After a stressful week, she tried to unbend by spending time in her garden.
During the informal dinner, he finally began to unbend and share some jokes with colleagues.
It took a while for the strict teacher to unbend and smile at the students' playful antics.
After a long workweek, she likes to unbend with a good book.
He finally allowed himself to unbend and enjoy the vacation.
It's important to unbend and unwind, especially during stressful times.

  • antonym: tense - in a state of mental or nervous strain; anxious (opposite sense)
  • antonym: tense up - to become tense or anxious
