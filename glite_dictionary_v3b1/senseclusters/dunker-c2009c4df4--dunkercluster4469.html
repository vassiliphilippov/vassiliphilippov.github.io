Dunker

noun
member of a baptistic Christian sect
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌŋkər / (AmE), / ˈdʌŋkə / (BrE)
a member of a Christian sect, especially one of the German Baptist Brethren, that practices baptism by immersion

Examples

A Dunker community
In 18th-century America, many Dunker communities settled in Pennsylvania.
My great-grandparents were Dunkers who valued simplicity and communal living.
The Dunker congregation gathered by the river for the annual baptism ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Baptist - a member of a Protestant Christian denomination advocating baptism only of adult believers
