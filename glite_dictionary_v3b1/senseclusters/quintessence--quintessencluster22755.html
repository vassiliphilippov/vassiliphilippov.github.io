In ancient and medieval philosophy, the fifth and highest element, believed to compose heavenly bodies and distinct from air, earth, fire, and water.

Examples

Heavenly quintessence
In ancient cosmology, the stars were thought to be made of quintessence rather than earthly elements.
Medieval thinkers believed that quintessence filled the heavens and gave celestial objects their perfect nature.
Philosophers once debated whether quintessence was present anywhere on Earth.
