ax

noun
tool for chopping, with heavy blade
Pronunciation: / æks / (AmE), / æks / (BrE)
A tool with a heavy bladed head mounted across a handle, used for chopping wood and other materials.

Examples

A sharp ax
An old, rusty ax was found in the shed behind the house.
He used an ax to split the firewood for the campfire.
The lumberjack skillfully swung his ax into the thick tree trunk.
He swung the axe skillfully to split the firewood into manageable pieces.
She used a sharp axe to chop down the dead tree in the garden.
The old axe hanging in the shed had belonged to his grandfather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adze - a cutting tool similar to an axe, but with the blade set at a right angle to the handle
  • synonym: hatchet - a small ax with a short handle
  • antonym: saw - a tool with a toothed blade for cutting through materials, especially wood
