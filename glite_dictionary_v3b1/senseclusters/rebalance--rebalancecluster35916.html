rebalance

verb
to restore balance
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbæləns / (BrE)
to adjust something so that it becomes balanced again, especially after a change or disruption

Examples

To rebalance investments
After the market changed, she needed to rebalance her investment portfolio.
The therapist suggested ways to rebalance work and personal life for better well-being.
Engineers had to rebalance the machine after it started vibrating unevenly.
After the market crash, investors needed to rebalance their portfolios to reduce risk.
She had to rebalance her schedule after starting a new job.
The therapist helped him rebalance his work and personal life for better well-being.

Related Terms

  • synonym: readjust - to change or correct something again
  • antonym: unbalance - to make something not balanced
