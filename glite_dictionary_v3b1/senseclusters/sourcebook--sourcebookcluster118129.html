sourcebook

noun
reference collection
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrsˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈsɔːsbʊk / (BrE)
a book that collects important information, documents, or original texts on a particular subject for reference or study

Examples

A sourcebook of documents
The history teacher assigned readings from a sourcebook containing original letters and speeches.
Researchers often consult a sourcebook to find primary documents related to their topic.
This sourcebook includes important legal cases and commentary for law students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthology - a published collection of writings or documents
  • antonym: textbook - a book used as a standard work for the study of a subject
