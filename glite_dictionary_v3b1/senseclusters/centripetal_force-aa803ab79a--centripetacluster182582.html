centripetal force

noun
force toward the center of a curve
5.4 0.03/m

the force that acts on a body moving in a curved path and is directed toward the center around which the body is moving

Examples

Centripetal force on a planet
A satellite in orbit is kept there by the centripetal force provided by gravity.
Physicists explain that any object moving in a circle must experience a centripetal force toward the center of its path.
When a car turns around a corner, friction supplies the centripetal force that prevents it from skidding outward.
