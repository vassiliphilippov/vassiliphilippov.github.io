regenerative

adjective
able to regrow
5.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (BrE)
having the ability to restore, renew, or regrow something that has been lost or damaged

Examples

Regenerative abilities
Scientists are studying the regenerative abilities of certain animals, like salamanders.
The new treatment has shown regenerative effects on damaged heart tissue.
A healthy diet can have a regenerative impact on your overall well-being.

Related Terms

  • antonym: degenerative - causing gradual decline or deterioration
  • synonym: restorative - having the ability to restore health or strength
