New World goldfinch

noun
a North American yellow-plumaged finch
Pronunciation: / nu wɝld ˈɡoʊldˌfɪntʃ / (AmE), / njuː wɜːld ˈɡəʊldˌfɪntʃ / (BrE)
An American finch (Spinus tristis), especially the male, which has bright yellow body plumage in summer; also called American goldfinch.

Examples

Bright New World goldfinch
During migration, flocks of New World goldfinches can often be seen feeding in open fields.
The New World goldfinch is easily recognized by its bright yellow summer plumage.
Unlike the European species, the New World goldfinch molts into a duller color for the winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: American goldfinch - common North American goldfinch (Spinus tristis)
  • antonym: European goldfinch - a related finch species native to Europe
