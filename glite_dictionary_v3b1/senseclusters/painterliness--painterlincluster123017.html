painterliness

noun
visible brushwork quality
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪntərlinəs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntəlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of having visible brushwork, texture, or a style that resembles a painting, especially in art, film, or photography

Examples

The painterliness of art
The painterliness of the portrait made the brushstrokes stand out to every viewer.
Many modern artists embrace painterliness to highlight the process of creation.
Her landscapes are admired for their rich painterliness and expressive textures.
The film's painterliness was evident in its use of color and composition.
Critics praised the painterliness of the photographs, noting their soft focus and rich textures.
Her artwork is admired for its painterliness, which gives each piece a dreamlike quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: picturesqueness - the quality of being visually attractive, like a picture
  • antonym: realism - the quality of representing things as they are, without idealization
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of being even and without texture
  • synonym: texture - the feel or appearance of a surface in art
