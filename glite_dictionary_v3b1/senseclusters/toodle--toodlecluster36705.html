toodle

verb
to travel leisurely
8.6 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀêd…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàtuÀêd…ôl / (BrE)
to travel or move in a slow, relaxed, or aimless manner, often for pleasure.

Examples

To toodle along
On Sunday afternoons, they like to toodle through the countryside with no particular destination.
We decided to toodle around the city, stopping wherever something caught our interest.
After lunch, she would often toodle along the river, enjoying the peaceful scenery.

Synonyms

  • tootle
    6.8 0.08/m
    tootle means moving in a relaxed, aimless way for pleasure, while toodle is an alternative form used in the same casual situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • synonym: wander - to walk or travel without a fixed purpose or direction
