charge

noun
official instruction or command
5.1 5.1
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a person or thing under someone's care or responsibility; a task or duty assigned

Examples

A charge under care
In his final charge, the chairman urged members to support the new policy.
The committee listened carefully to the charge given at the start of the investigation.
The judge delivered his charge to the jury, outlining their responsibilities.
As a mentor, he takes his charge's development very seriously.
She kept a watchful eye on her young charge at the park.
The teacher was responsible for her charges during the field trip.
As a camp counselor, she was responsible for the safety of each charge.
He tried to teach his charges the value of honesty and hard work.
The nanny read a bedtime story to her young charge every night.
