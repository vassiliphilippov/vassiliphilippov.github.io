charge

noun
official instruction or command
5.1 47/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
a person or thing under someone's care or responsibility; a task or duty assigned

Examples

A charge under care
In his final charge, the chairman urged members to support the new policy.
The committee listened carefully to the charge given at the start of the investigation.
The judge delivered his charge to the jury, outlining their responsibilities.
As a mentor, he takes his charge's development very seriously.
She kept a watchful eye on her young charge at the park.
The teacher was responsible for her charges during the field trip.
As a camp counselor, she was responsible for the safety of each charge.
He tried to teach his charges the value of honesty and hard work.
The nanny read a bedtime story to her young charge every night.

Synonyms

  • assignment
    1.2 31/m
    assignment is a task given by a teacher or boss to be done, while charge can mean a person or duty someone must care for.
  • commission
    4.9 10/m
    commission is an official order or grant of authority used by governments or firms, while charge usually means a person or assigned duty under care.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instruction - detailed information on how something should be done
  • synonym: mission - a special task or assignment given to a person or group
  • synonym: responsibility - a duty or task that you are required or expected to do
  • synonym: ward - a person, especially a minor, under the protection or care of a guardian or court
