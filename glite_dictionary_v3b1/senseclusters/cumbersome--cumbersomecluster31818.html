cumbersome

adjective
hard to handle due to size or weight

large or heavy and therefore difficult to carry, handle, or use; unwieldy.

Examples

Cumbersome luggage
She found the suitcases cumbersome to carry through the crowded airport.
The old printer was too cumbersome to fit on his small desk.
The procedure was so cumbersome that they decided to update their entire system.

Synonyms

    6.7 1.75/m
    unwieldy is hard to move or control because of shape or complexity, while cumbersome focuses on being large, heavy, and hard to carry.
  • bulky
    2.3 4.50/m
    bulky takes up a lot of space and feels large, while cumbersome suggests heavy or awkward to carry or use.
  • clunky
    5.5 1.47/m
    clunky feels awkward in design, often heavy or noisy, while cumbersome mainly means large or heavy and hard to handle.
  • awkward
    2.5 5.55/m
    awkward is generally hard to use or do because of shape or timing, while cumbersome emphasizes bulk and heaviness.
  • ungainly
    7.3 0.57/m
    ungainly looks or moves without grace and seems clumsy, while cumbersome stresses size or weight that makes handling hard.
