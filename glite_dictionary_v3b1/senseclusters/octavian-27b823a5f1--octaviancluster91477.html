Octavian

proper noun
first Roman emperor, originally named Octavian
9.2
Pronunciation: / ɑkˈteɪviən / (AmE), / ɒkˈteɪvɪən / (BrE)
The original name of Augustus, the first Roman emperor (63 BC – AD 14), who founded the Roman Empire and defeated Mark Antony and Cleopatra.

Examples

Octavian the emperor
Octavian became the first emperor of Rome after defeating his rivals at the Battle of Actium.
Before he became Augustus, Octavian was known for his shrewd political strategy.
Historians often refer to Octavian as Augustus after he was granted that title by the Senate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Augustus - The first Roman emperor, also known as Octavian
  • antonym: Mark Antony - Roman politician and general defeated by Octavian
