confounder

noun
distorting factor in study
8.6
Pronunciation: / kənˈfaʊndər / (AmE), / kənˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
a variable or factor in a scientific study that can distort or confuse the relationship between the variables being examined

Examples

A study confounder
The researchers adjusted their analysis to account for each confounder that could affect the results.
Smoking is a common confounder in studies examining the link between diet and heart disease.
If a confounder is not controlled, the study's conclusions may be misleading.
Researchers must identify any confounder that could influence the outcome of their experiment.
Smoking is often considered a confounder in studies about heart disease.
If a confounder is not controlled, the study's conclusions may be unreliable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - a systematic error that affects the results of a study
  • synonym: variable - a factor that can change in a scientific study
