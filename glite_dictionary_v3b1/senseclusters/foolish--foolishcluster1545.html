Examples

A foolish mistake

Synonyms

  • unwise
    3.5 3.07/m
    unwise describes making a poor decision or plan in a situation, while foolish describes acting without good sense or judgment.
  • silly
    1.0 37/m
    silly feels light, playful, or childish, while foolish suggests poor judgment that may cause trouble or look unwise.
  • lamebrained
    8.4 0.02/m
    lamebrained sounds informal and strong, while foolish is a more general word for acting without good judgment.
  • stupid
    1.0 107/m
    stupid describes lack of intelligence or sense in a blunt way, while foolish focuses on poor judgment or unwise choices.
  • dopey
    6.7 0.64/m
    dopey suggests slow or dull thinking and a casual, informal tone, while foolish is general and used in many situations.
  • doltish
    9.3 0.08/m
    doltish means slow-witted or showing low intelligence, while foolish means acting without good judgment rather than being mentally slow.
