pleural space

noun
space between layers of pleura in the chest
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplʊrəl speɪs / (AmE), / ˈplʊərəl speɪs / (BrE)
the thin potential space between the parietal and visceral layers of the pleura, normally containing a small amount of lubricating fluid

Examples

The pleural space fluid
Fluid can accumulate in the pleural space, leading to breathing difficulties.
In healthy individuals, only a thin layer of fluid is present within the pleural space.
The doctor explained that air had entered the pleural space, causing a collapsed lung.
