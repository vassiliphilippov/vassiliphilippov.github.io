pied piper

noun
enticing leader, often to a negative end
9.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /paɪd ˈpaɪ.pər/ (AmE), /paɪd ˈpaɪ.pə/ (BrE)
a person who attracts followers or a crowd, especially by means of charm or persuasive appeal, and often leading them to a negative outcome

The charismatic politician became a pied piper for radical change in the country.
