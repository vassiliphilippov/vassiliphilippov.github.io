Moorish architecture

noun
Islamic-inspired Spanish architectural style (13th–16th c.)
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmʊrɪʃ ˌɑːrkɪˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʊərɪʃ ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
a style of architecture prevalent in Spain from the 13th to 16th centuries, marked by horseshoe arches, ornate tilework, and intricate geometric patterns, influenced by Islamic art and culture.

Examples

Moorish architecture style
Many buildings with Moorish architecture feature intricately carved stucco and detailed tilework.
The Alhambra is a famous example of Moorish architecture in Spain.
Tourists marvel at the elegant arches and courtyards typical of Moorish architecture.
