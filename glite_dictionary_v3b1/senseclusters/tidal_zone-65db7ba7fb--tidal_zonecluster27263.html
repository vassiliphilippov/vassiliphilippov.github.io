tidal zone

noun
coastal area affected by tides
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ.dl zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.dl zəʊn / (BrE)
the area of a shore or coastline that is regularly covered and uncovered by tidal waves

Examples

The tidal zone area
Many unique species have adapted to survive in the tidal zone along rocky coasts.
Pollution in the tidal zone can have serious effects on coastal ecosystems.
Researchers measured temperature changes throughout the tidal zone during the spring season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intertidal zone - the part of the shore between high tide and low tide marks
