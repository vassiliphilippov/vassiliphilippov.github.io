derelict

adjective
abandoned and neglected (building or place)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛrəlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈdɛrəlɪkt / (BrE)
(of a building, place, or object) abandoned, neglected, or in very poor condition due to disuse

Examples

A derelict building
Photographers love exploring derelict factories for their eerie, atmospheric beauty.
Several families moved away, leaving the apartment block derelict and overgrown with weeds.
The city council plans to renovate the derelict warehouse by the river.
After decades of neglect, the warehouse became completely derelict and unsafe to enter.
The derelict building on the corner has been empty for years and is falling apart.
We passed a row of derelict cars rusting in the field behind the factory.
Her studio was once a derelict barn that she lovingly restored.
The old warehouse has become completely derelict after years of abandonment.
We avoided the derelict building at the edge of town because it looked unsafe.
