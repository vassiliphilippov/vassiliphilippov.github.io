wax

verb
cover or treat with wax
Pronunciation: / wæks / (AmE), / wæks / (BrE)
to cover or treat something with wax or a similar substance, especially to polish or protect it

Examples

To wax a car
After cleaning her skis, she decided to wax them for better performance.
He took an hour to wax the car until it shone like new.
You should wax the wooden floor to keep it looking good.
After sanding the table, he decided to wax it to protect the wood.
The dance studio floor is slippery because they just waxed it.
You should wax your car regularly to maintain its finish.
