deadhead

verb
to remove spent flowers
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈded.hed/ (AmE), /ˈded.hed/ (BrE)
to remove dead or faded flowers from a plant to encourage further blooming or growth

Examples

To deadhead roses
Gardeners often deadhead their flowers to keep the plants looking healthy and vibrant.
If you deadhead the daisies regularly, they will produce more blooms throughout the summer.
She deadheaded the wilted roses to help the bush grow new buds.
