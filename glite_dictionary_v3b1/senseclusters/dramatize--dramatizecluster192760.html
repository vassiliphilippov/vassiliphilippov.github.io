dramatize

verb
convert into dramatic form
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdræməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdræmətaɪz / (BrE)
to adapt (a story, novel, or real event) into a play, film, or other dramatic form

Examples

To dramatize a story
Several authors have dramatized historical incidents to appeal to modern audiences.
The director decided to dramatize the true events for the movie adaptation.
They plan to dramatize the classic novel for Broadway next year.
Many authors struggle to dramatise historical facts without distorting the truth.
She is planning to dramatise the events of the expedition as a stage play.
The director wanted to dramatise the novel for a television series.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dramatize - adapt for drama or performance (American spelling)
  • degree lower: novelize - to convert into the form of a novel
