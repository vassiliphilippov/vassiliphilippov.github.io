adaptationist

noun
proponent of adaptation theory
Pronunciation: / əˌdæpteɪˈʃənɪst / (AmE), / əˌdæpteɪˈʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who explains traits or behaviors as adaptations for survival or reproduction, especially in evolutionary biology

Examples

An adaptationist scientist
The adaptationist approach suggests that every trait has a specific evolutionary purpose.
As an adaptationist, she often debates the origins of animal behaviors with her colleagues.
Some critics argue that the adaptationist view can overlook other evolutionary factors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolutionist - a person who believes in or studies evolution
  • synonym: selectionist - a person who emphasizes natural selection in evolution
