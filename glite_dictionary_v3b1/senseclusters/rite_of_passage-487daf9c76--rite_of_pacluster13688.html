rite of passage

noun
ceremony marking a life transition
Pronunciation: / raɪt əv ˈpæsɪdʒ / (AmE), / raɪt əv ˈpɑːsɪdʒ / (BrE)
a ceremony or ritual marking an important transition in a person's life, such as from childhood to adulthood

Examples

A rite of passage ceremony
Graduating from high school is considered a rite of passage for many teenagers.
Hosting your first family holiday dinner can feel like an adult rite of passage.
The walkabout is a traditional rite of passage for young Aboriginal boys in Australia.
