unstop

verb
to remove a barrier
6.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstɑp / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɒp / (BrE)
to remove an obstacle or barrier that is preventing progress, expression, or flow, especially of something nonphysical like ideas or feelings

Examples

To unstop feelings
The counselor helped unstop his emotions so he could talk about his past.
A new approach can sometimes unstop creative thinking in a team.
The inspiring speech seemed to unstop a flood of new ideas among the students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: block - to prevent movement or progress
  • synonym: release - to allow something to move freely or be expressed
