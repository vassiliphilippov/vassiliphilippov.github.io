intercropping

noun
growing mixed crops
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkrɑːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkrɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
the agricultural practice of growing two or more crops together in the same field at the same time

Examples

Intercropping two crops
Many farmers use intercropping to improve soil health and crop yields.
By practicing intercropping, the farmer reduced pest problems and increased biodiversity in his fields.
Researchers are studying how intercropping can help small-scale farmers adapt to climate change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crop rotation - the practice of growing different crops in succession on the same land
  • antonym: monoculture - the cultivation of a single crop in a given area
