scrap

noun
small leftover piece
3.5 1.9
Pronunciation: /skræp/ (AmE), /skræp/ (BrE)
a small piece or fragment of something, especially what is left over or to be thrown away

Examples

A scrap of paper
He used every scrap of material to finish the project.
She wrote her phone number on a scrap of paper and handed it to me.
There wasn't a scrap of food left after the picnic.
Much of the old furniture ended up as scrap in the landfill.
She used a scrap of fabric to patch the hole in her jeans.
The mechanic collected all the scrap metal in a bin for recycling.
A scrap of metal flew off the saw and hit the wall.
He found a scrap of paper under the table and scribbled a note.
The archaeologist examined each scrap of pottery they uncovered.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: chunk - a thick, solid piece cut or broken off something
  • synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or detached
  • synonym: junk - objects or material that are considered useless or of little value
  • synonym: piece - a portion of an object or material
  • synonym: shred - a thin strip or small amount of something cut or torn off
  • degree lower: sliver - a thin, small piece cut or split off
  • antonym: treasure - something valuable or cherished
  • synonym: waste - unwanted or unusable materials; refuse
