crossbreed

verb
to breed from different varieties
4.6 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr…îsÀåbrid / (AmE), / Ààkr…ísÀåbriÀêd / (BrE)
to produce an animal or plant by mating individuals of different breeds, varieties, or species

Examples

To crossbreed plants
Scientists often crossbreed two plant species to create disease-resistant crops.
The farmer decided to crossbreed his sheep with another breed to improve wool quality.
To obtain unique flowers, gardeners frequently crossbreed different varieties of roses.

Synonyms

  • cross
    3.9 1.37/m
    a cross is breeding parents from different varieties or species, while crossbreed means producing offspring from those mixed parents.
  • interbreed
    6.5 0.08/m
    interbreed means to mate across different varieties or species, often naturally, while crossbreed often refers to deliberate breeding by people.
  • hybridize
    7.0 0.56/m
    hybridize means to make hybrids and appears in scientific or gardening talk, while crossbreed is the general term for mixing breeds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hybridize - to produce hybrids by crossbreeding
  • antonym: inbreed - to breed from closely related individuals
