valve

noun
device in brass instrument to change pitch
2.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /vælv/ (AmE), /vælv/ (BrE)
a device or structure that controls the flow of a liquid, gas, or air, such as in a machine, musical instrument, or the body

Examples

Water valve
Modern French horns use rotary valves to change pitch smoothly.
The trumpeter pressed the third valve to reach a lower note.

Each radiator has a valve to control the flow of hot water.
If you turn this valve clockwise, it will shut off the gas supply.

Blood flows through the heart in a single direction thanks to the valve structure.
Doctors replaced the damaged heart valve during surgery.
When the mitral valve fails, blood may leak backward into the atrium.
