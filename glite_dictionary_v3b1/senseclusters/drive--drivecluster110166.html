drive

noun
basic urge motivating behavior
3.3
Pronunciation: / draɪv / (AmE), / draɪv / (BrE)
a strong motivation, urge, or determination to achieve something

Examples

Strong drive
His strong drive for achievement pushed him to work harder than anyone else.
Hunger can be considered a biological drive that compels us to eat.
The survival drive ensures that animals seek food and shelter.
Despite many setbacks, his drive never seemed to fade.
Her drive to succeed inspired her entire team to work harder.
The job requires someone with creativity, drive, and attention to detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambition - a strong desire to achieve success
  • antonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • synonym: impulse - a sudden strong and unreflective urge to act
  • synonym: motivation - the reason or reasons one has for acting or behaving in a particular way
