clostridium

noun
genus of rod-shaped bacteria
9.9
Pronunciation: / klɒˈstrɪdiəm / (AmE), / klɒˈstrɪdiəm / (BrE)
A genus of rod-shaped, mostly anaerobic bacteria, some species of which can cause disease in humans and animals.

Examples

Clostridium bacteria species
Clostridium botulinum produces a toxin that can cause severe food poisoning.
Doctors tested the sample for clostridium to identify the cause of the infection.
Some species of clostridium are found in soil and can survive without oxygen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacillus - genus of rod-shaped, gram-positive bacteria
