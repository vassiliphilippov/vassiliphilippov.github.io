bootprint

noun
mark from a boot
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌprɪnt / (BrE)
a mark or impression left by a boot on a surface, often showing the pattern or design of the sole

Examples

A bootprint on carpet
The muddy bootprint on the carpet showed someone had entered through the back door.
After the rain, every bootprint was clearly visible in the soft garden soil.
The astronaut left the first bootprint on the surface of the moon.
The detective found a clear bootprint in the muddy field near the fence.
Each bootprint in the snow pointed toward the cabin in the woods.
The unique bootprint helped the police identify the suspect's footwear.
