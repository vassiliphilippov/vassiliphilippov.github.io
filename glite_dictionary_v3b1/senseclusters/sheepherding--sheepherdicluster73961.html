sheepherding

noun
herding sheep event
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈʃiːpˌhɜrdɪŋ /; / ˈʃiːˌhɜrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpˌhɜːdɪŋ /; / ˈʃiːˌhɜːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the activity or event of guiding and taking care of sheep, often with the help of dogs

Examples

A sheepherding competition
The annual sheepherding competition attracted participants from all over the country.
She trained her border collie for months before entering a sheepherding event.
Spectators watched in awe as the dogs displayed their sheepherding skills on the field.
He spent his weekends sheepherding with his border collie in the open fields.
Competitions in sheepherding test both the dog's obedience and the handler's skill.
Learning the basics of sheepherding can help farmers manage their flocks more efficiently.
He spent his summers sheepherding in the mountains.
Modern sheepherding often uses trained dogs to help manage the flock.
After years of sheepherding, she knew every trail in the valley.
