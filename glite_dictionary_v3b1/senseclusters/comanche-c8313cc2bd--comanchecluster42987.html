Comanche

noun
Native American people of the central U.S.
Pronunciation: / koʊˈmæn.tʃi / (AmE), / kəˈmæn.tʃi / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally living between Wyoming and the Mexican border, now chiefly in Oklahoma.

Examples

Comanche tribe member
He is proud of his Comanche heritage and often shares stories of his ancestors.
Many Comanche now live in Oklahoma, preserving their cultural traditions.
The Comanche were renowned for their skill as horsemen on the plains.
