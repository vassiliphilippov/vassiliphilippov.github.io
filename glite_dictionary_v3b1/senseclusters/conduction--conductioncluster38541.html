conduction

noun
transfer of heat, electricity, or sound
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which heat, electricity, or sound is transmitted through a material without the movement of the material itself

Examples

Heat conduction
Metal pans allow for rapid conduction of heat during cooking.
Poor conduction in the cable caused the lights to flicker.
Sound conduction through the walls was improved using insulation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convection - transfer of heat by movement of a fluid
  • antonym: radiation - transfer of energy as electromagnetic waves
