reality principle

noun
psychoanalytic principle guiding adaptation to reality
4.8 7.1
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈæl.ə.tiː ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /riː.ˈæl.ə.tiː ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /rɪ.ˈæl.ɪ.tiː ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈæl.ə.tiː ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /rɪ.ˈæl.ɪ.tiː ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the governing principle of the ego; the tendency to adapt behavior to the demands of the external, real world rather than to internal desires or fantasies.

Examples

The reality principle in ego
According to Freud, the reality principle helps the ego restrain impulsive behavior.
As children mature, they gradually learn to follow the reality principle rather than acting on pure desire.
Balancing the pleasure principle with the reality principle is central to healthy psychological development.
