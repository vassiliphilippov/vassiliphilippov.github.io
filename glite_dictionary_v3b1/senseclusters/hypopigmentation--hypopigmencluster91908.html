hypopigmentation

noun
loss of skin pigment; lighter skin patches
6.4 7.0
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.pɪɡ.mən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /haɪ.poʊ.pɪɡ.mən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.pɪɡ.mən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a condition in which areas of the skin become lighter than normal due to a loss or reduction of pigment

Examples

Skin hypopigmentation patch
After the injury healed, a patch of hypopigmentation developed on her arm.
Certain skin conditions can cause localized hypopigmentation, resulting in uneven skin tone.
People with albinism typically exhibit hypopigmentation all over their skin and hair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depigmentation - loss or removal of skin pigment
  • antonym: hyperpigmentation - excess pigment in skin, leading to darker areas
