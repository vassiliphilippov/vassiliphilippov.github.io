turnout

noun
railroad siding or passing track
7.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ùÀên.a ät / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀên.a ät / (BrE)
a short section of railroad track connected to the main line, used for storing trains or allowing them to pass each other

Examples

Railroad turnout
Maintenance crews stored their equipment on a nearby turnout.
The engineer directed the freight cars onto the turnout to clear the main line.
There was a turnout ahead where trains could pass each other safely.

Synonyms

  • siding
    6.7 0.20/m
    siding is a side track used to hold trains or let others pass, while turnout names the short connecting track on the main line.
  • sidetrack
    6.8 0.09/m
    sidetrack is a short side track for storing trains or letting others pass, while turnout refers to the connecting piece beside the main line.

Related Terms
