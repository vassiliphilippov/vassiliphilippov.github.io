decentration

noun
moving from egocentric to shared perspective (psychology)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀådiÀês…õnÀàtre…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀådiÀês…õnÀàtre…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
In psychology, especially in Piaget's theory of cognitive development, the process by which a person moves from a self-centered point of view to being able to consider perspectives other than their own.

Examples

Child decentration process
Jean Piaget described decentration as a key part of children's cognitive growth.
Mastering decentration allows a child to cooperate more effectively with peers.
Through decentration, children learn to understand that others can have different opinions than theirs.

Synonyms

  • decentering
    7.7 0.13/m
    decentering describes a child's move from seeing only their view during development, while decentration is Piaget's wider term for shifting perspective.
