concessionary

adjective
on favorable terms
7.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃənəri / (BrE)
provided on more favorable or preferential terms than usual, especially in financial arrangements, contracts, or rights

Examples

Concessionary loan terms
The government offered concessionary rates to help small businesses recover after the crisis.
Students can travel at concessionary fares on public transport with a valid ID.
Many charities rely on concessionary loans to fund their community projects.
The government offered concessionary terms to encourage investment in the new railway project.
A concessionary agreement allowed the company to manage the port facilities for twenty years.
They received a concessionary license to operate food stalls at the festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contractual - relating to a contract or agreement
  • synonym: preferential - giving an advantage or better terms than usual
  • antonym: restrictive - limiting or controlling, not granting rights
  • antonym: standard - usual or regular, not offering special terms
