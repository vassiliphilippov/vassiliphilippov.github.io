field sandbur

noun
grass with prickly burs
9.9
Pronunciation: / fiːld ˈsændˌbɝː / (AmE), / fiːld ˈsændˌbɜː / (BrE)
a grass native to the eastern United States and tropical America, known for its spikelets enclosed in prickly burs

Examples

Field sandbur grass
Children playing barefoot often get field sandbur burs stuck in their feet.
Gardeners try to remove field sandbur to prevent its prickly seeds from spreading.
The field sandbur can quickly spread across disturbed soils and lawns.
