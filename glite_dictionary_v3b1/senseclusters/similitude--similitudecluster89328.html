similitude

the quality or state of being similar or alike; a resemblance in appearance, character, or nature between persons or things

Examples

Similitude between ideas
Her speech had a certain similitude to his, suggesting they shared similar viewpoints.
In literature, authors often use similitude to draw comparisons between unrelated ideas.
The similitude between the two paintings was striking, though they were created decades apart.

Synonyms

  • similarity
    2.7 16/m
    similarity is the common, simple word for shared traits between things, while similitude sounds formal and less used in speech.
  • likeness
    3.5 3.44/m
    likeness often shows how things look or act similarly in a clear way, while similitude feels more formal or abstract.
  • resemblance
    3.0 8.81/m
    resemblance often points to similar appearance or surface features, while similitude covers broader likeness in character or nature.
  • alikeness
    6.8 0.01/m
    alikeness names the state of being alike but sounds old or rare, while similitude is more literary and formal in use.
