Paleolithic Age

noun
early Stone Age period
6.7
Pronunciation: /peɪ.liː.ə.ˈlɪ.θɪk eɪdʒ/ /; / /peɪ.liː.oʊ.ˈlɪ.θɪk eɪdʒ/ (AmE), /peɪ.lɪ.ə.ˈlɪ.θɪk eɪdʒ/ /; / /peɪ.liː.əʊ.ˈlɪ.θɪk eɪdʒ/ (BrE)
The earliest period of the Stone Age, marked by the use of stone tools, beginning roughly 2.5 million years ago and ending about 10,000 years ago with the close of the last ice age.

Examples

The Paleolithic Age period
Cave paintings in France were created during the Paleolithic Age.
Many early human artifacts from the Paleolithic Age have been unearthed in Africa.
Stone tools became increasingly sophisticated throughout the Paleolithic Age.
