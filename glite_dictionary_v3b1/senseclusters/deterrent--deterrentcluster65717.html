deterrent

adjective
serving to discourage action
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɜr.ənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛr.ənt / (BrE)
serving to discourage someone from taking a particular action, especially by instilling doubt or fear of the consequences

Examples

Deterrent measures
High interest rates are a deterrent factor for people considering taking out loans.
Strict penalties act as a deterrent measure against tax evasion.
The new security cameras have a clear deterrent effect on petty crime in the area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encouraging - giving someone support or confidence to do something
  • synonym: preventive - serving to prevent something from happening
