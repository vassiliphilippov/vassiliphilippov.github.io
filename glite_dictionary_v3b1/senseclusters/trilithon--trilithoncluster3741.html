trilithon

noun
stone structure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪlɪˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪlɪθɒn / (BrE)
a structure made of two large upright stones with a third stone placed horizontally across the top

Examples

A trilithon structure
The ancient builders erected a massive trilithon at the entrance to the monument.
Archaeologists studied the alignment of each trilithon to understand its purpose.
Visitors to Stonehenge are often amazed by the size of each trilithon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dolmen - a type of single-chamber megalithic tomb
  • synonym: megalith - a large stone used in ancient structures or monuments
