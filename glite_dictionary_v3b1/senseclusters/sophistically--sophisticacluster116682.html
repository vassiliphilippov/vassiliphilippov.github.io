sophistically

adverb
in a clever manner
9.7
Pronunciation: / səˈfɪstɪkli / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that is sophisticated, clever, or subtly argued, often showing complex reasoning or style

Examples

Sophistically explain a rule
The lawyer sophistically explained the contract, making the terms sound more appealing than they were.
She sophistically defended her position, using complex arguments to persuade the audience.
The report was sophistically written, impressing the committee with its depth and clarity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artfully - in a skillful or clever way
  • antonym: simply - in a plain or uncomplicated way
