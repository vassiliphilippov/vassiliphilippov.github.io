shoo

interjection
telling to go away
7.5
Pronunciation: / ʃuː / (AmE), / ʃuː / (BrE)
used to tell someone or something to go away or be quiet, often by making a hissing or waving gesture

Examples

Shoo! Go away
The farmer waved his hands and shouted shoo at the birds in the field.
When the cat jumped on the table, she said shoo to make it leave.
He whispered shoo to the children playing outside his window.

Related Terms

  • antonym: come here - an instruction to approach
  • synonym: go away - an instruction to leave
