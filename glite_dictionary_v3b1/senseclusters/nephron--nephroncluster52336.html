nephron

noun
functional unit of the kidney
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛˌfrɑn / (AmE), / ˈnɛfrɒn / (BrE)
the functional unit of the kidney, consisting of a small tubule through which blood is filtered to form urine

Examples

The nephron unit
Damage to the nephron can lead to impaired kidney function.
Each human kidney contains about a million nephrons, which filter the blood.
Scientists are studying how a nephron adapts to different levels of hydration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glomerulus - small network of capillaries in the nephron where filtration occurs
