programmatic

adjective
planned or systematic
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊɡrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌprəʊɡrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
planned or organized according to a formal program, plan, or set of policies

Examples

Programmatic plan
The new mayor introduced a programmatic approach to improving public transportation.
Their programmatic efforts focused on increasing access to education in rural areas.
A programmatic review of the company's policies led to several important changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spontaneous - done without planning or premeditation
  • synonym: systematic - done according to a fixed plan or system
