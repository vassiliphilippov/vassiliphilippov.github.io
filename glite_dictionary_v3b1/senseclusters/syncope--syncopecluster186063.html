syncope

noun
loss of sounds within a word (phonology)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkəpi / (BrE)
(Phonology) The omission of sounds or letters from the middle of a word, as in 'fo'c'sle' from 'forecastle'.

Examples

Syncope in spelling
Historical texts provide many examples of syncope in English word forms.
In some dialects, the syncope of unstressed vowels is common.
The word 'chocolate' often undergoes syncope in casual speech, pronounced as 'choc'late'.
