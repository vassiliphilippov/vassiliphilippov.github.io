salute

noun
act of honoring or showing respect
Pronunciation: / səˈlut / (AmE), / səˈluːt / (BrE)
an act or gesture of respect, greeting, or honor, often formal or ceremonial (such as raising a hand or bowing, especially in the military)

Examples

A salute to heroes
Her book is a loving salute to her late mentor.
The mayor's speech was a heartfelt salute to the retiring police chief.
The parade ended with a musical salute celebrating the country's independence.
She offered a polite salute by tipping her hat to the guests.
The soldiers gave a crisp salute as the general passed by.
With a friendly salute, he greeted his neighbors each morning.
Every morning at the base, the soldiers line up for the flag-raising salute.
She returned his salute with a smile, acknowledging his respect.
The officer gave a crisp salute as the general entered the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disregard - lack of consideration or respect; ignoring something deserving attention
  • antonym: farewell - an act of saying goodbye, often with a gesture
  • synonym: greeting - the act of giving a polite word or sign of welcome
  • synonym: tribute - an act, statement, or gift intended to show gratitude, respect, or admiration
