tatty

adjective
shabby or in poor condition
3.6 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtæti / (AmE), / ˈtæti / (BrE)
(of clothes, furniture, or other objects) in poor condition because of age or much use; shabby or worn.

Examples

Tatty old sweater
She wore a tatty sweater with several holes in the sleeves.
The tatty pages of the book were yellowed and torn.
The old sofa looked tatty after years of heavy use by the family.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled
  • synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long or hard use or lack of care
