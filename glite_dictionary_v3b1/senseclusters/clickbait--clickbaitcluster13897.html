clickbait

verb
to attract clicks dishonestly
6.0
Pronunciation: /klɪk.ˈbeɪt/ /; / /ˈklɪk.beɪt/ (AmE), /klɪk.ˈbeɪt/ /; / /ˈklɪk.beɪt/ (BrE)
to create or use sensational or misleading headlines or content to attract attention and increase clicks online

Examples

To clickbait readers
Some websites clickbait their articles with exaggerated headlines to get more visitors.
He was criticized for trying to clickbait readers with misleading photos.
If you clickbait too often, people may stop trusting your content.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inform - to give someone facts or information
  • synonym: mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
  • synonym: sensationalize - to present information in a way that provokes public interest or excitement, often at the expense of accuracy
