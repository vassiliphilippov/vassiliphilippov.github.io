indemnity

noun
money paid as compensation for loss or injury

A sum of money paid as compensation for loss, damage, or injury, especially under the terms of an insurance policy or legal contract.

Examples

Indemnity payment received
She received a large indemnity for the injuries sustained in the accident.
The contract specified an indemnity in case of unforeseen losses.
The insurance company paid an indemnity to cover the damages after the fire.

Synonyms

  • damages
    3.9 0.03/m
    damages are money a court orders to a harmed person, while indemnity is a contractual or insurance payment to cover loss.
  • compensation
    3.6 20/m
    compensation is money or other payment for loss or work, while indemnity refers to a contract or insurance payment that covers damage.
  • restitution
    6.9 2.02/m
    restitution means returning or restoring what was lost, while indemnity means paying money under a contract or insurance for loss.
  • amends
    3.5 0.00/m
    amends are actions or payments to try to repair wrongs, while indemnity is a formal monetary payment under contract or insurance.
