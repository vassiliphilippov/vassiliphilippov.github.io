Cartier

proper noun
French explorer
7.2
Pronunciation: / kɑːrˈtjeɪ / (AmE), / kɑːˈtjeɪ / (BrE)
Jacques Cartier (1491–1557), a French explorer known for his early voyages to eastern Canada and the St. Lawrence River.

Examples

Cartier explores Canada
Cartier is credited with mapping the Gulf of St. Lawrence during his first voyage.
Many Canadian history books discuss the impact of Cartier's explorations in the 16th century.
The river that Cartier explored became a major route for future French settlers.
