avalanche

noun
mass of snow/ice sliding downhill
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæv.əˌlæntʃ / (AmE), / ˈæv.əl.ɑːnʃ / (BrE)
A mass of snow, ice, and sometimes mud that rapidly slides down a mountainside.

Examples

A snow avalanche
Heavy snowfall increased the risk of an avalanche on the steep slopes of the mountain.
Several skiers were caught in a deadly avalanche in the Alps last winter.
The rescue team rushed to the village after an avalanche blocked the main road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: landslide - mass of earth or rock moving down a slope
