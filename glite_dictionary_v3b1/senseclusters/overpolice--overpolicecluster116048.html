overpolice

verb
to enforce too strictly
7.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.pə.ˈliːs/ /; / /oʊ.vər.pə.ˈlɪs/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.pə.ˈliːs/ /; / /əʊ.və.pə.ˈlɪs/ (BrE)
to police or enforce rules too strictly or with excessive intensity, often by authorities or institutions

Examples

To overpolice neighborhoods
Some communities feel that the authorities overpolice their neighborhoods, leading to tension and mistrust.
Critics argue that schools should not overpolice students for minor rule violations.
The city council debated whether new policies would overpolice public spaces and limit personal freedoms.
