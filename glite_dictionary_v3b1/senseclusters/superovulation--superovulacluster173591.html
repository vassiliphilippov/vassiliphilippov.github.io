superovulation

noun
increased egg production
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˌoʊvəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərˌəʊvəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the production of more eggs than normal in a single ovulation cycle, usually through medical stimulation

Examples

Superovulation treatment
Fertility specialists often use medication to induce superovulation in patients undergoing IVF.
The process of superovulation increases the chances of retrieving multiple eggs for fertilization.
After superovulation, several mature eggs were collected for the next stage of treatment.
