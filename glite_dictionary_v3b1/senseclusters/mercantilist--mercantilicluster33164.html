mercantilist

noun
supporter of mercantilism
Pronunciation: / mərˈkæntɪlɪst / (AmE), / mɜːˈkæntɪlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices mercantilism, the economic theory that trade generates wealth and should be regulated by governments

Examples

A mercantilist thinker
The 17th-century mercantilist believed that a nation's wealth depended on its stock of gold and silver.
Many mercantilists argued for strict government control over imports and exports.
As a mercantilist, he supported policies that favored national industries over foreign competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free trader - a person who supports minimal government interference in international trade
  • synonym: protectionist - a person who supports protecting domestic industries through tariffs or restrictions
