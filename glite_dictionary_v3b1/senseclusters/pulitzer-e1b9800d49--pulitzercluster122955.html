Pulitzer

proper noun
famous newspaper publisher
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊlɪtsər / (AmE), / ˈpʊlɪtsə / (BrE)
Joseph Pulitzer (1847–1911), a Hungarian-American newspaper publisher and journalist who established the Pulitzer Prizes.

Examples

Pulitzer the journalist
Joseph Pulitzer transformed American journalism with his innovative approach to reporting.
The legacy of Pulitzer lives on through the prestigious prizes that bear his name.
Many journalists admire Pulitzer for his commitment to investigative reporting.
