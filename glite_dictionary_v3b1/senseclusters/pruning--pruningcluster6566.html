pruning

noun
removing parts of a machine-learning model
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nɪŋ / (BrE)
(in machine learning and data processing) the process of removing unnecessary or redundant weights, nodes, or parameters from a model to reduce size, speed up inference, and sometimes improve generalization.

Examples

Model pruning
The team improved inference speed after pruning the neural network's redundant channels and weights.
Model pruning reduced the deployment memory footprint, allowing the app to run on older smartphones.
Researchers evaluated accuracy loss versus compression rate when applying structured pruning to convolutional layers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: model compression - techniques for reducing the size and computational cost of machine-learning models
  • synonym: sparsification - the process of making a model or matrix sparse by removing or zeroing parameters
