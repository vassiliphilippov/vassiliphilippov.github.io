amyloid plaque

noun
abnormal protein clump in nervous tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.i.lɔɪd ˈplæk / (AmE), / ˈæm.ɪ.lɔɪd ˈplɑːk / (BrE)
An abnormal clump of amyloid protein that accumulates in nerve tissue, often found in the brains of people with Alzheimer's disease.

Examples

Brain amyloid plaque
Recent therapies aim to reduce amyloid plaque buildup to slow cognitive decline.
Researchers found amyloid plaque deposits in several areas of the patient's brain tissue.
The presence of amyloid plaques is a hallmark of Alzheimer's disease diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neurofibrillary tangle - twisted fibers inside nerve cells, another marker of Alzheimer's disease
  • synonym: senile plaque - another term used for amyloid plaque in the context of Alzheimer's
