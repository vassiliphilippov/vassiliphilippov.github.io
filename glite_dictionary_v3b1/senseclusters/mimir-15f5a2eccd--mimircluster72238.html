Mimir

proper noun
Norse mythological giant; guardian of wisdom well
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmiː.mɪr / (AmE), / ˈmiː.mɪə / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) a giant who resides at the roots of Yggdrasil and guards the well of wisdom.

Examples

Mimir in Norse myths
In Norse legends, Mimir is renowned for his great wisdom and knowledge.
Legends say that Mimir's severed head continued to give counsel to the gods.
The god Odin visited Mimir to drink from his well and gain wisdom.
