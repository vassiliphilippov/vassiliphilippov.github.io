Colchicaceae

noun
family of flowering plants (e.g., Colchicum, Gloriosa)
9.9
Pronunciation: /kɑːl.ˈkɪk.ə.siː/ /; / /kɑːl.ˈkɪk.eɪ.siː/ (AmE), /kɒl.ˈkɪk.ə.siː/ /; / /kɒl.ˈkɪk.eɪ.siː/ (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes genera such as Colchicum and Gloriosa, sometimes considered part of or separate from the lily family (Liliaceae)

Examples

The Colchicaceae family
Many autumn-blooming bulb plants belong to the family Colchicaceae.
Plants in the Colchicaceae family are known for producing toxic alkaloids.
The classification of Colchicaceae has been a topic of debate among botanists.
Botanists recently reclassified several genera from Liliaceae to Colchicaceae.
Members of the Colchicaceae can often be found in temperate and subtropical regions.
The Colchicaceae family is known for plants that produce colchicine, a toxic alkaloid.
