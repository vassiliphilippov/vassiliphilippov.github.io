bogey

noun
archaic: goblin or evil spirit
3.1 9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈboʊ.ɡiː/ (AmE), /ˈbəʊ.ɡiː/ (BrE)
(archaic) A bogle, goblin, or evil spirit, sometimes used as a name for the Devil.

Examples

A scary bogey
In some stories, the bogey is blamed for unexplained disappearances.
The old tale warned children of the bogey lurking in the woods at night.
They spoke in hushed tones, fearful of summoning the bogey by name.
In old legends, the bogie lurked in the misty moors, waiting for travelers.
Some villagers believed misfortune meant the bogie was angered.
The children were scared the bogie would come for them in the night.

  • synonym: devil - the chief evil spirit in Christian and other religions
