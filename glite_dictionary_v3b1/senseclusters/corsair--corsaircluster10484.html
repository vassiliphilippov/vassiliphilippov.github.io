corsair

noun
pirate of the Barbary Coast
Pronunciation: / kɔːrˈsɛr / (AmE), / kɔːˈsɛə / (BrE)
a pirate, especially one operating along the Barbary Coast of North Africa during the 16th to 19th centuries

Examples

Barbary corsair
He boasted that his ancestor had once served as a notorious corsair off the coast of Tunis.
Many European navies tried to defend their ships from corsairs during the 17th century.
The feared corsair swept the Mediterranean, plundering merchant ships for treasure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea
