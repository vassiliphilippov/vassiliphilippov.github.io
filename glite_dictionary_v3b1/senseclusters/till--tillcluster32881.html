till

noun
drawer or box for holding cash
7.1 1.9
Pronunciation: /tɪl/ (AmE), /tɪl/ (BrE)
a drawer, box, or place for keeping money, especially in a shop or as a fund

Examples

Shop till
At the end of the day, Sarah counted the money in the till to balance the accounts.
He unlocked the till to give the customer the correct change.
The shop assistant placed the notes carefully in the till after the customer paid.
He was accused of embezzling money from the state till.
Taxes were collected and deposited into the royal till.
The kingdom’s till was nearly empty after years of war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cash register - a machine for recording sales and storing cash in shops
  • synonym: safe - a strong container for storing valuables, especially money and documents
  • synonym: treasury - a place where money is kept, especially by a government
