nightjar

noun
crepuscular or nocturnal insect-eating bird
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtˌdʒɑr / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌdʒɑː / (BrE)
A mainly nocturnal bird with mottled greyish-brown plumage and large eyes, which feeds on insects.

Examples

A nightjar bird
Nightjars are known for their excellent camouflage and elusive behavior.
The distinctive call of the nightjar echoed through the woodland at night.
We spotted a nightjar resting silently on a tree branch at dusk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whippoorwill - a North American nightjar with a distinctive call
