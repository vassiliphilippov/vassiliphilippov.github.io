collagen

noun
fibrous protein in connective tissue
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɑdʒən / (AmE), / kəˈlædʒən / (BrE)
a tough, fibrous protein that forms the main component of connective tissues such as skin, bone, cartilage, and tendons

Examples

The collagen protein
As we age, our bodies produce less collagen, leading to wrinkles and joint pain.
Bovine collagen is commonly used in cosmetic procedures such as fillers.
Scientists are developing synthetic collagen for tissue engineering and wound healing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gelatin - a jelly-like substance derived from collagen by boiling; used in food and pharmaceuticals
