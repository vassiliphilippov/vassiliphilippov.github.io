abreast

adjective
up to date; informed
6.6
Pronunciation: / əˈbrɛst / (AmE), / əˈbrɛst / (BrE)
having current knowledge of recent developments; up to date.

Examples

Stay abreast of news
I try to keep abreast of all the latest technological advances in my field.
It's important for executives to be abreast of changes in the market.
The newsletter helps readers stay abreast of important local events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness in general or about something
  • synonym: informed - having or showing knowledge; aware of facts
