semimetallic

adjective
partly metallic
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌməˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌmɛˈtælɪk / (BrE)
having properties that are partly like those of a metal and partly like those of a nonmetal

Examples

Semimetallic brake pads
The engineer recommended using semimetallic brake pads for improved durability and performance.
Some elements, like silicon, are considered semimetallic because they share properties with both metals and nonmetals.
The semimetallic coating provides a balance between strength and flexibility in the finished product.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: metallic - having the characteristics of a metal
  • degree lower: nonmetallic - not having the characteristics of a metal
