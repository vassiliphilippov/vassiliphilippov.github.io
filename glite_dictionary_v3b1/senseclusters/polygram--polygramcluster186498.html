polygram

noun
letter group in ciphers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈpɒliˌɡræm / (BrE)
a group of letters or symbols treated as a single unit in cryptography, often used in substitution ciphers

Examples

Cipher polygram
The cryptanalyst identified a polygram in the encoded message to help break the cipher.
A polygram cipher replaces groups of letters rather than single characters, making it harder to decode.
By analyzing the frequency of each polygram, the team uncovered patterns in the secret communication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digraph - a pair of letters representing one sound or unit
  • antonym: monogram - a single letter or symbol used as a unit
