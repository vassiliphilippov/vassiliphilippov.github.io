anthropoid ape

noun
large, tailless primate (e.g. chimpanzee, gorilla)
6.3 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈænθrəˌpɔɪd eɪp / (AmE), / ˈænθrəpɔɪd eɪp / (BrE)
any of various large, tailless primates of the families Pongidae and Hylobatidae, such as gibbons, orangutans, gorillas, and chimpanzees, that resemble humans more closely than other monkeys.

Examples

Large anthropoid apes
Scientists have observed complex social behavior among anthropoid apes in the wild.
The gorilla is the largest living anthropoid ape, known for its strength and intelligence.
Unlike most monkeys, an anthropoid ape lacks a tail and has a more upright posture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ape - a primate of the family Hominoidea, includes both anthropoid apes and lesser apes
  • antonym: monkey - a primate typically having a tail, in contrast to apes
