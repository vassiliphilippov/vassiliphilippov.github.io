antirealist

adjective
rejecting objective reality
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˈriːəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈrɪəlɪst / (BrE)
rejecting or opposing the belief that reality exists independently of perception or interpretation, especially in philosophy or art

Examples

Antirealist view in philosophy
The philosopher presented an antirealist argument against the existence of an objective world.
Her antirealist approach to literature focuses on symbolism rather than literal representation.
Some scientists criticize the antirealist position for denying the existence of facts independent of observation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realist - believing that reality exists independently of perception
  • synonym: subjectivist - emphasizing personal perspectives or interpretations over objective reality
