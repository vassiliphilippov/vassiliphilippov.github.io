play therapy

noun
psychotherapy for children using play
7.4 3.8
Pronunciation: /pleɪ ˈθer.ə.piː/ /; / /pleɪ ˈθɜːr.ə.piː/ (AmE), /pleɪ ˈθer.ə.piː/ /; / /pleɪ ˈθɜːr.ə.piː/ (BrE)
A form of psychotherapy in which play is used as a way for children to express feelings, reveal problems, and work towards psychological healing.

Examples

Play therapy with children
During play therapy, children often act out their feelings using dolls and toys.
Many schools now offer play therapy as part of their counseling services to support students' mental health.
The psychologist recommended play therapy to help the child work through her anxiety issues.
