outrage

noun
a shocking or disgraceful event
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌreɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.reɪdʒ / (BrE)
a shocking, cruel, or disgraceful event or action that causes strong anger or indignation

Examples

A shocking outrage
Many considered the government's decision to be an outrage against justice.
The destruction of the historic monument was an outrage that shocked the entire country.
The scandal caused public outrage and demands for accountability.
She considered the unfair dismissal an outrage against justice.
The government’s decision was seen as an outrage by many citizens.
The vandalism of the monument was an outrage that angered the entire community.
That act of senseless cruelty was an outrage that could not be ignored.
The dictator's latest outrage against his people stunned the international community.
The vandalism was seen as an outrage by local residents.
