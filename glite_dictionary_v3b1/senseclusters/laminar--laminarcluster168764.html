laminar

adjective
arranged in thin layers or plates
Pronunciation: / ˈlæm.ɪ.nɚ /; / ˈlæmɪnɑr / (AmE), / ˈlam.ɪ.nə /; / ˈlæmɪnɑː / (BrE)
arranged in or relating to thin layers, especially describing a smooth, regular flow of fluid in parallel layers

Examples

A laminar flow
Air flows smoothly in a laminar fashion through certain types of pipes.
The laminar arrangement of tissues is important in leaf anatomy.
The scientist observed the laminar structure of the mineral under the microscope.
The scientist observed laminar flow in the narrow glass tube during the experiment.
Air moves in a laminar pattern over the wings of an airplane at low speeds.
Engineers prefer laminar flow in pipes to reduce energy loss and noise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smooth - having an even, regular surface or movement
  • synonym: stratified - formed or arranged in layers
  • antonym: turbulent - describing a chaotic, irregular flow of fluid
