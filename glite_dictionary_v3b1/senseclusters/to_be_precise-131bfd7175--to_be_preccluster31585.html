to be precise

adverb
to indicate accuracy or exactness
Pronunciation: / tə bi prəˈsaɪs / (AmE), / tə bi prɪˈsaɪs / (BrE)
used to indicate that what you have just said is accurate or that you are about to give more exact information.

Examples

To be precise about dates
He was born in the spring of 1982, to be precise, on April 3rd.
The project will take three months, to be precise, ninety-two days.
We moved here a few years ago—five, to be precise.
