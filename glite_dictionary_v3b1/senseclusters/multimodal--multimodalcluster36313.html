multimodal

adjective
with multiple peaks
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈmoʊdəl / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈməʊdəl / (BrE)
having two or more distinct modes or peaks, especially in a statistical distribution or dataset

Examples

Multimodal distribution
The data showed a multimodal distribution, indicating several groups within the population.
Researchers noticed that the test results were multimodal, with peaks at different age ranges.
A multimodal pattern in the graph suggested more than one common outcome.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bimodal - having two modes or peaks
  • antonym: unimodal - having only one mode or peak
