summoner

noun
church court officer
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌm.ə.nər/ /; / /ˈsʌm.ən.ər/ (AmE), /ˈsʌm.ə.nə/ /; / /ˈsʌm.ən.ə/ (BrE)
a person whose job is to deliver official court summons, especially in historical or church contexts

Examples

Court summoner
In medieval England, a summoner would deliver official notices to villagers accused of breaking church law.
The summoner was often feared because he brought people before the church court.
Chaucer's Canterbury Tales features a colorful character who works as a summoner.
The summoner arrived at the office to deliver the official court documents.
As a summoner, she was responsible for ensuring people received their legal notices.
The judge asked the summoner if all parties had been properly notified.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bailiff - an officer of the court who maintains order and carries out court orders
  • synonym: beadle - a minor parish officer with various duties
  • antonym: defendant - a person required to appear in court to answer a summons
