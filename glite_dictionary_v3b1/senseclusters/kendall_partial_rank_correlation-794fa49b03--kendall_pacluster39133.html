Kendall partial rank correlation

noun
nonparametric partial correlation measure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛn.dəl ˈpɑːr.ʃəl ræŋk kəˌreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɛn.dəl ˈpɑː.ʃl̩ raŋk kəˌrɛɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A nonparametric statistical method used to measure the strength and direction of association between two variables while controlling for the effect of one or more additional variables.

Examples

Kendall partial rank correlation method
A statistician applied the Kendall partial rank correlation to examine relationships among non-normally distributed variables.
Researchers used the Kendall partial rank correlation to control for confounding variables in analyzing the data.
The Kendall partial rank correlation method is useful when data do not meet the assumptions of parametric tests.
