fool

noun
someone lacking good judgment
Pronunciation: / fuːl /; / ful / (AmE), / fuːl / (BrE)
a person who behaves unwisely, lacks good judgment, or is easily deceived

Examples

A fool makes mistakes
Despite his reputation as a fool, he occasionally showed surprising insight.
He acted like a complete fool during the meeting and embarrassed himself in front of his colleagues.
Only a fool would try to fix the electrical wiring without turning off the power first.
He felt like a fool when he realized he had fallen for the scam.
Only a fool would believe such an obvious lie.
She trusted him completely, but he treated her like a fool.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - a person who is easily deceived
  • synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • antonym: sage - a person who is wise
  • antonym: wise person - a person showing wisdom or good judgment
