Alexander II

proper noun
Russian emperor who emancipated the serfs
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪɡˈzændər ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændə ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(1818–1881) Emperor of Russia from 1855 to 1881, known for introducing reforms such as the emancipation of the serfs.

Examples

Alexander II emperor Russia
Alexander II is best remembered for liberating the Russian serfs in 1861.
Many historians regard Alexander II as one of Russia’s most progressive monarchs.
The assassination of Alexander II marked a turning point in Russian history.
