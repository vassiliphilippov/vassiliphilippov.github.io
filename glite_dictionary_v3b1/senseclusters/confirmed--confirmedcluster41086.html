confirmed

adjective
firmly established in habit or status
4.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàf…úrmd / (AmE), / k…ônÀàf…úÀêmd / (BrE)
(of a person or their habit) having a particular habit or belief that is firmly established and unlikely to change.

Examples

A confirmed smoker
After years of illness, he became a confirmed invalid and rarely left his room.
Despite his friends' encouragement, Mark remained a confirmed bachelor well into his fifties.
She is a confirmed skeptic who never accepts anything at face value.
