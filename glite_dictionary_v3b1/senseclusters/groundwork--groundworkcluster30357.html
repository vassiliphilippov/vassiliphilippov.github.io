groundwork

noun
basic foundation or basis for something
3.8 4.12/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°ra ändÀåw…úrk /; / Àà…°ra ändÀåw…ùk / (AmE), / Àà…°ra ändÀåw…úÀêk / (BrE)
the basic work, preparation, or foundation that makes something possible or successful

Examples

The groundwork for success
Negotiations between the two countries provided the groundwork for the peace treaty.
The researchers laid the groundwork for future studies with this important discovery.
Years of training formed the groundwork for her successful career as a dancer.
Before starting the new project, extensive groundwork was necessary to ensure its feasibility.
Her research provided important groundwork for future developments in the field.
The team's success was possible because of the groundwork laid by previous managers.
