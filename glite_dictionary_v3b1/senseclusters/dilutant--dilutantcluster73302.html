dilutant

noun
substance used to dilute another
4.5 6.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈlutənt / (AmE), / dɪˈljuːtənt / (BrE)
a substance added to another substance to dilute it, making it less concentrated

Examples

A dilutant substance
The laboratory technician added a dilutant to reduce the chemical's concentration.
Water is a common dilutant in many experiments.
When mixing paints, a dilutant is often required to achieve the desired consistency.
