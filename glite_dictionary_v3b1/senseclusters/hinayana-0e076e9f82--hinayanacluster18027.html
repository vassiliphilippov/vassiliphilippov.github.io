Hinayana

noun
school of Buddhism focused on personal salvation
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪniˈjɑːnə / (AmE), / ˌhɪniˈjɑːnə / (BrE)
a historical school of Buddhism emphasizing personal salvation through individual effort, contrasted with the Mahayana tradition

Examples

Hinayana Buddhism
Early Buddhist scriptures formed the doctrinal basis of what came to be known as Hinayana.
Scholars debate the relevance of Hinayana in contemporary Buddhist studies.
The term Hinayana is often considered pejorative by some Buddhist communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Mahayana - a major school of Buddhism teaching universal salvation, contrasted with Hinayana
