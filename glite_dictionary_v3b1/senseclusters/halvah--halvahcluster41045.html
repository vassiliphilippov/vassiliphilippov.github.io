halvah

noun
sweet grain dessert
6.8
Pronunciation: / hɑlˈvɑ / (AmE), / hælˈvɑː / (BrE)
a sweet dessert made from semolina, grain flour, or vegetables cooked with sugar and fat, popular in South Asian and Middle Eastern cuisines

Examples

Semolina halvah treat
She served homemade halvah after dinner, garnished with chopped pistachios.
During the festival, families often prepare halvah as a traditional treat.
The bakery offers several varieties of halvah, including ones with nuts and dried fruit.
