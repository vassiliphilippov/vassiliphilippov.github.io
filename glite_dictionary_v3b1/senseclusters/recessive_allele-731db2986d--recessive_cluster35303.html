recessive allele

noun
an allele expressed only when inherited from both parents

an allele that produces its characteristic effect only when paired with an identical allele, not masked by a dominant allele

Examples

Recessive allele inherited
Cystic fibrosis is caused by a recessive allele that must be inherited from both parents.
If only one recessive allele is present, the dominant allele will mask its effect.
Individuals with two copies of the recessive allele will express the trait.
