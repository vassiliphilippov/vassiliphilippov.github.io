downrush

noun
sudden downward flow
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.rʌʃ/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.rʌʃ/ (BrE)
a sudden, rapid downward movement or decrease, especially of people, things, or amounts

Examples

A downrush of people
The sudden downrush of students filled the hallways as the bell rang.
A downrush of cold air swept through the open window during the storm.
When the gates opened, a downrush of fans hurried toward the stage.
The unexpected downrush in sales figures alarmed the company's management.
After the announcement, there was a downrush in the value of the currency.
A sudden downrush of water flooded the lower fields after the dam broke.

  • synonym: plunge - a sudden and steep fall or drop
  • synonym: stampede - a sudden rush of a crowd, usually in panic
  • antonym: trickle - a slow, small flow
  • antonym: upsurge - a sudden increase or rise
