Agave

noun
genus of succulent plants
8.3 6.9
Pronunciation: / əˈɡɑːveɪ / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːviː / (BrE)
A genus of succulent plants native to the Americas, known for their rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves and tall flower spikes, including species used for making tequila and sisal.

Examples

Agave plant species
Several species of Agave are cultivated as ornamental plants in gardens.
The Agave produces a tall flowering stalk at the end of its life cycle.
The blue Agave is the primary source of tequila in Mexico.
