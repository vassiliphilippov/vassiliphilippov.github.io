blowpipe

noun
tube for intensifying flame heat
Pronunciation: / ˈbloʊˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌpaɪp / (BrE)
A tube through which air or gas is blown to intensify the heat of a flame, commonly used in metalworking or laboratory work.

Examples

Laboratory blowpipe
A blowpipe can be used to analyze minerals in a laboratory setting.
The glassblower inserted air through the blowpipe to shape the molten glass.
The jeweler used a blowpipe to heat the small piece of gold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blowlamp - a portable device that produces a flame for heating
