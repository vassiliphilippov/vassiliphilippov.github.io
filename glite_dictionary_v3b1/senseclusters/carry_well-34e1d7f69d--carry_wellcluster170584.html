carry well

verb
to be easily heard over distance
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri wɛl / (AmE), / ˈkæri wɛl / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) to be easily heard over a distance; to project clearly and distinctly.

Examples

Sound carries well
Her laughter carries well on a quiet evening, reaching neighbors across the street.
Thanks to the microphone, his speech carried well throughout the crowded hall.
The actor's voice carries well even to the back row of the theater.

Related Terms
