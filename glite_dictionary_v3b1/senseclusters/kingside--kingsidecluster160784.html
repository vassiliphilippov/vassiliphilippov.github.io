kingside

adjective
chess king's side
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪŋsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋsaɪd / (BrE)
relating to the side of a chessboard where the king begins the game, usually the right side for White

Examples

Kingside attack
The player prepared a kingside attack to pressure the opponent's defenses.
A kingside castle can help protect your king early in the game.
He advanced his pawns on the kingside to open lines for his rook.

Related Terms

  • antonym: queenside - relating to the side of a chessboard where the queen begins the game
