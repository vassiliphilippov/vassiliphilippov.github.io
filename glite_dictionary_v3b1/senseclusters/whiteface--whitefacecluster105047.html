whiteface

noun
breed of beef cattle with a white face
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌfeɪs / (BrE)
A hardy English breed of cattle, distinguished by its white face, extensively raised in the United States and other countries for beef.

Examples

Whiteface cattle breed
Whiteface cattle are valued for their adaptability to different climates.
Many ranches across the Midwest specialize in breeding whiteface for their high-quality beef.
The farmer added ten whiteface calves to his herd this spring.
