wretchedness

noun
state of being poor or miserable
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtʃɪdnɪs /; / ˈrɛtʃɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛtʃɪdnɪs / (BrE)
a state of great unhappiness, misery, or suffering, often caused by poverty or misfortune

Examples

After losing his home, he sank into a deep wretchedness that colored every day.
The novel vividly describes the wretchedness of life in the overcrowded city.
They could not escape the wretchedness of poverty during the harsh winter.
