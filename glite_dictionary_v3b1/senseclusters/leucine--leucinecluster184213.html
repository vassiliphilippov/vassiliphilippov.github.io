leucine

noun
an essential amino acid
6.0 0.1/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêsin / (AmE), / ÀàljuÀêsiÀên / (BrE)
an essential amino acid found in proteins, important for human nutrition and obtained from the breakdown of dietary proteins

Examples

Leucine amino acid
Leucine plays a key role in regulating blood sugar and promoting tissue growth.
Some protein supplements contain high levels of leucine to support muscle recovery.
The human body cannot synthesize leucine, so it must be obtained through diet.
