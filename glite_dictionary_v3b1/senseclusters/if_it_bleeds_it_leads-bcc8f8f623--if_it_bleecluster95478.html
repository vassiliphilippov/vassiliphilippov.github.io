if it bleeds, it leads

x
media prioritization of violent or sensational stories
5.3
Pronunciation: / ɪf ɪt bɫidz ɪt lidz / (AmE), / ɪf ɪt bliːdz ɪt liːdz / (BrE)
used to express the idea that violent or sensational news stories attract more attention and are more likely to be featured prominently by the media.

Examples

If it bleeds, it leads saying
Critics accuse the media of focusing on crime since if it bleeds, it leads often drives ratings up.
Many editors choose to put the most shocking stories on the front page because if it bleeds, it leads.
Our journalism professor warned us about unethical coverage by saying, 'if it bleeds, it leads isn't always the right approach.'
