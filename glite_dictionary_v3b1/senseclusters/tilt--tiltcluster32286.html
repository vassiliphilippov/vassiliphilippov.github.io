tilt

noun
a slant or inclination
7.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /tɪlt/ (AmE), /tɪlt/ (BrE)
a slant, lean, or inclination away from a straight or level position

Examples

A tilt in the wall
After the earthquake, the building's tilt was detected by engineers.
The old chair had a noticeable tilt to the left side.
You could see the tilt of the picture frame from across the room.
As the train rounded the sharp curve, a sudden tilt caused the passengers to grip their seats tightly.
Engineers monitored the tilt of the structure to ensure it remained stable during the reconstruction.
The ship developed a noticeable tilt after taking on water in the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inclination - the angle or degree of something sloping away from the vertical or horizontal
  • synonym: lean - the act of bending or inclining to one side
  • synonym: list - a tilt to one side, especially of a ship
  • antonym: upright - being in a vertical or straight position
  • antonym: vertical - being at a right angle to the horizon
