furphy

noun
false rumor
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝːfi / (AmE), / ˈfɜːfi / (BrE)
an untrue or unreliable rumor or story, often spread informally

Examples

A wild furphy
He dismissed the report as just another furphy.
Rumors about the company's closure turned out to be a furphy.
Don't believe every furphy you hear at the pub.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fact - something that is known or proven to be true
  • synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often untrue
  • synonym: rumor - an unverified story or piece of information spread among people
