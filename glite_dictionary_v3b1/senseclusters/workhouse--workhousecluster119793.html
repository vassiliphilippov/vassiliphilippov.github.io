workhouse

noun
county jail or institution for short-term prisoners
4.4 7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwɜrkˌhaʊs /; / ˈwɝːkˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a historical institution where poor people or minor offenders were housed and made to work, especially in Britain

Examples

The workhouse system
After his arrest, he spent several weeks in the local workhouse.
Many 19th-century towns maintained a workhouse for the poor and the convicted.

After losing his job, he feared he would be sent to the workhouse.
Hundreds of the city's destitute ended up in the workhouse during the harsh winter.
The novel describes the grim conditions of children living in a Victorian workhouse.
