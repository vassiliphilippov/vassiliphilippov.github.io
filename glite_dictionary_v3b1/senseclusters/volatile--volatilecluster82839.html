volatile

adjective
inconstant or changeable in feelings
7.4 2.35/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ëÀêl…ôtl / (AmE), / Ààv…íl…ôt å…™l / (BrE)
(of a person or their feelings) liable to sudden, unpredictable changes in affection or attachments; inconstant or fickle.

Examples

Volatile personality
His volatile nature meant he was affectionate one moment, then distant the next.
Sarah's volatile feelings made it hard for her friends to predict her reactions.
The relationship ended because of her partner's volatile attachments.

Synonyms

  • fickle
    6.2 2.09/m
    fickle often shifts loyalties and feelings without warning, while volatile describes sudden, unpredictable changes in attachment or mood.
  • mercurial
    8.2 0.51/m
    mercurial swings from one mood to another very quickly, while volatile implies sudden changes in feelings or attachments.
  • temperamental
    6.5 0.48/m
    temperamental has mood changes that may affect behavior in daily life, while volatile focuses on sudden, unpredictable shifts in affection.

Related Terms
