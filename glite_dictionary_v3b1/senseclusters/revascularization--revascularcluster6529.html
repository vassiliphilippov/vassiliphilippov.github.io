revascularization

noun
restoring blood flow
Pronunciation: / riˌvæskjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / riˌvæskjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən /; / riːˌvæskjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical process or procedure to restore blood flow to an organ or tissue by reopening, creating, or encouraging the growth of blood vessels

Examples

Revascularization procedure
After the blockage was discovered, the doctor recommended revascularization to restore proper blood flow.
Successful revascularization can greatly improve a patient's chances of recovery after a heart attack.
Advances in revascularization techniques have reduced the risks associated with treating blocked arteries.
The patient underwent revascularization to improve blood flow to his heart.
Doctors are researching new methods of revascularization for treating severe limb injuries.
Natural revascularization can sometimes occur after minor tissue damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angiogenesis - the formation of new blood vessels
  • synonym: angioplasty - a procedure to widen narrowed or blocked blood vessels
  • synonym: bypass surgery - surgery to create a new route for blood flow around a blocked artery
  • antonym: ischemia - an inadequate blood supply to an organ or part of the body
