co-sign

verb
sign jointly (esp. for a loan)
5.9 3.7
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ˈsaɪn/ /; / /ˈkoʊ.saɪn/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ˈsaɪn/ /; / /ˈkəʊ.saɪn/ (BrE)
to sign a document together with another person, especially to share responsibility for something (such as a loan or agreement)

Examples

To co-sign a loan
He regretted having co-signed his cousin’s loan when the payments were missed.
If you co-sign the apartment lease, you will be responsible for the rent if your friend defaults.
She agreed to co-sign the loan so her brother could buy a car.
Both employees were required to co-sign the expense report before submission.
Her father agreed to co-sign the loan so she could buy a car.
If you co-sign a credit application, you are legally responsible for repayment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guarantee - to formally assure the performance or reliability of something, often financially
  • synonym: sign - to write one's signature on a document
