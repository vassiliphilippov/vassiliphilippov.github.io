binucleated

adjective
having two nuclei (biology)
8.0
Pronunciation: / baɪˈnuːkliˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / baɪˈnjuːklɪˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell) having two nuclei.

Examples

Binucleated cell
A binucleated cell can result from a failure of cytokinesis during cell division.
Many liver cells are naturally binucleated, containing two distinct nuclei within a single membrane.
The researchers observed an increase in binucleated cells following the drug treatment.
