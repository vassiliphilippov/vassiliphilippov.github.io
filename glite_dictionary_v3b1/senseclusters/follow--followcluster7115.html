follow

verb
go after; pursue
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈfɒl.əʊ/ (BrE)
to go after or move behind someone or something, or to travel along a path or direction

Examples

To follow a path
If you follow the runner, you might catch up to her before the finish line.
The detective decided to follow the suspect down the dark alley.
The reporter followed the politician throughout the campaign to document every event.
Many people follow celebrities on social media and try to dress like them.
She tried to follow her older brother by joining the same sports teams he did.
Young artists often follow the techniques of famous painters to develop their own style.
Hikers are advised to follow the marked trail through the forest for safety.
We decided to follow the river upstream to find the waterfall.
You should follow the main road until you see the church on your left.

  • synonym: imitate - to copy someone's actions, appearance, or manner
  • antonym: lead - to show others how to do something by being a model
  • synonym: observe - to watch something carefully, especially for a period of time
  • synonym: pursue - to follow in order to catch; chase
