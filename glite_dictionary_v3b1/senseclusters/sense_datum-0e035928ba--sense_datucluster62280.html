sense datum

noun
basic sensory experience
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛns ˌdeɪ.təm / (AmE), / ˈsɛns ˌdeɪ.təm / (BrE)
A basic, immediate experience or awareness resulting from sensory stimulation, not yet interpreted or processed by the mind.

Examples

Basic sense datum
The philosopher argued that all knowledge begins with sense data gathered from our environment.
The study examined how the brain organizes raw sense data into meaningful perceptions.
Visual illusions show how easily our minds can misinterpret sense data.
