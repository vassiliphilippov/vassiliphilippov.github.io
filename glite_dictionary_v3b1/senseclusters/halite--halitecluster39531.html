halite

noun
mineral form of sodium chloride
Pronunciation: / ˈhæˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhælaɪt / (BrE)
A naturally occurring mineral form of sodium chloride (rock salt), typically found in sedimentary deposits.

Examples

A halite mineral
Geologists examined the crystal structure of the halite sample under a microscope.
Huge deposits of halite are often found where ancient seas have evaporated.
The miners extracted halite from deep underground to supply the chemical industry.
