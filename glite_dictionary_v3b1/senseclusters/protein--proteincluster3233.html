protein

noun
essential organic compound in living cells
2.8 36/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpro ä.tin / (AmE), / Ààpr…ô ä.tiÀên / (BrE)
any of a class of nitrogen-containing organic compounds composed of long chains of amino acids, essential for the structure and functioning of living cells and required in the diet for growth and tissue repair.

Examples

A protein deficiency
A protein deficiency can lead to serious health problems in children.
Eggs, beans, and fish are all excellent sources of protein.
People who exercise regularly often increase their protein intake to help build muscle.
