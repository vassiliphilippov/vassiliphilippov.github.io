facultative

adjective
optional; granting permission or choice
Pronunciation: / ˈfælkjəˌteɪtɪv /; / fəˈkʌl.təˌtɪv / (AmE), / fækˈʌltətɪv /; / fəˈkʌl.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
optional or not compulsory; allowing choice or discretion

Examples

Facultative attendance
Participation in the workshop is facultative, not a mandatory part of the program.
The new legislation includes a facultative clause, allowing cities to adopt the policy at their discretion.
The university offers several facultative courses that students can choose to take beyond their core requirements.
In some species, parental care is facultative rather than obligatory.
Participation in the conference is facultative, not required for graduation.
The special assignment was facultative, giving students the choice to complete it for extra credit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elective - optional; available as a choice, especially in education
  • antonym: mandatory - required by law or rules; compulsory
  • synonym: optional - not compulsory; left to choice
