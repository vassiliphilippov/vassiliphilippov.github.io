sterilize

verb
make unable to produce offspring
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛr.əˌlaɪz /; / ˈstɛr.ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈstɛr.ɪ.laɪz / (BrE)
to make a person or animal unable to produce offspring, typically by a medical procedure

Examples

To sterilize a cat
After the procedure, she discovered she was sterilized and could not have children.
In the past, some governments wrongly sterilized people without their consent.
The doctors had to sterilize the dog to prevent it from breeding.
She chose to sterilise her cat to prevent unwanted litters.
Some governments once tried to sterilise people against their will for eugenic reasons.
The clinic will sterilise the animals before offering them for adoption.

Related Terms

  • synonym: castrate - to remove the testes or ovaries, making an individual infertile
  • antonym: fertilize - to make fertile or able to produce offspring
  • synonym: sterilize - to make infertile; American spelling
