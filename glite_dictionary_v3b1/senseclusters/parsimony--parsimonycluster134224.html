parsimony

noun
extreme frugality
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːr.səˌmoʊ.ni /; / ˈpɑːrsəˌmoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.sɪ.mə.ni /; / ˈpɑːsɪˌməʊni / (BrE)
extreme unwillingness to spend money or use resources; stinginess

Examples

Extreme parsimony
Despite her wealth, she was known for her remarkable parsimony.
His parsimony led him to reuse envelopes and avoid unnecessary expenses.
The company's parsimony sometimes hindered important investments.
Critics accused the government of parsimony in funding public services.
Despite his wealth, his parsimony prevented him from making charitable donations.
Her parsimony was well known, and she would never spend a penny more than necessary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generosity - willingness to give or share money, help, or time freely
  • synonym: stinginess - unwillingness to spend money; mean with money
