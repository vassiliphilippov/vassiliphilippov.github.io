pyrrhic

adjective
(of a victory) gained at heavy cost
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɪrɪk / (BrE)
(of a victory) won at such great cost that it is almost tantamount to defeat; relating to or resembling the exploits of Pyrrhus, especially victories with heavy losses.

Examples

Pyrrhic victory
Historians often use the term pyrrhic to describe battles where both sides suffered heavy losses.
The CEO's decision was a pyrrhic triumph; profits rose briefly but morale plummeted.
Winning the championship turned out to be a pyrrhic victory, as most of the team was injured.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pyrrhic victory - a victory gained at too great a cost
  • degree lower: costly - involving great loss or sacrifice
