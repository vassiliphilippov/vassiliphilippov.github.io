spiral

verb
move or curve in a spiral pattern
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪ.rəl /; / ˈspaɪrəl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪ.rəl /; / ˈspaɪərəl / (BrE)
to move or travel in a continuous curve that winds around a central point, often rising or falling gradually

Examples

To spiral upwards

The hikers watched as the eagle spiraled higher into the sky.
Vines spiral around the old wooden fence.
Smoke spiraled up from the burning building, twisting into the sky.
The children watched the leaves spiral slowly to the ground in the autumn breeze.
The hawk began to spiral higher and higher above the valley.
Black smoke began to spiral up into the sky from the burning building.
The hawk spiraled gracefully above the valley, searching for prey.
Vines spiral around the old tree trunk, creating a beautiful natural design.
