depreciable

adjective
able to lose value
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpriːʃəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈpriːʃəbl / (BrE)
able to lose value over time, especially in accounting or for tax purposes

Examples

Depreciable assets
Company vehicles are considered depreciable assets for accounting purposes.
The accountant explained which office equipment was depreciable under current tax laws.
Not all property is depreciable; land, for example, does not lose value over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amortizable - able to be gradually written off as an expense
  • antonym: appreciable - able to increase in value
