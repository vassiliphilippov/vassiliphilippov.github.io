compactness

noun
the state of being closely packed
4.7 3.6
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpæk.tə.nəs/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpæk.tə.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being closely and tightly packed together; the state of being dense or taking up little space

Examples

The compactness of design
Engineers admired the compactness of the machine's design, which saves space.
The compactness of the city center makes it easy to walk everywhere.
The compactness of the furniture allowed more people to fit into the small apartment.
High compactness in soil often indicates low water penetration.
Scientists measured the compactness of the rock to determine its porosity.
The compactness of the material makes it suitable for building heavy-duty structures.
