black mustard

noun
Eurasian plant, source of mustard seeds
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈmʌstɚd / (AmE), / blæk ˈmʌstəd / (BrE)
a Eurasian annual plant (Brassica nigra) grown for its pungent seeds, used as a principal source of table mustard

Examples

Fields of black mustard
Fields of black mustard bloom in late spring across the countryside.
Spicy black mustard seeds are often used in Indian cuisine.
The chef added freshly ground black mustard to the salad dressing for an extra kick.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mustard - the condiment made from mustard seeds; also the group of plants producing these seeds
  • degree lower: white mustard - a related plant with pale seeds used for mild mustard
