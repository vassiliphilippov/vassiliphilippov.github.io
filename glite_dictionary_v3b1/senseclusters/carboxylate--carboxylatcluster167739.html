carboxylate

noun
carboxylic acid ion
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈbɑksɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒksɪˌleɪt / (BrE)
a negatively charged ion formed when a carboxylic acid loses a hydrogen ion

Examples

Carboxylate ion
The carboxylate group plays a key role in many biochemical reactions.
When a carboxylic acid loses a proton, it forms a carboxylate ion.
Enzymes often interact with the carboxylate part of amino acids during metabolism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anion - a negatively charged ion
  • antonym: carboxylic acid - an organic acid containing a carboxyl group
