musculus teres major

noun
muscle that moves and rotates the upper arm
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs ˌtɛr.iːz ˈmeɪ.dʒər / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ˌtɛː.riːz ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
A muscle of the upper limb that helps move the arm backward and rotate it inward toward the body.

Examples

Musculus teres major muscle
Injury to the musculus teres major can limit the range of motion in your shoulder.
Physical therapists may target the musculus teres major during rehabilitation exercises for rotator cuff injuries.
The musculus teres major works together with other muscles to help rotate the arm inward.
