overwind

verb
to make overly tense
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈwaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwaɪnd / (BrE)
to make someone or something excessively tense, nervous, or emotionally agitated

Examples

To overwind her nerves
The constant pressure from her boss began to overwind her nerves.
Loud noises can easily overwind some animals, making them anxious.
He tried not to overwind himself before the big presentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stress - to cause mental or emotional strain
