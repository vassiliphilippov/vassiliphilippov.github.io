factor VIII

noun
blood-clotting protein; deficiency causes hemophilia A
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tər eɪt / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ˈeɪt / (BrE)
a protein in blood plasma essential for normal blood clotting; its deficiency causes hemophilia A

Examples

Factor VIII protein
People with hemophilia A have low levels of factor VIII in their blood.
Routine monitoring of factor VIII is crucial in patients undergoing surgery for hemophilia.
The doctor administered an injection of factor VIII to help stop the bleeding.
