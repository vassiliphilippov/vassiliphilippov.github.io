bounce-back

adjective
quickly recovering
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊns ˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbaʊns ˌbæk / (BrE)
happening immediately after a setback and showing a quick recovery or improvement

Examples

Bounce-back attitude
The coach praised the team's bounce-back attitude after their tough defeat.
Her bounce-back performance impressed everyone following her recent injury.
They showed a bounce-back spirit by winning the next game after losing badly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged; not able to recover quickly
  • synonym: persistent - continuing firmly despite difficulties
  • synonym: resilient - able to recover quickly from difficulties
