regal

adjective
fit for or characteristic of royalty
5.3 1.85/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀê…°…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàriÀê…°…ôl / (BrE)
of, relating to, or suitable for a king or queen; royal or magnificent in appearance or manner.

Examples

Regal manner
His deep voice and regal manner made him perfect for the role of the king in the play.
She entered the hall with a regal bearing that commanded everyone's attention.
The hotel suite was decorated in a regal style, with velvet drapes and golden chandeliers.

Synonyms

  • royal
    3.1 24/m
    royal refers to the family or rank of a king or queen, while regal highlights a grand, magnificent appearance or manner.
  • kingly
    6.7 0.34/m
    kingly shows the rank and bearing of a king, while regal suggests a broad royal dignity and magnificent manner.
  • queenly
    5.6 0.09/m
    queenly shows the manners, grace, or rank of a queen, while regal means a grand, royal style or appearance.
  • majestic
    4.3 3.43/m
    majestic looks impressive, grand, and awe-inspiring, while regal points more to a royal, dignified appearance or manner.
  • imperial
    6.2 13/m
    imperial relates to an empire or emperor and formal power, while regal focuses on royal dignity and magnificent manner.
  • princely
    6.0 0.33/m
    princely suits a prince and means dignified or generous, sometimes grand, while regal describes royal appearance or suitability.
  • noble
    3.6 1.22/m
    noble means dignified, high in rank, or morally good, while regal specifically suggests royal grandeur and fit for monarchs.
