artificialism

noun
Czech surrealist art style, 1928
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪfɪʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪfɪʃəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A style of surrealist art developed in Czechoslovakia in 1928 by Jindřich Štyrský and Toyen, characterized by abstract compositions suggestive of dreamlike or subconscious imagery.

Examples

Artificialism style
Her paintings from the 1930s are marked by the influence of artificialism.
