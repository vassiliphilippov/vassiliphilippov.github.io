full-throated

adjective
expressed loudly and enthusiastically
Pronunciation: / ˌfʊlˈθroʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌfʊlˈθrəʊtɪd / (BrE)
loud and forceful in voice or expression; expressed with unrestrained enthusiasm.

Examples

Full-throated singing
His full-throated singing brought the entire audience to their feet.
She offered a full-throated defense of her beliefs during the debate.
The crowd erupted in a full-throated roar as the home team scored the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft-spoken - quiet or gentle in speech or manner
