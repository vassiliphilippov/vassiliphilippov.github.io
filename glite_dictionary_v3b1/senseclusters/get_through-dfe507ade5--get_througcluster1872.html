get through

verb
reach or affect emotionally or mentally
1.6 2.6
Pronunciation: /ɡɛt θruː/ /; / /ɡɛt θrʌf/ (AmE), /ɡɛt θruː/ /; / /ɡɛt θrʌf/ (BrE)
(of an idea, message, or feeling) to become understood, accepted, or felt by someone; to succeed in communicating or making an impression.

Sometimes it's hard for advice to get through to teenagers.
The speaker's passion really got through to the audience.
