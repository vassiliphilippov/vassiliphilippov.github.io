creaky

adjective
having a rasping or grating sound
4.1 0.90/m
Pronunciation: / Ëˆkriki /; / ËˆkriËki / (AmE), / ËˆkriËki / (BrE)
making a harsh, creaking sound, usually because of age, wear, or stiffness

Examples

A creaky door
She tried to open the creaky door as quietly as possible so no one would hear her.
The creaky voice of the elderly man filled the empty hall.
The old house had creaky wooden floors that echoed with every step.
After years of use, the office chair became creaky and uncomfortable.
The old floors in the house are so creaky that you can hear every footstep.
We stayed in a creaky cabin that felt like it might collapse in a strong wind.
After a long hike, my creaky knees needed some rest.
He complained that his creaky back made it hard to stand up straight in the morning.
The stairs were creaky, and so were my aging joints.

Synonyms

  • screaky
    6.6 0.00/m
    screaky is high, thin, and often caused by loose parts in movement, while creaky is a low harsh sound from age or stiffness.
  • squeaky
    1.3 2.46/m
    squeaky is high-pitched and shrill like a small mouse or hinge, while creaky is a rough, older sound from stiffness or wear.
