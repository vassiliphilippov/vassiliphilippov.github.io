azeotrope

noun
constant-boiling mixture
9.9
Pronunciation: / eɪˈziːəˌtroʊp / (AmE), / eɪˈziːətrəʊp / (BrE)
a mixture of liquids that retains the same composition when boiled, making it difficult to separate by distillation

Examples

An azeotrope mixture
An azeotrope cannot be separated into its components by simple distillation.
The ethanol-water azeotrope is a common example studied in chemistry classes.
Industrial processes must account for the presence of an azeotrope when purifying certain chemicals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mixture - a combination of two or more substances
  • synonym: solution - a liquid mixture in which the minor component is uniformly distributed
