washout

noun
erosion of soil or gravel by water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːʃˌaʊt /; / ˈwɑʃˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌaʊt / (BrE)
an area where soil or ground has been removed or damaged by water, especially after heavy rain or flooding, often causing damage to roads or railways

Examples

Road washout
After the storm, a huge washout made the road impassable for several days.
Every spring, heavy rains cause at least one washout along the riverbank.
The construction crew had to repair the washout beneath the railway tracks immediately.
Engineers worked through the night to repair the washout before the main road could reopen.
Heavy rains caused a washout that closed the railway line for a week.
The hiking trail was impassable after a washout swept away the bridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erosion - the gradual destruction or removal of soil or rock by natural forces
