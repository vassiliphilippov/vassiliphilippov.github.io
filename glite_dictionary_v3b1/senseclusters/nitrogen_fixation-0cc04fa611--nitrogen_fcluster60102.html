nitrogen fixation

noun
biological conversion of atmospheric nitrogen
1.3 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈnaɪ.trə.dʒən fɪkˈseɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈnaɪ.trə.dʒən fɪkˈseɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which certain soil bacteria convert atmospheric nitrogen into forms that plants can absorb and use

Examples

Nitrogen fixation by bacteria
Legumes play a key role in nitrogen fixation, supplying essential nutrients to the soil.
Some bacteria perform nitrogen fixation, making atmospheric nitrogen accessible to plants.
The process of nitrogen fixation helps maintain soil fertility in natural ecosystems.
