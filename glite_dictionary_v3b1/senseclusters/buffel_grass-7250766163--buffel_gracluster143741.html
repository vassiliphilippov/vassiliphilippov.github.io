buffel grass

noun
perennial pasture grass used for forage
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfəl ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbʌf(ə)l ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
an erect, perennial grass (Cenchrus ciliaris), forming dense tussocks, used as pasture and forage, especially in South Africa and Australia

Examples

Buffel grass pasture
Buffel grass is commonly planted in arid regions to improve grazing for livestock.
Ranchers in Australia rely on buffel grass to feed cattle during dry seasons.
Scientists are concerned that invasive buffel grass could increase the risk of wildfires.
