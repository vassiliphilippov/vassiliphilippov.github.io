good-humoredness

noun
cheerful, obliging disposition
Pronunciation: / ɡʊd ˈhjuːmərdnəs /; / ˌɡʊdˈhjuːmɚdnəs / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈhjuːmədnəs /; / ˌɡʊdˈhjuːmədnəs / (BrE)
a cheerful and pleasant disposition, especially marked by a ready willingness to be agreeable or accommodating

Examples

His good-humoredness shines
Despite the delays, his good-humoredness never seemed to waver.
Her good-humoredness made her a favorite among her colleagues.
I admire your good-humoredness when dealing with difficult customers.
He handled the criticism with remarkable good-humouredness and grace.
Her good-humouredness made even the most difficult days enjoyable for those around her.
The team's good-humouredness helped them overcome the stressful situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: good humor - cheerful disposition and pleasant temperament
  • antonym: ill-humor - a state of being in a bad temper
  • antonym: ill-humouredness - the quality of being irritable or unkind
