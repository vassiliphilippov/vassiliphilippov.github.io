pitiful

adjective
deserving contemptuous pity
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtɪfəl /; / ˈpɪtəfəl / (AmE), / ˈpɪtɪfəl / (BrE)
so bad, inadequate, or miserable as to inspire scornful pity or contempt

Examples

Pitiful excuse
He offered a pitiful excuse for missing the important deadline.
She gave a pitiful cry for help that barely reached the neighbors.
The team's pitiful performance left the fans both disappointed and embarrassed.
After the accident, his excuse was so pitiful that no one believed him.
She looked at the pitiful state of the abandoned house and felt sorry for its past inhabitants.
The team gave a pitiful performance and lost the match by a wide margin.
