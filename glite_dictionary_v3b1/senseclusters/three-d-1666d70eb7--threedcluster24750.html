three-D

noun
three-dimensional movie
3.1 3.5
Pronunciation: /θriː diː/ (AmE), /θriː diː/ (BrE)
a movie, image, or object that gives the illusion of three-dimensional depth (not flat)

Examples

A three-D movie
Many animated films are now released in both standard and three-D formats.
The kids were excited to wear their glasses for the three-D experience at the cinema.
We saw the new superhero movie in three-D and the special effects were amazing.
He prefers video games in three-D to the flat, old-fashioned ones.
The movie was shot entirely in three-D for a striking immersive experience.
With the new technology, children can learn geometry by manipulating objects in three-D.

Related Terms

  • antonym: 2D - two-dimensional; lacking depth
  • synonym: 3D film - a film using techniques to create three-dimensional visual effects
  • synonym: three-dimensional - having or showing three dimensions; not flat
