precut

noun
ready-cut material
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌkʌt / (BrE)
a piece or bundle of material that has been cut in advance to a specific size or shape for a particular use

Examples

A precut fabric
She bought a precut for the quilt and started sewing that afternoon.
The carpenter used a precut to speed up the construction process.
Many craft stores sell precuts in various colors and patterns for easy projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kit - a set of parts or materials prepared for assembly or use
  • antonym: raw material - material in its natural, unprocessed state
