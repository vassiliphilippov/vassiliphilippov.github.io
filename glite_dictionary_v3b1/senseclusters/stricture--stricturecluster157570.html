stricture

noun
severe criticism or censure
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪktʃə / (BrE)
a strong, formal criticism or censure

Examples

Stricture on behavior
He delivered a stricture on the lack of professionalism in the workplace.
Her speech was filled with strictures about modern education.
The report contained several strictures against the government's policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: censure - harsh criticism or disapproval
  • antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
