chloroform

noun
volatile liquid formerly used as anesthetic
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːrəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəˌfɔːm / (BrE)
a volatile, colorless liquid (CHCl₃) formerly used as an anesthetic and solvent

Examples

A bottle of chloroform
In the 19th century, chloroform was commonly used in surgeries, despite its potential risks.
Spills of chloroform must be handled carefully due to its toxic fumes.
The dentist used chloroform to put the patient to sleep during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anesthetic - a substance that induces insensitivity to pain
