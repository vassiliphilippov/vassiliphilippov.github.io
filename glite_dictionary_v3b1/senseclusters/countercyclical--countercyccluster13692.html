countercyclical

adjective
opposing economic cycles
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˈsɪklɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈsɪklɪkəl / (BrE)
moving or acting in the opposite direction to the general economic cycle, especially to reduce economic ups and downs

Examples

Countercyclical policies
The central bank used countercyclical policies to stabilize the economy during the recession.
Many economists recommend countercyclical government spending to offset downturns in the business cycle.
A countercyclical approach can help prevent unemployment from rising too quickly during economic slowdowns.
The government introduced countercyclical policies to help stabilize the economy during the downturn.
Many economists support countercyclical spending to reduce the impact of recessions.
A countercyclical approach can help prevent economic booms from turning into bubbles.
Government spending can have a countercyclical effect by boosting the economy during a recession.
Some investors prefer countercyclical stocks because they tend to rise when the market falls.
The central bank adopted a countercyclical policy to stabilize economic growth during downturns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defensive - remaining stable or performing well during economic downturns
  • antonym: procyclical - moving in the same direction as the business cycle
