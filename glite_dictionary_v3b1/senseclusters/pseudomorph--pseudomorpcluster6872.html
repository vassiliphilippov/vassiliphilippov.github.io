pseudomorph

noun
mineral with false form
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːdoʊˌmɔːrf / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəʊˌmɔːf / (BrE)
a mineral that has the external shape of another mineral due to replacement or alteration

Examples

Mineral pseudomorph
The museum displayed a rare pseudomorph that looked like quartz but was actually replaced by another mineral.
Geologists study pseudomorphs to understand the processes of mineral replacement in rocks.
A pseudomorph can sometimes be mistaken for the original mineral because of its shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystal - a solid material with a regular, repeating structure
  • synonym: mineral - a naturally occurring solid substance with a definite chemical composition
