emittance

noun
measure of radiation emission
8.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmɪt.əns/ /; / /ə.ˈmɪt.əns/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmɪt.əns/ /; / /ə.ˈmɪt.əns/ (BrE)
the act of emitting energy or substance, or a measure of how effectively something emits energy as radiation

Examples

Thermal emittance
The emittance of the coating determines how much heat it radiates into the environment.
Scientists measured the emittance of different materials to find the most efficient insulator.
A low emittance value means the surface does not emit much thermal energy.
The emittance of infrared radiation from the surface was measured using specialized equipment.
Scientists studied the emittance of particles from the new material under different temperatures.
Regulations now limit the emittance of harmful gases from industrial plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorbance - the ability of a material to absorb energy
  • antonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up energy or matter
  • synonym: emission - the act of sending out light, heat, gas, etc.
  • synonym: emissivity - the efficiency with which a surface emits thermal radiation
