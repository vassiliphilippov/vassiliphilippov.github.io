bipartizan

adjective
supported by both parties
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪˈpɑrtəzn̩ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈpɑːtɪzən / (BrE)
Supported or agreed upon by two opposing parties, especially referring to political parties.

Examples

A bipartizan agreement
A bipartizan committee was formed to address the state's infrastructure needs.
Despite their differences, the two leaders announced a bipartizan agreement on the budget.
The new law received bipartizan support in Congress, making its passage almost certain.
