Synonyms

  • thyroid-stimulating hormone
    5.1 0.02/m
    thyroid-stimulating hormone is the standard medical name for the pituitary hormone that makes the thyroid produce hormones, while thyrotropin is another name.
  • TSH
    7.1 0.20/m
    TSH is the short clinical abbreviation used in tests and reports for this pituitary hormone, while thyrotropin is the full formal name used in texts.
  • thyrotropic hormone
    7.5 0.00/m
    thyrotropic hormone names the pituitary substance that activates the thyroid, while thyrotropin is the established scientific name for the same hormone.
  • thyrotrophic hormone
    8.6 0.00/m
    thyrotrophic hormone is an alternate technical term seen in some writings, while thyrotropin is the common scientific name in endocrinology.
