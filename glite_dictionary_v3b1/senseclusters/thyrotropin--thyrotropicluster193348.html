thyrotropin

noun
hormone stimulating the thyroid gland
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪrəˈtroʊpɪn /; / ˌθaɪrəˈtroʊfɪn / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərəˈtrəʊpɪn /; / ˌθaɪrəʊˈtrɒfɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce hormones

Examples

Thyrotropin hormone level
An increase in thyrotropin can indicate hypothyroidism.
Disorders of the pituitary gland can alter thyrotropin production.
The doctor ordered a blood test to measure her thyrotropin levels.
A deficiency of thyrotrophin can lead to an underactive thyroid gland.
Doctors measured his thyrotrophin levels to assess his thyroid function.
The release of thyrotrophin is regulated by signals from the hypothalamus.
