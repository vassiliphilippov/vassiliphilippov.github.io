cognatic

adjective
tracing descent both sides
9.3
Pronunciation: / kɑgˈnætɪk / (AmE), / kɒgˈnætɪk / (BrE)
relating to descent or inheritance traced through both the mother's and father's line

Examples

A cognatic system
In anthropology, a cognatic system recognizes family ties from both parents.
The tribe follows a cognatic pattern of inheritance, allowing property to pass through either parent.
Some cultures have cognatic clans that include relatives from both the mother's and father's families.
