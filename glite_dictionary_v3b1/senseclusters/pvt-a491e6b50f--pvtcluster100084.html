PVT

noun
pressure–volume–temperature data
8.1
Pronunciation: / pi vi ti / (AmE), / piː viː tiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'pressure–volume–temperature', referring to the relationship or data showing how these three properties of a substance interact, especially in engineering and thermodynamics

Examples

PVT data analysis
Engineers use PVT data to design equipment for processing natural gas.
The PVT properties of a fluid are essential for understanding its behavior under different conditions.
Accurate PVT analysis helps predict how oil and gas will perform in a reservoir.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equation of state - a mathematical equation describing the relationship between pressure, volume, and temperature of a substance
  • synonym: thermodynamics - the science of the relations between heat, work, temperature, and energy
