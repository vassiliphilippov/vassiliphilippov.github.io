thudding

adjective
dull, muffled sound quality
Pronunciation: / ˈθʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a sound) dull and muffled, as if produced by striking something relatively soft; not clear or resonant.

Examples

The thudding footsteps
He heard the thudding footsteps approaching through the darkness.
Rain drummed a thudding rhythm against the window all night long.
The thudding beat of the music could be felt through the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thud - a dull, heavy sound made by an object falling or hitting something
