cardiogram

noun
heart activity recording
2.9 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrdiəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdiəˌɡræm / (BrE)
A graphical recording of the electrical activity of the heart, typically produced by an electrocardiograph.

Examples

A cardiogram result
She was nervous while waiting for the results of her cardiogram.
The doctor examined the cardiogram to check for any abnormalities in the patient's heartbeat.
The technician attached the electrodes and recorded a detailed cardiogram during the test.

Related Terms
