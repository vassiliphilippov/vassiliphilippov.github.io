hold-up

noun
robbery or unexpected delay
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊld.ʌp/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊld.ʌp/ (BrE)
a robbery committed with threats or violence, especially armed; or an unexpected delay or interruption

Examples

A hold-up at security
Our flight was late due to a hold-up at security.
The city experienced a hold-up at the jewelry store yesterday afternoon.
There was a hold-up on the bridge that caused a major traffic jam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delay - a period of waiting or being held back, causing something to be late
  • synonym: robbery - the action of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
