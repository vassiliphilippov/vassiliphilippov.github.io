biodefense

noun
protection against biological attacks
1.7 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊ.dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊ.dɪˈfɛns /; / ˌbʌɪ.əʊ.dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
measures and procedures designed to protect people and environments from attacks involving biological agents or diseases (for example viruses or bacteria used as weapons), especially in the context of warfare or terrorism

Examples

Effective biodefense measures
Effective biodefense requires coordination between public health agencies and military units.
Researchers are working on advanced biodefense strategies to detect and neutralize biological threats.
The government increased its investment in biodefense after reports of new infectious agents.
Developing new vaccines is an important part of modern biodefence strategies.
Many hospitals follow strict biodefence protocols in case of an outbreak of a dangerous pathogen.
The government is increasing funding for biodefence research to prepare for potential biological threats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biological warfare - the use of biological toxins or infectious agents as weapons
  • synonym: biosecurity - measures to protect against harmful biological agents
