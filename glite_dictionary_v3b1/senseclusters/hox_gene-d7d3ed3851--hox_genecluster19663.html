Hox gene

noun
developmental control gene
Pronunciation: / hɑks dʒin / (AmE), / hɒks dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that controls the body plan and development of an animal by regulating the formation of body structures

Examples

The Hox gene controls body
The Hox gene plays a crucial role in determining where limbs develop on an animal's body.
Researchers discovered a mutation in a Hox gene that caused unusual body patterns in fruit flies.
Understanding the function of each Hox gene helps scientists learn how complex organisms are formed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeobox gene - a gene containing a specific DNA sequence that regulates development
  • synonym: regulatory gene - a gene that controls the expression of other genes
