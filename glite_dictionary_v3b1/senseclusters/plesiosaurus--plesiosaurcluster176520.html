plesiosaurus

noun
extinct marine reptile with a long neck
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpliːsiəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌpliːsɪəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
an extinct marine reptile of the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, with a small head, long neck, short tail, and four paddle-like limbs

Examples

A plesiosaurus skeleton
Children at the museum were fascinated by the skeleton of a plesiosaurus.
Scientists believe the plesiosaurus used its long neck to hunt for prey in the water.
The fossilized remains of a plesiosaurus were discovered near the coast.
