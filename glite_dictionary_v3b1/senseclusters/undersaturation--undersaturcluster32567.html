undersaturation

noun
below saturation level
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a state in which a gas or solution contains less of a substance than it can hold at a given temperature, pressure, or equilibrium

Examples

Undersaturation of oxygen
The scientist measured the undersaturation of oxygen in the lake water after the storm.
Persistent undersaturation in the air can lead to faster evaporation of surface water.
Engineers monitor undersaturation levels to prevent corrosion in industrial systems.
The scientist measured the undersaturation of carbon dioxide in the lake water.
Low levels of dissolved oxygen indicated undersaturation in the aquarium.
During the experiment, the solution remained in a state of undersaturation until more salt was added.

Related Terms

  • antonym: saturation - the state of being fully saturated or holding as much as possible
  • degree higher: supersaturation - the state of containing more of a substance than can normally be held at a given temperature or pressure
