long shot

noun
unlikely venture with possible big reward
1.9
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
an attempt or chance that is unlikely to succeed but is worth trying

Examples

Take a long shot
Applying for the job felt like a long shot, yet she decided it was worth a try.
Investing in that startup is a long shot, but the potential returns are huge.
It might seem like a long shot, but winning the competition is still possible.
Getting a promotion this year is a long shot, but I'm still going to try.
She thought applying for the scholarship was a long shot, yet she was accepted.
They know it's a long shot, but they're hoping to raise enough money for the project.
