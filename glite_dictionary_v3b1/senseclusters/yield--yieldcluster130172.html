yield

verb
give way under pressure
4.3 7/m
Pronunciation: / jiÀêld / (AmE), / jiÀêld / (BrE)
to give way, surrender, or submit to pressure, force, or demands; to stop resisting

Examples

To yield to pressure
After hours of pounding, the heavy door finally yielded to the rescuers.
If the material yields under stress, it might not be strong enough for construction.
The rusty lid yielded when he applied more pressure with the crowbar.
After several hours of negotiation, the rebels finally yielded to government forces.
She refused to yield in the face of criticism, but eventually gave in.
The protestors did not yield until their demands were acknowledged by the mayor.
The metal rod will yield slightly before it breaks under pressure.
This plastic can yield to a certain extent without cracking.
Unlike steel, glass does not yield when a force is applied.
