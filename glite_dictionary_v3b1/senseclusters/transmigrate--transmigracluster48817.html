transmigrate

verb
move across a tissue or vessel barrier
Pronunciation: / trænzˈmaɪɡreɪt / (AmE), / trænzˈmaɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
(in biology/medicine) to move from one side of a tissue or vessel wall to the other; to cross a biological barrier (e.g., leukocytes crossing the endothelium).

Examples

Cells transmigrate barriers
During inflammation, leukocytes transmigrate across the endothelium into the affected tissue.
Researchers transmigrated labeled T cells across the blood–brain barrier in the mouse model.
Endothelial signalling controls whether monocytes are transmigrating through vessel walls during infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diapedesis - the passage of blood cells through the intact walls of the capillaries, typically during inflammation
  • synonym: extravasate - to leak or move out from a vessel into surrounding tissue; often used for fluid or cells
  • synonym: migrate - to move from one place to another; in biology, to move from one location to another within an organism or between organisms
