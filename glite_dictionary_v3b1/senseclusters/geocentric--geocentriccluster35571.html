geocentric

adjective
having the earth as the center
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiː.oʊˈsɛn.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː.əʊˈsɛn.trɪk / (BrE)
having or representing the earth as the center, especially as formerly held in astronomy.

Examples

A geocentric model
Modern science has replaced geocentric theories with heliocentric ones.
Some ancient philosophers argued for a geocentric view of the cosmos.
The geocentric model of the universe was widely accepted before the Renaissance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heliocentric - having the sun as the center
