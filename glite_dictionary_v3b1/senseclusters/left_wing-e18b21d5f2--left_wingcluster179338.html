left wing

noun
progressive or reformist political group
Pronunciation: / lɛft wɪŋ / (AmE), / lɛft wɪŋ / (BrE)
The section of a political group or society that favors progressive, reformist, or radical policies, especially those promoting social equality and welfare.

Examples

The left wing party
During the debate, the left wing argued for universal healthcare and climate action.
Many young voters identify with the left wing because of its focus on progressive policies.
The politician's strong left wing views attracted both support and criticism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liberal - open to new behavior or opinions and willing to discard traditional values (in politics, often left-leaning)
  • antonym: right wing - the section of a political group or society that favors conservative or traditional views and policies
