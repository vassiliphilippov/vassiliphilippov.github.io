ecdysis

noun
shedding of outer skin by animals
9.8 9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.dɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈek.dɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
the process of shedding the old outer skin or cuticle, especially by arthropods or reptiles, usually to allow for growth

Examples

Ecdysis process
Disorders interfering with ecdysis can cause health issues in reptiles kept in captivity.
Molting, or ecdysis, is crucial for an insect’s development to its next life stage.
The snake undergoes ecdysis several times a year as it grows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: molt - (in animals) to shed old feathers, hair, or skin to make way for new growth
