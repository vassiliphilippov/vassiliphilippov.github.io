power play

noun
ice hockey: team has advantage due to penalties
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.ɚ pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə pleɪ / (BrE)
In ice hockey, a situation in which one team has a numerical advantage over the other due to penalties, allowing it to play with more players on the ice.

Examples

Hockey power play
A well-executed power play can change the course of a hockey game.
He was sent to the penalty box, giving the opponents a crucial power play opportunity.
The team failed to score during their power play in the second period.
