theocracy

noun
government by religious authority
7.7 1.97/m
Pronunciation: / Œ∏iÀà…ëkr…ôsi /; / Œ∏iÀà…ëÀêkr…ôsi / (AmE), / Œ∏iÀà…íkr…ôsi / (BrE)
a system of government in which religious leaders rule in the name of a god or by claiming divine guidance

Examples

A theocracy government
Historically, many ancient societies functioned as theocracies, merging religion with governance.
In a theocracy, religious leaders may hold both spiritual and political power.
Iran is often cited as a modern example of a theocracy, with its rulers claiming authority from divine law.
Debates about the separation of church and state frequently mention historical theocracy as a cautionary example.
Iran is often cited as a modern example of a theocracy in which religious leaders hold political authority.
Under the theocracy, all laws were based on strict interpretations of religious texts.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.00/m

  • ecclesiarchy
    9.9 0.00/m
    ecclesiarchy is government run by the church and its officials, while theocracy stresses leaders ruling in the name of a god.
