snake dance

noun
ceremonial dance involving snakes
Pronunciation: / sneɪk dæns / (AmE), / sneɪk dɑːns / (BrE)
a ceremonial dance, especially among some Indigenous peoples such as the Hopi, in which snakes are handled or invoked as part of ritual practices

Examples

A snake dance ritual
Anthropologists have studied the symbolism behind the snake dance rituals in southwestern Native American cultures.
The Hopi people are well known for their annual snake dance, which is both spiritual and ceremonial.
Visitors watched in awe as participants performed the traditional snake dance with live rattlesnakes.
