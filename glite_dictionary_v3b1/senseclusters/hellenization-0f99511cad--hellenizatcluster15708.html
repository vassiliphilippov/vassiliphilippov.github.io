Hellenization

noun
spread of Greek culture
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛlənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which Greek culture, language, or customs are spread, adopted, or adapted by other people or regions

Examples

Many cities in the Near East experienced rapid Hellenization after Alexander's campaigns.
Art and architecture from this era show clear signs of Hellenization throughout the region.
