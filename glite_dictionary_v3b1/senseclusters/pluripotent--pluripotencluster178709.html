pluripotent

adjective
can become many cell types
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌplʊrɪˈpoʊtənt / (AmE), / ˌplʊərɪˈpəʊtənt / (BrE)
able to develop into many different types of cells or tissues in the body

Examples

Pluripotent stem cells
Researchers are studying pluripotent stem cells to find new treatments for various diseases.
A pluripotent cell can become muscle, nerve, or blood cells depending on the signals it receives.
The discovery of pluripotent cells was a major breakthrough in regenerative medicine.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: multipotent - able to develop into several, but not all, cell types
  • degree higher: totipotent - able to develop into any cell type, including a whole organism
