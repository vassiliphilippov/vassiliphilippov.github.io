blamable

adjective
deserving blame or censure
Pronunciation: / ˈbleɪməbəl / (AmE), / ˈbleɪməbəl / (BrE)
Deserving blame or censure; worthy of being blamed for something wrong, evil, or injurious.

Examples

Blamable conduct
Her blamable conduct caused significant harm to the organization.
The committee found his actions to be blamable and recommended disciplinary measures.
Though his intentions were good, the outcome was still blamable in the eyes of the law.

  • synonym: culpable - deserving blame for a wrong or harmful act
  • antonym: innocent - not deserving blame; without guilt
