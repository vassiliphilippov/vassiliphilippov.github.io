Deserving blame or censure; worthy of being blamed for something wrong, evil, or injurious.

Examples

Blamable conduct
Her blamable conduct caused significant harm to the organization.
The committee found his actions to be blamable and recommended disciplinary measures.
Though his intentions were good, the outcome was still blamable in the eyes of the law.

Synonyms

  • blameworthy
    6.5 0.20/m
    blameworthy means deserving clear fault or disapproval for wrong actions, while blamable means worthy of blame in general.
  • blameful
    7.9 0.01/m
    blameful is often used to say someone deserves disapproval for harm, while blamable is a general word for being open to blame.
  • culpable
    7.5 1.18/m
    culpable often points to legal or clear responsibility for a fault, while blamable covers blame in a wider, less precise sense.
  • censurable
    8.9 0.01/m
    censurable suggests formal criticism or censure by others in public or writing, while blamable simply means deserving blame in general.
  • reprehensible
    7.9 2.35/m
    reprehensible describes very bad actions that deserve strong moral blame and shock, while blamable covers ordinary blameworthy acts.

  • antonym: innocent - not deserving blame; without guilt
