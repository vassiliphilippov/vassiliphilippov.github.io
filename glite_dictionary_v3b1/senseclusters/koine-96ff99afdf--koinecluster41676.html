Koine

noun
common Greek dialect under Roman Empire
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɔɪˈneɪ / (AmE), / kɔɪˈniː / (BrE)
the common Greek dialect that developed and was widely used throughout the eastern Mediterranean and the Near East from about 300 BC to AD 300, especially under the Roman Empire.

Examples

Koine Greek
Many important early Christian texts were originally written in Koine Greek.
Scholars study Koine to better understand the language of ancient manuscripts.
The influence of Koine can be seen in later forms of the Greek language.
