suprascapular

noun
shoulder nerve
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːprəˈskæpjələr / (AmE), / ˌsuːprəˈskæpjʊlə / (BrE)
a nerve that passes above the scapula (shoulder blade) and supplies certain muscles of the shoulder

Examples

Suprascapular nerve
Injury to the suprascapular can lead to weakness in shoulder movement.
The doctor explained that the suprascapular is important for lifting the arm.
Physical therapy helped restore function after damage to the suprascapular.
