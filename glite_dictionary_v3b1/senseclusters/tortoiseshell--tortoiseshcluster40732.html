tortoiseshell

noun
mottled material from some turtle shells
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔrtəsˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtəsʃɛl / (BrE)
the mottled, yellow-and-brown, hard substance forming the shell of certain turtles, formerly used for decorative objects and jewelry.

Examples

Tortoiseshell comb
The comb was made of polished tortoiseshell and glimmered in the sunlight.
Trade in authentic tortoiseshell is now banned to protect endangered turtle species.
Victorian craftsmen often used tortoiseshell to create inlaid jewelry boxes and hairpins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mother-of-pearl - the iridescent inner shell layer of some mollusks, used decoratively
