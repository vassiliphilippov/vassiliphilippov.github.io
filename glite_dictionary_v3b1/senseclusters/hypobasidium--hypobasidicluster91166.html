hypobasidium

noun
cell at base of basidium in fungi
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊbəˈsɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊbəˈsɪdɪəm / (BrE)
a specialized cell forming the base of the basidium in certain fungi, particularly in the order Tremellales

Examples

The hypobasidium cell
Researchers identified the hypobasidium as the origin of the basidiospores in the Tremellales species.
The hypobasidium divides to produce chains of spores in some jelly fungi.
Under the microscope, the distinctive structure of the hypobasidium became clear.
