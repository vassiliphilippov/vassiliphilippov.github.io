Ironsides

proper noun
nickname for the strong
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪərnˌsaɪdz / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌsaɪdz / (BrE)
a nickname for a person or thing known for great strength or resilience, especially used for Oliver Cromwell or strong warships

Examples

Nicknamed Ironsides for strength
Oliver Cromwell earned the nickname Ironsides for his leadership and toughness in battle.
The old warship, known as Ironsides, survived many fierce naval battles.
He was called Ironsides by his friends because nothing seemed to defeat him.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stalwart - a person who is strong and dependable
  • antonym: weakling - a person lacking physical or moral strength
