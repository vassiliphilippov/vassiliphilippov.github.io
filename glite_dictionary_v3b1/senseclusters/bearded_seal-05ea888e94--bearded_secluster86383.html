bearded seal

noun
Arctic seal with whiskered muzzle
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪrdɪd sil / (AmE), / ˈbɪədɪd siːl / (BrE)
a medium-sized Arctic seal (Erignathus barbatus) with long bristles on each side of its muzzle, found in the Arctic Ocean

Examples

A bearded seal on ice
A bearded seal hauled itself out onto the ice to rest in the sunlight.
Researchers tracked several bearded seals during their migration across the Arctic Ocean.
The bearded seal is known for its long, white whiskers that help it sense prey under the ice.
