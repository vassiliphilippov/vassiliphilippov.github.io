zipline

verb
to ride on a cable
4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈzɪ.plaɪn/ (AmE), /ˈzɪ.plaɪn/ (BrE)
to travel quickly along a suspended cable, usually using a harness and pulley for recreation or transport

Examples

To zipline over trees
We decided to zipline over the forest canopy during our vacation in Costa Rica.
She had never ziplined before, but she loved the thrill of flying above the river.
Many adventure parks offer the chance to zipline between tall platforms.

  • synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously through the air
