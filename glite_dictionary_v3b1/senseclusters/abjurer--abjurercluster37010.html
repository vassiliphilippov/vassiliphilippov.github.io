abjurer

noun
one who formally renounces a belief or claim
9.9
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒʊrər / (AmE), / əbˈdʒʊərə / (BrE)
a person who renounces or formally rejects a belief, cause, or claim, especially under oath

Examples

An abjurer of faith
As an abjurer, she was required to sign a declaration of her changed beliefs.
In medieval times, an abjurer often faced public scrutiny for renouncing their faith.
The former leader became an abjurer of his party’s ideology after the scandal.

Related Terms
