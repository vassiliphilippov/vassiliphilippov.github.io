cryosphere

noun
Earth's frozen regions
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkraɪ.oʊˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.əʊˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the parts of a planet (especially Earth) where water or other substances exist in solid, frozen form (such as ice, snow, or frozen ground)

Examples

The cryosphere regions
Scientists study the cryosphere to understand how climate change affects polar regions.
Melting in the cryosphere can lead to rising sea levels worldwide.
The cryosphere includes glaciers, sea ice, and permafrost found in cold regions.
Climate change is causing the cryosphere to shrink in many polar regions.
Scientists study the cryosphere to understand how melting ice affects sea levels.
Satellites help monitor changes in the Earth's cryosphere over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atmosphere - the layer of gases surrounding a planet
  • synonym: hydrosphere - all the water on Earth's surface, including lakes, seas, and sometimes clouds
  • antonym: lithosphere - the rigid outer layer of the Earth, consisting of the crust and upper mantle
