handicapping

noun
placing at disadvantage
Pronunciation: / ˈhændiˌkæpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændikæpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of placing someone or something at a disadvantage, especially by applying rules or conditions that make success more difficult

Examples

Handicapping small businesses
The new regulations led to the handicapping of smaller businesses in the market.
Some sports use handicapping to level the playing field between competitors of different abilities.
Critics argued that the policy resulted in the unfair handicapping of certain groups.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disadvantaging - the act of making someone less likely to succeed
  • antonym: favoring - the act of giving an advantage to someone
