hypostasis

noun
accumulation of blood in an organ or area
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑstəsɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒstəsɪs / (BrE)
an accumulation of blood in an organ or body part, usually after death, causing discoloration of the tissue

Examples

Postmortem hypostasis
After death, hypostasis can cause a purplish discoloration in the dependent parts of the body.
Pathologists often examine areas of hypostasis to estimate the time of death.
The visible hypostasis confirmed the body's position had not changed after death.
