goutweed

noun
perennial weed plant
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaʊtˌwiːd /; / ˈɡaʊtˌwid / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊtˌwiːd /; / ˈɡaʊtwiːd / (BrE)
a perennial plant with umbrella-shaped clusters of small white flowers, often grown as ground cover or as an ornamental in gardens

Examples

Garden goutweed
The goutweed spread quickly and covered most of the flower beds by summer.
Gardeners often struggle to remove goutweed because of its persistent roots.
Some people plant goutweed as ground cover, but it can easily become invasive.
Gardeners often use goutweed to cover large shady areas where grass will not grow.
The variegated leaves of goutweed add brightness to the garden's borders.
Once established, goutweed can spread quickly and may be difficult to remove.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bishop's weed - another name for goutweed, a groundcover plant
  • synonym: ground elder - a perennial plant with white flowers, also known as goutweed
  • synonym: weed - an unwanted plant growing where it is not wanted
