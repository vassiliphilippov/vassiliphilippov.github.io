price

noun
amount of money to buy something
1.0 222/m
Pronunciation: / praÉªs / (AmE), / praÉªs / (BrE)
the amount of money that something costs or is worth; the money you must pay to buy something

Examples

Price tag
She asked the price of the beautiful vase in the shop window.
Some stores offer a discount if you buy more than one item at full price.
The price of bread has risen steadily over the past year.
He was surprised by the high price of the concert ticket.
The price of oil has increased significantly this year.
They reduced the price on all their summer clothing.

Synonyms

  • cost
    1.0 225/m
    cost is the money needed to produce or buy something, while price is the amount a buyer pays to purchase it.
  • monetary value
    3.3 0.71/m
    monetary value is the estimated money someone thinks an item is worth, while price is the actual amount set for sale.
  • worth
    1.4 31/m
    worth often means the value something has or could bring, while price is the concrete amount asked in a sale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charge - the amount asked for goods or services
  • synonym: cost - the amount that must be paid or spent to buy or obtain something
  • degree higher: value - the worth of something compared to its price or cost
