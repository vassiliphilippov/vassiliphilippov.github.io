excise tax

noun

A tax imposed on certain goods, services, or activities, measured by the quantity produced, sold, or consumed rather than on property or income.

Examples

The excise tax rate
Changes in the excise tax rate affected the prices of alcoholic beverages.
Importers must pay an excise tax on certain luxury vehicles.
The government imposed an excise tax on tobacco products to discourage smoking.
