lipsticked

adjective
superficially improved
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪpstɪkt / (AmE), / ˈlɪpstɪkt / (BrE)
made to look superficially attractive by minor cosmetic changes, without addressing underlying problems

Examples

Lipsticked building
The old building was simply lipsticked with a fresh coat of paint, but the plumbing was still broken.
Critics argued that the reforms were just lipsticked changes that did not solve the real issues.
The website was lipsticked to attract new users, but its functionality remained outdated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cosmetic - intended to improve appearance only
  • antonym: substantial - important or fundamental, not just on the surface
  • synonym: superficial - concerned only with the surface or appearance
