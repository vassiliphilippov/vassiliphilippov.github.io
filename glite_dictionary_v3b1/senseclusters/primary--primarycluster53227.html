primary

verb
to challenge in primary
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈpraɪməri / (BrE)
to challenge an incumbent politician in a primary election, especially by a member of the same party

Examples

To primary a senator
The congressman was primaried by a challenger from his own party.
Several incumbents fear they will be primaried if they do not support the new policy.
After being primaried last year, she decided not to run for re-election.

Related Terms

  • antonym: support - to back or endorse a candidate
  • synonym: unseat - to remove someone from a position, especially in politics
