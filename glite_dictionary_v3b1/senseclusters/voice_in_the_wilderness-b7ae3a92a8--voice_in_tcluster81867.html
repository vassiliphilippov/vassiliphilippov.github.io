voice in the wilderness

noun
an unheeded suggestion or warning
6.7
Pronunciation: / vɔɪs ɪn ðə ˈwɪldərnəs / (AmE), / vɔɪs ɪn ðə ˈwɪldənəs /; / vɔɪs ɪn ðə ˈwɪldəˌnɪs / (BrE)
a person or message whose warning or opinion is ignored or not accepted by others

Examples

A voice in the wilderness
In the early stages of the debate, his proposal was dismissed as a voice in the wilderness.
She felt like a voice in the wilderness when no one took her environmental concerns seriously.
The scientist became a voice in the wilderness warning of the potential dangers of the new technology.
For years, his warnings about financial risk made him a voice in the wilderness.
Many innovators have been a voice in the wilderness before their ideas gained acceptance.
She was a voice in the wilderness when she spoke out against pollution at the time.
