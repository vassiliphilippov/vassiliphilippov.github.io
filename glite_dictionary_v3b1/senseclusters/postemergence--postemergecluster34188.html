postemergence

noun
after plant emergence
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstɪˈmɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstɪˈmɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the period or stage after a plant has emerged from the soil, especially in crop growth or weed control

Examples

Postemergence stage
Farmers often use postemergence herbicides to control weeds after crops have sprouted.
The postemergence stage is critical for monitoring plant health and growth.
Applying fertilizer during the postemergence period can help boost crop yields.

Related Terms

  • antonym: preemergence - the period before a plant emerges from the soil
