Saturn

proper noun
Roman god of agriculture
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsætərn / (AmE), / ˈsætən / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of agriculture and vegetation, equivalent to the Greek Cronus.

Examples

Saturn in mythology
In Roman mythology, Saturn was overthrown by his son Jupiter.
Many ancient temples in Rome were dedicated to Saturn.
The festival of Saturnalia was held in honor of Saturn each winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cronus - Greek god of time and harvest; equivalent to Saturn
