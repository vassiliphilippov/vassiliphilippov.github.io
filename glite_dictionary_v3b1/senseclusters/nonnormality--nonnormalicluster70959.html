nonnormality

noun
failure of normality
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌnɔrˈmæləti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌnɔːˈmæləti / (BrE)
the state or condition of not satisfying the technical property of normality, especially in mathematics or related fields

Examples

Nonnormality in mathematics
The mathematician discussed the nonnormality of the group in her lecture.
Detecting nonnormality in the data set was crucial for choosing the right statistical test.
The proof relies on the nonnormality of the topological space to demonstrate the result.

  • antonym: normality - the state of satisfying the property of being normal
