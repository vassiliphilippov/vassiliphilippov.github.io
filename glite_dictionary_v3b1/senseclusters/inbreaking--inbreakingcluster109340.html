inbreaking

noun
making new items usable
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌbreɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbreɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making something new, such as shoes or equipment, comfortable or usable by wearing or using it repeatedly

Examples

Inbreaking of new boots
The inbreaking of her new boots lasted several days until they felt comfortable.
Proper inbreaking of sports equipment can help prevent injuries and improve performance.
He spent the weekend focused on the inbreaking of his new leather gloves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breaking in - the act of making new items comfortable through use
  • synonym: conditioning - the process of training or accustoming someone or something
