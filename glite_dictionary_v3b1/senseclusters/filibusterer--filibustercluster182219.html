filibusterer

noun
legislator who obstructs by long speeches
9.9 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪl.ə.bʌ.stər.ər/ /; / /ˈfɪl.ə.bʌ.stər/ (AmE), /ˈfɪl.ə.bʌ.stə/ /; / /ˈfɪl.ə.bʌs.tə/ (BrE)
a legislator who gives prolonged speeches in order to delay or obstruct the passage of legislation they oppose

Examples

A filibusterer delays votes
As a skilled filibusterer, he used lengthy speeches to delay the vote.
Critics accused the filibusterer of undermining the democratic process in parliament.
The senator became known as a filibusterer who regularly blocked important bills.
