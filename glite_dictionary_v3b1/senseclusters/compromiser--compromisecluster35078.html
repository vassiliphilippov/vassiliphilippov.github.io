compromiser

noun
person who seeks compromise
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːm.prəˌmaɪ.zər / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.prəˌmaɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who is willing to make concessions or reach agreements with others, especially in a negotiation

Examples

A compromiser in talks
In political discussions, being a compromiser is sometimes seen as a strength.
She was known as a skilled compromiser who could bring both sides to the table.
The company’s success was partly due to its CEO, a natural compromiser.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hardliner - a person who adheres strictly to a set of ideas or policies, opposite of compromiser
  • synonym: mediator - a person who tries to help other people come to an agreement
