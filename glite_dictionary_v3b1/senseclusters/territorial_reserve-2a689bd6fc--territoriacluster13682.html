territorial reserve

noun
reserve military unit for home defense
Pronunciation: / tɛˌrɪtɔriəl rɪˈzɜrv / (AmE), / tɛˌrɪtəriəl rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
a reserve military unit composed of territorial forces organized within a country, typically for home defense or support.

Examples

The territorial reserve unit
He served for several years in the territorial reserve before returning to civilian life.
Many citizens volunteered for the territorial reserve during times of heightened security concerns.
The government called up the territorial reserve in response to the national emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: militia - a military force that is raised from the civil population
  • antonym: regular army - the professional, permanent army of a nation
