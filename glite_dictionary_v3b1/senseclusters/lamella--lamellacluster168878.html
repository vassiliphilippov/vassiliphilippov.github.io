lamella

noun
thin layer in bone tissue
Pronunciation: / ləˈmɛlə / (AmE), / ləˈmɛlə /; / ləˈmiːlə / (BrE)
a thin layer, plate, or membrane, especially in biological or physical contexts (such as bone, leaves, or tissues)

Examples

Bone lamella
Each osteon in bone tissue contains several concentric lamellae.
Researchers studied the arrangement of lamellae under the microscope.
The lamellae add strength and resilience to the bone's structure.
A microscope revealed that the leaf's surface was covered with a delicate lamella.
The bone is made up of many lamellae arranged in concentric circles.
When the material cools, a lamella of crystals can form within its structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: layer - a single thickness of a material covering a surface or forming part of something
