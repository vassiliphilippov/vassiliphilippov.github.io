idiocracy

noun
rule by the foolish
Pronunciation: / ˌɪdiˈɑkrəsi / (AmE), / ˌɪdiˈɒkrəsi / (BrE)
a society or government in which foolishness, incompetence, or lack of intelligence is widespread or dominant

Examples

An idiocracy society
Some people fear that an idiocracy could develop if education is neglected.
The film satirizes a future idiocracy where intelligence is undervalued.
Critics argue that recent decisions reflect a growing idiocracy in government.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kakistocracy - a government run by the least qualified or most unprincipled citizens
  • antonym: meritocracy - a society or system in which people are chosen and moved into positions of success based on their ability
