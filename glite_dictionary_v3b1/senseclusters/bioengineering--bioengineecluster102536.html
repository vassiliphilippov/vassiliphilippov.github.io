bioengineering

noun
application of biological science in engineering
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the application of biological science and engineering principles to design and create products, systems, or environments, especially relating to living organisms, including humans

Examples

Bioengineering artificial organs
Recent innovations in bioengineering have led to the creation of artificial organs.
She decided to specialize in bioengineering to help develop advanced medical devices.
The university offers a degree program in bioengineering focusing on both biology and engineering disciplines.
