horizontal bar

noun
gymnastic apparatus; single horizontal bar
Pronunciation: / hɔːrˈɪz.ən.tl̩ bɑːr / (AmE), / ˌhɒr.ɪˈzɒn.tl̩ bɑː / (BrE)
a gymnastic apparatus consisting of a single bar fixed horizontally at a certain height, supported by uprights at both ends, used for exercises and routines in gymnastics, particularly in men's events

Examples

The horizontal bar exercise
Mastering the horizontal bar requires strength, precision, and years of practice.
The gymnast performed a flawless routine on the horizontal bar during the competition.
The new gym has a state-of-the-art horizontal bar for training advanced skills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uneven bars - a gymnastic apparatus consisting of two parallel bars set at different heights, used in women's gymnastics
