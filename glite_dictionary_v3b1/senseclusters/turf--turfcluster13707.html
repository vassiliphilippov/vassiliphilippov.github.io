turf

verb
to remove forcefully
Pronunciation: / tɝːf / (AmE), / tɜːf / (BrE)
to remove someone from a place or position, often suddenly or forcefully

Examples

To turf someone out
After the argument, he was turfed out of the meeting by security.
The landlord turfed the tenants for not paying their rent on time.
She was turfed from the team after missing several practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - to allow someone to enter a place
  • synonym: eject - to force someone to leave a place
  • synonym: expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
