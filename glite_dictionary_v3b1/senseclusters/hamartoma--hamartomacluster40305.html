hamartoma

noun
benign tissue growth due to faulty development
9.6 9.4
Pronunciation: /hæ.mər.ˈtoʊ.mə/ /; / /hæ.mɑːr.ˈtoʊ.mə/ (AmE), /hæ.mə.ˈtəʊ.mə/ /; / /hæ.mɑː.ˈtəʊ.mə/ (BrE)
a benign, tumor-like growth composed of an abnormal mixture of cells and tissues normally found in the area of the body where it occurs, resulting from faulty development rather than true neoplastic processes

Examples

A hamartoma growth
Some genetic conditions increase the risk of developing multiple hamartomas throughout the body.
The surgeon identified a small hamartoma in the patient's lung, which did not require immediate treatment.
Unlike a malignant tumor, a hamartoma usually grows slowly and generally does not invade surrounding tissues.

  • degree higher: neoplasm - a new and abnormal growth of tissue, such as a tumor
