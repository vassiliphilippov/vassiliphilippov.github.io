Quercus palustris

noun
species of oak tree; pin oak
Pronunciation: / kwɝːkəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs pəˈlʌstrɪs / (BrE)
A species of fast-growing, medium to large deciduous tree native to northeastern United States and southeastern Canada, with deeply lobed leaves that turn bright red in autumn and thriving in damp soil; commonly known as pin oak.

Landscapers often choose Quercus palustris for its rapid growth and pyramidal shape.

Wetlands restoration projects may use Quercus palustris to help stabilize soil and improve habitat.
