water yam

noun
a yam with edible white flesh; Australasian/Polynesian
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər jæm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə jæm / (BrE)
A type of yam grown in Australasia and Polynesia, known for its large root with fine edible white flesh.

Examples

A water yam root
Farmers harvested a bumper crop of water yam this year.
The water yam is a staple ingredient in many Pacific Island recipes.
Unlike sweet potatoes, water yam has a distinctive white interior when cooked.

Related Terms

  • synonym: yam - a starchy tuber, especially of the genus Dioscorea
