Jakobson

proper noun
noted linguist, expert in phonology
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑː.kəp.sən / (AmE), / ˈjæ.kəb.sən / (BrE)
Roman Jakobson (1896–1982), a Russian-American linguist noted for his influential work on phonology and linguistic universals.

Examples

Jakobson linguist theory
Jakobson was instrumental in developing the theory of distinctive features in phonology.
Many linguistic theories today are influenced by the work of Jakobson.
The conference honored Jakobson for his contributions to structural linguistics.
