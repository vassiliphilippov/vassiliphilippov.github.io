sacred cow

noun
person or thing immune to criticism
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌseɪ.krɪd ˈkaʊ /; / ˈseɪkrəd kaʊ / (AmE), / ˌseɪ.krɪd ˈkaʊ /; / ˈseɪkrɪd kaʊ / (BrE)
a person, thing, belief, or practice that is considered too important to be criticized or questioned

Examples

A sacred cow idea
For many years, the policy was a sacred cow that no politician would dare change.
Public broadcasting has long been a sacred cow in this country, protected from budget cuts.
The old professor is treated like a sacred cow in the department, and no one dares question his views.
Challenging the grading system is difficult because it's considered a sacred cow in this school.
Environmental regulations became a sacred cow in the debate, immune from any criticism.
In many organizations, the founder's decisions are treated as a sacred cow that no one dares question.

Related Terms

  • synonym: taboo - a social or cultural prohibition or restriction
  • synonym: untouchable - a person or thing not subject to criticism or attack
