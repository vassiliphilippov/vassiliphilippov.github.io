Mesopotamia

proper noun
ancient region between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsəpəˈteɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəpəˈteɪmiə / (BrE)
an ancient region of southwest Asia between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers, known as the site of some of the earliest human civilizations; largely corresponding to modern Iraq and parts of Syria and Turkey

Examples

Ancient Mesopotamia region
Many historians believe that Mesopotamia was the birthplace of writing and urban civilization.
The fertile land of Mesopotamia attracted settlers as early as 10,000 BCE.
Today, archaeologists continue to uncover ancient cities in the region once known as Mesopotamia.
