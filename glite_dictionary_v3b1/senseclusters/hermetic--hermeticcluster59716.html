hermetic

adjective
completely sealed; airtight
8.6 7.1
Pronunciation: /hər.ˈmet.ɪk/ (AmE), /hə.ˈmet.ɪk/ (BrE)
Completely sealed, especially so as to be airtight and impervious to external influence.

Examples

A hermetic seal
Make sure the jar is hermetic before placing it in storage to prevent spoilage.
Spacecraft require a hermetic environment to keep out the vacuum of space.
The laboratory used hermetic containers to store volatile chemicals safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airtight - not allowing air to escape or pass through
  • antonym: leaky - allowing liquid or gas to pass through a hole or crack
