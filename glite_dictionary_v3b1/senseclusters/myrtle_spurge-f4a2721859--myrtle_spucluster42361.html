myrtle spurge

noun
Mediterranean poisonous spurge plant
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜrtəl ˌspɝrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɜːtl ˌspɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a poisonous Mediterranean spurge plant (Euphorbia myrsinites) with trailing stems and fleshy blue-green leaves, sometimes naturalized elsewhere

Examples

myrtle spurge plant
Children should be warned not to touch myrtle spurge because of its poisonous sap.
Some landscapers use myrtle spurge as ground cover, unaware of its invasive nature.
The garden was overrun with myrtle spurge, which can be toxic if handled improperly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spurge - any plant of the genus Euphorbia, often with milky sap
