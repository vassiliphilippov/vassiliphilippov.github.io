drill down

verb
investigate in detail
Pronunciation: / drɪl daʊn / (AmE), / drɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to examine or analyze information or details more closely or thoroughly, especially by breaking data into smaller parts

Examples

To drill down data
During the meeting, the team chose to drill down into the root causes of the problem.
If we drill down further, we might uncover important trends in the data.
The report allows users to drill down into sales statistics by region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analyze - examine methodically and in detail
  • antonym: overview - a general or broad examination, not detailed
