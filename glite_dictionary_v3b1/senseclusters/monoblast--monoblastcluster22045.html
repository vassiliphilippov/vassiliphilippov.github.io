monoblast

noun
immature cell developing into a monocyte
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.nəˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈmɒ.nəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
a large, immature cell in the bone marrow that develops into a monocyte

Examples

A monoblast cell
A deficiency of monoblasts can indicate a bone marrow disorder.
Doctors examined the patient's bone marrow and identified several monoblasts.
In the early stages of leukemia, the number of monoblasts in the marrow may increase.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: monocyte - a type of large white blood cell, part of the immune system
