monocoque

noun
shell-based structure
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnəˌkɑk / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəkɒk / (BrE)
a type of structure in which the outer shell supports most or all of the load, rather than an internal frame

Examples

Aircraft monocoque
The airplane's monocoque design allows it to be both lightweight and strong.
Modern racing cars often use a carbon fiber monocoque for safety and performance.
Engineers chose a monocoque structure to improve the vehicle's rigidity and reduce weight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: space frame - a structure made from a network of struts supporting loads
  • synonym: unibody - a type of vehicle construction where the body and frame are a single unit
