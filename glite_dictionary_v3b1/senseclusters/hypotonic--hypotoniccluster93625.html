hypotonic

adjective
lacking normal muscle or tissue tension
9.9 7.0
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.ˈtɑːn.ɪk/ /; / /haɪ.poʊ.ˈtɑːn.ɪk/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈtɒn.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of living tissue or muscle) having less than normal tone or tension.

Examples

Hypotonic muscles
Babies with Down syndrome may have hypotonic muscle tone at birth.
During the examination, the doctor noted that the limbs felt unusually hypotonic.
The patient's muscles were hypotonic after the surgery, making movement difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flaccid - soft and hanging loosely or limply, especially of tissue
  • antonym: hypertonic - having abnormally high muscle or tissue tension
