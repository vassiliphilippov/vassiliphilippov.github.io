Elohist

adjective
of Elohist tradition
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛloʊˌhɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛləʊˌhɪst / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Elohist source or tradition in biblical criticism, especially in the analysis of the Hebrew Bible.

Examples

Elohist tradition
Scholars often debate which passages in Genesis are considered Elohist in origin.
The Elohist narrative tends to use the term 'Elohim' for God.
Her research focused on the unique features of Elohist texts in the Pentateuch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Yahwist - relating to the Yahwist source in biblical criticism
