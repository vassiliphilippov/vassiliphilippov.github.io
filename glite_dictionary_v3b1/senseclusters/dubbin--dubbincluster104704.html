dubbin

noun
leather-softening and waterproofing substance
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʌbɪn / (BrE)
A waxy or greasy substance made from tallow and oil, used to soften and waterproof leather.

Examples

A dubbin wax
Apply a layer of dubbin to your boots to keep the leather supple and water-resistant.
He used dubbin after cleaning his hiking shoes to prolong their lifespan.
Traditional saddlers often recommend dubbin for protecting equestrian equipment from damp weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grease - animal fat used for lubrication or waterproofing
  • degree lower: polish - substance used to make something, especially leather, smooth and shiny
