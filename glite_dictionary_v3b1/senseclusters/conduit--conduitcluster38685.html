conduit

noun
pipe or channel for water or wires
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.du.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.djuː.ɪt / (BrE)
a pipe, tube, or channel for conveying water, electrical wires, or other materials from one place to another

Examples

A conduit pipe
Rainwater flows away from the roof through a network of conduits.
The electrician ran the cables through a conduit to protect them from damage.
The new building features a hidden conduit for all its major utilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: channel - a passage or groove for conveying water or other substances
  • synonym: pipe - a tube through which fluids or gases can flow
