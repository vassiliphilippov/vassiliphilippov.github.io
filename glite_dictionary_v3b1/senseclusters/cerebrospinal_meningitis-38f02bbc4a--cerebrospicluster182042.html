cerebrospinal meningitis

noun
serious bacterial meningitis
Pronunciation: / səˌriːbroʊˌspaɪnəl ˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / sɛˌriːbrəʊˌspaɪnəl ˌmɛnɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A serious and often fatal bacterial infection causing inflammation of the membranes covering the brain and spinal cord.

Examples

cerebrospinal meningitis infection
Prompt diagnosis and treatment are crucial for patients suffering from cerebrospinal meningitis.
The outbreak of cerebrospinal meningitis led to widespread concern in the region.
Vaccination has significantly reduced the incidence of cerebrospinal meningitis worldwide.
