guano bat

noun
southern U.S. freetail bat species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡwɑːnoʊ bæt / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑːnəʊ bæt / (BrE)
A species of freetail bat (Tadarida brasiliensis) commonly found in the southern United States, known for its short velvety fur and southward migration in winter.

Examples

The guano bat migrates
During their seasonal migration, thousands of guano bats leave their roosts in massive swarms.
Researchers study the guano bat to better understand its role in cave ecosystems.
The guano bat is frequently seen at dusk, hunting insects over open fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freetail bat - a bat of the family Molossidae with free tail extending beyond the tail membrane
