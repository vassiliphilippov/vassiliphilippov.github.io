protrusile

adjective
able to be thrust forward (anatomy)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / pro äÀàtruÀês…ôl / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàtruÀêsa…™l / (BrE)
(of a body part) capable of being thrust forward or extended, especially used of anatomical parts such as the tongue or jaw.

Examples

Protrusile jaw
Many fish have a protrusile jaw, which allows them to extend their mouth outward.
Some birds possess protrusile beaks that help them access food hidden in crevices.
The frog uses its protrusile tongue to catch insects quickly.

Synonyms

  • protrusible
    9.9 0.00/m
    protrusible is a body part that can be pushed out beyond its normal limit, while protrusile means it can be thrust forward.
  • protractile
    9.9 0.00/m
    protractile is able to extend forward or protrude, often used for muscles or tongues, while protrusile tends to mean thrusting forward.
  • protractible
    9.6 0.00/m
    protractible is able to be lengthened or drawn out along its length, while protrusile means it can be pushed forward.

Related Terms
