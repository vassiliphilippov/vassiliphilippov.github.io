reinforcing stimulus

noun
stimulus increasing recurring behavior (psychology)

In psychology, a stimulus that increases the likelihood that the behavior which produced it will occur again.

Examples

A reinforcing stimulus effect
Food can act as a reinforcing stimulus for animals learning new behaviors.
The teacher used praise as a reinforcing stimulus to encourage participation in class.
Withdrawal of a reinforcing stimulus may lead to a decrease in the desired response.

Synonyms

  • reinforcer
