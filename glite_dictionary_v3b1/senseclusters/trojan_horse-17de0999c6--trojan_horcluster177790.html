Trojan Horse

noun
malicious software disguised as legitimate program
4.7
Pronunciation: / \u02c8tro\u028ad\u0292\u0259n h\u0254rs / (AmE), / \u02c8tr\u0259\u028ad\u0292\u0259n h\u0254\u02d0s / (BrE)
A type of malicious software that disguises itself as legitimate or useful software to trick a user into installing or running it, thereby allowing unauthorized access, data theft, or damage.

Examples

Trojan Horse virus
Security researchers discovered a Trojan Horse that secretly stole login credentials from employees' computers.
Many phishing emails try to trick users into downloading a Trojan Horse disguised as a useful installer.
After the breach, technicians found a hidden Trojan Horse that opened a backdoor for the attackers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: computer virus - a type of malware that can replicate itself and spread between systems
  • synonym: malware - software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise computers and networks
