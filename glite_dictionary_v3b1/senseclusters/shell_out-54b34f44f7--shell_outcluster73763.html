shell out

verb
pay or give out money (often reluctantly)
7.2
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl aʊt / (AmE), / ʃɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to pay or give money, especially when it is a large amount or given unwillingly

Examples

To shell out money
Despite complaining about the price, he finally shelled out for the concert tickets.
Every year, her parents shell out hundreds of dollars for her dance lessons.
I don't want to shell out a fortune on a new phone just yet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cough up - to reluctantly give money or something of value
  • synonym: fork out - to pay money, often unwillingly or reluctantly
  • synonym: pay - to give money in exchange for something
