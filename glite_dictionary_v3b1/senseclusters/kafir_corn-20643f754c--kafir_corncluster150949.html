kafir corn

noun
a type of grain sorghum
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkæfɚ kɔrn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːfə kɔːn / (BrE)
a variety of sorghum with sawtooth-edged leaves, grown chiefly for grain and animal fodder

Examples

Kafir corn plant
Kafir corn is widely cultivated in semi-arid regions for its drought resistance and nutritional value.
Farmers often use kafir corn as both livestock feed and a staple grain for human consumption.
The thick, fibrous stalks of kafir corn make it suitable for silage and fodder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sorghum - a genus of cereal grasses, cultivated for grain and fodder
