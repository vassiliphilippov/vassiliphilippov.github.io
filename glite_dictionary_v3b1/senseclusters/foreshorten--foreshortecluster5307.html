foreshorten

verb
depict in perspective to show depth
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔrˈʃɔrtən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈʃɔːtn̩ / (BrE)
to represent an object or figure in a picture or drawing so that it appears to recede sharply into the background, by shortening lines in proportion to the viewer’s perspective

Examples

To foreshorten a figure
In this drawing class, students learned how to foreshorten objects to create a sense of depth.
The artist skillfully foreshortened the figure's arm to make it look as if it was reaching out of the canvas.
The horse appears to leap forward because its body is dramatically foreshortened in the painting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perspective - the art technique of representing three-dimensional objects on a two-dimensional surface
