predict

verb
be a sign of a future event
7.2
Pronunciation: / prɪˈdɪkt / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪkt / (BrE)
to say or show that something will happen in the future

Examples

To predict the future
Experts say that these darkening skies predict heavy rainfall.
Some believe that animal behavior can predict an approaching earthquake.
The sudden drop in temperature seemed to predict a coming storm.
Experts predict that the new policy will boost the economy over the next year.
No one could predict how the weather would change so rapidly.
She predicted that the team would win, and she was right.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forecast - to predict or estimate a future event, especially the weather
  • synonym: foretell - to state or indicate what will happen in the future
  • degree lower: guess - to form an opinion or answer without sufficient evidence
  • synonym: portend - to be a sign or warning that something is likely to happen
  • synonym: project - to estimate or predict based on present trends
