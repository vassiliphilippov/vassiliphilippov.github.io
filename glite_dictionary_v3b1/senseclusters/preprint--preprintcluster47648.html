preprint

verb
to publish in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈprɪnt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to produce or distribute a preliminary version of a written work before its official publication.

Examples

To preprint research
The researchers decided to preprint their findings to receive early feedback from the scientific community.
Many authors now preprint their articles online before submitting them to journals.
She chose to preprint her thesis so colleagues could review it ahead of publication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: publish - to make a work available to the public
  • synonym: release - to make something available for use or publication
