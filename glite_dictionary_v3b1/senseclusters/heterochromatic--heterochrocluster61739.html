heterochromatic

adjective
relating to heterochromatin
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊkroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊkrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of heterochromatin, the densely packed, usually inactive regions of chromosomes.

Examples

Heterochromatic region
The centromere region of the chromosome is often highly heterochromatic and stains darkly under the microscope.
Researchers observed that certain genetic disorders are linked to heterochromatic regions of DNA.
In some species, the sex chromosomes are largely heterochromatic and contain few active genes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: euchromatic - relating to euchromatin, the less condensed, active regions of chromosomes
