birch bark

noun
canoe made from birch tree bark
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɝtʃ bɑrk / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ bɑːk / (BrE)
a type of canoe constructed using the bark of birch trees, traditionally made by Indigenous peoples of North America

Examples

A birch bark canoe
Building a birch bark requires knowledge of selecting and preparing the right materials.
He learned to paddle a birch bark on the quiet lake waters.
The museum displays a traditional birch bark made by Ojibwe craftsmen.
