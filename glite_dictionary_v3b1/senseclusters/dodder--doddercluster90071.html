dodder

verb
walk unsteadily with short steps
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːdɚ /; / ˈdɑdər / (AmE), / ˈdɒdə / (BrE)
to walk or move in a slow, unsteady, or shaky way, usually because of old age or weakness

Examples

To dodder along
After her surgery, she could only dodder around her room for short periods.
Children like to imitate their grandparents and sometimes dodder for fun.
The old man began to dodder across the street, clutching his cane tightly.
The elderly man doddered down the hallway, holding onto the wall for support.
After her illness, she doddered around the garden, moving much more slowly than before.
He doddered across the street, careful not to trip on the uneven pavement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily as if about to fall
  • antonym: stride - to walk with long, confident steps
  • synonym: totter - to walk or move in an unsteady way, as if about to fall
