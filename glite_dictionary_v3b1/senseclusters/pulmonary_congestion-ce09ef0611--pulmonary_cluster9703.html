pulmonary congestion

noun
build-up of fluid in the lungs
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊlməˌnɛri kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / ˈpʌlmənəri kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
Abnormal accumulation of fluid or blood in the lungs, often resulting in impaired breathing.

Examples

Pulmonary congestion symptoms
The patient was admitted to the hospital with severe pulmonary congestion after experiencing shortness of breath.
Treatment focused on reducing fluid buildup to relieve the pulmonary congestion.
X-rays revealed signs of pulmonary congestion caused by heart failure.
