trellis

verb
train (a plant) to grow on a trellis
9.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtre.lɪs/ /; / /ˈtre.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈtre.lɪs/ /; / /ˈtre.ləs/ (BrE)
to train or support a plant to grow on a trellis by tying or guiding its stems or branches

Examples

To trellis a vine
It takes skill and patience to trellis grapevines effectively in the vineyard.
The gardener carefully trellised the climbing roses along the wooden frame.
They spent the afternoon trellising their new cucumber plants to encourage upward growth.
She spent the morning trellising the climbing roses along the garden fence.
Gardeners often recommend trellising tomatoes to keep the fruit off the ground.
He carefully trellised the grapevines to encourage healthy growth and easy harvesting.
She carefully trellised the roses to help them grow upright along the garden wall.
The gardener trellises the vines every spring to keep them organized and healthy.
They had trellised the tomatoes to prevent the fruit from touching the ground.
