black apricot

noun
hybrid Asian apricot with purple fruit
3.1
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈæp.rɪ.kɑt / (AmE), / blæk ˈæp.rɪ.kɒt / (BrE)
A small hybrid apricot native to Asia and Asia Minor, with purplish twigs, white flowers, and inferior purple fruit.

Examples

The black apricot tree
Farmers in Asia Minor value the black apricot for its resistance to certain diseases.
The orchard recently planted several black apricot trees to diversify their fruit selection.
Unlike regular apricots, the black apricot produces purple fruit with a distinct flavor.
