churn

noun
rate of customer or data turnover
5.1
Pronunciation: /tʃɜːrn/ (AmE), /tʃɜːn/ (BrE)
the rate at which people, customers, subscribers, or items stop using or are replaced in a service, product, or dataset; turnover or frequent change within a system (e.g., customer churn, data churn).

Examples

Customer churn rate
The platform's churn rate rose sharply after the pricing change, alarming investors.
Customer support blamed the feature bug for increased churn across the subscription base last month.
High churn in the data stream forced the team to retrain models daily to maintain accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attrition - a gradual reduction in number or strength, especially of staff or customers
  • antonym: retention - the ability to keep customers, users, or employees; opposite of churn
  • synonym: turnover - the rate at which employees or customers leave and are replaced
