anticipate

verb
to realize or expect in advance
5.0
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt /; / ænˈtɪs.ɪ.peɪt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt /; / ænˈtɪs.ɪ.peɪt / (BrE)
to expect or predict that something will happen before it does

Examples

Experts anticipate changes
Economists anticipate an increase in inflation next year.
She could anticipate his reaction before he even spoke.
We must anticipate possible problems and prepare solutions in advance.
Economists anticipate that inflation will rise in the coming months.
She anticipated his next move and was ready with an answer.
We are anticipating a large crowd at the event this weekend.

  • synonym: expect - to regard something as likely to happen
  • synonym: predict - to say what will happen in the future
