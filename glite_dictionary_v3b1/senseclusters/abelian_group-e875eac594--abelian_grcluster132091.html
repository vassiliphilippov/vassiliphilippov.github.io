Abelian group

noun
mathematical group with commutative law

A group in mathematics in which the result of combining any two elements does not depend on the order in which they are combined.

Examples

Abelian group in math
Every subgroup of an Abelian group is itself Abelian.
In an Abelian group, the operation of addition is commutative for all elements.
The set of integers with addition forms an Abelian group.
