tetraplegia

noun
paralysis of all limbs
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈpliːdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈpliːdʒiə / (BrE)
a condition in which all four limbs and the torso are paralyzed, usually due to spinal cord injury or disease

Examples

Tetraplegia paralysis
After the accident, he was diagnosed with tetraplegia and required full-time care.
Advances in rehabilitation have improved the quality of life for people living with tetraplegia.
The patient developed tetraplegia following a severe spinal cord injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paraplegia - paralysis of the lower half of the body
  • synonym: quadriplegia - paralysis of all four limbs and the torso
