Aleppo grass

noun
a perennial grass species, often a weed
7.4
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛpoʊ ɡræs / (AmE), / əˈlɛpəʊ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall perennial grass (Sorghum halepense) that spreads by creeping rhizomes, grown for fodder but regarded as a serious weed in many cultivated areas, especially in the southern United States

Examples

Aleppo grass weed
Aleppo grass can quickly overtake farm fields, making it difficult for other crops to grow.
Although Aleppo grass is used for fodder in some regions, it is considered a noxious weed in others.
Farmers in the southern United States struggle to control the spread of Aleppo grass on their land.
