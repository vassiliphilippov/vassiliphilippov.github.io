chemoautotroph

noun
organism using chemicals for energy and CO2 for carbon
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / kiÀêmo äÀà…îto äÀåtro äf / (AmE), / kiÀêm…ô äÀà…îÀêt…ôt…π…ô äf / (BrE)
An organism that obtains energy by oxidizing inorganic substances and uses carbon dioxide as its main source of carbon.

Examples

Chemoautotroph bacteria
Hydrothermal vent communities are often supported by chemoautotroph bacteria.
Some bacteria are chemoautotrophs, using inorganic molecules for energy rather than sunlight.
Unlike plants, chemoautotrophs do not rely on photosynthesis for growth.
