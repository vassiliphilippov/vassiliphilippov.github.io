aspis

noun
a type of horned viper or asp snake
Pronunciation: / ˈæspɪs / (AmE), / ˈaspɪs / (BrE)
A name formerly used for several venomous snakes, especially horned vipers of the genus Vipera found in southern Europe and northern Africa.

Examples

The aspis snake
Cleopatra was famously said to have ended her life with the bite of an aspis.
In the dry regions, the aspis is both feared and respected by local communities.
The ancient Romans believed the deadly aspis guarded Egyptian tombs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asp - another name for a venomous snake, often the Egyptian cobra
