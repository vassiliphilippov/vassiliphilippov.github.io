univocality

noun
single, consistent meaning
9.9
Pronunciation: /juː.nɪ.ˈvoʊ.kə.lɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.nɪ.ˈvoʊ.kə.lɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /juː.nɪ.ˈvəʊ.kə.lɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.nɪ.ˈvəʊ.kə.lɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or doctrine that a word or concept has exactly the same meaning in different contexts, especially in philosophy or theology

Examples

Univocality in philosophy
The philosopher defended the univocality of the term 'being' in his argument.
Debates about univocality are common in discussions of religious language.
Some scholars question the univocality of ethical concepts across different cultures.

  • antonym: equivocality - the quality of having multiple possible meanings
