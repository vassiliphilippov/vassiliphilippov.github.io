inculpative

adjective
tending to incriminate or assign blame
7.2 8.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌlpeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌlpətɪv / (BrE)
(especially of evidence or statements) tending to incriminate or assign blame to someone.

Examples

Inculpative evidence
Any inculpative evidence must be disclosed to the defense team as well.
His confession contained inculpative details that implicated him in the crime.
The prosecutor presented several inculpative documents during the trial.
