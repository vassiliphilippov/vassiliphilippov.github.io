turquoise

noun
blue-green mineral used as a gemstone
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrˌk(w)oɪz / (AmE), / ˈtɜːkwɔɪz / (BrE)
a blue to green mineral consisting of a hydrous phosphate of copper and aluminum, used as a gemstone

Examples

Turquoise gemstone
Collectors prize high-quality turquoise for its vibrant color and rarity.
That necklace features polished beads of natural turquoise.
The artist set a bright turquoise in the center of the silver ring.
