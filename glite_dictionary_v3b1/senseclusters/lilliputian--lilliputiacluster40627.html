lilliputian

noun
a very small person
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪlɪˈpjuʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪlɪˈpjuːʃən / (BrE)
A very small person, especially one resembling the tiny inhabitants of Lilliput in Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels'.

Examples

A lilliputian child
In the world of skyscrapers, our old house seemed almost lilliputian.
Standing next to the basketball players, he looked like a lilliputian among giants.
The actor played a lilliputian in the stage adaptation of Swift's novel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dwarf - a person of unusually small stature
  • antonym: giant - a person of very great size
