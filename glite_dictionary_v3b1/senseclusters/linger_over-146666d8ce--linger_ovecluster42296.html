linger over

verb
spend extra time on; delay over
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋɡɚ ˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋɡə ˌəʊvə / (BrE)
to spend extra time on something, especially because you are enjoying it or do not want to finish quickly

Examples

To linger over coffee
Every morning, she would linger over her coffee, enjoying the quiet before work.
He tends to linger over his assignments, double-checking every detail.
They lingered over dinner, talking long after the plates had been cleared.
He likes to linger over the Sunday paper with a cup of tea.
She would linger over her dinner, savoring each bite and enjoying the conversation.
Tourists often linger over the paintings in the museum, admiring every detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dwell on - spend time thinking or talking about something at length
  • antonym: rush through - complete something quickly; do without delay
