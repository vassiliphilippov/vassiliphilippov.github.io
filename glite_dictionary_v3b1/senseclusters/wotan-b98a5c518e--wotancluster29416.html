Wotan

proper noun
chief god in Teutonic mythology
9.9 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààwo ät…ëÀên / (AmE), / Ààv…ô ätan / (BrE)
the chief god in Teutonic mythology, equivalent to the Norse Odin and Anglo-Saxon Woden

Examples

Wotan god of war
In Germanic mythology, Wotan was revered as the god of wisdom and war.
Richard Wagner's opera cycle prominently features Wotan as a central character.
Scholars often discuss the similarities between Wotan and other chief deities of ancient Europe.

Synonyms

  • Wodan
    9.9 0.01/m
    Wodan is the old continental Germanic name for the chief god in myths, while Wotan is the Teutonic form used in stories.
  • Woden
    9.9 0.05/m
    Woden is the Anglo-Saxon name for the chief god in old English tales, while Wotan is the Teutonic form known from myth and opera.
