bagwig

noun
18th-century wig with hair in a bag at the back
Pronunciation: / ˈbæɡˌwɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈbaɡwɪɡ / (BrE)
an 18th-century wig in which the hair is gathered and enclosed in a bag at the back.

Examples

A bagwig style
He took great care arranging his powdered bagwig before the grand ball.
In portraits from the late 1700s, you can often spot men with their hair tucked in a bagwig.
The judge entered the courtroom wearing a traditional bagwig as part of his ceremonial attire.
