macrosomia

noun
large newborn size
9.6
Pronunciation: /mæ.krə.ˈsoʊ.miː.ə/ /; / /mæk.rə.ˈsoʊ.miː.ə/ (AmE), /mæ.krə.ˈsəʊ.miː.ə/ /; / /mæk.rə.ˈsəʊ.miː.ə/ (BrE)
a condition in which a baby is born significantly larger than average, usually weighing more than 4,000 grams

Examples

Babies with macrosomia may require special care after delivery.

The incidence of macrosomia has increased due to rising rates of maternal diabetes.
