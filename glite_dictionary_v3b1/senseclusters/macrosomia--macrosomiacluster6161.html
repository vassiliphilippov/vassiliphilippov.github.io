macrosomia

noun
large newborn size
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmæk.rəˈsoʊ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmæk.rəˈsəʊ.mi.ə / (BrE)
a condition in which a baby is born significantly larger than average, usually weighing more than 4,000 grams

Examples

Baby with macrosomia
Babies with macrosomia may require special care after delivery.
Doctors monitor pregnant women for risk factors that could lead to macrosomia.
The incidence of macrosomia has increased due to rising rates of maternal diabetes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: large for gestational age - a baby whose size is above the normal range for its gestational age
  • antonym: low birth weight - a baby born weighing less than 2,500 grams
