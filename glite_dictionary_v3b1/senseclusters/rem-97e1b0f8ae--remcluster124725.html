REM

noun
stage of sleep with rapid eye movement and dreaming
Pronunciation: / ɑr i ɛm / (AmE), / ɑː r iː ɛm / (BrE)
a recurring stage of sleep characterized by rapid eye movements and vivid dreaming

Examples

The REM stage
A lack of REM can lead to mood and memory problems.
People experience most vivid dreams during REM sleep.
The study measured the amount of REM in participants each night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-REM - the stages of sleep that do not involve rapid eye movement or vivid dreaming
