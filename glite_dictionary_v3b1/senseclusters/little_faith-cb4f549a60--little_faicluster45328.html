little faith

noun
lack of confidence or belief
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬l ˈfeɪθ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˈfeɪθ / (BrE)
a lack of confidence, trust, or belief in something or someone

Examples

Little faith in people
During tough times, those with little faith often give up much sooner than others.
He approached the new project with little faith in its likelihood of success.
Her little faith in the team's abilities quickly changed once they started winning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doubt - feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • antonym: faith - strong belief or trust in someone or something
