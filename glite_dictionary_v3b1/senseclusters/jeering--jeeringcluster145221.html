jeering

noun
mocking or scornful remarks
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
mocking remarks or laughter showing contempt or scorn

Examples

Loud jeering
A chorus of jeering greeted the unpopular referee’s decision.
Her performance was met not with applause, but with outright jeering.
The speaker tried to continue despite the loud jeering from the crowd.

Related Terms

  • antonym: applause - praise shown by clapping, opposite of jeering
  • synonym: mockery - ridicule or derision
