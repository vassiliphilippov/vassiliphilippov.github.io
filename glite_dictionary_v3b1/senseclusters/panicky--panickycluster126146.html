panicky

adjective
suddenly overcome by fear or anxiety
2.9 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈpæn.ɪ.kiː/ (AmE), /ˈpæn.ɪ.kiː/ (BrE)
feeling or showing sudden, uncontrollable fear or anxiety, often in a way that affects one's actions

Examples

A panicky feeling
A panicky feeling swept through the office when the alarm sounded.
His panicky reaction made the situation more chaotic than it needed to be.
She grew panicky as the thunderstorm approached and didn't know what to do.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • synonym: panic-stricken - overcome by sudden and uncontrollable fear or anxiety
