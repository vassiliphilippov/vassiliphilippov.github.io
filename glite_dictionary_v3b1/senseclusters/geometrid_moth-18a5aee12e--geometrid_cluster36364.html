geometrid moth

noun
broad-winged moth of the Geometridae family

Pronunciation: /dʒiː.ˈɑː.mə.trɪd mɔːθ/ /; / /dʒiː.ˈɑː.mə.trɪd mɑːθ/ (AmE), /dʒiː.ˈɒm.ə.trɪd mɒθ/ /; / /dʒiː.ˈɒm.ə.trɪd məʊθ/ (BrE)
A slender-bodied, broad-winged moth of the family Geometridae, whose larvae are commonly known as measuring worms or inchworms.

Examples

A geometrid moth
Many gardeners notice geometrid moths around their lights during the summer months.
The caterpillar of the geometrid moth is often called a measuring worm because of the way it moves.
The wings of a typical geometrid moth are usually held flat when at rest.
