underseller

noun
seller offering lower prices than others
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈsɛlɚ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsɛlə / (BrE)
a seller who offers goods at a lower price than competitors

Examples

An underseller of goods
He looked for an underseller who could offer him the same product for less.
The new market was known as an underseller that attracted price-conscious customers.
To boost business, she became the chief underseller in her neighborhood, lowering prices on all items.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discounter - a seller who reduces prices, often for promotion
  • antonym: overpricer - a seller who sets prices higher than competitors
