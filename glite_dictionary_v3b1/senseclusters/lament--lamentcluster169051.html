lament

verb
express grief or sorrow aloud
Pronunciation: / ləˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ləˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to express sorrow, grief, or regret, especially by speaking, crying out, or complaining

Examples

To lament a loss
At the funeral, many people lamented the passing of their beloved friend.
She lamented the loss of her childhood home for years.
The poet lamented the suffering of his people in his moving speech.
Critics often lament the decline of traditional journalism in the digital age.
Many historians lament the destruction of ancient artifacts during the war.
She continued to lament the loss of her childhood home long after moving away.

Related Terms

  • antonym: celebrate - to acknowledge with joy or festivity, opposite of lament
  • synonym: deplore - to express strong disapproval or regret for something
  • synonym: mourn - to feel or show deep sorrow or regret for someone or something lost
  • antonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
