dip out

verb
leave unexpectedly or quietly (informal)
7.0 7.2
Pronunciation: /dɪp aʊt/ (AmE), /dɪp aʊt/ (BrE)
(informal) to leave a place, event, or situation suddenly or quietly, often without telling others or to avoid responsibility

Examples

To dip out early
I had to dip out before dessert because I wasn’t feeling well.
They dipped out of the party quietly to avoid being noticed.
Whenever the conversation gets dull, he just dips out without saying goodbye.
If you don't want to deal with this, you can't just dip out.
She dipped out of the meeting before her turn to present.
Whenever there's work to be done, he dips out and disappears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duck out - to leave quickly in order to avoid something or someone
  • synonym: leave - to go away from a place or event
