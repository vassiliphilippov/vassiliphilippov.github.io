intraocular lens

noun
artificial lens implanted in the eye
5.5 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.trəˈɑː.kyə.lɚ lɛnz / (AmE), / ˌɪn.trəˈɒk.jʊ.lə lɛnz / (BrE)
an artificial lens implanted in the eye to replace a damaged natural lens, commonly after cataract removal

Examples

An intraocular lens implant
After her cataract surgery, an intraocular lens was implanted to restore her vision.
Technological advances have improved the quality and durability of the intraocular lens used in eye procedures.
The doctor explained how the intraocular lens would function just like a natural lens.
