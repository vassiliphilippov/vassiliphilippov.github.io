treatment

noun
medical care or intervention
2.1 140/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtriÀêtm…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàtriÀêtm…ônt / (BrE)
medical care or procedures given to cure or relieve an illness or injury

Examples

Medical treatment
After months of physical treatment, her arm finally healed properly.
He sought treatment abroad when his local doctors could not help.
The hospital offers advanced treatment for cancer patients.
After her surgery, she needed months of physical treatment to recover fully.
Successful treatment can greatly improve a patient's quality of life.
The doctor recommended a different treatment for his chronic headaches.

Synonyms

  • medical care
    2.5 12/m
    medical care is professional management of illness by doctors and nurses, while treatment means the medical procedures to cure or relieve illness.
  • therapy
    3.2 59/m
    therapy is a planned set of medical or psychological sessions to help recovery, while treatment covers any medical care or procedures for illness.
  • intervention
    4.8 91/m
    intervention is a specific action or procedure to change a problem quickly, while treatment is broader medical care or longer-term management.
  • remedy
    3.4 6.60/m
    remedy is a medicine or simple cure people often use at home, while treatment is professional medical care or procedures to relieve illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cure - a treatment or remedy that gets rid of an illness or problem
  • antonym: diagnosis - the identification of a disease or other condition
  • synonym: therapy - the treatment of physical or mental problems, especially by talking or practical methods rather than drugs or operations
