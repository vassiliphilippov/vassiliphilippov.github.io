analog computer

noun
a computer using variable physical quantities
3.2 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈænəˌlɔg kəmˈpjuːtər / (AmE), / ˈænəlɒg kəmˈpjuːtə /; / ˈanəlɒɡ kəmˈpjuːtə / (BrE)
a computer that processes information represented by continuously variable physical quantities, such as electrical voltages or mechanical movements, rather than discrete numbers

Examples

An analog computer device
In the early 20th century, engineers often used an analog computer to solve complex differential equations.
The flight simulator relied on an analog computer to mimic the aircraft's dynamics.
Unlike a digital machine, an analog computer can simulate continuous changes in physical systems.
A flight simulator often uses an analogue computer to model real-world conditions.
Before digital technology became widespread, engineers relied on the analogue computer for complex calculations.
The analogue computer processes data by continuously changing voltages rather than using binary code.

Related Terms

  • antonym: digital computer - a computer that processes information represented in discrete numerical values
