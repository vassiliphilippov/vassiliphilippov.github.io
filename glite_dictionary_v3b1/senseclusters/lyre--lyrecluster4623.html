lyre

noun
ancient Greek stringed instrument
Pronunciation: / laɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪə / (BrE)
an ancient stringed musical instrument with a U-shaped frame, used especially by the Greeks for accompaniment

Examples

An ancient lyre
Archaeologists discovered an ancient lyre in the ruins of a Greek temple.
Depictions of Apollo often show him holding a lyre in his hands.
The poet played the lyre as he recited verses to the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harp - a larger stringed instrument related to the lyre
