concentrate

verb
make something more concise or condensed
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.sənˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sən.treɪt / (BrE)
to reduce something in length or volume by making it more concise or condensed

Examples

To concentrate a text
Can you concentrate this article so it fits the word limit?
She managed to concentrate the lengthy contract into a brief summary.
The editor asked me to concentrate my report into just two pages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condense - to make something shorter or more concise without losing the essential meaning
  • antonym: expand - to make something larger or more detailed in length or scope
