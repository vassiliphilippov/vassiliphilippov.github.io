Cooley's anemia

noun
severe inherited anemia disorder
8.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkuÀêliz …ôÀàniÀêmi…ô / (AmE), / ÀàkuÀêliz …ôÀàniÀêmi…ô / (BrE)
A severe, usually fatal form of thalassemia inherited from both parents, characterized by absence of hemoglobin, skeletal deformities, and enlargement of the heart, spleen, and liver.

Examples

Cooley's anemia disease
Advances in genetic counseling have helped families at risk for Cooley's anemia make informed decisions.
Due to the progression of Cooley's anemia, the patient required frequent blood transfusions throughout childhood.
The child was diagnosed with Cooley's anemia after several blood tests confirmed the absence of normal hemoglobin.
Advancements in medicine have improved the life expectancy of those diagnosed with Cooley's anaemia.
Children suffering from Cooley's anaemia require regular blood transfusions throughout their lives.
Genetic screening can identify carriers of the genes responsible for Cooley's anaemia.
