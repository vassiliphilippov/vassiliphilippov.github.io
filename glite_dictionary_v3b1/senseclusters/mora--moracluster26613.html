mora

noun
syllable weight unit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrə / (AmE), / ˈmɔːrə / (BrE)
a unit in phonology that measures the weight or length of a syllable in certain languages

Examples

A mora in Japanese
In Japanese poetry, each mora is counted to determine the structure of a verse.
Some languages use the mora instead of the syllable to measure rhythm.
A long vowel in Latin is considered to have two morae.

Related Terms

  • synonym: syllable - a unit of pronunciation containing a vowel sound
