delusively

adverb
in a deceptive or unrealistic way
5.6 6.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈluː.sɪv.liː/ /; / /də.ˈluː.sɪv.liː/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈluː.sɪv.liː/ /; / /də.ˈluː.sɪv.liː/ (BrE)
In a way that deceives or misleads, especially by creating a false or unrealistic impression.

Examples

Appears delusively close
At dawn, the mountains appeared delusively close to the hikers, tempting them to walk farther than planned.
The job sounded delusively easy in the advertisement, but it was much more complex in reality.
The negotiator spoke delusively of peace, while secretly planning for conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceptively - in a way that misleads or gives a false impression
  • antonym: realistically - in a way that is true to life or reality, not misleading
