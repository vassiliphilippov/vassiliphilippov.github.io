Dutch auction

noun
auction where price drops until sold
8.2 6.1
Pronunciation: /dʌtʃ ˈɑːk.ʃən/ /; / /dʌtʃ ˈɔːk.ʃən/ (AmE), /dʌtʃ ˈɔːk.ʃən/ (BrE)
A method of selling in which the price of an item is lowered until a buyer agrees to purchase it.

Examples

A Dutch auction sale
A Dutch auction can be advantageous for both sellers and buyers depending on market demand.
During the Dutch auction, the price kept falling until someone finally placed a bid.
The art gallery used a Dutch auction to sell all the paintings quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: English auction - An auction in which bidders publicly offer increasingly higher prices until no higher bid is made
