flip-flop

noun
electronic circuit with two stable states
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈflɪp.flɑːp/ /; / /ˈflɪp.flɔːp/ (AmE), /ˈflɪp.flɒp/ (BrE)

Examples

A flip-flop circuit
A flip-flop changes state every time it receives a clock pulse.
The D-type flip-flop is commonly used in digital memory circuits.
You can design a simple counter by connecting several flip-flops in sequence.
