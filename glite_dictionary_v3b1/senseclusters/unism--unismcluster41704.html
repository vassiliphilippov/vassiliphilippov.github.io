unism

noun
belief in unity
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical belief that all things are ultimately one and reality is a unified whole

Examples

Unism in philosophy
The philosopher's theory of unism suggests that all existence is fundamentally connected.
Many spiritual traditions embrace unism as a way to explain the unity of the universe.
In his lecture, he contrasted unism with dualism and pluralism in metaphysical thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monism - the doctrine that only one supreme being or principle exists
  • antonym: pluralism - the belief that reality consists of many different substances or principles
