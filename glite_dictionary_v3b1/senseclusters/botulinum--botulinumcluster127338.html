botulinum

noun
bacterium that produces botulin toxin
9.9 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / bo äÀàtjuÀêl…™n…ôm / (AmE), / b…ôÀàtjuÀêl…™n…ôm / (BrE)
An anaerobic bacterium of the genus Clostridium that produces botulin, the toxin responsible for botulism.

Examples

Botulinum bacterium
Botulinum is studied for both its toxic effects and its medical applications.
Food contaminated with botulinum can be extremely dangerous if consumed.
Scientists identified botulinum as the microbe responsible for botulism outbreaks.
