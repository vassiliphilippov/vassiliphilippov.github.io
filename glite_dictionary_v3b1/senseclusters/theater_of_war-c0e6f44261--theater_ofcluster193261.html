theater of war

noun
non-physical domain of military conflict
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈθiː.ə.tər əv wɔːr/ /; / /ˈθiː.eɪ.tər əv wɔːr/ /; / /ˈθiː.ə.tər əv wɑːr/ (AmE), /ˈθiː.ə.tə əv wɔː/ /; / /ˈθɪə.tə əv wɔː/ (BrE)
(in modern military and policy contexts) a non-physical domain (for example cyberspace or outer space) regarded as a theatre of war where hostile actions or military operations occur.

Examples

Cyber theater of war
Officials warned that critical infrastructure could be targeted in the new theater of war of cyberspace.
Strategists debate rules for conduct in a theater of war that has no clear national borders.
After the satellite strike, policymakers argued that outer space had become another theater of war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyberspace - the virtual environment of interconnected computer networks where digital communication and activities occur
  • synonym: outer space - the region beyond Earth's atmosphere; increasingly considered a domain for military operations
