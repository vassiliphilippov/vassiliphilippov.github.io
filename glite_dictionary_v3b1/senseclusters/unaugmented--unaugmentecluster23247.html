unaugmented

adjective
not increased (music)
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɔɡˈmɛntɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɔːɡˈmɛntɪd / (BrE)
in music, (of an interval or chord) not increased in size; having the standard number of semitones, not widened by a semitone

Examples

Unaugmented chord
The piece begins with an unaugmented triad, giving it a simple and clear sound.
He played an unaugmented interval to maintain the traditional harmony.
Many folk melodies use only unaugmented chords to preserve their original character.
