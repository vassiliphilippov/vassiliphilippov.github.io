diplacusis

noun
double pitch perception
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.plə.ˈkjuː.sɪs/ (AmE), /dɪ.plə.ˈkjuː.sɪs/ (BrE)
a condition in which the same sound is perceived as having a different pitch in each ear

Examples

Diplacusis in both ears
Musicians with diplacusis may struggle to play in tune because each ear hears a different pitch.
After his ear infection, he noticed symptoms of diplacusis when listening to music.
Doctors diagnosed her with diplacusis after she reported that voices sounded out of tune.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binaural diplacusis - a type of diplacusis where pitch difference is heard between both ears
  • synonym: hearing loss - partial or total inability to hear
