anticline

noun
arched rock fold
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæntɪˌklaɪn / (AmE), / ˈæntɪklaɪn / (BrE)
a fold in rock layers that forms an arch, with the oldest rocks at its center, often trapping oil or gas

Examples

A anticline fold
Geologists discovered an anticline that could potentially trap oil and natural gas.
The mountain range was formed by a massive anticline millions of years ago.
An anticline is often visible as a ridge on the earth's surface.
Geologists identified an anticline in the region, which could indicate the presence of oil reserves.
The river cut through the anticline, exposing layers of rock that were millions of years old.
Many natural gas fields are found trapped beneath the crest of an anticline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fold - a bend in rock layers caused by geological forces
  • antonym: syncline - a fold in rock layers that forms a trough with the youngest rocks at its center
