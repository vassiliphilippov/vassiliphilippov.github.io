pay dirt

noun
mineral-rich ore or source of profit
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪ ˌdɝt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪ ˌdɜːt / (BrE)
earth or ore that contains enough valuable minerals to be mined profitably; by extension, any source of great profit or success.

Examples

Investors are always hoping to find pay dirt in the next startup company.
The miners finally struck pay dirt after months of digging in the mountains.
