oculist

noun
medical doctor specializing in eye diseases
9.1 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.kjuː.lɪst/ /; / /ˈɑː.kjə.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.kjʊ.lɪst/ /; / /ˈɒ.kjə.lɪst/ (BrE)
a doctor or specialist who examines and treats eyes

Examples

Oculist eye doctor
After experiencing blurred vision, she made an appointment with an oculist for a thorough eye examination.
The oculist prescribed new medication to treat his recurring eye infections.
Though the term is less common today, an oculist was once the standard word for an eye doctor.
After noticing blurred vision, she scheduled an appointment with the oculist for an eye examination.
In nineteenth-century novels, a visit to the oculist was often suggested for reading difficulties.
The oculist recommended new glasses to correct his astigmatism.
