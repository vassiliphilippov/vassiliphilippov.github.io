Synonyms

  • grasp
    1.1 7.71/m
    grasp means taking something and keeping it firmly between your fingers and palm, while hold is a more general action with hands or arms.
  • grip
    1.3 11/m
    grip means taking something and keeping it very tightly, often during struggle or hard effort, while hold is a neutral way to keep objects.
  • take hold of
    3.1 2.33/m
    take hold of means deliberately grabbing something to start keeping it firmly in your hand, while hold covers grabbing and keeping generally.
  • clutch
    3.4 15/m
    clutch means grasping or fastening something tightly, often from fear or strong feeling, while hold is a simple physical action.
  • clasp
    2.6 5.14/m
    clasp means taking someone or something tightly in your hands or arms in an embrace, while hold is a general way to keep objects.
  • carry
    1.0 113/m
    carry means moving or supporting something from one place to another on your body or hands, while hold can mean keeping it without moving.
  • hold up
    3.4 8.23/m
    hold up means supporting or keeping something from falling by bearing its weight, while hold also means using your hands to keep or carry it.
  • support
    1.0 31/m
    support means bearing the weight of something or keeping it upright, while hold usually refers to using your hands or arms to keep or carry it.
