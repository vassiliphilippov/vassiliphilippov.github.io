hold

verb
to support or carry the weight of
Pronunciation: / hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
to physically grasp, carry, support, or keep something in your hands, arms, or body

Examples

To hold a baby
That pillar is designed to hold up the entire balcony.
The old shelf can barely hold the weight of those books.
This bracket will hold the painting securely on the wall.
Could you hold this book for me while I tie my shoe?
He held the umbrella over both of them during the rain.
She was holding her baby gently in her arms.
During the ceremony, many people held their noses because of the strong incense.
He held his ears to block out the loud siren.
She quickly held her nose as the bus passed the landfill.

  • antonym: release - to allow something, such as breath, to escape or be expelled
