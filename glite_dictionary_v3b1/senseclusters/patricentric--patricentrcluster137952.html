patricentric

adjective
focused on the father; male-centered
Pronunciation: / ˌpætrɪˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpætrɪˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
Centered on or dominated by the father or male head of a family or group.

Examples

A patricentric family
Many traditional societies are strongly patricentric in their structure and customs.
Some anthropologists argue that patricentric values influence legislation and governance.
The study explored the effects of a patricentric family dynamic on child development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: matricentric - centered upon the mother
