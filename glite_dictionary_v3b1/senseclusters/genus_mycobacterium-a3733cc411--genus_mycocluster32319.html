genus Mycobacterium

noun
bacterial genus including tuberculosis agents
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmaɪkoʊbækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌmʌɪkə(ʊ)bækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
A group of nonmotile, Gram-positive aerobic bacteria that includes the organisms responsible for tuberculosis and leprosy.

Examples

Genus Mycobacterium species
Cultures revealed that the patient was infected with a species belonging to the genus Mycobacterium.
Scientists are researching ways to control infections caused by bacteria from the genus Mycobacterium.
The genus Mycobacterium includes the causative agents of both tuberculosis and leprosy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mycobacterium tuberculosis - the species of bacteria causing tuberculosis
