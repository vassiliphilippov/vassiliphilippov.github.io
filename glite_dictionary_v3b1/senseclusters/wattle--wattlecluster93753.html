wattle

verb
interlace sticks to form a structure
9.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwɑː.təl/ (AmE), /ˈwɒ.təl/ (BrE)
to weave or interlace sticks or branches to make a structure, such as a fence or wall

Examples

To wattle a wall
The craftsmen wattled the walls before applying a layer of mud to complete the hut.
The early settlers would wattle branches together to create simple fences.
They spent the afternoon wattling hazel rods into a sturdy garden divider.
Historically, craftsmen wattled lightweight partitions from flexible branches.
In traditional building, they would wattle the walls before applying mud plaster.
The villagers wattled simple fences to keep animals out of the garden.
