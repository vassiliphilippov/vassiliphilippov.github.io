fang

noun
venom-injecting appendage of insects/arthropods
Pronunciation: / fæŋ / (AmE), / fæŋ / (BrE)
a pointed appendage of certain insects or arthropods, often capable of injecting venom, usually evolved from the legs

Examples

A spider's fang
Researchers studied how the scorpion’s fang evolved from leg segments.
Some insects possess a specialized fang to deliver their toxic bite.
The spider's fang pierced the prey, injecting venom instantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tooth - a hard, pointed structure in the mouth used for biting or chewing
