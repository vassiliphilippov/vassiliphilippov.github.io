transversus

adjective
across; crosswise
9.6
Pronunciation: / trænzˈvɜrsəs / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜːsəs / (BrE)
situated or extending across something, especially in reference to anatomical structures

Examples

Transversus muscle fibers
The transversus abdominis muscle helps stabilize the core during movement.
In anatomy, a transversus section cuts across the body horizontally.
The surgeon identified the transversus fibers running perpendicular to the body's midline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: longitudinal - running lengthwise rather than across
  • synonym: transverse - lying or placed across something; crosswise
