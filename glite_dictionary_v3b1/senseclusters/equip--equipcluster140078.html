equip

verb
to provide with knowledge or skills
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkwɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪp / (BrE)
to provide someone or something with what is needed for a particular purpose (such as tools, equipment, skills, or knowledge)

Examples

To equip with tools
He felt poorly equipped to handle the unexpected challenge.
Her education equipped her for a wide range of careers.
We aim to equip students with problem-solving skills for the workplace.
Before heading into the forest, the hikers made sure to equip themselves with maps and plenty of water.
Modern hospitals are carefully equipped to handle a wide range of emergencies.
The school decided to equip each classroom with new computers and smart boards this year.

  • synonym: outfit - to supply with necessary clothes or equipment
  • synonym: prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
  • synonym: train - to teach someone the skills for a job or activity
