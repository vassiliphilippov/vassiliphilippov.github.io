pinfeather

noun
stiff feather remnant
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪnˌfɛðər / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌfɛðə / (BrE)
a short, stiff feather or quill left in the skin of a bird after plucking, often sharp to the touch

Examples

Remove pinfeathers after plucking
After plucking the duck, a few pinfeathers remained stuck in the skin.
The chef carefully removed each pinfeather before cooking the chicken.
You can feel a pinfeather if you run your hand over the bird's skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quill - the hollow, stiff shaft of a feather
