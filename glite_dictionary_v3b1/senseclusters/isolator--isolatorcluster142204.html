isolator

noun
earthquake protection device
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəleɪtə / (BrE)
a structural device installed between a building and its foundation to reduce the transfer of ground motion during an earthquake

Examples

Earthquake isolator
The new hospital was built with a isolator system to protect it from earthquake damage.
Each support column under the building contains a large isolator to absorb seismic shocks.
Engineers recommended installing a isolator to minimize the risk of structural failure during earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplifier - a device that increases the strength of signals or motion
  • synonym: damper - a device that reduces vibration or oscillation in structures
