sucrose

noun
a common type of sugar; sweetening agent
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːkˌroʊs / (AmE), / ˈsjuːkrəʊs / (BrE)
a type of sugar found in many plants and commonly used as a sweetening agent; chemically, a disaccharide composed of glucose and fructose

Examples

Pure sucrose sugar
Excessive consumption of sucrose can contribute to various health problems.
Many processed foods contain added sucrose to improve taste.
Table sucrose is derived mainly from sugar cane or sugar beets.
