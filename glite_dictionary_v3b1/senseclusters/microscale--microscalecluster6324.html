microscale

adjective
very small scale
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌskeɪl / (BrE)
relating to or involving very small amounts, sizes, or dimensions, especially in scientific techniques or processes

Examples

Microscale experiments
Researchers used microscale methods to analyze tiny samples of the new material.
The microscale approach in chemistry helps reduce waste and improve safety in the lab.
Advances in microscale engineering have led to the development of smaller electronic devices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macroscale - relating to large amounts, sizes, or dimensions
  • synonym: miniature - very small in size or scale
