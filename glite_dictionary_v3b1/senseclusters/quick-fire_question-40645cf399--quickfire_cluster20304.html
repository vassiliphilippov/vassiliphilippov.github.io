quick-fire question

noun
a rapidly asked question
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪkˌfaɪɚ ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / ˈkwɪkˌfaɪə ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
a question asked immediately after another, often as part of a fast-paced sequence to elicit rapid responses

Examples

A quick-fire question round
During the trivia night, participants answered a series of quick-fire questions in sixty seconds.
She struggled to keep up with the quick-fire questions from the audience.
The interviewer opened with a quick-fire question to test the candidate's reflexes.
