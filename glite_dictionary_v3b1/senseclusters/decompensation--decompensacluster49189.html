decompensation

noun
loss of normal function
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌkɑːmpənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌkɒmpənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a failure of the body or mind to maintain normal function, often following stress or illness

Examples

Body decompensation
After months of chronic stress, the patient experienced decompensation and required immediate medical attention.
Doctors monitored her closely for any signs of decompensation during the recovery period.
Mental decompensation can occur when someone is unable to cope with ongoing emotional strain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breakdown - a collapse of physical or mental health
  • antonym: compensation - the process of counteracting a defect or weakness
