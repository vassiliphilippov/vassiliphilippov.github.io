paraboloid

noun
three-dimensional surface with parabolic sections
7.0 8.4
Pronunciation: /pær.ə.ˈbɑː.lɔɪd/ (AmE), /pær.ə.ˈbɒl.ɔɪd/ (BrE)
A three-dimensional surface where all cross-sections parallel to a particular axis are parabolas, and all cross-sections perpendicular to that axis are ellipses.

Examples

A paraboloid surface
The mathematician sketched a paraboloid to explain reflective properties.
The satellite dish is shaped like a paraboloid to focus incoming signals.
You can find a paraboloid in the design of some telescope mirrors.
