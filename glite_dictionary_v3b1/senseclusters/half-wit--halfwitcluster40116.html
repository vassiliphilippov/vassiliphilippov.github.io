half-wit

noun
a person of subnormal intelligence (offensive)
Pronunciation: / ˈhæfˌwɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɑːfˌwɪt / (BrE)
an offensive term for a person considered stupid or lacking intelligence

Examples

A half-wit insult
Calling someone a half-wit is considered highly rude and offensive today.
He dismissed his opponent as a half-wit unworthy of serious debate.
The play featured a comic character portrayed as a lovable half-wit.
Calling someone a half-wit is rude and likely to offend them.
Don't be such a half-wit and read the instructions before you start.
The movie portrayed the villain as a bumbling half-wit who couldn't get anything right.
