dragonfish

noun
ornamental freshwater fish
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡənˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdræɡənˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large freshwater fish, also known as an arowana, native to Asia and South America and valued as an ornamental aquarium fish

Examples

Aquarium dragonfish
The shimmering scales of the dragonfish make it a favorite among aquarium enthusiasts.
Many people believe that keeping a dragonfish brings good luck and prosperity.
The dragonfish can grow quite large, so it needs a spacious tank to thrive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arowana - a large, bony freshwater fish popular in aquariums
  • synonym: goldfish - a small, ornamental freshwater fish often kept in aquariums
