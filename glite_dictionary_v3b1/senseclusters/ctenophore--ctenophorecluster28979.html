ctenophore

noun
marine animal with cilia for locomotion
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtiː.nə.fɔːr/ /; / /ˈte.nə.fɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈtiː.nə.fɔː/ /; / /ˈte.nə.fɔː/ (BrE)
A marine animal of the phylum Ctenophora, characterized by biradial symmetry and eight rows of cilia used for locomotion, resembling jellyfish.

Examples

A ctenophore animal
Researchers observed a new species of ctenophore during their deep-sea expedition.
The ctenophore drifted gracefully through the illuminated waters, its cilia shimmering in the sunlight.
Unlike true jellyfish, a ctenophore moves by beating rows of tiny cilia.
