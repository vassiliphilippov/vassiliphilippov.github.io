time-scale factor

noun
ratio of simulated time to real time
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪm skeɪl ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm skeɪl ˈfæktə / (BrE)
the ratio between the time used in a simulation and the time of the actual process being simulated

Examples

A time-scale factor ratio
If you use a time-scale factor of 2, the simulated process runs twice as fast as the actual process.
Scientists adjusted the time-scale factor to model aging effects that would normally take decades.
The time-scale factor determines how quickly the events unfold in the simulation compared to real life.
