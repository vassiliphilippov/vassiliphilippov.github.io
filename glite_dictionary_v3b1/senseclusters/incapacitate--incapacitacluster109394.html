Examples

To incapacitate a person
Strong sedatives were used to incapacitate the suspect before arrest.
The accident incapacitated him for several months.
The virus can incapacitate a computer system within minutes.
A serious injury can incapacitate a professional athlete, ending their career prematurely.
Doctors tried to save the patient's arm, but the damage was enough to incapacitate it permanently.
The accident incapacitated him for the rest of his life.

Synonyms

  • disable
    3.5 3.42/m
    disable means making someone or something unable to work or do a specific task, while incapacitate means stop normal function more broadly.
  • paralyze
    5.3 12/m
    paralyze makes a person or system unable to move or act, while incapacitate covers loss of many normal functions.
  • immobilize
    6.0 1.81/m
    immobilize stops movement or makes something fixed in place, while incapacitate makes a person or thing unable to function generally.
