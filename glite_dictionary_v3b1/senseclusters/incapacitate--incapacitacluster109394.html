incapacitate

verb
to make unable to act or function
1.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt/ (BrE)
to make someone or something unable to function or perform normal actions, especially by injury or other means

Examples

To incapacitate a person
Strong sedatives were used to incapacitate the suspect before arrest.
The accident incapacitated him for several months.
The virus can incapacitate a computer system within minutes.
A serious injury can incapacitate a professional athlete, ending their career prematurely.
Doctors tried to save the patient's arm, but the damage was enough to incapacitate it permanently.
The accident incapacitated him for the rest of his life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cripple - to cause severe and permanent injury, preventing normal function
  • synonym: disable - to make unable to function normally
  • antonym: enable - to make able or capable of doing something
