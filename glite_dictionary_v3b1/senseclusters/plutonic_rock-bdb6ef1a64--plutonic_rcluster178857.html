plutonic rock

noun
igneous rock formed deep underground
7.3
Pronunciation: / pluˈtɑnɪk rɑk / (AmE), / pluˈtɒnɪk rɒk / (BrE)
an igneous rock that solidified deep within the Earth's crust, typically forming large masses

Examples

A plutonic rock sample
Geologists studied the plutonic rock samples to better understand the Earth's interior processes.
Granite is a common example of a plutonic rock found on Earth's continents.
The formation of plutonic rock involves the slow cooling of magma deep beneath the surface.
