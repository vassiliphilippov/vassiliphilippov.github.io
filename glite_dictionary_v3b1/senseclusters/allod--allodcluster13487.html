allod

noun
land owned outright
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.əd/ (AmE), /ˈæl.ɒd/ (BrE)
land owned outright and freely, without obligation to a feudal lord or superior

Examples

An allod land
In medieval Europe, an allod was rare compared to land held as a fief.
The family claimed their estate was an allod, free from any feudal duties.
Owning an allod meant the landholder owed no service to a lord.
