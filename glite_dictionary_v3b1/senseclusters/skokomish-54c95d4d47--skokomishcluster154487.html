Skokomish

proper noun
river in Washington
Pronunciation: / skoʊˈkoʊmɪʃ / (AmE), / skəˈkəʊmɪʃ / (BrE)
a river in western Washington State that flows from the Olympic Mountains into Hood Canal

Examples

Skokomish River
The Skokomish is known for its frequent flooding during the rainy season.
Salmon migrate up the Skokomish every year to spawn in its upper reaches.
Many hikers enjoy exploring the forests near the Skokomish River.
