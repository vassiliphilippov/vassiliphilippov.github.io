sissu

noun
East Indian hardwood tree
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪsu / (AmE), / ˈsɪsu / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Dalbergia sissoo) whose leaves are used for fodder and which yields a hard, durable dark brown timber used for shipbuilding and railroad ties.

Examples

Sissu tree leaves
Farmers often use the leaves of sissu as nutritious fodder for their livestock.
Many railway sleepers in India are made from the wood of the sissu.
The durable timber from the sissu tree is highly prized in construction and shipbuilding.
