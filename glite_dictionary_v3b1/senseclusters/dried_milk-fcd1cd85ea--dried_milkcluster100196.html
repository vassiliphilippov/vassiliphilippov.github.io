dried milk

noun
milk in powder form after dehydration
6.1 1.1
Pronunciation: /draɪd mɪlk/ /; / /dʒraɪd mɪlk/ (AmE), /draɪd mɪlk/ /; / /dʒraɪd mɪlk/ (BrE)
Milk that has had all its water removed, usually in powder form, for preservation or ease of storage.

Examples

A can of dried milk
Emergency food supplies often include cans of dried milk for nutrition.
Many bakers prefer to use dried milk for its longer shelf life and convenience.
To make the recipe, dissolve two tablespoons of dried milk in warm water.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: evaporated milk - milk with about 60% of the water removed, not powdered
  • synonym: powdered milk - milk that has been dried and made into a powder
