liberality

noun
tendency to favor progress and freedom
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪbəˈræləti / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈraləti / (BrE)
an attitude or inclination favoring progress, open-mindedness, and individual freedom, especially in political or social matters

Examples

Social liberality
Educational institutions often value liberality and open debate.
His liberality led him to support new social reforms.
The senator was known for her liberality on civil rights issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservatism - belief in traditional values and institutions; resistance to change
  • synonym: liberalism - belief in individual liberty and political and social reform
