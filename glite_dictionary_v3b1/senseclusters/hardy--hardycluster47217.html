hardy

adjective
strong and able to withstand hardship
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːrdi / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdi / (BrE)
strong and able to survive or endure difficult conditions; tough and resilient

Examples

Hardy plants survive
Only hardy plants can thrive in such a harsh climate.
She admired the hardy determination of the mountain climbers.
The hardy explorers survived weeks in the Arctic without shelter.
Only the most hardy adventurers attempt the climb during winter.
The hardy breed of sheep thrives in the mountainous region.
These winter vegetables are particularly hardy and can be left outside all season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delicate - easily damaged or susceptible to adverse conditions
  • antonym: frail - weak and delicate; easily broken
  • synonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous
  • synonym: tough - strong enough to withstand adverse conditions or rough handling
