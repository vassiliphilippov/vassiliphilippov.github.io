exaction

noun
demanding by force or authority
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzækʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzækʃən / (BrE)
the act of demanding or obtaining something, especially money, by force or authority

Examples

The exaction of money
Medieval kings often relied on the exaction of tribute from their subjects.
She considered the exaction of additional fees by the landlord to be unfair.
The exaction of high taxes led to widespread discontent among the people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: donation - something that is given voluntarily, without force or authority
  • synonym: extortion - the practice of obtaining something, especially money, through force or threats
