noun
comprehensive reference work
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkləˈpiːdiə /; / saɪkləˈpiːdiə / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪkləˈpiːdɪə /; / sʌɪkləˈpiːdɪə / (BrE)
A reference work, often in several volumes, containing articles on a wide range of topics or a particular subject, often arranged alphabetically.

Examples

A cyclopedia volume
He consulted the cyclopedia to gather information on ancient Egyptian history.
The school's library housed a complete set of the cyclopedia in its reference section.
This medical cyclopedia provides in-depth articles on a variety of diseases and treatments.
Many authors in the 18th century contributed to the famous Chambers's Cyclopaedia.
She consulted the cyclopaedia to write her research paper on ancient civilizations.
The library houses a rare Victorian cyclopaedia with hand-colored illustrations.
