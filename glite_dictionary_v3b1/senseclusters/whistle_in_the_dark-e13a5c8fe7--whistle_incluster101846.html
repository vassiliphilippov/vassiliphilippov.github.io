whistle in the dark

verb
make a futile or ignored gesture
5.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪs.əl ɪn ðə dɑːrk/ /; / /ˈhwɪs.əl ɪn ðə dɑːrk/ (AmE), /ˈwɪs.əl ɪn ðə dɑːk/ /; / /ˈhwɪs.əl ɪn ðə dɑːk/ (BrE)
To take an action or make a statement that is ineffective, futile, or likely to go unheeded, often as a way to comfort oneself.

Examples

To whistle in the dark bravely
He kept pushing his ideas, but they were little more than a whistle in the dark to the management.
Her complaints about the system were just a whistle in the dark and nobody paid attention.
Making promises now is like a whistle in the dark; the audience no longer trusts the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cry in the wilderness - a warning or request made without being heard or noticed
