monophthongal

adjective
single vowel sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˌnɑfˈθɑŋɡəl / (AmE), / məˌnɒfˈθɒŋɡəl / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of a single, unchanging vowel sound

Examples

Monophthongal vowel sound
In English, the vowel in 'cat' is a monophthongal sound.
Linguists study how monophthongal vowels differ from diphthongs in various languages.
The teacher explained that 'see' contains a monophthongal vowel, not a diphthong.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diphthongal - relating to a vowel sound that glides from one quality to another
