fray at the edges

verb
become weaker or less effective
Pronunciation: / freɪ ət ði ˈɛdʒɪz / (AmE), / freɪ ət ði ˈɛdʒɪz / (BrE)
(of a situation, organization, etc.) to gradually become weaker or less effective, especially under stress or pressure.

Examples

To fray at the edges under pressure
After months of relentless work, their patience began to fray at the edges.
The peace agreement started to fray at the edges as new conflicts emerged.
You could see morale in the department fraying at the edges as deadlines piled up.
