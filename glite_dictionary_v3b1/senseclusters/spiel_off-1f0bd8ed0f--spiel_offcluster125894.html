spiel off

verb
recite something rapidly and fluently
9.9
Pronunciation: / spiːl ɑf / (AmE), / spiːl ɒf / (BrE)
to recite or deliver something rapidly and fluently, often in a rehearsed or extravagant manner

Examples

To spiel off qualifications
At the interview, he began to spiel off his qualifications without pausing.
She can spiel off the company's history for any interested customer.
Tour guides often spiel off facts and trivia as if reading from a script.
