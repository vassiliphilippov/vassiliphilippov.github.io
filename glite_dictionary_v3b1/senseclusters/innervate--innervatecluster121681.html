innervate

verb
supply or stimulate with nerves
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnərˌveɪt /; / ˈɪn.ɚ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnəveɪt /; / ˈɪn.ə.veɪt / (BrE)
to supply an organ or body part with nerves, or to stimulate it through nerve impulses

Examples

To innervate body parts
Doctors studied how electrical impulses innervate cardiac tissue to regulate heartbeat.
Properly functioning nerves innervate the skin, allowing us to feel touch and temperature.
The nervous system innervates every muscle in the human body.
After the surgery, the doctor explained how the new tissue would be innervated by surrounding nerves.
Nerve fibers gradually innervate the muscle during fetal development.
Sensory neurons innervate the skin and enable us to feel touch and temperature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paralyze - to cause a loss of ability to move or feel in part or all of the body
  • synonym: stimulate - to encourage something to become more active; to prompt to action
