taste

noun
sensation of flavor in the mouth
1.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /teɪst/ (AmE), /teɪst/ (BrE)
the sense or ability to detect and recognize flavors with the mouth

Examples

A taste of chocolate
He savored the sweet taste of fresh strawberries.
That soup has a salty taste that lingers on the palate.
The medicine's bitter taste made it hard for her to swallow.
At wine school, students learn to refine their sense of taste.
Cats have a keen sense of taste but cannot taste sweetness.
She lost her taste after the illness and could no longer enjoy her favorite foods.
A cold can affect your sense of taste and smell.
After his surgery, John lost his taste and food became bland to him.
Children often have a different sense of taste compared to adults.

  • antonym: smell - the sensation caused by odors
  • synonym: taste bud - a sensory organ on the tongue that detects flavors
  • synonym: touch - the sense that enables physical perception through skin contact
