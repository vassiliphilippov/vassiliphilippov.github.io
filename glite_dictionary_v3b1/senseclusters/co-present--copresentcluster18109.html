co-present

verb
to present jointly
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to present or host something together with another person, such as a talk, show, or session

Examples

To co-present a show
They will co-present the conference session on climate policy next Monday.
Two experts were invited to co-present the new research findings at the seminar.
She was excited to co-present the awards ceremony with her colleague.

Related Terms

  • synonym: co-host - to host an event or program together with another person
  • degree lower: present - to give or host a talk, show, or event
