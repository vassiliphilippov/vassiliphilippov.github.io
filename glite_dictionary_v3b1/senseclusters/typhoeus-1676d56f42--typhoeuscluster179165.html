Typhoeus

proper noun
Greek mythological giant, son of Gaea and Tartarus
Pronunciation: / taɪˈfioʊəs / (AmE), / tɪˈfɪəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a monstrous giant with a hundred dragon heads who was the son of Gaea and Tartarus, known for creating whirlwinds and having a terrifying voice.

Examples

Typhoeus in mythology
According to legend, Typhoeus battled Zeus and was banished beneath Mount Etna.
Ancient poets described Typhoeus as a fire-breathing monster with a hundred heads.
The story of Typhoeus symbolizes the chaos that challenged the Greek gods.
