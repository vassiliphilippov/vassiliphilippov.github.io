reemploy

verb
to hire again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪmˈplɔɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈplɔɪ / (BrE)
to give someone their job back after they have left or lost it; to rehire

Examples

To reemploy a worker
After the project restarted, the manager decided to reemploy several experienced staff members.
The company promised to reemploy workers who had been laid off during the crisis.
If business improves, we may reemploy some of our former employees next year.
After the economy improved, the factory decided to reemploy many of its former staff.
The school board voted to reemploy the teacher after reviewing her case.
Several workers were happy to be reemployed following the company's reopening.
The company decided to reemploy several workers who had been laid off last year.
After completing her studies, he chose to reemploy her in the same department.
Many organizations are hesitant to reemploy staff after a major restructuring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dismiss - to remove someone from their job
  • synonym: rehire - to employ someone again after they have left a job
