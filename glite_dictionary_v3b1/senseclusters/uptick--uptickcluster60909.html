uptick

noun
stock market rise in transaction price
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌp.tɪk / (BrE)
a transaction in the stock market at a higher price than the previous trade

Examples

Stock uptick trade
After a period of stagnation, the market saw an uptick in trading activity.
Restrictions on short selling are sometimes triggered by an uptick rule.
The analyst noted a significant uptick in the stock’s closing price.
