undercount

noun
count lower than actual
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkant / (BrE)
a total or count that is less than the actual or true number of something

Examples

Census undercount
The census revealed a significant undercount in several rural communities.
Officials are concerned that the undercount could affect funding for local schools.
An undercount of cases may lead to an inaccurate understanding of the outbreak.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overcount - a count that is higher than the actual number
  • synonym: underestimate - an estimate that is lower than the actual value
