macadamize

verb
to surface a road with macadam
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈkædəˌmaɪz /; / ˌmækəˈdaɪz / (AmE), / məˈkædəmaɪz /; / ˌmakəˈdaɪz / (BrE)
to surface or cover a road or path with layers of small broken stone (macadam), often bound with tar or bitumen, using the macadam process.

Examples

To macadamize a road
Construction crews will begin macadamizing the main avenue next week.
The city decided to macadamize several rural roads to improve transportation.
They have macadamized the old highway, making it much smoother for drivers.
After being macadamised, the street became much smoother for cyclists and pedestrians.
The city council decided to macadamise the old country road to improve access.
Workers spent weeks macadamising the highway leading out of town.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphalt - to cover with asphalt (a mixture used for surfacing roads)
  • synonym: pave - to cover a surface, especially a road or sidewalk, with flat stones, bricks, or concrete
  • synonym: surface - to provide (a road, area, or material) with a particular type of surface
