detoxification

noun
environmental removal of toxins
4.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.tɑːk.sɪ.fɪk.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.tɑːk.sɪ.fɪk.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.tɒk.sɪ.fɪk.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.tɒk.sɪ.fɪk.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the removal, transformation, or neutralization of toxic substances from environmental media (air, water, soil) by chemical, biological, or physical treatment, often as part of pollution control or remediation.

Examples

Environmental detoxification
The factory paid for detoxification of the contaminated river to protect wildlife and downstream communities.
Engineers designed a treatment system for the detoxification of industrial wastewater before it reached municipal sewers.
Researchers study plant-based detoxification methods to remove heavy metals from urban soils near old factories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted or harmful substances in an environment; opposite of detoxification
  • synonym: decontamination - the removal of dangerous substances, such as chemicals or radioactive material, from people, equipment, or the environment
  • synonym: remediation - the process of cleaning up or improving polluted or contaminated land, water, or air
