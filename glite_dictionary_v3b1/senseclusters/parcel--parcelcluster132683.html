parcel

verb
wrap or package something
3.5 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ërs…ôl / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀês…ôl / (BrE)
to wrap or package something into a container, typically for transport or delivery

Examples

To parcel up gifts
He parceled up the old books and donated them to charity.
She parceled the gifts carefully before sending them to her friends overseas.
The factory workers parcel the fragile items to ensure they arrive safely.

Synonyms

  • package
    1.8 7.12/m
    package means putting items into boxes or wrapping for transport or storage, while parcel means wrapping something for delivery.
  • parcel up
    3.4 0.00/m
    parcel up means wrapping several items together for delivery or storage, while parcel usually refers to packaging a single item.
  • pack
    1.0 27/m
    pack means arranging and filling containers tightly for storage or travel, while parcel focuses on wrapping items for delivery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unwrap - to remove the wrapping from something
  • synonym: wrap - to cover or enclose in paper or soft material
