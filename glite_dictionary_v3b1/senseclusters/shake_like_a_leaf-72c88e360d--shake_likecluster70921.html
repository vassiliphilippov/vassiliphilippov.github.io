shake like a leaf

verb
tremble from fear or anxiety
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪk laɪk ə liːf / (AmE), / ʃeɪk laɪk ə liːf / (BrE)
to tremble or shake a lot because of fear, anxiety, or cold

Examples

To shake like a leaf with fear
After hearing the thunder, the little boy started to shake like a leaf.
She was so nervous before the interview that she began to shake like a leaf.
Standing alone in the dark, he could feel himself shake like a leaf.
After hearing the news, he shook like a leaf and could barely stand.
She was shaking like a leaf before her big speech on stage.
The child came inside, still shaking like a leaf from the cold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quiver - to shake with a slight, rapid motion
  • synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically from fear or cold
