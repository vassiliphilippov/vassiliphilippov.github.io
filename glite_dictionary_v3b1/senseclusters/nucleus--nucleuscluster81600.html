nucleus

noun
central core of an atom
3.4 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
the positively charged central core of an atom, consisting of protons and neutrons and containing most of its mass

Examples

Atomic nucleus
During nuclear fission, the atomic nucleus splits into smaller parts, releasing energy.
Electrons move around the nucleus in specific energy levels.
The nucleus contains protons and neutrons tightly bound together.
