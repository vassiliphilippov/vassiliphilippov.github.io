fidalgo

noun
Portuguese minor noble
Pronunciation: / fiˈdɑlɡoʊ / (AmE), / fiˈdælɡəʊ / (BrE)
a member of the lower Portuguese nobility, similar to a gentleman or minor nobleman

Examples

Portuguese fidalgo
The young fidalgo attended court to represent his family's interests.
As a fidalgo, he was respected in the local community despite his modest estate.
The novel describes the life of a rural fidalgo in 18th-century Portugal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commoner - a person who is not of noble rank
  • synonym: gentleman - a man of good social position, especially one of noble birth
  • synonym: nobleman - a man who belongs to the nobility
