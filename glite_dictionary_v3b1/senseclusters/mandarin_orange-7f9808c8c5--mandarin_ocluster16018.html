mandarin orange

noun
small sweet citrus fruit and its tree
Pronunciation: / ˈmændərɪn ˈɔrɪndʒ /; / ˌmændəˌrɪn ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈmændərɪn ˈɒrɪndʒ /; / ˌmændərɪn ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small, sweet, easy-to-peel orange citrus fruit (or the tree that produces it), originally from Asia

Examples

A mandarin orange snack
Children often enjoy eating mandarin oranges because they are easy to peel.
She peeled a mandarin orange and shared it with her friends.
The mandarin orange tree in our backyard produces fruit every winter.
Mandarin oranges are easy for children to open because of their loose skins.
She peeled a mandarin orange for her afternoon snack.
The dessert was topped with juicy slices of mandarin orange.
