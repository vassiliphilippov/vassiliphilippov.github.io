tooth shell

noun
marine mollusk with tapering tubular shell
3.5
Pronunciation: / tuːθ ʃɛl / (AmE), / tuːθ ʃɛl / (BrE)
A marine mollusk with a slender, tapering tubular shell open at both ends and a spade-shaped foot used for burrowing in sand.

Examples

A tooth shell mollusk
Scientists collected several tooth shells for laboratory study.
The distinctive shape of the tooth shell makes it easy to recognize among other seashells.
The sandy beach was littered with tooth shells washed up by the tide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scaphopod - a class of marine mollusks known as tusk shells or tooth shells
