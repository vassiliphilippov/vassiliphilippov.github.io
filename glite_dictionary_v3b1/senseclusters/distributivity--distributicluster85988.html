distributivity

noun
separate application
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˌstrɪbjəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / dɪˌstrɪbjʊˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the property in linguistics or semantics where an action or quality applies separately to each member of a group rather than collectively

Examples

Distributivity in language
In the sentence 'The students read books,' distributivity means each student read a book, not all together.
Understanding distributivity helps explain why some verbs can describe actions done by individuals in a group.
The concept of distributivity is important in both linguistics and logic when analyzing plural statements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collectivity - the property of applying to a group as a whole
