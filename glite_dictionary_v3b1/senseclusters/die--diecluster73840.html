die

verb
stop functioning (machine/device)
4.6 15/m
Pronunciation: / daÉª / (AmE), / daÉª / (BrE)
(of a machine, device, or system) to stop operating or functioning, especially permanently or unexpectedly.

Examples

The engine dies
If your car dies in the middle of the highway, turn on your hazard lights.
My laptop suddenly died right before I finished my assignment.
The old refrigerator died after years of reliable service.

Synonyms

  • fail
    2.2 19/m
    fail means to stop working or operate poorly, while die suggests a device stops completely, often unexpectedly or permanently.
  • crash
    3.2 5.49/m
    crash describes a sudden failure, often of computers or programs, while die implies the machine stops working entirely.
  • break down
    3.2 8.68/m
    break down refers to mechanical failure needing repair, while die suggests the device stops working, possibly beyond repair.
  • cut out
    4.1 1.69/m
    cut out means an engine or device stops suddenly while running, whereas die suggests it stops working for good or unexpectedly.
  • go dead
    3.9 1.35/m
    go dead means a battery or screen becomes silent or without power suddenly, while die implies permanent or unexpected stoppage.
  • give out
    4.1 1.96/m
    give out means a part or resource becomes worn or used up and stops, while die implies sudden or permanent failure.
  • go down
    3.4 6.18/m
    go down describes a service or system stopping, usually temporarily, while die suggests a complete or lasting stop, often unexpected.
