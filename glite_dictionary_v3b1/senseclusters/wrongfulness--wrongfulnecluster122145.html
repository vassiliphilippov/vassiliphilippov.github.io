wrongfulness

noun
the state of being unjust or unlawful
Pronunciation: / ˈrɔŋfʊlnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɒŋfʊlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being contrary to justice, law, or what is morally right

Examples

The wrongfulness of actions
She questioned the wrongfulness of the company's decision to dismiss her.
The wrongfulness of his actions became evident during the trial.
There was much debate over the wrongfulness of the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illegality - the state of being illegal
  • antonym: lawfulness - the quality of conforming to the law
