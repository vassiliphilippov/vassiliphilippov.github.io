Latin cross

noun
cross with a longer upright arm
Pronunciation: / ˈlætɪn krɔs / (AmE), / ˈlætɪn krɒs / (BrE)
A cross in which the upright arm is longer than the horizontal arms, typically used as a symbol of Christianity.

Examples

A Latin cross symbol
Many Christian churches feature a Latin cross atop their steeples.
The artist painted a simple Latin cross in the center of the canvas.
The floor plan of the cathedral was designed in the shape of a Latin cross.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Greek cross - a cross with all four arms of equal length
