rugrat

noun
informal young child
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌɡˌræt / (AmE), / ˈrʌɡˌræt / (BrE)
an informal term for a young child, especially one who is crawling or just learning to walk

Examples

A rugrat crawling
The living room was scattered with toys left by the rugrat after playtime.
She hired a babysitter to watch her two rugrats while she went shopping.
At the family picnic, every rugrat seemed to be running around with sticky hands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - a fully grown person
  • synonym: infant - a very young child or baby
  • synonym: toddler - a young child who is just beginning to walk
