watery

adjective
thin, weak, or diluted (of a liquid)
1.0 0.85/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ët…ôri / (AmE), / Ààw…îÀêt…ôri / (BrE)
containing too much water; weak and thin in taste or consistency

Examples

Watery soup
He complained that the soup was too watery to be satisfying.
I don't like watery coffee; I prefer a stronger brew.
The sauce turned out watery after she added too much milk.

Synonyms

  • washy
    7.6 0.03/m
    washy is thin and lacking in flavor, while watery describes something that contains too much water and tastes weak.
  • weak
    1.3 1.35/m
    weak is diluted and low in flavor when used for drinks, while watery means containing too much water and tasting thin.
  • runny
    1.0 1.57/m
    runny flows easily and can drip, while watery means containing too much water and therefore has a weak taste or thin feel.
