gliding bacteria

noun
bacteria that move along surfaces by gliding
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlaɪdɪŋ bækˈtɪɹiə / (AmE), / ˈɡlaɪdɪŋ bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
bacteria that move by gliding along surfaces, often forming colonies in slime; commonly found in moist soils, decaying plant matter, or animal waste.

Examples

Gliding bacteria move smoothly
Gliding bacteria secrete a slime that helps them move smoothly over wet surfaces.
Scientists discovered a new species of gliding bacteria in decaying wood samples.
The movement of gliding bacteria can be observed under a microscope in nutrient-rich environments.
