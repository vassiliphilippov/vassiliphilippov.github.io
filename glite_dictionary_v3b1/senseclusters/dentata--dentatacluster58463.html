dentata

adjective
toothed or serrated
Pronunciation: / dɛnˈtɑːtə / (AmE), / dɛnˈtɑːtə / (BrE)
having a toothed or serrated edge, especially used in scientific names for plants or animals

Examples

Dentata leaves
The oak species Quercus dentata is recognized by its deeply toothed leaves.
Many botanists use the term dentata to describe plants with serrated leaf edges.
The shell of this snail is labeled dentata due to its jagged rim.

Related Terms

  • synonym: serrated - having a jagged or saw-like edge
  • antonym: smooth - having an even, unbroken surface or edge
