slate

verb
to designate or schedule officially
Pronunciation: / sleɪt / (AmE), / sleɪt / (BrE)
to officially select or schedule someone or something for a particular role, job, or event

Examples

To slate a meeting
Several candidates have been slated for the upcoming election.
The committee slated her for a promotion next year.
The concert is slated to begin at 8 PM sharp.
She has been slated for promotion after her outstanding performance.
The board slated him as a potential candidate for the new CEO position.
Three authors have been slated to speak at the conference next month.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nominate - to formally propose someone for a position or role
  • synonym: schedule - to arrange or plan something to happen at a particular time
