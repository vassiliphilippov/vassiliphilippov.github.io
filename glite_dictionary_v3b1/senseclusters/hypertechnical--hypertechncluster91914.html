hypertechnical

adjective
overly technical
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈtɛknɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈtɛknɪkəl / (BrE)
extremely or excessively technical, especially with too much focus on technical details, rules, or complex terms

Examples

A hypertechnical objection
The judge criticized the lawyer's hypertechnical objections as unnecessary and confusing.
Her hypertechnical approach to the project slowed down the entire team.
Some regulations are written in such a hypertechnical way that few people can understand them.
The legal document was so hypertechnical that most people found it impossible to read.
Her explanation of the process became hypertechnical, confusing those without a background in engineering.
Some critics argue that the report is unnecessarily hypertechnical and should be more accessible.
