mason bee

noun
solitary bee that nests in mud and sand
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪsən biː / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.sn̩ biː / (BrE)
A solitary bee that builds its nest from mud and sand, often in crevices or hollow stems.

Examples

Mason bees build nests
Gardeners encourage mason bees to improve pollination in their orchards.
The mason bee uses mud to carefully seal each chamber of its nest.
Unlike honey bees, the mason bee leads a solitary life.
