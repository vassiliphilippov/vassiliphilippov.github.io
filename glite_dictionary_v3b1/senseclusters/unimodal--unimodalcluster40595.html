unimodal

adjective
having a single mode or peak
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈmoʊdəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈməʊdəl / (BrE)
Having a single mode; characterized by only one peak or maximum point in a distribution.

Examples

Unimodal distribution
Most normal distributions are unimodal, featuring only one mode.
The data followed a unimodal distribution with a single prominent peak.
The histogram was clearly unimodal, making analysis easier.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bimodal - having two modes or peaks
