smoosh

verb
to gently squeeze
7.7 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / smuÀê É / (AmE), / smuÀê É / (BrE)
to squeeze, press, or flatten something or someone gently, often in an affectionate or playful way

Examples

To smoosh a face
She loves to smoosh her dog's face when playing with him on the couch.
The children smooshed their clay into funny shapes during art class.
He gently smooshed the pillow to make it more comfortable before sleeping.

Synonyms

  • smush
    7.5 0.16/m
    smush is an informal word for pressing things flat in a rough, messy way, while smoosh is gentle and playful.
  • squish
    3.2 0.79/m
    squish means pressing until soft or noisy, often with a wet sound, while smoosh is gentler and more affectionate.
