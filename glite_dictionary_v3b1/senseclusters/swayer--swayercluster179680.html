swayer

noun
a ruler or commander (rare, literary)
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsweɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈsweɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who rules, governs, or has commanding influence over others, especially in a literary or poetic context

Examples

The swayer of people
In ancient texts, the king was often referred to as the swayer of his people.
Some believed fate itself to be the true swayer of the universe.
The poet described the monarch as the swayer of nations and fates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ruler - a person who rules or governs
  • antonym: subject - a person under the rule of another
