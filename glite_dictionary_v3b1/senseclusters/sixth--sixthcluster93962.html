sixth

noun
musical interval of six scale degrees
7.2
Pronunciation: / sɪksθ / (AmE), / sɪksθ / (BrE)
a musical interval spanning six consecutive notes in a standard diatonic scale; the difference in pitch between the first and sixth note.

Examples

A sixth interval
Composers often use a sixth to create a distinctive emotional effect.
She struggled to sing the sixth because of its wide span.
The melody leaps a sixth at the start of the chorus.
