run past

verb
move quickly by; let pass without action

to move quickly past someone or something, or to allow an opportunity or moment to pass without taking action

Examples

To run past a person
A cyclist ran past us on the narrow sidewalk.
Don't let this chance run past without trying your best.
I watched the children run past my window on their way to school.

Synonyms

  • pass by
    1.2 9.15/m

  • whisk by
    5.1 0.14/m
    whisk by moves very quickly and smoothly past someone, while run past can be faster but also mean letting a chance slip.
  • brush by
    5.2 0.19/m
    brush by moves very close and often without pause near someone, whereas run past focuses on moving quickly or missing a moment.
  • slip by
    3.8 0.64/m
    slip by passes quickly or without notice, especially for time or chance, while run past also means physically running past someone.
  • go past
    1.0 1.18/m

  • fly by
    2.2 0.85/m
    fly by refers to a flying object moving past something, while run past usually describes running on foot or letting time pass.
