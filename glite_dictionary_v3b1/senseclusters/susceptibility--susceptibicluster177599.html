susceptibility

noun
the state of being easily affected
6.6
Pronunciation: / səˌsɛptəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / səˌsɛptɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state of being easily influenced, affected, or harmed by something

Examples

High susceptibility to illness
Children have a higher susceptibility to colds during the winter months.
Genetic factors can influence a person's susceptibility to certain diseases.
His susceptibility to flattery made him an easy target for manipulation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immunity - the ability to resist a particular infection or toxin
  • synonym: vulnerability - the quality of being easily harmed or attacked
