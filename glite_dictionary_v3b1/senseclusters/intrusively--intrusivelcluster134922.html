intrusively

adverb
in a disruptive manner
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruːsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that interferes with, disrupts, or invades someone's privacy; unwelcome or unwanted entry

Examples

Intrusively ask questions
The security guard watched as the visitor moved intrusively through restricted areas.
Some apps collect data intrusively without clearly informing the user.
He asked questions so intrusively that everyone felt uncomfortable.
The photographer behaved intrusively at the event, making guests uncomfortable.
She asked intrusively about details I did not want to share.
Security guards stopped the man for acting intrusively near the restricted area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discreetly - in a careful and unobtrusive way
  • synonym: disruptively - in a way that interrupts or disturbs
  • synonym: offensively - in a way that causes displeasure or annoyance
  • antonym: unobtrusively - in a way that does not attract attention or interfere
