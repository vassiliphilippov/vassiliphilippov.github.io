subjectivize

verb
to make subjective
Pronunciation: / səbˈdʒɛktɪˌvaɪz / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛktɪvaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more subjective or interpret it based on personal feelings or opinions

Examples

To subjectivize a story
The author chose to subjectivize the narrative, focusing on the character's inner thoughts and feelings.
Debates can quickly subjectivize when participants rely on personal experiences rather than facts.
As the discussion continued, the facts started to subjectivize, blending opinion with evidence.
Some readers tend to subjectivize the story, relating it to their own experiences.
It is important not to subjectivize scientific data when presenting research findings.
The artist's unique style can subjectivize even the most ordinary scenes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectivize - to make something more objective or based on facts
  • synonym: personalize - to make something reflect personal feelings or characteristics
