firebrick

noun
heat-resistant brick for furnaces, chimneys, etc.
4.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər.brɪk/ /; / /ˈfaɪr.brɪk/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə.brɪk/ (BrE)
A brick made from fire clay, designed to withstand high temperatures, used for lining furnaces, chimneys, and fireplaces.

Examples

A firebrick wall
Because of its resistance to high temperatures, firebrick is commonly used in kilns and ovens.
The engineer inspected the firebrick lining inside the industrial furnace.
They replaced several cracked firebricks in the old fireplace to improve safety.
