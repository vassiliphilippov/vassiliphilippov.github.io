native cat

noun
cat-like marsupial of Australia and Tasmania
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪtɪv kæt / (AmE), / ˈneɪtɪv kat / (BrE)
a carnivorous, arboreal, cat-like marsupial native to Australia and Tasmania, especially the quoll

Examples

Australian native cat
Researchers observed a native cat hunting insects and small mammals at night.
Some farmers consider the native cat a pest, but it plays a crucial role in the ecosystem.
The native cat is now rarely seen in its former habitats due to deforestation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quoll - a carnivorous marsupial of Australia and New Guinea, also known as the native cat
