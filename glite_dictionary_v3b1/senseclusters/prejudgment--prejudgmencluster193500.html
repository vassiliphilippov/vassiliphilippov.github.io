prejudgment

noun
judgment formed before evidence is known
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt /; / ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt /; / ˌpriːˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
A judgment or opinion formed before all the necessary evidence is known or considered.

Examples

A prejudgment of facts
Her prejudgment of the situation resulted in an unfair evaluation.
The committee cautioned against making any prejudgment before hearing all the facts.
We must avoid prejudgment and keep an open mind during the investigation.
Her prejudgement of the case led to an unfair decision.
It's important to avoid prejudgement and wait for all the facts to emerge.
The committee was criticized for its obvious prejudgement of the witness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impartiality - the quality of being fair and not having a prejudgement or bias
  • synonym: prejudice - an opinion formed without sufficient knowledge or reason
