Dutch door

noun
a door split horizontally into two independently opening halves
6.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /dʌtʃ dɔːr/ /; / /dʌtʃ dɑːr/ (AmE), /dʌtʃ dɔː/ (BrE)
An exterior door divided horizontally into two sections, allowing either the top or bottom half to be opened independently.

Examples

A Dutch door
Installing a Dutch door in the kitchen allowed them to chat while keeping pets out.
She opened only the top half of the Dutch door to greet her neighbor.
The old cottage had a charming Dutch door that let in the summer breeze.
