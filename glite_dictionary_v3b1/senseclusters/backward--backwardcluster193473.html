backward

adjective
less developed or advanced
Pronunciation: / ˈbækwərd / (AmE), / ˈbækwəd / (BrE)
having made less progress or development than is normal or expected, especially in economic, social, or educational terms

Examples

Backward region
Educational opportunities are limited in backward regions.
Many rural areas remain backward due to a lack of investment.
The reforms aim to modernize backward sectors of the economy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advanced - highly developed or further along in progress
