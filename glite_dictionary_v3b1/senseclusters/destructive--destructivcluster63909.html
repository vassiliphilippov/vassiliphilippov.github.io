destructive

adjective
causing damage or harm
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
causing great damage or harm; tending to destroy or ruin.

Examples

Destructive criticism
Constant exposure to destructive criticism can erode a person's self-confidence.
He expressed his anger through destructive behavior, breaking furniture and yelling.
The hurricane had a truly destructive impact on the coastal towns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructive - having a positive effect; intended to help or improve
  • synonym: damaging - causing harm or injury
