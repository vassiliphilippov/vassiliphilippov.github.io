warehousing

noun
storing and organizing large datasets for analysis

(in computing and business) the activity of collecting, storing, organizing, and managing large amounts of structured data in a central repository (a data warehouse) so it can be analyzed and reported for business intelligence.

Examples

Data warehousing
After merging systems, the IT team prioritized warehousing to enable consolidated sales reports across regions.
The university course introduced students to principles of warehousing and its role in business intelligence.
Cloud providers offer managed warehousing services so companies can scale analytics without extra hardware.
