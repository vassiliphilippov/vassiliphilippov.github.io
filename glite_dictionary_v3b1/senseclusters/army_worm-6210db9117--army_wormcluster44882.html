army worm

noun
crop-destroying moth larva
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrmi wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi wɜːm / (BrE)
The larva of certain noctuid moths, especially known for migrating in large groups and destroying crops such as grass and grain.

Examples

Army worms destroy crops
After the rain, army worms began migrating across the lawn in thick lines.
Scientists are searching for natural predators to control the spread of army worms.
The invasion of army worms devastated the farmer's wheat fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cutworm - larvae of various moths that cut down young plants at soil level
