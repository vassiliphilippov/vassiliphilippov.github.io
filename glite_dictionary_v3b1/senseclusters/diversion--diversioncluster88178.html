diversion

noun
an act of turning aside or deviating
1.7
Pronunciation: / dəˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
an act or instance of turning aside from a course, activity, or concern; a deviation or detour

Examples

A diversion from work
During the meeting, the discussion made a diversion into unrelated topics.
His sudden interest in gardening was merely a diversion from his professional responsibilities.
The accident caused a diversion from the main road, adding an extra hour to our journey.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on with the current course
  • synonym: deflection - the action of turning aside from a straight course
  • synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
