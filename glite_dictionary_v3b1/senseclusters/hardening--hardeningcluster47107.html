hardening

noun
process of becoming hard or solid
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming hard or making something harder or more solid (especially in materials or substances)

Examples

Rapid hardening of concrete
Rapid hardening can affect the strength of certain materials.
The hardening of concrete requires careful temperature control.
The glue undergoes hardening after being exposed to air.
After the treatment, the hardening of the arteries became a major health concern.
Repeated freezing and thawing contribute to the hardening of soil in winter.
The gradual hardening of the clay made it suitable for sculpting intricate designs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: softening - process of becoming softer
  • synonym: solidification - process of becoming solid
