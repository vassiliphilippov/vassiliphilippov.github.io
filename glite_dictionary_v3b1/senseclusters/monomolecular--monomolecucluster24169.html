monomolecular

adjective
single-molecule process
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnoʊməˈlɛkjələr / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊməˈlɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to a chemical reaction or process involving only one molecule in the rate-determining step.

Examples

Monomolecular reaction
A monomolecular reaction depends on the concentration of only one reactant.
Scientists studied the monomolecular breakdown of the compound under different conditions.
The rate law for a monomolecular process is first order with respect to the reactant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bimolecular - involving two molecules in a chemical reaction step
  • synonym: unimolecular - involving a single molecule in a chemical reaction step
