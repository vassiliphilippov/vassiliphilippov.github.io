collectivize

verb
bring under collective ownership
9.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈlek.tɪ.vaɪz/ (AmE), /kə.ˈlek.tɪ.vaɪz/ (BrE)
to bring (land, property, or enterprises) under collective ownership and control, especially by a government or community

Examples

To collectivize farms
Debates continue about whether it is beneficial to collectivize major industries or leave them in private hands.

The government decided to collectivize agriculture in an effort to increase efficiency and central control.
After the revolution, the new government sought to collectivise all agricultural land.
Many farms were forcibly collectivised during the period of rapid industrialization.
The policy to collectivise small industries was met with considerable opposition.
