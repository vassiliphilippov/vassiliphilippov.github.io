sixth cranial nerve

noun
nerve controlling outward eye movement
7.0
Pronunciation: / sɪksθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜrv / (AmE), / sɪksθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the motor nerve that controls the lateral rectus muscle of the eye, enabling outward movement of the eyeball

Examples

Sixth cranial nerve controls eye
Damage to the sixth cranial nerve can lead to double vision and difficulty moving the eye outward.
During the neurological exam, the doctor tested her sixth cranial nerve function by asking her to look to the side.
Tumors near the brainstem may press on the sixth cranial nerve, affecting eye movement.
