glycerinated gelatin

noun
gelatin and glycerin base for ointments
Pronunciation: / ˌɡlɪsəˌreɪtɪd dʒəˈlæt̬ən / (AmE), / ˌɡlɪsəˌreɪtɪd dʒəˈlæt.ɪn / (BrE)
a jelly-like substance made from gelatin, glycerin, and water, used as a base for ointments and suppositories

Examples

Glycerinated gelatin ointment base
Doctors sometimes prescribe medicines compounded with glycerinated gelatin for patients who need topical treatments.
Suppositories made with glycerinated gelatin dissolve easily at body temperature.
The pharmacist prepared the ointment using glycerinated gelatin as the base.
