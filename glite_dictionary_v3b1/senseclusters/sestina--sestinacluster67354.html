sestina

noun
poetic form with repetition
Pronunciation: / sɛˈstinə / (AmE), / sɛˈstiːnə / (BrE)
a poem with six stanzas of six lines each, followed by a final three-line stanza, using a fixed pattern of word repetition

Examples

A sestina poem
The poet spent weeks crafting a complex sestina for the literary magazine.
Writing a sestina requires careful planning to repeat the end words correctly.
Her latest collection features a beautiful sestina about the changing seasons.
The poet spent weeks crafting a sestina for the upcoming competition.
In her literature class, she analyzed a famous sestina by a medieval writer.
Writing a sestina requires careful planning of repeated end-words in each stanza.

Related Terms

  • synonym: villanelle - a poem with nineteen lines and a fixed form
