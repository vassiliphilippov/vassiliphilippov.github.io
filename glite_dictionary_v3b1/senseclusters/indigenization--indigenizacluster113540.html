indigenization

noun
adaptation to local culture
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌdɪdʒənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɪdʒənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adapting something to suit the customs, culture, or needs of a local community

Examples

Indigenization of culture
The indigenization of technology ensures that new tools are relevant to local communities.
Many universities promote the indigenization of their curricula to reflect native perspectives.
Government policies encouraged the indigenization of the workforce in key industries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: globalization - the process of making something worldwide in scope or application
  • synonym: localization - the process of making something suitable for a particular place or people
