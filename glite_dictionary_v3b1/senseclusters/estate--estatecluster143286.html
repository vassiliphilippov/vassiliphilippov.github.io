estate

noun
large country property owned by a person or family
4.2 32/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªËˆsteÉªt / (AmE), / ÉªËˆsteÉªt / (BrE)
A large area of land, usually in the countryside, owned by one person, family, or organization, often with a large house.

Examples

A country estate
He inherited a vast estate including several farms and forests.
The family lived on a beautiful estate in the countryside for generations.
The gardens surrounding the main house on the estate are open to the public in summer.

Synonyms

  • demesne

  • landholding
