reimage

verb
to create new image
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to create a new visual image of something, especially by photographing, scanning, or imaging it again

Examples

To reimage a knee
The doctor decided to reimage the patient's knee to check for any changes.
If the first scan is unclear, the technician may need to reimage the area.
They had to reimage the artwork to capture the new details added by the artist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photograph - to take a picture of something
  • synonym: rescan - to scan something again
