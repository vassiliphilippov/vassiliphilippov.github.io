chordal

adjective
relating to chords in music
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of chords; emphasizing chords rather than melodies or counterpoint, especially in music.

Examples

Chordal music style
She prefers chordal arrangements to more melodic accompaniments.
The composer used chordal harmonies to create a dramatic effect.
The piece’s chordal structure gives it a rich and harmonious sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contrapuntal - relating to or involving counterpoint in music
