layering

noun
plant propagation method
5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈleɪ.ə.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.ə.rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈleɪ.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a horticultural method of plant propagation in which a stem is bent to the ground to form roots and produce a new plant

Examples

Plant layering method
Gardeners often use layering to propagate shrubs that are difficult to grow from cuttings.
The horticulturist demonstrated layering by bending a branch to the soil and covering it with earth.
Successful layering can result in a new plant that is genetically identical to the parent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cutting - a method of plant propagation using a piece of stem or leaf
  • synonym: grafting - joining parts from two plants so they grow as one
