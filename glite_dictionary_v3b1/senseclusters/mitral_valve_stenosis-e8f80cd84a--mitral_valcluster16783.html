mitral valve stenosis

noun
narrowing of the mitral heart valve
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtrəl vælv stəˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtrəl vælv stɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a condition in which the mitral valve of the heart is obstructed or narrowed, often due to scarring, impeding blood flow from the left atrium to the left ventricle

Examples

Mitral valve stenosis condition
Rheumatic fever is a common cause of mitral valve stenosis in developing countries.
Surgery may be required to treat severe mitral valve stenosis and restore normal blood flow.
The patient was diagnosed with mitral valve stenosis after experiencing shortness of breath and fatigue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aortic stenosis - narrowing of the aortic valve of the heart
