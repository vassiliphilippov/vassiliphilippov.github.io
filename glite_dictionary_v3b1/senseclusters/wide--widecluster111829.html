wide

adverb
to the fullest extent possible (adverb)
Pronunciation: / waɪd / (AmE), / waɪd / (BrE)
to a great extent, as far as possible, or over a large area (used as an adverb)

Examples

Arms wide apart
He stood with his arms wide apart, ready to catch the ball.
She opened her eyes wide in surprise when she heard the news.
The door was pushed wide open to let the fresh air in.
Rumors spread wide throughout the small village after the incident.
She cast her gaze wide in hopes of catching a glimpse of her friend.
The explorers journeyed wide across the continent in search of new lands.
She swung the door wide open to let in the fresh air.
The runner moved wide to the left to avoid the defender.
The two children stood wide apart, waiting for the ball to be thrown.

Related Terms

  • antonym: narrowly - with little space separating things
  • synonym: widely - over a large area or range; by many people (adverb)
