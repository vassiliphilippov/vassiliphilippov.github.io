well-worn

adjective
overused or overfamiliar (of phrases or ideas)
4.8 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛlˈwɔrn / (AmE), / ˌwɛlˈwɔːn / (BrE)
(of phrases, remarks, or ideas) used or repeated so often that it is no longer interesting or effective; overfamiliar through overuse.

Examples

Well-worn phrase
His speech was full of well-worn clichés that failed to inspire the audience.
She quoted a well-worn proverb everyone had heard before.
The movie follows a well-worn formula that has been used countless times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) lacking significance through having been overused
  • antonym: original - not derived from something else; fresh or novel
  • synonym: trite - lacking originality or freshness; overused and unoriginal
