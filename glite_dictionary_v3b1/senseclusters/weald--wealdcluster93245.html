weald

noun
open or wooded countryside
9.0
Pronunciation: / wild / (AmE), / wiːld / (BrE)
an area of open or wooded country, especially one with forests or rolling hills; often used of specific regions in England.

Examples

The weald region
Early settlers built their homes along the edges of the weald, taking advantage of the plentiful timber.
Much of the ancient weald has now been converted into farmland, but pockets of forest remain.
The South Downs rise above the weald like a green wave frozen in time.
