One half of the difference between the first and third quartiles in a set of data, used as a measure of spread in statistics.

Examples

The quartile deviation measure
Because the data contains outliers, the quartile deviation is considered a more robust measure of dispersion than the standard deviation.
The quartile deviation provides a quick way to assess the spread of scores in a distribution.
When comparing two datasets, a lower quartile deviation indicates less variability around the median.
