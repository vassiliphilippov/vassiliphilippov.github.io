genus Antennaria

noun
genus of small, woolly perennial herbs
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˌtɛˈnɛəri.ə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ænˌtɛnˈnɛː.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
A genus of small, woolly, perennial herbs with small whitish discoid flowers surrounded by a ring of club-shaped bristles.

Examples

Genus Antennaria plants
Genus Antennaria is native to regions across North America and Eurasia.
Several species of genus Antennaria are commonly called pussytoes due to their soft, woolly flower heads.
