monomer

noun
molecule that forms polymers
6.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.nə.mər/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.nə.mə/ (BrE)
A simple molecule that can join with identical or different molecules to form a polymer.

Examples

A monomer molecule
Glucose is a monomer that combines to form the polymer cellulose.
Proteins are made from monomer units called amino acids.
The scientist explained how each monomer links to another in the process of polymerization.
