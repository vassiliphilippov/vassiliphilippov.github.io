four-wheel drive

noun
vehicle system powering all four wheels
4.7 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔr wil ˈdraɪv /; / ˈfɔr wiːl ˌdraɪv / (AmE), / ˌfɔː wil ˈdraɪv /; / ˈfɔː wiːl ˌdraɪv / (BrE)
a system in a vehicle that powers all four wheels, or a vehicle equipped with this system, for better traction on rough or slippery surfaces

Examples

Four-wheel drive vehicle
He switched to four-wheel drive when the trail became muddy and steep.
Some cars offer four-wheel drive as an optional feature for off-road enthusiasts.
The truck's four-wheel drive allowed it to navigate the snowy roads safely.
He just bought a new four-wheel drive to explore off-road trails.
The snowy mountain roads can be handled easily with a four-wheel drive.
This rugged four-wheel drive is popular among outdoor enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: all-wheel drive - a vehicle system that powers all wheels, but not always equally or simultaneously
