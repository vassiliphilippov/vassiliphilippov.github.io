oligopsony

noun
few buyers market
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːlɪˈgɑːpsəni / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪˈgɒpsəni / (BrE)
a market situation in which a small number of buyers control the demand for a product or service

Examples

An oligopsony market
In an oligopsony, a few large companies dominate the purchasing of raw materials.
Farmers often face challenges when selling crops in an oligopsony where only a handful of buyers exist.
The labor market for professional athletes can resemble an oligopsony controlled by a few major teams.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monopsony - a market with only one buyer
  • antonym: oligopoly - a market with a small number of sellers
