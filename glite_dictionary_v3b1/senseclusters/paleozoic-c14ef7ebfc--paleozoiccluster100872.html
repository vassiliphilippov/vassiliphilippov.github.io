Paleozoic

adjective
of the Paleozoic era (geology)
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪ.li.əˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæl.i.əʊˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to or denoting the Paleozoic era, a major division of geological time characterized by the emergence of most major animal groups

Examples

Paleozoic rocks
Geologists studied Paleozoic rock formations to understand ancient marine environments.
Many important mineral deposits were formed during Paleozoic times.
The fossils found in this region are predominantly from the Paleozoic era.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Mesozoic - relating to or denoting the Mesozoic era, following the Paleozoic
  • degree lower: Precambrian - relating to or denoting the geological time before the Paleozoic era
