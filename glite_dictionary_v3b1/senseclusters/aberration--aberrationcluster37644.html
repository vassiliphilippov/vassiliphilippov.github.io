aberration

noun
optical flaw; image distortion
6.7 6.7
Pronunciation: /æ.bə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /æ.bə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
An optical phenomenon in which a lens or mirror fails to produce a clear or accurate image, causing blurring or distortion.

Examples

Lens aberration
Chromatic aberration can cause colored fringes around objects in photographs.
Spherical aberration makes the edges of the image appear blurry through cheap magnifying glasses.
The new telescope was designed to minimize optical aberration for sharper images.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distortion - alteration of the original form of an image
