Addison's disease

noun
adrenal gland hormone deficiency disorder
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈæd.ɪ.sənz dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ˈæd.ɪ.sənz ə.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /ˈæd.ɪ.sənz dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ˈæd.ɪ.sənz ə.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A rare disorder caused by the insufficient production of hormones by the adrenal cortex, resulting in symptoms such as weakness, low blood pressure, and skin darkening.

Examples

Addison's disease symptoms
People with Addison's disease often require lifelong hormone replacement therapy.
The patient was diagnosed with Addison's disease after experiencing chronic fatigue and weight loss.
Untreated Addison's disease can lead to a potentially life-threatening crisis.
