avoid

verb
keep away from; stay clear of
3.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈvɔɪd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈvɔɪd/ (BrE)
to keep away from or not do something, especially to prevent something bad from happening

Examples

To avoid making mistakes
He tried to avoid making eye contact with his boss.
Many people avoid driving in heavy rain whenever possible.
They avoided the area after hearing about the recent accidents.
Eating healthy foods can help you avoid illnesses.
He tried to avoid any topics that might lead to an argument.
She took a different route to avoid heavy traffic.
Doctors advise patients to avoid alcohol while taking certain medications.
During Lent, she avoids eating meat on Fridays.
Many people avoid gluten for health reasons.
