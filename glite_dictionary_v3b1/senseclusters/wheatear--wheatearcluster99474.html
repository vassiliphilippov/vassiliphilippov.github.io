wheatear

noun
small songbird with a white rump
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːtˌɪr / (AmE), / ˈwiːtɪə / (BrE)
A small songbird of northern regions of America and Eurasia, recognized by its distinctive white rump.

Examples

A wheatear bird
A wheatear perched on the stone wall, its white rump flashing as it hopped along.
Birdwatchers were thrilled to spot a rare wheatear during their hike.
In spring, wheatears migrate north to breed on open moorland.
