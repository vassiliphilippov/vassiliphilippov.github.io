industrial union

noun
union of all industry workers
5.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdʌs.trɪ.əl ˈjuː.njən/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʌs.trɪ.əl ˈjuː.nɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdʌs.trɪ.əl ˈjuː.njən/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʌs.trɪ.əl ˈjuː.nɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a labor union that includes all workers in a particular industry, regardless of the specific jobs or crafts they perform

Examples

An industrial union member
She joined an industrial union because it represented every worker on the production line.
The industrial union negotiated a new contract for all factory employees, regardless of their job titles.
Unlike a craft union, an industrial union admits members from all departments within an industry.
