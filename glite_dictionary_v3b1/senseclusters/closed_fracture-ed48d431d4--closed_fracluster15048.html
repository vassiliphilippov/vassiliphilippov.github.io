closed fracture

noun
fracture with unbroken skin
Pronunciation: / kloʊzd ˈfræk.tʃər / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈfræk.tʃə / (BrE)
A bone fracture in which the broken bone does not penetrate or break through the skin.

Examples

A closed fracture injury
After the fall, the doctor confirmed it was a closed fracture, so no surgery was required.
The X-ray revealed a closed fracture of his lower leg that would heal with a cast.
Unlike an open fracture, a closed fracture does not involve a wound breaking the skin.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open fracture - fracture in which the bone pierces the skin
