resettlement

noun
the process of moving people to a new place to live
3.9 1.58/m
Pronunciation: / riËˆsÉ›tlmÉ™nt / (AmE), / riËˆsÉ›tlmÉ™nt / (BrE)
the act or process of moving people to a new place to live, especially after they have been forced to leave their original homes

Examples

The resettlement process
After the flood, the resettlement process took several months to complete.
International agencies coordinated the resettlement of families displaced by the conflict.
The government organized the resettlement of refugees in new housing areas.

Synonyms

  • relocation
    4.0 0.83/m
    relocation is a general move of people to a new place, while resettlement focuses on settling displaced people into new communities.
  • rehousing
    3.9 0.01/m
    rehousing is the act of giving someone a new home, while resettlement is a wider process of moving displaced people and rebuilding lives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deportation - the act of expelling someone from a country
  • synonym: evacuation - the act of removing people from a place for safety
