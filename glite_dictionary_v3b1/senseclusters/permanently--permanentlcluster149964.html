Synonyms

  • in perpetuity
    7.8 0.7/m
    in perpetuity is a formal legal phrase for an endless time, while permanently is a neutral word for something meant to last.
  • for good
    2.8 11/m
    for good means stopping change forever in everyday speech, while permanently is neutral and fits formal or written contexts.
  • forever
    1.4 0.6/m
    forever means for a very long or endless time in casual talk, while permanently is more formal and used across contexts.
  • eternally
    5.2 2/m
    eternally means without end, often in religious or poetic speech, while permanently is a plain, general term for lasting.
  • everlastingly
    7.4 0.04/m

  • perpetually
