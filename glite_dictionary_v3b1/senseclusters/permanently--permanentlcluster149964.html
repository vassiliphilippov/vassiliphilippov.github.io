permanently

adverb
in a way that lasts for all time
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrmənəntli / (AmE), / ˈpɜːmənəntli / (BrE)
In a way that lasts or is intended to last or remain unchanged indefinitely; for all time.

Examples

To be permanently closed
After the accident, the road was permanently closed to traffic.
The museum's collection was permanently moved to a new facility downtown.
They decided to settle permanently in Spain after retiring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time
