organ of hearing

noun
part of the ear for hearing
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrɡən əv ˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡən əv ˈhɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the anatomical part of the ear responsible for detecting and processing sound

Examples

The organ of hearing
Damage to the organ of hearing can result in permanent hearing loss.
Scientists are studying how the organ of hearing transmits sound waves to the brain.
Some animals have a highly developed organ of hearing that allows them to detect even the faintest sounds.
