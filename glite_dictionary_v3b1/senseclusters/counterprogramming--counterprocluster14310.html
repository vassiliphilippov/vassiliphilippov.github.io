counterprogramming

noun
alternative event planning
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.proʊ.ɡræm.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.prəʊ.ɡræm.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of organizing or presenting alternative events or content to draw attention away from another event or program

Examples

Counterprogramming event
The activists planned counterprogramming to draw attention away from the controversial rally.
Television networks often use counterprogramming to compete with major sporting events.
The university scheduled a series of lectures as counterprogramming during the political convention.
The TV station used counterprogramming to draw viewers away from the popular sports event.
Networks often rely on counterprogramming during major award shows to attract different audiences.
By offering a comedy marathon, the channel engaged in counterprogramming against the drama series finale.

  • synonym: competition - the act of competing with others for the same audience
  • synonym: protest - an organized public demonstration expressing strong objection
  • synonym: scheduling - the process of planning when programs or events will occur
