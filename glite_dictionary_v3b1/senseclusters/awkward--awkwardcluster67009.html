awkward

adjective
lacking grace or skill; clumsy
2.1 2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɔkwərd /; / ˈɑːk.wɚd / (AmE), / ˈɔːkwəd /; / ˈɔːk.wəd / (BrE)
lacking grace, skill, or elegance in movement, manner, or expression; clumsy or inelegant

Examples

An awkward movement
He made an awkward attempt to jump over the fence and nearly fell.
Her awkward movements revealed she was new to dance class.
The speech ended with an awkward pause as he searched for the right words.
He has an awkward way of phrasing things that sometimes causes confusion.
Her speech sounded awkward and stilted compared to her usual confidence.
The author’s awkward writing style makes the book difficult to read.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clumsy - lacking physical coordination or grace
  • antonym: graceful - showing grace or elegance in movement
