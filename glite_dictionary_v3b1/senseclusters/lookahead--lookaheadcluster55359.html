lookahead

noun
regex assertion
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlʊkəˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈlʊkəˌhɛd / (BrE)
a zero-width assertion in a regular expression that checks if certain characters follow the current position without including them in the match

Examples

Regex lookahead
A lookahead allows you to match a word only if it is followed by a specific character.
The developer used a negative lookahead to exclude certain patterns from the search results.
Understanding how lookahead works is essential for writing advanced regular expressions.

Related Terms
