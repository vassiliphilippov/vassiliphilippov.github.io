secondary modern school

noun
former British vocational secondary school
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkəndɛri ˈmɒdərn skuːl / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndəri ˈmɒdən skuːl / (BrE)
a former type of British secondary school that focused on practical and vocational education rather than academic subjects

Examples

A secondary modern school
After failing the eleven-plus exam, he attended a secondary modern school in the 1960s.
Many students who went to a secondary modern school trained for trades or practical careers.
The abolition of the secondary modern school system led to major educational reforms in Britain.
