drift away

verb
gradually lose contact with someone
2.3 3.7
Pronunciation: / drɪft əˈweɪ / (AmE), / drɪft əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually lose contact or closeness with someone over time, especially in a relationship or friendship.

Examples

Friends drift away
After graduation, many friends drift away as life takes them in different directions.
As time passed, they began to drift away from each other and stopped keeping in touch.
Even though we promised to stay close, we slowly drifted away over the years.
