bacteriostatic

adjective
inhibiting bacterial growth
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /bæk.tə.rɪ.oʊ.ˈstæt.ɪk/ /; / /bæk.tə.rɪ.ə.ˈstæt.ɪk/ (AmE), /bæk.tə.rɪ.əʊ.ˈstæt.ɪk/ /; / /bæk.tə.rɪ.ə.ˈstæt.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a substance or action) preventing the growth and reproduction of bacteria, without necessarily killing them.

Examples

Bacteriostatic effect
Some disinfectants have a bacteriostatic effect rather than killing microbes outright.
The doctor prescribed a bacteriostatic agent to inhibit the infection.
Unlike bactericidal drugs, bacteriostatic antibiotics only prevent bacteria from multiplying.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bactericidal - capable of killing bacteria
