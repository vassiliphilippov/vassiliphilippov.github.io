nasogastric feeding

noun
feeding via nose-to-stomach tube
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌneɪzoʊˈgæstrɪk ˈfidɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌneɪzəʊˈɡæstrɪk ˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of administering liquid nutrients through a tube inserted through the nose into the stomach

Examples

Nasogastric feeding tube
After surgery, the patient received nasogastric feeding to ensure proper nutrition during recovery.
Doctors recommended nasogastric feeding because the child was unable to swallow safely.
The nurse checked the placement of the tube before starting nasogastric feeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enteral feeding - feeding that delivers nutrients directly into the gastrointestinal tract
