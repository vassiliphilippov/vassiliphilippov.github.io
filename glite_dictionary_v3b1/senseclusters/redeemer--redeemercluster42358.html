redeemer

noun
person who saves
5.6 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààdim…ôr / (AmE), / r…™ÀàdiÀêm…ô / (BrE)
a person who saves, rescues, or delivers someone or something from harm, danger, or difficulty

Examples

A redeemer of people
The villagers regarded the doctor as their redeemer after he stopped the epidemic.
In many stories, a redeemer appears when hope seems lost.
Some saw the new policy as a redeemer for the struggling economy.

Synonyms

  • savior
    3.2 9.50/m
    a savior is often used for a person who saves others, sometimes in religious stories, while a redeemer feels more formal and spiritual.
  • rescuer
    1.4 3.87/m
    a rescuer rushes to save people in emergencies or accidents, while a redeemer suggests broader saving from trouble or sin.
  • deliverer
    5.9 0.26/m
    a deliverer frees or brings someone out of danger, often in formal or old-fashioned speech, while a redeemer carries moral or religious weight.
  • saver
    4.6 0.29/m
    a saver protects or keeps things from loss in everyday situations, while a redeemer usually means someone who rescues from real danger or sin.
