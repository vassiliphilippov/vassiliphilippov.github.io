redeemer

noun
person who saves
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdimər / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːmə / (BrE)
a person who saves, rescues, or delivers someone or something from harm, danger, or difficulty

Examples

A redeemer of people
The villagers regarded the doctor as their redeemer after he stopped the epidemic.
In many stories, a redeemer appears when hope seems lost.
Some saw the new policy as a redeemer for the struggling economy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oppressor - a person who treats others cruelly or unfairly
  • synonym: rescuer - a person who saves someone from a dangerous or difficult situation
  • synonym: savior - a person who saves someone or something from danger or difficulty
