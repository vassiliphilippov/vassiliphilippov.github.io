tactus

noun
musical pulse
Pronunciation: / ˈtæktəs / (AmE), / ˈtæktəs / (BrE)
a steady beat or pulse used to measure time in early music, especially during the Renaissance and Baroque periods

Examples

Musical tactus
The choir followed the conductor's tactus to stay together during the performance.
In Renaissance music, the tactus helped musicians keep a consistent tempo.
Learning to feel the tactus is important for interpreting early Baroque pieces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat - the basic unit of time in music
  • synonym: rhythm - a pattern of sounds and silences in music
