Great Pyramid

noun
the largest Egyptian pyramid built for Khufu
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt ˈpɪrəmɪd / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈpɪərəmɪd / (BrE)
The largest and most famous Egyptian pyramid, built as a royal tomb for Pharaoh Khufu (Cheops) around 2570 BC, with a square base and four triangular sides.

Examples

The Great Pyramid of Giza
Archaeologists continue to study the structure of the Great Pyramid to uncover its construction secrets.
Millions of tourists visit the Great Pyramid of Giza each year to marvel at its ancient architecture.
The Great Pyramid stands as one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
