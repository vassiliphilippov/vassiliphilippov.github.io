modulator

noun
regulating substance
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.dʒə.leɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.dʒə.leɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a substance or factor that changes or regulates a biological or physiological process, such as neurotransmission or immune response

Examples

Neural modulator
The scientist developed a new modulator to help control abnormal nerve signals in the brain.
Certain drugs act as a modulator of the immune system, reducing inflammation in patients with autoimmune diseases.
Researchers are studying how this modulator affects the communication between different types of cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a process
  • synonym: regulator - a device or substance that controls or maintains a process
