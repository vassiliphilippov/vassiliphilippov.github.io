Ciliata

noun
class of ciliated protozoa
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪliˈeɪtə / (AmE), / sɪlɪˈɑːtə / (BrE)
a class of protozoa characterized by the presence of cilia or hairlike appendages covering all or part of the body during at least one stage of the life cycle

Examples

Ciliata species
Many species in the Ciliata group play important roles in aquatic ecosystems.
Scientists studied the movement of Ciliata under the microscope to understand their locomotion.
The classification of Ciliata has been revised as genetic analysis has improved.
