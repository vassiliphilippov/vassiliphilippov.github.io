peptide linkage

noun
chemical bond between amino acids in proteins
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛp.taɪd ˈlɪŋ.kɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpɛp.taɪd ˈlɪŋ.kɪdʒ / (BrE)
the chemical bond formed between the carboxyl group of one amino acid and the amino group of another, linking them in a protein or peptide chain

Examples

Peptide linkage formation
During protein synthesis, a peptide linkage is formed between two amino acids.
Each protein molecule consists of amino acids connected by a peptide linkage.
Researchers studied the stability of the peptide linkage under different chemical conditions.
