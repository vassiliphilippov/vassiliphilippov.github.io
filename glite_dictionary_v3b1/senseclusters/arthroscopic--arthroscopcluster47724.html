arthroscopic

adjective
joint surgery method
5.3
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.θrə.ˈskɑːp.ɪk/ (AmE), /ɑː.θrə.ˈskɒp.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to a type of surgery that uses a small camera inserted into a joint to diagnose or treat problems

Examples

An arthroscopic surgery
The doctor recommended an arthroscopic procedure to repair the torn ligament in her shoulder.
Recovery time after arthroscopic surgery is usually shorter than with traditional methods.
He was able to walk again soon after his arthroscopic knee operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minimally invasive - involving small incisions and minimal damage to the body
  • antonym: open surgery - surgery performed through a large incision
