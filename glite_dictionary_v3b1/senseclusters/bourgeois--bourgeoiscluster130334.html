bourgeois

noun
member of the middle class
8.2 1.31/m
Pronunciation: / b ärÀà íw…ë / (AmE), / Ààb ä…ô íw…ëÀê / (BrE)
a person who is a member of the middle class, especially in a capitalist society

Examples

A wealthy bourgeois
As a bourgeois, she enjoyed privileges not available to the working class.
He regarded himself as a proud bourgeois with solid values.
The revolution was aimed at the wealthy bourgeois who controlled industry.
As the city grew, many families joined the bourgeois and opened their own small businesses.
She married into a wealthy bourgeois family who owned several factories.
The bourgeois played a significant role in the expansion of the industrial economy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proletarian - a member of the working class
