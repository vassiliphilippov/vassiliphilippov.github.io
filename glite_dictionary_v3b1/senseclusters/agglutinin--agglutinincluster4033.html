agglutinin

noun
antibody causing clumping of cells
8.4
Pronunciation: / əˈɡluː.tən.ɪn / (AmE), / əˈɡluː.tɪ.nɪn / (BrE)
an antibody that causes particles, such as bacteria or red blood cells, to clump together by binding to their surface antigens

Examples

Agglutinin causes clumping
During the test, agglutinin caused the red blood cells to clump together visibly.
Some diseases, such as autoimmune hemolytic anemia, are associated with the presence of cold agglutinins.
The laboratory detected agglutinin in the patient's blood sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to neutralize pathogens
  • antonym: antigen - a substance that induces an immune response, often targeted by antibodies
