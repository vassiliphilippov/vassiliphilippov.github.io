crocket

noun
ornamental curved foliage in architecture
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑːkɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɒkɪt / (BrE)
an architectural ornament in the form of curved, stylized foliage, typically projecting from the sloping edges of spires, pinnacles, or gables

Examples

Stone crockets on gable
A row of stone crockets ran along the edges of the gothic gable.
Medieval architects used crockets to add decorative detail to rooflines.
The cathedral's spire was adorned with intricately carved crockets.
