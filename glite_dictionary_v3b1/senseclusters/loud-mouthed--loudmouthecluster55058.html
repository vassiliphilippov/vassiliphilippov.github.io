loud-mouthed

adjective
speaking noisily and offensively
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlaʊd ˌmaʊθt / (AmE), / ˈlaʊd ˌmaʊθt / (BrE)
tending to speak noisily and offensively; given to making loud or rude remarks.

Examples

loud-mouthed troublemaker
He was known as a loud-mouthed troublemaker at school and often drew attention for the wrong reasons.
The sports commentator was criticized for his loud-mouthed remarks during the game.
Tired of his loud-mouthed colleague, she asked for a different desk across the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boisterous - noisy, energetic, and cheerful
  • antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
