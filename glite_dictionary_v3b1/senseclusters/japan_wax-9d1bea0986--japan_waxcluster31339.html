Japan wax

noun
yellow wax from sumac berries, used in polishes
5.8
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæn wæks / (AmE), / dʒəˈpan waks / (BrE)
a yellowish wax obtained from the berries of some sumac trees, used in making polishes, candles, and other products

Examples

Japan wax berries
Candlemakers sometimes use Japan wax for its excellent burning qualities.
Traditional furniture polish often contains Japan wax for its smooth finish.
You can find Japan wax listed as an ingredient in some specialty crayons.
