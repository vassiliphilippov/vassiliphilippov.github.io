parietal pleura

noun
lining of chest wall and diaphragm
7.7
Pronunciation: / pəˈraɪət̬l ˈplʊrə / (AmE), / pəˈraɪətl ˈplʊərə / (BrE)
the layer of the pleura that lines the inner chest wall and covers the diaphragm

Examples

The parietal pleura layer
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the parietal pleura lining the chest cavity.
Pain from the parietal pleura is often sharp because it is innervated by somatic nerves.
The parietal pleura and the visceral pleura together form the pleural sac around the lungs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: visceral pleura - the layer of pleura covering the lungs
