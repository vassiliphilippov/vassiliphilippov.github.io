diapsid reptile

noun
reptile with two skull openings
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæp.sɪd ˈrɛp.taɪl / (AmE), / daɪˈap.sɪd ˈrɛp.taɪl / (BrE)
a reptile with a pair of openings in the skull behind each eye, belonging to the clade Diapsida

Examples

Ancient diapsid reptiles
Crocodiles, birds, and tuataras are living descendants of ancient diapsid reptiles.
Fossil evidence shows that early diapsid reptiles gave rise to modern lizards and snakes.
The skull structure of a diapsid reptile is characterized by two temporal openings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parapsid reptile - reptile with a single opening in the skull behind each eye
