radio astronomy

noun
astronomy using radio waves
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊ əˈstrɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊ əˈstrɒnəmi / (BrE)
The branch of astronomy concerned with detecting and studying the radio waves emitted by celestial bodies.

Examples

Radio astronomy studies waves
Radio astronomy allows scientists to observe cosmic phenomena invisible to optical telescopes.
Pulsars were first discovered through advances in radio astronomy.
The university's observatory has a strong focus on radio astronomy research.
