rabbit-eye blueberry

noun
blueberry shrub native to southeastern U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˈræbɪt aɪ ˈbluːbɛri / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt aɪ ˈbluːbəri / (BrE)
a shrub (Vaccinium virgatum) native to the southeastern United States, grown commercially for its small, sweet berries, especially used by the canning industry

Examples

Rabbit-eye blueberry bushes
Rabbit-eye blueberry bushes thrive in the warm, humid climates of the southeastern United States.
The commercial production of rabbit-eye blueberries supports many local growers in Georgia and Mississippi.
These muffins are made with fresh rabbit-eye blueberries picked from a nearby farm.
