whistle

noun
act of signalling by whistling or whistle blow
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
a high-pitched sound made by whistling, by a device, or by something moving quickly through the air

Examples

A loud whistle
At noon, the factory whistle sounded, and all the workers headed to lunch.
The referee's whistle brought the first half of the match to an end.
We waited for the train whistle before crossing the tracks.
A faint whistle signaled that the kettle was ready to pour.
The whistle of the train startled the waiting passengers.
We heard the sharp whistle of the wind outside the cabin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hiss - sharp sibilant sound like that of the letter 's', often made by escaping gas or steam
  • synonym: signal - an action or sound that gives information or instructions
