personification

noun
representation of ideas as people
Pronunciation: / pɚˌsɑnəfəˈkeɪʃən /; / pərˌsɑːnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɒnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of representing an abstract idea or nonhuman thing as if it were a person, especially in literature

Examples

Personification in poetry
In literature, personification can make abstract ideas more relatable to readers.
The author used personification to give the wind human-like emotions.
The phrase 'time waits for no one' is an example of personification.
Describing justice as blind is a classic example of personification.
In poetry, personification helps readers connect with abstract concepts.
The author uses personification to give the river a life of its own.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthropomorphism - attributing human traits or behavior to animals or objects
  • synonym: metaphor - figure of speech in which a word is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable
