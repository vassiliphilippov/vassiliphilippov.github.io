reprosecute

verb
to prosecute again
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈprɑːsɪˌkjuːt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈprɒsɪˌkjuːt / (BrE)
to bring legal action against someone again for the same or a related offense

Examples

To reprosecute a case
After the jury could not reach a verdict, the state decided to reprosecute the case.
The judge ruled that it would be unfair to reprosecute the defendant for the same charges.
Legal experts debated whether the government should reprosecute after the mistrial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquit - to declare someone not guilty of a crime
  • synonym: prosecute - to bring legal action against someone for a crime
