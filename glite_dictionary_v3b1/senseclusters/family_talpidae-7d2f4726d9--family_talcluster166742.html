family Talpidae

noun
the family of mammals known as moles
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli tælˈpɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tælˈpɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, burrowing mammals commonly known as moles, characterized by their velvety fur, reduced eyesight, and powerful forelimbs adapted for digging.

Examples

family Talpidae mammals
Members of the family Talpidae are highly adapted to a subterranean lifestyle.
Research on the family Talpidae has contributed to our understanding of evolutionary biology and adaptation.
The family Talpidae includes over 40 species distributed across Europe, Asia, and North America.
