human papilloma virus

noun
virus linked to warts and some cancers
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmən pəˌpɪləmə ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmən pəˌpɪləmə ˈvʌɪərəs / (BrE)
Any of a group of viruses that infect human skin or mucous membranes, some of which are associated with genital or oral cancers and benign genital tumors.

Examples

The human papilloma virus infection
A vaccine is available to help prevent infection with the human papilloma virus.
Doctors recommend regular screening for women to detect changes linked to the human papilloma virus.
The human papilloma virus can cause both benign warts and certain types of cancer.
