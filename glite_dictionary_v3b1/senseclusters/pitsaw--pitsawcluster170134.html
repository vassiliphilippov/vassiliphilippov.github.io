pitsaw

noun
large two-handed saw for cutting logs
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈpɪtˌsɔː / (BrE)
a large two-handed saw used for cutting logs into planks, with one person standing above the log and another below, often in a pit

Examples

A pitsaw tool
Before modern machinery, planks were often made by hand with a pitsaw.
Operating a pitsaw required great strength and coordination from both men involved.
The old lumber mill still displays a massive pitsaw used by workers a century ago.
