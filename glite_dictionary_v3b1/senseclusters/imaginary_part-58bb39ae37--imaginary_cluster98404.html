imaginary part

noun
component of a complex number multiplied by i
6.7 6.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmædʒ.ɪ.nər.iː pɑːrt/ /; / /ɪ.ˈmædʒ.ə.nər.iː pɑːrt/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmædʒ.ɪ.nə.riː pɑːt/ /; / /ɪ.ˈmædʒ.ə.nə.riː pɑːt/ (BrE)
the part of a complex number that is multiplied by the square root of -1 (i)

Examples

The imaginary part of a number
In electrical engineering, the imaginary part helps describe alternating current.
The imaginary part of the number 5 + 3i is 3.
When adding complex numbers, you add their real parts and their imaginary parts separately.
The imaginary part becomes zero when the complex number is purely real.
The imaginary part of 3 + 4i is 4.
When solving the equation, make sure to separate the real and imaginary part.

Related Terms

  • antonym: real part - the component of a complex number not multiplied by i
