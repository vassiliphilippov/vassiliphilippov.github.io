penicillinase-resistant antibiotic

noun
penicillin that resists deactivation by penicillinase
7.9
Pronunciation: / pɛnəˌsɪlɪneɪz rɪˈzɪstənt ˌæn(t)ɪˈbaɪɑːtɪk / (AmE), / pɛnɪˌsɪlɪneɪz rɪˈzɪstənt ˌæn.tɪˈbaɪ.ɒ.tɪk / (BrE)
an antibiotic derived from penicillin that is not deactivated by penicillinase enzymes, and therefore effective against certain resistant bacteria

Examples

A penicillinase-resistant antibiotic
Doctors may prescribe a penicillinase-resistant antibiotic when ordinary penicillins are ineffective.
Methicillin is a well-known penicillinase-resistant antibiotic used to treat certain bacterial infections.
The development of penicillinase-resistant antibiotics has been crucial in combating resistant strains of bacteria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that inhibits the growth of or destroys microorganisms
  • degree lower: penicillin - an antibiotic derived from the mold Penicillium, original form susceptible to penicillinase
