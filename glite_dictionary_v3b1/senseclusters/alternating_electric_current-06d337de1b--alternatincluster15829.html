alternating electric current

noun
electric current that reverses direction
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɔltɚˌneɪtɪŋ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkɝənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːltəˌneɪtɪŋ ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
An electric current that repeatedly reverses direction at regular intervals, typically in a sinusoidal pattern.

Examples

Alternating electric current flow
Generators at power stations produce alternating electric current for distribution to homes and businesses.
Most household appliances are designed to operate on alternating electric current.
The power lines in residential areas typically carry alternating electric current.

Related Terms

  • synonym: AC - abbreviation for alternating current
  • antonym: direct current - an electric current flowing in one direction only
