in town

adverb
in the city center
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪn taʊn / (AmE), / ɪn taʊn / (BrE)
in or within the area of a town or city (not in the countryside)

Examples

Shops in town
She works in town and commutes every day from the suburbs.
We decided to stay in town for the weekend instead of traveling.
There is a new restaurant in town that everyone is talking about.
The new restaurant is finally open in town and everyone is excited to try it.
She prefers living in town because everything she needs is within walking distance.
The bus only runs in town, so you will need a car to visit the countryside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: out of town - away from the city or town
  • antonym: rural - relating to the countryside rather than the town
