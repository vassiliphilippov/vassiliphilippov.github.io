maniac

adjective
wildly disordered or frenzied (adj)
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪniˌæk / (AmE), / ˈmanɪak / (BrE)
(of behavior or emotion) characterized by wild disorder or frenzied excitement.

Examples

Maniac energy
He described the storm as having a maniac energy, unpredictable and terrifying.
Her maniac laughter filled the silent room, startling everyone around her.
The fans erupted into a maniac frenzy when the team scored the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • synonym: frantic - wild or distraught with fear, anxiety, or other emotion
  • synonym: maniacal - showing wild or violent behavior
