fustian language

noun
pretentious or inflated language
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfʌs.tʃən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈfʌs.tɪ.ən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈfʌs.tɪ.ən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈfʌs.tʃən ˈlæŋ.gwɪdʒ/ (BrE)
Language that is pompous, pretentious, or inflated, often intended to impress but lacking real substance.

Examples

Fustian language style
Critics accused the novel of being filled with fustian language instead of genuine emotion.
Rather than impressing the audience, his fustian language made his argument sound insincere.
The politician's speech was criticized for its fustian language that obscured his real message.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plain language - language that is clear and straightforward
