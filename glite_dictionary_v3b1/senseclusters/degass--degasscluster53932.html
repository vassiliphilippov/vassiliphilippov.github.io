degass

verb
to release gas
Pronunciation: / diːˈɡæs / (AmE), / diːˈɡæs / (BrE)
to remove or release gas from a substance, often by heating or to prevent bubbles or unwanted reactions

Examples

To degass a liquid
When the solution is heated, it will degass and release bubbles into the air.
Volcanic rocks often degass as they cool, affecting the surrounding environment.
Engineers must allow the system to degass before performing maintenance.
Before starting the experiment, the chemist degassed the solution to ensure accuracy.
It is important to degass the water to prevent bubbles from forming during the process.
The laboratory technician degasses samples regularly to maintain equipment performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aerate - to introduce air or gas into a liquid or substance
  • synonym: purge - to remove unwanted substances from something
  • antonym: trap - to prevent gas or liquid from escaping
  • synonym: vent - to release gas or vapor from a confined space
