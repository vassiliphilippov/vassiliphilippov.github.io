secondary winding

noun
coil inducing current from the primary winding
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkənˌdɛri ˈwaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndəri ˈwaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
a coil in an electrical transformer or similar device in which an electric current is induced from the primary winding

Examples

The secondary winding coil
An electrician tested whether the secondary winding was functioning correctly in the transformer.
If the secondary winding is disconnected, no current will flow through the output circuit.
The voltage in the secondary winding depends on the number of turns relative to the primary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: primary winding - the coil in which current is first applied to induce current elsewhere
