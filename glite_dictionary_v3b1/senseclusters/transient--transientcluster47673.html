transient

noun

9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtræn.ʒənt/ /; / /ˈtræn.zɪ.ənt/ /; / /ˈtræn.ʃənt/ (AmE), /ˈtræn.zɪ.ənt/ /; / /ˈtræn.ʃənt/ /; / /ˈtræn.sɪ.ənt/ (BrE)
(physics) A short-lived oscillation in a system that occurs following a sudden change in voltage, current, or load.

Examples

Transient voltage spike
Electrical equipment can be damaged by a sudden transient on the power line.
Engineers analyzed the transient to understand the system's response to the power surge.
The amplifier produced a noticeable transient when it was first switched on.
