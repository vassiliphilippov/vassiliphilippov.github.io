whistle

verb
move making a whistling sound
3.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪs.əl/ /; / /ˈhwɪs.əl/ (AmE), /ˈwɪs.əl/ /; / /ˈhwɪs.əl/ (BrE)
to move quickly making a high-pitched, whistling sound, or to cause something to move away as if with a whistling sound (literal or figurative)

Examples

Wind whistles past
A cold wind whistled through the cracks in the old cabin walls.
The bullets whistled past his head as he ran for cover.
The train whistled down the tracks, startling the birds nearby.
His carefree attitude quickly whistled away any doubts about the plan.
She tried to whistle her fears away with a cheerful tune.
The coach's sharp words whistled the tension out of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blow away - to drive away or remove with a sudden current of air (or figuratively)
  • synonym: whoosh - to move quickly making a rushing sound
