spearfish

noun
a type of large pelagic billfish
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspɪəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, fast-moving pelagic fish related to marlins and sailfishes, with a long, pointed bill and a reduced first dorsal fin, found in oceans worldwide but uncommon.

Examples

A spearfish swims
Researchers track the migration patterns of spearfish to better understand their behavior.
The fisherman was thrilled to catch a rare spearfish off the coast of Hawaii.
Unlike marlins, the spearfish has a noticeably shorter dorsal fin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marlin - a large, fast marine fish with an elongated upper jaw
  • synonym: sailfish - a large, fast fish with a long bill and tall dorsal fin
