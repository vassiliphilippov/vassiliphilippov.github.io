anastomose

verb
join or connect by anastomosis
Pronunciation: / əˈnæstəˌmoʊz / (AmE), / əˈnæstəməʊz /; / əˈnastəməʊz / (BrE)
to join or connect tubular structures (such as blood vessels or ducts) to form a continuous channel or network, either surgically or naturally

Examples

To anastomose blood vessels
The surgeon will anastomose the arteries to restore blood flow.
Veins and arteries often anastomose to ensure continuous circulation.
When two segments of bowel are anastomosed, it allows passage through the digestive tract.
During surgery, the doctors carefully anastomosed the two ends of the intestine.
Roots of neighboring trees may anastomose underground to share nutrients and water.
Several small blood vessels anastomose to form a larger vein in this region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: connect - to join together so as to provide access or communication
  • synonym: fuse - to join together to form a single entity
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart
