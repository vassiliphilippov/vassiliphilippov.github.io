antinarrative

noun
nontraditional storytelling
9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ (BrE)
a literary work or style that intentionally rejects or subverts traditional storytelling structure and conventions

Examples

An antinarrative novel
The author's latest book is an antinarrative that challenges readers' expectations of plot and character.
Some critics praised the film's antinarrative approach for its originality and boldness.
Students discussed how the poem's antinarrative structure made it difficult to follow a clear storyline.
