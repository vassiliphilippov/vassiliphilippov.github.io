Origenist

noun
follower of Origen
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrɪdʒənɪst / (AmE), / ˈɒrɪdʒənɪst / (BrE)
a follower of the early Christian theologian Origen or someone who supports his teachings

Examples

An Origenist thinker
The council condemned the beliefs held by the Origenists.
As an Origenist, he often debated theological issues with other scholars.
Many early church leaders disagreed with the Origenist interpretation of scripture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Platonist - a follower of the philosophy of Plato
  • antonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to orthodox religious doctrine
