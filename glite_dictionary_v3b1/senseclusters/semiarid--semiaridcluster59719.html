semiarid

adjective
partially dry; with low rainfall
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈɛrɪd, ˌsɛmaɪˈɛrɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈærɪd, ˌsɛmaɪˈærɪd / (BrE)
(of a climate or region) having low annual rainfall, but not as dry as a desert.

Examples

Semi-arid region
Farmers in semiarid regions often have to rely on irrigation systems to grow their crops.
The semiarid grasslands only receive just enough rain to support hardy shrubs and drought-resistant plants.
The southwestern section of the country has a semiarid climate, making water conservation crucial.
