cotyloid joint

noun
type of freely moving ball-and-socket joint
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.tɪ.lɔɪd ˌdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.tɪ.lɔɪd ˌdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a type of freely moving joint where the rounded head of one bone fits into a cup-like socket of another bone, allowing movement in multiple directions

Examples

A cotyloid joint structure
Anatomists classify the shoulder as a cotyloid joint due to its cup-and-ball structure.
Damage to the cotyloid joint may severely limit an individual's mobility.
The hip is a classic example of a cotyloid joint, enabling a wide range of movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ball-and-socket joint - a joint allowing movement in many directions, such as the hip or shoulder
