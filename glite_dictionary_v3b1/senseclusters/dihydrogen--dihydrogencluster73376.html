dihydrogen

adjective
containing two hydrogens
6.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈhaɪdrədʒən / (AmE), / daɪˈhɪdrədʒən / (BrE)
used in chemical names to indicate the presence of two hydrogen atoms in a compound

Examples

Dihydrogen phosphate
The solution was labeled as dihydrogen phosphate to indicate its chemical composition.
In chemistry class, we learned that dihydrogen sulfide contains two hydrogen atoms bonded to sulfur.
The prefix dihydrogen is used in compound names to show the presence of two hydrogens.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monohydrogen - containing one hydrogen atom
  • degree higher: trihydrogen - containing three hydrogen atoms
