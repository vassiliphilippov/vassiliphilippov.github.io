cache

verb
to store for quick access
Pronunciation: / kæʃ / (AmE), / kæʃ / (BrE)
to store data or objects in a hidden or reserved place for quick access later

Examples

To cache files
Web browsers often cache images to speed up page loading.
The application will cache your login details for faster access next time.
To improve performance, the system caches frequently used files on the local drive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retrieve - to get something back from storage
  • synonym: store - to put something away for future use
