write home about

verb
to be noteworthy or remarkable (often used negatively)
Pronunciation: / raɪt hoʊm əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / raɪt həʊm əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to be particularly noteworthy or remarkable; usually used in negative constructions to mean something is unremarkable or not impressive

Examples

Not writing home about
Her performance in the play was solid, though not really worth writing home about.
The food was good, but honestly, it wasn’t anything to write home about.
The scenery was beautiful—now that’s something you could definitely write home about.
