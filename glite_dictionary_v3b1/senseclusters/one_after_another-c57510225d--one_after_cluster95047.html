one after another

adverb
in quick succession, sequentially
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: / wʌn ˈæftɚ əˈnʌðɚ /; / wʌn ˈæftər əˈnʌðər / (AmE), / wʌn ˈɑːftə əˈnʌðə / (BrE)
happening or appearing in succession, with each thing or event following the previous one

Examples

One after another events
Failures kept coming one after another, but he refused to give up.
The fireworks exploded one after another, lighting up the night sky.
The students entered the classroom one after another, filling every seat.
Books toppled from the shelf one after another as the earthquake hit.
He answered the questions one after another without hesitation.
The fire alarms went off one after another during the drill.
