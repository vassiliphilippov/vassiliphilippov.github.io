Synonyms

  • consecutively
    4.1 0.6/m
    consecutively describes actions with no interruption in time, while one after another is a common phrase for things happening in succession.
  • successively
    4.4 0.9/m
    successively means items follow one after the other in order, while one after another is the simple everyday phrase for that sequence.
  • sequentially
    4.5 1/m
    sequentially suggests a planned or fixed order of steps, while one after another is a general term for things following each other.
  • back-to-back
    3.7 4/m
    back-to-back means events occur immediately with no gap between them, while one after another is more general and can allow pauses.
  • one by one
    1.4 9/m
    one by one means doing things individually in turn, while one after another emphasizes a continuous sequence of events.
