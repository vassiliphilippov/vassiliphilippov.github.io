dihydrochloride

noun
compound with two hydrochlorides
Pronunciation: / daɪˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (AmE), / daɪˌhaɪdrəˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed when two hydrochloride groups are added to a molecule, often to improve solubility

Examples

A dihydrochloride compound
The active ingredient is provided as a dihydrochloride to help it dissolve in water.
Many medications use the dihydrochloride form to increase their stability and absorption.
Researchers synthesized the dihydrochloride salt to test its effectiveness in the new drug.
