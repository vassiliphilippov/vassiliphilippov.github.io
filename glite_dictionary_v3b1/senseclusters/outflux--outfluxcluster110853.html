outflux

noun
outward flow amount
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌflʌks / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌflʌks / (BrE)
the net amount of a quantity, such as energy or particles, passing outward through a surface in physics or mathematics

Examples

Outflux of heat
The outflux of heat from the system was measured to determine energy loss.
Researchers analyzed the outflux of particles across the membrane in their experiment.
A high outflux of water vapor can affect the local climate conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flux - the rate of flow of a property through a surface
  • antonym: influx - the net amount of a quantity entering a surface
