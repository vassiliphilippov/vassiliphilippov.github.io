naturalism

noun
philosophical view; world explained scientifically
3.4 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈnæ.tʃə.rəl.ɪz.əm/ /; / /ˈnæ.tʃ.rəl.ɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ˈnæ.tʃə.rəl.ɪz.əm/ /; / /ˈnæ.tʃ.rəl.ɪz.əm/ (BrE)
(philosophy) The doctrine that the world and everything in it can be explained through scientific observation and laws, without relying on spiritual or supernatural explanations.

Examples

Philosophical naturalism
Because of his commitment to naturalism, he rejects the idea of miracles.
Many scientists adhere to naturalism when interpreting the origins of the universe.
The debate between naturalism and supernaturalism has shaped much of modern philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: materialism - the doctrine that only physical matter exists, with no spiritual or supernatural elements
  • antonym: supernaturalism - the doctrine that supernatural forces or entities are needed to explain the world
