gnarled

adjective
rough and knotted in appearance
7.1 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / n…ërld / (AmE), / n…ëÀêld / (BrE)
(of a tree, branch, or person, especially an old one) having a rough, twisted appearance with prominent knots or bumpy areas.

Examples

A gnarled tree
An ancient, gnarled oak stood alone in the middle of the field, its branches twisted and thick.
She placed her gnarled hands gently on the table, her fingers twisted from years of hard work.
The old man's gnarled face broke into a warm, toothless smile.

Synonyms

  • knotted
    3.9 0.00/m
    knotted describes wood or hands with clear knots and bumps, while gnarled suggests a rough, twisted appearance.
  • gnarly
    8.4 0.43/m
    gnarly sounds informal and often describes rough, knotted bark or shapes, while gnarled refers to very old, twisted wood or people.
  • knotty
    7.0 0.40/m
    knotty means full of knots, lumps, or tangles often in wood or hair, while gnarled implies a twisted, rough, aged look.
