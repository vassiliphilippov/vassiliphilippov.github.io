nominally

adverb
in name only; not in reality
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑː.mə.nəl.i / (AmE), / ˈnɒ.mɪ.nəl.i / (BrE)
In name or title only, but not in reality; as a mere formality.

Examples

Nominally in charge
Nominally, she runs the department, but the real decisions are made by her assistant.
The club is nominally open to everyone, but few outsiders ever join.
They are nominally independent, though heavily influenced by external powers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: officially - according to proper authority or formal procedure
