galvanize

verb
stimulate into action
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡælvəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɡælvənaɪz / (BrE)
to shock, inspire, or excite someone into taking action

Examples

Galvanize the team
Her determination galvanized the team, leading to a remarkable comeback victory.
The discovery of fraud galvanized investors to take immediate legal action.
The president's speech managed to galvanize the entire nation into demanding reform.
The coach's passionate speech galvanized the team to play their best in the final match.
News of the environmental crisis galvanized the community into organizing a cleanup event.
Her determination galvanized others to join the campaign for better healthcare.
Her sudden resignation galvanised the company into addressing the underlying issues.
Seeing the devastation firsthand can really galvanise people to donate and volunteer.
The speech managed to galvanise the crowd into demanding change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to make someone less willing to do something
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular action
  • synonym: incite - to encourage someone to act, especially in a forceful way
  • synonym: motivate - to provide someone with a reason for doing something
  • synonym: stimulate - to encourage something to begin or develop
