endosperm

noun
nutritive seed tissue in plants
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndoʊˌspɝm / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌspɜːm / (BrE)
the nutritive tissue surrounding and nourishing the embryo within the seeds of flowering plants

Examples

The endosperm tissue
The endosperm provides nutrients to the developing plant embryo.
The coconut's edible part is mainly made up of endosperm.
Wheat grain contains a large amount of endosperm, which is milled to produce flour.
