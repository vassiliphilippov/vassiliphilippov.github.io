hoick

noun
sudden pull or jerk
Pronunciation: / hɔɪk / (AmE), / hɔɪk / (BrE)
a sudden, forceful pull or jerk, especially to lift or move something quickly.

Examples

A quick hoick
With a quick hoick, she lifted the heavy bag onto the table.
The mechanic gave the engine a sharp hoick to free the stuck part.
He managed to move the box with one strong hoick of his arms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tug - a strong or sudden pull
  • synonym: yank - a quick, forceful pull
