(of a right or property) impossible to take away, deny, or transfer to another person.

Examples

An unalienable right
Many believe that freedom of speech is an unalienable right belonging to every person.
Some legal philosophers argue that human dignity is an unalienable aspect of our identity.
The Declaration of Independence refers to certain unalienable rights, such as life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.

Synonyms

  • inalienable
    8.0 2.22/m
    inalienable means a right cannot be taken or transferred and is the more common legal word, while unalienable states the same.
  • nonforfeitable
    8.5 0.01/m
    nonforfeitable refers to money or benefits that law protects from loss, while unalienable refers to basic rights that cannot be taken away.
  • indefeasible
    9.9 0.05/m
    indefeasible describes something that cannot be annulled or made invalid in law, while unalienable focuses on a right that cannot be taken or transferred.
  • inviolable
    8.6 0.67/m
    inviolable describes something sacred or not to be broken, while unalienable refers specifically to rights that cannot be taken away or transferred.
