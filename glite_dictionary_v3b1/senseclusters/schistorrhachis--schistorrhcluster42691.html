schistorrhachis

noun
malformation of the vertebrae (birth defect)
9.9
Pronunciation: /skɪs.tə.ˈræk.ɪs/ /; / /skɪs.toʊ.ˈræk.ɪs/ (AmE), /skɪs.tə.ˈræk.ɪs/ /; / /skɪs.təʊ.ˈræk.ɪs/ (BrE)
A congenital defect where part of the vertebral column is malformed, typically with few symptoms unless multiple vertebrae are involved.

Examples

Congenital schistorrhachis defect
Advanced imaging techniques are often used to diagnose schistorrhachis before birth.
The prenatal scan revealed schistorrhachis in the fetus's lumbar spine.
Unless accompanied by other abnormalities, schistorrhachis may go unnoticed throughout life.
