epigenesis

noun
gradual organism development
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which an organism develops from a fertilized egg through gradual and progressive changes

Examples

Embryo epigenesis
The theory of epigenesis changed how scientists understood the development of living organisms.
Modern biology supports epigenesis as the main explanation for how complex life forms arise.
Research in embryology often discusses the principles of epigenesis and how cells differentiate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: development - the process of growth or change over time
  • antonym: preformation - the theory that organisms develop from miniature versions of themselves
