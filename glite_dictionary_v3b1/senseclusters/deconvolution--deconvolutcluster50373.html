deconvolution

noun
signal restoration process
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkɑːnvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkɒnvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
a process used to remove distortion or blurring from data, images, or signals to restore their original form

Examples

Deconvolution of images
The scientist used deconvolution to enhance the details in the microscopic images.
Modern telescopes rely on deconvolution algorithms to correct for atmospheric distortion.
Audio engineers apply deconvolution to remove echoes from recorded sound.
The deconvolution of the blurry photograph revealed important details that were previously hidden.
Scientists used deconvolution to clarify the noisy data from the telescope.
Accurate deconvolution is essential for analyzing complex chemical spectra in the laboratory.

  • synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original state
