bootful

adjective
plentiful amount
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtfəl / (AmE), / ˈbuːtfʊl / (BrE)
large in amount; plentiful or enough to fill a boot (informal, often used humorously).

Examples

A bootful harvest
After a day of apple picking, they returned with a bootful of fresh fruit.
The fishermen were delighted to catch a bootful of fish before noon.
We gathered a bootful of firewood to last us through the weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abundant - existing in large quantities
  • antonym: scarce - insufficient in quantity or number
