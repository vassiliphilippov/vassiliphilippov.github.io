antiplatelet

noun
blood clot prevention drug
5.1
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈpleɪt.lət/ /; / /æn.tɪ.ˈpleɪt.lɪt/ /; / /ən.tə.ˈpleɪt.lət/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈpleɪt.lət/ /; / /æn.tɪ.ˈpleɪt.lɪt/ /; / /ən.tə.ˈpleɪt.lət/ (BrE)
a drug or agent that prevents blood platelets from clumping together and forming clots

Examples

Take an antiplatelet
The doctor prescribed an antiplatelet to reduce the risk of stroke after the surgery.
Patients with heart disease often take an antiplatelet as part of their daily medication.
Taking an antiplatelet can help prevent dangerous blood clots from forming in the arteries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticoagulant - a drug that prevents blood from clotting
  • antonym: platelet aggregator - an agent that promotes platelets sticking together
