chironomid

noun
non-biting midge
9.9
Pronunciation: / kaɪˈrɑːnəmɪd / (AmE), / kɪˈrɒnəmɪd / (BrE)
a small non-biting midge (insect) of the family Chironomidae, found near water; can refer to the adult or its worm-like aquatic larva

Examples

During summer evenings, swarms of chironomids can be seen hovering above the water.
The aquarium store sells frozen chironomid larvae as a nutritious treat for tropical fish.
Scientists studied the life cycle of the chironomid to understand its role in freshwater ecosystems.
A red chironomid larva wriggled at the bottom of the pond, attracting hungry fish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloodworm - the red aquatic larva of a chironomid, used as fish food
  • synonym: midge - a small fly, often found near water or damp places
  • antonym: mosquito - a small flying insect that bites and feeds on blood
  • synonym: mosquito larva - the aquatic larval stage of a mosquito
