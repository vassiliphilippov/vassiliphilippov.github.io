nightglow

noun
faint night sky light
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtˌɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌɡləʊ / (BrE)
a faint natural light in the night sky caused by chemical reactions in the upper atmosphere

Examples

Atmospheric nightglow
The scientists observed the nightglow to gather data about the Earth's upper atmosphere.
On clear nights, a faint nightglow can sometimes be seen above the horizon.
Special cameras are used to capture images of the nightglow for research purposes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airglow - a faint emission of light by a planetary atmosphere
  • antonym: aurora - a natural light display in the sky, usually near the polar regions
