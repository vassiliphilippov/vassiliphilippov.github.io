auditory cortex

noun
part of brain processing hearing
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɔdəˌtɔri ˈkɔrtɛks / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt(ə)ri ˈkɔːtɛks / (BrE)
the part of the brain's cerebral cortex that processes auditory information from the ears

Examples

The auditory cortex area
Damage to the auditory cortex often results in difficulty processing complex sounds.
Researchers scanned the auditory cortex to study how the brain responds to different pitches.
The auditory cortex is essential for interpreting spoken language and music.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: primary auditory cortex - main region of the auditory cortex responsible for processing sound
