turn

noun
unexpected change or development
3.4 27/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn / (AmE), / t…úÀên / (BrE)
an unexpected or sudden change in a situation, event, or story

Examples

A sudden turn
After years of success, her career took a downward turn.
The conversation took a surprising turn when he mentioned his new job.
With another turn of events, the company was suddenly facing bankruptcy.
Just when you thought the hero would win, the story took another turn.
The investigation took a dark turn after new evidence was uncovered.
The plot took a surprising turn that left the audience speechless.

Synonyms

  • turn of events
    4.1 1.63/m
    turn of events is a phrase for a sudden, often important change in what happens, while turn is a short, general word.
  • twist
    4.1 14/m
    twist is an unexpected change often used in stories to surprise readers, while turn is a general sudden change in a situation.
  • unexpected twist
    4.2 0.09/m
    unexpected twist is a surprise event in a story that reverses expectations, while turn is a plain word for any sudden change.
