Andromeda

proper noun
princess in Greek mythology rescued by Perseus
8.6
Pronunciation: / ænˈdrɑːmədə / (AmE), / ænˈdrɒmɪdə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) an Ethiopian princess, daughter of Cassiopeia, who was chained to a rock as a sacrifice to a sea monster, but was rescued by Perseus and became his wife.

Examples

Andromeda chained to a rock
According to myth, Andromeda was chained to a rock to appease a sea monster.
In the legend, Andromeda was saved from certain death by the hero Perseus.
The story of Andromeda and Perseus is depicted in many classical paintings.
