ally

noun
friendly nation; supporter
Pronunciation: / ˈæ.laɪ /; / ˈæˌlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈæ.laɪ /; / ˈælaɪ / (BrE)
a person, group, or country that supports or helps another, especially in a conflict or for a common purpose

Examples

A close ally
As an ally of France, Canada provided military support in the conflict.
Several small countries became allies to protect themselves from invasion.
The United States regarded Britain as its most important ally during World War II.
It's important to have an ally when facing tough challenges.
She became a trusted ally during the negotiations.
The two countries remained allies throughout the war.

  • degree lower: friend - a person or entity who supports or helps another
