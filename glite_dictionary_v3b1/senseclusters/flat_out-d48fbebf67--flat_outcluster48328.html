flat out

adverb
bluntly, directly, without pretense
5.8
Pronunciation: / flæt aʊt / (AmE), / flæt aʊt / (BrE)
in a completely direct or blunt manner, without trying to hide one's feelings or intentions

Examples

Say flat out the truth
He asked her flat out if she was lying to him.
If you don't want the job, just say so flat out rather than making excuses.
She told him flat out that she disagreed with his proposal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluntly - in a very direct and honest way, sometimes rudely so
  • synonym: roundly - in a vehement or outspoken manner
