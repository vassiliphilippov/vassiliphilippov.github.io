light

noun
the property of emitting or reflecting visible rays
Pronunciation: / laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
the natural agent that makes things visible; brightness from the sun, lamps, or other sources

Examples

Sun light
Scientists measured the amount of light reflected from the surface of the moon.
She preferred working by natural light rather than using artificial lamps.
The intense light from the sunrise filled the room with warmth.
Plants need light to perform photosynthesis and grow.
She adjusted the lamp to get better light for reading.
The early morning light streamed through the curtains.
A single beam of light shone through the narrow window, illuminating the room.
As the dawn broke, light slowly filled the valley, chasing away the shadows.
She waited at the edge of the forest until the light from the house was visible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brightness - the state or quality of being bright; giving out or reflecting a lot of light
  • antonym: darkness - absence of light
  • antonym: shade - relative darkness caused by blocking light, especially in art
