buy up

verb
purchase all or most to gain control
1.6
Pronunciation: / baɪ ʌp / (AmE), / baɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to buy all or most of something, especially to control or monopolize it

Examples

To buy up farmland
A foreign company has been quietly buying up farmland in the area.
The large corporation tried to buy up all the smaller competitors in the region.
When news of the takeover broke, investors started to buy up shares in the company.
A major publisher attempted to buy up all the rights to the author's books.
During the recession, wealthy investors began to buy up properties at low prices.
The government decided to buy up surplus crops from farmers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquire - to gain possession or control of, especially of companies or assets
  • synonym: buy - to purchase; to acquire by paying money
  • antonym: sell - to give something in exchange for money
