Iron Age

noun
last, worst age in classical mythology
4.6 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪərn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaɪən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
In classical mythology, the last and most corrupt age of the world, marked by decline and suffering.

Examples

Iron Age in myths
According to Ovid, humanity entered the Iron Age when justice and virtue had vanished.
Poets often contrast the Iron Age with the earlier Golden Age to highlight moral decline.
The Iron Age in mythology is a period of violence and hardship, unlike the peace of previous ages.
