keiretsu

noun
Japanese business group
Pronunciation: / keɪˈrɛtsu / (AmE), / keɪˈrɛtsu / (BrE)
a group of companies in Japan that are closely linked through business relationships and shareholdings, often working together and coordinated by a central company or bank

Examples

Japanese keiretsu group
The automotive keiretsu kept parts suppliers closely tied to the main manufacturer for years.

The keiretsu structure allowed firms to coordinate their strategies and support each other financially.
The keiretsu structure helped Japanese companies cooperate and share resources after World War II.
Many international firms study the keiretsu model to understand Japanese business networks.
A keiretsu often includes companies from different industries, all connected through financial ties.
Many Japanese automakers belong to a keiretsu that shares suppliers and technology.
The keiretsu structure helps companies support each other during economic downturns.
Foreign firms often find it difficult to enter markets dominated by a keiretsu.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartel - a group of independent companies that work together to control production or prices
  • synonym: conglomerate - a large corporation made up of several different companies
  • synonym: zaibatsu - a large Japanese business conglomerate, especially before World War II
