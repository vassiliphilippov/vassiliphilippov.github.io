Keplerian

adjective
telescope design type
9.6
Pronunciation: / kɛˈplɪriən / (AmE), / kɛˈplɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to an astronomical telescope design that uses a convex objective and a convex eyepiece, producing an inverted image

Examples

Keplerian telescope
The observatory features a large Keplerian telescope for public viewing of the planets.
Early astronomers preferred the Keplerian design for its wider field of view.
A Keplerian telescope produces an inverted image, unlike the Galilean type.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Galilean - relating to a telescope design using a convex objective and a concave eyepiece
