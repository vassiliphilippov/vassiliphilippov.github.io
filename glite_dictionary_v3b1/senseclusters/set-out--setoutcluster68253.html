set-out

noun
statement or exposition
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɛt aʊt / (BrE)
a clear statement or presentation of facts, reasons, or plans, often as an exposition or introduction.

Examples

A set-out of reasons
The report began with a detailed set-out of the project's objectives and expected outcomes.
Her set-out of the facts helped everyone understand the situation clearly.
Before the debate, the moderator gave a set-out of the main issues to be discussed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exposition - a detailed explanation or presentation of an idea or theory
  • synonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points
