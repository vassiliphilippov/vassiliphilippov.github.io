sear

verb

to burn, scorch, or dry out something with intense heat, often causing it to become brown, black, or withered

Examples

To sear meat
A careless spark from the campfire made her sleeve sear at the edge.
The intense heat caused the surface of the bread to sear quickly.
When exposed to the open flame, the paper began to sear before catching fire.
A forest fire can sear entire landscapes, reducing greenery to ash.
Chefs sear the steak to lock in juices before roasting it.
The intense sun can sear the ground, leaving it cracked and lifeless.
Drought and heat seared the once-lush fields into a barren wasteland.
If you leave the leaves in direct sun, the heat will quickly sear them.
The relentless desert winds sear the grasslands every summer.

Synonyms

  • scorch
    3.5 1.70/m
    scorch slightly burns or sings a surface causing browning, while sear uses intense heat to burn, blacken, or dry out quickly.
  • char
    4.6 3.62/m
    char burns or blackens a surface strongly, while sear can mean intense browning, blackening, or drying by very high heat.
  • blacken
    3.2 0.82/m
    blacken makes the surface dark or black from burning or cooking, while sear often browns and may also dry or char.
  • brown
    2.0 7.62/m
    brown makes a surface turn golden or brown by cooking or sun, while sear focuses on intense burning or drying.
  • parch
    7.0 0.09/m
    parch dries something completely by heat or sun, whereas sear uses intense heat to burn, scorch, or blacken surfaces.
