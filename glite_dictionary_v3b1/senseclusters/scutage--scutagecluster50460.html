scutage

noun
medieval tax for service
Pronunciation: / ˈskjuːtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskjuːtɪdʒ / (BrE)
a tax paid by a vassal to a lord or king in medieval times to avoid military service

Examples

Medieval scutage tax
In the twelfth century, many nobles paid scutage instead of joining the king's army.
