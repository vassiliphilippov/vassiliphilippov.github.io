schoolmasterish

adjective
strict and pedantic
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskuːlˌmæstərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɑːstərɪʃ / (BrE)
having the qualities or manner of a strict, formal, or pedantic schoolmaster

Examples

He spoke in a schoolmasterish way, correcting every small mistake his colleagues made.
The manager's schoolmasterish approach discouraged open discussion during meetings.
Her schoolmasterish attitude made group discussions feel more like classroom lectures.
He corrected every minor mistake in a schoolmasterish manner that annoyed his colleagues.
The manager's schoolmasterish tone discouraged employees from sharing their ideas freely.

  • antonym: lenient - not strict; permissive
  • synonym: pedantic - overly concerned with formal rules or details
