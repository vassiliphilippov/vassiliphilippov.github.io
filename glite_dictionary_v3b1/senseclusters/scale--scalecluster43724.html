scale

verb
adjust or measure by a standard
Pronunciation: / skeɪl / (AmE), / skeɪl / (BrE)
to adjust, change, or regulate the size or proportion of something according to a particular standard or ratio

Examples

To scale production
Production was scaled to meet the increase in demand.
The results must be scaled so that they can be compared with previous years.
We need to scale the dosage according to the patient's weight.
Digital images can be scaled up or down without losing quality in some formats.
If we scale the model correctly, it should look exactly like the real building.
The architect decided to scale the drawings so they would fit on one sheet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calibrate - to adjust or measure precisely according to a standard
  • synonym: resize - to change the size of something
