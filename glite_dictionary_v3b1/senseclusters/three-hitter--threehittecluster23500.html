three-hitter

noun
baseball game with only 3 hits allowed by pitcher
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈθriˌhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈθriːˌhɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a game in which a pitcher allows the opposing team only three hits.

Examples

A three-hitter game
He was proud of his first career three-hitter in the major leagues.
Last night's game was a memorable three-hitter that kept fans on the edge of their seats.
The pitcher threw a three-hitter to lead his team to victory.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: five-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows five hits
  • degree higher: four-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows four hits
  • degree lower: no-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows no hits
  • degree lower: one-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows only one hit
  • degree lower: two-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows only two hits
