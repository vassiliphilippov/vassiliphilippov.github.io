seronegative

adjective
without specific antibodies
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪəroʊˈnɛɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˌsɪərəʊˈnɛɡətɪv / (BrE)
not showing specific antibodies in the blood for a particular infection or disease

Examples

Seronegative test result
After several tests, the patient remained seronegative for the disease.
A seronegative result means that no antibodies were detected in the blood sample.
Some people exposed to the virus may still be seronegative if their immune response is delayed.
