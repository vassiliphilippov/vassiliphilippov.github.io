exanthema subitum

noun
viral illness with fever and rash in infants
Pronunciation: / ɛɡˌzænˈθimə səˈbɪtəm / (AmE), / ɛɡˌzænˈθiːmə səˈbɪtəm / (BrE)
A viral illness in infants and young children, marked by sudden high fever followed by a brief pinkish skin rash.

Examples

Exanthema subitum fever
Most cases of exanthema subitum resolve on their own without medical treatment.
Parents often confuse exanthema subitum with other childhood illnesses due to its abrupt onset.
The doctor diagnosed the child with exanthema subitum after noticing the sudden fever and pink rash.
