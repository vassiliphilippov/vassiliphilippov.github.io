deacetylation

noun
removal of acetyl group
9.6
Pronunciation: / diˌæsəˌtɪlˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌæsɪˌtiːlˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing an acetyl group from a molecule, especially in biological or chemical reactions

Examples

Deacetylation of molecules
The deacetylation of histones plays a key role in gene regulation.
Researchers studied the deacetylation process to understand how certain drugs affect the liver.
Enzymes that catalyze deacetylation are important in many metabolic pathways.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acetylation - the addition of an acetyl group to a molecule
