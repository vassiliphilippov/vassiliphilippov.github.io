intersubjectively

adverb
shared understanding
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərsəbˈdʒɛktɪvli / (AmE), / ˌɪntəsʌbˈdʒɛktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is shared, agreed upon, or understood by more than one person

Examples

Discussed intersubjectively by experts
The theory was developed intersubjectively through discussions among several experts.
Scientific facts are often established intersubjectively by the agreement of independent researchers.
The meaning of the artwork was interpreted intersubjectively by the group during the seminar.
The meaning of the artwork was constructed intersubjectively by the viewers during the discussion.
Scientific knowledge is often developed intersubjectively through collaboration and peer review.
They reached an agreement intersubjectively, ensuring that everyone's perspective was considered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collectively - in a way that involves a group acting together
  • antonym: individually - in a way that involves only one person or thing
  • antonym: objectively - in a way not influenced by personal feelings
  • antonym: subjectively - in a way based on personal feelings or opinions
