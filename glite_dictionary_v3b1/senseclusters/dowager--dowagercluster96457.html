dowager

noun
widow with inherited title or property
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊ.ədʒɚ /; / ˈdaʊədʒər / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ.ɪdʒ.ə /; / ˈdaʊədʒə / (BrE)
a widow, especially an elderly woman who has inherited property or a title from her deceased husband

Examples

An elderly dowager
At the gala, the elderly dowager wore a string of pearls inherited from her late spouse.
Many novels feature a dowager as a formidable matriarch controlling the family's fortune.
The dowager presided over the estate with grace after her husband's passing.
The respected dowager hosted afternoon tea for the entire village.
At the ceremony, the dowager wore her late husband's medals with pride.
Rumors about the wealthy dowager spread quickly through the small town.

Related Terms

  • synonym: matron - an older married woman, often with a dignified presence
  • synonym: widow - a woman whose spouse has died
