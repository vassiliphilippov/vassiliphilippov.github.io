sultanism

noun
absolute personal rule
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌl.tə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsʌl.tən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈsʌl.tə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsʌl.tən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a political system or period in which a sultan (or single ruler) holds all power with few or no checks on authority

Examples

Sultanism political system
The country's leader was accused of practicing sultanism by ignoring the constitution and ruling alone.
Historians often compare sultanism to other forms of absolute rule in world history.
Critics warned that the new laws could lead to sultanism and undermine democratic institutions.
The historian wrote a book about the sultanism that shaped the region's political history.
Under sultanism, all major decisions were made by the sultan and his close advisors.
The decline of sultanism led to the rise of new forms of government in the area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autocracy - a system of government by one person with absolute power
  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: dictatorship - a government ruled by a dictator with total power
  • synonym: monarchy - a system of government with a monarch at the head
  • antonym: republic - a system of government without a monarch
  • synonym: sultanate - a state or country ruled by a sultan
