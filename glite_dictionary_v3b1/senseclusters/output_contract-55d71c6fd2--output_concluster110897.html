output contract

noun
contract to sell all production to one buyer
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtpʊt ˌkɑntrækt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtpʊt ˌkɒntrækt / (BrE)
a contract in which one party agrees to sell all of its production to a specified buyer, who is obligated to purchase it.

Examples

An output contract agreement
Many farmers prefer an output contract to ensure they have a committed purchaser for their entire crop.
The factory entered into an output contract with a retailer, guaranteeing that all goods produced would go to one buyer.
Under the output contract, the manufacturer is obliged to sell all finished products to a single distributor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: requirements contract - a contract in which a buyer agrees to purchase all that they require of a product from a seller
