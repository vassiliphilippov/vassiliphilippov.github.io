deposit

noun
the act of putting something somewhere
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpɑzɪt /; / dɪˈpɑːzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
a layer or mass of material, such as minerals or sediment, that has been left or accumulated by a natural process

Examples

A mineral deposit
His deposit of the package by the door went unnoticed.
The deposit of sediment at the river's mouth creates a fertile delta.
The careful deposit of the vase ensured it remained unbroken.
A white deposit formed on the inside of the kettle after repeated use.
Miners discovered a large deposit of gold deep underground.
The river left a thick deposit of silt on its banks after the flood.
Glaciers are responsible for the deposit of rocks and soil in many regions.
Mineral deposit occurs when substances are left behind as water evaporates.
The slow deposit of silt by the river created new land over centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deposition - the process by which material is laid down by natural processes
  • antonym: erosion - process of eroding or being eroded by wind, water, or other agents
  • synonym: placement - the act of putting something in a particular place
  • antonym: removal - the act of taking something away from a place
  • synonym: sediment - matter that settles to the bottom of a liquid
