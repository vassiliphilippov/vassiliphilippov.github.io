semblance

noun
outward or misleading appearance
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmbləns /; / ˈsɛm.bləns / (AmE), / ˈsɛmbləns /; / ˈsɛm.bləns / (BrE)
the outward appearance or form of something, especially when it is not the true situation or is misleading

Examples

A semblance of normalcy
Even after the accident, he tried to maintain a semblance of normalcy.
Her cheerful smile gave the semblance of happiness, though she was struggling inside.
The negotiations retained only a semblance of order amid the chaos.
Despite the chaos, she managed to maintain a semblance of calm for her children.
His office gave the semblance of order, but papers were hidden in every drawer.
The manager's apology had only a semblance of sincerity, leaving many employees unconvinced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance; a front
  • synonym: illusion - a false idea or belief; deceptive appearance
  • synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
