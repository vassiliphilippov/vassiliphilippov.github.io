warg

verb
to control animal's mind
Pronunciation: / wɑːrɡ / (AmE), / wɑːɡ / (BrE)
to enter the mind of an animal and control or experience its senses, especially as a magical ability in fantasy fiction

Examples

To warg into a wolf
In the novel, the wizard could warg into a hawk to scout the enemy camp.
She learned to warg into her wolf companion and see through its eyes.
The hero tried to warg into a horse to escape the locked stable.
