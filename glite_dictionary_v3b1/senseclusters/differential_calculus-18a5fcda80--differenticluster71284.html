differential calculus

noun
mathematical study of rates of change
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl ˈkælkjʊləs / (BrE)
the branch of calculus concerned with the rates at which quantities change, using the concepts of derivatives and differentials

Examples

Differential calculus branch
Isaac Newton and Gottfried Leibniz are credited with the development of differential calculus.
Many engineering problems require the practical application of differential calculus to model physical systems.
She struggled at first to understand the core principles of differential calculus in her advanced mathematics class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calculus - the branch of mathematics dealing with rates of change and accumulation
  • antonym: integral calculus - the branch of calculus concerned with integrals and accumulation of quantities
