spongiform

adjective
sponge-like texture
Pronunciation: / ˈspʌndʒɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈspʌndʒɪfɔːm / (BrE)
having a texture or appearance similar to that of a sponge, often referring to porous or absorbent tissue in medical contexts

Examples

Spongiform brain tissue
The pathologist noted spongiform changes in the brain tissue under the microscope.
Certain diseases can cause organs to develop a spongiform appearance.
The infected tissue became spongiform, losing its normal structure and firmness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: porous - having small holes that allow air or liquid to pass through
  • antonym: solid - having no holes or spaces inside; firm and hard
