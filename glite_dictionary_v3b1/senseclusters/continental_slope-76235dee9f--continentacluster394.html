continental slope

noun
steep descent from continental shelf to deep ocean
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.tɪˈnen.t̬əl sloʊp / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.tɪˈnen.təl sləʊp / (BrE)
the steep downward section of the seafloor between the edge of a continent (the continental shelf) and the deep ocean floor (abyssal zone)

Examples

The continental slope edge
Many underwater landslides can occur along the continental slope due to its steepness.
Scientists study the continental slope to understand how sediments are transported to the deep sea.
The submarine descended rapidly down the continental slope toward the deep ocean floor.
