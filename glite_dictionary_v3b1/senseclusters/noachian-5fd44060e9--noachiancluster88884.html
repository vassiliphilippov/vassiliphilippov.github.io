Noachian

adjective
relating to Mars' early geologic epoch
Pronunciation: / no8ace6aki59n / (AmE), / n598ace6ak6a59n / (BrE)
(in planetary geology) relating to the Noachian epoch on Mars, an early geologic period marked by heavy impact cratering and evidence suggesting ancient surface water activity.

Examples

Noachian Mars period
Researchers mapped Noachian terrains to study early Martian climate and habitability.
Images show that many Noachian craters were altered by flowing water billions of years ago.
Scientists use crater counts to estimate Noachian surface ages across ancient Martian highlands.
