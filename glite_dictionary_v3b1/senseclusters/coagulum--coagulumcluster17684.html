coagulum

noun
lump formed from a liquid
9.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈæɡjələm / (AmE), / kəʊˈaɡjʊləm / (BrE)
a thick, semi-solid lump formed when a liquid, especially blood, changes to a more solid state

Examples

A coagulum of blood
A coagulum of blood blocked the wound, stopping further bleeding.
Milk left to sour can form a coagulum at the surface.
The doctor examined the coagulum that had formed in the test tube.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clot - a thick mass of coagulated liquid, especially blood
