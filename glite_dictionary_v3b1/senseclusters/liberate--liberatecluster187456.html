liberate

verb
set free from imprisonment or oppression
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəreɪt / (BrE)
to set someone or something free from imprisonment, oppression, restrictions, or control

Examples

After years of captivity, the hostages were finally liberated by the rescue team.
Many activists worked tirelessly to liberate those imprisoned for their beliefs.
The military moved quickly to liberate the occupied city.
Activists work tirelessly to liberate people from injustice and discrimination.
The army was able to liberate the prisoners after months of captivity.
The novel tells the story of a young woman who struggles to liberate herself from societal expectations.
The army was able to liberate the city after months of fighting.
She felt liberated after finally speaking her mind to her boss.
The new policy aims to liberate students from unnecessary academic pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enslave - to make someone a slave or deprive of freedom
  • synonym: free - to make someone free; to release from confinement or oppression
  • antonym: imprison - to confine someone, especially as punishment for a crime
  • synonym: release - to allow someone or something to go free
