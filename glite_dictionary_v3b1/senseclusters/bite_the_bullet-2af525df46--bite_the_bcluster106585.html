bite the bullet

verb
face a difficult situation bravely
6.3
Pronunciation: / baɪt ðə ˈbʊlɪt / (AmE), / baɪt ðə ˈbʊlɪt / (BrE)
to face a painful or difficult situation with courage and determination, even though it is unpleasant or challenging

Examples

To bite the bullet bravely
After months of procrastination, she finally decided to bite the bullet and start her thesis.
He knew the project would be tough, but he was ready to bite the bullet and give it his best effort.
Sometimes you have to bite the bullet and tell your friend the truth, no matter how hard it is.

Related Terms

  • synonym: face the music - to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions
  • synonym: grit one's teeth - to prepare oneself to endure something painful or unpleasant
