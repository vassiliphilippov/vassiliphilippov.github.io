dihedral

noun
angle between planes
Pronunciation: / daɪˈhiːdrəl / (AmE), / daɪˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
the angle formed between two intersecting planes, commonly used in geometry, chemistry, and engineering

Examples

A dihedral in chemistry
In molecular chemistry, the dihedral determines the spatial arrangement of atoms.
The engineer calculated the dihedral to ensure the airplane wings had proper stability.
Students learned how to measure a dihedral using geometric models in class.

  • synonym: plane - a flat, two-dimensional surface
