diamondback terrapin

noun
a North American turtle with diamond-patterned shell
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəməndˌbæk ˈterəˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəməndˌbæk ˈterəpɪn / (BrE)
A North American turtle, Malaclemys terrapin, inhabiting brackish coastal marshes from the Atlantic to the Gulf Coast of the United States, known for the diamond-shaped markings on its shell.

Examples

The diamondback terrapin turtle
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the habitat of the diamondback terrapin.
Unlike other turtles, the diamondback terrapin is adapted to live in brackish water.
We saw a diamondback terrapin basking on a log in the salt marsh.
