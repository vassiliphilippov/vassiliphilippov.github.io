preopening

noun
early trading session
6.0
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈoʊ.pən.ɪŋ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈoʊ.pən.ɪŋ/ /; / /prə.ˈoʊ.pən.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈəʊ.pən.ɪŋ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈəʊ.pən.ɪŋ/ /; / /prə.ˈəʊ.pən.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a trading period on a financial exchange before the official market opens, when orders are collected and matched to set opening prices

Examples

Preopening trading
During the preopening, investors analyze overnight news to decide on their first trades.
The preopening session often determines the initial price direction for many stocks.
Large institutional orders are sometimes placed in the preopening to influence the opening price.
