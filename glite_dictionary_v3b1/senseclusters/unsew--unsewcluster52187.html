unsew

verb
to come unstitched
8.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsəʊ / (BrE)
to undo or remove stitches from something that has been sewn, or to become unstitched

Examples

To unsew a seam
After years of use, the hem began to unsew and needed repair.
If you pull too hard, the seam might unsew and ruin the shirt.
The old cushion started to unsew at the edges, exposing the stuffing inside.
She had to unsew the pocket to fix the tear underneath.
The tailor will unsew the lining before making any adjustments.
He carefully unsewed the patch to reuse the fabric.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sew - to join or repair with stitches using a needle and thread
  • synonym: unravel - to come apart or undone, especially of threads or fabric
  • synonym: unstitch - to take out stitches from fabric
