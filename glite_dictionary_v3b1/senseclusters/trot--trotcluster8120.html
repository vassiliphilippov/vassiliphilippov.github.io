trot

noun
a slow run, especially of a horse
Pronunciation: / trɑt / (AmE), / trɒt / (BrE)
a pace or gait that is faster than a walk but slower than a run, especially for horses or similar animals

Examples

A gentle trot
During their morning workout, the runners kept a gentle trot around the park.
The horse broke into a steady trot as it moved down the lane.
With a click of her tongue, she urged the pony into a quick trot across the field.
At the command, the riders urged their mounts into a trot across the field.
Joggers moved at the same pace as the dog’s steady trot through the park.
The horse broke into a brisk trot as it approached the finish line.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: canter - a moderate, easy pace of a horse between a trot and a gallop
  • degree lower: walk - a slow pace of movement on foot
