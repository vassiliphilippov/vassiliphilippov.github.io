civil right

noun
a fundamental legal right of citizenship
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪvəl raɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl raɪt / (BrE)
a right guaranteed to a citizen by law, especially fundamental freedoms and privileges ensuring legal and social equality

Examples

Basic civil rights
Discrimination based on race is a violation of basic civil rights.
Many activists fought bravely for civil rights during the 1960s.
The law was created to protect the civil rights of all citizens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: human right - a basic right to which all humans are entitled
