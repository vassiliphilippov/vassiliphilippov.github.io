rewire

verb
change mental processes; retrain habits
6.1 0.31/m
Pronunciation: / ccric8wa6a5a / (AmE), / ccrid0c8wa6a59 / (BrE)
(figurative) to change or retrain someone's mental processes, habits, or neural connections, typically through repeated practice or therapy.

Examples

To rewire your brain
Therapists aim to rewire clients' habits through cognitive exercises and repeated exposure.
After months of practice, he rewired his automatic responses to stress.
Mindfulness and new routines are helpful for rewiring the brain after traumatic experiences.

Synonyms

  • reprogram
    5.6 0.22/m
    reprogram often means training or conditioning someone to adopt new habits, while rewire focuses on changing brain connections through repeated practice or therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reprogram - to change established patterns of thought or behaviour, often deliberately.
  • synonym: retrain - to teach someone new skills or behaviours; to change habitual responses.
