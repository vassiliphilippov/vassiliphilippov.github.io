Turkish boxwood

noun
a hard, yellowish wood from the box tree
4.9 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtɜːr.kɪʃ ˈbɑːks.wʊd/ /; / /ˈtər.kɪʃ ˈbɑːks.wʊd/ (AmE), /ˈtɜː.kɪʃ ˈbɒks.wʊd/ (BrE)
a very hard, tough, close-grained light yellow wood of the box tree, especially the common box, used in delicate woodwork such as musical instruments, inlays, and engraving blocks

Examples

Turkish boxwood wood
Antique printers' blocks were often carved from Turkish boxwood due to its close texture.
For the intricate marquetry, only Turkish boxwood would provide the necessary strength and color.
The artisan chose Turkish boxwood for its fine grain and durability when making the violin bridge.
