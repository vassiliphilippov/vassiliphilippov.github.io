Having an excessive or inflated sense of one's own importance or abilities; excessively self-centered.

Examples

Narcissistic personality
His narcissistic attitude made it difficult for him to maintain genuine friendships.
Some psychologists warn about the dangers of narcissistic behavior in modern society.
The narcissistic celebrity constantly sought admiration from fans and the media.

Synonyms

  • conceited
    5.4 0.84/m
    conceited is proud and shows visible vanity about abilities, while narcissistic involves extreme self-importance and a strong need for constant admiration.
  • self-important
    5.8 0.68/m
    self-important acts as if they are very important and expects respect, while narcissistic has an inflated self-image and craves admiration.
  • arrogant
    4.5 12/m
    arrogant behaves overbearingly and treats others as less, while narcissistic centers on self-admiration and a constant need for praise.
  • self-centered
    3.9 2.69/m
    self-centered focuses on one's own needs and often ignores others, while narcissistic adds grand self-importance and craving for admiration.
  • self-loving
    7.7 0.01/m
    self-loving shows obvious self-admiration and vanity, while narcissistic implies a deeper, often harmful, inflated sense of self-importance.
