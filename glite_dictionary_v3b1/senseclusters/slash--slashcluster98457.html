slash

noun
long, deep cut or wound
Pronunciation: / slæʃ / (AmE), / slæʃ / (BrE)
a long, deep cut or wound made by a sharp object, or a strong, sweeping cut

Examples

A deep slash
He received a deep slash on his arm from the broken glass.
The doctor stitched up the slash quickly and bandaged it tightly.
There was a long slash across the villain’s cheek in the movie.
A single slash of the knife opened the package.
He made a wild slash at the thick undergrowth with his machete.
The sword left a deep slash across the enemy's shield.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - an opening or wound made by a sharp object
  • synonym: gash - a deep, long wound or cut
