discourager

noun
one who dissuades
5.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈskʌr.ə.dʒər/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskʌr.ɪ.dʒər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈskʌr.ə.dʒə/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskʌr.ɪ.dʒə/ (BrE)
a person or thing that causes someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm to do something

Examples

A discourager of effort
A lack of support can be a strong discourager for new artists.
Negative feedback from teachers acted as a discourager to her creative ambitions.
High costs are often the main discourager for people considering further education.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourager - a person who gives support or confidence to someone
