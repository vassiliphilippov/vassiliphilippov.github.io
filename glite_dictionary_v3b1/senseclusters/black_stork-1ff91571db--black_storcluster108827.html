black stork

noun
large wading bird with black upperparts and white underparts
4.8
Pronunciation: / blæk stɔrk / (AmE), / blæk stɔːk / (BrE)
A large stork of the Old World with glossy black upperparts and white underparts (Ciconia nigra).

Examples

A black stork bird
During their migration, black storks travel thousands of miles between Europe and Africa.
The black stork is less common than the white stork in Europe.
We watched a black stork fishing quietly on the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: white stork - a stork species with mainly white plumage and black wings
