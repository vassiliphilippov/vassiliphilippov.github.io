MMF

noun
magnetic potential measure
9.5
Pronunciation: /em em ef/ /; / /əm əm əf/ (AmE), /em em ef/ /; / /əm əm əf/ (BrE)
an abbreviation for magnetomotive force, which measures the magnetic potential that drives magnetic flux through a circuit

Examples

MMF of a coil
The MMF generated by the coil is proportional to the current flowing through it.
Engineers calculate the MMF to design efficient magnetic circuits in transformers.
A higher MMF results in a stronger magnetic field within the core material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electromotive force - the force that drives electric current through a circuit
  • synonym: magnetomotive force - the force that drives magnetic flux through a magnetic circuit
