flawless

adjective
perfect; without imperfections
3.7 4.50/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfl…îl…ôs / (AmE), / Ààfl…îÀêl…ôs / (BrE)
Without any imperfections or defects; perfect in every way.

Examples

A flawless performance
Her flawless performance earned her a standing ovation from the audience.
The chef presented a flawless dish that impressed all the judges.
The diamond was flawless and sparkled under the light.

Synonyms

  • unflawed
    5.6 0.02/m
    unflawed describes something without faults or errors, while flawless is used broadly to praise perfect condition or performance.
  • perfect
    1.0 141/m
    perfect means having all required qualities, while flawless focuses on lacking any small defects or mistakes you can notice.
  • impeccable
    6.3 2.99/m
    impeccable is a formal word for meeting highest standards, while flawless is more general praise for no defects.
  • immaculate
    5.9 2.42/m
    immaculate often shows perfect cleanliness or neatness, while flawless describes lack of any defects in form or quality.
  • unblemished
    5.1 0.68/m
    unblemished often describes a clean record or surface without marks, while flawless means totally free from any defects.
