vestibular fold

noun
upper laryngeal fold, not used in speech
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈves.tɪ.bjə.lər foʊld/ /; / /ˈves.tɪ.bjʊ.lər foʊld/ (AmE), /ˈves.tɪ.bjʊ.lə fəʊld/ /; / /ˈves.tɪ.bjə.lə fəʊld/ (BrE)
either of the two upper folds of mucous membrane in the larynx, not involved in producing speech sounds; also called the false vocal cord.

Examples

The vestibular fold in larynx
Damage to the vestibular fold rarely affects a person's ability to speak.
The vestibular fold helps protect the airway during swallowing.
The surgeon carefully avoided the vestibular fold during the procedure.
