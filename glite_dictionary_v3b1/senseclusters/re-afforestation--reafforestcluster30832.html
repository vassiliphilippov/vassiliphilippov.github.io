re-afforestation

noun
restoration of a depleted forest by replanting
8.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /riː.ə.fɔːr.ɪ.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ə.fɔːr.ɪ.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.ə.fɒr.ɪ.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ə.fɒr.ɪ.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process of replanting trees in an area where a forest has been depleted by fire, logging, or other causes.

Examples

Re-afforestation of forests
Re-afforestation is essential to combat the adverse effects of deforestation and climate change.
Many communities now participate in annual re-afforestation drives to help rebuild their local ecosystems.
The government launched a major re-afforestation campaign to restore the land after years of logging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afforestation - the process of planting trees to create a forest
  • antonym: deforestation - the removal or clearing of forests
