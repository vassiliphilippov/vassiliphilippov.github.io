carboxyhemoglobin

noun
carbon monoxide-hemoglobin compound
9.1
Pronunciation: / kɑrˌbɑksiˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / kɑːˌbɒksiˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
a compound in the blood formed when hemoglobin binds with carbon monoxide, which prevents normal oxygen transport

Examples

Carboxyhemoglobin levels
Doctors measured the patient's carboxyhemoglobin levels to assess the severity of carbon monoxide poisoning.
Exposure to car exhaust can increase carboxyhemoglobin in the bloodstream, reducing oxygen delivery.
Firefighters are at risk of elevated carboxyhemoglobin after working in smoke-filled environments.
High levels of carboxyhemoglobin in the blood can indicate carbon monoxide poisoning.
Doctors tested the patient's carboxyhemoglobin after he was rescued from the burning building.
The laboratory reported a dangerously elevated carboxyhemoglobin concentration in the victim's blood sample.
