by no means

adverb
not at all; definitely not
4.2 8.40/m
Pronunciation: / ba…™ Ààno ä Àåminz /; / ba…™ no ä minz / (AmE), / ba…™ Ààn…ô ä ÀåmiÀênz /; / ba…™ n…ô ä miÀênz / (BrE)
used to strongly say that something is not true or not possible; not at all

Examples

By no means true
She is by no means convinced that this is the best solution.
The outcome of the project is by no means guaranteed despite everyone's hard work.
The weather is by no means perfect for a picnic today.
By no means should you disclose this information to anyone outside the company.
Success is by no means guaranteed, even with hard work.
The work is by no means finished yet, so please be patient.

Synonyms

  • in no way, shape, or form
    5.6 0.20/m
    in no way, shape, or form stresses that something is not true in any circumstance, while by no means is a general emphatic denial.
  • not at all
    1.0 14/m
    not at all denies something completely or shows no agreement, while by no means sounds slightly more formal and emphatic in speech.
  • by no stretch of the imagination
    8.1 0.00/m
    by no stretch of the imagination uses a vivid image to deny possibility strongly, while by no means is less figurative and more common in general speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: by all means - certainly; of course (opposite meaning)
  • antonym: by any means - in any way at all; whatsoever
  • synonym: not at all - not in any way or to any degree
