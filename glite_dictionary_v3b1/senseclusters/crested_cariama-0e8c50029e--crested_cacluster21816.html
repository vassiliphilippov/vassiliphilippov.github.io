crested cariama

noun
large, crested South American bird
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛstəd kɑːriˈɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈkrɛstɪd kəˈriːəmə / (BrE)
a large, long-legged South American bird (Cariama cristata) with a distinctive crest, native to Brazil and adjacent regions; the only member of the genus Cariama

Examples

The crested cariama bird
A crested cariama can often be seen hunting insects and small reptiles in open fields.
The crested cariama uses its powerful legs to run swiftly across the grasslands of Brazil.
Unlike most birds, the crested cariama rarely flies and prefers to stay on the ground.
