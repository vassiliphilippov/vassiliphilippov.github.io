overestimate

verb
to assess as greater than the actual amount or value
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɛstɪmeɪt /; / ˌəʊvərˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to estimate or judge something as greater, more important, or more significant than it really is

Examples

To overestimate the cost
It is easy to overestimate the difficulty of this task.
She tends to overestimate how much money she needs for a trip.
They seriously overestimated the crowd's enthusiasm for the new product.
It is easy to overestimate your abilities when you are confident.
Many students overestimate the amount of time they will have to study before the test.
She overestimated how much the house was worth in today’s market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overvalue - to value something more highly than it deserves
  • antonym: underestimate - to estimate something as being less than the actual amount or value
