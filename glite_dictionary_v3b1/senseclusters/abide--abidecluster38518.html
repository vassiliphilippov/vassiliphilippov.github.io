abide

verb
to follow rules
3.4
Pronunciation: / əˈbaɪd / (AmE), / əˈbaɪd / (BrE)
to accept or follow a rule, law, or decision

Examples

To abide by rules
All employees are expected to abide by the company's safety regulations at all times.
If you cannot abide by the rules, you may be asked to leave the group.
The citizens must abide by the new laws introduced by the government.
All members must abide by the club's rules to remain in good standing.
She always abided by her parents' wishes, even when she disagreed.
If you cannot abide by the agreement, you may have to leave the group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disobey - to refuse to do what a law, rule, or person in authority tells you to do
  • synonym: obey - to do what a law, rule, or person in authority tells you to do
