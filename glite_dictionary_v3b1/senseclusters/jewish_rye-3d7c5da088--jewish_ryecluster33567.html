Jewish rye

noun
rye bread with caraway seeds, Jewish style
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuɪʃ raɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːɪʃ raɪ / (BrE)
A type of bread made with rye flour, often containing caraway seeds, commonly associated with Jewish cuisine.

Examples

A loaf of Jewish rye
At the bakery, I picked up a loaf of Jewish rye for our dinner party.
He prefers his morning toast made from Jewish rye rather than whole wheat bread.
The deli served fresh corned beef on Jewish rye with a side of pickles.
