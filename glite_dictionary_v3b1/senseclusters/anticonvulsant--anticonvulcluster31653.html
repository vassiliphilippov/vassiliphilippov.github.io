anticonvulsant

noun
drug that prevents convulsions
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.kənˈvʌl.sənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.kənˈvʌl.sənt / (BrE)
A drug that is used to prevent or reduce the severity of convulsions, particularly in the treatment of epilepsy.

Examples

An anticonvulsant drug
After starting the new anticonvulsant, he experienced fewer side effects.
Some anticonvulsant medications can also help treat certain mood disorders.
The doctor prescribed an anticonvulsant to help control her seizures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiepileptic - a drug used to prevent epileptic seizures
