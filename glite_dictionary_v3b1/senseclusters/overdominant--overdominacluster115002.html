overdominant

adjective
heterozygote advantage
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈdɑːmɪnənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdɒmɪnənt / (BrE)
in genetics, having greater fitness in the heterozygous state than in either homozygous state

Examples

Overdominant gene effect
The sickle-cell allele is considered overdominant because carriers are more resistant to malaria.
An overdominant gene can help maintain genetic diversity in a population.
Scientists studied how overdominant traits affect the survival of certain plant species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heterozygote advantage - greater fitness of heterozygotes compared to homozygotes
  • antonym: underdominant - having lower fitness in the heterozygous state than in either homozygous state
