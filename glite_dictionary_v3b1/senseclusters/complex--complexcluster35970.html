complex

adjective
complicated; made of interconnected parts
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.plɛks / (BrE)
consisting of many different and connected parts; not simple or straightforward; difficult to understand or analyze

Examples

A complex problem
She was working on a complex mathematical equation that took hours to solve.
The novel features a complex network of characters and timelines.
The rules governing international trade can be quite complex and difficult to understand.
His explanation of quantum mechanics was so complex that few people understood it.
The instructions for assembling the furniture were too complex for me to follow without help.
The political situation in the region became increasingly complex after the recent elections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complicated - involving many different and confusing aspects
  • antonym: simple - easily understood or done; not complicated
