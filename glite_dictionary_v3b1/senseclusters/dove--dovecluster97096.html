dove

noun
advocate of peaceful solutions in politics
3.7
Pronunciation: / doʊv / (AmE), / dəʊv / (BrE)
a person who advocates peaceful or diplomatic solutions instead of military action, especially in political or international affairs

Examples

A political dove
During the debate, Senator Graham positioned himself as a dove regarding military interventions.
Her reputation as a dove made her popular among voters seeking an end to the conflict.
The administration is divided between doves and hawks over how to handle the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hawk - someone who advocates for military action or aggressive policies
