Jatropha

noun
genus of tropical American plants
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈtroʊfə / (AmE), / dʒəˈtrəʊfə / (BrE)
A genus of mainly tropical American plants in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae), some species of which are used for oil production and ornamental purposes.

Examples

Jatropha plant species
Recent interest has grown in the use of Jatropha for producing biodiesel in developing countries.
Some species of Jatropha are valued for their ornamental flowers and drought resistance.
The seeds of Jatropha plants are toxic but can be processed to extract useful oil.
