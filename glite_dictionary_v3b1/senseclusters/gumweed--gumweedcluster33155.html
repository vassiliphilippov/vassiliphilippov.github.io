gumweed

noun
resinous North American plant (Grindelia)
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌmˌwid / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmˌwiːd / (BrE)
any of several western North American plants of the genus Grindelia, with resinous leaves and stems, sometimes used medicinally and often poisonous to livestock.

Examples

Gumweed plant leaves
Cattle can become ill if they eat gumweed growing in the pasture.
The sticky resin from gumweed was once used in traditional medicine by Native Americans.
We saw clusters of yellow-flowered gumweed along the dusty roadside.
