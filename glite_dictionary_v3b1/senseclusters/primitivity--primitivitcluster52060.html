primitivity

noun
primitive property in algebra
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌprɪməˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌprɪmɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the property of a ring or algebra being primitive, meaning it has a faithful simple module in algebra

Examples

Primitivity of an algebra
The mathematician discussed the primitivity of the algebra in her lecture.
Establishing primitivity can be challenging for certain classes of rings.
They proved the primitivity of the structure by constructing a faithful simple module.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simplicity - the property of having no nontrivial ideals or submodules
