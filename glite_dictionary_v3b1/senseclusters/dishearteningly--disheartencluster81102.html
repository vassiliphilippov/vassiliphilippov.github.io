dishearteningly

adverb
in a discouraging way
7.4
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈhɑːr.tən.ɪŋ.liː/ /; / /dɪs.ˈhɑːr.tən.ɪŋ.lɪ/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈhɑː.tən.ɪŋ.liː/ /; / /dɪs.ˈhɑː.tən.ɪŋ.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that causes discouragement, disappointment, or loss of hope or confidence

Examples

Performed dishearteningly poorly
The team performed dishearteningly poorly in the final match of the season.
Sales figures have been dishearteningly low despite the new advertising campaign.
She found the lack of progress dishearteningly obvious after months of hard work.
The students performed dishearteningly on the exam despite their hard work.
Sales figures have been dishearteningly low this quarter, worrying the management team.
He spoke dishearteningly about the lack of progress in the negotiations.
