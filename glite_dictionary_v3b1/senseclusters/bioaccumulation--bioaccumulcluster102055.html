bioaccumulation

noun
build-up of toxins
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊəˌkjuːmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊəˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the gradual build-up of harmful substances, such as chemicals or toxins, in living organisms over time

Examples

The bioaccumulation of toxins
Scientists are concerned about bioaccumulation of mercury in fish.
The bioaccumulation of pesticides can harm animals at the top of the food chain.
Regulations aim to reduce bioaccumulation of toxic chemicals in the environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biomagnification - the increasing concentration of substances in organisms higher up the food chain
  • antonym: detoxification - the process of removing toxic substances from a living organism
