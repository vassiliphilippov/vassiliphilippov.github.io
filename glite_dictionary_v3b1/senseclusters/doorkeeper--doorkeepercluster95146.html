doorkeeper

noun
person who guards an entrance
2.8 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îrÀåkiÀêp…ôr /; / Ààd…îrÀåkip…ôr / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀêÀåkiÀêp…ô / (BrE)
a person whose job is to guard or supervise the entrance to a building, room, or important place and control who is allowed to enter

Examples

Museum doorkeeper
As a doorkeeper at the museum, he greeted hundreds of visitors every day.
The doorkeeper checked everyone's identification before allowing them to enter the building.
The parliament's doorkeeper ensures only authorized personnel access the chamber.
As a doorkeeper, she was responsible for maintaining order outside the courtroom.
The doorkeeper checked everyone's credentials before allowing them into the council chamber.
The parliament doorkeeper announced the arrival of the members to the assembly.

Synonyms

  • door guard
    2.5 0.02/m
    a door guard watches an entrance and stops people as needed, while a doorkeeper formally controls who may enter.
  • porter
    4.3 0.69/m
    a porter often stands at an entrance to admit people and guard it, while a doorkeeper controls who may enter.
  • doorman
    1.2 2.93/m
    a doorman works at building entrances like hotels or clubs and greets guests, while a doorkeeper controls access to a place.
  • gateman
    4.6 0.01/m
    a gateman stands at an outdoor gate to open or close it, while a doorkeeper watches and controls entry to a place.
  • gatekeeper
    5.1 5.63/m
    a gatekeeper controls access to people or resources and can be an organization, while a doorkeeper is a person at an entrance.
  • security guard
    1.3 6.62/m
    a security guard protects property and people across a site and may patrol widely, while a doorkeeper stays at an entrance to control entry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guard - person who keeps watch, especially at an entrance
  • synonym: usher - an official who shows people to their seats or maintains order at events
