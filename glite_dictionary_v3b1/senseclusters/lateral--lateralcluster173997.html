lateral

adjective
at or toward the side
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl / (AmE), / ˈlat.ər.əl /; / ˈlæt.ər.əl / (BrE)
relating to the side or sides of something, rather than the center

Examples

Lateral movement
Branches growing in a lateral direction gave the tree a broad, sprawling shape.
The doctor observed a lateral movement in the patient's knee joint during the examination.
The team made a lateral pass to avoid the opponent in the game.
In anatomy, the ears are lateral to the nose.
The MRI scan revealed damage to the lateral meniscus of the knee.
The fracture is located on the lateral aspect of the forearm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: medial - relating to the middle; opposite of lateral
  • synonym: sideways - toward or from the side
