carboxylation

noun
adding carboxyl group
Pronunciation: / kɑːrˌbɑːksɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kɑːˌbɒksɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical or biochemical process in which a carboxyl group is added to a molecule

Examples

Carboxylation process
Vitamin K is essential for the carboxylation of certain proteins involved in blood clotting.
The enzyme catalyzes the carboxylation of glutamate residues in the protein structure.
Defective carboxylation can lead to problems with bone development and blood coagulation.
The process of carboxylation is essential in the first step of photosynthesis.
Enzymes often catalyze the carboxylation of organic molecules in living cells.
Researchers studied how temperature affects the rate of carboxylation in plant tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decarboxylation - the removal of a carboxyl group from a molecule
