daymark

noun
lighthouse identifier
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a visible marker, pattern, or structure (such as on a lighthouse) that helps sailors identify a location during the day

Examples

A daymark on lighthouse
The red and white stripes served as a clear daymark for ships approaching the harbor.
Sailors relied on the unique daymark of the lighthouse to confirm their location during daylight.
Each coastal tower was painted with a different daymark to help mariners distinguish them from a distance.
The lighthouse was painted with a distinctive daymark to help ships identify the coastline.
Sailors relied on the tall daymark to safely enter the harbor during daylight hours.
A new daymark was installed on the rocky point to improve navigation for passing vessels.
