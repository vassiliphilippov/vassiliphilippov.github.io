perdurantist

noun
philosophy of persistence
9.9
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈdʊr.ənt.ɪst/ /; / /pər.ˈdjʊr.ənt.ɪst/ (AmE), /pə.ˈdjʊə.rənt.ɪst/ /; / /pə.ˈdʊə.rənt.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes that objects persist through time by having different temporal parts at different times

Examples

A perdurantist philosopher
A perdurantist views a person as a series of temporal parts rather than a single enduring entity.
The debate between the perdurantist and the endurantist is central in metaphysics.
As a perdurantist, she argued that objects are like four-dimensional worms stretching through time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endurantist - a person who believes objects persist by being wholly present at each moment in time
