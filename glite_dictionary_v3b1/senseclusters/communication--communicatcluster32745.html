communication

noun
means of connection or access
2.3 15/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ôÀåmjuÀê.n…ôÀàke…™. É…ôn / (AmE), / k…ôÀåmjuÀê.n…™Ààke…™. É…ôn / (BrE)
a means of connecting people or places, such as a passage, channel, or system that allows access or the exchange of goods, people, or information

Examples

Communication channel
A narrow staircase provided communication from the cellar to the kitchen.
During the blackout, the city lost all communication with the outside world.
The engineer designed an underground tunnel to serve as communication between the two buildings.

Synonyms

  • communication channel
    3.4 0.49/m
    a communication channel is a method or system for sending messages between people or devices, while communication is any way of connecting people or places.
  • channel
    3.6 12/m
    a channel is an official route or system for sending information or requests, while communication is a broader means of connecting people or places.
  • medium
    4.1 14/m
    a medium is a physical or mass channel like TV or radio used to carry information, while communication is any connecting system or passage.
  • line
    3.2 59/m
    a line is a direct physical link such as a telephone or cable used to contact others, while communication covers many types of connections.
  • link
    1.4 65/m
    a link is a simple connection between things or places, often physical, while communication means a system or channel for exchange.
