hurly-burly

noun
noisy commotion or tumult
Pronunciation: / ˈhɝli ˌbɝli / (AmE), / ˈhɜːli ˌbɜːli / (BrE)
a commotion or noisy disorder, especially involving a crowd; tumult or chaos

Examples

The hurly-burly of crowds
After the scandal broke, the hurly-burly in the press did not subside for weeks.
Amid the hurly-burly of the marketplace, she struggled to hear her friend speak.
Shakespeare's plays often feature a hurly-burly of battles and political intrigue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of tranquility or quiet
  • synonym: tumult - a loud, confused noise, especially caused by a large mass of people
