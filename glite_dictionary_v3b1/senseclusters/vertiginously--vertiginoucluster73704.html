vertiginously

adverb
dizzily or steeply
Pronunciation: / vərˈtɪdʒənəsli / (AmE), / vɜːˈtɪdʒɪnəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is extremely steep, rapid, or causes dizziness

Examples

To move vertiginously fast
The road wound vertiginously up the side of the mountain, making drivers nervous.
Prices have risen vertiginously in the last few months, surprising many consumers.
She looked down from the vertiginously high balcony and felt a wave of dizziness.
The mountain path climbed vertiginously, making the hikers feel dizzy as they looked down.
Profits increased vertiginously after the company launched its new product line.
The skyscraper's glass elevator rose vertiginously to the top floor in seconds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dizzily - in a way that causes dizziness
  • antonym: gradually - in a slow and steady way
  • synonym: steeply - at a sharp angle or with a rapid rise
