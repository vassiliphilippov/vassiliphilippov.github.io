balance

noun
equality of distribution; state of equal parts
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈbæləns /; / ˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
a state or situation in which different elements are equal, stable, or in correct proportions; harmony or equilibrium

Examples

Work-life balance
A healthy diet is all about finding the right balance of nutrients.
The government must maintain a balance between spending and saving.
There needs to be a balance between work and leisure for a fulfilling life.
Good design relies on a sense of balance between visual elements on both sides of the page.
In geometry, balance refers to the exact reflection of shape on either side of a line.
The sculpture impresses viewers with its perfect balance and symmetry.
Good interior design relies on the careful balance of color and space.
The architect stressed the importance of balance in the building’s overall composition.
The painting achieves a perfect balance between light and shadow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chaos - complete disorder and lack of arrangement
  • synonym: equilibrium - a state of physical or mental balance
  • synonym: harmony - the quality of forming a pleasing and consistent whole
  • antonym: imbalance - a state of inequality or lack of balance
  • synonym: symmetry - the quality of being made up of exactly similar parts facing each other or around an axis
