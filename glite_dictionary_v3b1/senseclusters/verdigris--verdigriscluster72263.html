verdigris

noun
green patina on copper, brass, or bronze
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝːdəˌɡris /; / ˈvɜrdɪˌɡris / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdɪˌɡriːs /; / ˈvɜːdɪɡriːs / (BrE)
a green or blue substance that forms on copper, brass, or bronze, often used as a pigment in paints and dyes

Examples

Green verdigris layer
Over many years, a layer of verdigris developed on the old copper statue.
Rainwater exposure caused the brass handles to acquire a bright verdigris.
The artisan used chemicals to quickly produce a verdigris effect on the bronze sculpture.
Copper roofs often develop a layer of verdigris after years of exposure to the elements.
In medieval manuscripts, verdigris was commonly used to create vibrant green colors.
The artist mixed verdigris into the paint to achieve a rich turquoise hue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: patina - a green or brown film on the surface of metals from oxidation
