holdover

noun
official remaining in office past term
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊldˌoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an official who continues to serve in office after their official term has ended, often temporarily until a successor is appointed

Examples

A political holdover
After the election, the mayor became a holdover until the new official was sworn in.
Several holdover officials were asked to stay during the transition period.
The council viewed him as a holdover from the previous administration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incumbent - a person currently holding office
