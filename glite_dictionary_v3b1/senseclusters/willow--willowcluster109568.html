willow

verb
to sway gently
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
to move or sway gently back and forth, like the branches of a willow tree in the wind

Examples

Reeds willow in wind
The tall reeds willowed in the gentle breeze by the riverbank.
At sunset, her long hair willowed softly in the wind as she walked.
The curtains willowed with every draft that came through the open window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flutter - to move lightly and quickly in the air
  • antonym: stand still - to remain without moving
  • synonym: sway - to move slowly or rhythmically back and forth
