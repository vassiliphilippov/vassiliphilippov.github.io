steal

noun
an excellent bargain or deal
3.7 1.06/m
Pronunciation: / stil / (AmE), / stiÀêl / (BrE)
an unusually good bargain; something bought at a price much lower than its real value

Examples

A steal at this price
I can't believe I got these designer shoes for such a steal at the outlet mall.
That antique vase was a real steal at only twenty dollars.
Tickets to the concert were selling for half-price, which was an absolute steal.

Synonyms

  • bargain
    1.0 11/m
    a bargain is a cheaper item sold at a reduced price, while a steal is an unusually great deal far below true value.
  • buy
    1.0 6.01/m
    a buy is a purchase you make that seems good value, while a steal is an unusually cheap purchase worth much more.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bargain - something bought for less than the usual price
  • antonym: rip-off - something that is unreasonably expensive
