dawnlight

noun
beginning of hope
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔnˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɔːnˌlaɪt / (BrE)
the soft, pale light that appears in the sky at the beginning of the day, just before sunrise (can also mean the beginning of hope or a new era)

Examples

Soft dawnlight
The first dawnlight crept over the mountains, signaling a new beginning for the village.
After years of struggle, the election results felt like dawnlight for the country.
She saw a glimmer of dawnlight in his hopeful words about the future.
The forest was quiet as the first dawnlight filtered through the trees.
She woke early to watch the dawnlight spread across the fields.
Birds began to sing as dawnlight touched the rooftops of the village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daybreak - the time in the morning when light first appears
  • antonym: nightfall - the time when it becomes dark in the evening
  • synonym: sunrise - the time when the sun appears above the horizon in the morning
  • antonym: sunset - the time in the evening when the sun disappears below the horizon
  • synonym: twilight - the soft light in the sky just after sunset or before sunrise
