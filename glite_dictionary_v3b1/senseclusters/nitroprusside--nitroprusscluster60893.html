nitroprusside

noun
blood pressure drug
Pronunciation: / ˌnaɪtroʊˈpruːsaɪd / (AmE), / ˌnaɪtrəʊˈpruːsaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing iron and cyanide groups, used as a drug to quickly lower blood pressure

Examples

Nitroprusside injection
The doctor administered nitroprusside to rapidly reduce the patient's blood pressure.
In emergency situations, nitroprusside can help control dangerously high blood pressure.
Continuous monitoring is required when using nitroprusside due to its powerful effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antihypertensive - a drug used to lower high blood pressure
  • synonym: vasodilator - a drug that widens blood vessels
