pan

verb
wash gravel in a pan to find minerals
5.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /pæn/ (AmE), /pæn/ (BrE)
to wash earth or gravel in a pan in order to separate out valuable minerals such as gold

Examples

To pan for gold
During the Gold Rush, many prospectors panned for gold in the rivers of California.
He spent hours panning the stream, hoping to find some flakes of gold.
They taught visitors how to pan for gemstones in the shallow water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sluice - to wash or strain with a stream of water, often to separate precious minerals
