arise

verb
come into existence; begin to appear
Pronunciation: / əˈraɪz / (AmE), / əˈraɪz / (BrE)
to begin to exist, appear, or happen (especially problems, situations, or opportunities)

Examples

Problems arise
A number of problems can arise when working with new technology.
New opportunities may arise if you are willing to take risks.
Rumors about the merger began to arise within the company last week.
Conflicts often arise from misunderstandings between colleagues.
If new opportunities arise, she is always ready to take them.
Several complications can arise during the course of surgery.
If any problems arise during the process, please contact customer service.
Opportunities for advancement often arise when you least expect them.
Several concerns arose after the meeting regarding the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emerge - to become visible or known after being hidden
  • synonym: occur - to happen or take place
  • synonym: result - to happen as a consequence
  • antonym: vanish - to disappear or cease to exist
