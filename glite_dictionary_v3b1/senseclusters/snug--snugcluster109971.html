snug

verb
to fit closely
3.5
Pronunciation: / snʌɡ / (AmE), / snʌɡ / (BrE)
to fit closely and comfortably around or against something or someone

Examples

Snugged around him
The sleeping bag snugged around him, keeping him warm through the night.
She snugged the scarf tightly around her neck before heading outside.
The cat snugged itself into the small box, looking perfectly content.
The kitten snugged into the blanket and fell asleep quickly.
She snugged her scarf tightly around her neck before going outside.
The boat snugged up to the dock as the captain tied it securely.
She tried to snug the blanket around her shoulders before going to sleep.
He snugged his hat down over his ears to keep warm in the wind.
The mechanic snugged the bolt to ensure the part was secure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cuddle - to hold close for warmth or comfort
  • synonym: envelop - to wrap up or cover completely
  • antonym: loosen - to make less tight or close-fitting
  • synonym: nestle - to settle or lie comfortably within or against something
  • synonym: tuck - to push or fold something into a small space for a close fit
