zonality

noun
west–east flow pattern
9.9
Pronunciation: / zoʊˈnælɪti / (AmE), / zəʊˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the predominance of west–east flow or patterns, especially in meteorology or oceanography, rather than north–south movement

Examples

Atmospheric zonality
High zonality in the atmosphere can lead to more stable weather patterns across continents.
Meteorologists observed increased zonality in the jet stream during the mild winter.
Ocean currents sometimes display strong zonality, affecting global climate systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: meridionality - the predominance of north–south flow or patterns
  • synonym: zonal - relating to or arranged in zones, especially west–east direction
