retinol

noun
form of vitamin A
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtɪˌnɔl / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪnɒl / (BrE)
a form of vitamin A, an unsaturated alcohol found in fish liver oils and synthesized from carotene, important for vision and healthy skin

Examples

Retinol vitamin A
A deficiency in retinol can lead to problems with night vision and immune function.
Fish liver oils are a rich source of retinol, making them useful as dietary supplements.
Many anti-aging creams contain retinol to reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vitamin A - a group of fat-soluble vitamins including retinol, important for vision and health
