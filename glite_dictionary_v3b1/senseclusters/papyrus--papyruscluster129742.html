papyrus

noun
ancient writing material from papyrus plant
Pronunciation: / pəˈpaɪrəs / (AmE), / pəˈpaɪərəs / (BrE)
A material similar to thick paper made from the pith of the papyrus plant, used especially by ancient Egyptians, Greeks, and Romans for writing.

Examples

Papyrus sheets
Making papyrus required skilled craftsmanship in cutting and pressing the plant sheets.
Scholars have discovered many original texts on fragments of papyrus.
The ancient scroll was written on papyrus and preserved for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parchment - material made from animal skin used for writing
  • synonym: vellum - fine parchment made from calf skin
