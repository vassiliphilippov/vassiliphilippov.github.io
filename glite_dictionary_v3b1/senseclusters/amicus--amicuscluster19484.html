amicus

noun
legal adviser to court
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmiː.kəs/ /; / /ˈæ.mɪ.kəs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmiː.kəs/ /; / /ˈæ.mɪ.kəs/ (BrE)
a person or group not directly involved in a court case who offers information or support to assist the court's decision (legal term)

Examples

Amicus brief
The Supreme Court received an amicus brief from a civil rights organization.
Several amicus submissions provided valuable perspectives on the complex legal issue.
The judge considered the arguments presented by the amicus before making a decision.
The judge allowed the amicus to submit a brief supporting the defendant's position.
Several organizations acted as amicus in the landmark Supreme Court case.
An amicus can provide valuable expertise that helps the court make an informed decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amicus curiae - a person or group offering information to a court as a 'friend of the court'
  • antonym: party - a person or group directly involved in a legal case
