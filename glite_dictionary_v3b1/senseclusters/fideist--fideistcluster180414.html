fideist

noun
faith over reason
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːdiɪst /; / ˈfiːdiˌɪst / (AmE), / ˈfiːdɪɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that religious faith is independent of or superior to reason

Examples

A fideist believer
The fideist maintained that faith should guide all religious beliefs, regardless of logical arguments.
As a fideist, she rejected philosophical proofs in favor of spiritual conviction.
Some theologians debate whether a fideist approach undermines the role of reason in religion.
The fideist insisted that faith should guide all religious beliefs, not logical arguments.
Some philosophers debate whether a fideist can truly separate faith from reason.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rationalist - a person who believes reason is the main source of knowledge
