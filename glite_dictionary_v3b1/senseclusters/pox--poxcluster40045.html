pox

noun
disease causing pustules, esp. syphilis or smallpox
Pronunciation: / pɑks / (AmE), / pɒks / (BrE)
a disease that causes skin eruptions or blisters, such as smallpox or chickenpox

Examples

Chicken pox outbreak
During the 18th century, many feared both pox and plague outbreaks.
In Shakespeare's time, pox was a common term for syphilis.
The doctor identified the rash as a symptom of pox acquired congenitally.
During the eighteenth century, pox outbreaks were common in many European cities.
She bore scars on her face, likely left by a childhood pox.
Vaccines have nearly eradicated several forms of pox in most parts of the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chickenpox - a mild, common childhood illness with skin eruptions
  • degree lower: smallpox - a contagious and sometimes fatal infectious disease causing pustules
  • synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted infection caused by Treponema pallidum
