groove

noun
habitual, monotonous routine
4.6
Pronunciation: / ɡruv / (AmE), / ɡruːv / (BrE)
a fixed, habitual routine, especially one that has become dull or difficult to change

Examples

Stuck in a groove
After a few years at the same job, he realized he'd fallen into a comfortable groove.
She needed a change to break out of her daily groove and try something new.
The band was stuck in a creative groove and struggled to write new songs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rut - a monotonous routine or pattern of behavior, especially one that is difficult to change
