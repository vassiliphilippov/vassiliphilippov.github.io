bother

noun
source of trouble or annoyance
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːðər / (AmE), / ˈbɒðə / (BrE)
something or someone that causes trouble, difficulty, or annoyance

Examples

A real bother
Carrying all those bags home from the store was quite a bother.
It's not much of a bother to help you with your homework.
The broken lock was a real bother every time I tried to open the door.
She tried to avoid any bother by staying out of the argument.
The sudden bother at the office distracted everyone from their work.
We got into a bit of bother with the neighbors after the party got too loud.
