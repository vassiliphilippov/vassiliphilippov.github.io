Chautauqua

proper noun
adult education movement
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈtɔkwə / (AmE), / ʃəˈtɔːkwə / (BrE)
a late 19th- and early 20th-century American movement that promoted adult education, public lectures, and cultural programs

Examples

Chautauqua movement
The Chautauqua brought speakers and performers to small towns across the United States.
Many communities hosted annual Chautauqua events to encourage lifelong learning.
The influence of the Chautauqua can still be seen in modern adult education programs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lyceum - an organization for public lectures and educational events
