roach

verb
cut off a horse's mane
5.9
Pronunciation: / roʊtʃ / (AmE), / rəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to cut or style hair or a horse's mane so that it stands upright or is rolled back

Examples

To roach a mane
It took her just a few minutes to roach the manes of all the ponies.
Several horses at the show had their manes roached for a more traditional look.
The groom decided to roach the horse before the competition for a neater appearance.
He decided to roach his mane before the horse show.
In the early 1900s, some men would roach their hair to mimic popular cowboy styles.
She asked her stylist to roach her hair for the vintage photo shoot.
