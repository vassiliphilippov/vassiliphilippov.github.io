travesty

verb
parody or distort falsely
9.7 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈtræv.ə.stiː/ /; / /ˈtræv.ɪ.stiː/ (AmE), /ˈtræv.ə.stiː/ /; / /ˈtræv.ɪ.stiː/ (BrE)
to represent or treat something in a way that makes it appear absurd, grotesque, or distorted; to parody or mock falsely or unfairly.

Examples

To travesty the truth
It is dangerous when the media travesties the truth for sensational headlines.
Some critics argue that the play travestied Shakespeare's original intent.
The film completely travesties the historical events on which it is based.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distort - to twist or misrepresent, usually in a negative way
  • synonym: parody - to imitate with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
