unculturable

adjective
cannot be grown in culture
9.5
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌltʃərəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌltʃərəbəl / (BrE)
unable to be grown or maintained in artificial culture conditions, especially in a laboratory setting

Examples

Unculturable bacteria
Some microorganisms are considered unculturable because they cannot survive in laboratory media.
Researchers face challenges when studying unculturable bacteria found in extreme environments.
The majority of soil microbes remain unculturable with current scientific techniques.

Related Terms

  • antonym: culturable - able to be grown or maintained in artificial culture conditions
