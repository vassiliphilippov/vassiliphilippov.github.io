occupancy rate

noun
percentage of computing resources in use
4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.kjə.pən.siː reɪt/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.kjə.pən.siː reɪt/ (BrE)
In information technology and data-center management, the proportion of available computing resources (CPU, memory, storage, or virtual machines) that are currently in use.

Examples

Server occupancy rate
The monitoring dashboard showed an 82% occupancy rate for virtual machines during the spike.
Our team reduced the cluster's occupancy rate by moving low-priority workloads to cheaper instances overnight.
When planning capacity, they predicted future occupancy rates from historical CPU and memory usage patterns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idle capacity - the portion of available computing resources that is not currently used
  • synonym: utilization rate - the proportion of a resource's capacity that is being used; often used interchangeably with occupancy rate in IT contexts
