phenocopy

noun
trait mimicking mutation
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfiː.nə.kɑː.pi/ /; / /ˈfiː.noʊ.kɑː.pi/ (AmE), /ˈfiː.nə.kɒp.i/ /; / /ˈfiː.nəʊ.kɒp.i/ (BrE)
an organism or trait that looks like it is caused by a genetic mutation, but is actually caused by environmental factors

Examples

A phenocopy of a mutation
The drought produced a phenocopy of the mutation, showing the same leaf deformation.
Exposure to certain chemicals can result in a phenocopy that looks identical to a genetic disorder.
Researchers studied how environmental stress created a phenocopy in the fruit flies' wing shape.
The butterfly became a phenocopy of a different species after exposure to unusual temperatures during development.
Researchers observed that the plant was a phenocopy, showing mutant-like leaves due to chemical treatment.
Environmental stress can sometimes produce a phenocopy that mimics a true genetic mutation.
