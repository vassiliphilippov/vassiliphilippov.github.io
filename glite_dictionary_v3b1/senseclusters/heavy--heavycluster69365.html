heavy

adjective
containing a heavier isotope (science)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvi / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi / (BrE)
(of an atom or molecule, in physics or chemistry) containing an isotope with greater than average atomic mass or weight.

Examples

Heavy water
Deuterium is often referred to as heavy hydrogen due to its extra neutron.
Scientists study heavy water to better understand nuclear reactions.
The presence of heavy isotopes in a compound can affect its physical properties.
