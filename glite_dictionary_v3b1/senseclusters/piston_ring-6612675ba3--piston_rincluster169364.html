piston ring

noun
metal ring sealing piston in engine
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪstən rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɪstən rɪŋ / (BrE)
A split metal ring fitted into a groove of a piston in an engine to seal the gap between the piston and the cylinder wall, preventing loss of gases and oil leakage.

Examples

A piston ring seal
High-performance engines often require specialized piston rings for better sealing.
The mechanic replaced the worn piston ring to fix the engine's oil leak.
When a piston ring fails, it can cause a loss of compression in the engine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gasket - device or material used to prevent the escape of fluids between joined surfaces
