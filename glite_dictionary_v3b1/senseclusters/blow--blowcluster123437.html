blow

verb
move something by air current
Pronunciation: / bloʊ / (AmE), / bləʊ / (BrE)
to move or expel air forcefully, either by mouth, wind, or a device (including clearing the nose, cooling food, or moving objects)

Examples

To blow the leaves
Autumn gusts blow leaves across the sidewalk every year.
She used a fan to blow the smoke away from the room.
The strong wind began to blow the papers off my desk.
A stray balloon could be seen blowing across the empty playground.
During the storm, debris blew into the open garage.
The newspapers started to blow down the street as the wind picked up.
He tried to blow out the candle from across the table.
If you blow gently into the flute, you can make a soft sound.
She told the children to blow on their food so it wouldn't burn their tongues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drift - to be carried slowly by a current of air or water
  • synonym: exhale - to breathe out air from the lungs
  • synonym: gust - a sudden strong rush of wind
  • antonym: inhale - to draw air into the lungs
  • synonym: waft - to carry or move gently through the air
