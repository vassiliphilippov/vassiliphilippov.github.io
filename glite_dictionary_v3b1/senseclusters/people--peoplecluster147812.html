people

noun
human beings as a group
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːpəl /; / ˈpipəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːpəl / (BrE)
human beings considered as a group, or the citizens of a country, state, or community

Examples

Many people gather
Many people enjoy spending time outdoors during the summer.
The city is known for its friendly people and vibrant culture.
There were at least 200 people in the audience.
Revolutions are often driven by the will of the people.
She dedicated her life to the welfare of the people.
The politician promised to listen to the people rather than a privileged few.
A new law was introduced to protect the rights of the people.
All the people in the village gathered at the town hall for the meeting.
The people of Italy are known for their hospitality and passion for food.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elite - a select group superior in status or quality
  • synonym: masses - the majority of ordinary people in society
  • synonym: nation - A large group of people united by common descent, history, culture, or language, inhabiting a particular country.
  • degree lower: person - an individual human being
  • degree higher: population - all the inhabitants of a particular area
