white wine

noun
a pale wine made from white or skinless red grapes
Pronunciation: / waɪt waɪn / (AmE), / waɪt waɪn / (BrE)
a pale yellowish wine made from white grapes or from red grapes with the skins removed before fermentation

Examples

A glass of white wine
He prefers white wine over red because it's more refreshing in the summer.
She ordered a glass of white wine to have with her fish.
The crisp flavor of white wine pairs well with light appetizers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: red wine - wine made from dark-colored grapes with skins included during fermentation
  • antonym: rosé - wine with a pink color, produced by allowing some contact with grape skins
