flimsy argument

noun
weak or unconvincing argument
4.4 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪmzi ˈɑrgjəmənt / (AmE), / ˈflɪmzi ˈɑːgjʊmənt / (BrE)
An argument that is weak, unconvincing, or lacking in substance or evidence.

Examples

A flimsy argument
Relying on a flimsy argument will not persuade the committee of your case.
She gave a flimsy argument for her absence, and no one believed her.
The report was dismissed because it was based on a flimsy argument with little supporting evidence.
