border on

verb
be very close to (a quality or condition)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdər ɑn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə ɒn / (BrE)
to be very close to a particular quality, state, or condition (often something undesirable or extreme)

Examples

It borders on obsession
Her enthusiasm for details borders on obsession at times.
Some of the jokes in the show border on being offensive.
The chef's disregard for hygiene borders on negligence.
Her patience with the noisy children borders on saintly.
His jokes often border on being offensive, but he never quite crosses the line.
The enthusiasm at the concert bordered on hysteria as the band took the stage.
