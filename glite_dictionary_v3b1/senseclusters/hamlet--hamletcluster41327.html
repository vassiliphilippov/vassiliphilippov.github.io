hamlet

noun
very small settlement
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmlət / (AmE), / ˈhæmlət /; / ˈhæmlɪt / (BrE)
a very small settlement, smaller than a village, usually without its own church or local government

Examples

A small hamlet
The hamlet had only a dozen houses and no shops.
They live in a quiet hamlet surrounded by rolling hills and farmland.
We stopped to rest at a small hamlet nestled by the river.
The hamlet barely had a dozen houses huddled along a single lane.
They moved to a quiet hamlet nestled in the hills.
Tourists often overlook the picturesque hamlet on their way to the city.
