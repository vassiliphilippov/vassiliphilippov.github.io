granite

noun
hard crystalline igneous rock
3.7 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈgræn.ɪt/ (AmE), /ˈgræn.ɪt/ (BrE)
A very hard, coarse-grained, crystalline igneous rock composed mainly of quartz, feldspar, and mica, often used in building and monuments.

Examples

Granite countertop
Ancient statues carved from granite have survived for centuries.
The kitchen countertops were made of polished granite.
They chose a granite headstone for its durability and elegant appearance.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock
  • synonym: marble - a metamorphic rock used in sculpture and building
