survivance

noun
continuation of rights
Pronunciation: / sərˈvaɪvəns / (AmE), / səˈvʌɪvəns / (BrE)
the continuation of rights, property, or title by a surviving person after another's death, especially in legal contexts

Examples

Survivance of property
The survivance of the estate allowed the widow to remain in the family home.
Legal documents specified the survivance of certain rights to the eldest son.
In some cases, survivance ensures that property passes directly to a surviving partner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inheritance - the act of receiving property or rights after someone's death
  • synonym: survivorship - the right of a surviving person to take property or rights
