propositionally

adverb
in terms of propositions
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːpəˈzɪʃənəli / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃənəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on propositions or statements, especially in logic or philosophy

Examples

Propositionally explain logic
The theory can be explained propositionally to clarify its logical structure.
She approached the debate propositionally, focusing on the truth of each statement.
In formal logic, arguments are often analyzed propositionally rather than contextually.

  • synonym: logically - in a way that uses logic or reasoning
