clinocephaly

noun
congenital defect with a concave skull
Pronunciation: / ˌklaɪnoʊˈsɛfəli / (AmE), / ˌklɪnəʊˈsɛfəli / (BrE)
a congenital condition in which the top of the head is abnormally depressed, resulting in a concave shape instead of the normal convex contour

Examples

Congenital clinocephaly condition
Doctors studied cases of clinocephaly to better understand its causes and potential treatments.
Early detection of clinocephaly can help guide appropriate medical intervention.
The infant was diagnosed with clinocephaly after an examination of his skull shape.
