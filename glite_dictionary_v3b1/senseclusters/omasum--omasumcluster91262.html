omasum

noun
third compartment of a ruminant's stomach
9.9 9.4
Pronunciation: / oʊˈmeɪsəm / (AmE), / əʊˈmeɪsəm / (BrE)
the third compartment of the stomach in ruminant animals, situated between the reticulum and the abomasum, involved in the absorption of water and nutrients.

Examples

The omasum stomach
Food passes from the reticulum into the omasum before reaching the abomasum.
The omasum plays a crucial role in water absorption during a cow's digestion.
Veterinarians inspected the omasum to determine the cause of the animal's digestive problems.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: reticulum - second compartment of a ruminant's stomach, honeycomb-structured
  • degree lower: rumen - first compartment of a ruminant's stomach, where fermentation occurs
