dam

noun
barrier to hold back water
2.2 1.6
Pronunciation: /dæm/ (AmE), /dæm/ (BrE)
A barrier built across a river or stream to hold back water, control its flow, or create a reservoir.

Examples

A river dam
Farmers rely on the dam to irrigate their fields during the dry season.
Heavy rainfall caused water to overflow the dam and flood the surrounding areas.
The construction of the dam provided electricity and water to the nearby cities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embankment - a wall or bank of earth or stone built to prevent a river flooding an area
  • synonym: levee - an embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river
  • synonym: weir - a small dam built across a river to raise the level of water upstream
