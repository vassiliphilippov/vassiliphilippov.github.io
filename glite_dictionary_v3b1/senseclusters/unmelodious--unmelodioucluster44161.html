unmelodious

adjective
not tuneful; harsh-sounding
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.məˈloʊ.di.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.məˈləʊ.di.əs / (BrE)
not having a pleasant or musical sound; sounding harsh or discordant

Examples

An unmelodious sound
Crows are known for their harsh and unmelodious calls.
Despite his talent, his singing voice was rather unmelodious and flat.
The unmelodious notes from the poorly tuned piano filled the room.
His voice was harsh and unmelodious during the recital, disappointing the audience.
The parrot's unmelodious screeches startled everyone in the room.
They complained about the unmelodious clanging that echoed from the old factory.
