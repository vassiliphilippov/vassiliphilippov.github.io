sponge

verb
obtain things for free from others
7.9 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / spÊŒndÊ’ / (AmE), / spÊŒndÊ’ / (BrE)
to obtain money, food, or other benefits from someone by continually taking advantage of their generosity; to live at another's expense.

Examples

To sponge money
After losing his job, he started sponging money from his relatives to get by.
He always tries to sponge off his friends instead of paying for meals himself.
She didn't want to sponge on anyone's hospitality during her visit.

Synonyms

  • mooch
    8.5 0.36/m
    mooch asks for small amounts from friends or strangers, while sponge often means living off someone by constant taking.
  • leech
    7.5 0.33/m
    leech takes from others without giving back and feels very harsh, while sponge can sound more casual for living off someone.
  • cadge
    9.8 0.11/m
    cadge asks or persuades people for things, often one time, while sponge suggests staying at someone's expense over time.
  • bum
    7.5 0.01/m
    bum asks for free food or small favors in a casual way, while sponge implies depending on someone long term.
  • grub
    8.5 0.02/m
    grub begs for food or money in a low, greedy manner, while sponge focuses on taking ongoing support from others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mooch - to obtain or try to obtain something without paying for it
  • synonym: scrounge - to obtain something by seeking it from others, often without paying
