cooldown

noun
period for cooling
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkuːlˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈkuːlˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a period of time when a machine or device is allowed to cool after heavy use to prevent damage or ensure safety

Examples

Engine cooldown
After running at full speed, the engine required a cooldown before it could be safely inspected.
The technician scheduled a short cooldown to prevent the equipment from overheating.
Always allow a proper cooldown period after using heavy machinery to avoid accidents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: warm-up - a period of preparation before using a machine or device
