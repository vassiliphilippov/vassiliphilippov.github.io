garganey

noun
a small Eurasian species of duck
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːr.ɡəˌneɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑː.ɡə.ni / (BrE)
A small migratory duck (Anas querquedula) found across Eurasia, with a distinctive white stripe on the male's head.

Examples

A garganey duck
Every spring, birdwatchers gather at the wetlands hoping to spot a rare garganey.
Flocks of garganeys migrate from Africa to Europe during breeding season.
The male garganey can be identified by its striking white eye stripe.
