nester

noun
person settling on government land for ownership
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛstər / (AmE), / ˈnɛstə / (BrE)
a person who settles lawfully on government land with the intent to acquire title to it

Examples

A land nester
As a nester, she hoped her claim would soon become permanent.
Many nesters built cabins and started farms on the western frontier.
The homestead laws allowed a nester to settle on public land and eventually gain ownership.
