toot

verb
make a sharp sound with a horn or whistle
3.3 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀêt / (AmE), / tuÀêt / (BrE)
to make a short, sharp sound, especially with a horn, whistle, or similar instrument

Examples

To toot a horn
Children tooted on party horns as the parade passed by.
The impatient driver tooted his horn several times at the intersection.
The train tooted its whistle loudly as it approached the station.

Synonyms

  • beep
    1.3 2.54/m
    a beep is a short, high electronic or horn-like sound, while a toot is a briefer, sharper sound from small horns or whistles.
  • honk
    1.6 2.49/m
    a honk is a loud, harsh car horn noise used for warning, while a toot is a shorter, sharper sound from small horns or whistles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blare - to make a loud, harsh sound
  • synonym: honk - to make the characteristic sound of a car horn
