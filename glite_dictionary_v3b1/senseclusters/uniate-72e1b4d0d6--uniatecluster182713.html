Uniate

adjective
of an Eastern church united with Rome, retaining own rites
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪeɪt / (BrE)
relating to an Eastern Christian church or community that has entered into communion with the Roman Catholic Church but retains its own liturgy and traditions.

Examples

Uniate tradition
Historians have studied the Uniate movement in Eastern Europe extensively.
Some communities identify as Uniate, maintaining a distinct identity from both Orthodox and Roman Catholic churches.
The Uniate churches preserve ancient Byzantine rites within Roman Catholicism.
