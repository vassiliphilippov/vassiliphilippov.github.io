regeneration

noun
regrowth of tissue or organs (biology)
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriˌdʒɛnɚˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(biology) the process by which lost or damaged tissue, organs, or parts are renewed or grown again.

Examples

Lizard regeneration
Many lizards are capable of regeneration, allowing them to regrow their tails after losing them.
Scientists are studying the regeneration of nerve cells to help treat spinal injuries.
The regeneration of a starfish's arm can take several months.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: repair - the process of restoring damaged tissue without complete regrowth
