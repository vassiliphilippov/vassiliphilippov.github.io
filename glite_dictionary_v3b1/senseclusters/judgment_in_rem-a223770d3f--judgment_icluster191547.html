judgment in rem

noun
legal judgment about property or status
5.5 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɪn rem/ (AmE), /ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt ɪn rem/ (BrE)
a legal judgment that determines the status or ownership of a specific property or thing, rather than determining the rights or liabilities of individuals

Examples

A judgment in rem case
A judgment in rem is binding on all parties with an interest in the property, not just the named defendants.
Bankruptcy proceedings may result in a judgment in rem affecting creditors' rights to specific assets.
The court issued a judgment in rem declaring the ship to be the property of the plaintiff.
A judgement in rem determines the legal status of the property involved, not the personal liability of parties.
Disputes over ownership may be resolved through a judgement in rem judgment.
The court issued a judgement in rem declaring the ship subject to forfeiture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: judgement in personam - a legal judgment directed against a specific person
  • antonym: judgment in personam - a judgment directed at a specific person, imposing a personal liability or obligation
