Riesling

noun
a variety of white grape; aromatic white wine
Pronunciation: / ˈriːz.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈriːz.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a variety of white grape of German origin, used for making aromatic white wines, especially in Europe and California

Examples

Riesling grapes
Many vineyards in California produce award-winning Riesling wines each year.
She prefers white wines like Riesling for their floral and fruity aromas.
The chef recommended a dry Riesling to pair with the spicy Thai dish.
