ranchland

noun
land for livestock
Pronunciation: / ˈræntʃˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈrɑːntʃˌlænd / (BrE)
land used for raising cattle or other livestock, typically as part of a ranch

Examples

Ranchland for cattle
The family owns several acres of ranchland in Texas.
Much of the region's ranchland is used for raising cattle and horses.
Developers are interested in buying old ranchland to build new homes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farmland - land used for growing crops or raising animals
  • synonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals
