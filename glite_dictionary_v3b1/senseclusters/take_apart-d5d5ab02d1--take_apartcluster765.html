Synonyms

  • disassemble
    3.8 0.9/m
    disassemble is a formal word for carefully separating parts, while take apart is the common everyday phrase for the same action.
  • dismantle
    4.2 3/m
    dismantle is used for taking systems or machines apart, often removing parts, while take apart is more general for any object.
  • break down
    2.8 10/m
    break down can mean separating for study or by force, while take apart focuses on physically dismantling items.
  • pull apart
    3.2 1.0/m
    pull apart means using force to split pieces by pulling, while take apart is neutral and does not imply how you separate.
  • break apart
    3.0 1/m
    break apart suggests splitting into pieces, often suddenly or by force, while take apart is the neutral act of disassembly.
  • break up
    2.7 3/m
    break up often describes ending groups or splitting into parts socially, while take apart focuses on physically dismantling objects.
