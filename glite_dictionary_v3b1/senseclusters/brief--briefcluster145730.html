brief

noun
legal document outlining a case
5.2
Pronunciation: / briːf / (AmE), / briːf / (BrE)
a written legal document outlining the facts and points of law involved in a case, prepared by one side to support their argument in court

Examples

A legal brief
His main argument was outlined clearly in the brief prepared for the case.
The lawyer submitted a detailed brief to the court in support of her client.
You must read the brief before appearing in front of the judge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: memorandum - a written message, especially in business or diplomacy
  • synonym: pleading - a formal statement of the cause of an action or defense
