canonicity

noun
status as canonical
Pronunciation: / ˌkænəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌkænəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being recognized as part of an accepted group, standard, or canon

Examples

Canonicity of a text
The canonicity of certain ancient texts is still a topic of scholarly debate.
Questions about the canonicity of the painting arose after new evidence was discovered.
Literary critics often discuss the canonicity of works included in academic syllabi.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real
  • synonym: legitimacy - the quality of being lawful or accepted as valid
  • antonym: nonconformity - failure or refusal to follow accepted rules or standards
