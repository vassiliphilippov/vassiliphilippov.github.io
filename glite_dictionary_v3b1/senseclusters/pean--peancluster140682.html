pean

noun
ancient Greek hymn of praise
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːən / (AmE), / ˈpiːən / (BrE)
a song or hymn of praise, especially one sung in ancient Greece to invoke or thank a deity

Examples

An ancient pean song
Ancient warriors often sang a pean before marching into battle.
The poet compared her song to a pean sung by the Greeks.
The priests chanted a solemn pean in honor of Apollo during the ceremony.
