cradle

noun
origin or birthplace of development
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪdəl /; / ˈkreɪ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪ.dəl /; / ˈkreɪ.dl̩ / (BrE)
the place or period where something begins or develops in its early stages (literal or figurative)

Examples

Cradle of civilization
Egypt is often called the cradle of civilization because of its ancient history.
Many believe that the university is the cradle of innovative ideas.
The region is considered the cradle of modern democracy.
From the cradle, he was taught to value honesty above all else.
She was raised in the cradle of a small mountain village.
The tradition has been passed down from the cradle through countless generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: birthplace - the place where something originates
  • antonym: grave - place where a dead body is buried (often as a metaphor for death or end of life)
