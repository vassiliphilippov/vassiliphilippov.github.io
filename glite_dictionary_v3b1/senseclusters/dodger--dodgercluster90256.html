dodger

noun
shifty or deceptive person
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːdʒər /; / ˈdɑdʒər / (AmE), / ˈdɒdʒə / (BrE)
a person who avoids obligations, duties, or rules, often by dishonest or clever means

Examples

A clever dodger
Everyone knew him as a dodger who was always looking for a way around the rules.
She's no dodger, and always fulfills her responsibilities honestly.
The tax dodger was finally caught by the authorities after years of evasion.
The government is cracking down on tax dodgers who hide their income.
Draft dodgers faced criticism for avoiding military service during the war.
Some dodgers use fake identities to escape their legal responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - a person who behaves dishonestly to gain an advantage
  • antonym: complier - a person who follows rules or fulfills obligations
  • synonym: evader - a person who avoids or escapes something, especially by cleverness
  • antonym: honest person - a person who is truthful and does not deceive
  • synonym: shirker - a person who avoids work or responsibility
