pseudonym

noun
fictitious name used as an alias
7.7 2.62/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsuÀêd…ôÀån…™m / (AmE), / ÀàsjuÀêd…ôn…™m / (BrE)
A fictitious name, especially one used by an author or performer instead of their real name.

Examples

Use a pseudonym
His real identity was hidden behind the pseudonym he used in all his works.
Many authors choose to publish under a pseudonym to maintain their privacy.
The singer adopted a pseudonym early in her career.

  • nom de plume
    8.2 0.16/m
    nom de plume is a French-style name authors adopt for publication, while pseudonym is the general English term for a made-up name.
  • anonym
    9.6 0.00/m
    anonym is a name someone uses to hide their real identity in a role, while pseudonym covers many such made-up names.
  • alias
    7.0 2.67/m
    alias is a false or assumed name often used to hide identity in law or crime, while pseudonym is any chosen fake name.
  • assumed name
    6.3 0.24/m
    assumed name is a formally adopted name someone takes to hide identity, while pseudonym often refers to pen or stage names.
  • nom de guerre
    9.9 0.15/m
    nom de guerre is a name used for a public or political role, often in conflict, while pseudonym covers any made-up name.
  • false name
    3.9 0.36/m
    false name is a plain phrase for a name used to deceive or avoid detection, while pseudonym is a broader term for any invented name.
