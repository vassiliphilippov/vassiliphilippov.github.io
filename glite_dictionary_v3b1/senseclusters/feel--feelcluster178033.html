feel

verb
believe or think based on emotion/intuition
1.7 571/m
Pronunciation: / fil / (AmE), / fiÀêl / (BrE)
to believe or think something, especially based on emotion or intuition rather than definite evidence

Examples

To feel right
Do you feel we are making the right decision?
I feel that she will be perfect for the job.
Many people feel that the situation is getting out of control.

Synonyms

  • think
    1.0 2491/m
    think is a neutral word for having an opinion or idea, while feel implies belief based more on emotion or intuition.
  • find
    3.7 267/m
    find often means forming an opinion after experience or thought, while feel points to a belief rooted more in emotion or intuition.
  • believe
    1.0 721/m
    believe means accept something as true and sometimes trust it, while feel suggests a personal belief based more on emotion or intuition.

Related Terms
