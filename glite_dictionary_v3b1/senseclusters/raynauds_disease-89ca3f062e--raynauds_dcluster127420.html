Raynaud's disease

noun
reduced blood flow disorder
6.5
Pronunciation: / reɪˈnoʊz dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / reɪˈnəʊz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a medical condition in which blood flow to fingers and toes is reduced, usually triggered by cold or stress, causing them to become numb and turn white or blue

Examples

Raynaud's disease symptoms
During winter, her Raynaud's disease made her fingers turn pale and numb whenever she went outside.
People with Raynaud's disease are advised to keep their hands and feet warm to prevent attacks.
His doctor diagnosed him with Raynaud's disease after he reported frequent color changes in his toes.
During winter, people with Raynaud's disease may notice their fingers turning white or blue.
Her doctor diagnosed her with Raynaud's disease after she complained of numbness in her toes.
Wearing gloves can help prevent attacks of Raynaud's disease in cold weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Raynaud's phenomenon - a condition with similar symptoms, sometimes used as a broader term
  • degree lower: chilblain - a mild cold injury causing redness and swelling of the skin
  • antonym: frostbite - an injury caused by freezing of the skin and underlying tissues
