service

noun
work or employment for another
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrvɪs /; / ˈsɝːvɪs /; / ˈsɝː.vɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪs /; / ˈsɜː.vɪs / (BrE)
help, work, or assistance provided to others, especially as a job or commercial offering (including customer service, serving in restaurants, and work done for an employer or country)

Examples

Customer service
After twenty-five years of dedicated service, she finally retired from the company.
He received a medal in recognition of his service to the community.
Most soldiers serve a minimum term of service before they can retire.
Customer service representatives are available 24/7 to assist with your needs.
He performed an invaluable service for the community by organizing the event.
This online service allows users to back up their files securely.
She complained to the manager about the slow service during lunch.
The service at the new café was prompt and courteous, making us feel very welcome.
We chose that hotel because it is known for its excellent service in the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aid - help, typically of a practical nature
  • synonym: assistance - help or support provided to someone
  • antonym: disservice - an act that harms rather than helps
  • synonym: duty - a task or action someone is required to perform
  • synonym: employment - the state of having paid work
  • antonym: goods - physical items that are produced and sold, contrasted with services
  • synonym: hospitality - friendly and generous reception of guests or strangers
  • synonym: labor - work, especially physical work
  • antonym: neglect - failure to properly attend to someone or something
