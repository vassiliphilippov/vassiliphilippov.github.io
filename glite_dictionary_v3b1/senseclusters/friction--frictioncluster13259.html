friction

noun
obstacles in user experience or processes
3.9 1.25/m

Obstacles, unnecessary effort, or points of resistance in a process or digital interface that make it harder for users to complete a task.

Examples

User friction
Too much friction in the checkout flow frustrates customers and lowers conversion.
The new design reduced friction, allowing users to complete account setup in under two minutes.
Developers track analytics to identify where friction causes users to abandon tasks in the app.

Synonyms

  • drag
    4.2 6.24/m
    drag slows or delays progress in systems or interfaces, while friction describes resistance that makes user tasks harder to complete.
  • speed bump
    3.2 0.23/m
    speed bump describes a problem that briefly slows progress, while friction means ongoing resistance that makes user tasks harder to complete.
  • obstruction
    4.4 2.61/m
    obstruction physically blocks a path or flow, while friction refers to smaller process or interface resistances that slow or complicate tasks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obstacle - something that makes it difficult to achieve an aim or complete a task
  • synonym: usability issue - a problem in a user interface that prevents or hinders users from accomplishing goals easily
