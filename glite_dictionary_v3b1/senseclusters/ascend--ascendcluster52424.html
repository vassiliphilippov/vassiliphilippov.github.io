ascend

verb
go back in family or succession line
4.2 6.6
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsend/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsend/ (BrE)
(of inheritance or lineage) to go back in order of genealogical succession.

Examples

Ascend the family line
Inheritance laws sometimes require property to ascend the family line before passing to distant relatives.
The title may ascend several generations to find the rightful heir.
To trace ownership of the land, you must ascend through the family tree.
