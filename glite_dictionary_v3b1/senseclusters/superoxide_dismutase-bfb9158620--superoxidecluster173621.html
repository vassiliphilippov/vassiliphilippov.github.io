superoxide dismutase

noun
enzyme converting superoxide into oxygen and hydrogen peroxide
6.8
Pronunciation: / suːpərˌɑːksaɪd dɪsˈmjuːteɪs / (AmE), / suːpərˌɒksaɪd dɪsˈmjuːteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that speeds up the breakdown of superoxide radicals into hydrogen peroxide and oxygen, protecting cells from oxidative damage

Examples

Superoxide dismutase enzyme
A deficiency in superoxide dismutase has been linked to certain neurodegenerative diseases.
Researchers measured the activity of superoxide dismutase in the blood samples of patients with inflammation.
The presence of superoxide dismutase in cells helps protect tissues from oxidative stress.
