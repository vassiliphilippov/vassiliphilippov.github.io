(in elections with more than two options) the number of votes received by the candidate or party with the most votes, when it is less than half of the total votes cast.

Examples

Election plurality
In a three-way race, she secured a plurality, finishing ahead of her two opponents.
No party achieved a majority, but the Conservatives claimed a plurality in Parliament.
The candidate won with a plurality of votes, not an absolute majority.
