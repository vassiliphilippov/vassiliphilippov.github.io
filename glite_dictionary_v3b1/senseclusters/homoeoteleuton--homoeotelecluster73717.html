homoeoteleuton

noun
copying omission error
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊmiˌoʊtəˈluːtən / (AmE), / ˌhəʊmiəʊtɛˈluːtɒn / (BrE)
an error in writing or copying where a section of text is accidentally omitted because two lines or phrases end similarly

Examples

Homoeoteleuton in manuscripts
The ancient manuscript contained a homoeoteleuton that changed the meaning of the passage.
Scholars identified a homoeoteleuton in the copied text, where a line was accidentally skipped.
Careful proofreading can help prevent homoeoteleuton in important documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haplography - the accidental omission of a repeated letter or group of letters in writing
