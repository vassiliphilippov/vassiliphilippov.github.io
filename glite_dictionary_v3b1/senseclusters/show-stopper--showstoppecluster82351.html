show-stopper

noun
an outstanding performance that halts a show
5.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà Éo ä Àåst…ëÀêp…ôr / (AmE), / Àà É…ô ä Àåst…íp…ô / (BrE)
A performance or act in a show that is so impressive or exciting that it draws prolonged applause, temporarily halting the show.

Examples

Show-stopper performance
Every time he performs that song, it's a show-stopper and the crowd loves it.
Her solo was a true show-stopper that brought the audience to their feet.
The comedian's unexpected joke was such a show-stopper that the applause took several minutes to die down.
