rhetor

noun
teacher of rhetoric
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtɔr /; / ˈriːtɔr / (AmE), / ˈriːtɔː / (BrE)
a person skilled in the art of rhetoric (public speaking or persuasive writing), especially in ancient Greece or Rome

Examples

In ancient Athens, a skilled rhetor could influence political decisions through persuasive speech.

The ancient rhetor captivated the audience with his speech.
As a skilled rhetor, she could persuade even the most skeptical listeners.
In classical Athens, a rhetor played an important role in public debates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: audience - a group of people who listen to a speaker
  • synonym: orator - a person who gives formal speeches
  • synonym: rhetorician - an expert in the art of rhetoric
  • synonym: speaker - a person who speaks in public
