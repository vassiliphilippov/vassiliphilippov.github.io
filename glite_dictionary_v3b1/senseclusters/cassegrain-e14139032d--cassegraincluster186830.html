Cassegrain

noun
reflecting telescope type
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsəˌɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæsəɡreɪn / (BrE)
a type of reflecting telescope that uses a concave primary mirror and a convex secondary mirror to focus light

Examples

Cassegrain telescope
The astronomer adjusted the Cassegrain to get a clearer view of the planet.
Many modern observatories are equipped with a large Cassegrain for deep space observation.
She learned how to align the mirrors in a Cassegrain during her telescope workshop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Newtonian - a reflecting telescope design using a flat secondary mirror
  • synonym: reflector - a telescope that forms images using mirrors
