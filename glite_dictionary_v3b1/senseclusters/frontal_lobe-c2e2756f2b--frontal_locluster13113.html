frontal lobe

noun
front part of the brain's cortex
4.8 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfrʌn.təl loʊb/ (AmE), /ˈfrʌn.təl ləʊb/ (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex located at the front of each cerebral hemisphere, directly behind the forehead, responsible for voluntary movement, decision-making, and problem-solving

Examples

The frontal lobe brain
Damage to the frontal lobe can affect personality and behavior.
Scientists have discovered how the frontal lobe contributes to decision making.
The frontal lobe plays a crucial role in voluntary movement and higher-level thinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occipital lobe - rearmost portion of the cerebral cortex, involved in visual processing
  • synonym: parietal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex behind the frontal lobe involved in sensory perception
  • synonym: temporal lobe - part of the cerebral cortex beneath the frontal and parietal lobes, involved in auditory processing
