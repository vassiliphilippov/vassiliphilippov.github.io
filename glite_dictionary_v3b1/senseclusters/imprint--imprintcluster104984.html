imprint

verb
to establish firmly in the mind
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɪnt / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɪnt / (BrE)
to fix an idea, memory, or image firmly in someone's mind so that it is not easily forgotten

Examples

To imprint a memory
Her passion for music was imprinted on her mind from a young age.
The terrible images of the disaster were imprinted in everyone's memory.
These childhood lessons will imprint lasting values on future generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engrave - to impress deeply as if carved
  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember
