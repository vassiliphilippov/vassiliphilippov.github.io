Middle Greek

noun
Greek language from 600 to 1200 AD
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdəl ɡrik /; / ˈmɪdəl ˈɡrik / (AmE), / ˈmɪdəl ɡriːk /; / ˈmɪdəl ˈɡriːk / (BrE)
the stage of the Greek language spoken and written from about 600 to 1200 AD, between Ancient and Modern Greek

Examples

Middle Greek language
Many religious texts from the period are written in Middle Greek.
Scholars study Middle Greek to learn about the language of the Byzantine Empire.
The development of Middle Greek influenced later forms of the Greek language.
Many important Byzantine texts were written in Middle Greek.
Scholars often study Middle Greek to understand the transition from Ancient to Modern Greek.
The pronunciation of Middle Greek differs from both its ancient and modern forms.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Modern Greek - the Greek language as spoken and written since around 1453
