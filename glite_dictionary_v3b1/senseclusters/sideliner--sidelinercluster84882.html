sideliner

noun
non-participating observer
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.laɪ.nər/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.laɪ.nə/ (BrE)
a person who is present at an event but does not actively take part in it

Examples

A quiet sideliner
He remained a sideliner during the discussion, listening but not joining in.
As a sideliner at the match, she cheered for her team from the edge of the field.
Instead of being a sideliner, you should try participating in the activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bystander - a person who is present but not involved in an event
  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in an activity
