black vulture

noun
New World vulture species of the Americas
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈvʌltʃər /; / blæk ˈvʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / blæk ˈvʌltʃə / (BrE)
a large, dark-plumaged vulture found in various parts of the world, known for scavenging; may refer to different species in the Americas or Eurasia/Africa

Examples

A black vulture group
A group of black vultures gathered near the edge of the forest to feed.
The black vulture soared gracefully over the open fields in search of food.
Unlike the turkey vulture, the black vulture flaps its wings more frequently when flying.
Researchers observed a black vulture circling over the savanna in northern Africa.
The black vulture is one of the largest birds of prey found in Eurasia.
Unlike the New World species, the black vulture of Eurasia has a distinctive mane of feathers around its neck.
