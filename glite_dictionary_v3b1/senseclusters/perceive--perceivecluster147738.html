perceive

verb
become aware through the senses
3.7 12/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàsiv / (AmE), / p…ôÀàsiÀêv / (BrE)
to become aware of something through the senses, especially sight or hearing

Examples

To perceive a sound
From the deck, you could perceive the outline of mountains in the distance.
She immediately perceived the faint scent of perfume as she entered the room.
Some animals can perceive sounds at frequencies that are inaudible to humans.

Synonyms

  • discern
    5.8 9.48/m
    discern is to recognize something with effort or careful thought, while perceive is simply to become aware through the senses.
  • sense
    4.2 35/m
    sense is to detect or feel something physically or intuitively, while perceive focuses on becoming aware through the senses.
  • notice
    1.0 164/m
    notice is to see or pay attention to something quickly, while perceive is a broader, more sensory awareness.
  • detect
    3.2 43/m
    detect is to discover something often hidden or hard to find, while perceive means simply to become aware by the senses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: notice - to become aware of something, especially visually
  • synonym: sense - to become aware of something using the senses
