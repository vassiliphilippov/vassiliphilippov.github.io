lesser ape

noun
small agile ape, e.g. gibbon or siamang
8.8 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈles.ər eɪp/ (AmE), /ˈles.ə eɪp/ (BrE)
Any of a group of small, agile apes of the family Hylobatidae, including gibbons and siamangs, characterized by long arms and a lack of a tail.

Examples

A lesser ape species
Gibbons are the most well-known species of lesser ape found in Southeast Asia.
The siamang, a type of lesser ape, has a distinctive throat pouch used to amplify its calls.
Unlike great apes, the lesser ape family is known for their remarkable agility in the treetops.

  • antonym: great ape - large ape, such as gorilla or chimpanzee
