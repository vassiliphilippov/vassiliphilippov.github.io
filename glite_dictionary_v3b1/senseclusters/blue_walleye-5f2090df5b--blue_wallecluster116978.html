blue walleye

noun
extinct Great Lakes walleye variety
Pronunciation: / blu ˈwɑːl.aɪ / (AmE), / blu ˈwɒl.aɪ / (BrE)
a now-extinct variety of walleye (Sander vitreus glaucus), formerly found in the Great Lakes and distinguished by its bluish coloration

Examples

The blue walleye fish
Fishermen often recounted stories of catching blue walleye in the early 20th century.
The blue walleye once thrived in Lake Erie but is now considered extinct.
The disappearance of the blue walleye has been linked to overfishing and environmental changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: walleye - a North American freshwater fish, Sander vitreus
