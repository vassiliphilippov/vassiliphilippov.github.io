beam

noun
the widest side of a ship
Pronunciation: / biːm / (AmE), / biːm / (BrE)
the widest part of a ship (measured at its broadest point)

Examples

Ship's beam
Keep clear of the beam during rough weather to avoid capsizing.
The enemy vessel approached from the port beam at sunrise.
The lifeboat drifted alongside the ship's beam before being secured.
The captain measured the beam amidships to ensure compliance with regulations.
The yacht has a beam of nearly 20 feet, making it exceptionally stable.
With a wider beam, the vessel can carry more cargo without losing balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: length - the measurement of something from end to end (opposite of width)
  • synonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
