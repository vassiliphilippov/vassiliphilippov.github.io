feel

verb
experience an emotion or state of mind
1.0 550/m
Pronunciation: / fil /; / fiÀêl / (AmE), / fiÀêl / (BrE)
to experience or be aware of an emotion, physical sensation, or state

Examples

To feel happy
Do you ever feel overwhelmed by your responsibilities?
It's normal to feel disappointed after hearing bad news.
She began to feel anxious as the deadline approached.
Many families feel the consequences of economic hardship.
She felt the impact of the decision even though she was not involved.
We all felt the tension in the room when they arrived.
It feels strange to walk through the empty school at night.
The silk feels smooth and luxurious against the skin.
This chair feels sturdy and well-made.
