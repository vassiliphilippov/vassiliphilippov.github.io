hyperbilirubinemia of the newborn

noun
high bilirubin level in newborn infants
8.6
Pronunciation: / haɪpɚ bɪlɪˌruːbɪˈniːmiə ʌv ðə ˈnuːˌbɔrn / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəbɪlɪˌruːbɪˈniːmiə əv ðə ˈnjuːˌbɔːn / (BrE)
a condition in newborn infants characterized by an abnormally high level of bilirubin in the blood, typically caused by immaturity of the liver and usually resolving without treatment

Examples

Hyperbilirubinemia of the newborn condition
Doctors monitor babies closely for signs of hyperbilirubinemia of the newborn after birth.
Most cases of hyperbilirubinemia of the newborn are mild and resolve within a few days without treatment.
Phototherapy is sometimes used to treat hyperbilirubinemia of the newborn if bilirubin levels become too high.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neonatal jaundice - yellowing of the skin and eyes in newborns caused by high bilirubin levels
