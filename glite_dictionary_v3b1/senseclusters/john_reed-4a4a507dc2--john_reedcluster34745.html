John Reed

proper noun
American journalist and writer (1887–1920)
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn rid / (AmE), / dʒɒn riːd / (BrE)
(1887–1920) American journalist and writer known for his firsthand reporting on the Russian Revolution and for founding the Communist Labor Party in America.

Examples

John Reed Russian Revolution
John Reed reported from Petrograd during the Russian Revolution and published his accounts in the renowned book 'Ten Days That Shook the World.'
As a founder of the Communist Labor Party, John Reed played an important role in the history of American leftist movements.
The influence of John Reed can still be felt in political journalism today, especially among those covering revolutionary events.
