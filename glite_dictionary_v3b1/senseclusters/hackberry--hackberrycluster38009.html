hackberry

noun
small edible dark purple to black berry
Pronunciation: / ˈhækˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈhækˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A small edible dark purple to black berry with a large pit, produced by certain trees of the genus Celtis, native especially to the southern United States.

Examples

A hackberry fruit
Many birds feed on the hackberry during the late summer months.
Old country recipes sometimes include hackberries as a wild fruit ingredient.
She picked a ripe hackberry from the tree and tasted its sweet flesh.
