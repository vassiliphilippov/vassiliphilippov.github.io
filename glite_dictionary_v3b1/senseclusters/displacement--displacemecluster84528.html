displacement

noun
removal from office or employment
4.4 5.0
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of removing or relocating someone or something from its position or environment

Examples

Displacement of people
His sudden displacement from the company shocked the entire staff.
The displacement of the director was met with both relief and anger among employees.
Union leaders protested the unfair displacement of workers during the corporate restructuring.
Industrial pollution often results in the displacement of plants from their natural ecosystems.
Surging river levels can cause the displacement of soil and nutrients downstream.
The construction of the dam led to the displacement of several animal species from their habitats.
