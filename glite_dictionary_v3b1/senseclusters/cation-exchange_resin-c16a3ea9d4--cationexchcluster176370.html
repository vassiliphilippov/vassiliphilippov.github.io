cation-exchange resin

noun
synthetic resin for cation exchange
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkætaɪən ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / ˈkætiɒn ɪksˌtʃeɪndʒ ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic resin with negatively charged sites that exchanges cations in a solution, used for separation and purification processes

Examples

A cation-exchange resin
A cation-exchange resin is commonly used to remove calcium ions from hard water.
Laboratory technicians rely on cation-exchange resin columns to purify samples.
The performance of a cation-exchange resin depends on its capacity and selectivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anion-exchange resin - synthetic resin that exchanges anions in a solution
