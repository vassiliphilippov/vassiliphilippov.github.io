Xer

noun
member of Generation X
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈzɛr / (AmE), / ˈzɛː / (BrE)
a person who belongs to Generation X, typically born between the early 1960s and early 1980s

Examples

A Xer at work
Many Xers adapted quickly to new technology in the workplace.
As an Xer, she remembers life before the internet became common.
The survey showed that Xers value work-life balance more than previous generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boomer - a person born during the baby boom, typically between 1946 and 1964
  • antonym: millennial - a person born between the early 1980s and mid-1990s to early 2000s
