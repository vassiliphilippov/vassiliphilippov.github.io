baked blush

noun
long-lasting blush made by baking pigments
8.0
Pronunciation: / beɪkt blʌʃ / (AmE), / beɪkt blʌʃ / (BrE)
A type of cosmetic blush made by baking colored liquid pigments on terra cotta tiles, resulting in a long-lasting and vibrant powder used to color the cheeks.

Examples

A baked blush powder
Many makeup artists favor baked blush because it blends easily and lasts all day.
She prefers using a baked blush for its natural, luminous finish on her skin.
The baked blush in her makeup bag had a marbled appearance that looked beautiful in the compact.
