reapportionment

noun
redistribution of seats or representation, especially by census
9.1 0.28/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê…ôÀàp…îr É…ônm…ônt / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê…ôÀàp…îÀê É…ônm…ônt / (BrE)
the act of redistributing or reallocating something, especially the assignment of congressional seats in the United States according to changes in population as determined by the census

Examples

The reapportionment of seats
Every ten years, after the census, the government undertakes reapportionment to reflect population shifts.
Legal experts argued over the fairness of the latest reapportionment plan.
The contentious reapportionment of legislative districts sparked debate among lawmakers.

Synonyms

  • reallotment
    8.2 0.00/m
    reallotment refers to assigning congressional seats after the census, while reapportionment is the formal legal process for that change.
  • reallocation
    5.5 0.42/m
    reallocation means assigning or moving items again in many settings, while reapportionment specifically names U.S. congressional seat redistribution after the census.
  • redistribution
    4.5 6.27/m
    redistribution refers to sharing resources or wealth to reduce inequality, while reapportionment deals with assigning political seats after a census.
