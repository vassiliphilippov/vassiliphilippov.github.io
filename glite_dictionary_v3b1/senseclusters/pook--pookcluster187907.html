pook

noun
Celtic folklore spirit
Pronunciation: / puːk / (AmE), / puːk / (BrE)
a mischievous supernatural being in Celtic folklore, often taking animal form and playing tricks on people

A pook plays tricks

In old stories, a pook might appear as a black horse to lead travelers off their path.
Children were warned not to follow a pook into the woods after sunset.
