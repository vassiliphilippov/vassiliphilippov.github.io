Solanum pseudocapsicum

noun
ornamental shrub with toxic berries
Pronunciation: / soʊˈleɪnəm ˌsuːdoʊˈkæpsɪkəm / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm ˌsjuːdəʊˈkæpsɪkəm / (BrE)
A small South American shrub cultivated as a houseplant for its ornamental but poisonous red or yellow cherry-sized fruit.

Examples

Solanum pseudocapsicum plant
Gardeners are advised to keep Solanum pseudocapsicum out of reach of children and pets due to its toxicity.
Many people grow Solanum pseudocapsicum indoors for its attractive, cherry-sized fruits.
The bright berries of the Solanum pseudocapsicum add a festive touch to winter decor.
