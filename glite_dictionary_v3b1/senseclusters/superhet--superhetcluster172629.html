superhet

noun
type of radio receiver
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌhɛt / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəhɛt / (BrE)
a radio receiver that uses a process of combining a locally generated frequency with an incoming signal to produce an intermediate frequency for easier amplification and demodulation

Examples

A superhet radio
Many modern radios still use variations of the superhet design for reliable signal processing.
The engineer explained how a superhet works by converting incoming signals to an intermediate frequency.
The old radio in my grandfather's attic was actually a superhet model from the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superheterodyne - a radio receiver design using frequency mixing to convert a received signal to a fixed intermediate frequency
