methyl group

noun
chemical group – CH₃ (from methane)
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːθɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈmiːθɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a chemical group consisting of one carbon atom bonded to three hydrogen atoms (CH₃), commonly found in organic molecules

Examples

A methyl group atom
Adding a methyl group to the compound makes it more hydrophobic.
Chemists often replace a hydrogen atom with a methyl group to modify a molecule's properties.
The methyl group attaches to the DNA strand, affecting gene expression.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ethyl group - a chemical group consisting of two carbon atoms and five hydrogen atoms (C₂H₅)
  • degree lower: methane - a colorless, odorless, flammable gas (CH₄), the simplest alkane
  • synonym: radical - an atom or group of atoms with an unpaired electron, especially in chemical reactions
