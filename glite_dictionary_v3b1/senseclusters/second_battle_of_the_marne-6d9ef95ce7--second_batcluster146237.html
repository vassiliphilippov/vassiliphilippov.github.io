Second Battle of the Marne

noun
World War I battle in France, 1918
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkənd ˈbætəl əv ðə mɑrn / (AmE), / ˈsɛkənd ˈbætəl əv ðə mɑːn / (BrE)
the decisive World War I battle in July–August 1918 in northwestern France, where the Allies halted and defeated a major German offensive

Examples

The Second Battle of the Marne victory
Historians often cite the Second Battle of the Marne as beginning the final phase of the conflict.
Many soldiers lost their lives during the fierce fighting at the Second Battle of the Marne.
The Second Battle of the Marne marked a turning point in World War I in favor of the Allies.
