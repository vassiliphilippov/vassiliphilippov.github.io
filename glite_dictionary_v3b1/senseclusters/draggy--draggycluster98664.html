draggy

adjective
slow and dull
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡi / (AmE), / ˈdræɡi / (BrE)
tiring, slow, or lacking in energy or excitement

Examples

Draggy lecture
The lecture felt especially draggy after a long day at work.
I found the first half of the book quite draggy, but it got better later.
Their meeting was so draggy that several people struggled to stay awake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dull - not interesting or exciting
  • antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • synonym: tedious - boring and too slow or long
