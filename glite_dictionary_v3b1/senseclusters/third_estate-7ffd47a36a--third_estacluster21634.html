third estate

noun
the common people (historical)
Pronunciation: / θɝd ɪˈsteɪt / (AmE), / θɜːd ɪˈsteɪt / (BrE)
the class of common people, as distinct from the nobility and clergy, especially in pre-revolutionary France

Examples

The third estate class
During the French Revolution, the third estate demanded more rights and representation.
Most of the population in pre-revolutionary France belonged to the third estate.
The grievances of the third estate helped spark widespread social change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clergy - the religious class (historically the First Estate)
  • antonym: nobility - the aristocratic class (historically the Second Estate)
