varietal

noun
wine made from a single grape type
9.9
Pronunciation: / vəˈraɪɾəl / (AmE), / vəˈraɪətəl / (BrE)
A wine made primarily from one type of grape and named after that grape variety, such as Chardonnay or Merlot.

Examples

A varietal wine
She prefers a varietal over a blended wine because of its distinctive flavor profile.
The wine list features varietals from both the local region and international vineyards.
This vineyard produces several award-winning varietals, including a popular Pinot Noir.
