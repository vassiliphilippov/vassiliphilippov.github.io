upsell

verb
to promote upgrades
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsɛl / (BrE)
to encourage a customer to purchase a more expensive or upgraded product or service

Examples

To upsell a customer
The salesperson tried to upsell me to a premium membership with extra features.
During checkout, the website attempted to upsell accessories for my new phone.
Restaurants often upsell by suggesting appetizers or desserts to customers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cross-sell - to encourage a customer to buy additional related products
  • antonym: downsell - to encourage a customer to buy a less expensive product or service
