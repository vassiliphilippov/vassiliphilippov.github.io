adequacy

noun
being sufficient or suitable
Pronunciation: / ˈæd.ə.kwə.si /; / ˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ˈad.ɪ.kwə.si /; / ˈadɪkwəsi / (BrE)
the quality of being sufficient or suitable for a particular purpose or need

Examples

The adequacy of resources
Her adequacy for the role was never in question, given her years of experience.
The committee assessed the adequacy of the safety measures put in place.
There was some debate about the adequacy of the evidence to support the claim.
She doubted the adequacy of her preparation for the exam.
The adequacy of the emergency shelters was questioned after the hurricane.
We need to assess the adequacy of our current resources before launching the project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inadequacy - the state of being insufficient or not enough
  • synonym: sufficiency - the state of being sufficient; adequacy
