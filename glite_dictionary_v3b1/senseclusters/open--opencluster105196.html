open

adjective
not defended; vulnerable to attack
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.pən /; / ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.pən /; / ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
not closed or blocked; allowing access, passage, or view; exposed or not covered

Examples

An open door
After the army retreated, the borders were left open to invasion.
The commander declared the city open to avoid unnecessary destruction.
With its defenses down, the fortress became open to enemy forces.
He noticed the open door and realized someone had entered the house.
She found the open envelope lying on her desk.
The store left an open container in the fridge overnight.
Keep the doors open so that everyone can come and go easily.
The new policy makes government records more open to the public.
The store will remain open late on Fridays for holiday shoppers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defended - protected or guarded against attack
  • synonym: exposed - not protected or covered; left open to view or harm
  • antonym: protected - kept safe from harm or injury; shielded
