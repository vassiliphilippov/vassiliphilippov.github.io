swallow

verb
to take back words or statements
3.7 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈswɑː.loʊ/ /; / /ˈswɔː.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈswɒ.ləʊ/ (BrE)
to suppress, retract, or not express something such as words, feelings, or speech

Examples

To swallow your words
After realizing his mistake, he had to swallow his earlier remarks during the meeting.
She didn't want to swallow her words, but the evidence left her no choice.
When proven wrong, he reluctantly swallowed what he had said about the project.
Despite his frustration, he managed to swallow his anger and respond calmly.
He had to swallow his disappointment and congratulate the winner.
She tried to swallow her pride and accept the apology.
He swallowed his apology, making it barely audible to those around him.
Nervous about the announcement, he swallowed his words and hurried off the stage.
She often swallows the ends of her sentences when she's embarrassed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bottle up - to refuse to express your feelings
  • antonym: enunciate - to pronounce words clearly
  • synonym: mumble - to speak in a quiet and unclear way
  • synonym: recant - to publicly withdraw or renounce a previously held opinion or statement
  • synonym: retract - to withdraw a statement or accusation
  • synonym: suppress - to prevent yourself from showing an emotion or feeling
