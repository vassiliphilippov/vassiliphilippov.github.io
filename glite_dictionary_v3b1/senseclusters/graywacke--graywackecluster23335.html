graywacke

noun
coarse sedimentary rock
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪˌwæki / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌwæki / (BrE)
a dark, coarse-grained sedimentary rock made up of quartz, feldspar, and rock fragments

Examples

Dark graywacke rock
The geologist collected samples of graywacke from the riverbed for analysis.
Layers of graywacke can be seen in the exposed rock formations along the coast.
Many ancient mountain ranges are composed largely of graywacke and other sedimentary rocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock made mainly of sand-sized mineral particles
  • synonym: shale - a fine-grained sedimentary rock formed from mud
