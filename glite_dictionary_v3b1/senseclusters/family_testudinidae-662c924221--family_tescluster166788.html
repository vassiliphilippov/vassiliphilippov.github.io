family Testudinidae

noun
family of land tortoises
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli tɛˌstjuːdɪˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli tɛˌstjuːdɪˈniːdiː / (BrE)
A family of land-dwelling tortoises distinguished by their domed shells and heavy, stumpy legs, found in various regions worldwide.

Examples

The family Testudinidae tortoises
Researchers are studying the evolution of shells among members of the family Testudinidae.
The Galápagos giant tortoise belongs to the family Testudinidae, which includes many land tortoises worldwide.
Unlike aquatic turtles, the family Testudinidae consists of species adapted exclusively to life on land.
