steam locomotive

noun
train engine powered by steam
Pronunciation: / stiːm loʊˈkoʊməˌtɪv / (AmE), / stiːm ləʊˈkəʊmətɪv / (BrE)
A railway locomotive powered by a steam engine, typically using coal or wood to heat water and produce steam.

Examples

A steam locomotive engine
Rail enthusiasts gathered to watch the restored steam locomotive pass through the countryside.
She photographed a steam locomotive as it released clouds of white vapor at the station.
The steam locomotive was once the dominant mode of power for trains worldwide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diesel locomotive - a locomotive powered by a diesel engine
  • antonym: electric locomotive - a locomotive powered by electricity
