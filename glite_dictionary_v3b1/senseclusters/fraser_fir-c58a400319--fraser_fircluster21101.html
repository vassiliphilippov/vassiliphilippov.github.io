Fraser fir

noun
a North American species of fir tree
7.9 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfreɪzər fɪr / (AmE), / ˈfreɪzə fɜː / (BrE)
a small, fast-growing fir tree (Abies fraseri) native to the southern Appalachian Mountains of the United States, valued for its short, soft needles and often used as a Christmas tree

Examples

A Fraser fir tree
Many tree farms grow Fraser firs because of their pleasing fragrance and strong branches.
Scientists are monitoring the health of Fraser fir populations in the Appalachian Mountains.
The Fraser fir is a popular choice for Christmas trees in the United States.
