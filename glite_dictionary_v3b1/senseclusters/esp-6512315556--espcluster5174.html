E.S.P.

noun
supposed ability to perceive beyond normal senses
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåiÀê Àå…õs Ààpi / (AmE), / ÀåiÀê Àå…õs ÀàpiÀê / (BrE)
the supposed ability to gain information about an object, person, or event through means other than the known senses; extrasensory perception

Examples

E.S.P. ability
Many people claim to have E.S.P. and believe they can sense things others cannot.
Scientific studies have tried to prove the existence of E.S.P., but with little success.
The psychic was famous for her alleged E.S.P. abilities during investigations.

Synonyms

  • ESP
    8.8 0.77/m
    ESP is the same abbreviation written without dots, while E.S.P. uses punctuation and often appears in more formal or older writing.
  • extrasensory perception
    8.7 0.08/m
    extrasensory perception is the full phrase used in formal or scientific writing, while E.S.P. is the short, familiar abbreviation people use.
  • clairvoyance
    9.8 0.35/m
    clairvoyance is the claimed power to see things at a distance, while E.S.P. is a broader term for various extrasensory abilities.
