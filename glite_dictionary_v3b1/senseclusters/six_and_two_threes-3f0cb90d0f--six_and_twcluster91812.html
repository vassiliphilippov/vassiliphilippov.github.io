six and two threes

x
no real difference between options
Pronunciation: / sɪks ənd tu ˈθriz / (AmE), / sɪks ənd tu ˈθriːz / (BrE)
used to indicate that two options or situations are essentially the same or make no real difference

Examples

Six and two threes are equal
For me, Coke or Pepsi is six and two threes—they taste very similar.
Honestly, it's six and two threes whether we drive or take the train.
The results will be six and two threes no matter which route we choose.
