biotransformation

noun
biological chemical conversion
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˌtrænsfərˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˌtrænsfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of living organisms, cells, or enzymes to convert a compound into a desired product, especially in industrial or laboratory processes

Examples

Industrial biotransformation
The laboratory specializes in biotransformation to produce environmentally friendly chemicals.
Researchers studied the biotransformation of pollutants by bacteria in contaminated soil.
Pharmaceutical companies use biotransformation to create new drugs from natural substances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biocatalysis - the use of natural catalysts, like enzymes, to speed up chemical reactions
  • antonym: chemical synthesis - the process of creating chemical compounds by chemical reactions
