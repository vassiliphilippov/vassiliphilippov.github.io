rolling stone

noun
person who often moves or changes
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlɪŋ stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlɪŋ stəʊn / (BrE)
a person who does not settle in one place or job and is always moving or changing

Examples

A rolling stone moves
After college, he became a true rolling stone, traveling the world and never settling down.
His parents hoped he'd quit being a rolling stone and finally find a permanent job.
She admired his adventurous spirit but worried he was too much of a rolling stone to build a stable life.
Ever since graduating, he's been a rolling stone, traveling from country to country.
Her friends call her a rolling stone because she's always seeking new adventures.
Nobody was surprised when the rolling stone quit his job after just three months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drifter - a person who moves from place to place with no fixed home
  • antonym: settler - a person who settles in a fixed place
