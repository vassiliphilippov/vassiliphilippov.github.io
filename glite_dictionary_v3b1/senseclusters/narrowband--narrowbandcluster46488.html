narrowband

noun
limited frequency range
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊˌbænd / (BrE)
a signal, channel, or transmission that uses a small range of frequencies, typically for communication or data transfer

Examples

A narrowband channel
The radio station uses a narrowband to transmit emergency alerts to the local area.
Engineers recommended switching from narrowband to broadband for faster data transfer.
A narrowband is often less susceptible to certain types of interference in communication systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broadband - a signal or channel that uses a wide range of frequencies
