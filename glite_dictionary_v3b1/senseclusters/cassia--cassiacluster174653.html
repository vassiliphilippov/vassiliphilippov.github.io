cassia

noun
Chinese tree used for cinnamon
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsiə / (AmE), / ˈkæsiə / (BrE)
A Chinese tree with aromatic bark, used as a source of cinnamon that is considered less desirable than Ceylon cinnamon.

Examples

Cassia bark spice
Some people prefer the milder flavor of Ceylon cinnamon over that of cassia.
The bark of cassia is commonly sold as cinnamon in many grocery stores.
Traditional Chinese medicine often uses the bark of cassia for its warming properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cinnamon - A spice made from the inner bark of several tree species, including cassia.
