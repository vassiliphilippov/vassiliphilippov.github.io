bare

adjective
lacking in quantity; just enough
3.5
Pronunciation: / bɛr / (AmE), / beə /; / bɛə / (BrE)
only just enough; consisting of the minimum or most basic amount, with nothing extra

Examples

Bare minimum
After paying the bills, there was only a bare amount of money left for groceries.
His explanation covered only the bare essentials of the problem.
The refugees survived on a bare diet during the crisis.
The proposal passed with a bare majority of votes in favor.
There was just a bare possibility of finishing the work on time.
They achieved the bare minimum required to qualify for the competition.
She gave him only the bare facts of what happened, omitting all unnecessary details.
The report presents the bare truth, without any sugarcoating.
We just about managed to cover the bare necessities of life on our budget.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ample - more than enough; plentiful
  • synonym: meager - lacking in quantity or quality
  • synonym: mere - nothing more than; only
  • synonym: minimal - of the least possible amount or degree
  • synonym: scanty - insufficient in amount or quantity
  • synonym: simple - not elaborate or complicated; plain
