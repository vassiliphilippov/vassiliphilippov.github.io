bellwether

noun
leading sheep in a flock, marked with a bell
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛlˌwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌwɛðə / (BrE)
A sheep that leads the flock, often wearing a bell around its neck to help the shepherd locate the herd.

Examples

A bellwether sheep
The farmer relied on the bellwether to guide the other animals across the field.
The shepherd tied a small bell around the neck of the bellwether to keep track of the flock.
When the bellwether moved, the rest of the sheep instinctively followed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leader - a person or thing that leads others
