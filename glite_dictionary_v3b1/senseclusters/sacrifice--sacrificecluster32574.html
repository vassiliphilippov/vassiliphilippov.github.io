sacrifice

verb
give up something valuable for a purpose
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsækrəˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsak.rɪ.faɪs / (BrE)
to give up something important or valuable in order to help someone else or achieve something

Examples

To sacrifice your time
He sacrificed a promising career in order to care for his aging parents.
Many parents sacrifice their own needs to ensure their children have better lives.
She was willing to sacrifice her free time to help her friend finish the project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gain - to obtain or receive something, often contrasted with loss
  • synonym: relinquish - to give up or let go of something
  • synonym: surrender - to give up something voluntarily
