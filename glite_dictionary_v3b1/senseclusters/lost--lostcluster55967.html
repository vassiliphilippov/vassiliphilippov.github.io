lost

adjective
not noticed or understood because of distraction
1.4
Pronunciation: / lɔst / (AmE), / lɒst / (BrE)
not heard, seen, or understood because of noise, confusion, or other distractions

Examples

Words lost in noise
Her words were lost amid the loud applause from the audience.
Some details were lost as the radio crackled with static.
The meaning of his speech was lost in all the background noise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: missed - not caught or received; not noticed
  • antonym: perceived - noticed or apprehended by the senses or mind
