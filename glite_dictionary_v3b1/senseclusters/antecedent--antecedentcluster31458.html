antecedent

adjective
preceding; earlier in time or order
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.təˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
Preceding in time or order; coming before something else.

Examples

Antecedent law
Historical documents often contain laws antecedent to current regulations.
The committee reviewed all antecedent events before making a decision.
Their agreement was influenced by antecedent discussions held months earlier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preceding - existing or coming before in time or order
  • antonym: subsequent - coming after in time or order
