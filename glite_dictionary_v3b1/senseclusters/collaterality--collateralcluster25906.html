collaterality

noun
side-by-side arrangement
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˌlætəˈræləti / (AmE), / kəˌlætəˈræləti / (BrE)
the state of being situated at the side of or running alongside something, especially in anatomy or morphology

Examples

Nerve collaterality
The collaterality of the nerves allowed for alternative pathways in case of injury.
Researchers studied the collaterality of blood vessels in the affected tissue.
Understanding collaterality is important when mapping the structure of plant roots.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centrality - the quality of being in the center
  • synonym: laterality - the quality of having sides or being side-oriented
