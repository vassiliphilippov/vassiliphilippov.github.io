thrill

verb
to excite strongly; fill with intense emotion
1.0
Pronunciation: / θrɪl / (AmE), / θrɪl / (BrE)
to cause or experience a sudden strong feeling of excitement or pleasure

Examples

To thrill the crowd

It thrills me to see my children so happy.
The announcement of her victory thrilled everyone in the auditorium.
Riders thrill at the steep drops on the roller coaster.
She thrilled to the news that she had won first prize.
The concert crowd was thrilled by the unexpected encore.
