liposome

noun
artificial microscopic lipid vesicle
4.6 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪp.əˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈlɪp.ə.səʊm / (BrE)
a microscopic artificial vesicle composed of a lipid bilayer, used to deliver substances such as drugs or nucleic acids into cells

Examples

A liposome vesicle
By using liposome technology, vaccines can be made more stable and efficient.
Researchers use liposomes to deliver genetic material into target cells during gene therapy.
The new cancer drug is encapsulated in liposomes to improve its effectiveness and reduce side effects.
