austenite

noun
gamma-phase iron with dissolved carbon
6.3 9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.stə.naɪt/ /; / /ˈɔː.stə.naɪt/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.stə.naɪt/ /; / /ˈɔː.stə.naɪt/ (BrE)
A solid solution of carbon in gamma-phase iron, present in steel and iron alloys at high temperatures, which transforms into pearlite or martensite upon cooling.

Examples

Austenite steel phase
Metallurgists study the properties of austenite to improve manufacturing processes.
The transformation of austenite into martensite is crucial for hardening steel.
When steel is heated above a certain temperature, austenite forms in its crystalline structure.
