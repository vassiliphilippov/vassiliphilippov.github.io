whiff

noun
strikeout from swinging and missing (baseball)
Pronunciation: / wɪf / (AmE), / wɪf / (BrE)
(Baseball) A strikeout in which the batter swings at and misses the ball for the third strike.

Examples

A whiff in baseball
Fans cheered as the ace struck him out with a high fastball for the final whiff.
He walked to the dugout after a frustrating whiff at the plate.
The pitcher recorded his tenth whiff of the game in the seventh inning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strikeout - an instance when a batter is ruled out after three strikes
