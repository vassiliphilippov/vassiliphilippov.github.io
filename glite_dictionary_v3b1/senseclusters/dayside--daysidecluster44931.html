dayside

noun
sunlit hemisphere
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌsaɪd / (BrE)
the side of a planet, moon, or other celestial body that is illuminated by the sun at a given time

Examples

The dayside of Earth
The probe collected data from the dayside of Mars to study its atmosphere.
Temperatures on the dayside of Mercury can reach extreme highs due to direct sunlight.
Scientists compared the dayside and nightside weather patterns on the exoplanet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nightside - the side of a planet or moon not facing the sun and in darkness
