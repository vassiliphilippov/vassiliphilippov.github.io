rebalance

verb
to become balanced again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbæləns / (BrE)
to become balanced again, especially after a change or disruption, often through automatic or system action

Examples

System rebalances automatically
After the outage, the system automatically rebalanced to distribute the workload evenly.
The investment portfolio rebalanced itself after several market fluctuations.
When the weights shifted, the platform rebalanced to maintain stability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unbalance - to disturb the balance or stability of something
