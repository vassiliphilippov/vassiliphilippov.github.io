Volgograd

proper noun
city in southwest Russia; site of WWII battle
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊlɡəˌɡrɑd / (AmE), / ˈvɒlɡəˌɡrɑːd / (BrE)
A city in southwest Russia on the Volga River, formerly known as Stalingrad, famous as the site of a decisive World War II battle.

Examples

Volgograd city
During our trip to Russia, we spent three days exploring Volgograd and its famous war memorials.
Many visitors to Volgograd stop at Mamayev Kurgan to see the towering statue of The Motherland Calls.
The Battle of Stalingrad, now Volgograd, marked a turning point in World War II.
