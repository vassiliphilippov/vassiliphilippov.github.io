multiflora rose

noun
vigorous rose with many small flowers
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈflɔrə roʊz / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈflɔːrə rəʊz / (BrE)
A species of rose with vigorous growth and clusters of many small flowers, often used for hedges and as grafting stock.

Examples

Multiflora rose hedge
Gardeners often plant multiflora rose as a natural hedge for privacy.
The multiflora rose has become invasive in several regions due to its rapid spread.
The delicate blooms of the multiflora rose appear in clusters throughout the summer.
