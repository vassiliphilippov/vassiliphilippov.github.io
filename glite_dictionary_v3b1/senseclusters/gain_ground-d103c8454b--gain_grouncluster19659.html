gain ground

verb
to make progress or gain an advantage
5.4
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪn ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡeɪn ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to make progress or become more successful, especially in comparison to others; to improve one's position or advantage

Examples

To gain ground quickly
Renewable energy technologies are gaining ground as costs continue to decline.
She worked hard to gain ground on her competitors in the race for the promotion.
The team started slowly but began to gain ground in the second half of the game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose ground - to fail to maintain an advantage or position; to fall behind
