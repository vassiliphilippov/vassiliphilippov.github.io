consolidative

adjective
tending to combine into a single unit
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɑlɪˌdeɪtɪv /; / kənˈsɑːlɪˌdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / kənˈsɒlɪˌdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
having the tendency or effect of combining things into a single unit or unified whole

Examples

A consolidative approach
A consolidative approach was adopted to unify the various departments under one management.
Medical imaging can reveal consolidative changes in lung tissue characteristic of certain diseases.
The two companies entered into a consolidative merger to streamline their operations.
His approach was more consolidative than expansive, focusing on strengthening what was already achieved.
In medicine, consolidative therapy may be used to prevent a disease from returning after initial treatment.
The company pursued a consolidative strategy to combine its various business units.

Related Terms
