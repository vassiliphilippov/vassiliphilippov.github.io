escort

verb
accompany for protection or courtesy
4.0 11/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õsk…îrt /; / Àà…õsÀåk…îrt / (AmE), / Àà…õsk…îÀêt / (BrE)
to go with someone to protect, guide, or as a courtesy

Examples

To escort someone home
She asked her friend to escort her home after the party.
The police escorted the VIP through the crowded airport.
Two armored vehicles escorted the truck carrying valuable cargo.
Parents are asked to escort their children inside the building after dark.
She politely escorted her guests to the door at the end of the party.
The security guard escorted the visitor to the conference room.

Synonyms

    3.5 16/m
    accompany means going with someone as a companion on any trip, while escort means going with someone to protect or guide.
  • see
    3.9 27/m
    see often means walking someone to a door or place as a courtesy, while escort includes protection or guidance.
  • lead
    1.0 86/m
    lead means going in front to show direction and take someone somewhere, while escort stresses going with and protecting.
  • guide
    1.0 22/m
    guide means showing the way or directing movement, often with instruction, while escort focuses on accompanying and protection.
  • chaperone
    5.5 0.18/m
    chaperone means watching and supervising someone in social situations, often to ensure good behavior, while escort is more general accompaniment.
