escort

verb
accompany for protection or courtesy
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskɔrt /; / ˈɛsˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɛskɔːt / (BrE)
to go with someone to protect, guide, or as a courtesy

Examples

To escort someone home
She asked her friend to escort her home after the party.
The police escorted the VIP through the crowded airport.
Two armored vehicles escorted the truck carrying valuable cargo.
Parents are asked to escort their children inside the building after dark.
She politely escorted her guests to the door at the end of the party.
The security guard escorted the visitor to the conference room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accompany - to go somewhere with someone
  • antonym: follow - to go after or behind someone
  • synonym: lead - to guide someone to a place
