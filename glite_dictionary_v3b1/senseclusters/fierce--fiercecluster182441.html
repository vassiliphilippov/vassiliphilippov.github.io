fierce

adjective
intense and violent in force or energy
3.9 3.0
Pronunciation: / fɪrs / (AmE), / fɪəs / (BrE)
having or showing strong intensity, aggression, force, or passion (used for people, animals, emotions, weather, or competition)

Examples

A fierce competition
She gave a fierce glare that silenced the noisy classroom instantly.
The fierce storm uprooted trees and damaged several houses along the coast.
The debate became fierce as both sides defended their opinions passionately.
Despite his fierce criticism, the team remained determined to succeed.
He spoke with fierce conviction about the need for justice.
Her fierce loyalty to her friends never wavered, even when things got difficult.
A fierce storm disrupted flights across the region.
The argument erupted into a fierce shouting match.
The coastline was battered by fierce winds all night.
