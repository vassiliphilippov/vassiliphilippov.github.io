class structure

noun
organization of social classes in society
4.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /klæs ˈstrʌk.tʃər/ (AmE), /klɑːs ˈstrʌk.tʃə/ (BrE)
the way in which a society is organized into social classes, often based on economic, social, or occupational status.

Examples

The class structure of society
Her research focuses on mobility within the class structure of modern societies.
Many sociologists study how changes in the economy affect the class structure of a country.
The class structure of Victorian England was rigid and highly stratified.

Related Terms

  • synonym: social hierarchy - the arrangement of individuals into a ranked order within a society
