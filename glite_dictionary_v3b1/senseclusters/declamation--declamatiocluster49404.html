declamation

noun
formal, forceful speech
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal speech delivered with strong emotion and practiced expression, often as a performance or exercise

Examples

A declamation speech
During the competition, each student presented a passionate declamation of a famous speech.
His fiery declamation on freedom resonated with the audience.
The politician’s declamation stirred strong emotions among the listeners.
At the annual contest, competitors are judged on their declamation and ability to engage the audience.
His dramatic declamation of the famous speech captivated the entire class.
The student delivered a declamation from Shakespeare with remarkable poise and feeling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extempore - spoken or done without preparation (contrast with prepared speech)
  • degree higher: harangue - a lengthy and aggressive speech
  • degree lower: lecture - an educational talk to an audience
  • synonym: oration - a formal speech, especially one given on a ceremonial occasion
  • synonym: recitation - the action of repeating something aloud from memory
