erg

noun
cgs unit of work or energy
9.5 8.1
Pronunciation: / ɜrɡ / (AmE), / ɜːɡ / (BrE)
a unit of work or energy in the centimetre–gram–second (cgs) system, equal to the work done by a force of one dyne acting over a distance of one centimetre

Examples

One erg unit
One erg is a very small unit, so it is rarely used outside physics and astronomy.
Scientists often convert ergs to joules for easier calculations in the SI system.
The explosion released an energy of several billion ergs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: joule - SI unit of work or energy equal to 10 million ergs
