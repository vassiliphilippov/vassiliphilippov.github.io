blind

noun
hunter's shelter or concealment
6.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /blaɪnd/ (AmE), /blaɪnd/ (BrE)
A shelter or hiding place used by hunters to conceal themselves from game, especially ducks.

Examples

Hunting blind
From their camouflaged blind, they watched the birds gather in the early morning light.
He built a simple wooden blind near the edge of the field to conceal himself from deer.
The hunters sat quietly in the blind, waiting for ducks to land on the pond.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hide - a shelter used to observe or shoot game animals without being seen
