dry up

verb
lose water or moisture
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdraɪ ˈʌp /; / draɪ ˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌdraɪ ˈʌp /; / draɪ ˈʌp / (BrE)
to lose all water or moisture and become completely dry, often becoming shriveled or withered

Examples

To dry up completely
All the paint will dry up if you leave the can open overnight.
His creativity seemed to dry up after years of working nonstop.
The river began to dry up during the long summer drought.
After months without rain, the fields have completely dried up.
If you don’t water the plant, it will dry up and die.
The lake began to dry up during the drought, leaving cracked mud behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dehydrate - to lose or remove water from; to become dry
  • antonym: moisten - to make or become slightly wet
  • synonym: shrivel - to contract and wrinkle from loss of moisture
  • synonym: wither - to become dry and shriveled
