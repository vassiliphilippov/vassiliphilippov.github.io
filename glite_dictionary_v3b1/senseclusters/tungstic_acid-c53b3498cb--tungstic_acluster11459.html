tungstic acid

noun
a chemical acid of tungsten
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌŋ(st)stɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈtʌŋ(st)stɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
an oxyacid of tungsten, typically yellow and polymeric, obtained by acidifying tungstate solutions

Examples

Tungstic acid solution
The laboratory synthesized tungstic acid by adding hydrochloric acid to sodium tungstate.
When heated, tungstic acid decomposes to form tungsten trioxide.
Yellow tungstic acid is often used as a precursor for tungsten metal production.
