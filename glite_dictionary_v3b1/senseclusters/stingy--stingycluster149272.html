stingy

adjective
unwilling to spend; miserly
5.2 1.09/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÉªndÊ’i / (AmE), / ËˆstÉªndÊ’i / (BrE)
Unwilling to spend money, time, or resources; mean or miserly.

Examples

Stingy with money
Despite being wealthy, she was surprisingly stingy with her donations.
Don't be stingy with your compliments when someone does a good job.
My landlord is so stingy that he refuses to turn on the heating in winter.

Synonyms

  • miserly
    7.9 0.31/m
    miserly sounds more formal and harsher about refusing money, while stingy is the common everyday word.
  • parsimonious
    9.9 0.61/m
    parsimonious is a formal word for extreme frugality, while stingy is a more casual word for not spending.
  • penurious
    9.9 0.05/m
    penurious describes very extreme or poor-sounding stinginess in formal writing, while stingy is used in everyday speech.
  • penny-pinching
    7.5 0.04/m
    penny-pinching shows very small careful saving in daily life, while stingy simply means unwilling to spend or give.
  • tightfisted
    8.0 0.16/m
    tightfisted describes someone who keeps money tightly and refuses gifts, while stingy is a broader basic word for meanness.
  • tight
    7.7 1.29/m
    tight is an informal word for being careful with money, while stingy sounds slightly more negative and personal.
  • closefisted
    8.2 0.00/m
    closefisted means unwilling to spend or give money in a firm way, while stingy is the usual plain term.
  • hardfisted
    9.9 0.00/m
    hardfisted describes a tough, money-holding person who resists spending, while stingy focuses more on lack of generosity.
  • cheap
    6.5 6.53/m
    cheap often describes low cost or a person who embarrassingly avoids spending, while stingy is about being mean with money.
  • chintzy
    8.8 0.05/m
    chintzy feels informal and suggests small, poor-looking stinginess, while stingy is the general word for not giving or spending.
  • mingy
    9.1 0.00/m
    mingy is an informal British word for meanly keeping or sharing things, while stingy is more widely used everywhere.
  • ungenerous
    6.2 0.18/m
    ungenerous describes a general lack of giving or sharing, while stingy specifically focuses on being tight with money or resources.
