driven

adjective
compelled by external force
5.0 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪvən / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvən / (BrE)
very motivated or compelled to act, either by strong internal drive or by outside forces

Examples

Highly driven person
Many people are driven to crime due to poverty.
She felt driven by circumstances beyond her control.
The crowd was driven by fear to take drastic action.
His driven personality helped him become the youngest company CEO.
She is a driven employee who never gives up on a project.
The driven students stayed late every night to prepare for the competition.
Despite the obstacles, his driven nature kept him focused on his goals.
Many social reformers are driven by a deep sense of justice.
She was a driven young lawyer, determined to make partner within five years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambitious - having a strong desire for success or achievement
  • antonym: apathetic - having or showing little or no emotion or motivation
  • synonym: determined - firmly decided, not easily swayed
  • degree lower: motivated - having a strong reason to act, especially from internal desires
  • antonym: unmotivated - lacking the desire or interest to achieve or succeed
