dissolutely

adverb
in a morally unrestrained, licentious way
9.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd…™s…ôÀàluÀêtli / (AmE), / Àåd…™s…ôÀàluÀêtli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of moral restraint or indulges in sensual pleasures; in a licentious or dissolute manner.

Examples

Behave dissolutely at party
He behaved dissolutely at the party, disregarding all sense of propriety.
She accused him of spending his youth dissolutely rather than pursuing an education.
The king was known to live dissolutely, squandering wealth on pleasure and vice.

Synonyms

  • licentiously
    9.9 0.03/m
    licentiously shows sexual freedom and promiscuity, while dissolutely means general lack of moral restraint and indulgent living.
  • wantonly
    9.3 0.05/m
    wantonly means acting without restraint, often sexual or cruel, while dissolutely refers to indulgent, morally loose behaviour.
  • profligately
    9.9 0.02/m
    profligately describes wasteful, reckless excess and luxury, while dissolutely focuses more on sensual indulgence and moral looseness.
