succinctness

noun
concise verbal or written expression
9.9
Pronunciation: / səkˈsɪŋkt.nəs / (AmE), / səkˈsɪŋkt.nɪs / (BrE)
the quality of expressing much in few words; terseness and economy in writing or speech

Examples

Succinctness in writing
His report lacked succinctness, making it difficult to identify the main points.
Newspapers value succinctness in reporting the news accurately and quickly.
The succinctness of her presentation impressed the entire audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief but comprehensive
  • antonym: verbosity - the use of too many words
