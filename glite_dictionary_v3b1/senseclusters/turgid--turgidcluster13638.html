turgid

adjective
pompous or inflated in style
8.9 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ùÀêd í…™d / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀêd í…™d / (BrE)
(of language or style) pompous, inflated, or overblown; attempting to sound important or impressive but often perceived as tedious or pretentious.

Examples

Turgid writing style
Critics panned the novel for its turgid prose and lack of clarity.
Her turgid speech was filled with long, complicated words meant to impress the audience.
The professor's turgid lecture left the students confused and bored.

Synonyms

  • bombastic
    8.5 0.81/m
    bombastic is loud and high-sounding with little real meaning, while turgid sounds pretentious and often feels long and boring.
  • tumid
    9.9 0.00/m
    tumid is formally inflated and feels pretentious in style, while turgid shows pompous language that tires the reader.
  • orotund
    9.9 0.01/m
    orotund is rich and grand in sound or style, while turgid is inflated to seem important but often feels dull.
  • declamatory
    8.3 0.09/m
    declamatory is loud and theatrical in speech to make an impression, while turgid is wordy and pompous on the page.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombastic - inflated or pretentious in speech or writing
  • synonym: grandiloquent - using lofty or extravagant language
  • antonym: simple - plain, clear, and straightforward in style
