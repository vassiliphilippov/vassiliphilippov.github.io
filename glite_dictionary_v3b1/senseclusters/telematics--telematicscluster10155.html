telematics

noun
technology integration field
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləˈmætɪks / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the technology that combines telecommunications and information processing to send, receive, and store data, often used in vehicles or equipment

Examples

Telematics technology
Many modern cars use telematics to provide navigation and emergency services.
The company invested in telematics to track its delivery vehicles in real time.
Universities now offer degrees in telematics due to the growing demand for connected technologies.
Many modern cars are equipped with telematics systems that track driving behavior and vehicle health.
Fleet managers use telematics to monitor the location and performance of their trucks in real time.
Advances in telematics have improved emergency response by providing automatic crash notifications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: informatics - the science of processing data for storage and retrieval
  • synonym: information technology - the use of computers and telecommunications to store, retrieve, and send information
  • synonym: telecommunications - the transmission of information over distances for communication
