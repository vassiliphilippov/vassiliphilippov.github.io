Synonyms

  • demolish
    3.7 4/m
    demolish means to destroy a building completely and is common in formal speech, while raze stresses leaving nothing behind.
  • level to the ground
    6.0 0.01/m
    level to the ground describes flattening a building completely so nothing remains above the soil, while raze means utter destruction.
  • tear down
    3.7 4/m
    tear down means to destroy or remove a building, often casually, while raze implies complete destruction leaving nothing behind.
  • pull down
    3.8 0.3/m
    pull down means to demolish a building until it is level with the ground, while raze stresses total removal of all remains.
  • knock down
    3.2 3/m
    knock down means to cause a building to fall suddenly, often by force, while raze means to destroy it completely.
  • level
    3.7 5/m
    level means to make something flat or even, not only buildings, while raze specifically means destroy until nothing remains.
  • take down
    3.4 1/m
    take down means to dismantle or remove parts of a structure carefully, while raze means to destroy the whole place completely.
