Free French

noun
French WWII resistance movement led by de Gaulle
5.1
Pronunciation: / fri ˈfrɛntʃ / (AmE), / friː ˈfrɛntʃ / (BrE)
the French forces and political movement led by Charles de Gaulle during World War II, formed to resist German occupation and restore the French Republic

Examples

Free French forces
After D-Day, the Free French took active part in the liberation of Paris.
General de Gaulle led the Free French from exile, rallying support for the liberation of France.
The Free French played a crucial role in the Allied victory in Western Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: French Resistance - various movements opposing German occupation of France
