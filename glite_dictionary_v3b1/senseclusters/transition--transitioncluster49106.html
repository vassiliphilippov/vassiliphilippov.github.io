transition

noun
a change or transformation event
Pronunciation: / trænˈzɪʃən /; / trænˈsɪʃən / (AmE), / trænˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or period of changing from one state, condition, place, subject, or activity to another

Examples

Life transition
Adolescence is often seen as a difficult transition between childhood and adulthood.
The transition to renewable energy will be essential for addressing climate change.
The country is undergoing a transition from a military regime to democracy.
After moving to a new city, she found the transition difficult at first but soon adapted.
The transition from childhood to adulthood can be challenging for many people.
The team managed the transition to remote work smoothly during the pandemic.
Her career transition from teaching to management was challenging but rewarding.
The company is undergoing a major transition as it adopts new technologies.
There is often a difficult transition period when moving from school to university.

Related Terms

  • synonym: change - the process of becoming different
  • antonym: continuity - the state of being continuous without significant change
  • antonym: stability - the state of being stable, without change
  • synonym: transformation - a thorough or dramatic change in form or appearance
  • antonym: transitionless - without a process of change from one state to another
