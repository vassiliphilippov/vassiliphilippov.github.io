water pill

noun
a medicine that helps the body lose excess water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ ˌpɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌpɪl / (BrE)
a medication or substance that increases the production of urine to help the body eliminate excess water, commonly known as a diuretic

Examples

A water pill medicine
He asked if a water pill would help with his bloating.
Many people take water pills to reduce swelling caused by fluid retention.
The doctor prescribed a water pill to help lower her blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diuretic - a substance that promotes the production of urine
