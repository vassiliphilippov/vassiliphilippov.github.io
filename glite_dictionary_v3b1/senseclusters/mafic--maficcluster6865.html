mafic

adjective
rich in magnesium and iron
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmæfɪk / (AmE), / ˈmæfɪk / (BrE)
describing igneous rocks or minerals that are rich in magnesium and iron

Examples

mafic rock
Basalt is a typical mafic rock formed from volcanic activity.
Geologists classify the lava as mafic due to its high iron and magnesium content.
The mafic minerals give the rock its dark color and dense structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: felsic - describing rocks rich in silica and light-colored minerals
