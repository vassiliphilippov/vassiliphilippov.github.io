streptobacillus

noun
a rod-shaped Gram-negative bacterium
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌstɹɛptə bəˈsɪləs / (AmE), / ˌstɹɛptə bəˈsɪləs / (BrE)
A rod-shaped Gram-negative bacterium, often forming chains, some species of which can cause diseases in humans and animals.

Examples

A streptobacillus bacterium
Researchers isolated a streptobacillus strain from the contaminated water sample.
The doctor explained that streptobacillus infection can lead to rat-bite fever.
Under the microscope, the streptobacillus appears as a chain of rod-shaped cells.
