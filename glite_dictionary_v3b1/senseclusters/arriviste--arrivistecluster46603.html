arriviste

noun
person newly risen in status, often unaccepted
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrəˈvist /; / æɹɪˈvist / (AmE), / ˌarɪˈviːst /; / aɹiˈviːst / (BrE)
a person who has recently gained wealth or social status but is not fully accepted by established groups, often seen as showing off or lacking refinement

Examples

An arriviste person
Her flashy style and ambition marked her as an arriviste in the eyes of the old-money families.
Many saw the new CEO as an arriviste who lacked the sophistication of his peers.
The exclusive club was wary of welcoming any arriviste members.
She dismissed him as an arriviste who cared more about showing off than fitting in.
The city's elite viewed the arriviste newcomers with barely concealed disdain.
The party was attended by several arrivistes anxious to impress their old-money neighbors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parvenu - a person who has recently or suddenly risen to a higher social or economic position but has not yet gained acceptance by others in that class
