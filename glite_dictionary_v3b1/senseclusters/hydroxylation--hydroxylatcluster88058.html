hydroxylation

noun
adding hydroxyl group
7.5
Pronunciation: / haɪˌdrɑksəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪˌdrɒksɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which a hydroxyl group (–OH) is added to a molecule

Examples

The hydroxylation process
The hydroxylation of certain drugs in the liver makes them easier to eliminate from the body.
Enzymes often catalyze the hydroxylation of organic compounds during metabolism.
Vitamin D becomes active in the body after a process of hydroxylation in the kidneys.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction involving the loss of electrons
  • antonym: reduction - a chemical reaction involving the gain of electrons
