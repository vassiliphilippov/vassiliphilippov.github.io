tape

noun
plastic strip for recording or storing sound, video, or data
1.8 72/m
Pronunciation: / teÉªp / (AmE), / teÉªp / (BrE)
a long, thin strip of plastic used for recording or storing sound, video, or data, or a recording made on such a strip

Examples

A music tape
He found an old tape of his favorite band from the 1980s.
She labeled each tape so she wouldn't mix up the recordings.
The museum archived historical interviews on magnetic tape for preservation.
She listened to the old tape of her grandmother singing in the choir.
The band spent months perfecting their demo tape before sending it to record labels.
The police reviewed the surveillance tape for evidence of the crime.

Synonyms

  • magnetic tape
    3.9 0.17/m
    magnetic tape is a plastic strip coated to hold recorded signals, while tape can mean the strip or the recording itself.
  • mag tape
    8.9 0.00/m
    mag tape is an informal short name used in speech or notes, while tape is the general medium or a recording.
  • tape recording
    2.9 0.92/m
    tape recording is the sound or video made on a tape, while tape also refers to the physical strip used for storage.
  • taping
    3.2 1.74/m
    taping refers to the act or event of making a recording, while tape usually names the medium or the recorded item.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recording - the process or result of capturing sounds or images
