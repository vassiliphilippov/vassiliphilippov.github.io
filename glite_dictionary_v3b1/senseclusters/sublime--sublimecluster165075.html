sublime

verb
change (solid) directly to vapor
Pronunciation: / səˈblaɪm / (AmE), / səˈblaɪm / (BrE)
to change directly from a solid to a gas (or vice versa) without becoming liquid, especially in a scientific context

Examples

To sublime iodine
In the lab, they sublimed the iodine by gently heating it.
Not all solids will sublime when exposed to heat; many will melt first.
Under reduced pressure, solid carbon dioxide can sublime without ever becoming liquid.
Chemists often sublime compounds to purify them without using solvents.
In the laboratory, iodine can easily sublime when heated and then redeposit as crystals.
When dry ice sublimes, carbon dioxide gas is released and can then be made to solidify again under pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sublimate - to change a solid directly into a vapor without passing through a liquid phase
  • synonym: sublimation - the process of a substance changing directly from solid to gas and vice versa
