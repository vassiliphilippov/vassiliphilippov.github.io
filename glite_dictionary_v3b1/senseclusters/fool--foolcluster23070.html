fool

verb
deceive or trick someone
Pronunciation: / fuːl /; / ful / (AmE), / fuːl / (BrE)
to trick or deceive someone; to make someone believe something that is not true

Examples

To fool a friend
Don't let them fool you with their empty promises.
He tried to fool his teacher by copying his friend's homework.
She realized too late that she had been fooled by a scam.
Don’t let yourself be fooled by his friendly smile—he’s not what he seems.
He tried to fool his classmates with a magic trick, but they saw right through it.
She fooled everyone into believing she was wealthy.

  • synonym: dupe - to trick someone into believing something untrue
  • antonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding
  • synonym: mislead - to give someone the wrong idea or impression
  • synonym: outwit - to defeat or trick by being more clever
  • synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone as a prank or scheme
