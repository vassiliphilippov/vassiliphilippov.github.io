actualism

noun
philosophical doctrine
Pronunciation: / ˈæktʃuəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæktʃuəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical belief that only actual, existing things are real, and possible or potential things do not exist

Examples

Metaphysical actualism
According to actualism, only things that truly exist in the present are real.
Some philosophers debate whether actualism can explain the nature of possibility.
Her research paper explored the differences between actualism and other metaphysical theories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: possibilism - the belief that possible things or states can exist
  • synonym: realism - the belief that reality exists independently of perception
