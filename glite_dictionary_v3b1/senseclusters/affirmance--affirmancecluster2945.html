affirmance

noun
formal legal declaration
Pronunciation: / əˈfɜrməns / (AmE), / əˈfɜːməns / (BrE)
a formal declaration that something is true, especially made instead of an oath in legal settings

Examples

Legal affirmance
The witness gave an affirmance before testifying, as she did not wish to swear an oath.
In some courts, an affirmance is accepted instead of a traditional oath for those with religious objections.
He made an affirmance that his statement was true to the best of his knowledge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affirmation - a formal declaration that something is true
  • antonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
