dimethyl ketone

noun
a solvent; another name for acetone
7.6 4.5
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈmeθ.əl ˈkiː.toʊn/ /; / /dɪ.ˈmeθ.əl ˈkiː.toʊn/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈmeθ.əl ˈkiː.təʊn/ /; / /dɪ.ˈmeθ.əl ˈkiː.təʊn/ (BrE)
a colourless, highly flammable liquid ketone (C3H6O) used as a solvent and in the manufacture of plastics; also known as acetone

Examples

Dimethyl ketone solvent use
Dimethyl ketone is often used in laboratories as an effective solvent for cleaning glassware.
As a key ingredient in nail polish remover, dimethyl ketone evaporates quickly and leaves no residue.
Industrial manufacturers rely on dimethyl ketone for producing plastics and synthetic fibres.
