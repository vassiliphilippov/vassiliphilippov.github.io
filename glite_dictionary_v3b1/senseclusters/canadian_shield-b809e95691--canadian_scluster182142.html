Canadian Shield

noun
vast rocky plateau in eastern and central Canada
6.5
Pronunciation: / kəˈneɪdiən ʃild / (AmE), / kəˈneɪdiən ʃiːld / (BrE)
a large area of exposed Precambrian rocks forming a plateau that covers much of eastern and central Canada, stretching from the Great Lakes to the Arctic Ocean

Examples

The Canadian Shield plateau
Glacial lakes and thin soils are common features of the Canadian Shield.
Much of northern Ontario lies within the Canadian Shield region.
The Canadian Shield is rich in minerals such as nickel, gold, and copper.
