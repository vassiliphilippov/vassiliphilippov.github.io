nasopharyngeal leishmaniasis

noun
tropical disease affecting nose and throat membranes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌneɪsoʊfəˈrɪndʒiəl ˌliːʃməniˈaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌneɪsəʊfəˈrɪndʒɪəl ˌliːʃmənaɪˈəsɪs / (BrE)
a form of leishmaniasis in which sores are limited to the skin and mucous membranes of the nose and throat, typically found in Mexico and Central and South America

Examples

nasopharyngeal leishmaniasis skin sores
Communities in affected regions are at increased risk of nasopharyngeal leishmaniasis due to the prevalence of sandflies.
Early treatment of nasopharyngeal leishmaniasis can prevent severe complications and disfigurement.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with nasopharyngeal leishmaniasis after observing characteristic lesions in the nasal passages.
