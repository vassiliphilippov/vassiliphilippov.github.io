delineator

noun
roadside marker
7.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈlɪn.iː.eɪ.tər/ /; / /də.ˈlɪn.iː.eɪ.tər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈlɪn.iː.eɪ.tə/ /; / /də.ˈlɪn.iː.eɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a post, marker, or device placed beside a road to indicate the edge, alignment, or a hazard, often to guide drivers.

Examples

Roadside delineator post
The highway was lined with delineators to help drivers see the road at night.
After the accident, workers installed new delineator posts along the dangerous curve.
Reflective delineators are especially useful in foggy or low-visibility conditions.
