hyperbaric chamber

noun
pressure-increased medical treatment chamber
8.3 4.5
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.ˈbær.ɪk ˈtʃeɪm.bər/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈbær.ɪk ˈtʃeɪm.bə/ (BrE)
a sealed medical chamber in which air pressure and sometimes oxygen concentration are increased above normal atmospheric levels, often used to treat various medical conditions

Examples

A hyperbaric chamber treatment
Some athletes use a hyperbaric chamber to speed up recovery from injuries.
The patient was placed in a hyperbaric chamber to receive oxygen therapy after carbon monoxide poisoning.
Treatment in a hyperbaric chamber can help heal wounds that won't heal with standard methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decompression chamber - a chamber used to allow gradual decompression, especially for divers
