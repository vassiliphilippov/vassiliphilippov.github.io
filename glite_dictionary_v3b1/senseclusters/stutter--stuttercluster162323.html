stutter

noun
brief playback glitch
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈstʌt.ər/ (AmE), /ˈstʌt.ə/ (BrE)
A brief interruption, hesitation, or repeated small segment in audio or video playback that causes the sound or image to appear jerky or to repeat briefly.

Examples

Video stutter
The livestream showed a sudden stutter during the keynote, frustrating online viewers.
Gamers complained the cutscene had a brief stutter that ruined the cinematic moment.
After the update, recorded lectures sometimes have a repeating stutter for a second or two.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glitch - a short fault or error in a system that causes unexpected behaviour
