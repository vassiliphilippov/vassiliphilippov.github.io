forswear

verb
formally renounce a belief or practice
9.9 7.6
Pronunciation: /fər.ˈsweər/ /; / /fɔːr.ˈsweər/ (AmE), /fɔː.ˈsweə/ /; / /fə.ˈsweə/ (BrE)
to formally and seriously promise to give up, reject, or stop doing something (such as a belief, habit, or practice)

Examples

To forswear old habits
After joining the new faith, she decided to forswear her former religious beliefs.
He solemnly forswore any involvement with the controversial group.
Many politicians are urged to forswear dishonest practices when they take office.
After much thought, she decided to forswear social media for a month.
Many people forswear certain foods during religious observances.
He forswore his former lifestyle and started anew in a different city.
After years of unhealthy eating, she forswore junk food and started cooking at home.
He forswore all forms of gambling after losing a large sum of money.
Many people have forsworn plastic bags to help protect the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adopt - to take up or start to use something
  • antonym: embrace - to accept or take up willingly
  • synonym: recant - to withdraw a statement or belief formally
  • synonym: renounce - to formally give up a claim, right, or habit
