dhimmitude

noun
protected non-Muslim status
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪ.mɪ.tuːd/ /; / /ˈdɪ.mə.tuːd/ (AmE), /ˈdɪ.mɪ.tuːd/ /; / /ˈdɪ.mɪ.tjuːd/ (BrE)
the status or condition of non-Muslims living as protected but subordinate people under Islamic rule, often with special taxes and restrictions

Examples

Medieval dhimmitude
The historian explained how dhimmitude shaped the lives of non-Muslims in medieval Islamic societies.
Under dhimmitude, certain communities paid a tax in exchange for protection and limited autonomy.
Debates about dhimmitude often arise in discussions of religious tolerance and minority rights.
