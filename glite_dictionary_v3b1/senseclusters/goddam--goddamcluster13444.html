goddam

adjective, adverb
emphatic intensifier, often vulgar
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːdæm /; / ˈɡɑdˌdæm / (AmE), / ˈɡɒdæm /; / ˈɡɒd.dæm / (BrE)
used informally to strongly emphasize anger, annoyance, or surprise (as an expletive or intensifier)

Examples

A goddam mess
He was so late, it made me really goddam angry.
I don't give a goddam what you think about my decision.
That was the best goddam burger I've ever had.
He's just another goddam fool trying to pick a fight.
I lost my goddam keys again this morning.
Why is there a goddam delay every time I need a cab?

Related Terms

  • synonym: damn - used for emphasis or to express anger
  • synonym: goddamn - used for emphasis, expressing strong emotion
  • synonym: goddamned - containing or characterized by being damned by God; used informally as an expletive
