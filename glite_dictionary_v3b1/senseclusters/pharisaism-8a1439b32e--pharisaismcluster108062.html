Pharisaism

noun
Pharisees' beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈfærəˌsaɪˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfærəsɪˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the religious beliefs, teachings, and practices of the Pharisees, a Jewish sect during the Second Temple period

Examples

Ancient Pharisaism
Historians study Pharisaism to understand the development of Jewish religious traditions.
The influence of Pharisaism can be seen in later interpretations of Jewish law.
Some scholars argue that Pharisaism emphasized both written and oral traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pharisee - a member of an ancient Jewish sect known for strict observance of the law
  • antonym: Sadducee - a member of a rival Jewish sect during the Second Temple period
