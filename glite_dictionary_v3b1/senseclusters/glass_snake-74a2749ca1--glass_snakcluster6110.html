A legless lizard of Europe, Asia, and North America, with vestigial hind limbs and the ability to shed and regenerate its long, fragile tail.

Examples

A glass snake tail
Many people mistake a glass snake for a regular snake, but scientists identify it as a lizard.
The glass snake can shed its tail to escape predators, a feature unique among many reptiles.
Unlike true snakes, the glass snake has movable eyelids and ear openings.
