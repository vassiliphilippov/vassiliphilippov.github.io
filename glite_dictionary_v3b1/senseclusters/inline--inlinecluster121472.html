inline

verb
to embed directly
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to insert or embed something, such as an image or code, directly within a document or file instead of linking to it externally

Examples

To inline a picture
Please inline the company logo in the document so it displays for everyone.
The developer decided to inline the CSS styles to improve email compatibility.
If you inline small images, the webpage may load faster for users.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embed - to place something firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
  • antonym: link - to connect or refer to something externally
