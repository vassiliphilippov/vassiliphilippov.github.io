inrushing

noun
sudden inward flow
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a sudden inward flow or movement of something, such as water, air, or people

Examples

An inrushing of air
The inrushing of cold air made everyone in the room shiver.
Engineers designed barriers to control the inrushing of water during storms.
The sudden inrushing of fans filled the stadium within minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influx - a large arrival or inward flow of people or things
  • antonym: outflow - a movement outward, away from a place
