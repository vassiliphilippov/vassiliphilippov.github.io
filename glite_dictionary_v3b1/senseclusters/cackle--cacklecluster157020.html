cackle

verb
laugh loudly and unpleasantly
3.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæk.əl/ (AmE), /ˈkæk.əl/ (BrE)
to make a loud, shrill, repeated sound like a hen or bird, or to laugh or talk in a loud, harsh, high-pitched way

Examples

To cackle loudly
He couldn't help but cackle at his friend's terrible joke.
The old woman cackled with delight as she recounted the prank.
The witch began to cackle as she stirred her bubbling potion.
After laying an egg, the old hen proudly cackled for several minutes.
Every morning, I hear the chickens cackling in the backyard as they lay eggs.
The hens began to cackle loudly as soon as the farmer entered the coop.
From the lake, a flock of birds could be heard cackling at dawn.
Suddenly, several geese began to cackle, startling the nearby ducks.
The geese cackled loudly as they flew overhead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chatter - talk rapidly in a trivial way
  • degree lower: chuckle - to laugh quietly or to oneself
  • synonym: cluck - make a short, guttural sound (of hens)
  • synonym: giggle - laugh lightly in a silly or nervous way
  • synonym: guffaw - to laugh loudly and boisterously
  • synonym: honk - to make the characteristic noise of a goose
