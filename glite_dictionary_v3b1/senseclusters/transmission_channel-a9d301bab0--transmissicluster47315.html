transmission channel

noun
pathway for transmitting signals or data
1.1
Pronunciation: / trænzˈmɪʃ.ən ˈtʃæn.əl / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃ.ən ˈtʃæn.əl / (BrE)
a physical or logical pathway over which electrical signals or data are transmitted from one point to another

Examples

A transmission channel signal
Each television station is assigned a separate transmission channel to prevent interference.
Fiber optic cables serve as an efficient transmission channel for internet data across long distances.
The phone call was disrupted due to noise on the transmission channel.
