Eulerian

adjective
fixed-point fluid analysis
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɔɪ.ˈlɪə.riː.ən/ /; / /ɔɪ.ˈlɪə.rɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ɔɪ.ˈlɪə.riː.ən/ /; / /ɔɪ.ˈlɪə.rɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to a method of studying fluid flow by observing properties at fixed locations in space over time

Examples

Eulerian approach
The Eulerian perspective is commonly used in meteorology to study atmospheric changes at specific locations.
Researchers used an Eulerian grid to monitor pollution levels in the city over several months.
In fluid dynamics, the Eulerian approach contrasts with the Lagrangian method, which tracks individual particles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Lagrangian - relating to following individual particles in fluid flow
