Isthmus of Corinth

proper noun
narrow land bridge in Greece with a canal
Pronunciation: / ˈɪsθməs əv kəˈrɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈɪsθməs əv kəˈrɪnθ / (BrE)
a narrow strip of land connecting the Peloponnese peninsula with mainland Greece, separating the Gulf of Corinth from the Saronic Gulf, and crossed by the Corinth Canal

Examples

The Isthmus of Corinth canal
Ships can travel more quickly between the Aegean and Ionian seas by crossing the Isthmus of Corinth via the canal.
The Isthmus of Corinth has been a strategic passageway since ancient times.
Tourists often visit the Isthmus of Corinth to see both natural scenery and the famous canal.
