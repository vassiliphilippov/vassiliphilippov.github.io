repay

verb
give back or compensate for something received
2.7 2.14/m
Pronunciation: / riËˆpeÉª / (AmE), / riËˆpeÉª / (BrE)
to give something in return for help, kindness, or a service; to compensate or requite for an action or behavior.

Examples

To repay kindness
He never forgot a favor and always repaid generosity with generosity.
It may take years to repay the debt of gratitude I owe you.
She wanted to repay his kindness by helping him find a job.

Synonyms

  • compensate
    4.2 7.55/m
    compensate is to pay or give something to make up for loss or work, while repay means return help or a favor.
  • recompense
    7.4 0.08/m
    recompense is to pay or make amends for loss or effort, while repay focuses on returning help or a service.
  • requite
    9.9 0.07/m
    requite is to give something back for love or injury in formal language, while repay is more general return for help.
  • reciprocate
    6.2 1.93/m
    reciprocate is to respond with a similar action or feeling, while repay is often used for money or favors.
  • return the favor
    2.9 1.45/m
    return the favor is to do something nice back for someone, while repay can also mean paying money or a debt.
  • give back
    3.0 1.38/m
    give back is to return goods or contribute to a community as thanks, while repay often means returning help or debt.
  • reward
    1.3 21/m
    reward is to give money, praise, or a prize for good action, while repay means give back for a favor or service.
  • pay back
    3.2 0.33/m
    pay back is to do something in return often to get even or revenge, while repay usually means give back money, thanks, or help.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retaliate - to return like for like, often in a negative sense
  • synonym: reward - to give something in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
