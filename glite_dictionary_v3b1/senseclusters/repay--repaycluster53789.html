repay

verb
give back or compensate for something received
Pronunciation: / riˈpeɪ / (AmE), / riˈpeɪ / (BrE)
to give something in return for help, kindness, or a service; to compensate or requite for an action or behavior.

Examples

To repay kindness
He never forgot a favor and always repaid generosity with generosity.
It may take years to repay the debt of gratitude I owe you.
She wanted to repay his kindness by helping him find a job.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retaliate - to return like for like, often in a negative sense
  • synonym: reward - to give something in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
