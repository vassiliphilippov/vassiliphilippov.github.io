correlatively

adverb
in a reciprocal way
8.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛlətɪvli / (AmE), / kɒˈrɛlətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a mutual, reciprocal, or corresponding relationship between two or more things

Economic growth and technological advancement often develop correlatively over time.
The rights and duties of citizens exist correlatively within a society.
The two laws were enacted correlatively to ensure consistency in the legal system.
As the price of oil increased, transportation costs rose correlatively throughout the year.
The teacher adjusted her methods correlatively to the needs of her students.
