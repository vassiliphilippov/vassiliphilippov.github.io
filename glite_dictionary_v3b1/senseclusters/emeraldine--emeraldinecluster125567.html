emeraldine

noun
form of polyaniline
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmərəldiːn / (AmE), / ˈɛmərəldiːn / (BrE)
a chemical form of polyaniline in an intermediate oxidation state, notable for its electrical conductivity

Examples

Emeraldine chemical form
The unique properties of emeraldine make it valuable in the production of electronic sensors.
Researchers studied the conductivity of emeraldine to improve battery technology.
Polyaniline in its emeraldine form is often used in antistatic coatings.
