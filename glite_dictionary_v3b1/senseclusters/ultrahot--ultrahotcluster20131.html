ultrahot

adjective
extremely hot
Pronunciation: / ˈʌltrəˌhɑt / (AmE), / ˈʌltrəˌhɒt / (BrE)
extremely hot; having a temperature far above what is considered very hot, often used in scientific contexts

Examples

Ultrahot plasma
The scientists created an ultrahot plasma to study the behavior of stars.
Special equipment is needed to measure ultrahot temperatures in the laboratory.
The ultrahot gas emitted intense light during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: boiling - at or near the temperature at which a liquid boils; very hot
  • antonym: freezing - very cold; at or below the temperature at which water freezes
  • synonym: scorching - very hot; burning
