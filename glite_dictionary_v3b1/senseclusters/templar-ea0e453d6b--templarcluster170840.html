Templar

noun
member of the Knights Templar
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛm.plɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.plə / (BrE)
a member of a religious military order (the Knights Templar) founded in 1118 to protect Christian pilgrims and holy sites in Jerusalem

Examples

A Templar knight
During the Crusades, a Templar was sworn to protect pilgrims traveling to Jerusalem.
He dressed as a Templar for the historical reenactment, complete with a white mantle and red cross.
The legend of the lost Templar treasure has fascinated historians for generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hospitaller - member of another medieval religious and military order, the Knights Hospitaller
  • antonym: Saracen - historical term for a Muslim opponent of the Crusaders
  • synonym: Teutonic Knight - member of the Teutonic Order, a medieval Christian military order
