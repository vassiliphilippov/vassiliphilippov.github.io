phonography

noun
sound-based writing system
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / fəˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a system of writing in which symbols represent the sounds of speech

Examples

Phonography system
The development of phonography allowed languages to be written more accurately.
Many modern alphabets are based on the principles of phonography.
Scholars studied ancient scripts to determine if they used phonography or another system.

Related Terms
