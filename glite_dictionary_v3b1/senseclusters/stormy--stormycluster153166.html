stormy

adjective
characterized by storms or turmoil
1.0 2.13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààst…îrmi / (AmE), / Ààst…îÀêmi / (BrE)
(especially of weather) characterized by storms, strong winds, and often heavy rain; tumultuous or marked by disturbance.

Examples

Stormy weather
The ship struggled through the stormy waters all night.
Their relationship had many stormy moments, but they always made up afterwards.
We decided to postpone the picnic because the weather was stormy.

Synonyms

  • tempestuous weather
    7.0 0.01/m
    tempestuous weather is very violent and turbulent, while stormy describes weather with strong winds and heavy rain.
  • tempestuous
    8.4 0.48/m
    tempestuous is often used for strong feelings or troubled situations, while stormy mainly describes rough, windy weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - (of weather or situations) not windy, stormy, or disturbed
  • synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion; not controlled or calm
