temper

noun
hardness and elasticity of a metal
4.2 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
the degree of hardness and elasticity in a metal or metal object, determining its ability to absorb energy without cracking

Examples

The temper of metal
If the temper is too soft, the metal may bend rather than retain its shape.
The knife blade's temper allows it to withstand repeated use without chipping or breaking.
To improve the temper of the steel, it was heated and then cooled at a controlled rate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brittleness - the quality of breaking easily rather than bending; lack of elasticity
  • synonym: hardness - the quality or state of being hard; resistance to deformation
