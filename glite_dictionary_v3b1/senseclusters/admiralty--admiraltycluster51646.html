admiralty

noun
maritime law
Pronunciation: / ˈædmərəlti / (AmE), / ˈædmɪrəlti / (BrE)
the branch of law that deals with ships, shipping, and maritime matters

Examples

Admiralty law
The admiralty court has jurisdiction over shipping disputes.
Lawyers specializing in admiralty handle cases involving international waters.
He studied admiralty to better understand the rules of maritime trade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: civil law - the system of law concerned with private relations between members of a community
  • synonym: maritime law - the body of law governing shipping and navigation
