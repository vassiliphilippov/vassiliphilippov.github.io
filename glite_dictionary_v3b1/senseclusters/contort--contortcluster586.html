contort

verb
twist out of shape
7.5 1.95/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàt…îrt / (AmE), / k…ônÀàt…îÀêt / (BrE)
to twist or bend something out of its normal shape, often in a violent or unnatural way

Examples

To contort the body
He could contort his body into shapes that seemed impossible for most people.
Her face began to contort with pain as the cramp intensified.
The metal bars seemed to contort under the intense heat of the fire.

Synonyms

  • deform
    4.8 3.79/m
    deform means to change something's normal shape, often making it bent or twisted, while contort implies a violent, unnatural twist.
  • distort
    4.3 6.67/m
    distort means to change shape or truth so things look wrong, while contort suggests a sharp physical twisting out of shape.
  • warp
    4.9 1.65/m
    warp means to bend or twist from heat, pressure, or damp over time, while contort suggests a sudden violent twist.
  • misshape
    6.9 0.07/m
    misshape means to give something an incorrect or deformed form, while contort describes a strong, often violent twisting bend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distort - to pull or twist out of shape; to alter the normal appearance
  • synonym: twist - to turn something so that it has a different shape or position
