myosin

noun
muscle protein involved in contraction
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪəsɪn / (BrE)
a fibrous protein found in muscle tissue that interacts with actin to enable muscle contraction

Examples

Myosin protein fibers
Myosin and actin filaments slide past each other during muscle contraction.
Myosin plays a crucial role in cellular movements beyond just muscle function.
Scientists have developed drugs that influence the activity of myosin to treat heart failure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actin - another protein in muscle that works with myosin to enable contraction
