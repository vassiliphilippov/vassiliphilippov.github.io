consecutively

adverb
in succession, without interruption
3.1
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛk.jə.tɪv.li /; / kənˈsɛkjətɪvli / (AmE), / kənˈsɛk.jʊ.tɪv.li /; / kənˈsɛkjʊtɪvli / (BrE)
one after another without interruption; in succession

Examples

Work consecutively hours
She worked consecutively for twelve hours without taking a break.
The numbers were arranged consecutively from one to twenty.
The team won six games consecutively, setting a new record.
The machines were tested consecutively, each for exactly five minutes.
The winners were announced consecutively, starting with third place.
You should number the invoices consecutively to avoid confusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: randomly - without method or conscious decision; without a specific sequence
  • synonym: sequentially - in a sequence, one right after the other
  • synonym: successively - one after another in order
