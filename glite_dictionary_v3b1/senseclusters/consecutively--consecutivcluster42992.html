Synonyms

  • successively
    4.4 0.2/m
    successively shows actions follow one after another in order, while consecutively stresses no interruption between them.
  • sequentially
    4.5 0.4/m
    sequentially puts events in a clear order or step-by-step process, while consecutively means they happen without any break.
  • serially
    5.8 0.2/m
    serially often describes things in a set series, especially in technical contexts, while consecutively highlights continuous succession.
  • one after another
    2.3 0.8/m
    one after another describes items or events that follow without pause in a row, while consecutively focuses on uninterrupted order.
  • one after the other
    2.0 0.8/m
    one after the other shows things happening immediately one following the next, while consecutively stresses a continuous, unbroken sequence.
  • one by one
    1.4 9/m
    one by one means doing each item separately in turn, often slowly, while consecutively means they happen in an unbroken flow.
  • one at a time
    1.9 5/m
    one at a time stresses handling items singly to avoid doing them together, while consecutively focuses on events occurring without interruption.
