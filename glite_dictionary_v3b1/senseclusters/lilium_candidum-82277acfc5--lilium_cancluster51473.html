Lilium candidum

noun
Mediterranean white lily species
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪliəm ˈkændɪdəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm ˈkændɪdəm / (BrE)
A species of lily native to the eastern Mediterranean and the Balkans, known for its broad, funnel-shaped white flowers.

Examples

Lilium candidum flower
Gardeners prize Lilium candidum for its beauty and its fragrance in early summer.
Historically, Lilium candidum has been used in medicinal remedies throughout the Mediterranean.
The Lilium candidum is often referred to as the Madonna lily for its striking white blooms.
