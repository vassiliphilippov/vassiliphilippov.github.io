fraudulent

adjective
intended to deceive or cheat

Pronunciation: / Ààfr…îd í…ôl…ônt / (AmE), / Ààfr…îÀêdj äl…ônt / (BrE)
intended to deceive or cheat; involving fraud or deception.

Examples

Fraudulent online scheme
Customers lost thousands of dollars to a fraudulent online scheme.
He was arrested for his role in a fraudulent insurance claim.
The company was found guilty of fraudulent accounting practices that misled investors.

Synonyms

  • deceitful
    5.0 1.46/m
    deceitful often describes a person's dishonest behavior that aims to mislead others, while fraudulent refers to deliberate illegal deception.
  • deceptive
    5.3 4.30/m
    deceptive gives a false impression or hides the truth, while fraudulent means deliberate cheating to gain money or advantage.
  • bogus
    5.6 5.92/m
    bogus describes something fake and intended to trick people, while fraudulent stresses illegal deception and cheating for gain.
  • sham
    6.8 0.01/m
    sham looks like the real thing but is pretended to fool people, while fraudulent focuses on planned deceit for gain.
  • phony
    5.3 8.14/m
    phony is an informal word for something not genuine and meant to fool, while fraudulent is often a legal word for cheating.
  • dishonest
    2.5 6.87/m
    dishonest describes a person who lies or cheats in general, while fraudulent refers to specific planned deception or illegal schemes.
  • spurious
    7.9 1.85/m
    spurious means false or not authentic, often used for ideas or claims, while fraudulent implies intentional, often illegal, deception.
