gas

noun
substance in the gaseous state of matter
Pronunciation: / ɡæs / (AmE), / ɡæs / (BrE)
a substance in a state of matter that has no fixed shape and fills any container (such as air, oxygen, or helium)

Examples

Oxygen is a gas
Carbon dioxide is the gas we exhale when we breathe.
Oxygen is a gas at room temperature, while water is a liquid.
The balloon inflated as more gas was pumped inside.
Helium, a gas, is often used to fill balloons so they float.
Oxygen is an example of a gas that we need to breathe.
Water vapor is simply water in the gas phase.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liquid - state of matter with fixed volume but no fixed shape
  • antonym: solid - state of matter with fixed shape and volume
