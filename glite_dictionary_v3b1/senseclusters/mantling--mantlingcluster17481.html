mantling

noun
bird food-covering behavior
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmænt.lɪŋ/ /; / /ˈmænt.ə.lɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈmænt.lɪŋ/ /; / /ˈmænt.ə.lɪŋ/ (BrE)
the behavior of a bird of prey spreading its wings and body over captured food to hide or protect it

Examples

Hawk mantling prey
The hawk displayed mantling after catching its prey to prevent other animals from stealing it.
During feeding, the eagle's mantling made it difficult for other birds to approach.
Young falcons learn mantling by watching adult birds protect their food.
