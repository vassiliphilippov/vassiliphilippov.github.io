allotype

noun
opposite-sex type specimen
Pronunciation: / ˈæləˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈaləʊˌtaɪp / (BrE)
a specimen of the opposite sex to the holotype, designated when describing a new species in taxonomy

Examples

Male allotype
The researcher selected a male allotype to accompany the female holotype in the species record.
In the museum collection, the allotype is stored alongside the holotype for reference.
Taxonomists often designate an allotype to provide a complete representation of both sexes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holotype - the single specimen designated as the type of a species
  • synonym: paratype - a specimen other than the holotype used in the original description of a species
