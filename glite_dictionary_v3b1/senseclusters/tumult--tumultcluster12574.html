tumult

noun
noisy disturbance or confusion
7.7 1.56/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀê.m ålt / (AmE), / ÀàtjuÀê.m ålt / (BrE)
a loud, confused, and disorderly situation, especially involving a crowd

Examples

A tumult of voices
After the announcement, tumult erupted in the meeting room as everyone started shouting their opinions.
The city streets were in a state of tumult following the unexpected blackout.
The crowd's tumult outside the stadium could be heard from miles away.
Children rushed into the playground in a tumult of shouting and laughter.
News of the unexpected resignation caused a tumult in the stock market.
The protesters created a tumult outside the government building, making it difficult to hear anything.
After the announcement, there was a tumult of shouting and applause.
She struggled to make herself heard above the tumult in the crowded hall.
The stadium erupted in tumult as the winning goal was scored.
