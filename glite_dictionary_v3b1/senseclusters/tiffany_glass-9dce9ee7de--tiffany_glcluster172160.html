Tiffany glass

noun
decorative opalescent colored glass
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪfəni ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈtɪfəni ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A type of opalescent colored glass, developed by Louis Comfort Tiffany in the early 1900s, used especially in stained-glass windows and lamps.

Examples

Tiffany glass lamps
Antique collectors highly value Tiffany glass for its vibrant colors and craftsmanship.
He replaced the broken window with a panel of Tiffany glass to match the home's original decor.
The museum exhibited several lamps made from Tiffany glass, showcasing their intricate designs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stained glass - glass colored or decorated for use in windows or decorative pieces
