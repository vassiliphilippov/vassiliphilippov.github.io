Sir Thomas More

proper noun
English statesman and author of 'Utopia'
7.2
Pronunciation: /sɜːr ˈtɑː.məs mɔːr (AmE), /sɜː ˈtɒ.məs mɔː/ (BrE)
(1478–1535) English statesman, lawyer, and writer who opposed Henry VIII's divorce, was executed for his beliefs, and is known for writing 'Utopia'.

Examples

Sir Thomas More wrote Utopia
Sir Thomas More wrote 'Utopia', a book that introduced the concept of an ideal society.
Many scholars regard Sir Thomas More as a martyr for his steadfast opposition to Henry VIII's religious reforms.
The legacy of Sir Thomas More continues to influence discussions on law, ethics, and political philosophy.
