intersection

noun
shared point or region of geometric figures
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.t̬ɚˈsɛk.ʃən /; / ˌɪntərˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈsɛk.ʃən /; / ˌɪntəˈsɛkʃən / (BrE)
the point, set, or part that is shared by two or more things, especially in mathematics (geometry or set theory)

Examples

Set intersection
If two lines are parallel, their intersection does not exist.
In geometry, we often calculate the intersection of different planes.
The intersection of those two circles forms a lens-shaped area.
In set theory, the intersection of two sets contains only the elements they share.
The intersection of A and B is denoted as A ∩ B.
Their intersection is an empty set because the two groups have no members in common.
