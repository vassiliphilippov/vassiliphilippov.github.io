Federal National Mortgage Association

proper noun
US government-sponsored mortgage corporation
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛdərəl ˈnæʃənl ˈmɔrgɪdʒ əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl ˈnæʃnəl ˈmɔːgɪdʒ əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a U.S. government-sponsored corporation that purchases and guarantees mortgages to support the mortgage market

Examples

The Federal National Mortgage Association guarantees loans
Congress created the Federal National Mortgage Association in 1938 to expand the flow of mortgage money.
The Federal National Mortgage Association is often referred to as Fannie Mae.
The Federal National Mortgage Association plays a critical role in the US housing finance system.
