crank

verb
rotate using a crank
3.5 3.7
Pronunciation: /kræŋk/ (AmE), /kræŋk/ (BrE)
to turn or operate something by using a crank, especially to start an engine or machine

Examples

To crank an engine
After plugging it in, he cranked the generator to start the power.
He had to crank the old engine by hand every morning.
She cranked the handle to raise the heavy blinds.
He cranked the engine with a strong, quick motion.
I had to crank the old tractor because the battery was dead.
In the early days of driving, you would often crank your car to get it started.
He loves to crank his motorcycle down the highway on weekends.
She could crank her bike so loud the neighbors would complain every time.
They met up and cranked their hogs through the city until midnight.
