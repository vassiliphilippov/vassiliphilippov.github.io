damningly

adverb
showing guilt or blame
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈdæmɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that shows or emphasizes strong criticism, blame, or evidence of guilt

Examples

Report damningly exposed
The report damningly exposed the company's role in the scandal.
She spoke damningly about the lack of safety measures at the factory.
The evidence was presented damningly during the trial.
The report damningly revealed the company's failure to follow safety regulations.
She spoke damningly about the lack of progress in the project.
The evidence was damningly clear, leaving no room for doubt.
The report spoke damningly of the company's safety record after the accident.
She looked damningly at the evidence, leaving no doubt about her opinion.
The documentary damningly exposed the corruption within the organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accusingly - in a way that suggests blame or accusation
  • synonym: critically - in a way that expresses disapproval or judgment
  • antonym: favorably - in a way that shows approval or support
  • synonym: incriminatingly - in a way that suggests someone is guilty
