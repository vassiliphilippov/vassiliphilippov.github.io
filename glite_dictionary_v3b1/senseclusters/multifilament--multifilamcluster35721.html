multifilament

noun
thread of many filaments
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈfɪləmənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈfɪləmənt / (BrE)
a thread or yarn made by twisting together many small filaments, often used for added strength or flexibility

Examples

A multifilament line
The fishing line was made from a durable multifilament to handle larger catches.
Many modern tennis rackets use multifilament strings for improved comfort and playability.
The textile factory specializes in producing high-quality multifilament threads for industrial use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: yarn - a continuous strand of twisted threads used in knitting or weaving
