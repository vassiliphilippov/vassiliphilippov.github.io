U. S. Code

noun
the official codified laws of the United States
7.0
Pronunciation: / juː ɛs koʊd / (AmE), / juː ɛs kəʊd / (BrE)
The official compilation of the general and permanent federal laws of the United States, arranged by subject matter and published by the U.S. House of Representatives.

Examples

The U. S. Code laws
Every new federal law passed by Congress is eventually incorporated into the U. S. Code.
Lawyers frequently reference the U. S. Code to interpret statutory requirements in legal cases.
Title 18 of the U. S. Code covers federal crimes and criminal procedure.
