nonreduced

adjective
not chemically reduced
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.rɪˈduːst / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈdjuːst / (BrE)
not having undergone a reduction reaction, especially in chemistry, and remaining in a higher oxidation state

Examples

Nonreduced sample
The sample remained nonreduced even after several hours of heating in the laboratory.
Scientists measured the nonreduced form of the compound to compare its properties with the reduced form.
It is important to keep the solution nonreduced during this stage of the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxidized - having undergone a reaction involving the loss of electrons
  • antonym: reduced - having gained electrons or hydrogen in a chemical reaction
