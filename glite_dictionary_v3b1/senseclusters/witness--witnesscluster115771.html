witness

noun
person who observes and reports an event
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtnəs /; / ˈwɪtnɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs /; / ˈwɪtnɪs / (BrE)
a person who sees or observes an event and can report what happened

Examples

An accident witness
As a witness to the robbery, she gave a detailed statement to the authorities.
The court called the witness to testify about what he observed.
The police interviewed every witness who saw the accident.
As a curious witness to the parade, she took many photographs.
During the eclipse, hundreds of witnesses gathered to observe the rare event.
The police interviewed every witness who saw the incident in the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bystander - a person who is present at an event but does not take part
  • synonym: observer - a person who watches or notices something
  • synonym: onlooker - someone who watches something happening without taking part
  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in an activity
  • synonym: spectator - a person who watches at an event, show, game, or other activity
  • antonym: suspect - a person thought to be guilty of a crime
