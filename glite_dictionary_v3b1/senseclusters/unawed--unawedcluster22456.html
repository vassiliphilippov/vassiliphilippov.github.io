unawed

adjective
not feeling or showing awe
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɔd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːd / (BrE)
not feeling or showing awe; not impressed or intimidated by something remarkable or powerful.

Examples

Completely unawed students
Despite the grand setting, the students seemed entirely unawed.
He listened to the expert's lecture with an unawed expression, showing no sign of intimidation.
She remained unawed by the fame of the celebrities around her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: awed - feeling awe; impressed or filled with wonder
