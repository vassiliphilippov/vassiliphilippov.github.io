congealment

noun
the act of becoming solid or thick
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒilmənt / (AmE), / kənˈdʒiːlmənt / (BrE)
The process of becoming thick or solid, often as a result of cooling or freezing.

Examples

The congealment of fat
Overnight, the fat underwent congealment and formed a solid layer on top of the stew.
The congealment of the sauce made it difficult to pour over the food.
The scientists observed the congealment of lava as it cooled on the mountain's slope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liquefaction - the process of becoming liquid
  • synonym: solidification - the process of becoming solid
