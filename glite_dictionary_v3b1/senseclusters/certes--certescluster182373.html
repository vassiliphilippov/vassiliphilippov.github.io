certes

adverb
certainly; truly
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜːrtɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɜːtɪz / (BrE)
certainly; truly (used in archaic or literary English)

Examples

He certes returns
He will, certes, return before nightfall.
The knight, certes, kept his promise to the king.
It is, certes, a tale worth telling to future generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certainly - without doubt; surely
  • antonym: doubtfully - in a way that shows uncertainty
