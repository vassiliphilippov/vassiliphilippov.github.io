wholesaler

noun
person or business selling goods in bulk to retailers
3.5 1.46/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhoÊŠlËŒseÉªlÉš / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ™ÊŠlËŒseÉªlÉ™ / (BrE)
a person or company that buys goods in large quantities from producers or importers and sells them to retailers, not directly to the public

Examples

The wholesaler sells goods
After the manufacturer, the wholesaler is the next step in the supply chain.
The wholesaler supplied hundreds of stores with fresh fruit every week.
We buy our products from a local wholesaler to get better prices.

Synonyms

  • wholesale house
    3.6 0.01/m
    a wholesale house sells goods in large quantities to shops at lower prices, while a wholesaler buys from producers and sells to retailers.
  • jobber
    7.8 0.13/m
    a jobber buys large lots from producers and resells them to retailers quickly, while a wholesaler sells to retailers rather than to the public.
  • grosser
    7.4 0.00/m
    a grosser sells goods in bulk to shops and traders in older usage, while a wholesaler is the common modern term for that business.
  • distributor
    3.5 5.07/m
    a distributor supplies goods and acts as an intermediary for producers and shops, while a wholesaler buys and resells large quantities to retailers.
  • middleman
    3.4 0.71/m
    a middleman connects producers and shops and may arrange sales, while a wholesaler directly buys large quantities to resell to retailers.
