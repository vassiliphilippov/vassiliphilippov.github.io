American white pine

noun
a tall North American pine tree
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən waɪt paɪn / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən waɪt paɪn / (BrE)
A tall pine tree (Pinus strobus) native to eastern North America, with brown fissured bark when mature, valued for its timber.

Examples

The American white pine tree
Botanists are working to protect the American white pine from diseases like white pine blister rust.
Many old homes in New England were built using American white pine as a primary timber.
The American white pine can reach heights of over 60 meters in ideal conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: white pine - a term for several pine species with pale wood
