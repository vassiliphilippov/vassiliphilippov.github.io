deep freeze

noun
period of extreme cold
4.3 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / diÀêp friÀêz / (AmE), / diÀêp friÀêz / (BrE)
A period of extremely cold or freezing temperatures, especially one lasting for several days.

Examples

Winter deep freeze
After the deep freeze, pipes burst in several homes due to the intense cold.
Meteorologists warned that another deep freeze was approaching the northern states.
The entire region was hit by a deep freeze that lasted more than a week.

Synonyms

  • cold wave
    4.0 0.04/m
    cold wave is a long, widespread period of unusually low temperatures, while deep freeze is an especially severe stretch lasting several days.
  • freeze
    1.4 3.05/m
    freeze is a time when liquids turn solid and ice forms, while deep freeze describes a longer, very severe cold period.
  • cold spell
    3.5 0.11/m
    cold spell is a short run of unusually low temperatures, while deep freeze is a longer, more intense multi-day freezing period.
  • cold weather
    1.0 2.08/m
    cold weather is generally uncomfortable low temperatures, while deep freeze refers to very extreme cold that lasts for several days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cold snap - a short period of cold weather
  • antonym: heatwave - a prolonged period of excessively hot weather
