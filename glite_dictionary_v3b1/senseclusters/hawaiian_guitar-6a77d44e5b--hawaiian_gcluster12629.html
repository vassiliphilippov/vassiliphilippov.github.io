Hawaiian guitar

noun
guitar played with steel bar for glissando
6.8
Pronunciation: / həˈwaɪ.i.ən ɡɪˈtɑr / (AmE), / həˈwaɪ.i.ən ɡɪˈtɑː / (BrE)
A guitar with steel strings, played by twanging the strings while pressing them with a movable steel bar to produce a glissando effect.

Examples

Hawaiian guitar with steel strings
He learned to play the Hawaiian guitar during his vacation in Honolulu.
Many classic surf tunes feature the unique tones of a Hawaiian guitar.
The sound of the Hawaiian guitar added a smooth, sliding melody to the song.
