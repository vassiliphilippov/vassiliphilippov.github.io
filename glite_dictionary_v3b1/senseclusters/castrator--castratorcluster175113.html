castrator

noun
organism causing sterility
Pronunciation: / ˈkæstreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkæstreɪtə / (BrE)
an organism, especially a parasite, that sterilizes or prevents reproduction in another organism by damaging reproductive organs or altering physiology

Examples

Parasite castrator
The parasite acts as a castrator, preventing its host from reproducing.
Scientists studied the effects of the castrator on the local snail population.
A castrator can significantly impact the reproductive success of its host species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sterilizer - an agent or organism that makes another unable to reproduce
