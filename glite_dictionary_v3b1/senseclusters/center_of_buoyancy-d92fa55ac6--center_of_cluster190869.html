center of buoyancy

noun
center of mass of submerged part of a floating body
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntər əv bjuˈɑːnsi /; / ˈsɛntɚ əv bjuːˈjænsi / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv bjuˈɒənsi /; / ˈsɛntə əv bjuːˈjænsi / (BrE)
the point representing the center of mass of the submerged part of a floating body, through which the buoyant force acts

Examples

The center of buoyancy point
Engineers calculated the center of buoyancy to ensure the ship would remain stable under load.
The center of buoyancy shifts as more cargo is submerged in the hull.
When designing submarines, it's crucial to align the center of buoyancy with the center of gravity.
Engineers calculated the centre of buoyancy to ensure the submarine would surface properly.
Flooding the ballast tanks changed the centre of buoyancy, improving the vessel’s balance.
The ship remained stable because its centre of buoyancy was correctly aligned with its center of gravity.
