panic-stricken

adjective
overcome by intense fear or panic
4.5 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpæn.ɪk ˈstrɪk.ən/ /; / /ˈpæn.ɪk.strɪk.ən/ (AmE), /ˈpæn.ɪk ˈstrɪk.ən/ /; / /ˈpæn.ɪk.strɪk.ən/ (BrE)
Suddenly overcome by intense fear, anxiety, or desperation, often leading to irrational or frenzied behavior.

Examples

panic-stricken crowd
Firefighters struggled to control the panic-stricken crowd outside the burning building.
She gave a panic-stricken glance at the clock, realizing she was late for her meeting.
The panic-stricken passengers rushed to the exits when the alarm sounded.
