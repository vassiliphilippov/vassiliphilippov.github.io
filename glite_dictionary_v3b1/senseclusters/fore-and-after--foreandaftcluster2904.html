fore-and-after

noun
sailing vessel with fore-and-aft rig
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔr.əndˈæf.tər / (AmE), / ˌfɔː.rəndˈɑːf.tə / (BrE)
A sailing vessel rigged with sails set along the line of the keel rather than across it (i.e., equipped with a fore-and-aft rig).

Examples

A fore-and-after vessel
A well-maintained fore-and-after can be a joy to sail on coastal waters.
Many fishermen preferred the fore-and-after for its ease of maneuvering.
The old fore-and-after handled beautifully in strong winds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fore-and-aft rig - a rigging arrangement where sails are set along the line of the keel
