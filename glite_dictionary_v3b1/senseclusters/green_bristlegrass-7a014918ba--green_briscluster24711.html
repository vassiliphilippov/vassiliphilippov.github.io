green bristlegrass

noun
a species of foxtail grass, often a weed
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrin ˈbrɪsəlˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˌɡriːn ˈbrɪslɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European species of foxtail grass (Setaria viridis) that has become naturalized in North America and is often considered a troublesome weed.

Examples

Green bristlegrass weed
Green bristlegrass quickly invaded the edges of the cornfield, making weed control more difficult.
Farmers are advised to remove green bristlegrass early in the season to avoid competition with crops.
The park authorities identified the plant as green bristlegrass, a fast-growing invasive weed.
