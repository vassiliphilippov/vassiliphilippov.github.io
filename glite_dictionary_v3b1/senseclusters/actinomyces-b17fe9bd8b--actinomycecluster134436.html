Actinomyces

noun
genus of filamentous bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.tɪ.noʊˈmaɪ.siːz / (AmE), / ˌæk.tɪ.nəʊˈmaɪ.siːz / (BrE)
A genus of filamentous, Gram-positive bacteria, some species of which can cause disease in humans and animals.

Examples

Actinomyces bacteria
Some species of Actinomyces are responsible for infections in humans known as actinomycosis.
The laboratory identified the bacteria as Actinomyces based on their filamentous structure.
Veterinarians are familiar with Actinomyces because it can cause disease in livestock.
