sellout

noun
someone who betrays their principles
8.8 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛl.aʊt /; / ˈsɛlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.aʊt / (BrE)
a person who betrays their principles, cause, or associates for personal gain

Examples

He is a sellout
His colleagues viewed him as a sellout when he abandoned their campaign for a corporate job.
Many fans accused the band of being a sellout after they signed with a major label.
She refused to become a sellout even when offered a lot of money to change her art.
He was labeled a sellout for supporting policies he once opposed.
Many fans viewed the popular artist as a sellout after she changed her music style to appeal to a wider audience.
The politician was accused of being a sellout when he accepted donations from large corporations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or party
  • synonym: traitor - a person who betrays someone or something, such as a friend, cause, or principle
