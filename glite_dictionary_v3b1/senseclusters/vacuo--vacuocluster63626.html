vacuo

adjective
empty; lacking content
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvækjuˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvækjuːəʊ / (BrE)
empty or lacking content, especially used in older English texts

Examples

Vacuo statement
The philosopher described the argument as vacuo and unconvincing.
In the old manuscript, the room was depicted as vacuo and silent.
His words seemed vacuo, offering no real comfort to the listeners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled
  • antonym: full - containing as much as possible; not empty
