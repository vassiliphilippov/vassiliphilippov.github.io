spue

verb
expel saliva or phlegm from the mouth
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / spjuÀê / (AmE), / spjuÀê / (BrE)
to forcefully eject something from the mouth, such as saliva, phlegm, or vomit

Examples

To spue vomit
In disgust, he spued the bitter medicine into the sink.
She spued out the salty water after an unexpected wave hit her face.
The dragon spued fire and smoke from its nostrils during the play.
After a night of heavy drinking, she found herself spuing in the bathroom.
He was so seasick that he began to spue over the side of the boat.
The child felt ill and spued his dinner onto the floor.
