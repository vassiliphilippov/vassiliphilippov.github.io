modest

adjective
humble; unassuming
4.3 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.dəst/ /; / /ˈmɑː.dɪst/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.dɪst/ /; / /ˈmɒ.dəst/ (BrE)
not having or showing an overly high opinion of yourself; humble or unassuming

Examples

Modest about success
Despite her great success, she remained modest and never boasted about her achievements.
He gave a modest reply when praised for his hard work.
Her modest demeanor made her popular among her colleagues.
Despite winning the award, he remained modest and declined to give a speech.
She is very modest about her achievements and rarely mentions them.
They live in a modest house that is comfortable but not luxurious.
