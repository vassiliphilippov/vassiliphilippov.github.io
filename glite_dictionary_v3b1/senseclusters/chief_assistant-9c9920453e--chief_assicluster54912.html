chief assistant

noun
most important assistant
Pronunciation: / tʃif əˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / tʃiːf əˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
the most important or senior assistant in an organization or group, often with additional authority or responsibilities

Examples

After the manager resigned, the chief assistant took over her responsibilities until a replacement was found.

The chief assistant played a key role in organizing the annual conference.
