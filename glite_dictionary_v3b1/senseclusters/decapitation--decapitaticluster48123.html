decapitation

noun
act of cutting off the head
Pronunciation: / dɪˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌkæpɪˈteɪʃən /; / dɪˌkapɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of cutting off the head of a person or animal, especially as a form of execution or killing

Examples

The decapitation of a prisoner
Historical records document several cases of decapitation used as punishment for serious crimes.
The guillotine became a symbol of decapitation during the French Revolution.
The threat of decapitation was intended to deter traitors from opposing the king.
Many cultures had rituals involving decapitation as a form of capital punishment.
The historical film depicted the decapitation of the king after a dramatic trial.
The sudden decapitation of the general left the army in chaos.
