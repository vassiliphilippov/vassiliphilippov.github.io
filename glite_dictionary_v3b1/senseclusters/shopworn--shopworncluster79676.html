shopworn

adjective
stale from overuse
8.3 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑːpˌwɔrn / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpˌwɔːn / (BrE)
(of an idea, phrase, etc.) no longer interesting or effective because it has been used too often; overfamiliar through overuse.

Examples

Shopworn idea
Critics dismissed the novel for its reliance on shopworn themes and predictable twists.
His speech was filled with shopworn clichés that failed to impress the audience.
The movie's plot felt shopworn, as if it had been seen a hundred times before.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hackneyed - lacking in freshness or originality due to overuse
  • antonym: original - new and not derived from something else; not repeated too often
  • synonym: trite - (of a remark or idea) lacking originality or freshness
