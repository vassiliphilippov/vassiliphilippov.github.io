middleman

noun
person who arranges deals or acts as intermediary
3.9 0.87/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmÉªdÉ™lËŒmÃ¦n / (AmE), / ËˆmÉªdÉ™lËŒman / (BrE)
a person who acts as an intermediary, especially one who arranges deals or helps others gain access or assistance by connecting them with the right people

Examples

A middleman connects people
He acted as a middleman to help negotiate the contract between the two companies.
If you need an introduction, I can put you in touch with a reliable middleman.
Large companies often prefer to deal directly with suppliers, cutting out the middleman.

Synonyms

  • intermediary
    6.8 4.14/m
    an intermediary is a formal, neutral person who links parties to reach agreement, while a middleman sounds more informal.
  • go-between
    4.9 0.63/m
    a go-between is a casual negotiator who passes messages or ideas, while a middleman often arranges deals or contacts directly.
  • dealmaker
    4.3 0.36/m
    a dealmaker is someone who actively creates and closes business agreements, while a middleman mainly connects people or helps arrange deals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: client - a person who receives assistance or services, as opposed to the provider
  • synonym: go-between - a person who acts as an intermediary between two people or groups
  • synonym: intermediary - a person who acts as a link between people in order to try to bring about an agreement
