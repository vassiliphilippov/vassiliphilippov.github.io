ivy-leaved geranium

noun
a trailing plant with shield-shaped leaves and rosy flowers
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪvi livd dʒəˈreɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪvi liːvd dʒəˈreɪniəm / (BrE)
a trailing plant native to South America, commonly grown for its peltate (shield-shaped) leaves and rosy flowers

Examples

The ivy-leaved geranium plant
Gardeners often choose the ivy-leaved geranium for its attractive foliage and flowers.
She decorated her balcony with pots of ivy-leaved geranium to add a splash of color.
The ivy-leaved geranium is perfect for cascading over the sides of a hanging basket.
