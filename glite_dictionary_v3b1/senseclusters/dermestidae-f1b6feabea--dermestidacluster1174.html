Dermestidae

noun
family of beetles known as carpet beetles
Pronunciation: / dɚˈmɛstəˌdi /; / dərˈmɛstəˌdi / (AmE), / dɜːˈmɛstɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of beetles, commonly known as carpet beetles, whose larvae feed on dry animal or plant matter and are pests of carpets, stored products, and museum specimens

Examples

Adult Dermestidae beetles
Adult Dermestidae are often found near windows, attracted by light.
Many museums take precautions against Dermestidae to protect their textile collections.
The larvae of Dermestidae can cause significant damage to carpets and clothing stored in homes.
Museums must take precautions against infestations of Dermestidae in their collections.
Several species within the Dermestidae family are notorious for invading stored food products.
The larvae of Dermestidae can cause significant damage to woollen fabrics.
