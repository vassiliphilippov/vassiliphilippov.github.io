mitral valve

noun
valve between left atrium and ventricle in the heart
7.3 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.trəl vælv/ /; / /ˈmɪ.trəl vælv/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.trəl vælv/ /; / /ˈmɪ.trəl vælv/ (BrE)
the valve with two cusps, located between the left atrium and the left ventricle of the heart, that prevents blood from flowing backward

Examples

A defect in the mitral valve can cause blood to leak backward into the left atrium.
Mitral valve replacement surgery may be necessary if the mitral valve becomes severely damaged.
The surgeon repaired the patient's mitral valve to resolve the heart murmur.
