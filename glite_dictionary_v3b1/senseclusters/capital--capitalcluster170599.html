capital

noun
top section of a column (architecture)
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl / (BrE)
the uppermost part of a column or pillar, often decorative, that supports the entablature in classical architecture

Examples

Column capital
Damage to the capital weakened the entire column's support.
Restorers spent months repairing the marble capital on the ancient temple.
The Corinthian capital is known for its ornate acanthus leaves.
