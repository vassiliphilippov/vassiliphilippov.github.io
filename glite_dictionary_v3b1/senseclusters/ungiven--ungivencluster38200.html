ungiven

adjective
not previously mentioned
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡɪvən / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡɪvən / (BrE)
not previously introduced or known in a conversation, text, or context; new to the listener or reader

Examples

Ungiven information
In the sentence, the subject is ungiven and must be inferred from the context.
The teacher explained that any ungiven information should be treated as new by the reader.
Linguists often analyze how speakers introduce ungiven elements in conversation.
