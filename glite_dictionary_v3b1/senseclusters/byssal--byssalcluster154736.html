byssal

adjective
relating to byssus fibers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪsəl / (BrE)
made from, containing, or relating to byssus, the fine silky fibers produced by certain molluscs and sometimes used as a textile

Examples

Byssal fabric
The ancient scarf was woven from byssal threads collected from Mediterranean mussels.
Researchers studied the byssal attachments that help mussels cling to rocks in strong currents.
A rare byssal fabric, known as sea silk, was displayed at the textile exhibition.
