Bignonia capreolata

noun
woody flowering vine of the southern US
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɪɡˈnoʊniə ˌkæpriˈoʊlətə / (AmE), / bɪɡˈnəʊniə ˌkæpriˈɒlətə / (BrE)
A woody flowering vine native to the southern United States, noted for its cross-shaped stem in cross-section and showy trumpet-shaped flowers.

Examples

Bignonia capreolata vine
Bignonia capreolata is often grown for its beautiful orange-red flowers in southern gardens.
Gardeners prize Bignonia capreolata for its ability to quickly cover trellises and fences.
The stems of Bignonia capreolata show a distinctive cross pattern when cut in cross-section.
