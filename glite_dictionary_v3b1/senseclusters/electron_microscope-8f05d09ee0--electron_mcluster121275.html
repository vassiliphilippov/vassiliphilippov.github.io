electron microscope

noun
microscope using electron beams for high magnification
4.8 4.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɑn ˈmaɪkrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɒn ˈmaɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
a powerful microscope that uses a beam of electrons instead of light to form highly magnified images of very small objects

Examples

An electron microscope image
An electron microscope can reveal viruses and tiny particles invisible to light microscopes.
Images taken with an electron microscope are often black and white but highly detailed.
The scientist used an electron microscope to observe the detailed structure of the cell.
