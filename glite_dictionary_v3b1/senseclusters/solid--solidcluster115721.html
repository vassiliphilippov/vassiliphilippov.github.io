solid

adjective
not liquid or gas; firm and stable
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːlɪd /; / ˈsɑː.lɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd /; / ˈsɒl.ɪd / (BrE)
firm, hard, or having a definite shape and volume; not liquid, gas, hollow, or mixed with other materials

Examples

Solid metal block
At room temperature, iron remains solid and maintains its shape.
Chocolate can melt in your hand, going from solid to liquid.

A sphere is one of the basic solid shapes in geometry.
Children learn the difference between a solid and a plane figure in math class.
Engineers poured a solid block of concrete as the building's foundation.
He handed me a solid gold ring that felt surprisingly heavy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firm - hard or resistant to pressure
  • antonym: gas - substance with no fixed shape or volume, expands freely
  • antonym: hollow - having an empty space inside; not solid throughout
  • antonym: liquid - fluid with no fixed shape, flows easily
  • antonym: plane - a flat, two-dimensional surface
  • synonym: pure - consisting of only one substance or element; not mixed
  • antonym: soft - not hard or firm; yielding easily to pressure
