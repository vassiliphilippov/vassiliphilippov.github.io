soutane

noun
long priestly garment
Pronunciation: / suˈtæn / (AmE), / suːˈtæn / (BrE)
A long, close-fitting garment with buttons down the front, traditionally worn by Roman Catholic priests.

Examples

Black soutane buttons
He recognized the visiting bishop by the distinctive buttons on his black soutane.
New members of the clergy are often given a new soutane as part of their vestments.
The priest adjusted his soutane before stepping into the church.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cassock - a long, close-fitting garment worn by clergy
