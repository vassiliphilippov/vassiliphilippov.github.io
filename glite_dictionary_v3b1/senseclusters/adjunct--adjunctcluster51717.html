adjunct

noun
supplementary addition; not essential
9.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt/ (AmE), /ˈædʒ.ʌŋkt/ (BrE)
a thing added to something else as a supplementary rather than an essential part

Examples

An adjunct therapy
Her meditation practice is an adjunct to the medical treatment she receives for anxiety.
Many herbs are used as an adjunct to traditional recipes to enhance flavor.
The professor served as an adjunct to the department, teaching only a few courses each semester.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integral - essential to completeness
