Ciceronian

adjective
of Cicero's ideas
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪsəˈroʊniən / (AmE), / ˌsɪsəˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to Cicero, especially his political ideas, writings, or beliefs rather than his style of speaking

Examples

Ciceronian political ideas
The scholar's Ciceronian arguments reflected a deep respect for Roman republican values.
Her essay took a Ciceronian approach to justice and civic duty.
Many philosophers admire the Ciceronian tradition of combining ethics with political thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ciceronianism - the study or imitation of Cicero's ideas or style
