elastic demand

noun
demand highly responsive to price changes
5.4 4.5
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlæs.tɪk dɪ.ˈmænd/ /; / /ə.ˈlæs.tɪk də.ˈmænd/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlæs.tɪk dɪ.ˈmɑːnd/ /; / /ə.ˈlæs.tɪk dɪ.ˈmɑːnd/ (BrE)
demand for a good or service that changes significantly when its price changes, increasing as prices fall and decreasing as prices rise.

Examples

Elastic demand changes
As gasoline prices dropped, the elastic demand led to a noticeable increase in sales.
Economists study elastic demand to understand how pricing changes affect consumer behavior.
Luxury cars often experience elastic demand because buyers can postpone purchases if prices rise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inelastic demand - demand that changes very little when price changes
