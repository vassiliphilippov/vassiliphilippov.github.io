contingency procedure

noun
alternative procedure for anticipated unusual events
5.7 5.0
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈtɪn.dʒən.siː prə.ˈsiː.dʒər/ /; / /kən.ˈtɪn.dʒən.siː proʊ.ˈsiː.dʒər/ (AmE), /kən.ˈtɪn.dʒən.siː prə.ˈsiː.dʒə/ /; / /kən.ˈtɪn.dʒən.siː prəʊ.ˈsiː.dʒə/ (BrE)
a set of planned actions or steps to be followed if an unusual but anticipated situation arises, as an alternative to the normal procedure

Examples

A contingency procedure plan
Every laboratory must have a contingency procedure in place for chemical spills.
Staff receive annual training on the contingency procedure for fire emergencies.
The contingency procedure was triggered after the main power system failed.
