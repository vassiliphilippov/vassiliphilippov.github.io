tension

noun
balance of opposing elements or ideas
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈten.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈten.ʃən/ (BrE)
a balance or interplay between opposing elements or tendencies, especially in art, literature, or ideas, that creates interest or complexity

Examples

Tension between ideas
In the debate, there was clear tension between competing interpretations of history.
Modern architecture often explores the tension between form and function.
The tension between tradition and innovation gives this novel its dynamic quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmony - the combination of different elements in a pleasing way, or agreement
