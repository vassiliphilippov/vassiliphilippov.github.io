assimilation

noun
merging observations into models
7.0 0.08/m

(in meteorology, climatology, and other sciences) the process of incorporating observational data into a numerical model or simulation to improve its accuracy, initial conditions, or forecasts.

Examples

Data assimilation
Meteorologists use assimilation to combine satellite observations with models before each forecast run.
Improved assimilation of ocean temperature data helped the climate model produce more accurate seasonal predictions.
The research team tested a new assimilation technique to merge noisy sensor readings into their simulation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data fusion - combining multiple observational sources to produce a consistent dataset for analysis or modeling
  • synonym: integration - the act of combining parts into a whole; here, combining observations with model state information
