ordinal

noun
number showing position in sequence
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrdənl / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪnəl / (BrE)
a number that shows the position of something in a sequence, such as first, second, or third

Examples

An ordinal
Children learn to count both cardinal and ordinal numbers in early mathematics.
In the list, the ordinal for her position was fourth.
The teacher asked each student to write the ordinal next to their name.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cardinal - a number that shows quantity rather than position (one, two, three, etc.)
