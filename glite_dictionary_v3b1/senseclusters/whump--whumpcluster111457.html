whump

noun
dull muffled sound
Pronunciation: / wʌmp / (AmE), / wʌmp / (BrE)
a dull, muffled sound, especially one made by a heavy object falling or hitting something

Examples

A whump of snow
There was a sudden whump as the snow slid off the roof.
We heard a heavy whump from the basement and rushed downstairs to check.
The box landed with a loud whump on the wooden floor.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bang - a sudden loud noise
  • degree lower: rustle - a soft, light sound like leaves moving
  • synonym: thud - a dull sound made by something heavy falling or hitting
