rights offering

noun
discounted share offer to existing shareholders
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪts ˌɔfərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraɪts ˌɒfərɪŋ / (BrE)
an offer by a company to its existing shareholders to buy additional shares, usually at a discount, before the shares are offered to the public

Examples

A rights offering discount
A rights offering often allows investors to purchase shares at a price lower than the market value.
During the rights offering, shareholders had the chance to buy extra shares before they were available to others.
The company announced a rights offering to raise additional capital from its current shareholders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: initial public offering - the first offering of stock by a private company to the public
  • synonym: share issue - the process of offering new shares to investors
