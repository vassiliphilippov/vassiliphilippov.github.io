Anguillidae

noun
family of migratory freshwater eels
Pronunciation: / ˌæŋˌɡwɪˈlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌæŋˌɡwɪˈlɪːdɪ / (BrE)
A family of eels that live in fresh water as adults but migrate to the sea to spawn.

Examples

The Anguillidae family
Members of the Anguillidae family are known for their remarkable migratory journeys.
Scientists study Anguillidae to better understand the decline of freshwater eel populations.
The European eel, part of the Anguillidae, travels thousands of miles to breed in the sea.
