militia

noun
body of civilians eligible for military service
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪʃə / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪʃə / (BrE)
an organized group of ordinary citizens who act as soldiers but are not part of the regular army, usually called up in emergencies

Examples

The militia group
At that time, all able-bodied men were considered part of the militia.
Debate continues about whether a modern society still needs a militia.
The government has the power to call up the militia in times of national emergency.
During the revolution, volunteers joined the local militia to defend their community.
Members of the militia received only basic training, unlike regular soldiers.
The new governor called up the militia to help maintain order during the protests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: army - the organized military force of a nation
  • synonym: national guard - military reserve force made up of civilians, especially in the US
