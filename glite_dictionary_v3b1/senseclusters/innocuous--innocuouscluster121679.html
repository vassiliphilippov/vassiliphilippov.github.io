innocuous

adjective
not harmful or offensive
9.0 5.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈnɑː.kjuː.əs/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈnɒk.juː.əs/ (BrE)
not harmful or offensive; not likely to cause injury or strong reaction

Examples

An innocuous remark
Her remark was intended to be innocuous, but it was misunderstood by the group.
Most of the insects in the garden are innocuous and pose no threat to humans.
The medicine appeared completely innocuous but caused severe side effects in rare cases.
Although the insect looks scary, its sting is actually innocuous to humans.
Her comment seemed innocuous at first, but it was later misunderstood.
The conversation was entirely innocuous, with nothing that could offend anyone.
Her comment was completely innocuous and did not upset anyone at the meeting.
The doctor assured me that the procedure was innocuous and involved no risk.
They exchanged a few innocuous pleasantries before starting the discussion.
