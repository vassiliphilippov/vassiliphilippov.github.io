skew

verb
to distort or bias
5.4
Pronunciation: / skju / (AmE), / skjuː / (BrE)
to change or distort something so that it is no longer true, accurate, or balanced

Examples

To skew the results
If you skew the results, the conclusions of the experiment may be invalid.
The media coverage can sometimes skew public perception of an event.
Be careful not to skew the data when entering it into the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - to influence in a way that is unfair or unbalanced
  • synonym: distort - to change the shape or meaning of something so it is no longer accurate
  • antonym: straighten - to make something straight or correct again
