low-information source

noun
source giving little or unreliable information
1.0
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən sɔrs / (AmE), / ləʊ ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən sɔːs / (BrE)
a source that provides minimal, incomplete, or unreliable information

Examples

A low-information source
He cited a low-information source, so his facts could not be verified.
News from a low-information source often leads to misunderstandings or misinformation.
Researchers warn against relying on a low-information source for important decisions.
