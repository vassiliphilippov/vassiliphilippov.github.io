hansel

noun
first sale or money
Pronunciation: / ˈhænzəl / (AmE), / ˈhænzəl / (BrE)
the first money or sale received by a person or business, especially at the start of trading (chiefly Scottish, dated)

Examples

First hansel earned
The baker was delighted with the hansel he received on the opening morning.
Traditionally, shopkeepers would cherish their hansel as a sign of good luck for the business.
He framed the first coin as his hansel to remember the start of his career.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earnings - money received for work or services
  • synonym: profit - money gained from business after expenses
