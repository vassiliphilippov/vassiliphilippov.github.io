noncontingent

adjective
not dependent on conditions
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈtɪndʒənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈtɪndʒənt / (BrE)
not dependent on circumstances, events, or conditions; independent (used in logic for statements that are always true or always false, and in general for things not affected by other factors)

Examples

Noncontingent statement
In logic, a noncontingent statement is true or false in every possible world.
The mathematician explained that certain principles are noncontingent and do not rely on empirical evidence.
Philosophers debate whether moral truths can be considered noncontingent or if they depend on context.
The company offered a noncontingent bonus to all employees, regardless of their performance.
Access to the resource was noncontingent on membership or prior approval.
The grant was awarded as a noncontingent payment, not tied to any specific results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contingent - dependent on circumstances; not necessarily true or false
  • synonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others
  • synonym: necessary - true in all possible circumstances
