Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky

proper noun
Russian composer (1840–1893), famous for expressive music
7.6
Pronunciation: / piˈjɔt̬ɚ ilˈjitʃ tʃaɪˈkɑvski / (AmE), / pɪˈɒtə ɪlˈjiːtʃ tʃaɪˈkɒfski / (BrE)
(1840–1893) An important Russian composer known for his expressive melodies, whose works include ballets such as 'Swan Lake' and 'The Nutcracker'.

Examples

Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky ballet composer
Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky composed 'The Nutcracker,' a ballet that remains popular around the world.
Many orchestras perform Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky's works, especially during the holiday season.
The music of Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky is beloved for its emotional depth and memorable melodies.
