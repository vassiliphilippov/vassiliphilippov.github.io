repleteness

noun
excessive bodily fullness
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpliːtnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈpliːtnəs / (BrE)
a state of excessive fullness or overabundance in the body, especially of blood or bodily fluids, used historically in medicine

Examples

Bodily repleteness
Victorian doctors often attributed illness to an imbalance or repleteness of bodily humours.
The physician warned that repleteness could lead to headaches and other discomforts.
In historical texts, repleteness is sometimes mentioned as a cause of disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deficiency - a lack or shortage of something
  • synonym: plethora - an excessive amount, especially of bodily fluid
