involucrum

noun
whale ear bone covering
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnvəˈluːkrəm / (AmE), / ˌɪnvəˈluːkrəm / (BrE)
a thickened bony covering of the auditory bulla in whales and their fossil relatives

Examples

Whale involucrum
Scientists identified the involucrum in the fossil, confirming it belonged to an ancient whale.
The unique structure of the involucrum helps researchers distinguish cetacean fossils from other mammals.
A thickened involucrum is a key feature in the evolution of whale hearing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulla - a rounded bony structure, especially in the ear
