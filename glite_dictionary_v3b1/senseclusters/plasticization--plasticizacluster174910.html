plasticization

noun
making material flexible
Pronunciation: / ˌplæstɪsɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌplæstɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making a material, especially a polymer or plastic, softer and more flexible by adding a plasticizer or using heat

Examples

Plasticization of PVC
The plasticization of PVC allows it to be used in flexible hoses and cables.
Proper plasticization is essential for producing high-quality molded plastic products.
During manufacturing, plasticization helps the polymer flow smoothly into the mold.
The plasticization of polymers allows manufacturers to create products with varying degrees of flexibility.
During plasticization, additives are mixed into the material to improve its softness.
Proper plasticization is essential for producing flexible cables and hoses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hardening - the process of making something harder or more rigid
  • synonym: softening - the process of making something softer
