Tanna

noun
Jewish Mishnah scholar
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːnə / (AmE), / ˈtænə / (BrE)
a Jewish scholar who contributed to the Mishnah between the 1st and 3rd centuries CE

Examples

Tanna Rabbi Akiva
The Tanna Rabbi Akiva played a major role in shaping Jewish law.
Many teachings of a Tanna are preserved in the Mishnah.
Each Tanna contributed unique interpretations to the oral tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rabbi - a Jewish religious teacher and leader
