Phaseolus coccineus

noun
runner bean plant; food bean with red flowers
Pronunciation: / fəˌziːoʊləs kɒkˈsɪniəs / (AmE), / fəˌziːələs kɒkˈsɪniəs / (BrE)
A tropical American perennial bean plant with red flowers and mottled black beans, commonly known as the runner bean, widely grown for food, especially in Britain.

Examples

Phaseolus coccineus plant
Botanists often refer to the runner bean as Phaseolus coccineus when discussing plant classifications.
In Great Britain, Phaseolus coccineus is a popular vegetable for both its beans and pods.
The bright red flowers of the Phaseolus coccineus are a striking sight in British gardens.
