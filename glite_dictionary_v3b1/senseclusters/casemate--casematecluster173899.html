casemate

verb
to enclose with fortification
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsmeɪt / (BrE)
to enclose or protect something by building a fortified structure or shelter around it, especially in military contexts

Examples

To casemate a gun
The engineers decided to casemate the artillery to shield it from enemy fire.
During the renovation, workers casemated the old cannons to preserve them for future generations.
Military planners recommended that the command post be casemated for maximum protection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expose - to leave unprotected or uncovered
  • synonym: fortify - to strengthen a place with defensive works
