plummet

verb
fall or drop sharply
5.8 6.38/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆplÊŒmÉ™t / (AmE), / ËˆplÊŒmÉªt / (BrE)
to fall or drop suddenly and steeply, either physically or in amount/value

Examples

To plummet down
Her mood plummeted when she heard the disappointing news.
House prices continued to plummet after the financial crisis hit.
Shares in the company plummeted by more than 30% in a single day.
Stock prices began to plummet after the disappointing earnings report was released.
The bird seemed to plummet from the sky before catching itself and soaring again.
Temperatures can plummet at night in the desert, so bring warm clothing.

Synonyms

  • plunge
    3.8 7.00/m
    plunge is a rapid fall from a higher place, often physical or dramatic, while plummet means a sudden, steep drop.
  • dive
    3.9 0.72/m
    dive is used for quick sharp falls in prices or numbers, often informal, while plummet is more neutral and steep.
  • crash
    4.8 4.81/m
    crash describes a sudden severe failure or collapse, often catastrophic, while plummet focuses on a steep rapid fall in value.
  • tumble
    4.4 1.73/m
    tumble means to fall or decline in a rough or disorderly way, often smaller steps, while plummet is a sharp steep drop.
  • slump
    4.1 1.10/m
    slump is a longer, often slower large decrease in value or activity, while plummet is a sudden steep fall.
