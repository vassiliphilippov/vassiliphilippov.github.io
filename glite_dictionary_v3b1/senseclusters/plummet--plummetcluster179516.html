plummet

verb
fall or drop sharply
9.5 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈplʌ.mət/ (AmE), /ˈplʌ.mɪt/ /; / /ˈplʌ.mət/ (BrE)
to fall or drop suddenly and steeply, either physically or in amount/value

Examples

To plummet down
Her mood plummeted when she heard the disappointing news.
House prices continued to plummet after the financial crisis hit.
Shares in the company plummeted by more than 30% in a single day.
Stock prices began to plummet after the disappointing earnings report was released.
The bird seemed to plummet from the sky before catching itself and soaring again.
Temperatures can plummet at night in the desert, so bring warm clothing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descend - to move or fall downward
  • synonym: drop - to decrease or fall to a lower level
  • antonym: soar - to rise very quickly to a high level
