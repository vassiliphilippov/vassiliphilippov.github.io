multipartisan

adjective
involving many parties
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈpɑːrtɪzən / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈpɑːtɪzən / (BrE)
involving or supported by members of more than one party, group, or organization (often used about politics, but can be broader)

Examples

Multipartisan agreement
The new law was passed with multipartisan agreement from across the political spectrum.
A multipartisan committee was formed to address the urgent environmental issues.
The proposal gained multipartisan backing, making it more likely to succeed.
The new policy was developed through a multipartisan effort involving educators, parents, and local businesses.
A multipartisan coalition worked together to address the city's transportation challenges.
The agreement was praised as a rare multipartisan achievement in the region.
