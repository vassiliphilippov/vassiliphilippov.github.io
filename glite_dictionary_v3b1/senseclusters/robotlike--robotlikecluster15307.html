robotlike

adjective
mechanical or unemotional; like a robot
6.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆroÊŠËŒbÉ‘tËŒlaÉªk / (AmE), / ËˆrÉ™ÊŠbÉ’tËŒlaÉªk / (BrE)
Behaving or functioning in a way that appears mechanical, unemotional, or lacking in independent thought.

Examples

Robotlike behavior
During the interview, she gave robotlike answers, sticking strictly to rehearsed lines.
His movements were robotlike, showing no emotion or spontaneity.
The customer service felt robotlike, as if a real person wasn't behind the responses.

Synonyms

  • robotic
    5.2 0.66/m
    robotic sounds clinical and often describes stiff, machine-controlled actions, while robotlike means acting mechanically without emotion or independent thought.
  • automatonlike
    9.3 0.00/m
    automatonlike describes someone who moves or thinks like a mindless machine, while robotlike means acting mechanically and without feeling.
  • machinelike
    6.7 0.03/m
    machinelike describes precise, regular, and emotionless movement like a tool, while robotlike means behaving mechanically and without independent thought.
  • automatic
    3.7 3.62/m
    automatic refers to actions done without thinking or by reflex, while robotlike implies a cold, machine-like lack of feeling or thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automatic - acting without conscious thought; mechanical or habitual
  • antonym: human - characteristic of people with feelings and individual thought; not mechanical
  • synonym: machinelike - resembling the operation of a machine; mechanical and automatic
