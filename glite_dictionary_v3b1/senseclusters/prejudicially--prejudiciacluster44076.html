prejudicially

adverb
in a harmful way
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛdʒəˈdɪʃəli / (AmE), / ˌprɛdʒʊˈdɪʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that causes harm or unfair disadvantage to someone or something

Examples

To prejudicially affect rights
The judge ruled that the comments could prejudicially influence the outcome of the trial.
The new policy prejudicially affects small businesses in the area.
Information released to the media prejudicially shaped public opinion before the case was heard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beneficially - in a way that produces good or helpful results
  • synonym: harmfully - in a way that causes damage or injury
  • synonym: unfairly - in a way that is not just or equitable
