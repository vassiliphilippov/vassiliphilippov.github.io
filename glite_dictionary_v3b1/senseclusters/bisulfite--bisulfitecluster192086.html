bisulfite

noun
HSO3− compound
Pronunciation: / baɪˈsʌlfaɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈsʌlfaɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the HSO3- ion, used in industry, laboratories, and food as a preservative or reagent

Examples

Bisulfite solution
The scientist added bisulfite to the solution to prevent oxidation.
Food manufacturers sometimes use bisulfite as a preservative in dried fruits.
The bisulfite reaction is important in DNA methylation analysis.
The laboratory used bisulfite treatment to analyze DNA methylation patterns in the samples.
A solution of bisulfite was added to the reaction to convert unmethylated cytosines.
Researchers rely on bisulfite sequencing to study epigenetic changes in genetic material.
Many processed foods contain bisulfite to help preserve freshness and prevent spoilage.
Some people are sensitive to bisulfite and may experience allergic reactions after consuming it.
Winemakers often add bisulfite to wine to protect it from oxidation during storage.
