trip wire

noun
small force triggering bigger military response
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪp ˌwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp ˌwaɪə / (BrE)
A small military force positioned as a first line of defense, whose involvement in hostilities will prompt the intervention of larger forces.

Examples

Trip wire force
NATO stationed a trip wire force in the Baltic states to assure immediate response if attacked.
The deployment of a trip wire in the region serves to dissuade aggression from neighboring countries.
The presence of a trip wire increases the risks for any adversary contemplating an incursion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deterrent force - a force intended to discourage an enemy from action
