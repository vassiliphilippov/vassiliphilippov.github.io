analepsis

noun
literary flashback
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈlɛpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænəˈlɛpsɪs / (BrE)
a literary device in which a past event is described or recounted, also known as a flashback

Examples

Analepsis in stories
The author uses analepsis to reveal important details about the protagonist's past.
Through analepsis, the film explains how the two characters first met years ago.
A sudden analepsis in the story helps the reader understand the main conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flashback - a scene in a story set in a time earlier than the main story
  • antonym: prolepsis - a literary device in which a future event is described, also known as a flashforward
