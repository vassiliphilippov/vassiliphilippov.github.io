housemother

noun
woman supervising residents in dormitory
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌmʌðɚ /; / \u02c8ha\u028as\u02ccm\u028c\u00f0\u025a / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌmʌðə /; / \u02c8ha\u028as\u02ccm\u028c\u00f0\u0259 / (BrE)
a woman who supervises, cares for, and mentors members of a group living together or forming a close community, such as in a dormitory, boarding house, or a drag/ballroom house

Examples

The housemother cares
As the housemother, Mrs. Green offered guidance and support to the university freshmen.
The housemother checked that all the girls were in their rooms before lights out.
The boarding school appointed a new housemother for the junior dormitory this year.
The housemother of the local drag house gave each newcomer advice before their first performance.
At the ball, the housemother announced the lineup and coached contestants between walks.
When the youngest queen struggled, the housemother arranged practice sessions and introduced her to promoters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: housefather - a man employed as a chaperon in a residence for young people
  • synonym: matriarch - a leading or dominant woman in a family or group
  • synonym: mentor - an experienced person who advises and supports less experienced people
