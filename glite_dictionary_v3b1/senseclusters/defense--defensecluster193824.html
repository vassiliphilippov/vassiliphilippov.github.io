defense

noun
argument or justification for an act or belief
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
an argument or statement made to support or justify something, or to respond to criticism

Examples

Defense of an idea
He wrote a passionate defense of free speech in his editorial.
Her defense of the controversial painting persuaded some critics to reconsider their stance.
The scientist delivered a thorough defense of her new hypothesis at the conference.
During the debate, the candidate's defense was logical and convincing.
His lawyer's defense centered on the lack of evidence presented.
When challenged in the meeting, she gave a strong defense of her proposal.
In his defence, he explained that he acted out of concern for safety.
She wrote a compelling defence of her decision in the newspaper editorial.
The professor's defence of the controversial theory sparked heated debate among scholars.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong
  • synonym: apology - a statement in defense of something
  • antonym: attack - a criticism or challenge to someone's statements or actions
  • antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval of someone or something
  • synonym: defense - alternative (American English) spelling of 'defence'
  • synonym: justification - the action of showing something to be right or reasonable
  • synonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or charge to be false
