twice-pinnate

adjective
having divisions twice pinnate (botanical)

Pronunciation: /twaɪs ˈpɪn.ət/ /; / /twaɪs ˈpɪn.eɪt/ (AmE), /twaɪs ˈpɪn.ət/ /; / /twaɪs ˈpɪn.eɪt/ (BrE)
(of a leaf) having divisions that are themselves pinnate; doubly pinnate.

Examples

Twice-pinnate leaf
Each twice-pinnate leaf is divided into many smaller leaflets arranged in pairs along the stem.
Some acacias display twice-pinnate foliage, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.
The fern has twice-pinnate leaves that give it a delicate and feathery appearance.
