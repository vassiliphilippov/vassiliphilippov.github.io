awful

adjective
inspiring awe or reverence (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfəl / (AmE), / ˈɔːfəl / (BrE)
(archaic) inspiring awe, reverence, or wonder; filled with or producing a sense of amazement or respect

Examples

Awful majesty
He gazed at the mountains with an awful sense of reverence and wonder.
The cathedral had an awful majesty that left visitors speechless.
The ceremony took place in an awful silence, as everyone respected the sacred moment.
In the awful silence, he found himself humbled before nature's power.
The cathedral had an awful stillness that left visitors speechless.
They gazed at the awful majesty of the mountains at dawn.

  • synonym: dreadful - causing fear or dread, can also imply awe (archaic sense)
