argue

verb
present reasons or debate
2.8 130/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ërgju / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀêgjuÀê / (BrE)
to give reasons or cite evidence in support of or against an idea, action, or theory; to discuss or debate an issue logically

Examples

To argue a point
Scientists often argue that more research is necessary before drawing conclusions.
She tried to argue her point using clear and logical reasons.
The lawyer will argue the case before the court tomorrow.

Synonyms

  • debate
    2.8 30/m
    debate is a formal discussion with opposing sides, while argue is a general act of giving reasons for or against something.
  • make a case
    3.7 2/m
    make a case is to give clear reasons in support, while argue can mean giving reasons both for or against an idea.
  • put the case
    7.1 0.05/m
    put the case is to present a point or argument to others, while argue covers general logical discussion for or against ideas.
  • contend
    4.6 15/m
    contend is to state or claim something firmly in discussion, while argue involves giving reasons and evidence for or against.
  • dispute
    3.5 13/m
    dispute is to question or deny a claim and often oppose it, while argue also includes offering reasons for or against.
  • reason
    3.3 10/m
    reason is to think or use logic to reach conclusions on your own, while argue is to give reasons to others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debate - to discuss a question by considering opposed arguments
  • synonym: persuade - to cause someone to do something through reasoning or argument
