blamably

adverb
deserving blame
Pronunciation: / ˈbleɪməbli / (AmE), / ˈbleɪməbli / (BrE)
in a manner that deserves blame or is worthy of criticism

Examples

Acted blamably
The manager acted blamably by failing to address the team's concerns.
If the equipment was used blamably, the accident could have been prevented.
She responded blamably by ignoring the clear instructions given to her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blamelessly - in a way that is free from blame or fault
  • synonym: culpably - in a way that is deserving of blame or guilt
