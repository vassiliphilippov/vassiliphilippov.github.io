underlie

verb
to lie beneath or form a foundation
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈlaɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈlaɪ / (BrE)
to be the basis or foundation of something, either physically or as the underlying cause

Examples

Ruins underlie city
Ancient ruins underlie much of the modern city.
Thick layers of rock underlie the surface soil in this region.
Unseen emotions often underlie people's visible actions.
Economic factors often underlie social changes within a country.
Many health problems underlie stress and anxiety in modern life.
The principles that underlie our laws are rooted in history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overlie - to lie over or on top of
  • synonym: support - to bear the weight of or hold up
