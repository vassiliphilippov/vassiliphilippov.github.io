extension

noun
set of things a term refers to
3.7
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnʃən / (BrE)
the class of things or objects to which a word, phrase, or expression refers; the specific denotation of a term

Examples

Extension of a term
In logic, the extension of a term includes all things it accurately describes.
Philosophy students debated the extension of technical expressions in the lecture.
The extension of the word 'bird' covers all feathered, flying creatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intension - the internal content or inherent sense of a term as opposed to its extension
