undersigned

verb
to have signed below
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈsaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsaɪnd / (BrE)
having signed one's name at the end of a document, especially to indicate approval or authorship.

Examples

Contract undersigned
The contract was undersigned by all parties before it became effective.
Each member of the board undersigned the final report to confirm their agreement.
The letter, undersigned by the principal, was sent to all parents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authorize - to give official approval or permission
  • synonym: sign - to write one's name on a document
