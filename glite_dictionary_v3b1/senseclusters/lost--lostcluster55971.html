lost

adjective
doomed or irretrievably destroyed
5.3 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / lÉ”st / (AmE), / lÉ’st / (BrE)
Having no hope of being saved, recovered, or rescued; spiritually or physically doomed or destroyed.

Examples

The lost souls
He felt like one of the lost after the collapse of his community.
The message was meant to give hope to the lost and those without direction.
There was a sense of despair among the lost survivors stranded at sea.

Synonyms

  • doomed
    8.8 0.01/m
    doomed describes being fated to fail or die by unavoidable forces, while lost means beyond hope of being saved.
  • gone
    5.5 31/m
    gone often describes something already destroyed or dead, while lost means having no hope of being saved.
  • done for
    7.7 1.23/m
    done for is an informal phrase meaning doomed and unable to be saved, while lost sounds more neutral and formal.
  • sunk
    7.5 0.00/m
    sunk often describes a plan or chance that fails completely, while lost refers to being hopelessly beyond rescue.
  • hopeless
    2.5 2.37/m
    hopeless describes a feeling or situation with no hope left, while lost focuses on being beyond any chance of rescue.
