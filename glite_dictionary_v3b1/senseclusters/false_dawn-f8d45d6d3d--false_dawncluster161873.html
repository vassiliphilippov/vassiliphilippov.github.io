false dawn

noun
misleading sign of hope or improvement
7.3
Pronunciation: / fɔls dɔn / (AmE), / fɔːls dɔːn / (BrE)
an event or sign that appears to indicate improvement or hope but turns out to be misleading or disappointing

Examples

A false dawn of hope
After the peace talks, there was a false dawn of reconciliation, but conflict soon resumed.
Many believed the new government would bring real change, but it turned out to be a false dawn.
The company's brief rise in stock prices proved to be a false dawn for investors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disillusionment - disappointment resulting from the discovery that something is not as good as believed
  • synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression of reality
