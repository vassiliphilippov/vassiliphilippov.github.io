furl

verb
to roll or fold up, esp. a sail or flag
Pronunciation: / fɝl / (AmE), / fɜːl / (BrE)
to roll or fold up something, especially a sail or flag, neatly and securely

Examples

To furl a sail
She began to furl the canvas after painting in the park.
The crew furled the sails as the storm approached.
When the ceremony ended, they carefully furled the flag.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unfurl - to spread out or unroll something, especially sails or flags
