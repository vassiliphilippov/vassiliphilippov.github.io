oversample

noun
extra data subgroup
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.sæm.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.sæm.pəl/ (BrE)
an additional set of observations collected to increase the representation or accuracy for a specific subgroup in a study or survey

Examples

A study oversample
The researchers included an oversample of rural residents to ensure their views were accurately represented.
To study rare conditions, scientists often use an oversample of affected individuals in their surveys.
The survey design called for an oversample of young adults to improve the reliability of age-based comparisons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sample - a group of observations or data taken from a larger population
  • antonym: undersample - a smaller set of observations collected from a subgroup, often leading to less representation
