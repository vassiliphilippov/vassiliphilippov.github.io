comparatively

adverb
in comparison to something else
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpær.ə.tɪv.li / (AmE), / kəmˈpær.ətɪv.li / (BrE)
in a way that involves comparison with something else; to a degree when compared to others or to a previous state

Examples

A comparatively small loss
Despite the setback, their losses were comparatively small.
The climate here is comparatively mild, even in winter.
The new software is comparatively easy to use for beginners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relatively - to a certain degree when compared with others
