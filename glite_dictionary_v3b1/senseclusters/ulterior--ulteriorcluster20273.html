ulterior

adjective
happening at a later stage
Pronunciation: / ʌlˈtɪriər / (AmE), / ʌlˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
occurring or coming at a later time or stage; subsequent or future

Examples

Ulterior events
Her plans had no ulterior stages; she acted only on the present situation.
Research has focused on the immediate impact rather than the ulterior consequences.
The court dismissed any ulterior objections that arose after the decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: posterior - coming after in time or order; later
  • antonym: prior - existing or coming before in time, order, or importance
