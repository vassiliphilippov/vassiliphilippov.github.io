damages

noun
money awarded as compensation for loss or injury
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈdæmɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
A sum of money paid as compensation to a person for loss or injury.

Examples

Pay damages
She sued the company for damages after the accident.
The court awarded damages to the family of the victim.
The plaintiff received substantial damages for the breach of contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone as a recompense for loss or injury
  • antonym: penalty - a punishment imposed for breaking a law, rule, or contract
