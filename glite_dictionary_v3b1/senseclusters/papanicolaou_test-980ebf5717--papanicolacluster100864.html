Papanicolaou test

noun
test for early cervical cancer detection
4.4
Pronunciation: /pæp.ə.nɪ.ˈkoʊ.loʊ test/ (AmE), /pæp.ə.nɪ.ˈkəʊ.ləʊ test/ (BrE)
a medical test that involves examining cells taken from the cervix to detect early signs of cervical cancer and other abnormalities

Examples

Papanicolaou test for cancer
Her doctor performed a Papanicolaou test to screen for cervical abnormalities.
Regular Papanicolaou test results can help detect potential issues before they become serious.
The Papanicolaou test is recommended for women every three years as a preventative health measure.
