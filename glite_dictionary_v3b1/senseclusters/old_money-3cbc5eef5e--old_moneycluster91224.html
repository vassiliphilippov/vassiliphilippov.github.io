old money

noun
inherited wealth over generations
Pronunciation: / oʊld ˈmʌni / (AmE), / əʊld ˈmʌni / (BrE)
People or families whose wealth has been inherited over several generations, often associated with long-standing social status and traditional values.

Examples

Old money families
Many influential figures in the city come from old money backgrounds.
She always associated the understated style of old money with true sophistication.
The charity's donors include several members of old money families.

Related Terms

  • antonym: new money - people or families who have recently acquired wealth
