peptidoglycan

noun
bacterial cell wall molecule
9.3
Pronunciation: /pep.tɪ.doʊ.ˈglaɪ.kən/ /; / /pep.tɪ.də.ˈglaɪ.kən/ (AmE), /pep.tɪ.dəʊ.ˈglaɪ.kən/ /; / /pep.tɪ.də.ˈglaɪ.kən/ (BrE)
a complex molecule made of sugars and amino acids that forms the main structure of most bacterial cell walls

Examples

Bacterial peptidoglycan layer
The antibiotic targets the peptidoglycan layer in bacterial cell walls.
Gram-positive bacteria have a thick peptidoglycan layer that stains purple in laboratory tests.
Scientists study peptidoglycan to understand how bacteria build their protective cell walls.
