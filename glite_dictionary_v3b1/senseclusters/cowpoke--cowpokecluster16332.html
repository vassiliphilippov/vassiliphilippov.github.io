cowpoke

noun
cattle worker on horseback (esp. US)
9.1 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkaÊŠËŒpoÊŠk / (AmE), / ËˆkaÊŠËŒpÉ™ÊŠk / (BrE)
a person, especially in the western United States, who is employed to tend cattle and perform ranch duties, often on horseback

Examples

A cowpoke on horseback
At the rodeo, a skilled cowpoke demonstrated his lasso technique.
The cowpoke rode across the open plain, checking on the herd.
The old cowpoke shared stories of life on the ranch.

Synonyms

  • cowpuncher

  • puncher
    8.4 0.04/m
    a puncher is a hired ranch hand who rides to tend cattle on the range, while a cowpoke is a general ranch worker.
  • cowhand
    4.6 0.19/m
    a cowhand does many ranch jobs, sometimes off the horse, while a cowpoke often implies a horse-riding cattle worker.
  • cowman
    5.5 0.03/m
    a cowman is a man who cares for cattle on farms or ranches, while a cowpoke focuses on western, horseback work.
  • cowherd
    6.8 0.08/m
    a cowherd looks after cattle in fields and pastures, often quietly, while a cowpoke suggests active ranch work on horseback.
