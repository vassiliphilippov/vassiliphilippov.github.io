cumulus

adjective
relating to cumulus clouds
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːmjələs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːmjʊləs / (BrE)
of or relating to cumulus clouds, which are dense, white, fluffy clouds with a flat base and rounded outlines, typically seen in fair weather.

Examples

Cumulus formation
Artists often paint landscapes with cumulus clouds to create a sense of calm and clarity.
Satellite images revealed a pattern of cumulus cloud development over the region.
The meteorologist described the cumulus formations drifting across the summer sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cirrus - relating to cirrus clouds, which are thin, wispy clouds at high altitude
  • antonym: stratus - relating to stratus clouds, which are low, gray, and sheet-like
