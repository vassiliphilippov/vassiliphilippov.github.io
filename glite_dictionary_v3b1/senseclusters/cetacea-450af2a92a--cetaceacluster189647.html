Cetacea

noun
order of aquatic mammals (whales, dolphins, porpoises)
Pronunciation: / sɪˈteɪʃiə / (AmE), / sɪˈteɪsɪə / (BrE)
an order of aquatic mammals that includes whales, dolphins, and porpoises

Examples

The Cetacea order
Marine biologists study various species within the Cetacea order to understand aquatic mammal behavior.
Scientists believe that the evolution of Cetacea began around 50 million years ago.
The Cetacea are highly adapted to life in water, with streamlined bodies and flippers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dolphin - aquatic mammal of the family Delphinidae, within Cetacea
  • synonym: porpoise - small toothed whale within Cetacea, distinguished from dolphins by shorter beaks
  • synonym: whale - large marine mammal of the order Cetacea
