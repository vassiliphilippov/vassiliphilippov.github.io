amputation

noun
loss of a limb or part of a limb
Pronunciation: / ˌæmpjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæmpjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a limb or other body part, or the condition of having lost a limb or body part

Examples

Surgical amputation of limb
After the accident, his amputation meant he had to adapt to a prosthetic leg.
Support groups can help people cope with the challenges of life after an amputation.
The patient suffered an amputation below the knee following severe infection.
Advances in prosthetics have greatly improved the quality of life for people following amputation.
After the accident, the doctors had to perform an amputation of his right leg to save his life.
The risk of amputation increases in diabetic patients with untreated foot infections.
