trendline

noun
line showing market direction
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛndˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈtrɛndˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line on a graph or chart that shows the general direction or pattern of data over time

Examples

A trendline on graph
The analyst drew a trendline to help predict future movements in the stock price.
A clear trendline can indicate whether the market is generally rising or falling.
Breaking below the trendline signaled a possible change in the market direction.
The analyst drew a trendline to highlight the steady growth in profits over five years.
By examining the trendline, we can predict future sales more accurately.
The trendline on the graph indicates a gradual decline in customer complaints.
The trendline on the sales chart shows steady growth over the past year.
Analysts drew a trendline to predict future stock prices based on historical data.
If the trendline continues upward, the company will likely expand next quarter.

  • synonym: curve - a line on a graph representing data points
  • synonym: pattern - a repeated or regular way in which something happens
  • synonym: regression line - a statistical line showing the relationship between variables
  • synonym: resistance - a price level where an upward trend may pause due to selling interest
  • synonym: support - a price level where a downward trend may pause due to buying interest
  • synonym: trajectory - the path or direction of something over time
