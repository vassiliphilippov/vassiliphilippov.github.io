freakout

noun
emotional breakdown
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːkaʊt / (AmE), / ˈfriːkaʊt / (BrE)
a sudden and intense emotional reaction, often involving panic, fear, anger, or excitement, and sometimes a loss of control

Examples

Major freakout
After hearing the unexpected news, he had a complete freakout in the office.
Her freakout lasted for hours before she finally calmed down.
The team tried to help their friend through his freakout after the stressful exam.
He had a complete freakout when he realized he had lost his passport at the airport.
The announcement caused a freakout among the students who were worried about their grades.
After seeing the surprise party, she went into a happy freakout and started laughing and shouting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breakdown - a sudden failure of mental or emotional stability
  • antonym: calm - a state of being relaxed and not upset
  • synonym: meltdown - an emotional breakdown or loss of control
  • synonym: panic attack - a sudden episode of intense fear or anxiety
