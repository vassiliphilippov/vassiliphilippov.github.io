tune out

verb
ignore or stop paying attention
4.4 1.17/m
Pronunciation: / tun a ät / (AmE), / tjuÀên a ät / (BrE)
to stop listening to or giving attention to something; to ignore or lose interest in what is happening around you

Examples

To tune out noise
It's important not to tune out when someone is sharing their feelings with you.
Many students tune out during lengthy online classes.
When the meeting dragged on, she started to tune out and think about lunch.

Synonyms

  • ignore
    1.0 118/m
    ignore is to choose not to pay attention to someone, while tune out means losing interest and stopping listening.
  • turn a deaf ear
    6.1 0.22/m
    turn a deaf ear is to refuse to listen to someone on purpose, while tune out often means drifting away and losing interest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone
  • antonym: listen - to pay attention to sound or to what someone is saying
  • synonym: zone out - to lose concentration or awareness of your surroundings
