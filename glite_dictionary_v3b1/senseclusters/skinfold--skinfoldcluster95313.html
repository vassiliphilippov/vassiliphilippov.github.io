skinfold

noun
fold of skin
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈskɪnˌfəʊld / (BrE)
a fold of skin (sometimes with underlying fat) on the body, often measured to estimate body fat

Examples

The skinfold on arm
The doctor measured the skinfold on the patient's arm to assess body composition.
A small skinfold developed at the base of the puppy's neck as it grew.
He noticed a skinfold forming behind his knee after sitting for a long time.
The nutritionist used calipers to measure the skinfold on my abdomen.
Regular skinfold measurements can help track changes in body fat over time.
He learned how to properly pinch a skinfold for accurate assessment during his training course.

Related Terms

  • synonym: body fat - the total amount of fat in a person's body
  • synonym: crease - a line or ridge produced on paper, cloth, or skin by folding or pressing
  • synonym: subcutaneous fat - fat stored directly under the skin
  • synonym: wrinkle - a small line or fold in the skin, often due to aging
