Carcharhinus

noun
genus of requiem sharks
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrkəˈraɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌkɑːkəˈraɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of requiem sharks, which includes many common shark species such as the bull shark and blacktip shark.

Examples

The Carcharhinus shark
Many species within Carcharhinus are commonly found in warm, coastal waters around the world.
Scientists studied the migration patterns of several Carcharhinus species in the Pacific Ocean.
The bull shark belongs to the genus Carcharhinus and is known for its aggressive behavior.
