alembic

noun
obsolete distillation apparatus
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛmbɪk / (AmE), / əˈlɛmbɪk / (BrE)
an obsolete distillation apparatus consisting of two vessels connected by a tube, formerly used in chemistry and alchemy

Examples

An old alembic device
During the Middle Ages, the alembic was a vital tool in alchemical laboratories.
The chemist carefully poured the liquid into the alembic to begin the distillation process.
The museum displayed a collection of ancient alembics and other scientific instruments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retort - a type of vessel used in distillation
