stuck

adjective
unable to move; caught or trapped
Pronunciation: / stʌk / (AmE), / stʌk / (BrE)
unable to move from a particular position or place, typically because something is holding or trapping one there.

Examples

Stuck in traffic
He reached for the jar, but the lid was stuck tight.
Her car got stuck in the snow and she couldn't move it.
I'm stuck in traffic, so I'll be late to the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - able to move or go as one wishes; not stuck or restrained
  • synonym: trapped - unable to escape from a place or situation
