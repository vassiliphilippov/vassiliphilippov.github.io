stuck

adjective
unable to move; caught or trapped
1.0 28/m
Pronunciation: / stÊŒk / (AmE), / stÊŒk / (BrE)
unable to move from a particular position or place, typically because something is holding or trapping one there.

Examples

Stuck in traffic
He reached for the jar, but the lid was stuck tight.
Her car got stuck in the snow and she couldn't move it.
I'm stuck in traffic, so I'll be late to the meeting.

Synonyms

  • trapped
    4.4 0.02/m
    trapped is unable to escape from a confining place or danger, while stuck is held fast by something and cannot move.
  • immobile
    4.4 1.87/m
    immobile means fixed and not able to be moved, while stuck usually means held by something and cannot move.
  • mired
    7.8 0.05/m
    mired describes being bogged down in a difficult situation, while stuck is often a physical inability to move.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - able to move or go as one wishes; not stuck or restrained
  • synonym: trapped - unable to escape from a place or situation
