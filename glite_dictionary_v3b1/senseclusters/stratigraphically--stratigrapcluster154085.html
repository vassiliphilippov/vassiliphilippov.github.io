stratigraphically

adverb
by rock layers
Pronunciation: / ˌstræt̬ɪˈɡræfɪkli / (AmE), / ˌstrætɪˈɡræfɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the arrangement or analysis of layers of rock or soil

Examples

Examined stratigraphically
The site was examined stratigraphically to understand the sequence of historical events.
Archaeologists recorded the artifacts stratigraphically to preserve information about their original positions.
The layers were dated stratigraphically to establish a timeline for the region's geological history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geologically - in a way that relates to the science of the Earth's structure
