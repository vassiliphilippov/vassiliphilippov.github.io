coffle

verb
to chain in a line
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːfəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒfəl / (BrE)
to tie or chain people or animals together in a line and lead or drive them from one place to another

Examples

To coffle animals
Traders would coffle camels together before leading them across the arid plains.
In the past, slave drivers would coffle people for forced marches over long distances.
The herders coffled the goats to keep them from wandering during the journey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shackle - to restrain with chains or cuffs
  • antonym: unbind - to release from bonds or chains
