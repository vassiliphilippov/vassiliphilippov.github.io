drapability

noun
fabric's flowing quality
7.5
Pronunciation: /dreɪ.pə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /dreɪ.pə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /dreɪ.pə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /dreɪ.pə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality of a fabric or garment that allows it to hang or flow smoothly and form graceful folds

Examples

The drapability of fabric
The drapability of silk makes it a popular choice for elegant evening gowns.
Designers test the drapability of new fabrics before using them in their collections.
A fabric with poor drapability may look stiff and unflattering when worn.

Cotton blends are popular because their drapability allows for comfortable and stylish clothing.
