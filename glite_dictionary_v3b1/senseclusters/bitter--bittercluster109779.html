bitter

noun
harsh, unpleasant taste
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬ər /; / ˈbɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə / (BrE)
a sharp, unpleasant taste that is not sweet, as found in some foods or drinks

Examples

A strong bitter
He could not drink the medicine because of its strong bitter.
Some people enjoy the bitter found in dark chocolate.
The bitter of the grapefruit was too intense for some people.
Some people enjoy the bitter of dark chocolate, while others find it too intense.
The coffee's bitter complemented the sweetness of the dessert.
Tonic water has a distinct bitter that comes from quinine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: salty - tasting of or containing salt
  • antonym: sour - having an acidic taste like lemon or vinegar
  • degree lower: sourness - the property of having a sour taste
  • antonym: sweet - having a pleasantly sugary taste
  • antonym: sweetness - the property of having a sweet taste
