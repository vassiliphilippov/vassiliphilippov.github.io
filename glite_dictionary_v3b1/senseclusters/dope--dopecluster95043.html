dope

verb
add impurities to a semiconductor
8.6
Pronunciation: / doʊp /; / ˈdoʊpɪŋ / (AmE), / dəʊp /; / ˈdəʊpɪŋ / (BrE)
to add a small amount of another element or impurity to a material (such as a semiconductor) to change its properties

Examples

To dope silicon
Adding boron is a common way to dope silicon wafers in the industry.
Modern microchips rely on precisely doped semiconductors for their function.
To improve conductivity, manufacturers often dope silicon with small amounts of phosphorus.
The engineers improved the semiconductor by doping it with phosphorus atoms.
Careful doping of materials is essential in the production of modern electronic devices.
They achieved the desired conductivity by doping the crystal with a trace amount of arsenic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impregnate - to fill or saturate a material with another substance
  • antonym: purify - to remove impurities from a material
