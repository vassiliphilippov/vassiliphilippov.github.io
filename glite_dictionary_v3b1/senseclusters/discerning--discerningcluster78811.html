discerning

adjective
showing insight and good judgment
5.5 5.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsɜːr.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsɜː.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
having or showing good judgment and the ability to notice or understand things, especially subtle details

Examples

A discerning shopper
She is a discerning shopper who always chooses quality over quantity.
The critic was known for his discerning eye when evaluating new plays.
With a discerning ear, he quickly identified the subtle differences between the two recordings.
Only the most discerning customers will notice the craftsmanship in these details.
She is a discerning reader who can recognize literary quality in any genre.
The manager made a discerning choice when hiring new employees.
A discerning reader will appreciate the subtle themes woven into the novel's narrative.
She has a discerning eye for quality artwork and always notices the smallest details.
The restaurant is popular with discerning diners who value fresh ingredients and creative cuisine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perceptive - quick to notice or understand things
  • antonym: unimaginative - lacking creativity or insight; not showing good judgment
  • antonym: unperceptive - lacking ability to notice or understand things quickly
