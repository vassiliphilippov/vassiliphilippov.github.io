germinal area

noun
cell layer inside blastula
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɝːmɪnəl ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmɪnəl ˈɛəriə / (BrE)
a layer of cells on the inside of the blastula in early embryonic development, from which tissues originate

Examples

The germinal area layer
Damage to the germinal area can disrupt normal cell differentiation.
During early embryonic development, the germinal area gives rise to essential tissue layers.
Scientists study the germinal area to understand how organs begin to form in embryos.
