Hobbesian

adjective
favoring strong authority
9.5
Pronunciation: / hɑːbˈziːən /; / hɑbˈziːən / (AmE), / hɒbˈziːən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas or philosophy of Thomas Hobbes, especially the belief that human nature is self-interested and that strong authority is needed to prevent chaos

Examples

Hobbesian philosophy
The leader's Hobbesian approach emphasized the need for strict laws to prevent chaos.
Some critics argue that a Hobbesian society sacrifices too much personal freedom for security.
Her essay compared Hobbesian and democratic theories of government.
After the government collapsed, the city fell into a Hobbesian state where everyone fought for survival.
The novel depicts a Hobbesian world where trust between people has completely broken down.
Some critics argue that the internet can become a Hobbesian environment without proper rules.
The novel presents a Hobbesian world where everyone competes for survival.
Some political theorists argue that international relations are fundamentally Hobbesian in nature.
Her view of human behavior is often described as Hobbesian, focusing on self-interest and conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Machiavellian - cunning, scheming, and unscrupulous, especially in politics
  • synonym: anarchic - lacking order or control; chaotic
  • synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to authority
  • antonym: libertarian - favoring minimal government and maximum individual freedom
  • antonym: orderly - organized and controlled; not chaotic
  • antonym: utopian - idealistic or aiming for a perfect society
