rondeau

noun
a French poetic verse form with repeated refrain
7.1 9.9
Pronunciation: / rɒnˈdoʊ / (AmE), / rɒnˈdəʊ / (BrE)
a French verse form of 10 or 13 lines with two rhymes, in which the opening phrase is repeated as the refrain of the second and third stanzas

Examples

French rondeau poem
Her favorite form was the rondeau, which allowed her to play with repetition and rhyme.
In literature class, we studied the structure of a rondeau and wrote our own versions.
The poet composed a rondeau to capture the cyclical nature of the seasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ballade - a form of verse, often narrative, set to music
  • synonym: villanelle - a 19-line poetic form with two rhymes and repeating lines
