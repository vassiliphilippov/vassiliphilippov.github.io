divergent strabismus

noun
outward-turning eye misalignment
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɝdʒənt strəˈbɪzməs / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːdʒənt strəˈbɪzməs / (BrE)
A form of strabismus in which one or both eyes turn outward away from the nose.

Examples

Divergent strabismus eye turn
Early intervention can improve outcomes for patients with divergent strabismus.
The doctor diagnosed the child with divergent strabismus after noticing her right eye turning outward.
Treatment options for divergent strabismus may include corrective lenses, vision therapy, or surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convergent strabismus - strabismus in which one or both eyes are directed inward
  • synonym: exotropia - strabismus with outward deviation of an eye
