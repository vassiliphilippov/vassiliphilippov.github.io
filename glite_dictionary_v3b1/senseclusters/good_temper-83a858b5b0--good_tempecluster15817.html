good temper

noun
cheerful and agreeable mood
Pronunciation: / ɡʊd ˈtɛmpɚ / (AmE), / ɡʊd ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
a cheerful and agreeable mood or disposition; the quality of being good-natured and pleasant

Examples

Good temper during delays
Despite the delays, the group remained in good temper throughout the trip.
His good temper makes him popular with colleagues.
She is always in a good temper even when things go wrong.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bad temper - an irritable or easily angered mood
