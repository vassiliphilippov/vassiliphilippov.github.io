overseason

verb
to treat too long
8.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàsiÀêz…ôn / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàsiÀêz…ôn / (BrE)
to dry, cure, age, or treat materials such as wood, meat, or cookware for too long, causing damage or unwanted changes

Examples

To overseason wood
If you overseason the wood, it may become brittle and crack during use.
The chef warned not to overseason the cast iron pan, as it could develop a sticky surface.
They accidentally overseasoned the meat, making it tough and dry after cooking.
