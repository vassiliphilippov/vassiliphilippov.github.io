protoplast

noun
cell contents including nucleus and cytoplasm
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊtəˌplæst / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəʊˌplɑːst / (BrE)
the contents of a cell, consisting of the nucleus and the surrounding cytoplasm, regarded as a unit

Examples

A protoplast cell
Researchers observed the fusion of two protoplasts under a microscope.
The scientist isolated the protoplast from the plant cell to study its functions.
When the cell wall is removed, the protoplast is left exposed.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cytoplasm - the material within a cell, excluding the nucleus
  • degree lower: nucleus - the central and most important part of a cell, containing genetic material
