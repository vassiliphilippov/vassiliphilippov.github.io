blowfly

noun
large metallic fly laying eggs in carrion or wounds
Pronunciation: / ˈbloʊˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a large usually metallic blue or green fly that lays its eggs in carrion, dung, or wounds

Examples

A blowfly buzzing
A blowfly was buzzing around the compost bin in the backyard.
Forensic scientists studied the stages of blowfly larvae development to estimate the time of death.
The wound became infected after a blowfly laid eggs in it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluebottle - a common type of blowfly with blue metallic coloring
