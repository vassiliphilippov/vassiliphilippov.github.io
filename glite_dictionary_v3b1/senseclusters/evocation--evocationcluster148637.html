evocation

noun
imaginative re-creation
Pronunciation: / ˌiː.voʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌiː.vəˈkeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌiː.vəʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing something to mind or recreating it in the imagination, especially a feeling, memory, or idea

Examples

A powerful evocation of memories
Her paintings are an evocation of childhood memories and lost innocence.
The film’s evocation of the 1960s is both nostalgic and deeply moving.
The novel is a brilliant evocation of post-war Paris.
His speech was noted for its evocation of patriotism among the crowd.
The evocation of childhood memories brought tears to her eyes.
The therapist used music for the evocation of emotional responses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conjuring - the act of calling something to mind or into existence
  • synonym: elicitation - the act of drawing out or bringing forth something, such as information or a response
  • synonym: invocation - the act of calling upon something, often a deity, for inspiration or help
