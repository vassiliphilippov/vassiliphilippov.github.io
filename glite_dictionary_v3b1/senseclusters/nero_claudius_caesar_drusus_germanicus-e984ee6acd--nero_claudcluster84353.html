Nero Claudius Caesar Drusus Germanicus

proper noun
notorious Roman Emperor (37–68 CE)
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪəroʊ ˈklaʊdiəs ˈsiːzər ˈdruːsəs dʒərˈmænɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈnɪərəʊ ˈklaʊdɪəs ˈsiːzə ˈdruːsəs dʒɜːˈmænɪkəs / (BrE)
(37–68 CE) Roman Emperor, known for extravagant excesses, alleged cruelty, and for being blamed for the Great Fire of Rome; his rule is also marked by continued prosperity in the empire.

Examples

Nero Claudius Caesar Drusus Germanicus Roman Emperor
Many historians debate whether Nero Claudius Caesar Drusus Germanicus was truly responsible for the Great Fire of Rome.
Suetonius wrote several accounts of the excesses of Nero Claudius Caesar Drusus Germanicus during his time as emperor.
The reign of Nero Claudius Caesar Drusus Germanicus is often remembered for its extravagance and turmoil.
