water parting

noun
land ridge dividing river systems
9.8 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər ˈpɑːr.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər ˈpɑːr.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə ˈpɑː.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈwɒ.tə ˈpɑː.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
A ridge of land that separates the drainage basins of two adjacent river systems; also called a watershed or divide.

Examples

A water parting ridge

Heavy rainfall on either side of the water parting drains into different seas.
The Continental Divide is a famous water parting in North America.
