carbon black

noun
fine black carbon powder used as pigment
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən blæk / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən blæk / (BrE)
A fine black powder made almost entirely of pure carbon, used as a pigment and in the manufacture of inks, paints, and rubber products.

Examples

Carbon black pigment
Printers often use inks based on carbon black for sharp, dark images.
Research showed that inhaling carbon black particles can pose health risks in industrial settings.
The manufacturer uses carbon black to pigment their high-quality tires.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lampblack - a black pigment made from soot, similar to carbon black
