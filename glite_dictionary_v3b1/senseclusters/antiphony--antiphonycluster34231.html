antiphony

noun
alternate choral singing
6.1 8.8
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪfəni / (AmE), / ænˈtɪfəni / (BrE)
a style of singing or a chant in which two groups or parts sing or respond to each other in alternation, especially in religious music

Examples

Antiphony in music
Medieval compositions often feature antiphony between male and female choristers.
The cathedral choir employed antiphony to create a beautiful, echoing effect during the service.
The performance was unique in its use of antiphony, heightening the drama of the music.
Medieval liturgical music often utilizes antiphony to create a sense of dialogue between singers.
The choir performed an antiphony during the evening service, with voices alternating from each side of the chapel.
The haunting antiphony echoed through the cathedral, captivating everyone present.
