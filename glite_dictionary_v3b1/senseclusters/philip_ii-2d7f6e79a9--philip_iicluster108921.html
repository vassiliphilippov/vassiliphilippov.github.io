Philip II

proper noun
King of France (1165–1223)
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlɪp ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(1165–1223) King of France, son of Louis VII, whose reign was marked by wars with England, resulting in the recovery of Normandy, Anjou, and most of Poitou.

Examples

Philip II of France
Philip II expanded the French royal domain more than any of his predecessors.
Many historians credit Philip II with laying the foundations of the modern French state.
The victory at the Battle of Bouvines in 1214 secured Philip II's power in France.
