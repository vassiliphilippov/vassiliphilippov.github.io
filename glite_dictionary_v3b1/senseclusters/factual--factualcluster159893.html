factual

adjective
relating to facts
Pronunciation: / ˈfæktʃuəl /; / ˈfæk.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈfæktʃuəl /; / ˈfæk.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
based on facts or reality, not opinions or imagination

Examples

A factual report
Her report was praised for being clear and factual, without personal bias.
Please separate factual information from your opinions in your essay.
The documentary provided a factual account of the events.
She insisted that her report was strictly factual and free from personal opinions.
The documentary provided a factual account of the historical events.
We must base our conclusions on factual evidence, not on guesswork.

  • antonym: fictional - relating to fiction; invented or imagined
