at a loss

adjective
confused; unsure what to do
Pronunciation: / æt ə lɔs /; / ət ə lɔs / (AmE), / ət ə lɒs / (BrE)
not knowing what to do or say; feeling confused or uncertain

Examples

Feel at a loss
During the difficult meeting, he felt completely at a loss.
I was at a loss to explain the sudden change in her attitude.
She was at a loss for words when she heard the surprising news.
He stood at a loss in the middle of the busy street, unsure where to go.
She seemed at a loss after receiving the strange letter.
When the teacher asked an unexpected question, I was at a loss for words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bewildered - feeling extremely confused or puzzled
  • antonym: certain - sure and confident
  • synonym: confused - unable to think clearly or to understand something
