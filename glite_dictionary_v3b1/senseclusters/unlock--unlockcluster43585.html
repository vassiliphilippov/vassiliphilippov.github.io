unlock

verb
open or release by removing a lock
7.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɒk / (BrE)
to make something that is locked become open or accessible, usually by using a key, code, or similar method

Examples

To unlock the door
I always unlock the front door before my family arrives home.
She forgot to unlock her bike, so she had to wait for help.
The manager will unlock the office at nine o'clock sharp.
The phone unlocked after I entered the correct passcode.
When the timer reached zero, the vault unlocked automatically.
With a quiet click, the door unlocked in the middle of the night.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: open - to make or become no longer closed
