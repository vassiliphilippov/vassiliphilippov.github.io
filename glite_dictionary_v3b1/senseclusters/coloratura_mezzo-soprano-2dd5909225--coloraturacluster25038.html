coloratura mezzo-soprano

noun
mezzo-soprano with agility and wide range
Pronunciation: / ˌkʌl.əˈrɑː.tʃər.ə ˌmɛt.soʊ səˈprɑː.noʊ / (AmE), / ˌkɒl.ər.əˈtjʊə.rə ˌmɛt.səʊ səˈprɑː.nəʊ / (BrE)
A mezzo-soprano singer with the ability to perform elaborate ornamentation and rapid passages, typically with a range from about G3 to B5.

Examples

A coloratura mezzo-soprano singer
As a coloratura mezzo-soprano, she effortlessly navigated complicated passages that challenged even experienced singers.
Many operatic roles written for coloratura mezzo-sopranos require both vocal agility and expressive power.
She was renowned as a coloratura mezzo-soprano with remarkable precision in her rapid runs and trills.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: coloratura soprano - a soprano singer capable of elaborate ornamentation and high range
  • degree lower: mezzo-soprano - a female singing voice with a range between soprano and contralto
