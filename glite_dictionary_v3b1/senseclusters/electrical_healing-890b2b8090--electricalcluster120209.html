electrical healing

noun
therapy using electricity for healing
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl ˈhiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪkəl ˈhiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of electrical currents applied to the body for therapeutic purposes, especially in medical treatment of conditions such as paralysis

Examples

Electrical healing therapy
Modern physical therapy sometimes incorporates techniques reminiscent of electrical healing.
Research into electrical healing has led to new understandings of how nerves respond to stimulation.
Some doctors in the 19th century promoted electrical healing as a cure for nervous disorders.
