saltgrass

noun
salt-tolerant grass
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔltˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a type of grass that grows in salty or alkaline soils, especially in coastal or marshy areas

Examples

Saltgrass in wetlands
The coastal wetland was covered with thick patches of saltgrass that thrived in the salty soil.
Birds built their nests among the dense saltgrass along the edge of the marsh.
After the tide receded, the resilient saltgrass remained green and healthy.
The rancher let his cattle graze on the saltgrass that thrived in the coastal fields.
Restoration projects often use saltgrass to stabilize soil in areas with high salinity.
Wildlife such as birds and insects depend on saltgrass habitats near salt marshes.
The coastal plain was dotted with patches of saltgrass swaying in the breeze.
Birds built their nests among the thick saltgrass near the shoreline.
After the storm, the saltgrass helped prevent erosion along the marsh banks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cordgrass - a grass found in salt marshes, especially of the genus Spartina
  • synonym: halophyte - a plant adapted to grow in salty soil
  • synonym: marsh grass - any grass that grows in wet, marshy areas
