pharyngealization

noun
speech sound modification
Pronunciation: / fəˌrɪndʒiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˌrɪndʒiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a speech sound modification produced by constricting the pharynx during articulation

Examples

Pharyngealization of consonants
In some languages, pharyngealization distinguishes different consonant sounds.
The teacher explained how pharyngealization affects the pronunciation of certain Arabic letters.
Linguists study pharyngealization to understand how speech sounds are produced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: palatalization - modification of a speech sound by moving the tongue toward the hard palate
  • synonym: velarization - modification of a speech sound by raising the back of the tongue toward the velum
