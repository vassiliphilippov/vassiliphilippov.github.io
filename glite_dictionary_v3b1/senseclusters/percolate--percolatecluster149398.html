percolate

verb
spread gradually through
9.0 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpɜːr.kə.leɪt/ /; / /ˈpɜːr.kjə.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈpɜː.kə.leɪt/ /; / /ˈpɜː.kjə.leɪt/ (BrE)
for an idea, feeling, or information to spread gradually through a group or area; to become known little by little

Examples

News percolates through town
A sense of excitement percolated among the students as graduation day approached.
Rumors about the policy change began to percolate through the office on Monday.
The light from the hallway slowly percolated into the dark room.
It took a while for the news to percolate through the company.
Rainwater can percolate slowly through layers of soil and rock.
The aroma of freshly baked bread percolated throughout the house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diffuse - to spread over a wide area or among many people
  • synonym: filter - to pass or leak slowly through something (literal sense)
  • synonym: penetrate - to go into or through something
  • degree higher: saturate - to cause something to become thoroughly soaked
  • synonym: seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material
