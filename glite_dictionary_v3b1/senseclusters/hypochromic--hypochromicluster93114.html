hypochromic

adjective
reduced light absorption
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈkroʊmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈkrəʊmɪk / (BrE)
showing a decrease in the intensity of light absorption compared to a reference, especially in spectroscopy or chemistry

Examples

Hypochromic effect
The scientist observed a hypochromic effect when the sample was exposed to the new reagent.
A hypochromic shift in the absorption spectrum indicated a structural change in the molecule.
After the chemical reaction, the solution became hypochromic compared to the original sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperchromic - showing an increase in the intensity of light absorption
