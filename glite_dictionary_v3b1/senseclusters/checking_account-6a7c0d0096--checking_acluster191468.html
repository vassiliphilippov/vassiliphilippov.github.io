checking account

noun
bank account for writing checks; funds payable on demand
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkɪŋ əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkɪŋ əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
a bank account from which the account holder can withdraw money by writing checks or using a debit card, with funds available for withdrawal at any time

Examples

A checking account balance
He noticed a suspicious charge on his checking account statement last week.
Most bills are paid directly out of my checking account each month.
She opened a checking account to manage her daily expenses more easily.
Most employers deposit salaries directly into employees’ chequing accounts.
She opened a new chequing account to manage household expenses.
You can pay your rent directly from your chequing account each month.

Related Terms

  • synonym: checking account - A bank account against which checks can be drawn; primarily American English.
  • synonym: current account - a bank account from which money can be withdrawn without notice, primarily UK term
  • antonym: savings account - A bank account intended for saving money, typically earning interest.
