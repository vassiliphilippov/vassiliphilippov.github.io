preemergence

adjective
before plant emergence
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪˈmɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪˈmɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
applied or occurring before the emergence of plants or seedlings, especially to prevent weed growth

Examples

Preemergence herbicide
Farmers often use preemergence herbicides to control weeds before crops sprout.
A preemergence application can help ensure a cleaner field during the growing season.
The gardener recommended a preemergence treatment to prevent unwanted plants from appearing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postemergence - applied or occurring after the emergence of plants or seedlings
