Vietnam

noun
war in Vietnam (1954–1975); the Vietnam War
3.6
Pronunciation: /viː.et.ˈnɑːm/ /; / /vjet.ˈnæm/ /; / /vɪ.et.ˈnæm/ (AmE), /vjet.ˈnɑːm/ /; / /viː.ə.ˈnɑːm/ (BrE)
the prolonged conflict from 1954 to 1975 between North Vietnam (supported by China and other communist allies) and South Vietnam (supported by the United States and other non-communist allies), commonly known as the Vietnam War.

Examples

The Vietnam War
His father served in Vietnam and rarely spoke about his experiences.
Many young Americans protested against the Vietnam war during the late 1960s.
The legacy of Vietnam has influenced U.S. foreign policy for decades.
