quietist

noun
follower of quietism; religious contemplative
6.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪətɪst / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪətɪst / (BrE)
A person who believes in or practices quietism, especially in religious mysticism, emphasizing inner stillness and passive contemplation.

Examples

A quietist believer
A true quietist seeks inner peace through surrender of the will.
During the 17th century, Madrid became a center for quietist thought.
The quietist withdrew from worldly affairs to devote herself fully to contemplation and prayer.
