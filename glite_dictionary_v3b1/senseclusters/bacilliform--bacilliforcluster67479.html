bacilliform

adjective
rod-shaped; like a bacillus
9.9
Pronunciation: / bəˈsɪl.ɪ.fɔrm / (AmE), / bəˈsɪl.ɪ.fɔːm / (BrE)
Shaped like a bacillus; rod-shaped, especially when referring to bacteria or microscopic structures.

Examples

bacilliform bacteria shape
Many bacteria exhibit a bacilliform appearance under the microscope.
Researchers identified a bacilliform structure in the new microorganism.
The bacilliform virus infected the plant tissue, causing it to wilt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coccus - spherical bacterium or structure
  • antonym: spirillum - spiral-shaped bacterium
