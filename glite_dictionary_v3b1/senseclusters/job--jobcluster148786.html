job

noun
work or position to earn money
1.0 562/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’É‘b / (AmE), / dÊ’É’b / (BrE)
a regular position or work that you do to earn money; employment or workplace

Examples

A full-time job
He enjoys his job because it allows him to help others.
Many people lost their jobs during the economic downturn.
Sarah found a new job at the hospital last month.
He was injured on the job while operating heavy machinery.
She met her best friend at her job and they have worked together for years.
There was a safety drill at the job yesterday afternoon.

Synonyms

  • occupation
    2.7 15/m
    occupation often names a whole profession used in formal contexts, while job refers to a specific position you do for pay.
  • line of work
    3.1 2.86/m
    line of work is an informal phrase for the general area or type of work someone does, while job means a particular position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occupation - a person's usual or principal work or business
  • antonym: unemployment - the state of not having a job
