deductively

adverb
using general principles
6.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʌktɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that uses general principles or rules to reach specific conclusions

Examples

To deductively reason
The mathematician solved the problem deductively, starting from known laws and working toward a solution.
Arguments made deductively are often considered more reliable in scientific research.
She reasoned deductively to determine the cause of the error in the experiment.
