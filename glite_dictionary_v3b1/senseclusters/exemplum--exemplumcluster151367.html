exemplum

noun
model or example
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛmpləm / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmpləm / (BrE)
a person, thing, or story that serves as a model or example to be imitated or learned from

Examples

A model exemplum
The teacher used an ancient exemplum to illustrate the importance of honesty to her students.
His bravery in the face of danger became an exemplum for others in the community.
Writers in the Middle Ages often included an exemplum in their sermons to teach moral lessons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: counterexample - an instance that disproves a rule or proposition
  • synonym: example - a person or thing regarded as typical or worthy of imitation
  • synonym: model - a person or thing considered as a standard for imitation
