witting

adjective
aware or knowing; conscious
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
knowing about something and doing it deliberately or consciously

Examples

A witting accomplice
He was a witting accomplice to the crime, fully aware of what he was doing.
She gave her approval as a witting decision, not by accident.
The report revealed the existence of a witting participant in the secret operation.
His witting involvement in the fraud was discovered during the investigation.
She made a witting decision to share the confidential information with her supervisor.
There is a clear difference between witting and unwitting participation in the scheme.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • antonym: unwitting - not aware or conscious; unintentional
