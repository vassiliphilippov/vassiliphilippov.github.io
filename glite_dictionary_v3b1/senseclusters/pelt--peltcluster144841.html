pelt

verb
throw objects repeatedly at
7.1
Pronunciation: / pɛlt / (AmE), / pɛlt / (BrE)
to throw things at someone or something repeatedly, or to attack or bombard repeatedly

Examples

To pelt someone with stones
Angry fans pelted the football field with bottles and cans after the match.
Every winter, children pelt each other with snowballs in the park.
Protesters began to pelt the police with stones during the demonstration.
After the announcement, the crowd began to pelt the stage with tomatoes.
Children pelted each other with snowballs on their way home.
Reporters pelted the politician with questions as he left the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrange - to subject to a rapid outpouring, such as of questions
  • synonym: bombard - to attack persistently with bombs or other objects
  • synonym: shower - to fall or throw in large quantities
