global shift

noun
major worldwide change in approach or policy
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbəl ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbəl ʃɪft / (BrE)
a major change in approach, policy, or conditions that occurs on a worldwide scale

Examples

A global shift in policy
Experts agree that climate change is causing a global shift in agriculture and food production.
In response to recent economic crises, there has been a global shift towards renewable energy sources.
The invention of the internet marked a global shift in the way people communicate.

  • synonym: paradigm shift - a fundamental change in underlying assumptions or methodology
