supercycle

noun
long-term cycle
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.pər.saɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈsuː.pə.saɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
a very long-term cycle in natural phenomena, such as solar activity or geological processes

Examples

Solar supercycle
Scientists believe the sun is currently in a supercycle of increased activity.
The concept of a supercycle helps explain unusual patterns in solar radiation over centuries.
Researchers are analyzing historical data to identify the start and end of each supercycle.
Scientists believe the current supercycle of tectonic activity has lasted over 200 million years.
The concept of a supercycle helps geologists explain patterns in ancient sea level changes.
Each supercycle is marked by distinct phases of sediment deposition and erosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epoch - a long period of time marked by significant events or changes
  • synonym: period - a length or portion of time
