flat

adjective
dull, tasteless (of food or drink)
2.2
Pronunciation: / flæt / (AmE), / flæt / (BrE)
dull, lacking liveliness, interest, or variation (such as in taste, sound, performance, color, or business)

Examples

Flat soda
After a few days, the soda became flat and lost its fizz.
The soup tasted flat, so I added more salt and herbs.
This wine has gone flat and lacks its usual vibrant flavor.
After all the hype, the movie seemed unexpectedly flat and disappointing.
Her presentation was rather flat, lacking energy and enthusiasm.
The comedian's last routine fell flat and failed to amuse the crowd.
He spoke in a flat tone that revealed no emotion.
Her voice sounded flat as she read the list to the class.
The teacher's flat delivery made the lecture rather dull.
