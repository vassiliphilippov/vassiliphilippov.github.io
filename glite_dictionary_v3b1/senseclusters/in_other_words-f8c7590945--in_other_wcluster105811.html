in other words

adverb
to express something differently or more clearly
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈʌðɚ wɝdz /; / ɪn ˈʌðɚ wɝːdz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈʌðə wɜːdz / (BrE)
used to introduce a different way of saying something, often to clarify or explain it more clearly

Examples

In other words explained
He was let go from his job—in other words, he was fired.
She found the task challenging; in other words, it was very difficult for her.
The project is over budget; in other words, we spent more than we planned.
She stopped responding to calls; in other words, she wanted to end the conversation.
The instructions were unclear; in other words, nobody knew what to do.
You must pay the full amount in advance—in other words, no installments are allowed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: namely - used to introduce more exact information
  • synonym: that is to say - used to explain or clarify something just mentioned
