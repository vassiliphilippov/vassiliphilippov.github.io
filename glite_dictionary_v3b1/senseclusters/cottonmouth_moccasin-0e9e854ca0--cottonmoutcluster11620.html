cottonmouth moccasin

noun
venomous snake of southern U.S. swamps
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːtnˌmaʊθ ˈmɑːkəsɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtnˌmaʊθ ˈmɒkəsɪn / (BrE)
a venomous semiaquatic snake (Agkistrodon piscivorus) found in swamps and waterways of the southern United States, known for the white coloration inside its mouth and its potent bite

Examples

A cottonmouth moccasin snake
Many locals warn swimmers about the presence of cottonmouth moccasins in the swamp.
The bite of a cottonmouth moccasin requires immediate medical attention due to its venom.
We spotted a cottonmouth moccasin basking on a log by the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: water moccasin - a common name for the same snake, Agkistrodon piscivorus
