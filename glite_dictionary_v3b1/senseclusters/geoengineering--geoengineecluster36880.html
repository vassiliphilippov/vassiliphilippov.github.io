geoengineering

noun
environmental modification technology
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒioʊˌɛnʤəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˌɛnʤɪˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the deliberate use of technology or scientific methods to change the Earth's climate or environment, especially to counteract climate change

Examples

Geoengineering to cool Earth
Some scientists believe geoengineering could help slow global warming.
Debates about the risks and benefits of geoengineering are becoming more common in environmental policy discussions.
Large-scale geoengineering projects might include reflecting sunlight or removing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.
Many scientists are researching geoengineering as a possible solution to global warming.
The conference included a panel on the risks and benefits of geoengineering projects.
Some experts warn that large-scale geoengineering could have unexpected environmental consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: climate engineering - the use of technology to deliberately alter the climate
  • synonym: environmental engineering - the branch of engineering focused on protecting the environment
  • synonym: mitigation - actions to reduce the severity of climate change
