easting

noun
eastward map distance
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈiːstɪŋ / (BrE)
a measurement of distance eastward from a reference point, especially used in map coordinates

Examples

Map easting value
To locate the campsite, you need both the easting and northing values from the map grid.
The easting increases as you move further to the right on the map.
Surveyors recorded the easting to determine the exact position of the new building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: northing - a measurement of distance northward from a reference point on a map
