prestudy

verb
to study in advance
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈstʌdi / (BrE)
to study or review material in advance in order to prepare for a course, lesson, or task

Examples

To prestudy the lesson
It is helpful to prestudy the main concepts before attending the lecture.
Students who prestudy often feel more confident during class discussions.
He decided to prestudy the vocabulary list to improve his understanding of the reading.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preview - to look at or review something before it is used or shown
  • synonym: review - to go over material again to prepare or remember
