scoff

verb
mock or laugh at with scorn
4.2 5.3
Pronunciation: /skɔːf/ (AmE), /skɒf/ (BrE)
to speak or laugh at someone or something in a scornful or mocking way; to show contempt or ridicule

Examples

To scoff at ideas
He scoffed when I told him about my new business plan.
Many people scoffed at her dreams, but she proved them wrong.
The critics scoffed at the idea of launching an electric car company.
He scoffed when I suggested they might win the championship this year.
Many critics scoff at the idea that this technology will change the world.
She tends to scoff at traditions she doesn't understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flout - to openly disregard or show contempt for something
  • synonym: jeer - to make rude and mocking remarks, typically in a loud voice
  • synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule someone or something
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something
