scoff

verb
mock or laugh at with scorn
6.5 4.09/m
Pronunciation: / skÉ‘f / (AmE), / skÉ’f / (BrE)
to speak or laugh at someone or something in a scornful or mocking way; to show contempt or ridicule

Examples

To scoff at ideas
He scoffed when I told him about my new business plan.
Many people scoffed at her dreams, but she proved them wrong.
The critics scoffed at the idea of launching an electric car company.
He scoffed when I suggested they might win the championship this year.
Many critics scoff at the idea that this technology will change the world.
She tends to scoff at traditions she doesn't understand.

Synonyms

  • ridicule
    4.6 6.31/m
    ridicule treats someone with harsh public mockery and contempt, while scoff is a quicker, often lighter mocking laugh.
  • deride
    7.8 2.42/m
    deride expresses strong, formal contempt in words, while scoff is more casual and often shows scorn with a laugh.
  • sneer
    5.6 4.08/m
    sneer often shows a cold smile or voice of scorn, while scoff usually adds a short mocking laugh.
  • jeer
    6.2 1.25/m
    jeer is a loud shout or rude cry to mock someone, while scoff is often quieter and more personal.
  • jibe
    8.5 0.06/m
    jibe is a quick mocking remark often used to taunt someone, while scoff is broader and can include laughter.
  • make fun of
    2.3 10/m
    make fun of teases or mocks someone in a casual way, while scoff shows scorn and often sounds more contemptuous.
  • laugh at
    2.0 21/m
    laugh at is to laugh about someone, sometimes kindly or cruelly, while scoff always shows scorn or mocking contempt.
  • barrack
    8.6 0.01/m
    barrack loudly boos or shouts at someone to interrupt or mock them, while scoff is more a verbal or laughing show of contempt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flout - to openly disregard or show contempt for something
  • synonym: jeer - to make rude and mocking remarks, typically in a loud voice
  • synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule someone or something
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of someone or something
