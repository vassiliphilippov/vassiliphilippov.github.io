rebalance

noun
restoring balance
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbæləns / (BrE)
the act or process of making something balanced again, especially by adjusting the distribution of assets, weight, or resources

Examples

Portfolio rebalance
The quarterly rebalance of the investment portfolio helps maintain the desired risk level.
After the merger, a rebalance of staff roles was necessary to improve efficiency.
A regular rebalance ensures that resources are allocated according to the latest priorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjustment - a small change made to achieve a desired result
  • antonym: imbalance - a lack of balance or proportion
  • synonym: redistribution - the act of distributing something again in a different way
