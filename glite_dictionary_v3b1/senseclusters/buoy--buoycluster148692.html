buoy

noun
floating marker on water
Pronunciation: / ˈbuː.i, ˈbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ, ˈbʊ.i / (BrE)
an anchored floating object used as a marker to indicate channels, hazards, or navigational points in a body of water

Examples

A red buoy
A red buoy marked the edge of the safe swimming area near the beach.
Divers located the wreck site thanks to a yellow buoy floating above it.
The sailor steered the boat carefully between the green and red buoys in the harbor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beacon - a fire or light set up in a high or prominent position as a warning or signal
