look up and down

verb
examine closely from head to toe
Pronunciation: / lʊk ʌp ənd daʊn / (AmE), / lʊk ʌp ənd daʊn / (BrE)
to examine someone or something closely by moving one's gaze from head to toe or top to bottom, often with a sense of judgment or assessment.

Examples

To look up and down a person
He looked up and down the stranger, clearly trying to size him up.
She looked up and down the dress to check for any flaws.
The guard looked up and down each visitor before letting them enter.
