bop

verb
dance to bebop music
5.9
Pronunciation: / bɑp / (AmE), / bɒp / (BrE)
to dance or move to music with lively, rhythmic, or playful movements

Examples

To bop to music
Crowds gathered to bop to the live jazz band at the club.
They bopped all night, swept up in the energy of the music.
Young dancers love bopping to classic bebop tracks during festivals.
The children bopped around the living room when their favorite song came on.
At the concert, everyone started to bop to the catchy beat.
She loves to bop along to energetic pop music while cleaning the house.
The kids started to bop as soon as the upbeat song came on.
She loves to bop around the living room when no one is watching.
At the party, everyone was bopping to the latest hits.

  • synonym: bebop - a fast style of jazz music from the 1940s
  • synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music
  • synonym: groove - to dance or move in a relaxed, rhythmic way
  • antonym: stand still - to not move or dance at all
  • degree lower: sway - to move slowly or rhythmically from side to side
