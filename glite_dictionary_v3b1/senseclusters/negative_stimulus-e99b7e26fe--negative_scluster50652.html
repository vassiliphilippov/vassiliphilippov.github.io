negative stimulus

noun
stimulus causing undesirable effect
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛgətɪv ˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
a stimulus that produces an undesirable or unpleasant effect, often used to decrease a specific behavior

Examples

A negative stimulus shock
If a rat presses a lever and receives a shock, the shock acts as a negative stimulus.
In experiments, a loud noise is often used as a negative stimulus to study avoidance learning.
The teacher used a negative stimulus to discourage disruptive behavior in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: positive stimulus - a stimulus with desirable or pleasant consequences
