dilutive

adjective
reducing concentration
7.7
Pronunciation: / daɪˈluːtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈluːtɪv / (BrE)
causing a reduction in the concentration, strength, or effectiveness of something, especially by adding another substance.

Examples

Dilutive effect on strength
Adding too much water to the solution had a dilutive effect on its strength.
The company's new shares were seen as dilutive to existing shareholders' value.
Mixing the paint with extra solvent made it more dilutive and less vibrant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrated - having a high amount of a substance in a mixture
  • synonym: weakening - making something less strong or effective
