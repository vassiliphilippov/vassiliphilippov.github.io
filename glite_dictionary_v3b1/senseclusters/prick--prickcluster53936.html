prick

verb
cause a small stinging pain
5.2
Pronunciation: / prɪk / (AmE), / prɪk / (BrE)
to cause a sharp, stinging, or tingling sensation, either physical or emotional

Examples

To prick your finger
As he walked barefoot, a tiny stone pricked his foot, making him wince.
He pricked the balloon with a pin, causing it to pop.
The sharp thorn pricked her finger as she picked the rose.
His conscience pricked him after he told the lie.
She accidentally pricked her finger with a sewing needle.
The rosebush pricked his hand when he reached for a flower.
A sense of fear would prick at him whenever he heard the strange noise.
The cold wind began to prick her cheeks as she walked outside.
Tiny needles seemed to prick his skin when he entered the icy water.
