governor plum

noun
tropical shrub with small plum-like fruit
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌvərnər plʌm / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvənə plʌm / (BrE)
a small, shrubby tree native to Madagascar, cultivated in tropical regions as a hedge and for its small, deep red acidic fruits resembling plums

Examples

The governor plum tree
Locals often use the acidic fruit of the governor plum to make jams and jellies.
The governor plum thrives in warm, tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
The hedge was planted with governor plum to provide both privacy and fruit.
