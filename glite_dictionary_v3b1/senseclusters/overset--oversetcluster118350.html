overset

verb
to exceed layout space
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɛt / (BrE)
to cause text or content to exceed the available space, resulting in some material not being displayed or printed

Examples

To overset the text
If you add too much text to the box, the program will overset the content and some words will disappear.
The designer noticed that the headline was overset and fixed the layout before printing.
Always check for overset text in your document to ensure nothing important is missing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overflow - to spill over the limits of a container or space
  • synonym: truncate - to shorten by cutting off part
