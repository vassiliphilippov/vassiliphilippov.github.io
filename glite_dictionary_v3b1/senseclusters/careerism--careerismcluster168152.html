careerism

noun
pursuit of career advancement at any cost
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɪrˌɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈrɪə.rɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The practice of prioritizing personal career advancement, often at the expense of ethics or personal integrity.

Examples

Blatant careerism
His blatant careerism made him unpopular among colleagues who valued teamwork over self-promotion.
She was criticized for her careerism, as she often put her own advancement above ethical considerations.
The organization's culture of careerism led employees to compete rather than collaborate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • synonym: ambition - a strong desire to achieve success
