duad

noun
pair; set of two items
Pronunciation: / ˈduːˌæd / (AmE), / ˈdjuːˌad / (BrE)
A pair; two items of the same kind considered together.

Examples

A duad of gloves
Each pair of gloves is counted as a single duad in inventory.
In ancient philosophy, a duad often symbolized balance and unity.
The physicist spoke of a fundamental duad that defined the phenomenon.
