single

adjective
botanical: flower with one row of petals
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡl̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl / (BrE)
(of a flower) having only one row or whorl of petals.

Examples

Single flower
Gardeners often prefer single flowers for their more natural and delicate look.
Some single dahlias have a simple, elegant appearance compared to their double-flowered counterparts.
The rose variety produces single blossoms, each with just five petals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: double - (of a flower) having more than one row or whorl of petals
