anastrophe

noun
reversal of normal word order
7.4
Pronunciation: / əˈnæstrəfi / (AmE), / əˈnæstrəfi / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which the normal order of words is reversed, often for emphasis or poetic effect

Examples

Anastrophe in writing
Authors employ anastrophe to emphasize a particular word or phrase in their writing.
In the poem, the use of anastrophe gives the line a distinctive rhythm.
Yoda's speech in 'Star Wars' often exemplifies anastrophe for dramatic effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inversion - the syntactic reversal of the normal order of words
