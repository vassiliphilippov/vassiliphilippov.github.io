still water

noun
calm, unmoving water
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪl ˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈstɪl ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that is calm and not moving, typically found in lakes, ponds, or reservoirs

Examples

Calm still water
The reflection of the mountains was perfectly clear on the still water of the lake.
Frogs gathered at the edge of the still water in the early morning.
The canoe glided silently across the still water, leaving barely a ripple behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lake - a large body of still water surrounded by land
  • synonym: pond - a small body of still water
  • antonym: running water - water that is flowing, such as in a river or stream
