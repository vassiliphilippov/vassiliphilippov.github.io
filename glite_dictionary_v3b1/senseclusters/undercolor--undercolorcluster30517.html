undercolor

noun
color beneath surface
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.kʌl.ər/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.kʌ.lə/ (BrE)
the natural color of an animal's fur, feathers, or a plant part that lies beneath the surface or outer layer

Examples

Animal undercolor
The rabbit's soft undercolor was visible when its fur was brushed backward.
Birdwatchers often identify species by the undercolor of their wings during flight.
The plant's leaves had a silvery undercolor that caught the morning light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: topcoat - the outermost layer of fur or feathers
