nitrogen

noun
a common atmospheric gas element
1.6 2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪ.trə.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈnɪ.trə.dʒən / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless, tasteless, and mostly inert gas that constitutes about 78% of Earth's atmosphere and is essential to living organisms

Examples

Nitrogen gas
Ammonia is synthesized from nitrogen and hydrogen through the Haber process.
Plants require nitrogen in the soil to grow healthy and strong.
The Earth's atmosphere contains a high percentage of nitrogen compared to other gases.
