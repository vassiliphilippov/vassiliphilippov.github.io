inconvenience

verb
cause trouble or difficulty to
5.7 4.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kən.ˈviː.njəns/ /; / /ɪn.kən.ˈvɪn.jəns/ (AmE), /ɪn.kən.ˈviː.nɪ.əns/ /; / /ɪn.kən.ˈvɪn.jəns/ (BrE)
to cause trouble or difficulty to someone; to make things less convenient or more difficult for someone

Examples

To inconvenience people
Due to the repairs, some residents were inconvenienced by a temporary loss of water service.
I don't want to inconvenience you, so please let me know what time works best.
She apologized for inconveniencing her guests with the last-minute change of plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bother - to take the trouble to do something, often causing inconvenience
  • antonym: help - to make it easier or more convenient for someone
  • synonym: trouble - to cause distress or inconvenience to
