dreadful

noun
very bad thing
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɛdfəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɛdfʊl / (BrE)
something that is very bad, shocking, or unpleasant, often referring to sensational or low-quality stories or things.

Examples

A dreadful story
The critics called the new movie a dreadful that should never have been released.
Victorian children would secretly read dreadfuls filled with sensational stories.
He described the food at the event as a real dreadful compared to last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delight - something that brings great pleasure or joy
  • synonym: horror - something that causes fear or shock
