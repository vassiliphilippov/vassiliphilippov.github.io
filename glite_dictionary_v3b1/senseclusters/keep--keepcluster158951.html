keep

verb
prevent food from spoiling
4.4
Pronunciation: / kip / (AmE), / kiːp / (BrE)
to prevent food from spoiling; to remain fresh or suitable for use

Examples

To keep fresh
Cheese will keep better if you wrap it tightly in wax paper.
If you store them in a cool place, the apples will keep for weeks.
This container helps keep the bread fresh for longer.
If stored in the fridge, the jam will keep for several months.
Milk doesn't keep as long once it's been opened.
These homemade cookies keep well in an airtight container.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preserve - to treat or process food to prevent decay
  • antonym: spoil - (of food) to become unfit for consumption
