Henry II

proper noun
King of England, 1154–1189; legal reformer
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛnri ðə ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ðə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
(1133–1189) King of England from 1154 to 1189, first of the Plantagenet dynasty, known for instituting judicial and financial reforms and for his conflict with Archbishop Thomas Becket.

Examples

English King Henry II
Henry II reformed the English legal system, laying foundations for common law.
Many historians consider Henry II one of the most effective medieval English kings.
The conflict between Henry II and Thomas Becket changed church-state relations in England.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Plantagenet - the royal dynasty established by Henry II
  • antonym: Thomas Becket - Archbishop of Canterbury who clashed with Henry II
