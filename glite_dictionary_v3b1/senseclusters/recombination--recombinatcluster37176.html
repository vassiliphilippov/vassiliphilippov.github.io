recombination

noun
neutralization of ions in physics
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəmˌbaɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkɒmˌbɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) The process by which positive and negative ions or electrons in a gas combine to form neutral atoms or molecules, often following the passage of high-energy particles.

Examples

Ion recombination
Scientists observed recombination after exposing the gas to high-energy radiation in the laboratory.
The process of recombination is essential for understanding how auroras form in the Earth's atmosphere.
When the charged particles in the plasma experience recombination, they release energy as light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ionization - the process of forming ions by adding or removing electrons
