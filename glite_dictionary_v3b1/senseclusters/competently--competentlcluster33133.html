competently

adverb
with competence or skill
3.6 0.90/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêmp…ôt…ôntli / (AmE), / Ààk…ímp…™t…ôntli / (BrE)
In a competent and capable manner; with sufficient skill, ability, or knowledge.

Examples

To competently handle tasks
She handled the project competently and delivered it on time.
The new employee performed all tasks competently during his first week.
The repairs were carried out competently, leaving no trace of the damage.

Synonyms

  • capably
    3.8 0.22/m
    capably handles tasks with clear skill and confidence, while competently emphasizes having sufficient skill, ability, or knowledge for tasks.
  • ably
    3.5 0.91/m
    ably shows skill and effectiveness in doing tasks, while competently focuses on having enough ability and knowledge to do the job.
  • adequately
    3.0 11/m
    adequately is good enough for a purpose without being excellent, while competently means doing something with real skill and knowledge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incompetently - in a manner lacking competence or skill
