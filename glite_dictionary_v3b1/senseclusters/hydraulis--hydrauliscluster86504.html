hydraulis

noun
ancient water organ
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɔːlɪs / (AmE), / hʌɪˈdrɔːlɪs / (BrE)
an ancient Greek musical instrument, a water-powered pipe organ used in public performances

Examples

Ancient hydraulis instrument
The ancient Greeks invented the hydraulis as one of the earliest keyboard instruments.
A restored hydraulis was played during the historical reenactment at the amphitheater.
Scholars study the hydraulis to understand the development of musical technology in antiquity.

  • synonym: organ - a large keyboard musical instrument with pipes
