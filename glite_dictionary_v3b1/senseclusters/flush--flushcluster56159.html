flush

noun
reddening of the face from emotion
Pronunciation: / flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden redness or warm feeling in the face or skin, often from emotion, embarrassment, excitement, or heat

Examples

A flush of embarrassment
A deep flush rose to her cheeks as she realized her mistake.
He tried to hide the flush that revealed his embarrassment.
The flush of guilt was evident on his normally pale face.
Menopausal women often report having a sudden flush during the night.
She experienced a flush that made her cheeks turn bright red.
The medication sometimes caused a flush as a side effect.
A flush of adrenaline rushed through him before he stepped onto the stage.
He felt a flush of excitement as he crossed the finish line first.
Winning the award gave her a sudden flush of pride and joy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blush - a reddening of the face caused by embarrassment, shyness, or shame
  • antonym: boredom - the state of feeling bored and lacking excitement
  • synonym: hot flash - a sudden feeling of heat, usually due to menopause
  • synonym: rush - a sudden feeling of excitement or exhilaration
  • synonym: thrill - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or pleasure
