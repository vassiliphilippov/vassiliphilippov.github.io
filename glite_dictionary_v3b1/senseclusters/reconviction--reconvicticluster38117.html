reconviction

noun
being convicted again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
the act of being found guilty of a crime again after having been previously convicted

Examples

A second reconviction
The judge took the defendant's reconviction into account when deciding the sentence.
A reconviction can lead to harsher penalties under the law.
Programs aim to reduce the risk of reconviction among former offenders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of a crime
  • synonym: conviction - the act of being found guilty of a crime
