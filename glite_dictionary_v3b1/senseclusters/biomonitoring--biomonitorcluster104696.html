biomonitoring

noun
measuring chemicals in organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈmɑnɪtərɪŋ /; / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɑːnɪtɔːrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈmɒnɪtərɪŋ /; / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈmɒnɪtərɪŋ / (BrE)
the use of living organisms or measuring substances in them to assess environmental conditions or exposure to pollution

Examples

Biomonitoring of pollution
Scientists use biomonitoring to detect harmful chemicals in the blood of local residents.
Biomonitoring of river fish helps scientists track pollution levels.
Government agencies rely on biomonitoring data to set safety standards for drinking water.
Scientists rely on biomonitoring to detect early signs of pollution in rivers and lakes.
The government launched a biomonitoring program to track the effects of industrial waste on local wildlife.
Advances in biomonitoring have improved our ability to protect endangered ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioindicator - an organism used to assess the health of an environment
  • synonym: biosurveillance - the monitoring of biological data to detect threats to health
  • synonym: environmental monitoring - the process of observing and measuring environmental conditions
