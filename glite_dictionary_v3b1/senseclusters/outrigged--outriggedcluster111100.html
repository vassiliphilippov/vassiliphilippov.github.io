outrigged

adjective
fitted with a side structure (boat)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌrɪgd / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌrɪgd / (BrE)
equipped with a projecting structure on the side of a boat for purposes such as preventing capsizing, supporting an oarlock, or securing a mast

Examples

An outrigged sailboat
An outrigged sailboat is more stable in rough waters than a standard one.
He admired the craftsmanship of the outrigged fishing vessel anchored in the harbor.
The crew rowed swiftly in their outrigged canoe along the coastline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outrigger - a structure projecting from the side of a boat
