flashforward

noun
future mental image
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈflæʃ.fɔːr.wərd/ /; / /ˈflæʃ.fɑːr.wərd/ (AmE), /ˈflæʃ.fɔː.wəd/ (BrE)
a sudden, vivid mental image or scene that projects a possible future event, often experienced involuntarily

Examples

A sudden flashforward
During the meeting, she had a sudden flashforward of herself giving a successful presentation.
The movie uses a flashforward to show what might happen to the main character in ten years.
After the accident, he experienced a disturbing flashforward of losing control of the car again.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flashback - a sudden, vivid memory of a past event
  • synonym: premonition - a strong feeling that something is going to happen, especially something unpleasant
