Quechua language

noun
South American indigenous language
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈke.tʃwə ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ /; / ˈkɛtʃwə ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkɛtʃwə ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the language of the Quechua people, originally spoken by the Incas and still used in many Andean regions of South America

Examples

Quechua language spoken
He learned basic phrases in the Quechua language to communicate with local villagers.
Many traditional Andean stories are preserved in the Quechua language.
The Quechua language is still widely spoken in Peru and Bolivia today.
Many Peruvian children still learn Quechua language as their first language at home.
She studied the Quechua language to understand ancient Inca texts.
The Quechua language has dozens of dialects spoken across the Andes.
