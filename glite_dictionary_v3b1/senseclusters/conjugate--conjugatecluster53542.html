conjugate

verb
unite chemically, allowing easy separation
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.dʒəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʒʊ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to unite or join together, especially chemically or biologically

Examples

To conjugate chemicals
After ingestion, the toxin is conjugated in the liver and rendered less harmful.
Some drugs are designed to conjugate with proteins for more effective delivery in the body.
The chemical quickly conjugates with glucuronic acid to facilitate its elimination.
Fungi often conjugate during reproduction to form new spores.
In certain conditions, bacteria can conjugate to exchange genetic material.
Some verbs conjugate irregularly in the past tense.
