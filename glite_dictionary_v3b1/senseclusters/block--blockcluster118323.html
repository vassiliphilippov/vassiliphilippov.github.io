block

noun
solid rectangular piece of material
Pronunciation: / blɑk /; / bl / (AmE), / blɒk /; / bl / (BrE)
a solid piece or unit of material, often rectangular, used for building, as a toy, in machines, in computing, or as a group of things

Examples

A wooden block
He placed a heavy concrete block to keep the door open.
The children played with colorful wooden blocks in the playroom.
The sculpture was carved from a single block of marble.
Children stacked each block on top of another to build a tower.
She picked up a wooden block and handed it to her brother.
The toy set includes several colored blocks of different sizes.
Each file is stored in one or more blocks on the disk.
If a block is damaged, accessing the data in that section becomes difficult.
The database had to recover corrupted blocks after the power outage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: batch - a quantity produced at one operation
  • synonym: blockage - an obstruction that prevents flow, especially in a pipe or vessel
  • synonym: brick - a small rectangular block used in building walls
  • degree higher: cube - a three-dimensional shape with six equal square sides
  • synonym: distributed ledger - a ledger of transactions shared and synchronized across multiple computers; a blockchain is a type of distributed ledger
  • synonym: engine block - the main part of an engine containing cylinders and other components
  • antonym: individual - a single item, as opposed to a group
  • synonym: sector - smallest addressable unit of data storage on a disk
  • synonym: set - a collection of things that belong together
  • synonym: transaction - a recorded transfer of value or data that is included in a block
