upstander

noun
person who stands up
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌstændər / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstændə / (BrE)
a person who rises to a standing position, especially when prompted or required to do so

Examples

A quiet upstander
When the coach called everyone to attention, the upstander quietly rose and joined the huddle.
The teacher asked for a volunteer, and the first upstander was given the chance to speak.
In the crowded room, the upstander was easy to spot among those still seated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sitter - a person who is sitting
  • synonym: stander - a person who is standing
