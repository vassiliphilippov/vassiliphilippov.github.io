stratum germinativum

noun
innermost layer of the epidermis
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtəm dʒərˌmɪnəˈtɪvəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm dʒəˌmɪnəˈtɪvəm / (BrE)
the innermost layer of the epidermis, responsible for the generation of new skin cells

Examples

The stratum germinativum layer
Damage to the stratum germinativum can impair the skin's ability to heal.
Melanocytes, which give skin its color, are found in the stratum germinativum.
The stratum germinativum continually produces new cells to replace those lost from the skin's surface.
