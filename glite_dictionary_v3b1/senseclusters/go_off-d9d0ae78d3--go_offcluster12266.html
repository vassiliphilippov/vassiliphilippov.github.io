go off

verb
(of a gun) to fire or discharge
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ɔf / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf / (BrE)
to be activated, to start making a loud noise, or to explode suddenly (used for alarms, guns, bombs, or similar devices)

Examples

The alarm went off
During the fireworks show, several rockets went off at the same time.
Everyone ducked when the gun suddenly went off in the crowded room.
The alarm will go off if someone opens the door after midnight.
Her phone alarm goes off every morning at 6 a.m. to wake her up.
The bomb went off minutes after the building was cleared.
The fire alarm suddenly went off during our meeting, causing everyone to evacuate.
Everyone jumped when the bomb went off unexpectedly.
The fire alarm went off in the middle of the night, waking everyone up.
When the timer went off, she knew the cookies were ready.
