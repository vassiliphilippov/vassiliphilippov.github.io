living

adjective
used to intensify a noun (informal)
6.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪ.vɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈlɪ.vɪŋ/ (BrE)
(informal) Used as an intensifier before certain nouns to emphasize an extreme or absolute degree; often in set phrases such as 'the living daylights' or 'living hell.'

Examples

The living daylights
He threatened to beat the living hell out of anyone who crossed him.
That performance scared the living daylights out of me.
The children screamed the living daylights out of each other at the haunted house.
