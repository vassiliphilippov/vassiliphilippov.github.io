venepuncture

noun
puncturing a vein
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnəˌpʌŋktʃər / (AmE), / ˈviːnɪˌpʌŋktʃə / (BrE)
the act of puncturing a vein with a needle, usually to draw blood or give an injection

Examples

A venepuncture procedure
The doctor explained that a venepuncture would be necessary to collect the blood sample.
Proper technique is important when performing venepuncture to avoid complications.
The patient felt only a slight pinch during the venepuncture procedure.
The nurse performed a venepuncture to collect a blood sample for testing.
Proper technique is essential to minimize discomfort during venepuncture procedures.
After the venepuncture, the patient was advised to keep the area clean and dry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: injection - the act of putting a liquid, especially a drug, into a person's body using a needle
  • synonym: phlebotomy - the process of making an incision in a vein to draw blood
