cannibalization

noun
loss of sales to own product
Pronunciation: / ˌkænəbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkænɪbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a new product reduces sales or market share of an existing product from the same company

Examples

Product cannibalization
The launch of the new tablet resulted in the cannibalization of the company's older models.
Marketers must consider the risk of cannibalization when introducing similar products to the market.
The CEO explained that cannibalization was expected as part of their growth strategy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: competition - rivalry between products or companies for market share
  • antonym: expansion - the process of increasing in size or scope
