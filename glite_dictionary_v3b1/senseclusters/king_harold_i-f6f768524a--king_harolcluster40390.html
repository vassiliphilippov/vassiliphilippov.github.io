King Harold I

proper noun
English king, ruled 1035–1040
Pronunciation: / kɪŋ ˌhɛrɪld ðə ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˌhærəld ðə ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
King of England (1035–1040), the illegitimate son of Canute who seized the throne in 1037.

Examples

King Harold I of England
King Harold I was known for his rapid seizure of the English throne in 1037.
Many historians refer to King Harold I by his nickname, 'Harold Harefoot.'
Medieval chronicles describe the brief reign of King Harold I following Canute's death.
