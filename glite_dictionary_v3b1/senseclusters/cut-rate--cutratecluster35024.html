cut-rate

adjective
offered at a reduced price
3.7 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkÊŒtËŒreÉªt / (AmE), / ËˆkÊŒtËŒreÉªt / (BrE)
(of goods or services) offered at a price lower than the usual or standard rate.

Examples

Cut-rate prices
The company is known for selling cut-rate electronics online.
They hired a cut-rate moving service and regretted it later.
We decided to stay at a cut-rate hotel to save money on our trip.

Synonyms

  • cut-price
    3.0 0.01/m
    cut-price labels goods sold at a lower cost to attract buyers, while cut-rate simply means offered below the usual price.
  • bargain-priced
    3.2 0.15/m
    bargain-priced suggests a good deal or extra value for buyers, while cut-rate only means sold below the usual price.
