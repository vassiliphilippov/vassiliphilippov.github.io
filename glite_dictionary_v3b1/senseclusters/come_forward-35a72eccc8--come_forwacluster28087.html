come forward

verb
offer oneself or volunteer, make oneself known
3.2 12/m
Pronunciation: / k åm Ààf…îr.w…öd /; / k åm Ààf…îrw…öd / (AmE), / k åm Ààf…îÀê.w…ôd /; / k åm Ààf…îÀêw…ôd / (BrE)
to offer yourself or volunteer, especially to provide information or help

Examples

To come forward with information
After the appeal, several witnesses came forward to help the investigation.
No one was willing to come forward with information about the incident.
She finally decided to come forward and tell her side of the story.
After the announcement, no one was willing to come forward and claim responsibility.
Several witnesses finally came forward with new evidence.
She decided to come forward and tell the police what she had seen.

Synonyms

  • volunteer
    2.0 17/m
    volunteer means to offer help willingly without being asked, while come forward is to offer information or help.
  • step forward
    3.1 8.37/m
    step forward is to offer help or take responsibility visibly, while come forward often focuses on offering information or help.
  • step up
    3.7 6.74/m
    step up means to take stronger action or accept more responsibility, while come forward usually means to offer help or information.
  • step to the fore
    7.0 0.01/m
    step to the fore means to become prominently visible and take the lead, while come forward mainly means to offer information or help.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hold back - to not volunteer or make oneself known
  • synonym: step forward - to offer to help or give information
  • synonym: volunteer - to offer to do something without being forced or paid to do it
