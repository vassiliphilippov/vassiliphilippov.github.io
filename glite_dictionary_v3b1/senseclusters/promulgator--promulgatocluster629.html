promulgator

noun
person who officially puts law into effect
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.məlˌɡeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / prəˈmʌl.ɡeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who formally declares or puts a law or decree into effect

Examples

The promulgator of laws
As the official promulgator, she was responsible for ensuring all relevant parties were informed of the law’s enactment.
Many citizens wrote letters to the promulgator seeking clarification about the law's details.
The promulgator of the new regulation made a public announcement in the courthouse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: promulgate - to officially announce or put a law into effect
