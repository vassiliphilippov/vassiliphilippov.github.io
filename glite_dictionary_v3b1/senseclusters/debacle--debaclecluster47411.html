debacle

noun
flooding from river ice breakup
Pronunciation: / dəˈbɑkəl / (AmE), / deɪˈbɑːkəl / (BrE)
A sudden flooding caused by the violent breakup of ice in a river, especially during the spring or summer thaw.

Examples

River debacle
During the 19th century, a debacle could devastate entire riverside communities.
Residents prepared for the annual debacle by moving valuables away from the flood-prone areas.
The spring debacle caused flooding along the riverbanks after the ice suddenly broke up.
