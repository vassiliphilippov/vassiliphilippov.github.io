German measles

noun
contagious viral disease; also called rubella
3.0 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊ’ÉËmÉ™n ËˆmizÉ™lz / (AmE), / ËˆdÊ’ÉœËmÉ™n ËˆmiËzlz / (BrE)
a contagious viral disease, also known as rubella, that is milder than measles and typically lasts three or four days; it can cause serious harm to a fetus if contracted by a pregnant woman during the first trimester

Examples

German measles disease
German measles can have serious consequences if contracted by pregnant women.
The symptoms of German measles are usually milder than those of regular measles.
Vaccination has greatly reduced the incidence of German measles worldwide.

Synonyms

    4.2 0.00/m

  • epidemic roseola
    9.0 0.00/m
