Yucca filamentosa

noun
species of yucca plant with threadlike leaves
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈjʌkə ˌfɪl.ə.mənˈtoʊ.sə / (AmE), / ˈjʌkə ˌfɪl.ə.menˈtəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A species of yucca plant with long, stiff leaves featuring filament-like threads along the edges, commonly known as Adam's needle.

Examples

Yucca filamentosa plant
Gardeners appreciate Yucca filamentosa for its resilience and ease of care in sandy soils.
The Yucca filamentosa plant is often used in landscaping for its striking foliage and drought tolerance.
The stiff, sword-shaped leaves of Yucca filamentosa have distinctive white threads along their edges.
