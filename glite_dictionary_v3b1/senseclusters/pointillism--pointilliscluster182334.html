pointillism

noun
painting technique using dots of color
4.1 7.7
Pronunciation: /pɔɪn.ˈtɪl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /pɔɪn.ˈtɪl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a painting technique where small, distinct dots of color are applied in patterns to form an image, developed by Georges Seurat and his followers in late 19th-century France

Examples

Pointillism painting technique
Modern artists sometimes adapt pointillism techniques in digital art projects.
She studied pointillism to better understand how painters use color and perception.
The museum's new exhibit features several masterpieces of pointillism by Georges Seurat.
Georges Seurat is famous for developing pointillism, a technique using tiny dots of color to create images.
Her artwork showcases modern pointillism, blending vibrant dots into stunning landscapes.
The museum's new exhibit features several masterpieces of pointillism from the late 19th century.
