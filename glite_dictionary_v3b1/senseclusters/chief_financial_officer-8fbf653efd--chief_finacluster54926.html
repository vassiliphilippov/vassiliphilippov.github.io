chief financial officer

noun
senior executive in charge of financial management
Pronunciation: / tʃif fəˈnænʃəl ˈɑfɪsər / (AmE), / tʃiːf faɪˈnænʃəl ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
the senior executive in a company or organization responsible for managing its financial actions, including budgeting, reporting, and expenditure authorization

Examples

Chief financial officer role
As chief financial officer, Maria is responsible for overseeing the company's investment strategy and financial planning.
The chief financial officer presented the quarterly earnings report to the board of directors.
The appointment of a new chief financial officer brought fresh perspectives to the company’s budget management.
