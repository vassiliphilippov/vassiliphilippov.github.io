humiliation

noun
state of shame or loss of dignity
Pronunciation: / hjuˌmɪliˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / hjuˌmɪlɪˈeɪʃən /; / hjuːˌmɪlɪˈeɪʃən /; / hjuˌmɪliˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling or experience of shame, embarrassment, or loss of self-respect, often caused by being made to feel foolish or inferior

Examples

Public humiliation
After the public apology, he felt a deep sense of humiliation that lasted for weeks.
She could not forget the humiliation of being laughed at by her classmates.
Winning the game was sweet, but the memory of last year's humiliation still lingered.
Every mistake he made felt like another humiliation in front of his colleagues.
She could not forgive the public humiliation she had suffered.
The defeat was a terrible humiliation for the team.
His childish prank ended in unexpected humiliation when everyone started laughing at him.
She felt deep humiliation after forgetting her lines in front of the audience.
The loss was a source of great humiliation for the proud athlete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness
  • synonym: indignity - an act that causes shame or a loss of dignity
  • antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's own achievements
