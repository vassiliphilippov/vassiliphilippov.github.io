IDDM

noun
insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus (Type 1 diabetes)
7.7 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / a…™ diÀê diÀê …õm / (AmE), / a…™ diÀê diÀê …õm / (BrE)
abbreviation for insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus, a form of diabetes requiring regular insulin administration due to the body's inability to produce insulin

Examples

IDDM disease
Children diagnosed with IDDM often require careful monitoring of their blood sugar levels.
Managing IDDM involves a balance of diet, exercise, and daily insulin injections.
The risk of complications from IDDM can be reduced with proper medical care.

Synonyms

  • insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus
    4.9 0.04/m
    insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus is the full medical name used in writing and reports, while IDDM is its abbreviation used in notes.
  • type I diabetes
    3.5 0.09/m
    type I diabetes is the common modern term for the condition, while IDDM is an older abbreviation you may see in records.
  • autoimmune diabetes
    4.5 0.00/m
    autoimmune diabetes describes immune attack on the pancreas causing insulin loss, while IDDM names the condition as an abbreviation.
  • juvenile-onset diabetes
    4.1 0.03/m
    juvenile-onset diabetes refers to early-starting diabetes needing insulin, while IDDM is the abbreviation sometimes used for that diagnosis.
  • juvenile diabetes
    3.2 0.13/m
    juvenile diabetes names diabetes that starts in childhood, while IDDM is a medical abbreviation sometimes used for that older term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: NIDDM - non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus; Type 2 diabetes
  • synonym: Type 1 diabetes - autoimmune diabetes marked by a lack of insulin production
