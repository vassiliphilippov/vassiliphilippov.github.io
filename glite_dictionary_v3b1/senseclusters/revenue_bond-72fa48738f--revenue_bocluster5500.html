revenue bond

noun
bond repaid from specific project revenue
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛvənju ˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ˈrɛvənjuː ˌbɒnd / (BrE)
a bond issued by a government agency to finance public projects, with repayments made from the revenue generated by those projects

Examples

A revenue bond issue
Investors were attracted to the revenue bond because it was backed by toll collections from the highway.
The city issued a revenue bond to fund the construction of the new bridge.
The museum's expansion was made possible by a revenue bond that will be repaid from ticket sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym: municipal bond - a bond issued by a city or local government to raise funds for public projects
