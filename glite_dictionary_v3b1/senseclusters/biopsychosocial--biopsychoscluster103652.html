biopsychosocial

adjective
biology, mind, and society
7.2
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.saɪ.kə.ˈsoʊ.ʃəl/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.saɪ.kə.ˈsəʊ.ʃəl/ (BrE)
relating to the combination of biological, psychological, and social factors, especially in understanding health or illness

Examples

The biopsychosocial model
The biopsychosocial approach considers how stress and relationships affect physical health.
Doctors now use a biopsychosocial model to treat chronic pain more effectively.
Understanding addiction requires a biopsychosocial perspective that includes environment and mental health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biomedical - relating only to biological and medical factors
  • synonym: holistic - considering the whole rather than just parts
