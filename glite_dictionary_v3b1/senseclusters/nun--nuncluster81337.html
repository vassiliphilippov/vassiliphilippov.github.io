nun

noun
woman in a religious order
2.7 19/m
Pronunciation: / nÊŒn / (AmE), / nÊŒn / (BrE)
a woman who belongs to a religious community and usually lives under vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience

Examples

Catholic nun
At the museum, there was a painting of a medieval nun reading in a candlelit room.
She decided to become a nun after spending several years volunteering in the community.
The nuns at the convent dedicate their lives to prayer and helping others.

Synonyms

  • Sister
    2.5 7.18/m
    Sister is a title used to address women in Catholic religious communities, while nun is a woman who lives under religious vows.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monk - a man who is a member of a religious community
