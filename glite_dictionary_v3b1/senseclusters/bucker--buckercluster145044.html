bucker

noun
two-person logging saw
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkər / (AmE), / ˈbʌkə / (BrE)
a large saw, machine, or tool used for cutting logs into shorter pieces

Examples

A bucker saw
The workers used a bucker to cut the massive log into manageable pieces.
Before chainsaws, a bucker was essential for preparing firewood in the forest.
It took two strong people to operate the heavy bucker efficiently.
The experienced bucker made quick work of the fallen pine tree.
A powerful bucker can turn a large log into firewood in minutes.
The rodeo featured a fierce bucker that threw every rider off its back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bronco - an untrained or wild horse that bucks
  • antonym: chainsaw - a power-driven saw with a chain for cutting wood
  • synonym: crosscut saw - a saw designed for cutting wood across the grain
  • synonym: logger - a person who fells trees and prepares timber
  • synonym: sawyer - a person who saws timber into planks or boards
