exalt

verb
to fill with noble or intense emotion
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɔlt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɑːlt / (BrE)
to fill (someone) with a noble or heightened emotion; to elate or inspire intensely

Examples

To exalt the spirit
A breathtaking sunrise can exalt even the most ordinary morning into something magical.
He was exalted by the news of his long-awaited promotion.
The inspiring speech seemed to exalt the spirits of everyone in the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depress - to make someone feel sad or dispirited
  • synonym: thrill - to cause a sudden feeling of excitement and pleasure
