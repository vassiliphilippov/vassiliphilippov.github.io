cut

verb
absent oneself intentionally (from a class, etc.)
Pronunciation: / kʌt / (AmE), / kʌt / (BrE)
to intentionally not attend (a class, meeting, or event); to remove someone from a group or organization

Examples

To cut class
He decided to cut class and spend the afternoon at the park instead.
If you cut too many meetings, your supervisor will notice.
She often cuts lectures when she has other commitments.
After the audition, the director decided to cut three actors from the final cast.
Budget constraints forced the club to cut several long-time members.
If you don't improve your grades, the coach might cut you from the team next season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attend - to be present at an event or place
  • synonym: dismiss - to remove someone from a position or group
  • antonym: retain - to keep someone as a member of a group
  • synonym: skip - to not attend something on purpose
