multifactor

adjective
involving many factors
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈfæktə / (BrE)
involving or relating to several different factors or elements

Examples

Multifactor causes
The causes of climate change are multifactor and require a broad approach to address.
A multifactor analysis was used to understand the company's recent growth.
Effective health interventions often take a multifactor approach to address complex issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-factor - involving only one factor or element
