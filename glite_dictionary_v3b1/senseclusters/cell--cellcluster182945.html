cell

noun
basic unit of life in organisms
2.2 1.8
Pronunciation: /sel/ (AmE), /sel/ (BrE)
The smallest structural and functional unit of an organism, typically microscopic and consisting of cytoplasm and a nucleus enclosed in a membrane.

Examples

Human cell
Every living thing is made up of one or more cells.
Red blood cells carry oxygen throughout the human body.
The scientist examined the plant cell under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: organism - an individual living thing, made up of one or more cells
  • degree higher: tissue - a group of similar cells in an organism performing a specific function
