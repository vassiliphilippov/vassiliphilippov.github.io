Candida albicans

noun
parasitic fungus causing infections
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.dɪ.də ælˈbeɪ.kənz / (AmE), / ˈkæn.dɪ.də ælˈbɪː.kænz / (BrE)
A species of parasitic fungus that can cause infections in the mouth, skin, intestines, or vagina, particularly in people with weakened immune systems.

Examples

Candida albicans infection
Doctors prescribed antifungal medication to treat the Candida albicans infection.
Oral thrush is often caused by an overgrowth of Candida albicans in the mouth.
Patients with weakened immune systems are more susceptible to infections by Candida albicans.
