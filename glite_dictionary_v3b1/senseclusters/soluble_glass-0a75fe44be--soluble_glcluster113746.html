soluble glass

noun
solution of sodium silicate used industrially
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑljəbəl ˈɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈsɒljʊb(ə)l ˈɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a viscous solution of sodium silicate, used as a cement, protective coating, or for egg preservation

Examples

Soluble glass coating
Coating the wood with soluble glass helped protect it from moisture and fungi.
Farmers once used soluble glass to preserve eggs before refrigeration was common.
The laboratory used soluble glass to seal the cracks in the ceramic tiles.
