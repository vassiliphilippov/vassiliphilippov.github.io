charger

noun
a horse ridden into battle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑːrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːdʒə / (BrE)
a strong, swift horse used in battle, especially in the past

Examples

A knight's charger
In medieval tournaments, noblemen paraded their finest charger before the crowds.
The charger galloped across the field, its armor glinting in the sunlight.
The knight rode his charger fearlessly into the thick of battle.
