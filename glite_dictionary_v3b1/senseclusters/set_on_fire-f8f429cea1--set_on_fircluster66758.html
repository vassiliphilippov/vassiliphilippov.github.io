set on fire

verb
cause to begin burning
Pronunciation: / sɛt ɑn faɪɚ / (AmE), / sɛt ɒn faɪə / (BrE)
to cause something to begin burning; to ignite something intentionally or by accident

Examples

To set on fire dry grass
A spark from the campfire could easily set on fire the dry grass nearby.
He accidentally set on fire the old newspapers in the garage.
The protestors threatened to set on fire the abandoned building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extinguish - to cause a fire or light to stop burning
  • synonym: ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
