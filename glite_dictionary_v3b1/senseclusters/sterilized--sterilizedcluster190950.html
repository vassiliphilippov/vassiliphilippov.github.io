sterilized

adjective
made unable to reproduce
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛrəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈstɛrɪˌlaɪzd /; / ˈstɛr.ɪ.laɪzd / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) made unable to produce offspring through a medical or surgical procedure.

Examples

Sterilized cat
After the operation, the cat was sterilized and could no longer have kittens.
Many countries encourage owners to have their pets sterilized to help control the animal population.
The doctor explained that once someone is sterilized, the procedure is usually permanent.
After the surgery, the cat was sterilised to prevent unwanted litters.
Some plants have sterilised seeds that cannot germinate.
The government provided free clinics where stray dogs were sterilised.

  • antonym: fertile - able to produce offspring
