ford

noun
act of crossing a shallow river or stream
Pronunciation: / fɔrd / (AmE), / fɔːd / (BrE)
an act of crossing a shallow part of a river or stream by wading, riding, or driving through it

Examples

A quick ford
During the hike, we came across a shallow ford that was easy to cross.
Early pioneers often used a ford to avoid building bridges over wide streams.
The travelers searched for a safe ford before attempting to cross the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wade - to walk through water or another substance that impedes movement
