autotelic

adjective
having purpose in itself; self-contained
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtəˈtɛlɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈtɛlɪk / (BrE)
(of an activity or person) having a purpose or meaning in itself, rather than as a means to an end.

Examples

Autotelic activity
Many artists are autotelic, creating not for fame or money, but for the joy of creating.
She finds painting to be an autotelic activity, enjoyed purely for its own sake.
The psychologist described flow as an autotelic state, where action is its own reward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: instrumental - serving as a means to an end, not for its own sake
