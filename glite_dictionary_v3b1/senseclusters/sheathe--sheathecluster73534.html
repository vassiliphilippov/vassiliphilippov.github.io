sheathe

verb
plunge a blade into flesh
Pronunciation: / ʃið / (AmE), / ʃiːð / (BrE)
to put a knife, sword, or other blade into a sheath, or to plunge a blade into something

Examples

To sheathe a sword
He sheathed his dagger in the traitor's chest without hesitation.
The assassin silently sheathed the blade into his victim.
With a cry, she sheathed her sword deeply into the beast's flank.
After the ceremony, the knight carefully sheathed his sword.
Once the danger was over, she slowly sheathed the blade.
Please sheathe the knife when you are finished using it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stab - to thrust a pointed weapon into someone or something
  • antonym: unsheathe - to draw (a sword, knife, etc.) from its sheath
