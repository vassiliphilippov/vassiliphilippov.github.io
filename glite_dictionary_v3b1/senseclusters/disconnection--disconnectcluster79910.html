disconnection

noun
state of being disconnected
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃ(ə)n /; / ˌdɪskəˈnɛkʃən / (BrE)
the state or act of being disconnected or separated, either physically or in terms of communication or understanding

Examples

A disconnection from work
Some people experience a sense of disconnection from their work or community.
The faulty cable caused the disconnection of the internet service.
There was a sudden disconnection in the power supply during the storm.
A disconnection between theory and practice can hinder real-world progress.
The film explores the disconnection people feel in modern urban life.
There was a profound disconnection between management and staff after the policy changes.
A sudden disconnection of the power supply left the entire city in darkness.
Internet disconnection can be frustrating during important online meetings.
She felt an emotional disconnection from her coworkers after moving to a different department.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disconnect - a lack of connection or agreement, especially between ideas or people
  • synonym: separation - the action of moving or being moved apart
