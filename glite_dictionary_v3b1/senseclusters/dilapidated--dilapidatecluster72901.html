dilapidated

adjective
in disrepair or ruin
Pronunciation: / dɪˈlæpəˌdeɪtɪd / (AmE), / dɪˈlæpɪdeɪtɪd / (BrE)
In a state of disrepair or ruin as a result of age or neglect.

Examples

A dilapidated building
After years of neglect, the school building had become dangerously dilapidated.
The dilapidated house at the end of the street was finally condemned by the city.
We drove past a dilapidated barn with a collapsed roof and peeling paint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decrepit - worn out or ruined due to age
  • antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired in any way; complete
  • synonym: ramshackle - badly built or in poor condition
  • synonym: ruined - having suffered damage severely impairing its condition
