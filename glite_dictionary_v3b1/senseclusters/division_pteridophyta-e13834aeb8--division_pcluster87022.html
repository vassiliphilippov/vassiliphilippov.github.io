division Pteridophyta

noun
group of vascular plants without seeds
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒən təˌrɪdəˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən tɛˌrɪdɪˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
a former taxonomic group of vascular plants, including ferns, horsetails, club mosses, and whisk ferns, distinguished by lacking seeds; now often considered a subdivision or obsolete within modern classifications

Examples

division Pteridophyta plants
Many botany textbooks describe the division Pteridophyta as comprising ferns and several related groups.
Recent research has reorganized the division Pteridophyta, reflecting advances in plant phylogeny.
The division Pteridophyta once included all vascular plants except those with seeds, such as ferns and horsetails.
