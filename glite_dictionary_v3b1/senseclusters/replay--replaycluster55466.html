replay

noun
instant rebroadcast of recorded action, often in sports
Pronunciation: / riˈpleɪ / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪ / (BrE)
an immediate broadcast of a previously recorded action, especially during a sports event, typically to review a key moment

Examples

A replay of the goal
The network showed a slow-motion replay of the winning shot immediately after the game.
The referee requested a replay to review the controversial goal.
Viewers watched the replay of the touchdown from several different angles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: highlight - a short excerpt showcasing a notable moment, especially in sports
