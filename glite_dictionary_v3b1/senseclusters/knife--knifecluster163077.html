knife

verb
to move smoothly through
7.4
Pronunciation: / naɪf / (AmE), / naɪf / (BrE)
to move quickly and smoothly through something, as if cutting it with a knife

Examples

To knife through water
The speedboat was knifing through the choppy sea at high speed.
A dolphin knifed through the water, barely making a splash.
The skier knifed down the icy slope with impressive control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slice - to move through something cleanly and easily
