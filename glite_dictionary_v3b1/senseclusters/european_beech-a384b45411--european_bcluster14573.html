European beech

noun
a species of beech tree native to Europe
3.7
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən bitʃ / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən biːtʃ / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of Europe with smooth gray bark and minutely-toothed leaves, widely cultivated as an ornamental in many regions

Examples

The European beech tree
The European beech is prized for its broad canopy and attractive bark in parks and gardens.
The forest was dominated by ancient European beech, their trunks smooth and silvery.
We planted a row of European beech trees along the driveway to provide shade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beech - a deciduous tree of the genus Fagus, with smooth gray bark and small nuts
