biofilter

noun
contaminant-removing device
5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.oʊ.fɪl.tər/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.əʊ.fɪl.tə/ (BrE)
a device or system that uses living organisms to remove contaminants or pollutants from water or air

Examples

A biofilter system
The wastewater treatment plant uses a biofilter to reduce pollution before releasing water into the river.
A biofilter can help improve indoor air quality by removing harmful chemicals.
They installed a biofilter in the aquarium to keep the water clean for the fish.
Modern wastewater treatment plants often use biofilters to clean water before it is released into rivers.
A biofilter can help reduce odors and harmful gases in industrial facilities.
Many aquariums rely on a biofilter to keep the water safe for fish and plants.
