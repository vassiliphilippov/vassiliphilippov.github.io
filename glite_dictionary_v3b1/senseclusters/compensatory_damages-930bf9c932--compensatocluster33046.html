compensatory damages

noun
legal compensation for proven losses or injuries
2.4 5.1
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɛnsəˌtɔri ˈdæmɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / kəmˈpɛnsətəri ˈdamɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
(law) money awarded to a person as compensation for actual loss or injury proven to have been caused by another party's actions.

Examples

Compensatory damages awarded
If you can prove your financial losses, you may be entitled to compensatory damages.
The jury awarded compensatory damages to cover the costs of the plaintiff's medical treatment.
Unlike punitive awards, compensatory damages are intended to make the injured party whole.

Related Terms

  • antonym: punitive damages - damages awarded to punish the wrongdoer, not just compensate
