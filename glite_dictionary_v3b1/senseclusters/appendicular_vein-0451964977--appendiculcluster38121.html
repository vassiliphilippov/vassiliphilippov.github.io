appendicular vein

noun
vein draining blood from the appendix
7.2
Pronunciation: /ə.pen.ˈdɪk.jə.lər veɪn/ (AmE), /ə.pen.ˈdɪk.jʊ.lə veɪn/ (BrE)
a vein that accompanies the appendicular artery and drains blood from the appendix into the ileocolic vein

Examples

The appendicular vein
During an appendectomy, identifying the appendicular vein is important for safe removal of the organ.
Inflammation of the appendix can sometimes lead to thrombosis of the appendicular vein.
The surgeon carefully ligated the appendicular vein to prevent postoperative bleeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appendicular artery - artery supplying the appendix
  • synonym: ileocolic vein - vein receiving blood from the appendix and parts of the colon
