breakover

noun
vehicle clearance angle
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbreɪ.k.oʊ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈbreɪ.k.əʊ.və/ (BrE)
the angle or point at which a vehicle's underside may touch the ground when crossing a ridge or obstacle

Examples

Breakover angle
The truck's high breakover angle allows it to cross steep ridges without scraping the underside.
Off-road vehicles are designed with a greater breakover to handle rough terrain more effectively.
A low breakover can cause a car to get stuck when driving over sharp crests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approach angle - the maximum angle a vehicle can approach an obstacle without the front touching
  • synonym: departure angle - the maximum angle a vehicle can descend from an obstacle without the rear touching
  • synonym: ground clearance - the distance between the lowest part of a vehicle and the ground
