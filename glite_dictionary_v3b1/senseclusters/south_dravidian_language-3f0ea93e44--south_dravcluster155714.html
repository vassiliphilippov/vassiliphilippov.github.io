South Dravidian language

noun
Dravidian languages of southern India
Pronunciation: / saʊθ drəˈvɪdiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / saʊθ drəˈvɪdiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a branch of the Dravidian language family, comprising several languages spoken primarily in southern India, including Tamil, Kannada, Malayalam, and Tulu

Examples

South Dravidian language family
Kannada and Malayalam are both classified as South Dravidian languages.
Linguists study how the South Dravidian languages have evolved over time.
Tamil is one of the most widely spoken South Dravidian languages in India.
