graphicness

noun
visual quality
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræfɪknəs / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪknəs / (BrE)
the quality or degree to which something is visually striking or uses images and graphic elements

Examples

Graphicness of the design
The graphicness of the presentation captured everyone's attention with bold colors and sharp images.
Some parents were concerned about the graphicness of the video shown in class.
The graphicness of the magazine's layout made it appealing to younger readers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plainness - the quality of lacking decoration or visual elements
  • synonym: visuality - the quality of being visual or related to sight
