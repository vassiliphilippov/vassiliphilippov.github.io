jetpack

verb
to move rapidly
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛtpæk / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛtpæk / (BrE)
to travel, move, or transport someone or something quickly using a small rocket device worn on the back, or to give a rapid boost in speed or progress (figurative)

Examples

To jetpack across town
In the futuristic movie, the hero jetpacked across the city skyline to escape danger.
The company hopes to jetpack its new product to market success with an aggressive campaign.
Rescuers jetpacked supplies to the stranded hikers after the storm cleared.

Related Terms
