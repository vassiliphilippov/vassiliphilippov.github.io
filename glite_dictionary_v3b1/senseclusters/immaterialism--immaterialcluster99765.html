immaterialism

noun
lack of importance
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˈtɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtɪəriəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or condition that something is unimportant or lacks material significance

Examples

Immaterialism of details
The manager dismissed the complaint as mere immaterialism and moved on to more pressing issues.
Some critics argue that focusing on such details is an exercise in immaterialism.
He accused his opponent of resorting to immaterialism to avoid addressing the main topic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: importance - the quality of being significant or valuable
  • synonym: insignificance - the state of being unimportant
  • synonym: irrelevance - the quality of not being related or important
