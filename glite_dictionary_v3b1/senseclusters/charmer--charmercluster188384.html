charmer

noun
someone who easily wins others' liking
1.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑrmər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːmə / (BrE)
a person who is attractive, pleasant, and easily makes others like or admire them

Examples

A charmer at party
Everyone was immediately drawn to the charmer at the party, who had a smile for everyone he met.
She knew he was a charmer, but she couldn't help enjoying his company.
The new hire turned out to be quite the charmer with clients, making a great impression from day one.
Everyone said Julia was a real charmer at the party last night.
He's such a charmer that people can't help but enjoy his company.
The new teacher is a charmer, winning over both students and parents quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bore - a person who is dull or uninteresting
  • synonym: enchanter - a person who enchants or puts under a spell; figuratively, a very charming person
  • synonym: flatterer - someone who compliments others insincerely to gain favor
  • antonym: manipulator - someone who controls or influences others cleverly or unscrupulously
