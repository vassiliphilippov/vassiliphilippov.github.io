workfare

noun
welfare-for-work policy
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːkˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌfeə / (BrE)
a government policy or program that requires people to work or do job-related activities in order to receive welfare benefits

Examples

Workfare program
The government introduced workfare to encourage unemployed people to gain job skills.
Critics argue that workfare programs can be unfair to those unable to work due to health issues.
Many countries have debated the effectiveness of workfare in reducing long-term welfare dependency.
The government introduced workfare to encourage unemployed people to gain job experience.
Critics argue that workfare programs can be unfair to those unable to work.
Many participants in workfare perform community service in exchange for their benefits.
The government introduced workfare to encourage people on welfare to gain job experience.

Participants in workfare must complete community service to continue receiving benefits.
