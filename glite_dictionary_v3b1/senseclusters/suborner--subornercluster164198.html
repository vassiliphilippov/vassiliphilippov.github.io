suborner

noun
one who induces someone to commit a wrongful act
5.6
Pronunciation: / səˈbɔrnər / (AmE), / səˈbɔːnə / (BrE)
a person who induces or persuades someone to commit a wrongful or illegal act, especially by offering payment

Examples

The suborner offers bribes
In the bribery scheme, the suborner offered large sums of money for confidential information.
She was charged as a suborner after persuading an employee to destroy evidence.
The court found that he acted as a suborner by arranging for witnesses to give false testimony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • synonym: instigator - a person who initiates something, especially something bad
