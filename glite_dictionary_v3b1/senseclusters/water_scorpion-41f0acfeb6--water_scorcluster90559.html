water scorpion

noun
aquatic insect with grasping legs and breathing tube
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər skɔːrˈpiən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈskɔːpiən / (BrE)
a long-legged aquatic insect of the family Nepidae, with front legs adapted for catching prey and a long, tail-like breathing tube

Examples

A water scorpion insect
A water scorpion uses its long breathing tube to get air while remaining underwater.
