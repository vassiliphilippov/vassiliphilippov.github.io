monogamy

noun
having only one spouse or partner at a time
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / məˈnɒɡəmi / (BrE)
The practice or state of being married to or having a sexual relationship with only one partner at a time.

Examples

Practice of monogamy
After years of serial dating, he decided that monogamy suited him best.
Scientists study monogamy in animals to understand social bonds.
Some cultures place a high value on monogamy in marriage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polygamy - the practice or custom of having more than one wife or husband at the same time
