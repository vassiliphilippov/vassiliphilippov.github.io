learned profession

noun
profession requiring advanced learning and ethics
Pronunciation: / ˈlɝːnɪd prəˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɜːnɪd prəˈfɛʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a profession, such as law, medicine, or theology, traditionally regarded as requiring advanced education, specialized knowledge, and high ethical standards

Examples

A learned profession like medicine
He aspired to enter a learned profession like medicine or theology.
Law is often considered a learned profession due to its demand for advanced study and ethical standards.
The university has produced many leaders in each learned profession over the past century.
