revision

noun
act of rewriting or making changes
1.4 2.5
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈvɪʒ.ən/ /; / /rə.ˈvɪʒ.ən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈvɪʒ.ən/ /; / /rə.ˈvɪʒ.ən/ (BrE)
the act of changing, improving, or correcting something, or the new version that results from this process

Examples

A revision of policy
A thorough revision of the policy document was required to address recent concerns.
After receiving feedback from her professor, she started a complete revision of her essay.
Many authors spend hours on revision before submitting their final manuscripts.
After feedback from the editor, a complete revision of the report was necessary.
The author made several revisions before submitting the final manuscript.
The first revision included significant changes to the plot.
After reading the draft, the editor suggested a comprehensive revision of the entire chapter.
Our company went through a major revision of its employee handbook last year.
The scientific theory was improved following a careful revision of the experimental data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amendment - a minor change or addition, especially to a document
  • synonym: edit - the process of preparing written material for publication
  • synonym: modification - the action of changing something
  • antonym: original - something in its initial, unaltered state
  • synonym: rewrite - a piece of writing that has been written again to improve or change it
