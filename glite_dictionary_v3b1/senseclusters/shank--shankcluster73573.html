shank

noun
cylindrical part of a drill bit
5.7 6.5
Pronunciation: /ʃæŋk/ (AmE), /ʃæŋk/ (BrE)
the long, narrow part of an object, such as a tool, bolt, drill bit, spoon, or the part of a shoe connecting the heel and sole

Examples

Tool shank
A longer shank allows the drill to reach deeper surfaces.
Be sure to clean the shank before inserting the bit into the drill.
The shank of the drill bit must fit securely into the chuck.
A metal shank is often inserted to strengthen high-heeled shoes.
The shank of the boot provides stability and support for the wearer.
Without a proper shank, the shoe may not hold its shape over time.
A bolt with a damaged shank may fail under stress.
Make sure the shank is long enough to pass through the two pieces being joined.
The shank of the bolt needs to be perfectly smooth for proper fitting.
