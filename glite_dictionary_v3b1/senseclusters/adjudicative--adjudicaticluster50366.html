adjudicative

adjective
relating to judging or deciding (legal)
3.4
Pronunciation: / əˈdʒudɪˌkeɪdɪv / (AmE), / əˈdʒuːdɪkətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the act of making a formal judgment or decision about a problem or disputed matter, especially in a legal context.

Examples

Adjudicative legal process
Many legal scholars have written about the differences between investigative and adjudicative functions in law.
She pursued an adjudicative career, eventually becoming a judge in the superior court.
The adjudicative role of the tribunal was strictly outlined in the new legislation.
