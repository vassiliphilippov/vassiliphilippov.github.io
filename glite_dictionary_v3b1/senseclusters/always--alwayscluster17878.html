Synonyms

  • invariably
    4.2 8.76/m
    invariably is a formal word meaning in every case with no change, while always is more general everyday use.
  • unfailingly
    5.6 0.54/m
    unfailingly is used to praise steady reliability without fail, while always is more neutral and fits many contexts.
  • all the time
    1.0 77/m
    all the time is a casual phrase for happening very often or continuously, while always fits formal and informal use.
  • constantly
    1.0 50/m
    constantly suggests frequent or repeated action with little break, while always covers every occasion or complete regularity.
  • perpetually
    4.8 3.49/m
    perpetually implies long, ongoing or never-ending states, while always is more general and suits many short or long situations.
  • continuously
    2.3 9.89/m
    continuously means without any interruption over time, while always can mean every occasion rather than unbroken action.
