resident commissioner

noun
Puerto Rico's representative in US House
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛzɪdənt kəˌmɪʃənər / (AmE), / ˈrɛzɪdənt kəˌmɪʃənə / (BrE)
the elected non-voting representative of Puerto Rico in the United States House of Representatives

Examples

The resident commissioner in Congress
Elections are held every four years to choose the resident commissioner to represent Puerto Rico in Congress.
The resident commissioner from Puerto Rico often advocates for increased federal funding.
Unlike voting members, the resident commissioner cannot cast a final vote on legislation.
