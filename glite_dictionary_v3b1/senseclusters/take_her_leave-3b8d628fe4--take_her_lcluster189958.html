take her leave

verb
depart formally or ceremonially (female subject)
Pronunciation: / teɪk hɚ liv / (AmE), / teɪk hə liːv / (BrE)
(of a woman) to formally say goodbye and depart, especially in a polite or ceremonial way.

Examples

To take her leave politely
After a brief conversation, she stood up to take her leave from the gathering.
Before sunset, the queen chose to take her leave and return to her chambers.
Everyone thanked her for her help as she prepared to take her leave.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrive - to come to a place, opposite of depart
  • synonym: depart - to leave, especially a place or position
  • synonym: take one's leave - to formally say goodbye and depart
