nontautological

adjective
not always true
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌtɔtəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌtɔːtəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
not being a tautology; not true in every possible interpretation or situation

Examples

Nontautological statement
The argument is nontautological because it can be false under certain conditions.
A nontautological statement requires evidence to prove its truth.
Mathematicians often distinguish between tautological and nontautological propositions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tautological - true in every possible interpretation; always true
