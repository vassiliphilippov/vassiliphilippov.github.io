nonpoint

noun
diffuse pollution source
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
pollution that comes from many diffuse sources rather than a single, identifiable source

Examples

Nonpoint pollution
Heavy rainfall can increase nonpoint pollution as water washes chemicals from fields into rivers.
Controlling nonpoint sources of pollution is challenging because they are spread across large areas.
Laws targeting nonpoint pollution often focus on reducing fertilizer and pesticide use in agriculture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: point source - a single, identifiable source of pollution
  • synonym: runoff - water that flows over land and can carry pollutants
