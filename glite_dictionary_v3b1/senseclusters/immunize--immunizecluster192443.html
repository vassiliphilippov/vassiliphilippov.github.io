immunize

verb
make resistant to a disease by vaccination
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪmjʊnaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something resistant to a particular disease, especially by giving them a vaccine

Examples

To immunize a child
Before traveling abroad, she decided to immunize herself against yellow fever.
Doctors recommend that parents immunize their children against measles and other preventable diseases.
The campaign aims to immunize millions of people in rural areas each year.
Doctors recommend that children be immunised against measles at an early age.
If you travel to certain countries, you may need to be immunised for yellow fever.
The government launched a campaign to immunise the entire population against influenza.

Related Terms

  • antonym: infect - to affect with a disease-causing microorganism
  • synonym: vaccinate - to administer a vaccine to protect against disease
