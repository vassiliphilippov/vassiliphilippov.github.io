live

adjective
broadcast or performed in real time
3.1 30/m
Pronunciation: / laÉªv / (AmE), / laÉªv / (BrE)
(of a broadcast, performance, etc.) actually happening and being transmitted or shown at the time of occurrence, not pre-recorded or reproduced.

Examples

Live TV show
Listeners can call in to ask questions during the live radio show.
The concert was broadcast live on television for viewers at home.
We watched a live football match at the stadium Saturday evening.

Synonyms

  • on air
    3.6 1.74/m

Related Terms

  • antonym: recorded - (of television, radio, or performances) reproduced from a recording, not happening in real time
