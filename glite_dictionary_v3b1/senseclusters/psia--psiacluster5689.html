psia

noun
absolute pressure unit
Pronunciation: / piː ɛs aɪ eɪ / (AmE), / piː ɛs aɪ eɪ / (BrE)
a unit of pressure measuring pounds per square inch relative to a vacuum, used in engineering and science

Examples

psia pressure unit
The engineer recorded the system's pressure as 50 psia during the test.
To ensure safety, the vessel must not exceed 100 psia under normal operation.
The manual specifies that the reading should be given in psia rather than gauge pressure.
