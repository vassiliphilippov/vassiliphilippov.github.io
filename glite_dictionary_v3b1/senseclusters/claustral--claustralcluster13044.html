claustral

adjective
sealed in nest
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːstrəl / (AmE), / ˈklɔːstrəl / (BrE)
in entomology, describing an insect, especially a queen ant or bee, that remains sealed in the nest and rears the first brood without leaving to forage

Examples

Claustral queen ant
The claustral queen ant did not leave her chamber during the early stages of colony founding.
Some species of ants have a claustral phase where the queen relies on stored energy to raise her first brood.
Unlike foraging queens, claustral queens remain hidden and protected until their workers mature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foraging - searching for food outside the nest
