Francis Ferdinand

proper noun
Austrian archduke whose assassination sparked World War I
Pronunciation: / ˈfrænsɪs ˌfɜːrdɪˈnænd / (AmE), / ˈfrɑːnsɪs ˌfɜːdɪˈnænd / (BrE)
(1863–1914) Archduke of Austria-Hungary, whose assassination in Sarajevo in 1914 triggered the outbreak of World War I.

Examples

Francis Ferdinand assassination
Before his death, Francis Ferdinand was the heir apparent to the Austro-Hungarian throne.
Many history books discuss the impact of Francis Ferdinand's death on Europe.
The assassination of Francis Ferdinand is widely considered the immediate cause of World War I.
