rack up

verb
accumulate points or achievements
6.6
Pronunciation: / ræk ʌp / (AmE), / ræk ʌp / (BrE)
to accumulate or achieve something, especially a large number of points, victories, or other measurable things

Examples

To rack up points
By the end of the evening, he had racked up quite a few wins at the poker table.
She has racked up numerous awards for her volunteer work this year.
The basketball team managed to rack up over a hundred points in the final match.
