predecessor

noun
a thing or person that comes before another
4.9 1.64/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…õd…ôÀås…õs…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàpriÀêd…ôs…õs…ô / (BrE)
something or someone that comes before another and often indicates or prepares the way for their arrival or occurrence.

Examples

Predecessor of a storm
Many see dark clouds as a predecessor of an approaching storm.
The rapid growth in technology was a clear predecessor to the digital revolution that followed.
The traditional method served as a predecessor to the modern techniques now used in the field.

Synonyms

  • forerunner
    6.2 1.60/m
    a forerunner comes before and helps prepare the way, while a predecessor is simply what comes before another in time or role.
  • precursor
    7.2 3.78/m
    a precursor signals or announces a coming change or event, while a predecessor is the thing or person that comes before.
  • harbinger
    8.7 1.85/m
    a harbinger signals the approach of something important, while a predecessor simply comes earlier and does not signal the event.
  • herald
    6.1 5.68/m
    a herald announces an important arrival publicly or loudly, while a predecessor simply comes before someone or something in time or position.
  • forbearer
    8.1 0.01/m
    a forbearer is an ancestor or earlier holder of a role in a family or tradition, while a predecessor can be anything that comes before.
