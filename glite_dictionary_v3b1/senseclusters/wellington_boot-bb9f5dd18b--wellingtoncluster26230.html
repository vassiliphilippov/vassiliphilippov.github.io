Wellington boot

noun
a waterproof knee-high boot
1.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwel.ɪŋ.tən buːt/ (AmE), /ˈwel.ɪŋ.tən buːt/ (BrE)
A knee-length waterproof rubber or leather boot, typically worn outdoors, named after the 1st Duke of Wellington; originally a man's high boot but now worn by anyone.

Examples

A pair of Wellington boots
After a heavy rain, Wellington boots are essential for gardeners.
He bought new Wellington boots before going on the fishing trip.
She wore her Wellington boots while walking across the muddy field.
