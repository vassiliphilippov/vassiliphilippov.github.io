(physical chemistry) A sample of matter consisting of substances in different phases that are in equilibrium with each other.

Examples

A closed system
A closed system does not exchange matter with its surroundings, but may exchange energy.
In thermodynamics, a system is defined by the substances and their boundaries being studied.
When two phases coexist in a system, they are said to be in equilibrium.
