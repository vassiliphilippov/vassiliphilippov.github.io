geometric pace

noun
unit of length (5 feet) in surveying
7.5
Pronunciation: / dʒiˈɑmɛtrɪk peɪs / (AmE), / dʒiˈɒmɪtrɪk peɪs / (BrE)
a unit of length equal to 5 feet, used in modern adaptations of the Roman pace, especially in surveying and historical measurements.

Examples

A geometric pace unit
A geometric pace is standardized at five feet in modern surveying.
Historical texts sometimes refer to the geometric pace as a way to measure land boundaries.
The surveyor measured the distance in geometric paces for accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pace - a unit of length based on a natural walking step; also used as a Roman unit
