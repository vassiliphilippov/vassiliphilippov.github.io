Barosaurus

noun
genus of large Jurassic herbivorous dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˌbæroʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌbærəʊˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of huge quadrupedal herbivorous dinosaurs from the late Jurassic period, known for its extremely long neck

Examples

The Barosaurus dinosaur
Many exhibits feature the towering Barosaurus to illustrate the diversity of Jurassic sauropods.
Paleontologists uncovered a nearly complete Barosaurus skeleton in the western United States.
The Barosaurus was one of the longest-necked dinosaurs ever discovered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Diplodocus - a closely related genus of long-necked Jurassic dinosaurs
