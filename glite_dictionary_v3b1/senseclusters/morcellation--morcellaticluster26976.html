morcellation

noun
surgical tissue cutting
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔrsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɔːsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which tissue is cut into smaller pieces for removal from the body

Examples

Surgical morcellation procedure
The surgeon used morcellation to remove the large tumor through a small incision.
Advances in morcellation have made minimally invasive surgeries more common.
Concerns about the safety of morcellation have led to new surgical guidelines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excision - the act of cutting out tissue, especially surgically
  • synonym: resection - surgical removal of part of an organ or structure
