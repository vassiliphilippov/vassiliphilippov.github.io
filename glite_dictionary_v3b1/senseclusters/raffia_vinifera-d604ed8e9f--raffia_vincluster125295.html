Raffia vinifera

noun
African palm species yielding raffia fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈræfiə vɪˈnɪfərə / (AmE), / ˈrafɪə vɪˈnɪfərə / (BrE)
A species of palm native to tropical Africa, known for its large leaves which yield raffia fiber.

Examples

Raffia vinifera palm leaves
In West Africa, Raffia vinifera is cultivated for its strong, flexible leaves.
Textiles made from Raffia vinifera fibers are prized for their durability and texture.
The leaves of the Raffia vinifera are harvested to produce natural fibers.

Related Terms
