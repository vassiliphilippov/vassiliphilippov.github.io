fissiped mammal

noun
carnivorous mammal with separated toes
7.4 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪ.sɪ.ped ˈmæm.əl/ /; / /ˈfɪs.ɪ.ped ˈmæm.əl/ (AmE), /ˈfɪ.sɪ.ped ˈmæm.əl/ /; / /ˈfɪs.ɪ.ped ˈmæm.əl/ (BrE)
A member of a group of terrestrial carnivorous mammals with toes that are separated to the base, such as dogs, cats, bears, and raccoons.

Examples

Fissiped mammals like dogs
Most members of the dog and cat families are considered fissiped mammals because of their separated toes.
The zoologist explained that raccoons are classified as fissiped mammals due to their distinct foot structure.
Unlike seals, fissiped mammals such as bears walk on land with individual toes.
