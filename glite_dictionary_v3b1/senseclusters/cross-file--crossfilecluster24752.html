cross-file

verb
run as candidate for multiple parties
9.0
Pronunciation: / krɑs faɪl / (AmE), / krɒs faɪl / (BrE)
to have one's name listed as a candidate for more than one political party in an election

Examples

To cross-file in election
He decided to cross-file, seeking endorsements from both the Democratic and Republican parties.
In some states, candidates can cross-file to appear on the ballot for multiple parties.
Laws regarding the ability to cross-file vary significantly from one jurisdiction to another.
