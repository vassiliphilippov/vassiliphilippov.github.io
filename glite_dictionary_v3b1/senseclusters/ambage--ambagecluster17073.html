ambage

noun
indirect style of expression
Pronunciation: / ˈæmˌbeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæmˌbeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an indirect or roundabout way of speaking or writing; circumlocution

Examples

In ambage speech
He spoke in ambage, never giving a direct answer to the question.
Literary critics often highlight the poetic ambage found in classical prose.
The novel’s excessive ambage made the plot difficult to follow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circumlocution - the use of many words where fewer would do, usually in a deliberate attempt to be vague or evasive
