reinitiate

verb
to start again
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˈnɪʃieɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈnɪʃieɪt / (BrE)
to start something again after it has been stopped or interrupted, especially a process or system

Examples

To reinitiate the process
After the technical issue was resolved, the engineers decided to reinitiate the testing process.
The school will reinitiate its annual science fair after a two-year break.
If negotiations fail, the committee may need to reinitiate discussions with all parties involved.
If the installation fails, you may need to reinitiate the process from the beginning.
The system will automatically reinitiate after a power outage to restore normal operations.
Engineers decided to reinitiate the test sequence to ensure accurate results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: halt - to stop or bring to a stop
  • synonym: initiate - to begin or start something
  • synonym: restart - to start something again from the beginning
  • synonym: resume - to begin again after a pause or interruption
  • antonym: terminate - to bring something to an end
