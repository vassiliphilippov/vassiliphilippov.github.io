A monetary system in which the value of the basic unit of currency is defined by set amounts of two metals, usually gold and silver, at a fixed ratio.

Examples

Bimetallism monetary system
Advocates of bimetallism argued it would increase the money supply and support economic growth.
Many economists believed that bimetallism would help stabilize international exchange rates.
The debate over bimetallism versus the gold standard was central to late 19th-century American politics.
