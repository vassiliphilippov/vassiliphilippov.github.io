butane

noun
a flammable hydrocarbon gas used as fuel
Pronunciation: / ˈbjuːteɪn / (AmE), / ˈbjuːteɪn / (BrE)
a flammable hydrocarbon gas, C4H10, found in natural gas and petroleum, used as a fuel and in the manufacture of rubber and other chemicals

Examples

Butane gas
A portable stove was fueled by butane during the camping trip.
In laboratories, butane is often used as a reference gas in experiments.
Some cigarette lighters are refilled using butane canisters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propane - a similar flammable hydrocarbon gas, C3H8, used as fuel
