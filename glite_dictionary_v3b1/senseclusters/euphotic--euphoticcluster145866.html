euphotic

adjective
well-lit for photosynthesis
9.6 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / juÀêÀàfo ät…™k / (AmE), / juÀêÀàf…ô ät…™k / (BrE)
receiving enough sunlight to allow photosynthesis, especially describing the upper layer of water where light penetrates

Examples

The euphotic zone
Algae can only grow in the euphotic zone of the lake where sunlight penetrates.
The euphotic layer of the ocean supports a wide variety of plant and animal life.
Scientists measured the depth of the euphotic region to study photosynthetic activity.
Many types of plankton are found in the euphotic zone of lakes and oceans.
The euphotic layer supports most aquatic plant life due to the availability of sunlight.
Fish often gather in the euphotic region where food is abundant.
