pectoral

noun
a large chest muscle
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛktɚəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛktərəl / (BrE)
Either of the two large muscles on the front of the chest that assist in movements of the shoulder and arm.

Examples

Strong pectorals
He strengthened his pectorals by doing push-ups every day.
Stretching helps prevent injuries to the pectoral muscle group.
The athlete complained of pain in his left pectoral after the workout.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: pectoralis major - the larger of the two pectoral muscles
  • degree lower: pectoralis minor - the smaller, underlying pectoral muscle
