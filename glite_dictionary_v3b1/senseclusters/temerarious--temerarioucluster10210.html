temerarious

adjective
recklessly daring; rash
Pronunciation: / tɛməˈrɛriəs / (AmE), / tɛməˈrɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
showing reckless or presumptuous daring; rash or bold in a way that ignores danger or consequences

Examples

A temerarious act
Critics called his financial decision temerarious, risking everything on a single investment.
Her temerarious disregard for safety surprised even those who knew her best.
The temerarious explorers pressed on into the uncharted wilderness despite warnings of danger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cautious - showing care and avoidance of risk
  • synonym: reckless - without care for danger or consequences
