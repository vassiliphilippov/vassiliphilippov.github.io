force majeure

noun
unforeseeable event preventing obligations
9.8 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs m…ëÀà í…úr / (AmE), / f…îÀês m…ëÀêÀà í…úÀê / (BrE)
An extraordinary and unforeseeable event, such as a natural disaster or war, that prevents someone from fulfilling a contract or obligation.

Examples

A force majeure event
Due to force majeure, the delivery was delayed by several weeks after the hurricane.
The contract made an exception for force majeure events such as earthquakes or floods.
Their insurance policy did not cover losses resulting from force majeure circumstances.
