prevaricator

noun
person who avoids the truth; habitual liar
Pronunciation: / prɪˈvær.əˌkeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / prɪˈvær.ɪ.keɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who speaks or acts in an evasive way or who habitually avoids telling the truth

Examples

A prevaricator avoids truth
Everyone knew him as a skilled prevaricator who rarely gave a straight answer.
She confronted the prevaricator and demanded the truth at last.
The politician was labeled a prevaricator after several of his claims were proven false.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liar - a person who tells lies
  • antonym: truth-teller - a person who tells the truth
