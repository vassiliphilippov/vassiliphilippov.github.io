SEM

noun
electron microscope
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs iː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛs iː ˈɛm / (BrE)
a type of microscope that uses a focused beam of electrons to produce detailed images of a specimen's surface

Examples

SEM image
The researchers used an SEM to examine the fine details of the insect's wing.
Images from the SEM revealed tiny cracks on the surface of the material.
Training is required to operate an SEM safely in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: TEM - transmission electron microscope, which images internal structures
  • synonym: scanning electron microscope - a microscope that uses electrons to scan and image surfaces
