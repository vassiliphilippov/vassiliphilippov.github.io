torchwood

noun
tree with flammable wood
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔrtʃˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃˌwʊd / (BrE)
a tropical or subtropical tree, or its wood, that burns easily and is used for making torches or for lighting

Examples

Tropical torchwood tree
The villagers collected torchwood to light their homes during the festival.
Because of its high resin content, torchwood is ideal for making torches.
Birds often nest in the branches of the tall torchwood near the river.
Early explorers used torchwood to light their way at night.
The villagers gathered torchwood before the festival to illuminate the paths.
Because torchwood burns brightly, it was highly valued in ancient times for lighting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firewood - wood used as fuel for burning
  • synonym: kindling - small pieces of wood used to start a fire
  • synonym: pitchwood - wood rich in resin that burns easily
