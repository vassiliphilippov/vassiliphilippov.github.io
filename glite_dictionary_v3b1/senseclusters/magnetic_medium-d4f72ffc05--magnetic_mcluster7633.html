magnetic medium

noun
storage device using magnetic recording
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / maɡˈnɛtɪk ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
a storage device or material that uses magnetic patterns to record and retrieve digital information, such as hard disks or tapes

Examples

A magnetic medium device
A hard drive is a common magnetic medium found in desktop computers.
Floppy disks are an example of a magnetic medium used for data storage in early computers.
Many companies still archive data on magnetic media for long-term backup.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnetic storage - the general technique of storing data on a magnetic medium
  • antonym: optical medium - storage medium that uses light to read and write data, like CDs or DVDs
