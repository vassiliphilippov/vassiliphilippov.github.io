electron paramagnetic resonance

noun
microwave spectroscopy for paramagnetic substances
7.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trɑːn pæ.rə.mæg.ˈnet.ɪk ˈrez.ə.nəns/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trɒn pæ.rə.mæg.ˈnet.ɪk ˈrez.ə.nəns/ (BrE)
a form of microwave spectroscopy in which radiation is absorbed resonantly by substances with unpaired electrons (paramagnets) to study their magnetic properties

Examples

Electron paramagnetic resonance spectroscopy
Advances in electron paramagnetic resonance have improved the analysis of transition metal complexes.
Scientists use electron paramagnetic resonance to study free radicals in biological systems.
The electron paramagnetic resonance spectrum can reveal details about the molecular structure of materials.
