freshwater cordgrass

noun
North American grass species found in freshwater areas
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɛʃˌwɔtɚ ˈkɔrdˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈfrɛʃˌwɔːtə ˈkɔːdˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A North American species of cordgrass with dry membranous leaf margins and long awned glumes, typically found in freshwater habitats.

Examples

Freshwater cordgrass plant
Researchers noticed that freshwater cordgrass supports a diverse array of insects and birds in marsh ecosystems.
The wetland restoration project included planting freshwater cordgrass to stabilize the riverbanks.
Unlike its saltwater relatives, freshwater cordgrass thrives in non-saline wetlands.
