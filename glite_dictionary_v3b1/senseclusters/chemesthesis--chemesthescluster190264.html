chemesthesis

noun
chemical skin sensitivity
Pronunciation: / kɛˈmɛsθəsɪs / (AmE), / kɛˈmɛsθiːsɪs / (BrE)
the chemical sensitivity of the skin and mucous membranes to certain substances, resulting in sensations like tingling or burning

Examples

Chemesthesis sensation
The burning sensation from wasabi is a classic example of chemesthesis.
Some people experience chemesthesis when they eat spicy foods like chili peppers.
Menthol in mint gum can cause a cooling feeling due to chemesthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: somatosensation - the body's ability to sense touch, temperature, pain, and body position
  • antonym: taste - the sense that detects flavors in substances like food and drink
