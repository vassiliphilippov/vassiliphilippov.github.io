unshored

adjective
unsupported by evidence
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɔːd / (BrE)
not supported or justified by evidence, facts, or logical argument; lacking a firm foundation, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Unshored claim rejected
The theory remained unshored by any scientific data, so few experts accepted it.
Her argument was unshored and quickly dismissed by the panel.
Without reliable sources, the claim was considered unshored and unconvincing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: justified - supported by good reasons or evidence
  • synonym: unsubstantiated - not proven or supported by evidence
  • synonym: unsupported - not given support or justification
