myringitis

noun
eardrum inflammation
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪrɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs /; / ˌmɪrɪnˈɡaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌmɪrɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs /; / ˌmɪrɪnˈɡaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the eardrum, often causing pain or discomfort in the ear

Examples

Myringitis of eardrum
The doctor diagnosed myringitis after examining the patient's painful, swollen eardrum.
Children with myringitis may experience sudden ear pain and sometimes fever.
Treatment for myringitis often includes pain relief and, if needed, antibiotics.

Severe ear pain and redness can be symptoms of myringitis.
