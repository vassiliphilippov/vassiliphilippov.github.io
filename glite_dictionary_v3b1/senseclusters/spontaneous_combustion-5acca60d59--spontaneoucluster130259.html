spontaneous combustion

noun
ignition from internal chemical reaction
4.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /spɑːn.ˈteɪ.niː.əs kəm.ˈbʌs.tʃən/ /; / /spən.ˈteɪ.niː.əs kəm.ˈbʌs.tʃən/ (AmE), /spɒn.ˈteɪ.niː.əs kəm.ˈbʌs.tʃən/ /; / /spən.ˈteɪ.niː.əs kəm.ˈbʌs.tʃən/ (BrE)
The ignition of a material resulting from an internal chemical reaction, rather than from an external flame or heat source.

Examples

Spontaneous combustion of materials
Some fertilizers can undergo spontaneous combustion if stored improperly.
The fire marshal explained the incident as a case of spontaneous combustion.
The oily rags were a fire hazard due to the risk of spontaneous combustion in the workshop.
