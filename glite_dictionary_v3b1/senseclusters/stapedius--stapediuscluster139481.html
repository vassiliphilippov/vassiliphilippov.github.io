stapedius

noun
middle ear muscle
Pronunciation: / steɪˈpiːdiəs / (AmE), / steɪˈpiːdɪəs / (BrE)
a small muscle in the middle ear that stabilizes the stapes bone and helps control sound vibrations

Examples

The stapedius muscle
The stapedius contracts in response to loud sounds to protect the inner ear.
Damage to the stapedius can result in increased sensitivity to noise.
Surgeons must be careful not to injure the stapedius during middle ear procedures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tensor tympani - a muscle in the middle ear that dampens sounds
