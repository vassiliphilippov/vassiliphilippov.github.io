Lombardic

noun
extinct Germanic language
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɑmˈbɑrdɪk / (AmE), / lɒmˈbɑːdɪk / (BrE)
an extinct Germanic language that was once spoken by the Lombards, a medieval people in Italy

Examples

Lombardic language records
Few written records of Lombardic have survived from the early Middle Ages.
Scholars study Lombardic to understand the migration of Germanic tribes into Italy.
The museum displayed ancient artifacts inscribed with Lombardic script.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gothic - an extinct East Germanic language once spoken by the Goths
  • synonym: Old High German - an early form of the German language spoken in medieval times
