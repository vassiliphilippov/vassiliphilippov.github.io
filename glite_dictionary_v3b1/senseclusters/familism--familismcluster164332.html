familism

noun
favoring family members
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfæmɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or belief of giving special favor or priority to one's own family members, especially in jobs or resource distribution

Examples

Familism in hiring
The company's culture of familism made it difficult for outsiders to advance.
Critics argue that familism can lead to less qualified people being hired.
In some societies, familism is seen as a positive value that strengthens family bonds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impartiality - the quality of treating all people equally without favoritism
  • synonym: nepotism - the unfair practice of giving jobs or advantages to relatives
