adhesion

noun
property of sticking together; joining of surfaces
6.2 1.0/m
Pronunciation: / …ôdÀàhi í…ôn / (AmE), / …ôdÀàhiÀê í…ôn / (BrE)
the tendency or property of surfaces or materials to stick together or join, especially due to attractive forces between different materials

Examples

Strong adhesion between surfaces
Proper surface preparation ensures optimal adhesion when gluing metal parts together.
The adhesion between the tires and the road is crucial for safe driving.
The adhesion of the paint to the wall was remarkably strong.

Synonyms

  • adhesiveness
    6.9 0.02/m
    adhesiveness describes how well surfaces cling together, while adhesion names the physical force that makes them join.
  • adherence
    6.1 0.3/m
    adherence means sticking firmly to a surface in practice, while adhesion refers to the underlying attraction between different materials.
  • stickiness
    3.9 0.4/m
    stickiness is the everyday feel of being sticky on touch, while adhesion is the material property caused by attractive forces.
  • tackiness
    4.2 0.02/m
    tackiness is a light, often unpleasant stickiness felt to the touch, while adhesion is a general material tendency to bond.
