sweep

verb
win a complete victory
3.2 2.28/m
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiÀêp / (BrE)
to win an overwhelming victory in or over (an opponent, a competition, or a series of contests)

Examples

To sweep the elections
Her party is expected to sweep the elections this year.
In last weekend's tournament, he swept all his opponents and took first place.
The team managed to sweep the entire series, winning every game.

Synonyms
