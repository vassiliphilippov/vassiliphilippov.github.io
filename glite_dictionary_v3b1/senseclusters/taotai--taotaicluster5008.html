taotai

noun
Chinese regional official
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊˌtaɪ /; / ˈtaʊˌtaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊˌtaɪ /; / ˈtaʊˌtaɪ / (BrE)
a former Chinese official who governed a region or city, especially during the Qing dynasty (often involved in administration and foreign trade in treaty ports)

Examples

The taotai official
The taotai played a crucial role in managing the affairs of the region.
During the Qing dynasty, a taotai was responsible for collecting taxes and maintaining order.
Foreign diplomats often negotiated with the local taotai when visiting Chinese provinces.
The taotai played a crucial role in negotiating trade agreements with foreign powers.
Merchants had to obtain permits from the taotai before conducting business in the port.
During the late Qing dynasty, the taotai managed both customs and diplomatic affairs in Shanghai.

Related Terms

  • synonym: governor - an official in charge of a region or province
  • synonym: magistrate - a civil officer who administers the law
