beep

verb
make a short, high-pitched sound
1.3 2.54/m
Pronunciation: / biÀêp / (AmE), / biÀêp / (BrE)
to make a short, loud, high-pitched sound, like those produced by horns or electronic devices

Examples

To beep the horn
As I waited at the crosswalk, a car behind me beeped impatiently.
She accidentally pressed a button, and the microwave suddenly beeped loudly.
The alarm clock beeped every morning at six o'clock, waking him up on time.

Synonyms

  • bleep
    4.4 0.23/m
    bleep is a single short electronic signal used as a warning or to censor, while beep is a more general short sound.
  • bing
    7.7 0.04/m
    bing is a short, bell-like electronic sound often used in apps or notifications, while beep is a more general short signal.
  • ding
    6.1 0.47/m
    ding is a clear, small bell sound that marks an event, while beep is broader and covers many short electronic or horn sounds.
  • toot
    3.3 0.34/m
    toot is a quick horn or whistle sound used for short signals or greetings, while beep covers both horns and devices.
  • ping
    4.8 0.27/m
    ping is a short sharp sound like a struck metal or sonar echo, while beep usually names simple device or horn sounds.
  • honk
    1.6 2.49/m
    honk is a loud, harsh car horn sound used to warn or get attention, while beep is shorter and higher-pitched.
