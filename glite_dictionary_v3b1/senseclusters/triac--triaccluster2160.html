triac

noun
bidirectional electronic switch
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌæk / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌæk / (BrE)
an electronic component that can control and switch current flow in both directions in a circuit

Examples

Triac circuit switch
The technician replaced the faulty triac to restore the dimmer switch's function.
A triac is often used in circuits that control the brightness of lights.
You can regulate the speed of a fan by adjusting the triac in the control circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thyristor - a semiconductor device used to switch and control electric current
  • antonym: transistor - a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals
