analytical

adjective
necessarily true by meaning, not by fact
6.4
Pronunciation: / əˌnælɪˈtɪkəl / (AmE), / əˌnælɪˈtɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a proposition) necessarily true by virtue of its meaning, independent of fact or experience.

Examples

Analytical statement
Distinguishing between analytical and synthetic judgments was central to Kant's philosophy.
In philosophy, an analytical proposition is one that is true by definition alone.
The statement 'All bachelors are unmarried' is analytical because the predicate is contained in the subject.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synthetic - (of a proposition) not necessarily true by meaning, but by how it relates to experience or fact
