Dibranchia

noun
group of cephalopods excluding nautiluses
Pronunciation: / daɪˈbræŋkiə / (AmE), / daɪˈbraŋkɪə /; / daɪˈbræŋkɪə / (BrE)
a group of cephalopods (sea animals) that includes octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish, but not nautiluses

Examples

Dibranchia sea animals
Cephalopods in the Dibranchia group are known for their advanced nervous systems.
Modern squids and octopuses are classified under the subclass Dibranchia.
Unlike nautiluses, all members of Dibranchia possess two gills.
Many marine biologists study the locomotion of Dibranchia in order to understand their unique adaptations.
The Dibranchia contain most of the well-known cephalopods, such as octopuses and squids.
Unlike the nautiluses, members of Dibranchia possess only two gills.
