Examples

Plans go up in smoke
After the company lost its biggest client, all their expansion plans went up in smoke.

When the deal fell through, their hopes for a quick profit went up in smoke.

Synonyms

  • end in smoke
    8.1 0.00/m
    end in smoke is a plain phrase for complete failure in plans or efforts, while go up in smoke sounds more visual and sudden.
  • flame out
    7.5 0.40/m
    flame out often means a fast, dramatic failure after a strong start, while go up in smoke can mean any sudden total loss.
  • go in the toilet
    7.9 0.01/m
    go in the toilet is crude and informal for plans ruined, while go up in smoke is more visual and common in neutral speech.
