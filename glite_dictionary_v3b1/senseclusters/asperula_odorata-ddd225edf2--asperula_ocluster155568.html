Asperula odorata

noun

A perennial plant of the bedstraw family, native to Europe and Asia, with fragrant white flowers and narrow leaves, often used as flavoring or in sachets and cultivated as a ground cover; also known as sweet woodruff.

Examples

Asperula odorata plant
Because of its dense growth, Asperula odorata is often planted as a ground cover in shady gardens.
Chefs sometimes use Asperula odorata to flavor traditional German May wine.
The garden was lined with Asperula odorata, giving off a sweet scent in the spring.
