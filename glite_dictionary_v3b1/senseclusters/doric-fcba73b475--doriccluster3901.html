Doric

adjective
relating to Doric Greek architecture
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔr.ɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɒr.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Doric order, the simplest and sturdiest of the classical orders of ancient Greek architecture, characterized by plain columns without bases

Examples

Doric column
Many public buildings in the 19th century were designed in the Doric style.
The ancient temple featured impressive Doric columns along its entrance.
The architect explained the differences between Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian columns.

  • degree higher: Ionic - relating to the Ionic order of classical architecture, known for its scroll-like capitals
