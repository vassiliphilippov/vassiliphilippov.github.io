Ishmael

proper noun
son of Abraham in the Old Testament
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɪʃˈmeɪəl / (AmE), / ˈɪʃmeɪl / (BrE)
In the Old Testament, the son of Abraham who was cast out after the birth of Isaac, traditionally regarded as the ancestor of 12 Arabian tribes.

Examples

Biblical Ishmael
Ishmael is considered a patriarch by many Arab peoples.
Many historians discuss the legacy of Ishmael in the context of Middle Eastern ancestry.
The biblical story of Ishmael highlights themes of exile and survival.
