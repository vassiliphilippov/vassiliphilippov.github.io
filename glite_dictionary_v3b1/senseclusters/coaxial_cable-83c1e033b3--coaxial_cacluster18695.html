coaxial cable

noun
high-frequency signal transmission cable
Pronunciation: / koʊˈæksiəl ˈkeɪbəl / (AmE), / kəʊˈæksiəl ˈkeɪbəl / (BrE)
a type of electrical cable with an inner conductor surrounded by a concentric insulating layer and shield, used to transmit high-frequency signals with low loss

Examples

A coaxial cable wire
He replaced the old wire with a new coaxial cable to improve the sound system.
If you want to reduce signal loss, use a high-quality coaxial cable for your network setup.
The television was connected to the antenna with a coaxial cable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: optical fiber - thin flexible fiber used to transmit light signals for communication
