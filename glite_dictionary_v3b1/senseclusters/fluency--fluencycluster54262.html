fluency

noun
ability to speak or write smoothly
1.8 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfluː.ən.siː/ /; / /ˈfluː.ən.sɪ/ (AmE), /ˈfluː.ən.siː/ /; / /ˈfluː.ən.sɪ/ (BrE)
the ability to express yourself easily and smoothly in speech or writing, especially in a language

Examples

Fluency in English
His fluency in writing allows him to communicate ideas clearly and persuasively.
Reading books in English helped improve her fluency in the language.
She speaks Spanish with remarkable fluency after living there for only a year.
Children often achieve greater fluency in a new language than adults do.
Her fluency in Spanish impressed everyone at the conference.
Public speakers need both confidence and fluency to engage their audiences effectively.
Achieving fluency in academic writing takes practice and a strong grasp of vocabulary.
He spoke with a natural fluency that made his speech captivating and memorable.
Her fluency during the debate impressed the judges and the audience alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: articulacy - the ability to express oneself clearly and effectively
  • synonym: eloquence - fluent or persuasive speaking or writing
  • antonym: haltingness - lack of fluency; awkward or hesitant speech
  • antonym: hesitancy - the quality of being hesitant or uncertain in speech or writing
  • antonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or showing uncertainty while speaking or writing
  • synonym: proficiency - advanced skill or competence in a subject
