potch

noun
dull opal material
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɑtʃ / (AmE), / pɒtʃ / (BrE)
common opal or clay material lacking the color and brilliance of precious opal, especially in gemology or ceramics

Examples

Common potch opal
The miner separated the valuable opal from the surrounding potch.
Most of the rock was just potch, with only a small section containing precious opal.
Ceramic artists sometimes use potch as a base material for their glazes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clay - a natural earthy material used in ceramics
  • antonym: opal - a precious gemstone known for its play of color
