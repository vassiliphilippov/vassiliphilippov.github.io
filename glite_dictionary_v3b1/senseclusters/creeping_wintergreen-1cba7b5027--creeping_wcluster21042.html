creeping wintergreen

noun
a North American aromatic shrub with spicy red berries
5.7 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkriː.pɪŋ ˈwɪn.tər.griːn/ (AmE), /ˈkriː.pɪŋ ˈwɪn.tə.griːn/ (BrE)
A low, spreading shrub native to eastern North America, with shiny aromatic leaves, white bell-shaped flowers, and spicy red berries, often used for its wintergreen oil.

Examples

creeping wintergreen plant
Gardeners value creeping wintergreen for its attractive red berries and evergreen foliage.
In traditional medicine, creeping wintergreen was used to make soothing teas.
The creeping wintergreen produces a refreshing aroma when its leaves are crushed.
