chariot race

noun
race with ancient horse-drawn chariots
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæriət reɪs / (AmE), / ˈtʃæriət reɪs / (BrE)
a race between ancient horse-drawn chariots, popular in classical antiquity, especially as a sport in ancient Greece and Rome

Examples

An ancient chariot race
Archaeologists found evidence of a chariot race etched on a fragment of pottery.
Ben-Hur won the most memorable chariot race in literature and film history.
The ancient Romans were famous for organizing thrilling chariot races in the Circus Maximus.
