treasonous

adjective
having the qualities of a traitor
8.5 0.85/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtriÀêz…ôn…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàtriÀêz…ôn…ôs / (BrE)
Having the character or qualities of a traitor; involving or constituting treason.

Examples

Treasonous acts
Historians debated whether the conspiracy was truly treasonous or merely misguided dissent.
Many were shocked by the senator's treasonous statements against the government.
The general's treasonous actions led to his immediate arrest and court-martial.

Synonyms

  • treasonable
    9.1 0.11/m
    treasonable is a formal legal word for acts that betray a country, while treasonous is the common adjective for such betrayal.
  • traitorous
    8.3 0.65/m
    traitorous is a broader word for betraying people or groups, while treasonous focuses on betrayal of a country or state.
