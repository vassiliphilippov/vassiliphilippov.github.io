ring rot

noun
bacterial plant disease affecting crops
5.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /rɪŋ rɑːt/ (AmE), /rɪŋ rɒt/ (BrE)
A bacterial disease that affects tomatoes, potatoes, tobacco, and other plants, causing decay and a characteristic ring of rot in the stems or tubers.

Examples

Ring rot disease
Experts warned that ring rot could devastate tomato fields if not controlled.
Proper sanitation can help prevent the spread of ring rot among susceptible plants.
The farmer lost much of his potato crop to ring rot last year.
