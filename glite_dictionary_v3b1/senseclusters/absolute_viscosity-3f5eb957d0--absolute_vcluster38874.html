absolute viscosity

noun
resistance of a fluid to flow under force
7.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà√¶bs…ôÀåluÀêt v…™Ààsk…ís…™ti / (AmE), / Àà√¶bs…ôluÀêt v…™sÀàk…ís…™ti / (BrE)
the measure of a fluid's resistance to flow when subjected to an applied force, expressed as the ratio of shear stress to shear rate

Examples

Absolute viscosity measurement
Chemists measure absolute viscosity using a viscometer in the laboratory.
Engine oils are often rated by their absolute viscosity at different temperatures.
When the absolute viscosity of a liquid is high, it flows very slowly.

Synonyms

  • dynamic viscosity
    7.4 0.00/m
    dynamic viscosity names the fluid's internal friction used in textbooks and labs, while absolute viscosity is a less common alternate term.
  • coefficient of viscosity
    9.0 0.00/m
    coefficient of viscosity is used as the numerical factor in formulas and engineering data, while absolute viscosity names the same property more plainly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dynamic viscosity - the same concept as absolute viscosity; another term
  • degree lower: kinematic viscosity - measure of a fluid's resistance to flow considering density
