pyocyanase

noun
antibiotic mixture from Pseudomonas bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪoʊˈsaɪəˌneɪs / (AmE), / ˌpʌɪə(ʊ)ˈsʌɪəneɪs / (BrE)
a yellow-green mixture of antibiotics produced by the bacterium Pseudomonas aeruginosa, formerly used to treat infections

Examples

Pyocyanase antibiotic mixture
Pyocyanase has largely been replaced by modern antibiotics in clinical practice.
Early 20th-century doctors experimented with pyocyanase as a treatment for bacterial infections.
The laboratory grew a strain of bacteria that naturally produced pyocyanase.
