corded

adjective
with a cord attached
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrdɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdɪd / (BrE)
having a cord or wire attached, especially for power or connection

Examples

Corded power tool
He bought a corded drill because it provides continuous power for long projects.
Many people still use corded headphones for better sound quality.
The old office had only corded telephones on every desk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corded phone - a telephone with a cord connecting the handset to the base
  • antonym: wireless - not having a cord or wire; operating without physical connection
