sticking point

noun
obstacle causing a deadlock
7.1 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈstɪk.ɪŋ pɔɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈstɪk.ɪŋ pɔɪnt/ (BrE)
an issue or detail in discussions or negotiations that causes disagreement and prevents progress

Examples

A sticking point in talks
His refusal to compromise on working hours proved to be a sticking point for the team.
The question of funding became a major sticking point during the negotiations.
They managed to resolve every sticking point and finally signed the agreement.
One major sticking point was who would take responsibility for future costs.
The salary increase became a sticking point in the contract negotiations.
Their partnership broke down due to a sticking point over project deadlines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - consensus or resolution in negotiation
  • synonym: deal breaker - an issue that prevents agreement being reached
  • synonym: impasse - a situation where no progress is possible
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress
