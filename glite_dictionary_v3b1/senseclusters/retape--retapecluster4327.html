retape

verb
to tape again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈteɪp / (AmE), / ˌriːˈteɪp / (BrE)
to apply tape again to something in order to cover, fasten, or secure it

Examples

To retape a box
After checking the box, she decided to retape it to make sure it was secure.
You should retape the wires if the original tape has come loose.
The coach asked the player to retape his ankle before returning to the game.
After the box opened during shipping, they had to retape it before sending it again.
The athlete retaped his ankle to make sure it was properly supported before the game.
She noticed the wrapping was loose and decided to retape the present.

  • antonym: untape - to remove tape from something
