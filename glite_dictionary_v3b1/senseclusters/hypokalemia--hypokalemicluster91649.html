hypokalemia

noun
abnormally low blood potassium level
6.3 7.5
Pronunciation: /haɪ.poʊ.kə.ˈliː.miː.ə/ (AmE), /haɪ.pəʊ.kə.ˈliː.miː.ə/ (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by an abnormally low level of potassium in the blood, which may cause weakness or heart problems

Examples

Low hypokalemia level
Doctors carefully monitored her potassium levels to prevent hypokalemia during her treatment.
Prolonged use of certain diuretics may result in hypokalemia and increase the risk of muscle cramps.
The patient was admitted to hospital with severe hypokalemia after experiencing ongoing vomiting and diarrhea.
