coniferous

adjective
bearing cones and evergreen leaves
7.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɪf.ɚ.əs / (AmE), / kəˈnɪf.ər.əs / (BrE)
(of a tree or shrub) bearing cones and usually having evergreen leaves.

Examples

Coniferous tree leaves
Certain birds nest high in coniferous branches where they are sheltered by thick needles.
Many forests in northern climates are dominated by coniferous trees like pines and spruces.
The coniferous woodland remained green throughout winter, providing a stark contrast to the snowy ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deciduous - (of a tree or shrub) shedding its leaves annually
