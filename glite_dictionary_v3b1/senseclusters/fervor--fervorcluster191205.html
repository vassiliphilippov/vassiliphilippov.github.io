fervor

noun
intense passion or enthusiasm
6.3 3.26/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…úÀêrv…ôr /; / Ààf…úrv…ôr / (AmE), / Ààf…úÀêv…ô / (BrE)
intense and passionate feeling; great enthusiasm or excitement

Examples

Religious fervor
Religious fervor can inspire people to extraordinary acts.
She spoke with such fervor that everyone listened intently.
The fervor of the crowd at the concert was electrifying.
Debates on social media are often marked by unexpected fervor.
Her speech was delivered with such fervor that the audience gave a standing ovation.
The team played with great fervor in the final minutes of the match.
Political debates often inspire intense fervour among supporters.
She spoke about her beliefs with such fervour that everyone listened intently.
The crowd cheered with great fervour as the hero took the stage.

Synonyms

  • passion
    2.5 20/m
    passion is a very strong feeling that drives people to act, while fervor is intense, focused enthusiasm.
  • ardor
    7.5 1.05/m
    ardor is a warm, often lasting energy for someone or something, while fervor is a burning, urgent enthusiasm.
  • zealousness
    5.6 0.08/m
    zealousness means eager and active support for a cause, while fervor is strong feeling that may be less organized.
  • enthusiasm
    2.2 24/m
    enthusiasm is lively interest and joy for an activity, while fervor is deeper passion that often feels more intense.
  • excitement
    1.0 32/m
    excitement is a short, lively feeling of eagerness or joy, while fervor is lasting, intense passion for something.
  • gusto
    5.2 1.63/m
    gusto is lively, vigorous enjoyment of an activity, while fervor is deep emotional passion and intense commitment.
