clear-cutting

verb
to remove all trees
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪr ˌkʌtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪə ˌkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
to cut down all the trees in a particular area at the same time, usually for logging purposes

Examples

To clear-cut a forest
The company was fined for clear-cutting large sections of the protected forest.
Environmentalists protested against clear-cutting because it destroys animal habitats.
After clear-cutting the land, the hillside was left exposed to erosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deforest - to remove trees from an area of land
  • antonym: reforest - to plant trees in an area where there were none before
