maul oak

noun
oak tree native to southwestern North America
Pronunciation: / mɔl oʊk / (AmE), / mɔːl əʊk / (BrE)
A medium-sized evergreen tree of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, with oblong, leathery, often spiny-edged leaves.

Examples

The maul oak tree
Botanists studied the maul oak to understand its adaptation to drought conditions.
The maul oak thrives in arid regions where few other trees can survive.
We spotted a maul oak along the trail, its distinctive leaves easy to recognize.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oak - a tree of the genus Quercus, known for its acorns and strong wood
