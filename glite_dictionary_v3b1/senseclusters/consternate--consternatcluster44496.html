consternate

verb
to fill with anxiety or dismay
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.stɚ.neɪt /; / ˈkɑnstərˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stə.neɪt /; / ˈkɒnstəˌneɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel sudden anxiety, confusion, or dismay

Examples

Loud noises consternated crowd

News of the sudden layoffs consternated everyone in the office.
The unexpected outcome seemed to consternate even the most experienced analysts.
The unexpected announcement consternated everyone in the office.
She was consternated by the sudden change in his attitude.
The news of the cancellation consternated the entire team.
