Haphtarah

noun
Jewish prophetic scripture reading
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɑfˈtɑrə / (AmE), / hæfˈtɑːrə / (BrE)
A short passage from the Prophets read in a Jewish synagogue service on the Sabbath and certain festivals, following the Torah reading.

Examples

The Haphtarah reading
Each Sabbath, a different Haphtarah is chosen to reflect the theme of the Torah reading.
The child practiced reciting the Haphtarah for his bar mitzvah.
The rabbi read the Haphtarah after the Torah portion was completed.
