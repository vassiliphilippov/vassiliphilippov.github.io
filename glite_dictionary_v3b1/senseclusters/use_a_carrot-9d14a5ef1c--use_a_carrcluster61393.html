use a carrot

verb
offer an incentive as motivation
Pronunciation: / juːz ə ˈkærət / (AmE), / juːz ə ˈkærət / (BrE)
to offer a reward or incentive in order to persuade someone to do something

Examples

To use a carrot to motivate
In order to boost productivity, the manager decided to use a carrot instead of threats.
Rather than resorting to punishment, she used a carrot to get her kids to do their homework.
The principal used a carrot by promising extra recess time if students behaved well.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carrot and stick - a system of both rewards and punishments to induce behavior
