Examples

Synonyms

  • round-eyed
    4.8 0.06/m
    round-eyed shows large, round, open eyes that suggest innocence or wonder, while wide-eyed is a general term for surprised open eyes.
  • saucer-eyed
    7.2 0.02/m
    saucer-eyed describes very wide, round eyes that show surprise or amazement, while wide-eyed can also mean simple childlike wonder.
  • goggle-eyed
    7.6 0.10/m
    goggle-eyed means eyes that appear large or protruding from surprise or fear, while wide-eyed mainly suggests open, amazed innocence.
  • popeyed
    8.6 0.01/m
    popeyed describes bulging, very wide eyes often used in casual speech to show shock, while wide-eyed is less crude and more general.
