afterword

noun
book's closing section
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæftərˌwɝːd / (AmE), / ˈɑːftəwɜːd / (BrE)
a section at the end of a book in which the author or another person adds final comments or information

Examples

The afterword section
The afterword explained the historical context behind the novel's events.
In the afterword, the author thanked everyone who supported the project.
Readers found the afterword helpful for understanding the book's main themes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epilogue - a concluding section at the end of a literary work
  • antonym: foreword - an introductory section at the beginning of a book
