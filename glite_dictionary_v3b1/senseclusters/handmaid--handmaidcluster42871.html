handmaid

noun
subordinate or assistant
1.8 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌmeɪd / (AmE), / ˈhændˌmeɪd / (BrE)
A person or thing that serves in a subordinate or supportive role; an assistant or subordinate to another.

Examples

Handmaid of progress
Education is often considered the handmaid of progress in modern societies.
In the report, technology is described as a handmaid to the advancement of medical care.
She argued that philosophy should not merely be a handmaid to science, but an independent pursuit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: master - a person with authority over others, especially a servant
  • synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others, especially a person employed in a house on domestic duties or as a personal attendant
  • synonym: subordinate - a person under the authority or control of another
