upspeak

noun
rising intonation speech
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌspiːk / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌspiːk / (BrE)
a way of speaking in which statements end with a rising intonation, making them sound like questions

Examples

A hint of upspeak
Some people find upspeak makes the speaker sound less confident.
Linguists have studied the rise of upspeak in young adults' speech.
Her use of upspeak confused listeners about whether she was asking a question.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative intonation - a way of speaking where statements end with a falling tone
  • synonym: uptalk - a speaking style where statements have a rising intonation
