(of a ship, boat, or similar object) to sink or become submerged, especially in water.

Examples

The ship goes down
During the storm, we watched as the lifeboat seemed to go down in the rough sea.
Many fish scatter when the sun starts to go down behind the hills, but some linger.
The old ship began to go down after hitting the iceberg.

Synonyms

  • sink
    1.0 15/m
    sink means moving below the water surface until covered, while go down describes a ship or boat becoming submerged.
  • go under
    4.4 0.65/m
    go under means sinking fully beneath the water surface, while go down is a general phrase for a vessel becoming submerged.
  • submerge
    3.9 4.19/m
    submerge means to put or move completely under water, while go down focuses on a ship sinking or becoming submerged.
