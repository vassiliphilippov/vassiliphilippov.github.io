natural logarithm

noun
logarithm with base e
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃrəl ˈlɑɡərɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl ˈlɒɡərɪðəm / (BrE)
the logarithm to the base e (approximately 2.718), often used in mathematics and sciences

Examples

The natural logarithm function
In calculus, the natural logarithm commonly appears when solving integrals.
She learned how to use the natural logarithm function on her calculator.
The natural logarithm of 1 is always zero.
