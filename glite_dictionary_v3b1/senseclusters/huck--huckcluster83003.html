huck

noun
coarse absorbent cotton or linen fabric
Pronunciation: / hʌk / (AmE), / hʌk / (BrE)
A type of coarse, absorbent cotton or linen fabric, often used for toweling and cleaning cloths.

Examples

Huck towel
Hotel rooms are often supplied with thick huck towels for guests to use.
She preferred cleaning the windows with a huck cloth because of its absorbency.
The linen closet was filled with old huck towels that had stood the test of time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: huckaback - a loosely woven cotton or linen fabric used for towels
