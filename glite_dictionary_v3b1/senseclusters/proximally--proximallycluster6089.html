proximally

adverb
closer to center
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑksɪməli / (AmE), / ˈprɒksɪməli / (BrE)
in a position closer to the center of the body or point of attachment, especially in anatomy

Examples

Proximally on the bone
The fracture was located proximally on the femur, near the hip joint.
Muscles that attach proximally tend to provide greater stability to the limb.
The surgeon made an incision proximally to the affected area to access the artery.
