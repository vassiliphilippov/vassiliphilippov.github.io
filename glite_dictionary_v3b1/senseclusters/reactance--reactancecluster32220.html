reactance

noun
opposition to AC current from inductance and capacitance
6.8 7.9
Pronunciation: / riˈæktəns / (AmE), / riˈæktəns / (BrE)
the opposition that inductance and capacitance provide to the flow of alternating electric current, measured in ohms

Examples

Electrical reactance
Capacitive reactance decreases as the frequency of the AC supply rises.
In this circuit, both resistance and reactance must be considered to calculate total impedance.
The inductor's reactance increases with frequency, making it harder for current to pass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resistance - the opposition to current flow in an electrical circuit, caused by resistive elements
