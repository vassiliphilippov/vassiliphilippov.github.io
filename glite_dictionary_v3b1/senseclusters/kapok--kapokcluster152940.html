kapok

noun
plant fiber used for stuffing
8.4 9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪˌpɑk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpɒk / (BrE)
A silky fiber from the seed pods of the kapok tree, used for stuffing mattresses, pillows, and for insulation.

Examples

Kapok fiber stuffing
After harvesting, the kapok fibers are cleaned and sorted for use in bedding.
Pillows filled with kapok are known for being light and comfortable.
The old chair was stuffed with kapok instead of modern foam.
