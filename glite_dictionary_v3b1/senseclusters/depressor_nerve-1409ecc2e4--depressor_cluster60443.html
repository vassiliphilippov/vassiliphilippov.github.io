depressor nerve

noun
nerve that decreases activity/tone of a part
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛsər nɝːv / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsə nɜːv / (BrE)
a nerve whose action reduces the activity or tone of the body part it supplies

Examples

Depressor nerve function
Damage to the depressor nerve can result in increased muscle tone in the affected area.
Researchers studied the function of a newly discovered depressor nerve in the digestive system.
The depressor nerve plays a key role in lowering the heart rate during rest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: motor nerve - a nerve that stimulates muscle contraction
