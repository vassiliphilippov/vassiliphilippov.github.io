squeeze out

verb
obtain with difficulty
3.3
Pronunciation: / skwiz aʊt / (AmE), / skwiːz aʊt / (BrE)
to obtain or produce something with difficulty or great effort

Examples

To squeeze out the truth
After much questioning, he finally squeezed out the information needed for his report.
It took hours to squeeze out the truth from the reluctant witness.
She managed to squeeze out a few more dollars from her tight budget.
Despite limited funding, the scientists squeezed out some impressive results from their experiments.
He squeezes out enough money each month to pay the rent and utilities.
She managed to squeeze out a meager living by selling handmade jewelry.
