bank note

noun
paper money; currency note
Pronunciation: / ˈbæŋk ˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈbæŋk nəʊt / (BrE)
A piece of paper money issued by a central bank, used as legal tender.

Examples

A bank note
He carefully examined the bank note for signs of counterfeiting before accepting it.
She withdrew a crisp fifty-dollar bank note from the ATM.
The collector displayed rare bank notes from around the world in his album.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bill - A piece of paper money, chiefly in American English
  • antonym: coin - A flat, typically round piece of metal used as money
