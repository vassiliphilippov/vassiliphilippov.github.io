creakiness

noun
quality of creaking
Pronunciation: / ˈkriːkinəs / (AmE), / ˈkriːkinəs / (BrE)
the quality of making a creaking sound or being old, stiff, or worn out

Examples

The creakiness of floor
The creakiness of the wooden floor made it impossible to sneak around quietly.
He complained about the creakiness in his knees after a long hike.
Despite its creakiness, the old rocking chair was still comfortable to sit in.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of being even and free from roughness or noise
  • synonym: stiffness - the quality of being rigid or difficult to move
