home ground

noun
natural environment; habitat; familiar setting
3.0 3.6
Pronunciation: / hoʊm ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / həʊm ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a place or environment where someone or a group feels most comfortable or is most familiar, especially the regular field or stadium for a sports team

Examples

The home ground advantage
She always performs best on her home ground where she feels confident.
The rainforest is the home ground of countless unique species.
The wolves returned to their home ground after several years away.
Our team rarely loses a match on its home ground.
She always feels more confident performing on her home ground.
The visiting players seemed nervous on the unfamiliar home ground of their opponents.
