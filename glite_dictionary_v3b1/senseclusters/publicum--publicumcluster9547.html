publicum

noun
public property (Latin)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpʌ.blɪ.kəm/ /; / /ˈpuː.blɪ.kəm/ (AmE), /ˈpʌ.blɪ.kəm/ /; / /ˈpuː.blɪ.kəm/ (BrE)
a Latin term referring to public property or matters belonging to the state, especially in legal contexts

Examples

Publicum land
In Roman law, publicum referred to land or resources owned by the state.
The river was considered publicum, meaning it was accessible to all citizens.
Legal scholars often discuss the distinction between publicum and private property in ancient texts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: private property - property owned by individuals rather than the state
  • synonym: res publica - Latin for 'the public thing', referring to the state or commonwealth
