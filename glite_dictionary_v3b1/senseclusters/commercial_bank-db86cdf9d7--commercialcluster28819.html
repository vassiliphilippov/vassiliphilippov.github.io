commercial bank

noun
bank serving public with deposits and loans
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɝːʃəl bæŋk / (AmE), / kəˈmɜːʃəl bæŋk / (BrE)
a bank that accepts deposits from the public, makes loans, and provides related financial services to individuals and businesses

Examples

A commercial bank account
I opened a savings account at a local commercial bank.
Many commercial banks now offer online banking services to their customers.
The commercial bank approved his loan application within a week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: investment bank - a bank specializing in large and complex financial transactions
  • synonym: retail bank - a bank that provides services to individual consumers rather than companies
