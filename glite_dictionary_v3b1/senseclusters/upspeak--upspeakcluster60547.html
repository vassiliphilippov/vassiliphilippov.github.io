upspeak

verb
to use rising intonation
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌspiːk / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌspiːk / (BrE)
to speak with a rising intonation at the end of statements, making them sound like questions.

Examples

To upspeak nervously
Some people upspeak when they are nervous or unsure about what they are saying.
Her habit to upspeak sometimes makes her statements sound like questions.
Teachers often advise students not to upspeak during presentations to sound more confident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declare - to state something firmly and clearly
  • synonym: uptalk - to use a rising intonation at the end of statements
