enjoin

verb
to legally order (especially via injunction)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to order or command someone to do or not do something with authority

Examples

To enjoin a rule
A federal judge can enjoin the enforcement of the disputed law while the case proceeds.
The city was enjoined from proceeding with the demolition until further notice.
The court decided to enjoin the company from dumping waste into the river.
Her doctor enjoined her to get more rest and exercise regularly.
The court enjoined the company to stop using the trademark.
The manager enjoined his staff to complete the report by Friday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command - to give an authoritative order
  • antonym: forbid - to order not to do something
  • synonym: order - to instruct or command someone to do something
  • antonym: permit - to allow or authorize
  • synonym: prohibit - to formally forbid something by law or authority
