gothic

adjective
gloomy and mysterious; grotesque style
2.5 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
(of writing, buildings, or art) characterized by an atmosphere of gloom, mystery, and the grotesque, especially as in certain novels or films.

Examples

Gothic novel
His paintings are known for their gothic themes and unsettling imagery.
Mary Shelley's 'Frankenstein' is a classic example of a gothic novel.
The old mansion had a gothic atmosphere, filled with shadows and strange noises.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macabre - gruesome; having a grim or ghastly atmosphere
  • antonym: romantic - relating to a literary and artistic movement emphasizing emotion and individualism; sometimes contrasted with gothic
