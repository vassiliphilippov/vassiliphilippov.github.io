attrition

noun
sorrow for sin out of fear of punishment (theology)
8.2
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / əˈtrɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) sorrow for sin arising chiefly from fear of divine punishment rather than from a pure motive of love of God.

Examples

Attrition for forgiveness
In the confession, she was told that attrition is sufficient for forgiveness when accompanied by the sacrament.
Many theologians have debated whether attrition truly leads to spiritual transformation.
The priest explained that attrition differs from true contrition because it arises from fear rather than love.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: contrition - sincere remorse for sin out of love for God
