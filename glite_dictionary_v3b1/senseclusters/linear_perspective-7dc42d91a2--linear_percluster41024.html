linear perspective

noun
technique for creating depth by size and distance
3.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪn.ɪ.ər pər.ˈspek.tɪv/ /; / /ˈliː.ni.ər pər.ˈspek.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈlɪn.ɪ.ə pə.ˈspek.tɪv/ /; / /ˈliː.ni.ə pə.ˈspek.tɪv/ (BrE)
a technique in drawing and painting that creates the illusion of depth and distance by depicting objects smaller as they become farther from the viewer

Examples

Linear perspective in art
In art class, we learned how linear perspective makes distant buildings appear smaller than those close to us.
Renaissance artists perfected the use of linear perspective to achieve realistic depth in their paintings.
The careful application of linear perspective creates a convincing three-dimensional effect on a flat canvas.

  • synonym: perspective - a method of representing objects in three dimensions on a two-dimensional surface
