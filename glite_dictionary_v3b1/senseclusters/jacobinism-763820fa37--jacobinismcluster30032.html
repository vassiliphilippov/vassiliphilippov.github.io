Jacobinism

noun
radical ideology of French Revolution Jacobins
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒækəbɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒækəbɪnɪzəm / (BrE)
The political principles and practices associated with the radical Jacobins during the French Revolution, especially their advocacy of revolutionary change and use of terror.

Examples

Jacobinism during revolution
Many historians debate whether Jacobinism was necessary to defend the revolution from its enemies.
Some critics warn that modern movements risk sliding into a new form of Jacobinism.
The harsh methods endorsed by Jacobinism left a lasting mark on French political culture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: moderation - the avoidance of excess or extremes, especially in political opinions
  • synonym: radicalism - the beliefs or actions of people who advocate thorough or complete political or social reform
