distomatosis

noun
liver disease caused by flukes
Pronunciation: / dɪˌstoʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / dɪˌstɒməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease of the liver in animals or humans caused by infection with liver flukes, especially affecting sheep and cattle

Examples

Distomatosis in cattle
Cattle affected by distomatosis may show weight loss and decreased milk production.
Proper management of wet pastures can help prevent distomatosis in livestock herds.
The veterinarian confirmed that the sheep had distomatosis after examining the liver lesions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fascioliasis - infection caused by liver flukes, especially in humans
