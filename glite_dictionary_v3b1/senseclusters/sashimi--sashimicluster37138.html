sashimi

noun
thinly sliced raw fish (Japanese dish)
Pronunciation: / sɑːˈʃiːmi / (AmE), / sæˈʃiːmi / (BrE)
A Japanese dish consisting of very thinly sliced raw fish, often served with soy sauce and condiments.

Examples

A sashimi platter
At the Japanese restaurant, we ordered a platter of assorted sashimi.
Many people enjoy sashimi for its fresh flavor and delicate texture.
The chef carefully prepared sashimi using the freshest tuna available.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sushi - a Japanese dish of vinegared rice with various fillings and toppings, often including raw fish
