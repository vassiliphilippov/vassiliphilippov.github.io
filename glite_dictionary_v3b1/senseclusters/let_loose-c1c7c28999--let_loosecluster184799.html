let loose

verb
release; set free
Pronunciation: / lɛt lus / (AmE), / lɛt luːs / (BrE)
to release or set free from restraint or confinement

Examples

Let loose the dogs
During the celebration, the organizers let loose hundreds of balloons into the sky.
She accidentally let loose her secrets during the conversation.
The scientist warned that the new virus could let loose a series of unpredictable consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: release - to allow something to move freely, to let go
  • antonym: restrain - to hold back or keep under control
