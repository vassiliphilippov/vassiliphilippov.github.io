AC

noun
alternating electric current
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪˈsi / (AmE), / ˌeɪˈsiː / (BrE)
(abbreviation) An electric current that reverses direction periodically, typically in a sinusoidal pattern; alternating current.

Examples

AC electric current
Most household appliances in the U.S. operate on AC provided by the electrical grid.

The laboratory experiment compared the effects of AC and DC on different circuits.
