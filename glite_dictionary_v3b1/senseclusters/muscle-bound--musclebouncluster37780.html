muscle-bound

adjective
having overly developed, stiff muscles
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌsəl ˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈmʌsəl ˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
Having unusually large and stiff muscles, typically as a result of excessive exercise, which can limit flexibility or movement.

Examples

Muscle-bound arms
His muscle-bound arms made it difficult for him to put on a fitted jacket.
She feared becoming muscle-bound if she focused only on strength training and not on stretching.
The muscle-bound athlete struggled to touch his toes during the warm-up.

Related Terms

  • antonym: limber - able to move and bend easily
  • synonym: muscular - having well-developed muscles
