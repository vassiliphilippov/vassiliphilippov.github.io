a mouthful

noun
long or hard-to-say word or phrase
Pronunciation: / ə ˈmaʊθfəl / (AmE), / ə ˈmaʊθfʊl / (BrE)
a word or phrase that is long or difficult to pronounce or say

Examples

Say a mouthful
She hesitated before attempting a mouthful like 'antidisestablishmentarianism.'
That technical term is really a mouthful to say quickly.
The town's name was a mouthful for visitors unfamiliar with the language.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short - not long or elaborate
  • synonym: tongue-twister - a phrase difficult to articulate clearly
