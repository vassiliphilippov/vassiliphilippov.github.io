Anglo-French language

noun
historical form of French used in medieval England
Pronunciation: / ˌæŋɡloʊ ˈfrɛntʃ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌaŋɡləʊ ˈfrɛntʃ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the variety of the French (Norman) language spoken and used for legal and administrative purposes in medieval England

Examples

The Anglo-French language in England
After the Norman Conquest, the Anglo-French language became the language of the English court and administration.
Legal documents in medieval England were often written in Anglo-French language.
The influence of the Anglo-French language can still be seen in modern English legal terms.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Middle English - form of English spoken during the same period, often influenced by Anglo-French
  • synonym: Norman French - the variety of Old French spoken in Normandy, closely related historical dialect
