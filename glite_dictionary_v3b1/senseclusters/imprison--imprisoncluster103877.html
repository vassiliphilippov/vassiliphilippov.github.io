imprison

verb
confine or put in prison
1.8 3.4
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɪzən / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɪz(ə)n /; / ɪmˈprɪzən / (BrE)
to put someone in prison or keep them confined, especially as a punishment or by force

Examples

To imprison a criminal

The activists were unfairly imprisoned for speaking out against the regime.
The government decided to imprison the rebel leader after his capture.
