Synonyms

  • incarcerate
    8.4 0.68/m
    incarcerate is a formal legal word for putting someone in prison, while imprison is more general and used in many contexts.
  • put behind bars
    4.3 0.64/m
    put behind bars is an informal phrase that talks about sending someone to prison, while imprison is neutral, formal and direct.
  • put away
    5.8 1.85/m
    put away often means locking someone up, sometimes in prison and sounds informal or vague, while imprison is formal and specific.
  • jail
    2.1 4.63/m
    jail usually means placing someone in a jail for short-term holding, while imprison implies formal long-term confinement.
  • lock up
    4.0 7.01/m
    lock up usually means putting someone somewhere secure to stop escape or keep safe, while imprison means sending them to prison.
