ophthalmoscope

noun
instrument for examining the eye's retina
2.9
Pronunciation: / ɑfˈθæl.məˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ɒfˈθal.mə.skəʊp / (BrE)
A medical instrument used by doctors to examine the interior of the eye, particularly the retina.

Examples

A ophthalmoscope device
Every ophthalmology clinic is equipped with at least one ophthalmoscope.
Students practiced examining each other's eyes with the ophthalmoscope during the medical workshop.
The doctor used an ophthalmoscope to check for any signs of damage to the patient's retina.
