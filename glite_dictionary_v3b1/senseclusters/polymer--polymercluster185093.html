polymer

noun
large molecule made of repeating units
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlɪmər / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪmə / (BrE)
a natural or synthetic substance composed of very large molecules, called macromolecules, made up of repeating structural units (monomers) joined together

Examples

A polymer chain
Plastics such as polyethylene are types of polymer made from petrochemicals.
Proteins are an example of a natural polymer found in living organisms.
The durability of the container is due to the strong polymer material used.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monomer - a molecule that can join with others to form a polymer
