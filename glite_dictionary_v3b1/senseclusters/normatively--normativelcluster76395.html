normatively

adverb
according to norms
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔːrmətɪvli / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is based on rules, standards, or what should be done (including moral principles)

Examples

Normatively required by rules
Normatively, all students are expected to follow the school's code of conduct.
The law was interpreted normatively to ensure fairness and equality.
Some cultures view certain behaviors normatively, while others see them as unusual.
The law is often interpreted normatively to guide how people should behave in society.
Educational policies are sometimes evaluated normatively rather than based on actual outcomes.
Philosophers discuss justice both normatively and descriptively in their writings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptively - in a way that describes rather than prescribes
  • synonym: prescriptively - in a way that sets rules or directions
