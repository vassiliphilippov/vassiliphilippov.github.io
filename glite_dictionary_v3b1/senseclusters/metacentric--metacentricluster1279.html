metacentric

adjective
relating to the metacenter
7.4 9.0
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈsen.trɪk/ (AmE), /me.tə.ˈsen.trɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or situated at the metacenter; having properties pertaining to the metacenter, especially in reference to stability in floating bodies or chromosome structure in biology.

Examples

Metacentric height
A ship with a high metacentric height is more stable but may roll uncomfortably.
Engineers ran calculations to determine the vessel's metacentric stability in rough waters.
The scientist described the chromosome as metacentric due to its centrally located centromere.
