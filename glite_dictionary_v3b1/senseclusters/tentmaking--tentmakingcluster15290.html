tentmaking

noun
self-supporting ministry
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛntˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛntˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of supporting oneself financially through secular work while engaged in religious or missionary activities

Examples

Modern tentmaking
Many missionaries use tentmaking to support themselves while serving in countries with limited resources.
Her tentmaking job as an engineer allowed her to continue her volunteer work in the community.
The church encourages tentmaking as a way for members to combine their careers with ministry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bivocational - having two jobs, especially combining ministry with secular work
