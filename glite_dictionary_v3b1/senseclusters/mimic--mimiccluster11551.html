mimic

verb
imitate, especially for effect
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪk / (BrE)
to imitate someone or their manner, especially in order to entertain or mock

Examples

To mimic a teacher
Children often mimic the gestures and expressions of adults around them.
She loved to mimic her teacher's voice to make her classmates laugh.
The comedian perfectly mimicked famous politicians during the show.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitate - to copy the behavior or actions of someone
  • synonym: mock - to imitate for the purpose of ridicule
