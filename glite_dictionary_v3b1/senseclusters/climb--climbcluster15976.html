climb

verb
slope or go upward (of a route)
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /klaɪm/ (AmE), /klaɪm/ (BrE)
to go or move upward, especially by using your hands and feet, or when a path or road goes up

Examples

To climb a mountain
From here, the footpath climbs up the eastern slope to the summit.
The road climbs gently towards the mountain pass.
The trail climbs steadily as it winds through the forest.
She plans to climb out of debt by saving more each month.
The sun began to climb above the horizon just after dawn.
They watched the balloon climb steadily into the sky.
Children love to climb on the jungle gym at the playground.
She had to climb over the rocks to reach the summit.
The cat managed to climb up the tall tree using its sharp claws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascend - to go up or move upward; rise
  • antonym: descend - to slope or go downward
