kibitka

noun
Russian covered wagon
9.9
Pronunciation: /kɪ.ˈbɪt.kə/ (AmE), /kɪ.ˈbɪt.kə/ (BrE)
a Russian covered wagon or carriage, often used for travel or to transport people (sometimes prisoners or exiles) across the steppe

Examples

A kibitka wagon
The family loaded their belongings into the kibitka before setting out on their journey.
During the harsh winter, travelers relied on a sturdy kibitka to cross the snowy plains.
In old Russian tales, a kibitka often appears as a symbol of nomadic life.
The guards escorted the prisoners to the waiting kibitka for their journey into exile.
In 19th-century Russia, a kibitka was often used to transport people across vast distances.
The old kibitka creaked as it rolled along the snowy road toward Siberia.
