standing ground

noun
maintaining position or beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈstændɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈstændɪŋ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
continuing to hold one's position or maintain one's beliefs despite opposition or difficulty

Examples

Standing ground firmly
Activists were praised for standing ground against unfair policies.
Despite criticism, she kept standing ground on her environmental views.
The coach admired the team's standing ground under pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stand one's ground - to refuse to change your opinion or position; to refuse to retreat
