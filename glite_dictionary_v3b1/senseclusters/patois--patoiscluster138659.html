patois

noun
regional or group dialect, nonstandard speech
9.5
Pronunciation: / pæˈtwɑ /; / ˈpætwɑ / (AmE), / ˈpatwɑː /; / ˈpætwa / (BrE)
a regional or social form of speech or dialect that is different from the standard language

Examples

Caribbean patois
Her grandmother still uses a Caribbean patois at home.
The villagers spoke in a patois that was difficult for outsiders to understand.
This book explores the unique patois that developed among immigrant workers in the city.
She grew up speaking a rural French patois at home.
Some communities in Jamaica still speak their native patois alongside English.
The local patois is difficult for outsiders to understand.

  • antonym: standard language - the form of a language considered the norm or most accepted version
