neurographic

adjective
creative drawing method
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to a creative or therapeutic drawing technique that uses flowing lines and shapes to express emotions or access the subconscious mind

Examples

Neurographic art therapy
The therapist introduced neurographic techniques to help clients express their feelings through art.
Her neurographic drawings featured intricate lines that seemed to flow across the page.
Many people find neurographic art helpful for relaxation and self-discovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: art therapy - the use of artistic methods to treat psychological disorders
