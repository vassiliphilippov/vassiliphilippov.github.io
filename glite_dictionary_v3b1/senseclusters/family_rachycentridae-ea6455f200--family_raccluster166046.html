family Rachycentridae

noun
fish family containing the cobia
Pronunciation: / ˌrækiˌsɛntrɪˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌrækiˌsɛntrɪˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of pelagic fishes, containing only the cobia (Rachycentron canadum).

Examples

Family Rachycentridae fishes
Members of the family Rachycentridae are known for their elongated bodies and large size.
Scientists studying the family Rachycentridae are primarily interested in its only species, Rachycentron canadum.
The family Rachycentridae consists solely of the cobia, a fast-swimming marine fish.
