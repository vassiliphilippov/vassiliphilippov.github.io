usufruct right

noun
legal right to use another's property
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàjuÀês…ôÀåfr åkt ra…™t / (AmE), / ÀàjuÀêzjuÀêfr åkt ra…™t / (BrE)
The legal right to use and benefit from property that belongs to another, provided the property itself is not damaged or diminished.

Examples

A usufruct right granted
She was granted a usufruct right over the property until her death.
The usufruct right allows her to collect rent but not to sell the land.
Under the will, he holds the usufruct right while the children retain ownership.
