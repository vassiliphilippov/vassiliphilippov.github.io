pentarchy

noun
group of five rulers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛntɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈpɛntɑːki / (BrE)
a system of government or leadership in which power is shared by five people or groups, especially in historical or church contexts

Examples

The pentarchy system
The early Christian church was led by the pentarchy of major patriarchates.
Historians often discuss the influence of the pentarchy in shaping religious doctrine.
A pentarchy can provide balance by distributing power among five leaders.
The ancient alliance was governed by a pentarchy of city-states, each with equal power.
Historians studied the brief pentarchy that emerged after the collapse of the monarchy.
A pentarchy can sometimes lead to disagreements if the five leaders cannot reach consensus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monarchy - a government ruled by a single person, such as a king or queen
  • synonym: oligarchy - a government ruled by a small group of people
  • degree lower: triumvirate - a group of three leaders or rulers
