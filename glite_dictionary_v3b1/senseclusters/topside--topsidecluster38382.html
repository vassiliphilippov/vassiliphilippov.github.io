topside

noun
part of a ship above the waterline
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑpˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɒp.saɪd / (BrE)
the part of a ship's hull that is above the waterline, especially the weather decks; often used in the plural

Examples

Ship's topside
After the storm, the crew inspected the topside for any damage caused by the waves.
Saltwater corrosion was starting to affect the ship's topside.
The sailors repainted the topside to protect it from the harsh marine environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deck - the floor-like surface in a ship, especially the main one
