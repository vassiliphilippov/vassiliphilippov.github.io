nonreplication

noun
failure to replicate
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the failure or prevention of biological replication, especially of DNA, cells, or viral genomes

Examples

Nonreplication of virus
The new treatment led to nonreplication of the virus, stopping the infection from spreading.
Researchers observed nonreplication of certain cells after exposure to the experimental drug.
Effective nonreplication of DNA is crucial in preventing the growth of harmful bacteria.

Related Terms

  • antonym: replication - the process of making copies of DNA, cells, or viruses
