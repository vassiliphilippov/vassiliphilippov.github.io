hard liquor

noun
distilled alcoholic beverage
1.0
Pronunciation: / hɑrd ˈlɪkər / (AmE), / hɑːd ˈlɪkə / (BrE)
an alcoholic drink that is distilled, such as whiskey, vodka, or rum, rather than fermented like beer or wine

Examples

A glass of hard liquor
He ordered a glass of hard liquor to warm himself up on the cold evening.
Some people prefer wine, but others enjoy hard liquor such as whiskey or gin.
The bar doesn't serve beer, only hard liquor and cocktails.
