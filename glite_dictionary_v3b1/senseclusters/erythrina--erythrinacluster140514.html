erythrina

noun
genus of tropical trees with red flowers
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɛrəˈθraɪnə / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈθrʌɪnə / (BrE)
Any of various shrubs or small trees of the genus Erythrina, known for trifoliate leaves and racemes of scarlet to coral red flowers, often grown as ornamentals.

Examples

The erythrina tree
Every spring, the erythrina blooms and attracts dozens of hummingbirds.
Local craftsmen use wood from the erythrina tree to make musical instruments.
The erythrina is prized in tropical gardens for its brilliant red blossoms.
