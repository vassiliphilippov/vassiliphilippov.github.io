Tyrannidae

noun
family of New World tyrant flycatchers
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɪˈrænɪˌdeɪ / (AmE), / tɪˈrænɪdiː / (BrE)
A large family of New World birds known as tyrant flycatchers, most numerous in Central and South America, also found in the United States and Canada.

Examples

Tyrannidae bird family
Birdwatchers in Mexico often spot members of the Tyrannidae during spring migration.
Researchers discovered a new species belonging to the Tyrannidae in the Amazon rainforest.
The Tyrannidae family contains more than 400 species of tyrant flycatchers.
