condemn

verb
pronounce punishment in court
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
(of a court or authority) to state officially that someone is to receive a specific punishment, especially after being found guilty of a crime.

Examples

Court condemns a criminal
He was condemned in court after a lengthy trial.
The judge condemned the defendant to life imprisonment for his crimes.
The tribunal condemned several officers for war crimes at the end of the conflict.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquit - to officially declare someone not guilty of a crime
