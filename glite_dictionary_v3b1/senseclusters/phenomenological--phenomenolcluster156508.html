phenomenological

adjective
based on observation
9.6 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / f…ôÀån…ëÀêm…ôÀån…ëÀêÀàl…ëÀêd í…™k…ôl / (AmE), / f…ôÀån…ím…™n…ôÀàl…íd í…™k…ôl / (BrE)
based on observed phenomena or experience rather than on theory or detailed explanation

Examples

Phenomenological approach
The scientist used a phenomenological approach to describe the results without relying on complex theories.
In psychology, a phenomenological study focuses on individuals' lived experiences.
The model is phenomenological, summarizing the data without explaining the underlying mechanisms.

Synonyms

  • empirical
    5.9 16/m
    empirical relies on measurement and observation in science, while phenomenological focuses on how things appear in lived experience.
  • experiential
    6.5 4.66/m
    experiential draws on direct personal feeling and practice, while phenomenological examines how things appear in lived experience.
  • observational
    5.0 3.74/m
    observational reports facts seen by watching events, while phenomenological looks at how those events feel or appear to people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: theoretical - based on theory rather than practical observation
