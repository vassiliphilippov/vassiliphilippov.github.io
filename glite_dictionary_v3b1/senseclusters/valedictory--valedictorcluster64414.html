valedictory

adjective
relating to farewell or departure
Pronunciation: / ˌvæl.əˈdɪk.tɔːr.i /; / ˌvælɪˈdɪktəri / (AmE), / ˌval.ɪˈdɪk.təri /; / ˌvælɪˈdɪktəri / (BrE)
relating to or serving as a farewell, especially at a formal event or in a speech

Examples

Valedictory speech
He was honored with a valedictory dinner before relocating abroad.
Her valedictory speech at the ceremony moved everyone to tears.
The CEO delivered a valedictory address on his final day at the company.
As his valedictory remarks concluded, the audience gave him a standing ovation.
Her valedictory speech at the graduation ceremony moved many of the students to tears.
The ambassador gave a valedictory dinner to mark the end of his service.
