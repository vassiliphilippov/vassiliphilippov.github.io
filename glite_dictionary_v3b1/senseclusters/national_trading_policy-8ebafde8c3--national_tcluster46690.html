national trading policy

noun
government policy on foreign trade
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənl ˈtreɪdɪŋ ˈpɑləsi / (AmE), / ˈnaʃənəl ˈtreɪdɪŋ ˈpɒləsi / (BrE)
A government's policy regulating and controlling trade with foreign countries.

Examples

A national trading policy controls trade
A country's national trading policy often reflects its economic priorities and political alliances.
Critics argue that the current national trading policy puts local farmers at a disadvantage.
The new government prioritized reforming the national trading policy to encourage exports and attract investment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free trade policy - a policy of minimal restrictions on imports and exports
  • synonym: protectionism - the theory or practice of shielding a country's domestic industries from foreign competition
