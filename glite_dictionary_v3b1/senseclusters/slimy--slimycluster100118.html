slimy

adjective
morally disgusting or repellent
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈslaɪmi / (AmE), / ˈslaɪmi / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) morally disgusting, dishonest, or repellent.

Examples

Slimy person
He played the role of a slimy villain who tricked everyone around him.
Her slimy tactics in the office made her unpopular with her coworkers.
Nobody trusted the slimy salesman who twisted the truth to make a sale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dishonest - not honest; likely to lie or cheat
  • antonym: upstanding - honest and respectable in character
  • synonym: vile - extremely unpleasant or morally bad
