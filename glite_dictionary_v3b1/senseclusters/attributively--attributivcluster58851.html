attributively

adverb
in an attributive grammatical manner
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɪbjətɪvli / (AmE), / əˈtrɪbjʊtɪvli / (BrE)
In a manner that functions as an attribute, especially modifying a noun directly.

Examples

Used attributively with nouns
Genitive nouns frequently appear attributively in English grammar.
In the phrase 'chicken soup', 'chicken' is used attributively as a modifier of 'soup'.
Some adjectives can be used attributively before a noun or predicatively after a verb.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attributive - (linguistics) modifying a noun, usually preceding it
