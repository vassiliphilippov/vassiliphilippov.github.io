succumb

verb
consent reluctantly, yield
5.3
Pronunciation: / səˈkʌm / (AmE), / səˈkʌm / (BrE)
to give in or consent to something reluctantly or after resistance

Examples

To succumb to pressure
After hours of bargaining, she finally succumbed to their demands.
He tried to resist, but he succumbed to the temptation of a second dessert.
The pressure was overwhelming, and even the most stubborn opponent eventually succumbed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of
  • synonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
