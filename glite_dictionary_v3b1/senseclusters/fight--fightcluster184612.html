fight

noun
violent struggle or contest
Pronunciation: / faɪt / (AmE), / faɪt / (BrE)
a struggle or contest between people or groups, often involving physical force, such as a battle, conflict, or sports match

Examples

A fight breaks out
After a long legal fight, the company finally won the case.
The two boxers prepared for their fight in front of a cheering crowd.
There was a fierce fight over the last piece of cake at the party.
Last night's fight ended in a dramatic knockout in the fifth round.
The heavyweight fight drew thousands of spectators to the arena.
Tickets for the championship fight sold out within hours.
After the fight, both sides claimed victory, but neither gained much ground.
The general planned his strategy carefully before the fight began.
Thousands of soldiers lost their lives in the bloody fight at the river crossing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battle - a sustained fight between large organized armed forces
  • synonym: bout - a short period of intense activity of a specified kind, especially in sport; a boxing or wrestling match
  • antonym: friendly - a match or contest played for practice rather than as part of a competition
  • synonym: match - a contest in which people compete against each other, especially in a sport
  • antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or absence of conflict
  • degree lower: skirmish - a short, unplanned fight between small groups in a war
  • synonym: struggle - a forceful or violent effort to get free of restraint
  • degree higher: war - a state of armed conflict between countries or groups
