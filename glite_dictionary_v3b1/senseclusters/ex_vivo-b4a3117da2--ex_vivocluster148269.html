ex vivo

adjective, adverb
outside a living organism (in vitro)
Pronunciation: / ɛks ˈviːvoʊ / (AmE), / ɛks ˈviːvəʊ / (BrE)
happening or done outside a living organism but in an artificial environment (used to describe biological processes or experiments)

Examples

Ex vivo experiment
Doctors evaluated organ function ex vivo before transplantation to check for compatibility.
Many drugs are first tested ex vivo to determine their basic effects before moving on to animal trials.
The researchers studied the cells ex vivo to better understand their behavior outside the body.
An ex vivo perfusion system keeps organs viable for transplantation.
The researchers studied tissue regeneration ex vivo before attempting in vivo experiments.
This drug's effectiveness was first demonstrated ex vivo using isolated human cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in vitro - outside a living organism but within a test tube or similar environment
  • antonym: in vivo - within a living organism
