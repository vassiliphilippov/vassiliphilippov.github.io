voracious

adjective
devouring food in great quantities
Pronunciation: / vəˈreɪʃəs / (AmE), / vəˈreɪʃəs /; / vɔːˈreɪʃəs / (BrE)
having a very eager or insatiable appetite or desire for something (especially food, but also knowledge, experiences, etc.)

Examples

A voracious appetite
She has a voracious appetite for adventure and new experiences.
The voracious lion devoured its meal within minutes.
Young children often have a voracious hunger after playing outside all afternoon.
Many people around the world are voracious consumers of online content.
She has a voracious appetite for mystery novels, finishing several each week.
The company's voracious acquisition strategy alarmed its competitors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something than is strictly necessary
  • synonym: greedy - having or showing an intense and selfish desire for something
  • synonym: insatiable - impossible to satisfy, especially in terms of appetite
  • synonym: ravenous - extremely hungry or eager for food
  • antonym: satiated - fully satisfied, especially from eating
