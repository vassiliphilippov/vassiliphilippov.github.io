septuplet

noun
group of seven notes
Pronunciation: / sɛpˈtʌplət / (AmE), / sɛpˈtʌplɪt / (BrE)
a group of seven musical notes played in the time usually given to a different number of notes, especially in a tuplet.

Examples

A musical septuplet
The pianist played a rapid septuplet passage that challenged her timing.
In this measure, the composer uses a septuplet to create a unique rhythmic effect.
Learning to count a septuplet accurately can be difficult for beginner musicians.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quintuplet - a group of five notes played in the time of four
  • synonym: triplet - a group of three notes played in the time of two
