sooty

adjective
extremely black; like soot
3.2 0.89/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsuÀêti /; / Ààs äti / (AmE), / Ààs äti / (BrE)
black or covered with soot; having the appearance or color of soot

Examples

Sooty black fur
At night, the cat's sooty fur blended into the darkness.
She wore a sooty cloak that made her nearly invisible in the shadows.
The chimney sweep emerged with his face covered in sooty smudges.
A sooty smell lingered in the air long after the fire was put out.
She tried to clean her sooty hands after tending the fireplace.
The walls of the old factory were visibly sooty after years of use.

Synonyms

  • soot-black
    5.5 0.00/m
    soot-black is very dark like heavy soot on a surface, while sooty means being actually covered with soot or dirt.
  • coal-black
    4.1 0.17/m
    coal-black is a very deep, steady black like coal in the mine, while sooty suggests a dirty, smoky coating.
  • jet-black
    3.9 0.22/m
    jet-black is an intense smooth black often used to describe shiny objects, while sooty means dull or covered with ash.
  • pitchy
    7.9 0.02/m
    pitchy looks deep black and tarry, while sooty describes something coated with soot from smoke or fire.
