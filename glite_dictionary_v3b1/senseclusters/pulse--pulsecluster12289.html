pulse

noun
heart's rhythmic beat in arteries
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /pʌls/ /; / /pəls/ (AmE), /pʌls/ (BrE)
the regular beating or throbbing of arteries caused by the heartbeat, especially as felt at the wrist or neck

Examples

A strong pulse
After running up the stairs, her pulse was racing.
She placed two fingers on his wrist to feel his pulse.
The nurse checked his pulse to monitor his heart rate.
During exercise, your pulse rate increases to supply more oxygen to your muscles.
If you can't find a pulse, it may be a sign of a medical emergency.
The nurse checked his pulse to make sure his heart was beating regularly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heartbeat - the regular movement or sound of the heart as it pumps blood
  • degree higher: palpitation - an irregular, rapid beating of the heart
