tillage

noun
cultivation of soil for crops
8.9 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪl.ɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈtɪl.ɪdʒ/ (BrE)
the preparation and cultivation of land or soil for growing crops

Examples

Soil tillage methods
Conservation tillage methods help reduce soil erosion and preserve soil moisture.
Excessive tillage can degrade the quality of the soil over time.
Modern farmers often use machinery to speed up the tillage process before planting season.
After years of tillage, the soil became especially rich and productive.
Modern methods have greatly increased the efficiency of tillage across the region.
The farmer rotated crops to maintain the fertility of the tillage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cultivation - the process of preparing land for crops
  • antonym: fallow - land left unplanted after plowing, contrast with cultivated land
  • synonym: ploughing - the act of turning over the soil
