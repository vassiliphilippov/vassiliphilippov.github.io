tremor

noun
a small earthquake
4.9 0.85/m

A slight or minor earthquake; a small seismic movement of the earth's surface.

Examples

A small tremor
A series of minor tremors preceded the larger earthquake.
Residents felt a tremor early in the morning and rushed outside.
The city experienced a mild tremor that rattled windows but caused no damage.

Synonyms

  • earth tremor
    3.7 0.04/m
    earth tremor is a small, mild quake that is often not destructive, while tremor names a slight, short seismic movement of the ground.
  • temblor
    8.6 0.25/m
    temblor is a general word for any earthquake, while tremor usually means a smaller, milder shake you can feel.
  • seism
    9.5 0.00/m
    seism is a technical term for an earth shaking used by scientists, while tremor is a common small, minor quake people feel.
  • seismic disturbance
    6.1 0.03/m
    seismic disturbance is a broad, formal term for any sudden earth movement, while tremor usually means a small, mild quake.
