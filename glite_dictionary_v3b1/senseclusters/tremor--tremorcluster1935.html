tremor

noun
a small earthquake
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmə / (BrE)
A slight or minor earthquake; a small seismic movement of the earth's surface.

Examples

A small tremor
A series of minor tremors preceded the larger earthquake.
Residents felt a tremor early in the morning and rushed outside.
The city experienced a mild tremor that rattled windows but caused no damage.

  • degree higher: earthquake - a sudden and violent shaking of the ground
