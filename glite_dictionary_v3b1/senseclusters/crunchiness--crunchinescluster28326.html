crunchiness

noun
gritty audio quality
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌntʃinəs / (AmE), / ˈkrʌntʃɪnəs / (BrE)
a slightly distorted, gritty sound quality in music or audio that gives a sharp, biting tone

Examples

Guitar crunchiness
The guitarist adjusted the amp settings to add more crunchiness to the solo.
Producers often seek a certain crunchiness in rock music to make the sound more energetic.
That old speaker gives the music a unique crunchiness that some people really enjoy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and pure in sound
  • synonym: distortion - an effect that alters the sound to make it rougher or harsher
