Aesopian

noun
indirect critic
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / iÀàso äpi…ôn / (AmE), / iÀêÀàs…ô äpi…ôn / (BrE)
a person who communicates opinions or criticism indirectly, especially through allegory, fable, or coded language to avoid censorship or punishment

Examples

An Aesopian writer
During the crackdown, he became an Aesopian, speaking truths only through allegory and parable.
Writers under strict regimes often turn into Aesopians to avoid direct confrontation with authorities.
Her Aesopian approach allowed her to criticize the government without attracting unwanted attention.

Synonyms

  • allegorizer
    9.9 0.00/m

Related Terms

  • antonym: outspoken - expressing opinions directly and openly
  • synonym: satirist - a person who uses satire to criticize indirectly
