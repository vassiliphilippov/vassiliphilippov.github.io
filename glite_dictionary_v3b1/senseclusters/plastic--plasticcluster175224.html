plastic

adjective
easily influenced or shaped
9.9 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈplæ.stɪk/ (AmE), /ˈplæ.stɪk/ (BrE)
easily influenced or adaptable; able to change or be changed (especially of a person, mind, or quality)

Examples

Plastic mind
Early education is crucial due to the plastic nature of the young brain.
Her plastic personality made her susceptible to peer pressure.
The plastic minds of children absorb new information quickly.
Children's attitudes are plastic, adapting to new ideas quickly.
The clay is especially plastic when wet, making it ideal for sculpting.
Under high temperatures, some metals become plastic and can be reshaped.
