spill out

verb
to flow or move out suddenly
1.4 3.0
Pronunciation: /spɪl aʊt/ (AmE), /spɪl aʊt/ (BrE)
(of people or things) to flow or move out of a place suddenly and often in large numbers or quantities.

Examples

Fans spill out quickly
After the game, fans spilled out of the stadium and into the parking lot.
The contents of her bag spilled out onto the floor when she dropped it.
When the bell rang, students spilled out of the classrooms into the hallway.
