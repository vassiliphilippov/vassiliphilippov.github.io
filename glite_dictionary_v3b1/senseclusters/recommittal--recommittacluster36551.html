recommittal

noun
return to previous state
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəˈmɪtəl / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˈmɪtəl / (BrE)
the act of sending someone or something back to a previous place, state, or condition, especially by official order

Examples

Court recommittal order
The court ordered the recommittal of the case for further investigation.
After violating his parole, he faced recommittal to prison.
The committee discussed the recommittal of the bill for additional review.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remand - to send someone back into custody or detention
