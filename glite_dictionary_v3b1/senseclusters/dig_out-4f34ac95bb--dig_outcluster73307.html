dig out

verb
find or remove something hidden or buried
Pronunciation: / dɪɡ aʊt / (AmE), / dɪɡ aʊt / (BrE)
to find or retrieve something that has been buried, hidden, or forgotten, or to remove something from being covered or trapped

Examples

To dig out old photos
I had to dig out my passport from a box in the closet.
She finally dug out her childhood letters from the bottom drawer.
They used shovels to dig out the car buried under the snow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bury - to put or hide underground; antonym of 'dig out'
  • synonym: unearth - to dig up or discover something buried; synonym of 'dig out'
