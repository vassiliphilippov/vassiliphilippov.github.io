arteriola

noun
small artery ending in a capillary
9.6
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.tə.riˈoʊ.lə/ /; / /ɑːr.tɪ.riˈoʊ.lə/ (AmE), /ɑː.tə.riˈəʊ.lə/ /; / /ɑː.tɪ.riˈəʊ.lə/ (BrE)
a small, thin-walled artery that leads to a capillary

Examples

Small arteriola branch
Each arteriola eventually branches into a network of capillaries.
Inflammation sometimes affects the walls of an arteriola, leading to tissue damage.
The narrowing of an arteriola can increase blood pressure in the surrounding tissue.
