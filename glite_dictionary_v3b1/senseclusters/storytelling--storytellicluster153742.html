storytelling

noun
telling stories aloud
2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈstɔːr.iː.tel.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈstɔːr.ɪ.tel.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈstɔː.iː.tel.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈstɔː.ɪ.tel.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the activity, art, or skill of telling stories to entertain, inform, teach, or persuade others

Examples

Traditional storytelling
The teacher used storytelling to make the history lesson more engaging for her students.
At the festival, people gathered around the fire for an evening of traditional storytelling.
He developed his storytelling skills by reading books and practicing in front of friends.
Traditional storytelling is an important way to pass down history and culture.
She used her storytelling skills to captivate the children during the library event.
Modern storytelling often includes digital media and interactive elements.
Effective storytelling can help a brand connect emotionally with its customers.
The politician's storytelling made her campaign speeches memorable and persuasive.
In business, storytelling is often used to make data more engaging and understandable.
