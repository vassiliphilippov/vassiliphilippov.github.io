veinlet

noun
small vein
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪnlət / (AmE), / ˈveɪnlət / (BrE)
a very small or minor vein, such as those found in leaves, minerals, or as tiny blood vessels

Examples

Tiny veinlets on leaves
The botanist examined the veinlets on the leaf to determine its species.
Tiny veinlets branch out from the main vein, creating a delicate pattern.
In some minerals, gold can be found in narrow veinlets running through the rock.
The microscope revealed a network of veinlets running through the leaf.
During the operation, the surgeon repaired a damaged veinlet to restore proper blood flow.
Tiny veinlets can be seen branching off from the main vein in the diagram.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arteriole - a small branch of an artery leading to capillaries
  • degree higher: vein - a tube that carries blood in animals or sap in plants
  • synonym: venule - a small vein that collects blood from capillaries
