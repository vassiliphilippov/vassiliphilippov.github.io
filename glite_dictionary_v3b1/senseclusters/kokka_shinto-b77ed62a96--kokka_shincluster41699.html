Kokka Shinto

noun
state branch of Shinto in Japan
Pronunciation: / koʊkɑː ʃɪnˈtoʊ / (AmE), / kɒka ʃɪnˈtəʊ / (BrE)
the branch of Shinto that was recognized as the official state religion of Japan, especially during the Meiji period and until the end of World War II

Examples

Kokka Shinto religion
After World War II, the Allied Occupation abolished Kokka Shinto as a state-supported religion.
During the Meiji era, Kokka Shinto played a central role in shaping Japan's national identity.
Textbooks often discuss the influence of Kokka Shinto on Japanese society and government policies.
