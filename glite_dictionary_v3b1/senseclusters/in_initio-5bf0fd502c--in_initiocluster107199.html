in initio

x
at the beginning
Pronunciation: / ɪn ɪˈniːʃioʊ / (AmE), / ɪn ɪˈnɪtɪəʊ / (BrE)
a Latin phrase meaning 'at the beginning', used especially in legal contexts to indicate something from the start

Examples

Contract void in initio
The judge ruled that the agreement was invalid in initio because of a legal error.
The lawyer argued that the contract was void in initio due to fraud.
If a law is unconstitutional in initio, it has no legal effect from the start.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ab initio - from the beginning; from the start
