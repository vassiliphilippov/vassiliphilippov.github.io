snort

verb
exhale sharply through the nose
Pronunciation: / snɔrt / (AmE), / snɔːt / (BrE)
to make a sudden, harsh sound through the nose by forcefully exhaling air, often to show amusement, anger, or disrespect

Examples

To snort with laughter
He snorted at the suggestion, clearly unimpressed.
She couldn't help but snort with laughter at the comedian's joke.
The horse snorted and stamped its hoof impatiently.
During the meeting, Janet snorted her disapproval without saying a word.
He snorted at the idea that the plan could ever work.
Whenever she disagreed, she would snort loudly in protest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scoff - to speak to someone or about something in a scornfully derisive or mocking way
  • degree lower: sniff - to draw air audibly through the nose; to inhale sharply through the nose
