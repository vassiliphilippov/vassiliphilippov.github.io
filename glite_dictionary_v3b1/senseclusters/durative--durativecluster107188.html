durative

noun
aspect indicating duration (grammar)
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʊrətɪv / (AmE), / ˈdjʊərətɪv / (BrE)
In linguistics, a grammatical aspect of a verb expressing that an action or state lasts for a period of time rather than being instantaneous or completed.

Examples

The durative aspect
In Mandarin Chinese, the progressive marker expresses a durative aspect.
Linguists often discuss the differences between durative and punctual aspects in verb analysis.
Some verbs naturally convey a durative meaning, such as 'sit' or 'wait.'

Related Terms
