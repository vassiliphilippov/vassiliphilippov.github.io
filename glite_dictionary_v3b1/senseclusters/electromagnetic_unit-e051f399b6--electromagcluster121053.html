electromagnetic unit

noun
unit for measuring electricity or magnetism
1.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊmæɡˈnætɪk ˈjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmæɡˈnætɪk ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement used to quantify electrical or magnetic phenomena, especially within specific systems such as the CGS electromagnetic system

Examples

An electromagnetic unit system
Each electromagnetic unit is defined based on fundamental physical constants.
Early scientists used a different electromagnetic unit system before the SI system became standard.
The gauss is an example of an electromagnetic unit used to measure magnetic induction.
