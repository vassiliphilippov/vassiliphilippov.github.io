unendowed

adjective
not provided or equipped with something
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɛnˈdaʊd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɛnˈdaʊd / (BrE)
not possessing a particular quality, talent, or resource; not provided or equipped with something.

Examples

People unendowed with wealth
Many were unendowed with either wealth or social standing.
She felt unendowed with artistic ability, but still loved to paint.
The school is unendowed and relies entirely on student fees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endowed - provided with a quality, ability, or asset
