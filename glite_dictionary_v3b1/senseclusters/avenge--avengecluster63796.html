to punish or harm someone in return for a wrong or injury done to oneself or another, especially as an act of justice or retribution

Examples

To avenge a wrong
He swore he would avenge his family after the attack.
Some people seek to avenge their friends when they have been wronged.
The heroine set out to avenge her father's unjust death.

Synonyms

  • revenge
    4.2 0.25/m
    revenge is a personal act of hurting someone to pay back a wrong, while avenge often means punishing someone as an act of justice.
  • retaliate
    7.3 4.31/m
    retaliate means responding with similar harm in return, while avenge suggests seeking punishment often for another's wrong or for justice.
  • strike back
    4.9 1.16/m
    strike back is to hit or attack in return quickly, while avenge is a more formal act of punishing a wrong.
  • get back at
    5.6 1.58/m
    get back at is an informal way to punish someone for a wrong, while avenge often implies justice or formal punishment.
  • get even
    5.4 1.64/m
    get even is an informal phrase for settling a score or revenge, while avenge often means punishing as justice rather than personal settling.
  • pay back
    3.2 0.33/m
    pay back is doing something in return to get even or hurt someone, while avenge usually carries a sense of formal punishment.
  • get back
    4.2 0.47/m
    get back is an informal, ambiguous phrase meaning to take revenge or regain something, while avenge specifically means punish for a wrong.
