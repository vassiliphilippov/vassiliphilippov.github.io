frontlash

noun
preemptive opposition
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfrʌntlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈfrʌntlæʃ / (BrE)
a reaction or opposition that happens in advance of an expected event or change

Examples

A frontlash reaction
The government faced a significant frontlash from citizens even before the new law was introduced.
Activists organized a frontlash to protest the rumored policy changes ahead of the official announcement.
There was a visible frontlash in the community as soon as the plans for redevelopment were leaked.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backlash - a strong negative reaction to a recent event or change
  • synonym: resistance - opposition to an idea or change
