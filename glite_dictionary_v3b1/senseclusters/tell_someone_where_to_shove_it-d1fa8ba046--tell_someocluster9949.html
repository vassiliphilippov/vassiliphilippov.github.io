tell someone where to shove it

verb
angrily dismiss or reject someone
7.4 8.5
Pronunciation: /tel ˈsʌm.wən wer tə ʃʌv ɪt/ (AmE), /tel ˈsʌm.wən weə tə ʃʌv ɪt/ (BrE)
(informal, vulgar) to angrily tell someone to go away or reject what they are saying or demanding, usually as a strong dismissal or refusal.

Examples

To tell someone where to shove it angrily
After Mark insulted her, she told him where to shove it and left the room.
If my boss talks to me like that again, I'll tell him where to shove it.
You can't just tell people where to shove it when you don't like their feedback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tell someone off - to speak angrily to someone for doing something wrong
  • synonym: tell someone to get lost - to rudely tell someone to go away
