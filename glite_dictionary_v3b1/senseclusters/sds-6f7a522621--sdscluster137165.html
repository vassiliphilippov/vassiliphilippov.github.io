SDS

noun
chemical safety document
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs diː ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛs diː ˈɛs / (BrE)
a document that provides essential information about the safe handling, storage, and disposal of chemicals or substances

Examples

Read the SDS
Before working with any new chemical, you should read the SDS to understand the risks.
The laboratory keeps an SDS for every substance stored on the premises.
Employees are trained to locate and interpret the SDS in case of a chemical spill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: MSDS - Material Safety Data Sheet, an older term for SDS
