neurotropic

adjective
affecting the nervous system preferentially
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈtroʊpɪk, ˌnʊrəˈtrɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
(of a virus, toxin, or chemical) tending to attack or affect the nervous system preferentially.

Examples

Neurotropic virus effect
Certain toxins exhibit neurotropic properties, leading to targeted nerve tissue damage.
Researchers are studying neurotropic agents for their potential effects on neurological diseases.
The rabies virus is highly neurotropic, often causing severe damage to the brain and spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neurophilic - having an affinity for nerve tissue
