Jayhawk

noun
anti-slavery fighter
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒeɪ.hɔːk/ /; / /ˈdʒeɪ.hɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈdʒeɪ.hɔːk/ (BrE)
a member of a militant anti-slavery group from Kansas active during the mid-19th century, especially around the time of the American Civil War

Examples

A Jayhawk raider
During the 1850s, a group of Jayhawks raided settlements along the Kansas border.
The Jayhawk became a symbol of Kansas's fight against slavery in American history.
Many stories from the Civil War era mention the bravery of a Jayhawk leading his men.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pro-slavery - supporting the continuation of slavery
