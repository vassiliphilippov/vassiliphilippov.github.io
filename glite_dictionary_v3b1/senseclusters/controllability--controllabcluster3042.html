controllability

noun
degree of control
8.1
Pronunciation: / kənˌtroʊləˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˌtrəʊləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which an action or event can be intentionally controlled by the subject, especially in language or grammar

Examples

Verb controllability
In linguistics, controllability helps determine whether a verb is used in the active or passive voice.
The controllability of an event can influence how speakers choose their words in a sentence.
Researchers analyzed the controllability of different actions to understand language patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agency - the capacity of a subject to act intentionally
  • antonym: involuntariness - the quality of not being under intentional control
