Tay-Sachs disease

noun
inherited disorder causing nerve cell damage
Pronunciation: / teɪ sæks dɪˈziz / (AmE), / teɪ saks dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder in which harmful quantities of fatty substances accumulate in nerve cells of the brain and spinal cord, typically leading to death in early childhood.

Examples

Tay-Sachs disease symptoms
Doctors diagnosed the infant with Tay-Sachs disease after observing neurological decline.
Genetic testing can determine whether couples are carriers of Tay-Sachs disease.
There is currently no cure for Tay-Sachs disease, but research into treatments continues worldwide.
