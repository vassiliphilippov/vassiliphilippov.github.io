outcoach

verb
to coach better
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈkoʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈkəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to coach a team or person more effectively than an opponent, especially in a competitive setting

Examples

To outcoach a rival
The home team's manager outcoached his rival with a brilliant game plan.
Despite having less experience, she managed to outcoach her opponent in the championship.
They were outcoached in the second half, leading to their defeat.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: coach - to train or instruct a team or person
  • synonym: outperform - to do better than someone else in a competition
  • antonym: underdeliver - to perform less well than expected
