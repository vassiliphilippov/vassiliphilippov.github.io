paperbark

noun
tree with papery bark
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪpərˌbɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəbɑːk / (BrE)
a tree, especially of the genus Melaleuca, with bark that peels off in thin, papery layers

Examples

Paperbark tree
The paperbark is easily recognized by its soft, peeling bark.
Many birds build nests in the branches of the paperbark tree.
In Australia, paperbark trees are often found near rivers and wetlands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eucalyptus - a type of Australian tree with aromatic leaves
  • synonym: melaleuca - a genus of trees and shrubs known for papery bark
