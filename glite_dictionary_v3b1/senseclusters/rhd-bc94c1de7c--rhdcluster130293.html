RhD

noun
blood group gene
Pronunciation: / ɑr eɪtʃ di / (AmE), / ɑː eɪtʃ diː / (BrE)
the gene that encodes the RhD protein, which determines whether a person's blood type is Rh positive or Rh negative

Examples

RhD gene test
Doctors tested her RhD status to ensure a safe blood transfusion.
The presence or absence of the RhD gene determines if someone is Rh positive or negative.
Researchers are studying how RhD variations affect pregnancy outcomes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ABO blood group - a system for classifying human blood based on antigens
  • synonym: Rh factor - a protein on red blood cells that determines Rh blood type
