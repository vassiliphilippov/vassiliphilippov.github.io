spookiness

noun
easily frightened quality
Pronunciation: / ˈspuːkinəs / (AmE), / ˈspuːkinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily frightened or startled, especially in animals or people

Examples

Horse's spookiness
The spookiness of the young colt made it difficult to train.
Her dog's spookiness around loud noises meant he needed extra care during storms.
Riders must be aware of a horse's spookiness when approaching unfamiliar objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmness - the quality of being relaxed and not easily startled
  • synonym: nervousness - the state of being easily agitated or alarmed
