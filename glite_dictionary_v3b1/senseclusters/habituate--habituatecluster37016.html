habituate

verb
consume regularly as a habit
Pronunciation: / həˈbɪtʃuˌeɪt / (AmE), / həˈbɪtjʊeɪt / (BrE)
to take or consume (a substance) regularly or as a habit

Examples

To habituate to drugs
After years of smoking, he became habituated to nicotine and found it hard to quit.
Patients may gradually habituate to certain medications if they use them regularly.
Some people habituate themselves to caffeine by drinking several cups of coffee a day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accustom - to make familiar by use or experience
