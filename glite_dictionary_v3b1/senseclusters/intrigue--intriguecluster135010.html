intrigue

verb
make secret plans; scheme
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtriɡ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtriːɡ / (BrE)
to make secret plans, often for dishonest or underhand purposes; to scheme or plot

Examples

To intrigue against someone
It was whispered that some employees had intrigued for his dismissal.
She spent years intriguing her way into powerful circles of government.
The rival nobles intrigued against the king, creating unrest at court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conspire - to make secret plans jointly to commit an unlawful or harmful act
  • synonym: plot - to make secret plans to achieve something, especially something illegal or harmful
