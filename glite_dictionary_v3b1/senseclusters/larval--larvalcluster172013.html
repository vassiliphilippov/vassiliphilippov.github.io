larval

adjective
relating to a larva
1.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈlɑːr.vəl/ (AmE), /ˈlɑː.vəl/ (BrE)
relating to the early, immature stage of an animal (especially insects) before it becomes an adult

Examples

Larval stage
Many diseases can be transmitted during the larval phase of parasites.
Scientists studied the larval development of the marine species.
The butterfly is in its larval stage before undergoing metamorphosis.
Insects undergo dramatic changes from their larval form to adulthood.
Many diseases can affect larval fish more severely than adult ones.
The caterpillar is a classic example of a larval stage in butterflies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - fully developed and mature animal or organism
  • synonym: larva - an immature, wingless, feeding stage of an insect
