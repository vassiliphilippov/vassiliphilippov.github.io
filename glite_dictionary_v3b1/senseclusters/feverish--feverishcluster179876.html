Showing or involving intense excitement, agitation, or activity.

Examples

Feverish excitement
After hearing the news, the crowd grew feverish with anticipation.
She worked at a feverish pace to meet the deadline.
There was a feverish excitement in the air before the concert began.

Synonyms

  • fevered
    5.3 0.64/m
    fevered is highly emotional and intense, while feverish describes strong excitement or activity in a more general, restless way.
  • frenzied
    6.5 2.24/m
    frenzied is wild and out of control with fierce energy, while feverish shows intense excitement or activity but is less chaotic.
  • hectic
    3.9 3.30/m
    hectic is full of busy activity and rush, while feverish highlights high excitement and agitation in people or events.
  • febrile
    8.5 0.51/m
    febrile relates to an actual fever or medical symptoms, while feverish usually means strong excitement or restless activity.
