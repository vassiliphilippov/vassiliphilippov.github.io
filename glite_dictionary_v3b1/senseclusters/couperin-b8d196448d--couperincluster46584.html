Couperin

proper noun
French baroque composer and organist
Pronunciation: / kuː.pəˈræ̃ / (AmE), / kuː.pəˈræ̃ / (BrE)
(François) Couperin (1668–1733), a French composer and organist, renowned for his baroque keyboard music and as a member of a distinguished family of organists.

Examples

Couperin music
Many harpsichordists admire the delicate ornamentation in Couperin's music.
She performed a suite by Couperin at the recital.
The works of Couperin are central to the French baroque repertoire.
