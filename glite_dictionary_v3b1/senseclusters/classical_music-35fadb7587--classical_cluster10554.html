classical music

noun
European art music genre
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuzɪk / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
A genre of music characterized by sophisticated structure and formality, originating mainly in Europe from the 18th to early 19th centuries, and typically involving orchestras or ensembles.
