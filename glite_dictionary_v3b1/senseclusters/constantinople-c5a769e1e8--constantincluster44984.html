Constantinople

noun
second ecumenical council (AD 381) expanding the Nicene Creed
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.stæn.tɪˈnoʊ.pəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stæn.tɪˈnəʊ.pəl / (BrE)
the second ecumenical council of the Christian church, held in 381 AD in Constantinople, which expanded the Nicene Creed, particularly in regard to the Holy Spirit.

Examples

Constantinople council
Scholars debate the political context of Constantinople and its impact on early Christianity.
The First Council of Constantinople clarified Christian doctrine on the Holy Spirit.
The Nicene Creed as recited today reflects changes made at Constantinople in 381.

  • degree lower: Council of Nicaea - the first ecumenical council of the Christian Church (AD 325)
