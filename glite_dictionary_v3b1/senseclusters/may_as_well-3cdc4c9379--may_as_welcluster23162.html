may as well

verb
to indicate a reasonable or practical option
1.1
Pronunciation: / meɪ æz wɛl / (AmE), / meɪ əz wɛl / (BrE)
used to suggest that a certain action is practical or reasonable when there are no better alternatives

Examples

You may as well try
If I’m going to be late anyway, I may as well stop for coffee.
It’s nearly closing time; we may as well leave now.
We’ve missed the last bus, so we may as well walk home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: might as well - used similarly to suggest a practical or reasonable choice
