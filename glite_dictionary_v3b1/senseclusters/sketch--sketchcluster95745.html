sketch

noun
preliminary drawing for later elaboration
2.6 2.1
Pronunciation: /skɛtʃ/ (AmE), /skɛtʃ/ (BrE)
a simple, quickly made drawing, often as a preliminary study or for humor

Examples

A quick sketch
Architects often begin with a simple sketch to visualize their ideas.
Before painting the mural, the artist completed a sketch on paper to plan the composition.
She showed me a sketch of the landscape she intended to paint in oils.
Every Sunday, the newspaper features a political sketch that pokes fun at government policies.
She admired the artist’s latest sketch lampooning celebrity culture.
The magazine’s editorial page always includes a witty sketch about current events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drawing - a picture made with a pencil, pen, or crayon rather than paint
  • synonym: study - a preparatory drawing, especially for a painting
