Dar al-harb

noun
areas outside Islamic rule; non-Islamic lands
Pronunciation: / dɑr ɑl ˈhɑrb / (AmE), / dɑːr æl ˈhɑːb / (BrE)
(in Islamic law) territories outside Islamic rule, where Muslims are in the minority and may be subject to persecution.

Examples

Dar al-harb territories
Medieval scholars sometimes referred to Europe as Dar al-harb in their writings.
Some historians examine the implications of living in Dar al-harb for Muslim communities.
The concept of Dar al-harb has been interpreted in various ways over the centuries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Dar al-Islam - territories under Islamic rule
