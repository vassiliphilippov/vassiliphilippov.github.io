alligatoring

noun
cracking paint pattern
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtərɪŋ / (BrE)
a pattern of cracks on a surface, especially paint, that looks like alligator skin, usually caused by aging or weathering

Examples

Alligatoring paint cracks
The wooden fence showed signs of alligatoring after years of harsh weather.
If you apply paint too thickly, alligatoring may develop as it dries.
Restorers had to remove the old varnish because of severe alligatoring on the surface.
The old paint on the house showed clear signs of alligatoring after years of sun exposure.
Roof shingles may suffer from alligatoring if they are not properly maintained.
Engineers inspected the bridge for alligatoring to assess the extent of surface damage.
