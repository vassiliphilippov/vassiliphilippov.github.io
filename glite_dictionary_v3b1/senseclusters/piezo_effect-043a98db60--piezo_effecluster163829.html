piezo effect

noun
electrical effect from pressure on crystals
8.1
Pronunciation: / piˈɛzoʊ ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / piːˈɛzəʊ ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
The generation of an electric charge in certain crystals, such as quartz, when mechanical pressure is applied, or the deformation of such crystals when an electric field is applied.

Examples

The piezo effect in crystals
Certain microphones rely on the piezo effect to convert sound vibrations into electrical signals.
Quartz watches keep accurate time thanks to the piezo effect in their tiny crystals.
The piezo effect is used in lighters to generate a spark without batteries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: piezoelectricity - electricity produced by mechanical pressure on certain crystals
