amice

noun
archaic outer garment
Pronunciation: / ˈæmɪs / (AmE), / ˈæmɪs / (BrE)
an archaic term for a cloak, hood, or outer garment used as a covering or wrap

Examples

Embroidered amice
She draped an embroidered amice over her shoulders to ward off the chill on the veranda.
The old portrait showed a nobleman wearing a richly decorated amice around his neck.
In medieval times, a traveler might carry an amice to protect against the cold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloak - a loose outer garment worn over indoor clothing
  • synonym: hood - a covering for the head and neck
