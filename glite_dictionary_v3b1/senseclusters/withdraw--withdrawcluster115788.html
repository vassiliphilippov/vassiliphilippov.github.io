withdraw

verb
retract a statement
3.1
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈdrɔ / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔː / (BrE)
to retract or take back something that one has said, announced, or claimed, especially publicly

Examples

To withdraw a statement
After realizing his error, the politician decided to withdraw his earlier statement.
If you feel your words were unjust, you should withdraw them completely.
She publicly withdrew her accusation after learning the full story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recant - to publicly withdraw a statement or belief, especially formally
  • synonym: retract - to withdraw or take back a statement, accusation, or promise
