Cathars

noun
medieval Christian religious sect in France
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθɑrz / (AmE), / ˈkɑːθɑːz / (BrE)
members of a Christian religious sect that flourished in southern France in the 12th and 13th centuries, known for their dualistic beliefs and opposition to the Catholic Church

Examples

Cathars religious sect
Historians have studied the rise and fall of the Cathars in southern France.
The Cathars were often persecuted for their beliefs by the medieval Catholic Church.
The castle once served as a refuge for the fleeing Cathars during the Albigensian Crusade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Albigensians - members of a related Christian sect in southern France, sometimes considered synonymous with Cathars
