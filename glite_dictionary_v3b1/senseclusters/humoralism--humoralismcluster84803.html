humoralism

noun
ancient medical theory
Pronunciation: / hjuːˈmɔrəlɪzəm / (AmE), / hjuːˈmɔːrəlɪzəm / (BrE)
an ancient theory that explained health, disease, and personality by the balance of bodily fluids (humors) in the body

Examples

Ancient humoralism theory
In ancient Greece, physicians practiced humoralism to explain the causes of illness.
The decline of humoralism marked a major shift in the history of medicine.
Treatments based on humoralism often involved balancing the four bodily fluids.
In ancient Greece, humoralism was used to explain both physical and mental illnesses.
The decline of humoralism marked a major shift in the history of medical science.
Many Renaissance doctors practiced medicine based on the principles of humoralism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: germ theory - the theory that diseases are caused by microorganisms
  • synonym: humorism - the theory that health depends on the balance of bodily humors
  • antonym: modern medicine - the current scientific approach to medicine based on evidence and research
