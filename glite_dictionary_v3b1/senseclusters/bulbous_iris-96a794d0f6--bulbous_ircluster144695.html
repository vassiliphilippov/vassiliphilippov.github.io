bulbous iris

noun
iris plant with bulb-like rootstock
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌlbəs ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈbʌlbəs ˈaɪərɪs / (BrE)
any of several irises characterized by a bulb-like rootstock, commonly grown as ornamental plants.

Examples

The bulbous iris flower
Many gardeners prefer the bulbous iris because it blooms early in the spring.
She planted several bulbous iris bulbs along the garden path for a burst of color.
The bulbous iris differs from the bearded iris due to its bulb-like base.
