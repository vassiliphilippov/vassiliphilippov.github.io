Giovanni da Verrazzano

proper noun
Italian explorer of North America's east coast
Pronunciation: / dʒoʊˈvɑːni də vɛˈrɑːtsəˌnoʊ / (AmE), / dʒɒˈvɑːni də vɛˈrætsəˌnəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 1485–1528) An Italian explorer from Florence who is known for exploring the eastern coast of North America.

Examples

Giovanni da Verrazzano explored America
Giovanni da Verrazzano mapped much of the Atlantic coast in the early 16th century.
Many historians credit Giovanni da Verrazzano with being the first European to enter New York Bay.
The Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge is named in honor of Giovanni da Verrazzano and his exploration.
