duet

verb
to perform together
3.5
Pronunciation: / duˈɛt / (AmE), / djuˈɛt / (BrE)
to perform a song or piece of music together with another person

Examples

To duet with her
They decided to duet on stage for the final song of the concert.
At the wedding, the bride and her friend duetted a beautiful ballad.
Many singers enjoy duetting with artists from different genres to create unique performances.
The two singers duet beautifully on the final track of the album.
Last night, they duetted on stage for the first time in years.
Many famous artists have duetted with each other at charity concerts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collaborate - to work together with someone on a project or activity
  • antonym: solo - to perform alone without another person
