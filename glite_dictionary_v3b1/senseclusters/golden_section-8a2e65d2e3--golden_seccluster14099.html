golden section

noun
proportional division with aesthetic ratio
2.3 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈgoʊl.dən ˈsek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈgəʊl.dən ˈsek.ʃən/ (BrE)
the division of a line or figure so that the ratio of the smaller part to the larger is the same as that of the larger part to the whole; often considered aesthetically pleasing in art and architecture.

Examples

The golden section ratio
Graphic designers often apply the golden section when creating visually appealing layouts.
Many artists use the golden section to achieve balance and beauty in their compositions.
The proportions of the Parthenon are said to follow the golden section.
