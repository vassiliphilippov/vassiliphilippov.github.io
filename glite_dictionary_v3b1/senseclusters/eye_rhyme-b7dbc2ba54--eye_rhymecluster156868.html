eye rhyme

noun
rhyme with similar spelling but different sounds
Pronunciation: / aɪ raɪm / (AmE), / aɪ raɪm / (BrE)
a rhyme in which two words are spelled similarly but pronounced differently, such as 'love' and 'move'

Examples

Eye rhyme in poetry
Many poets use eye rhyme for stylistic effect even when the words do not sound alike.
Students were asked to identify an eye rhyme in the excerpt from Shakespeare.
The words 'cough' and 'bough' are sometimes noted as an eye rhyme in English poetry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: perfect rhyme - rhyme in which final stressed vowel and all following sounds are identical
  • synonym: rhyme - correspondence of sound between words
