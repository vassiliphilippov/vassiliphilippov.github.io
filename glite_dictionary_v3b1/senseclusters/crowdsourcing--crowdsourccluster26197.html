crowdsourcing

noun
obtaining input from a large group, often online
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkraʊdˌsɔrsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkraʊdˌsɔːsɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of obtaining information, services, or ideas by soliciting contributions from a large group of people, typically from an online community.

Examples

Crowdsourcing online ideas
Many apps rely on crowdsourcing to update their maps with real-time traffic information.
Scientific research has benefited greatly from crowdsourcing data collection from volunteers around the world.
The company used crowdsourcing to gather customer feedback for their new product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outsourcing - the practice of obtaining goods or services from external sources
