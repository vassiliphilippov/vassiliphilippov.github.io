deep

adjective
low in pitch or tone
3.1 9.24/m
Pronunciation: / dip / (AmE), / diÀêp / (BrE)
having a low pitch (of a voice or sound) or strong, intense quality (of a color or sound)

Examples

She sang a deep note that surprised the audience.
The cello produces a deep and resonant sound.

The artist mixed several pigments to achieve the deep red hue he desired.
