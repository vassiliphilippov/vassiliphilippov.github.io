venial sin

noun
a minor, pardonable sin in Christian theology
9.4 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈviːniəl sɪn / (AmE), / ˈviːniəl sɪn / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) a sin that does not result in complete separation from God and is regarded as less serious than a mortal sin.

Examples

Confess venial sins
Confessing her venial sins gave her a sense of relief and peace.
The priest explained that forgetting to say a prayer was only a venial sin, not a mortal one.
While he felt guilty about telling a white lie, he knew it was considered a venial sin.
