Dis

proper noun
Roman god of the underworld
Pronunciation: / dɪs / (AmE), / dɪs / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the god of the underworld; the Roman counterpart of the Greek Hades.

Examples

Dis the god
In Virgil's "Aeneid", Aeneas travels to the realm of Dis.
Some Romans believed that souls went to Dis after death.
The ancient temple was said to be dedicated to Dis and Prosperine.
