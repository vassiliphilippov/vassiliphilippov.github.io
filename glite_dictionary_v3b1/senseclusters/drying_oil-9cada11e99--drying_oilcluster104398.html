drying oil

noun
oil that hardens in air, used in paints/varnishes
2.6 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪɪŋ ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪɪŋ ɔɪl / (BrE)
an oil that hardens when exposed to air due to oxidation, commonly used as a base in paints or varnishes

Examples

Common drying oil
Linseed oil is a common drying oil used by artists for mixing paints.
The wooden furniture was coated with a layer of drying oil to protect the surface.
Unlike mineral oil, a drying oil will form a hard film after exposure to air.
