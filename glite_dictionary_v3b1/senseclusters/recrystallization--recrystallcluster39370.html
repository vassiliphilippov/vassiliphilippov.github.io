recrystallization

noun
metal grain formation
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in metallurgy where new, strain-free grains form in a deformed metal during heating, restoring ductility and reducing hardness

Examples

Metal recrystallization
The recrystallization of the alloy improved its flexibility after it had been heavily deformed.
Engineers monitored the recrystallization process to ensure the metal regained its original properties.
Proper recrystallization is essential in metallurgy to restore ductility lost during cold working.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annealing - the process of heating and then slowly cooling metal to make it softer
  • antonym: work hardening - the process of strengthening metal by plastic deformation
