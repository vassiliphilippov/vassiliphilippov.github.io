ignorant

adjective
lacking knowledge or awareness
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɪɡnərənt /; / ˈɪɡnɚənt / (AmE), / ˈɪɡnərənt / (BrE)
lacking knowledge, information, or awareness (about something or in general); uneducated or uninformed

Examples

Be ignorant of facts
He admitted that he was ignorant of the local customs when he first arrived.
Many people are ignorant about the environmental impact of their actions.
She remained ignorant of the changes until it was too late to respond.
Although he is talented at sports, he is completely ignorant of classical music.
Many people remain ignorant of the basic principles of nutrition.
She admitted she was ignorant of the local customs before moving to the country.
It is difficult to have a serious discussion with someone who is ignorant of basic facts.
Many ignorant people believed the superstition because they had little access to education.
She felt ignorant about art until she took a history class at university.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illiterate - unable to read or write; lacking knowledge in a particular subject
  • antonym: knowledgeable - having knowledge; well-informed
  • synonym: unaware - not knowing about something
  • synonym: uneducated - lacking formal education or schooling
