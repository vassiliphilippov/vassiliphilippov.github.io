minster

noun
large or important church, often originally monastic
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnstər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnstə / (BrE)
a large or important church, especially one that was originally connected to a monastery or has cathedral status.

Examples

A large minster
The original minster was founded in the 7th century as a monastic church.
The service was held at the ancient minster overlooking the city.
York Minster is one of the largest Gothic cathedrals in Northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cathedral - the principal church of a diocese, containing the bishop's throne
