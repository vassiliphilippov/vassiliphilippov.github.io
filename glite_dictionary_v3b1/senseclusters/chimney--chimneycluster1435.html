chimney

verb
to climb between walls
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪmni / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪmni / (BrE)
to climb up or down between two close vertical surfaces by pressing the body against opposite faces, as in a narrow shaft or rock cleft.

Examples

To chimney a crack
The climber chimneyed up the narrow gap between the two boulders with careful movements.
She chimneyed her way up the rock face, using her back and feet for support.
He had never chimneyed before, so the instructor demonstrated the technique in the climbing gym.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descend - to move downward, especially by climbing
  • synonym: scramble - to climb quickly using hands and feet
