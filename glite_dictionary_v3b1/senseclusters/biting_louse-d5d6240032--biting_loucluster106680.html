biting louse

noun
wingless insect parasite of birds
8.6 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.tɪŋ laʊs/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.tɪŋ laʊs/ (BrE)
a small, wingless insect with mouthparts adapted for biting, most often parasitic on birds

Examples

A biting louse on birds
Poultry farmers often must deal with infestations of biting lice among their birds.
The veterinarian found a biting louse on the parrot's feathers.
Unlike the sucking louse, a biting louse feeds mainly on feathers and skin.
