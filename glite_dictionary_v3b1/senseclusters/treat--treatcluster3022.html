treat

verb
subject to a process for improvement or remedy
3.2 18/m
Pronunciation: / trit / (AmE), / triÀêt / (BrE)
to subject something to a process or action in order to prepare it for a use, improve it, or remedy a problem

Examples

To treat water
After the spill, workers treated the affected soil to remove pollutants.
The company treats the water before releasing it into the river.
You should treat the wood with a preservative to prevent rot.

Synonyms

  • condition
    3.7 0.14/m
    condition makes something fit and improved for use, while treat means applying actions to prepare, improve, or fix it.
  • process
    3.0 28/m
    process uses a series of ordered steps to change or handle something, while treat means subjecting it to actions to prepare or fix it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: process - to subject something to a series of actions or steps to achieve a result
  • synonym: purify - to make something pure, especially by removing contaminants
