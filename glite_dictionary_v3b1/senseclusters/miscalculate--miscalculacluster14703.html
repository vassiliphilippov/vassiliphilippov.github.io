miscalculate

verb
calculate or judge incorrectly
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈkæl.kjə.leɪt /; / ˌmɪsˈkælkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkæl.kjʊ.leɪt /; / ˌmɪsˈkælkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to calculate or estimate something incorrectly; to make a mistake in calculation or judgment

Examples

To miscalculate the amount
I realized too late that I had miscalculated the amount of paint needed for the room.
She miscalculated how much time the journey would take, so she arrived late.
The company miscalculated its budget, resulting in a financial shortfall.
If you miscalculate the amount of paint needed, you might run out before finishing the wall.
The engineer miscalculated the load capacity, leading to a structural failure.
We miscalculated how long the project would take and had to work overtime.

  • synonym: misjudge - to judge incorrectly
