poikilothermous

adjective
having variable body temperature; cold-blooded
Pronunciation: / ˌpɔɪkɪloʊˈθɜrməs / (AmE), / ˌpɔɪkɪləʊˈθɜːməs / (BrE)
(of animals, except birds and mammals) having a body temperature that varies with the surrounding environment; cold-blooded.

Examples

A poikilothermous animal
Amphibians are typically poikilothermous, which means their internal temperature fluctuates with their surroundings.
Because it is poikilothermous, the frog becomes sluggish in cooler weather.
Reptiles such as snakes are poikilothermous and depend on sunlight to regulate their body heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ectothermic - relying on external sources for body heat
  • antonym: homeothermic - having a stable body temperature regardless of environment
