riddle

verb
pose a difficult question
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪdəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪdəl / (BrE)
to pose a difficult question or to speak or write in a mysterious or puzzling way

Examples

To riddle a friend
Can you riddle me this: what gets wetter as it dries?
He loved to riddle his friends with impossible questions.
The old storyteller would riddle the children before revealing his tales.
Instead of giving a straight answer, he began to riddle with obscure metaphors.
She tends to riddle when talking about her plans, making it hard to know what she really means.
The professor would often riddle when students asked tough questions, challenging them to think deeper.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarify - to make something clear or easy to understand
  • synonym: puzzle - to perplex or confuse by presenting a difficult problem
  • synonym: quiz - to test someone by asking questions
