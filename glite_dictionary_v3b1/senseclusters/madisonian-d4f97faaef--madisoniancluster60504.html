Madisonian

noun
supporter of Madison
Pronunciation: / ˌmædɪˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌmædɪˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the political views and constitutional principles of James Madison

Examples

A Madisonian supporter
As a proud Madisonian, he advocated for a strong system of checks and balances in government.
The debate featured a Madisonian who emphasized the importance of constitutional limits on power.
Many scholars identify as Madisonians when discussing the separation of powers in American politics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Federalist - a supporter of the federal system of government in the United States
  • synonym: Jeffersonian - a supporter of the political principles of Thomas Jefferson
  • antonym: anti-Federalist - a person opposed to the ratification of the U.S. Constitution
