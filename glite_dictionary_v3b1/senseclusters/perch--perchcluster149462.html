perch

verb
place or set in a high position
4.4 8.81/m
Pronunciation: / p…ùt É / (AmE), / p…úÀêt É / (BrE)
to sit, rest, or place something on a high, narrow, or precarious position

Examples

To perch on a branch
He perched the vase on the narrow windowsill, hoping it wouldn't fall.
She perched her sunglasses on top of her head before stepping outside.
The mother perched her toddler on the kitchen counter while she prepared a snack.
A cat perched on the fence, watching the birds in the garden.
She perched on the edge of the sofa, anxiously waiting for the call.
The eagle perched on the top of a dead tree, scanning the prairie below.
A little squirrel was perching atop the garden gate, watching the world go by.
She perched on the armrest to get a better view of the painting.
The hawk chose to perch on the edge of the wooden fence.
