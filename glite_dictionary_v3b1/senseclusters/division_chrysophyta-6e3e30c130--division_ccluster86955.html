division Chrysophyta

noun
group of yellow-green/golden-brown algae
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒən ˌkrɪsoʊˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən ˌkrɪsəˈfʌɪtə / (BrE)
A group of mostly freshwater eukaryotic algae in which chlorophyll is masked by brown or yellow pigment, including the yellow-green and golden-brown algae and diatoms; sometimes replaced or subsumed by Heterokontophyta in some classification systems.

Examples

Division Chrysophyta algae
Many species within the division Chrysophyta are important components of freshwater ecosystems.
Scientists study the division Chrysophyta to understand the diversity of microscopic algae.
The division Chrysophyta includes both yellow-green algae and diatoms.
