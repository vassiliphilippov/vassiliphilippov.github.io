Danu

proper noun
Celtic mother goddess in mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːnuː / (AmE), / ˈdɑːnuː / (BrE)
An ancient Celtic goddess regarded as the mother of the Tuatha De Danann, and identified with the Welsh goddess Don.

Examples

Danu goddess
According to legend, Danu was revered as the mother of the Irish gods.
In Celtic lore, the Tuatha De Danann trace their ancestry to Danu.
The myths surrounding Danu link her with fertility and rivers.
