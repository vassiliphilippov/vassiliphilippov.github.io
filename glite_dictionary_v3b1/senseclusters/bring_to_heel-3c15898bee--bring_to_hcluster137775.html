bring to heel

verb
force obedience or submission
8.0 6.1
Pronunciation: /brɪŋ tə hiːl/ /; / /brɪŋ tuː hiːl/ (AmE), /brɪŋ tə hiːl/ /; / /brɪŋ tuː hiːl/ (BrE)
to force someone or something to obey or submit, especially after resistance or unruliness

Examples

To bring to heel a team

It took months of training to bring to heel the energetic puppy.
The government introduced strict regulations to bring to heel the financial sector.
