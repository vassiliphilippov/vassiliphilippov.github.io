slavery

noun
work done under harsh, exploitative conditions
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsleɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvəri / (BrE)
very hard work done under harsh conditions for little or no pay, comparable to being treated as a slave

Examples

Modern slavery at work
He compared his exhausting schedule to slavery, though he knew it wasn't truly the same.
Many factory workers endured slavery for years before labor reforms improved their conditions.
The documentary exposed the modern forms of slavery still present in some industries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the state of being free, not under the control or power of another
  • synonym: servitude - the condition of being a servant or subject, often with limited freedom
