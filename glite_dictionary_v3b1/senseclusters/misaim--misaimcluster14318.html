misaim

noun
incorrect aim
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈeɪm / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈeɪm / (BrE)
an act of aiming incorrectly or directing something at the wrong target, either literally or figuratively

Examples

An archer's misaim
The archer's misaim caused the arrow to miss the target completely.
Her angry comment was a cruel misaim that hurt someone who had tried to help.
A simple misaim in communication can lead to misunderstandings between friends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hit - a successful strike or aim at a target
  • synonym: mistake - an action or judgment that is wrong
