urging

noun
earnest encouragement or prompting
1.6 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɜːr.dʒɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈɜː.dʒɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of strongly encouraging or pressing someone to do something, or the strong feeling or desire that motivates action

Examples

Her mother's urging
At her mother's urging, she finally applied for the scholarship.
Despite his friend's constant urging, he decided not to change his major.
The bill passed only after strong urging from local activists.
After repeated urging from her coach, she entered the competition.
At his friend's constant urging, he finally applied for the job.
Despite her family's urging, she chose to travel alone.
After much urging from her friends, she finally applied for the job.
Despite their urging, he refused to change his mind.
His constant urging made her reconsider her decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discouragement - the act of attempting to deter someone from doing something
  • synonym: encouragement - the act of giving support, confidence, or hope
  • antonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or being uncertain before acting
  • synonym: importunity - persistent, often annoying demand or request
  • synonym: impulse - a sudden strong desire to act
  • synonym: solicitation - the act of asking for or trying to obtain something from someone
