contractionary

adjective
causing tightening
Pronunciation: / kənˈtrækʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / kənˈtrækʃənəri / (BrE)
causing or relating to the tightening or shortening of muscles or tissues, especially in medical or physiological contexts

Examples

Contractionary muscle effect
The medication had a contractionary effect on the patient's muscles during the procedure.
Doctors monitor for contractionary responses when testing nerve function in the clinic.
Some herbs are believed to have contractionary properties that affect smooth muscle tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contractile - having the ability to contract or shorten
  • antonym: relaxant - causing muscles or tissues to relax
