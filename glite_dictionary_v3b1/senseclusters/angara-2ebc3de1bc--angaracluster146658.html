Angara

proper noun
river in southeastern Siberia, Russia
Pronunciation: / ænˈɡɑːrə / (AmE), / ænˈɡɑːrə / (BrE)
a river in southeastern Siberia, Russia, that flows northwest from Lake Baikal to join the Yenisei River.

Examples

The Angara River
Huge hydroelectric dams have been built along the Angara.
Scientists have studied the Angara basin’s unique ecosystem for decades.
The Angara River is the only river that flows out of Lake Baikal.
