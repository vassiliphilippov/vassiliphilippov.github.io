Sequoya

proper noun
Cherokee creator of Cherokee writing system
9.6
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈkɔɪ.ə/ /; / /sɪ.ˈkɔɪ.ə/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈkɔɪ.ə/ /; / /sə.ˈkɔɪ.ə/ (BrE)
(c. 1770–1843) A Cherokee silversmith and scholar who created a system of writing for the Cherokee language, known as the Cherokee syllabary.

Examples

Sequoya Cherokee scholar
Sequoya is celebrated for inventing the Cherokee syllabary, which greatly advanced literacy among the Cherokee people.
Many schools and institutions are named after Sequoya in recognition of his contributions to language and culture.
Without Sequoya's work, the preservation of the Cherokee language would have been far more difficult.
