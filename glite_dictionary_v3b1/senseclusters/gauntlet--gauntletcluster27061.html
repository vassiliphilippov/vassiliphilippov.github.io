gauntlet

noun
a challenge; test of courage or ability
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːnt.lət /; / ˈɡɔnt.lət / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːnt.lɪt / (BrE)
a challenge or invitation to fight or compete, especially as used in the phrases 'throw down the gauntlet' or 'take up the gauntlet'

Examples

To throw down the gauntlet
She was quick to take up the gauntlet and defend her research findings against criticism.
The new policy is sure to throw down the gauntlet to traditional competitors.
When the CEO threw down the gauntlet, the sales team knew they had to exceed last year's results.
His statement was seen as throwing the gauntlet before the entire industry.
The company threw down the gauntlet to its rivals with its bold new strategy.
When her leadership was questioned, she was quick to take up the gauntlet.

  • synonym: challenge - something that tests one's abilities or resources
