sea line

noun
sea horizon
3.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀê la…™n / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀê la…™n / (BrE)
the apparent line on the horizon where the surface of the sea seems to meet the sky

Examples

Sunrise sea line
The ship appeared as a tiny dot on the distant sea line at sunrise.
As the storm cleared, a rainbow stretched across the sea line.
They watched the sun slowly sink below the sea line in the evening.

Synonyms

  • apparent horizon
    6.7 0.01/m
    apparent horizon is the distant line where sky and land or sea seem to meet, while sea line refers only to the sea-sky meeting.
  • sensible horizon
    7.6 0.00/m
    sensible horizon is the line where sky and earth or sea appear to meet from one point, while sea line names the sea-sky meeting.
  • visible horizon
    4.1 0.00/m
    visible horizon is the apparent distant line where earth and sky seem to meet, while sea line refers specifically to that meeting at sea.
  • horizon
    2.0 22/m
    horizon is the general line where earth and sky seem to meet, while sea line describes that line specifically where sea meets the sky.
