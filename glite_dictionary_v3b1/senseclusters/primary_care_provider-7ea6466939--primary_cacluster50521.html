primary care provider

noun
main healthcare professional for basic medical care
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri ˈkɛr prəˌvaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈpraɪməri ˈkeə prəˌvaɪdə / (BrE)
a healthcare professional who serves as a patient's main point of contact for the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of common medical conditions

Examples

A primary care provider
Many insurance plans require you to choose a primary care provider for regular check-ups.
My primary care provider referred me to a specialist for further treatment.
You should contact your primary care provider if you develop a new health concern.
