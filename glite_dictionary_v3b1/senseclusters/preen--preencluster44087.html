preen

verb
show pride or self-satisfaction
6.7 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / priÀên / (AmE), / priÀên / (BrE)
(usually reflexive) To show satisfaction and pride in oneself, especially after an achievement.

Examples

To preen after praise
After their team's victory, the coach was preening in the spotlight, basking in praise.
He likes to preen himself on his clever business strategies.
She couldn't help but preen after receiving the award at the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congratulate oneself - to praise or feel pleased with oneself
  • antonym: humble oneself - to act modestly or lower one's pride
