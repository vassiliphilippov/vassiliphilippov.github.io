Geb

proper noun
Egyptian earth god; father of Osiris and Isis
Pronunciation: / ɡɛb / (AmE), / ɡɛb / (BrE)
An Egyptian god of the earth, regarded as the father of Osiris and Isis in ancient Egyptian mythology.

Examples

Geb the earth god
According to myth, Geb separated the sky goddess Nut from himself to form the world.
Priests offered prayers and sacrifices to Geb for fertile soil and abundant crops.
The ancient Egyptians believed that Geb was the personification of the earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nut - Egyptian sky goddess and consort of Geb
