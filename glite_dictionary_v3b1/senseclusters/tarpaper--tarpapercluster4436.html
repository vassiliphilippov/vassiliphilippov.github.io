tarpaper

verb
to cover with tar paper
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrˌpeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈtɑːˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
to cover a roof or building with tar paper, usually to make it waterproof or provide temporary protection

Examples

To tarpaper a roof
They tarpapered the roof quickly before the first heavy snowfall arrived.
Workers tarpapered the shed to protect it from the rain until repairs could be made.
Before installing shingles, the builders tarpapered the entire surface of the roof.
