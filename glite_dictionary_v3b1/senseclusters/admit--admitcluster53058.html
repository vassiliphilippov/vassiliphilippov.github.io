admit

verb
allow entrance or access
Pronunciation: / ədˈmɪt / (AmE), / ədˈmɪt / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to enter a place or have access to it

Examples

To admit a visitor
Only employees are allowed to admit visitors through this entrance.
The museum does not admit children under twelve without an adult.
This ticket will admit you to the evening performance.
Due to safety regulations, the stadium will only admit as many fans as there are seats.
The new conference hall can admit up to 400 people for special events.
This elevator does not admit more than ten passengers at a time.
Only those with proper identification will be admitted to the secured area.
The museum does not admit children under five without an adult.
These tickets admit you to all the film screenings at the festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accommodate - to have enough space for someone or something
  • synonym: allow - give permission for entry or action
  • antonym: exclude - to prevent someone or something from entering a place
  • antonym: refuse - deny permission to enter
