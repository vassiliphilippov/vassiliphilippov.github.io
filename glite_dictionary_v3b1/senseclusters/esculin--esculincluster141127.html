esculin

noun
plant chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskjʊlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛskjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a natural chemical compound found in certain plants, often used in laboratory tests to identify bacteria

Examples

Esculin test
The laboratory technician added esculin to the culture to test for bacterial growth.
Some plants, such as horse chestnut, naturally contain esculin in their bark.
A positive reaction to esculin can help identify certain types of bacteria in medical tests.
