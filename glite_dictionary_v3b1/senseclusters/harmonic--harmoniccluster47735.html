harmonic

noun
tone in a complex sound

a musical or acoustic tone whose frequency is a whole-number multiple of a fundamental frequency (an overtone)

Examples

A harmonic tone
Engineers studied the sound wave to analyze its harmonic components.
The musician carefully listened for each harmonic as he tuned his violin.
When you pluck the string lightly, you can produce a clear harmonic.
Composers often use harmonics to add brightness and color to orchestral music.
The string vibrated and produced a clear harmonic above the fundamental note.
When plucking the guitar gently at certain points, you can create natural harmonics.

Synonyms

  • partial tone
    7.7 0.00/m
    partial tone is a tone with an exact whole number multiple frequency, while harmonic is the name for that overtone in music.
  • partial
    8.6 0.03/m
    partial is any component tone in a sound and can be inharmonic, while harmonic is a multiple of the fundamental frequency.
  • overtone
    7.4 0.36/m
    overtone is a higher tone heard above the basic note and is not always an exact multiple, while harmonic is an exact whole number multiple.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fundamental - the primary or lowest frequency of a sound
  • synonym: overtone - a higher tone in the harmonic series above the fundamental
