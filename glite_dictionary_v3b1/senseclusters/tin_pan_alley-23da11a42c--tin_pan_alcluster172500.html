Tin Pan Alley

proper noun
historic center of U.S. popular music publishing
Pronunciation: / tɪn pæn ˈæli / (AmE), / tɪn pæn ˈæli / (BrE)
A district in New York City, especially during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, where many composers and publishers of popular music were based, and by extension, the American popular music industry of that era.

Examples

Tin Pan Alley music
He wrote several hits during the heyday of Tin Pan Alley.
Many of the greatest American songwriters got their start in Tin Pan Alley.
The influence of Tin Pan Alley on modern music is still evident today.
