Synonyms

  • combined
    1.5 0.00/m
    combined is formed by joining parts into a single unit, while conjoined suggests things are joined closely or permanently.
  • joined
    1.9 0.00/m
    joined means connected by a link or fastener in objects, while conjoined often suggests a closer, unified state.
  • conjoint
    7.7 0.22/m
    conjoint describes things joined to act as one, while conjoined is the more common word for being joined together.
  • merged
    3.7 0.00/m
    merged describes two units that become a single entity over time, while conjoined stresses being joined together.
  • fused
    4.1 0.00/m
    fused means melted or blended into one mass, while conjoined means joined or united without implying melting.
  • coalesced
    6.1 0.00/m
    coalesced often describes parts slowly coming together into a single whole, while conjoined simply means joined or united.
  • consolidated
    4.9 0.03/m
    consolidated means combined to make something stronger or more organized, while conjoined focuses on being joined together.
  • amalgamated
    6.0 0.07/m
    amalgamated means mixed into a single whole, often by formal process, while conjoined just means joined together.
  • interwoven
    5.5 0.49/m
    interwoven describes parts tightly mixed so they are hard to separate, while conjoined indicates things are joined together.
  • coupled
    3.5 0.02/m
    coupled means linked as a pair or partners, often loosely, while conjoined means joined closely or made into one.
