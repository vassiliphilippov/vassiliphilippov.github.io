cerate

noun
medicated waxy ointment
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪr.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.reɪt / (BrE)
a hard medicated paste made from lard or oil mixed with wax or resin, used especially as a topical ointment

Examples

A cerate ointment
Because cerate is less greasy than ointment, it was favored for treating certain skin conditions.
Pharmacists once prepared cerate using beeswax, olive oil, and various medicinal ingredients.
The doctor applied a layer of cerate to the patient’s wound to protect and soothe it.
