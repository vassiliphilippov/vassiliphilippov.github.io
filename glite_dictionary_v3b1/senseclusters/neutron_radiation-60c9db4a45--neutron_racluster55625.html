neutron radiation

noun
ionizing radiation of free neutrons
2.0 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.trɑn ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.trɒn ˌreɪ.diˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a form of ionizing radiation consisting of free neutrons, typically produced in nuclear reactions or by certain radioactive substances

Examples

Neutron radiation exposure
Certain medical treatments use neutron radiation to target and destroy cancer cells.
Protective shielding is essential when working in areas where neutron radiation levels may be high.
The scientists measured the intensity of neutron radiation emitted during the experiment.
