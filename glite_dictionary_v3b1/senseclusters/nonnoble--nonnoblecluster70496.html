nonnoble

adjective
not precious or reactive
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
not noble, especially describing metals that are reactive, easily corroded, or not considered precious

Examples

Nonnoble metal type
Iron is considered a nonnoble metal because it corrodes easily.
Unlike gold, which is resistant to corrosion, zinc is a nonnoble metal.
Many industrial processes use nonnoble metals due to their lower cost and availability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: base - describing metals that are common and easily corroded
  • antonym: noble - describing metals that are precious and resistant to corrosion
