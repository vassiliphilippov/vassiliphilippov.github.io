carry the can

verb
accept responsibility, often unfairly
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri ðə kæn / (AmE), / ˈkæri ðə kæn / (BrE)
to accept responsibility or blame for something, especially when others avoid it

Examples

To carry the can alone
As team leader, she had to carry the can when the project failed.
He refused to carry the can for mistakes made by his predecessor.
Sometimes employees are forced to carry the can for management decisions.
After the scandal broke, the director was left to carry the can for the company's mismanagement.
It's unfair that she always has to carry the can when things go wrong at work.
No one wanted to carry the can for the failed project, so everyone tried to shift the blame.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pass the buck - to shift responsibility to someone else
  • synonym: shoulder the blame - to accept responsibility for a mistake or wrongdoing, especially on behalf of others
  • synonym: take the blame - to accept responsibility for something bad or wrong
  • synonym: take the fall - to accept blame or punishment, often to protect others
