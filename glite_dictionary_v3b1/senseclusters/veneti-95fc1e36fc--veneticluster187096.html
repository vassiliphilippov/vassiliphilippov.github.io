Veneti

proper noun
ancient Celtic tribe
Pronunciation: / vəˈniːtaɪ / (AmE), / vəˈniːti / (BrE)
an ancient Celtic tribe from Armorica (northwestern France) known for their seafaring and resistance to Julius Caesar in the 1st century BC

Examples

Armorican Veneti tribe
The Veneti were famous for their strong navy and control of the Armorican coast.
Julius Caesar described his campaign against the Veneti in his writings on the Gallic Wars.
Archaeologists have found evidence of the Veneti settlements along the coast of Brittany.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gauls - Celtic peoples living in ancient France and neighboring regions
  • antonym: Romans - people of ancient Rome, often in conflict with Celtic tribes
