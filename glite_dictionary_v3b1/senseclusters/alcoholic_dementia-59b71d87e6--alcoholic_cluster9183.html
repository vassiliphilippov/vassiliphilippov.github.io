alcoholic dementia

noun
dementia due to chronic alcoholism
1.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /æl.kə.ˈhɑː.lɪk dɪ.ˈmen.ʃiː.ə/ /; / /æl.kə.ˈhɑː.lɪk dɪ.ˈmen.ʃə/ (AmE), /æl.kə.ˈhɒl.ɪk dɪ.ˈmen.ʃə/ (BrE)
A form of dementia occurring in the advanced stages of severe chronic alcoholism, characterized by memory loss for recent events while long-term memory remains intact.

Examples

Alcoholic dementia memory loss
Alcoholic dementia can lead to significant difficulties with daily functioning and quality of life.
Doctors diagnosed him with alcoholic dementia after many years of heavy drinking.
Unlike Alzheimer's, alcoholic dementia often spares long-term memory but affects the ability to remember recent events.
