barrow-boy

noun
male street vendor of fruit and vegetables
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbæroʊ bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈbærəʊ bɔɪ / (BrE)
a man, especially in Britain, who sells fruit and vegetables from a barrow in the street

Examples

The barrow-boy sells fruit
Famous for his charm, the local barrow-boy quickly sold out of apples and oranges every morning.
He started his working life as a barrow-boy in London, pushing his cart through the bustling streets.
The barrow-boy shouted out the day’s specials to attract customers passing by the market.
