direction

noun
formal instruction or order
1.0
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkʃən, daɪˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃən, daɪˈrɛkʃən /; / dɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
an official or practical instruction or order telling someone what to do or how to do something

Examples

Court direction
The court's direction must be followed exactly to avoid penalties.
The judge issued a direction to the jury before deliberations began.
Under the board's direction, all staff must complete the new training module.
He ignored the directions and ended up lost in the city center.
Please read the directions carefully before starting the experiment.
She handed me a card with directions for assembling the bookshelf.
