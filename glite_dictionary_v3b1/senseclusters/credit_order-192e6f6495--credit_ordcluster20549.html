credit order

noun
an order billed for later payment
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛdɪt ˌɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈkrɛdɪt ˌɔːdə / (BrE)
an order for goods or services placed without immediate payment, with the payment to be billed at a later date

Examples

Place a credit order
Before placing a credit order, the supplier checks the buyer’s credit history.
Many businesses prefer a credit order arrangement to improve cash flow.
The company shipped the merchandise on a credit order, expecting payment in thirty days.
