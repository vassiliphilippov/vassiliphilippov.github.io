Menelaus

proper noun
king of Sparta in Greek mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛnəˈleɪəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛnɪˈleɪəs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the king of Sparta at the time of the Trojan War, brother of Agamemnon, and husband of Helen.

Examples

Menelaus in Greek mythology
In Homer's Iliad, Menelaus is determined to reclaim his wife Helen from Troy.
Some historians debate whether Menelaus was based on an actual Mycenaean king.
The legend says that Menelaus ruled Sparta with wisdom after the war ended.
