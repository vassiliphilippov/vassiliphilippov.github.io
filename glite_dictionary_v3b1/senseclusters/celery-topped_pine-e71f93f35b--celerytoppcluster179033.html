celery-topped pine

noun
Tasmanian evergreen coniferous tree
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛləri tɑpt paɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛləri tɒpt paɪn / (BrE)
a medium-sized evergreen coniferous tree (Phyllocladus aspleniifolius) native to Tasmania, with leaves resembling celery and valued for its timber

Examples

The celery-topped pine tree
The celery-topped pine is a distinctive feature of Tasmania's cool temperate rainforest.
Timber from the celery-topped pine is highly prized for its durability and fine texture.
We spotted several celery-topped pines during our hike through the Tasmanian wilderness.
