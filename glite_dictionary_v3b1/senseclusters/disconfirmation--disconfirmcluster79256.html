disconfirmation

noun
evidence against belief
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskənˌfɜːrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskɒnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
evidence or process that proves a belief, theory, or hypothesis is incorrect

Examples

Scientific disconfirmation
The scientist welcomed disconfirmation as an important part of the research process.
New data provided clear disconfirmation of the widely accepted theory.
Without disconfirmation, some false beliefs can persist for years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirmation - evidence that supports a belief or theory
  • synonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement or theory to be wrong
