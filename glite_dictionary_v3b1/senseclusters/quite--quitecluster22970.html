quite

adverb
completely; to the greatest extent
1.0 62/m
Pronunciation: / kwaÉªt / (AmE), / kwaÉªt / (BrE)
to the greatest extent; completely or entirely.

Examples

Quite alone
After the meeting ended, she was quite alone in the office.
He was quite mistaken about the date of the event.
You are quite correct in your assessment of the situation.

Synonyms

  • completely
    1.0 160/m
    completely describes something finished in every part and without anything left, while quite means complete or entire in extent.
  • entirely
    2.2 79/m
    entirely means all parts are included and nothing is missing, while quite is used to say something is completely whole.
  • fully
    1.0 102/m
    fully describes being complete to the maximum or capacity, while quite expresses a full or total degree.
  • totally
    1.0 109/m
    totally says something is absolute and without exception, while quite can mean complete but sometimes feels less strong.
  • utterly
    3.6 18/m
    utterly is extreme and forceful to show highest degree, while quite can mean complete but is often milder.
  • wholly
    3.4 11/m
    wholly means in every respect and is more formal, while quite is common and can be less formal or less intense.
  • altogether
    3.3 12/m
    altogether often means completely or in total, especially in sums or results, while quite is more general and spoken.
  • perfectly
    1.0 33/m
    perfectly suggests exactness and no fault, while quite means complete extent but not necessarily perfect or exact.
