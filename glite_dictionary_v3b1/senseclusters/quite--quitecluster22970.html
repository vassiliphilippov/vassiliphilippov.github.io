Synonyms

  • completely
    1.0 150/m
    completely describes something finished in every part and without anything left, while quite means complete or entire in extent.
  • entirely
    2.2 50/m
    entirely means all parts are included and nothing is missing, while quite is used to say something is completely whole.
  • fully
    1.0 60/m
    fully describes being complete to the maximum or capacity, while quite expresses a full or total degree.
  • totally
    1.0 130/m
    totally says something is absolute and without exception, while quite can mean complete but sometimes feels less strong.
  • utterly
    3.6 11/m
    utterly is extreme and forceful to show highest degree, while quite can mean complete but is often milder.
  • wholly
    3.4 4/m
    wholly means in every respect and is more formal, while quite is common and can be less formal or less intense.
  • altogether
    3.3 7/m

  • perfectly
