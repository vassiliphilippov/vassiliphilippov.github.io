Mughal

proper noun
Indian ruling dynasty
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmʊɡəl / (BrE)
the dynasty or empire that ruled much of South Asia from the 16th to 19th centuries, or a member of that dynasty

Examples

The Mughal Empire
The Mughal Empire was known for its impressive architecture, including the Taj Mahal.
Many Mughal paintings are celebrated for their intricate detail and vibrant colors.
Akbar was one of the most famous Mughal emperors in Indian history.
The Mughal rulers were known for their impressive architecture and cultural achievements.
Many historical sites in India were built during the Mughal period.
Akbar was one of the most famous emperors of the Mughal dynasty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mongol - a member of the Central Asian ethnic group from which the Mughal dynasty descended
  • synonym: Mughal Empire - the empire established by the Mughal dynasty in South Asia
