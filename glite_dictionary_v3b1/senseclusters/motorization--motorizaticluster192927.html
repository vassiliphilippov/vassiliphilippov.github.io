motorization

noun
conversion to use of motors or motor vehicles
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊtərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌməʊtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of equipping vehicles, machinery, or other things with motors or motor vehicles, or the conversion of operations, systems, areas, industries, or societies to use or become increasingly reliant on motor-driven equipment or motor vehicles.

Examples

Rapid motorization growth
Rapid motorization led to the expansion of road infrastructure in many developing countries.
The motorization of agriculture in the 20th century revolutionized food production.
The city's motorization resulted in an increase in both traffic and air pollution.
After World War II, the motorisation of agriculture accelerated dramatically in Europe.
Government policies have supported the motorisation of public transport fleets in recent years.
The rapid motorisation of rural areas has transformed local economies and lifestyles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mechanization - the process of introducing machines or automatic devices
  • synonym: motorization - alternative spelling; process of equipping with motors or vehicles
