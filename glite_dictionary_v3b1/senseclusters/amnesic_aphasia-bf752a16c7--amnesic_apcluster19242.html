amnesic aphasia

noun
inability to name or recognize names of objects
6.9
Pronunciation: / æmˈniːzɪk əˈfeɪʒiə / (AmE), / amˈniːsɪk əˈfeɪziə / (BrE)
A neurological condition characterized by the inability to name objects or to recognize written or spoken names of objects.

Examples

Amnesic aphasia symptoms
People with amnesic aphasia may recognize objects but struggle to recall what they are called.
Speech therapy is a common treatment for individuals with amnesic aphasia.
The patient was diagnosed with amnesic aphasia after he could no longer name everyday objects.
