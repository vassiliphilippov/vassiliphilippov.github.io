correlational statistics

noun
statistical analysis of relationships between variables
Pronunciation: / kəˌreɪləˈʃənl stəˈtɪstɪks / (AmE), / kɒˌrɛləˈʃəʊnəl stəˈtɪstɪks / (BrE)
statistical methods used to determine and measure the relationships between two or more variables, where changes in one variable are systematically related to changes in another

Examples

Correlational statistics methods
In psychology, correlational statistics help reveal how different behaviors relate to one another.
Researchers used correlational statistics to examine the relationship between exercise habits and blood pressure.
Understanding correlational statistics is essential for interpreting data in social science studies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: causal statistics - statistical analysis focusing on cause-and-effect relationships, not just associations
  • synonym: correlation - a mutual relationship or connection between two or more things
