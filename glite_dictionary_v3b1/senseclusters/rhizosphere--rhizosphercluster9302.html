rhizosphere

noun
soil around roots
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪzəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈraɪzəˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the region of soil directly around plant roots, affected by and interacting with the roots and microorganisms

Examples

The rhizosphere soil
Scientists study the rhizosphere to understand how soil microbes help plants absorb nutrients.
Healthy plants often have a diverse rhizosphere that protects them from disease.
The rhizosphere plays a crucial role in the cycling of nutrients in ecosystems.
Many beneficial bacteria live in the rhizosphere and help plants absorb nutrients.
Scientists study the rhizosphere to understand how roots interact with soil organisms.
Fertilizers can change the chemical balance of the rhizosphere and affect plant growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bulk soil - soil not directly influenced by plant roots
  • antonym: phyllosphere - the surface of plant leaves as a habitat for microorganisms
  • synonym: root zone - the area of soil where plant roots grow
  • synonym: soil microbiome - the community of microorganisms living in soil
