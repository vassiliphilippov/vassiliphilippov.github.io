genus Claviceps

noun
genus of parasitic fungi on grasses
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs kləˈvɛsɪps / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs kləˈvɛsɪps / (BrE)
a genus of fungi that are parasitic on the ovaries of various grasses, notably including the ergot fungus

Examples

Genus Claviceps fungi
Members of the genus Claviceps are known for producing toxic alkaloids affecting both humans and livestock.
Scientists studied the life cycle of genus Claviceps to understand its impact on agriculture.
The genus Claviceps includes fungi responsible for the disease ergot in cereals such as rye.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ergot - a fungus of the genus Claviceps, especially C. purpurea, that infects rye and other cereals
