live it up

verb
enjoy oneself extravagantly
3.8 7.2
Pronunciation: / lɪv ɪt ʌp /; / ˈlɪv ɪt ʌp / (AmE), / lɪv ɪt ʌp /; / ˈlɪv ɪt ʌp / (BrE)
to enjoy yourself in a lively, extravagant, or carefree way, often by spending money or celebrating

Examples

To live it up at parties
After finishing their exams, the students decided to live it up at a beach party.
He plans to live it up on his birthday with his friends and family.
She likes to live it up when she goes on vacation, visiting fancy restaurants and clubs.
After getting his bonus, he decided to live it up and booked a five-star cruise.
They plan to live it up in Paris for their anniversary next month.
Whenever they're together, the old friends live it up until the early hours of the morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be frugal - to be economical with resources; the opposite of spending extravagantly
  • synonym: celebrate - to acknowledge a significant or happy day or event with festivities
  • synonym: let one's hair down - to behave in an uninhibited or relaxed manner
  • antonym: take it easy - to relax and not do anything strenuous or exciting
