murmur

noun
a quiet complaint or protest
3.5 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmɜːr.mər/ (AmE), /ˈmɜː.mə/ (BrE)
a low, continuous, indistinct sound, often made by people speaking quietly, by soft movement, or as a quiet complaint

Examples

A quiet murmur
Despite the harsh decision, no more than a quiet murmur was heard from the students.
She heard a faint murmur of protest from the back of the room.
There was a murmur of discontent among the employees after the new policy was announced.
A gentle murmur drifted through the audience as the speaker approached the podium.
She could hear the soft murmur of voices from the next room.
The forest was filled with the murmur of leaves rustling in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - continuous low, murmuring sound, especially of water or indistinct speech
  • degree higher: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym: grumble - a muted complaint or mutter, especially about something minor
  • antonym: roar - loud, deep, continuous sound
  • degree lower: whisper - speak very softly using one's breath without vocal cords
