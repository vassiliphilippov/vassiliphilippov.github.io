flite

verb
to quarrel noisily
9.9
Pronunciation: / flaɪt / (AmE), / flaɪt / (BrE)
to quarrel or argue noisily, especially in a persistent or nagging way

Examples

To flite with neighbors
The neighbors would often flite over the smallest issues, disturbing the whole street.
She did not want to flite with her sister, so she kept silent.
In old tales, villagers might flite for hours about land boundaries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or accept a suggestion
  • synonym: argue - to exchange opposing views, often angrily
  • synonym: quarrel - to have an angry disagreement
