aver

verb
state or assert confidently
7.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.vər/ /; / /ə.ˈvɜːr/ (AmE), /ə.ˈvɜː/ /; / /ˈeɪ.və/ (BrE)
to state or assert that something is true, especially in a confident or forceful way

Examples

To aver a fact
In his statement, he averred that the company had always followed ethical practices.
She avers her innocence despite the evidence against her.
The witness averred that he was nowhere near the scene of the crime.
During the meeting, she averred that all safety protocols had been followed.
He averred his innocence despite the mounting evidence against him.
In court, the witness averred that she saw the defendant at the scene of the crime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • antonym: deny - to state that something declared or believed is not true
