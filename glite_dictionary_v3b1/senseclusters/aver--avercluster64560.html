aver

verb
state or assert confidently
7.9 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàv…ùÀê /; / …ôÀàv…ù / (AmE), / …ôÀàv…úÀê / (BrE)
to state or assert that something is true, especially in a confident or forceful way

Examples

To aver a fact
In his statement, he averred that the company had always followed ethical practices.
She avers her innocence despite the evidence against her.
The witness averred that he was nowhere near the scene of the crime.
During the meeting, she averred that all safety protocols had been followed.
He averred his innocence despite the mounting evidence against him.
In court, the witness averred that she saw the defendant at the scene of the crime.

Synonyms

  • assert
    3.9 28/m
    assert is a common verb used to state facts or opinions firmly, while aver often sounds more formal and forceful.
  • asseverate
    9.9 0.02/m
    asseverate is a formal, emphatic way to state something strongly, while aver is used more generally to assert truth.
  • avouch
    9.9 0.01/m
    avouch is a blunt or open statement of truth, often old-fashioned, while aver is more neutral and argumentative.
  • avow
    7.6 0.60/m
    avow is a public or bold admission of belief or fact, while aver focuses on stating something as true.
  • affirm
    4.1 14/m
    affirm is to state or support that something is true or right, often politely, while aver is more forceful and assertive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • antonym: deny - to state that something declared or believed is not true
