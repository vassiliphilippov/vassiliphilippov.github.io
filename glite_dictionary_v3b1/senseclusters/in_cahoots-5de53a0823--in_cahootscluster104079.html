in cahoots

adverb
secretly collaborating
7.6
Pronunciation: / ɪn kəˈhuːts / (AmE), / ɪn kəˈhuːts / (BrE)
secretly working together with someone, especially for a dishonest or hidden purpose

Examples

Be in cahoots secretly
The two politicians were rumored to be in cahoots to influence the election results.
It turned out the manager and the supplier were in cahoots to overcharge the company.
Many people suspected the referee was in cahoots with the home team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conspiring - planning together in secret, especially to do something illegal
  • synonym: in league - secretly cooperating with someone, often for a dishonest reason
  • antonym: opposing - acting against someone or something
