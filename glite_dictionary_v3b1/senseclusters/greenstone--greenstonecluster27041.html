greenstone

noun
green volcanic rock
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːnˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌstəʊn / (BrE)
a metamorphosed mafic volcanic rock, usually green in color due to minerals like chlorite or epidote, found in ancient geological formations

Examples

Greenstone belt
The ancient greenstone belts are important for understanding the Earth's early history.
Miners searched for gold deposits within the region's greenstone formations.
A sample of greenstone was collected for analysis in the geology lab.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock
  • antonym: granite - a hard, coarse-grained igneous rock
