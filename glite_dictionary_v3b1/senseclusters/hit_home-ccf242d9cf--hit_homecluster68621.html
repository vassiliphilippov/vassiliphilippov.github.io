hit home

verb
to have a strong, relevant personal impact
Pronunciation: / hɪt hoʊm / (AmE), / hɪt həʊm / (BrE)
to have a strong emotional effect on someone or to be fully understood, often making someone realize the importance or seriousness of something

Examples

To hit home hard
Her words about the importance of health really hit home after my recent illness.
It wasn't until she saw the empty room that it hit home he had left for good.
When he lost his job, the reality of the situation finally hit home.
Hearing about the accident hit home for the entire community.
Her criticism about my work ethic really hit home and made me reflect on my habits.
The reality of the situation hit home when I saw the empty apartment.
