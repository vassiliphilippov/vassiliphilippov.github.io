inferior

adjective
(astronomy) orbit inside Earth's
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɪriɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriə / (BrE)
(of a planet) having an orbit closer to the Sun than that of the Earth.

Examples

Inferior planet
Mercury is classified as an inferior planet because it orbits closer to the Sun than Earth.
Unlike Mars, which is superior, Mercury is an inferior planet.
Venus, an inferior planet, never strays far from the Sun in our sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: superior - (of a planet) having an orbit outside the Earth's orbit
