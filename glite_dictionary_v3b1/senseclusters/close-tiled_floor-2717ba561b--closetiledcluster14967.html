close-tiled floor

noun
floor with tightly spaced tiles
4.1 5.2
Pronunciation: /kloʊs taɪld flɔːr/ /; / /kloʊz taɪld flɔːr/ /; / /kloʊs taɪld flɑːr/ (AmE), /kləʊs taɪld flɔː/ /; / /kləʊz taɪld flɔː/ (BrE)
A floor in which tiles are placed closely together, typically leaving minimal gaps between them.

Examples

A close-tiled floor
Designers recommend a close-tiled floor to make small spaces appear larger and neater.
Moisture does not easily seep between the tiles of a close-tiled floor.
The kitchen renovation included installing a close-tiled floor for a modern, seamless look.
