game theory

noun
mathematical theory of competition and cooperation
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪm ˌθiːəri / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪm ˌθɪəri / (BrE)
The study of mathematical models of competition and cooperation between intelligent decision-makers, especially to analyze strategies in situations involving conflicting interests.

Examples

Game theory models
He wrote his thesis on applications of game theory in political science.
Many economic models rely on game theory to explain how people make decisions in competitive markets.
Nash equilibrium is a fundamental concept in game theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: game - an activity involving skill, knowledge, or chance, following fixed rules
  • synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term aim
