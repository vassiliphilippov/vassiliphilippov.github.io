Russian thistle

noun
prickly Eurasian weed
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌʃən ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃən ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
a prickly, bushy plant native to Eurasia, now a common weed in central and western North America

Examples

Russian thistle plant
Russian thistle quickly spread across the prairies, invading farmers' fields.
Many people mistake Russian thistle for other types of weeds.
The dry Russian thistle tumbled across the road in the hot summer wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tumbleweed - a type of plant that breaks off and tumbles in the wind, often referring to Russian thistle in the US
