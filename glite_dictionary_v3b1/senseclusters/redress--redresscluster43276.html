redress

noun
act of correcting a wrong or fault
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdrɛs / (AmE), / rɪˈdrɛs / (BrE)
the act of correcting a wrong or giving compensation for a loss or injustice

Examples

Seek redress for harm
He demanded redress for the injustice he had endured.
The victims sought redress through the courts for the damages they suffered.
There is little hope of redress if the government ignores the complaint.
If you suffer a financial loss, you may seek redress through legal means.
Many people feel they have no redress when bureaucracy makes a mistake.
The court awarded substantial redress to the victims of the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to make up for a loss or injury
  • synonym: remedy - a means of correcting a wrong or making things right
