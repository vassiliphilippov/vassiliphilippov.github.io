fickle

adjective
liable to sudden change; inconstant
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪk.əl /; / ˈfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪk.əl /; / ˈfɪkəl / (BrE)
changing often and unpredictably, especially in feelings, loyalty, or interests; not reliable

Examples

A fickle heart
Her fickle affections made it hard for anyone to trust her promises.
Spring weather can be so fickle, sunny in the morning and stormy by afternoon.
The public’s fickle attention quickly shifted to a new scandal.
He quickly lost interest in hobbies, proving just how fickle his enthusiasm could be.
Her fickle moods made it difficult to make plans together.
The public can be fickle, showing great support one day and forgetting the next.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inconstant - not staying the same, changing frequently
  • antonym: steady - not changing; stable and consistent
