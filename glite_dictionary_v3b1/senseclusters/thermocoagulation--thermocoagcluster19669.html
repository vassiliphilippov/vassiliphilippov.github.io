thermocoagulation

noun
tissue congealing by heat (medical)
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊkoʊˌæɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊkəʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of congealing tissue by the application of heat, particularly through the use of electric current, typically for medical purposes

Examples

Thermocoagulation treatment
Thermocoagulation is often employed in the removal of tumors or abnormal growths.
Advances in thermocoagulation have improved the treatment of certain vascular malformations.
The surgeon used thermocoagulation to control bleeding during the operation.
