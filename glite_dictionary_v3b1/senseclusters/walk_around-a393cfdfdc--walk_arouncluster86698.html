walk around

verb
move on foot around an object
1.1 3.88/m
Pronunciation: / w…ëk …ôÀàra änd / (AmE), / w…îÀêk …ôÀàra änd / (BrE)
to move on foot so as to go around the edge or perimeter of something, rather than through or over it.

Examples

To walk around a puddle
She decided to walk around the puddle instead of stepping through it.
Tourists walk around the old city walls every evening.
We had to walk around the construction site to get to the park.

Synonyms

  • skirt
    4.4 2.08/m
    skirt means moving along the edge or border of something, while walk around emphasizes moving on foot around its perimeter.
  • move around
    1.3 1.25/m
    move around means passing to the other side of an object by changing direction, while walk around stresses doing this on foot.
  • round
    3.0 5.57/m
    round means travel along a curved or circular path, while walk around means going on foot around an object's edge or perimeter.
  • circle
    1.8 14/m
    circle means moving all the way around or in a ring, while walk around means walking on foot around an object's edge.

Related Terms
