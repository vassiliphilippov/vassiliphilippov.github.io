jade

verb
become bored or weary
Pronunciation: / dʒeɪd / (AmE), / dʒeɪd / (BrE)
to make someone tired, bored, or less enthusiastic, especially through overuse or repetition; to become tired or bored

Examples

To jade the audience
After years of attending the same conferences, she began to jade of the endless speeches.
He tried not to jade of his favorite hobby, but eventually lost interest.
Some students jade quickly when faced with repetitive assignments.
Don't jade yourself with too many responsibilities at once.
The long, uphill walk began to jade even the most athletic hikers.
Years of repetitive work had jaded her enthusiasm for the job.
Her constant complaints started to jade my enthusiasm.
Negative feedback over time might jade his enthusiasm for the project.
The daily repetition of chores can sometimes jade your interest in them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bore - to make or become uninterested
  • synonym: exhaust - to make someone very tired
  • antonym: refresh - to make someone less tired or revive their energy
  • synonym: weary - to become physically or mentally tired of something
