Syriac

noun
ancient script
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriˌæk / (AmE), / ˈsɪriːæk / (BrE)
the writing system (script) used for the Syriac language and related languages, especially in early Christian texts

Examples

Syriac script
The ancient manuscript was written in Syriac, making it valuable for historians.
She studied the Syriac script to better understand early Christian writings.
Many religious texts were preserved in Syriac during the first centuries AD.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aramaic - a Semitic language closely related to Syriac
  • synonym: Coptic - an ancient Egyptian script and language used in Christian texts
