salt-rising bread

noun
white bread leavened by salt-tolerant bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔlt ˌraɪzɪŋ brɛd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlt ˌraɪzɪŋ brɛd / (BrE)
A type of white wheat bread leavened by salt-tolerant bacteria, typically made with a mixture containing salt and cornmeal or potato pulp.

Examples

A salt-rising bread loaf
She learned to bake salt-rising bread from her grandmother, using an old family recipe.
Some bakeries still offer salt-rising bread for customers seeking traditional recipes.
The unique flavor of salt-rising bread comes from its unusual fermentation process.
