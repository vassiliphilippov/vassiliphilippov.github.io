ramrod

noun
strict, demanding overseer
Pronunciation: / ˈræmˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈræm.rɒd / (BrE)
a person who is stern, strict, and in charge, often demanding obedience from others; a harshly demanding overseer

Examples

A strict ramrod
Despite his reputation as a ramrod, he was respected for his fairness and attention to detail.
Few employees appreciated working under such a ramrod, but productivity did increase.
The new manager was a real ramrod, insisting that everyone arrive early and stay late.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slave driver - someone who makes people work extremely hard
  • synonym: taskmaster - a person who imposes hard work or discipline
  • synonym: tyrant - a cruel and oppressive ruler or boss
