Aristotelianism

noun
the philosophy of Aristotle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌærɪstəˈtɪliənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈtɪliənɪzəm / (BrE)
The philosophy and teachings of Aristotle, especially concerning logic, metaphysics, ethics, poetics, politics, and natural science.

Examples

Aristotelianism philosophy
Aristotelianism profoundly influenced medieval scholastic thought and the development of Western philosophy.
He wrote a comparative analysis of Aristotelianism and Platonism in his dissertation.
Modern science was, in part, shaped by a gradual departure from Aristotelianism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aristotelian - relating to Aristotle or his philosophy
  • antonym: Platonism - the philosophy of Plato
