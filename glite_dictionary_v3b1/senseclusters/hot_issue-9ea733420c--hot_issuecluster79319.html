hot issue

noun
new, highly demanded stock (finance)
2.2
Pronunciation: / hɑt ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / hɒt ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
A newly issued stock or security that is in great public demand, often selling at a premium shortly after issuance.

Examples

A hot issue stock
A hot issue often commands a much higher price than the original offering.
Many investors hope to make quick profits by buying a hot issue on the first trading day.
The latest hot issue was oversubscribed within minutes of opening.
