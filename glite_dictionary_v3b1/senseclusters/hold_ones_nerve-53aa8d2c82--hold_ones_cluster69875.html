hold one's nerve

verb
remain calm under pressure
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /hoʊld wʌnz nɜːrv/ /; / /hoʊld wənz nɜːrv/ (AmE), /həʊld wʌnz nɜːv/ /; / /həʊld wənz nɜːv/ (BrE)
to remain calm and in control of your emotions, especially in a difficult or stressful situation

Examples

To hold one's nerve under pressure
Despite the pressure from the crowd, she managed to hold her nerve and complete the routine flawlessly.
He struggled at first but ultimately held his nerve and delivered a confident speech.
It's important to hold your nerve during negotiations if you want to secure the best deal.
