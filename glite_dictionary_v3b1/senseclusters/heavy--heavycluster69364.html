heavy

adjective
slow or clumsy from weight
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvi / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi / (BrE)
slow, clumsy, or lacking in lightness or liveliness

Examples

Heavy steps
He climbed the stairs with a heavy tread, exhausted from work.
The ancient dinosaur left a heavy trail in the muddy earth.
The horse's heavy steps echoed through the empty street.
He tried to lighten the heavy atmosphere at dinner with a joke, but no one laughed.
Her writing style is often criticized for being too heavy and lacking in wit.
The play's heavy themes made the audience reflect deeply throughout the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • antonym: light - easy to move or carry; not heavy
  • synonym: ponderous - slow and clumsy because of great weight
  • synonym: serious - not joking, thoughtful or earnest in tone
