snowbelt

noun
mountain snow zone
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsnoʊ.belt/ (AmE), /ˈsnəʊ.belt/ (BrE)
a high-elevation area on a mountain where snow remains for long periods, often lasting through the summer

Examples

Mountain snowbelt
The hikers set up camp just below the snowbelt, where the ground was still covered in white.
Wildflowers bloomed at the edge of the snowbelt as the snow slowly melted in late July.
Above the tree line, the snowbelt remained visible even during the warmest months.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: glacier - a large mass of ice and snow moving slowly down a mountain
  • synonym: snowfield - a large area of permanent or long-lasting snow
