hallucinosis

noun
mental state with persistent hallucinations
7.3
Pronunciation: / həˌluːsɪˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / həˌluːsɪˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A mental condition characterized by persistent or recurrent hallucinations, typically occurring without significant confusion or clouding of consciousness.

Examples

Alcohol hallucinosis
Alcohol withdrawal can sometimes lead to a state known as hallucinosis.
The patient was diagnosed with hallucinosis after experiencing vivid visual hallucinations over several weeks.
Unlike schizophrenia, hallucinosis generally does not involve significant disruption of overall mental clarity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hallucination - an experience involving the perception of something not present
