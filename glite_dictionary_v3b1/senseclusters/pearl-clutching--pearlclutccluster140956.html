pearl-clutching

noun
exaggerated display of shock or outrage
Pronunciation: / pɝl ˈklʌtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / pɜːl ˈklʌtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
an exaggerated or affected display of shock, outrage, or moral disapproval, often in response to something perceived as socially improper

Examples

The pearl-clutching reaction
Amid all the pearl-clutching, few people actually listened to what was being said.
Social media erupted in a wave of pearl-clutching after the controversial advertisement aired.
The director dismissed the pearl-clutching of critics who found the play too provocative.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outrage - an intense feeling of anger or shock
  • synonym: prudishness - excessive propriety or modesty
