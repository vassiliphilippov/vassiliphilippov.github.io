Marattiaceae

noun
family of large, tropical ferns
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˌræt.iˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / məˌratɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of chiefly tropical ferns with large fronds, forming the order Marattiales.

Examples

The Marattiaceae family
Scientists study the evolutionary history of the Marattiaceae to understand fern diversification.
Several members of the Marattiaceae can be found in humid tropical forests worldwide.
The Marattiaceae are known for their massive fronds and distinctive spore-producing structures.
