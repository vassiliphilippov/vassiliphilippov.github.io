Ygdrasil

proper noun
mythical tree in Norse mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈɪɡdrəsɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪɡdrəsɪl / (BrE)
(in Norse mythology) a huge sacred ash tree that connects the heavens, earth, and underworld, holding the cosmos together.

Examples

The Ygdrasil tree
According to Norse myths, Ygdrasil was the axis around which the world revolved.
Legends say that the fate of the universe depends on the well-being of Ygdrasil.
The gods would gather daily beneath the branches of Ygdrasil to hold their assemblies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ash tree - type of tree believed to be the species of Ygdrasil
  • synonym: world tree - a tree that connects different realms in mythology
