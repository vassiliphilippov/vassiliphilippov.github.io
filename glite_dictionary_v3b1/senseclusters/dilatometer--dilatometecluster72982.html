dilatometer

noun
volume-measuring instrument
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪləˈtɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌdɪləˈtɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure changes in the volume or dimensions of a material, often due to temperature or pressure

Examples

A dilatometer instrument
The laboratory technician used a dilatometer to monitor the expansion of the glass sample.
A dilatometer can help scientists understand how materials respond to changes in temperature.
Before starting the experiment, she carefully calibrated the dilatometer for accurate measurements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calorimeter - an instrument for measuring heat changes in a chemical reaction
  • synonym: thermometer - an instrument for measuring temperature
