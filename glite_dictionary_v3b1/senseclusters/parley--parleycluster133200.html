parley

verb
hold a negotiation, especially between enemies
8.6 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêrli / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀêli / (BrE)
(of opposing sides, especially enemies) to hold a conference or discussion with the aim of reaching an agreement or peace.

Examples

To parley for peace
After hours of fighting, the leaders decided to parley rather than risk further losses.
The two commanders agreed to parley in a neutral location under a flag of truce.
They parleyed late into the night, trying to avoid bloodshed.

Synonyms

  • negotiate
    3.7 35/m
    negotiate is a formal discussion to reach agreements in business or politics, while parley is a meeting between enemies seeking peace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fight - engage in a battle or conflict
  • synonym: negotiate - discuss something formally in order to reach an agreement
