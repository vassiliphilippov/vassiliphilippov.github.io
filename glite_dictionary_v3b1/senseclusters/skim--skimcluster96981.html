skim

verb
read or examine quickly
8.0 2.3
Pronunciation: / skɪm / (AmE), / skɪm / (BrE)
to read or look through something quickly, without paying attention to details

Examples

To skim a report
I skimmed the report before the meeting, just to catch the highlights.
She likes to skim through articles to get the main idea without reading every word.
While studying for exams, he often skims his notes to refresh his memory.
I only had time to skim the article before the meeting.
If you skim the first chapter, you might miss important details.
She skimmed the report to get the main points.
