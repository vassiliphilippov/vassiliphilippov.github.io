sensationalist

adjective
exaggerated for effect
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
using shocking, exaggerated, or dramatic details to attract attention or provoke strong emotions

Examples

Sensationalist headlines
The newspaper was criticized for its sensationalist coverage of the celebrity scandal.
Many viewers are drawn to sensationalist news stories that focus on crime and disaster.
Some documentaries avoid a sensationalist approach and present the facts calmly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dramatic - intended to create a strong effect or impression
  • antonym: objective - presenting facts without exaggeration or bias
