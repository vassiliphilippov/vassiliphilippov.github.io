veritably

adverb
truly; really
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛrɪtəbli / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪtəbli / (BrE)
truly or really; used to emphasize that something is actually the case

Examples

A veritably true fact
The city was veritably transformed by the new public art installations.
She was veritably amazed by the generosity of her friends.
The old castle is veritably a piece of living history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: falsely - in a way that is not true
  • synonym: really - in fact; actually
  • synonym: truly - in a truthful or real way
