common measure

noun
the typical metre of a ballad stanza
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈmɛʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈmɛʒə / (BrE)
the metrical pattern of ballad stanza consisting typically of four lines with alternating iambic tetrameter and trimeter, usually rhymed abcb or abab.

Examples

Common measure in poetry
Emily Dickinson often used common measure in her poetry, giving her verses a familiar rhythmic structure.
Many traditional hymns and folk songs are written in common measure to make them easy to remember and sing.
The ballad's common measure creates a natural, singable cadence that appeals to a wide audience.
