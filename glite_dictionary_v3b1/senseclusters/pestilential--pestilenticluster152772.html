pestilential

adjective
causing or spreading epidemic disease
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛstɪˈlɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌpɛstɪˈlɛnʃəl / (BrE)
Likely to cause or spread a contagious, epidemic disease; relating to or resembling pestilence.

Examples

A pestilential outbreak
A pestilential outbreak swept through the crowded city.
In the novel, a pestilential wind signaled the arrival of the deadly plague.
The pestilential conditions in the refugee camp worried the health workers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagious - capable of being transmitted by infection
  • antonym: healthful - beneficial to health, promoting health
  • synonym: infectious - likely to spread infection
