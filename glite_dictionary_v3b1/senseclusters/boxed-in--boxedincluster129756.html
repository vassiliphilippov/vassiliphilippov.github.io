boxed-in

adjective
confined or restricted
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbɑkstˈɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɒkstˈɪn / (BrE)
Feeling enclosed or restricted, as though surrounded by barriers or confined within narrow limits.

Examples

Feeling boxed-in tightly
After the accident, she felt boxed-in by the constant medical supervision.
Many employees complain of feeling boxed-in by strict office regulations.
The narrow alley made him feel boxed-in and anxious.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not under the control or in the power of another; able to act or be as one wishes
  • synonym: trapped - unable to escape from a place or situation
