both sides

noun
two opposing parties or viewpoints
Pronunciation: / boʊθ saɪdz / (AmE), / bəʊθ saɪdz / (BrE)
the two opposing parties or viewpoints in a dispute, discussion, or argument.

Examples

Both sides of argument
It's important to consider both sides of every argument before forming an opinion.
The judge gave both sides an equal opportunity to present their evidence.
The mediator listened carefully to both sides before making a recommendation.
