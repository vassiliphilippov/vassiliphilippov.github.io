mudroom

noun
entryway for shoes
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌruːm / (BrE)
a small room or entryway in a house where people remove and store wet or dirty shoes and outerwear before entering

Examples

The mudroom door
Please leave your boots in the mudroom before coming inside.
The mudroom has hooks for coats and shelves for shoes to keep the house clean.
After playing in the rain, the children dropped their wet jackets in the mudroom.
We always leave our wet coats and boots in the mudroom to keep the rest of the house clean.
The new house has a spacious mudroom with plenty of storage for shoes and jackets.
After playing outside in the rain, the children rushed into the mudroom to dry off.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foyer - an entrance hall or open area in a house or building
  • synonym: vestibule - a small entrance hall or lobby next to the outer door
