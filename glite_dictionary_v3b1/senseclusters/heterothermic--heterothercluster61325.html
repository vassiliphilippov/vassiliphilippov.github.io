heterothermic

adjective
having variable body temperature (not constant)
6.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬.əroʊˈθɝː.mɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.ərəʊˈθɜː.mɪk / (BrE)
(of an animal, except birds and mammals) having a body temperature that varies with the temperature of the environment.

Examples

Heterothermic animal body
Desert lizards display heterothermic behavior, adjusting their temperature by basking or seeking shade.
Many reptiles are heterothermic and rely on sunlight to regulate body warmth.
The study compared heterothermic fish to their homeothermic counterparts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homeothermic - having a constant body temperature regardless of environment
  • synonym: poikilothermic - having a variable body temperature that tends to fluctuate with the ambient environment
