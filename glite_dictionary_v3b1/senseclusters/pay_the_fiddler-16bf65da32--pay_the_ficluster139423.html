pay the fiddler

verb
face the consequences for one's actions
9.8
Pronunciation: / peɪ ðə ˈfɪdlɚ / (AmE), / peɪ ðə ˈfɪdlə / (BrE)
to accept or face the consequences for one's actions, especially after having enjoyed certain benefits or pleasures

Examples

To pay the fiddler finally
After years of reckless spending, he finally had to pay the fiddler and declare bankruptcy.
She knew she would eventually have to pay the fiddler for skipping so many classes.
They enjoyed the party, but now it's time to pay the fiddler and clean up the mess.
