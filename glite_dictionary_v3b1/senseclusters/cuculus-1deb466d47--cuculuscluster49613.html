Cuculus

proper noun
genus of cuckoo birds
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːkjələs / (AmE), / ˈkjuːkjʊləs / (BrE)
A genus of birds in the cuckoo family (Cuculidae), which includes the common cuckoo and related species.

Examples

Cuculus genus birds
Scientists recently discovered a new species within the Cuculus genus in Southeast Asia.
The characteristic call of birds in the Cuculus genus is well known in many cultures.
The common cuckoo belongs to the genus Cuculus, which is found across Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cuckoo - a bird of the Cuculidae family, known for laying eggs in other birds' nests
