narcotizing

adjective
inducing stupor or narcosis
4.3 7.5
Pronunciation: /nɑːr.kə.ˈtaɪ.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /nɑː.kə.ˈtaɪ.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
Inducing stupor or a narcotic state; causing drowsiness or unconsciousness, especially by means of a drug; causing narcosis.

Examples

Narcotizing drug effects
She avoided painkillers with narcotizing properties, preferring to stay alert after the operation.
The narcotizing fumes from the chemicals quickly made the workers drowsy.
The anesthesia had a narcotizing effect on the patient, causing him to fall into a deep sleep.
Some plants contain narcotising compounds that can have powerful effects on the nervous system.
The narcotising properties of the medication caused the patient to fall into a deep sleep.
The doctor administered a narcotising injection before the surgery began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anesthetizing - causing loss of sensation, especially by drugs
  • synonym: sedative - having a calming or sleep-inducing effect
  • antonym: stimulant - causing increased alertness or activity, opposite of narcotizing
  • antonym: stimulating - making active or more alert, opposite of narcotising
