linstock

noun
stick used to fire cannons (historical)
7.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪn.stɑk / (AmE), / ˈlɪn.stɒk / (BrE)
A stick about a meter long with a pointed end and a forked head for holding a lighted match, formerly used to fire cannons.

Examples

A linstock stick
A lighted match was fitted into the forked end of the linstock during the demonstration.
Museum guides showed visitors how a linstock was used before the invention of lanyard firing.
The gunner held the linstock carefully as he prepared to fire the cannon.
