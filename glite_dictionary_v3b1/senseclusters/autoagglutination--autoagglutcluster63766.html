autoagglutination

noun
self-cell clumping
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtoʊəˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊəˌɡluːtɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the clumping together of an individual's own cells (such as blood cells or bacteria), either due to antibodies or spontaneously

Examples

Certain autoimmune diseases can cause autoagglutination of red blood cells, leading to complications.

The laboratory observed autoagglutination in the blood sample, indicating a possible underlying condition.
Researchers noted that autoagglutination can interfere with accurate blood typing results.
Some bacterial strains exhibit autoagglutination when grown in certain culture media.
