Bayes' postulate

noun
assumption of equal probabilities in Bayes' theorem
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪzɪz ˈpɑstjələt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪzɪz ˈpɒstjʊlət / (BrE)
In statistics, an assumption that the probabilities of different causes are equal when no further information is available, often used when applying Bayes' theorem.

Examples

Bayes' postulate in statistics
In many statistical models, Bayes' postulate is invoked to allow for further analysis despite ignorance.
The scientist applied Bayes' postulate when lacking specific prior information about the probabilities.
When the prior probabilities are unknown, Bayes' postulate assumes all causes are equally likely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bayes' theorem - a mathematical formula for determining conditional probability
