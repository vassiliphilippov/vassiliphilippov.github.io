Constantinople (Sixth Ecumenical Council)

noun
sixth ecumenical council, 680–681
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.stæn.tɪˈnoʊ.pəl / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.stæn.tɪˈnəʊ.pəl / (BrE)
the sixth ecumenical council held in 680–681 in Constantinople, which condemned Monothelitism and defined that Christ has two wills, divine and human.

Examples

Constantinople council 680
Scholars often refer to Constantinople III when discussing the controversy over Monothelitism.
The council of Constantinople in 680–681 condemned Monothelitism and clarified Christological doctrine.
The decisions of Constantinople (Sixth Ecumenical Council) had a lasting impact on Christian doctrine.
