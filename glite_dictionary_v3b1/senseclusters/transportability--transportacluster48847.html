transportability

noun
ability to be transferred
6.1
Pronunciation: /træns.pɔːr.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ /; / /træns.pɑːr.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /træns.pɔː.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ /; / /træn.spɔː.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to be moved, transferred, or carried from one place, system, or context to another without significant loss or change

Examples

Transportability of data
The transportability of medical records between hospitals improves patient care and efficiency.
Software transportability allows programs to run on different operating systems without modification.
The transportability of benefits is important for employees who move between countries for work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immobility - the state of being unable to move or be moved
  • synonym: portability - the ability to be easily carried or moved
