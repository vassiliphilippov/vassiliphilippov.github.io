limp

adjective
not firm, soft, lacking rigidity
Pronunciation: / lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
soft, weak, or lacking firmness, energy, or strength

Examples

Limp lettuce
After a long run, her muscles felt limp and exhausted.
His handshake was so limp that it felt as if he had no strength in his hand.
The lettuce had become limp after sitting in the fridge for too long.
After several hours in the sun, the children grew limp and tired.
His limp response showed he had little interest in the discussion.
She gave a limp handshake that made a poor impression at the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flaccid - soft and hanging loosely; lacking firmness or stiffness
  • synonym: listless - lacking energy or enthusiasm
  • antonym: rigid - stiff; unable to bend or be forced out of shape
  • antonym: vital - full of life or energy
