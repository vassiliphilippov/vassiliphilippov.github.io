pay the piper

verb
face the consequences of actions
Pronunciation: / peɪ ðə ˈpaɪpər / (AmE), / peɪ ðə ˈpaɪpə / (BrE)
to accept the unpleasant consequences of one's actions or decisions

Examples

To pay the piper finally
After years of overspending, he finally had to pay the piper by selling his house.
If you break the rules, you will eventually have to pay the piper.
She skipped her assignments all semester and had to pay the piper at exam time.
