chiffonade

verb
to cut into thin strips
8.8
Pronunciation: / ʃɪfəˈnɑd / (AmE), / ʃɪfəˈnɑːd / (BrE)
to cut leafy vegetables or herbs into thin, ribbon-like strips, usually for use as a garnish or in cooking

Examples

To chiffonade spinach
The chef asked her to chiffonade the spinach before adding it to the soup.
To garnish the pasta, simply chiffonade some fresh basil leaves and sprinkle them on top.
He learned how to chiffonade herbs in his first cooking class.
