scar tissue

noun
connective tissue that forms a scar
1.7 3.0
Pronunciation: /skɑːr ˈtɪʃ.uː/ /; / /skɑːr ˈtɪs.juː/ (AmE), /skɑː ˈtɪs.juː/ /; / /skɑː ˈtɪʃ.uː/ (BrE)
the tissue that forms over a wound or injury as part of healing, replacing normal tissue and resulting in a scar

Examples

Scar tissue forms
Burn victims often develop extensive scar tissue that can restrict movement and require ongoing treatment.
Physical therapy can help break down scar tissue and improve mobility after an injury.
The surgeon explained that some of the pain was due to scar tissue forming around the incision site.
After surgery, he developed a small area of scar tissue near the incision.
Sometimes, scar tissue can cause pain long after the original injury has healed.
The therapist worked to break down the scar tissue in her shoulder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cicatrix - a scar resulting from the formation of new connective tissue
  • degree higher: keloid - a raised, thickened type of scar tissue
  • synonym: scar - a mark left on the skin after a wound or injury has healed
