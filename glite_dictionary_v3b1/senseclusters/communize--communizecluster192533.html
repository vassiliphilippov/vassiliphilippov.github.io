communize

verb
make property owned collectively by the state
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmjəˌnaɪz /; / ˈkɑː.mjəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊnaɪz /; / ˈkɒm.juː.naɪz / (BrE)
to make property, land, or resources collectively owned or to bring something in line with Communist principles

Examples

To communize land
After the revolution, the new regime sought to communize agricultural land throughout the region.
Efforts to communize the factories were met with resistance from local workers.
The party's platform aimed to communize industries considered vital to national security.
Radical leaders attempted to communize the land owned by wealthy farmers.
Some historians debate whether it is possible to communize a society without force.
The new regime sought to communize the entire economy within five years.
After the revolution, many institutions were rapidly communised to reflect the new ideology.
The new regime sought to communise the country's industries and agriculture.
There were heated debates on whether it was wise to communise private property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communize - to make something Communist
  • synonym: nationalize - to bring under state ownership or control
  • antonym: privatize - to transfer from public or state ownership to private control
