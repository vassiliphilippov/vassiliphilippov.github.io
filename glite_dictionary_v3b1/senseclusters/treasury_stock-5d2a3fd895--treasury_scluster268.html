treasury stock

noun
stock repurchased and held by the company
3.6 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛʒəri stɑk / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri stɒk / (BrE)
Shares that have been repurchased by the issuing corporation, held by the company and not considered outstanding; they do not have voting rights or pay dividends.

Examples

Treasury stock shares
Shares held as treasury stock do not have voting rights or receive dividends.
The company increased its treasury stock by buying back 100,000 shares from the open market.
To improve its financial ratios, the board authorized a share repurchase, adding more to its treasury stock.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outstanding shares - shares currently held by investors, excluding treasury stock
