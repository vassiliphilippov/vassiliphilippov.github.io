Milton Friedman

proper noun
American economist, monetarism advocate
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪl.tən ˈfriːd.mən/ (AmE), /ˈmɪl.tən ˈfriːd.mən/ (BrE)
(1912–2006) A prominent American economist known for his advocacy of monetarism and opposition to government intervention in the economy.

Examples

Milton Friedman economist
Milton Friedman won the Nobel Prize in Economics for his work on consumption analysis and monetary history.
