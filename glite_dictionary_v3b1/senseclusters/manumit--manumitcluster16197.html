manumit

verb
release from slavery or servitude
Pronunciation: / ˌmæn.jəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌman.juːˈmɪt / (BrE)
to release someone from slavery or servitude; to set free

Examples

To manumit slaves
He hoped to manumit all those held in bondage in his colony.
Some Roman masters would occasionally manumit loyal servants as a reward for their service.
The landowner decided to manumit his slaves after years of campaigning by abolitionists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emancipate - to set free, especially from legal, social, or political restrictions
  • antonym: enslave - to make someone a slave
