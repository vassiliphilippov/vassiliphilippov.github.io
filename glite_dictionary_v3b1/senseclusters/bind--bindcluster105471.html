bind

verb
make someone obligated by agreement or duty
Pronunciation: / baɪnd / (AmE), / baɪnd / (BrE)
to make someone feel obligated or legally required to do something by an agreement, contract, promise, or duty

Examples

Bind by a promise
His promise to help her binds him even now.
The contract will bind both parties for five years.
You are bound by law to pay your taxes on time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - to release from an obligation
  • synonym: obligate - to bind legally or morally
