point of order

noun
formal objection about meeting procedure
2.7 5.1
Pronunciation: /pɔɪnt əv ˈɔːr.dər/ /; / /pɔɪnt ə ˈɔːr.dər/ /; / /pɔɪnt əv ˈɑːr.dər/ (AmE), /pɔɪnt əv ˈɔː.də/ /; / /pɔɪnt ə ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
an interjection or question raised in a formal meeting to call attention to a possible breach of the rules or improper procedure

Examples

Raise a point of order
A point of order was raised regarding the confusion over voting procedures.

The chair recognized her point of order and paused the debate.
