incoherent

adjective
not having a definite phase relation (physics, waves)
7.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.koʊ.ˈhɪr.ənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.kəʊ.ˈhɪə.rənt/ (BrE)
(Physics) of waves, having no stable or definite phase relationship with each other.

Examples

Incoherent light waves
Because sunlight is incoherent, its waves do not reinforce each other in interference experiments.
Lasers produce coherent light, while ordinary bulbs emit incoherent waves.
Radio emissions from distant galaxies are often incoherent due to variable origins and timing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coherent - (physics) of waves, having a constant phase relationship with each other
