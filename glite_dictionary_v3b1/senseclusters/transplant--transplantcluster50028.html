transplant

verb
move organ to another body
Pronunciation: / trænsˈplænt / (AmE), / trænsˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to move an organ or tissue from one body and place it into another, typically as a medical procedure

Examples

To transplant a heart
Doctors successfully transplanted a liver from a donor after hours of surgery.
The surgeons will transplant a healthy kidney into the patient tomorrow morning.
With modern techniques, it's possible to transplant hearts, kidneys, and even lungs.
