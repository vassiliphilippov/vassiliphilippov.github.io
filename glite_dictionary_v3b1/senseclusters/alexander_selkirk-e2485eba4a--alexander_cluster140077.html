Alexander Selkirk

proper noun
Scottish sailor marooned on an island, inspiration for 'Robinson Crusoe'
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪɡˈzæn dər ˈsɛl kɜrk / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændə ˈsɛl kɜːk / (BrE)
(1676–1721) A Scottish sailor who was marooned on an uninhabited island off the coast of Chile for five years, and whose experiences are said to have inspired Daniel Defoe's novel 'Robinson Crusoe.'

Examples

Alexander Selkirk the sailor
After returning to England, Alexander Selkirk became a figure of public interest.
Historians believe Alexander Selkirk's ordeal inspired the character of Robinson Crusoe.
The adventures of Alexander Selkirk have fascinated readers for centuries.
