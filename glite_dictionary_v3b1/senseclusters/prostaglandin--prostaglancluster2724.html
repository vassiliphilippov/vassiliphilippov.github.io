prostaglandin

noun
hormone-like substance in tissues
8.6 6.8
Pronunciation: /proʊ.stə.ˈglæn.dɪn/ /; / /prəʊ.stə.ˈglæn.dɪn/ (AmE), /prɒ.stə.ˈglæn.dɪn/ (BrE)
any of a group of physiologically active substances, resembling hormones, found in many tissues, which influence processes such as inflammation, blood pressure, and contraction of smooth muscle

Examples

Prostaglandin production
Certain painkillers work by blocking the production of prostaglandin in the body.
Researchers discovered elevated levels of prostaglandin in patients suffering from chronic inflammation.
The body's response to injury often includes the release of prostaglandin, leading to swelling and pain.
