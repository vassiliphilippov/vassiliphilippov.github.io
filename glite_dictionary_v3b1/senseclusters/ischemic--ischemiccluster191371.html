ischemic

adjective
relating to or affected by ischemia
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskimɪk /; / ɪsˈkiːmɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈskiːmɪk /; / ɪsˈkiːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by ischemia (a restriction in blood supply to tissues, causing a shortage of oxygen needed for cellular metabolism).

Examples

Ischemic injury
After the surgery, there were concerns about ischemic injury to the tissues.
An ischemic heart attack occurs when the blood flow to part of the heart is blocked.
The MRI revealed an ischemic stroke affecting the patient's left hemisphere.
Chronic ischaemic heart disease is a major cause of mortality worldwide.
Doctors identified the tissue as ischaemic due to the sudden loss of blood flow.
The patient suffered an ischaemic stroke that required immediate treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ischemic - relating to or affected by ischemia (alternative American spelling)
  • antonym: nonischemic - not relating to or affected by ischemia
