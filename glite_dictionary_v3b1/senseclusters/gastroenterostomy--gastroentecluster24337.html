gastroenterostomy

noun
surgical opening between stomach and small intestine
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæstroʊˌɛntəˈrɑstəmi / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˌɛntəˈrɒstəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an opening is created between the stomach and the small intestine, usually to bypass a blocked or damaged part of the digestive tract

Examples

A gastroenterostomy surgery
A gastroenterostomy may be necessary when normal digestive pathways are blocked by disease.
The patient recovered well after undergoing a gastroenterostomy to relieve his stomach obstruction.
The surgical team discussed the risks and benefits of performing a gastroenterostomy in this case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colostomy - surgical creation of an opening from the colon to the surface of the body
  • synonym: gastrectomy - surgical removal of part or all of the stomach
