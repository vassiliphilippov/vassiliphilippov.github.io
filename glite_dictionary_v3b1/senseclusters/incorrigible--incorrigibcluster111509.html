incorrigible

adjective
not able to be corrected or reformed
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɔr.ɪ.dʒə.bəl /; / ɪnˈkɔrɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒr.ɪ.dʒə.bəl /; / ɪnˈkɒrɪdʒəbl / (BrE)
impossible to correct, improve, or reform (used about people or things)

Examples

An incorrigible troublemaker
Despite numerous detentions, the boy remained incorrigible in his disruptive behavior.
Her friends tried to change her mind, but she was incorrigible in her beliefs.
Many felt that the criminal was incorrigible and unlikely to ever be rehabilitated.
Despite many attempts at reform, the old system proved incorrigible.
He is considered an incorrigible optimist no matter what happens.
The classroom was left in an incorrigible state after the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incurable - impossible to cure or reform
  • antonym: reformable - able to be improved or corrected
  • synonym: unmanageable - difficult or impossible to manage
