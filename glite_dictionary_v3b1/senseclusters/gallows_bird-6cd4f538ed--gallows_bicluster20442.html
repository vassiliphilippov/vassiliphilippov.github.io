gallows bird

noun
person deserving to be hanged
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæloʊz bɝd / (AmE), / ˈɡaləʊz bɜːd / (BrE)
A person thought to deserve hanging, especially a persistent criminal or villain.

Examples

A gallows bird criminal
In the old novel, the villain was frequently referred to as a gallows bird by townsfolk.
Many believed the notorious thief was a true gallows bird, destined for the noose.
The sheriff called him a gallows bird after catching him robbing the stagecoach again.
