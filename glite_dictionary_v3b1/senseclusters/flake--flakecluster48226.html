flake

noun
a small piece, especially of snow
3.5
Pronunciation: / fleɪk / (AmE), / fleɪk / (BrE)
a small, thin, flat piece that has broken off from something larger, such as snow, glass, or paint

Examples

A flake of snow
A single flake of snow landed on her cheek.
Delicate flakes drifted down from the gray winter sky.
He caught a flake on his glove and examined its intricate pattern.
A flake of glass landed on the table during the accident.
He found a flake of blue stone in the sand.
She brushed a flake of paint from her shoulder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chip - a small fragment broken off from a larger object
  • synonym: shard - a piece of broken ceramic, metal, glass, or rock
  • synonym: snowflake - a single crystal of snow
