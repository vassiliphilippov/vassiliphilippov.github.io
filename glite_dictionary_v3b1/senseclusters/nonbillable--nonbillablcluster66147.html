nonbillable

noun
unbilled work unit
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈbɪləbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈbɪləbəl / (BrE)
a unit of work, time, or expense that is not charged to a client or counted as billable

Examples

A nonbillable task
The hours spent on training sessions were marked as nonbillable in the project report.
Staff meetings are usually considered nonbillable and are not charged to any client account.
She tracked both billable and nonbillable activities to manage her workload efficiently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: billable - a unit of work or time that can be charged to a client
