denaturalization

noun
loss of naturalness
8.8
Pronunciation: /diː.nætʃ.ə.rəl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.nætʃ.ə.rəl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.nætʃ.ə.rəl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.nætʃ.ə.rəl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making something lose its natural qualities, causing it to appear artificial or not genuine

Examples

Denaturalization of food
The denaturalization of traditional foods has led to concerns about health and authenticity.
Some artists criticize the denaturalization of landscapes through excessive urban development.
The denaturalization of emotions in modern society can make genuine feelings seem rare.
