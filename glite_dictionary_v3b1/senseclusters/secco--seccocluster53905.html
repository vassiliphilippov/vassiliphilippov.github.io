secco

noun
painting on dry plaster
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsek.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈsek.əʊ/ (BrE)
a painting technique in which pigments are applied to dry plaster rather than wet plaster

Examples

Secco painting
The artist chose the secco method to restore the faded colors on the ancient wall.
Unlike true fresco, secco allows for more detailed work after the plaster has dried.
Conservationists debated whether to use secco or fresco techniques for the mural restoration.
