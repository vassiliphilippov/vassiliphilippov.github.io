stand to reason

verb
to be logical or obvious
Pronunciation: / stænd tə ˈɹiːzən / (AmE), / stænd tə ˈɹiːzən / (BrE)
to be logical or clear from the facts; to be obvious or natural given the situation

Examples

It stands to reason
Given the circumstances, it stands to reason that she would be upset.
It stands to reason that you’ll improve if you keep practicing every day.
With prices rising, it stands to reason that many people will cut back on spending.
