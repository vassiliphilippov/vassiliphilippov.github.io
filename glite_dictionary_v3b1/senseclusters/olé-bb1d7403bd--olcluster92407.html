olé

interjection
exclamation of approval
8.6
Pronunciation: / oʊˈleɪ / (AmE), / əʊˈleɪ / (BrE)
an exclamation used to express approval, excitement, or encouragement, especially during performances or sports events

Examples

Shout olé at a show
The audience shouted olé as the matador completed his daring move.
With every impressive step, the crowd responded with a loud olé.
After the guitarist's solo, someone in the front row yelled olé in admiration.
The audience shouted olé as the dancer finished her routine with a flourish.
When the team scored the final goal, fans erupted in cheers of olé throughout the stadium.
He raised his hat and called out olé to celebrate his friend's success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boo - an exclamation expressing disapproval or dissatisfaction
  • synonym: bravo - an exclamation expressing approval or applause
