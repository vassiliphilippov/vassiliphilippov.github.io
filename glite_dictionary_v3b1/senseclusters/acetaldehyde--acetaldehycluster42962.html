acetaldehyde

noun
a volatile liquid aldehyde used in industry
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌæsəˈtældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈtældəˌhaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile liquid aldehyde, CH₃CHO, used mainly to manufacture acetic acid, perfumes, and drugs

Examples

Acetaldehyde chemical compound
Acetaldehyde is produced naturally in plants, coffee, and ripe fruit as well as during the fermentation of alcohol.
In industry, acetaldehyde is a key intermediate in the production of acetic acid and various chemicals.
Prolonged exposure to acetaldehyde can cause irritation of the eyes, skin, and respiratory system.
