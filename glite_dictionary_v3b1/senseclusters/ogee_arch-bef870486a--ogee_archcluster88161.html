ogee arch

noun
a pointed arch with S-shaped sides
Pronunciation: / oʊˈdʒi ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / əʊˈdʒi ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
A pointed arch with double-curved S-shaped sides, commonly found in Gothic architecture.

Examples

The ogee arch shape
Medieval cathedrals often used the ogee arch to create an elegant, intricate appearance.
Restoring the old manor house involved repairing several damaged ogee arches.
The windows are framed by beautiful ogee arches typical of the period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ogee - an S-shaped curve consisting of two arcs that curve in opposite senses
