scutch

verb
to clean plant fibers
9.9
Pronunciation: /skʌtʃ/ (AmE), /skʌtʃ/ (BrE)
to beat or scrape plant fibers (such as flax or hemp) to remove woody parts or impurities and prepare them for spinning

Examples

To scutch flax
Workers spent the afternoon scutching flax to prepare it for spinning into linen.
After harvesting, the next step is to scutch the hemp to separate the fibers from the stalks.
Traditional methods of scutching cotton are still used in some rural communities.
Workers scutch the flax after retting to remove the woody stems.
It is important to scutch the hemp thoroughly before spinning the fibers into thread.
The old mill was used to scutch and process local flax crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: card - to comb fibers to separate and clean them
  • synonym: hackle - to comb plant fibers to separate them
  • synonym: rett - to soak plant stems to loosen fibers
