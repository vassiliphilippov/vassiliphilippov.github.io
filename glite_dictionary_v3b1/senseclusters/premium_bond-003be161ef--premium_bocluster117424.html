Premium Bond

noun
government bond with lottery prizes
7.2 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːmiəm bɑnd / (AmE), / ˈpriːmiəm bɒnd / (BrE)
A government savings bond that pays no interest but gives holders the chance to win prizes through a regular lottery.

Examples

Premium Bonds prize draw
Instead of earning interest, holders of Premium Bonds have the chance to win cash prizes.
Many British savers prefer Premium Bonds because of the excitement of monthly prize draws.
My grandmother bought me a Premium Bond when I was born, hoping I might someday win a prize.
