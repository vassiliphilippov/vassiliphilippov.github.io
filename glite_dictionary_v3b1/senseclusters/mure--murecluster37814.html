mure

verb
to wall in
Pronunciation: / mjʊr / (AmE), / mjʊə / (BrE)
to shut up or confine someone or something within walls; to wall in, often in a literal or figurative sense

Examples

To mure someone alive
The cruel king ordered his enemies to be mured alive within the castle walls.
Legends tell of a treasure mured behind a secret stone in the old abbey.
In her grief, she felt as if she had mured her heart away from the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immure - to enclose or confine within walls
  • antonym: release - to set free from confinement
