polychete worm

noun
bristle-bearing marine annelid worm
8.1 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌkit wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkiːt wɜːm / (BrE)
A chiefly marine annelid worm belonging to the class Polychaeta, having paired, bristle-bearing appendages and typically both male and female organs.

Examples

A polychete worm species
During the biology class, students observed a live polychete worm under the microscope.
Many species of polychete worm play a vital role in marine ecosystems by recycling organic material.
The sandy seabed was teeming with polychete worms, each leaving tiny trails behind.
