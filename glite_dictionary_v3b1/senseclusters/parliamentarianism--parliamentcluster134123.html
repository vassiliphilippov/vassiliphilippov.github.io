parliamentarianism

noun
support for parliament
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrləməntəˈrɪənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɑːlɪəˌmɛntəˈrɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
support for or advocacy of a parliamentary system of government, especially as opposed to royalist rule.

Examples

Support for parliamentarianism
During the English Civil War, parliamentarianism was a defining political stance against the monarchy.
The rise of parliamentarianism changed the balance of power in many European countries.
His research focuses on the development of parliamentarianism in seventeenth-century England.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parliamentarism - the principle of government by a parliament
  • antonym: royalism - support for rule by a monarch or king
