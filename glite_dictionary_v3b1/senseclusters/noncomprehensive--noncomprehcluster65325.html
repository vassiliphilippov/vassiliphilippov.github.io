noncomprehensive

adjective
not comprehensive; incomplete coverage
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkɑmˌprɛhənˈsɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkɒmprɪˈhɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Not comprehensive; not including all or nearly all elements or aspects of something.

Examples

A noncomprehensive list
Faculty expressed concern that the curriculum was noncomprehensive and omitted several key topics.
The report was criticized for its noncomprehensive analysis of the current economic issues.
This insurance policy offers only noncomprehensive coverage for medical expenses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects
