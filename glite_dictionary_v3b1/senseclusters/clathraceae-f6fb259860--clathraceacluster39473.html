Clathraceae

noun
family of fleshy fungi
9.9
Pronunciation: / klæˈθreɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / klæˈθræsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of fleshy fungi resembling stinkhorns, found chiefly in tropical regions.

Examples

Clathraceae fungi family
Members of the Clathraceae family often emit a strong odor to attract insects.
Recent studies have identified several new species within the Clathraceae.
The Clathraceae are known for their distinctive, lattice-like fruiting bodies.
