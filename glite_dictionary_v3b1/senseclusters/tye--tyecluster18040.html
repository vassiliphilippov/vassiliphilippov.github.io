tye

verb
to fasten with rope
9.9
Pronunciation: / taɪ / (AmE), / taɪ / (BrE)
to fasten or bind with a rope, cord, or similar material (archaic or dialectal spelling of tie).

Examples

To tye a bundle
He began to tye the bundle with twine before loading it onto the cart.
Sailors would tye the sails securely before a storm approached.
In old letters, you may find instructions to tye parcels with string.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tie - to fasten or secure with a string, rope, or cord
  • antonym: unbind - to release from being tied or fastened
