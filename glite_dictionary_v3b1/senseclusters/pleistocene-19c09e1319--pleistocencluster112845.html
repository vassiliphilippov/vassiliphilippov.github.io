Pleistocene

noun
geological epoch with major glaciations
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈplaɪstəˌsin / (AmE), / ˈplaɪstəsiːn / (BrE)
the geological epoch lasting from about two million to eleven thousand years ago, characterized by repeated glaciations and the evolution of humans

Examples

The Pleistocene epoch
Many large mammals, such as the woolly mammoth, lived during the Pleistocene.
Scientists study Pleistocene fossils to learn about ancient climates and ecosystems.
The end of the Pleistocene marked the disappearance of several species due to climate change.
