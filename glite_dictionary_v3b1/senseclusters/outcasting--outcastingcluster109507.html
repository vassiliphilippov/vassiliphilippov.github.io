outcasting

noun
exclusion from group
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of excluding someone from a group or society

Examples

The outcasting of students
The outcasting of new students made it difficult for them to feel welcome at school.
Social outcasting can have long-term effects on a person's mental health.
Historically, outcasting was used as a punishment for breaking community rules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inclusion - the act of including someone in a group or activity
  • synonym: ostracism - the practice of excluding someone from a society or group
