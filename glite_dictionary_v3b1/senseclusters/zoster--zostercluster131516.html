zoster

noun
shingles; painful nerve rash
7.8 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆzoÊŠstÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆzÉ’stÉ™ / (BrE)
A medical condition, also known as shingles, characterized by painful skin eruptions along the path of a nerve caused by reactivation of the varicella-zoster virus.

Examples

zoster infection
People who had chickenpox as children are at risk of developing zoster later in life.
The doctor diagnosed the rash as zoster after noticing the band-like pattern on her side.
Treatment for zoster focuses on relieving pain and reducing the risk of complications.
