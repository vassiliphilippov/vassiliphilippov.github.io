molybdenum steel

noun
steel alloy with molybdenum
6.3 6.5
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈlɪb.də.nəm stiːl/ /; / /mə.ˈlɪb.dɪ.nəm stiːl/ (AmE), /mə.ˈlɪb.də.nəm stiːl/ /; / /mə.ˈlɪb.dɪ.nəm stiːl/ (BrE)
a type of steel alloy containing 10–15% molybdenum, valued for its hardness and durability, and similar in properties to tungsten steel

Examples

molybdenum steel alloy
Engineers recommend molybdenum steel for parts requiring durability under high stress.
Many industrial tools use molybdenum steel because of its strength and resistance to wear.
The new turbine blades are made from molybdenum steel to withstand extreme heat and pressure.
