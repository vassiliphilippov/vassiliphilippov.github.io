hyoid

noun
U-shaped bone at the base of the tongue
9.3 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhaɪ.ɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈhaɪ.ɔɪd/ (BrE)
a U-shaped bone at the base of the tongue that supports the tongue muscles and is not directly attached to any other bone

Examples

A hyoid bone
A fractured hyoid can indicate trauma to the neck area.
In humans, the hyoid is unique because it does not articulate directly with any other bone.
The hyoid bone is essential for speech and swallowing.
