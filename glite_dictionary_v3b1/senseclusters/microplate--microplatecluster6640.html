microplate

noun
small tectonic plate
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌpleɪt / (BrE)
a small tectonic plate that forms a minor or fragmentary section of the Earth's lithosphere and moves independently of larger plates

Examples

Pacific microplate
Scientists discovered a new microplate beneath the Pacific Ocean during their research.
The movement of the microplate caused minor earthquakes in the region.
Geologists study how each microplate interacts with larger tectonic plates.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: tectonic plate - a large, rigid section of the Earth's lithosphere that moves as a unit
