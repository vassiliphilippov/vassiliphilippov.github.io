Cooper's hawk

noun
a North American hawk with bluish-grey feathers
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkuːpərz hɑk / (AmE), / ˈkuːpəz hɔːk / (BrE)
A medium-sized, bluish-grey North American hawk known for its rapid, darting flight, found mainly in woodlands and forests.

Examples

A Cooper's hawk flying
A Cooper's hawk swooped down to catch a songbird in the backyard.
Researchers tracked the migration routes of several Cooper's hawks across the continent.
The Cooper's hawk is often seen gliding through dense woodlands in search of prey.
