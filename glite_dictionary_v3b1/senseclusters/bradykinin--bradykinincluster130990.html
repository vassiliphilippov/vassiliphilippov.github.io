bradykinin

noun
vasodilator peptide
Pronunciation: / ˌbreɪdiˈkaɪnɪn / (AmE), / ˌbrædɪˈkɪnɪn / (BrE)
a peptide in the body that causes blood vessels to widen and can produce pain or inflammation

Examples

Bradykinin peptide
Scientists discovered that bradykinin plays a key role in the body's inflammatory response.
Certain medications can increase the levels of bradykinin, leading to swelling or pain.
The release of bradykinin causes blood vessels to expand and may result in a drop in blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: histamine - a compound involved in immune responses and widening blood vessels
  • antonym: vasoconstrictor - a substance that narrows blood vessels
