live in the past

verb
dwell on past experiences
Pronunciation: / lɪv ɪn ðə pæst / (AmE), / lɪv ɪn ðə pɑːst / (BrE)
to dwell on or remain preoccupied with memories, emotions, or experiences from the past, often at the expense of engaging with the present

Examples

To live in the past sadly
Her tendency to live in the past made it difficult for her to enjoy the present.
It's hard to move forward if you live in the past and keep thinking about old mistakes.
Many people who live in the past struggle to embrace new opportunities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: let go - stop holding on to the past or to memories
