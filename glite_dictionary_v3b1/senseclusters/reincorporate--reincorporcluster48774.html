reincorporate

verb
to add again
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈkɔːrpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈkɔːpəreɪt / (BrE)
to add or include something again into a group, system, organization, or structure

Examples

To reincorporate old members
After the merger, the company decided to reincorporate several former employees into the new team.
The chef chose to reincorporate the original ingredients after experimenting with new recipes.
Following the review, the committee voted to reincorporate the omitted section into the final report.
After several years, the museum decided to reincorporate the historic paintings into its main exhibit.
The teacher plans to reincorporate group discussions into the curriculum next semester.
They hope to reincorporate traditional methods alongside modern technology in their production process.
