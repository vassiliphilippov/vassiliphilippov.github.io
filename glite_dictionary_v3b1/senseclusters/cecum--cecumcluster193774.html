cecum

noun
beginning pouch of the large intestine
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːkəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːkəm / (BrE)
a pouch located at the beginning of the large intestine, into which the ileum opens

Examples

The cecum pouch
An obstruction near the cecum can cause abdominal pain and discomfort.
Food passes from the small intestine into the cecum during digestion.
The surgeon carefully examined the cecum for signs of inflammation.
Food passes from the ileum into the caecum before entering the large intestine.
In certain animals, the caecum plays an important role in digesting plant material.
The surgeon examined the caecum for signs of infection during the procedure.
