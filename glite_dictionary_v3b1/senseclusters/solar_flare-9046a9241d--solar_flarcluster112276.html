solar flare

noun
burst of radiation from the sun
2.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.lər fleər/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.lə fleə/ (BrE)
A sudden, intense burst of high-energy radiation emitted from the sun's surface, often linked to sunspots and causing disruptions such as radio interference.

Examples

A solar flare burst
A powerful solar flare can cause spectacular auroras near Earth's poles.
Scientists observed a solar flare disrupting communication satellites last week.
The radio blackout was traced back to a recent solar flare on the sun's surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sunspot - a dark area on the sun's surface with lower temperature, often linked to solar activity
