defense force

noun
military group defending a country or area
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns fɔrs / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns fɔːs / (BrE)
an organized group or body of people trained and equipped and responsible for defending a country or area against attack, typically serving as its military or armed forces.

Examples

The defense force
He decided to join the defense force after graduating from university.
Natural disasters often see the defense force assisting with rescue and recovery efforts.
The country's defense force was well-prepared to respond to any threats.
After graduating from university, he decided to join the defence force to serve his country.
Many countries have both a regular army and a volunteer defence force for emergencies.
The nation’s defence force responded quickly to the threat along the border.

Related Terms

  • synonym: army - a large organized body of armed personnel trained for war
  • synonym: military - the armed forces of a country
  • synonym: militia - a military force that is not part of a regular army
  • antonym: police force - an organized body of people maintaining order and enforcing law
