apoptosis

noun
programmed cell death
8.2 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæp.əpˈtoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæp.ɒpˈtəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
a process by which a cell deliberately destroys itself through regulated biochemical events, often to control cell number or remove damaged cells

Examples

Apoptosis process
During development, apoptosis helps shape organs by removing unnecessary cells.
Improper regulation of apoptosis can lead to diseases such as cancer or autoimmune disorders.
Researchers found that certain drugs can trigger apoptosis in cancer cells.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cell death - the end of a cell's life by any mechanism
  • antonym: necrosis - accidental cell death caused by injury or disease
