hand-holding

adjective
overly supportive
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændˌhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
providing close, often excessive, guidance or support that limits independence or self-reliance

Examples

Hand-holding teaching style
The teacher's hand-holding style made it hard for students to solve problems on their own.
Some employees dislike hand-holding management because it limits their creativity and independence.
The training program was criticized for being too hand-holding and not challenging enough.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independent - able to do things without help or support
  • synonym: overprotective - giving too much protection or support
