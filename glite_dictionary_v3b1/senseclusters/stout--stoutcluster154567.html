stout

adjective
having rugged physical strength
Pronunciation: / staʊt / (AmE), / staʊt / (BrE)
physically strong, thick, or heavily built (used for people or things)

Examples

A stout rope
Only stout men could carry the supplies across the rough terrain.
The stout sailor braved the stormy seas without hesitation.
The team recruited several stout workers for the physically demanding job.
The stout walls of the castle have stood for centuries.
He wore a stout coat to protect himself from the cold wind.
They built a stout fence to keep the animals out of the garden.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: robust - strong and healthy; sturdy
  • synonym: solid - firm and stable in form
  • synonym: sturdy - strong and solidly built; robust
  • antonym: weak - lacking physical strength or robustness
