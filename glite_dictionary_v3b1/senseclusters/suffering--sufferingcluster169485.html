suffering

noun
acute pain or distress
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfərɪŋ /; / ˈsʌf.ər.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌfərɪŋ /; / ˈsʌf.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the experience or state of pain, distress, or hardship (physical, mental, or emotional)

Examples

Human suffering
He dedicated his life to easing the suffering of others.
Her illness brought years of suffering and hardship to the family.
The refugees endured unimaginable suffering during the war.
Her long illness caused a great deal of suffering for the whole family.
The documentary showed scenes of intense suffering during the famine.
We must do what we can to relieve the suffering of others.
After the accident, he endured years of suffering and uncertainty.
Her suffering was evident to everyone who saw her after the tragedy.
Meditation helped him cope with the emotional suffering caused by his loss.
