resistance pyrometer

noun
temperature-measuring device using resistance
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstəns paɪˈrɑmɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns paɪˈrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
A pyrometer that measures high temperatures by detecting changes in the electrical resistance of a heated wire.

Examples

A resistance pyrometer device
A resistance pyrometer is often used in industries that require precise temperature measurements at very high heat.
The laboratory technician calibrated the resistance pyrometer before conducting the experiment.
Unlike other thermometers, a resistance pyrometer relies on the change in resistance of a wire to measure temperature.

Related Terms
