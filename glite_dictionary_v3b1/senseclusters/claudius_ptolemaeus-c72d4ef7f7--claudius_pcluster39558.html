Claudius Ptolemaeus

proper noun
2nd-century Alexandrian astronomer and mathematician
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːdiəs toʊˌlɛmˈiːəs / (AmE), / ˈklɔːdɪəs təʊlɪˈmeɪəs / (BrE)
An Alexandrian astronomer and mathematician of the 2nd century who proposed the geocentric model of the universe, influential until the Renaissance.

Examples

Claudius Ptolemaeus geocentric model
Claudius Ptolemaeus developed a geocentric model that dominated astronomy for over a thousand years.
Many early maps were influenced by the works of Claudius Ptolemaeus.
The theories of Claudius Ptolemaeus were eventually replaced by the heliocentric model.
