crawlway

noun
narrow crawl passage
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkrɔːlˌweɪ / (BrE)
a narrow passage or tunnel just large enough for a person to crawl through

Examples

A narrow crawlway
The maintenance worker squeezed into the crawlway to inspect the pipes.
Rescuers used a narrow crawlway to reach the trapped miners.
The old building had a crawlway beneath the floor for electrical access.
The explorers squeezed through a tight crawlway to reach the next chamber.
A narrow crawlway connected the two sections of the cave system.
Rescuers had to move carefully through the crawlway to avoid getting stuck.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corridor - a long passage in a building from which doors lead into rooms
  • synonym: crawlspace - a shallow area beneath a building, just high enough to crawl through
  • synonym: shaft - a vertical or sloping passage in a cave or mine
  • synonym: tunnel - an underground or enclosed passage
