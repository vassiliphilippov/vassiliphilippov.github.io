collapse

noun
sudden failure or exhaustion
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæps / (AmE), / kəˈlæps / (BrE)
a sudden falling down, breakdown, or failure of something, such as a person, structure, business, or system

Examples

A sudden collapse
After running the marathon, she was close to collapse from exhaustion.
His physical collapse was attributed to months of overwork and stress.
The sudden collapse of the computer network brought all work to a halt.
After months of instability, a sudden collapse in oil prices devastated the industry.
The government rushed to intervene to prevent a banking collapse.
The stock market collapse in 1929 triggered the Great Depression.
Following weeks of heavy rain, the hillside was threatened with collapse.
The bridge was closed to traffic due to the risk of collapse.
The sudden collapse of the tunnel trapped several workers underground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth or rising prices
  • synonym: breakdown - a failure to function; a sudden collapse
  • synonym: crash - a sudden dramatic decline in prices or value, especially in financial markets
  • synonym: fall - to drop or come down freely under the influence of gravity
  • antonym: recovery - a return to normal functioning after collapse or illness
