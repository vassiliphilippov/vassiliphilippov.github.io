supercool

adjective
below freezing, still liquid
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈkuːl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈkuːl / (BrE)
cooled below its normal freezing point without turning into a solid, especially describing a liquid that remains unfrozen at subfreezing temperatures

Examples

Supercool water
The scientist studied a supercool liquid that did not freeze even at minus ten degrees Celsius.
Water can remain supercool in clean containers if there are no impurities to trigger freezing.
The experiment required a supercool solution to observe the effects of rapid crystallization.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: chill - to make something cold but not frozen
  • antonym: freeze - to become solid due to cold
