floodway

noun
channel for floodwater
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌdˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈflʌdˌweɪ / (BrE)
a channel or area (natural or built) that carries or directs excess floodwater away from populated or important places

Examples

City floodway project
The city constructed a floodway to divert water during the rainy season.
Engineers inspected the floodway after the river rose dangerously high.
A well-maintained floodway can prevent serious damage to nearby homes.
The government constructed a floodway to protect the city from rising river levels during heavy rains.
Engineers inspected the floodway regularly to ensure it could handle sudden surges of water.
After the storm, the floodway successfully diverted water away from residential neighborhoods.
The city built a floodway to protect neighborhoods from seasonal river flooding.
During heavy rains, water quickly filled the floodway and flowed safely away from the town.
Wildlife often gathers near the floodway after storms because of the abundant water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canal - an artificial waterway for navigation or drainage
  • synonym: channel - a passage for water to flow through
  • synonym: dam - a barrier constructed to hold back water and raise its level
  • synonym: levee - an embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river
