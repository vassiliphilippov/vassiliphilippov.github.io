institutional

adjective
uniform or dull, like a large institution
1.9 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.stɪˈtu.ʃə.nəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
(especially of places, products, or services) Characteristic of a large organization, especially by being uniform, dull, or lacking individuality.

Examples

Institutional look
Her office had an institutional atmosphere, with gray walls and identical furniture.
People often complain about the institutional feel of hospitals.
The cafeteria served bland, institutional food to hundreds of students every day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individual - distinct or unique, not uniform or institutional
  • synonym: institutionalized - established as a norm or practice within an institution, often suggesting rigidity
