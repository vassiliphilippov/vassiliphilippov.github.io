zero-coupon security

noun
bond or security sold at discount, no interest payments
9.8 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈziː.roʊ ˈkuː.pɑːn sɪ.ˈkjʊr.ə.tiː/ /; / /ˈzɪ.roʊ ˈkjuː.pɑːn sɪ.ˈkjʊr.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈzɪə.rəʊ ˈkuː.pɒn sɪ.ˈkjʊə.rɪ.tiː/ /; / /ˈziː.rəʊ ˈkuː.pən sɪ.ˈkjʊə.rɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
A security, typically a bond, that does not make periodic interest payments and is issued at a substantial discount to its face value, with the return realized at maturity when the full face value is paid.

Examples

A zero-coupon security bond
He purchased a zero-coupon security as a long-term investment for his retirement fund.
Many investors value the simplicity of a zero-coupon security because there are no periodic interest payments to track.
The zero-coupon security will mature in ten years at its full face value, yielding a profit to the buyer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: zero-coupon bond - a bond that makes no interest payments but is sold at a discount
