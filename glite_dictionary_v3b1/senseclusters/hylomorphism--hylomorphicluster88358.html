hylomorphism

noun
matter and form theory
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪloʊˈmɔːrfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhɪləʊˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical theory that everything is composed of both matter and form

Examples

Hylomorphism theory
Aristotle developed the concept of hylomorphism to explain how objects exist in the world.
Many medieval philosophers adopted hylomorphism as a way to reconcile matter and form.
The debate between hylomorphism and materialism continues in some areas of philosophy today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dualism - the belief that reality consists of two fundamental substances or principles
  • antonym: idealism - the philosophical theory that reality is fundamentally mental or immaterial
  • antonym: materialism - the doctrine that only matter is real and everything can be explained by physical processes
