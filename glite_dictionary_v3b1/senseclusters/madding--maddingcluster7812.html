madding

verb
to drive insane
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmædɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmædɪŋ / (BrE)
to make someone mentally disturbed, extremely angry, or infuriated (archaic or literary)

Examples

To madding a person
The constant noise from the street was madding him to the point of despair.
She found the endless delays at the station utterly madding after a long day.
In the old novel, the hero's jealousy was described as madding his mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful or relaxed
  • synonym: enrage - to make someone very angry
  • synonym: infuriate - to make someone extremely angry
