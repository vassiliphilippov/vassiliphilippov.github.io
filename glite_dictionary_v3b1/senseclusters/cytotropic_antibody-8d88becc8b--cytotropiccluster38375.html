cytotropic antibody

noun
antibody that binds specific cells via Fc region
Pronunciation: / saɪtoʊˈtrɑprɪk ˈæntɪˌbɑdi / (AmE), / saɪtəʊˈtrɒprɪk ˈantɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
an antibody that binds to certain types of cells due to properties of its Fc region, independently of its antigen specificity

Examples

cytotropic antibody test
A test for cytotropic antibody levels may help diagnose certain immune disorders.
Researchers studied how cytotropic antibodies attach to immune cells and affect their function.
The presence of cytotropic antibodies can trigger strong allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
