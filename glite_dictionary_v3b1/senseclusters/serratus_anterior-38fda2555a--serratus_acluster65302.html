serratus anterior

noun
chest muscle that rotates scapula
Pronunciation: / səˌreɪtəs ænˈtɪriər / (AmE), / səˌreɪtəs ænˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a muscle on the side of the chest that rotates the scapula and helps elevate the rib cage

Examples

serratus anterior muscle
Proper push-up technique engages the serratus anterior to support the shoulders.
The serratus anterior plays a vital role in stabilizing the shoulder blade during movement.
Weakness in the serratus anterior can cause winging of the scapula.

Related Terms
