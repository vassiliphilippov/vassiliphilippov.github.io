frontcourt

noun
basketball offensive area
Pronunciation: / ˈfrʌntˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˈfrʌntˌkɔːt / (BrE)
the area of a basketball court near the opponent's basket, or the players (forwards and center) who play in that area

Examples

The frontcourt players
The team's frontcourt dominated the game with strong rebounding and inside scoring.
He was known as one of the best frontcourt players in the league.
After halftime, the coach made changes to the frontcourt lineup to improve defense.
The team's frontcourt dominated the game with their strong defense and rebounding skills.
In the second half, the coach substituted a taller player into the frontcourt to block more shots.
Analysts praised the frontcourt for scoring most of the points in last night's victory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backcourt - the area of a basketball court near a team's own basket
