resampling

noun
changing sampling rate
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɑːmplɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of changing the sampling rate of a digital signal, such as audio or images, often using interpolation or decimation

Examples

Audio resampling
The audio file required resampling before it could be played on the older device.
Image quality can be affected by improper resampling during editing.
Engineers use resampling to match different data formats in digital processing.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: downsampling - reducing the sampling rate of a signal
  • degree higher: upsampling - increasing the sampling rate of a signal
