approximation

noun
act of bringing tissue edges together (medical)
3.4
Pronunciation: / əˌprɑksəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌprɒksɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing together the edges of tissue, especially during surgery, to promote healing

Examples

Surgical approximation
During surgery, careful approximation of the wound edges is essential for proper healing.
Poor approximation can lead to delayed recovery and complications.
Sutures are used to ensure approximation of the skin after an incision.
