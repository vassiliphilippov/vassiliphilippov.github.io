derepression

noun
ending of repression
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːrɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːrɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of ending or reducing political or social repression, leading to greater freedom and civil liberties

Examples

Political derepression
The new government promised derepression and the restoration of basic human rights.
Years of activism finally led to the derepression of political opposition groups.
International observers welcomed the country's gradual derepression after decades of strict control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liberalization - the process of making laws or rules less strict
  • antonym: repression - the act of subduing someone or something by force
