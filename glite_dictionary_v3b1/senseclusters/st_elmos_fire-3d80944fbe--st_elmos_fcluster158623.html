St. Elmo's fire

noun
electrical weather phenomenon; bluish atmospheric glow
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌseɪnt ˈɛlmoʊz ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌseɪnt ˈɛlməʊz ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
A weather phenomenon in which a bluish electrical glow is visible near pointed objects, caused by ionization of the air during thunderstorms.

Examples

Blue St. Elmo's fire
Pilots sometimes report St. Elmo's fire appearing on cockpit windows when flying through thunderstorms.
Sailors often saw St. Elmo's fire glowing on the masts during severe storms at sea.
The mysterious blue light of St. Elmo's fire was once considered an omen by those at sea.
