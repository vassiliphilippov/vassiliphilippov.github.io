staph

noun
type of bacteria (Staphylococcus) causing infections
Pronunciation: / stæf / (AmE), / stæf / (BrE)
A type of spherical Gram-positive bacterium of the genus Staphylococcus, some of which cause infections such as boils or septicemia.

Examples

Staph infection
He was hospitalized with a severe staph infection following surgery.
Some strains of staph have become resistant to antibiotics, making them hard to treat.
The doctor suspected a staph infection after seeing the red, swollen area on her arm.
