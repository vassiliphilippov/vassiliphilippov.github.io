frail

adjective
lacking moral strength or will
Pronunciation: / freɪl / (AmE), / freɪl / (BrE)
lacking moral strength, courage, or will; weak in character or resolve.

Examples

Frail character
Even the most principled leaders sometimes reveal their frail human nature under pressure.
He confessed his frail character after struggling to resist temptation.
In moments of crisis, our frail resolve can lead us to make poor decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resolute - admirably purposeful, determined, and unwavering
  • synonym: weak - lacking the power to resist or endure; not strong
