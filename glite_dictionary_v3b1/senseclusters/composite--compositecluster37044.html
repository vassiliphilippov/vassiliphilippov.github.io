composite

noun
a plant with dense flower heads (Asteraceae family)
4.7
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
a plant of a large family (Compositae or Asteraceae) characterized by dense flower heads composed of many small florets that together resemble a single flower.

Examples

A composite plant
Botanists often study composite species because of their evolutionary success.
Many composites have brightly colored florets that attract pollinators to the dense flower head.
The sunflower is a well-known example of a composite in the plant kingdom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aster - a plant in the Asteraceae family with star-shaped flower heads
  • synonym: daisy - a common type of composite flower
