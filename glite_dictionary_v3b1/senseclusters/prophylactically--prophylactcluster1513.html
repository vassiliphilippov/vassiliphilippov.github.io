prophylactically

adverb
preventively
7.9
Pronunciation: /proʊ.fɪ.ˈlæk.tɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /prə.fɪ.ˈlæk.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (AmE), /prəʊ.fɪ.ˈlæk.tɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /prəʊ.fə.ˈlæk.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (BrE)
in a manner intended to prevent disease or a problem before it occurs

Examples

Used prophylactically before surgery
Doctors sometimes prescribe antibiotics prophylactically before certain surgeries to prevent infection.
The vaccine was administered prophylactically to all staff members at the clinic.
She took medication prophylactically during flu season to reduce her risk of illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preventively - in a way that aims to stop something from happening
  • antonym: therapeutically - in a way that treats or heals a disease or condition
