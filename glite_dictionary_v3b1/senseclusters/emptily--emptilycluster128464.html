emptily

adverb
in a vacant way
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmptɪli / (AmE), / ˈɛmptɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is empty, lacking contents, real feeling, or emotion

Examples

To speak emptily
After the guests left, the house echoed emptily in the quiet night.
She stared emptily out the window, lost in her thoughts.
The abandoned playground stood emptily under the gray sky.
He smiled emptily, not really listening to what was being said.
The room echoed emptily after everyone had left the party.
She repeated the apology emptily, showing no real regret for her actions.
She stared emptily at the wall, not reacting to the news.
After the argument, he spoke emptily, as if he no longer cared.
The child waved emptily at the passing train, lost in thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blankly - in a way that shows no expression or understanding
  • antonym: emotionally - in a way that shows strong feelings
  • antonym: full - containing as much as possible; not empty
  • synonym: hollowly - in a way that lacks real feeling or sincerity
  • antonym: sincerely - in a genuine and heartfelt way
  • synonym: vacantly - in a way that shows emptiness or lack of interest
