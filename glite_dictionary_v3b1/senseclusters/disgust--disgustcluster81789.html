disgust

verb
to cause strong dislike or revulsion
3.7 2.1
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɡʌst / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡʌst / (BrE)
to make someone feel strong revulsion or disapproval

Examples

To disgust someone
She was disgusted by his rude remarks during the meeting.
The foul odor from the garbage truly disgusted everyone in the room.
The idea of eating insects disgusts many people in Western countries.
He was disgusted by their lack of respect for the rules.
Her arrogant attitude disgusts everyone in the office.
The cruelty of the experiment disgusted many viewers.
