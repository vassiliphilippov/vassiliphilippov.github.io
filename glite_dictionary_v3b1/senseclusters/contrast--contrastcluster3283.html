contrast

verb
to compare to show differences
1.7 2.7
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræst / (AmE), / kənˈtrɑːst / (BrE)
to compare two or more things in order to show how they are different, or to be noticeably different when compared

Examples

To contrast two styles
Artists often contrast light and shadow to create a dramatic effect in their paintings.
In her research paper, she contrasted the two theories to demonstrate their opposing viewpoints.
The documentary contrasts urban and rural lifestyles to highlight the challenges people face.
Her bright red coat contrasted sharply with the snowy white background.
The report contrasts the economic growth of two neighboring countries over the past decade.
When you contrast the two paintings, you notice the difference in style and technique.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compare - to examine two or more items to note their similarities and differences
