failure

noun
an act of not succeeding
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪljɚ /; / ˈfeɪljər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
the fact of not achieving what was intended or expected; lack of success or an unsuccessful event

Examples

A failure to win
Despite repeated failures, he kept trying until he succeeded.
Her failure to respond to the invitation disappointed everyone.
The project was considered a failure after it missed all its deadlines.
After several failures, the team finally completed the project successfully.
He regarded his missed opportunity as a personal failure.
The new software launch was a failure because users could not access key features.
A sudden power failure caused all the computers to shut down.
Despite her failure in the competition, she remained optimistic about the future.
The project was considered a complete failure after none of its goals were achieved.
