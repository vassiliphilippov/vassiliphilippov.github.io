generalize

verb
become widespread in the body (medical)
7.4 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɚəˌlaɪz /; / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz /; / ˈdʒɛn(ə)r(ə)laɪz / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) to become widespread and affect the entire body rather than remaining localized.

Examples

Disease generalizes in body
Doctors were concerned that the cancer would generalize, spreading beyond the initial tumor site.
If the infection is not treated quickly, it may generalize and affect multiple organs.
Sepsis can occur when bacteria generalize from a local site to the bloodstream.
Doctors are concerned when a localized illness starts to generalise in the patient.
If left untreated, the infection may generalise and become life-threatening.
Some rashes can generalise from a single spot to cover most of the skin.

Related Terms

  • antonym: localise - (of a disease) to remain confined to a particular area of the body
