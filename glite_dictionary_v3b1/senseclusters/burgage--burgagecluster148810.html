burgage

noun
medieval town tenure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrɡɪdʒ /; / ˈbɜːrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
a property or the system of holding property in a town in medieval times, usually in exchange for rent, sometimes giving the right to vote

Examples

A burgage property
The merchant's house stood on land held by burgage in the center of the medieval town.
Under the burgage system, townspeople paid rent to the local lord for their property.
Historical records show that burgage plots shaped the layout of many old English towns.
In medieval England, owning a burgage often meant having a say in local government.
The right to vote in some boroughs was tied to possession of a burgage property.
Historical records show that burgage plots were frequently bought and sold for political influence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: serfdom - the status of peasants under feudalism, tied to land and subject to a lord
  • synonym: tenement - a piece of land held by an owner or tenant
  • synonym: tenure - the holding or occupation of land or property
