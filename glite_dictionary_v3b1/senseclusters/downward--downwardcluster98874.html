downward

adverb
toward a lower level or position
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnwɚd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwəd / (BrE)
In or toward a lower place, level, or position; from a higher to a lower point.

Examples

Move downward quickly
After the announcement, the company's stocks moved sharply downward.
She glanced downward at the street below from her balcony.
The leaves drifted downward gently from the trees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upward - toward a higher place, level, or position
