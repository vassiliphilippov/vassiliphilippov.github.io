flooding

noun
overload of network traffic (cyberattack)
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈflʌd.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈflʌd.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
(computing, cybersecurity) the act of overwhelming a computer system, network, or service with a large volume of traffic or requests, often to disrupt normal operation.

Examples

Flooding a server
The website suffered a massive flooding of bot requests, causing the server to crash during the sale.
Engineers traced the outage to a deliberate flooding that targeted the API endpoints overnight.
Cloud providers use filtering to mitigate flooding by blocking traffic from suspicious IP addresses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: DDoS - distributed denial-of-service attack; a flooding attack originating from many distributed sources
  • synonym: denial-of-service attack - an attack intended to make a machine or network resource unavailable to its intended users
