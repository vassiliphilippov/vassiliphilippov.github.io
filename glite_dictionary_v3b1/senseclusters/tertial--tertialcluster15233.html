tertial

noun
innermost wing feather
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜːrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈtɜːʃəl / (BrE)
a feather located on the innermost part of a bird's wing, closest to the body

Examples

Tertial feather
The ornithologist carefully examined the tertial feathers for signs of molting.
Bright markings on the tertials help identify certain species of birds.
Damage to the tertial can affect a bird's ability to fly efficiently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: primary - the outermost flight feather on a bird's wing
  • antonym: secondary - a flight feather located between the primaries and tertials on a bird's wing
