dwarf

noun
a person of very short stature
Pronunciation: / dwɔrf / (AmE), / dwɔːf / (BrE)
a person of unusually short stature, often due to a medical condition; in modern usage, considered insensitive or offensive by some, with 'person of short stature' or 'person with dwarfism' preferred

Examples

A famous dwarf actor
A famous dwarf actor played memorable roles in several blockbuster movies.
Medical professionals now prefer the term 'person with dwarfism' instead of calling someone a dwarf.
The story centers around a dwarf who overcomes prejudice in his village.

  • antonym: giant - a person of extremely large size
