thymosin

noun
hormone from the thymus gland
Pronunciation: / θaɪˈmoʊsɪn / (AmE), / θɪˈməʊsɪn / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the thymus that stimulates immunological activity in lymphoid tissue

Examples

Thymosin hormone
Doctors measured the levels of thymosin in the patient's blood to assess thymus function.
Scientists are studying how thymosin can help boost immune function in patients with weakened immunity.
The production of thymosin decreases as people age, affecting the immune response.
