abducent

adjective
drawing away from the midline (esp. muscles)
Pronunciation: / æbˈdusənt / (AmE), / æbˈdjuːsənt / (BrE)
(of a muscle) causing movement away from the midline of the body or from an adjacent part.

Examples

Abducent muscle injury
Damage to abducent muscles may result in limited ability to move the limb outward.
During physical therapy, exercises targeting abducent muscles can help improve mobility.
The abducent muscles are responsible for moving the limbs away from the central axis of the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adducent - (of a muscle) drawing toward the midline of the body or adjacent part
