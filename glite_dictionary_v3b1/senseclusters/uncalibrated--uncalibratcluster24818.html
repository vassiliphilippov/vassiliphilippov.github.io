uncalibrated

adjective
not adjusted to standard
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈkælɪˌbreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈkælɪbreɪtɪd / (BrE)
not adjusted or set to a standard, so results or measurements may be unreliable or inaccurate

Examples

An uncalibrated device
The laboratory results were unreliable because the device was uncalibrated.
Using uncalibrated instruments can lead to significant measurement errors.
The technician discovered that the scale was uncalibrated before starting the experiment.
The scientist realized the uncalibrated instrument was giving misleading temperature readings.
If the model is uncalibrated, its predictions may not reflect real-world outcomes.
Using uncalibrated data can lead to errors in important calculations.
