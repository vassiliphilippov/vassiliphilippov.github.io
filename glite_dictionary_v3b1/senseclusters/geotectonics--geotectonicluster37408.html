geotectonics

noun
study of Earth's crust
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːoʊtɛkˈtɑnɪks / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊtɛkˈtɒnɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the structure, features, and large-scale movements of the Earth's crust

Examples

Geotectonics study Earth plates
Scientists use geotectonics to understand how earthquakes and volcanoes are related to plate movements.
The field of geotectonics has greatly advanced our knowledge of mountain formation.
Modern geotectonics combines data from geology, physics, and chemistry to study Earth's crust.
Scientists use geotectonics to understand how continents drift and mountains form.
The conference focused on recent advances in geotectonics and their impact on earthquake prediction.
A deep knowledge of geotectonics is essential for studying volcanic activity.
