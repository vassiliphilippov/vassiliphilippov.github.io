sciara

noun
genus of fungus gnats
9.9
Pronunciation: / siˈɑːrə / (AmE), / siˈɑːrə / (BrE)
A genus of small, dark flies (family Sciaridae) that are often found in groups and are destructive to mushrooms and seedlings.

Examples

Sciara fly group
Sciara infestations can cause serious damage to commercial mushroom crops.
Growers often struggle to control sciara populations, as their larvae are difficult to detect.
The larvae of sciara are notorious for feeding on plant roots in greenhouses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fungus gnat - small flies whose larvae feed on fungi and plant roots
