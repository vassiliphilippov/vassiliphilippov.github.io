respite

noun
short period of relief or rest
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛspɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛspɪt / (BrE)
a short period of rest or relief from something difficult or unpleasant

Examples

A welcome respite
After working for hours, we were grateful for a brief respite from our tasks.
She took a respite from her studies to relax in the garden.
The rain provided a welcome respite from the summer heat.
After a long day at work, she welcomed the respite the quiet evening offered.
The refugees found a brief respite from their journey in the small village.
There was no respite from the relentless heat during the summer months.
After hours of hiking in the heat, the hikers welcomed a respite in the cool shade.
She found a moment of respite from her busy schedule during lunch.
The negotiations provided a brief respite from months of conflict.
