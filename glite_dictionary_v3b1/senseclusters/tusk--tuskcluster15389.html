tusk

noun
a long, pointed tooth in some animals
1.5 2.95/m
Pronunciation: / tÊŒsk / (AmE), / tÊŒsk / (BrE)
a long, pointed tooth that sticks out from the mouth of some animals, such as elephants, walruses, or wild pigs, used for fighting, digging, or as ivory

Examples

Elephant tusk
A walrus uses its tusks to pull itself out of the water and onto the ice.
Poachers often target animals for their valuable tusks.
The elephant used its tusk to dig for water beneath the dry earth.
Poachers hunt elephants illegally for the valuable ivory in their tusks.
The elephant protected itself with its massive tusk during the fight.
Walrus tusks are used for digging and fighting among males.
