browse

verb
look casually at goods for sale
1.1
Pronunciation: / braʊz / (AmE), / braʊz / (BrE)
to look at things in a casual way, especially in shops, websites, or collections, without a specific plan or intention to buy

Examples

To browse the shops
I enjoy browsing the local bookshops when I have free time.
Many customers like to browse before asking for assistance.
She spent an hour browsing before deciding not to buy anything.
I spent the afternoon browsing in the antique market.
Many people browse online stores without intending to buy anything.
She likes to browse the shelves at the library for new books.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buy - to acquire something by paying for it
  • synonym: shop - to visit stores in search of goods to buy
