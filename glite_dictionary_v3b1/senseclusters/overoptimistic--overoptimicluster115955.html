overoptimistic

adjective
unrealistically positive
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˌɑptɪˈmɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˌɒptɪˈmɪstɪk / (BrE)
expecting only the best possible outcome, often in an unrealistic or impractical way

Examples

An overoptimistic plan
It was overoptimistic to think the team could finish the project in just two days.
Her overoptimistic view of the situation led to disappointment when things did not go as planned.
Analysts warned that the company's sales forecasts were overoptimistic given the current market conditions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: optimistic - expecting good things to happen
  • antonym: pessimistic - expecting the worst possible outcome
  • antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are and dealing with them practically
