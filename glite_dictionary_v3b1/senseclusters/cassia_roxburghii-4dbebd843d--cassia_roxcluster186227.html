Cassia roxburghii

noun
East Indian tree used in veterinary medicine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæsiə rɒksˈbɜːrɡi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæsiə rɒksˈbɜːɡi.aɪ / (BrE)
An East Indian tree with long pods containing a black pulp traditionally used as a cathartic, especially in veterinary medicine.
