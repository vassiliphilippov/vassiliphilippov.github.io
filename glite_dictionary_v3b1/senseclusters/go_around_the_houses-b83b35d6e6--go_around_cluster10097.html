go around the houses

verb
be indirect or avoid the point
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd ðə ˈhaʊzɪz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd ðə ˈhaʊzɪz / (BrE)
to avoid getting to the point or giving a straight answer; to be unnecessarily indirect or circuitous

Examples

To go around the houses before answering
Every time I ask her a simple question, she goes around the houses before answering.
He tends to go around the houses rather than address the issue directly.
Stop going around the houses and tell us what you really think.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat around the bush - avoid talking about what is important or directly addressing a topic
  • antonym: get to the point - to say something directly and without unnecessary details
