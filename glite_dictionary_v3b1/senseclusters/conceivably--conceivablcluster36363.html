conceivably

adverb
possibly; within the realm of possibility
5.3 3.86/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsiÀêv…ôbli / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsiÀêv…ôbli / (BrE)
Possibly; in a way that can be imagined or believed to be possible.

Examples

It is conceivably possible
It is conceivably possible that we could finish the project ahead of schedule.
She could conceivably win the competition if she performs at her best.
There may conceivably be other explanations for his behavior.

Synonyms

  • imaginably
    4.5 0.04/m
    imaginably invites a clear mental image, while conceivably is a more common, neutral adverb for something that could be true.
  • potentially
    3.4 42/m
    potentially suggests something may become real or happen in future, while conceivably says it can be imagined as possible now.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inconceivably - in a way that is impossible to imagine
  • synonym: possibly - may happen or be true
