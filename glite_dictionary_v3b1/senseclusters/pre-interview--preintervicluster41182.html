pre-interview

noun
initial screening meeting
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈɪntərvjuː / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈɪntəvjuː / (BrE)
a meeting, call, or screening that takes place before a formal interview to assess suitability or provide information

Examples

A pre-interview call
Before the main interview, she had a brief pre-interview with the HR coordinator.
The company uses a pre-interview phone call to narrow down the list of candidates.
He prepared for the pre-interview by researching the company's background and values.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: interview - a formal meeting to assess a candidate
  • synonym: screening - an initial evaluation to determine suitability
