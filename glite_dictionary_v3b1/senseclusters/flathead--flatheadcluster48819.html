flathead

noun
screw with flat top
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈflætˌhɛd / (BrE)
a screw with a flat top that is designed to sit flush with a surface when installed

Examples

Flathead screw
The carpenter used a flathead to secure the hinge so it would sit evenly.
Make sure the flathead is flush with the wood to avoid snags.
You will need a special screwdriver to remove a flathead from this panel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: countersunk screw - a screw designed to sit flush with a surface
  • antonym: roundhead - a screw with a rounded top that sits above the surface
