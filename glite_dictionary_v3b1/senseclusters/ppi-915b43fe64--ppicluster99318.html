PPI

noun
producer price index
7.3 0.1/m
Pronunciation: / piÀê piÀê a…™ / (AmE), / piÀê piÀê a…™ / (BrE)
an economic index that measures the average change over time in prices received by domestic producers for their goods and services

Examples

Monthly PPI report
The government releases the PPI every month to track changes in wholesale prices.
A sudden increase in the PPI can indicate rising inflation in the economy.
Many analysts use the PPI to predict future trends in consumer prices.
