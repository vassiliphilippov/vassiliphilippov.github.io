pelycosaur

noun
large primitive extinct reptile of the Permian period
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛl.ɪ.kəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈpɛl.ɪ.kəˌsɔː / (BrE)
a large, primitive, extinct reptile from the Permian period, often with a tall sail-like structure on its back, native to Europe and North America

Examples

The pelycosaur fossil
Fossils of the pelycosaur have been found across North America and Europe.
Scientists believe that pelycosaurs were among the earliest large land vertebrates.
The pelycosaur is recognizable by the distinctive sail on its back.
