redisposition

noun
reassignment of forces for greater effectiveness
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.dɪs.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌriː.dɪs.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of withdrawing and repositioning forces in order to use them more effectively

Examples

Rapid redisposition of forces
Recent intelligence prompted a rapid redisposition of naval assets in the region.
The commander ordered a redisposition of troops to strengthen the left flank.
Their success was partly due to the enemy's poorly timed redisposition of forces.
