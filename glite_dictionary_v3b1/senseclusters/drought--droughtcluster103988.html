drought

noun
a prolonged lack of rainfall
Pronunciation: / draʊt / (AmE), / draʊt / (BrE)
A long period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to a shortage of water.

Examples

Severe drought
Authorities implemented water restrictions due to the ongoing drought.
Many crops failed because the drought reduced the water supply to dangerously low levels.
The region is suffering from a severe drought that has lasted for months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flood - an overflow of water that submerges land
