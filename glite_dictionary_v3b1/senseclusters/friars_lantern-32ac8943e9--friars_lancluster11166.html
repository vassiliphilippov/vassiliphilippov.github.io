friar's lantern

noun
pale light seen over marshy ground at night
Pronunciation: / ˈfraɪɚz ˈlæntɚn / (AmE), / ˈfraɪəz ˈlæntən / (BrE)
A pale, often flickering light sometimes seen at night over marshy ground, traditionally attributed to supernatural causes; also known as will-o'-the-wisp.

Examples

A friar's lantern light
As night fell on the moor, a friar's lantern flickered in the distance, puzzling the villagers.
Legends say that a friar's lantern can lead travelers astray in the foggy wetlands.
Some believe that a friar's lantern is simply methane gas igniting over the marsh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: will-o'-the-wisp - a phosphorescent light seen at night, especially over marshy ground
