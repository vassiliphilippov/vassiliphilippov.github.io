boiling point

noun
extreme anger or agitation
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪlɪŋ pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a moment or state when anger or stress becomes extreme and is about to be expressed

Examples

Anger boiling point
After repeated delays, her patience finally reached boiling point and she stormed out of the room.
Public frustration over the policy changes is at boiling point among commuters.
The debate grew more heated, and tensions in the room rose to boiling point.
After hours of delay, the crowd's frustration finally reached its boiling point.
If you keep pushing him, he'll hit his boiling point soon.
Tensions in the office have been rising, and now they're at a boiling point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breaking point - the moment of greatest strain; a critical moment
  • antonym: calm - a state of peacefulness and lack of anger or stress
