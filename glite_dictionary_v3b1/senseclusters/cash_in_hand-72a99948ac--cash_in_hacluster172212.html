cash in hand

noun
money readily available
Pronunciation: / kæʃ ɪn hænd / (AmE), / kæʃ ɪn hand / (BrE)
Money that is immediately available, rather than invested or owed.

Examples

Cash in hand money
He always makes sure to keep some cash in hand for emergencies.
The company had very little cash in hand at the end of the quarter.
With plenty of cash in hand, they were able to seize new opportunities quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cash flow - the total amount of money being transferred into and out of a business
  • synonym: liquid assets - assets that can be easily converted into cash
