aerie

noun
nest of a bird of prey
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈeri /; / ˈɪri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
A large nest of a bird of prey, such as an eagle or hawk, typically built high in a tree or on a cliff.

Examples

Eagle aerie
Researchers carefully climbed to the aerie to observe the behavior of the young hawks.
The abandoned aerie was nestled in the tallest pine at the edge of the forest.
Through her binoculars, she spotted a golden eagle’s aerie perched high atop the rocky cliff.
From our campsite, we could see the distant eyrie perched atop the crag.
The golden eagle built its eyrie on a rocky cliff high above the valley.
The naturalist observed two chicks in the eyrie nestled in the pine tree.
