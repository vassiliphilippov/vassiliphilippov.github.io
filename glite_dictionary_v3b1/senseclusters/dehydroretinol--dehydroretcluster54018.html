dehydroretinol

noun
alcohol form of vitamin A, less active than vitamin A₁
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˌhaɪdroʊˈrɛtɪnɒl / (AmE), / diˌhaɪdrəʊˈrɛtɪnɒl / (BrE)
A viscous alcohol that is less active in mammals than vitamin A₁; a form of vitamin A prevalent in some aquatic animals.

Examples

The dehydroretinol compound
Recent studies show that dehydroretinol is common in freshwater fish species.
The laboratory compared the effects of dehydroretinol and retinol on mammalian tissues.
Unlike vitamin A₁, dehydroretinol is significantly less active in most mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retinol - the main alcohol form of vitamin A (vitamin A₁)
  • synonym: vitamin A - a group of fat-soluble compounds including retinol and its derivatives
