lapsi

noun
repentant apostate
Pronunciation: / ˈlæpsi / (AmE), / ˈlæpsi / (BrE)
a person who renounced their faith under persecution but later sought to be readmitted to the church

Examples

Early church lapsi
The early church struggled with how to reintegrate the lapsi after the period of persecution.
Some bishops argued that the lapsi should perform public penance before being accepted again.
Historical records show that many lapsi sincerely regretted their actions and sought forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apostate - a person who abandons their religious faith
