reviewable

adjective
able to be examined
4.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvjuːəbl /; / rɪˈvjuːəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈvjuːəbl /; / rɪˈvjuːəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be examined, checked, or reassessed (for example, by an authority or as part of a regular process)

Examples

A reviewable decision
The judge determined that the contract was reviewable under the current regulations.
Only certain administrative decisions are reviewable by the Supreme Court.
The policy is reviewable every five years to ensure it remains effective.
The contract is reviewable every year to ensure it meets current standards.
Only certain decisions made by the board are reviewable by the court.
All submitted documents are reviewable by the committee before approval.

  • antonym: final - not able to be changed or reviewed
