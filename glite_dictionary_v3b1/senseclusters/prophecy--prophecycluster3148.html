prophecy

noun
divinely inspired prediction
6.2 5/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêf…ôsi / (AmE), / Ààpr…íf…ôsi / (BrE)
A statement that predicts what will happen in the future, especially one made under divine inspiration.

Examples

A prophecy comes true
According to the prophecy, a chosen one will unite the divided lands.
Her prophecy was recorded and studied for generations.
The ancient seer delivered a prophecy about the fall of the kingdom.

Synonyms

  • vaticination
    9.9 0.00/m
    vaticination is the act of foretelling by divine inspiration, while prophecy is the common name for such revealed predictions.
  • prognostication
    9.3 0.6/m
    prognostication is a prediction based on signs or analysis, while prophecy usually implies a divinely inspired statement about the future.
  • prevision
    9.9 0.00/m
    prevision is a prophetic vision seen in dreamlike or supernatural form, while prophecy is often a spoken or written future statement.
  • oracle
    9.1 0.7/m
    oracle is a message or priestly source often seen as infallible, while prophecy is any predicted statement about the future.
