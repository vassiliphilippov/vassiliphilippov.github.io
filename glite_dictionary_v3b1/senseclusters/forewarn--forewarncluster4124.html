forewarn

verb
warn in advance
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈwɔrn / (AmE), / fɔːˈwɔːn / (BrE)
to warn someone in advance of possible danger or trouble

Examples

To forewarn of danger
If only someone had forewarned me about the risks, I would have been more careful.
Police attempted to forewarn residents about the upcoming storm.
She forewarns her students that the exam will be challenging.
