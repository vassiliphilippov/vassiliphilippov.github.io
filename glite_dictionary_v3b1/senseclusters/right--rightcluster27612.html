right

noun
legal entitlement to something (often intangible)
1.9 2.0
Pronunciation: /raɪt/ (AmE), /raɪt/ (BrE)
a legal or moral entitlement; something a person is allowed to have or do

Examples

Human rights
Broadcast rights for the game were purchased by a major TV network.
Owning the mineral rights allows you to profit from resources found underground.
The author sold the rights to his book to a movie studio.
Every citizen has the right to freedom of speech and expression.
She fought for the rights of women and children all her life.
The company must respect the rights of its workers under the law.

  • synonym: privilege - a special right or advantage granted to a particular person or group
