break of serve

noun
win against opponent's serve (tennis)
6.2
Pronunciation: / breɪk əv sɝv / (AmE), / breɪk əv sɜːv / (BrE)
(in tennis) a situation in which a player wins a game when their opponent is serving.

Examples

A break of serve point
He celebrated each break of serve as the crowd cheered his comeback.
If you can score a break of serve, you increase your chances of winning the set.
Winning a break of serve early in the set gave her a significant advantage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break point - a point which, if won, results in a break of serve
  • antonym: hold of serve - winning a game while serving (in tennis)
