orthopterous insect

noun
insect of the Orthoptera order
4.0
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈθɑptərəs ˈɪnsɛkt / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒptərəs ˈɪnsɛkt / (BrE)
an insect of the order Orthoptera, typically having leathery forewings, membranous hind wings, and chewing mouthparts, such as a grasshopper or cricket

Examples

A orthopterous insect cricket
Crickets, which are orthopterous insects, are well known for their distinctive chirping sounds.
Many farmers consider certain orthopterous insects to be pests because they feed on crops.
The grasshopper is an example of an orthopterous insect commonly found in fields.

  • synonym: grasshopper - a jumping insect of the family Acrididae, order Orthoptera
