foozle

verb
to bungle clumsily
Pronunciation: / ˈfuːzəl / (AmE), / ˈfuːzəl / (BrE)
to bungle or handle something clumsily, especially in sports, games, or other tasks

Examples

To foozle a shot
He managed to foozle his shot and missed the hole entirely.
She didn't want to foozle her lines during the school play.
The team foozled their chance to win by making a simple mistake.
He foozled his first attempt at the presentation and had to start over.
She foozled the golf shot, sending the ball only a few feet forward.
Despite his experience, he foozled the simple task under pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • synonym: bungle - to do something badly or clumsily
  • synonym: fumble - to handle something awkwardly or clumsily
  • antonym: succeed - to achieve the desired result or do something well
