prospectively

adverb
with future focus
6.7
Pronunciation: / prəˈspɛktɪvli / (AmE), / prəˈspɛktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or affects the future, not the past

Examples

Designed prospectively
The researchers designed the study prospectively to track participants over several years.
We need to think prospectively about how technology will impact our business.
By planning prospectively, the team avoided many potential problems.
The new law will apply prospectively, affecting only future contracts signed after its enactment.
Any changes to the policy will be implemented prospectively and will not impact previous agreements.
The judge clarified that the ruling would take effect prospectively, not altering past decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forward-looking - thinking about and planning for the future
  • antonym: retroactively - applying to events or cases in the past
  • antonym: retrospectively - in a way that looks back at or considers the past
