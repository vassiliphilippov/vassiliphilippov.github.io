hard-core

adjective
very difficult game mode (permadeath)
Pronunciation: / c8h51rdcck54r / (AmE), / c8h51d0dcck54d0 / (BrE)
(in video gaming) very demanding or unforgiving; describing a game mode that increases risk, often by disabling saves or applying permadeath so progress is permanently lost on defeat.

Examples

Hard-core mode
The designers introduced a hard-core mode that deletes characters permanently when they die.
Many players enjoy hard-core servers for the intense risk and greater sense of achievement.
After losing his saved game, he tried the hard-core option but found it too punishing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ironman mode - a gaming mode that restricts saving or increases consequences to create a continuous, high-stakes playthrough
  • synonym: permadeath - a game mechanic where a character's death is permanent and cannot be reversed by reloading a save
