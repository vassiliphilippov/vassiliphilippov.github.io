nonconsumption

noun
not using resources
7.4
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.kən.ˈsʌmp.ʃən/ /; / /nən.kən.ˈsʌmp.ʃən/ (AmE), /nɒn.kən.ˈsʌmp.ʃən/ /; / /nən.kən.ˈsʌmp.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or state of not using or spending income or resources for immediate consumption, often referring to saving or withholding in economics

Examples

The economist analyzed the effects of nonconsumption on national investment rates.
