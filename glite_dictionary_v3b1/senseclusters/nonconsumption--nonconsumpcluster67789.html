nonconsumption

noun
not using resources
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of not using or spending income or resources for immediate consumption, often referring to saving or withholding in economics

Examples

Nonconsumption of income
High levels of nonconsumption can indicate that people are saving more money than usual.
Government policies sometimes aim to reduce nonconsumption to boost economic growth.
The economist analyzed the effects of nonconsumption on national investment rates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consumption - the act of using goods or services
  • synonym: saving - the act of keeping money instead of spending it
