characterization

noun
describing distinctive characteristics
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌker.ɪktər.əˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkɛrɪktərəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌkɛrɪktəraɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌkærɪktərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkær.ɪktər.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkærɪktəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of describing or presenting the distinctive qualities or features of someone or something, especially in a vivid or detailed way (in general, literature, or speech)

Examples

Detailed characterization
Accurate characterization of the symptoms is essential for a correct diagnosis.
The documentary was criticized for its unfair characterization of certain groups.
The novel's characterization of its main protagonist is both subtle and compelling.
Her detailed characterization of the old mansion was so precise that readers could picture every room.
The author's characterization of the city brought its streets and people vividly to life.
The pamphlet included brief characterizations of each historical figure mentioned.
Her novel is known for its subtle characterisation of complex emotions and personalities.
The film's characterisation of the villain was praised for its depth and realism.
The journalist's characterisation of the politician sparked controversy among the public.
