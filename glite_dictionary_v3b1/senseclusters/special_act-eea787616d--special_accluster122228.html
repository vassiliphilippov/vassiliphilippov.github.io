special act

noun
act applying to specific person or district
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ækt / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ækt / (BrE)
a legislative measure enacted to apply to a specific person, group, or district rather than to the public at large

Examples

A special act of legislature
A special act of the legislature was required to authorize the construction of the private road.
Each special act must specify the individuals or districts it intends to benefit.
The city council passed a special act granting tax exemptions to local farmers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general act - a legislative act that applies to the entire public or a broad category
