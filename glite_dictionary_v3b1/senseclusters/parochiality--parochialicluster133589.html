parochiality

noun
narrow-mindedness
Pronunciation: / pəˌroʊkiˈæləti / (AmE), / pəˌrəʊkiˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of having a narrow or limited outlook, especially by focusing only on local or small group interests

Examples

The parochiality of committee
The parochiality of the committee made it difficult to address issues beyond their own town.
Her parochiality was evident when she dismissed ideas from outside her community.
Overcoming parochiality is important for understanding global challenges.
The town's parochiality made it difficult for new ideas to gain acceptance.
Critics accused the council of parochiality for ignoring issues outside their immediate community.
Her parochiality was evident in her reluctance to support projects beyond the parish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitanism - an outlook that is open to and interested in the wider world
  • antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas or opinions
  • synonym: provincialism - narrowness of mind or outlook, especially due to local interests
