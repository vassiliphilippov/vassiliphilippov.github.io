parochiality

noun
narrow-mindedness
Pronunciation: / pəˌroʊkiˈæləti / (AmE), / pəˌrəʊkiˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of having a narrow or limited outlook, especially by focusing only on local or small group interests

Examples

The parochiality of the committee made it difficult to address issues beyond their own town.

Her parochiality was evident in her reluctance to support projects beyond the parish.
