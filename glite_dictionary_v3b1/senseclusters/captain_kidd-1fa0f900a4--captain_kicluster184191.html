Captain Kidd

proper noun
Scottish sea captain and alleged pirate
Pronunciation: / ˌkæptɪn ˈkɪd / (AmE), / ˌkæptɪn ˈkɪd / (BrE)
(1645–1701) A Scottish sea captain hired to protect British shipping in the Indian Ocean, later accused of piracy and hanged.

Examples

Captain Kidd pirate trial
Historians debate whether Captain Kidd was truly a pirate or a scapegoat.
