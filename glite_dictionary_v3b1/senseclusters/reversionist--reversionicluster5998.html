reversionist

noun
person reverting to old behaviors
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrʒənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who reverts to previous, often undesirable, patterns of behavior.

Examples

A reversionist patient
After months of progress, the patient became a reversionist and returned to his old habits.
Organizations sometimes struggle with reversionist members who resist positive change.
The therapist warned against becoming a reversionist during stressful times.
