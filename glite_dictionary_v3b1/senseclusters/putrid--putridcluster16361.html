putrid

adjective
decaying with a foul odor
7.0 0.65/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpjuÀê.tr…™d /; / Ààpjutr…™d / (AmE), / ÀàpjuÀê.tr…™d /; / ÀàpjuÀêtr…™d / (BrE)
decaying or rotting and having a very unpleasant, foul smell

Examples

Putrid smell
His socks had a putrid odor after being left in a gym bag for days.
The putrid smell coming from the garbage bin made her gag.
They found a putrid carcass in the woods, swarming with flies.
She recoiled at the sight of the putrid meat in the abandoned refrigerator.
The cellar was filled with a putrid odor that made it impossible to enter.
The smell from the garbage was absolutely putrid after sitting out in the sun for days.

Synonyms

  • putrescent
    9.9 0.05/m
    putrescent means starting to rot and smell faintly, while putrid describes something fully decayed with a very strong, foul smell.
  • rotten
    1.0 3.65/m
    rotten means decayed or spoiled and often useless, while putrid stresses active decay and a very strong, nasty smell.
  • fetid
    7.9 0.74/m
    fetid has a very strong, formal-sounding bad smell, while putrid suggests that the smell comes from actual rot.
  • rank
    6.7 0.01/m
    rank describes a very strong, often sharp smell or taste, while putrid refers to things that are rotten and smell foul.
  • foul-smelling
    4.1 0.47/m
    foul-smelling simply means giving off a bad odor, while putrid adds that the bad odor comes from decay or rotting matter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - recently made or obtained; not decayed
  • synonym: rotten - decayed or decomposed, usually emitting a bad smell
