melancholy

noun
thoughtful sadness
5.5 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmel.əŋ.kɑː.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈmel.əŋ.kɒl.ɪ/ (BrE)
a deep, long-lasting feeling of sadness or gloom

Examples

A deep melancholy
A wave of melancholy swept over him as he remembered his childhood home.
Despite the celebration, she felt a quiet melancholy in her heart.
The music had an air of melancholy that lingered long after it ended.
Despite her outward smile, she was burdened by an inner melancholy that would not lift.
His melancholy was apparent to everyone who saw his somber expression and slow movements.
There was a sense of melancholy in the air after the team lost the final match.
