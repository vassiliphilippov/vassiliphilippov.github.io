hardtail

noun
bike without rear suspension
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːrdteɪl /; / ˈhɑrdˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdeɪl /; / ˈhɑːdˌteɪl / (BrE)
a bicycle or motorcycle that does not have rear suspension

Examples

Mountain hardtail
He bought a new hardtail for cross-country mountain biking.
Many riders choose a hardtail because it is lighter and easier to maintain.
The hardtail handled the gravel roads well, but was less comfortable on rough trails.
He prefers riding a hardtail for cross-country biking.
A hardtail is often lighter and easier to maintain than a full-suspension bike.
Many beginners start with a hardtail because it is more affordable and simple to use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: full-suspension - a bicycle or motorcycle with both front and rear suspension
  • synonym: mountain bike - a bicycle designed for off-road cycling
  • degree lower: rigid - a bicycle with no suspension at all
