reunite

verb
bring together again after separation
3.2 6.43/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêjuÀàna…™t / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêjuÀêÀàna…™t / (BrE)
to bring people or things together again after they have been separated

Examples

To reunite family members
After years apart, the two brothers finally reunited at their childhood home.
Many families hope to reunite with loved ones who have been displaced.
The concert aims to reunite members of the original band.
Efforts to reunite the divided nation have gained international support.
The organization works tirelessly to reunite families separated by conflict.
They hope the peace treaty will eventually reunite the two regions.

Synonyms

  • rejoin
    3.5 3.94/m
    rejoin means joining someone or a group again after separation, while reunite stresses bringing people or things together again.
  • regather
    3.9 0.02/m
    regather means bringing scattered people or things back to one place, while reunite means bringing them together again after separation.
  • reconnect
    3.7 4.29/m
    reconnect is about restoring a link or relationship, while reunite focuses on physically bringing people or things together again.
  • reunify
    5.9 0.49/m
    reunify means making groups or regions one again, often formally, while reunite is more general and can be personal or physical.
