call

verb
read a list of names to check attendance
2.0 81/m
Pronunciation: / k…îl /; / k…ël / (AmE), / k…îÀêl / (BrE)
to request, demand, or summon someone or something, especially formally or officially

Examples

To call a meeting
At the start of each meeting, the secretary would call the roll.
Please call the list to ensure everyone has arrived.
The teacher began to call the names of the students to see who was present.
The opposition party called a vote of no confidence in the government.
The principal called an emergency meeting after the incident.
The referee called a timeout to allow the players to rest.
Our train will call at several small towns before reaching the city.
The cruise ship will call at Naples on its way to Greece.
The ferry does not call at this island during the winter months.

Synonyms

  • summon
    5.3 9.88/m
    summon is an official, urgent order to come, while call can mean a formal request, demand, or to ask someone to arrive.
  • send for
    2.7 3.32/m
    send for is asking or ordering someone to come because they are needed, while call is a general formal request or summons.
  • call in
    3.0 5.66/m
    call in is asking someone to come in or help, often an expert, while call can mean a formal request or to summon.
  • convoke
    8.1 0.11/m
    convoke is formally calling people together for a meeting or assembly, while call covers many kinds of requests or summons.
  • request
    1.0 45/m
    request is politely or formally asking for something, while call also includes stronger acts like demanding or summoning someone.
  • call for
    3.3 22/m
    call for is publicly asking or demanding that something happen or be provided, while call is a broader request or summons.
