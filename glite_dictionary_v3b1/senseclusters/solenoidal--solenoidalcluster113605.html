solenoidal

adjective
divergence-free field
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɑː.lə.ˈnɔɪ.dəl/ /; / /soʊ.lə.ˈnɔɪ.dəl/ (AmE), /sɒ.lə.ˈnɔɪ.dəl/ /; / /sə.lə.ˈnɔɪ.dəl/ (BrE)
having zero divergence, especially describing a vector field or flow such as a magnetic field or incompressible fluid.

Examples

Solenoidal vector field
In fluid dynamics, an incompressible flow is described as solenoidal because its divergence is zero.
The magnetic field around a current-carrying wire is a classic example of a solenoidal vector field.
Engineers often analyze solenoidal fields when studying electromagnetic phenomena.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divergence-free - having zero divergence in a vector field
  • antonym: irrotational - having zero curl in a vector field
