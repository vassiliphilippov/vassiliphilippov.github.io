Anu

proper noun
mother goddess in Irish mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːnu / (AmE), / ˈɑːnu / (BrE)
A mother goddess in ancient Irish mythology, sometimes identified with Danu as the mother of the gods.

Examples

Anu the mother goddess
In Irish mythology, Anu is revered as the mother of the gods.
Many ancient sites in Ireland are believed to be sacred to Anu.
The two hilltops known as the Paps of Anu are named after this goddess.
