vow

noun
a solemn promise or pledge
3.4 9.81/m
Pronunciation: / vaÊŠ / (AmE), / vaÊŠ / (BrE)
a solemn promise or pledge to do something or behave in a certain way, often with religious or formal significance

Examples

A marriage vow
He broke his vow and revealed the secret to everyone.
Monks often take a vow of silence as part of their discipline.
She made a vow to never lie again after the incident.

Synonyms

  • oath
    3.6 14/m
    oath is a formal promise often sworn in courts or ceremonies, while vow is more personal and tied to religion.
  • pledge
    3.7 11/m
    pledge is a public commitment often to give or support a cause, while vow is a solemn personal promise.
  • word of honor
    4.0 0.24/m
    word of honor is an everyday spoken guarantee of honesty between people, while vow is more formal and religious.
  • promise
    1.0 47/m
    promise is a general assurance about future actions used in many situations, while vow is stronger and often religious.

Related Terms

  • antonym: curse - a solemn utterance intended to invoke harm or misfortune on someone
  • synonym: oath - a solemn promise, often invoking a divine witness
  • synonym: pledge - a serious or formal promise or agreement
