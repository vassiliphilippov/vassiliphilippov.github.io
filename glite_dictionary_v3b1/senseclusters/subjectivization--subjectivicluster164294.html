subjectivization

noun
development of subjectivity
9.9
Pronunciation: / səbˌdʒɛktɪvəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˌdʒɛktɪvəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person or group develops subjectivity, self-awareness, or a distinct personal or social identity

Examples

Subjectivization of identity
The memoir traces the author's gradual subjectivization amid changing social and political pressures.
Philosophers debate how subjectivization shapes our understanding of personal identity.
Social movements often encourage the subjectivization of marginalized groups to foster empowerment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: individualization - the process of becoming an individual with a unique identity
  • antonym: objectification - the process of treating a person as an object rather than as a subject
