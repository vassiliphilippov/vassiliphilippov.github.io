deviously

adverb
in an underhanded or indirect way
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːviəsli / (AmE), / ˈdiːviəsli / (BrE)
In a way that uses indirect or underhanded methods to achieve something; not straightforward.

Examples

To deviously avoid questions
He deviously avoided answering the question directly.
She deviously manipulated the conversation to get what she wanted.
The plan was deviously clever and difficult to uncover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devious - not straightforward; shifty or crooked
  • antonym: honestly - in an honest or straightforward manner
