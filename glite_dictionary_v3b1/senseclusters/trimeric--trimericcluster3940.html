trimeric

adjective
having three parts
9.5
Pronunciation: / traɪˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / traɪˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
consisting of or relating to three joined parts, especially in reference to molecules or molecular structures

Examples

Trimeric molecule structure
The enzyme is active only when it is in its trimeric form.
Scientists studied the trimeric structure of the viral protein to understand how it infects cells.
A trimeric complex can be more stable than a single molecule alone.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dimeric - consisting of two joined parts
  • degree higher: tetrameric - consisting of four joined parts
