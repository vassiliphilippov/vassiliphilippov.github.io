senior

noun
an older person; person of greater age
2.7 11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêni…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêni…ô / (BrE)
a person who is older than someone else, often used to indicate seniority by age

Examples

A senior member
At the meeting, each senior was asked to share their experience with the younger members.
My senior in the company gave me valuable advice about my career.
She always respects her senior at family gatherings.

Synonyms

  • elder
    3.1 16/m
    elder is used for an older person with experience or authority in a group, while senior simply means someone older.
  • older adult
    1.7 11/m
    older adult refers to an adult in later years, often used in health or services, while senior is a general older person.
  • senior citizen
    2.0 3.40/m
    senior citizen describes an older person meeting a legal or retirement age, while senior can mean any older individual.
  • old person
    1.0 14/m
    old person sounds plain or sometimes rude, while senior is a common, polite word for someone who is older.

Related Terms

  • antonym: junior - a person who is younger than someone else
