trihydrate

adjective
with three water molecules
Pronunciation: / traɪˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
containing three molecules of water chemically bound to each formula unit, especially in a hydrated compound

Examples

Tritrate form of salt
The chemist measured the mass of the trihydrate sample before heating it to remove the water.
Calcium chloride trihydrate is commonly used in laboratories for drying gases.
The medication is supplied as a trihydrate to improve its stability and shelf life.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dihydrate - containing two molecules of water per formula unit
  • degree lower: monohydrate - containing one molecule of water per formula unit
  • degree higher: tetrahydrate - containing four molecules of water per formula unit
