corrupt

verb
to bribe or influence dishonestly
1.0
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌpt / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to make someone act dishonestly or become immoral, often by bribing or leading them into bad actions

Examples

To corrupt a judge
He was arrested after attempting to corrupt a police officer with a large sum of money.
The company tried to corrupt government officials to win the contract.
They were accused of trying to corrupt the judge to secure a favorable verdict.
His wealth and power eventually began to corrupt his once honest character.
Some believe that exposure to violent media can corrupt young children.
The controversial book was said to corrupt the minds of impressionable readers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bribe - to dishonestly persuade someone to act in one's favor by a gift of money or other inducement
  • synonym: debauch - to lead someone into immoral behavior
  • antonym: improve - to make someone or something better or morally upright
  • synonym: subvert - to undermine someone's morals or principles
