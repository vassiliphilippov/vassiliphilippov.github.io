getaway

noun
rapid escape, especially by criminals
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛtəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtəweɪ / (BrE)
a rapid escape from a place, especially by criminals after committing a crime

Examples

A getaway car
After the heist, the group's getaway vehicle was found abandoned outside the city.
The robbers made a swift getaway before the police arrived.
Their getaway was so well planned that no one saw them leave the bank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capture - the act of catching or being caught, especially after a pursuit
  • synonym: escape - an act of breaking free from confinement or control
