tone

noun
quality or character of someone's voice
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: / toʊn / (AmE), / təʊn / (BrE)
the way someone's voice, writing, or actions express emotion, attitude, or mood

Examples

A friendly tone
His soothing tone helped to calm the anxious child.
She replied in a sharp tone that took everyone by surprise.
You could tell by her tone that she was upset, even though she tried to hide it.
Her sharp remarks quickly changed the tone of the conversation.
The tone of the restaurant was relaxed and inviting, making it popular for family dinners.
The manager set a positive tone for the meeting by welcoming everyone warmly.
Her letters always have a cheerful tone that brightens my day.
The tone of his speech was respectful, reflecting his gratitude to the audience.
The author's sarcastic tone was obvious throughout the article.

Related Terms

  • antonym: content - the subject matter or substance of something
  • synonym: intonation - the rise and fall in pitch of the voice in speech
  • synonym: mood - the emotional state or feeling evoked by something
  • synonym: pitch - the degree of highness or lowness of a sound
  • synonym: style - the manner of expression in writing, speech, or performance
