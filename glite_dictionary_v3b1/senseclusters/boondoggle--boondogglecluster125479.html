boondoggle

verb
do pointless or wasteful work
Pronunciation: / ˈbunˌdɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbuːnˌdɒɡl / (BrE)
to do work or engage in an activity that is pointless, wasteful, or of little or no value, often to give the appearance of useful activity

Examples

To boondoggle at work
Instead of improving the process, the committee spent months boondoggling on unnecessary details.
The consultant was accused of trying to boondoggle instead of finding real solutions.
We can't afford to boondoggle when there are real problems to solve.

Related Terms

  • synonym: busywork - work that keeps someone busy but has little practical value
  • synonym: waste time - spend time doing unnecessary things
