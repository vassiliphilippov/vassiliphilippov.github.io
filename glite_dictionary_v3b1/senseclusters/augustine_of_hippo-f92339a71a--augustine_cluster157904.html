Augustine of Hippo

proper noun
early Christian theologian and bishop
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.gə.stiːn əv ˈhɪp.oʊ/ /; / /ˈɑː.gə.stɪn əv ˈhɪp.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.gə.stiːn əv ˈhɪp.əʊ/ /; / /ˈɔː.gʌs.tɪn əv ˈhɪp.əʊ/ (BrE)
(354–430) An influential early Christian theologian and philosopher, bishop of Hippo Regius, known for shaping Western Christianity and emphasizing the necessity of divine grace.

Examples

Bishop Augustine of Hippo
Bishop Augustine of Hippo played a crucial role in shaping the doctrine of original sin.
Many scholars regard Augustine of Hippo as one of the most influential figures in Christian theology.
The writings of Augustine of Hippo, such as 'Confessions', continue to inspire philosophers today.
