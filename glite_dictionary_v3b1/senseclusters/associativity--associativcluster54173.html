associativity

noun
property of grouping
8.0
Pronunciation: / əˌsoʊsiəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / əˌsəʊsiəˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the property of an operation in which the way elements are grouped does not affect the result

Examples

Math associativity property
In mathematics, associativity means that changing the grouping of numbers does not change the outcome.
Multiplication shows associativity because (2 × 3) × 4 equals 2 × (3 × 4).
Understanding associativity helps students solve complex equations more easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commutativity - the property of an operation where the order of elements does not affect the result
