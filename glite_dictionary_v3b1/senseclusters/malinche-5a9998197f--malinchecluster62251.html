Malinche

proper noun
symbol of betrayal
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪntʃeɪ / (AmE), / məˈlɪntʃeɪ / (BrE)
a woman seen as a traitor for helping foreign conquerors, especially as a symbol of betrayal or collaboration in Mexican history and culture

Examples

Called a Malinche
Some people called her a Malinche for supporting the foreign company's interests over her own community.
The term Malinche is often used in Mexico to describe someone seen as betraying national values.
He was unfairly labeled a Malinche after working closely with international organizations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: patriot - a person who strongly supports their country
  • synonym: traitor - a person who betrays their country or group
