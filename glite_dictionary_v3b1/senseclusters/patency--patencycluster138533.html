patency

noun
state of being open (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈpætənsi / (BrE)
the state of being open or unobstructed, especially in reference to a bodily passage or duct

Examples

Airway patency
Maintaining airway patency is crucial during emergency care.
Regular scans monitor the patency of the patient's stents to detect any problems early.
The surgeon confirmed the patency of the coronary artery after the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: occlusion - the blockage or closing of a bodily passage or duct
