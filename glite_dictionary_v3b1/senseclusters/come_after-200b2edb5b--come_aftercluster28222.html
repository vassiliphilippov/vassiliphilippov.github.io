come after

verb
happen after in time
Pronunciation: / kʌm ˈæftər / (AmE), / kʌm ˈɑːftə / (BrE)
(of an event or thing) to happen after something else in time, especially as a consequence or result.

Examples

A problem comes after a mistake
A lengthy debate came after the proposal was announced.
A sharp decline in sales came after the product recall.
The celebrations came after months of hard work.
