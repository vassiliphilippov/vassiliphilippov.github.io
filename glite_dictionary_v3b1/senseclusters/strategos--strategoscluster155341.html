strategos

noun
ancient Greek general
Pronunciation: / strəˈtiːɡoʊs / (AmE), / strəˈtiːɡɒs / (BrE)
a military general or commander in ancient Greece, especially in Athens

Examples

Ancient strategos leads
The strategos devised a clever plan to outmaneuver the enemy forces.
In ancient Athens, the strategos was elected by the citizens to lead the army.
The council listened carefully to the advice of the experienced strategos before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commander - a person in authority, especially over a military force
  • synonym: general - a high-ranking military officer
