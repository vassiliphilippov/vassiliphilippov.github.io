Synonyms

  • dig up
    2.0 4.25/m
    dig up means to remove or find something from the ground, while dig out focuses on freeing or creating space under a covering.
  • excavate
    6.0 1.14/m
    excavate is a formal verb for hollowing out large amounts of earth for building or study, while dig out is more everyday and smaller.
  • unearth
    4.2 1.41/m
    unearth is used for discovering something hidden or forgotten, while dig out often means physically removing or clearing things from under cover.
  • grub up
    7.7 0.01/m
    grub up is mainly used for digging out plants, roots, or stumps, while dig out covers removing many buried things or making space.
  • delve
    6.3 0.12/m
    delve originally means to dig or search deeply, often in a careful or figurative way, while dig out is simple physical removal.
