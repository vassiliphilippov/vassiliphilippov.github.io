private practice

noun
independent professional work, not as employee
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvət ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
the work of a professional, such as a doctor or lawyer, who operates independently and is not employed by an organization or government

Examples

A private practice office
After completing her residency, she decided to open her own private practice in the city.
He left the law firm to start his own private practice specializing in family law.
Many patients prefer seeing doctors who run a private practice for the personalized attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: group practice - shared practice by two or more professionals
