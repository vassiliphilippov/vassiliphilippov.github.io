toothed whale

noun
whale with teeth, not baleen
9.9 4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːtɪd weɪl / (AmE), / ˈtuːtɪd weɪl / (BrE)
a whale belonging to the suborder Odontoceti, characterized by having simple conical teeth, as opposed to baleen plates, and feeding mainly on fish or squid

Examples

A toothed whale species
Dolphins and porpoises are both considered toothed whales by scientists.
The sperm whale is the largest toothed whale in the world.
Unlike baleen whales, toothed whales hunt and eat fish and squid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Odontoceti - scientific suborder for toothed whales
  • antonym: baleen whale - whale with baleen plates instead of teeth
