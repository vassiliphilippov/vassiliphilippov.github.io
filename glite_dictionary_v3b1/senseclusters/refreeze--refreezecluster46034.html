refreeze

verb
to freeze again (policy)
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfriːz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfriːz / (BrE)
to impose or restore a suspension or ban on changes, such as to wages, prices, or hiring, after a previous freeze was lifted

Examples

To refreeze wages
The government decided to refreeze public sector wages after a brief period of increases.
Due to budget concerns, the university will refreeze all new hiring until further notice.
After negotiations failed, the company refroze employee benefits to control costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze - to stop or suspend changes, especially in policy or administration
  • antonym: unfreeze - to remove a suspension or ban on changes
