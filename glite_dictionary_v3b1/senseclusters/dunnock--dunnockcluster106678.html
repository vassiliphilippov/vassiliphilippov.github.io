dunnock

noun
small European brownish songbird
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌnək / (AmE), / ˈdʌnək / (BrE)
a small, brownish European songbird related to the accentor, often found in gardens and hedgerows

Examples

A dunnock bird
A pair of dunnocks was feeding near the garden shed early this morning.
The dunnock is often overlooked due to its drab plumage, but its song is surprisingly melodious.
Unlike the robin, the dunnock prefers to stay close to thick hedges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accentor - a bird of the genus Prunella, related species to the dunnock
