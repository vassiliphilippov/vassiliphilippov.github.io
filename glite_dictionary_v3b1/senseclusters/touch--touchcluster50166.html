touch

noun
slight suggestion of a quality
Pronunciation: / tʌtʃ / (AmE), / tʌtʃ / (BrE)
a small amount or slight trace of something (such as a quality, feeling, illness, etc.)

Examples

A touch of color
His speech had a touch of optimism despite the grim news.
I like the way you added a touch of lemon to the recipe.
There was a touch of irony in her response.
After eating the seafood, he developed a touch of food poisoning.
She had a touch of the flu but recovered quickly.
The doctor said it was probably just a touch of bronchitis.
She added a touch of elegance to the room with fresh flowers.
The soup needed just a touch of salt to bring out the flavor.
There's a touch of irony in his voice when he speaks.
