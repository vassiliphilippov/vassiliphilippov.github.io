filefish

noun
a warm-water fish with leathery skin and spiny dorsal fin
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A narrow, flattened warm-water fish with tough, leathery skin and a long, file-like dorsal spine, belonging to the family Monacanthidae.

Examples

A filefish species
Several species of filefish are kept by marine aquarium enthusiasts.
The diver spotted a colorful filefish hiding among the coral reefs.
With its rough skin and unique shape, the filefish is easily distinguished from other reef fish.
