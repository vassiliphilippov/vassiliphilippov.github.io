Francophone

noun
French-speaking person
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfræŋkəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfræŋkəfəʊn / (BrE)
a person or group whose native language is French or who speaks French fluently

Examples

A Francophone person
The city has a large Francophone community that celebrates French culture and traditions.
Many Francophones in Canada live in the province of Quebec.
The conference welcomed Francophone delegates from several African countries.
Canada has a large Francophone population, especially in the province of Quebec.
Many African countries have significant Francophone communities due to their colonial history.
The conference brought together Francophone writers from around the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Anglophone - an English-speaking person or group
  • synonym: Francophonic - relating to French-speaking people or regions
