sparseness

noun
degree of data sparsity
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈspɑːr.snəs/ /; / /ˈspɑːrs.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈspɑːs.nəs/ (BrE)
(in data science and numerical computing) the degree to which a data structure (for example, a vector or matrix) contains zero or near-zero elements; the proportion of empty or negligible entries.

Examples

Sparseness in data
The sparseness of the feature matrix reduced the model's training time and memory usage.
Researchers measured the sparseness of the user-item matrix to decide whether to use dense algorithms.
High sparseness in the sensor data meant many values were zero and the model overfit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: density - the degree to which elements are non-zero or the data structure is filled; often used as the antonym in numerical contexts
  • synonym: sparsity - the property of having many zero or insignificant elements in a data structure; an alternative technical term for this concept
