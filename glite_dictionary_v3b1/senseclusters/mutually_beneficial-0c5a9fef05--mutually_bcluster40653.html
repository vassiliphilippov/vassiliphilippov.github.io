mutually beneficial

adjective
advantageous to both sides
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtʃuəli bɛˈnɪfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtjuəli bɪˈnɪfɪʃəl / (BrE)
(of an arrangement, relationship, or agreement) advantageous or helpful to all parties involved; providing benefits for each side.

Examples

Mutually beneficial agreement
Finding a mutually beneficial solution was key to resolving the dispute.
The two companies formed a mutually beneficial partnership to expand their markets.
They agreed to a mutually beneficial arrangement that would reduce costs for both.

Related Terms

  • synonym: symbiotic - involving interaction between two different organisms living in close association, typically to the advantage of both
