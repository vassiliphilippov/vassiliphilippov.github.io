excusal

noun
justifying explanation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskjuːzəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːzəl / (BrE)
a reason or explanation given to justify or defend a fault, failure, or unacceptable action

Examples

A weak excusal
The manager did not accept his excusal for being late to work again.
She offered an excusal that did not convince anyone in the room.
After hearing his excusal, the teacher still marked the assignment as late.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accusation - a claim that someone has done something wrong
  • synonym: excuse - a reason given to explain or justify a fault or mistake
  • synonym: justification - a reason or explanation that shows something is reasonable or necessary
