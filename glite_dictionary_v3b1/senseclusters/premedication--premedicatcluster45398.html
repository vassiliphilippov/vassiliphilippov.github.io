premedication

noun
medicine before procedure
4.5
Pronunciation: /priː.med.ɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.med.ɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
medicine given, or the act of giving medicine, before a medical procedure or treatment to prepare or protect the patient

Examples

A premedication dose
The doctor prescribed a premedication to help the patient relax before the operation.
Children often receive premedication to reduce anxiety before dental procedures.
The nurse checked the chart to confirm the correct premedication was given before surgery.
The nurse administered premedication to help reduce the risk of an allergic reaction before the procedure.
Patients often receive premedication to minimize discomfort during certain medical treatments.
Doctors may recommend premedication before vaccinations to prevent possible side effects.
The nurse checked the chart to confirm the premedication had been administered before surgery.
Doctors often use premedication to help reduce anxiety in patients before an operation.
Allergies to certain drugs must be considered when choosing a premedication for a patient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anesthesia - the use of drugs to prevent pain during surgery
  • synonym: anesthetic - a drug that causes loss of sensation before surgery
  • synonym: medication - a drug or medicine used to treat or prevent disease
  • synonym: prophylaxis - treatment given to prevent disease or a specific health problem
  • synonym: sedation - the act of calming a patient with medication
  • synonym: sedative - a drug used to calm or relax a patient
