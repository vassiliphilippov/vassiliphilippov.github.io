blind

verb
to make someone lose their sight
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd / (AmE), / blaɪnd / (BrE)
to make someone unable to see, either temporarily or permanently

Examples

To blind someone
In ancient times, criminals were sometimes blinded for their crimes.
The attackers blinded their captives so they could not escape.
The brutal ruler ordered his enemies to be blinded as punishment.
Chemical fumes can blind people exposed to them without protection.
The headlights momentarily blinded the driver coming from the other direction.
The intense flash of the camera blinded him for a few seconds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dazzle - to blind temporarily by bright light
  • synonym: deprive of sight - to cause someone to lose the ability to see
  • antonym: sight - the ability to see; vision
