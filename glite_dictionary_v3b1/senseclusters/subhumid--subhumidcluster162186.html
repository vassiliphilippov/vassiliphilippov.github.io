subhumid

adjective
moderately humid
6.5
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈhjuːmɪd / (AmE), / sʌbˈhjuːmɪd / (BrE)
having a moderate level of humidity, between humid and semi-arid conditions

Examples

A subhumid climate
The subhumid climate of the region allows for both farming and grazing.
Many types of trees can thrive in a subhumid environment.
Researchers studied how crops adapt to subhumid conditions over several years.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: humid - having a high amount of moisture in the air
  • degree lower: semi-arid - having low to moderate rainfall, drier than subhumid
