polygenesis

noun
independent origins theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɑːl.ɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /pɒl.ɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the theory that something (such as a group of people, a species, or a language) originated from several independent sources rather than a single origin

Examples

Polygenesis of languages
The idea of polygenesis was once used to explain the diversity of human populations.
Some linguists debate whether language families arose through polygenesis or a single origin.
Modern science generally rejects polygenesis in favor of a common ancestry for all humans.
The debate between polygenesis and monogenesis is central to the study of language origins.
Some scientists support polygenesis to explain the development of similar traits in different species.
The theory of polygenesis challenges the idea that all humans share a single ancestral origin.
