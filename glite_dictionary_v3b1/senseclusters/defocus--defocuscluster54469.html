defocus

verb
to reduce attention
6.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / diÀêÀàfo äk…ôs / (AmE), / diÀêÀàf…ô äk…ôs / (BrE)
to reduce attention, emphasis, or concentration on a subject or issue; to divert focus away from something

Examples

To defocus attention
The teacher tried to defocus the discussion from the argument and move on to the lesson.
Sometimes, leaders defocus attention from problems by emphasizing positive achievements.
He deliberately defocused the conversation to avoid answering the difficult question.

  • deflect

  • unfocus
