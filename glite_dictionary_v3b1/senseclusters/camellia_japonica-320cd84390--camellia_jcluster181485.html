Camellia japonica

noun
ornamental shrub with large rose-like flowers
8.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈmiːliə dʒəˈpoʊniə / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliə dʒəˈpəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
A species of evergreen shrub native to East Asia, valued for its glossy leaves and large, showy, rose-like flowers; widely grown as an ornamental plant.

Examples

Camellia japonica flower
At the flower show, she presented a rare variety of Camellia japonica.
Many gardeners prize Camellia japonica for its elegant, rose-like flowers and dark green leaves.
The Camellia japonica in her garden blooms beautifully each spring.
