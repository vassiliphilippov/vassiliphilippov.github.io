pseudoskeptical argument

noun
argument that only pretends to be skeptical
7.3 8.8
Pronunciation: /suː.doʊ.ˈskep.tɪ.kəl ˈɑːr.gjə.mənt/ /; / /sjuː.doʊ.ˈskep.tɪ.kəl ˈɑːr.gjə.mənt/ /; / /suː.doʊ.ˈskep.tɪ.kəl ˈɑːr.gjuː.mənt/ (AmE), /sjuː.dəʊ.ˈskep.tɪ.kəl ˈɑː.gjʊ.mənt/ /; / /suː.dəʊ.ˈskep.tɪ.kəl ˈɑː.gjʊ.mənt/ (BrE)
An argument that pretends to be skeptical but does not genuinely engage with evidence or reason; instead, it dismisses claims without fair consideration.

Examples

A pseudoskeptical argument tactic
Her article identified common tactics used in a pseudoskeptical argument rather than promoting honest doubt.
Some online forums are flooded with pseudoskeptical arguments that only serve to shut down productive discussion.
The debate was interrupted by a pseudoskeptical argument that dismissed all new data without consideration.
