inexhaustibility

noun
quality of being impossible to exhaust
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪɡˌzɔstəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪɡˌzɔːstəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to exhaust; the condition of having unlimited supply or endurance.

Examples

The inexhaustibility of energy
Her inexhaustibility amazed her colleagues, as she never seemed to run out of ideas.
Philosophers often debate the inexhaustibility of human creativity.
The inexhaustibility of the sun's energy makes solar power an attractive resource for the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finiteness - the quality or state of having limits
  • synonym: limitlessness - the quality of having no limits
