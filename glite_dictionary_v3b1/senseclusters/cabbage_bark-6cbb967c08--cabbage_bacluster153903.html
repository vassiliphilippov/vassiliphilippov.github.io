cabbage bark

noun
a toxic tropical tree with medicinal bark and seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈkæbɪdʒ bɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪdʒ bɑːk / (BrE)
a tropical American tree with rough, unpleasant-smelling toxic bark, valued for its strong, durable wood; its bark and seeds are used as purgatives, vermifuges, and narcotics

Examples

cabbage bark tree
Because of its strong scent, cabbage bark trees are easy to identify in the forest.
The cabbage bark is known for its unusually tough timber and medicinal properties.
Traditional healers sometimes use the bark of the cabbage bark to treat intestinal parasites.
