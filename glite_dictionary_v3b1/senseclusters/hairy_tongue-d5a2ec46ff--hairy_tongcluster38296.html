hairy tongue

noun
benign condition with dark, hairy tongue
9.6 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈheə.riː/ /; / /ˈheə.rɪ/ (AmE), /ˈheə.riː/ /; / /ˈheə.rɪ/ (BrE)
A harmless condition in which the surface of the tongue appears dark and hairy due to overgrowth of papillae, sometimes as a side effect of antibiotics.

Examples

The hairy tongue condition
Proper oral hygiene can help prevent and treat hairy tongue.
The patient developed hairy tongue after taking antibiotics for several weeks.
Your doctor can diagnose hairy tongue by simply looking at the surface of your tongue.
