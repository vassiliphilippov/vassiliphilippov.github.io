main rotor

noun
rotor blades producing lift on a helicopter
5.1
Pronunciation: / meɪn ˈroʊt̬ɚ / (AmE), / meɪn ˈrəʊtə / (BrE)
the large rotating assembly of blades on a helicopter that provides lift and supports flight

Examples

The main rotor blades
Damage to the main rotor can severely compromise a helicopter's performance.
Pilots routinely inspect the main rotor before every flight.
The main rotor spun rapidly as the helicopter prepared for takeoff.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tail rotor - the smaller rotor at the tail of a helicopter that provides stability and yaw control
