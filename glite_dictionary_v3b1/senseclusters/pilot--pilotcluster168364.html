pilot

noun
person who guides ships in/out of harbors
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
a person who operates or guides an aircraft or ship

Examples

An airline pilot
Every large ship entering the harbor must be accompanied by a pilot.
The pilot boarded the vessel to guide it safely into the port.
Without a skilled pilot, the ship could have run aground on the rocks.
After many years as a military pilot, she joined a commercial airline.
Each pilot must pass rigorous training before being allowed to fly alone.
The pilot made a smooth landing despite poor weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aviator - a person who flies an aircraft; synonym for 'pilot'
  • synonym: harbor master - an official in charge of a harbor
  • antonym: passenger - a person who is not operating the vehicle but is being transported; antonym for 'pilot' in aviation context
