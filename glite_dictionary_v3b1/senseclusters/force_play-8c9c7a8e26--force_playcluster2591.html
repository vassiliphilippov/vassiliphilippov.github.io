force play

noun
baseball play where runner must advance and can be put out
7.3 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs ple…™ / (AmE), / f…îÀês ple…™ / (BrE)
(in baseball) a play in which a baserunner is put out because they are required to advance to the next base and a fielder, holding the ball, touches that base before the runner arrives.

Examples

A force play at first
A quick throw to first completed the force play and prevented a run.
The shortstop made a force play at second to end the inning.
With runners on first and second, the defense looked for a force play at third base.
