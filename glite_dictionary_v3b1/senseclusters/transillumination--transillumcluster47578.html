transillumination

noun
light passing through
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of shining light through an object or material to reveal its internal features or create a visual effect

Examples

Artistic transillumination
The artist used transillumination to create glowing stained-glass panels that changed with sunlight.
By applying transillumination, the designer revealed the delicate patterns hidden within the paper.
The museum exhibit featured shells whose inner structures were visible thanks to transillumination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backlighting - lighting from behind to highlight shapes or details
  • antonym: opaque - not allowing light to pass through
