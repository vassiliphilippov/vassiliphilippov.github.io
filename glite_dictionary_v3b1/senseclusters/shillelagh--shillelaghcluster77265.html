shillelagh

noun
Irish hardwood cudgel
8.2
Pronunciation: / ʃɪˈleɪli /; / ʃəˈleɪli / (AmE), / ʃɪˈleɪli / (BrE)
a thick stick of hardwood, especially oak or blackthorn, traditionally used in Ireland as a cudgel or walking stick

Examples

The old man proudly displayed his shillelagh, saying it had been in his family for generations.
A genuine shillelagh can be an impressive keepsake or walking stick.
