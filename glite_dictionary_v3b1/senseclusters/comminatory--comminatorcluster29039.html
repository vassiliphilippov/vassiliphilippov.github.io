comminatory

adjective
threatening punishment; warning of penalty
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːməˌneɪtəri / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ɪ.nə.tər.i / (BrE)
(of language or documents) containing a warning of punishment or intended to warn or threaten punishment.

Examples

Comminatory language
The king issued a comminatory edict to deter rebellion among his subjects.
The priest spoke in a comminatory tone, warning of the consequences of sin.
These laws are comminatory rather than actually enforced, serving to intimidate potential offenders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: threatening - expressing a threat or warning
