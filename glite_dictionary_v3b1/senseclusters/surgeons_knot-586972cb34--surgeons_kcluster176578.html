surgeon's knot

noun
knot used for stitches or ligatures
3.4 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːdʒənz nɑt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒənz nɒt / (BrE)
a knot with an extra twist in the first throw, used for tying stitches or ligatures, especially in surgery

Examples

A surgeon's knot for stitches
A surgeon's knot provides extra security compared to a standard square knot.
The doctor tied a surgeon's knot to secure the suture tightly.
When fishing, some people prefer using a surgeon's knot for joining lines of different thicknesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reef knot - a simple binding knot, also called a square knot
