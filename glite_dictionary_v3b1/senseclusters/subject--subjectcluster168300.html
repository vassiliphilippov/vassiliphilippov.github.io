subject

verb
to bring under control; make submit
7.7 4.91/m
Pronunciation: / sÉ™bËˆdÊ’É›kt / (AmE), / sÉ™bËˆdÊ’É›kt / (BrE)
to bring a person, group, or place under control or domination; to make someone or something submit to authority or power

Examples

To subject a country
He refused to subject his family to further danger by staying in the war-torn region.
Many indigenous peoples were subjected to foreign rule during the era of colonization.
The regime would often subject political prisoners to harsh interrogations.

Synonyms

  • subjugate
    9.2 1.70/m
    subjugate is to forcefully bring people or places under control, while subject means to make someone submit to authority or power.
  • subdue
    5.5 2.28/m
    subdue is to bring someone under control by force or effort, often temporarily, while subject means to make someone submit fully.
  • rule over
    4.2 1.44/m
    rule over is to have official control or authority over people or a place, while subject means to force people to submit.
  • dominate
    3.7 25/m
    dominate is to have strong influence or power over someone or something, while subject means to bring them under control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominate - to have control or power over someone or something
  • synonym: subjugate - to bring under domination or control, especially by conquest
