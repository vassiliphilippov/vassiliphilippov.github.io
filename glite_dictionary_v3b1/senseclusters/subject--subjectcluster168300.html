subject

verb
to bring under control; make submit
Pronunciation: / səbˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to bring a person, group, or place under control or domination; to make someone or something submit to authority or power

Examples

To subject a country
He refused to subject his family to further danger by staying in the war-torn region.
Many indigenous peoples were subjected to foreign rule during the era of colonization.
The regime would often subject political prisoners to harsh interrogations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominate - to have control or power over someone or something
  • synonym: subjugate - to bring under domination or control, especially by conquest
