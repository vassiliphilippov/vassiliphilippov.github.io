diplopod

noun
millipede arthropod
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpləˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈdɪpləˌpɒd / (BrE)
an arthropod of the class Diplopoda, commonly known as a millipede, with many body segments each bearing two pairs of legs

Examples

A diplopod crawls
The forest floor was covered with leaf litter and several diplopods crawling slowly beneath it.
A diplopod can curl into a tight coil when threatened by predators.
Scientists study the unique movement of the diplopod to understand its adaptation to different environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centipede - an arthropod with a long body and one pair of legs per segment
  • synonym: millipede - a long, segmented arthropod with many legs
