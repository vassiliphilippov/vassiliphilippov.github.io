Alcaic

noun
verse form with four-line stanzas used by Alcaeus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ælˈkeɪɪk / (AmE), / ælˈkeɪɪk / (BrE)
a verse form used in Greek and Latin poetry consisting of strophes of four lines in a specific metrical pattern, reputedly invented by the poet Alcaeus

Examples

The Alcaic verse
Classical poets often wrote in the Alcaic to emulate ancient Greek traditions.
His experiment with the Alcaic resulted in a unique blend of structure and expression.
The ode was composed in strict Alcaic stanzas, echoing the works of Horace.
