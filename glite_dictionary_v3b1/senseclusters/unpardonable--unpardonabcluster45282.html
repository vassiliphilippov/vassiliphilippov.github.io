unpardonable

adjective
too serious to be forgiven
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpɑrdənəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɑːdənəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of an action or behavior) too severe to be forgiven or excused.

Examples

An unpardonable act
His betrayal was considered an unpardonable act by everyone in the community.
Many believe that lying under oath is simply unpardonable.
She made an unpardonable error during the presentation, which cost the team the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgivable - able to be forgiven; pardonable
