inroad

noun
hostile attack or incursion
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.rəʊd / (BrE)
A sudden attack or incursion into a place, typically to seize or plunder it; an invasion or hostile attack.

Examples

Enemy inroad at dawn
Historians studied the inroad as a turning point in the region's political development.
The army made a daring inroad into enemy territory under the cover of darkness.
Viking inroads frequently threatened the stability of coastal villages during the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incursion - a sudden attack or invasion
  • synonym: raid - a sudden attack for the purpose of stealing or causing damage
  • antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing from enemy forces after an attack
