jumbie

noun
Caribbean bogeyman
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌmbi / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmbi / (BrE)
a frightening or mischievous spirit or ghost in Caribbean folklore

Examples

A jumbie haunts
In many Caribbean stories, a jumbie is said to haunt the dark forests at night.
Children were afraid to go outside after sunset because of tales about the jumbie.
The old woman warned that a jumbie would visit anyone who misbehaved.
Many children in the village were afraid of the jumbie stories told at night.
Some people leave offerings outside their homes to keep the jumbie away.
The old house was said to be haunted by a mischievous jumbie.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bogeyman - an imaginary evil spirit used to frighten children
  • synonym: duppy - a ghost or spirit in Jamaican folklore
  • synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, often believed to appear to the living
