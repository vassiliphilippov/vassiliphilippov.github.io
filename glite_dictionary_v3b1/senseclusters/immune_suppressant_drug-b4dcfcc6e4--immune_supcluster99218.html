immune suppressant drug

noun
drug that suppresses the immune system
6.4 3.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmjuːn sə.ˈpres.ənt drʌg/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmjuːn sə.ˈpres.ənt drʌg/ (BrE)
A drug that reduces or suppresses the normal activity of the body's immune system, often used to prevent rejection of transplanted organs or to treat autoimmune diseases.

Examples

An immune suppressant drug
After her kidney transplant, she had to take an immune suppressant drug to reduce the risk of rejection.
Doctors prescribed an immune suppressant drug to help manage his autoimmune disorder.
The patient developed an infection as a side effect of long-term immune suppressant drug therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunosuppressant - a substance that suppresses the immune response
