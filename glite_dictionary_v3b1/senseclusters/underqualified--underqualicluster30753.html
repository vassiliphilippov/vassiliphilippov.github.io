underqualified

adjective
lacking required qualifications
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈkwɑːlɪfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not having enough skills, experience, or education for a particular job or position

Examples

Underqualified for job
He was underqualified for the position, so his application was not successful.
Many recent graduates feel underqualified when applying for their first jobs.
Despite being underqualified, she decided to apply and gained valuable interview experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overqualified - having more skills or education than needed for a job
  • antonym: qualified - having the necessary skills, experience, or education
  • synonym: unqualified - not having the necessary skills or qualifications
