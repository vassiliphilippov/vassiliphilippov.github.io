microgrid

noun
local energy network
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌɡrɪd / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌɡrɪd / (BrE)
a small, local energy network that can operate independently from the main power grid

Examples

A microgrid system
The university campus installed a microgrid to provide reliable power during emergencies.
Many remote communities rely on a microgrid powered by solar panels and batteries.
A hospital's microgrid can keep critical equipment running even if the main grid fails.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distributed generation - electricity produced close to where it is used, rather than at a large plant
  • antonym: power grid - a large network for delivering electricity over a wide area
