landlocked salmon

noun
freshwater form of Atlantic salmon
8.1 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈlænd.lɑːkt ˈsæm.ən/ /; / /ˈlænd.lɑːkt ˈsæl.mən/ (AmE), /ˈlænd.lɒkt ˈsæm.ən/ /; / /ˈlænd.lɒkt ˈsæl.mən/ (BrE)
a form of Atlantic salmon that lives and breeds entirely in freshwater lakes, rather than migrating to the sea, especially found in New England and southeastern Canada.

Examples

Landlocked salmon population
Researchers observed an increase in the landlocked salmon population after conservation efforts.
The landlocked salmon thrives in the cold, clear lakes of Maine.
Unlike typical Atlantic salmon, the landlocked salmon does not migrate to the sea to spawn.
