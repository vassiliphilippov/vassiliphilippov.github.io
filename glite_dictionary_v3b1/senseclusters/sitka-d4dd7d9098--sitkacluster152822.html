Sitka

proper noun
town in southeastern Alaska
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtkə / (AmE), / ˈsɪtkə / (BrE)
a town in southeastern Alaska, formerly the capital of Russian America and the capital of Alaska from 1867 to 1906

Examples

Sitka town Alaska
Sitka served as the capital of Alaska before Juneau was designated as the new capital.
Many tourists visit Sitka each year to experience its rich blend of Russian and Native Alaskan history.
The coastal scenery around Sitka is renowned for its natural beauty and wildlife.
