semisacred

adjective
partly sacred
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈseɪkrɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈseɪkrɪd / (BrE)
partly but not completely considered holy or sacred

Examples

A semisacred place
The ancient forest was considered a semisacred place by the local villagers.
Only certain rituals were allowed in the semisacred area of the temple.
The statue stood in a semisacred corner, respected but not worshipped.

Related Terms

  • antonym: profane - not sacred or religious; secular
  • degree higher: sacred - regarded as holy or deserving great respect
