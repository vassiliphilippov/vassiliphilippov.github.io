to criticize someone or something in a way that shows you do not respect or value them; to speak about in a derogatory manner

Examples

To disparage competitors
He tends to disparage his competitors whenever he is interviewed.
Her achievements were often disparaged by those jealous of her success.
It is unfair to disparage someone based on rumors rather than facts.

Synonyms

  • belittle
    5.0 2.45/m
    belittle is to make someone or something seem small and unimportant, while disparage means to speak without respect.
  • denigrate
    7.8 3.38/m
    denigrate is to say unfair, damaging things that harm a person's image, while disparage is a general disrespectful criticism.
  • derogate
    8.4 0.20/m
    derogate is to speak or act to make someone seem less worthy, while disparage is more common everyday criticism.
  • demean
    6.8 2.46/m
    demean is to lower someone's dignity by words or actions, while disparage focuses on saying disrespectful things.
  • vilify
    7.9 2.22/m
    vilify is to attack someone's character and spread harmful claims, while disparage is general disrespect without that campaign.
  • malign
    7.7 2.21/m
    malign is to speak badly and often falsely about someone to hurt them, while disparage is wider criticism.
  • slur
    7.7 0.05/m
    slur is to make an insulting or offensive remark that harms reputation, while disparage is broader disrespectful talk.
  • deride
    7.8 2.42/m
    deride is to laugh at and mock someone in a cruel way, while disparage is less about mocking and more about belittling.
  • insult
    2.1 18/m
    insult is to say rude, hurtful words directly to someone, while disparage often criticizes them more generally or indirectly.
