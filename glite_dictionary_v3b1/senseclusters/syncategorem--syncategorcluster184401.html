syncategorem

noun
word not used alone in logic
Pronunciation: / sɪnˌkætɪˈɡɔrəm / (AmE), / sɪnˌkætɪˈɡɔːrɛm / (BrE)
A word or expression that cannot stand alone as a term in a logical proposition, such as a logical quantifier, adverb, preposition, or conjunction.

Examples

A syncategorem word
Conjunctions and prepositions serve as syncategorems rather than as stand-alone terms.
In medieval logic, scholars paid close attention to the distinction between syncategorems and categorems.
Logical quantifiers like 'all' and 'some' are typical syncategorems in formal logic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: categorem - a word that can be used alone as a term in a logical proposition
