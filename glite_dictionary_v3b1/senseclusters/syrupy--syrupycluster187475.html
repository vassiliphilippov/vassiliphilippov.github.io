syrupy

adjective
thick and sticky like syrup
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəpi / (AmE), / ˈsɪrəpi / (BrE)
having a thick, sticky, and sweet quality like syrup (used for liquids, foods, or voices)

Examples

Syrupy sauce
He poured a syrupy sauce over his pancakes for breakfast.
The drink had a syrupy texture that made it hard to swallow.
The medicine was so syrupy that it coated the spoon.
Her voice took on a strangely syrupy tone when she spoke to the children.
The dessert had a syrupy texture thanks to the added honey.
The pancakes were covered in a syrupy sauce that made them extra sweet.
