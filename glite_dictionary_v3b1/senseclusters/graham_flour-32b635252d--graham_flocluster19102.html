graham flour

noun
whole-wheat flour with bran and germ retained
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪ.əm flaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.əm flaʊə / (BrE)
a type of whole-wheat flour made by grinding the entire wheat grain, including the bran

Examples

Graham flour whole wheat
Graham flour is a good source of fiber because it contains the wheat bran.
She baked the bread with graham flour to give it a hearty texture.
The recipe calls for equal parts graham flour and white flour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wholemeal flour - British term for flour made from the whole wheat grain, including bran and germ
