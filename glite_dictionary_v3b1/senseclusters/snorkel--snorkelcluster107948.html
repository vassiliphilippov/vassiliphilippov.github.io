snorkel

noun
air device for submarines
7.8 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsn…îrk…ôl / (AmE), / Ààsn…îÀêk…ôl / (BrE)
A retractable device with intake and exhaust pipes that provides an air passage, allowing a submarine to remain submerged for extended periods while running its diesel engines.

Examples

Submarine snorkel device
During extended missions, the crew relied on the snorkel to avoid surfacing and detection.
Engineers repaired the damaged snorkel so the submarine could operate its diesel engines underwater.
The submarine used its snorkel to take in air while staying just below the surface.

Synonyms

  • snorkel breather
