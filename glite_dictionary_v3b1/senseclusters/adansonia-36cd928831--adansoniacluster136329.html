Adansonia

proper noun
baobab tree genus
Pronunciation: / ˌædænˈsoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌædænˈsəʊniə / (BrE)
a genus of large, distinctive trees known as baobabs, native to Africa, Madagascar, and Australia

Examples

Adansonia genus
The Adansonia is easily recognized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk.
Many legends in Africa feature the Adansonia as a symbol of life and resilience.
Scientists study the Adansonia to learn about drought resistance in trees.
