godmother

noun
female patron or honorary mentor
3.7
Pronunciation: / \u02c8\u0261\u0251d\u02ccm\u028c\u00f0\u025a / (AmE), / \u02c8\u0261\u0252d\u02ccm\u028c\u00f0\u0259 / (BrE)
a woman who acts as an influential supporter, patron, or mentor of a person, project, movement, or creative field; usually an honorary or secular title rather than a religious role.

Examples

Godmother of jazz
She was hailed as the godmother of the indie film scene for mentoring many directors.
The tech investor became the startup's unofficial godmother, providing funding and key introductions.
At the award ceremony they called her the godmother of the movement for her lifelong support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mentor - an experienced and trusted adviser who provides guidance and support
  • synonym: patron - a person who gives support, especially financial or influential backing, to someone or something
