dip

verb
scoop up liquid with hand or tool

Pronunciation: / dÉªp / (AmE), / dÉªp / (BrE)
to put something quickly or briefly into a liquid and take it out again (for eating, coating, cleaning, coloring, or making candles)

Examples

To dip bread in soup
As the stream cooled, I dipped my hands to collect fresh water.
He dipped soup from the pot using a ladle.
She used a cup to dip some water from the bucket.
Candle makers often dip each wick repeatedly until the candle reaches the desired thickness.
The children learned how to dip wicks in wax to form homemade candles.
To make traditional candles, you must dip the wicks into molten wax several times.
He dipped the brush into varnish to stain the wood.
She carefully dipped the eggs into the blue paint for Easter decorations.
To achieve a deeper colour, you need to dip the fabric in the dye for several minutes.

Synonyms

    2.7 1.01/m
    duck means to push something quickly into liquid and take it out again, while dip is used for many quick liquid actions.
  • dunk
    2.7 0.90/m
    dunk means to plunge food quickly into a drink, while dip covers quickly putting many kinds of things into liquids.
  • sop
    7.3 0.08/m
    sop means briefly soaking bread in sauce to soak up flavor, while dip is a quick in-and-out action for many uses.
