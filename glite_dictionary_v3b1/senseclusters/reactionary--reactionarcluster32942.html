reactionary

adjective
opposing political or social reform
7.6 6.7
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈæk.ʃə.nər.iː/ /; / /riː.ˈæk.ʃɪ.nər.iː/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən.ə.ri/ /; / /riː.ˈæk.ʃən.ə.ri/ (BrE)
(of a person or their views) opposing political or social progress or reform; favoring a return to a previous state.

Examples

Reactionary views
His reactionary ideas were out of step with the changing times.
Some members of the board held reactionary attitudes toward educational reform.
The government was criticized for its reactionary policies against social change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values
  • antonym: progressive - favoring social reform or new ideas
