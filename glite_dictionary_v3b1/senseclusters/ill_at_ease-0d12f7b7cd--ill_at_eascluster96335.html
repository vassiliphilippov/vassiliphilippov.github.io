ill at ease

adjective
uncomfortable or self-conscious
6.5
Pronunciation: / ɪl ət iz / (AmE), / ɪl ət iːz / (BrE)
feeling uncomfortable, anxious, or self-conscious, especially in social situations

Examples

Ill at ease in company
He appeared ill at ease during the formal dinner, glancing around nervously.
She always feels ill at ease when speaking in front of large crowds.
Whenever new people join our group, I become a bit ill at ease until I get to know them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: at ease - feeling relaxed and confident, not nervous or uncomfortable
  • synonym: uneasy - anxious or uncomfortable
