decrown

verb
remove from power or throne
2.9
Pronunciation: / diˈkraʊn / (AmE), / dɪˈkraʊn /; / diˈkraʊn / (BrE)
to remove someone, especially a king or queen, from a position of power or authority

Examples

To decrown a king
After many years of tyranny, people finally managed to decrown their oppressive leader.
The board voted to decrown the CEO after the scandal emerged.
The revolutionaries vowed to decrown the corrupt ruler and restore justice.
History books recount how the people rose together to decrown the king.
It is rare for a revolution to successfully decrown a monarch without violence.
The rebels plotted to decrown the monarch and establish a republic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dethrone - to remove a monarch from the throne
  • antonym: enthrone - to install as a monarch or in a position of power
