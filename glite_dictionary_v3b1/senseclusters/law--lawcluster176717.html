law

noun
fundamental rule or body of rules governing society
1.0
Pronunciation: / lɑː / (AmE), / lɔː / (BrE)
a rule or set of rules regarded as inherent in human nature and essential or binding on human society

Examples

Natural law
Many philosophers have debated whether natural law exists independently of human societies.
Some believe there are universal laws that all human societies must respect.
The concept of moral law has guided the ethics of many cultures for centuries.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: custom - a traditional and widely accepted way of behaving or doing something, which may have the force of law
  • degree lower: regulation - a rule made and maintained by an authority
  • synonym: statute - a written law enacted by a legislative body
