biosystematics

noun
biological classification using genetic and biochemical data
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˌsɪs.təˈmæt.ɪks / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.əʊˌsɪs.tɪˈmat.ɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of the classification and relationships of living organisms using genetic, biochemical, and morphological data, especially in an evolutionary context

Examples

Biosystematics research
Advances in biosystematics have reshaped how scientists classify plant species.
Modern biosystematics integrates molecular and morphological data to resolve evolutionary relationships.
The research team used DNA analysis as part of their biosystematics study of reptiles.

  • synonym: taxonomy - the science of classification of organisms
