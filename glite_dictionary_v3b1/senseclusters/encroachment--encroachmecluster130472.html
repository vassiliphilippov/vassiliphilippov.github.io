encroachment

noun
intrusion on another's property or rights
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkroʊtʃmənt /; / ɪnˈkroʊt͡ʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊtʃmənt /; / ɪnˈkrəʊt͡ʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of entering or intruding on someone else's property, rights, or territory, especially gradually and without permission

Examples

Property encroachment
He was fined for the encroachment caused by building his fence over the property line.
Legal action was taken to stop the continual encroachment on the wildlife reserve.
The neighbors were concerned about the encroachment of the new building onto their land.
Strict laws exist to prevent the encroachment of commercial fishing into protected marine zones.
The villagers protested the encroachment of tourists onto their sacred land.
There is growing concern about the encroachment of urban development into rural areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intrusion - the act of entering without permission
  • synonym: invasion - the act of entering a place in large numbers, often forcefully
  • synonym: trespass - entry onto someone's land or property without permission
  • antonym: withdrawal - the act of leaving or moving away from a place
