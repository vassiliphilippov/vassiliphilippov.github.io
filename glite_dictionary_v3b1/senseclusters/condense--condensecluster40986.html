condense

verb
change gas or vapor into liquid
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛns / (AmE), / kənˈdɛns / (BrE)
to change from a gas or vapor into a liquid, or to make something become denser or more compact (often by cooling or removing water)

Examples

To condense steam
Engineers use cooling systems to condense steam back into water in power plants.
The sudden drop in temperature caused the water vapor to condense on the windows.
When you breathe out on a cold day, your breath will condense into tiny droplets.
The recipe says to condense the soup before adding it to the casserole.
They condensed the tomato sauce by boiling off the excess water.
To make fudge, you often condense the milk by simmering it slowly.
As the fog cooled, it began to condense on the window panes.
Her ideas gradually condensed into a clear plan of action.
Over time, the story condensed into a short, memorable anecdote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coalesce - to come together and form one mass or whole
  • synonym: compress - to press or squeeze something so that it becomes smaller or more compact
  • synonym: concentrate - make a substance denser by removing liquid
  • antonym: dilute - to make or become less concentrated
  • antonym: evaporate - to change from a liquid into a vapor
  • antonym: expand - to increase in size, number, or importance
