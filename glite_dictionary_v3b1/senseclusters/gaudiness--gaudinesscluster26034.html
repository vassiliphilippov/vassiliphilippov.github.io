gaudiness

noun
excessive showiness or ornamentation
9.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈgɑː.dɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈgɔː.dɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈgɔː.dɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being excessively showy, flashy, or decorated in a way that is considered tasteless or vulgar

Examples

Excessive gaudiness
Her outfit was criticized for its gaudiness rather than its taste.
Some people are attracted to the gaudiness of flashy jewelry, while others prefer a more understated look.
The gaudiness of the neon signs made the street look even more chaotic at night.
She disliked the gaudiness of his new suit, finding it much too flashy for the occasion.
The gaudiness of the decorations distracted from the otherwise elegant event.
Their house stood out for its gaudiness among the more subtle and tasteful homes on the street.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elegance - the quality of being graceful and stylish
  • synonym: garishness - the quality of being extremely bright or showy
  • synonym: gaudy - excessively showy or flashy, especially in a tasteless way
