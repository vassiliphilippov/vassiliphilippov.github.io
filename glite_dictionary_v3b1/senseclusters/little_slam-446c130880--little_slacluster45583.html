little slam

noun
twelve-trick contract in bridge
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬.əl slæm / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl slæm / (BrE)
In bridge, a contract that succeeds in winning twelve out of the thirteen possible tricks.

Examples

A little slam contract
Achieving a little slam requires both skillful bidding and careful play.
Missing only one trick, their team celebrated their successful little slam.
They were delighted to bid and make a little slam in hearts.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: grand slam - winning all thirteen tricks in a hand of bridge
