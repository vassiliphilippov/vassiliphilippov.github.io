busy

adjective
full of activity; bustling
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ˈbɪzi / (BrE)
actively engaged in work or activity; having a lot to do (applies to people, places, or periods)

Examples

A busy day
After a busy morning at work, she finally took a short break.
It’s hard to find parking on this busy street during rush hour.
The busy market was crowded with shoppers searching for fresh produce.
He's been busy with meetings all week.
Our restaurant is always busy on Friday nights.
She couldn't answer the phone because she was busy preparing dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crowded - full of people or things; very full
  • synonym: hectic - full of frantic activity; very busy
  • antonym: idle - not active or not doing anything; not busy
  • synonym: occupied - being busy or engaged in activity
  • antonym: quiet - not busy, lacking activity or noise
