protoplanet

noun
developing planet
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊtoʊˌplænɪt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊtəʊˌplænɪt / (BrE)
a young celestial body that is in the process of developing into a planet from dust and gas in space

Examples

A protoplanet in space
Astronomers discovered a protoplanet in a distant star system using advanced telescopes.
A protoplanet gathers material from the surrounding disk of dust and gas as it grows.
The collision of two protoplanets can lead to the formation of a larger planet.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: planet - a large celestial body orbiting a star
  • degree lower: planetesimal - a small body from which a planet originated in the early stages of development
