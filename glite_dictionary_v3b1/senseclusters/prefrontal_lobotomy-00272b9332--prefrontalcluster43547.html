prefrontal lobotomy

noun
surgical procedure severing frontal brain connections

a surgical procedure involving the severing of nerve connections to and from the prefrontal cortex of the brain, formerly used to treat certain mental disorders, often resulting in significant changes in personality and cognition

Examples

A prefrontal lobotomy operation

Synonyms

  • prefrontal leukotomy
    9.9 0.00/m
    prefrontal leukotomy is a medical term for cutting frontal brain pathways in clinical reports, while prefrontal lobotomy is the common public name.
  • frontal lobotomy
    9.9 0.03/m
    frontal lobotomy cuts nerve pathways in the frontal lobes, while prefrontal lobotomy involves the prefrontal cortex and often causes big changes in thinking.
  • leukotomy
    9.9 0.00/m
    leukotomy severs nerve fibers connecting the frontal lobe to other brain parts, while prefrontal lobotomy names the specific operation on the front brain.
  • lobotomy
    9.9 0.65/m
    lobotomy cuts nerve pathways in the frontal lobe in general, while prefrontal lobotomy refers specifically to the prefrontal cortex and its effects.
