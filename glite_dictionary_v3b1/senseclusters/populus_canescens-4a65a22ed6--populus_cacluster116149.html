Populus canescens

noun
species of grey poplar tree
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑpjələs kəˈnɛsɛnz / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləs kəˈnɛsɛnz / (BrE)
A species of large, fast-growing poplar tree with leaves that are grey on the underside, native to Europe but found elsewhere.

Examples

Populus canescens tree
Scientists studied the growth patterns of Populus canescens in different soil types.
Several Populus canescens were planted to provide shade along the avenue.
The leaves of Populus canescens are easily identified by their grey undersides and wavy edges.
