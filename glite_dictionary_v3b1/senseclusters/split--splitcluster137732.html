split

verb
end a relationship or association
1.6
Pronunciation: / splɪt / (AmE), / splɪt / (BrE)
to separate or go in different directions, or to end a relationship or association

Examples

To split up
After ten years of working together, the artists decided to split and pursue solo careers.
Once the project ended, the team split and went their separate ways.
The couple chose to split because they could not resolve their differences.
After years of working together, the partners decided to split and start their own businesses.
As soon as the concert ended, the group agreed to split and meet up later.
We usually take the same route, but today we had to split at the intersection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break up - to end a romantic relationship or partnership
  • antonym: reunite - to come together again after being apart
  • synonym: separate - to stop living together as a couple or to end a partnership
