clothespin

noun
device for fastening clothes to a line
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊðzˌpɪn / (AmE), / ˈkləʊðzˌpɪn / (BrE)
A small device, typically made of wood or plastic, used to fasten clothes to a clothesline while they dry.

Examples

A clothespin on line
He accidentally dropped a clothespin while taking down the laundry.
Several clothespins are missing from the basket after the storm.
She used a clothespin to hang her wet shirt on the line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peg - a short cylindrical piece used for fastening or holding things together; used for clothes in British English
