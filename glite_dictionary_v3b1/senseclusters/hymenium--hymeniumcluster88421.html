hymenium

noun
spore-bearing tissue in fungi
7.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈmiːniəm / (AmE), / hɪˈmiːniəm / (BrE)
The spore-bearing tissue layer of certain fungi, containing cells such as asci or basidia, where spores are produced.

Examples

The hymenium layer
In some fungi, the hymenium forms inside a cup-shaped structure.
Scientists observed changes in the hymenium during the fungus's reproductive stage.
The hymenium develops on the surface of the mushroom's gills.
