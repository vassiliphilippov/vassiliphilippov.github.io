Calvinistic Baptist

noun
Baptist denomination following Calvinist doctrine
8.9
Pronunciation: /kæl.vɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk ˈbæp.tɪst/ /; / /kæl.vɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk ˈbæp.təst/ (AmE), /kæl.vɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk ˈbæp.tɪst/ /; / /kæl.vɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk ˈbæp.təst/ (BrE)
a member of a Baptist denomination that adheres to the teachings of John Calvin, especially the doctrine of predestination

Examples

Calvinistic Baptist church
As a Calvinistic Baptist, he deeply studied the works of John Calvin.
Many Calvinistic Baptists emphasize the doctrine of predestination in their teachings.
The church was founded by a group of Calvinistic Baptists in the eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Arminian Baptist - Baptists not adhering to Calvinist predestination, but Arminian theology
  • synonym: Reformed Baptist - a similar Baptist group emphasizing Reformed theology
