salt

noun
a white substance for seasoning and preserving food
1.0 37/m
Pronunciation: / sÉ”lt / (AmE), / sÉ’lt / (BrE)
a white crystalline substance (sodium chloride) used to season and preserve food, or the sharp, savory taste it gives

Examples

A pinch of salt
She sprinkled a pinch of salt on her scrambled eggs before eating.
The recipe calls for a tablespoon of salt to enhance the flavor.
Too much salt in your diet can be harmful to your health.
She wrinkled her nose at the strong salt in the cheese.
Some people are sensitive to the taste of salt in processed foods.
The soup needs more salt to bring out the flavors.

Synonyms

  • table salt
    1.0 0.28/m
    table salt is the refined salt people use to season food at the table, while salt is the general word for the substance and its flavor.
  • common salt
    1.0 0.03/m
    common salt is the ordinary edible salt used for cooking and preserving, while salt also names the sharp, savory taste you get in food.
  • sodium chloride
    2.3 0.21/m
    sodium chloride is the chemical name for the salt compound used in labs and industry, while salt is the everyday food word and flavor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bitter - having a sharp, sometimes unpleasant taste, one of the basic tastes
  • synonym: sodium chloride - the chemical compound NaCl, which is common salt
  • antonym: sugar - sweet-tasting crystalline substance used for food contrast
  • antonym: sweetness - the quality of tasting sweet, another basic human taste
