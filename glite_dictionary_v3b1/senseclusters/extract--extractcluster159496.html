extract

verb
take out a passage from a text
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstrækt, ˈɛkstrækt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstrækt, ˈɛkstrækt / (BrE)
to take out a passage or piece from a book, article, or other literary work, usually to cite or copy it

Examples

To extract a quote
He carefully extracted quotations from the report to support his argument.
Teachers often extract passages from classic novels to use in classroom discussions.
The researcher decided to extract several key paragraphs from the article for her analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insert - to put something into a text or document
  • synonym: quote - to repeat or copy out words from a source, usually with acknowledgment
