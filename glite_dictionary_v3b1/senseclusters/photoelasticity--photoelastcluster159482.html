photoelasticity

noun
stress visualization method
9.9
Pronunciation: /foʊ.tə.ɪ.læs.ˈtɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /foʊ.toʊ.ɪ.læs.ˈtɪs.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /fəʊ.tə.ɪ.læs.ˈtɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /fəʊ.təʊ.ɪ.læs.ˈtɪs.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the property of some materials to change how they let light through when under stress, used to study or measure stress in objects

Examples

Photoelasticity in materials
Engineers used photoelasticity to study how the bridge would handle heavy traffic loads.
The colorful patterns seen in photoelasticity experiments reveal areas of high stress in the material.
By applying photoelasticity, researchers can identify weak points in complex structures before construction.
Researchers use photoelasticity to visualize stress distribution in transparent plastic models.
The experiment demonstrated photoelasticity by showing colorful patterns under polarized light.
Understanding photoelasticity helps engineers design safer and more efficient structures.
