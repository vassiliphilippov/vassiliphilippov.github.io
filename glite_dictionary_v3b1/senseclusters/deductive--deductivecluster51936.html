deductive

adjective
relating to logical deduction
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
relating to reasoning that starts from general ideas or principles and moves to specific conclusions

Examples

A deductive approach
A deductive approach starts with a general theory and tests it through observation.
In mathematics, many proofs are based on deductive reasoning.
She used deductive logic to solve the complex problem.
A deductive argument proceeds from general premises to reach a specific result.
Philosophers often rely on deductive reasoning to draw logical conclusions.
The scientist used a deductive approach to test her hypothesis based on established laws.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inductive - relating to reasoning from specific instances to general principles
