choking

noun
suffocation by constricting the windpipe
1.9 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃoʊ.kɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtʃəʊ.kɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act or condition of being unable to breathe because the airway is blocked or due to suffocation

Examples

Choking on food
Emergency responders arrived quickly after the reported choking incident.
The defendant denied any involvement in the choking of the victim.
There was no evidence that the choking had been accidental.
He started choking when a piece of steak got stuck in his throat.
Immediate action is necessary in cases of choking to restore normal breathing.
The teacher demonstrated how to help someone who is experiencing choking during lunch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphyxiation - a condition caused by lack of oxygen due to blocked breathing
  • antonym: breathing - the process of taking air into and expelling it from the lungs
  • synonym: strangulation - the act of killing or injuring by squeezing the throat
  • synonym: suffocation - when breathing stops due to lack of air
