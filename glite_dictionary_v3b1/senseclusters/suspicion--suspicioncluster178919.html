suspicion

noun
impression something may be true
Pronunciation: / səˈspɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a feeling or belief that something may be true or exist, often without sure proof

Examples

A suspicion grows
My suspicion is that he forgot to send the email.
She had a strong suspicion that her keys were lost in the garden.
There was a growing suspicion that the company would announce bad news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - firm conviction that something is the case
  • synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something immediately, without conscious reasoning
