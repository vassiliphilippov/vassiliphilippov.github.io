Egyptian cobra

noun
large African venomous snake; symbol of Egyptian Pharaohs
Pronunciation: / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈkoʊbrə / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈkəʊbrə / (BrE)
a large, venomous snake (Naja haje) native to Africa, historically used by Pharaohs as a symbol of power

Examples

The Egyptian cobra snake
The Egyptian cobra was often depicted on the foreheads of ancient Pharaohs' crowns.
The bite of an Egyptian cobra can be fatal without prompt medical treatment.
Zookeepers exercised caution when caring for the Egyptian cobra due to its potent venom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cobra - a highly venomous snake of the family Elapidae
