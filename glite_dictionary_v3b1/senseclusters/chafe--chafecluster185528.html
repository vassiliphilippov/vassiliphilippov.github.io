chafe

verb
to become sore by rubbing
8.4 4.5
Pronunciation: /tʃeɪf/ (AmE), /tʃeɪf/ (BrE)
to make or become sore or irritated by rubbing or friction

Examples

To chafe the skin
If you chafe your skin by scratching too much, it may become red and painful.
The tight collar began to chafe against his neck during the long meeting.
Wearing wet shoes for hours can chafe your feet and make them very sore.
Constant movement can chafe the skin, causing irritation and discomfort.
New shoes may chafe your heels until they are broken in.
The tight collar began to chafe his neck after a few hours.
If your shoes are too tight, they might chafe your heels and cause blisters.
The dog's collar began to chafe her neck after several hours of wear.
The rough seams of the shirt chafed his skin during the long hike.
