water tower

noun
elevated structure storing water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ ˌtaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌtaʊə / (BrE)
a tall structure used to store water at a height to provide pressure for a water supply system

Examples

A water tower structure
During the drought, the level in the water tower dropped significantly.
From the highway, you could easily spot the town's distinctive blue water tower.
The city recently renovated its old water tower to ensure clean drinking water for all residents.
