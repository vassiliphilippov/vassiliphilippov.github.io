term

noun
statue or bust on a square pillar (architecture)
6.0
Pronunciation: / tɝːm / (AmE), / tɜːm / (BrE)
(Architecture) A statue or human bust, or sometimes an animal, carved from the top part of a square pillar, originally used as a boundary marker in ancient Rome.

Examples

A stone term
In ancient times, a term would mark the end of one landowner's property and the start of another's.
On the estate’s boundary, a weathered term stood as a silent sentinel.
The museum displayed a beautifully restored term from a Roman villa.

Related Terms
