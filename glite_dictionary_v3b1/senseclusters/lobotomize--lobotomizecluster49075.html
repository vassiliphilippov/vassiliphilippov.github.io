lobotomize

verb
to dull or weaken
9.4 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / lo äÀàb…ëÀêt…ôÀåma…™z / (AmE), / l…ôÀàb…ít…ôma…™z / (BrE)
to dull, weaken, or remove the ability to think or function effectively, often used figuratively to describe making something less creative or effective.

Examples

To lobotomize creativity
Some critics argue that strict regulations lobotomize the creative process in the film industry.
The new policy could lobotomize the department, leaving it unable to respond quickly to emergencies.
He felt that repetitive tasks at work had slowly lobotomized his enthusiasm and motivation.

Synonyms

  • stultify
    9.8 0.12/m
    stultify makes a person or idea lose energy and usefulness, while lobotomize means removing creative thought and effective mental function.
  • debilitate
    7.7 1.72/m
    debilitate makes someone or something physically weak or less able to work, while lobotomize means dulling minds and creative ability.
  • devitalize
    8.1 0.03/m
    devitalize strips life or energy from things and people, while lobotomize refers to dulling thought and making mental action ineffective.

Related Terms
