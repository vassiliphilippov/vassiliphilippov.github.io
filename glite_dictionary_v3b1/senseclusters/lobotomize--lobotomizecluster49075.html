lobotomize

verb
to dull or weaken
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈloʊ.bə.tə.maɪz/ (AmE), /ˈlɒb.ə.tə.maɪz/ (BrE)
to dull, weaken, or remove the ability to think or function effectively, often used figuratively to describe making something less creative or effective.

Examples

To lobotomize creativity
Some critics argue that strict regulations lobotomize the creative process in the film industry.
The new policy could lobotomize the department, leaving it unable to respond quickly to emergencies.
He felt that repetitive tasks at work had slowly lobotomized his enthusiasm and motivation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cripple - to severely damage or weaken something
  • antonym: strengthen - to make stronger or more effective
  • synonym: weaken - to make less strong or effective
