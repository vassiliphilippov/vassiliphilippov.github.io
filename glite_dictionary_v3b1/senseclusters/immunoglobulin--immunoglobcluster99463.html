immunoglobulin

noun
antibody protein in immune response
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˈɡlɑːbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˈɡlɒbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
any of a class of proteins produced by lymphoid tissue in vertebrates, functioning as antibodies in the immune response

Examples

An immunoglobulin test
An immunoglobulin test can help diagnose immune system disorders.
There are several types of immunoglobulins, each with a different role in immunity.
Vaccines stimulate the body to produce specific immunoglobulins against diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibody - a blood protein produced to counteract a specific antigen
