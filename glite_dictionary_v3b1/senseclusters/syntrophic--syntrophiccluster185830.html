syntrophic

adjective
mutually beneficial cooperation
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / sɪnˈtrəʊfɪk / (BrE)
describing organisms that cooperate by exchanging nutrients for mutual benefit

Examples

syntrophic bacteria
Some bacteria are syntrophic, working together to break down substances neither could digest alone.
The syntrophic relationship between these microbes is essential for nutrient cycling in the environment.
Researchers discovered a new syntrophic partnership in the soil that improves plant growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parasitic - involving one organism benefiting at the expense of another
  • synonym: symbiotic - involving interaction between two different organisms living together
