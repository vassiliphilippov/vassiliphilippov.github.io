shaming

adjective
causing shame
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
causing someone to feel ashamed or embarrassed, especially in a public way.

Examples

Shaming remarks
The teacher's shaming comments made the student feel very uncomfortable in front of the class.
Many people criticized the shaming tactics used on social media to embarrass others.
Instead of offering help, his friends gave him a shaming look when he made a mistake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: humiliating - making someone feel very embarrassed or ashamed
  • antonym: supportive - giving help or encouragement instead of criticism
