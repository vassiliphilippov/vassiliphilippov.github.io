equal-area map projection

noun
map projection preserving area proportions
8.4 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.kwəl ˈer.i.ə mæp prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈiː.kwəl ˈeə.ri.ə mæp prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (BrE)
a type of map projection in which areas on the map are proportional to the corresponding areas on the globe, ensuring accurate representation of spatial extents.

Examples

An equal-area map projection type
Cartographers choose an equal-area map projection when they need area comparisons to remain valid.
The equal-area map projection is often used for world maps to accurately represent the size of continents.
While shapes may be distorted, an equal-area map projection preserves the correct proportions of land masses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformal map projection - a map projection that preserves local shape but not area
