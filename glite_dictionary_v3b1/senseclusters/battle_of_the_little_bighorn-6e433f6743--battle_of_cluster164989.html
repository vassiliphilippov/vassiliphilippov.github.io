Battle of the Little Bighorn

proper noun
1876 battle in Montana where Custer's forces were defeated
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbæt.əl əv ðə ˈlɪt.əl ˈbɪg.hɔːrn/ (AmE), /ˈbæt.əl əv ðə ˈlɪt.əl ˈbɪg.hɔːn/ (BrE)
a famous 1876 battle near the Little Bighorn River in Montana, where United States cavalry led by General Custer was defeated by a coalition of Native American tribes, including Sioux and Cheyenne

Examples

The Battle of the Little Bighorn fight
Books and documentaries often recount the events of the Battle of the Little Bighorn.
Custer and his men were overwhelmed during the Battle of the Little Bighorn.
The Battle of the Little Bighorn marked a devastating defeat for the U.S. Army in 1876.
