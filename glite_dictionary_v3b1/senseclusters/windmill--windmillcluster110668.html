windmill

verb
to spin arms widely
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndˌmɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪndˌmɪl / (BrE)
to move your arms or legs in wide, circular motions like the blades of a windmill

Examples

To windmill your arms
He windmilled his arms to keep his balance on the ice.
The dancer windmilled her arms as part of the energetic routine.
Trying not to fall, the child windmilled his arms wildly in the playground.
The boxer began to windmill his arms wildly in the final round.
Children often windmill their arms when they lose their balance.
She tried to windmill her legs to keep from falling off the skateboard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flail - to wave or swing something about wildly
  • synonym: swing - to move back and forth or in a curve
  • synonym: whirl - to spin or move quickly in circles
