snooker

verb
prevent opponent's direct shot (cue sports)
8.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsn äk…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàsnuÀêk…ô / (BrE)
(in snooker or similar cue sports) to leave your opponent unable to make a direct shot at the object ball by positioning the cue ball behind another ball.

Examples

To snooker an opponent
He managed to snooker his opponent with a clever safety shot.
If you snooker your rival, they have little chance to score on their next turn.
She snookered me so well that I couldn't even see the object ball.
