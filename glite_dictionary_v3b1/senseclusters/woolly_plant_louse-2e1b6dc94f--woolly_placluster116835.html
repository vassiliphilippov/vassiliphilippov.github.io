woolly plant louse

noun
a wax-covered, cottony-looking plant insect
7.5 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwʊl.ɪ plænt laʊs/ (AmE), /ˈwʊl.ɪ plɑːnt laʊs/ (BrE)
a small insect that excretes a waxy substance, giving it the appearance of being covered in fine, white, cotton- or wool-like threads; commonly found on plants and considered a pest

Examples

A woolly plant louse pest
Clusters of woolly plant lice look like fluffy white spots on the branches and leaves.
Gardeners often mistake the woolly plant louse for mold due to its cotton-like wax secretion.
The woolly plant louse can cause severe damage to apple orchards if not controlled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphid - a small sap-sucking insect, often a pest on plants
