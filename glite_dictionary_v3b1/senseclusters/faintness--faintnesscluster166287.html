Examples

Sudden faintness
He felt a sudden faintness in his limbs and had to sit down.
The faintness of the signal made it difficult to hear the message clearly.
There was a faintness in her voice as she spoke after the long illness.
During the long ceremony, several people complained of faintness and needed to sit down.
She felt a sudden faintness as she stood up quickly from her chair.
The patient's faintness was caused by dehydration and lack of food.

Synonyms

    lightheadedness feels like a dizzy, floating head sensation, while faintness is a broader state of weakness and near loss of consciousness.
  • headedness
    7.8 0.26/m
    headedness makes you feel dizzy and unsteady in your head, while faintness includes general physical weakness and possible collapse.
  • dizziness
    2.9 5.99/m
    dizziness gives a sense of spinning or unsteadiness, while faintness adds real physical weakness and the chance of passing out.
  • wooziness
    5.4 0.04/m
    wooziness is a slightly confused, lightheaded feeling, while faintness is stronger physical weakness and a real risk of fainting.
  • feebleness
    4.8 0.02/m
    feebleness means long-term physical weakness often from age or illness, while faintness is a sudden dizzy weakness that may cause passing out.
