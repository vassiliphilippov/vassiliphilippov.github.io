psychodramatic

adjective
theatrically emotional
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊdrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊdrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
showing intense, theatrical, or exaggerated emotional expression, as if part of a psychodrama.

Examples

Psychodramatic reaction
Her psychodramatic reaction to the news surprised everyone in the office.
The film's psychodramatic scenes made the audience feel the characters' intense emotions.
He tends to become psychodramatic when discussing personal setbacks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melodramatic - showing exaggerated emotions or drama
  • antonym: restrained - showing controlled or limited emotion
