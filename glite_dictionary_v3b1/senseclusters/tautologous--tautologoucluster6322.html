tautologous

adjective
needlessly repetitive
Pronunciation: / tɔˈtɑlədʒəs / (AmE), / tɔːˈtɒl.ə.ɡəs /; / tɔˈtɒlədʒəs / (BrE)
expressing the same idea more than once in different words, usually unnecessarily or without adding new information

Examples

Tautologous statement
His explanation was tautologous, simply repeating the same point in different words.
The statement 'free gift' is often considered tautologous because a gift is by definition free.
Critics found the politician's answer tautologous and lacking in real substance.
The politician's tautologous speech repeated the same points several times.
Her writing became tautologous when she described the same event in multiple ways.
Avoiding tautologous statements can make your arguments clearer and more effective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concise - expressing much in few words
  • synonym: redundant - unnecessarily repetitive or superfluous
