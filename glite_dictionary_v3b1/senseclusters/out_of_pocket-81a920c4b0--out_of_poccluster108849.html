out of pocket

adjective
expenses paid personally, not reimbursed
6.4
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / aʊt əv ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
having to pay for something with one's own money, especially when reimbursement is not provided

Examples

Out of pocket expenses
Many people end up out of pocket after unexpected emergencies.
She had to pay for the hotel out of pocket because her company wouldn't cover the cost.
The insurance doesn't cover dental care, so any treatments are strictly out of pocket expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outlay - an amount of money spent on something
  • antonym: reimbursed - compensated for expenses paid by someone else
