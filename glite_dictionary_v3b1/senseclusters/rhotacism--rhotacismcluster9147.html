rhotacism

noun
sound change to /r/
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊtəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəsɪzəm / (BrE)
a historical or phonological sound change in which a consonant, often /s/ or /z/, becomes /r/, especially between vowels

Examples

Latin rhotacism change
The shift from /s/ to /r/ in some Latin words is an example of rhotacism.
Linguists study rhotacism to understand how languages evolve over time.
In early Latin, rhotacism changed the word 'honos' to 'honor'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fortition - a sound change in which a consonant becomes stronger or harder
  • synonym: lenition - a sound change in which a consonant becomes softer or weaker
