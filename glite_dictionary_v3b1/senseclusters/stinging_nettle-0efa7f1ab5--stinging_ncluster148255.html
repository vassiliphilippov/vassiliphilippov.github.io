stinging nettle

noun
a plant with stinging hairs and toothed leaves
9.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈstɪŋ.ɪŋ ˈnet.əl/ /; / /ˈstɪŋ.əŋ ˈnet.əl/ (AmE), /ˈstɪŋ.ɪŋ ˈnet.əl/ /; / /ˈstɪŋ.əŋ ˈnet.əl/ (BrE)
a perennial plant with coarsely toothed leaves covered in stinging hairs that cause skin irritation on contact, native to Eurasia and now common in North America

Examples

A stinging nettle plant
After brushing against a patch of stinging nettle, he developed a painful irritation on his arm.
Be careful when hiking through the woods, as stinging nettle can cause a sharp rash if touched.
She brewed tea from dried stinging nettle leaves to take advantage of its health benefits.
