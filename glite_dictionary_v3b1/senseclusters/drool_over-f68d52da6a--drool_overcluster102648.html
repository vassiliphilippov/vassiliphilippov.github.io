drool over

verb
look at with intense desire or envy
6.8 0.40/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdruÀêl Àåo äv…ö /; / ÀàdruÀêl Àào äv…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàdruÀêl Àå…ô äv…ô /; / ÀàdruÀêl Àà…ô äv…ô / (BrE)
to look at or react to someone or something with strong desire, longing, or admiration, often in an obvious or exaggerated way

Examples

Every time she saw the sports car in the showroom, she couldn't help but drool over it.

The students drooled over the display of delicious pastries at the bakery window.
