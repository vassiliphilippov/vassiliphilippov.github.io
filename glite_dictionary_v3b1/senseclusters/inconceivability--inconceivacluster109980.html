inconceivability

noun
impossibility of being imagined or believed
2.9 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkənˌsiːvəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˌsiːvəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to imagine, believe, or comprehend

Examples

The inconceivability of ideas
His theory was dismissed due to the sheer inconceivability of its fundamental premise.
She tried to express the inconceivability of such a cruel act being committed by anyone she knew.
The inconceivability of life on other planets fascinates many scientists and philosophers.
