paregoric

noun
opium-based medicine for diarrhea
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpærɪˈɡɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærɪˈɡɒrɪk / (BrE)
A medicinal preparation containing opium, formerly used to treat diarrhea and coughs.

Examples

A paregoric medicine
Due to its opium content, paregoric is strictly regulated today.
Old medical kits often contained a bottle of paregoric as a remedy for stomach upsets.
The doctor prescribed paregoric for the patient's severe diarrhea.
