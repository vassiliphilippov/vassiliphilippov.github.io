Doppler

proper noun
Austrian physicist
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑplər / (AmE), / ˈdɒplə / (BrE)
the surname of Christian Doppler, an Austrian physicist known for describing the Doppler effect

Examples

Christian Doppler
Christian Doppler first described the phenomenon that now bears his name in 1842.
The Doppler effect is named after the Austrian scientist who discovered it.
Many physics students learn about Doppler and his contributions to wave theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Doppler effect - the change in frequency or wavelength of a wave in relation to an observer
