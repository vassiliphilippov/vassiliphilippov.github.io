under the radar

adverb
not noticed; unnoticed
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚ ðə rəˌdɑr / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ðə ˈreɪdɑː / (BrE)
If someone or something is under the radar, they are not noticed by other people, often deliberately.

Examples

Work under the radar
Many artists work under the radar before achieving fame.
She prefers to keep her achievements under the radar, avoiding attention from the media.
The new company stayed under the radar until its groundbreaking product was revealed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prominent - very noticeable or important
  • synonym: unnoticed - not seen or observed
