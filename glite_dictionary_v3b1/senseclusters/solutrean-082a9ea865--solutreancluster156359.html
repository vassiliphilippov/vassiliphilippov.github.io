Solutrean

noun
Paleolithic culture
Pronunciation: / səˈluːtriən / (AmE), / səˈluːtriən / (BrE)
a Paleolithic archaeological culture of western Europe, about 22,000–17,000 years ago, known for its finely crafted stone tools

Examples

The Solutrean culture
The Solutrean is famous for its beautifully crafted stone blades and points.
Researchers discovered new evidence of Solutrean settlements in southwestern France.
Many museums display Solutrean artifacts to illustrate advances in prehistoric toolmaking.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Magdalenian - a later Upper Paleolithic culture in western Europe
