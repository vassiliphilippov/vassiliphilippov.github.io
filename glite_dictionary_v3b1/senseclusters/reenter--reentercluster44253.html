reenter

verb
to go back in
3.2 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀà…õnt…ôr / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀà…õnt…ô / (BrE)
to go back into a place or situation after having left it

Examples

To reenter the room
After a short break, the actor reentered the stage to continue the performance.
You must scan your badge each time you reenter the building for security reasons.
Once the storm passed, residents were allowed to reenter their homes.
After the fire drill, everyone reentered the building in an orderly manner.
He paused outside the classroom before he reentered to collect his forgotten notebook.
Once the repairs were finished, residents were allowed to reenter their homes.

Synonyms

  • return
    1.0 30/m
    return is coming back to a place or situation after being away, while reenter focuses on going back into a place or situation.
  • come back
    1.3 102/m
    come back is commonly used to say someone returns or recovers, while reenter stresses physically going back into a place.
  • come again
    2.1 1.28/m
    come again is an informal phrase for visiting soon after a short time away, while reenter implies going back inside a place or situation.
  • revisit
    3.6 1.53/m
    revisit means visiting a place again after some time and often for a purpose, while reenter simply means going back into it.
