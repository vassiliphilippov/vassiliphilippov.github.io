logistically

adverb
in terms of planning
5.8
Pronunciation: / ləˈdʒɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ləˈdʒɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the planning, organization, or practical details of complex activities or operations

Examples

To logistically plan events
Logistically, organizing a conference for hundreds of people is a major challenge.
The event was logistically complex due to the number of international guests.
They found it logistically impossible to deliver all the supplies in one day.
It was logistically challenging to organize the conference in such a remote location.
The team considered whether the plan was logistically possible before agreeing to proceed.
Moving hundreds of people in one day is logistically complex and requires careful planning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: practically - in a way that relates to actual use or practice
  • synonym: strategically - in a way that relates to planning or strategy
