primitive

noun
person from an early civilization
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪməˌtɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
a person who lived in or belongs to an early stage of civilization, especially before the development of complex societies

Examples

A primitive person
Early primitives developed tools from stone and wood to survive in their environment.
Some anthropologists studied the art created by the primitives of the region.
The novel describes the encounter between explorers and the primitives they discovered.
