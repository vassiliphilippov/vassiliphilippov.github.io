biopiracy

noun
commercial exploitation of native biological materials
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈpaɪ.rə.si / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪ.ə(ʊ)ˈpʌɪ.rə.si / (BrE)
The practice of commercially exploiting naturally occurring biological materials, such as plants or genetic resources, especially by obtaining patents that restrict their traditional use, often without compensation to the indigenous communities from which they originate.

Examples

Biopiracy of plants
International agreements attempt to address the issue of biopiracy by protecting the rights of native communities.
Several indigenous groups have protested against biopiracy of their traditional medicinal plants.
The pharmaceutical company's actions were criticized as biopiracy by local activists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioprospecting - the search for and commercialization of new products based on biological resources
