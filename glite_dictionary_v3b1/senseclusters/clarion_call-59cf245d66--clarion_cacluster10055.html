clarion call

noun
urgent and inspiring request for action
Pronunciation: / ˈklæriən kɔl / (AmE), / ˈklæriən kɔːl / (BrE)
a strong and clear request or demand for action, especially one that inspires people to act

Examples

A clarion call for action
Her article sounded a clarion call for equality and social justice.
The climate march was a clarion call for immediate environmental reforms.
The president's statement acted as a clarion call to support the new policy initiative.

Related Terms

  • synonym: call to arms - an appeal to prepare for a challenge or conflict
