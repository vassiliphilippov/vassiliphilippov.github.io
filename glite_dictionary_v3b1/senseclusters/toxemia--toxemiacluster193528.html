toxemia

noun
blood poisoning by bacterial toxins
8.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / t…ëkÀàsiÀêmi…ô /; / t…ë…°ÀàziÀêmi…ô / (AmE), / t…íkÀàsiÀêmi…ô / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the blood contains poisonous substances (toxins), which can be caused by bacteria entering the bloodstream and leading to blood poisoning; the term is sometimes used specifically for a complication in pregnancy (pre-eclampsia).

Examples

Severe toxemia symptoms
Doctors monitored her closely for any signs of toxemia during her recovery.
Early recognition of toxemia is vital for effective treatment and patient survival.
The patient developed toxemia following a severe bacterial infection.
Doctors monitored her closely for signs of pre-eclampsia and toxaemia during pregnancy.
The patient was diagnosed with toxaemia after showing symptoms of severe infection.
Without prompt treatment, toxaemia can become life-threatening.
Swelling in her legs raised concerns about possible toxaemia.
The doctor diagnosed her with toxaemia after noticing high blood pressure and protein in her urine.
Untreated, toxaemia during pregnancy can lead to serious complications for both mother and child.

Synonyms

  • blood poisoning
    3.6 0.14/m
    blood poisoning is a general infection where bacteria or toxins enter the bloodstream, while toxemia often means pregnancy-related blood toxins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preeclampsia - a disorder of pregnancy characterized by high blood pressure and protein in the urine
  • synonym: sepsis - a serious medical condition caused by the body's response to infection in the bloodstream
  • synonym: septicemia - blood poisoning caused by the presence of bacteria or their toxins in the blood
