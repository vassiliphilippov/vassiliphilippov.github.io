shock

verb
to inflict trauma or intense disturbance
1.0 2.1
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk / (AmE), / ʃɒk / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel sudden surprise, distress, or disbelief

Examples

To shock the audience
She was shocked by the traumatic experience and needed time to recover.
The news of his sudden death shocked everyone in the community.
The violent images in the documentary shocked many viewers.
Her sudden resignation shocked everyone at the company.
It shocked me to learn about his secret past.
The tragic accident shocked the entire community.
His rude comments shocked many people at the meeting.
I was completely shocked when I found out I had won the lottery.
The news of her sudden resignation really shocked everyone in the office.
