hogget

noun
young sheep before first shearing
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑɡɪt / (AmE), / ˈhɒɡɪt / (BrE)
A young sheep, especially one between weaning and its first shearing, usually up to about one year old.

Examples

Young hogget sheep
Each spring, the young hoggets are shorn for wool for the first time.
In some regions, hogget meat is prized for its unique flavor.
The farmer separated the hogget from the older sheep to prepare for their first shearing.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: lamb - a very young sheep, especially less than a year old
  • degree higher: mutton - meat from a mature sheep
