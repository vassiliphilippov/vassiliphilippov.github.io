toroid

noun
ring-shaped surface or solid
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔːrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɔːrɔɪd / (BrE)
a surface or solid shaped like a ring or doughnut, formed by rotating a circle around an axis outside the circle

Examples

A toroid shape
A toroid can be visualized as a doughnut-shaped surface of revolution.
Engineers often use toroid cores in transformers to reduce energy loss.
The magnetic field is confined within the toroid in this experiment.
Engineers designed a toroid to confine the plasma in the fusion reactor.
The magnetic field inside a toroid is uniform along its circular path.
To illustrate his point, the professor drew a large toroid on the whiteboard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: torus - a doughnut-shaped solid formed by rotating a circle about an axis in its plane
