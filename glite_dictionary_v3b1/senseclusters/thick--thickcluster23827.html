thick

adjective
having a large distance between surfaces
2.7
Pronunciation: / θɪk / (AmE), / θɪk / (BrE)
having a large distance between sides, or being dense, compact, hard to see or pass through, or filled with something

Examples

Thick layer of jam
He spread a thick layer of jam on his toast.
She wore a thick sweater to stay warm in the winter.
The wall is so thick that it blocks out all noise.
Early morning travelers struggled to see through the thick fog covering the highway.
She poured the thick smoothie into a tall glass, impressed by its creamy texture.
The stew became thick after simmering for hours on the stove.
It was almost impossible to push through the thick hedge surrounding the old house.
The thick undergrowth made it difficult for the hikers to find the path.
We wandered through the thick forest until the sunlight disappeared.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compact - closely and neatly packed together
  • synonym: deep - used for strong or intense darkness or shadow
  • synonym: dense - closely compacted in substance; thick
  • degree lower: dim - not bright or clear (of light or darkness)
  • antonym: sparse - thinly dispersed or scattered
  • antonym: thin - having a small distance between opposite sides; the opposite of thick
  • synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency
