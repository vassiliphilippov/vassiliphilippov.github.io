Nolina

noun
genus of yucca-like perennial plants
Pronunciation: / noʊˈliːnə / (AmE), / nəʊˈliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of perennial plants resembling yucca, native to the southern United States and Mexico, with long, narrow leaves and a woody stem.

Examples

Nolina plant species
Botanists have classified this unusual specimen as part of the Nolina genus.
Some species of Nolina produce tall flower stalks in late spring.
The desert garden includes several Nolina plants thriving in the sandy soil.
