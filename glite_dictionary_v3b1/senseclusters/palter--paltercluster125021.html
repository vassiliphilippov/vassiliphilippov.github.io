palter

verb
evade or equivocate to mislead
9.9 9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpæl.tər/ /; / /ˈpɔːl.tər/ (AmE), /ˈpæl.tə/ /; / /ˈpɒl.tə/ /; / /ˈpɔːl.tə/ (BrE)
to act or speak evasively or ambiguously, especially in order to mislead or avoid making a definite statement

Examples

To palter with words
He tried to palter his way out of trouble, but no one was fooled by his evasions.
She would not palter with her principles, even when pressured by her colleagues.
When asked about the incident, the minister began to palter, avoiding a direct answer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confess - to admit the truth or wrongdoing
  • synonym: equivocate - to use ambiguous language so as to conceal the truth or avoid committing oneself
  • antonym: lie - to say something that is not true in order to deceive
  • synonym: prevaricate - to speak or act in an evasive way
