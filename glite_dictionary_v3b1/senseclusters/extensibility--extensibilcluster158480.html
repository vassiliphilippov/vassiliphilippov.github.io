extensibility

noun
ability to be stretched
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɛnsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɛnsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a material, tissue, or membrane to be stretched or lengthened without breaking or tearing

Examples

Extensibility of fabric
The extensibility of the fabric makes it ideal for sportswear and active clothing.
Doctors assess the extensibility of muscles during physical therapy sessions.
High extensibility in certain tissues helps the body recover from injuries more effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elasticity - the ability of a material to return to its original shape after being stretched
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not easily bent or stretched
