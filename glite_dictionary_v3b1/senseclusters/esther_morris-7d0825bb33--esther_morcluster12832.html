Esther Morris

proper noun
U.S. suffragist and first female justice of the peace
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɛstər ˈmɔrɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛstə ˈmɒrɪs / (BrE)
(1814–1902) An American suffragist from Wyoming, known as the first woman to serve as a justice of the peace in the United States and a prominent advocate for women's voting rights.

Examples

Esther Morris suffragist
Esther Morris played a significant role in securing the right to vote for women in Wyoming.
A statue honoring Esther Morris stands outside the Wyoming State Capitol.
Many historians credit Esther Morris as a trailblazer in the American women's suffrage movement.
