poeciliid

noun
a small livebearing tropical fish
Pronunciation: / piˈsɪliɪd / (AmE), / piˈsɪliɪd / (BrE)
A small, usually brightly colored livebearing fish of the Poeciliidae family, common in warm freshwater or brackish habitats.

Examples

Bright poeciliid fish
Many aquarists keep poeciliids such as guppies and mollies in their home tanks.
Some species of poeciliids are introduced to ponds to help control mosquito populations.
The poeciliid family includes popular aquarium fish known for their vibrant colors.
