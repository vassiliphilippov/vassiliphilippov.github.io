muttonbird

noun
seabird meat
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌtənˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈmʌtənˌbɜːd / (BrE)
the flesh or meat of a muttonbird, a type of seabird, used as food

Examples

Cooked muttonbird
The islanders rely on muttonbird as a traditional source of protein.
She prepared a stew using smoked muttonbird, which had a rich, gamey flavor.
During the festival, locals serve dishes made from muttonbird to celebrate their heritage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fish - the flesh of fish used as food
  • synonym: poultry - meat from domesticated birds such as chickens or turkeys
