introvert

verb
turn thoughts or feelings inward
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntrəˌvɝt / (AmE), / ˈɪntrəˌvɜːt / (BrE)
to turn (one's thoughts or feelings) inward; to focus attention on one's own inner life or emotions rather than external events.

Examples

To introvert feelings
After the argument, she introverted her emotions and spoke very little.
He introverted his feelings, making it difficult for others to understand what he was going through.
Some people tend to introvert their worries rather than express them openly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extravert - to direct (thoughts or energy) outward rather than inward
  • synonym: internalize - to make (attitudes or behavior) part of one's nature by learning
