wood-fern

noun
a fern of the genus Dryopteris
Pronunciation: / wʊd fɜrn / (AmE), / wʊd fɜːn / (BrE)
Any of several ferns of the genus Dryopteris, typically found in woodland areas and known for their divided, leathery fronds.

Examples

A wood-fern plant
Gardeners often plant wood-fern to add texture and greenery to shaded areas.
The wood-fern thrives in shaded forest environments with moist, well-drained soil.
We identified several species of wood-fern growing along the hiking trail.
