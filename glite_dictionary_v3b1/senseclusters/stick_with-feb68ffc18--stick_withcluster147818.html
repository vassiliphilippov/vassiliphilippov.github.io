stick with

verb
continue to follow or support
Pronunciation: / stɪk wɪð / (AmE), / stɪk wɪð / (BrE)
to continue to do, support, or stay with something or someone, especially when it is difficult

Examples

To stick with a plan
Even when it was hard, he stuck with his training routine.
If you stick with the plan, you’re more likely to succeed.
She decided to stick with her original choice.
Despite the challenges, she decided to stick with her original plan.
If you stick with him, you'll have a loyal friend for life.
Many students find it hard to stick with a new habit after the first week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to leave something behind or stop supporting it
  • antonym: give up - to stop trying to do something before finishing, usually because it is too difficult
  • synonym: persist - to continue firmly in a course of action
