air-raid shelter

noun
shelter for protection from air raids
7.7 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈer.reɪd ˈʃel.tər/ (AmE), /ˈeə.reɪd ˈʃel.tə/ (BrE)
A strong structure, often underground, built to protect people from bombs dropped during air raids, usually equipped with basic living facilities.

Examples

The air-raid shelter
During World War II, families would often hide in the air-raid shelter when sirens sounded.
The old air-raid shelter beneath the school is now a small museum.
They stocked the air-raid shelter with canned food and blankets in case of emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bomb shelter - a structure designed to provide protection during bombing raids
  • synonym: bunker - a reinforced underground shelter, typically for military use
