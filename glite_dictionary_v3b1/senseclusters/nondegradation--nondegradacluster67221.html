nondegradation

noun
resistance to decay
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɛɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪɡrəˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality or state of not breaking down, decaying, or decomposing over time or under certain conditions

Examples

Nondegradation of materials
The nondegradation of the new coating makes it ideal for outdoor use.
Scientists are studying materials with high nondegradation for use in medical implants.
The nondegradation of plastics is a major concern for environmentalists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: degradation - the process of breaking down or deteriorating
  • synonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
