oversee

verb
supervise, direct work
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˈsi / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsiː / (BrE)
to supervise or watch over and direct the work or performance of others

Examples

To oversee the work
An external auditor will oversee the voting process to ensure fairness.
It's important to oversee the project closely so deadlines are met.
She was hired to oversee the company's financial operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or give proper attention to something
  • synonym: supervise - to be in charge of and direct other people at work
