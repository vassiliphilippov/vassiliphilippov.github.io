ectopic pregnancy

noun
pregnancy occurring outside the uterus
6.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /ek.ˈtɑːp.ɪk ˈpreɡ.nən.siː/ /; / /ɪk.ˈtɑːp.ɪk ˈpreɡ.nən.siː/ (AmE), /ek.ˈtɒp.ɪk ˈpreɡ.nən.siː/ /; / /ɪk.ˈtɒp.ɪk ˈpreɡ.nən.siː/ (BrE)
a pregnancy in which the fertilized egg implants and develops outside the uterus, most commonly in a fallopian tube

Examples

Ectopic pregnancy in tube
Advances in ultrasound technology have improved the early detection of ectopic pregnancy.
If left untreated, an ectopic pregnancy can be life-threatening for the mother.
The doctor suspected an ectopic pregnancy after the patient reported severe abdominal pain.
The ultrasound revealed an ectopic pregnancy that required urgent medical attention.
Treatment of an ectopic pregnancy often involves medication or surgery to prevent complications.
Women with an ectopic pregnancy may experience abdominal pain and unusual bleeding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal pregnancy - pregnancy in which the embryo develops inside the uterus
  • synonym: tubal pregnancy - a pregnancy located specifically in the fallopian tube
