disownment

noun
refusal to acknowledge as one's own
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈoʊnmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈəʊnmənt / (BrE)
the act of refusing to acknowledge or accept someone or something as one's own

Examples

Family disownment
Her decision was met with threats of disownment from her conservative parents.
In some cultures, disownment is seen as a last resort for serious family disputes.
The disownment of his son created a permanent rift in the family.
