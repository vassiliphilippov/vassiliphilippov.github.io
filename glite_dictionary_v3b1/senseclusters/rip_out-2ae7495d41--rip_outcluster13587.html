rip out

verb
remove suddenly or forcefully
4.2
Pronunciation: / rɪp aʊt / (AmE), / rɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something quickly or forcefully, especially by pulling; also used figuratively to mean remove or eliminate suddenly and completely.

Examples

To rip out a tooth
The dentist had to rip out the broken tooth because it couldn't be saved.
The new leadership wants to rip out inefficient practices from the company.
They decided to rip out the old carpet before installing hardwood floors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insert - to put something into something else
  • synonym: tear out - to remove by tearing or pulling forcibly
