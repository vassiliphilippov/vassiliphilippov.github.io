leverage

noun
power or strategic advantage
5.2 7.48/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…õv…ôr…™d í / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêv…ôr…™d í / (BrE)
the power or ability to influence people, events, or decisions; strategic advantage

Examples

Political leverage
Having experience in both fields gave her significant leverage in the job market.
The company used its financial leverage to negotiate better contract terms.
The organization gained political leverage after its successful campaign.
