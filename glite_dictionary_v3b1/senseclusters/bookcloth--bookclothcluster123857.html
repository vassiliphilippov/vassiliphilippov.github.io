bookcloth

noun
fabric for covering book covers
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌklɒθ / (BrE)
a woven fabric, typically made of cotton, used to cover and bind the outside of books

Examples

Cotton bookcloth
Restoring old books sometimes requires replacing the original bookcloth to maintain their appearance.
She carefully selected a navy bookcloth for the custom journal.
The rare edition was bound in a beautiful red bookcloth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bookbinding - the process of physically assembling a book from materials
