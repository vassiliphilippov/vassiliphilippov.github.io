high-concept

adjective
having an easily communicated, innovative premise
4.7
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈkɑnsɛpt / (AmE), / haɪ ˈkɒnsept / (BrE)
(of a film, idea, or other creative work) having a clear, easily communicated premise, often with an original or innovative quality.

Examples

A high-concept film
Her high-concept novel intrigued readers with its unique take on time travel.
The studio invested heavily in the high-concept action film, because its premise was easy to market globally.
They are searching for a high-concept idea that can be summed up in a single sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-concept - lacking a clear or easily summarized premise; relying on subtlety or character rather than a striking idea
