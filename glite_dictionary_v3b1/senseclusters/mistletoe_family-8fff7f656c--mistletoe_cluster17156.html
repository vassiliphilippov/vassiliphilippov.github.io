mistletoe family

noun
a family of shrubs including mistletoes
9.9 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsəlˌtoʊ ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmɪsəlˌtəʊ ˈfæməli / (BrE)
a family of parasitic or hemiparasitic shrubs or small trees, including mistletoes, that attach to host plants to get nutrients

Examples

The mistletoe family plants
Some botanists classify the mistletoe family as a subfamily of Loranthaceae.
Species in the mistletoe family are often seen growing on the branches of trees.
The mistletoe family contains several species of parasitic plants found worldwide.
Ecologists are studying members of the mistletoe family for their unique way of extracting nutrients from other plants.
Some botanists now classify certain species separately from the traditional mistletoe family.
The mistletoe family includes several parasitic plants that attach themselves to host trees.
