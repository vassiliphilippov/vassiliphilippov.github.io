lobscuse

noun
sailors' stew of meat, vegetables, and hardtack
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑbˌskjus / (AmE), / ˈlɒbˌskjuːs / (BrE)
A stew of meat, vegetables, and hardtack, traditionally eaten by sailors.

Examples

A lobscuse stew
During the voyage, the cook prepared lobscuse every Friday to use up leftover meat and vegetables.
She found an old recipe for lobscuse in her grandfather's naval journal.
The sailors eagerly awaited their evening meal of lobscuse after a long day at sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lobscouse - a similar stew made with meat, potatoes, and hardtack eaten by sailors
