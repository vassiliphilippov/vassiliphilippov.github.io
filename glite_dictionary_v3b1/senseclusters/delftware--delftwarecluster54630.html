delftware

noun
Dutch blue pottery
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛlftˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈdɛlftˌweə / (BrE)
glazed earthenware pottery, usually blue and white, originally produced in Delft, Netherlands

Examples

Blue and white delftware
The antique shop had a beautiful collection of delftware plates and vases.
She inherited a set of delftware from her grandmother, who loved Dutch pottery.
Many tourists buy delftware as a souvenir when visiting the Netherlands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faience - glazed earthenware pottery, especially from France or Italy
  • antonym: porcelain - a hard, white, translucent ceramic material
