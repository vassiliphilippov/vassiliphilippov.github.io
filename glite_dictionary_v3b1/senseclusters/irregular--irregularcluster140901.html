irregular

adjective
unconventional or independent; nonconforming
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
not following a regular pattern, standard, or rule; unusual or not normal

Examples

Irregular behavior
He was known for his irregular opinions, often challenging the status quo.
Her irregular approach to business set her apart from her peers.
The group's irregular methods sometimes led to unexpected successes.
Children displaying irregular behavior at school may need extra attention.
His irregular sleeping habits made it hard for him to keep a routine.
The scientist noticed an irregular pattern in the data during the experiment.
An irregular heartbeat can be a sign of a medical condition.
Several staff members were accused of irregular conduct during the hiring process.
The company was fined for its irregular accounting procedures.
