spontaneous generation

noun
disproved theory that life arises from nonliving matter
Pronunciation: / spɑnˈteɪniəs ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / spɒnˈteɪnɪəs ˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the disproved scientific theory that living organisms can arise from nonliving matter without external influence

Examples

Spontaneous generation theory
Louis Pasteur's experiments provided evidence against spontaneous generation.
Many scientists in the 17th century believed in spontaneous generation as an explanation for the appearance of maggots in rotting meat.
The concept of spontaneous generation was an important step in the development of biology, despite later being disproven.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abiogenesis - the origin of living organisms from nonliving matter; often contrasted with biogenesis
  • antonym: biogenesis - the principle that life arises from preexisting life, not from nonliving matter
