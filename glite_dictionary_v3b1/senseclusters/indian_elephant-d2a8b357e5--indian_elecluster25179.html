Indian elephant

noun
subspecies of Asian elephant native to India
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈɛlɪfənt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈɛlɪfənt / (BrE)
A subspecies of Asian elephant native to the Indian subcontinent, characterized by smaller ears and tusks found mainly in males.

Examples

The Indian elephant
Efforts to conserve the Indian elephant are crucial due to habitat loss and poaching.
Many Indian elephants live in protected reserves across India and Southeast Asia.
The Indian elephant is distinguished from its African cousin by its smaller ears.
