inalienability

noun
quality of not being transferable or surrenderable
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌeɪliənəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌeɪlɪənəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unable to be taken away, surrendered, or transferred to another.

Examples

The inalienability of rights
Many philosophers argue for the inalienability of certain personal freedoms.
The constitution guarantees the inalienability of fundamental human rights for all citizens.
The treaty respects the inalienability of the country's territory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inalienable - not able to be taken away or given away by the possessor
