tyrosine

noun
an amino acid found in proteins
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ.rəˌsin / (AmE), / ˈtɪə.rəˌsiːn / (BrE)
an amino acid found in most proteins, serving as a precursor to several hormones and important in metabolism

Examples

Tyrosine amino acid
A deficiency of tyrosine can affect the production of important hormones like dopamine and adrenaline.
Many protein-rich foods, such as cheese and eggs, contain significant amounts of tyrosine.
Phenylalanine is converted into tyrosine in the human body through an enzymatic process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phenylalanine - an amino acid that is a precursor to tyrosine in the body
