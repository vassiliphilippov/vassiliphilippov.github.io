livelong

adjective
entire duration; all day/night (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪvˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪvlɒŋ / (BrE)
(Archaic) Constituting the entire duration; lasting the whole period of time, especially used with 'day' or 'night.'

Examples

The livelong day
He spent the livelong night worrying about the exam results.
She waited for him all the livelong day, but he never arrived.
The children played outside through the livelong summer afternoon.
