autocoid

noun
locally acting body secretion
Pronunciation: / ˈɔtəˌkɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəkɔɪd / (BrE)
a physiologically active substance produced within the body, acting locally and often of uncertain classification

Examples

An autocoid substance
Histamine serves as an autocoid, exerting effects near its site of release.
Researchers are still discovering new types of autocoids that regulate local physiological functions.
Some hormones and neurotransmitters act as autocoids within certain tissues.
