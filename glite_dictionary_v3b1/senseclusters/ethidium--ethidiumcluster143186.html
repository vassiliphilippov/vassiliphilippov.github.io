ethidium

noun
DNA staining compound
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈθɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪdiəm / (BrE)
a chemical compound used as a fluorescent stain to visualize DNA in laboratory research

Examples

Ethidium fluorescent stain
Scientists added ethidium to the gel to make the DNA bands visible under UV light.
Wearing gloves is important when handling ethidium in the laboratory.
The research team used ethidium to confirm the presence of genetic material in their samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethidium bromide - a common fluorescent dye for staining DNA in gels
