stammer

noun
speech disorder with hesitations/repetitions
Pronunciation: / ˈstæmər / (AmE), / ˈstamə / (BrE)
A speech disorder characterized by involuntary pauses, repetitions, or prolongations of sounds, syllables, or words.

Examples

A stammer problem
Because of his stammer, he often felt anxious about speaking in public.
Her stammer became less noticeable as she practiced daily exercises.
The speech therapist helped children cope with their stammer and gain confidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stutter - a similar speech disorder involving involuntary repetition or prolongation of sounds
