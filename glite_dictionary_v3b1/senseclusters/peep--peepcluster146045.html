peep

noun
a young bird's weak cry
1.0 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / pip / (AmE), / piÀêp / (BrE)
a short, soft, high-pitched or faint sound, especially made by a young bird

Examples

A chick's peep
From the nest, the mother heard the constant peep of her hungry offspring.
Not a single peep was heard after the ducklings fell asleep.
The tiny chick let out a soft peep as it hatched from its egg.
He listened through the wall but didn't hear a single peep.
Not a peep came from the noisy classroom after the teacher entered.
The startled bird made a tiny peep before flying away.

Synonyms

  • cheep
    4.0 0.15/m
    cheep is a short, weak, high-pitched cry of young birds, while peep is a softer, fainter young-bird sound.
  • tweet
    2.5 0.55/m
    tweet is a light, high chirp often from small birds, while peep is a very soft, faint sound from baby birds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirp - a short, sharp sound made by small birds or insects
