come in for

verb
receive (criticism, praise, etc.)
3.6
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɪn fər / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn fə / (BrE)
to receive or be subjected to something, especially criticism or blame

Examples

To come in for criticism
After the incident, the coach came in for a lot of praise for his leadership.
The company came in for some harsh criticism after the report was published.
The proposal comes in for frequent debate at city council meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: escape - avoid being subjected to something
  • synonym: receive - be given or presented with something
