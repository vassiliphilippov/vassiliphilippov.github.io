Rommany

noun
member of Romany ethnic group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑː.mə.ni / (AmE), / ˈrɒ.mə.ni / (BrE)
A member of a traditionally itinerant ethnic group originally from northern India, now living worldwide, especially in Europe, North Africa, and North America, known for their own language and cultural customs.

Examples

A Rommany family
Many Rommany families continue to practice traditional crafts and trades.
She learned several phrases in Rommany during her time traveling across Eastern Europe.
The Rommany have a rich tradition of music and storytelling that has influenced European culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gypsy - older term for a member of the Romany people (now often considered offensive)
  • synonym: Romani - the Indo-Aryan language spoken by the Rommany people
