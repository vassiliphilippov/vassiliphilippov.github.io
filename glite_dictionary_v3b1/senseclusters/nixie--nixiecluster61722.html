nixie

noun
water spirit in folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪksi / (AmE), / ˈnɪksi / (BrE)
a water spirit or nymph in Germanic and Scandinavian folklore, often said to inhabit rivers or lakes

Examples

Lake nixie
The villagers believed a nixie lived in the lake and guarded its treasures.
In the old tale, a nixie would sing to lure people into the water.
Artists often depict the nixie as a beautiful woman with long flowing hair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mermaid - a mythical sea creature with the upper body of a woman and the tail of a fish
  • synonym: nymph - a mythological spirit of nature, often depicted as a beautiful maiden
