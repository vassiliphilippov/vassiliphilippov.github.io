dint

noun
small indentation
Pronunciation: / dɪnt / (AmE), / dɪnt / (BrE)
a small hollow or indentation made by a blow or pressure

Examples

A dint in metal
The metal sheet had a noticeable dint after being struck by a hammer.
She ran her fingers over the dint in the wooden table.
A small dint appeared on the wall where the chair had hit it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bump - a raised area on a surface, often from a blow
  • synonym: dent - a slight hollow in a surface made by a blow or pressure
