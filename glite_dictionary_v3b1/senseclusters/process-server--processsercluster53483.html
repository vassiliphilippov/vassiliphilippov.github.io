process-server

noun
person delivering legal documents
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑsɛs ˌsɜrvər / (AmE), / ˈprəʊsɛs ˌsɜːvə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to personally deliver legal documents such as writs or subpoenas to a defendant in a court case

Examples

A process-server delivers papers
As a process-server, she must ensure that all legal documents are delivered according to the law.
It's often difficult for a process-server to locate defendants who are trying to avoid being served.
The process-server arrived at the house early in the morning to deliver the court papers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bailiff - an officer of the court who may serve legal documents
