contemp

noun
informal contemporary
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛmp / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmp / (BrE)
an informal abbreviation for a person who is the same age or lived at the same time as another

Examples

A contemp of Picasso
She was a contemp of the author and often attended the same literary events.
Many of his contemps admired his innovative approach to painting.
He enjoyed discussing politics with his contemps from university.
