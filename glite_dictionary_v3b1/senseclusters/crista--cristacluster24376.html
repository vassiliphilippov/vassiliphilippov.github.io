crista

noun
inner ear sensory ridge
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstə / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstə / (BrE)
a ridge of sensory hair cells and supporting tissue in the semicircular canal of the inner ear that detects rotational movement

Examples

Ear crista
The doctor explained that the crista helps us sense when we turn our heads quickly.
Damage to the crista can result in problems with balance and dizziness.
Researchers studied how the crista responds to different types of head movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crista ampullaris - the specific sensory ridge in the ampulla of the semicircular canal
  • antonym: macula - a sensory patch in the inner ear that detects linear acceleration
