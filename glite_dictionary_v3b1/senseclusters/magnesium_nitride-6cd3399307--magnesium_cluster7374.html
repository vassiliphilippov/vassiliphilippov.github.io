magnesium nitride

noun
a compound of magnesium and nitrogen
7.6
Pronunciation: / mægˌniʒiəm ˈnaɪtraɪd / (AmE), / mægˌniːziəm ˈnɪtraɪd / (BrE)
a yellow-green chemical compound consisting of magnesium and nitrogen, with the formula Mg3N2

Examples

Magnesium nitride compound
Magnesium nitride is formed when magnesium metal reacts with nitrogen gas at high temperatures.
The production of magnesium nitride is important in the preparation of other magnesium compounds.
When water is added to magnesium nitride, it decomposes to release ammonia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnesium oxide - a white powder formed when magnesium burns in air
