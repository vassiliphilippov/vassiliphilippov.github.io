ebb out

verb
recede or flow back, especially of water
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɛb ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˌɛb ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to flow back or recede, especially of water such as tides; to gradually diminish or lessen

Examples

Tides ebb out
As the storm passed, the floodwaters gradually ebbed out from the village.
Tensions in the room slowly ebbed out as the discussion shifted to lighter topics.
The tide began to ebb out after noon, revealing hidden sandbanks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flood in - to come in or rise in again, especially of tides
  • synonym: flow back - to return or move in the reverse direction, especially of water
  • synonym: recede - to move back or away; withdraw
