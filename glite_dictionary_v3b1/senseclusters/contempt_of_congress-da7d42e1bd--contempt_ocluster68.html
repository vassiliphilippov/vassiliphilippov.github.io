contempt of Congress

noun
offense of defying U.S. Congress
2.7
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛmpt əv ˈkɑŋɡrəs / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpt əv ˈkɒŋɡrəs / (BrE)
The offense of obstructing or defying the authority, procedures, or orders of the United States Congress.

Examples

Contempt of Congress charge
He was cited for contempt of Congress after failing to comply with the subpoena.
Penalties for contempt of Congress can include fines or imprisonment.
The committee charged the witness with contempt of Congress for refusing to answer their questions.
