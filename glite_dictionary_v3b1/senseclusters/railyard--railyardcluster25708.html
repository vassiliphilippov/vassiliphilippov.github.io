railyard

noun
area with railway tracks for storage/maintenance
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌjɑrd / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌjɑːd / (BrE)
An area with a network of railway tracks and sidings used for storing, sorting, and maintaining railroad cars and engines.

Examples

The railyard tracks
Engineers performed maintenance on the locomotives in the railyard every weekend.
He spent his childhood exploring the abandoned railyard near his house.
The freight cars were lined up in the railyard, waiting to be assembled into trains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: railway station - place where trains stop for passengers or goods
