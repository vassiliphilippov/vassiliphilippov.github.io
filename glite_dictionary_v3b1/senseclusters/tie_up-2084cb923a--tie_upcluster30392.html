tie up

verb
secure a boat or ship at a dock
2.4
Pronunciation: / taɪ ʌp / (AmE), / taɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to fasten or secure something (such as a boat or object) with rope, string, or similar material so it cannot move

Examples

Tie up a boat
After a long day at sea, they tied up their yacht and went ashore.
Can you help me tie up the dinghy so it doesn't drift away?
We decided to tie up the boat at the marina before sunset.
Could you help me tie up these loose wires so no one trips over them?
Please tie up those boxes with string before moving them.
The boat was tied up at the dock overnight for safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bind - to tie or fasten something tightly
  • synonym: moor - to fasten a boat or ship with cables or anchors
  • antonym: untie - to undo or release something that has been tied
