persecute

verb
treat someone cruelly or unfairly, often for their beliefs
1.3 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpɜːr.sɪ.kjuːt/ /; / /ˈpɜːr.sə.kjuːt/ (AmE), /ˈpɜː.sɪ.kjuːt/ /; / /ˈpɜː.sə.kjuːt/ (BrE)
to treat someone cruelly or unfairly over a period of time, especially because of their race, religion, or political beliefs

Examples

To persecute a group
Activists argue that refugees should be protected, not persecuted for seeking safety.
He felt persecuted by the authorities due to his outspoken opinions.
Throughout history, many minority groups have been persecuted for their religious beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by the unjust exercise of authority
  • antonym: support - to give assistance to; opposite of persecute in context of treatment
