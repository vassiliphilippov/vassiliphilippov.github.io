conciliatory

adjective
intended to pacify or reconcile
5.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈsɪl.iː.ə.tɔːr.iː/ /; / /kən.ˈsɪl.jə.tɔːr.iː/ (AmE), /kən.ˈsɪl.ɪə.tɔː.riː/ /; / /kən.ˈsɪl.iə.tɔː.riː/ (BrE)
intended or likely to calm, pacify, or make peace; showing a willingness to ease tension or make concessions

Examples

Conciliatory gestures
His conciliatory gestures eased the tension between the two sides.
She adopted a conciliatory tone to help resolve the disagreement.
The manager wrote a conciliatory letter to reassure the upset customer.
Despite the criticism, she responded in a conciliatory manner, seeking to diffuse tension.
During the heated debate, a conciliatory voice urged both sides to find common ground.
His conciliatory gesture helped repair the damaged relationship between the two companies.

  • synonym: compromising - willing to make concessions or yield
  • antonym: provocative - causing anger or another strong reaction, especially deliberately
