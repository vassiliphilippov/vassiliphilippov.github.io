hydraulic system

noun
system using liquid pressure for power
2.7
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑːlɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A system or mechanism that uses the pressure of a liquid, usually oil or water, to transmit force and operate machinery.

Examples

The hydraulic system pressure
Engineers inspected the hydraulic system to ensure there were no leaks or pressure drops.
Modern airplanes rely on a sophisticated hydraulic system for controlling the landing gear and brakes.
The car's hydraulic system allows it to lift heavy loads with minimal effort.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pneumatic system - system that uses compressed air or gas to transmit power
