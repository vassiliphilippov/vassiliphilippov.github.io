bottom-feeding

adjective
feeding at the bottom of water
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑtəm ˈfidɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a fish or other aquatic animal) feeding on or near the bottom of a body of water.

Examples

Bottom-feeding fish
Catfish are well known as bottom-feeding species in many freshwater ecosystems.
Some bottom-feeding organisms can tolerate very low oxygen levels in the water.
The lake contains several bottom-feeding fish that help clean up organic matter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: surface-feeding - feeding at or near the surface of water
