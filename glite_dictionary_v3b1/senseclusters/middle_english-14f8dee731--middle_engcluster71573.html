Middle English

noun
English from about 1100 to 1450
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪdəl ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌmɪdəl ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ / (BrE)
the form of the English language spoken and written between roughly 1100 and 1450

Examples

Middle English literature
Chaucer's works are famous examples of literature written in Middle English.
Pronunciation rules in Middle English differ significantly from those in Modern English.
Scholars study Middle English texts to learn about language evolution.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Old English - the earliest form of the English language, spoken from c.450 to c.1100
