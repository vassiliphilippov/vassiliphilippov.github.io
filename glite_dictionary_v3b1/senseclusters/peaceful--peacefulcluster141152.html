peaceful

adjective
nonviolent, not using force
1.0 7.88/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpisf…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàpiÀêsf…ôl / (BrE)
Not involving violence or aggression, especially when responding to injustice; advocating or practicing nonviolent resistance.

Examples

Peaceful protest
His response to unfair treatment was always peaceful, believing that violence would only worsen the situation.
Nations around the world praised the leader for his peaceful approach to conflict resolution.
The students staged a peaceful protest in the city square, carrying banners but refusing to shout or confront police.

Synonyms

  • nonviolent
    4.0 3.60/m
    nonviolent describes actions that do not use force or harm, often in protest, while peaceful can mean calm or free from violence.
  • noncombative
    5.3 0.02/m
    noncombative describes people who avoid fighting or arguing and do not act aggressively, while peaceful includes calm situations and nonviolence.
  • peaceable
    4.1 0.82/m
    peaceable describes a calm, conflict-free attitude or place that does not start fights, while peaceful often highlights quiet and nonviolence.
  • peace-loving
    3.4 0.68/m
    peace-loving describes people who prefer peace and avoid fights in life, while peaceful can mean calm or nonviolent action.
  • pacifistic
    7.8 0.09/m
    pacifistic expresses a firm belief against all war and violence, often as an ideology, while peaceful can be practical calm or nonviolent.
