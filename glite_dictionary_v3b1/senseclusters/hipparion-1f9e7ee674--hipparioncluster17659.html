Hipparion

noun
extinct three-toed horse
Pronunciation: / hɪˈpɛriən / (AmE), / hɪˈpæriən / (BrE)
an extinct genus of three-toed horses that lived from the Miocene to the Pleistocene epochs

Examples

Fossil Hipparion
The paleontologist carefully examined the fossilized skull of a Hipparion found in the ancient riverbed.
Scientists believe that Hipparion roamed the grasslands of Eurasia millions of years ago.
A display at the natural history museum features a reconstructed skeleton of Hipparion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Equus - the genus that includes modern horses, zebras, and donkeys
