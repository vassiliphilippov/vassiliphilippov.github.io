Jerome

proper noun
Christian saint; translator of the Bible into Latin
8.4
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈroʊm / (AmE), / dʒəˈrəʊm / (BrE)
A Christian saint and Doctor of the Church (c. 347–420), best known for translating the Bible into Latin (the Vulgate).

Examples

Jerome the saint
Many scholars have studied the influence of Jerome's biblical translations on Western Christianity.
Saint Jerome is often depicted in Christian art as a scholar or a hermit.
The Vulgate, translated by Jerome, became the authoritative Latin version of the Bible for centuries.
