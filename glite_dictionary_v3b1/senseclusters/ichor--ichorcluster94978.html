ichor

noun
mythical fluid in gods' veins
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪkər / (AmE), / ˈaɪkɔː / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) an ethereal fluid that flows in the veins of the gods, instead of blood.

Examples

Gods' ichor
According to legend, ichor spilled from the wounded god and gave life to the earth.
In ancient tales, mere mortals could not bear the sight or smell of ichor.
The poet described the hero's blood as turning to ichor in the presence of the divine.
