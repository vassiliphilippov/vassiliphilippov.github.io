coplanar

adjective
lying in the same plane (geometry)
Pronunciation: / koʊˈpleɪnər / (AmE), / kəʊˈpleɪnə / (BrE)
(of points, lines, or objects) lying or situated in the same plane.

Examples

Coplanar points
If two lines are coplanar, they either intersect or are parallel.
The four points are coplanar, as they all lie in the same flat surface.
Three non-coplanar vectors define a three-dimensional space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collinear - lying on the same straight line
