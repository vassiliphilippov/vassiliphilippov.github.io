rover

noun
robotic planetary vehicle
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.və/ (BrE)
a robotic vehicle designed to move across and study the surface of a planet or other celestial body, often carrying scientific instruments and operating with some autonomy.

Examples

Mars rover
NASA's Mars rover transmitted the first high-resolution images of the Martian terrain.
Engineers tested the new rover in a desert to simulate Martian dust and slopes.
The research team deployed an autonomous rover to collect soil samples from the crater rim.

Related Terms

  • synonym: robot - a machine capable of carrying out complex actions automatically, especially one programmable by a computer
  • synonym: space probe - an unmanned spacecraft sent into space to collect data and transmit it back to Earth
