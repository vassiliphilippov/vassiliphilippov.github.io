bite

noun
sharp, pungent taste or smell
4.8
Pronunciation: / baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
a sharp, stinging, or strong quality (in taste, smell, cold, or wit)

Examples

Chili bite
Chili peppers give the salsa its distinctive bite.
The salad dressing had a pleasant bite from the added horseradish.
There is a certain bite to the smell of freshly chopped onions.
Although his review was critical, it lacked the bite of true satire.
Her remarks were delivered with surprising bite for such a pleasant occasion.
The satire had a certain bite that made the audience uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blandness - lacking strong features or characteristics, the opposite of sharpness in speech or writing
  • antonym: mildness - the quality of being gentle or not sharp in taste or smell
  • synonym: pungency - the quality of having a strong, sharp taste or smell
