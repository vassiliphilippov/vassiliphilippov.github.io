notecase

noun
small case for money and papers
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊt.keɪs / (AmE), / ˈnəʊt.keɪs / (BrE)
A small, flat case for carrying paper money and personal papers, typically kept in a pocket.

Examples

A notecase for money
He pulled out his notecase to pay the bill at the restaurant.
Her father's old leather notecase was worn but still functional.
The thief snatched the man's notecase and disappeared into the crowd.
