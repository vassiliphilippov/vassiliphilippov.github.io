rambling

noun
disorganized speech or writing
Pronunciation: / ˈræmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræmblɪŋ / (BrE)
speech or writing that is disorganized, lengthy, and difficult to follow

Examples

A rambling on stage
His speech turned into a series of ramblings that confused the audience.
The article was filled with rambling and lacked a clear point.
She apologized for her ramblings and tried to summarize her main idea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - confused or meaningless talk
  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • synonym: rant - a long, angry, and confused speech
