permaculture

noun
sustainable farming method
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrməkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːməkʌltʃə / (BrE)
an approach to designing sustainable systems by working with natural ecosystems

Examples

Permaculture design
The community garden was designed using permaculture principles to conserve water and soil.
Many farmers are turning to permaculture to create more resilient food systems.
Workshops on permaculture teach people how to grow food in harmony with nature.
The farm adopted permaculture principles to reduce waste and improve soil health.
Many urban gardens now use permaculture to create productive and sustainable green spaces.
She attended a workshop on permaculture to learn about eco-friendly land management.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monoculture - the cultivation of a single crop in a given area
  • synonym: sustainable agriculture - farming that meets current needs without harming future resources
