finfish

noun
fish with fins
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪnˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɪnˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish with fins (not a shellfish or other aquatic animal)

Examples

A finfish species
Salmon is a popular type of finfish eaten around the world.
Unlike shellfish, finfish have a backbone and swim using their fins.
Many rivers are home to several species of finfish important for local fisheries.
Salmon is a popular type of finfish served in many restaurants around the world.
Unlike shellfish, finfish have a backbone and are often caught in large numbers.
The market offers a wide variety of fresh finfish every morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fish - a cold-blooded aquatic animal with gills and fins
  • antonym: shellfish - an aquatic animal with a shell, such as a crab or clam
