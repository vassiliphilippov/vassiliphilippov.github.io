internegative

noun
film negative for prints
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈnɛɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnɛɡətɪv / (BrE)
a photographic film negative made from an original negative, used to produce release prints in motion picture production

Examples

A internegative print
The film laboratory produced an internegative to ensure the final prints would have consistent quality.
After editing the original footage, the team created an internegative for mass production of the movie.
Preserving the internegative is important for restoring classic films in the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interpositive - a positive film made from a negative, used in film production
  • synonym: negative - a photographic image in which the lightest areas appear darkest
