rubbing

noun
impression made by rubbing paper over an object
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
a picture or impression made by placing paper over an object and rubbing it with something like charcoal or crayon to reproduce the object's texture or design

Examples

A rubbing of a gravestone
He made a rubbing of the ancient gravestone to preserve its inscription.
She taught students how to create a rubbing using charcoal and paper.
The museum displayed rubbings of medieval brass memorials.
