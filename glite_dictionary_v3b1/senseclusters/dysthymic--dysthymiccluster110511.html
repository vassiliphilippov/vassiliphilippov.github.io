dysthymic

adjective
chronic mild depression
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈθaɪmɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈθaɪmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by a chronic, mild form of depression

Examples

Dysthymic symptoms
The doctor described her mood as dysthymic after several months of low energy and sadness.
People with a dysthymic temperament may struggle with motivation over long periods.
A dysthymic disorder can affect daily life but is less severe than major depression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depressed - feeling or showing sadness or unhappiness
  • antonym: euthymic - having a normal, stable mood
