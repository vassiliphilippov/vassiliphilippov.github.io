only

adverb
as a result; in the final outcome
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊnli / (AmE), / ˈəʊnli / (BrE)
no more than, nothing or nobody except the one or ones mentioned; used to limit to one or none

Examples

Only one option

She studied hard, only to find the exam was postponed.
Telling him the truth will only make things worse.
He was only a child when he moved to the city.
I'm only asking you to listen for a moment.
This car only costs a few hundred dollars.
He worked all night only to realize he had misunderstood the instructions.
She traveled across the city only to find the store was closed.
The team fought hard only to be defeated in the last minute.
