flesh-eating

adjective
causing tissue destruction (necrotizing)
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈflɛʃ ˌɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛʃ ˌiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
(in medicine) (of an infection, bacterium, or other pathogen) causing rapid destruction or death of body tissue (necrosis).

Examples

Flesh-eating infection
Doctors identified a flesh-eating infection that required immediate surgical removal of damaged tissue.
The emergency team raced to treat a patient with a rapidly progressing flesh-eating bacterial infection.
Public health warnings described several cases of flesh-eating bacteria linked to contaminated seawater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: necrotizing - causing death of cells or tissue; used to describe rapidly destructive infections (e.g., necrotizing fasciitis).
