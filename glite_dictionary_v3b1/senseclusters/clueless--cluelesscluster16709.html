clueless

adjective
completely uninformed or unaware
4.3 5.33/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkluÀêl…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàkluÀêl…™s / (BrE)
having no knowledge, understanding, or ability regarding a particular subject or situation; completely uninformed or unaware.

Examples

Completely clueless
Despite several hints, he remained clueless about the surprise party.
Many new students are clueless about the campus layout during their first week.
She felt completely clueless when it came to fixing her car.

Synonyms

  • uninformed
    3.7 3.41/m
    uninformed is lacking facts or knowledge about a topic, while clueless suggests a complete lack of understanding or practical ability.
  • ignorant
    3.7 23/m
    ignorant is lacking knowledge or education and can sound insulting, while clueless is more informal and often means baffled.
  • unaware
    2.9 15/m
    unaware is not noticing or knowing a fact or event, while clueless means having no understanding or skill about a situation.
  • unfamiliar with
    4.1 2.35/m
    unfamiliar with is used to say someone has little knowledge or experience of something, while clueless means totally without understanding.
  • unacquainted
    6.5 0.07/m
    unacquainted is not familiar with a person or topic in a neutral, formal way, while clueless is informal and shows a total lack of know-how.
  • uninitiated
    8.0 0.83/m
    uninitiated is someone without experience in a field and often used for beginners, while clueless feels more casual and more complete.

Related Terms

  • antonym: informed - having or showing knowledge of a particular subject or situation
