Battle of Rossbach

noun
decisive Seven Years' War battle (1757)
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ˈrɒsbɑːk / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˈrɒsbæk / (BrE)
a decisive battle in the Seven Years' War (1757) where Prussian forces under Frederick the Great defeated French and Austrian armies

Examples

The Battle of Rossbach victory
Historians often discuss the tactics used at the Battle of Rossbach to illustrate Frederick the Great's military genius.
Many European powers underestimated Prussia before the Battle of Rossbach demonstrated its strength.
The Battle of Rossbach marked a turning point for Prussia in the Seven Years' War.
