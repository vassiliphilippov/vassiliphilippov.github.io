gravure

noun
intaglio print made by gravure process
7.8 8.0
Pronunciation: /grə.ˈvʊr/ /; / /ˈgræ.vər/ (AmE), /grə.ˈvʊə/ /; / /ˈgræ.və/ (BrE)
a printing process where an image is engraved or etched onto a plate, which is then used to make high-quality prints; also, the print or plate made by this process

Gravure printing process
Collectors prize early twentieth-century gravure prints for their rich detail and tonal range.

Modern packaging often employs gravure for high-quality color printing.

Modern gravure allows for high-speed, high-volume printing of packaging materials.
The magazine was printed using gravure to achieve detailed and rich images.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engraving - an image or design cut into a hard surface, also a method of printing
  • synonym: intaglio - a printing process in which the image is incised into a surface
  • antonym: relief printing - a printing process in which the printing surface is raised above the nonprinting area
