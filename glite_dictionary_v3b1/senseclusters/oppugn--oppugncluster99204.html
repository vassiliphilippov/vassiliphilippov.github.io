oppugn

verb
to dispute or question the validity of
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpjun / (AmE), / …ôÀàpjuÀên / (BrE)
to challenge or call into question the truth, validity, or propriety of something.

Examples

To oppugn a claim
Several critics have oppugned the findings of the official report.
Some historians oppugn the traditional accounts of the event, offering alternative explanations.
The defense attorney sought to oppugn the witness's credibility during cross-examination.

Synonyms

  • impugn
    8.1 1.18/m
    impugn attacks someone's honesty or motive in formal language, while oppugn means to challenge the truth, validity, or appropriateness.
  • call into question
    4.5 3.23/m
    call into question is used to show doubt about an idea in discussion, while oppugn means to formally challenge its truth or appropriateness.
  • question
    3.5 43/m
    question expresses doubt or asks for information in everyday speech, while oppugn means to formally challenge truth, validity, or appropriateness.
