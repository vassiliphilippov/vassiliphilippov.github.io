deluge

noun
a heavy rain or flood
6.3 0.84/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…õl.juÀêd í /; / Ààd…õl.jud í / (AmE), / Ààd…õl.juÀêd í / (BrE)
a severe flood or a heavy, drenching rain

Examples

A deluge of rain
A sudden deluge forced everyone to seek shelter under the bridge.
After a week of sunshine, a deluge washed out the picnic plans.
The city was unprepared for the deluge that flooded the streets overnight.
After days of heavy rain, the river burst its banks, causing a deluge that stranded several villages.
The ancient text describes a great deluge that swept across the entire region.
When the dam failed, a sudden deluge washed away the farmland near the riverbanks.

Synonyms

  • flood
    1.0 18/m
    a flood is a large amount of water covering land, while a deluge often refers to very heavy rain and severe flooding.
  • flooding
    2.1 8.83/m
    flooding is the process of water covering dry land, while a deluge often means both heavy rain and a severe flood.
  • inundation
    7.7 0.29/m
    an inundation is the formal term for land covered by water, while a deluge also suggests heavy, drenching rain.
  • cloudburst
    6.3 0.17/m
    a cloudburst is a sudden, very heavy short rain, while a deluge can mean heavy rain or a large, lasting flood.
  • downpour
    2.7 1.90/m
    a downpour is a heavy, sudden fall of rain, while a deluge can mean that rain or a much larger flood.
