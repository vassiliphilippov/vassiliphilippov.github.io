offseason

noun
period of low demand
3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːf.siː.zən/ /; / /ˈɔːf.siː.zən/ (AmE), /ˈɒf.siː.zən/ (BrE)
the period of the year when regular activity, business, or sports is reduced or paused

Examples

The offseason months
Many resorts close some facilities during the offseason due to the drop in visitors.
Tickets are much cheaper in the offseason when fewer people travel.
Local businesses often use the offseason to renovate or make improvements.
Many athletes use the offseason to recover from injuries and focus on training.
During the offseason, stadiums are often used for concerts and other events.
Some players take vacations with their families in the offseason.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in-season - the period when regular sports competitions are taking place
  • synonym: low season - the time of year when business or activity is at its lowest
  • antonym: peak season - the period when demand or activity is at its highest
  • degree higher: postseason - the period after the regular sports season, often involving playoffs
  • degree lower: preseason - the period before the regular sports season begins
