elicitor

noun
stimulus for response
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪsɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪsɪtə / (BrE)
a stimulus or prompt used to provoke a response, especially in research or therapy settings

Examples

Picture elicitor
The therapist used a series of images as elicitors to help the patient express their feelings.
In the experiment, each sound acted as an elicitor to trigger specific reactions from participants.
Researchers selected questions carefully to serve as elicitors during the interview process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prompt - a cue or signal to encourage a response
  • synonym: stimulus - something that causes a response or reaction
