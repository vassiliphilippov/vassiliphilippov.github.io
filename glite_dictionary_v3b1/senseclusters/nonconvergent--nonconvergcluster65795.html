nonconvergent

adjective
not meeting or crossing (in geometry)
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈvɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈvɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
(of lines, planes, or surfaces) never meeting or crossing; not converging at any point.

Examples

Nonconvergent lines
Engineers designed the beams as nonconvergent to ensure the structure remained stable.
In mathematics, nonconvergent surfaces never touch or cross each other.
The two nonconvergent lines extend side by side forever without intersecting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convergent - tending to meet or come together at a point (math/geometry)
