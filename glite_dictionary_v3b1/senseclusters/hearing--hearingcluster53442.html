hearing

noun
official opportunity to present one's case
5.7 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈhiːr.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈhɪr.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈhɪə.rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈhiː.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
an official meeting (such as in a court or committee) where evidence or viewpoints are presented and considered

Examples

Court hearing
She requested a hearing before the disciplinary committee to explain her actions.
The court set a hearing for next Monday to review the evidence.
Without a proper hearing, the accused was denied the chance to defend himself.
After the initial hearing, the case was dismissed due to lack of proof.
Lawyers prepared their arguments for the upcoming hearing in court.
The judge scheduled a hearing to review the new evidence.
Public hearings are often required before new regulations are adopted.
She gave her testimony during the hearing at the courthouse.
The committee scheduled a hearing to gather information from witnesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appeal - legal process of requesting a higher court to review a decision
  • synonym: inquiry - an official investigation or hearing
  • synonym: investigation - the process of inquiring into a matter
  • synonym: trial - a formal examination of evidence in court
