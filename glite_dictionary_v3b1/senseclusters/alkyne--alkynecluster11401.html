alkyne

noun
hydrocarbon with a triple bond
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈælˌkaɪn / (AmE), / ˈal.kʌɪn / (BrE)
a hydrocarbon compound containing at least one carbon–carbon triple bond, belonging to a series with the general formula CnH2n−2

Examples

An alkyne compound
Acetylene is the most well-known alkyne, commonly used in welding.
An alkyne is distinguished from other hydrocarbons by its carbon–carbon triple bond.
Chemists often use reactions involving alkynes to synthesize complex organic molecules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetylene - the simplest alkyne (C2H2)
  • degree lower: alkane - a hydrocarbon with only single bonds
  • degree lower: alkene - a hydrocarbon containing a double bond
