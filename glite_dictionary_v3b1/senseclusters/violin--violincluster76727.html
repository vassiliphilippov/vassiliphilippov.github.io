violin

noun
bowed string musical instrument
3.2
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈoʊlɪn / (AmE), / vaɪəˈlɪn / (BrE)
A bowed string instrument with four strings and a hollow body, played with a bow and producing the highest pitch in the violin family.

Examples

Play the violin
His daughter plays the violin in the school orchestra.
She practiced the violin for two hours every evening to prepare for the concert.
The violin section started the symphony with a gentle melody.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiddle - another term for the violin, especially when used in folk music
