cannulization

noun
insertion of a cannula or tube
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæn.jə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæn.jʊ.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkæn.juː.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of inserting a cannula or tube into a hollow body organ, typically to provide access, administer or remove fluids, or to allow drainage

Examples

Cannulization of veins
Complications may arise during cannulization, particularly in patients with small or fragile veins.
Proper training is essential for safe and effective cannulization in clinical practice.
The surgeon performed cannulization of the vein to administer the medication directly.
Repeated cannulisation may cause irritation or scarring of the vessel wall.
Skilled cannulisation is essential in many emergency medical procedures.
The cannulisation of the vein was performed to begin intravenous therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cannulation - the process of inserting a cannula, especially the spelling in American English
  • synonym: catheterization - the process of inserting a catheter into the body
