archeologist

noun
person who studies ancient cultures through artifacts
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrkɪˈɑlədʒɪst /; / ˌɑːr.kiˈɑː.lə.dʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈɒlədʒɪst /; / ˌɑː.kiˈɒl.ə.dʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies human history and prehistory through the excavation and analysis of artifacts and other physical remains

Examples

An archeologist studies artifacts
As an archeologist, she often traveled to remote sites in search of ancient ruins.
Many archeologists specialize in the study of prehistoric tools and artifacts.
The archeologist carefully brushed dirt away from the newly discovered pottery shard.
After years of study, she became an expert archaeologist specializing in Roman ruins.
The archaeologist carefully brushed away the dirt to reveal an ancient artifact.
The archaeologist gave a fascinating lecture on prehistoric cave paintings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthropologist - a scientist who studies human societies and cultures
  • synonym: archaeologist - alternative spelling: person who studies ancient artifacts and sites
  • synonym: historian - a person who studies or writes about history
  • synonym: paleontologist - a scientist who studies fossils to understand ancient life
