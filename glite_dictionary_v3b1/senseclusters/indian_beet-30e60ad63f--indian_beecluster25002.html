Indian beet

noun
North American perennial with blue flowers
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˌbiːt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˌbiːt / (BrE)
A stout perennial plant native to eastern and central North America, having palmate leaves and showy racemose blue flowers.

Examples

The Indian beet plant
Many gardeners choose the Indian beet for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
The Indian beet is often found alongside streams in its native North American habitat.
The vibrant blue flowers of the Indian beet bloom in late spring across woodland edges.
