hate speech

noun
speech inciting hatred or violence
1.0 4.1
Pronunciation: /heɪt spiːtʃ/ (AmE), /heɪt spiːtʃ/ (BrE)
public speech or expression that promotes hatred, violence, or discrimination against a particular group, often on the basis of race, religion, gender, or other characteristics.

Examples

Hate speech online
His comments were widely denounced as hate speech by advocacy groups.
Several organizations campaign for laws against hate speech to protect vulnerable communities.
The company established clear policies to combat hate speech on its social media platforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free speech - the right to express any opinions without censorship; often contrasted with hate speech
  • synonym: incitement - the act of urging or provoking unlawful behavior; related to stirring up hate
