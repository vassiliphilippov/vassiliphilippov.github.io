brushback

noun
baseball pitch near batter
Pronunciation: / ˈbrʌʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃˌbæk / (BrE)
a pitch in baseball thrown close to a batter to force them away from the plate

Examples

Brushback pitch
The pitcher threw a brushback to intimidate the batter early in the game.
After a close brushback, the batter stepped out of the box to regain his composure.
A well-timed brushback can make hitters think twice about crowding the plate.
