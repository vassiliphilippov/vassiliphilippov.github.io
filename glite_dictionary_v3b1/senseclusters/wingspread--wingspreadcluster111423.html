wingspread

noun
distance between tips of fully extended wings
3.4 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪŋ.spred/ /; / /ˈwɪŋz.spred/ (AmE), /ˈwɪŋ.spred/ /; / /ˈwɪŋz.spred/ (BrE)
the distance from the tip of one wing to the tip of the other when the wings are fully extended (as in birds, insects, or similar structures)

Examples

Large wingspread
Heron species are easily recognized by their large wingspread in flight.
Scientists measured the butterfly's wingspread carefully to identify the species.
The albatross has an impressive wingspread of over three meters.
At the museum, visitors marveled at the ancient pterosaur fossil with its remarkable wingspread.
The eagle’s wingspread can exceed two meters, allowing it to soar for long distances.
When designing the new glider, engineers increased the wingspread to improve lift and efficiency.
