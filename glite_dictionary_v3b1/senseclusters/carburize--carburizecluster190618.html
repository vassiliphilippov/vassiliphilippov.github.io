carburize

verb
infuse metal with carbon
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ërbj…ôÀåra…™z /; / Ààk…ëÀêrbj…ôra…™z / (AmE), / Ààk…ëÀêbj är å…™z /; / Ààk…ëÀêbj ära…™z / (BrE)
to infuse or combine a material, especially metal, with carbon, usually by heating and typically to harden its surface.

Examples

To carburize metal parts
Before assembly, the technician carburized the metal parts in a special furnace.
Engineers often carburize gears to create a hard, wear-resistant surface.
The company decided to carburize the steel components to improve their durability.
Before being used in machine parts, the component is often carburised for added durability.
Engineers carburise the steel to enhance its surface hardness for industrial applications.
The process will carburise the metal, making it more resistant to wear.

Synonyms

  • carbonize
    8.4 0.00/m
    carbonize converts material into carbon through heating or burning, while carburize is a precise heat process to harden a metal's surface.
  • carburet
    9.9 0.13/m
    carburet combines a substance with carbon in chemical treatment, while carburize refers to heating metal to add surface carbon and harden it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carburize - unite metal with carbon (US spelling)
  • antonym: decarburize - to remove carbon from a material, especially metal
