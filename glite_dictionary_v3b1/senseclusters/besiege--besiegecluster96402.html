besiege

verb
surround with armed forces to force surrender
7.8 6.6
Pronunciation: /bɪ.ˈsiːdʒ/ /; / /bə.ˈsiːdʒ/ (AmE), /bə.ˈsiːdʒ/ /; / /bɪ.ˈsiːdʒ/ (BrE)
to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force those inside to surrender

Examples

To besiege a city
During the war, several cities were besieged for months at a time.
The army decided to besiege the fortress until the defenders surrendered.
The rebels are besieging the capital from all sides, cutting off supplies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relieve - to free a place or person from siege, distress, or difficulty
  • synonym: storm - to attack suddenly with great force, especially to capture a fortified place
  • synonym: surround - to encircle or enclose completely
