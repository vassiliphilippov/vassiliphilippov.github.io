hen-peck

verb
to nag or criticize persistently
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛnˌpɛk / (AmE), / ˈhɛnˌpɛk / (BrE)
(of a woman) to criticize or nag (her husband or partner) persistently, especially over trivial matters.

Examples

To hen-peck a husband
He felt completely hen-pecked after years of constant criticism.
She would hen-peck her husband about every small detail in the house.
Some people think it's wrong to hen-peck their partners over minor issues.
