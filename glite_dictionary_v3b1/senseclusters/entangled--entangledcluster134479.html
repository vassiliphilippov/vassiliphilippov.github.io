entangled

adjective
involved in a complicated situation
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæŋɡəld / (BrE)
involved in a complicated or difficult situation, or twisted together in a confusing way

Examples

Entangled in problems
He got entangled in something far more complex than he had expected.
She found herself entangled in the office politics despite her best efforts to avoid them.
The company became entangled in a web of legal disputes.
Negotiations became entangled in a web of legal complications and delays.
She found herself entangled in the wild rose bushes while hiking through the forest.
The wires behind the computer were so entangled that I had to unplug everything to sort them out.
He found himself entangled in legal disputes that dragged on for years.
She became entangled in a web of political intrigue that was hard to escape.
Their finances were entangled after years of poor investments and risky loans.

  • synonym: involved - taking part in an activity or situation, especially one that is complicated or difficult
  • synonym: tangled - twisted together in a disorderly mass
