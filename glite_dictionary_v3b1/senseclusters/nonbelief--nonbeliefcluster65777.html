nonbelief

noun
absence of faith
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.bɪˈliːf / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.bɪˈliːf / (BrE)
the lack or absence of belief, especially in religion, God, or the supernatural

Examples

Religious nonbelief
His nonbelief was a topic of discussion among his religious family members.
Many people are open about their nonbelief in modern society.
She explained her nonbelief as a result of personal reflection and study.
His nonbelief in ghosts made him unafraid to walk through the old house at night.
The debate included people of faith as well as those whose nonbelief shaped their views.
She explained her nonbelief in superstitions during the discussion about luck and fate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atheism - the belief that there is no God or gods
  • antonym: belief - acceptance that something exists or is true, especially without proof
  • synonym: disbelief - the inability or refusal to accept something as true
  • antonym: faith - strong belief or trust in something, especially without proof
