hermitlike

adjective
solitary or reclusive
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜrmɪtˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈhɜːmɪtˌlaɪk / (BrE)
living in a solitary or reclusive way, like a hermit (sometimes for religious or spiritual reasons)

Examples

Hermitlike lifestyle
After moving to the countryside, he adopted a hermitlike lifestyle, rarely seeing his neighbors.
Her hermitlike habits made it difficult for her to make new friends in the city.
The artist became increasingly hermitlike as he focused on his work in isolation.
After the scandal, he adopted a hermitlike life of prayer and fasting in the monastery garden.
Her hermitlike habits made her a mystery to her neighbors, who rarely saw her outside.
The author led a hermitlike existence in the mountains, far from the distractions of city life.

  • antonym: sociable - enjoying the company of others; friendly
