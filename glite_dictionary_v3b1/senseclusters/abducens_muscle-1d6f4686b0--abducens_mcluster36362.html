abducens muscle

noun
muscle for outward eye movement
8.6
Pronunciation: /æb.ˈdjuː.sənz ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /æb.ˈdʒuː.sənz ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /əb.ˈdjuː.sənz ˈmʌs.əl/ (AmE), /æb.ˈdjuː.sənz ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /æb.ˈdʒuː.sənz ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /əb.ˈdjuː.sənz ˈmʌs.əl/ (BrE)
the muscle that controls outward movement of the eye by turning the eyeball away from the midline

Examples

Abducens muscle movement
Damage to the abducens muscle can cause double vision and difficulty moving the eye outward.
The abducens muscle allows the eye to move laterally away from the nose.
The surgeon carefully examined the abducens muscle during the procedure to correct the patient's strabismus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lateral rectus muscle - muscle responsible for outward movement of the eyeball; another name for abducens muscle
  • antonym: medial rectus muscle - muscle that moves the eye toward the nose (opposite action)
