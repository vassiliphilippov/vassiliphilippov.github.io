prescriptivism

noun
doctrine prescribing language rules
9.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /prɪ.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ /; / /prə.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /prɪ.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ /; / /prə.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The doctrine or belief that certain rules or norms should be prescribed and followed, especially in language use.

Examples

Language prescriptivism
Linguists often debate the merits of prescriptivism versus descriptivism in language teaching.
Some teachers favor prescriptivism, insisting on strict grammar rules in the classroom.
The rise of prescriptivism influenced how dictionaries listed standard word usage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptivism - The doctrine that describes how language is actually used, rather than prescribing rules.
