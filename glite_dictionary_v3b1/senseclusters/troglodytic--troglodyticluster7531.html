troglodytic

adjective
cave-dwelling; primitive
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɑːɡləˈdɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrɒɡləˈdɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resembling a person or animal that lives in caves or is reclusive and primitive

Examples

Troglodytic cave homes
The ancient village featured troglodytic homes carved directly into the rocky cliffs.
His troglodytic habits made him avoid social gatherings and live in isolation.
Some animals have developed troglodytic lifestyles, spending most of their lives in underground burrows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cavernous - resembling a cave, large and hollow
  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times; not ancient or primitive
  • synonym: primitive - relating to an early stage of development; simple or crude
