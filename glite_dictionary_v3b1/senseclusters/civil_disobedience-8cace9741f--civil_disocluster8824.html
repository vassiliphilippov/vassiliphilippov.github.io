civil disobedience

noun
peaceful protest by refusing to obey laws
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪvəl ˌdɪsəˈbiːdiəns / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.əl ˌdɪs.əˈbiː.di.əns / (BrE)
the refusal to obey certain laws or government demands, usually as a form of peaceful political protest against perceived injustice

Examples

Civil disobedience protest
Gandhi used civil disobedience as a strategy to resist British rule in India.
Henry David Thoreau's essay on civil disobedience inspired later social movements worldwide.
Many activists engage in civil disobedience to draw attention to laws they believe are unjust.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obedience - compliance with an order or law
  • synonym: protest - an organized public demonstration expressing strong objection to something
