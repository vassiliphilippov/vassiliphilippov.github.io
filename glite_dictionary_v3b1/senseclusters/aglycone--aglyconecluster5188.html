aglycone

noun
non-sugar part of glycoside
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪɡlaɪˌkoʊn / (AmE), / ˈeɪɡlɪˌkəʊn / (BrE)
the non-sugar part of a glycoside compound that remains after the sugar is removed by hydrolysis

Examples

The aglycone part
When the glycoside is hydrolyzed, the aglycone is separated from the sugar component.
Researchers studied the biological activity of the aglycone after removing the sugar group.
The aglycone portion often determines the medicinal properties of the compound.
The aglycone is released when the glycoside undergoes hydrolysis in the laboratory.
Researchers studied the biological activity of the aglycone separated from the plant compound.
After removing the sugar, only the aglycone remained for further chemical analysis.
