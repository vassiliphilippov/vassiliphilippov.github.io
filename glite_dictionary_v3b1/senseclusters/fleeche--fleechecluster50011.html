fleeche

noun
sheep's wool coat
the woolly coat of a sheep, or a sheepskin or mass of wool (archaic)

Examples

A fleeche of wool
The shepherd carried a fresh fleeche from the sheared sheep to the market.
In old texts, a fleeche was often used to make warm clothing for winter.
The merchant displayed a thick fleeche at the village fair, attracting many buyers.
