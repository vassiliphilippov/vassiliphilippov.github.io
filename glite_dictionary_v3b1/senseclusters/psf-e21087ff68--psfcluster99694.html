PSF

noun
imaging system response
Pronunciation: / pi ɛs ɛf / (AmE), / piː ɛs ɛf / (BrE)
a function that describes how a single point of light is spread or blurred by an imaging system

Examples

Camera PSF function
The quality of the camera lens can be evaluated by analyzing its PSF.
Astronomers use the PSF to correct images of stars that appear blurred.
A narrow PSF indicates that the imaging system produces sharp images.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - a lack of sharpness in an image
  • synonym: point spread function - the full term for PSF; describes how a point source is imaged
