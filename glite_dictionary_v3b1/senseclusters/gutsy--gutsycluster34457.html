Synonyms

    8.6 0.03/m
    gutty is an informal way to say someone shows brave determination when facing trouble, while gutsy sounds more common and bold.
  • plucky
    6.8 0.8/m
    plucky is a small, lively kind of courage shown in hard times, while gutsy is rougher and more direct in tone.
  • courageous
    3.3 8/m
    courageous is a formal word for facing danger calmly, while gutsy feels more casual, bold, and full of rough spirit.
  • stouthearted
    8.2 0.04/m
    stouthearted is steady and strongly determined in hard times, while gutsy is more everyday and shows quick, bold action.
  • nervy
    7.5 0.06/m
    nervy shows boldness that can seem rude or risky, while gutsy shows brave determination in a positive, likable way.
  • fearless
    3.4 5/m
    fearless has no fear and feels very strong in danger, while gutsy still shows risk but keeps a warm, human side.
  • gallant
    5.6 0.4/m
    gallant is brave in a polite or romantic way, while gutsy is rougher and more about bold, unpolished courage.
  • valorous
    8.8 0.1/m
    valorous is very noble bravery often in battle or great danger, while gutsy is everyday boldness shown in small, tough moments.
