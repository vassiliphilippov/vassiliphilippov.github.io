mescal

noun
a small cactus with hallucinogenic buttons
9.9
Pronunciation: / mɛsˈkæl / (AmE), / mɛsˈkæl / (BrE)
a small, spineless, globe-shaped cactus native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, whose dried tops are used as mescal buttons and have hallucinogenic properties

Examples

Mescal cactus
He collected seeds of the mescal cactus during his trip to Texas.
Many researchers study mescal to understand traditional medicine practices.
The Native Americans used mescal in certain rituals for its psychoactive effects.

Related Terms
