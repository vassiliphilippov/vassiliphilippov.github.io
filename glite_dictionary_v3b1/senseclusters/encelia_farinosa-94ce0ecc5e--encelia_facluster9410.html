Encelia farinosa

noun
a shrub of SW US and Mexico used in incense and folk medicine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈsiːliə fəˈrinoʊsə / (AmE), / ɛnˈsiːlɪə fəˈriːnəʊsə / (BrE)
A fragrant, rounded shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, with brittle stems, blue-green leaves, and yellow flowers, used in incense, varnish, and folk medicine.

Examples

Encelia farinosa yellow flowers
Encelia farinosa is often called brittlebush and is recognized by its yellow spring flowers.
Many gardens in arid regions feature Encelia farinosa for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
Traditionally, the resin from Encelia farinosa was used in folk medicine by indigenous people.
