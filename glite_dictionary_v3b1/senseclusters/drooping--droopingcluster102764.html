drooping

adjective
bending or hanging downward
2.8 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdruː.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdruː.pɪŋ/ (BrE)
hanging or bending downward, often because of weakness, tiredness, or lack of support

Examples

Drooping flower heads
Many tulips had drooping heads after the heavy rain.
The drooping branches of the old willow tree touched the surface of the pond.
The drooping leaves signaled that the plant needed to be watered.
By noon, the heat left everyone drooping and tired.
He sat on the bench, his shoulders drooping with disappointment.
The flowers in the vase were drooping after a week without water.
After hiking all day in the heat, the children were drooping with tiredness by the time they got home.
His drooping posture revealed just how drained he was after the marathon.
She walked into the room with drooping shoulders, clearly exhausted from her long shift.

Related Terms

  • antonym: energetic - full of energy and vitality
  • synonym: nodding - bending or hanging downward, especially of flowers
  • synonym: pendulous - hanging down loosely; swinging freely
  • synonym: sagging - hanging or sinking downward in the middle from weight or lack of support
  • antonym: upright - standing straight up, not bending or hanging down
