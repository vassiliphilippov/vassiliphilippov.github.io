acute-angled triangle

noun
triangle with all acute angles
3.7
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut ˌæŋɡəld ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ˌaŋɡəld ˈtraɪæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
A triangle in which all three interior angles are less than 90 degrees.

Examples

An acute-angled triangle
In geometry class, we learned that an acute-angled triangle has all its angles less than 90 degrees.
Unlike a right triangle, an acute-angled triangle does not have any right angles.
You can draw an acute-angled triangle by making each side meet at a sharp angle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: right-angled triangle - a triangle with one right angle
