strategic buyout

noun
business acquisition for strategic benefits
6.4 5.7
Pronunciation: /strə.ˈtiː.dʒɪk ˈbaɪ.aʊt/ (AmE), /strə.ˈtiː.dʒɪk ˈbaɪ.aʊt/ (BrE)
an acquisition of a company by another based on a calculated analysis of the benefits, such as increased earnings, resulting from consolidation

Examples

A strategic buyout deal
A strategic buyout often aims to capitalize on potential synergies between two firms.
After evaluating the potential benefits, the board approved a strategic buyout of its main competitor.
The company pursued a strategic buyout to strengthen its position in the technology sector.

Related Terms

  • synonym: merger - the combination of two companies into one
