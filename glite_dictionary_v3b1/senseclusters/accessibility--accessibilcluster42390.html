accessibility

noun
digital usability for people with disabilities
3.8 8/m
Pronunciation: / 59kccs5bs59c8b6al59ti / (AmE), / 59kccs5bs59c8b6al59ti / (BrE)
(in computing and disability rights) the degree to which digital content, websites, apps, and electronic services can be used, understood, and navigated by people with disabilities, including compatibility with assistive technologies and adherence to accessibility standards.

Examples

Digital accessibility
The website improved its accessibility by adding alt text and keyboard navigation for screen-reader users.
New guidelines require developers to test accessibility with assistive technologies like screen readers and magnifiers.
Failing to consider accessibility can prevent many people with disabilities from using online government services.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inaccessibility - the quality of digital content being difficult or impossible for people with disabilities to use
  • synonym: usability - the ease with which people can use a product to achieve a particular goal
