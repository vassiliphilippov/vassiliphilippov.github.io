sea pink

noun
plant with pink flowers found on seacoasts
9.9
Pronunciation: / si ˈpɪŋk / (AmE), / siː ˈpɪŋk / (BrE)
a perennial plant with tufted pink or purple flowers, commonly found on seacoasts and mountains in temperate regions; also used as ground cover.

Examples

The sea pink flower
Clusters of sea pink bloom brightly along the rocky shoreline each spring.
Gardeners often plant sea pink as a colorful ground cover in well-drained soil.
The resilient sea pink thrives where few other flowers can survive, battering salt winds on coastal cliffs.
