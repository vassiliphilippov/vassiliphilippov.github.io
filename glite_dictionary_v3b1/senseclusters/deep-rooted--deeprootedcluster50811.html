deep-rooted

adjective
firmly established; difficult to change
4.8 0.85/m
Pronunciation: / ÀådipÀàrut…™d / (AmE), / ÀådiÀêpÀàruÀêt…™d / (BrE)
(especially of feelings, beliefs, or traditions) very firmly established and difficult to change.

Examples

Deep-rooted beliefs
Efforts to combat deep-rooted prejudice require patience and commitment from everyone involved.
His deep-rooted mistrust of outsiders made it difficult for him to make new friends.
The country still struggles with deep-rooted traditions that shape its culture.

Synonyms

  • ingrained
    6.4 0.61/m
    ingrained describes habits or attitudes fixed by long practice, while deep-rooted refers more to feelings, beliefs, or traditions.
  • deep-seated
    4.7 1.95/m
    deep-seated describes feelings or ideas that lie very deep and last a long time, while deep-rooted names something firmly established.
  • entrenched
    6.7 0.65/m
    entrenched often implies strong resistance to change and is used for positions or habits, while deep-rooted stresses long history.
  • rooted
    3.7 0.14/m
    rooted often means firmly tied to a place, idea, or feeling, while deep-rooted stresses being very hard to change.
