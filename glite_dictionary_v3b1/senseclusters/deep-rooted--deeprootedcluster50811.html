deep-rooted

adjective
firmly established; difficult to change
3.2 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌdipˈrutɪd / (AmE), / ˌdiːpˈruːtɪd / (BrE)
(especially of feelings, beliefs, or traditions) very firmly established and difficult to change.

Examples

Deep-rooted beliefs
Efforts to combat deep-rooted prejudice require patience and commitment from everyone involved.
His deep-rooted mistrust of outsiders made it difficult for him to make new friends.
The country still struggles with deep-rooted traditions that shape its culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ingrained - (of habits, attitudes, etc.) firmly fixed; difficult to change
  • antonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface; not deep or firmly established
