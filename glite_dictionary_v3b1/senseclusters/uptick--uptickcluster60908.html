uptick

noun
a small increase or rise
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌtɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌp.tɪk / (BrE)
a small increase or rise, especially in the level or value of something

Examples

An uptick in sales
Analysts reported an uptick in consumer confidence this quarter.
There has been a recent uptick in housing prices in the area.
We noticed an uptick in website traffic after the campaign launched.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decrease - an instance of becoming smaller or less
  • synonym: increase - an instance of becoming larger or greater
