interspace

verb
to put spaces between
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈspeɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈspeɪs / (BrE)
to place or insert spaces or intervals between things

Examples

To interspace the seats
The designer decided to interspace the chairs evenly around the table.
Tall trees were interspaced with flowering bushes along the path.
He likes to interspace his study sessions with short breaks to stay focused.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cluster - to group things closely together
  • synonym: separate - to set or keep apart
