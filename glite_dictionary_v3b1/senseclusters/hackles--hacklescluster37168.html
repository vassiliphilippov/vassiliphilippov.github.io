hackles

noun
animal neck hairs
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhækəlz / (AmE), / ˈhækəlz / (BrE)
the hairs along the back of an animal's neck that stand up when it is angry or frightened

Examples

A cat's hackles rise
When the cat saw the unfamiliar dog, its hackles immediately rose.
The wolf's hackles bristled as it prepared to defend its territory.
You could tell the animal was scared because its hackles were standing straight up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mane - the long hair on the neck of a horse, lion, or similar animal
