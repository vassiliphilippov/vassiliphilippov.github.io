John Paul II

proper noun
Pope (1978–2005), Polish-born head of Catholic Church
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn pɔl səˈkənd / (AmE), / dʒɒn pɔːl sɪˈkənd / (BrE)
(1920–2005) the Pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 1978 to 2005, born in Poland as Karol Wojtyła; the first non-Italian Pope in over 450 years.

Examples

John Paul II Pope
John Paul II was widely admired for his efforts to promote peace and human rights around the world.
During his visit in 1983, John Paul II addressed a large crowd in Poland.
Many people remember John Paul II as one of the most influential religious leaders of the 20th century.
