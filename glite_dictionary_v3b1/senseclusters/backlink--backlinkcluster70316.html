backlink

noun
incoming website link
Pronunciation: / ˈbæklɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈbæklɪŋk / (BrE)
a hyperlink from one website or web page to another, often used to improve search engine ranking or to reference another page

Examples

Quality backlinks from websites
The website gained higher search rankings after receiving several quality backlinks from reputable sources.
SEO experts recommend building backlinks to increase a site's authority and visibility online.
She checked the backlinks to her blog to see which sites were referencing her articles.
The article included a backlink to the original research paper for further reading.
Websites often use backlinks to improve their search engine rankings.
She created a backlink from her new post to an older tutorial on the same topic.
The marketing team focused on building high-quality backlinks to improve their website's visibility.
A single backlink from a popular site can boost your search engine ranking significantly.
Webmasters often exchange backlinks to increase traffic to their sites.

  • synonym: reference - a mention or link to another source or document
