profusely

adverb
to a great or excessive degree
4.8 1.88/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ôÀàfjusli / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàfjuÀêsli / (BrE)
In large quantities; to a great or excessive degree.

Examples

Profusely bleeding
After the accident, blood was flowing profusely from his arm.
She apologized profusely for the misunderstanding, hoping to make amends.
The supporters cheered profusely when their team scored the winning goal.

Synonyms

  • copiously
    6.1 0.40/m
    copiously is used when something happens in large amounts or with many details, while profusely often suggests excess.
  • abundantly
    3.5 2.68/m
    abundantly shows plenty in a clear, positive way, while profusely can also imply an overwhelming or excessive amount.
  • plentifully
    2.5 0.15/m
    plentifully means there is a large supply for use, while profusely stresses quantity and sometimes a sense of too much.
  • bountifully
    4.6 0.08/m
    bountifully suggests generous giving or nature's plenty, while profusely focuses on sheer amount and can seem excessive.
  • excessively
    3.0 3.81/m
    excessively means more than is reasonable or proper, while profusely can mean very abundant and not always wrong.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abundantly - in a plentiful or ample manner
  • antonym: sparingly - in a limited or moderate manner
