cestus

noun
ancient boxing glove
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛstəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛstəs / (BrE)
a leather glove or band worn by ancient boxers to protect their hands and add force to blows

Examples

Ancient cestus glove
In ancient Rome, a cestus was used by fighters to protect their hands during matches.
The museum displayed a well-preserved cestus alongside other ancient sporting equipment.
Before the contest, the athlete carefully wrapped a cestus around his fists.
