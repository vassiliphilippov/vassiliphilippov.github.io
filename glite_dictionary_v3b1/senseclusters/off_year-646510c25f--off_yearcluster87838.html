off year

noun
year of lower-than-usual productivity or activity
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɑf jɪr / (AmE), / ɒf jɪə / (BrE)
A year in which productivity, success, or activity is lower than average.

Examples

An off year for sales
After last season's record harvest, this was an off year for the vineyard.
It's common for fruit trees to alternate between a strong year and an off year.
The company experienced an off year, with profits lower than expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: banner year - a year of exceptional success or growth
