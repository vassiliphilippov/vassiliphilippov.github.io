electively

adverb
by election
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktɪvli / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktɪvli / (BrE)
by means of an election or through the process of being chosen by vote

Examples

Electively chosen leader
The president was electively appointed by the board after a formal vote.
In some organizations, leaders are chosen electively rather than by seniority.
The council members serve electively, representing the interests of their constituents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: democratically - in a way that involves voting or majority rule
  • antonym: hereditarily - by inheritance or family lineage
