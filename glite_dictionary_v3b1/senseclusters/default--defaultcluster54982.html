default

verb
fail to pay a debt or obligation
5.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɔlt / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːlt / (BrE)
to fail to pay money that you owe or to meet a financial obligation, especially on a loan

Examples

To default on a loan
Companies sometimes default despite having made large profits previously.
If you default on your loan, the bank can repossess your car.
Many homeowners defaulted during the financial crisis and lost their homes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: repay - to pay back money that you owe
