layback

noun
climbing technique
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.bæk/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.bæk/ (BrE)
a climbing move in which the climber leans away from a hold while pulling with the hands and pushing with the feet

Examples

Climbing layback move
The climber used a layback to move up the vertical crack in the rock face.
Mastering the layback technique can help you tackle more challenging climbing routes.
She struggled at first but finally managed a smooth layback around the corner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jam - a climbing move where a body part is wedged into a crack for support
  • synonym: mantle - a climbing move where the climber pushes down on a hold to get over it
