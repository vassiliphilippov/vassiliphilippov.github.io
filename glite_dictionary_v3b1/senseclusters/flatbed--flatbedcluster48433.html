flatbed

noun
freight car without sides or roof
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌbɛd / (AmE), / ˈflætˌbɛd / (BrE)
a vehicle or platform with a flat, open bed and no sides or roof, used for carrying large or heavy items (such as a railroad car, truck, or trailer)

Examples

A flatbed truck
A flatbed is ideal for oversized loads that wouldn't fit in a boxcar.
The heavy machinery was transported on a flatbed across the country.
Workers secured the crates to the flatbed before the train departed.
A large beam was transported on a flatbed truck across the city.
The movers loaded the piano onto a flatbed for delivery to the new house.
They hired a flatbed to move heavy construction equipment to the site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flatcar - a railroad freight car with a flat floor and no sides; especially in American English
