golden shiner

noun
a small North American freshwater fish
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən ˈʃaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈʃaɪnə / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of eastern North America, Notemigonus crysoleucas, with golden glints on its scales, often used as bait.

Examples

The golden shiner fish
Researchers studied the migration patterns of the golden shiner in regional lakes.
The golden shiner is commonly used as bait by anglers fishing for bass.
We spotted a school of golden shiners glinting in the shallow water near the shore.
