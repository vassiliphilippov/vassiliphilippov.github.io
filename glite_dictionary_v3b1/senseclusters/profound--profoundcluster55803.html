profound

adjective
of deep or far-reaching effect
5.1
Pronunciation: / prəˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / prəˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
very great or intense; complete or absolute (used for effects, feelings, impressions, states, or sleep)

Examples

Profound effect
Her research led to profound changes in the field.
The discovery had a profound impact on our understanding of the universe.
The reforms brought about profound improvements in education.
After hearing the news, Maria felt a profound sadness settle over her.
His apology was marked by a profound sense of regret that was impossible to ignore.
She let out a profound sigh, revealing her deep disappointment.
His speech left a profound impression on everyone who heard it.
She felt a profound shock at the news of her friend's sudden departure.
The room fell into a profound silence after his announcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deep - extending far down from the top or surface; (figuratively) intense or extreme
  • synonym: fundamental - forming a necessary base or core; of central importance
  • synonym: intense - of extreme force, degree, or strength
  • antonym: superficial - not deep or thorough; on the surface only
