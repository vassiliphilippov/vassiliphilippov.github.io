Cardinal Newman

proper noun
English cardinal and Oxford Movement leader
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrdɪnəl ˈnuːmən / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdɪnəl ˈnjuːmən / (BrE)
(1801–1890) English cardinal, theologian, and leader of the Oxford Movement, who converted from Anglicanism to Roman Catholicism.

Examples

Cardinal Newman played a pivotal role in the religious reawakening known as the Oxford Movement.
