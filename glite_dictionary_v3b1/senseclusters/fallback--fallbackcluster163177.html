fallback

adjective
reserve or backup
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlbæk / (BrE)
kept or used as a reserve or substitute when the main option is not available

Examples

A fallback plan
They had a fallback plan in case the original idea did not work.
The team used a fallback strategy when their main approach failed.
We kept a fallback option ready for unexpected problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backup - serving as a substitute or reserve
  • antonym: primary - main or most important
