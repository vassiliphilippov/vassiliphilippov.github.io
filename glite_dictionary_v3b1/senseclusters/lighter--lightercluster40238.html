lighter

verb
transport goods in a flat-bottomed boat
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtə / (BrE)
to transfer cargo between ships or to shore using a lighter (a flat-bottomed boat or barge)

Examples

To lighter containers
Due to the shallow harbor, containers were lightered ashore by small boats.
Special vessels are designed to lighter oil from tankers at sea.
The crew used a barge to lighter cargo from the anchored ship to the quay.
The crew began lightering the oil to smaller vessels to prevent the tanker from running aground.
Due to the shallow harbor, they had to lighter the cargo onto barges before reaching the dock.
After the accident, emergency teams quickly lightered supplies from the damaged ship to safety.
