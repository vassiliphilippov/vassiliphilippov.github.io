panic grass

noun
grass of the genus Panicum
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpænɪk ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈpænɪk ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
Any grass of the genus Panicum, grown for its grain or as fodder for animals.

Examples

The panic grass plant
Many farmers plant panic grass to help restore soil fertility and prevent erosion.
Several species of panic grass are valued for their drought resistance in arid regions.
The seeds of panic grass can be harvested and used as animal feed.
