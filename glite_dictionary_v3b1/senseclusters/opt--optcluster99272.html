opt

verb
choose or select
3.2 21/m
Pronunciation: / É‘pt / (AmE), / É’pt / (BrE)
to make a choice from a range of possibilities; to choose one thing instead of another

Examples

To opt for choice
After much thought, she opted for a smaller apartment downtown.
Many people opt for online shopping because of its convenience.
You can opt to take the earlier train if you wish.

Synonyms

  • choose
    1.0 276/m
    choose is the general word for picking one option in everyday speech, while opt is slightly more formal and often used for planned choices.
  • select
    2.5 99/m
    select is a careful, often formal choice from a group, while opt usually describes choosing between stated options.
  • pick
    1.0 128/m
    pick is informal and often means choosing quickly or casually, while opt is more neutral and fits formal contexts.
  • go with
    1.1 6.23/m
    go with is a casual phrasal verb people use in speech to choose, while opt is more formal and common in writing.
  • decide
    1.0 224/m
    decide means making a judgment after thought or discussion, while opt simply says you choose one option among others.
  • take
    1.0 73/m
    take often means accepting or picking an item, sometimes buying it, while opt focuses on choosing between alternatives.
  • prefer
    1.3 85/m
    prefer expresses liking one thing more than another, while opt describes actually making a choice between available options.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choose - to select from a number of possibilities
  • synonym: decide - to make a choice or come to a resolution
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or choose
