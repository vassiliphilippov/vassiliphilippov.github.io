criollo

noun
a superior variety of cocoa bean
Pronunciation: / kriˈoʊjoʊ / (AmE), / kriˈɒljoʊ / (BrE)
A variety of cocoa bean considered to be of superior quality, prized for its fine flavor and aroma.

Examples

Criollo cocoa beans
Due to its low yield and susceptibility to disease, criollo cocoa is more expensive than other varieties.
Many artisanal chocolate makers prefer using criollo beans for their delicate and complex taste.
The region is famous for cultivating rare criollo cocoa prized by fine chocolatiers worldwide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forastero - a common variety of cocoa bean with a stronger, less refined flavor
  • synonym: trinitario - a hybrid variety of cocoa bean combining Criollo and Forastero traits
