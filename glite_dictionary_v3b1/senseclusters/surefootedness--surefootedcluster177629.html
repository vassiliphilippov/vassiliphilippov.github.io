surefootedness

noun
confidence and steadiness
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌʃʊrˈfʊtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːˈfʊtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being confident, steady, and reliable in judgment or action

Examples

Surefootedness in decisions
Her surefootedness in negotiations secured the best deal for the company during difficult talks.
The climber's surefootedness allowed him to cross the rocky path without slipping.
A leader's surefootedness inspires trust and confidence in the whole team.
