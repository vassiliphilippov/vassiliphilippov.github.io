viviparity

noun
seeds sprouting on parent
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌvɪvəˈpærəti / (AmE), / ˌvɪvɪˈpærɪti / (BrE)
the condition in some plants where seeds or buds begin to develop into new plants while still attached to the parent

Examples

Plant viviparity
Some mangrove species show viviparity, with seedlings growing while still attached to the parent tree.
The unique viviparity of certain grasses allows them to quickly colonize new areas after storms.
Researchers studied viviparity in coastal plants to understand how they adapt to harsh environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: germination - the process by which a plant grows from a seed
  • synonym: vivipary - the process of seeds or buds developing while still attached to the parent plant
