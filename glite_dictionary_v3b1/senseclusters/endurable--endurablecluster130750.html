endurable

adjective
able to be tolerated or endured
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʊrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊərəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be tolerated or withstood, even though unpleasant or difficult.

Examples

An endurable pain
Despite the harsh conditions, the work was just endurable enough to continue.
He found the long meetings barely endurable without a cup of coffee.
The pain after the operation was endurable, so she needed only minimal medication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tolerable - able to be tolerated; bearable
  • antonym: unbearable - impossible to tolerate or endure
