overspecialize

verb
to become overly specialized
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˈspɛʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɛʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to become or make overly specialized, limiting one's knowledge or expertise to a very narrow area

Examples

To overspecialize in one field
He overspecialized in medieval poetry and struggled to find teaching positions.
It's easy to overspecialize in a technical job and lose sight of important general skills.
Some scientists worry they might overspecialize and miss broader connections in their field.
If you overspecialise, you may struggle to adapt to new roles in your career.
Researchers sometimes overspecialise and lose sight of the broader context.
She realised she had overspecialised when she couldn't discuss any topics outside her field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generalise - to make less specific, to broaden focus or application
  • degree lower: specialise - to focus on a particular area of study or activity
  • degree lower: specialize - to concentrate on and become expert in a particular subject or skill
