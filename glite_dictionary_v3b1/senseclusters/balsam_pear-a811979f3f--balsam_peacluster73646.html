balsam pear

noun
tropical vine with yellow-orange fruit
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔl.səm ˌpɛr / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.səm ˌpɪə / (BrE)
A tropical Old World vine, Momordica charantia, cultivated for its yellow-orange, bitter fruit, often used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine.

Examples

Fresh balsam pear
She chopped the balsam pear and added it to the stir-fry.
Some herbal remedies use extracts from the balsam pear for their medicinal properties.
The balsam pear is prized in many Asian cuisines for its distinct bitter flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bitter melon - another common name for Momordica charantia, the same plant as balsam pear
