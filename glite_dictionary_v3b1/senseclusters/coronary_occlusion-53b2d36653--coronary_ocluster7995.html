coronary occlusion

noun
blockage of a coronary artery
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrəˌneri əˈkluːʒən / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri əˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
the blockage of a coronary artery, typically caused by the build-up of atherosclerotic plaque or a blood clot, which can lead to a heart attack

Examples

A coronary occlusion event
A coronary occlusion can lead to serious complications such as a heart attack.
Doctors used an angiogram to detect the site of the coronary occlusion.
The patient suffered a coronary occlusion that required immediate medical intervention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: myocardial infarction - heart attack; damage to heart muscle due to interruption of its blood supply
