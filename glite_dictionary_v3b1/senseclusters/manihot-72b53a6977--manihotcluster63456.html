Manihot

proper noun
genus of tropical plants; includes cassava
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪˌhɑt / (AmE), / ˈmænɪˌhɒt / (BrE)
A genus of tropical plants in the spurge family, including cassava, cultivated for their edible starchy roots.

Examples

Manihot esculenta plant
Manihot esculenta is cultivated worldwide for its starchy tuberous roots, commonly known as cassava.
Botanists have identified several wild species within the Manihot genus native to South America.
The economic value of Manihot crops is significant in tropical agriculture.
