wheedle

verb
persuade by flattery or gentle urging
7.9 0.5/m
Pronunciation: / Ààwid…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàwiÀêd…ôl / (BrE)
To influence or persuade someone by gentle urging, flattering, or coaxing.

Examples

To wheedle a favor
He managed to wheedle his way into the exclusive party.
She tried to wheedle an extra piece of cake from her grandmother.
The salesperson attempted to wheedle the customer into buying the expensive suit.

Synonyms

  • cajole
    7.7 1/m
    cajole uses gentle urging and flattery to get someone to act, while wheedle often implies persistent, soft coaxing.
  • coax
    4.1 5/m
    coax means gently and patiently encouraging someone to do something, while wheedle adds flattering or ingratiating talk to persuade.
  • sweet-talk
    5.3 0.5/m
    sweet-talk is to flatter someone with smooth, pleasing words to get them to agree, while wheedle adds coaxing and urging.
  • blarney
    9.5 0.00/m
    blarney is charming, flattering talk often used in friendly chat to persuade, while wheedle implies more coaxing and pleading.
  • palaver
    9.9 0.00/m
    palaver can mean long or idle talk, sometimes to persuade or delay, while wheedle focuses on gentle urging and flattery.
  • inveigle
    9.5 0.1/m
    inveigle means to win someone over by clever or sneaky means, while wheedle stays more gentle, friendly, and coaxing.
