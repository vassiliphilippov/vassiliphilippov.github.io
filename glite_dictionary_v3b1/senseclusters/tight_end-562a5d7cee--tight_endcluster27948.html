tight end

noun
football position combining blocker and receiver
2.8
Pronunciation: / taɪt ɛnd / (AmE), / taɪt ɛnd / (BrE)
In American football, an offensive player who lines up close to the tackle and serves as both a blocker and a receiver.

Examples

A tight end player
He was drafted as a tight end because of his size and agility.
Many teams use the tight end as both a blocker and a receiver.
The tight end caught the pass and ran for a touchdown.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: offensive tackle - a player who lines up on the outside of the offensive line
  • degree higher: wide receiver - an offensive player positioned far from the line to catch passes
