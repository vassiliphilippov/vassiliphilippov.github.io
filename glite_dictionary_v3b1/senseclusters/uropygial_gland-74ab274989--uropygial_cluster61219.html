uropygial gland

noun
oil-secreting gland at base of bird's tail
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌjʊrəˈpaɪdʒiəl ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəˈpɪdʒɪəl ɡlænd / (BrE)
a gland located at the base of a bird's tail that secretes oil used to maintain and waterproof feathers

Examples

The uropygial gland secretes oil
If a bird's uropygial gland is damaged, it may struggle to maintain healthy feathers.
Some scientists study the uropygial gland to understand how birds keep their plumage waterproof.
The uropygial gland produces a special oil that birds use for preening their feathers.
