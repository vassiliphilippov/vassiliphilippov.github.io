offset

verb
to counterbalance or make up for
Pronunciation: / ˈɑfˌsɛt /; / ˈɔfˌsɛt, ɑf- / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt / (BrE)
to counterbalance or compensate for something; to make up for or reduce an effect

Examples

To offset the cost
A healthy diet can offset some negative effects of stress.
His creative solutions offset her lack of experience on the project.
The company planted trees to offset its carbon emissions.
He planted new trees to offset the damage caused by deforestation.
Tax credits can offset the amount of tax you owe.
The increased costs were offset by higher sales revenue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensate - to make up for a loss or deficiency
  • synonym: counteract - to act against something in order to reduce its force or neutralize it
  • synonym: counterbalance - to balance or offset the effect of something
