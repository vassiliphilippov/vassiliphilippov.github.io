tug-of-war

noun
team sport using a rope
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌɡ əv ˌwɔr / (AmE), / ˌtʌɡ əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a contest in which two teams pull on opposite ends of a rope, with the aim of dragging the other team across a central line

Examples

School tug-of-war game
As a child, she loved participating in the annual tug-of-war at school sports day.
Every summer, our village holds a tug-of-war during the festival to test the teams' strength.
The tug-of-war ended quickly when one team slipped on the muddy ground.

  • synonym: game - an activity that one engages in for amusement or competition
