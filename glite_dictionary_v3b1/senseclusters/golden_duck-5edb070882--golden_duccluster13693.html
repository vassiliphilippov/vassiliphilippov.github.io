golden duck

noun
cricket term: out first ball for zero runs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən dʌk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən dʌk / (BrE)
In cricket, a dismissal in which a batsman is out for zero runs on the very first ball faced.

Examples

A golden duck dismissal
Scoring a golden duck put immediate pressure on the rest of the team.
She was consoled by her teammates after suffering a golden duck on debut.
The opening batsman was disappointed to get a golden duck in such an important match.
