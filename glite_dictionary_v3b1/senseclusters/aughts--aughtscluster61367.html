aughts

noun
zeros in numbers
Pronunciation: / ɔts / (AmE), / ɔːts / (BrE)
the digit zero or a group of zeros, especially when spoken or written as part of a number.

Examples

Aughts in a code
The old safe's code had three aughts followed by a single five.
When reading the serial number, he paused at the two aughts in the middle.
Some people refer to the zeros in a phone number as aughts instead of zeros.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nought - the digit zero, especially in British English
  • synonym: zero - the number 0; nothing
