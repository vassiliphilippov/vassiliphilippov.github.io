Jacksonian

noun
follower of Andrew Jackson
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒækˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒækˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
a follower of Andrew Jackson or his political principles, particularly those associated with American democracy in the early 19th century

Examples

A Jacksonian leader
Historians debate the impact that Jacksonians had on American democracy.
Many southern farmers identified as Jacksonians during the 1830s.
The candidate positioned himself as a modern Jacksonian in his campaign rhetoric.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Jeffersonian - a follower of Thomas Jefferson or his ideas
