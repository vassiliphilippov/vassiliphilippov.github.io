Wimshurst machine

noun
electrostatic generator device
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪmshɜrst məˌʃin / (AmE), / ˈwɪmshɜːst məˌʃiːn / (BrE)
a type of electrostatic generator that produces high voltage by building up a charge of static electricity, typically using two counter-rotating disks

A well-maintained Wimshurst machine can still produce impressive static discharges today.
The Wimshurst machine was a key invention in the field of early electricity experiments.
Using a Wimshurst machine, the students generated visible sparks in their science class.
