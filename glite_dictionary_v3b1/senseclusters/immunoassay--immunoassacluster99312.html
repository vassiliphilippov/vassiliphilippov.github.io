immunoassay

noun
lab test using antibody-antigen reaction
9.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɪ.mjuː.noʊ.ˈæs.eɪ/ /; / /ɪ.mjʊ.noʊ.ˈæs.eɪ/ (AmE), /ɪ.mjuː.nəʊ.ˈæs.eɪ/ /; / /ɪ.mjʊ.nəʊ.ˈæs.eɪ/ (BrE)
a laboratory technique for identifying or quantifying a substance, especially a protein, by using its reaction with a specific antibody or antigen

Examples

An immunoassay test
Advances in immunoassay technology have improved the early diagnosis of many diseases.
Researchers developed a new immunoassay that is both faster and more accurate than previous methods.
The laboratory used an immunoassay to detect tiny amounts of hormones in the patient's blood.
