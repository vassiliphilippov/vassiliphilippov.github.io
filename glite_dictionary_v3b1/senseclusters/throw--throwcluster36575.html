throw

noun
a single attempt or chance
5.5
Pronunciation: / θroʊ / (AmE), / θrəʊ / (BrE)
an act of throwing or propelling something, or an attempt or chance in a game

Examples

A throw in a game
He paid ten dollars for each throw at the ring toss game.
It costs twenty pounds a throw to enter the contest.
With only one throw left, she concentrated hard before aiming.
He risked his life savings on a single throw at the card table.
The outcome of the game depended on the final throw of the dice.
With a lucky throw, she won the entire jackpot.
One bad throw cost the team the winning run.
She practiced her throw every afternoon in preparation for the contest.
The outfielder's throw to home plate was fast and accurate.
