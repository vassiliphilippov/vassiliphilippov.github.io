League of Iroquois

proper noun
historic confederation of Iroquois tribes
8.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / liÀêg …ôv …™r…ôÀåkw…î…™ / (AmE), / liÀêg …ôv …™…ôr…ôÀåkw…î…™ / (BrE)
The confederation of five (later six) Iroquois tribes in North America, comprising the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca (and joined by the Tuscarora in 1722), also known as the Iroquois Confederacy or Six Nations.

Examples

The League of Iroquois confederacy
Historians often study the structure of the League of Iroquois to understand Indigenous governance.
The League of Iroquois played a central role in the colonial history of North America.
The political system of the League of Iroquois inspired aspects of American democracy.

Synonyms

  • Iroquois League
    8.6 0.00/m
    Iroquois League is the common English name for the confederation, while League of Iroquois is an alternative phrasing.
  • Six Nations
    7.4 0.77/m
    Six Nations refers to the confederation after the Tuscarora join, while League of Iroquois names the same allied tribes.
  • Five Nations
    8.2 0.04/m
    Five Nations names the original five tribes before the Tuscarora joined, while League of Iroquois covers the confederation then or later.
  • Haudenosaunee
    9.9 0.20/m
    Haudenosaunee is the people's own name in their language, while League of Iroquois is the English term used in histories.
