cowcatcher

noun
frame on the front of a locomotive
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊˌkætʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌkatʃə / (BrE)
an inclined metal frame attached to the front of a locomotive to clear obstacles from the tracks

Examples

A cowcatcher on locomotive
After the accident, the cowcatcher was bent and needed repair.
The cowcatcher was designed to push debris off the railroad tracks.
The old steam engine still has a cowcatcher mounted on its front.
