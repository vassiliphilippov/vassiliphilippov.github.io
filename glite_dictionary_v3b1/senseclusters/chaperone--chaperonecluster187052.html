chaperone

verb
to accompany and supervise
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæpəˌroʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃæpərəʊn / (BrE)
to accompany and supervise someone, especially to ensure proper behavior in social situations

Examples

To chaperone a school trip
Parents often chaperone school dances to ensure everyone behaves appropriately.
She chaperoned her younger brother at the birthday party.
Two teachers were assigned to chaperone the students on the field trip.
