monster

noun
grossly malformed, nonviable fetus
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnstər / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.stər / (BrE)
(medicine, historical) A fetus that is grossly malformed and usually nonviable.

Examples

[historical] Medical monster
In 18th-century medical literature, the term monster was used for severely malformed fetuses.
Modern medicine now uses different terminology, as calling a fetus a monster is considered outdated and insensitive.
The physician recorded the rare birth of a monster in the hospital’s historical records.
