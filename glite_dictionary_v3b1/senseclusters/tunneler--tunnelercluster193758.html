tunneler

noun
surgical tunneling tool
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌnələr / (AmE), / ˈtʌnələ / (BrE)
a surgical instrument used to create a passage under the skin for placing a catheter or graft

Examples

Surgical tunneler
The nurse prepared the tunneler before the surgeon began the procedure.
A tunneler is essential for safely placing long-term catheters beneath the skin.
After sterilizing the tunneler, the doctor created a path for the new graft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catheter introducer - a device used to insert a catheter into the body
  • antonym: scalpel - a small, sharp knife used in surgery
