Douglas squirrel

noun
tree squirrel of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌɡ.ləs ˈskwɝl / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡ.ləs ˈskwɪəl / (BrE)
a small tree-dwelling squirrel (Tamiasciurus douglasii) native to the forests of the Pacific coastal region of western North America

Examples

The Douglas squirrel calls
Hikers in California forests often hear the rapid calls of the Douglas squirrel before they see it.
The Douglas squirrel has a distinctive rust-colored belly and grayish back.
We spotted a Douglas squirrel chattering loudly in the pine tree above us.

Related Terms

  • synonym: red squirrel - tree squirrel (Sciurus vulgaris or Tamiasciurus hudsonicus) found in Eurasia and eastern North America
