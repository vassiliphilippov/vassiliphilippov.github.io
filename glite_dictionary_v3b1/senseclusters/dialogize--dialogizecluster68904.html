dialogize

verb
to make into dialogue
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌlɑɡaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪələɡaɪz / (BrE)
to present or express something in the form of a dialogue

Examples

To dialogize a story
The author decided to dialogize the novel to make it more engaging for readers.
In the adaptation, the director chose to dialogize several key scenes for dramatic effect.
Teachers sometimes dialogize historical events to help students better understand the perspectives involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dramatize - to present something as a drama or play
  • antonym: narrate - to tell a story or describe events
