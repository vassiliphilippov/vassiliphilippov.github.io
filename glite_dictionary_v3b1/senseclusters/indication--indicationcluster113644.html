indication

noun
sign or suggestion of something
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
something (such as a sign, suggestion, or action) that shows, suggests, or points out that something exists, is true, or is likely

Examples

Clear indication
His silence gave every indication that he disagreed with the plan.
The sudden drop in temperature was an indication that a storm was approaching.
There was a clear indication that the policy would change soon.
Her nervous smile was an indication that she was not confident.
The doctor looked for any indication of an infection before prescribing antibiotics.
There was no clear indication that the plan would succeed.
Her nod was an indication that she agreed with the plan.
The doctor's indication of the patient's name helped clarify the records.
There was no clear indication of who was responsible for the error.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evidence - the available body of facts or information indicating whether a belief or proposition is true or valid
  • synonym: sign - an object, quality, or event whose presence indicates the probable presence of something else
  • synonym: suggestion - an idea or indication pointing toward a possible course of action
  • synonym: symptom - a sign of the existence of something, especially of an undesirable situation
