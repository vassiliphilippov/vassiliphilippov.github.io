identify

verb
feel associated; share identity or similarities
3.2 3.4
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdɛntəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / aɪˈdɛntɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to feel or consider oneself to be closely associated with or similar to someone or something; to share the feelings, experiences, or characteristics of another person or group

Examples

To identify with others
After moving to a new country, he found it difficult to identify with the local culture.
Many young people identify with the struggles portrayed in the novel.
She couldn't identify with the values of her parents' generation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alienate - cause to feel isolated or estranged
  • synonym: empathize - to understand and share the feelings of another
