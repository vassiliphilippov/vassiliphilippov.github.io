liberty cap

noun
conical cap symbolizing liberty
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbɚti ˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti ˌkap / (BrE)
A close-fitting conical cap worn as a symbol of liberty, especially during the French Revolution and in the United States before 1800.

Examples

A liberty cap statue
A statue at the museum depicts a woman holding a pole topped with a liberty cap.
During the parade, several marchers wore a liberty cap to honor revolutionary ideals.
The liberty cap became an enduring emblem on coins and political cartoons of the era.
