broad

adjective
strongly regional in speech
Pronunciation: / brɔd / (AmE), / brɔːd / (BrE)
(of speech) strongly marked by regional features; heavily and noticeably regional.

Examples

Broad accent
He spoke with a broad Yorkshire accent that was hard to miss.
Her broad Australian vowels made her easy to identify as a local.
The comedian exaggerated his broad Irish accent for comic effect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mild - (of an accent) not strongly marked
  • synonym: strong - (of an accent) clearly showing regional characteristics
