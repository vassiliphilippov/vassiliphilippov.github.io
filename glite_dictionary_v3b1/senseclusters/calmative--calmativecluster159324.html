calmative

adjective
soothing or calming
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmətɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɑːmətɪv / (BrE)
having the quality of calming or soothing, especially by reducing anxiety or agitation

Examples

Calmative voice
The therapist used a calmative tone to help the anxious child relax.
Some herbal teas are known for their calmative properties and are used to ease stress.
Listening to calmative music before bed can improve sleep quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: soothing - having a calming or relaxing effect
  • antonym: stimulating - increasing activity or excitement
