order Lepidoptera

noun
insect order: moths and butterflies
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər ˌlɛpɪˈdɒptərə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌlɛpɪˈdɒptərə / (BrE)
The group of insects comprising moths and butterflies, characterized by wings covered in scales.

Examples

The order Lepidoptera insects
Many species in the order Lepidoptera play important roles in pollinating plants.
Researchers study the order Lepidoptera to understand patterns of mimicry and evolution.
The order Lepidoptera includes both butterflies and moths, which are known for their scaly wings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butterfly - an insect with large, brightly colored wings, typically active by day
  • synonym: moth - an insect closely related to the butterfly, usually nocturnal
