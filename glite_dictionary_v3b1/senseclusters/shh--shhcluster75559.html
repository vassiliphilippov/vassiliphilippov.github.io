shh

interjection
request for silence
Pronunciation: / ʃ / (AmE), / ʃ / (BrE)
used to ask or tell someone to be quiet, often as a soft sound

Examples

The teacher put her finger to her lips and whispered shh to the noisy students.
He said shh as the movie was about to start.
During the ceremony, someone in the audience quietly said shh to remind others to be silent.
The teacher raised her finger to her lips and whispered shh to the noisy class.
During the play, someone in the audience said shh when people started talking.
