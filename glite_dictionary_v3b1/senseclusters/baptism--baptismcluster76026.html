baptism

noun
Christian rite of purification and admission
1.0 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbæp.tɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈbæp.tɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the Christian religious ceremony of sprinkling water on someone or immersing them in water, symbolizing purification or rebirth and admission into the Christian Church

Examples

Christian baptism ceremony
John regarded his baptism as a deeply meaningful spiritual experience.
Many denominations hold baptism ceremonies for both infants and adults.
She decided to have her baby undergo baptism at their local church.

Related Terms

  • synonym: christening - the ceremony of baptizing and naming a child; often used interchangably with 'baptism'
  • antonym: confirmation - a Christian sacrament in which a baptized person strengthens their commitment to their faith
