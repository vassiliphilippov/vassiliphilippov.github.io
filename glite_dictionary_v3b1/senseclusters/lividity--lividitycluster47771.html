Examples

Postmortem lividity
The forensic examiner observed pronounced lividity on the victim's back, indicating the body had not been moved.
After several hours, lividity becomes fixed and can help estimate the time of death.
The presence of lividity helped the investigators determine the original position of the body.

Synonyms

  • livor
    9.9 0.01/m
    livor is a bluish or purplish skin discoloration from blood pooling after death or injury, while lividity is the postmortem darkening of skin.
  • hypostasis
    9.9 0.00/m
    hypostasis is the accumulation of blood in organs or parts causing tissue discoloration, while lividity refers specifically to postmortem skin darkening from pooled blood.
