lividity

noun
postmortem skin discoloration
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɪˈvɪdəti / (AmE), / lɪˈvɪdəti / (BrE)
the dark discoloration of the skin after death caused by the pooling of blood in the lower parts of the body

Examples

Postmortem lividity
The forensic examiner observed pronounced lividity on the victim's back, indicating the body had not been moved.
After several hours, lividity becomes fixed and can help estimate the time of death.
The presence of lividity helped the investigators determine the original position of the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pallor mortis - the pale appearance of the body after death
  • synonym: postmortem staining - the discoloration of the body after death due to blood settling
