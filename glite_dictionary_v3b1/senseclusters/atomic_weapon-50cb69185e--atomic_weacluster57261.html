atomic weapon

noun
nuclear explosive weapon
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑmɪk ˈwɛpən / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈwɛpən / (BrE)
A weapon with immense destructive power whose explosive force is produced by a nuclear reaction, such as fission or fusion.

Examples

An atomic weapon
The development of the first atomic weapon marked a turning point in modern warfare.
There is international concern over the spread of atomic weapons in unstable regions.
Treaties exist to control the testing and proliferation of atomic weapons worldwide.
