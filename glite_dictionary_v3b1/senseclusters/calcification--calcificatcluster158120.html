calcification

noun
inflexibility or rigidity (figurative)
9.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /kæl.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kæl.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or state of becoming rigid, inflexible, or unchanging, often used metaphorically to describe ideas, institutions, or situations

Examples

Calcification of ideas
Negotiations broke down due to the growing calcification of each side's demands.
Over time, the organization suffered from bureaucratic calcification that made change impossible.
The committee's refusal to consider new ideas led to a complete calcification of policy.

  • synonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and unyielding
