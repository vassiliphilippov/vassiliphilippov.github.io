nonanalytical

adjective
inflection-based grammar
9.6
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ /; / /nɑːn.æn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /nɒn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ /; / /nɒn.æn.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
describing a language or construction that expresses grammatical relationships mainly by inflection rather than by separate words

Examples

Nonanalytical language structure
Latin is considered a nonanalytical language because it relies heavily on inflectional endings.
In a nonanalytical system, word endings change to show tense, case, or number.
Many ancient languages are nonanalytical, unlike modern English, which is more analytic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: synthetic - describing languages that use inflection to express grammatical relationships
