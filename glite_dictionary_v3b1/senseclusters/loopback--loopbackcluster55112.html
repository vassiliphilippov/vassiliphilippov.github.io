loopback

noun
data sent to source
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈluːp.bæk/ (AmE), /ˈluːp.bæk/ (BrE)
a process in which data is sent back to its source, often for testing or communication purposes in computing or networking

Examples

Loopback data check
The technician performed a loopback to ensure the network cable was functioning correctly.
A loopback address allows a computer to send messages to itself for testing.
Engineers often use loopback tests to diagnose communication problems in hardware devices.
