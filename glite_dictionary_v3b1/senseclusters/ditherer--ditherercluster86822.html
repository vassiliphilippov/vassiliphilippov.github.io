ditherer

noun
noise-adding device
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪðərər / (AmE), / ˈdɪðərə / (BrE)
a device, program, or algorithm that adds small amounts of noise to digital audio or image signals to reduce distortion during processing or conversion

Examples

Audio ditherer
The audio engineer used a ditherer to improve the quality of the final recording.
A ditherer can help prevent unwanted artifacts when converting high-resolution images to lower bit depths.
Modern editing software often includes a built-in ditherer for processing digital signals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dither - to add noise to a digital signal to reduce distortion
  • antonym: quantizer - a device or algorithm that converts a signal into discrete values
