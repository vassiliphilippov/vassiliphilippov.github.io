cyclodestructive surgery

noun
surgery to treat glaucoma by destroying ciliary body
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkloʊdɪˈstrʌktɪv ˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkləʊdɪˈstrʌktɪv ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for treating glaucoma by destroying part of the ciliary body, usually using a laser, to reduce intraocular pressure

Examples

Cyclodestructive surgery for glaucoma
Advances in laser technology have made cyclodestructive surgery a safer option for those with refractory glaucoma.
After undergoing cyclodestructive surgery, the patient's intraocular pressure significantly decreased.
The ophthalmologist recommended cyclodestructive surgery to manage the patient's severe glaucoma.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glaucoma - a condition of increased pressure in the eyeball causing gradual loss of sight
