crazing

noun
fine surface cracks
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
a network of fine cracks that appears on the surface of materials like pottery, glass, or glaze

Examples

Crazing on pottery
The old ceramic plate had visible crazing that made it look even more antique.
Experts examined the glass for signs of crazing before declaring it safe to use.
Prolonged exposure to sunlight can cause crazing on painted surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cracking - the process of breaking or splitting into cracks
  • synonym: fracture - a break or crack in a hard material
