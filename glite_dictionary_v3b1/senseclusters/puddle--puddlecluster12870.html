puddle

verb
form or make a small pool of liquid
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌdəl /; / ˈpʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈpʌdəl /; / ˈpʌd.əl / (BrE)
to form or cause to form a small pool of liquid, especially water, on a surface

Examples

Water puddles on floor
After the dog jumped in, the floor began to puddle with muddy water.
If you splash too much, you'll puddle the kitchen floor.
The children loved to puddle in the rain after the storm.
Heavy rain can puddle the ground, making it difficult to walk through.
The water puddled in the old tire, attracting mosquitoes.
Too much irrigation will puddle the soil, damaging plant roots.
The heavy rain puddled the playground, making it unusable for the day.
After the pipe burst, water puddled the basement floor and ruined the carpet.
The gardener accidentally puddled the flower beds by overwatering them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - to remove moisture from something
  • synonym: pool - a small area of still water
  • synonym: soak - to make something very wet by immersing it in liquid
