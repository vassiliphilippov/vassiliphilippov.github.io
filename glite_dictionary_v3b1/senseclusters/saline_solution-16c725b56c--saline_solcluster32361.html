saline solution

noun
sterile salt and water solution for medical use
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪlin səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈseɪliːn səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
A sterile solution of sodium chloride (salt) in distilled water, typically used for medical purposes such as cleaning wounds or intravenous infusion.

Examples

A saline solution drip
Doctors often use saline solution as an intravenous fluid for dehydrated patients.
Rinsing contact lenses with saline solution helps to keep them clean and safe to wear.
The nurse cleaned the wound with saline solution to prevent infection.
