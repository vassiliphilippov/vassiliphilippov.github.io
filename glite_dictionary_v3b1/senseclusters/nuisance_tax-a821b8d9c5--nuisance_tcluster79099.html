nuisance tax

noun
minor tax on specific purchases
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːsəns tæks / (AmE), / ˈnjuːsəns tæks / (BrE)
A minor tax imposed on certain goods or transactions, often based on the cost of the purchased item and collected directly from the buyer.

Examples

A nuisance tax on goods
Many consumers complained about the new nuisance tax added to small online purchases.
Some argue that the nuisance tax on plastic bags helps to reduce waste.
The city council introduced a nuisance tax on bottled water to encourage the use of tap water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sales tax - a tax on sales or on the receipts from sales
