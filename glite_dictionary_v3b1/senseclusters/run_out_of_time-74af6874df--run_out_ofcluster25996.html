run out of time

verb
have no time left; reach a deadline
Pronunciation: / rʌn aʊt əv taɪm / (AmE), / rʌn aʊt ɒv taɪm / (BrE)
to have no time left to complete something or reach a deadline

Examples

To run out of time in exam
He was so focused on details that he ran out of time during the exam.
They realized they were running out of time to submit their application.
We can't afford to run out of time before finishing the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be out of time - to have no time remaining
  • antonym: have time - to possess time to spare or complete something
