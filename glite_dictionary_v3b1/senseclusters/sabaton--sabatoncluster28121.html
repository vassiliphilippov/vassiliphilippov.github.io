sabaton

noun
armored foot covering (historical armor)
Pronunciation: / ˈsæbəˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈsæbətən / (BrE)
A piece of armor made to protect the foot, typically consisting of mail and solid plates covering the toe and heel, used especially in medieval times.

Examples

A sabaton piece
During battle reenactments, participants often wear replica sabatons to protect their feet.
Many museum collections include a complete suit of armor with ornate sabatons.
The knight’s sabaton glinted in the sunlight as he strode across the field.
