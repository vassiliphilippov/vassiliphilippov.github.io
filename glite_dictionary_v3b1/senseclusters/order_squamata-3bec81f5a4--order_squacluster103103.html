order Squamata

noun
taxonomic group: snakes and lizards
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɚ skwəˈmeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə skwəˈmeɪtə / (BrE)
the zoological order of diapsid reptiles that includes snakes and lizards

Examples

The order Squamata reptiles

The order Squamata contains both snakes and lizards, making it one of the largest groups of reptiles.
The order Squamata is distinguished by members having scales and, in most cases, movable quadrate bones.
