subjecthood

noun
state of being a subject
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbdʒɪkthʊd / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɪkthʊd / (BrE)
the state or condition of being a subject, especially in grammar or philosophy

Examples

Philosophical subjecthood
The philosopher explored the idea of subjecthood in relation to personal identity.
In linguistics, subjecthood refers to the grammatical role of the subject in a sentence.
Her research focused on how subjecthood is expressed in different cultural contexts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objecthood - the state or condition of being an object
  • synonym: subjectivity - the quality of being based on or influenced by personal feelings or opinions
