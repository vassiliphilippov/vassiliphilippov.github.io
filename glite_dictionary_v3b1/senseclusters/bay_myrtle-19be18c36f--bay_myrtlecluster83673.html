bay myrtle

noun
aromatic evergreen shrub of southeastern US
Pronunciation: / beɪ ˈmɜr.tl̩ / (AmE), / beɪ ˈmɜː.tl̩ / (BrE)
an evergreen aromatic shrub or small tree of the southeastern United States, with small hard berries thickly coated with white wax used for making candles

Examples

A bay myrtle bush
A row of bay myrtle bushes lines the edge of the old plantation garden.
Early settlers used the berries of the bay myrtle to make candles for lighting their homes.
The aromatic leaves of the bay myrtle are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
