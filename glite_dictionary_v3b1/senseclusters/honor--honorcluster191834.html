honor

noun
a woman's chastity or virtue (archaic)
2.1 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.nər/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.nə/ (BrE)
(archaic) A woman's virtue or chastity, especially as regarded in terms of her reputation.

Examples

A woman's honor
He was accused of besmirching her honor with rumors and lies.
In Victorian novels, a woman's honor was considered vital to her family's reputation.
She defended her honor against the unjust accusations of impropriety.
Defending one's honour was often depicted as a matter of life and death in ancient tales.
In the old novel, the family was concerned with restoring their daughter's honour after the scandal.
She felt her honour had been called into question by the townsfolk's cruel gossip.
