host

noun
archaic term for army
Pronunciation: / hoʊst / (AmE), / həʊst / (BrE)
a large group of people or things; a multitude or army (literary/archaic)

Examples

A host of people
A great host gathered on the plains before the city walls.
Medieval kings often depended on the loyalty of their host to win battles.
The chronicles describe a vast host advancing across the countryside.
A host of tourists arrived in the city for the festival.
She faced a host of challenges during her first year at the job.
There are a host of reasons why the plan might not succeed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: army - an organized military force
  • antonym: few - a small number of things or people
  • synonym: horde - a large group, often used for troops or warriors
  • synonym: multitude - a large number of people or things
  • synonym: myriad - a countless or extremely great number
