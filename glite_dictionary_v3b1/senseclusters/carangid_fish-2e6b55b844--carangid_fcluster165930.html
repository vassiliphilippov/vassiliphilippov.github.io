carangid fish

noun
fish of the Carangidae family
Pronunciation: / kəˈræn.dʒɪd fɪʃ / (AmE), / kəˈran.dʒɪd fɪʃ / (BrE)
Any marine fish belonging to the family Carangidae, such as jacks, pompanos, and trevallies.

Examples

A carangid fish
A carangid fish can often be recognized by its streamlined body and forked tail.

Some carangid fish are prized catches for sport fishermen due to their speed and strength.
