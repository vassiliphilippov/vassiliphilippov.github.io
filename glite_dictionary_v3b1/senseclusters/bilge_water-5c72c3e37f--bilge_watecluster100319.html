bilge water

noun
water collected at the bottom of a ship
7.3
Pronunciation: / bɪldʒ ˌwɑtɚ / (AmE), / bɪldʒ ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that has collected in the bilge, the lowest part of a ship, often contaminated with oil, dirt, or other substances

Examples

Pump out bilge water
After several days at sea, the bilge water needed to be pumped out to prevent flooding.
Regulations require proper disposal of bilge water to avoid polluting the ocean.
The unpleasant smell coming from the hold was caused by stagnant bilge water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bilge - the lowest compartment on a ship where water collects
