Joseph Marie Jacquard

proper noun
French inventor of the Jacquard loom
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌʒoʊzəf məˈri ʒaˈkɑrd / (AmE), / ˌʒəʊzɛf maˈriː ʒaˈkɑːd / (BrE)
(1752–1834) A French inventor known for creating the Jacquard loom, which automated the weaving of complex patterns.

Examples

Joseph Marie Jacquard inventor
Historians often credit Joseph Marie Jacquard as a pioneer in early programmable technology.
Many museums display portraits of Joseph Marie Jacquard alongside his innovative loom.
The invention of the Jacquard loom by Joseph Marie Jacquard revolutionized the textile industry.
