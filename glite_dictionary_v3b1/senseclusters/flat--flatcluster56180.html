flat

adjective
lowered by a semitone (music)
3.5
Pronunciation: / flæt / (AmE), / flæt / (BrE)
(of a musical note) lower in pitch by a semitone; denoted by the '♭' symbol in musical notation.

Examples

B flat note
If you play a B flat, you must lower the pitch by one semitone.
The note was slightly flat throughout the performance, causing it to sound off-key.
The orchestra tuned carefully to avoid any flat notes during the concert.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sharp - (of a musical note) raised in pitch by a semitone
