consecrated

adjective
dedicated for sacred use
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnsəˌkreɪtɪd /; / ˈkɑːn.sɪˌkreɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɪˌkreɪtɪd /; / ˈkɒn.sɪˌkreɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
made or declared sacred, especially for a religious purpose

Examples

A consecrated place
She regarded her work in the orphanage as a consecrated mission.
The church was officially consecrated in a ceremony attended by local dignitaries.
The grounds are consecrated and only members of the parish may be buried there.
Only the consecrated bread and wine are used during the religious service.
She walked quietly past the consecrated ground where her ancestors were buried.
The newly built cathedral was consecrated in a grand ceremony attended by hundreds of worshippers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dedicated - devoted to a particular task or purpose
  • antonym: desecrated - treated a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect
  • antonym: profane - not sacred or religious; secular
  • synonym: sacred - connected with God or a deity; considered holy
  • synonym: sanctified - made holy by religious rite
