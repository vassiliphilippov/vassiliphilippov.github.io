bencher

noun
senior legal official
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈbɛntʃə / (BrE)
a senior member of the governing body of an Inn of Court, responsible for overseeing and disciplining barristers

Examples

A senior bencher
After years of distinguished service, she became a bencher at Lincoln's Inn.
The bencher presided over the disciplinary hearing for the young barrister.
He was honored to be elected as a bencher by his peers in the legal profession.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: barrister - a lawyer entitled to represent clients in higher courts
  • degree higher: judge - an official who presides over court proceedings
