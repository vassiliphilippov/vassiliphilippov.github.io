drumbeat

noun
military drum signal at sundown
1.6 0.41/m

the sound or act of beating a drum, especially repeatedly (such as in music or as a signal)

Examples

Steady drumbeat
A single steady drumbeat echoed across the field, calling everyone to attention.
At sunset, the soldiers stood silent as the drumbeat signaled the lowering of the flag.
The ceremonial drumbeat marked the end of the day's duties at the fort.
A rapid drumbeat signaled the beginning of the parade.
He listened to the distant drumbeat coming from the festival grounds.
The steady drumbeat echoed through the hall during the ceremony.

Synonyms

  • roll
    2.7 5.50/m
    a roll is a long, fast, continuous drum sound that builds tension, while a drumbeat is any single or repeated strike on a drum.
  • drumming
    1.8 0.11/m
    drumming is the act of playing drums to create rhythms and music, while a drumbeat is the specific sound or hit you hear.
