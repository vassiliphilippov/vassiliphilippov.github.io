prereading

verb
to read in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
to read something in advance, especially before an event, meeting, or formal study.

Examples

To preread chapters
She spent the afternoon prereading the chapters before the class discussion.
The manager asked everyone to finish prereading the report before tomorrow's meeting.
By prereading the material, he felt more confident during the seminar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preview - to look at or read something before it is officially available or required
  • antonym: review - to read or look at something again for study or checking
