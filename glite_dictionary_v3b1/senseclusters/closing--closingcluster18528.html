closing

adjective
final; concluding
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊ.zɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
coming at the end of an event, activity, or period; final or concluding.

Examples

Closing moments
In the closing minutes of the game, the team scored the winning goal.
The novel's closing chapters reveal the fate of the main characters.
The president delivered his closing remarks at the end of the conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: final - coming at the end; last
  • antonym: opening - serving as a beginning; first
