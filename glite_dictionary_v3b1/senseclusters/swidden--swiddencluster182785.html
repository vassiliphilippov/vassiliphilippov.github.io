swidden

noun
slash-and-burn field
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈswɪd.ən/ (AmE), /ˈswɪd.ən/ (BrE)
a piece of land cleared for farming by cutting and burning vegetation, especially for temporary or shifting agriculture

Examples

A swidden field
Farmers created a new swidden each season to grow their crops in the rainforest.
After several years, the old swidden was left to regrow before being used again.
The villagers relied on swidden agriculture to provide food for their families.
The villagers created a new swidden to grow their crops after the forest was cleared.
After harvesting, the old swidden was left to regrow before being used again.
Traditional farmers often rely on the swidden method to prepare land for planting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallow - land left unplanted to restore its fertility
  • synonym: field - an area of open land used for farming
  • synonym: slash-and-burn - a method of agriculture involving cutting and burning plants to clear land
