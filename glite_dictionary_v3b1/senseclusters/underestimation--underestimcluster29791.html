underestimation

noun
an estimate lower than reality
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˌɛstəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌɛstəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
an estimate that is lower than the actual value or reality

Examples

Early underestimation of damage
Early underestimation of the storm's severity put many residents at risk.
His underestimation of the project's complexity led to unexpected delays.
The company suffered losses due to their underestimation of production costs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overestimation - an estimate that is higher than the actual value
