triumphator

noun
Roman victory honoree
9.9
Pronunciation: / traɪˌʌmfəˈtɔr / (AmE), / traɪˌʌmfəˈtɔː / (BrE)
a person, especially a general, who was awarded a triumph in ancient Rome for a great military victory

Examples

The Senate welcomed the triumphator with a grand procession through the city.
As a triumphator, he wore a laurel crown and rode in a decorated chariot.
Ancient Roman citizens cheered for the triumphator after his successful campaign.
