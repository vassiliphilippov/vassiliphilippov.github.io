nondogmatic

adjective
open-minded
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɔgˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɒgˈmætɪk / (BrE)
willing to consider different ideas and not rigid or fixed in beliefs or opinions

Examples

A nondogmatic style
The manager's nondogmatic style allowed team members to share their ideas freely.
A nondogmatic attitude is important when working with people from different backgrounds.
He remained nondogmatic and listened carefully to all sides of the debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dogmatic - unwilling to consider other ideas; rigid in beliefs
  • synonym: flexible - able to change or adapt easily
  • synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas
