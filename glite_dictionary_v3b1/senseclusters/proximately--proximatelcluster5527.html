proximately

adverb
directly causing
8.6 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêks…™m…ôtli / (AmE), / Ààpr…íks…™m…ôtli / (BrE)
in a way that is directly and immediately related to the cause of something, especially in legal contexts.

Examples

Proximately caused by negligence
The injury was proximately caused by the unsafe working conditions at the factory.
In law, damages are only awarded if the harm was proximately caused by the defendant's actions.
The judge ruled that the accident was proximately related to the company's failure to maintain equipment.

Synonyms

  • proximally
    7.9 0.02/m
    proximally means nearer in origin or a primary factor in general use, while proximately is the legal word for a direct causal link.

Related Terms

  • synonym: directly - in a direct manner; without anything in between
  • antonym: indirectly - not in a direct way; through something else
