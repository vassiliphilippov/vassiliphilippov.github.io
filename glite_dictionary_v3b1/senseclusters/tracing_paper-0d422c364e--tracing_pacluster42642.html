tracing paper

noun
thin semitransparent paper for copying drawings
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪsɪŋ ˌpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsɪŋ ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a very thin, semitransparent paper used for copying drawings or designs by tracing over them

Examples

Tracing paper sheet
Architects often use tracing paper to revise their blueprints.
He bought a roll of tracing paper for his design class.
She placed a sheet of tracing paper over the drawing to copy its outline.
