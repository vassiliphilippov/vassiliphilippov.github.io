College of Cardinals

noun
body of Catholic Church cardinals
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːlɪdʒ əv ˈkɑːrdn̩əlz / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪdʒ əv ˈkɑːdn̩əlz / (BrE)
the body of all cardinals in the Roman Catholic Church, responsible for electing and advising the pope

Examples

The College of Cardinals
Membership in the College of Cardinals is one of the highest honors in the Catholic Church.
The College of Cardinals also serves to advise the pope on important matters of doctrine and governance.
The College of Cardinals convened in Rome to elect a new pope after the previous pope's resignation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cardinal - a senior official of the Roman Catholic Church, appointed by the pope
