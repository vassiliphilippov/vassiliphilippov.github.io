dipeptide

noun
two-amino-acid molecule
Pronunciation: / daɪˈpɛptaɪd / (AmE), / daɪˈpɛptaɪd / (BrE)
a molecule formed by two amino acids joined together by a single peptide bond

Examples

A dipeptide molecule
The scientist analyzed the structure of the dipeptide under a microscope.
A dipeptide is created when two amino acids are linked by a peptide bond.
Researchers studied how the dipeptide reacted with different enzymes.
