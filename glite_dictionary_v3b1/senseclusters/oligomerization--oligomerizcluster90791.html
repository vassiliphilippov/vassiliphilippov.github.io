oligomerization

noun
molecule combining process
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌlɪɡəˌmɛrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌlɪɡəˌmɛrɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a small number of molecules join together to form a larger, complex structure

Examples

Protein oligomerization
The oligomerization of proteins can influence their biological activity in the cell.
Researchers studied the oligomerization process to understand how certain enzymes assemble.
Abnormal oligomerization may lead to diseases related to protein misfolding.

  • degree higher: polymerization - the process of combining many small molecules to form a large chain or network
