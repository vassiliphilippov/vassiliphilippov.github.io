Allhallows Eve

noun
evening before All Saints' Day; Halloween
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔlˈhæloʊz iːv / (AmE), / ˌɔːlˈhæləz iːv / (BrE)
The evening before All Saints' Day, traditionally observed on October 31st; also known as Halloween, often marked by celebrations and pranks.

Examples

Allhallows Eve celebrations
Many traditions associated with Allhallows Eve stem from ancient Celtic festivals.
On Allhallows Eve, children dress in costumes and go trick-or-treating.
The village organizes a bonfire every Allhallows Eve to celebrate the occasion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Halloween - the night of October 31st, celebrated with costumes and trick-or-treating
