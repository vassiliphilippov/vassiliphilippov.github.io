bring down the house

verb
receive loud applause; entertain or impress greatly
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ daʊn ðə haʊs / (AmE), / brɪŋ daʊn ðə haʊs / (BrE)
to receive an enthusiastic, loud or prolonged applause or appreciation from an audience; to entertain or impress greatly

Examples

To bring down the house with applause
The lead actor's dramatic monologue brought down the house on opening night.
The young violinist's stunning solo brought down the house at the final concert.
Their dance routine brought down the house and earned a standing ovation.
