Scotch woodcock

noun
British dish: scrambled eggs and anchovy toast
Pronunciation: / skɑtʃ ˈwʊdkɑk / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ˈwʊdkɒk / (BrE)
A traditional British dish consisting of creamy scrambled eggs served on toast spread with anchovy paste.

Examples

Scotch woodcock dish
For breakfast, she made Scotch woodcock with fresh eggs and anchovy paste.
The traditional recipe for Scotch woodcock is a favorite in some English colleges.
You can try Scotch woodcock as an alternative to the usual eggs on toast.
