prewarn

verb
to warn in advance
4.2
Pronunciation: / priːˈwɔrn / (AmE), / priːˈwɔːn / (BrE)
to warn someone in advance about a possible danger, problem, or event

Examples

To prewarn of danger
The emergency team is prewarning villagers of the expected flood so they can evacuate early.
Teachers often prewarn students about upcoming tests to help them prepare.
Authorities will prewarn residents if severe weather is approaching the area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - to make someone aware of a danger or problem
  • synonym: warn - to tell someone about a possible danger or problem
