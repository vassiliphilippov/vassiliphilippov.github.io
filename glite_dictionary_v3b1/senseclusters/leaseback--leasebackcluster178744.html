leaseback

noun
property sale and rental
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈliːs.bæk/ (AmE), /ˈliːs.bæk/ (BrE)
an arrangement in which property is sold and then immediately rented back by the seller from the buyer

Examples

Leaseback agreement
The company arranged a leaseback to continue using its office after selling the building.
A leaseback can help businesses raise funds without relocating their operations.
After the leaseback, the original owner became the tenant of the property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rental - the act of paying to use something for a period of time
  • synonym: sale and leaseback - a transaction where an asset is sold and then leased back by the seller
