drive around

verb
take someone for a ride in a vehicle
Pronunciation: / draɪv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / draɪv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to take someone on a trip or for transport in a vehicle, usually for leisure or while showing places of interest

Examples

To drive around a visitor
He drove around his friend to see the university campus before classes started.
She offered to drive around her parents when they visited the city.
Tourists love to be driven around in open-top buses on warm days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chauffeur - to drive someone else as a paid job or service
