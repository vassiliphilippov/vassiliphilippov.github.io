babesiosis

noun
tick-borne disease
Pronunciation: / bəˌbiːziˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / bəˌbiːsiˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease in humans or animals caused by Babesia parasites, usually transmitted by ticks and leading to fever and anemia

Examples

Babesiosis disease
The doctor diagnosed her with babesiosis after she developed a high fever and fatigue.
People who spend time in wooded areas should be aware of the risk of babesiosis from tick bites.
Early treatment is important to prevent complications from babesiosis.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with babesiosis after noticing its high fever and pale gums.
Farmers are advised to check their cattle regularly for ticks to prevent babesiosis outbreaks.
In some regions, babesiosis can also infect humans, especially those with weakened immune systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anaplasmosis - a tick-borne disease affecting animals and humans, causing fever and anemia
  • synonym: lyme disease - a tick-borne disease caused by bacteria
  • synonym: malaria - a disease caused by parasites that infect red blood cells, transmitted by mosquitoes
