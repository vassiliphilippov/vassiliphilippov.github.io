drip

noun
protective projection on a cornice or sill
Pronunciation: / drɪp / (AmE), / drɪp / (BrE)
(architecture) A projection from a cornice or sill designed to direct rainwater away from the building, protecting the area below (such as over a window or doorway).

Examples

A window drip
Architects paid careful attention to the design of the drip above the entryway.
Over time, the drip on the stone sill eroded but continued to keep the wall below dry.
The old mansion featured a drip over every window to prevent water damage to the façade.
