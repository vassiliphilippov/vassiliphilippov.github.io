isolative

adjective
analytic language type
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈaɪsələtɪv / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a language in which words are typically single morphemes and grammatical relationships are shown by word order rather than inflection

Examples

Isolative language type
Vietnamese is considered an isolative language because it uses word order instead of inflection.
In isolative languages, each word usually consists of a single morpheme.
Chinese is another example of an isolative language with minimal grammatical endings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analytic - relating to a language type with little or no inflection
  • antonym: synthetic - relating to a language type with many inflections
