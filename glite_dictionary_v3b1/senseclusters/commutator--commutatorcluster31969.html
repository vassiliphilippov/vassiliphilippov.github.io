commutator

noun
switch reversing electric current direction
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmjəˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmjʊteɪtə / (BrE)
a device or switch used to reverse the direction of an electric current, especially in electric motors or generators.

Examples

Electric commutator device
He carefully cleaned the commutator to ensure smooth operation of the electric generator.
If the commutator is damaged, the motor will not function properly.
The commutator is essential for changing the direction of current in a DC motor.
