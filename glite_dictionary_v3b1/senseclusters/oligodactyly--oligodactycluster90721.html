oligodactyly

noun
congenital absence of some fingers or toes
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊlɪɡoʊˈdæk.tɪ.li / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəʊˈdæk.tɪli / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which some fingers or toes are missing.

Examples

Congenital oligodactyly condition
Cases of oligodactyly are often detected during routine prenatal scans.
He became an advocate for children born with oligodactyly after his daughter was diagnosed.
The doctor diagnosed the newborn with oligodactyly affecting her left hand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polydactyly - a congenital condition in which there are extra fingers or toes
