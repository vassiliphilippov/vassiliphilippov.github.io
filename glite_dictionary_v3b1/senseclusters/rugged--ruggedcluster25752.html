(of land or terrain) having a rough and uneven surface; rocky or broken.

Examples

Rugged mountain path
The hikers had difficulty crossing the rugged mountain pass after the storm.
The landscape became increasingly rugged as we approached the highlands.
We camped on a rugged stretch of coastline, surrounded by cliffs and boulders.

Synonyms

  • craggy
    5.7 1.31/m
    craggy is full of sharp rocks and steep faces, while rugged means generally rough, uneven land that can be rocky or broken.
  • broken
    3.6 0.09/m
    broken describes land with many uneven parts and abrupt drops, while rugged refers to rough, often rocky terrain as a whole.
  • rocky
    1.0 9.30/m
    rocky means covered in stones or large rocks, while rugged focuses on a rough, uneven surface that need not be all rock.
  • rubbly
    5.1 0.03/m
    rubbly is covered in loose stones and small broken rock, while rugged describes general rough, uneven land possibly with larger features.
