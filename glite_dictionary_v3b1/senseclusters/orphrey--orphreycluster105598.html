orphrey

noun
richly embroidered vestment edging
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrfri / (AmE), / ˈɔːfri / (BrE)
A decorative band or edging, richly embroidered, especially on ecclesiastical vestments.

Examples

An orphrey band
An antique vestment with its original orphrey is especially valuable to collectors.
Medieval churches often invested in intricate orphreys for their altar cloths.
The bishop's robe was adorned with a gold orphrey along the hem.
