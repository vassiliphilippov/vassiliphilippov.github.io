precut

verb
to cut in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈkʌt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkʌt / (BrE)
to cut something in advance, before it is needed or before a later stage of processing or assembly

Examples

To precut the wood
The factory workers precut the fabric to speed up the assembly process.
For convenience, they precut the vegetables before starting to cook the meal.
All the wooden boards were precut to the correct size before delivery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - to divide or shape something with a sharp tool
  • synonym: prepare - to make something ready in advance
