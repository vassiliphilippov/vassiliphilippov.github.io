kerf

noun
width of a cut
9.8
Pronunciation: / kɜrf / (AmE), / kɜːf / (BrE)
the width of a cut or the amount of material removed by a saw or cutting tool

Examples

Kerf width
The kerf left by the saw was about two millimeters wide.
Make sure the kerf is straight to ensure the pieces fit together properly.
A wider blade will create a larger kerf in the wood.
The kerf left by the saw blade was wider than expected, so the pieces did not fit together.
Always consider the kerf when measuring wood for a project to avoid running short.
A thinner blade produces a smaller kerf, resulting in less wasted material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - an opening or incision made by a sharp tool
  • synonym: gap - an opening or space between two objects
  • synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface
  • synonym: waste - material that is not used and is discarded
