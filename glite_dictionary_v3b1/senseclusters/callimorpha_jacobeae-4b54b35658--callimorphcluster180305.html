Callimorpha jacobeae

noun
species of European moth used to control ragwort
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæ.ləˈmɔːr.fə ˌdʒæ.kəˈbi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈmɔːfə ˌdʒækəˈbiːiː / (BrE)
a large red-and-black European moth, whose larvae feed on ragwort; introduced to the United States as a biological control for ragwort

Examples

The Callimorpha jacobeae moth
Biologists introduced Callimorpha jacobeae to North America to help manage invasive ragwort populations.
Farmers welcomed the arrival of Callimorpha jacobeae as an effective, natural means of controlling ragwort.
The caterpillars of the Callimorpha jacobeae are easily recognized by their yellow and black stripes.
