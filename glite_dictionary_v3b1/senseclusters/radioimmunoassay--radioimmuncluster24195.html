radioimmunoassay

noun
lab technique using radioactive antibodies
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊɪˌmjunəʊəˈseɪ / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊɪˌmjuːnəʊəˈseɪ / (BrE)
a laboratory technique for measuring concentrations of substances, such as hormones, in the blood by using antibodies and radioactively labeled substances

Examples

A radioimmunoassay test
Advances in radioimmunoassay have made diagnostic tests more sensitive and accurate.
Our research relied on radioimmunoassay techniques to detect the presence of the virus in blood samples.
The laboratory used a radioimmunoassay to measure the patient's hormone levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ELISA - enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay, a non-radioactive alternative
