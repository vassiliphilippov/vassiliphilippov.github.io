seigniorage

noun
protocol revenue from issuing tokens
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈseɪ.n.jə.rɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈseɪ.n.jə.rɪdʒ/ (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency and blockchain contexts) revenue or value captured by a protocol or platform when it issues new tokens or expands the token supply, often used to fund a treasury, pay contributors, or stabilize the system.

Examples

Crypto seigniorage revenue
The protocol's seigniorage mechanism converts newly issued tokens into revenue for its treasury.
During network growth, the DAO used seigniorage to fund development and pay contributors.
Investors debated whether the project's inflationary seigniorage model would harm long-term token value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seigniorage (government) - profit made by a government from issuing currency, especially the difference between face value and production cost
