cursorily

adverb
in a hasty or superficial manner
7.7 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…úr.s…ö.…™.li / (AmE), / Ààk…úÀê.s…ôr.…™.li / (BrE)
In a quick and superficial way, without paying close attention or taking care.

Examples

To clean cursorily
He cleaned the room cursorily, leaving dust in the corners.
She glanced cursorily at the report before the meeting started.
The document was only cursorily reviewed, so many errors went unnoticed.

Synonyms

  • superficially
    5.3 1.78/m
    superficially is concerned only with surface details and appearances, while cursorily adds the idea of doing something quickly and without care.
  • perfunctorily
    9.4 0.26/m
    perfunctorily means doing something as a routine with little interest, while cursorily focuses on doing it quickly and not deeply.
  • glancingly
    6.7 0.02/m
    glancingly means mentioning or seeing something briefly and in passing, while cursorily implies quick and superficial attention.
