Pyrularia pubera

noun
a parasitic shrub of the southeastern US
9.9
Pronunciation: / paɪˌruːˈlɛriə pjuːˈbɛrə / (AmE), / paɪˌruːˈlɛəriə pjuːˈbɪərə / (BrE)
A shrub native to the southeastern United States, parasitic on the roots of hemlocks, with sparse spikes of greenish flowers and pulpy drupes.

Examples

Pyrularia pubera shrub
Pyrularia pubera can often be found growing near hemlock trees in the Appalachian region.
Botanists studied how Pyrularia pubera parasitizes the roots of its host trees.
The drupes of Pyrularia pubera are a distinguishing feature of the plant.
