aghast

adjective
filled with shock or horror
Pronunciation: / əˈɡæst / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːst / (BrE)
filled with shock, horror, or amazement; struck with fear or consternation

Examples

Look aghast
He listened aghast as the bad news was delivered over the phone.
She stood aghast when she saw the destruction caused by the storm.
The audience was aghast at the shocking revelation during the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appalled - greatly shocked or horrified
  • synonym: astonished - very surprised or amazed
  • antonym: delighted - greatly pleased or happy
