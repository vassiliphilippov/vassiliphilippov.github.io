separation

noun
legal cessation of cohabitation by spouses
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) The situation in which a married couple stops living together, either by mutual agreement or by a court order, without being formally divorced.

Examples

Legal separation
A separation can sometimes help spouses work through their issues without ending the marriage.
After years of unhappiness, they agreed to a separation before considering divorce.
The court granted a legal separation to the couple last month.

  • degree higher: divorce - the legal dissolution of a marriage
