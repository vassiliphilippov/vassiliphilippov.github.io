Western sand cherry

noun
dwarf shrub with sweet edible fruit
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn sænd ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən sænd ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A dwarf ornamental shrub native to the western United States, known for its large black, red, or yellow sweet edible fruits.

Examples

The Western sand cherry fruit
Farmers sometimes harvest the fruit of the Western sand cherry to make jams and preserves.
In its native habitat, the Western sand cherry thrives in sandy soils and open areas.
The Western sand cherry is often planted in gardens for both its fruit and attractive appearance.
