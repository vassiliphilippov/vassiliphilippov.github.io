premarket

verb
to advertise before launch
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈmɑːrkɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
to promote, advertise, or offer a product or service before its official public launch

Examples

To premarket a product
The company decided to premarket its new smartphone to generate excitement before the official release.
They will premarket the software to select clients for early feedback.
By premarketing the product, the team hopes to build anticipation among potential customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: launch - to officially introduce a product or service to the public
  • synonym: promote - to advertise or publicize a product or service
