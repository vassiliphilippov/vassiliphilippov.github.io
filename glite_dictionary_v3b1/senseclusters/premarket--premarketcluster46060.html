premarket

verb
to advertise before launch
6.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpriÀêÀàm…ëÀêrk…™t / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀêÀàm…ëÀêk…™t / (BrE)
to promote, advertise, or offer a product or service before its official public launch

Examples

To premarket a product
The company decided to premarket its new smartphone to generate excitement before the official release.
They will premarket the software to select clients for early feedback.
By premarketing the product, the team hopes to build anticipation among potential customers.

Synonyms

  • prelaunch
    5.1 0.00/m
    prelaunch means to promote or release something to a limited group before full launch, while premarket focuses on advertising or offering it publicly beforehand.
  • presell
    4.8 0.02/m
    presell means selling a product to customers before it is available, while premarket refers to promoting or offering it ahead of launch.
  • prerelease
    4.9 0.01/m
    prerelease means giving early access to a product to a few people before launch, while premarket emphasizes promotion or offering broadly.
  • preview
    2.9 8.98/m
    preview is an advance showing or short advertisement for a film or play, while premarket applies to promoting or offering products before launch.
