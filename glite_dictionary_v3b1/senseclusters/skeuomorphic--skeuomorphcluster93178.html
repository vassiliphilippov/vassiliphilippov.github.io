skeuomorphic

adjective
imitating real objects
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåskjuÀê…ôÀàm…îÀêrf…™k / (AmE), / ÀåskjuÀê…ôÀàm…îÀêf…™k / (BrE)
designed to imitate the appearance or style of another material or object, especially in digital interfaces

Examples

skeuomorphic design style
The designer chose a skeuomorphic style, making the digital notebook look like a real paper one.
Many early smartphone apps featured skeuomorphic icons that resembled physical objects.
Some users prefer skeuomorphic interfaces because they feel more familiar and intuitive.

Synonyms

  • simulative
    7.5 0.01/m
    simulative copies how something looks or works to imitate a real item, while skeuomorphic names that style in digital design.
  • mimetic
    8.1 1.37/m
    mimetic means copying natural or artistic forms in general, while skeuomorphic specifically describes imitation of real materials in digital interfaces.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minimalist - using simple and basic design elements
  • synonym: realistic - closely resembling real life or objects
