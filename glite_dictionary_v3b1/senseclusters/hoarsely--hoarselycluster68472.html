hoarsely

adverb
in a rough or husky voice
4.9 0.90/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…îrsli / (AmE), / Ààh…îÀêsli / (BrE)
in a rough or harsh-sounding voice, as if affected by a sore throat or fatigue

Examples

To speak hoarsely
'Leave me alone,' he muttered hoarsely in the dim light.
He tried to speak, but his voice came out hoarsely after hours of shouting.
She whispered hoarsely, barely able to make herself heard.

Synonyms

  • huskily
    6.0 0.18/m
    huskily is low, rough, and often breathy in speech, while hoarsely sounds strained as from a sore throat.
  • throatily
    5.8 0.03/m
    throatily sounds deep and like it comes from the throat, while hoarsely suggests a tired or sore voice.
  • raspingly
    8.2 0.01/m
    raspingly is harsh, grating, and irritating to hear, while hoarsely is rough but often sounds tired rather than sharp.
