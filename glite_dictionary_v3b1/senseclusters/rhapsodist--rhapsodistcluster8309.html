rhapsodist

noun
enthusiastic speaker
Pronunciation: / ˈræpsədɪst / (AmE), / ˈræpsədɪst / (BrE)
a person who speaks or writes in an extremely enthusiastic, ecstatic, or extravagant manner

Examples

Rhapsodist speaks passionately
The poet was known as a rhapsodist for his passionate readings at public events.
Some listeners found the speaker to be a true rhapsodist, swept up in his own excitement.
Her review was so glowing that she was called a rhapsodist by her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: critic - a person who judges or evaluates something, often with restraint
  • synonym: enthusiast - a person who shows intense and eager enjoyment
