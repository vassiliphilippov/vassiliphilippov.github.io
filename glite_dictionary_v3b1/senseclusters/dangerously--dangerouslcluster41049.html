dangerously

adverb

In a way that is likely to cause harm or injury; in a dangerous manner.

Examples

To be dangerously ill
She was dangerously ill for several weeks but eventually recovered.
The car skidded dangerously close to the edge of the cliff during the storm.
The chemicals were stored dangerously near a heat source, posing a serious risk.

Synonyms

  • perilously
    7.1 1.03/m
    perilously describes being in great immediate danger or near harm, while dangerously means acting in a way likely to cause harm.
  • hazardously
    6.8 0.02/m
    hazardously is often used in technical or legal contexts to mark risky conditions, while dangerously is a general word for harmful action.
  • unsafely
    2.8 0.06/m
    unsafely describes failing to be safe through poor care or rules, while dangerously stresses the chance of real harm or injury.
  • riskily
    6.4 0.02/m
    riskily shows taking a choice that may bring danger, while dangerously focuses on acting in a way likely to cause harm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hazardously - in a way that involves risk or danger
  • antonym: safely - in a manner that avoids danger
