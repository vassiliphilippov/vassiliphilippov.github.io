ostentatious

adjective
intended to impress or attract notice
7.7 1.07/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…ëÀêst…ôn'te…™ É…ôs /; / Àå…ëÀê.st…õnÀàte…™. É…ôs / (AmE), / Àå…íst…ônÀàte…™ É…ôs /; / Àå…ís.t…õnÀàte…™. É…ôs / (BrE)
showy or done in a way that is meant to attract attention or impress others, often in a pretentious or exaggerated way

Examples

An ostentatious display
His ostentatious display of wealth made him unpopular with his neighbors.
She wore an ostentatious diamond necklace to the gala.
The hotel lobby was decorated in an ostentatious manner, with gold accents everywhere.
His ostentatious generosity was intended to impress rather than to help.
She wore an ostentatious necklace encrusted with huge diamonds to the party.
The hotel lobby was decorated with ostentatious gold fixtures and velvet drapes.
