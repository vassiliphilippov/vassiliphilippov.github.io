sarcastic

adjective
expressing sarcasm or ridicule
7.6
Pronunciation: / sɑrˈkæstɪk / (AmE), / sɑːˈkæstɪk / (BrE)
using or showing sarcasm; expressing ridicule or irony, often in a way that can hurt or offend

Examples

Sarcastic tone
His sarcastic tone made it clear he didn't really mean what he said.
She gave a sarcastic comment when asked about the meeting's productivity.
Whenever he's upset, he tends to make sarcastic remarks that sting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ironic - expressing meaning by using language that means the opposite, often for humorous effect
  • antonym: sincere - expressing genuine feelings without irony or pretense
