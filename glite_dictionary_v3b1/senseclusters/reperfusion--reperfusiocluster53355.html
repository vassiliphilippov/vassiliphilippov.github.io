reperfusion

noun
restoring blood flow
7.0 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêp…ôrÀàfjuÀê í…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêp…ôÀàfjuÀê í…ôn / (BrE)
the restoration of blood flow to an organ or tissue after it has been reduced or blocked

Examples

Rapid reperfusion therapy
Rapid reperfusion after a stroke can greatly improve a patient's chances of recovery.
Doctors monitor the heart closely during reperfusion therapy to watch for complications.
The success of the surgery depended on timely reperfusion of the affected tissue.

Synonyms

  • recanalization
    8.9 0.02/m
    recanalization is reopening a blocked vessel to let blood flow again, while reperfusion is the return of blood to damaged tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ischemia - an inadequate blood supply to an organ or part of the body
  • synonym: perfusion - the process of delivering blood to tissue
