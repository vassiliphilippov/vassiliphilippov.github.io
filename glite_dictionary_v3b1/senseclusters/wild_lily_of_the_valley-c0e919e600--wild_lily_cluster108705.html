wild lily of the valley

noun
a North American evergreen plant
4.1
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (AmE), / waɪld ˈlɪli əv ðə vəˈli / (BrE)
A North American evergreen plant with oblong leaves and small pinkish bell-shaped flowers, formerly used for making shinplasters.

Examples

Wild lily of the valley leaves
Early settlers sometimes used the leaves of wild lily of the valley as a remedy for bruises.
The wild lily of the valley thrives in shady North American forests and is known for its delicate, bell-shaped flowers.
Unlike true lilies, the wild lily of the valley blooms with pinkish bells in the spring.
