epigenetic

adjective
formed after rock
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
formed or introduced after the formation of the surrounding rock, especially referring to mineral deposits or veins in geology or mineralogy

Examples

Epigenetic mineral deposits
The epigenetic gold veins were deposited long after the host rock solidified.
Geologists distinguish between syngenetic and epigenetic mineral formations when studying ore deposits.
The presence of epigenetic minerals can reveal important information about a region's geological history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: syngenetic - formed at the same time as the surrounding rock
