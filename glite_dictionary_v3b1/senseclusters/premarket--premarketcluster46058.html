premarket

adjective
before market opens
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈmɑːrkɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
happening or existing before the official opening of a stock market, especially relating to trading or prices

Examples

Premarket trading
The company's stock price rose sharply in premarket trading after the earnings report was released.
Investors often watch premarket activity to predict how the market will open.
Significant news can cause large movements in premarket prices before regular trading begins.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aftermarket - relating to trading or activity after the market closes
