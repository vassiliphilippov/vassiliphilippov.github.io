tutelary

adjective
serving as a guardian or protector
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːtəˌleri / (AmE), / ˈtjuːtɪləri / (BrE)
having the role of guarding, protecting, or watching over; serving as a guardian, especially in a spiritual or supervisory sense.

Examples

Tutelary spirit
In folklore, forests are often said to have tutelary spirits watching over the land.
Many ancient societies honored tutelary deities believed to protect their cities.
The school appointed a tutelary figure to oversee the welfare of all students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guardian - a person who protects or defends something
  • antonym: neglectful - failing to give proper care or attention
  • synonym: protective - giving or intended to protect someone or something
