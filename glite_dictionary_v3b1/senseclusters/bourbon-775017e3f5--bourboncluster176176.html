Bourbon

noun

(historical, US) A reactionary politician, especially from the South, who opposed progressive reforms after the Civil War.

Examples

Bourbon Democrat leader
Some historians refer to post-Civil War leaders in Louisiana as Bourbon Democrats because of their conservative stance.
The Southern Bourbon politicians resisted most attempts at social change during the Reconstruction era.
The influence of the Bourbon faction declined as more progressive reformers gained power.
