In mathematics, the lowest point on a curve, where the slope of the tangent changes from negative to positive.

Examples

Curve minimum
A quadratic equation forms a parabola that opens upward, with its vertex at the minimum.
The function has a local minimum at x = 2, where the graph reaches its lowest point.
To locate the global minimum, examine where the derivative equals zero and the slope changes sign.
