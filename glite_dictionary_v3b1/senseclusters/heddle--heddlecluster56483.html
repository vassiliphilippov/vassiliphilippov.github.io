heddle

noun
loom thread separator
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhed.əl/ (AmE), /ˈhed.əl/ (BrE)
a thin wire or cord loop in a loom through which a warp thread passes to guide or separate threads during weaving

Examples

A heddle thread
The weaver carefully threaded each warp yarn through a heddle before starting her project.
If a heddle breaks, it can disrupt the entire weaving process until it is replaced.
Modern looms often use metal heddles for greater durability and precision.
