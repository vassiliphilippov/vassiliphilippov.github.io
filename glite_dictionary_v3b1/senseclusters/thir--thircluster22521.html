thir

determiner
Scots demonstrative
Pronunciation: / θɪr / (AmE), / θɪr / (BrE)
a Scots demonstrative word used to refer to these or those people or things

Examples

Thir people here
She pointed to thir books on the table and asked who owned them.
In the old tale, the farmer gathered thir sheep before the storm arrived.
You can take any of thir chairs if you need a seat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: these - the plural of 'this', referring to people or things near the speaker
  • synonym: those - the plural of 'that', referring to people or things farther from the speaker
