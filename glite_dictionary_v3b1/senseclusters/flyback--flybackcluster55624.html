flyback

noun
high-voltage pulse
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbæk / (BrE)
a sudden high-voltage pulse generated when current through an inductor or transformer is abruptly interrupted

Examples

Flyback voltage spike
The engineer installed a diode to protect the circuit from flyback voltage.
A sudden flyback can damage sensitive electronic components if not properly managed.
Technicians often check for flyback pulses when troubleshooting transformer circuits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spike - a sharp, brief rise in electrical voltage
  • synonym: surge - a sudden, brief increase in voltage or current
