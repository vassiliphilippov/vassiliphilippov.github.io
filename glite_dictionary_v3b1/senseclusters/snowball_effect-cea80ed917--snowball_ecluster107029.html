snowball effect

noun
rapidly worsening or accelerating situation
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊbɔl ɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊbɔːl ɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
a situation in which something increases in size, importance, or impact at an accelerating rate, often becoming increasingly difficult to control

Examples

The snowball effect grows
Her positive attitude at work created a snowball effect, motivating the whole team to achieve more.
Missing just one payment had a snowball effect on his finances, leading to more debt.
Once the rumor started, the snowball effect made it impossible to stop misinformation from spreading.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chain reaction - a process in which an event causes a sequence of similar events
  • synonym: vicious circle - a situation in which a problem causes other problems, making the original problem worse
