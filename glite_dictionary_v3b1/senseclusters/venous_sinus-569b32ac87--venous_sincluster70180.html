venous sinus

noun
large blood-filled channel without vessel coating
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnəs ˈsaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs ˈsaɪnəs / (BrE)
a large blood-filled channel or cavity that lacks the usual muscular and elastic coat of a blood vessel, typically found in the brain and other parts of the body

Examples

A venous sinus in brain
An obstruction in a venous sinus can lead to increased intracranial pressure.
Surgeons carefully avoided the venous sinus during the operation to prevent excessive bleeding.
The dural venous sinus plays a crucial role in draining blood from the brain.
