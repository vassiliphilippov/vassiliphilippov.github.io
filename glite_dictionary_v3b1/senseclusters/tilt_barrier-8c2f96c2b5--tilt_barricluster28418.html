tilt barrier

noun
barrier used in jousting tournaments
Pronunciation: / tɪlt ˈbæriɚ / (AmE), / tɪlt ˈbæriə / (BrE)
A wooden fence or barrier placed between jousting combatants to prevent collisions and to keep them at the correct angle for breaking the lance.

Examples

The tilt barrier fence
During the tournament, the tilt barrier ensured that the knights charged safely without veering into each other's path.
The museum's replica of a tilt barrier demonstrates how jousting matches were organized in medieval times.
Without a tilt barrier, early jousts could become dangerously chaotic and unpredictable.
