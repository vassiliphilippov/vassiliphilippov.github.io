latching relay

noun
relay retaining state without power
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlætʃɪŋ rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈlatʃɪŋ rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
an electrical relay that maintains its switched state without power supplied to its coil.

Examples

A latching relay switch
A latching relay is ideal for emergency systems where power may be interrupted.
The technician installed a latching relay to control the circuit with minimal power consumption.
Using a latching relay, the device remained on even after the power to the coil was cut.
