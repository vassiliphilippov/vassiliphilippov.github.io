all right

adverb
certainly; without doubt
Pronunciation: / ɔl ˈraɪt / (AmE), / ɔːl ˈraɪt / (BrE)
certainly; without doubt (used to emphasize an assertion or statement).

Examples

He is angry all right
He was angry all right, though he tried to hide it.
It's an expensive restaurant all right, but the food is worth it.
That storm was dangerous all right—I'm glad we stayed indoors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certainly - used to emphasize the speaker's belief that something is true
  • synonym: definitely - without doubt; for sure
