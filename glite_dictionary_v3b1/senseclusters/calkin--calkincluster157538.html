calkin

noun
metal cleat on a horseshoe
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːlkɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɔːlkɪn / (BrE)
a metal projection or cleat on the underside or toe of a horseshoe, designed to prevent slipping

Examples

A calkin on horseshoe
Special attention must be paid to the placement of each calkin during shoeing.
The blacksmith added a calkin to the horseshoe for better grip on icy roads.
Without a proper calkin, the horse might slip on wet surfaces.
