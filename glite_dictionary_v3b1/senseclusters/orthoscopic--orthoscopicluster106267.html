orthoscopic

adjective
correctly proportioned image
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθəˈskɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
showing objects in their correct proportions, especially in an optical device or image

Examples

Orthoscopic image
The camera lens provided an orthoscopic image, making the photograph look very natural.
Scientists prefer orthoscopic microscopes for studying specimens without visual distortion.
An orthoscopic view is important in medical imaging to ensure accurate diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distorted - not showing the true shape or proportions
