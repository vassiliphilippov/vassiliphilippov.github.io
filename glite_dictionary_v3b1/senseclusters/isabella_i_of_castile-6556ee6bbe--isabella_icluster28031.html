Isabella I of Castile

proper noun
queen of Castile who unified Spain
8.8
Pronunciation: /ɪ.zə.ˈbel.ə ðə fɜːrst əv kə.ˈstiːl/ /; / /ɪ.zə.ˈbel.ə ðə fɜːrst əv kæ.ˈstiːl/ (AmE), /ɪ.zə.ˈbel.ə ðə fɜːst əv kə.ˈstiːl/ /; / /ɪ.zə.ˈbel.ə ðə fɜːst əv kæ.ˈstiːl/ (BrE)
(1451–1504) The queen of Castile whose marriage to Ferdinand of Aragon unified Spain, known for instituting the Spanish Inquisition and sponsoring Christopher Columbus's voyages.

Examples

Isabella I of Castile queen

The voyages of Columbus were sponsored by Isabella I of Castile and Ferdinand II of Aragon.
