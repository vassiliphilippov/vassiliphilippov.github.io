haul off

verb
remove or take away by vehicle
Pronunciation: / hɔl ɑf / (AmE), / hɔːl ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or take away something, especially by using a vehicle

Examples

To haul off equipment
After the concert, workers began to haul off the equipment from the stage.
The city will haul off your bulk trash if you schedule a pickup in advance.
The company sent a truck to haul off the old appliances from the building.
