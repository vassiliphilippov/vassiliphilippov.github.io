knight errantry

noun
the conduct or practice of a knight errant
Pronunciation: / naɪt ˈɛrəntɹi /; / ˈnaɪt ˌɛrəntri / (AmE), / naɪt ˈɛɹəntri /; / ˈnaɪt ˌɛrəntri / (BrE)
the practice, behavior, or ideals of a knight-errant, involving traveling in search of chivalrous or adventurous deeds

Examples

Knight errantry ideals
His fascination with medieval knight errantry inspired him to take up fencing and horseback riding.
In tales of the Middle Ages, knight errantry was celebrated as the height of noble adventure.
The legend of Don Quixote satirizes the concept of knight errantry and unrealistic heroism.
Despite the dangers, he devoted himself to knight errantry, helping strangers and fighting perceived injustices.
His pursuit of justice sometimes descended into mere knight errantry, driven by an impossible sense of chivalry.
The novel mocks the knight errantry of its idealistic protagonist as disconnected from reality.

  • antonym: pragmatism - a practical approach to problems and affairs
