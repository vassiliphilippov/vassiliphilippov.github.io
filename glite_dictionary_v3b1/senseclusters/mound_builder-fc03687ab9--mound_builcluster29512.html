any of several large-footed, short-winged birds of Australasia, known for building mounds of decaying vegetation to incubate their eggs; also called megapodes.

Examples

Mound builder bird
A mound builder relies on the heat of rotting vegetation rather than body warmth to incubate its eggs.
Many species of mound builders are native to Australia and the surrounding islands.
The mound builder carefully constructed a large mound of decaying leaves to protect its eggs.
