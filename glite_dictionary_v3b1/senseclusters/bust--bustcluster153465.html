bust

noun
a complete failure
4.5 4.21/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒst / (AmE), / bÊŒst / (BrE)
a complete failure, especially of an event, enterprise, or performance

Examples

The event was a bust
Despite months of planning, the fundraiser was a complete bust.
His big idea was a bust and cost the company a lot of money.
The new movie turned out to be a total bust at the box office.

Synonyms

  • flop
    4.3 2.23/m
    flop is an event that fails badly and visibly in public, while bust names any complete failure of an event or enterprise.
  • failure
    1.3 45/m
    failure is the general idea of not succeeding in plans or projects, while bust feels more informal and often means a total collapse.
  • fiasco
    6.2 4.22/m
    fiasco is a public, often embarrassing disaster that brings shame, while bust is a more general, less emotional word for failure.
  • debacle
    8.5 4.21/m
    debacle is a sudden, large disaster that ruins plans for many people, while bust usually names a failed event or business.
  • dud
    4.7 0.65/m
    dud is something that fails to work or meet hopes, often quietly, while bust means a full failure of an event or enterprise.
  • fizzle
    6.0 0.14/m
    fizzle is when something slowly loses energy and ends without success, while bust signals a clear, total failure from the start or end.
