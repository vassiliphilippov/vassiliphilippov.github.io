Akkadian

noun
ancient Semitic language of Mesopotamia
Pronunciation: / əˈkeɪdiən / (AmE), / əˈkeɪdiən / (BrE)
an extinct Semitic language spoken in ancient Mesopotamia, used from the third millennium BCE

Examples

Akkadian language
Many myths and epics were first recorded in Akkadian during the time of Hammurabi.
Scholars have studied Akkadian texts to learn about the ancient laws of Mesopotamia.
The Akkadian language used cuneiform script, a writing system adopted from the Sumerians.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Assyrian - dialect of Akkadian used in Assyria
  • synonym: Babylonian - dialect of Akkadian used in Babylonia
