sand eel

noun
small silvery eel-like coastal fish
7.4
Pronunciation: / sænd iːl / (AmE), / sænd iːl / (BrE)
A small, slender, silvery fish, resembling an eel, that lives in sandy coastal waters and often buries itself in the sand.

Examples

A sand eel fish
Fishermen caught a bucketful of sand eels to use as bait for larger fish.
Seabirds rely on sand eels as a primary source of food during breeding season.
The marine biologist observed sand eels burrowing quickly into the wet sand after the tide receded.
