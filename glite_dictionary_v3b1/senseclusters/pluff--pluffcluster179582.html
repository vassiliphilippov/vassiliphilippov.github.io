pluff

verb
to fluff up
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / plÊŒf / (AmE), / plÊŒf / (BrE)
to make something soft and fluffy by shaking, patting, or teasing its filling, especially a cushion or pillow

Examples

To pluff a pillow
Every morning, she would pluff the pillows to keep them looking fresh.
He pluffed the sofa cushions before the guests arrived to make the room more inviting.
After washing the comforter, you should pluff it to restore its shape.

Synonyms

  • fluff
    4.1 1.29/m
    fluff makes fabrics or hair look fuller and softer by shaking or brushing, while pluff targets a cushion's filling.
  • plump up
    4.5 0.17/m
    plump up makes a cushion fuller and rounder by patting or fluffing it, while pluff usually reshapes the filling inside.
  • shake up
    3.5 0.24/m
    shake up makes an object fluffier by vigorous shaking to loosen filling, while pluff means gentle patting or teasing its filling.
  • tease
    6.8 1.74/m
    tease lifts and separates fibers with a comb or brush to add volume, while pluff uses patting or shaking inside cushions.
