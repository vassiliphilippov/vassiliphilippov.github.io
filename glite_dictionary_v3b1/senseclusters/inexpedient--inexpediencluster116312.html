Examples

Synonyms

  • inadvisable
    5.5 0.2/m
    inadvisable is not recommended in normal advice, while inexpedient sounds more formal and stresses impracticality.
  • unwise
    3.5 3/m
    unwise is a plain word for poor judgment, while inexpedient sounds more formal and focuses on practicality.
  • impractical
    5.3 4/m
    impractical means unlikely to work in practice, while inexpedient implies action is unwise or not suitable now.
