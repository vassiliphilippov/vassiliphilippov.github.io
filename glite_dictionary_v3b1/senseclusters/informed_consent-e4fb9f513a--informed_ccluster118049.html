informed consent

noun
permission after full disclosure of risks (medical/legal)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔrmd kənˈsɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmd kənˈsɛnt / (BrE)
permission granted by a patient to undergo a medical procedure or participate in research after understanding the associated risks and information

Examples

Sign informed consent form
Hospitals require patients to sign an informed consent form before any major procedure.
Researchers cannot begin the study without obtaining informed consent from participants.
The doctor explained the surgery thoroughly before asking for informed consent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consent - permission for something to happen or agreement to do something
