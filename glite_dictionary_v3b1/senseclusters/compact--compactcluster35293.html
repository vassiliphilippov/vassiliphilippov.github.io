compact

adjective
small and solid in build
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpækt / (AmE), / kəmˈpækt / (BrE)
having a small, solid, and tightly packed form or structure; not large or spread out

Examples

Compact body
Despite his compact frame, he was exceptionally strong and agile.
She preferred cars that were small and compact for city driving.
The dog was bred to be compact and sturdy, perfect for working in dense brush.
Many city cars have a compact design to make parking easier.
The archaeologist discovered a compact layer of clay beneath the surface.
The living room was furnished with a compact sofa that fit perfectly in the small space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dense - closely packed in substance or parts
  • antonym: loose - not firmly or closely packed together
  • synonym: stocky - broad and sturdily built
  • synonym: thickset - having a broad, sturdy form
