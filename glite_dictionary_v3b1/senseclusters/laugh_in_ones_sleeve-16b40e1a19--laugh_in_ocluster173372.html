laugh in one's sleeve

verb
to be secretly amused
6.0 7.6
Pronunciation: /læf ɪn wʌnz sliːv/ /; / /læf ɪn wʌns sliːv/ /; / /læf ə n wʌnz sliːv/ (AmE), /lɑːf ɪn wʌnz sliːv/ /; / /lɑːf ɪn wʌns sliːv/ /; / /lɑːf ə n wʌnz sliːv/ (BrE)
to be secretly amused or to mock someone without showing it openly

Examples

To laugh in his sleeve
He laughed in his sleeve, pretending sympathy while secretly mocking the mistake.
She laughed in her sleeve when she realized her plan had worked perfectly.
Though he appeared serious, he was laughing in his sleeve at their confusion.
