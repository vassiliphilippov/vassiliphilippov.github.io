luteinization

noun
formation of luteal cells
Pronunciation: / ˌluːtiənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌluːtiənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which cells develop into luteal-like (yellowish) cells, often under hormonal influence, outside the ovary

Examples

Cell luteinization
The pathologist noted luteinization in the ovarian stroma during the examination.
Hormonal changes can trigger luteinization in tissues outside the ovary.
Researchers studied the effects of medication on luteinization in the endometrium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: luteinize - to undergo or cause to undergo luteinization
  • synonym: ovulation - the release of an egg from the ovary
