drag on forever

verb
continue for a very long time (tediously)
2.1
Pronunciation: / dræɡ ɑn fəˈrɛvɚ / (AmE), / dræɡ ɒn fəˈrɛvə / (BrE)
(of an event or activity) to continue for a much longer time than expected or desired, often so that it feels tedious.

Examples

To drag on forever slowly
Her explanation would drag on forever if nobody interrupted.
It felt like the wait for the results would drag on forever.
The movie seemed to drag on forever, and I nearly fell asleep before it ended.
