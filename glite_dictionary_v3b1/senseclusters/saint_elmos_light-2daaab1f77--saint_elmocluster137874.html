Saint Elmo's light

noun
electrical discharge on objects during storms
7.1
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈɛlmoʊz laɪt / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈɛlməʊz laɪt / (BrE)
A bluish or violet electrical discharge visible on pointed objects such as ship masts or aircraft during thunderstorms, caused by ionization of the surrounding air.

Examples

Saint Elmo's light on masts
Pilots sometimes see Saint Elmo's light glowing on their aircraft windows during thunderstorms.
Superstitious seafarers once believed Saint Elmo's light was a sign of protection from danger.
The sailors watched in awe as Saint Elmo's light danced on the tips of the ship's masts.
