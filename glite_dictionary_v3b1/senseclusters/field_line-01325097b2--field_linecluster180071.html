field line

noun
imaginary line indicating force direction in a field
7.6 0.4/m

an imaginary line in a field of force, such as a magnetic or electric field, where the direction of the line at any point indicates the direction of the force at that location.

Examples

Electric field lines
Electric field lines never cross each other in a uniform field.
Physicists used diagrams of field lines to illustrate the pattern of forces surrounding the charged object.
The direction of a magnetic field line at any point shows the orientation of the magnetic force.
