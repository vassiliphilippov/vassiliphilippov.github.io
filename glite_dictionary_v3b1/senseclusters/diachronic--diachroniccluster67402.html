diachronic

adjective
concerned with changes over time
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.əˈkrɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈkrɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
concerned with the way in which something, especially language, changes over time

Examples

Diachronic language study
Her dissertation provides a diachronic analysis of English vowel shifts.
Historians often take a diachronic approach to trace societal changes across generations.
The field of diachronic linguistics examines how languages evolve over centuries.

Related Terms
