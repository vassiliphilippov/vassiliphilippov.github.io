food stamp

noun
government-issued voucher for buying food
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfuːd stæmp / (AmE), / ˈfuːd stæmp / (BrE)
a government-issued voucher or stamp, formerly distributed to low-income individuals, that can be exchanged for food at authorized stores

Examples

Food stamps program
After losing his job, he applied for food stamps to afford groceries.
Many families relied on food stamps to help put meals on the table.
The government replaced the paper food stamp system with electronic benefit cards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: SNAP - Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, the modern replacement for food stamps in the United States
