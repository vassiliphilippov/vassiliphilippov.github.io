interrogation point

noun
punctuation mark indicating a question
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɛrəˈɡeɪʃən pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a punctuation mark (‘?’) placed at the end of a sentence to indicate a question

Examples

An interrogation point mark
Every direct question in English ends with an interrogation point.
Some old style guides prefer the term interrogation point instead of 'question mark'.
You forgot to put an interrogation point at the end of your sentence.
