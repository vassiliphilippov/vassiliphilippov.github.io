nuclear RNA

noun
RNA found in the cell nucleus
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliɚ ɑːr ɛn eɪ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːklɪə ɑːr ɛn eɪ / (BrE)
Ribonucleic acid present in the nucleus of a cell, especially as found in the nucleolus.

Examples

nuclear RNA in cells
Researchers examined the nuclear RNA to better understand gene expression in the cell nucleus.
Scientists isolated nuclear RNA from the nucleolus during their experiment on cellular transcription.
The presence of nuclear RNA was significantly higher in cells actively producing ribosomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cytoplasmic RNA - RNA found in the cytoplasm of the cell
