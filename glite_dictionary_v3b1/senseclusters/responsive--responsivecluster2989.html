responsive

adjective
reacting to a stimulus; quick to respond
1.0 4.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɑːnsɪv /; / rɪˈspɑnsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsɪv / (BrE)
reacting quickly and positively to people, events, or stimuli; showing a willingness to respond

Examples

Responsive customer service
Good customer service means being responsive to clients' needs and feedback.
Some animals are more responsive to light and noise than others.
The patient's skin was highly responsive to temperature changes during the examination.
Her face was responsive, showing every emotion as she listened to the story.
Teachers appreciate students who are responsive and engaged during lessons.
The new customer service team is very responsive to inquiries and feedback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reactive - tending to react to a stimulus or influence
  • antonym: unresponsive - not reacting or showing willingness to respond
