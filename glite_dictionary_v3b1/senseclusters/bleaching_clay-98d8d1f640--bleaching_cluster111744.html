bleaching clay

noun
adsorbent clay for decolorizing oils
6.1 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbliː.tʃɪŋ kleɪ/ (AmE), /ˈbliː.tʃɪŋ kleɪ/ (BrE)
a type of adsorbent clay used to remove color from oils, especially in industrial processes

Examples

Bleaching clay for oil
Different grades of bleaching clay are used depending on the specific oil being processed.
Employing high-quality bleaching clay ensures that the final product is clear and free from impurities.
The oil refinery uses bleaching clay to purify vegetable oils before bottling.
