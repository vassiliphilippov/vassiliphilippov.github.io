mechanization

noun
use of machines to perform tasks
3.8 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛkənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmiːkənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌmɛkənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌmɛkənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of using machines or technology to do work that was previously done by people or animals

Examples

Industrial mechanization
Recent advances in mechanization have increased productivity in the factory.
The mechanization of agriculture dramatically reduced the number of farm laborers needed.
Widespread mechanization changed the landscape of industrial production in the twentieth century.
Factory mechanization transformed the textile industry in the nineteenth century.
Many traditional crafts declined with the widespread mechanization of manufacturing.
The mechanization of agriculture dramatically increased productivity and reduced the need for manual labor.
Many traditional jobs were lost with the mechanisation of the textile industry.
Rapid mechanisation has brought both economic growth and social challenges.
The mechanisation of agriculture greatly increased food production across the country.
