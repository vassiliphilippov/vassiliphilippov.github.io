Tertiary

noun
former geological period (63–2 million years ago)
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɜːr.ʃə.riː/ /; / /ˈtɜːr.ʃriː/ (AmE), /ˈtɜː.ʃə.riː/ /; / /ˈtɜː.ʃriː/ (BrE)
(in geology) The former name for the geologic period from about 63 million to 2 million years ago, situated between the Cretaceous and the Quaternary.

Examples

The Tertiary period
Climate changes during the Tertiary had a profound effect on mammalian evolution.
Many paleontologists study fossils that first appeared in the Tertiary period.
The extinction of the dinosaurs occurred before the start of the Tertiary.
