antiseptic

adjective
free from disease-causing organisms
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈsɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈsɛp.tɪk / (BrE)
completely clean and free from disease-causing organisms; able to destroy harmful microorganisms

Examples

Antiseptic wipes
Alcohol wipes have an antiseptic effect on the skin before injections.
Everything in the laboratory must be antiseptic to ensure accurate results.
The surgery was performed in an antiseptic environment to minimize infection risk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contaminated - made impure by exposure to or addition of a harmful substance
  • synonym: sterile - completely free from all microorganisms
