warrantable

adjective
justifiable or defensible
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrəntəbl / (AmE), / ˈwɒrəntəbl / (BrE)
able to be justified, defended, or authorized by law or good reason

Examples

Warrantable action
The judge decided that the use of force was not warrantable in that situation.
Her concerns about the safety of the building were entirely warrantable.
It is not warrantable to make accusations without clear evidence.
The judge ruled that the search was warrantable based on the evidence presented.
His actions were completely warrantable given the circumstances.
A warrantable reason must exist before the authorities can take such measures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defensible - able to be supported by argument or evidence
  • synonym: justifiable - able to be shown as right or reasonable
  • antonym: unjustifiable - not able to be shown as right or reasonable
  • antonym: unwarranted - not justified or authorized
