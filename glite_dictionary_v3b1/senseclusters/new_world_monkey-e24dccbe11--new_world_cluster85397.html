New World monkey

noun
a monkey from Central or South America
6.7
Pronunciation: / nuː ˈwɜrld ˈmʌŋki / (AmE), / njuː ˈwɜːld ˈmʌŋki / (BrE)
a monkey native to Central and South America, typically with a flat nose, outward-facing nostrils, and often a prehensile tail

Examples

New World monkey species
Spider monkeys are a well-known type of New World monkey found in Central and South America.
The New World monkeys display a remarkable variety of social behaviors in the rainforest canopy.
Unlike the Old World macaques, the New World monkey often has a prehensile tail.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Old World monkey - a monkey native to Africa or Asia with downward-facing nostrils
