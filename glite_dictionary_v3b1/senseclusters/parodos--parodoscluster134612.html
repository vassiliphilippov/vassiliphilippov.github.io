parodos

noun
Greek theatre entrance
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌdɒs / (AmE), / ˈpærədɒs / (BrE)
a side entrance or passage in an ancient Greek theatre used by the chorus or actors to enter and exit the orchestra

Examples

Theatre parodos entrance
The actors entered the stage through the parodos as the audience watched in anticipation.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a parodos at the ancient theatre site.
The chorus made their dramatic entrance via the parodos during the festival performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orchestra - the circular space in front of the stage in ancient Greek theatres
  • synonym: skene - the stage building behind the orchestra in ancient Greek theatres
