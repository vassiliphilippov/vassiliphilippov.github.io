report

verb
inform authorities about someone's actions
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɔrt / (AmE), / rɪˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to inform a person in authority about someone's actions, especially if they have broken rules or laws

Examples

To report a problem
Employees are required to report any workplace accidents to their supervisor within 24 hours.
If you see suspicious activity, you should report it to the police immediately.
She reported her neighbor to the landlord for violating the noise policy.
After witnessing the theft, he reported the incident to the police immediately.
If you see bullying at school, you should report it to a teacher.
She reported her colleague for inappropriate behavior at the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep information secret, especially from authorities
  • synonym: denounce - to publicly accuse someone of wrongdoing
  • synonym: inform - to notify authorities about someone's wrongdoing
  • synonym: notify - to formally inform someone of something
