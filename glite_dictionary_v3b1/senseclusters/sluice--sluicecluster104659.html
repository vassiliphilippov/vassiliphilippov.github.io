sluice

verb
let water flow through a sluice
7.4 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / sluÀês / (AmE), / sluÀês / (BrE)
to let water or something else flow through or out by means of a sluice, or to wash or transport something using a flow of water

Examples

To sluice the sediment
Engineers decided to sluice the sediment out of the reservoir to increase capacity.
The mill was sluiced each morning to ensure proper flow.
They sluiced the excess water from the canal after the heavy rain.
After the festival, workers sluiced the streets to clean away all the debris.
Farmers sluice their fields to ensure the crops receive enough water during dry spells.
The miners sluiced the earth to separate gold from the silt and mud.
During the gold rush, miners would sluice gravel to separate out the gold.
The company plans to sluice waste water through a separate channel for treatment.
Workers would sluice the logs down the river to the mill.

Synonyms

  • flush
    2.7 5.59/m
    flush forces water through something to wash it away, while sluice means using a gate or strong flow to wash or move things.
  • drain
    1.0 16/m
    drain removes or lets liquid flow out until empty, while sluice means using a gate or strong flow to wash or move things.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flush - to cleanse something by causing large quantities of water or other liquid to pass through it
