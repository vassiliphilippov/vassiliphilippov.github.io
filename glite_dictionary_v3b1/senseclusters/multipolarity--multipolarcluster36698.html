multipolarity

noun
having multiple poles
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltipoʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪpəˈlarəti / (BrE)
the state or property of having more than two poles or centers, especially in physics or engineering contexts

Examples

Molecular multipolarity
The multipolarity of the molecule affects how it interacts with electric fields.
Engineers consider multipolarity when designing complex electrical circuits.
Recent advances in antenna technology rely on multipolarity to improve signal transmission.
