bullpup

adjective
firearm design type
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlˌpʌp / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌpʌp / (BrE)
describing a firearm with the action and magazine located behind the trigger, resulting in a shorter overall length

Examples

Bullpup rifle design
The new rifle uses a bullpup configuration to make it more compact for urban operations.
Many modern military forces are adopting bullpup weapons for their improved maneuverability.
A bullpup design allows for a longer barrel without increasing the overall length of the firearm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - having the action and magazine in front of the trigger
