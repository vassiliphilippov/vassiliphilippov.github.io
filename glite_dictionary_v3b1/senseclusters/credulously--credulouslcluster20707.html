credulously

adverb
in a credulous manner
7.7 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / kr…ôÀàd íuÀêl…ôsli / (AmE), / kr…™ÀàdjuÀêl…ôsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a readiness to believe things too easily or without enough evidence.

Examples

To credulously believe
He credulously assumed that every email he received was genuine.
Many investors credulously believed the promise of high returns and lost their money.
She credulously accepted the stranger's wild story as the truth.

Synonyms

  • gullibly
    6.5 0.01/m
    gullibly means believing things easily without doubt, while credulously describes being ready to accept things without enough evidence.
  • believingly
    8.9 0.00/m
    believingly shows trust or conviction in someone or something, whereas credulously means accepting claims too easily without enough evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credulous - having or showing too great a readiness to believe things
  • antonym: skeptically - in a skeptical manner, showing doubt
