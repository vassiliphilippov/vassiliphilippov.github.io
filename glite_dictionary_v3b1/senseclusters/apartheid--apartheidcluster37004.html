apartheid

noun
systemic non-racial segregation or exclusion
7.9 0.75/m
Pronunciation: / \u0259\u02c8p\u0251\u02d0r.ta\u026at / (AmE), / \u0259\u02c8p\u0251\u02d0.ta\u026ad / (BrE)
(figurative) a system or practice that enforces separation, exclusion, or deep inequality between groups in a non-racial domain (for example, digital access, housing, education, or services).

Examples

Digital apartheid
Critics argue that unequal internet access has become a form of apartheid in the twenty-first century.
The housing policy created economic apartheid between neighborhoods with vastly different schools and services.
Many activists warn that algorithmic decisions could lead to digital apartheid by locking out disadvantaged groups.

Synonyms

  • segregation
    6.1 4.53/m
    segregation is enforced separation of groups, often by race or religion, while apartheid is a wider system creating deep, unequal exclusion across many services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from participating in or having access to something
  • antonym: integration - the process of combining or bringing groups together; the opposite of enforced separation
  • synonym: segregation - the action or state of setting people apart from others
