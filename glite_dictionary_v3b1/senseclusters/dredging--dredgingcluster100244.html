dredging

noun
coating food before cooking
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdre.dʒɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdre.dʒɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of coating food lightly with a dry ingredient such as flour or breadcrumbs before cooking

Examples

Dredging with flour
A light dredging of flour helps the fish develop a crispy crust when fried.
Before frying, the chef recommends dredging the vegetables in seasoned cornmeal.
Proper dredging ensures the chicken pieces are evenly coated and cook to a golden brown.

Related Terms

  • antonym: battering - the process of coating food with a liquid mixture before frying
  • synonym: breading - the process of covering food with breadcrumbs before cooking
