bell-type furnace

noun
metal furnace for annealing steel strips
8.3 7.7
Pronunciation: /bel taɪp ˈfɜːr.nəs/ (AmE), /bel taɪp ˈfɜː.nɪs/ (BrE)
a furnace in which steel strips are annealed by covering them with a bell-shaped enclosure, used to relieve strain hardening as an alternative to continuous annealing.

Examples

A bell-type furnace enclosure
The company installed a bell-type furnace to improve the efficiency of its steel annealing process.
The maintenance team inspected the bell-type furnace to ensure it was functioning correctly before starting production.
Unlike continuous annealing lines, a bell-type furnace allows for precise control of heating rates.
