supersensitivity

noun
abnormally increased response
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˌsɛnsəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
an abnormally strong or exaggerated response in the body, such as in cells, tissues, or the immune system, often to a substance or stimulus

Examples

Drug-induced supersensitivity
Prolonged use of certain medications can lead to supersensitivity in nerve receptors.
Researchers observed supersensitivity to the hormone after the treatment was stopped.
Patients with supersensitivity may experience stronger side effects from standard drug doses.
The patient's supersensitivity to pollen caused severe allergic reactions every spring.
Researchers are studying supersensitivity in the immune system to better understand autoimmune diseases.
Repeated exposure to the chemical led to supersensitivity in some laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypersensitivity - an excessive or abnormal sensitivity to a substance
  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of response or reduced sensitivity to a substance
