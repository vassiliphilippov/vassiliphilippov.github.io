autonomous

adjective
acting independently; self-governing
8.7
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑːnəməs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒnəməs / (BrE)
having the freedom to govern or control oneself, without being controlled by others (used for people, regions, or things)

Examples

An autonomous region
After gaining sovereignty, the region became fully autonomous with its own government and legislature.
Each division of the company operates as an autonomous unit within the larger organization.
The university's colleges are autonomous and manage their own administrative affairs.
She prided herself on being an autonomous thinker, forming opinions without outside influence.
Teenagers often seek to become more autonomous as they grow older and crave independence from their parents.
The committee valued having autonomous members who could make impartial judgments.
After years under colonial rule, the region finally became autonomous in its decision-making.
Each province in the country has an autonomous assembly responsible for local legislation.
The university is proud to be an autonomous institution, free from government interference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dependent - relying on or controlled by someone else
  • synonym: independent - not influenced or controlled by others; self-sufficient
