proleptic

adjective
anticipating in grammar
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / prəˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
relating to a grammatical construction in which a word refers forward to a later clause or phrase in the sentence.

Examples

Proleptic pronoun
In the sentence 'It is clear that he will win,' the proleptic 'it' refers to the following clause.
Linguists often discuss proleptic constructions when analyzing sentence structure.
The use of a proleptic subject can make complex sentences easier to understand.
