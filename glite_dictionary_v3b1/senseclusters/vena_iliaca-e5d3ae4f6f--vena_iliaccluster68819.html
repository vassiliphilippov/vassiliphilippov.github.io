vena iliaca

noun
pelvic veins draining lower limbs
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪnə ɪˈlaɪ.əkə / (AmE), / ˌviː.nə ɪˈlaɪ.ə.kə / (BrE)
Any of the three veins (common, external, or internal iliac veins) that collect blood from the pelvic area and lower limbs.

Examples

Vena iliaca vein
A blood clot in the vena iliaca can lead to serious medical conditions such as deep vein thrombosis.
During pelvic surgery, care must be taken not to damage the vena iliaca.
The vena iliaca is crucial for returning blood from the lower limbs to the heart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: iliac vein - any of the large veins in the pelvis (common, external, or internal)
