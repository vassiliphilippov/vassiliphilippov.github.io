tough

adjective
violent and lawless (of people/groups)
1.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /tʌf/ (AmE), /tʌf/ (BrE)
(of people or groups) violent and willing to break the law; aggressive and threatening.

Examples

Tough kids
Despite her small size, she hung out with some very tough kids at school.
Many considered that to be a tough part of the city, where crime was common.
The police struggled to control the tough gang that ruled the neighborhood.
