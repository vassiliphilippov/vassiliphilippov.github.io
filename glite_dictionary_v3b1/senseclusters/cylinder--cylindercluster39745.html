cylinder

noun
solid figure with circular bases
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlɪndər /; / ˈsɪlɪndɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪndə / (BrE)
a solid or surface in geometry with straight parallel sides and a circular or oval cross section, like a tube or can

Examples

A cylinder shape
A cylinder is different from a sphere because of its parallel sides and flat ends.
In geometry class, we learned to calculate the volume of a cylinder.
The soup can was shaped like a cylinder with two flat circular bases.
A right circular cylinder has its axis perpendicular to its base.
In mathematics, a cylinder is defined as all points at a fixed distance from a given line.
The equation describes the surface of a cylinder in three-dimensional space.
