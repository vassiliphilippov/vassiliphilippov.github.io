substitute

adjective
artificial and inferior replacement
3.2 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.stə.tuːt/ /; / /ˈsʌb.stɪ.tuːt/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.stɪ.tjuːt/ /; / /ˈsʌb.stɪ.tʃuːt/ (BrE)
serving or used as a replacement for something or someone, sometimes regarded as artificial or inferior

Examples

Substitute teacher
Many critics found the film a poor substitute for the original classic.
She refuses to drink substitute coffee, insisting on the real thing.
The recipe called for real butter, not a substitute product.
A substitute teacher covered the class while the regular teacher was away.
She accepted a substitute gift until her actual present arrived.
They brewed coffee with a substitute ingredient when they ran out of beans.
