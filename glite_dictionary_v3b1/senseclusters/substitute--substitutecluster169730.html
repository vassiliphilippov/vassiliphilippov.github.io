substitute

adjective
artificial and inferior replacement

serving or used as a replacement for something or someone, sometimes regarded as artificial or inferior

Examples

Substitute teacher
Many critics found the film a poor substitute for the original classic.
She refuses to drink substitute coffee, insisting on the real thing.
The recipe called for real butter, not a substitute product.
A substitute teacher covered the class while the regular teacher was away.
She accepted a substitute gift until her actual present arrived.
They brewed coffee with a substitute ingredient when they ran out of beans.

Synonyms

  • substitutive
    7.2 0.00/m
    substitutive is a formal adjective meaning used to replace something directly, while substitute is common and sometimes neutral in tone.
  • alternate
    3.6 8.22/m
    alternate acts in place of another, often as a planned replacement, while substitute is the general term for a replacement.
  • ersatz
    9.6 0.95/m
    ersatz looks and feels like a poor imitation, while substitute can be neutral and not always of lower quality.
  • makeshift
    4.8 4.63/m
    makeshift is a quick, temporary fix made from what is at hand, while substitute can be permanent or planned.
  • alternative
    4.2 46/m
    alternative offers a different option or choice, while substitute means something that stands in for the original item.
