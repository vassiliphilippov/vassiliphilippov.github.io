wittol

noun
man aware and tolerant of wife's infidelity (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪtəl / (BrE)
An archaic term for a man whose wife is unfaithful and who knows and tolerates her infidelity.

Examples

A wittol man
He was no mere cuckold, but a wittol, accepting his wife's betrayal without protest.
In the play, the aging merchant became a wittol, enduring his wife's open affairs.
Literature of the era sometimes portrayed the wittol as a figure of ridicule.
