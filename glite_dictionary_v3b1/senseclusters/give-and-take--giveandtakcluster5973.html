give-and-take

noun
friendly verbal banter; repartee
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɡɪv ən ˈteɪk /; / ˈɡɪv ən ˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡɪv ən ˈteɪk / (BrE)
a friendly or mutual exchange of ideas, opinions, or teasing in conversation, often involving compromise or back-and-forth discussion

Examples

Friendly give-and-take
Good friendships often include a lot of give-and-take that keeps everyone laughing.
I enjoyed the lively give-and-take between the hosts on the morning radio show.
Their give-and-take always made meetings entertaining, as they joked and teased each other with ease.
Healthy relationships rely on honest give-and-take rather than just one-sided opinions.
Our team meetings are based on open give-and-take to find the best solutions.
There was a lively give-and-take between the two debaters during the forum.
A productive meeting should encourage give-and-take among all participants.
In their marriage, there was plenty of give-and-take, which helped them resolve conflicts.
Negotiations require honest give-and-take to be successful.

  • synonym: exchange - the act of giving one thing and receiving another
