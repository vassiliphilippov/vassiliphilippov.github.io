sharklet

noun
shark-skin-like surface
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑːrklət / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːklət / (BrE)
a microstructured surface or material designed to mimic shark skin and prevent the growth or attachment of microorganisms

Examples

Sharklet surface coating
The laboratory tested a new sharklet coating to reduce bacterial growth on medical devices.
Many hospitals are adopting sharklet surfaces to help prevent the spread of infections.
Researchers found that the sharklet pattern significantly decreased the attachment of harmful microbes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antimicrobial surface - a surface designed to resist or kill microorganisms
  • antonym: biofouling - the accumulation of microorganisms on surfaces in water
