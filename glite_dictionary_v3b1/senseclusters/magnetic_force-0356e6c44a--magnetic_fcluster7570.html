magnetic force

noun
force exerted by magnets or currents
4.9 3.3
Pronunciation: /mæɡ.ˈnet.ɪk fɔːrs/ (AmE), /mæɡ.ˈnet.ɪk fɔːs/ (BrE)
the force exerted by a magnet or a current-carrying wire on iron or on other magnets, characterized by fields of force and associated with electric currents as well as magnets

Examples

Strong magnetic force
A strong magnetic force can attract metal objects from a distance.
Scientists study how the magnetic force interacts with electric currents in physics.
The compass needle aligns itself with the magnetic force of the Earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic force - the combined force of electricity and magnetism; one of the four fundamental physical forces
