sacramental oil

noun
consecrated oil for religious ceremonies
Pronunciation: / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəl ɔɪl / (AmE), / ˌsakrəˈmɛntəl ɔɪl / (BrE)
A consecrated mixture of oil and balsam used in certain religious rites and ceremonies.

Examples

Sacramental oil for blessing
Sacramental oil is stored in a special vessel in many churches.
The priest anointed the child with sacramental oil during the baptism ceremony.
They prepared the sacramental oil before the Easter vigil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chrism - consecrated oil used in Christian rites
