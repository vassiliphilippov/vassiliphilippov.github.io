expansivity

noun
rate of expansion with temperature
5.6 7.7
Pronunciation: /ɪk.spæn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /ək.spæn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɪk.spæn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /ək.spæn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the rate at which a material's length, area, or volume changes in response to a change in temperature at constant pressure

Examples

Thermal expansivity
Knowledge of water's expansivity explains why pipes can burst in freezing weather.
Scientists measured the expansivity of the new polymer to determine its suitability for use in electronics.
The expansivity of metals must be considered when constructing bridges to prevent structural damage from temperature changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermal expansion - an increase in the size of a material as its temperature increases
