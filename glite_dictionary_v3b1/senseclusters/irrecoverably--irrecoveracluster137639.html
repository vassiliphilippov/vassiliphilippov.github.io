irrecoverably

adverb
beyond recovery
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈkʌvərəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈkʌvərəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be regained, restored, or fixed

Examples

Are irrecoverably lost
The documents were irrecoverably lost after the hard drive crashed.
Trust between the two friends was irrecoverably damaged by the betrayal.
The painting was irrecoverably ruined when water seeped into the gallery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever or cannot be changed
  • antonym: reversibly - in a way that can be undone or restored
