chain lightning

noun
lightning with a zigzag, branching path
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of lightning that travels quickly in a zigzag path, often branching at one end like a fork

Examples

Chain lightning zigzags
As the thunder rumbled, a bolt of chain lightning split into several forks above the field.
Scientists studied photos of chain lightning to understand its complex, zigzagging pattern.
The storm was illuminated by streaks of chain lightning arcing across the sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forked lightning - lightning appearing as a branching line in the sky
