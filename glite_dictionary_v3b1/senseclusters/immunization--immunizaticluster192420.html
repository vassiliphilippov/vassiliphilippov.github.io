immunization

noun
process of making immune, usually by vaccine
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmjənəˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪˌmjunəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making someone immune to a disease, especially by introducing a vaccine or similar agent

Examples

Immunization with vaccine
Immunization has significantly reduced the incidence of many infectious diseases worldwide.
Parents are encouraged to ensure their children receive all recommended immunizations.
The community health center offered free immunization clinics every month.
Many countries require proof of immunisation before children start school.
Routine childhood immunisation helps prevent serious illnesses.
The government's immunisation program has successfully reduced the incidence of measles.
