acetic acid

noun
a colorless, pungent liquid acid; main component of vinegar
4.8 3.5
Pronunciation: / əˈsit̬ɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˈsiːtɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent liquid organic acid widely used in manufacturing plastics, pharmaceuticals, and as the main component of vinegar

Examples

Diluted acetic acid
Diluted acetic acid is commonly used as a food preservative in the form of vinegar.
Exposure to concentrated acetic acid can cause irritation to the eyes and lungs.
The laboratory ordered more acetic acid for its chemical experiments.
