rectal artery

noun
artery supplying the rectum
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktl ˈɑːr.təri / (AmE), / ˈrɛktl ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
any of several arteries that supply blood to the rectum

Examples

Rectal artery bleeding
Damage to a rectal artery can cause significant bleeding during surgery.
Doctors may assess the rectal artery in cases of suspected rectal cancer.
The rectal artery plays a crucial role in supplying oxygenated blood to the lower digestive tract.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: inferior rectal artery - the artery supplying the lower part of the rectum
  • degree lower: middle rectal artery - the artery supplying the middle part of the rectum
  • degree higher: superior rectal artery - the artery supplying the upper part of the rectum
