take in

verb
allow into one's family or household
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to allow someone to stay in your home or provide shelter or care, especially when they need help

Examples

To take in a guest
After their parents died, their aunt decided to take in the three children.
She kindly took in her cousin when he had nowhere else to go.
The Johnsons often take in foster kids until permanent homes are found.
After the storm, many families decided to take in those who had lost their homes.
She was kind enough to take in her friend's child when the family was in trouble.
The shelter aims to take in stray animals and find them new homes.
The shelter agreed to take in those affected by the storm for the night.
They have often taken in stray animals during the winter months.
When her cousin lost his job, she decided to take in him until he found a new place.
