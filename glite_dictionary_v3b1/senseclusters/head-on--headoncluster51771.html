head-on

adjective, adverb
directly in front; with the front foremost

Examples

Head-on crash
A truck hit the barrier head-on during the storm.
They tackled the problem head-on rather than avoiding it.
Two trains crashed head-on in the tunnel.
Both teams are prepared for a head-on confrontation in tomorrow's debate.
The two leaders faced each other in a head-on argument over policy.
There was a dangerous head-on collision on the highway this morning.

Synonyms

  • frontally
