whole blood

noun
blood as drawn, not separated into components
Pronunciation: / hoʊl blʌd / (AmE), / həʊl blʌd / (BrE)
blood that has been drawn from the body with only an anticoagulant added, not separated into components

Examples

Whole blood sample
All donated whole blood is tested before being used for transfusions.
The doctor explained that a whole blood transfusion was necessary due to severe blood loss.
Unlike plasma, whole blood contains all the cells and clotting factors.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: plasma - the pale yellow liquid component of blood
