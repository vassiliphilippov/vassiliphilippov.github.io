fiddle

noun
a bowed stringed instrument; a violin
9.5 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
A bowed stringed instrument, especially the violin, with four strings, a hollow body, and an unfretted fingerboard, played with a bow.

Examples

A fiddle player
He placed his old fiddle carefully back in its worn case after the concert.
She learned to play the fiddle as a child, performing at folk festivals every summer.
The lively music filled the room as the fiddle echoed alongside the guitar.
