vermiform appendix

noun
narrow pouch attached to the cecum
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝː.mə.fɔrm əˈpɛn.dɪks / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.mɪ.fɔːm əˈpɛn.dɪks / (BrE)
a narrow, tube-shaped pouch that projects from the lower end of the cecum in the intestine, often considered vestigial

Examples

Pain in the lower right abdomen may be a sign of an infected vermiform appendix.
Scientists once believed the vermiform appendix had no function, but recent studies suggest it may play a role in immunity.
The surgeon removed the inflamed vermiform appendix to prevent further complications.
