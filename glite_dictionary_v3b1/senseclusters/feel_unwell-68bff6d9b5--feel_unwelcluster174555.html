feel unwell

verb
to experience illness or discomfort
1.8 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / fil …ônÀàw…õl / (AmE), / fiÀêl  ånÀàw…õl / (BrE)
to experience illness or a general sense of being unwell, usually without specifying the cause

Examples

To feel unwell suddenly
I started to feel unwell after lunch, so I left work early.
If you feel unwell, it's best to rest and drink plenty of fluids.
She suddenly began to feel unwell during the meeting and had to excuse herself.

Synonyms

    5.3 0.55/m
    under the weather is a common phrase for being slightly ill or tired, while feel unwell is a neutral, general description of illness.
  • feel off
    6.3 0.10/m
    feel off describes a vague physical or emotional unease, while feel unwell names a general medical or sickness state.
  • out of sorts
    5.9 0.57/m
    out of sorts means slightly unwell or grumpy, while feel unwell focuses on a general illness without mood detail.
  • feel lousy
    6.9 0.24/m
    feel lousy is a more informal, stronger way to say you feel sick or miserable, while feel unwell stays neutral and general.
