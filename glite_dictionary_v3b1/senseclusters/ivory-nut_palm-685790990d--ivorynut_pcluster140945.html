ivory-nut palm

noun
South American palm producing ivory nuts
6.8 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.və.riː nʌt pɑːm/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.və.riː nʌt pɑːm/ (BrE)
A South American palm tree, lacking a visible stem, that produces ivory nuts used as a substitute for elephant ivory.

Examples

The ivory-nut palm tree
Conservationists support the cultivation of the ivory-nut palm as an alternative to harvesting elephant ivory.
Many artisans use the nuts from the ivory-nut palm to carve buttons and ornaments.
The ivory-nut palm is valued for its seeds, which closely resemble animal ivory.
