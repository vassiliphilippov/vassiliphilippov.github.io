fearsomeness

noun
timidity or nervousness
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪrsəmnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪəsəmnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being easily frightened or showing timidity and nervousness

Examples

His fearsomeness at work
His fearsomeness kept him from speaking up in meetings at work.
The child's fearsomeness was obvious when she clung to her mother in new situations.
Despite his fearsomeness, he decided to try the roller coaster for the first time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bravery - the quality of being courageous and not easily frightened
  • synonym: timidity - the quality of being shy or lacking courage
