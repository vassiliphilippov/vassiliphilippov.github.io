unreason

noun
irrationality; lack of reason
6.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈriːzən / (AmE), / ʌnˈriːzn̩ / (BrE)
The state or quality of being irrational or lacking the ability to think logically.

Examples

The unreason of fear
In moments of panic, unreason can overcome even the most logical minds.
Superstition, he argued, is a product of unreason and fear.
The philosopher warned against the dangers of unreason in public debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrationality - the quality of being illogical or unreasonable
  • antonym: reason - the power of the mind to think logically
