double down

verb
commit more strongly; reinforce commitment
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
to strengthen one's commitment to a course of action or decision, especially despite risk or opposition

Examples

To double down on effort
After the first attempt failed, the company decided to double down on their new approach.
Instead of retreating, he doubled down and worked even harder to achieve his goals.
When criticized for her views, she chose to double down rather than apologize.

Related Terms

  • antonym: back down - to withdraw from a commitment or position
  • synonym: recommit - to commit again to something
