take in

verb
accept or receive (information, people, etc.)
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /teɪk ɪn/ (AmE), /teɪk ɪn/ (BrE)
to understand or absorb information mentally; to grasp or receive ideas or knowledge

Examples

To take in information
She found it hard to take in all the new information at once.
The shelter agreed to take in three more homeless families.
This sponge can take in a lot of water quickly.
He tried to take in all the details of the painting during his brief visit.
I needed a moment to take in everything she had just told me.
The plant's roots take in water from the soil.
After reading the article, I needed a moment to take in its main points.
He couldn't take in what she was saying because he was distracted.
It was difficult for her to take in all the information during the lecture.
