clingstone

noun
fruit with flesh attached to the pit
9.3 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪŋˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋstəʊn / (BrE)
A fruit, especially a peach, whose flesh adheres strongly to the pit, making it difficult to separate.

Examples

A clingstone peach
The variety of peach we bought at the market turned out to be a clingstone.
Unlike freestone peaches, clingstone fruits can be messier to eat.
When making jam, some prefer clingstone peaches because of their rich flavor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freestone - fruit whose flesh separates easily from the pit
