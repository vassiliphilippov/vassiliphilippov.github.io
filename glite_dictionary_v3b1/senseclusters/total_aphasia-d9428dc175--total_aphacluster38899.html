total aphasia

noun
complete loss of communication ability
9.2 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtoʊ.təl ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /ˈtoʊ.təl ə.ˈfeɪ.zɪə/ (AmE), /ˈtəʊ.təl ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /ˈtəʊ.təl ə.ˈfeɪ.zɪə/ (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by the complete loss of all ability to communicate, including both understanding and producing language

Examples

Total aphasia diagnosis
Doctors diagnosed her with total aphasia after she lost all capacity for speech and comprehension.
Following the stroke, the patient developed total aphasia and was unable to communicate even basic needs.
Treatment for total aphasia often involves intensive speech and language therapy.
