Cheops

proper noun
ESA exoplanet space telescope
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkiː.ɑːps/ (AmE), /ˈkiː.ɒps/ (BrE)
A European Space Agency (ESA) space telescope mission (CHEOPS) that measures exoplanet transits to determine planet sizes and study known exoplanets.

Examples

ESA Cheops mission
ESA's Cheops satellite measured the tiny dip in starlight as the planet passed in front.
The research team used data from Cheops to refine the exoplanet's radius and density estimates.
During the press briefing, the scientist announced that Cheops had found a smaller-than-expected planet.
