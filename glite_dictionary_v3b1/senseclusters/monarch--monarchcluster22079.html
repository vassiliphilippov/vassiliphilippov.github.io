monarch

noun
hereditary ruler or head of state
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnɑːrk / (AmE), / ˈmɒnək / (BrE)
a nation's ruler or head of state, especially one who inherits the position by birth

Examples

A monarch rules
Each monarch is expected to serve as a symbol of national unity.
Queen Elizabeth II was the longest-reigning monarch in British history.
The country abolished its monarch and became a republic in 1946.

Related Terms

  • synonym: king - male hereditary ruler of a nation or territory
  • antonym: president - elected head of a republic, contrasted with monarch
  • synonym: queen - female hereditary ruler of a nation or territory
