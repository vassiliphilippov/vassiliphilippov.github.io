favor

verb
grant a privilege or show special kindness
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪvɚ /; / ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to show preference, support, or special kindness to someone or something; to treat with special care

Examples

To favor a friend
She felt favored when her supervisor gave her the most interesting assignments.
The policy favors individuals with prior experience, giving them an advantage in hiring.
The teacher favored certain students by allowing them extra time on the exam.
His promotion was seen as proof that the manager favored him above the rest of the team.
Many people believe the teacher tends to favor the most outspoken students during class discussions.
The company was criticized for favoring certain suppliers over others.
During the renovation, workers were instructed to favor the original woodwork, preserving as much as they could.
She walked on her injured ankle, but tried to favor it as much as possible.
The coach tended to favor the younger players by giving them extra attention during training.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disfavor - to withhold approval or privileges from someone
  • antonym: disfavour - to treat with disapproval or less preference
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly
  • synonym: prefer - to like one person or thing better than another
  • synonym: privilege - special right or advantage granted to a person
  • synonym: spare - to refrain from treating harshly; to show mercy to
