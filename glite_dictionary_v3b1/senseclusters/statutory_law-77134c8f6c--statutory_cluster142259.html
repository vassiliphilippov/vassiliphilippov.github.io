statutory law

noun
laws created by legislatures
5.9 0.18/m

The body of laws enacted by a legislature, as opposed to laws derived from custom, judicial decisions, or executive action.

Examples

Statutory law enacted by legislature
Attorneys must interpret both statutory law and judicial decisions when preparing cases.
The new regulations were passed as part of statutory law by the state legislature.
Unlike common law, statutory law is written and codified.

Synonyms

  • statute law
    6.2 0.03/m
    statute law consists of written, codified acts passed by a legislature, while statutory law names the formal laws made by that body.
  • legislation
    4.4 41/m
    legislation refers to laws or a set of laws passed by a legislature, while statutory law specifically means those enacted and codified laws.

Related Terms
