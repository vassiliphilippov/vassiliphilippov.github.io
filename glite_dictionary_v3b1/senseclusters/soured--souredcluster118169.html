soured

adjective
turned bad or unpleasant
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊɚd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəd / (BrE)
(of food, relationships, or situations) having become bad, unpleasant, or spoiled, often due to fermentation, decay, or a change in attitude.

Examples

A soured relationship
A soured atmosphere lingered in the office following the announcement.
The milk had soured overnight and was no longer safe to drink.
Their friendship soured after the disagreement last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - of food: not spoiled or decayed
  • synonym: spoiled - of food: having become unfit for eating
