woody

adjective
covered with trees; woodland-like
2.0 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwÊŠdi / (AmE), / ËˆwÊŠdi / (BrE)
(of an area) having many trees; covered with or resembling woodland.

Examples

Woody area
Spring flowers bloom early in the woody corners of the old park.
The air felt cool and fresh in the woody hills above the town.
They decided to camp in a woody area for more shade and privacy.

Synonyms

  • wooded
    2.4 2.82/m
    wooded describes land that is actually covered in trees, while woody describes areas that are tree-filled or resemble woodland.
  • timbered
    4.2 0.04/m
    timbered describes land covered with growing trees, while woody means an area that is tree-filled or resembles woodland.
  • forested
    2.3 0.06/m
    forested means covered by forests or dense woods, while woody describes places with trees or that look like woodland.
  • forestlike
    3.5 0.01/m
    forestlike looks like a full forest in appearance or mood, while woody covers any area with trees or woodland qualities.
  • woodsy
    4.4 0.46/m
    woodsy has the smells or cozy look of woods and feels informal, while woody describes an area simply covered with trees.
