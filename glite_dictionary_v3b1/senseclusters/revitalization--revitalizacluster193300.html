revitalization

noun
the process of making something active or important again
Pronunciation: / riˌvaɪtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / riːˌvaɪtᵊlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / riˌvaɪtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of bringing something back into active existence, prominence, or vitality.

Examples

The revitalization of wetlands
Environmental groups are advocating for the revitalization of local wetlands to support wildlife.
The city invested heavily in the revitalization of its historic downtown area.
The economic revitalization brought new jobs and a renewed sense of community.
After years of decline, the theatre underwent major revitalisation and attracted new audiences.
The government's investment policy focused on the revitalisation of local industries.
The new urban park project led to the revitalisation of the once-neglected city center.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength or quality
  • synonym: restoration - the action of returning something to a former condition
  • synonym: revitalization - making something active or prominent again (American spelling)
  • synonym: revival - an instance of something becoming active or popular again
