Tasmanian tiger

noun
extinct striped carnivorous Tasmanian marsupial
9.5
Pronunciation: / tæzˈmeɪniən ˈtaɪɡər / (AmE), / tazˈmeɪniən ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
a doglike carnivorous marsupial native to Tasmania, with stripes on its back, thought to be extinct since the 20th century

Examples

The Tasmanian tiger animal
Many people believe the Tasmanian tiger became extinct in the 20th century.
Scientists are searching for evidence that the Tasmanian tiger might still exist in remote parts of Tasmania.
The stripes on the back of the Tasmanian tiger resembled those of a real tiger.
