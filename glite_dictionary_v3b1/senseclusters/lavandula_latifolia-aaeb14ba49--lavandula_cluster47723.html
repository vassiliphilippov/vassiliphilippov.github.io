Lavandula latifolia

noun
species of spike lavender plant
8.4
Pronunciation: / ləˌvændjələ læˈtɪfoʊliə / (AmE), / ləˌvændjʊlə læˈtɪfəʊliə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean plant with pale purple flowers, also known as spike lavender, that is cultivated for its aromatic oil.

Examples

Lavandula latifolia plant
Lavandula latifolia is commonly grown in Mediterranean gardens for its fragrant oils.
The essential oil of Lavandula latifolia is often used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
Unlike common lavender, Lavandula latifolia has broader leaves and a more pungent scent.
