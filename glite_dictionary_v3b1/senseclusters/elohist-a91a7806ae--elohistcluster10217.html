Elohist

noun
biblical source theorist
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈe.lə.hɪst/ /; / /ˈe.loʊ.hɪst/ (AmE), /ˈe.lə.hɪst/ /; / /ˈe.əʊ.hɪst/ (BrE)
a scholar or supporter of the theory that parts of the Hebrew Bible were written by an author referred to as the Elohist

Examples

An Elohist scholar
The Elohist perspective emphasizes the use of 'Elohim' as the name for God in certain biblical passages.
As an Elohist, she contributed to debates about the origins of the Pentateuch.
Many scholars identify as either a Elohist or a Yahwist when discussing biblical authorship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Documentary Hypothesis - the theory that the Hebrew Bible is composed of sources from different authors
  • antonym: Yahwist - a scholar or supporter of the theory that parts of the Hebrew Bible were written by the Yahwist source
