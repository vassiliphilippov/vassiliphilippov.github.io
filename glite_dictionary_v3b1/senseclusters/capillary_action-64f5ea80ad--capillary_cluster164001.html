capillary action

noun
movement of liquid in narrow spaces due to surface tension
7.7 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkæp.ɪ.lər.iː ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /kə.ˈpɪl.ər.iː ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈpɪl.ə.riː ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkæp.ɪ.lə.riː ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a liquid moves upwards or downwards through a narrow space, such as a thin tube, due to the forces of cohesion, adhesion, and surface tension

Examples

Capillary action in tubes
Engineers consider capillary action when designing devices that involve very small tubes.
Ink spreads through paper fibers by capillary action when you touch a pen to the page.
The movement of water up a plant stem is an example of capillary action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surface tension - the elastic tendency of a fluid surface that makes it acquire the least surface area possible
