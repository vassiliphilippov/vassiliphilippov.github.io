Niger-Kordofanian language

noun
African language family south of the Sahara
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪdʒər kɔrˌdoʊfəˈneɪniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈniːdʒə kɔːˌdɒfəˈneɪniən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of languages spoken primarily in sub-Saharan Africa, characterized by tonal languages and including major languages such as Swahili and Fula

Examples

Niger-Kordofanian language family
Comparative studies of the Niger-Kordofanian language family reveal both similarities and significant differences.
Many linguists consider Swahili to be a member of the Niger-Kordofanian language family.
The Niger-Kordofanian language group encompasses hundreds of African languages.
