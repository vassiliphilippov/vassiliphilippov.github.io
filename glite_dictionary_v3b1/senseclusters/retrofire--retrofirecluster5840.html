retrofire

verb
to slow with rockets
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌfaɪə / (BrE)
to ignite a rocket engine in the opposite direction of travel to slow down or change course

Examples

To retrofire engines
The spacecraft will retrofire its engines before re-entering the atmosphere.
Mission control instructed the probe to retrofire in order to enter orbit around Mars.
If the rocket does not retrofire at the correct time, it could miss its landing site.
