long term

noun
extended period of time
1.0
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ tɜrm / (AmE), / lɒŋ tɜːm / (BrE)
a period of time that extends far into the future; a duration that is not short or immediate

Examples

The long term future
Investing in education pays off in the long term.
Quick fixes may help now, but they're often harmful in the long term.
She prefers to focus on goals that will benefit her in the long term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short term - a brief or immediate period
