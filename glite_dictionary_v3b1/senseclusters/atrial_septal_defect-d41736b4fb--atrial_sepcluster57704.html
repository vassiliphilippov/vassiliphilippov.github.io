atrial septal defect

noun
abnormal opening in heart atria (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˈsɛp.təl dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.tri.əl ˈsɛp.təl dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a congenital heart condition characterized by an abnormal opening between the left and right atria

Examples

An atrial septal defect condition
Early detection of an atrial septal defect is important for effective treatment.
Surgery may be required to repair a congenital atrial septal defect in adults.
The doctor diagnosed the child with an atrial septal defect after noticing unusual heart sounds.
