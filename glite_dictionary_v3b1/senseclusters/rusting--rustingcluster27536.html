rusting

noun
formation of rust on iron
3.7 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌ.stɪŋ/ /; / /ˈrʌs.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈrʌ.stɪŋ/ /; / /ˈrʌs.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
The process by which iron or steel is chemically changed into a reddish-brown oxide, especially through exposure to water and oxygen.

Examples

Rusting metal
Exposure to water accelerated the rusting of the garden tools.
Scientists study rusting to find ways to protect steel structures.
The rusting of the old bridge weakened its metal supports over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrosion - the process of gradual destruction of metals by chemical reaction
