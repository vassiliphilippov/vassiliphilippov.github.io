paraboloid reflector

noun
device producing/focusing parallel beams
5.7 7.3
Pronunciation: /pær.ə.ˈbɑːl.ɔɪd rɪ.ˈflek.tər/ (AmE), /pær.ə.ˈbɒl.ɔɪd rɪ.ˈflek.tə/ (BrE)
A concave mirror or surface shaped like a paraboloid, used either to produce a parallel beam of light or other energy when a source is placed at its focus, or to focus incoming parallel rays onto its focus.

Examples

A paraboloid reflector mirror
A paraboloid reflector is commonly used in telescope design to collect and focus light from distant stars.
Engineers installed a paraboloid reflector in the headlamp to create a concentrated beam of light.
The efficiency of the radio dish depends largely on the precision of its paraboloid reflector.

  • synonym: parabolic reflector - a reflecting surface shaped as a paraboloid of revolution
