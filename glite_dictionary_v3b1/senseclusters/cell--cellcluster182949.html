cell

verb
to confine in a cell
Pronunciation: / sɛl / (AmE), / sɛl / (BrE)
to confine someone in a prison or holding cell.

Examples

To cell a prisoner
The police celled the suspect for the night until bail was posted.
After the protest, several people were celled for disturbing the peace.
He was celled alone to prevent him from contacting other inmates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imprison - to put someone in prison
  • antonym: release - to set someone free from confinement
