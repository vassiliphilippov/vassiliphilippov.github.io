corner

noun
temporary monopoly in a market
4.8 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔːr.nər/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.nə/ (BrE)
a temporary monopoly or control of the supply of a particular commodity or product in order to manipulate its price

Examples

A corner on the market
A group of investors tried to gain a corner in the cocoa trade by buying up all supplies.
During the 1980s, there was an attempt to establish a corner on the silver market.
The company managed to corner the market in rare gems for a few months.
