propertied

adjective
having property or wealth
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.pɚ.tid / (AmE), / ˈprɒp.ə.tid / (BrE)
owning property, especially land or other valuable assets, usually as a principal source of income or wealth.

Examples

The propertied class
Many propertied families have remained wealthy through generations of land ownership.
The propertied classes held significant influence over political decisions in the nineteenth century.
Unlike the majority of citizens, the propertied elite were eligible to vote in early elections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: propertyless - without property or land
