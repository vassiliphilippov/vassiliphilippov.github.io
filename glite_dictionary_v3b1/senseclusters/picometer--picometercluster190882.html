picometer

noun
metric unit, one trillionth of a meter
9.3 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪkoʊˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈpɪkəˌmiːtə / (BrE)
A metric unit of length equal to one trillionth (10⁻¹²) of a meter.

Examples

A picometer unit
A picometer is far too small to be seen with a regular microscope.
Scientists measure atomic bond lengths in picometers.
The width of a hydrogen atom is about 50 picometers.
Some atomic radii are as small as 50 picometres.
The bond length between certain atoms is less than 100 picometres.
The wavelength of X-rays can be measured in picometres.
