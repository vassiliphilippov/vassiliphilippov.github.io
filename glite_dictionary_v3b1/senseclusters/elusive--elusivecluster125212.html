elusive

adjective
difficult to understand or solve
5.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
difficult to understand, define, or describe; hard to grasp mentally

Examples

Elusive meaning
For many, the meaning of the ancient symbol is still elusive.
Success proved elusive for the young athlete in her first year.
The solution to the puzzle remained elusive, despite hours of effort.
Despite months of research, the solution to the problem remained elusive.
Happiness can sometimes feel like an elusive goal, always just out of reach.
The meaning behind his cryptic message was elusive, leaving everyone puzzled.
She tried to capture the elusive aroma in words, but nothing seemed quite accurate.
The meaning of the poem remained elusive even after several readings.
There was an elusive charm about the old painting that was hard to explain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baffling - impossible to understand; perplexing
  • antonym: comprehensible - able to be understood; intelligible
  • synonym: evasive - tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation
  • synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe
  • antonym: obvious - easy to perceive or understand; clear, self-evident
  • antonym: tangible - clear and definite, able to be touched or perceived
