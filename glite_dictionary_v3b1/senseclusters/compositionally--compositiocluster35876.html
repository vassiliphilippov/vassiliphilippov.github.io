compositionally

adverb
in terms of structure
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃənəli / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃənəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to how something is made, organized, or structured, especially in language, art, or writing

Examples

Compositionally analyze a sentence
The sentence can be interpreted compositionally by analyzing the meaning of each word.
Artists often think compositionally when arranging elements in a painting.
To solve the problem, the team approached it compositionally, breaking it down into smaller parts.
The sculpture is compositionally complex, with many interlocking shapes and forms.
Her essay is compositionally strong, with clear organization and logical flow.
The music is compositionally innovative, blending different genres and rhythms.

  • synonym: structurally - in a way that relates to structure or arrangement
