drain

verb
(of liquid) flow off gradually
4.6
Pronunciation: / dreɪn / (AmE), / dreɪn / (BrE)
to remove or let liquid flow out of something; (of liquid) to flow away from somewhere

Examples

To drain the water
After the heavy rain, the water began to drain from the flooded fields.
Make sure the pasta water drains completely before adding the sauce.
The bathtub wouldn’t drain because the pipe was blocked.
After cooking the pasta, remember to drain the water from the pot.
Rainwater collectors are designed to drain water away from the roof quickly.
The mechanic had to drain the oil before changing the filter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accumulate - to gather or collect (opposite: not flow away)
  • synonym: discharge - (of liquid) to flow out or be released
  • synonym: empty - remove or make free of contents, especially liquid
  • antonym: fill - to make full, especially with liquid; opposite of drain
