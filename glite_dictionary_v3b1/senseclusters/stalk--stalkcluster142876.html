stalk

verb
move stealthily to hunt prey
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)
to move quietly and carefully through an area in search of prey

Examples

To stalk prey
He spent hours stalking pheasants in the fields.
In autumn, many hunters stalk the forest hoping to find deer.
The tiger silently stalked its prey through the dense undergrowth.
