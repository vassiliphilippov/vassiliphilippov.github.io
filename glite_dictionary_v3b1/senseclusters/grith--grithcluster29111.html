grith

noun
period of truce
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪθ / (AmE), / ɡrɪθ / (BrE)
a state or period of peace or truce, especially an agreement of safety between former enemies

Examples

Grith between rivals
The two kingdoms declared a grith after decades of bitter fighting.
During the annual festival, a sacred grith was observed by all clans.
Merchants could travel safely under the protection of the grith.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peace - a state of calm or absence of conflict
  • synonym: truce - an agreement to stop fighting for a time
  • antonym: war - a state of armed conflict between groups
