tenotomy

noun
surgical cutting of tendon
Pronunciation: / tɛˈnɑtəmi / (AmE), / tɪˈnɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a tendon is cut to relieve tension or correct a deformity

Examples

A tenotomy surgery
The surgeon recommended a tenotomy to help the patient regain movement in his foot.
After the tenotomy, the child's walking improved significantly.
A tenotomy may be necessary when other treatments for tight tendons are not effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: myotomy - surgical cutting of a muscle
  • antonym: tenorrhaphy - surgical repair of a tendon
