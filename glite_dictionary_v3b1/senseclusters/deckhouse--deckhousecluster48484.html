deckhouse

noun
ship's deck structure
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈdɛkˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a small structure built on the deck of a ship, used for shelter, storage, or as a workspace

Examples

Ship deckhouse
The crew took shelter in the deckhouse when the weather turned rough.
Supplies were stored safely inside the deckhouse during the long voyage.
From the deckhouse, the captain had a clear view of the entire ship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cabin - a small room or shelter on a ship
  • antonym: hold - the main storage area of a ship below deck
