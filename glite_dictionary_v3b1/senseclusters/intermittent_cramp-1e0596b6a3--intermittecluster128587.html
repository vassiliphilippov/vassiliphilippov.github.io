intermittent cramp

noun
recurring muscular cramps (medical)
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tɚˈmɪt.ənt kræmp / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈmɪt.ənt kræmp / (BrE)
A clinical syndrome characterized by periodic muscular twitching, cramps, and sometimes seizures, often linked to calcium or vitamin D deficiency.

Examples

Painful intermittent cramp
She was diagnosed with intermittent cramp after reporting episodes of painful muscle twitching.
The patient experienced intermittent cramp in his legs due to a calcium deficiency.
Treatments for intermittent cramp often focus on correcting underlying biochemical imbalances.
