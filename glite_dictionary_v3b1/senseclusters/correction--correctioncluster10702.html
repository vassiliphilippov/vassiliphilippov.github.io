correction

noun
something replacing an error
1.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛkʃən /; / kəˈrɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkʃən /; / kəˈrɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of fixing, remedying, or making right a mistake, error, or defect

Examples

A correction to the report

After reviewing my math homework, the teacher made a correction in the last problem.
He sent the editor a correction for the factually incorrect statement in the article.
Please submit any correction before the final report is published.
He underwent correction of his vision through laser eye surgery.
Proper dental correction can help prevent long-term oral health issues.
The correction of spinal curvature may require surgery in severe cases.
