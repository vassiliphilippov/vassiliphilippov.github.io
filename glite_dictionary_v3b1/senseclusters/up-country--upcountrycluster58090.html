up-country

adverb
in or towards the interior of a country
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌkʌntri / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkʌntri / (BrE)
towards or in the interior, away from the coastal or urban areas of a country or region

Examples

Travel up-country
After the long train ride, we finally arrived up-country just before sunset.
She moved up-country for a quieter lifestyle and more space.
Supplies are harder to get up-country than in the bustling capital.
