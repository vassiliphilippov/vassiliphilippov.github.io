drowse

verb
sleep lightly; doze
6.7
Pronunciation: / draʊz / (AmE), / draʊz / (BrE)
to sleep lightly or be half asleep, often for a short time

Examples

To drowse in a chair
I tend to drowse on trains, even if I'm not tired.
She began to drowse in her chair during the long meeting.
The warm afternoon sun made him drowse by the window.
After lunch, he often drowses at his desk for a few minutes.
She began to drowse as the train gently rocked back and forth.
The professor noticed a few students drowsing in the warm lecture hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doze - to sleep lightly and briefly, especially during the day
  • degree higher: slumber - to sleep
