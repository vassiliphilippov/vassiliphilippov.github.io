tartuffian

adjective
hypocritically moral
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɑrˈtuːfiən / (AmE), / tɑːˈtuːfiən / (BrE)
behaving in a hypocritical or insincerely moral manner, especially by pretending to be virtuous

Examples

Tartuffian behavior
The politician's tartuffian promises failed to convince the public of his honesty.
She grew tired of his tartuffian lectures about morality while he ignored his own advice.
Their leader's tartuffian behavior eventually led to distrust among the group.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypocritical - pretending to have moral standards or beliefs that are not actually held
  • antonym: sincere - genuine and honest in feelings or actions
