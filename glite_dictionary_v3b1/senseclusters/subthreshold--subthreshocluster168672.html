subthreshold

adjective
below diagnostic criteria
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈθrɛʃhoʊld / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
not strong or numerous enough to meet the official criteria for a diagnosis or condition, especially in medicine or psychiatry

Examples

Subthreshold symptoms
The patient displayed subthreshold symptoms that did not qualify for a formal diagnosis.
Researchers studied individuals with subthreshold anxiety to better understand early warning signs.
Her mood changes were considered subthreshold and did not meet the criteria for depression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minimal - very small in amount or degree
  • antonym: threshold - the level at which something starts or changes, especially for diagnosis
