American redstart

noun
North American warbler with orange markings
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈrɛdˌstɑrt / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈrɛdˌstɑːt / (BrE)
a small North American warbler (Setophaga ruticilla) known for the male's bright orange patches on its sides, wings, and tail

Examples

The American redstart bird
Female American redstarts have more subdued colors compared to the males.
The American redstart migrates south for the winter each year.
We saw a brilliant male American redstart flashing its orange feathers in the forest.
