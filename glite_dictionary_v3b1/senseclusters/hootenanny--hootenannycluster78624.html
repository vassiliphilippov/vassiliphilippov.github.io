hootenanny

noun
folk music gathering
9.7
Pronunciation: /huː.tə.ˈnæn.i/ (AmE), /huː.tə.ˈnæn.i/ (BrE)
an informal, lively gathering or party with folk music, singing, and sometimes dancing

Examples

A lively hootenanny
We brought our guitars to the hootenanny and sang folk songs late into the night.
Anyone can join in and perform at a hootenanny, making it a welcoming event for all.
The community center hosts a monthly hootenanny where people share music and stories.
We spent Saturday night at a lively hootenanny with friends and local musicians.
Anyone can join in and sing at the weekly hootenanny downtown.
The tradition of the hootenanny brings people together to share songs and stories.

The community center hosted a hootenanny that brought neighbors together for food and fun.
Everyone was welcome to join the hootenanny and sing along with the band.
