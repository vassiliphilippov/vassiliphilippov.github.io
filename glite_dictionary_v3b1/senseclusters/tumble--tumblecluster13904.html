Synonyms

  • take a tumble
    5.4 0.21/m
    take a tumble is an informal phrase for falling suddenly, while tumble describes falling or rolling uncontrolled.
  • fall over
    1.0 3.06/m
    fall over means losing balance and hitting the ground, while tumble adds turning or rolling in an uncontrolled way.
  • fall down
    1.0 7.45/m
    fall down is to suddenly drop to the ground after losing balance, while tumble often includes turning or rolling.
  • roll
    1.0 77/m
    roll means turning over or moving smoothly, while tumble means falling or rolling suddenly and out of control.
  • fall
    1.0 164/m
    fall is a general drop by gravity from a higher place, while tumble adds sudden turning or uncontrolled rolling.
  • topple
    4.1 7.56/m
    topple means to fall over or make something fall suddenly, while tumble more often describes uncontrolled turning and rolling.
  • tip over
    3.0 1.40/m
    tip over means to cause an upright thing to fall or overturn, while tumble usually describes a person falling and rolling.
