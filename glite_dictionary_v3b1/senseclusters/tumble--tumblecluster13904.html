tumble

verb
make fall or topple by pushing
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌm.bəl /; / ˈtʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʌm.bəl /; / ˈtʌmbəl / (BrE)
to fall, roll, or move suddenly and uncontrollably, often turning over and over

Examples

To tumble down stairs
A gust of wind tumbled the vase off the windowsill.
He reached out and tumbled his opponent with a quick shove.
She accidentally tumbled the stack of books onto the floor.
Rocks tumbled down the hillside after the heavy rain.
She tumbled down the stairs but wasn't badly hurt.
The old bookshelf began to shake and then tumbled to the ground with a crash.
Clothes tumble in the dryer until they are dry and fluffy.
The leaves tumbled around the yard in the autumn wind.
Thick, dark smoke tumbled from the burning building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall - to drop down from a higher place
  • antonym: settle - to stop moving or spinning and come to rest
  • synonym: spin - to turn around and around quickly
  • synonym: topple - to make something lose its balance and fall over
  • synonym: whirl - to move rapidly around in a circle or similar curve
