Lavandula angustifolia

noun
species of lavender with fragrant lilac flowers
9.1
Pronunciation: / ləˌvændʒələ ænˌɡʌstɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ləˌvændjʊlə ænˌɡʌstɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A species of aromatic Mediterranean shrub, commonly called English lavender, widely cultivated for its fragrant lilac flowers used in sachets and perfumes.

Examples

Lavandula angustifolia plant
Lavandula angustifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
Gardeners often plant Lavandula angustifolia for its beautiful aroma and attractive lilac flowers.
The essential oil extracted from Lavandula angustifolia is widely used for its calming properties.
