propaganda

noun
biased information promoting a cause
1.0 5.0
Pronunciation: /prɑː.pə.ˈgæn.də/ /; / /prə.pə.ˈgæn.də/ (AmE), /prɒ.pə.ˈgæn.də/ /; / /prə.pə.ˈgæn.də/ (BrE)
Information, ideas, or rumors deliberately spread widely to help or harm a person, group, movement, institution, nation, or cause.

Examples

Political propaganda
During wartime, governments often distribute propaganda to gain public support for their actions.
Social media has become a new tool for spreading political propaganda.
The documentary explores how propaganda shaped public opinion in the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: brainwashing - indoctrination that forces a person to adopt radically different beliefs
  • synonym: disinformation - false information spread deliberately to deceive
  • degree lower: publicity - the giving out of information about a product or person for promotional purposes
