lie detector

noun
device measuring signs of lying (polygraph)
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪ dɪˌtɛktər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ dɪˌtɛktə / (BrE)
A device, commonly known as a polygraph, that measures and records physiological indicators such as blood pressure, pulse, and respiration, which are sometimes taken as evidence of whether a person is lying.

Examples

Lie detector test
He volunteered to take a lie detector test to clear his name.
Some people question the reliability of a lie detector in court proceedings.
The police used a lie detector during their investigation to determine if the suspect was telling the truth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polygraph - an instrument that records physiological data and is used as a lie detector
