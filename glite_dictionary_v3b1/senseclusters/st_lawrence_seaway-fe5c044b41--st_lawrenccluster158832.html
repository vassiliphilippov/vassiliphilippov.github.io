St. Lawrence Seaway

proper noun
waterway linking Great Lakes to the Atlantic Ocean
4.9
Pronunciation: /seɪnt ˈlɔːr.əns ˈsiː.weɪ/ (AmE), /seɪnt ˈlɒ.rəns ˈsiː.weɪ/ (BrE)
a system of locks, canals, and channels linking the Great Lakes with the Atlantic Ocean through the Saint Lawrence River, allowing oceangoing ships to reach the interior of North America

Examples

The St. Lawrence Seaway system
Many shipping companies rely on the St. Lawrence Seaway for efficient transportation of goods.
Oceangoing vessels travel through the St. Lawrence Seaway to reach cities on the Great Lakes.
The construction of the St. Lawrence Seaway transformed North American maritime trade.
