phonotactic

adjective
sound combination rules
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊnəˈtæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊnəˈtæktɪk / (BrE)
relating to the rules that determine which sound combinations are allowed in a particular language

Examples

The phonotactic rules
The phonotactic rules of Japanese do not allow certain consonant clusters found in English.
Linguists study phonotactic constraints to understand why some words are easier to pronounce.
Children learn the phonotactic patterns of their native language as they acquire speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phonological - relating to the sound system of a language
