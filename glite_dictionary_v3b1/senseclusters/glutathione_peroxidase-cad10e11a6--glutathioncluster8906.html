glutathione peroxidase

noun
enzyme protecting against oxidative damage
Pronunciation: / gluːˌtæθiˈoʊn ˌpɛrəksaɪˈdeɪs / (AmE), / ɡlʊˌtæθɪˈəʊn ˌpɛrɒksaɪˈdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme in the body that protects cells from oxidative damage by breaking down hydrogen peroxide and organic peroxides

Examples

Glutathione peroxidase enzyme
A deficiency in glutathione peroxidase can increase susceptibility to cell damage.
Researchers measured glutathione peroxidase levels in the patients' blood samples.
The activity of glutathione peroxidase helps protect the body from oxidative stress.
