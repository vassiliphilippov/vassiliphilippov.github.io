beta rhythm

noun
type of brainwave in wakefulness
8.8 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.tə ˈrɪð.əm/ (AmE), /ˈbiː.tə ˈrɪð.əm/ (BrE)
a pattern of brainwaves seen in an awake and alert person, with a frequency between 12 and 30 hertz, as recorded on an electroencephalogram (EEG)

Examples

Beta rhythm brainwaves
Doctors noted a decrease in beta rhythm activity during the patient's drowsy state.
Researchers use electroencephalograms to measure the presence of beta rhythm in different mental conditions.
The beta rhythm is most prominent when a person is focused and alert.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: alpha rhythm - brainwave pattern prominent during relaxed wakefulness
  • degree higher: gamma rhythm - faster brainwave pattern linked to heightened perception and consciousness
  • degree lower: theta rhythm - slower brainwave pattern, common in sleep or drowsiness
