time-out

noun
a break in play during a game
Pronunciation: / taɪmˈaʊt / (AmE), / taɪmˈaʊt / (BrE)
a brief period during which play is suspended in a sporting event, usually to discuss strategy or allow players to rest

Examples

A time-out in game
After the referee blew the whistle, both teams returned to their benches for a time-out.
The coach called a time-out to give his players a quick rest.
We only have one time-out left for the second half, so use it wisely.
