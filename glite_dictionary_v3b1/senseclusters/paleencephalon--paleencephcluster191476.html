paleencephalon

noun
primitive parts of the brain
7.0 9.9
Pronunciation: /peɪ.liː.en.ˈsef.əl.ɑːn/ /; / /peɪ.lɪ.en.ˈsef.əl.ɑːn/ (AmE), /peɪ.liː.en.ˈsef.əl.ɒn/ /; / /peɪ.lɪ.en.ˈsef.əl.ɒn/ (BrE)
the primitive parts of the brain, phylogenetically older than the cerebral cortex, including structures such as the brainstem and basal ganglia

Examples

The paleencephalon parts
Research on the paleencephalon has helped scientists understand the evolution of the nervous system.
The paleencephalon controls many basic survival functions that are present in all vertebrates.
Unlike the neocortex, the paleencephalon is responsible for instinctive behaviors.
Damage to the palaeencephalon can affect basic functions such as breathing and heart rate.
The evolutionary distinction between the palaeencephalon and neocortex is crucial in brain research.
Unlike the neocortex, the palaeencephalon manages instinctive and survival behaviors.
