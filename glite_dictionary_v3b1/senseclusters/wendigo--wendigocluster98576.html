wendigo

noun
extremely greedy person
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛndɪˌɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈwɛndɪɡəʊ / (BrE)
a person who is extremely greedy or insatiable, especially for money or possessions, used figuratively

Examples

A wendigo for money
After the company layoffs, some employees saw the new CEO as a wendigo who valued profit over people.
His endless desire for more property made him seem like a wendigo to his neighbors.
The novel's villain is portrayed as a modern wendigo, always hungry for more wealth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little as possible
  • antonym: philanthropist - a person who seeks to promote the welfare of others, especially by donating money
