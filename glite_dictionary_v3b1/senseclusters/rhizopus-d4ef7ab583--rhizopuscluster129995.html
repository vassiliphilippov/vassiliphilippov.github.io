Rhizopus

noun
genus of rot-causing fungi
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪzəpəs / (AmE), / ˈrɪzəpəs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi that causes rot and is characterized by columnar hemisphere-shaped aerial sporangia anchored by rhizoids.

Examples

Rhizopus fungus
Rhizopus is commonly found on decaying bread and fruit.
Scientists study Rhizopus to understand how fungi cause spoilage in food products.
The presence of Rhizopus in the soil helps decompose organic matter.
