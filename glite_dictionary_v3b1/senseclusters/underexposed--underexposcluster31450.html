underexposed

adjective
insufficiently invested
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərɪkˈspoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪkˈspəʊzd / (BrE)
having too little exposure to a particular risk, market, or asset, especially in finance or risk management

Examples

Underexposed to risk
The portfolio remained underexposed to international markets throughout the year.
Analysts warned that being underexposed to technology stocks could limit potential gains.
Some investors prefer to stay underexposed to volatile sectors to minimize risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diversified - having investments spread across different assets to reduce risk
  • antonym: overexposed - having too much exposure to a particular risk, market, or asset
