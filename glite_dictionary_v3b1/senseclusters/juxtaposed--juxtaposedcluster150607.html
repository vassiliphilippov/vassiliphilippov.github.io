juxtaposed

adjective
placed side by side for comparison or contrast
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌkstəˌpoʊzd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌkstəpəʊzd / (BrE)
Placed close together or side by side, especially for comparison or contrast.

Examples

Juxtaposed colors
Bright colors are often juxtaposed with dark tones in modern design.
The artist's latest exhibit features juxtaposed images to highlight cultural differences.
The two arguments were juxtaposed to make the differences clear.
