acrylic acid

noun
liquid acid used in resins and plastics
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / əˈkrɪlɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent liquid carboxylic acid, CH2=CHCOOH, used in making acrylic resins and polymers

Examples

acrylic acid liquid
Factories use acrylic acid to produce a wide range of acrylic plastics and paints.
Safety procedures are crucial when handling acrylic acid because it can cause irritation.
The demand for acrylic acid has increased due to its applications in superabsorbent polymers.
