reach out

verb
attempt to contact or communicate
2.7 20/m
Pronunciation: / rit É a ät / (AmE), / riÀêt É a ät / (BrE)
to try to communicate with someone, offer help, or make a connection (often in a supportive or friendly way)

Examples

To reach out for help
If you need any assistance, please reach out to our customer service team.
She decided to reach out to her old friend after many years apart.
The organization encourages people to reach out if they are struggling with stress.
During the crisis, many volunteers reached out to help those affected.
She decided to reach out to her old friend after years of silence.
The organization encourages people to reach out if they need support.

Synonyms

  • get in touch
    2.3 5.02/m
    get in touch is a casual way to begin communication after a break, while reach out often implies offering help or support.
  • connect
    1.8 35/m
    connect means forming a real relationship or understanding with someone, while reach out focuses on trying to contact or offer help.
  • reach
    1.3 51/m
    reach means to contact someone by phone or email to get a reply, while reach out suggests a friendly offer or support.
  • make contact
    2.8 3.62/m
    make contact means to establish communication often for the first time or formally, while reach out is more friendly and offers help.
  • extend a hand
    2.9 0.43/m
    extend a hand means to offer help or friendship in a direct way, while reach out also means to contact someone for support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contact - to communicate with someone, especially by phone or letter
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not pay attention or respond to someone
