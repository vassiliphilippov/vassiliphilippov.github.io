Nicaea

proper noun
first ecumenical Christian council (325 AD)
9.9
Pronunciation: / naɪˈsiː.ə / (AmE), / naɪˈsiː.ə / (BrE)
the first ecumenical council of the Christian Church, held in 325 AD in Nicaea, which formulated the Nicene Creed and condemned Arianism as heresy

Examples

First Council of Nicaea
Debates at Nicaea led to the creation of the Nicene Creed.
Many historical discussions refer to the decisions made at Nicaea in 325 AD.
The Council of Nicaea marked a turning point in the development of Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Arianism - a Christian heresy condemned at the Council of Nicaea
  • synonym: Nicene Creed - a statement of Christian faith formalized by the Council of Nicaea
