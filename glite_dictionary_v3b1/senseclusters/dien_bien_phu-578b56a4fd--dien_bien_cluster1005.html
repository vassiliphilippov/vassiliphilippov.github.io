Dien Bien Phu

proper noun
site of decisive 1954 battle in Vietnam
Pronunciation: / diːˌɛn biːˈɛn fuː / (AmE), / diːˌɛn biːˈɛn fuː / (BrE)
A town in northwestern Vietnam, known for the site of a major 1954 battle in which Vietnamese forces defeated the French, ending French rule in Indochina.

Examples

Dien Bien Phu battle
Many historians consider the defeat at Dien Bien Phu the end of French colonial influence in the region.
The Battle of Dien Bien Phu marked a turning point in the history of Southeast Asia.
The museum in Dien Bien Phu documents the events of the famous siege.
