ampholyte

noun
chemical that can act as acid or base
8.8 9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæm.fə.laɪt/ /; / /ˈæm.fə.lɪt/ (AmE), /ˈæm.fə.laɪt/ /; / /ˈæm.fə.lɪt/ (BrE)
A substance that can act as either an acid or a base depending on the pH of its environment.

Examples

An ampholyte substance
Amino acids are classic examples of ampholytes in biological systems.
During isoelectric focusing, ampholytes help establish a stable pH gradient.
The effectiveness of an ampholyte depends on the pH conditions present in the solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buffer - a solution that resists changes in pH when acid or base is added
