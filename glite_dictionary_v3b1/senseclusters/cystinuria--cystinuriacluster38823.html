cystinuria

noun
genetic kidney disorder
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪs.tɪ.njʊ.ˈraɪ.ə/ /; / /sɪs.tɪ.nə.ˈrɪ.ə/ (AmE), /sɪs.tɪ.njʊ.ˈraɪ.ə/ /; / /sɪs.tɪ.nə.ˈrɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a medical disorder in which too much cystine is present in the urine, often causing kidney stones

Examples

Cystinuria causes stones
Doctors often diagnose cystinuria in patients who develop kidney stones at a young age.
A family history of cystinuria increases the risk of recurrent kidney stones.
Treatment for cystinuria usually involves drinking plenty of water and dietary changes.
The doctor diagnosed him with cystinuria after repeated kidney stones were found.
Routine screening revealed cystinuria in the patient's urine sample.
People with cystinuria may need to follow a special diet to prevent stone formation.

  • synonym: nephrolithiasis - the medical term for kidney stones
