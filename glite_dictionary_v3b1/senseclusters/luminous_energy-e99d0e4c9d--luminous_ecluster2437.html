luminous energy

noun
energy associated with visible light
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈluːmɪnəs ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəs ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the energy carried by visible light, often measured as the total light output from a source

Examples

Luminous energy emitted
Scientists measured the luminous energy emitted by the star during the explosion.
The efficiency of a light bulb is often evaluated by how much luminous energy it produces.
The solar panel converts luminous energy from the sun into electricity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radiant energy - energy transmitted in the form of electromagnetic waves, including light
