metatarsal vein

noun
vein in the metatarsal region of the foot
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈtɑrsəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈtɑːsəl veɪn / (BrE)
Any of the veins located in the metatarsal region of the foot, including both dorsal and plantar branches.

Examples

Metatarsal vein injury
An injury to the metatarsal vein may cause significant swelling and bruising in the area.
Doctors monitored blood flow in the patient's metatarsal veins after surgery.
The dorsal metatarsal vein can be seen near the surface of the foot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vein - blood vessel carrying blood to the heart
