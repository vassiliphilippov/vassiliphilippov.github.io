photon

noun
particle of light or electromagnetic radiation
5.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.tɑːn/ /; / /ˈfoʊ.tən/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.tɒn/ /; / /ˈfəʊ.tən/ (BrE)
A particle representing a quantum of light or other electromagnetic radiation, having zero mass and carrying energy.

Examples

A photon of light
A single photon of light can trigger a chemical reaction in the retina.
Lasers work by emitting many photons of the same wavelength in a synchronized way.
The energy of a photon is proportional to its frequency.

  • synonym: quantum - the smallest discrete quantity of a physical property
