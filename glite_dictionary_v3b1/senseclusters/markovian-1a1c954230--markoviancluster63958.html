Markovian

adjective
relating to a Markov process
5.6 7.7
Pronunciation: /mɑːr.ˈkoʊ.vɪ.ən/ (AmE), /mɑː.ˈkəʊ.vɪ.ən/ (BrE)
Relating to or generated by a Markov process, especially one in which the future state depends only on the current state and not on the prior history.

Examples

Markovian chain
A Markovian chain is central to many probabilistic algorithms in computer science.
Because the system is Markovian, its future state does not depend on its history.
The model uses a Markovian approach to predict changes in stock prices over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stochastic - randomly determined, having a random probability distribution or pattern
