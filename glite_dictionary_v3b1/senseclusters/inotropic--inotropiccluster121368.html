inotropic

adjective
affecting muscle strength
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪnəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affecting the strength or force of muscle contraction, especially in the heart

Examples

Inotropic agent
The doctor administered an inotropic agent to increase the patient's heart contractility.
Certain medications have inotropic effects that can help treat heart failure.
Researchers studied the inotropic properties of the new drug in clinical trials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chronotropic - affecting the rate or timing of heart contractions
  • antonym: lusitropic - affecting the relaxation of heart muscle
