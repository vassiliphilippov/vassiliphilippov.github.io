horse latitude

noun
region near 30° N or S with calm winds
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɔrs ˈlætɪtud / (AmE), / hɔːs ˈlætɪtjuːd / (BrE)
either of two regions of calm winds and little precipitation located around 30 degrees north or south of the equator in both hemispheres, typically characterized by light and variable air movements.

Examples

The horse latitude region
Meteorologists study the horse latitude regions to better understand patterns of global air circulation.
Ships were often stranded in the horse latitude areas due to a lack of strong winds.
The horse latitude zones are associated with dry, desert climates on land, such as the Sahara and the Australian Outback.
