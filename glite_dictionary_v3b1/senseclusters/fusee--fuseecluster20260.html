fusee

noun
spirally grooved spindle in mechanical clocks
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / fjuÀêÀàzi / (AmE), / fj äÀàziÀê / (BrE)
a spirally grooved tapered spindle in a mechanical clock or watch, used to equalize the force delivered by the mainspring as it unwinds

Examples

Clock fusee mechanism
A broken fusee can cause a clock to lose power unevenly as the spring unwinds.
Restoring the 18th-century clock required carefully cleaning the fusee and its chain.
The antique pocket watch still had its original fusee to maintain accurate timekeeping.

Synonyms

  • fusee drive
    9.9 0.00/m
    fusee drive refers to the drive mechanism that evens a mainspring's force, while fusee is the tapered grooved spindle part.
