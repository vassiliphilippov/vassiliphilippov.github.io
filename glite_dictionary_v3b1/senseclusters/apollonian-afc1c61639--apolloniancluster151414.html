Apollonian

noun
person of reason
9.9
Pronunciation: /æ.pə.ˈloʊ.niː.ən/ /; / /æ.pə.ˈloʊ.nɪ.ən/ (AmE), /æ.pə.ˈləʊ.niː.ən/ /; / /æ.pə.ˈləʊ.nɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a person who embodies calmness, rationality, order, and self-restraint, often in contrast to emotional or chaotic behavior

Examples

An Apollonian leader
Her calm and thoughtful approach made her the true Apollonian of the group.
In debates, he played the Apollonian role, always favoring logic over emotion.
The novel contrasts an impulsive character with an Apollonian who values order and restraint.
