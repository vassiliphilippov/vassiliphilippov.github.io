obviating

adjective
making something impossible or preventing it
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑb.viˌeɪ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒb.viˌeɪ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
preventing something from happening or making it unnecessary; making something impossible or redundant.

Examples

An obviating solution
He offered an obviating solution that made further discussion unnecessary.
The new procedure acts as an obviating measure against common errors.
The policy has an obviating effect on potential conflicts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allowing - making something possible
  • synonym: preventing - stopping something from happening
