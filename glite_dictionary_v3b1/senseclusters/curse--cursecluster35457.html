curse

verb
to wish harm or invoke evil
1.0
Pronunciation: / kɜrs / (AmE), / kɜːs / (BrE)
to wish harm or misfortune on someone or something, often by invoking supernatural powers

Examples

To curse an enemy
He believed the artifact was cursed and refused to touch it.
Legends say that the gods will curse those who break their oaths.
The old woman threatened to curse anyone who stole from her garden.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bless - to wish good or invoke positive outcomes upon someone
  • synonym: damn - to condemn someone to a bad fate, especially in a religious sense
