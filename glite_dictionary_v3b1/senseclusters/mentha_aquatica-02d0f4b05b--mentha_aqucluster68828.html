Mentha aquatica

noun
a species of European mint with bergamot-like scent
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnθə ˌækwəˈtiːkə / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθə ˌak.wəˈtiː.kə / (BrE)
A species of European mint that grows in wet places, with a fragrance similar to bergamot orange, and naturalized in parts of North America.

Examples

Mentha aquatica plant
Mentha aquatica is often found along the banks of streams and in marshes.
Gardeners appreciate the aromatic leaves of Mentha aquatica for use in herbal teas.
The distinctive scent of Mentha aquatica is reminiscent of bergamot orange and is prized in perfumery.
