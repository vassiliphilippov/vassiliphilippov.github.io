snarl

noun
angry or threatening facial expression
6.9
Pronunciation: / snɑrl / (AmE), / snɑːl / (BrE)
an angry or threatening expression or sound, like a growl, showing hostility or aggression

Examples

A dog's snarl
He greeted me with a snarl instead of a smile.
Her snarl warned everyone to stay away from her desk.
The dog bared its teeth in a threatening snarl.
His voice took on a snarl when he responded to the accusation.
The lion's snarl warned the zookeepers to stay back.
We heard the snarl of the dog as we approached the gate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: growl - a low guttural sound of hostility or anger
  • synonym: scowl - an angry or bad-tempered expression
  • antonym: smile - a happy or friendly facial expression
