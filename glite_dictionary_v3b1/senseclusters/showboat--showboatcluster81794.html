showboat

noun
river steamboat for theatre performances
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊbəʊt / (BrE)
a river steamboat designed or used to offer theatrical performances, especially on the Mississippi River in the 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

A showboat theater
Audiences flocked to see musicals and plays performed on the showboat as it traveled down the Mississippi.
She dreamed of joining a showboat and performing for crowds along the riverbanks.
The arrival of the showboat was the highlight of the summer for the small river town.
