radial-ply

adjective
constructed with cords at right angles (tires)
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiəl plaɪ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl plaɪ / (BrE)
(of a tire) constructed with the cords arranged at 90 degrees to the direction of travel, resulting in a strip under the tread and relatively flexible sidewalls.

Examples

Radial-ply tires
Consumers often prefer radial-ply tires because of their longer tread life.
Many modern vehicles are equipped with radial-ply tires for improved performance and safety.
The radial-ply design allows for a smoother ride compared to older tire constructions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bias-ply - tire construction with diagonal layers (plies) of fabric
  • synonym: radial tire - an automobile tire constructed with radial-ply technology
