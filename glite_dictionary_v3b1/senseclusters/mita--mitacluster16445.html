mita

noun
colonial forced labor system
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːtə / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
a system of forced labor or public service imposed on indigenous people in colonial South America, especially in mining.

Examples

The mita system
The Spanish authorities used the mita to supply workers for the silver mines in Peru.
Under the mita system, indigenous communities were forced to send men to work far from home.
Historians study the impact of mita on the population decline of Andean societies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encomienda - a Spanish colonial system granting colonists the right to demand labor from indigenous people
  • synonym: slavery - the system of owning people as property and forcing them to work
