hoggin

noun
path surfacing material
Pronunciation: / ˈhɒɡɪn / (BrE)
a mixture of clay, gravel, and sand used to surface paths and roadways, especially in gardens

Examples

Hoggin surface layer
The garden path was covered with hoggin to create a natural appearance.
Many traditional English driveways use hoggin instead of concrete or tarmac.
After the rain, the hoggin surface remained firm and easy to walk on.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asphalt - a black, sticky material used for road surfaces
  • synonym: gravel - small stones used for making paths or roads
