disconcerted

adjective
feeling unsettled or thrown off
6.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd…™s.k…ônÀàs…ùÀê.t…™d / (AmE), / Àåd…™s.k…ônÀàs…úÀê.t…™d / (BrE)
feeling unsettled or disturbed, lacking composure because of surprise or confusion

Examples

Disconcerted by surprise
He was disconcerted by her blunt remark, unsure how to respond.
She appeared visibly disconcerted by the unexpected question during the interview.
The sudden applause left the speaker momentarily disconcerted as he searched for his next point.

Synonyms

  • discomposed
    8.3 0.00/m
    discomposed is a formal word for someone whose composure is clearly disturbed, while disconcerted is more common and slightly less formal.
  • discombobulated
    9.9 0.08/m
    discombobulated is an informal, often comic word for being confused and flustered, while disconcerted is more neutral and serious.
  • nonplussed
    8.9 0.18/m
    nonplussed is stunned and unsure how to react, often very surprised, while disconcerted describes being unsettled or disturbed.
  • consternated
    8.4 0.01/m
    consternated feels sudden shock, alarm, and dismay at an event, while disconcerted feels unsettled and off-balance.
  • perturbed
    6.4 0.04/m
    perturbed means anxious and bothered, often over a problem or worry, while disconcerted comes from surprise or confusion.
  • disquieted
    7.0 0.04/m
    disquieted shows a quiet anxious feeling or worry over something, while disconcerted is more about surprise or sudden unsettlement.
