disconcerted

adjective
feeling unsettled or thrown off
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.kənˈsɝː.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kənˈsɜː.tɪd / (BrE)
feeling unsettled or disturbed, lacking composure because of surprise or confusion

Examples

Disconcerted by surprise
He was disconcerted by her blunt remark, unsure how to respond.
She appeared visibly disconcerted by the unexpected question during the interview.
The sudden applause left the speaker momentarily disconcerted as he searched for his next point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: composed - calm and in control of one's emotions
  • synonym: discombobulated - confused and disconcerted
  • antonym: unruffled - not agitated or disturbed; calm
