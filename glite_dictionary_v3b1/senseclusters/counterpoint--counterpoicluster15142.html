counterpoint

noun
contrasting element
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
something that contrasts with or balances another thing, such as an idea, argument, or element

Examples

A counterpoint idea
The bright colors in the design serve as a counterpoint to the otherwise muted palette.
In the novel, the two main characters act as a counterpoint to each other, highlighting their differences.
The lively melody provides a perfect counterpoint to the slow, steady rhythm in the music.
During the debate, she presented a thoughtful counterpoint to the main argument.
The bright colors in the painting serve as a counterpoint to the dark background.
He always tries to find a counterpoint to every suggestion in meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - a situation where two or more parties share the same opinion
  • synonym: contrast - a difference between things that are compared
  • antonym: harmony - a pleasing combination or arrangement of different things
  • synonym: opposition - the act of resisting or contrasting something
