veto pen

noun
pen used to reject legislation
8.3 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.toʊ pɛn/ /; / /ˈvɪ.toʊ pɛn/ (AmE), /ˈviː.təʊ pɛn/ (BrE)
a pen used by a president, governor, or other executive authority to formally reject legislation, symbolizing the act of issuing a veto

Examples

The veto pen used
Political analysts speculated whether the mayor would take out her veto pen on the new ordinance.
The governor's veto pen was responsible for halting several controversial initiatives last year.
The president threatened to use his veto pen if the bill passed both houses of Congress.
