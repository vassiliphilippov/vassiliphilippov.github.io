claypan

noun
shallow clay basin
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkleɪ.pæn/ /; / /ˈkleɪ.pən/ (AmE), /ˈkleɪ.pæn/ /; / /ˈkleɪ.pən/ (BrE)
a shallow natural basin or flat area with a hard, impermeable clay layer that temporarily holds water after rain

Examples

Water in a claypan
After heavy rain, water collected in the claypan and attracted many birds.
Farmers avoid planting crops in a claypan because the soil drains poorly.
The children explored the dried-up claypan looking for animal tracks.
