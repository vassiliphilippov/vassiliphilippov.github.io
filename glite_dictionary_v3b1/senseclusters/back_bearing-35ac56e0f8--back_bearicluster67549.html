back bearing

noun
opposite direction in navigation
6.8
Pronunciation: /bæk ˈber.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /bæk ˈbeə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a direction that is exactly opposite to a given bearing, used in navigation to confirm position or direction

Examples

Take a back bearing
The navigator took a back bearing to ensure they were on the correct route.
By using a back bearing, the team confirmed their exact location on the map.
A back bearing is helpful when retracing your steps in unfamiliar terrain.
