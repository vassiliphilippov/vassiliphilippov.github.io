final solution

noun
Nazi plan for extermination of Jews
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪnəl səˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl səˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the Nazi policy of systematically exterminating the Jews of Europe during World War II.

Examples

The final solution policy
Historians continue to study the implementation of the final solution during World War II.
Testimonies from survivors provide vital evidence of the atrocities committed under the final solution.
The term final solution refers to the systematic murder of millions of Jews by the Nazis.
