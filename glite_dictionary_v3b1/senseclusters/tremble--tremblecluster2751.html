tremble

noun
involuntary shaking movement
2.7 2.65/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtrÉ›mbÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆtrÉ›mbl / (BrE)
A sudden shaking or quivering movement caused by cold, fear, or excitement.

Examples

A slight tremble
A slight tremble passed through her hands as she gripped the cup.
He noticed a tremble in his voice when he spoke about the accident.
The sudden cold breeze made me feel a tremble run down my spine.

Synonyms

  • shiver
    1.4 4.33/m
    a shiver is an involuntary, often cold-driven shaking in waves, while tremble is a sudden quiver from fear, cold, or excitement.
  • quiver
    3.9 0.70/m
    a quiver is a slight, emotion-linked shaking often in small parts of the body, while tremble is a sudden whole-body quiver.
  • tremor
    5.3 2.42/m
    a tremor is an involuntary, sometimes medical shaking that can last or repeat, while tremble is a sudden short quiver from fear or cold.
  • shake
    1.8 3.23/m
    a shake is a broad back-and-forth movement for objects or people, while tremble is a sudden, small quiver from cold, fear, or excitement.
