sense organ

noun
organ that perceives stimuli
3.5 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / s…õns Àà…îr…°…ôn / (AmE), / s…õns Àà…îÀê…°…ôn / (BrE)
an organ in the body containing nerve endings that respond to external or internal stimuli, enabling the detection of sensations such as sight, hearing, touch, taste, and smell

Examples

A sense organ like the eye
Damage to any sense organ can affect a person's ability to interact with the environment.
Scientists are studying how certain animals develop specialized sense organs for survival.
The human body has five main sense organs: the eyes, ears, skin, nose, and tongue.

Synonyms

  • sensory receptor
    5.8 0.04/m

  • synonym: sense - a faculty by which the body perceives an external stimulus
