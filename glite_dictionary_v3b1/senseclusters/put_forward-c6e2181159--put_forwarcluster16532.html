put forward

verb
propose as a candidate
3.1 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / p ät Ààf…îrw…ôrd / (AmE), / p ät Ààf…îÀêw…ôd / (BrE)
to suggest someone or something as a candidate for an honor, award, or position

Examples

To put forward a candidate
She was put forward for the award by her colleagues due to her outstanding work.
The committee decided to put forward his name as a nominee for the prize.
Three candidates were put forward for the vacant position at the meeting.

Synonyms

  • nominate
    3.4 20/m
    nominate is a formal official suggestion used in committees or awards, while put forward is a more general way to suggest a candidate.
  • propose
    3.4 2.28/m
    propose often means formally suggest a person in meetings or votes, while put forward is used more widely for any candidate suggestion.
  • put up
    4.0 0.23/m
    put up is an informal phrase used to name someone for a post, while put forward is a neutral phrase used in formal settings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nominate - to formally propose as a candidate
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove from consideration as a candidate
