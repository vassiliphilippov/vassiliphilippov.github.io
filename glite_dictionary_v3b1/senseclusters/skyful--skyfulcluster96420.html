skyful

noun
large quantity
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪfʊl / (AmE), / ˈskaɪfʊl / (BrE)
a very large amount of something, often used figuratively to emphasize abundance.

Examples

A skyful of applause
After the concert, the fans gave the band a skyful of applause.
She received a skyful of compliments on her performance at the event.
The garden was filled with a skyful of colorful butterflies in the summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • antonym: scarcity - a very small supply or amount of something
