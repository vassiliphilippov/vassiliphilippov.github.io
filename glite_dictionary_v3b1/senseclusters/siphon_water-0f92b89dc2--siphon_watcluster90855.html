siphon water

verb
draw water through a tube
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪfən ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪfən ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to draw water off or transfer it from one place to another by means of a tube, relying on atmospheric pressure and gravity.

Examples

To siphon water illegally
It's illegal to siphon water from public reservoirs without permission.
She cleverly used a straw to siphon water out of the fish tank without disturbing the fish.
They needed a long hose to siphon water from the flooded cellar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drain - to cause liquid to flow away from something
  • synonym: pump - to move a liquid from one place to another using a pump
