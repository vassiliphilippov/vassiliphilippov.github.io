hin

noun
ancient Hebrew liquid measure (~1.5 gallons)
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɪn / (AmE), / hɪn / (BrE)
an ancient Hebrew unit of liquid measure, equal to about 1.5 gallons (5.7 liters)

Examples

A hin of oil
In Leviticus, a hin of oil was required for the grain offering.
Scholars estimate that a hin is approximately 1.5 gallons in modern units.
The priest measured a hin of wine for the ritual.
