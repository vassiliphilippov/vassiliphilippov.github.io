biogeochemical

adjective
chemical cycles in nature
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˌdʒiːoʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˌdʒiːəʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the movement and interaction of chemical elements between living organisms and the environment

Examples

Biogeochemical cycles
Scientists study biogeochemical cycles to understand how nutrients move through ecosystems.
The biogeochemical processes in the soil affect plant growth and water quality.
Climate change can disrupt biogeochemical cycles, impacting both nature and human societies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemical - relating to chemicals or the science of chemistry
  • synonym: ecological - relating to the relationships between living things and their environment
