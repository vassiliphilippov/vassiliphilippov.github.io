shilling

noun
Kenyan currency unit
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the basic unit of money in several East African countries (such as Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, and Somalia), equal to 100 cents

Examples

Kenyan shilling
A cup of coffee costs around fifty shillings in Nairobi.
He exchanged his dollars for Kenyan shillings at the bank.
The taxi fare was 300 shillings from the airport to the city center.
He paid the taxi driver in shillings after arriving at the hotel.
The price of a cup of tea in Mogadishu is about 1,000 shillings.
Tourists can exchange US dollars for Somali shillings at local banks.
She paid in shillings and received her change promptly.
The market price was listed as 4,000 Tanzanian shillings per kilogram.
Tourists often exchange dollars for shillings upon arrival in Tanzania.
