attest

verb
officially confirm as true or genuine
9.5 5.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtest/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtest/ (BrE)
to officially confirm, provide evidence for, or bear witness that something is true, especially in a formal or legal context

Examples

To attest a document
I hereby attest that all the information provided is accurate and complete.
She attested to the authenticity of the document in court.
The notary public must attest the signatures on the contract.
He will attest in court that he saw the incident happen.
Several witnesses were called to attest to the defendant's alibi.
The expert was asked to attest to the authenticity of the signature.
Linguists attest numerous changes in word usage over time.
No evidence could attest the use of the symbol prior to 1800.
The dictionary does not attest this meaning before the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certify - to officially recognize as true or meeting standards
  • synonym: confirm - to establish the truth or correctness
  • synonym: prove - to demonstrate the truth or existence of something by evidence or argument
  • antonym: refute - to prove that something is not true
  • synonym: testify - to give evidence as a witness in a law court
  • synonym: verify - to confirm the truth or accuracy of something
