Horatian ode

noun
type of structured lyric poem
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɔːˈreɪʃən oʊd / (AmE), / hɒˈreɪʃən əʊd / (BrE)
a lyric poem composed of several stanzas of equal length and meter, modeled after the odes of the Roman poet Horace

Examples

A Horatian ode poem
In English literature, the Horatian ode is known for its calm and reflective tone.
Keats wrote more than one Horatian ode inspired by classical models.
The poet composed a Horatian ode to celebrate the beauty of the countryside.
