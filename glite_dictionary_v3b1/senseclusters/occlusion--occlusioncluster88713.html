occlusion

noun
the act of blocking or obstructing
4.6 8.7
Pronunciation: / əˈkluːʒən /; / əˈkluʒən / (AmE), / əˈkluːʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of blocking or an obstruction that prevents normal flow in a passage, pipe, blood vessel, or organ

Examples

Artery occlusion
A blood clot can cause the occlusion of a major artery.
Fog led to the occlusion of the road, making it dangerous for drivers.
The dentist noticed an occlusion in the patient's tooth alignment.
A buildup of debris can cause an occlusion in your home's water pipes.
The plumber discovered an occlusion in the kitchen drain that needed to be removed.
The technician used specialized equipment to detect the location of the occlusion in the tube.
Dental occlusion refers to how the upper and lower teeth come together.
Sudden occlusion of the carotid artery can lead to a stroke.
The doctor identified an occlusion in the patient's coronary artery during the angiogram.

  • antonym: clearance - the removal of an obstruction
  • synonym: obstruction - the act of blocking or being blocked
  • antonym: unblocking - the removal of a blockage or obstruction
