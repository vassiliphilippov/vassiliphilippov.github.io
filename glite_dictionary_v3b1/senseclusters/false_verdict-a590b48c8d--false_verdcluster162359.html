false verdict

noun
manifestly unjust legal verdict
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈvɜrdɪkt / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈvɜːdɪkt / (BrE)
a verdict in a legal case that is clearly unjust or not supported by the evidence

Examples

A false verdict
A false verdict undermines public trust in the justice system.
The defense argued that the jury had delivered a false verdict based on weak evidence.
Years later, new DNA tests proved the false verdict and exonerated the accused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miscarriage of justice - a wrongful conviction or legal judgment
