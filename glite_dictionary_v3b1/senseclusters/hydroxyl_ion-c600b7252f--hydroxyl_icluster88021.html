hydroxyl ion

noun
negatively charged OH− ion
7.4
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑksɪl aɪˌɑn / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksɪl aɪˌɒn / (BrE)
a negatively charged ion (OH−) consisting of one oxygen and one hydrogen atom

Examples

Hydroxyl ion in solution
The presence of the hydroxyl ion increases the alkalinity of a solution.
The reaction produces both a hydroxyl ion and a sodium ion.
When sodium hydroxide dissolves in water, it releases a hydroxyl ion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydronium ion - the cation H3O+ formed when an acid donates a proton to water
