seamed

adjective
marked with lines or seams (esp. skin)
Pronunciation: / siːmd / (AmE), / siːmd / (BrE)
(especially of skin) marked with lines, ridges, or scars resembling seams.

Examples

Seamed old hands
Her seamed forehead reflected the hardships she had endured.
His cheeks were seamed by years of hard labor under the sun.
The old man's seamed hands told stories of a lifetime of work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lined - marked with lines or wrinkles (especially on the face)
  • antonym: smooth - having an even texture or surface without lines or seams
