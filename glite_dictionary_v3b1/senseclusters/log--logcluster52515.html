log

verb
record events or data in a logbook
Pronunciation: / lɑɡ / (AmE), / lɒɡ / (BrE)
to make an official or systematic record of events, activities, or information

Examples

To log a call
Every phone call to air traffic control is carefully logged for record-keeping purposes.
Pilots are required to log their flight hours accurately.
The crew must log each change in speed in the ship’s journal.
Don't forget to log your findings before leaving the site for the day.
Inspectors are required to log every safety issue they discover during their rounds.
Researchers must log each experiment's results in the official laboratory notebook.

Related Terms

  • synonym: document - to create an official record of something
  • antonym: erase - to remove recorded information or data
  • synonym: record - to set down in writing or some other permanent form for later reference
