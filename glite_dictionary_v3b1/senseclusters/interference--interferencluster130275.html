interference

noun
the act of hindering or obstructing
3.1 3.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈfɪr.əns/ /; / /ɪn.ər.ˈfɪr.əns/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈfɪə.rəns/ (BrE)
the act of getting involved in a situation or process in a way that hinders, obstructs, or impedes progress or activity

Examples

Unwanted interference
He resented his boss’s constant interference in his project.
The referee called a penalty for interference during the hockey game.
Too much government interference can slow down economic growth.
He accused the foreign nation of undue interference in their trade negotiations.
International law prohibits interference in the domestic affairs of sovereign states.
The government denied any interference in the neighboring country's political crisis.
Heavy rain caused an interference with our travel plans yesterday.
She considered his suggestions as unwanted interference in her personal affairs.
The constant interference from the audience made it difficult for the speaker to continue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assistance - the act of helping or supporting
  • synonym: hindrance - something that prevents or delays progress
  • synonym: intervention - the action of becoming intentionally involved in a difficult situation, in order to improve it or prevent it from getting worse
  • synonym: obstruction - the act of blocking or hindering progress
