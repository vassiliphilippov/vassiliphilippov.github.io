rebato

noun
17th-century stiff lace collar
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbeɪtoʊ / (AmE), / rɪˈbɑːtəʊ / (BrE)
A stiffened or wired lace collar, elaborate in design, worn in the 17th century, especially by women.

Examples

A rebato collar
Her costume included a replica rebato to capture the elegance of 17th-century fashion.

The queen entered the hall with a splendid rebato framing her face.
