idle

verb
be inactive or do nothing
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.dəl/ (BrE)
to spend time doing nothing; to exist in an inactive or unchanging state

Examples

To idle away time
He prefers to idle away his afternoons reading on the balcony.
Some workers idle when the manager is not present.
The car engine continued to idle while they talked outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laze - to spend time in a relaxed or lazy way
  • degree lower: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax
  • antonym: work - to be engaged in activity; antonym of idle
