splash

noun
sensational but brief news event
3.8
Pronunciation: / splæʃ / (AmE), / splæʃ / (BrE)
a prominent or attention-grabbing but short-lived news event or public appearance

Examples

A news splash
Although the scandal created a huge splash, it faded from public attention within weeks.
Her sudden resignation made a big splash in the media.
The invention caused quite a splash when it was first announced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sensation - a sudden, intense, and widely publicized event
