Slavophile

noun
admirer of Slavs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈslɑːvəˌfaɪl /; / ˈslævəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈslɑːvəˌfaɪl /; / ˈslævəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a person who admires or supports Slavic peoples, their culture, or the ideas of Slavophilism (especially in 19th-century Russia)

Examples

A Slavophile thinker
In the nineteenth century, the Slavophile movement influenced Russian literature and politics.
She considered herself a Slavophile after studying the history and languages of Eastern Europe.
The conference attracted many Slavophiles interested in Slavic traditions and art.
As a proud Slavophile, he promoted Russian art and literature over Western styles.
The debate between the Slavophiles and Westernizers shaped Russian intellectual life in the 1800s.
Her grandfather was known as a Slavophile who cherished Orthodox traditions and Russian folk customs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Russophile - a person who admires Russian people or culture
  • antonym: Slavophobe - a person who dislikes or is prejudiced against Slavic peoples
  • antonym: Westernizer - a supporter of adopting Western European ideas and culture in Russia
