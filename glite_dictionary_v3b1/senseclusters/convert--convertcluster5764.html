convert

verb
cause to adopt a new faith
1.0
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝːt / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to change your religion, beliefs, or opinions, or to persuade someone else to do so

Examples

To convert to Christianity
He tried to convert his friends to his new religion, but they resisted.
The missionaries sought to convert the villagers to Christianity.
Throughout history, conquerors have attempted to convert populations to their faith.
He managed to convert most of his friends to his new philosophy.
It took years to convert her to his political views.
The passionate speech converted several skeptics in the audience.
After years of searching, she finally converted to Buddhism.
He converted from Christianity to Islam in his early twenties.
Many people convert to a new faith during times of personal crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apostasy - the abandonment or renunciation of a religious or political belief
  • synonym: convince - to cause someone to believe firmly in the truth of something
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
  • synonym: persuade - to induce someone to do or believe something through reasoning or argument
  • synonym: proselytize - to try to persuade someone to change religious faith
