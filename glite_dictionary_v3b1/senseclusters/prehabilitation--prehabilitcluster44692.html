prehabilitation

noun
injury prevention training
7.9
Pronunciation: /priː.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.hæ.bɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a program of exercise and conditioning performed before sports or physical activity to prevent injury and improve performance

Examples

Sports prehabilitation exercises
Athletes often include prehabilitation in their routines to reduce the risk of injuries during the season.
The physical therapist designed a prehabilitation plan for the patient before surgery.
Many coaches now recommend prehabilitation to help players perform better and stay healthy.
