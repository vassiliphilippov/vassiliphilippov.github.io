acutely

adverb
intensely or sharply felt or perceived
5.5 0.50/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàkjutli / (AmE), / …ôÀàkjuÀêtli / (BrE)
In a way that is intensely or sharply felt or perceived.

Examples

Acutely felt pain
He acutely felt the loss of his childhood friend.
She became acutely aware of the silence in the room.
The patient was acutely sensitive to any changes in temperature.

Synonyms

  • intensely
    3.1 8.27/m
    intensely is very strong and full of force in feelings or actions, while acutely describes a sharp, focused pain or sense.
  • keenly
    4.1 2.84/m
    keenly gives a strong sense of close attention or feeling toward something, while acutely stresses sharp intensity in pain or notice.
  • sharply
    2.3 12/m
    sharply often means sudden or clear change in tone or direction, while acutely means something is felt very intensely.
