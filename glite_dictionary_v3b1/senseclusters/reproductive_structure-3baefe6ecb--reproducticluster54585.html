reproductive structure

noun
part of a plant for reproduction
Pronunciation: / ɹiˈpɹʌdəktɪv ˈstɹʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ɹɪˈprʌdʌktɪv ˈstɹʌktʃə / (BrE)
a part of a plant that is involved in its reproduction, such as a flower, fruit, or spore-producing organ

Examples

A reproductive structure in plants
Each flower is a reproductive structure designed to attract pollinators and produce seeds.
Spores are produced within the reproductive structure of ferns and other non-flowering plants.
The sunflower's reproductive structure consists of a central disc surrounded by petals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flower - the seed-bearing part of a plant; a main reproductive structure for many plants
  • synonym: fruit - the mature ovary of a flower, containing seeds; a reproductive body in plants
