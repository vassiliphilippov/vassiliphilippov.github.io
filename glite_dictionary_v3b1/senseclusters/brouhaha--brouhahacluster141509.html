brouhaha

noun
noisy and confused commotion
Pronunciation: / ˈbruː.hɑː.hɑː / (AmE), / ˈbruː.hɑː.hɑː / (BrE)
a noisy and confused situation, often with many people expressing strong opinions or emotions, usually about something not very important

Examples

There was quite a brouhaha over the new policy changes.
Despite the brouhaha, the issue was resolved quickly and quietly.
The media created a brouhaha over the politician's minor mistake.
There was quite a brouhaha at the office when the memo was misunderstood.
