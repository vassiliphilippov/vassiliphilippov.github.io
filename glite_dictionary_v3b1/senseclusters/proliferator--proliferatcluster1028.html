proliferator

noun
agent causing rapid growth
8.2
Pronunciation: / prəˈlɪfəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfəreɪtə / (BrE)
a substance or agent that causes cells or tissues to divide and grow rapidly, especially in medical or biological contexts

Examples

A cell proliferator
The scientists identified a new proliferator that could help regenerate damaged tissue.
Doctors are cautious about using any proliferator due to the risk of uncontrolled cell growth.
The study focused on the effects of the proliferator on cancer cells in the laboratory.
