GRP

noun
advertising exposure metric
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒiː.ɑːr.piː/ (AmE), /ˈdʒiː.ɑː.piː/ (BrE)
a measure in advertising that represents the total exposure of an advertisement to an audience, calculated as reach multiplied by frequency

Examples

A GRP measure
The marketing team aimed for a GRP of 100 to maximize the campaign's impact.
A higher GRP indicates that more people have seen the advertisement multiple times.
Advertisers use GRP to compare the effectiveness of different media channels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impression - a single instance of an advertisement being displayed to a viewer
  • synonym: reach - the total number of different people exposed to an advertisement
