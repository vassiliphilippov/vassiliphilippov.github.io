DSP

noun
digital signal processor
Pronunciation: / diː ɛs piː / (AmE), / diː ɛs piː / (BrE)
a specialized microprocessor or chip designed to process digital signals rapidly, often used in audio, video, or communications systems

Examples

Audio DSP chip
The new smartphone features a powerful DSP for enhanced audio quality.
Engineers rely on a DSP to process real-time signals in communication devices.
Upgrading the DSP improved the performance of the car's sound system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: CPU - the central processing unit of a computer
  • synonym: microprocessor - an integrated circuit that contains the functions of a computer's central processing unit
