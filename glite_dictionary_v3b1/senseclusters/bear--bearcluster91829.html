bear

verb
accept responsibility or financial cost
Pronunciation: / bɛr / (AmE), / beə /; / bɛə / (BrE)
to tolerate, endure, or accept something difficult, unpleasant, or costly

Examples

To bear pain
Her parents refused to bear any further debts on her behalf.
If you break the contract, you must bear the financial consequences.
The company agreed to bear all the legal costs incurred during the trial.
Do you bear any ill will toward your former employer?
It's hard to bear a grudge for so many years.
She continued to bear feelings of resentment long after the argument.
I can't bear the noise from the construction sites every morning.
It's difficult for many children to bear long car journeys without getting restless.
She had to bear unfriendly comments from her colleagues at the new job.

  • synonym: shoulder - to accept responsibility for something, especially difficult or burdensome
  • synonym: tolerate - to allow something that is unpleasant or disliked to exist or happen
  • synonym: withstand - to resist the effect of something, especially something strong or unpleasant
