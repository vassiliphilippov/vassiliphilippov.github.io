influence

noun
effect of someone or something on another
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnfluəns /; / ˈɪn.flu.əns / (AmE), / ˈɪnflʊəns /; / ˈɪn.flu.əns / (BrE)
the power or ability to affect how someone thinks, behaves, or how things develop

Examples

Influence on society
Global warming is partly due to human influence on the environment.
His parents had a strong influence on his choice of career.
The media can have a powerful influence over public opinion.
Her parents' influence helped shape her values and beliefs.
Peer influence often plays a significant role during adolescence.
The teacher's positive influence was evident in his students' enthusiasm for learning.
His political influence grew as he gained the trust of more community leaders.
She used her parents' influence to secure an internship at the company.
The teacher's influence on her students extended beyond the classroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders, make decisions, and enforce obedience
  • synonym: control - the power to direct or influence
  • synonym: effect - a change produced by an action or cause
  • antonym: helplessness - the lack of ability to influence or control events
  • synonym: impact - strong effect or influence
  • antonym: insignificance - lack of influence or effect
  • synonym: power - the ability or capacity to do something or act in a particular way
