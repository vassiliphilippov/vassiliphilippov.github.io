unrightfully

adverb
unjustly
8.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈraɪtfəli / (AmE), / ʌnˈraɪtfəli / (BrE)
in a way that is not right, fair, or just; unjustly

Examples

To be unrightfully accused
He was unrightfully accused of the crime and spent years proving his innocence.
The property was unrightfully taken from its original owners during the conflict.
Many people felt the decision was unrightfully made without considering all the facts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rightfully - in a way that is fair or just
  • synonym: unjustly - in a manner that is not fair or just
  • synonym: wrongfully - in a way that is not morally or legally correct
