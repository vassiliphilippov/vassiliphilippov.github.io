runoff

noun
final election to resolve undecided vote
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɒf / (BrE)
A final election held to determine a winner when an earlier vote did not produce a decisive result.

Examples

Election runoff
If no candidate wins a majority, a runoff will be held between the top two finishers.
The runoff election is scheduled for next Tuesday after neither candidate reached the required threshold.
Voters turned out in record numbers for the runoff to choose the new mayor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ballot - a process of voting, in writing and typically in secret
