elegist

noun
writer of elegies for the dead
Pronunciation: / ˈɛlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪdʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who writes elegies, especially one who composes poems lamenting the dead

Examples

An elegist writes poems
As an aspiring elegist, she devoted much of her writing to commemorating lost loved ones.
The renowned elegist captured the nation's grief in a moving poem after the tragedy.
Throughout history, every era has produced its own influential elegist to articulate collective sorrow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elegiac - relating to or characteristic of an elegy
  • synonym: elegy - a mournful poem, especially for the dead
