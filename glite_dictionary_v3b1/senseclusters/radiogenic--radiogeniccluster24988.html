radiogenic

adjective
caused by radiation
9.5
Pronunciation: /reɪ.dɪ.oʊ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ /; / /reɪ.diː.oʊ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (AmE), /reɪ.dɪ.əʊ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ /; / /reɪ.diː.əʊ.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (BrE)
caused by or resulting from exposure to ionizing radiation, especially relating to injury or disease.

Examples

Radiogenic cancer
The doctor explained that the tumor was likely radiogenic due to previous radiation treatments.
Scientists are studying the long-term effects of radiogenic diseases in populations exposed to nuclear accidents.
Some rare cancers are considered radiogenic because they develop after significant radiation exposure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radiation-induced - caused by exposure to radiation
  • antonym: spontaneous - occurring without external cause
