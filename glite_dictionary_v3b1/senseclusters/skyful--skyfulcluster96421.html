skyful

noun
amount filling the sky
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌfʊl / (BrE)
an amount that fills the sky or as much as the sky can contain

Examples

A skyful of stars
A skyful of stars lit up the night, making it look magical.
After the storm, a skyful of clouds drifted slowly across the horizon.
We watched a skyful of fireworks burst into color on New Year's Eve.

Related Terms

  • synonym: handful - as much as can be held in one hand
