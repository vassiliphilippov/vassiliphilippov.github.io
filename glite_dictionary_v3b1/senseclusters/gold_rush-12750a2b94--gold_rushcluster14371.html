gold rush

noun
sudden rush for quick wealth
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊld rʌʃ / (AmE), / ɡəʊld rʌʃ / (BrE)
a situation in which many people suddenly move to a place or begin an activity in search of quick wealth or success, especially as a result of a new opportunity

Examples

Tech gold rush
After oil was discovered in the region, there was a modern-day gold rush with investors pouring in.
His startup joined the gold rush to develop new artificial intelligence applications.
The tech industry experienced a gold rush when smartphones first became popular.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boom - a period of sudden economic growth or activity
  • antonym: bust - a period of sudden economic decline following a boom or rush
