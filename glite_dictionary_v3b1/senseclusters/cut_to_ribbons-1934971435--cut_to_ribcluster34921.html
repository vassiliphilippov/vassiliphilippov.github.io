cut to ribbons

verb
defeat or destroy completely
Pronunciation: / kʌt tə ˈrɪb.ənz / (AmE), / kʌt tə ˈrɪb.ənz / (BrE)
to defeat or destroy completely; to thoroughly damage or ruin someone or something, especially in a competition, argument, or criticism.

Examples

To cut to ribbons an argument
Her argument was cut to ribbons by the expert panel during the debate.
The defending champions cut to ribbons their rivals in the final match, winning by a huge margin.
Their proposal was cut to ribbons during the meeting, leaving them with nothing to present.

  • synonym: demolish - to defeat or destroy utterly
  • synonym: trounce - to defeat decisively
