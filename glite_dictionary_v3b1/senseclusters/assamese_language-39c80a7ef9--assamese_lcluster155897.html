Assamese language

noun
language of the people of Assam, India
8.1
Pronunciation: / əˈsæˌmiz ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌæsəˈmiːz ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The Indo-Aryan language of the Assamese people, spoken primarily in the state of Assam in northeastern India.

Examples

Assamese language spoken
Many schools in Assam teach in the Assamese language as the medium of instruction.
She is fluent in Hindi and the Assamese language.
The Assamese language uses its own unique script based on the Eastern Nagari script.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bengali language - the Indo-Aryan language spoken mainly in Bangladesh and West Bengal, closely related to Assamese
