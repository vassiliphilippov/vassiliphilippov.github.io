rut

noun
monotonous routine that's hard to escape
Pronunciation: / rʌt / (AmE), / rʌt / (BrE)
a fixed and monotonous routine or pattern of behavior that is difficult to change

Examples

Stuck in a rut
After years at the same job, she felt stuck in a rut and longed for change.
It's easy to slip into a rut if you never try anything new.
Their marriage had fallen into a comfortable but predictable rut.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groove - a fixed, monotonous way of life or habit
  • synonym: habit - a settled or regular tendency or practice
  • synonym: routine - a regular way of doing things that is unvarying
