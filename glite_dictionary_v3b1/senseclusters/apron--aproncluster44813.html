apron

verb
to cover with apron
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.prən/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.prən/ (BrE)
to put an apron on someone or to cover or edge something with an apron or protective strip

Examples

To apron the stage
The builders aproned the stage with a wooden border for safety.
They aproned the kitchen counter with a metal strip to prevent damage.
The craftsman aprons each shelf with a decorative edge for a finished look.
Before the messy lesson, she carefully aproned the child to protect his clothes.
He aproned himself before starting to cook dinner for the family.
The teacher aprons each student before the art class begins.

  • antonym: expose - to leave something uncovered or unprotected
