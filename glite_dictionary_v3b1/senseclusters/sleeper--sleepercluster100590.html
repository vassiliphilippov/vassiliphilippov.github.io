sleeper

noun
railway cross-brace (railroad tie)
4.7 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːpɚ / (AmE), / ˈsliːpə / (BrE)
a heavy wooden or concrete beam laid crosswise under railway tracks to support and keep them in place; known as a railroad tie in American English.

Examples

Railway sleeper beam
Concrete sleepers are often used instead of wooden ones on modern railways.
In the UK, a sleeper is what Americans call a railroad tie.
The crew replaced the old sleeper beneath the track to improve stability.
