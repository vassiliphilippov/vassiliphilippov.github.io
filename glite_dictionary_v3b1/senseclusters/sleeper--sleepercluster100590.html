sleeper

noun
railway cross-brace (railroad tie)
6.3 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsliÀêp…ö / (AmE), / ÀàsliÀêp…ô / (BrE)
a heavy wooden or concrete beam laid crosswise under railway tracks to support and keep them in place; known as a railroad tie in American English.

Examples

Railway sleeper beam
Concrete sleepers are often used instead of wooden ones on modern railways.
In the UK, a sleeper is what Americans call a railroad tie.
The crew replaced the old sleeper beneath the track to improve stability.

Synonyms

  • railroad tie
    4.1 0.26/m
    railroad tie is the American name for the heavy beam under tracks, while sleeper is the British term for it.
  • crosstie
    7.5 0.03/m
    crosstie is another common name for the wooden or concrete beam under rails, while sleeper is the British form.
  • tie
    7.9 1.23/m
    tie is a shorter, more general word for a beam or even neckwear, while sleeper specifically means the rail support.
