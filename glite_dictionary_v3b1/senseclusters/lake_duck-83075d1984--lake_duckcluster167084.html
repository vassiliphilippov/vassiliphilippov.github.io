lake duck

noun
a North American duck, also called common scaup
6.0
Pronunciation: / leɪk dʌk / (AmE), / leɪk dʌk / (BrE)
A North American diving duck of the species Aythya marila, distinguished by the male's purplish head; also known as the common scaup.

Examples

A lake duck bird
Bird watchers gathered by the marsh to catch a glimpse of a rare lake duck.
Male lake ducks are easily identified by their distinctive purplish heads.
The lake duck is often seen diving for food in the shallows during spring.
