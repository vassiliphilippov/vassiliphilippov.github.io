rehabilitate

verb
restore to former status, especially politically
5.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /riː.hə.ˈbɪ.lə.teɪt/ /; / /riː.ə.ˈbɪ.lə.teɪt/ (AmE), /riː.hə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.teɪt/ /; / /riː.ə.ˈbɪ.lɪ.teɪt/ (BrE)
to restore someone to a former position or status, especially after being disgraced or dismissed, often used in a political context

Examples

To rehabilitate an official
After the political turmoil, the government decided to rehabilitate several officials who had been dismissed.
Plans are underway to rehabilitate leaders whose reputations were tarnished during the crisis.
The president was rehabilitated and returned to power after years of exile.
