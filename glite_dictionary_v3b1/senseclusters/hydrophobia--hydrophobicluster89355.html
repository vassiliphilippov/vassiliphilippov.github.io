hydrophobia

noun
old term for rabies
6.7 8.3
Pronunciation: /haɪ.drə.ˈfoʊ.biː.ə/ (AmE), /haɪ.drə.ˈfəʊ.bi.ə/ (BrE)
an old term for rabies, an acute viral disease of the nervous system in warm-blooded animals, usually transmitted by the bite of a rabid animal

Examples

Hydrophobia in old medicine
In the nineteenth century, rabies was commonly referred to as hydrophobia.
Symptoms of hydrophobia include difficulty swallowing and a fear of water.
The old textbook described hydrophobia as a deadly threat to both humans and animals.
