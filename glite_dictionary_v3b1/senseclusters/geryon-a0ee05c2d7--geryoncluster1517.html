Geryon

proper noun
three-headed monster in Greek mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡɪər.i.ɒn / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a mythical monster with three heads who was slain by Hercules as one of his twelve labors.

Examples

Geryon three heads
In art, Geryon is often depicted with three heads or three bodies.
Many ancient authors described Geryon as dwelling on the far western edge of the world.
The legendary Geryon was defeated by Hercules in a famous myth.
