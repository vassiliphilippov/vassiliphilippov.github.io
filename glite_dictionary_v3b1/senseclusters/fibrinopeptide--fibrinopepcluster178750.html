fibrinopeptide

noun
peptide released during blood clotting
9.9
Pronunciation: /faɪ.brɪ.noʊ.ˈpep.taɪd/ /; / /faɪ.brɪ.nə.ˈpep.taɪd/ (AmE), /faɪ.brɪ.nəʊ.ˈpep.taɪd/ /; / /faɪ.brɪ.nə.ˈpep.taɪd/ (BrE)
a peptide released from the amino end of fibrinogen by the action of thrombin during blood clotting

Examples

Fibrinopeptide release
During clot formation, fibrinopeptide is released from fibrinogen by the enzyme thrombin.
Researchers detected elevated fibrinopeptide concentrations in patients with thrombosis.
The measurement of fibrinopeptide levels can help assess the activity of blood clotting.
