due process of law

noun
legal principle of fair procedure
Pronunciation: / duː ˈprɑː.sɛs əv lɔː / (AmE), / djuː ˈprəʊ.sɛs əv lɔː / (BrE)
the principle that a person cannot be deprived of life, liberty, or property without appropriate legal procedures and safeguards, according to established rules and principles

Examples

The due process of law principle
Every individual has the right to due process of law before being punished for a crime.
The court ruled that his due process of law rights had been violated during the investigation.
Without due process of law, government actions against citizens would lack legitimacy.
