heterogametic

noun
organism with two sex chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəɡəˈmitɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəɡəˈmiːtɪk / (BrE)
an individual or sex that has two different types of sex chromosomes and produces two kinds of gametes regarding sex chromosomes

Examples

Heterogametic sex
In mammals, the male is the heterogametic sex, carrying both X and Y chromosomes.
Some insects have females as the heterogametic individuals, producing two types of eggs.
The concept of heterogametic and homogametic sexes is important in genetics and biology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homogametic - an individual with two of the same kind of sex chromosome
