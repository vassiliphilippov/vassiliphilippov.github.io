buttressing

noun
architectural wall support
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌtrəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtrəsɪŋ / (BrE)
A structure, usually made of stone or brick, built against a wall to provide support or reinforcement.

Examples

Stone buttressing
Engineers inspected the buttressing to ensure the building's stability after the earthquake.
The ancient cathedral relied on massive buttressing to keep its walls upright.
Without proper buttressing, the old stone wall began to lean and crack.
