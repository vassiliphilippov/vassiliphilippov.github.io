strike

verb
hit sharply with hand or weapon
2.3 2.1
Pronunciation: /straɪk/ (AmE), /straɪk/ (BrE)
to hit, deliver a blow, or come into forceful contact with something or someone

Examples

To strike a person
He was suspended for striking another student during the argument.
She struck the ball with incredible force, sending it across the field.
The protester attempted to strike the officer but missed.
A sudden note of music struck his ears in the quiet room.
The moonlight struck the lake, making it shimmer.
The morning sun struck her face as she opened the curtains.
A sudden pain struck his chest, alarming the doctor.
A wave of cold air struck my face as I stepped outside.
The arrow struck the target and lodged deep inside.

  • antonym: miss - to fail to hit, reach, or come into contact with
  • synonym: pierce - to penetrate into or go through something
  • synonym: punch - to strike with a closed fist
  • synonym: shine - (of light) to appear on or over something
  • degree lower: tap - to strike lightly or gently
