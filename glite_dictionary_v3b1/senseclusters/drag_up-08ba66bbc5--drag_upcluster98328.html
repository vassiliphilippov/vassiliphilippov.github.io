drag up

verb
mention something unpleasant from the past
1.9
Pronunciation: / dræɡ ʌp / (AmE), / dræɡ ʌp / (BrE)
to mention or bring up something unpleasant or forgotten from the past, especially when it would be better not to

Examples

To drag up old stories
Every family gathering, my uncle drags up stories from when we were kids.
Please don't drag up that argument again; we've already moved on.
She hates it when people drag up her mistakes from years ago.
He tends to drag up old grievances whenever he's upset.
She always drags up embarrassing stories at family gatherings.
Why did you drag up that argument from last year?

Related Terms

  • synonym: bring up - to mention or introduce a topic in conversation
  • antonym: bury - to deliberately not talk about or recall something from the past
  • antonym: let go - to stop mentioning or thinking about something, especially past events
