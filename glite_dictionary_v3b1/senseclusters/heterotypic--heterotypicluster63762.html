heterotypic

adjective
of different types
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈtaɪpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈtɪpɪk / (BrE)
differing in type or form, especially referring to cells or structures in biology that are not of the same kind

Examples

Heterotypic cell interaction
The researchers observed heterotypic interactions between different cell types in the tissue sample.
A heterotypic response can occur when immune cells react to unrelated pathogens.
In developmental biology, heterotypic cell contacts are important for organ formation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety; very different
  • antonym: homotypic - of the same type or form
