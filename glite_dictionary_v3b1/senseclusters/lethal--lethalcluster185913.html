lethal

adjective
deadly; capable of causing death
4.8 8.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliŒ∏…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêŒ∏…ôl / (BrE)
Able to cause death; deadly or fatal, especially when referring to weapons, substances, or actions.

Examples

Lethal weapon
A lethal injection was administered as the method of execution.
Certain chemicals are lethal even in very small doses.
The suspect was carrying a lethal weapon during the robbery.

Synonyms

  • deadly
    2.8 25/m
    deadly describes something very dangerous and able to kill, while lethal especially refers to weapons, doses, or actions that cause death.
  • fatal
    3.7 12/m
    fatal means causing death as a result, while lethal highlights the ability of weapons, substances, or actions to kill.
  • mortal
    6.0 1.37/m
    mortal often describes people or wounds with the chance to cause death and sounds more formal, while lethal names weapons or doses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmless - not able or likely to cause harm or death
