narcoleptic

noun
sleep-inducing drug
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑːr.kəˈlɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɑː.kəˈlɛp.tɪk / (BrE)
A drug that induces an uncontrollable desire to sleep; a soporific agent.

Examples

A narcoleptic drug
Certain medications may act as a narcoleptic if taken in large doses.
She was warned that the effects of the narcoleptic would last for several hours.
The doctor administered a narcoleptic to help the patient finally get some rest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: soporific - a drug or substance that induces sleep
