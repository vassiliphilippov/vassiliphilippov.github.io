overburn

verb
to exceed disc capacity
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.bɜːrn/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.bɜːn/ (BrE)
to write data to an optical disc beyond its stated capacity by using extra available space

Examples

To overburn a DVD
The technician attempted to overburn the DVD to fit the entire movie onto one disc.
Some older CD players may not read discs that have been overburned with extra data.
He used special software to overburn the audio CD and include all the bonus tracks.
