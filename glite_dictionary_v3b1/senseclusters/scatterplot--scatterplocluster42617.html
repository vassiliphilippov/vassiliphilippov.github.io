scatterplot

noun
graph with data dots
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈskæt.ər.plɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈskæt.ə.plɒt/ (BrE)
a graph that shows the relationship between two variables using dots to represent data points

Examples

A scatterplot chart
The scatterplot revealed a clear trend in the data.
Each dot on the scatterplot represents a single observation from the experiment.
By examining the scatterplot, the students identified a positive correlation between height and weight.
The scientist used a scatterplot to visualize the relationship between temperature and plant growth.
A scatterplot can help you see if there is a pattern in the data.
By examining the scatterplot, the students identified several outliers in their experiment results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bar chart - a graph that uses bars to show quantities or numbers
  • synonym: graph - a diagram showing the relationship between variables
  • antonym: line graph - a graph that uses lines to connect data points
