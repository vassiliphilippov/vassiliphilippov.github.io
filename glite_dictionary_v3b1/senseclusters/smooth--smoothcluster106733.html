smooth

adjective
even; free from roughness or bumps
Pronunciation: / smuð /; / smuːð / (AmE), / smuːð / (BrE)
having a surface or edge that is even and not rough, bumpy, or jagged (can refer to objects, leaves, or water)

Examples

Smooth surface
After sanding the wood, the surface became perfectly smooth.
She admired the smooth silk of her new dress.
The table had a smooth finish that felt pleasant to the touch.
Many trees in this region have smooth leaves, which makes them easy to identify.
The smooth margins of the leaves helped distinguish the species from others with jagged edges.
You can tell it's an elm by its smooth leaf edge, lacking any teeth or lobes.
The ferry enjoyed a smooth crossing because the sea was unusually calm.
The lake was perfectly smooth at dawn, reflecting the mountains like a mirror.
We waited until the water was smooth before launching the small boat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calm - (of water) peaceful and still, not rough
  • antonym: rough - having an uneven or irregular surface; not smooth
  • antonym: serrated - having a notched or tooth-like edge, as in some leaves
