dry

adjective
(of wine, etc.) not sweet; with little sugar
5.2
Pronunciation: / draɪ / (AmE), / draɪ / (BrE)
(of an alcoholic drink) containing little or no sweetness or sweetener

Examples

Dry wine
He ordered a dry martini with his dinner.
She prefers dry wines to sweet ones.
This vineyard is famous for its dry Riesling.
A dry cocktail typically lacks sweet mixers and focuses on the spirit.
He ordered a dry martini with just a hint of vermouth.
Some people prefer a dry Manhattan for its stronger taste.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sweet - (of wine or drinks) containing noticeable sugar and tasting sugary
