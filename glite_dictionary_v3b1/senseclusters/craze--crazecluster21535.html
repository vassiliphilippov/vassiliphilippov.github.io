craze

noun
fine crack in a glaze or surface
Pronunciation: / kreɪz / (AmE), / kreɪz / (BrE)
A fine crack on the surface of a glaze, ceramic, or similar material, often forming a network of lines.

Examples

A craze in pottery
Exposure to moisture can cause a craze to develop in ceramic glazes over time.
Light caught the network of craze lines on the pottery, highlighting its age.
The antique vase displayed a beautiful craze pattern across its surface.
