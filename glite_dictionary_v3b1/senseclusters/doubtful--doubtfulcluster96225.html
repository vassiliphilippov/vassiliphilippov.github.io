doubtful

adjective
uncertain or unsettled in mind

feeling or showing uncertainty; not sure or convinced about something

Examples

Doubtful about the answer
Even after the explanation, I'm still doubtful about the results.
He sounded doubtful about whether the project would succeed under current conditions.
She gave me a doubtful look, unsure whether to believe my story.
He gave a doubtful look when asked about the missing report.
It seems doubtful that the project will be finished on time.
She was doubtful that her idea would be accepted by the team.

Synonyms

  • uncertain
    3.9 4.34/m
    uncertain means lacking clear certainty about a fact or future, while doubtful is a similar feeling of not being sure.
  • unsure
    1.5 7.14/m
    unsure describes a person who feels not confident or certain, while doubtful often sounds slightly more formal or negative.
  • unconvinced
    5.1 1.50/m
    unconvinced means not persuaded after hearing reasons, while doubtful can describe a more general uncertainty about something.
  • skeptical
    4.2 19/m
    skeptical shows active doubt and a need for proof, while doubtful simply describes feeling unsure or not convinced.
  • tentative
    5.1 6.39/m
    tentative describes plans or actions that are not yet fixed and are made with caution, while doubtful is an uncertain feeling.
  • hesitant
    3.2 5.60/m
    hesitant means slow to act or speak because of doubt or low confidence, while doubtful is an internal uncertain feeling.
  • dubious
    6.5 8.03/m
    dubious suggests something seems unreliable or suspicious, while doubtful mainly means you feel unsure or not convinced.
