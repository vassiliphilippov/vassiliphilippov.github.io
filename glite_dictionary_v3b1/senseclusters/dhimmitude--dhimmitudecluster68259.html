dhimmitude

noun
submission to authority
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪ.mɪ.tuːd/ /; / /ˈdɪ.mə.tuːd/ (AmE), /ˈdɪ.mɪ.tuːd/ /; / /ˈdɪ.mɪ.tjuːd/ (BrE)
a modern, often critical term for a state of submission or accommodation to Islamic authority or influence, especially used to criticize perceived appeasement

Examples

Modern dhimmitude debate
The politician was accused of dhimmitude for supporting policies seen as overly accommodating.
Some commentators use the term dhimmitude to criticize what they view as excessive tolerance.
Debates about cultural integration sometimes include accusations of dhimmitude from critics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appeasement - the act of giving in to demands to avoid conflict
  • antonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply with authority
