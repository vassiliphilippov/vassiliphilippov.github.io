romance

verb
exaggerate or romanticize a story
Pronunciation: / roʊˈmæns / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæns / (BrE)
to present (something) in a romanticized or exaggerated way, often making it more appealing or exciting than it really is

Examples

To romance the past
He loves to romance his childhood adventures when telling stories to his friends.
The journalist romanced the dull facts to make the article more engaging.
The travel blogger romanced her experiences to attract more followers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embellish - to make a story more interesting by adding details that are not always true
  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger, more important, or more impressive than it really is
