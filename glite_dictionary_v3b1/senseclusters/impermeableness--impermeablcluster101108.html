impermeableness

noun
quality of being impermeable to liquids
6.8 9.8
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɝːmiəbəlnəs / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːmiəbəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impermeable, especially to liquids; inability to be penetrated by fluids

Examples

The impermeableness of fabric
Researchers tested the impermeableness of several fabrics used for raincoats.
The impermeableness of the waterproof membrane ensures no water leaks into the basement.
The success of the dam depends on the impermeableness of its concrete core.

  • antonym: permeability - the quality of allowing liquids or gases to pass through
