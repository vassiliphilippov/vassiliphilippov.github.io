boiler suit

noun
one-piece protective work garment
4.6 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…î…™l…ôr suÀêt / (AmE), / Ààb…î…™l…ô suÀêt / (BrE)
A loose one-piece protective garment worn over ordinary clothes, usually for dirty work or manual labor.

Examples

A boiler suit for work
After a day of painting, her boiler suit was covered in splashes of color.
He put on his boiler suit before heading into the workshop to start repairs.
The factory required all employees to wear a boiler suit for safety reasons.

Synonyms

  • boilersuit
    6.3 0.00/m
    boilersuit is written as one word and refers to a full-body protective garment for dirty work, while boiler suit is the two-word form.
  • overall
    3.9 2.38/m
    overall is a loose protective garment that can be coveralls or bibbed trousers to keep clothes clean, while boiler suit is a one-piece coverall.
