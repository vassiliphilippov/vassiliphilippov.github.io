underpowered

adjective
not strong enough
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈpaʊərd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpaʊəd / (BrE)
not having enough power, strength, or effectiveness to do something well

Examples

An underpowered study
The study was considered underpowered because it included too few participants to show a clear result.
Many critics argued that the engine in the new model was underpowered for such a large vehicle.
An underpowered analysis may fail to detect important differences between the two groups.
The underpowered engine made it difficult for the car to climb steep hills.
Many users complained that the underpowered phone could not handle modern apps smoothly.
Because the generator was underpowered, it failed to supply electricity to the entire house.

  • synonym: insufficient - not enough in amount or degree
  • antonym: powerful - having great strength or effectiveness
  • synonym: weak - lacking strength or power
