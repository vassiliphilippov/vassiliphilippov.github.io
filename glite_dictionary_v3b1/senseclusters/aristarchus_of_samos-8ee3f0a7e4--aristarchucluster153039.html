Aristarchus of Samos

proper noun
ancient Greek astronomer; early heliocentric theory proponent
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌærɪˈstɑrkəs əv ˈseɪmɒs / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈstɑːkəs əv ˈseɪmɒs / (BrE)
(circa 270 BC) An ancient Greek astronomer who was one of the first to propose that the Earth revolves around the Sun.

Examples

Aristarchus of Samos heliocentric model
Aristarchus of Samos was one of the earliest scientists to suggest a heliocentric model of the universe.
Many historians credit Aristarchus of Samos with anticipating the work of Copernicus.
The revolutionary ideas of Aristarchus of Samos were largely ignored until much later in history.
