Mary I of England

proper noun
Queen of England (1553–1558), restored Catholicism
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛri ðə fɜrst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈmeəri ðə fɜːst əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
Queen of England from 1553 to 1558, known for her attempt to restore Roman Catholicism and the persecution of Protestants, daughter of Henry VIII and Catherine of Aragon.

Examples

Mary I of England queen
Mary I of England ascended to the throne in 1553 after the death of her half-brother Edward VI.
During the reign of Mary I of England, Protestant reformers were persecuted as heretics.
Many historians debate the legacy of Mary I of England and her restoration of Catholicism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Elizabeth I of England - Queen of England after Mary I, known as the Virgin Queen
  • synonym: Mary, Queen of Scots - Contemporary monarch, cousin of Mary I
