alerce

noun
South American evergreen tree
Pronunciation: / aˈleɾseɪ / (AmE), / aˈleɾseɪ / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to South America, especially Chile and Argentina, also known as Patagonian cypress

Examples

Ancient alerce tree
The ancient alerce trees in Chile can live for thousands of years.
Many conservationists are working to protect the endangered alerce forests.
The wood from the alerce is highly valued for its durability and beauty.
