guard

noun
military unit assigned to protection
Pronunciation: / ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɡɑːd / (BrE)
a person whose job is to protect people or property, such as a soldier, security guard, or ceremonial guard

Examples

Security guard
A guard was stationed at each entrance to the building.
The palace is always protected by a special guard of elite soldiers.
The president travels with a military guard for security.
A ceremonial guard marched in perfect unison along the avenue.
He served as a guard in the Queen's household regiment.
The palace guards stood at attention during the ceremony.
A security guard stood by the entrance, checking everyone's ID before they entered.
Prisoners are escorted by a guard whenever they leave their cells.
The bank hired an armed guard to watch over the vault at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bodyguard - a person or group hired to protect another person
  • synonym: escort - a group of persons or vehicles accompanying another for protection
  • antonym: prisoner - a person who is kept in prison; someone a guard oversees
  • synonym: sentinel - a soldier or guard whose job is to stand and keep watch
  • synonym: watchman - a person who guards a building, especially at night
