house of worship

noun
building for religious worship
2.9 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / Àåha äs …ôv Ààw…úÀêr É…™p / (AmE), / Àåha äs …ôv Ààw…úÀê É…™p / (BrE)
A building where people gather for religious services or prayer.

Examples

A house of worship building
Each house of worship in the city opened its doors for the interfaith ceremony.
People traveled from distant towns to attend the festival at the local house of worship.
The new house of worship was designed to accommodate hundreds of congregants for prayer and celebration.

Synonyms

  • place of worship
    2.9 0.80/m
    place of worship is a general name for any site used for religious acts, while house of worship refers to a building people use.
  • house of God
    3.1 0.31/m
    house of God sounds religious and personal, often used by believers or in prayers, while house of worship is a neutral building term.
  • house of prayer
    3.8 0.07/m
    house of prayer is focused on silent or communal prayer and devotion, while house of worship covers services and other religious gatherings.
  • meetinghouse
    8.4 0.17/m
    meetinghouse is a building used for religious assemblies by specific groups like Quakers, while house of worship is a general term for any prayer building.
