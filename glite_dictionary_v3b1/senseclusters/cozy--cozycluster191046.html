cozy

adjective
suggesting secret collusion or insider dealings
6.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkoʊ.ziː/ (AmE), /ˈkəʊ.ziː/ (BrE)
suggesting a situation in which people are colluding or acting together in a secret or unethical way for mutual benefit

Examples

Cozy relationship
Many felt that the deal was a little too cozy to be legitimate.
She denied any cozy arrangement with her rival.
There was talk of a cozy relationship between the company and regulators.
Some journalists accused the mayor of being too cosy with business leaders.
The deal had a cosy feel, raising suspicions of under-the-table arrangements.
There was a cosy relationship between the company and the regulators.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aboveboard - open and honest, not secretive or underhand
  • synonym: collusive - acting together secretly to achieve a fraudulent or deceitful purpose
  • synonym: complicit - involved with others in an illegal or morally wrong activity
