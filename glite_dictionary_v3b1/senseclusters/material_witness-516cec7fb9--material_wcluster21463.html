material witness

noun
crucial legal witness
7.4 4.8
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈtɪr.iː.əl ˈwɪt.nəs/ /; / /mə.ˈtɪr.iː.əl ˈwɪt.nɪs/ (AmE), /mə.ˈtɪə.riː.əl ˈwɪt.nəs/ /; / /mə.ˈtɪə.riː.əl ˈwɪt.nɪs/ (BrE)
A witness whose testimony is considered essential and directly relevant to the central issue of a legal case.

Examples

A material witness
As a material witness, her statement was crucial for the prosecution’s case.
Police detained him briefly because they considered him a material witness in the investigation.
The court issued a warrant to ensure the material witness would appear to testify at the trial.
