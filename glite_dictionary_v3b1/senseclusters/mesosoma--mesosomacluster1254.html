mesosoma

noun
insect middle section
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsəˈsoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəˈsəʊmə / (BrE)
the middle section of the body in certain arthropods, such as insects (ants, wasps) and arachnids (scorpions), between the head and abdomen

Examples

The mesosoma section
The mesosoma of an ant contains the muscles that move its legs.
Scientists examined the mesosoma to identify the species of the wasp.
In many insects, the wings are attached to the mesosoma.
The mesosoma of the scorpion contains both the lungs and the reproductive organs.
Scientists examined the structure of the mesosoma to better understand arachnid anatomy.
Protective plates cover the mesosoma in many species of scorpions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abdomen - the rear section of an insect's body
  • antonym: prosoma - the front part of the body in arachnids, also called the cephalothorax
  • synonym: thorax - the middle part of an insect's body where the legs and wings attach
