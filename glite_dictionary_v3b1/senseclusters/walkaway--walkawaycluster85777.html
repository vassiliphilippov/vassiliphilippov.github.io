walkaway

noun
an easy victory
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔkəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkəweɪ /; / ˈwɔːkəˌweɪ / (BrE)
an easy or overwhelming victory, especially in a competition or contest

Examples

A walkaway victory
Her performance in the competition was so strong that it was a walkaway victory.
Many predicted the match would be close, but it turned out to be a walkaway.
The team's win was a real walkaway, with a final score of 8–0.
The championship game turned out to be a walkaway for the home team.
With such a strong candidate, the election was a walkaway from the start.
Everyone expected a close match, but it ended up as a walkaway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: landslide - an overwhelming victory, especially in an election
  • antonym: struggle - a difficult or hard-fought contest
