apoptotic

adjective
causing cell death
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəpˈtɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæpɒpˈtɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing apoptosis, the process of programmed cell death in living organisms

Examples

Apoptotic cell death
The scientist observed apoptotic changes in the tissue sample.
Certain drugs can trigger apoptotic pathways to eliminate cancer cells.
Researchers measured the number of apoptotic cells after the treatment was applied.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apoptosis - programmed cell death in living organisms
  • antonym: necrotic - relating to the death of cells through injury or disease
