universal

adjective
applicable to all; common to all
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜrsəl /; / ˌjunəˈvɝsəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəl / (BrE)
existing or true everywhere or for everyone; applicable in all cases or to all people or things

Examples

Universal truth
Her speech received universal praise from critics and fans alike.
The scientist searched for a universal law that explains the phenomenon.
There was universal agreement that action needed to be taken.
A universal language would make international communication much easier.
Access to clean water is a universal need shared by all people.
The concept of human rights has a universal appeal that transcends borders and cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: general - involving or true for most people, places, or things, but not necessarily all
  • synonym: global - relating to or involving the whole world
  • antonym: local - relating to or occurring in a particular area, city, or town
  • antonym: particular - relating to only one or a few; not universal
