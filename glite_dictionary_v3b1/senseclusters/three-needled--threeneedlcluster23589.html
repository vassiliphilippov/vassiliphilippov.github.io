three-needled

adjective
having three needles per bundle (conifers)
Pronunciation: / θriː ˈniːdld / (AmE), / θriː ˈniːdld / (BrE)
(of conifers) having three needles in each bundle or cluster.

Examples

Three-needled pine
Because it is three-needled, this species can be easily distinguished from its relatives.
Many botanists identify pine trees by determining whether they are three-needled, two-needled, or five-needled.
The ponderosa pine is a typical example of a three-needled species.
