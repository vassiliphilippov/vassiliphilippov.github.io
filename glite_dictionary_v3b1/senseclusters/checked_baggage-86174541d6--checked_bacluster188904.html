checked baggage

noun
luggage given to airline for cargo hold
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkt ˈbæɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkt ˈbaɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
luggage that is handed over to an airline for transport in the cargo hold, rather than carried into the cabin by the passenger.

Examples

Checked baggage weight limit
Passengers must pay extra fees if their checked baggage exceeds the weight limit.
She accidentally left her laptop in her checked baggage, so she couldn't use it during the flight.
Your checked baggage will be available for collection at the baggage carousel after landing.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: baggage - suitcases and bags used for travel
  • antonym: carry-on baggage - luggage that a passenger keeps with them in the cabin
