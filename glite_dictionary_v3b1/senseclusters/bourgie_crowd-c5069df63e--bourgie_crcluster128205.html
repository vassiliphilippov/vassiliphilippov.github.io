bourgie crowd

noun
group of upper-class or pretentious people
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊrʒi kraʊd / (AmE), / ˈbʊəʒi kraʊd / (BrE)
A group of people who are considered upper-class or pretentious in attitude or behavior.

Examples

The bourgie crowd acts
At college, he made friends outside the bourgie crowd he grew up with.
He always felt out of place with the bourgie crowd at those exclusive parties.
She complained that the art opening was filled with a bourgie crowd more interested in networking than the work.
