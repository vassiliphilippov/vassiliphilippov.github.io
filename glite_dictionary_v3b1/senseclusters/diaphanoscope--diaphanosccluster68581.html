diaphanoscope

noun
instrument for illuminating body cavities
7.4
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæf.ə.noʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / daɪˈæf.ə.nə.skəʊp / (BrE)
an instrument used to illuminate the inside of a cavity to assess the translucency of its walls

Examples

A diaphanoscope instrument
Researchers introduced the diaphanoscope to medical diagnostics in the late nineteenth century.
The structure of the organ's wall was observed by placing a diaphanoscope against it.
The surgeon used a diaphanoscope to examine the patient's sinus cavity.
