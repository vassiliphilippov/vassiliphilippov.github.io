wind up late

verb
to arrive late in the end
Pronunciation: / waɪnd ʌp leɪt / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp leɪt / (BrE)
to eventually arrive in a place later than expected or planned, often unintentionally

Examples

To wind up late for work
He always seems to wind up late for work, no matter how early he gets up.
If you leave now, you won't wind up late for the show.
She didn't plan to wind up late, but the traffic was terrible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be late - to arrive later than scheduled
  • antonym: show up on time - to arrive at the scheduled time
