enrhythmic

adjective
biological rhythms
9.8
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈrɪðmɪk / (AmE), / ɛnˈrɪðmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the natural biological or physiological rhythms of living organisms, such as circadian or cardiac cycles

Examples

Enrhythmic heart patterns
The scientist studied the enrhythmic patterns of heartbeats in different animal species.
Certain medications can disrupt the body's enrhythmic cycles, leading to sleep problems.
Researchers observed enrhythmic hormone release that matched the animals' daily activity cycles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrhythmic - lacking a regular rhythm or pattern
  • synonym: rhythmic - having a regular repeated pattern of movement or sound
