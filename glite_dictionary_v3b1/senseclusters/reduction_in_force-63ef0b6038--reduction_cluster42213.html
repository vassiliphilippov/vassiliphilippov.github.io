reduction in force

noun
workforce downsizing; job cuts
Pronunciation: / ɹɪˈdʌkʃən ɪn fɔɹs / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌkʃən ɪn fɔːs / (BrE)
an act of decreasing the number of employees in a workforce, typically involving layoffs or job eliminations

Examples

A reduction in force layoff
After the reduction in force, many departments had to adjust to fewer staff members.
Management tried to minimize the impact of the reduction in force through voluntary retirements.
The company announced a reduction in force to address declining sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym: layoff - temporary or permanent termination of employment
