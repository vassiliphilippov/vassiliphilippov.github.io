nonconservative

adjective
not conserving energy
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈsɜːrvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈsɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
describing a force or process that does not conserve energy or another physical quantity

Examples

Nonconservative force
Friction is a nonconservative force because it dissipates energy as heat.
In physics, air resistance is considered a nonconservative force since it removes energy from the system.
Unlike gravity, a nonconservative force does not allow energy to be fully recovered.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - describing a force or process that conserves energy or another quantity
