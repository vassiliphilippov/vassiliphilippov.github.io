pooling of interest

noun
accounting method for company mergers
Pronunciation: / ˈpulɪŋ əv ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ˈpuːlɪŋ əv ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
an accounting method used in company mergers in which the balance sheets of the companies are combined item by item, typically resulting in a tax-free merger

Examples

The pooling of interest method
The company avoided taxes on the merger by applying the pooling of interest approach.
The merger was accounted for using the pooling of interest method, simplifying the financial reporting process.
Under pooling of interest accounting, the assets and liabilities of both firms are combined without revaluation.
