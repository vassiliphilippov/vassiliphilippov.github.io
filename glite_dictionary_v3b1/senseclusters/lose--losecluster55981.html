lose

verb
fail to perceive or understand
Pronunciation: / luːz / (AmE), / luːz / (BrE)
to miss, fail to get, or fail to understand something (such as an opportunity, information, or a point)

Examples

To lose a chance
By the time he finished the explanation, I had already lost his point.
I'm sorry, I lost what you just said—could you repeat it?
She often loses the thread of the conversation when people talk too fast.
He realized too late that he would lose the grant if he didn't apply on time.
If you hesitate now, you might lose the offer.
She didn't want to lose her only chance at getting the job.
