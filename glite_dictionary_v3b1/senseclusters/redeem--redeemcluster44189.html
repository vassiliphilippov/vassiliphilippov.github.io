redeem

verb
save from sin or evil
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdim / (AmE), / rɪˈdiːm / (BrE)
(in religious contexts) to save someone from sin, error, or evil.

Examples

To redeem a soul

Many believe that faith can redeem even the most troubled soul.
The story is about a fallen woman who is ultimately redeemed through love and forgiveness.
