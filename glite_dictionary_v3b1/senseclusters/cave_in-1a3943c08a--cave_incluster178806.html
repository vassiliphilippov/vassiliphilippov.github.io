cave in

verb
yield to pressure or demands
Pronunciation: / keɪv ɪn / (AmE), / keɪv ɪn / (BrE)
To yield or submit to pressure, persuasion, or force, often reluctantly.

Examples

Cave in to pressure
After hours of debate, the city council finally caved in and approved the proposal.
Although she was tempted, she didn't cave in to the peer pressure at the party.
When negotiations got tough, the manager refused to cave in to the demands of the union.

Related Terms

  • synonym: give in - to stop resisting; to yield
  • antonym: resist - to refuse to yield to pressure or force
