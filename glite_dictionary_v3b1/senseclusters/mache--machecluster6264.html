mache

noun
old radon unit
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈmæʃeɪ / (BrE)
an obsolete unit formerly used to measure the concentration of radon in air

Examples

Mache unit
Scientists in the early 20th century measured radon levels in mache before modern units were adopted.
The report mentioned a concentration of ten mache in the underground laboratory.
Today, the mache is no longer used in scientific measurements of radioactivity.
