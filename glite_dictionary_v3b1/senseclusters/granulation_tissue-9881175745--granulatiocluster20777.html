granulation tissue

noun
new connective tissue formed during wound healing
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɡræn.jəˈleɪ.ʃən ˈtɪʃ.u / (AmE), / ˌɡran.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən ˈtɪs.juː / (BrE)
new connective tissue and small blood vessels that develop on the surface of a wound as it heals

Examples

Healthy granulation tissue
Healthy granulation tissue is a sign that the wound is healing properly.
If granulation tissue does not form, the wound may be slow to heal.
The surgeon noted the presence of granulation tissue at the site of the injury.
