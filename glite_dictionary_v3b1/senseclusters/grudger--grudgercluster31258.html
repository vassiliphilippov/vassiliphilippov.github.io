grudger

noun
reluctant helper
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌdʒər / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌdʒə / (BrE)
a person who does something reluctantly or gives help, praise, or money unwillingly

Examples

A grudger gives help
Everyone knew Mark was a grudger who only helped when absolutely necessary.
As a grudger, she rarely offered praise unless it was unavoidable.
The team needed support, but their only volunteer was a known grudger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: begrudger - a person who resents or is unwilling to give or allow something
  • antonym: willing participant - a person who does something gladly or without reluctance
