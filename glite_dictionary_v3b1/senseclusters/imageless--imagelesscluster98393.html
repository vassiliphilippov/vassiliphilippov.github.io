imageless

adjective
without images
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmɪdʒləs / (AmE), / ˈɪmɪdʒləs / (BrE)
lacking images or imagery, either in writing, thought, or dreams

Examples

Imageless writing style
The philosopher's writing style is often imageless and difficult to visualize.
Unlike the colorful paintings, the sculpture was intentionally imageless and minimalistic.
Her speech was powerful but almost completely imageless, relying on facts rather than stories.
Some people report having imageless dreams that consist only of thoughts or emotions.
The philosopher described a type of imageless thinking that does not rely on pictures in the mind.
Her memories of the event were almost completely imageless, lacking any visual detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstract - not representing concrete objects or images
  • antonym: visual - relating to seeing or sight; producing images in the mind
  • antonym: vivid - producing strong, clear images in the mind
