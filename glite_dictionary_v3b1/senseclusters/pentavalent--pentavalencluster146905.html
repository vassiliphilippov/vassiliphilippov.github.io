pentavalent

adjective
protecting against five diseases (vaccine)
6.8
Pronunciation: /pen.tə.ˈveɪ.lənt/ /; / /ˈpen.tə.və.lənt/ (AmE), /pen.tə.ˈveɪ.lənt/ /; / /ˈpen.tə.və.lənt/ (BrE)
(of a vaccine) designed to protect against five different diseases or strains by containing five antigens or components.

Examples

Pentavalent vaccine
The new pentavalent vaccine protects infants against five common childhood diseases in a single injection.
Public health officials recommended the pentavalent immunization for all babies in the region this year.
Researchers compared antibody responses after children received either the pentavalent or the monovalent vaccine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: five-in-one vaccine - a common term for a vaccine that protects against five diseases; often used interchangeably with 'pentavalent' in public-health contexts.
  • degree higher: hexavalent - having six protective antigens (e.g., hexavalent vaccine).
  • degree lower: trivalent - having three protective antigens (e.g., trivalent vaccine).
