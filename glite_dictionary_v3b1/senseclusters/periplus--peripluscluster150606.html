periplus

noun
sea voyage itinerary
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəˌplʌs / (AmE), / ˈpɛrɪˌplʌs / (BrE)
an ancient written account or document describing a sea voyage, listing ports, anchorages, and coastal features

Examples

Greek periplus document
The ancient periplus described every port along the African coast in great detail.
Historians use the periplus to understand ancient maritime trade routes.

Historians study the periplus to understand ancient trade routes and maritime knowledge.
A periplus often included descriptions of local peoples and resources found at each harbor.
