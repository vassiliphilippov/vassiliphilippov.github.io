gibbose

adjective
more than half full (of the moon)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪboʊs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪbəʊs / (BrE)
(of the moon) more than half full; having a convex shape, used especially in reference to the lunar phase between first quarter and full moon or last quarter and new moon.

Examples

The gibbose moon
Ancient observers used the term gibbose to describe the swelling moon in various stages of its cycle.
On the night before the full moon, the lunar disk appeared distinctly gibbose against the dark sky.
The astronomer pointed out that the moon was gibbose, signaling an approaching full phase.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gibbous - (of the moon) more than half full; convex in shape
