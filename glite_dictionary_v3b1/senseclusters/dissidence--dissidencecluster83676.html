dissidence

noun
disagreement with authority or policy
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsədəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪdəns / (BrE)
disagreement or opposition, especially towards official policy or established authority, such as a government

Examples

Political dissidence
Academic dissidence played a role in the movement for democratic reforms.
The government's harsh response failed to stamp out growing dissidence among the population.
Voices of dissidence were suppressed during the military regime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissent - the holding or expression of opinions at variance with those commonly or officially held
