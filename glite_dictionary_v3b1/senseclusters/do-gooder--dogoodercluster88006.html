do-gooder

noun
person who tries to promote social welfare, often self-righteously
8.2 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈduː.ɡʊd.ər/ /; / /də.ˈɡʊd.ər/ (AmE), /ˈduː.ɡʊd.ə/ /; / /də.ˈɡʊd.ə/ (BrE)
a person who is overly concerned with promoting the welfare of others, sometimes in a way that is regarded as interfering or self-righteous

Examples

A do-gooder person
He has a reputation as a do-gooder thanks to his constant efforts to improve the community.
Some people see her as a do-gooder who can't mind her own business.
The town was suspicious of the latest do-gooder campaign promising to solve all their problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: busybody - a person who meddles in the affairs of others; often seen as nosy or interfering
  • synonym: philanthropist - a person who seeks to promote the welfare of others, especially by the generous donation of money to good causes
