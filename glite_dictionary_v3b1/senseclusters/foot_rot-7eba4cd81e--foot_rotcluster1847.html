foot rot

noun
contagious hoof infection in livestock
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊt ˌrɑt / (AmE), / ˈfʊt ˌrɒt / (BrE)
a contagious degenerative infection affecting the feet of hoofed animals, especially cattle and sheep, causing swelling, lameness, and sometimes severe tissue damage

Examples

Foot rot in sheep
If foot rot is left untreated, it can lead to severe lameness in sheep.
Regularly cleaning the barn can help prevent foot rot in livestock.
The farmer treated several cows suffering from foot rot in the wet pastures.
