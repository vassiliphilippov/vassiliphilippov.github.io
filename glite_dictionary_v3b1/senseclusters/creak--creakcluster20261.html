creak

noun
a harsh squeaking sound
4.0
Pronunciation: / kɹik / (AmE), / kɹiːk / (BrE)
a harsh, high-pitched squeaking sound, typically produced by something being moved or put under strain, such as a floorboard or hinge

Examples

A floorboard creak
A sudden creak from the attic made everyone pause and listen.
She heard the creak of a door opening slowly in the hallway.
The old stairs made a loud creak every time someone climbed them.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: groan - a low, mournful sound, often from strain
  • synonym: squeak - a short, high-pitched sound
