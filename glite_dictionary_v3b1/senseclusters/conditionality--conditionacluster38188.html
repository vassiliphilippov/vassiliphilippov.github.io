conditionality

noun
state of being conditional; requirement of conditions
7.3
Pronunciation: / kənˌdɪʃ.əˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / kənˌdɪʃ.əˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being dependent on certain conditions or requirements

Examples

The conditionality of aid
Debates over conditionality are common when discussing international development policies.
He questioned the conditionality attached to foreign aid, arguing it could undermine sovereignty.
The conditionality of the loan agreement required the country to implement economic reforms.
