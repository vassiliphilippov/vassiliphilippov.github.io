dermestid

noun
beetle feeding on dry material
Pronunciation: / dərˈmɛstɪd / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪd / (BrE)
a beetle of a family whose larvae feed on dry animal or plant material, often used to clean animal skeletons

Examples

A dermestid beetle
The museum uses a colony of dermestid beetles to clean animal bones for display.
A single dermestid infestation can damage stored natural history specimens.
Scientists rely on dermestid larvae to remove flesh from skeletons without harming the bones.
