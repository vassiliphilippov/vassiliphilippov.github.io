nominal

adjective
named for a specific person
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːmɪnəl /; / ˈnɑː.mə.nəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl /; / ˈnɒm.ɪ.nəl / (BrE)
existing in name only, or bearing a name without the substance, power, or reality usually associated with it

Examples

Nominal leader
A nominal roll contains the names of all eligible participants.
Certain accounts must be held in a nominal form to identify the holder.
The company issued nominal shares to its founders.
All taxable individuals were included in the government's nominal rolls.
Despite being the nominal leader, she held little actual power in the organization.
The candidates appeared on the nominal list but had no real influence in the decision-making process.
Her position as president is largely nominal, with little real power.
The monthly subscription fee is nominal, making it affordable for everyone.
They were fined a nominal amount for the minor infraction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: real - actually existing or having actual authority
  • synonym: titular - holding a title without real power or authority
  • synonym: token - very small or symbolic in amount or effort
