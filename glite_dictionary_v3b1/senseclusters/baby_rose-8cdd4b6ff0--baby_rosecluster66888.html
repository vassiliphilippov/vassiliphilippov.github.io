baby rose

noun
variety of rose with small clustered flowers
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.bi roʊz / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi rəʊz / (BrE)
A variety of rose bush that grows vigorously and produces clusters of numerous small flowers, often used for hedges and as grafting stock.

Examples

Baby rose bush
Many horticulturists use baby rose as grafting stock because of its vigorous growth.
The gardener recommended baby rose bushes as an attractive, low-maintenance option for a hedge.
We planted a row of baby roses along the fence for their dense, flowering appearance.
