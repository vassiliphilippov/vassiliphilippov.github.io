sirrah

noun
contemptuous term of address (archaic)
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪ.rə/ (AmE), /ˈsɪ.rə/ (BrE)
a term of address formerly used contemptuously to refer to a man or boy of lower status, often in anger

Examples

The word sirrah
"Sirrah, you will listen to me and obey my orders!" thundered the lord of the manor.
In many Shakespearean plays, a master might address his servant as sirrah.
The angry teacher shouted, 'sirrah, do not speak out of turn!'
