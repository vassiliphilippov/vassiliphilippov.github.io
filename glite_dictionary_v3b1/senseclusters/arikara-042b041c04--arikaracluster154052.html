Arikara

noun
a Native American people of the Dakotas
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪkərə / (AmE), / əˈrɪkərə / (BrE)
a member of a North American Indigenous people who formerly lived in the Dakotas, west of the Missouri River; also, the people collectively.

Examples

An Arikara person
Many descendants of the Arikara now live in North Dakota.
She is studying the history and language of the Arikara people.
The Arikara played a significant role in the early fur trade of the Great Plains.
