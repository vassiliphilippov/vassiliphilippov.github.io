wallow

verb
indulge or delight greatly in something
7.3 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈwɑː.loʊ/ /; / /ˈwɔː.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈwɒ.ləʊ/ (BrE)
to spend a lot of time indulging in a particular emotion or situation, often without trying to change it

Examples

To wallow in sadness
After winning the award, she allowed herself to wallow in the joy of her achievement.
He tends to wallow in self-pity when things don't go his way.
They spent the afternoon wallowing in luxury at the spa.
After the breakup, he would wallow in self-pity for weeks.
It's easy to wallow in luxury when life is going well.
She tended to wallow in her disappointment rather than try to move on.
