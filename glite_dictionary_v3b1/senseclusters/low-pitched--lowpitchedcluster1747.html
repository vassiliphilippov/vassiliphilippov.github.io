low-pitched

adjective
having a low pitch (sound/voice)
2.6 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈloʊ.pɪtʃt/ /; / /ˈlaʊ.pɪtʃt/ (AmE), /ˈləʊ.pɪtʃt/ /; / /ˈlaʊ.pɪtʃt/ (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) having a low frequency; not high in pitch.

Examples

Low-pitched voice
His voice became low-pitched and soothing as he reassured the children.
The cello produces a deep, low-pitched sound compared to the violin.
We heard a low-pitched hum coming from the engine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high-pitched - having a high frequency or pitch (of a sound/voice)
