meadow

verb
to become grassy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəʊ / (BrE)
to become or make into a meadow (covered with grasses and wildflowers)

Examples

To meadow a field
After years of neglect, the old field meadowed and became a haven for butterflies.
In spring, the hillside meadowed with a burst of wildflowers and tall grass.
The abandoned lot slowly meadowed as nature reclaimed the space.
The city decided to meadow the empty lot to create a natural park for residents.
Farmers sometimes meadow fields to encourage wildlife and improve soil quality.
After the construction, the company meadowed the area to restore its natural beauty.
