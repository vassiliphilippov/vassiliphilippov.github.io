clumsy

adjective
lacking grace or elegance in expression
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈklʌmzi / (AmE), / ˈklʌmzi / (BrE)
(of expression) lacking elegance, polish, or grace; awkward or inept.

Examples

Clumsy sentence
His clumsy apology only made the situation worse.
She tried to explain her reasons in a rather clumsy fashion.
The novel's dialogue felt clumsy and unnatural at key moments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: awkward - lacking grace or skill; ungainly, especially in movement or expression
  • antonym: elegant - pleasingly graceful and stylish in appearance or manner
  • synonym: inept - lacking skill or ability; clumsy
