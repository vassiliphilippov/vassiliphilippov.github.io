take control

verb
operate a device remotely
4.1
Pronunciation: /teɪk kən.ˈtroʊl/ (AmE), /teɪk kən.ˈtrəʊl/ (BrE)
to connect to and operate another person's computer or device remotely using software, typically for technical support or system administration.

Examples

To take control of a computer
The technician asked permission before he took control of her laptop to install the update.
During the training, the instructor showed how to take control of a remote desktop to troubleshoot problems.
If you allow us, we will take control of the session and apply the security patch for you.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assume control - to begin exercising control or authority over something
  • synonym: remote access - the ability to access and use a computer or network from a different location
