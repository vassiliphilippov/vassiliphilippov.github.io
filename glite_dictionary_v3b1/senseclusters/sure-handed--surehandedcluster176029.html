sure-handed

adjective
confident and skillful in performance
Pronunciation: / ʃʊrˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ʃɔːˈhændɪd / (BrE)
Proficient and confident in performing a task, especially with skill and steadiness.

Examples

A sure-handed player
Despite the pressure, the pianist's sure-handed performance captivated the audience.
Her sure-handed leadership guided the team to success.
The surgeon was sure-handed throughout the delicate operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or action; lacking skill or grace
  • synonym: skillful - having or showing skill
