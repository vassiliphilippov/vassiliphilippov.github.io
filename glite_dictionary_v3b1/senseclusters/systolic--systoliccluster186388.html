systolic

adjective
relating to heart's contraction phase
5.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈstɑːl.ɪk/ /; / /sɪs.ˈtɑːl.ɪk/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈstɒl.ɪk/ /; / /sɪs.ˈtɒl.ɪk/ (BrE)
Relating to the phase of the heartbeat when the heart muscle contracts and pumps blood from the chambers into the arteries.

Examples

Systolic blood pressure
An elevated systolic reading may indicate potential heart problems.
Her systolic blood pressure was higher than normal during the examination.
The doctor explained that systolic pressure measures the force when the heart contracts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diastolic - relating to diastole; the relaxation phase of the heartbeat
