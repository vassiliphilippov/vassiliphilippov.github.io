apothecaries' ounce

noun
historical apothecary weight unit
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑθəˌkɛriz aʊns / (AmE), / əˈpɒθəkəriz aʊns / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in the apothecaries' system, equal to 480 grains or one twelfth of a troy pound.

Examples

An apothecaries' ounce unit
In the old medical texts, dosages were often listed in apothecaries' ounces and grains.
Pharmacists no longer use the apothecaries' ounce as a standard measure, but it appears in historical documents.
The recipe called for one apothecaries' ounce of powdered rhubarb root.
