inhalation anesthetic

noun
gas or vapor used for anesthesia
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌheɪlˈeɪʃən ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk /; / ɪnˌheɪləˈʃən ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ɪnˌheɪlˈeɪʃən ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk /; / ɪnˌheɪləˈʃən ˌanɪsˈθiːtɪk / (BrE)
a substance in the form of a gas or vapor that induces general anesthesia when breathed in

Examples

Modern inhalation anesthetics
Modern inhalation anesthetics are safer and more effective than those used decades ago.
The choice of inhalation anesthetic can affect a patient's recovery time.
The patient was given an inhalation anesthetic before the surgery began.
Children undergoing surgery are often given an inhalation anaesthetic because it works quickly and is easy to control.
Most modern inhalation anaesthetics are administered through a mask or breathing tube.
The surgeon used an inhalation anaesthetic to ensure the patient felt no pain during the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anesthetic - a substance that induces insensitivity to pain
  • synonym: general anaesthetic - Drug that causes loss of consciousness and sensation
  • antonym: intravenous anesthetic - an anesthetic administered by injection into the veins
