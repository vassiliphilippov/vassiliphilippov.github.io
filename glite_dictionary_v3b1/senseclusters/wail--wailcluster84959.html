wail

verb
emit a long, loud cry
Pronunciation: / weɪl / (AmE), / weɪl / (BrE)
to make a long, loud, or mournful cry of pain, sorrow, or grief

Examples

To wail in pain
Ambulance sirens wailed through the quiet night.
He would wail in pain after stubbing his toe on the table.
The baby began to wail when her mother left the room.
Late at night, you could hear someone wailing quietly in the hallway.
She wailed in pain when she touched her injured arm.
The child began to wail softly after falling off her bicycle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: howl - to make a long, loud, mournful cry
  • antonym: laugh - to make sounds expressing amusement or happiness
  • synonym: sob - to cry noisily, making loud, convulsive gasps
  • synonym: weep - to shed tears; to cry
