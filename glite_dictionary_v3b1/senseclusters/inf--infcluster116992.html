mathematical infimum symbol
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪn / (AmE), / ɪn / (BrE)
the greatest lower bound (infimum) of a set in mathematics, often represented as 'inf'.

Examples

Set inf in math
In real analysis, the inf of a set may not be a member of the set itself.
The inf of all positive real numbers is 0, even though 0 is not included.
Mathematicians often write inf to denote the greatest lower bound in proofs.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: minimum - the smallest value in a set
  • antonym: sup - the least upper bound (supremum) of a set in mathematics
