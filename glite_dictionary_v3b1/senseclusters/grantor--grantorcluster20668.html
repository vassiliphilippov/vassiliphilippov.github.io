grantor

noun
person who legally grants property or rights
5.5 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈgræn.tər/ (AmE), /ˈgræn.tə/ (BrE)
a person or legal entity that makes a grant, especially one who transfers property or rights to another in legal form

Examples

The grantor transfers property
According to the contract, the grantor must disclose any known defects in the title.
The grantor and grantee must both appear at the closing to complete the transaction.
The grantor signed the deed to transfer the land to the new owner.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grantee - the person to whom a grant is made (especially of property)
