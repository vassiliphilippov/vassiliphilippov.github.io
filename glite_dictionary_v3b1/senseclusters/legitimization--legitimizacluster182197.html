legitimization

noun
legal recognition of child
Pronunciation: / lɪˌdʒɪtəməˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˌdʒɪtɪmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the legal process of declaring a child born outside marriage as legitimate, granting them the same rights as a child born within marriage

Examples

Legitimization of a child
The couple applied for the legitimization of their daughter after getting married.
Laws regarding legitimization vary from country to country and can affect inheritance rights.
After a court hearing, the judge granted legitimization to the child, ensuring equal legal status.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adoption - the legal process of taking another's child as one's own
  • antonym: illegitimacy - the state of being born to parents not lawfully married
  • synonym: legitimation - the act of making something lawful or legitimate
