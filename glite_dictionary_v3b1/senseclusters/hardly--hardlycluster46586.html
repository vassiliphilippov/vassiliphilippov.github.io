hardly

adverb
only just; barely
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdli / (AmE), / ˈhɑːdli / (BrE)
almost not; only just; with great difficulty

Examples

Hardly see anything
He could hardly believe his eyes when he saw the surprise party.
She was so tired she could hardly keep her eyes open.
The engine was so old it hardly worked anymore.
I can hardly hear what you are saying over the noise.
It was so dark that she could hardly see her own hands.
We hardly ever go out to restaurants these days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barely - only just; by a very small margin
  • antonym: easily - without difficulty or effort
  • synonym: scarcely - only just; almost not
