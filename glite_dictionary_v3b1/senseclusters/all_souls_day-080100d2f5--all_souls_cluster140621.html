All Souls' Day

noun
Christian commemoration day for souls of the dead
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɔl soʊlz deɪ / (AmE), / ɔːl səʊlz deɪ / (BrE)
A Christian holiday observed on November 2, dedicated to praying for the souls of the deceased, especially those believed to be in purgatory.

Examples

All Souls' Day prayers
Many Catholics attend special masses on All Souls' Day to pray for their loved ones who have passed away.
On All Souls' Day, families often visit cemeteries to honor and remember the deceased.
The tradition of All Souls' Day reflects the belief in the power of prayer to help souls in purgatory.
