imitativeness

noun
tendency to imitate or copy
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmɪˈteɪtɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˈteɪtɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or tendency of imitating or copying the behavior or actions of others; unoriginality due to imitation

Examples

The imitativeness of artists
Artists sometimes struggle with imitativeness when searching for their unique style.
In some animal species, imitativeness plays a crucial role in learning survival techniques.
The child's imitativeness was evident as she copied every move her older brother made.
