sonic

adjective
at or near the speed of sound
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of speed) having or caused by a speed approximately equal to that of sound in air at sea level.

Examples

Sonic speed
The experiment measured the pressure waves produced by sonic movement in the atmosphere.
The fighter jet created a sonic boom as it broke the sound barrier.
Traveling at sonic speed, the aircraft reached its destination in record time.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: subsonic - (of speed) less than the speed of sound in air at sea level
  • degree higher: supersonic - (of speed) greater than the speed of sound in air at sea level
