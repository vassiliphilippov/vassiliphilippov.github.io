oligopolistic

adjective
dominated by a few
9.4
Pronunciation: /oʊ.lɪ.gə.poʊl.ˈɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /ɒ.lɪ.ɡɒ.pə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
describing a market or industry that is controlled or dominated by a small number of large companies or organizations

Examples

An oligopolistic market
The telecommunications industry in many countries is highly oligopolistic, with only a few major providers.
Critics argue that the market has become too oligopolistic, reducing choices for consumers.
An oligopolistic structure can make it difficult for new companies to enter the field.
The telecommunications sector is often described as oligopolistic because only a few companies control most of the market.
In an oligopolistic market, firms may avoid price wars to maintain stable profits.
Government regulations can influence the behavior of oligopolistic industries to encourage fair competition.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: competitive - characterized by many participants competing equally
  • degree higher: monopolistic - characterized by control by a single entity
