just-in-time

adjective
manufacturing system that minimizes inventory
Pronunciation: / dʒʌst ɪn taɪm / (AmE), / dʒʌst ɪn taɪm / (BrE)
(of a production or inventory system) designed to receive or produce parts or materials exactly when needed, reducing or eliminating stored inventory.

Examples

A just-in-time system
The factory switched to a just-in-time system to reduce inventory and lower storage costs.
Our supplier guarantees just-in-time deliveries so the assembly line never runs out of parts.
Adopting just-in-time practices helped the small manufacturer cut warehousing expenses dramatically.

Related Terms

  • synonym: just-in-time manufacturing - a production approach that reduces inventory by receiving goods only as they are needed in the production process
  • antonym: stockpiling - the practice of keeping large inventories of goods; contrasts with just-in-time approaches
