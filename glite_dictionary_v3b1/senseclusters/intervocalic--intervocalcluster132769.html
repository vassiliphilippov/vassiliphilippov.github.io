intervocalic

noun
sound between vowels
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərvoʊˈkælɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪntəvəʊˈkælɪk / (BrE)
a sound, especially a consonant, that occurs between two vowels in a word or phrase

Examples

An intervocalic sound
The linguist explained that the /t/ in 'water' is an intervocalic sound in American English.
In some languages, an intervocalic consonant may be pronounced differently than at the start of a word.
The teacher asked students to identify the intervocalic sounds in several example words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consonant - a speech sound produced with some closure of the vocal tract
  • antonym: vowel - a speech sound produced without significant constriction of airflow
