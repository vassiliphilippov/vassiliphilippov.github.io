Ambrosia artemisiifolia

noun
a North American annual weed (common ragweed)
Pronunciation: / æmˈbroʊʒi.ə ɑːrˌtɛmɪˈsiːfəʊliə / (AmE), / æmˈbrəʊzi.ə ɑːˌtɛmɪˈsiːfiːliə / (BrE)
A North American annual weed with finely divided leaves and spikes of green flowers; commonly known as common ragweed.

Examples

Ambrosia artemisiifolia plant
Ambrosia artemisiifolia is a major cause of seasonal allergies in many parts of the United States.
Efforts are underway to control Ambrosia artemisiifolia in Europe, where it was accidentally introduced.
The rapid spread of Ambrosia artemisiifolia has become a concern for local farmers.
