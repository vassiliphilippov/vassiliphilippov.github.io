executory

adjective
not yet completed
9.1
Pronunciation: /ɪg.ˈzek.jə.tɔːr.iː/ /; / /ɪg.ˈzek.jʊ.triː/ (AmE), /ɪg.ˈzek.jə.tɔːr.iː/ /; / /ɪg.ˈzek.jʊ.triː/ (BrE)
not yet completed or in the process of being carried out, especially in law (such as a contract or legal obligation)

Examples

An executory contract
The contract was still executory because neither party had delivered on their promises.
An executory agreement requires further action before it becomes fully effective.
The judge explained that the lease remained executory until all conditions were met.
The contract remains executory until all parties have fulfilled their obligations.
An executory judgment requires further action before it is fully effective.
The lawyer explained the difference between an executory and an executed agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: completed - finished or fully carried out
  • synonym: enforceable - able to be carried out or put into effect by law
  • synonym: pending - awaiting completion or decision
  • antonym: void - having no legal force or effect
