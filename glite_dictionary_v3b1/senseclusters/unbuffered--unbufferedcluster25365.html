unbuffered

adjective
without a buffer
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbʌfərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈbʌfəd / (BrE)
not protected, separated, or stabilized by a buffer; lacking a buffer.

Examples

Unbuffered memory module
The unbuffered memory module is commonly used in personal computers.
Because the solution was unbuffered, its pH changed rapidly when acid was added.
Some systems are more vulnerable to errors when using unbuffered components.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buffered - protected or separated by a buffer
