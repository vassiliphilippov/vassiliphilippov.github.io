cavefish

noun
blind cave-dwelling fish
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪvˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪvˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish that lives in caves and is adapted to darkness, often lacking eyes or pigment

Examples

The cavefish lives underground
The cavefish has evolved to survive without eyesight in total darkness.
Researchers discovered a new species of cavefish deep inside the limestone cavern.
Unlike most fish, the cavefish has no pigment and appears almost transparent.
Scientists discovered a new species of cavefish deep within the limestone caverns.
The cavefish has adapted to total darkness by losing its eyesight over generations.
Unlike most fish, the cavefish relies on other senses to find food in underground streams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blindfish - a fish that is blind, often living in caves
  • antonym: surface fish - a fish that lives in open water, not in caves
