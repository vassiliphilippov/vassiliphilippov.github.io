St. Basil

proper noun
4th-century Christian saint and theologian
7.9
Pronunciation: /seɪnt ˈbæz.əl/ /; / /seɪnt ˈbeɪz.əl/ (AmE), /seɪnt ˈbæz.əl/ (BrE)
a 4th-century bishop of Caesarea, celebrated for defending Christian doctrine against heresies, recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church

Examples

St. Basil the Great
St. Basil is venerated for his contributions to Christian theology and monasticism.
Many towns and churches across Europe bear the name of St. Basil in his honor.
The feast day of St. Basil is celebrated on January 1st in the Eastern Orthodox Church.
