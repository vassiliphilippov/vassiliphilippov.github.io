vacuum bomb

noun
bomb using fuel-air explosive
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈvæk.jum bɑm / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb that uses a fuel-air explosive to produce a powerful blast by creating a vacuum and then igniting the fuel in the air

Examples

A vacuum bomb
A vacuum bomb creates a deadly blast wave by dispersing and igniting fuel in the air.
Experts warn that the effects of a vacuum bomb can be more devastating than conventional explosives.
The military was accused of using a vacuum bomb in the conflict zone, causing widespread damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermobaric bomb - a bomb that produces a powerful blast by heating and rapidly expanding air; another term for a vacuum bomb
