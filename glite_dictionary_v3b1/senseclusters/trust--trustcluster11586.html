trust

noun
arrangement where assets are held for another's benefit
Pronunciation: / trʌst / (AmE), / trʌst / (BrE)
an arrangement in which property or assets are held by a trustee for the benefit of another person or group, known as the beneficiary

Examples

A family trust
Her inheritance is managed through a trust until she turns twenty-one.
The lawyer explained the benefits of setting up a trust for charitable giving.
They established a family trust to protect their assets for future generations.
