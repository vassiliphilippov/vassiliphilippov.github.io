modulation

noun
variation in voice pitch
6.3 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / Àåm…ëd í…ôÀàle…™ É…ôn /; / Àåm…ëÀêd í…ôÀàle…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåm…ídj äÀàle…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the variation of pitch, loudness, or tone in the voice when speaking to convey meaning or emotion

Examples

Modulation of voice
Actors often use careful modulation of their voices to bring characters to life.
Good modulation helps a public speaker keep the audience engaged and attentive.
Her gentle modulation conveyed both confidence and warmth during the interview.
A lack of modulation can make a speaker sound dull or monotonous.
Actors often train to improve their vocal modulation for stage performances.
She impressed the audience with her careful modulation of tone during the speech.

Synonyms

  • inflection
    5.9 1.09/m
    inflection shows small voice changes in pitch, tone, and loudness to mark meaning, while modulation is the general control of those variations.
  • intonation
    4.6 0.67/m
    intonation is the rise and fall of pitch that shapes meaning and expression, while modulation includes pitch plus loudness and tone changes.
  • tone of voice
    3.1 1.87/m
    tone of voice is the overall sound or attitude showing emotion, while modulation means actively changing pitch, loudness, or tone to express meaning.
  • pitch contour
    6.5 0.02/m
    pitch contour is the specific pattern of rising and falling pitch across an utterance, while modulation covers pitch and other vocal changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intonation - the variation of pitch when speaking
  • antonym: monotone - a way of speaking in which the pitch, loudness, or tone does not vary
