modulation

noun
variation in voice pitch
3.7 5.1
Pronunciation: /mɑː.dʒə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mɑː.dʒʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mɒ.djʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mɒ.dʒə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the variation of pitch, loudness, or tone in the voice when speaking to convey meaning or emotion

Examples

Modulation of voice
Actors often use careful modulation of their voices to bring characters to life.
Good modulation helps a public speaker keep the audience engaged and attentive.
Her gentle modulation conveyed both confidence and warmth during the interview.
A lack of modulation can make a speaker sound dull or monotonous.
Actors often train to improve their vocal modulation for stage performances.
She impressed the audience with her careful modulation of tone during the speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intonation - the variation of pitch when speaking
  • antonym: monotone - a way of speaking in which the pitch, loudness, or tone does not vary
