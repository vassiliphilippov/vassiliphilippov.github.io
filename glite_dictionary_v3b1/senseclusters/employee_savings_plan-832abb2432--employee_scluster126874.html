employee savings plan

noun
workplace program for saving and investing
2.0
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈplɔɪi ˈseɪvɪŋz plæn / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɔɪi ˈseɪvɪŋz plan / (BrE)
a program offered by an employer that allows employees to contribute a portion of their earnings to an investment fund, usually for retirement or future expenses

Examples

The employee savings plan program
Many workers participate in the company's employee savings plan to prepare for retirement.
Thanks to her employee savings plan, she was able to build a solid financial foundation.
The employee savings plan allows you to automatically deduct money from your paycheck each month.
