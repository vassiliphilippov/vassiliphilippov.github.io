achromat

noun
corrective optical lens
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪkroʊˌmæt / (AmE), / ˈeɪkrəˌmæt / (BrE)
a lens designed to reduce chromatic and spherical aberration, producing clearer images in optical devices

Examples

Microscope achromat lens
The scientist replaced the old lens with an achromat to improve the clarity of the microscope images.
Many modern cameras use an achromat to minimize color distortion in photographs.
An achromat is essential in telescopes to ensure sharp and accurate views of distant stars.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: apochromat - a lens that corrects chromatic aberration for more colors than an achromat
  • synonym: lens - a piece of glass or other transparent material used to focus light
