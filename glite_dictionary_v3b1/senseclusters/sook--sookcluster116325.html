sook

noun
timid or cowardly person
Pronunciation: / sʊk / (AmE), / sʊk / (BrE)
a person who is easily upset, frightened, or overly sensitive

Examples

A sook at school
Don't be such a sook—it's just a harmless spider in the corner.
His friends teased him for being a sook when he refused to watch the scary movie.
She called her brother a sook after he backed out of the roller coaster ride.
Don't be such a sook just because you lost the game.
Her brother called her a sook when she started crying over the small mistake.
If you keep acting like a sook, people might not take you seriously.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brave - showing courage and not easily frightened
  • synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
  • synonym: crybaby - a person who cries or complains easily
  • antonym: stoic - a person who does not show or complain about pain or hardship
  • synonym: whiner - someone who complains a lot
