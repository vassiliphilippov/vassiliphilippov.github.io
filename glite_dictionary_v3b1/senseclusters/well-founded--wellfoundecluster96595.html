well-founded

adjective
supported by evidence or sound reasoning

(of a belief, suspicion, or claim) supported by good evidence or sound reasoning.

Examples

A well-founded claim
Her concerns about the safety of the building were well-founded.
Only well-founded arguments will be seriously considered in this debate.
The judge determined that there was a well-founded fear of persecution.

Synonyms

    4.4 0.41/m

  • grounded
    4.2 0.00/m
    grounded is based on solid facts and a realistic foundation, while well-founded refers to a belief or claim supported by evidence.
  • tenable
    7.1 0.64/m
