solar parallax

noun
astronomical measurement angle
3.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ.lɚ ˈpær.əˌlæks / (AmE), / ˈsəʊ.lə ˈpær.ə.læks / (BrE)
the angle subtended by the mean equatorial radius of the Earth as seen from a distance of one astronomical unit, used in astronomy to measure distances within the solar system

Examples

Solar parallax angle
Astronomers use the solar parallax to calculate the distance from Earth to the Sun.
Measuring solar parallax was historically important for understanding the size of the solar system.
The value of the solar parallax was revised after new observations were made.

  • synonym: parallax - the apparent shift in position of an object when viewed from different angles
