linguistic relation

noun
relationship between linguistic forms
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between linguistic forms or constituents, such as those found within grammar or language structure

Examples

A linguistic relation in grammar
A key linguistic relation is agreement between adjectives and nouns.
The study analyzed the linguistic relation between subject and verb in different languages.
Understanding the linguistic relation of words helps explain sentence structure.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: semantic relation - relationship based on meaning between words or phrases
  • synonym: syntactic relation - relationship between grammatical elements in sentence structure
