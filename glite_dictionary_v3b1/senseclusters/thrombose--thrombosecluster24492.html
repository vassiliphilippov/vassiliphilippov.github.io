thrombose

verb
become blocked by a blood clot
Pronunciation: / θrʌmˈboʊs / (AmE), / θrɒmˈbəʊz / (BrE)
(of a blood vessel) to become blocked by a blood clot (thrombus).

Examples

To thrombose a vein
If the vein thromboses, blood flow to the affected area will be restricted.
Repeated injury can cause the artery to thrombose suddenly.
The catheter was removed after the vessel thrombosed during treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clot - a thick mass of coagulated liquid, especially blood
  • synonym: embolize - to become or cause to become obstructed by an embolus
