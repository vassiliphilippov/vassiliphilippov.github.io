immunochemistry

noun
study of chemical processes in immunology
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪm.jə.noʊˈkɛm.ɪ.stri / (AmE), / ˌɪm.juː.nəʊˈkɛm.ɪ.stri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry that deals with the chemical structure and processes of the immune system, especially the interactions between antigens and antibodies

Examples

Immunochemistry research
Advancements in immunochemistry have improved the accuracy of many diagnostic laboratory tests.
He published a pioneering paper on the immunochemistry of viral antigens in the early 1980s.
Researchers in immunochemistry have developed new tests to detect infections more quickly.
