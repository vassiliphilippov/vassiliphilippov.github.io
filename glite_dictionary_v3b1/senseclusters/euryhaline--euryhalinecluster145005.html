euryhaline

adjective
tolerant of salinity range
Pronunciation: / jʊˈrɪhəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɪhəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
able to tolerate a wide range of salinity in water, especially referring to aquatic organisms

Examples

A euryhaline fish
The euryhaline fish can survive in both fresh and salt water environments.
Scientists study euryhaline species to understand how animals adapt to changing salinity.
Some crabs are euryhaline, allowing them to live in estuaries where salt levels vary.
