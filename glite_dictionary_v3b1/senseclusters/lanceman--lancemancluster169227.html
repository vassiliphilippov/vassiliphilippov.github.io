lanceman

noun
soldier with a lance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlænsˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnsmən / (BrE)
an old term for a soldier who fights with a lance, especially in cavalry

Examples

Cavalry lanceman leads charge
The lanceman rode at the front of the cavalry charge, leading his unit into battle.
In medieval times, a skilled lanceman was highly valued for his bravery and strength.
The king's army included many lancemen who trained for years to master the lance.
