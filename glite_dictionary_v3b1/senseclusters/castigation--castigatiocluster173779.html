castigation

noun
severe scolding or criticism
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkæstɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkæstɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
severe criticism or punishment, especially using harsh words

Examples

Harsh castigation
After the team's poor performance, the coach's castigation left the players feeling dejected.
Despite the public castigation, she remained calm and refused to argue back.
The manager's castigation of the employee was heard by everyone in the office.
He accepted the castigation from his peers with humility and promised to improve.
Public castigation of the company's actions forced it to issue an apology.
The teacher's castigation of the student's behavior was harsh but necessary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp criticism or disapproval
  • synonym: reprimand - an official or formal scolding
