distracted

adjective
unable to concentrate; attention diverted
2.4 2.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪd/ /; / /dɪ.ˈstræk.təd/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstræk.tɪd/ /; / /dɪ.ˈstræk.təd/ (BrE)
Unable to concentrate because one’s attention is diverted, especially due to worry or anxiety.

Examples

To be distracted by worry
After receiving the bad news, I was too distracted to finish my work.
He drove past his exit because he was distracted by his thoughts.
She seemed distracted during the meeting and kept glancing at her phone.

  • antonym: focused - directing all attention to a particular task or subject
