splice

noun
a junction where things have been joined
9.9
Pronunciation: / splaɪs / (AmE), / splaɪs / (BrE)
a place or joint where two pieces of something, such as rope, wire, film, or tape, have been joined together

Examples

A rope splice
After examining the rope, he found the splice was holding up well under tension.
An imperfect splice caused the film to break during the projection.
The technician noticed a weak splice in the cable and repaired it immediately.
A strong splice is essential when joining two cables for safety reasons.
She inspected the splice in the rope to ensure it would hold under tension.
The electrician finished the job with a neat splice in the wiring.

  • synonym: seam - a line where two edges are joined
