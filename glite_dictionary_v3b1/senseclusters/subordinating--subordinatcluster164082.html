subordinating

adjective
connecting a subordinate clause to a main clause (grammar)
Pronunciation: / səˈbɔrdɪˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / səˈbɔːdɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
serving to connect a subordinate clause to a main clause, especially as a type of conjunction in grammar

Examples

Subordinating conjunction
A subordinating conjunction such as 'because' links a dependent clause to an independent clause.
Teachers often explain the difference between subordinating and coordinating conjunctions.
The word 'although' is used as a subordinating conjunction in the sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coordinating - serving to connect words or clauses of equal rank
