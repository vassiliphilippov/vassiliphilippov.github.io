civil censorship

noun
military censorship of civilian communications
7.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪv.əl ˈsen.sər.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ˈsɪv.əl ˈsen.sə.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
the official examination and restriction of civilian communications, such as letters, printed material, or films, by the military in territories under armed forces' control

Examples

Civil censorship during war
During wartime, civil censorship ensured that sensitive information did not reach enemy hands.
Letters sent from occupied territories were often subject to civil censorship by the governing military authorities.
The presence of civil censorship made it difficult for people to communicate freely across borders.
