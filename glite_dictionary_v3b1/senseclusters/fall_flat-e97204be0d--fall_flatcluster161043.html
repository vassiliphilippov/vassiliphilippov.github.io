fall flat

verb
fail completely (esp. to amuse or impress)
3.2
Pronunciation: / fɔl flæt / (AmE), / fɔːl flæt / (BrE)
to fail completely, especially in making people laugh or attracting interest

Examples

Jokes fall flat
His jokes fell flat and no one in the audience even smiled.
If the proposal falls flat, we'll need to come up with a new plan.
The marketing campaign fell flat, failing to generate any interest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flop - to fail completely, especially in a performance or attempt
  • antonym: succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result, opposite of fail
