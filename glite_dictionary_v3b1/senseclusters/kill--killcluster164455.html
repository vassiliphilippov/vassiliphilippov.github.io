kill

verb
cause death; be fatal
3.8 1.0
Pronunciation: /kɪl/ (AmE), /kɪl/ (BrE)
to cause the death of a person, animal, or other living thing (intentionally or unintentionally)

Examples

To kill a person
Drinking and driving kills thousands every year.
Smoking cigarettes can kill even after years of use.
Too much salt in your diet can kill over time.
Farmers sometimes kill animals for food before major holidays.
His careless driving could have killed someone on the road.
The police arrested him after he killed his business partner.
A falling branch killed the hiker as she walked beneath the tree.
The patient was killed by a reaction to the medication.
Thousands of birds are accidentally killed by wind turbines every year.
