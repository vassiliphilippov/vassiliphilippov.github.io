betray

verb
unintentionally reveal something hidden
4.5
Pronunciation: / bɪˈtreɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈtreɪ / (BrE)
to unintentionally reveal (a feeling, secret, or fact) through words, actions, or appearance

Examples

Betray a feeling
Although he tried to act calm, his hands betrayed his excitement.
Her nervous glance betrayed her anxiety during the interview.
The shaky voice betrayed the fear he tried to hide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden
  • synonym: disclose - to make something known
