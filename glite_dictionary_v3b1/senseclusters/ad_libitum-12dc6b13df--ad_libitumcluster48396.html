ad libitum

adverb
without preparation; as desired
7.1
Pronunciation: / æd ˈlɪbɪtəm / (AmE), / ad ˈlɪbɪtəm / (BrE)
without prior preparation; according to one's wishes, especially in music or performance

Examples

Ad libitum in music
In jazz performances, musicians often play ad libitum passages to showcase their creativity.
She addressed the guests ad libitum, rather than relying on her prepared speech.
The actor chose to ad libitum during his closing monologue, surprising the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impromptu - done without being planned or rehearsed
  • antonym: planned - done with prior intention or preparation
