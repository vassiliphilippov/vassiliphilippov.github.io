step on

verb
treat someone badly to gain advantage
Pronunciation: / stɛp ɑn / (AmE), / stɛp ɒn / (BrE)
to treat someone badly, especially in order to gain an advantage or assert power

Examples

To step on people
His manager often steps on employees to climb the corporate ladder.
I refuse to step on others just to get a promotion.
She felt that her ideas were constantly stepped on during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abuse - to treat someone cruelly or violently
  • synonym: mistreat - to treat someone badly or unfairly
  • antonym: respect - to treat someone with consideration and honor
