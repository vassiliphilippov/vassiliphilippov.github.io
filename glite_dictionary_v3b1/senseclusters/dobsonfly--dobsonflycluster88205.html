dobsonfly

noun
large insect; aquatic larvae used as bait
7.4 9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑːb.sən.flaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdɒb.sən.flaɪ/ (BrE)
A large, soft-bodied insect whose adult male has long, slender mandibles; its aquatic larvae are often used as fishing bait.

Examples

A male dobsonfly
A male dobsonfly can be easily identified by its very long mandibles.
Because the dobsonfly breeds in clean streams, its presence often indicates good water quality.
The dobsonfly is well known among anglers for its large, fierce-looking larvae.
