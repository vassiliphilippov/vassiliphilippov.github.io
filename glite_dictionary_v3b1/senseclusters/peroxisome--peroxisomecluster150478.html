peroxisome

noun
cellular organelle
9.0
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɑksɪˌsoʊm / (AmE), / pəˈrɒksɪˌsəʊm / (BrE)
a small organelle in cells that breaks down fatty acids and detoxifies harmful substances

Examples

The peroxisome in cells
The peroxisome plays a key role in breaking down fatty acids within the cell.
Scientists study the peroxisome to understand how cells detoxify harmful chemicals.
A defect in the peroxisome can lead to serious metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lysosome - a cell organelle that contains digestive enzymes
  • synonym: mitochondrion - an organelle that produces energy for the cell
