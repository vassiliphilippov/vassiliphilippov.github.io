Hebrews

noun
ancient people descended from Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhiˌbroʊz / (AmE), / ˈhiːbruːz / (BrE)
An ancient people traditionally descended from Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, regarded as the ancestors of the Jewish people and recipients of God's covenant according to the Hebrew Bible.

Examples

Ancient Hebrews
According to tradition, Moses led the Hebrews out of slavery in Egypt.
The Hebrews are often described as the ancestors of the Jewish people in religious texts.
The language spoken by the ancient Hebrews developed into what is now known as Hebrew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Israelites - members of the ancient Hebrew nation, especially in the context of the Exodus
  • synonym: Jews - members of the Jewish people, descendants of the Hebrews
