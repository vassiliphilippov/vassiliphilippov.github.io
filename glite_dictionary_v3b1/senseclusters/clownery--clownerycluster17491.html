clownery

noun
silly behavior
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈklaʊnəri / (AmE), / ˈklaʊnəri / (BrE)
silly or foolish behavior, especially intended to amuse others

Examples

Classroom clownery
The teacher was not amused by his constant clownery during the lesson.
Her clownery at the party made everyone laugh and relax.
Tired of the clownery, the coach asked the team to focus on practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: seriousness - the quality of being serious or earnest
  • synonym: tomfoolery - foolish or silly behavior
