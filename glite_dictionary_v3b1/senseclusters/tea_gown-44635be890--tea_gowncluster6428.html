tea gown

noun
long, loose dress for informal afternoon wear
6.8
Pronunciation: / ti ˈɡaʊn / (AmE), / tiː ˈɡaʊn / (BrE)
a long, loose-fitting dress, traditionally worn by women in the late 19th and early 20th centuries for informal afternoon at-home occasions, especially tea.

Examples

A tea gown dress
In Edwardian novels, the heroine often changes into a tea gown before visitors arrive.
She wore a lovely pale blue tea gown for the afternoon gathering.
The costume exhibit included a beautifully preserved Victorian tea gown.
