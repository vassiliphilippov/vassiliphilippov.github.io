heavy water

noun
water with deuterium used in nuclear reactors
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvi ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water in which a significant proportion of the hydrogen atoms are replaced with deuterium, used in some types of nuclear reactors as a moderator or coolant.

Examples

Heavy water reactors
Canada uses heavy water reactors to generate nuclear energy.
Scientists studied the effects of heavy water on chemical reactions.
The plant requires tons of heavy water to function safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deuterium - a stable isotope of hydrogen with one proton and one neutron
