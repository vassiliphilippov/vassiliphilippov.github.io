extubate

verb
to remove a tube
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈstuːbeɪt / (AmE), / ɛkˈtjuːbeɪt / (BrE)
to remove a tube, especially from a patient's airway, after medical treatment

Examples

To extubate a patient
The medical team decided to extubate the patient after confirming he could breathe on his own.
It is important to monitor patients closely after they are extubated in the recovery room.
Nurses prepared the necessary equipment before the doctor began to extubate the child.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intubate - to insert a tube into a body passage, especially the airway
