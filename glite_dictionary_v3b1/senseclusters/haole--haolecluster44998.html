haole

noun
non-native Hawaiian
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊli / (AmE), / ˈhaʊli / (BrE)
a person who is not native to Hawaii, especially a white person

Examples

A haole visitor
Many locals refer to newcomers as haole when they first arrive in Hawaii.
He felt out of place as a haole in the small Hawaiian community.
Some traditions are unfamiliar to a haole visiting the islands for the first time.
Many haole tourists visit Hawaii each year.
Some locals feel that haole newcomers do not understand Hawaiian traditions.
The guide explained the meaning of the word haole to the visitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreigner - a person from another country or place
  • antonym: local - a person native to a particular place
