LCA

noun
last common ancestor
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl siː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛl siː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'last common ancestor', meaning the most recent shared ancestor of two or more groups or species

Examples

LCA of two species
Scientists debate when the LCA of modern birds and reptiles lived.
The LCA of humans and gorillas is estimated to have existed millions of years ago.
Genetic studies help researchers identify the LCA of different animal species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancestor - a person or animal from whom one is descended
  • antonym: descendant - a person or animal that is descended from a particular ancestor
