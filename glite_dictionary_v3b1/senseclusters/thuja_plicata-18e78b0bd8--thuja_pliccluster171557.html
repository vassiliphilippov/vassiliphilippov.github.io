Thuja plicata

noun
large evergreen tree, western red cedar
Pronunciation: / ˈθuːdʒə plaɪˈkeɪtə / (AmE), / ˈθjuːdʒə plɪˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
A large evergreen tree native to the northwestern United States and Canada, commonly known as western red cedar; valued for its timber and aromatic wood.

Examples

Thuja plicata tree
Native peoples have traditionally used Thuja plicata bark for weaving baskets and clothing.
The Thuja plicata can grow over 60 meters tall in old-growth forests.
The dense wood of the Thuja plicata is prized for its resistance to rot.
