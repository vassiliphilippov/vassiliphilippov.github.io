job

verb
to do casual work
2.6
Pronunciation: / dʒɑb / (AmE), / dʒɒb / (BrE)
to do occasional or short-term work, often for payment

Examples

To job as a tutor
During college, she jobbed as a tutor to earn extra money.
Many actors job between major roles to support themselves.
He jobbed for several companies before finding a permanent position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freelance - to work independently for different companies or people
  • antonym: retire - to stop working permanently, usually due to age
  • synonym: work - to perform tasks or duties for payment
