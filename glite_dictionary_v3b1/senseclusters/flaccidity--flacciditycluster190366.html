flaccidity

noun
state of being soft or limp
7.4 9.6
Pronunciation: / flæˈsɪdəti / (AmE), / flakˈsɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being soft, limp, or lacking firmness; flabbiness.

Examples

Soft flaccidity
Critics noted a certain flaccidity in the writing, lacking the tension of his earlier work.
Muscle flaccidity can occur after a nerve injury, leading to weakness in the affected limb.
The flaccidity of the old sofa made it uncomfortable to sit on for long.
