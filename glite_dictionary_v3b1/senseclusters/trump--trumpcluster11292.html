trump

verb
to surpass or defeat
6.6 3.9
Pronunciation: /trʌmp/ (AmE), /trʌmp/ (BrE)
to surpass or defeat someone or something by doing something better or more effective

Examples

To trump a rival
His quick thinking trumped all of his competitors in the debate.
Innovation often trumps experience in rapidly changing industries.
No matter what plan they proposed, she always seemed to trump them with a better idea.
He hoped to trump his rivals with a new strategy.
Her skills will trump any competition.
The company managed to trump its competitors by launching an innovative product first.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excel - to be exceptionally good at or proficient in an activity or subject
  • antonym: lose - to fail to win or succeed against an opponent
  • antonym: lose to - to be beaten by a competitor or rival
  • synonym: outdo - to do better than someone else
  • synonym: outperform - perform better than someone or something
  • synonym: surpass - to exceed or go beyond
