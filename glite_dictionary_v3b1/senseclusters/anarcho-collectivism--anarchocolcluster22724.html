anarcho-collectivism

noun
anarchist doctrine favoring collective ownership
9.9 9.4
Pronunciation: /ə.nɑːr.koʊ.kəl.ˈekt.ɪ.vɪ.zəm/ /; / /æn.ɑːr.koʊ.kəl.ˈekt.ɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ə.nɑː.kəʊ.kə.ˈlek.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ /; / /æn.ɑː.kəʊ.kə.ˈlek.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A doctrine within anarchism advocating the abolition of the state and private ownership of the means of production, favoring collective ownership and management.

Examples

Anarcho-collectivism doctrine
Debates over anarcho-collectivism vs. individualist anarchism continue in modern political philosophy.
His political writings reveal a leaning toward anarcho-collectivism rather than anarcho-communism.
Many 19th-century radicals were inspired by anarcho-collectivism as a vision for reorganizing society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anarchism - Political philosophy advocating self-governed societies with voluntary institutions
  • degree higher: anarcho-communism - A branch of anarchism advocating common ownership with distribution based on need, rather than work contributed
  • synonym: collectivism - Principle of collective ownership of property and production
