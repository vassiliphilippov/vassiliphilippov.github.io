epiphytotic

adjective
widespread plant disease outbreak
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪfaɪˈtɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪfaɪˈtɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease) spreading rapidly and extensively among plants of a single kind, especially over a wide area; relating to or resembling an epidemic in plant populations.

Examples

Epiphytotic plant disease
Researchers are studying new methods to predict epiphytotic outbreaks in monoculture fields.
The epiphytotic spread of rust fungus prompted farmers to alter their planting patterns.
The late blight of potatoes in the 1840s was an epiphytotic that devastated crops across Ireland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epidemic - a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community at a particular time
