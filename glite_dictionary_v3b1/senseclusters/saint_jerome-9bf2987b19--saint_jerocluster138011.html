Saint Jerome

proper noun
Christian saint and translator of the Vulgate Bible
8.0
Pronunciation: /seɪnt dʒə.ˈroʊm/ (AmE), /seɪnt dʒə.ˈrəʊm/ (BrE)
a Christian theologian and scholar (c. 347–420) known for translating the Bible from Hebrew and Greek into Latin (the Vulgate); recognized as a saint and Doctor of the Church

Examples

Saint Jerome the scholar
Saint Jerome is best known for his translation of the Bible into Latin, which became known as the Vulgate.
Many Renaissance artists depicted Saint Jerome in their paintings, often showing him at work in his study.
The feast day of Saint Jerome is celebrated on September 30th in the Western Christian tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Saint Augustine - another prominent early Christian theologian and Church Father
  • synonym: Vulgate - the Latin translation of the Bible produced by Jerome
