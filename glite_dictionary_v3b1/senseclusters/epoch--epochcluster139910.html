epoch

noun
one training pass in machine learning
7.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆiË.pÉ‘k / (AmE), / ËˆiË.pÉ’k / (BrE)
(in machine learning) one complete pass through the entire training dataset, during which model parameters may be updated.

Examples

Training epoch
During training, the model completed its first epoch, and the engineer checked initial loss curves carefully.
They scheduled fifty epochs to ensure the network converged before fine-tuning the hyperparameters on the validation set.
If each epoch processes millions of images, training can take several hours or even days on standard GPUs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: iteration - one step or update in a training loop (often refers to a batch update); sometimes confused with an epoch
  • synonym: pass - a single traversal of the training data; often used interchangeably with 'epoch' in ML contexts
