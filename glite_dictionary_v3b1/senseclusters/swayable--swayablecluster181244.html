swayable

adjective
easily influenced
Pronunciation: / ˈsweɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈsweɪəbəl / (BrE)
easily influenced or persuaded to change an opinion or position

Examples

Swayable voters
The committee members were not swayable by the passionate speech.
Some voters are more swayable than others during election campaigns.
Her opinion on the matter proved surprisingly swayable after hearing new evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inflexible - unwilling to change or be influenced
  • synonym: persuadable - easily convinced or influenced
