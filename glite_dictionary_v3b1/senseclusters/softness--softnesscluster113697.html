softness

noun
gentle, subdued visual quality
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔft.nəs /; / ˈsɔft.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɒft.nəs /; / ˈsɒft.nɪs / (BrE)
a gentle or subdued visual quality, such as soft light or indistinct edges

Examples

Softness of the light
I love the softness of the candlelight during dinner.
The softness of the evening sky gave the landscape a calming effect.
The artist captured the softness of the distant hills in pastel tones.
Photographers often avoid too much softness in an image unless they want a particular mood.
The softness of the painting's edges created a dreamlike atmosphere.
The softness of the sunlight made the landscape appear hazy and serene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - an indistinct or hazy appearance
  • synonym: gentleness - the quality of being gentle, calm, or mild
  • antonym: harshness - quality of being visually strong, sharp, or glaring
  • antonym: sharpness - the quality of having clear, distinct edges or outlines
