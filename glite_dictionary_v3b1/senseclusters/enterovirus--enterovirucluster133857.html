enterovirus

noun
group of viruses infecting the gut
9.1 6.1
Pronunciation: /en.tə.roʊ.ˈvaɪ.rəs/ /; / /en.tə.rə.ˈvaɪ.rəs/ (AmE), /en.tə.rəʊ.ˈvaɪ.rəs/ /; / /en.tə.rə.ˈvaɪ.rəs/ (BrE)
Any of a group of picornaviruses that infect the gastrointestinal tract and can spread to other parts of the body, especially the nervous system.

Examples

An enterovirus infection
Poliovirus, which can lead to paralysis, is a well-known type of enterovirus.
Recent outbreaks of enterovirus have prompted health officials to remind the public about hygiene practices.
The doctor suspected that the child’s fever was caused by an enterovirus infection.
