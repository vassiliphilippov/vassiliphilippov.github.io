nonsensical

adjective
having no intelligible meaning
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɛnsɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɛnsɪkəl / (BrE)
having no meaning, or being so illogical or absurd that it does not make sense

Examples

A nonsensical idea
She dismissed his nonsensical remarks with a wave of her hand.
The children invented a nonsensical language just for fun.
The theory he proposed sounds completely nonsensical to experts in the field.
His explanation for missing work was completely nonsensical and no one believed him.
She grew frustrated with his nonsensical arguments during the debate.
The film was criticized for its nonsensical plot that confused the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable or illogical; ridiculous
  • synonym: meaningless - lacking meaning or significance
  • synonym: ridiculous - deserving or inviting derision or mockery; absurd
  • antonym: sensible - done or chosen in accordance with wisdom or prudence; reasonable
  • antonym: sensical - having a clear or understandable meaning
