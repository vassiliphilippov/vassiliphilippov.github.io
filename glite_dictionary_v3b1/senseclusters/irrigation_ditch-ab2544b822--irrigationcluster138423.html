irrigation ditch

noun
artificial channel for supplying water
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪr.əˈɡeɪ.ʃən dɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən dɪtʃ / (BrE)
a narrow channel artificially constructed to supply water to dry land, especially for agricultural purposes

Examples

Old irrigation ditch
After many years, the old irrigation ditch was in need of repair to prevent water loss.
Crops grew abundantly thanks to the irrigation ditch that supplied a steady flow of water.
The farmer dug an irrigation ditch to bring water to his fields during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canal - an artificial waterway for navigation, irrigation, or drainage
  • antonym: drainage ditch - a ditch constructed to carry away excess water
