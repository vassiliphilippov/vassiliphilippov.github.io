hangover

noun
aftereffects of alcohol or drug use
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhæŋˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈhæŋˌəʊvə / (BrE)
A feeling of illness or discomfort following the use of alcohol or certain drugs, typically involving headache, nausea, and sensitivity to light and sound.

Examples

A bad hangover
Drinking plenty of water can help with a hangover.
He called in sick because his hangover was so bad.
She had a terrible hangover after the party last night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intoxication - the state of being affected by alcohol or drugs
  • degree higher: withdrawal - symptoms following the cessation of drug or alcohol use
