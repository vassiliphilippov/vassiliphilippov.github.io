loosen the apron strings

verb
allow someone to become independent
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsən ði ˈeɪprən strɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈluːsən ði ˈeɪprən strɪŋz / (BrE)
to allow someone, especially a child or dependent, to become more independent by granting greater freedom or responsibility

He realized he needed to loosen the apron strings and let his daughter make her own decisions.
Some mothers find it difficult to loosen the apron strings as their children grow up.
