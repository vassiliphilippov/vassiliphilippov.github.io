loadout

noun
equipment removal process
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊdaʊt / (AmE), / ˈləʊdaʊt / (BrE)
the process of loading or removing equipment, especially for transport after an event or mission

Examples

Stage loadout
After the concert, the crew began the loadout to clear the stage quickly.
The team organized the loadout of all equipment before leaving the site.
Proper planning made the loadout much faster after the event ended.

Related Terms

  • antonym: setup - the process of arranging or installing equipment before an event
  • antonym: unloading - the act of removing goods or equipment from a vehicle or place
