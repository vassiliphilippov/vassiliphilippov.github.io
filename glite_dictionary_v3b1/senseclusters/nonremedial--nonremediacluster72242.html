nonremedial

adjective
not correctable
8.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.rɪ.ˈmiː.di.əl/ /; / /nən.rɪ.ˈmiː.di.əl/ (AmE), /nɒn.rɪ.ˈmiː.di.əl/ /; / /nən.rɪ.ˈmiː.di.əl/ (BrE)
not capable of being corrected, repaired, or remedied; incurable or irreparable.

Examples

Nonremedial damage
The damage to the ancient painting was nonremedial after the fire destroyed much of its surface.
Some environmental changes are nonremedial and cannot be reversed by human intervention.
The court ruled that the harm caused by the company was nonremedial and required compensation.
