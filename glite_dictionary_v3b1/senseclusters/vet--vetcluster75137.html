vet

verb
to examine or check carefully
Pronunciation: / vɛt / (AmE), / vɛt / (BrE)
to examine or check something or someone carefully to make sure they are acceptable, suitable, or qualified

Examples

To vet candidates
All job applicants are thoroughly vetted before being invited for an interview.
The publisher will vet the manuscript before it goes to print.
We need to vet all the data sources to ensure their reliability.
Before signing the contract, we need to vet the supplier thoroughly.
The committee will vet all applicants before making a decision.
We should always vet suppliers to ensure they meet our quality standards.
It's important to vet all candidates for high-level positions carefully.
She spent hours vetting each candidate's resume to ensure they met the criteria.
The company will vet your application before proceeding to the next stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: check - to examine for accuracy, quality, or suitability
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • synonym: inspect - to look at something carefully in order to learn more about it or find problems
  • synonym: investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry into something
  • synonym: screen - to examine something carefully to decide if it is suitable
  • synonym: scrutinize - to examine or observe carefully with great attention to detail
