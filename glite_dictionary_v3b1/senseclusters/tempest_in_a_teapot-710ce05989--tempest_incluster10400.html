tempest in a teapot

noun
fuss over a trivial issue
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpəst ɪn ə ˈtiˌpɑt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpɪst ɪn ə ˈtiːˌpɒt / (BrE)
An unnecessary or exaggerated commotion or dispute about a trivial matter.

Examples

A tempest in a teapot fuss
Everyone acted as if the small mistake was a disaster, but it was really just a tempest in a teapot.
The controversy over the policy change ended up being a tempest in a teapot with little lasting impact.
The disagreement between the neighbors turned out to be a tempest in a teapot and quickly blew over.

Related Terms

  • synonym: storm in a teacup - British idiom for an exaggerated reaction to a trivial matter
