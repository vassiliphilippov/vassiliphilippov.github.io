craftsmanship

noun
skill in a craft or trade
4.1 2.73/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr√¶ft.sm…ônÀå É…™p / (AmE), / Ààkr…ëÀêft.sm…ôn. É…™p / (BrE)
Skill and artistry in a particular craft, occupation, or trade, especially as evident in the quality of work produced.

Examples

Fine craftsmanship
The exquisite craftsmanship of the wooden table impressed everyone who saw it.
This pottery shows remarkable craftsmanship for such a young artist.
Traditional Japanese craftsmanship is highly valued around the world.

Synonyms

  • workmanship
    4.1 1.77/m
    workmanship shows the technical skill used to make an object, while craftsmanship adds artistic care and quality in the result.
  • artisanry
    6.7 0.01/m
    artisanry refers to the work or products made by an artisan, while craftsmanship emphasizes skilled artistry and overall quality of that work.
  • craft
    3.7 44/m
    craft names the skilled activity or trade, while craftsmanship focuses on the artistic skill and the quality shown in the finished work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amateurishness - lack of skill or refinement in a craft or occupation
  • synonym: artisanship - skill characteristic of an artisan or craftsman
