Kerbela

proper noun
holy city in central Iraq for Shiite Muslims
Pronunciation: / kərˈbɛlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɛlə / (BrE)
a city in central Iraq, regarded as a holy city by Shiite Muslims, known as the site of the tomb of Husayn ibn Ali, the grandson of the Prophet Muhammad, who was killed there in 680 CE

Examples

Kerbela city
After visiting Baghdad, they continued their journey southward to Kerbela.
Every year, thousands of pilgrims travel to Kerbela to commemorate the martyrdom of Husayn ibn Ali.
The city of Kerbela holds immense significance in Shiite history and tradition.
