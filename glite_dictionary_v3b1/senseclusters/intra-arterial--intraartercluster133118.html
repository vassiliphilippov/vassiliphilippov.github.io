intra-arterial

noun
artery-based procedure
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.trə ɑːr.ˈtɪr.iː.əl/ /; / /ɪn.trə.ɑːr.ˈtɪr.iː.əl/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.trə ɑː.ˈtɪə.rɪ.əl/ /; / /ɪn.trə.ɑː.ˈtɪə.rɪ.əl/ (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a drug or therapy is delivered directly into an artery.

Examples

An intra-arterial procedure
The doctor recommended an intra-arterial to deliver the medication directly to the affected area.
An intra-arterial can be used to target tumors with chemotherapy more precisely.
After the intra-arterial, the patient was monitored closely for any side effects.
