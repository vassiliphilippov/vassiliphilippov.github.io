counterthrust

noun
responsive attack
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌθrʌst / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌθrʌst / (BrE)
a quick attack made in response to an opponent's move, especially in fencing or battle

Examples

A counterthrust in battle
The general ordered a counterthrust to push back the advancing enemy forces.
With perfect timing, she delivered a counterthrust that caught her opponent off guard.
A well-executed counterthrust can quickly change the outcome of a fencing match.
