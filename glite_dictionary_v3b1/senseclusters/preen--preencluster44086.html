preen

verb
dress or groom oneself carefully
7.0 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / priÀên / (AmE), / priÀên / (BrE)
to dress or groom oneself with great care and attention to appearance

Examples

To preen before a mirror
Before the party, she spent an hour to preen in front of the mirror.
He would always preen himself before important meetings, choosing his clothes carefully.
The actors preened backstage, making sure they looked perfect before stepping on stage.
