pump

verb
move up and down repeatedly
Pronunciation: / pʌmp / (AmE), / pʌmp / (BrE)
to move something up and down or back and forth repeatedly, especially in a regular or forceful way

Examples

To pump iron
He pumped iron every morning to build his strength.
She pumped her arms as she ran up the hill.
The children pumped their legs to swing higher in the playground.
He pumped the bellows up and down to keep the fire going.
He pumped the brakes furiously as the car started to skid.
She pumped the handle to draw water from the well.
