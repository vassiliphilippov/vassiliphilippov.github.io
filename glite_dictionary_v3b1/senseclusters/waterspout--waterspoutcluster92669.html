waterspout

noun
channel for draining water from a roof
4.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…îÀêt…ôrÀåspa ät / (AmE), / Ààw…îÀêt…ôÀåspa ät / (BrE)
a channel or pipe through which water is carried away, especially one that drains water from the gutter of a roof

Examples

Roof waterspout
After the storm, debris clogged the waterspout and caused a leak in the roof.
He climbed a ladder to clear the leaves from the waterspout before winter arrived.
The old house had a decorative metal waterspout shaped like a dragon's head.

Synonyms

  • downspout
    3.4 0.28/m
    a downspout is the vertical pipe fixed to a roof gutter that directs rain down to the ground, while waterspout names that roof drain.
  • downpipe
    3.0 0.01/m
    a downpipe is a British term for the pipe that takes rain from the gutter to drains, while waterspout refers to that roof channel.
  • rainspout
    2.6 0.01/m
    a rainspout is a less common name for a roof drain or small pipe that carries rain down, while waterspout is the standard term.

Related Terms
