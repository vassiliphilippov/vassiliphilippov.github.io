oxymoron

noun
rhetorical device joining contradictory terms
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːksiˈmɔːrɒn / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈmɔːrɒn / (BrE)
A figure of speech in which apparently contradictory terms are combined for effect, such as 'deafening silence'.

Examples

An oxymoron phrase
Her use of oxymoron in her poetry adds depth and complexity to the language.
The phrase 'bittersweet' is a classic example of an oxymoron.
To describe the meal as 'awfully good' is to use an oxymoron.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paradox - a statement that seems self-contradictory but may express truth
