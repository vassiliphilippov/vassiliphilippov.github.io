compounder

noun
settler of offenses
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpaʊndər / (AmE), / kəmˈpaʊndə / (BrE)
a person who agrees to settle or not prosecute an offense in exchange for money or other benefit

Examples

Legal compounder
The compounder negotiated a payment to avoid taking the case to court.
Authorities warned that acting as a compounder in criminal cases is illegal in many countries.
The lawyer was accused of being a compounder after settling the matter privately for a fee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mediator - a person who helps settle disputes between others
  • antonym: prosecutor - a person who brings legal action against someone
