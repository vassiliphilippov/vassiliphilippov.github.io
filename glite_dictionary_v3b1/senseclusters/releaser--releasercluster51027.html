releaser

noun
release mechanism
6.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààlis…ôr / (AmE), / r…™ÀàliÀês…ô / (BrE)
a small device or mechanism used to release a catch, fastener, parachute, camera shutter, or similar object

Examples

Camera releaser
The photographer used a cable releaser to avoid shaking the camera during long exposures.
Before jumping, the skydiver checked that the parachute releaser was working properly.
He pressed the releaser to open the locked compartment quickly in an emergency.

Synonyms

  • ejector
    7.0 0.20/m
    an ejector forcefully pushes out objects like shells or parts, while a releaser lets a catch or shutter open in a controlled way
  • expeller
    7.7 0.02/m
    an expeller forces things out mechanically and often pushes them away, while a releaser simply lets a catch or fastener open gently
