surety

noun
one who guarantees another's obligation
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʊrəti, ˈsjʊrəti /; / ˈʃʊrəti /; / ˈʃʊrəti, ˈʃɝːəti / (AmE), / ˈʃʊərəti, ˈʃɔːrəti /; / ˈʃɔːrəti / (BrE)
a person who takes responsibility for another's obligation, such as paying a debt or ensuring someone appears in court; a guarantor

Examples

A surety signs
As a surety, she was financially responsible if her brother defaulted on the loan.
Each surety signed an agreement guaranteeing payment should the primary debtor fail.
The court required a surety before releasing the accused on bail.
As a surety, the negotiator was detained by the opposing side until the ransom was paid.
In medieval times, important dignitaries were often kept as sureties during delicate negotiations.
The governor agreed to release the surety once the terms of the treaty were fulfilled.
A surety is sometimes needed for release on bail.
The bank accepted the property as surety for the loan.
The contractor was required to provide a surety before beginning the work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guarantee - a formal assurance that certain conditions will be fulfilled
  • synonym: guarantor - a person who guarantees to pay back a loan if the borrower does not
  • synonym: hostage - a person seized or held as security for the fulfillment of a condition
  • synonym: insurance - a means of guaranteeing protection against loss or risk
  • antonym: principal - the person for whom someone acts as surety
