lysin

noun
agent that causes cell lysis
5.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsɪn / (BrE)
a substance, such as an antibody or enzyme, that is capable of causing lysis (the disintegration of cells)

Examples

A lysin enzyme
Researchers discovered a new lysin that attacks the bacterium's cell wall.
Some antibiotics function as lysins, destroying harmful microbes by causing cell lysis.
The immune system produces lysins to target and break down foreign cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system that can neutralize pathogens
  • synonym: lysozyme - an enzyme that catalyzes the destruction of cell walls
