intercolumn

noun
space between columns
8.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈkɑː.ləm/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈkɒl.əm/ (BrE)
the space or distance between two adjacent columns, especially in architecture, typography, or page layout

Examples

Magazine intercolumn
The designer adjusted the intercolumn to improve the readability of the magazine layout.
In classical architecture, the intercolumn often determines the overall rhythm of the colonnade.
Wider intercolumn spaces can make a newspaper page look less crowded and more inviting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gutter - the space between columns of text on a page
  • antonym: margin - the blank space around the edges of a page
