take someone's side

verb
support someone in an argument
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz saɪd / (AmE), / teɪk ˈsʌmwʌnz saɪd / (BrE)
to support or defend someone in a dispute, argument, or conflict, especially when others disagree

Examples

To take someone's side in argument
Despite the evidence, the teacher took Tom's side in the dispute.
She refused to take my side during the argument, choosing to stay neutral instead.
Whenever there is a disagreement, Mark always takes his sister's side.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oppose - to act against or disagree with someone
  • synonym: support - to show approval of or agreement with someone
