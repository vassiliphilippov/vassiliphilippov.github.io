In large quantities or amounts; abundantly.

Examples

To write copiously
Sweat poured copiously from their brows as they worked under the hot sun.
The author wrote copiously during his years in exile, producing dozens of books and essays.
The trees were copiously covered with blossoms in the spring.

Synonyms

  • abundantly
    3.5 2.68/m
    abundantly describes something present in large, more-than-enough amounts in everyday speech, while copiously sounds slightly more formal and bookish.
  • plentifully
    2.5 0.15/m
    plentifully means there is a large supply people can use, while copiously often highlights great amounts recorded or written.
  • plenteously
    6.3 0.00/m
    plenteously suggests a rich, generous supply like full fields or tables, while copiously is neutral abundance used in many contexts.
  • profusely
    4.8 1.88/m
    profusely often shows excess or strong emotion like bleeding or apologizing, while copiously stays neutral about amount.
  • bountifully
    4.6 0.08/m
    bountifully gives a sense of generous giving or gifts, while copiously simply points to large amounts without a giver.
  • voluminously
    7.5 0.09/m
    voluminously usually refers to large physical or written volume, while copiously means abundant amount in many situations.
