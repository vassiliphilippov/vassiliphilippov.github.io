amphimacer

noun
metrical foot in poetry
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæmfɪˌmeɪsər / (AmE), / ˈæmfɪˌmeɪsə / (BrE)
a metrical foot in poetry consisting of a stressed syllable, followed by an unstressed syllable, and then another stressed syllable

Examples

An amphimacer foot
In classical poetry, an amphimacer creates a strong rhythmic pattern within a line.
The poet used an amphimacer to emphasize the central word in the verse.
Words like 'attitude' can serve as an amphimacer in English poetry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amphibrach - a metrical foot with an unstressed, stressed, and unstressed syllable
  • antonym: dactyl - a metrical foot with one stressed syllable followed by two unstressed syllables
