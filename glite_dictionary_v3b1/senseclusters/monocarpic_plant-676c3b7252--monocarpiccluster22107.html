monocarpic plant

noun
plant that fruits once then dies
8.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkɑːrpɪk plænt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkɑːpɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that flowers and produces fruit only once before dying.

Examples

A monocarpic plant flowers once
Bamboo is considered a monocarpic plant because it flowers once and then perishes.
Some gardeners avoid planting monocarpic plants since they will not flower or fruit again after their first season.
The agave is a well-known monocarpic plant that dies after its single, spectacular flowering.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polycarpic plant - a plant that fruits repeatedly during its lifespan
