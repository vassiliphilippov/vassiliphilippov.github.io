low-down

adjective
of jazz, soulful and bluesy
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ ˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌdaʊn / (BrE)
(of jazz) having a raw, soulful quality reminiscent of early blues music.

Examples

Low-down jazz sound
Her saxophone solo had a low-down edge that gave the song real emotion.
Some musicians seek that low-down feeling to capture the spirit of early blues.
The band played a low-down tune that got everyone on their feet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: funky - having a strong dance rhythm and bluesy feeling (esp. in jazz)
  • antonym: smooth - jazz with a calm, soft, and polished sound
