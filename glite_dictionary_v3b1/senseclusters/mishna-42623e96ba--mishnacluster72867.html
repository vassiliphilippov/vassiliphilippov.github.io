Mishna

noun
first part of the Jewish Talmud
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪʃnə / (AmE), / ˈmɪʃnə / (BrE)
the first part of the Talmud, consisting of a compilation of early oral Jewish traditions and interpretations of the scriptures, compiled around AD 200

Examples

The Mishna text
In class, we analyzed a passage from the Mishna to understand its historical context.
Many Jewish scholars devote years to the study of the Mishna and its commentaries.
The Mishna serves as the foundation of rabbinic law within Judaism.
