cut away

verb
switch to another scene (film/TV)
4.8 5.5
Pronunciation: / kʌt əˈweɪ / (AmE), / kʌt əˈweɪ / (BrE)
(of a film or television director or editor) to switch rapidly to another scene or angle during filming or broadcasting.

Examples

To cut away to another scene
At the crucial moment, the camera cut away from the speaker to show reaction shots.
The program will often cut away to the audience during a performance.
When the dialogue grew tense, the director signaled to cut away to a wide shot.
