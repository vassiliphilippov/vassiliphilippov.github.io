urgent

adjective
requiring immediate action
2.9 17/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…úrd í…ônt / (AmE), / Àà…úÀêd í…ônt / (BrE)
requiring immediate attention or action; very important and needing to be dealt with at once.

Examples

An urgent problem
It's urgent that we repair the leak in the roof before the next rainstorm.
She left him an urgent message asking for help as soon as possible.
The hospital is admitting only urgent cases during the emergency.

Synonyms

  • pressing
    4.1 0.00/m
    pressing describes a problem needing quick attention right now, while urgent means needing immediate action and care.
  • imperative
    5.6 6/m
    imperative expresses a strong need or order that must be followed, while urgent refers to something needing immediate attention.
  • exigent
    9.6 0.2/m
    exigent describes a very serious need that demands action at once in formal contexts, while urgent is the everyday word for immediate attention.
