Syringa vulgaris

noun
common lilac species; ornamental shrub
Pronunciation: / sɪˈrɪŋɡə vʌlˈɡɛrɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈrɪŋɡə vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of lilac, native to Europe, with heart-shaped leaves and large clusters of fragrant purple or white flowers, widely cultivated as an ornamental shrub.

Examples

Syringa vulgaris flower clusters
Botanists often recommend planting Syringa vulgaris for its beautiful and fragrant flower clusters.
The Syringa vulgaris in our garden bursts into bloom every spring, filling the air with its sweet scent.
We trimmed the old Syringa vulgaris to encourage more blossoms next season.
