executive decision

noun
decision by person in authority
1.5
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a decision made by someone with authority, especially at a high level in an organization or government, often without consulting others

Examples

An executive decision made
After much debate, an executive decision was taken to terminate the project.
As CEO, she made an executive decision to expand the company overseas.
Sometimes a quick executive decision is necessary to keep a business running smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decision - the act of making a choice or judgment
