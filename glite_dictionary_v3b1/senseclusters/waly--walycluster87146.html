waly

interjection
expression of grief
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪli / (AmE), / ˈwɑːli / (BrE)
an exclamation used to express grief, pity, or lament, especially in archaic or dialectal English

Examples

Waly! such sorrow
Hearing the sad news, she cried out waly in a trembling voice.
In old ballads, the word waly often appears to express deep sorrow.
The villagers gathered, and a chorus of waly echoed through the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alas - an exclamation expressing sorrow or regret
  • antonym: hooray - an exclamation expressing joy or approval
