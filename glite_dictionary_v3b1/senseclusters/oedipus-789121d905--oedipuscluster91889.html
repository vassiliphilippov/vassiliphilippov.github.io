Oedipus

proper noun
mythological king of Thebes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdɪpəs, ˈidɪpəs / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪpəs / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) a tragic king of Thebes who, unknowingly, killed his father and married his mother; the subject of Sophocles' tragedy 'Oedipus Rex'.

Examples

Oedipus Rex tragedy
Freud named the psychological concept after the myth of Oedipus.
Sophocles' play about Oedipus remains a classic of ancient Greek theatre.
The tragedy of Oedipus has inspired countless works of literature and psychology.
