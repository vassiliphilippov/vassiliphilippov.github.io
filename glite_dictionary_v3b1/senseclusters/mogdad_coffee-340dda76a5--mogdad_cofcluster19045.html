mogdad coffee

noun
a tropical shrub used as coffee adulterant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑɡdæd ˈkɑfi / (AmE), / ˈmɒɡdad ˈkɒfi / (BrE)
a tropical shrub (Cassia occidentalis) with malodorous leaves, whose seeds are sometimes used as an adulterant for coffee

Examples

mogdad coffee seeds
Despite its use, mogdad coffee is considered inferior due to its unpleasant odor and taste.
Some unscrupulous dealers mix mogdad coffee seeds into real coffee to increase their profits.
The seeds of mogdad coffee are sometimes roasted and ground as a coffee substitute in certain regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: senna - another name for shrubs in the genus Cassia, sometimes used for related plants
