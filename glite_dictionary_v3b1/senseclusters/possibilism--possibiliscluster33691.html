possibilism

noun
gradual political reform
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːsəbɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəbɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a political approach that favors gradual, practical reforms within existing systems rather than seeking radical or revolutionary change

Examples

Political possibilism
The party's shift toward possibilism led to a series of moderate policy changes.
Many leaders embraced possibilism to achieve progress without provoking strong opposition.
Debates between advocates of possibilism and those demanding revolution shaped the movement's direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reformism - support for gradual change through reforms
