Judith

noun
apocryphal book about Judith saving her people
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːdɪθ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdɪθ / (BrE)
A book of the Apocrypha that tells the story of Judith, who saves her people from the Assyrians through courage and strategy.

Examples

The Book of Judith
In Judith, the heroine outwits the Assyrian general Holofernes to save her city.
Many Bible editions include the Book of Judith among the Apocryphal books.
The story of Judith is often cited as an example of bravery in biblical literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Apocrypha - biblical or related writings not forming part of the accepted canon of Scripture
