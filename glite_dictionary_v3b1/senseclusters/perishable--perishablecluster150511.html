perishable

adjective
liable to decay or spoil
2.8 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈper.ɪ.ʃə.bəl/ /; / /ˈpɜːr.ɪ.ʃə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈpe.rɪ.ʃə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of an object or substance) likely to decay, deteriorate, or spoil, especially in a short time.

Examples

Perishable goods
Flowers are highly perishable and should be delivered quickly to retain their freshness.
Fruit and vegetables are perishable goods that need to be stored carefully.
You should keep all perishable food items in the refrigerator to prevent spoilage.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: durable - able to withstand wear, pressure, or damage; lasting
  • antonym: imperishable - not subject to decay, spoilage, or destruction
