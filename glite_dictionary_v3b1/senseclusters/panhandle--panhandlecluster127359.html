panhandle

noun
narrow projecting strip of land
Pronunciation: / ˈpænˌhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpænˌhændl̩ / (BrE)
a narrow strip of territory projecting from the main part of a larger area, often likened to the handle of a pan

Examples

Texas panhandle
Alaska's southeastern panhandle includes many islands and coastal towns.
Oklahoma's panhandle is known for its wide-open spaces and flat terrain.
The Florida panhandle suffered heavy damage during the hurricane.
