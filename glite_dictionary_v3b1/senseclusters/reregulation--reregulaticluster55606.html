reregulation

noun
restoring regulations
7.6
Pronunciation: /riː.re.ɡjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.re.ɡjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.re.ɡjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.re.ɡjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of introducing or restoring government rules or controls after a period of deregulation

Examples

Government reregulation measures
After years of financial crises, the government implemented reregulation to prevent future instability.
Many experts debated whether reregulation would benefit or harm the telecommunications industry.
The process of reregulation often involves restoring rules that were previously removed.
After several financial crises, the government implemented reregulation to increase oversight of the banking sector.
The airline industry faced new reregulation measures to improve passenger safety standards.
Debates about reregulation often arise when existing rules are seen as too relaxed.
