caregiver

noun
person who supports the ill or disabled
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkeə.gɪ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈkeə.gɪ.və/ (BrE)
a person who looks after or helps someone who is ill, disabled, elderly, or a child and needs support with daily activities

Examples

A caregiver helps
After her surgery, Maria hired a caregiver to assist with daily tasks at home.
Being a caregiver for a family member requires a lot of patience and dedication.
Many elderly people rely on a caregiver for companionship and medical support.
After her father's illness, she became his primary caregiver.
Many caregivers experience emotional stress from their responsibilities.
The hospital provides support for family caregivers of patients with dementia.
