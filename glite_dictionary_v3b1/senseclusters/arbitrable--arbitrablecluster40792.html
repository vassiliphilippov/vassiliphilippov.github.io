arbitrable

adjective
able to be settled by arbitration
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbɪtrəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of a dispute or matter) able to be resolved by arbitration rather than through the courts.

Examples

An arbitrable dispute
All arbitrable issues must be resolved outside of court, according to the company's policy.
The contract stated that only arbitrable matters should be referred to the arbitration panel.
The lawyers argued whether the dispute was arbitrable under the existing agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonarbitrable - not able to be settled by arbitration
