ecclesiarchy

noun
church-based government
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkliːziˌɑːrki / (AmE), / ɪˈkliːziːˌɑːki / (BrE)
a system of government in which the church or religious leaders hold ruling power

Examples

Ecclesiarchy rules the country
The medieval kingdom was ruled by an ecclesiarchy rather than a monarchy.
Under the ecclesiarchy, church officials made all major political decisions.
Some historians debate the effectiveness of an ecclesiarchy in maintaining social order.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monarchy - a government ruled by a king or queen
  • synonym: theocracy - a government ruled by religious authorities
