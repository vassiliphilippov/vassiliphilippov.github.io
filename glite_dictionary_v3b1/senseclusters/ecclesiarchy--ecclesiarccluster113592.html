ecclesiarchy

noun
church-based government
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.kliː.zi.ˈɑːr.ki/ /; / /ə.kliː.zi.ˈɑːr.ki/ (AmE), /ɪ.kliː.zi.ˈɑː.ki/ /; / /ə.kliː.zi.ˈɑː.ki/ (BrE)

Examples

Ecclesiarchy rules the country
The medieval kingdom was ruled by an ecclesiarchy rather than a monarchy.
Under the ecclesiarchy, church officials made all major political decisions.
Some historians debate the effectiveness of an ecclesiarchy in maintaining social order.
