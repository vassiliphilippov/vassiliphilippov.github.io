carnivorous bat

noun
bat species feeding mainly on insects
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈnɪvərəs bæt / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɪvərəs bæt / (BrE)
a type of bat characterized by feeding primarily on insects, often with large ears, and found worldwide

Examples

Carnivorous bat species
Many caves are home to several species of carnivorous bat.
The carnivorous bat uses its sharp senses to hunt insects in the dark.
Unlike fruit bats, a carnivorous bat relies on echolocation to find its prey.
