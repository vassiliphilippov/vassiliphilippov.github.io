descension

noun
decline or deterioration
8.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnʃən / (BrE)
a decline or fall in condition, status, or quality, often involving deterioration or moral decay

Examples

Descension into poverty
The novel traces the descension of a once-great family into poverty and obscurity.
Many historians have studied the descension of ancient civilizations after periods of glory.
His gradual descension into bad habits worried his friends and family.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ascent - the act of rising or improving in status or condition
  • synonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss of strength, numbers, or quality
  • synonym: deterioration - the process of becoming worse
