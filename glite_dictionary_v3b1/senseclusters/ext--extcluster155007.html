ext

noun
script abbreviation
Pronunciation: / ɛkst / (AmE), / ɛkst / (BrE)
an abbreviation used in film and television scripts to indicate that a scene takes place outdoors (exterior)

Examples

Ext scene heading
The script began with ext to show the action was set outside the building.
Directors look for ext in the script to plan outdoor filming locations.
Each scene heading starts with ext or 'int' to indicate where it takes place.

Related Terms

  • antonym: int - an abbreviation for 'interior' in scripts, indicating an indoor scene
