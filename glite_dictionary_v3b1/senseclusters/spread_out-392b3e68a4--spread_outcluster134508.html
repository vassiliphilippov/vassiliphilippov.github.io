spread out

verb
move apart from each other
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /spred aʊt/ /; / /sprɪd aʊt/ (AmE), /spred aʊt/ /; / /spriːd aʊt/ (BrE)
(of people or things) to move apart from each other over a wider area; to disperse.

Examples

People spread out in a field

Make sure everyone spreads out so you don't block the entrance.
The search team spread out across the field to look for clues.
