tonic

noun
medicine for improving health or energy
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
A medicinal substance intended to restore or increase a person's strength, energy, or health.

Examples

A health tonic
Historically, doctors often recommended a tonic for fatigue and general weakness.
Many people believe that a morning tonic helps boost their energy for the day.
The old herbalist prescribed a tonic to help improve his patient's strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
