nonobservable

adjective
not directly detectable
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnəbˈzɝːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnəbˈzɜːvəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be observed or detected directly by the senses or instruments

Examples

Nonobservable effects
Some quantum properties are nonobservable with current technology.
The effects of the medication may be nonobservable in the early stages of treatment.
Certain environmental changes are nonobservable without specialized equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invisible - not able to be seen
  • antonym: observable - able to be seen or noticed
  • synonym: undetectable - impossible to discover or notice
