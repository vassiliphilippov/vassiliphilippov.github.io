Riemannian geometry

noun
geometry of curved spaces
9.9
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈmæ.ni.ən dʒiː.ˈɑː.mə.tri/ /; / /riː.ˈmɑː.ni.ən dʒiː.ˈɑː.mə.tri/ (AmE), /riː.ˈmæ.ni.ən dʒiː.ˈɒm.ɪ.tri/ /; / /riː.ˈmɑː.ni.ən ˈdʒɒm.ɪ.tri/ (BrE)
A branch of non-Euclidean geometry in which space is considered to be curved, most commonly like the surface of a sphere, and straight lines are analogous to great circles.

Examples

Riemannian geometry branch
Einstein's general theory of relativity is based on the principles of Riemannian geometry.
He spent years studying Riemannian geometry and its role in modern physics.
The curved surface of a sphere provides a simple example of Riemannian geometry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Euclidean geometry - geometry dealing with flat, two- or three-dimensional spaces
  • synonym: elliptic geometry - type of non-Euclidean geometry where space is positively curved
