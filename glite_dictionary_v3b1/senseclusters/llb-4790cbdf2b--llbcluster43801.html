LLB

noun
Bachelor of Laws degree
Pronunciation: / ɛl ɛl bi / (AmE), / ɛl ɛl biː / (BrE)
A university degree in law, typically studied over three years, often required for legal practice in many countries.

Examples

An LLB degree
After completing her LLB, she applied for a position at a leading law firm.
He proudly displayed his LLB certificate on the office wall.
In some countries, an LLB is required before taking the bar exam.
