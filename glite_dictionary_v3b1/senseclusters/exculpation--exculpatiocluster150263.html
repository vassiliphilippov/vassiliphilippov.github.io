exculpation

noun
the act of clearing from blame
9.3 8.1
Pronunciation: /ɪks.kəl.ˈpeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪks.kəl.ˈpeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of clearing someone from blame or proving their innocence

Examples

Complete exculpation
After months of investigation, the official report resulted in his complete exculpation.

Public exculpation is sometimes necessary when a celebrity is accused of misconduct.
The manager's repeated exculpation of her team's mistakes was not well received by the board.
