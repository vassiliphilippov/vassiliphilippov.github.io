insidiousness

noun
subtle or gradual harmfulness
7.3 7.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈsɪd.ɪ.ə.snəs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈsɪd.ɪ.ə.snəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being harmful in a gradual, hidden, or sneaky way, often without being noticed at first

Examples

The insidiousness of disease
Doctors warned of the insidiousness of symptoms that develop without clear warning signs.
She underestimated the insidiousness of the new policy's impact on small businesses.
The insidiousness of the disease made it difficult to detect in its early stages.
Many were alarmed by the insidiousness with which the disease spread through the community.
She underestimated the insidiousness of his schemes and was caught off guard.
The insidiousness of the plan made it difficult to detect until it was too late.
