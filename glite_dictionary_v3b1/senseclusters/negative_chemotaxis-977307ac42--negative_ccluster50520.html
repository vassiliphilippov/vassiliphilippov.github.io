negative chemotaxis

noun
movement away from a chemical stimulus
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˌkiːmoʊˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv ˌkiːməʊˈtæksɪs / (BrE)
movement of an organism or cell away from a chemical stimulus

Examples

Negative chemotaxis movement
Certain bacteria exhibit negative chemotaxis by moving away from harmful chemicals in their environment.
Scientists observed negative chemotaxis when the cells withdrew from the repellent substance.
The process of negative chemotaxis is essential for understanding how immune cells avoid toxic compounds.
