calotte

noun
small close-fitting cap
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɑt / (AmE), / kəˈlɒt / (BrE)
a small, round, close-fitting cap or skullcap, often worn by clergy or as part of academic dress

Examples

Clergy calotte
The bishop adjusted his calotte before entering the cathedral.
At the graduation ceremony, each student wore a traditional calotte as part of their academic dress.
The museum displayed a 19th-century calotte once worn by a famous scholar.
The priest wore a black calotte during the church service.
Graduates in some universities receive a calotte as part of their academic regalia.
He admired the intricate embroidery on the bishop's calotte.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skullcap - a small, rounded cap fitting closely to the head
  • synonym: zucchetto - a small skullcap worn by Roman Catholic clergy
