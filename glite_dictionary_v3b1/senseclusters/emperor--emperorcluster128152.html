emperor

noun
male ruler of an empire
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmpəɹɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛmpərə / (BrE)
the male ruler of an empire, typically the sovereign head of a vast realm comprising several nations or territories

Examples

Roman emperor
After ascending the throne, the new emperor initiated sweeping reforms.
Historians debate the legacy of every major emperor in world history.
The Roman emperor governed a vast and multicultural empire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empress - female ruler of an empire or wife of an emperor
  • synonym: king - male monarch of a kingdom
  • antonym: president - elected head of a republic, not royalty
