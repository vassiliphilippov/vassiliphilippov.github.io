Ruth

proper noun
book of the Old Testament about Ruth and Naomi
Pronunciation: / ruːθ / (AmE), / ruːθ / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament that tells the story of Ruth, a Moabite woman who remained with her Israelite mother-in-law Naomi after her husband's death.

Examples

Book of Ruth
He delivered a sermon based on the story from Ruth about loyalty and faith.
Many scholars have analyzed the narrative structure of Ruth in the Old Testament.
The Book of Ruth is often read during the Jewish festival of Shavuot.
