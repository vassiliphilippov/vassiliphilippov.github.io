dilatation

noun
widening of an opening, especially in the body
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪləˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪləˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of becoming wider or more open, especially in a bodily structure (medical or scientific use)

Examples

Blood vessel dilatation
Certain medications can cause the dilatation of blood vessels, lowering blood pressure.
During labor, dilatation of the cervix is closely monitored by medical staff.
The doctor measured the dilatation of the pupil in response to the light.
Abnormal dilatation of the blood vessels may be a sign of an underlying condition.
Regular exercise can help prevent the dilatation of veins that leads to varicose veins.
The doctor noted a marked dilatation of the pupil during the examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constriction - the act of making something narrower or tighter
  • synonym: dilation - the act of becoming wider or more open, especially of a bodily passage or opening
  • synonym: distension - the act of swelling or stretching out
