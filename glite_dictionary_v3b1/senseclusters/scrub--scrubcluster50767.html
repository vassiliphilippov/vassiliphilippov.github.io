scrub

verb
clean thoroughly by rubbing
1.0 2.71/m
Pronunciation: / skrÊŒb / (AmE), / skrÊŒb / (BrE)
to clean something by rubbing it hard, usually with a brush, cloth, or sponge and water or cleaning agent

Examples

To scrub the floor
She used a stiff brush to scrub the kitchen floor until it was spotless.
The doctor must scrub his hands before surgery to prevent infection.
We had to scrub the table to remove the sticky syrup left from breakfast.
He scrubbed his hands thoroughly before surgery.
I had to scrub the kitchen floor after the party.
She spent an hour scrubbing the stains from her shirt.

Synonyms

    4.8 1.30/m

  • synonym: wash - to clean with water and, typically, soap or detergent
