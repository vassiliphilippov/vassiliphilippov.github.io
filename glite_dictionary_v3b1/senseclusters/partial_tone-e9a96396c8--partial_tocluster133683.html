partial tone

noun
harmonic overtone of a musical note
7.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ër É…ôl to än / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀê É…ôl t…ô än / (BrE)
A musical tone that has a frequency which is a whole-number multiple of the fundamental frequency; a harmonic.

Examples

A partial tone frequency
A flute produces a clear partial tone when the player overblows the instrument.
Each partial tone adds complexity to the original note, creating unique timbres.
The richness of a sound depends on the number of partial tones it contains.

Synonyms

  • harmonic
    6.7 0.21/m
    harmonic is the strict technical name for a tone at a whole-number multiple of a fundamental, while partial tone names that component in acoustics.
  • partial
    8.6 0.03/m
    partial refers to a sound component in music with a multiple frequency, while partial tone is the full phrase for that specific harmonic.
  • overtone
    7.4 0.36/m
    overtone is a higher tone heard above the main note and can be any higher frequency, while partial tone requires a whole-number multiple.
