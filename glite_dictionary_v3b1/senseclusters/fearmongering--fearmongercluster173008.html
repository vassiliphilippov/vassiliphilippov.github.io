fearmongering

adjective
spreading fear deliberately
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪrˌmʌŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪəˌmʌŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
deliberately intended to make people afraid in order to influence their opinions or behavior

Examples

Fearmongering speech
The politician's fearmongering statements made the public anxious about the future.
Many criticized the news outlet for its fearmongering coverage of the health crisis.
Social media can sometimes amplify fearmongering messages during uncertain times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarmist - causing unnecessary fear or worry
  • antonym: reassuring - making people feel less worried or afraid
