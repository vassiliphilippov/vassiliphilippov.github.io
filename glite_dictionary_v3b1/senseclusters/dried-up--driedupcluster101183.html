Examples

A dried-up riverbed

Synonyms

  • desiccated
    7.2 0.02/m
    desiccated means completely dried out for storage or science, while dried-up is the everyday word for lacking moisture.
  • withered
    4.6 0.08/m
    withered looks dry, limp, and dead on plants, while dried-up can describe many objects or surfaces missing water.
  • shriveled
    5.2 0.03/m
    shriveled looks wrinkled and smaller from loss of juice, while dried-up is a general word for things without moisture.
  • parched
    4.7 1.15/m
    parched is extremely dry from heat or sun, whereas dried-up is a neutral term for anything that has lost moisture.
  • sere
    9.9 0.12/m
    sere describes dry, pale plants in old or literary writing, while dried-up is the everyday phrase speakers commonly use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: moist - slightly wet; not dry
  • synonym: parched - extremely dry, especially due to lack of rain or moisture
  • synonym: withered - dried up, shriveled, or faded (plants or parts of plants)
