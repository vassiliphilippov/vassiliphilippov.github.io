drool

verb
let saliva flow from the mouth
2.1 1.54/m
Pronunciation: / druÀêl / (AmE), / druÀêl / (BrE)
to let saliva flow from the mouth, usually unintentionally

Examples

To drool in sleep
She tried not to drool during her nap on the couch.
The baby started to drool while he was teething.
The dog would drool at the sight of food.

Synonyms

  • slaver
    9.8 0.18/m
    slaver means letting saliva run or dribble from the mouth in a steady way, while drool is usually unintentional saliva flow.
  • dribble
    2.8 1.32/m
    dribble means letting liquid fall in small drops from the mouth, while drool is the general unintentional flow of saliva.
  • slobber
    4.8 0.88/m
    slobber means letting saliva run messily and often noticeably, while drool is usually a small, unintentional saliva flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dribble - to let saliva or liquid flow in small drops
