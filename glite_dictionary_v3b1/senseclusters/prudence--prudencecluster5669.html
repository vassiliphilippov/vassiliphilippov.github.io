prudence

noun
careful good judgment in practical matters
Pronunciation: / ˈpru.dəns /; / ˈpruːdəns / (AmE), / ˈpruː.dəns /; / ˈpruːdns / (BrE)
the quality of being careful and sensible; showing good judgment, especially in practical matters or when avoiding risks

Examples

Financial prudence
His financial prudence allowed him to save enough money for retirement.
The success of the company was largely due to the prudence of its management team.
You should always exercise prudence when handling sensitive information at work.
In matters of finance, prudence is often more valuable than boldness.
She handled the sensitive situation with remarkable prudence and tact.
The company praised his prudence in negotiating the deal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discretion - the quality of being careful about what you do and say
  • antonym: imprudence - the state of being unwise or lacking caution
  • synonym: wisdom - the ability to make sound decisions based on knowledge and experience
