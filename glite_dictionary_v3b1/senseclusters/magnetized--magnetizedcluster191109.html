magnetized

adjective
having magnetic properties
Pronunciation: / ˈmægnəˌtaɪzd /; / ˈmæɡ.nə.taɪzd / (AmE), / ˈmægnɪtaɪzd /; / ˈmæɡ.nɪ.taɪzd / (BrE)
Having acquired the properties of a magnet, such as the ability to attract iron or steel.

Examples

A magnetized rod
After passing through the coil, the rod became strongly magnetized.
All credit cards contain a magnetized strip that stores banking information.
The magnetized nail easily picked up small metal filings from the table.
All the screws had to be magnetised so they would stick to the screwdriver.
Some toys contain magnetised parts that let them connect together.
The compass needle became magnetised after being near the speaker for too long.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demagnetized - having lost magnetic properties
  • synonym: magnetic - having the properties of a magnet; capable of attracting iron
