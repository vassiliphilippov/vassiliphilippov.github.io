retrack

verb
to retrace route
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtræk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtræk / (BrE)
to go back along the same route or path that was previously taken

Examples

To retrack your steps
After realizing they were lost, the group decided to retrack their path to the last landmark.
The dog managed to retrack its way home after wandering off in the park.
We had to retrack our steps when we noticed we had dropped the map.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backtrack - to return along a route you have just taken
  • synonym: retrace - to go back over a path or route again
