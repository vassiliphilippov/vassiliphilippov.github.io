Whitweek

noun
week after Whitsunday (Christian holiday)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtˌwiːk / (AmE), / ˈwɪtwiːk / (BrE)
The week beginning on Whitsunday, observed especially in Christian tradition, often focusing on the first three days after Whitsunday.

Examples

The Whitweek festival
Many English villages once held special processions and fairs during Whitweek.
She always looked forward to Whitweek celebrations after Whitsunday.
The first three days of Whitweek were marked by religious observances and community gatherings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pentecost - the Christian festival celebrating the descent of the Holy Spirit, held on the seventh Sunday after Easter
  • synonym: Whitsun - the seventh Sunday after Easter, also called Pentecost
