Roth IRA

noun
US tax-free retirement account
5.1
Pronunciation: /rɔːθ ˈaɪ.rə/ /; / /rɔːθ aɪ.ɑːr.ˈeɪ/ (AmE), /rɒθ ˈaɪ.rə/ /; / /rɒθ aɪ.ɑː.ˈeɪ/ (BrE)
a type of retirement savings account in the US where contributions are made with after-tax income and withdrawals are tax-free

Examples

A Roth IRA account
Many young professionals open a Roth IRA to take advantage of tax-free growth for their retirement savings.
You can contribute to a Roth IRA as long as you have earned income and meet the income limits.
Withdrawals from a Roth IRA in retirement are not subject to federal income tax.

Related Terms

  • synonym: 401(k) - a US employer-sponsored retirement savings plan
  • antonym: traditional IRA - a US retirement account with tax-deductible contributions and taxable withdrawals
