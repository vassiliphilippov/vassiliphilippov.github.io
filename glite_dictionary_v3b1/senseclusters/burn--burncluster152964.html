burn

verb
execute by setting alight at the stake
Pronunciation: / bɝn / (AmE), / bɜːn / (BrE)
to execute (someone) by tying them to a stake and setting them alight, especially as a form of capital punishment in the past

Examples

To burn at the stake
During the Inquisition, heretics were burned at the stake as punishment.
Several martyrs were burned for their religious beliefs during the Middle Ages.
Stories tell of accused witches being burned alive in early modern Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immolate - to kill as a sacrifice, especially by fire
