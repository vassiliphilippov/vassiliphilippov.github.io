dermoskeleton

noun
skin-based skeleton
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɜːrmoʊˈskɛlɪtən / (AmE), / ˌdɜːməʊˈskɛlɪtən / (BrE)
a hard, protective outer covering of some animals, such as bony plates, scales, or shells, that supports and protects the body

Examples

The dermoskeleton structure
The ancient fish's dermoskeleton was made up of thick, overlapping bony plates.
Scientists believe the dermoskeleton helped protect early vertebrates from predators.
Unlike mammals, some reptiles have a partial dermoskeleton beneath their scales.
The turtle's dermoskeleton protects it from predators in the wild.
Fish scales are a type of dermoskeleton that help shield their bodies.
Scientists studied the dermoskeleton of armadillos to understand its structure and function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endoskeleton - an internal skeleton, such as the bones in humans and other vertebrates
  • synonym: exoskeleton - an external skeleton that supports and protects an animal's body
