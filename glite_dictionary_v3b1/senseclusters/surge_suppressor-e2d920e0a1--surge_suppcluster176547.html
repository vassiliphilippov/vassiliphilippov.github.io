surge suppressor

noun
device protecting against voltage surges
5.0 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / s…ùd í s…ôÀàpr…õs…ö / (AmE), / s…úÀêd í s…ôÀàpr…õs…ô / (BrE)
A device connected to an electrical power line to protect equipment from sudden increases in voltage or current.

Examples

A surge suppressor device
Make sure to plug your computer into a surge suppressor to prevent damage during storms.
She replaced the old surge suppressor to better protect her sensitive electronics.
The surge suppressor tripped when there was a power spike, saving the television.

Synonyms

  • surge protector
    3.2 0.10/m
    surge protector is the common everyday name for devices that stop sudden voltage spikes, while surge suppressor is a technical term for the same device.
  • spike suppressor
    6.3 0.01/m
    spike suppressor protects against very short current or voltage bursts at outlets, while surge suppressor covers both short spikes and longer surges.
  • spike arrester
    7.2 0.00/m
    spike arrester sits on power lines and diverts sudden high-voltage spikes to ground, while surge suppressor protects equipment at outlets.
  • arrestor
    7.5 0.01/m
    arrestor diverts or limits very high voltage to ground in industrial systems, while surge suppressor is a general device for protecting electronics.
