subgenre

noun
category within genre
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌʒɑnrə / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌʒɒnrə / (BrE)
a smaller or more specific category within a larger genre, especially in art, literature, music, or entertainment

Examples

A subgenre of music
Horror is a broad genre that includes the subgenre of psychological thrillers.
Jazz fusion is a subgenre that blends jazz with other musical styles.
The detective novel is a popular subgenre of crime fiction.
Science fiction has many subgenres, such as cyberpunk and space opera.
The festival features films from every subgenre of horror.
He prefers the romantic comedy subgenre over traditional romance movies.
