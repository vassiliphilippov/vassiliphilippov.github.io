waterway

noun
navigable body of water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌweɪ /; / ˈwɔt̬ərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəweɪ /; / ˈwɔːtəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a river, canal, or channel that boats or ships can travel through, especially for transportation

Examples

A busy waterway
Barges and cargo ships frequently travel along this busy waterway.
The Panama Canal is an important waterway connecting the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
The city developed rapidly because of its location on a major waterway.
Cargo ships use the main waterway to transport goods between the two ports.
The city is famous for its extensive network of waterways and picturesque bridges.
They spent the afternoon kayaking along a peaceful waterway surrounded by trees.
