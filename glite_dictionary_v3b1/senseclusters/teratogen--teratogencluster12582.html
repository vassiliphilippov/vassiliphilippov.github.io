teratogen

noun
agent causing birth defects
6.2 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛrəˌtoʊdʒən / (AmE), / təˈratədʒən / (BrE)
an agent or factor that causes malformation or abnormal development in an embryo or fetus

Examples

Teratogen causes defects
Alcohol is a known teratogen that can lead to developmental disorders.
Certain prescription drugs can act as teratogens and should be avoided during pregnancy.
Research focuses on identifying environmental teratogens to reduce birth defects.
