un-

x
negative prefix
Pronunciation: / ʌn / (AmE), / ʌn / (BrE)
a prefix added to words to mean 'not' or 'the opposite of'

Examples

Un-happy child
Adding un- to 'happy' creates the word 'unhappy', meaning 'not happy'.
The prefix un- is used in words like 'unfair' and 'unknown' to show the opposite meaning.
If you put un- before 'certain', you get 'uncertain', which means 'not sure'.
