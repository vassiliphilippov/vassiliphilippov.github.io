chalk and cheese

noun
two things that are completely different
Pronunciation: / tʃɑk ənd tʃiz / (AmE), / tʃɔːk ənd tʃiːz / (BrE)
used to refer to two people or things that are completely different or incompatible with each other

Examples

Chalk and cheese difference
The two proposals are chalk and cheese, making comparison difficult.
Their personalities are chalk and cheese; they rarely agree on anything.
While the brothers are twins, their interests are chalk and cheese.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alike - similar in nature or appearance
  • synonym: oil and water - two things that do not mix or get along
