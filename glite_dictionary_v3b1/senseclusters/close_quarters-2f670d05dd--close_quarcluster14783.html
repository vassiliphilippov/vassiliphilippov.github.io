close quarters

noun
being in tight, confined space
5.4 0.84/m
Pronunciation: / klo äs Ààkw…îrt…öz / (AmE), / kl…ô äs Ààkw…îÀêt…ôz / (BrE)
A situation in which people or things are uncomfortably near to each other, often with limited space.

Examples

Living in close quarters
Living in such close quarters can make privacy very difficult to maintain.
On the crowded train, commuters found themselves standing in close quarters with strangers.
The soldiers fought at close quarters during the intense battle.

Synonyms

  • closeness
    2.3 0.37/m
    closeness is the simple idea of being near in space, while close quarters describes a cramped situation with little room.
  • proximity
    4.2 11/m
    proximity is a more formal, general word for nearness in space or time, while close quarters describes a cramped situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cramped - having less space than needed, uncomfortable due to lack of room
  • antonym: spacious - having plenty of space; roomy
