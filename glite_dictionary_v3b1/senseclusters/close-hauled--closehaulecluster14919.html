close-hauled

adjective
sailing as close to the wind as possible
5.6 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkloʊs.hɔːld/ /; / /ˈkloʊs.hɑːld/ (AmE), /ˈkləʊs.hɔːld/ (BrE)
(of a sailboat) having the sails set to sail as nearly into the wind as possible.

Examples

A close-hauled sailboat
Sailing close-hauled demands skillful trimming of the sails and constant attention to the wind.
The yacht remained close-hauled for most of the afternoon, barely losing any speed.
With the wind shifting, the captain ordered the boat to sail close-hauled toward the next mark.
