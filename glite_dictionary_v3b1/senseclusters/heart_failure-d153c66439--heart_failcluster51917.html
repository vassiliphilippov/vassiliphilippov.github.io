heart failure

noun
inability of the heart to pump blood effectively
Pronunciation: / hɑrt ˈfeɪljɚ / (AmE), / hɑːt ˈfeɪljə / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the heart is unable to pump enough blood to meet the body's needs.

Examples

Heart failure symptoms
Effective treatment can help people live longer with heart failure.
Long-term high blood pressure can increase the risk of heart failure.
The patient was admitted to the hospital with severe heart failure.
