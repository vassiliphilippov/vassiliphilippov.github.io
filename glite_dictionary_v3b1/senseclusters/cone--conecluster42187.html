cone

noun
retinal cell for color vision
Pronunciation: / koʊn / (AmE), / kəʊn / (BrE)
a type of photoreceptor cell in the retina of the eye that is sensitive to bright light and enables color vision

Examples

Eye cone cell
Damage to the cones in the retina can result in color blindness.
During the day, cones are primarily responsible for our ability to see colors clearly.
The human retina contains three types of cone cells, each sensitive to different wavelengths of light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rod - a type of retinal photoreceptor cell sensitive to low light and peripheral vision
