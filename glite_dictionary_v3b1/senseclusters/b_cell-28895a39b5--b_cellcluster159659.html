B cell

noun
type of antibody-producing white blood cell
Pronunciation: / bi sɛl / (AmE), / biː sɛl / (BrE)
A type of lymphocyte that develops in the bone marrow and produces antibodies to help defend the body against infection.

Examples

B cells produce antibodies
Doctors can measure the number of B cells in your blood to diagnose certain immune disorders.
Vaccines often work by training B cells to recognize and fight future infections.
When exposed to an infection, B cells produce specific antibodies to fight off the invading pathogens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: T cell - type of lymphocyte that matures in the thymus and coordinates cell-mediated immunity
  • degree higher: lymphocyte - a type of white blood cell involved in the immune response
