bollock

noun
nautical pulley-block at top of mast
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑlək / (AmE), / ˈbɒlək / (BrE)
a pulley-block located at the head of a topmast, used in sailing for handling ropes and rigging

Examples

Sailor's bollock
A new bollock was fitted to the topmast after the old one had worn out from years of use.
Repairing a damaged bollock required specialized tools and knowledge of the ship’s rigging system.
The sailor inspected the bollock to ensure the rigging was secure before they raised the sails.
