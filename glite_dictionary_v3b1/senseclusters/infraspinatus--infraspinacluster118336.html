infraspinatus

noun
shoulder muscle
9.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.frə.spɪ.ˈneɪ.təs/ (AmE), /ɪn.frə.spɪ.ˈneɪ.təs/ (BrE)
a muscle located on the back of the shoulder blade that helps rotate the arm outward

Examples

The infraspinatus muscle
The infraspinatus plays a key role in rotating the arm outward during a tennis serve.
Physical therapists often target the infraspinatus when treating shoulder injuries.
An injury to the infraspinatus can cause pain and limit shoulder movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deltoid - the large muscle covering the shoulder joint
  • synonym: supraspinatus - a muscle above the spine of the shoulder blade involved in lifting the arm
