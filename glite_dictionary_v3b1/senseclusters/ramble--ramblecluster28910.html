ramble

verb
talk or write in an aimless way
Pronunciation: / ˈræm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈræm.bəl / (BrE)
to talk or write at length in a confused or aimless way

Examples

To ramble about stories
He tends to ramble when telling stories, often losing his main point.
Her letter rambled for pages without ever making her request clear.
The professor began to ramble after being asked an unrelated question.

Related Terms

  • antonym: focus - to pay particular attention or be concise
  • synonym: meander - speak or write aimlessly or without structure
  • synonym: wander - move or talk without a clear direction or purpose
