valetudinarianism

noun
excessive concern about health
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌvælɪˌtjuːdəˈnɛriənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌvalɪˌtjuːdɪˈnɛːrɪənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the state of being overly concerned with one's health, especially involving a chronic preoccupation with illness or poor health

Examples

Chronic valetudinarianism
His lifelong valetudinarianism kept him indoors and away from most social events.
Some critics saw her attention to diet and medicine as a sign of valetudinarianism.
The novel's main character is afflicted with a sense of valetudinarianism that colors every aspect of his life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypochondria - excessive worry about having a serious illness
