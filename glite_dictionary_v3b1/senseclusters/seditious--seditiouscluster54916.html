seditious

adjective
inciting opposition to authority or government
7.5 8.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈdɪ.ʃəs/ /; / /sɪ.ˈdɪ.ʃəs/ (AmE), /sə.ˈdɪ.ʃəs/ /; / /sɪ.ˈdɪ.ʃəs/ (BrE)
encouraging or supporting people to oppose or rebel against authority or government

Examples

Seditious speech
During times of unrest, seditious activity is often met with harsh punishment.
He was arrested for making seditious speeches that encouraged people to revolt.
The government banned the book, calling its ideas seditious and dangerous.
Authorities feared the seditious pamphlet would spark a revolt among the workers.
The government has banned the publication of seditious materials.
The leader was arrested for making seditious speeches that encouraged an uprising.
