MKO

noun
Iranian political-militant group (Mujahedin-e Khalq Organization)
Pronunciation: / ɛm keɪ oʊ / (AmE), / ɛm keɪ əʊ / (BrE)
abbreviation for the Mujahedin-e Khalq Organization, an Iranian political-militant group founded in the 1960s that has been designated as a terrorist organization by some countries.

Examples

MKO terrorist group
Many countries debated whether to remove the MKO from their lists of terrorist organizations.
The MKO has played a controversial role in Iranian and international politics.
The history of the MKO reflects the shifting dynamics of Iranian opposition movements.
