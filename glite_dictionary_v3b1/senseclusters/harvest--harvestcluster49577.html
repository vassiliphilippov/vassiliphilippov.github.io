harvest

verb
remove organ or tissue for transplantation
5.6 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːr.vəst/ /; / /ˈhɑːr.vɪst/ (AmE), /ˈhɑː.vəst/ /; / /ˈhɑː.vɪst/ (BrE)
to remove an organ, tissue, or cells from a living or dead body, typically for the purpose of transplantation or medical research

Examples

To harvest organs
Organs are typically harvested within hours after a donor's death for transplantation.
Researchers harvest bone marrow to help treat leukemia patients.
The doctors will harvest stem cells from the patient before chemotherapy.
