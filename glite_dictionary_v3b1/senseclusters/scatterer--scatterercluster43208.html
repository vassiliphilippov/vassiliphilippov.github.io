scatterer

noun
object that disperses
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskætərər / (AmE), / ˈskætərə / (BrE)
an object or agent that causes waves, particles, or signals (such as light or sound) to spread out in different directions

Examples

The scatterer object
The scatterer in the laboratory setup redirected the laser beam in multiple directions.
Raindrops can act as a scatterer for sunlight, creating a rainbow effect.
In physics, a scatterer is often used to study the behavior of particles and waves.
The scientist measured how the scatterer changed the direction of the laser beam.
Tiny particles in the atmosphere act as scatterers for sunlight, making the sky appear blue.
Engineers designed a new scatterer to improve the performance of the radar system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorber - an object that takes in energy or matter instead of scattering it
  • synonym: diffuser - a device that spreads out light or sound evenly
  • synonym: disperser - something that spreads things over a wide area
  • antonym: reflector - an object that bounces waves, such as light or sound, back
