interpretatively

adverb
in an explanatory way
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɜːrprəˈteɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪtɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that involves explaining or understanding the meaning of something

Examples

Interpretatively analyze a text
The teacher discussed the poem interpretatively, encouraging students to find deeper meanings.
She responded interpretatively to the ambiguous statement, offering her own perspective.
Historians often write interpretatively about past events to explain their significance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analytically - in a way that involves careful examination or analysis
  • antonym: literally - in a way that uses the exact meaning of words
