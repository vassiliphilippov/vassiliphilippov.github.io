connecting rod

noun
rod transmitting motion between crank and piston
2.4 5.1
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈnek.tɪŋ rɑːd/ (AmE), /kə.ˈnek.tɪŋ rɒd/ (BrE)
a rod in an engine or similar machine that connects a rotating wheel, such as a crank, to a reciprocating part, typically a piston, and transmits motion between them

Examples

A connecting rod in engine
A broken connecting rod can cause severe damage to a car's engine.
He inspected the connecting rod for signs of bending or cracks during the overhaul.
The connecting rod in the engine needs to be replaced after years of wear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crankshaft - shaft driven by or driving the connecting rod in an engine
