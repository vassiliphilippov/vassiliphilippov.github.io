greenstick

noun
fresh flexible twig
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːnˌstɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnˌstɪk / (BrE)
a fresh, flexible twig or young branch that is still green and pliable

Examples

A fresh greenstick
She used a greenstick to mark the planting rows in the garden.
The children bent a greenstick into a circle to make a wreath.
A greenstick is easier to shape than a dry, brittle branch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: branch - a part of a tree that grows out from the trunk
  • antonym: dry stick - a stick that is no longer green or flexible
  • synonym: sapling - a young tree, especially one with a slender trunk
