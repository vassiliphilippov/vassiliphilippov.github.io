saki

noun
species of small South American monkey
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːki / (AmE), / ˈsɑːki / (BrE)
A small arboreal monkey native to tropical South America, noted for its long hair and bushy nonprehensile tail.

Examples

A saki monkey
Researchers observed a family of sakis feeding on fruits high in the canopy.
The saki uses its bushy tail to help balance while leaping between branches.
Unlike some monkeys, the saki has a nonprehensile tail.
