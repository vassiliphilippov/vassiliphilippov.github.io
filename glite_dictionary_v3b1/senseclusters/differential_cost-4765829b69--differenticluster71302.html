differential cost

noun
cost change per unit produced
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl kɑst / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl kɒst / (BrE)
the increase or decrease in total cost resulting from producing one more or one less unit of output

Examples

The differential cost increase
If the differential cost exceeds the additional revenue, it may not be profitable to expand production.
The company analyzed the differential cost of manufacturing an extra batch before increasing production.
Understanding the differential cost helps managers make informed decisions about changes in output levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marginal cost - the cost of producing one additional unit of output
