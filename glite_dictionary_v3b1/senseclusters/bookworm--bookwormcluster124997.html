bookworm

noun
a person who loves to read
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈbʊk.wɜːm /; / ˈbʊkˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a person who loves reading and spends a lot of time doing it

Examples

A bookworm reader
As a child, Sarah was known as the family bookworm because she always had her nose in a novel.
He spent most of his weekends at the library, proving he was a real bookworm.
The bookworm in our group prefers a quiet night with a good story to a party.
Growing up as a bookworm, Lisa often lost herself in novels instead of playing outside.
Sarah has always been a bookworm, spending hours at the library each weekend.
Some consider him a bookworm because he prefers studying to going out with friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bibliophile - a person who collects or has a great love of books
  • antonym: philistine - person indifferent or hostile to culture and the arts
  • synonym: scholar - person who is highly educated or has an aptitude for study
