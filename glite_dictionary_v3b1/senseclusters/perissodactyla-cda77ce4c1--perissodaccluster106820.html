Perissodactyla

noun
order of odd-toed hoofed mammals
9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.rɪ.sə.ˈdæk.tɪ.lə/ /; / /per.ɪ.sə.ˈdæk.tɪ.lə/ (AmE), /pə.rɪ.sə.ˈdæk.tɪ.lə/ /; / /pe.rɪ.sə.ˈdæk.tɪ.lə/ (BrE)
an order of hoofed mammals with an odd number of toes on each foot, including horses, tapirs, rhinoceroses, and their extinct relatives

Examples

The Perissodactyla order
Extinct species of Perissodactyla roamed the ancient grasslands millions of years ago.
Horses and tapirs are grouped together in the mammalian order Perissodactyla.
The rhinoceros is a member of the Perissodactyla order, characterised by an odd number of toes.
Tapirs are members of the Perissodactyla and are native to Central and South America.
The order Perissodactyla includes well-known species such as horses and rhinoceroses.
Unlike cattle, which belong to Artiodactyla, animals of Perissodactyla have an odd number of toes.
