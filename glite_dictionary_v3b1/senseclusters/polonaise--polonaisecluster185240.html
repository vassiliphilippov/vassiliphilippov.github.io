polonaise

noun
historical women's dress style
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊ.ləˈneɪz / (AmE), / ˌpɒl.əˈneɪz / (BrE)
A woman's dress with a tight-fitting bodice and an overskirt gathered up at the back to reveal a contrasting or colorful underskirt, popular in the late 18th century.

Examples

A polonaise dress
Designers often draw inspiration from the polonaise for modern haute couture collections.
She wore a pale blue polonaise for the historical reenactment ball.
The museum displayed a stunning polonaise from the 1770s, featuring intricate embroidery.
