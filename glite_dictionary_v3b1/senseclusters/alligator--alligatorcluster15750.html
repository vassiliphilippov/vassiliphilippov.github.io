alligator

noun
large semi-aquatic reptile with broad snout
Pronunciation: / ˈæ.ləˌɡeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈæ.lɪˌɡeɪ.tə / (BrE)
A large, semi-aquatic reptile similar to a crocodile, with a shorter, broader snout, found chiefly in the southeastern United States and China.

Examples

Wild alligator
She spotted an alligator swimming slowly through the murky water.
The alligator basked on the riverbank, its jaws slightly open in the sun.
Zookeepers warned visitors not to feed the alligators during their tour.
