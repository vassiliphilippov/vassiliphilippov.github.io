deviancy

noun
degree of difference
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːviənsi / (AmE), / ˈdiːviənsi / (BrE)
the degree or amount by which something differs from a standard, norm, or expected value, especially in technical or statistical contexts

Examples

Statistical deviancy
The scientist measured the deviancy of the results to determine if the experiment was successful.
A high level of deviancy in the data set may indicate an error in measurement.
The report analyzed the deviancy from the expected performance standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deviation - the amount by which something differs from a norm or standard
  • antonym: normality - the state of being normal or standard
