untempered

adjective
not properly hardened or adjusted
6.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtɛmpɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛmpəd / (BrE)
(of a substance) not brought to the desired consistency or hardness, especially by heat or additives, as in construction or metallurgy.

Examples

Untempered steel
A sword made from untempered steel is more likely to bend or break.
Mixing with water alone, the clay remained untempered and fragile.
The walls began to crumble because the builders used untempered mortar.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tempered - brought to a desired hardness or consistency, especially by heat-treatment
