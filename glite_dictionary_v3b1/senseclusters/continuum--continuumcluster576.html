continuum

noun
a continuous sequence or range
7.2 7.29/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàt…™nju…ôm / (AmE), / k…ônÀàt…™njuÀê…ôm / (BrE)
A continuous sequence or range in which adjacent elements are not perceptibly different from each other, though the extremes are quite distinct.

Examples

A continuum of emotions
Emotional experiences occur along a continuum rather than in distinct categories.
Language proficiency exists on a continuum, from beginner to native speaker.
The colors of the rainbow blend together to form a continuum with no sharp boundaries between them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spectrum - a range of values or qualities, especially one that is gradual or not sharply divided
