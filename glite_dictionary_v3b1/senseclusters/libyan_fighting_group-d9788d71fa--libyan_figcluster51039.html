Libyan Fighting Group

proper noun
Libyan militant Islamist group, founded 1995
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbiən ˈfaɪtɪŋ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈlɪbiən ˈfaɪtɪŋ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a Libyan militant Islamist organization founded in 1995, aligned with al-Qaeda, and known for efforts to overthrow the Libyan government and its attempts to assassinate Muammar Qaddafi

Examples

Libyan Fighting Group members
Reports indicated that members of the Libyan Fighting Group had received training from al-Qaeda affiliates.
Several governments have designated the Libyan Fighting Group as a terrorist organization due to its violent activities.
The Libyan Fighting Group played a significant role in opposition activities against Qaddafi's regime in the 1990s.
