plasma

noun
plasma display (TV/monitor)
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplæz.mə / (AmE), / ˈplɑːz.mə / (BrE)
a type of flat-panel display that produces images by illuminating many tiny cells of ionized gas (plasma), commonly used in television and monitor screens.

Examples

Plasma TV screen
We upgraded to a plasma TV for richer colors and higher contrast.
The store had several large plasma monitors on display, drawing customers with vivid images.
Older cinemas replaced their projectors with compact plasma panels for brighter lobby screens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flat-panel display - a thin electronic display, such as LCD, OLED, or plasma, used for TVs and monitors
  • synonym: plasma display - a display device that uses small cells of ionized gas to create images
