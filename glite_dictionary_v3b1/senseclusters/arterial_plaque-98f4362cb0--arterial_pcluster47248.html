arterial plaque

noun
fatty deposit inside an artery
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈtɪriəl plæk / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriəl plæk / (BrE)
A fatty deposit that forms inside the wall of an artery, often contributing to atherosclerosis and cardiovascular disease.

Examples

Arterial plaque buildup
A diet rich in fiber can help prevent the development of arterial plaque.
Doctors warn that high cholesterol may increase arterial plaque and lead to heart problems.
Regular exercise can help reduce the risk of arterial plaque buildup.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atherosclerosis - disease involving plaque buildup in arteries
  • degree higher: cholesterol - a fatlike substance in blood, linked to plaque formation
