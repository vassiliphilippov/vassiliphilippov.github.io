presupposition

noun
assumption made in advance
7.7 1.69/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpriÀêÀås åp…ôÀàz…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀêÀås åp…ôÀàz…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
An assumption made beforehand or taken for granted, especially as a basis for argument or action.

Examples

The presupposition of truth
Before we proceed, let's clarify the presuppositions underlying our discussion.
Her argument is based on the presupposition that everyone values honesty.
The theory relies on several presuppositions that have not yet been tested.

Synonyms

  • assumption
    3.5 54/m
    an assumption is a general idea people accept without proof, while presupposition is an implicit idea taken for argument or action.
  • presumption
    5.8 3.10/m
    a presumption is an idea accepted as likely or treated as true by default, while presupposition is a background idea taken for granted.
  • premise
    5.9 26/m
    a premise is a clear statement used as the starting point for an argument, while presupposition is often an unstated prior assumption.
  • postulate
    8.1 0.93/m
    a postulate is a formal principle assumed without proof for reasoning, while presupposition is a more general idea taken for granted.
  • supposition
    7.3 2.32/m
    a supposition is a tentative idea suggested for thought or testing, while presupposition is an assumption taken beforehand as given.
