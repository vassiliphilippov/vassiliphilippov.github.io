presupposition

noun
assumption made in advance
7.3 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
An assumption made beforehand or taken for granted, especially as a basis for argument or action.

Examples

The presupposition of truth
Before we proceed, let's clarify the presuppositions underlying our discussion.
Her argument is based on the presupposition that everyone values honesty.
The theory relies on several presuppositions that have not yet been tested.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assumption - something that is accepted as true or certain to happen, without proof
