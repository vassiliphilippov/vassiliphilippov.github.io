atomic weight

noun
average mass of element's atoms
1.6
Pronunciation: / əˌtɑmɪk ˈweɪt / (AmE), / əˌtɒmɪk ˈweɪt / (BrE)
The average mass of the atoms of a chemical element, expressed in atomic mass units.

Examples

Atomic weight value
Chemists use atomic weight to calculate the relative masses of elements in compounds.
Tables in the textbook list the atomic weight of each known element.
The atomic weight of carbon is approximately 12.01.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atomic mass - the mass of an individual atom in atomic mass units
