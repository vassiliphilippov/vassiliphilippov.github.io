rough

adjective
approximate; not exact or precise
1.0
Pronunciation: / rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
not finished, not exact, or only a preliminary version; approximate or lacking detail

Examples

Rough estimate
He gave me a rough estimate of how much the repairs would cost.
It's just a rough idea, but we can refine it later.
The rough time for the meeting is around two o'clock.
Although this is just a rough version, it gives a sense of the final design.
She submitted a rough outline of her proposal to get feedback before finishing.
The architect showed us a rough model of the new building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approximate - Nearly correct or exact; close in value or amount
  • antonym: perfected - made completely free from faults or defects
  • antonym: precise - Exact and accurate in all details
  • synonym: unfinished - not completed or perfected
