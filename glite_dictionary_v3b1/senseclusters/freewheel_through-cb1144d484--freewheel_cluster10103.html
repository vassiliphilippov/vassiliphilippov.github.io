freewheel through

verb
proceed without fixed plan or structure
Pronunciation: / ˈfriˌwil ˈθru / (AmE), / ˈfriːwiːl ˈθruː / (BrE)
to proceed through something with little planning, control, or structure

Examples

To freewheel through a task
Instead of writing an outline, she decided to freewheel through her final paper.
Sometimes, it's exciting to freewheel through a conversation and see where it leads.
They tried to freewheel through the meeting, but it quickly became chaotic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plan - to decide on and arrange in advance
