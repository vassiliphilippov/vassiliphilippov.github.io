true bacteria

noun
major group of bacteria with rigid cell walls
5.9
Pronunciation: / tru bəˈktɪriə / (AmE), / truː bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
a major group of bacteria characterized by rigid cell walls; includes most familiar bacteria distinguished from archaea

Examples

true bacteria group
Flagella enable some true bacteria to move independently in their environment.
Many pathogens that cause disease in humans are classified as true bacteria.
The true bacteria are distinguished from archaea by differences in their cell wall composition.
