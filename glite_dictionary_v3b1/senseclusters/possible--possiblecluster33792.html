possible

adjective
able to happen or exist
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəbl̩ / (BrE)
that can happen, exist, or be done; able to be achieved

Examples

Possible outcome
It is possible that the train will arrive on time despite the delay.
New technology makes it possible to communicate instantly across the globe.
Please let me know as soon as possible.
Do you think it’s possible that they misplaced your application?
It is possible to finish the project by Friday if we all work together.
Scientists are exploring possible ways to cure the disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impossible - not able to happen or exist
  • synonym: potential - having or showing the capacity to develop into something in the future
  • degree higher: probable - likely to happen or be true
  • degree lower: unlikely - not likely to happen
