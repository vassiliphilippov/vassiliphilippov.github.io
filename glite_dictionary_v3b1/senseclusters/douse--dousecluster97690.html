douse

verb
quickly immerse in liquid
4.9 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / daÊŠs / (AmE), / daÊŠs / (BrE)
to plunge or dip something briefly or suddenly into a liquid

Examples

Before painting, he doused the brush in primer.
She doused her hands in cold water to soothe the burn.
The chef quickly doused the vegetables in boiling water to blanch them.

Synonyms

  • dip
    1.0 8.89/m
    dip means putting something briefly into liquid for eating, cleaning, or coating, while douse implies a quick, sudden plunge.
  • duck
    2.7 1.01/m
    duck means dipping something quickly into liquid and taking it out again, while douse suggests a deeper, more sudden plunge.
  • dunk
    2.7 0.90/m
    dunk means quickly dipping food into liquid, while douse means plunging something suddenly into liquid for many different uses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dip - to put something quickly into a liquid and then take it out
  • synonym: immerse - to dip or submerge completely in a liquid
  • synonym: submerge - to put or sink below the surface of a liquid
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove from being dipped or submerged
