A group of small bracts resembling a calyx, occurring beneath the actual calyx in some flowers, such as carnations or hibiscus.

Examples

Flower calyculus
A botanist pointed out a well-developed calyculus in the hibiscus flower he dissected.
Some species display a prominent calyculus that can be mistaken for part of the true calyx.
The carnation's calyculus is easily seen beneath its showy petals.

Synonyms

  • calycle
    9.9 0.00/m
    calycle is a small group of bracts found directly beneath a flower, while calyculus denotes those bracts under the calyx.
  • epicalyx
    9.9 0.00/m
    epicalyx is a whorl of bracts lying outside the true calyx, while calyculus refers to the bracts beneath it.
  • false calyx
    9.3 0.00/m
    false calyx is a plain phrase for bracts that look like a calyx, while calyculus is the technical botanical term.
