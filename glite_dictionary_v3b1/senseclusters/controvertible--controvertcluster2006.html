controvertible

adjective
open to dispute
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntrəˈvɜːrtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈvɜːtɪbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be disputed, questioned, or argued about; not certain or universally accepted

Examples

A controvertible theory
The scientist presented a controvertible theory that sparked much debate among her peers.
Whether the policy will succeed remains controvertible at this stage.
His explanation was controvertible, and many experts disagreed with his conclusions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debatable - open to discussion or argument
  • antonym: indisputable - impossible to question or doubt
