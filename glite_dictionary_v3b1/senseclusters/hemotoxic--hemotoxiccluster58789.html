hemotoxic

adjective
damaging blood cells
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈtɑksɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈtɒksɪk / (BrE)
causing damage to blood cells, blood vessels, or tissues, often resulting in bleeding or destruction of red blood cells

Examples

Hemotoxic venom
The snake's hemotoxic venom can cause severe internal bleeding in its prey.
Doctors treated the patient for hemotoxic effects after a dangerous animal bite.
Some viper species are known for their hemotoxic toxins, which destroy blood cells.
The viper's hemotoxic venom can cause severe internal bleeding if not treated quickly.
Researchers studied the hemotoxic effects of the chemical on laboratory animals.
Some snakebites are hemotoxic, leading to destruction of red blood cells and tissue damage.
