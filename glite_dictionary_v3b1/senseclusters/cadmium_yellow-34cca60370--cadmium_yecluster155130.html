cadmium yellow

noun
a vivid yellow pigment used in art
Pronunciation: / ˌkæd.mi.əm ˈjɛl.oʊ / (AmE), / ˌkæd.mɪ.əm ˈjel.əʊ / (BrE)
a bright yellow pigment made from cadmium sulfide and barium sulfate, used especially in painting

Examples

Cadmium yellow pigment
Many Impressionist painters favored cadmium yellow for its intensity and durability.
The artist used cadmium yellow to create vibrant highlights in her painting.
You should be careful when handling cadmium yellow, as it contains toxic substances.
