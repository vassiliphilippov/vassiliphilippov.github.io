surface

noun
aerodynamic part of an aircraft
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.fɪs/ /; / /ˈsɜːr.fəs/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.fɪs/ /; / /ˈsɜː.fəs/ (BrE)
an aerodynamic part of an aircraft, such as a wing, rudder, or flap, which provides lift or control by reacting against the air during flight

Examples

Aircraft surface
A damaged surface on the wing can compromise an aircraft’s stability.
The aileron is a type of surface used to control the roll of a plane.
The pilot adjusted the angle of the surface to maintain steady flight.
