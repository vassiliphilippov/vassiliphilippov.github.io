Australian turtledove

noun
small dove species native to Australia
Pronunciation: / əˈstreɪljən ˈtɜrt̬l̩ˌdoʊv / (AmE), / ɒˈstreɪliən ˈtɜːtl̩ˌdəʊv / (BrE)
a small species of dove native to Australia, known for its soft cooing sound

Examples

The Australian turtledove coos
Several Australian turtledoves perched on the garden fence early in the morning.
She recognized the gentle cooing of an Australian turtledove outside her window.
The Australian turtledove can often be seen foraging for seeds in open woodlands.
