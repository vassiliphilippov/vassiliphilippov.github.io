cellmate

noun
roommate in monastery
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛlˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛlˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a person who shares a small private room, such as a cell, with another in a monastery, convent, or similar religious community

Examples

Monastery cellmate
During the retreat, she and her cellmate spent many quiet hours in prayer and study.
Each monk was assigned a cellmate to encourage companionship and support.
The nuns grew close to their cellmates through daily routines and shared responsibilities.
