outage

noun
amount lost in storage or transit
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtɪdʒ / (BrE)
the amount of a substance, such as liquid or gas, lost during storage or transportation

Examples

Oil outage
Companies measure the outage of a liquid to manage inventory accurately.
Regular checks help minimize outage in the whiskey barrels during aging.
The outage of crude oil during the long shipment was higher than expected.
