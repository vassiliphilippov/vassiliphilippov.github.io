T-bill

noun
short-term U.S. government security
6.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtiː.bɪl/ (AmE), /ˈtiː.bɪl/ (BrE)
a short-term U.S. government security sold at a discount and maturing in one year or less, without paying periodic interest

Examples

A T-bill investment
Many investors prefer to hold T-bills because of their low risk and liquidity.
The government issued new T-bills to help cover short-term financing needs.
Unlike bonds, a T-bill does not pay regular interest but is sold at a discount.
