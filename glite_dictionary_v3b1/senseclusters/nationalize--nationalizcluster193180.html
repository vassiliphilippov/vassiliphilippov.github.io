nationalize

verb
make national in character or scope
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz /; / ˈnæʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something national in character, scope, or relevance

Examples

To nationalize culture
Efforts were made to nationalize local traditions during the centenary celebrations.
The media nationalized the achievements of the athletes, celebrating them as symbols of national pride.
The organization aimed to nationalize education standards across all regions.
The government aimed to nationalise the campaign to increase public awareness across the country.
The politician tried to nationalise the festival, turning it into a celebration of cultural unity.
Writers often nationalise folk tales by emphasizing their country's values and traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: internationalize - to make international in character or scope
