translation

noun
rendered text in another language
Pronunciation: / trænzˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / trænzˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a written text in one language that has been rendered from an original text in another language, maintaining the original meaning

Examples

A translation of a book
His translation of the document was praised for its accuracy.
She read both the French poem and its English translation.
The translation of the novel captured the humor and style of the original.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the initial text from which a translation is made
  • synonym: version - a particular form or variant of something, often used for an adaptation or translation
