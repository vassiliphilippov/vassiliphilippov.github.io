Producing a large amount of something, especially works of art or literature; highly productive intellectually.

Examples

Prolific writer
His prolific mind produced inventions that changed the industry.
She is a prolific author who has published more than fifty novels.
The artist remained prolific even in the later years of her career.

Synonyms

  • productive
    2.7 5.33/m
    productive is used for making large amounts in work or industry, while prolific refers especially to many artistic or intellectual works.
  • fecund
    9.1 0.14/m
    fecund is a formal word for a mind or place that creates many ideas, while prolific focuses on artistic output.
  • fertile
    4.3 1.70/m
    fertile describes a mind or land that can produce many new ideas or crops, while prolific stresses actual large output.
  • fruitful
    3.9 5.17/m
    fruitful produces good or useful results, whereas prolific emphasizes quantity and many works, often in art or writing.
