boom

noun
a sudden period of rapid growth or prosperity
2.8 15/m
Pronunciation: / buÀêm / (AmE), / buÀêm / (BrE)
a period of rapid growth or economic prosperity

Examples

An economic boom
After the war, there was a major boom in the housing market.
The tech industry experienced a boom due to new innovations.
Tourism brought an economic boom to the region over the last decade.
During the economic boom of the 1950s, unemployment rates were at historic lows.
Some investors are cautious, fearing that the current boom could be followed by a downturn.
The tech sector experienced a significant boom in the early 2000s, leading to many startups.

Synonyms

  • flush
    7.1 0.27/m
    flush is a short period of high prosperity or productivity, while boom is a longer phase of rapid economic growth.
  • bonanza
    7.1 1.68/m
    bonanza is an unexpected event that brings a sudden large gain, while boom is a broader period of sustained economic growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expansion - the action of becoming larger or more extensive, often used to describe an economic upswing
  • antonym: recession - a period of temporary economic decline
  • antonym: slump - a period of economic decline or stagnation
