counter-reform

adjective
opposing reforms
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntər rɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə rɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
opposing, undoing, or reversing reforms or changes that have been made

Examples

Counter-reform policies
The new administration introduced several counter-reform policies to reverse recent changes in education.
Many citizens protested the counter-reform agenda that aimed to restore outdated regulations.
Analysts debated whether the counter-reform measures would undo years of progress.
