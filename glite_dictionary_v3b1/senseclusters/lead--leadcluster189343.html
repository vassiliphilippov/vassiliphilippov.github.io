lead

verb
guide or take someone to a place
1.0 86/m
Pronunciation: / lid / (AmE), / liÀêd / (BrE)
to guide or take someone to a place by going with them or showing the way

Examples

To lead someone home
Could you lead me to the nearest subway station?
Our tour guide will lead us through the museum this afternoon.
She offered to lead the visitors to the conference room.

Synonyms

    1.0 22/m
    guide shows people the route and helps them along, while lead means to go with someone and show the way.
  • conduct
    4.3 0.67/m
    conduct guides people in a careful, formal way to a place, while lead is the everyday verb for going with someone and showing the way.
  • escort
    4.0 11/m
    escort goes with someone for protection or courtesy, while lead means going with someone to show or point the way.
  • take
    1.0 655/m

  • direct
    1.6 21/m
    direct gives orders or clear instructions about what to do, while lead means going with someone in person to show the way.
