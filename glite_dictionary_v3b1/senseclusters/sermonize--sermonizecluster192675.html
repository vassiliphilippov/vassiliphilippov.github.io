sermonize

verb
speak as if giving a sermon; moralize
8.0 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…úÀêrm…ôÀåna…™z / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀêm…ôna…™z / (BrE)
to speak in a manner typical of delivering a sermon, especially by expressing moral judgments or giving moral advice in a lengthy, tedious, or preachy way

Examples

To sermonize at gatherings
He tends to sermonize at family gatherings, which annoys his siblings.
Please don't sermonize about responsibility every time we talk.
She sermonized at length about the importance of honesty in business.
I wish you wouldn't sermonise every time someone makes a mistake.
She tends to sermonise instead of offering practical advice.
Whenever he disagreed, he would sermonise about the right way to live.
