newsy

adjective
fond of sharing news or gossip
Pronunciation: / ˈnuzi / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzi / (BrE)
full of news or information, or fond of sharing news in a friendly and informal way (used for people, letters, or messages)

Examples

A newsy letter
Grandpa likes to write newsy letters filled with family gossip and stories.
Her newsy emails always keep me updated on everything happening in town.
The magazine’s tone is light and newsy, making it enjoyable to read.
His newsy updates always keep us informed about what’s happening in his life.
I love receiving newsy letters from my family when I'm away at college.
She wrote me a newsy email that told me all about her travels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chatty - talking a lot in a friendly, informal way
  • synonym: informative - providing useful or interesting information
  • antonym: taciturn - reserved or uncommunicative in speech
