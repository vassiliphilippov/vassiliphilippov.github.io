face-off

noun
direct confrontation or contest
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪsˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈfeɪsɒf / (BrE)
a direct confrontation or conflict between two opposing parties, often involving a tense or competitive atmosphere

Examples

A tense face-off
The debate turned into a heated face-off over policy differences.
The two leaders had a tense face-off during the meeting.
Yesterday’s game ended with an exciting face-off between the rival teams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confrontation - a hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties
  • synonym: showdown - a decisive confrontation or contest
