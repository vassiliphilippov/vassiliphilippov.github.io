stereographic

adjective
3D to 2D representation
8.8
Pronunciation: /ste.ri.oʊ.ˈgræf.ɪk/ /; / /ste.ri.ə.ˈgræf.ɪk/ (AmE), /ste.ri.əʊ.ˈgræf.ɪk/ /; / /ste.ri.ə.ˈgræf.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or using a method of representing three-dimensional objects or forms in two dimensions

Examples

Stereographic projection
The stereographic projection is often used in cartography to map the Earth onto a flat surface.
Scientists use stereographic diagrams to analyze the orientation of crystals in geology.
A stereographic image can help visualize complex three-dimensional structures in two dimensions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orthographic - relating to a method of projection where objects are represented without perspective
  • synonym: perspective - a technique for representing three-dimensional objects on a two-dimensional surface
