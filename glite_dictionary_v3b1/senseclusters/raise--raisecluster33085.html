raise

verb
initiate or provoke (an activity, feeling, etc.)
1.0
Pronunciation: / reɪz /; / ˈreɪz / (AmE), / reɪz /; / ˈreɪz / (BrE)
to mention, bring up, or cause something to be expressed or to happen (such as an issue, feeling, support, or noise)

Examples

To raise an issue
He tried to raise support for his cause among the workers.
The news began to raise suspicion within the community.
They hoped to raise a protest against the new regulations.
During the interview, he unexpectedly raised concerns about safety measures.
If you have any questions, please feel free to raise them now.
She decided to raise the issue during the team meeting.
He decided to raise his concerns at the meeting so everyone could hear them.
Several residents raised a protest against the new construction project.
When the referee made a controversial call, the fans raised a loud shout from the stands.
