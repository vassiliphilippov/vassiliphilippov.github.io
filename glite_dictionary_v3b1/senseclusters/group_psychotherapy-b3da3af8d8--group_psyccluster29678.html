group psychotherapy

noun
therapy conducted with a group of people
5.2 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / …°ruÀêp Àåsa…™ko äÀàŒ∏…õr…ôpi / (AmE), / …°ruÀêp Àåsa…™k…ô äÀàŒ∏…õr…ôpi / (BrE)
A form of psychotherapy in which a small group of people meet regularly with a therapist to discuss their problems, with the interactions among group members contributing to the therapeutic process.

Examples

Group psychotherapy sessions
In group psychotherapy, participants support each other while working through personal challenges.
Many people find group psychotherapy helpful because they realize they are not alone in their struggles.
The clinic offers both individual and group psychotherapy for patients with anxiety disorders.

Synonyms

  • group therapy
    3.4 0.84/m
    group therapy focuses on shared problem-solving and practical support, while group psychotherapy emphasizes member interactions for deeper therapeutic change.
  • psychotherapy group
    5.1 0.01/m
    psychotherapy group is the label for people who meet to receive therapy, while group psychotherapy describes the therapy method focused on member interactions.
