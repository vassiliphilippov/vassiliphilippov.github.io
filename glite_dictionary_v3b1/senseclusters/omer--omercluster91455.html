omer

noun
Jewish unit or ritual period
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈəʊmə / (BrE)
an ancient Hebrew unit of dry measure, or the period of counting days between Passover and Shavuot in Judaism

Examples

The omer count
During the festival, Jews count each day of the omer until Shavuot arrives.
An omer was used as a unit of measure for offerings in ancient Israel.
The tradition of counting the omer connects Passover to the giving of the Torah.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sabbath - the Jewish day of rest, observed from Friday evening to Saturday evening
  • synonym: sheaf - a bundle of grain stalks tied together after reaping
