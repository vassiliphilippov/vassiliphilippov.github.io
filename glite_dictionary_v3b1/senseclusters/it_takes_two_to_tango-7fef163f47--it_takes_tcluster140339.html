it takes two to tango

x
both parties are responsible; requires two people
Pronunciation: / ɪt teɪks tuː tə ˈtæŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪt teɪks tuː tə ˈtæŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
used to say that certain actions or situations require two people, especially that both parties involved share responsibility for something

Examples

It takes two to tango in talks
Negotiations failed because it takes two to tango, and both companies refused to compromise.
When a conflict escalates, remember that it takes two to tango—both sides play a part.
You can't blame John alone for the argument; it takes two to tango.
