shoot one's load

verb

(informal, vulgar) To expend all one's energy, effort, or enthusiasm at once, often leaving nothing in reserve.

Examples

Don't shoot your load early
Don't shoot your load too early in the competition or you'll run out of steam.
He shot his load at the meeting and was too exhausted to contribute to the next project.
The musician shot her load on stage, leaving the crowd in awe of her energy.
