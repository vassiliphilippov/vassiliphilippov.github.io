harp

verb
talk or complain persistently
6.6 1.25/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërp / (AmE), / h…ëÀêp / (BrE)
to talk or complain repeatedly or persistently about something, often in a tiresome or excessive way (usually with 'on')

Examples

To harp on problems
He tends to harp on his grievances, even after the issue is resolved.
Please don't harp on my mistakes; I've already apologized several times.
She continues to harp on the need for punctuality at every meeting.
