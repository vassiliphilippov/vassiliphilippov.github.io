flop

verb
fall or drop heavily
2.3
Pronunciation: / flɑp / (AmE), / flɒp / (BrE)
to fall, drop, sit, or lie down heavily and suddenly, often in a relaxed or tired way

Examples

To flop onto a bed
After a long day, he flopped onto the sofa and closed his eyes.
Exhausted from the hike, she flopped down on the grass to rest.
The fish flopped around on the deck before being thrown back into the water.
After a long day, she flopped onto the couch and sighed with relief.
He was so tired that he just flopped into the nearest chair without saying a word.
The dog flopped down on the cool kitchen floor to rest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • synonym: plop - to fall or drop with a soft sound
