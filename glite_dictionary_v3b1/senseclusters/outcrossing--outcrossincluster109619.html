outcrossing

noun
breeding for diversity
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of breeding individuals from different groups or populations to increase genetic diversity

Examples

Outcrossing in breeding
Many farmers use outcrossing to produce healthier and more resilient crops.
The conservation program relies on outcrossing to prevent genetic disorders in endangered species.
By practicing outcrossing, breeders can introduce new traits into a population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crossbreeding - breeding animals or plants from different breeds or varieties
  • antonym: inbreeding - breeding between closely related individuals
