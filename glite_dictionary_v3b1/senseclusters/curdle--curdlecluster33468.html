curdle

verb
separate into curds, especially of milk
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝː.dl̩ /; / ˈkɝːdəl / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.dl̩ /; / ˈkɜːdəl /; / ˈkɜːdl / (BrE)
(of milk or another liquid) to become thick and partly solid, often because of souring, heat, or the addition of acid

Examples

To curdle milk
If you add lemon juice to milk, it will curdle quickly.
She noticed the milk had curdled and could not be used for drinking.
The hot soup caused the cream to curdle on top.
Heat can cause eggs to curdle if added too quickly to a hot mixture.
The horror story was so frightening, it made my blood curdle.
The milk began to curdle after being left out overnight.
If you leave milk out in warm weather, it will curdle quickly.
She tasted the soup and realized that the cream had curdled.
The sauce began to curdle after boiling for too long.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulate - to change from a fluid into a thickened mass
  • synonym: solidify - to make or become hard or solid
  • synonym: sour - to make or become sour
  • synonym: spoil - (of food) to become unfit for eating
