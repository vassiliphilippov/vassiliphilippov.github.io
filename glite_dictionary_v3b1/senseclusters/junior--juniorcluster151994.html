junior

noun
third-year college or university student (esp. US)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːniɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə / (BrE)
A student in the third year of study at a college or university, especially in the United States.

Examples

A college junior
After two years as an undergraduate, Emily became a junior last fall.
As a junior, he started taking more advanced courses related to his major.
Most juniors begin thinking seriously about internships and future careers.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: senior - a student in their final year of high school or college (US)
  • degree lower: sophomore - a student in the second year of high school or college (US)
