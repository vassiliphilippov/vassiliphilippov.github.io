dodo

noun
extinct flightless bird of Mauritius
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊˌdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊdəʊ / (BrE)
a large, flightless bird native to Mauritius, now extinct, related to pigeons

Examples

Extinct dodo bird
Scientists study the dodo to understand the impact of human activity on extinction.
The dodo became extinct in the late 17th century due to overhunting and introduced predators.
The expression 'as dead as a dodo' is used to describe something that is completely extinct or obsolete.
