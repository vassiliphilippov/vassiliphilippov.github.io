infectious disease

noun
disease transmissible between people or organisms
3.0 2.71/m

A disease caused by pathogenic microorganisms and capable of being transmitted from one person or organism to another.

Examples

An infectious disease spreads
Measles is an example of an infectious disease that can quickly spread through a community.
Proper handwashing is important to prevent the transmission of infectious diseases.
The doctor specialized in the treatment of rare infectious diseases.

Synonyms

  • communicable disease
    3.6 0.34/m
    communicable disease is a term for any illness that spreads between people or animals, while infectious disease adds the idea of germs causing it.
  • contagious disease
    3.2 0.38/m
    contagious disease spreads easily by contact between people, while infectious disease focuses on illnesses caused by germs that can also spread.
  • infection
    3.1 46/m
    infection is the process or condition of germs in the body, while infectious disease names the illness that can be passed to others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagious disease - a disease that is easily transmitted by contact with an infected person or surface
