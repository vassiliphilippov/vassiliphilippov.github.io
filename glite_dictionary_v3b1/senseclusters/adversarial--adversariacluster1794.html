adversarial

adjective
intended to mislead
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌædvərˈsɛriəl / (AmE), / ˌædvəˈsɛːriəl / (BrE)
designed to deceive or mislead, especially by intentionally causing errors in a system or process

Examples

Adversarial attack
The security team tested the system with adversarial attacks to find its weaknesses.
Researchers developed adversarial examples to trick the image recognition software.
An adversarial approach can reveal vulnerabilities in artificial intelligence models.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benign - harmless or not intended to cause harm
  • synonym: deceptive - intended to make someone believe something that is not true
