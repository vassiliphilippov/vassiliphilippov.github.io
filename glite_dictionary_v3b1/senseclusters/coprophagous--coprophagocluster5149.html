coprophagous

adjective
eating feces
Pronunciation: / kəˈprɑːfəɡəs / (AmE), / kɒˈprɒfəɡəs / (BrE)
describing an animal that eats feces as part of its diet

Examples

Coprophagous beetles
Certain beetles are coprophagous and play an important role in recycling nutrients in the soil.
Rabbits are known to be coprophagous, re-ingesting their own feces to absorb more nutrients.
The study focused on coprophagous insects and their impact on the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scatophagous - feeding on dung or feces
