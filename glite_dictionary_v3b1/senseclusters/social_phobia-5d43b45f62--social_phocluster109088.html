social phobia

noun
fear of social situations or being judged
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl ˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
an intense and persistent fear of social situations involving possible judgment or scrutiny by others, often leading to avoidance of such situations

Examples

Social phobia fear
He avoided job interviews due to his social phobia.
Her social phobia made it difficult for her to attend parties or meet new people.
Public speaking often triggers severe anxiety in people with social phobia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agoraphobia - fear of open or public spaces, contrasted with social phobia
  • degree lower: shyness - mild discomfort in social situations; less intense than social phobia
  • synonym: social anxiety disorder - an alternative clinical term for social phobia
