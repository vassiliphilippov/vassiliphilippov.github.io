ghoulishness

noun
morbid cruelty
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈguː.lɪʃ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈguː.lɪʃ.nəs/ (BrE)
a morbid or cruel interest in death, suffering, or the macabre

Examples

Ghoulishness in reporting
The journalist's ghoulishness was evident as he described the disaster with a disturbing smile.
Some viewers were disturbed by the ghoulishness of the crime documentary's detailed reenactments.
She was shocked by the ghoulishness of those who found amusement in others' tragedies.
The journalist's ghoulishness was evident in the way he reported on tragic events.
Some people are disturbed by the ghoulishness of certain horror movies.
Her ghoulishness made her the perfect host for the haunted house tour.
