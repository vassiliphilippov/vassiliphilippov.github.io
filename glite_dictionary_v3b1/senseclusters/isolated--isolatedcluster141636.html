isolated

adjective
remote; set apart from others
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.səˌleɪ.tɪd /; / ˈaɪsəˌleɪtəd / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.sə.leɪ.tɪd /; / ˈaɪsəleɪtɪd / (BrE)
far away from other places or people; physically separated or cut off from others

Examples

Isolated village
Because of its isolated location, few tourists visit the island.
The community felt isolated after the bridge was destroyed by flooding.
They lived in an isolated farmhouse miles from the nearest town.
A few isolated trees stood in the wide, empty field.
She felt isolated after moving to a new city where she knew no one.
The isolated village had no telephone or internet service for miles around.
After the bridge collapsed, the small village became isolated from the rest of the region.
That isolated house on the cliff seemed both peaceful and lonely.
The hiker felt isolated and anxious after losing phone signal deep in the forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marooned - left trapped and alone in an inaccessible place
  • synonym: remote - far away from other places or people
  • synonym: solitary - existing alone; without companions
