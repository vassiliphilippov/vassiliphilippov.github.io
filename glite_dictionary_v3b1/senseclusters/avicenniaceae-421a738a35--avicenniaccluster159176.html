Avicenniaceae

noun
former plant family (Avicennia genus)
Pronunciation: / əˌvɪsəˈniːəsiː / (AmE), / əˌvɪsəˈnɪːəsiː / (BrE)
A former botanical family used in some classifications, comprising the genus Avicennia, now usually included in Acanthaceae.

Examples

The Avicenniaceae family
Many taxonomists now include Avicenniaceae within the broader family Acanthaceae.
Recent botanical studies have questioned the validity of Avicenniaceae as a separate family.
The Avicenniaceae contain mangrove trees found in tropical coastal regions worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Acanthaceae - family of flowering plants including Avicennia in recent classifications
  • synonym: Avicennia - genus of tropical mangrove trees formerly placed in Avicenniaceae
