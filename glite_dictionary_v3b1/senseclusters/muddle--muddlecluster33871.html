muddle

noun
a confused or disorganized situation
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌd.əl /; / ˈmʌdəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌd.əl /; / ˈmʌdəl / (BrE)
a confused or disorganized state or situation

Examples

A big muddle
After the sudden resignation, the company was left in a bit of a muddle.
He always seems to find himself in a muddle when trying to organize his finances.
The instructions were so unclear that I ended up in a complete muddle.
After the move, their lives were in a complete muddle for weeks.
Her desk was such a muddle that she couldn't find anything she needed.
The report was lost in a muddle of papers on his table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mess - a situation that is dirty, confused, or full of problems
  • antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged or organized properly
