ditto

noun
mark indicating repetition
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a mark or word used to show that something already said should be repeated

Examples

Ditto mark
In old documents, a ditto is often used instead of rewriting words.
Please use the ditto mark if the answer in row two is the same as above.
She put a ditto in the column to indicate repetition of the address.
When asked about my favorite color, I simply said ditto after hearing her answer.
He wrote ditto marks under the previous entry to show the information was the same.
If you agree with the previous statement, you can just reply with ditto.
