social process

noun
process of group formation and interaction
1.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.ʃəl ˈprɑː.ses/ /; / /ˈsoʊ.ʃəl ˈprɔː.ses/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.ʃəl ˈprəʊ.ses/ /; / /ˈsəʊ.ʃəl ˈprə.ses/ (BrE)
a process by which individuals form groups, interact, and establish social relationships, influencing the structure and development of society

Examples

A social process of groups
The development of language within a community can be understood as a social process.
Understanding prejudice requires examining the social process by which attitudes are formed.
Urbanization is a complex social process that changes how people live together.
