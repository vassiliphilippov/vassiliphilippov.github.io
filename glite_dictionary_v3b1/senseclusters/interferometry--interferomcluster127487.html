interferometry

noun
wave interference measurement
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərfəˈrɑːmətri / (AmE), / ˌɪntəfəˈrɒmətri / (BrE)
a scientific technique that uses the interference of waves, such as light or radio waves, to make precise measurements

Examples

Interferometry technique
Scientists use interferometry to measure tiny changes in distance with extreme accuracy.
Modern telescopes often rely on interferometry to observe faint objects in space.
Advances in interferometry have improved the precision of many scientific instruments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: measurement - the process of determining the size, amount, or degree of something
  • synonym: spectroscopy - the study of the interaction between matter and electromagnetic radiation
