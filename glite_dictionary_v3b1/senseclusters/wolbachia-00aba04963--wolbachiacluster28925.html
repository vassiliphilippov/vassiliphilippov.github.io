Wolbachia

proper noun
bacterial genus in insects
Pronunciation: / woʊlˈbækiə / (AmE), / wɒlˈbækiə / (BrE)
a genus of bacteria that lives inside many insects and other arthropods, often affecting their reproduction

Examples

Wolbachia bacteria
Scientists introduced Wolbachia into mosquito populations to help control the spread of diseases.
Many species of insects naturally carry Wolbachia in their cells.
The presence of Wolbachia can influence the reproduction of its insect hosts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteria - microscopic single-celled organisms
