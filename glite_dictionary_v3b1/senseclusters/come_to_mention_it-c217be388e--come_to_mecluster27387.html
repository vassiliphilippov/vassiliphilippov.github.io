come to mention it

interjection
used to introduce a remembered point
Pronunciation: / kʌm tə ˈmɛn.tʃən ɪt / (AmE), / kʌm tə ˈmen.tʃən ɪt / (BrE)
used to indicate that something has just been remembered or realized, often introducing a related remark or topic

Examples

Come to mention it now
Come to mention it, do you know where my keys are?
Come to mention it, we never finished that conversation from yesterday.
I forgot to water the plants—come to mention it, I haven't seen the gardener this week.
