overclaim

noun
exaggerated statement
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌkleɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌkleɪm / (BrE)
a statement or claim that is exaggerated or not fully justified by the evidence

Examples

False overclaim
The advertisement was criticized for making an overclaim about the product's effectiveness.
His report contained an overclaim that was not supported by the data.
Journalists should avoid any overclaim that could mislead the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggeration - a statement that makes something seem better or worse than it really is
  • antonym: understatement - a statement that makes something seem less important than it really is
