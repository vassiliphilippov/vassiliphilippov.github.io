pupilage

noun
period of tutelage
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpjuːpɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpjuːpɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period or condition of being a pupil or trainee under the instruction, training, or supervision of a teacher or mentor (especially in a profession such as law)

Examples

Legal pupilage
During his pupilage, he gained valuable experience working alongside experienced lawyers.
The artist's pupilage under a renowned painter shaped her unique style.
After completing her pupilage, she was ready to start her own practice.
After finishing law school, she began her pupilage at a prestigious chambers.
During his pupilage, he learned practical skills from experienced lawyers.
Completing a pupilage is required before becoming a qualified barrister in some countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apprenticeship - a period of learning a trade or skill under a skilled worker
  • antonym: independence - the state of being self-reliant and not under supervision
  • synonym: internship - a period of work experience offered by an organization
  • synonym: mentorship - guidance provided by a mentor
