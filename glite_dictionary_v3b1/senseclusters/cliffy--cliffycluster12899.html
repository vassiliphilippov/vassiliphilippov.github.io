cliffy

adjective
full of cliffs
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪfi / (AmE), / ˈklɪfi / (BrE)
having many cliffs or resembling a cliff, especially in being steep or rugged

Examples

A cliffy coastline
The cliffy coastline made hiking difficult for the group.
We admired the cliffy landscape as we drove along the winding road.
Birds nested on the cliffy edges overlooking the sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flat - having a level surface without slopes or cliffs
  • synonym: steep - having a sharp slope or incline
