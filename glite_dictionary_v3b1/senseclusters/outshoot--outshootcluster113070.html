outshoot

verb
to shoot better
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈʃuːt / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈʃuːt / (BrE)
to shoot farther, more accurately, or more often than someone else, especially in a competition

Examples

To outshoot a rival
In the final round, she managed to outshoot all her opponents and win the gold medal.
Despite his experience, he could not outshoot the younger competitor in the tournament.
The team practiced daily, hoping to outshoot their rivals in the upcoming match.
The basketball team managed to outshoot their rivals in the second half.
If you want to win, you will have to outshoot the competition.
Despite their efforts, they could not outshoot the defending champions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: miss - to fail to hit a target
  • synonym: outperform - to do better than someone else in a particular activity
  • synonym: outscore - to score more points than someone else
  • antonym: underperform - to do worse than expected or compared to others
