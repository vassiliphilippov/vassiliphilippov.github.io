clean

noun
weightlifting movement: barbell lifted to shoulders
7.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /kliːn/ (AmE), /kliːn/ (BrE)
(in weightlifting) a movement in which the barbell is lifted from the floor to the shoulders in a single motion, usually as the first part of a clean and jerk.

Examples

A heavy clean
During her workout, she increased the weight on her cleans for greater intensity.
He executed a perfect clean before moving on to the jerk portion of the lift.
The clean requires explosive power and precise technique to lift the bar to shoulder height.
