morose

adjective
gloomy or sullen in mood
Pronunciation: / məˈroʊs / (AmE), / məˈrəʊs / (BrE)
sullen, gloomy, or ill-tempered in mood or manner

Examples

A morose mood
He became noticeably morose after hearing the disappointing news.
Her morose expression made everyone around her feel uneasy.
The film’s protagonist is a morose and solitary young man.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: sullen - bad-tempered and sulky; gloomy
