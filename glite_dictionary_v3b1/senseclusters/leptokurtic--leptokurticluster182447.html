leptokurtic

adjective
sharp-peaked distribution
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlɛptəˈkɜrtɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɛptəˈkɜːtɪk / (BrE)
describing a statistical distribution that has a sharp peak and heavy tails compared to a normal distribution

Examples

A leptokurtic distribution
A leptokurtic distribution often indicates a higher probability of extreme outcomes.
The financial analyst noted that the returns were leptokurtic, with more frequent large deviations.
Researchers observed a leptokurtic pattern in the test scores, suggesting more students scored far from the average.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mesokurtic - describing a distribution with kurtosis similar to a normal distribution
  • antonym: platykurtic - describing a distribution with a flat peak and thin tails
