Psilotatae

noun
group of primitive vascular plants (whisk ferns)
Pronunciation: / saɪˈlɒtəˌtiː / (AmE), / saɪˈlɒtətiː / (BrE)
A division or group of primitive vascular plants, commonly known as whisk ferns, characterized by the absence of true roots, simple leaf structures, and rudimentary spore sacs.

Examples

Botanists often study Psilotatae to understand the evolution of plant root systems.
The Psilotatae represent some of the most ancient vascular plants still existing today.
Unlike most ferns, Psilotatae lack true leaves and roots, setting them apart in the plant kingdom.
