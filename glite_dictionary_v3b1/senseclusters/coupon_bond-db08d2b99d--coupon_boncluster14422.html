coupon bond

noun
bond with detachable interest coupons
7.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkuËpÉ‘n bÉ‘nd / (AmE), / ËˆkuËpÉ’n bÉ’nd / (BrE)
A bond issued with detachable coupons that must be presented to the issuer in order to receive interest payments.

Examples

A coupon bond payment
He invested in a coupon bond to ensure steady interest income over the years.
The company issued a coupon bond that pays interest every six months to those who present the coupons.
Unlike electronic bonds, a coupon bond requires physical presentation of coupons for payment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bearer bond - a bond not registered in the owner's name, payable to whoever holds it
