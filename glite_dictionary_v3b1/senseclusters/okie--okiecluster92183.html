okie

noun
person from Oklahoma
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊki / (AmE), / ˈəʊki / (BrE)
a person from the U.S. state of Oklahoma, especially a migrant from Oklahoma to California during the 1930s Dust Bowl, sometimes considered offensive

Examples

A proud okie
During the festival, a proud okie wore a shirt celebrating Tulsa's music scene.
Some people consider the term okie offensive because of its historical use during the Dust Bowl.
He introduced himself as an okie after moving to California in the 1930s.
