latent content

noun
hidden meaning in dreams (psychoanalysis)
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪtənt ˈkɑːntɛnt / (AmE), / ˈleɪtənt ˈkɒntɛnt / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) the hidden or unconscious meaning of a dream or fantasy, as opposed to its obvious, surface content.

Examples

The latent content of dreams
Analyzing the latent content can uncover issues not immediately obvious in the dream's storyline.
Freud believed that the latent content revealed a person's deepest, unconscious desires.
The psychiatrist helped her interpret the latent content of her recurring dreams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manifest content - the actual images, thoughts, and content of a dream as recalled by the dreamer
