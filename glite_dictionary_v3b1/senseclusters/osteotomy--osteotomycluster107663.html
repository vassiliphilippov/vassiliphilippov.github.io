osteotomy

noun
surgical cutting of bone
Pronunciation: / ˌɑstiˈɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌɒstiˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation in which a bone is cut to shorten, lengthen, or change its alignment

Examples

An osteotomy surgery
An osteotomy is sometimes performed to relieve pain caused by arthritis in the knee.
Orthopedic surgeons may recommend an osteotomy as an alternative to joint replacement in younger patients.
The patient underwent an osteotomy to correct the alignment of her leg bones.
