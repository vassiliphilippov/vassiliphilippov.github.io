depth

noun
lowest state, morally or emotionally
5.0 5.1
Pronunciation: / dɛpθ / (AmE), / dɛpθ / (BrE)
(usually plural) a very low or extreme state of morality, emotion, or condition; used to describe the worst level of something, especially bad behavior or misery.

Examples

Depths of despair
Many believed he could never recover from the depths to which he had fallen.
She found herself in the depths of despair after the loss of her job.
The politician's corruption had reached the depths of moral depravity.
