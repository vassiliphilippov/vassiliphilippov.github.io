chancel

noun
area around altar in a church
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.səl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.səl / (BrE)
The part of a church near the altar, reserved for the clergy and choir and often separated from the nave by a screen or railing.

Examples

The chancel area
Only clergy and choir members are permitted behind the railings in the chancel.
Stained glass windows bathed the chancel in colorful light during the evening mass.
The choir entered the chancel and took their seats before the service began.
