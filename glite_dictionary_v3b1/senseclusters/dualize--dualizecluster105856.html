dualize

verb
to make double
Pronunciation: / ˈduːəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something have two parts, aspects, or functions, especially by dividing or duplicating it

Examples

To dualize a road
The government decided to dualize the main road to reduce congestion.
Efforts to dualize the curriculum aimed to balance theory and practice.
The engineer proposed to dualize the power supply for greater reliability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divide - to separate into two or more parts
  • antonym: unify - to make or become united or whole
