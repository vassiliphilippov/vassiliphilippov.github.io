mortal

adjective
causing loss of divine grace (theology)
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtl̩ / (BrE)
(of a sin) causing the loss of divine grace or resulting in spiritual death.

Examples

Mortal sin
According to Catholic doctrine, a mortal sin must be confessed to receive forgiveness.
Committing a mortal sin, according to some beliefs, puts the soul in grave danger.
He worried that missing Mass would be considered a mortal offense by the Church.

Related Terms

  • antonym: venial - a sin that does not result in a complete separation from God
