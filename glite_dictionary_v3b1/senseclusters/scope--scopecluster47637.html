scope

noun
extent or range of something's influence

the extent or range covered by an activity, subject, or authority; the area within which something has effect or influence

Examples

Scope of the project
The scope of her research includes both ancient and modern history.
The committee debated whether the project fell outside their scope.
These rules are only valid within the scope of this agreement.

Synonyms

  • ambit
    8.1 0.29/m
    ambit is a formal word for the limits of an activity or influence, while scope is a general range of effect.
  • range
    2.9 74/m
    range is a broad term for limits or area across values or movement, while scope focuses on subject or authority.
  • compass
    6.0 2.50/m
    compass is a slightly literary term for the full range of ability or influence, while scope names practical limits.
  • reach
    3.0 15/m
    reach is the distance or power to affect people or places, while scope is the area something covers.
  • purview
    8.0 1.89/m
    purview is the range of authority or responsibility for a person or office, while scope covers general subject areas.
  • extent
    2.3 67/m
    extent is the degree or amount to which something happens or is true, while scope describes range or influence.
