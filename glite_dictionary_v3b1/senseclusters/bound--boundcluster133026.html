bound

verb
form the boundary of; be adjacent to
7.9 4.3
Pronunciation: /baʊnd/ (AmE), /baʊnd/ (BrE)
to form the boundary of something or to restrict or confine within limits

Examples

Mountains bound the country
The country is bounded on three sides by mountains.
The river bounds the property on the north side.
Their land is bounded by a forest to the east and a road to the west.
Laws bound how much pollution factories can emit into the air.
The city decided to bound the number of taxis allowed on the streets.
We must bound our spending if we want to save for a new house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: border - to form the edge or boundary of something
  • synonym: enclose - to surround or close off on all sides
  • synonym: limit - to restrict the size, amount, or range of something
  • synonym: restrict - to confine within limits; to limit access or amount
