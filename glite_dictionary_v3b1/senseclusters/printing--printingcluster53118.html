printing

noun
making 3D objects by layering material
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of producing three-dimensional objects by additive manufacturing, creating successive layers of material from a digital design (also called 3D printing).

Examples

3D printing
The startup uses printing to produce custom prosthetic parts at a lower cost.
Engineers tested new materials suitable for printing complex aerospace components under high stress.
As printing technology improves, schools can teach students design and rapid prototyping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: additive manufacturing - the technical term for making objects by adding material layer by layer; often used synonymously with 3D printing
  • antonym: subtractive manufacturing - a manufacturing approach that creates parts by removing material (for example, milling or cutting); often contrasted with additive methods
