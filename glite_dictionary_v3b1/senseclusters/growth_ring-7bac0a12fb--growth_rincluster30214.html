growth ring

noun
annual layer in wood; tree ring
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊθ rɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡrəʊθ rɪŋ / (BrE)
a layer of wood produced during a single year of growth in a tree or shrub, visible as a ring in the cross-section of the trunk or branch

Examples

A growth ring layer
A narrow growth ring usually indicates a year of drought or poor growing conditions.
Scientists examine growth rings to study historic climate changes.
The age of a tree can be determined by counting its growth rings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tree ring - a concentric ring representing a year of growth in the wood of a tree
