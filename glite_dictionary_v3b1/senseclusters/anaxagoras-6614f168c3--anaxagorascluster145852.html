Anaxagoras

proper noun
presocratic Athenian philosopher
Pronunciation: / ˌænəkˈsæɡərəs / (AmE), / ˌanəkˈsaɡərəs / (BrE)
(c. 500–428 BC) A presocratic Athenian philosopher who argued that everything is composed of infinitely divisible small particles arranged by an eternal intelligence.

Examples

Anaxagoras philosopher
Anaxagoras was among the first philosophers to bring scientific thinking to Athens.
In his lectures, the professor often refers to Anaxagoras and his concept of a cosmic mind.
The theories of Anaxagoras influenced later philosophers such as Socrates and Plato.
