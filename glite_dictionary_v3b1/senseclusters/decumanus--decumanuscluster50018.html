decumanus

noun
Roman main street
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkjuˈmeɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌdɛkjuˈmɑːnəs / (BrE)
the main east-west street in a Roman city or military camp

Examples

The decumanus street
Archaeologists traced the ancient decumanus beneath the modern city streets.
Shops and public buildings lined the decumanus in many Roman towns.
The decumanus intersected with the cardo at the city center.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cardo - the main north-south street in a Roman city or camp
