uncast

adjective
not formed or chosen
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈkæst / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈkɑːst / (BrE)
not formed by casting or not chosen for a role in a play or film

Examples

An uncast statue
The bronze sculpture remained uncast because the artist could not afford the materials.
Several important roles in the production were still uncast just weeks before opening night.
The jewelry design was left uncast until the client made a final decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cast - chosen for a role in a play or film
  • synonym: unselected - not chosen or picked for something
