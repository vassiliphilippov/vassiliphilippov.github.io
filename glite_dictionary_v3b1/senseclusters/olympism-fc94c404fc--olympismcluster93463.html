Olympism

noun
ideals of the Olympic Games
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪmˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈlɪm.pɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the philosophy and ideals of the Olympic Games, emphasizing fair play, respect, and the harmonious development of people through sport.

Examples

Olympism philosophy
Educational programs around the world promote Olympism among young students.
Her speech at the opening ceremony highlighted the importance of Olympism in uniting nations through sport.
The values of Olympism encourage athletes to respect their opponents and play fair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Olympic spirit - the attitude of goodwill and fair play inspired by the Olympic Games
