Spanish Sahara

proper noun
former Spanish territory in northwest Africa
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈspæn.ɪʃ sə.ˈhɑːr.ə/ /; / /ˈspæn.ɪʃ sə.ˈhær.ə/ (AmE), /ˈspæn.ɪʃ sə.ˈhɑː.rə/ (BrE)
a former territory in northwestern Africa, known for its phosphate deposits, which was a Spanish colony before coming under Moroccan control in 1976 and commonly referred to today as Western Sahara.

Examples

The Spanish Sahara territory
After Spain withdrew from the Spanish Sahara, the territory became the subject of dispute between Morocco and local groups.
Maps from the early 20th century still label the region as Spanish Sahara.
The rich phosphate resources of the Spanish Sahara have attracted international interest for decades.
