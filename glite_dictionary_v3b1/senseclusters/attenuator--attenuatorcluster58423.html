attenuator

noun
device that reduces signal strength
9.8 7.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈten.juː.eɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ə.ˈten.juː.eɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a device used to reduce the strength of an electrical signal without significantly distorting its waveform

Examples

An attenuator device
An attenuator is often used to protect sensitive electronic equipment from strong input signals.
Damaged wiring can reduce the effectiveness of an attenuator in maintaining balanced signal levels.
The technician installed an attenuator to control the volume level in the audio system.
