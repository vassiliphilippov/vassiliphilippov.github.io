highly active antiretroviral therapy

noun
combination treatment for HIV/AIDS
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪli ˈæktɪv ˌæntaɪrɪˌtroʊˈvaɪrəl ˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˈhaɪli ˈæktɪv ˌæntaɪrɪˌtrəʊˈvaɪrəl ˈθerəpi / (BrE)
a treatment for HIV/AIDS using a combination of protease inhibitors and reverse transcriptase inhibitors to reduce the virus in the body

Examples

Highly active antiretroviral therapy treatment
After testing positive for HIV, she began highly active antiretroviral therapy to manage the infection.
Doctors recommend starting highly active antiretroviral therapy as soon as possible after diagnosis.
Thanks to highly active antiretroviral therapy, many people with HIV are living longer, healthier lives.
