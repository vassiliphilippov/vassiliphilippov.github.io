revisionary

adjective
able to review decisions
7.5
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
having the authority or power to review and change decisions, laws, or official acts, especially in legal or administrative contexts

Examples

Revisionary court powers
The court has revisionary powers to reconsider previous judgments if new evidence emerges.
A revisionary committee was established to review outdated regulations and propose changes.
Only a revisionary authority can overturn the original decision made by the board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appellate - having the power to review and change decisions of a lower court
  • antonym: final - not subject to further review or change
