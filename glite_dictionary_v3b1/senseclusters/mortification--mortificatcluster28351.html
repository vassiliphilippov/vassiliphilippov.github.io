(Christianity) The act of suppressing bodily desires or passions, especially by self-denial, privation, or inflicting discomfort or pain on oneself as a religious discipline.

Examples

Religious mortification
In medieval times, physical mortification was seen as a means to overcome worldly temptations.
Some saints are known for their rigorous mortification of the flesh, seeking holiness through self-denial.
The monk practiced mortification through fasting and sleep deprivation as part of his spiritual discipline.
