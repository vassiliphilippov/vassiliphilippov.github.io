impenetrable

adjective
very dense, allowing no light through
4.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpen.ə.trə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpen.ɪ.trə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpen.ə.trə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpen.ɪ.trə.bəl/ (BrE)
so dense that little or no light can pass through; impossible to see through

Examples

Impenetrable forest
An impenetrable forest blocked out the sunlight, casting deep shadows on the ground.
The smoke from the fire formed an impenetrable barrier that made it impossible to find the exit.
The thick morning fog was so impenetrable that we could barely see the street ahead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • antonym: translucent - allowing light, but not detailed shapes, to pass through
  • antonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so objects behind can be seen clearly
