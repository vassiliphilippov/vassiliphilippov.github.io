wild pansy

noun
European wildflower, progenitor of garden pansies
6.9
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈpænzi / (AmE), / waɪld ˈpænzi / (BrE)
a small European wildflower (Viola tricolor), often called heartsease, from which most garden pansies are derived

Examples

The wild pansy flower
Gardeners value the wild pansy for its delicate colors and historical significance.
Many cultivated pansies can trace their origin back to the wild pansy.
The wild pansy blooms in European meadows from spring to early summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heartsease - another common name for wild pansy
  • degree higher: pansy - a cultivated garden flower derived from the wild pansy
