caudillo

noun
authoritarian leader
9.9
Pronunciation: / kaʊˈdiːjoʊ / (AmE), / kaʊˈdiːjəʊ / (BrE)
a military or political leader, especially one who rules with strong personal or authoritarian power in a Spanish-speaking country

Examples

A caudillo leader
The caudillo maintained strict control over the country's military and government.
Many people feared the return of a caudillo after years of democracy.
Under the rule of the caudillo, opposition parties were banned and dissent was suppressed.
Francisco Franco was known as the caudillo of Spain during his long rule.
The general declared himself caudillo after seizing power in the coup.
In some countries, the term caudillo is used to describe a strongman leader.
The caudillo maintained strict control over the country's government for decades.
Many people feared the power of the caudillo during that turbulent period.
After the revolution, the region was ruled by a new caudillo who promised stability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: democrat - a person who supports democracy or has democratic views
  • synonym: dictator - a ruler with total power over a country, typically one who has obtained control by force
  • synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
