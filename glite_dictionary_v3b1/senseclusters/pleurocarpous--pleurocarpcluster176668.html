pleurocarpous

adjective
having archegonia on lateral branches (of mosses)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / pl ä…πo äÀàk…ëÀêrp…ôs / (AmE), / pl ä…ô.r…ôÀàk…ëÀê.p…ôs / (BrE)
(of mosses) having the female reproductive organs (archegonia) on short side branches rather than at the tip of the main stem.

Examples

pleurocarpous moss growth
A pleurocarpous growth form allows these mosses to cover large areas like a green carpet.
Many moss species in temperate forests are pleurocarpous, spreading extensively along the soil surface.
Unlike acrocarpous mosses, the pleurocarpous types have reproductive organs on lateral branches.

Related Terms
