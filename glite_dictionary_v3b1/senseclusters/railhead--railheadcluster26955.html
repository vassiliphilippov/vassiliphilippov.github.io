railhead

noun
military supply depot at end of a railway
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a railroad depot in a military area where supplies and equipment are unloaded for distribution

Examples

Military railhead
Supplies were unloaded at the railhead before being transported by truck to the troops.
The army established a railhead near the front lines to speed up the delivery of munitions.
The new railhead became the focal point for all logistical operations in the region.
