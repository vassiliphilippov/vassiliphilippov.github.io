blank check

noun
signed check with no amount filled in
4.8
Pronunciation: / blæŋk tʃɛk / (AmE), / blæŋk tʃɛk / (BrE)
A check that has been signed but has the amount left blank, allowing the holder to fill in any value.

Examples

Sign a blank check
It's risky to give someone a blank check because they can write any amount.
She accidentally left a blank check in her notebook.
The thief cashed the blank check that he found in the drawer.
He accidentally left a blank cheque on his desk, which could have been risky.
Never sign a blank cheque unless you completely trust the person you are giving it to.
The company gave the negotiators a blank cheque to finalize the deal as they saw fit.
