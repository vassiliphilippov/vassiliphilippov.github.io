total parenteral nutrition

noun
intravenous feeding for complete nutrition
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊ.t̬əl pəˌrɛn.təˈræl nuˈtrɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.təl pəˌrɛn.təˈræl njuːˈtrɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the medical practice of supplying all necessary nutrients to a patient intravenously, bypassing the digestive system entirely

Examples

Total parenteral nutrition therapy
Doctors decided that total parenteral nutrition was necessary due to severe gastrointestinal disease.
Many burn victims depend on total parenteral nutrition during the initial stages of recovery.
The patient received total parenteral nutrition after surgery because he could not eat by mouth.
