Westphalia

proper noun
Napoleonic German kingdom
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɛstˈfeɪliə / (AmE), / wɛstˈfeɪliə / (BrE)
a short-lived kingdom (1807–1813) in central Germany created by Napoleon as a client state of the French Empire

Examples

Kingdom of Westphalia
Napoleon appointed his brother as king of Westphalia after conquering the region.
The Kingdom of Westphalia existed for only a few years before being dissolved in 1813.
Many reforms were introduced in Westphalia to modernize its administration under French influence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kingdom of Saxony - a German kingdom established by Napoleon in 1806
  • antonym: Prussia - a major German kingdom and state in central Europe
