Siamese

adjective
of a twin neural-network architecture
Pronunciation: / saɪəˈmiz / (AmE), / saɪəˈmiːz / (BrE)
(in machine learning) of or relating to a Siamese network: a neural-network architecture consisting of two or more identical subnetworks with shared weights, used to compare inputs or learn similarity between them.

Examples

Siamese network
Researchers trained a Siamese network to compare face images and estimate similarity scores for verification.
The team used a Siamese architecture when labelled pairs were available but individual class labels were scarce.
Fine-tuning the Siamese model improved one-shot learning performance on the new object recognition task.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neural network - a computing model made of interconnected nodes (neurons) used in machine learning; Siamese networks are a specific kind.
