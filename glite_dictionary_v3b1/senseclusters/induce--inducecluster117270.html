induce

verb
persuade or influence to do something
4.5 4.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdus / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to persuade or influence someone to do something; to cause someone to act in a particular way

Examples

To induce someone to act
Nothing could induce him to change his mind about the decision.
She was induced to accept the offer despite her initial hesitation.
The promise of a bonus induced several employees to work overtime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular action
  • synonym: persuade - to cause someone to do something through reasoning or argument
