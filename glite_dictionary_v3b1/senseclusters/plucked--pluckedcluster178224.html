plucked

adjective
sounded by pulling strings with fingers or plectrum
4.2 4.2
Pronunciation: /plʌkt/ (AmE), /plʌkt/ (BrE)
(of a stringed instrument or its sound) produced by pulling and releasing the strings with the fingers or a plectrum, rather than by bowing or striking.

Examples

Plucked guitar strings
Many traditional Asian instruments produce music using plucked techniques.
She preferred the plucked sound of the harp to the bowed tones of the violin.
The guitar belongs to the family of plucked string instruments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bowed - (of a stringed instrument or sound) produced by drawing a bow across the strings
