heteroploid

noun
organism or cell with an irregular chromosome number
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛt̬ɚˌplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərəʊˌplɔɪd / (BrE)
(genetics) an organism or cell having a chromosome number that is not an exact multiple of the haploid number for that species.

Examples

A heteroploid organism
A heteroploid can result from irregular cell division during reproduction.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of heteroploid strains in the laboratory.
Some cancer cells are classified as heteroploid due to their abnormal chromosome numbers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: euploid - organism or cell with a chromosome number that is an exact multiple of the haploid number
