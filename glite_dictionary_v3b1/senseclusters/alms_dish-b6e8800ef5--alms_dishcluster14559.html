alms dish

noun
dish for collecting church offerings
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɑːlmz dɪʃ / (AmE), / ɑːlmz dɪʃ / (BrE)
a shallow dish or plate used for collecting offerings from a congregation, typically during a religious service

Examples

A alms dish on altar
A silver alms dish was displayed on the altar during the special service.
The alms dish was filled with coins and bills by the end of the ceremony.
The usher passed the alms dish along the pews to collect donations.
