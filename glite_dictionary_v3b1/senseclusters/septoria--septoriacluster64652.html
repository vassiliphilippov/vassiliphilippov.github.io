septoria

noun
plant-pathogenic fungus
Pronunciation: / sɛpˈtɔːriə / (AmE), / sɛpˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi that causes leaf spot diseases in plants, especially affecting crops like wheat and tomatoes

Examples

Septoria fungus infection
Farmers are concerned about septoria spreading quickly through their wheat fields this year.
The tomato plants showed signs of septoria infection, with brown spots appearing on the leaves.
Effective crop rotation can help reduce the risk of septoria in future harvests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blight - a plant disease that causes damage or death to crops
  • synonym: fungus - an organism such as a mold, yeast, or mushroom
