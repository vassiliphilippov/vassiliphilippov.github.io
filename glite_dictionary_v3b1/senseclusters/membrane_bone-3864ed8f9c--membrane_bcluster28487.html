membrane bone

noun
a bone formed in membrane, not cartilage
6.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmem.breɪn boʊn/ (AmE), /ˈmem.breɪn bəʊn/ (BrE)
a bone that develops within membranous tissue rather than from cartilage, such as the clavicle or skull bones

Examples

The membrane bone skull
Certain skull bones, like the parietal bone, are classified as membrane bones.
The clavicle is a membrane bone that forms directly within connective tissue.
Unlike long bones, a membrane bone does not develop from cartilage.
