mechanical energy

noun
energy of motion and position (physics)
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /mək.ˈæn.ɪ.kəl ˈen.ər.dʒiː/ (AmE), /mək.ˈæn.ɪ.kəl ˈen.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
Energy possessed by an object due to its motion or position, typically as the sum of kinetic energy and potential energy.

Examples

Mechanical energy of motion
A windmill captures the wind's kinetic energy and turns it into mechanical energy to grind grain.
The roller coaster converts gravitational potential into mechanical energy as it speeds down the track.
The total mechanical energy of a pendulum is conserved if there is no friction.
