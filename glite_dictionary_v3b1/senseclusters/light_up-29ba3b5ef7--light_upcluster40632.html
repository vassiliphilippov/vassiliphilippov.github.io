light up

verb
begin to burn brightly
Pronunciation: / laɪt ʌp /; / laɪt ˈʌp / (AmE), / laɪt ʌp /; / laɪt ˈʌp / (BrE)
to become bright or to make something bright, either by shining light or by burning

Examples

To light up a room
He stood back as the campfire lit up the surrounding darkness.
The logs in the fireplace lit up as soon as I struck the match.
The streetlights light up the entire avenue at dusk.
As soon as the storm ended, the sun lit up the entire valley.
She used a match to light up the old lantern in the cabin.
The fireworks lit up the night sky for miles around.
Every evening the city landmarks light up with colorful decorations.
She pressed the button to light up the sign outside the store.
The candles light up the whole room during power outages.
