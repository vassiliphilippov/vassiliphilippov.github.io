hunchback

noun
person with a hunched back
5.4 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhʌntʃ.bæk/ (AmE), /ˈhʌntʃ.bæk/ (BrE)
a person with a hunched or rounded back caused by abnormal curvature of the spine, or the condition itself

Examples

A hunchback person
Doctors explained the cause of the boy’s hunchback was a severe spinal disorder.
She wrote a story about a hunchback who overcame prejudice to achieve his dreams.
The old man was a well-known hunchback in the village, walking slowly down the cobblestone streets.
Early intervention and therapy can sometimes help prevent a hunchback in children with spinal disorders.
The doctor explained that the patient's hunchback was caused by a severe form of curvature in the spine.
Victorian literature often depicted characters with a hunchback as outcasts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kyphosis - medical term for excessive curvature of the spine
  • antonym: lordosis - inward curvature of the spine, opposite of kyphosis
  • antonym: straightback - person or posture with an unnaturally straight back
