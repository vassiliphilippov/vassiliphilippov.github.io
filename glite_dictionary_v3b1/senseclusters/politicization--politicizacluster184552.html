politicization

noun
becoming political
Pronunciation: / pəˌlɪtɪsəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌlɪtɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something political or subject to political influence or control

Examples

The politicization of education
The politicization of education has led to heated debates about curriculum changes.
Some worry that the politicization of science undermines public trust in research.
After the scandal, there was a noticeable politicization of community organizations.
The politicization of the media has made it difficult to find unbiased news sources.
Many people are concerned about the politicization of scientific research in recent years.
The politicization of education policies often leads to heated debates in parliament.
The politicization of education has led to heated debates among parents and teachers.
Many worry about the politicization of public health decisions during a crisis.
Some scientists argue that the politicization of climate change hinders effective action.

  • synonym: politicise - to make something political or influenced by politics
  • synonym: politicize - to make something political or influenced by politics
