tandem

adverb
one behind the other; sequentially
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtændəm / (AmE), / ˈtændəm / (BrE)
(adverb) In a line, one behind the other; together or in sequence.

Examples

Horses move tandem
Horses were harnessed tandem to pull the carriage through the field.
The trucks moved tandem along the highway during the convoy.
The two cyclists rode tandem down the narrow country lane.

Related Terms

  • antonym: side by side - positioned next to each other, not in sequence
