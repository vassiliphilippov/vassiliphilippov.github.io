eyeshine

noun
reflected eye glow
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈaɪʃaɪn / (BrE)
a visible glow or bright reflection seen in the eyes of animals (or sometimes people) when light shines on them, such as in photographs or at night

Examples

Eyeshine in animals
The cat's eyeshine appeared green in the photograph taken at night.
Photographers often use special techniques to reduce eyeshine in portraits.
The flash caused noticeable eyeshine in everyone's eyes in the group photo.
The eyeshine of the raccoon was easy to spot in the beam of the flashlight.
Photographers often capture eyeshine when taking pictures of animals at night.
We saw the eyeshine of several deer as we drove through the forest after dark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: red-eye - the red reflection in eyes in flash photography
  • synonym: reflection - the return of light from a surface
