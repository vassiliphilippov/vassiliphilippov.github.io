economic libertarian

noun
advocate of minimal government in economics
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌiː.kəˈnɑː.mɪk ˌlɪ.bərˈtɛː.ri.ən / (AmE), / ˌiː.kəˈnɒm.ɪk ˌlɪ.bəˈtɛə.ri.ən / (BrE)
A person who believes in maximizing individual economic freedom and minimizing government intervention in the economy.

Examples

Economic libertarian beliefs
As an economic libertarian, she opposes most forms of government regulation in business.
He identifies as an economic libertarian but supports some social safety nets.
The candidate's economic libertarian views appeal to voters who favor free markets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: libertarian - a person who believes in free will and minimal state intervention
