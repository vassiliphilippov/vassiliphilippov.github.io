ostracon

noun
ancient Greek ballot
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.strə.kən/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.strə.kən/ (BrE)
a piece of pottery or stone used as a ballot in ancient Greek voting, especially to vote for someone's exile

Examples

Voting ostracon
The archaeologist discovered an ostracon inscribed with the name of a famous Athenian.
Citizens wrote the names of those they wished to exile on an ostracon during the assembly.
Several ostraca were found at the site, providing insight into ancient Greek democracy.
