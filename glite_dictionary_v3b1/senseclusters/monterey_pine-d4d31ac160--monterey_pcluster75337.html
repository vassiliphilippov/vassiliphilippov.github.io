Monterey pine

noun
a species of tall California pine tree
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəˈteɪ paɪn / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈteɪ paɪn / (BrE)
A tall pine tree native to California, with long needles grouped in clusters of three, a dense rounded crown, and deeply fissured dark brown bark. It is commonly grown for timber and as an ornamental tree in many parts of the world.

Examples

The Monterey pine tree
Researchers are studying the decline of the native Monterey pine groves in California.
Several Monterey pines were planted along the coastal highway to prevent soil erosion.
The Monterey pine is widely planted throughout Australia and New Zealand for its fast growth.
