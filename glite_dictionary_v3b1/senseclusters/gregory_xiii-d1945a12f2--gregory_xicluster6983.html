Gregory XIII

proper noun
Pope who introduced the Gregorian calendar
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɛɡəri ˌθɜrˈtiːnθ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛɡəri ˌθɜːˈtiːnθ / (BrE)
(1502–1585) Pope from 1572 to 1585, best known for sponsoring the reform of the calendar now known as the Gregorian calendar.

Examples

Gregory XIII pope
Gregory XIII's introduction of the Gregorian calendar corrected inaccuracies in the old Julian system.
Many countries adopted the new calendar established under Gregory XIII only centuries later.
The papacy of Gregory XIII was marked by significant reforms and the promotion of Catholic education.
