Danelaw

noun
era of Danish rule
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪnˌlɔ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnlɔː / (BrE)
the region of England in the early Middle Ages that was under Danish rule or influence, where Danish laws and customs were established

Examples

The Danelaw region
The region known as the Danelaw was heavily influenced by Norse customs and language.
Many English towns in the Danelaw still have names of Scandinavian origin.
After the fall of the Danelaw, English kings regained control over the northern territories.
The Danelaw covered much of northern and eastern England during the 9th and 10th centuries.
Many English place names in the Danelaw region have Scandinavian origins.
The legal system in the Danelaw differed from that of the Anglo-Saxon kingdoms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Anglo-Saxon England - the period of English history before the Norman Conquest
  • synonym: Viking Age - the period of Scandinavian exploration and settlement from the late 8th to 11th centuries
  • antonym: Wessex - an Anglo-Saxon kingdom in southern England
