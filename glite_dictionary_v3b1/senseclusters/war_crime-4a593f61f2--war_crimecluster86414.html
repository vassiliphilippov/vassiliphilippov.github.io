war crime

noun
serious crime under the laws of war
3.1
Pronunciation: / wɔr kraɪm / (AmE), / wɔː kraɪm / (BrE)
a serious violation of the laws or customs of war, such as killing civilians or prisoners, considered a criminal offense under international law

Examples

A war crime trial
Deliberate destruction of cultural heritage can constitute a war crime under international law.
She dedicated her career to investigating war crimes committed during the conflict.
The court found the general guilty of a war crime for ordering the attack on civilians.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crime against humanity - a deliberate act, typically as part of a systematic campaign, that causes human suffering or death on a large scale
