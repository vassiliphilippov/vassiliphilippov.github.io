mollifier

noun
linguistic mitigator
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑlɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a word or expression used to soften the impact or harshness of a statement

Examples

A mollifier word
The word 'somewhat' acted as a mollifier in her otherwise blunt comment.
Writers often use a mollifier to make criticism sound less harsh.
Adding a mollifier can help maintain politeness in sensitive discussions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intensifier - a word that increases the force of a statement
  • synonym: mitigator - something that lessens the severity of something
