propulsion

noun
force that drives something forward
Pronunciation: / prəˈpʌlʃən / (AmE), / prəˈpʌlʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of driving or pushing something forward; a force that moves something forward

Examples

Rocket propulsion
Rocket propulsion allows spacecraft to escape the Earth's atmosphere.
The boat moved through the water using jet propulsion.
Wind provided the main source of propulsion for sailing ships.
Birds use wing movement as their main source of propulsion during flight.
Modern ships rely on advanced propulsion technologies for greater efficiency.
The rocket's propulsion system was powerful enough to escape Earth's gravity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retardation - the act of slowing down or delaying movement
  • synonym: thrust - the force that pushes something forward, especially in aviation and space travel
