qualmish

adjective
uneasy or doubtful
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːlmɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlmɪʃ / (BrE)
feeling uneasy, doubtful, or having moral misgivings about an action or decision

Examples

Qualmish about cheating
She felt qualmish about lying to her friend, even though it seemed harmless.
After making the decision, he grew qualmish and wondered if it was the right thing to do.
The student was qualmish about submitting work that was not entirely his own.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confident - feeling sure about what you are doing
  • synonym: uneasy - feeling worried or uncomfortable
