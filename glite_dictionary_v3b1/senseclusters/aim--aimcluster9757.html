aim

verb
direct or intend toward a goal
Pronunciation: / eɪm / (AmE), / eɪm / (BrE)
to direct, design, or intend something (such as remarks, products, or actions) toward a particular goal, group, or purpose

Examples

To aim a message
He aimed his remarks at the entire audience, hoping to inspire everyone.
She aimed to finish the project before the deadline.
The company is aiming its new product line at young professionals.
The new advertisement campaign is aimed at young adults.
The workshop was aimed at improving communication among employees.
This class is aimed specifically at beginners with no prior experience.
He enjoys aiming witty comments at his colleagues during meetings.
She chose her words carefully to aim her criticism at the right person.
The remarks were clearly aimed at the opposition party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: address - to direct a speech, comment, or attention to someone
  • synonym: direct - to cause to move or be aimed toward a position or goal
  • synonym: intend - to have a plan or purpose in mind; to mean
  • synonym: target - to select as the object of an action or intention
