Babinski reflex

noun
upward toe reflex when foot is stroked
6.8
Pronunciation: / bəˈbɪnski ˈriːˌflɛks / (AmE), / bəˈbɪnski ˈriːflɛks / (BrE)
an involuntary upward extension of the toes when the sole of the foot is stroked, normal in infants but may indicate nervous system damage in older children or adults

Examples

Positive Babinski reflex
In adults, a positive Babinski reflex can signal a problem with the central nervous system.
The doctor checked for the Babinski reflex to assess the patient's neurological function.
The presence of the Babinski reflex is normal in babies under two years old.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plantar reflex - flexion of the toes when the sole is stroked, considered the normal response in adults
