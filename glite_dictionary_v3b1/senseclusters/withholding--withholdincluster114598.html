withholding

noun
income tax deducted from wages by employer
9.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /wɪð.ˈhoʊl.dɪŋ/ /; / /wɪθ.ˈhoʊl.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /wɪð.ˈhəʊl.dɪŋ/ /; / /wɪθ.ˈhəʊl.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the amount of money, usually tax, taken out of an employee's pay by an employer and sent directly to the government

Examples

Tax withholding
If your withholding is too low, you might owe taxes at the end of the year.
My pay stub shows how much federal withholding was taken out this pay period.
The company is responsible for calculating the correct amount of withholding for each worker.
She checked her pay stub to see how much withholding had reduced her net pay.
The government updated the withholding tables to reflect new tax rates.
Your employer is responsible for withholding income taxes from your paycheck.
