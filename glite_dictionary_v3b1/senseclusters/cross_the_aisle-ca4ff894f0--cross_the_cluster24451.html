cross the aisle

verb
cooperate with opposing political party
6.0
Pronunciation: / krɑs ði ˈaɪl / (AmE), / krɒs ði ˈaɪl / (BrE)
(of a politician) to cooperate with or show support for members of the opposing political party, especially by voting with them or working together on legislation.

Examples

To cross the aisle in politics
In order to pass the healthcare reform, several lawmakers agreed to cross the aisle.
She gained respect for her willingness to cross the aisle when necessary.
The senator chose to cross the aisle and support the education bill proposed by the other party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reach across the aisle - to attempt to cooperate with the opposing party; synonym for political collaboration
