lotus tree

noun
Mediterranean shrubby deciduous tree
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊtəs triː / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs triː / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or tree of the Mediterranean region, historically associated with ancient myths and sometimes identified as the jujube or Ziziphus lotus.

Examples

The lotus tree plant
Botanists still debate the true identity of the mythical lotus tree mentioned in ancient texts.
The ancient Greeks believed that eating the fruit of the lotus tree brought forgetfulness.
We saw a lotus tree growing near the edge of the village, its fruit hanging in clusters.
