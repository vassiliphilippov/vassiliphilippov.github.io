precharge

noun
advance payment
7.7
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈtʃɑːrdʒ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈtʃɑːrdʒ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈtʃɑːdʒ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈtʃɑːdʒ/ (BrE)
a charge or payment made in advance, often used to verify a payment method before a transaction is completed

Examples

Precharge on a card
The hotel placed a precharge on my credit card to confirm my reservation.
A small precharge appeared on my statement before the actual purchase was processed.
Some online services use a precharge to check if your payment method is valid.

Related Terms

  • antonym: refund - a repayment of money, especially for returned goods or overpayment
