literalist

noun
strict interpreter
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬ərəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who interprets words, rules, or texts in a strict and exact way, without considering figurative or alternative meanings

Examples

A strict literalist
As a literalist, she refused to accept any interpretation beyond the exact words of the contract.
The teacher explained that a literalist reading of the poem would miss its deeper meaning.
Some legal scholars are known as literalists because they strictly follow the written law.
As a literalist, he often misunderstood jokes and metaphors.
The literalist approach to the text ignores its symbolic meanings.
Some critics argue that a literalist reading of the law can lead to unfair outcomes.
As a literalist, he often misunderstood jokes that relied on wordplay.
The debate included a literalist who insisted on the exact meaning of every statement.
Teachers sometimes find it challenging to teach poetry to a literalist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: figurativist - a person who interprets language in a figurative or metaphorical way
  • antonym: flexible - willing to change or adapt; not strict
  • synonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
  • synonym: realist - a person who accepts things as they are and deals with them practically
