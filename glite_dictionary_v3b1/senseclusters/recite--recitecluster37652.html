recite

verb
specify or list items individually
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈsaɪt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈsaɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈsaɪt/ /; / /rə.ˈsaɪt/ (BrE)
to list or give a detailed account of items, facts, or events, often one after another

Examples

To recite the facts
During her speech, she recited every reason for her decision, leaving nothing unsaid.
He calmly recited the names of each team member who contributed to the project.
The teacher recited all the steps required to complete the assignment.
During the meeting, he recited a long list of their recent achievements.
She could recite every detail of the trip from memory.
The witness was asked to recite exactly what occurred that night.
