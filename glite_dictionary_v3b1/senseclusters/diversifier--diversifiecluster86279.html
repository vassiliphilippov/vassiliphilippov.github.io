diversifier

noun
one who adds variety
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɜːrsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person or thing that adds variety, especially to reduce risk or increase balance in a group or collection

Examples

A diversifier in investments
Adding international stocks can be a good diversifier in your investment portfolio.
A skilled diversifier introduces new products to keep the business competitive.
Planting different crops acts as a natural diversifier for the farm's ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrator - a person or thing that focuses on a single area or type
  • synonym: variety - the quality or state of being different or diverse
