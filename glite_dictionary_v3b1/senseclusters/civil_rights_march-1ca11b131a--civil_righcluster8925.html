civil rights march

noun
public protest for civil rights
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts mɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌsɪvəl ˈraɪts mɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a public protest or demonstration advocating for the protection or expansion of civil rights, especially racial equality

Examples

A civil rights march
Dr. King led a famous civil rights march from Selma to Montgomery in 1965.
Many people joined the civil rights march to demand equal voting rights.
The televised images of the civil rights march drew national attention to the struggle for justice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protest march - a public procession expressing objection to something
