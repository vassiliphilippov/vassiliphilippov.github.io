hold on

verb
retain possession; keep
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒn / (BrE)
to keep or retain possession of something

Examples

To hold on to memories
Despite many offers to buy them, she decided to hold on to her childhood books.
It's wise to hold on to your receipts just in case you need to return an item.
When moving to a new apartment, he wanted to hold on to his grandmother's old chair.
