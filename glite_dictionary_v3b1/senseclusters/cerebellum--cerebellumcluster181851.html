cerebellum

noun
part of the brain controlling movement and balance
4.2 6.1
Pronunciation: /se.rə.ˈbe.ləm/ /; / /se.rɪ.ˈbe.ləm/ (AmE), /se.rɪ.ˈbe.ləm/ (BrE)
the part of the brain at the back of the skull in vertebrates, responsible for coordinating movement and balance

Examples

The cerebellum controls movement
Damage to the cerebellum can affect coordination and balance.
Scientists studied how the cerebellum interacts with other brain regions during learning tasks.
The cerebellum plays a key role in fine-tuning voluntary movements.
