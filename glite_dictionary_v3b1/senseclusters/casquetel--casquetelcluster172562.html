casquetel

noun
light open helmet (historical)
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkæs.kəˈtɛl / (AmE), / ˌkæs.kwɪˈtɛl / (BrE)
A light, open helmet without a visor or beaver, used especially in the 16th and 17th centuries.

Examples

A casquetel helmet
Museums often display the casquetel alongside other historical armor from the Renaissance period.
The soldier wore a casquetel to keep his head protected during training exercises.
Unlike heavier helmets, the casquetel provided more comfort and better visibility.
