corroding

noun
chemical erosion of materials
6.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
The gradual destruction or wearing away of a substance, especially metal, by chemical action.

Examples

Metal corroding
Scientists study the corroding of metal structures exposed to saltwater environments.
The corroding of the pipes led to a major leak in the factory.
The bridge was closed due to extensive corroding of its support beams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrosion - the process of corroding, especially of metals
