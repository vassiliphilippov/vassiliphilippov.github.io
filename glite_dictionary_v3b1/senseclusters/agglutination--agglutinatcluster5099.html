agglutination

noun
word formation by joining morphemes
Pronunciation: / əˌɡluː.t̬əˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌɡluː.tɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process in linguistics of forming words by combining morphemes, each retaining its original form and meaning

Examples

Word agglutination
In agglutination, morphemes retain their shape and meaning as they are joined together.
The study of agglutination reveals how different languages construct complex meanings efficiently.
Turkish is often cited as an example of a language that uses agglutination to form words.
