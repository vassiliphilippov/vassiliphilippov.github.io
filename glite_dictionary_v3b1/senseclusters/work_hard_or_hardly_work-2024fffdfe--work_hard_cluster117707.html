work hard or hardly work

x
humorous phrase about working little or a lot
Pronunciation: / wɝk hɑrd ɔr ˈhɑrdli ˈwɝk / (AmE), / wɜːk hɑːd ɔː ˈhɑːdli ˈwɜːk / (BrE)
used humorously to question whether someone is working diligently or barely working at all, playing on the similar sounds of 'hard' and 'hardly' to create a witty contrast.

Examples

To work hard or hardly work
At the company meeting, several employees laughed when the CEO said, 'Do we work hard or hardly work around here?'
Every time my manager walks by, he asks if I work hard or hardly work.
The phrase 'We work hard or hardly work' is often used to lighten up a busy office.
