laparoscopic cholecystectomy

noun
gall bladder removal surgery using a laparoscope
Pronunciation: / ˌlæpərəˈskɑːpɪk ˌkoʊlɪsɪsˈtɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌlæpərəˈskɒpɪk ˌkɒlɪsɪsˈtɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove the gall bladder using a laparoscope inserted through small incisions in the abdomen

Examples

Laparoscopic cholecystectomy surgery
After his laparoscopic cholecystectomy, he recovered quickly and went home the next day.
Many hospitals now prefer laparoscopic cholecystectomy over traditional open surgery due to its minimally invasive nature.
The surgeon recommended a laparoscopic cholecystectomy to treat her gallstones.
