montagnard

noun
French revolutionary group member
Pronunciation: / ˌmɒntɑːnˈjɑːrd / (AmE), / ˌmɒntænˈjɑː / (BrE)
a member of the radical 'Mountain' political group active during the French Revolution

Examples

Montagnard leader
The montagnard argued passionately for radical reforms in the National Convention.
During the Revolution, being a montagnard often meant supporting the execution of the king.
Many historians study the influence of each montagnard on the course of the French Revolution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Girondist - a member of a moderate political group during the French Revolution
  • synonym: Jacobins - members of a radical revolutionary group during the French Revolution
