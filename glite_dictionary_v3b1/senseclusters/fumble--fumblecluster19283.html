fumble

verb
handle clumsily and make a mistake
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
to handle or do something clumsily, resulting in a mistake or failure

Examples

To fumble a speech
He fumbled his speech, forgetting half of the crucial points.
She fumbled the paperwork, resulting in a delay in processing.
The chef fumbled the recipe and the cake came out flat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: botch - to carry out a task badly or carelessly
  • synonym: bungle - to carry out a task clumsily or incompetently
  • antonym: succeed - to accomplish something successfully
