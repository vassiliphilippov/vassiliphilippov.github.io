CFD

noun
financial contract
9.9
Pronunciation: /siː ef diː/ (AmE), /siː ef diː/ (BrE)
a financial contract that pays the difference between the opening and closing prices of an asset, without owning the asset itself

Examples

Trade a CFD
He used a CFD to profit from the rise in oil prices without buying any oil.
Many investors trade CFDs to speculate on stock market movements.
A CFD allows you to gain exposure to an asset's price changes without owning it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: derivative - a financial instrument whose value depends on another asset
  • synonym: futures contract - an agreement to buy or sell an asset at a future date for a set price
