connect

verb
join or link together
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / kəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to join or link things together so they form a whole or work as a unit (physically or structurally)

Examples

To connect two wires
Can you connect these wires for me before we turn on the power?
Please connect the trailer to the back of the car.
She quickly connected the two hoses to make one longer one.
A hallway connects the main building to the annex.
All the computers in the office are connected to the same server.
The new bridge will connect the two islands for the first time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disconnect - to break the connection between things
  • synonym: join - to bring or put together two or more things
  • synonym: link - to make a physical or logical connection between two things
