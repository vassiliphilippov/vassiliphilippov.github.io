immunosuppressive

adjective
reducing immune response
7.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /ɪ.mjuː.noʊ.sə.ˈpre.sɪv/ /; / /ɪ.mjʊ.noʊ.sə.ˈpre.sɪv/ (AmE), /ɪ.mjuː.nəʊ.sə.ˈpre.sɪv/ /; / /ɪ.mjʊ.nəʊ.sə.ˈpre.sɪv/ (BrE)
(of a drug, agent, or process) reducing or inhibiting the body's normal immune response.

Examples

Immunosuppressive treatment
Doctors may prescribe immunosuppressive drugs to prevent organ transplant rejection.
Long-term use of immunosuppressive therapy can increase the risk of infections.
The patient responded well to the immunosuppressive treatment regimen.
