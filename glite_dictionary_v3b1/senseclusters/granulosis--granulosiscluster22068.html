granulosis

noun
viral insect disease
9.9
Pronunciation: /græ.njʊ.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ /; / /grə.njə.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /græ.njʊ.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ /; / /grə.njə.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a viral disease affecting insect larvae, especially moths and butterflies, characterized by granular inclusion bodies and often resulting in death

Examples

Granulosis in caterpillars
The outbreak of granulosis devastated the population of caterpillars in the field.
Scientists studied the effects of granulosis on butterfly larvae to develop better pest control methods.
Farmers noticed that infected larvae with granulosis were covered in tiny granules before dying.
