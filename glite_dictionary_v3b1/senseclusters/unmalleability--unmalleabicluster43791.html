unmalleability

noun
lack of malleability
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.mæl.ɪ.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ʌn.mæl.ɪ.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being not able to be shaped or bent; lack of malleability

Examples

Metal unmalleability
Its unmalleability is a key disadvantage in applications requiring flexibility.
Researchers noted the unmalleability of certain metals at low temperatures.
The unmalleability of the material made it difficult to form into the desired shape.
