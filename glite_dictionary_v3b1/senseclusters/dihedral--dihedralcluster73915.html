dihedral

noun
polygon symmetry group
Pronunciation: / daɪˈhiːdrəl / (AmE), / daɪˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
the group of symmetries of a regular polygon, including both rotations and reflections, in mathematics

Examples

Dihedral group symmetries
The dihedral of order six represents the symmetries of a regular triangle.
In group theory, the dihedral is often used to illustrate concepts of rotation and reflection.
Students learned how the dihedral can be applied to analyze polygonal shapes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cyclic group - a group generated by a single element
  • synonym: symmetry group - a group describing all symmetries of a shape
