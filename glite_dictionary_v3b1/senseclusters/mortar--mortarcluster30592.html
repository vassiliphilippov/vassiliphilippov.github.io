mortar

noun
short, high-angle artillery gun
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə / (BrE)
a short, smoothbore gun designed to fire shells at high angles, typically for delivering explosives over a short distance

Examples

A mortar shell
Each team was equipped with a light mortar for launching explosive shells over enemy lines.
Heavy rain made it difficult to aim the mortar accurately during the battle.
The soldiers quickly assembled the mortar to provide cover fire for their advancing unit.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cannon - a large, heavy gun typically mounted on wheels or fixed mounts
  • synonym: howitzer - a type of artillery piece with a shorter barrel, used for firing shells on trajectories between those of guns and mortars
