jacketed

verb
to cover with a jacket
5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒæk.ɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ˈdʒæk.ə.tɪd/ (AmE), /ˈdʒæk.ɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ˈdʒæk.ə.tɪd/ (BrE)
to cover something with a protective or decorative outer layer, especially a jacket for books, pipes, or cables

Examples

To jacket a book
The publisher jacketed the limited edition books with special covers.
All the pipes in the basement were jacketed to prevent heat loss.
Technicians jacketed the cables to protect them from moisture and damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encase - to completely cover or surround something
  • antonym: unwrap - to remove a covering from something
