aortic orifice

noun
opening between left ventricle and aorta
Pronunciation: / eɪˌɔrtɪk ˈɔrɪfɪs / (AmE), / eɪˌɔːtɪk ˈɒrɪfɪs / (BrE)
the opening between the lower left chamber of the heart (left ventricle) and the aorta, through which blood is pumped into the aorta

Examples

The aortic orifice opening
Blood flows through the aortic orifice from the left ventricle into the aorta.
Doctors examined the aortic orifice during the cardiac procedure to check for abnormalities.
The aortic orifice can become narrowed due to a condition known as aortic stenosis.
