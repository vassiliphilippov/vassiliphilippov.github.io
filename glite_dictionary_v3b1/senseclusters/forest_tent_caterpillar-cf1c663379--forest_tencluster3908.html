forest tent caterpillar

noun
North American moth larva; tree defoliator
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr.ɪst tɛnt ˈkæt̬.ɚˌpɪl.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪst tɛnt ˈkæt.ə.pɪl.ə / (BrE)
the larval stage of a North American moth (Malacosoma disstria) that lives in groups and is a serious pest of deciduous trees, spinning a web that looks like a carpet rather than a tent

Examples

The forest tent caterpillar larva
A distinctive blue stripe helps to identify the forest tent caterpillar from other moth larvae.
Every spring, swarms of forest tent caterpillars appear in the local woodlands, causing concern among foresters.
The forest tent caterpillar can devastate entire stands of aspen and oak trees if left unchecked.
