imbue

verb
to fill or permeate with a quality or feeling
6.9 2.69/m
Pronunciation: / …™mÀàbju / (AmE), / …™mÀàbjuÀê / (BrE)
to fill or permeate someone or something with a particular quality, feeling, or idea

Examples

To imbue a story with meaning
Centuries-old traditions still imbue the ceremonies of the village.
His writing is imbued with a deep sense of nostalgia for his childhood.
The artist sought to imbue the sculpture with a sense of movement and life.
Her speech managed to imbue the audience with a sense of hope and determination.
The artist's work is imbued with a deep sense of nostalgia and longing.
Traditions and values imbue every aspect of the small town's culture.

Synonyms

  • infuse
    4.6 5.03/m
    infuse pours a quality into something, often gently and actively, while imbue spreads a quality more deeply through it.
  • instill
    4.8 5.56/m
    instill teaches ideas or values bit by bit into a person, while imbue gives a fuller, deeper quality to someone or something.
  • permeate
    6.0 2.85/m
    permeate moves a feeling or quality through all parts of a place or thing, while imbue means to fill or inspire deeply.
  • pervade
    6.7 3.84/m
    pervade spreads a feeling so it is noticed everywhere in a place, while imbue often implies actively giving that quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infuse - to fill something with a quality or emotion
  • synonym: permeate - to spread throughout; to pass through every part
  • synonym: soak - to make thoroughly wet or saturated
