undervalue

verb
regard as less valuable or important
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈvælju / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈvaljuː / (BrE)
to rate or esteem something or someone as being less important or less valuable than they really are

Examples

To undervalue hard work
It's a mistake to undervalue the importance of mental health.
Many people tend to undervalue their own achievements.
She realized too late that she had undervalued her employees' dedication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciate - to recognize the full worth of something
  • synonym: depreciate - to reduce the value or worth of something
  • antonym: overvalue - to rate or esteem too highly
