hydroxide ion

noun
negatively charged OH− ion in chemistry
7.4 4.2
Pronunciation: /haɪ.ˈdrɑːk.saɪd ˈaɪ.ən/ (AmE), /haɪ.ˈdrɒk.saɪd ˈaɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a negatively charged ion (OH−) consisting of one oxygen and one hydrogen atom, commonly found in alkaline solutions

The reaction releases a hydroxide ion into the solution, raising the pH.
