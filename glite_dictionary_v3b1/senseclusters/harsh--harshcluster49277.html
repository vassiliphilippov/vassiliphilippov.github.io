harsh

adjective
severely unpleasant or strict
3.7
Pronunciation: / hɑrʃ / (AmE), / hɑːʃ / (BrE)
severe, strict, or unkind in actions, words, or treatment; lacking gentleness or sympathy

Examples

Harsh punishment
He faced harsh consequences for breaking the rules.
She had to accept the harsh reality of her situation.
The critics delivered a harsh review of the performance.
His tone was harsh when he criticized the team's performance.
Many people felt the new law was unnecessarily harsh and unfair.
The judge imposed a harsh penalty on the defendant for his actions.
He regretted his harsh words after seeing her reaction.
Her boss gave her a very harsh criticism during the meeting.
Some people thought the new law was too harsh on offenders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentle - mild and not harsh
  • antonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, or considerate nature
  • antonym: lenient - not strict; permissive or tolerant, especially in punishment
  • synonym: severe - very strict or harsh in treatment or punishment
