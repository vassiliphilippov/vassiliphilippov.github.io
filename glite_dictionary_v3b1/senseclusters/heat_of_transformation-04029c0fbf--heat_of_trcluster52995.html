heat of transformation

noun
heat change during phase transition
1.0
Pronunciation: / hit əv ˌtrænsfɚˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv ˌtrænsfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the amount of heat absorbed or released during a change of phase at constant temperature and pressure

Examples

The heat of transformation energy
During the solid-to-liquid transition, the heat of transformation is absorbed from the environment.
Knowing the heat of transformation is essential for designing efficient cooling systems.
The heat of transformation determines how much energy is needed to melt ice into water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latent heat - heat absorbed or released during a phase change without change in temperature
