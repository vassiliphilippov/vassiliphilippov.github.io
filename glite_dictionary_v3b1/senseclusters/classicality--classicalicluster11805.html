classicality

noun
classical style quality
Pronunciation: / ˌklæsɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌklæsɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having characteristics typical of classical style or form, especially in art, music, or literature

Examples

Classicality in art
The classicality of the building's columns gives it a timeless appearance.
Many critics praised the classicality of the novel's structure and language.
Her paintings are admired for their classicality and attention to traditional techniques.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classic - having a traditional and long-established style
  • antonym: modernity - the quality of being modern or contemporary
