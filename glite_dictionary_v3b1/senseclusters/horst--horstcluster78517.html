horst

noun
raised block of crust between faults
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /hɔːrst/ (AmE), /hɔːst/ (BrE)
A raised block of the Earth's crust that has been pushed upward between two faults, making it higher than the surrounding areas.

Examples

A horst formation
Geologists studied the formation of the horst to understand the region's tectonic history.
Several towns are built on the horst, where the ground is less prone to flooding.
The horst towered over the surrounding plain, creating a dramatic landscape feature.
