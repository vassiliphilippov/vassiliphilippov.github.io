Guaiacum

noun
genus of tropical American trees or shrubs
Pronunciation: / ˈɡwaɪ.ə.kəm / (AmE), / ˈɡwaɪ.əkəm / (BrE)
A small genus of evergreen resinous trees or shrubs native to warm and tropical regions of America, known for their hard wood and medicinal resin.

Examples

Guaiacum tree extract
Many herbal remedies use extracts from Guaiacum for their purported medicinal properties.
The Guaiacum trees are valued for their dense and durable wood.
The hard resin from Guaiacum has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
