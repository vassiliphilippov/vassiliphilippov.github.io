trade deficit

noun
an excess of imports over exports
6.3 3.8
Pronunciation: /treɪd ˈdef.ə.sɪt/ (AmE), /treɪd ˈdef.ɪ.sɪt/ (BrE)
A situation in which a country's imports exceed its exports, leading to a negative balance of trade.

Examples

A trade deficit problem
A persistent trade deficit can impact a nation's currency and economic stability.
Efforts to reduce the trade deficit include promoting exports to new international markets.
The country recorded a significant trade deficit last year due to increased imports.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trade surplus - an excess of exports over imports
