bypass

noun
electrical conductor that diverts current

An electrical conductor placed in parallel with another device in a circuit to divert a portion of the current away from it.

Examples

Circuit bypass wire
A bypass is often used to stabilize voltage across sensitive components.
The engineer installed a bypass to prevent the resistor from overheating.
Without a bypass, too much current would flow through the main circuit path.

Synonyms

  • electrical shunt
    5.5 0.00/m
    electrical shunt is a low-resistance conductor used in circuits to divert some current, while bypass names a conductor placed in parallel to divert current.
  • shunt
    8.4 0.05/m
    shunt is a general word used in different fields for a device that diverts current, while bypass specifically names the parallel conductor in a circuit.
