preshow

noun
event before main show
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌʃəʊ / (BrE)
an event or activities that take place before the main show or performance to entertain or prepare the audience

Examples

A preshow event
The preshow included interviews with the cast before the movie started.
Arriving early, we enjoyed a lively preshow that set the mood for the concert.
The theater offered a special preshow performance for children before the main play.
We arrived early to watch the preshow before the concert started.
The preshow included interviews with the performers and behind-the-scenes footage.
Snacks and drinks were served during the preshow to entertain the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main event - the primary or most important performance
  • synonym: opening act - a performer or group that appears before the main act
  • synonym: preview - a showing or performance before the official opening
