voicedness

noun
vocal cord vibration
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔɪstnəs / (AmE), / ˈvɔɪstnəs / (BrE)
the quality of a speech sound being produced with vibration of the vocal cords

Examples

The voicedness of consonants
The voicedness of a consonant distinguishes 'b' from 'p' in English.
Linguists study voicedness to understand how different languages use sound.
Changes in voicedness can affect the meaning of words in some languages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: voicelessness - the quality of a speech sound produced without vocal cord vibration
  • synonym: voicing - the presence or absence of vocal cord vibration in speech sounds
