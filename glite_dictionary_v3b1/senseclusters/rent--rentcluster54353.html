rent

verb
let property in return for payment
1.8 15/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ›nt / (AmE), / rÉ›nt / (BrE)
to allow someone to use or occupy property or goods in exchange for payment, usually under a formal agreement for a period of time

Examples

To rent a room
She is planning to rent her spare room to students every summer.
The owners rented the apartment to a young couple last month.
They decided to rent their house while they traveled overseas for a year.
She decided to rent her downtown apartment while she traveled abroad for work.
The company plans to rent its conference hall to other businesses on weekends.
They agreed to rent the cottage for a year starting in June.

Synonyms

  • rent out
    2.1 1.60/m
    rent out is a phrasal verb used when you let someone use your property for money, while rent is the single-word form.
  • let
    6.7 42/m
    let is commonly used in British English for allowing someone to occupy property under a contract, while rent is the general verb used internationally.
  • lease
    3.5 4.57/m
    lease is a formal, often long legal contract when an owner lets land or property, while rent is more general everyday use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hire - to obtain the use of something temporarily in exchange for payment (in British English, often opposite direction)
  • synonym: lease - to let property for a specified period in return for payment
  • synonym: let - to allow someone to use property in exchange for payment, chiefly British
