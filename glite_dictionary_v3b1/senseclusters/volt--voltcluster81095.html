volt

noun
unit of electric potential difference
2.4 3.3
Pronunciation: /voʊlt/ (AmE), /vɒlt/ /; / /vəʊlt/ (BrE)
the unit of electric potential difference or electromotive force, equal to the difference across a conductor when a current of one ampere dissipates one watt of power

Examples

A volt unit
A standard wall outlet in the United States supplies 120 volts of electricity.
The battery provides a consistent output of twelve volts for the device.
When one ampere of current flows through a resistance of one ohm, the potential difference is one volt.

  • degree higher: watt - unit of power
