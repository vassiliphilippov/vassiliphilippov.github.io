Scyphozoa

noun
class of true jellyfish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌskɪfəˈzoʊə /; / ˌsaɪfəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / ˌsɪfəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
a class of marine animals (true jellyfish) that have a dominant medusa stage and a reduced or absent polyp stage

Examples

The Scyphozoa jellyfish
Scientists study the life cycles of Scyphozoa to learn more about jellyfish development.
The Scyphozoa class includes species like the moon jelly and the lion's mane jellyfish.
Unlike other cnidarians, Scyphozoa typically lack a prominent polyp stage.
Many Scyphozoa spend most of their life cycle as free-swimming medusae.
Members of the Scyphozoa can be found in oceans worldwide.
The Scyphozoa are commonly known as the true jellyfish.
