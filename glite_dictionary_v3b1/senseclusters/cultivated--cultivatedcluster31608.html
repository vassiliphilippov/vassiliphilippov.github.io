cultivated

adjective
prepared for raising crops (of land)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪveɪtɪd /; / ˈkʌltɪˌveɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of land or plants) prepared, developed, or improved by people for growing crops or for use in agriculture or horticulture (not wild)

Examples

Cultivated land
After years of hard work, the once-barren land became fully cultivated.
Rice is grown on cultivated land near the riverbanks.
The cultivated fields stretched as far as the eye could see.
Only cultivated mushrooms are sold in most supermarkets.
The cultivated fields stretched for miles beyond the village.
These cultivated varieties yield more fruit than their wild relatives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arable - (of land) suitable for growing crops
  • antonym: uncultivated - (of land) not prepared or used for growing crops
  • antonym: wild - growing or living in a natural state, not cultivated or domesticated
