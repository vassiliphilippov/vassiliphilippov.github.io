in one's own right

adverb
by virtue of one's own achievements
4.6
Pronunciation: / ɪn wʌnz oʊn raɪt /; / ɪn ˌoʊn ˈraɪt / (AmE), / ɪn wʌnz əʊn raɪt /; / ɪn ˌəʊn ˈraɪt / (BrE)
having a status, position, or quality independently, not because of a connection with someone else

Examples

In his own right
He became a respected scientist in his own right after years of dedicated research.
She is an accomplished artist in her own right, not just the wife of a famous painter.
The book, while part of a series, stands out in its own right as a remarkable work.
He is a respected scientist in his own right, not just because of his family's legacy.
She became a successful author in her own right, separate from her famous parents.
The charity is an important institution in its own right, even apart from its national affiliations.
Each member of the team is an expert in their own right.
She became a renowned scientist in her own right, not just as the daughter of famous parents.
The painting is valuable in its own right, even aside from its historical significance.
