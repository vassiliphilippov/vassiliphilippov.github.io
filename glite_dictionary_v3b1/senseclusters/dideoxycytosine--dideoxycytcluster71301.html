dideoxycytosine

noun
chain-terminating nucleoside for DNA sequencing
9.9
Pronunciation: /daɪ.diː.ˈɑːk.sɪ.saɪn/ (AmE), /daɪ.diː.ˈɒk.sɪ.saɪn/ (BrE)
(in molecular biology) a dideoxynucleoside used to terminate DNA chain elongation in Sanger sequencing and related methods, producing fragments that reveal nucleotide sequence.

Examples

Dideoxycytosine in DNA sequencing
The technician added a small amount of dideoxycytosine to terminate synthesis at specific bases.
During the Sanger run, they used fluorescent dideoxycytosine to create readable fragment lengths.
Students learned how dideoxycytosine stops DNA polymerase, producing fragments for sequence analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ddC - common abbreviation for dideoxycytosine; used both in lab and clinical contexts.
  • degree higher: dideoxynucleotide - a nucleotide lacking a 3'-hydroxyl group (ddNTP) used to terminate DNA synthesis in sequencing.
