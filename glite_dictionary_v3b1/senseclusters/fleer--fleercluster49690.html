fleer

noun
scornful look or remark; mockery
7.3
Pronunciation: / flɪr / (AmE), / flɪə / (BrE)
a look or remark expressing scornful mockery or contempt

Examples

A cold fleer
He endured the fleer of his opponents in silence.
The audience greeted his suggestion with a derisive fleer.
With a curling lip and a cutting fleer, she dismissed the idea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: applause - approval or praise expressed by clapping
  • synonym: sneer - a contemptuous or mocking smile, remark, or tone
  • synonym: taunt - a remark made in order to anger, wound, or provoke someone
