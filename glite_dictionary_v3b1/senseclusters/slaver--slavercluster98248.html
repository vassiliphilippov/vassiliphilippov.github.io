slaver

noun
person engaged in slave trade
Pronunciation: / ˈsleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvə / (BrE)
a person who buys, sells, or owns slaves

Examples

A cruel slaver
Abolitionists fought to bring slavers to justice for their crimes.
During the 18th century, a slaver could make a fortune on a single voyage.
The ship was manned by a notorious slaver who profited from human misery.
Historians have documented the lives of many slavers during the 18th century.
The abolition of slavery ended the power once held by the slaver.
The plantation owner was a notorious slaver in the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolitionist - a person who advocates ending slavery
  • synonym: slave trader - a person who buys and sells slaves
  • synonym: slaveholder - a person who owns slaves
