line

noun
railroad track and roadbed for trains
5.6
Pronunciation: / laɪn / (AmE), / laɪn / (BrE)
a long, thin, flexible object such as a rope, wire, pipe, or cable used for carrying, connecting, or transporting

Examples

A power line
After the storm, several sections of the line were damaged and needed repairs.
The company built a new line between the two cities to improve freight transport.
This old line has been in operation since the nineteenth century.
A new line was installed to carry natural gas from the mainland to the island.
Oil flowed steadily through the line that connected the rig to the refinery.
The engineers checked the line regularly for leaks or weaknesses.
An electrician repaired the faulty power line outside our house.
Fiber-optic lines are used to provide high-speed internet connections.
The telephone line went dead during the thunderstorm.
