extractive

adjective
NLP method selecting existing text
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstræktɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstræktɪv / (BrE)
(in natural language processing) relating to methods that create summaries or answers by selecting and reusing exact words or sentences from source text rather than generating new phrasing.

Examples

Extractive summarization
The research team chose an extractive approach to summarization to preserve exact quotes from the interviews.
Many news aggregators rely on extractive methods that select full sentences rather than generate new phrasing.
Because the model is limited, it produced only an extractive summary consisting of three original article sentences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstractive - creating summaries by generating new text that may paraphrase or fuse information from the source
  • synonym: extractive summarization - a summarization technique that constructs a summary by selecting and combining existing sentences or passages
