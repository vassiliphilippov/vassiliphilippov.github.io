innocent

adjective
free from sin or moral wrongdoing
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnəsənt / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsənt / (BrE)
not guilty of a crime or wrongdoing; morally pure or free from sin

Examples

Innocent of the crime
Legends spoke of a land populated only by the pure and the innocent.
She was regarded as truly innocent in the eyes of the church.
The child was believed to be innocent of any sin.
Children are often seen as innocent because they do not yet understand the complexities of right and wrong.
Despite the accusations, she insisted she was completely innocent.
The jury found the defendant innocent of all charges after a lengthy trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blameless - free from blame; innocent
  • synonym: guiltless - not responsible for any wrongdoing
  • antonym: guilty - responsible for a specified wrongdoing or crime
  • antonym: sinful - full of or characterized by sin
