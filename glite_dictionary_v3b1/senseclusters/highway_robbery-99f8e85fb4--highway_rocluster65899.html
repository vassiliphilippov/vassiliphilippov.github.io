highway robbery

noun
robbery of travellers on a public road
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌweɪ ˈrɑːbəri / (AmE), / ˈhaɪweɪ ˈrɒbəri / (BrE)
the act of robbing travellers on or near a public road, especially by force or threat

Examples

Highway robbery at night
In old England, highway robbery was punished by death.
Many believe charging such high prices is a kind of highway robbery.
The notorious gang committed several highway robberies during the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: burglary - illegal entry into a building to commit a crime, especially theft
  • synonym: robbery - the crime of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
