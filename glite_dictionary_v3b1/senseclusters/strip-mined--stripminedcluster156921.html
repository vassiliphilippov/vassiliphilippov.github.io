strip-mined

adjective
mined by stripping surface layers
Pronunciation: / strɪp maɪnd / (AmE), / strɪp maɪnd / (BrE)
(of land) having been mined near the earth's surface by removing soil and rock (stripping) to access mineral deposits.

Examples

Strip-mined land
Much of the forest was strip-mined for coal during the 20th century.
Rehabilitation of strip-mined land is a major environmental challenge.
The area around the old quarry is now a strip-mined landscape, devoid of vegetation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strip mining - the process of mining by removing surface layers to access minerals
