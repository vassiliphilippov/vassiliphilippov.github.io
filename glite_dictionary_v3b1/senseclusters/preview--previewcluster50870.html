preview

verb
to watch before public release
4.1 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpriː.vjuː/ (AmE), /ˈpriː.vjuː/ (BrE)
to watch, view, or examine something before it is officially available to the public

Examples

To preview a film
Only selected critics were invited to preview the highly anticipated film before its official premiere.
The studio will preview their new blockbuster for executives and investors next week.
We were lucky enough to preview the award-winning play during a special media event.
Fans had a chance to preview the movie before its official release.
She likes to preview books before deciding to buy them.
We previewed the new software to see its features.

Related Terms

  • synonym: review - to evaluate or assess something, especially after its release
  • synonym: screen - to show a movie to an audience, often before general release
