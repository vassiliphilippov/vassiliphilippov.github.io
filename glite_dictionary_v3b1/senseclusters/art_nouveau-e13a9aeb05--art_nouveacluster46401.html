art nouveau

noun
decorative art and architecture style (1890s)
Pronunciation: / ɑrt nuˈvoʊ / (AmE), / ɑː nuːˈvəʊ / (BrE)
A style of art and architecture that emerged in France in the 1890s, marked by flowing lines and natural forms resembling leaves, vines, and flowers.

Examples

Art nouveau style
She collects jewelry that reflects the curves and motifs of art nouveau.
The Paris Metro entrances are famous examples of art nouveau design.
The museum features a special exhibition on art nouveau architecture and its influence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: art deco - An artistic style popular in the 1920s and 1930s, characterized by precise and bold geometric shapes and strong colors.
