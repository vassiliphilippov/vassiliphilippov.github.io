aspartate

noun
salt of aspartic acid
Pronunciation: / əˈspɑːrˌteɪt / (AmE), / əˈspɑːteɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of aspartic acid, commonly found in biological systems and important in metabolism

Examples

Blood aspartate level
The doctor measured the patient's aspartate levels to assess liver function.
In the experiment, aspartate was used as a substrate for the enzyme reaction.
Athletes sometimes monitor their aspartate concentrations to track muscle recovery.
