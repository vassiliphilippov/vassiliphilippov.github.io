Battle of the Philippine Sea

proper noun
decisive WWII naval battle (1944) in the Pacific
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬.əl əv ðə ˌfɪl.ɪˈpiːn ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl əv ðə ˌfɪl.ɪˈpiːn ˈsiː / (BrE)
a major naval battle in World War II (1944), in which United States forces decisively defeated the Japanese fleet, preventing it from interfering with American military operations in the Pacific.

Examples

Battle of the Philippine Sea in 1944
Historians often cite the Battle of the Philippine Sea as a turning point in the Pacific theater of World War II.
In 1944, the Battle of the Philippine Sea ensured U.S. naval dominance in the Pacific.
The Battle of the Philippine Sea is sometimes referred to as the 'Great Marianas Turkey Shoot' because of the heavy losses suffered by Japanese aircraft.
