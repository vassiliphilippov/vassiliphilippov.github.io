Jesus

proper noun
central figure of Christianity
1.0 103/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íiÀêz…ôs / (AmE), / Ààd íiÀêz…ôs / (BrE)
The central figure of Christianity, believed by Christians to be the Son of God and whose teachings form the basis of the religion.

Examples

Jesus Christ
During Christmas, people around the world celebrate the birth of Jesus.
Many Christians pray to Jesus for guidance and strength in their daily lives.
The teachings of Jesus emphasize love, forgiveness, and compassion for others.

Synonyms

  • Jesus Christ
    3.0 11/m
    Jesus Christ is the full formal name used in worship and books, while Jesus is the shorter personal name used widely.
  • Christ
    3.5 86/m
    Christ is the title showing his role as Messiah in religion, while Jesus is the actual personal name people use.
  • Jesus of Nazareth
    5.1 0.36/m
    Jesus of Nazareth is the historical name linking him to a place and time, while Jesus is the general religious figure name.
  • the Nazarene
    8.0 0.06/m
    the Nazarene is a title that points to his origin in Nazareth, while Jesus is the central name Christians use.
