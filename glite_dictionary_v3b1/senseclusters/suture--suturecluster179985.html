suture

noun
stitch used in surgery
5.1 1.09/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsuÀêt É…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàsjuÀêt É…ô / (BrE)
a stitch or the thread/wire used by surgeons to close a wound or incision in surgery

Examples

A surgical suture
Dissolvable suture materials are often used for internal wounds.
It is important to remove the suture after the wound has healed.
The surgeon carefully placed a suture to close the incision.
After the stitches dissolved, no trace of the suture was visible on her skin.
The nurse carefully removed each suture from the patient's incision site.
The surgeon used a suture to close the wound after the operation.

Synonyms
