evasion

noun
deliberate avoidance of payment
Pronunciation: / ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of avoiding something you are supposed to do, such as paying money or fulfilling a duty, especially by deceit or trickery

Examples

Tax evasion
His frequent evasion of rent payments led to his eviction from the apartment.
The company was fined for tax evasion after an investigation by authorities.
The new laws are designed to make financial evasion more difficult for individuals and businesses.
His evasion of paying taxes eventually led to a hefty fine.
The manager noticed her frequent evasion of responsibility at work.
There are legal consequences for the evasion of customs duties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoidance - the action of keeping away from something
  • antonym: compliance - the act of conforming to or fulfilling a requirement or request
