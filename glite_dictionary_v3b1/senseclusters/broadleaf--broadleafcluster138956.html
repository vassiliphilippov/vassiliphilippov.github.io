broadleaf

adjective
having wide, flat leaves
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔdˌlif / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌliːf / (BrE)
Having relatively wide and flat leaves, rather than needlelike or scalelike leaves, as in certain plants or trees.

Examples

Broadleaf plants
Gardeners often prefer broadleaf plants for their lush foliage and shade.
Many deciduous trees in the forest are classified as broadleaf species.
The area is dominated by broadleaf evergreens rather than coniferous trees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: needleleaf - having needlelike leaves
