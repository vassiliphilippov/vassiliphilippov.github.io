Hibiscus cannabinus

noun
fiber plant cultivated in tropics
8.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈbɪskəs ˌkænəˈbaɪnəs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs ˌkanəˈbaɪnəs / (BrE)
A tropical plant cultivated for its valuable fiber, native to the East Indies and now widely grown elsewhere.

Examples

Hibiscus cannabinus plant fiber
Hibiscus cannabinus is widely grown for its strong, useful fiber known as kenaf.
Researchers are developing new paper products using Hibiscus cannabinus fiber.
The stems of Hibiscus cannabinus are harvested to make rope and coarse cloth.
