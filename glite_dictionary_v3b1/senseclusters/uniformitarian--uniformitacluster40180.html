uniformitarian

noun
believer in gradual change
9.9
Pronunciation: /juː.nə.fɑːr.mɪ.ˈter.ɪ.ən/ /; / /juː.nə.fɔːr.mɪ.ˈter.ɪ.ən/ (AmE), /juː.nə.fɔː.mɪ.ˈteə.riː.ən/ (BrE)
a person who believes that geological changes occur gradually over long periods rather than by sudden events

Examples

A uniformitarian scientist
The uniformitarian explained that mountains are shaped by slow, continuous processes over millions of years.
As a uniformitarian, she argued that river valleys formed slowly rather than by sudden floods.
Many geologists today are uniformitarians, supporting the idea of gradual change in Earth's history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catastrophist - a person who believes that geological changes occur mainly through sudden, violent events
