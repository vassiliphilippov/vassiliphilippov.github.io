mucho

adjective
very many; great amount
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːtʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmuːtʃəʊ / (BrE)
informal adjective used before a noun to mean 'very many' or 'a great amount of', borrowed from Spanish

Examples

Mucho energy
The new singer has mucho talent and confidence for someone so young.
He brought mucho energy to the team, inspiring everyone to work harder.
There was mucho excitement in the city when the festival began.

Related Terms

  • antonym: few - a small number of people or things
  • synonym: many - a large number of people or things
  • synonym: much - a large amount or quantity
