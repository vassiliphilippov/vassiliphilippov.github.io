robusticity

noun
strength or durability
Pronunciation: / roʊˌbʌˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / rəʊˌbʌˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being strong, durable, or able to withstand stress or disturbance without failing

Examples

Bridge robusticity
The robusticity of the bridge was tested under extreme weather conditions.
Scientists studied the robusticity of the new material to determine its suitability for construction.
The robusticity of ancient bones can reveal information about the lifestyle of early humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and able to withstand stress
