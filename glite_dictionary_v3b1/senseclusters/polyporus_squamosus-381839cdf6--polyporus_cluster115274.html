Polyporus squamosus

noun
a bracket fungus with a scaly cap
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑlɪˈpɔrəs skwəˈmoʊsəs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpɔːrəs skwəˈməʊsəs / (BrE)
A widely distributed bracket fungus of the Northern Hemisphere, with a scaly cap that turns nearly black when mature, sometimes with a lateral stalk.

Examples

Polyporus squamosus fungus
Mycology students often search for Polyporus squamosus in spring, when its distinctive scaly cap is most visible.
The chef prepared a wild mushroom risotto using freshly foraged Polyporus squamosus.
The massive Polyporus squamosus was growing on the trunk of an old maple in the park.
