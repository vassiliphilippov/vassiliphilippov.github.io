Peru-Chile Trench

noun
deep ocean trench off Peru and Chile
Pronunciation: / pəˌru tʃɪˈleɪ trɛntʃ / (AmE), / pəˌruː tʃɪˈleɪ trɛntʃ / (BrE)
A deep depression in the floor of the Pacific Ocean, located off the coasts of Peru and Chile.

Examples

The Peru-Chile Trench location
Earthquakes along the Peru-Chile Trench are often caused by the subduction of the oceanic plate beneath the South American plate.
Marine biologists conduct research in the Peru-Chile Trench to study deep-sea creatures and ecosystems.
The Peru-Chile Trench is one of the deepest parts of the Pacific Ocean and plays a vital role in plate tectonics.
