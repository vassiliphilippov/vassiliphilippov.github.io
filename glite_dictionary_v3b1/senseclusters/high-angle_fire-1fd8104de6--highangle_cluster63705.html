high-angle fire

noun
artillery fire at a high elevation angle
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ ˌæŋɡəl ˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌæŋɡəl ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
fire from artillery (such as a cannon or mortar) in which the projectile is launched at an elevation greater than that required for maximum range, resulting in a high, arching trajectory.

Examples

High-angle fire from artillery
In mountainous terrain, high-angle fire is often necessary to reach hidden positions.
Mortars are particularly effective for high-angle fire due to their steep trajectories.
The artillery unit used high-angle fire to target enemy troops on the reverse slope of the hill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-angle fire - artillery fire at a lower elevation angle, usually for maximum range
