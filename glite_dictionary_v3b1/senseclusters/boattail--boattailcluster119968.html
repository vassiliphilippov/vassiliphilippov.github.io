boattail

adjective
tapered rear design
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊtˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊtˌteɪl / (BrE)
having a rear end that tapers smoothly like the stern of a boat, designed to reduce aerodynamic drag.

Examples

Boattail shape
The new sports car features a boattail rear to improve its aerodynamic performance.
Engineers chose a boattail design for the trailer to help reduce fuel consumption on highways.
The classic vehicle's boattail shape makes it stand out among other vintage cars.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blunt - having a flat or rounded end, not tapered
  • synonym: streamlined - designed to reduce resistance to motion through air or water
