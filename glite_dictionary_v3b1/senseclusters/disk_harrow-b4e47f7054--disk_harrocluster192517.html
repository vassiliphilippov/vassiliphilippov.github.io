disk harrow

noun
agricultural implement with angled disks
Pronunciation: / dɪsk ˈhæroʊ / (AmE), / dɪsk ˈharəʊ / (BrE)
an agricultural implement with a series of concave disks set on edge at an angle, used for breaking up and smoothing soil

Examples

A disk harrow tool
After plowing, the disk harrow was run over the soil to break up large clods.
Modern disk harrows are often attached to tractors for greater efficiency.
The farmer used a disk harrow to prepare the field for planting.
After plowing, the land was smoothed and leveled with a disc harrow.
Modern disc harrows make soil preparation much more efficient than traditional methods.
The farmer used a disc harrow to break up the clods in the field before sowing.
