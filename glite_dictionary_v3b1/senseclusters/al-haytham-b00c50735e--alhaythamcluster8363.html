al-Haytham

proper noun
Arab mathematician and physicist (c.965–c.1040)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ælˈhaɪθəm / (AmE), / ælˈhaɪθəm / (BrE)
(c. 965–c. 1040) An Arab mathematician, physicist, and philosopher known for his influential work in geometry and optics, and for establishing experimental methods in science.

Examples

al-Haytham the scientist
al-Haytham made significant advances in the field of optics during the Islamic Golden Age.
Many historians regard al-Haytham as the father of modern scientific methodology.
The pioneering work of al-Haytham influenced generations of European scientists.
