expediency

noun
quality of being suitable or practical
6.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspiː.dɪ.ən.sɪ/ /; / /ek.ˈspiː.dɪ.ən.sɪ/ (AmE), /ek.ˈspiː.dɪ.ən.sɪ/ /; / /ɪk.ˈspiː.dɪ.ən.sɪ/ (BrE)
The quality of being convenient and practical despite possibly being improper or immoral; suitability for achieving a particular end.

Examples

A matter of expediency
Choosing convenience over ethics, they acted with a sense of pure expediency.
He questioned the expediency of the new policy, suggesting it sacrificed principles for quick results.
The chairman made the decision out of political expediency rather than genuine concern for the matter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convenience - the state of being easy or suitable for a particular purpose
  • antonym: inexpediency - the quality of being not suitable or advisable in a given situation
