Synonyms

    8.4 0.06/m
    cajolery uses smooth flattery and gentle urging to change someone's mind, while wheedling adds repeated pleas and soft charm.
  • gentle persuasion
    3.9 0.04/m
    gentle persuasion uses mild, polite requests or hints to influence someone, while wheedling presses more with flattery and persistence.
  • coaxing
    4.4 0.44/m
    coaxing guides someone with kind words and small requests, while wheedling adds extra flattery and longer, repeated urging.
  • sweet talk
    4.3 0.07/m
    sweet talk uses flattering or charming speech to get a yes, while wheedling often feels more constant and quietly pushy.
  • blandishment
    9.8 0.26/m
    blandishment is a formal flattering phrase or action to persuade someone, while wheedling often uses casual charm and steady urging.
  • soft soap
    7.6 0.00/m
    soft soap means exaggerated praise or flattering talk to influence someone, while wheedling adds repeated pleading and softer, personal cajoling.
  • ingratiation
    9.1 0.02/m
    ingratiation is a longer effort to win favor by pleasing acts, while wheedling is a short push using flattery to persuade.
