(of an organism) not undergoing metamorphosis or only undergoing very slight changes during development

Examples

Ametabolic insects
Some insects are described as ametabolic because they do not change form significantly after hatching.
Springtails are considered ametabolic, exhibiting no notable metamorphic stages.
Unlike holometabolous insects, ametabolic species show little difference between juveniles and adults.
Many primitive insects are ametabolic and do not undergo significant changes after hatching.
Species with ametabolic development have juveniles that look almost identical to the adults.
Unlike butterflies, silverfish are ametabolic and resemble adults throughout their lives.
