enhanced interrogation

noun
coercive or harsh interrogation techniques
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhænst ˌɪntəˌroʊˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɑːnst ˌɪntəˈrɒɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of coercive or harsh methods during interrogation, especially by government agencies, often criticized as inhumane or as a euphemism for torture

Examples

Enhanced interrogation techniques
Human rights organizations have condemned enhanced interrogation as a violation of international law.
The government report examined the effectiveness of enhanced interrogation methods.
The senator criticized the use of enhanced interrogation in the intelligence community.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: interrogation - the act of formally questioning someone
  • synonym: torture - the act of inflicting severe pain as a punishment or to force information
