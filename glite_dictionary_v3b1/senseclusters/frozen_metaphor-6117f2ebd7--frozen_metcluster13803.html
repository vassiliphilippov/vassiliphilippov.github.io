frozen metaphor

noun
conventionalized metaphor with lost figurative sense
Pronunciation: / ˈfroʊzən ˈmɛtəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈfrəʊzən ˈmɛtəfɔː / (BrE)
A metaphor that has become so common through repeated use that it is accepted as a standard meaning or phrase, often losing its original figurative sense.

Examples

A frozen metaphor
Linguists often study how a frozen metaphor becomes part of everyday vocabulary.
The phrase 'the leg of a table' is a classic example of a frozen metaphor.
When a metaphorical expression loses its imagery, it is said to have become a frozen metaphor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dead metaphor - a metaphor that has lost its figurative meaning and is perceived as a literal term
  • degree lower: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to something to which it is not literally applicable
