breast milk

noun
mother's milk for infants
1.8
Pronunciation: / brɛst mɪlk / (AmE), / brɛst mɪlk / (BrE)
the milk produced by a female mammal (especially a human mother) to feed her baby, containing essential nutrients and antibodies

Examples

Breast milk feeding
Doctors recommend exclusive breast milk feeding for the first six months of a baby's life.
Some mothers find it challenging to produce enough breast milk for their newborns.
Donor breast milk is sometimes used in hospitals for premature infants.
Doctors recommend feeding newborns breast milk because it contains important nutrients and antibodies.
Some mothers choose to pump breast milk so their babies can be fed even when they are not present.

Related Terms

  • antonym: formula - manufactured milk substitute for infants
