superposition principle

noun
geological law stating oldest rock layer is at the bottom
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərpəˈzɪʃən ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəpəˈzɪʃən ˈprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
The principle in geology that, in a sequence of stratified sedimentary rocks, the lowest layer is the oldest.

Examples

The superposition principle in geology
According to the superposition principle, fossils found in deeper strata are generally older than those in higher layers.
The superposition principle allows geologists to determine the relative ages of sedimentary rock layers.
When the rock formation was analyzed, the superposition principle helped to reconstruct its geological history.
