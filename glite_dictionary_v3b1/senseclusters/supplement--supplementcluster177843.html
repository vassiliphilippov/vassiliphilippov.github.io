supplement

verb
add extra at end to improve or complete
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌpləˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪmənt /; / ˈsʌplɪˌmɛnt /; / ˈsʌplɪmɛnt / (BrE)
to add something extra to improve, enhance, or make up for a lack (including adding to a publication or document)

Examples

To supplement income
The author decided to supplement her book with an additional chapter on recent discoveries.
To make the presentation clearer, he supplemented it with detailed diagrams.
You can supplement your report by adding a summary at the end.
He decided to supplement his income by taking a part-time job.
Many people supplement their diets with vitamins and minerals.
The company supplemented its staff with temporary workers during the busy season.
Doctors often recommend that patients supplement their diets with vitamins.
He decided to supplement his lecture notes with additional reading materials.
Many people supplement their income by working a part-time job on weekends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: append - to add as a supplement or attachment at the end
  • synonym: augment - to make something greater by adding to it; to increase
  • antonym: omit - to leave out or exclude
  • antonym: reduce - to make something smaller or less in amount
