wimp

verb
to act cowardly
7.8
Pronunciation: / wɪmp / (AmE), / wɪmp / (BrE)
to act in a weak or cowardly way, especially by avoiding something difficult or challenging

Examples

To wimp out
He wimped out of the challenge at the last minute.
She almost wimped when it was her turn to speak in front of the class.
Don't wimp out now; you have come this far already.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brave - to face something difficult or dangerous without fear
  • synonym: chicken out - to decide not to do something because of fear
