sugarcane

noun
tall plant yielding commercial sugar
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌɡərˌkeɪn /; / ˈʃuɡərˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌɡəˌkeɪn /; / ˈʃʊɡəˌkeɪn / (BrE)
a tall tropical grass with thick, sweet stems that is grown to produce sugar

Examples

Fresh sugarcane juice
Fresh sugarcane juice is a popular drink in many countries.
The factory processes sugarcane into refined white sugar.
The workers harvested the sugarcane fields under the hot tropical sun.
Molasses is a byproduct obtained during the processing of sugarcane into sugar.
Much of the world's refined sugar is made from sugarcane grown in tropical regions.
The farmers harvested the sugarcane early in the morning to avoid the midday heat.
