cork tree

noun
tree with thick corky bark
Pronunciation: / kɔrk tri / (AmE), / kɔːk triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree with thick, corky bark, found in parts of Asia and other regions, sometimes cultivated for its bark or appearance.

Examples

Asian cork tree
Commercial use of the cork tree bark is common in certain Asian countries.
He admired the deeply fissured bark of the old cork tree near the temple.
The cork tree in our garden turns a brilliant yellow each autumn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Phellodendron - genus of cork trees native to eastern Asia
