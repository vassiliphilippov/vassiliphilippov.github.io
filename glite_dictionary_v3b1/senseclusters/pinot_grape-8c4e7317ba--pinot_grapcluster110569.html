Pinot grape

noun
type of grape used in wines
6.8 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpiː.noʊ greɪp/ /; / /ˈpɪ.noʊ greɪp/ (AmE), /ˈpiː.nəʊ greɪp/ /; / /ˈpɪ.nəʊ greɪp/ (BrE)
Any of several varieties of purple or white wine grapes, notably used in making Burgundy and Champagne wines.

Examples

The Pinot grape variety

The renowned Champagne houses often use the Pinot grape in their blends.
This vineyard is known for its exceptional quality of Pinot grapes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pinot gris - a white variety of Pinot grape, used to make white wine
  • synonym: Pinot noir - a variety of Pinot grape with dark skin, used to make red wine
