cakewalk

verb
perform the cakewalk dance
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪkˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪkˌwɔːk / (BrE)
to perform the cakewalk dance, a style characterized by high steps and strutting, originating in African American communities in the 19th century

Examples

To cakewalk on stage
During the festival, children enthusiastically cakewalk to the lively tune played by the band.
In the old photograph, couples cakewalked with exaggerated elegance and comic grace.
The performers delighted the audience as they cakewalked across the stage in elaborate costumes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dance - to move rhythmically to music, typically following a set sequence of steps
