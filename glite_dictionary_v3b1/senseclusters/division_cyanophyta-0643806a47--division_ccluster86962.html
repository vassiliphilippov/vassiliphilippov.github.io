division Cyanophyta

noun
taxonomic group: cyanobacteria (blue-green algae)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒən saɪˌænoʊˈfaɪtə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒ.ən saɪˌan.əʊˈfaɪ.tə / (BrE)
A former taxonomic division or phylum comprising the cyanobacteria, also known as blue-green algae: photosynthetic, prokaryotic microorganisms found in water and soil.

Examples

division Cyanophyta bacteria
Photosynthetic bacteria in the division Cyanophyta play a vital role in freshwater ecosystems.
Scientists once classified cyanobacteria under the division Cyanophyta due to their algae-like appearance.
The division Cyanophyta includes organisms commonly known as blue-green algae.
