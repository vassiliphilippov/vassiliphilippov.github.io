nonmeasurable

adjective
not having a measure
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈmɛʒərəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈmɛʒərəbəl / (BrE)
not having a defined measure in a particular mathematical measure system, such as Lebesgue measure.

Examples

Nonmeasurable set
A nonmeasurable set cannot be assigned a consistent size using Lebesgue measure.
The concept of nonmeasurable functions is important in advanced mathematical analysis.
He explained why certain sets are nonmeasurable in the context of modern measure theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: measurable - having a defined measure in a given mathematical system
