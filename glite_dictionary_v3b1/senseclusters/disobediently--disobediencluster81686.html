disobediently

adverb
in a manner showing disobedience
5.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd…™so äÀàbiÀêdi…ôntli / (AmE), / Àåd…™s…ôÀàbiÀêdi…ôntli / (BrE)
In a way that shows a refusal to obey rules, orders, or authority.

Examples

To disobediently refuse orders
She disobediently left class before the teacher gave permission.
The child disobediently ignored his parents' instructions to stay indoors.
They disobediently challenged the rules, risking punishment.

Synonyms

  • insubordinately
    9.2 0.00/m
    insubordinately shows refusal to obey orders in formal or work settings, while disobediently means a general refusal to obey rules.
  • defiantly
    5.4 2.20/m
    defiantly shows open, bold resistance to orders or rules, while disobediently simply means refusing to obey authority.
  • rebelliously
    6.6 0.06/m
    rebelliously expresses a wider desire to fight control or norms, while disobediently describes refusing to obey specific rules.
