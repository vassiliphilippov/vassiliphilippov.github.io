overzeal

noun
excessive zeal or enthusiasm
9.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…öÀàziÀêl / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàziÀêl / (BrE)
Excessive zeal or enthusiasm; the state of being excessively eager or fervent about something.

Examples

Civic overzeal
Civic overzeal can be counterproductive when it ignores important regulations.
Her overzeal at work sometimes led to burnout by the end of the week.
The coach's overzeal annoyed some players who preferred a calmer approach.

Synonyms

  • overzealousness
    8.4 0.05/m
    overzealousness means unreasonably eager behavior that often ignores limits or thought, while overzeal is general excessive fervor.
  • overenthusiasm
    5.1 0.01/m
    overenthusiasm shows strong excited feeling that often fades quickly in normal life, while overzeal is deeper, more intense commitment.
  • overeagerness
    5.5 0.06/m
    overeagerness pushes someone to act too soon or without listening, while overzeal is a broader, fervent obsession.
