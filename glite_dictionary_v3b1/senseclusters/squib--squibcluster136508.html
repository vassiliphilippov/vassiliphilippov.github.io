squib

verb
to perform weakly
8.7
Pronunciation: / skwɪb / (AmE), / skwɪb / (BrE)
to fail to perform as expected, especially in a weak or disappointing way

Examples

The team squibbed a chance
The team squibbed their chance to win in the final minutes.
He squibbed his speech, leaving the audience unimpressed.
Despite high expectations, the new product launch squibbed and sales were disappointing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fizzle - to end weakly or fail after a promising start
  • antonym: succeed - to achieve the desired result or perform well
