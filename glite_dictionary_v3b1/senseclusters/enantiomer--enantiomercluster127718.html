enantiomer

noun
one of a pair of mirror-image molecules
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnæntiˌoʊmər / (AmE), / ɪˈnantɪəʊmə / (BrE)
either of a pair of molecules or compounds that are mirror images of each other but are not identical

Examples

Enantiomers in chemistry
Chemists often separate enantiomers to study their individual effects in biological systems.
Thalidomide exists as two enantiomers, one of which is therapeutic and the other harmful.
The enantiomer of a drug can sometimes have very different physiological actions than its counterpart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stereoisomer - each of two or more compounds with the same formula but different spatial arrangements
