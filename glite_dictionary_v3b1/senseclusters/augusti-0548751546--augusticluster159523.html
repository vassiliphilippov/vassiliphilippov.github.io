Augusti

noun
Latin genitive of Augustus
9.9
Pronunciation: / aʊˈɡʌsti /; / ɔːˈɡʌstaɪ / (AmE), / aʊˈɡʌsti /; / ɔːˈɡʌstiː / (BrE)
a Latin grammatical form of 'Augustus' (either showing possession or referring to more than one Augustus), used in historical or classical contexts

Examples

Augusti inscription
The ancient coin bore the inscription 'Divus Augusti' to honor the emperor.
Scholars studied the phrase 'Res Gestae Divi Augusti' to learn about Roman history.
In Latin documents, the name Augusti often appears to indicate possession by Augustus.
During the Tetrarchy, two Augusti governed the eastern and western parts of the Roman Empire.
Coins from the era often depicted both Augusti to symbolize shared power.
The Augusti met to discuss military strategies for defending the empire's borders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Caesars - Roman emperors, especially after Julius Caesar
