load

noun
vein or deposit of ore in rock
Pronunciation: / loʊd / (AmE), / ləʊd / (BrE)
A deposit of valuable ore occurring within distinct boundaries, separating it from the surrounding rock, especially in mining and geology.

Examples

Geologists mapped the load to estimate how much ore could be extracted from the region.
The miners struck a rich load of gold beneath the hillside.
