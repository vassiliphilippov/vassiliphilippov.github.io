reinstruct

verb
to instruct again
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to give instructions to someone again, especially to clarify or correct previous guidance

Examples

To reinstruct the class
After the students misunderstood the assignment, the teacher had to reinstruct the class.
The manager decided to reinstruct the staff on safety procedures after the incident.
If the directions are not clear, it may be necessary to reinstruct the team.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: instruct - to give someone directions or orders
  • synonym: retrain - to teach someone new skills or train again
