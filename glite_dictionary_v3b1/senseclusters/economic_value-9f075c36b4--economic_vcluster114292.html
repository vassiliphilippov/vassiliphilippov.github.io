economic value

noun
exchangeable worth in money, goods, or services

the amount of money, goods, or services regarded as a fair equivalent for something when exchanged or assessed

Examples

The economic value of goods
Before making the purchase, she wanted to assess the economic value of the antique furniture.
Some environmentalists argue that we cannot assign an economic value to clean air and water.
The economic value of the land increased after the new shopping center was built nearby.

Synonyms

  • monetary value
    3.3 0.71/m
    monetary value is the amount of money something can be sold for, while economic value covers money, goods, or services.
  • worth
    1.4 31/m
    worth describes what someone thinks an item equals in money or benefit, while economic value is its fair exchange amount.
  • value
    1.3 175/m
    value often means usefulness or importance as well as money, while economic value focuses on fair monetary or goods exchange.

Related Terms

  • synonym: market value - the price at which something can be sold in an open market
