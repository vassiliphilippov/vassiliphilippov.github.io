palaeogeographic

adjective
ancient landforms
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˌoʊdʒiəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊdʒiəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the study of the physical geography and landforms of the Earth in past geological periods

Examples

Palaeogeographic study
Scientists use palaeogeographic data to reconstruct the positions of ancient continents.
A palaeogeographic map can reveal how mountain ranges have shifted over millions of years.
The palaeogeographic history of the region helps explain its current climate and biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geographic - relating to the natural features of the Earth's surface
  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
