Jacobite

noun
supporter of James II and the Stuart dynasty
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪ.kəˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.ə.baɪt / (BrE)
a supporter of James II of England or his descendants, especially one who participated in efforts to restore the Stuart monarchy after the Glorious Revolution of 1688

Examples

Scottish Jacobites loyal
Many Scottish clans remained loyal as Jacobites long after James II's exile.
The Jacobites staged several uprisings in an attempt to reclaim the throne.
Wearing tartan was once associated with being a Jacobite sympathizer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: royalist - supporter of a monarch or royalist cause
