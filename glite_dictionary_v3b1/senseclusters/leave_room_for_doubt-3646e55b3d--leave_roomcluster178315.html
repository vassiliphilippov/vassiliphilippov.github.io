leave room for doubt

verb
allow for uncertainty or skepticism
1.4
Pronunciation: / liv ruːm fər daʊt / (AmE), / liːv ruːm fə daʊt / (BrE)
to allow for the possibility that something may not be true or certain; to fail to be entirely convincing or conclusive.

Examples

To leave room for doubt
Her explanation left room for doubt about what really happened that evening.
The evidence presented left room for doubt, so the case was not resolved.
The judge's instructions left room for doubt in the minds of the jurors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cast doubt on - to make something seem less certain or trustworthy
  • antonym: leave no room for doubt - to be completely certain or conclusive
