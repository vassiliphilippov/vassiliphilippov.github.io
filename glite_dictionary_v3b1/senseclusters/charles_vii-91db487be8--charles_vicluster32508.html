Charles VII

proper noun
King of France, ended Hundred Years' War
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrlz səˈvɛnθ / (AmE), / tʃɑːlz səˈvɛnθ / (BrE)
(1403–1461) King of France who, with the help of Joan of Arc, led the French to victory over the English and ended the Hundred Years' War.

Examples

Charles VII of France
Charles VII was crowned King of France in Reims in 1429 after the city's liberation.
Joan of Arc played a decisive role in helping Charles VII reclaim his throne.
The reign of Charles VII marked the end of English domination in northern France.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Joan of Arc - French heroine who helped Charles VII drive the English from France
