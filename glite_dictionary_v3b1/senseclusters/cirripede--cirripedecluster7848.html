cirripede

noun
marine crustacean (barnacle)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəˌpid / (AmE), / ˈsɪrɪpiːd / (BrE)
A marine crustacean with feathery appendages for feeding, living attached to submerged surfaces as an adult; commonly known as a barnacle.

Examples

A cirripede barnacle
As larvae, cirripedes swim freely before settling and transforming into adults attached to surfaces.
Studies of cirripedes have revealed fascinating adaptations for filter-feeding in harsh marine environments.
The rocky tide pool was covered in cirripedes clinging tightly to the stones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barnacle - a marine crustacean that attaches itself to submerged surfaces
