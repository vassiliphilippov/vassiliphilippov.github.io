noun

A building or institution used to detain people who are in government custody, such as those awaiting trial or serving a sentence.

Examples

County jailhouse
He spent two nights in the county jailhouse before making bail.
The sheriff escorted the prisoner back to the jailhouse after the trial was postponed.
The small town's jailhouse was built over a century ago and is now a historical site.

Synonyms

  • jail
    1.0 60/m
    jail is the common word for a building that holds accused or convicted people, while jailhouse is slightly more informal.
  • gaol
    8.9 0.2/m
    gaol is the British, older spelling seen in formal texts, while jailhouse is the more common modern American term for the same place.
  • lockup
    5.5 0.9/m
    lockup is a small police detention room used briefly after arrest, while jailhouse is a larger facility for holding prisoners longer.
