obviation

noun
prevention by anticipation
Pronunciation: / ɑb.viˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ɒb.viˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of preventing a problem or difficulty by anticipating it and taking effective measures to counter it.

Examples

The obviation of problems
Effective communication is often key to the obviation of misunderstandings in the workplace.
Good planning leads to the obviation of many potential issues before they arise.
The obviation of errors in the manufacturing process saved the company significant time and money.
