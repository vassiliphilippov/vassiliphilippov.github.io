syllogize

verb
reason using syllogisms
7.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪl.əˌdʒaɪz /; / ˈsɪləˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪl.əˌdʒaɪz /; / ˈsɪləˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to reason or argue using syllogisms, a form of logical deduction

Examples

To syllogize logically
It is tempting to syllogize from these premises, but the conclusion may not follow.
She syllogized that if all men are mortal and Socrates is a man, then Socrates is mortal.
The philosopher loved to syllogize about ethics and morality in his lectures.
It is not always effective to syllogise about real-world situations that involve uncertainty.
Students are trained to syllogise when studying classical logic in university.
The philosopher would often syllogise to demonstrate the logical structure of his arguments.
