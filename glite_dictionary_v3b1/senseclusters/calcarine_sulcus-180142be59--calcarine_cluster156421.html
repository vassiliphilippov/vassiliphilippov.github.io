calcarine sulcus

noun
groove in the occipital lobe of the brain
9.9
Pronunciation: / kælˈkɛrɪn ˈsʌlkəs / (AmE), / kælˈkærɪn ˈsʌlkəs / (BrE)
a deep groove or fissure located on the medial surface of the occipital lobe in the brain, associated with the primary visual cortex

Examples

The calcarine sulcus groove
Damage to the calcarine sulcus can result in partial or complete loss of vision.
During the dissection, the calcarine sulcus was easily identified by its deep and prominent appearance.
The primary visual cortex is located along the calcarine sulcus in the occipital lobe of the brain.
