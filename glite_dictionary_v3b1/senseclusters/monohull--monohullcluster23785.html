monohull

adjective
single-hulled
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnoʊˌhʌl / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəʊˌhʌl / (BrE)
having or relating to a boat or ship with only one hull, as opposed to more than one hull.

Examples

Monohull design
The monohull design is popular among traditional sailors for its handling in rough seas.
Compared to a catamaran, a monohull boat usually heels more when sailing upwind.
They chose a monohull yacht for the race because of its classic style and performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multihull - having more than one hull, such as a catamaran or trimaran
