nondeclarative

adjective
not a statement
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˈklærətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈklærətɪv / (BrE)
not expressed as a declarative sentence; relating to questions, commands, or exclamations rather than statements.

Examples

Nondeclarative sentences
In English, questions and commands are considered nondeclarative sentences.
The teacher asked students to identify which sentences were nondeclarative in the exercise.
Exclamations like 'What a surprise!' are also nondeclarative forms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative - expressed as a statement or declaration
  • synonym: imperative - expressing a command or request
  • synonym: interrogative - relating to questions
