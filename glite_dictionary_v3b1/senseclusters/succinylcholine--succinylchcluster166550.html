succinylcholine

noun
muscle relaxant drug for surgery
9.8
Pronunciation: / səkˌsɪn.əlˈkoʊ.lin / (AmE), / səkˌsɪn.ɪlˈkəʊ.liːn / (BrE)
a synthetic compound used as a muscle relaxant during surgical procedures, especially as an adjunct to anesthesia

Examples

Succinylcholine muscle relaxant
Because succinylcholine acts rapidly, it is commonly used to facilitate endotracheal intubation.
Some patients may have an adverse reaction to succinylcholine, requiring alternative muscle relaxants.
The anesthesiologist administered succinylcholine to induce temporary muscle paralysis for the operation.
