Victor Emanuel III

proper noun
King of Italy, 1900–1946
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪktər ɪˌmænjuˈɛl ˈðɜrd / (AmE), / ˈvɪktə ɪˌmænjʊˈɛl ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(1869–1947) King of Italy from 1900 to 1946, known for appointing Mussolini as prime minister and abdicating before the abolition of the monarchy.

Examples

Victor Emanuel III king of Italy
Victor Emanuel III reigned during both World Wars and played a significant role in Italian history.
After abdicating, Victor Emanuel III spent his remaining years in exile in Egypt.
It was Victor Emanuel III who appointed Mussolini prime minister, shaping the course of Italian politics.
