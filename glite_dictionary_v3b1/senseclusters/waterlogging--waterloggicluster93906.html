waterlogging

noun
soil saturated with water
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌlɔɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌlɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
a condition where soil or land becomes saturated with water, making it unsuitable for normal use or plant growth

Examples

Soil waterlogging
Prolonged waterlogging can damage crops by depriving their roots of oxygen.
Farmers use drainage systems to prevent waterlogging in their fields after heavy rains.
