halliard

noun
rope for raising a sail or flag
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhæljərd / (AmE), / ˈhæliəd / (BrE)
A rope used for raising or lowering a sail, yard, or flag on a ship.

Examples

Halliards on ship
Old ships often had several halliards rigged for different sails and flags.
She checked that the flag halliard was properly secured to prevent it from slipping.
The sailor tightened the halliard to raise the sail in the morning breeze.

Related Terms

  • synonym: halyard - alternative spelling of halliard, same nautical meaning
