remove

verb
kill intentionally; euphemism, esp. in criminal context
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmuːv / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːv / (BrE)
(informal, euphemistic) To kill someone intentionally and with premeditation, especially as an organized crime act.

Examples

To remove a rival
It was rumored that the gang planned to remove anyone who betrayed them.
She feared the cartel would remove him if he refused to cooperate.
The mob was hired to remove the witness before the trial could begin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assassinate - to murder (an important person) for political or religious reasons
  • synonym: murder - to kill someone intentionally and unlawfully
