tetrahedral

adjective
four-faced shape
7.9
Pronunciation: /te.trə.ˈhiː.drəl/ (AmE), /te.trə.ˈhiː.drəl/ (BrE)
having the shape or characteristics of a solid with four triangular faces

Examples

A tetrahedral shape
The methane molecule has a tetrahedral structure.
A diamond crystal is made up of carbon atoms arranged in a tetrahedral pattern.
The geometry of the molecule is best described as tetrahedral due to its four bonding pairs.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: octahedral - having eight faces, typically triangular
  • degree lower: triangular - having three sides or faces
