white feather

noun
symbol of cowardice
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈfɛðər / (AmE), / waɪt ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
A symbol of cowardice, especially one given as a form of accusation or insult.

Examples

A white feather symbol
During the war, receiving a white feather in the mail was considered a serious insult.
She refused to give out a white feather, believing it was an unfair way to judge others.
The film depicts a young man tormented by the white feather he received from local women.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bravery - courage; behavior showing courage
  • synonym: cowardice - lack of bravery
