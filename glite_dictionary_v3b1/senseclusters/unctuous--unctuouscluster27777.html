unctuous

adjective
excessively flattering or ingratiating
Pronunciation: / ˈʌŋk.tʃu.əs / (AmE), / ˈʌŋk.tjʊ.əs / (BrE)
(of a person or speech) excessively or insincerely flattering; having an oily, ingratiating manner that is unpleasant.

Examples

An unctuous manner
His unctuous manner made everyone in the meeting feel uncomfortable.
She greeted us with an unctuous smile that seemed anything but genuine.
The politician's unctuous promises failed to win the trust of the voters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sincere - genuine, honest in speech or manner
  • synonym: smarmy - excessively flattering or ingratiating; insincerely earnest
