(of a person or speech) excessively or insincerely flattering; having an oily, ingratiating manner that is unpleasant.

Examples

An unctuous manner
His unctuous manner made everyone in the meeting feel uncomfortable.
She greeted us with an unctuous smile that seemed anything but genuine.
The politician's unctuous promises failed to win the trust of the voters.

  • oily

    oily comes across as slick and insincere in speech or smile, while unctuous means excessively flattering and unpleasantly ingratiating.
  • fulsome
    9.3 0.23/m
    fulsome is overly lavish praise that can feel excessive or clumsy, while unctuous is more about an oily, insincere manner.
  • oleaginous
    9.9 0.03/m

  • buttery

  • soapy
