varve

noun
annual sediment layer
Pronunciation: / vɑrv / (AmE), / vɑːv / (BrE)
a thin layer of sediment or sedimentary rock deposited in a single year, especially in lakes, often used to study past climates

Examples

A varve layer
The geologist examined each varve to determine how the climate changed over thousands of years.
A single varve can reveal information about one year in a lake's history.
By counting the varves, scientists estimated the age of the sediment core.
Each varve in the lake bed represents one year of sediment accumulation.
