sneak peek

noun
early preview of something unreleased
3.4 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / snik pik / (AmE), / sniÀêk piÀêk / (BrE)
a preview or early look at something, especially a product or show that has not yet been released to the public

Examples

A sneak peek trailer
Fans got a sneak peek at the upcoming movie in a special trailer.
The company offered a sneak peek of its new smartphone during the conference.
The magazine published a sneak peek of the designer's new collection.

Synonyms

  • sneak preview
    3.7 0.35/m
    sneak preview shows a short advance screening to a select audience before release, while sneak peek offers a quick early look for the public.
  • preview
    3.1 1.85/m
    preview gives an official early viewing or excerpt for the public or press, while sneak peek suggests an informal short look before release.
  • peek
    2.1 5.59/m
    peek is a quick, often secret look that can be private or casual, while sneak peek refers to an early short preview shown before public release.
