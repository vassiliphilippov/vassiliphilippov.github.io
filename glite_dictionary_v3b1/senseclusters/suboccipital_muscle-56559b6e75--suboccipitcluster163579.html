suboccipital muscle

noun
muscle beneath the occipital bone
7.1
Pronunciation: / sʌbˌɑkˈsɪpɪtəl ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / sʌbˌɒkˈsɪpɪtəl ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
Any of several small muscles located beneath the occipital bone at the base of the skull, involved in head movement and posture.

Examples

The suboccipital muscle group
A physical therapist may target the suboccipital muscle to relieve neck pain.
Injury to the suboccipital muscle can affect your ability to turn your head smoothly.
The suboccipital muscle group can become tense after prolonged computer use.
