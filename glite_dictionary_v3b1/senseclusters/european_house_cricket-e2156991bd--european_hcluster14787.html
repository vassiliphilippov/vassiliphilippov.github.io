European house cricket

noun
a species of cricket often found in houses
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən haʊs ˈkrɪkɪt / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən haʊs ˈkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
a small species of cricket (Acheta domesticus) commonly found in human dwellings, naturalized in parts of America

Examples

The European house cricket
Scientists study the European house cricket to understand urban insect adaptation.
Some people keep European house crickets as food for pet reptiles and amphibians.
The European house cricket is often heard chirping indoors during the summer months.
