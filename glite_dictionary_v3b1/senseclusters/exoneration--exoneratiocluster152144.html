exoneration

noun
the act of clearing from blame or criticism
9.1
Pronunciation: / ɪgˌzɑnəˈreɪʃən /; / ɪɡˌzɑnəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪgˌzɒnəˈreɪʃən /; / ɪɡˌzɒnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of officially being cleared from blame, criticism, or responsibility, especially after being accused of something

Examples

Complete exoneration
After years of legal battles, the DNA evidence led to his complete exoneration.
Public exoneration restored her reputation after months of damaging rumors.
The committee's report was a welcome exoneration of the company's safety record.
Her exoneration came as a relief to her family and friends.
Public support for exoneration increased after the new DNA tests were revealed.
The evidence led to his exoneration after years of suspicion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condemnation - the act of expressing strong disapproval; sentencing someone as guilty
  • antonym: conviction - the finding of guilt in a court of law
  • synonym: vindication - the act of clearing someone of blame or suspicion
