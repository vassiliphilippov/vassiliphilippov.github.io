parade

noun
an ostentatious display; show of something
Pronunciation: / pəˈreɪd / (AmE), / pəˈreɪd / (BrE)
an open or showy display of something, or a series of people or things passing by or shown

Examples

A parade of stars
He put on a parade of confidence, though he was nervous inside.
She made a parade of her generosity at every opportunity.
Their argument was just a parade of grievances and complaints.
During the conference, there was a parade of guest speakers presenting new ideas.
The city witnessed a parade of vintage cars along its main street.
The fashion show ended with a parade of the designers' most extravagant creations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: display - an act of showing something, usually for people to look at or admire
  • synonym: procession - an organized group or line moving forward in an orderly way, especially for a ceremony
  • synonym: show - a display or exhibition; a visible presentation
