Ferdinand Julius Cohn

proper noun
German botanist; founder of bacteriology
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrdɪnænd ˈdʒuːliəs ˈkoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːdɪnænd ˈdʒuːliəs ˈkəʊn / (BrE)
(1828–1898) A German botanist recognized as one of the founders of bacteriology for his pioneering classification of bacteria as plants.

Examples

Ferdinand Julius Cohn bacteriology founder
Ferdinand Julius Cohn was instrumental in establishing bacteriology as a scientific discipline.
Many textbooks attribute the classification of bacteria as plants to Ferdinand Julius Cohn.
The pioneering work of Ferdinand Julius Cohn laid the foundation for modern microbiology.
