creep

noun
slow deformation or movement under stress
Pronunciation: / krip / (AmE), / kriːp / (BrE)
slow, continuous deformation or movement in a material, usually caused by long-term stress or exposure to heat

Examples

Metal creep under heat
Engineers monitor creep in metal parts exposed to high temperatures over long periods.
Plastic creep can occur if a constant load is applied for an extended time.
The creep of glaciers shapes the landscape over thousands of years.
