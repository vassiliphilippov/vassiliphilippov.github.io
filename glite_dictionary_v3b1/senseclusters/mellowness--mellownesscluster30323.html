mellowness

noun
pleasant richness or smoothness
4.7 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈmel.oʊ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈmel.əʊ.nəs/ (BrE)
a quality of being pleasantly smooth, rich, and full, often used to describe sensations such as taste, sound, or mood

Examples

Mellowness of taste
Aged cheese develops a mellowness that makes it especially enjoyable to eat.
The mellowness of the jazz band’s performance created a wonderful atmosphere in the lounge.
There is a certain mellowness in his voice that comforts listeners.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harshness - the quality of being rough or unpleasant, the opposite of mellowness
  • synonym: richness - the quality of being rich, full, and pleasing
