symphysiotomy

noun
pelvic widening surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmfɪziˈɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌsɪmfɪziˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the pubic symphysis is divided to widen the pelvis during childbirth

Examples

A symphysiotomy operation
In rare cases, a symphysiotomy may be performed when a cesarean section is not possible.
The patient recovered well after undergoing a symphysiotomy during a complicated labor.
Medical guidelines now limit the use of symphysiotomy due to potential risks and complications.
