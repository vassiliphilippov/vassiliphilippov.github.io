overcontrol

noun
excessive corrective control
7.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.kən.ˈtroʊl/ /; / /ə.vər.kən.ˈtroʊl/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.kən.ˈtrəʊl/ /; / /ə.və.kən.ˈtrəʊl/ (BrE)
the act of applying too much corrective control, causing instability or repeated oscillations, especially in engineering or piloting.

Examples

Aircraft overcontrol
The pilot's overcontrol of the aircraft led to dangerous oscillations during landing.
Inexperienced drivers sometimes exhibit overcontrol when trying to correct a skid.
Automatic systems are designed to prevent overcontrol and maintain stability in modern vehicles.

  • antonym: undercontrol - insufficient corrective control
