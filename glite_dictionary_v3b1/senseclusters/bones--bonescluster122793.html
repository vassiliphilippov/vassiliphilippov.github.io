bones

noun
pair of percussive sticks played by clicking together
Pronunciation: / boʊnz / (AmE), / bəʊnz / (BrE)
A percussion instrument consisting of a pair of hollow pieces of wood or bone, typically held in one hand and clicked together rhythmically, especially used in traditional and folk music.

Examples

To play the bones
She taught herself to play the bones after watching a street performer.
The Irish musician played the bones with remarkable speed during the session.
Traditional folk bands sometimes include bones as part of their percussion section.

Related Terms

  • synonym: castanets - a percussion instrument of two shells clicked together in the hand
