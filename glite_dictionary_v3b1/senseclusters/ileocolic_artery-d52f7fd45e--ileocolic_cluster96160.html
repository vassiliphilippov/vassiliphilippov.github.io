ileocolic artery

noun
artery supplying ileum and colon
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.li.oʊˈkɑː.lɪk ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.li.əˈkɒ.lɪk ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an artery branching from the superior mesenteric artery that supplies blood to the terminal ileum, cecum, appendix, and ascending colon

Examples

The ileocolic artery branch
A blockage in the ileocolic artery may compromise blood flow to the appendix and ascending colon.
During the operation, the ileocolic artery was ligated to control bleeding.
The surgeon carefully identified the ileocolic artery before proceeding with the resection of the cecum.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: superior mesenteric artery - major artery supplying the midgut
