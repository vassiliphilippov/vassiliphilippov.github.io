Jim Crow law

noun
law enforcing racial segregation (U.S. history)
9.0 0.36/m
Pronunciation: / d í…™m kro ä l…î / (AmE), / d í…™m kr…ô ä l…îÀê / (BrE)
(in the United States) any of the laws that enforced racial segregation, especially in the southern states, from the late 19th century until the mid-20th century.

Examples

Jim Crow laws in South
Many activists fought bravely to challenge and overturn the Jim Crow laws.
The Jim Crow laws institutionalized discrimination against African Americans for nearly a century.
The legacy of the Jim Crow law period can still be seen in some American communities today.

Synonyms

  • Jim Crow
    8.9 2.19/m
    Jim Crow names a broad social system of segregation and daily customs in society, while Jim Crow law refers to specific legal statutes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: civil rights law - a law protecting individual rights and prohibiting discrimination
  • synonym: segregation law - a law enforcing the separation of groups based on race or other criteria
