proscription

noun
official prohibition by decree
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈskrɪpʃən / (AmE), / prəʊˈskrɪpʃən / (BrE)
an official order or act that forbids or bans something or someone, especially by law or authority

Examples

A proscription order
During the regime, there was a proscription on all opposition parties.
The proscription of the book led to widespread controversy and debate.
The government issued a proscription against the use of certain chemicals.
During the Roman era, proscription lists were used to eliminate political enemies.
The proscription of certain books was enforced by harsh penalties.
The new government's proscription of the political party led to widespread protests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • synonym: banishment - the action of sending someone away as a punishment
  • synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • antonym: permission - the action of allowing something
  • antonym: permit - to allow something officially
  • synonym: prohibition - the action of forbidding something by law
