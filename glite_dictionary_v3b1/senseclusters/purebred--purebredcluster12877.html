purebred

adjective
bred from a recognized breed (animals)
6.2 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpjʊr.bred/ /; / /ˈpjuːr.bred/ (AmE), /ˈpjʊə.bred/ /; / /ˈpjuː.bred/ (BrE)
(of an animal) bred for many generations from members of a recognized breed or strain, without outside crossbreeding.

Examples

Purebred dog
Many horse owners prefer purebred animals for their predictable traits and lineage.
She bought a purebred Siamese cat from a reputable breeder.
The kennel only accepts purebred dogs for registration in the show.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mongrel - an animal resulting from the crossbreeding of different breeds or strains
  • synonym: pedigree - having a documented ancestry or lineage
