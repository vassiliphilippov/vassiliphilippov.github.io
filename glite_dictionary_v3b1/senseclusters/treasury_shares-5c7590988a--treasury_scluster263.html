treasury shares

noun
shares held by issuing corporation
6.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtreʒ.ə.rɪ ʃerz/ /; / /ˈtreʒ.rɪ ʃerz/ (AmE), /ˈtreʒ.ə.ri ʃeəz/ /; / /ˈtreʒ.rɪ ʃeəz/ (BrE)
Shares that have been issued and subsequently bought back by the issuing corporation, held in the company's treasury and not considered outstanding.

Examples

Treasury shares held
Treasury shares do not entitle the corporation to vote at shareholder meetings.
After the merger, the business held a significant amount of treasury shares for future corporate actions.
The company increased its treasury shares after buying back stock from the open market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outstanding shares - shares currently held by shareholders and not by the issuing corporation
  • synonym: treasury stock - shares bought back and held by the issuing company
