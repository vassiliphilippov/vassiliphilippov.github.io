wadi

noun
dry riverbed or valley in North Africa/Middle East
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑː.di / (AmE), / ˈwæd.i / (BrE)
A dry riverbed or valley in North Africa or the Middle East that contains water only during the rainy season.

Examples

A wadi valley
After a rare storm, the wadi was suddenly filled with rushing water.
During the dry season, the wadi was home to desert plants and animals.
The explorers camped near the wadi and waited for the rains to bring water.

  • synonym: arroyo - a dry creek or stream bed in Spain or the southwest United States
