speed

verb
move or travel quickly
Pronunciation: / spiːd / (AmE), / spiːd / (BrE)
to move or travel quickly, often faster than usual or allowed

Examples

To speed down the road
Ambulances often speed through traffic in emergencies.
If you speed on this road, you risk getting a ticket.
She watched the train speed past the platform in a blur.
As the deadline approached, she sped through the last few pages of her report.
Seagulls speed across the water when chasing after a fishing boat.
The ambulance sped through the city streets with its siren blaring.
He was fined after he was caught speeding on the highway.
Police officers monitor certain areas to deter drivers from speeding.
The camera automatically photographs vehicles that speed past the school zone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accelerate - to begin to move faster; to increase in speed
  • antonym: crawl - to move very slowly
  • antonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully, especially to avoid being noticed
  • synonym: drive - to operate and control the direction and speed of a motor vehicle
  • synonym: hasten - to move or act quickly
  • synonym: race - to move very quickly or compete to be first
  • synonym: rush - to move or do something with great haste
  • antonym: slow - to reduce speed or move slowly
  • antonym: slow down - to reduce speed; to become slower
