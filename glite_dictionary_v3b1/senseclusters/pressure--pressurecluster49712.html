pressure

noun
a stressful or oppressive situation
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃɚ /; / ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
a difficult situation or strong demands that cause stress, urgency, or anxiety

Examples

Work pressure
Financial pressure forced the family to make tough choices.
She felt enormous pressure at work as deadlines piled up.
The pressure to perform well in school can be overwhelming for some students.
Amidst the pressure of competing demands, he remained calm and focused.
She struggled to cope with the pressure of her new responsibilities at work.
The pressure of deadlines forced the team to work late into the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insistence - persistent demand for attention or action
  • antonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from distress
  • synonym: stress - mental or emotional strain resulting from demanding circumstances
