seriality

noun
publishing in parts
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌsɪəriˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or practice of presenting or happening in a series or sequence, especially in art, literature, or media

Examples

Seriality in storytelling
The seriality of the television show kept viewers eagerly awaiting each new episode.
Many classic novels gained popularity through seriality in newspapers before being published as books.
The author embraced seriality to build suspense and maintain reader interest over several months.
The seriality of the episodes kept viewers engaged week after week.
Modern artists often explore seriality by repeating patterns or themes in their work.
The novel's seriality allowed readers to follow the story across several installments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuity - the state of being continuous or connected
  • antonym: randomness - the quality of lacking any predictable order or plan
  • synonym: sequence - a particular order in which related things follow each other
  • synonym: serialization - the act of publishing or presenting in a series
