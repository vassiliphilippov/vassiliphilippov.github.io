palisading

noun
building a palisade
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpælɪˈseɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈseɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of building or forming a palisade, especially as a defensive barrier

Examples

The palisading of a camp
The palisading of the village was completed before the onset of winter.
Early settlers relied on palisading to protect their homes from wild animals.
The engineer supervised the palisading along the riverbank to prevent erosion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demolition - the act of destroying or tearing down structures
  • synonym: fortification - the act of building defensive structures
