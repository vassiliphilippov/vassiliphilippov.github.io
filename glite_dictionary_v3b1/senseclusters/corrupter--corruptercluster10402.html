corrupter

noun
cause of data damage
8.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌptər / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a device, program, or fault that causes digital data or files to become corrupted or unreadable

Examples

File corrupter
The faulty hard drive acted as a corrupter and destroyed several important documents.
A power surge can be a silent corrupter of your computer's files.
The technician warned that outdated software might become a corrupter if not updated regularly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protector - something that keeps data safe from harm or corruption
  • synonym: virus - a malicious program that can damage or corrupt files
