potamogale

noun
African amphibious insectivore mammal
7.4
Pronunciation: / poʊˌtæməˈɡeɪli / (AmE), / pəˌtaməˈɡɑːli / (BrE)
an African amphibious insectivorous mammal resembling an otter, native to Central and West Africa, known for its webbed tail and aquatic habits

Examples

The potamogale mammal
Researchers are studying the habitat of the potamogale to better understand its role in the river ecosystem.
The potamogale glided silently through the shallow stream in search of insects and small fish.
Unlike most insectivores, the potamogale spends much of its life swimming with its broad, flattened tail.
