semidouble

noun
crochet stitch height
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈdʌbəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈdʌbəl / (BrE)
a crochet stitch that is taller than a single crochet but shorter than a double crochet

Examples

Semidouble crochet stitch
The pattern instructs you to use a semidouble stitch for the next row.
After mastering the single crochet, she learned how to make a semidouble for added texture.
A semidouble is perfect when you want a stitch that is not too tall or too short.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: double crochet - a crochet stitch taller than a semidouble
  • degree lower: single crochet - a basic crochet stitch with the shortest height
