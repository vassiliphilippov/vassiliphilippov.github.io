smallclothes

noun
historical men's breeches
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɔlˌkloʊðz / (AmE), / ˈsmɔːlˌkləʊðz / (BrE)
knee-length breeches or short trousers worn by men in the 17th and 18th centuries as part of formal dress, often fastened at the knee

Examples

Silk smallclothes
In the portrait, the gentleman is dressed in elegant smallclothes and a powdered wig.
During the 18th century, men of status wore silk smallclothes to formal events.
The museum displayed a set of embroidered smallclothes from the colonial era.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breeches - short trousers fastened just below the knee
  • degree higher: trousers - an outer garment covering the body from the waist to the ankles, with a separate part for each leg
