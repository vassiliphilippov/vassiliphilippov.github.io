Delphic

adjective
obscurely prophetic or ambiguous
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛlfɪk / (AmE), / ˈdɛlfɪk / (BrE)
deliberately mysterious, ambiguous, or hard to interpret, especially in a way that seems prophetic or refers to the oracle at Delphi

Examples

Delphic statement
Analysts struggled to interpret the CEO's Delphic statement about the company's future.
Her response was so Delphic that nobody could tell what she truly meant.
The politician's Delphic remarks left the press guessing about his real intentions.
Her advice was so Delphic that nobody in the group understood what she really meant.
Many ancient rulers sought guidance from the Delphic oracle before starting wars.
The senator made a Delphic remark that left many wondering about his true intentions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - easy to understand
