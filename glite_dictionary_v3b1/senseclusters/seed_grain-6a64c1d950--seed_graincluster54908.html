seed grain

noun
grain or seeds reserved for planting
3.4 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / sid …°re…™n / (AmE), / siÀêd …°re…™n / (BrE)
good quality seeds, such as kernels of corn, that are kept for planting rather than for consumption.

Examples

Good seed grain
Farmers must store seed grain in dry conditions to prevent spoilage.
Only the best seed grain was selected to ensure a healthy crop for the next season.
The agricultural cooperative supplied certified seed grain to local growers.

Synonyms

  • seed corn
    4.1 0.03/m
    seed corn is high-quality corn kernels saved for planting, while seed grain means good grain seeds kept for sowing.
  • seed stock
    3.6 0.07/m
    seed stock is seeds or tubers held for future planting, while seed grain means good grain seeds kept for sowing.
