entrain

verb
synchronize with an external rhythm
Pronunciation: / ənˈtreɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtreɪn / (BrE)
(in neuroscience, music, and related sciences) to synchronize a biological or physical rhythm or oscillation with an external rhythm or stimulus; to cause or become phase-locked.

Examples

To entrain brain rhythms
The brain's neurons entrain to the steady rhythm of the metronome during the experiment.
By the second chorus the choir had entrained to the conductor's subtle changes in tempo.
Shift workers often use timed light exposure to entrain their circadian rhythms and improve sleep.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phase-lock - to fix the phase relationship between two oscillations so they operate in step
  • synonym: synchronize - to cause events or processes to happen at the same time or rate
