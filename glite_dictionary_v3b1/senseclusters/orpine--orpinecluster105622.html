orpine

noun
a perennial temperate plant with purplish-white flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.pɪn / (AmE), / ˈɔː.pɪn / (BrE)
a perennial plant native to northern temperate regions, with toothed leaves and clusters of small purplish-white flowers, often known as 'live-forever' or 'sedum'

Examples

The orpine plant
Botanists studied how orpine adapted to rocky soils in northern climates.
She identified the succulent as orpine, also known as 'live-forever' due to its hardy nature.
The orpine thrived at the edge of the garden, its clusters of flowers attracting bees all summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sedum - a genus of flowering plants, also called stonecrops, closely related to orpine
