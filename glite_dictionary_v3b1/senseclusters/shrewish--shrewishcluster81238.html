shrewish

adjective
habitually nagging or quarrelsome (person)
Pronunciation: / ˈʃruɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃruːɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a woman) habitually complaining, nagging, or quarrelsome.

Examples

A shrewish woman
Despite her shrewish complaints, the project moved forward successfully.
He found her shrewish attitude exhausting after only a few days.
Her shrewish remarks made the dinner party uncomfortable for everyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: even-tempered - calm and not easily annoyed or angered
  • synonym: nagging - persistently faultfinding or complaining
