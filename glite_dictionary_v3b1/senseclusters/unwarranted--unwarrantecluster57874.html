unwarranted

adjective
not justified or deserved
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɔrən.tɪd /; / ʌnˈwɔr.ən.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɒr.ən.tɪd /; / ʌnˈwɒr.əntɪd / (BrE)
not justified, deserved, or done without a good reason or official permission

Examples

Unwarranted assumptions
He made unwarranted assumptions about her motives without evidence.
Her criticism was completely unwarranted and unfair.
The company issued an apology for the unwarranted delay in service.
His criticism was entirely unwarranted and hurt her feelings unnecessarily.
Many felt the new regulations imposed unwarranted restrictions on businesses.
The police conducted an unwarranted search of the premises, violating the residents' rights.
His arrest was unwarranted and later proved to be a mistake.
The manager's criticism of her work was completely unwarranted.
There was unwarranted fear about the safety of the new vaccine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authorized - officially approved or permitted
  • synonym: groundless - not based on any good reason
  • antonym: justified - having a good reason or justification
  • synonym: unfounded - having no basis in fact; not justified
  • synonym: unjustified - not shown to be right or reasonable
