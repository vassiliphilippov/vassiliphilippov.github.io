vestige

noun
small remaining trace or sign
8.4 2.62/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆvÉ›stÉªdÊ’ / (AmE), / ËˆvÉ›stÉªdÊ’ / (BrE)
a small remaining part or sign of something that no longer exists or is disappearing

Examples

A vestige of history
Archaeologists discovered a vestige of the ancient civilization buried beneath the desert sands.
Modern buildings now stand where only a vestige of the forest remains.
There was not a vestige of doubt in her mind after she saw the evidence.

Synonyms

  • trace
    3.3 20/m
    a trace is a small mark or sign left behind, while a vestige is the last remaining sign of something disappearing.
  • remanence
    9.6 0.00/m
    remanence is a lingering leftover or effect often used in science or formal texts, while a vestige is a common sign of something lost.
  • remnant
    4.2 10/m
    a remnant is a physical piece left after use or destruction, while a vestige is usually a small sign that something has disappeared.

Related Terms
