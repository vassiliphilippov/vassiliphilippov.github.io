hoot

verb
to make a loud sound
2.2 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / huÀêt / (AmE), / huÀêt / (BrE)
to make a loud, deep sound like that of an owl or a car horn

Examples

Hoot a horn
The owl began to hoot as night fell in the quiet forest.
Drivers sometimes hoot their horns to warn others at busy intersections.
We heard someone hooting with laughter at the back of the theater.

Synonyms

  • hoo
    9.9 0.01/m
    hoo is the low, hollow call of an owl in nature, while hoot can mean that owl sound or a loud car horn.
  • whoo
    9.9 0.00/m
    whoo is a soft, owl-like call often used to copy bird sounds, while hoot also means the real owl cry or a car horn.
  • honk
    1.6 2.49/m
    honk is a loud, harsh sound made by a car horn in traffic, while hoot can mean that sound or an owl's deep cry.
  • toot
    3.3 0.34/m
    toot is a short, sharp horn or whistle sound often quick or playful, while hoot is a longer, deeper owl cry or car horn.

Related Terms
