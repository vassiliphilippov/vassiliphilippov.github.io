ball up

verb
mess up or ruin (informal)
8.7
Pronunciation: / bɔl ʌp / (AmE), / bɔːl ʌp / (BrE)
to make a mess of something; to ruin or spoil through clumsiness or error

Examples

Don't ball up the report
Don't ball up the report this time; we need it done correctly.
He managed to ball up the whole presentation with his constant interruptions.
She really balled up her chances by missing the deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bungle - to carry out a task clumsily or incompetently
  • antonym: improve - to make something better or less disordered
  • synonym: mess up - to make a mistake or cause confusion in something
