roast

verb
subject to humorous ridicule
4.6
Pronunciation: / roʊst / (AmE), / rəʊst / (BrE)
to subject someone to good-natured ridicule, often in a humorous or public setting

Examples

To roast a friend
His classmates playfully roasted him for his unusual fashion sense.
The comedian will roast several celebrities during the televised event.
They invited friends to roast the retiring manager at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mock - to make fun of or ridicule someone
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration of
