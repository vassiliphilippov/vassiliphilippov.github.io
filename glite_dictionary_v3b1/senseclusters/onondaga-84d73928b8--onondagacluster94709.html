Onondaga

noun
Iroquoian language of the Onondaga people
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːnənˈdɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˌɒnənˈdɑːɡə / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language spoken by the Onondaga people, native to the area of present-day New York.

Examples

Onondaga language
He studied both Onondaga and Mohawk to understand their linguistic similarities.
Linguists are working to preserve Onondaga as part of broader indigenous language revitalization efforts.
Only a handful of elders still speak Onondaga fluently in the community.
