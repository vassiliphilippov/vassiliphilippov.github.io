tremor

noun
involuntary shaking or vibration
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛmər /; / ˈtrɛmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmə / (BrE)
an involuntary shaking or quivering movement, such as in the body or voice, often caused by illness, fear, or nervousness

Examples

A hand tremor
A slight tremor ran through his body as he stepped onto the stage.
She tried to hide the tremor in her voice when she delivered the sad news.
The patient's hands displayed a noticeable tremor as a symptom of the disease.
She tried to hide the tremor in her voice as she spoke.
The earthquake caused a slight tremor that was felt across the city.
The patient experienced a mild tremor in his right hand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quiver - to tremble or shake with a slight rapid motion
  • synonym: shake - to move with short, quick movements
  • synonym: shiver - a slight shaking movement caused by cold, fear, or excitement
  • antonym: steadiness - the quality of being steady and not shaking
