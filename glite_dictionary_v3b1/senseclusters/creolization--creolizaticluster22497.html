creolization

noun
cultural blending process
Pronunciation: / ˌkriːələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkriːələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which elements from different cultures blend and develop into new cultural identities, practices, or expressions

Examples

Creolization of cultures
The creolization of language in the Caribbean led to the development of unique dialects.
Modern cuisine often reflects the creolization of different culinary traditions.
Scholars study creolization to understand how new cultural forms emerge from contact between societies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assimilation - the process of absorbing and integrating into a different culture
  • synonym: hybridization - the process of combining different elements to form something new
