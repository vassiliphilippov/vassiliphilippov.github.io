oversaturate

verb
to supply too much
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsætʃəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsætʃəreɪt / (BrE)
to supply or fill something, especially a market or area, with more than it can absorb or use, reducing its value or effectiveness

Examples

To oversaturate the market
If you oversaturate the market with similar products, customers may lose interest.
Too many advertisements can oversaturate viewers and make them ignore the message.
Artists worry that social media can oversaturate audiences with content, making it harder to stand out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deplete - to reduce the amount of something so that very little is left
  • synonym: flood - to fill or supply with an excessive amount
  • degree lower: saturate - to fill something completely so that no more can be added
