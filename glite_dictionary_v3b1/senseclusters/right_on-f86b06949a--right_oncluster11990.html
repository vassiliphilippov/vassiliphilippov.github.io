right on

interjection
used to show agreement or approval
4.6
Pronunciation: / raɪt ɑn / (AmE), / raɪt ɒn / (BrE)
used to express strong agreement or approval, especially in informal contexts.

Examples

Right on! Cheers
When someone proposed a toast to friendship, everyone around the table shouted, “Right on!”
“Are you coming to the concert?” “Right on! I wouldn’t miss it for anything.”
“You think people should be treated equally?” “Right on, that’s exactly what I believe.”

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolutely - used as an exclamation to express emphatic agreement
  • synonym: exactly - used to show agreement or assent in conversation
