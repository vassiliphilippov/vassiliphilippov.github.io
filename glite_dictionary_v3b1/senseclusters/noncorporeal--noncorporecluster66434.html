noncorporeal

adjective
without physical form
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkɔrˈpɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkɔːˈpɔːriəl / (BrE)
not having a physical body or form; immaterial or intangible

Examples

Noncorporeal entity
Some philosophers argue that the mind is a noncorporeal entity, separate from the body.
The story described a noncorporeal presence that could move through walls.
Many cultures have legends about noncorporeal beings such as spirits or ghosts.
Patents are considered noncorporeal property because they do not exist in a physical form.
The artist spoke about the noncorporeal nature of inspiration and creativity.
In legal documents, trademarks are often listed as noncorporeal assets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immaterial - not consisting of matter; intangible
  • synonym: incorporeal - lacking material form or substance
  • synonym: intangible - unable to be touched or physically measured
