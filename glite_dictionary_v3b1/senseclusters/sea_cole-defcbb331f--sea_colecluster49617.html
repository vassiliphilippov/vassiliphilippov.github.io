sea cole

noun
coastal perennial plant, edible leaves
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / siÀê ko äl / (AmE), / siÀê k…ô äl / (BrE)
a perennial plant of coastal sands in northern Europe and around the Baltic and Black Seas, with racemes of small white flowers and large blue-green leaves, often used as a potherb

Examples

The sea cole plant
Botanists study sea cole because it thrives in salty, sandy environments near the sea.
In spring, sea cole produces delicate white flowers atop sturdy stems on the shoreline.
The leaves of sea cole were once commonly gathered and cooked as a vegetable along European coasts.
