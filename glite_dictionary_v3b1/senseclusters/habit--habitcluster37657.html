habit

noun
religious attire or dress
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhæbɪt / (AmE), / ˈhæbɪt / (BrE)
a distinctive garment worn by a member of a religious order, especially as a symbol of their commitment

Examples

A nun's habit
Each religious order has its own distinctive habit to identify its members.
The nun donned her white habit before morning prayers.
Visitors were instructed not to touch the habit worn by the monks during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: robe - a long, loose outer garment often worn as clothing for special occasions or by certain professionals
  • synonym: uniform - the distinctive clothing worn by members of the same organization or body
