follow

verb
accept and act under someone's leadership or guidance
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈfɒl.əʊ/ (BrE)
to accept, obey, or act according to rules, advice, instructions, or beliefs

Examples

To follow instructions
After much thought, she decided to follow her mentor's advice.
Many young artists follow the teachings of classical painters.
The soldiers were willing to follow their captain into battle.
He decided to follow an environmental approach to business.
Many students follow the teachings of this influential philosopher.
Throughout her career, she has followed progressive educational policies.
All employees are expected to follow the new safety regulations.
Children should follow the instructions given by their teachers.
If you follow my advice, you will succeed.
