froth

noun
a mass of bubbles on a liquid
6.7
Pronunciation: / frɑθ / (AmE), / frɒθ / (BrE)
A mass of small bubbles that forms on or in a liquid, often produced by agitation or fermentation.

Examples

Milk froth
She skimmed the froth off the boiling jam to keep it clear.
The waves crashed onto the shore, leaving behind a white froth.
There was a layer of froth at the top of his cappuccino.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foam - a mass of small bubbles formed on or in liquid
