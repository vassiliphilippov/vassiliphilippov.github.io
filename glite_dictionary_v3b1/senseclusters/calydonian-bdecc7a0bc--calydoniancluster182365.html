Calydonian

noun
person from Calydon
Pronunciation: / ˌkælɪˈdoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈdəʊniən / (BrE)
a person from Calydon, an ancient Greek city, either historical or mythical

Examples

A brave Calydonian
The ancient poet described the bravery of a Calydonian in battle.
Legends often mention a famous Calydonian boar hunt involving many Greek heroes.
Artifacts from the region were believed to have belonged to a wealthy Calydonian family.
