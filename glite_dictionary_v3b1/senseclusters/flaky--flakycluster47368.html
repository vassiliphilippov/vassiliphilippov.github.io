flaky

adjective
informal: unreliable, inconsistent
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪki / (AmE), / ˈfleɪki / (BrE)
(informal) not reliable or consistent; tending to fail, behave unpredictably, or change without warning — often used of software tests, systems, or connections.

Examples

Flaky internet connection
The continuous integration suite reported a flaky test failing sporadically.
Engineers spent hours investigating a flaky integration test that passed then failed.
Her phone's connection was flaky, dropping calls and messages without warning all day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted.
  • synonym: unreliable - not able to be trusted or depended on.
