exergy

noun
maximum useful work
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksɚdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛksədʒi / (BrE)
the maximum amount of useful work that can be obtained from a system as it comes into equilibrium with its environment

Examples

Maximum exergy output
The exergy of the steam cycle determines how efficiently the power plant can operate.
By analyzing exergy losses, engineers can improve the design of energy systems.
Understanding exergy helps identify where energy is wasted in industrial processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: energy - the capacity to do work or produce change
  • antonym: entropy - a measure of disorder or randomness in a system
