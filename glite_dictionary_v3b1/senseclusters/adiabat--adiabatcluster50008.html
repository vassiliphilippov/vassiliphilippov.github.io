adiabat

noun
thermodynamic curve
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.di.ə.bæt/ /; / /ˈæd.i.ə.bæt/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.di.ə.bæt/ /; / /ˈæd.i.ə.bæt/ (BrE)
a line or curve on a thermodynamic diagram representing a process in which no heat is exchanged with the surroundings

Examples

The adiabat curve
The scientist plotted the adiabat to analyze the gas expansion in the chamber.
On the pressure-volume diagram, the adiabat shows the path of the process without heat transfer.
Understanding the adiabat is essential for studying thermodynamic cycles in physics.
