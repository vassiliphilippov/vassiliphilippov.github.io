dysteleological

adjective
without purpose or design
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪstɛliəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪstɛliəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
denying or lacking a purpose or final cause, especially in natural processes

Examples

A dysteleological view
The theory presents a dysteleological view of evolution, suggesting that changes occur without any specific goal.
Some philosophers argue that the universe is fundamentally dysteleological and lacks inherent purpose.
Her research supports a dysteleological interpretation of natural selection, focusing on random variation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: purposeless - having no purpose or aim
  • antonym: teleological - relating to or involving the explanation of phenomena by the purpose they serve
