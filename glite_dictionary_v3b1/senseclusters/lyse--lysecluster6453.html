lyse

verb
to break open cells
Pronunciation: / laɪs / (AmE), / laɪz / (BrE)
to break open or dissolve a cell by disrupting its membrane, usually to release its contents

Examples

To lyse cells
The researchers used a special solution to lyse the bacterial cells before extracting DNA.
If you lyse the red blood cells, you can analyze the proteins inside them.
Certain viruses can lyse host cells as part of their replication process.
The scientist added a chemical to lyse the cells and release their contents.
If the solution is too dilute, the red blood cells may lyse rapidly.
Some viruses cause host cells to lyse as part of their replication process.

The virus will lyse the host cell to release its genetic material.
Scientists observed that the bacteria began to lyse after exposure to the antibiotic.
