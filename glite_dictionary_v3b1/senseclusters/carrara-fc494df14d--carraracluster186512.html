Carrara

noun
Italian marble
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑrə / (AmE), / kəˈrɑːrə / (BrE)
a fine white or light-grey marble quarried near the city of Carrara in Italy, used for sculpture and building

Examples

Carrara marble statue
The statue was carved from pure Carrara to achieve a smooth, elegant finish.
Many famous Renaissance sculptures were made using Carrara because of its quality.
Architects often choose Carrara for luxury floors and countertops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marble - a hard, crystalline limestone used in sculpture and building
