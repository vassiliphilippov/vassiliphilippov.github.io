Ultraism

noun
Spanish/Latin American poetic movement after WWI
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌltrəˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈʌltrəˌɪzəm / (BrE)
A movement in Spanish and Spanish American poetry after World War I, characterized by the use of free verse, complex metrical innovations, and bold imagery and symbolism in place of traditional forms.

Examples

Ultraism poetry movement
Ultraism emphasized radical new imagery instead of established poetic norms.
Many poets embraced Ultraism to break away from traditional narrative style.
The influence of Ultraism can be seen in Borges's early poetry.
