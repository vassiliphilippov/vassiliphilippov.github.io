epithet

noun
a descriptive word or phrase
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpəˌθɛt / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪθɛt / (BrE)
a word or phrase used to characterize someone or something, often highlighting a notable quality or feature

Examples

Famous epithet
She earned the epithet 'the Iron Lady' because of her strong leadership style.
The author used the epithet 'swift-footed' to describe Achilles in the epic.
The poet was often referred to by the epithet 'The Bard of Avon'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nickname - an informal, often descriptive name given instead of or in addition to the real name
