(Physics) A property of quarks related to the strong force, determining their role in the strong interaction, having three types usually referred to as 'colors'.

Examples

Color charge
In particle physics, each quark carries a type of color charge, though it is unrelated to visual color.
Physicists use the term color to describe a property of quarks that comes in three varieties.
The theory of quantum chromodynamics explains how color charge mediates the strong nuclear force between quarks.
In particle physics, each quark possesses a colour charge that is essential for strong interactions.
Physicists use the term colour to distinguish the different states of quark charge.
The concept of colour explains why quarks are always confined within hadrons.
