stovepiping

noun
retrieval of information from unconnected databases
9.9 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈstoʊv.paɪ.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈstəʊv.paɪ.pɪŋ/ (BrE)
The practice of accessing information from separate, unconnected databases, making it necessary to switch systems; sometimes done for security reasons.

Examples

Stovepiping information access
Security experts sometimes encourage stovepiping to minimize the risk of data breaches.
The company's outdated IT system resulted in stovepiping that made data analysis slow and inefficient.
The main challenge was overcoming stovepiping so information could flow easily between departments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: siloing - the process of keeping departments or information isolated
