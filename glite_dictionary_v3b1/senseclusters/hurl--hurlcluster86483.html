hurl

verb
say something with force or anger
Pronunciation: / hɜrl / (AmE), / hɜːl / (BrE)
to say something, especially words or accusations, with great force or anger

Examples

To hurl insults
During the heated debate, he began to hurl accusations at his opponent.
In frustration, he hurled criticism at the team's performance after the match.
She was upset when her colleague hurled insults at her in front of everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: throw - to propel something forcefully through the air by a movement of the arm and hand
  • antonym: whisper - speak very quietly and softly
