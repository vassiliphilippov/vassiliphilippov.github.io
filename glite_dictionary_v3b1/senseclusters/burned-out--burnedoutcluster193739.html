burned-out

adjective
destroyed or damaged by fire

Pronunciation: / bÉËnd aÊŠt /; / bÉËnt aÊŠt / (AmE), / bÉœËnd aÊŠt /; / bÉœËnt aÊŠt / (BrE)
Destroyed or badly damaged by fire; reduced to ruins by burning.

Examples

Burned-out building
Only the burned-out shell of the house was left after the blaze.
She walked past several burned-out cars abandoned along the street.
The firefighters examined the burned-out remains of the warehouse.
A row of burnt-out cars lined the street after the riots.
Firefighters arrived too late to save the burnt-out warehouse.
The burnt-out building was condemned and fenced off for safety reasons.

Synonyms

  • burned
    1.0 0.00/m
    burned describes something damaged or destroyed by fire, while burned-out suggests a place is ruined and unfit for use.
  • burned-over
    6.2 0.07/m
    burned-over describes an area badly damaged by fire, while burned-out means destroyed and often left in ruins.
  • reduced to ashes
    6.4 0.15/m
    reduced to ashes means completely burned until only ash remains, while burned-out can mean badly damaged but not always destroyed.
  • up in flames
    6.9 0.66/m
    up in flames describes a sudden, complete loss in fire, while burned-out often describes the ruined remains still left afterwards.
  • engulfed in flames
    5.4 0.20/m
    engulfed in flames shows being completely surrounded by fire, while burned-out describes the later ruined condition after the fire.
  • scorched
    4.6 0.15/m
    scorched means lightly burned, dried, or damaged by heat, while burned-out means destroyed or reduced to ruins by fire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired in any way; complete
  • degree lower: scorched - damaged by burning slightly but not destroyed
