Synonyms

  • burned
    1.0 0.00/m
    burned describes something damaged or destroyed by fire, while burned-out suggests a place is ruined and unfit for use.
  • burned-over
    6.2 0.07/m
    burned-over describes an area badly damaged by fire, while burned-out means destroyed and often left in ruins.
  • reduced to ashes
    6.4 0.10/m
    reduced to ashes means completely burned until only ash remains, while burned-out can mean badly damaged but not always destroyed.
  • up in flames
    6.9 0.7/m
    up in flames describes a sudden, complete loss in fire, while burned-out often describes the ruined remains still left afterwards.
  • engulfed in flames
    5.4 0.3/m
    engulfed in flames shows being completely surrounded by fire, while burned-out describes the later ruined condition after the fire.
  • scorched
    4.6 0.2/m
    scorched means lightly burned, dried, or damaged by heat, while burned-out means destroyed or reduced to ruins by fire.
