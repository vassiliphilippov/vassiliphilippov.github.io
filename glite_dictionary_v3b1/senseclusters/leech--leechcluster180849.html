leech

verb
to draw blood using leeches (historical)
9.6 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / liÀêt É / (AmE), / liÀêt É / (BrE)
to draw blood from a person or animal by applying leeches, typically for medical treatment, especially in the past

Examples

To leech a patient

Historically, physicians believed that to leech someone could balance their bodily humors.
The healer decided to leech the wound, hoping it would reduce swelling and pain.
