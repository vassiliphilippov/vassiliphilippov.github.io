coinfection

noun
multiple infections together
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the simultaneous infection of a host by two or more pathogens at the same time

Examples

A coinfection with viruses
The patient was found to have a coinfection with both bacteria and viruses.
Researchers studied the effects of coinfection on the severity of the disease.
Treatment can be more complicated when a coinfection is present in the body.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: infection - the invasion of the body by harmful microorganisms
  • synonym: superinfection - an infection occurring on top of an existing one
