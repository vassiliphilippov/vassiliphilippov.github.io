heteronomy

noun
differentiated body segments
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˈrɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒnəmi / (BrE)
the condition in which repeated body parts or segments differ from each other in structure or function, especially in biology

Examples

Heteronomy of body parts
The scientist observed heteronomy in the crustacean's limbs, each adapted for a specific purpose.
Insects often display heteronomy with their legs and wings serving different functions.
The study focused on the evolutionary advantages of heteronomy in segmented animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homonomy - the condition where repeated body parts are similar in structure and function
