reactivation

noun
return to activity
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which something that was inactive or dormant becomes active again, especially a disease or infection

Examples

Reactivation of infection
The reactivation of the infection surprised the doctors after years without symptoms.
Scientists are studying what triggers the reactivation of dormant viruses in the body.
After a period of remission, the patient experienced a sudden reactivation of the disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relapse - the return of a disease or symptoms after improvement
  • antonym: remission - a period when symptoms of a disease are reduced or disappear
