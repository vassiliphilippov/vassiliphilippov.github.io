fluke

noun
barb on a harpoon or arrow
4.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /fluːk/ (AmE), /fluːk/ (BrE)
a barb or projection on a harpoon or arrow, designed to prevent it from being easily withdrawn

Examples

Arrow fluke
Ancient arrows sometimes had a single fluke to ensure they stayed lodged in the target.
The fluke of the harpoon made it especially effective for hunting large marine animals.
The fisherman examined the fluke on his harpoon before heading out to sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barb - sharp projection at the end of an arrow or fishhook that makes extraction difficult
