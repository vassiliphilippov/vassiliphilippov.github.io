tired

adjective
overused; lacking originality
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪɚd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪəd / (BrE)
(of words, ideas, or expressions) lacking freshness or originality; used so often as to become boring or uninteresting.

Examples

Tired ideas
Critics complained about the tired plot that offered nothing new to audiences.
She wanted to avoid using tired metaphors in her speech.
The author's writing is full of tired clichés and stock phrases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - (of ideas or expressions) new and original
  • synonym: hackneyed - (of a phrase or idea) overused and unoriginal
  • synonym: trite - lacking originality; boring due to overuse
