sisal

noun
a plant yielding stiff fiber for rope, twine, etc.
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈsaɪs(ə)l /; / ˈsɪzəl / (BrE)
a tropical plant with large leaves, or the strong fiber from its leaves used to make rope and twine

Examples

Sisal plant fiber
Farmers in Kenya have started cultivating sisal as a cash crop.
He purchased a sisal rug for its natural look and resilience.
The strong fiber from sisal is used to make durable ropes and twine.
She bought a roll of sisal twine for her gardening projects.
The company uses sisal to manufacture environmentally friendly rope products.
Traditional mats in the region are woven from sisal fibers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agave - a genus of plants that includes sisal
  • synonym: hemp - a plant fiber used for rope and textiles
