Disraeli

proper noun
British Prime Minister and statesman (1804–1881)
9.1
Pronunciation: /dɪz.ˈreɪ.liː/ (AmE), /dɪz.ˈreɪ.lɪ/ (BrE)
(1804–1881) The Earl of Beaconsfield; British statesman and novelist who served twice as Prime Minister, notable for purchasing the controlling interest in the Suez Canal and making Queen Victoria Empress of India.

Examples

Disraeli the Prime Minister
Disraeli played a key role in expanding the British Empire during the 19th century.
Queen Victoria relied on Disraeli for political advice and valued his loyalty.
The rivalry between Gladstone and Disraeli shaped Victorian-era British politics.
