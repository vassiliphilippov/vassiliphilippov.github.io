Joseph

proper noun
Old Testament patriarch, 11th son of Jacob
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒoʊzəf / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊzɪf / (BrE)
(In the Old Testament) the 11th son of Jacob, known for his multicolored coat and his brothers selling him into slavery in Egypt; regarded as one of the twelve patriarchs of Israel.

Examples

Joseph and the coat
Joseph was renowned for his ability to interpret dreams while in Egypt.
Many paintings depict Joseph wearing his coat of many colors.
The story of Joseph and his brothers is a famous biblical narrative.
