pretend

verb
put forward a claim or guess
Pronunciation: / prɪˈtɛnd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnd / (BrE)
to claim or assert something, such as a right, title, or opinion, often without certainty

Examples

To pretend a right
Few can pretend to offer a better solution than the experts.
I cannot pretend to know what the outcome will be.
She would not pretend to understand quantum physics, but she listened carefully.
He attempted to pretend a right to the old family estate.
Several nobles sought to pretend the crown after the king's death.
She does not pretend any connection to the royal bloodline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claim - to state or assert something as a fact
  • synonym: profess - to declare or announce formally
