polymyxin

noun
a class of toxic antibiotics from soil bacteria
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈmɪksɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɪksɪn / (BrE)
any of several toxic antibiotics obtained from soil bacteria, effective especially against Gram-negative bacteria

Examples

Polymyxin antibiotic
Because of its toxicity, polymyxin is usually reserved for serious infections when other antibiotics fail.
Researchers are developing new forms of polymyxin with reduced side effects.
The doctor prescribed polymyxin to treat the severe bacterial infection.
