dreary

adjective
dull and depressing
7.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdrɪ.riː/ /; / /ˈdriː.riː/ (AmE), /ˈdrɪə.riː/ (BrE)
dull, bleak, and depressing; lacking excitement or hope

Examples

A dreary day
After a dreary Monday at work, Jen decided to treat herself to dinner.
He stared out the window at the dreary landscape, longing for a hint of sunshine.
The constant rain made the whole week feel dreary and endless.
After months of dreary routine, he craved some excitement.
She found his lectures unbearably dreary and struggled to stay awake.
The weather was so dreary that no one wanted to leave the house.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement
  • antonym: exciting - causing great enthusiasm and eagerness
  • synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, especially in a way that makes one feel depressed
