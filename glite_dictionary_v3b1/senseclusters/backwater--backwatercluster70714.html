backwater

noun
part of a river or water held back from the main flow
6.2 6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌwɑtɚ / (AmE), / ˈbækwɔːtə / (BrE)
a part of a river or other body of water that is out of the main current, often created by flood, tide, or by being held or forced back, such as by a dam

Examples

River backwater
Frogs and insects thrive in the backwater formed by the rising tide.
The dam created a calm backwater behind its walls.
The flood left a large backwater on the edge of the field.
