filibuster

verb
to delay legislation using obstruction
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪˌbʌstə / (BrE)
to deliberately obstruct or delay the progress of legislation, especially by making long speeches or using other delaying tactics in a parliamentary procedure

Examples

To filibuster a bill
She threatened to filibuster if her concerns were not addressed in the new law.
The senator decided to filibuster the bill in order to prevent it from coming to a vote.
They filibustered for hours, delivering long speeches to delay the passing of the legislation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - to prevent movement or progress
  • antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier
