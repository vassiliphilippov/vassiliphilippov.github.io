monounsaturated fatty acid

noun
fatty acid with one double bond
4.5 3.0
Pronunciation: /moʊ.noʊ.ʌn.ˈsætʃ.ə.reɪ.tɪd ˈfæt.ɪ ˈæs.əd/ /; / /moʊ.noʊ.ʌn.ˈsætʃ.ə.reɪ.tɪd ˈfæt.ɪ ˈæs.ɪd/ (AmE), /məʊ.nəʊ.ʌn.ˈsætʃ.ə.reɪ.tɪd ˈfæt.ɪ ˈæs.ɪd/ (BrE)
A type of unsaturated fatty acid with only one double bond in its carbon chain, commonly found in olive oil, avocados, and certain nuts.

Examples

A monounsaturated fatty acid source
Avocados are a rich source of monounsaturated fatty acid, which is considered heart-healthy.
Nutritionists often recommend increasing your intake of monounsaturated fatty acid to improve cholesterol levels.
Olive oil contains a high proportion of monounsaturated fatty acid compared to other cooking oils.
