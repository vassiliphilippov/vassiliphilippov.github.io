warehouse

noun
a building for storing goods
1.4 14/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwÉ›rËŒhaÊŠs / (AmE), / ËˆweÉ™.haÊŠs / (BrE)
A large building where goods and merchandise are stored before distribution or sale.

Examples

A warehouse building
Fire safety regulations require all exits in the warehouse to remain clear at all times.
The company rented a warehouse to store its products before shipping them to retailers.
They visited the old warehouse that had been converted into modern office space.

Synonyms

  • depot
    3.3 3.67/m
    depot holds large quantities near transport sites for quick delivery, while warehouse is any large building for storing goods before sale.
  • godown
    9.9 0.02/m
    godown is a regional term used in South Asia for storing goods in buildings, while warehouse is the general international word.
  • storehouse
    3.1 1.24/m
    storehouse keeps goods safe for future use or local supply, while warehouse stores merchandise mainly before distribution or sale.
