solidary

adjective
joint legal responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːlɪˌdɛri / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪˌdɛəri / (BrE)
in law, involving joint responsibility or liability so that each party can be held responsible for the entire obligation

Examples

Solidary liability
The partners were solidary obligors, so each could be required to pay the full debt.
A solidary guarantee means any one guarantor can be held liable for the entire amount.
In some legal systems, solidary liability ensures creditors can recover debts from any responsible party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: joint - shared by two or more parties
  • antonym: several - separate or individual, not shared
