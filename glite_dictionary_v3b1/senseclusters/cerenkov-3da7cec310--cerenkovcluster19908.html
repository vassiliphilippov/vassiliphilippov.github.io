Cerenkov

noun
blue light from fast particles
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʃəˈrɛŋkɒf / (AmE), / tʃəˈrɛŋkɒf / (BrE)
a blue light produced when a charged particle moves faster than light in a particular medium, such as water

Examples

Cerenkov glow
The scientists observed a faint Cerenkov glow in the water surrounding the reactor core.
Special detectors use Cerenkov light to identify high-speed particles in physics experiments.
The blue color in the pool was caused by Cerenkov radiation from the nuclear reactor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cherenkov radiation - the blue glow produced by charged particles moving faster than light in a medium
