nigh

adjective, adverb
not far; near in space or time
Pronunciation: / naɪ / (AmE), / naɪ / (BrE)
(literary or archaic) almost or nearly; close in time, space, or degree

Examples

Nigh impossible
It was nigh impossible to see through the heavy fog that morning.
The end of the year draws nigh, and everyone is preparing for the celebrations.
With the enemy camp so nigh, the soldiers prepared for battle.
The hour of their departure was drawing nigh.
The town lies nigh the border with Scotland.
Winter is nigh, and the days grow shorter.
His strength was nigh gone after working all day in the field.
It was nigh midnight when the guests finally went home.
The marathon was nigh impossible for most participants due to the heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: almost - very nearly but not completely
  • antonym: far - at a great distance
  • synonym: near - close to; not far away
  • synonym: nearly - not completely; only a little less than
