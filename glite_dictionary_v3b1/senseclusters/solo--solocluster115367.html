solo

verb
perform alone (music)
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊloʊ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊləʊ / (BrE)
to perform a musical piece or part alone, typically intended for one performer

Examples

To solo on stage
During the concert, the guitarist soloed in the middle of the song.
He loves to solo at jazz clubs whenever he gets the chance.
She was nervous the first time she had to solo with the orchestra.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accompany - to play music along with another performer
