barbarize

verb
become uncivilized or crude
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːrbəˌraɪz /; / ˈbɑrbəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbəraɪz /; / ˈbɑːbərʌɪz / (BrE)
To make or become crude, savage, or uncivilized in behavior or language.

Examples

To barbarize a community
After years of conflict, the community began to barbarize as respect for laws and customs eroded.
If we abandon education and civil discourse, our conversations may slowly barbarize into shouting and insults.
Some critics argued that the film would barbarize popular taste with its crude humor and violence.
Left without guidance, the children began to barbarise until they could hardly recognize their own manners.
Some argue that violent media can barbarise society over time.
When civility breaks down, even the most cultured communities may barbarise.
Continuous warfare can barbarise even the most stable societies.
Some fear that violent media may barbarise young audiences over time.
The isolation tended to barbarise the small, neglected community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brutalize - to make brutal or less humane
  • antonym: civilise - to bring out of a barbaric or savage state; to refine
  • antonym: civilize - to bring out of a savage or uncultured state
