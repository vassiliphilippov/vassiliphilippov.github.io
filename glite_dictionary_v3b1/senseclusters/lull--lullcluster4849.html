lull

verb
make calm or quiet
3.8
Pronunciation: / lʌl / (AmE), / lʌl / (BrE)
to make someone or something calm, quiet, or less alert, sometimes by giving a false sense of security

Examples

To lull a child
Her calm voice managed to lull his fears before the interview.
The gentle music helped lull the baby to sleep.
They tried to lull the audience into a false sense of security.
Don't let yourself be lulled into a false sense of security by the apparent calm.
The steady voice of the leader lulled the crowd into believing everything was under control.
They were lulled into complacency by the repeated assurances of safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agitate - to make someone nervous or upset
  • synonym: soothe - to gently calm someone
