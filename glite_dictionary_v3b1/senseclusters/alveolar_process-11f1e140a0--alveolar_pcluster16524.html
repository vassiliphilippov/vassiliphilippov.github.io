alveolar process

noun
ridge in jaws holding teeth sockets
4.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /æl.ˈviː.ə.lər ˈprɑː.ses/ /; / /æl.ˈvɪə.lər ˈprɑː.səs/ (AmE), /æl.ˈviː.ə.lə ˈprɒ.ses/ /; / /æl.ˈvɪə.lə ˈprɒ.ses/ (BrE)
the ridge of bone that forms the borders of the upper and lower jaws and contains the sockets of the teeth

Examples

The alveolar process ridge
In a dental X-ray, the alveolar process can be clearly seen supporting the roots of the teeth.
Periodontal disease can lead to the loss of bone in the alveolar process.
The upper alveolar process is crucial for holding the front teeth in place.
