stick

verb
remain loyal or continue supporting
1.5 2.9
Pronunciation: /stɪk/ (AmE), /stɪk/ (BrE)
to remain, persist, or stay with someone, something, or in a place (including loyalty or something continuing over time)

Examples

To stick with friends
Even when things got difficult, she continued to stick to her own moral code.
He has always stuck with his childhood friends through thick and thin.
They advised him not to stick with that political party, but he refused to change.
He decided to stick at his current job rather than move abroad.
I wish you would stick with me until the end of the project.
Why don't you stick around for the meeting and see what happens?
Despite his apology, the nickname stuck and followed him throughout school.
Her reputation as a troublemaker stuck with her even after she moved to another city.
Once a rumor starts, it can sometimes stick for years, regardless of the truth.
