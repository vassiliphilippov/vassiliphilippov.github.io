Mb

noun
unit of information (megabit, 1,000,000 bits)
3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈme.gə.bɪt/ /; / /em.ˈbiː/ (AmE), /ˈme.gə.bɪt/ /; / /em.ˈbiː/ (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000,000 (one million) bits, commonly used to measure data size or transfer rates

Examples

Mb data size
Be careful not to confuse Mb (megabits) with MB (megabytes) when testing download speeds.
Our internet speed is advertised as 100 Mb per second.
The file size is 20 Mb, which means it will take a while to download.
