reinfect

verb
to infect again
4.1
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪn.ˈfekt/ /; / /rɪ.ɪn.ˈfekt/ /; / /rə.ɪn.ˈfekt/ (AmE), /riː.ɪn.ˈfekt/ /; / /rɪ.ɪn.ˈfekt/ /; / /rə.ɪn.ˈfekt/ (BrE)
to infect someone or something again after they have recovered from a previous infection

Examples

To reinfect a person
If you do not take precautions, you might reinfect yourself with the same bacteria.
Doctors warned that the patient could reinfect others if proper hygiene was not maintained.
After recovering from the flu, he was careful not to reinfect his family.
After recovering from the flu, he was reinfected by a different strain of the virus.
Doctors are studying how likely it is for patients to become reinfected after treatment.
If you do not finish your antibiotics, you might reinfect yourself with the same bacteria.
If you do not finish your antibiotics, the bacteria may reinfect you.
Doctors are concerned that the virus could reinfect people who have already recovered.
Poor sanitation in the area allowed the parasites to reinfect the community.
