ichnography

noun
study of trace fossils
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈnɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɪkˈnɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a record, map, or study of tracks, footprints, or other trace fossils used to interpret past animal activity

Examples

Dinosaur ichnography
The paleontologist analyzed the ichnography of the site to trace dinosaur movements.
Detailed ichnography revealed the migration patterns of prehistoric animals in the region.
Researchers used ichnography to reconstruct the behavior of extinct species from their fossilized tracks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ichnology - the scientific study of trace fossils
  • synonym: paleontology - the scientific study of fossils and ancient life
