reciprocate

verb
move or cause to move back and forth
6.7 5.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈsɪ.prə.keɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈsɪ.prə.keɪt/ (BrE)
(of a part or device) to move alternately back and forth in a straight line; to cause such motion.

Examples

Reciprocate the blade
A crankshaft is used to reciprocate the saw blade.
The mechanism allows the lever to reciprocate smoothly within its housing.
The piston in the engine will reciprocate hundreds of times a minute.
