cut off your nose to spite your face

verb
harm yourself out of spite
Pronunciation: / kʌt ɔf jɔr noʊz tu spaɪt jɔr feɪs / (AmE), / kʌt ɒf jɔː nəʊz tuː spaɪt jɔː feɪs / (BrE)
to deliberately do something out of anger or spite that ends up causing more harm to yourself than to the person you want to punish

Examples

To cut off your nose to spite your face
If you quit your job just to make your boss angry, you might be cutting off your nose to spite your face.
She refused to apologize even though it meant missing the event, but I think she was just cutting off her nose to spite her face.
Sometimes, holding a grudge can make you cut off your nose to spite your face.
