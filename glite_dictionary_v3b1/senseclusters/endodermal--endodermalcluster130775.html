endodermal

adjective
of the endoderm
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈdɜrməl / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈdɜːməl / (BrE)
relating to or derived from the innermost cell layer of an embryo, known as the endoderm

Examples

Endodermal cell layer
The endodermal layer gives rise to the lining of the digestive tract.
Researchers studied the development of endodermal tissues in early embryos.
Certain organs, such as the liver, originate from endodermal cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ectodermal - relating to the outermost cell layer of an embryo
  • antonym: mesodermal - relating to the middle cell layer of an embryo
