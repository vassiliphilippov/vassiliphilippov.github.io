mis-

x
prefix for failure or wrong
Pronunciation: / mɪs / (AmE), / mɪs / (BrE)
a prefix added to words to show that something is done wrongly, badly, or incorrectly

Examples

To misinterpret instructions
If you misinterpret the instructions, you might assemble the furniture incorrectly.
The old clock would often miscount the hours, chiming at the wrong time.
A single misstep on the icy path caused her to fall.
If you mis-understand the instructions, you might make a mistake.
He tends to mis-judge people's intentions when meeting them for the first time.
It is easy to mis-place your keys when you are in a hurry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dis- - a prefix meaning not or opposite of
  • synonym: mal- - a prefix meaning bad or wrong
