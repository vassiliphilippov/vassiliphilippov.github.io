ill-formed

adjective
not grammatical; incorrectly constructed
Pronunciation: / ɪl ˈfɔrmd / (AmE), / ɪl ˈfɔːmd / (BrE)
(of a phrase, sentence, etc.) not conforming to the rules of grammar or accepted usage; not correctly constructed.

Examples

An ill-formed sentence
Correcting ill-formed expressions helps learners master the rules of grammar.
Linguists often analyze why certain constructions are ill-formed while others are acceptable.
The sentence 'She go to store' is ill-formed in standard English.

Related Terms

  • antonym: well-formed - conforming to grammatical or accepted usage; correctly constructed
