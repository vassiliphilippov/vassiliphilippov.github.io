fairyland

noun
imaginary magical or beautiful place
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛriˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈfeəriˌlænd / (BrE)
an imaginary or magical place of great beauty and charm, often thought to be inhabited by fairies

Examples

A magical fairyland
After the snowfall, the entire landscape looked like a fairyland shimmering under the moonlight.
Children sometimes escape into a fairyland of their own imagination, far from the troubles of reality.
The story transports readers to a magical fairyland filled with mysterious creatures and enchanted forests.
In stories, children often stumble into fairyland through a magical portal.
The forest looked so beautiful in the morning mist that it seemed like fairyland.
The garden was transformed into a glowing fairyland with thousands of twinkling lights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fantasy - imaginative creation, especially one removed from reality
  • antonym: reality - the true, actual world as opposed to fantasy
  • synonym: wonderland - an imaginary or magical place of marvelous things
