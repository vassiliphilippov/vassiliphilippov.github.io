constrict

verb
to become tight or narrower
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to become or make something tighter or narrower by pressure or shrinking

Examples

To constrict tightly
As the temperature dropped, the metal pipes began to constrict.
Her throat constricted with fear when she heard the strange noise.
The snake's body slowly constricted around its prey.
A tight collar can constrict your breathing if worn for too long.
Blood vessels constrict in response to cold temperatures to conserve heat.
The snake began to constrict its prey, making escape impossible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contract - to decrease in size, number, or range
  • antonym: expand - to become or make larger or more extensive
  • antonym: loosen - to make or become less tight
  • synonym: tighten - to make or become tight or tighter
