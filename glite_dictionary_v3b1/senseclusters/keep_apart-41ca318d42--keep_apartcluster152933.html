keep apart

verb
prevent from mixing; separate
3.2 1.13/m
Pronunciation: / kip …ôÀàp…ërt / (AmE), / kiÀêp …ôÀàp…ëÀêt / (BrE)
to prevent two or more people or things from coming together or mixing; to separate or isolate.

Examples

To keep apart groups
Please keep apart the clean and dirty laundry.
The teacher tried to keep apart the two students who were arguing.
They were advised to keep apart during the quarantine period.

Synonyms

  • separate from
    2.7 16/m
    separate from means to keep two things distinct and not mixed, while keep apart emphasizes preventing them coming together.
  • isolate
    4.2 16/m
    isolate means to make someone or something alone and separated from others, while keep apart focuses on stopping things from mixing.
  • seclude
    6.7 0.15/m
    seclude means to keep someone away from other people for privacy or safety, while keep apart means to prevent mixing or contact.
  • keep away
    1.6 6.21/m
    keep away means to stop someone or something from coming close, while keep apart means to prevent things from mixing or joining.
