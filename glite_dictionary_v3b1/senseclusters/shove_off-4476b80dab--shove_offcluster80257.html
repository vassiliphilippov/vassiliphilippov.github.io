shove off

verb
leave abruptly or rudely (informal)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ʃʌv ɑf / (AmE), / ʃʌv ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) To leave a place, especially abruptly or in a dismissive manner.

Examples

To shove off quickly
He rudely told us to shove off when we tried to help.
If you don’t like it here, you can just shove off.
The kids finished their lunch and quickly shoved off to the park.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrive - to reach a place; the opposite of leaving
  • synonym: go away - to leave a place; depart
