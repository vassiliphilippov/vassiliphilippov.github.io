slide valve

noun
mechanical valve operating by sliding motion
Pronunciation: / slaɪd vælv / (AmE), / slaɪd vɑːlv / (BrE)
a valve that regulates the flow of fluid by sliding over an opening to open or close a passageway, commonly used in engines and machinery

Examples

A slide valve in engine
A malfunctioning slide valve can greatly reduce the efficiency of the machinery.
Engineers replaced the worn-out slide valve with a new, more durable component.
The old steam engine was fitted with a slide valve to control the admission of steam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poppet valve - a valve that opens or closes by lifting from its seat, used in internal combustion engines
