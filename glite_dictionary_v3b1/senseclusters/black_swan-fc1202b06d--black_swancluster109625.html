black swan

noun
unpredictable, high-impact event
Pronunciation: / blæk swɑn / (AmE), / blæk swɒn / (BrE)
an unpredictable or unforeseen event, typically one with extreme consequences

Examples

Black swan event
A pandemic was the kind of black swan event many governments failed to prepare for.
His sudden success was a black swan that none of his competitors had anticipated.
The financial crisis of 2008 is often cited as a black swan that caught economists and policymakers by surprise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal event - an expected or routine occurrence
  • synonym: outlier - something outside the norm; rare or exceptional case
