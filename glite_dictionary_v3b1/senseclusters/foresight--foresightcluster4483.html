foresight

noun
ability to foresee events
3.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔːr.saɪt/ (AmE), /ˈfɔː.saɪt/ (BrE)
the ability to predict or prepare for what will happen in the future

Examples

Good foresight
Her foresight helped the company avoid a costly mistake.
Investing for retirement requires a great deal of foresight.
Thanks to his foresight, we packed extra food for the trip.
Effective leaders are often praised for their foresight in anticipating market changes.
Her foresight saved the company from financial problems during the economic downturn.
Without foresight, people may repeat the same mistakes in the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hindsight - understanding of a situation or event only after it has happened
  • synonym: prescience - the ability to know what will happen in the future
  • synonym: prudence - careful good judgment that allows someone to avoid danger or risks
