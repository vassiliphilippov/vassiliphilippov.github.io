soaprock

noun
soft, soapy-feeling variety of talc
8.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊpˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpˌrɒk / (BrE)
A soft, dense variety of talc with a soapy feel, used for making hearths, tabletops, and decorative objects.

Examples

A soaprock hearth
Soaprock is prized for both its softness and its ability to take a fine polish.
Old fireplaces were often made with soaprock for its heat resistance.
The artisan carved the statue from a single block of soaprock.
