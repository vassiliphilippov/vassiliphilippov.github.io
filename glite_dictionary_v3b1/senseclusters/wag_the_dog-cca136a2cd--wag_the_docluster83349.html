wag the dog

verb
divert attention from an important issue
9.1
Pronunciation: / wæɡ ðə dɒɡ / (AmE), / wæɡ ðə dɒɡ / (BrE)
to intentionally divert attention from an important matter to something less significant, especially in politics or the media

Examples

To wag the dog politically
Critics accused the administration of trying to wag the dog with a sudden foreign policy distraction.
Some believe the scandal was created simply to wag the dog and deflect from economic issues.
Whenever bad news breaks, politicians are quick to wag the dog with a new controversy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distract - to prevent someone from giving full attention to something
