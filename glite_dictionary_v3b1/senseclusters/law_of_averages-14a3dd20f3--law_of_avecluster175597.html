law of averages

noun
principle that outcomes average out over time
7.0
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ˈævɚɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ˈævərɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
the idea that, over many tries, results will tend to match what is expected on average

Examples

The law of averages
He believed the law of averages guaranteed that his team would win eventually.
Investors often rely on the law of averages when making long-term financial decisions.
The law of averages suggests that eventually, your luck will even out.
Many gamblers wrongly believe in the law of averages to guarantee their next win.
She hoped the law of averages would work in her favor after so many failed attempts.
The coach reminded the team that the law of averages does not ensure victory in every game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance
  • synonym: probability - the likelihood or chance that something will happen
