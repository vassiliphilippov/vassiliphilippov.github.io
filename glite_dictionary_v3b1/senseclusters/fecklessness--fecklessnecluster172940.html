fecklessness

noun
state of being ineffective or weak-willed
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛkləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɛkləsnɪs / (BrE)
lack of strength, determination, or effectiveness; the state of being feeble and ineffectual.

Examples

Her fecklessness showed
Her fecklessness became evident when she missed every deadline for the project.
Many criticized the government's fecklessness in dealing with the crisis.
The board was frustrated by the CEO's fecklessness in addressing critical business challenges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: effectiveness - ability to produce a desired result
  • synonym: ineffectiveness - lack of ability to produce a desired result
  • synonym: ineptitude - lack of skill or ability
