drabble

verb
to write short fiction
Pronunciation: / ˈdræbəl / (AmE), / ˈdræbəl / (BrE)
to write a very short piece of fiction, typically around one hundred words in length.

Examples

To drabble a story
She likes to drabble every morning as a creative exercise before starting her novel.
Writers often drabble to challenge themselves to tell a story in just one hundred words.
He decided to drabble about his day, capturing the main events in a tiny story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose - to create or write a piece of writing or music
  • antonym: expand - to make something longer or more detailed
