Rh-negative blood type

noun
blood group lacking Rh factor
3.9 4.8
Pronunciation: /ɑːr eɪtʃ ˈnɛɡ.ə.tɪv blʌd taɪp/ (AmE), /ɑː eɪtʃ ˈnɛɡ.ə.tɪv blʌd taɪp/ (BrE)
A blood group whose red blood cells lack the Rh factor (Rh antigen).

Examples

Rh-negative blood type group
All blood donors are tested for their Rh-negative blood type status.
He discovered he has an Rh-negative blood type after a routine blood test.
Pregnant women with an Rh-negative blood type often require special care to prevent complications.
