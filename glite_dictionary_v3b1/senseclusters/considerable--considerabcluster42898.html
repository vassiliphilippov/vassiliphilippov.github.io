considerable

adjective
large in amount or degree
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɪd.ɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / kənˈsɪd.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
large in size, amount, or degree; significant or substantial

Examples

A considerable amount
She invested a considerable amount of time in the project.
The new law had a considerable impact on public health.
Their offer was rejected despite being of considerable value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minor - small in importance, size, or degree
  • synonym: significant - important or large enough to be noticed
  • synonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth
