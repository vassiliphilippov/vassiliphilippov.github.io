SOS

noun
international distress signal (Morse code)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs oʊ ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛs əʊ ˈɛs / (BrE)
an internationally recognized signal used to indicate distress, especially in radio communication, originally represented in Morse code as three dots, three dashes, and three dots.

Examples

An SOS signal
During emergencies, SOS signals are still recognized worldwide.
He tapped out an SOS on the radio in hopes of alerting rescuers.
The ship sent out an SOS when it began to sink in the storm.
