casuist

noun
person using subtle, often specious reasoning
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkæʒuˌɪst /; / ˈkæʒuɪst / (AmE), / ˈkazjʊɪst /; / ˈkæzjuɪst / (BrE)
a person who uses detailed or subtle reasoning to solve moral or ethical problems, sometimes in a tricky or overly clever way

Examples

A casuist in ethics
History remembers him as a casuist skilled in the art of moral justification.
She accused the politician of being a casuist who twists arguments to suit his goals.
The seasoned casuist found a loophole in every regulation put before him.
The philosopher was known as a skilled casuist in ethical debates.
As a casuist, she carefully analyzed each dilemma before offering advice.
Some critics argue that a casuist can justify almost any action by clever reasoning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethicist - a person who studies or writes about moral principles
  • synonym: moralist - a person who teaches or promotes morality
  • synonym: sophist - a person who uses clever but fallacious reasoning
