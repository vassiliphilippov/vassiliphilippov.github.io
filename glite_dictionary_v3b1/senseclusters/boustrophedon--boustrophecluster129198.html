boustrophedon

noun
ancient writing with alternating direction
Pronunciation: / buːˌstrɑːfəˈdɑːn /; / buːˌstrɑːfrəˈdɑːn / (AmE), / buːˌstrɒfɪˈdiːɒn /; / buːˌstrɒfrɪˈdɒn / (BrE)
an ancient method of writing in which each line alternates direction, going left to right and then right to left

Examples

Ancient boustrophedon writing
Some early Etruscan manuscripts use a boustrophedon format rather than writing every line left to right.
The ancient Greek tablets were inscribed in boustrophedon, making them challenging to read.
The term boustrophedon comes from the Greek words for 'ox' and 'to turn.'
Some early Greek texts were recorded using the boustrophedon method.
The archaeologists discovered an inscription written in boustrophedon.
Understanding boustrophedon scripts requires adjusting to the alternating line directions.
