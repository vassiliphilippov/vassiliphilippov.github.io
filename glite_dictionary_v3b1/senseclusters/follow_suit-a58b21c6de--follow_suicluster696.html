follow suit

verb
do the same as someone else
3.8 2.89/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ëlo ä Ààsut / (AmE), / Ààf…íl…ô ä ÀàsuÀêt / (BrE)
to do the same thing as someone else, especially after they have done it first

Examples

To follow suit quickly
After the team introduced flexible work hours, other departments decided to follow suit.
If the manager wears casual clothes on Friday, employees usually follow suit.
When one student began recycling, many of her classmates chose to follow suit.
If you leave early, others might follow suit and do the same.
The company cut prices, and its competitors followed suit within weeks.
When one department adopted the new software, others quickly followed suit.

Synonyms

  • copy
    1.0 7.67/m
    copy is doing something the same way as someone else, while follow suit means doing the same thing after someone else does it.
  • take a leaf out of someone's book
    4.1 0.01/m
    take a leaf out of someone's book means copying a successful person's methods, while follow suit means doing the same action afterward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: break the mold - to do something differently from what others are doing, not to conform
  • synonym: copycat - a person who copies another's behavior or actions
  • synonym: imitate - copy or mimic someone's actions or behavior
