Monte Bianco

proper noun
the highest mountain in the Alps
Pronunciation: / ˌmɒnteɪ biˈɑŋkoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmɒnteɪ biˈæŋkəʊ / (BrE)
the highest mountain peak in the Alps, located on the border between France and Italy, south of Geneva, rising to 15,781 feet (4,810 meters)

Examples

Climb Monte Bianco
Many climbers attempt to reach the summit of Monte Bianco every year.
Scientists have measured how glaciers on Monte Bianco are changing due to global warming.
The view of Monte Bianco from Chamonix is breathtaking, especially at sunrise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mont Blanc - the French name for the highest mountain in the Alps, identical to Monte Bianco
