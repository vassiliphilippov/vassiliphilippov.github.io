name and shame

verb
publicly identify and criticize wrongdoers
Pronunciation: / neɪm ənd ʃeɪm / (AmE), / neɪm ənd ʃeɪm / (BrE)
to publicly identify individuals or organizations that have done something wrong, in order to expose and criticize them

Examples

To name and shame polluters
Environmental groups often name and shame corporations responsible for major pollution incidents.
Many newspapers name and shame tax evaders to discourage others from breaking the law.
The council decided to name and shame restaurants that failed health inspections.
