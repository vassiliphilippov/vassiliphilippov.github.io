demi-bastion

noun
fortification with one face and one flank
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛmiˈbæstʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛmiˈbɑːstɪən / (BrE)
A fortification structure with one face and one flank, used in military architecture.

Examples

A demi-bastion structure
Engineers debated the advantages of a full bastion over a demi-bastion in the new fort design.
Historical records describe the construction of a demi-bastion to cover the fort's vulnerable approach.
The old fortress included a demi-bastion at its northern corner for added defense.
