innocence

noun
quality of being naïve or inexperienced
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnəsəns / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsəns / (BrE)
lack of experience or knowledge of the more complex or unpleasant aspects of life; the quality of being simple and naïve.

Examples

Childlike innocence
Her innocence was clear from the way she believed everyone had good intentions.
The film captures the innocence of childhood before reality sets in.
There was a charming innocence about his questions that made everyone smile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • antonym: worldliness - having experience and knowledge of the world
