bad actor

noun
dishonest, disruptive, or poor performer
Pronunciation: / bæd ˈæktɚ / (AmE), / bad ˈaktə / (BrE)
a person who behaves dishonestly, disruptively, or performs their role poorly

Examples

A bad actor in business
A single bad actor can damage the reputation of an entire group.
Authorities are working hard to prevent any bad actors from exploiting the new system.
The company took swift action to remove the bad actor from their team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: troublemaker - a person who intentionally causes problems
  • synonym: villain - a person who is deliberately harmful or wicked
