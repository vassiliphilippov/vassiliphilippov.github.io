resow

verb
to plant seeds again
5.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàso ä / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàs…ô ä / (BrE)
to plant seeds again in the same area, especially after the first attempt failed or the seeds were removed

Examples

To resow seeds
After the birds ate most of the seeds, the gardener decided to resow the vegetable patch.
The farmer had to resow the field because the first crop did not sprout.
Heavy rains forced them to resow the lawn to ensure even grass coverage.
After heavy rain washed away the seeds, the farmers had to resow the entire field.
Gardeners often resow grass in bare patches to keep the lawn looking healthy.
If the first crop fails, it is common practice to resow before the season ends.
