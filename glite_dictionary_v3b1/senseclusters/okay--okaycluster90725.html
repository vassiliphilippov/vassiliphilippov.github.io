okay

verb
give permission or sanction
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊˈkeɪ / (BrE)
to give formal approval or permission to something; to sanction

Examples

To okay a plan
Before you start the project, make sure the client has okayed all the requirements.
My boss okayed my request to work from home next week.
The committee finally okayed the proposed changes after much debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approve - to agree with or accept as satisfactory
  • antonym: disapprove - to not approve; to reject or refuse to sanction
