motte

noun
castle earth mound
Pronunciation: / mɑt / (AmE), / mɒt / (BrE)
a mound of earth, often artificial, serving as the site for a castle or fortification

Examples

Castle motte
The ancient castle was built on a high motte to provide a better view of the surrounding land.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a wooden tower atop the old motte.
Many medieval fortifications featured a motte and a surrounding bailey for added defense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bailey - the outer courtyard of a castle, often enclosed by a wall
