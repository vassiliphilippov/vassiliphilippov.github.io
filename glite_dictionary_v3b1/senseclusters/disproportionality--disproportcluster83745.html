disproportionality

noun
lack of proportion
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsprəˌpɔrʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌdɪsprəˌpɔːʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the state of being out of proportion or not balanced in size, amount, degree, or fairness

Examples

The disproportionality of punishment
The judge criticized the disproportionality of the punishment given for such a minor offense.
Activists argued that there was clear disproportionality in how the law was enforced across different communities.
The report highlighted the disproportionality between the resources allocated and the actual needs of the project.
The disproportionality in funding between schools in different neighborhoods is a serious concern.
Activists highlighted the disproportionality of punishments given to students from different backgrounds.
There is growing awareness of disproportionality in healthcare access across various regions.
The report highlighted the disproportionality in access to healthcare among different communities.
Activists are concerned about the disproportionality of disciplinary actions affecting minority students.
Efforts to reduce disproportionality in hiring practices have become a priority for many organizations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disparity - a great difference or inequality
  • synonym: disproportion - a lack of proper relation in size or amount between things
  • synonym: imbalance - a lack of balance or equality
  • antonym: proportionality - the quality of corresponding in size or amount to something else
