iodine-131

noun
radioactive isotope of iodine used medically
8.4 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪəˌdin wʌn θɜrˈti wʌn / (AmE), / ˈaɪədiːn wʌn θɜːˈti wʌn / (BrE)
a radioactive isotope of iodine, with a half-life of about 8 days, used in medical diagnosis and treatment, especially of thyroid disorders

Examples

iodine-131 therapy
Doctors may use iodine-131 therapy to treat certain types of thyroid cancer.
Exposure to iodine-131 can occur during some nuclear accidents.
The thyroid scan involved administering iodine-131 to trace the gland's activity.
