overbake

verb
to bake too long
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbeɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbeɪk / (BrE)
to bake something for too long, making it too dry, hard, or burnt

Examples

To overbake cookies
If you overbake the cookies, they will turn out hard and dry.
She accidentally overbaked the bread, so the crust was too tough to eat.
Be careful not to overbake the pie or the filling will dry out.
If you overbake the cookies, they will turn out hard and crunchy.
She accidentally overbaked the bread, making the crust too tough to eat.
Be careful not to overbake the pie or the filling will dry out.
If you overbake the cake, it will turn out dry and crumbly.
She accidentally overbaked the bread, so the crust was much too hard.
Be careful not to overbake the lasagna or the cheese will become tough.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn - to cook food so much that it becomes blackened or inedible
  • antonym: undercook - to cook food for too little time so it is not fully done
