vexatious litigation

noun
malicious and unfounded legal action
Pronunciation: / vɛkˈseɪʃəs ˌlɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / vɛkˈseɪʃəs ˌlɪtɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
Legal action initiated maliciously and without sufficient grounds, often to harass or subdue an opponent.

Examples

Vexatious litigation cases
Laws exist to protect defendants from vexatious litigation brought by disgruntled parties.
The court issued a restraining order to prevent further vexatious litigation against the organization.
Their repeated attempts at vexatious litigation ultimately led to sanctions by the judge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frivolous lawsuit - a lawsuit with no sound legal basis, mainly intended to harass
  • antonym: good faith litigation - a lawsuit brought with honest intent and reasonable grounds
