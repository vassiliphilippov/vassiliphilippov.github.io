nut tool

noun
climber's tool for removing nuts or chocks
9.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / n åt tuÀêl / (AmE), / n åt tuÀêl / (BrE)
A specialized tool used by climbers to remove a nut or chock from a crack in the rock after it has been placed for protection.

Examples

A nut tool for climbing
Every climber should carry a nut tool to retrieve stuck gear after a difficult lead.
He used his nut tool to carefully extract the chock from the narrow crack.
Without a nut tool, retrieving passive protection can be almost impossible on multipitch climbs.

Synonyms

  • nut key
    8.9 0.00/m
    nut key is the climber's name for the small tool used to pull out a nut, while nut tool is a more descriptive, general term.
