porphyrin

noun
group of organic pigments in living tissues
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔːrfɚɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɔːfərɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of organic pigments, widely distributed in living tissues, that contain four pyrrole rings and are critical for such biological functions as the transport of oxygen and photosynthesis

Examples

A porphyrin pigment
Heme, an essential component of hemoglobin, is a type of porphyrin.
Scientists study porphyrin structures to better understand cellular respiration.
Some disorders are caused by an accumulation of porphyrin compounds in the body.
