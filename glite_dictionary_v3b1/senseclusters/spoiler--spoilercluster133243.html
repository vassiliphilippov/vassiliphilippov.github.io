spoiler

noun
candidate who affects election outcome
8.4 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈspɔɪ.lər/ (AmE), /ˈspɔɪ.lə/ (BrE)
a candidate in an election who is unlikely to win but may draw enough votes to affect the chances of the leading candidates

Examples

Election spoiler
Some analysts believe that the independent candidate played the role of a spoiler in the tight gubernatorial race.
Strategists feared the third-party hopeful would act as a spoiler, splitting the vote.
The presence of a spoiler on the ballot can change the outcome between the top contenders.
