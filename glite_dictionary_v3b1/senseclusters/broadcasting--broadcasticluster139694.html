broadcasting

noun
transmitting programs by radio, television, or telecommunications
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔːdˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the distribution of audio or video content to a large audience via television, radio, or other telecommunications media

Examples

Live broadcasting of events
Modern broadcasting allows millions of people to watch live events from their homes.
She pursued a career in broadcasting after graduating from journalism school.
The rapid evolution of broadcasting has transformed how society receives news and entertainment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: narrowcasting - the transmission of information to a specific, limited audience
  • synonym: radio - the transmission of audio programs via electromagnetic waves
  • synonym: television - the transmission of moving images and sound to viewers
