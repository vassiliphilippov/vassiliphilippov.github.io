vesical vein

noun
vein draining blood from the bladder
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛsɪkəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈvɛsɪkəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the vesical plexus of the bladder to the internal iliac veins

Examples

Vesical vein drains bladder
Damage to the vesical vein can lead to complications with urinary function.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully ligated the vesical vein to prevent bleeding.
The vesical vein plays a critical role in draining blood from the bladder.
