solitudinarian

noun
person who lives in solitude
Pronunciation: / sɑːlɪˌtuːdəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / sɒlɪˌtjuːdəˈneəriən / (BrE)
a person who chooses to live in solitude, away from society

Examples

A solitudinarian lifestyle
After retiring, he became something of a solitudinarian, rarely seen outside his cottage.
Despite being a solitudinarian, she maintained a few close friendships through letters.
The writer explained that her solitudinarian lifestyle helped her focus on her work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hermit - a person who lives alone, often for religious reasons
  • synonym: recluse - a person who lives a solitary life and tends to avoid other people
  • antonym: socialite - a person who is well known in fashionable society and frequently attends social events
