ineloquence

noun
lack of eloquence
8.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈel.ə.kwəns/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈel.ə.kwəns/ (BrE)
the quality of lacking fluency, elegance, or persuasiveness in speech or writing

Examples

His ineloquence in speech
Despite his passion, his ineloquence made it difficult for the audience to follow his argument.
The author's ineloquence was evident in the awkward phrasing throughout the essay.
Her ineloquence in public speaking did not reflect her deep understanding of the subject.
Despite his ineloquence, his message was sincere and heartfelt.
Her ineloquence made it difficult for the audience to follow her argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: awkwardness - the quality of being clumsy or lacking grace, especially in speech
  • antonym: eloquence - fluent, persuasive, or elegant expression
  • synonym: inelegance - lack of grace or refinement in expression
