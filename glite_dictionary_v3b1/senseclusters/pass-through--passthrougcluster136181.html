pass-through

noun
an opening for passing items between rooms
4.5 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsˌθru / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsˌθruː / (BrE)
an opening, often with a shelf, between two rooms—especially a kitchen and dining room—used for passing dishes or food.

Examples

Kitchen pass-through
She handed the plates through the pass-through from the kitchen to the dining room.
The new house design includes a convenient pass-through between the bar and the lounge area.
They placed fresh pastries on the pass-through so guests could help themselves during the party.
