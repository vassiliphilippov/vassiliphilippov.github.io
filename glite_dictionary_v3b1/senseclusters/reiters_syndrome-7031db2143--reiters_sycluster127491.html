Reiter's syndrome

noun
inflammatory syndrome causing arthritis, conjunctivitis, urethritis
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.tərz ˈsɪn.droʊm/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.təz ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ (BrE)
a medical condition, chiefly affecting men, characterized by inflammation of joints (arthritis), the eyes (conjunctivitis), and the urinary tract (urethritis), with cause often unknown

Examples

Reiter's syndrome arthritis symptoms
Reiter's syndrome often presents with arthritis and inflammation of the eyes and urinary tract.
After months of joint pain and eye redness, the doctor diagnosed him with Reiter's syndrome.
Treatment for Reiter's syndrome typically involves managing symptoms and controlling inflammation.
