precision cookie

noun
permanently stored computer cookie
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪʒən ˈkʊki / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪʒ.ən ˈkʊk.i / (BrE)
A computer cookie that is stored permanently on the user's hard drive, allowing websites to remember information across sessions.

Examples

Precision cookie stored permanently
Some websites use precision cookies to remember your preferences for future visits.
Unlike session cookies, precision cookies remain on your device after you close your browser.
You can remove precision cookies by clearing your browser’s stored data manually.

Related Terms

  • synonym: persistent cookie - a cookie that remains on the user's device for a specified period or until deleted
  • antonym: session cookie - a cookie that is deleted when the user closes the browser
