go the way of

verb
to share the fate or decline of something else
2.8
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ðə weɪ əv / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ðə weɪ ɒv / (BrE)
(of something) to experience the same fate as something else, especially to decline or disappear in a similar manner.

Examples

To go the way of dinosaurs
If we ignore these warning signs, our traditions could go the way of so many before them.
Many predict that print newspapers will go the way of the typewriter and become obsolete.
Several of the company’s earlier products have gone the way of the dodo, fading into oblivion.
