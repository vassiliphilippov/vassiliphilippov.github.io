crossing

noun
intersection point of two paths or lines
2.4 1.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsɪŋ /; / ˈkrɑsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
a place where two things cross, such as roads, paths, or a shallow part of a river

Examples

Pedestrian crossing
A new pedestrian crossing was built near the school to improve safety.
The train slowed down as it approached the crossing.
You should be careful when walking at the crossing of busy streets.
Early settlers used this crossing when traveling west.
The wagon train found a safe crossing downstream.
We searched for a crossing where the horses could get across the river.
Always look both ways before using the crossing at a busy intersection.
The train slowed down as it approached the railroad crossing.
There is a pedestrian crossing near the school for students' safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barrier - an obstacle that prevents crossing
  • synonym: crosswalk - a marked part of a road for pedestrians to cross
  • synonym: footbridge - a bridge designed for pedestrians to cross over
  • synonym: ford - a shallow place in a river or stream allowing one to walk or drive across
  • synonym: intersection - the point where two or more things intersect, especially roads
