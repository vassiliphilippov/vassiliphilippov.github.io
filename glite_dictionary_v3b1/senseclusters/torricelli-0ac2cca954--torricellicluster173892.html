Torricelli

proper noun
Italian physicist and barometer inventor
Pronunciation: / ˌtɔːrəˈtʃɛli / (AmE), / ˌtɒrɪˈtʃɛli / (BrE)
Evangelista Torricelli (1608–1647), Italian physicist and mathematician best known for inventing the mercury barometer.

Examples

Torricelli mercury barometer
Torricelli is credited with inventing the mercury barometer in the 17th century.
Many physics textbooks reference Torricelli when discussing atmospheric pressure.
The work of Torricelli greatly influenced the development of experimental physics.
