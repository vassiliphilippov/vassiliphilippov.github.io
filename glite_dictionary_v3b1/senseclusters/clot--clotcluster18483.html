clot

verb
to change from liquid to thickened mass
3.6
Pronunciation: / klɑt / (AmE), / klɒt / (BrE)
to cause to change from a liquid to a solid or thickened mass, especially by coagulation

Examples

To clot the milk
As the blood began to clot, the wound finally stopped bleeding.
If milk is left out too long, it will clot and turn sour.
Some chemicals can clot oil during a spill, making cleanup easier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulate - to change from a liquid to a solid or semi-solid state
  • antonym: liquefy - to make or become liquid
