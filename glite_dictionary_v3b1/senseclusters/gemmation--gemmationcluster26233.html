a type of asexual reproduction in which a growth or bud forms on the parent organism and develops into a new individual

Examples

Gemmation reproduction
Sponges are known to propagate by gemmation under favorable conditions.
The process of gemmation allows simple organisms to multiply efficiently without fertilization.
Yeast reproduces through gemmation, forming tiny buds that detach as new cells.

Synonyms

  • budding
    6.9 1.38/m
    budding is a common asexual process where a new organism grows from a small outgrowth, while gemmation refers to that bud development.
  • blastogenesis
    9.9 0.00/m
    blastogenesis is a technical term for asexual budding in lower organisms, while gemmation is the general term for bud formation.
  • pullulation
    9.9 0.00/m
    pullulation is when new individuals form from growth on or inside a parent, while gemmation usually means an external bud on the parent.
