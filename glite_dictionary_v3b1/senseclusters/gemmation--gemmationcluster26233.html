gemmation

noun
asexual reproduction by budding
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒɛmˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dʒɛmˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a type of asexual reproduction in which a growth or bud forms on the parent organism and develops into a new individual

Examples

Gemmation reproduction
Sponges are known to propagate by gemmation under favorable conditions.
The process of gemmation allows simple organisms to multiply efficiently without fertilization.
Yeast reproduces through gemmation, forming tiny buds that detach as new cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: budding - asexual reproduction in which a new organism develops from an outgrowth or bud
