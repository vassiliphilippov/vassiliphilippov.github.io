transform

verb
change voltage or current in AC electrical circuits
Pronunciation: / trænˈsfɔrm / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to increase or decrease the voltage or current of an alternating electric signal, typically using a transformer.

Examples

To transform voltage
Modern power stations use devices that transform the voltage for efficient transmission over long distances.
The transformer can transform an alternating current from a lower to a higher voltage.
To supply the correct power, the system must transform the input voltage appropriately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: step down - to decrease voltage, especially in electrical engineering
  • synonym: step up - to increase voltage, especially in electrical engineering
