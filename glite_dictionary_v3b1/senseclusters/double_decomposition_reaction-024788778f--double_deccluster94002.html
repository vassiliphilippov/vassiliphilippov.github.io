double decomposition reaction

noun
type of chemical reaction where compounds exchange parts
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌb.əl diː.kəm.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ən riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈdʌb.əl diː.kɒm.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ən riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /ˈdʌb.əl diː.kɒm.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ən rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
A chemical reaction in which two compounds exchange parts to form two new compounds, typically represented as AB + CD → AD + CB.

Examples

A double decomposition reaction occurs
A double decomposition reaction occurs when two solutions are mixed and a precipitate forms.
In a typical double decomposition reaction, silver nitrate reacts with sodium chloride to produce silver chloride and sodium nitrate.
The chemistry teacher demonstrated a double decomposition reaction by mixing two clear solutions that suddenly formed a solid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metathesis reaction - another term for a double decomposition reaction, where ions are exchanged between two reactants
