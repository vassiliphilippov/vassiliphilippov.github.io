mantilla

noun
short cape or cloak for women
5.4
Pronunciation: / mænˈtɪl.jə /; / mænˈtiːjə / (AmE), / manˈtɪl.jə /; / mænˈtɪl.jə / (BrE)
a traditional light scarf or cloak, usually of lace or silk, worn by women over the head and shoulders, especially in Spain and South America

Examples

A mantilla scarf
Her traditional dress was completed with an embroidered silk mantilla.
She wore a black mantilla over her shoulders at the evening reception.
The museum showcased a beautiful mantilla from the nineteenth century.
Many women wore a white mantilla during the religious procession.
She draped a mantilla over her hair before entering the old church.
The black lace mantilla complemented her traditional dress beautifully.
