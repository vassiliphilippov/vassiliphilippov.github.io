raku

noun
Japanese pottery style
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːkuː / (AmE), / ˈrɑːkuː / (BrE)
a type of Japanese pottery made by rapidly firing and cooling clay, often resulting in unique colors and textures

Examples

A raku vase
The artist created a beautiful raku vase with striking metallic glazes.
During the workshop, students learned the unique firing process of raku pottery.
Collectors value raku pieces for their unpredictable patterns and colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceramics - objects made from clay and hardened by heat
  • synonym: porcelain - a white, fine, hard type of pottery
