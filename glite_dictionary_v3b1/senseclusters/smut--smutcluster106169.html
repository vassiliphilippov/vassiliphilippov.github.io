smut

noun
parasitic fungus causing plant disease
Pronunciation: / smʌt / (AmE), / smʌt / (BrE)
a plant disease caused by parasitic fungi that produce black, powdery masses of spores, especially on grains

Examples

Smut fungus infection
Farmers must monitor for signs of smut to protect their barley and corn.
Scientists are developing new methods to control smut in cereal crops.
The wheat crop was destroyed by a smut infection that spread rapidly through the fields.
Black smut appeared on the leaves, signaling a fungal infection in the barley field.
Farmers monitor their fields closely during the rainy season to prevent outbreaks of smut.
Wheat crops can be severely damaged by smut if not treated properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blight - a plant disease causing withering and death without fungal spores
  • synonym: fungus - group of organisms including molds, mushrooms, and yeasts
  • synonym: rust - a plant disease caused by a fungus, producing reddish spores
