low pitch

noun
sound with deep, low frequency
Pronunciation: / loʊ pɪtʃ / (AmE), / ləʊ pɪtʃ / (BrE)
a musical or vocal sound that has a frequency perceived as lower than others; a deep tone.

Examples

A low pitch sound
The cello produces a low pitch that adds depth to the orchestra.
Whales communicate by emitting sounds of very low pitch across the ocean.
When he spoke, his voice dropped to a surprisingly low pitch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baritone - a male singing voice of fairly low pitch
  • antonym: high pitch - a sound perceived as above other pitches; a sharp, high frequency sound
