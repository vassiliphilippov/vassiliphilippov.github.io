restoration

noun
model of past landscape or extinct organism
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛstəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a model or reconstruction representing the appearance of a landscape in a former geological period or of an extinct animal or plant.

Examples

Dinosaur restoration
A new restoration shows how the region looked during the Jurassic period.
Researchers created a restoration of the extinct saber-toothed tiger for the exhibit.
The museum features a detailed restoration of a prehistoric forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reconstruction - the act or result of reconstructing, especially of a historical scene or extinct organism
