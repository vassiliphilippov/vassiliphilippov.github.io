hogtie

verb
to restrict movement
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːg.taɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhɒg.taɪ/ (BrE)
to severely restrict or prevent someone or something from acting, moving, or making progress

Examples

Rules hogtie progress
Strict regulations can hogtie innovation in the technology sector.
The team's efforts were hogtied by a lack of funding and resources.
Bureaucratic procedures often hogtie quick decision-making in large organizations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - to allow someone or something to act or move without restriction
  • synonym: hamper - to make it difficult for someone to do something
  • synonym: impede - to slow down or block progress
