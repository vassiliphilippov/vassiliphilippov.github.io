George Berkeley

proper noun
Irish philosopher and Anglican bishop
8.5
Pronunciation: /dʒɔːrdʒ ˈbɜːr.kliː/ (AmE), /dʒɔːdʒ ˈbɑːk.liː/ (BrE)
(1685–1753) Irish philosopher and Anglican bishop, known for his rejection of materialism and his theory of immaterialism (idealism).

Examples

The immaterialist philosophy of George Berkeley had a profound influence on later thinkers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bishop Berkeley - Alternative reference to George Berkeley as a religious figure
  • synonym: idealism - Berkeley's philosophical doctrine that reality consists only of minds and ideas
  • antonym: materialism - The doctrine opposed by Berkeley, which asserts physical matter is the only reality
