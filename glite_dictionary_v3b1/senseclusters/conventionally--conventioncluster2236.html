conventionally

adverb
in a usual or traditional manner
5.1
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛnʃənəli / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəli / (BrE)
In a manner that is usual, traditional, or accepted by most people.

Examples

Dressed conventionally
She dressed conventionally for the interview to make a good impression.
The building was conventionally designed, following standard architectural norms.
The solution, though conventionally accepted, may not be the most effective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unconventionally - in a way that is not traditional or usual
