phycoerythrin

noun
bright fluorescent protein label (PE)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / \u02ccfa\u026ako\u028a\u02c8\u025br\u026a\u03b8r\u026an / (AmE), / \u02ccf\u028c\u026ak\u0259\u028a\u02c8\u025br\u026a\u03b8r\u026an / (BrE)
(in biomedical laboratories) a bright fluorescent protein, originally from red algae, used as a dye or label (often conjugated to antibodies) for detecting molecules in flow cytometry and fluorescence imaging.

Examples

Phycoerythrin dye label
In flow cytometry, phycoerythrin is used as a bright fluorescent label to detect cell-surface proteins.
She conjugated the antibody to phycoerythrin so the immune cells would fluoresce under the microscope.
The lab ordered extra phycoerythrin conjugates for the multi-color staining panel next week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluorescent label - a fluorescent molecule attached to another molecule (for example, an antibody) to allow detection
  • synonym: fluorophore - a molecule that emits light (fluoresces) when excited and is used as a label in imaging or assays
