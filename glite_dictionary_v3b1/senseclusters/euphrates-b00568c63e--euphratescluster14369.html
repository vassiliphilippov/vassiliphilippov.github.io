Euphrates

proper noun
major river in southwestern Asia
Pronunciation: / juˈfreɪtiz / (AmE), / juːˈfreɪtiːz / (BrE)
a major river in southwestern Asia, flowing through Turkey, Syria, and Iraq into the Persian Gulf; significant in the development of ancient Mesopotamian civilizations

Examples

The Euphrates River
Ancient cities like Babylon were established along the Euphrates for access to water and fertile land.
The Euphrates is one of the two great rivers that define Mesopotamia, the other being the Tigris.
Today, the Euphrates remains vital for agriculture and life in the region.
