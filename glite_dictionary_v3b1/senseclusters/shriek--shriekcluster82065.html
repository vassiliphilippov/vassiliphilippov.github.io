shriek

verb
utter a high-pitched cry
2.9 7.17/m
Pronunciation: /  Érik / (AmE), /  ÉriÀêk / (BrE)
to utter a loud, high-pitched cry, often due to fear, pain, or excitement

Examples

To shriek in fear
He shrieked in pain when he accidentally stepped on the sharp stone.
She was so startled she let out a loud shriek of terror.
The children began to shriek with laughter as they played in the yard.

Synonyms

  • scream
    1.0 84/m
    scream is a loud emotional cry used in fear or anger, while shriek is higher, sharper, and more sudden.
  • shrill
    4.8 0.36/m
    shrill describes a high, piercing sound often as an adjective, while shriek is a specific loud human cry.
  • screech
    4.0 5.91/m
    screech is a harsh, piercing sound often from animals or brakes, while shriek is a sharp human cry from fear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scream - to make a loud, piercing sound, usually from fear or pain
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly, using one's breath rather than one's throat
