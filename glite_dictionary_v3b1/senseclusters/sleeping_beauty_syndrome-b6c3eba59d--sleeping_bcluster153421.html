Sleeping Beauty syndrome

noun
recurrent hypersomnia (Kleine–Levin) syndrome
Pronunciation: / c8slid0p6a4b c8bjud0ti c8sɪndroʊm / (AmE), / c8slid0p6a4b c8bjud0ti c8sɪndr598am / (BrE)
(informal, medicine) a neurological sleep disorder, also called Kleine–Levin syndrome, marked by recurrent episodes of excessive sleep (hypersomnia) often with confusion, altered behaviour, and cognitive problems.

Examples

Sleeping Beauty syndrome episodes
After recurring episodes of prolonged sleep, doctors diagnosed her with Sleeping Beauty syndrome last spring.

Her family learned to manage school absences after each episode of Sleeping Beauty syndrome lasted several days.
