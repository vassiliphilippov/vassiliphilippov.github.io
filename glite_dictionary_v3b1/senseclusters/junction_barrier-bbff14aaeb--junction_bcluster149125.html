junction barrier

noun
device for connecting cables without plugs
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən ˌbæriɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən ˌbæriə / (BrE)
a device that connects two cables or wires directly, without the use of plugs

Examples

A junction barrier device
Each circuit was linked through a junction barrier for easy maintenance.
The technician installed a junction barrier to ensure the wires were securely connected.
Using a junction barrier allows for cable connections without needing special plugs.
