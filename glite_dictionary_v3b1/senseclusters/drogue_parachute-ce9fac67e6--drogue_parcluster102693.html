drogue parachute

noun
small parachute to decelerate vehicles
8.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /droʊg ˈpær.ə.ʃuːt/ /; / /droʊg ˈper.ə.ʃuːt/ (AmE), /drəʊg ˈpær.ə.ʃuːt/ /; / /drəʊg ˈper.ə.ʃuːt/ (BrE)
a small parachute used to slow down a fast-moving object or to help deploy the main parachute

Examples

A drogue parachute slows
After separation, the spacecraft’s drogue parachute helped slow its descent through the atmosphere.
Racing cars sometimes use a drogue parachute to stop quickly after crossing the finish line.
The pilot deployed the drogue parachute before landing on the short runway.
In high-speed aircraft, a drogue parachute is crucial for safe ejection seat operation.
The drogue parachute deployed first to ensure a smooth release of the main canopy.
Without the drogue parachute, the main parachute might not deploy correctly.
