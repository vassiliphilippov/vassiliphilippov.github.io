conniption

noun
sudden fit of anger or frustration
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɪpʃən / (AmE), / kəˈnɪpʃən / (BrE)
A sudden outburst of anger or frustration; a fit of bad temper.

Examples

Throw a conniption
She throws a conniption whenever someone moves her things without asking.
The coach nearly had a conniption after the team made yet another error.
When he saw the bill, he had a complete conniption in the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fit - a sudden and violent outburst of emotion
  • synonym: tantrum - an uncontrolled outburst of anger and frustration
