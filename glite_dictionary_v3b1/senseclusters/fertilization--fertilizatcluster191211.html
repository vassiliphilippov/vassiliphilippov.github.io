fertilization

noun
union of gametes to begin development
5.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfɜrtələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌfɝːtɪləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜːtɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an egg or ovule joins with a sperm or pollen to begin the development of a new individual in animals or plants

Examples

Egg fertilization
In flowering plants, fertilization occurs when pollen reaches the ovule.
Scientists observed the process of fertilization under a microscope.
The fertilization of the ovum by the sperm marks the beginning of pregnancy.
After fertilisation, the embryo begins to develop rapidly.
Scientists study fertilisation to better understand genetic inheritance.
The fertilisation of the ovule by pollen is crucial in plant reproduction.

  • synonym: pollination - transfer of pollen to fertilise plants
  • antonym: sterilisation - process that makes an organism unable to reproduce
