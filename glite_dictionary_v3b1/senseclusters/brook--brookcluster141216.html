brook

verb
tolerate or allow (formal/literary use)
8.8 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / brÊŠk / (AmE), / brÊŠk / (BrE)
to tolerate or allow something, especially something unpleasant or unwelcome; usually used in the negative or formal contexts.

Examples

To brook no criticism
He refuses to brook any delays to the project.
She would not brook any criticism of her work.
The manager will brook no interference in the decision-making process.

Synonyms

  • tolerate
    3.5 15/m
    tolerate is to allow or endure something unpleasant without protest, while brook is usually a formal verb used especially in negative statements.
  • abide
    4.6 2.59/m
    abide means to tolerate or put up with something unpleasant, while brook is more formal and often appears in negative phrases.
  • stand for
    3.5 2.61/m
    stand for means to accept something wrong without protest, while brook is a formal verb often used in the negative.
  • bear
    3.9 49/m
    bear means to endure or cope with something difficult for a long time, while brook is more about allowing or permitting.
  • stand
    3.1 91/m
    stand often means to tolerate in everyday speech, while brook is mainly a formal verb meaning to allow or permit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endure - to suffer patiently, to tolerate over time
  • antonym: forbid - to order not to do something; refuse to allow
  • synonym: tolerate - to allow the existence or occurrence of something one dislikes
