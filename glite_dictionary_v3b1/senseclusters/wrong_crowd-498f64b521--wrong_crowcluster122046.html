wrong crowd

noun
group with bad influence
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɔŋ kraʊd / (AmE), / rɒŋ kraʊd / (BrE)
a group of people considered to have a negative or harmful influence, especially on someone's behavior or reputation

Examples

The wrong crowd
After he started hanging out with the wrong crowd, his grades began to slip.
His parents worried that he would fall in with the wrong crowd at his new school.
She left her old friends to avoid getting mixed up with the wrong crowd.
