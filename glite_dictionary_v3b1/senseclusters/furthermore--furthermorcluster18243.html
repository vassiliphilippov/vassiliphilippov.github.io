furthermore

used to introduce an additional point or information that strengthens or emphasizes what has already been said

Examples

Used furthermore to add
He was late for the meeting; furthermore, he forgot to bring the reports.
The new manager is highly qualified; furthermore, she brings valuable experience to the team.
The project will be expensive; furthermore, it could take several years to complete.

Synonyms

  • moreover
    3.8 47/m
    moreover is formal and adds a strong supporting point, while furthermore feels slightly more neutral and general in tone.
  • in addition
    1.2 50/m
    in addition is a neutral link for extra facts or points, while furthermore usually emphasizes points more strongly.
  • additionally
    3.6 24/m
    additionally sounds slightly more formal and written, while furthermore is common in both speech and formal writing.
  • further
    1.0 0.08/m
    further often appears inside sentences or before verbs, while furthermore normally starts a sentence to add a point.
  • what is more
    3.5 0.60/m
    what is more is a spoken, emphatic phrase for surprising extra facts, while furthermore stays more neutral and formal.
  • besides
    1.0 39/m
    besides is casual and can dismiss earlier points, while furthermore is formal and clearly adds supporting information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: however - used to introduce a contrast or contradiction
  • synonym: moreover - used to add information or support for a point just made
