preterist

noun
a person believing biblical prophecies are already fulfilled
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛtərɪst / (AmE), / ˈpriːtərɪst / (BrE)
a theologian or believer who holds that the prophecies of the Apocalypse (Book of Revelation) have already been fulfilled, especially those in the past relating to the first century AD

Examples

A preterist theologian
As a preterist, he viewed the fall of Jerusalem as a key prophetic event.
Many preterists argue that Nero was the antichrist referenced in the Bible.
The preterist interpretation asserts that most prophecies in Revelation were fulfilled in the first century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: futurist - a person who believes biblical prophecies will be fulfilled in the future
