St. Simon

proper noun
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus
6.7
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈsaɪmən / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈsɪmən / (BrE)
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus Christ in the first century, also known as Simon the Zealot.

Examples

St. Simon the Apostle
According to tradition, St. Simon traveled widely to spread the Christian faith.
Many churches are named after St. Simon in honor of his dedication as an apostle.
The feast day of St. Simon is often celebrated with special prayers and readings.
