checkpoint

verb
to set up checkpoint
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkpɔɪnt / (BrE)
to set up or operate a checkpoint, or to stop and inspect people or vehicles at a checkpoint

Examples

Police checkpoint cars
During the parade, police checkpointed the main street to ensure everyone's safety.
The border guards checkpointed every vehicle entering the restricted area.
After the incident, authorities checkpointed the highway to check for suspicious activity.
All vehicles were checkpointed before entering the secure area.
The travelers were checkpointed at the border crossing by the guards.
During the emergency, every person was checkpointed for identification and safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to or not examine
  • synonym: inspect - to examine or check carefully
  • synonym: monitor - to watch and check something carefully over a period of time
  • synonym: screen - to examine or check for security or suitability
  • synonym: search - to look through or examine for something hidden or prohibited
