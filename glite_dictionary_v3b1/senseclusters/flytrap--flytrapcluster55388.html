flytrap

noun
insect-eating plant
4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈflaɪ.træp/ (AmE), /ˈflaɪ.træp/ (BrE)
a plant that catches and digests insects for food, typically using a specialized trapping mechanism

Examples

Venus flytrap
The flytrap snapped shut as soon as the insect touched its sensitive hairs.
Many people keep a flytrap on their windowsill to help control bugs indoors.
A flytrap needs plenty of sunlight and water to stay healthy and catch insects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pitcher plant - a carnivorous plant that traps insects in a deep cavity filled with liquid
