The quality of being excessively sensitive, especially to emotional stimuli, leading to easy irritation or upset.

Examples

His oversensitiveness to feelings
His oversensitiveness made it difficult for him to handle criticism at work.
She struggled with oversensitiveness, often feeling hurt by harmless remarks.
Some children exhibit oversensitiveness to loud noises or bright lights.

Synonyms

  • oversensitivity
    5.8 0.06/m
    oversensitivity means reacting too strongly emotionally, while oversensitiveness stresses an excessive sensitivity that leads to frequent upset or irritation.
  • touchiness
    7.2 0.05/m
    touchiness shows being easily annoyed by small remarks, while oversensitiveness describes a broader, often emotional, tendency to become upset.
  • hypersensitivity
    7.0 0.54/m
    hypersensitivity often means an extreme, sometimes medical, reaction to stimuli, while oversensitiveness focuses on emotional upset and irritation.
  • supersensitivity
    8.1 0.00/m
    supersensitivity describes very strong physical or emotional reactivity, while oversensitiveness mainly refers to being easily emotionally upset.
