at all costs

adverb
no matter what; whatever it takes
3.7 3.39/m
Pronunciation: / …ôt …îl k…îsts / (AmE), / …ôt …îÀêl k…ísts / (BrE)
no matter what the expense, effort, or risk may be; whatever happens or is required.

Examples

At all costs protect
He promised to protect his family at all costs.
The rescue team was determined to reach the stranded climbers at all costs.
You should maintain your integrity at all costs, even when it is difficult.

Synonyms

  • at any cost
    4.1 0.93/m
    at any cost insists on doing something no matter the difficulty or price, while at all costs sounds slightly more emphatic and formal.
  • at any expense
    6.0 0.00/m
    at any expense focuses on money or sacrifice above all, while at all costs covers money, effort, and risk in any situation.
  • no matter what happens
    3.2 2.35/m
    no matter what happens says do it despite any event or problem, while at all costs stresses paying any price or taking any risk.
  • come what may
    6.3 0.29/m
    come what may expresses firm acceptance of whatever happens, often poetic or old-fashioned, while at all costs stresses paying any price or risk.
  • by any means necessary
    7.0 0.12/m
    by any means necessary allows any method to reach a goal, sometimes harsh or illegal, while at all costs focuses on price, effort, or risk.
