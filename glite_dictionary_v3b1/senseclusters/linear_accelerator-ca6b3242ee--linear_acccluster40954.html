linear accelerator

noun
device accelerating charged particles in a straight line
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪniɚ əkˈsɛləˌreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə əksɛlɪˌreɪtə / (BrE)
A device that accelerates charged particles or ions along a straight path by applying successive voltage differences across electrodes.

Examples

A linear accelerator device
Maintenance of the linear accelerator is crucial for ensuring accurate radiation therapy.
Scientists use a linear accelerator to study the properties of subatomic particles.
The hospital acquired a new linear accelerator to improve its cancer treatment capabilities.
