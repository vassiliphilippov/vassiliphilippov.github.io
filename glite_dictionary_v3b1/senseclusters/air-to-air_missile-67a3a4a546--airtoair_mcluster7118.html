air-to-air missile

noun
missile launched from one aircraft at another
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛr tə ˈɛr ˈmɪsaɪl / (AmE), / ˌɛə tə ˈɛə ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
a missile designed to be launched from one aircraft to destroy another aircraft in flight

Examples

Modern air-to-air missiles
Modern air-to-air missiles use advanced guidance systems to lock onto their targets.
Pilots must be trained extensively to effectively deploy air-to-air missiles in combat situations.
The fighter jet fired an air-to-air missile at the approaching enemy aircraft.
