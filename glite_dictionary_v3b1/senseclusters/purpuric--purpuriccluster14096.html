purpuric

adjective
purple-colored
9.8
Pronunciation: / pərˈpjʊrɪk / (AmE), / pəˈpjʊərɪk / (BrE)
having a purple or purplish color, especially relating to discoloration of the skin or tissues, often due to purpura (a condition causing purple spots on the skin)

Examples

Purpuric skin spots
The doctor noted several purpuric spots on the patient's arms during the examination.
Artists sometimes use a purpuric hue to create dramatic effects in their paintings.
The fabric was dyed a deep purpuric shade for the costume design.
The doctor noted several purpuric spots on the patient's legs during the examination.
A purpuric rash can be a sign of a serious underlying medical condition.
Children with certain infections may develop purpuric lesions on their skin.

  • degree lower: reddish - having a red or slightly red color
