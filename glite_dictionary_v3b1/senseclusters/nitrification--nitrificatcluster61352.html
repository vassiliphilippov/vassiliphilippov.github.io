nitrification

noun
chemical process adding a nitro group or converting ammonia
6.8 7.0
Pronunciation: /naɪ.trə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /naɪ.trə.fə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /naɪ.trɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /naɪ.trə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the chemical process by which a nitro group is introduced into an organic compound, or substituted for another group; also, the process by which ammonia is converted into nitrites and then nitrates by bacteria in soil.

Examples

Chemical nitrification reaction
Nitrification is a key step in the nitrogen cycle, converting ammonia into nitrites and nitrates.
Chemical nitrification is used in the production of explosives and fertilizers.
High rates of nitrification in soils can lead to groundwater contamination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denitrification - the process by which nitrates are reduced to nitrogen gas, typically by bacteria
