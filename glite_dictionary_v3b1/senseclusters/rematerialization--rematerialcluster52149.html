rematerialization

noun
becoming physical again
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːməˌtɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːməˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming material or physical again after having previously disappeared or become immaterial

Examples

Rematerialization of a spaceship
The sudden rematerialization of the spaceship startled the crew on the ground.
After the magician's trick, the coin's rematerialization amazed the audience.
Scientists in the story studied the rematerialization of objects after teleportation experiments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dematerialization - the process of becoming immaterial or ceasing to be physical
  • synonym: reappearance - the act of appearing again after being gone
