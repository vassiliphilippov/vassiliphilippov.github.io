spoof

noun
comedically mocking imitation
5.3 1/m
Pronunciation: / spuÀêf / (AmE), / spuÀêf / (BrE)
a humorous imitation of something, especially a film, television program, or style, intended to mock or satirize the original

Examples

A spoof movie
Her new book is essentially a spoof of old-fashioned detective novels.
The show opened with a clever spoof of a famous political debate.
They created a hilarious spoof of the latest blockbuster movie.

Synonyms

  • parody
    5.0 7/m
    parody is a close, often creative imitation of a work for humour or critique, while spoof is lighter.
  • send-up
    8.4 0.4/m
    send-up is an informal, exaggerated mock of a person or show, while spoof is a general comic imitation.
  • takeoff
    6.3 0.4/m
    takeoff is a quick, comic copy of someone's style used in speech or TV, while spoof is broader.
  • burlesque
    8.7 0.1/m
    burlesque is a theatrical, often exaggerated and bawdy imitation of style, while spoof is usually gentler and comic.
  • lampoon
    8.0 0.6/m
    lampoon is a sharp written satire that attacks people or ideas, while spoof is a milder, playful imitation.
  • travesty
    7.7 2/m
    travesty is an absurd or distorted version that makes something seem ridiculous, while spoof is lighter and more playful.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homage - something that shows respect or tribute, often by imitation
  • synonym: parody - a work created to imitate, make fun of, or comment on an original work
  • synonym: satire - use of humor, irony, or ridicule to criticize people's stupidity or vices
