Muskogean

noun
member of a southeastern US indigenous group
9.9
Pronunciation: / mʌsˈkoʊɡiən / (AmE), / mʌsˈkəʊɡiən / (BrE)
a member of any of the indigenous peoples formerly living in the southeastern United States and speaking Muskogean languages

Examples

Muskogean peoples
Archaeologists have studied the ancient settlements of Muskogean peoples for evidence of their way of life.
Several Muskogean tribes still maintain cultural traditions in the southeastern United States.
The Muskogean were known for their mound-building cultures and complex societies.
