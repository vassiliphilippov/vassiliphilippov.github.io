bid

verb
ask or instruct someone to do something
6.5
Pronunciation: / bɪd / (AmE), / bɪd / (BrE)
to ask or instruct someone, often politely or formally, to do something; to ask or request earnestly or formally; to command or enjoin

Examples

To bid someone enter
He bid us enter the room with a welcoming gesture.
She bid him farewell as he left for college.
The queen bid her guests to be seated at the banquet.
She bade her guests farewell as they left the party.
The king bid his subjects to remain calm during the crisis.
The teacher bids the students to pay attention in class.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: command - to give an authoritative order
  • antonym: forbid - to order not to do something
  • synonym: order - to give a command or authoritative instruction
  • synonym: request - to politely or formally ask for something
