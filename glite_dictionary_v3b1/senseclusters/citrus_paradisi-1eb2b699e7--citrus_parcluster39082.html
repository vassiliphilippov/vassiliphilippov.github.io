Citrus paradisi

noun
species of tree producing grapefruits
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪtrəs pəˈrædɪˌsaɪ / (AmE), / ˌsɪtrəs pəˈreɪdɪsiː / (BrE)
A species of citrus tree that produces grapefruits, large round edible fruits with a thick yellow rind and juicy, slightly acidic pulp.

Examples

Citrus paradisi fruit
Botanists studied the Citrus paradisi to understand the origins of the grapefruit.
The Citrus paradisi thrives in subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
The commercial production of Citrus paradisi has increased significantly in recent years.
