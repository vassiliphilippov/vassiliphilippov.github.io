sconcheon arch

noun
arch supporting a wall section in architecture
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsk…ën.t É…ôn …ërt É / (AmE), / Ààsk…ín.t É…ôn …ëÀêt É / (BrE)
an arch built in the thickness of a wall to support part of the wall above an opening, such as a window or door

Examples

A sconcheon arch in wall
A sconcheon arch allows for the stability of the stone wall despite the large door opening.
Architects often use a sconcheon arch to redistribute the weight of masonry over an alcove.
The restoration of the manor house revealed a beautiful sconcheon arch above the medieval window.
