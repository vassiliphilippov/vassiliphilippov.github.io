pelt down

verb
to run or move quickly
Pronunciation: / pɛlt daʊn / (AmE), / pɛlt daʊn / (BrE)
to move or run somewhere very quickly, especially in a headlong or uncontrolled way

Examples

To pelt down stairs
He pelted down the stairs when he heard the phone ring.
The children pelted down the street, laughing and shouting as they went.
When the final bell rang, students pelted down the hallway to get outside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crawl - to move slowly
  • synonym: dash - to run or move quickly and suddenly
  • synonym: race - to move at great speed
