submarine earthquake

noun
undersea earthquake
4.0
Pronunciation: / sʌbˌmɛrin ˈɜrθkweɪk / (AmE), / sʌbˌmɑːriːn ˈɜːθkweɪk / (BrE)
An earthquake that occurs beneath the sea bed, often capable of generating tsunamis.

Examples

A submarine earthquake
A submarine earthquake can trigger devastating tsunamis along coastlines.
After the submarine earthquake, warnings were issued for several islands in the region.
Scientists measured the magnitude of the submarine earthquake that struck near the Pacific trench.
