brittleness

noun
fragility of systems or models
7.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / c8br6atn59s / (AmE), / c8br6atn6as / (BrE)
The tendency of a system, model, policy, or software to fail, break, or perform poorly when exposed to small changes, unusual inputs, or unexpected conditions; lack of robustness.

Examples

Software brittleness
The model's brittleness under adversarial inputs revealed a need for robust training.
Policy makers worried that the new regulations' brittleness would cause failure when applied to unusual cases.
Engineers ran tests to measure the software's brittleness after small changes to the input data.

Synonyms

  • fragility
    5.2 1.98/m
    fragility refers to being easily broken or damaged in many contexts, while brittleness means failing under small changes in systems or models.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged; similar notion of vulnerability
  • antonym: robustness - the ability of a system or model to cope with variation or unexpected inputs; opposite of brittleness
