socialization

noun
placing under socialist or state control
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsəʊʃəlʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of placing industries or services under state ownership or control according to socialist principles

Examples

Socialization of industries
During the 20th century, several countries underwent the socialization of their healthcare systems.
The socialization of industries was a major policy objective of the new administration.
The government announced the socialization of electricity production as part of its economic reforms.
After the revolution, there was a rapid socialisation of all major industries.
Debates arose regarding the benefits and drawbacks of the socialisation of railways.
The socialisation of healthcare aimed to make services accessible to everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nationalization - the process of bringing an industry or assets under government control
  • antonym: privatization - the transfer of business, industry, or service from public to private ownership and control
