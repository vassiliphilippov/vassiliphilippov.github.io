Hemerobiidae

noun
family of brown lacewings (insects)
Pronunciation: / hɛmərəˈbaɪɪdiː / (AmE), / hɛmərəˈbaɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects known as brown lacewings, characterized by delicate, net-veined wings and predatory larvae, commonly found in temperate climates.

Examples

Family Hemerobiidae insects
Brown lacewings of the family Hemerobiidae are common predators of aphids in gardens.
Entomologists study Hemerobiidae to understand their role in biological pest control.
Members of Hemerobiidae can be distinguished from green lacewings by their coloring and wing structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lacewing - Any insect of the order Neuroptera, typically with delicate, net-like wings.
