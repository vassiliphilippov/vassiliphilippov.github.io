suspensive

adjective
causing a delay
7.7
Pronunciation: / səˈspɛnsɪv / (AmE), / səˈspɛnsɪv / (BrE)
causing or relating to a temporary stop, delay, or suspension of action or process

Examples

Suspensive order
The court issued a suspensive order to temporarily halt the construction project.
A suspensive effect was granted, delaying the implementation of the new law.
The contract included a suspensive clause that postponed payment until delivery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • synonym: suspending - temporarily stopping or delaying something
