trifoliolate

adjective
having three leaflets (botany)
Pronunciation: / traɪˈfoʊliəˌleɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈfəʊliəˌleɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) having three leaflets.

Examples

Trifoliolate leaf
Many legumes produce trifoliolate leaves, which help botanists identify them easily.
The trifoliolate pattern is typical among certain species within the pea family.
The clover is known for its trifoliolate leaves that are commonly considered lucky.

  • antonym: unifoliolate - (of a leaf) having a single leaflet
