laudably

adverb
in an admirable or praiseworthy manner
7.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…îÀêd…ôbli / (AmE), / Ààl…îÀêd…ôbli / (BrE)
In a way that deserves praise or approval; in an admirable manner.

Examples

Handled laudably well
He handled the difficult situation laudably, earning the respect of everyone involved.
She acted laudably in coming forward with the important information.
The committee performed their duties laudably despite limited resources.

Synonyms

  • admirably
    3.3 1.68/m
    admirably shows an action done in a way people admire and respect, while laudably names the same praiseworthy quality.
  • commendably
    5.3 0.16/m
    commendably describes a clearly praiseworthy act people openly applaud, while laudably sounds more formal and used for praise.
  • praiseworthily
    8.8 0.00/m
    praiseworthily describes actions done in a way that clearly deserves praise, while laudably is a similar formal way to praise.
  • meritoriously
    7.9 0.01/m
    meritoriously describes deserving reward or official merit for work or action, while laudably focuses on praise or approval in general.
  • worthily
    6.9 0.08/m
    worthily means behaving or acting in a manner that earns respect and dignity, while laudably highlights actions that win praise.
  • creditably
    6.3 0.06/m
    creditably means doing something well enough to deserve some praise or credit, while laudably implies stronger or clearer approval.
