sacrifice

verb
sell something at a loss
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsækrəˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsak.rɪ.faɪs / (BrE)
to sell something at a loss, especially out of necessity or in order to raise money quickly

Examples

To sacrifice a car
In a hurry to pay his debts, he had to sacrifice his car for far less than it was worth.
Retailers are often forced to sacrifice inventory at the end of the season.
They chose to sacrifice several assets to keep the business afloat.
