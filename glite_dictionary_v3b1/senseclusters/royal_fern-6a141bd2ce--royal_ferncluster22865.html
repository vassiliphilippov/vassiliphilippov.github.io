royal fern

noun
a large, widespread fern with tufted fronds
Pronunciation: / ˈrɔɪ.əl fɝn / (AmE), / ˈrɔɪ.əl fɜːn / (BrE)
a large fern with upright, bipinnate fronds, widely distributed across the world, known for its tufted appearance

Examples

The royal fern plant
Along the stream bank, clusters of royal fern created a lush, green backdrop for the wildflowers.
The royal fern thrives in moist, shady woodlands throughout much of the temperate world.
Unlike some ferns, the royal fern can grow quite tall when given the right conditions.
