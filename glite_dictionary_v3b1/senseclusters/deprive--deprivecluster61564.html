deprive

verb
take something away from
5.4 2.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈpraɪv/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈpraɪv/ (BrE)
to take something important or necessary away from someone, or to prevent them from having it

Examples

To deprive someone of rights
Chronic illness can deprive people of their independence.
No one should deprive children of an education.
The new policy may deprive some families of essential benefits.
Being isolated like that can deprive a person of human contact.
No one should deprive children of access to education.
The new law will deprive many people of their basic rights.
His illness may deprive him of the chance to travel this year.
She was deprived of sleep for several nights due to the noise outside.
The new law will deprive low-income families of vital support.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grant - to give or allow someone something
  • antonym: restore - to give back or return something that was taken away
  • synonym: rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place
  • synonym: strip - to take something away from someone, especially rights or possessions
