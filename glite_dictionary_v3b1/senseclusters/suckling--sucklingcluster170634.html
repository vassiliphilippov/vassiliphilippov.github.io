suckling

noun
infant or animal still nursing
7.0 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌk.lɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsʌk.lɪŋ/ (BrE)
a baby or young animal that is still feeding on its mother's milk and has not yet been weaned

Examples

A suckling animal
In ancient times, the health of a suckling was often closely linked to the mother's well-being.
The lioness kept her suckling cubs close to protect them from danger.
The mother gently cradled her suckling in her arms as she sang a lullaby.
A lioness is fiercely protective of her suckling cubs.
The farmer kept the sucklings separate from the adult sheep for their own protection.
The vet checked the health of the sucklings in the barn.
