proinflammatory

adjective
causing inflammation (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊɪnˌflæməˈtɔri / (AmE), / ˌprəʊɪnˌflæməˈtɔːri / (BrE)
Promoting or tending to cause inflammation, especially in biological tissues.

Examples

Proinflammatory molecules
Research focuses on ways to block proinflammatory pathways in patients with chronic diseases.
Several cytokines are known to have proinflammatory effects in the immune response.
The doctor recommended avoiding foods that trigger a proinflammatory reaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anti-inflammatory - reducing or preventing inflammation
