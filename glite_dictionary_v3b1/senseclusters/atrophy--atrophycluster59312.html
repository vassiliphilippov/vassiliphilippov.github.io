atrophy

verb
waste away or shrink (of tissue or organ)
4.0 7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈætrəfi / (AmE), / ˈætrəfi / (BrE)
(of body tissue or an organ) to waste away or decrease in size, typically due to disuse or disease.

Examples

To atrophy from disuse
After the injury, her leg muscles began to atrophy from lack of use.
If you do not exercise regularly, your muscles may atrophy over time.
Without mental stimulation, certain cognitive skills may also atrophy with age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deteriorate - to become worse or degenerate
  • synonym: wither - to become weak and dry, shrink (as of plants or body parts)
