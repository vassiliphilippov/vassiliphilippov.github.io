seat

noun
membership position in a legislative body
2.4
Pronunciation: / siːt / (AmE), / siːt / (BrE)
a position as a member of a legislative or similar official body, gained by election or appointment

Examples

A seat in parliament
He lost his seat in the city council in the last election.
She won a seat in Parliament after a hard-fought campaign.
There are only fifty seats available in the regional assembly.
