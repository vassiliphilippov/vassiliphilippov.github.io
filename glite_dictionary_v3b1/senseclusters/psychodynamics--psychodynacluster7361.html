psychodynamics

noun
interaction of mental processes affecting personality
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the study or theory of how conscious and unconscious mental and emotional processes interact and influence personality, motivation, and behavior

Examples

Psychodynamics theory
Many theories of personality are rooted in the study of psychodynamics.
Modern therapy often explores the psychodynamics underlying a patient's behavior.
Understanding the psychodynamics of a group can improve teamwork and communication.
An understanding of psychodynamics can help explain certain patterns of behavior in relationships.
He specialized in psychodynamics, focusing on the emotional drivers behind human motivation.
The therapist employed techniques based on psychodynamics to explore her patient's unconscious conflicts.

  • synonym: psychoanalysis - A set of therapeutic and theoretical approaches to understanding the unconscious mind
