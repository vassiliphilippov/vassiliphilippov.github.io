unload

verb
vent feelings to someone
4.2
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈloʊd/ /; / /ʌn.ˈloʊd/ (AmE), /ən.ˈləʊd/ /; / /ʌn.ˈləʊd/ (BrE)
to express strong negative feelings, complaints, or anger to someone; to vent or pour out one's frustrations onto another person (often used with on).

Examples

To unload anger
After the meeting she began to unload her frustrations on anyone who would listen.
He unloaded on his friend about the unfair treatment at work last night.
Rather than keep quiet, she started unloading her anger at the support group meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bottle up - to keep emotions inside rather than expressing them
  • synonym: vent - to express strong emotions or complaints in order to get relief
