long tail

noun
business strategy for niche sales
4.9
Pronunciation: /lɔːŋ teɪl/ /; / /lʊŋ teɪl/ (AmE), /lɒŋ teɪl/ /; / /lʊŋ teɪl/ (BrE)
a business or market concept where many unique or rare items, each selling in small quantities, together make up a significant total (often used for online sales or statistics)

Examples

Long tail sales
Many online stores use the long tail to profit from selling rare or niche products.
The long tail allows small creators to reach customers interested in unique items.
Streaming services benefit from the long tail by offering a wide variety of movies and shows.
Online retailers often profit from the long tail by selling many unique, low-demand products.
The music industry has shifted to embrace the long tail of independent artists and niche genres.
In data analysis, the long tail can reveal important trends among less common items.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - the most common or popular items or trends
  • synonym: niche market - a specialized segment of the market for a particular kind of product
