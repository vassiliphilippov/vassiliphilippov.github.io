rappel

verb
descend a surface using a rope
4.7
Pronunciation: / rəˈpɛl / (AmE), / raˈpel / (BrE)
to descend a vertical surface, such as a cliff or wall, by sliding down a rope while controlling the speed with specialized equipment

Examples

To rappel down
Before you rappel, always check that your harness and rope are secure.
She had never rappelled before but quickly learned the proper technique during the training session.
The climber decided to rappel down the steep rock face rather than take the longer trail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abseil - to descend by means of a rope, especially in climbing (mainly British term)
