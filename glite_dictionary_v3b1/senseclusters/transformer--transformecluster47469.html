transformer

noun
electrical device that changes AC voltage
1.5
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfɔrmɚ /; / trænsˈfɔrmər / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːmə / (BrE)
a device that transfers electrical energy between circuits, usually to change voltage or current

Examples

Power transformer
Many rural areas use a step-up transformer to transmit electricity over long distances.
The electrician installed a new transformer to supply power to the building.
You should never touch a transformer without proper training, as it can be very dangerous.
The technician replaced the damaged transformer to restore electricity to the building.
Large transformers are used in power stations to adjust voltage for transmission.
A small transformer is needed to use this appliance with a different voltage supply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: converter - a device that changes something from one form to another, especially voltage
  • antonym: generator - a machine that produces electricity
