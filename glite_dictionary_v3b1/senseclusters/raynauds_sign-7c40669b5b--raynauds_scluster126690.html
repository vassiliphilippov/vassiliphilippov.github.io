Raynaud's sign

noun
bluish extremities from blood vessel spasm
9.1
Pronunciation: /reɪ.ˈnoʊz saɪn/ /; / /rə.ˈnoʊz saɪn/ (AmE), /reɪ.ˈnəʊz saɪn/ (BrE)
A bluish discoloration (cyanosis) of the fingers or toes due to spasms of blood vessels, typically triggered by cold or emotional stress.

Examples

Raynaud's sign fingers
If you experience Raynaud's sign, it's important to protect your hands from extreme temperatures.
Persistent Raynaud's sign may be an indicator of an underlying vascular condition.
The doctor noted Raynaud's sign in several of the patient's fingers after exposure to cold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Raynaud's phenomenon - condition involving episodic reduction in blood flow to extremities
