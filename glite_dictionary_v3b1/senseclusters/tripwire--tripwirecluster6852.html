tripwire

verb
to set a triggering wire
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪpˌwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪpˌwaɪə / (BrE)
to set up a wire or device so that it will trigger an alarm or response when disturbed.

Examples

To tripwire a door
The security team decided to tripwire the entrance to detect any unauthorized access.
They carefully tripwired the path to alert them if anyone approached the campsite at night.
Before leaving, she made sure to tripwire the storage room for extra protection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: booby-trap - to set a hidden device intended to trigger when disturbed
  • antonym: disarm - to remove or deactivate a triggering device
