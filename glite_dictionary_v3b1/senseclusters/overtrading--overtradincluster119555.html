overtrading

noun
excessive business activity
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of conducting more business or sales than can be supported by available resources, often leading to financial problems

Examples

Company overtrading
The company suffered losses because of overtrading during its first year of rapid growth.
Banks often warn small businesses about the risks of overtrading when expanding too quickly.
Due to overtrading, the retailer could not pay its suppliers on time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prudence - careful management of resources
