end-of-life choice

noun
decision about care at life's end
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnd əv ˈlaɪf tʃɔɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛnd əv ˈlaɪf tʃɔɪs / (BrE)
a decision regarding the care or treatment options for a person who is near the end of life, such as whether to continue life-prolonging medical interventions or to pursue comfort care.

Examples

Discussing end-of-life choices
Discussing end-of-life choices early can help avoid confusion and stress for families.
Doctors must balance medical recommendations with the individual's end-of-life choice.
Many patients want to ensure their end-of-life choice is respected by their healthcare providers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance directive - a legal document stating a person's wishes about medical treatment if they become unable to communicate
  • synonym: euthanasia - the painless killing of a patient suffering from an incurable disease or in an irreversible coma
