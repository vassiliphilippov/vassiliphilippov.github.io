sashay

verb
walk in a showy or confident manner
Pronunciation: / sæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / sæˈʃeɪ / (BrE)
to walk or move in a confident, lively, or gliding way, often to attract attention or as part of a dance

Examples

To sashay down the runway
Confident contestants sashay down the runway during the fashion show.
He sashayed across the stage as if he owned the place.
She sashayed into the room, turning every head with her dramatic entrance.
He sashayed past the waiting crowds with a confident smile.
She sashayed gracefully across the dance floor, catching everyone’s attention.
The model sashayed sideways to avoid the obstacle on the runway.
As part of their routine, the dancers sashayed from one side of the studio to the other.
He learned how to sashay elegantly as his ballet skills improved.
The ballerina sashayed gracefully across the stage during the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chasse - a gliding dance step with feet coming together
  • antonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging one's feet along or without lifting them fully off the ground
  • synonym: strut - to walk in a proud or vain manner
  • synonym: swagger - to walk or behave in a very confident and typically arrogant or aggressive way
