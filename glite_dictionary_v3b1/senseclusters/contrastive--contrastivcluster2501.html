contrastive

adjective
relating to contrastive machine-learning methods
Pronunciation: / kənˈstræstɪv / (AmE), / kənˈstrɑːstɪv / (BrE)
(in machine learning and data science) relating to methods or objectives that train models by comparing or contrasting similar and dissimilar data pairs (e.g., contrastive learning, contrastive loss).

Examples

Contrastive learning
The team trained a model using a contrastive loss to learn robust image representations.
Researchers use contrastive techniques to teach neural networks which pairs of samples are similar.
A contrastive approach improved retrieval by pulling related items together and pushing others apart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrast - to compare or show differences between two or more things
  • synonym: contrastive learning - a machine-learning technique that trains representations by pulling similar samples together and pushing dissimilar ones apart
