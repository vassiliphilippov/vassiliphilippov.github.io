feu

verb
to grant land (Scots law)
9.9
Pronunciation: / fjuː / (AmE), / fjuː / (BrE)
to grant or hold land under a feudal system, especially according to historical Scottish law

Examples

To feu land
The lord agreed to feu several acres to the local farmers for annual rent.
In the 18th century, many Scottish estates were feued to tenants under strict conditions.
The right to feu land was an important part of Scottish property law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lease - to grant the use of land or property for a period in exchange for payment
  • antonym: own - to have legal possession of something
