provisionally

adverb
temporarily; subject to confirmation
2.5 4.6
Pronunciation: / prəˈvɪʒənəli / (AmE), / prəˈvɪʒənəli / (BrE)
in a way that is intended to be temporary or dependent on further confirmation; temporarily and conditionally.

Examples

Accepted provisionally
He was provisionally accepted into the program, pending the results of his background check.
The agreement will be signed provisionally until both parties approve the final terms.
The results are provisionally promising, but more research is needed to confirm them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: definitively - with finality or certainty
  • synonym: temporarily - for a limited time only
