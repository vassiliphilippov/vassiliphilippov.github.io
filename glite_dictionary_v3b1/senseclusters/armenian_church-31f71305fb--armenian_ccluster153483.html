Armenian Church

proper noun
independent Christian church of Armenia
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈmiːniən tʃɜːrtʃ / (AmE), / ɑːˈmiːniən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
An independent Christian church established in Armenia since around 300 AD, influenced by both Roman and Byzantine traditions.

Examples

The Armenian Church tradition
Many Armenians around the world remain members of the Armenian Church, preserving their religious traditions.
The Armenian Church celebrates Christmas on January 6th, unlike many Western Christian denominations.
The ancient cathedral at Etchmiadzin is the spiritual center of the Armenian Church.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Roman Catholic Church - largest Christian church, which influenced the Armenian Church
