settle in

verb
become comfortable in a new place or role
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl ɪn / (BrE)
to begin to feel comfortable and adjust to a new place, job, or situation

Examples

To settle in a new home
After moving to the city, it took her a month to fully settle in.
It will take some time for the new students to settle in at the school.
Once you settle in, everything will start to feel more familiar.
