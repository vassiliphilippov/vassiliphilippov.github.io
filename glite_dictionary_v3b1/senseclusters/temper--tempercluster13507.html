Synonyms

  • conniption
    9.9 0.26/m
    conniption is a sudden, dramatic fit of anger or shock, while temper can mean either a small outburst or a lasting mood.
  • short temper
    4.1 0.12/m
    short temper means a quick tendency to get angry in many small situations, while temper can mean a single outburst or a general mood.
  • bad temper
    3.4 0.34/m
    bad temper is a lasting tendency to be angry or moody, while temper can also mean one short, strong outburst.
  • irascibility
    9.3 0.05/m
    irascibility is a formal word for being easily angered, while temper is more common and can mean quick outbursts or a mood.
  • irritability
    4.2 1.12/m
    irritability is being easily annoyed or bothered in small ways, while temper can be stronger anger or a sudden outburst.
  • rage
    3.4 23/m
    rage is an intense, often uncontrollable burst of anger, while temper can be milder or simply a habit of quick anger.
  • fury
    4.6 7.46/m
    fury is violent, extreme anger that can destroy control, while temper often labels smaller outbursts or a person's general anger level.
  • enragement
    7.9 0.00/m
    enragement is a strong state of being enraged and very angry, while temper also covers small fits or a usual tendency to anger.
