panpsychism

noun
theory of universal consciousness
Pronunciation: / pænˈsaɪkɪzəm / (AmE), / pænˈsʌɪkɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical theory that all matter has some form of consciousness or mental quality

Examples

Panpsychism theory
The philosopher explained panpsychism as the idea that even atoms possess a form of consciousness.
Debates about panpsychism often arise in discussions about the nature of the mind.
Some scientists are skeptical of panpsychism because it challenges traditional views of matter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idealism - the theory that reality is fundamentally mental or spiritual
  • antonym: materialism - the theory that only physical matter exists and consciousness is a result of material interactions
