Sarcostemma acidum

noun
leafless East Indian vine; source of intoxicant
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑɹkoʊˈstɛmə əˈsaɪdəm / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkəʊˈstɛmə əˈsɪdəm / (BrE)
A species of leafless vine native to East India, known for its sour milky juice that was formerly used to make an intoxicating drink.
