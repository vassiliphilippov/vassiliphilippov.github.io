Neptunian

adjective
of Neptunism theory
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / n…õpÀàtuÀêni…ôn / (AmE), / n…õpÀàtjuÀêni…ôn / (BrE)
relating to Neptunism, the historical geological theory that rocks were formed by the action of water, especially ancient oceans.

Examples

Neptunian theory
The Neptunian view held that basalt was deposited from ancient seas.
Many eighteenth-century scientists supported the Neptunian explanation for the formation of sedimentary rocks.
The debate between Neptunian and Plutonian theories shaped early geology.

Related Terms
