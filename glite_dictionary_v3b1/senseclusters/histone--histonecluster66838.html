histone

noun
basic protein in cell nuclei
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɪstəʊn / (BrE)
Any of a group of simple, basic proteins found in cell nuclei, associated with DNA and involved in the packaging of genetic material.

Examples

Histone proteins
Histone proteins help organize DNA into structural units called nucleosomes.
Recent studies show that chemical modification of histone molecules can affect gene expression.
Scientists are exploring how changes in histone structure influence cell function.
