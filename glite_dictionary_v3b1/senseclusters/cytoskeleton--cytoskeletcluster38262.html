cytoskeleton

noun
network providing structure in cells
4.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /saɪ.toʊ.ˈskel.ə.tən/ /; / /saɪ.toʊ.ˈskel.ɪ.tən/ (AmE), /saɪ.təʊ.ˈskel.ə.tən/ /; / /saɪ.təʊ.ˈskel.ɪ.tən/ (BrE)
the network of protein filaments and microtubules in the cytoplasm of many living cells that gives the cell its shape, support, and enables movement

Examples

The cytoskeleton network
Damage to the cytoskeleton can disrupt important cellular functions.
Scientists study the cytoskeleton to understand how cells move and divide.
The cytoskeleton helps maintain the cell's shape and supports cellular movement.
