rerate

verb
to assign new rating
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈreɪt / (BrE)
to assign a new rating, classification, or value to something after a review or reassessment

Examples

To rerate a product
After the inspection, the agency decided to rerate the restaurant based on its improved cleanliness.
The film was rerated from PG-13 to R after additional scenes were reviewed.
Insurance companies may rerate your policy if your circumstances change significantly.
After the property was renovated, the bank decided to rerate its value.
The agency will rerate the company's credit status based on the latest financial reports.
If new information comes to light, the insurer may need to rerate your policy.

  • synonym: reassess - to evaluate again or differently
  • synonym: reclassify - to assign to a different class or category
