sanctifier

noun
one who legitimizes
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋktɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsæŋktɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person or authority that gives formal approval to or legitimizes something

Examples

Sanctifier of rules
The board acted as the sanctifier of the new company policy.
Without a recognized sanctifier, the agreement could not be made official.
The council became the sanctifier of reforms that changed the entire organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approver - a person who gives official consent or acceptance
  • synonym: legitimizer - someone who makes something legitimate or acceptable
  • antonym: opposer - a person who disagrees with or resists something
