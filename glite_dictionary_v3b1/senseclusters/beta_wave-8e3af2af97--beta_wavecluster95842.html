beta wave

noun
a brainwave seen in alert, awake states
5.1 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.tə weɪv/ /; / /ˈbiː.tə weɪv/ (AmE), /ˈbiː.tə weɪv/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.tə weɪv/ (BrE)
a type of brainwave, recorded in an electroencephalogram, typically observed in people who are awake and alert, with a frequency between 12 and 30 hertz

Examples

Beta waves in brain
During meditation, the frequency of beta waves in the brain may decrease as relaxation increases.
Researchers noticed increased beta wave activity during intense mental concentration.
The doctor explained that a beta wave is usually present when the patient is awake and actively thinking.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: alpha wave - a type of brainwave associated with relaxed, wakeful states
  • degree lower: delta wave - a type of brainwave associated with deep sleep
  • degree higher: gamma wave - a type of brainwave with a frequency higher than beta waves, linked to higher cognitive function
  • degree lower: theta wave - a type of brainwave associated with light sleep or drowsiness
