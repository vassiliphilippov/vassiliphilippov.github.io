wave

noun
oscillation in a medium (physics)
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /weɪv/ (AmE), /weɪv/ (BrE)
a moving ridge or swell on water, or a shape or movement that rises and falls in a smooth, curved way; also, a scientific disturbance that travels through a medium (like sound or light)

Examples

A wave on water
A sound wave travels through air by vibrating particles back and forth.
Light travels as an electromagnetic wave across space.
The boat rocked gently with each passing wave of energy in the water.
The artist painted a series of wave patterns along the border.
The fabric fell in soft wave shapes over the table.
The graph displayed a wave that oscillated across the page.
A huge wave swept over the deck, soaking everyone on board.
After the storm, the waves were much larger and more powerful than usual.
The children ran toward the sea, chasing the rolling waves as they crashed on the shore.

  • synonym: vibration - rapid oscillatory motion about an equilibrium point
