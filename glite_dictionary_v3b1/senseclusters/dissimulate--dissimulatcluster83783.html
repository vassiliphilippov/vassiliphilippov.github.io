dissimulate

verb
conceal true feelings or motives
3.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to conceal or disguise one's true feelings or intentions; to hide under a false appearance

Examples

To dissimulate feelings
Despite her disappointment, she managed to dissimulate her true feelings and smiled politely.
He tried to dissimulate his anger during the meeting, but his clenched fists gave him away.
Politicians often dissimulate their real intentions to gain public approval.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disguise - to give a false appearance; to hide the truth
  • antonym: reveal - to make known; to show something that was hidden
