wamble

verb
move with a weaving motion
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːmbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒmb(ə)l / (BrE)
to move unsteadily with a weaving or rolling motion

Examples

To wamble unsteadily
After drinking the strong medicine, he began to wamble across the room.
As the train started, it seemed to wamble along the winding tracks.
The old boat wambled on the rough sea, making everyone uneasy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • synonym: swerve - to turn aside sharply from a straight course or path
