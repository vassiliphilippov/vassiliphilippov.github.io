Ruskinian

noun
follower of Ruskin
Pronunciation: / rʌsˈkɪniən / (AmE), / rʌsˈkɪniən / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the ideas of John Ruskin, especially regarding art, architecture, or social reform

Examples

A Ruskinian thinker
As a dedicated Ruskinian, he believed that art should reflect moral values and social responsibility.
The museum's director, a well-known Ruskinian, emphasized the importance of preserving traditional craftsmanship.
Many Ruskinians advocated for reforms in both art and society during the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aesthetician - a person who studies or is an expert in aesthetics
  • antonym: modernist - a person who supports modern ideas in art or architecture
