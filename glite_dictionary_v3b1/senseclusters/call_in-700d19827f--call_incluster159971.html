call in

verb
summon someone to enter
Pronunciation: / kɔl ɪn / (AmE), / kɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
to ask or summon someone to come in, help, or take part, especially an expert, professional, or replacement

Examples

To call in an expert
After waiting for an hour, my name was finally called in for the interview.
She pressed the button to call in the students one by one.
The doctor asked the nurse to call in the next patient.
After the injury, the trainer immediately called in a replacement to take over.
The coach decided to call in his exhausted forward and bring on a fresh substitute.
With only ten minutes left, she called in her star midfielder to shift the momentum.
After the accident, they called in a team of investigators to examine the scene.
The company decided to call in a consultant to resolve the issue.
We had to call in a plumber when the pipe burst last night.
