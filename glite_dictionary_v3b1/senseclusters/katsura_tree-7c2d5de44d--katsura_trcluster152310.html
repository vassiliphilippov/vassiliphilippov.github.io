katsura tree

noun
ornamental Asian deciduous tree
Pronunciation: / kɑːtˈsuːrə triː / (AmE), / kætsˈjʊərə triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to China and Japan, grown as an ornamental for its blue-green, sweetly scented foliage that turns yellow to scarlet in autumn.

Examples

A katsura tree leaf
A beautiful katsura tree stands at the center of their Japanese garden, its leaves glowing golden in the fall.
Gardeners often choose the katsura tree for its ornamental value and vibrant autumn foliage.
The air was filled with a cotton candy scent as the katsura tree's leaves began to change color.
