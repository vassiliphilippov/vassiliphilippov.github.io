keep

verb
protect; preserve from harm or danger
Pronunciation: / kip / (AmE), / kiːp / (BrE)
to prevent, protect, or restrain someone or something from change, harm, or action

Examples

To keep safe
It is our duty to keep the environment free from pollution.
May God keep you from all harm during your travels.
Please keep the children safe while I am away.
He tried hard to keep his voice from trembling during the speech.
Please keep your dog from jumping on the furniture.
The locked gate keeps children from running into the road.
Security will keep everyone in the building until it is safe.
She kept him in her office for further questioning.
The teacher decided to keep the class inside during the storm.

  • synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening or someone from doing something
  • synonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
  • antonym: release - to allow someone or something to leave a place
