encephalitis lethargica

noun
historic encephalitis epidemic, 1915–1926
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs ləˈθɑrdʒɪkə / (AmE), / ɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtɪs lɪˈθɑːdʒɪkə / (BrE)
A type of encephalitis that occurred in an epidemic between 1915 and 1926, marked by paralysis of the eye muscles and severe muscular weakness.

Examples

Encephalitis lethargica epidemic
Cases of encephalitis lethargica baffled doctors during the early twentieth century.
Oliver Sacks wrote about survivors of encephalitis lethargica in his book 'Awakenings'.
The patients afflicted with encephalitis lethargica experienced profound drowsiness and muscle weakness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encephalitis - inflammation of the brain, usually caused by infection
