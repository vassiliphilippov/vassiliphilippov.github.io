epidural injection

noun
anesthetic injection into the epidural space
6.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /e.pɪ.ˈdɜːr.əl ɪn.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ /; / /e.pə.ˈdjʊr.əl ɪn.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (AmE), /e.pɪ.ˈdjʊə.rəl ɪn.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ /; / /e.pɪ.ˈdjɔː.rəl ɪn.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (BrE)
an injection of an anesthetic into the epidural space around the spinal cord to cause loss of sensation, especially during childbirth or surgery

Examples

An epidural injection during childbirth
After receiving the epidural injection, she felt numbness in her lower body.
Many women choose an epidural injection for pain relief during labor and childbirth.
The doctor recommended an epidural injection to help manage the patient's back pain.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: local anesthesia - loss of sensation in a limited area by anesthetic injection
  • synonym: spinal anesthesia - anesthesia produced by injecting a local anesthetic into the subarachnoid space
