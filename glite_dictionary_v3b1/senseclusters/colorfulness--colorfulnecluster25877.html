colorfulness

noun
vivid or lively quality
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈkʌləfəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being vivid, lively, or interesting, especially in speech, writing, or description

Examples

Colorfulness of her stories
The colorfulness of her storytelling made the characters come alive for the children.
He admired the colorfulness of the festival decorations throughout the city.
Critics praised the colorfulness of the author's language in the new novel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dullness - the quality of being uninteresting or lacking brightness
  • synonym: vividness - the quality of being very clear, bright, or lively
