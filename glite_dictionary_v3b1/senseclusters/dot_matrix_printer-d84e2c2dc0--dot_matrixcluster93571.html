dot matrix printer

noun
printer that prints by forming dots in a matrix
6.0 5.7
Pronunciation: / dɑt ˈmeɪ.trɪks ˈprɪn.tɚ / (AmE), / dɒt ˈmeɪ.trɪks ˈprɪn.tə / (BrE)
A printer that forms characters and images as patterns of tiny dots within a matrix, typically used in older computer systems.

Examples

An old dot matrix printer
A dot matrix printer is known for its ability to print multi-part forms using carbon paper.
Despite being noisy, a dot matrix printer is valued for its reliability in industrial settings.
The old office still uses a dot matrix printer for printing invoices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inkjet printer - printer that sprays ink onto paper to create images and text
  • synonym: laser printer - printer that uses a laser beam to produce an image on a drum
