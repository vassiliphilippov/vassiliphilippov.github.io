imperative

noun
urgent or essential duty or task
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛr.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈper.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
A thing that is extremely important and must be done or dealt with urgently.

Examples

A moral imperative
Addressing poverty remains an imperative for any responsible society.
Climate change action is now an imperative for governments around the world.
It is a moral imperative to help those less fortunate than ourselves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: necessity - something that is needed or unavoidable
  • antonym: optional - not compulsory or required
  • synonym: requirement - something that is demanded or needed
