monotone

adjective
spoken/sounded in a single, unvarying pitch
5.8 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ëÀê.n…ôÀåto än / (AmE), / Ààm…ín.…ô.t…ô än / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) sounded or spoken in a single, unvarying tone of pitch.

Examples

Monotone voice
His speech was delivered in a monotone voice that made it hard to stay attentive.
She recited the poem in a flat monotone, without emotion.
The teacher's monotone way of speaking bored the students.

Synonyms

  • monotonic
    8.1 0.01/m
    monotonic describes speech with a steady, unchanging pitch in technical or formal talk, while monotone names that single tone.
  • monotonal
    7.3 0.02/m
    monotonal means using only one musical or speech pitch, while monotone refers to a single, unvarying spoken tone.
  • inflectionless
    7.9 0.01/m
    inflectionless is speech given without any pitch change or emphasis, while monotone means a voice that stays on one tone.
  • toneless
    6.3 0.16/m
    toneless is a flat, emotionless voice lacking pitch or feeling, while monotone is specifically a single unvarying pitch.
  • monotonous
    5.3 0.49/m
    monotonous is dull and repeating in sound or activity, while monotone describes a voice that uses one unchanging pitch.
  • flat
    4.4 17/m
    flat sounds dull or without life in speech or music, while monotone means a voice that stays on one pitch.
