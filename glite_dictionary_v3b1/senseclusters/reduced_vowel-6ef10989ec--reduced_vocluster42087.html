reduced vowel

noun
a central, unstressed vowel sound
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdjuːst ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːst ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
a vowel sound that is produced with less force and is more central in the mouth, typically occurring in unstressed syllables

Examples

A reduced vowel sound
Many English unstressed syllables are pronounced with a reduced vowel.
The pronunciation of 'about' starts with a reduced vowel.
The word 'sofa' contains a reduced vowel in its second syllable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: schwa - the most common reduced vowel sound, written as /ə/ in phonetics
