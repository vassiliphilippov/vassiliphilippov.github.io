objecthood

noun
state of being an object
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑbdʒɛkthʊd / (AmE), / ˈɒbdʒɪkthʊd / (BrE)
the state or quality of being an object, especially as distinct from being a subject or person

Examples

Philosophical objecthood
The philosopher discussed the objecthood of physical things compared to human consciousness.
In her essay, she questioned the objecthood of art and its role in society.
The museum exhibit focused on the objecthood of everyday items, highlighting their form and function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: objectivity - the quality of being objective or unbiased
  • antonym: subjectivity - the quality of being a subject or having personal perspective
