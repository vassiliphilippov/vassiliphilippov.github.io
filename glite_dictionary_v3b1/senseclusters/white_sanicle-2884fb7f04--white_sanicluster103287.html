white sanicle

noun
North American herb with small white flower heads
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈsænɪkəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈsænɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
A North American herb with flat-topped clusters of small white flower heads, sometimes associated with milk sickness and trembles.

Examples

White sanicle plant
Local farmers used to blame white sanicle for milk sickness in their cattle.
She identified the flat-topped flower clusters as belonging to white sanicle.
The woods in early spring are often dotted with white sanicle blooming among the ferns.
