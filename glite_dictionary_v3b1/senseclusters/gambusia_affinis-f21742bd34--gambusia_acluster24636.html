Gambusia affinis

noun
a fish species used in mosquito control
Pronunciation: / ɡæmˈbuːziə əˈfɪnɪs / (AmE), / ɡæmˈbjuːziə əˈfɪnɪs / (BrE)
a small, silvery freshwater fish native to North America and the West Indies, known for feeding on mosquito larvae and used in mosquito control

Examples

The Gambusia affinis fish
Gambusia affinis play a crucial role in controlling mosquito populations in wetlands.
Biologists introduced Gambusia affinis to several ponds to reduce disease-carrying insects.
The presence of Gambusia affinis can help maintain a balanced aquatic ecosystem.
