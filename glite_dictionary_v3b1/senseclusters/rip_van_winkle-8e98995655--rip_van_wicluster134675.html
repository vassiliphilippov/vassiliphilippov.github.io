Rip van Winkle

proper noun
title character who sleeps for 20 years
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪp væn ˈwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˌrɪp van ˈwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
the title character in a story by Washington Irving who sleeps for twenty years and wakes to a changed world; often used to describe someone oblivious to change over time
