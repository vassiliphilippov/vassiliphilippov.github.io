heat

verb
make hot or hotter
1.0 23/m
Pronunciation: / hit / (AmE), / hiÀêt / (BrE)
to make something hot or warmer; to increase the temperature of something

Examples

To heat the soup
Please heat the soup before dinner.
Some people prefer to heat their homes with wood stoves.
The engineer heated the metal until it glowed red.
Could you heat the oven to 200 degrees Celsius, please?
They heated the house using a wood stove last winter.
We need to heat the soup before serving it.
If you heat the oil too much, it will start to smoke.
The oven needs to heat before we can bake the bread.
The sun will heat the water in the pool by midday.

Synonyms

  • warm
    1.0 25/m
    warm means making something slightly or gently higher in temperature, while heat implies a stronger or more deliberate rise in temperature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool - to make cooler or less hot
  • degree higher: overheat - to make or become excessively hot
  • synonym: warm - to make or become warm
