straightedge

noun
tool for drawing or testing straight lines
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtˌɛdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtˌɛdʒ / (BrE)
a tool consisting of a flat, straight bar, typically made of metal or wood, used for drawing straight lines or checking their straightness

Examples

A straightedge tool
He used a straightedge to ensure the lines were perfectly aligned on the blueprint.
Students are often asked to bring a ruler or a straightedge to geometry class.
The carpenter checked the flatness of the surface with a straightedge.
