shaft

noun
narrow beam or column of light
Pronunciation: / ʃæft / (AmE), / ʃɑːft / (BrE)
a narrow beam or column of light, especially one that appears to fall or stream in a straight line

Examples

Shaft of light
A shaft of sunlight broke through the clouds and lit the field.
Dust danced in the shaft of light coming through the attic window.
The lighthouse sent a bright shaft of light into the night sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beam - a ray or shaft of light
