resistance

noun
mechanical force opposing motion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
a mechanical force that tends to slow down or oppose the motion of an object

Examples

Air resistance
Engineers must account for resistance when designing vehicles to increase fuel efficiency.
The car slows down due to air resistance as it speeds up on the highway.
Water resistance makes it harder to swim quickly in a pool.

Related Terms

  • synonym: friction - the resistance met by an object moving through another substance or along a surface
