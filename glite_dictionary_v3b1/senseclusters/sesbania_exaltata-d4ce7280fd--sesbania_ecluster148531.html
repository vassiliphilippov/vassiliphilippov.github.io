Sesbania exaltata

noun
species of annual plant used as green manure
Pronunciation: / sɛzˈbeɪni ə ɛɡˈzɔltətə / (AmE), / sɛzˈbeɪni ə ɛɡˈzɔːltətə / (BrE)
A tall annual plant native to the southwestern United States, widely grown as green manure and formerly used for its strong bast fiber in cordage by Indigenous peoples.

Examples

Tall Sesbania exaltata plant
Farmers often plant Sesbania exaltata to improve soil fertility before the growing season.
In areas with poor soil, Sesbania exaltata helps restore nutrients through its rapid growth.
The strong fibers of Sesbania exaltata were traditionally used by Indigenous people to make rope and nets.
