carrier wave

noun
radio wave used to transmit signals
3.0 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.rɪ.ər weɪv/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.rɪ.ə weɪv/ (BrE)
A radio wave used to carry information by modulating its amplitude, frequency, or phase in radio transmission.

Examples

A carrier wave signal
A carrier wave is necessary for both AM and FM radio transmission.
By modulating the carrier wave, information such as voice or data can be transmitted over long distances.
The radio receiver detects changes in the carrier wave to reconstruct the original broadcast audio.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: modulated wave - a wave whose characteristics have been modified to carry information
  • degree higher: signal wave - a wave that represents information to be transmitted
