Aristotelian

noun
follower of Aristotle or his philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˌærəstəˈtɪliən / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəˈtɪliən / (BrE)
A follower of Aristotle or a person who adheres to the philosophy and methods of Aristotle.

Examples

An Aristotelian thinker
In philosophical debates, the Aristotelians often opposed the Platonists.
Many scholars in the Middle Ages were considered Aristotelians for their devotion to Aristotle’s works.
The Aristotelian argued that reality is best understood through empirical observation and logic.
