outrange

verb
have a greater range than something else
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / aʊtˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to have a greater range than something else, especially another weapon or piece of equipment

Examples

Ships outranged enemies
During the naval battle, their ships outranged ours, forcing us to retreat.
Modern missiles often outrange traditional anti-aircraft defenses.
The new artillery can outrange enemy guns with ease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outdistance - to go farther than someone or something else
