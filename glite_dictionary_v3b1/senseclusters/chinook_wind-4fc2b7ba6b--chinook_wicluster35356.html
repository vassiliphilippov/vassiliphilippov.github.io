Chinook wind

noun
warm, dry wind from the Rockies
Pronunciation: / ʃɪˈnʊk wɪnd / (AmE), / ʃɪˈnʊk wɪnd / (BrE)
A warm, dry wind that blows down the eastern slopes of the Rocky Mountains, often causing rapid increases in temperature.

Examples

The Chinook wind
Farmers welcome the Chinook wind for its ability to warm the land in early spring.
Temperatures can rise dramatically within hours when a Chinook wind sweeps through the region.
The sudden arrival of a Chinook wind melted most of the snow overnight.
