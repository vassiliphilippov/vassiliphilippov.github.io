PAC

noun
US political fundraising committee
Pronunciation: / piː eɪ siː / (AmE), / piː eɪ siː / (BrE)
(in the US) a committee formed by a special-interest group to raise money and support political candidates or causes.

Examples

A PAC committee
Candidates often rely on PAC contributions to fund their political advertisements.
Several PACs spent millions during the election campaign to influence voters.
The senator received a large donation from a PAC supporting education reform.
