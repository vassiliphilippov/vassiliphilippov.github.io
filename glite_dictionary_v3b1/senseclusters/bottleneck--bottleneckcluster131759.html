bottleneck

verb
to become narrow or constricted
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑt.l̩ˌnɛk /; / ˈbɑːt̬lˌnɛk / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.l̩.nɛk / (BrE)
to become narrow or blocked, or to slow down progress by causing a restriction

Examples

Production bottlenecks
As production increases, the assembly line may bottleneck near the packaging station.
At one point, the hiking trail bottlenecks between two cliffs, forcing everyone into single file.
Traffic tends to bottleneck here during rush hour, causing long delays for commuters.
Construction on the main road has been bottlenecking commuters for weeks.
Heavy traffic can bottleneck the delivery of goods to the city center.
The outdated software bottlenecked our ability to process customer orders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier
  • synonym: impede - to slow the progress of something
  • synonym: narrow - become or make less wide
  • synonym: obstruct - to block or get in the way of something
