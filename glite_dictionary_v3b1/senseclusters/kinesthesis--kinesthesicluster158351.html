kinesthesis

noun
awareness of body movement and position
Pronunciation: / ˌkɪnəsˈθiːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɪsˈθiːsɪs / (BrE)
the ability to sense the position and movement of one's body parts

Examples

Kinesthesis sense
Children develop kinesthesis as they learn new physical skills such as walking and catching.
Good kinesthesis allows dancers to move gracefully and stay aware of their body posture.
Gymnasts rely heavily on kinesthesis to perform complex routines with precision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proprioception - the sense of the position and movement of the body
