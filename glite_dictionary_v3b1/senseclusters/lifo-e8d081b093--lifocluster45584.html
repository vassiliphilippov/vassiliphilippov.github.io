LIFO

noun
inventory accounting method: last in, first out
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪˌfoʊ /; / ˈlaɪfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪfəʊ / (BrE)
a method where the last item added is the first to be removed or used, especially in accounting (inventory valuation) and computing (data structures)

Examples

LIFO inventory method
Many companies switched to LIFO to reduce their taxable income during periods of inflation.
Our accountant explained the differences between LIFO and FIFO during the meeting.
The use of the LIFO method can affect reported profits and inventory values.
In computer science, a LIFO structure is often called a stack.
The warehouse used a LIFO system to manage its inventory.
Accountants sometimes apply LIFO to calculate the cost of goods sold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stack - a data structure that uses the LIFO principle
