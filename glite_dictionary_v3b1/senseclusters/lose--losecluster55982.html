lose

verb
fail to make money; not profit in business
Pronunciation: / luːz / (AmE), / luːz / (BrE)
to experience a reduction in quality, value, effectiveness, or money

Examples

To lose money
He warned that the restaurant might lose much of its capital if business does not improve.
Many investors fear they will lose their savings in a volatile market.
The company continues to lose money each quarter despite new strategies.
He felt the argument lost its strength when more evidence became available.
The show loses much of its impact when you watch it online instead of live.
This wine loses its flavor quickly after opening the bottle.
