eigenstate

noun
quantum system state
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.ɡən.steɪt/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.ɡən.steɪt/ (BrE)
a possible state of a quantum system in which a measurable property has a definite value

Examples

A eigenstate of energy
An electron in a hydrogen atom can occupy a specific eigenstate with a certain energy.
When measured, the system collapses into one eigenstate corresponding to the observed value.
Physicists use the concept of an eigenstate to predict the outcomes of quantum experiments.
In quantum mechanics, an eigenstate corresponds to a specific value of an observable property.
The measurement forced the particle into a single eigenstate of energy.
Physicists often discuss how a system transitions between different eigenstates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quantum state - the state of a system in quantum mechanics
  • antonym: superposition - a combination of different quantum states
