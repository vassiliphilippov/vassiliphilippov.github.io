nonbiodegradable

adjective
not breaking down naturally
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌbaɪoʊdaɪˈɡreɪdəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌbaɪəʊdaɪˈɡreɪdəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be broken down naturally by bacteria or other living organisms

Examples

Nonbiodegradable plastic waste
Many types of plastic packaging are nonbiodegradable and remain in landfills for decades.
The city is working to reduce the use of nonbiodegradable materials in everyday products.
Throwing nonbiodegradable waste into the ocean can seriously harm marine life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biodegradable - able to be broken down naturally by living organisms
