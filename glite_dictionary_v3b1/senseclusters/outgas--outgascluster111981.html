outgas

verb
to release trapped gas
7.9
Pronunciation: /aʊt.ˈgæs/ /; / /ˈaʊt.gæs/ (AmE), /aʊt.ˈgæs/ /; / /ˈaʊt.gæs/ (BrE)
to release gas from a solid or liquid material, often due to heat, vacuum, or chemical change

Examples

To outgas chemicals
Newly manufactured plastics may outgas chemicals into the air for several days.
The spacecraft was left in a vacuum chamber to outgas before launch.
If you notice a strange smell, the materials might still be outgassing.
The new carpet may outgas chemicals for several days after installation.
Spacecraft materials are carefully chosen so they do not outgas in a vacuum.
When heated, some plastics outgas substances that can affect sensitive equipment.
Some plastics outgas harmful chemicals when they are heated in the sun.
Engineers must ensure that materials do not outgas in the vacuum of space.
The paint will outgas for several days after application, so keep the room ventilated.
