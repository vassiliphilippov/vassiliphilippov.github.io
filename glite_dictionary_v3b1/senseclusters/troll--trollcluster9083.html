troll

noun
fishing lure used in trolling
3.6
Pronunciation: / troʊl / (AmE), / trəʊl / (BrE)
a fishing lure or the method of fishing by dragging a line or lure behind a moving boat

Examples

A fishing troll
He prefers using a brightly colored troll when fishing for trout in deep water.
Several different trolls can be used to increase the chances of catching large fish.
The fisherman attached a new troll to his line before casting it into the lake.
During our fishing trip, we decided to troll along the river’s edge to attract more fish.
He prefers to troll for bass in the early morning when the lake is calm.
Some anglers use electronic motors to troll slowly and maintain the perfect lure speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bait - food or lure used to attract fish or animals
  • synonym: lure - an artificial fishing bait
  • synonym: trawl - to fish with a cone-shaped net dragged through the water
