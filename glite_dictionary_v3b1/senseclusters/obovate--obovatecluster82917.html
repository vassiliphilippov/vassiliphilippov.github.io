obovate

adjective
leaf shape: egg-shaped with narrow base
Pronunciation: / oʊˈboʊveɪt / (AmE), / ɒˈbəʊveɪt / (BrE)
(of a leaf) egg-shaped with the narrower end at the base.

Examples

Obovate leaf shape
Many tropical plants have obovate or elliptical foliage.
The obovate leaves of the tree make it easy to distinguish from similar species.
The botanist noted the obovate shape of the specimen's leaves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ovate - egg-shaped, with the broader end at the base (opposed to obovate)
