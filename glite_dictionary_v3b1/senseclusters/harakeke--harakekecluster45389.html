harakeke

noun
New Zealand flax fiber
Pronunciation: / hɑːrəˈkɛki / (AmE), / hɑːrəˈkɛki / (BrE)
the strong fiber obtained from the New Zealand flax plant, used for weaving, rope, and traditional crafts

Examples

Harakeke fiber
Traditional Māori baskets are often woven from harakeke fibers collected by hand.
She learned how to prepare harakeke for weaving mats and decorative items.
The rope made from harakeke was strong enough to secure the canoe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flax - a plant whose fibers are used to make linen and rope
