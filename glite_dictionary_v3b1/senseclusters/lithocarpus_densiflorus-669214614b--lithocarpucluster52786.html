Lithocarpus densiflorus

noun
Pacific coast evergreen tree (tanoak) yielding tanbark
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɪθəˈkɑːrpəs dɛnˈsɪflərəs / (AmE), / lɪθəˈkɑːpəs dɛnˈsɪflərəs / (BrE)
an evergreen tree of the Pacific coast of North America, with large leathery leaves, that yields tanbark; also known as tanoak

Examples

The Lithocarpus densiflorus tree
A grove of Lithocarpus densiflorus grows near the northern California coastline, thriving in the moist environment.
Tanoak, or Lithocarpus densiflorus, is an important source of tannins used in leather processing.
The leaves of the Lithocarpus densiflorus are thick and leathery, helping the tree conserve water in dry climates.
