osteodystrophy

noun
abnormal bone development (often due to kidney disease)
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɑs.ti.oʊˈdɪs.trə.fi / (AmE), / ˌɒs.ti.əʊˈdɪs.trə.fi / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by defective bone development, usually caused by kidney disease or disturbances in calcium and phosphorus metabolism.

Examples

Osteodystrophy bone disease
A deficiency in calcium and phosphorus can contribute to the development of osteodystrophy.
Recent studies focus on treatments to prevent osteodystrophy in individuals with renal failure.
The patient was diagnosed with osteodystrophy after years of chronic kidney disease.
