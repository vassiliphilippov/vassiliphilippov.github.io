reroof

verb
to replace roof
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈruːf / (AmE), / ˌriːˈruːf / (BrE)
to put a new roof on a building, replacing the old or damaged one

Examples

To reroof a house
After the hailstorm, the owners decided to reroof their house to prevent leaks.
The company will reroof the school building during the summer break.
It took the workers three days to completely reroof the warehouse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demolish - to completely destroy a building or structure
  • synonym: repair - to fix or restore something that is damaged
