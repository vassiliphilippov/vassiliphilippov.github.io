hollow

verb
remove the inner part or core
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊ / (BrE)
to remove the inside part or core of something, leaving an empty space

Examples

To hollow a log
Over decades, underground streams can hollow out large caverns in limestone rock.
The chef began to hollow the pumpkin for the soup.
They used special tools to hollow a log and turn it into a canoe.
If you hollow a pumpkin, you can use it as a lantern.
The sculptor decided to hollow the log before starting the carving.
They used chisels to hollow the inside of the stone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: core - to remove the central part, especially of fruit
  • synonym: excavate - to dig out material from the ground, especially to form a cavity or hole
  • antonym: fill - to make something full, opposite of making hollow
