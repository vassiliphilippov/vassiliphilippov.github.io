hollow

verb
remove the inner part or core
4.8 1.77/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ëÀêlo ä / (AmE), / Ààh…íl…ô ä / (BrE)
to remove the inside part or core of something, leaving an empty space

Examples

To hollow a log
Over decades, underground streams can hollow out large caverns in limestone rock.
The chef began to hollow the pumpkin for the soup.
They used special tools to hollow a log and turn it into a canoe.
If you hollow a pumpkin, you can use it as a lantern.
The sculptor decided to hollow the log before starting the carving.
They used chisels to hollow the inside of the stone.

Synonyms

  • hollow out
    4.0 0.52/m
    hollow out is a phrasal verb used in everyday instructions to remove an object's inside, while hollow is the general verb form.
  • scoop out
    3.3 0.73/m
    scoop out uses a scoop or tool to remove inner contents and make a hollow space, while hollow means removing the inside more generally.
  • empty
    1.0 12/m
    empty removes all contents so a container becomes bare, while hollow focuses on taking out the inner core to leave a cavity.
  • recess
    7.4 1.10/m
    recess makes a small set-back or indentation in a surface, while hollow means removing the inside to create a larger empty space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: core - to remove the central part, especially of fruit
  • synonym: excavate - to dig out material from the ground, especially to form a cavity or hole
  • antonym: fill - to make something full, opposite of making hollow
