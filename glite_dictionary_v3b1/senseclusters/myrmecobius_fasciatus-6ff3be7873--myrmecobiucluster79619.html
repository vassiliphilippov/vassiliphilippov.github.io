Myrmecobius fasciatus

noun
Australian termite-eating marsupial, numbat
Pronunciation: / mɜrˌmɛkoʊˈbaɪəs fæsˈiːeɪtəs / (AmE), / mɜːˌmiːkəˈbaɪəs fæsˈiːeɪtəs / (BrE)
a small Australian marsupial with a long snout and strong claws, feeding mainly on termites; also known as the numbat

Examples

The Myrmecobius fasciatus animal
Researchers are working to protect the habitat of the endangered Myrmecobius fasciatus in Western Australia.
The Myrmecobius fasciatus is recognized for its distinctive stripes and long, slender tongue.
Unlike other marsupials, the Myrmecobius fasciatus is mainly active during the day as it searches for termites.
