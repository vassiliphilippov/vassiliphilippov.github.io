upstage

verb
move to the rear of the stage (theatre)
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌpsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
(theatre) To move towards the rear of the stage, causing other actors to turn away from the audience.

Examples

To upstage other actors
During rehearsal, the director asked Mark to upstage so the others would face the audience.
If you upstage your fellow actors, it can disrupt the entire scene's flow.
She subtly upstaged him, causing him to turn his back to the audience.
