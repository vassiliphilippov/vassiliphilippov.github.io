divorce rape

noun
offensive term for perceived exploitation in divorce
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɔrs reɪp / (AmE), / dɪˈvɔːs reɪp / (BrE)
an offensive term describing perceived exploitation, coercion, or manipulation of one party (often men) during divorce proceedings, especially in asset division or custodial decisions

Examples

Divorce rape claims
Advocacy groups have criticized the use of the term divorce rape as being deeply inappropriate and offensive.
Online forums sometimes discuss divorce rape in the context of unfair legal outcomes.
Some men fear divorce rape when facing child custody battles and asset division.
