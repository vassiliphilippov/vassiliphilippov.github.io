wheelhouse

noun
enclosed compartment for navigating a ship
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈwiːlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
an enclosed compartment on a ship from which it is steered and navigated

Examples

Ship's wheelhouse
The captain remained in the wheelhouse during the storm to guide the ship safely to shore.
Thick fog made it difficult for those in the wheelhouse to see more than a few meters ahead.
Visitors were allowed to tour the wheelhouse and see the old wooden steering wheel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bridge - the elevated platform or structure on a ship from which it is commanded
