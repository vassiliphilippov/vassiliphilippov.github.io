dendron

noun
nerve cell branch
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛnˌdrɑn / (AmE), / ˈdɛndrɒn / (BrE)
a branching extension of a nerve cell that receives signals from other cells

Examples

Neuron dendron
The dendron receives electrical signals from neighboring neurons.
Damage to a neuron's dendron can affect how it communicates with other cells.
Scientists observed the growth of a new dendron in the developing brain tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: axon - the long extension of a nerve cell that transmits signals away from the cell body
  • synonym: dendrite - a branched extension of a nerve cell that receives signals
