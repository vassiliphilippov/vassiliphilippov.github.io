expurgated

adjective
with objectionable material removed
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.spɚˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛk.spəˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a text or edition) having objectionable or sensitive material removed, especially before publication.

Examples

Expurgated edition
Scholars criticized the expurgated Shakespeare editions for omitting significant scenes and dialogue.
Some films are shown in an expurgated form on public television to comply with broadcasting standards.
The publisher released an expurgated version of the controversial novel for younger readers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unabridged - not shortened or condensed; complete
  • antonym: uncensored - not having any material removed or altered
