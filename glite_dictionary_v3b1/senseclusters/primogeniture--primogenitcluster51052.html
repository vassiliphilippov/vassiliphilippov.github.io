primogeniture

noun
law granting inheritance to eldest son
6.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪmoʊˈdʒɛnɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˌprɪməʊˈdʒɛnɪtʃə / (BrE)
the custom or law by which the right of inheritance belongs exclusively to the eldest son

Examples

Law of primogeniture
Debates around primogeniture influenced social and economic structures in many countries.
Some European monarchies were governed by the laws of primogeniture.
The system of primogeniture ensured the family estate passed to the eldest son.
