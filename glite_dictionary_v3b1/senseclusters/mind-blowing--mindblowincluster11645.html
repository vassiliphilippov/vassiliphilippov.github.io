mind-blowing

adjective
extremely impressive or astonishing
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪndˌbloʊɪŋ /; / ˈmaɪnd ˌbloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndˌbləʊɪŋ /; / ˈmaɪnd ˌbləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely impressive, surprising, or shocking; powerfully affecting the mind or emotions

Examples

A mind-blowing experience
Her performance was so mind-blowing that the audience gave a standing ovation.
It was a mind-blowing discovery that changed the course of science.
The documentary offered a mind-blowing look into the universe.
Her performance in the concert was so mind-blowing that it left the audience speechless.
The special effects in that movie were absolutely mind-blowing.
The view from the top of the mountain was mind-blowing and unforgettable.
