white-footed mouse

noun
North American mouse with white feet
Pronunciation: / ˌwaɪt ˈfʊtɪd maʊs / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˈfʊtɪd maʊs / (BrE)
a small North American woodland rodent with white feet and underparts, scientifically known as Peromyscus leucopus

Examples

A white-footed mouse
A white-footed mouse darted under the leaves as we walked through the forest.
Researchers studied the white-footed mouse to understand its role in spreading ticks.
The white-footed mouse is commonly found in wooded areas across eastern North America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deer mouse - a closely related rodent species, Peromyscus maniculatus, found in North America
