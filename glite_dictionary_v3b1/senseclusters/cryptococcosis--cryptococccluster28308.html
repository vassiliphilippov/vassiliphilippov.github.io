cryptococcosis

noun
fungal infection affecting lungs and nervous system
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪp.toʊ.kɑːˈkoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪp.tə.kɒˈkəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A fungal infection primarily affecting the lungs, that can spread to the nervous system, caused by fungi of the genus Cryptococcus.

Examples

Cryptococcosis lung infection
Effective treatment of cryptococcosis typically involves long-term antifungal medication.
People with weakened immune systems are more susceptible to developing cryptococcosis.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with cryptococcosis after identifying fungal nodules in his lungs.
