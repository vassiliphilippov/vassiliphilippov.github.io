Daedalus

proper noun
mythical Greek inventor and craftsman
Pronunciation: / ˈdedələs, ˈdiː- / (AmE), / ˈdiːdələs, ˈdɛd- / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) an ingenious Athenian craftsman who built the labyrinth for King Minos of Crete and escaped with his son Icarus by making wings of feathers and wax.

Examples

Daedalus built wings
Daedalus constructed the intricate labyrinth to imprison the Minotaur.
Many artists have depicted Daedalus fleeing Crete with wings of his own invention.
The myth of Daedalus and Icarus warns about the dangers of hubris.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Icarus - son of Daedalus in Greek mythology, who flew too close to the sun
