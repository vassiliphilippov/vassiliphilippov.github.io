black bean

noun
a type of small, black edible bean
Pronunciation: / blæk biːn / (AmE), / blak biːn / (BrE)
A small, shiny, black-seeded bean native to South America, commonly used in cooking and usually sold dried.

Examples

Cooked black beans
After soaking the black beans overnight, they cooked them in a spicy sauce.
She likes to make salads with black beans, corn, and fresh tomatoes.
The chili recipe called for a can of black beans to add flavor and texture.
