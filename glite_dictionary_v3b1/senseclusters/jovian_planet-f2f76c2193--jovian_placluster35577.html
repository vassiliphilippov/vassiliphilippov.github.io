Jovian planet

noun
large, gaseous outer planet (e.g. Jupiter, Saturn)
4.8 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒoʊ.vi.ən ˈplæn.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒəʊ.vi.ən ˈplæn.ɪt / (BrE)
Any of the four outermost planets in the solar system—Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune—which are much larger than Earth and primarily composed of gases.

Examples

The Jovian planet Jupiter
Jupiter is the largest Jovian planet in our solar system.
Scientists believe that each Jovian planet has a system of rings and many moons.
Unlike terrestrial planets, a Jovian planet has a thick atmosphere of hydrogen and helium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gas giant - a large planet composed mostly of gases
