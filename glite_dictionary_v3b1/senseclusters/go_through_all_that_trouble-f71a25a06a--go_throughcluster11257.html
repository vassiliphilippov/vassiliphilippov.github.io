go through all that trouble

verb
make significant effort, often unnecessarily
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ θru ɔl ðæt ˈtrʌbəl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ θruː ɔːl ðæt ˈtrʌbəl / (BrE)
to make a great effort or face difficulties in order to do something, especially when it may not have been necessary

Examples

To go through all that trouble
I can't believe you went through all that trouble just to bake me a cake.
She didn't need to go through all that trouble for a simple favor.
Why did you go through all that trouble if you weren't sure I'd be home?
