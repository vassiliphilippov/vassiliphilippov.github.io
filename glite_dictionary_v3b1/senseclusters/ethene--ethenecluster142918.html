ethene

noun
a flammable colorless gaseous hydrocarbon (C2H4)
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθiːn / (AmE), / ˈiːθiːn / (BrE)
A flammable, colorless gaseous hydrocarbon (C2H4) obtained from petroleum and natural gas, used in manufacturing plastics and other chemicals; also known as ethylene.

Examples

Ethene gas
As a plant hormone, ethene influences fruit ripening and flower opening.
Industrial production of plastics relies heavily on ethene as a raw material.
Scientists separated pure ethene gas during the laboratory experiment.
