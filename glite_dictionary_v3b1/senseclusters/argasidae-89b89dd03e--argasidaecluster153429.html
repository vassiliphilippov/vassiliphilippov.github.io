Argasidae

noun
family of soft-bodied ticks
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈɡæsɪdi /; / ˌɑːrɡəˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ɑːˈɡæsɪdiː /; / ˌɑːɡəˈsiːdiː / (BrE)
a family of soft-bodied ticks without a hard dorsal shield, many of which are parasites of birds and mammals

Examples

Argasidae soft ticks
Scientists studied the feeding habits of Argasidae to better understand their role as disease vectors.
The Argasidae include several species that transmit diseases to livestock.
Unlike hard ticks, members of the Argasidae have soft, leathery bodies and lack a scutum.
Several species within the Argasidae family are known to transmit diseases to livestock and humans.
The Argasidae are noteworthy for their lack of a hard dorsal shield, distinguishing them from other tick families.
Unlike hard ticks, members of the Argasidae often inhabit bird nests and bat roosts.
