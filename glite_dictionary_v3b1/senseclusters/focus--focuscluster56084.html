focus

verb
become clear or come into focus
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəs /; / ˈfoʊ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəs /; / ˈfəʊ.kəs / (BrE)
to make an image or light clear and sharp, or to bring rays or things together at a point (such as with a lens or optical device)

Examples

To focus a camera
As the fog lifted, the distant mountains gradually focused in the morning light.
It took a few seconds for the camera lens to focus after adjusting the settings.
The image on the screen focused suddenly, revealing the details of the painting.
The scientist used a lens to focus the sunlight onto a single spot.
Try to focus your attention on the main objective during the meeting.
You need to focus the camera lens before taking a photo.
Could you focus the camera before taking the picture?
She quickly focused the binoculars to see the birds clearly.
The technician had to focus the microscope to reveal the tiny details.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blur - to become less clear or less distinct
  • synonym: concentrate - to bring something to a single point or focus; to direct attention or effort
