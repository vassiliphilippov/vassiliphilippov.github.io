remit

verb
to slacken or reduce tension
9.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈmɪt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈmɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈmɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈmɪt/ (BrE)
to become less severe, to diminish, or to make less tense or firm

Examples

The wind remits
After the storm, the wind began to remit, leaving the sails slack.
If you remit the tension on the rope, it will no longer hold the weight.
The doctor advised him to remit his grip on the handle to avoid strain.
Her symptoms began to remit after she started the new treatment.
If the pain does not remit within a week, consult your doctor.
The fever may remit for a few days before returning.
