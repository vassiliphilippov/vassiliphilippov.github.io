renvoi

noun
legal conflict rule
Pronunciation: / rɑ̃ˈvwɑ / (AmE), / rɑ̃ˈvwɑ / (BrE)
a legal doctrine in which a court refers to the laws of a foreign country, including its conflict of law rules, to resolve a case

Examples

Doctrine of renvoi
The court applied the doctrine of renvoi to determine which country's law should govern the dispute.
In international family law cases, renvoi can complicate the choice of applicable legal rules.
Law students often find the concept of renvoi challenging when studying private international law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conflict of laws - a set of rules determining which jurisdiction's law applies in legal disputes
