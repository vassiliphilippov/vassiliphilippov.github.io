ply

verb
work steadily at a trade or activity
7.2 5.5
Pronunciation: / plaɪ / (AmE), / plaɪ / (BrE)
to work at or pursue an activity, trade, or skill steadily; to use a tool or instrument repeatedly or diligently

Examples

To ply a trade
After retiring from the navy, he began to ply his trade as a carpenter.
Despite the harsh conditions, the fishermen plied their trade throughout the winter.
She continued to ply her skills in the bustling market every day.
She plied her knitting needles with great enthusiasm every evening.
The blacksmith plied his hammer against the glowing metal.
With impressive strength, he plied the oars through the choppy water.
She plies her trade as a street artist in the city center.
The lawyer plied his arguments skillfully in court.
You must ply your wits to solve this puzzle.
