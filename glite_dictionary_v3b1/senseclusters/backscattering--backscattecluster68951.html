backscattering

noun
reflection of waves
Pronunciation: / ˈbækskætərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækskætərɪŋ / (BrE)
the reflection of waves, particles, or signals back toward the direction from which they originated

Examples

Backscattering of waves
Scientists study backscattering to understand how radar waves interact with rain and snow.
The amount of backscattering can reveal details about the surface of distant planets.
In fiber optics, backscattering is used to detect faults in the cable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflection - the return of light, heat, or sound after striking a surface
  • synonym: scattering - the process by which particles or waves are deflected in many directions
