scene

noun
place or environment where something happens
1.5 47/m
Pronunciation: / sin /; / siÀên / (AmE), / siÀên / (BrE)
the place or setting where something happens, especially an important event

Examples

Crime scene
He quickly became a prominent figure in the jazz scene of the 1960s.
The Paris fashion scene attracts designers from all over the world.
The movie perfectly captures the atmosphere of the Los Angeles art scene.
Emergency crews worked tirelessly at the scene to help the injured.
Reporters arrived quickly at the scene of the protest to cover the story.
The detectives carefully examined the scene for evidence after the accident.

Synonyms

  • setting
    3.2 65/m
    setting shows the time and surroundings for a story or event, while scene points to the specific place where action happens.
  • locale
    4.8 4.63/m
    locale often names the physical site of an event in writing or reports, while scene suggests the dramatic place where something important occurs.
