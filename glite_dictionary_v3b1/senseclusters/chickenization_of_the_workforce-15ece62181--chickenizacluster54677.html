chickenization of the workforce

noun
degradation of jobs and work conditions
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtʃɪkənəˈzeɪʃən əv ðə ˈwɝːkˌfɔrs / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪkɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ðə ˈwɜːkfɔːs / (BrE)
The process by which work conditions and jobs are degraded to lower quality, especially with reduced pay, job security, and independence, as compared to traditional standards.

Examples

The chickenization of the workforce
Labor activists are concerned about the chickenization of the workforce in the gig economy.
Many scholars study the chickenization of the workforce in retail and service industries.
Policies are needed to counteract the chickenization of the workforce across different sectors.
