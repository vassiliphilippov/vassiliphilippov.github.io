intercostal muscle

noun
muscle between the ribs
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈkɑstl̩ ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈkɒstl ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
Any of the muscles situated between the ribs that contract to help with breathing, especially during inhalation.

Examples

The intercostal muscle contracts
An injury to the intercostal muscle can cause sharp pain when breathing.
During deep breaths, the intercostal muscles contract to expand the chest cavity.
The doctor explained that strained intercostal muscles were causing his discomfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rib - one of the bones forming the framework of the chest
