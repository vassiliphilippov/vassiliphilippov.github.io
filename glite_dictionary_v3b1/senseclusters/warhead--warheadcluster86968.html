warhead

noun
explosive tip of a missile or rocket
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːˌhɛd / (BrE)
the front part of a missile, rocket, or torpedo that carries the explosive, nuclear, chemical, or biological charge

Examples

A warhead missile
Engineers are developing a smaller but more powerful warhead for advanced weapons.
The missile's warhead was designed to penetrate reinforced structures.
The treaty aims to reduce the number of nuclear warheads worldwide.
