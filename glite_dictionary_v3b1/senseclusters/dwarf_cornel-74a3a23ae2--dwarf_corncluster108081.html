dwarf cornel

noun
creeping plant with red berries
Pronunciation: / dwɔrf ˈkɔrnəl / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈkɔːnəl / (BrE)
a low-growing perennial plant with clustered whorls of leaves and red berries, native to northern regions from Greenland to Alaska

Examples

The dwarf cornel plant
Botanists identified the plant as dwarf cornel by its clustered leaves and creeping stems.
In the Arctic tundra, patches of dwarf cornel can be seen among the mosses.
The bright red berries of dwarf cornel are a striking addition to the landscape in summer.
