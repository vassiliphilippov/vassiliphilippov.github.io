touch off

verb
provoke or trigger an event
Pronunciation: / tʌtʃ ɔf / (AmE), / tʌtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to cause something (especially an event, reaction, or explosion) to start suddenly; to provoke or trigger

Examples

To touch off a fire
A single spark could touch off a forest fire in these dry conditions.
Her comments touched off a heated debate among the audience.
The scandal touched off a series of protests across the country.
A spark can touch off a massive forest fire if the conditions are right.
The announcement touched off a heated argument among the group.
The politician's speech touched off angry demonstrations across the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prevent - to stop something from happening
  • antonym: quell - to put an end to (especially by force); to suppress
  • synonym: set off - to cause something to begin or happen
  • synonym: spark off - to cause something to happen, especially suddenly or violently
  • synonym: trigger - to cause something to start
