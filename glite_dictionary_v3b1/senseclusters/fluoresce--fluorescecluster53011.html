fluoresce

verb
emit light from fluorescence
7.9 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / fluÀê…ôÀàr…õs / (AmE), / fluÀê…ôÀàr…õs / (BrE)
to emit light as a result of absorbing radiation, typically ultraviolet, without noticeable rise in temperature; to undergo fluorescence

Examples

To fluoresce brightly
Certain types of jellyfish naturally fluoresce, which helps scientists study cell processes.
Researchers observed that the dye would only fluoresce in the presence of specific proteins.
Some minerals fluoresce under ultraviolet light, creating vivid colors in the dark.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phosphoresce - to emit light that lasts after radiation ends, unlike fluorescence
