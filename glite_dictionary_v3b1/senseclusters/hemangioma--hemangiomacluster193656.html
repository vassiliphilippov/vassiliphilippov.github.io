hemangioma

noun
benign tumor of blood vessels
Pronunciation: / hɪˌmændʒiˈoʊmə /; / hiːˌmændʒiˈoʊmə / (AmE), / hɪˌmandʒɪˈəʊmə /; / hiːˌmandʒiˈəʊmə / (BrE)
A benign tumor consisting of a mass of blood vessels, often appearing as a birthmark.

Examples

A hemangioma birthmark
A large hemangioma can sometimes cause complications, depending on its location.
Most hemangiomas in infants fade away without treatment over time.
The doctor diagnosed the red birthmark on her arm as a hemangioma.
Many parents worry when their child develops a haemangioma, but most are harmless and disappear over time.
Surgical removal of a haemangioma is considered only if it causes complications or disfigurement.
The doctor explained that the red mark on the baby's skin was a haemangioma and would likely fade with age.
