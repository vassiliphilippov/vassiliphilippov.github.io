sickle-cell disease

noun
hereditary anemia with crescent-shaped cells
6.5 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪk.əl sel dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ˈsɪk.əl sel də.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /ˈsɪk.əl sel dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A hereditary form of anemia characterized by crescent-shaped red blood cells, primarily affecting people of African descent.

Examples

Sickle-cell disease symptoms
Advances in medicine have improved the quality of life for patients living with sickle-cell disease.
Genetic testing can help identify if someone carries the gene for sickle-cell disease.
People with sickle-cell disease often experience episodes of pain due to blocked blood vessels.
