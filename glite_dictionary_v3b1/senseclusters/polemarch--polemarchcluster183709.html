polemarch

noun
ancient military leader
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊləˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a chief military officer or commander, especially in ancient Greece

Examples

Ancient Greek polemarch
The polemarch was responsible for leading the troops into battle.
In ancient Athens, the polemarch held significant authority over military affairs.
Historians studied the role of the polemarch in classical Greek warfare.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commander - a person in authority, especially over a military force
  • synonym: general - the highest-ranking military officer in an army
