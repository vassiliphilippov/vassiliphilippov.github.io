czardom

noun
rule of a czar
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈzɑːrˌdəm / (AmE), / ˈzɑːdəm / (BrE)
the domain, rule, or period during which a czar governs a country, especially in Russia

Examples

The Russian czardom
The Russian czardom was known for its powerful rulers and vast territories.
During the czardom, many reforms were introduced to modernize the country.
Historians often debate the legacy of the czardom in shaping Russian society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monarchy - a system of government with a monarch at the head
  • antonym: republic - a state in which supreme power is held by the people and their elected representatives
  • synonym: tsardom - the rule or domain of a tsar
