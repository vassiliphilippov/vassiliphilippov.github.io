photostatic

noun
early document copier
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˈstætɪk / (BrE)
a machine that makes photographic copies of documents, especially an early type of copier used before modern photocopiers

Examples

Old photostatic machine
The university archive still keeps an old photostatic in the basement for historical interest.
Before digital technology, offices relied on a photostatic to duplicate important documents.
He learned to operate the photostatic during his first job at the library.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photocopier - a machine that makes paper copies of documents and images
  • synonym: scanner - a device that digitizes documents or images
