reasoned

adjective
based on careful thought or logic
4.5 1.10/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêz…ônd / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêz…ônd / (BrE)
(of an argument or statement) based on careful thought, logic, or reasoning; logically valid.

Examples

A reasoned decision
A reasoned decision is always preferable to a rushed one.
She presented a reasoned analysis of the evidence during the meeting.
Their disagreement was settled through a reasoned discussion rather than an argument.

Synonyms

  • logical
    3.8 22/m
    logical is clear and consistent thinking that follows strict rules, while reasoned shows careful thought and step-by-step justification.
  • well-grounded
    4.4 0.41/m
    well-grounded is based on good evidence and reasons, while reasoned focuses on careful thinking that leads to a logical conclusion.
  • sound
    4.5 5.66/m
    sound is correct and reliable in its results, while reasoned shows an argument made by careful thought and reasoning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable
  • synonym: logical - using or according to strict principles of validity
