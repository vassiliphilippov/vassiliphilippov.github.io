myokymia

noun
muscle twitching condition
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪoʊˈkɪmiə /; / ˌmaɪoʊˈkaɪmiə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəʊˈkɪmɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition where muscles twitch or quiver involuntarily, often affecting the eyelids or face

Examples

Myokymia in eyelid
After several hours of computer work, she noticed myokymia in her lower eyelid.
The doctor explained that myokymia is usually harmless and often related to fatigue or stress.
Persistent myokymia in the facial muscles prompted him to seek medical advice.
The patient complained of persistent myokymia in her eyelid, which was visible as fine twitching.
Doctors diagnosed myokymia after observing continuous muscle movements during the examination.
Electromyography confirmed the presence of myokymia in the affected limb.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fasciculation - a brief, involuntary muscle twitch
  • antonym: paralysis - loss of muscle function in part of the body
  • synonym: spasm - a sudden, involuntary muscle contraction
