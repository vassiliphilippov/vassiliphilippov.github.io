magnetometer

noun
instrument measuring magnetic fields
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmæɡ.nəˈtɑː.mə.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌmæɡ.nɪˈtɒm.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
an instrument for measuring the strength and direction of magnetic fields

Examples

A magnetometer device
A magnetometer is often used in archaeological surveys to locate buried artifacts.
Engineers installed a magnetometer on the spacecraft to measure the magnetic environment of Mars.
The geologist used a magnetometer to detect subtle changes in the Earth's magnetic field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnetograph - an instrument for recording the intensity and position of magnetic fields
