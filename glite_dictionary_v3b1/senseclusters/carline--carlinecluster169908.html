carline

noun
old woman (dialect)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɑːlaɪn / (BrE)
an old woman, especially one regarded as a hag or crone, chiefly in Scottish or dialect use

Examples

Old carline
The villagers whispered that the solitary carline at the cottage was feared by children.
In the old tale, a wise carline lived at the edge of the moor and gave advice to travelers.
Some believed the carline could cast spells, so they avoided her house after dark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crone - an old woman, often one who is thin and ugly
  • synonym: hag - an old woman, especially one who is unpleasant or frightening
