oxide

noun
compound of oxygen and another element
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːk.saɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒk.saɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound consisting of oxygen and one other element or radical

Examples

Metal oxide
Many metals, when exposed to air, develop a thin layer of oxide on their surface.
Rust is a common oxide formed when iron reacts with oxygen in the air.
Silicon oxide is widely used in the production of electronic devices.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: peroxide - a compound containing an oxygen-oxygen single bond, often used as a strong oxidizer
