deliver

verb
formally hand over or surrender
5.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈlɪv.ər/ /; / /də.ˈlɪv.ər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈlɪv.ə/ /; / /də.ˈlɪv.ə/ (BrE)
to formally hand over or give someone or something to another person or authority

Examples

To deliver the evidence
Following the trial, the judge ordered the officials to deliver the evidence to the court.
She refused to deliver the confidential documents to her competitor.
The guard delivered the prisoner to the authorities after his arrest.
He was delivered to the requesting state after a lengthy legal battle.
The government refused to deliver its citizens to another nation without substantial evidence.
Under the treaty, the country agreed to deliver the suspect to the foreign authorities for trial.
According to the contract, tenants must deliver the premises in good condition at the end of the lease.
She agreed to deliver the property to its rightful owner.
The company was ordered to deliver the confidential documents to the authorities.
