settle

verb
become clear by sedimentation
4.3 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈse.təl/ (AmE), /ˈse.təl/ (BrE)
to sink, come to rest, or form a layer at the bottom (of particles, substances, or objects in a liquid or air)

Examples

Particles settle at bottom
After mixing, the particles will settle at the bottom of the container.
Allow the muddy water to settle before pouring it off.
The winemaker let the juice settle overnight to separate the solids.
As the old ship filled with water, it began to settle slowly beneath the waves.
During the storm, the overloaded boat started to settle after taking on water.
Mud caused the small raft to settle just below the surface of the river.
After stirring the pond, it took hours for the dirt to settle.
If you let the juice stand, the pulp will eventually settle at the bottom of the bottle.
The sand began to settle as the current slowed down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clarify - make a liquid clear by removing suspended matter
  • synonym: descend - to move downward and come to rest
  • antonym: float - to rest on or near the surface of a liquid instead of sinking
  • synonym: land - to come down and rest on a surface, especially after flying
  • synonym: precipitate - to cause a substance to be deposited in solid form from a solution
  • synonym: sink - to go down below the surface of something, especially water
