mystagogue

noun
one who obscures meaning
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪs.tə.gɑːg/ /; / /ˈmɪs.tə.gɔːg/ (AmE), /ˈmɪs.tə.gɒg/ (BrE)
a person who makes things seem mysterious or obscure, often to impress or manipulate others

Examples

A mystagogue hides meaning
The professor was accused of being a mystagogue who made simple ideas seem complicated.
Some leaders act as mystagogues to keep their followers dependent on them for answers.
Instead of explaining the process, he played the mystagogue and left everyone confused.

  • synonym: obfuscator - someone who makes things unclear or confusing
