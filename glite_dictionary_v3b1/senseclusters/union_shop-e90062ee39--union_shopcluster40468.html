union shop

noun
workplace requiring employees to join union after hiring
5.1 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàjuÀêni…ôn  É…ëp / (AmE), / ÀàjuÀêni…ôn  É…íp / (BrE)
A company or workplace in which employees are not required to be union members when hired, but must join the union within a certain period of employment.

Examples

A union shop workplace
He accepted a job at a union shop and joined the union soon after starting.
The company operates as a union shop, so all employees are required to join the union within thirty days.
Union shop agreements are common in industries with strong labor organizations.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: closed shop - a workplace where all employees must be union members before being hired
  • degree lower: open shop - a workplace where employees are not required to join a union
