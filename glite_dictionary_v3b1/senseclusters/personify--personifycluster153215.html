personify

verb
represent or embody as a person
3.1 4.9
Pronunciation: / pərˈsɑːnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / pəˈsɒnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to represent or describe something (such as an idea, quality, or thing) as a person or with human qualities

Examples

To personify an idea
He personifies the villain with chilling authenticity throughout the play.
In her writing, she would often personify love as a gentle guide.
The actor managed to personify the spirit of hope on stage.
In his sculpture, he tried to personify the spirit of freedom.
Poems often personify nature to convey emotions more vividly.
The author chooses to personify death as a silent woman dressed in black.
Many cultures personify death as a cloaked figure carrying a scythe.
The company's mascot personifies their brand values in a friendly and approachable way.
Writers often personify nature, describing the wind as if it could whisper secrets.
