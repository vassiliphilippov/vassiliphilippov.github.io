compact

noun
formal written agreement between parties
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpækt / (AmE), / kəmˈpækt / (BrE)
a formal signed written agreement between two or more parties, especially nations, to do or refrain from doing something

Examples

A peace compact
The countries signed a compact to protect the shared river from pollution.
The historic Mayflower Compact established guidelines for self-government among the colonists.
Under the new compact, both nations promised to reduce tariffs over the next decade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties
  • antonym: disagreement - a situation in which parties do not agree
  • synonym: treaty - a formally concluded and ratified agreement between countries
