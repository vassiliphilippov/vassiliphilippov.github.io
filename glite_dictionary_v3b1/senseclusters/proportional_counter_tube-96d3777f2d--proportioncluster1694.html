proportional counter tube

noun
type of radiation detector
8.2
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈpɔːr.ʃən.əl ˈkaʊn.tər tuːb/ /; / /prə.ˈpɔːr.ʃən.əl ˈkaʊn.tər tjuːb/ (AmE), /prə.ˈpɔː.ʃən.əl ˈkaʊn.tə tjuːb/ (BrE)
a type of radiation detector in which the output electrical pulse is proportional to the number of ions produced by incoming radiation

Examples

A proportional counter tube detector
Each proportional counter tube in the array was calibrated individually for accuracy.
The proportional counter tube allows scientists to measure the intensity of radiation more precisely.
Unlike a Geiger counter, a proportional counter tube provides information about the energy of detected particles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Geiger-Müller tube - radiation detector producing pulses not proportional to ionization
