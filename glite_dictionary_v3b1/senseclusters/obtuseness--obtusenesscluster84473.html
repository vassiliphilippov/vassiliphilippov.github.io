obtuseness

noun
slowness to understand
3.7
Pronunciation: / əbˈtuːsnəs, ɑbˈtuːsnəs / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːsnəs, ɒbˈtjuːsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being slow to understand or perceive things; dullness of comprehension.

Examples

His obtuseness in class
His obtuseness made it difficult for his colleagues to explain the project to him.
She was frustrated by the obtuseness of the bureaucratic process.
The teacher addressed the student's obtuseness with patience and repeated explanations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dullness - lack of intelligence or quickness of mind
  • antonym: sharpness - quickness of mind or understanding
