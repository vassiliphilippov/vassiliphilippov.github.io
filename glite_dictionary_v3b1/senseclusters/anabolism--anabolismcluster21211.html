anabolism

noun
the synthesis of complex molecules in organisms
4.8
Pronunciation: / əˈnæbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnabəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the chemical process in living organisms by which complex molecules are built from simpler ones, usually requiring energy

Examples

Anabolism process
During periods of recovery, the body shifts its focus to anabolism.
Muscle growth is a result of anabolism stimulated by exercise and proper nutrition.
Steroid hormones can significantly enhance anabolism in certain tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catabolism - the breakdown of complex molecules to simpler ones, releasing energy in organisms
