filtration surgery

noun
eye surgery for glaucoma treatment
6.0
Pronunciation: / fɪlˈtreɪʃən ˈsɜrdʒəri / (AmE), / fɪlˈtreɪʃən ˈsɜːdʒəri / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for glaucoma that creates an opening in the eye to allow excess aqueous humor to drain, reducing intraocular pressure

Examples

Filtration surgery for glaucoma
After undergoing filtration surgery, her vision stabilised and the risk of blindness was reduced.
Complications following filtration surgery may include infection or excessive drainage of eye fluid.
The ophthalmologist recommended filtration surgery to lower the pressure in my right eye.
