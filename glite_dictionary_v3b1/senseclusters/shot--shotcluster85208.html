shot

noun
explosive charge used in blasting
Pronunciation: / ʃɑt / (AmE), / ʃɒt / (BrE)
the act of firing or launching a projectile (such as a bullet, explosive, or missile) from a weapon

Examples

A shot from a gun
A single shot was enough to bring down the entire section of rock.
The workers placed a shot in the mine tunnel before evacuating the area.
They carefully calculated the size of the shot needed for the demolition.
He heard a loud shot echoing through the forest.
She lined up her shot and scored a bullseye.
The hunter took a careful shot at the deer but missed.
Each shot is closely monitored by engineers in the control room.
The success of the satellite shot marked a major step forward for the program.
The test shot of the new rocket took place last night at the space center.
