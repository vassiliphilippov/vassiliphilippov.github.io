public

adjective
relating to the people as a whole
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affecting all people; open or accessible to everyone; not private

Examples

Public space
She works in public health, focusing on improving community wellness.
The mayor called for more public investment in local schools and parks.
The new law is intended to serve the public interest rather than private business.
The city has invested in more public transportation options to serve its residents.
The museum is open to the public every day except Monday.
There was a public outcry over the decision to close the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communal - shared by all members of a community; for common use
  • antonym: private - relating to an individual person or limited group, not public
