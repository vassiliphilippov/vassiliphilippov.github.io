widemouthed

adjective
mouth open wide
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪdˌmaʊðd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdˌmaʊðd / (BrE)
having the mouth open wide, especially due to surprise, shock, or amazement

Examples

Widemouthed with surprise
The children stood widemouthed, staring at the magician's final, impossible trick.
She listened widemouthed as her friend described the incredible adventure.
The audience sat widemouthed in awe as the fireworks lit up the night sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closed-mouthed - with the mouth shut
  • synonym: gaping - with the mouth wide open in surprise or wonder
