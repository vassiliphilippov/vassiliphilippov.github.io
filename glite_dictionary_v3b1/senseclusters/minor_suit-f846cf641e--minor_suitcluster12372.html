minor suit

noun
bridge suit: diamonds or clubs
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnɚ suːt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə sjuːt / (BrE)
In the card game bridge, a suit with a lower scoring value—diamonds or clubs—as opposed to major suits.

Examples

A minor suit in bridge
Clubs and diamonds are each considered a minor suit in the rules of bridge.
In bridge, bidding a minor suit can help you find a safe contract.
She held only low cards in the minor suit and decided not to bid.

Related Terms

  • antonym: major suit - in bridge, a suit of higher scoring value—hearts or spades
