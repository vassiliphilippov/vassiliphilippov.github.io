conjunctural

adjective
related to circumstances
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒʌŋktʃərəl / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋktʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to a particular set of circumstances or conditions existing at a specific time

Examples

Conjunctural economic factors
The economic crisis was caused by several conjunctural factors that happened at the same time.
Analysts must distinguish between conjunctural changes and long-term trends in the market.
Her success was not just due to talent but also to favorable conjunctural circumstances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: situational - related to or dependent on a particular situation
  • antonym: structural - relating to the structure rather than temporary conditions
