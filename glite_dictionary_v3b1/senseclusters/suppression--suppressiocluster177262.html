suppression

noun
withholding publication or circulation
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / səˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the act of preventing a book or piece of writing from being published or distributed

Examples

Suppression of a book
Police justified the suppression of the article on the grounds of national security.
The government ordered the suppression of the controversial book.
There was public outrage over the suppression of independent newspapers during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: censorship - the control or banning of speech or writing considered objectionable
