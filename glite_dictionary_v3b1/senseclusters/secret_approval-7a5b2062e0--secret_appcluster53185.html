secret approval

noun
tacit or unspoken consent (law)
1.2 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.krət ə.ˈpruː.vəl/ /; / /ˈsiː.krɪt ə.ˈpruː.vəl/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.krət ə.ˈpruː.vəl/ /; / /ˈsiː.krɪt ə.ˈpruː.vəl/ (BrE)
Tacit or unspoken consent, especially to someone's wrongdoing, often implying knowledge without direct acknowledgment.

Examples

Secret approval given silently
By keeping silent, the officials gave their secret approval to the illegal activities.
Even without direct orders, employees took the supervisor's nod as secret approval of their actions.
The manager's secret approval of the scheme allowed unethical practices to continue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit approval - approval that is clearly and openly expressed
  • synonym: tacit approval - approval that is understood or implied without being stated
