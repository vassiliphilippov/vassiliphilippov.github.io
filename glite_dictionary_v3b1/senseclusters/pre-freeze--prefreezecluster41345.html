pre-freeze

noun
initial freezing stage
5.7
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈfriːz/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfriːz/ (AmE), /priː.ˈfriːz/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfriːz/ (BrE)
a short initial freezing process or the state resulting from briefly freezing something before main freezing or storage

Examples

A quick pre-freeze
The technician performed a pre-freeze to stabilize the tissue samples before the main preservation process.
A quick pre-freeze helps prevent large ice crystals from forming in the food.
Before long-term storage, the lab always does a pre-freeze to protect sensitive materials.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: freeze - to make something become solid by cooling it below its freezing point
  • antonym: thaw - to become unfrozen or to make something unfrozen
