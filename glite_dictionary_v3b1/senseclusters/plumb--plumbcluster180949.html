plumb

verb
measure depth (esp. with a weighted line)
Pronunciation: / plʌm / (AmE), / plʌm / (BrE)
to measure depth or check if something is exactly vertical using a plumb line or weight

Examples

To plumb the depth
Divers would often plumb the sea to chart underwater features.
She attempted to plumb the well but found it was deeper than expected.
The sailors used a rope to plumb the depth of the river before docking.
After installing the door frame, she plumbed it to make sure it was exactly vertical.
He used a string and weight to plumb the wall before starting to tile.
The carpenter carefully plumbed the post before securing it to the floor.
Engineers often plumb wells to ensure accurate measurements for construction projects.
She plumbed the depths of the old well to check if it was safe to use.
The sailor used a rope to plumb the river's depth before crossing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: level - to make something horizontal or even
  • synonym: sound - to measure the depth of water, especially with a line
