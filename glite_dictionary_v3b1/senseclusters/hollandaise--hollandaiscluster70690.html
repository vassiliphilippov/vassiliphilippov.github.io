hollandaise

noun
rich sauce of eggs, butter, and lemon
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhɑːlənˈdeɪz / (AmE), / ˌhɒlənˈdeɪz / (BrE)
a rich, creamy sauce made with egg yolks, butter, and lemon juice, typically served with vegetables, eggs, or fish

Examples

Rich hollandaise sauce
Eggs Benedict are often topped with creamy hollandaise sauce.
He poured hollandaise over his asparagus for a delicious touch.
The chef whisked the hollandaise until it was smooth and glossy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: béarnaise - a similar sauce made with egg yolks, butter, and tarragon vinegar
