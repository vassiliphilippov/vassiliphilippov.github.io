ash-key

noun
winged seed of the ash tree
Pronunciation: / ˈæʃ ˌki / (AmE), / ˈæʃ ˌkiː / (BrE)
the winged seed of the ash tree, typically dispersed by the wind

Examples

A ash-key seed
Children often collect ash-keys in autumn to watch them spin to the ground.
Each ash-key contains a seed encased in a papery wing.
The park was covered in fallen ash-keys after the strong winds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: samara - a type of winged fruit or seed, especially from ash, maple, or elm trees
