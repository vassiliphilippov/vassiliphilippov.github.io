perceptual constancy

noun
tendency for perception of objects to remain stable
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈsep.tʃəl ˈkɑːn.stən.siː/ /; / /pər.ˈsep.tʃuː.əl ˈkɑːn.stən.siː/ (AmE), /pə.ˈsep.tʃʊə ˈkɒn.stən.siː/ /; / /pə.ˈsep.tʃəl ˈkɒn.stən.siː/ (BrE)
the tendency for perceived objects to appear stable and unchanged despite variations in sensory input, such as changes in lighting, distance, or angle

Examples

Perceptual constancy in vision
Children develop perceptual constancy as their visual system matures.
Thanks to perceptual constancy, we recognize a friend's face even in dim lighting.
The concept of perceptual constancy helps explain why objects look the same size from different distances.
