drypoint

noun
intaglio printmaking method
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a print made by scratching an image into a metal plate with a hard-pointed needle and then printing from it

Examples

A drypoint print
The museum displayed a rare drypoint created by a famous 19th-century artist.
She learned how to make a drypoint in her printmaking class at college.
Collectors often seek out original drypoints for their unique textures and lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engraving - a print made by cutting lines into a metal plate
  • synonym: etching - a printmaking process using acid to cut into a metal plate
