scrape

verb
collect resources with difficulty
7.1 4.6
Pronunciation: /skreɪp/ (AmE), /skreɪp/ (BrE)
to collect or gather money or resources with effort or difficulty, often gradually or over time

Examples

To scrape together money
After losing his job, he had to scrape together enough money to pay the bills.
She scraped by for months until she found a better-paying job.
They managed to scrape up the funds needed to repair the roof.
