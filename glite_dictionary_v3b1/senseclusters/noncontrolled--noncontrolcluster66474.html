noncontrolled

adjective
without experimental controls
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈtroʊld / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈtrəʊld / (BrE)
not involving a control group or lacking experimental controls, especially in scientific studies or trials

Examples

Noncontrolled study
The study was noncontrolled, so its findings should be interpreted with caution.
A noncontrolled trial may not provide strong evidence compared to a controlled one.
Researchers often prefer controlled experiments over noncontrolled ones for more reliable results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: controlled - involving a control group or condition in an experiment
  • synonym: uncontrolled - not regulated or managed; lacking control
