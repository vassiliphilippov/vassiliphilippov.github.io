parodist

noun
person who creates parody
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəˌdɪst / (AmE), / ˈpærədɪst / (BrE)
A person who imitates the style of a particular writer, artist, or genre for comic effect.

Examples

A parodist of music
Many comedians began their careers as parodists of popular music and culture.
She is considered a gifted parodist for her witty imitations of classic films.
The parodist expertly captured the author's style, turning the novel into a hilarious spoof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: satirist - a person who uses satire to criticize or mock
