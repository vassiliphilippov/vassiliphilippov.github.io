chattel

noun
movable personal property
8.6 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæt.əl / (BrE)
A piece of personal property that is movable, such as furniture, animals, or vehicles, as opposed to real estate.

Examples

Personal chattel property
All furniture and vehicles are considered chattel in property law.
The court ruled that the stolen chattel must be returned to its rightful owner.
Under the agreement, only real estate was excluded; all other possessions counted as chattel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: real property - land and immovable property, as opposed to chattel
