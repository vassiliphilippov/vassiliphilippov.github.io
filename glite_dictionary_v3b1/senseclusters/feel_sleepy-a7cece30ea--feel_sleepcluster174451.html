feel sleepy

verb
to become drowsy or wanting sleep
1.0 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / fil ÀàsliÀêpi / (AmE), / fiÀêl ÀàsliÀêpi / (BrE)
to begin to experience drowsiness or the desire to sleep

Examples

To feel sleepy during class
During the lecture, she started to feel sleepy and struggled to keep her eyes open.
I always feel sleepy after having a large lunch.
It's normal to feel sleepy late at night when your body is ready to rest.

Synonyms

  • sleepy
    1.0 8.04/m
    sleepy is the common adjective for being ready to fall asleep, while feel sleepy describes starting to feel drowsy.
  • drowsy
    2.7 1.74/m
    drowsy is used for a heavy, slow feeling often from tiredness, while feel sleepy means beginning to notice that drowsiness.
  • dozy
    4.3 0.03/m
    dozy feels slightly silly or half awake in informal talk, while feel sleepy describes the start of real drowsiness.
  • somnolent
    8.4 0.25/m
    somnolent is a formal or medical word often for caused sleepiness, while feel sleepy is everyday and describes beginning drowsiness.
