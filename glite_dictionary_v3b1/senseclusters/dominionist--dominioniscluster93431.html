dominionist

adjective
favoring religious rule
9.9
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɪnjənɪst / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnjənɪst / (BrE)
supporting or relating to the belief that government and public institutions should be based on dominionist religious principles

Examples

Dominionist policies
The party's dominionist agenda was criticized for blurring the line between church and state.
Some activists voiced concern over dominionist influence in the new education policy.
A dominionist approach to lawmaking can challenge the separation of religion and government.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
  • synonym: theocratic - relating to government by religious leaders or principles
