give out

verb
emit or discharge (light, heat, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv aʊt / (AmE), / ɡɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to produce or send forth something such as light, heat, sound, or a substance; to emit or discharge.

Examples

Give out a smell
Some flowers give out a strong fragrance in the evening.
The campfire gives out a comforting warmth on cold nights.
This machine gives out a low humming noise when it's operating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emit - to send out (light, heat, sound, etc.)
  • synonym: release - to let something flow or go out
