nettle

verb
to annoy or irritate
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛtəl /; / ˈnɛt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈnɛtəl /; / ˈnɛt.əl / (BrE)
to annoy, irritate, or provoke someone, especially in a way that feels sharp or stinging

Examples

To nettle a friend
Her critical remarks often nettle her colleagues during meetings.
I was nettled by his constant interruptions while I was speaking.
The delays at the airport began to nettle even the most patient travelers.
His constant criticism can nettle even the most patient person.
She was nettled by his rude remarks and could not hide her irritation.
The journalist's questions seemed to nettle the politician, making him visibly uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone slightly angry
  • synonym: irritate - to make someone annoyed or a part of the body sore or painful
  • antonym: soothe - to make someone calmer or less irritated
