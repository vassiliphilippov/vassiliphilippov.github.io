metamere

noun
body segment in segmented animals
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtəˌmɪr / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌmɪə / (BrE)
one of a series of similar segments into which the body of some animals is divided, especially in annelids and arthropods

Examples

A metamere segment
In earthworms, each metamere contains repeated sets of organs and muscles.
Many invertebrates have bodies divided into metameres that aid in their movement.
The scientist examined the metameres of the centipede under a microscope.
