nemesis

noun
an agent of downfall or retribution
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɛmɪsɪs /; / ˈnɛməsɪs / (BrE)
a person or thing that is a powerful rival or enemy, or the cause of someone's defeat or downfall

Examples

A nemesis rival
For years, his arrogant attitude proved to be his greatest nemesis.
Procrastination is often the nemesis of productivity.
Rising sea levels are a potential nemesis for many coastal cities.
For years, the detective pursued his nemesis across the city.
In the final match, the team finally defeated their old nemesis.
The superhero's nemesis always seemed to be one step ahead.

  • antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or relief
  • synonym: opponent - someone who competes against or opposes another
  • synonym: scourge - a person or thing that causes great trouble or suffering
