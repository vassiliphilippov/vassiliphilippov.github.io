spinalis

adjective
having spines
Pronunciation: / spaɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / spaɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
used in scientific Latin names to indicate that a plant or animal is spiny or has spines

Examples

Spinalis cactus species
The cactus was classified as spinalis due to its sharp, prominent spines.
In the species name Echinocereus spinalis, the term refers to the plant's spiny stems.
Many zoologists use spinalis to describe animals with noticeable spines or thorns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth - having an even surface without spines or roughness
  • synonym: spinous - having spines or thorns
