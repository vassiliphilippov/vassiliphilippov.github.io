mycophage

noun
organism that eats fungi
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkəˌfɑːdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪkə(ʊ)ˌfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
an organism, such as a person or animal, that feeds on fungi, especially mushrooms

Examples

Mycophages eat mushrooms
Certain insects are considered mycophages because they feed primarily on wild mushrooms.
Scientists observed a previously unknown mycophage in the rainforest ecosystem.
The mycophage habits of some mammals help in the dispersal of fungal spores.
