Acoma

noun
Native pottery style
Pronunciation: / ˈækəmə / (AmE), / ˈækəmə / (BrE)
a traditional style of handmade pottery created by the Acoma people, known for thin walls and geometric painted designs

Examples

Acoma pottery
The museum displayed a beautiful Acoma bowl with intricate black and white patterns.
Collectors value Acoma pottery for its delicate construction and unique designs.
She learned about the history of Acoma ceramics during her visit to New Mexico.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceramics - objects made from clay and hardened by heat
  • synonym: pueblo pottery - handmade pottery from Native American Pueblo peoples
