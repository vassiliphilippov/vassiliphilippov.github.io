therapsid

noun
extinct reptile ancestor of mammals
Pronunciation: / θəˈræp.sɪd / (AmE), / θəˈræp.sɪd / (BrE)
a member of an extinct group of reptile-like animals from which mammals are believed to have evolved

Examples

A therapsid species
Most therapsid species disappeared by the end of the Permian period.
Scientists believe that therapsids were probably warm-blooded, unlike their reptilian ancestors.
The discovery of therapsid fossils has shed light on the early evolution of mammals.
