squeam

noun
feeling of unease
9.9
Pronunciation: / skwiːm / (AmE), / skwiːm / (BrE)
an uneasy or uncomfortable feeling; a qualm or slight fear (archaic)

Examples

A squeam of fear
He felt a sudden squeam as he approached the abandoned building at night.
Despite a slight squeam, she decided to speak in front of the large audience.
The old tale gave him a squeam that lingered long after he finished reading.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or nervousness
  • antonym: confidence - a feeling of self-assurance or certainty
  • synonym: qualm - a sudden feeling of doubt or worry
