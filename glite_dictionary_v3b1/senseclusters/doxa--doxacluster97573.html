doxa

noun
accepted belief
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːksə / (AmE), / ˈdɒksə / (BrE)
a belief or opinion, especially one that is widely accepted or held in philosophical or theological contexts

Examples

Philosophical doxa
In ancient philosophy, doxa referred to common beliefs held by the majority.
The scholar challenged the prevailing doxa with new evidence from recent studies.
Religious traditions often distinguish between doxa and deeper, reasoned understanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • antonym: heresy - belief or opinion contrary to orthodox doctrine
  • synonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice
