undercast

noun
cloud layer below
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkɑːst / (BrE)
a continuous layer of cloud lying below the observer, especially as seen from an aircraft.

Examples

Thick undercast from the plane
The pilot reported a thick undercast that made it impossible to see the ground.
From above the undercast, the mountains looked like islands in a white sea.
As the plane climbed, passengers marveled at the endless undercast stretching beneath them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloud cover - the amount of sky covered by clouds
  • antonym: overcast - a sky completely covered with clouds
