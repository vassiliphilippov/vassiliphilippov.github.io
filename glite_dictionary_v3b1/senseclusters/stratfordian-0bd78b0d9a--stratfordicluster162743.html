Stratfordian

adjective
Shakespeare authorship belief
9.8
Pronunciation: /stræt.ˈfɔːr.dɪ.ən/ /; / /ˈstræt.fər.dɪ.ən/ (AmE), /stræt.ˈfɔː.di.ən/ /; / /ˈstræt.fə.di.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the belief that William Shakespeare of Stratford-upon-Avon was the true author of the works attributed to him.

Examples

Stratfordian theory
The Stratfordian view is widely accepted among most Shakespeare scholars.
He wrote a book defending the Stratfordian position against alternative authorship theories.
Many debates between Stratfordian and anti-Stratfordian scholars have taken place over the years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anti-Stratfordian - believing someone other than Shakespeare wrote the works attributed to him
