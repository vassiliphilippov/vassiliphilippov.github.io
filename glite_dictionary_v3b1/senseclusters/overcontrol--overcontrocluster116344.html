overcontrol

verb
to control too much
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to apply excessive corrective control to a vehicle, machine, or system, often causing instability or unwanted movement

Examples

To overcontrol a plane
Pilots must avoid overcontrolling the aircraft during turbulence to maintain stability.
If you overcontrol the steering wheel, the car may start to swerve dangerously.
Technicians warn not to overcontrol the system, as it can lead to unpredictable behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oversteer - to turn a vehicle's steering too much, causing loss of control
  • antonym: undercontrol - to control too little, resulting in insufficient correction
