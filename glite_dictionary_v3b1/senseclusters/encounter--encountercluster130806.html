encounter

noun
hostile confrontation or direct conflict
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkaʊntər /; / ɪnˈkaʊntɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
a confrontation or conflict, especially one that happens by chance

Examples

A tense encounter
The heated encounter between the two leaders lasted for more than an hour.
Their encounter on the football field quickly turned into a bitter dispute.
Yesterday’s political debate became a hostile encounter when neither side backed down.
The soldiers had a brief encounter with the enemy patrol at dawn.
The troops retreated after a minor encounter with local resistance forces.
There was a violent encounter between rival gangs in the alley.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically binding arrangement between parties
  • degree higher: battle - a sustained fight between large organized armed forces
  • synonym: clash - a violent confrontation or conflict
  • synonym: confrontation - a hostile or argumentative meeting or situation between opposing parties
  • synonym: skirmish - a minor fight, especially one between small, outlying parts of armies
