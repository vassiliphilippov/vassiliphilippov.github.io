myeloma

noun
malignant bone marrow tumor
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪəˈloʊmə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəˈləʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor of the bone marrow composed of cells normally found in the bone marrow.

Examples

A myeloma tumor
After several tests, she was diagnosed with a rare form of myeloma.
The doctor explained that the severe back pain was due to a myeloma affecting the spine.
Treatment options for myeloma include chemotherapy and stem cell transplantation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: multiple myeloma - a cancer of plasma cells in the bone marrow
