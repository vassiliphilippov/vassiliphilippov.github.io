put one's name in the hat

verb
enter oneself for selection or competition
5.0
Pronunciation: / pʊt wʌnz neɪm ɪn ðə hæt / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz neɪm ɪn ðə hæt / (BrE)
to formally enter oneself as a candidate or express one's intention to participate or be considered for an opportunity, selection, or competition

Examples

To put one's name in the hat for election
After years of hesitation, she finally put her name in the hat for the board position.
He wasn't sure at first, but he chose to put his name in the hat for the upcoming election.
Several employees decided to put their names in the hat for the leadership program.
