unwieldy

adjective
hard to manage due to size or shape
7.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈwiːl.diː/ /; / /ʌn.ˈwiːl.diː/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈwiːl.diː/ /; / /ən.ˈwiːl.diː/ (BrE)
difficult to move, handle, or manage because of size, shape, weight, or complexity; awkward or clumsy

Examples

An unwieldy machine
As technology advanced, the company replaced its unwieldy equipment with more compact machines.
He struggled to steer the unwieldy cart through the narrow doorway.
The piano was too unwieldy for two people to carry safely down the stairs.
He struggled to carry the unwieldy package up the narrow staircase.
The committee found the current tax system too unwieldy to reform quickly.
The new software update made the once intuitive interface unwieldy and confusing to navigate.
Because of its unwieldy shape, the old armchair was difficult to move through the narrow doorway.
His unwieldy movements on the dance floor drew amused glances from the crowd.
The large suitcase was so unwieldy that she struggled to carry it up the stairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clumsy - lacking skill or grace in movement or action
  • synonym: cumbersome - large or heavy and therefore difficult to carry or use
  • antonym: graceful - showing grace or elegance in movement or form
  • antonym: manageable - able to be handled or controlled easily
