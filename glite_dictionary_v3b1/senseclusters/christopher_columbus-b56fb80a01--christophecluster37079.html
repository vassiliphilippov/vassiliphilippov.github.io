Christopher Columbus

proper noun
Italian explorer who crossed the Atlantic; discovered Americas
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪstəfɚ kəˈlʌmbəs / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəfə kəˈlʌmbəs / (BrE)
(1451–1506) An Italian explorer and navigator who completed four voyages across the Atlantic Ocean, opening the way for the widespread European exploration and colonization of the Americas.

Examples

Christopher Columbus voyage
Christopher Columbus set sail in 1492, seeking a new route to Asia.
Many cities and monuments in the Americas bear the name Christopher Columbus.
The voyages of Christopher Columbus marked a turning point in world history.
