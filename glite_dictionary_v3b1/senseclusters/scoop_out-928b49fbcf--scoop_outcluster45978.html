scoop out

verb
remove the inside by scooping
3.3 0.73/m
Pronunciation: / skuÀêp a ät / (AmE), / skuÀêp a ät / (BrE)
to remove the inside or contents of something using a scoop or similar tool, often making it hollow

Examples

To scoop out seeds
She used a spoon to scoop out the seeds from the pumpkin.
The archaeologist carefully scooped out soil from the ancient pot.
You need to scoop out the center of the bread before adding the filling.
She scooped out the seeds from the melon before serving it.
The workers scooped out mud from the drain after the heavy rain.
Use this spoon to scoop out the ice cream into your bowl.

Synonyms

  • hollow out
    4.0 0.52/m
    hollow out removes the inside of something by many methods, while scoop out suggests using a scoop to make it hollow.
  • hollow
    4.8 1.77/m
    hollow often means make an empty space by removing the core, while scoop out specifically suggests using a scoop tool.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fill - to put something into a space or container
  • synonym: hollow out - to make empty inside by removing the inside material
