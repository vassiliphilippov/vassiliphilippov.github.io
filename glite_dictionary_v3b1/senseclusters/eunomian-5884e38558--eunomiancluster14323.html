Eunomian

proper noun
early Christian sect member
Pronunciation: / juːˈnoʊmiən / (AmE), / juːˈnəʊmiən / (BrE)
a member of an early Christian sect that followed the teachings of Eunomius

Examples

A Eunomian leader
The Eunomian doctrine was considered heretical by mainstream church leaders.
Historians study the beliefs and practices of the Eunomian community in the fourth century.
A Eunomian was often at odds with other Christian groups of the time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arian - a follower of the Arian branch of early Christianity
  • antonym: orthodox - adhering to accepted or traditional beliefs, especially in religion
