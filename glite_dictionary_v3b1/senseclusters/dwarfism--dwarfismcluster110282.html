dwarfism

noun
compact or reduced plant growth
6.2 0.01/m

(in botany and horticulture) the condition or trait in plants that results in unusually small or compact growth, whether genetic or induced.

Examples

Plant dwarfism trait
The nursery introduced a new dwarfism trait in several conifer varieties for small urban gardens.
After years of selective breeding, the rose breeder promoted dwarfism to make plants suitable for containers.
Cold stress during early development can cause temporary dwarfism in young seedlings, reducing their height.
