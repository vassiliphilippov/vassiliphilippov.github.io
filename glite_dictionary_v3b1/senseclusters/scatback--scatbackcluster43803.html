scatback

noun
quick football runner
Pronunciation: / ˈskætˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈskætˌbæk / (BrE)
a fast and agile running back in American football, typically smaller and known for quick movements

Examples

Fast scatback runner
The rookie scatback impressed everyone with his speed and ability to avoid tackles.
Coaches value a scatback for his agility and quick acceleration on the field.
Despite his small size, the scatback scored two touchdowns in the first half.
