Dalmatian laburnum

noun
shrub with yellow flowers, poisonous seeds, Balkan native
9.9
Pronunciation: / dælˈmeɪʃən ləˈbɝːnəm / (AmE), / dælˈmeɪʃən ləˈbɜːnəm / (BrE)
A shrub native to the Balkans, especially Yugoslavia, with large trifoliate leaves, dense clusters of yellow flowers, and poisonous seeds; sometimes placed in the genus Cytisus.

Examples

The Dalmatian laburnum shrub
Gardeners should be cautious, as the seeds of the Dalmatian laburnum are highly poisonous.
In botanical collections, the Dalmatian laburnum is prized for its striking foliage and blossoms.
The Dalmatian laburnum blooms in early summer, filling gardens with vibrant yellow flowers.
