consequentialist

noun
ethics based on outcomes
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːnsɪˈkwɛnʃəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsɪˈkwɛnʃəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that the morality of an action depends on its consequences or outcomes

Examples

A consequentialist thinker
As a consequentialist, he argued that the results of an action are what truly matter in ethics.
The philosopher was known as a leading consequentialist in debates about moral theory.
Many consequentialists support policies that produce the greatest good for the greatest number.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deontologist - a person who believes that actions are right or wrong based on rules, not outcomes
  • synonym: utilitarian - a person who believes in maximizing overall happiness or utility
