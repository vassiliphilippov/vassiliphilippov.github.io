revenue stamp

noun
stamp showing tax has been paid
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛv.ə.nju ˌstæmp / (AmE), / ˈrɛv.ən.juː ˌstæmp / (BrE)
a small piece of adhesive paper attached to an object to indicate that a government tax has been paid on it

Examples

A revenue stamp on paper
Collectors often seek rare revenue stamps issued in the nineteenth century.
Every imported bottle carried a revenue stamp to prove the tax had been paid.
He showed the official revenue stamp on the back of the certificate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stamp - a small adhesive label used for postage or revenue
