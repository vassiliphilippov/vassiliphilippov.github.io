noncommissioned

adjective
of military officers not holding a commission
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.kəˈmɪʃ.ənd / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.kəˈmɪʃ.ənd / (BrE)
(of military officers) not holding a commission; appointed from among the enlisted personnel.

Examples

A noncommissioned officer
A noncommissioned officer commands respect due to experience rather than rank.
He joined the army as an enlisted man and later became a noncommissioned officer.
The noncommissioned ranks are critical to maintaining discipline within the unit.
