level

verb
demolish, make flat with the ground
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
to make something flat, even, or smooth; to demolish a structure so it is flat with the ground

Examples

To level a building
A tornado leveled several homes in the town.
The old warehouse was levelled to make space for a new park.
They plan to level the abandoned factory next month.
After heavy rain, the path finally levelled out and became easier to walk on.
If you wait a moment, the bubbles will rise and the surface will level.
The plane began to level as it reached cruising altitude.
After the old building was demolished, the site was leveled to create a parking lot.
She carefully leveled the soil in her garden to prepare for planting.
The workers used heavy machinery to level the ground before construction began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demolish - to pull or knock down a building
  • synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists
  • synonym: even - to make or become flat or smooth
  • synonym: flatten - make or become flat or flatter
