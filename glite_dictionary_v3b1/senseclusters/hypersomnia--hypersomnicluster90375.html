hypersomnia

noun
excessive sleepiness; inability to stay awake
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈsɑːmniə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈsɒmnɪə / (BrE)
A condition characterized by an inability to stay awake or by excessive sleepiness during the day.

Examples

Severe hypersomnia symptoms
Patients with hypersomnia may find it hard to stay awake throughout the day despite getting enough sleep at night.
People affected by hypersomnia often struggle to function in work or social settings because of persistent drowsiness.
The doctor diagnosed her with hypersomnia after she reported feeling excessively sleepy every afternoon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insomnia - inability to sleep
