speculative

adjective
involving high financial risk
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛkjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / spɛkjuˈleɪtɪv / (BrE)
(of an investment or business enterprise) involving a high risk of loss; not financially safe or secure.

Examples

Speculative investment
Buying shares during a market bubble can be incredibly speculative and unpredictable.
He was drawn to speculative ventures, hoping for quick returns despite the risks.
The company’s speculative investments led to large gains but also substantial losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: risky - involving or exposing to danger or loss
