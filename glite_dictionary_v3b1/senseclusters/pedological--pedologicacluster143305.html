pedological

adjective
of soil science
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛdəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpiːdəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the scientific study of soils and their formation

Examples

Pedological soil analysis
The pedological characteristics of the area influence which crops can be grown successfully.
A pedological analysis is essential before starting any large-scale agricultural project.
Researchers conducted a pedological study to understand the soil's ability to retain water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: geological - relating to the study of the Earth's physical structure
