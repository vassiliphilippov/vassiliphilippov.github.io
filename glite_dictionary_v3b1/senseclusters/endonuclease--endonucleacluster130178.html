endonuclease

noun
enzyme that cuts nucleic acids internally
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈnuː.kliˌeɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈnjuː.kli.eɪz / (BrE)
An enzyme that cleaves the internal bonds of nucleic acids, producing fragments of various sizes.

Examples

An endonuclease enzyme
Mutations in the endonuclease gene can impair DNA repair mechanisms.
Restriction endonuclease enzymes are frequently used in genetic engineering research.
The scientist studied how the endonuclease cuts DNA at specific recognition sites.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exonuclease - an enzyme that removes nucleotides one at a time from the end of a polynucleotide chain
  • synonym: nuclease - an enzyme capable of cleaving the phosphodiester bonds between nucleotide subunits of nucleic acids
