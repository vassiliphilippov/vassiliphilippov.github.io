assistant professor

noun
university teacher below associate professor
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪstənt prəˈfɛsər / (AmE), / əˈsɪstənt prəˈfɛsə / (BrE)
a teacher or academic at a college or university who holds a rank lower than an associate professor

Examples

Assistant professor position
After earning his doctorate, he applied for several assistant professor positions across the country.
She was recently promoted to assistant professor in the biology department.
The committee interviewed each assistant professor before making recommendations for tenure.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: associate professor - university teacher ranked above assistant professor and below full professor
  • degree higher: full professor - highest academic rank at a university or college
  • degree lower: lecturer - university teacher, sometimes equivalent to assistant professor in some regions
