linear algebra

noun
branch of mathematics involving linear equations and transformations
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪniɚ ˈældʒəbrə / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə ˈældʒəbrə / (BrE)
The branch of mathematics that deals with the theory and methods of solving linear equations and with linear transformations and matrices.

Examples

Linear algebra theory
He decided to focus his research on linear algebra during his graduate studies.
Many computer graphics algorithms are based on concepts from linear algebra.
Understanding linear algebra is essential for data science and machine learning.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: linear equation - an equation between two variables that gives a straight line when plotted on a graph
  • synonym: matrix - a rectangular array of numbers or expressions used in linear algebra
