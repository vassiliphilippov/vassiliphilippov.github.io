urbanism

noun
city planning principles
5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɜːr.bən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈɜː.bən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the study or practice of designing, planning, and organizing towns and cities; how cities are formed, grow, and function

Examples

Urbanism in city planning
The principles of urbanism influence how cities grow and function.
Modern urbanism focuses on creating walkable neighborhoods and green spaces.
She studied urbanism to help design more sustainable cities in the future.
She decided to study urbanism to better understand the challenges facing modern cities.
The conference on urbanism attracted architects, planners, and sociologists from around the world.
His research in urbanism focuses on how public spaces influence community life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: architecture - the art and practice of designing buildings
  • antonym: ruralism - the way of life or characteristics of the countryside
  • synonym: urban planning - the process of designing and regulating the use of spaces in cities
  • synonym: urbanization - the process by which cities grow or people move to cities
