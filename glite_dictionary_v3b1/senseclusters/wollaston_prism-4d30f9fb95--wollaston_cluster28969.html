Wollaston prism

noun
optical device for polarizing light
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑl əstən ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈwɒl əstən ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
an optical device consisting of two prisms of calcite cemented together, used to produce plane-polarized light

Examples

A Wollaston prism device
A Wollaston prism was used in the experiment to split the light beam into two polarized rays.
Scientists prefer the Wollaston prism for producing plane-polarized light in their studies.
The microscope is equipped with a Wollaston prism for advanced optical analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nicol prism - an optical device for producing polarized light, made from calcite
