mass defect

noun
difference in mass of nucleus vs. summed particles
4.7 5.9
Pronunciation: /mæs ˈdef.ekt/ (AmE), /mɑːs ˈdef.ekt/ (BrE)
the difference between the mass of an atomic nucleus and the sum of the masses of its constituent protons and neutrons

Examples

The mass defect value
A large mass defect indicates a stable nucleus with significant binding energy.
Scientists can calculate nuclear mass defects using precise measurements of atomic masses.
The mass defect explains why atoms release energy during nuclear reactions.
