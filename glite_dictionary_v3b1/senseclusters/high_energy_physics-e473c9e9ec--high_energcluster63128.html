high energy physics

noun
field studying subatomic particles and forces
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ ˈɛnɚdʒi ˈfɪzɪks / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ ˈɛnədʒi ˈfɪzɪks / (BrE)
The branch of physics that studies subatomic particles and the forces by which they interact, often involving particle accelerators and high collision energies.

Examples

High energy physics experiments
Many groundbreaking discoveries in high energy physics have come from experiments at particle accelerators.
She decided to pursue a PhD in high energy physics to study the fundamental forces of nature.
The conference attracted leading experts in high energy physics from around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: particle physics - The branch of physics that studies the elementary constituents of matter and radiation
