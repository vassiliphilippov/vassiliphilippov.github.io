inlining

noun
integrating into process
8.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈlaɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ˈɪn.laɪ.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈlaɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ˈɪn.laɪ.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
the integration of a process or check directly into a production or processing sequence rather than handling it separately

Examples

Inlining quality checks
The company improved efficiency by inlining quality inspections within the assembly process.
By inlining safety checks, the factory reduced the number of defective products reaching the end of the line.
Modern manufacturing often uses inlining to ensure problems are detected and fixed immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: integration - the act of combining parts into a whole
  • antonym: separation - the act of keeping things apart or handling them individually
