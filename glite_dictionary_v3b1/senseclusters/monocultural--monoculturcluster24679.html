monocultural

adjective
single culture
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkʌltʃərəl / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkʌltʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a single culture or way of life

Examples

Monocultural society
The company had a monocultural workforce, with little diversity among its employees.
Critics argue that a monocultural society can limit creativity and innovation.
The region's monocultural traditions have remained unchanged for generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homogeneous - consisting of parts or people that are all the same kind
  • antonym: multicultural - relating to or including several different cultures
