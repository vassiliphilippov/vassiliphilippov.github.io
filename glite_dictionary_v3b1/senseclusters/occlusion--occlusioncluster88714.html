occlusion

noun
meteorological front where cold air lifts warm air
5.9
Pronunciation: / əˈkluːʒən / (AmE), / əˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
(Meteorology) A weather front that forms when colder air surrounds and lifts a mass of warm air above the ground.

Examples

Weather occlusion
During the occlusion, warm air was forced aloft by advancing cold air on all sides.
Meteorologists tracked the development of the occlusion as it moved across the continent.
The storm system weakened after the occlusion formed, reducing the risk of severe weather.
