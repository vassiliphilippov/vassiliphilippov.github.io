system-wide

adjective, adverb
affecting whole system
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstəmˌwaɪd /; / ˈsɪstəm waɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəmˌwaɪd /; / ˈsɪstəm waɪd / (BrE)
affecting or involving the whole system, not just a part (used as an adjective or adverb)

Examples

System-wide changes
The new security policy will have system-wide implications for all employees.
A system-wide update was required to fix the software vulnerabilities.
Management implemented system-wide changes to improve overall efficiency.
The company implemented the new policy system-wide to ensure consistency across all departments.
After the update, the software changes were applied system-wide without any manual intervention.
Security protocols were strengthened system-wide following the recent data breach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • antonym: locally - in a limited area or part of a system
  • antonym: partial - affecting only a part, not the whole
  • synonym: universally - in a way that applies everywhere or to everyone
