reinitiation

noun
restart of a process
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˌnɪsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of starting something again, especially the restarting of protein synthesis by a ribosome on the same mRNA after a previous round of translation

Examples

Reinitiation of translation
The scientist studied reinitiation of translation in the presence of specific regulatory proteins.
After the first round of protein synthesis, reinitiation can occur on the same mRNA strand.
Understanding reinitiation is important for developing new treatments in molecular biology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: initiation - the act of beginning or starting something
  • antonym: termination - the act of ending or finishing something
