buckler fern

noun
a group of shield-covered ferns
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌk.lɚ fɝn / (AmE), / ˈbʌk.lə fɜːn / (BrE)
any of several types of ferns belonging to the genera Dryopteris, Polystichum, or Lastreopsis, distinguished by their somewhat shield-shaped coverings on the sori.

Examples

The buckler fern plant
Gardeners often plant buckler fern to add lush greenery to shaded areas.
The unique shield-like covering of the sori distinguishes the buckler fern from other species.
The woodland was full of buckler ferns thriving in the damp shade.
