Corinthian

adjective
of the Corinthian style of architecture
8.2 7.7
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈrɪn.θiː.ən/ /; / /kə.ˈrɪn.θɪ.ən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrɪn.θi.ən/ /; / /kə.ˈrɪn.θɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to or denoting the Corinthian order, a classical style of architecture characterized by slender columns with elaborate capitals decorated with acanthus leaves.

Examples

Corinthian columns
Many architects admire the delicate details of Corinthian capitals in classical buildings.
The courthouse was built in the Corinthian style, showcasing ornate columns and intricate carvings.
The museum's entrance features towering Corinthian columns that recall the grandeur of ancient Greece.
