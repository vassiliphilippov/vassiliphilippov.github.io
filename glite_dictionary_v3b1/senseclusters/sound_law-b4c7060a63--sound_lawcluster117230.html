sound law

noun
historical linguistics rule for sound change
7.7
Pronunciation: / saʊnd lɔ / (AmE), / saʊnd lɔː / (BrE)
a principle or rule describing systematic changes in speech sounds during the historical development of a language or languages

Examples

A sound law principle
Grimm's Law is a well-known sound law that explains some changes in Germanic languages.
Linguists study sound laws to understand how languages evolve over time.
The Great Vowel Shift is a famous example of a sound law in English.
