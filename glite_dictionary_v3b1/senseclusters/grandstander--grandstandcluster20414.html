grandstander

noun
person who seeks applause by showing off
8.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈgrænd.stænd.ər/ /; / /ˈgrænd.stən.dər/ (AmE), /ˈgrænd.stænd.ə/ /; / /ˈgrænd.stən.də/ (BrE)
a person who seeks attention or applause by making a showy or ostentatious display, especially in public situations

Examples

A grandstander in public
She doesn't want to be seen as a grandstander when she volunteers for every project.
The debate turned sour when one candidate acted like a grandstander to impress the crowd.
The mayor was accused of being a grandstander after making a dramatic speech at the rally.

  • synonym: show-off - a person who acts in a way intended to attract attention or admiration
