scour

noun
area eroded by running water
Pronunciation: / ˈskaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈskaʊə / (BrE)
an area that has been eroded or hollowed out, especially by the action of running water

Examples

A river scour
After the heavy rain, a deep scour formed at the base of the hillside.
Engineers studied the scour around the bridge piers to assess stability.
Fish often gather in the scours below river bends, where the water runs fastest.
