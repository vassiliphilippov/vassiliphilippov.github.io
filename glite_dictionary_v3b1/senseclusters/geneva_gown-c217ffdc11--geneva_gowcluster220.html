Geneva gown

noun
robe worn by Protestant clergy
5.6
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnivə ɡaʊn / (AmE), / dʒɪˈniːvə ɡaʊn / (BrE)
a black academic robe traditionally worn by Protestant clergy, especially in Reformed churches

Examples

A black Geneva gown
During the ceremony, she wore a simple black Geneva gown in keeping with church tradition.
Many seminaries still require students to wear the traditional Geneva gown at graduation.
The minister donned his Geneva gown before the Sunday service.
