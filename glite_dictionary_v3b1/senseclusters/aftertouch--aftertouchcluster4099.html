aftertouch

noun
keyboard pressure feature
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈæftɚˌtʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɑːftətʌtʃ /; / ˈɑːftəˌtʌtʃ / (BrE)
a feature on electronic keyboards that detects and responds to pressure applied to a key after it is pressed, allowing for more expressive playing or control of sound effects

Examples

Aftertouch feature
The new synthesizer offers aftertouch for more expressive playing.
By using aftertouch, musicians can add vibrato or volume changes to their notes.
Some keyboards lack aftertouch, limiting the range of sound control available to performers.
The synthesizer responds to aftertouch, allowing musicians to add vibrato by pressing harder on the keys.
He customized the aftertouch settings to control the volume of his electronic piano.
Many modern keyboards feature aftertouch for more expressive performances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modulation - the process of changing sound parameters in electronic music
  • synonym: velocity - the speed or force with which a key is struck on a keyboard
  • synonym: velocity sensitivity - a keyboard feature that responds to how quickly a key is pressed
