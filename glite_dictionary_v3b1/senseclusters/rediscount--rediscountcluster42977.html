rediscount

noun
discounted financial instrument
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (BrE)
a bill, note, or other financial instrument that has been discounted again by a bank, usually after being discounted by another institution

Examples

Rediscount bill
The central bank offered a rediscount facility to help commercial banks manage liquidity.
After the initial sale, the note became a rediscount when it was sold to another bank.
Banks sometimes use a rediscount to obtain funds quickly by selling previously discounted paper.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discount - a reduction in the value of a bill or note before its maturity
