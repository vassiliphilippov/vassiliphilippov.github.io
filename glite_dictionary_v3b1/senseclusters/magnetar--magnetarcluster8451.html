magnetar

noun
highly magnetic neutron star
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡnɪˌtɑr / (AmE), / ˈmæɡnɪˌtɑː / (BrE)
a type of neutron star with an extremely strong magnetic field, often emitting bursts of X-rays and gamma rays

Examples

A magnetar star
Scientists observed a magnetar releasing a sudden burst of gamma rays in a distant galaxy.
The intense magnetic field of a magnetar can disrupt nearby electronic equipment in space.
Astronomers believe that only a small fraction of neutron stars become magnetars.
Scientists detected a powerful burst of X-rays from a distant magnetar in our galaxy.
A magnetar can have a magnetic field a thousand times stronger than a typical neutron star.
The sudden flare from the magnetar surprised astronomers monitoring the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neutron star - a very dense star formed after a supernova, made mostly of neutrons
  • synonym: pulsar - a rotating neutron star that emits regular pulses of radiation
