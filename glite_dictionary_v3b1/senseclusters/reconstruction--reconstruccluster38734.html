reconstruction

noun
the act of rebuilding
4.2 5.15/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê.k…ônÀàstr åk. É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê.k…ônÀàstr åk. É…ôn / (BrE)
the act or process of building something again after it has been damaged or destroyed

Examples

Post-war reconstruction
After the earthquake, reconstruction efforts focused on rebuilding homes and schools.
The reconstruction of the museum took nearly two years to complete.
The city began the reconstruction of the historic bridge after the flood.

Synonyms

  • rebuilding
    3.0 3.34/m
    rebuilding is the process of building something again after damage, while reconstruction is a planned, often technical rebuilding of form and function.
  • restoration
    3.4 14/m
    restoration is the act of returning something to its original state, while reconstruction means rebuilding structure after serious damage.
  • re-creation
    4.8 0.00/m
    re-creation is creating something again, often as a new copy or idea, while reconstruction focuses on rebuilding the original object or place.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restoration - the action of returning something to a former condition
