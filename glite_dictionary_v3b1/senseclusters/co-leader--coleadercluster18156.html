co-leader

noun
joint leader
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌliːdər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌliːdə / (BrE)
a person who shares leadership responsibilities equally with one or more others

Examples

A co-leader of the team
As a co-leader, he worked closely with his partner to guide the team.
The club elected two co-leaders to ensure fair decision-making.
Each co-leader brought unique strengths to the organization.
After the third round, she became a co-leader in the golf tournament.
The two scientists acted as co-leaders of the research project.
Both teams finished with the same score and were named co-leaders of the league.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follower - a person who supports and is guided by another
  • synonym: leader - a person who leads a group or organization
