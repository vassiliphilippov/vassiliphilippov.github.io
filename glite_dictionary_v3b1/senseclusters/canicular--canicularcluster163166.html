canicular

adjective
relating to the Dog Star or hottest summer days
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɪkjələr / (AmE), / kəˈnɪkjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to the hottest period of summer, known as the dog days

Examples

The canicular period
Ancient Romans associated the canicular period with increased fevers and storms.
The festival was scheduled for the canicular part of the summer, when Sirius appeared in the dawn sky.
The oppressive heat during the canicular days made it difficult to sleep at night.
The canicular sun beat down relentlessly on the parched fields.
We sought shelter from the canicular afternoons by staying indoors.
Writers often describe the mood of August as slow and heavy with canicular heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dog days - the hottest period of the year, traditionally associated with the rising of Sirius
