infrasound

noun
low-frequency sound
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnfrəˌsaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɪnfrəsaʊnd / (BrE)
sound waves with frequencies below the lower limit of human hearing, typically below 20 hertz

Examples

Infrasound waves
Some animals, like elephants, use infrasound to communicate over long distances.
Scientists study infrasound to detect volcanic eruptions and earthquakes.
The storm produced powerful infrasound waves that were recorded by special sensors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sound - vibrations that travel through air or another medium and can be heard
  • antonym: ultrasound - sound waves with frequencies above the range of human hearing
