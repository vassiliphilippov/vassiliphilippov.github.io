erythropoiesis

noun
production of red blood cells
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˌrɪθroʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs / (AmE), / ɪˌrɪθrəʊpɔɪˈiːsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which red blood cells are produced, especially in the bone marrow

Examples

Erythropoiesis in bone marrow

The hormone erythropoietin plays a crucial role in regulating erythropoiesis.
