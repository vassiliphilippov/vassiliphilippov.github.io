squabby

adjective
short and fat; squat
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɑːbi / (AmE), / ˈskwɒbi / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) short and fat; squat in form.

Examples

A squabby person
His squabby frame allowed him to squeeze into tight spaces with surprising agility.
She described the man as squabby, with a round face and stubby legs.
The squabby puppy waddled happily after its mother.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slender - thin in an attractive or healthy way
  • synonym: squat - short and thickset; low in height
