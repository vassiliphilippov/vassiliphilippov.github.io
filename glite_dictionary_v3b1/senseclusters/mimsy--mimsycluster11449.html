mimsy

adjective
whimsical or feeble
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪmzi / (AmE), / ˈmɪmzi / (BrE)
whimsical, feeble, or prim in a delicate or fanciful way, especially as used in literary contexts

Examples

Mimsy flowers
The mimsy flowers seemed almost too delicate for the wild garden.
In the story, a mimsy child wandered through the enchanted forest.
Her mimsy manner made her stand out among the bold personalities at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feeble - lacking strength or force
  • antonym: robust - strong and healthy
  • synonym: whimsical - playfully quaint or fanciful
