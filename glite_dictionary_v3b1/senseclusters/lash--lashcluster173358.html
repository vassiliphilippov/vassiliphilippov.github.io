lash

verb
move or swing violently, especially a tail
7.9
Pronunciation: / læʃ / (AmE), / læʃ / (BrE)
to move or swing something quickly and violently from side to side or back and forth, especially as a sign of agitation or excitement

Examples

To lash a rope
She lashed the rope in frustration, sending the end flying through the air.
The lion lashed its tail in warning before charging.
Waves lashed against the rocks during the storm, spraying seawater everywhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flick - to make a sudden, quick movement
  • synonym: whip - to move or cause to move rapidly and forcefully
