on one's guard

adjective
be vigilant or cautious
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɑn wʌnz ɡɑrd / (AmE), / ɒn wʌnz ɡɑːd / (BrE)
be alert and ready to deal with possible danger or problems; vigilant or cautious.

Examples

On her guard always
She was always on her guard when dealing with unfamiliar situations.
The security team remained on their guard throughout the event.
You have to stay on your guard when traveling alone in a new city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to danger or problems
  • antonym: off one's guard - not being watchful or vigilant
