relent

verb
cease resisting; give in
5.8 2.43/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªËˆlÉ›nt / (AmE), / rÉªËˆlÉ›nt / (BrE)
to cease resisting or to give in to influence or pressure after initially refusing

Examples

To relent in pressure
After hours of negotiations, the manager finally relented and agreed to the employees' demands.
She refused to let her son stay up late, but eventually she relented when he promised to wake up early.
Though the weather was bad, the storm did not relent until the following morning.
