burn rate

noun
rate tokens are destroyed
7.4
Pronunciation: /bɜːrn reɪt/ (AmE), /bɜːn reɪt/ (BrE)
(in cryptocurrency and blockchain contexts) the rate at which tokens are intentionally destroyed or removed from circulation, typically measured over a set time period.

Examples

Token burn rate
The team published the monthly burn rate showing how many tokens were permanently removed.
Investors watch the token's burn rate to judge whether scarcity will increase market value.
After the hard fork, developers adjusted the protocol's burn rate to slow token deflation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: issuance rate - the rate at which new tokens are created and added to circulation (opposite effect on supply)
  • synonym: token burn - the act of permanently removing tokens from circulation by sending them to an unrecoverable address
