bargain

noun
something bought cheaply; good deal
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑɹɡən / (AmE), / ˈbɑːɡɪn / (BrE)
something bought or offered for sale at a price lower than its true value; an advantageous purchase.

Examples

A bargain price
At the flea market, he considered the vintage lamp a real bargain for only five dollars.
I found a fantastic bargain on winter coats during the end-of-season sale.
She always looks for a bargain when shopping online for electronics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deal - an agreement or arrangement, especially one that is advantageous
