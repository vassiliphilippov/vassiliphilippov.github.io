stubbornly

adverb
in a stubborn manner
3.7 3.19/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÊŒbÉ™rnli / (AmE), / ËˆstÊŒbÉ™nli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows refusal to change one's attitude or position, despite good reasons to do so.

Examples

To stubbornly refuse
She stubbornly refused to apologize, even after realizing her mistake.
The old dog stubbornly stayed outside in the rain despite repeated calls.
They stubbornly clung to their beliefs, unwilling to consider new evidence.

Synonyms

  • obstinately
    7.2 0.34/m
    obstinately refuses to change an opinion or action, while stubbornly describes refusing despite good reasons to change.
  • obdurately
    9.1 0.07/m
    obdurately holds a fixed, often harsh position, while stubbornly means refusing to change one's mind.
  • intransigently
    8.2 0.01/m
    intransigently refuses to compromise in talks or negotiations, while stubbornly simply means not changing one's mind.
  • uncompromisingly
    7.5 0.20/m
    uncompromisingly sticks to demands or rules without making concessions, while stubbornly refuses to change one's opinion.
  • mulishly
    9.3 0.02/m
    mulishly acts like a mule, refusing to change even when it harms them, while stubbornly means refusing despite reasons.
  • pig-headedly
    8.1 0.00/m
    pig-headedly sounds rude and insulting, showing strong unreasonable stubbornness in speech, while stubbornly is more neutral and general.
