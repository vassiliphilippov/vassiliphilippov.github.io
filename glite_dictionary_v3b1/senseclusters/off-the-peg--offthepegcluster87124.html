(especially of clothing) made in standard sizes and available ready to buy, rather than made to measure.

Examples

An off-the-peg suit
His suit looked expensive, though it was actually off-the-peg from a high street retailer.
Many people choose off-the-peg clothing for its convenience.
She prefers to buy off-the-peg dresses because they are quicker and more affordable than bespoke options.

Synonyms

  • ready-to-wear
    3.9 0.02/m
    ready-to-wear is mass-produced clothing sold in standard sizes from shops, while off-the-peg is the British term for the same idea.
  • off-the-rack
    4.4 0.36/m
    off-the-rack is the common American phrase for clothes sold from stock in set sizes, while off-the-peg is used mainly in British English.
  • off-the-shelf
    4.1 0.31/m
    off-the-shelf describes ready-made products sold from stock in many stores, while off-the-peg refers specifically to ready-made clothing.
