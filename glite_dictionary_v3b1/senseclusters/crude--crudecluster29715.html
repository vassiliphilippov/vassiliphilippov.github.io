crude

adjective
roughly or simply made
4.6 4.78/m
Pronunciation: / kruÀêd / (AmE), / kruÀêd / (BrE)
made in a simple or rough way, not showing skill or careful construction

Examples

Crude tool
He fashioned a crude tool from a piece of stone and some twine.
The shelter they built was a crude structure of branches and leaves.
They used a crude bench made from a wooden plank balanced on rocks.

Synonyms

  • coarse
    4.2 1.52/m
    coarse is rough and shows little refinement in style or texture, while crude focuses on poor construction or lack of skill.
  • rough-and-ready
    6.6 0.08/m
    rough-and-ready is simple and not finely finished yet still useful in practice, while crude suggests poor skill or care.
  • hacky
    8.5 0.10/m
    hacky is made quickly in a makeshift way and sounds informal, while crude often refers to poor build or lack of skill.
  • rough
    1.0 14/m
    rough means not smooth or even in surface or finish, while crude points to poor skill or careless construction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finished - made with careful and skillful workmanship
  • synonym: rough - not finished with care or precision
