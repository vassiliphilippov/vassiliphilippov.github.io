lumpishly

adverb
awkwardly or heavily
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌmpɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈlʌmpɪʃli / (BrE)
in a dull, heavy, slow, or awkward way (physically or mentally)

Examples

Move lumpishly
He walked lumpishly across the room, dragging his feet with every step.
After the long flight, she moved lumpishly through the airport, exhausted and slow.
The dog sat lumpishly by the door, uninterested in playing outside.
He answered the teacher's question lumpishly, barely making sense.
The dog moved lumpishly after waking from a long nap.
She sat lumpishly in the corner, uninterested in the lively conversation around her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alertly - in a quick and attentive manner
  • synonym: awkwardly - in a way that is not graceful or is uncomfortable
  • synonym: clumsily - in a way that lacks grace or skill
  • antonym: gracefully - in an elegant or smooth manner
