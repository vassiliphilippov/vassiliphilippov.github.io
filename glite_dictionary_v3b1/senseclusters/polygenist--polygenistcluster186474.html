polygenist

adjective
separate human origins
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːlɪˈdʒɛnɪst / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdʒɛnɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the theory that humans or groups have separate origins rather than a single origin

Examples

Polygenist theory
The scientist was known for his polygenist views on the origins of different human populations.
In the nineteenth century, some scholars promoted polygenist theories to explain human diversity.
Modern genetics has largely discredited polygenist ideas about separate human origins.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monogenist - relating to the theory that all humans have a single origin
