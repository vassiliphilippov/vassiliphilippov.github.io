mux

noun
signal combiner device
9.5
Pronunciation: / mʌks / (AmE), / mʌks / (BrE)
a device or process in electronics that combines several input signals into one output, or selects one input to send to the output

Examples

A video mux
The technician used a mux to combine several video feeds into one cable.
In digital communications, a mux is essential for efficient data transmission.
After troubleshooting, they discovered the mux was causing the signal loss.
The engineer replaced the faulty mux to restore the signal path in the circuit.
A single mux can greatly simplify the wiring in complex electronic systems.
When designing the board, they chose a four-input mux for flexibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demultiplexer - a device that separates one input signal into multiple outputs
  • antonym: demux - a device that separates a single input signal into multiple output signals
  • synonym: multiplexer - a device that selects one of several input signals and forwards it to a single output
