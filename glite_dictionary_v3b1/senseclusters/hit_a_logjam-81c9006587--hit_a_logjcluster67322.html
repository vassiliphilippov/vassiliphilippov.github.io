hit a logjam

verb
reach a deadlock or impasse
Pronunciation: / hɪt ə ˈlɔɡˌdʒæm / (AmE), / hɪt ə ˈlɒɡˌdʒæm / (BrE)
to encounter a situation in which progress stops because of a disagreement or obstacle; to reach an impasse or deadlock

Examples

To hit a logjam suddenly
After hours of discussion, the project finally hit a logjam and progress stopped.
Negotiations hit a logjam when neither side was willing to compromise.
Talks over the new contract hit a logjam last week due to unresolved differences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: break the deadlock - to resolve a situation in which no progress is possible
  • synonym: reach a stalemate - to get to a situation where no progress can be made
