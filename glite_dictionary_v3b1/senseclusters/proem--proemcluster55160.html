proem

noun
introductory poem
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈproʊ.əm/ (AmE), /ˈprəʊ.əm/ (BrE)
an introduction or preface, especially at the beginning of a book, poem, or speech

Examples

A proem to a book
The ancient manuscript began with a proem that introduced the main themes of the story.
Before the epic unfolded, the poet delivered a proem to set the tone for the audience.
In her collection, each section opens with a brief proem reflecting on the poems that follow.
The poet wrote a thoughtful proem to set the tone for the collection.
Before the main text, the historian included a brief proem explaining his research methods.
The speaker began with a proem to introduce the topic to the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epilogue - a section at the end of a book or play that comments on or concludes the story
  • synonym: introduction - the opening part of a book, speech, or event
  • synonym: preface - an introduction to a book or other written work
