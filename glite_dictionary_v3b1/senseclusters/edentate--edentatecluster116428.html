edentate

noun
a mammal with few or no teeth (e.g., anteaters, sloths)
Pronunciation: / iˈdɛnˌteɪt / (AmE), / iːˈdɛn.teɪt / (BrE)
a primitive mammal with few or no teeth, formerly classified in the order Edentata, native to Central and South America, such as armadillos, anteaters, and sloths

Examples

An edentate mammal
Despite being called edentates, some species like armadillos still have simple, peg-like teeth.
Early naturalists classified armadillos as edentates due to their reduced teeth.
The giant anteater is an edentate native to Central and South America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: toothless - lacking teeth
