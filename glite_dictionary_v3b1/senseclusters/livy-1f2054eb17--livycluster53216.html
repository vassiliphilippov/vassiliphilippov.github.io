Livy

proper noun
Roman historian (c. 59 BC–AD 17)
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪvi / (AmE), / ˈlɪvi / (BrE)
(c. 59 BC–AD 17) A Roman historian renowned for his monumental history of Rome, 'Ab Urbe Condita', of which only 35 of the original 142 books survive.

Examples

Livy Roman historian
Although much of Livy's work is lost, what remains is invaluable to classical scholarship.
Many modern historians rely on Livy's accounts for information about early Roman history.
The war with Hannibal was extensively documented by Livy in his famous writings.
