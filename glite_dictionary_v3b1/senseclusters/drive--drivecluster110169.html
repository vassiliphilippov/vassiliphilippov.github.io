drive

verb
hit a golf ball with a driver club
Pronunciation: / draɪv / (AmE), / draɪv / (BrE)
to hit or strike a ball forcefully, especially in sports

Examples

To drive the ball
After missing the green, he managed to drive the next shot with perfect technique.
He can drive the ball over 300 yards with ease.
She practiced how to drive straight down the fairway every morning.
He drove the ball down the fairway with a powerful swing.
She expertly drove the tennis ball past her opponent with a quick forehand shot.
The batter drove the pitch over the center-field fence for a home run.
She managed to drive the ball over the fence with a single swing.
The golfer drove the ball straight down the fairway.
With one swift kick, he drove the puck into the goal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hit - to strike with sudden force
  • synonym: smash - hit (a ball) very hard, usually downward, in sports like tennis and badminton
  • antonym: tap - hit (a ball) lightly or gently
  • synonym: tee off - to hit a golf ball from the tee at the beginning of a hole
  • synonym: throw - to propel through the air by a movement of the arm and hand
