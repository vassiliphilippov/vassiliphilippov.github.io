margin

noun
blank space around text on a page
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
the blank space at the edge of a page or area, or an empty area used as a buffer

Examples

Page margin
He wrote a question in the margin of his notebook.
Please leave a wide margin when formatting your essay.
Small notes were scribbled in the margins of the textbook pages.
Please leave a margin of at least one inch on all sides of the paper.
The document requires a wider margin for binding.
There was not enough margin between the painting and the frame.

Related Terms

  • synonym: border - the edge or boundary of something
