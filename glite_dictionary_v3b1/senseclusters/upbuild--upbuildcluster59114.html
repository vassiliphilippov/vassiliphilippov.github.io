upbuild

verb
to strengthen morally
Pronunciation: / ˌʌpˈbɪld / (AmE), / ˌʌpˈbɪld / (BrE)
to build up or strengthen, especially in a moral, spiritual, or emotional way

Examples

To upbuild confidence
The teacher worked hard to upbuild her students' confidence throughout the year.
Community leaders aim to upbuild trust among residents after the recent challenges.
His words were meant to upbuild the team's spirit before the big game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • antonym: undermine - to weaken or damage something or someone
  • synonym: uplift - to improve the moral or spiritual condition of someone
