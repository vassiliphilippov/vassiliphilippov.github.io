overshadow

verb
to be more important than something else
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
to make someone or something seem less important, successful, or impressive by comparison

Examples

Her success overshadows others
Her talent tends to overshadow that of her peers.
The argument overshadowed what was supposed to be a joyful reunion.
The scandal managed to overshadow all the company's recent achievements.
Despite her achievements, her brother's fame tended to overshadow her.
His generosity can sometimes overshadow his flaws.
The excitement of the festival was overshadowed by reports of bad weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dwarf - to cause to seem small or unimportant by comparison
  • synonym: eclipse - to make something less important or noticeable by comparison
  • antonym: highlight - to make something more prominent or important
