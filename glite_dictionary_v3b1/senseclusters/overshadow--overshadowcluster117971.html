Examples

Synonyms

  • shadow
    5.3 0.60/m
    shadow is used to make someone seem less important by comparison, while overshadow often implies a stronger, lasting loss of attention or praise.
  • eclipse
    6.0 3.04/m
    eclipse means making someone seem much less impressive by comparison, while overshadow is more general about losing importance or notice.
  • dwarf
    4.9 3.87/m
    dwarf makes someone seem very small or weak by comparison, while overshadow describes something simply becoming less important.
  • outshine
    4.8 0.93/m
    outshine means being more impressive or brighter than others, while overshadow focuses on making someone seem less important by comparison.
  • upstage
    6.5 0.81/m
    upstage means stealing attention, often deliberately and performatively, while overshadow covers any cause of making someone seem less important.
