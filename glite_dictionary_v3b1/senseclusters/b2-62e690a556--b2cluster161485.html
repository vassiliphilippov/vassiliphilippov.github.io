B2

noun
CEFR upper-intermediate level
3.9
Pronunciation: / bi ˈtu / (AmE), / biː ˈtuː / (BrE)
A CEFR level indicating upper-intermediate proficiency in a language (especially English), showing the ability to understand complex texts, communicate fluently, and produce clear, detailed text on many subjects.

Examples

B2 language level
She achieved a B2 level in English after completing the language program.
The language school requires applicants to demonstrate B2 competency before enrolling in advanced courses.
Employers often list B2 as the minimum English requirement for customer-facing positions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: B1 - CEFR level below B2 (intermediate)
  • degree higher: C1 - CEFR level above B2 (advanced)
  • synonym: upper-intermediate - a proficiency band between intermediate and advanced; similar general level to CEFR B2
