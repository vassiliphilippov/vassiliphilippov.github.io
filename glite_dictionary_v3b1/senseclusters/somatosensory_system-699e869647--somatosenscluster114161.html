somatosensory system

noun
bodily sensory system
5.7 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊmətoʊˈsɛnsəri ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌsəʊmətəʊˈsɛnsəri ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
the part of the nervous system responsible for processing sensory information from the body, including touch, temperature, pain, and proprioception

Examples

The somatosensory system function
Damage to the somatosensory system can result in loss of sensation in certain body parts.
Scientists study the somatosensory system to better understand how the brain interprets bodily signals.
The somatosensory system enables people to feel physical sensations like touch and pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of one's own body parts
