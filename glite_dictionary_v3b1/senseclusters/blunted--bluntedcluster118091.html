blunted

adjective
made dull, less sharp or intense
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌntɪd / (AmE), / ˈblʌntɪd / (BrE)
made less sharp, intense, or effective; having the edge or point made dull or blunt.

Examples

A blunted feeling
His sense of excitement was blunted by the disappointing news.
The surgeon used a blunted needle to minimize the risk of injury.
Years of heavy smoking had blunted his sense of taste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dull - lacking sharpness or edge
  • antonym: sharp - having a fine edge or point
