ethnobotany

noun
study of plant use by cultures
8.3
Pronunciation: /eθ.noʊ.ˈbɑː.tə.niː/ (AmE), /eθ.nə.ˈbɒ.tə.niː/ (BrE)
the scientific study of how people from different cultures use and understand plants

Examples

Ethnobotany research
The professor specializes in ethnobotany, researching how indigenous groups use local plants for healing.
A course in ethnobotany explores the relationship between people and the plants they depend on.
Her interest in ethnobotany began after learning about traditional herbal remedies in rural communities.
She decided to study ethnobotany to learn how different cultures use medicinal plants.
The museum's new exhibit on ethnobotany highlights traditional plant knowledge from around the world.
Researchers in ethnobotany often work closely with indigenous communities to document plant uses.
