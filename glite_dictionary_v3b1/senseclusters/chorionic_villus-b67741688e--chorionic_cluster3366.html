chorionic villus

noun
tiny projection forming part of the placenta
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔriˈɑnɪk ˈvɪləs / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈɒnɪk ˈvɪləs / (BrE)
any of the small, finger-like projections on the fetal chorion that combine with maternal uterine tissue to form the placenta

Examples

A chorionic villus sample
During early pregnancy, a chorionic villus can be sampled to test for genetic disorders.
Each chorionic villus enables nutrient exchange between the mother and developing fetus.
The growth of a chorionic villus is critical for proper placental development.
