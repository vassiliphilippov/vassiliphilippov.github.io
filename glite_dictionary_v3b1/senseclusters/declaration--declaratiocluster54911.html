declaration

noun
emphatic or explicit statement
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a formal or official statement, especially one made publicly or agreed upon by a group

Examples

Public declaration
He issued a formal declaration at the press conference.
Her declaration of innocence was both firm and convincing.
The government made a declaration of emergency to address the crisis.
Her declaration of innocence was convincing to everyone in the courtroom.
The government issued a declaration regarding the new environmental policy.
The host made a formal declaration of the competition results.
After lengthy debate, the assembly adopted a declaration supporting climate action.
The United Nations issued a declaration on human rights in 1948.
The committee's declaration was circulated to all members for their approval.

Related Terms

  • synonym: announcement - a public or formal statement about something
  • antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true
  • synonym: proclamation - a formal public statement
