Synonyms

  • vitreous
    8.6 0.04/m
    vitreous sounds technical and describes a hard, glass-like finish often used for ceramics, while glassy is a general visual quality.
  • glassine
    8.5 0.05/m
    glassine is smooth, glossy, and slightly translucent, often used for paper or film, while glassy means simply like glass.
  • glazed
    3.5 0.01/m
    glazed has a coated, shiny surface from a glaze usually on pottery or tiles, while glassy means naturally glass-like.
  • vitrified
    8.6 0.01/m
    vitrified means fused by heat to form a hard, nonporous layer often on ceramics, while glassy simply means like glass in appearance.
  • glossy
    2.6 4.57/m
    glossy looks smooth and highly reflective on many materials like paper or leather, while glassy suggests a glass-like surface.
  • shiny
    1.0 18/m
