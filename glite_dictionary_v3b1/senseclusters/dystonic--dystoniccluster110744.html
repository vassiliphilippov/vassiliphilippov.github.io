dystonic

adjective
not harmonious
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
not harmonious or coordinated; marked by conflict, awkwardness, or lack of agreement between parts.

Examples

Dystonic teamwork
The team's dystonic approach led to confusion and overlapping responsibilities.
Her dystonic ideas clashed with the rest of the group's vision for the project.
A dystonic atmosphere developed as members disagreed on every major decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discordant - lacking harmony or agreement
  • antonym: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole
