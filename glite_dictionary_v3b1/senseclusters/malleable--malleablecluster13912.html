malleable

adjective
easily influenced or changed
3.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmæ.liː.ə.bəl/ /; / /ˈmæl.ɪ.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈmæ.lɪ.ə.bəl/ /; / /ˈmæ.liː.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a person or their character) easily influenced or changed; adaptable.

Examples

Malleable mind
Children are often malleable and can easily absorb new ideas from their environment.
Her opinions are quite malleable, changing depending on who she's talking to.
The media can play a powerful role in shaping the malleable minds of the public.
