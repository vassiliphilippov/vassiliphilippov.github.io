question

verb
express doubt or uncertainty
1.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkwes.tʃən/ /; / /ˈkwes.tjən/ /; / /ˈkweʃ.tən/ /; / /ˈkweʃ.tʃən/ (AmE), /ˈkwes.tʃən/ /; / /ˈkwes.tjən/ /; / /ˈkweʃ.tən/ /; / /ˈkweʃ.tʃən/ (BrE)
to express doubt about the truth, validity, or quality of something; to challenge or dispute

Examples

To question a decision
It is natural to question your abilities before taking on a new challenge.
Many people question whether the policy will actually work as intended.
She began to question the motives behind his sudden generosity.
Her motives were questioned by her colleagues after the decision was announced.
It is important to question assumptions rather than accept them blindly.
The board began to question the CEO's explanation after inconsistencies emerged.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to agree with or believe something without dispute
  • synonym: challenge - to dispute the truth or validity of something
  • synonym: doubt - to feel uncertain about something
  • antonym: trust - to believe in the reliability or truth of something
