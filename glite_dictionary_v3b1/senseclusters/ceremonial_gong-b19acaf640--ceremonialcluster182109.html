ceremonial gong

noun
gong used in ceremonies or rituals
5.7
Pronunciation: / sɛˌrəˈmoʊniəl ɡɑŋ / (AmE), / sɛˌrɪˈməʊniəl ɡɒŋ / (BrE)
a large percussion instrument, typically a metal disc, used in formal or ritual ceremonies to mark important moments or transitions

Examples

The ceremonial gong struck
During the opening of the temple festival, the ceremonial gong was struck to announce the beginning of the rituals.
In many cultures, a ceremonial gong is played to bring good luck at weddings.
The sound of the ceremonial gong echoed through the hall as the graduates entered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gong - a large metal disc that produces a resonant sound when struck
