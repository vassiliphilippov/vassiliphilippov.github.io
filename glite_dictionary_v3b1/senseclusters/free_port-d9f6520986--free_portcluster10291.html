free port

noun
duty-free area in a port for reshipment
2.0
Pronunciation: / fri pɔrt / (AmE), / friː pɔːt / (BrE)
a port or area where goods can be imported, stored, and exported without paying customs duties

Examples

A free port zone
Businesses can import goods into a free port and re-export them without paying duties.
Many countries use free ports as part of their economic development strategies.
The government established a free port to encourage international trade in the city.
Goods arrived at the free port and were transferred to ships bound for other countries without customs delays.
Several countries have developed free ports to compete for global shipping business.
The government established a free port to encourage international trade and investment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: free trade zone - an area where goods may be landed, stored, handled, and re-exported without intervention from customs authorities
  • synonym: free zone - an area where goods can be imported, stored, or processed without customs duties
