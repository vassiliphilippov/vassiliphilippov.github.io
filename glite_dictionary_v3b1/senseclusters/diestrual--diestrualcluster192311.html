diestrual

adjective
in a period of sexual inactivity between estrous cycles (animals)
9.8
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɛstruəl /; / daɪˈiː.strʊ.əl / (AmE), / daɪˈɛstruːəl /; / daɪˈiː.strʊ.əl / (BrE)
(of animals) being in a period of sexual inactivity between estrous cycles within a single breeding season.

Examples

A diestrual period
A diestrual period allows the reproductive system to rest before the next estrus cycle begins.
After ovulation, the hamster enters the diestrual phase, during which sexual receptivity ceases temporarily.
Wild mice alternate between estrous and diestrual phases throughout the breeding season.
After the mating season, the deer entered a dioestrual phase, showing no signs of reproductive activity.
During the dioestrual period, hormone levels in the animals were significantly lower than during estrus.
Researchers observed that the subjects remained dioestrual for several weeks before the next estrous cycle.
