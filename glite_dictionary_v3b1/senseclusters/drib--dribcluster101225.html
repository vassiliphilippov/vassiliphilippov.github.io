drib

noun
small amount
9.5
Pronunciation: / drɪb / (AmE), / drɪb / (BrE)
a small amount or drop of something, especially a liquid

Examples

A drib of water
Only a drib of rain fell during the long drought.
He received his pay in dribs rather than all at once.
There was just a drib of paint left in the can after the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drop - a small round-shaped amount of liquid
  • antonym: flood - a large amount of water or liquid
  • synonym: trickle - a small, slow flow of liquid
