mobilization

noun
organizing and preparing for action
2.8 5.4
Pronunciation: /məʊ.bəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /məʊ.bəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /məʊ.bəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /məʊ.bəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of organizing and preparing people or resources for action, especially in response to a need, crisis, or emergency

Examples

Emergency mobilization
Rapid mobilization of medical personnel was vital during the outbreak.
Successful mobilization of support from all departments ensured the project finished on time.
The government ordered the mobilization of emergency services after the hurricane struck.
Rapid mobilization of medical teams saved many lives after the earthquake.
The country’s economic mobilization ensured supplies reached those in need during the crisis.
The government began the mobilization of reserve troops in response to the growing threat.
Rapid mobilisation of resources was crucial during the natural disaster.
The country's extensive mobilisation efforts surprised international observers.
The government ordered the mobilisation of all available troops along the border.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demobilisation - the act of standing down or disbanding forces or activities
  • antonym: demobilization - the process of standing down or disbanding organized resources or troops
  • synonym: mobilization - preparation and assembly for war or emergency (American spelling)
