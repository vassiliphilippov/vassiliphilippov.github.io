King Ferdinand

proper noun
king of Castile and Aragon, united Spain, supported Columbus
7.2
Pronunciation: / kɪŋ ˈfɜrdɪnænd / (AmE), / kɪŋ ˈfɜːdɪnænd / (BrE)
The king of Castile and Aragon (1452–1516) who, with his wife Isabella I, united Spain, instituted the Spanish Inquisition, and supported Columbus's 1492 voyage to the Americas.

Examples

King Ferdinand of Spain
King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella were known as the Catholic Monarchs of Spain.
Many historians credit King Ferdinand for supporting Columbus’s voyage to the New World.
The unification of Spain began under the reign of King Ferdinand and his wife.
