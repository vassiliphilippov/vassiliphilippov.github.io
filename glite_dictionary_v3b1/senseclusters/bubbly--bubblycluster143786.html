Synonyms

  • fizzy
    1.6 0.5/m
    fizzy is a casual word for drinks with many small bubbles and a sharp taste, while bubbly means containing lots of bubbles.
  • sparkling
    1.7 0.2/m
    sparkling describes wine or water with clear rising bubbles and a bright feel, while bubbly means containing or producing many bubbles.
  • effervescent
    7.3 0.1/m
    effervescent sounds more formal or literary and suggests lively tiny bubbles, while bubbly is plain and means full of bubbles.
  • carbonated
    3.4 0.2/m
    carbonated refers to liquids with dissolved carbon dioxide often added in factories, while bubbly describes any drink that has many bubbles.
  • frothy
    3.2 0.9/m
    frothy means covered in foam or large, soft bubbles from washing or fermentation, while bubbly means having many small bubbles.
  • foamy
    2.2 0.5/m
    foamy describes a thick layer of foam on a surface, often with big bubbles, while bubbly means the whole liquid has many small bubbles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flat - not fizzy or not containing bubbles, especially in drinks
  • synonym: frothy - full of or covered with a mass of small bubbles
