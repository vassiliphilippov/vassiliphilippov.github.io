Africanization

noun
increasing African representation
Pronunciation: / ˌæfrɪkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæfrɪkənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or policy of increasing African representation, leadership, or ownership in organizations, government, or industry, often by replacing non-African staff

Examples

Africanization of jobs
After independence, the government promoted Africanization to ensure more local citizens held key positions.
The Africanization of the banking sector led to more opportunities for African professionals.
Many companies adopted Africanization policies to support national development goals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decolonization - the process of a country gaining independence from colonial rule
  • synonym: localization - the process of making something more local in character or leadership
