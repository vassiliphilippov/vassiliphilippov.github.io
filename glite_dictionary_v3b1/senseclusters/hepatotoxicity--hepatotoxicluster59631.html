hepatotoxicity

noun
liver damage from toxins
9.8
Pronunciation: / hɛˌpætəˌtɑkˈsɪɾəti / (AmE), / hɛˌpætəʊtɒkˈsɪsɪti / (BrE)
liver damage or the quality of being toxic to the liver, usually caused by drugs, chemicals, or toxins

Examples

Hepatotoxicity from drugs
The new medication was withdrawn after several patients developed hepatotoxicity during clinical trials.
Doctors monitor liver enzymes to detect early signs of hepatotoxicity in patients taking certain drugs.
Alcohol abuse can increase the risk of hepatotoxicity when combined with some medications.
The new medication was discontinued after cases of hepatotoxicity were reported in several patients.
Doctors monitor liver function to detect any signs of hepatotoxicity during long-term drug therapy.
Researchers are studying ways to reduce hepatotoxicity caused by certain cancer treatments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hepatoprotection - prevention of liver damage
  • synonym: hepatotoxic - harmful or poisonous to the liver
  • synonym: liver injury - damage to the liver from various causes
