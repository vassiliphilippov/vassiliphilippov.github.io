postulant

noun
person applying to join a religious order
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːstʃələnt / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊlənt / (BrE)
A person who submits a formal request or application, especially someone seeking admission to a religious order before being officially accepted.

Examples

A postulant in convent
Before becoming a nun, she spent a year as a postulant in the convent.
Only after completing the postulant period can one move on to being a novice.
The young postulant nervously awaited her interview with the Mother Superior.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: novice - a person who has entered a religious order but has not yet taken final vows
