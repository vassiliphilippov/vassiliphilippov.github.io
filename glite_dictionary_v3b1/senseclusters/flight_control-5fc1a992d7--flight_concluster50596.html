flight control

noun
autonomous onboard flight system
4.1
Pronunciation: /flaɪt kən.ˈtroʊl/ (AmE), /flaɪt kən.ˈtrəʊl/ (BrE)
a system or software, usually onboard an aircraft or drone, that automatically controls, stabilizes, or guides flight without real-time human input.

Examples

Drone flight control
The new consumer drone uses onboard flight control to avoid obstacles and maintain stable altitude.
Engineers tested the flight control software in a simulator before releasing it to customers.
Regulators require documentation showing how the flight control system handles failures and loss of GPS.

Related Terms

  • antonym: air traffic control - the ground-based system and people who direct aircraft to ensure safe operation in the air and on the ground
  • synonym: autopilot - a device or system that controls the trajectory of an aircraft without constant human control
