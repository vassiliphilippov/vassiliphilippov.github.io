nuclear reaction

noun
a process changing atomic nuclei
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.ɚ riˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə riˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a process in which the structure, energy, or composition of atomic nuclei is changed, often resulting in the release or absorption of energy

Examples

A nuclear reaction in sun
A nuclear reaction in the sun provides the energy that supports life on Earth.
A controlled nuclear reaction is essential for producing nuclear power safely.
The scientists studied the products of a nuclear reaction in the laboratory.
