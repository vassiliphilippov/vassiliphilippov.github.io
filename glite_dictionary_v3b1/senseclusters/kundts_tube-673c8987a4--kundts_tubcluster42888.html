Kundt's tube

noun
apparatus for measuring sound speed
Pronunciation: / kʊnts tuːb / (AmE), / kʊnts tjuːb / (BrE)
an apparatus used to determine the speed of sound in a gas or solid by observing the positions of stationary waves in a tube

Examples

A Kundt's tube apparatus
A Kundt's tube makes the standing waves visible with the help of fine powder inside the tube.
In the physics laboratory, the students used a Kundt's tube to measure the speed of sound in air.
The invention of the Kundt's tube was an important step in the study of acoustics.
