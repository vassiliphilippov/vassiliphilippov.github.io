higgler

noun
persistent bargainer
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhɪg.lər/ /; / /ˈhɪ.glər/ (AmE), /ˈhɪg.lə/ /; / /ˈhɪ.glə/ (BrE)
a person who sells goods in a small-scale way, often by traveling from place to place or bargaining in markets

Examples

A higgler sells goods
The higgler spent hours at the market trying to get the best deals on fresh produce.
As a skilled higgler, she rarely paid the full price for anything she bought.
Every Saturday, the local higgler could be found bargaining with vendors in the busy square.
The higgler arrived early in the morning with baskets of fruit and vegetables.
Villagers looked forward to the weekly visit from the higgler who brought goods from the city.
As a higgler, she traveled from farm to farm selling household items and fresh produce.
The higgler traveled from village to village selling fresh produce and household items.
At the market, a higgler offered handmade baskets and pottery to passing customers.
Many people relied on the local higgler for affordable goods and friendly conversation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haggler - a person who argues about the price of something
  • synonym: hawker - a person who sells goods in public places, often by calling out
  • degree higher: merchant - a person who buys and sells goods in large quantities
  • synonym: negotiator - a person who discusses and arranges terms or agreements
  • synonym: peddler - a person who travels to sell goods, especially in small quantities
  • antonym: spender - a person who spends money freely without bargaining
  • synonym: vendor - someone who sells goods, especially in the street
