parallelization

noun
dividing tasks for simultaneity
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˌlɛləˌzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpærəˌlɛlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of dividing a task into parts that can be performed simultaneously to increase efficiency, especially in computing

Examples

Parallelization of tasks
The parallelization of data processing allows computers to handle large datasets more efficiently.
Effective parallelization can significantly reduce the time needed to complete complex calculations.
Software engineers often focus on parallelization to improve the performance of their applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concurrency - the ability to execute multiple tasks at the same time
  • antonym: serialization - the process of performing tasks one after another
