problematics

noun
inherent difficulties or issues in a situation
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːbləˈmætɪks / (AmE), / ˌprɒblɪˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the complex uncertainties or difficulties that are inherent in a particular situation, concept, or field of study.

Examples

The problematics of technology
Researchers discussed the problematics of implementing new technologies in rural areas.
The book explores the problematics of identity in multicultural societies.
We must consider the problematics involved in balancing economic growth with environmental protection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: problem - a matter or situation regarded as unwelcome or harmful and needing to be dealt with
