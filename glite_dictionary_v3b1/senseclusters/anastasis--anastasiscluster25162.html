anastasis

noun
cell recovery; resurrection
Pronunciation: / əˈnæstəsɪs / (AmE), / əˈnæstəsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which cells recover after beginning to die, or resurrection in religious contexts

Examples

Cell anastasis
Scientists discovered that anastasis allows damaged cells to recover instead of dying.
The concept of anastasis is important in both biology and theology.
Researchers are exploring how anastasis could help develop new treatments for tissue damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apoptosis - the process of programmed cell death
  • synonym: resurrection - the act of rising from the dead or returning to life
