reaper

noun
person who gathers crops at harvest
5.5 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêp…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêp…ô / (BrE)
a person who gathers crops at harvest, especially by hand or with a simple tool.

Examples

A reaper in the field
During the wheat harvest, every reaper in the village worked from dawn until dusk.
In traditional farming communities, a skilled reaper was highly valued for a successful harvest.
The reaper moved steadily through the golden field, bundling sheaves of grain.

Synonyms

  • harvester
    2.0 0.77/m
    a harvester gathers crops using machines or people, while a reaper is a person who usually harvests by hand or simple tools.
  • picker
    1.4 1.45/m
    a picker selects and gathers fruit or crops by hand, while a reaper specifically harvests field crops, often with a scythe or simple tool.
  • cropper
    7.5 0.03/m
    a cropper is a small farmer who grows or harvests crops on their land, while a reaper focuses on gathering crops at harvest.
