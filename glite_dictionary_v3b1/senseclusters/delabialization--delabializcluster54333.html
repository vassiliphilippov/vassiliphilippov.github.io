delabialization

noun
loss of lip rounding in speech sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˌleɪbiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌleɪbɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of pronouncing a sound without lip rounding, typically involving the loss of lip rounding in a speech sound

Examples

Delabialization of sounds
Delabialization can occur when speakers simplify the articulation of rounded vowels.
A linguist might study delabialization in the evolution of certain languages.
The change from a rounded to an unrounded vowel in the dialect is an example of delabialization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: labialization - the process of pronouncing a sound with lip rounding
