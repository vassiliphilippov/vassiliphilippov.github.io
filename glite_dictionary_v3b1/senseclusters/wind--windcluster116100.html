wind

noun
intestinal or stomach gas (flatulence)

gas in the intestines or stomach, especially when expelled from the body through the anus; flatulence.

Examples

Baby has wind
Babies often suffer from wind and may cry until it is expelled.
She felt embarrassed when she passed wind during the meeting.
The doctor explained that wind can be caused by eating certain foods.

Synonyms

  • flatulence
    5.5 0.59/m
    flatulence means gas in the stomach or intestines that people often release, while wind is the everyday word learners hear.
  • flatus
    9.4 0.04/m
    flatus is the medical name for gas passed from the bowels, while wind is the common, nonmedical word people use.
  • flatulency
    8.2 0.01/m
    flatulency describes the state of having too much digestive gas and bloating, while wind names the gas itself in everyday talk.
  • breaking wind
    6.9 0.01/m
    breaking wind describes the act of releasing gas with sound, while wind is the short everyday word for that gas.
  • fart
    5.7 3.92/m
    fart is a blunt, informal word for a noisy or smelly release of gas, while wind is the milder or polite British term.
  • gas
    2.0 4.85/m
    gas means any air or vapour in the body that causes bloating, while wind specifically refers to intestinal gas usually expelled.
