Asiatic beetle

noun
Asian beetle harmful to crops
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪʒiˈætɪk ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ˌeɪzɪˈætɪk ˈbiːtəl / (BrE)
a small beetle native to Asia and introduced into the United States, whose larvae feed on the roots of sugarcane and other grasses

Examples

The Asiatic beetle larvae
Gardeners are advised to monitor for Asiatic beetle infestations during the summer months.
Larvae of the Asiatic beetle often damage the roots of turf grass and crops like sugarcane.
The Asiatic beetle has become a significant pest in American gardens in recent decades.
