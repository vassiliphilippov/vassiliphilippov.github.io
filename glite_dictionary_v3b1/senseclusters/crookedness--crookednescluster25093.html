crookedness

noun
dishonesty; deceitfulness
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʊkɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈkrʊkɪdnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being deceitful, dishonest, or underhanded

Examples

Political crookedness
Many people were shocked by the crookedness that pervaded the company’s leadership.
She refused to participate in any crookedness, insisting on running a fair and honest campaign.
The politician's crookedness was eventually exposed by the investigative journalists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceitfulness - the quality of being deceitful or misleading
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and truthful
  • antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
