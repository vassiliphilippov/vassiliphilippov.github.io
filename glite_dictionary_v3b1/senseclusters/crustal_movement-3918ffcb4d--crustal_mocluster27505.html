crustal movement

noun
movement or deformation of the Earth's crust
5.6 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkrʌs.təl ˈmuːv.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈkrʌs.təl ˈmuːv.mənt/ (BrE)
the movement or shifting of the Earth's crust that causes its deformation, often leading to earthquakes, mountain formation, or other geological phenomena

Examples

Crustal movement causes earthquakes
A sudden crustal movement caused the ground to split open in several places.
Geologists study crustal movement to better understand earthquakes and volcanic activity.
Satellite measurements help scientists track slow crustal movement over many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tectonic movement - movement of the Earth's plates, often causing earthquakes and mountains
