preserve

verb
keep safe from harm or danger
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈzɜːrv/ /; / /prə.ˈzɜːrv/ /; / /priː.ˈzɜːrv/ (AmE), /prə.ˈzɜːv/ /; / /prɪ.ˈzɜːv/ (BrE)
to keep someone or something safe, unchanged, or protected from harm, damage, or decay

Examples

To preserve nature
It is important to preserve endangered species so they do not become extinct.
She worked hard to preserve her family's traditions for future generations.
The treaty was created to preserve peace between the two nations.
Strict laws help preserve endangered wildlife in the area.
The museum works hard to preserve rare manuscripts for future generations.
We must preserve our natural resources for the benefit of our children.
Local landowners have preserved their lake to ensure exclusive fishing rights.
The wealthy family preserves the entire woodland for their annual hunting trips.
They hope to preserve the marsh, allowing only a select group access during duck season.
