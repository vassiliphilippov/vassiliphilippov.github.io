tantalum

noun
chemical element, metallic, symbol Ta
Pronunciation: / ˈtæn.t̬ə.ləm / (AmE), / ˈtæn.tə.ləm / (BrE)
a hard, gray, lustrous metallic chemical element (symbol Ta), highly resistant to corrosion, used in electronics and metal alloys

Examples

Tantalum metal
Tantalum is often extracted from minerals such as tantalite.
Researchers developed new alloys using tantalum to improve durability in medical implants.
The capacitor contains tantalum because of its excellent resistance to corrosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: niobium - a similar chemical element, often found with tantalum in minerals
