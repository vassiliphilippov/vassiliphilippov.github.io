escalatory

adjective
causing intensification
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskələˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈɛskələˌtəri /; / ˈɛskələtəri / (BrE)
causing or relating to an increase in intensity, seriousness, amount, or scope

Examples

Escalatory actions
The leaders avoided making any escalatory statements during the tense negotiations.
Analysts warned that escalatory measures could worsen the already fragile situation.
His escalatory rhetoric increased fears of a broader conflict.
The government warned that escalatory actions could lead to a larger conflict.
Doctors sometimes use an escalatory approach to adjust medication dosages for patients.
The negotiations broke down after a series of escalatory statements from both sides.
The government warned against taking any escalatory steps that could worsen the conflict.
Analysts observed an escalatory trend in the negotiations between the two companies.
The military responded with an escalatory show of force after the incident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deescalatory - intended to reduce intensity or seriousness
  • synonym: escalating - increasing or becoming more intense
  • synonym: incremental - increasing gradually by small amounts
  • synonym: provocative - causing a reaction, especially anger or increased tension
