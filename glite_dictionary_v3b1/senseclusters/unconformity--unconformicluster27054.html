unconformity

noun
gap in rock layers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkənˈfɔːrməti / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
a surface between layers of rock that represents a gap in the geological record, often due to erosion or lack of deposition

Examples

Rock layer unconformity
The geologist pointed out the unconformity that marked millions of years missing from the rock record.
A clear unconformity separates the older sandstone from the younger limestone above it.
Erosion caused the unconformity seen in the exposed cliff face.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformity - continuous sequence of rock layers without a gap
  • synonym: disconformity - a type of unconformity where the rock layers are parallel but there is a gap in the record
