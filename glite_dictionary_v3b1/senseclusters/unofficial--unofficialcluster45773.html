unofficial

adjective
not officially recognized or authorized
1.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnəˈfɪʃəl /; / ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəl / (AmE), / ʌnəˈfɪʃəl /; / ˌʌnəˈfɪʃəl / (BrE)
not formally recognized, approved, or authorized by an official authority

Examples

An unofficial spokesperson
He acted as the unofficial spokesperson during the meeting.
People shared an unofficial report before the official announcement was made.
They arranged an unofficial gathering to discuss the project informally.
She attended the meeting in an unofficial capacity, representing her own interests.
The results of the poll are still unofficial and subject to verification.
There were unofficial reports that the company was planning to expand overseas.
