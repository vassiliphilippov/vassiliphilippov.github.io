ethnohistory

noun
study of cultural history
9.2
Pronunciation: /eθ.noʊ.ˈhɪs.tə.riː/ /; / /əθ.nə.ˈhɪs.tə.riː/ (AmE), /eθ.əʊ.ˈhɪs.tə.riː/ /; / /əθ.nə.ˈhɪs.tə.riː/ (BrE)
the study of a people's or ethnic group's history using cultural, oral, and written records, often from their own perspective

Examples

Ethnohistory study
The professor introduced ethnohistory as a way to understand indigenous perspectives on colonization.

The professor specializes in ethnohistory and has written several books about indigenous cultures.
Through ethnohistory, researchers can better understand the traditions and changes within a community.
The museum's new exhibit is based on ethnohistory collected from local elders and artifacts.
