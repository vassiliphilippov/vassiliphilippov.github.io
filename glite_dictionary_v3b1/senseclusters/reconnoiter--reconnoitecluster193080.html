reconnoiter

verb
make a preliminary survey, especially for military purposes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.kəˈnɔɪ.tɚ /; / rəˈkɑː.nɔɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəˈnɔɪ.tə /; / rɪˈkɒn.ɔɪ.tə / (BrE)
to make a preliminary survey of an area, especially to gather military information or to find something or someone

Examples

To reconnoiter enemy position
A small group was sent ahead to reconnoiter the enemy's position discreetly.
The soldiers were ordered to reconnoiter the area before the main force advanced.
We need to reconnoiter the mountain trail before tomorrow's expedition.
Before advancing, the patrol paused to reconnoitre the surrounding area for possible dangers.
Scientists were sent to reconnoitre the uninhabited island for potential research sites.
The general ordered his troops to reconnoitre the enemy camp before dawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scout - to explore or observe an area to gather information
  • synonym: survey - to examine an area carefully, especially to gather information
