soggy

adjective
wet and soft from water
6.3 1.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːɡi / (AmE), / ˈsɒɡi / (BrE)
(of soil or other material) very wet and soft, often unpleasantly so, because of water saturation.

Examples

Soggy ground
After the heavy rain, the football field became soggy and difficult to play on.
Her shoes were soaked from walking across the soggy grass in the morning.
The bread turned soggy after sitting in the soup for too long.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boggy - soft, wet, and spongy ground; marshy
  • antonym: dry - not containing or covered with water or moisture
