anywho

interjection
informal transition
Pronunciation: / ˈɛniˌhu / (AmE), / ˈɛniˌhu / (BrE)
an informal interjection used to change the subject, end a conversation, or move on from a topic

Examples

Well, anywho
Well, anywho, let's get back to the main point of the meeting.
She finished her story and said, 'anywho, what are your weekend plans?'
It's getting late, so anywho, I should probably head home now.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anyway - an interjection used to return to a topic or move on
  • synonym: so - an interjection used to introduce a new topic or resume a conversation
