hedge

verb
avoid answering or committing directly
7.9
Pronunciation: / hɛdʒ / (AmE), / hɛdʒ / (BrE)
to avoid giving a direct answer or clear commitment; to avoid or try to avoid fulfilling a duty or answering a question directly

Examples

To hedge a question
During the interview, the politician tried to hedge every controversial question.
He tends to hedge whenever his responsibilities are brought up.
When asked about the mistake, she began to hedge instead of admitting the error.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confront - face up to a duty or question directly
  • synonym: dodge - avoid a question or duty by being evasive
  • synonym: evade - avoid giving a direct answer or fulfilling a responsibility
