low-level

adjective
not intense; small in amount or degree
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊ ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
not intense; existing, occurring, or produced in small amounts or degrees

Examples

Low-level noise
Residents were reassured that the low-level noise from the construction would not be disruptive.
She experienced low-level anxiety during the first days of her new job.
The laboratory only detected low-level radiation in the water sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high-level - very intense; of greater degree or amount
  • degree higher: moderate - of medium intensity; average degree
