proboscidian

noun
large mammal with trunk and tusks (e.g. elephant)
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈbɑː.sɪ.dɪ.ən/ (AmE), /prə.ˈbɒs.ɪ.dɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a member of an order of large, herbivorous mammals characterized by a trunk and tusks, including elephants and their extinct relatives such as mammoths and mastodons

A proboscidian elephant
Elephants are the only living proboscidians, but many extinct species once roamed the earth.
Scientists believe that the decline of proboscidian populations was linked to changes in climate.
The museum's display included the skeleton of a prehistoric proboscidian discovered in the region.
