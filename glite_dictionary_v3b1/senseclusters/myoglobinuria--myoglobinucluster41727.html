myoglobinuria

noun
myoglobin in urine; medical condition
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪoʊˌɡloʊbɪˈnjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəʊˌɡləʊbɪˈnjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of myoglobin in the urine, often indicating muscle breakdown or injury.

Examples

Myoglobinuria in urine
Athletes with excessive physical exertion may be at risk of developing myoglobinuria.
Rhabdomyolysis can lead to myoglobinuria, which gives urine a dark reddish color.
The doctor ordered tests to confirm the presence of myoglobinuria after the patient complained of muscle pain.
