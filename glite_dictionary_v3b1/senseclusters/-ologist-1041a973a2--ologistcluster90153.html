person who studies
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɑˈlɑdʒɪst /; / ɑˈlɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ɒˈlɒdʒɪst /; / ɒˈlɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a suffix used to form nouns meaning a person who studies or is an expert in a particular field (such as science, knowledge, or practice)

Examples

A biologist
A geologist examines rocks and minerals to understand Earth's history.
If you love animals, you might become a zoologist and study their behavior.
The cardiologist explained how the heart works to the patient.
A -ologist is someone who specializes in a particular branch of knowledge, such as a biologist or psychologist.
The word 'geologist' uses the suffix -ologist to indicate an expert in geology.
If you are interested in the mind, you might become a psychologist, which ends with -ologist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -logist - a person who studies or is an expert in a subject
  • degree lower: -ology - the study of a particular subject
