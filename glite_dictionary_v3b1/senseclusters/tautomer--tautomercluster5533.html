tautomer

noun
interconvertible chemical form
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔtəmər / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtəmə / (BrE)
a chemical compound that can exist in two or more easily interconvertible forms differing in the position of a proton and a double bond

Examples

A tautomer compound
The chemist explained how the molecule could act as a tautomer under certain conditions.
Keto and enol forms are classic examples of a tautomer pair in organic chemistry.
Understanding which tautomer is present can be crucial for predicting a compound's reactivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isomer - a compound with the same formula but a different arrangement of atoms
