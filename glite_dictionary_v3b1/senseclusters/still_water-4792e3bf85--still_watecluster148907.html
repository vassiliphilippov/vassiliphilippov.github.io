still water

noun
non-carbonated water
Pronunciation: / stɪl ˈwɔtər / (AmE), / stɪl ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that is not carbonated and does not contain bubbles, often served or sold in bottles for drinking

Examples

A bottle of still water
She prefers still water to sparkling when dining out at restaurants.
The waiter brought a bottle of still water to the table before taking their order.
Many people choose still water for hydration during exercise because it is gentle on the stomach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mineral water - water from a mineral spring, often bottled
  • antonym: sparkling water - carbonated water with bubbles
