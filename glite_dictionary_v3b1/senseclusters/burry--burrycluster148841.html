burry

adjective
covered with burs or prickly growths
8.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…úri / (AmE), / Ààb…úÀêri / (BrE)
having or covered with burs, barbs, or similar prickly or spiny growths, especially as found on plants or animals

Examples

Burry plants
Hikers complained about burry plants sticking to their clothes.
The dog's fur became tangled with burry seeds after our walk through the meadow.
They avoided the burry stems of the wild raspberry bushes.

Synonyms

  • burred
    9.1 0.01/m
    burred is covered in burs that stick to fur or fabric and is used in plain descriptions, while burry describes the same prickly covering.
  • burrlike
    9.1 0.01/m
    burrlike looks like a burr with small hooks or bristles on its surface, while burry refers to being covered in burs.
  • spiny
    3.9 0.32/m
    spiny has long sharp points or spines on it, while burry emphasizes a surface covered in burs or small hooks.
  • thorny
    4.2 0.69/m
    thorny has obvious thorns that can hurt when touched, while burry describes plants with many burs or hooked seed cases.
  • prickly
    3.3 2.15/m
    prickly is generally covered in small sharp points that prick skin, while burry refers specifically to being full of burs.
  • barbed
    4.4 0.26/m
    barbed has hooked or pointed barbs like on wire or feathers, while burry focuses on plant burs that cling or catch.
  • bristly
    4.6 0.32/m
    bristly has short stiff hairs or bristles you can feel, while burry means covered in hooked burs rather than soft hairs.
