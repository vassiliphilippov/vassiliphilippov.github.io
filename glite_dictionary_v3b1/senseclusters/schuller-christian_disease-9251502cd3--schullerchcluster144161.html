Schuller-Christian disease

noun
rare childhood inflammatory disease with skull lesions
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuːlɚ ˈkrɪstʃən dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈʃʊlə ˈkrɪstʃən dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A rare inflammatory disease, mainly affecting young children, characterized by histiocytosis with disturbance of cholesterol metabolism, cystic lesions in the skull, and sometimes diabetes insipidus.

Examples

Schuller-Christian disease in children
Advances in treatment have improved the prognosis for patients with Schuller-Christian disease.
Children suffering from Schuller-Christian disease may show symptoms including excessive thirst due to diabetes insipidus.
The doctor diagnosed the child with Schuller-Christian disease after observing characteristic lesions in the skull.
