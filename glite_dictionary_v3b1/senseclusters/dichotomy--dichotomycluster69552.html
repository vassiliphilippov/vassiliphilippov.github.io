dichotomy

noun
division into two opposed parts
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a division or contrast between two things that are represented as being entirely different or opposed

Examples

A dichotomy between ideas
The debate revealed a clear dichotomy between the two political ideologies.
The novel explores the dichotomy of good and evil within human nature.
There is often a dichotomy in attitudes towards technology across generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrast - a stark difference between two things
  • antonym: unity - state of being united or joined as a whole
