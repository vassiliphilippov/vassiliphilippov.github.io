logic

noun
reasoned or sound judgment
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːdʒɪk /; / ˈlɑː.dʒɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪk /; / ˈlɒdʒ.ɪk / (BrE)
the principles or process of reasoning, or a particular system of reasoning

Examples

Clear logic
Her argument had a certain logic, even if I didn't agree with it.
There is a clear logic behind this decision.
You can't argue with the logic of his conclusion.
His argument followed the logic of classical mathematics.
She questioned the moral logic behind the new policy.
The scientific method is based on a strict logic that seeks to eliminate bias.
Her argument is consistent with the logic of scientific inquiry.
Military commanders had to obey the harsh logic of war.
The company followed the logic of maximizing profit in all their decisions.

  • synonym: reasoning - the action of thinking about something in a logical, sensible way
