versatility

noun
ability to do many things well
1.4 4.7
Pronunciation: /vɜːr.sə.ˈtɪ.lə.tiː/ (AmE), /vɜː.sə.ˈtɪ.lɪ.ti/ (BrE)
the ability to adapt or be adapted to many different functions, activities, or fields; having a wide variety of skills.

Examples

Wide versatility
Actors are often praised for their versatility when they excel in both comedy and drama roles.
Her versatility made her an invaluable member of the team, able to handle many different tasks.
The chef's versatility allows him to create dishes from a wide range of cuisines.
