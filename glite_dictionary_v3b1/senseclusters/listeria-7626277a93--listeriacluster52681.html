Listeria

noun
genus of rod-shaped bacteria
Pronunciation: / lɪˈstɪriə / (AmE), / lɪsˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A genus of small, rod-shaped, Gram-positive, aerobic bacteria, some species of which can cause foodborne illness in humans.

Examples

Listeria bacteria
Listeria can contaminate unpasteurized dairy products and cause serious illness.
Doctors advise pregnant women to avoid foods that may carry Listeria bacteria.
The outbreak was traced to a strain of Listeria found in soft cheese.
