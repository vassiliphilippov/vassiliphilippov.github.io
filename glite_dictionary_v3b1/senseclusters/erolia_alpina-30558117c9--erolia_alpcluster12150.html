Erolia alpina

noun
species of migratory sandpiper
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛˈroʊliə ælˈpiːnə / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊliə ælˈpiːnə / (BrE)
A small sandpiper species that breeds in northern or Arctic regions and migrates to southern United States or Mediterranean areas for winter.

Examples

The Erolia alpina bird
Birdwatchers in the Mediterranean often spot Erolia alpina during its winter migration.
Every spring, thousands of Erolia alpina migrate north to breed in Arctic regions.
Researchers studied the feeding habits of Erolia alpina along the southern U.S. coastline.
