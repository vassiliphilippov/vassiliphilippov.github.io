battle of Tewkesbury

proper noun
final battle of Wars of the Roses (1471)
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬əl əv ˈtuːksbəri / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈtjuːksbəri / (BrE)
the final major battle of the Wars of the Roses, fought in 1471, in which Edward IV defeated the Lancastrians

Examples

The battle of Tewkesbury
During the battle of Tewkesbury, many leading Lancastrian nobles were killed or captured.
Historians often cite the battle of Tewkesbury as a turning point in the Wars of the Roses.
The battle of Tewkesbury marked the end of significant Lancastrian resistance to Edward IV.
