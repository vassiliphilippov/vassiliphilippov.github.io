swept

adjective
angled backward, especially of aircraft wings
4.9 6.5
Pronunciation: / swɛpt / (AmE), / swɛpt / (BrE)
(of an object, especially an aircraft wing) angled or slanted backward from its root to its tip, often for aerodynamic purposes.

Examples

Swept wing design
Modern commercial aircraft often have swept wing designs for greater fuel efficiency.
The fighter jet featured swept wings to improve its speed at high altitudes.
The museum's restored bomber displayed its distinctive swept wings proudly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: straight - (of a wing or edge) not angled or swept back
  • synonym: sweptback - (of wings) having a backward slant or angle for aerodynamic advantage
