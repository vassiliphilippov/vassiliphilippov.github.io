sneak out

verb
leave secretly or furtively
3.7 3.17/m
Pronunciation: / snik a ät / (AmE), / sniÀêk a ät / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly and secretly so that no one notices you

Examples

To sneak out quietly
Several students managed to sneak out during the long assembly.
She tried to sneak out of the house after midnight without waking her parents.
The dog often sneaks out through a gap in the fence when no one is looking.
