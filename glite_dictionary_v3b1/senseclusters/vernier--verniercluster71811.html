vernier

noun
precision sliding scale on measuring instruments
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜːrniər / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪə / (BrE)
a small movable scale that slides along a main scale, used on measuring instruments to indicate fractional divisions with high precision

Examples

The vernier scale
He learned how to read the vernier on a micrometer during his engineering class.
Most laboratory calipers are equipped with a vernier to provide precise readings.
The technician adjusted the vernier to read the measurement with greater accuracy.
