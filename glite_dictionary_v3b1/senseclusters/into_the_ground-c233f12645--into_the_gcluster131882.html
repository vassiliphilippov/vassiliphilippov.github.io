into the ground

adverb
to exhaustion or ruin (by overwork)
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn tu ðə ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tuː ðə ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to the point of exhaustion, destruction, or ruin; used to describe the act of exhausting or overworking someone or something excessively

Examples

Work into the ground
If you keep pushing yourself into the ground, you'll eventually burn out.
She ran her company into the ground by expanding too quickly without proper planning.
The new manager drove his employees into the ground by demanding constant overtime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: run into the ground - to overwork, exhaust, or misuse something or someone
