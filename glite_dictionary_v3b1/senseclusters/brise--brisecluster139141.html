brise

noun
broken chord style
Pronunciation: / briːˈzeɪ / (AmE), / briːˈzeɪ / (BrE)
a style or pattern of broken chords, especially used in Baroque lute and harpsichord music.

Examples

Baroque brise style
The musician used a delicate brise to bring out the beauty of the Baroque piece.
Learning the brise technique is essential for authentic harpsichord performance.
She admired how the brise created a shimmering effect in the music.
