burn out

verb
to stop working due to overheating or overuse
4.7 1.06/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùn a ät / (AmE), / b…úÀên a ät / (BrE)
(of electrical or mechanical devices) to stop working by melting, breaking, or becoming otherwise unusable, typically due to overheating or excessive use.

Examples

To burn out a fuse
If you use that fuse with too much current, it will burn out.
Several lights burned out during the thunderstorm.
The old lamp in my room finally burned out last night.

Synonyms

  • blow out
    4.1 0.66/m
    blow out is a sudden failure of bulbs or fuses from too much current, while burn out is melting or breaking from overheating or long use.
  • blow
    3.5 63/m
    blow is a sudden bursting or failure often from pressure or overload, while burn out is failure by heat or excessive use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blow out - to fail suddenly and stop working (of electrical devices)
  • synonym: break down - to stop functioning (especially of machines)
