neuroepithelium

noun
sensory epithelium in sense organs
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˌɛpəˈθiliəm / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˌɛpɪˈθiːlɪəm / (BrE)
A type of epithelium found in special sense organs that contains sensory nerve endings.

Examples

The neuroepithelium tissue
Damage to the neuroepithelium can impair a person’s sense of taste or smell.
Researchers examined the neuroepithelium under a microscope to study its cellular structure.
The olfactory neuroepithelium is responsible for detecting odors in the nasal cavity.
