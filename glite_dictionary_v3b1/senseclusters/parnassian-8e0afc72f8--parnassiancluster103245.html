Parnassian

noun
French poetic movement
Pronunciation: / pɑrˈnæsiən / (AmE), / pɑːˈnæsiən / (BrE)
a poet associated with the 19th-century French Parnassian movement, known for emphasizing formal technique and emotional restraint

Examples

A Parnassian poet
The literary critic praised the Parnassian for his mastery of poetic form and structure.
Many 19th-century French poets were influenced by the ideals of the Parnassian movement.
Her precise and restrained verses led some to call her a modern Parnassian.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classicist - a poet who values classical forms and traditions
  • antonym: symbolist - a poet of the Symbolist movement, focusing on symbolic imagery and emotions
