X-linked SCID

noun
genetic immune deficiency (X-linked)
Pronunciation: / ɛks lɪŋkt ɛs si aɪ di / (AmE), / ɛks lɪŋkt ɛs siː aɪ diː / (BrE)
A severe form of immune deficiency, occurring mostly in male children, caused by a genetic mutation on the X chromosome affecting T-cell development.

Examples

X-linked SCID disease
Because X-linked SCID is inherited, the family was offered genetic counseling.
Doctors diagnosed the infant with X-linked SCID after he suffered from recurrent infections.
Gene therapy has shown promise in treating children affected by X-linked SCID.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Severe Combined Immunodeficiency - general form of immune deficiency
