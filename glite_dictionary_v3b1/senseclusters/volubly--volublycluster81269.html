volubly

adverb
in a fluent, talkative way
8.7 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ëÀêlj…ôbli / (AmE), / Ààv…ílj äbli / (BrE)
in a fluent and readily talkative manner; with a rapid flow of speech

Examples

To speak volubly
At the meeting, Mark volubly expressed his concerns to everyone present.
She spoke volubly about her travels, barely pausing to catch her breath.
The children chattered volubly as they waited for the show to begin.

Synonyms

  • loquaciously
    9.9 0.00/m
    loquaciously suggests talking a great deal and often for long chats, while volubly implies fluent, rapid speech that flows easily.
  • talkatively
    5.8 0.00/m
    talkatively means speaking eagerly or at length in casual talk, while volubly means speaking quickly and smoothly with fluent flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluently - in a smooth and flowing manner (especially speech or writing)
  • antonym: taciturnly - in a reserved, quiet, or uncommunicative manner
