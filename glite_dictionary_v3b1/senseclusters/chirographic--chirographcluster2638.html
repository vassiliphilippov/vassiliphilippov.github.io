chirographic

adjective
unsecured (legal/finance)
Pronunciation: / ˌkaɪrəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌkaɪərəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
in law or finance, describing a debt or claim that is unsecured and not backed by collateral

Examples

Chirographic debt
The court classified the loan as chirographic because it was not backed by any property.
A chirographic claim may be riskier for lenders since there is no collateral involved.
Investors were cautious about the chirographic nature of the company's outstanding debts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unsecured - not protected by collateral or security
