Cuban bast

noun
forest tree of Cuba and Jamaica, valued for timber
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkjuː.bən bæst/ /; / /ˈkjʊb.ən bæst/ (AmE), /ˈkjuː.bən bɑːst/ /; / /ˈkjuː.bən bæst/ (BrE)
an erect forest tree native to Cuba and Jamaica, noted for its hairy leaves, orange-yellow or orange-red flowers, and moderately dense timber used for cabinetwork and gunstocks

Examples

Cuban bast tree
Because of its durability, Cuban bast was once used to produce gunstocks.
Botanists found several mature Cuban bast trees thriving on the island.
The wood of the Cuban bast is highly prized for fine furniture making.
