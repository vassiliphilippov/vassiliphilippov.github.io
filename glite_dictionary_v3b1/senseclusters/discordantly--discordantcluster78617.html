discordantly

adverb
in a manner lacking harmony or agreement
6.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈkɑːr.dənt.lɪ/ /; / /dɪs.ˈkɔːr.dənt.lɪ/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈkɔː.dənt.lɪ/ (BrE)
In a manner that lacks harmony or agreement; in a way that is harsh or jarring to the senses.

Examples

Colors discordantly clash
Colors in the room were chosen so poorly that they discordantly competed for attention.
She laughed discordantly, breaking the tense silence in the room.
The orchestra's instruments clashed discordantly during the rehearsal.
