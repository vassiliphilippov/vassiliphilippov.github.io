excuse

noun
a note explaining an absence
1.7 36/m
Pronunciation: / …™kÀàskjus / (AmE), / …™kÀàskjuÀês / (BrE)
a reason or explanation given to justify or defend a fault, absence, or failure (including a written note)

Examples

A doctor's excuse
Employees are often required to submit an excuse if they miss work unexpectedly.
She handed the principal an excuse explaining her absence due to illness.
The teacher asked for an excuse from his parents before letting him return to class.
He always has an excuse for being late to meetings.
No excuse can make up for his bad behavior.
That is not a valid excuse for breaking your promise.

Synonyms

    4.0 19/m

  • reason

  • explanation
    1.0 66/m
