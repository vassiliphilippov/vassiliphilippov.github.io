escapist

noun
person who seeks escape in fantasy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskeɪpɪst / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪpɪst / (BrE)
a person who seeks distraction and relief from unpleasant realities, especially by engaging in fantasy or entertainment

Examples

An escapist reader
As an escapist, she often turned to books to avoid her daily problems.
His critics called him an escapist who refused to face reality.
Many films cater to the escapist desires of their audiences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realist - a person who accepts a situation as it is and deals with it accordingly
