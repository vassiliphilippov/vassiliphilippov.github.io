catch flies with vinegar

verb
try to attract with harshness (usually negative)
7.9 7.7
Pronunciation: / kætʃ flaɪz wɪð ˈvɪnəɡɚ / (AmE), / kæʧ flaɪz wɪð ˈvɪnɪɡə / (BrE)
to attempt to attract or persuade people by being unpleasant or harsh, often used in the negative to mean it is more effective to use kindness

Examples

To catch flies with vinegar

My grandmother always said you can't catch flies with vinegar, so try to be polite.
