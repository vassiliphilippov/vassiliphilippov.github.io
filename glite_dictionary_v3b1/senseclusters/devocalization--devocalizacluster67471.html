devocalization

noun
loss of vocal sound
9.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.voʊ.kəl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.voʊ.kəl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.vəʊ.kəl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.vəʊ.kəl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process in phonetics by which a vowel or voiced sound becomes voiceless or loses its vocal quality

Examples

Phonetic devocalization
The devocalization of certain consonants is common in the evolution of many languages.
Linguists study devocalization to understand historical changes in pronunciation.
In some dialects, devocalization affects the final sounds of words.
