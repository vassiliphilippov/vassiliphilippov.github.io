provisionality

noun
temporary nature
8.3
Pronunciation: / prəˌvɪʒəˈnæləti / (AmE), / prəˌvɪʒəˈnæləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being temporary or likely to change

Examples

The provisionality of policy
The provisionality of the new policy means it could be revised at any time.
Because of its provisionality, the contract was not considered legally binding.
Scientists acknowledged the provisionality of their findings pending further research.

Related Terms
