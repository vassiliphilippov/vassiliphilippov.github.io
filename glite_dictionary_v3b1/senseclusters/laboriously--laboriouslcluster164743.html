laboriously

adverb
with great effort or difficulty
6.0 5.3
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈbɔː.rɪ.ə.sliː/ /; / /lə.ˈbɔː.rɪ.ə.slɪ/ (AmE), /lə.ˈbɔː.rɪ.ə.sli/ /; / /lə.ˈbɔː.rɪ.ə.slɪ/ (BrE)
In a way that requires a lot of effort and hard work; in a painstaking or tedious manner.

Examples

To laboriously work
The artist laboriously restored the old painting to its former glory.
The essay was laboriously written over the course of several weeks.
They climbed the steep hill laboriously, stopping often to catch their breath.
