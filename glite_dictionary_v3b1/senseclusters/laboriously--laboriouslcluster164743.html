laboriously

adverb
with great effort or difficulty
6.6 0.99/m
Pronunciation: / l…ôÀàb…îri…ôsli / (AmE), / l…ôÀàb…îÀêri…ôsli / (BrE)
In a way that requires a lot of effort and hard work; in a painstaking or tedious manner.

Examples

To laboriously work
The artist laboriously restored the old painting to its former glory.
The essay was laboriously written over the course of several weeks.
They climbed the steep hill laboriously, stopping often to catch their breath.

Synonyms

  • arduously
    7.8 0.11/m
    arduously is done with long, very hard effort and persistence, while laboriously suggests slow, often tedious hard work.
  • effortfully
    5.4 0.04/m
    effortfully shows strong physical or mental effort, while laboriously suggests slow, repetitive work that tires and takes much time.
  • painstakingly
    6.0 2.16/m
    painstakingly means careful and very thorough work, while laboriously suggests hard, slow, and often tedious effort rather than only carefulness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easily - without difficulty or effort
  • synonym: painstakingly - with great care and thoroughness
