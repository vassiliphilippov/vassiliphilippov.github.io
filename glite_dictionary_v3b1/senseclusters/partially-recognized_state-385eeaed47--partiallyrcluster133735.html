partially-recognized state

noun
territory with limited international recognition
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrʃəli rɪˈkɑɡnaɪzd steɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɑːʃəli rɪˈkɒɡnaɪzd steɪt / (BrE)
A region or country that exercises de facto independence and governance but lacks full recognition as a sovereign state by the international community.

Examples

A partially-recognized state government
A partially-recognized state may have its own government and borders but lack a seat at the United Nations.
Kosovo is often described as a partially-recognized state in international affairs.
Many partially-recognized states issue their own passports and currency despite limited diplomatic ties.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: unrecognized state - a territory that claims statehood but is not recognized by any other country
