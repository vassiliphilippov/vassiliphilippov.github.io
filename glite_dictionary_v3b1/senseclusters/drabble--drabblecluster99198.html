drabble

verb
to soil with water
Pronunciation: / ˈdræbəl / (AmE), / ˈdræbəl / (BrE)
to make something wet or dirty by splashing, especially with mud or water; to soil by dribbling liquid (chiefly dialect or archaic)

Examples

To drabble shoes
The children drabbled their shoes in the puddles after the rain.
Please don't drabble the carpet with muddy shoes after playing outside.
Her dress was drabbled with water as she walked through the wet grass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clean - to remove dirt or make something free from dirt
  • synonym: dirty - to make something unclean
  • synonym: soil - to make something dirty, especially with earth or mud
