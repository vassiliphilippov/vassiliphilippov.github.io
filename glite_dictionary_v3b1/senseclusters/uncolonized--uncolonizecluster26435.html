uncolonized

adjective
not inhabited by organisms
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈkɑːləˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈkɒləˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
not inhabited, occupied, or overgrown by living organisms such as microbes, fungi, or plants

Examples

Uncolonized petri dish
The researchers ensured the petri dish remained uncolonized before introducing the bacteria.
It is important to keep surgical instruments uncolonized to prevent infection.
The soil sample was found to be completely uncolonized by any plant roots or fungi.
