orthopnea

noun
difficulty breathing when lying down
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθəpˈniə / (AmE), / ˌɔːθɒpˈniːə / (BrE)
Difficulty in breathing that occurs when lying down but improves when standing or sitting up, often seen in conditions such as heart failure or lung disease.

Examples

Orthopnea when lying down
Her symptoms of orthopnea improved significantly after starting the new medication.
In advanced heart failure, orthopnea is a common and distressing symptom.
The patient reported experiencing orthopnea and needed multiple pillows to sleep comfortably.

  • degree lower: dyspnea - difficult or labored breathing
