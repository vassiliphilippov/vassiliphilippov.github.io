let-off

noun
bow draw weight reduction
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlet.ɔːf/ /; / /ˈlet.ɑːf/ (AmE), /ˈlet.ɒf/ /; / /ˈlet.ɔːf/ (BrE)
the percentage reduction in draw weight of a compound bow at full draw, making it easier to hold the bowstring back

Examples

Let-off percentage
The new compound bow offers a 75% let-off, making it easier for archers to aim steadily.
With a high let-off, she could hold the bow at full draw without tiring quickly.
Many modern bows are designed with adjustable let-off to suit different archers' needs.
