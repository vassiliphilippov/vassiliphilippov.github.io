termagant

noun
quarrelsome, scolding woman
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɚməˌɡænt / (AmE), / ˈtɜːməɡənt / (BrE)
a woman who is quarrelsome, scolding, or bad-tempered, especially in a harsh or overbearing way

Examples

A termagant woman
His mother was a notorious termagant, always ready with a sharp word and a stern look.
No one dared contradict the termagant who ran the household with an iron fist.
The character of Katherine in Shakespeare’s play is often described as a termagant by critics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shrew - a woman of violent temper and speech
  • synonym: virago - a domineering, violent, or bad-tempered woman
