pouched mammal

noun
mammal with a pouch to carry young
Pronunciation: / paʊtʃt ˈmæməl / (AmE), / paʊtʃt ˈmaməl / (BrE)
a mammal whose females have a pouch (the marsupium) in which the young are carried and fed

Examples

A pouched mammal carries young
Koalas, another type of pouched mammal, carry their young in a marsupium.
Scientists study pouched mammals to understand the evolution of reproductive strategies.
The kangaroo is perhaps the most famous pouched mammal native to Australia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marsupial - a mammal with a pouch for carrying young; the scientific term for pouched mammals
