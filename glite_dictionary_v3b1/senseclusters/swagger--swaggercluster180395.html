swagger

verb
discourage or intimidate with threats
Pronunciation: / ˈswæɡər / (AmE), / ˈswæɡə / (BrE)
to discourage or frighten someone with threats or a domineering manner; to intimidate

Examples

To swagger someone into silence
She refused to let anyone swagger her out of telling the truth in court.
The villain tried to swagger the witnesses into silence with his menacing glare.
They attempted to swagger their way through the negotiations, but the opposition stood firm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • synonym: intimidate - to frighten or overawe, especially to make someone do what one wants
