Sicarii

noun
ancient political assassins
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkɑːriaɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈkɑːriaɪ / (BrE)
members of an ancient Jewish group known for carrying out secret political assassinations with daggers, especially against Roman collaborators.

Examples

Ancient Sicarii assassins
The Sicarii were feared for their surprise attacks in crowded places during Roman times.
Historians debate the influence of the Sicarii on later revolutionary groups.
The term Sicarii is sometimes used today to describe secretive political assassins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assassin - a person who murders someone for political or religious reasons
  • synonym: zealot - a member of a fanatical Jewish sect resisting Roman rule
