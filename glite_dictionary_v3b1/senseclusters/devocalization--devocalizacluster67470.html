devocalization

noun
loss of voice
9.7
Pronunciation: / diˌvoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌvəʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the loss or severe reduction of the ability to produce voice, often due to illness, injury, or nerve damage

Examples

Sudden devocalization
The patient's devocalization was caused by nerve damage after surgery.
After a severe infection, she experienced temporary devocalization and could only whisper.
Doctors monitored his recovery from devocalization following the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphonia - loss of the ability to speak or produce voice
  • degree lower: dysphonia - difficulty or pain in speaking due to a problem with the voice
