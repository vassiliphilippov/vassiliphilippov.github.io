comportment

noun
formal or dignified behavior
8.2 0.37/m
Pronunciation: / k…ômÀàp…îrtm…ônt / (AmE), / k…ômÀàp…îÀêtm…ônt / (BrE)
The way in which a person behaves or conducts themselves, especially in a formal or dignified manner.

Examples

Calm comportment
Even under pressure, his calm comportment made a strong impression on the audience.
Her impeccable comportment earned her the respect of colleagues at every event.
The judge's stern comportment emphasized the seriousness of the proceedings.

Synonyms

  • bearing
    5.3 0.03/m
    bearing shows how someone carries their body with dignity and control, while comportment means their general formal behavior and conduct.
  • mien
    9.1 0.35/m
    mien shows a person's look or outward manner that hints at mood or character, while comportment describes their formal behavior.
  • decorum
    7.3 1.92/m
    decorum refers to correct, polite behavior in a situation, while comportment focuses on a person's dignified manner and conduct.

Related Terms

  • synonym: behavior - the way in which one acts or conducts oneself
  • synonym: conduct - the manner in which a person behaves
  • synonym: demeanor - outward behavior or bearing
