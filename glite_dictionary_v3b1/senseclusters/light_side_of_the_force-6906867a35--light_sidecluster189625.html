light side of the Force

noun
metaphorical 'morally good choice'
7.3
Pronunciation: /laɪt saɪd əv ðə fɔːrs/ /; / /laɪt saɪd əv ðiː fɔːrs/ (AmE), /laɪt saɪd əv ðə fɔːs/ /; / /laɪt saɪd əv ðiː fɔːs/ (BrE)
used metaphorically (from Star Wars) to refer to choosing the morally good, virtuous, or ethical option in real-world situations; not meant literally.

Examples

Choose the light side of the Force
During the debate, many urged the candidates to take the light side of the Force on climate policy.
She joked that by donating, she was choosing the light side of the Force over selfishness today.
Advisers told him his best course was to align with the light side of the Force for public support.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dark side of the Force - the evil or immoral aspect of the Force; rhetorically opposed to the 'light side of the Force'.
  • synonym: moral choice - a decision between actions judged by standards of right and wrong.
