Ottomanism

noun
Ottoman-inspired style
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɑtəmənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒtəmənˌɪzəm / (BrE)
a style or influence in art, architecture, textiles, or design that is derived from Ottoman decorative and aesthetic traditions

Examples

Ottomanism in design
The new hotel lobby features Ottomanism in its intricate tilework and luxurious fabrics.
Designers often incorporate elements of Ottomanism to create a sense of historical elegance.
Her living room, decorated with Ottomanism, displays rich colors and elaborate patterns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernism - a style in art and architecture characterized by a deliberate break from traditional forms
  • synonym: orientalism - a style in Western art and design inspired by Asian and Middle Eastern cultures
