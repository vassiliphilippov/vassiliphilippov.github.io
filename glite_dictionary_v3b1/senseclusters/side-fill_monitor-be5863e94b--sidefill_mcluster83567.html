side-fill monitor

noun
stage loudspeaker at side for performers
9.2 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.fɪl ˈmɑːn.ɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈsaɪd.fɪl ˈmɑː.nə.tər/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.fɪl ˈmɒn.ɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈsaɪd.fɪl ˈmɒn.ə.tə/ (BrE)
A loudspeaker positioned at the side of a stage, used to deliver sound to areas not reached by floor monitors.

Examples

A side-fill monitor speaker
Large stages often require both floor and side-fill monitors to ensure all performers hear the mix.
The band asked the sound engineer to increase the volume in the side-fill monitor during the concert.
Without a side-fill monitor, the drummer struggled to hear the vocalist clearly on stage.
