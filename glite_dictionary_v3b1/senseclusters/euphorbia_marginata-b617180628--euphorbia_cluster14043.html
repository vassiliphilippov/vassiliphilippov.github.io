Euphorbia marginata

noun
annual spurge with white-margined bracts
Pronunciation: / juːˈfɔrbiə ˌmɑrdʒɪˈneɪtə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə ˌmɑːdʒɪˈneɪtə / (BrE)
A species of annual spurge native to the western United States, known for its showy white-margined bracts and extremely poisonous milky sap.

Examples

Euphorbia marginata plant
Euphorbia marginata is often grown as an ornamental plant for its decorative foliage.
The garden was lined with Euphorbia marginata, its white-edged leaves providing a striking contrast to the green lawn.
Wear gloves when handling Euphorbia marginata because its milky sap is highly toxic.
