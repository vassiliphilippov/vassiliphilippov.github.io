contemn

verb
to look down on with contempt
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛm / (AmE), / kənˈtɛm / (BrE)
to regard or treat with contempt; to look down on with disdain

Examples

To contemn others
He was taught never to contemn another person, regardless of their status.
Those who contemn established traditions often struggle to win popular support.
Throughout her career, she refused to contemn the less fortunate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: despise - to feel contempt or a deep repugnance for
  • antonym: respect - to admire (someone or something) deeply, as a result of their abilities, qualities, or achievements
  • synonym: scorn - to feel or express contempt or derision for
