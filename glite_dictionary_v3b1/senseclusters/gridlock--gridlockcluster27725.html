gridlock

verb
to block completely
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɪdˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪdˌlɒk / (BrE)
to block or bring to a complete standstill, especially by causing severe traffic congestion or procedural deadlock

Examples

Accident gridlocked traffic
A major accident gridlocked the downtown area for several hours during rush hour.
Negotiations were gridlocked by disagreements between the two sides, delaying any progress.
Heavy snow gridlocked the city, making it impossible for vehicles to move.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jam - to block or become stuck so that movement is impossible
  • synonym: paralyze - to make unable to move or function
  • antonym: unblock - to remove an obstruction or allow movement again
