retrack

verb
to record again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtræk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtræk / (BrE)
to record or replace a musical or audio track again, usually in a studio setting

Examples

To retrack the vocals
The band decided to retrack the guitar solo to achieve a cleaner sound.
After listening to the playback, she chose to retrack her vocals for better clarity.
Producers often ask artists to retrack certain parts to improve the final mix.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overwrite - to replace existing recorded material with new material
  • synonym: re-record - to record something again
