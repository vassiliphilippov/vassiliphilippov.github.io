consonance

noun
harmonious combination of sounds
9.6 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.sə.nəns/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.sə.nəns/ (BrE)
A combination of sounds or notes that are harmonious when heard together; the quality of being agreeable or compatible in sound.

Examples

Musical consonance
In music, consonance is achieved when notes blend smoothly without harshness.
Poets often use consonance to give their verses a pleasing rhythm and sound.
The choir's consonance created a soothing and peaceful atmosphere in the church.
