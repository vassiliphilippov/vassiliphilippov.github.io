acrocentric chromosome

noun
chromosome with centromere near one end
Pronunciation: / ˌækrəˈsɛntrɪk ˈkroʊməˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˌækrəʊˈsɛntrɪk ˈkrəʊməsəʊm / (BrE)
a chromosome in which the centromere is positioned near one end, resulting in one very short arm and one long arm

Examples

An acrocentric chromosome
Down syndrome is associated with a trisomy of an acrocentric chromosome, specifically chromosome 21.
In humans, several chromosomes are classified as acrocentric chromosomes because of the position of their centromeres.
Researchers observed that the acrocentric chromosome had a short p arm and a much longer q arm under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: metacentric chromosome - a chromosome with a centrally placed centromere, resulting in arms of equal length
  • antonym: submetacentric chromosome - a chromosome with the centromere slightly off center, giving arms of unequal length but not as extreme as acrocentric
