quench

verb
reduce luminescence in a material (chemistry)
6.6 6.3
Pronunciation: / kwɛntʃ / (AmE), / kwɛntʃ / (BrE)
to reduce or stop a physical phenomenon such as light, fluorescence, or an electrical discharge (technical/scientific use)

Examples

To quench fluorescence
Researchers were able to quench the fluorescence by introducing a specific chemical agent.
The dye's emission can be quenched by adding copper ions to the solution.
To prevent interference in the experiment, the technician quenched the luminescence rapidly with a solvent.
An additional circuit was added to quench the unwanted oscillations in the amplifier.
Proper design helps quench electrical discharges before they can damage sensitive components.
The capacitor is designed to quench the spark created when the switch is opened.

  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to something, especially forcibly
