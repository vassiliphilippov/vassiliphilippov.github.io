tear apart

verb
harshly criticize or find major faults
2.7
Pronunciation: / tɛr əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / tɪə əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to criticize something or someone very severely; to find and discuss all the faults of something or someone

Examples

To tear apart an argument
Her argument was torn apart by the opposition during the debate.
The book was torn apart in several newspaper reviews after its release.
The critics tore apart the director’s latest film for its weak plot and poor performances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pan - criticize harshly, especially in a review
  • antonym: praise - express approval or admiration for someone or something
