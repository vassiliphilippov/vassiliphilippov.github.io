sweet

adjective
having a high sugar content (wine)
Pronunciation: / swit / (AmE), / swiːt / (BrE)
having a sugary taste; tasting of sugar or something sweet (including food, drink, or wine)

Examples

Sweet chocolate cake
She prefers sweet wines over dry reds for their fruity flavors.
The menu lists several sweet options, including a late-harvest Zinfandel.
This dessert pairs perfectly with a sweet Riesling.
Is this iced tea sweet or plain?
She prefers her coffee sweet, with extra sugar.
The baker offered both sweet and unsweetened pastries.
She prefers sweet desserts like chocolate cake over salty snacks.
The lemonade was too sweet for my taste, so I added more water.
The mangoes are perfectly ripe and deliciously sweet this season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bitter - having a sharp, often unpleasant taste; not sweet
  • antonym: dry - (of wines) lacking sweetness; having no or little residual sugar
  • antonym: savory - not sweet; salty or spicy in taste
  • antonym: sour - having an acidic taste; not sweet
  • synonym: sugary - containing or tasting like sugar
  • antonym: unsweetened - (of food or drink) not having added sugar or sweetener
