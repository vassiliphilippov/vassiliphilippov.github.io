frogmarch

verb
force a decision or bill through
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfrɑːg.mɑːrtʃ/ /; / /ˈfrɔːg.mɑːrtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈfrɒg.mɑːtʃ/ (BrE)
(figurative) to force a proposal, bill, decision, or policy through a process by using heavy-handed or coercive pressure, especially in a legislative or organizational setting.

Examples

To frogmarch a bill
The minister frogmarched the controversial bill through the committee despite fierce objections from MPs.
Publishers accused the board of frogmarching the new policy into effect without consulting authors.
Union leaders said management had frogmarched the contract approval through during emergency talks last night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: force through - to make something (such as a law or policy) happen despite opposition or obstacles.
  • synonym: railroad - to push something (for example, a bill or decision) through quickly and forcefully, often ignoring opposition or proper process.
