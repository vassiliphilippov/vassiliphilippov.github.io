logical positivism

noun
philosophy: theory emphasizing empirically based knowledge
6.1 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl ˈpɑː.zə.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl ˈpɑː.zɪ.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a philosophical theory asserting that all knowledge is derived from logical inference based on empirical, perceptual experience, rejecting metaphysics and intuition as sources of knowledge

Examples

Logical positivism theory
Many early 20th-century philosophers were deeply influenced by logical positivism and its view of science.
She wrote her thesis on the impact of logical positivism on analytic philosophy.
The decline of logical positivism led philosophers to reconsider the limits of scientific inquiry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empiricism - the theory that all knowledge is derived from sense experience
  • antonym: metaphysics - branch of philosophy exploring fundamental nature of reality
