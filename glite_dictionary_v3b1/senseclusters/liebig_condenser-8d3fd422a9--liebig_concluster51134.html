Liebig condenser

noun
water-cooled laboratory condenser
Pronunciation: / ˈliːbɪɡ kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈliːbɪɡ kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a laboratory device used in distillation, consisting of a tube surrounded by a water-cooled jacket through which vapor passes and condenses to liquid.

Examples

A Liebig condenser device
A Liebig condenser is often used in school chemistry labs for simple distillation procedures.
After assembling the Liebig condenser, she began circulating cold water to ensure efficient condensation.
The Liebig condenser was connected to the flask to cool and condense the vapor during the experiment.
