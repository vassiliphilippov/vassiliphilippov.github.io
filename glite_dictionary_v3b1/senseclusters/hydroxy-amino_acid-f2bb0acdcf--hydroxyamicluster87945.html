hydroxy-amino acid

noun
amino acid with a hydroxy group
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑksi əˈminoʊ ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒksi əˈmiːnəʊ ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
An amino acid that contains a hydroxy group attached to its side chain.

Examples

Common hydroxy-amino acid
Serine is a common hydroxy-amino acid found in many proteins.
The structure of a hydroxy-amino acid includes both an amino group and a hydroxy group.
Threonine and serine are examples of hydroxy-amino acids important in metabolism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amino acid - an organic compound with amino and carboxyl groups, the building block of proteins
