water of life

noun
something essential for survival; vital drink
2.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər əv laɪf/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər əv laɪf/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə əv laɪf/ /; / /ˈwɔː.tə ə laɪf/ (BrE)
something regarded as essential for survival or well-being, especially a vital drink; historically, a term for certain spirits such as whiskey or brandy.

Examples

The water of life
Clean, fresh drinking water is truly the water of life in many drought-stricken areas.
He called art the water of life for his soul, suggesting it was essential to his happiness.
Some people refer to whiskey as the water of life due to its importance in certain cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aqua vitae - Latin term for distilled spirits such as whiskey or brandy
  • synonym: elixir - a magical or medicinal potion, especially one thought to prolong life indefinitely
