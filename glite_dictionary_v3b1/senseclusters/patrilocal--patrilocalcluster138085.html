patrilocal

adjective
living with husband's family
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpætrɪˈloʊkəl / (AmE), / ˌpætrɪˈləʊkəl / (BrE)
describing a custom in which a married couple lives with or near the husband's family

Examples

Patrilocal society
In a patrilocal society, newlyweds often move into the husband's family home.
Anthropologists study how patrilocal traditions affect family relationships in different cultures.
The village maintained a patrilocal pattern, with sons remaining close to their parents after marriage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: matrilocal - describing a custom where a married couple lives with or near the wife's family
