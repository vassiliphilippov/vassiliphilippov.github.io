ferment

noun
substance that causes fermentation
Pronunciation: / fərˈmɛnt / (AmE), / fəˈmɛnt / (BrE)
a substance, such as an enzyme or microorganism, that causes fermentation

Examples

Yeast ferment
Early scientists discovered that yeast acted as a natural ferment in bread making.
The brewer added a special ferment to the mixture to help convert sugar into alcohol.
Without the right ferment, the process of making cheese would not succeed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a catalyst in living organisms
