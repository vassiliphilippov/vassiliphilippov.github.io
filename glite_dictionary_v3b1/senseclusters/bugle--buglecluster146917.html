bugle

verb
to make a resonant call
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbjuː.gəl/ /; / /ˈbuː.gəl/ (AmE), /ˈbjuː.gəl/ /; / /ˈbuː.gəl/ (BrE)
to make a loud, deep, resonant call, especially as some male animals do during mating season

Examples

Elk bugle loudly
During the autumn, male elk bugle to attract mates and establish territory.
We heard the distant sound of a stag bugling in the early morning mist.
The guide pointed out how the moose would bugle to warn others of danger.
The elk bugled loudly across the valley at dawn.
During the ceremony, a soldier bugled the national anthem with great skill.
In the distance, we heard a stag bugling to attract a mate.
