Benedictine order

noun
Catholic monastic order founded in the 6th century
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛnəˈdɪktɪn ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈdɪktɪn ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic monastic order founded in the 6th century, known for its liturgical worship and scholarly activities.

Examples

The Benedictine order monastery
He spent several years in a monastery belonging to the Benedictine order.
The Benedictine order played a crucial role in preserving literature during the Middle Ages.
The traditions of the Benedictine order continue to influence monastic life today.
