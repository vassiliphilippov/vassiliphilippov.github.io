urography

noun
urinary tract imaging
6.8
Pronunciation: / jʊˈrɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / jʊəˈrɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique that uses X-rays to examine the urinary tract (including the kidneys, ureters, and bladder)

Examples

Urography X-ray test
The doctor ordered a urography to help diagnose the cause of the patient's blood in urine.
After the urography, the radiologist explained the results to the patient.
Modern urography techniques can provide detailed images of the kidneys and bladder.
The doctor ordered a urography to help diagnose the patient's kidney problem.
After reviewing the urography, the radiologist explained the results to the medical team.
Modern urography techniques can detect even small abnormalities in the urinary tract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angiography - an X-ray examination of blood vessels
  • synonym: cystography - X-ray imaging of the bladder
  • synonym: pyelography - X-ray imaging of the renal pelvis and urinary tract
