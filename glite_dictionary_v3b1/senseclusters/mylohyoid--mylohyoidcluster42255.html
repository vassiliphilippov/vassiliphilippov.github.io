mylohyoid

noun
mouth floor muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.lə.ˈhaɪ.ɔɪd/ /; / /maɪ.loʊ.ˈhaɪ.ɔɪd/ (AmE), /maɪ.ləʊ.ˈhaɪ.ɔɪd/ (BrE)
a muscle forming the floor of the mouth, connecting the lower jaw to the hyoid bone

Examples

The mylohyoid supports swallowing
The dentist explained that the mylohyoid supports the tongue and helps with swallowing.
During the procedure, the surgeon identified the mylohyoid to avoid damaging important structures.
Pain in the mylohyoid can sometimes be mistaken for a sore throat.

Related Terms
