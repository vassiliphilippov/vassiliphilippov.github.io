perdurantist

adjective
philosophy of persistence
9.9
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈdʊr.ənt.ɪst/ /; / /pər.ˈdjʊr.ənt.ɪst/ (AmE), /pə.ˈdjʊə.rənt.ɪst/ /; / /pə.ˈdʊə.rənt.ɪst/ (BrE)
relating to the philosophical view that objects persist through time by having different temporal parts at different times.

Examples

Perdurantist theory
The perdurantist approach explains how objects can change over time yet remain the same.
Many philosophers debate whether a perdurantist or endurantist theory better describes identity.
Her argument was based on a perdurantist understanding of time and existence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endurantist - relating to the view that objects persist by being wholly present at each moment
