metallization

noun
becoming metallic
8.4
Pronunciation: / məˌtæləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌtælɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a material changes from a nonmetallic or insulating state to a metallic, electrically conductive state

Examples

Metallization under pressure
Scientists study metallization to understand how certain materials become electrically conductive under pressure.
The metallization of hydrogen is a topic of great interest in physics research.
During metallization, the material's properties change dramatically, allowing it to conduct electricity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insulation - the state of being non-conductive or nonmetallic
  • degree higher: metallic - having the properties of a metal
