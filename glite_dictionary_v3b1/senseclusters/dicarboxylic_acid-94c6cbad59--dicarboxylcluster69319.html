dicarboxylic acid

noun
compound with two carboxyl groups
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.kɑːrˌbɑkˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.kɑːˌbɒkˈsɪlɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an organic compound containing two carboxyl functional groups (-COOH) attached to its molecule

Examples

A dicarboxylic acid molecule
One common dicarboxylic acid used in chemistry is oxalic acid.
Phthalic acid is an aromatic dicarboxylic acid important in industrial applications.
The synthesis of nylon involves the use of a dicarboxylic acid as a key reactant.
