undersupply

verb
to provide too little
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərsəˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəsəˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to provide less of something than is needed or demanded

Examples

To undersupply medicine
If hospitals undersupply vaccines, many people may not get immunized in time.
The store often undersupplies popular items during the holiday season, leading to empty shelves.
When companies undersupply essential goods, prices can rise due to high demand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oversupply - to provide more of something than is needed
  • degree higher: supply - to provide what is needed or demanded
  • synonym: underprovide - to supply less than is necessary
