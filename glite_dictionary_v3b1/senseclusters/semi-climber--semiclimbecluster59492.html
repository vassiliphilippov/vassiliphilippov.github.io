semi-climber

noun
a plant that partially climbs; can grow like a vine
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmiˌklaɪmər / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌklaɪmə / (BrE)
a plant that exhibits a tendency to climb but may also grow as a vine without fully attaching itself for support

Examples

A semi-climber plant
The garden includes several semi-climbers that provide both ground cover and vertical interest.
This species is a semi-climber, often spreading along the ground unless support is provided.
Unlike true climbers, semi-climbers may need some training to grow up a trellis.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: climber - a plant that climbs up surfaces by twining or gripping
  • degree lower: vine - a plant with a long, thin stem that grows along the ground or climbs
