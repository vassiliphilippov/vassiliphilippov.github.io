F-actin

noun
filamentous form of actin protein
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɛf ˌæktɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛf ˌæktɪn / (BrE)
the filamentous, polymerized form of actin found in cells, forming part of the cytoskeleton

Examples

F-actin filament
Drugs that disrupt F-actin can inhibit the division of certain cancer cells.
Researchers observed the rapid assembly of F-actin during the process of cell migration.
The cytoskeleton relies on F-actin to support cellular shape and movement.
