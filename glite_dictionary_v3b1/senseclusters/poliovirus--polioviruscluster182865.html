poliovirus

noun
virus causing poliomyelitis
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊlioʊˌvaɪrəs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊliəʊˌvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a virus that causes poliomyelitis, an infectious disease that can result in paralysis

Examples

poliovirus infection
Global efforts to eradicate poliovirus have significantly reduced the number of cases worldwide.
Scientists have developed vaccines to prevent diseases caused by the poliovirus.
The poliovirus is transmitted primarily through contaminated water and food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poliomyelitis - infectious disease caused by the poliovirus
