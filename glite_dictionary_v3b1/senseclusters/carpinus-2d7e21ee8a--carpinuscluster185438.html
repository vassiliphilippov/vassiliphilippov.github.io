Carpinus

noun
genus of trees (hornbeams)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrpɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪnəs / (BrE)
A genus of mostly deciduous trees or shrubs, commonly known as hornbeams, found in temperate regions.

Examples

Carpinus tree species
Gardeners appreciate Carpinus for its dense foliage and its usefulness in hedges.
The leaves of Carpinus are distinctive, with a serrated edge and prominent veins.
There are several species within the genus Carpinus found throughout Europe and Asia.
