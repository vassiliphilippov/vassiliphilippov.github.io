rosette

noun
circular window with decorative tracery
Pronunciation: / roʊˈzɛt / (AmE), / rəʊˈzɛt / (BrE)
a circular window with decorative stonework or tracery, typically found in Gothic architecture

Examples

Gothic rosette window
Architects restored the original rosette painstakingly, preserving its intricate Gothic design.
The cathedral's magnificent rosette illuminated the nave with colored light at sunset.
Through the stained glass rosette, morning sunlight cast vibrant patterns on the floor.
