disbeliever

noun
person who doubts
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsbɪˈliːvər / (AmE), / ˌdɪsbɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
a person who does not believe something, especially a particular idea, claim, or religion

Examples

She was known as a disbeliever who always questioned popular opinions.

Despite the evidence, some disbelievers remained unconvinced by the results.
He was known as a disbeliever who rarely accepted things at face value.
The disbeliever challenged the traditional views presented at the meeting.
As a known disbeliever, she often engaged in debates about religion and science.
Some communities may treat a disbeliever with suspicion or distance.
