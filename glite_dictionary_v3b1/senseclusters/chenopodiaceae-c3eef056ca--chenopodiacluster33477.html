Chenopodiaceae

noun
former plant family including spinach and beets
9.9
Pronunciation: /kiː.nə.poʊ.di.ˈeɪ.siː/ /; / /kɪ.nə.poʊ.di.ˈeɪ.siː/ (AmE), /kiː.nə.pəʊ.di.ˈeɪ.siː/ /; / /kɪ.nə.pəʊ.di.ˈeɪ.siː/ (BrE)
a former family of flowering plants, including spinach and beets, now generally treated as a subfamily (Chenopodioideae) of the Amaranthaceae family

Examples

Chenopodiaceae plant family
Botanists now consider Chenopodiaceae a subfamily within Amaranthaceae, but the older name is still encountered.
Spinach and beets were once classified under Chenopodiaceae, before taxonomic revisions.
The family Chenopodiaceae includes many species adapted to saline environments.
