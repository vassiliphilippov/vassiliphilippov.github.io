water vascular system

noun
fluid-filled canal system in echinoderms
3.1 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər ˈvæs.kjə.lər ˈsɪs.təm/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər ˈvæs.kjə.lər ˈsɪs.təm/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə ˈvæs.kjə.lə ˈsɪs.təm/ /; / /ˈwɔː.tə ˈvæs.kjʊ.lə ˈsɪs.təm/ (BrE)
a system of fluid-filled canals and tubes in echinoderms, used for locomotion, feeding, and respiration

Examples

The water vascular system in echinoderms
Damage to the water vascular system can impair an echinoderm's ability to survive.
Starfish rely on their water vascular system to move their tube feet across the ocean floor.
The water vascular system helps sea urchins capture food and exchange gases.
