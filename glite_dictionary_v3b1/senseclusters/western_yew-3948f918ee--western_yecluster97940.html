western yew

noun
a Pacific coast North American tree; Taxus brevifolia
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn juː / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən juː / (BrE)
a small or medium-sized tree (Taxus brevifolia) native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for its irregular branching and fine, hard, close-grained wood

Examples

Western yew tree
Western yew trees can be found in moist forests along the Pacific Northwest.
Conservationists are concerned about overharvesting of the western yew for pharmaceutical use.
The wood from the western yew is highly valued for making archery bows.
