artificial pacemaker

noun
implanted device regulating heartbeat
4.3 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.tɪ.ˈfɪʃ.əl ˈpeɪs.meɪ.kər/ /; / /ɑːr.tə.ˈfɪʃ.əl ˈpeɪs.meɪ.kər/ (AmE), /ɑː.tɪ.ˈfɪʃ.əl ˈpeɪs.meɪ.kə/ /; / /ɑː.tə.ˈfɪʃ.əl ˈpeɪs.meɪ.kə/ (BrE)
A small electronic device implanted in the body to regulate the heartbeat when the heart's natural pacemaker is not functioning properly.

Examples

An artificial pacemaker device
After his surgery, an artificial pacemaker was implanted to help regulate his heartbeat.
Doctors recommended an artificial pacemaker for the patient with a dangerously slow heart rate.
Many elderly patients rely on an artificial pacemaker to maintain a steady heart rhythm.
