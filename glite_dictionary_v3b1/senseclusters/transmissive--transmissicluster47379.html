transmissive

adjective
allowing passage
6.7
Pronunciation: / trænzˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
allowing the passage of light, energy, or signals through a material or medium

Examples

Transmissive glass surface
The transmissive coating on the glass allows sunlight to enter the room efficiently.
Engineers selected a transmissive material for the screen to improve image clarity.
Some insects have transmissive wings that let light shine through them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opaque - not allowing light to pass through
  • synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
