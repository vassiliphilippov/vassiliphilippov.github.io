counterbalanced

adjective
offset by an equal opposing force or weight
3.7 4.6
Pronunciation: /kaʊn.tər.ˈbæl.ənst/ /; / /kaʊn.ər.ˈbæl.ənst/ (AmE), /kaʊn.tə.ˈbæl.ənst/ (BrE)
offset by an equal weight or force to achieve balance; brought into equilibrium by something exerting an opposite effect

Examples

Her cautious optimism was counterbalanced by a lingering sense of doubt.
The bridge's structure is counterbalanced to ensure stability during high winds.
The heavy lid was counterbalanced by a hidden weight inside the mechanism.
