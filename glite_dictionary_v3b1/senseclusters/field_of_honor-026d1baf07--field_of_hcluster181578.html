field of honor

noun
site of a duel
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld əv ˈɑːnɚ / (AmE), / ˈfiːld əv ˈɒnə / (BrE)
the place where a duel is or was fought, especially as a matter of personal honor

Examples

Duel field of honor
He refused to apologize, insisting they settle their differences on the field of honor.
In many stories, disputes were settled on a field of honor rather than in court.
The two rivals met on the field of honor at dawn, pistols drawn.
