sprightly

adjective
lively; full of energy
5.7 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆspraÉªtli / (AmE), / ËˆspraÉªtli / (BrE)
full of energy, spirit, and enthusiasm; lively and active, especially in a way that is cheerful or light-hearted

Examples

A sprightly dance
Despite her age, she remains a sprightly participant in the dance class.
He greeted the guests with a sprightly step and a cheerful smile.
The puppy jumped around with a sprightly enthusiasm that made everyone smile.

Synonyms

  • lively
    1.3 10/m
    lively is active and full of life in many settings, while sprightly feels lighter, more cheerful, and playfully bright.
  • spirited
    4.1 0.07/m
    spirited is energetic and shows strong, lively action in events, while sprightly keeps a gentler, more light-hearted liveliness.
  • effervescent
    7.1 0.28/m
    effervescent seems bubbly and brightly warm in manner, while sprightly is more gently cheerful and lightly active in mood.
  • ebullient
    8.2 0.75/m
    ebullient is loudly joyful and often shows strong excitement, while sprightly is calmer, light-hearted energy without loud intensity.
  • buoyant
    5.8 0.64/m
    buoyant stays cheerful and hopeful even after problems, while sprightly focuses on quick, light cheer and lively movement.
  • peppy
    3.1 0.46/m
    peppy sounds informal and bright in casual speech or pop music, while sprightly feels slightly more old-fashioned and gently refined.
  • spry
    5.3 0.36/m
    spry describes quick, light movement, often for older people, while sprightly stresses cheerful, playful energy rather than agility.
