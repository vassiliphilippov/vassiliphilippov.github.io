opposite

noun
an antonym; word with opposite meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈɑpəzɪt /; / ˈɑːpəzɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒpəzɪt / (BrE)
something or someone that is completely different from or the reverse of another; an antonym

Examples

The opposite of happy
"Happy" and "sad" are common opposites in English.
Can you name an opposite for the word "increase"?
She taught the children to match each word with its opposite.
Her actions were the complete opposite of what she had promised.
In the experiment, the machine moved in the opposite direction to what was expected.
They have entirely different personalities—one is quiet and the opposite is true for the other.
Light and darkness can be seen as opposites in many cultures.
The results were the exact opposite of what we expected.
Though they were twins, their personalities were complete opposites.
