Schmidt telescope

noun
wide-field reflecting telescope with correcting plate
Pronunciation: / ʃmɪt tɛləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ʃmɪt ˈtelɪskəʊp / (BrE)
A reflecting telescope equipped with a specially shaped correcting plate that reduces optical aberrations, allowing a wide area of the sky to be photographed.

Examples

A Schmidt telescope design
Bernhard Schmidt invented the Schmidt telescope to minimize distortions and capture clearer images of stars.
The observatory just installed a new Schmidt telescope to survey the night sky.
Using a Schmidt telescope, astronomers can photograph large regions of the Milky Way in a single exposure.
