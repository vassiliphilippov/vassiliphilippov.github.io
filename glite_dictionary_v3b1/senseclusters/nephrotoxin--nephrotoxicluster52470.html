nephrotoxin

noun
toxin harmful to the kidneys
5.2 8.1
Pronunciation: /nef.rə.ˈtɑːk.sɪn/ (AmE), /nef.rə.ˈtɒk.sɪn/ (BrE)
A substance that is toxic to the kidneys; any toxin that causes damage to kidney tissue.

Examples

A nephrotoxin substance
Exposure to a nephrotoxin can result in acute kidney failure if not promptly treated.
Some antibiotics are classified as nephrotoxins due to their potential to damage renal tissues.
The research focused on identifying the effects of each nephrotoxin in laboratory animals.

  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living cells or organisms
