straight-line method

noun
depreciation method with equal yearly expense
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtˌlaɪn ˌmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt.laɪn ˌmeθ.əd / (BrE)
(accounting) a method of calculating depreciation in which an equal portion of an asset's cost is expensed each year over its useful life.

Examples

The straight-line method of depreciation
Many companies use the straight-line method to depreciate office equipment over five years.
The accountant explained that the straight-line method results in equal annual depreciation expenses.
Unlike the straight-line method, accelerated methods allocate larger expenses to the early years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declining balance method - depreciation method with higher expenses in earlier years
