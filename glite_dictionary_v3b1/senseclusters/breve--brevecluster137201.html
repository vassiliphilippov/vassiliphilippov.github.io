breve

noun
short vowel mark; long note
9.9
Pronunciation: / briːv / (AmE), / briːv / (BrE)
a mark (˘) placed over a vowel to indicate it is short, or a musical note equal to two whole notes in length

Examples

Breve musical note
In the word 'cup', the breve above the 'u' shows it is a short vowel.
The choir held the breve for its full value during the performance.
A breve is often used in dictionaries to help with pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: double whole note - a musical note lasting twice as long as a whole note
  • antonym: macron - a mark (¯) indicating a long vowel
