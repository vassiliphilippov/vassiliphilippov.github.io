brace

verb
support or strengthen with a brace
5.3 3.6
Pronunciation: /breɪs/ (AmE), /breɪs/ (BrE)
to support, strengthen, or steady something, or to prepare yourself for something difficult or unpleasant

Examples

To brace for impact
Engineers braced the old bridge to prevent collapse.
The doctor braced her injured leg with a splint.
You should brace the shelves to keep them from falling.
He braced himself before stepping onto the stage to give his first speech.
She tried to brace herself for the bad news about her job.
The villagers braced for the approaching storm by securing their homes.
She braced herself against the wind as she walked across the bridge.
The doctor told him to brace his leg to avoid further injury.
You should brace the ladder before climbing up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reinforce - to make something stronger; to strengthen, especially by adding support
  • antonym: relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
  • synonym: steady - to make steady or stable
  • synonym: steel oneself - to make yourself ready to face something difficult or unpleasant
  • antonym: weaken - to make or become less strong
