springwood

noun
soft spring tree ring
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsprɪŋ.wʊd/ (AmE), /ˈsprɪŋ.wʊd/ (BrE)
the softer, lighter part of a tree's annual growth ring that forms in the spring

Examples

The springwood band
The pale springwood is visible as the lighter band in each annual ring of the tree.
Scientists study springwood to learn about the climate conditions during the tree's growth.
Compared to latewood, springwood is less dense and easier to cut.

Related Terms

  • antonym: latewood - the denser, darker part of a tree's annual ring formed later in the growing season
