corymb

noun
type of flower cluster (botany)
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkÉ”r.Éªm / (AmE), / ËˆkÉ’.rÉªm / (BrE)
A flat-topped or slightly rounded flower cluster (inflorescence) in which individual flower stalks arise at different points along the main stem but reach about the same height; the outer flowers open first.

Examples

A corymb flower cluster
A corymb can help ensure that all flowers receive equal access to sunlight.
In many species of hawthorn, the blossoms are arranged in a corymb at the tips of branches.
The wild parsley displays a distinctive corymb of tiny white flowers in midsummer.
