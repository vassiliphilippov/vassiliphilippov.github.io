thermoplastic resin

noun
material that softens with heat and hardens when cooled
Pronunciation: / ˌθɝːmoʊˈplæstɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈplastɪk ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic material that softens when heated and hardens again when cooled, allowing it to be molded repeatedly

Examples

Thermoplastic resin material
Advances in thermoplastic resin technology have led to lighter, more flexible car parts.
Many manufacturers prefer thermoplastic resin because it can be easily reshaped with heat.
The toy was made from a durable thermoplastic resin that withstands rough handling.
