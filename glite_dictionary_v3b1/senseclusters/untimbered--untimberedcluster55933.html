untimbered

adjective
without trees; not forested
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtɪmbɚd / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɪmbəd / (BrE)
(of land or an area) having no trees; not covered with forest.

Examples

Untimbered land
The region's untimbered landscape made it ideal for large-scale farming.
The vast untimbered plains stretched for miles in every direction.
We camped in an untimbered valley, far from the dense forests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forested - (of land) covered with trees or forest
