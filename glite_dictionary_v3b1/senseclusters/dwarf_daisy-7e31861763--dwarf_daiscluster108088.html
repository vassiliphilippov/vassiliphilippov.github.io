dwarf daisy

noun
small woolly annual flower native to southwestern US
Pronunciation: / dwɔrf ˈdeɪzi / (AmE), / dwɔːf ˈdeɪzi / (BrE)
A small, woolly annual plant with small golden-yellow flower heads, native to the southwestern United States, sometimes classified in the genus Eriophyllum.

Examples

The dwarf daisy plant
Botanists noted the unique woolly leaves of the dwarf daisy during their field survey.
Gardeners appreciate the dwarf daisy for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
The dwarf daisy blooms early in spring, covering desert slopes with tiny yellow flowers.
