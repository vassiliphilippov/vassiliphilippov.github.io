Petrine

adjective
of Saint Peter
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːtraɪn / (AmE), / ˈpiːtraɪn / (BrE)
relating to Saint Peter or his teachings, especially in Christian contexts

Examples

Petrine doctrine
The Petrine epistles are letters in the New Testament attributed to Saint Peter.
Catholic tradition emphasizes the Petrine authority of the Pope as Peter's successor.
Scholars often debate the influence of Petrine teachings on early Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Pauline - relating to Saint Paul or his teachings
