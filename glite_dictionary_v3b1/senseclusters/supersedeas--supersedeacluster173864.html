supersedeas

noun
order suspending judgment
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈsiːdiːəs / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈsiːdiːæs / (BrE)
a legal order that suspends the enforcement of a court judgment or decision until an appeal is heard

Examples

A supersedeas order
The attorney requested a supersedeas to prevent the immediate enforcement of the judgment.
A supersedeas was granted, allowing the company time to appeal the court's decision.
Without a supersedeas, the losing party would have to comply with the judgment right away.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enforcement - the act of making people obey a law or court order
  • synonym: injunction - a court order requiring someone to do or stop doing something
