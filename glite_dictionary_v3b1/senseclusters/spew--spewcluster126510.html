spew

verb
eject or discharge forcefully in large amounts
5.1 7.06/m
Pronunciation: / spju / (AmE), / spjuÀê / (BrE)
to eject or discharge something forcefully and in large amounts, often in a sudden or uncontrolled way; also used metaphorically for producing large amounts rapidly.

Examples

To spew smoke
She tends to spew complaints whenever something goes wrong at work.
The broken pipe began to spew water all over the basement.
The factory chimneys spew smoke into the sky every morning.

Synonyms

  • spew out
    5.9 0.94/m
    spew out is the common phrase used to send large amounts out suddenly, while spew is the single-word verb.
  • spout
    3.7 0.70/m
    spout sends a strong, steady stream of liquid, while spew often suggests a larger, messier, and less controlled discharge.
  • spurt
    3.1 0.80/m
    spurt shoots out in a short, sudden burst of liquid or gas, while spew implies larger, continuous or repeated flow.
  • jet
    3.0 0.28/m
    jet forces a narrow, high-speed stream from a small opening, while spew suggests large amounts and less narrow direction.
  • eject
    4.1 1.55/m
    eject means to force something out, often by mechanical action, while spew describes uncontrolled, messy discharge or heavy output.
  • eruct
    9.9 0.00/m
    eruct emits or belches out in an explosive, literary way, while spew is the common, ordinary word for loud, large discharge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contain - to keep something within limits or under control
  • synonym: gush - to flow or send out suddenly and in large amounts
  • synonym: vomit - to eject stomach contents through the mouth (also used metaphorically)
