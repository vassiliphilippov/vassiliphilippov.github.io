Synonyms

  • spew out
    5.9 0.7/m
    spew out is the common phrase used to send large amounts out suddenly, while spew is the single-word verb.
  • spout
    3.7 0.5/m
    spout sends a strong, steady stream of liquid, while spew often suggests a larger, messier, and less controlled discharge.
  • spurt
    3.1 0.3/m
    spurt shoots out in a short, sudden burst of liquid or gas, while spew implies larger, continuous or repeated flow.
  • jet
    3.0 0.2/m
    jet forces a narrow, high-speed stream from a small opening, while spew suggests large amounts and less narrow direction.
  • eject
    4.1 0.9/m
    eject means to force something out, often by mechanical action, while spew describes uncontrolled, messy discharge or heavy output.
  • eruct
    9.9 0.00/m
    eruct emits or belches out in an explosive, literary way, while spew is the common, ordinary word for loud, large discharge.
