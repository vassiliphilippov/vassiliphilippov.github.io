poison

verb
to spoil or corrupt (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzən / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən / (BrE)
to spoil, corrupt, or harm the quality or character of something, often in a figurative sense (including corrupting data in computing)

Examples

To poison a friendship
He tried to poison their friendship by spreading lies.
Negative rumors can poison the atmosphere in a workplace.
She warned them not to let jealousy poison their trust.
Hackers attempted to poison the training data to bias the model during evaluation.
The research team discovered that someone had poisoned several datasets with mislabeled images.
Defensive tools detect and remove examples used for poisoning machine-learning systems before retraining.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contaminate - to introduce harmful or unwanted elements into a substance or dataset
  • synonym: corrupt - to make data or information unreliable or incorrect
  • antonym: purify - to remove contaminants or corruption from something
  • synonym: taint - to contaminate or pollute, especially morally
