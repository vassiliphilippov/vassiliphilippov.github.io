choil

noun
notch at knife base
Pronunciation: / tʃɔɪl / (AmE), / tʃɔɪl / (BrE)
a small cutout or notch at the base of a knife blade near the handle, often used to aid sharpening or grip

Examples

The choil on blade
The choil on this chef's knife makes it easier to sharpen the entire edge.
Some knife makers add a choil to improve grip and control during detailed cutting tasks.
He placed his finger on the choil for better precision while slicing vegetables.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ricasso - the unsharpened section of a knife blade near the handle
