patronymic

noun
surname from father's or ancestor's name
Pronunciation: / ˌpætɹəˈnɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpætrəˈnɪmɪk / (BrE)
a family name derived from the name of one's father or a paternal ancestor, often with a prefix or suffix added

Examples

A patronymic ending
'MacDonald' is a classic Scottish patronymic, meaning 'son of Donald'.
In Iceland, each person's last name is a patronymic rather than a family surname.
The use of patronymic surnames was once common in many parts of Europe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: matronymic - a name derived from one's mother's or female ancestor's name
