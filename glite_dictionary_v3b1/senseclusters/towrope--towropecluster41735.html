towrope

noun
rope for towing objects
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊˌroʊp / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌrəʊp / (BrE)
a strong rope used for pulling or towing something, especially a boat or vehicle

Examples

A strong towrope
During the rescue operation, a strong towrope was essential for safety.
The boat was secured with a towrope and pulled back to shore.
They attached a towrope to the truck to haul it out of the mud.

Related Terms

  • synonym: towline - a rope, line, or cable used for towing, especially a boat or ship
