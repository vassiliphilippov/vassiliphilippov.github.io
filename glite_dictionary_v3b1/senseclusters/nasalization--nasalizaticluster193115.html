nasalization

noun
modifying sound using the nose
Pronunciation: / ˌneɪzələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌneɪzəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌneɪz(ə)laɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the production of a sound by allowing air to escape through the nose, often modifying the quality of a vowel or consonant

Examples

Nasalization of vowels
Some languages use nasalization as a distinctive feature in their phonetic system.
Speech therapists sometimes assess nasalization to diagnose certain speech disorders.
The nasalization of vowels is common in French, especially at the end of words.
He could clearly hear the nasalisation in the singer's pronunciation of specific words.
Speech pathologists often study nasalisation to better understand certain speech disorders.
The French language has a high degree of nasalisation in some of its vowels.
