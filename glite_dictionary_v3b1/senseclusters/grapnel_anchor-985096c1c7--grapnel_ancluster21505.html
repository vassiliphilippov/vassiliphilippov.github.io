grapnel anchor

noun
light anchor with prongs for small boats
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræp.nəl ˈæŋ.kɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡræp.nəl ˈæŋ.kə / (BrE)
A lightweight anchor with multiple prongs, used for securing small boats.

Examples

A grapnel anchor with prongs
A grapnel anchor is ideal for rocky or weedy bottoms where its prongs can catch easily.
Before swimming to shore, we made sure the grapnel anchor was secure so the dinghy wouldn't drift away.
The fisherman lowered the grapnel anchor to keep his boat steady in the shallow bay.
