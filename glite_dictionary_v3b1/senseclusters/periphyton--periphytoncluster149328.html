periphyton

noun
algae on surfaces
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˈfaɪtɑn / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈfaɪtɒn / (BrE)
a community of algae and microorganisms that grow attached to underwater surfaces in aquatic environments

Examples

Periphyton on rocks
The scientist collected samples of periphyton from the rocks at the bottom of the lake.
Healthy streams often have a diverse community of periphyton covering submerged surfaces.
Changes in water quality can affect the growth of periphyton in rivers and ponds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biofilm - a thin layer of microorganisms adhering to a surface
  • antonym: plankton - microscopic organisms floating in water
