surreal

adjective
having qualities of surrealism; dreamlike or bizarre
Pronunciation: / səˈriəl / (AmE), / səˈrɪəl / (BrE)
strange, dreamlike, or unreal in a way that is fantastical or confusing

Examples

A surreal dream
The artist's paintings are known for their surreal landscapes and fantastical creatures.
The entire experience felt so surreal that I wasn't sure if I was dreaming.
Walking through the abandoned amusement park at night was a truly surreal experience.
After the accident, everything felt surreal, as if I were walking through a dream.
The artist's paintings often have a surreal quality that draws viewers into a strange world.
The streets were empty and silent, creating a surreal atmosphere in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dreamlike - having qualities similar to a dream; unreal or fantastic
  • antonym: realistic - representing things in a way that is true to life; the opposite of surreal
