stream of consciousness

noun
literary technique showing continuous flow of thoughts
4.8
Pronunciation: / striːm əv ˈkɑːnʃəsnəs / (AmE), / striːm əv ˈkɒnʃəsnəs / (BrE)
a literary style or method that shows a character's thoughts and feelings as they happen, in a continuous and unstructured flow

Examples

Stream of consciousness writing style
In her essay, she analyzed the use of stream of consciousness in Virginia Woolf's works.
Many modernist writers experiment with stream of consciousness as a narrative device.
The novel employs stream of consciousness to depict the protagonist's inner turmoil.
During the interview, she spoke in a stream of consciousness, sharing everything that came to mind.
James Joyce's novels are famous for their use of stream of consciousness to reveal characters' thoughts.
Many modern writers experiment with stream of consciousness to create a sense of realism in their characters' minds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interior monologue - literary device presenting characters' thoughts directly
  • degree lower: narration - the act or process of telling a story
