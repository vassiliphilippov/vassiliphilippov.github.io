parallelism

noun
correspondence or similarity in form or meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌlɛlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈparəlɛlɪzəm / (BrE)
the state of being similar or corresponding in some way, often in structure, meaning, or development

Examples

Parallelism in ideas
The parallelism between their careers was striking, as both became leaders in their fields.
There is a noticeable parallelism in the development of these two scientific theories.
Writers often use parallelism to give rhythm and balance to their sentences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contrast - the state of being strikingly different from something else
  • synonym: similarity - resemblance or likeness
