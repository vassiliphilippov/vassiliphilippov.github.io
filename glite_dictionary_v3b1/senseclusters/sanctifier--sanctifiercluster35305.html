sanctifier

noun
one who makes holy
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋktɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsaŋktɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something holy, sacred, or purified

Examples

Sanctifier in church
The priest acted as the sanctifier during the ceremony.
In many traditions, water is used as a sanctifier to cleanse and bless objects.
He was regarded as a sanctifier because of his role in purifying the temple.
The priest was regarded as a sanctifier of the community's traditions.
In many cultures, water is seen as a sanctifier during religious ceremonies.
The ancient ritual required a sanctifier to bless the new temple before use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blesser - a person who gives a blessing or makes something sacred
  • synonym: consecrator - a person who performs a ceremony to make something sacred
  • antonym: defiler - a person or thing that makes something impure or unholy
  • synonym: purifier - a person or thing that cleans or makes something pure
