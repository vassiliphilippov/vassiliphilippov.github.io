sundog

noun
bright spot by the sun caused by ice crystals
9.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌn.dɑːg/ /; / /ˈsʌn.dɔːg/ (AmE), /ˈsʌn.dɒg/ /; / /ˈsʌn.dɔːg/ (BrE)
a bright spot appearing on either side of the sun, caused by the refraction of sunlight by ice crystals in the atmosphere; also called a parhelion

Examples

A bright sundog
A sundog was visible to the left of the sun as we drove across the snowy plain.
Photographing a sundog requires cold weather and the right atmospheric conditions.
Several sundogs appeared in the winter sky, creating a stunning optical display.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parhelion - a bright spot appearing beside the sun, similar meteorological phenomenon
