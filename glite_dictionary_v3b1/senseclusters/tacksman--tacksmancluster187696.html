tacksman

noun
land sublessor (Scotland)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtæksmən / (AmE), / ˈtæksmən / (BrE)
a person, especially in historical Scotland, who leased land from a landlord and sublet it to tenant farmers

Examples

A tacksman leases land
In 18th-century Scotland, a tacksman often managed large areas of farmland for the estate owner.
The tacksman negotiated leases with several families who worked the land.
After the reforms, the role of the tacksman gradually disappeared from Scottish society.
