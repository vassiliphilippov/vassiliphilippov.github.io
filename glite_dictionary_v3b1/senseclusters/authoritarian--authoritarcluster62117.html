expecting, demanding, or enforcing strict obedience to authority, often at the expense of personal freedom

Examples

Authoritarian leader
Her authoritarian teaching style left little room for student creativity or input.
The authoritarian manager expected his staff to follow every order without question.
They grew up in a highly authoritarian household where speaking out was discouraged.
His authoritarian style left little room for dissent among employees.
Many people fled the authoritarian government in search of freedom.
The country was governed by an authoritarian regime for over two decades.

Synonyms

  • dictatorial
    7.7 1.47/m
    dictatorial is having total power and forcing unquestioning obedience, while authoritarian focuses on strict control that limits personal freedom.
  • autocratic
    8.0 2.34/m
    autocratic concentrates power in one leader and leaves little input from others, while authoritarian stresses strict obedience and limited freedom.
  • oppressive
    6.3 4.41/m
    oppressive applies harsh, unfair pressure that harms people, while authoritarian emphasizes enforcing obedience and authority over personal liberty.
  • despotic
    8.4 0.84/m
    despotic rules with absolute, cruel power and ignores rights, while authoritarian focuses on strict obedience often at the expense of freedom.
  • tyrannical
    7.9 2.17/m
    tyrannical uses cruel and unjust power to harm people, while authoritarian demands strict obedience and firm control over daily life.
  • tyrannic
    9.1 0.01/m
    tyrannic shows the harsh, absolute power of a tyrant in history or books, while authoritarian speaks of strict modern control.
  • iron-fisted
    7.6 0.07/m
    iron-fisted uses a strong, harsh hand to crush dissent, while authoritarian denotes a system that enforces obedience and limits freedom.
