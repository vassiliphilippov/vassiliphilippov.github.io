intrusive

adjective
tending to intrude or disturb
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruːsɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːsɪv / (BrE)
tending to intrude or enter where one is not wanted or invited, especially in a way that disturbs someone’s privacy or peace.

Examples

Intrusive questions
She apologized for being intrusive and promised to respect their privacy.
Some advertisements can feel intrusive when they interrupt your online videos.
The reporter's intrusive questions made the celebrity uncomfortable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discreet - careful not to cause embarrassment or attract much attention
  • synonym: invasive - tending to intrude aggressively or spread undesirably
