unseasoned

adjective
not aged or processed (timber, etc.)
7.1 4.5
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈsiː.zənd/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈsiː.zənd/ (BrE)
not aged or processed to develop desired qualities, especially referring to timber or other materials

Examples

Unseasoned wood
Freshly cut logs are usually unseasoned and need time to dry before use.
Furniture made from unseasoned wood is more likely to crack and shrink.
The builder refused to use unseasoned timber for the framing due to its tendency to warp.
