revocability

noun
ability to be revoked
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛvəkəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌriːvəkəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be revoked, cancelled, or withdrawn

Examples

The revocability of a contract
The revocability of the contract allows either party to end the agreement at any time.
Lawyers debated the revocability of the will in court.
Before signing, she asked about the revocability of her consent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cancellation - the act of making something no longer valid
  • antonym: irrevocability - the quality of being impossible to revoke or cancel
