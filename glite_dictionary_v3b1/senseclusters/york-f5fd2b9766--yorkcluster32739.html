York

noun
English royal house, 1461–1485
Pronunciation: / jɔrk / (AmE), / jɔːk / (BrE)
the English royal house, a branch of the Plantagenet dynasty, that ruled from 1461 to 1485, symbolized by a white rose

Examples

York dynasty
Edward IV was a prominent monarch from the York dynasty.
The House of York was represented by a white rose during the Wars of the Roses.
The defeat of Richard III ended the reign of the York royal house.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Lancaster - the rival English royal house in the Wars of the Roses
