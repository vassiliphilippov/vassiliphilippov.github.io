nonrecourse

noun
loan limited to collateral
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.rɪˈkɔrs / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈkɔːs / (BrE)
a type of loan in which the lender can only claim the specified collateral if the borrower defaults, not the borrower's other assets

Examples

A nonrecourse loan
The company secured a nonrecourse loan to finance the construction of the new building.
With a nonrecourse agreement, the lender cannot pursue the borrower's other assets if the loan is unpaid.
Investors often prefer nonrecourse loans to limit their financial risk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recourse - a loan where the lender can claim other assets if the collateral is insufficient
