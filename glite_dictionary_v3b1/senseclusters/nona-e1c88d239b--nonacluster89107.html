Nona

proper noun
Roman Fate goddess
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnoʊ.nə/ /; / /ˈnoʊ.nɑː/ (AmE), /ˈnəʊ.nə/ /; / /ˈnəʊ.nɑː/ (BrE)
in Roman mythology, one of the three Fates (Parcae), a goddess who determined the length of a person's life and was associated with childbirth

Examples

Roman goddess Nona
A marble statue of Nona stood in the ancient Roman temple dedicated to the Fates.
Legends say that Nona spun the thread that determined each person's lifespan.
During childbirth, Roman women would pray to Nona for protection and good fortune.
