incriminatory

adjective
implying guilt or blame
4.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkrɪməˌnɛtɔri / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɪmɪnətəri / (BrE)
suggesting or implying that someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing

Examples

Incriminatory remarks
Her remarks were highly incriminatory and cast doubt on the witness’s honesty.
The detective presented incriminatory evidence during the trial.
The report contained several incriminatory statements linking him to the incident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exculpatory - clearing from alleged fault or guilt
  • synonym: inculpatory - implying blame or responsibility for a crime
