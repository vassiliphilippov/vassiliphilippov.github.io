foreclose

verb
take possession of mortgaged property for nonpayment
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈkloʊz / (AmE), / fɔːˈkləʊz / (BrE)
to take legal action to take possession of a mortgaged property when the borrower fails to keep up their mortgage payments, thereby ending the right of redemption

Examples

To foreclose a house
Due to rising unemployment, more lenders have been forced to foreclose this year than last.
If you fail to make your mortgage payments, the lender may foreclose on your home.
The bank decided to foreclose on the property after several missed payments.
