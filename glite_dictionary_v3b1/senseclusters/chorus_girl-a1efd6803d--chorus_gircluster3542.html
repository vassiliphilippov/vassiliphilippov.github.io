chorus girl

noun
female dancer in a theatrical chorus line
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəs ɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrəs ɡɜːl / (BrE)
a woman who dances as part of the chorus line in a theatrical production, such as a musical or revue

Examples

Chorus girl dances
In the 1920s, being a chorus girl was a glamorous yet demanding profession.
She dreamed of becoming a chorus girl on Broadway after graduating high school.
The talented chorus girls brought energy and excitement to the final act of the show.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chorus boy - a man who dances in a chorus line
  • synonym: showgirl - a woman who performs in musical or theatrical productions, often in elaborate costumes
