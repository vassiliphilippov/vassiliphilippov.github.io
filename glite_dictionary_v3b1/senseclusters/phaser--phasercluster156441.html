phaser

noun
audio effect device
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfeɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈfeɪ.zə/ (BrE)
an electronic audio effect or device that alters the phase of a signal to create a sweeping, whooshing sound

Examples

Guitar phaser effect
The guitarist used a phaser to add a swirling effect to his solo.
Many keyboard players love the unique sound a phaser brings to their music.
She adjusted the settings on her phaser pedal to get the perfect tone for the song.

  • synonym: flanger - an audio effect that mixes two identical signals with a slight delay to create a swirling sound
