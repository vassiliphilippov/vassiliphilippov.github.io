Arctic hare

noun
a large, white-furred hare of northern North America
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɑɹktɪk hɛɹ /; / ˈɑrktɪk hɛr / (AmE), / ˈɑːktɪk heə / (BrE)
a large hare native to northern North America that turns almost completely white in winter and is adapted to cold Arctic environments

Examples

The Arctic hare in winter
During the harsh winter months, the Arctic hare relies on its white fur for camouflage.
Unlike other hares, the Arctic hare can dig shelters in the snow to keep warm.
We spotted a group of Arctic hares hopping across the tundra in search of food.
During winter, the fur of the Arctic hare becomes almost completely white for camouflage.
The Arctic hare can survive in extremely cold environments due to its thick fur.
We spotted a group of Arctic hares while hiking in northern Canada.
