minister

noun

a member of the clergy, especially in Protestant churches, authorized to conduct religious worship and administer religious rites.

Examples

Church minister
After the service, the minister greeted members of the congregation at the door.
She trained for many years before becoming a minister in the local church.
The new minister delivered an inspiring sermon on Sunday.

Synonyms

  • minister of religion
    3.8 0.04/m
    minister of religion is the formal legal title used in laws and official records, while minister is the everyday church word.
  • pastor
    3.0 22/m
    pastor leads a local congregation and cares for people, while minister is a general clergy member who can have various roles.
  • parson
    8.2 0.76/m
    parson serves a parish and often lives in the community, while minister is a wider term for Protestant clergy.
  • cleric
    6.4 4.64/m
    cleric is any member of the clergy across religions or ranks, while minister refers especially to Protestant worship leaders.
