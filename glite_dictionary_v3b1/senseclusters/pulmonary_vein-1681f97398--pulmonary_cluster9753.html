pulmonary vein

noun
vein carrying blood from lungs to heart
3.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpʌl.mə.nər.iː veɪn/ /; / /ˈpʊl.mə.nər.iː veɪn/ (AmE), /ˈpʌl.mə.nə.riː veɪn/ /; / /ˈpʊl.mə.nə.riː veɪn/ (BrE)
any of the veins that carry oxygenated blood from the lungs to the left atrium of the heart

Examples

The pulmonary vein carries blood
A blood clot in a pulmonary vein can cause serious complications.
Oxygen-rich blood flows through the pulmonary vein back to the heart.
The surgeon located the pulmonary vein during the heart operation.
