Synonyms

  • amalgamation
    7.5 0.75/m
    amalgamation is the process of joining two or more entities like companies into one, while incorporation is a general act of forming a single whole.
  • consolidation
    5.5 5.25/m
    consolidation combines parts into a single, stronger, more coherent whole for efficiency, while incorporation simply means forming one whole.
  • integration
    4.0 14/m
    integration brings separate parts together so they work as a whole, while incorporation means forming a single whole without focus on function.
  • unification
    4.3 4.51/m
    unification joins groups or people into one unit often for a common goal, while incorporation is the general act of forming a single whole.
  • coalescence
    7.8 0.28/m
    coalescence is a gradual coming together of different parts into one natural whole, while incorporation means the act of forming one whole.
