Coprinus atramentarius

noun
a type of mushroom (inky cap)
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈpraɪnəs ˌætrəmənˈtɛriəs / (AmE), / kəʊˈprɪnəs ˌætrəmɛnˈteəriəs / (BrE)
A species of mushroom, known as the inky cap, whose cap dissolves into a black, inky fluid after the spores mature.

Examples

The Coprinus atramentarius mushroom
After picking some Coprinus atramentarius, he warned not to consume alcohol with them due to their toxicity.
Overnight, the caps of the Coprinus atramentarius melted into a black, inky liquid.
The Coprinus atramentarius is often found growing in clusters on rotting wood or lawns.
