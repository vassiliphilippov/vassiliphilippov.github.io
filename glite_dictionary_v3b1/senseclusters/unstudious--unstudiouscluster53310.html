unstudious

adjective
not inclined to study
7.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàstuÀêdi…ôs / (AmE), /  ånÀàstjuÀêdi…ôs / (BrE)
not inclined to study or read; not studious.

Examples

An unstudious student
His unstudious habits made it hard for him to keep up with the rest of the class.
She was criticized for being unstudious and uninterested in academic achievement.
The teacher encouraged even the most unstudious students to participate more actively.

Synonyms

    6.6 0.09/m

  • uninquisitive
    8.1 0.00/m
    uninquisitive shows little desire to ask questions or explore ideas, while unstudious means someone who does not study regularly.
  • aliterate
    9.9 0.00/m
    aliterate is someone who can read but chooses not to read books, while unstudious avoids study and formal learning.
