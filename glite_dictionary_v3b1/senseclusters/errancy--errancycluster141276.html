errancy

noun
belief in the fallibility of doctrine or teaching
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrənsi / (AmE), / ˈɛrənsi / (BrE)
the holding of beliefs or views that are considered to deviate from accepted doctrine, especially in the context of Christian theology and papal infallibility.

Examples

Papal errancy
He was criticized by church leaders for promoting the idea of papal errancy.
Some theologians have written extensively about the errancy of certain church teachings.
The debate over biblical errancy continues to divide scholars and believers alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heresy - holding beliefs that disagree with established doctrine, especially in religion
