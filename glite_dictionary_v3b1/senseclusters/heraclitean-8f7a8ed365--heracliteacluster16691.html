Heraclitean

adjective
of constant change
9.9
Pronunciation: /hə.rə.ˈklɪ.tɪ.ən/ /; / /hə.rə.ˈklaɪ.tɪ.ən/ (AmE), /hə.rə.ˈklɪ.tɪ.ən/ /; / /hə.rə.ˈklaɪ.tɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the philosophy of Heraclitus, especially the belief that everything is constantly changing and that opposites are interconnected

Examples

Heraclitean change
The philosopher's Heraclitean view emphasized that nothing stays the same for long.
