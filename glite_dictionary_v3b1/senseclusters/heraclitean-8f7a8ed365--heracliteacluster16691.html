Heraclitean

adjective
of constant change
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛrəˈklaɪtiən / (AmE), / ˌhɛrəˈklaɪtiən / (BrE)
relating to the philosophy of Heraclitus, especially the belief that everything is constantly changing and that opposites are interconnected

Examples

Heraclitean change
The philosopher's Heraclitean view emphasized that nothing stays the same for long.
Many modern scientists adopt a Heraclitean perspective, seeing the universe as always evolving.
Her poetry reflects a Heraclitean sense of life as a series of constant transformations.
The philosopher's Heraclitean view emphasized that change is the only constant in life.
Her argument was deeply Heraclitean, focusing on the unity of opposites in nature.
Many modern thinkers draw on Heraclitean ideas to explain social and scientific change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialectical - relating to the interaction of opposing forces or ideas
  • synonym: flux - continuous change or movement
  • antonym: static - lacking movement, change, or development
  • antonym: unchanging - not changing; remaining the same
