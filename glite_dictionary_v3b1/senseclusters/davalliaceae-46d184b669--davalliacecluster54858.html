Davalliaceae

noun
a family of ferns
Pronunciation: / dəˌvæliˈeɪsiˌi /; / dəˌvæliˈeɪsiːˌiː / (AmE), / dəˌvælɪˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
a family of ferns, sometimes considered separate from the larger family Polypodiaceae, containing about 110 species

Examples

Davalliaceae fern family

Several ferns classified under Davalliaceae can be found in tropical and subtropical regions.
The Davalliaceae are often known for their creeping rhizomes and delicate fronds.

The ferns belonging to the Davalliaceae are often epiphytic and have creeping rhizomes.
You can identify members of the Davalliaceae by their distinctive hairy rootstocks.
