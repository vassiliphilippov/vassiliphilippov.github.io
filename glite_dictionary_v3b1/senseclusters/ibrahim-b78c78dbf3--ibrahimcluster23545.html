Ibrahim

proper noun
Islamic name for Abraham, patriarch and prophet
Pronunciation: / ˌiːbrəˈhiːm / (AmE), / ˌiːbrəˈhiːm / (BrE)
(in Islam) the first patriarch and prophet, known as the father of Isaac; revered as a prophet in Islam, corresponding to Abraham in Judaism and Christianity.

Examples

Ibrahim in Islam
During Eid al-Adha, the story of Ibrahim’s willingness to sacrifice his son is commemorated.
Many mosques and Islamic schools are named after the prophet Ibrahim.
Muslims believe that Ibrahim rebuilt the Kaaba in Mecca with his son Ismail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Abraham - biblical patriarch, father of Isaac; foundational figure in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam
