Mark the Evangelist

proper noun
author of the second Gospel, apostle
Pronunciation: / mɑrk ði ɪˈvæn.dʒə.lɪst / (AmE), / mɑːk ði ɪˈvan.dʒə.lɪst / (BrE)
Apostle and companion of Saint Peter, regarded as the author of the second Gospel in the New Testament.

Examples

Mark the Evangelist author
Many scholars attribute the second book of the New Testament to Mark the Evangelist.
The feast day of Mark the Evangelist is celebrated on April 25th in the Christian calendar.
Tradition holds that Mark the Evangelist founded the church in Alexandria, Egypt.
