whole kit

noun
everything; the whole lot
3.3
Pronunciation: / hoʊl kɪt / (AmE), / həʊl kɪt / (BrE)
(informal) Everything that is available or involved; the entirety of things being considered, often used in phrases like 'the whole kit (and caboodle)'.

Examples

The whole kit and caboodle
After the renovation, they installed the whole kit, including new furniture and lighting.
She bought the whole kit when she started painting—brushes, paints, easel, and all.
We'll take the whole kit with us on the camping trip, just to be prepared.

Related Terms

  • antonym: part - a portion or piece of a whole
  • synonym: whole caboodle - the entire lot or group
  • synonym: whole shebang - the whole thing; everything involved
