preconscious

adjective
not fully conscious
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈkɑːnʃəs / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈkɒnʃəs / (BrE)
existing in the mind but not currently in conscious awareness, yet able to become conscious if needed

Examples

Preconscious thoughts
Some memories remain preconscious until a specific event brings them to mind.
Therapists often help patients access preconscious thoughts during counseling sessions.
A preconscious idea may influence your decisions without you realizing it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conscious - aware of and responding to one's surroundings
  • antonym: unconscious - not within conscious awareness and not easily accessible
