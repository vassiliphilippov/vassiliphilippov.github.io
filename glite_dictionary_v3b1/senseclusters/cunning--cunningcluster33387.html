cunning

noun
skill in deception or trickery
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
the ability to achieve things by being clever, especially by tricking or deceiving people

Examples

Show great cunning
He showed great cunning in negotiating the complex contract.
Her cunning allowed her to outwit her much stronger opponents.
Through sheer cunning, the fox managed to escape the trap.
His cunning enabled him to outsmart his rivals.
She relied on cunning rather than brute force to win the competition.
The fox is often a symbol of cunning in fables and folklore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: craftiness - skill in achieving goals by deceit or trickery
  • synonym: guile - deceitful cleverness
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and fair
