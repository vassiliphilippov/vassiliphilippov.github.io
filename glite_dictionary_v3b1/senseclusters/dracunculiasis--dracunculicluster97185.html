dracunculiasis

noun
disease caused by Guinea worm infestation
9.0 9.9
Pronunciation: /dræk.ən.kjuː.ˈleɪ.sɪs/ /; / /dræk.ən.ˈkjuː.lə.sɪs/ (AmE), /dræk.ən.kjuː.ˈleɪ.sɪs/ /; / /dræk.ən.ˈkjuː.lə.sɪs/ (BrE)
A painful and debilitating disease caused by infestation with Guinea worm larvae, contracted through drinking contaminated water, in which the adult worm emerges through the skin.

Examples

Dracunculiasis disease
Public health campaigns have helped to drastically reduce cases of dracunculiasis worldwide.
Safe drinking water is essential to prevent dracunculiasis in rural communities.
The village doctor diagnosed the child with dracunculiasis after noticing the emerging worm in her leg.
