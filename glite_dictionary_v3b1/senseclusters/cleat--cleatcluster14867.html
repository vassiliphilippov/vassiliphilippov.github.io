cleat

verb
fasten a rope to a cleat
Pronunciation: / kliːt / (AmE), / kliːt / (BrE)
to fasten or secure (a rope or line) by tying it to a cleat

Examples

To cleat a rope
After hoisting the sail, she cleated the halyard to keep it in place.
The sailor quickly cleated the line to prevent it from slacking.
You should always cleat the rope securely when docking the boat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unfasten - to undo or detach something that was fastened
