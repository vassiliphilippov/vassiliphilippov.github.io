crocodile

noun
large tropical aquatic reptile
5.2 1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑː.kəˌdaɪl / (AmE), / ˈkrɒk.ə.daɪl / (BrE)
A large, carnivorous, semi-aquatic reptile with a long snout, powerful jaws, sharp teeth, and tough, bony skin, typically found in tropical regions.

Examples

A large crocodile
A crocodile can live for decades in the right environment.
The crocodile slid silently into the muddy river.
We saw a huge crocodile basking on the riverbank during our safari.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alligator - a large reptile similar to a crocodile but with a broader snout, found mostly in the Americas and China
