once and for all

adverb
conclusively; finally and completely
1.0
Pronunciation: / wʌns ənd fər ɔl / (AmE), / wʌns ənd fə ɔːl / (BrE)
in a way that finally and completely settles a matter, leaving no doubt or possibility of further discussion or action

Examples

Settle once and for all
Let's clean the garage once and for all this weekend.
She told him once and for all that she would not change her mind.
We need to solve this issue once and for all so it never comes up again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: for good - permanently; forever
  • antonym: tentatively - in a way that is not certain or conclusive
