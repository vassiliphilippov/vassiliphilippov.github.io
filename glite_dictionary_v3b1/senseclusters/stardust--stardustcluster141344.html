stardust

noun
star particles; magic
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑːrˌdʌst / (AmE), / ˈstɑːdʌst / (BrE)
tiny particles of matter from stars, or a poetic sense of magic and wonder

Examples

Ancient stardust
The scientist explained that all living things contain traces of stardust from ancient stars.
There was a sense of stardust in the air as the performers took the stage.
Children often look at the night sky and dream of stardust and distant galaxies.
