mortar fire

noun
artillery fire from a mortar
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtər faɪər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtə faɪə / (BrE)
Artillery fire delivered by one or more mortars, often involving explosive shells lobbed in an arc toward a target.

Examples

Mortar fire attack
Civilians fled the town as mortar fire intensified in the distance.
Enemy forces launched heavy mortar fire on the base at dawn.
The soldiers took cover quickly when they heard the mortar fire begin.
