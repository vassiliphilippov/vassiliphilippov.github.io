slip out

verb
leave quietly or secretly
Pronunciation: / slɪp aʊt / (AmE), / slɪp aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place quietly or secretly, without being noticed

Examples

To slip out quietly
During the crowded party, Emily managed to slip out without anyone noticing her departure.
He tried to slip out of the office early so his boss wouldn't see him leave.
Several guests slipped out before the speeches even began.
He slipped out after dinner to make a private phone call.
She slipped out of the crowded room without anyone noticing.
The secret slipped out during the conversation before I could stop myself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sneak out - leave a place secretly or quietly to avoid detection
