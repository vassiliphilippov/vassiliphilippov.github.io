augite

noun
dark-green to black pyroxene mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːdʒaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːdʒaɪt / (BrE)
a dark-green to black, glassy mineral of the pyroxene group containing aluminum, iron, and magnesium, commonly found in igneous rocks

Examples

Dark augite mineral
Geologists often study augite to better understand the formation of volcanic rocks.
The basalt sample contained significant amounts of augite and plagioclase feldspar.
Under the microscope, augite crystals can be identified by their distinctive greenish hue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pyroxene - a group of important rock-forming silicate minerals
