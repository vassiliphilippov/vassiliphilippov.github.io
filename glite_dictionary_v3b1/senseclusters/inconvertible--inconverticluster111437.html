inconvertible

adjective
not able to be exchanged for another currency
4.5 7.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kən.ˈvɜːr.tə.bəl/ /; / /ɪn.kən.ˈvɜːr.tɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.kən.ˈvɜː.tɪ.bəl/ /; / /ɪn.kən.ˈvɜː.tə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a currency or asset) not able to be exchanged for another currency or asset of equal value.

Examples

Inconvertible currency
After the new regulations, their notes became inconvertible and could not be exchanged for foreign money.
In times of crisis, some governments issue inconvertible paper money to control outflow of capital.
The country's inconvertible currency made international trade complicated for businesses.
