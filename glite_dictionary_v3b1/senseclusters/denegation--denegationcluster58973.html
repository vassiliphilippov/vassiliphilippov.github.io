denegation

noun
denial or negation
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛnɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːnɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of denying, rejecting, or negating something (such as a statement, request, or idea)

Examples

The denegation of a claim
In formal logic, the denegation of a proposition is often represented by a special symbol.

The concept of denegation is fundamental in understanding how statements can be contradicted.
The denegation of his application surprised everyone on the committee.
Her constant denegation of responsibility frustrated her colleagues.
Legal documents often require explicit consent or denegation from all parties involved.
