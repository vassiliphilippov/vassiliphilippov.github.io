orientalizing

noun
Greek art style phase
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːriˈɛntəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɔːriˈɛntəlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
an art-historical phase, especially in early Greek art, characterized by the adoption of motifs and techniques from the Near East and Egypt

Examples

The orientalizing period
The orientalizing period introduced new animal motifs into Greek pottery designs.
Historians study the orientalizing of Greek sculpture to understand cultural exchange in the ancient world.
Many artifacts from the orientalizing phase show clear influences from Egyptian and Near Eastern art.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaic period - the early period of ancient Greek art and culture
  • synonym: orientalism - the imitation or depiction of aspects of Eastern cultures in the West
