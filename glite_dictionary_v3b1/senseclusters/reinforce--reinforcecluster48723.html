reinforce

verb
strengthen or support, especially with rewards
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈfɔrs / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfɔːs / (BrE)
to strengthen or support an attitude, behavior, or response, especially by providing rewards or encouragement

Examples

To reinforce good habits
It's important to reinforce healthy habits with encouragement and support.
Small rewards can reinforce students' desire to study regularly.
The teacher used praise to reinforce positive behavior in the classroom.
