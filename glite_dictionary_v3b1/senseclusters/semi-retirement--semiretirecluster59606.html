semi-retirement

noun
partial retirement
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmi raɪˈtaɪərmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmi rɪˈtaɪəmənt / (BrE)
a state in which someone has reduced their working hours or responsibilities but has not fully retired from work

Examples

Enter semi-retirement
After decades in the classroom, she entered semi-retirement and now teaches only one course each semester.
Many professionals choose semi-retirement to stay active while enjoying more free time.
During his semi-retirement, he started volunteering at the local library a few days a week.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: employment - the state of having a paid job
  • degree higher: retirement - the period of life after leaving one's job permanently
