revolve around

verb
have as main focus or center
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɑlv əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rɪˈvɒlv əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to have something or someone as the main focus or most important part of an activity, situation, or discussion

Examples

Talks revolve around work
Discussions at work often revolve around upcoming projects and deadlines.
For many people, daily routines revolve around meal times.
Much of her life seemed to revolve around her children.
Discussions at the conference revolved around climate change and sustainability.
His entire schedule seems to revolve around his children's activities.
Many television dramas revolve around complicated family relationships.
