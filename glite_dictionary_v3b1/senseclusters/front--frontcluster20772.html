front

noun
person used as a cover for illicit activity
8.8 2.84/m
Pronunciation: / frÊŒnt / (AmE), / frÊŒnt / (BrE)
a person who serves as a cover for illegal or questionable activities, concealing the real operators or purpose

Examples

A front for crime
Everyone suspected he was merely a front for the criminal organization.
The company was just a front for laundering money.
The real boss remained hidden, using a front to conduct business.
