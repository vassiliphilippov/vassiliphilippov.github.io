kulak

noun
wealthy Russian peasant
Pronunciation: / kuˈlɑk / (AmE), / kuˈlæk / (BrE)
a relatively wealthy peasant in Russia, especially one targeted during Soviet collectivization policies

Examples

Russian kulak
During the Soviet era, many kulaks were accused of hoarding grain and resisting collectivization.
The government labeled him a kulak because he owned several cows and a large plot of land.
Stories about the fate of kulaks are still discussed in Russian history classes today.
During the Soviet era, many kulaks were persecuted for owning land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peasant - a poor farmer of low social status
  • antonym: proletarian - a member of the working class, especially in Marxist theory
