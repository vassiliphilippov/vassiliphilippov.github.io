sweet-talk

verb
persuade using flattery or charm
1.6 4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈswit tɔk / (AmE), / ˈswiːt tɔːk / (BrE)
To influence or persuade someone by using flattery or smooth, appealing talk.

Examples

To sweet-talk a friend
Don't let him sweet-talk you into spending more money than you can afford.
He sweet-talked his way out of trouble with his charming smile and compliments.
She tried to sweet-talk the teacher into giving her extra time on the assignment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coax - to gently persuade someone to do something
  • synonym: flatter - to praise excessively, often to gain favour
