woolly rhinoceros

noun
extinct, thick-haired prehistoric rhinoceros
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli raɪˈnɑsərəs / (AmE), / ˈwʊli raɪˈnɒsərəs / (BrE)
an extinct species of rhinoceros with a thick, hairy coat that lived during the Ice Age in Arctic and subarctic regions

Examples

The woolly rhinoceros fossil
Fossils of the woolly rhinoceros have been discovered in Siberia and northern Europe.
Paleontologists study the remains of the woolly rhinoceros to learn about prehistoric climates.
The woolly rhinoceros roamed the frozen plains of Eurasia during the last Ice Age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mammoth - large, hairy extinct elephant of the Ice Age
  • degree lower: rhinoceros - large, thick-skinned herbivorous animal with one or two horns on its snout
