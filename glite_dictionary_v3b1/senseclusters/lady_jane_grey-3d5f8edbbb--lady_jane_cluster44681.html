Lady Jane Grey

proper noun
Queen of England for nine days in 1553
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪdi dʒeɪn ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˌleɪdi dʒeɪn ɡreɪ / (BrE)
(1536–1554) The Queen of England for nine days in 1553 before being deposed by Mary Tudor; she was executed for treason.

Examples

Lady Jane Grey queen
Lady Jane Grey was executed at the Tower of London after just nine days as queen.
Historians often debate whether Lady Jane Grey was a victim of circumstance or a political pawn.
There are several paintings depicting Lady Jane Grey in the moments leading up to her execution.
