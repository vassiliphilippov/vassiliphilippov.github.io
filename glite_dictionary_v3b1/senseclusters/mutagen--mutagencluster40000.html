mutagen

noun
agent that causes genetic mutation
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtəˌdʒɛn / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtədʒən / (BrE)
an agent, such as a chemical substance or radiation, that causes genetic mutation or increases the rate of mutation

Examples

A mutagen agent
Exposure to radiation can introduce a mutagen into living cells, increasing the risk of genetic disorders.
Many industrial pollutants are classified as mutagens because they can alter DNA.
Scientists tested whether the chemical acted as a mutagen on bacteria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carcinogen - a substance capable of causing cancer
  • synonym: mutagenic - having the ability to cause genetic mutation
