arborization

noun
branching formation
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrbərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑːbərəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming branches or a branched structure, especially in biological systems such as nerves or trees

Examples

Arborization of nerves
The arborization of neurons in the brain is essential for complex thought processes.
Botanists study the arborization patterns of different tree species to understand their growth.
After injury, the arborization of new axons helps restore some neural circuits in the damaged area.
