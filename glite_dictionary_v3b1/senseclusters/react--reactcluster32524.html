react

verb
respond to an event or stimulus
Pronunciation: / riˈækt / (AmE), / riˈækt / (BrE)
to behave or respond in a particular way because of something that has happened or been said

Examples

People react quickly
Children often react differently to new environments.
How you react under pressure can reveal a lot about your character.
She didn't react when she heard the news, remaining completely silent.
He tried not to react to the rude comments during the meeting.
Many people react strongly to changes in their work environment.
Whenever criticized, she tends to react defensively rather than listen calmly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • synonym: oppose - to act against or resist something
  • synonym: respond - to say or do something in reply or as a reaction
