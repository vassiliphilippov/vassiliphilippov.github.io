hold up

verb
to withstand pressure or stress
1.2
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ʌp / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp / (BrE)
to remain strong, effective, or unchanged under stress, pressure, criticism, or use; to withstand or endure successfully

Examples

Hold up under stress
Her arguments didn't hold up well under close scrutiny from the committee.
How long can the foundation of the house hold up if the flooding continues?
The old dam has managed to hold up against many severe storms over the years.
Do you think his excuse will hold up if anyone questions him closely?
The scientist wanted to see if her hypothesis would hold up after further testing and debate.
These old bridges hold up remarkably well, despite decades of harsh weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collapse - to fall down or fail under stress or pressure
  • synonym: endure - to continue despite suffering or adversity
  • synonym: withstand - to resist successfully; to endure
