on the face of it

adverb
judging by appearances only
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə feɪs əv ɪt / (AmE), / ɒn ðə feɪs əv ɪt / (BrE)
judging by appearances only; based on what is immediately obvious, without further investigation

Examples

On the face of it seems true
On the face of it, he seemed qualified for the job, but his references raised concerns.
On the face of it, the proposal looks like a good idea.
The plan appears simple on the face of it, but it's actually quite complex.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seemingly - so as to appear to be true
