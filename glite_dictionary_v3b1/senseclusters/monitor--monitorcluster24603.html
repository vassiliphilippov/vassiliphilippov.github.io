monitor

noun
person who observes or warns
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnɪtər /; / ˈmɑːnɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
a person who observes, supervises, or enforces rules during an activity or exam

Examples

Classroom monitor
A safety monitor checked each machine to prevent any potential accidents.
As a test monitor, she ensured no one cheated during the exam.
The hall monitor reminded the students not to run in the corridors.
As a monitor, she was responsible for collecting all test papers at the end.
Each classroom had a monitor assigned to oversee the final exams.
The monitor walked around the room, making sure no one was cheating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: observer - a person who watches or checks for a specific purpose
  • synonym: proctor - person who supervises students during an examination
  • synonym: supervisor - a person who oversees others' work or conduct
