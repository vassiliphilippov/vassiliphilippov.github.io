backlog

noun
large log at back of a hearth fire
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌlɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈbak.lɒɡ / (BrE)
the large log placed at the back of a hearth fire, traditionally used to keep the fire burning for a long period

Examples

A backlog in the fireplace
She placed a fresh backlog behind the kindling to slow the fire’s burn.
The old cabin had a backlog in the fireplace to ensure the room stayed warm through the night.
Throughout winter, they relied on a sturdy backlog to keep the hearth glowing.
