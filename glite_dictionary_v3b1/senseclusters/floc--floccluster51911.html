floc

verb
to form clumps
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / flÉ‘k / (AmE), / flÉ’k / (BrE)
to form or cause to form into small clumps or masses, especially in a liquid.

Examples

Particles floc together
During the treatment process, the particles floc and settle at the bottom of the tank.
Engineers added chemicals to help impurities floc in the wastewater.
If the solution is stirred, the suspended solids may floc and become easier to remove.

Synonyms

  • flocculate
    9.9 0.03/m
    flocculate is a technical verb used by scientists to make particles clump in liquids, while floc is a shorter, more general verb.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggregate - to gather or form into a group or mass
  • antonym: disperse - to break up and spread in different directions
