wrangle

verb
argue or quarrel noisily
Pronunciation: / ˈræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈræŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to quarrel or argue noisily, angrily, or disruptively, often for a prolonged period

Examples

To wrangle over money
Despite their efforts to stay calm, they began wrangling loudly in the middle of the meeting.
The two neighbors often wrangle over the boundary between their properties.
They wrangled for hours about which movie to watch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion; not to argue
  • synonym: argue - to express opposite opinions in a conversation
