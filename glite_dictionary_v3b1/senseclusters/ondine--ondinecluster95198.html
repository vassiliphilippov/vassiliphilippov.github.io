ondine

noun
water spirit in folklore
Pronunciation: / ɑnˈdiːn / (AmE), / ɒnˈdiːn / (BrE)
a mythological water spirit or nymph in European folklore, often associated with lakes, rivers, or streams

Examples

A magical ondine
The legend tells of an ondine who fell in love with a mortal man.
Artists have often depicted the mysterious beauty of an ondine rising from the water.
In the old tale, the ondine would sing enchanting songs by the river at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mermaid - a mythical sea creature with the upper body of a woman and the tail of a fish
  • synonym: nymph - a minor female nature deity in mythology
