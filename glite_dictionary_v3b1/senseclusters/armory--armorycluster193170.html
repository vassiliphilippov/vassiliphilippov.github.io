armory

noun
a country's weapons and equipment
5.3 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêrm…ôri / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀêm…ôri / (BrE)
all the weapons and military equipment that a country possesses

Examples

A country's armory
A well-equipped armory is crucial for ensuring national security.
The nation's armory has been expanded with advanced missile systems.
The treaty requires both countries to reduce their nuclear armory.
Diplomatic relations helped reduce the size of their nuclear armoury.
Modern armies rely on an extensive armoury of advanced technology.
The country expanded its armoury to prepare for potential threats.

Synonyms

  • arsenal
    5.2 4.47/m
    an arsenal is the full collection of a country's weapons for war, while an armory is the building or stock where they are kept.
  • arms
    2.8 0.06/m
    arms are all kinds of weapons and military kit in use, while an armory often means the place or store of those weapons.
  • armament
    7.0 1.66/m
    armament refers to the weapons and equipment a country uses for fighting, while an armory names the stock or place holding them.
  • weaponry
    4.2 4.23/m
    weaponry means the types and technology of weapons a group uses, while an armory means their actual stock or storage place.
  • ordnance
    8.7 1.10/m
    ordnance is heavy weapons, ammunition, and artillery used in war, while an armory is the full collection or storage of all weapons.
