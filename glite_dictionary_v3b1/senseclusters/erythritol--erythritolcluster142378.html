erythritol

noun
low-calorie sweetener
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɪθrɪˌtɔl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɪθrɪtɒl /; / ɪˈrɪθrɪˌtɒl / (BrE)
a sugar alcohol used as a low-calorie sweetener in foods and drinks

Examples

erythritol sweetener
Many diet sodas are sweetened with erythritol instead of sugar.
Bakers often use erythritol to reduce the calorie content of desserts.
Some people prefer erythritol because it does not raise blood sugar levels.
Many sugar-free products use erythritol as a sweetener because it has almost no calories.
Some people prefer erythritol over other sugar substitutes due to its mild taste.
Naturally occurring erythritol can be found in small amounts in grapes and melons.
Many sugar-free candies use erythritol as a sweetener to reduce calories.
Some people prefer erythritol because it does not raise blood sugar levels.
The cosmetic cream contains erythritol to keep the skin moist without adding sugar.
