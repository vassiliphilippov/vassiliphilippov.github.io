thick

adjective
indistinct or slurred (of speech)
5.8 5.6
Pronunciation: /θɪk/ (AmE), /θɪk/ (BrE)
(of speech or pronunciation) sounding indistinct or hard to understand, as if affected by a thick tongue, especially due to intoxication, illness, or emotion.

Examples

Thick accent
After his surgery, his thick speech made it difficult for others to understand him.
Nerves caused her voice to sound thick and shaky during the presentation.
The man's thick accent and slurred words suggested he had too much to drink.
