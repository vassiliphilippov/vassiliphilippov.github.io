hit

noun
act of striking/contact
Pronunciation: / hɪt / (AmE), / hɪt / (BrE)
an act of striking or making forceful contact with someone or something; a collision or impact

Examples

One hit was enough to knock the bottle over.
The baseball player scored a hit in the final inning.
A single hit between atoms can release a significant amount of energy.
Scientists studied the effects of each hit during the high-speed collision experiments.

He celebrated after his crucial hit drove in the winning run.
She recorded three hits in the doubleheader against Boston.
The rookie got his first major league hit last night.
