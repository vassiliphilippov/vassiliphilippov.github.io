recuperate

verb
regain financial status after loss
4.7
Pronunciation: / rəˈkuːpəˌreɪt /; / rɪˈkuːpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈkjuːpəreɪt / (BrE)
to regain something lost, especially money or financial condition, after a loss or setback

Examples

To recuperate losses
After the market downturn, it took several months to recuperate financially.
Investors were relieved when the business started to recuperate and show profits again.
The company hopes to recuperate its losses by the end of the fiscal year.
After a bad investment, it took years to recuperate his financial losses.
She hopes to recuperate her confidence after a series of setbacks.
The company is working hard to recuperate from last quarter's downturn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recover - to return to a normal state after a financial loss
