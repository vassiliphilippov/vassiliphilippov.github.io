flare

verb
erupt or intensify suddenly
Pronunciation: / flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
(of emotion or activity) to erupt or intensify suddenly, often referring to anger or violence.

Examples

Tempers flare up
Old tensions flared up again after the announcement.
Tempers began to flare as the debate grew more heated.
Violence can flare unexpectedly at large protests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erupt - to break out suddenly and violently
  • antonym: subside - to become less intense or violent
