overspeak

verb
to talk excessively
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈspiːk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspiːk / (BrE)
to speak too much or say more than is appropriate in a situation

Examples

To overspeak in meetings
During the interview, he accidentally overspoke and revealed confidential information.
She realized she had overspoken when everyone else fell silent.
Try not to overspeak in group discussions so others have a chance to share their ideas.
She realized she had overspoken and quickly tried to change the subject.
During the interview, he tended to overspeak when he was nervous.
If you overspeak in meetings, others may not get a chance to share their ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • antonym: be concise - to express much in few words
  • synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking by speaking yourself
  • antonym: listen - to pay attention to what someone is saying
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
