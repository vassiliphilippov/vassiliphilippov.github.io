factor

verb
include as a relevant element when deciding
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tɚ /; / ˈfæktɚ / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə /; / ˈfæktə / (BrE)
to include something as a relevant element, or to have an influence on a result or decision

Examples

To factor in costs
Did you factor the shipping costs before placing the order?
We need to factor possible delays into our schedule.
When planning for retirement, people often forget to factor inflation.
Competition can factor into a company's strategy for growth.
Rising costs factor into our decision to delay the project.
Unexpected delays often factor into construction timelines.

  • synonym: consider - think about carefully, especially when making decisions
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • synonym: influence - to have an effect on the character or outcome of something
