reprogram

verb
to change behavior
5.6
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈproʊ.græm/ /; / /riː.prə.ˈgræm/ (AmE), /riː.ˈprəʊ.græm/ /; / /riː.prə.ˈgræm/ (BrE)
to cause someone to change their attitudes, beliefs, or habitual behavior, often through training or conditioning

Examples

To reprogram your mind
Therapists tried to reprogram his fear responses through repeated exposure and coping techniques.
The school developed a plan to reprogram students' study habits for better academic performance.
After years of unhealthy routines, she worked hard to reprogram her daily habits.
