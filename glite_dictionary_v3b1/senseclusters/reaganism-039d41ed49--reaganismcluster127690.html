Reaganism

noun
Reagan's policies
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪɡənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈreɪɡənɪzəm / (BrE)
the political and economic ideas, policies, and style associated with Ronald Reagan, especially during his presidency in the 1980s (emphasizing free markets, limited government, optimism, and traditional values)

Examples

Reaganism economic policies
The economic reforms of the 1980s were strongly influenced by Reaganism and its emphasis on free markets.
Many politicians today still debate the long-term effects of Reaganism on American society.
Some critics argue that Reaganism led to increased inequality and reduced social programs.
Many politicians reference Reaganism when discussing tax cuts and deregulation.
The economic boom of the 1980s is often attributed to the influence of Reaganism.
Debates about government spending frequently mention the legacy of Reaganism in American politics.
Many conservatives still admire the principles of Reaganism in modern political debates.
The candidate promised to bring back the optimism and values of Reaganism to the country.
Some critics argue that Reaganism shaped economic policy for decades after the 1980s.

  • synonym: conservatism - a political philosophy favoring traditional values and limited government
  • antonym: liberalism - a political philosophy emphasizing individual freedoms and limited government
