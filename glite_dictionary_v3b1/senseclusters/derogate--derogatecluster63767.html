derogate

verb
lessen importance or value
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛrəɡeɪt /; / ˈdɛrəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to speak or act in a way that makes someone or something seem less important or worthy of respect

Examples

To derogate someone's work
He tried to derogate his colleague's achievements during the meeting.
The article seemed designed to derogate the importance of scientific research.
The report did not derogate from the seriousness of the incident.
It is unfair to derogate others based on their background.
The article did not intend to derogate the achievements of the team.
She refused to derogate her colleagues, even when criticized herself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: belittle - regard or portray as less impressive or important
  • synonym: diminish - make or become less
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
