circumspection

noun
careful consideration; caution
8.4 0.47/m
Pronunciation: / Àås…úrk…ômÀàsp…õk É…ôn /; / Àås…ùÀê.k…ômÀàsp…õk. É…ôn / (AmE), / Àås…úÀêk…ômÀàsp…õk É…ôn /; / Àås…úÀê.k…ômÀàspek. É…ôn / (BrE)
the quality of being careful to consider all circumstances and possible consequences before acting or speaking; prudence or caution

Examples

Show circumspection in decisions
Due to his circumspection, he gained a reputation for sound judgment among his peers.
He acted with great circumspection when handling the sensitive negotiations.
Her circumspection prevented her from making any impulsive decisions.
Approaching the delicate topic required tact and circumspection from the entire team.
His circumspection in business dealings earned him a reputation for reliability and wisdom.
She handled the sensitive situation with great circumspection, ensuring no one was offended.

Synonyms

  • carefulness
