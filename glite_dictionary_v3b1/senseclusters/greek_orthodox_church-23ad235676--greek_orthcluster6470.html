Greek Orthodox Church

proper noun
main Christian church in Greece
Pronunciation: / ɡrik ˈɔrθəˌdɑks tʃɝtʃ / (AmE), / ɡriːk ˈɔːθədɒks tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the established Christian church of Greece, forming an autonomous part of the Eastern Orthodox Church.

Examples

The Greek Orthodox Church
Many historic buildings in Athens belong to the Greek Orthodox Church.
The Greek Orthodox Church celebrates Easter according to the Julian calendar.
The Greek Orthodox Church plays a significant role in Greek cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - the branch of Christianity including the Greek, Russian, and other Orthodox churches
