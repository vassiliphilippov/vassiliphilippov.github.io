Muskogean language

noun
a family of Native American languages
Pronunciation: / mʌˈskoʊɡiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / mʌˈskəʊɡiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a family of Native American languages spoken in the southeastern United States, including Creek, Choctaw, and Chickasaw.

Examples

Muskogean language family
Efforts to preserve each Muskogean language are vital for cultural heritage and history.
Linguists classify Creek as a Muskogean language spoken across parts of Alabama and Georgia.
The Muskogean language family includes Choctaw, Chickasaw, and several others.
