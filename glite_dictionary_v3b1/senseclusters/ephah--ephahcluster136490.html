ephah

noun
ancient Hebrew unit of dry measure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈiːfə, ˈeɪfə / (AmE), / ˈiːfə, ˈɛfə /; / ˈiːfə, ˈeɪfə / (BrE)
an ancient Hebrew unit of dry measure, about the size of a bushel (around 22 liters), used in biblical times

Examples

An ephah of wheat
A merchant in Jerusalem sold wheat by the ephah, a common unit of measure in biblical times.
According to the ancient records, an ephah of barley was offered every day as part of the ritual.
The recipe called for nearly an ephah of flour, reflecting the large scale of the feast.
Archaeologists discovered vessels marked with the word ephah near the ruins.
In the ancient marketplace, grains were often measured by the ephah.
The prophet instructed the people to bring an ephah of flour for the offering.
