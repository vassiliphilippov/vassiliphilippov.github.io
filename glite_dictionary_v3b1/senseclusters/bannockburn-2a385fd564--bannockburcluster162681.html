Bannockburn

proper noun
historic 1314 Scottish victory over England
Pronunciation: / ˈbænəkˌbɜrn / (AmE), / ˈbanəkˌbɜːn / (BrE)
the decisive 1314 battle near Bannockburn, Scotland, where the Scots, led by Robert the Bruce, defeated the English and secured Scottish independence

Examples

Bannockburn battle 1314
Many Scots consider Bannockburn a defining moment in their nation's history.
Robert the Bruce became a national hero after his triumph at Bannockburn.
The victory at Bannockburn is celebrated annually in Scotland.
