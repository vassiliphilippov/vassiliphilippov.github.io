conformability

noun
compatibility of dimensions
8.6
Pronunciation: /kən.fɔːr.mə.ˈbɪl.ə.ti/ /; / /kən.fɑːr.mə.ˈbɪl.ə.ti/ (AmE), /kən.fɔː.mə.ˈbɪl.ə.ti/ (BrE)
the property of mathematical objects having compatible dimensions or shapes that allow a specific operation to be performed

Examples

Conformability of matrices
The conformability of the two arrays was checked before performing the addition operation.
Matrix multiplication requires the conformability of the involved matrices' dimensions.
A lack of conformability can cause errors in mathematical computations involving multiple objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compatibility - the ability to exist or work together without conflict
  • antonym: incompatibility - the state of being unable to exist or work together
