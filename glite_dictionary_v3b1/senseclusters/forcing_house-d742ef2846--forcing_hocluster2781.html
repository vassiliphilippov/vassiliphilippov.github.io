forcing house

noun
place fostering rapid development
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrsɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːsɪŋ ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A place or environment that promotes rapid development or growth, especially in artistic, intellectual, or creative fields.

Examples

A forcing house of art
During the Renaissance, Florence was a forcing house of new artistic techniques.
The art school acted as a forcing house for the country's best young talent.
The university became a forcing house for innovation in science and technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hotbed - a place where something, especially something bad, grows or develops quickly
