Ammophila

proper noun
genus of dune grass
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæməˈfɪlə / (AmE), / ˌæməˈfɪlə / (BrE)
a genus of grasses, commonly called marram grass, that stabilize sand dunes and prevent coastal erosion

Examples

Ammophila grass dunes
Ammophila is often planted along coastlines to protect beaches from erosion.
The long roots of Ammophila help hold sand dunes together during strong winds.
Many conservation projects use Ammophila to restore damaged coastal habitats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marram grass - a grass that grows on sand dunes and helps stabilize them
