pleochroism

noun
property of crystals showing different colors from different angles
8.0 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpliː.ə.kroʊ.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpliː.oʊ.kroʊ.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈpliː.ə.krəʊ.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpliːəʊ.krəʊ.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the property of certain crystals showing different colors when viewed from different directions, due to differences in light absorption

Examples

pleochroism in crystals
Gemologists use pleochroism to help identify certain gemstones by their changing colors.
The mineral cordierite displays striking pleochroism when rotated under polarized light.
Tourmaline is well known for exhibiting intense pleochroism, often appearing green from one angle and pink from another.
