deport

verb
to expel someone from a country

Pronunciation: / d…™Ààp…îrt / (AmE), / d…™Ààp…îÀêt / (BrE)
to officially force someone to leave a country, usually for legal or political reasons

Examples

To deport an immigrant
She was deported to her home country due to immigration violations.
The authorities threatened to deport anyone found in the country without proper documentation.
The government decided to deport the illegal immigrant after his visa expired.
Immigration officials are currently deporting those found without valid documents.
Several people were deported for entering the country illegally.
The government decided to deport the foreign journalist after his visa expired.

  • expatriate
