tacit consent

noun
implicit, unspoken consent
9.5 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtæs.ɪt ˈkɑːn.sent/ (AmE), /ˈtæs.ɪt ˈkɒn.sent/ (BrE)
Consent that is given implicitly, without being stated openly, especially by not objecting to something.

Examples

Tacit consent given
By not raising any objections, the board effectively gave tacit consent to the proposal.
His inaction was interpreted as tacit consent to the questionable practices.
The authorities viewed the public's silence as tacit consent to the new regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit consent - consent that is clearly and directly stated
  • synonym: implicit consent - consent that is implied rather than directly expressed
