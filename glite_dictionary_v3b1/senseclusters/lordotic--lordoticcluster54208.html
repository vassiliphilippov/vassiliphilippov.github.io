lordotic

adjective
relating to abnormal spine curvature
6.6 8.4
Pronunciation: /lɑːr.ˈdɑː.tɪk/ /; / /lɔːr.ˈdɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /lɔː.ˈdɒ.tɪk/ (BrE)
having an abnormally excessive inward curvature of the spine, especially in the lower back; used particularly in medical and veterinary contexts.

Examples

A lordotic spine curve
A severe lordotic curve can affect both mobility and comfort in animals.
Patients with a lordotic posture may experience lower back pain.
The veterinarian diagnosed the horse as lordotic due to the noticeable dip in its back.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kyphotic - having excessive outward curvature of the spine
  • synonym: lordosis - excessive inward curvature of the spine (noun)
