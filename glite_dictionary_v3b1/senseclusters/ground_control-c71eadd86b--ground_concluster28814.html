ground control

noun
team/system guiding aircraft during landing
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /graʊnd kən.ˈtroʊl/ (AmE), /graʊnd kən.ˈtrəʊl/ (BrE)
the team or system responsible for communicating with aircraft and guiding them during ground-controlled approaches, especially during landing.

Examples

Ground control team
Communication between the cockpit and ground control must remain clear at all times.
During poor visibility, ground control plays a crucial role in ensuring safe landings.
The pilot received instructions from ground control before making his approach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: air traffic control - the system and personnel managing aircraft movements in controlled airspace and at airports
