a highly flammable compound made by treating cellulose with nitric acid, used in making lacquers, films, and explosives

Examples

Cellulose nitrate film
Due to its explosive properties, cellulose nitrate must be stored and handled with great care.
Early photographic films were made with cellulose nitrate, which proved to be highly flammable.
The furniture's glossy finish is due to a cellulose nitrate lacquer applied to the wood.

Synonyms

  • nitrocellulose
    9.9 0.06/m
    nitrocellulose is the common technical name used in industry for lacquers and film, while cellulose nitrate is the older, wider name for treated cellulose.
  • nitrocotton
    9.9 0.00/m
    nitrocotton is an older name for nitric-ester cellulose used in lacquers and celluloid, while cellulose nitrate is the broader modern term.
  • guncotton
    9.9 0.01/m
    guncotton is made from cotton and used as a powerful explosive, while cellulose nitrate refers to treated cellulose used for films and lacquers.
