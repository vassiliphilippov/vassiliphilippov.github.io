phantasy

noun
false belief; illusion
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
a widely held but false belief; illusion or mistaken idea

Examples

A phantasy of success
He lived under the phantasy that he was destined for greatness.
Some politicians exploit the phantasy that social problems can be solved overnight.
The idea that the lost city is filled with gold is mere phantasy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delusion - a belief that is not true; a false idea
  • synonym: illusion - something that deceives by producing a false or misleading impression of reality
