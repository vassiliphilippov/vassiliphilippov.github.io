Louis Joliet

proper noun
French-Canadian explorer of the Mississippi River
Pronunciation: / luˈi ʒoʊˈljeɪ / (AmE), / luːˈiː ʒɒˈljeɪ / (BrE)
A French-Canadian explorer known for his exploration of the upper Mississippi River valley with Jacques Marquette in the 17th century.

Examples

Louis Joliet explorer
Louis Joliet is remembered for his pioneering journeys through North America's interior in the 1600s.
Many historians credit Louis Joliet with advancing European knowledge of the Great Lakes region.
The expedition led by Jacques Marquette and Louis Joliet was crucial for mapping the Mississippi River.
