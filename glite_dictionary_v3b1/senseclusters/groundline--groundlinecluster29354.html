groundline

noun
line for ground level
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line in a diagram or drawing that represents the ground, or an electrical wire used for grounding

Examples

The groundline in drawing
The engineer marked the groundline on the blueprint to show where the building meets the earth.
In the electrical diagram, the groundline is essential for safety and proper function.
Artists often use a groundline to establish perspective in architectural sketches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseline - a line serving as a basis, especially in drawings or measurements
  • synonym: ground wire - an electrical wire that provides a path to the ground for safety
