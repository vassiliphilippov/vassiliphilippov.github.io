lush

adjective
tender and juicy (of food or plants)
Pronunciation: / lʌʃ / (AmE), / lʌʃ / (BrE)
(of plants, food, gardens, or style) rich, abundant, luxurious, or full of life and color

Examples

Lush green garden
She served a salad of lush tomatoes and fresh mozzarella drizzled with olive oil.
The garden was overflowing with lush vegetables and herbs after the rain.
The peaches were so lush that their juice ran down my hands as I ate them.
The garden was filled with lush green plants after the rainy season.
The valley is known for its lush meadows and vibrant wildflowers.
They enjoyed a picnic under the shade of lush trees in the park.
The hotel was decorated in a lush style, featuring gold accents and velvet drapes.
The magazine featured photographs of lush homes owned by celebrities.
They attended a party in a lush penthouse overlooking the city skyline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: austere - severely simple; without luxury or decoration
  • antonym: barren - not producing vegetation; empty or sparse
  • synonym: lavish - sumptuously rich, elaborate, or luxurious
  • synonym: luxuriant - growing profusely; lush and rich
  • synonym: luxurious - very comfortable and expensive; giving great pleasure
  • synonym: succulent - tender, juicy (especially of food or plants)
