bill of lading

noun
document acknowledging receipt and terms of shipped goods
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪl əv ˈleɪdɪŋ /; / ccb6al 59v 8le6ad6a4b / (AmE), / ˌbɪl əv ˈleɪdɪŋ /; / ccb6al 59v 8le6ad6a4b / (BrE)
an official document (paper or electronic) issued by a carrier to acknowledge receipt of cargo for shipment, detailing the terms of transport and delivery

Examples

A bill of lading document
Each bill of lading specifies the terms and responsibilities of both shipper and carrier.
The shipping company required a bill of lading before releasing the cargo at the port.
Without a bill of lading, it is difficult to prove ownership of the goods during transit.
Shippers now use an electronic bill of lading to speed up customs clearance and reduce delays.
The company uploaded the electronic bill of lading to the secure platform for the customs agent.
Several countries now accept an electronically signed bill of lading as sufficient evidence of title.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bill of lading (paper) - the traditional printed or paper bill of lading used to acknowledge receipt and set terms of carriage.
  • synonym: eBL - abbreviation for electronic bill of lading; a commonly used short form in shipping and digital trade.
