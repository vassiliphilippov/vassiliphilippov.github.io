mutualization

noun
conversion to mutual ownership
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːtʃuələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting a company or organization so that it is owned by its members or policyholders

Examples

Mutualization of a company
The insurance company completed its mutualization to give policyholders a direct stake in the business.
After mutualization, the credit union was owned and governed by its members.
The board discussed the potential benefits and challenges of mutualization for the organization.
The insurance company completed its mutualization to allow policyholders to become part owners.
After mutualization, members gained the right to vote on important company decisions.
The process of mutualization can increase customer loyalty by giving them a stake in the business.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demutualization - the process of converting a mutual organization into a company owned by shareholders
