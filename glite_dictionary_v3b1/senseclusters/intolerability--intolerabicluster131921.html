intolerability

noun
unbearable quality
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɑːlərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɒlərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to accept or endure

Examples

The intolerability of heat
The intolerability of the heat forced everyone to leave the room.
She spoke about the intolerability of injustice in her community.
Many complained about the intolerability of the working conditions at the factory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tolerability - the quality of being able to be tolerated or endured
  • synonym: unbearableness - the state of being impossible to tolerate
