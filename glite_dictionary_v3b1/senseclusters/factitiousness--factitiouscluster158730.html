factitiousness

noun
artificial or false quality
Pronunciation: / fækˈtɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / fækˈtɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being artificially created or deliberately false rather than genuine or natural

Examples

The factitiousness of evidence
The factitiousness of the evidence led the jury to doubt the entire case.
Critics pointed out the factitiousness of the celebrity's public persona.
After careful analysis, the scientist exposed the factitiousness of the experiment's results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artificiality - the quality of being made or produced by humans rather than occurring naturally
  • antonym: genuineness - the quality of being real or authentic
