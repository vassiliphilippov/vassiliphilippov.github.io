sloughy

adjective
soft and muddy (of land or soil)
8.6 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈslaʊ.ɪ/ /; / /ˈslʌf.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈslaʊ.ɪ/ /; / /ˈslʌf.ɪ/ (BrE)
(of soil or land) soft, wet, and muddy, often making movement difficult.

Examples

A sloughy field
After the heavy rain, the field became so sloughy that boots sank deep into the mud.
The farmer warned us about the sloughy ground near the pond after last night's storm.
The hikers avoided the sloughy shoreline, choosing a firmer path for their trek.
