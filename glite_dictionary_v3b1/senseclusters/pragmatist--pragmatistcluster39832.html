pragmatist

noun
person focused on practical results
Pronunciation: / ˈpræɡmətɪst / (AmE), / ˈpræɡmətɪst / (BrE)
a person who deals with problems or situations sensibly and realistically, focusing on practical results rather than abstract theories

Examples

A practical pragmatist
As a pragmatist, she looked for effective solutions instead of dwelling on theoretical possibilities.
He approached the negotiations as a true pragmatist, focusing on achievable outcomes.
The company hired a pragmatist to steer it through the financial crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealist - a person guided by ideals rather than practical considerations
