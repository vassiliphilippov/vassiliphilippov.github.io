transdermal patch

noun
adhesive medicated skin patch
Pronunciation: / trænsˈdɜrməl pætʃ / (AmE), / trænzˈdɜːməl pætʃ / (BrE)
A medicated adhesive pad applied to the skin that delivers a controlled dose of medication through the skin into the bloodstream over time.

Examples

A transdermal patch on arm
He wore a transdermal patch on his upper arm to manage chronic pain.
Some medications are available in transdermal patch form for steady, long-term absorption.
The doctor prescribed a transdermal patch to help her quit smoking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: patch - a piece of material used to cover or protect a wound or sore
