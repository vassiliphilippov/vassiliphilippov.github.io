in no uncertain terms

adverb
emphatically and clearly stated
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪn noʊ ʌnˈsɝːtn̩ tɝːmz / (AmE), / ɪn nəʊ ʌnˈsɜːtn̩ tɜːmz / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is stated clearly, directly, and without any doubt or ambiguity

Examples

Said in no uncertain terms
Her manager told her in no uncertain terms that repeated lateness would not be tolerated.
The judge stated the consequences in no uncertain terms during the hearing.
They made their disappointment clear in no uncertain terms after the failed performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: explicitly - in a clear and detailed manner, leaving no room for confusion
  • antonym: implicitly - in a way that is not directly stated; indirectly
