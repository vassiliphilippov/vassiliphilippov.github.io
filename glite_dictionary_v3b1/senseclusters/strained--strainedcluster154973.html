strained

adjective
showing effort or artificiality
6.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /streɪnd/ (AmE), /streɪnd/ (BrE)
not natural, relaxed, or easy; showing signs of tension, stress, or being forced

Examples

A strained smile
He adopted a strained posture, as if trying too hard to impress the crowd.
Her smile looked a bit strained after hours of interviews.
The film's humor felt strained and failed to make the audience laugh.
After hours of negotiation, their conversation became increasingly strained.
He greeted us with a strained enthusiasm that seemed insincere.
Her strained smile hinted at her discomfort during the meeting.
During the meeting, his strained manner worried his colleagues.
Her strained smile made it clear she was trying to hide her true feelings.
The conversation became strained after the disagreement.

  • antonym: natural - spontaneous or genuine, lacking artificiality
