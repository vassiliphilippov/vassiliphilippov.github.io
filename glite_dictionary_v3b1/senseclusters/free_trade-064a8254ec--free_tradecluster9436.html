free trade

noun
international trade without restrictions
Pronunciation: / friː treɪd / (AmE), / friː treɪd / (BrE)
A system of international trade without government restrictions such as tariffs or quotas.

Examples

Free trade system
Debates about free trade and protectionism are common in discussions of economic policy.
Many economists believe that free trade promotes economic growth for all participating countries.
The two nations signed an agreement to encourage free trade between them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protectionism - the policy of restricting imports to protect domestic industries
