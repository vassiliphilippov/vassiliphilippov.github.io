biliously

adverb
in a sickly way
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪliəsli / (AmE), / ˈbɪliəsli / (BrE)
in a way that shows irritation, anger, or an unpleasant, sickly mood

Examples

He scowled biliously
He glared biliously at the noisy children, clearly annoyed by the disturbance.
The walls were painted a biliously bright shade of green that made some guests feel uneasy.
After the long, bumpy ride, she looked biliously pale and needed to sit down.
She responded biliously to the suggestion, making everyone uncomfortable.
The critic wrote biliously about the new play, leaving no kind words.
He looked biliously at the mess in the kitchen after the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irritably - in a way that shows annoyance or bad temper
  • synonym: nauseously - in a way that causes or shows nausea
  • antonym: pleasantly - in a way that is enjoyable or agreeable
