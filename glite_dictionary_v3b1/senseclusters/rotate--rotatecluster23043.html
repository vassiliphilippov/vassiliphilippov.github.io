rotate

verb
turn around a central point or axis
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊteɪt /; / ˈroʊˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊteɪt /; / ˈrəʊ.teɪt /; / ˈrəʊˌteɪt / (BrE)
to turn or cause something to turn around a central point or axis (including in machines, anatomy, or movement)

Examples

To rotate a wheel
Engineers designed the satellite dish to rotate automatically toward the strongest signal.
Please rotate the handle clockwise to open the hatch.
The children watched in awe as the planets rotate around the Sun in the model.
Engineers rotate the gears to test the mechanism.
Please rotate the knob clockwise to increase the volume.
She rotated the chair so she could see out the window.
Anatomically, some birds can rotate their toes outward to grasp branches.
During the performance, the dancer had to rotate her legs outward for the perfect arabesque.
The instructor demonstrated how to rotate your hip so that your foot points away from your body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revolve - move in a circle around a central point
  • synonym: spin - turn rapidly around an axis
  • synonym: splay - to spread or turn outwards, especially of limbs
  • antonym: stationary - not moving or not turning
  • antonym: stop - to come to a halt and cease motion or turning
  • synonym: twist - to turn something around its axis
