baldish

adjective
somewhat plain or blunt
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔldɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːldɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat plain, blunt, or lacking embellishment in style, language, or presentation

Examples

Baldish style
His report was baldish, giving only the basic facts without any extra details.
The review was helpful but a bit baldish, lacking the usual polite language.
She preferred a baldish style of communication, saying exactly what she meant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blunt - direct and without trying to be polite or soften the message
  • antonym: embellished - decorated or made more attractive with details
  • synonym: plain - simple and without decoration or embellishment
