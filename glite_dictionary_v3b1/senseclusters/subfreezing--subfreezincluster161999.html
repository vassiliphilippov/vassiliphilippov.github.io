subfreezing

adjective
below freezing
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈfriːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈfriːzɪŋ / (BrE)
describing temperatures that are lower than the freezing point of water (0°C or 32°F)

Examples

Subfreezing temperatures
The weather forecast warned of subfreezing temperatures overnight, so farmers covered their crops.
After several subfreezing days, the lake began to freeze over.
Hikers must wear special gear to stay warm in subfreezing conditions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cold - having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body
  • degree higher: freezing - at or below the temperature at which water turns to ice
