genus Larix

noun
genus of larch trees
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlærɪks / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˈlærɪks / (BrE)
A genus of deciduous coniferous trees in the pine family, commonly known as larches, native to cooler regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Examples

The genus Larix trees
Forests dominated by the genus Larix are common in Siberia and parts of Canada.
Many species in the genus Larix are valued for their strong, flexible wood.
The genus Larix is unusual among conifers because its trees are deciduous.
