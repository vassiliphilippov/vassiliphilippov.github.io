Fordist

adjective
mass production methods
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrdɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdɪst / (BrE)
relating to the system of mass production, standardized processes, and industrial organization developed by Henry Ford

Examples

Fordist production methods
The company implemented Fordist techniques to boost productivity and reduce costs.
Many early twentieth-century factories were organized along Fordist principles.
Critics argue that Fordist systems can lead to repetitive and monotonous work for employees.
The factory adopted a Fordist approach, focusing on standardized products and assembly line techniques.
Many economists debate whether Fordist principles are still relevant in today's globalized market.
During the mid-20th century, Fordist policies shaped labor relations and economic growth in many countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Taylorist - relating to scientific management and efficiency in industrial production
  • synonym: industrial - relating to industry or manufacturing
  • antonym: post-Fordist - relating to flexible production and labor practices after the Fordist era
