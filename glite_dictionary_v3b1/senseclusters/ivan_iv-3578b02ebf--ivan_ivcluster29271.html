Ivan IV

proper noun
first czar of Russia (1530–1584)
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪvɑn ðə ˈfɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈaɪvən ðə ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
(1530–1584) The first czar of Russia, known for his centralization of power and ruthless policies; also called Ivan the Terrible.

Examples

Ivan IV the Terrible
Many historians consider Ivan IV responsible for transforming Russia into a centralized state.
Russian literature often depicts Ivan IV as both a visionary ruler and a cruel tyrant.
The reign of Ivan IV was marked by military expansion and harsh autocratic rule.
