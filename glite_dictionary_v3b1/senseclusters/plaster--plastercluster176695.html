plaster

noun
healing paste for skin treatment
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstər / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
A soft, often heated mixture of meal or clay spread on a cloth and applied to the skin to treat inflammation or improve circulation.

Examples

A hot plaster on skin
After the injury, a clay plaster was placed on his arm to reduce inflammation.
In the past, people would use a mustard plaster as a home remedy for chest congestion.
The doctor applied a hot plaster to her swollen knee to relieve the pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poultice - a soft, moist mass applied to the body to relieve soreness
