method of least squares

noun
technique for curve fitting by minimizing squared errors
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛθəd əv list ˈskwɛrz / (AmE), / ˈmɛθəd əv liːst ˈskwɛəz / (BrE)
a mathematical and statistical technique for fitting a curve to data points by minimizing the sum of the squares of the distances (errors) between the points and the curve

Examples

The method of least squares technique
By applying the method of least squares, researchers minimized the error between predicted and observed values.
Many statistical software packages implement the method of least squares for curve fitting and regression analysis.
The method of least squares is widely used to determine the best-fitting straight line through a set of data points.
