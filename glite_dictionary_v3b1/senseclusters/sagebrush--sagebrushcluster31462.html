sagebrush

noun
aromatic shrub of arid North America
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈseɪdʒˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
a woody shrub with silvery-grey leaves and a strong aroma, common in arid regions of North America, especially of the genus Artemisia

Examples

The sagebrush plant
Coyotes often hide among the sagebrush while hunting for food.
Early settlers used sagebrush for fuel and shelter in the barren plains.
The desert is dotted with sagebrush as far as the eye can see.
Pronghorn antelope can often be seen grazing among the sagebrush in the desert.
The aroma of sagebrush filled the air after the rain.
Vast areas of the western United States are covered in sagebrush.
