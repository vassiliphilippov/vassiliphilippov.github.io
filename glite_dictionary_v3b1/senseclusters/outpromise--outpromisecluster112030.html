outpromise

verb
to make more promises
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
to promise more or make greater promises than someone else, often in a way that is exaggerated or unrealistic

Examples

To outpromise others
During the campaign, each politician tried to outpromise the others to win more votes.
It is easy to outpromise your competitors, but harder to deliver on those promises.
The two companies continued to outpromise each other with new features for their products.
The politician tried to outpromise his rivals by making bold claims about tax cuts.
If you constantly outpromise your customers, they will eventually lose trust in your company.
She realized it was better to be honest than to outpromise and disappoint her team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outdo - to do better than someone else
  • synonym: overpromise - to promise more than can be delivered
  • antonym: underpromise - to make fewer or less ambitious promises
