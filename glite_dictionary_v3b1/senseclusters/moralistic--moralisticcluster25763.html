moralistic

adjective
judging others by strict moral standards
1.0 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔːrəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒrəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
having or showing a narrow and conventional view of morality; often judging others according to a strict moral code.

Examples

A moralistic attitude
His moralistic attitude made it hard for him to accept other people's viewpoints.
Rather than a fair discussion, the debate turned into a moralistic lecture.
The novel was criticized for being too moralistic and preachy in tone.
