gegenschein

noun
faint light patch opposite the Sun in the night sky
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪgənˌʃaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪɡənʃaɪn / (BrE)
A faint, diffuse patch of light in the night sky that is visible directly opposite the Sun, caused by the reflection of sunlight by interplanetary dust.

Examples

The gegenschein glow
On clear, moonless nights, experienced astronomers may observe the gegenschein as a faint glow in the sky.
Scientists study the gegenschein to learn more about the distribution of interplanetary dust.
The gegenschein is best seen in rural areas far from city lights.
