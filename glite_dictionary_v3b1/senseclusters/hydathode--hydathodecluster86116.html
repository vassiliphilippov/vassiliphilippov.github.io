hydathode

noun
plant leaf pore that exudes water
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdæθoʊd / (AmE), / haɪˈdæθəʊd / (BrE)
a pore on the surface or edge of a leaf in higher plants that exudes water

Examples

Leaf hydathodes
Guttation in grasses often occurs through specialized hydathodes at the leaf edges.
Some plants use hydathodes to release excess water as droplets on their leaf tips.
When studying the leaf, the biologist noted several active hydathodes along the margin.
