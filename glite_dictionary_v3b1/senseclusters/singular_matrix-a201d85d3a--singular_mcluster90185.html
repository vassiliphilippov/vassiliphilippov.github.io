singular matrix

noun
square matrix with zero determinant
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡjələr ˈmeɪtrɪks / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡjʊlə ˈmeɪtrɪks / (BrE)
A square matrix in which the determinant is zero, making it non-invertible.

Examples

A singular matrix
A singular matrix cannot be inverted, which affects many operations in linear algebra.
If the determinant is zero, the matrix is called a singular matrix.
Systems of equations with a singular matrix of coefficients typically have no unique solution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonsingular matrix - a square matrix whose determinant is nonzero
