at the mercy of

preposition
completely controlled or affected by someone or something
Pronunciation: / æt ðə ˈmɝsi əv / (AmE), / ət ðə ˈmɜːsi ɒv / (BrE)
completely in the power of someone or something; unable to control what happens and subject to their decisions or actions

Examples

At the mercy of others
During the long flight delay, passengers were truly at the mercy of the airline staff.
Sailors lost at sea are completely at the mercy of the weather.
Small companies are often at the mercy of larger corporations in competitive markets.
