gad about

verb
go from place to place for pleasure or aimlessly
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɡæd əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / ɡad əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to go from place to place aimlessly or for pleasure, often without a definite plan

Examples

To gad about town
During her year abroad, she liked to gad about the city, discovering hidden cafes and shops.
He spent the summer gadding about the countryside with his friends instead of working.
Rather than stay indoors on weekends, she always finds a reason to gad about.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roam - to travel or move about without a fixed plan or destination
  • synonym: wander - to move about with no fixed course or aim
