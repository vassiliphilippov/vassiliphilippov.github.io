sec

noun
trigonometric function; secant
8.3
Pronunciation: /sek/ (AmE), /sek/ (BrE)
the trigonometric function that gives the ratio of the hypotenuse to the adjacent side in a right-angled triangle; the secant

Examples

Sec function value
In trigonometry, sec is often used alongside sine, cosine, and tangent.
The sec of an angle is equal to one divided by the cosine of that angle.
To solve the problem, she calculated the sec of 45 degrees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cos - the cosine function, reciprocal of secant
  • synonym: secant - the trigonometric function representing the ratio of the hypotenuse to the adjacent side
