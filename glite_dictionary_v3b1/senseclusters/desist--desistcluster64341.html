desist

verb
to stop action
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈzɪst /; / dɪˈsɪst / (BrE)
to stop doing something, especially something that is not allowed, discouraged, or unwanted

Examples

To desist from action
The protesters were asked to desist from blocking the entrance to the building.
If you do not desist from making noise, you will be asked to leave.
The company agreed to desist from using the disputed trademark after the court ruling.
The protesters agreed to desist from blocking the entrance after negotiations with the police.
If you do not desist from making noise, you will be asked to leave the library.
The company was warned to desist from using copyrighted material in its advertisements.
After several warnings, the students finally desisted from making noise in the library.
The judge ordered the company to desist from polluting the river.
He promised to desist from contacting her again after their argument.
