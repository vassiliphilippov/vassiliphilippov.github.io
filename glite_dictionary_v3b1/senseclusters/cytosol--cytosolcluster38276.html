cytosol

noun
liquid part of cell cytoplasm
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪtəˌsɑl / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪtəsɒl / (BrE)
the liquid component of the cytoplasm in which cell organelles and particles are suspended

Examples

The cytosol liquid
Proteins and enzymes are dissolved in the cytosol and carry out essential cellular functions.
Researchers observed changes in the cytosol composition during the experiment.
The cytosol is the fluid where organelles such as mitochondria are suspended.
