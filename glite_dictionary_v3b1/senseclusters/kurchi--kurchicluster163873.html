kurchi

noun
tropical Asian tree (Holarrhena) used in traditional medicine
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜːrtʃi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtʃi / (BrE)
A tropical Asian tree (Holarrhena antidysenterica) with hard white wood, whose bark was formerly used as a remedy for dysentery and diarrhea.

Examples

The kurchi tree
Botanists study kurchi for its potential pharmacological applications.
Many traditional healers value kurchi for its medicinal properties.
The bark of the kurchi tree was once used to treat intestinal diseases.
