solar month

noun
one-twelfth of a solar year
4.7 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.lər mʌnθ/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.lə mʌnθ/ (BrE)
A period of time equal to one-twelfth of a solar or tropical year, based on the Earth's position relative to the Sun.

Examples

A solar month
A solar month is longer than a lunar month because it is based on the Earth's orbit around the Sun.
In astronomy, a solar month helps divide the year into equal segments for observation.
The ancient Egyptians used a solar month to align their calendar with the seasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lunar month - a month measured by the cycles of the Moon
