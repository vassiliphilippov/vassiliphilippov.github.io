sereness

noun
being withered and dry
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪənɪs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being withered and dry.

Examples

The sereness of plants
Gardeners can recognize the sereness of plants that desperately need water.
In the harsh desert sun, the sereness of the landscape stretched as far as the eye could see.
The sereness of the old leaves was clear after the long, harsh drought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freshness - the state of being fresh rather than withered
  • synonym: sere - withered or dry, especially of vegetation
