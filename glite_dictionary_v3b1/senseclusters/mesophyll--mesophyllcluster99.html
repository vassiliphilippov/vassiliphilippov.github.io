mesophyll

noun
leaf inner tissue
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈme.zə.fɪl/ /; / /ˈme.sə.fɪl/ /; / /ˈmiː.zə.fɪl/ /; / /ˈmiː.sə.fɪl/ (AmE), /ˈme.zə.fɪl/ /; / /ˈme.sə.fɪl/ /; / /ˈmiː.zə.fɪl/ /; / /ˈmiː.sə.fɪl/ (BrE)
the inner tissue of a leaf, containing cells where most photosynthesis occurs

Examples

Leaf mesophyll tissue
The mesophyll is packed with chloroplasts that help the plant make food through photosynthesis.
Scientists studied the mesophyll to understand how leaves absorb sunlight efficiently.
Water moves from the veins into the mesophyll before reaching the surface of the leaf.
