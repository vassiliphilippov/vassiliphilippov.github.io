harakeke

noun
New Zealand flax plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɑːrəˈkɛki / (AmE), / ˌhɑːrəˈkɛki / (BrE)
a New Zealand plant with long, sword-shaped leaves, also known as New Zealand flax, used for weaving and fiber

Examples

Harakeke plant
The Māori have used harakeke for centuries to make baskets and mats.
We admired the tall, green leaves of the harakeke growing by the riverbank.
Artists today still use harakeke fibers to create traditional and modern crafts.
