red-baiting

adjective
accusing of communism
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌbeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌbeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
describing actions or remarks intended to discredit someone by accusing them of being a communist or communist sympathizer

Examples

Red-baiting remarks
The candidate's red-baiting comments were criticized as unfair and misleading by several journalists.
During the debate, she accused her opponent of red-baiting to distract from real issues.
Many historians have studied the impact of red-baiting on political discourse in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: McCarthyist - relating to making unfair accusations of subversion or treason without evidence
  • synonym: smearing - making false or damaging statements about someone
