ungag

verb
to lift a gag order
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡæɡ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡæɡ / (BrE)
to remove a restriction or order that prevents someone from speaking or publishing information, especially in a legal context

Examples

To ungag a witness
The judge decided to ungag the witness, allowing her to speak freely in court.
After the law changed, the government was forced to ungag the press and release the information.
Activists campaigned to ungag journalists who had been silenced by official orders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allow - to permit or let something happen
  • antonym: censor - to suppress or restrict speech or information
  • antonym: silence - to prevent someone from speaking or expressing themselves
