inrush

verb
to flow inward suddenly
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
to move or flow inward suddenly and forcefully, especially of a liquid or gas.

Examples

Water inrushes inside
Seawater kept inrushing through the damaged hull despite every pump running.
When the door opened, cold air began inrushing into the warm room.
The miners were caught off guard by the inrushing water after the wall collapsed.
