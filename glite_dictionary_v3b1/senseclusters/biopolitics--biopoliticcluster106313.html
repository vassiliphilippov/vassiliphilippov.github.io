biopolitics

noun
politics of biology
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈpɑlətɪks / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈpɒlɪtɪks / (BrE)
the study or practice of how political decisions and debates affect biological resources, ecosystems, animals, and biotechnology

Examples

Biopolitics of resources
The conference on biopolitics addressed the ethical implications of gene editing in agriculture.
Many activists are concerned about how biopolitics shapes laws on animal testing and conservation.
Debates in biopolitics often involve balancing economic growth with the protection of natural resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ecopolitics - politics related to environmental issues
  • synonym: environmental policy - government rules and actions regarding the environment
