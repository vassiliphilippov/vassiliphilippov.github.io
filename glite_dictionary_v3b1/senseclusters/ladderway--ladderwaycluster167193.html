ladderway

noun
passage with ladder
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlædərˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈlædəˌweɪ / (BrE)
a passage or vertical shaft with a ladder for moving between different levels, especially in ships or buildings

Examples

The ladderway shaft
The engineer descended the ladderway to inspect the machinery on the lower deck.
Emergency exits on the ship included a narrow ladderway between the decks.
Workers used the ladderway to access the maintenance area below the main floor.
The workers climbed the ladderway to reach the upper floor of the construction site.
In old ships, a ladderway connected the lower decks to the main deck.
Emergency exits sometimes include a ladderway instead of a staircase.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elevator shaft - a vertical passage housing an elevator
  • synonym: hatchway - an opening in a floor or deck, often with a cover
  • synonym: stairwell - a vertical shaft in a building containing a staircase
