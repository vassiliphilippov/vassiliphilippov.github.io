theory-based

adjective
founded on theory, not experiment
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪri beɪst / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri beɪst / (BrE)
based on theory rather than practical experience or experiment

Examples

Theory-based policies
Critics argue that theory-based policies may not always work in real-world situations.
His doctoral research was largely theory-based, with minimal empirical data.
The new curriculum takes a theory-based approach to teaching mathematics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empirical - relying on experience or observation rather than theory
