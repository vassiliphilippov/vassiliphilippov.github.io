overspender

noun
person who overspends
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈspɛndər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈspɛndə / (BrE)
a person, group, or organization that spends more money than is wise, necessary, or allowed

Examples

An overspender person
After reviewing his bank statements, he realized he had become an overspender without noticing.
Credit card companies often target the overspender with tempting offers and rewards.
Being an overspender can lead to serious financial problems if not controlled.
The committee was criticized as an overspender after exceeding its annual budget.
He admitted to being an overspender and started tracking his expenses more carefully.
Many governments are labeled overspenders when they fail to control public spending.
