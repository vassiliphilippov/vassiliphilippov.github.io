collapsibility

noun

in statistics, the property of a measure or association to remain unchanged when calculated across different groups or conditions

Examples

Statistical collapsibility
The collapsibility of a statistical measure is important when comparing results across different studies.
Researchers must consider collapsibility to ensure their findings are not misleading when combining data.
Understanding collapsibility helps statisticians interpret associations between variables accurately.
