ward-heeler

noun
local political operative in a party machine
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /wɔːrd ˈhiː.lər/ (AmE), /wɔːd ˈhiː.lə/ (BrE)
A local political operative who belongs to a small group that controls a party or political machine, typically for personal or party gain rather than public service.

Examples

A ward-heeler politician
He gained a reputation as an effective ward-heeler, delivering blocks of votes to his party.
Many citizens distrusted the influence wielded by the city's most notorious ward-heeler.
The ward-heeler offered favors in exchange for votes during the local election.
