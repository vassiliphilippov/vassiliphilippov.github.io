Fleet Street

proper noun
historic center of British newspaper publishing, London
Pronunciation: / fliːt strit / (AmE), / fliːt striːt / (BrE)
A street in central London historically known as the center of British newspaper publishing; often used to refer to the British press as a whole.

Examples

Walk down Fleet Street
After the major newspapers left Fleet Street, the area changed its character.
Many journalists began their careers on Fleet Street in the 1970s.
The power of Fleet Street has shaped public opinion in Britain for decades.
