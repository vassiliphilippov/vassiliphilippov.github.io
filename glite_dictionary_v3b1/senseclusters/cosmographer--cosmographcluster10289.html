cosmographer

noun
expert in cosmography
Pronunciation: / kɑzˈmɑɡrəfər / (AmE), / kɒzˈmɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who studies or is an expert in cosmography, the science that maps the general features of the universe or the earth

Examples

A cosmographer studies universe
As a leading cosmographer, she was fascinated by the structure and scale of the universe.
During the Age of Discovery, a cosmographer played a vital role in creating maps of the known world.
The detailed star charts were compiled by a renowned cosmographer in the seventeenth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: astronomer - a scientist who studies celestial bodies
  • synonym: cartographer - a person who draws or produces maps
