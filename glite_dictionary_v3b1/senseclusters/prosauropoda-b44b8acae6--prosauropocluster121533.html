Prosauropoda

noun
early group of herbivorous dinosaurs
Pronunciation: / proʊˌsɔːrəˈpoʊdə / (AmE), / prəʊˌsɔːrəˈpəʊdə / (BrE)
an extinct suborder of early herbivorous dinosaurs that lived before the sauropods

Examples

The Prosauropoda dinosaurs
Paleontologists have discovered new specimens of Prosauropoda in South America.
The fossils of Prosauropoda provide valuable insight into the evolution of early dinosaurs.
Unlike their larger descendants, Prosauropoda were generally smaller and less massive.

Many paleontologists believe that Prosauropoda were the evolutionary precursors to the giant sauropods.
The Prosauropoda lived during the Late Triassic and Early Jurassic periods.
