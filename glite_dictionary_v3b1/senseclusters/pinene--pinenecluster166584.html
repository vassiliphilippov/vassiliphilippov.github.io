pinene

noun
pine-scented hydrocarbon
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪniːn / (AmE), / ˈpaɪniːn / (BrE)
a colorless liquid hydrocarbon found in pine resin, responsible for the scent of pine trees and used in industry

Examples

Pinene from pine
Pinene is commonly extracted from pine trees for use in cleaning products and fragrances.
The fresh smell in a pine forest is largely due to the presence of pinene in the air.
Industrial chemists use pinene as a starting material to make other useful chemicals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: terpene - a large class of organic compounds produced by plants, often with strong odors
