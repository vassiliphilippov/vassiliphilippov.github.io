white backlash

noun
hostile reaction by whites to Black civil rights advances
3.4 6.8
Pronunciation: /waɪt ˈbæk.læʃ/ /; / /hwaɪt ˈbæk.læʃ/ (AmE), /waɪt ˈbæk.læʃ/ (BrE)
A hostile or negative reaction by some white people, especially white racists, to the progress of civil rights for Black people.

Examples

White backlash reaction
Researchers have studied the effects of white backlash on election outcomes in the United States.
Some commentators warned that rapid policy changes could provoke a white backlash at the polls.
The civil rights movement of the 1960s was often met with a white backlash in many Southern states.
