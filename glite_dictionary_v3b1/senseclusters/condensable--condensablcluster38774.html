condensable

noun
substance that can condense
7.4
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈden.sə.bəl/ (AmE), /kən.ˈden.sə.bəl/ /; / /kɒn.ˈden.sə.bəl/ (BrE)
a substance, especially a vapor, that can be changed from a gas to a liquid by cooling or compression

Examples

A condensable vapor
A condensable such as water vapor can turn into liquid when cooled.
Engineers must remove any condensable from the system to prevent blockages.
The laboratory experiment measured how quickly the condensable formed droplets on the cold surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym: noncondensable - a substance that cannot be easily changed from a gas to a liquid
  • synonym: vapor - a substance in the gas phase at a temperature lower than its critical point
