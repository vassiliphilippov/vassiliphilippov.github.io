bronchus

noun
airway branch of the trachea
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑŋ.kəs / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋ.kəs / (BrE)
either of the two main tubes that branch off from the trachea and carry air to and from the lungs

Examples

The bronchus tube
An infection in the bronchus can cause bronchitis and breathing problems.
During the procedure, the doctor examined the inside of each bronchus.
The right bronchus is typically wider and shorter than the left bronchus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bronchi - plural form of bronchus; airways branching to the lungs
  • synonym: trachea - the windpipe; the main airway leading to the bronchi
