accretionary

adjective
formed by gradual accumulation
8.5
Pronunciation: / əˈkriːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / əˈkriːʃənəri / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from gradual accumulation or growth, especially by external addition.

Examples

Accretionary wedges
Geologists studied the accretionary wedges formed at tectonic plate boundaries.
The accretionary processes in the early solar system led to the formation of planets.
The mountain range is an accretionary structure, built up over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accretive - tending to increase by accretion or growth
