dystonia

noun
muscle contraction disorder
8.1
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtoʊniə / (AmE), / dɪsˈtəʊniə / (BrE)
a medical condition where involuntary muscle contractions cause twisting movements or abnormal postures

Examples

Muscle dystonia
The neurologist explained that dystonia could affect any part of the body.
Her hand cramped painfully due to dystonia during the piano recital.
Physical therapy helped reduce the symptoms of dystonia in his neck muscles.
The neurologist explained that dystonia can affect people of any age.
Physical therapy helped reduce the severity of her dystonia symptoms.
Some musicians develop dystonia in their hands after years of repetitive movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move muscles
  • synonym: spasm - a sudden, involuntary muscle contraction
