deportation

noun
expulsion of someone from a country
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: /diː.pɔːr.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.pɔː.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the official act of removing someone from a country, usually for legal, immigration, or political reasons

Examples

To deport a person
Legal aid organizations help those at risk of deportation understand their rights.
She faced deportation after her visa expired and her appeal was denied.
The threat of deportation forced him to leave the country voluntarily.
After years of legal battles, his deportation was finally halted by the court.
Stories of deportation have made many immigrants fearful for their families' futures.
The government ordered the deportation of several foreign nationals suspected of illegal activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • synonym: expulsion - forcing someone to leave a place, especially a country
  • antonym: immigration - the act of coming to live permanently in a foreign country
  • antonym: repatriation - the act of returning someone to their own country
