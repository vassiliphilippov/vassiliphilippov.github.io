archconservative

adjective
extremely conservative
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrtʃkənˈsɜːrvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃkənˈsɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
extremely conservative in politics or opinions, strongly opposing change or reform

Examples

Archconservative politician
The senator's archconservative views made him a leading opponent of the new legislation.
Many young voters disagreed with the archconservative policies proposed by the committee.
Her archconservative stance on education reform was clear during the debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressive - favoring or advocating progress, change, or reform
  • synonym: reactionary - strongly opposed to political or social change
