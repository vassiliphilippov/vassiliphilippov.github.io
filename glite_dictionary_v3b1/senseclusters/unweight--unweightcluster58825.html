unweight

verb
to remove statistical weights
8.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈweɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈweɪt / (BrE)
to remove statistical weights from data or calculations, so that all items are treated equally

Examples

To unweight data
The researchers decided to unweight the survey results to analyze the raw data.
After applying the weights, you can unweight the figures to check for consistency.
Statisticians often unweight data to compare it with the original sample.
The researchers decided to unweight the survey results to avoid bias from overrepresented groups.
If you unweight the data, each response will have the same influence on the outcome.
Some analysts prefer to unweight their calculations for a straightforward comparison.
