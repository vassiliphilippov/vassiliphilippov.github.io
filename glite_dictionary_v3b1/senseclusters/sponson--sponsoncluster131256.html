sponson

noun
side projection for stability
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑnsən / (AmE), / ˈspɒnsən / (BrE)
a projection on the side of a boat, ship, aircraft, or vehicle, especially one that provides stability or supports equipment

Examples

Boat sponson
The engineers added a sponson to the hovercraft to improve its balance on water.
Each side of the seaplane was fitted with a sponson for extra buoyancy during takeoff.
The new boat design features a reinforced sponson to help it handle rough seas.
The engineers added a sponson to each side of the boat to improve its balance in rough water.
Helicopters used for sea rescue often have a sponson to hold extra equipment.
The old warship's sponsons were reinforced to support heavier guns.
The rescue boat's sponson helped keep it steady in rough water.
Engineers added a sponson to the vehicle to mount additional equipment.
The ship's sponsons provided extra stability during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: float - a structure that provides buoyancy to keep something afloat
  • antonym: hull - the main body of a ship or boat
  • synonym: outrigger - a projecting structure attached to a boat for stability
