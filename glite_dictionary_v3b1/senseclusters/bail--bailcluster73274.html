bail

verb
to leave abruptly
Pronunciation: / beɪl / (AmE), / beɪl / (BrE)
to leave suddenly or avoid a responsibility, often unexpectedly or without warning

Examples

To bail on plans
He bailed on the meeting without telling anyone, leaving the team confused.
When things got difficult, she decided to bail instead of facing the problem.
They were counting on him, but he bailed at the last minute.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ditch - to leave someone or something behind suddenly
  • antonym: stay - to remain in a place or situation
