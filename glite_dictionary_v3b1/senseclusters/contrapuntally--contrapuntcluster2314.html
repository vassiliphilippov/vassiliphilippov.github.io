contrapuntally

adverb
in counterpoint style
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntrəˈpʌntəli / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈpʌntəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or resembles counterpoint in music, involving the combination of two or more independent melodies

Examples

Voices contrapuntally arranged
The composer arranged the voices contrapuntally to create a complex and beautiful harmony.
In the fugue, the themes are developed contrapuntally throughout the piece.
The two instruments played contrapuntally, weaving independent melodies that complemented each other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polyphonically - in a manner involving multiple independent melodies
