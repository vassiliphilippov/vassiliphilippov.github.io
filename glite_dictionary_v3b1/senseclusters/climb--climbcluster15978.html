climb

noun
an upward journey, act of going up
1.6 7.62/m
Pronunciation: / klaÉªm / (AmE), / klaÉªm / (BrE)
the act or process of going up or moving to a higher position (literal or figurative)

Examples

A tough climb
After the long climb, the view from the top was spectacular.
Her first climb up the rock wall was challenging but rewarding.
The steep climb left everyone breathless by the time we reached the summit.
Her rapid climb to the top of the company surprised everyone.
The climb in temperature this week has been unusually sharp.
There was a steady climb in the price of oil throughout the year.

Synonyms

  • ascent
    4.2 2.89/m
    ascent is the act of moving upward and people use it for physical climbs or career rises, while climb means going up.
  • ascension
    7.5 1.13/m
    ascension is a formal or religious word for a rise to higher status or heaven, while climb means the general act of going up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascent - the act of rising or going up
  • antonym: descent - the act of going downwards
