French ultramarine

noun
artificial bright blue pigment
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌfrɛntʃ ˌʌltrəˈmɛriːn / (AmE), / ˌfrɛntʃ ˌʌltrəˈmɑːriːn / (BrE)
a bright blue pigment similar to natural ultramarine, produced artificially from sodium aluminosilicate and used in paint and other applications.

Examples

French ultramarine pigment
Many artists prefer French ultramarine for its vibrant hue and consistent texture.
She mixed French ultramarine with other pigments to achieve the desired shade of blue.
The formula for making French ultramarine was a major breakthrough in 19th-century chemistry.
