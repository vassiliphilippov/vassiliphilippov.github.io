Newtonianism

noun
Newton's scientific principles
Pronunciation: / nuːˈtoʊniənɪzəm / (AmE), / njuːˈtəʊniənɪzəm / (BrE)
the scientific principles, philosophy, or worldview based on the theories and discoveries of Isaac Newton

Examples

Newtonianism scientific principles
The development of Newtonianism marked a turning point in the history of science.
Many scientific advances in the 18th century were influenced by Newtonianism.
Modern physics has moved beyond Newtonianism in some areas, but its principles are still widely taught.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classical mechanics - the branch of physics based on Newton's laws of motion
  • antonym: relativity - the scientific theory developed by Einstein that replaced Newtonian physics at high speeds and gravity
