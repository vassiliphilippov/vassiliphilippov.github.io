possessive case

noun
grammatical case showing possession
Pronunciation: / pəˈzɛsɪv keɪs / (AmE), / pəˈzɛsɪv keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case used to indicate ownership or possession

Examples

The possessive case form
In English, the possessive case is often formed by adding an apostrophe and 's' to a noun.
Teachers often explain the possessive case when teaching beginner English grammar.
The possessive case shows that something belongs to someone, as in ‘John’s book’.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genitive case - the grammatical case marking a relationship of possession, origin, or association
