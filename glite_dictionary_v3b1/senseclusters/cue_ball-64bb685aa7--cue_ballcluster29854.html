cue ball

noun
ball struck by the cue in billiards or pool
Pronunciation: / ˈkju ˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈkjuː ˌbɔːl / (BrE)
the ball that the player strikes with the cue in billiards or pool, usually white and used to hit other balls

Examples

The cue ball strike
He carefully lined up his shot and struck the cue ball with precision.
In pool, the cue ball must always be hit first to avoid committing a foul.
She accidentally knocked the cue ball off the table during her turn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: billiard ball - any of the balls used in the game of billiards or pool
  • antonym: object ball - any ball other than the cue ball, which is intended to be struck by the cue ball
