riser

noun
vertical part of a stair step
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪzər / (AmE), / ˈraɪzə / (BrE)
the vertical part between two stair treads in a set of steps or a staircase

Examples

Staircase riser
Building codes often specify the maximum height for a staircase riser.
Don't forget to paint the riser as well as the tread for a complete finish.
The carpenter measured each riser to make sure the stairs were even.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tread - the horizontal part of a step where the foot rests
