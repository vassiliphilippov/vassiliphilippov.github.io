bring in

verb
convey or allow something to come in
3.2 2.8
Pronunciation: /brɪŋ ɪn/ /; / /brɪŋ ən/ (AmE), /brɪŋ ɪn/ /; / /brɪŋ ən/ (BrE)
to convey or allow something, such as a sound, signal, or information, to come into a particular place, often by means of equipment or a process

Examples

To bring in a signal
The new antenna will bring in more radio stations from neighboring cities.
The security cameras bring in live video feeds to the control room.
This advanced microphone can bring in even the faintest sounds from across the hall.
