isobase

noun
line connecting equal land uplift on a map
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsoʊˌbeɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌbeɪs / (BrE)
A line drawn on a map connecting points where land has risen by the same amount.

Examples

An isobase line
Geologists use isobases to track historical changes in the Earth's surface elevation.
New isobase measurements help scientists understand tectonic activity in the region.
The map shows several isobases indicating varying degrees of post-glacial land uplift.
