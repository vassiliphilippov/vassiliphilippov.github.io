crest

verb
form or lie at the top of
Pronunciation: / krɛst / (AmE), / krɛst / (BrE)
to reach or form the highest point of something, such as a wave, river, or enthusiasm

Examples

To crest a wave
A wave crested just before breaking onto the shore.
The mountain ridges were crested with fresh snow after the storm.
The rolling hills crested with wildflowers in the spring sunlight.
Enthusiasm for the project crested just before the grand opening ceremony.
If the levels crest above the dam, nearby homes may be at risk.
The river is expected to crest tomorrow evening after days of heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cap - to cover the top of something
  • antonym: ebb - reach a lowest point or decline
  • synonym: peak - reach a maximum level or point
