cede

verb
formally give up control or ownership
Pronunciation: / sid /; / siːd / (AmE), / siːd / (BrE)
to formally give up control, ownership, or rights (especially land or power) to another person, country, or group

Examples

To cede territory
After the war, the country was forced to cede part of its territory to its neighbor.
He refused to cede his authority, even when the board demanded it.
The company agreed to cede control of its assets to a government regulator.
After long negotiations, they finally ceded the disputed land.
He agreed to cede control of the project to his colleague.
The country was forced to cede large portions of its territory after the war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim something
  • antonym: retain - to continue to have or keep possession
  • synonym: surrender - to give up possession or control, often under compulsion
