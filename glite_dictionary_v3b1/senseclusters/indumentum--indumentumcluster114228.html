indumentum

noun
covering of fine hairs or scales
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndjʊˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / ˌɪndjuːˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
a covering of fine hairs or sometimes scales found on the surface of leaves, stems, or insects

Examples

Leaf indumentum
Many plant species develop an indumentum on their leaves to reduce water loss.
The furry indumentum on the caterpillar's body protects it from predators.
Under the microscope, the indumentum on the stem was clearly visible.
