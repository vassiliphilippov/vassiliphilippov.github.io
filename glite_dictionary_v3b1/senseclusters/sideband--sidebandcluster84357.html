sideband

noun
radio frequency band
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌbænd /; / ˈsaɪdbænd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌbænd /; / ˈsaɪdbænd / (BrE)
a band of frequencies on either side of a carrier wave in radio transmission

Examples

The sideband frequencies
The technician adjusted the sideband to improve the clarity of the radio transmission.
Understanding the sideband is essential for anyone studying radio communications.
The interference was traced to a problem with the sideband frequencies.
The technician monitored the sideband to ensure the radio signal was clear.
Interference in the sideband can affect the quality of the transmitted audio.
Modern communication systems often use both the upper and lower sideband for efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carrier wave - a wave that is modulated to carry a signal in radio transmission
  • synonym: frequency band - a range of frequencies within a given spectrum
  • synonym: modulation - the process of varying a wave to transmit a signal
