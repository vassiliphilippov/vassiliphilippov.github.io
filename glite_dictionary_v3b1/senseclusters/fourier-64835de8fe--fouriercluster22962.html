Fourier

proper noun
French social philosopher
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊrieɪ / (AmE), / ˈfʊərieɪ / (BrE)
Charles Fourier (1772–1837), a French social philosopher known for his theories on cooperative communities and utopian socialism.

Examples

Fourier philosophy
The theories of Fourier influenced many utopian communities in the nineteenth century.
Historians often discuss Fourier when examining the roots of socialist thought.
Many reformers admired the social experiments inspired by Fourier in France and beyond.
