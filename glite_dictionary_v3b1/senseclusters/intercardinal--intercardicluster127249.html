intercardinal

noun
compass midpoint direction
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈkɑːrdɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkɑːˈdɪnəl / (BrE)
a direction on a compass that is halfway between two main cardinal points, such as northeast or southwest

Examples

An intercardinal point
The ship changed course to follow the intercardinal direction of southeast.
On the map, each intercardinal is marked clearly between the main compass points.
Hikers often use intercardinal directions to navigate more precisely in the wilderness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cardinal point - one of the four main compass directions: north, south, east, or west
  • synonym: ordinal direction - a direction between the cardinal points, such as northeast or southwest
