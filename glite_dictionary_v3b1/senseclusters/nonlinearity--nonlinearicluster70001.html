nonlinearity

noun
out-of-order storytelling
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌlɪniˈærəti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnlɪniˈærəti / (BrE)
the quality of a narrative or story in which events are not presented in chronological order

Examples

Nonlinearity in storytelling
The nonlinearity of the novel made it both challenging and exciting to follow.
Directors often use nonlinearity to create suspense or reveal character backgrounds gradually.
Some viewers find nonlinearity in films confusing, while others enjoy piecing the story together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chronology - the arrangement of events in the order they occurred
  • synonym: flashback - a scene set in a time earlier than the main story
