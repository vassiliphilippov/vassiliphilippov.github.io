imputable

adjective

Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpjuː.tə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpuː.tə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpjuː.tə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpuː.tə.bəl/ (BrE)
Able to be attributed or ascribed to a particular person, cause, or source.

Examples

Imputable to causes
His initial success was largely imputable to good timing and the support of his colleagues.
The accountant argued that the error was not imputable to him but to a software glitch.
The failure of the project was imputable to poor planning rather than lack of effort.
