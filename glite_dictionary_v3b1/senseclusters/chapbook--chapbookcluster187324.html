chapbook

noun
small poetry book
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæpˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈtʃæpˌbʊk / (BrE)
a small, inexpensive book containing poems, stories, or essays, often self-published or produced in limited editions

Examples

A poetry chapbook
The poet was thrilled to see her first chapbook in print at the local bookstore.
Many writers use a chapbook to share their early work with a small audience.
He collected rare chapbooks from independent presses around the country.
The poet released a limited edition chapbook to share her latest work with friends and fans.
He found an old chapbook of folk tales at the local market.
Many writers start by publishing a chapbook before moving on to full-length books.

  • synonym: pamphlet - a small booklet or brochure containing information or arguments
