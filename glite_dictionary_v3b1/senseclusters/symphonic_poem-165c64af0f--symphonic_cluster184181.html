symphonic poem

noun
orchestral composition based on a story or theme
4.3 6.3
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈfɑnɪk ˈpoʊəm / (AmE), / sɪmˈfɒnɪk ˈpəʊəm / (BrE)
A piece of orchestral music, usually in a single continuous movement, that illustrates or evokes the content of a poem, story, painting, or other non-musical source.

Examples

A symphonic poem piece
Franz Liszt is often credited with inventing the symphonic poem as a musical form.
The symphonic poem painted a vivid picture of the legendary hero's adventures through sound.
The orchestra performed a symphonic poem inspired by Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet.
