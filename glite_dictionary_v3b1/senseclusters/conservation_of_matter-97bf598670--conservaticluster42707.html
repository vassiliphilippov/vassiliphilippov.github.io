conservation of matter

noun
principle that matter is neither created nor destroyed
1.7 3.2
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.sər.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv ˈmæt.ər/ /; / /kən.sər.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv ˈmæt.ər/ (AmE), /kɒn.sə.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv ˈmæt.ə/ /; / /kən.sə.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv ˈmæt.ə/ (BrE)
the scientific principle that matter cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system; it can only change forms

Examples

Conservation of matter principle
According to the conservation of matter, mass remains constant in a closed system.
