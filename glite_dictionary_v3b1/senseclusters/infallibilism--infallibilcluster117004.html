infallibilism

noun
doctrine of no error
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.fə.ˈlɪ.bɪ.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ɪn.fə.ˈlɪ.bɪ.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that a person or institution, especially the Pope, is incapable of error in certain teachings on faith or morals

Examples

Papal infallibilism
The doctrine of infallibilism is central to some religious debates about papal authority.
Many theologians have written extensively on the concept of infallibilism in church history.
Some critics argue that infallibilism can lead to unquestioned acceptance of official teachings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fallibilism - the belief that no one can be completely certain or free from error
  • synonym: inerrancy - the belief that something is free from error
