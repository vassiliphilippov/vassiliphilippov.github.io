dignitary

noun
important or high-ranking person
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪɡ.nəˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡ.nɪ.təri / (BrE)
A person who holds an important or high-ranking position, especially in government or the church.

Examples

A dignitary official
A local dignitary was invited to cut the ribbon at the opening ceremony.
Several foreign dignitaries attended the state banquet hosted by the president.
The mayor, a prominent dignitary, gave a speech to mark the occasion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: VIP - a very important person
