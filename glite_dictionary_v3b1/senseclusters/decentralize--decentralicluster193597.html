decentralize

verb
transfer control from center to local units
1.0
Pronunciation: / diˈsɛntrəˌlaɪz /; / ˌdiːˈsɛntrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈsɛntrəlaɪz /; / ˌdiːˈsɛntrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to transfer authority, control, or responsibility from a central organization or government to local branches or smaller units

Examples

To decentralize power
After the system was decentralized, local managers gained significant autonomy.
Many companies decentralize their operations to respond more quickly to regional needs.
There is a growing movement to decentralize power within government.
After the revolution, food distribution was decentralised across all regions.
Many governments are working to decentralise power to better serve local communities.
The company decided to decentralise its management structure to improve efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralise - to concentrate control or authority in a central organization
  • antonym: centralize - to bring under central control or authority
