rumbling

noun
loud, low, continuous noise
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌm.blɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌm.blɪŋ / (BrE)
a loud, low, dull, continuous noise; a deep sound that continues for a while, often produced by thunder, machinery, or internal bodily processes

Examples

Rumbling of thunder
He woke up to the rumbling of his empty stomach.
Suddenly, a deep rumbling came from beneath the ground, shaking the windows.
The rumbling of the distant train could be heard throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hum - a low, steady, continuous sound
  • synonym: roar - a loud, deep, long sound
