flatteringly

adverb
in a complimentary way
Pronunciation: / ˈflætərɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈflætərɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that makes someone or something appear more attractive, impressive, or praised, often by exaggerating positive qualities

Examples

Speak flatteringly about
She smiled flatteringly at her friend after seeing the new haircut.
The review described the performance flatteringly, highlighting the actor's strengths.
He spoke flatteringly about his colleague's achievements during the meeting.
The artist painted her portrait flatteringly, making her look younger than she actually was.
He spoke flatteringly about his boss during the meeting to gain her approval.
The review described the film flatteringly, overlooking its obvious flaws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complimentarily - in a way that expresses praise or admiration
  • antonym: critically - in a way that expresses disapproval or criticism
