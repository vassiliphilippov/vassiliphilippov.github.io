lunar latitude

noun
imaginary east–west line on the Moon
Pronunciation: / ˈluːnɚ ˈlætɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈluːnə ˈlætɪtjuːd / (BrE)
an imaginary line around the Moon, parallel to its equator, used to specify north–south position on the lunar surface

Examples

Lunar latitude line
Each point on the Moon can be described by its lunar latitude and longitude, similar to coordinates on Earth.
Mapping the lunar latitude and longitude of craters helps scientists understand the Moon's geography.
The rover landed at a lunar latitude of 20 degrees north, in the Moon's Mare Serenitatis region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latitude - imaginary east–west line used on Earth's surface for geographic coordinate
