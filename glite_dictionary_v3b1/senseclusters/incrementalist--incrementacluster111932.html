incrementalist

adjective
favoring gradual change
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪŋkrəˈmɛntəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪŋkrɪˈmɛntəlɪst / (BrE)
favoring or describing a policy or approach that introduces change gradually in small steps rather than all at once

Examples

Incrementalist approach
The committee took an incrementalist approach, making small adjustments to the policy each year.
Critics argued that the incrementalist reforms were too slow to address urgent problems.
An incrementalist strategy can help avoid resistance by introducing changes step by step.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gradualist - supporting gradual rather than sudden change
  • antonym: radical - favoring major or fundamental change
