treason

noun
betrayal of one's country
4.4 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtriː.zən/ (AmE), /ˈtriː.zən/ (BrE)
the crime of betraying one's country, especially by attempting to kill the sovereign or overthrow the government

Examples

Committing treason
Committing treason can lead to life imprisonment in many countries.
He was arrested and charged with treason against the state.
The general's secret communication with the enemy was considered an act of treason.
Accusations of treason were used to silence political opponents during the regime.
Committing treason is punishable by the most severe penalties under the law.
He was found guilty of treason for conspiring against the government.
Betraying military secrets to a foreign enemy is considered an act of treason.
He was arrested and charged with treason after the failed coup.
In history, many leaders have been executed for treason against their own nation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betrayal - the act of betraying someone or something
  • antonym: loyalty - faithful allegiance to a person or cause
  • antonym: patriotism - devotion to one's country
  • synonym: sedition - conduct or speech inciting people to rebel against authority
