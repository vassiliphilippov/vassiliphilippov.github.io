molucca balm

noun
aromatic annual plant with bell-shaped calyces
Pronunciation: / məˈlʌkə bɑm / (AmE), / məˈlʌkə bɑːm / (BrE)
An aromatic annual plant with tall stems bearing small whitish flowers, each enclosed in a greatly enlarged saucer-shaped or bell-shaped calyx; also known as bells of Ireland.

Examples

molucca balm plant
Despite its name, molucca balm is not native to the Moluccas but to western Asia.
Gardeners often plant molucca balm for its striking green calyces in summer bouquets.
The unique, bell-shaped flowers of molucca balm make it popular in floral arrangements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bells of Ireland - common name for molucca balm, a plant with green bell-shaped calyces
