loudish

adjective
somewhat noisy
Pronunciation: / ˈlaʊdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlaʊdɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat loud or tending to be a little noisy

Examples

Loudish music
The music at the party was loudish, but not enough to bother the neighbors.
Her voice became loudish when she got excited about the story.
The restaurant was loudish during lunch, but it quieted down in the afternoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: noisy - making a lot of noise
  • antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
