stipple

verb
engrave or draw using dots or strokes
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪpəl /; / ˈstɪp.əl / (AmE), / ˈstɪp(ə)l /; / ˈstɪp.əl /; / ˈstɪpəl / (BrE)
to create an image, pattern, or texture by making many small dots or short strokes, especially in drawing, painting, or engraving

Examples

To stipple a picture
He stippled tiny dots along the edge of the image to add depth and dimension.
If you stipple the surface gently, you can create a soft, textured look.
The artist began to stipple the copper plate to achieve a subtle shading effect.
After stippling blue dots along the edge, she set the painting aside to dry.
He carefully stippled the shadows to give the portrait a lifelike appearance.
The artist stipples paint onto the canvas to achieve a textured background.
An artist stipples the canvas to create texture and depth.
Raindrops stippled the surface of the lake, creating a pattern of tiny circles.
The sunlight stippled the forest floor with patches of gold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dab - to touch or apply lightly with a repeated motion
  • synonym: dot - to mark with a small round spot; pointillism technique
  • synonym: engrave - to cut or carve a design into a hard surface
  • synonym: mottle - to mark with spots or smears of color
  • synonym: spot - to mark with small round marks
