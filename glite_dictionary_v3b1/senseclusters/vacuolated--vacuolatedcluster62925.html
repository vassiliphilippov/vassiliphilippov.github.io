vacuolated

adjective
containing vacuoles (cell biology)
Pronunciation: / ˈvæk.ju.əˌleɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈvæk.ju.ə.leɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or tissue) formed into or containing one or more vacuoles, or small membrane-bound cavities.

Examples

Vacuolated cells
Damage to tissue can cause cells to become vacuolated as they accumulate fluid.
Many plant cells appear vacuolated due to their large central vacuoles.
The pathologist noted that the tumor cells were highly vacuolated under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonvacuolated - not containing vacuoles
