lineally

adverb
by direct descent
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪniəli / (AmE), / ˈlɪniəli / (BrE)
in a direct line of descent from an ancestor, or in a straight line

Examples

Passes lineally from father
Inheritance passes lineally from father to eldest son in that tradition.
She is related lineally to the royal family through her grandfather.
The title can only be claimed by those who are lineally descended from the founder.
The property was inherited lineally from father to son for generations.
The statues were arranged lineally along the garden path.
He could trace his ancestry lineally back to the original settlers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collaterally - related through a sibling of an ancestor, not direct line
  • synonym: directly - in a straight or direct way
