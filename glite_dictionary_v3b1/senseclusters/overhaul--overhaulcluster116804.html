overhaul

noun
thorough repair or renovation
5.1 4.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àào äv…ôrÀåh…îl /; / Àào äv…öÀåh…îl / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàh…îÀêl / (BrE)
a thorough examination and repair or renovation to improve something, especially a system, machine, or process

Examples

A complete overhaul
A major overhaul of the tax system is expected next year.
After several years of neglect, the factory finally underwent a complete overhaul.
The company announced an overhaul of its safety protocols.
After years of heavy use, the engine finally received a complete overhaul.
Regular overhaul of machinery helps prevent breakdowns and costly repairs.
The car needed an overhaul before the long journey across the country.

Synonyms

  • reconditioning
    3.9 0.04/m
    reconditioning focuses on restoring a machine to usable condition through specific repairs, while overhaul is a full, detailed examination and repair.
  • refurbishment
    3.7 0.42/m
    refurbishment cleans and updates equipment to look and work like new, while overhaul inspects and repairs systems more deeply.
  • renovation
    3.1 5.94/m
    renovation updates buildings or rooms to make them like new, while overhaul implies a full technical check and repair.
  • repair
    2.3 19/m
    repair fixes a broken part to make it work again, while overhaul examines the whole system and renews many parts.
  • makeover
    3.2 2.73/m
    makeover changes the look or style to create a fresh appearance, while overhaul focuses on deep inspection and technical repair.
  • reorganization
    3.7 3.84/m
    reorganization changes the structure or rules of a company, while overhaul centers on detailed repair and renewal of systems or machines.
