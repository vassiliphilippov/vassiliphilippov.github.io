delicense

verb
to remove license requirement
8.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈlaɪ.səns/ /; / /də.ˈlaɪ.səns/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈlaɪ.səns/ /; / /də.ˈlaɪ.səns/ (BrE)
to remove the legal requirement for a license so that an activity, profession, or business no longer needs to be licensed

Examples

To delicense an industry
The government decided to delicense certain industries to encourage more competition.
After the law passed, small businesses were delicensed and no longer needed special permits.
The proposal aims to delicense street performers, making it easier for them to work legally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: license - to grant official permission for an activity, profession, or business
