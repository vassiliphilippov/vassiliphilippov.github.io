demythologized

adjective
stripped of mythical elements
Pronunciation: / diːˈmɪθəˌlɑdʒaɪzd / (AmE), / diːˈmɪθəlɒdʒaɪzd /; / diːˈmɪθ.ə.lə.dʒaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a literary, historical, or religious account) having mythical elements removed in order to reveal underlying meaning or facts.

Examples

A demythologized story
After a demythologized reading, the ancient text revealed its historical core.
The demythologized biography focused on verifiable facts rather than legends.
The historian presented a demythologized version of the country's origins.
Much of modern science is based on demythologised understandings of natural phenomena.
The demythologised account sought to separate historical fact from religious lore.
The author presented a demythologised version of the ancient legend.
