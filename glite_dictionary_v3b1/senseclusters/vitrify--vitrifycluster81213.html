vitrify

verb
preserve biologically by rapid cooling (cryopreservation)
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪ.trɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /ˈvɪ.trɪ.faɪ/ (BrE)
(in cryobiology/medicine) to preserve biological material by rapidly cooling it so that liquids solidify into a glass-like, amorphous state without forming ice crystals.

Examples

To vitrify cells
The embryologist decided to vitrify the oocytes to improve survival after thawing.
They vitrified the tissue samples quickly, preventing damaging ice crystals from forming.
Vitrifying embryos requires precise cooling rates and suitable cryoprotectants in IVF clinics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cryopreserve - to preserve biological material at very low temperatures, often by freezing or vitrification
