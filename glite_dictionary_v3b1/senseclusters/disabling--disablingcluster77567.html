disabling

adjective
causing incapacity or inability
1.7
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪsˈeɪblɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to be unable to do certain things; making someone less able or incapable, often due to injury or illness.

Examples

Disabling injury
Her disabling back injury prevented her from returning to work.
Some diseases can have a disabling impact on patients' daily lives.
The accident left him with a disabling condition affecting his mobility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enabling - making someone able to do something or more capable
  • synonym: incapacitating - making someone unable to function normally
