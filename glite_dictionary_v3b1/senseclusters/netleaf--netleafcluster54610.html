netleaf

adjective
net-veined leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛtˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌliːf / (BrE)
having leaves with a net-like pattern of veins, typically used to describe certain plants or foliage

Examples

Netleaf pattern
The netleaf pattern on the plant's foliage made it stand out in the garden.
Botanists often classify species by whether they have netleaf or parallel-veined leaves.
She admired the netleaf structure that gave the tree's leaves a delicate appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parallel-veined - having veins that run parallel to each other in leaves
  • synonym: reticulate - having a net-like pattern, especially of veins in leaves
