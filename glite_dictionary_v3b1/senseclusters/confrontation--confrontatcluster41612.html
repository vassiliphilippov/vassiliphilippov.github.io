confrontation

noun
hostile opposition between groups
3.8 3.7
Pronunciation: /kən.frən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kɑːn.frən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒn.frən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.frən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a situation where people or groups are in conflict, opposition, or a direct challenge, often face-to-face or hostile

Examples

A confrontation with police
A confrontation with management over working conditions seemed inevitable.
Diplomats worked late into the night to avoid further confrontation between the nations.
The threat of a violent confrontation between rival gangs forced police to intervene.
She did not shy away from a confrontation when she felt something was wrong.
The football match ended in a tense confrontation between fans of both teams.
The heated confrontation between the two leaders lasted for nearly an hour.
She tries to avoid confrontation and prefers to resolve conflicts peacefully.
The new policy has sparked a confrontation between employees and management.
There was a heated confrontation between the two leaders during the debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accord - A situation of harmonious agreement or peace between groups
  • antonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties
  • synonym: conflict - A serious disagreement or argument; a clash between opposing parties
  • synonym: dispute - a disagreement, argument, or debate
  • antonym: harmony - agreement or concord; a state without conflict
  • antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations after conflict
