nearsightedly

adverb
without foresight
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪrˌsaɪtɪdli / (AmE), / ˈnɪəˌsaɪtɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a lack of foresight or concern for long-term consequences

Examples

Act nearsightedly on plans
The committee acted nearsightedly by ignoring the potential risks of the new policy.
Investors who focus nearsightedly on quick returns may miss out on long-term growth.
He nearsightedly dismissed the warnings, thinking only about immediate benefits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prudently - in a way that shows careful thought for the future
  • synonym: shortsightedly - in a way that lacks consideration for the future
