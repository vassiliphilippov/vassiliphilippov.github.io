ornamentality

noun
use of musical ornaments
9.5
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.nə.mən.ˈtæl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɔː.nə.mən.ˈtæl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality or use of decorative musical ornaments, such as trills or embellishments, in a melody or composition

Examples

Musical ornamentality
The ornamentality of the pianist's performance brought a lively character to the classical piece.
Some composers prefer minimal ornamentality to keep their music clear and direct.
Baroque music is often recognized for its high degree of ornamentality in melodic lines.

  • antonym: simplicity - the quality of being plain or uncomplicated
