yack

verb
talk incessantly or tiresomely
Pronunciation: / jæk / (AmE), / jæk / (BrE)
to talk incessantly and in a tiresome or trivial way, especially about unimportant matters

Examples

To yack about nothing
My coworkers yack all day about reality TV shows when I'm trying to focus.
Please stop yacking on the phone and help me with this task.
They yacked for hours about everything and nothing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chatter - to talk quickly and continuously about trivial matters
  • synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
