Pluteus

noun
a genus of fungi in Pluteaceae family
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpluːti.əs / (AmE), / ˈpluːtɪ.əs / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Pluteaceae, characterized by cap shapes resembling a roof, often found abundantly in early summer.

Examples

The Pluteus mushroom
Mycologists discovered abundant Pluteus during their early summer survey.
Several species in the genus Pluteus grow on decaying wood in forests.
The Pluteus mushroom is easily recognized by its distinctive roof-like cap.
