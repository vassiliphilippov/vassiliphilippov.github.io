unflooded

adjective
no longer flooded
3.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈflʌdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈflʌdɪd / (BrE)
not covered by floodwater (either never flooded or now dry after being flooded)

Examples

The unflooded basement
The basement was finally unflooded after hours of pumping out the water.
Once the fields were unflooded, the farmers could begin planting again.
The road remained closed until it was completely unflooded and safe for cars.
The emergency team focused on the unflooded areas to set up shelters.
After the storm, only a few unflooded roads remained open for traffic.
Farmers were relieved that their crops were in unflooded fields this season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dry - not wet or covered with water
  • antonym: flooded - covered or filled with water, especially due to flooding
