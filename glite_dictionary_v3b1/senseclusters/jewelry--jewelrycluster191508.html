jewelry

noun
decorative objects made from precious materials
1.3 23/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íuÀê…ôlri / (AmE), / Ààd íuÀê…ôlri / (BrE)
Small decorative objects worn for personal adornment, such as rings, necklaces, or bracelets, typically made from precious metals and sometimes set with gems.

Examples

Gold jewelry
He gave his wife a box of gold jewelry for their anniversary.
She wore elegant jewelry to the gala, including diamond earrings and a pearl necklace.
The store specializes in handcrafted jewelry made by local artists.
Antique jewellery can often be worth a great deal of money.
She wore her finest jewellery to the wedding ceremony.
The thief stole a box full of expensive jewellery during the night.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: costume jewelry - imitation jewelry made from inexpensive materials
  • synonym: jewel - a precious stone, typically set in jewelry
  • synonym: jewelry - Variant spelling used in American English
