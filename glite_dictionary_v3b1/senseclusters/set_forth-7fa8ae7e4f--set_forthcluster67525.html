set forth

verb
to begin a journey
Pronunciation: / sɛt fɔrθ / (AmE), / sɛt fɔːθ / (BrE)
to begin a journey; to depart for a particular place

Examples

To set forth on a trip
After saying goodbye to her family, she set forth on her new adventure.
The explorers will set forth at dawn on their expedition.
They set forth for Paris early in the morning to avoid the traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrive - to reach a destination after a journey
  • synonym: depart - to leave, especially to start a journey
