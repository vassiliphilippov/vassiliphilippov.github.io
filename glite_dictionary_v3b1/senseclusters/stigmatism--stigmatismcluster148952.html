stigmatism

noun
optical condition where rays converge at one point
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪɡ.məˌtɪz.əm /; / ˈstɪɡməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡ.mə.tɪz.əm /; / ˈstɪɡmətɪzəm / (BrE)
the condition in which an optical system (like a lens or the eye) focuses light correctly, resulting in a clear image or vision
