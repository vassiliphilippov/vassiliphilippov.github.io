middle fifty

the range containing the central 50 percent of a data set, equal to the difference between the upper and lower quartiles (the interquartile range)

Examples

The middle fifty range
If the middle fifty is small, most of the data values are close together.
Teachers often use the middle fifty to evaluate students' scores by focusing on the central portion of the distribution.
The middle fifty gives a better idea of the typical values than the full range, because it ignores outliers.
