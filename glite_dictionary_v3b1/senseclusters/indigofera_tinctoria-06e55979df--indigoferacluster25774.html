Indigofera tinctoria

noun
plant source of indigo dye
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˌgoʊˈfɛrə tɪŋkˈtɔriə / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˌgəʊˈfɪərə tɪŋkˈtɔːrɪə / (BrE)
A deciduous subshrub native to southeastern Asia, valued for its pinnate leaves, red or purple flowers, and as the primary source of natural indigo dye.

Examples

Indigofera tinctoria plant
Indigofera tinctoria has been cultivated for centuries to produce natural indigo dye for textiles.
The vibrant blue color extracted from Indigofera tinctoria was highly prized in ancient trade.
Traditional farmers still grow Indigofera tinctoria in some regions of India and Southeast Asia.
