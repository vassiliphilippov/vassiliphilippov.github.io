waterflood

noun
oil recovery method
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌflʌd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌflʌd / (BrE)
a method of injecting water into an oil reservoir to increase the amount of oil that can be recovered

Examples

Oil field waterflood
The engineers implemented a waterflood to improve oil production in the mature field.
A successful waterflood can significantly extend the life of an oil reservoir.
The company invested in new technology to monitor the progress of the waterflood operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: primary recovery - the initial extraction of oil using natural pressure
  • synonym: secondary recovery - methods used to increase oil extraction after primary recovery
