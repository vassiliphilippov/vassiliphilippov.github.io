erythema multiforme

noun
red rash due to hypersensitivity
Pronunciation: / ˌɛr.ɪˈθiː.mə mʌl.tɪˈfɔːr.mi / (AmE), / ˌɛr.ɪˈθiː.mə mʌl.tɪˈfɔː.miː / (BrE)
A skin condition characterized by a red rash, often caused by hypersensitivity to a drug, infection, or other allergen.

Examples

erythema multiforme rash
Certain medications are known to trigger erythema multiforme in sensitive individuals.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with erythema multiforme after noticing the characteristic target-shaped lesions.
Treatment of erythema multiforme often involves identifying and removing the underlying cause.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rash - a temporary eruption or discoloration of the skin
  • synonym: urticaria - a rash of round, red welts on the skin that itch intensely
