scalenus

noun
neck muscle involved in movement and breathing
8.0 9.0
Pronunciation: /skə.ˈliː.nəs/ /; / /ˈskeɪ.lə.nəs/ (AmE), /skə.ˈliː.nəs/ /; / /ˈskeɪ.lə.nəs/ (BrE)
any of several muscles on each side of the neck, extending from the cervical vertebrae to the first or second rib, which are involved in neck movement and breathing

Examples

The scalenus muscle
Tension in the scalenus muscles can lead to neck pain and restricted movement.
The scalenus group assists with elevating the first and second ribs during deep inhalation.
The physical therapist explained exercises to strengthen the scalenus for better postural support.
