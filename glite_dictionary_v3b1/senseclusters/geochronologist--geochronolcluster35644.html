geochronologist

noun
scientist dating rocks
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːoʊkrəˈnɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊkrəˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who determines the age and timing of geological events and rock formations

Examples

A geochronologist studies rocks
The geochronologist used radiometric dating to estimate the age of the volcanic rocks.
A team led by a geochronologist discovered that the fossils were millions of years old.
To understand Earth's history, a geochronologist analyzes minerals from deep within the crust.
