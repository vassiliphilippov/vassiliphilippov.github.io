horse around

verb
play or act in a boisterous way
Pronunciation: / hɔrs əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / hɔːs əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to play or behave in a silly, playful, or noisy way, often with physical activity and sometimes when it is not appropriate

Examples

To horse around loudly
During the lecture, some students horsed around instead of paying attention.
Stop horsing around and help me set the table, please.
The kids were horsing around in the backyard, laughing and shouting.
During class, Jake kept horsing around instead of paying attention to the teacher.
She warned her friends to stop horsing around before someone got hurt.
The kids were horsing around in the backyard, laughing and chasing each other.
He got in trouble for horsing around in the hallway at school.
Please stop horsing around and help me set the table.
The children started to horse around as soon as the teacher left the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fool around - to behave in a silly way; to play instead of working
  • synonym: mess around - to spend time doing things that are not important; to behave playfully
