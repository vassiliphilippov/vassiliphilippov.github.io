sulfur hexafluoride

noun
ultrasound contrast agent (microbubbles)
4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌl.fər heks.ə.ˈfluːr.aɪd/ /; / /ˈsʌl.fər heks.ə.ˈflʊr.aɪd/ (AmE), /ˈsʌl.fə heks.ə.ˈflʊə.raɪd/ /; / /ˈsʌl.fə heks.ə.ˈflɔː.raɪd/ (BrE)
(in medicine) a gas used in microbubble form as an ultrasound contrast agent to improve the visibility of organs or lesions during diagnostic imaging.

Examples

Sulfur hexafluoride microbubbles
Doctors use sulphur hexafluoride microbubbles to enhance ultrasound images of patients' livers during diagnostic scans.
The radiologist observed the sulphur hexafluoride contrast noticeably improving the tumor's visibility on ultrasound.
Clinical trials found that sulphur hexafluoride microbubbles are safe and effective as an ultrasound contrast agent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrast agent - a substance used in medical imaging to improve the contrast of structures or fluids within the body
  • synonym: microbubble - a tiny gas-filled bubble used as an intravascular ultrasound contrast agent to enhance imaging
