wilt

noun
plant disease causing drooping
Pronunciation: / wɪlt / (AmE), / wɪlt / (BrE)
A plant disease characterized by drooping and shriveling, usually caused by parasites attacking the roots.

Examples

Tomato wilt disease
Certain fungi in the soil can cause wilt in susceptible plants.
Farmers are searching for solutions to protect their fields from bacterial wilt.
The tomato crop was devastated by a severe case of wilt last summer.
