CT scan

verb
to use computed tomography
3.7
Pronunciation: / si ti skæn / (AmE), / siː tiː skæn / (BrE)
to examine a person or object using computed tomography to produce detailed internal images

Examples

To CT scan a patient
The doctor decided to CT scan the patient to check for internal injuries after the accident.
They will CT scan your head to look for any signs of bleeding or swelling.
If the symptoms persist, we may need to CT scan your abdomen for a clearer diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: MRI scan - to examine using magnetic resonance imaging
  • degree lower: X-ray - to examine using X-ray imaging
  • synonym: scan - to examine something with a machine that produces images
