wester

noun
wind blowing from west to east
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstər / (AmE), / ˈwɛstə / (BrE)
A wind that blows from the west toward the east.

Examples

A strong wester
After days of calm, a cold wester swept through the valley.
Fishermen often watch for a wester before sailing out to sea.
The strong wester carried the clouds quickly across the sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easter - a wind that blows from the east toward the west
  • synonym: westerly - a wind blowing from the west
