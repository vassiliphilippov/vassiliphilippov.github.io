subsoiling

noun
deep soil loosening
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌsɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a deep tillage process that loosens soil below the surface without turning it over, to improve soil structure and drainage

Examples

Subsoiling the soil
Many farmers use subsoiling to break up compacted layers and improve water movement in their fields.
The agricultural expert recommended subsoiling before planting to encourage deeper root growth.
After several years of heavy machinery use, subsoiling was necessary to restore the soil's health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plowing - turning over the upper layer of soil to prepare for planting
  • synonym: tillage - the preparation of land for growing crops by mechanical agitation
