enchainment

noun
linked climbs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈtʃeɪnmənt / (AmE), / ɛnˈtʃeɪnmənt / (BrE)
the act of linking two or more climbs, routes, or summits into a single continuous effort in mountaineering

Examples

Alpine enchainment
Their daring enchainment of three Alpine ridges in one day amazed the climbing community.
Completing an enchainment requires careful planning and excellent physical condition.
The team celebrated after their successful enchainment of several challenging peaks.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ascent - the act of climbing or going up a mountain
  • synonym: traverse - a climbing route that crosses a mountain or ridge from one side to another
