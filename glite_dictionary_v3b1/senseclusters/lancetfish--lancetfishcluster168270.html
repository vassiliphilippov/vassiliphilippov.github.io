lancetfish

noun
large oceanic fish with sail-like dorsal fin
Pronunciation: / ˈlæn.sɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlan.sɪt.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a large, elongated oceanic fish with sharp teeth and a long, sail-like dorsal fin, lacking scales

Examples

A large lancetfish
A lancetfish can be identified by its elongated body and numerous sharp teeth.
Scientists were surprised to find a lancetfish washed ashore, as it usually inhabits the open sea.
The lancetfish is known for its long, sail-like dorsal fin and predatory habits in deep ocean waters.
