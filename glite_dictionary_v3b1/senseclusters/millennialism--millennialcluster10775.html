millennialism

noun
belief in radical change
9.5
Pronunciation: /mɪ.ˈle.nɪ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /mɪ.ˈle.njə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /mɪ.ˈle.nɪ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /mɪ.ˈle.njə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief or ideology that a sudden, transformative change will soon create a new, ideal era in society or politics

Examples

Political millennialism
The leader's speeches were filled with millennialism, promising a new era of justice and equality.
Some political movements are driven by millennialism and the hope for immediate, sweeping reforms.
Critics argued that the group's millennialism ignored the complexities of real social change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apocalypticism - the belief in an impending, dramatic end of the current world order
  • antonym: pessimism - the belief that things will turn out badly or cannot improve
  • synonym: utopianism - the belief in or pursuit of a perfect society
