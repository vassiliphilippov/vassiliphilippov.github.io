crisis intervention

noun
psychotherapy for acute critical situations
3.6 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkraɪ.sɪs ɪn.tər.ˈven.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkraɪ.səs ɪn.tər.ˈven.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈkraɪ.sɪs ɪn.tə.ˈven.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkraɪ.səs ɪn.tə.ˈven.ʃən/ (BrE)
A form of psychotherapy aimed at helping individuals deal with acute, critical situations by restoring their previous level of functioning.

Examples

Crisis intervention therapy
After her suicide attempt, she received immediate crisis intervention at the hospital.
Many mental health organizations offer crisis intervention services to those facing sudden trauma.
Proper crisis intervention can prevent long-term psychological harm following acute distress.
