eelpout

noun
elongate freshwater fish with barbels
Pronunciation: / ˈilˌpaʊt / (AmE), / ˈiːlpaʊt / (BrE)
A long, slender freshwater fish found in northern Europe, Asia, and North America, with barbels around its mouth.

Examples

Freshwater eelpout catch
During winter, catching an eelpout can be a rare but exciting event for anglers.
Fishermen often recognize the eelpout by its long body and chin barbels.
The eelpout lurks in cold freshwater lakes beneath the thick ice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burbot - a species of freshwater cod also called an eelpout
