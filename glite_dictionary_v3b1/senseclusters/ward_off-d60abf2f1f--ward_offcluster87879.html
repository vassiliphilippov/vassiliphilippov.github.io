ward off

verb
prevent or repel something unpleasant
5.6
Pronunciation: / wɔrd ɔf /; / wɔrd ɑf / (AmE), / wɔːd ɒf / (BrE)
to prevent something unpleasant, harmful, or dangerous from affecting or approaching you; to keep something away

Examples

To ward off illness
Doctors recommend getting vaccinated to ward off the flu.
She wore a charm to ward off evil spirits.
The villagers lit fires to ward off wild animals.
She wore a scarf to ward off the cold wind.
Some believe garlic can ward off evil spirits.
The company took urgent measures to ward off bankruptcy.
Extra security cameras were installed to ward off potential thieves.
She wore a bracelet of garlic to ward off evil spirits.
Vitamin C can help ward off colds during the winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avert - to prevent something bad or dangerous from happening
  • synonym: deflect - to cause something to change direction; to prevent something from being directed at you
  • synonym: fend off - to defend oneself against (an attack or attacker)
  • antonym: invite - to make something undesirable likely to happen
