hypertrophic rosacea

noun
a severe form of rosacea with nose enlargement
Pronunciation: / haɪpərˈtroʊfɪk roʊˈzeɪʃə / (AmE), / hʌɪpəˈtrɒfɪk rəʊˈzeɪʃə / (BrE)
A severe form of rosacea characterized by the thickening and enlargement of the skin on the nose, with redness, visible blood vessels, and enlarged pores, often linked to excessive alcohol consumption.

Examples

Hypertrophic rosacea on nose
People with hypertrophic rosacea often experience significant self-consciousness about their appearance.
The dermatologist diagnosed him with hypertrophic rosacea due to the pronounced swelling and redness of his nose.
Treatment for hypertrophic rosacea may include medication, laser therapy, or in severe cases, surgery.
