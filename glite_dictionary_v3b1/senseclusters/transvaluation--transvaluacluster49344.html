transvaluation

noun
re-evaluation of values
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtranzvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌtrænzvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of re-evaluating and replacing the values or principles of something, especially in a cultural, moral, or social context

Examples

Moral transvaluation
Nietzsche's philosophy called for a transvaluation of all traditional morals and beliefs.
The movement led to a transvaluation of cultural norms in the early twentieth century.
A transvaluation of priorities is often necessary during times of major social change.
The philosopher argued for a transvaluation of traditional moral standards in his writings.
Social movements often seek transvaluation to challenge and change established norms.
A complete transvaluation of values can lead to significant cultural transformation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservatism - the tendency to preserve existing conditions or values
  • synonym: reappraisal - the act of reassessing or evaluating again
  • synonym: revaluation - the process of assessing something again, especially in terms of value or worth
