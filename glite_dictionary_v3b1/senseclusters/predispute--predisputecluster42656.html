predispute

adjective
before a dispute
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːdɪˈspjuːt / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪˈspjuːt / (BrE)
occurring or existing before a dispute arises, especially in legal or contractual contexts

Examples

Predispute arbitration clause
The contract included a predispute arbitration clause to handle any future disagreements.
Lawyers often recommend adding predispute procedures to commercial agreements.
A predispute settlement offer can sometimes prevent conflicts from escalating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postdispute - occurring after a dispute has arisen
