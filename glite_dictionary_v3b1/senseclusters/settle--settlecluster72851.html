settle

verb
take revenge for a wrong
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl / (BrE)
to take revenge for a wrong or injury; to retaliate against someone who has caused harm

Examples

To settle a score
After years of silence, he finally settled with the man who had cheated him.
She vowed she would settle the score one day.
They waited patiently, planning to settle with those who betrayed them.
