(Physics) The transfer of energy from one physical body or system to another, measured as the product of the force applied and the distance moved in the direction of the force.

Examples

Work done by force
In physics, work is done when a force moves an object over a distance.
Lifting a box from the floor to the table requires work to be done against gravity.
The equation for work is W = F √ó d, where F is force and d is distance.
