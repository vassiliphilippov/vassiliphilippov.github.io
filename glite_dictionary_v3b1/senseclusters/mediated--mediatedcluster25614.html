mediated

adjective
brought about through an intermediary
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈmiːdɪeɪtɪd / (BrE)
acted or brought about with the help of an intervening agency or person; not direct.

Examples

A mediated agreement
Her promotion was a result of a mediated decision involving both departments.
Many international disputes are settled through mediated processes to avoid direct confrontation.
The two companies reached a mediated agreement after weeks of negotiation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct - without intervention or intermediaries
