ethnocentricity

noun
belief in cultural superiority
9.8
Pronunciation: /eθ.noʊ.sen.ˈtrɪs.ə.tɪ/ /; / /əθ.noʊ.sen.ˈtrɪs.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /eθ.əʊ.sen.ˈtrɪs.ə.tɪ/ /; / /əθ.əʊ.sen.ˈtrɪs.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the belief that one's own culture or group is superior and using it as the standard to judge others

Examples

Strong ethnocentricity
His ethnocentricity made it difficult for him to appreciate foreign customs and traditions.
Teachers are encouraged to challenge ethnocentricity in the classroom to promote understanding.
Ethnocentricity can lead to misunderstandings and conflicts between different cultural groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cultural relativism - the idea that all cultures should be understood in their own context
  • synonym: ethnocentrism - the attitude that one's own group is superior to others
