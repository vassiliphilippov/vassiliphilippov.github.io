sophism

noun
deceptively clever but false argument
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑː.fɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˈsɒ.fɪ.zəm / (BrE)
a clever but fallacious argument, especially one used deliberately to deceive

Examples

A sophism argument
He recognized the sophism in her reasoning and refused to be deceived.
The politician's argument was dismissed as a mere sophism meant to mislead the public.
Throughout history, philosophers have warned against the dangers of sophism in debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallacy - a mistaken belief or faulty argument
  • antonym: truth - that which is true or in accordance with fact
