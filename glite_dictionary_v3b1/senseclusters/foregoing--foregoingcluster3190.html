Examples

Synonyms

  • aforementioned
    6.1 4.83/m
    aforementioned is a formal adjective used before a noun to refer back in text, while foregoing refers to what was just mentioned.
  • above-mentioned
    4.1 1.44/m
    above-mentioned points to something stated earlier in the same document, while foregoing is a general term for what came before.
  • foresaid
    9.2 0.01/m
    foresaid appears mainly in legal or formal documents to name earlier items, while foregoing is used more broadly in texts.
  • aforesaid
    8.6 0.23/m
    aforesaid is a legal word often used to repeat earlier named people or things, while foregoing is less strictly legal.
  • hereinbefore
    9.9 0.02/m
    hereinbefore refers only to earlier parts of the same document or section, while foregoing can mean anything just mentioned.
  • above
    1.0 92/m
    above usually acts as a short reference to earlier text or lines, while foregoing is a fuller adjective meaning previously mentioned.
