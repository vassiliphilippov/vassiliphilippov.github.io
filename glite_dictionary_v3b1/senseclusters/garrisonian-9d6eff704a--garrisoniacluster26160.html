Garrisonian

adjective
of Garrison's abolitionism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɡærɪˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌɡærɪˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas, beliefs, or followers of William Lloyd Garrison, especially in the context of abolitionism

Examples

Garrisonian abolitionist ideas
The Garrisonian approach to abolition rejected any compromise with slaveholders.
Many activists adopted Garrisonian principles in their fight for social justice.
Her Garrisonian views made her a prominent figure in the anti-slavery movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abolitionist - supporting the ending of slavery
