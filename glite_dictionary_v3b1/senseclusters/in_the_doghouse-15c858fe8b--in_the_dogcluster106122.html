in the doghouse

adjective
out of favor; in trouble
9.3
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə ˈdɔɡˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈdɒɡˌhaʊs / (BrE)
out of favor or in trouble with someone, especially because of one's actions

Examples

In the doghouse with someone

If you don't apologize, you'll be in the doghouse with your boss.
She's always in the doghouse after missing deadlines.
