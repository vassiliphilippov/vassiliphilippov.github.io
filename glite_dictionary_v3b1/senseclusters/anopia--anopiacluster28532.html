anopia

noun
loss of vision due to eye defect or absence
Pronunciation: / əˈnoʊ.pi.ə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊ.pi.ə / (BrE)
Complete or partial loss of vision, especially due to a structural defect or absence of the eye.

Examples

Complete anopia
Certain congenital disorders can result in anopia because the eyes do not develop properly.
Researchers are investigating new treatments for conditions leading to anopia in children.
The patient was diagnosed with anopia following a traumatic injury to his optic nerve.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blindness - total or partial lack of vision
