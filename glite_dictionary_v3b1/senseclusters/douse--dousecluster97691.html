douse

verb
to wet or soak thoroughly
4.4 0.99/m
Pronunciation: / daÊŠs / (AmE), / daÊŠs / (BrE)
to pour liquid over someone or something, making them completely wet; to soak or drench

Examples

To douse clothes
She accidentally doused her clothes with juice while pouring a drink.
The firefighter doused the burning logs with water until the flames were out.
They doused the campfire to make sure it was completely out before leaving.
He quickly doused the flames with a bucket of sand before they could spread.
She doused his shirt in water to help cool him down after the race.
The children laughed as they doused each other with water during the hot summer day.

Synonyms

  • drench
    3.0 2.96/m
    drench soaks something completely by covering it with a lot of liquid, while douse often means pouring water over quickly to wet or extinguish.
  • soak
    1.0 7.38/m
    soak means to make something very wet by immersing it or letting it absorb liquid, while douse means to pour liquid over quickly.
  • souse
    8.8 0.01/m
    souse means to immerse or soak something thoroughly, often in informal or older speech, while douse means to pour liquid over to wet quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - to remove moisture or become free from moisture
  • synonym: saturate - to soak thoroughly; cause to become thoroughly soaked with liquid
  • synonym: soak - to make something very wet by immersing it in liquid
