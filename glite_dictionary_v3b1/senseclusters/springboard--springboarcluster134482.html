springboard

noun
starting point for further development
Pronunciation: / ˈsprɪŋˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsprɪŋˌbɔːd / (BrE)
something (such as an idea, event, or experience) that helps start or advance an activity, development, or career

Examples

A springboard for success
He used his previous job experience as a springboard to start his own business.
Her research acted as a springboard for many subsequent studies in the field.
The festival served as a springboard for young artists to gain wider recognition.
Her first book became a springboard for her career as a novelist.
The training program acted as a springboard to new job opportunities.
Winning the competition provided a valuable springboard for his career.

Related Terms

  • synonym: launchpad - a platform or starting point for launching something, used similarly in figurative sense
