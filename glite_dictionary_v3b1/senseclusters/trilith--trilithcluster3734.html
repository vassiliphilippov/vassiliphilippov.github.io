trilith

noun
stone structure
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪlɪθ / (BrE)
a structure made of two large upright stones supporting a third stone laid horizontally across the top

Examples

A trilith monument
The ancient monument featured a prominent trilith at its entrance.
Archaeologists studied the construction techniques used to build each trilith at the site.
Visitors were amazed by the size and balance of the trilith structures at Stonehenge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dolmen - a type of single-chamber megalithic tomb
  • synonym: megalith - a large stone used to construct a structure or monument
