crown prince

noun
figurative designated successor
3.9 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / kraÊŠn ËˆprÉªns / (AmE), / kraÊŠn ËˆprÉªns / (BrE)
used figuratively to refer to a person widely regarded as the likely successor or the favored candidate to take a leading position in an organization, field, or movement.

Examples

Crown prince of business
Business columnists called him the crown prince of tech after his startup's rapid success.
After the CEO announced retirement, many named the young CFO the company's crown prince.
Political commentators labeled the mayor the crown prince of the opposition after his popular speech.

Synonyms

  • dauphin
    9.9 0.01/m
    dauphin is a formal or historical word for an expected heir in an organization, while crown prince is the more common modern term.
