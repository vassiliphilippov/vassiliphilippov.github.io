Ozark chinkapin

noun
North American shrubby tree with large leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.zɑrk ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.zɑːk ˈtʃɪŋ.kə.pɪn / (BrE)
a shrubby tree native to the southern midwestern United States, closely related to the Allegheny chinkapin but with larger leaves

Examples

The Ozark chinkapin tree
Many wildlife species feed on the nuts of the Ozark chinkapin in the fall.
Restoration projects are underway to reintroduce the Ozark chinkapin to its native range.
The Ozark chinkapin has become increasingly rare due to disease and habitat loss.
