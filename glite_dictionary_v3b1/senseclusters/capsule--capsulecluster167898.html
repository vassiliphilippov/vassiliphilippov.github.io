capsule

noun
small container, often cylindrical
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.səl /; / ˈkæp.sul / (AmE), / ˈkæp.sjuːl / (BrE)
a small container, often cylindrical or oval, used to hold medicine, scientific samples, or a tiny medical device

Examples

A medicine capsule
She opened the capsule to take her daily vitamins.
The scientists stored each sample in a sterile capsule for analysis.
The time capsule was buried in the schoolyard to be opened in fifty years.
Make sure you do not open the capsule, as the medicine inside could irritate your mouth.
Some people find it easier to swallow a capsule than a tablet.
The doctor prescribed a capsule to be taken twice a day after meals.
Doctors recommended swallowing a small capsule that would record images as it passed through.
She had a capsule endoscopy scheduled to check for bleeding in her small intestine.
After reviewing the images from the capsule, the gastroenterologist identified a small ulcer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capsule endoscopy - a medical procedure that uses a swallowable camera capsule to image the digestive tract
  • synonym: container - an object used for holding or transporting something
  • synonym: pill - a small round mass of solid medicine to be swallowed
  • synonym: pill camera - informal name for a swallowable camera used to image the intestines
  • synonym: tablet - a pill made by compressing powder into a solid form
