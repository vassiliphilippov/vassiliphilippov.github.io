numbers game

noun
decided by statistics or metrics
4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈnʌm.bərz ɡeɪm/ (AmE), /ˈnʌm.bəz ɡeɪm/ (BrE)
a situation in which success or outcomes depend mainly on numerical data, statistics, probabilities, or metrics rather than individual judgment or qualities

Examples

Business numbers game
Many investors treat markets as a numbers game, prioritizing data over intuition.
In modern hiring, the process has become a numbers game of applications, keywords, and response rates.
Campaign strategists know elections are ultimately a numbers game decided by turnout and demographics.
