matriarchal

adjective
relating to a society led by women
2.4 5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmeɪ.triˈɑr.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmeɪ.triˈɑː.kəl / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of a social system in which women, particularly mothers, have primary power and authority.

Examples

A matriarchal society
Anthropologists studied the village's matriarchal system, noting the significant authority held by women.
Some animal groups, such as elephant herds, have a matriarchal structure led by older females.
The tribe practiced a matriarchal tradition in which inheritance was passed down through the mother's line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: matriarchy - a social system in which women hold primary power
  • antonym: patriarchal - relating to or characteristic of a social system led by men
