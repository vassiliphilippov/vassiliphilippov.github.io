reciprocal

noun
hybridization with reversed parental sexes
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl / (BrE)
A hybridization experiment involving two crosses in which the sexes associated with each genotype are reversed, to observe the influence of parental sex on the resulting offspring.

Examples

A reciprocal cross
By performing a reciprocal, researchers can detect maternal effects in genetic transmission.
The reciprocal revealed that the trait depended on which parent contributed which allele.
The scientist set up a reciprocal to study the inheritance patterns in the hybrid plants.
