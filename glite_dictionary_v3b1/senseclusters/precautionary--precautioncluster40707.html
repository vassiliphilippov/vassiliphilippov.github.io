precautionary

adjective
intended to prevent harm or danger
5.0 1.26/m
Pronunciation: / pr…™Ààk…î É…ôÀån…õri / (AmE), / pr…™Ààk…îÀê É…ôn…ôri / (BrE)
done or used to prevent possible harm or trouble before it happens; intended as a safeguard.

Examples

A precautionary measure
After hearing the weather report, we made a precautionary evacuation of the coastal area.
Doctors gave her precautionary antibiotics after the minor surgery.
The company took precautionary measures to avoid any data breaches.

Synonyms

  • preventative
    4.1 1.92/m
    preventative aims to stop problems before they begin, while precautionary refers to measures taken beforehand to avoid possible harm.
  • protective
    2.4 12/m
    protective is meant to shield people or things from harm, while precautionary describes steps taken before danger appears.
  • prophylactic
    7.5 0.43/m
    prophylactic is mainly used for medical prevention against disease, while precautionary covers general safeguards taken before harm.
