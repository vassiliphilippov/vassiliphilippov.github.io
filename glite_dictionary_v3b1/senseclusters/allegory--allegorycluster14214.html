allegory

noun
symbol representing an abstract idea
3.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.ə.gɔːr.iː/ (AmE), /ˈæl.ɪ.gɔː.riː/ (BrE)
A symbol, often visible or concrete, that represents an abstract idea, principle, or moral, typically used in art or literature.

Examples

A painting's allegory
Artists often use colors and objects as an allegory to convey deeper meanings in their work.
In the mural, the rising sun is an allegory for hope and new beginnings.
The statue of Justice, blindfolded and holding scales, is an allegory of impartiality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: symbol - something that stands for or suggests something else by reason of relationship, association, or convention
