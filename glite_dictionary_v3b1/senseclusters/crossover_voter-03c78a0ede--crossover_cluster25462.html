crossover voter

noun
person who votes in another party's primary
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔːsˌoʊvər ˈvoʊtər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə ˈvəʊtə / (BrE)
a person registered as a member of one political party who votes in the primary election of another party

Examples

Crossover voter in primary
Because he wanted to influence the opposition's candidate selection, he became a crossover voter in the primary.
Many analysts speculate that crossover voters could decide the outcome in closely contested districts this year.
The election saw a surge in crossover voters hoping to have a say in the rival party's nomination.
