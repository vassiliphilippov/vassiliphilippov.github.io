involution

noun
reduction in organ size (medical)
7.2 8.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.və.ˈluː.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.və.ˈluː.ʃən/ (BrE)
A reduction in size of an organ or tissue, typically returning to a normal or former state, especially the return of the uterus to normal size after childbirth.

Examples

Uterine involution
After childbirth, the uterus undergoes involution to return to its pre-pregnancy size.
Doctors monitored the involution of her uterus during the postpartum period.
The process of involution is essential for restoring normal organ function after pregnancy.
