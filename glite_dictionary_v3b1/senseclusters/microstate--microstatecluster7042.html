microstate

noun
microscopic configuration
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌsteɪt / (BrE)
a specific microscopic configuration of a physical system, representing one possible arrangement of its components and energies

Examples

A microstate of particles
Each microstate corresponds to a unique way the particles in the system can be arranged.
The entropy of a system increases with the number of possible microstates.
Physicists use the concept of a microstate to explain the behavior of gases at the molecular level.

Related Terms

  • synonym: configuration - the arrangement of parts or elements in a system
  • antonym: macrostate - the overall state of a system described by macroscopic variables
