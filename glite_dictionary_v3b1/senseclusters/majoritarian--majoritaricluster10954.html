majoritarian

adjective
majority rule principle
Pronunciation: / məˌdʒɔːrɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / məˌdʒɒrɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
relating to or based on the principle that the majority's decision should prevail

Examples

Majoritarian system
The new law was passed under a majoritarian system, despite strong opposition from minority groups.
Critics argue that majoritarian politics can sometimes ignore the needs of smaller communities.
A majoritarian approach means the majority's wishes determine the outcome of important decisions.
The country adopted a majoritarian system where the majority's decision always prevails.
Critics argue that majoritarian policies can sometimes ignore the needs of minorities.
A majoritarian approach was used to resolve the dispute within the committee.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consensual - based on general agreement rather than majority rule
  • synonym: democratic - relating to the idea that all people have equal say in decisions
  • antonym: minoritarian - relating to or favoring a minority within a group
