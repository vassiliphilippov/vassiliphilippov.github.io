throttle

verb
reduce air/fuel supply to an engine
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɑːt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒtəl / (BrE)
to reduce or control the flow of air or fuel to an engine, thereby decreasing its power or speed

Examples

To throttle the engine
She carefully throttled back the motor as they approached the dock.
The mechanic had to throttle the engine to prevent it from overheating.
You can throttle the airflow by adjusting this lever on the carburetor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choke - to restrict the air supply to an engine
