precontrol

verb
to regulate in advance
7.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpriÀêk…ônÀàtro äl / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀêk…ônÀàtr…ô äl / (BrE)
to monitor or regulate something in advance, before the main operation or process begins

Examples

To precontrol a process
Technicians precontrol the system to ensure a smooth startup sequence.
It is important to precontrol variables before running the main experiment.
The team decided to precontrol the temperature to prevent sudden fluctuations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: control - to direct or regulate something
  • synonym: preempt - to act before something happens to prevent it
