Theridiidae

noun
family of comb-footed spiders
9.9
Pronunciation: / θəˌrɪdiˈaɪdi / (AmE), / θɛˌrɪdɪˈɪːdiː / (BrE)
A family of spiders commonly known as comb-footed spiders, characterized by a comb of serrated bristles on their fourth pair of legs.

Examples

The Theridiidae family
Many Theridiidae construct irregular, tangled webs to catch their prey.
Scientists discovered a new genus within the Theridiidae in South America.
The Theridiidae family includes well-known species such as the black widow spider.
