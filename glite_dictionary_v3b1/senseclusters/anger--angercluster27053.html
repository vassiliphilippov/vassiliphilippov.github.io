anger

verb
become angry (verb)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡə / (BrE)
to become angry or to make someone feel angry

Examples

To anger someone
He angers easily when things don't go his way.
Sometimes, unfair criticism can anger even the most patient person.
The new regulations are likely to anger many business owners.
Her disrespectful remark angered everyone at the meeting.
It angers me when people lie so blatantly.
The unfair decision angered the entire community.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: annoy - to irritate or mildly anger someone
  • antonym: calm - to become less angry; to become tranquil
  • synonym: enrage - to make very angry
