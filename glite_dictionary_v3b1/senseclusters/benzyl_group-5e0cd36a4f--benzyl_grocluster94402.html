benzyl group

noun
chemical group derived from toluene
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛn.zɪl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.zɪl ɡruːp / (BrE)
A univalent group derived from toluene by removal of a hydrogen atom from the methyl group, represented as C6H5CH2−.

Examples

A benzyl group molecule
Many pharmaceuticals contain a benzyl group as part of their molecular structure.
The benzyl group is commonly used in organic synthesis as a protecting group for alcohols.
When a hydrogen atom is removed from the methyl group of toluene, a benzyl group is formed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phenyl group - the univalent group C6H5− derived from benzene
