yellow oleander

noun
a poisonous tropical shrub with yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ oʊˈliːˌændər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ əʊˈliːændə / (BrE)
a tropical American shrub or small tree with glossy dark green leaves and fragrant yellow to orange or peach-colored flowers, all parts of which are highly poisonous

Examples

The yellow oleander plant
After learning about its poisonous nature, we removed the yellow oleander from our backyard.
Children should never touch the yellow oleander because every part of it is toxic.
The garden was lined with flowering yellow oleander that added a splash of color.
