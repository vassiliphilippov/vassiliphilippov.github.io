confusion

noun
state of disorder or chaos
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuːʒən /; / kənˈfjuʒən / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a state of disorder, uncertainty, or not being able to think or act clearly

Examples

Total confusion
The army retreated in confusion after the sudden attack.
The meeting ended in confusion, with everyone talking at once.
There was confusion in the classroom when the fire alarm unexpectedly rang.
After waking from the nap, a brief confusion lingered before she recognized her surroundings.
His sudden question caused confusion among the team members.
She felt a wave of confusion when she misread the instructions.
His speech added to the confusion in the room.
In the confusion after the accident, no one knew who was hurt.
She stared at the complicated instructions in total confusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear
  • synonym: disarray - a lack of order or organization
