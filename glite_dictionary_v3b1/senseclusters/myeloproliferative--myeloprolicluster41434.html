myeloproliferative

adjective
excess bone marrow cells
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪəloʊprəˈlɪfərətɪv / (AmE), / ˌmaɪələʊprəˈlɪfərətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or involving the excessive production of cells in the bone marrow

Examples

Myeloproliferative disorder
The doctor explained that a myeloproliferative disorder can lead to an overproduction of blood cells.
Researchers are studying new treatments for myeloproliferative diseases affecting the bone marrow.
A myeloproliferative condition may cause symptoms such as fatigue and frequent infections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leukemic - relating to leukemia or an abnormal increase in white blood cells
  • antonym: myelodysplastic - relating to abnormal development of bone marrow cells
