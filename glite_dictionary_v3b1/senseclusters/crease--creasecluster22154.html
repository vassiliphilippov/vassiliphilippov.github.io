crease

verb
make a line or fold in a surface
2.3 2.43/m
Pronunciation: / kris /; / k…πis / (AmE), / kriÀês /; / k…πiÀês / (BrE)
to make or become marked with a line, fold, or wrinkle by folding, pressing, or crushing

Examples

To crease paper
Be careful not to crease your shirt while packing for the trip.
If you crease the paper down the middle, it will fit better in the envelope.
She creased the napkin neatly to create a decorative fold.
He creased his forehead in concentration.
She accidentally creased her dress while packing the suitcase.
Try not to crease the paper when you fold it.
Be careful with that shirt or it will crease easily in your suitcase.
If you sit on your dress, it might crease and look untidy.
The paper began to crease after being folded so many times.

Synonyms

  • wrinkle
    2.1 4.23/m
    wrinkle forms small folds in skin or cloth over time, while crease is a single sharp line from folding or pressing.
  • crinkle
    3.7 1.46/m
    crinkle makes many small, crisp folds or lines on paper or cloth, while crease refers to a single pressed fold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fold - to bend something, especially paper or fabric, so that one part lies on another part
  • synonym: wrinkle - to make a small line or ridge on a surface, usually of skin or fabric
