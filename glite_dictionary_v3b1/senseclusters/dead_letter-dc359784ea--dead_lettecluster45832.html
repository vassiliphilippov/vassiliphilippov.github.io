dead letter

noun
rule or law no longer effective
2.0 6.1
Pronunciation: / dɛd ˈlɛtər / (AmE), / dɛd ˈlɛtə / (BrE)
a law, rule, or practice that is no longer in effect, has lost its relevance, or is no longer enforced

Examples

An old dead letter law
Although the law still exists on the books, it is considered a dead letter by most legal experts.
Many of the treaty's provisions quickly became dead letters due to changing political circumstances.
The regulation is a dead letter now and has not been enforced in decades.
