Synonyms

  • tendency
    2.8 15/m
    tendency is a general likelihood to act or develop in a way, while inclination is a personal preference or wish.
  • propensity
    7.3 2/m
    propensity is a natural, often strong tendency to behave a certain way, while inclination is a softer wish or preference.
  • penchant
    8.2 2/m
    penchant is a strong habitual liking for something, while inclination is a broader feeling that makes you want to choose it.
  • predilection
    8.4 0.6/m
    predilection is a strong liking or preference for something, often used formally, while inclination is a general tendency.
  • proclivity
    8.5 0.9/m
    proclivity is a natural leaning often toward bad habits or actions, while inclination is a general preference or wish.
  • leaning
    3.4 0.9/m
    leaning is a mild preference or tilt toward an idea or choice, while inclination is a general feeling or natural preference.
  • affinity
    5.6 2/m
    affinity is a natural liking or connection between people or things, while inclination focuses on a personal choice or desire.
  • disposition
    4.6 2/m
    disposition is a person's usual mood or temperament, while inclination is a specific feeling or preference that makes one choose.
