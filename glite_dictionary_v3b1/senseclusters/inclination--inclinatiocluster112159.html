inclination

noun
a preference or mental tendency
9.1 3.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.klə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.klɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.klɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a feeling, tendency, or natural preference that makes you want to do or choose something

Examples

An inclination to help
Despite his natural inclination toward caution, he agreed to take the risk.
My inclination is to trust her judgment in this matter.
She always had an inclination to help those in need.
The child's inclination to help others made her popular at school.
There was a marked inclination for the company to embrace new technologies.
This species of plant shows an inclination to grow toward the light.
Despite his inclination for spicy food, he ordered something mild.
My natural inclination is to wake up early and get started on work.
She has a strong inclination towards abstract art.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • antonym: disinclination - a reluctance or lack of desire to do something
  • synonym: preference - greater liking for one alternative over others
  • synonym: tendency - an inclination toward a particular characteristic or type of behavior
