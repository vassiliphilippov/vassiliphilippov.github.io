mussel shrimp

noun
tiny crustacean with bivalve shell
6.5 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌs.əl ʃrɪmp/ (AmE), /ˈmʌs.əl ʃrɪmp/ (BrE)
A tiny marine or freshwater crustacean with a shrimp-like body enclosed in a bivalve shell.

Examples

The protective bivalve shell helps the mussel shrimp avoid many predators.
Unlike true shrimps, the body of a mussel shrimp is entirely enclosed by its shell.
