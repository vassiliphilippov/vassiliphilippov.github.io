spring vetch

noun
climbing plant used for fodder and soil-building
Pronunciation: / sprɪŋ vɛtʃ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ vɛtʃ / (BrE)
a climbing or bushy plant of the vetch family, grown for livestock fodder, improving soil fertility, and having violet-blue flowers

Examples

The spring vetch adds a lovely touch of color to the meadow in early summer.
We identified several wildflowers, including spring vetch, along the hillside.
