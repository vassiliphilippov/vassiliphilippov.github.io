ascetic

adjective
practicing severe self-denial
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈsɛtɪk / (BrE)
characterized by strict self-discipline and avoidance of all forms of indulgence, especially for religious or spiritual reasons

Examples

Ascetic lifestyle
He led an ascetic lifestyle, refusing all luxuries and comforts.
In pursuit of enlightenment, she adopted an ascetic diet and daily routine.
The monk's ascetic habits astonished his visitors.
His ascetic approach to life inspired those seeking simplicity and discipline.
Monks in the monastery followed an ascetic lifestyle, abstaining from all worldly pleasures.
The artist adopted ascetic practices, living with minimal possessions to focus on his work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: austere - severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance
  • antonym: self-indulgent - allowing oneself to have or do anything they enjoy, especially often or too much
  • synonym: spartan - showing or characterized by austerity or a lack of comfort or luxury
