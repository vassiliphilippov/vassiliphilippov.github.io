Binet-Simon Scale

noun
early standardized intelligence test
Pronunciation: / bəˌneɪ siːˈmɔːn skeɪl / (AmE), / bɪˌneɪ siːˈmɒn skeɪl / (BrE)
the first standardized test created to measure human intelligence, developed by Alfred Binet and Théodore Simon in the early 20th century.

Examples

The Binet-Simon Scale test
In early studies, psychologists relied heavily on the Binet-Simon Scale to assess cognitive development.
The Binet-Simon Scale laid the groundwork for modern intelligence testing methods.
The Binet-Simon Scale revolutionized the way intelligence was measured in children.
