Charles's law

noun
gas law relating volume and temperature
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃɑːrl.zɪz lɔː/ /; / /ˈtʃɑːrl.zɪz lɑː/ (AmE), /ˈtʃɑːlz.ɪz lɔː/ (BrE)
a law in physics stating that the volume of an ideal gas at constant pressure is directly proportional to its absolute temperature

Examples

Charles's law in physics
A hot air balloon rises because Charles's law causes the air inside the balloon to expand when heated.
According to Charles's law, the volume of a gas increases as its temperature rises, provided the pressure is constant.
When explaining gas behavior, teachers often refer to Charles's law alongside Boyle's law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Boyle's law - a law stating that the pressure and volume of a gas have an inverse relationship at constant temperature
