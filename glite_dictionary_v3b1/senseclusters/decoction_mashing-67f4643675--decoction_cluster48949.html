decoction mashing

noun
brewing process involving boiling part of the mash
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkɑkʃən ˈmæʃɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈkɒkʃən ˈmæʃɪŋ / (BrE)
A brewing process in which a portion of the mash is removed, boiled, and then returned to the main mash to raise its temperature and enhance starch conversion.

Examples

Decoction mashing process
Homebrewers sometimes experiment with decoction mashing to achieve complex malt profiles in their beer.
The decoction mashing method involves boiling a portion of the grain mash before mixing it back in.
Traditional German breweries often use decoction mashing to produce richly flavored lagers.
