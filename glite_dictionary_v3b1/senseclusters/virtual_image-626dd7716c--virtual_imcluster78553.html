virtual image

noun
optical image appearing from reflected rays
7.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ùt Éu…ôl Àà…™m…™d í / (AmE), / Ààv…úÀêtj ä…ôl Àà…™m…™d í / (BrE)
An optical image formed by the apparent divergence of rays from a point, such as the image seen in a plane mirror, which cannot be projected on a screen.

Examples

Mirror virtual image
A virtual image cannot be projected onto a screen because the light rays do not actually meet there.
The magnifying glass can produce both real and virtual images depending on the position of the object.
When you look into a mirror, the face you see is actually a virtual image.

Related Terms
