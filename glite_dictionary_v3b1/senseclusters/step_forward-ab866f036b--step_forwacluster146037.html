step forward

verb
come forward; volunteer or take action
1.0
Pronunciation: / stɛp ˈfɔrwərd /; / stɛp ˈfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / stɛp ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to offer to help, take responsibility, or make oneself noticed by volunteering or taking action

Examples

To step forward to help
During the meeting, she was the first to step forward and offer a solution.
Many volunteers have stepped forward to help after the disaster.
Police are asking witnesses to step forward with any information about the incident.
Several witnesses stepped forward to give their statements to the police.
We're looking for someone to step forward and organize the charity event.
When nobody else would help, John decided to step forward and lead the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: come forward - to present oneself, especially voluntarily
  • antonym: hang back - to be unwilling to take action or become involved
  • antonym: hold back - to hesitate or not offer oneself for something
  • synonym: volunteer - to offer to do something willingly without being forced or paid
