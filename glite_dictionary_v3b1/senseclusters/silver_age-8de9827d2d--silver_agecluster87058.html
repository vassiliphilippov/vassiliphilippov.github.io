silver age

noun
a period of lesser greatness than a golden age
7.5 0.01/m

A period of time considered to be of high achievement, though not as great as a 'golden age'; originally, in classical mythology, the second age of the world marked by relative prosperity and decline in virtue.

Examples

The silver age period
In mythology, the silver age followed the golden age, representing a decline in both virtue and happiness.
Many consider the silver age of comic books to have produced some of the genre's most enduring characters.
The poetry of the silver age was impressive, though it did not attain the heights of the previous era.
