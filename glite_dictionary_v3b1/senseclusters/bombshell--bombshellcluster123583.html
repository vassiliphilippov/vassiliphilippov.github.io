bombshell

noun
a shocking surprise or revelation
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːmˌʃel /; / ˈbɑmˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈbɒmˌʃɛl /; / ˈbɒmʃɛl / (BrE)
an event or piece of news that is very surprising or shocking

Examples

A news bombshell
News of the merger landed like a bombshell in the industry.
She dropped a bombshell when she announced her immediate departure from the company.
The committee's resignation was a bombshell that stunned everyone watching.
The resignation of the CEO was a bombshell that few saw coming.
The scandal came as a bombshell to the fans of the politician.
When the results were announced, they dropped a bombshell on the entire community.
