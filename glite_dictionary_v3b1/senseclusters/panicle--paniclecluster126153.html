panicle

noun
branched flower cluster
Pronunciation: / ˈpænɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkl̩ / (BrE)
A loosely branched cluster of flowers in which the branches are racemes; a compound raceme.

Examples

A panicle of flowers
A panicle can support numerous small flowers, making it ideal for wind pollination.
Botanists classify this type of branched inflorescence as a panicle.
The oat plant produces its seeds in a delicate panicle at the top of the stem.
