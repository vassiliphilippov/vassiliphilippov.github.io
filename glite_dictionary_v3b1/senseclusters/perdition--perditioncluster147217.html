perdition

noun
eternal damnation; hell
Pronunciation: / pərˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
(in Christian belief) a state of eternal punishment and damnation into which a sinful person passes after death; hell.

Examples

Eternal perdition
Classical literature is filled with warnings about the agony of perdition.
She feared her choices would lead her soul to perdition.
The preacher warned of perdition for those who turned away from virtue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hell - the place regarded in religions as a realm of evil and punishment after death
