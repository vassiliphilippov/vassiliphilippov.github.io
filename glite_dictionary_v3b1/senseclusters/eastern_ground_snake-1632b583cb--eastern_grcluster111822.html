eastern ground snake

noun
a small North American snake species
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstɚn ɡraʊnd sneɪk / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ɡraʊnd sneɪk / (BrE)
a small reddish-grey snake native to eastern North America, sometimes classified in the genus Haldea

Examples

The eastern ground snake species
Farmers in the southern states sometimes encounter the harmless eastern ground snake in their fields.
Herpetologists classify the eastern ground snake under several different genera depending on the taxonomy used.
The eastern ground snake is rarely seen because it burrows beneath leaves and soil.
