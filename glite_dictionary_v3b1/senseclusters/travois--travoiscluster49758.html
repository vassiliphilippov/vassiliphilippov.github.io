travois

noun
load-carrying frame
Pronunciation: / trəˈvɔɪ / (AmE), / trəˈvɔɪ / (BrE)
a simple vehicle made of two long poles joined at one end and dragged by an animal, used for carrying loads

Examples

A travois vehicle
The explorers used a travois to transport their supplies across the plains.
A horse pulled the travois loaded with firewood back to the camp.
Native peoples often built a travois to carry belongings when moving to a new area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sled - a vehicle for carrying loads over snow or rough ground
  • antonym: wagon - a four-wheeled vehicle for transporting goods
