net

adjective
remaining after all deductions
Pronunciation: / nɛt / (AmE), / nɛt / (BrE)
(of an amount or result) remaining after all deductions such as expenses, taxes, or costs have been subtracted

Examples

Net income
After expenses, their net income was lower than expected.
The net results show a significant improvement over last year.
They reported a net profit of $2 million.
After considering all expenses, our net profit was lower than expected.
If you subtract the refunds, the net sales for the quarter are $20,000.
The net result of the negotiations was an agreement both sides could accept.

Related Terms

  • synonym: final - coming at the end; conclusive
  • antonym: gross - total before deductions
