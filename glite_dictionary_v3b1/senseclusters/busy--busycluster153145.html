busy

adjective
cluttered with detail (design, pattern)
4.0 1.52/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÉªzi / (AmE), / ËˆbÉªzi / (BrE)
(of a design, pattern, or picture) containing too many details, making it appear cluttered or distracting.

Examples

Busy pattern
Her dress had a busy pattern that distracted from her natural beauty.
The logo design is too busy and needs to be simplified.
The wallpaper was so busy that it made the room feel smaller.

Synonyms

    5.0 0.57/m
    fussy contains many small, picky details that draw the eye, while busy simply looks crowded with lots of patterns.
  • cluttered
    3.0 2.53/m
    cluttered is filled with objects or mess that block the view, while busy refers to detailed patterns that distract.
  • overdecorated
    4.7 0.03/m

  • over-embellished
    7.3 0.00/m

  • overinvolved
    7.4 0.00/m
    overinvolved adds many complex parts that make a design hard to follow, while busy mainly looks visually crowded.
