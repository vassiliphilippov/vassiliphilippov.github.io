yielding

noun
verbal admission of defeat
Pronunciation: / ˈjiːldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of giving in, surrendering, or conceding in a discussion, contest, or disagreement

Examples

Yielding in an argument
After hours of debate, his yielding signaled the end of the argument.
Her quiet yielding during the meeting surprised her colleagues.
The general's public yielding was broadcast to the entire nation.
His yielding to their demands was seen as a sign of weakness.
She was frustrated by his constant yielding in arguments.
The negotiation ended with the yielding of several important points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capitulation - the act of ceasing to resist; surrender
  • synonym: concession - the act of conceding or granting something
  • antonym: resistance - the refusal to accept or comply with something; the attempt to prevent something by action or argument
  • synonym: surrender - the act of giving up or yielding to another
