throng

verb
to crowd together tightly
Pronunciation: / θrɔŋ / (AmE), / θrɒŋ / (BrE)
to crowd or press closely together in a particular place

Examples

To throng the streets
Fans began to throng the stadium hours before the concert started.
Locals and tourists alike throng the main square every evening in summer.
Shoppers thronged the mall during the holiday sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crowd - to gather in large numbers; to fill a place
