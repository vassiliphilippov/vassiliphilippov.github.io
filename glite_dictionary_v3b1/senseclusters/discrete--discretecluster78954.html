discrete

adjective
separate; distinct; unconnected
6.1 5.40/m
Pronunciation: / d…™Ààskrit / (AmE), / d…™ÀàskriÀêt / (BrE)
separate and distinct; not connected to or part of something else.

Examples

A discrete variable
In mathematics, a discrete variable can only take on certain specific values.
The company is organized into discrete departments, each with its own responsibilities.
The project was divided into several discrete phases, each managed by a different team.

Synonyms

  • separate
    2.1 61/m
    separate means not joined or connected and often describes physical items, while discrete means separate and clearly distinct.
  • distinct
    3.5 24/m
    distinct is clearly different in quality or idea, while discrete stresses being separate and not connected physically or logically.
  • disjunct
    9.9 0.02/m
    disjunct describes things split into isolated groups in technical contexts, while discrete is general for separate, individual items.
  • discontiguous
    8.9 0.01/m
    discontiguous means not touching in space or time, while discrete means separate and distinct without implying physical gaps.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinct - clearly separate and different
