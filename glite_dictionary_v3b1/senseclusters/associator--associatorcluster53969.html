associator

noun
synesthete with internal sensations
Pronunciation: / əˈsoʊsiˌeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈsəʊsiˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a person with synesthesia who experiences associated sensations, such as colors for letters or numbers, only in their mind and not as external perceptions

Examples

Synesthesia associator
As an associator, she sees the color of numbers only in her mind, not on objects.
Many associators describe their synesthetic experiences as vivid but entirely internal.
Unlike projectors, an associator does not see colors on the page but imagines them mentally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: projector - a synesthete who perceives associated sensations as if they appear in the external world
