ineluctably

adverb
inevitably, unavoidably
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnəˈlʌktəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈlʌktəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be avoided or escaped; inevitably or necessarily.

Examples

To ineluctably fail
He realized the project was ineluctably heading toward failure despite their best efforts.
History moves ineluctably forward, regardless of individual wishes.
The consequences of pollution will ineluctably affect future generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: escapably - in a manner that can be avoided
  • synonym: inevitably - in such a way that it is certain to happen
