smog

noun
air pollution by smoke and fog
1.7 1.7
Pronunciation: /smɑːg/ /; / /smɔːg/ (AmE), /smɒg/ (BrE)
A type of air pollution consisting of a mixture of smoke and fog, often found in urban or industrial areas.

Examples

Heavy smog pollution
Air quality alerts were issued due to dangerous levels of smog during the summer.
Heavy smog blanketed the city, making it difficult to see the buildings.
The city's efforts to reduce smog have led to noticeable improvements in air quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clean air - air free of pollution, especially smoke and chemicals
  • synonym: haze - reduced visibility caused by fine particles in the air
