carpet grass

noun
a dense, mat-forming grass species
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrpɪt ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪt ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a wiry-stemmed grass native to the West Indies, commonly found in the southern United States, often forming dense mats and sometimes infested with dark fungus

Examples

Carpet grass grows thick
After a rainy season, the carpet grass grew rapidly, forming thick clumps across the yard.
Many golf courses in the South use carpet grass because it creates a dense, uniform turf.
The lawn was covered in carpet grass, giving it a lush, green appearance.
