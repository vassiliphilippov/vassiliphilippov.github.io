brattice

verb
to supply a mine with a brattice
Pronunciation: / ˈbrætɪs / (AmE), / ˈbrætɪs / (BrE)
to equip or supply (a mine) with a brattice in order to improve ventilation

Examples

To brattice a tunnel
Properly bratticed passages are essential to prevent gas buildup in coal mines.
The safety inspector ordered the workers to brattice the tunnel for better airflow.
Work crews were hired to brattice the new mine shaft before winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ventilate - to cause air to enter and circulate freely in a space
