closed universe

noun
finite universe model with enough mass to halt expansion
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / klo äzd Ààjun…™Àåv…ùs / (AmE), / kl…ô äzd ÀàjuÀên…™Àåv…úÀês / (BrE)
(in cosmology) a model of the universe in which space is spatially closed and contains enough matter to eventually halt its expansion, possibly leading to a future contraction.

Examples

A closed universe model
Evidence for dark matter may be essential to prove the existence of a closed universe.
Some cosmologists believe that the universe is a closed universe that will eventually stop expanding and begin to contract.
The fate of a closed universe contrasts with that of an open universe, which expands forever.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flat universe - a model with just enough matter to stop expansion but never contract
  • antonym: open universe - a model in which the universe will expand forever
