investment

noun
the act of investing money for profit
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
the act of putting money, time, effort, or other resources into something (such as a business, project, or activity) with the expectation of getting a benefit or profit

Examples

A smart investment
Her biggest investment was buying shares in a promising startup.
Making an investment in technology can help a company stay competitive.
They considered real estate a safe investment for their retirement savings.
Her investment in learning a new language opened up many career opportunities.
Parents often make an enormous investment of time and energy in raising their children.
This relationship required a significant emotional investment from both partners.
Many people see education as an important investment in their future.
She made a wise investment in real estate that paid off after a few years.
The company attracted new investment to expand its operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commitment - state or quality of being dedicated to a cause or activity
  • synonym: expenditure - the act of spending resources, usually time or energy, on something
  • degree lower: saving - the act of setting aside money rather than spending it
  • degree higher: speculation - buying assets with high risk in hope of significant profit
