relativist

noun
supporter of relativity
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or studies the scientific theory of relativity, especially as developed by Einstein, or who believes that physical quantities depend on the observer's frame of reference

Examples

An Einstein relativist
The young relativist defended Einstein's ideas in the debate on gravity.
As a dedicated relativist, she often discussed how time and space are not absolute.
Many scientists consider themselves relativists when it comes to understanding the universe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolutist - a person who believes in absolute principles rather than relativity
  • synonym: physicist - a scientist who studies or is an expert in physics
