flap

noun
motion of flapping up and down
Pronunciation: / flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
a quick or repeated movement made by flapping up and down, especially of wings or flat objects

Examples

A flap of wings
The bird's wings made a loud flap as it took off from the ground.
The helicopter's blades gave a heavy flap as it hovered overhead.
With each flap of the sheet, more dust filled the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flutter - quick, light movement up and down or back and forth, often of wings
