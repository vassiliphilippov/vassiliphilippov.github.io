surety

noun
property or guarantee securing a debt or obligation
5.3 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈʃʊr.ə.tiː/ /; / /ˈʃʊr.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈʃɔː.rɪ.tiː/ /; / /ˈʃʊə.rɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
property or a guarantee pledged to secure the fulfillment of an obligation, especially the repayment of a loan; security given to assure payment or performance.

Examples

Surety for a loan
She offered her house as surety for her brother's debts.
The lender required additional surety before approving the loan.
Without proper surety, it is difficult to obtain large amounts of credit.
