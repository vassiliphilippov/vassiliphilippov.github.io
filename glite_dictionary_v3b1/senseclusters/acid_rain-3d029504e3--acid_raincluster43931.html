acid rain

noun
rain made acidic by pollution
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪd reɪn / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd reɪn / (BrE)
rain that is made acidic by atmospheric pollution, particularly from sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides released by industrial processes.

Examples

Acid rain damage
Many forests have suffered from acid rain damaging trees and soil quality.
Scientists monitor lakes for changes caused by acid rain each year.
The city has taken steps to reduce pollution and minimize the effects of acid rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acid deposition - the wider fall of acids from the atmosphere, including dry forms as well as rain
