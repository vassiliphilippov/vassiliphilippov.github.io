milk snake

noun
nonvenomous North American king snake
9.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /mɪlk sneɪk/ (AmE), /mɪlk sneɪk/ (BrE)
a nonvenomous North American king snake, typically banded with red, black, and white or yellow, often mimicking the appearance of the venomous coral snake

Examples

A milk snake spotted
A milk snake was spotted slithering across the garden path in the late afternoon.
Many people keep milk snakes as pets because they are harmless and easy to care for.
The milk snake is often confused with the coral snake due to its colorful bands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coral snake - a venomous snake species that milk snakes mimic in appearance
  • synonym: king snake - a group of nonvenomous snakes to which milk snakes belong
