lovegrass

noun
forage or erosion grass
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌvˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈlʌvˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a type of grass found in warm regions, often grown for animal feed or to prevent soil erosion

Examples

Lovegrass plant
The farmer planted lovegrass to help prevent soil erosion on the hillside.
Cattle grazed on the lush lovegrass that covered the pasture.
Many parks use lovegrass because it grows well in hot, dry climates.
