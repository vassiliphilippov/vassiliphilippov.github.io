Examples

Synonyms

  • turn around
    2.0 13/m
    turn around is a general phrase for facing the opposite direction, while swing around suggests a quick, abrupt physical or figurative turn.
  • swing about
    4.8 0.08/m
    swing about means to turn abruptly in body or attitude, while swing around is used for the same quick physical or figurative turn.
  • turn round
    1.4 0.08/m
    turn round is mainly British and means to move your body to face the opposite way, while swing around implies a quicker, more sudden turn.
  • wheel around
    4.2 0.46/m
    wheel around suggests turning as if on a pivot or wheels, often dramatic, while swing around focuses on an abrupt bodily or figurative turn.
  • about-face
    6.1 0.05/m
    about-face denotes a sudden 180-degree turn often in formal or military settings, while swing around is a more casual abrupt turn.
  • about turn
    5.8 0.01/m
    about turn is a noun or command for turning completely, often in drill, while swing around is a casual verb phrase for a quick turn.
