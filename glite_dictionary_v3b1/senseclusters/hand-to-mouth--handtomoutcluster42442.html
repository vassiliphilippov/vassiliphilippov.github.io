hand-to-mouth

adjective
having only basic necessities; living day to day
6.0 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhænd tə ˈmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˌhænd tə ˈmaʊθ / (BrE)
having only enough money or resources to meet immediate or basic needs, with no savings or security

Examples

A hand-to-mouth life
After losing his job, he was forced to live a hand-to-mouth existence.
Her hand-to-mouth lifestyle left her with no savings for emergencies.
Many people in the village survived on a hand-to-mouth income.
After losing his job, he was forced to live a hand-to-mouth existence for several months.
Many artists live hand-to-mouth until their work gains recognition.
She escaped her hand-to-mouth upbringing by securing a well-paying job.

  • synonym: subsistence - the condition of having just enough to survive
