colostrum

noun
first milk after childbirth
6.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɑstrəm / (AmE), / kəˈlɒstrəm / (BrE)
the thick, yellowish fluid produced by the mammary glands in the first days after childbirth, rich in antibodies and nutrients, before the onset of true milk.

Examples

Thick colostrum fluid
Colostrum contains antibodies that help protect newborns from infections.
Mothers are encouraged to let their babies drink colostrum immediately after birth.
The nurse explained the benefits of colostrum to the new parents in the maternity ward.
