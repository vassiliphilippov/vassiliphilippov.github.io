idiophone

noun
sensory-evoking word
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɪ.dɪ.ə.foʊn/ (AmE), /ˈɪ.dɪ.ə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
a word that imitates or vividly suggests a sound, movement, color, or other sensory impression, often used for effect in language

Examples

A comic idiophone
Writers often use an idiophone to make their stories more vivid and engaging.
The word 'buzz' is an example of an idiophone that imitates the sound of a bee.
In some languages, an idiophone can describe not only sounds but also colors or movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ideophone - a word that evokes a sensory impression, especially sound or movement
  • synonym: onomatopoeia - a word that imitates the sound it represents
