second-guess

verb
try to anticipate or predict
3.8 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkəndˈɡɛs / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈɡɛs / (BrE)
to try to anticipate or predict what will happen or what someone else will do or think, often with the implication of questioning someone's decisions

Examples

It's impossible to second-guess how the market will react to this news.
She tried to second-guess her boss's preferences before the meeting.
