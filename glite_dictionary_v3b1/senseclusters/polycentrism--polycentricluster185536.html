polycentrism

noun
multiple urban centers
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
the organization of a region or city with several distinct centers of population, commerce, or activity rather than a single central core

Examples

Urban polycentrism
Urban planners are increasingly interested in polycentrism to reduce congestion in city centers.
The region's polycentrism allows for more balanced economic development across several towns.
By encouraging polycentrism, the government hopes to create more accessible jobs throughout the metropolitan area.
