corruption

noun
dishonest or fraudulent conduct
1.0 4.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌpʃən /; / kəˈɹʌpʃən / (AmE), / kəˈrʌpʃən /; / kəˈɹʌpʃən / (BrE)
dishonest or illegal conduct by people in power, especially involving bribery or abuse of authority

Examples

Political corruption
Allegations of corruption have damaged the reputation of several government officials.
Fighting corruption is essential for building trust in public institutions.
He lost his job after being accused of corruption and accepting bribes.
The mayor resigned after allegations of corruption surfaced in the city council.
They launched a campaign to fight corruption in government offices.
Widespread corruption in law enforcement undermines trust in the justice system.

  • synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest or truthful
  • antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
