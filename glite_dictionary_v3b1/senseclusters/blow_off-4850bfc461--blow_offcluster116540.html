blow off

verb
deliberately ignore or skip (an event or duty)
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ɑf / (AmE), / bləʊ ɒf / (BrE)
to deliberately ignore, avoid, or fail to attend something, often out of lack of interest or a desire to do something else

She can't believe you would blow off such an important appointment.
Tom decided to blow off the meeting and go to the movies instead.
