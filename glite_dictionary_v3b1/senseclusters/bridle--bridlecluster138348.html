bridle

verb
put a bridle on an animal
7.8 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbraɪ.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈbraɪ.dəl/ (BrE)
to put a bridle on a horse or to control or guide a horse using a bridle

Examples

To bridle a horse
As he bridled the mare, he checked the straps for any signs of wear.
It took several minutes to bridle the restless stallion.
She carefully bridled the horse before their morning ride.
If the animal refuses to bridle, it can be difficult for the rider to maintain control.
Some horses naturally bridle better than others, making them easier to ride.
The young horse quickly learned to bridle during its training sessions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unbridle - to remove the bridle from (an animal)
