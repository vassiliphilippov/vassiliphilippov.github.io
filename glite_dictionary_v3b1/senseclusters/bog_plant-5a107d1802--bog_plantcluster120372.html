bog plant

noun
plant growing in boggy, wetland areas
Pronunciation: / bɑɡ plænt / (AmE), / bɒɡ plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that grows in wet, spongy land such as bogs or marshes, typically adapted to saturated and low-nutrient conditions.

Examples

Bog plants grow
After heavy rains, bog plants thrived in the marshy corners of the reserve.
Sphagnum moss is a well-known type of bog plant used for soil enhancement.
The garden's new section features many beautiful bog plants native to wetland areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marsh plant - a plant that grows in a marsh (wetland environment)
