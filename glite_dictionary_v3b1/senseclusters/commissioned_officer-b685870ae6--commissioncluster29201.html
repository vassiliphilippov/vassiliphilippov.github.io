commissioned officer

noun
military officer with formal commission
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪʃənd ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃənd ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
A member of the armed forces who holds a position of authority by virtue of a formal commission from the state or sovereign.

Examples

A commissioned officer rank
After graduating from the academy, she was appointed as a commissioned officer.
He served as a commissioned officer in the army for over twenty years.
Only a commissioned officer can give that order in this division.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-commissioned officer - a military officer who does not hold a commission
