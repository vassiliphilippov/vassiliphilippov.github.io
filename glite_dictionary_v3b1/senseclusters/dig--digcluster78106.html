dig

verb
work hard at something
Pronunciation: / dɪg / (AmE), / dɪg / (BrE)
To work hard at a task, often persistently or with sustained effort.

Examples

To dig into work
He dug away at the task until it was finished.
She dug into her project for hours without a break.
The team kept digging through the long night to meet their deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drudge - to do hard, menial, or dull work
  • synonym: toil - work extremely hard or incessantly
