Walpurgis Night

noun
eve of May Day celebration
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɑlˈpɜrdʒɪs naɪt / (AmE), / wɔːlˈpɜːdʒɪs naɪt / (BrE)
The night of April 30th, celebrated in parts of Europe as the eve of May Day, often associated with bonfires, festivals, and the supposed gathering of witches.

Examples

Walpurgis Night bonfires
In Germany, people mark Walpurgis Night with bonfires and celebrations.
Many legends claim that witches meet on Walpurgis Night atop the Brocken mountain.
My friends and I stayed up late to enjoy the festivities of Walpurgis Night.
