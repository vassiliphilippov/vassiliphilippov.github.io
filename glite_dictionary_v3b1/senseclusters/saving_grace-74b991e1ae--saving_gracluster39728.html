saving grace

noun
state of divine sanctification (Christianity)
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪvɪŋ ɡreɪs / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋ ɡreɪs / (BrE)
(Christian theology) the state of sanctification bestowed by God, placing a person under divine influence and regarded as bringing salvation.

Examples

Receive saving grace
Many Christians believe that baptism is the means by which saving grace is received.
She prayed daily, trusting in the saving grace of God to guide her life.
The concept of saving grace is central to many theological debates about redemption.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grace - divine favor or assistance granted by God
