solar eclipse

noun
when the moon blocks the sun's light
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊlər ɪˈklɪps / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlə ɪˈklɪps / (BrE)
an event when the moon passes between the earth and the sun, blocking all or part of the sun's light from reaching the earth

Examples

A solar eclipse event
During the solar eclipse, it became unusually dark for several minutes in the afternoon.
Many schools organized trips to observe the rare solar eclipse safely.
The next solar eclipse will be visible in parts of North America.
Many students gathered outside the school to witness the rare solar eclipse.
The next total solar eclipse will be visible from South America.
We wore special glasses to view the solar eclipse safely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lunar eclipse - an occasion when the earth comes between the sun and the moon, causing the moon to appear darkened
