unfocus

verb
to make less clear
7.4
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to make vision or attention less clear or sharp; to stop focusing

Examples

To unfocus the eyes
He tried to unfocus his eyes to relax after reading for hours.
The photographer accidentally unfocused the camera, making the picture blurry.
Sometimes it helps to unfocus your attention and let your mind wander.
She realized she was unfocusing during the lecture and tried to pay attention again.
If you unfocus your eyes, the image will become blurry.
He tends to unfocus when the conversation gets too technical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - to make or become unclear or less distinct
  • antonym: concentrate - to focus one's attention or mental effort
  • synonym: distract - to draw someone's attention away from something
  • antonym: focus - to make something clear or concentrated
