behind

adjective, adverb
having a lower score in a contest
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
in a position that is less advanced, lower in score, or late compared to others (in competition, schedule, or payment)

Examples

The business fell behind with its tax bills and faced penalties.
They were constantly behind in paying their rent, which worried the landlord.
After missing several deadlines, the project slipped behind schedule.
If you don't practice regularly, you'll quickly fall behind your classmates.
The company was losing money and had started to lag behind its rivals.
