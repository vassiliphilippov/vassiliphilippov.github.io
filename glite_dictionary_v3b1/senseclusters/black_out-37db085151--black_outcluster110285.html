black out

verb
make completely dark; deprive of light
6.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /blæk aʊt/ (AmE), /blæk aʊt/ (BrE)
to make a place or area completely dark, especially by turning off or covering all lights

Examples

To black out a city
During the storm, the city decided to black out all buildings to avoid detection.
If you black out your room, you'll sleep much better with no light distractions.
The crew needed to black out the theater for the special effects to work properly.
