CMB

noun
faint cosmic radiation background
9.9
Pronunciation: / si ɛm bi / (AmE), / si ɛm bi / (BrE)
(cosmology) the faint radiation left over from the early universe, observable today as a nearly uniform background at about 2.7 kelvin.

Examples

The CMB radiation
Scientists analyze data from the CMB to learn about the early universe.
The CMB provides crucial evidence supporting the Big Bang theory.
Tiny variations in the CMB reveal information about cosmic structure formation.
