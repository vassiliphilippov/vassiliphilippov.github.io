eclosion

noun
insect emergence
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkloʊʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈkləʊʒən / (BrE)
the act or process of an insect or animal emerging from its egg, pupa, or similar enclosure

Examples

Eclosion of butterflies
The eclosion of the moths was carefully observed by the researchers.
After several days in the cocoon, the butterfly's eclosion finally took place.
Temperature and humidity can affect the timing of eclosion in many insect species.
The butterfly's eclosion from its chrysalis was a delicate and beautiful process.
Scientists observed the timing of eclosion in the laboratory to study insect development.
After several days, the larvae completed eclosion and began searching for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emergence - the act of coming out or becoming visible
  • synonym: hatching - the process of an animal emerging from an egg
  • antonym: pupation - the process of becoming a pupa
