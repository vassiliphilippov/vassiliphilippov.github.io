shill

verb
act as a decoy or deceptive promoter
Pronunciation: / ʃɪl / (AmE), / ʃɪl / (BrE)
to act as a decoy or promoter, especially by pretending to be a genuine customer in order to encourage others to participate or buy; to promote or endorse deceptively, often for personal gain

Examples

To shill a product
During the auction, she shilled to drive up the price of the painting.
He was accused of shilling for the company by posting fake positive reviews online.
The celebrity was criticized for shilling products she clearly never used herself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tout - to attempt to sell or promote something, often aggressively or slyly
