curvilinear correlation

noun
nonlinear statistical relationship
Pronunciation: / ˌkɝvəˌlɪniɚ ˌkɔrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɜːvɪˌlɪniə ˌkɒrəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a correlation between two variables in which the relationship is not constant but changes direction or magnitude, forming a curved rather than straight line on a graph

Examples

A curvilinear correlation pattern
A curvilinear correlation is observed when plotting age against happiness in some studies.
Researchers noted a curvilinear correlation between income and life satisfaction.
Unlike a linear trend, a curvilinear correlation may rise to a peak and then fall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: linear correlation - a correlation where the relationship between variables forms a straight line
