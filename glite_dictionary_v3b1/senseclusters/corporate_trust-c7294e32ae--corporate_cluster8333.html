corporate trust

noun
a consortium of companies restricting competition
6.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îr.p…ôr.…ôt Ààtr åst / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀê.p…ôr.…ôt Ààtr åst / (BrE)
a group of independent companies that join together to control the production and distribution of a product or service, often to restrict competition

Examples

A corporate trust group
Legislation was introduced to break up the corporate trust and encourage free market competition.
Several railroad companies formed a corporate trust to fix prices and divide up territories.
The oil industry was dominated by a powerful corporate trust in the early twentieth century.

Synonyms

  • cartel
