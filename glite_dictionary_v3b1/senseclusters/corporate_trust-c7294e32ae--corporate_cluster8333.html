corporate trust

noun
a consortium of companies restricting competition
6.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îr.p…ôr.…ôt Ààtr åst / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀê.p…ôr.…ôt Ààtr åst / (BrE)
a group of independent companies that join together to control the production and distribution of a product or service, often to restrict competition

Examples

A corporate trust group
Legislation was introduced to break up the corporate trust and encourage free market competition.
Several railroad companies formed a corporate trust to fix prices and divide up territories.
The oil industry was dominated by a powerful corporate trust in the early twentieth century.

Synonyms

  • trust
    7.0 3.49/m
    trust is a group of companies joined to control production and sales, while corporate trust is the formal name for that same business grouping.
  • cartel
    7.9 9.43/m
    cartel is an association that sets production, price, and marketing rules to limit competition, while corporate trust focuses on control of production and distribution.
  • combine
    7.1 0.15/m
    combine is a group of companies that join to control production and reduce competition, while corporate trust often names a formal business alliance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartel - an association of manufacturers or suppliers that maintains prices at a high level and restricts competition
