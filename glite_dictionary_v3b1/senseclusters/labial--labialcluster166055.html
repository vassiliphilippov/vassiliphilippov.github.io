labial

noun
consonant articulated with the lips
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bi.əl / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced with movement or closure of the lips.

Examples

A labial consonant
Some African languages have more labials than English does.
The English sound /p/ is a classic example of a labial.
The teacher pointed out the difference between a labial and a dental sound.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bilabial - a consonant articulated with both lips, such as 'p' or 'b'
  • antonym: dental - a consonant articulated with the tongue against the teeth
