unmagical

adjective
not magical
6.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmædʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈmædʒɪkəl / (BrE)
lacking magic, enchantment, or a sense of wonder

Examples

An unmagical evening
Despite the beautiful setting, the evening felt strangely unmagical to everyone present.
The story was interesting but ultimately too realistic and unmagical for young children.
After the festival ended, the town seemed quiet and a bit unmagical.

Related Terms

  • antonym: magical - having the qualities of magic or enchantment
  • synonym: ordinary - not special or unusual; normal
