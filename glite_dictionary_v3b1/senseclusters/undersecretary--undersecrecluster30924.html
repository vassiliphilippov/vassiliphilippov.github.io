undersecretary

noun
junior administrator in a government department
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈsɛkrəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsɛkrətəri / (BrE)
an official who is immediately subordinate to the head of a government department, often responsible for assisting in administration and decision-making

Examples

The undersecretary role
After years of service, she was promoted from undersecretary to deputy secretary.
As undersecretary of health, he played a crucial role in shaping national healthcare policy.
The undersecretary briefed the committee on the ministry's recent policy changes.
