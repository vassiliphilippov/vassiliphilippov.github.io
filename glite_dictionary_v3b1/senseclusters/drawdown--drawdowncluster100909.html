drawdown

noun
military reduction
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɔˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a planned or ordered reduction or withdrawal of military forces, personnel, or resources

Examples

Military drawdown
The government announced a gradual drawdown of combat troops from the region next month.
After years of conflict, the international community supported a drawdown of military presence.
The drawdown was completed ahead of schedule, leaving only a small peacekeeping force.
The government ordered a drawdown of troops from the region after the peace agreement.
There was a significant drawdown in the company's cash reserves during the economic downturn.
The environmental group warned about the drawdown of water levels in the lake due to overuse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decrease - a reduction in size, amount, or number
  • antonym: deployment - the movement of military forces into position for action
  • antonym: increase - a rise in the amount or number of something
  • synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
  • synonym: withdrawal - the act of removing military forces from an area
