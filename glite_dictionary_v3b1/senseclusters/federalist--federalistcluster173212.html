federalist

noun
supporter of federalism
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɛdərəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates federalism, especially in the organization of government

Examples

A federalist supporter
During the debates, she identified herself as a prominent federalist.
Many federalists attended the convention to support the new constitution.
The federalist argued that a strong central government would benefit the nation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anti-federalist - someone opposed to federalism, especially in U.S. historical context
