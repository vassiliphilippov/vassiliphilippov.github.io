allosaurus

noun
a large carnivorous Jurassic dinosaur
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæloʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌaləˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a large carnivorous dinosaur of the late Jurassic period, somewhat smaller and earlier than Tyrannosaurus, characterized by a massive skull and sharp teeth

Examples

The allosaurus skeleton
At the museum, the enormous skeleton of an allosaurus towered over the visitors.
Many paleontologists believe the allosaurus was one of the top predators of its time.
The fossilized bones of an allosaurus were discovered in the Utah desert.
