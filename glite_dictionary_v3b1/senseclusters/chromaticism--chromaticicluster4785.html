chromaticism

noun
use of vivid colors
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmætɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / krəʊˈmætɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the use of strong or varied colors, especially to create a vivid effect in art or design

Examples

Artistic chromaticism
The artist's chromaticism made the painting stand out in the gallery.
Modern designers often use chromaticism to attract attention to their work.
Her bold chromaticism brought energy and life to the mural.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colorfulness - the quality of having bright or varied colors
  • antonym: monochrome - the use of only one color or shades of one color
