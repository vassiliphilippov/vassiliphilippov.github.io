existentialist philosopher

noun
proponent of existentialism in philosophy
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛɡˌzɪstəˈnʃəlɪst fəˈlɑːsəfər / (AmE), / ˌɛɡˌzɪstəˈnʃəlɪst fəˈlɒsəfə / (BrE)
a philosopher who emphasizes individual freedom and responsibility, and views human existence as fundamentally unexplainable or absurd in a hostile universe

Examples

The existentialist philosopher Kierkegaard
As a student, she became fascinated by the ideas of the Danish existentialist philosopher Søren Kierkegaard.
Jean-Paul Sartre is often cited as the most influential existentialist philosopher of the twentieth century.
The existentialist philosopher argues that meaning in life must be created by the individual, not imposed externally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: existentialism - philosophical theory emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and choice
  • synonym: philosopher - a person engaged in philosophy
