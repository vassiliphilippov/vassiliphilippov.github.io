hold

verb
remain valid or true
2.2
Pronunciation: / hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
to maintain a state, position, or condition; to remain steady, valid, or unchanged

Examples

Hold steady
Despite new evidence, the original hypothesis still holds.
The old saying holds true even today.
These rules hold for all employees, not just managers.
Despite the pressure, her voice held steady during the speech.
The rain held all afternoon, so the picnic could go ahead as planned.
The team held first place in the rankings until the end of the season.
Does the conclusion still hold if we change the initial assumptions?
The rule about safety holds even during emergencies.
This explanation holds for other similar cases as well.
