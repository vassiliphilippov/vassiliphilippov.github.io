swacked

adjective
badly damaged
Pronunciation: / swækt / (AmE), / swækt / (BrE)
severely damaged, crushed, or beaten, especially as the result of a blow or accident

Examples

Swacked car after crash
After the storm, the boat was completely swacked and needed major repairs.
The phone was so swacked after the fall that it would not turn on.
His old bicycle looked swacked after years of rough use.
