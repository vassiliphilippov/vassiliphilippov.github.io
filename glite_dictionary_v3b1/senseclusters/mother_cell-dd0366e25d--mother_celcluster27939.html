mother cell

noun
cell from which another cell develops
5.6 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌð.ər sel/ (AmE), /ˈmʌð.ə sel/ (BrE)
a cell from which another cell, often of a different type, develops during the growth or reproduction of an organism

Examples

A mother cell divides
A mother cell divides to give rise to two genetically identical daughter cells.
Spores are produced when a mother cell undergoes meiotic division.
The yeast mother cell produces a small bud that eventually separates as a new organism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: daughter cell - a cell that results from the division of a mother cell
