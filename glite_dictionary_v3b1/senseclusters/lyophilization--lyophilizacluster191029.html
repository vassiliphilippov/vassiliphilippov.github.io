lyophilization

noun
freeze-drying process
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: / laɪˌɑːfəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / laɪˌɑːfələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / laɪˌɒfɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
A process of freeze-drying in which material (such as food, blood plasma, pharmaceuticals, or biological samples) is frozen and then dried under reduced pressure so that frozen water sublimes directly from solid to gas; used to preserve products and maintain their structure.

Examples

Lyophilization process
Many pharmaceuticals are prepared by lyophilization to increase their shelf life.
The laboratory relies on lyophilization to preserve sensitive samples for long-term storage.
Through lyophilization, nutrients and flavor are retained in freeze-dried foods.
Advances in lyophilisation have made it possible to store sensitive vaccines without refrigeration.
Pharmaceutical companies often use lyophilisation to preserve injectable medications for long-term storage.
The lyophilisation of fruit retains much of its original flavor and nutritional value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze-drying - the process of removing water from a frozen material using low pressure
