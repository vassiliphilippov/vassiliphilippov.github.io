Mniaceae

noun
a family of mosses
Pronunciation: / ˌniːiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˌniːiˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of mosses characterized by erect growth, club-shaped paraphyses, and hexagonal cells on the upper leaf surface.

Examples

Mniaceae moss family
Botanists distinguish Mniaceae from other moss families by their hexagonal leaf cells and club-shaped paraphyses.
Many forests in the Northern Hemisphere are home to mosses belonging to the Mniaceae family.
The species in Mniaceae are often found in moist, shaded habitats near streams and wetlands.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Bryaceae - a family of mosses sometimes considered to include Mniaceae
