horseless

adjective
without a horse
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrsləs / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsləs / (BrE)
lacking a horse, especially used to describe vehicles powered by engines instead of horses

Examples

Horseless carriage
The horseless carriage was a revolutionary invention in the late nineteenth century.
Early advertisements promoted the benefits of horseless travel over traditional horse-drawn vehicles.
Many people were skeptical of horseless vehicles when they first appeared on city streets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: horse-drawn - pulled by a horse
  • synonym: motorized - powered by a motor or engine
