driving force

noun
force that propels or moves something
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪvɪŋ fɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ fɔːs / (BrE)
the force or agency that causes something to move or be propelled forward

Examples

The driving force behind
A strong wind provided the driving force for the sailboat to move quickly across the lake.
Gravity acts as the driving force in this experiment, pulling the object downward.
The steam engine was once the driving force behind modern transportation.
