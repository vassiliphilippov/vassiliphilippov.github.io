fetch

verb
take away; carry off (often by supernatural means)
3.1 6.5
Pronunciation: /fetʃ/ (AmE), /fetʃ/ (BrE)
(often humorous or literary) to take or carry away, especially to capture or claim someone, as by death or a supernatural force.

Examples

To fetch a soul
'Be careful, or the spirits will fetch you away!' warned the old storyteller.
He joked that if he stayed up any later, the Sandman would fetch him to sleep.
The villagers believed the ghost would fetch anyone who wandered near the graveyard at night.
