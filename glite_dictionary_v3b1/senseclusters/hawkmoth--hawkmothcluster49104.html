hawkmoth

noun
a moth with strong flight and hovering ability
8.1 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ëkÀåm…îŒ∏ / (AmE), / Ààh…îÀêkÀåm…íŒ∏ / (BrE)
A medium to large moth of the family Sphingidae, known for its long, narrow forewings, powerful flight, and ability to hover while feeding from flowers.

Examples

A hawkmoth flying
Many gardeners appreciate the presence of hawkmoths because they help pollinate evening flowers.
Scientists studied how the hawkmoth is able to fly steadily even in windy conditions.
The hawkmoth hovered in front of the blossom, sipping nectar with its long proboscis.
