swarf

verb
to create metal filings
9.9
Pronunciation: /swɑːrf/ /; / /swɔːrf/ (AmE), /swɔːf/ (BrE)
to produce or remove material in the form of small pieces or filings, especially during cutting, grinding, or filing processes

Examples

To swarf a metal rod
The machinist carefully swarfed the metal rod to achieve a smooth finish.
Modern lathes are designed to efficiently swarf excess material during production.
After hours of work, the technician had swarfed a pile of fine metal shavings.
