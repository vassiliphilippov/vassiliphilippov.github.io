propylene glycol

noun
a common synthetic liquid in industry and cosmetics

Pronunciation: / Ààpro ä.p…ôÀålin Àà…°la…™.k…îl / (AmE), / Ààpr…ô ä.p…™ÀåliÀên Àà…°la…™.k…íl / (BrE)
a sweet, colorless, viscous liquid used in antifreeze, brake fluids, cosmetics, and personal care products, sometimes with potential harmful effects if absorbed through the skin

Examples

Propylene glycol antifreeze
Automotive antifreeze often includes propylene glycol because it is less toxic than ethylene glycol.
Many skin creams contain propylene glycol as a humectant to retain moisture.
The manufacturer adjusted the formula to reduce the amount of propylene glycol due to safety concerns.
