quadratic polynomial

noun
polynomial of degree two
3.9 6.2
Pronunciation: /kwə.ˈdræt.ɪk pə.lɪ.ˈnoʊ.mɪ.əl/ (AmE), /kwɒ.ˈdræt.ɪk pɒ.lɪ.ˈnəʊ.mɪ.əl/ (BrE)
a polynomial of the second degree, typically in the form ax² + bx + c, where a, b, and c are constants and a ≠ 0

Examples

A quadratic polynomial equation
Many natural phenomena can be modeled using a quadratic polynomial.
The equation x² + 5x + 6 is a classic example of a quadratic polynomial.
To find the roots of a quadratic polynomial, you can use the quadratic formula.
