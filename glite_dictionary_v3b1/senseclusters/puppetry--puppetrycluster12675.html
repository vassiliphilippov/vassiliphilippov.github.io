puppetry

noun
stiff or artificial dramatic performance
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌp.ɪ.tri / (AmE), / ˈpʌp.ɪ.tri / (BrE)
A style of dramatic performance that is stiff, artificial, or lacking in natural expression, as if performed by puppets.

Examples

Puppetry acting
Critics complained about the puppetry of the performances, noting the lack of emotion.
Her reading of the lines had a certain puppetry that detracted from the play's realism.
The actor's puppetry made it hard for the audience to connect with the character.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naturalness - the quality of being natural or genuine in performance
  • synonym: wooden - lacking in natural expression, awkward
