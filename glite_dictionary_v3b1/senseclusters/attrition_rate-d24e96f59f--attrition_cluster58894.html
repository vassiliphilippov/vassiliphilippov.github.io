attrition rate

noun
rate of decrease in group size over time
8.5
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɪʃ.ən reɪt / (AmE), / əˈtrɪʃ.ən reɪt / (BrE)
the rate at which members of a group, organization, or population decrease through resignation, retirement, or death over a specified period

Examples

High attrition rate
A high attrition rate among students can indicate underlying issues at a university.
Improving employee engagement helped reduce the attrition rate in the organization.
The company monitored its attrition rate closely to avoid staff shortages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turnover rate - the rate at which employees leave and are replaced in an organization
