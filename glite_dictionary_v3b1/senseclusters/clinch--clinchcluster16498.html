clinch

verb
secure by bending nail/bolt end
5.6 3.9
Pronunciation: / klɪntʃ / (AmE), / klɪntʃ / (BrE)
to fasten or secure something firmly, especially by bending or flattening a nail, bolt, or using a device or knot

Examples

To clinch a nail
After driving the nails through the board, she clinched them for extra strength.
He clinched the bolt with a hammer to keep it from coming loose.
Make sure to clinch the ends of the nails so the pieces stay tightly connected.
After hammering the boards together, he used the back of the hammer to clinch the nail ends.
In old barns, you'll often see where carpenters clinched the nails to prevent them from working loose.
The metalworker carefully clinched the rivets to ensure a strong bond.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loosen - make less tight or less secure
  • synonym: secure - make something fast or tight
