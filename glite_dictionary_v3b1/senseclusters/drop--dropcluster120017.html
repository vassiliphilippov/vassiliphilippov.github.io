drop

noun
climactic beat/bass moment in music
5.5 0.28/m
Pronunciation: / dr5p / (AmE), / dr52p / (BrE)
(in electronic dance music and pop) the climactic moment when the music shifts from a build-up to the main beat and bass, creating a sudden increase in intensity.

Examples

The music drop
The crowd erupted when the DJ hit the drop after the long build-up.
Producers often spend hours perfecting the tension before the drop to make it powerful.
When the drop hit, the festival field shook with people jumping and cheering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bass drop - a dramatic entry of low-frequency bass lines used to mark the drop; a common synonym in EDM contexts.
  • synonym: beat drop - the moment when the beat and bass enter after a build-up; essentially synonymous with this sense of 'drop'.
  • antonym: build-up - a rising section of music that increases tension and leads into the drop; often contrasted with the drop.
