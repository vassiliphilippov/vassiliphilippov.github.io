gleam

noun
a brief flash of light
2.9 3.77/m
Pronunciation: / …°lim / (AmE), / …°liÀêm / (BrE)
a brief or faint flash or beam of light, especially a reflected one

Examples

A gleam of light
A sudden gleam from the polished mirror caught my eye.
The diamond gave off a bright gleam when it hit the sunlight.
There was a faint gleam of light on the horizon as dawn approached.
A gleam of sunlight caught the edge of the mirror.
She saw a gleam of light in the distance as the sun set.
The jewels sparkled with every gleam of the chandelier.

Synonyms

  • glint
    2.9 2.49/m
    glint is a quick, sharp flash from a shiny surface, while gleam is a softer, brief reflected beam.
  • glimmer
    3.7 0.85/m
    glimmer is a faint, often flickering light seen at a distance, while gleam is a brief, reflected flash.
  • twinkle
    2.3 0.41/m
    twinkle is a rapid, repeated sparkle like stars or lights, while gleam is a single brief or faint reflected beam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flash - a sudden brief burst of bright light
  • synonym: glimmer - a faint or wavering light
  • synonym: glint - a tiny, quick flash of light
  • degree higher: glow - a steady radiance of light or heat without flame
  • synonym: sparkle - a series of small flashes of light
