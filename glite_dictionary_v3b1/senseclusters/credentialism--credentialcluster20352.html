credentialism

noun
emphasis on qualifications
8.6
Pronunciation: /krə.ˈden.ʃə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /krɪ.ˈden.ʃə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /krə.ˈden.ʃə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /krɪ.ˈden.ʃə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice of placing excessive importance on formal qualifications or certificates rather than actual skills or abilities

Examples

Rely on credentialism
Some companies are criticized for their reliance on credentialism when hiring new employees.
The rise of credentialism has made it harder for skilled workers without degrees to find good jobs.
Many believe that credentialism overlooks real talent and practical experience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: meritocracy - a system where advancement is based on ability or achievement
  • synonym: qualification - an official record or certificate showing completion of a course or training
