cut to pieces

verb
to destroy or tear into small parts
5.4 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / k åt t…ô Ààpis…™z / (AmE), / k åt t…ô ÀàpiÀês…™z / (BrE)
to shred, tear, or break something into small parts; also, to cause something or someone to experience great emotional or physical damage.

Examples

To cut to pieces quickly
After the hurricane, the old barn was cut to pieces by the wind and flying debris.
For the salad, the chef quickly cut the vegetables to pieces with expert precision.
Her harsh criticism cut him to pieces, leaving him feeling completely defeated.

Synonyms

  • cut to ribbons
    7.4 0.06/m
    cut to ribbons means a total defeat in a contest or debate, while cut to pieces can be literal tearing or general severe damage.
  • rip to shreds
    6.9 0.29/m
    rip to shreds is used to describe violent tearing or very harsh criticism, while cut to pieces also covers physical and emotional damage more generally.
  • shred
    2.6 11/m
    shred means to cut into small thin strips, often literally, while cut to pieces also implies wider damage or strong emotional harm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tear apart - to rip into pieces; to destroy emotionally or physically
