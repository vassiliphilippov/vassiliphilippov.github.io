bone-deep

adjective
extremely deep or intense
7.0 0.14/m
Pronunciation: / bo änÀàdiÀêp / (AmE), / b…ô änÀàdiÀêp / (BrE)
Extremely deep or intense; affecting one to the core or to the innermost level.

Examples

Bone-deep exhaustion
He felt a bone-deep exhaustion after working for twelve hours straight.
Her sadness was bone-deep after hearing the news.
The cold was so bone-deep that no amount of clothing helped.

Synonyms

  • profound
    6.2 1.05/m
    profound describes very deep thought or feeling and is more formal, while bone-deep shows intense, personal emotion at the core.
  • deep-seated
    4.7 1.95/m
    deep-seated means firmly rooted over time and hard to change, while bone-deep means an intense feeling that reaches one's core.
