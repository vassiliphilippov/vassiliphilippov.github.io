boarding school

noun
school where students live and study
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdɪŋ skuːl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdɪŋ skuːl / (BrE)
A private school where students live as well as study during the term.

Examples

A boarding school student
After her parents moved abroad, she was sent to a boarding school in Switzerland.
He attended a strict boarding school throughout his teenage years.
Many lifelong friendships are formed at boarding schools due to the close living environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: day school - a school where students return home daily after classes
