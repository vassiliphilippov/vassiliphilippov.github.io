scholarly

adjective
relating to scholars or academic study
5.6 10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsk…ëÀê.l…ö.li / (AmE), / Ààsk…íl.…ô.li / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of scholars or academic study; showing knowledge, learning, or academic rigor.

Examples

Scholarly article
He is known for his scholarly approach to literary criticism.
Her scholarly work has been widely cited in academic journals.
The debate took on a scholarly tone, with participants referencing historical texts.

Synonyms

  • erudite
    8.4 1.04/m
    erudite is deeply learned and shows wide, deep knowledge, while scholarly relates to academic study and careful, formal research.
  • academic
    4.2 93/m
    academic relates to schools, theory, or institutions, while scholarly describes work that shows learning and careful academic rigor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: academic - relating to education or scholarship
  • antonym: unacademic - not scholarly or academic
