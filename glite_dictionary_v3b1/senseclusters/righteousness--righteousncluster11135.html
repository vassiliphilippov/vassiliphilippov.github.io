righteousness

noun
moral uprightness; adherence to principles
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪtʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˈraɪtʃəsnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being morally right or justifiable; adherence to moral or ethical principles.

Examples

Moral righteousness
Many admired his righteousness in the face of corruption.
She devoted her life to living in righteousness and helping others.
The novel explores the difference between self-righteousness and true righteousness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
  • antonym: wickedness - the state of being evil or morally wrong
