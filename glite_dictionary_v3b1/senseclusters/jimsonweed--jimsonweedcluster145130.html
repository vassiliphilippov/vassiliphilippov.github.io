jimsonweed

noun
poisonous annual plant with large trumpet flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪmsənˌwid / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪmsənˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall, poisonous annual plant with strong-smelling leaves, large white or violet trumpet-shaped flowers, and spiky seed pods, often found in warm regions.

Examples

Tall jimsonweed plant
Children should be taught to recognize and avoid jimsonweed because of its toxic properties.
In late summer, the jimsonweed bloomed with large, trumpet-shaped flowers by the roadside.
The farmer warned us not to touch the jimsonweed growing near the edge of the field.
