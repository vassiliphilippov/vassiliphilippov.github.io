A musical interval equivalent to two semitones or half steps; the distance between two notes separated by an intervening note.

Examples

A whole step interval
If you play C and then D on a piano, the interval is a whole step.
The melody moves up by a whole step between the two phrases.
Transposing the scale up a whole step changes the key signature.
