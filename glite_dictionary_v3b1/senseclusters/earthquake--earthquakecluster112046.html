earthquake

noun
sudden shaking of the Earth's surface
2.2 26/m

A sudden shaking of the Earth's surface caused by underground movement, often resulting in damage.

Examples

A strong earthquake
Scientists use seismographs to detect the vibrations caused by an earthquake.
Small earthquakes are common in this region, but most go unnoticed.
The city was devastated by a powerful earthquake last year.

Synonyms

  • quake
    5.1 6.42/m
    quake is the everyday short word people use for shaking events, while earthquake is the standard formal term often used in reports.
  • temblor
    8.6 0.25/m
    temblor is a less common US or literary word for a shaking event, while earthquake is the widely used technical and everyday term.
  • seism
    9.5 0.00/m
    seism is a technical word scientists use for ground shaking, while earthquake is the common public term for the same damaging event.
