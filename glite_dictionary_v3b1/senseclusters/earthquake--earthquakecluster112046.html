earthquake

noun
sudden shaking of the Earth's surface
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrθˌkweɪk / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌkweɪk / (BrE)
A sudden shaking of the Earth's surface caused by underground movement, often resulting in damage.

Examples

A strong earthquake
Scientists use seismographs to detect the vibrations caused by an earthquake.
Small earthquakes are common in this region, but most go unnoticed.
The city was devastated by a powerful earthquake last year.

  • synonym: temblor - another term for earthquake
  • synonym: tremor - a small or minor earthquake
