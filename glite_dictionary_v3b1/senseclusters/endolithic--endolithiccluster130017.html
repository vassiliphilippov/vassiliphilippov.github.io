endolithic

adjective
inside rock
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
living or growing inside rock, or within the pores of minerals or stones

Examples

endolithic algae
Certain endolithic algae can survive in the harsh conditions of Antarctica.
Scientists discovered endolithic microorganisms living deep within the porous rocks of the desert.
The study focused on endolithic bacteria that thrive in mineral-rich environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endobiotic - living inside another organism or substrate
  • antonym: epilithic - living on the surface of rocks
