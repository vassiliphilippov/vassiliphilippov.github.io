annelid worm

noun
segmented worm; phylum Annelida
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.ə.lɪd wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈæn.əl.ɪd wɜːm / (BrE)
a worm with a cylindrical, segmented body, belonging to the phylum Annelida

Examples

A annelid worm body
A leech is an example of a parasitic annelid worm found in freshwater habitats.
Some species of annelid worm can regenerate body parts if they are damaged.
The annelid worm plays a vital role in decomposing organic matter in the soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthworm - common type of annelid worm in soil
  • synonym: leech - parasitic or carnivorous annelid worm
