indivisible

adjective
impossible to divide or separate
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈvɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈvɪzɪbəl / (BrE)
impossible to divide or separate into parts; not capable of being divided.

Examples

Indivisible number
Mathematics considers the number 1 to be indivisible by anything but itself.
The national leaders insisted that the country must remain indivisible in the face of crisis.
Their friendship was so strong it seemed almost indivisible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divisible - able to be divided into parts
