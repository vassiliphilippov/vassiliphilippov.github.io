rack up

verb
arrange objects (esp. pool balls) in a rack
Pronunciation: / ræk ʌp / (AmE), / ræk ʌp / (BrE)
to arrange or place (objects, especially balls in billiards or pool) in a rack in preparation for play

Examples

To rack up the balls
After every round, someone needs to rack up the balls for the next break.
Can you rack up the cues and balls while I get drinks?
He offered to rack up the pool balls before the game started.
