négociant

noun
dealer or broker
9.9
Pronunciation: /nə.ˈgoʊ.ʃən/ /; / /nə.ˈgoʊ.ʃɑːn/ (AmE), /nə.ˈgəʊ.ʃən/ /; / /nə.ˈgəʊ.ʃɑːn/ (BrE)
a wine merchant or broker who buys grapes or wine from growers and sells the wine, often under their own label

Examples

A négociant sells wine
The vineyard sold its grapes to a négociant who blended and bottled the wine.
Many small producers rely on a négociant to reach international markets.
The négociant arranged a deal between the local farmers and a large distributor.
The négociant sourced wine from various vineyards and sold it under his own label.
Many small farmers rely on a négociant to bring their produce to larger markets.
As a successful négociant, she built strong relationships with both producers and buyers.
The négociant visited several vineyards to select the best grapes for the new vintage.
Many famous French wines are produced by a négociant rather than the vineyard itself.
The local négociant is known for blending unique wines from different regions.
