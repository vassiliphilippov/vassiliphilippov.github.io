fledgeling

noun
new or inexperienced participant
Pronunciation: / ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflɛdʒlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person, organization, or system that is new, inexperienced, or just beginning in a particular activity or field

Examples

Fledgeling company
As a fledgeling in the industry, she eagerly absorbed advice from her mentors.
Our band was once only a fledgeling, but now we play to sold-out crowds.
The company hired a fledgeling designer who quickly learned the ropes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: novice - a person new to and inexperienced in a job or situation
  • antonym: veteran - a person with much experience in a particular field
