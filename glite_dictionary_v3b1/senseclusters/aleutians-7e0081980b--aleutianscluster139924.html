Aleutians

proper noun
Alaskan island chain in the North Pacific
Pronunciation: / əˈluʃənz / (AmE), / əˈluːʃənz / (BrE)
a chain of islands in the North Pacific Ocean extending southwest from Alaska, forming part of the boundary between the Bering Sea and the Pacific.

Examples

The Aleutians islands
During World War II, battles were fought on the Aleutians between American and Japanese forces.
Many migratory birds nest on the Aleutians each year.
The Aleutians are known for their rugged landscapes and frequent fog.
