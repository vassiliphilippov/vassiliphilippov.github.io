clockwise

adjective, adverb
in the direction of a clock's hands
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑːkˌwaɪz /; / ˈklɑkˌwaɪz / (AmE), / ˈklɒkˌwaɪz / (BrE)
moving or turning in the same direction as the hands of a clock (can be used as an adjective or adverb)

Examples

Move clockwise direction
The children ran clockwise around the playground during the game.
The dancers moved clockwise in a large circle.
Turn the screw clockwise to tighten it.
The dancers moved clockwise around the central fire.
Turn the handle clockwise to tighten the screw.
Water swirled clockwise down the drain in the sink.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticlockwise - moving in the opposite direction to the hands of a clock
  • antonym: counterclockwise - in the opposite direction to the way the hands of a clock move
