borrowed capital

noun
funds a business borrows from others
2.5 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːr.oʊd ˈkæp.ɪ.təl/ (AmE), /ˈbɒr.əʊd ˈkæp.ɪ.təl/ (BrE)
money that a business obtains by borrowing from others, usually to be repaid with interest, such as loans or debentures

Examples

High borrowed capital
A high level of borrowed capital can increase financial risk for a business.
Banks often provide borrowed capital in the form of long-term loans.
The company financed its expansion using a combination of equity and borrowed capital.

Related Terms

  • antonym: equity capital - money provided by owners, not borrowed
