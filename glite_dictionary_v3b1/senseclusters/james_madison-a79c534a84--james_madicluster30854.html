James Madison

proper noun
fourth President of the United States (1751–1836)
4.1
Pronunciation: /dʒeɪmz ˈmæ.dɪ.sən/ /; / /dʒeɪmz ˈmæ.də.sən/ (AmE), /dʒeɪmz ˈmæ.dɪ.sən/ /; / /dʒeɪmz ˈmæ.də.sən/ (BrE)
(1751–1836) The fourth President of the United States, a key contributor to the U.S. Constitution and Bill of Rights, and a leading figure at the Constitutional Convention.

Examples

James Madison the president
James Madison played a central role in drafting the United States Constitution.
Many historians regard James Madison as the 'Father of the Constitution'.
The Federalist Papers were co-authored by James Madison, Alexander Hamilton, and John Jay.
