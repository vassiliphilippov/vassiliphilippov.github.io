elasticity of shear

noun
property allowing recovery after shearing force
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlæstɪˈsɪti əv ʃɪr / (AmE), / ɪˌlæsˈtɪsɪti əv ʃɪə / (BrE)
The property of a material that allows it to regain its original shape after being deformed by a shearing force.

Examples

Elasticity of shear property
Engineers measure the elasticity of shear to determine how well a material can withstand lateral forces.
Metals like steel have a greater elasticity of shear compared to brittle materials such as glass.
Rubber exhibits a high elasticity of shear, making it useful in shock absorbers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shear modulus - measure of a material's rigidity due to shear stress
