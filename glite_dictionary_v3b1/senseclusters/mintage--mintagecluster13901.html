mintage

noun
fee for minting coins
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪn.teɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
a fee paid to a mint by the government for producing a coin

Examples

Mintage fee
Debate arose over how the mintage was calculated in the contract with the Royal Mint.
Historically, the mintage was an important revenue source for sovereigns issuing new coins.
The government agreed to pay the mintage for each batch of coins produced.
