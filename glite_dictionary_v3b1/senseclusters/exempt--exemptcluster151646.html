exempt

verb
officially free from a duty or obligation
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmpt / (BrE)
to officially free someone from an obligation, duty, or rule

Examples

To exempt a student
His long service exempts him from the usual training requirements.
Students with medical conditions can be exempted from physical education classes.
The new law will exempt small businesses from paying the new tax.
Many students were exempted from the final project due to their excellent attendance.
She argued that her condition should exempt her from jury duty.
The new policy exempts small businesses from certain taxes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excuse - to release from a duty or requirement
  • antonym: oblige - to require someone to do something by law or rule
  • antonym: require - to demand as necessary
