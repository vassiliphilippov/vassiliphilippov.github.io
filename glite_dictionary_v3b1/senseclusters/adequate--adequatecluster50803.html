Examples

An adequate amount
His explanation was adequate to satisfy the committee's concerns.
The lighting in this room is just adequate for reading, but not for detailed work.
We had adequate supplies for the entire trip, so no one went without.

Synonyms

  • sufficient
    2.8 40/m
    sufficient means having enough to meet a need, while adequate describes something that is enough but not outstanding.
  • satisfactory
    2.3 7.11/m
    satisfactory is good enough to meet a need or standard, while adequate suggests meeting the minimum without excellence.
  • acceptable
    2.5 40/m
    acceptable meets a required standard and is allowed by others, while adequate focuses on simply being enough for a need.
  • good enough
    1.9 20/m
    good enough is an informal way to say that something meets requirements, while adequate sounds more neutral or formal.
  • passable
    4.4 0.60/m
    passable means barely acceptable or only just good enough, while adequate covers any acceptable level that meets the need.
  • competent
    4.2 2.47/m
    competent describes a person or skill that works well enough, while adequate describes a thing or level that simply meets requirements.
  • enough
    1.0 269/m
    enough shows there is as much as needed or required, while adequate adds a sense that the amount or quality is merely satisfactory.
