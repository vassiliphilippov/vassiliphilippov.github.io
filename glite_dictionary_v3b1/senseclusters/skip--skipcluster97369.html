skip

verb
intentionally not attend
Pronunciation: / skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to intentionally not attend or to pass over something in a sequence

Examples

To skip a class
If you skip another class, you might fail the course.
Many students skipped school on the last day before the holiday.
She decided to skip the meeting and went home instead.
If you skip the introduction, you'll miss important information.
Please skip any questions that don't apply to you.
She accidentally skipped a chapter in the book and got confused.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attend - to be present at an event or place
  • antonym: include - to make something part of a group or set; the opposite of omitting
  • synonym: miss - to fail to be present at something, often unintentionally
  • synonym: omit - to leave out or exclude something, either intentionally or forgetfully
