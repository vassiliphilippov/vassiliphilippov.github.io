echolalia

noun
involuntary repetition of others' words
9.9 8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkoʊˈleɪliə / (AmE), / ˌiːkəʊˈleɪlɪə /; / ˌɛkəʊˈleɪlɪə / (BrE)
the involuntary or automatic repetition of another person's spoken words or sounds, often seen in certain medical or developmental conditions

Examples

Echolalia in autism
In patients with autism, echolalia can be a way of processing language.
The child's echolalia made communication challenging for the teachers.
The doctor noted the presence of echolalia during the psychiatric evaluation.
In some developmental disorders, persistent echolalia can be a symptom to watch for.
Infants often display echolalia as they learn to imitate the sounds of adults.
The speech therapist observed echolalia in several children during the session.

Related Terms

  • synonym: palilalia - involuntary repetition of one's own words or phrases
