slash

verb
cut with a sweeping stroke
Pronunciation: / slæʃ / (AmE), / slæʃ / (BrE)
to cut or make a long, forceful cut with a sharp instrument, such as a knife or machete

Examples

To slash the vines
He slashed at the thick vines with his machete as he moved forward.
She angrily slashed the envelope open with a letter opener.
The knight slashed his sword through the air during the battle.
He threatened to slash the canvas with a knife if the painting wasn't sold.
She accidentally slashed her finger while chopping vegetables.
The branches slashed his arms as he ran through the dense woods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chop - to cut something into pieces with repeated sharp blows
  • synonym: gash - to make a long, deep cut in something
  • synonym: hack - to cut with heavy blows
  • synonym: slash - to cut or wound using a sweeping motion; other sense - reduce drastically
  • antonym: stab - to pierce with a pointed object or weapon
