perspectivism

noun
viewpoint-based theory
9.9
Pronunciation: / pərˈspɛktɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈspɛktɪˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that knowledge or interpretation is always influenced by an individual's point of view

Examples

Perspectivism theory
Nietzsche's perspectivism challenges the idea of a single, absolute truth.
Many modern philosophers discuss perspectivism when debating the nature of reality.
The concept of perspectivism suggests that everyone's understanding is shaped by their unique experiences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectivism - the belief that certain things, especially moral truths, exist independently of human knowledge or perception
  • synonym: relativism - the belief that truth and morality are not absolute but depend on context or perspective
