Synoptics

noun
the first three Gospels of the New Testament
9.7 8.6
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɑptɪks / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒptɪks / (BrE)
the first three books of the New Testament—Matthew, Mark, and Luke—considered together because of their similar accounts of the life of Christ
