gleam

verb
shine with a soft, often wet-looking light
1.9 9.49/m
Pronunciation: / …°lim / (AmE), / …°liÀêm / (BrE)
to shine with a soft, bright, or reflected light

Examples

To gleam brightly
Freshly waxed floors gleam under the hallway lights at night.
Her eyes gleamed with excitement when she saw the surprise.
The dew on the grass made the lawn gleam in the early morning sunlight.
A golden light began to gleam through the clouds at dawn.
A smile gleamed briefly on his face before disappearing.
Her eyes would gleam with excitement whenever she talked about her travels.
Her eyes gleamed with excitement as she opened the present.
Streetlights gleamed on the wet pavement after the rain.
The sun began to gleam through the morning clouds.

Synonyms

  • glisten
    4.1 6.59/m
    glisten means shining softly as if wet or polished, while gleam means a brief soft shine from reflected light.
  • shine
    1.0 24/m
    shine can mean giving or reflecting strong light over time, while gleam names a soft, brief, reflected glow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - not bright or shiny
  • degree higher: flash - to shine suddenly and briefly
  • synonym: glimmer - to shine with a weak or unsteady light
  • synonym: glisten - to shine with a sparkling light, especially as if wet
  • degree higher: glow - to shine steadily with a soft light
  • synonym: shimmer - to shine with a flickering or wavering light
  • synonym: shine - to emit light; to be bright by reflecting or emitting light
