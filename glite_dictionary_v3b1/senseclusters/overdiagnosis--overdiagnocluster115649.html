overdiagnosis

noun
excessive diagnosis
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdaɪˈɑːɡnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədaɪˈɑːɡnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the diagnosis of a disease or condition in people who do not actually need treatment, often because the condition would not cause harm or is just a normal variation

Examples

Routine screening for certain cancers can lead to overdiagnosis and unnecessary treatments.
Doctors are concerned about the risks of overdiagnosis in populations with frequent health checkups.
The study found that overdiagnosis often results in anxiety and unneeded medical procedures.
Routine screening can sometimes lead to overdiagnosis and unnecessary anxiety for patients.
Researchers are studying the impact of overdiagnosis in population-wide health programs.
Doctors warn that overdiagnosis may result in treatments that do more harm than good.
There is growing concern about the overdiagnosis of mild conditions that may not require treatment.
Some experts warn that overdiagnosis can lead to unnecessary anxiety and medical procedures.
The rise in overdiagnosis has prompted calls for more careful evaluation of symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: misdiagnosis - an incorrect diagnosis of a disease or condition
  • synonym: overtreatment - medical treatment that is unnecessary or excessive
  • antonym: underdiagnosis - failing to diagnose a disease or condition when it is present
