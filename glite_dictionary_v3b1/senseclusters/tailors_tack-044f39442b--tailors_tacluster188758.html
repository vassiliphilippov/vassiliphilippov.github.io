tailor's tack

noun
loose sewing stitch for marking fabric
1.0 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈteɪ.lərz tæk/ (AmE), /ˈteɪ.ləz tæk/ (BrE)
a loose, looped stitch used in sewing to temporarily mark fabric for darts, pleats, or other construction details by transferring markings from a pattern to the material

Examples

A tailor's tack stitch
Before sewing the seam, check that each tailor's tack is marking the correct position on both layers.
Make a tailor's tack to accurately transfer the pattern lines to your fabric pieces.
She used a bright colored thread for the tailor's tack so it would be easy to remove after stitching.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basting stitch - a long, loose temporary stitch in sewing for holding fabric together
