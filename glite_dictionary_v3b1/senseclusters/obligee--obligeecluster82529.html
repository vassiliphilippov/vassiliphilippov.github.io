obligee

noun
recipient of obligation
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːblɪˈdʒiː / (AmE), / ˌɒblɪˈdʒiː / (BrE)
a person or organization to whom a legal or contractual obligation is owed

Examples

The obligee in contract
The obligee is entitled to receive payments as specified in the agreement.
If the obligee does not receive the service, they may take legal action.
A contract clearly defines the rights of the obligee and the duties of the obligor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creditor - a person or institution to whom money is owed
  • antonym: obligor - a person or entity who owes an obligation to another
