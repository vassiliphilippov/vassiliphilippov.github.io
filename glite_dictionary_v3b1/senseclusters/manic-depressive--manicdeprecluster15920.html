manic-depressive

adjective
having alternating mania and depression
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (AmE), / ˌmænɪk dɪˈprɛsɪv / (BrE)
suffering from a mental disorder characterized by alternating periods of mania and depression; having bipolar disorder.

Examples

Manic-depressive disorder
His manic-depressive episodes made daily life unpredictable and sometimes exhausting.
She was diagnosed as manic-depressive in her early twenties, before the term 'bipolar disorder' was widely used.
The doctor explained that her symptoms were typical of a manic-depressive patient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bipolar - having or relating to bipolar disorder; characterized by episodes of mania and depression
