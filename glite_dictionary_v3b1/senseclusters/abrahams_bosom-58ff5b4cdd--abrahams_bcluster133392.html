Abraham's bosom

noun
place of rest for the righteous after death
5.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.brəˌhæmz ˈbʊz.əm /; / əˈbreɪhəmz ˈbʊzəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.brəˌhæmz ˈbʊz.əm /; / əˈbreɪəmz ˈbʊzəm / (BrE)
a place of comfort or paradise for the souls of the righteous after death, especially in biblical or Christian tradition

Examples

Abraham's bosom place
He believed that after death, the souls of the faithful would be received into Abraham's bosom.
In ancient texts, Abraham's bosom is depicted as a place of rest for the righteous.
The parable in the Bible describes Lazarus being carried to Abraham's bosom.
After a long illness, the family believed their father had gone to Abraham's bosom.
In his sermon, the priest spoke of the peace found in Abraham's bosom.
The old hymns often mention Abraham's bosom as a symbol of ultimate rest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hell - the place of punishment for the wicked after death
  • synonym: paradise - a place of perfect happiness, often used synonymously for heaven
