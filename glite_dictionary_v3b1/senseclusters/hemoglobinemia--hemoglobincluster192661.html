hemoglobinemia

noun
excess hemoglobin in blood plasma
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˌɡloʊbɪˈniːmiə /; / hiːˌmoʊˌɡloʊbɪˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈniːmiə /; / hiːˌməʊ.ɡləʊ.bɪˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
The presence of an abnormally high concentration of hemoglobin in the blood plasma.

Examples

Prolonged haemoglobinemia can result in kidney damage if not treated promptly.
