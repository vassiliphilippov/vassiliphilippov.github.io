transversion

noun
DNA base mutation
9.9
Pronunciation: / trænzˈvɜːrʒən / (AmE), / trænzˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
a genetic mutation in which a purine is replaced by a pyrimidine or vice versa in DNA or RNA

Examples

A DNA transversion
A single transversion in the DNA sequence can alter the function of a gene.
Scientists observed a transversion mutation that changed a purine to a pyrimidine.
The study compared the rates of transversion and transition mutations in different species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutation - a change in the DNA sequence of an organism
  • antonym: transition - a genetic mutation where a purine is replaced by another purine or a pyrimidine by another pyrimidine
