tweediness

noun
looseness or roughness in fabric texture
8.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtwiÀêd.i.n…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàtwiÀêd.i.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being loose or rough in texture, especially as in tweed cloth

Examples

Fabric tweediness
She preferred the tweediness of traditional fabrics over synthetics for her winter coat.
The tweediness of his jacket gave it a classic, rustic charm.
The interior designer admired the subtle tweediness of the upholstery fabric.
