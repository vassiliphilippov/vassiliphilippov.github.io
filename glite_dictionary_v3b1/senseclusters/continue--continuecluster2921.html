continue

verb
remain in a place, position, or role
1.0 42/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàt…™n.ju / (AmE), / k…ônÀàt…™n.juÀê / (BrE)
to remain in a particular place, position, or situation; to allow something or someone to stay in a state or condition

Examples

Continue at the company
After finishing her degree, she continued at the university as a researcher.
Despite his injury, he continued as team captain for the rest of the season.
She plans to continue in her current role until she retires next year.
Due to budget cuts, we cannot continue all the support staff in their roles.
The club voted to continue the president for another term despite some controversy.
This fabric continues its softness even after repeated washing.

Synonyms

  • remain
    1.0 316/m
    remain is to stay in the same place or state for some time, while continue often means to move forward or carry on.
  • stay
    1.0 422/m
    stay means to remain in a place or condition without leaving, while continue is more general and can cover actions or states.
  • keep
    1.0 452/m
    keep means to cause something or someone to stay in a state or position, while continue can be used without an agent.
  • maintain
    3.1 86/m
    maintain means to actively cause something to stay the same over time, while continue can happen without active effort.
  • keep on
    2.2 1.03/m
    keep on means to carry on doing an action in an informal way, while continue can refer to both actions and states.
  • hold
    1.4 45/m
    hold means to keep a state, position, or rule steady, while continue often means to last or keep going without change.
  • retain
    3.4 26/m
    retain means to keep possession or a quality, often deliberately, while continue focuses on staying or going on in time.
  • stay on
    3.4 6.12/m
    stay on means to remain after others leave or after an expected time, while continue has a broader sense of lasting.
  • persist
    5.2 1.98/m
    persist is formal and suggests lasting despite difficulty or time, while continue is more neutral and suits many situations.
