Burgoyne

proper noun
British general in the American Revolution
9.8
Pronunciation: / bərˈɡɔɪn / (AmE), / bəˈɡɔɪn / (BrE)
John Burgoyne (1722–1792), a British general known for his role in the American Revolutionary War, notably the capture of Fort Ticonderoga and his defeat at the Battle of Saratoga.

Examples

General Burgoyne at Saratoga
General Burgoyne surrendered his army at Saratoga, marking a turning point in the American Revolution.
Historians often debate Burgoyne's strategy during the Saratoga campaign.
The play dramatizes Burgoyne's failed attempt to divide the American colonies.
