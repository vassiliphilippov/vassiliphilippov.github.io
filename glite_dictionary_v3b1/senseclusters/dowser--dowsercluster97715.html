dowser

noun
divining rod for underground water or oil
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊzər / (AmE), / ˈdaʊzə / (BrE)
a forked stick or similar instrument said to detect the presence of underground water, minerals, or oil by dipping or twitching when held over such sources

Examples

A dowser stick
Many people remain skeptical about the effectiveness of a dowser in locating underground water.
She carried a hazel dowser as she walked across the field, looking for a place to drill.
The farmer used a dowser to find a new well on his property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divining rod - a stick or rod used in dowsing to locate underground water or minerals
