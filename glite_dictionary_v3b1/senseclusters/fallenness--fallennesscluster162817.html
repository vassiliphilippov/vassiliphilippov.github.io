fallenness

noun
state of moral decline
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔlənnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlənnəs / (BrE)
the state of having fallen from a higher moral, spiritual, or ideal condition

Examples

Character's fallenness
The novel explores the fallenness of its main character as he struggles with guilt.
Many religious texts discuss the fallenness of humanity and the need for redemption.
Her poetry often reflects on the fallenness of society and the loss of innocence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruption - the state of being morally degraded
  • antonym: innocence - the state of being morally pure or free from guilt
