charge

verb
to excite or rouse emotionally
4.1 4.72/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
to fill a place or person with strong emotion or energy

Examples

To charge the room with energy
Her passionate words charged the room with excitement and anticipation.
Music has the power to charge an audience like nothing else.
The coach's speech charged the team for the last quarter of the game.
Her words charged the meeting with a sense of urgency.
The air in the courtroom seemed charged with anticipation as the verdict was about to be read.
The atmosphere was charged with excitement before the performance started.
