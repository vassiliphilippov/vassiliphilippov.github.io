bridge the gap

verb
reduce differences or connect groups/ideas
3.9
Pronunciation: / brɪdʒ ðə gæp / (AmE), / brɪdʒ ðə ɡap / (BrE)
to make it easier for two groups, ideas, or situations to connect, communicate, or understand each other; to reduce differences between them

Examples

To bridge the gap between groups
An open dialogue can help bridge the gap between management and employees.
Efforts are underway to bridge the gap in access to healthcare across the country.
The new training program aims to bridge the gap between experienced and junior staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: connect - bring together or join
  • synonym: reconcile - restore friendly relations or make compatible
  • antonym: widen the gap - increase differences or separation between groups/ideas
