Synonyms

  • fatigue
    4.6 1.06/m
    fatigue is a clinical long-lasting exhaustion often from strain or illness, while lethargy is a general lack of energy and activity.
  • tiredness
    1.0 1.02/m
    tiredness is the everyday feeling of being worn out after work or activity, while lethargy suggests deeper inactivity or illness.
  • lassitude
    9.3 0.33/m
    lassitude is formal tiredness often used in medical or literary context, while lethargy is common for general lack of energy.
  • listlessness
    6.8 0.19/m
    listlessness is a lack of interest and energy that shows in behaviour, while lethargy focuses on low energy and inactivity.
  • weariness
    3.1 2.41/m
    weariness is a strong tired feeling after hard work or travel, while lethargy is a general slow lack of energy.
  • sluggishness
    4.4 0.26/m
    sluggishness is moving or working slowly and without energy, while lethargy is a broader state of low energy or inactivity.
  • torpor
    8.9 0.38/m
    torpor is a deep, often medical-like inactivity with very low response, while lethargy can be lighter, sometimes temporary lack of energy.
  • languor
    8.3 0.29/m
    languor is a dreamy, sometimes pleasant tired stillness, while lethargy is an unhealthy or stronger loss of energy and activity.
  • enervation
    9.1 0.01/m
    enervation is a formal, extreme weakening of strength and power, while lethargy is a milder state of low energy or inactivity.
