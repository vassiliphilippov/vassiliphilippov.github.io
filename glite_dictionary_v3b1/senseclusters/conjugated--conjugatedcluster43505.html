conjugated

adjective
joined together in pairs or groups
5.4 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑndʒəˌɡeɪtɪd /; / ˈkɑːn.dʒəˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒʊɡeɪtɪd /; / ˈkɒn.dʒəˌɡeɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
joined or combined with something else, especially in chemistry or biology (such as molecules or compounds joined together)

Examples

Conjugated molecules
Many medicines are conjugated with other molecules to improve effectiveness.
Some bacteria move using conjugated flagella that operate in pairs.
The conjugated double bonds in the molecule allow it to absorb visible light.
A conjugated protein consists of a protein joined with a non-protein component.
Bilirubin in the body may exist in unconjugated or conjugated forms.
Insulin can be attached to other molecules, creating a conjugated hormone.
