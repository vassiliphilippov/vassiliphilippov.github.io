SIDS

noun
unexplained infant death during sleep
6.7
Pronunciation: /sɪdz/ /; / /ˈe.saɪ.diː.es/ (AmE), /sɪdz/ /; / /ˈe.saɪ.diː.es/ (BrE)
sudden and unexplained death of an apparently healthy infant, typically during sleep; also called sudden infant death syndrome

Examples

SIDS in infants
Doctors are still researching the causes of SIDS in healthy babies.
She founded a charity after losing her son to SIDS.
The risk of SIDS can be reduced by placing infants on their backs to sleep.
