tolerationism

noun
policy of tolerance
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑːləˈreɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌtɒləˈreɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the principle or practice of allowing and accepting beliefs or practices different from one's own

Examples

Tolerationism principle
The government adopted tolerationism to ensure all religious groups could practice freely.
Advocates of tolerationism believe that diversity strengthens society.
The rise of tolerationism led to greater acceptance of different cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intolerance - unwillingness to accept different views or behaviors
  • synonym: tolerance - willingness to accept different views or behaviors
