bacteriochlorophyll

noun
pigment in photosynthetic bacteria
Pronunciation: / bækˌtɪrioʊˈklɔrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / bækˌtɪəriəʊˈklɔːrəˌfɪl / (BrE)
A pigment found in photosynthetic bacteria, similar to chlorophyll in higher plants but differing in structure and light absorption properties.

Examples

Bacteriochlorophyll pigment
Bacteriochlorophyll allows certain bacteria to harvest light energy in low-light environments.
Scientists compared the spectral properties of bacteriochlorophyll and plant chlorophyll to study evolutionary differences.
The green color of some hot spring mats is due to bacteriochlorophyll in photosynthetic bacteria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chlorophyll - the green pigment in plants that absorbs light for photosynthesis
