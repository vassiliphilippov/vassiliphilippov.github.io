protogynous

adjective
female organs mature first
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈtɑdʒɪnəs /; / proʊˈtɑːdʒɪnəs / (AmE), / prəʊˈtɒdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
describing a plant or animal in which the female phase comes before the male phase (for example, a plant whose female parts mature before the male parts, or an animal that starts life as female and may later become male)

Examples

Protogynous fish species
Some species of fish are protogynous, starting life as females and later changing to males.
The protogynous flowers of this plant help prevent self-pollination and encourage cross-pollination.
Researchers observed that the protogynous timing in these blossoms increased genetic diversity.
Many wrasse species are protogynous, with individuals changing from female to male as they grow.
In some coral reef fish, a protogynous life cycle helps maintain population balance.
Researchers observed that the protogynous group began as all females before some transitioned to males.
