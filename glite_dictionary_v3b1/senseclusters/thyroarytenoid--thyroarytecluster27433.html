thyroarytenoid

adjective
laryngeal muscle
Pronunciation: / ˌθaɪroʊˌærɪˈtɛnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌθaɪərəʊˌærɪˈtɛnɔɪd / (BrE)
relating to the muscle or structures connecting the thyroid and arytenoid cartilages in the larynx

Examples

Thyroarytenoid muscle function
The thyroarytenoid muscle plays a key role in adjusting vocal cord tension.
Damage to the thyroarytenoid region can affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
Surgeons must be careful when operating near the thyroarytenoid structures in the larynx.
