incog

noun
private browsing mode
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɑɡ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɒɡ / (BrE)
a private or hidden mode, especially in computing, where a user's activity is not recorded or visible to others

Examples

Incog mode online
He switched to incog to check his bank account on a shared computer.
Many people use incog when shopping for gifts online to keep it a secret.
The browser's incog feature helps protect your privacy from other users of the same device.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incognito - in a way that conceals one's identity or activity
  • synonym: private mode - a browser setting that does not save browsing history
  • antonym: public mode - a standard browsing mode where activity is recorded
