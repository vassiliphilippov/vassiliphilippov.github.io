photolithography

noun
printing process using photographic plates
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊlɪˈθɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊlɪˈθɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a process of printing or creating patterns using plates or masks made from photographic images, especially in lithography and semiconductor manufacturing

Examples

Photolithography process
Researchers are working to improve the resolution of photolithography for nanoscale manufacturing.
The development of photolithography revolutionized the production of microchips in the electronics industry.
Traditional printing methods have been largely replaced by photolithography in many technical applications.
