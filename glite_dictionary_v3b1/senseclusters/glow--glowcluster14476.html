glow

verb
shine strongly, often with heat
1.0 1.2
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊ / (AmE), / ɡləʊ / (BrE)
to shine or emit a steady, soft light, especially as a result of heat or chemical reaction

Examples

To glow in the dark
As the sun set, the city's lights began to glow against the darkening sky.
Her cheeks seemed to glow with warmth from the fireplace.
The campfire continued to glow long after the flames had died down.
Her cheeks glowed in the candlelight as she smiled softly.
The embers glowed long after the fire had died down.
The lanterns glowed gently along the pathway, providing a warm atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burn - to be on fire or give off heat and light
  • antonym: flare - to burn with a sudden intensity and strong, brief light
  • synonym: shine - to emit light
