red giant star

noun
large, old luminous star with reddish color
9.4 5.1
Pronunciation: /red ˈdʒaɪ.ənt stɑːr/ /; / /red ˈdʒaɪnt stɑːr/ (AmE), /red ˈdʒaɪ.ənt stɑː/ /; / /red ˈdʒaɪnt stɑː/ (BrE)
A large, old star that shines brightly with a reddish color, has a relatively low surface temperature, and a diameter much greater than that of the sun.

Examples

A bright red giant star
Astronomers study the life cycle of a red giant star to understand the future of our own sun.
Betelgeuse is a well-known example of a red giant star visible to the naked eye.
When a star runs out of hydrogen, it expands into a red giant star before shedding its outer layers.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: main sequence star - a star in the primary stage of its lifecycle, before expanding into a red giant
  • degree higher: supergiant star - an even larger and more luminous star than a red giant
  • degree higher: white dwarf - the small, hot stellar remnant that a red giant becomes
