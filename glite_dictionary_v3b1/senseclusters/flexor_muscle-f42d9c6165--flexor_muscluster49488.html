flexor muscle

noun
muscle that bends a joint
3.2 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈflɛksər ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈflɛksə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a skeletal muscle that, when contracted, bends a joint or decreases the angle between bones

Examples

The flexor muscle contracts
After his injury, he had difficulty contracting the flexor muscle in his leg.
Physical therapy exercises can strengthen the flexor muscle group to improve mobility.
The flexor muscle in your arm helps you bend your elbow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extensor muscle - a muscle whose contraction straightens a limb or increases the angle between body parts
