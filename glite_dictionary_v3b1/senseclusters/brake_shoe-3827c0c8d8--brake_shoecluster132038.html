brake shoe

noun
vehicular part pressing against brake drum
9.9 3.9
Pronunciation: /breɪk ʃuː/ (AmE), /breɪk ʃuː/ (BrE)
a curved block that presses against a vehicle's brake drum to slow or stop the wheel's rotation

Examples

A brake shoe wears
The mechanic said my car's brake shoe needs to be replaced due to excessive wear.
When you press the brake pedal, the brake shoe creates friction against the drum to slow the car.
Worn-out brake shoes can reduce braking efficiency and increase stopping distance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brake pad - flat component pressed against a brake disc to slow a vehicle
