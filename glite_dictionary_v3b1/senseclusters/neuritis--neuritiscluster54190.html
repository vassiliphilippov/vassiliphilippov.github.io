neuritis

noun
inflammation of a nerve
6.7 6.1
Pronunciation: /nʊ.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ /; / /njuː.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ (AmE), /njʊ.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ /; / /nə.ˈraɪ.tɪs/ (BrE)
Inflammation of a nerve, usually causing pain, tenderness, and sometimes loss of function.

Examples

Neuritis causes pain
Some cases of neuritis can result in temporary loss of muscle function.
The patient was diagnosed with neuritis after complaining of persistent pain in her arm.
Vitamin B deficiency is one of the possible causes of neuritis in adults.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neuralgia - intense, typically intermittent pain along the course of a nerve
  • synonym: neuropathy - disease or dysfunction of one or more peripheral nerves
