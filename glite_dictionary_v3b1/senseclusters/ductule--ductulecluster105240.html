ductule

noun
very small duct (esp. in the body)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌk.tʃuːl / (AmE), / ˈdʌk.tjuːl / (BrE)
A very small duct, especially one in the body that conveys fluids such as bile or tears.

Examples

Small ductules in body
Damage to the bile ductules can lead to liver disease.
The liver contains numerous ductules that merge to form larger ducts.
The tear glands release fluid through tiny ductules around the eyes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: duct - a tube or channel that carries fluid or gas in the body or a machine
