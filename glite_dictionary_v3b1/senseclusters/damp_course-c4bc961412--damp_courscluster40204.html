damp course

noun
impermeable layer in a building's foundation to block moisture
Pronunciation: / dæmp kɔrs / (AmE), / dæmp kɔːs / (BrE)
A layer of impermeable material built into the foundation walls of a building, near the ground, to stop moisture from rising into the structure.

Examples

A damp course layer
It is essential to install a damp course to protect the house from rising moisture.
Modern construction regulations require a damp course in all new buildings.
The builder noticed that the damp course was missing, which caused problems with mold.
