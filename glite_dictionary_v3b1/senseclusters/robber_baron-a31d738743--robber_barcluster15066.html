robber baron

noun
unscrupulous wealthy businessman
5.3 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈrɑː.bər ˈbær.ən/ /; / /ˈrɑː.bər ˈber.ən/ (AmE), /ˈrɒb.ə ˈbær.ən/ (BrE)
a wealthy and powerful businessman who uses unethical or unscrupulous methods to gain wealth, especially one from the late 19th or early 20th centuries

Examples

A robber baron businessman
During the Gilded Age, several robber barons amassed fortunes by manipulating markets and politicians.
Many viewed the railroad magnate as a robber baron who exploited his workers for personal gain.
Some historians debate whether Carnegie should be labeled a robber baron or a great philanthropist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: philanthropist - a person who seeks to promote the welfare of others, especially by donating money
  • synonym: tycoon - a wealthy, powerful business person
