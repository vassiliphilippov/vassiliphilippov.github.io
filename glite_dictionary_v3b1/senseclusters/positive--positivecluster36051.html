The primary form of an adjective or adverb, expressing a quality without comparison or degree.

Examples

Positive form
'Quick' is the positive form, while 'quicker' is comparative and 'quickest' is superlative.
In the adjective 'happy', the positive is simply 'happy', without any comparison.
The grammar book explains how to change an adjective from the positive to the comparative form.
