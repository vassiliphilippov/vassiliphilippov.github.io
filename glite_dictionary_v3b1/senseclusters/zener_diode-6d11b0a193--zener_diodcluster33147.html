Zener diode

noun
semiconductor voltage-regulating diode
Pronunciation: / ˈziːnɚ ˈdaɪˌoʊd / (AmE), / ˈziːnə ˈdaɪəʊd / (BrE)
A semiconductor device that allows current to flow in the reverse direction when a specific, well-defined voltage is reached, commonly used for voltage regulation.

Examples

A Zener diode voltage
By connecting a Zener diode in reverse, you can regulate the output voltage precisely.
He replaced the faulty Zener diode to stabilize the power supply in the device.
The Zener diode is essential for protecting sensitive circuits from voltage spikes.

  • synonym: diode - an electronic component that conducts current in one direction
