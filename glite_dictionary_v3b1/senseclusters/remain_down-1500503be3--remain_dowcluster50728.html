remain down

verb
stay down during referee's count (boxing)
8.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªËˆmeÉªn daÊŠn / (AmE), / rÉªËˆmeÉªn daÊŠn / (BrE)
(of a boxer) to stay on the ground without standing up while the referee counts to ten, resulting in being counted out of the match.

Examples

To remain down after count
After the heavy blow, the boxer chose to remain down as the referee counted to ten.
Despite the crowd's encouragement, he decided to remain down and lose the match by knockout.
The fighter could not get up and remained down until the count was finished.

Synonyms

  • take the count
    8.8 0.00/m
    take the count is staying on the ring floor through the referee's ten count after a hit, while remain down is staying until counted out.
  • knock down for the count
    8.2 0.00/m
    knock down for the count describes being hit and left unable to continue in a fight, while remain down means staying on the ground until counted out.

Related Terms
