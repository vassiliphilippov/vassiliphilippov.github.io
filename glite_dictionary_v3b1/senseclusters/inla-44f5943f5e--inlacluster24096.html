INLA

noun
Bayesian inference method (INLA)
Pronunciation: / aɪ ɛn ɛl eɪ / (AmE), / aɪ ɛn ɛl eɪ / (BrE)
Acronym for Integrated Nested Laplace Approximations, a fast method for approximate Bayesian inference, especially for latent Gaussian models.

Examples

INLA method
Researchers often use INLA to fit spatial models faster than traditional MCMC approaches.
During the workshop, she demonstrated how INLA provides quick approximate posterior estimates for large datasets.
They compared results from INLA and found them consistent with longer-running Bayesian simulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: MCMC - Markov chain Monte Carlo, a class of algorithms for sampling from probability distributions used in Bayesian computation.
  • synonym: latent Gaussian model - A statistical model where observations depend on unobserved Gaussian processes; a common target for INLA methods.
