bird of night

noun
nocturnal bird of prey, esp. owl
3.6
Pronunciation: / bɝd əv naɪt / (AmE), / bɜːd əv naɪt / (BrE)
A nocturnal bird of prey with a hawk-like beak and claws, large head, and front-facing eyes, especially an owl.

Examples

The bird of night flies
Legends often feature the bird of night as a mysterious messenger in the darkness.
She spotted a bird of night perched silently atop the old fence post.
The eerie call of the bird of night echoed through the forest.
