tear out

verb
remove forcefully or extract
1.0
Pronunciation: / tɛr aʊt / (AmE), / tɛə aʊt / (BrE)
to remove something forcefully or with effort; to extract something, sometimes in an abstract sense

Examples

To tear out pages
He tried to tear out the secrets hidden in the document.
She had to tear out several pages of her notebook.
The dentist will tear out the damaged tooth tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extract - to remove or take out, especially by effort or force
  • synonym: pull out - to remove something by pulling, often with force
