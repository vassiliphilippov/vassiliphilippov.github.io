muck

noun
thick, sticky, often dirty matter
Pronunciation: / mʌk / (AmE), / mʌk / (BrE)
any wet, dirty, or unpleasant substance, especially manure or animal waste

Examples

Sticky muck
Please wipe that muck off the kitchen floor before it dries.
The boots were covered in muck after walking through the swamp.
Thick muck clogged the drain, making the water back up.
After cleaning the stable, my boots were covered in muck.
Heavy rains turned the barnyard into a sea of muck.
The farmer spread the muck over the fields as fertilizer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean, free from dirt or impurities
  • synonym: filth - disgusting dirt or refuse
  • synonym: manure - animal dung used for fertilizing land
  • synonym: sludge - thick, soft, wet mud or a similar viscous mixture
