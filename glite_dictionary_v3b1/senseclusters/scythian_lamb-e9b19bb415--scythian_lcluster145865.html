Scythian lamb

noun
legendary Asiatic plant or tree fern
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪθiən læm / (AmE), / ˈsɪθiən lam / (BrE)
A legendary Asiatic plant, sometimes identified as a tree fern, believed to produce lambs as fruit and occasionally referred to for its dense matted hairs used as a styptic.

Examples

The Scythian lamb plant
Early botanists sometimes identified certain ferns with the legendary Scythian lamb.
In medieval literature, the Scythian lamb was believed to be both plant and animal.
The Scythian lamb fascinated explorers searching for wonders of the natural world.
