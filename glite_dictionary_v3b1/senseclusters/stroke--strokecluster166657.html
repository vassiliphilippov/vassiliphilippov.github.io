stroke

verb
treat carefully to win favor
4.3
Pronunciation: / stroʊk / (AmE), / strəʊk / (BrE)
to treat someone carefully or with gentle attention in order to maintain their favor or approval

Examples

To stroke the boss
She tried to stroke the manager by always agreeing with his ideas.
Some politicians know exactly how to stroke the right people to get ahead.
You don't have to stroke his ego every time you see him.
