Polyporus tenuiculus

noun
a fungus species with kidney-shaped cap
Pronunciation: / pəˌlaɪˌpɔrəs ˌtɛniˈkjuləs / (AmE), / pɒˌlɪpərəs ˌtɛnjʊˈkjuːləs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a whitish, kidney-shaped cap and elongated pores, known for causing white rot in dead hardwoods.

Examples

Polyporus tenuiculus fungus species
Polyporus tenuiculus is commonly found growing on decaying hardwood logs in tropical forests.
Scientists studied the effect of Polyporus tenuiculus on the decomposition of fallen tree branches.
The distinctive pores on the underside of Polyporus tenuiculus help differentiate it from similar fungi.
