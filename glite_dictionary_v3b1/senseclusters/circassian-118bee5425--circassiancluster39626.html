Circassian

noun
member of an ethnic group from northwestern Caucasia
9.9
Pronunciation: / sərˈkæʃən / (AmE), / səˈkæsiən / (BrE)
a member of a mostly Sunni Muslim ethnic group native to northwestern Caucasia

Examples

Circassian people

Many Circassians migrated to the Ottoman Empire in the nineteenth century.
The Circassian language belongs to the Northwest Caucasian language family.
Many Circassians were displaced during the 19th century and settled in the Middle East.
She is proud of her Circassian heritage and often shares stories about their culture.
The Circassian community has preserved its traditions for centuries in the Caucasus.
