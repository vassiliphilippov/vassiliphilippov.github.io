short-handed

adjective
not enough staff or helpers
1.8 3.6
Pronunciation: /ʃɔːrt ˈhæn.dɪd/ /; / /ʃɔːrt ˈhæn.dəd/ (AmE), /ʃɔːt ˈhæn.dɪd/ /; / /ʃɔːt ˈhæn.dəd/ (BrE)
lacking enough workers or helpers; having fewer people available than needed for a task or situation.

Examples

Short-handed team
Due to the flu outbreak, the office is short-handed this week and unable to process orders quickly.
The restaurant was so short-handed last night that the manager had to help serve tables.
They found themselves short-handed during the busy holiday season and had to hire temporary staff.
