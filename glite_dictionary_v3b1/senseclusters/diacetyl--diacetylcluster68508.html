diacetyl

noun
buttery flavor compound
8.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæsɪtil / (AmE), / daɪˈæsɪtiːl / (BrE)
a chemical compound with a buttery flavor, used as a food flavoring and in industry; it can be harmful if inhaled

Examples

Artificial diacetyl flavor
The artificial butter flavor in popcorn often contains diacetyl.
Workers in some factories are exposed to diacetyl during the production of food flavorings.
Scientists study the effects of diacetyl on lung health in industrial settings.
The buttery flavor in microwave popcorn often comes from added diacetyl.
Inhaling high concentrations of diacetyl can lead to serious lung problems for factory workers.
Food scientists monitor the levels of diacetyl in dairy products to ensure safety.
