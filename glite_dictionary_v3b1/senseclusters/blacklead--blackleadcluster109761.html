blacklead

verb
to coat with graphite to give a shine
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌlɛd / (AmE), / ˈblækˌlɛd / (BrE)
to coat or polish (especially a stove or grate) with graphite, typically to give a shiny black finish

Examples

To blacklead a stove
After blackleading the stove, it gleamed and looked almost new.
She used to blacklead the old kitchen stove every Saturday morning.
The housekeeper blackleaded the fireplace grate as part of her daily chores.
