ideogrammatic

adjective
using idea symbols
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiˌoʊɡrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiəʊɡrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to symbols or writing systems where characters represent ideas or concepts rather than sounds

Examples

Ideogrammatic writing system
Chinese characters are often described as ideogrammatic because they represent ideas rather than sounds.
The ancient script was considered ideogrammatic, making it difficult to pronounce without knowing the meaning.
Researchers studied the ideogrammatic nature of early writing systems in Mesopotamia.
The ancient script is highly ideogrammatic, with each symbol standing for a whole concept.
Her artwork features ideogrammatic shapes that suggest universal ideas rather than specific objects.
Some modern emojis can be considered ideogrammatic because they represent feelings or actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alphabetic - relating to an alphabet, a set of letters representing sounds
  • antonym: phonetic - relating to sounds in speech or writing systems based on sounds
  • synonym: pictographic - using pictures or symbols to represent words or ideas
