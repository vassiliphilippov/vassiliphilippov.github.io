instill

verb
impart gradually
3.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈstɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɪl / (BrE)
to gradually cause someone to have a feeling, idea, value, or quality

Examples

To instill good values
Parents try to instill good values in their children from an early age.
Teachers can instill a love of reading through engaging stories and discussions.
The coach worked hard to instill confidence in his team.
Her teacher hoped to instill a love of reading in all her students.
Parents often try to instill good manners in their children from an early age.
The coach works hard to instill confidence and discipline in the team.
Her parents hoped to instill a sense of responsibility in her from a young age.
Leaders must instill trust and integrity within their teams.
The teacher worked hard to instill confidence in his students through constant encouragement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eradicate - to remove or destroy something completely
  • synonym: imbue - to inspire or permeate with a feeling or quality
  • synonym: implant - to fix an idea, attitude, etc. firmly in someone's mind
  • synonym: inculcate - to teach by persistent instruction
  • synonym: infuse - to fill or pervade with a particular quality
