intrude on

verb
enter or interfere without permission
3.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruːd ɑn / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːd ɒn / (BrE)
to enter or interfere with a place, situation, or someone's affairs without permission or welcome

Examples

To intrude on privacy
I don't want to intrude on your conversation, so I'll wait outside.
Please let me know if I intrude on your personal space.
Reporters were criticized for trying to intrude on the grieving family's privacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encroach on - to gradually intrude on or go beyond acceptable limits
  • antonym: respect - to regard the boundaries or privacy of others and not intrude
  • synonym: violate - to breach or fail to respect (rules, boundaries, privacy, etc.)
