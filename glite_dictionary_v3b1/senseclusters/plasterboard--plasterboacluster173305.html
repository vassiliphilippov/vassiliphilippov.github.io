plasterboard

noun

a building material consisting of a rigid board with a gypsum plaster core sandwiched between layers of paper or fiberboard, used for making interior walls and ceilings

Examples

A plasterboard wall
After the flood, we had to replace the damaged plasterboard in the basement.
The builders installed plasterboard to create smooth interior walls throughout the house.
Using plasterboard is a quick and efficient way to finish room partitions.

Synonyms

  • gypsum board
    3.5 0.03/m
    gypsum board is the standard technical name for panels made from gypsum and paper, while plasterboard is the British term for the same material.
  • drywall
    2.9 1.98/m
    drywall is the common American word for gypsum panels used indoors, while plasterboard is the more British or formal term.
  • Sheetrock
    3.5 0.45/m
    Sheetrock is a brand name often used to mean gypsum wall panels in everyday speech, while plasterboard is the general material name.
  • wallboard
    3.9 0.29/m
    wallboard is a broader term for any flat interior panel made from various materials, while plasterboard refers specifically to gypsum panels.
