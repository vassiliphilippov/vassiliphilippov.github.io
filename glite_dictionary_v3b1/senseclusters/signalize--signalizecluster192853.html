signalize

verb
to point out clearly; make notable
5.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪg.nə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈsɪg.nə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to make something notable or draw special attention to it; to highlight or mark out

Examples

To signalize an achievement
Her achievements were signalized by the award of a prestigious medal.
The director signalized the new policy changes at the meeting.
The historian signalized the importance of this event in his speech.
The architect used a striking entrance to signalize the importance of the building.
The ceremony was intended to signalize the nation's independence.
They signalized their success with a grand celebration.
He hoped his generous donation would signalise his commitment to the cause.
The award signalised her achievements in the field of science.
The organization decided to signalise the anniversary with a special celebration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: highlight - to emphasize or make something stand out
  • antonym: obscure - to make something less visible or clear
  • synonym: signalize - make conspicuous or noteworthy; US spelling
