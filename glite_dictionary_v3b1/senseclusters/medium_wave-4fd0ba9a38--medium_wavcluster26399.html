medium wave

noun
radio wave band used for AM broadcasting
5.6 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈmiː.dɪ.əm weɪv/ (AmE), /ˈmiː.dɪ.əm weɪv/ (BrE)
A radio wave with a wavelength between 100 and 1000 meters, corresponding to frequencies from 300 kilohertz to 3000 kilohertz, commonly used for AM radio broadcasting.

Examples

The medium wave band
AM radio in many countries is broadcast using the medium wave band.
Reception on medium wave tends to be better at night due to atmospheric conditions.
Some portable radios allow you to switch between FM and medium wave frequencies.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: short wave - radio wave with a wavelength shorter than medium wave, used for long-distance broadcasting
