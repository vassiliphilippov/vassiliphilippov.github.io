tensor tympani

noun
small middle ear muscle protecting the eardrum
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛn.sɚ tɪmˈpaɪ.naɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.sə tɪmˈpæ.ni / (BrE)
a small muscle in the middle ear that contracts to dampen loud sounds and protect the eardrum

Examples

The tensor tympani muscle
Damage to the tensor tympani can result in increased sensitivity to sound.
During reflex action, the tensor tympani contracts to protect the eardrum from loud sounds.
The tensor tympani muscle helps to reduce the impact of sudden loud noises on the ear.
