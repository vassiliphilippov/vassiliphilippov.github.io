sensory nerve

noun
nerve transmitting sensory information
1.7 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.sə.rɪ nɜːrv/ (AmE), /ˈsen.sə.rɪ nɜːv/ (BrE)
A nerve that carries signals from sensory receptors toward the central nervous system.

Examples

A sensory nerve signal
A doctor explained that the pain was due to irritation of a facial sensory nerve.
Damage to a sensory nerve can result in numbness or tingling in the affected area.
The sensory nerve endings in your skin help you feel pain and pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: motor nerve - a nerve that carries impulses from the central nervous system to muscles or glands
