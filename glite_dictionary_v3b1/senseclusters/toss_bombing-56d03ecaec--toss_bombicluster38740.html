toss bombing

noun
bombing method involving a pull-up before release
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /tɔːs ˈbɑːm.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /tɒs ˈbɒm.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
A technique of aerial bombing in which the aircraft approaches the target at low altitude and sharply pulls up before releasing the bomb to achieve greater range or to avoid flying directly over the target.

Examples

Toss bombing technique
Toss bombing allows aircraft to strike targets accurately without flying directly overhead.
During the drill, the squadron executed precise toss bombing maneuvers over the training range.
The pilots practiced toss bombing to minimize their exposure to enemy air defenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dive bombing - A bombing method where the bomber dives towards the target before release
