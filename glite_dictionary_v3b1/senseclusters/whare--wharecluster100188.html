whare

noun
New Zealand house
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːreɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɑːreɪ / (BrE)
a house or building in New Zealand, especially a traditional Maori house used for living or ceremonies

Examples

A whare house
They invited us to their whare for dinner after the school celebration.
The traditional Māori whare was built from wood and flax.
Many tourists visit a reconstructed whare to learn about local culture.
The elders welcomed us into the whare for the traditional ceremony.
Each village has a central whare where important meetings are held.
Visitors are often invited to sleep in the whare during special occasions.
