reeducative

adjective
teaching again
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɛdʒʊˌkeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛdjuːkətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the process of teaching someone again, especially to change behavior, skills, or attitudes

Examples

A reeducative program
The hospital developed a reeducative program to help patients regain their mobility after surgery.
Teachers used reeducative methods to help students overcome negative habits.
A reeducative approach can be effective in changing long-standing behaviors.

  • synonym: rehabilitative - helping someone return to health or normal life through training or therapy
