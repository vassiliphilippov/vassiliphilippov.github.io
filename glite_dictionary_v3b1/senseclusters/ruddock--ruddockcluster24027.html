ruddock

noun
archaic word for robin
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌdək / (AmE), / ˈrʌdək / (BrE)
an old or poetic word for a robin, a small songbird with a red breast

Examples

A ruddock bird
In the quiet garden, a ruddock perched on the stone wall and sang.
The poet described the cheerful ruddock as a symbol of hope in winter.
Long ago, people believed the ruddock brought good luck to their homes.
