Ustilago maydis

noun
fungus causing corn smut disease
9.9
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈstɪl.ə.goʊ ˈmaɪ.dɪs/ /; / /ʌ.ˈstɪl.ə.goʊ ˈmaɪ.dɪs/ (AmE), /juː.ˈstɪl.ə.gəʊ ˈmaɪ.dɪs/ /; / /ʌ.ˈstɪl.ə.gəʊ ˈmaɪ.dɪs/ (BrE)
a fungal species that infects maize, causing greyish-white swellings (corn smut) which eventually burst to reveal a mass of black spores

Examples

Ustilago maydis fungus infection
Ustilago maydis infects the ears of corn, producing swellings that later turn black and dusty.
In some Mexican cuisine, Ustilago maydis is harvested as a delicacy called huitlacoche.
Researchers are studying Ustilago maydis for its role in plant pathology and possible biotechnological uses.
