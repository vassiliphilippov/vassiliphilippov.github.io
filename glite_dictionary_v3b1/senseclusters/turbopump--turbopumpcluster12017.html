turbopump

noun
turbine-driven pump
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝːboʊˌpʌmp / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌpʌmp / (BrE)
a pump powered by a turbine, commonly used to move fluids such as fuel in engines, especially in rockets

Examples

A turbopump in rockets
The rocket's turbopump failed during the launch sequence, causing the mission to be aborted.
Engineers inspected the turbopump for signs of overheating after the test run.
A malfunction in the turbopump can lead to a dangerous drop in fuel pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressor - a machine that increases the pressure of a gas
  • synonym: pump - a device for moving fluids
