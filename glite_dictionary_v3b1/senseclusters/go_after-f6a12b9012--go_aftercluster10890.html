Synonyms

  • pursue
    3.5 71/m
    pursue is a formal word for trying to get or follow something persistently, while go after is more common everyday speech.
  • chase after
    1.9 0.11/m
    chase after is a casual phrase for running or moving quickly to catch someone, while go after covers both catching and trying.
  • give chase
    4.1 0.56/m
    give chase means to begin actively chasing someone or something immediately, while go after can mean either chasing or trying to obtain.
  • go for
    2.5 6.77/m
    go for is used when trying to obtain something or make a choice, while go after emphasizes pursuing or following.
  • seek
    1.0 90/m
    seek means to search or try to gain something in a planned way, while go after often suggests more active following.
  • seek out
    3.0 10/m
    seek out means look for a specific person or thing with effort and care, while go after is broader and quicker.
  • hunt
    1.0 9.35/m
    hunt implies a relentless, often long pursuit to capture or kill, while go after can mean simply try to get or follow.
  • trail
    2.5 3.51/m
    trail means to follow someone closely, sometimes secretly, usually to catch them, while go after can also mean trying to get.
  • tail
    5.9 2.15/m
    tail means to follow someone secretly and closely to watch or catch them, while go after can mean chasing or attempting to obtain.
