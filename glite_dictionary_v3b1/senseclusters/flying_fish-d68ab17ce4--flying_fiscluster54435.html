flying fish

noun
tropical fish that glides above water
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ fɪʃ / (BrE)
a tropical marine fish with enlarged winglike fins, allowing it to glide above the water's surface for short distances

Examples

A flying fish glides
Several flying fish landed on the deck, surprising the sailors.
The flying fish escaped predators by leaping out of the water and soaring for several meters.
We spotted a flying fish gliding above the waves during our boat trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exocoetid - tropical marine fish with winglike fins for gliding (scientific term)
