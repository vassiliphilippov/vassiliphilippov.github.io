doxepin hydrochloride

noun
tricyclic antidepressant medication
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑk.sɪ.pɪn haɪˌdrɑː.kləˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈdɒk.sɪ.pɪn haɪˌdrɒ.kləˌraɪd / (BrE)
a tricyclic antidepressant medication used to treat depression and anxiety disorders, known for causing side effects such as dry mouth and sedation

Examples

Her doctor prescribed doxepin hydrochloride to help manage her depression and insomnia.
Patients taking doxepin hydrochloride should be aware of potential side effects, such as drowsiness and dry mouth.
