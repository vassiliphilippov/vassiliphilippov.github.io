zygomorphic

adjective
symmetrical in only one plane (biology)
Pronunciation: / ˌzaɪɡəˈmɔr.fɪk / (AmE), / ˌzɪɡ.əˈmɔː.fɪk / (BrE)
(of a flower or other structure) capable of being divided into symmetrical halves by only one specific longitudinal plane passing through its axis.

Examples

Zygomorphic flower shape
Many legumes bear zygomorphic flowers, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects.
The orchid is an example of a zygomorphic flower, as it can only be divided symmetrically in one way.
Unlike actinomorphic flowers, those that are zygomorphic show bilateral symmetry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actinomorphic - (of a flower) symmetrical in multiple planes through its center
