Sambucus nigra

noun
European black elder shrub or tree
9.6
Pronunciation: /sæm.ˈbjuː.kəs ˈnɪ.ɡrə/ /; / /səm.ˈbjuː.kəs ˈnɪ.ɡrə/ (AmE), /sæm.ˈbjuː.kəs ˈnɪ.ɡrə/ /; / /sæm.ˈbjʊ.kəs ˈnɪ.ɡrə/ (BrE)
A species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Europe and Asia, known as European black elder, whose black fruit is used in wines, jellies, and traditional remedies.

Examples

Sambucus nigra black fruit
Sambucus nigra is commonly found along hedgerows and woodlands throughout Europe.
Local traditions use extracts from Sambucus nigra flowers for homemade cordials and teas.
The dark berries of Sambucus nigra are often harvested to make elderberry wine.
