circus act

noun
chaotic or entertaining situation
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrkəs ækt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkəs ækt / (BrE)
An event or situation characterized by chaos or lively entertainment, often implying confusion or disorder.

Examples

A circus act of chaos
After the new manager arrived, the office meetings quickly turned into a circus act.
Her birthday party was a real circus act, with kids running everywhere and adults yelling instructions.
Trying to coordinate all our schedules for the trip was nothing short of a circus act.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shambles - A state of total disorder or confusion
  • synonym: spectacle - An impressive or remarkable display or event
