wad

verb
to pack tightly to capacity
8.2 5.1
Pronunciation: /wɑːd/ (AmE), /wɒd/ (BrE)
to compress, pack, or roll something into a tight or compact mass

Examples

To wad up paper
Boxes were wadded into the corner of the storage room, leaving no free space.
People wadded around the entrance, eager to get inside the venue.
Tourists wadded into the subway car until there was barely room to stand.
Don’t wad your clothes; fold them neatly instead.
He wadded the letter and tossed it in the trash.
She wadded up the napkin and put it in her pocket.
