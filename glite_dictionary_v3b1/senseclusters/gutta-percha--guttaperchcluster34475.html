gutta-percha

noun
type of tough, rubbery material
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /gʌt.ə.ˈpɜːr.kə/ (AmE), /gʌt.ə.ˈpɜː.kə/ (BrE)
A tough, whitish rubber obtained from the latex of certain tropical trees, used especially for insulating electrical cables and in dentistry.

Examples

White gutta-percha rubber
Dentists sometimes use gutta-percha to fill root canals after cleaning out an infection.
Early underwater telegraph cables were insulated with gutta-percha to prevent electrical leakage.
The decline in gutta-percha supply led to the search for alternative insulating materials.
