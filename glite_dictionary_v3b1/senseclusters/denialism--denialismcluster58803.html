denialism

noun
refusal to accept facts
Pronunciation: / dɪˈnaɪəlɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the refusal or systematic rejection of well-established facts, evidence, or reality, especially in science or history

Examples

Denialism of facts
Climate change denialism remains a challenge for scientists and policymakers worldwide.
Public health campaigns often struggle against denialism about the effectiveness of vaccines.
The rise of denialism in social media can spread misinformation quickly.
The spread of denialism about vaccines has led to a rise in preventable diseases.
Some politicians have been accused of denialism regarding climate change evidence.
Historians warn that denialism can distort public understanding of important events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acceptance - the act of agreeing that something is true or real
  • degree lower: skepticism - doubt or disbelief about accepted ideas or facts
