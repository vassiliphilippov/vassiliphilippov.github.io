putt

verb
hit a golf ball gently on the green
5.1
Pronunciation: / pʌt / (AmE), / pʌt / (BrE)
to gently hit a golf ball along the ground towards or into the hole, usually using a putter

Examples

To putt the ball
After lining up the shot carefully, she putted straight into the hole.
He putted poorly on the 18th green and lost the match.
She putted confidently, sinking the ball with a single stroke.
After missing the green, he carefully putted from the edge and nearly made it in.
Golfers often practice for hours to perfect their ability to putt accurately.
She putted the ball slowly towards the hole and waited anxiously for the result.
After a long approach shot, he lined up his putt carefully.
It took him three tries to putt from just a few feet away.
She calmly putted the ball into the hole to win the match.
