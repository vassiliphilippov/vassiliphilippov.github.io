web-toed salamander

noun
a salamander with webbed toes and long tongue
Pronunciation: / wɛbˌtoʊd ˈsæləˌmændər / (AmE), / wɛbˌtəʊd ˈsæləˌmændə / (BrE)
A type of salamander with webbed toes and a very long extensile tongue, known for its excellent climbing ability and ease of movement on smooth rock surfaces.

Examples

A web-toed salamander climbs
Many web-toed salamanders inhabit streams in mountainous regions, where their webbed toes give them a strong grip.
Researchers observed a web-toed salamander climbing easily across wet, smooth rocks.
The web-toed salamander uses its long tongue to catch insects on slippery stones.
