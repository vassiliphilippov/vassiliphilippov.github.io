glacier

verb
to cover with glaciers
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃərd / (AmE), / ˈɡlæsiəd / (BrE)
to cover with glaciers or to be shaped or eroded by the movement of glaciers

Examples

Land glaciered in the past
During the last ice age, much of the region was glaciered, leaving behind unique landforms.
The mountain valleys were glaciered over thousands of years, creating deep U-shaped valleys.
Scientists study areas that have been glaciered to understand past climate changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glaciate - to cover with glaciers or to be affected by glacial action
