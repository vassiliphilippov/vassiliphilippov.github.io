piggyback

verb
carry on one's back and shoulders
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪɡiˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɪɡibæk /; / ˈpɪɡiˌbæk / (BrE)
to carry or ride on the back or shoulders of another person

Examples

To piggyback a child
During the festival, parents often piggyback their children to give them a better view.
He piggybacked his friend after she twisted her ankle during the hike.
She offered to piggyback her little brother all the way home.
He piggybacked his friend across the flooded street to keep her shoes dry.
She gave her little brother a piggyback after he got tired of walking.
The children love to piggyback on their father's back at the park.
