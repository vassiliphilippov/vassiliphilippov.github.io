get

verb
attract and hold attention
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt / (AmE), / ɡɛt / (BrE)
to attract or hold someone's attention, interest, or emotions; to annoy or irritate someone

Examples

Her bright red dress really got everyone's eyes at the party.
If you want to get the waiter's attention, try raising your hand.
The speaker managed to get everyone's attention before starting her presentation.
She warned me that the book would get you if you're not careful.
That song always gets me when I hear it on the radio.
The ending of the film really got me—I couldn't stop crying.
It gets him when people interrupt without listening.
Petty arguments like this just get me after a long day.
Sometimes loud noises really get me when I'm trying to work.
