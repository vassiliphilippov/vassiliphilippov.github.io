band-tailed pigeon

noun
wild pigeon of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈbænd teɪld ˈpɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈbænd teɪld ˈpɪdʒɪn / (BrE)
a wild pigeon of western North America, recognizable by a broad dark band on its tail; often mistaken for the now extinct passenger pigeon

Examples

A band-tailed pigeon flock
A flock of band-tailed pigeons visited the forest clearing at dawn.
The band-tailed pigeon is often seen feeding on acorns in oak woodlands.
Unlike the extinct passenger pigeon, the band-tailed pigeon still inhabits much of western North America.
