backstop

noun
barrier behind home plate in baseball
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstɑp / (AmE), / ˈbækstɒp / (BrE)
A fence, screen, or barrier behind the home plate in baseball, designed to prevent the ball from leaving the playing area.

Examples

Baseball backstop fence
A tall chain-link backstop protected spectators seated behind home plate.
The foul ball bounced off the backstop and rolled back onto the field.
The team installed a new backstop to ensure safety during games.
