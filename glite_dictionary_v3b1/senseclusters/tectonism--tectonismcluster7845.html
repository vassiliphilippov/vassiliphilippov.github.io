tectonism

noun
crustal movement process
9.1
Pronunciation: /tek.ˈtɑːn.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /tek.ˈtɒn.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the process by which the Earth's crust is formed, deformed, or changed by movements within the planet

Examples

Intense tectonism activity
The Himalayas are a result of intense tectonism over millions of years.
Scientists study tectonism to understand how earthquakes and mountain ranges form.
Evidence of ancient tectonism can be seen in the folded rocks of the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orogeny - the process of mountain formation by folding of the Earth's crust
  • synonym: tectonics - the study of the structure and movement of the Earth's crust
