arbutus

noun
evergreen shrub or small tree of genus Arbutus
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈbjuːtəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈbjuːtəs / (BrE)
Any of several evergreen shrubs or small trees of the genus Arbutus, native to temperate regions of Europe and America, often with white or pinkish flowers and red berries.

Examples

An arbutus tree
An arbutus can thrive in rocky, well-drained soil and is often admired for its attractive bark.
The garden features several arbutuses that bloom beautifully in the spring.
We stopped to pick the bright red fruit from the arbutus near the forest trail.
