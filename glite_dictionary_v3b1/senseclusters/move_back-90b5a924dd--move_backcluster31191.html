move back

verb
retreat or go backward
Pronunciation: / muːv bæk / (AmE), / muːv bæk / (BrE)
to pull away or retreat to a previous or more distant position; to go backward or away from a place, often in response to a threat, order, or circumstance

Examples

To move back slowly
If you stand too close to the painting, the alarm will sound and ask you to move back.
Please move back to give the firefighters some space.
The troops were forced to move back when they came under attack.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward toward a place
  • synonym: withdraw - to move away or back, especially from a place of danger
