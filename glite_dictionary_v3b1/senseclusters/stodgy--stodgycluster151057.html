stodgy

adjective
old-fashioned or dull (pejorative)
7.8 0.61/m
Pronunciation: / Ààst…ëÀêd íi / (AmE), / Ààst…íd íi / (BrE)
(of people, attitudes, or things) dull, old-fashioned, or resistant to change; lacking originality or excitement

Examples

Stodgy ideas
His stodgy views on music made him seem much older than he was.
She wanted to modernize the stodgy image of the company.
The committee's decisions were criticized as being stodgy and out of touch with contemporary needs.
His stodgy attitude made him resistant to any innovative suggestions at work.
The lecture felt stodgy, offering nothing new to spark interest among the students.
They avoided restaurants with a stodgy atmosphere, preferring lively places instead.
