offset

noun
compensating factor; counterbalance
2.7 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːf.set/ /; / /ˈɑːf.set/ (AmE), /ˈɒf.set/ (BrE)
something that counterbalances or compensates for something else; a factor or amount that reduces or neutralizes an effect.

Examples

An offset to losses
His apology was seen as an offset to his earlier harsh remarks.
Tax credits act as an offset against your total tax liability.
The company's extra costs were a necessary offset for better working conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplifier - something that increases the effect of something else
  • synonym: counterbalance - something that balances or offsets something else
