installment credit

noun
credit repaid in scheduled installments
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈstɑlmənt ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈstɔːlmənt ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
a type of credit in which a loan is repaid, with interest, in equal periodic payments over a set period

Examples

Installment credit payments
Because of her excellent payment history with installment credit, she qualified for a lower interest rate.
Furniture stores often offer installment credit plans to make larger purchases more affordable.
Many people use installment credit to finance the purchase of a new car.

Related Terms

  • antonym: revolving credit - credit with a pre-approved limit that can be used repeatedly; payments vary
