double bar

noun
programming: logical OR operator (||)
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌb.əl bɑːr/ (AmE), /ˈdʌb.əl bɑː/ (BrE)
(in programming) the symbol || used as the logical OR operator in many programming languages; it returns true if at least one operand is true.

Examples

Double bar operator
In most languages, the double bar returns true when either left or right expression is true.
She replaced the nested if-statements with a single double bar condition to simplify the code.
During debugging he noticed the double bar was short-circuiting, so the second function never ran.

Related Terms

  • synonym: logical OR - a boolean operator that yields true when one or more operands are true
  • synonym: || - the symbol commonly used to represent the logical OR operator in many programming languages
