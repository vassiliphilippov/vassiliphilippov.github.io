thermobaric bomb

noun
a bomb using a fuel-air explosive
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊˈbærɪk bɑm / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈbærɪk bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb that produces a high-temperature explosion by dispersing and igniting a fuel-air mixture, resulting in a powerful blast wave and intense heat

Examples

A thermobaric bomb explosion
A thermobaric bomb generates a massive overpressure wave, making it especially destructive in enclosed spaces.
Reports confirmed that a thermobaric bomb was responsible for the intense fire and shockwave.
The military used a thermobaric bomb to destroy the fortified building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fuel-air bomb - a bomb that disperses a fuel which is then ignited to produce an explosion
