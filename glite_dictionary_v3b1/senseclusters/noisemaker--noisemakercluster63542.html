noisemaker

noun
person who creates public attention
5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnɔɪz.meɪ.kər/ (AmE), /ˈnɔɪz.meɪ.kə/ (BrE)
a person or organization that deliberately creates public attention, controversy, or loud publicity (figurative).

Examples

Media noisemaker
The political consultant acted as a relentless noisemaker, keeping scandals in the headlines.
Local activists became noisemakers by organizing loud protests outside the city hall.
Companies sometimes hire a noisemaker to attract media attention during product launches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attention-seeker - a person who tries to attract attention, often loudly or theatrically
  • synonym: provocateur - someone who intentionally provokes controversy or strong reactions
