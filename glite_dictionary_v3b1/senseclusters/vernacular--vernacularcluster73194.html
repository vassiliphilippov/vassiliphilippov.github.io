vernacular

noun
language of a specific group or region
7.7
Pronunciation: / vərˈnækjələr / (AmE), / vəˈnakjʊlə /; / vəˈnækjʊlə / (BrE)
the everyday language or dialect spoken by ordinary people in a particular country, region, or group

Examples

Local vernacular
In New Orleans, local musicians often blend their vernacular with jazz traditions.
The group's secret vernacular made it difficult for outsiders to understand their conversations.
The novel was written in the vernacular to make it accessible to everyday people.
Many writers sought to capture the vernacular of their home regions in their novels.
Medieval scholars often wrote in Latin rather than in the vernacular of the people.
The government issued pamphlets in the local vernacular so everyone could understand them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colloquial - language used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary
  • synonym: dialect - a form of a language specific to a region or group
  • synonym: jargon - specialized language used by a particular group
  • antonym: literary - relating to the more formal, written language
