page

noun
youthful ceremonial attendant
7.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /peɪdʒ/ (AmE), /peɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a young person who attends and assists at official ceremonies, in noble households, or as a knight's attendant

Examples

A royal page
At the royal wedding, the pages wore traditional uniforms and helped manage the bride's train.
During the ceremony, a page stood ready to assist the officials if needed.
The young page carried the senator's messages during the session.
At the age of seven, he became a page at the lord's castle.
Every page hoped one day to earn the honor of becoming a knight.
The young page eagerly polished the knight's armor each morning.
As a page, he learned the customs of the royal court and delivered important notes.
The knight was often attended by his faithful page during tournaments.
The young page hurried through the grand hall, carrying a message for the queen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others, especially in a household
  • degree higher: squire - a young nobleman acting as an attendant to a knight before becoming a knight himself
