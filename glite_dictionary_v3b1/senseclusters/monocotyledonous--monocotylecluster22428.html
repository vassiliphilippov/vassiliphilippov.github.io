monocotyledonous

adjective
having one cotyledon (in botany)
9.0 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəˌkɑtɪˈlidnəs / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəkɒtɪˈliːdənəs / (BrE)
(of a flowering plant) having a single cotyledon or embryonic leaf in the seed, as in grasses and lilies.

Examples

Monocotyledonous flowering plants
Lilies are a familiar example of a monocotyledonous species found in gardens.
Many cereal crops such as wheat and rice are monocotyledonous plants.
The leaves of monocotyledonous plants are typically narrow and parallel-veined.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dicotyledonous - having two cotyledons in the seed
