yolk

noun
yellow part of an egg
2.0
Pronunciation: / joʊk / (AmE), / jəʊk / (BrE)
the yellow, nutrient-rich part of an egg, found in the center and surrounded by the white

Examples

Egg yolk
She broke the egg and let the yolk slide into the bowl.
The chef separated the yolk from the white to make the custard.
When the yolk is firm, the egg is hard-boiled.
She separated the yolk from the egg white before baking the cake.
Some recipes call for only the yolk to achieve a creamier texture.
The bright yellow yolk is rich in nutrients essential for the embryo's growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: albumen - the clear, viscous substance surrounding the yolk of an egg; egg white
  • antonym: white - the colorless or white part of an egg that surrounds the yolk
