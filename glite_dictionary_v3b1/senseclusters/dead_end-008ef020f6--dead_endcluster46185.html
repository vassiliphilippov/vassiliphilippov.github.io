dead end

noun
road or passage closed at one end
2.7 1.19/m
Pronunciation: / dÉ›d É›nd / (AmE), / dÉ›d É›nd / (BrE)
A passage or road that is closed at one end and does not lead anywhere.

Examples

A dead end street
She parked her car at the dead end and walked the rest of the way.
The new development is being built at the far end of a dead end street.
They realized too late that the alley was a dead end and had to turn back.

Synonyms

    2.1 0.21/m

  • cul-de-sac
    4.7 1.17/m
    a cul-de-sac is a rounded residential street closed at one end, while a dead end is any passage that does not lead through.
  • blind alley
    4.1 0.01/m
    a blind alley is a narrow street or passage with only one way in and out, while a dead end is any closed road.
  • impasse
    8.1 0.03/m
    an impasse is a single-word term for a street with one entrance and exit, while a dead end is the common everyday phrase for a road that stops.
