Chassidism

noun
sect of Orthodox Jewish beliefs and practices
Pronunciation: / hɑːˈsid.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / haˈsɪd.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
The religious beliefs and practices of a sect of Orthodox Jews, characterized by mysticism, religious fervor, and emphasis on community and spiritual leadership.

Examples

Chassidism religious beliefs
Her grandparents were devoted adherents of Chassidism throughout their lives.
Many Jews who follow Chassidism gather for spiritual study and communal prayer.
The roots of Chassidism can be traced back to 18th-century Eastern Europe.
