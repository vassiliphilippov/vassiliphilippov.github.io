tank engine

noun
steam locomotive with onboard fuel and water
Pronunciation: / tæŋk ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / tæŋk ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a steam locomotive that carries its own fuel and water on board, eliminating the need for a separate tender

Examples

A tank engine locomotive
Many short-distance passenger trains used a tank engine for greater efficiency.
The old railway museum proudly displays a restored tank engine from the early 1900s.
Unlike a traditional locomotive, a tank engine does not need to haul a tender behind it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tender engine - a locomotive that pulls a separate vehicle (tender) carrying its fuel and water
