aphakia

noun
absence of the eye's natural lens
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈfeɪkiə / (AmE), / əˈfeɪkiə / (BrE)
absence of the natural lens of the eye, usually as a result of cataract removal

Examples

Aphakia after surgery
Advancements in intraocular lens implants have reduced the incidence of aphakia following cataract procedures.
After surgery, the patient developed aphakia and required corrective lenses.
Uncorrected aphakia can result in significant visual impairment if not managed properly.
