erodibility

noun
susceptibility to erosion
Pronunciation: / ɪˌroʊdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌrəʊdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a material, especially soil, that makes it likely to be worn away by water or wind

Examples

Soil erodibility
The erodibility of the hillside increased after the trees were removed.
Farmers test the erodibility of their soil to prevent loss during heavy rains.
High erodibility means the land is at greater risk of being damaged by wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erosion - the process of being worn away by natural forces
  • antonym: resistance - the ability to withstand being worn away
