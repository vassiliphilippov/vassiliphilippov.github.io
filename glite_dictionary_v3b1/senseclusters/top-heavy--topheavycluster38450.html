top-heavy

adjective
having too much weight at the top
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑp ˌhɛvi /; / ˈtɑpˌhɛvi / (AmE), / ˈtɒp ˌhɛvi /; / ˈtɒpˌhɛvi / (BrE)
having too much weight or mass at the top, so as to be unbalanced or likely to fall over; also used figuratively to mean feeling unsteady, dizzy, or imbalanced in composition

Examples

A top-heavy cake
A top-heavy cake can easily collapse if it isn't supported properly.
That old truck is top-heavy, so take the turns slowly to avoid tipping it over.
The bookshelf became top-heavy after she stacked too many books on the upper shelves.
After several strong drinks, she felt top-heavy and struggled to walk straight.
The bookshelf was top-heavy and threatened to tip over if not secured to the wall.
Their cake was top-heavy with frosting, making it difficult to slice evenly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bottom-heavy - having most of the weight in the lower part
  • synonym: imbalanced - not balanced; lacking equilibrium
