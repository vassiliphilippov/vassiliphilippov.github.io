purl

noun
knitting stitch with raised, bumpy surface
9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /pɜːrl/ (AmE), /pɜːl/ (BrE)
a basic knitting stitch made by passing the needle through the front of the stitch from the right-hand side, creating a raised, bumpy row

Examples

A purl stitch
Alternating rows of purl and knit stitches form a ribbed pattern.
Learning how to purl is essential for beginner knitters.
