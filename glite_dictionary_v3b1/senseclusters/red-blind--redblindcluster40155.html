red-blind

adjective
unable to see red due to color vision deficiency
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
Unable to see the color red or to distinguish between red and bluish-green, due to a type of color vision deficiency.

Examples

Red-blind person
Because he is red-blind, he cannot tell the difference between red and green traffic lights.
Certain genetic mutations are known to cause people to become red-blind.
The test identified several red-blind individuals in the group of volunteers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colorblind - unable to distinguish certain colors, often red and green
  • degree lower: green-blind - unable to see green; a type of color vision deficiency
  • synonym: red-blindness - inability to see red or distinguish red from green
