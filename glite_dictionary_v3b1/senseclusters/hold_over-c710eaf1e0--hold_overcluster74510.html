hold over

verb
keep goods to sell next season
7.7 3.8
Pronunciation: /hoʊld ˈoʊ.vər/ (AmE), /həʊld ˈəʊ.və/ (BrE)
to postpone, extend, or continue something beyond its original time or period

Examples

To hold over a meeting
Due to poor sales, the company decided to hold over its summer inventory until next year.
Farmers sometimes hold over their crops for better market prices in the following season.
The shop didn't sell all its winter coats, so it will hold over the remaining stock.
He was asked to hold over in office for an additional three months.
Sometimes, officials hold over if there is a delay in confirming their successors.
The governor will hold over until a replacement is appointed.
Due to the storm, the committee decided to hold over the meeting until next week.
The theater will hold over the film's release because the renovations aren't finished.
We agreed to hold over discussion of the new policy to the next session.

  • synonym: carry over - to transfer or extend to another time or situation
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something until a later time
  • synonym: remain - to continue to exist or stay in a position
  • synonym: retain - to continue to have or keep something
  • synonym: stockpile - to accumulate goods for future use or sale
