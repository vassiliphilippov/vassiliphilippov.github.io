hoot

verb
make a loud shout or cry
3.4
Pronunciation: / huːt / (AmE), / huːt / (BrE)
to make a loud, clamorous shout or cry, often to express disapproval or excitement

Examples

Crowds hoot loudly
Fans would hoot loudly whenever their team scored a goal.
He hooted in disbelief when he heard the surprising news.
The angry crowd began to hoot at the politician as she took the stage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheer - to shout in encouragement or approval
  • synonym: jeer - to shout mocking or insulting remarks
