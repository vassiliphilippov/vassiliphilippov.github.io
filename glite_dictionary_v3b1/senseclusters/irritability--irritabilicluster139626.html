irritability

noun
tendency to become annoyed or angered
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪtəˈbɪləti /; / ˌɪr.ɪ.t̬əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪtəˈbɪlɪti /; / ˌɪr.ɪ.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily annoyed or angered

Examples

Constant irritability
His irritability made working with him quite challenging at times.
She experienced increased irritability due to lack of sleep.
The medication reduced his irritability and improved his mood.
After several sleepless nights, her irritability became noticeable to everyone.
Children may show signs of irritability when they are unwell.
The constant noise in the office increased his irritability over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or excitement
  • antonym: calmness - a state of being calm and not easily upset
  • synonym: petulance - the quality of being childishly sulky or bad-tempered
