tonus

noun
persistent muscle or tissue tension
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtəʊ.nəs / (BrE)
the steady, slight tension present in muscles, blood vessels, and other tissues in living organisms, enabling them to respond appropriately to stimuli

Examples

Muscle tonus level
After the surgery, the patient had to do exercises to restore normal tonus in his legs.
Low muscle tonus can lead to difficulties in movement and posture.
The doctor measured the arterial tonus to assess vascular health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tone - the normal level of firmness or tension in muscles or tissues
