hyperpolarize

verb
to increase polarization
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈpoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈpəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to increase the electric polarization of a material or system, resulting in greater separation of electric charges or alignment of dipoles

Examples

To hyperpolarize a material
Scientists can hyperpolarize certain materials to enhance their magnetic resonance signals in experiments.
Applying a strong electric field will hyperpolarize the membrane, affecting how ions move across it.
Researchers developed a new method to hyperpolarize molecules for improved imaging techniques.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depolarize - to reduce or remove the polarization of something
  • degree lower: polarize - to cause something to acquire polarity or become polarized
