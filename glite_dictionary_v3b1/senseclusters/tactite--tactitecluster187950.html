tactite

noun
metamorphic rock type
Pronunciation: / ˈtæktaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtæktaɪt / (BrE)
a hard, coarse-grained rock formed by contact metamorphism, especially in limestone, often rich in silicate minerals

Examples

A tactite rock
The geologist collected a sample of tactite from the edge of the limestone deposit.
Minerals such as garnet and pyroxene are commonly found in tactite formations.
The presence of tactite indicated that the area had undergone intense contact metamorphism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skarn - a metamorphic rock formed at the contact zone between igneous and carbonate rocks
