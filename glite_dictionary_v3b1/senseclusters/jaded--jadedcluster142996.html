jaded

adjective
dull or bored from overindulgence
3.6 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒeɪ.dɪd/ /; / /ˈdʒeɪ.dəd/ (AmE), /ˈdʒeɪ.dɪd/ /; / /ˈdʒeɪ.dəd/ (BrE)
bored, tired, or lacking enthusiasm because you have had too much of something or experienced it for too long

Examples

Feeling jaded and tired
After years of working in customer service, she felt completely jaded about dealing with complaints.
He approached the new project with a jaded attitude, expecting it to be boring like the last one.
The critics were so jaded that no new album could excite them anymore.
After years of working overtime, she felt completely jaded and unable to enjoy her weekends.
By the end of the conference, many attendees looked jaded and ready for a break.
The constant stream of bad news had left him feeling jaded about politics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enthusiastic - having or showing intense interest or eagerness
  • antonym: fresh - full of energy and not tired
  • synonym: tired - in need of sleep or rest; weary
  • synonym: weary - feeling or showing tiredness, especially as a result of excessive exertion or lack of sleep
  • synonym: world-weary - feeling or showing fatigue from the difficulties of life or excess experience
