botulism

noun
serious food poisoning from botulinum toxin
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊ.tʃəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈbɒ.tjʊˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
A rare but serious illness caused by toxins from the bacterium Clostridium botulinum, usually resulting in food poisoning that affects the nervous system and can be fatal if untreated.

Examples

Home-canned botulism
Doctors warned that home-canned foods could be a source of botulism if not processed correctly.
If botulism is suspected, immediate medical treatment is crucial to prevent serious complications.
The outbreak of botulism was traced back to improperly canned vegetables.
