hydrophily

noun
water pollination
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑːfɪli / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒfɪli / (BrE)
the process of pollination in which pollen is transferred by water, especially in aquatic plants

Examples

Hydrophily in plants
Some aquatic plants depend on hydrophily to transfer pollen between flowers.
The study focused on the efficiency of hydrophily in submerged environments.
Unlike most land plants, those with hydrophily use water as a medium for pollination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anemophily - pollination by wind
  • antonym: entomophily - pollination by insects
