unilateral descent

noun
kinship traced through one side of a family
7.5 7.3
Pronunciation: /juː.nə.ˈlæ.tə.rəl dɪ.ˈsent/ /; / /juː.nɪ.ˈlæ.tə.rəl dɪ.ˈsent/ (AmE), /juː.nɪ.ˈlæt.ə.rəl dɪ.ˈsent/ /; / /juː.nə.ˈlæt.ə.rəl dɪ.ˈsent/ (BrE)
(Anthropology) a system of tracing family lineage through only one parent's line, either the father (patrilineal) or the mother (matrilineal).

Examples

Unilateral descent system
Anthropologists study cultures that use unilateral descent to determine clan membership.
In a unilateral descent system, children belong exclusively to either their mother's or father's family line.
Some societies recognize unilateral descent as the primary basis for inheritance and kinship.
