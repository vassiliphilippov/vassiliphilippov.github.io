receptive

adjective
carrying signals toward the central nervous system
3.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
(of a nerve fiber or impulse) carrying signals from the body's periphery toward the central nervous system.

Examples

Receptive neuron
An afferent neuron is also known as a receptive neuron in medical literature.
Damage to receptive nerves can reduce sensations like pain and temperature.
The receptive fibers transmit sensory information to the brain for processing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afferent - (of nerves or impulses) conducting signals toward the central nervous system
  • antonym: efferent - (of nerves or impulses) carrying signals away from the central nervous system
