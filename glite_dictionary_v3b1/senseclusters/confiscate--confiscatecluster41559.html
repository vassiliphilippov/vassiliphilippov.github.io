confiscate

verb
officially take away; seize by authority
5.5 6.59/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên.f…ô.ske…™t / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.f…™.ske…™t / (BrE)
to officially take something away from someone, especially as a penalty or for legal reasons

Examples

To confiscate illegal items
Police were forced to confiscate the counterfeit goods during the raid.
School authorities have the right to confiscate mobile phones if they are used during class.
The airport officials decided to confiscate any forbidden items found in passengers' luggage.

Synonyms

  • seize
    4.6 8.02/m
    seize is taking property by legal authority, often temporarily for security or evidence, while confiscate is usually a permanent penalty.
  • impound
    7.2 1.21/m
    impound means authorities take and store items for safekeeping or evidence, while confiscate means take them as a legal penalty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impound - to take legal possession of something until a debt or claim is settled
  • antonym: return - to give back something taken or borrowed
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly, especially by legal authority
