defervesce

verb
to have a fever subside
9.9 9.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.fər.ˈves/ /; / /diː.fər.ˈves/ (AmE), /diː.fə.ˈves/ /; / /dɪ.fə.ˈves/ (BrE)
(of a person or patient) to experience a reduction or subsiding of a fever; to return to normal temperature after having a fever.

Examples

To defervesce after fever
After taking the medication, the child's fever began to defervesce by the following morning.
Doctors monitored whether the patient would defervesce naturally or require more aggressive treatment.
Patients typically defervesce within 48 hours of starting antibiotic therapy for bacterial infections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recur - to occur again, especially of a fever returning
  • synonym: remit - (of a fever or symptom) to decrease in intensity or severity
