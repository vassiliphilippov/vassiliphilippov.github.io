precarious

adjective
dangerous or unstable
7.0
Pronunciation: / prɪˈkɛriəs / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəs / (BrE)
in a dangerous, unstable, or insecure position; likely to fall, collapse, or fail

Examples

A precarious position
After the storm, the bridge was in a precarious condition and unsafe for traffic.
She balanced the vase on a precarious stack of books.
They found themselves in a precarious financial situation after losing their jobs.
After months of negotiation, the two sides finally agreed to a precarious truce.
Her financial situation became increasingly precarious after losing her job.
The ladder was balanced in a precarious position against the wall.
Her financial situation has been precarious ever since she lost her job.
The climbers found themselves in a precarious position on the narrow ledge.
The poorly constructed bridge looked precarious after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • antonym: secure - free from danger or threat
  • synonym: unstable - not steady or secure; likely to change or collapse
