ledge

noun
projecting ridge of rock
Pronunciation: / lɛdʒ / (AmE), / lɛdʒ / (BrE)
a narrow, flat surface that sticks out from a wall, cliff, or rock

Examples

Rock ledge
A family of birds built their nest on a small ledge high above the ground.
Schools of fish darted in and out of the rocky ledge beneath the water's surface.
The climbers rested for a moment on a narrow ledge halfway up the cliff.
The climber carefully balanced on a narrow ledge halfway up the cliff.
She placed her flower pots on the window ledge to catch the sunlight.
A bird built its nest on a ledge just below the roof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shelf - a flat, horizontal surface for placing objects
  • synonym: sill - a horizontal ledge at the base of a window
