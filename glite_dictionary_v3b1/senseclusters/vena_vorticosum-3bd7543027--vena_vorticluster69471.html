vena vorticosum

noun
vein in the eye draining to ophthalmic veins
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àåve…™n…ô v…îÀêrÀàt…™k…ôs…ôm / (AmE), / Àåve…™n…ô v…îÀêÀàt…™k…ôs…ôm / (BrE)
A vein formed by branches from the back surface of the eye and the ciliary body that empties into the ophthalmic veins.

Examples

The vena vorticosum vein
Blockage of the vena vorticosum can lead to increased intraocular pressure and visual disturbances.
Researchers studied the drainage patterns of the vena vorticosum in patients with glaucoma.
The ophthalmologist observed that the vena vorticosum was clearly visible during the retinal examination.

Synonyms

  • vortex vein
    9.1 0.00/m
    vortex vein is the common English anatomical name for the eye's posterior drainage vein, while vena vorticosum is the Latin anatomical term.
  • vorticose vein
    9.9 0.00/m
    vorticose vein names any of several veins draining the eye's back and ciliary body in anatomical texts, while vena vorticosum denotes the specific vein.
