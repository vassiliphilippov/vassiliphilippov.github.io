eusociality

noun
advanced social structure
9.9
Pronunciation: /juː.soʊ.ʃə.ˈlɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /juː.səʊ.ʃə.ˈlɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
a social organization in which some individuals give up reproduction to help others, as seen in ants, bees, or termites

Examples

Eusociality in ants
Ant colonies are classic examples of eusociality in the animal kingdom.
The study explored how eusociality evolved independently in both insects and mammals.
Bees display eusociality by dividing labor among workers, drones, and queens.
