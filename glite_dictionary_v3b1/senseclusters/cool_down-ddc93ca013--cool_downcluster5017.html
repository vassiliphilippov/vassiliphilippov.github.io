cool down

verb
to become less intense or emotional
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /kuːl daʊn/ (AmE), /kuːl daʊn/ (BrE)
to become less angry, excited, or intense; to calm down emotionally

Examples

To cool down after anger
After the argument, she needed time to cool down before talking again.
His excitement about the new project began to cool down after a few weeks.
When tempers cool down, we can discuss the issue rationally.
He went for a walk to cool down after the argument.
Let the soup cool down for a few minutes before serving.
The weather should cool down once the sun sets.
