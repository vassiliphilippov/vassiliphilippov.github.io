cool down

verb
to become less intense or emotional
2.5 0.4/m
Pronunciation: / kul da än /; / kuÀêl da än / (AmE), / kuÀêl da än / (BrE)
to become less angry, excited, or intense; to calm down emotionally

Examples

To cool down after anger
After the argument, she needed time to cool down before talking again.
His excitement about the new project began to cool down after a few weeks.
When tempers cool down, we can discuss the issue rationally.
He went for a walk to cool down after the argument.
Let the soup cool down for a few minutes before serving.
The weather should cool down once the sun sets.

Synonyms

  • calm down
    1.4 12/m
    calm down means become less angry or upset in the moment, while cool down focuses on reducing strong emotion.
  • cool off
    2.7 1/m
    cool off means become calmer or less angry and can also mean stop being hot, while cool down is mainly emotional.
  • cool
    4.1 5/m
    cool means emotion or interest grows weaker over time, while cool down is a quick calming of strong feeling.
  • simmer down
    4.1 0.3/m
    simmer down means become slowly less angry or excited, while cool down can mean calming more quickly.
  • cool it
    6.2 0.9/m
    cool it tells someone to stop and be quiet in a direct way, while cool down describes calming in general.
  • chill out
    4.9 2/m
    chill out means relax and stop worrying in informal speech, while cool down is a more neutral calm of emotion.
  • settle down
    2.5 2/m
    settle down means become quiet or peaceful, often for longer, while cool down usually means calm in the short term.
  • relax
    1.0 40/m
    relax means rest and reduce tension or stress, while cool down focuses specifically on calming strong emotion.
  • ease off
    4.0 0.4/m
    ease off means lessen in pressure, force, or speed slowly, while cool down refers to calming emotions more directly.
  • quieten
    4.6 0.09/m
    quieten means make less noisy or active, often physically, while cool down means reduce heated feelings or anger.
