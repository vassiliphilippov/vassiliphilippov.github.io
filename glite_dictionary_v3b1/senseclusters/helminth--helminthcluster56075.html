helminth

noun
parasitic intestinal worm
7.7 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhel.mɪnθ/ (AmE), /ˈhel.mɪnθ/ (BrE)
a parasitic worm, especially one that lives in the intestines of vertebrates, such as roundworms, tapeworms, or flukes

Examples

A helminth infection
Many developing countries have public health programs to reduce helminth infections among children.
Some common symptoms of helminth infestation include abdominal pain and digestive problems.
The doctor diagnosed the infection as being caused by a helminth in the patient's intestines.
