coreid bug

noun
a true bug of the family Coreidae
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːr.i.ɪd bʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.rɪ.ɪd bʌɡ / (BrE)
A member of the family Coreidae, a group of true bugs commonly known as leaf-footed bugs, recognized by their elongated bodies and, in some species, broadened hind legs.

Examples

A coreid bug species
Many species of coreid bug are known for the expanded segments on their hind legs.
The coreid bug feeds on many kinds of plants using its piercing-sucking mouthparts.
We found a coreid bug resting on the underside of a leaf in the garden.
