basal placentation

noun
type of ovule arrangement in plant ovary
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.səl pleɪ.sən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.zəl pleɪ.sən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.səl pleɪ.sən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.zəl pleɪ.sən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a type of placentation in which one or a few ovules develop at the base of a simple or compound ovary, as seen in some flowering plants

Examples

Basal placentation in plants
In basal placentation, the ovules are attached to the base rather than the sides or center.
Plant species with basal placentation can be distinguished by the position of their ovules in the ovary.
The sunflower exhibits basal placentation, with its ovules clustered at the bottom of the ovary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axile placentation - type of placentation with ovules attached to a central axis
  • synonym: parietal placentation - type of placentation with ovules attached to the ovary wall
