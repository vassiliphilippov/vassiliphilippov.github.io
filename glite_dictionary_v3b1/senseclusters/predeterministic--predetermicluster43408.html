predeterministic

adjective
determined in advance
8.6
Pronunciation: /priː.dɪ.ɜːr.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ /; / /prɪ.dɪ.ɜːr.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /priː.dɪ.ɜː.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ /; / /prɪ.dɪ.ɜː.mɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
describing something (such as a process, system, or belief) where the outcome is decided in advance by prior conditions, fate, or a higher power

Examples

A predeterministic process
Some scientists argue that the universe operates in a predeterministic manner, with every event set by initial conditions.
The model assumes a predeterministic process, leaving no room for chance or randomness.
In a predeterministic system, knowing the starting state allows you to predict all future outcomes.
The philosopher presented a predeterministic argument about the course of human history.
Some religious traditions hold a predeterministic view of fate and destiny.
Her essay criticized the predeterministic approach to explaining human behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deterministic - having outcomes determined by prior causes or conditions
  • antonym: random - happening without a definite plan, cause, or pattern
