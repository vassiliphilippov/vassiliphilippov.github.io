Luo language

noun
Nilotic language of Adamawa Region
Pronunciation: / ˈluː.oʊ ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈluː.əʊ ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a Nilotic language spoken primarily in parts of the Adamawa Region, especially in the Mayo-Banyo division, Bankim subdivision, and a section of Atta

Examples

Luo language spoken in Adamawa
Many people in the Mayo-Banyo division still speak Luo language as their mother tongue.
Researchers are studying the grammar of the Luo language to document its unique features.
The vocabulary of the Luo language reflects the culture and history of its speakers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nilotic language - language family in the Nile Valley and surrounding regions
