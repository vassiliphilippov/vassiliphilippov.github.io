gallate

noun
salt of gallic acid
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡæleɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester of gallic acid, often used as an antioxidant in food and pharmaceuticals

Examples

Propyl gallate
Propyl gallate is commonly added to processed foods to prevent them from spoiling.
The laboratory tested the effectiveness of gallate compounds as antioxidants in pharmaceuticals.
Some cosmetics contain gallate to help preserve their ingredients and extend shelf life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antioxidant - a substance that prevents or slows damage caused by oxidation
  • synonym: gallic acid - a type of organic acid found in plants
