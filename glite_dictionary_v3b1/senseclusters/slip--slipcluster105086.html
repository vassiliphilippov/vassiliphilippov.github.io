slip

verb
move smoothly and quietly
1.0
Pronunciation: / slɪp / (AmE), / slɪp / (BrE)
to move smoothly, quietly, or easily, often without being noticed, or to slide unintentionally

Examples

To slip into a room
He likes to slip into the room quietly when everyone's distracted.
She managed to slip past the security guards without being seen.
The fish can easily slip through your fingers if you’re not careful.
He quietly slipped out of the meeting before anyone noticed.
Sunlight slipped through the narrow gap in the curtains.
The ring slipped over her finger with ease.
He slipped his hand into his pocket without drawing any attention.
She carefully slipped the letter under the door so no one would notice.
The jeweler slipped the ring onto her finger smoothly and gently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creep - to move slowly and quietly in order to avoid being noticed
  • synonym: glide - move smoothly and continuously with little noise or resistance
  • synonym: skid - to slide, typically sideways or obliquely, often out of control
  • synonym: slide - move smoothly along a surface while maintaining contact with it
  • synonym: sneak - to move in a secretive or furtive manner
