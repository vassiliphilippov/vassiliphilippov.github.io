gabble

noun
rapid, indistinct speech
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡæbəl / (BrE)
rapid and indistinct speech that is difficult to understand

Examples

Loud gabble
All I could hear was the gabble of children playing in the playground.
The speaker's gabble made it hard for the audience to follow his argument.
Through the gabble of voices, she could barely make out her friend's words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - talk that is foolish, excited, or not clear
