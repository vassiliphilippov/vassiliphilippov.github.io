coil

noun
tubing or wire wound in a spiral
4.1 3.66/m
Pronunciation: / kÉ”Éªl / (AmE), / kÉ”Éªl / (BrE)
an electrical or mechanical component made of wire wound into a spiral, used in devices like heaters or engines

Examples

Heating coil
An electric stove heats up quickly because of its metal heating coil.
She carefully wound the hose into a neat coil after watering the garden.
The copper coil inside the air conditioner helps transfer heat efficiently.
Old cars often needed their coil checked during routine maintenance.
The mechanic replaced the faulty coil to fix the misfiring engine.
Without a functioning coil, the spark plugs won't receive the necessary voltage.
He measured the resistance of the coil to ensure it was working properly.
If the coil is damaged, the circuit will not function as intended.
The radio contains a coil that helps tune the signal to the correct frequency.

Synonyms

  • inductor
    7.4 0.04/m
    An inductor is a coil-like electrical part that opposes changes in electric current, while a coil is a general wound wire used in many devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignition coil - the full term for the component generating high voltage in engines
  • synonym: inductor - an electrical component that stores energy in a magnetic field, usually a coil
  • synonym: spiral - curve that winds around a fixed center point at increasing or decreasing distance
