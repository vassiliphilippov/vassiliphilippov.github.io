major axis

noun
longest axis of an ellipse or ellipsoid
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
the longest axis of an ellipse or ellipsoid, passing through its two foci

Examples

The major axis length
Astronomers measured the major axis of the planet's orbit around the star.
The major axis connects the two farthest points on the ellipse.
The length of the major axis determines the overall size of the ellipse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minor axis - the shortest axis of an ellipse, perpendicular to the major axis
