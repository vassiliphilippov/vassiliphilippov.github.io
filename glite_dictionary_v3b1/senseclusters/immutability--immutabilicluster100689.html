immutability

noun
unchangeable data in computing
Pronunciation: / cc6am.jud0.t59c8b6al.59.ti / (AmE), / 6accmjud0.t59c8b6al.6a.ti / (BrE)
(in computing) the property of data, values, or objects that cannot be changed after they are created, often used to enable safer concurrent code and referential transparency.

Examples

Immutability in code
In functional programming, immutability helps prevent bugs by ensuring data cannot be changed.
The library's API enforces immutability for its objects to simplify reasoning about state changes.
Because of immutability, concurrent threads can read shared data without locks, improving performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mutability - the quality of being changeable; the opposite of immutability
  • synonym: unchangeability - the state or quality of not being able to change
