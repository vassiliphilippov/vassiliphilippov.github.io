weasel out

verb
avoid responsibility or commitment
Pronunciation: / ˈwizəl aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwiːzl aʊt / (BrE)
to avoid doing something that one should do, especially by being dishonest or cunning

Examples

To weasel out of work
Don’t weasel out now that things are getting difficult.
The manager accused her of trying to weasel out of her obligations.
Whenever there is extra work, he finds a way to weasel out of it.

  • synonym: evade - to escape or avoid, especially by cleverness or trickery
