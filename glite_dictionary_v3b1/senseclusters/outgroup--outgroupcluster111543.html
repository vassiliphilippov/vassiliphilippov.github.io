outgroup

noun
group outside community
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of people who are not part of a particular community or with whom someone does not identify

Examples

Social outgroup
The research compared the attitudes of the outgroup with those of the main community.
Children sometimes exclude others they see as part of the outgroup at school.
Prejudice can arise when people view the outgroup as very different from themselves.
During the discussion, she realized she was seen as part of the outgroup by the others.
New employees sometimes feel like they are in the outgroup until they get to know their colleagues.
Social scientists study how people treat members of the outgroup differently from those in their own group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ingroup - a group to which a person belongs or feels loyalty
  • synonym: outsider - a person who does not belong to a particular group
