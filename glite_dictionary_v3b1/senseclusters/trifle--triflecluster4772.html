trifle

verb
waste time idly or inefficiently
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪfəl / (BrE)
to spend time idly or without serious purpose; to waste time on unimportant matters

Examples

To trifle away time
She trifled away the afternoon instead of finishing her work.
The coach warned the team not to trifle during practice sessions.
We must not trifle with our responsibilities if we hope to succeed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dawdle - to waste time or move slowly
  • synonym: idle - to spend time doing nothing or very little
  • antonym: work - to engage in productive or purposeful activity
