high-level

adjective
of elevated rank or importance
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
of advanced rank or importance; involving people or matters at senior or important positions within an organization.

Examples

High-level meeting
A high-level discussion between the two governments is scheduled for next week.
She was promoted to a high-level position after years of hard work.
The company invited several high-level executives to the annual strategy meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-level - of relatively low rank or importance
  • synonym: senior - of a higher rank or status
