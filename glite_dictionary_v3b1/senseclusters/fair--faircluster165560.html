fair

adjective
attractive or beautiful in appearance
Pronunciation: / fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
attractive or pleasing in appearance, or having light-colored hair or skin

Examples

Fair hair
He wrote a poem about the fair maidens of the village.
The countryside looked especially fair in the morning sunlight.
The princess was so fair that everyone admired her beauty.
In the old tales, knights often fought for the honor of a fair princess.
Many poets wrote verses praising the fair maidens of their time.
She was considered the most fair lady at the ball, admired by all.
His fair complexion made his blue eyes stand out.
People with fair skin need to be especially careful in strong sunlight.
She has naturally fair hair that shines in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beautiful - pleasing the senses or mind aesthetically
  • synonym: blond - light-colored hair, especially yellowish or golden
  • synonym: comely - pleasing in appearance; attractive; fair
  • antonym: dark - having little or no light in color (of skin or hair)
