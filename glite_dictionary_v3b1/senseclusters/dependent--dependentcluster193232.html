(of a clause) unable to stand alone as a complete sentence and requiring another clause for meaning.

Examples

Dependent clause
A dependent clause must be attached to an independent clause to form a complete sentence.
Grammar exercises often ask students to identify dependent clauses within complex sentences.
The sentence, 'Although it was raining, we went outside,' begins with a dependent clause.
