Tarquinius

proper noun
last legendary Etruscan king of Rome
Pronunciation: / tɑːrˈkwɪniəs / (AmE), / tɑːˈkwɪnɪəs / (BrE)
according to legend, the seventh and last Etruscan king of Rome, expelled for his cruelty and ruled from 534 to 510 BC

Examples

Tarquinius the king
Tarquinius was overthrown by the Romans, marking the end of the monarchy and the beginning of the Republic.
Historians debate the historical accuracy of Tarquinius and his actions during his reign.
Legend claims that Tarquinius ruled with great cruelty, leading to his eventual expulsion.
