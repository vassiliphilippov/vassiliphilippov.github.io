chicken feed

noun
a very small amount of money
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn fiːd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪk.ɪn fiːd / (BrE)
an amount of money that is so small as to be insignificant or trivial

Examples

Chicken feed salary
Compared to their profits, the bonus was chicken feed.
For a company of that size, the fine was chicken feed.
He shrugged off the expense, saying it was just chicken feed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fortune - a large amount of money
  • synonym: pittance - a very small or inadequate amount of money
