corymbose

adjective
having or resembling a corymb (flower cluster)
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɪmˌboʊs / (AmE), / kəˈrɪmbəʊs / (BrE)
(of a plant or inflorescence) having or resembling the form of a corymb, with flower stalks arising at different points but reaching the same level to form a flat or slightly convex cluster.

Examples

Corymbose flower cluster
Botanists often describe certain inflorescences as corymbose because of their flattened appearance.
The plant produces corymbose clusters of tiny white flowers in late spring.
The remarkable corymbose grouping made the blossoms appear as if arranged in a single layer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corymb - a flower cluster whose lower stalks are longer so that the flowers form a flat or round head
