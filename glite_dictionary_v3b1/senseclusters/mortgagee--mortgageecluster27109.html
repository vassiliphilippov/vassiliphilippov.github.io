mortgagee

noun
person or organization lending money against a mortgage
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔrɡɪˈdʒi / (AmE), / ˌmɔːɡɪˈdʒiː / (BrE)
the person or organization, such as a bank, that lends money in a mortgage and holds the security interest until the debt is repaid

Examples

The mortgagee bank
If you default on your payments, the mortgagee can take possession of the property.
The mortgagee has the right to foreclose if the borrower fails to repay the loan.
The bank became our mortgagee when we signed the home loan agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mortgagor - the person who borrows money and gives a mortgage as security
