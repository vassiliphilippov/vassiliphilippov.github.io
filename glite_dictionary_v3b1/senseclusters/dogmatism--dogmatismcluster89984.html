dogmatism

noun
assertion of principles as undeniably true
9.2 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑːg.mə.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈdɔːg.mə.tɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈdɒg.mə.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true, without consideration of evidence or the opinions of others.

Examples

Strong dogmatism
Her dogmatism made it difficult to have a constructive conversation about the topic.
Many criticize political dogmatism for its refusal to accept new evidence or viewpoints.
The professor's dogmatism stifled any possibility of open debate in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogmatic - adjective form relating to dogmatism
  • antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider different ideas or opinions
