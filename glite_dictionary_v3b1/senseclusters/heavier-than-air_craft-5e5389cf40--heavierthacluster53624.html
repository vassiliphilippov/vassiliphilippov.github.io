heavier-than-air craft

noun

A type of aircraft that is not buoyant and requires a source of power to stay aloft and move, such as airplanes and helicopters, as opposed to lighter-than-air craft like balloons and airships.

Examples

Heavier-than-air craft like airplanes
Helicopters and airplanes are examples of heavier-than-air craft that rely on aerodynamic lift.
The Wright brothers were the first to build a successful heavier-than-air craft.
Unlike a balloon, a heavier-than-air craft depends on engines or rotors to stay in the sky.
