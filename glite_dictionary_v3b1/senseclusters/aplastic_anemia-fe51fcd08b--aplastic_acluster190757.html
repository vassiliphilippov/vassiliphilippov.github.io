aplastic anemia

noun
failure of bone marrow to produce blood cells
Pronunciation: / əˈplæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / əˈplæstɪk əˈniːmiə /; / əˈplɑːstɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A serious condition in which the bone marrow fails to produce enough blood cells, leading to a deficiency of red cells, white cells, and platelets; caused by factors such as disease or exposure to toxins.

Examples

Aplastic anemia diagnosis
Exposure to certain chemicals and radiation has been linked to cases of aplastic anemia.
People with aplastic anemia often require frequent blood transfusions to manage their condition.
The doctor diagnosed her with aplastic anemia after extensive blood tests and a bone marrow biopsy.
Certain medications and chemical exposure can trigger aplastic anaemia in susceptible individuals.
Her fatigue and frequent infections were ultimately traced to underlying aplastic anaemia.
The patient was diagnosed with aplastic anaemia after routine blood tests revealed severely low blood counts.
