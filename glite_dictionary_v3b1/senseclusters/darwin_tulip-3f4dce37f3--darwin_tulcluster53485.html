Darwin tulip

noun
group of tall, late-blooming tulips
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑrwɪn ˈtuːlɪp / (AmE), / ˈdɑːwɪn ˈtjuːlɪp / (BrE)
a type of very tall, late-blooming tulip with large, squarish flowers on strong stems, commonly grown in gardens

Examples

Darwin tulips bloom late
After planting Darwin tulips in the fall, we enjoyed their colorful blooms late into the spring.
Many gardeners choose Darwin tulips for their impressive height and durability in spring displays.
The Darwin tulip stands out because of its sturdy stem and strikingly large flower.
