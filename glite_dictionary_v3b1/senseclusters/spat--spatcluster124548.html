spat

noun
cloth or leather shoe and ankle covering
6.3
Pronunciation: / spæt / (AmE), / spæt / (BrE)
A cloth or leather covering, usually fastened under the foot and around the ankle, worn over the shoe and lower part of the leg, typically as a part of formal or military dress.

Examples

A pair of spats
During the 1920s, it was fashionable for men to wear spats with their suits.
The gentleman's white spats contrasted sharply with his black shoes.
The military parade featured soldiers in full uniform, including polished shoes and spats.
