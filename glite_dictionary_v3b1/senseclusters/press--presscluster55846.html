press

noun
machine applying pressure to materials
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / prɛs / (AmE), / prɛs / (BrE)
a machine or device that applies pressure to shape, cut, extract, compress, or hold things (such as a printing press or racket press)

Examples

A printing press
He operated the printing press in the factory.
The grapes are crushed in a mechanical press during winemaking.
The olive oil was extracted using a traditional press.
A wooden badminton racket can last longer if stored in a press.
He always puts his old tennis racket in a press to maintain its shape.
The vintage racquet came with its original press attached to prevent warping.
The invention of the printing press revolutionized the spread of information.
The old newspaper press in the basement is still in good condition.
Workers loaded paper into the press before the morning run.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clamp - device to hold objects tightly together
  • synonym: crusher - a machine that crushes material to extract juice or break down solids
  • synonym: printing press - a machine for printing text or pictures, especially one powered by hand or mechanically
