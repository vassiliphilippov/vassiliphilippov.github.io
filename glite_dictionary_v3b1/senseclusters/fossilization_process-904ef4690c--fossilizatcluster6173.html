fossilization process

noun
process by which remains become fossils
1.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /fɑː.sə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprɑː.ses/ /; / /fɑː.sə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprɔː.ses/ (AmE), /fɒs.ɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprɒ.ses/ /; / /fɒs.ɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprəʊ.ses/ (BrE)
the series of natural changes through which the remains of a living organism become a fossil over time

Examples

The fossilization process of bones
Scientists study the fossilization process to learn how organisms were preserved over millions of years.
The fossilization process can preserve bones, shells, and even traces of ancient plants.
Understanding the fossilization process helps paleontologists reconstruct prehistoric environments.
