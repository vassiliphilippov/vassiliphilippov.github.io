hypercalcemia

noun
abnormally high calcium in the blood
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərkælˈsiːmiə /; / ˌhaɪpərˌkælsiˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəkælˈsiːmɪə /; / ˌhʌɪpəˌkalsɪˈmiːə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by abnormally high levels of calcium in the blood, often caused by excessive bone resorption or certain diseases

Examples

Severe hypercalcemia symptoms
Severe hypercalcemia can cause symptoms such as confusion, muscle weakness, and dehydration.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hypercalcemia after reviewing the blood test results.
Treatment for hypercalcemia often involves addressing the underlying cause and managing calcium levels.
After her blood tests revealed hypercalcaemia, the doctor ordered further investigations to find the cause.
Chronic hypercalcaemia can lead to kidney stones and other complications if left untreated.
Some cancers are known to cause hypercalcaemia as a paraneoplastic syndrome.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypocalcaemia - abnormally low level of calcium in the blood
