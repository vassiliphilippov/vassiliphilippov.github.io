die out

verb
become extinct or disappear
3.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /daɪ aʊt/ (AmE), /daɪ aʊt/ (BrE)
to become extinct or to cease to exist completely, especially gradually over time

Examples

To die out slowly
Polar bears could die out if climate change continues unchecked.
Scientists warn that the rare butterfly may die out without urgent conservation efforts.
Some cultural traditions have died out over the centuries.
If we do not protect endangered species, they may die out completely.
Many languages have died out due to lack of use over generations.
The custom began to die out as modern technology became more widespread.

Related Terms

  • synonym: become extinct - to cease to exist as a species or group
  • synonym: disappear - to cease to be visible or to exist
  • antonym: survive - to continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship
  • synonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
