RNA polymerase

noun
enzyme that makes RNA from DNA
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrˌɛnˈeɪ pəˈlɪmərəˌseɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːˌɛnˈeɪ pəˈlɪmərˌeɪs / (BrE)
an enzyme that synthesizes RNA by copying a DNA sequence

Examples

RNA polymerase enzyme
RNA polymerase is essential for the transcription of genetic information in all living organisms.
Certain antibiotics inhibit bacterial RNA polymerase, preventing the bacteria from multiplying.
Scientists studied how RNA polymerase recognizes specific DNA sequences to start transcription.
