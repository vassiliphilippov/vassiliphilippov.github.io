bone china

noun
fine porcelain containing bone ash
Pronunciation: / boʊn ˈtʃaɪnə / (AmE), / bəʊn ˈtʃaɪnə / (BrE)
A fine, translucent porcelain made with bone ash, known for its strength and whiteness.

Examples

A bone china cup
Her wedding set was made of delicate bone china imported from England.
Many people choose bone china for formal dining because of its elegance and durability.
The museum displayed a rare collection of bone china teacups decorated with floral designs.
