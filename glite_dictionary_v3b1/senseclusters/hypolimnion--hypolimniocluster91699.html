hypolimnion

noun
lower lake layer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈlɪmnɪˌɒn / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈlɪmnɪən / (BrE)
the lower, colder layer of water in a stratified lake, lying beneath the warmer upper layer

Examples

The hypolimnion layer
During the summer, the hypolimnion remains much colder than the surface water in the lake.
Scientists measured oxygen levels in the hypolimnion to study the effects of pollution.
Fish species that prefer cold water often inhabit the hypolimnion during warm months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epilimnion - the upper, warmer layer of water in a stratified lake
  • synonym: thermocline - the layer in a body of water where temperature changes rapidly with depth
