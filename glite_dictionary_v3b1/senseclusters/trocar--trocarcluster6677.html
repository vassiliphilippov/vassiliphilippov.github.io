trocar

noun
surgical insertion tool
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊˌkɑr / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊˌkɑː / (BrE)
a sharp-pointed surgical instrument used to make an opening in the body, usually to drain fluids or allow access for other instruments

Examples

A trocar instrument
The surgeon carefully inserted a trocar to allow the fluid to drain from the abdomen.
During the procedure, a trocar was used to create an entry point for the laparoscopic instruments.
Nurses prepared the trocar and other tools before the operation began.
The surgeon inserted a trocar to drain the excess fluid from the patient's abdomen.
A trocar is commonly used in laparoscopic procedures to create an entry point for instruments.
After sterilizing the area, the doctor carefully used a trocar to access the cavity.
The surgeon inserted a trocar to allow access for the laparoscopic instruments.
A trocar is often used to drain fluid from a body cavity during surgery.
After the procedure, the nurse carefully removed the trocar from the patient's abdomen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cannula - a thin tube inserted into the body, often with a trocar
  • synonym: catheter - a flexible tube inserted into the body to remove or introduce fluids
  • synonym: needle - a slender, pointed instrument for piercing tissues
  • antonym: scalpel - a small, sharp knife used in surgery
