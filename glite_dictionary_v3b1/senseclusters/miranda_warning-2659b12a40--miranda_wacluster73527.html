Miranda warning

noun
police rights statement
4.7
Pronunciation: /mɪ.ˈræn.də ˈwɔːr.nɪŋ/ /; / /mər.ˈæn.də ˈwɔːr.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /mɪ.ˈræn.də ˈwɔː.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
the official statement of legal rights that police must give to a person before questioning them in custody (especially in the United States)

Examples

The Miranda warning
The police officer read the Miranda warning before starting the interrogation.
If the Miranda warning is not given, statements made by the suspect may not be used in court.
Every person taken into custody in the U.S. must hear the Miranda warning before being questioned.
The officer read the Miranda warning to the suspect before starting the interrogation.
If police fail to give a Miranda warning, statements made by the suspect may not be admissible in court.
Every American TV crime show features the Miranda warning during an arrest scene.
