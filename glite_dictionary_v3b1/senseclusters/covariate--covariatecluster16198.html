covariate

noun
control variable
8.6
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ˈve.rɪ.eɪt/ /; / /kə.ˈve.rɪ.eɪt/ (AmE), /əʊ.ˈve.rɪ.eɪt/ /; / /kəʊ.ˈve.rɪ.eɪt/ (BrE)
a variable in a statistical or scientific study that is included in the analysis to control for or account for its effect, but is not the main variable being studied

Examples

A covariate variable
The researchers included age as a covariate to account for its influence on the results.
Education level was treated as a covariate in the statistical model to reduce confounding.
By adding gender as a covariate, the analysis became more accurate and reliable.

In the analysis, income was treated as a covariate to adjust for socioeconomic differences.
Age often serves as a covariate in medical studies to account for its effect on health outcomes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confounder - a variable that influences both the dependent and independent variables
  • antonym: dependent variable - the outcome variable being measured in a study
  • synonym: independent variable - a variable that is manipulated to observe its effect on the outcome
  • antonym: outcome - the result or dependent variable in a study
  • antonym: predictor - a variable used to predict the outcome in statistical analysis
