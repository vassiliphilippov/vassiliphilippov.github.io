pretest

verb
to test in advance
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtɛst / (BrE)
to test or check something in advance to identify problems or ensure it works properly

Examples

To pretest new questions
The research team will pretest the survey questions with a small group before the main study.
We are pretesting the new training program to ensure it meets employees' needs.
Before releasing the advertisement nationwide, the company decided to pretest it in select markets.
The researchers pretested the survey questions to make sure they were clear.
Before the official launch, the team will pretest the new app with a small group of users.
It is important to pretest equipment to avoid problems during the main event.
