prosecute

verb
charge and try someone in court for a crime
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.sɪ.kjuːt /; / ˈprɑːsɪˌkjuːt / (AmE), / ˈprɒs.ɪ.kjuːt /; / ˈprɒsɪkjuːt / (BrE)
to officially bring someone to court for a crime and try to prove they are guilty

Examples

To prosecute a case
If there is enough evidence, the state will prosecute the suspect in court.
She was prosecuted for fraud after an extensive investigation.
The district attorney decided to prosecute the company for environmental violations.
He was prosecuted for fraud in a federal court last year.
If they find sufficient evidence, the attorney will prosecute the offender.
The state decided to prosecute the company for violating environmental laws.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defend - to represent or support someone in a court of law against attack or prosecution
  • synonym: sue - to start a civil legal case against someone, especially for compensation
