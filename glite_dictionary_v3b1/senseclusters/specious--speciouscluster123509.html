specious

adjective
seemingly true but actually false
8.7 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàspiÀê É…ôs /; / Ààspi É…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàspiÀê É…ôs / (BrE)
seeming to be true or correct, but actually false or misleading

Examples

A specious argument
Her specious reasoning hid the real flaws in her plan.
The advertisement made specious claims about the product's effectiveness.
The politician presented a specious argument that persuaded many people at first.
His proposal sounded convincing, but it was based on specious assumptions.
She saw through the specious charm and refused to trust his promises.
The lawyer's specious arguments briefly convinced the jury, but the truth soon emerged.

Synonyms

  • sophistical
    9.9 0.08/m
    sophistical describes arguments that sound plausible but are actually flawed, while specious focuses on a false appearance of truth.
  • sophistic
    9.9 0.07/m
    sophistic describes reasoning that sounds clever and aims to deceive, while specious appears true but is actually false.
  • fallacious
    8.3 1.32/m
    fallacious means built on wrong or unsound logic, while specious emphasizes a deceptive surface that seems true but is false.
  • misleading
    4.4 12/m
    misleading gives a wrong impression that may confuse people, while specious specifically seems true even though it is false.
