subplate

noun
transient brain layer
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.pleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.pleɪt/ (BrE)
a temporary layer of neurons beneath the cortical plate in the developing brain, important for early neural connections

Examples

Brain subplate
Scientists study the subplate to understand how early brain circuits are formed.
Damage to the subplate during development can affect later brain function.
The subplate disappears as the brain matures and new neural pathways are established.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cortical plate - the developing layer of the cerebral cortex above the subplate
