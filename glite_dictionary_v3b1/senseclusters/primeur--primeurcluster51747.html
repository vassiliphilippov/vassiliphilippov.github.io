primeur

noun
early-season produce
Pronunciation: / priːˈmɜr / (AmE), / priːˈmɜː / (BrE)
a fresh fruit or vegetable harvested and sold early in the season, often before the usual harvest time

Examples

Fresh primeur vegetables
The market was filled with primeur vegetables, freshly picked and bursting with flavor.
Chefs often seek out primeur produce to create seasonal dishes in early spring.
She paid extra for the primeur strawberries, which arrived weeks before the main harvest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: late crop - produce harvested later in the season
  • synonym: new season - produce available at the start of the harvest
