palpebral conjunctiva

noun
lining of eyelids' inner surface
Pronunciation: / pælˈpibrəl ˌkɑn(d)ʒʌŋkˈtaɪvə / (AmE), / palˈpiːbrəl ˌkɒndʒʌŋkˈtʌɪvə / (BrE)
the portion of the conjunctiva that lines the inner surface of the eyelids and is continuous with the bulbar conjunctiva

Examples

Palpebral conjunctiva inflammation
Inflammation of the palpebral conjunctiva is often seen in allergic conjunctivitis.
Redness of the palpebral conjunctiva may indicate an underlying eye condition.
The doctor examined her palpebral conjunctiva for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bulbar conjunctiva - the part of the conjunctiva covering the eyeball
