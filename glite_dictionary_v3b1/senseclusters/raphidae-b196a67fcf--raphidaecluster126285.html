Raphidae

noun
family of extinct dodos and solitaires
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈræf.ɪ.deɪ/ /; / /ˈræf.ɪ.diː/ (AmE), /ˈræf.ɪ.diː/ /; / /ˈræf.ɪ.deɪ/ (BrE)
a former family of extinct flightless birds, including the dodo and solitaires, native to the islands of the Indian Ocean.

Examples

The Raphidae family
Scientists study the bones of Raphidae to understand the evolution of flightlessness in island birds.
Taxonomic revisions have reclassified members of the former Raphidae into different families.
The Raphidae included some of the most famous extinct birds, such as the dodo.

Related Terms
