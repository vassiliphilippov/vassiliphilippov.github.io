overdraft

noun
withdrawal exceeding account balance
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌdræft / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌdrɑːft / (BrE)
An amount of money that is withdrawn from a bank account in excess of the available balance, resulting in a negative balance.

Examples

Bank overdraft
Managing your account carefully can help you avoid an overdraft.
She accidentally incurred an overdraft after making several large purchases.
The bank charges a fee whenever your account goes into overdraft.

Related Terms

  • antonym: credit - a sum of money added to an account
  • synonym: debit - a recorded withdrawal from an account
