legislative body

noun
group authorized to make laws
5.8 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌlɛdʒɪsˈleɪtɪv ˈbɑdi / (AmE), / ˌlɛdʒɪsˈleɪtɪv ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
An official group of people that has the authority to make, change, or repeal laws for a country, state, or other political unit.

Examples

The legislative body meets
Debates in the legislative body often shape the direction of national policy.
Each state in the union has its own legislative body that creates local laws.
The parliament is the country's most prominent legislative body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congress - the national legislative body of the United States
  • antonym: executive branch - branch of government responsible for enforcing laws (contrasts with legislative body)
  • synonym: parliament - a country's legislative body, especially in the UK and some other countries
