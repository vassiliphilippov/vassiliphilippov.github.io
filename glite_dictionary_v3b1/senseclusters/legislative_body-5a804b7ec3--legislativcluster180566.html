legislative body

noun
group authorized to make laws
4.5 0.84/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒlÉ›dÊ’ÉªsËˆleÉªtÉªv ËˆbÉ‘di / (AmE), / ËŒlÉ›dÊ’ÉªsËˆleÉªtÉªv ËˆbÉ’di / (BrE)
An official group of people that has the authority to make, change, or repeal laws for a country, state, or other political unit.

Examples

The legislative body meets
Debates in the legislative body often shape the direction of national policy.
Each state in the union has its own legislative body that creates local laws.
The parliament is the country's most prominent legislative body.

Synonyms

  • legislature
    5.3 30/m
    legislature is the formal name for a law-making group in a region, while legislative body is a general term for such groups.
  • legislative assembly
    5.4 0.10/m
    legislative assembly is a body of elected representatives for a region or state, while legislative body covers any law-making group.
  • general assembly
    4.9 0.42/m
    general assembly is a name for representative law-making groups in governments or organizations, while legislative body is the wider term.
  • parliament
    3.5 25/m
    parliament is the main law-making body in some countries with specific traditions, while legislative body is a neutral general term.
  • congress
    3.7 0.53/m
    congress is the national legislature in some countries, especially the United States, while legislative body can mean any law-making group.
  • legislative council
    6.8 0.09/m
    legislative council is often a single-chamber law-making body in some areas, while legislative body is the general term for any law-making group.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congress - the national legislative body of the United States
  • antonym: executive branch - branch of government responsible for enforcing laws (contrasts with legislative body)
  • synonym: parliament - a country's legislative body, especially in the UK and some other countries
