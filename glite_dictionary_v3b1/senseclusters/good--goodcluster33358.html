Synonyms

  • fine
    1.0 136/m
    fine often praises something as satisfactory or pleasant in everyday speech, while good is a broad word for positive quality.
  • acceptable
    2.5 40/m
    acceptable means good enough for a purpose or rule, while good is a general positive description used in many contexts.
  • satisfactory
    2.3 7.11/m
    satisfactory means it meets needs though it is not excellent, while good covers any positive or desirable quality in general.
  • decent
    2.3 22/m
    decent describes something fairly good and proper for a situation, while good is a wider, more general praise word.
  • okay
    1.0 105/m
    okay means just acceptable and often informal in speech, while good is a stronger, more general word for positive quality.
  • all right
    1.0 49/m
    all right describes something acceptable or good enough in casual situations, while good is a general positive description used everywhere.
  • adequate
    2.7 28/m
    adequate means barely enough in amount or quality, while good suggests a clearly positive or desirable quality beyond mere sufficiency.
