dogman

noun
crane signal worker
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɡˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌmæn / (BrE)
a worker on a construction site who attaches loads to cranes and signals or guides the crane operator during lifting operations

Examples

Construction dogman
The dogman used hand signals to direct the crane operator during the lift.
Before the heavy beam was moved, the dogman checked that all safety measures were in place.
On every large construction site, a skilled dogman is essential for safe crane operations.
The experienced dogman made sure the load was safely attached before giving the signal to lift.
Every construction site needs a skilled dogman to coordinate with the crane operator.
The dogman used hand signals to guide the crane as it moved the steel beams into place.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crane operator - a person who operates a crane on a construction site
  • synonym: rigger - a worker who sets up and secures loads for lifting
  • synonym: slinger - a person who attaches and detaches loads to lifting equipment
