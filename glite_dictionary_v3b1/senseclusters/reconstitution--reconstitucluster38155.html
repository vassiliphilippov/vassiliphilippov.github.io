reconstitution

noun
restoring to original state
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkɑːnstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkɒnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the process of restoring something to its original state or form, especially by adding a necessary component

Examples

Reconstitution of milk
The reconstitution of the powdered milk was simple with the addition of water.
After years of neglect, the museum began the reconstitution of the ancient artifact.
The chef oversaw the reconstitution of the sauce by mixing in fresh cream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissolution - the act of breaking something apart or ending its existence
  • synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original condition
