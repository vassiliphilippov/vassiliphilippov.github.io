eagle-eyed

adjective
having very keen eyesight
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈiɡəl aɪd / (AmE), / ˈiːɡl aɪd / (BrE)
having exceptionally keen eyesight or able to notice details from a great distance

Examples

An eagle-eyed observer
Park rangers are often eagle-eyed, noticing wildlife that others would miss.
The detective was so eagle-eyed that he spotted the tiniest clue at the crime scene.
With her eagle-eyed focus, she quickly found the error in the document.

Related Terms
