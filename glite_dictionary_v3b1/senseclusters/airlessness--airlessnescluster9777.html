airlessness

noun
lack of liveliness
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈeələsnəs / (BrE)
a quality of lacking liveliness, freshness, or imagination in style, speech, or atmosphere

Examples

Airlessness of a speech
The airlessness of the meeting made it hard for anyone to feel inspired or creative.
Some critics noted the airlessness in the novel's style, which lacked energy and originality.
Despite the beautiful setting, there was an airlessness to the performance that left the audience unmoved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dullness - the quality of being uninteresting or lacking excitement
  • synonym: staleness - the quality of being no longer fresh or interesting
  • antonym: vitality - the state of being lively and energetic
