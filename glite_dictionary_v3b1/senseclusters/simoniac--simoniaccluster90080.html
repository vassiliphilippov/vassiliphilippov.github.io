simoniac

noun
seller of church offices
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / sɪˈməʊniˌak / (BrE)
a person who buys or sells church offices or sacred things, especially for personal gain

Examples

A simoniac priest
The medieval church condemned any simoniac who tried to purchase a position of power.
Dante's Inferno describes the punishment of the simoniac in the eighth circle of Hell.
Accusations of being a simoniac could ruin a clergyman's reputation in the Middle Ages.
The bishop accused the simoniac of corrupting the church with his actions.
In medieval times, a simoniac could face severe punishment from religious authorities.
Dante's Inferno places the simoniac among the damned for their spiritual crimes.
