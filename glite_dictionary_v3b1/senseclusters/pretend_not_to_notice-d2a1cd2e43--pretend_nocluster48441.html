pretend not to notice

verb
deliberately act as if unaware
Pronunciation: / prɪˈtɛnd nɑt tu ˈnoʊtɪs / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛnd nɒt tʊ ˈnəʊtɪs / (BrE)
to deliberately act as if one is unaware of something, often to avoid embarrassment or confrontation

Examples

To pretend not to notice a mistake
During the meeting, she pretended not to notice the awkward silence that followed his comment.
I saw him spill coffee on his shirt, but I pretended not to notice to spare him embarrassment.
The teacher pretended not to notice the students passing notes at the back of the classroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acknowledge - to admit or recognize the existence of something
  • synonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone
