hold one's head high

verb
behave proudly and confidently despite difficulty
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /hoʊld wʌnz hɛd haɪ/ /; / /hoʊld wʌns hɛd haɪ/ (AmE), /həʊld wʌnz hɛd haɪ/ /; / /həʊld wʌns hɛd haɪ/ (BrE)
to behave in a proud and confident way, not ashamed or embarrassed, especially after experiencing difficulty or criticism

Examples

To hold his head high proudly
After the unfair criticism, he managed to hold his head high in front of everyone.
Despite the setback, she continued to hold her head high and work toward her goals.
You should always hold your head high when you know you've done the right thing.
Despite making mistakes on stage, he managed to hold his head high and finish his speech.
Even after the unfair dismissal, she continued to hold her head high at work.
They lost the match, but the coach told them to hold their heads high.
