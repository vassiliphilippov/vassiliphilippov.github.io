passive air defense

noun
protection against air attacks without active engagement
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪv ˈɛr dɪˌfɛns / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪv ˈeə dɪˌfɛns / (BrE)
Measures taken to protect against air attacks by using deception, dispersion, or protective construction rather than direct engagement with enemy aircraft.

Examples

Passive air defense measures
Concrete bunkers are a classic example of passive air defense used during wartime.
Effective passive air defense can reduce vulnerability to enemy air raids without firing a single shot.
The military implemented passive air defense techniques, such as camouflage and dispersing important assets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active air defense - defensive measures involving direct action to destroy or repel attacking aircraft
