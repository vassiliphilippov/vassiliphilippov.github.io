suborder Zygoptera

noun
suborder of damselflies (insects)
Pronunciation: / zoʊˈɡɑːptərə / (AmE), / zaɪˈɡɒptərə / (BrE)
A suborder of insects in the order Odonata, comprising damselflies, which are slender-bodied, delicate-winged insects distinct from dragonflies.

Examples

Suborder Zygoptera damselflies
Scientists discovered a new member of the suborder Zygoptera in the rainforest.
The suborder Zygoptera includes all species commonly known as damselflies.
Unlike dragonflies, insects in the suborder Zygoptera typically hold their wings together when at rest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damselfly - a slender, delicate insect in the order Odonata
  • antonym: suborder Anisoptera - suborder comprising dragonflies (insects related to damselflies)
