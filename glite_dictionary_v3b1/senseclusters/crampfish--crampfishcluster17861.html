crampfish

noun
electric ray fish of order Torpediniformes
9.9 9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkræmp.fɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈkræmp.fɪʃ/ (BrE)
any sluggish, bottom-dwelling electric ray of the order Torpediniformes, having a rounded body and electric organs on each side of the head that can emit strong electric discharges.

Examples

The crampfish fish
A crampfish can deliver a powerful electric shock as a defense mechanism.
Fishermen once believed the shock from a crampfish could cure headaches.
The crampfish relies on its electric organs to stun prey on the ocean floor.
