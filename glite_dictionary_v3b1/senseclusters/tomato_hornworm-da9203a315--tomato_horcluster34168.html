tomato hornworm

noun
a caterpillar pest of tomato and potato plants
Pronunciation: / təˈmeɪɾoʊ ˈhɔrnˌwɜrm / (AmE), / təˈmɑːtəʊ ˈhɔːnwɜːm / (BrE)
a large green and white-striped caterpillar that feeds on tomato and potato plants, the larval stage of a hawkmoth

Examples

A tomato hornworm caterpillar
A single tomato hornworm can defoliate a young tomato plant within days.
Gardeners often struggle with tomato hornworm infestations late in summer.
Handpicking tomato hornworms from your plants is an effective organic control method.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tobacco hornworm - a similar green caterpillar that feeds on tobacco and other plants
