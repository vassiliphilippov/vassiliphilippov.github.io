rebuttal

noun
counter-argument; act of refuting
5.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈbʌt.əl/ /; / /riː.ˈbʌt.əl/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈbʌt.əl/ (BrE)
a statement or argument made to oppose or disprove another argument or accusation

Examples

A rebuttal statement
In her article, she offered a strong rebuttal to her opponent's claims.
The debate team prepared a detailed rebuttal for every possible argument.
The lawyer's rebuttal to the allegations was concise and persuasive.
After the plaintiff's surrejoinder, the defendant filed an extensive rebuttal.
The attorney offered a rebuttal to the opposing counsel's closing arguments.
The judge allowed a brief rebuttal to address the new evidence presented.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confirmation - the act of supporting a statement as true
  • synonym: refutation - the act of proving a statement to be wrong
  • degree lower: rejoinder - (Law) The defendant's answer to the plaintiff's replication
