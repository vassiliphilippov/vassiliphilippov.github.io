wink

noun
quick closing and opening of one eye
2.4
Pronunciation: / wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk / (BrE)
a quick closing and opening of one eye, often as a signal, greeting, or reflex

Examples

A friendly wink
He tried to hide his excitement, but a nervous wink betrayed him.
She gave him a quick wink as she walked by.
The bright light made my eye do a reflexive wink.
She gave me a playful wink across the table to let me know she was joking.
The magician ended his trick with a wink to the amazed audience.
With a sly wink, he signaled that he understood the plan.
