metatheory

noun
theory about theories
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtəˌθɪəri / (AmE), / ˈmɛtəˌθɪəri / (BrE)
a theory that examines or explains the structure, methods, or assumptions of other theories

Examples

Metatheory of science
The professor introduced the concept of metatheory to help students understand how scientific frameworks are evaluated.
Philosophers often use metatheory to analyze the foundations of different schools of thought.
A strong metatheory can clarify the assumptions behind competing psychological models.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metaphilosophy - the study of the nature and methods of philosophy itself
  • degree lower: theory - a set of ideas intended to explain something
