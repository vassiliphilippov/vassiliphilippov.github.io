live in the shadow

verb
exist less recognized due to another
Pronunciation: / lɪv ɪn ðə ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / lɪv ɪn ðə ˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
to exist in a state of being less recognized or important because of the presence or achievements of someone else

Examples

To live in the shadow of success
After his famous sister won the award, he began to live in the shadow of her success.
Growing up with a brilliant older sibling, she always thought she would live in the shadow of his achievements.
Many artists feel frustrated when they must live in the shadow of a renowned mentor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overshadow - make less important or prominent by comparison
