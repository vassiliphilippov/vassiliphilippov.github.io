myriad

noun
a very large number
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˈmɪr.i.əd / (BrE)
an extremely large, but unspecified, number of things or people

Examples

A myriad of stars
A myriad of stars filled the night sky, twinkling above the quiet town.
He faced a myriad of details when planning the event.
The internet has opened up a myriad of new opportunities for learning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: countless - too many to be counted
  • synonym: multitude - a large number of people or things
