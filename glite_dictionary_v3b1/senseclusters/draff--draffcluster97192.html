draff

noun
residue of malt/grain after mashing
9.6
Pronunciation: / dræf / (AmE), / drɑːf / (BrE)
the residue of malt or grain left in the mash-kettle after mashing and lautering, often used as animal feed

Examples

Brewing draff residue
After brewing, a thick layer of draff was left at the bottom of the kettle.
Modern distilleries often process draff for use as nutritious animal fodder.
The brewery donated its draff to local farmers to feed their cattle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spent grain - the byproduct left after mashing grains in brewing
