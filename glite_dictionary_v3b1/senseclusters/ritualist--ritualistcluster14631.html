ritualist

noun
person advocating strict observance of rituals
3.2 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈrɪt.juː.ə.lɪst/ /; / /ˈrɪt.ʃʊə.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈrɪt.ʃʊə.lɪst/ /; / /ˈrɪt.juː.ə.lɪst/ (BrE)
a person who advocates or practices strict observance of established rituals, especially in religion or ceremony

Examples

Strict ritualist
As a devoted ritualist, she insisted on following every detail of the ceremony.
His ritualist approach made the meetings long but highly organized.
Some members of the church accused the new priest of being too much of a ritualist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formalist - a person who places excessive emphasis on formality or established forms
  • antonym: iconoclast - a person who attacks or rejects cherished beliefs or institutions, particularly established rituals
