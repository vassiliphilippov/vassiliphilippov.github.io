out of the way

adverb
murdered; eliminated as an obstacle
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ðə weɪ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə weɪ / (BrE)
killed or removed as an obstacle, especially by murder or other violent means.

Examples

Put someone out of the way
Rumors spread that the witness was put out of the way to protect the secret.
She was determined to get her competitor out of the way by any means necessary.
The mob boss wanted his rival out of the way before making his move.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eliminated - removed or killed as an opponent
  • antonym: safe - free from being threatened or attacked
