Old World crayfish

noun
small crayfish of Europe, Asia, and W. North America
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊld ˈwɝːld ˈkreɪˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈwɜːld ˈkreɪˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A group of small freshwater crayfish native to Europe, Asia, and western North America.

Examples

Old World crayfish species
Researchers are studying how pollution affects Old World crayfish populations in Asia.
The Old World crayfish is often found in European rivers and streams.
Unlike its American counterparts, the Old World crayfish has a narrower range in North America.
