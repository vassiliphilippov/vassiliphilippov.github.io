unpossible

adjective
not possible (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpɑːsəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɒsəbəl / (BrE)
archaic or dialectal form of 'impossible', meaning not able to be done or achieved

Examples

An unpossible task
In the old tale, the hero faced an unpossible challenge that no one had ever overcome.
She declared it was simply unpossible to cross the river during the storm.
The villagers believed such a feat was unpossible in their time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impossible - not able to be done or achieved
  • antonym: possible - able to be done or achieved
