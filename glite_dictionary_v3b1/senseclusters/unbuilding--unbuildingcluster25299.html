unbuilding

noun
dismantling of systems
9.5
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈbɪl.dɪŋ/ /; / /ən.ˈbɪl.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈbɪl.dɪŋ/ /; / /ən.ˈbɪl.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of deliberately dismantling or undoing established systems, structures, or institutions, often used figuratively.

Examples

Gradual unbuilding of traditions
The slow unbuilding of the old traditions made way for new ideas in the community.
After years of reform, the unbuilding of the outdated system was finally complete.
Critics argued that the policy led to the unbuilding of important social institutions.
