lob a question

verb
ask a question casually
Pronunciation: / lɑb ə ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (AmE), / lɒb ə ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
to ask a question in a casual or nonchalant way, often without expectation of a challenging response

Examples

To lob a question casually
During the interview, she lobbed a question about the company's future plans.
He likes to lob a question to break the ice at meetings.
The reporter lobbed a question from the back of the crowded room.
