bauble

noun
mock scepter of a court jester
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔbəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔːbəl / (BrE)
a mock scepter, often ornamented, carried by a court jester as a symbol of their role

Examples

Jester's bauble
A court jester's bauble often featured a carved head with bells.
In medieval times, the bauble was a recognizable sign of the jester's profession.
The jester spun his bauble in the air to amuse the king.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scepter - an ornamented staff carried by rulers as a symbol of authority
