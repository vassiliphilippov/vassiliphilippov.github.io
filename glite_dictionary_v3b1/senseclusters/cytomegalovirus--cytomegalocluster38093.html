cytomegalovirus

noun
a virus causing enlarged cells, birth defects
9.9 7.6
Pronunciation: / saɪ.toʊˌmɛɡ.ə.loʊˈvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / saɪ.təʊˌmɛɡ.ə.ləʊˈvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
a virus of the herpesvirus group that causes enlargement of infected cells, can lead to birth defects, and often affects people with weakened immune systems

Examples

cytomegalovirus infection
Doctors screened the newborn for cytomegalovirus after symptoms of hearing loss appeared.
Many transplant patients are at risk for infections like cytomegalovirus because their immune systems are suppressed.
Researchers are developing vaccines to protect against cytomegalovirus in pregnant women.
