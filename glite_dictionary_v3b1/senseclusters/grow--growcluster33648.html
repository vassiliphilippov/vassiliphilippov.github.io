grow

verb
develop and reach maturity
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊ / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ / (BrE)
to become larger, more developed, or more mature; to increase in size, amount, or importance

Examples

Children grow taller
After the surgery, he was able to grow into a healthy adult.
All children grow at their own pace and reach maturity in their own time.
Young animals must grow for several years before they reach full maturity.
As the city started to grow, new neighborhoods appeared on its outskirts.
Profits have continued to grow steadily over the past five years.
What began as a hobby quickly grew into a successful business.
After the holiday, he decided to grow a mustache for the first time.
Many teenagers grow taller rapidly during puberty.
She started to grow her hair long after college.

Related Terms

  • synonym: develop - to become more advanced or elaborate; grow or cause to grow
  • synonym: expand - to become or make larger or more extensive
  • synonym: mature - to become fully developed or grown
  • antonym: shrink - to become smaller in size or amount
