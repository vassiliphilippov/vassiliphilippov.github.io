syncretist

adjective
combining beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkrətɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrɪtɪst / (BrE)
favoring or characterized by the combination of different beliefs, schools of thought, or practices

Examples

Syncretist philosophy
The syncretist approach of the philosopher allowed him to draw from many different traditions.
Her syncretist views made her open to blending various cultural practices in her ceremonies.
A syncretist attitude can help bridge gaps between different religious communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eclectic - selecting ideas or styles from a broad range of sources
  • antonym: orthodox - adhering strictly to traditional beliefs or practices
