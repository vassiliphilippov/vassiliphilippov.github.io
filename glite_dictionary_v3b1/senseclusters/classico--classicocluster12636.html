classico

adjective
Italian wine designation
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈklæs.ɪ.koʊ/ /; / /ˈklæs.iː.koʊ/ /; / /ˈklɑːs.ɪ.koʊ/ (AmE), /ˈklɑːs.ɪ.kəʊ/ /; / /ˈklæs.ɪ.kəʊ/ (BrE)
used in the names of Italian wines to indicate that the wine comes from the original or historic production area, often signifying traditional quality.

Examples

Chianti classico wine
They ordered a bottle of Chianti classico to pair with their dinner.
The label indicated that the wine was a classico, produced in the region's historic area.
Many wine enthusiasts prefer the taste of a classico over newer varieties.
