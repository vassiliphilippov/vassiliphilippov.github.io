interpellation

noun
formal demand for official explanation in parliament
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˌpɛˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌpɛˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A formal parliamentary procedure in which a member demands that a government official explain an act or policy.

Examples

Parliamentary interpellation
During yesterday's session, the opposition filed an interpellation regarding the government's new tax plan.
The minister was required to respond to the interpellation submitted by several members of parliament.
The practice of interpellation serves as a check on executive power in many legislative bodies.
