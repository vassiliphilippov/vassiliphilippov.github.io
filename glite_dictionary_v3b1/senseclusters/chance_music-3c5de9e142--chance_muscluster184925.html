chance music

noun
music using chance or indeterminate elements
6.8 8.0
Pronunciation: /tʃæns ˈmjuː.zɪk/ (AmE), /tʃɑːns ˈmjuː.zɪk/ (BrE)
A style of 20th-century music in which some elements are left to chance or to the performer's discretion, often resulting in indeterminate outcomes for each performance.

Examples

Chance music style
John Cage is well-known for his innovative use of chance music in composition.
Many performers enjoy the unique possibilities that chance music offers during each concert.
The unpredictability of chance music challenges both the audience and musicians alike.
