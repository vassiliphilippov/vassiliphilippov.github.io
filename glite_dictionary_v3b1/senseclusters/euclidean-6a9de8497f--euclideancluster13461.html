Euclidean

adjective
relating to classical geometry developed by Euclid
7.1 6.4
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈkliː.dɪ.ən/ /; / /juː.ˈklɪd.ɪ.ən/ (AmE), /juː.ˈklɪd.ɪ.ən/ /; / /juː.ˈkliː.dɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the system of geometry based on the work of the ancient Greek mathematician Euclid, characterized especially by the idea that parallel lines never meet and by flat, two-dimensional space.

Examples

Euclidean geometry
In a Euclidean space, the sum of the angles in a triangle is always 180 degrees.
Most maps use Euclidean distances to calculate the shortest path between two points.
The rules of Euclidean geometry do not apply on the surface of a sphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geometry - branch of mathematics concerned with the properties of space
  • antonym: non-Euclidean - relating to geometries that are not based on Euclid's parallel postulate
