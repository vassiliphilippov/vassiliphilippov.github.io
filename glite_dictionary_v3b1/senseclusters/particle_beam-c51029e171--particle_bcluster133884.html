particle beam

noun
narrow stream of particles
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrtɪkəl ˌbiːm / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪkəl ˌbiːm / (BrE)
A narrow, focused stream of particles, such as atoms, electrons, or molecules, typically used in scientific or medical applications.

Examples

A particle beam stream
Doctors use a particle beam to target cancer cells during certain types of radiation therapy.
The particle beam passed through several magnetic lenses to focus it precisely.
The accelerator generates a particle beam for advanced physics experiments.
