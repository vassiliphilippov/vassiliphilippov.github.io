welterweight

noun
weight class in boxing and combat sports
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛltərˌweɪt /; / ˈwɛltɚˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɛltəweɪt /; / ˈwɛltəˌweɪt / (BrE)
a weight class in boxing, wrestling, and other combat sports for competitors heavier than lightweights but lighter than middleweights (typically around 147 pounds or 67 kg); also, an athlete who competes in this class

The welterweight division is often regarded as one of the most competitive in boxing.
