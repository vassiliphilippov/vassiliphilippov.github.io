no-win

adjective
certain to end in failure or disappointment
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊ wɪn / (AmE), / ˈnəʊ wɪn / (BrE)
(of a situation or outcome) certain to end in failure or disappointment, with no possibility of success.

Examples

A no-win situation
Caught between two angry friends, she faced a real no-win dilemma.
It was a no-win situation, no matter how hard we tried.
The company found itself in a no-win scenario, forced to choose between layoffs and bankruptcy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lose-lose - a situation where all options result in a bad outcome
