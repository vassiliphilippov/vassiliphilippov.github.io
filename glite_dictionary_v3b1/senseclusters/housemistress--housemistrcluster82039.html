housemistress

noun
female boarding house head
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌmɪstrəs / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌmɪstrɪs / (BrE)
a woman in charge of the welfare, discipline, and daily life of students or residents in a boarding school, dormitory, or similar house

Examples

As a housemistress, she was responsible for both discipline and support in the girls' house.
The housemistress checked that all the girls were in their rooms before lights out.
As a respected housemistress, she organized events and cared for the students' well-being.

The housemistress greeted each new student and showed them to their rooms.
As a housemistress, she ensured the boarding house was always clean and welcoming.
Parents trusted the housemistress to care for their children during the school term.
