corrupt

verb
to change from original, often to invalid
2.7 4.2
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈrʌpt/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrʌpt/ (BrE)
to alter something, especially data or information, from its original form, often making it invalid or unusable

Examples

To corrupt data

He accidentally corrupted the document by saving it in the wrong format.
If the database is corrupted, it may be impossible to recover the original information.
