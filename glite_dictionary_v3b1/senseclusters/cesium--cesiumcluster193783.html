cesium

noun
a soft, highly reactive metallic element (Cs)
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːziəm / (AmE), / ˈsiːziəm / (BrE)
a soft, silver-white, ductile metallic element that is liquid at or near room temperature and is the most electropositive and alkaline of all elements

Examples

Liquid cesium
Liquid cesium is stored carefully due to its extreme reactivity with water.
Scientists use cesium in atomic clocks because of its precise frequency.
The soft metallic cesium melted quickly in the scientist's hand.
Atomic clocks often use caesium to achieve extremely precise measurements of time.
Because caesium is liquid at room temperature, it is unique among the alkali metals.
The scientists carefully stored the caesium in sealed containers to prevent it from reacting with moisture in the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkali metal - any of the group of univalent metals (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr) with similar properties
  • synonym: cesium - American English spelling of caesium
  • degree higher: francium - an alkali metal, more electropositive than caesium
  • degree lower: potassium - a soft, silvery-white alkali metal element
