sectarianly

adverb
in a divisive way
9.7
Pronunciation: /sekˈter.ɪ.ən.lɪ/ (AmE), /sekˈteə.rɪ.ən.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that supports a particular religious or political group, often causing division or bias

Examples

Applied sectarianly in law
The law was applied sectarianly, favoring one group over another.
Debates in the region are often conducted sectarianly, deepening existing divisions.
The leader was accused of acting sectarianly during the negotiations.
The debate was conducted sectarianly, with each side defending its own religious traditions.
He analyzed the conflict sectarianly, focusing on the divisions between the groups.
The article approached the issue sectarianly, emphasizing the doctrines of each faction.
The discussion was handled sectarianly, with each side refusing to listen to the other.
Policies were implemented sectarianly, favoring only members of one group.
The media reported the events sectarianly, highlighting only their preferred perspective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divisively - in a way that causes disagreement or hostility
  • antonym: impartially - in a way that is fair and not biased toward any side
  • antonym: neutrally - in a way that does not support any side
  • synonym: partisanly - in a way that strongly supports a particular group or cause
  • antonym: tolerantly - in a way that shows acceptance of different views or beliefs
