narrow

verb
make or become less wide or more restricted
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
to make or become less wide or more restricted in extent, amount, or scope (physically or abstractly)

Examples

The road narrows ahead
The path narrowed suddenly, forcing us to walk single file.
The river began to narrow as it flowed toward the mountains.
We need to narrow our list of candidates before making a decision.
Her throat narrowed as she tried to swallow the large pill.
His eyes narrowed in suspicion as he listened to her story.
The path narrows sharply between the two cliffs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constrict - become tighter or narrower, especially in a passage of the body
  • antonym: widen - to make or become wider
