polyculture

noun
multiple species together
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
a system or method of growing two or more species or crops together in the same area

Examples

Polyculture farming system
Farmers are increasingly adopting polyculture to improve soil health and reduce pests naturally.
In the laboratory, a polyculture can reveal how different bacteria interact with each other.
Aquatic polyculture systems often combine fish and plants to create a balanced environment.
The farm switched to polyculture to increase biodiversity and reduce pests naturally.
Many sustainable agriculture experts recommend polyculture over monoculture for healthier soil.
By practicing polyculture, farmers can harvest different crops throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intercropping - growing two or more crops together in proximity
  • synonym: mixed culture - a culture containing more than one type of organism
  • antonym: monoculture - the cultivation of a single species in a given area
