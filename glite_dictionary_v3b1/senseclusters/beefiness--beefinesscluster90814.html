beefiness

noun
substantial quality
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːfinəs / (AmE), / ˈbiːfinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being substantial, full, or having a lot of important content or substance

Examples

Article beefiness
The beefiness of the article impressed the editor, who valued detailed analysis.
Her presentation lacked the beefiness needed to convince the board of directors.
Critics admired the beefiness of the novel, noting its rich themes and complex characters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flimsiness - the quality of lacking substance or strength
  • synonym: substance - the quality of being meaningful or important
  • synonym: weight - importance or seriousness in content
