Big Bad Wolf

noun
villainous wolf in folklore and fairy tales
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ bæd wʊlf / (AmE), / bɪɡ bæd wʊlf / (BrE)
a fictional wolf character in folklore and fairy tales, often portrayed as a villain or antagonist, especially in stories such as 'Little Red Riding Hood' and 'The Three Little Pigs'

Examples

The Big Bad Wolf story
My son was terrified of the Big Bad Wolf after hearing the fairy tale.
Teachers use the Big Bad Wolf to teach children about caution and consequences.
The actor wore a furry costume to play the Big Bad Wolf on stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: villain - a character whose evil actions or motives are important to the plot
  • degree lower: wolf - large carnivorous mammal; often portrayed as a symbol of danger or cunning
