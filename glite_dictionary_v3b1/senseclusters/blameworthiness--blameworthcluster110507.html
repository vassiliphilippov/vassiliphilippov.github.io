blameworthiness

noun
deserving blame or criticism
Pronunciation: / ˈbleɪmwɝðinəs / (AmE), / ˈbleɪmwəːðɪnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of deserving blame or criticism for something wrong or bad.

Examples

The blameworthiness of actions
Her actions were of such blameworthiness that even her friends could not defend her.
Questions of blameworthiness often arise in ethical discussions about responsibility.
The jury discussed the blameworthiness of each defendant before delivering the verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culpability - responsibility for a fault or wrong; guilt
  • antonym: innocence - lack of guilt or blame; not deserving blame
