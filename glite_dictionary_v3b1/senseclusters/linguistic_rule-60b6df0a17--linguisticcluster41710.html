linguistic rule

noun
rule describing or prescribing language use
1.3
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ruːl / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ruːl / (BrE)
a principle or guideline that describes or prescribes how language is used, often forming part of the grammar of a language

Examples

Basic linguistic rules
Children subconsciously acquire linguistic rules as they learn to speak their native language.

This grammar book outlines each linguistic rule with clear examples.
