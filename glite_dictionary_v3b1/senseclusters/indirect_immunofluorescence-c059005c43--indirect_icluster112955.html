indirect immunofluorescence

noun
antigen detection using fluorescently labeled antibodies
6.7 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndəˈrɛkt ɪˌmjunəflʊˈrɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈrɛkt ɪˌmjuːnəflʊˈrɛsns / (BrE)
a laboratory technique in which an antigen is detected by first binding it with a specific antibody, then applying a fluorescent label to a secondary antibody that binds to the first, making the antigen visible under a fluorescence microscope

Examples

Indirect immunofluorescence test
A positive indirect immunofluorescence test indicated the presence of autoimmune activity.
Researchers employed indirect immunofluorescence to detect specific antibodies in the patient's serum.
The laboratory confirmed the diagnosis using indirect immunofluorescence on a tissue biopsy sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct immunofluorescence - method where the fluorescent antibody binds directly to the antigen
