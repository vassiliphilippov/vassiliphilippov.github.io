disturbed

adjective
having been moved or altered in position
6.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
(of an object or place) altered in position, order, or arrangement; having been moved from its original state.

Examples

Disturbed papers
After the meeting, the disturbed files still littered the conference table.
The disturbed sand revealed where the animal had buried something.
You could tell by the disturbed tracks that someone had recently walked through the snow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: undisturbed - not altered or moved, left in its original place or state
