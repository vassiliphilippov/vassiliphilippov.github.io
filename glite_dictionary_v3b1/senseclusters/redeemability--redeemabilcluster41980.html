redeemability

noun
capacity for forgiveness
Pronunciation: / rɪˌdiːməˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / rɪˌdiːməˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be forgiven, saved, or restored to good standing after a fault or wrongdoing

Examples

Redeemability of a person
The novel explores the redeemability of its main character after a series of serious mistakes.
Some people questioned the redeemability of the company following the financial scandal.
He believed in the redeemability of everyone, no matter what they had done.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forgiveness - the act of forgiving someone for a mistake or wrongdoing
  • antonym: irredeemability - the state of being unable to be saved or forgiven
