breed line

noun
descendants from closely related breeding
Pronunciation: / brid laɪn / (AmE), / briːd laɪn / (BrE)
A line of offspring descended from matings between individuals that are closely related genetically, often within animal or plant breeding.

Examples

A breed line group
Genetic testing confirmed the breed line traces back several generations.
The kennel specializes in maintaining a pure breed line of rare terriers.
Years of careful selection improved the breed line for faster racehorses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloodline - ancestral line of descent, particularly regarding animals or humans
  • synonym: lineage - direct descent from an ancestor; ancestry or pedigree
