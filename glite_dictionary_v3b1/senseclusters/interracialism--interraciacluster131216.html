interracialism

noun
support for mixed-race relationships
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈreɪʃəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈreɪʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or support of romantic or sexual relationships, especially marriage, between people of different races

Examples

Interracialism in marriage
The civil rights movement promoted interracialism as a way to challenge discriminatory laws.
Some communities embraced interracialism and encouraged friendships and marriages across racial lines.
Debates about interracialism often reflect broader social attitudes toward diversity and equality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: integration - the process of combining people of different races into equal participation
  • antonym: segregation - the practice of keeping people of different races separate
