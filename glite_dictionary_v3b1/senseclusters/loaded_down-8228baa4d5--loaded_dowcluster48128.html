loaded down

adjective
weighed down with burdens or responsibilities
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊdɪd ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌləʊdɪd ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
having to carry or endure a heavy burden, especially of work, responsibility, or worries

Examples

Loaded down with work
He was loaded down with packages when he returned from shopping.
She felt loaded down with work as deadlines piled up.
The manager looked loaded down by the pressure of running the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overburdened - given more than can be easily managed or carried
  • synonym: weighed down - burdened or oppressed by something heavy or worrisome
