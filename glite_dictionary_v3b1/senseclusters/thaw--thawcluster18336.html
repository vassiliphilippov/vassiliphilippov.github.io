thaw

noun
period when snow and ice melt
Pronunciation: / θɔ / (AmE), / θɔː / (BrE)
a period or process when snow and ice melt due to warmer weather

Examples

Spring thaw
After a long winter, the thaw brought relief to the entire village.
Heavy rains during the thaw often lead to flooding in the lowlands.
The early spring thaw made hiking trails muddy and slippery.
Many roads became muddy after the early spring thaw.
The river flooded its banks during the thaw.
Wildlife becomes more active once the thaw begins.
After the long winter, the countryside slowly emerged from thaw.
Food left out during a power outage will begin to thaw quickly.
The sudden thaw in early spring caused the river to flood.
