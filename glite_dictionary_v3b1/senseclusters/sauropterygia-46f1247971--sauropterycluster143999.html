Sauropterygia

noun
extinct order of marine reptiles
Pronunciation: / ˌsɔːroʊpˌtɛˈrɪdʒiə /; / sɔːroʊptəˈrɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌsɔːrəpˈtɛrɪdʒɪə /; / sɔːrəʊptəˈrɪdʒiə / (BrE)
an extinct order of marine reptiles, including plesiosaurs and nothosaurs, known for their aquatic adaptations such as long necks and flipper-like limbs

Examples

Sauropterygia marine reptiles

Scientists believe that Sauropterygia evolved specialized fins for efficient swimming in open water.
The Sauropterygia thrived during the Mesozoic Era, dominating ancient seas as formidable predators.
