archenteron

noun
central embryonic cavity forming the gut
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈkɛntəˌrɑn / (AmE), / ɑːˈkɛntərɒn / (BrE)
the central cavity within the gastrula stage of an embryo, which develops into the intestinal or digestive cavity

Examples

The archenteron cavity
Cells lining the archenteron give rise to the endodermal tissues.
Scientists observed the formation of the archenteron in sea urchin embryos under the microscope.
The archenteron deepens during gastrulation to form the future gut of the embryo.
