impeachable

adjective
able to be challenged
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpiːtʃəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpiːtʃəbəl / (BrE)
able to be discredited, challenged, or shown to be false, especially regarding testimony or evidence.

Examples

Impeachable evidence
The lawyer argued that the evidence was impeachable due to inconsistencies in the report.
If a witness is found to be unreliable, their statements may be considered impeachable in court.
The committee reviewed whether the official's actions were impeachable under the law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: questionable - open to doubt or challenge
  • antonym: unimpeachable - impossible to discredit or challenge
