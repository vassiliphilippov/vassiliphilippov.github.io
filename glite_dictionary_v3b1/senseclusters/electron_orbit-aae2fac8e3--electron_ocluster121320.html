electron orbit

noun
path of an electron around an atom's nucleus
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛk.trɑn ˈɔr.bɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛk.trɒn ˈɔː.bɪt / (BrE)
the path or course taken by an electron moving around the nucleus of an atom

Examples

An electron orbit path
In chemistry, the concept of an electron orbit helps explain atomic structure.
The Bohr model proposes that each electron orbit has a specific energy level.
Transitions between electron orbits often result in emission or absorption of energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atomic orbital - a region around an atom's nucleus where electrons are likely to be found
