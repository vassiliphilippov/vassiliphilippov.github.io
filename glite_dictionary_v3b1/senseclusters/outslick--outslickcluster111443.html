outslick

verb
to outsmart someone
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈslɪk / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈslɪk / (BrE)
to be more clever or cunning than someone else, especially in a competitive situation

Examples

To outslick a rival
He tried to outslick his rival during the intense debate.
The young lawyer managed to outslick the more experienced attorney in court.
You cannot always outslick everyone; sometimes honesty is the best policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be outsmarted - to be defeated by someone more clever
  • synonym: fool - to trick or deceive someone
  • synonym: outsmart - to defeat or get the better of someone by being more clever
  • synonym: outwit - to gain an advantage over someone by being more intelligent or clever
