Brechtian

adjective
of Brecht's theater
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛktiən / (AmE), / ˈbrɛktiən / (BrE)
relating to the theatrical style or ideas of Bertolt Brecht, especially techniques that distance the audience emotionally

Examples

Brechtian theater style
The director used Brechtian techniques to remind the audience they were watching a play.
Her performance was intentionally Brechtian, breaking the fourth wall to engage viewers critically.
Many modern productions adopt a Brechtian approach to challenge traditional storytelling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epic theatre - a style of theater developed by Brecht to provoke rational thought
