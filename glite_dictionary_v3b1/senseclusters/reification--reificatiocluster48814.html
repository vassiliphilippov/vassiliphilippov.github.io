reification

noun
making a relation a first‑class object (computing)
9.9
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɪ.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɪ.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in computer science and data modeling) the process of turning a relationship, assertion, or abstract statement into a distinct, first‑class object or entity so it can be referenced, annotated, or given metadata.

Examples

Data reification
In the graph database, the reification of the user-item relationship created a node to store timestamps and ratings.
Developers used reification to attach provenance metadata to assertions in the RDF triple store.
Without reification, it was difficult to record who made each claim about a fact.
