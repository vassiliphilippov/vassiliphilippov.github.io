embryonal carcinosarcoma

noun
malignant tumor of immature cells
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmbriˈoʊnəl ˌkɑːrkɪnoʊsɑːrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌɛmbriˈəʊnəl ˌkɑːsɪnəʊˌsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor made up of immature, undifferentiated cells, typically showing both carcinomatous and sarcomatous features.

Examples

Embryonal carcinosarcoma tumor
Because embryonal carcinosarcoma is rare, case studies are important for understanding its progression.
Researchers are studying new therapies to improve outcomes for patients diagnosed with embryonal carcinosarcoma.
