low-water mark

noun
the lowest point or level
6.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààlo ä Àåw…ît…ôr Ààm…ërk / (AmE), / Ààl…ô ä Àåw…îÀêt…ô Ààm…ëÀêk / (BrE)
the lowest or worst point of a situation or condition

Examples

Confidence low-water mark
His confidence reached its low-water mark after repeated failures.
That period was the low-water mark of her political career.
The company's sales hit a low-water mark during the recession.

Synonyms

  • nadir
    9.2 0.53/m
    nadir describes a very formal, extreme low point in a situation, while low-water mark is a neutral phrase for the worst point.
  • rock bottom
    4.4 1.15/m
    rock bottom is an informal phrase for the absolute lowest point in life or money, while low-water mark sounds more neutral and formal.
