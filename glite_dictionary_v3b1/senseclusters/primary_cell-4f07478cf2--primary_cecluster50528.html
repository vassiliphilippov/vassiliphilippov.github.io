primary cell

noun
non-rechargeable electric cell
5.9 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpraɪ.mə.ri sel/ (AmE), /ˈpraɪ.mə.ri sel/ (BrE)
An electric cell that produces electricity through an irreversible chemical reaction and cannot be recharged after its energy is used.

Examples

A primary cell battery
A primary cell cannot be reused once its chemical energy is exhausted.
Flashlights often use primary cells because they are affordable and readily available.
Unlike a rechargeable battery, a primary cell must be disposed of after a single use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secondary cell - an electric cell that can be recharged by reversing the chemical reaction
