yellow fever

noun
infectious mosquito-borne disease
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ ˈfiːvər /; / ˈjɛl.oʊ ˌfi.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈfiːvə /; / ˈjɛl.əʊ ˌfiː.və / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease caused by a virus and spread by mosquitoes, causing fever and yellowing of the skin (jaundice), found mainly in tropical regions such as Africa and South America

Examples

Yellow fever virus
Doctors quickly diagnosed the patient with yellow fever after observing his symptoms.
The epidemic of yellow fever in the 19th century devastated entire communities.
Vaccines are recommended for travelers to areas where yellow fever is common.
An outbreak of yellow fever prompted urgent public health measures.
Many countries require proof of yellow fever vaccination for travelers from at-risk areas.
The symptoms of yellow fever include high fever and yellowing of the skin.
