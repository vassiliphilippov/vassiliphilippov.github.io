carbon-14 dating

noun
method for dating ancient organic materials
9.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːr.bən fɔːrˈtiːn ˈdeɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.bən fɔːˈtiːn ˈdeɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
A method of estimating the age of organic materials by measuring the amount of radioactive carbon-14 they contain.

Examples

Carbon-14 dating method
carbon-14 dating has helped archaeologists establish the age of ancient artifacts.
Advances in carbon-14 dating have improved the accuracy of identifying historical timelines.
The team used carbon-14 dating to determine the remains were over 5,000 years old.
