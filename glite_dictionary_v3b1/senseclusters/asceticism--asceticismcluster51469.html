asceticism

noun
strict self-discipline; abstention from indulgence
8.7 7.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈse.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ə.ˈse.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice of strict self-discipline and avoiding all forms of pleasure, usually for religious or spiritual reasons

Examples

Strict asceticism practice
Her commitment to asceticism was evident in her simple lifestyle and strict routine.
Many philosophies regard asceticism as a path to enlightenment or higher wisdom.
The monks practiced asceticism, forsaking all material comforts to focus on spiritual growth.
His dedication to asceticism meant he lived with only the bare essentials.
The monks' asceticism was admired by many seeking a simpler life.
Through asceticism, she sought spiritual clarity and inner peace.
He practiced asceticism by living in a small hut and eating only plain food.
Some philosophers have argued that asceticism leads to greater spiritual awareness.
The monk's life of asceticism was admired by many for its discipline and simplicity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstinence - the practice of restraining oneself from indulging in something
  • antonym: hedonism - the pursuit of pleasure as the highest good
  • synonym: self-denial - the denial of one's own interests and needs
  • antonym: self-indulgence - the practice of allowing oneself to enjoy pleasures freely
