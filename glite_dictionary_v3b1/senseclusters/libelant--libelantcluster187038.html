libelant

noun
person suing for libel
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪbələnt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪbələnt / (BrE)
a person who brings a legal action or files a formal complaint, especially for libel or in admiralty law

Examples

Libelant files a complaint
The libelant presented evidence that the newspaper article contained false statements.
After reading the defamatory post, she became the libelant in a high-profile lawsuit.
The court ruled in favor of the libelant, awarding damages for the harm caused.
The libelant presented detailed documents to support the maritime claim.
In the admiralty court, the libelant argued that the shipowner was responsible for the damages.
After filing the complaint, the libelant awaited the court's decision on the case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - a person being sued or accused in a court of law
  • synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
