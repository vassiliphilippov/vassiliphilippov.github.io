oxidize

verb
combine with oxygen; form an oxide
3.6 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːk.sə.daɪz/ /; / /ˈɑːk.sɪ.daɪz/ (AmE), /ˈɒk.sɪ.daɪz/ /; / /ˈɒk.sə.daɪz/ (BrE)
to combine with oxygen or cause something to combine with oxygen, usually as a chemical reaction

Examples

To oxidize metal
If you leave apple slices out in the air, they quickly oxidize and turn brown.
Some metals oxidize more rapidly than others, affecting their durability.
The iron fence began to oxidize after years of exposure to rain.
Certain chemicals can oxidize pollutants in water, making them less harmful.
Iron will oxidize over time, leading to the formation of rust.
The apple slices began to oxidize and turn brown after being left out.
Copper pipes can oxidise when they come into contact with certain chemicals in water.
Fruit slices often oxidise and turn brown when left out in the open.
If iron is exposed to air and moisture, it will slowly oxidise and form rust.
