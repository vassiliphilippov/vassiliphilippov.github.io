steep

adjective
having a sharp slope or angle
Pronunciation: / stip /; / stiːp / (AmE), / stiːp / (BrE)
rising or falling sharply; having a very high or almost vertical slope or angle

Examples

A steep hill
A steep staircase can be difficult for the elderly to climb.
The hill behind the house is surprisingly steep and covered in wildflowers.
The road became dangerously steep as it wound up the mountain.
It was difficult to climb the steep mountain trail after the rain.
The steep stairs made carrying boxes up to the attic quite a challenge.
The house sits at the top of a steep driveway that can be slippery in winter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentle - (of a slope or incline) not steep or abrupt
