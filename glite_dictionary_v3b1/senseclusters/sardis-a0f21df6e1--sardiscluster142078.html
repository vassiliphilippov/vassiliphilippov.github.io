Sardis

proper noun
ancient city; capital of Lydia, Asia Minor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑrdɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdɪs / (BrE)
an ancient city in western Asia Minor (now Turkey), formerly the capital of the kingdom of Lydia and a major cultural center of the ancient world.

Examples

Ancient Sardis city
In antiquity, Sardis was known for its wealth and splendor as the capital of Lydia.
The capture and destruction of Sardis by Tamerlane marked the end of its prominence.
The ruins of Sardis attract archaeologists from around the world every year.
