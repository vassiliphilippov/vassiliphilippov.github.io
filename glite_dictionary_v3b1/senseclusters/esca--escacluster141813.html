esca

noun
grapevine fungal disease
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈe.skə/ (AmE), /ˈe.skə/ (BrE)
a chronic fungal disease of grapevines that causes wood decay, leaf discoloration, and decline of the vine

Examples

Esca disease in vines
The vineyard lost many vines to esca after a particularly wet spring.
Farmers are researching new ways to prevent esca from spreading among their grapevines.
Symptoms of esca include yellowing leaves and rotting wood in the affected plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: black measles - a grapevine disease causing dark spots and decline
  • antonym: phylloxera - an insect pest that damages grapevines
