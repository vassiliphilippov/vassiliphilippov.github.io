identifiability

noun
ability to be identified
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdɛntəˌfaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / aɪˌdɛntɪfaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be identified or distinguished from others

Examples

Identifiability of a person
The identifiability of the suspect was confirmed by several witnesses at the scene.
Researchers discussed the identifiability of individuals in the anonymized data set.
Improving the identifiability of products can help prevent counterfeiting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anonymity - the state of being unknown or unidentifiable
  • synonym: recognizability - the quality of being easy to recognize
