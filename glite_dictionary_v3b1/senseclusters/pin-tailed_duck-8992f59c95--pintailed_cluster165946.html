pin-tailed duck

noun
a duck species with elongated tail feathers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪn teɪld dʌk / (AmE), / ˈpɪn teɪld dʌk / (BrE)
a long-necked river duck of the Old and New Worlds, notable for its elongated central tail feathers

Examples

A pin-tailed duck
During migration, flocks of pin-tailed ducks can often be seen in northern wetlands.
The pin-tailed duck is easily recognized by its slender, elongated tail feathers.
We spotted a male pin-tailed duck gliding across the marsh at sunrise.
