misery

noun
great suffering or unhappiness
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪzəri / (AmE), / ˈmɪzəri / (BrE)
a state or feeling of great unhappiness or suffering

Examples

Deep misery
After losing his job, he sank into a deep misery that lasted for months.
Decades of war left the population living in constant misery.
She tried to hide her misery behind a forced smile.
He endured years of misery after losing his job.
Her illness caused her great physical and emotional misery.
The refugees spoke of their misery during the harsh winter.
