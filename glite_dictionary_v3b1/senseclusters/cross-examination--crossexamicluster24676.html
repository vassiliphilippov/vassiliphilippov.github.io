cross-examination

noun
questioning of a witness by the opposing side in court
1.1 6.1
Pronunciation: /krɔːs.ɪg.zæm.ɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /krɑːs.ɪg.zæm.ɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /krɒs.ɪg.zæm.ɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the formal questioning of a witness by the opposing side in a court of law after direct examination, typically to challenge or clarify the witness's testimony

Examples

The cross-examination of witness
During the cross-examination, the defense attorney exposed inconsistencies in the witness's earlier statements.
Effective cross-examination can sometimes change the outcome of a trial.
The witness became visibly nervous under cross-examination by the prosecution's lawyer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct examination - initial questioning of a witness by the party who called them
