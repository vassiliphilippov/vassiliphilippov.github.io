factor VII

noun
blood clotting protein (coagulation factor)
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tɚ sɪvˈɛn / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə sɪˈvɛn / (BrE)
a protein in the blood that helps it clot, produced in the kidneys under the influence of vitamin K

Examples

Factor VII protein
A deficiency of factor VII can lead to excessive bleeding after surgery.
Doctors measured the patient's factor VII levels to assess his risk for clotting disorders.
Vitamin K is necessary for the synthesis of factor VII in the body.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: factor IX - another coagulation factor, playing a related role in the clotting cascade
  • degree higher: factor VIII - a different coagulation factor involved in blood clotting
  • synonym: proconvertin - another name for factor VII, a blood-clotting protein
