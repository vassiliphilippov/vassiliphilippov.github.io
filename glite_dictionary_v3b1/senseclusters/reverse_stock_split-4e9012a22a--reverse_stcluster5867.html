reverse stock split

noun
a consolidation of a company's shares to reduce their number
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜrs stɑk splɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs stɒk splɪt / (BrE)
a corporate action in which a company reduces the number of its outstanding shares without affecting the total value of shareholders' equity, usually by consolidating shares

Examples

A reverse stock split action
A reverse stock split is often used to attract institutional investors by increasing share price.
After the reverse stock split, shareholders owned fewer shares, but each was worth more.
The company announced a reverse stock split to raise the per-share price above the minimum required by the stock exchange.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stock split - an increase in the number of a company's outstanding shares without changing the value of shareholders' equity
