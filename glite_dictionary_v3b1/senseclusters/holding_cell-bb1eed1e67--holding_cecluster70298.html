holding cell

noun
temporary jail cell in courthouse or police station
4.8 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊl.dɪŋ sel/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊl.dɪŋ sel/ (BrE)
a secure room in a courthouse or police station where people are kept temporarily while awaiting a court appearance, transfer, or questioning

Examples

Police holding cell
After his arrest, he was taken to a holding cell at the police station.
The courthouse holding cell was crowded with people awaiting their court hearings.
The suspect waited in the holding cell until it was time for his trial to begin.
