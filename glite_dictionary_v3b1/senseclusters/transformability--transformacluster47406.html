transformability

noun
capacity to be changed
Pronunciation: / trænsˌfɔrməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / trænsˌfɔːməˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be changed or converted in form, appearance, or function

Examples

Image transformability
The transformability of digital images allows them to be edited and reused in many ways.
Scientists study the transformability of materials to develop new products with unique properties.
Understanding the transformability of data is crucial for effective system integration.
The transformability of plastic allows it to be molded into many different shapes.
Scientists are studying the transformability of certain cells for medical treatments.
The transformability of the city has made it a model for urban renewal projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convertibility - the quality of being able to be changed into a different form or use
  • antonym: immutability - the quality of being unchangeable
