war of nerves

noun
psychological tactic to undermine morale
3.7
Pronunciation: / wɔr əv nɜrvz / (AmE), / wɔː əv nɜːvz /; / wɔːr əv nɜːvz / (BrE)
a situation or contest where psychological tactics, tension, or pressure are used to weaken or unsettle an opponent, rather than physical force

Examples

The war of nerves
During the war of nerves, rumors and misinformation were deliberately spread to unsettle the enemy.
Negotiations turned into a war of nerves as both sides waited for the other to concede first.
The two nations engaged in a prolonged war of nerves, each trying to outmaneuver the other without firing a shot.
Both teams were locked in a war of nerves before the crucial match began.
Enduring the constant uncertainty felt like being in a war of nerves.
The negotiations became a war of nerves as each side refused to back down.
