royal charter

noun
an official document granted by a monarch
Pronunciation: / ˌrɔɪəl ˈtʃɑrtər / (AmE), / ˌrɔɪəl ˈtʃɑːtə / (BrE)
an official document issued by a monarch granting rights or powers to an individual, company, or institution, especially in the United Kingdom

Examples

A royal charter document
Many early trading companies received a royal charter to regulate their activities overseas.
The University of Oxford operates under a royal charter granted in the thirteenth century.
To become a city in the UK, a town often needs a royal charter.
