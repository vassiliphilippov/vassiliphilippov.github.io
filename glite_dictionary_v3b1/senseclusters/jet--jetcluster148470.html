jet

verb
to stream out forcefully
7.1
Pronunciation: / dʒɛt / (AmE), / dʒɛt / (BrE)
(of a liquid, gas, flame, etc.) to come out or be sent out in a narrow, forceful stream.

Examples

Water jets out
A fountain of water jetted from the cracked pipe, flooding the basement.
Flames jetted out of the engine as the car sped down the track.
Steam jetted from the tea kettle, whistling loudly in the morning air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spurt - to gush out suddenly in a stream
  • synonym: squirt - to forcefully eject liquid in a stream
