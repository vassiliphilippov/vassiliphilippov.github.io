sugar of lead

noun
poisonous compound; lead acetate
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʊɡɚ əv lɛd / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə əv lɛd / (BrE)
a poisonous white crystalline compound (lead acetate) formerly used in dyeing fabrics and in the manufacture of enamels and varnishes

Examples

White sugar of lead
Historically, sugar of lead was used in cosmetics despite its toxicity.

The old recipe called for sugar of lead to improve the glaze on pottery.
