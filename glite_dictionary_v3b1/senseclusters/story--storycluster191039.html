story

noun
fictional account or narrative
1.0 547/m
Pronunciation: / Ààst…îri /; / Ààst…î…πi / (AmE), / Ààst…îÀêri /; / Ààst…îÀê…πi / (BrE)
a description or account of events, either real or imaginary, told to entertain, inform, or report (in books, films, news, or conversation)

Examples

Tell a story
Children love to listen to a story about magical adventures.
She enjoyed reading a short story before bed every night.
The author published his first story in a well-known magazine.
Her interview became the lead story on national television.
That story was the headline on every major news site this morning.
The reporter worked late to finish the story for the evening broadcast.
Children love listening to a bedtime story before they go to sleep.
She told a fascinating story about her travels in Africa.
The movie's story is based on true events.

Synonyms

  • narrative
    3.9 38/m
    a narrative is a detailed or artistic telling of connected events, while a story can be any account, real or imagined, for many purposes.
  • account
    3.0 38/m
    an account is a plain report of events for information or records, while a story aims to entertain, inform, or describe imaginatively.
  • tale
    1.0 40/m
    a tale is often an imaginative or old-fashioned telling meant to amuse, while a story can be real or used in many contexts.
  • relation
    3.9 6.92/m
    a relation is an older or formal word for telling events, while a story is the common modern term used in books or speech.
