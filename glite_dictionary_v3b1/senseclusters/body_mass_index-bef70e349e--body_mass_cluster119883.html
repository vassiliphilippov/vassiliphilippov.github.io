body mass index

noun
weight-to-height measure of body fat
3.8 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑː.dɪ mæs ˈɪn.deks/ (AmE), /ˈbɒd.ɪ mæs ˈɪn.deks/ (BrE)
a measure of body fat based on a person's weight in relation to their height, used to classify underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obesity

Examples

Body mass index value
Her body mass index placed her in the overweight category, so she began a new exercise routine.
Many doctors use the body mass index to assess whether a patient is at a healthy weight.
The body mass index is not always accurate for athletes with high muscle mass.
