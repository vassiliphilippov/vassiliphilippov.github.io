factoring

noun
breaking expression into factors (mathematics)
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tər.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(mathematics) The process of resolving an expression into a product of factors that, when multiplied together, give the original expression.

Examples

Factoring algebra expressions
She learned factoring techniques to simplify complex polynomial expressions.
Successful factoring of quadratic equations is a key skill in algebra.
The factoring of large numbers is essential in some cryptographic algorithms.
