false pregnancy

noun
condition with pregnancy symptoms but no pregnancy
1.6 5.6
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈprɛɡnənsi / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈprɛɡnənsi / (BrE)
a condition in which a woman shows symptoms of pregnancy, such as nausea and missed periods, but is not actually pregnant

Examples

false pregnancy symptoms
Some women experiencing a false pregnancy report physical and emotional changes similar to those of real pregnancies.
The doctor diagnosed her with a false pregnancy after several tests ruled out actual conception.
Treatment for false pregnancy often involves addressing any underlying psychological factors.
