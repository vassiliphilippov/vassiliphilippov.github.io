trussed

adjective
bound or tied up tightly
8.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / trÊŒst / (AmE), / trÊŒst / (BrE)
Bound or secured tightly with ropes, string, or similar; especially with arms and legs tied together or with limbs bound close to the body.

Examples

A trussed chicken
She prepared the roast chicken by making sure it was tightly trussed before cooking.
The burglar was discovered trussed behind the counter, unable to move.
The hostages were found trussed with their hands and feet bound together.

Synonyms

  • bound
    4.2 0.38/m
    bound is tied or wrapped to stop movement and often used broadly, while trussed means limbs tied tightly close to the body.
  • pinioned
    9.7 0.01/m
    pinioned has arms or wings held and restrained by binding, while trussed describes limbs tied tightly close to the body.
  • tied
    1.0 0.00/m
    tied means fastened with rope or string in many situations, while trussed implies limbs are bound tightly and held close together.
