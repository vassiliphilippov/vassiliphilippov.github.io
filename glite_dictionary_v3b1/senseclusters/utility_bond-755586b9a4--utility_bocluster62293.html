utility bond

noun
bond for funding public utility projects
3.1 5.0
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈtɪl.ə.tiː bɑːnd/ /; / /jə.ˈtɪl.ə.tiː bɑːnd/ (AmE), /juː.ˈtɪl.ɪ.tiː bɒnd/ /; / /jə.ˈtɪl.ɪ.tiː bɒnd/ (BrE)
a bond issued by a government or municipality to finance the construction or maintenance of public utility services, such as water, electricity, or transportation.

Examples

Utility bonds finance services
Investors consider utility bonds relatively safe because they are backed by essential public services.
The city issued a utility bond to pay for improvements to the water treatment facility.
The construction of the new subway line was financed largely through a utility bond.
