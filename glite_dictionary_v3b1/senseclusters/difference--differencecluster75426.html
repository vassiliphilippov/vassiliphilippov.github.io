difference

noun
state of being unlike or dissimilar
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪfərəns /; / ˈdɪfɚəns /; / ˈdɪf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ˈdɪfərəns /; / ˈdɪf.ər.əns / (BrE)
the way in which two or more things are not the same, or the amount by which they are not the same (including in mathematics)

Examples

Difference between two things
It's easy to spot the difference between these two photographs.
The difference in taste between the brands was noticeable.
Their opinions show a clear difference in perspective.
A small difference from the expected outcome can change the entire experiment.
Statisticians analyze the difference between observed and theoretical values to understand the data.
The difference between the test result and the average value was significant.
Even a small difference in measurement can affect the final calculation.
The difference between eight and three is five.
To solve the problem, find the difference between the two amounts.

  • antonym: similarity - the state of being similar or alike
