g

noun
unit of force equal to Earth's gravity
Pronunciation: / dʒi / (AmE), / dʒi / (BrE)
a unit of force equal to the force of gravity on Earth, used to measure the force experienced by an object when it is accelerated

Examples

High g force
Astronauts must train to withstand high g forces during launch.
Fighter pilots can experience as many as nine g during sharp turns.
The roller coaster subject riders to more than three g at its fastest point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gravity - the force that attracts objects toward the center of the earth
