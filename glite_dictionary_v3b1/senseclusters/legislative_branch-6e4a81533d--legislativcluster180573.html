legislative branch

noun
government branch that makes laws
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtɪv bræntʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɛdʒɪˌsleɪtɪv brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
the branch of government responsible for making laws, especially in the United States

Examples

The legislative branch makes laws
Checks and balances ensure that the legislative branch does not exceed its authority.
Many debates in Congress shape the work of the legislative branch.
The legislative branch is responsible for creating new federal laws in the United States.

Related Terms

  • antonym: executive branch - branch of government responsible for enforcing laws
  • antonym: judicial branch - branch of government responsible for interpreting laws
