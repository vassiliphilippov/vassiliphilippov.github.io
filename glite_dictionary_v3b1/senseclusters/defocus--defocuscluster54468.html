defocus

verb
to make blurry
Pronunciation: / diˈfoʊkəs /; / diːˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / diˈfəʊkəs /; / diːˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to make an image or view less sharp or clear by adjusting the focus

Examples

To defocus the camera
If you defocus the camera, the photograph will appear soft and unclear.
The optometrist asked me to defocus my eyes and then refocus on the letter chart.
Artists sometimes defocus their vision to better see the overall composition of a painting.
If you defocus the lens, the photograph will appear soft and unclear.
The director chose to defocus the background to highlight the main character.
Sometimes I defocus my eyes when I am tired and everything looks fuzzy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - to make something unclear or less distinct
  • antonym: focus - to make an image or view sharp and clear
