Lao-tse

proper noun
Chinese philosopher, founder of Taoism
Pronunciation: / laʊˈdzə / (AmE), / laʊˈtsɜː / (BrE)
(c. 6th century BC) Chinese philosopher regarded as the founder of Taoism; influential in the development of Chinese philosophy and religion.

Examples

Lao-tse teachings
Many of Lao-tse's teachings are recorded in the Tao Te Ching.
Scholars continue to debate whether Lao-tse was a real historical figure or a legendary one.
The ideas of Lao-tse have influenced philosophies and religions across East Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Confucius - Chinese philosopher and founder of Confucianism
  • synonym: Taoism - Philosophical and religious tradition founded by Lao-tse
