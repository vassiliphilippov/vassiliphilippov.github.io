triterpene

noun
plant chemical compound
Pronunciation: / traɪˈtɜːrpiːn / (AmE), / traɪˈtɜːpiːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound made of six isoprene units, commonly found in plants and some animals

Examples

A triterpene compound
Scientists discovered a rare triterpene in the bark of the ancient tree.
Many traditional medicines use plants that are rich in triterpenes for their healing properties.
The laboratory analyzed the chemical structure of the new triterpene compound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: steroid - a type of organic compound derived from triterpenes
  • synonym: terpene - a large class of organic compounds made from isoprene units
