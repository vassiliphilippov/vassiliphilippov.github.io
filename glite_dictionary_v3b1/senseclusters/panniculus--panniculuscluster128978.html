panniculus

noun
subcutaneous tissue layer
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈnɪkjələs / (AmE), / pəˈnɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
a layer or sheet of tissue (fat or muscle) just beneath the skin

Examples

Panniculus of fat
The doctor explained that the panniculus can become enlarged in some medical conditions.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully lifted the panniculus to access deeper tissues.
Obesity may lead to a more prominent panniculus in some patients.
In some animals, the panniculus allows them to twitch their skin to remove insects.
The surgeon noted a thick panniculus of fat beneath the patient's abdominal skin.
Damage to the panniculus can affect the movement of the skin in certain mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fascia - a band or sheet of connective tissue beneath the skin
  • synonym: muscle - tissue that contracts to produce movement
  • synonym: subcutaneous fat - fat stored directly under the skin
