thought-reader

noun
device decoding brain signals
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈθɔːt.riː.dər/ /; / /θɔːt ˈriː.dər/ (AmE), /ˈθɔːt.riː.də/ /; / /θɔːt ˈriː.də/ (BrE)
A device or system (especially a brain–computer interface) that decodes neural activity to produce written or spoken output, enabling communication or control.

Examples

A thought-reader device
Researchers unveiled a thought-reader that translates neural signals into speech for paralyzed patients.
The hospital trial used a wearable thought-reader to help participants control a computer cursor.
Startups race to improve accuracy of the thought-reader so users can dictate longer messages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brain–computer interface - a system that enables direct communication between the brain and an external device
  • synonym: neural decoder - software or algorithms that translate neural signals into meaningful outputs like text or actions
  • synonym: neuroprosthesis - an implanted or external device that replaces or assists a lost nervous-system function
