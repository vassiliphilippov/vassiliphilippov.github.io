expurgation

noun
removal of offensive content from a work
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.spɚˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the removal of objectionable or offensive parts from a literary or artistic work

Examples

The expurgation of scenes
Following the expurgation of several scenes, the play was approved for general audiences.
Some scholars argue that the expurgation of classic works distorts their original meaning.
The new edition of the novel underwent significant expurgation to remove controversial passages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: censorship - suppression or prohibition of parts considered unacceptable
  • synonym: redaction - editing or preparing a text for publication
