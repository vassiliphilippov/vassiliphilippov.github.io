aniseed

noun
liquorice-flavored seeds used in cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.ɪˌsid / (AmE), / ˈæn.ɪ.siːd / (BrE)
The small, brown, liquorice-flavored seeds of the anise plant, used as a spice in cooking, in making liquors, and in traditional medicines.

Examples

A few aniseed seeds
She chewed a few aniseed seeds to freshen her breath after the meal.
The recipe calls for a pinch of aniseed to give a subtle, sweet flavor.
Traditional Italian biscotti often include aniseed for its distinctive taste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anise - the plant from which aniseed is obtained
  • synonym: fennel - aromatic seeds sometimes used similarly in cooking
