tokenize

verb
to create digital token
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtəʊkənaɪz / (BrE)
to replace sensitive information with a digital token for secure storage, authentication, or processing, especially in computing or finance

Examples

To tokenize card data
Many modern payment systems tokenize credit card information to enhance security.
The app will tokenize your login credentials before sending them to the server.
Developers often tokenize sensitive data to comply with privacy regulations.
Many payment processors tokenize credit card numbers to protect customer information.
The company decided to tokenize all sensitive data before storing it in the cloud.
After you tokenize the information, only the token is used for future transactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decode - to convert coded information back to its original form
  • antonym: decrypt - to convert coded data back into its original form
  • synonym: encrypt - to convert information into a code to prevent unauthorized access
