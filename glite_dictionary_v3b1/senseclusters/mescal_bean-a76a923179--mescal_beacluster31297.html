mescal bean

noun
poisonous shrub or small tree with red seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛskəl ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈmɛskæl ˌbiːn / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree (Dermatophyllum secundiflorum) native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, known for its pinnate leaves, bright blue flowers, and red seeds that are poisonous to livestock

Examples

The mescal bean shrub
Native American tribes once used the mescal bean in traditional ceremonies, despite its poisonous nature.
The bright red seeds of the mescal bean are highly toxic to animals and humans.
We found a mescal bean growing near the riverbank, its blue flowers attracting bees.
