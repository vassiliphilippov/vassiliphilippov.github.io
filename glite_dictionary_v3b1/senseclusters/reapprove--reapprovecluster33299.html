reapprove

verb
to approve again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈpruːv / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈpruːv / (BrE)
to give approval to something again after it was previously approved

Examples

To reapprove the policy
The committee must reapprove the policy before it can be implemented again.
If the changes are significant, the board will need to reapprove the project.
After the initial approval expired, the council reapproved the construction plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approve - to officially agree to or accept something
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or approve something
