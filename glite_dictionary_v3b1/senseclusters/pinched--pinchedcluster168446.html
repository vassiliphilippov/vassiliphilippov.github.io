pinched

adjective
lacking enough money; financially strained
2.4
Pronunciation: / pɪntʃt / (AmE), / pɪntʃt / (BrE)
(of a person) lacking enough money to meet basic needs; financially strained.

Examples

Pinched for money
After losing his job, Mark found himself pinched for cash most months.
Many families feel pinched during the holiday season when expenses rise.
She's a bit pinched right now and can't afford to go on vacation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfortable - having enough money to live without financial stress
  • degree higher: destitute - extremely poor, lacking the means to provide for oneself
  • synonym: impoverished - lacking sufficient money or material possessions
