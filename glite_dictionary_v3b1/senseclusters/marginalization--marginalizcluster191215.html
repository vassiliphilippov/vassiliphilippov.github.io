marginalization

noun
the act of making someone or something unimportant
3.7 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑrdʒənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɑːdʒɪnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person or group is treated as insignificant or pushed to the margins of a society or group

Examples

Marginalization of groups
Marginalization of minority groups can lead to social and economic disparities.
Many activists work to raise awareness about the marginalization of indigenous communities.
The new policy aims to combat the marginalization of people with disabilities.
Activists are raising awareness about the marginalisation of minority communities in urban areas.
Economic shifts can lead to the marginalisation of traditional industries and their workers.
Educational reform aims to combat the marginalisation of certain subjects in the curriculum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from entering or taking part
  • antonym: integration - the process of combining or incorporating into a whole, often used as the opposite of marginalization in social contexts
