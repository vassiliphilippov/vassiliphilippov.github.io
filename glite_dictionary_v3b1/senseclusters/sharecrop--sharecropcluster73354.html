sharecrop

verb
to exploit for profit
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛrˌkrɑp / (AmE), / ˈʃeəˌkrɒp / (BrE)
to exploit someone by taking a large share of their earnings or output while giving little in return, especially in a figurative sense.

Examples

To sharecrop new writers
The publisher was accused of trying to sharecrop new authors by taking most of their royalties.
Some tech platforms sharecrop content creators, keeping the majority of advertising revenue.
Artists felt they were being sharecropped when their work was used without fair compensation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exploit - to use someone or something unfairly for one's own advantage
  • antonym: support - to help or give assistance to someone
