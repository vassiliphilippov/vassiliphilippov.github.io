work to rule

noun
industrial action by minimal compliance with rules
5.8 5.8
Pronunciation: /wɜːrk tuː ruːl/ /; / /wɜːrk tə ruːl/ (AmE), /wɜːk tuː ruːl/ /; / /wɜːk tə ruːl/ (BrE)
a form of industrial action where employees slow down work by strictly following all rules and regulations, doing only the minimum required by their contracts

Examples

Work to rule protest
As part of their protest, employees began to work to rule, causing delays in production.
The union threatened to work to rule if management refused to negotiate.
When the dispute was not resolved, staff decided to work to rule rather than strike.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lockout - an exclusion of employees by their employer from their workplace
  • synonym: slowdown - a deliberate reduction in the rate of work
  • synonym: strike - a refusal to work as a form of protest
