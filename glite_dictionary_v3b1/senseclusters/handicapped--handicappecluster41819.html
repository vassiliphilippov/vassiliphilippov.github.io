handicapped

adjective
having a disability (outdated)
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhændiˌkæpt / (AmE), / ˈhændɪkæpt / (BrE)
(offensive, outdated) Having a physical or mental disability, typically referring to the total or partial absence of normal bodily or mental functions.

Examples

Handicapped person
Policies from decades ago used the term handicapped, but it is now considered outdated and inappropriate.
She advocates for inclusive workplaces for both handicapped and non-handicapped employees.
The building was not accessible to handicapped people, which drew criticism from activists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disabled - having a physical or mental condition that limits movements, senses, or activities
