meristem

noun
region of plant growth tissue
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛr.ɪˌstɛm / (AmE), / ˈmɛr.ɪ.stɛm / (BrE)
A region of undifferentiated plant tissue, usually found at the tips of roots and shoots, where growth by cell division occurs.

Examples

Plant meristem tissue
Damage to the root meristem can severely affect a tree's ability to absorb nutrients.
Researchers observed new cell formation within the shoot meristem.
The plant's growth was stimulated by increased activity in the meristem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cambium - a layer of actively dividing cells between xylem and phloem tissues
