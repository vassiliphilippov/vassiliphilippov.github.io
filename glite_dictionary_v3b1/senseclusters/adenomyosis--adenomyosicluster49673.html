adenomyosis

noun
uterine disorder; endometrial tissue in uterine wall
Pronunciation: / ˌæd.ə.noʊ.maɪˈoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæd.əʊ.maɪˈəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which endometrial tissue exists within and grows into the muscular wall of the uterus, often causing premenstrual pain and heavy menstrual bleeding.

Examples

adenomyosis in uterus
The ultrasound revealed signs consistent with adenomyosis in the patient's uterus.
Treatment options for adenomyosis include medication and, in some cases, surgery.
Women with adenomyosis may experience severe menstrual cramps and heavy periods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endometriosis - a related disorder involving endometrial tissue outside the uterus
