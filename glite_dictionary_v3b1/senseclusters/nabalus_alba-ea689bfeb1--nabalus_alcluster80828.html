Nabalus alba

noun
a North American plant with white flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnæb.ə.ləs ˈæl.bə/ /; / /ˈnæb.ə.ləs ˈɑːl.bə/ (AmE), /ˈnæb.ə.ləs ˈæl.bə/ /; / /ˈnæb.ə.ləs ˈɑːl.bə/ (BrE)
A herbaceous plant native to northeastern North America, with drooping clusters of yellowish-white flowers; also known as white rattlesnake root and sometimes classified in the genus Prenanthes.

Examples

Nabalus alba plant
Nabalus alba grows in shaded woodlands across the northeastern United States and Canada.
Botanists often debate whether Nabalus alba should be placed in the genus Prenanthes.
The drooping, yellowish-white flowers of Nabalus alba make it distinctive among woodland herbs.
