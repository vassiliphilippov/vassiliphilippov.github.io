backscatter

verb
to scatter radiation back toward its source
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌskætər / (AmE), / ˈbækˌskætə / (BrE)
(of radiation) to be scattered by atoms of a medium so that some of it is deflected back toward the source.

Examples

X-rays backscatter in air
Scientists observed how X-rays backscattered after passing through different materials.
Some imaging techniques rely on measuring how particles backscatter within the sample.
The radar signal will backscatter when it strikes rough surfaces such as terrain or vegetation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scatter - to cause to separate and go in different directions
