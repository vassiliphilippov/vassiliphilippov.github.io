publicum

noun
the general public
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪkʊm / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪkʊm / (BrE)
the people or community as a whole; the general public, especially in classical or legal Latin contexts

Examples

The publicum
In Roman law, the rights of the publicum were often discussed in legal texts.
The senator argued that any new policy should benefit the publicum rather than a select few.
Ancient inscriptions sometimes referred to the publicum when describing civic duties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citizenry - all the citizens of a place collectively
  • synonym: populace - the people living in a particular country or area
  • antonym: private - relating to an individual or a small group, not the public
