cyclostome

noun
jawless aquatic vertebrate (e.g. lamprey, hagfish)
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkləˌstoʊm / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪkləstəʊm / (BrE)
A primitive jawless aquatic vertebrate of the group Cyclostomata, such as a lamprey or hagfish.

Examples

A cyclostome fish
Lampreys and hagfish are two well-known examples of living cyclostomes.
Scientists studied the evolutionary history of the cyclostome to better understand vertebrate origins.
The cyclostome possesses a circular, jawless mouth adapted for sucking or rasping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hagfish - a primitive jawless fish with a round mouth
  • synonym: lamprey - a jawless fish with a sucker-like mouth
