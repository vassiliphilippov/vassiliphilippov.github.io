Synonyms

  • transitory
    6.8 1.53/m
    transitory is brief and often used in formal writing, while temporary is a general label for something not permanent.
  • transient
    7.0 4.07/m
    transient feels short and passing in everyday use, while temporary is the common word for things that do not last.
  • impermanent
    6.5 0.29/m
    impermanent sounds slightly formal and describes lack of lasting quality, while temporary is the everyday word for non-permanent things.
  • short-lived
    3.9 4.88/m
    short-lived describes things that end quickly or fade soon, while temporary is a broader word for not lasting.
  • short-term
    3.3 21/m
    short-term talks about plans or effects that last a brief period, while temporary fits many situations and objects.
  • non-permanent
    3.8 0.00/m
    non-permanent simply says something will not last long, while temporary is the usual single-word choice in speech and writing.
  • nonpersistent
    6.0 0.01/m
    nonpersistent means not continuing and is often used in technical contexts, while temporary is the common everyday term for short duration.
  • interim
    5.0 7.49/m
    interim describes a stopgap role or period until something permanent appears, while temporary covers many short-lived states.
