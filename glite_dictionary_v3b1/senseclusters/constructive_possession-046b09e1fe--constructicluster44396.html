(Law) Possession of property that a person does not physically hold but has the power and intention to control.

Examples

Constructive possession of property
He argued that he had no constructive possession of the firearm found in his car.
The court found her guilty based on constructive possession of the illegal drugs in her apartment.
Under the law, constructive possession can be established even if the defendant is not physically holding the item.
