tenon

verb
to join with a tenon
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈten.ən/ (AmE), /ˈten.ən/ (BrE)
to join pieces of wood by shaping a projecting piece (a tenon) on one and fitting it into a matching hole (a mortise) on another.

Examples

To tenon two boards
The carpenter carefully tenoned each beam to ensure a strong frame.
They tenoned the table legs into the top for extra stability.
Before assembling the door, she tenoned the panels to fit perfectly into the frame.

Related Terms

  • synonym: join - to connect or fasten things together
  • antonym: mortise - to join by making a hole (mortise) to receive a tenon
