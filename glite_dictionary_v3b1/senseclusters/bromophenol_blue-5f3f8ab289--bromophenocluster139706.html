bromophenol blue

noun
a dye and pH indicator (pH 3.0–4.6)
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbroʊmoʊfəˌnoʊl ˈbluː / (AmE), / ˌbrəʊməˌfiːnɒl ˈbluː / (BrE)
a bluish dye used as an acid-base indicator, with a useful pH range between 3.0 and 4.6

Examples

bromophenol blue indicator
Because bromophenol blue changes color in acidic conditions, it is useful for titration experiments.
Many gel electrophoresis protocols call for a trace of bromophenol blue as a tracking dye.
The laboratory technician added bromophenol blue to the solution to monitor pH changes.
