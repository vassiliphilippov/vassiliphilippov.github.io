smoke hole

noun
vent in roof for smoke to escape
Pronunciation: / ˈsmoʊk hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsməʊk həʊl / (BrE)
an opening in the roof or wall of a structure, especially a traditional dwelling, to allow smoke to escape

Examples

A smoke hole in roof
Early cabins often relied on a simple smoke hole instead of a chimney.
The teepee had a smoke hole at the top to let the fire’s smoke out.
They gathered around the hearth, watching the smoke drift up and out through the smoke hole.
