shrift

noun
confession and absolution, esp. in Christianity
7.5
Pronunciation: / ʃrɪft / (AmE), / ʃrɪft / (BrE)
the act of confessing sins to a priest and receiving absolution, especially in the Christian tradition; the state of being shriven.

Examples

To take shrift
During Lent, it was customary to receive shrift from the village priest.
He died without shrift, leaving his soul burdened by unconfessed sins.
The knight sought shrift before going into battle, hoping for forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolution - the formal release from guilt, obligation, or punishment
