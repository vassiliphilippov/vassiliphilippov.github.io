genus Bacillus

noun
a genus of rod-shaped bacteria
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈsɪl.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs bəˈsɪl.əs / (BrE)
A taxonomic genus of rod-shaped bacteria, including many species involved in the decay of organic matter and some that are parasitic.

Examples

Genus Bacillus bacteria
Scientists discovered a new species within the genus Bacillus that helps decompose organic waste.
Some members of the genus Bacillus are used commercially for their probiotic properties.
The genus Bacillus is known for producing endospores that allow the bacteria to survive harsh conditions.
