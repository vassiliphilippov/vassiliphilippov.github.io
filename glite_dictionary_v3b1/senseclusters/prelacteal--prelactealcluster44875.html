prelacteal

adjective
before first breastfeeding
Pronunciation: / priːˈlæk.ti.əl / (AmE), / priːˈlæk.ti.əl / (BrE)
given to or relating to a newborn before the first breastfeeding

Examples

Prelacteal feeding practice
In some regions, prelacteal feeds like sugar water are given to babies before breastfeeding begins.
Doctors advise against prelacteal feeding because it may increase the risk of infection in newborns.
The nurse explained that prelacteal substances are not necessary for healthy infants.
Doctors monitored the baby's health during the prelacteal phase before breastfeeding started.
Some cultures have traditional prelacteal feeding practices for newborns.
The study focused on hormonal changes in the prelacteal period after childbirth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postlacteal - relating to the period after lactation has begun
  • synonym: postnatal - relating to the period after birth
