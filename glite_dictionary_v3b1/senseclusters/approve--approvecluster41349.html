approve

verb
give official consent or sanction
1.6 62/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpruv / (AmE), / …ôÀàpruÀêv / (BrE)
to officially agree to or accept something; to think something is good or acceptable

Examples

To approve a plan
Many parents did not approve of the new school policy.
The board will not approve the project until all requirements are met.
The government must approve the proposed changes before they take effect.
Her parents did not approve of her decision to move abroad.
I fully approve of your plan to start your own business.
The committee will approve the new regulations next week if there are no objections.

Synonyms

  • assent
    6.6 0.63/m
    assent is a formal agreement given after careful thought, while approve is a common decision to accept or allow something.
  • sanction
    6.3 3.61/m
    sanction gives official permission from an authority, while approve is a general agreement or acceptance people use in many settings.
  • accept
    1.3 83/m
    accept means to take or agree to something in many ways, while approve focuses on formally agreeing or allowing it.
  • clear
    5.5 5.80/m
    clear means to authorize or pass something officially in a process, while approve covers general agreement or acceptance across contexts.
  • endorse
    4.2 12/m
    endorse means publicly support or recommend someone or something, while approve can be a private or formal acceptance without public backing.
  • okay
    3.1 0.55/m
    okay means give informal permission or say something is fine in casual talk, while approve is more formal or official consent.
  • agree
    1.0 296/m
    agree means to have the same opinion or say yes in conversation, while approve often implies formal acceptance or official permission.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disapprove - to think that something is bad or wrong; to not approve
  • synonym: endorse - to declare one's public approval or support of something
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something
