dot product

noun
scalar value from multiplying vectors
5.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /dɑːt ˈprɑː.dəkt/ /; / /dɑːt ˈprɑː.dʌkt/ (AmE), /dɒt ˈprɒ.dʌkt/ /; / /dɒt ˈprɒ.dəkt/ (BrE)
a scalar value obtained by multiplying corresponding components of two vectors and summing the results, used in mathematics and physics

Examples

The dot product of vectors
The dot product of perpendicular vectors is always zero.
To calculate the dot product of two vectors, you multiply their corresponding components and add the results.
Understanding the dot product is essential for physics students studying work and energy.
