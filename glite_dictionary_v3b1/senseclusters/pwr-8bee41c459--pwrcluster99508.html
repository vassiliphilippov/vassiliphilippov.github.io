PWR

noun
pressurized water reactor, nuclear reactor type
Pronunciation: / pi dʌbəl.juː ɑr / (AmE), / piː dʌbljuː ɑː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'pressurized water reactor', a type of nuclear reactor that uses water as a coolant and moderator, producing steam to drive a turbine

Examples

PWR nuclear plant
Engineers inspected the core of the PWR after the scheduled shutdown.
Many commercial nuclear power stations rely on PWRs for electricity generation.
The new plant will be built with a modern PWR design for enhanced safety.
