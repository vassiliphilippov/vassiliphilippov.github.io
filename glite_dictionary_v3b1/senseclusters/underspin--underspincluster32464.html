underspin

noun
backward ball spin
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌspɪn / (BrE)
a type of spin on a ball that causes it to rotate backward and bounce low when it lands

Examples

A ball with underspin
The table tennis player used underspin to make the ball stay low over the net.
Adding underspin to your shot can make it harder for your opponent to return.
Coaches often teach beginners how to create underspin for better control of the ball.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backspin - a spin that makes the ball rotate backward
  • antonym: topspin - a spin that makes the ball rotate forward
