temperate

adjective
showing moderation or self-restraint
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpərət / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərət / (BrE)
showing moderation or self-restraint; not extreme or excessive in behavior, opinions, or conditions

Examples

Temperate behavior
Despite the challenging situation, she remained temperate in her response.
He was known for his temperate habits and moderate lifestyle.
It is wise to be temperate in your comments, even when you feel provoked.
A temperate climate attracts many people who dislike extreme heat or cold.
His temperate lifestyle contributed to his excellent health.
She always tries to remain temperate in her opinions, even during heated arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extreme - exceeding what is usual or reasonable; far from moderate
  • synonym: moderate - avoiding extremes; average in amount, intensity, or degree
