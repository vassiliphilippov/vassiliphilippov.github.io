decolor

verb
remove or reduce color
7.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdiÀêÀåk ål…ö /; / d…™Ààk ål…ö / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀêÀåk ål…ô /; / d…™Ààk ål…ô / (BrE)
to remove or reduce the color from something; to cause to become white or lighter in color

Examples

To decolor fabric
Exposure to sunlight can decolor some types of dyes over time.
The laboratory used chemicals to decolor the stained fabric for analysis.
The process will decolor the liquid, turning it from red to clear.
He used a special solution to decolour the stained paper for preservation.
Prolonged exposure to sunlight will decolour the curtains, making them look old.
Several chemicals can decolour fabric when left to soak overnight.

Synonyms

  • decolorize
    7.0 0.02/m
    decolorize means removing color from an item as a process, while decolor describes causing that item to become white or lighter.
  • bleach
    3.2 3.73/m
    bleach uses chemicals or sunlight to make things whiter, while decolor means to remove or reduce color without naming the method.

Related Terms
