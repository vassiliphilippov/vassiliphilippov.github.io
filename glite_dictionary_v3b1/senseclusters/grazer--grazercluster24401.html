grazer

noun
grass-eating animal
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪzə / (BrE)
an animal that feeds mainly on grass or other low-growing plants

Examples

A grazer in the field
Cows are typical grazers that spend much of the day eating grass in the fields.
The African savanna is home to many grazers such as zebras and antelopes.
Sheep, as grazers, help maintain the health of the pasture by eating weeds and grass.
The field was full of grazers like sheep and cows eating the fresh grass.
Many African savannas are home to large grazers such as zebras and wildebeests.
A grazer spends most of its day searching for and eating plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: herbivore - an animal that eats only plants
  • antonym: predator - an animal that hunts and eats other animals
