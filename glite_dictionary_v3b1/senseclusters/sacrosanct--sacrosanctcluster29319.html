sacrosanct

adjective
too important to be interfered with
3.1 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsæk.rə.sæŋkt/ /; / /ˈsæk.roʊ.sæŋkt/ (AmE), /ˈsæk.rə.sæŋkt/ /; / /ˈsæk.rəʊ.sæŋkt/ (BrE)
regarded as too important or valuable to be interfered with; considered sacred and inviolable

Examples

A sacrosanct tradition
Family traditions were sacrosanct and never questioned in their household.
Freedom of the press is considered sacrosanct in a democratic society.
The committee treated the old rules as sacrosanct, refusing to discuss any changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inviolable - never to be broken, infringed, or dishonored
  • antonym: profane - relating to something not sacred or showing disrespect for the sacred
