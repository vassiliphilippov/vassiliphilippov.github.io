ginning

noun
removing seeds from cotton
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of removing seeds from cotton using a machine called a cotton gin

Examples

Cotton ginning
The invention of ginning revolutionized the cotton industry in the nineteenth century.
Modern ginning facilities can process large amounts of cotton quickly and efficiently.
After harvesting, the cotton undergoes ginning to separate the fibers from the seeds.
Ginning removes the seeds from raw cotton before it is sent to textile mills.
Modern ginning machines can process large amounts of cotton quickly and efficiently.
After ginning, the clean cotton fibers are ready for spinning into thread.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cotton processing - the preparation of raw cotton for use in textiles
  • degree lower: harvesting - the act of gathering crops from the fields
  • degree higher: spinning - the process of twisting fibers into yarn or thread
