nitro

noun
nitrate-based material
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrəʊ / (BrE)
a highly flammable nitrate-based material, especially nitrocellulose, formerly used in film stock, propellants, and lacquers

Examples

Nitro film
Old movie reels made of nitro can catch fire easily if not stored properly.
The laboratory handled nitro with extreme caution due to its explosive properties.
Many early film archives were lost because nitro deteriorated and became unstable over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: celluloid - a type of plastic made from nitrocellulose and camphor
  • synonym: nitrocellulose - a flammable compound used in film and lacquers
