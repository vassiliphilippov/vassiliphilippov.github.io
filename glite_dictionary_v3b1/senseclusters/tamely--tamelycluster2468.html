tamely

adverb
in a submissive, unresisting manner
7.4 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆteÉªmli / (AmE), / ËˆteÉªmli / (BrE)
in a way that shows a lack of resistance, spirit, or force; submissively or meekly.

Examples

To act tamely
She accepted the decision tamely, without any objection or protest.
The committee members tamely agreed to all the proposals without debate.
The dog obeyed tamely when given commands by its owner.

Synonyms

  • meekly
    4.3 1.15/m
    meekly shows quiet, gentle submission in action and voice, while tamely suggests a lack of resistance or spirit.
  • submissively
    5.3 0.16/m
    submissively means obeying or yielding to others willingly, while tamely implies being passive with little resistance.
  • docilely
    5.5 0.09/m
    docilely describes being easily managed or taught, while tamely focuses on lack of spirit or active resistance.
