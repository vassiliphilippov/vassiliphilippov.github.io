rattail

noun
deep-sea fish with tapering tail
Pronunciation: / ˈræˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈræt.eɪl / (BrE)
A deep-sea fish with a large head, a tapering body, and a long, slender tail, also known as grenadier.

Examples

Deep-sea rattail
Scientists study the rattail to learn about life in the ocean's depths.
The rattail is distinguished by its long, slender tail and large head.
The fisherman was excited to find a rattail among his deep-sea catch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grenadier - another name for the rattail fish, especially in fisheries
