memorialize

verb
honor or commemorate a person or event
5.8
Pronunciation: / məˈmɔːriəˌlaɪz /; / məˈmɔriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈmɔːrɪəlaɪz /; / mɪˈmɔːriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to honor or remember someone or something in a lasting or formal way, especially by creating a memorial or holding a ceremony

Examples

To memorialize a hero
Every year, the community memorializes the tragic event with a candlelight vigil.
The city plans to memorialize the fallen heroes with a new monument downtown.
They memorialized their friend through a scholarship fund in his name.
An annual ceremony is held to memorialize the victims of the tragedy.
She memorialized her grandmother by writing a heartfelt tribute.
The city decided to memorialize the fallen soldiers with a new monument.
A plaque was installed to memorialise all those who contributed to the community center.
Historically significant events are often memorialised through annual public ceremonies.
The town decided to memorialise its founders with a new statue in the park.
