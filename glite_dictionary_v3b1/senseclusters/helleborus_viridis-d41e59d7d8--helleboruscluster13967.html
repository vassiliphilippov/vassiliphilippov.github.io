Helleborus viridis

noun
a European plant with green flowers
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛləˈbɔɹəs vəˈraɪdɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪˈbɔːrəs vɪˈriːdɪs / (BrE)
A species of deciduous plant with large deep green divided leaves and nodding, saucer-shaped green flowers, native to parts of Europe; also known as the green hellebore.

Examples

Helleborus viridis plant
Botanists recorded several specimens of Helleborus viridis growing along the forest edge.
The woodland garden featured a beautiful clump of Helleborus viridis in early spring.
Unlike other hellebores, Helleborus viridis is easily identified by its green flowers and divided leaves.
