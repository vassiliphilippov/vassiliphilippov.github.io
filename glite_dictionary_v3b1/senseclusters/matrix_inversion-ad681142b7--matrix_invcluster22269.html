matrix inversion

noun
determining a matrix's multiplicative inverse
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ.trɪks ɪnˈvɜr.ʒən / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks ɪnˈvɜː.ʃən / (BrE)
the mathematical process of finding a matrix that, when multiplied by a given matrix, results in the identity matrix

Examples

The matrix inversion process
Calculating the matrix inversion is essential for solving systems of linear equations.
His research focuses on developing new algorithms for rapid matrix inversion.
The software can perform matrix inversion for both small and large matrices efficiently.
