jump-start

noun
starting a vehicle with external power
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌmp stɑːrt / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmp stɑːt / (BrE)
an act of starting the engine of a vehicle, especially one with a weak or dead battery, by connecting it to another vehicle's battery with jumper cables

Examples

A jump-start for the battery
If your battery is dead, you might need a jump-start to get moving again.
My car wouldn't start until I got a jump-start from a friend.
She offered to give me a jump-start when she saw me stranded in the parking lot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jump - to start or move suddenly
