Rh-positive

adjective
having the Rh factor in blood
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɑr eɪ ˈpɑz.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɑː ɡ eɪ ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
Having the Rh factor present in the red blood cells.

Examples

Rh-positive blood type
If you are Rh-positive, your blood contains a specific protein on the surface of red cells.
She discovered she was Rh-positive during a routine prenatal checkup.
The blood donor must be Rh-positive to match the recipient's type.

  • antonym: Rh-negative - lacking the Rh factor in blood
