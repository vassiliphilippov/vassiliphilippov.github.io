Defense Secretary

noun
head of the US Department of Defense
3.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns ˈsɛkrəˌtɛri /; / dɪˈfɛns ˌsɛkrəˌtɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns ˈsɛkrətri /; / dɪˈfɛns ˌsɛkrəˈtɛəri / (BrE)
the head of the Department of Defense in the United States, responsible for national defense and military policy

Examples

The Defense Secretary role
Historically, the Defense Secretary plays a crucial role in coordinating national security policies.
The Defense Secretary announced changes to military strategy during the press conference.
The president appointed a new Defense Secretary after the previous one resigned.
After a long search, the President nominated a new Defense Secretary to oversee national security.
The Defense Secretary has the authority to deploy troops in emergencies.
The Defense Secretary met with congressional leaders to discuss military funding.
