wood-rat

noun
a small North American rodent (Neotoma)
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌræt / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.ræt / (BrE)
a small rodent of the genus Neotoma found in North America, noted for its bushy tail, large ears, and habit of collecting various objects; also called pack rat

Examples

A wood-rat nest
A wood-rat built its nest out of bits of paper and leaves behind the shed.
Scientists observed that the wood-rat population increased after the mild winter.
The wood-rat, sometimes called a pack rat, is known for its habit of collecting shiny objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pack rat - another name for the wood-rat, especially referring to its hoarding habits
