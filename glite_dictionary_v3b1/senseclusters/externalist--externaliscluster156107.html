externalist

noun
believer in external causes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɜrnəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes or explains that meaning, mind, knowledge, or behavior depends mainly on factors outside the individual

Examples

A leading externalist
The externalist argued that language meaning is shaped by social context.
As an externalist, she emphasized the importance of environment in understanding the mind.
Many philosophers debate whether the externalist or internalist view better explains knowledge.
The philosopher was known as an externalist because he focused on social influences.
As an externalist, she believed that upbringing shapes personality more than genetics.
Debates between the externalist and internalist views are common in psychology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: environmentalist - a person who emphasizes the influence of environment on behavior or development
  • antonym: internalist - a person who believes that meaning or mind depends on internal factors
