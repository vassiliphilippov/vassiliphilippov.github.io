barricade

noun
barrier set up by police to block passage
4.1 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbærɪˌkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈbærɪkeɪd /; / ˌbærɪˈkeɪd / (BrE)
a barrier put up to block or defend a place and prevent movement through it

Examples

A street barricade
Protesters built a makeshift barricade to slow down the advancing vehicles.
The police set up a barricade at the end of the street to control traffic.
We were stopped by a barricade and asked to show our identification.
After the flood, a barricade was set up to prevent access to dangerous areas.
Police erected a barricade at the end of the street to keep protesters back.
The rebels used overturned cars to make a makeshift barricade in the city center.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrier - a structure that prevents or controls movement
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks one's way or prevents progress
  • synonym: roadblock - a barrier put up across a road by police, often to check traffic or stop suspects
