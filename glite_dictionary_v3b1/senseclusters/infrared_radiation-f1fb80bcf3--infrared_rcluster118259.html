infrared radiation

noun
type of electromagnetic radiation
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.frə.red reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.frə.red reɪ.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
Electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light but shorter than microwaves, often experienced as heat.

Examples

Infrared radiation waves
Human bodies emit infrared radiation that can be detected by thermal cameras.
Most television remote controls use infrared radiation to transmit signals to the device.
Some night vision goggles detect infrared radiation to help soldiers see in the dark.
