orbit

noun
sphere of influence or activity
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrbɪt /; / ˈɔrbət / (AmE), / ˈɔːbɪt / (BrE)
a sphere or range of influence, activity, or control

Examples

Orbit of influence
After losing his job, he found himself outside his usual orbit of colleagues and acquaintances.
Her charity work brought her into the orbit of many influential figures.
The artist moved in a completely new orbit after relocating to Paris.
After his promotion, she came under the orbit of the company's executive board.
New discoveries often happen at the orbit of multiple scientific disciplines.
The small nation found itself within the orbit of a powerful neighboring country.
