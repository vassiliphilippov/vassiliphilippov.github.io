misprision

noun
concealment of a crime
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈprɪʒən / (AmE), / mɪsˈprɪʒən / (BrE)
the deliberate failure to report or the concealment of a known crime by someone who is aware of it

Examples

Misprision of a crime
He was found guilty of misprision after failing to report the fraud he knew about.
The lawyer explained that misprision is a serious offense in many legal systems.
By hiding evidence of the crime, she committed misprision and faced legal consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complicity - involvement with others in an illegal activity or wrongdoing
  • antonym: disclosure - the act of making something known, especially a secret or crime
