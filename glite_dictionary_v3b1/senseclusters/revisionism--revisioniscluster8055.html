revisionism

noun
reinterpretation of beliefs
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɪʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒənɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice of reinterpreting or challenging established beliefs, theories, or historical accounts, often to present a new perspective

Examples

Historical revisionism
The historian's revisionism sparked debate among scholars about the accuracy of accepted facts.
Political revisionism can lead to new interpretations of a country's past events.
Some critics accuse the author of revisionism for challenging widely held historical views.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orthodoxy - traditional or established beliefs and practices
  • synonym: reinterpretation - the act of explaining or understanding something in a new way
