rationed

adjective
distributed in limited portions
6.9 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.ʃənd/ /; / /ˈreɪ.ʃənd/ (AmE), /ˈræ.ʃənd/ (BrE)
(of something) distributed in fixed or limited amounts, especially by authority or during times of scarcity.

Examples

Rationed food supplies
During the war, families survived on rationed food and supplies.
Gasoline was rationed to ensure that everyone could have enough for essential travel.
With water rationed in the city, residents had to be careful not to waste any.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unrestricted - not limited or controlled in amount
