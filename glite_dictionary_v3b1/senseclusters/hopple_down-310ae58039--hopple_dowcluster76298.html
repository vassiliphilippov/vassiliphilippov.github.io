hopple down

verb
move awkwardly downward
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑp.əl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈhɒp.əl daʊn / (BrE)
to walk or move downward awkwardly or with difficulty, typically due to an impediment

Examples

To hopple down steps
After surgery, it took effort just to hopple down the front steps.
He watched her hopple down the hallway after spraining her ankle.
The injured player tried to hopple down the sideline for help.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limp - to walk with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot
  • synonym: stumble - to trip or lose one's balance while walking
