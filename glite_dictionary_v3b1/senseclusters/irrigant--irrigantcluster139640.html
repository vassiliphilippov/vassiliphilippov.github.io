irrigant

noun
liquid for cleaning
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪɡənt / (BrE)
a liquid or substance used to wash out or clean wounds, body cavities, or soil, especially in medicine or agriculture

Examples

Wound irrigant
The surgeon applied an irrigant to thoroughly clean the wound before stitching it.
Dentists often use a special irrigant to flush out bacteria during root canal procedures.
Farmers sometimes add nutrients to the irrigant used for watering crops.
