read

verb
interpret signs, omens, or behavior
1.8 56/m
Pronunciation: / …πid / (AmE), / …πiÀêd / (BrE)
to interpret or understand the meaning of something (such as signs, behavior, results, or omens)

Examples

To read body language
He tried to read her nervous glances for a clue about how she felt.
Sailors often read the clouds to predict changes in the weather.
The fortune teller claimed she could read my future in the lines of my hand.
How should we read the results of this survey?
Many people read her silence as disapproval, but she was simply deep in thought.
The court read his statement as an admission of guilt.

Synonyms

  • interpret
    3.3 37/m
    interpret explains or decides the meaning of signs and actions, while read means noticing and making sense of those signs.
  • understand
    1.0 392/m
    understand means to grasp the idea or reason behind something, while read is about interpreting signs, results, or behavior.
  • comprehend
    2.7 12/m
    comprehend is a more formal word for fully grasping an idea, while read often means interpreting signs or behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: misread - to interpret incorrectly
