keratosis nigricans

noun
skin disease with dark patches in body folds
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs nɪˈɡrɪkænz / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs nɪˈɡrɪkanz / (BrE)
A skin condition characterized by dark, wart-like patches typically found in body folds, which may be benign or malignant.

Examples

Keratosis nigricans skin patches
Because keratosis nigricans can sometimes signal more serious issues, any new skin changes should be evaluated.
The dermatologist diagnosed him with keratosis nigricans after examining the darkened areas under his arms.
Treatment of underlying health conditions may improve the appearance of keratosis nigricans in some patients.
