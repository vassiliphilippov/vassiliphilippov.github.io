Namtar

proper noun
demon of death in Mesopotamian mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnæm.tɑːr/ (AmE), /ˈnæm.tɑː/ (BrE)
In Mesopotamian mythology, a demon who personifies death and serves as the herald of Ereshkigal, queen of the underworld.

Examples

Namtar demon
In the myth, Namtar brings plagues upon humanity by the order of Ereshkigal.
Priests would perform rituals to appease Namtar and ward off disease.
The ancient texts describe Namtar as the relentless harbinger of death.
