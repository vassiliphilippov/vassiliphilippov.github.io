wisdom

noun
sensible and prudent judgment
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪzdəm / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm / (BrE)
the ability to use knowledge and experience to make good decisions and give good advice

Examples

Great wisdom
He handled the situation with great wisdom and patience.
Her wisdom helped the company avoid making costly mistakes.
Listening to elders is often a way to gain wisdom about life.
His wisdom comes from years of experience and careful thought.
Many cultures value the wisdom of elders and listen to their advice.
She showed remarkable wisdom in making that difficult decision.
Over the years, the professor’s wisdom earned him great respect among his peers.
She relied on her grandfather’s wisdom to solve the difficult problem.
We seek the wisdom of those who have experienced these challenges before us.

  • synonym: knowledge - facts, information, and skills acquired through experience or education
  • synonym: prudence - the quality of being prudent; cautiousness
