real presence

noun
Christian doctrine of Christ's actual presence in the Eucharist
3.7 8.7
Pronunciation: /riːl ˈprez.əns/ /; / /ˈriː.əl ˈprez.əns/ (AmE), /riːl ˈprez.əns/ /; / /ˈriː.əl ˈprez.əns/ (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the doctrine that the body and blood of Christ are truly and substantially present in the Eucharist, not merely symbolically.

Examples

The real presence doctrine
Belief in the real presence sets some Christian denominations apart from others.
During the service, the priest spoke about the real presence in the Eucharist.
Some reformers rejected the doctrine of real presence, favoring a purely symbolic interpretation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: symbolic presence - the belief that Christ is present in the Eucharist only symbolically, not physically
  • synonym: transubstantiation - the Roman Catholic doctrine explaining how the bread and wine become Christ's body and blood in the Eucharist
