fortified wine

noun
wine with added spirits
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔːr.tɪ.faɪd waɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.tɪ.faɪnd waɪn / (BrE)
A wine to which a distilled spirit, usually grape brandy, has been added to increase its alcohol content.

Examples

A glass of fortified wine
After dinner, they enjoyed a glass of fortified wine with cheese and nuts.
Many dessert recipes call for a splash of fortified wine like sherry or port.
The region is famous for producing several distinctive fortified wines enjoyed worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sherry - A type of fortified wine from Spain
  • antonym: table wine - Wine that has not been fortified
