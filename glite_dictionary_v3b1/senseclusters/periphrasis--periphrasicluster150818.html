the use of more words than necessary to express an idea, often in an indirect or roundabout way

Examples

Skillful periphrasis use
He admired the poet's skillful periphrasis in describing simple emotions.
In academic writing, periphrasis can sometimes obscure the intended meaning.
The politician's answer was full of periphrasis, avoiding a direct response to the question.
Instead of saying 'before long', the author used the periphrasis 'in the not too distant future'.
Teachers often encourage students to avoid periphrasis and write more directly.
The phrase 'the father of my mother' is a periphrasis for 'grandfather'.

Synonyms

  • circumlocution
    9.2 0.17/m
    circumlocution uses many words to avoid saying something directly, while periphrasis names the same roundabout use more broadly.
  • verbosity
    7.3 0.11/m
    verbosity means using more words than needed to explain, while periphrasis often points to a deliberate roundabout phrasing.
  • wordiness
    4.8 0.09/m
    wordiness fills speech or writing with extra words and detail, while periphrasis often implies an indirect, roundabout wording.
  • prolixity
    9.9 0.04/m
    prolixity uses long, tedious language that bores listeners, while periphrasis is broader and can be simply roundabout.
  • long-windedness
    7.5 0.02/m
    long-windedness sounds casual and complains about boring speech, while periphrasis is a neutral term for roundabout wording.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conciseness - the quality of expressing much in few words
