periphrasis

noun
indirect or roundabout expression
6.0
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɪfrəsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈrɪfrəsɪs / (BrE)
the use of more words than necessary to express an idea, often in an indirect or roundabout way

Examples

Skillful periphrasis use
He admired the poet's skillful periphrasis in describing simple emotions.
In academic writing, periphrasis can sometimes obscure the intended meaning.
The politician's answer was full of periphrasis, avoiding a direct response to the question.
Instead of saying 'before long', the author used the periphrasis 'in the not too distant future'.
Teachers often encourage students to avoid periphrasis and write more directly.
The phrase 'the father of my mother' is a periphrasis for 'grandfather'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circumlocution - the use of many words where fewer would do, especially in a deliberate attempt to be vague or evasive
  • antonym: conciseness - the quality of expressing much in few words
