dissect

verb
analyze in detail; break into parts
Pronunciation: / daɪˈsɛkt, dɪˈsɛkt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛkt, daɪˈsɛkt / (BrE)
to examine or analyze something in detail by breaking it down into its component parts

Examples

To dissect a problem
Economists often dissect financial trends to understand the causes behind market changes.
She dissected every argument in his essay to find flaws in his reasoning.
The professor asked the students to dissect the poem line by line.
