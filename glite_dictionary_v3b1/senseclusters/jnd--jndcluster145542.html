jnd

noun
minimum perceptible difference between stimuli
Pronunciation: / dʒeɪ ɛn di / (AmE), / dʒeɪ ɛn di / (BrE)
(psychology) the smallest difference between two sensory stimuli that can be perceived half of the time (just noticeable difference).

Examples

The jnd threshold
If the jnd is large, people need a significant change to notice the difference.
Psychologists often use the concept of jnd to quantify sensory perception limits.
The experiment measured the jnd in loudness for different participants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: difference limen - the smallest detectable difference between two stimuli
  • synonym: threshold - the point at which a stimulus is strong enough to be noticed
