stratify

verb
form into social classes or levels
Pronunciation: / ˈstræt̬.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to divide or organize people or groups into different social levels, classes, or castes

Examples

To stratify society
Many economists warn that unchecked inequality will further stratify society.
Over time, the community began to stratify into distinct upper and lower classes.
Rapid urbanization can stratify populations along educational and income lines.
Economic inequality tends to stratify people based on wealth and opportunity.
Historians study how ancient civilizations stratified their communities into distinct groups.
The new education policies could further stratify society along income lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: segregate - to separate or set apart, especially by social categories
