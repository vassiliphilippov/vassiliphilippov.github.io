kingfisher

noun
brightly colored, fish-eating bird
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪŋˌfɪʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋˌfɪʃə / (BrE)
A brightly colored, crested bird with a large head, short tail, and long sharp bill, feeding mainly on fish and often found near rivers and lakes.

Examples

A kingfisher bird
A vibrant kingfisher darted over the river, its blue feathers flashing in the morning light.
Kingfishers are often found near clean, fast-flowing streams where they hunt for small fish.
We spotted a kingfisher perched on a branch, patiently watching the water below.
