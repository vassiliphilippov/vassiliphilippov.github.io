devaluate

verb
to decrease in value (esp. currency)
2.3 6.0
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈvæl.juː.eɪt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈvæl.juː.eɪt/ (BrE)
(of money or currency) to decrease in value, especially in relation to foreign currencies, often by governmental action or due to economic factors.

Examples

To devaluate a currency
If the company’s shares devaluate further, investors may lose confidence.
Many countries fear that their currency might devaluate due to global market instability.
The new policy caused the franc to devaluate sharply against the euro.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciate - to increase in value over time or due to economic factors
  • synonym: depreciate - to decrease in value, especially over time or due to economic factors
  • synonym: devalue - to lower the value of (esp. currency or assets)
