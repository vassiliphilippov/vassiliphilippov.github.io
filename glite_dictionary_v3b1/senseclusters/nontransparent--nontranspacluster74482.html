nontransparent

adjective
not clear or see-through
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑntrænˈspɛrənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒntrænˈspærənt / (BrE)
not clear, open, or easy to understand; not allowing light through or not honest about actions or decisions

Examples

A nontransparent process
The committee criticized the nontransparent decision-making process used by the board.
The glass was nontransparent, so we could not see what was inside the room.
Many people are concerned about the nontransparent nature of the new regulations.
The company's nontransparent hiring process made it difficult for applicants to understand the criteria.
Critics argued that the committee's nontransparent methods undermined public confidence in the results.
A nontransparent system can lead to confusion and mistrust among stakeholders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opaque - not letting light through; hard to understand
  • synonym: secretive - keeping things hidden or not revealing information
  • antonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through; easy to understand
