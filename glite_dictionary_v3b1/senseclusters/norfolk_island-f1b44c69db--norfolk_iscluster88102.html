Norfolk Island

proper noun
island territory of Australia in the Pacific Ocean
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrfək ˈaɪ.lənd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːfək ˈaɪ.lənd / (BrE)
an island territory of Australia in the Pacific Ocean, east of the Australian mainland; formerly used as a British penal colony

Examples

Norfolk Island territory
Norfolk Island was once used by the British as a penal colony in the 18th and 19th centuries.
Many tourists visit Norfolk Island to enjoy its natural beauty and unique history.
The citizens of Norfolk Island have a distinctive culture influenced by British and Polynesian heritage.
