isocyclic

adjective
having a closed ring of same atoms (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
(of a compound) containing a closed ring of atoms of the same kind, especially carbon atoms, in its molecular structure.

Examples

Isocyclic compound ring
Chemists often compare isocyclic rings with heterocyclic rings during structural analysis.
Compounds with an isocyclic configuration typically display unique chemical properties.
Many organic molecules, such as benzene, are described as isocyclic due to their carbon ring structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterocyclic - containing a ring structure with atoms of more than one kind
