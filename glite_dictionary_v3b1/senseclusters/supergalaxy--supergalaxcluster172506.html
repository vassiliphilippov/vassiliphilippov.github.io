supergalaxy

noun
very large galaxy
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈɡæləksi / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈɡæləksi / (BrE)
an extremely large galaxy or a group of galaxies forming a massive structure in the universe

Examples

A supergalaxy group
The astronomers discovered a supergalaxy at the edge of the observable universe.
A supergalaxy can contain billions of stars and many smaller galaxies within it.
Scientists are mapping the structure of the local supergalaxy to understand cosmic evolution.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: galaxy - a large system of stars, gas, and dust held together by gravity
