crucible for

noun
testing situation for ideas or people
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːsəbəl fər / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪb(ə)l fə / (BrE)
a situation or environment in which new ideas are tested, developed, or refined under pressure or challenges

Examples

A crucible for change
Her challenging childhood served as a crucible for her creativity and resilience.
The movement's early years were a crucible for many important social theories.
The university became a crucible for innovation during the tech boom.
