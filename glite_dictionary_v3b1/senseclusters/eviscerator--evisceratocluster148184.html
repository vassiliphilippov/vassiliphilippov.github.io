eviscerator

noun
one who destroys essentials
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈvɪs.ə.reɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈvɪs.ə.reɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a person or thing that severely weakens, destroys, or strips away the essential parts of something, often through harsh criticism or removal.

Examples

A harsh eviscerator
The harsh reviewer became known as an eviscerator of new plays in the theater community.
Many saw the new policy as an eviscerator of the company’s original mission.
The editor acted as an eviscerator, cutting out the most important parts of the article.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critic - a person who judges or evaluates something, often harshly
  • synonym: destroyer - a person or thing that causes destruction
  • antonym: protector - a person who keeps something safe from harm or destruction
