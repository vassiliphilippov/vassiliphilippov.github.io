Burkean

adjective
of Edmund Burke's ideas
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.kɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈbɜː.kɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the ideas or philosophy of Edmund Burke, especially regarding tradition, society, and gradual change

Examples

Burkean philosophy
The politician's Burkean approach emphasized respect for long-standing institutions.
Many scholars debate whether modern conservatism truly reflects Burkean principles.
Her essay offered a Burkean defense of gradual social change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values
  • antonym: radical - favoring thorough or complete political or social reform
