travel

noun
the act of going from place to place
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtrævəl / (AmE), / ˈtrævəl / (BrE)
the act of going from one place to another, especially over a distance (for business, pleasure, or other reasons)

Examples

Business travel
Business travel has increased significantly in recent years.
He found the constant travel exhausting, despite his love for new places.
Her job requires frequent travel to Europe and Asia.
Her work required constant travel between cities.
Mechanical engineers measured the travel of the piston inside the engine.
The rapid travel of information has transformed our society.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: commute - regular travel to and from work
  • synonym: journey - the act of traveling from one place to another, usually taking a long distance
  • antonym: stationary - not moving or changing position
