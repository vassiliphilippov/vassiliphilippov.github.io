picky

adjective
very selective or hard to please
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪki / (AmE), / ˈpɪki / (BrE)
Very selective or difficult to please, especially about details or particular things.

Examples

A picky eater
Hiring managers can be picky when reviewing job applications.
My son has always been a picky eater and refuses many vegetables.
She is very picky about where she likes to eat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easygoing - relaxed and not easily upset or bothered
  • synonym: finicky - extremely particular or hard to please
  • synonym: fussy - hard to please, especially about small details
