flank

noun
side of something; distinct section
8.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /flæŋk/ (AmE), /flæŋk/ (BrE)
the side part of something, such as a body, mountain, or group (including military formations)

Examples

The left flank
The left flank of the mountain was covered with dense forest.
The soldiers advanced quickly along the enemy's right flank.
We built a small garden on the eastern flank of the house.
After the fall, she complained of pain in her left flank.
He placed his hand gently on her flank to support her.
The horse was branded on its right flank.
A surprise attack came from the enemy's southern flank, causing confusion among the troops.
During the battle, the cavalry charged the enemy's exposed flank.
The general ordered a small unit to guard the army's left flank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: front - the forward part of a military force or formation
  • antonym: rear - the back part of a military formation
  • synonym: side - a position to the left or right of an object, place, or central point
