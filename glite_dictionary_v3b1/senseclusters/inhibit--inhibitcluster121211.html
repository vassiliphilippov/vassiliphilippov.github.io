inhibit

verb
prevent or restrain
4.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈhɪ.bət/ /; / /ɪn.ˈhɪ.bɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈhɪ.bɪt/ (BrE)
to prevent, limit, or make something less likely to happen

Examples

To inhibit growth
Parental supervision is needed so as not to inhibit children's creativity.
Some medications may inhibit the body's natural response to stress.
Strict laws can inhibit the growth of small businesses.
Certain medications can inhibit the absorption of essential nutrients.
Strong emotions may inhibit your ability to think clearly during difficult situations.
The scientist explained how cold temperatures inhibit the growth of bacteria.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to let someone do something or let something happen
  • synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening or someone from doing something
  • synonym: restrain - to prevent something from happening; to hold back
  • antonym: stimulate - to encourage something to happen or develop; the opposite of inhibit
