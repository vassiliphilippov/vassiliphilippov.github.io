Brachiosaurus

proper noun
large long-necked dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˌbrækiəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌbrækiəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
an informal term for any very large, long-necked sauropod dinosaur, often used for similar dinosaurs beyond the true Brachiosaurus genus

Examples

Any big Brachiosaurus
Kids often call any huge, long-necked dinosaur a Brachiosaurus when visiting the museum.
In movies, the gentle giant eating leaves is usually shown as a Brachiosaurus.
Although it was actually a Diplodocus, he excitedly shouted, 'Look, a Brachiosaurus!'

Related Terms

  • synonym: diplodocus - a genus of long-necked sauropod dinosaur
  • synonym: sauropod - a large, long-necked, herbivorous dinosaur
