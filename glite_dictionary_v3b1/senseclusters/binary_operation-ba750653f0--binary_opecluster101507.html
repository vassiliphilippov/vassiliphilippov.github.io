binary operation

noun
operation combining two values, often in Boolean algebra

an operation in mathematics or logic that combines two elements (operands) to produce another element, often according to the rules of Boolean algebra, where each operand and the result can have only two possible values (often 0 and 1).

Examples

A binary operation in math
Addition and multiplication are typical examples of a binary operation in mathematics.
In Boolean algebra, each binary operation works with values limited to true or false.
The logical AND is a common binary operation in computer programming.
