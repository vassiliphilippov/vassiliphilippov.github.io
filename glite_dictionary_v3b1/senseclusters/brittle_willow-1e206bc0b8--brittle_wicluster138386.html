brittle willow

noun
type of willow tree with brittle branches
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪtəl ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbrɪtəl ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A large willow tree (Salix fragilis) known for its stiff, easily broken branches.

Examples

The brittle willow tree
Because of its weak branches, the brittle willow is often damaged by strong winds.
Her favorite spot by the river was shaded by a majestic brittle willow.
The brittle willow in the garden lost several branches during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crack willow - another name for the brittle willow tree
