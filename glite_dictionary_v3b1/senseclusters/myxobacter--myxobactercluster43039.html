myxobacter

noun
slime-forming soil bacterium
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪk.soʊˌbæk.tər / (AmE), / ˈmɪksəʊˌbæktə / (BrE)
a type of bacterium that forms colonies in a self-produced slime, typically found in moist soils, decaying plant matter, or animal waste

Examples

A myxobacter colony
Decaying logs and compost piles often harbor colonies of myxobacter.
Scientists observed the myxobacter as it moved and clustered in the moist soil sample.
The myxobacter produces a slimy substance to help its colony survive.
