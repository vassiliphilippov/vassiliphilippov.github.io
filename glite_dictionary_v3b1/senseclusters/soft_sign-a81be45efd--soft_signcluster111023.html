soft sign

noun
Cyrillic letter (Ь, ь) denoting a soft consonant
8.6
Pronunciation: / sɔft saɪn / (AmE), / sɒft saɪn / (BrE)
a letter (Ь, ь) in the Cyrillic alphabet used to indicate that the preceding consonant is palatalized; in Old Church Slavonic it represented a short front vowel

Examples

The soft sign letter
In Russian, the soft sign changes the pronunciation of the preceding consonant.
The letter Ь, known as the soft sign, plays an important role in Ukrainian orthography.
When learning Russian, understanding when to use the soft sign can be challenging for beginners.
