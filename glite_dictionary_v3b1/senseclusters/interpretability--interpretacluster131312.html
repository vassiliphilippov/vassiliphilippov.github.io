interpretability

noun
ability to be represented
8.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tɜːr.prə.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /ɪn.tɜː.prə.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to be represented, defined, or simulated within another theory or system, especially in mathematics or logic

Examples

Interpretability in logic
The interpretability of a model is crucial for understanding its predictions in scientific research.
Mathematicians discussed the interpretability of one logical system within another during the seminar.
Improving the interpretability of algorithms helps users trust and adopt new technologies.
