penicillin-resistant bacteria

noun
bacteria unaffected by penicillin
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnɪˌsɪlɪn rɪˈzɪstənt bəˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˌsɪlɪn rɪˈzɪstənt bəˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
bacteria that are not affected by penicillin and continue to grow in its presence.

Examples

Penicillin-resistant bacteria spread
Hospitals are concerned about the spread of penicillin-resistant bacteria among patients.
Researchers are developing new drugs to combat penicillin-resistant bacteria.
The emergence of penicillin-resistant bacteria has complicated the treatment of common infections.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: antibiotic-resistant bacteria - bacteria resistant to one or more antibiotics
  • antonym: penicillin-sensitive bacteria - bacteria easily killed or inhibited by penicillin
