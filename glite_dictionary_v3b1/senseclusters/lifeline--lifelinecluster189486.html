lifeline

noun
rescue rope for saving people from drowning
4.4 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈlaɪ.flaɪn/ (AmE), /ˈlaɪ.flaɪn/ (BrE)
a rope or line used to rescue or keep someone safe, especially in water or dangerous situations

Examples

Throw a lifeline
During the drill, each crew member practiced tossing a lifeline to a simulated victim.
The sailor quickly threw the lifeline to the man struggling in the water.
Waves crashed as the rescued swimmer clung to the lifeline until the boat reached him.
Before entering the water, she checked that her lifeline was secured to the boat.
The diver clung tightly to the lifeline as he descended into the dark waters.
Workers made sure the lifeline was free of knots and strong enough to support the diver's weight.
