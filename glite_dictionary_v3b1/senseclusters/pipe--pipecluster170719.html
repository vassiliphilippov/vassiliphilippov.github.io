pipe

verb
utter a shrill, high-pitched sound
6.2
Pronunciation: / paɪp / (AmE), / paɪp / (BrE)
to utter or make a high-pitched, shrill sound, especially in the manner of a bird or child

Examples

To pipe with joy
Excited children piped with delight as they opened their presents.
He piped up in a shrill voice, announcing the winner of the contest.
The small bird began to pipe its song at dawn, waking everyone nearby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirp - to make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound (often of a small bird)
  • synonym: whistle - to produce a high-pitched sound by forcing air through the lips or a hole
