bunya

noun
Australian evergreen tree
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌnjə / (AmE), / ˈbʌnjə / (BrE)
a large evergreen tree native to Australia, known for its edible nuts and also called bunya-bunya

Examples

A tall bunya tree
The bunya is famous for its large cones that contain edible seeds.
Aboriginal Australians have gathered nuts from the bunya for thousands of years.
A tall bunya stands at the edge of the rainforest, towering over the other trees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bunya-bunya - another name for the bunya tree
  • synonym: pine - a type of evergreen tree with needle-like leaves
