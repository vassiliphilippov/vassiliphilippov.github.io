gutta balata

noun
hard rubbery material from tropical trees
Pronunciation: / ˌɡʌtə bəˈlɑːtə / (AmE), / ˌɡʌtə bəˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A tough, rubbery substance obtained from the latex of certain tropical trees, used especially in the past for making golf balls and other items.

Examples

Old gutta balata balls
Some antique sports equipment collectors prize old gutta balata balls for their historical value.
The core of early golf balls was often made from gutta balata for its resilience.
The invention of synthetic materials led to the decline of gutta balata in golf ball manufacturing.
