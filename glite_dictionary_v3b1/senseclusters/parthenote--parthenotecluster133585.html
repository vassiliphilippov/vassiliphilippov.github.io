parthenote

noun
cell formed by parthenogenesis
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːrθəˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈpɑːθɪnəʊt / (BrE)
a cell formed by parthenogenesis, developing from an unfertilized egg

Examples

A parthenote cell
A parthenote differs from a zygote because it has no paternal genetic material.
Ethical debates continue around the creation of human parthenotes for research purposes.
Scientists observed the development of a parthenote under laboratory conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: zygote - a cell formed by the fusion of two gametes; fertilized egg cell
