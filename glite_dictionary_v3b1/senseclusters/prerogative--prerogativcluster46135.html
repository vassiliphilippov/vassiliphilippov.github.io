prerogative

noun
exclusive right or privilege
8.2 4.30/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ôÀàr…ëÀê…°…ôt…™v / (AmE), / pr…™Ààr…í…°…ôt…™v / (BrE)
a right or privilege exclusive to a particular individual or group, especially one that is officially or hereditarily determined

Examples

The prerogative to decide
As president, it is his prerogative to appoint cabinet members.
Granting pardons is a royal prerogative that dates back centuries.
Parents sometimes act as if discipline is their unique prerogative.

Synonyms

  • exclusive right
    7.1 1.13/m
    an exclusive right is a legally reserved power for one person or group, while prerogative is often an official or hereditary privilege.
  • privilege
    3.7 25/m
    privilege is a special advantage given to some people, while prerogative usually means an official or hereditary right tied to power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: privilege - special advantage or entitlement given to a person or group
  • synonym: right - a legal, social, or ethical principle of freedom or entitlement
