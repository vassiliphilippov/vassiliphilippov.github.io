arteria lacrimalis

noun
artery supplying the lacrimal gland and eyelid
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪriə ləˌkraɪˈmeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪərɪə læˌkraɪˈmeɪlɪs / (BrE)
A small artery branching from the ophthalmic artery that supplies blood to the lacrimal gland, rectus eye muscles, upper eyelid, and part of the forehead.

Examples

The arteria lacrimalis supplies blood
Anatomists trace the path of the arteria lacrimalis to study blood flow in the orbit.
Blockage of the arteria lacrimalis can result in inadequate nourishment of the lacrimal gland.
The arteria lacrimalis is often carefully preserved during eye surgeries to maintain proper blood supply.
