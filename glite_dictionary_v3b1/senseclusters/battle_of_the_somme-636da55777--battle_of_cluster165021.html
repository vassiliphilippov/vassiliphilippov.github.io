Battle of the Somme

noun
major World War I battle in 1916
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ðə ˈsʌm / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ðə ˈsɒm / (BrE)
a major battle fought between British and French forces against Germany in World War I, lasting from July to November 1916, noted for its heavy casualties

Examples

The Battle of the Somme 1916
Historians often study the Battle of the Somme to understand the tactics and tragedy of trench warfare.
Many soldiers lost their lives in the Battle of the Somme, making it one of the bloodiest conflicts in World War I.
The Battle of the Somme began on July 1, 1916, with Allied forces launching a massive offensive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Battle of Verdun - long and deadly World War I battle in 1916 between France and Germany
  • synonym: Second Battle of Ypres - another major World War I battle involving British forces
