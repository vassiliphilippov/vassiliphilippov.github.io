plastid

noun
organelle in plants storing pigment or reserves
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈplæs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈplæs.tɪd / (BrE)
a small organelle found in the cytoplasm of plant and some protozoan cells, containing pigment or storing substances such as starch, oil, or protein

Examples

A plastid organelle
Amyloplasts are a form of plastid that stores starch in roots and tubers.
Chloroplasts are a type of plastid responsible for photosynthesis in plant cells.
The researcher studied how each plastid contributes to the cell's metabolism.
