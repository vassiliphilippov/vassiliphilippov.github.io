retch

verb
attempt to vomit without success
Pronunciation: / rɛtʃ / (AmE), / rɛtʃ / (BrE)
to make a strong, involuntary effort to vomit, sometimes bringing up stomach contents

Examples

To retch loudly
He tried not to retch when cleaning up the mess.
She covered her mouth and began to retch over the sink.
The smell made him retch repeatedly, though nothing came up.
She began to retch after seeing the graphic scene in the movie.
The unpleasant smell made him retch in the hallway.
Trying to clean the old fridge, I couldn't help but retch from the stench.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gag - to experience a reflex that almost leads to vomiting
  • synonym: regurgitate - to bring food up from the stomach into the mouth
  • synonym: vomit - to eject the contents of the stomach through the mouth
