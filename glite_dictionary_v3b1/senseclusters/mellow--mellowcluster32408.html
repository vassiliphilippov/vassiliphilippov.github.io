mellow

adjective
relaxed and unhurried
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɛləʊ / (BrE)
(of a person, mood, or atmosphere) relaxed, gentle, or softened, often as a result of age or experience

Examples

Mellow mood
She became more mellow with age and stopped worrying about the small things.
The evening had a mellow atmosphere, with soft music and gentle conversation.
We enjoyed a mellow afternoon at the café, watching the world go by.
Over the years, his mellow temperament made him easy to get along with.
She offered mellow advice, speaking with the gentle wisdom of age.
Their friendship grew deeper and more mellow as they both matured.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harsh - severe or cruel in tone, manner, or effect
  • synonym: mature - having reached full development or growth; emotionally or intellectually developed
  • synonym: relaxed - free from tension and anxiety
  • antonym: tense - feeling or showing nervousness or anxiety
