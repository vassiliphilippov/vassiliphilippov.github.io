phenotype

noun
traits inferred from digital data
Pronunciation: / c8fid0n59ccta6ap / (AmE), / c8fid0n598ata6ap / (BrE)
(in digital health and computational research) a set of behavioural or health-related traits inferred about a person from digital data sources (for example, smartphones, wearables, or social-media activity).

Examples

Digital phenotype
Researchers use smartphone data to build a phenotype that reflects a patient's digital health profile.
The study derived a wearables-based phenotype showing sleep patterns and activity levels over months.
Clinicians can use a patient's social media phenotype to screen for mood changes between visits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digital biomarker - a measurable signal or feature derived from digital data that indicates health status or physiological state
  • antonym: genotype - the genetic makeup of an organism; contrasts with phenotype, which is the observable expression
