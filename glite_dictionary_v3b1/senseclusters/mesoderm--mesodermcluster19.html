mesoderm

noun
middle embryonic germ layer
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛsəˌdɝm or ˈmiːsəˌdɝm / (AmE), / ˈmɛs.əˌdɜːm or ˈmiːs.əˌdɜːm / (BrE)
the middle embryonic germ layer in animals, which develops into muscle, bone, cartilage, blood, and connective tissue

Examples

The mesoderm layer
Defects in the mesoderm can lead to serious developmental disorders.
During embryonic development, the mesoderm forms tissues such as muscles and bones.
The heart is derived from cells that originated in the mesoderm.
