helminthic

adjective
relating to worm-based therapy
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (AmE), / hɛlˈmɪnθɪk / (BrE)
(in medicine) relating to the deliberate use of live parasitic worms (helminths) or their products as a treatment to modify immune responses in autoimmune or inflammatory conditions.

Examples

Helminthic therapy
Some researchers are exploring helminthic therapies as alternatives to conventional immunosuppressants.
The clinic offered a small trial of helminthic treatment to patients with severe inflammatory bowel disease.
After months without improvement, he decided to try a monitored helminthic protocol under doctor supervision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anthelmintic - a substance or treatment that expels or destroys parasitic worms; conceptually opposite to using helminths therapeutically
  • synonym: helminth therapy - therapy that uses live parasitic worms or their products to treat autoimmune or inflammatory diseases
