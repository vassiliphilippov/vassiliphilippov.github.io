optic chiasma

noun
crossing of optic nerves at brain base
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪk kaɪˈæzmə / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪk kaɪˈazmə / (BrE)
the X-shaped structure formed by the crossing of the optic nerves from each eye at the base of the brain

Examples

The optic chiasma structure
Damage to the optic chiasma can result in loss of peripheral vision.
The optic chiasma is a crucial site for the partial crossing of nerve fibers from the eyes.
Tumors near the optic chiasma may affect both visual fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: optic nerve - the nerve that carries visual information from the eye to the brain
