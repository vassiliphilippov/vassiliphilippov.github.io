newness

noun
the quality of being new or recent
1.9 1.20/m
Pronunciation: / ÀànuÀê.n…ôs / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀê.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being new or recent; the condition of not having existed before

Examples

The newness of things
She was excited by the newness and energy of moving to a different city.
The newness of the technology attracted a lot of early adopters.
There was an undeniable newness to the air after the storm passed.

Synonyms

  • novelty
    4.7 6.07/m
    novelty is being new, original, and often interesting or surprising, while newness is simply the state of being new.
  • freshness
    2.5 0.47/m
    freshness suggests a lively or recent quality and sometimes a pleasant smell, while newness focuses on not having existed before.
  • recency
    6.4 0.19/m
    recency means something happened not long ago and focuses on time, while newness means the condition of being newly created.
  • recentness
    3.9 0.01/m
    recentness shows that something appeared or existed a short time ago, while newness means it did not exist before.
