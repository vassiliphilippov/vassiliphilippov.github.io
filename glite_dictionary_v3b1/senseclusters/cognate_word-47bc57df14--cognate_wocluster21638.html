cognate word

noun
word with the same ancestral origin as another
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑɡ.neɪt wɜrd / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.neɪt wɜːd / (BrE)
a word that shares a common origin with another word in a different language, both having derived from the same ancestral word

Examples

A cognate word example
Latin 'frater' and English 'brother' are classic examples of cognate words in linguistics.
Many Spanish and Italian cognate words make learning vocabulary easier for language students.
The English word mother and the German word Mutter are cognate words because they share a common origin.
