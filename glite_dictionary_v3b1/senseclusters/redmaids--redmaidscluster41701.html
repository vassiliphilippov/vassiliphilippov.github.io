redmaids

noun
succulent plant with reddish-pink flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌmeɪdz / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌmeɪdz / (BrE)
A low-growing succulent plant with small, bright reddish-pink flowers, native to the southwestern United States.

Examples

Bright redmaids flowers
Gardeners often plant redmaids in rock gardens for their vibrant color and easy care.
In spring, the hills were dotted with blooming redmaids after the rains.
The desert landscape comes alive when redmaids cover the ground with brilliant pink flowers.
