dragoness

noun
fierce or domineering woman
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡənɛs / (AmE), / ˈdræɡənɪs / (BrE)
a woman who is fierce, domineering, or terrifying, often used disapprovingly to describe someone seen as overbearing

Examples

A strict dragoness
The new manager quickly gained a reputation as a dragoness among her staff.
Some students feared the strict teacher, calling her a dragoness behind her back.
Despite her dragoness image, she cared deeply for her family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battle-axe - an aggressive and domineering older woman
  • antonym: gentlewoman - a woman who is polite and kind
  • synonym: tyrant - a person who uses power in a cruel or harsh way
