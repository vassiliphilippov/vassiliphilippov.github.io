profess

verb
declare belief or allegiance openly
3.8
Pronunciation: / prəˈfɛs / (AmE), / prəˈfɛs / (BrE)
to declare, claim, or admit something openly, especially feelings, beliefs, innocence, or expertise (sometimes insincerely or falsely)

Examples

To profess support
Although he professes to support the cause, his actions suggest otherwise.
She professed loyalty to the organization in her public statement.
They professed their dedication to the team after the victory.
Dr. Lee professes physics and enjoys mentoring students.
He professes to be an expert in Renaissance art.
She professes organic chemistry at the university.
He finally professed that he was responsible for the error.
The official professed to accepting the blame for the failed project.
Under pressure, she professed her involvement in the incident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affirm - to state something firmly and publicly
  • synonym: confess - to admit to a fault or crime
  • synonym: declare - to state something in a formal or emphatic way
  • antonym: deny - to state that something is not true or refuse to accept it
  • synonym: pretend - to behave as if something is true when it is not
