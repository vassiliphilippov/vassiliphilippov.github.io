dilatory

adjective
tending to delay; slow-acting
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪləˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈdɪlətəri / (BrE)
tending to cause delay or to waste time; slow to act or respond.

Examples

A dilatory response
She received a warning for her dilatory approach to completing assignments.
The committee's dilatory tactics frustrated progress on the project for several weeks.
They were criticized for being dilatory in addressing important safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expeditious - done with speed and efficiency
  • synonym: procrastinating - delaying action; putting off tasks
  • antonym: punctual - acting or arriving at the correct time
