martial law

noun
military rule over civilians in an emergency
5.0 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrʃəl lɔ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl lɔː / (BrE)
the temporary rule by military authorities over civilian affairs, often during times of war or civil crisis, which replaces or suspends normal civil law

Examples

Martial law declared
The country remained under martial law until order was restored.
The government declared martial law in response to the widespread riots.
Under martial law, civil liberties such as freedom of assembly were suspended.
