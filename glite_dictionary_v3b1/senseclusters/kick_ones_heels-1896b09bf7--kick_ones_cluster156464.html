kick one's heels

verb
wait idly, often when delayed
Pronunciation: / kɪk wʌnz hilz / (AmE), / kɪk wʌnz hiːlz / (BrE)
to wait around or be forced to wait, especially when bored or frustrated, usually because someone or something is delayed

Examples

To kick her heels
She spent the afternoon kicking her heels at the airport after her flight was postponed.
The meeting was delayed, so I had to kick my heels in the lobby for almost an hour.
We were left kicking our heels outside the restaurant until they opened the doors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kill time - to do something to make time pass more quickly, often while waiting
  • synonym: wait - to stay in one place or remain inactive until something happens
