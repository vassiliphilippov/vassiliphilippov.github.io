kick one's heels

verb
wait idly, often when delayed
5.4 6.2
Pronunciation: /kɪk wʌnz hiːlz/ /; / /kɪk wʌns hiːlz/ (AmE), /kɪk wʌnz hiːlz/ /; / /kɪk wʌns hiːlz/ (BrE)
to wait around or be forced to wait, especially when bored or frustrated, usually because someone or something is delayed

To kick her heels

We were left kicking our heels outside the restaurant until they opened the doors.
