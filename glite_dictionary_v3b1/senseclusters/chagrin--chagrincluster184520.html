chagrin

verb
to embarrass or annoy
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈɡrɪn / (AmE), / ʃəˈɡrɪn / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel embarrassed, annoyed, or disappointed, especially because of failure or a setback

Examples

To chagrin a friend
The coach's harsh criticism chagrinned the young player after the match.
She was chagrinned by her failure to solve the problem in time.
His careless mistake chagrinned him in front of his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone feel slightly angry or irritated
  • antonym: delight - to give someone great pleasure or satisfaction
  • synonym: embarrass - to make someone feel awkward or ashamed
