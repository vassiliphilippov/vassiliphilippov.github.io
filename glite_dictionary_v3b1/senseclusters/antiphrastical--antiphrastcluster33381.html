antiphrastical

adjective
opposite meaning
Pronunciation: / ˌæntɪˈfræstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈfræstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the use of words in a sense opposite to their usual meaning, often for irony or sarcasm

Examples

An antiphrastical comment
Her antiphrastical comment, 'What a beautiful mess,' made everyone laugh.
The teacher explained the antiphrastical use of language in classic literature.
Without context, antiphrastical statements can easily be misunderstood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ironic - using words to express the opposite of their literal meaning
  • antonym: literal - using words in their usual or most basic sense
