contextualization

noun
placing in context
7.2
Pronunciation: /kən.tekst.ʃuː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kən.tekst.ʃuː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of placing something within its context to make it clearer or easier to understand

Examples

Language contextualization
Effective contextualization helps students understand new vocabulary in real-life situations.
The film's contextualization of historical events made the story more engaging for viewers.
Teachers often use gestures and tone for the contextualization of classroom instructions.
The contextualization of historical events helps students understand their significance.
Effective contextualization can make complex scientific concepts easier to grasp.
Teachers often use contextualization to connect new information to students' prior knowledge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decontextualization - the removal of something from its context
  • synonym: explanation - the act of making something clear or easy to understand
  • synonym: interpretation - the act of explaining or understanding the meaning of something
  • antonym: isolation - the act of separating something from its context
