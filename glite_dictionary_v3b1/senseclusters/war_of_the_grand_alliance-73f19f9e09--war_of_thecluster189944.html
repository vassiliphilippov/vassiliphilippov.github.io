War of the Grand Alliance

noun
European war (1689–1697) against France
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɔr əv ðə ˌɡrænd əˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / wɔː əv ðə ˌɡrænd əˈlaɪəns / (BrE)
A European war (1689–1697) in which France, under Louis XIV, fought against a coalition of England, the Holy Roman Empire, Spain, the Dutch Republic, and other states.

Examples

The War of the Grand Alliance
England and the Dutch Republic were prominent members of the coalition during the War of the Grand Alliance.
Scholars have written extensively about the political consequences of the War of the Grand Alliance.
The War of the Grand Alliance dramatically shifted the balance of power in late 17th-century Europe.
