traditionalize

verb
to make seem traditional
Pronunciation: / trəˈdɪʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something become or seem like a tradition or part of a longstanding custom

Examples

To traditionalize customs
The museum sought to traditionalize the new holiday by connecting it to local folklore.
Some leaders try to traditionalize modern practices to gain wider acceptance in their communities.
The author traditionalized the ceremony by describing it as rooted in ancient customs.
The community decided to traditionalize the annual parade by including folk dances.
Over time, the recipe was traditionalized and became a staple at family gatherings.
They hope to traditionalize this practice so future generations will continue it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conventionalize - to make something conform to established conventions or traditions
  • antonym: modernize - to make something more modern or up to date
