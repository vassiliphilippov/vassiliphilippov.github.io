high-pitched

adjective
having a high frequency or pitch
2.1
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈpɪtʃt / (AmE), / haɪ ˈpɪtʃt / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) having a high frequency or pitch; shrill or sharp in tone.

Examples

High-pitched voice
Dogs are able to hear high-pitched noises that humans cannot detect.
Her high-pitched laughter echoed across the playground.
The fire alarm emitted a high-pitched sound that could be heard throughout the building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-pitched - (of a sound) having a low frequency or pitch
  • synonym: shrill - (of a sound) high-pitched and piercing
