squint

verb
partly close the eyes
Pronunciation: / skwɪnt / (AmE), / skwɪnt / (BrE)
to partly close one's eyes, especially when looking at something bright or trying to see more clearly

Examples

To squint in sunlight
He squinted against the glare of the afternoon sun.
If you squint, you might be able to make out the distant mountains.
She had to squint to read the tiny print on the label.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peer - to look searchingly or with difficulty
