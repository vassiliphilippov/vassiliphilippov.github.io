saltspoonful

noun
small measure of salt
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔːltˌspuːnfʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltˌspuːnfʊl / (BrE)
an amount equal to what fills a small spoon used for serving salt, about one quarter teaspoon

Examples

A saltspoonful of salt
The recipe calls for a saltspoonful of salt to enhance the flavor.
She measured a saltspoonful of sugar to add to her tea.
A saltspoonful is a traditional measurement rarely used in modern cooking.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: teaspoonful - an amount equal to what fits in a teaspoon
