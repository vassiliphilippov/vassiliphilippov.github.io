free

adjective
not chemically bound; able to move freely (science)
1.0 5.1
Pronunciation: / fri / (AmE), / friː / (BrE)
(of a particle or element) not combined with other substances or constrained; able to move independently, especially in scientific contexts.

Examples

Free electron
A free electron can move through a conductor and contribute to electric current.
Ozone decomposes to release free oxygen atoms in the upper atmosphere.
Pure hydrogen exists in nature as free molecules rather than being chemically bound to other elements.
