Chrysopidae

noun
family of green lacewings (insects)
9.9
Pronunciation: / kraɪˈsɑːpɪˌdiː / (AmE), / kraɪˈsɒpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of insects commonly known as green lacewings, characterized by their delicate green bodies and lacy wings, and known for their role in controlling pest populations.

Examples

The Chrysopidae family
Farmers often encourage the presence of Chrysopidae to reduce the need for chemical pesticides.
Researchers studied various species within the Chrysopidae family to assess their effectiveness as biological control agents.
The larvae of Chrysopidae are voracious predators of aphids in many gardens.
