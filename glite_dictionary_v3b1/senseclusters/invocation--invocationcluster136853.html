invocation

noun
prayer for divine help or guidance
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnvoʊˈkeɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.voʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnvəˈkeɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.vəˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a prayer or request for help, guidance, or presence from God or a higher power, especially at the start of a religious service or in times of need

Examples

Opening invocation prayer
Each meeting opens with an invocation led by a member of the clergy.
The choir stood quietly as the invocation was offered before the service.
The minister began the ceremony with an invocation to seek blessings for the couple.
During times of crisis, people often turn to invocation for strength and comfort.
Her speech ended with an invocation of unity and peace among all communities.
The priest began the ceremony with an invocation to the heavens for guidance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benediction - a short invocation for divine help, blessing, and guidance, usually at the end of worship service
  • synonym: supplication - the action of asking or begging for something earnestly or humbly
