marshland

noun
area of low, grassy wetland
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrʃˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃˌlænd / (BrE)
An area of low, often waterlogged land with grassy vegetation, typically forming a transition between dry land and bodies of water.

Examples

A marshland area
Birdwatchers often visit the marshlands to spot rare species during migration season.
Much of the area was drained for farming, but pockets of marshland still remain.
The nature reserve protects a large stretch of marshland along the river's edge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with acidic, peat-rich soil
  • synonym: swamp - a wetland area with standing water and often tree cover
  • antonym: upland - high or hilly land above the level of marshes
