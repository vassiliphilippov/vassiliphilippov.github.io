oyez

noun
attention call
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊjeɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔɪɛz / (BrE)
a traditional call used by a town crier or court officer to command attention before making an announcement

Examples

A loud oyez announcement
The court officer shouted oyez three times to signal the start of the proceedings.
In historical reenactments, the town crier often begins with a loud oyez to gather the crowd.
The judge waited until the clerk called out oyez before entering the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hear ye - an old-fashioned phrase used to call for attention
