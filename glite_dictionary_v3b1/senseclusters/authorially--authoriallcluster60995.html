authorially

adverb
in an author's manner
8.5
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈθɔːriəli / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɔːriəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or is characteristic of an author of a work

Examples

Written authorially
The story is authorially shaped, with the writer's voice clearly present throughout.
Some critics argue that the film is authorially driven, reflecting the director's personal style.
The essay was authorially influenced, showing the writer's unique perspective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritative - having the confident quality of someone who is respected or obeyed
