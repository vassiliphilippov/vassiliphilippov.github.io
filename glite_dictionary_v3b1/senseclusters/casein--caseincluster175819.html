casein

noun
a milk protein used in industry and food
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.siˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.siː.ɪn / (BrE)
a protein found in milk and used in making products such as plastics, adhesives, and foods

Examples

Casein protein in milk
Casein can trigger allergies in some individuals sensitive to milk proteins.
Many cheeses are made by curdling milk to separate the casein from the whey.
Some paints use casein as a binding agent for pigments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: whey - the watery part of milk separated from the curds, remaining after casein is removed
