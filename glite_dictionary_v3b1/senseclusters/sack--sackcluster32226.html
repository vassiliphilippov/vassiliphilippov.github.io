sack

verb
to plunder a city after capture
5.8
Pronunciation: / sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
to plunder and destroy a town or city, typically after capturing it in war

Examples

To sack a town
After months of siege, the army finally sacked the fortress and looted its treasures.
Many towns were sacked during the turbulent years of the empire's decline.
The invaders sacked the ancient city, leaving nothing but ruins behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loot - to steal goods, typically during war or chaos
  • synonym: pillage - to rob a place, especially in war, using violence
