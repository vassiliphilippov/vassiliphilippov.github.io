duper

noun
person who deceives
Pronunciation: / ˈduːpər / (AmE), / ˈdjuːpə / (BrE)
a person who deceives or tricks others, especially for personal gain

Examples

A clever duper
The skilled duper managed to trick dozens of people with his elaborate story.
Authorities finally caught the duper who had been running scams for years.
She realized too late that the charming man was actually a duper after her money.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fraudster - someone who commits fraud
  • synonym: swindler - a person who cheats others out of money or assets
  • antonym: victim - a person who is deceived or tricked
