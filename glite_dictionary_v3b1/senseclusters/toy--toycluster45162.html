toy

verb
handle or consider lightly or playfully
5.1 4.5
Pronunciation: /tɔɪ/ (AmE), /tɔɪ/ (BrE)
to handle, consider, or deal with something in a playful or unserious way

Examples

To toy with ideas
He often toyed with the idea of moving abroad but never made concrete plans.
She absentmindedly began to toy with her pen during the meeting.
Stop toying with your food and eat your dinner.
Don't toy with the proposal if you have no intention of accepting it.
He liked to toy with dangerous ideas, never taking them seriously.
She realized he was only toying with her emotions.
Don't toy with his emotions if you don't mean what you say.
He toyed with his food, clearly not interested in eating.
She would sometimes toy with the idea of moving to another country but never acted on it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiddle - handle or manipulate something nervously or idly
  • synonym: play - manipulate or engage with something for amusement
  • degree lower: toy - an object for a child to play with (the noun sense)
  • synonym: trifle - to treat something as unimportant or not serious
