radical mastectomy

noun
extensive breast removal surgery
9.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈræd.ɪ.kəl mæs.ˈtek.tə.miː/ /; / /ˈræd.ɪ.kəl mə.ˈstek.tə.miː/ (AmE), /ˈræd.ɪ.kəl mæs.ˈtek.tə.miː/ /; / /ˈræd.ɪ.kəl mə.ˈstek.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical operation involving the removal of a breast, the underlying chest muscles, and lymph nodes in the adjacent armpit, typically to treat breast cancer

Examples

A radical mastectomy operation
After her diagnosis, the doctor recommended a radical mastectomy to prevent the spread of cancer.
She underwent a radical mastectomy followed by chemotherapy as part of her breast cancer treatment.
The radical mastectomy was more common before modern breast-conserving treatments became available.
