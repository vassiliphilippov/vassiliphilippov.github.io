verb
to credit as author
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪlaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈbaɪlaɪnd / (BrE)
to credit someone as the author of a published article or piece, typically by including their name.

Examples

To byline a story
The article was bylined by a well-known journalist.
She was thrilled to see her name bylined on the front page.
Many stories in the magazine are bylined, but some remain anonymous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anonymize - to remove or withhold the name of the author
  • synonym: credit - to publicly acknowledge someone as the creator of something
