pressure to excel

noun
stress or urge to achieve high standards
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər tə ɪkˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə tə ɪkˈsɛl / (BrE)
the stress or feeling of obligation to achieve very high standards or be highly successful

Examples

Strong pressure to excel
Many students experience a strong pressure to excel during exam season.
Parents sometimes unintentionally create pressure to excel by setting very high expectations.
The pressure to excel in sports can lead to anxiety for young athletes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peer pressure - influence from members of one's peer group to behave in a certain way
  • antonym: relaxation - the feeling of being free from tension and anxiety
  • synonym: strive - to make great efforts to achieve or obtain something
