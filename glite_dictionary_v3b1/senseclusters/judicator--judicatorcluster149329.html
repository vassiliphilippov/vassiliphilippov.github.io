judicator

noun
person who judges
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdɪkeɪtə /; / ˈdʒuːdɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who acts as a judge or makes decisions in disputes, competitions, or performances

Examples

A judicator in court
The judicator listened carefully before announcing the verdict.
Each team presented their arguments to the judicator during the debate competition.
A fair judicator is essential for resolving conflicts in any organization.
The judicator carefully reviewed each painting before announcing the winner of the art contest.
As a judicator, she must remain impartial and fair throughout the entire competition.
Each performance was scored by a panel of judicators with years of experience in the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arbiter - a person with power to decide a dispute
  • synonym: judge - an official who decides cases in a court of law
  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something but does not judge
  • synonym: referee - an official who supervises a game or contest and enforces the rules
