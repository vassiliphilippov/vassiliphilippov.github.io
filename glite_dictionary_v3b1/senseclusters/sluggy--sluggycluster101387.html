sluggy

adjective
thick and slimy
Pronunciation: / ˈslʌɡi / (AmE), / ˈslʌɡi / (BrE)
thick, viscous, or slimy in texture and slow to flow, especially describing mud or a surface.

Examples

Sluggy mud
After the rain, the path became sluggy and difficult to walk on.
The mixture turned sluggy as more clay was added to the water.
Her boots were covered in sluggy mud after hiking through the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slimy - covered with or resembling slime; slippery and thick
  • synonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency
  • antonym: watery - thin and liquid, not thick or sticky
