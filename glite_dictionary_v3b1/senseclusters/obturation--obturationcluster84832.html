obturation

noun
sealing in firearms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑbtʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒbtjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the sealing of a firearm barrel or cartridge by the expansion of the projectile to prevent the escape of propellant gas

Examples

Barrel obturation
Proper obturation is essential to ensure that no gas escapes from the firearm barrel during firing.
The design of the bullet allows for effective obturation when the gun is discharged.
Engineers tested new materials to improve obturation in high-pressure cartridges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leakage - the escape of gas or liquid through a hole or crack
  • synonym: sealing - the act of closing something tightly
