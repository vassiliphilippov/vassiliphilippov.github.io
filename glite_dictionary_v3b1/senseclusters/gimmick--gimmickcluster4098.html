gimmick

verb
to add a trick device
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪmɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪmɪk / (BrE)
to equip or alter something with a trick or device in order to attract attention or interest

Examples

To gimmick a product
The company gimmicked their new phone with flashy lights to attract young customers.
Some magicians gimmick their props to create surprising effects during their shows.
He gimmicked the advertisement to make the product seem more exciting than it really was.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embellish - to make something more attractive by adding details or features
  • synonym: enhance - to improve or increase the quality or value of something
  • antonym: simplify - to make something easier or less complicated
