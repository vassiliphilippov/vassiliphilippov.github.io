inherently

adverb
in a natural or essential way
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɛrəntli / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪərəntli / (BrE)
In a way that is a natural, fundamental, or essential part of something.

Examples

Is inherently dangerous
Some risks are inherently unavoidable in any adventure sport.
The painting is inherently beautiful because of its simplicity.
This system is inherently flawed and needs to be replaced.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accidentally - not in a natural or essential way, but by chance
  • synonym: essentially - used to emphasize the basic, fundamental nature of something
