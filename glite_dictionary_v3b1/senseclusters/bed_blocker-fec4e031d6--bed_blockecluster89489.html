bed blocker

noun
patient occupying hospital bed unnecessarily
Pronunciation: / bɛd ˈblɑkə / (AmE), / bɛd ˈblɒkə / (BrE)
(in healthcare) a patient who remains in hospital longer than medically necessary, delaying discharge and reducing bed availability for others.

Examples

Hospital bed blocker
The ward manager said a single bed blocker was preventing several urgent admissions all week.
Many families complained when their elderly relative was labelled a bed blocker in the local paper.
After the assessment, social services arranged support so the bed blocker could be safely discharged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delayed discharge - the situation in which a patient remains in hospital after they are medically ready to leave, often due to lack of social or community support
  • antonym: discharge - the process of releasing a patient from hospital care back into the community or another care setting
