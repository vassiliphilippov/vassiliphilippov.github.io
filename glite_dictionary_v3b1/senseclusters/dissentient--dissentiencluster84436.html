dissentient

adjective
disagreeing with the majority
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛnʃənt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛn.ti.ənt /; / dɪˈsɛnʃənt / (BrE)
disagreeing with the majority or refusing to conform, especially to official or commonly accepted opinions or rules

Examples

Dissentient opinion
His dissentient opinion was recorded in the official minutes.
She was the only dissentient voice during the committee's vote.
The report was approved with one dissentient member refusing to sign.
During the reign of Elizabeth I, many dissentient Catholics worshipped in secret to avoid penalties.
In historical records, dissentient voices often represent a struggle for religious freedom.
The government imposed fines on those deemed dissentient for refusing to attend Anglican services.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformist - someone who conforms, especially to accepted norms or majority opinion
  • synonym: dissenting - expressing disagreement, especially in a formal context
  • synonym: nonconformist - a person who does not conform to established church practices, especially in England
