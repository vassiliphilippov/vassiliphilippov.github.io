onomatoclast tendency

noun
dislike of sound-imitating words
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːnəˌmætəˈklæst tənˈdɛnsɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒnəməˌklɑːst tɛnˈdɛnsɪ / (BrE)
A dislike or aversion to sound-imitating words, such as onomatopoeia.

Examples

Onomatoclast tendency in language
Her onomatoclast tendency meant she preferred more abstract language in her poetry.
In linguistic discussions, a strong onomatoclast tendency is considered quite rare.
The author's onomatoclast tendency led her to avoid using sound-imitative expressions.
