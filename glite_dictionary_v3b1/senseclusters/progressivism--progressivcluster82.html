progressivism

noun
support for social and political reform
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪvɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A political or social philosophy that advocates for reform and progress, emphasizing the need for improvements in government and society.

Examples

Progressivism in politics
Critics argue that progressivism sometimes underestimates the challenges of implementing reforms.
Many social movements in the 20th century were inspired by ideals of progressivism.
The senator has long been a strong advocate of progressivism in social policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservatism - a political philosophy favoring traditional values and institutions
  • synonym: liberalism - a political theory advocating individual freedoms and government by law with the consent of the governed
