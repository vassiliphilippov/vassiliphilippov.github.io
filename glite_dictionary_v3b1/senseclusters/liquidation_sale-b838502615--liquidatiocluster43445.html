liquidation sale

noun
sale to dispose of assets at reduced prices
4.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /lɪ.kwɪ.ˈdeɪ.ʃən seɪl/ /; / /lɪ.kwə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən seɪl/ (AmE), /lɪ.kwə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən seɪl/ /; / /lɪ.kwɪ.ˈdeɪ.ʃən seɪl/ (BrE)
a sale of all or most of a company's assets or inventory, often at reduced prices, typically when a business is closing down or going out of business

Examples

A liquidation sale event
Many employees lost their jobs when the company held a liquidation sale and shut down operations.
The furniture store announced a liquidation sale after deciding to close its doors permanently.
We bought several appliances at the liquidation sale for a fraction of the original price.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: auction - a sale in which goods are sold to the highest bidder
  • synonym: clearance sale - a sale to clear out stock, often at reduced prices
