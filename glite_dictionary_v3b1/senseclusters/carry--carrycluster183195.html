carry

verb
continue or extend into another area
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri / (AmE), / ˈkæri / (BrE)
to extend, continue, or advance something (such as an idea, action, or influence) into another place, area, or period

Examples

To carry an idea forward
Her influence carried into many different aspects of the project.
Tensions from the conflict began to carry into neighboring countries.
The excitement of the festival carried into the following week.
Let's not carry this argument to extremes.
She tends to carry her jokes to the point where they stop being funny.
You shouldn't carry your criticism of his work too far.
It takes determination to carry such an ambitious project forward.
She worked tirelessly to carry the campaign to success despite many setbacks.
The senator hopes to carry the initiative through the next stage in parliament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance - to move something forward or promote its progress
  • synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as being larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • synonym: extend - to spread or stretch something out to a wider area or a longer period
  • synonym: further - to help forward or promote (something, such as a cause)
  • antonym: hinder - to make it more difficult for something to happen or develop
  • synonym: overdo - to do something to an excessive degree
  • antonym: restrain - to keep something within limits
