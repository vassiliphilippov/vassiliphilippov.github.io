Cyrus II

proper noun

Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðiː ˈsek.ənd/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.rəs ðiː ˈsek.ənd/ (BrE)
(circa 600–529 BC) King of Persia and founder of the Persian Empire, known for uniting the Medes and Persians and creating one of the largest empires of the ancient world.

Examples

Cyrus II the Great
Cyrus II is credited with the unification of the Medes and Persians into a powerful empire.
Historians regard Cyrus II as a tolerant ruler who respected the customs and religions of the lands he conquered.
The reign of Cyrus II marked a turning point in the history of the ancient Near East.
