juggle

verb
manipulate or adjust cunningly
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡl̩ /; / ˈdʒʌɡ(ə)l / (BrE)
to manipulate or adjust things, such as facts, accounts, or schedules, in a clever or deceptive way

Examples

To juggle the numbers
He attempted to juggle the accounts so the discrepancy wouldn't be noticed.
She often has to juggle different priorities to keep her business running smoothly.
The accountant tried to juggle the numbers to conceal the losses.
He was accused of juggling the schedule to benefit his friends.
Politicians often juggle facts to present their policies in a better light.
The accountant tried to juggle the numbers to hide the losses from investors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjust - to change slightly in order to improve or make fit
  • synonym: deceive - to mislead or trick someone
  • synonym: falsify - to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence cleverly or unscrupulously
  • antonym: reveal - to make known, opposite of concealing or manipulating
