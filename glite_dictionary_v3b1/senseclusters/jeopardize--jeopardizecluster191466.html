Examples

To jeopardize success
His careless remarks may jeopardize his chances of promotion.
Revealing his plans could jeopardize the entire operation.
She didn't want to jeopardize her friendship by telling the truth.
Cutting corners on safety can jeopardize the entire project.
He refused to jeopardize his reputation by taking part in the risky deal.
Pollution can jeopardize the health of local communities.

The new policy may jeopardise our chances of winning future contracts.

Synonyms

  • endanger
    3.7 5.98/m
    endanger means putting a person or thing into clear danger, while jeopardize emphasizes risking harm, loss, or failure.
  • imperil
    7.8 1.01/m
    imperil is a formal word that puts someone in danger, while jeopardize is more common for risking plans or goals.
  • risk
    3.0 39/m
    risk often means choosing to do something that may cause harm, while jeopardize means to put someone or something in danger.
  • threaten
    3.0 58/m
    threaten often means to warn of harm or be likely to cause it, while jeopardize means to place at risk of loss or failure.
  • menace
    4.6 1.68/m
    menace suggests a person or thing that causes fear or danger, while jeopardize focuses on putting something at risk.
