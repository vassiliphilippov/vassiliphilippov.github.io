eat up

verb
finish all the food
1.0 0.5/m
Pronunciation: / it  åp / (AmE), / iÀêt  åp / (BrE)
to eat all the food that is served or on your plate; to finish eating everything

Examples

To eat up your dinner
He quickly ate up his breakfast so he wouldn't be late for work.
Please eat up your vegetables before you leave the table.
The children ate up every last crumb of cake at the party.
Before we leave, make sure you eat up your breakfast.
Please eat up your dinner so we can have dessert.
The children quickly ate up all the cookies on the plate.

Synonyms

  • polish off
    3.8 0.4/m
    polish off is often casual for finishing all food or a task quickly and easily, while eat up focuses on clearing your plate.
  • devour
    3.9 3/m
    devour means to eat with great hunger and speed, while eat up simply means to finish all the food on your plate.
  • gobble up
    3.0 2/m
    gobble up describes eating quickly and noisily in a greedy way, while eat up focuses on finishing everything you are served.
