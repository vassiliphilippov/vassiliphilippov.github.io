pulp

noun
soft inner tissue of a tooth
4.6 5.7
Pronunciation: /pʌlp/ /; / /pʊlp/ (AmE), /pʌlp/ (BrE)
the soft tissue in the center of a tooth, containing nerves and blood vessels

Examples

Tooth pulp
A cavity that goes untreated can reach the pulp of the tooth and cause pain.
Dentists check the health of the pulp using a special test to see if the nerves are functioning.
Injuries can sometimes expose the dental pulp, increasing the risk of infection.
