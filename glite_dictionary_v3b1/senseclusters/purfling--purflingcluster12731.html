purfling

noun
instrument edge inlay
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrflɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːflɪŋ / (BrE)
a narrow decorative strip or inlay set into the edge of a stringed instrument, such as a violin or guitar, to protect and decorate it

Examples

Violin purfling
The violin maker selected ebony and maple for the purfling to create a striking contrast.
Careful installation of purfling helps prevent cracks along the edge of the instrument.
The intricate purfling on her cello was a mark of fine craftsmanship.
The luthier carefully inserted the purfling around the edge of the violin for both strength and beauty.
Traditional guitars often feature intricate purfling made from different colored woods.
Replacing damaged purfling can help restore the appearance of an old cello.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binding - a strip of material used to protect or decorate the edge of an instrument
  • synonym: inlay - a decorative material set into the surface of an object
