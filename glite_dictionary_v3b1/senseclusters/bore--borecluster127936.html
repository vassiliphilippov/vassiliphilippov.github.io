bore

verb
to make someone feel uninterested
2.5
Pronunciation: / bɔr / (AmE), / bɔː / (BrE)
to make someone feel uninterested or weary through dullness or monotony

Examples

To bore the class
His long speech began to bore everyone in the audience.
I hope I don't bore you with too many details about my trip.
She tends to bore her friends with endless stories about her cat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: entertain - to provide someone with amusement or enjoyment
  • antonym: interest - to engage the attention or curiosity
