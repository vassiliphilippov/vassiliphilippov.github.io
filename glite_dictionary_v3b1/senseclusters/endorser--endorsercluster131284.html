endorser

noun
person who signs to transfer rights
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɔrsər / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːsə / (BrE)
a person who signs a check or negotiable security to transfer ownership or legal rights to another party

Examples

Check endorser
Every endorser assumes certain legal liabilities after signing a negotiable instrument.
If the endorser fails to provide a valid signature, the transfer cannot be completed.
The endorser must sign the back of the check before depositing it at the bank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endorsee - the person to whom a negotiable instrument is transferred by endorsement
  • synonym: indorser - person who signs their name to transfer a legal document; alternate spelling
