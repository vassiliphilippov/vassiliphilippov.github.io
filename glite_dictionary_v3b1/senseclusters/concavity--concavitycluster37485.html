concavity

noun
inwardly curved shape or surface
Pronunciation: / kɑnˈkæv.ə.ti /; / kɑnˈkævəti / (AmE), / kɒŋˈkæv.ɪ.ti /; / kɒnˈkævəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being curved or bent inward; a surface or shape that curves inward

Examples

Tooth concavity
Dentists check each tooth for any concavity that could indicate a cavity or decay.
The concavity of the bowl allowed it to hold more soup than a flat plate.
The archway's concavity created a cool, shaded area beneath the stone structure.
If a lens has a concavity on one side, it will bend light differently than a convex lens.
The architect designed a dramatic concavity in the building’s facade to channel light.
There was a noticeable concavity in the center of the old wooden table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concave - curved inward like the inner surface of a sphere
  • antonym: convexity - the state of being curved outward
