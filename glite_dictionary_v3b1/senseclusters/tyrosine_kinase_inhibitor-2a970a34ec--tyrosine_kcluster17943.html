tyrosine kinase inhibitor

noun
drug blocking tyrosine kinases (cancer treatment)
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪrəˌsin ˈkaɪˌneɪs ɪnˈhɪbɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtɪərəsiːn ˈkaɪneɪz ɪnˈhɪbɪtə / (BrE)
a drug that blocks the action of enzymes known as tyrosine kinases, used especially to treat certain cancers such as chronic myeloid leukemia

Examples

A tyrosine kinase inhibitor drug
New advances in tyrosine kinase inhibitor therapy have improved outcomes for cancer patients.
Some patients develop resistance to a tyrosine kinase inhibitor over time.
The doctor prescribed a tyrosine kinase inhibitor to manage his leukemia.
