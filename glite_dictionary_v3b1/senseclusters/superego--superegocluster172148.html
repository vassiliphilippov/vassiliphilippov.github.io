superego

noun
the conscience part of the mind (psychoanalysis)
Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.pɚˈiː.ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈriː.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
The part of a person's mind in psychoanalytic theory that acts as a self-critical conscience, reflecting social standards learned from parents and teachers.

Examples

Strict superego
Her strict superego sometimes prevented her from enjoying simple pleasures.
In Freud's theory, the superego develops during early childhood as children internalize parental standards.
The superego often compels people to feel guilty when they act against their moral values.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ego - the part of the mind that mediates between the conscious and the unconscious
  • synonym: id - the part of the mind that contains basic instinctual drives
