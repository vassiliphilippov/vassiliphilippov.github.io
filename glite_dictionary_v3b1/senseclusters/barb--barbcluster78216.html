barb

noun
hurtful or critical remark
Pronunciation: / bɑrb / (AmE), / bɑːb / (BrE)
a remark intended to hurt or criticize someone in a sharp, direct way

Examples

A barb in speech
Even in his farewell speech, he slipped in a final barb directed at his rivals.
His barb at her expense left everyone in the room silent.
She couldn't resist adding a barb about his punctuality during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
  • synonym: dig - an unkind or mocking remark
  • synonym: jibe - an insulting or mocking remark
