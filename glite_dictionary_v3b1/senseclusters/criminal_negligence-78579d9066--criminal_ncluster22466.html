criminal negligence

noun
reckless legal disregard causing risk of harm
2.8 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nəl ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/ /; / /ˈkrɪm.nəl ˈneɡ.lə.dʒəns/ (AmE), /ˈkrɪm.ɪ.nəl ˈneɡ.lɪ.dʒəns/ /; / /ˈkrɪm.nəl ˈneɡ.lə.dʒəns/ (BrE)
(law) Reckless disregard for the safety or lives of others, where reasonable precautions are not taken, resulting in risk of injury or death and potentially leading to criminal liability.

Examples

Criminal negligence in law
Failing to provide adequate supervision at the daycare could be considered criminal negligence.
The court found the company guilty of criminal negligence for ignoring repeated safety complaints.
The driver was charged with criminal negligence after causing a serious accident while texting behind the wheel.
