Spenserian

noun
nine-line stanza
Pronunciation: / spɛnˈsɪəriən / (AmE), / spɛnˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
a stanza of nine lines, with eight lines in iambic pentameter and a final line in iambic hexameter, as used by Edmund Spenser

Examples

Spenserian stanza form
The poet chose to write the final section in a Spenserian to give it a classical feel.
Her anthology includes a beautiful Spenserian that pays tribute to traditional English verse.
Learning to compose a Spenserian can help students understand complex poetic forms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sonnet - a poem of fourteen lines with a specific rhyme scheme
  • synonym: stanza - a grouped set of lines in a poem
