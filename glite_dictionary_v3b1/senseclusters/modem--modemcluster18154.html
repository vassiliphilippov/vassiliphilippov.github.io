modem

noun
device for sending/receiving data over lines
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊ.dəm / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dəm / (BrE)
an electronic device that enables a computer to send and receive data over telephone lines or other communication channels

Examples

A modem device
Many computers used to connect to the internet with a dial-up modem before broadband became common.
She set up a new wireless modem to improve the Wi-Fi signal at home.
The internet connection was down because the modem malfunctioned during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: router - device directing data traffic on a network
