see for yourself

verb
check or experience personally
Pronunciation: / si fɔr jərˈsɛlf / (AmE), / siː fə jɔːˈsɛlf / (BrE)
to check or experience something personally in order to form your own opinion about it

Examples

To see for yourself the place
Before making a decision, it's wise to see for yourself how the job is done.
If you don't believe me, go and see for yourself.
She told me the museum was impressive, but I wanted to see for myself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: find out - to discover information, especially by asking a question or looking for it
  • antonym: hear secondhand - to learn about something from someone else rather than experiencing it directly
