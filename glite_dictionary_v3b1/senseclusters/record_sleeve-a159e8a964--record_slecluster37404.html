record sleeve

noun
cover for storing a vinyl record
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkərd sliːv / (AmE), / ˈrɛkɔːd sliːv / (BrE)
a paper or cardboard cover for storing and protecting a phonograph (vinyl) record

Examples

A record sleeve cover
He carefully slid the vinyl into its record sleeve to avoid getting dust on it.
The original record sleeve featured artwork by a famous graphic designer.
Without a proper record sleeve, old records can easily get scratched or damaged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: album cover - the outer jacket featuring artwork, title, and information about the record
