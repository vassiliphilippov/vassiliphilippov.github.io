doo-wop

noun
1950s vocal harmony music genre
8.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀê Àåw…ëp / (AmE), / ÀàduÀê Àåw…íp / (BrE)
A style of rhythm and blues music from the 1950s, typically featuring vocal group harmony, often a cappella, with the use of nonsense syllables in the backing vocals.

Examples

Classic doo-wop music
Her father loved collecting old doo-wop records and playing them on Sundays.
The group performed a classic doo-wop hit from the 1950s.
The influence of doo-wop can still be heard in modern pop music.
