vocal cord

noun
folds in the larynx that produce sound
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊkəl kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəl kɔːd / (BrE)
either of two folds of mucous membrane in the larynx that vibrate to produce the voice

Examples

Inflamed vocal cord
Inflammation of a vocal cord can result in hoarseness or loss of voice.
Teachers often strain their vocal cords by speaking for long periods every day.
The surgeon carefully examined her vocal cord for any signs of damage.
After the operation, the singer feared her vocal cords might never recover fully.
He lost his voice because of an infection in his vocal cords.
Long speeches can strain your vocal cords and make your voice hoarse.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: false vocal cord - folds of mucous membrane above the true vocal cords, not used directly in sound production
  • synonym: vocal fold - alternative term for vocal cord, the folds in the larynx that produce sound
