moot

adjective
of no practical or legal significance
Pronunciation: / mut / (AmE), / muːt / (BrE)
(of a point or question) having no relevance or legal significance, typically because the issue has already been resolved.

Examples

Moot in court
Because the contract had expired, any claims about it were considered moot in court.
The case was dismissed as the issue had become moot after new legislation was passed.
Whether he was present at the meeting is now a moot point, as the decision cannot be changed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrelevant - not related to the matter at hand
  • antonym: relevant - closely connected or appropriate to what is being done or considered
