prim

adjective
excessively formal or proper
8.0
Pronunciation: / prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
very formal, proper, or showing exaggerated good manners, sometimes in an old-fashioned way

Examples

A prim dress
Dressed in a prim suit, she carefully avoided all the muddy puddles.
His prim response to the joke made everyone else feel self-conscious.
She always sits upright and acts so prim at dinner parties.
Despite her young age, she always sat in a prim and proper manner at the table.
He gave her a prim nod as he passed, barely acknowledging her presence.
The teacher’s prim attitude made her seem distant and unapproachable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: informal - having a relaxed and friendly style or manner
  • synonym: proper - behaving in a socially correct way
  • synonym: straitlaced - having very strict morals
