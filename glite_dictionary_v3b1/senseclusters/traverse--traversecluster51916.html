traverse

verb
to travel across or through
5.6 2.14/m
Pronunciation: / tr…ôÀàv…ùs /; / tr…ôÀàv…úrs / (AmE), / tr…ôÀàv…úÀês / (BrE)
to travel across, move through, or extend across an area, place, or period of time

Examples

To traverse a desert
The hikers planned to traverse the mountain range in a single week.
They traversed the desert on camels to reach the oasis.
We must traverse the dense forest before reaching the river.
A network of underground pipes traverses the city, delivering water to every neighborhood.
The exhibit traverses the evolution of art from the Renaissance to the present day.
The railway lines traverse the entire continent from east to west.

Synonyms

  • transit
    4.2 0.30/m
    transit is a more formal word often used for moving people or goods through places, while traverse means to travel across or through an area.
  • move through
    2.1 7.02/m
    move through describes physically passing inside or across something during a journey, while traverse often implies going across or extending through an area.
  • cross
    1.0 83/m
    cross means going from one side to the other, often quickly, while traverse suggests moving across or extending through an area more broadly.
  • pass through
    2.7 15/m
    pass through means to travel across a place without staying long, while traverse means to travel across or extend through an area more generally.
  • pass
    1.0 108/m
    pass means to move past or beyond something in many ways, while traverse focuses on traveling across or extending through an area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cross - to go from one side to the other
  • synonym: pass - to move past or across something
  • synonym: span - to extend across a period or area
