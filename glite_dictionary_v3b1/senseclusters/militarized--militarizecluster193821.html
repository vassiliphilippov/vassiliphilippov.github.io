militarized

adjective
equipped with military arms
Pronunciation: / mɪˈlɪtəˌraɪzd /; / ˈmɪlɪtəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪtəraɪzd /; / ˈmɪlɪtəraɪzd / (BrE)
Equipped with military arms or organized for military purposes.

Examples

A militarized police force
Many local police departments have become increasingly militarized in recent years, with new weapons and vehicles.
The government has created a militarized zone along the border to increase security.
The region became militarized after the outbreak of conflict, restricting civilian access.
After the coup, the government established a militarised zone around the capital.
Many protesters opposed the militarised response by the police to peaceful demonstrations.
The border has become increasingly militarised in recent years due to security concerns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demilitarised - having all military forces or installations removed
  • antonym: demilitarized - removed from military control or stripped of military arms
