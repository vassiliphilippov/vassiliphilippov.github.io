chamois

noun
soft leather used for cleaning and polishing
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæmi / (AmE), / ˈʃæmi / (BrE)
a soft, pliable leather made originally from the skin of the chamois antelope, but now also made from sheepskin, used especially for cleaning and polishing

Examples

A soft chamois cloth
He used a chamois to dry off the car after washing it.
She polished her shoes with a piece of chamois for a brilliant shine.
The soft texture of chamois leather makes it ideal for cleaning delicate surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suede - a type of soft leather with a napped finish
