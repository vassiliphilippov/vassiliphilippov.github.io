ACTH

noun
hormone stimulating adrenal cortex
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ ˌsi ː ˌti ː ˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌsiː ˌtiː ˈeɪtʃ / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the adrenal cortex to secrete corticosteroids

Examples

ACTH hormone
High levels of ACTH can indicate a problem with the adrenal glands or pituitary gland.
Production of ACTH increases during times of physical or emotional stress.
The doctor ordered an ACTH stimulation test to assess adrenal function.
