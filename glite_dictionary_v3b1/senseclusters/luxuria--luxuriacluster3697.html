luxuria

noun
excessive sexual desire; deadly sin
9.9
Pronunciation: / lʌgˈʒʊəriə / (AmE), / lʌkˈsjʊəriə / (BrE)
excessive or self-indulgent sexual desire, especially as personified as one of the seven deadly sins in Christian tradition

Examples

The sin luxuria
Early Christian writers condemned luxuria as a major moral failing.
In medieval art, luxuria was often depicted as a woman surrounded by symbols of temptation.
Treatises on the seven deadly sins discuss luxuria at length alongside gluttony and avarice.
