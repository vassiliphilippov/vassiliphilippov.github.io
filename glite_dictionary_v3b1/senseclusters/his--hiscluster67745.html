his

pronoun
male possessive pronoun
Pronunciation: / hɪz / (AmE), / hɪz / (BrE)
used to show that something belongs to or is connected with a male person or animal previously mentioned

Examples

His blue backpack
That blue backpack is his, not yours.
The dog wagged its tail when it saw his favorite toy.
After the meeting, everyone took their coats except for his.
He left his keys on the kitchen table before going to work.
The dog wagged his tail when he saw his owner return.
Each student must bring his own notebook to class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: her - possessive pronoun for a female person or animal
  • antonym: hers - possessive pronoun for something belonging to a female
  • antonym: mine - possessive pronoun for something belonging to the speaker
  • antonym: their - possessive pronoun for more than one person or for gender-neutral reference
  • antonym: theirs - possessive pronoun for something belonging to multiple people
