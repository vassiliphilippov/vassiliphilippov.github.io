moderate

verb
to make less intense; restrain
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdərˌeɪt /; / ˈmɑd.əˌreɪt /; / ˈmɑdərˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəreɪt /; / ˈmɒdərət, ˈmɒdəreɪt /; / ˈmɒd.əˌreɪt / (BrE)
to make something less extreme, intense, or severe; to control or restrain

Examples

To moderate your speed
Doctors advise patients to moderate their salt intake for better health.
She tried to moderate her criticism so as not to offend anyone.
You should moderate your speed when driving in the rain.
He moderated his criticism to avoid offending his colleagues.
She tried to moderate the discussion by encouraging everyone to speak respectfully.
The government took action to moderate the sudden rise in fuel prices.
Drivers are advised to moderate their speed in residential areas.
His tone moderated as he realized he might have been too harsh.
She tried to moderate her criticism during the meeting.
