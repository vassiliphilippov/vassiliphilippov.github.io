Trinitarian

noun
believer in the Christian Trinity
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌtrɪn.ɪˈtɛr.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌtrɪn.ɪˈteə.ri.ən / (BrE)
a person who believes in the doctrine of the Trinity, especially in Christianity

Examples

A devoted Trinitarian
As a devoted Trinitarian, she attended church services every Sunday.
Historically, some Christian sects rejected Trinitarian doctrine in favor of other interpretations.
The theological debate centered on the beliefs of Trinitarians and Unitarians.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Unitarian - a person who denies the doctrine of the Trinity
