color bar

noun
barrier excluding people based on race
7.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk ål…ôr b…ër / (AmE), / Ààk ål…ô b…ëÀê / (BrE)
a social or legal system that prevents people of different races, especially black people and white people, from mixing, particularly by barring black people from certain rights or activities

Examples

The color bar system
After years of activism, the color bar was finally abolished in public institutions.
He campaigned tirelessly against the color bar in sports clubs.
The color bar in schools denied many children access to quality education.
After decades of struggle, the colour bar in employment was finally dismantled.
Many sports clubs maintained a colour bar until the mid-twentieth century.
The government strictly enforced the colour bar in schools and public places.

Synonyms

  • color barrier
    5.9 0.10/m
    a color barrier is a social or legal obstacle that stops people joining activities, while a color bar names that same system.
  • color line
    7.9 0.20/m
    a color line marks the division between groups in society, while a color bar is the wider system that enforces that division.
  • racial segregation
    6.5 0.65/m
    racial segregation separates people by race, often by law in schools or towns, while a color bar stresses banning Black people from rights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color bar - US spelling of 'colour bar'
  • antonym: integration - the process of ending racial segregation
  • synonym: segregation - the enforced separation of different racial groups
