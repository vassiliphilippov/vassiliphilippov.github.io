seize up

verb
stop functioning or moving properly
6.5 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / siÀêz Àà åp / (AmE), / siÀêz Àà åp / (BrE)
(of a machine, part of the body, etc.) to stop functioning or moving properly, usually because of lack of lubrication, injury, or excessive use.

Examples

Legs seize up
After sitting still for hours on the plane, my knees began to seize up.
If you don't change the oil, your car's engine could seize up.
The lock may seize up if it's exposed to cold and moisture.

Synonyms

  • gum up
    7.2 0.29/m
    gum up makes parts stick and clog a machine, while seize up describes internal parts locking from lack of lubrication or damage.
  • jam up
    4.7 0.25/m
    jam up blocks or crowds moving parts or traffic, while seize up means parts lock and stop moving due to lack of lubrication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze - to become or make immobile
  • synonym: jam - become stuck and unable to move
