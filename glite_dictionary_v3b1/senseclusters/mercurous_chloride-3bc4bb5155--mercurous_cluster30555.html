mercurous chloride

noun
a white powdery chemical used medicinally
9.4
Pronunciation: / mərˈkjʊrəs ˈklaɪəˌraɪd / (AmE), / məˈkjʊə.rəs ˈklɔː.raɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, tasteless powder formerly used as a cathartic in medicine; chemically known as calomel, with the formula Hg2Cl2.

Examples

Mercurous chloride powder
Due to toxicity concerns, doctors no longer use mercurous chloride in modern medicine.
The chemist identified the white substance as mercurous chloride by its reactions.
Until the twentieth century, mercurous chloride was commonly prescribed as a purgative.
