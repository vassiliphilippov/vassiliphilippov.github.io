centerboard

noun
retractable fin to prevent sailboat drift

A retractable, flat board or fin installed along the midline of a sailboat's hull, lowered into the water to prevent the boat from drifting sideways.

Examples

The centerboard lowered
The small sailboat's centerboard was lowered to help it sail upwind.
Without a centerboard, the vessel would drift sideways in strong winds.
You should always retract the centerboard before transporting the boat on a trailer.
After hitting shallow water, they quickly raised the centreboard to avoid damage.
Many small dinghies are designed with a retractable centreboard for greater versatility.
The centreboard makes it easier to sail upwind by preventing the boat from drifting sideways.

Synonyms

  • sliding keel
    8.1 0.00/m
    a sliding keel is a retractable fin under the hull that improves stability and reduces sideways drift, while a centerboard is flatter and unweighted.
  • drop keel
    8.3 0.00/m
    a drop keel is a retractable, often heavier keel that gives strong resistance to leeward drift, while a centerboard is a lighter, flat board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keel - the structural backbone or bottom ridge of a boat
  • synonym: leeboard - a board fastened to the side of a sailing vessel to prevent drifting
