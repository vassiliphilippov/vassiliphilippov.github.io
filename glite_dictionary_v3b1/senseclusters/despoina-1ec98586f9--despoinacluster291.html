Despoina

proper noun
Greek myth: daughter of Zeus and Demeter, queen of the underworld
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɛsˈpɔɪnə / (AmE), / dɛsˈpɔɪnə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the daughter of Zeus and Demeter, who became queen of the underworld; identified with the Roman Proserpina.

Examples

Despoina in mythology
According to legend, Despoina was taken to the underworld by Pluto.
Art from the classical period often depicts Despoina alongside her mother, Demeter.
Some ancient Greeks held special festivals in honor of Despoina.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Persephone - daughter of Zeus and Demeter in Greek mythology, also queen of the underworld
  • synonym: Proserpina - Roman mythology equivalent of Persephone/Despoina
