tolerable

adjective
able to be endured or accepted
3.7 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːlɚəbl̩ /; / ˈtɑːl.ɚ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒlərəbl̩ /; / ˈtɒl.ər.ə.bəl / (BrE)
able to be accepted or endured; not especially good but not bad enough to be rejected

Examples

A tolerable situation
Despite the noise in the apartment, the living conditions were tolerable.
She found the pain after surgery surprisingly tolerable.
The weather was hot, but still tolerable for a long walk.
Although the heat was high, the shade made it tolerable for an afternoon picnic.
Her mood was only tolerable after the long meeting, not quite cheerful but not angry either.
The food at the airport café was just tolerable, but I was too hungry to complain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acceptable - satisfactory; good enough to be allowed
  • synonym: bearable - able to be endured; not unendurably painful or difficult
  • antonym: intolerable - too bad, harsh, or severe to be endured
  • antonym: unbearable - too painful or difficult to endure
