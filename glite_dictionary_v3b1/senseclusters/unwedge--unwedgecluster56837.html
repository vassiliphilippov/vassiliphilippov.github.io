unwedge

verb
to free by removing wedge
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɛdʒ / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɛdʒ / (BrE)
to release or free something by removing a wedge or obstruction, making it unstuck or able to move again

Examples

To unwedge a stuck door
She carefully unwedged the stuck window so it could be opened again.
After several attempts, he finally unwedged the heavy box from behind the door.
The mechanic unwedged the part that was blocking the engine's movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dislodge - to remove something from its fixed position
  • antonym: jam - to become stuck or unable to move
