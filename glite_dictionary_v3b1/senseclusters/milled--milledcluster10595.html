milled

adjective
having husk/outer layers removed (of grains)
3.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / mÉªld / (AmE), / mÉªld / (BrE)
(of grains, especially rice) having had the husk or outer layers removed by grinding or processing.

Examples

Milled rice grains
After harvesting, the wheat is milled to remove the tough outer layers.
Most of the rice sold in supermarkets is milled to improve its cooking properties.
This recipe calls for milled rice instead of wholegrain rice for a softer texture.

Synonyms

  • polished rice
    2.8 0.00/m
    polished rice is rice with its husk and brown layers removed by polishing, while milled means grain outer layers removed by processing.
  • pearled
    5.2 0.00/m
    pearled describes grains polished until the hull is gone, while milled refers to grains, especially rice, whose outer layers are removed by grinding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refined - having impurities or unwanted elements removed by processing
  • antonym: wholegrain - containing all of the original kernel, including bran, germ, and endosperm
