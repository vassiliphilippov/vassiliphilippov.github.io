meronym

noun
word for a part of a whole
9.2 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈme.rə.nɪm/ (AmE), /ˈme.rə.nɪm/ (BrE)
a word denoting a part of something to which it refers, especially in relation to linguistic meaning

Examples

A meronym of a word
'Leaf' functions as a meronym of 'tree' in the sentence.
'Wheel' is a meronym of 'car' because a wheel is a part of a car.
Linguists often discuss how meronym relations help organize vocabulary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holonym - a word that names the whole of which a meronym is a part
