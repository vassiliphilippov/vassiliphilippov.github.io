tenderization

noun
process of making meat tender
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛndɚaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌtɛn.dɚ.ɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛndəraɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌtɛn.dər.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making meat more tender, typically by pounding, marinating, or other methods

Examples

Meat tenderization
Marinating is a common method of tenderization used in many cuisines.
Proper tenderization ensures the meat remains juicy and flavorful after cooking.
The chef recommended tenderization to improve the quality of the steak.
Modern cooking often relies on chemical tenderisation to improve tough cuts of meat.
Prolonged marinating is a common method of tenderisation in many cuisines.
The tenderisation of the steak made it much easier to chew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tenderization - the act of making meat tender by pounding or marinating it (American spelling)
  • synonym: tenderize - make meat or food softer and easier to eat
