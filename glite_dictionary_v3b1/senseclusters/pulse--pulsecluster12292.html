pulse

verb
expand and contract rhythmically; beat
5.0
Pronunciation: / pʌls / (AmE), / pʌls / (BrE)
to move, expand, or contract in a regular, rhythmic way, like a heartbeat or a wave

Examples

To pulse with energy
Excitement seemed to pulse through the crowd as the singer walked onto the stage.
He rested his head on her wrist and could feel the artery pulse beneath the skin.
Lights pulse softly in the distance, giving the club a mesmerizing atmosphere.
A gentle current pulsed through the wires, powering the device.
Energy seemed to pulse through the crowd as the concert began.
The strobe lights pulsed in time with the music, creating a lively atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quiver - to shake or move with a slight trembling motion
  • synonym: throb - to beat with a strong, regular rhythm
