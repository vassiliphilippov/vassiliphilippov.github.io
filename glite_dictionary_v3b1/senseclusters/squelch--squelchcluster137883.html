squelch

noun
an electronic noise-suppressing circuit
9.9 7.3
Pronunciation: /skweltʃ/ (AmE), /skweltʃ/ (BrE)
an electronic circuit in a receiver that automatically suppresses the output when the signal is weaker than background noise

Examples

Radio squelch control
Adjust the squelch control to prevent background noise from coming through the speakers.
Most modern walkie-talkies include an automatic squelch circuit to improve audio clarity.
The radio's squelch feature eliminated the annoying static between transmissions.
