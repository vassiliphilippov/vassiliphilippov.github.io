paper electrophoresis

noun
electrophoresis using filter paper as medium
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪpər ɪˌlɛktroʊfəˈriːsɪs / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ɪˌlɛktrəʊfəˈriːsɪs / (BrE)
a method of electrophoresis in which filter paper is used as the supporting medium to separate substances, typically biomolecules, based on their movement in an electric field

Examples

Paper electrophoresis method
Advances in technology have largely replaced paper electrophoresis with more modern techniques, but it is still valuable for teaching purposes.
Due to its simplicity and cost-effectiveness, paper electrophoresis was widely used in early biochemical research.
The laboratory technician performed paper electrophoresis to analyze the protein samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electrophoresis - the movement of charged particles through a medium under an electric field
