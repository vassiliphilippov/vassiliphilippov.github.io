bronchoconstrictor

adjective
causing airway narrowing
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɑːŋkoʊkənˈstrɪktər / (AmE), / ˌbrɒŋkəʊkənˈstrɪktə / (BrE)
causing the narrowing of the bronchial tubes in the lungs, making breathing more difficult

Examples

Bronchoconstrictor effect
Some medications have a bronchoconstrictor effect and should be avoided by people with asthma.
Exposure to cold air can act as a bronchoconstrictor for sensitive individuals.
Doctors monitor patients for any bronchoconstrictor reactions during allergy testing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bronchodilator - causing the widening of the bronchial tubes
