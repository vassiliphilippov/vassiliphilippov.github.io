disputatious

adjective
inclined to argue or dispute
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspjuˈteɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌdɪspjʊˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
tending to argue or disagree; inclined to dispute or engage in controversy

Examples

A disputatious person
His disputatious nature often led to heated debates during meetings.
The disputatious politician was known for challenging almost every policy proposal.
Their disputatious discussions sometimes frustrated those seeking a quick decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: argumentative - given to expressing or involving argument or controversy
  • synonym: contentious - causing or likely to cause an argument; controversial
  • antonym: peaceable - inclined to avoid argument or conflict
