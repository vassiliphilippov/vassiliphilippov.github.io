miter box

noun
tool for guiding saws in angled cuts
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtɚ bɑks /; / ˈmaɪtər bɑks / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə bɒks / (BrE)
A hand tool with slots that guide a saw to make precise crosscuts or miter joints, especially in woodwork.

Examples

A miter box tool
A miter box makes it much easier to achieve precise cuts for picture frames.
Before buying a miter box, consider the size and type of projects you plan to do.
He held the piece of wood firmly in the miter box while sawing it at a 45-degree angle.
A mitre box helps carpenters achieve precise angles when making picture frames.
He used a mitre box to make sure each piece was cut at exactly 45 degrees.
Without a mitre box, it's hard to get clean, accurate miter joints by hand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miter joint - a joint made by beveling each of two parts to be joined, usually at a 45° angle, to form a corner
