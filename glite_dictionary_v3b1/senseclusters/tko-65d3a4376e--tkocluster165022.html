TKO

noun
technical knockout in boxing
Pronunciation: / tiː keɪ oʊ / (AmE), / tiː keɪ əʊ / (BrE)
an act of stopping a boxing match by the referee when a fighter is judged unable to continue safely; technical knockout

Examples

Win by TKO
She won her previous match by TKO in just two minutes.
The fight ended in the fifth round with a TKO after the boxer couldn't defend himself.
The referee decided on a TKO to protect the injured fighter.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: knockout - a winning blow in boxing in which the opponent cannot get up
