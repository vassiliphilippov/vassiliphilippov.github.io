parting of the ways

noun
point where people or groups separate
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrtɪŋ əv ðə weɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪŋ əv ðə weɪz / (BrE)
a moment or point at which people, organizations, or ideas separate or go in different directions, often as a result of differing opinions or goals

Examples

A parting of the ways moment
After much discussion, their company reached a parting of the ways and split into two separate businesses.
College provided a natural parting of the ways for many high school friends who went to different universities.
Their different visions for the future meant a parting of the ways was inevitable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations
  • synonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart
