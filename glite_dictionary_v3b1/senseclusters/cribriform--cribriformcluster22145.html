cribriform

adjective
perforated like a sieve
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪbrəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪbrɪfɔːm / (BrE)
having a structure that is perforated with many small holes, like a sieve

Examples

Cribriform plate
The cribriform plate allows nerves from the nose to pass into the brain.
Certain glands have a cribriform appearance under the microscope due to their many small holes.
The surgeon noted the cribriform structure of the tissue during the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perforated - having a surface full of holes
  • antonym: solid - having no holes or openings
