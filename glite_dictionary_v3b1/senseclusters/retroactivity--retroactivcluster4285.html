retroactivity

noun
applying to the past
8.9
Pronunciation: /re.troʊ.æk.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /riː.troʊ.æk.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /re.trəʊ.æk.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /riː.trəʊ.æk.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a law, rule, or decision applying to events or actions that happened in the past

Examples

Concerns about retroactivity often arise when governments change important regulations.
