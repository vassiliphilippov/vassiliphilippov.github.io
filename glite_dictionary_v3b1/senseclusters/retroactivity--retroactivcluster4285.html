retroactivity

noun
applying to the past
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəʊækˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality of a law, rule, or decision applying to events or actions that happened in the past

Examples

The retroactivity of law
The retroactivity of the new tax law caused confusion among business owners.
Lawyers debated whether the court's decision should have retroactivity or only apply going forward.
Concerns about retroactivity often arise when governments change important regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prospectivity - the quality of applying only to future events
  • synonym: retrospectivity - the state of looking back or applying to the past
