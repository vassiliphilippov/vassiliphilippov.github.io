alienator

noun
one who causes alienation
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.li.əˌneɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.li.ə.neɪ.tə / (BrE)
a person who causes others to become unfriendly, distant, or hostile

Examples

An alienator of friends
As an alienator, he frequently sowed discord within his circle of friends.
Don't let a workplace alienator undermine the team's spirit with negativity.
Her role as the family's alienator became clear when relatives stopped speaking to each other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alienate - to cause someone to feel isolated or estranged
