MEDLINE

noun
computerized medical bibliographic database
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmed.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈmed.laɪn/ (BrE)
a computerized medical bibliographic database produced by the United States National Library of Medicine, providing access to references and abstracts from biomedical literature.

Examples

MEDLINE medical database
Access to MEDLINE has become essential for healthcare professionals seeking reliable information.
Most reputable journals in medicine are indexed in MEDLINE, ensuring wide visibility for new research.
Researchers often use MEDLINE to find recent studies on various medical treatments.
