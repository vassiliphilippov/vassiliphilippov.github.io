arioso

noun
melodic passage between recitative and aria (music)
5.4 9.1
Pronunciation: /ə.riː.ˈoʊ.soʊ/ /; / /æ.riː.ˈoʊ.soʊ/ (AmE), /ə.riː.ˈəʊ.səʊ/ /; / /æ.riː.ˈəʊ.səʊ/ (BrE)
(in music) a short vocal passage that is more melodic than a recitative but not as formally structured as an aria.

Examples

A arioso passage
In the opera, the arioso bridged the transition from recitative to aria.
Mozart often wrote an arioso to highlight a character's inner feelings.
The soprano performed the arioso with great emotional depth and subtlety.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: aria - a long, elaborate song for solo voice in an opera
  • degree lower: recitative - a style of delivery in which a singer adopts rhythms of ordinary speech
