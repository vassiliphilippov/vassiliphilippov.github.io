dextrose

noun
a form of glucose found in honey and fruits
6.4 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdek.stroʊs/ (AmE), /ˈdek.strəʊz/ (BrE)
a form of glucose (the D-isomer or D-glucose) found especially in honey and sweet fruits, used as a sweetener and in medicine.

Examples

Natural dextrose sugar
Honey contains high levels of natural dextrose, making it a quick source of energy.
Many commercial candies use dextrose as a sweetener instead of regular sugar.
The patient’s blood sugar was stabilized using an intravenous dextrose solution.
