inlet manifold

noun
pipe distributing fuel and air in an engine
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnlɛt ˈmænɪˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈɪnlət ˈmænɪfəʊld / (BrE)
A pipe or chamber in an internal combustion engine that distributes the air-fuel mixture or air to the intake ports of the cylinders.

Examples

The inlet manifold pipe
A faulty inlet manifold can result in misfires and reduced fuel efficiency.
Cleaning the inlet manifold can improve airflow and boost engine performance.
The mechanic discovered a crack in the inlet manifold, which was causing the engine to run poorly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exhaust manifold - pipe collecting exhaust gases from several cylinders
