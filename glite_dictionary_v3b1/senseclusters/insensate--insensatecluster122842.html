insensate

adjective
without feeling or compassion
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɛnˌseɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛn.seɪt / (BrE)
lacking physical or emotional feeling, awareness, or compassion; unfeeling or without sensation

Examples

An insensate rage
Driven by insensate rage, he destroyed everything in his path.
Such insensate cruelty shocked the whole community.
The dictator's insensate regime showed no mercy to its people.
He viewed the destruction with an insensate detachment that unsettled everyone around him.
The ancient statue stood insensate in the corner, unseeing and unmoving.
Unlike animals, a rock is completely insensate and incapable of any sensation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: callous - emotionally insensitive or unfeeling
  • antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others
  • synonym: insentient - not capable of perception or feeling
