hemimetabolous

adjective
incomplete metamorphosis in insects
9.9 9.7
Pronunciation: /he.mɪ.mə.ˈtæb.ə.ləs/ (AmE), /he.mɪ.mə.ˈtæb.ə.ləs/ (BrE)
(of an insect) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis, in which the young does not resemble the adult and the transformation is gradual.

Examples

Hemimetabolous insect development
Dragonflies are hemimetabolous insects, with young that live in water and differ greatly from adults.
Grasshoppers are classic examples of hemimetabolous development.
Unlike butterflies, which have a pupal stage, hemimetabolous insects do not undergo a drastic transformation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holometabolous - insects undergoing complete metamorphosis
