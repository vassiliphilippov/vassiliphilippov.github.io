prevailing westerly

noun
westward wind in temperate zones
2.6
Pronunciation: / prɪˈveɪlɪŋ ˈwɛstərli / (AmE), / prɪˈveɪlɪŋ ˈwɛstəli / (BrE)
A wind that blows predominantly from the west in the temperate regions of the Earth.

Examples

The prevailing westerly wind
Climate patterns in the region are strongly influenced by the prevailing westerly.
During the voyage, the ship relied on the prevailing westerly to make steady progress across the Atlantic.
The prevailing westerly often brings mild, wet weather to northern Europe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trade wind - A wind blowing steadily toward the equator, from the northeast in the Northern Hemisphere or the southeast in the Southern Hemisphere
  • synonym: westerly - A wind coming from the west
