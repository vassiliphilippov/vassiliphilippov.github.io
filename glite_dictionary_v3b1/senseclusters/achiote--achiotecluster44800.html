achiote

noun
natural red dye
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːtʃiˈoʊteɪ / (AmE), / ˌæʃiˈəʊti / (BrE)
a reddish spice or dye made from the seeds of the achiote plant, used to color and flavor food and sometimes textiles or cosmetics

Examples

Achiote spice powder

Traditional fabrics are often dyed with achiote to achieve vibrant red and orange hues.

Traditional Mexican dishes often use achiote to give food a rich red hue.
You can find achiote seeds in specialty stores or Latin American markets.
