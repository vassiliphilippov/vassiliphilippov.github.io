shirk

verb
avoid or neglect a duty or responsibility
6.3 1.07/m
Pronunciation: /  É…ùÀêk /; /  É…ùrk / (AmE), /  É…úÀêk / (BrE)
to avoid or neglect a duty or responsibility, especially because it is difficult or unpleasant

Examples

To shirk duties
He was known to shirk his household chores whenever possible.
She tried to shirk her homework by pretending to be ill.
Workers who shirk their responsibilities may face disciplinary action.
He was accused of trying to shirk his military service.
Some employees tend to shirk their responsibilities when no one is watching.
You can't just shirk your chores and expect someone else to do them.
