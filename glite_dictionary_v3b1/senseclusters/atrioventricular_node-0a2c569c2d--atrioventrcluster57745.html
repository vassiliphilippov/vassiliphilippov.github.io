atrioventricular node

noun
part of the heart's electrical conduction system
5.8 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtrioʊvɛnˈtrɪkjələr noʊd / (AmE), / ˌeɪtrɪəʊvɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə nəʊd / (BrE)
a specialized mass of tissue in the wall of the right atrium of the heart that receives electrical impulses from the sinoatrial node and transmits them to the ventricles.

Examples

The atrioventricular node tissue
Damage to the atrioventricular node can cause serious heart rhythm disorders.
Doctors may implant a pacemaker if the atrioventricular node is not functioning properly.
The atrioventricular node helps coordinate the timing of the heart's contractions.
