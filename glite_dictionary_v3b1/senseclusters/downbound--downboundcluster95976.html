downbound

adjective
moving downward or downstream
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, vessel, or route) moving or directed in a direction conventionally considered as 'down,' such as downstream or towards a destination of lower elevation or latitude.

Examples

All downbound trains
All downbound trains are scheduled to arrive before noon.
Many delays occurred because the bridge was raised to let a downbound tugboat through.
The downbound freighter passed us early in the morning, heading towards the river's mouth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upbound - moving in a direction conventionally considered as 'up' (e.g., upstream, inland)
