plasmatic

adjective
shapeless or fluid
Pronunciation: / plæzˈmætɪk / (AmE), / plæzˈmætɪk / (BrE)
having a shapeless, fluid, or easily changeable form; capable of being shaped or transformed.

Examples

Plasmatic movements
The dancer's movements were so plasmatic that she seemed to flow across the stage.
His ideas were plasmatic, constantly shifting and adapting to new information.
The clouds took on a plasmatic appearance as the wind shaped them into new forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amorphous - having no definite shape or form
  • antonym: rigid - not able to be changed or adapted; stiff
