scaffold

noun
porous template for tissue growth

(in biomedical engineering) a three-dimensional porous material, often biodegradable, used as a temporary template to support cell attachment and guide tissue growth and regeneration.

Examples

Tissue scaffold
The surgeon implanted a biodegradable scaffold to guide bone regeneration over several months.
Researchers seeded stem cells onto a porous scaffold and observed tissue growth in the lab.
The team designed a polymer scaffold that slowly degrades while new bone forms inside it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biomaterial - a material designed to interact with biological systems, often used to support or replace tissue
  • synonym: extracellular matrix - the natural network of molecules around cells that provides structural support and biochemical signals, analogous to a scaffold
