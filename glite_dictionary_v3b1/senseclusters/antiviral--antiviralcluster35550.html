antiviral

noun
drug that inhibits viruses
5.6 3.2
Pronunciation: /æn.tiː.ˈvaɪ.rəl/ /; / /æn.tɪ.ˈvaɪ.rəl/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈvaɪ.rəl/ (BrE)
A drug or substance that inhibits the growth or replication of viruses, often used to treat viral infections.

Examples

An antiviral drug
Patients with shingles may benefit from taking an antiviral early in the course of the disease.
Research is ongoing to develop new antiviral medications for emerging viruses.
The doctor prescribed an antiviral to help combat the flu virus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antifungal - a drug used to treat fungal infections
