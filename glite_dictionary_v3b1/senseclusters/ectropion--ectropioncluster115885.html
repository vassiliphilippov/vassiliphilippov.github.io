ectropion

noun
cervical cell condition
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈtroʊpiən / (AmE), / ɛkˈtrəʊpɪən / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the inner lining of the cervix is present on the outer surface, often leading to discharge or bleeding

Examples

Cervical ectropion
The gynecologist diagnosed her with ectropion after noticing abnormal cervical cells during the exam.
Light bleeding after intercourse can sometimes be a symptom of ectropion in women.
Treatment for ectropion is usually only needed if it causes discomfort or persistent symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endocervicitis - inflammation of the inner lining of the cervix
  • synonym: erosion - wearing away of tissue, especially at a body surface
