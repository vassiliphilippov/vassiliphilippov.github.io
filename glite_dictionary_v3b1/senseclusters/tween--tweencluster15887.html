tween

noun
intermediate animation frame
Pronunciation: / twiːn / (AmE), / twiːn / (BrE)
a frame or image created between two key frames in animation or computer graphics to produce smooth motion

Examples

Animation tween
The software automatically generates each tween to make the animation look smooth and natural.
She spent hours perfecting every tween to ensure the character's movement was fluid.
By adjusting the timing of each tween, the animator achieved a more realistic walking cycle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: keyframe - a main frame that defines the start or end point of an animation sequence
