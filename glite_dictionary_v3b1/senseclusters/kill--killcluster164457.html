kill

verb
make a device stop operating
2.8 27/m
Pronunciation: / kÉªl / (AmE), / kÉªl / (BrE)
to bring something to an end, stop it from continuing, or destroy its effect (such as a plan, machine, process, or idea)

Examples

To kill the engine
After the boat docked, the captain killed the engine.
I had to kill the power before repairing the appliance.
Please kill the lights when you leave the room.
She worked hard to kill the rumors spreading about her team.
Strict new rules may kill innovation in the industry.
The government is planning new policies to kill corruption for good.
Adding too much sugar can kill the natural flavor of the fruit.
If you serve strong coffee with dessert, it might kill the taste of the cake.
Loud noises often kill the atmosphere in a romantic restaurant.

Synonyms

  • terminate
    4.6 8.77/m
    terminate is formal and used for contracts or processes, while kill is more general and used in many settings.
  • stop
    1.0 539/m
    stop simply means cause something to end or not move, while kill suggests destroying its effect or stopping permanently.
  • put a stop to
    3.0 2.17/m
    put a stop to means cause something to end, often by choice, while kill implies complete destruction or removal.
  • eliminate
    3.4 39/m
    eliminate means get rid of something completely, often as a choice, while kill focuses on ending its activity or effect.
  • cease
    3.6 23/m
    cease means to stop or come to an end, often without an agent, while kill implies actively ending or destroying it.
  • eradicate
    6.6 5.77/m
    eradicate means remove something harmful completely, often by long effort, while kill can mean stop or end more generally.
  • stamp out
    4.2 0.85/m
    stamp out means forcefully end a problem across society, while kill can mean stop a single plan or machine.
  • snuff out
    7.7 0.40/m
    snuff out means extinguish or kill suddenly and finally, often with a violent image, while kill is more neutral.
  • torpedo
    5.1 0.50/m
    torpedo means ruin or sabotage something suddenly to stop it, while kill is a more general word for ending or destroying.
  • defeat
    2.7 10/m
    defeat means prevent a proposal's success, often by vote or argument, while kill can end many things by different actions.
  • shoot down
    3.8 1.35/m
    shoot down means reject an idea sharply or dismiss it quickly, while kill can also stop plans but in many ways.
