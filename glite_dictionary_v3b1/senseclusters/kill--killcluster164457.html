kill

verb
make a device stop operating
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /kɪl/ (AmE), /kɪl/ (BrE)
to bring something to an end, stop it from continuing, or destroy its effect (such as a plan, machine, process, or idea)

Examples

To kill the engine
After the boat docked, the captain killed the engine.
I had to kill the power before repairing the appliance.
Please kill the lights when you leave the room.
She worked hard to kill the rumors spreading about her team.
Strict new rules may kill innovation in the industry.
The government is planning new policies to kill corruption for good.
Adding too much sugar can kill the natural flavor of the fruit.
If you serve strong coffee with dessert, it might kill the taste of the cake.
Loud noises often kill the atmosphere in a romantic restaurant.
