gerrymander

verb
manipulate voting district boundaries
9.9 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛriˌmændɚ /; / ˈdʒɛriˌmændər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛriˌmændə /; / ˈdʒɛrɪˌmændə / (BrE)
to change the boundaries of voting districts in an unfair way to help one political party or group

Examples

To gerrymander voting districts
Activists argued that the new voting districts were gerrymandered to diminish minority representation.
It is illegal to gerrymander districts for the sole purpose of protecting incumbents.
The ruling party attempted to gerrymander the electoral map to weaken the opposition’s chances.
The ruling party tried to gerrymander the districts to secure more seats in the election.
Activists protested after discovering officials had gerrymandered the voting map to favor their own party.
Laws have been proposed to prevent politicians from gerrymandering electoral boundaries for unfair advantage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redistrict - to divide or organize an area into new political districts
  • antonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve a system or practice
  • synonym: rig - to manipulate or control something dishonestly for personal gain
