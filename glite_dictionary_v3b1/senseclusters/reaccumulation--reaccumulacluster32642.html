reaccumulation

noun
gathering again
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˌkjuːmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of gathering or collecting something again after it has been removed or lost

Examples

Ice reaccumulation
After the snow melted, the reaccumulation of ice on the roof became a concern.
The scientist studied the reaccumulation of minerals in the riverbed over several years.
Efforts to clean the area were followed by a rapid reaccumulation of debris.
After the flood, the reaccumulation of debris along the riverbank was carefully monitored by the cleanup crew.
The reaccumulation of dust in the old attic happened quickly after it was cleaned.
Scientists studied the reaccumulation of sediment on the ocean floor following the underwater landslide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accumulation - the process of collecting or gathering things together
  • antonym: dissipation - the process of something disappearing or being dispersed
  • antonym: removal - the act of taking something away
