gymnosperm

noun
type of seed plant with unenclosed seeds
7.6 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɪm.nə.spɜːr/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɪm.nə.spɜːm/ (BrE)
A plant of a group (including conifers) whose seeds are not enclosed within an ovary, unlike those of flowering plants.

Examples

A gymnosperm plant
Pine trees are a common example of a gymnosperm found in many forests.
The ancient ginkgo tree is classified as a gymnosperm due to its naked seeds.
Unlike flowering plants, a gymnosperm produces seeds that are exposed rather than enclosed in fruit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: angiosperm - a plant whose seeds are enclosed within an ovary (flowering plant)
  • synonym: conifer - a type of gymnosperm with needle-shaped leaves, such as pine or fir
