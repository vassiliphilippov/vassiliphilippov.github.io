rousseauite

adjective
of Rousseau's ideas
9.9
Pronunciation: / ruːˈsoʊaɪt / (AmE), / ruːˈsəʊaɪt / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the ideas or style of the philosopher Jean-Jacques Rousseau, especially his views on natural human goodness and society.

Examples

Rousseauite philosophy
The novel presents a rousseauite vision of childhood innocence and natural virtue.
Her political theory is often described as rousseauite because it emphasizes the importance of the general will.
Critics argue that his rousseauite approach overlooks the complexities of modern society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enlightenment - relating to the Enlightenment period or its ideas
  • synonym: romantic - relating to the Romantic movement or its ideals
