linguistic performance

noun
actual language use by a speaker
2.8 5.7
Pronunciation: /lɪŋ.ˈgwɪs.tɪk pər.ˈfɔːr.məns/ (AmE), /lɪŋ.ˈgwɪs.tɪk pə.ˈfɔː.məns/ (BrE)
the actual use of language in real situations by a speaker, including errors, hesitations, and other non-linguistic features, as opposed to abstract knowledge of language

Examples

Children's linguistic performance
Children's linguistic performance often does not fully reflect their underlying knowledge of grammar.
Nervousness can affect one's linguistic performance during a public speaking event.
When analyzing linguistic performance, researchers consider how people actually speak, including their mistakes and pauses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: linguistic competence - a speaker's implicit knowledge of their language
