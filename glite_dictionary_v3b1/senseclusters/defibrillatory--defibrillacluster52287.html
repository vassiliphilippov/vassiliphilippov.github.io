defibrillatory

adjective
restoring heart rhythm
Pronunciation: / diˌfɪbrɪˈleɪtəri / (AmE), / diˌfɪbrɪˈleɪtəri / (BrE)
relating to the use or effect of restoring a normal heart rhythm, especially by electrical means

Examples

Defibrillatory shock
The doctor applied a defibrillatory shock to restart the patient's heart.
A defibrillatory device is essential in emergency cardiac care units.
The nurse prepared the defibrillatory equipment before the procedure began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defibrillation - the process of restoring normal heart rhythm by electrical shock
