cosine curve

noun
graph of a cosine function
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊsaɪn kɝːv / (AmE), / ˈkəʊsaɪn kɜːv / (BrE)
a curve described by the equation y = a cos x, where the value of y is a cosine function of x, typically producing a smooth, periodic wave shape

Examples

The cosine curve graph
By shifting the cosine curve horizontally, you can compare it to the corresponding sine curve.
The cosine curve is often used to model wave patterns in physics and engineering.
The teacher drew a cosine curve on the board to demonstrate its periodic behavior.
