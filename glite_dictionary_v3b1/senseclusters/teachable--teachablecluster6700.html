teachable

adjective
able and willing to be taught
3.5 0.83/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtiÀêt É…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàtiÀêt É…ôbl / (BrE)
willing and able to learn; capable of being taught or instructed

Examples

A teachable student
As a teachable student, she quickly absorbed new ideas and skills in the classroom.
He has a teachable attitude that makes him a pleasure to work with on any project.
The coach values athletes who are teachable because they respond well to feedback.

Synonyms

  • coachable
    4.1 0.09/m
    coachable is willing to learn from feedback and quickly improve in practice, while teachable means open and able to learn.
  • educable
    3.9 0.08/m
    educable can be taught with proper instruction and support, while teachable adds being willing and open to learn.
  • trainable
    2.9 0.10/m
    trainable can learn new skills or behaviors when trained, while teachable suggests both ability and a willingness to learn.
  • docile
    5.5 1.67/m
    docile is calm and easily led or taught by others, while teachable describes someone both willing and able to learn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: docile - ready to accept instruction; submissive
  • antonym: unteachable - not able to be taught or unwilling to learn
