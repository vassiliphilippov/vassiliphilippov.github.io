advise

verb
to give advice or guidance
1.0 1.9
Pronunciation: /əd.ˈvaɪz/ /; / /æd.ˈvaɪz/ (AmE), /əd.ˈvaɪz/ /; / /æd.ˈvaɪz/ (BrE)
to give advice, suggestions, or guidance to someone about what they should do

Examples

To advise a friend
I strongly advise you to double-check your work before submitting it.
She often advises her friends on financial matters.
The doctor advised him to get more rest after his surgery.
She plans to advise the board on a new direction for the company.
The committee will advise major changes to the current policy tomorrow.
The government may advise stricter regulations next year.

  • antonym: discourage - to advise someone against doing something
  • synonym: propose - to put forward a plan or suggestion for consideration
  • synonym: recommend - to suggest that something is good or suitable for a particular purpose
