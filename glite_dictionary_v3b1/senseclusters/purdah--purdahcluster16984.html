purdah

noun
system of secluding women (Hindu/Muslim)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrdə / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdə / (BrE)
The traditional practice among some Hindu and Muslim communities of keeping women secluded from the sight of men or strangers, often by means of physical segregation and special clothing.

Examples

Live in purdah
After her marriage, she entered purdah and participated less in community events.
Many women in the village still observe purdah, rarely appearing in public without a veil.
The novel explores the theme of purdah and its effect on women's lives within the family.
