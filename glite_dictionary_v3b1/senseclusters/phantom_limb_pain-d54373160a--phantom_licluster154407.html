phantom limb pain

noun
pain perceived in a missing limb
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfæntəm lɪm peɪn / (AmE), / ˈfæntəm lɪm peɪn / (BrE)
pain experienced by an amputee that appears to originate from the missing limb or part of a limb

Examples

Phantom limb pain sensation
He described his phantom limb pain as a burning sensation in the leg that was removed.
Many amputees experience phantom limb pain months or even years after their surgery.
Treatments for phantom limb pain include medications, physical therapy, and mirror therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phantom pain - pain that feels like it's coming from a body part that's no longer there
