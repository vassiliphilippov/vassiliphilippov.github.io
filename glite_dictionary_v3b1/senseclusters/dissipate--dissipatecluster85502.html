dissipate

verb
live a self-indulgent, pleasure-seeking life
3.7 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪ.sə.peɪt/ /; / /ˈdɪ.sɪ.peɪt/ (AmE), /ˈdɪ.sɪ.peɪt/ /; / /ˈdɪ.sə.peɪt/ (BrE)
to live a life indulging in pleasure, often with excessive drinking; to spend one's time and energy in self-indulgence

Examples

To dissipate his life
After inheriting his fortune, he began to dissipate and neglected all family responsibilities.
Many poets from the era would dissipate in taverns, losing themselves in drink and revelry.
She warned him not to dissipate his youth in reckless pleasures.

  • synonym: debauch - to lead away from virtue or excellence, especially by self-indulgence
