karakul

noun
breed of Central Asian sheep; prized for lambs' fur
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəkʊl / (AmE), / ˈkærəkʊl / (BrE)
A hardy breed of coarse-haired sheep originating from Central Asia, valued especially for the soft, curly, black fur of its newborn lambs.

Examples

karakul sheep
Farmers raise karakul sheep primarily for their valuable hides.
Many traditional hats in Central Asia are made using karakul fur.
The karakul is prized for the soft, curly black pelts of its young lambs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: astrakhan - the tightly curled, glossy fur of young karakul lambs, used in clothing
