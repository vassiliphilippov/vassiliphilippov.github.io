pariah

noun
an outcast; socially rejected person
Pronunciation: / pəˈraɪ.ə / (AmE), / pəˈraɪ.ə / (BrE)
a person who is rejected or excluded from social or home life; an outcast.

Examples

A pariah person
After he was accused of cheating, he became a pariah within the company.
Fearing they would be seen as pariahs, the students hesitated to speak out against the group.
The writer was once celebrated but later treated like a pariah for her controversial views.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insider - a person who is accepted as a member of a group
  • synonym: outcast - a person who has been rejected by society or a social group
