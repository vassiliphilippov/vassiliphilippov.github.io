hemorrhagic stroke

noun
stroke caused by brain bleeding
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /he.məˈrædʒ.ɪk stroʊk/ /; / /he.məˈræɡ.ɪk stroʊk/ (AmE), /he.məˈrædʒ.ɪk strəʊk/ /; / /he.məˈræɡ.ɪk strəʊk/ (BrE)
A type of stroke caused by bleeding from a ruptured blood vessel in the brain, leading to damage to surrounding brain tissue.

Examples

A hemorrhagic stroke event
A hemorrhagic stroke can be life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention.
Her grandfather suffered a hemorrhagic stroke after a blood vessel burst in his brain.
Recovery from a hemorrhagic stroke often involves extensive rehabilitation and therapy.
A haemorrhagic stroke requires immediate medical attention to minimize brain damage.
She was diagnosed with a haemorrhagic stroke after collapsing at home.
The patient survived a haemorrhagic stroke but needed extensive rehabilitation afterward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ischemic stroke - stroke caused by blockage of blood supply to the brain
