spacey

adjective
dazed or inattentive in manner
7.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆspeÉªsi / (AmE), / ËˆspeÉªsi / (BrE)
(of a person) appearing confused, dazed, or inattentive, as if under the influence of a drug or lost in thought.

Examples

Spacey expression
His spacey look made me wonder if he was daydreaming or unwell.
I get really spacey if I don't get enough sleep at night.
She felt so spacey after taking the medication that she could barely focus on the conversation.

Synonyms

  • spaced-out
    8.7 0.02/m
    spaced-out is very dazed and not fully aware, often like someone on drugs or exhausted, while spacey looks similarly absent.
  • dreamy
    3.3 1.26/m
    dreamy is quietly lost in thought and gentle in mood, while spacey suggests a stronger daze or not paying attention.
  • moony
    7.3 0.05/m
    moony is soft and romantically absent with a silly stare, while spacey suggests real confusion or a drug-like daze.
