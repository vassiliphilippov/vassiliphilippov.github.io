go off on one

verb
become suddenly angry or passionate in speech
9.3
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ɑf ɑn wʌn / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf ɒn wʌn / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry or to speak passionately, often in a loud or excited way, especially for a prolonged period

Examples

To go off on one suddenly
If you criticize his taste in music, he tends to go off on one without warning.
She really went off on one when she heard the bad news at work.
Whenever someone brings up football, Tom can go off on one for hours about his favorite team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm down - to become less angry or excited
  • synonym: lose it - to lose control of one's emotions
