proof

noun
validation or demonstration of truth
Pronunciation: / pruːf / (AmE), / pruːf / (BrE)
evidence or argument that shows something is true or real

Examples

Proof of purchase
She demanded proof before she would believe his story.
The scientist conducted several experiments in search of proof.
They presented documents as proof of ownership.
She asked for proof of purchase before processing the return.
The detective presented new proof in the case to support his theory.
Without solid proof, the allegations could not be taken seriously.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disproof - evidence that shows something is not true
  • synonym: evidence - facts or information indicating whether a belief or proposition is true or valid
