Egyptian onion

noun
perennial onion with bulbils instead of flowers
4.2 5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈʌniən / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɪpʃən ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
A type of perennial onion grown mainly for early salad onions or as a curiosity, having bulbils that replace its flowers; also known as tree onion or walking onion.

Examples

The Egyptian onion plant
My grandmother grows Egyptian onions in her backyard for their unique bulbils.
The Egyptian onion is sometimes called the walking onion because it spreads by top-set bulbs.
You can use Egyptian onion greens in salads for an early, mild onion flavor.
