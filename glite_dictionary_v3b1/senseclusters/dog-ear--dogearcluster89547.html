dog-ear

noun
folded-down page corner in a book
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɡ ɪr / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ ɪə / (BrE)
a corner of a page in a book that has been folded down to mark one's place or as a reminder

Examples

Folded dog-ear page
Over time, the cookbook's pages were filled with dog-ears and stains from use.
She left a dog-ear in her novel so she would know where to continue reading.
The librarian asked patrons not to make a dog-ear in any of the borrowed books.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bookmark - a marker used to show where you have left off reading in a book
