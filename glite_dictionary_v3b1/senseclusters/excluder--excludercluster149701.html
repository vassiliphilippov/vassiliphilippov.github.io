excluder

noun
person or device that keeps out
6.2
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːdər / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːdə / (BrE)
a person or thing that keeps someone or something out or prevents them from being included

Examples

An excluder screen
The new screen door acts as an excluder to keep insects out of the house.
He was seen as an excluder because he decided who could join the club.
Farmers use a special excluder to prevent birds from eating their crops.
The strict dress code served as an excluder for many potential guests at the event.
As an excluder, the security system only allowed authorized personnel into the building.
He did not want to be seen as an excluder in the group, so he invited everyone to join.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admitter - a person or thing that allows entry
  • synonym: barrier - something that blocks or keeps things apart
  • synonym: gatekeeper - a person who controls access to something
  • antonym: includer - a person or thing that makes sure others are included
