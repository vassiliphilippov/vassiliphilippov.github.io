sudd

noun
floating vegetation mass
Pronunciation: / sʌd / (AmE), / sʌd / (BrE)
a large floating mass of vegetation, especially found in the Nile or similar rivers, that can block waterways

Examples

The sudd mass
The explorers struggled to move their boat through the thick sudd on the river.
During the rainy season, the sudd can cover vast areas and block navigation.
Fishermen often avoid regions where the sudd is especially dense and difficult to cross.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or hinders passage
  • synonym: raft - a floating mass or structure, often of vegetation or debris
