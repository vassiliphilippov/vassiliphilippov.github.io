post horse

noun
horse for mail or travelers (historical)
Pronunciation: / poʊst hɔrs / (AmE), / pəʊst hɔːs / (BrE)
a horse kept at an inn or post house for use by mail carriers or rented to travelers, especially in former times

Examples

A post horse at inn
After changing to a new post horse at the post house, the messenger quickly resumed his route.
In the eighteenth century, travelers often relied on a post horse to continue their journey between towns.
The innkeeper offered a fresh post horse to the weary mail carrier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coach horse - horse used to draw a coach or carriage
  • synonym: post - relay system for mail and travelers
