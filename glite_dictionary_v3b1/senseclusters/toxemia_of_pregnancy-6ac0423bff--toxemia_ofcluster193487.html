toxemia of pregnancy

noun
pregnancy disorder with high blood pressure and swelling
Pronunciation: / tɑkˈsiːmiə əv ˈprɛgnənsi /; / tɑɡˈzimɪə əv prəˈɡnænsi / (AmE), / tɒkˈsiːmiə əv ˈprɛgnənsi /; / tɒkˈsiːmiə əv prɪɡˈnænsi / (BrE)
a medical condition during pregnancy characterized by high blood pressure, swelling, and protein in the urine, also known as preeclampsia

Examples

Toxemia of pregnancy symptoms
Symptoms of toxemia of pregnancy include edema, protein in the urine, and elevated blood pressure.
The doctor diagnosed her with toxemia of pregnancy after noticing her high blood pressure and swelling.
Women with toxemia of pregnancy require careful monitoring for the health of both mother and child.
Toxaemia of pregnancy can pose serious risks to both mother and child if left untreated.
Swelling in the ankles and protein in the urine are warning signs of toxaemia of pregnancy.
The doctor monitored her closely for signs of toxaemia of pregnancy during her third trimester.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pre-eclampsia - pregnancy complication involving high blood pressure and organ damage
  • synonym: preeclampsia - a condition in pregnancy with similar symptoms: high blood pressure, edema, and proteinuria
