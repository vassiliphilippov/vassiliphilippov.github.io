phytolith

noun
microscopic plant silica
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪtoʊˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtəʊˌlɪθ / (BrE)
a microscopic silica particle formed within the cells of many plants, often preserved in soil after the plant decays

Examples

Microscopic phytolith particles
Archaeologists analyzed phytoliths to learn about the types of plants that grew in ancient times.
The presence of phytoliths in the soil helped scientists reconstruct the prehistoric environment.
Researchers identified different species by examining the shapes of phytoliths under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pollen - fine powder produced by plants for reproduction
  • synonym: silica - a hard, unreactive compound found in sand and quartz
