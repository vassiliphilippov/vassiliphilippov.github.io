synchronous operation

noun
clock-coordinated operation
3.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ɑː.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ɒp.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
An operation that is initiated and coordinated according to a clock signal, ensuring predictable timing.

Examples

Synchronous operation with clock
In electronics, synchronous operation allows for better coordination between different circuit components.
Many network protocols use synchronous operation to ensure consistent data transmission rates.
Processors that run in synchronous operation rely on the system clock for timing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asynchronous operation - operation not initiated by a clock, occurring independently
