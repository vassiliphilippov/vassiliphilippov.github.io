apolipoprotein

noun
fat-transport protein
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˌlaɪpəˈproʊtiːn / (AmE), / ˌæpəʊˌlɪpəˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a protein that binds to lipids and helps transport fats in the blood or body fluids

Examples

apolipoprotein levels
Doctors may check apolipoprotein levels to assess a patient's risk of heart disease.
Research has shown that certain apolipoprotein types are linked to cholesterol levels.
The function of an apolipoprotein is essential for the transport of fats in the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lipoprotein - a compound of lipids and proteins that transports fats in the blood
