hybridize

verb
crossbreed to create hybrids
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪbrɪˌdaɪz /; / ˈhaɪbrəˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪbrɪdaɪz / (BrE)
to breed animals or plants by crossing individuals of different breeds, varieties, species, or genera to produce a hybrid

Examples

To hybridize plants
Modern agriculture relies on the ability to hybridize crops for greater yield and adaptability.
Scientists often hybridize different species of plants to develop new varieties with desirable traits.
The farmer decided to hybridize his cattle to improve milk production and disease resistance.
Attempts to hybridise the wild cats resulted in a new breed with unique traits.
Scientists often hybridise different plant species to develop new, resistant crops.
Some gardeners hybridise roses to achieve more vibrant colors and hardier plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crossbreed - to breed animals or plants of different kinds
  • antonym: inbreed - to breed from closely related people or animals
  • synonym: interbreed - to breed or cause to breed with another of a different kind
