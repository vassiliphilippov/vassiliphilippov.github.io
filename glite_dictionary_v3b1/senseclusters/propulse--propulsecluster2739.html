propulse

verb
to drive forward
8.3
Pronunciation: / prəˈpʌls / (AmE), / prəˈpʌls / (BrE)
to drive or push something or someone forward, either physically or into a new situation

Examples

To propulse forward
The new marketing campaign will propulse the company into international markets.
Her innovative ideas propulsed her career to new heights within a year.
Technological advances often propulse entire industries forward at a rapid pace.
The powerful engines propulse the ship through the water at high speed.
Modern rockets are designed to propulse satellites into orbit efficiently.
Special fans propulse the air to keep the laboratory well ventilated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: restrain - to hold back or prevent forward movement
  • synonym: thrust - to push something suddenly or forcefully
