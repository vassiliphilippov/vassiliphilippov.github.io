troglodytism

noun
cave-dwelling adaptations
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɑːɡləˌdaɪtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɒɡləˌdaɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
the evolutionary or physiological adaptations of organisms to living in caves or underground habitats, such as loss of eyes or pigment

Examples

Troglodytism in insects
The troglodytism of certain insects is evident in their lack of eyesight and pale bodies.
Researchers are fascinated by the troglodytism seen in cave-dwelling fish species.
Adaptations like reduced pigmentation are common features of troglodytism in underground animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cave adaptation - adaptations that enable organisms to live in caves
  • antonym: surface-dwelling - living on or near the surface, not underground
