metatarsal arch

noun
curved structure of metatarsal bones in the foot
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈtɑrsəl ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈtɑːsəl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the curved structure formed by the heads of the metatarsal bones in the foot, supporting part of the weight during standing and walking

Examples

The metatarsal arch of foot
Flat feet may result from a poorly developed metatarsal arch.
Proper support for the metatarsal arch can reduce discomfort during long periods of standing.
The metatarsal arch helps absorb shock while walking or running.

Related Terms

  • synonym: longitudinal arch - the lengthwise arch of the foot running from heel to toe
