laryngeal artery

noun
artery supplying blood to the larynx
Pronunciation: / lərɪnˈdʒiəl ˈɑrˌtɛri / (AmE), / larɪnˈdʒiːəl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
either of two arteries that supply blood to the larynx

Examples

The laryngeal artery
During surgery on the throat, care must be taken not to damage the laryngeal artery.
The laryngeal artery is essential for providing oxygen-rich blood to the vocal cords.
The superior laryngeal artery branches from the superior thyroid artery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: larynx - the voice box; part of the throat containing the vocal cords
