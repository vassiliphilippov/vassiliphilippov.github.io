mearstone

noun
old boundary stone marker
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪərˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɪəstəʊn / (BrE)
An old term for a boundary marker consisting of a pile of stones topped with an upright slab.

Examples

A mearstone marker
In the old maps, every mearstone is carefully noted as a key landmark.
Surveyors recorded the position of each mearstone along the parish border.
The farm's boundary was once marked by an ancient mearstone in the meadow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: landmark - an object or feature that is easily seen and recognized from a distance, often used for navigation or marking boundaries
  • synonym: milestone - a stone set up beside a road to mark distance in miles
