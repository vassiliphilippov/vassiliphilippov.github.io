resubordinate

verb
to become subordinate again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːsəˈbɔːrdəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːsəˈbɔːdɪneɪt / (BrE)
to become subordinate again or return to a lower or dependent status after having been independent or equal.

Examples

To resubordinate under a boss
After the reorganization, the team had to resubordinate to the new department head.
The branch office will resubordinate under the main headquarters after years of independence.
When the project failed, the group was forced to resubordinate within the larger organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: promote - to raise to a higher or more important position
  • synonym: subordinate - to place in a lower or dependent position
