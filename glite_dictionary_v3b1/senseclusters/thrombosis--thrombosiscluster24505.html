thrombosis

noun
formation of a blood clot in a vessel
2.4 5.8
Pronunciation: /θrɑːm.ˈboʊ.sɪs/ /; / /θrɑːm.ˈboʊ.səs/ (AmE), /θrɒm.ˈbəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /θrɒm.ˈbəʊ.səs/ (BrE)
the formation or presence of a blood clot inside a blood vessel, which may obstruct the flow of blood

Examples

Thrombosis in vessels
A history of smoking can increase your risk of thrombosis and other cardiovascular diseases.
Deep vein thrombosis is a serious condition that can lead to complications if left untreated.
The patient developed a thrombosis in his leg after surgery, requiring immediate treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embolism - obstruction of a blood vessel by a foreign substance or blood clot that has traveled from elsewhere
  • antonym: hemorrhage - an escape of blood from a ruptured blood vessel
