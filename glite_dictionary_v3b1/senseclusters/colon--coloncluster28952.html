colon

noun
Costa Rican currency unit
3.8
Pronunciation: / koʊˈloʊn /; / ˈkoʊlɑn / (AmE), / kɒˈlɒn /; / ˈkəʊlɒn / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in Costa Rica and formerly in El Salvador

Examples

Costa Rican colon
I exchanged my dollars for several thousand colones at the airport.
The colon has been the official currency of Costa Rica since 1896.
The price of a coffee in San José is around five hundred colones.
Before switching to the U.S. dollar, El Salvador used the colon as its official currency.
My grandfather still keeps a few colones as a reminder of the old monetary system.
The price for the souvenir was listed as twenty-five colones.
