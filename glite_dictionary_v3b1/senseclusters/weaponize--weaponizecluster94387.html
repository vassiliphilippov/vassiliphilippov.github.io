weaponize

verb
to convert for use as a weapon
1.0 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwep.ə.naɪz/ (AmE), /ˈwep.ə.naɪz/ (BrE)
to use or adapt something as a weapon, or to turn it into a tool for attack or advantage

Examples

To weaponize social media
Social media platforms can be weaponized to spread misinformation during elections.
Some countries fear their rivals may weaponize new scientific discoveries.
The regime was accused of weaponizing food supplies as a means of control.
Some groups have tried to weaponize information to influence elections.
Scientists warned that new technology could be weaponized if not carefully regulated.
The debate quickly turned heated when one side began to weaponize personal stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disarm - to take weapons away or remove the military potential
  • synonym: militarize - to give a military character to something
