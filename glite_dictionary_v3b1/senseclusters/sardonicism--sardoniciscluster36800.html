sardonicism

noun
grim mockery
Pronunciation: / sɑrˈdɑnɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / sɑːˈdɒnɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality or state of being grimly mocking, cynical, or sarcastic in attitude or expression

Examples

Her sardonicism tone
Her sardonicism often made people unsure whether she was being serious or joking.
The comedian's sardonicism gave his jokes a sharp, biting edge.
He used sardonicism to hide his true feelings about the situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irony - the expression of meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite
  • synonym: sarcasm - the use of irony to mock or convey contempt
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being honest and genuine
