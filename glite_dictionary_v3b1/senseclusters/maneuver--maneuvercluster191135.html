maneuver

noun
an evasive or strategic action
6.6 5.1
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːvɚ /; / məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / məˈnuːvə /; / məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
a skillful or strategic action or move designed to achieve a specific goal or advantage

Examples

A clever maneuver
He executed a clever maneuver to get past his opponent in the chess match.
Her quick maneuver on the basketball court left her defender behind.
The pilot performed a daring maneuver to evade enemy fire.
She devised an ingenious maneuver to secure the contract ahead of her competitors.
The company’s survival depended on a financial maneuver that impressed many analysts.
The politician's latest maneuver was intended to sway public opinion before the election.
Her clever maneuver during the negotiation gained her company a better deal.
The general ordered a maneuver to outflank the enemy's left side.
With a quick maneuver, he avoided the cyclist coming around the corner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: move - an action taken to achieve a particular result, especially in a game or competition
  • synonym: operation - an action or movement performed for a purpose
  • synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term goal
  • synonym: tactic - a method or action intended to achieve a specific result
