fibrous

adjective
having fibers or fiber-like texture
5.7 1.02/m

consisting of or resembling fibers; having a texture or structure made up of fibers, especially those used in making cordage such as jute

Examples

Fibrous material
Coconut husk is known for its fibrous material, widely used in making mats and brushes.
Many types of meat become fibrous if cooked for too long, resulting in a chewy texture.
The stem of the plant was fibrous and tough, making it ideal for rope making.

Synonyms

  • fibered
    4.6 0.00/m
    fibered is made of or contains fibers in general materials, while fibrous often describes a natural, rough fiber texture like jute.
  • fibrillar
    7.7 0.04/m
    fibrillar refers to very small, threadlike fibers in tissue or cells, while fibrous refers to larger material fibers like jute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fibrousless - lacking fibers
  • synonym: filamentous - composed of long, thread-like structures
