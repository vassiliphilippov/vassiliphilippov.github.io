rejecter

noun
signal-blocking device
8.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛktər / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛktə / (BrE)
a device, circuit, or component that blocks or removes unwanted frequencies or signals, especially in electronics

Examples

Signal rejecter
The engineer installed a rejecter to eliminate interference from the unwanted frequency range.
A high-quality rejecter can greatly improve the clarity of audio signals in electronic equipment.
The technician tested the rejecter to ensure it was blocking only the specified signals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acceptor - a device or component that allows certain signals to pass
  • synonym: filter - a device that removes unwanted parts from a signal
