recoil

noun
backward movement of a gun when fired
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌkɔɪl /; / rɪˈkɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈriː.kɔɪl /; / rɪˈkɔɪl / (BrE)
the sudden backward movement of something, especially after an impact or when a gun is fired

Examples

Gun recoil
Proper stance can help manage the recoil of powerful rifles.
The recoil from the shotgun nearly knocked him over.
You must brace yourself for the recoil when firing this weapon.
He braced himself to absorb the recoil after hitting the punching bag.
The recoil of the shotgun surprised the new shooter.
When the cannon fired, the heavy recoil shook the ground.
