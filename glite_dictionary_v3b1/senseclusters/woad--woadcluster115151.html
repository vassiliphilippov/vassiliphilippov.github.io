woad

noun
blue dye from the woad plant
Pronunciation: / woʊd / (AmE), / wəʊd / (BrE)
a blue dye obtained from the leaves of the woad plant (Isatis tinctoria), formerly used for coloring fabrics

Examples

Woad blue dye
Before indigo became common, European textiles were often colored with woad.
Historically, the Celts used woad to dye their clothing and even their bodies.
The artisan demonstrated how to extract blue dye from woad during the workshop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indigo - a deep blue dye obtained from certain plants, used as a modern alternative to woad
