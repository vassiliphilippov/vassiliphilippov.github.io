profiling

noun
examining dataset structure and quality
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
(in data management) the process of examining a dataset to determine its structure, content, quality, and metadata, typically to support cleaning, integration, or governance.

Examples

Data profiling
Before integration, engineers performed profiling to assess data quality and structure.
The automated tool runs nightly profiling to detect schema changes and missing values.
During the audit, the team reviewed the database profiling reports for compliance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data profiling - the process of analyzing datasets to summarize their content, structure, and quality
  • synonym: data quality assessment - the evaluation of data accuracy, completeness, consistency, and suitability for use
