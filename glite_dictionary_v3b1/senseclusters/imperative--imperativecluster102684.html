imperative

adjective
relating to verbs expressing commands
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛrətɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrətɪv / (BrE)
(of a verb or sentence) expressing a command, request, or instruction; relating to the imperative mood.

Examples

Imperative verb
'Sit down' is an example of an imperative command.
In the sentence 'Close the door', the verb is in the imperative form.
Teachers often use imperative sentences to give instructions to students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indicative - relating to verbs used for stating facts (indicative mood)
  • antonym: subjunctive - relating to verbs expressing wishes or possibilities (subjunctive mood)
