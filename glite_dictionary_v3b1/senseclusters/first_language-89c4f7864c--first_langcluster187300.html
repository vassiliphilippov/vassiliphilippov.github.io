first language

noun
the language learned from birth; native language
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
The language that a person learns from birth, typically spoken at home; one's native language.

Examples

A first language
Children usually develop literacy skills more easily in their first language.
English is his first language, but he is also fluent in French and Spanish.
Immigrants often teach their first language to their children at home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mother tongue - the language that a person has spoken from earliest childhood
  • antonym: second language - a language learned after one's native language
