medal

noun
award made of metal for achievement or event
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
A flat piece of metal, often round, given as an award for achievement in a competition or to commemorate an event.

Examples

A gold medal
A special medal was struck to commemorate the anniversary of the discovery.
She proudly wore her gold medal after winning the race.
The soldier received a medal for bravery in combat.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: certificate - an official document attesting to a status or achievement
  • degree higher: order - a formal honor usually awarded by a government
  • synonym: trophy - an object awarded as a prize for victory or achievement
