inerton

noun
hypothetical insulator
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɜːrtən / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːtən / (BrE)
a hypothetical substance proposed to not conduct electricity or heat

Examples

Non-conducting inerton
Scientists debated whether the mysterious inerton could exist in nature.
The theory suggests that an inerton would block both electrical and thermal energy.
No one has yet discovered a real material matching the properties of an inerton.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conductor - a material that allows electricity or heat to pass through it
  • synonym: insulator - a material that does not conduct electricity or heat
