carve a totem

verb
create a cultural or spiritual sculpture
Pronunciation: / kɑrv ə ˈtoʊtəm / (AmE), / kɑːv ə ˈtəʊtəm / (BrE)
to create a sculpture, especially from wood, representing cultural or spiritual beliefs, often as an act of honor or tradition

Examples

To carve a totem pole
As part of the ceremony, he helped his father carve a totem in the village square.
Many indigenous cultures still carve a totem to honor spiritual traditions and ancestral heritage.
The artist spent weeks learning to carve a totem that reflected the community's values.

Related Terms

  • synonym: totem pole - a tall carved wooden pole featuring symbolic figures, often representing family lineage or cultural beliefs
