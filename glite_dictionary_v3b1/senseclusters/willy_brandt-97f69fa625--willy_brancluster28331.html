Willy Brandt

proper noun
German chancellor, 1969–74; promoted peace with Eastern Europe
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪli ˈbrɑnt / (AmE), / ˈvɪli ˈbrant / (BrE)
(1913–1992) German statesman; served as chancellor of West Germany from 1969 to 1974 and was known for his efforts to reduce Cold War tensions with Eastern Europe.

Examples

Willy Brandt Nobel Prize
Willy Brandt received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1971 for his policy of Ostpolitik.
As chancellor, Willy Brandt sought to normalize relations with East Germany and other Eastern Bloc countries.
Many historians consider Willy Brandt a key architect of modern German diplomacy.
