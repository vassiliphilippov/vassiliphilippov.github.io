because

conjunction
introducing a reason
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɪˈkəz / (AmE), / bɪˈkɒz / (BrE)
used to introduce the reason for something or to explain why something happens

Examples

Late because traffic
He missed the meeting because his car broke down on the way.
They decided to leave early because the weather was getting worse.
I brought an umbrella because I thought it might rain.
