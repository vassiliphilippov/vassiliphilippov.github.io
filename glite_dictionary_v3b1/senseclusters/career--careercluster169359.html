career

verb
move swiftly and out of control
8.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɪr / (AmE), / kəˈrɪə / (BrE)
to move swiftly and uncontrollably in a specified direction, especially in an out-of-control or headlong manner

Examples

The car careered downhill
Spectators gasped as the cyclist careered off the track during the final lap.
The children careered through the hallway, laughing and shouting as they went.
The runaway truck careered down the steep hill, narrowly missing several cars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hurtling - moving rapidly, often uncontrollably
  • synonym: plunge - to move or fall suddenly and quickly
