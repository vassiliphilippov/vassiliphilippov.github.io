yellow perch

noun
North American freshwater fish
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ pɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a freshwater fish (Perca flavescens) native to North America, noted for its yellowish body with dark vertical bars and valued for food and sport

Examples

A yellow perch fish
Fishermen often catch yellow perch through the ice during winter months.
The yellow perch is a favorite among anglers for its tasty, flaky flesh.
The lake is well known for its abundant yellow perch population.
