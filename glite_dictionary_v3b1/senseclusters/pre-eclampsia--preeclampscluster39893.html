pre-eclampsia

noun
pregnancy complication involving high blood pressure
6.0 8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpri ɪˈklæmpsiə / (AmE), / ˌpriː ɪˈklampsɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition of pregnancy characterized by high blood pressure, fluid retention, and protein in the urine, which may develop into eclampsia if untreated

Examples

Pre-eclampsia in pregnancy
Doctors monitor blood pressure regularly to check for signs of pre-eclampsia during pregnancy.
Early detection of pre-eclampsia is crucial for the safety of both mother and baby.
Untreated pre-eclampsia can quickly progress to a more serious condition called eclampsia.
