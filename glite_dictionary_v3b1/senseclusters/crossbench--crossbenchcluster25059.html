crossbench

noun
seat for neutral or independent parliamentary members
4.8 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɑːs.bentʃ/ /; / /ˈkrɔːs.bentʃ/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒs.bentʃ/ (BrE)
In some parliamentary systems, a seat or position held by a member who does not consistently support either the government or the opposition.

Examples

A crossbench member
Several crossbench members abstained from the crucial vote, reflecting their independent stance.
She took a seat on the crossbench, signaling she would not support either major party.
The House of Lords contains many crossbench peers who provide non-partisan expertise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backbencher - member of parliament who does not hold government office or opposition shadow office
  • antonym: frontbench - seat or position held by leading members of government or opposition in parliament
