keel

noun
ridge on birds' breastbone for flight muscles
9.3
Pronunciation: / kil / (AmE), / kiːl / (BrE)
a ridge or projecting structure that looks like the keel of a boat, especially on a bird's breastbone or in anatomy, botany, or engineering

Examples

A bird's keel
Birds that do not fly generally lack a prominent keel on their breastbone.
The large keel of the eagle supports the powerful muscles needed for flight.
The ornithologist explained how the keel distinguishes flying and flightless bird species.
On the breastbone of many birds, there is a pronounced keel for flight muscle attachment.
Some flowers have a central keel formed by fused petals.
The dinosaur fossil displayed a bony keel running along its back.
