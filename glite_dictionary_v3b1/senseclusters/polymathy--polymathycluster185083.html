polymathy

noun
broad knowledge
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪməθi / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪməθi / (BrE)
the possession of knowledge in a wide range of different subjects or fields

Examples

His polymathy in subjects
His polymathy allowed him to contribute to discussions on science, art, and philosophy.
The university values polymathy and encourages students to explore multiple disciplines.
Throughout history, polymathy has been admired in figures like Leonardo da Vinci.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erudition - extensive knowledge acquired chiefly from books
  • antonym: specialization - focus on a single area of study or expertise
