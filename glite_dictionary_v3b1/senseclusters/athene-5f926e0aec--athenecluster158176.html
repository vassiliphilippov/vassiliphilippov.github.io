Athene

proper noun
Greek goddess of wisdom and arts
Pronunciation: / əˈθini / (AmE), / əˈθiːni / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the goddess of wisdom, practical skills, and strategic warfare, and the protector of Athens; identified with the Roman goddess Minerva.

Examples

Athene goddess of wisdom
In Greek mythology, Athene emerged fully grown and armored from Zeus's head.
Many ancient statues depict Athene wearing a helmet and carrying a shield.
The people of Athens built a great temple to honor Athene on the Acropolis.
