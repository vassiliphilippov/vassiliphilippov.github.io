fire-breather

noun
inflammatory, provocative speaker
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər.briː.ðər/ /; / /ˈfaɪr.briː.ðər/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə.briː.ðə/ /; / /ˈfaɪ.briː.ðə/ (BrE)
a person who speaks or writes in an aggressively inflammatory way, deliberately arousing strong emotions or controversy.

Examples

Political fire-breather
During the debate, the candidate sounded like a fire-breather, fanning fears instead of offering clear policies.
Local activists dismissed their rival as one of those fire-breathers who stir up trouble at every meeting.
The columnist warned that unchecked rhetoric could empower fire-breather figures and polarize the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demagogue - a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than rational argument
  • synonym: firebrand - a person who is passionate and often incites strong feelings or actions
