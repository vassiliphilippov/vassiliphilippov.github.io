forfeiture

noun
seizure of property by the state (civil forfeiture)
Pronunciation: / c8f54d0rf6at8359r / (AmE), / c8f54d0f6at8359 / (BrE)
(law) the state's right or legal process of seizing property or assets believed to be connected to crime, especially under civil proceedings where a criminal conviction is not required.

Examples

Legal forfeiture process
Under civil law, the government pursued the forfeiture of cars linked to the drug operation.
She protested the unjust forfeiture of her savings after police seized them during the investigation.
Civil forfeiture laws allow authorities to keep property even if no criminal conviction is obtained.

Related Terms

  • synonym: civil forfeiture - a civil legal procedure allowing authorities to seize property suspected of involvement in crime without a criminal conviction
  • synonym: confiscation - the official taking of property, often by the state
  • synonym: seizure - the act of taking possession of property by legal authority
