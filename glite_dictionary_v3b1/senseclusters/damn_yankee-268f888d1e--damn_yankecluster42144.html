damn Yankee

noun
insult for Union soldier
Pronunciation: / dæm ˈjæŋki / (AmE), / dæm ˈjæŋki / (BrE)
an offensive or insulting term used in the American South to refer to a person from the Northern United States, especially with hostility or resentment (including Union soldiers during the Civil War)

Examples

The old general still refused to speak kindly of any damn Yankee even decades after the war.
In the heated debate, he called his opponent a damn Yankee to express his Southern pride.
Stories from her grandfather often included tales of fighting against the damn Yankees in the 1860s.
The old man refused to trust any damn Yankee who came into town.
She was called a damn Yankee when she moved to the rural South.
Some locals still use the phrase damn Yankee to express resentment toward Northerners.
