fiduciary

adjective
related to a legal trust or trust-based duty
8.6 7.4
Pronunciation: / fɪˈduːʃiˌɛri / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃɪəri / (BrE)
relating to a relationship of trust, especially when one person is responsible for managing assets for another's benefit

Examples

Fiduciary duty
Banks are required to adhere to strict fiduciary standards to protect clients' money.
He acted in a fiduciary capacity when managing her financial affairs.
The company has a fiduciary duty to act in the best interests of its shareholders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trustee - a person who holds property or assets for the benefit of another
