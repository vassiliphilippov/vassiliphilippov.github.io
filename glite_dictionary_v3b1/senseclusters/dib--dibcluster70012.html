dib

noun
small planting hole
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪb / (AmE), / dɪb / (BrE)
a small hole or depression made in soil for planting a seed or seedling

Examples

A dib in soil
The gardener made a dib in the soil for each tomato seedling.
Use a stick to create a dib before planting each seed.
She carefully placed the seed into the dib and covered it with earth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: furrow - a long, narrow trench made in the ground by a plow
  • synonym: hole - an opening or hollow space in a surface
