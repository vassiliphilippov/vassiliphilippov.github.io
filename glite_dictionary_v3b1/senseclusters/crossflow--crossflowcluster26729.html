crossflow

noun
fluid flow across main direction
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɑːs.floʊ/ /; / /ˈkrɔːs.floʊ/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒs.fləʊ/ (BrE)
a flow of fluid or gas that moves across the main direction, or a system/device that uses this type of flow (especially in engines, filters, or ventilation systems)

Examples

A crossflow system
The new air conditioning system uses crossflow to improve ventilation throughout the building.
Engineers designed the filter to maximize efficiency by encouraging crossflow of water.
A crossflow cylinder head can help increase engine performance and fuel efficiency.
The engineers designed a crossflow filter to improve air quality in the laboratory.
A crossflow heat exchanger is commonly used in automotive cooling systems.
They replaced the old system with a modern crossflow unit for better efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: counterflow - a flow of fluid in the opposite direction to another flow
  • synonym: heat exchanger - a device that transfers heat between two or more fluids
  • antonym: parallel flow - a flow of fluid in the same direction as another flow
