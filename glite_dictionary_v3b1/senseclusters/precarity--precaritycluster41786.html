precarity

noun
insecure conditions
Pronunciation: / prɪˈkɛrəti / (AmE), / prɪˈkærɪti / (BrE)
a state of being insecure, unstable, or at risk, especially in work, living conditions, or social status

Examples

Job precarity
The rise of gig economy jobs has increased precarity for many workers.
Young people today often experience precarity in both housing and employment.
Activists are calling for policies to reduce precarity in the labor market.
Many workers today face precarity due to short-term contracts and lack of job security.
The precarity of her living situation made it difficult for her to plan for the future.
Economic downturns often increase the precarity experienced by vulnerable communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insecurity - the state of being unsafe or not firmly fixed
  • synonym: instability - the state of being likely to change or fail
  • antonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat
  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and not likely to change
