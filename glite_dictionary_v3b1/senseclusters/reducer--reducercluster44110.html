reducer

noun
substance causing reduction (esp. in chemistry/photography)
8.3 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààdus…ôr / (AmE), / r…™ÀàdjuÀês…ô / (BrE)
A substance that causes the reduction of another substance by being oxidized itself, especially used in photography to decrease the density of a negative or print.

Examples

Chemical reducer
A common reducer in chemistry is hydrogen, which donates electrons to other substances.
The developer and the reducer were mixed separately before adding them to the photographic plate.
Using too much reducer can make your photographs appear too light.

Synonyms

  • reducing agent
    5.8 0.03/m
    a reducing agent is a common chemistry phrase for a substance that gives electrons and causes reduction, while reducer often refers to photographic use.
  • reductant
    9.1 0.00/m
    a reductant gives electrons in chemical reactions and photography, while reducer is the photographic substance used to lower negative density.

Related Terms
