save-all

noun
a small sail that catches spilled wind
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪv ˌɔl / (AmE), / ˈseɪv ˌɔːl / (BrE)
a small sail set near a larger sail to catch wind that would otherwise be lost

Examples

A save-all sail
A skilled sailor knows how to rig a save-all to make the most of the breeze.
The crew hoisted the save-all to improve their speed in light winds.
When the main sail could not catch enough wind, they deployed a save-all.
