St. Matthew the Apostle

proper noun
disciple of Jesus; writer of the first Gospel
7.6
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈmæθju ði əˈpɑsəl / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈmæθjuː ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
one of the twelve disciples of Jesus, traditionally regarded as the author of the first Gospel in the New Testament

Examples

St. Matthew the Apostle disciple
According to tradition, St. Matthew the Apostle wrote the Gospel that bears his name.
Many churches around the world are named after St. Matthew the Apostle in honor of his legacy.
The feast day of St. Matthew the Apostle is celebrated on September 21st in the Western Christian calendar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: St. John the Apostle - another disciple of Jesus and Gospel author
