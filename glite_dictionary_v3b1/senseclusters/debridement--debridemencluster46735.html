debridement

noun
removal of dead tissue from a wound
9.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / dəˈbridmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈbriːdmənt / (BrE)
the removal of dead, damaged, or infected tissue from a wound, especially by surgical means, to improve healing and prevent infection

Examples

Debridement of tissue
After the accident, debridement was necessary to remove all the contaminated tissue.
Proper debridement can significantly speed up the recovery process for burn patients.
The surgeon performed a thorough debridement to ensure the wound was clean before closure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decontamination - removal of contaminants such as dirt or microorganisms
