shoot yourself in the foot

verb
to inadvertently harm your own interests
Pronunciation: / ʃuːt jərˈsɛlf ɪn ðə fʊt / (AmE), / ʃuːt jɔːˈsɛlf ɪn ðə fʊt / (BrE)
to harm your own interests or cause damage to yourself through your own actions, often unintentionally

Examples

To shoot yourself in the foot accidentally
He realized too late that he had shot himself in the foot by missing the deadline.
If you don't check your work, you might shoot yourself in the foot by making unnecessary errors.
She didn't want to shoot herself in the foot by arguing with her supervisor during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut off your nose to spite your face - to act out of spite in a way that is damaging to oneself
  • synonym: sabotage oneself - to deliberately or accidentally hinder one's own efforts
