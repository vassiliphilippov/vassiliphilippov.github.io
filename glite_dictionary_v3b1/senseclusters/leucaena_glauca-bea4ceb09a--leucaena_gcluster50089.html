Leucaena glauca

noun
tropical shrub; lead tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / luːˈsiː.nə ˈɡlaʊ.kə / (AmE), / luːˈsiː.nə ˈɡlaʊ.kə / (BrE)
A low, shrubby tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of North America, with white flowers tinged with yellow and long, flattened seed pods; also known as wild tamarind or lead tree.

Examples

Leucaena glauca tree
Farmers sometimes plant Leucaena glauca as a source of fodder for livestock.
The Leucaena glauca thrives in sandy soils and full sunlight across tropical regions.
You can recognize Leucaena glauca by its distinctive yellowish-white blossoms and long pods.
