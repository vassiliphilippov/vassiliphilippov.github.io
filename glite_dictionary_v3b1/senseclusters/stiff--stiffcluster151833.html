stiff

verb
to cheat by not paying
8.4
Pronunciation: /stɪf/ (AmE), /stɪf/ (BrE)
to cheat someone by not paying them money that is owed

Examples

To stiff a worker
The contractor was angry because the client tried to stiff him after the job was finished.
If you stiff your server, it is unfair and disrespectful to their hard work.
She warned her friends not to stiff the taxi driver after a long ride.
He was upset when his friend stiffed him after dinner at the restaurant.
The contractor stiffed several workers by not paying them for their labor.
If you agree to split the bill, don't stiff your friends at the end.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • synonym: defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • antonym: pay - to give money that is owed for goods or services
