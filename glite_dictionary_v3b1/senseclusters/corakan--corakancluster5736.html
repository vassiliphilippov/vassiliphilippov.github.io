corakan

noun
East Indian cereal grass; staple food crop
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îÀêr…ôk…ôn / (AmE), / Ààk…ír…ôk…ôn / (BrE)
An East Indian cereal grass (Eleusine coracana) whose seeds are used to make a somewhat bitter flour, commonly used as a staple food in parts of Asia and Africa.

Examples

Corakan grain
Because of its resilience to drought, corakan is an important food source in several countries.
Farmers in the region grow corakan as a reliable crop in dry seasons.
The flour ground from corakan is used to make flatbreads and porridge.

Synonyms

  • Eleusine coracana

  • finger millet
