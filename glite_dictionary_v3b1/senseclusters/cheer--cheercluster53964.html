cheer

noun
feeling or quality of cheerfulness
Pronunciation: / tʃɪr / (AmE), / tʃɪə / (BrE)
a feeling or quality of cheerfulness and the ability to make people feel less sad or gloomy

Examples

A little cheer
He sent flowers to bring a little cheer to his friend in the hospital.
Her laughter brought a welcome sense of cheer to the family gathering.
The bright colors in the classroom added some cheer to the gloomy winter day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheerfulness - the state of being noticeably happy and optimistic
  • antonym: gloom - a state of depression or sadness
