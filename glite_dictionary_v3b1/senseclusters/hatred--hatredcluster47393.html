Synonyms

  • hate
    1.0 23/m
    hate is a common, direct word for very strong dislike often used in everyday speech, while hatred names intense, often lasting hostility.
  • detestation
    8.6 0.10/m
    detestation expresses strong disgust mixed with hatred, while hatred can be broader and may lead someone to act.
  • loathing
    5.2 3.13/m
    loathing is an intense, disgust-filled hatred felt strongly in the body, while hatred often names deep hostility that can cause action.
  • abhorrence
    7.8 0.41/m
    abhorrence is strong disgust and rejection often used in formal speech, while hatred focuses more on angry hostility and possible action.
  • animosity
    6.1 3.34/m
    animosity is active, ongoing hostility or bitterness between people, while hatred is a very strong feeling that may lead to action.
  • enmity
    8.0 1.68/m
    enmity is mutual or long-standing hostility between people or groups, while hatred is the intense personal feeling that can spark action.
  • animus
    9.0 1.30/m
    animus is a formal word for targeted ill will toward someone, while hatred is a very strong emotional dislike.
  • hostility
    4.7 11/m
    hostility is unfriendly or aggressive behavior or attitude, while hatred is a deep, often emotional feeling that can cause those actions.
  • antipathy
    8.3 1.19/m
    antipathy is a feeling of dislike or aversion that may be calm or mild, while hatred is a much stronger, often active hostility.
