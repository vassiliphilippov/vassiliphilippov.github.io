sequestration

noun
automatic government spending cuts
8.9 2/m
Pronunciation: / \u02ccsi\u02d0kw\u0259\u02c8stre\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (AmE), / \u02ccsi\u02d0kw\u026a\u02c8stre\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (BrE)
a government policy or legal mechanism that mandates automatic, across-the-board reductions in public spending, typically triggered when budget targets or deficit limits are not met.

Examples

Federal sequestration cuts
Facing a large deficit, Congress imposed sequestration that cut funding across many federal programs.
The automatic sequestration reduced discretionary spending by an equal percentage in every department.
Economists warned that prolonged sequestration could slow growth and harm public services in poorer areas.
