waft

verb
be carried lightly through air or over water
9.4 4.2
Pronunciation: /wɑːft/ /; / /wæft/ (AmE), /wɒft/ /; / /wɑːft/ (BrE)
to move or be carried gently through the air, especially by a light breeze (used for scents, sounds, or light objects)

Examples

To waft a scent
A gentle melody wafted from the concert hall and filled the courtyard.
Her words barely wafted above a whisper, lost to the hum of the room.
The scent of jasmine wafted through the open window on the evening breeze.
A gentle breeze wafted the curtains into the room.
She watched the soap bubbles waft slowly across the garden.
The scent of fresh bread wafted from the bakery down the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: float - to move slowly or lightly through the air or on water
