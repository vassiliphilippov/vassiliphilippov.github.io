webbing moth

noun
moth that spins a web to live in
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛbɪŋ mɔθ / (AmE), / ˈwɛbɪŋ mɒθ / (BrE)
A moth that creates a web-like structure for living and protection.

Examples

Webbing moth web shelter
Each webbing moth weaves a protective web as its shelter and breeding ground.
Farmers noticed that webbing moths were damaging their crops by nesting in the fields.
The infestation was caused by the webbing moth, which spun silky webs around the leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: webworm - larva of a moth that forms a web-like nest on tree branches
