dole out

verb
distribute something in small amounts
Pronunciation: / doʊl aʊt / (AmE), / dəʊl aʊt / (BrE)
to give or distribute something to several people, usually in small amounts or over a period of time

Examples

To dole out money
She patiently doles out advice to anyone who asks for help at work.
The coach doled out praise to the team after their impressive victory.
The government will dole out food supplies to families in need during the crisis.

During the festival, volunteers doled out food to the crowd.
The teacher doled out the test papers to the students one by one.
During the crisis, the government began to dole out emergency funds to communities in need.
She was known to dole out compliments generously, making everyone feel appreciated.
The manager agreed to dole out bonuses to the top-performing employees.
