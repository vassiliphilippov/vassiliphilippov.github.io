pin down

verb
place in a confining or embarrassing position
Pronunciation: / pɪn daʊn / (AmE), / pɪn daʊn / (BrE)
to place someone in a confining or embarrassing position from which it is difficult to escape

Examples

Reporters pin down a politician
Despite their efforts, no one could pin down the CEO about the company's future plans.
Journalists attempted to pin down the candidate on her stance, but she avoided answering directly.
The lawyer tried to pin down the witness with tough questions.
