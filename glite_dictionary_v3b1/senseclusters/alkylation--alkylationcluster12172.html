alkylation

noun
adding alkyl group
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.kəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌalkɪˈleɪʃən /; / ˌæl.kɪˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which an alkyl group is added to a molecule, often changing its structure or function

Examples

Alkylation process
The refinery uses alkylation to produce high-octane gasoline.
Scientists study alkylation to understand how chemicals interact in the body.
Modern pharmaceuticals often rely on alkylation to modify drug molecules for better effectiveness.
The scientist studied the alkylation of DNA to understand how certain chemicals cause mutations.
Industrial processes often use alkylation to modify the properties of fuels and chemicals.
Uncontrolled alkylation in the body can lead to harmful changes in proteins and genetic material.
