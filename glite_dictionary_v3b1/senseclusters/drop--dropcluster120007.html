drop

verb
let liquid fall in drops
1.0 60/m
Pronunciation: / drÉ‘p / (AmE), / drÉ’p / (BrE)
to let something fall or to fall down, often suddenly or unintentionally (including objects, people, liquids, etc.)

Examples

To drop a glass
Please drop a few drops of lemon juice into the sauce.
She dropped water onto the hot pan, causing it to sizzle.
They carefully dropped the medicine into her eyes.
After running the marathon, he was so tired he thought he might drop right there on the track.
She warned us not to overwork ourselves or we would drop from exhaustion before the end of the week.
The soldiers marched for hours without rest, determined not to drop despite their fatigue.
Be careful not to drop your keys when you cross the bridge.
She watched the leaf drop from the tree and float to the ground.
The temperature will drop sharply overnight due to the cold front.

Synonyms

  • fall
    1.0 164/m
    fall means moving down by gravity or from a height, while drop often means letting or causing something to fall suddenly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catch - to seize or hold something that is falling or being thrown
  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often due to exhaustion or weakness
  • synonym: descend - to move or come downwards
  • synonym: dribble - let liquid fall slowly in small drops
  • synonym: fall - to come down freely from a higher position
  • synonym: remove - to take something away or off
  • antonym: rise - to move upwards from a lower to a higher position
  • synonym: shed - to discard or remove something, especially clothing or a disguise
