laurel

noun
victor's wreath; sign of honor
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
(in ancient times) a wreath made from the leaves of the laurel tree, worn on the head as a sign of victory or honor.

Examples

Laurel wreath of victory
In Rome, generals would wear a laurel to celebrate their military triumphs.
Poets in antiquity were sometimes honored with a laurel placed upon their heads.
The victorious athlete was presented with a laurel after the ancient Olympic Games.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crown - a circular ornamental headdress, often symbolic of victory, rank, or honor
