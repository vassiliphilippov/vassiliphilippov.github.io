indent

verb
cut or tear so parts match for authentication
7.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛnt / (BrE)
to cut or tear along an irregular or notched line so that parts can be matched for verification or authentication

Examples

To indent two papers
The clerk will indent the two copies of the contract to ensure their authenticity.
They indented the official documents so only the original pair would fit together perfectly.
To prevent fraud, the certificates were carefully indented along one edge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: notch - to make a V-shaped cut in
