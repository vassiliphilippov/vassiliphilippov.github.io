melanism

noun
abnormal dark pigmentation due to excess melanin
9.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…õl…ôÀån…™z…ôm / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀêl…ôn…™z…ôm / (BrE)
A condition characterized by abnormal or excessive deposits of melanin, often resulting in dark pigmentation of the skin, feathers, or other tissues.

Examples

Dark melanism condition
Scientists are studying melanism to better understand genetic mutations affecting pigmentation.
Some animal species develop melanism as an evolutionary adaptation to dark environments.
The panther shows a striking example of melanism in the wild.

Synonyms

  • melanosis
    9.7 0.00/m
    melanosis is a medical term for dark melanin deposits that form patches on skin, while melanism often describes dark pigmentation across skin, feathers, or tissues.
  • hyperpigmentation
    7.7 0.08/m
    hyperpigmentation is a general darkening of skin often from sun or injury, while melanism describes abnormal melanin deposits often affecting animals or tissues.
