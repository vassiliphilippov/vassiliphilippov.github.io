heterotrophic

adjective
organism requiring external organic food
6.6 8.1
Pronunciation: /he.tə.ˈtroʊ.fɪk/ /; / /he.tə.rə.ˈtrɑː.fɪk/ (AmE), /he.tə.ˈtrɒ.fɪk/ (BrE)
(of an organism) requiring organic compounds of carbon and nitrogen for nourishment, rather than producing its own food.

Examples

Heterotrophic organisms
Bacteria may be either autotrophic or heterotrophic depending on their environment.
Fungi are heterotrophic because they absorb nutrients from organic material.
Most animals are heterotrophic and must consume other organisms to survive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autotrophic - (of an organism) able to produce its own food from inorganic substances
