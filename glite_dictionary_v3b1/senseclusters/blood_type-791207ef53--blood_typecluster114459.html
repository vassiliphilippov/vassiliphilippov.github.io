blood type

noun
category of blood based on antigens
Pronunciation: / blʌd taɪp / (AmE), / blʌd taɪp / (BrE)
any of the various categories into which human blood is classified according to the presence or absence of specific antigens on the surface of red blood cells.

Examples

Blood type group
A person's blood type determines who they can safely receive blood from during a transfusion.
Knowing your blood type is important in case of a medical emergency.
The hospital needed donors with type O blood type after the accident.
