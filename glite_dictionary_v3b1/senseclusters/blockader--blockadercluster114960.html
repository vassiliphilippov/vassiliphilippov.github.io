blockader

noun
person enforcing blockade
8.1
Pronunciation: / blɑːˈkeɪdər / (AmE), / blɒˈkeɪdə / (BrE)
a person or ship that prevents goods or people from entering or leaving a place, especially by force or authority

Examples

Navy blockader
During the war, the blockader patrolled the coast to stop enemy ships.
The navy sent a blockader to prevent supplies from reaching the port.
As a skilled blockader, he was responsible for enforcing the trade restrictions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blockade - the act of preventing access to or from a place
  • synonym: interceptor - a person or thing that stops or intercepts something
  • antonym: smuggler - a person who illegally moves goods or people across borders
