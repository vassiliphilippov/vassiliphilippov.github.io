waver in

verb
hesitate or be indecisive
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvər ɪn / (AmE), / ˈweɪvə ɪn / (BrE)
to hesitate or show uncertainty about making a decision or taking a course of action

Examples

To waver in decision
Don't let doubt make you waver in your commitment to your goals.
She began to waver in her resolve when she heard the counterarguments.
Whenever he wavers in his beliefs, he seeks advice from his mentor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - come to a resolution in the mind as a result of consideration
  • synonym: hesitate - pause before saying or doing something, often due to uncertainty
