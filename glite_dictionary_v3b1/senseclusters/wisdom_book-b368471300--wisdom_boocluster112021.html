wisdom book

noun
biblical book containing wisdom
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪzdəm bʊk / (AmE), / ˈwɪzdəm bʊk / (BrE)
any of the biblical books, such as Proverbs or Ecclesiastes, considered to offer moral or spiritual wisdom

Examples

A wisdom book in Bible
Ecclesiastes, considered a wisdom book, explores existential questions and the meaning of life.
Many scholars have studied the wisdom books to understand ancient moral teachings.
The Book of Proverbs is often cited as a classic example of a wisdom book in the Bible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Proverbs - a book of the Old Testament offering wise teachings
