conditioned avoidance

noun
anticipatory learned avoidance response
6.4 6.0
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd ə.ˈvɔɪ.dəns/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd ə.ˈvɔɪ.dəns/ (BrE)
a learned behavioral response that enables an organism to anticipate and avoid an aversive stimulus before it occurs, typically as a result of conditioning

Examples

Conditioned avoidance response
Behavioral therapists often study conditioned avoidance to understand anxiety disorders.
The experiment demonstrated how conditioned avoidance can develop quickly when the aversive stimulus is highly unpleasant.
Through conditioned avoidance, the rats learned to move to the other side of the cage when they heard the warning tone.
