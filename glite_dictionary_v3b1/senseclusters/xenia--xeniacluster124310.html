xenia

noun
pollen effect on seed
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈziːniə / (AmE), / ˈziːniə / (BrE)
the effect of pollen on the characteristics of the developing seed or fruit, visible in the seed or fruit itself

Examples

Xenia effect in seeds
Scientists studied xenia to understand how pollen influences the color of corn kernels.
The breeder noticed xenia when the seeds developed unexpected traits after cross-pollination.
In some plants, xenia can affect the size and taste of the fruit produced.
