metatherian

noun
a mammal group including marsupials
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈθɪriən / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈθɪəriən / (BrE)
A member of a group of mammals, including marsupials and their extinct relatives, characterized by giving birth to relatively undeveloped young, often raised in a pouch.

Examples

A metatherian mammal
Fossil evidence suggests that metatherians first appeared in the Early Cretaceous period.
Kangaroos and opossums are living examples of metatherian mammals.
Unlike placental mammals, a metatherian gives birth to undeveloped young, which often complete development in a pouch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eutherian - mammals that develop their young internally until a relatively late stage (placental mammals)
  • synonym: marsupial - a subgroup of metatherians with pouches, like kangaroos and opossums
  • antonym: prototherian - primitive egg-laying mammals including the monotremes
