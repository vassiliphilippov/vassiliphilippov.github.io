pile

noun
large quantity or amount
Pronunciation: / paɪl / (AmE), / paɪl / (BrE)
a heap or mass of things; a large number or amount of something collected together

Examples

A pile of books
After the party, we had a pile of dishes to wash.
She left a pile of books on the kitchen table after studying all night.
There was a pile of dirty laundry in the corner of the room.
A pile of dirty clothes was stacked in the corner of the room.
She made a pile of books on the table before starting to sort them.
The children jumped into the pile of autumn leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heap - an untidy collection of things piled up together
  • synonym: mass - a large body of matter with no definite shape
  • synonym: stack - a neat pile of objects, typically one on top of another
