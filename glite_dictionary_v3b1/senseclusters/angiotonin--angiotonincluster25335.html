angiotonin

noun
vasoconstrictor substance
9.1
Pronunciation: /æn.dʒɪ.ə.ˈtoʊ.nɪn/ /; / /æn.dʒiː.ə.ˈtoʊ.nɪn/ (AmE), /æn.dʒɪ.ə.ˈtəʊ.nɪn/ /; / /æn.dʒiː.ə.ˈtəʊ.nɪn/ (BrE)
A vasoconstrictor substance that causes narrowing of blood vessels, also known as Hypertensin.

Examples

Angiotonin hormone
Doctors may administer angiotonin to patients experiencing dangerously low blood pressure.
Scientists are studying the effects of angiotonin on cardiovascular health.
The presence of angiotonin in the bloodstream can result in an increase in blood pressure.
