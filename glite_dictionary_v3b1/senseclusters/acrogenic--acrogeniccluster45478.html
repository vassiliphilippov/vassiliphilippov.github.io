(of certain plants) growing only at the apex or tip of the main stem, as in some ferns or mosses.

Examples

Acrogenic plant growth
Many ferns exhibit an acrogenic mode of growth, elongating only at their shoot tips.
Mosses that are acrogenic develop new tissue exclusively from the apex of the main stem.
The acrogenic nature of certain plants helps scientists distinguish them from those with lateral growth.
