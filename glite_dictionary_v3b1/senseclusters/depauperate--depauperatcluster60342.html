depauperate

adjective
lacking diversity
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpɔpəˌreɪt /; / dɪˈpɔːpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɔːpəreɪt /; / dɪˈpɔːpərət / (BrE)
lacking in variety, richness, or development; poor or underdeveloped, especially in biology or ecology

Examples

A depauperate forest
The forest is depauperate compared to others, with only a few tree species present.
Scientists studied the depauperate fish community in the isolated lake.
A depauperate ecosystem may struggle to recover from environmental changes.
The soil in this region is so depauperate that few plants can grow well.
Critics described the novel as depauperate, lacking depth and originality.
A depauperate ecosystem often supports only a limited number of species.
The island's depauperate flora is the result of centuries of deforestation.
A depauperate ecosystem often supports fewer animal species than a healthy one.
After the drought, the river was left depauperate of fish and aquatic plants.

  • synonym: barren - lacking in life, interest, or substance
  • synonym: impoverished - lacking in richness or resources
  • antonym: rich - having a large amount or variety, especially of species or resources
