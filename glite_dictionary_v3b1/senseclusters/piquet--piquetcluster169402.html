piquet

noun
historical military punishment involving a pointed stake
Pronunciation: / pɪˈkɛt / (AmE), / pɪˈkɛt / (BrE)
A form of military punishment, formerly used in Britain in the late 17th century, in which a soldier was forced to stand on one foot on a pointed stake.

Examples

Piquet punishment
British troops feared the cruel piquet punishment inflicted for minor offenses.
Historical records describe piquet as both painful and humiliating for soldiers.
The officer sentenced the deserter to endure the piquet for several hours.
