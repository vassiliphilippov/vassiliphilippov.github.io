diffeomorphism

noun
smooth invertible mapping
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfioʊˈmɔːrfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌdɪfiəˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
a smooth, invertible function between geometric spaces whose inverse is also smooth

Examples

A diffeomorphism between spaces
A diffeomorphism is used in mathematics to show that two spaces are smoothly equivalent.
The transformation was proven to be a diffeomorphism between the two manifolds.
Understanding diffeomorphism is essential in advanced geometry and physics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeomorphism - a continuous, invertible function between topological spaces with a continuous inverse
