popularist

adjective
appealing to the public
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːpjələrɪst / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊlərɪst / (BrE)
relating to or intended to appeal to the general public or to gain broad popular support

Examples

Popularist policies
The candidate's popularist message resonated with many voters across the country.
Critics accused the government of taking a popularist approach to win public approval.
The new policy was seen as a popularist move rather than a well-considered reform.

Related Terms

  • synonym: populist - relating to representing the interests of ordinary people
