rectitude

noun
moral uprightness; righteousness
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the quality of being morally correct and honorable; righteousness

Examples

Moral rectitude
His unwavering rectitude made him a respected leader in the community.
In times of temptation, her rectitude guided her actions.
The judge was admired for his rectitude and fairness in handling every case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immorality - the state of being wicked or lacking morals
  • synonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
  • synonym: righteousness - the quality of being morally right or justifiable
