three-toed sloth

noun
a sloth with three claws on each foot
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈθriː toʊd sloʊθ / (AmE), / ˈθriː təʊd sləʊθ / (BrE)
a sloth of Central and South America with three long claws on each foot, known for its slow movements and arboreal lifestyle

Examples

A three-toed sloth animal
Researchers observed a three-toed sloth using its long claws to grip onto branches.
The three-toed sloth spends most of its life hanging upside down in the rainforest canopy.
Unlike its cousin, the three-toed sloth moves even more slowly and has a lighter coat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: two-toed sloth - a sloth with two claws on each forefoot and three on each hindfoot
