uncast

verb
to remove a cast
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈkæst / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈkɑːst / (BrE)
to remove a medical cast from a person's limb after an injury has healed sufficiently

Examples

To uncast a leg
The nurse will uncast your leg once the X-ray confirms the bone has healed.
After six weeks, the doctor decided to uncast his arm and start physical therapy.
It is important not to uncast the limb too early to avoid further injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apply - to put something on, such as a cast
  • synonym: remove - to take something away or off
