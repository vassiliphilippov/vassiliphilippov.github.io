ferment

noun
state of agitation or rapid change
4.9 7.2
Pronunciation: / fɚˈmɛnt / (AmE), / fəˈmɛnt / (BrE)
A state of great excitement, agitation, or turbulence, especially during a period of rapid change or development.

Examples

Political ferment
The country was in a state of ferment following the controversial election results.
The university campus was a center of intellectual ferment during the 1960s.
Years of social ferment eventually led to major reforms in the industry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and not changing
  • synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • synonym: unrest - a state of dissatisfaction, disturbance, or agitation typically involving public demonstration or disorder
