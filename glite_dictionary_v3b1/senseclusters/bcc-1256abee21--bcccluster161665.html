BCC

verb
to send blind copy
5.3
Pronunciation: /biː siː siː/ (AmE), /biː siː siː/ (BrE)
to send someone a blind carbon copy of an email so that other recipients cannot see they received it

Examples

To BCC your boss
If you want to keep your manager informed, you can BCC her on the project update email.
He always BCCs himself on important messages for his records.
Remember to BCC the client so their email address stays private.

Related Terms

  • antonym: CC - to send a carbon copy of an email to someone, visible to all recipients
