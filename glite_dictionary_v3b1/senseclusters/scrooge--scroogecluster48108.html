scrooge

noun
a person who is very stingy
8.5 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / skruÀêd í / (AmE), / skruÀêd í / (BrE)
a person who is very stingy and unwilling to spend money or share with others

Examples

A scrooge person
Despite his wealth, he lived like a scrooge and rarely bought anything new.
Don't be such a scrooge; it's just a small donation for charity.
Every office has at least one scrooge who refuses to contribute to the holiday party.

Synonyms

  • skinflint
    9.1 0.11/m
    skinflint is extremely miserly and refuses to spend even small sums, while scrooge is famously stingy and hoards money.
  • cheapskate
    7.0 0.65/m
    cheapskate avoids paying for things and may seem rude about money, while scrooge is very stingy and unwilling to share.
  • tightwad
    8.7 0.30/m
    tightwad watches every expense and rarely buys treats, while scrooge is harsher and openly unwilling to spend or share.
  • penny pincher
    5.8 0.05/m
    penny pincher counts small coins and saves carefully, while scrooge is more extreme and refuses to spend or share.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miser - a person who hoards wealth and spends as little money as possible
  • synonym: skinflint - a person who is extremely reluctant to spend money
  • antonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money in an extravagant, irresponsible way
