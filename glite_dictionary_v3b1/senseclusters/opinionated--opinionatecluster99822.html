opinionated

adjective
software enforcing design conventions
7.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈpɪn.jə.neɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈpɪn.jə.neɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
(in computing) describing a software framework, library, or tool that enforces a particular design philosophy by providing strong defaults, conventions, or prescribed patterns that guide how developers build applications.

Examples

Opinionated software
The new web framework is opinionated, guiding developers with strong defaults and prescribed patterns.
Many teams choose an opinionated stack to speed up development and ensure consistent project structure.
Because it's highly opinionated, the library makes some architectural decisions for beginners and veterans alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prescriptive - enforcing specific rules, patterns, or conventions rather than leaving choices open
  • antonym: unopinionated - not enforcing particular design choices; offering minimal defaults and greater flexibility
