inject

verb
force fluid or gas into something
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to force a liquid, drug, or other substance into something, especially into the body using a needle or syringe

Examples

To inject a vaccine
Doctors often inject vaccines directly into a muscle.
The technician will inject dye into your bloodstream for the scan.
They had to inject hydrogen into the balloon to make it float.
He admitted to having injected heroin in the past.
Some patients choose to inject insulin themselves every day.
The nurse will inject the vaccine into your arm.
Doctors sometimes inject saline solution to keep patients hydrated during surgery.
He was trained to inject insulin when needed for his diabetes.
The nurse will inject the medication directly into your vein.

Related Terms

  • synonym: administer - to give a drug or treatment to someone
  • antonym: extract - to remove or take out, often by force
  • synonym: insert - to put something into something else
  • antonym: withdraw - to take a fluid out from the body with a syringe or needle
