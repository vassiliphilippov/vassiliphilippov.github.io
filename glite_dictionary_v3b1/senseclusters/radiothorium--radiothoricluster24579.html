radiothorium

noun
radioactive isotope of thorium (thorium-228)
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈθɔriəm / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈθɔːriəm / (BrE)
A radioactive isotope of thorium with mass number 228, also known as thorium-228, used in scientific research.

Examples

Radiothorium isotope
Because of its strong radioactivity, handling radiothorium requires strict safety precautions.
Scientists once used radiothorium in early experiments studying radioactive elements.
The decay of radiothorium emits alpha particles detectable with sensitive equipment.
