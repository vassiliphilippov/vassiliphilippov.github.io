sequestration

noun
the act of separating or isolating
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsiːkwəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsiːkwɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of separating or isolating someone or something from others, especially in a legal or official context.

Examples

Carbon sequestration
Environmental sequestration of carbon dioxide is essential in fighting climate change.
Legal sequestration may occur when sensitive documents are removed from public access.
The sequestration of the jury ensured a fair and unbiased verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolation - the process of separating something from others
  • synonym: seclusion - the state of being private and away from other people
