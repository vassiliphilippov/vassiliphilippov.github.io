perfidiously

adverb
in a treacherous or deceitful manner
Pronunciation: / pərˈfɪdiəsli / (AmE), / pəˈfɪdiəsli / (BrE)
In a deceitful and untrustworthy manner; treacherously.

Examples

Act perfidiously
He acted perfidiously to further his own ambitions, betraying his closest ally.
The diplomat was accused of negotiating perfidiously with both sides to gain personal advantage.
They trusted her completely, not realizing she was plotting perfidiously against them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faithfully - in a loyal, trustworthy manner
