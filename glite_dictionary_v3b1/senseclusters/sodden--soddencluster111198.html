Synonyms

    1.0 0.00/m
    soaking is used to describe something extremely wet from water, while sodden sounds slightly older and heavier in tone.
  • sopping
    3.5 0.00/m
    sopping is a very informal word for being drenched with water, while sodden is slightly more formal and heavy in feeling.
  • drenched
    3.9 0.02/m
    drenched describes being soaked through or covered in liquid and looks very wet, while sodden often suggests heavy, waterlogged wetness.
  • soaked to the skin
    3.2 0.04/m
    soaked to the skin means clothing is wet right through after heavy rain, while sodden can describe items as waterlogged and heavy.
  • soppy
    4.3 0.00/m
    soppy often means wet and soft and can sound informal or emotional, while sodden mainly means very wet and heavy.
  • saturated
    3.4 0.09/m
    saturated is used in formal or technical contexts to mean no more liquid can be absorbed, while sodden is a plain word for very wet.
  • soggy
    1.9 0.8/m
    soggy describes something unpleasantly wet and soft, like soaked bread or soil, while sodden implies heavy, fully soaked wetness.
