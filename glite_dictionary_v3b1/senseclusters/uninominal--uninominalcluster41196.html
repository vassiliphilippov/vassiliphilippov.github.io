uninominal

adjective
having one representative per district (voting system)
8.5 8.5
Pronunciation: /juː.nɪ.ˈnɑː.mɪ.nəl/ /; / /juː.nə.ˈnɑː.mɪ.nəl/ (AmE), /juː.nɪ.ˈnɒ.mɪ.nəl/ /; / /juː.nə.ˈnɒ.mɪ.nəl/ (BrE)
(of a voting system) having only one representative elected from each district, or using a single name or term for each candidate or option

Examples

Many democracies operate under a uninominal electoral system, where voters choose only one candidate per district.
Some experts argue that uninominal voting systems strengthen the link between representatives and their constituents.

The country switched to a uninominal electoral system to simplify the voting process.
In a uninominal contest, each voter selects only one candidate for the position.
The committee adopted a uninominal voting system for the election.
