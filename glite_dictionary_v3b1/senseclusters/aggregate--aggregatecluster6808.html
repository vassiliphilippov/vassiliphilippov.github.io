aggregate

verb
to collect into a whole
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡ.rəˌɡeɪt /; / ˈæɡ.rɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæɡ.rɪ.ɡeɪt /; / ˈaɡ.rɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to collect or gather things together into a whole; to amount to a total when combined

Examples

To aggregate data
Researchers often aggregate results to identify overall trends.
The data from several sources were aggregated to provide a comprehensive report.
We are aggregating user feedback to improve the service.
Her personal assets and investments aggregate to a considerable sum.
The annual sales of all branches aggregate to over $2 million.
The donations from individuals and corporations aggregated to a record-breaking amount this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combine - to join or merge things together
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart
  • synonym: sum - the total amount resulting from the addition of two or more numbers, amounts, or items
  • synonym: total - to amount to a particular total
