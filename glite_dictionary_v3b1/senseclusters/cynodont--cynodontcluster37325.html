cynodont

noun
extinct reptile related to mammals
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪnəˌdɑnt / (AmE), / ˈsɪnədɒnt / (BrE)
A member of a group of extinct, small, carnivorous reptiles that are ancestors of mammals, having mammal-like teeth and other features.

Examples

Fossil cynodont skull
Fossils of cynodonts provide valuable insight into the evolution of mammals.
Paleontologists believe cynodonts were agile hunters with sharp teeth.
The museum exhibit displayed a skeleton of a small cynodont from the Triassic period.
