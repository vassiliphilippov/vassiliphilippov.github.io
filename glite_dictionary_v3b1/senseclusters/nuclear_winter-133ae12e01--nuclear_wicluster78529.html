nuclear winter

noun
period of extreme cold after nuclear war
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈwɪn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈwɪn.tə / (BrE)
A theoretical period of severe global cold and darkness following widespread nuclear explosions, caused by atmospheric dust and smoke blocking sunlight, with catastrophic effects on life.

Examples

A nuclear winter period
Documentaries have examined how a nuclear winter would affect agriculture and human survival.
Many scientists warn that a full-scale conflict could trigger a nuclear winter lasting for years.
The possibility of nuclear winter has influenced global disarmament talks since the 1980s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nuclear fallout - radioactive particles falling to earth after a nuclear explosion
