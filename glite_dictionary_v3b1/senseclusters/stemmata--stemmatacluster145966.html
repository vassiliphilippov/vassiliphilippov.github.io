stemmata

noun
simple eyes in larvae
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛm.ə.tə / (AmE), / ˈstɛm.ə.tə / (BrE)
simple eyes found in some insect larvae and arthropods, used mainly for detecting light

Examples

Insect stemmata
Caterpillars use their stemmata to sense changes in light and darkness.
The scientist examined the arrangement of stemmata on the larva's head under a microscope.
Unlike adult insects with compound eyes, larvae rely on stemmata for basic vision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compound eye - an eye made up of many small visual units, common in adult insects
  • synonym: ocellus - a simple eye found in many invertebrates
