ingrafting

noun
joining by grafting
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡræftɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrɑːftɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of joining or inserting a part (such as plant tissue or skin) into another so that it becomes part of the host

Examples

Ingrafting plant tissue
The horticulturist explained the benefits of ingrafting for creating stronger fruit trees.
Successful ingrafting can help preserve rare plant varieties in a garden.
He learned about ingrafting as a method to combine different rose species.
Successful ingrafting of the donor tissue is essential for the patient's recovery.
The botanist explained the importance of proper technique for effective ingrafting in fruit trees.
After several weeks, the surgeon confirmed the ingrafting of the skin graft into the patient's wound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grafting - the process of joining a plant part to another plant so that they grow together
  • antonym: rejection - the failure of a graft to become established in the host
