Seneca the Younger

proper noun
Roman philosopher, statesman, playwright (c. 4 BC–65 AD)
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnɪkə ðə ˈjʌŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪkə ði ˈjʌŋɡə / (BrE)
(circa 4 BC–65 AD) A Roman statesman, Stoic philosopher, and playwright, known for his role as advisor to Emperor Nero and his tragedies modeled on Greek originals.

Examples

Seneca the Younger philosopher
Seneca the Younger is often quoted for his contributions to Stoic philosophy.
As an advisor to Nero, Seneca the Younger navigated the dangerous politics of the Roman Empire.
The tragedies attributed to Seneca the Younger had a lasting impact on Renaissance drama.
