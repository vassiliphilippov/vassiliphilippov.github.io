Synonyms

  • considerably
    3.4 7/m
    considerably means to a large degree in general use, while substantially sounds slightly more formal and used in writing.
  • to a great extent
    3.5 0.5/m
    to a great extent expresses much the same idea in plain speech, while substantially is a slightly more formal adverb.
  • a great deal
    1.8 16/m
    a great deal is a casual way to say something happens very much, while substantially sounds more formal and precise.
  • greatly
    1.0 16/m
    greatly means to a very large degree in feelings or change, while substantially is used for general amounts or impact.
  • significantly
    3.5 16/m
    significantly means in a way that causes a noticeable effect, while substantially focuses on the size or degree of change.
  • appreciably
    4.9 0.3/m
    appreciably means enough to be noticed or measured, while substantially covers larger or more general degrees without measurement focus.
  • markedly
    4.6 1.0/m
    markedly means in a clearly noticeable way, while substantially describes a large degree without stressing a visible change.
  • largely
    2.4 30/m
    largely means mostly or for the most part, while substantially means to a great degree and not about what makes up something.
  • notably
    3.5 5/m
    notably means in a way that is especially noticed or important, while substantially means simply to a large degree or amount.
