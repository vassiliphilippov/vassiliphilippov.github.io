stand

noun
stop for a performance on a tour
3.1 2.0
Pronunciation: /stænd/ (AmE), /stænd/ (BrE)
a place or structure for performances or for people to watch events

Examples

A theater stand
During their European tour, the group scheduled a five-night stand at the famous theater.
The band added another stand in Chicago due to high demand.
Their New York stand sold out within hours of tickets going on sale.
A temporary stand was set up for the summer music festival.
Spectators gathered near the stand to enjoy the concert in the park.
The brass band performed classics from the stand in the town square.
Rain began to fall, sending spectators in the open stand scrambling for cover.
The crowd cheered from the stands as the runners crossed the finish line.
We arrived early to get good seats in the stand before the football match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandstand - a structure for a band to play music outdoors
  • synonym: engagement - a scheduled performance or period of employment, especially for entertainers
  • synonym: gig - a single performance by a musician or group
  • synonym: grandstand - a large and often more elaborate main stand at a sports ground
