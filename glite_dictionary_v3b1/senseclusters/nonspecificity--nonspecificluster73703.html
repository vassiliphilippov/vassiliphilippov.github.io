nonspecificity

noun
lack of specificity
8.6
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.spə.sɪ.ˈfɪ.sɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /nɒn.spə.sɪ.ˈfɪ.sɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of not being limited to or referring to a particular kind, category, individual, or cause; being general or vague

Examples

Nonspecificity of symptoms
The doctor noted the nonspecificity of the patient's symptoms, making diagnosis more difficult.
Because of its nonspecificity, the test cannot confirm a single disease.
The nonspecificity of the guidelines led to confusion among the staff.
The nonspecificity of the term made the instructions difficult to follow.
In linguistics, nonspecificity can affect how a sentence is interpreted by listeners.
The doctor noted the nonspecificity of the symptoms, making diagnosis challenging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: specificity - the quality of being clearly defined or identified
