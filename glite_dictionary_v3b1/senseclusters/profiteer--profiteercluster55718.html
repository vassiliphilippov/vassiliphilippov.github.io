profiteer

verb
make excessive profit, especially in shortage
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑː.fɪˈtɪr /; / ˌprɑːfəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌprɒf.ɪˈtɪə /; / ˌprɒfɪˈtɪə / (BrE)
to make or try to make excessive or unfair profits, especially by charging high prices during a crisis or shortage

Examples

To profiteer during shortages
During the crisis, some unscrupulous vendors began to profiteer by drastically raising the prices of basic supplies.
Laws were enacted to punish those who would profiteer from the food shortages during wartime.
The company was accused of trying to profiteer by selling essential medicines at marked-up prices.
Some companies were accused of profiteering during the natural disaster by raising prices on essential goods.
Authorities are investigating whether local businesses have been profiteering from the recent fuel shortage.
The government introduced new laws to prevent firms from profiteering in times of crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aid - to help or support, especially in a crisis
  • synonym: exploit - to use a situation or person unfairly for your own advantage
  • synonym: overcharge - to charge someone too much for something
