old

adjective
former; previous in time or order
Pronunciation: / oʊld / (AmE), / əʊld / (BrE)
of or relating to something that existed or was used before the current one; former or past

Examples

Old job
I sold my old car before buying a new one.
She still has photos from her old apartment.
We went back to our old school for the reunion.
He went back to his old school to visit some former teachers.
She met her old friend for coffee after many years apart.
The team hopes to restore its old reputation as champions.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ancient - belonging to a very distant past
  • synonym: former - having previously filled a particular role or had a particular status; synonym for old in this sense
  • antonym: new - recently created, acquired, or introduced; opposite of old in this context
  • synonym: previous - immediately before in time or order; synonym for old in this sense
