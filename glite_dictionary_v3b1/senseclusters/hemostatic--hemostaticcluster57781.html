hemostatic

adjective
tending to stop bleeding
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈstætɪk / (BrE)
(of a substance or process) tending to stop bleeding by contracting tissues or blood vessels.

Examples

Hemostatic dressing
After the injury, a hemostatic dressing was used to quickly stop the blood flow.
Certain medications have a hemostatic effect and are used to treat excessive nosebleeds.
The surgeon applied a hemostatic agent to control the bleeding during the operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticoagulant - a substance that prevents blood from clotting
  • synonym: astringent - a substance that contracts body tissues to reduce bleeding
