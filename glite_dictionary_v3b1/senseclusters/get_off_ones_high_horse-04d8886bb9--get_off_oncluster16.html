get off one's high horse

verb
stop being arrogant or self-righteous
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɔf wʌnz haɪ hɔrs / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf wʌnz haɪ hɔːs / (BrE)
to stop behaving in a superior or self-righteous way

Examples

To get off her high horse
After being corrected, she finally decided to get off her high horse and listen to others’ opinions.
He told his colleague to get off his high horse and stop acting as if he knew everything.
If you want people to cooperate, you need to get off your high horse and talk to them respectfully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: get down from one's high horse - to stop behaving as if you are better than others
  • synonym: humble oneself - to act in a less arrogant or self-important way
