off schedule

adjective
not on the planned timetable
3.2 0.14/m
Pronunciation: / …íf Ààsk…õd íuÀêl / (AmE), / …íf Àà É…õdjuÀêl / (BrE)
not happening at the time planned or arranged; behind or away from the intended timetable

Examples

The train is off schedule
Due to heavy traffic, the train is currently off schedule and will arrive late.
If we continue at this pace, we may fall even further off schedule.
The project went off schedule after several unexpected delays.

Synonyms

    3.2 0.02/m
    behind time is used for things that arrive or happen after the arranged time, while off schedule means not following the planned timetable.
  • late
    1.0 141/m
    late describes arriving or starting after the expected time, while off schedule means falling away from the planned timetable.
  • tardy
    3.9 0.85/m
    tardy is a formal word often used for people who arrive slowly, while off schedule refers to plans not on their timetable.
