swamp chestnut oak

noun
a deciduous oak tree of the southeastern US
6.6
Pronunciation: /swɑːmp ˈtʃes.nʌt oʊk/ /; / /swɔːmp ˈtʃest.nʌt oʊk/ (AmE), /swɒmp ˈtʃes.nʌt əʊk/ /; / /swɒmp ˈtʃest.nʌt əʊk/ (BrE)
A medium to large deciduous tree (Quercus michauxii) of moist areas in the southeastern United States, similar to the basket oak.

Examples

A swamp chestnut oak tree
A stand of swamp chestnut oaks bordered the wetland, providing shade and habitat for wildlife.
Many animals feed on the acorns produced by the swamp chestnut oak each autumn.
The leaves of the swamp chestnut oak are larger than those of other oak species in the region.
