hunt

verb
to chase and kill wild animals
Pronunciation: / hʌnt / (AmE), / hʌnt / (BrE)
to chase and kill or capture wild animals for food or sport

Examples

To hunt wild animals
Every autumn, local people hunt deer in the surrounding forests.
Lions often hunt at night when their prey is less alert.
She learned how to hunt rabbits with a bow and arrow.
She learned how to hunt with a bow and arrow from her grandfather.
Some animals hunt at night to avoid predators and competition.
The group set out early to hunt deer in the forest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poach - to illegally hunt or catch animals on someone else's land
  • synonym: search - to look for something carefully
