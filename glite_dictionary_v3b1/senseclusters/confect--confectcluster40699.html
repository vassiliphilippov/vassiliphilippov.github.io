confect

verb
make or construct skillfully
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛkt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛkt / (BrE)
to make or construct something, especially with skill or care, often used in reference to preparing food or creating something elaborate

Examples

To confect a plan
Engineers will confect a new solution to deal with the recurring traffic problems.
She managed to confect a plausible excuse for being late to the meeting.
The chef skillfully confected a series of delicate pastries for the dessert table.
