underprice

verb
sell below market value
4.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈpraɪs/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈpraɪs/ (BrE)
to sell something at a price lower than its real or market value, often intentionally

Examples

To underprice products
Competitors accused the company of attempting to underprice everyone else to gain market share.
If you underprice your services, clients may not take your work seriously.
The store decided to underprice old stock to clear its shelves quickly.
