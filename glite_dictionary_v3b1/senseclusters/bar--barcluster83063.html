bar

noun
ridge in water near a shore or river
7.0 4.53/m
Pronunciation: / b…ër / (AmE), / b…ëÀê / (BrE)
a submerged or partly submerged ridge of sand, gravel, or other material in a river or along a shore, often forming a hazard to navigation

Examples

A sand bar
As the tide receded, a sandy bar became visible along the shoreline.
Storms frequently change the location of the bar near the river's mouth.
The ship nearly hit a bar that lay hidden beneath the surface of the lake.

Synonyms

  • sandbar
    2.8 1.14/m
    a sandbar is a long narrow strip of sand shaped by currents, while a bar can be a submerged ridge of sand, gravel, or rock.
  • sandbank
    3.2 0.10/m
    a sandbank is a broad raised area of sand often seen at low tide, while a bar can be any submerged or partly submerged ridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandbar - a long, narrow sandbank, especially at the mouth of a river
