Triticum turgidum

noun
species of wheat used for bread and pasta
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪtɪkəm tɚˈdʒɪdəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪtɪkəm tɜːˈdʒɪdəm / (BrE)
A species of wheat with hard, dark-colored kernels high in gluten, commonly used for bread and pasta, and cultivated mainly in southern Russia, North Africa, and northern central North America.

Examples

Triticum turgidum wheat species
Triticum turgidum is an important crop for regions that rely on pasta and bread as dietary staples.
Farmers in North America have increased the cultivation of Triticum turgidum due to its resilience in varying climates.
Researchers are studying Triticum turgidum for its high gluten content and suitability for different culinary uses.
