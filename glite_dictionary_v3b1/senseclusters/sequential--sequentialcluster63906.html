Examples

In sequential order

Synonyms

  • consecutive
    4.0 10/m
    consecutive shows items that follow in one unbroken run, while sequential describes things placed in a set order with each part after the previous.
  • successive
    4.3 8.30/m
    successive refers to things coming one after another without interruption, while sequential stresses an arranged order where each step follows the last.
  • serial
    3.7 9.13/m
    serial describes events or items presented as a series over time, while sequential focuses on their strict order and immediate following.
  • sequent
    8.9 0.01/m
    sequent means following in order, often as a result in formal texts, while sequential means arranged in a clear, stepwise sequence.
  • step-by-step
    2.8 2.23/m
    step-by-step describes a process done in clear, separate stages, while sequential is a broader term for things arranged one after another.
