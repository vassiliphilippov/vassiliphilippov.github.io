(of a space or situation) uncomfortably small, crowded, or restrictive; making one feel trapped or enclosed.

Examples

Confining space
Many people consider airplane seats to be confining on long-haul flights.
She found the small windowless office rather confining after working in open spaces.
The confining classroom left the students eager for recess outside.

Synonyms

  • claustrophobic
    6.7 0.94/m
    claustrophobic is used for spaces that make people feel trapped and anxious, while confining means uncomfortably small or restrictive.
  • cramped
    3.2 2.59/m
    cramped is a small space with little room to move, while confining also emphasizes feeling trapped or enclosed.
  • cooped up
    5.5 0.66/m
    cooped up is a phrase about people stuck in a small place and feeling restless, while confining describes the small enclosing situation.
  • boxed in
    6.3 0.10/m
    boxed in feels like being trapped as if inside a box, while confining describes spaces that make you feel enclosed or limited.
  • incommodious
    9.9 0.01/m
    incommodious sounds formal and rare, and means inconveniently small, while confining is common and focuses on feeling trapped in a space.
  • constricted
    5.2 0.00/m
    constricted means narrowed or squeezed in width or scope, while confining suggests being enclosed and unable to move freely.
  • close
    2.6 18/m
    close is a broad word for tight or dense conditions, often meaning stuffy, while confining stresses feeling trapped by smallness or limits.
