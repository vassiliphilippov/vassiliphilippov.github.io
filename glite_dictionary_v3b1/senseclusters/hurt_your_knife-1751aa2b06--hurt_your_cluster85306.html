hurt your knife

x
sarcastic phrase about betrayal
Pronunciation: / hɝt jʊr naɪf / (AmE), / hɜːt jɔː naɪf / (BrE)
A sarcastic expression used in response to betrayal, implying that someone has metaphorically stabbed you in the back, and you mockingly ask if the act hurt their knife.

Examples

Hurt your knife back
After hearing about the gossip, I asked, 'Did my back hurt your knife?'
She messaged, 'Did my back hurt your knife?' after learning of the betrayal.
When he found out his friend had lied, he said, 'Did my back hurt your knife?' in a bitter tone.
