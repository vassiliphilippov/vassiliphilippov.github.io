hold the baby

verb
be left with responsibility for a problem
3.8
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ðə ˈbeɪ.bi / (AmE), / həʊld ðə ˈbeɪ.bi / (BrE)
to be left with sole responsibility for a difficult situation or problem, especially when others have avoided it or left

Examples

To hold the baby alone
After the project failed, Sarah was the one holding the baby.
Don't leave me holding the baby when things go wrong.
When the team quit, he was left holding the baby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carry the can - to take responsibility or blame, often unfairly
  • antonym: pass the buck - to evade responsibility by shifting it to someone else
