earthmover

noun
agent of major change
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrθˌmuːvər / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌmuːvə / (BrE)
something, such as an action, idea, or person, that causes a very large or fundamental change.

Examples

Earthmover idea
The new technology was an earthmover that transformed the entire industry.
Her innovative approach acted as an earthmover in the field of education.
Many considered the policy an earthmover because it led to sweeping reforms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: game changer - something that significantly alters a situation or outcome
  • synonym: revolutionary - a person or thing causing or relating to a complete change
  • antonym: status quo - the existing state of affairs, especially regarding social or political issues
