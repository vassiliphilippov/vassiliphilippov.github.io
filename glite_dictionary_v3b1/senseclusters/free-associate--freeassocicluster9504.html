free-associate

verb
mentally link ideas without constraint
Pronunciation: / fri əˈsoʊsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / fri əˈsəʊsiˌeɪt / (BrE)
to mentally link ideas, words, or images in a spontaneous and unconstrained way, often as a psychological technique

Examples

To free-associate ideas
During therapy, the patient was encouraged to free-associate about her childhood memories.
He found it helpful to free-associate before starting his painting sessions.
Writers sometimes free-associate to overcome creative blocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: associate - to make a mental connection between ideas or things
