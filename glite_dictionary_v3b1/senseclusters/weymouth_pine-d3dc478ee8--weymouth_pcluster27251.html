Weymouth pine

noun
a North American tall pine tree; timber source
5.5 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈweɪ.məθ paɪn/ (AmE), /ˈweɪ.məθ paɪn/ (BrE)
a tall pine tree (Pinus strobus) native to eastern North America, valued for its soft, straight-grained wood and distinctive brown bark with longitudinal fissures; also known as the eastern white pine

Examples

Tall Weymouth pine
A stand of Weymouth pine provides important habitat for many bird species.
The distinctive bark of the Weymouth pine makes it easy to identify in the forest.
The settlers used Weymouth pine to build houses and ships due to its excellent timber quality.
