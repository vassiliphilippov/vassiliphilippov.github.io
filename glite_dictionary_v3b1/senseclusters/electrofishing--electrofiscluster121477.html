electrofishing

noun
fishing with electricity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈfɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈfɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of catching fish by using electric current to temporarily stun them in water

Examples

Electrofishing survey
Scientists used electrofishing to study the fish species living in the shallow stream.
The team performed electrofishing surveys to estimate the population of trout in the lake.
Because electrofishing temporarily stuns fish, it allows researchers to collect data without harming them.
Scientists used electrofishing to study the fish population in the river without harming them.
The team prepared the electrofishing equipment before starting their survey of the lake.
Many fisheries managers rely on electrofishing as a safe way to sample aquatic life.
