Streptomyces griseus

noun
bacterium producing streptomycin
Pronunciation: / strɛpˈtoʊmaɪsiːz ˈɡraɪsiəs / (AmE), / strɛpˈtɒmɪsiːz ˈɡriːsiːəs / (BrE)
a species of soil-dwelling bacterium notable as the source of the antibiotic streptomycin

Examples

Streptomyces griseus bacterium species
Streptomyces griseus was first isolated from soil in 1943 by Selman Waksman and his colleagues.
Scientists continue to study Streptomyces griseus for its ability to produce a variety of bioactive compounds.
The discovery of Streptomyces griseus led to the development of streptomycin, an important antibiotic for tuberculosis.
