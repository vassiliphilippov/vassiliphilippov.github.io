calcinator

noun
substance for calcination
Pronunciation: / ˈkælsɪˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkælsɪneɪtə / (BrE)
a chemical or material added to a substance to promote calcination or the formation of oxides during heating

Examples

Calcinator powder
The laboratory technician added a calcinator to the mixture to speed up the reaction.
A calcinator is often necessary when converting minerals into their oxide forms.
Without a proper calcinator, the process of calcination would take much longer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flux - a substance used to promote melting or chemical change in metallurgy
