Nyquist rate

noun
minimum signal sampling rate for accurate reconstruction
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪ.kwɪst reɪt / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.kwɪst reɪt / (BrE)
the minimum sampling rate required to accurately reconstruct a sampled analog signal without introducing distortion or loss of information

Examples

The Nyquist rate frequency
Engineers use the Nyquist rate to determine the appropriate sampling frequency for digital audio recordings.
The Nyquist rate is twice the highest frequency contained in a signal.
To avoid aliasing, the signal must be sampled at a rate greater than the Nyquist rate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sampling rate - the rate at which samples are taken from a continuous signal
