periapsis

noun
point of closest approach in orbit
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛriˈæpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛriˈæpsɪs / (BrE)
(astronomy) the point in the orbit of an object around a body when it is closest to that body.

Examples

The periapsis point
Adjustments to the orbit were made to ensure the satellite's periapsis stayed above the atmosphere.
After passing periapsis, the probe began to move farther away from the asteroid.
The spacecraft will reach its periapsis in just five minutes, coming closest to the planet's surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apoapsis - the point in an orbit farthest from the body being orbited
