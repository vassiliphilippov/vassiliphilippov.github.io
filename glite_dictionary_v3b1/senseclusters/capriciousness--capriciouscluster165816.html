capriciousness

noun
tendency to act unpredictably or on impulse
Pronunciation: / kəˈprɪʃ.əs.nəs /; / kəˈprɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / kəˈprɪʃ.əs.nəs /; / kəˈprɪʃəsnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unpredictable or likely to change suddenly without reason

Examples

The capriciousness of weather
He blamed his frequent job changes on the capriciousness of his own interests.
Her capriciousness often frustrates her colleagues, who prefer a more stable work environment.
The capriciousness of the weather made it difficult to plan the outdoor event.
He attributed his constant change of hobbies to his capriciousness.
Her capriciousness at work made it hard for colleagues to predict her reactions.
The weather's capriciousness kept the sailors constantly on their toes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act on sudden urges rather than careful thought
  • antonym: predictability - the quality of being likely to happen as expected; consistency
  • antonym: steadiness - the quality of being regular or predictable in behavior
