aam

noun
old liquid measure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑm / (AmE), / ɑːm / (BrE)
an old Dutch and English unit for measuring liquids, especially wine or beer

Examples

An aam of wine
The merchant imported an aam of wine from overseas.
Records show that the brewery produced several aams of beer each year.
In the 17th century, taxes were often calculated by the aam of imported spirits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrel - a large container or unit for measuring liquids
  • synonym: gallon - a unit for measuring liquid volume
