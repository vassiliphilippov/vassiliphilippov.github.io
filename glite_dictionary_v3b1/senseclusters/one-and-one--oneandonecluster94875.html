one-and-one

noun
basketball free-throw rule: earn second shot by making the first
7.3
Pronunciation: /wʌn ənd wʌn/ /; / /hwʌn ənd hwʌn/ /; / /wən ənd wən/ (AmE), /wʌn ənd wʌn/ /; / /wɒn ənd wɒn/ /; / /wən ənd wən/ (BrE)
In basketball, a free-throw situation in which the player must make the first shot to earn a second attempt.

Examples

A one-and-one free throw
During the last quarter, she was awarded a one-and-one after being fouled.
He missed the first of the one-and-one and lost the chance for a second shot.
Practicing one-and-one situations is crucial for improving free-throw performance in tight games.
