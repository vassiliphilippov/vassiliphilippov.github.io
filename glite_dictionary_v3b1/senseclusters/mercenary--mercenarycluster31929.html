mercenary

adjective
motivated by profit rather than principles
7.3 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…úr.s…ôÀån…õr.i /; / Ààm…úÀêr.s…ôÀåner.i / (AmE), / Ààm…úÀê.s…ôn.…ôri / (BrE)
motivated mainly by money or material gain, especially at the expense of ethics or loyalty

Examples

Mercenary motives
He took on the job for entirely mercenary reasons, not because he believed in the cause.
Many critics saw the movie as a mercenary attempt to cash in on a popular trend.
She accused him of having a mercenary attitude toward relationships, caring more about money than loyalty.
His motives for helping were strictly mercenary, as he expected generous payment.
She refused to take a mercenary approach to her art, insisting that passion was more important than profit.
The decision to accept the offer was criticized as cold and mercenary.
He took a mercenary interest in the job, caring little for the company's values.
Many considered the soldiers' actions to be mercenary, since they fought only for personal profit.
The government hired mercenary forces from abroad to bolster its military.

  • synonym: materialistic - focused excessively on material possessions or money
  • antonym: patriotic - showing love and support for one's own country, not mercenary
