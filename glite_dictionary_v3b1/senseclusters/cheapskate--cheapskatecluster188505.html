cheapskate

noun
miserly or stingy person
7.0 0.65/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt ÉipÀåske…™t / (AmE), / Ààt ÉiÀêpske…™t / (BrE)
A person who is unwilling to spend money; a miserly or stingy person.

Examples

A cheapskate person
Don't be a cheapskate; let's leave a decent tip for the waiter.
My uncle is such a cheapskate that he waits for everything to go on sale before buying it.
The company was accused of being a cheapskate when it cut employee benefits.

Synonyms

  • penny pincher
    5.8 0.05/m
    a penny pincher is extremely careful and fusses over small costs, while a cheapskate is simply unwilling to spend money.
  • skinflint
    9.1 0.11/m
    a skinflint is famously miserly and refuses to share money, while a cheapskate is generally unwilling to spend.
  • tightwad
    8.7 0.30/m
    a tightwad holds on to money and avoids extra costs, while a cheapskate is unwilling to spend even small amounts.
  • scrooge
    8.5 0.04/m
    a scrooge is mean with money and withholds help, while a cheapskate focuses mainly on not spending.
  • cheapo
    9.3 0.04/m
    a cheapo sounds informal and can mean a low-quality person or thing, while a cheapskate describes a stingy person.
  • piker
    9.9 0.14/m
    a piker is often British and may mean someone who avoids risks or spends little, while a cheapskate simply avoids spending.
