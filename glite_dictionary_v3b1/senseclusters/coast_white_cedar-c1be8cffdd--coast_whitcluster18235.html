coast white cedar

noun
evergreen tree of the eastern US
Pronunciation: / koʊst waɪt ˈsiːdɚ / (AmE), / kəʊst waɪt ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a slow-growing, medium-sized evergreen tree of the eastern United States with scale-like leaves, resembling the American arborvitae

Examples

The coast white cedar tree
Conservationists are working to preserve remaining stands of coast white cedar in their native habitat.
The coast white cedar grows slowly along the marshy shores of the East Coast.
The wood of the coast white cedar is valued for its resistance to decay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: American arborvitae - a similar North American evergreen tree (Thuja occidentalis)
