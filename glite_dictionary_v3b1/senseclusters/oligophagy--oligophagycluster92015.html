oligophagy

noun
feeding on few foods
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌlɪɡəˈfeɪdʒi / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪɡəˈfeɪdʒi / (BrE)
the practice of feeding on a limited variety of food, especially by certain insects or animals

Examples

Insect oligophagy
The butterfly species is known for its oligophagy, feeding on only a few types of host plants.
Researchers studied the oligophagy of certain beetles that eat only specific tree leaves.
Because of its oligophagy, the insect population is sensitive to changes in plant availability.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monophagy - feeding on only one type of food
  • degree higher: polyphagy - feeding on many different kinds of food
