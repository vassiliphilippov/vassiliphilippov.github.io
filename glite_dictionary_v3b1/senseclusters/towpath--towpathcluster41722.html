towpath

noun
path beside a river/canal for towing boats
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊˌpæθ / (AmE), / ˈtəʊˌpɑːθ / (BrE)
a path beside a river or canal, originally used by animals or people pulling boats along the waterway

Examples

A towpath beside river
Horses once walked the towpath, pulling barges upstream.
The old towpath along the canal is now a popular cycling route.
We took a leisurely stroll down the towpath on a sunny afternoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canal - an artificial waterway for boats or irrigation
