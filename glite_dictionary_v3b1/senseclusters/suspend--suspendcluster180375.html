suspend

verb
temporarily stop or halt
4.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈspend/ (AmE), /sə.ˈspend/ (BrE)
to officially stop something from continuing or being active, usually for a short or temporary period

Examples

To suspend operations
After failing to meet safety standards, the factory had to suspend its operations temporarily.
The government will suspend aid payments pending further investigation.
The university decided to suspend classes due to the severe weather conditions.
Due to financial difficulties, the club had to suspend its weekly meetings.
The company decided to suspend operations until further notice.
The government can suspend a driver's license for repeat offenses.
Because of his actions, his license was suspended for six months.
The committee decided to suspend the membership of those involved in the incident.
The company will suspend operations until further notice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to keep going without stopping
  • synonym: halt - to bring or come to an abrupt stop
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something to a later time
  • antonym: resume - to begin again after being stopped or suspended
  • antonym: revoke - to officially cancel or take back (a decision, right, or law)
