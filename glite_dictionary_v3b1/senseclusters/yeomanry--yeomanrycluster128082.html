yeomanry

noun
class of small freeholding cultivators
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjoʊmənri / (AmE), / ˈjəʊmənri / (BrE)
A class of small landowners who cultivated their own land, especially in England; historically, a social class between the gentry and the laborers.

Examples

English yeomanry farmers
In medieval times, the yeomanry often served as skilled archers in the king's army.
Many members of the yeomanry owned their land outright, unlike tenant farmers.
The English yeomanry played a vital role in the country’s agricultural development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peasantry - class of rural agricultural laborers or small farmers
