biochemical

noun
substance in living things
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈkɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
a substance produced by or involved in the chemical processes of living organisms

Examples

A biochemical marker
Researchers analyzed the biochemical found in the plant to understand its medicinal properties.
The doctor ordered tests to measure certain biochemicals in the patient's blood.
Many diseases are caused by imbalances in important biochemicals within the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemical - a substance with a distinct molecular composition
  • synonym: metabolite - a substance formed in or necessary for metabolism
