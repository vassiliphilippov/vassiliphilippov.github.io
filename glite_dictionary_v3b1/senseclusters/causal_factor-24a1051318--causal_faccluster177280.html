causal factor

noun
element that directly influences an outcome
1.3 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔː.zəl ˈfæk.tər/ /; / /ˈkɑː.zəl ˈfæk.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.zəl ˈfæk.tə/ (BrE)
an element or condition that directly influences the outcome of an event or situation

Examples

Genetic predisposition is one important causal factor for many inherited diseases.
Increased stress is considered a likely causal factor in the rise of heart disease rates.
