causal factor

noun
element that directly influences an outcome
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔzəl ˈfæktər / (AmE), / ˈkɔːzəl ˈfæktə / (BrE)
an element or condition that directly influences the outcome of an event or situation

Examples

A causal factor in disease
Genetic predisposition is one important causal factor for many inherited diseases.
Increased stress is considered a likely causal factor in the rise of heart disease rates.
Poor nutrition has been identified as a significant causal factor in childhood development issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cause - something that brings about a result or effect
  • synonym: determinant - a factor that decisively affects the nature or outcome of something
