recommit

verb
commit again; send back
4.3 5.7
Pronunciation: /riː.kə.ˈmɪt/ /; / /rɪk.ˈɑː.mɪt/ (AmE), /riː.kə.ˈmɪt/ /; / /rɪk.ˈɑː.mɪt/ (BrE)
to send something or someone back to a previous place, authority, or state, especially in legal or procedural contexts

Examples

To recommit a case
After reviewing new evidence, the judge decided to recommit the case to the jury.
Parliament voted to recommit the bill for further consideration.
The patient was recommitted to the rehabilitation facility for further treatment.
After much discussion, the council decided to recommit the proposal to the finance committee.
If the committee raises new concerns, the chair may recommit the resolution for further debate.
The Senate voted to recommit the bill, delaying its passage until next session.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commit - to send someone to a place, especially for confinement or official care
  • synonym: refer - send (a matter) to a committee or authority for decision
