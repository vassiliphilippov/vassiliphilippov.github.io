Greenback Party

noun
US political party (1870s-1880s) opposing reduction of paper money
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrinˌbæk ˌpɑrti / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnbæk ˌpɑːti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, active in the late 19th century, which opposed reducing the amount of paper money in circulation

Examples

Greenback Party members
Members of the Greenback Party advocated for inflationary monetary policies.
The Greenback Party emerged in response to economic turmoil after the Civil War.
Though short-lived, the Greenback Party influenced future political movements in the United States.
