flakeboard

noun
engineered wood board
Pronunciation: / ˈfleɪkˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfleɪkˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a type of engineered wood made by compressing wood flakes and resin into boards

Examples

A flakeboard panel
The furniture was constructed from flakeboard to keep the project affordable.
Many modern cabinets use flakeboard instead of solid wood for the interior panels.
Because flakeboard is lightweight, it is popular for making temporary structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: particleboard - a board made from wood particles and adhesive
  • synonym: plywood - a board made from thin layers of wood glued together
