lawfully

adverb
in a way that is legal or permitted
2.5 2.22/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…îÀêf…ôli / (AmE), / Ààl…îÀêf…ôli / (BrE)
in a way that is allowed or recognized by law; legally

Examples

To act lawfully
He claimed that the land had been acquired lawfully and not by force.
Only those who participate lawfully will be eligible for the prize.
The company acted lawfully when it obtained the necessary permits.
The contract was lawfully signed by both parties.
They were lawfully married last weekend in a small ceremony.
You may only enter the premises if you are lawfully authorized.

Synonyms

  • legally
    2.8 20/m
    legally means in a way that follows the law and is common in speech and writing, while lawfully focuses on legal permission.
  • licitly
    8.2 0.03/m
    licitly is a formal word for actions allowed by law, while lawfully is a common way to say the same legal permission.
  • legitimately
    3.7 3.85/m
    legitimately often means rightly or proper in social terms, while lawfully means specifically allowed by law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illegally - in a way that is not permitted by law
  • synonym: legally - in a way that is permitted by law
