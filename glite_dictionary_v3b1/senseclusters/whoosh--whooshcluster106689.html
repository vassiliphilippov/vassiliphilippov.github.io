whoosh

noun
rushing sound of air or liquid
4.2 1.16/m
Pronunciation: / wu É / (AmE), / wuÀê É / (BrE)
a rushing sound made by a sudden movement of air, liquid, or another fluid

Examples

A loud whoosh
I heard a whoosh as the rocket shot into the sky.
The loud whoosh of the passing train startled the children on the platform.
There was a sudden whoosh when the air conditioning turned on.

Synonyms

  • swoosh
    5.8 0.17/m
    swoosh is a soft, fast rushing sound of moving air or water, while whoosh describes a similar sudden rush.
  • whizzle
    9.8 0.00/m
    whizzle is a sharp whizzing or hissing noise from a quick rush of air, while whoosh is a fuller, deeper rush.
  • whiz
    6.4 0.26/m
    whiz is a high buzzing or hissing sound from something flying fast, while whoosh is a broader fluid rush noise.
  • voom
    9.9 0.19/m
    voom is a loud engine-like rushing noise from vehicles, while whoosh refers to a sudden rush of air or liquid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swish - a soft rushing or swirling sound
