relocation

noun
the act of moving people to a new place
1.7 3.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.loʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.ləʊˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of moving people or a group to a new place to live, especially after being forced to leave their home

Examples

Forced relocation
After years of conflict, relocation became necessary for thousands of families.
The relocation of the entire village was completed within a few months.
The government ordered the relocation of the residents after the flooding destroyed their homes.

Related Terms
