relocation

noun
the act of moving people to a new place
4.0 0.83/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê.lo äÀàke…™. É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê.l…ô äÀàke…™. É…ôn / (BrE)
the act of moving people or a group to a new place to live, especially after being forced to leave their home

Examples

Forced relocation
After years of conflict, relocation became necessary for thousands of families.
The relocation of the entire village was completed within a few months.
The government ordered the relocation of the residents after the flooding destroyed their homes.

Synonyms

  • resettlement
    3.9 1.58/m
    resettlement moves whole communities to new towns or countries, while relocation focuses on moving groups after forced displacement.
  • rehousing
    3.9 0.01/m
    rehousing provides people with new homes, often locally for displaced families, while relocation is the act of moving them to a new place to live.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resettlement - the process of settling people in a new place after being forced to leave their previous home
