sure

adjective
confident; having no doubt
1.0 434/m
Pronunciation: /  É är / (AmE), /  É…îÀê / (BrE)
having or feeling no doubt or uncertainty; confident and certain of something

Examples

Sure about that
Are you sure this is the right address?
He was not sure how to respond to the unexpected question.
She sounded absolutely sure of her decision during the meeting.

Synonyms

  • certain
    1.0 43/m
    certain is firm and used in formal statements, while sure is more common and used in everyday speech to mean no doubt.
  • convinced
    2.8 20/m
    convinced expresses being strongly persuaded by evidence or argument, while sure is a general feeling of no doubt.
  • confident
    2.5 39/m
    confident shows inner self-assurance or skill in facing tasks, while sure simply means having no doubt.
  • assured
    4.1 0.00/m
    assured often sounds formal and can mean guaranteed or very confident, while sure is the common everyday word for no doubt.
