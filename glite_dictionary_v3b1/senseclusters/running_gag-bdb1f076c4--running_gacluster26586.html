running gag

noun
joke repeated for humorous effect
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnɪŋ ɡæɡ / (AmE), / ˈrʌnɪŋ ɡaɡ / (BrE)
A joke or humorous remark that is repeated throughout a conversation, show, or event, often becoming funnier through repetition.

Examples

A running gag joke
The director included a running gag involving a missing umbrella in every episode of the series.
Their family dinners usually featured at least one running gag about Uncle Bob's fishing stories.
There was a running gag in the office about the boss's terrible coffee-making skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inside joke - A private joke understood only by a specific group
