Strait of Malacca

proper noun
major strait between Malay Peninsula and Sumatra
3.9
Pronunciation: / streɪt əv məˈlækə / (AmE), / streɪt əv məˈlækə / (BrE)
A narrow stretch of water between the Malay Peninsula and the island of Sumatra, serving as a major maritime route connecting the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

Examples

The Strait of Malacca route
Many cargo ships travel through the Strait of Malacca on their way between Europe and East Asia.
Pirates once posed a frequent threat to vessels in the Strait of Malacca.
The Strait of Malacca is one of the busiest shipping lanes in the world.
