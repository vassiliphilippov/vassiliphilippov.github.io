deluge

verb
overwhelm with a great quantity
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛludʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɛljuːdʒ / (BrE)
To overwhelm someone with a large quantity of something, especially tasks, requests, or information.

Examples

To deluge with work
After the news broke, the press office was deluged with calls from journalists.
Customers deluged the company with complaints about the faulty product.
She worried that her new assistant would be deluged with paperwork during her absence.
