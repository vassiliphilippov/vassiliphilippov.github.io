amyotrophic lateral sclerosis

noun
progressive neurological disease; ALS
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.mi.oʊˌtroʊ.fɪk ˈlæt̬.ə.rəl skləˈroʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæm.i.əˌtrɒf.ɪk ˈlæt.ər.əl sklɪəˈrəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
A progressive neurological disease characterized by the degeneration of motor neurons in the brain and spinal cord, leading to muscle weakness and atrophy.

Examples

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis disease
Many people know amyotrophic lateral sclerosis by its abbreviation, ALS.
The diagnosis of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis was devastating for the entire family.
There is ongoing research to find an effective treatment for amyotrophic lateral sclerosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ALS - abbreviation of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis
  • synonym: Lou Gehrig's disease - another name for amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, especially in the US
