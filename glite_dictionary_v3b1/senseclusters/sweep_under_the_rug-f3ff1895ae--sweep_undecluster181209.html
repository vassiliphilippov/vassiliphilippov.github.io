sweep under the rug

verb
conceal a problem or mistake
2.8
Pronunciation: / swip ˌʌndər ðə rʌɡ /; / swip ˈʌndɚ ðə rʌg / (AmE), / swiːp ˌʌndə ðə rʌɡ /; / swiːp ˈʌndə ðə rʌg / (BrE)
to try to hide a problem or mistake instead of dealing with it openly

Examples

To sweep under the rug a problem
Instead of addressing the issue, management chose to sweep it under the rug and hope people would forget.
Many companies try to sweep under the rug any complaints they receive from customers.
She refused to sweep her mistakes under the rug and admitted what she had done.
Every time an issue comes up, the management tries to sweep it under the rug.
Instead of addressing financial irregularities, they swept the problem under the rug for years.
We can't just sweep our differences under the rug and pretend everything is fine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bring to light - to make something known that was previously hidden or secret
  • synonym: cover up - to hide the truth about something, especially a mistake or crime
  • antonym: face up to - to confront or deal with a problem
