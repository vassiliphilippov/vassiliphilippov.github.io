Roman Church

noun
the Christian Church led by the Pope in Rome
3.4 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / Ààro äm…ôn t É…ùÀêt É / (AmE), / Ààr…ô äm…ôn t É…úÀêt É / (BrE)
the Christian Church based in the Vatican, presided over by the Pope and a hierarchy of bishops; also known as the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

The Roman Church leader
He converted to the Roman Church after years of spiritual searching.
Many global traditions can trace their roots back to the Roman Church.

Synonyms

  • Roman Catholic Church
    3.7 1.57/m
    Roman Catholic Church is the common formal name for the Vatican-led global church, while Roman Church is an alternative or shorter name for it.
  • Church of Rome
    4.7 0.09/m
    Church of Rome stresses the Pope's seat in Rome and historical identity, while Roman Church is the general name for that Vatican-led church.
  • Western Church
    6.5 0.01/m
    Western Church refers to the Latin-rite branch of Christianity centered on Rome, while Roman Church names the Vatican-led Catholic Church.
