burster

noun
object emitting bursts
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrstər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːstə / (BrE)
an astronomical object or system that emits sudden, intense bursts of radiation, especially X-rays or gamma rays

Examples

X-ray burster
Astronomers observed a powerful burster in a distant galaxy emitting regular X-ray flashes.
The discovery of a new burster helped scientists study the behavior of neutron stars.
Each time the burster released energy, telescopes recorded a sharp increase in radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pulsar - a highly magnetized rotating neutron star that emits beams of electromagnetic radiation
  • synonym: quasar - an extremely luminous active galactic nucleus powered by a supermassive black hole
