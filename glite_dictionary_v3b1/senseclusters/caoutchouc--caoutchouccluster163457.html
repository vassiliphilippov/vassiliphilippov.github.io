caoutchouc

noun
natural elastic material from tree latex
Pronunciation: / kaʊˈtʃuːk / (AmE), / kaʊˈtʃuːk / (BrE)
a natural elastic material obtained from the latex of certain trees, especially rubber trees, and used in making various products such as tires and waterproof goods

Examples

A caoutchouc strip
A strip of caoutchouc was used to seal the leaking pipe temporarily.
Many early waterproof garments were made with caoutchouc before the advent of synthetic materials.
The scientist explained how caoutchouc is processed from the sap of rubber trees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rubber - a tough elastic polymeric substance made from the latex of a tropical plant or synthetically
