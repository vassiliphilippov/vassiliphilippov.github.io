rotation

noun
mathematical transformation involving turning around a point
Pronunciation: / roʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / rəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A transformation that turns a figure or coordinate system around a fixed point, usually the origin, by a certain angle.

Examples

A rotation of a shape
In three dimensions, a rotation can occur about any axis passing through the origin.
The rotation of the coordinate axes changes the values of the variables but preserves distances.
This equation describes a rotation of 90 degrees about the origin in the plane.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reflection - a transformation flipping a figure over a line
  • synonym: rotation - the general act of turning around a center point
  • antonym: translation - a transformation moving a figure without rotating it
