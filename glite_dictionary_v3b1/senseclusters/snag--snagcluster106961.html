snag

noun
sharp projection or protuberance
Pronunciation: / snæɡ / (AmE), / snæɡ / (BrE)
a projection, sharp point, or rough outgrowth, especially one on a tree, piece of wood, or underwater obstacle

Examples

Wooden snag
Be careful when walking; there is a snag on that log that could rip your clothes.
Old trees often have a snag sticking out where branches once grew.
The diver injured his hand on a hidden snag in the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spike - a sharply pointed projection
  • degree lower: stub - a short, thick protruding part
