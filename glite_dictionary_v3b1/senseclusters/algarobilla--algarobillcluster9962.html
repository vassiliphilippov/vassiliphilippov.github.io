algarobilla

noun
mesquite pod used in tanning and dyeing
Pronunciation: / ˌælɡəroʊˈbiːjə / (AmE), / ˌalɡəˌrəʊˈbiːjə / (BrE)
A pod from certain South American mesquite trees, used in tanning and dyeing for its high tannin content.

Examples

The algarobilla pod
Because of its astringent properties, algarobilla is still used by some artisans today.
The tannins in algarobilla make it a valuable resource for leather production.
Traditional dyeing processes in South America often use crushed algarobilla pods.
