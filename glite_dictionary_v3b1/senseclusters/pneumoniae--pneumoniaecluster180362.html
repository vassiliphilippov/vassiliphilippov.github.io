pneumoniae

noun
bacteria causing pneumonia
9.8
Pronunciation: / nuːˈmoʊniˌiː / (AmE), / njuːˈməʊniˌiː / (BrE)
a Latin term used in scientific names for bacteria associated with pneumonia or lung infections

Examples

Streptococcus pneumoniae bacteria
Streptococcus pneumoniae is a leading cause of bacterial pneumonia worldwide.
Doctors often test for Klebsiella pneumoniae in patients with severe lung infections.
The vaccine protects against several strains of pneumoniae bacteria.
The bacterium Klebsiella pneumoniae is a common cause of hospital-acquired infections.
In scientific names, pneumoniae indicates a connection to pneumonia or lung disease.
Researchers studied several strains labeled with the epithet pneumoniae to understand their effects on the lungs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteria - microscopic organisms, some of which cause disease
  • synonym: pneumococcus - a type of bacteria that causes pneumonia
  • synonym: pneumonia - a serious lung infection causing inflammation and fluid in the lungs
