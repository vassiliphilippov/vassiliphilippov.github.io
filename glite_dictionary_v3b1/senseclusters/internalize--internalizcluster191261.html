internalize

verb
make (an idea or belief) part of oneself
2.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɝːnəˌlaɪz /; / ɪnˈtɜrnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜː.nə.laɪz /; / ɪnˈtɜːnəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make a belief, attitude, or value part of one's own thinking or character, usually by accepting it as true or applying it personally

Examples

To internalize beliefs
After years of therapy, he began to internalize a more positive self-image.
Children often internalize the values of their parents over time.
She found it hard to internalize the criticism and continued to repeat the same mistakes.
Children often internalise what they see in their parents' behaviour.
He gradually internalised the lessons he learned from his failures.
It took time for her to internalise the company's core values.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - take in or assimilate (information, ideas, or experience)
  • antonym: externalise - express or show (a feeling or thought) outwardly
  • antonym: externalize - to attribute (one's thoughts or feelings) to external objects or sources
