Apodiformes

noun
order of birds: swifts, hummingbirds
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæpədəˈfɔːrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌapədɪˈfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
An order of birds that includes swifts and hummingbirds, characterized by small size and fast, agile flight.

Examples

Order Apodiformes birds
Hummingbirds belong to the order Apodiformes, known for their rapid wing movement.
Swifts, which can spend months in flight, are also classified under Apodiformes.
There are hundreds of species within the Apodiformes, ranging from tiny hummingbirds to agile swifts.
