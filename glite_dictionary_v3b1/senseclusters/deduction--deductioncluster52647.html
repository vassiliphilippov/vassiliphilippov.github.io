deduction

noun
subtraction of a part from a whole, often money
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
an amount that is subtracted from a total, especially from money, price, or income

Examples

Tax deduction
After taxes and insurance, the total deduction from his paycheck was quite high.
The company made a deduction for his missed days of work.
There will be a deduction for late submission of the assignment.
A seasonal deduction on winter clothing attracted more shoppers to the store.
The customer received a significant deduction for buying in bulk.
The price tag showed a 15% deduction during the clearance sale.
After all deductions, her taxable income was significantly lower.
The new law increased the standard deduction for individuals and families.
You may claim a deduction for charitable donations on your tax return.

Related Terms

  • antonym: addition - the process or result of adding something

  • synonym: discount - a reduction from the usual cost of something
  • synonym: exemption - an amount of income not subject to tax
  • antonym: markup - an increase on the price of goods
  • synonym: subtraction - the process of taking one quantity away from another
