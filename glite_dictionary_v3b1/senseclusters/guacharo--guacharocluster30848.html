guacharo

noun
nocturnal fruit-eating bird of South America
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡwɑː.tʃɑːˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡwɑː.tʃɑː.rəʊ / (BrE)
A nocturnal, fruit-eating bird of northern South America, known for its fatty young that yield oil used as a butter substitute.

Examples

Young guacharos oil
Early explorers collected oil from young guacharos to use in cooking.
The guacharo nests in caves throughout northern South America.
The eerie calls of the guacharo echo through the caverns at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oilbird - another common name for the same nocturnal, fruit-eating bird
