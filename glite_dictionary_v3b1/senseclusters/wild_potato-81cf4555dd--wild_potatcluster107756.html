wild potato

noun
perennial plant with potato-like tubers
2.4
Pronunciation: / waɪld pəˈteɪtoʊ / (AmE), / waɪld pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a perennial plant of the southwestern United States and Mexico with small, pale brown to cream-colored tubers that resemble potatoes

Examples

The wild potato plant
Researchers are studying the genetic diversity of the wild potato to improve cultivated potato varieties.
Some indigenous communities traditionally harvested wild potato tubers as a source of food.
The wild potato grows in arid regions of the southwestern United States and Mexico.
