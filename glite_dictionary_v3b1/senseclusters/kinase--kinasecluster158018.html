kinase

noun
enzyme that transfers phosphate groups
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪˌneɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɪˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that facilitates the transfer of phosphate groups to other molecules, often activating or deactivating enzymes and other proteins

Examples

A protein kinase
A protein kinase plays a vital role in cell signaling pathways.
Scientists are studying a new kinase inhibitor as a potential cancer treatment.
The activation of the enzyme requires phosphorylation by a specific kinase.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phosphatase - an enzyme that removes phosphate groups from molecules
