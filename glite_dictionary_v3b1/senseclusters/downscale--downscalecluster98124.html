downscale

verb
reduce size or scope
4.3
Pronunciation: /daʊn.ˈskeɪl/ /; / /ˈdaʊn.skeɪl/ (AmE), /daʊn.ˈskeɪl/ /; / /ˈdaʊn.skeɪl/ (BrE)
to reduce the size, scope, amount, or quality of something, often to cut costs or make it more suitable or affordable

Examples

To downscale a company
Facing shrinking demand, the company decided to downscale its product line to focus on core models.
After budget cuts, they are downscaling operations at three regional warehouses this quarter.
The management team downscaled the marketing department before seeking new investment opportunities.
The company decided to downscale its operations to focus on its most profitable products.
They had to downscale their wedding plans due to budget constraints.
The images were downscaled to fit the website's requirements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decrease - to become or make less
  • synonym: downsize - to make something smaller, especially by reducing staff or output
  • antonym: expand - to increase in size, number, or scope; opposite of reduce or downscale
  • synonym: reduce - to make something smaller in size, amount, or degree
  • antonym: upscale - to increase the size, amount, or quality of something
