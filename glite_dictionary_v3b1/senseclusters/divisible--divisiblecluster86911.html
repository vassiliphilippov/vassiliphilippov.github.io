divisible

adjective
able to be divided
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪzə bəl / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪzɪbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being divided into separate parts or groups; especially able to be divided by another number without leaving a remainder.

Examples

Divisible by numbers
A nation must not be divisible if it seeks lasting unity.
The land was easily divisible among the heirs.
Twelve is divisible by both three and four.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indivisible - not able to be divided or separated
