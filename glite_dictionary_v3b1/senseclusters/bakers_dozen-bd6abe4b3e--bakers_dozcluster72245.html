baker's dozen

noun
thirteen; group of thirteen
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪkərz ˈdʌzən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkəz ˈdʌz(ə)n /; / ˈbeɪkəz ˈdʌzən / (BrE)
thirteen; a group of thirteen items, especially as traditionally supplied by bakers instead of twelve

Examples

A baker's dozen of eggs
The shopkeeper tossed an extra apple into the bag, making it a baker's dozen.
To be safe, he ordered a baker's dozen bagels for the meeting.
Traditionally, a baker's dozen refers to thirteen items rather than twelve.
Every Saturday, she buys a baker's dozen of muffins for her family.
The shop gives a baker's dozen when you purchase a dozen bagels.
We picked up a baker's dozen of fresh rolls from the bakery.
