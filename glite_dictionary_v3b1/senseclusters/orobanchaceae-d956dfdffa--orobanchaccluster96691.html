Orobanchaceae

noun
a family of parasitic flowering plants
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːrəˌbænˈkeɪsiː /; / ˌɔːrəˌbænˈkeɪsiːˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɒrəˌbaŋˈkiːsiː /; / ˌɒrəbænˈkeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a family of mainly leafless, brown or yellow, parasitic herbs, commonly known as the broomrape family

Examples

Orobanchaceae parasitic plants
Members of the Orobanchaceae are known for their parasitic lifestyle on other plants.
Several species in the Orobanchaceae lack chlorophyll and rely entirely on their hosts for nutrients.
The Orobanchaceae are studied for their unique interactions with agricultural crops.
Botanists have debated whether Orobanchaceae should be classified in the order Scrophulariales.
Several members of the Orobanchaceae can be found in temperate regions worldwide.
The Orobanchaceae family includes many parasitic plant species that lack chlorophyll.
