sail foam

noun
foam created by moving boats
Pronunciation: / seɪl foʊm / (AmE), / seɪl fəʊm / (BrE)
Foam that forms on the surface of water as a result of a boat's movement, especially under sail.

Examples

Sail foam on water
Children watched the sail foam dance on the surface beside their small sailboat.
The sail foam glimmered in the morning light as the yacht sped across the bay.
Waves and sail foam trailed behind the boat as it cut through the water.
