complex conjugate

noun
the paired number of a complex number with the sign of the imaginary part reversed
8.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpleks ˈkɑːn.dʒə.gət/ /; / /ˈkɑːm.pleks ˈkɑːn.dʒə.geɪt/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpleks ˈkɒn.dʒə.gət/ /; / /ˈkɒm.pleks ˈkɒn.dʒə.geɪt/ (BrE)
either of a pair of complex numbers having identical real parts and imaginary parts that differ only in sign; for example, for a + bi, its complex conjugate is a - bi.

Examples

A pair of complex conjugates
In the equation, the roots are a pair of complex conjugates.
Multiplying a complex number by its complex conjugate results in a real number.
The complex conjugate of 3 + 4i is 3 - 4i.
