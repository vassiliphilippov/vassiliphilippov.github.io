retranscribe

verb
to arrange music again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːtrænˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌriːtrænˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to make a new transcription or arrangement of a musical composition for different instruments or voices

Examples

To retranscribe music
The composer decided to retranscribe the piano sonata for a full string quartet this season.
After hearing the new instruments, she chose to retranscribe the piece to better suit their sound.
He was asked to retranscribe the choral work for a solo piano performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to adapt a musical composition for performance by different instruments or voices
  • synonym: transcribe - to write or arrange music for a different instrument or voice
