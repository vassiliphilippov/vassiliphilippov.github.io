march out

verb
to leave a place in formation, usually into open ground
Pronunciation: / mɑrtʃ aʊt / (AmE), / mɑːtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
(of troops or people) to leave a confined or sheltered place in a marching formation, especially entering open ground from a narrow or enclosed area.

Examples

To march out in rows
After the ceremony, the graduates marched out of the hall in neat rows.
The soldiers marched out of the canyon into the open plain.
With practiced discipline, the troops marched out from behind cover to take their positions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debouch - to emerge from a narrow or confined place into open ground, especially of troops
