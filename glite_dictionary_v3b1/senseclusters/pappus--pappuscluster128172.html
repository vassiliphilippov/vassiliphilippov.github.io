pappus

noun
botanical structure aiding seed dispersal
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpæp.əs / (AmE), / ˈpap.əs / (BrE)
a tuft of fine hairs, bristles, or scales on the calyx of certain plants, especially in the daisy family, which aids in the dispersal of seeds

Examples

The pappus of seeds
Botanists use the structure of the pappus to help identify different species within the daisy family.
Each thistle seed is topped with a white, feathery pappus that helps it float on the breeze.
The pappus of a dandelion makes it easy for the wind to carry its seeds far away.
