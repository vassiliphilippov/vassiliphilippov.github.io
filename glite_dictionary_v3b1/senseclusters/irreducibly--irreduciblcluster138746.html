irreducibly

adverb
in an indivisible way
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈduːsəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdjuːsəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be made simpler, divided, or reduced further; fundamentally or essentially

Examples

An irreducibly complex molecule
The molecule is irreducibly complex and cannot be separated into simpler substances.
Some problems in mathematics are irreducibly difficult and cannot be simplified further.
The concept is irreducibly linked to the foundation of the theory.
The concept is irreducibly complex and cannot be explained in simpler terms.
Some problems are irreducibly difficult, no matter how much you try to simplify them.
The two issues are irreducibly linked and must be addressed together.
The two theories are irreducibly different and cannot be combined.
Some problems are irreducibly complex and have no simple solution.
Her identity is irreducibly tied to her cultural background.
