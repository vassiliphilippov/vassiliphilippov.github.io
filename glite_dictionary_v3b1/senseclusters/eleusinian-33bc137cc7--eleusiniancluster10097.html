Eleusinian

noun
initiate of Greek rites
Pronunciation: / ˌɛljuˈsɪniən / (AmE), / ˌɛljuːˈsɪniən / (BrE)
a person who has been initiated into the secret religious rites of the Eleusinian Mysteries in ancient Greece

Examples

An Eleusinian initiate
As an Eleusinian, he was sworn to secrecy about the sacred ceremonies he attended.
The ancient Eleusinians believed the rites brought them closer to the gods.
Becoming an Eleusinian was considered a great honor in classical Athens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: initiate - a person who has been admitted to a secret or special group
  • antonym: outsider - someone not belonging to a particular group or activity
