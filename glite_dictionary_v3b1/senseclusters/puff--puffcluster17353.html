puff

noun
short, light gust of air
Pronunciation: / pʌf / (AmE), / pʌf / (BrE)
a short, light burst or gust of air, smoke, or breath

Examples

A puff of smoke
A puff of smoke drifted from the chimney.
A sudden puff of wind blew the papers off the table.
She blew out the candle with a single puff.
After running, she paused for a puff before continuing.
He felt a puff of warm air as the door opened.
The child blew out the candles with a single puff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blast - a strong rush of air
  • antonym: gasp - a sudden, short intake of breath
  • degree higher: gust - a strong, brief rush of wind
