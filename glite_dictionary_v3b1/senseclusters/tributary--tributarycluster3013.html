tributary

adjective
(of a stream) flowing into a larger stream
6.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɪb.jə.tər.iː/ /; / /ˈtrɪb.jʊ.tər.iː/ (AmE), /ˈtrɪb.jʊ.tə.riː/ /; / /ˈtrɪb.jə.tə.riː/ (BrE)
(of a stream or river) flowing into a larger stream or river rather than directly into the sea.

Examples

Tributary stream
A tributary channel carries water from the hills to the main waterway.
Several tributary creeks feed into the larger river during the rainy season.
The tributary stream joined the main river just north of the village.
