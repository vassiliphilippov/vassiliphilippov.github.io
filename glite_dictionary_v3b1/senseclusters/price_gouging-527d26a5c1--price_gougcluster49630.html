price gouging

noun
charging unfairly high prices when alternatives are unavailable
9.6 5.3
Pronunciation: /praɪs ˈgaʊ.dʒɪŋ/ (AmE), /praɪs ˈgaʊ.dʒɪŋ/ (BrE)
the practice of charging customers excessively high prices, especially when no alternative sources are available, often considered unethical or illegal

Examples

Price gouging during crisis
After the hurricane, several stores were accused of price gouging on bottled water and basic supplies.
Authorities warned that price gouging during the crisis would result in severe penalties.
Laws against price gouging are intended to protect consumers during emergencies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fair pricing - charging reasonable and justifiable prices
  • synonym: overcharging - the act of charging too much for something
