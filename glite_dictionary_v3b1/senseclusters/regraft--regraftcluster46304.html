regraft

verb
to graft again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɡræft / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɡrɑːft / (BrE)
to graft again, especially a plant or tissue after an initial attempt has failed or not taken.

Examples

To regraft tissue
After the first skin transplant failed, the doctors decided to regraft the tissue.
Gardeners sometimes need to regraft a branch if the initial graft does not take.
The surgeon explained that it was necessary to regraft the area to ensure proper healing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: graft - to join a piece of plant or tissue to another so that they grow together
