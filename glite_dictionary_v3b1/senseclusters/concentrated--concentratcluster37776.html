concentrated

adjective
intensely focused or directed
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsəntreɪtɪd /; / ˈkɒnsənˌtreɪtɪd / (BrE)
showing or involving intense focus, effort, or attention directed toward a particular task or purpose

Examples

Concentrated effort
A concentrated effort by the team led to a successful product launch.
His concentrated gaze made it clear he was deeply engaged in the conversation.
She gave the project her concentrated attention, ignoring all distractions.
He read the book with concentrated attention, barely noticing the noise around him.
The team made a concentrated effort to finish the project before the deadline.
This solution is too concentrated—you need to add water to dilute it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diluted - made thinner or weaker by adding something; opposite of concentrated
  • antonym: distracted - unable to concentrate or focus
  • synonym: focused - directing attention or effort to a specific task
  • synonym: intense - having or showing strong force, degree, or focus; similar to concentrated (of effort or feeling)
