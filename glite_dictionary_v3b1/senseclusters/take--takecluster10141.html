Synonyms

  • choose
    1.0 276/m
    choose means to decide between options calmly, while take often means choosing or buying one item in a shop or moment.
  • pick
    1.0 128/m
    pick suggests selecting carefully from a group, while take is more general and can mean accepting or buying something quickly.
  • go with
    1.1 6.23/m
    go with is an informal choice people use in speech, while take can be used both for choosing and buying directly.
  • select
    2.5 99/m
    select is a formal, careful choice used in writing or testing, while take is more casual and used in speech or shops.
  • opt
    3.2 21/m
    opt means to make a deliberate choice, often with 'for' or 'to', while take is a simple choice or purchase in daily speech.
  • take your pick
    3.6 0.66/m
    take your pick invites someone to choose freely from options, while take usually means personally choosing or buying a single item.
