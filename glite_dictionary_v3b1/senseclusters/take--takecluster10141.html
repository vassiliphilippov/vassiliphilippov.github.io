take

verb
buy or select something
1.7
Pronunciation: / teɪk / (AmE), / teɪk / (BrE)
to choose, select, or buy something from among options

Examples

To take two apples
At the market, he said he would take two kilograms of apples.
I'll take a slice of pie, please.
She decided to take the blue dress instead of the red one.
He always takes the blue marker when given a choice.
She couldn't decide which dessert to take from the tray.
You may take whichever seat you prefer in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buy - to acquire something in exchange for money
  • synonym: choose - to select from a number of possibilities
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or choose
  • synonym: select - to carefully choose as being the best or most suitable
