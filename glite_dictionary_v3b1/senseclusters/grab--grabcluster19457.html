grab

verb
seize power or rights unlawfully
Pronunciation: / ɡræb / (AmE), / ɡræb / (BrE)
to obtain or seize something, especially power, money, or rights, in an illegal or unscrupulous way

Examples

To grab power
Corrupt officials tried to grab as much power as they could after the coup.
He managed to grab control of the organization by exploiting a loophole in the rules.
Several companies attempted to grab a larger share of the market through underhanded tactics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appropriate - to take something for one's own use, typically without permission
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly or forcibly
