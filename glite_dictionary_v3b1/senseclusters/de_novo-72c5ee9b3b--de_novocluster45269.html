de novo

adverb
from the beginning; anew
9.9
Pronunciation: / də ˈnoʊvoʊ / (AmE), / deɪ ˈnəʊvəʊ / (BrE)
starting from the beginning; anew, especially used in legal, scientific, or technical contexts.

Examples

De novo review
If the court finds errors, the case may be tried de novo.
Researchers grew the tissue de novo from stem cells in the laboratory.
The experiment was repeated de novo to verify the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afresh - again, in a new or different way
  • synonym: anew - again, in a new way or from the beginning
