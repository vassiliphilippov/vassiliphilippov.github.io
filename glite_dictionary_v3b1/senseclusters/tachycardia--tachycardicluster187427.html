tachycardia

noun
abnormally rapid heartbeat
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtækiˈkɑrdiə / (AmE), / ˌtækɪˈkɑːdiə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the heart beats unusually fast, typically over 100 beats per minute in an adult

Examples

Fast tachycardia beats
High levels of stress or caffeine can sometimes trigger temporary tachycardia.
The doctor monitored her tachycardia closely during the emergency.
The patient was diagnosed with tachycardia after his heart rate exceeded 120 beats per minute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bradycardia - slow heart rate (under 60 beats per minute)
