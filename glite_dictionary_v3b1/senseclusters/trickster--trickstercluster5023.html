trickster

noun
someone who plays tricks or pranks
2.7 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɪk.stər/ (AmE), /ˈtrɪk.stə/ (BrE)
a person who deceives, cheats, or plays tricks on others, often for fun or advantage

Examples

A clever trickster
He always saw himself as a harmless trickster, making his friends laugh with his antics.
In many folktales, the trickster outwits his enemies using clever schemes.
The carnival was full of tricksters pulling pranks on unsuspecting visitors.
In many fairy tales, a clever trickster outwits the more powerful characters.
She warned her son not to trust any unknown trickster who made big promises.
The trickster convinced the villagers that he could turn stones into gold.

  • antonym: honest person - a person who is truthful and does not deceive others
