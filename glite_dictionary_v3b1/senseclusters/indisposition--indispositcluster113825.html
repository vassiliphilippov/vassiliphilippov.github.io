indisposition

noun
slight reluctance or unwillingness
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
a slight lack of willingness or readiness to do something; reluctance

Examples

Indisposition to help
Despite her indisposition to travel, she agreed to attend the conference.
He showed a marked indisposition to participate in the group project.
Julia's indisposition to speak openly made it difficult to resolve the disagreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eagerness - keen or enthusiastic willingness to do something
  • synonym: reluctance - unwillingness or disinclination to do something
