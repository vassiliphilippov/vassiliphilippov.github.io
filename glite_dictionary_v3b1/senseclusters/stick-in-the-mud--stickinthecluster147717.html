stick-in-the-mud

noun
old-fashioned, unadventurous person
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪk ɪn ðə mʌd / (AmE), / ˈstɪk ɪn ðə mʌd / (BrE)
a person who is old-fashioned, set in their ways, and does not like to try new things or accept change

Examples

A stick-in-the-mud person
Don't be such a stick-in-the-mud—come join the dance with us!
Her stick-in-the-mud attitude made it difficult to introduce any changes at work.
They called him a stick-in-the-mud because he refused to try the new technology.
Don't be such a stick-in-the-mud—come join us at the party!
Everyone called him a stick-in-the-mud because he never wanted to travel.
My brother can be a real stick-in-the-mud when it comes to trying new foods.
