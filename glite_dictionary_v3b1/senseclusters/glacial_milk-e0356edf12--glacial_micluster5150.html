glacial milk

noun
cloudy meltwater with rock flour from glaciers
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃəl mɪlk / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪsiəl mɪlk / (BrE)
Cloudy or milky meltwater from glaciers, suspended with fine rock particles known as rock flour.

Examples

Glacial milk water
Hikers noticed the river's turquoise color, a result of suspended particles in glacial milk.
Scientists collected samples of glacial milk to study the sediment content from retreating glaciers.
The stream was filled with glacial milk, giving it a cloudy, pale appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meltwater - water formed by the melting of snow and ice, especially from a glacier
