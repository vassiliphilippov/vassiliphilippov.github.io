acerbically

adverb
in a sour or sharp way
Pronunciation: / əˈsɜrbɪkli / (AmE), / əˈsɜːbɪkli / (BrE)
in a sharp, bitter, or harsh way, especially when criticizing or speaking

Examples

To speak acerbically
The critic wrote acerbically about the new play, sparing no harsh words for the director.
He responded acerbically to the suggestion, making everyone in the meeting uncomfortable.
Her comments were delivered acerbically, leaving the team feeling stung by her tone.
The journalist acerbically questioned the politician's motives during the interview.
She responded acerbically to the unfair criticism from her colleague.
His reviews are often written acerbically, making them both entertaining and biting.
