perpendicularity

noun
quality of being perpendicular
Pronunciation: / pərˌpɛndɪkjəˈlærɪti /; / pərˌpɛndɪkjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / pəˌpɛndɪkjʊˈlærɪti /; / pəˌpɛndɪkjʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being perpendicular (at right angles), especially between lines, planes, or surfaces

Examples

Check perpendicularity of beams
Engineers check the perpendicularity of the beams during construction.
The perpendicularity of the walls ensures the building is stable.
The artist admired the perpendicularity of the mountains rising from the ground.
Carpenters often use a level to check for perpendicularity in their constructions.
The perpendicularity of the two walls was confirmed by the architect’s measuring tools.
The theorem relies on the perpendicularity of the lines to each other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orthogonality - state of being at right angles; independence (especially in mathematics)
  • antonym: parallelism - the quality of being parallel to a given line or plane
  • synonym: perpendicular - at an angle of 90° to a given line or surface
