hypercompetitive

adjective
extremely competitive
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərkəmˈpɛtətɪv / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəkəmˈpɛtɪtɪv /; / ˌhaɪpəkəmˈpɛtətɪv / (BrE)
extremely competitive; having or showing a very high level of competition or desire to win

Examples

A hypercompetitive market
The smartphone industry is now so hypercompetitive that companies must release new models every year.
In a hypercompetitive job market, candidates need to constantly update their skills to stay relevant.
The hypercompetitive nature of online retail has driven prices lower and increased pressure on suppliers.
The hypercompetitive environment at the company pushed employees to work long hours.
His hypercompetitive nature sometimes made it hard for him to enjoy friendly games.
In today's hypercompetitive market, businesses must constantly innovate to stay ahead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressive - behaving in a forceful or determined way to succeed
  • degree lower: competitive - involving or based on competition
  • antonym: cooperative - involving mutual assistance rather than competition
  • synonym: cutthroat - fiercely competitive and ruthless
