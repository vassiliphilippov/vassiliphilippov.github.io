Ottoman dynasty

noun
ruling family of the Ottoman Empire
Pronunciation: / ˈɑdəˌmæn ˈdɪnəsti / (AmE), / ˈɒtəmən ˈdɪnəsti / (BrE)
the Turkish royal family that ruled the Ottoman Empire from the 13th century until its dissolution after World War I

Examples

The Ottoman dynasty rulers
After World War I, the last sultan of the Ottoman dynasty was deposed and sent into exile.
Many historical palaces in Istanbul were built during the reign of the Ottoman dynasty.
The Ottoman dynasty ruled over a vast and diverse empire for more than six centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ottoman Empire - the empire ruled by the Ottoman dynasty, covering Southeast Europe, Western Asia, and North Africa
