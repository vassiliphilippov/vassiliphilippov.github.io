buy off

verb
bribe to prevent action
6.8 0.70/m
Pronunciation: / baÉª ËˆÉ”f / (AmE), / baÉª ËˆÉ’f / (BrE)
to give someone money or a reward so that they will do something for you, especially something dishonest, or so that they will not take action against you

Examples

To buy off a person
No amount of money could buy off the journalist from publishing the story.
The corrupt official tried to buy off the inspectors with expensive gifts.
They attempted to buy off witnesses in order to avoid prosecution.

Synonyms

  • pay off
    5.0 0.75/m
    pay off is commonly used to mean secretly paying someone to be silent or help you, while buy off stresses buying their loyalty or silence.
  • bribe
    4.1 4.92/m
    bribe is an illegal payment to influence a person in power, while buy off focuses on paying someone to avoid trouble or action.
  • kick back
    8.4 0.39/m
    kick back is a secret payment given as a reward for a favor, while buy off means paying someone to act or stay silent.
  • buy
    8.9 20/m
    buy often means illegally paying someone for favors or influence, while buy off focuses on paying to stop someone acting against you.
