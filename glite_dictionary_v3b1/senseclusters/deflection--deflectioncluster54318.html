deflection

noun
the act or state of being bent or turned aside
Pronunciation: / dɪˈflɛk.ʃən /; / dɪˈflɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛk.ʃən /; / dɪˈflɛkʃən / (BrE)
the action or process of turning or being turned aside from a straight course or direction

Examples

Deflection of the ball
A sudden deflection of the ball changed the outcome of the game.
Engineers measured the deflection of the bridge under heavy loads.
The material's deflection shows how much it can bend before breaking.
His deflection from the issue made it hard to reach a solution.
Minor setbacks occasionally caused a deflection from their original project plan.
The sudden deflection of the conversation surprised everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deviation - the act of departing from an established course or norm
  • synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
  • synonym: diversion - the act of turning something aside from its course
  • antonym: focus - the center of interest or activity; opposite of turning aside
  • antonym: straightness - the quality or state of being straight, without bending
