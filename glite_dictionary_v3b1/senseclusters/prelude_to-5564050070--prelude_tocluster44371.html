prelude to

noun
introduction to something to come
4.4 1.30/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…õlÀåjud tu / (AmE), / Ààpr…õljuÀêd t ä / (BrE)
an event or action that serves as an introduction or precursor to something more important

Examples

A prelude to event
A sudden drop in temperature can be a prelude to snowfall in the region.
The negotiations were merely a prelude to the much larger agreement signed later.
The rain was a prelude to the storm, signaling what was about to come.

Synonyms

  • prelude
    5.9 2.25/m
    prelude is a brief event that introduces what follows, while prelude to names that introduction directly before a specific event.
  • overture
    7.2 0.44/m
    overture is a formal or musical opening often used for offers, while prelude to refers broadly to any introductory event before something more important.
  • preliminary
    5.2 0.52/m
    preliminary is a preparatory action that readies people or things, while prelude to is an introduction that may simply come before something important.
  • precursor
    7.2 3.78/m
    precursor is something that signals or predicts what will come, while prelude to emphasizes an actual introductory event before something important.
  • lead-in
    4.9 0.41/m
    lead-in is a short intro used for shows or stories, while prelude to means any event that acts as a general introduction.
