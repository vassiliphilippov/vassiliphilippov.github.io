gelignite

noun
a type of explosive used in mining and demolition
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛlɪɡˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛlɪɡnaɪt / (BrE)
a type of explosive, similar to dynamite, in which nitroglycerin is absorbed in a base of wood pulp and sodium or potassium nitrate

Examples

Gelignite explosive
Because of its stability, gelignite was preferred for demolition work in the early 20th century.
Police discovered several sticks of gelignite hidden in the abandoned building.
The miners used gelignite to blast through the rock and access the new tunnel.
