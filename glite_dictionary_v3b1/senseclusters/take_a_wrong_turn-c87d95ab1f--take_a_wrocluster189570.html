take a wrong turn

verb
make a mistake or bad decision
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə rɔŋ tɜrn / (AmE), / teɪk ə rɒŋ tɜːn / (BrE)
to make a mistake or incorrect decision, often resulting in unintended or problematic consequences

Examples

To take a wrong turn in life
After the merger, the company took a wrong turn and quickly lost its competitive edge.
I'm sorry I'm late; I took a wrong turn on my way here and got completely lost.
Sometimes, even the best leaders take a wrong turn despite careful planning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: take a right turn - to follow the correct path or make the correct decision
