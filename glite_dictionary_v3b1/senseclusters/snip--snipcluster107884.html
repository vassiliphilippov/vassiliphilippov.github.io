snip

noun
act of cutting with small strokes
Pronunciation: / snɪp / (AmE), / snɪp / (BrE)
the act of cutting something with quick, small strokes, especially with scissors or shears

Examples

A quick snip
He gave the rose bush a gentle snip to encourage new growth.
With a quick snip, she trimmed the loose thread from her sleeve.
You could hear the snip of scissors as the tailor worked.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - the act of making an incision or opening
