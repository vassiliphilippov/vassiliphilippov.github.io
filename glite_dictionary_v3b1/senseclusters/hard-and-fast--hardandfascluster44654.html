hard-and-fast

adjective
strictly enforced; rigid (of rules)
Pronunciation: / hɑrd ənd fæst / (AmE), / hɑːd ənd fɑːst / (BrE)
(of rules) clearly defined and strictly enforced, leaving no room for doubt or flexibility.

Examples

A hard-and-fast rule
In science, hard-and-fast conclusions are rarely possible due to the complexity of research.
The company has hard-and-fast policies regarding confidentiality and data protection.
There are no hard-and-fast rules about what you should wear to the event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - able to be changed or adapted easily
  • synonym: strict - demanding that rules be followed exactly
