scotosis

noun
state of obscurity
Pronunciation: / skoʊˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / skəʊˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a state of darkness, obscurity, or being hidden, especially in a figurative or literary sense

Examples

Social scotosis
The philosopher argued that ignorance keeps society in a state of scotosis.
For centuries, the true meaning of the symbol remained in scotosis.
Literary critics tried to bring the poet's obscure works out of scotosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • synonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or difficult to understand
