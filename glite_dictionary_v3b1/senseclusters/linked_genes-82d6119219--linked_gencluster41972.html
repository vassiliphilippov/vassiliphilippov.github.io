linked genes

noun
genes inherited together due to proximity
Pronunciation: / lɪŋkt dʒinz / (AmE), / lɪŋkt dʒiːnz / (BrE)
any pair of genes that tend to be inherited together because they are located close to each other on the same chromosome.

Examples

Linked genes on chromosome
Not all genes assort independently; linked genes remain together unless separated by recombination.
Scientists discovered that certain linked genes in fruit flies are almost always inherited together.
The frequency of crossing over between linked genes can be used to map their positions on a chromosome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: linkage group - a group of genes inherited together on the same chromosome
