cat's-tail

noun
marsh plant with cylindrical seed heads
Pronunciation: / ˈkætsˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈkætsˌteɪl / (BrE)
A tall marsh plant with cylindrical brown seed heads and long flat leaves, used for making mats and chair seats.

Examples

Tall cat's-tail plant
Artisans use the long leaves of cat's-tail to weave traditional mats and baskets.
In late summer, the cat's-tail releases fluffy seeds that float across the marsh.
The pond was edged with thick clusters of cat's-tail swaying in the breeze.
