wild chamomile

noun
Eurasian herb similar to chamomile
4.3
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈkæməˌmaɪl / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkæməmʌɪl / (BrE)
an annual Eurasian herb resembling chamomile in fragrance and medicinal uses, but with a more bitter taste and considered less effective

Examples

Wild chamomile plant
Although wild chamomile is similar medicinally, some herbalists prefer the traditional variety for its milder taste.
Many people use wild chamomile in teas, though its flavor is notably more bitter than true chamomile.
We found wild chamomile growing along the field, its scent filling the summer air.
