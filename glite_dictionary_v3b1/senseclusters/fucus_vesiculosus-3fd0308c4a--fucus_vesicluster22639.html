Fucus vesiculosus

noun
species of brown seaweed (bladderwrack)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːkəs vəˌsɪkjəˈloʊsəs / (AmE), / ˈfjuːkəs vɛˌsɪkjʊˈləʊsəs / (BrE)
a species of brown seaweed, commonly known as bladderwrack, used in preparing kelp and as fertilizer

Examples

Fucus vesiculosus seaweed
Fucus vesiculosus is often harvested for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of iodine.
Gardeners sometimes collect Fucus vesiculosus to use as a natural fertilizer for their plants.
The coastal rocks were covered with Fucus vesiculosus, easily identifiable by its air bladders.
