overvote

noun
excess votes in election
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.voʊt/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ˈvoʊt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.vəʊt/ /; / /əʊ.və.ˈvəʊt/ (BrE)
a situation in which a voter selects more choices than allowed in an election contest, causing the vote to be invalid

Examples

An overvote on a ballot
The election officials discarded the ballot because of an overvote in the city council race.
If you mark more than one candidate, your vote may be counted as an overvote and not included.
Training poll workers helps reduce the number of overvotes during elections.
