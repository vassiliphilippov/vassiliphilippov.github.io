execution

noun
legal enforcement of a court judgment
7.7 6.4
Pronunciation: /ek.sə.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ /; / /ek.sɪ.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /ek.sɪ.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ /; / /ek.sə.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
(Law) A court order authorizing the enforcement of a judgment, typically by directing a sheriff to carry it out.

Examples

Court execution order
A writ of execution was delivered to the sheriff to enforce the court's decision.
The court issued an execution against the defendant's property to satisfy the judgment debt.
The plaintiff requested an execution to recover the damages awarded by the court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enforcement - the act of compelling observance of or compliance with a law
  • antonym: stay of execution - a court order to temporarily suspend the enforcement of a judgment
