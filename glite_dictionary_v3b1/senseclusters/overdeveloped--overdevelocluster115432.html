overdeveloped

adjective
processed too much
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˈvɛləpt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˈvɛləpt / (BrE)
processed for too long or too strongly, resulting in excessive density, contrast, or loss of detail, especially in photographic film or images

Examples

Overdeveloped photo
The photograph appeared overdeveloped, with the highlights almost completely lost.
If the film is overdeveloped, the resulting image may have harsh contrast and little detail.
She realized the prints were overdeveloped when the shadows looked unnaturally dark.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underdeveloped - processed for too short a time, resulting in insufficient density or contrast
