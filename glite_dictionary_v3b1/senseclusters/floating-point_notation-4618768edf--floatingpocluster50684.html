floating-point notation

noun
system for representing numbers using an exponent
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊtɪŋ pɔɪnt noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtɪŋ pɔɪnt nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A method of representing real numbers in which the decimal point can 'float'; the position of the decimal point is specified by an exponent, commonly used in computing and scientific calculations.

Examples

Floating-point notation format
Converting integers to floating-point notation can introduce rounding errors in computer programs.
Engineers often write scientific data in floating-point notation for clarity and consistency.
Many computer systems perform calculations using floating-point notation to handle very large or very small numbers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scientific notation - a way of expressing numbers as a product of a number and a power of ten
