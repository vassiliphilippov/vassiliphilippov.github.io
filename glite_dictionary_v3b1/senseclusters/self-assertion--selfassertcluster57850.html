self-assertion

noun
confident forceful expression of one's opinions
2.9 6.9
Pronunciation: / sɛlf əˈsɜrʃən /; / ˌsɛlf əˈsɝːʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf əˈsɜːʃ(ə)n /; / ˌself əˈsɜːʃən / (BrE)
the confident and forceful expression or defense of one's own opinions, rights, or desires

Examples

Strong self-assertion

The therapist encouraged him to work on self-assertion as part of his personal growth.

  • antonym: humility - modest view of one's importance; lack of self-assertion
  • antonym: submissiveness - the quality of being willing to submit to others; lack of self-assertion
