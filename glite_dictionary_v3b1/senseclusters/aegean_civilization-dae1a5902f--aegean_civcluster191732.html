Aegean civilization

noun
prehistoric culture of the Aegean region
7.9
Pronunciation: /eɪ.ˈdʒiː.ən sɪ.və.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.ˈdʒiː.ən sɪ.və.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /iː.ˈdʒiː.ən sɪ.vɪ.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.ˈdʒiː.ən sɪ.vɪ.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the prehistoric civilization that developed on the islands and coasts of the Aegean Sea, including the cultures of Minoan Crete, Mycenaean Greece, and others, flourishing from about 3000 to 1100 BCE.

Examples

The Aegean civilization culture
Archaeologists have uncovered intricate pottery from the Aegean civilization on the island of Santorini.
The decline of the Aegean civilization is often attributed to natural disasters and invasions.
The influence of the Aegean civilization can be seen in later Greek art and architecture.
Archaeologists have uncovered new evidence about the collapse of the Aegean civilisation in the late Bronze Age.
Art and architecture from the Aegean civilisation greatly influenced later Greek culture.
The ancient Aegean civilisation left behind magnificent palaces and intricate pottery on Crete and other islands.

  • synonym: Mycenaean civilisation - Late Bronze Age civilisation on mainland Greece, considered part of Aegean civilisation
