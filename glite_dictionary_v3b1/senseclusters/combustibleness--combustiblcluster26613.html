combustibleness

noun
ability to catch fire and burn
3.8
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbʌstəbəlnəs / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌstɪb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being able to catch fire and burn; the property of being combustible.

Examples

High combustibleness
Because of its high combustibleness, gasoline should be kept away from open flames.
Experts tested the combustibleness of the fabric before approving it for use in uniforms.
The combustibleness of certain materials requires special handling and storage procedures.
