unfound

adjective
without evidence
7.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
not supported by evidence or facts; lacking a basis or justification

Examples

Unfound claims
The accusations were completely unfound and quickly dismissed by the committee.
She realized her fears were unfound after learning the facts.
Many rumors spread online are unfound and should not be trusted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseless - without foundation in fact
  • synonym: groundless - not based on any good reason or facts
  • antonym: justified - supported by good reason or evidence
