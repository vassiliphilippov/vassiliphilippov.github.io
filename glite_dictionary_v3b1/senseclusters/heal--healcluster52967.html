heal

verb
recover or become healthy again
3.5
Pronunciation: / hil / (AmE), / hiːl / (BrE)
to become healthy again or to make someone or something healthy again, especially after injury, illness, or emotional pain

Examples

To heal a wound
After her operation, she needed time to heal both physically and emotionally.
Doctors said the bone would heal faster if he rested properly.
It took several weeks for his wound to heal fully.
Her heart will heal with time after the loss of her pet.
The cut began to heal after I kept it clean and covered.
The doctor told me it would take six weeks for my arm to heal.
Some believe that herbal remedies can heal both body and mind.
The doctor was able to heal the wound with special treatment.
Time and rest have finally healed his broken leg.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cure - to relieve a person or animal of the symptoms of a disease or condition
  • antonym: infect - to make a person or organism sick with a disease
  • antonym: injure - to cause physical harm
  • synonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health, mind, or strength
  • synonym: treat - to give medical care to a person or illness
  • antonym: worsen - to become or make worse
