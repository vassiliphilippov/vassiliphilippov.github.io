surgical incision

noun
cut made in body during surgery
3.3 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːdʒɪkəl ɪnˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɜːdʒɪkəl ɪnˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
a cut made through body tissue or an organ by a surgeon, usually as part of a medical operation

Examples

A surgical incision wound
Proper care of a surgical incision helps prevent infection and promotes healing after surgery.

The doctor made a surgical incision along the patient's abdomen to begin the operation.
