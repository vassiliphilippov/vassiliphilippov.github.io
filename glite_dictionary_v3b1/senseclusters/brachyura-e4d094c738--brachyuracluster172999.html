Brachyura

noun
order of true crabs
Pronunciation: / brəˈkjuːrə / (AmE), / brəˈkjʊərə / (BrE)
an order of crustaceans—including true crabs—characterized by a reduced abdomen folded under the thorax

Examples

The Brachyura crabs
Many familiar shoreline species belong to the Brachyura, recognized by their short abdomens.
Scientists study the evolutionary adaptations of Brachyura to understand crustacean diversity.
The Brachyura comprise the true crabs found in marine, freshwater, and terrestrial habitats.
