leaning

adjective
not upright or level; slanting
4.2 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliÀên…™≈ã / (AmE), / ÀàliÀên…™≈ã / (BrE)
(of a structure or object) departing or slanting away from the true vertical or horizontal position; not upright or level.

Examples

Leaning tower
The old barn had become noticeably leaning over the years due to strong winds.
The picture frame was leaning to one side and needed to be straightened.
We visited the famous leaning tower of Pisa on our trip to Italy.

Synonyms

  • tilted
    2.5 0.03/m
    tilted is turned or sloping from a straight position, while leaning implies resting or bending partly for support.
  • slanted
    3.9 0.01/m
    slanted slopes at an angle like a roof or sign, while leaning often suggests a whole object tilting to one side.
  • tipped
    3.8 0.00/m
    tipped leans because one end is lifted or pushed, while leaning is the general state of not being upright or level.
  • canted
    8.1 0.01/m
    canted is set at an angle often for style or technical use, while leaning simply means not vertical or level.
  • inclined
    3.9 0.72/m
    inclined slopes or tilts and can also mean having a tendency, while leaning focuses on physical slanting of objects.
  • sloping
    3.1 0.01/m
    sloping rises or falls gently like a hill or roof, while leaning describes an object that tilts from upright position.
  • askew
    6.0 1.00/m
    askew looks crooked or turned to one side, while leaning describes a simple tilt often caused by weight or touch.
  • atilt
    9.8 0.01/m
    atilt means slightly tipped or slanted in a delicate way, while leaning is a common word for any object not upright.
  • skew
    6.4 0.02/m
    skew describes something set off a true line, often used in technical contexts, while leaning is plain for a simple tilt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upright - in a vertical position, not slanted
