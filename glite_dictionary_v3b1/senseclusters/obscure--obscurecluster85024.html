obscure

adjective
not well-known or famous
5.2
Pronunciation: / əbˈskjʊr / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊə / (BrE)
not well-known, famous, or widely recognized; remote or not easily accessible

Examples

An obscure village
He grew up in an obscure village far from any major city.
Many important discoveries were made by obscure scientists whose names are now forgotten.
The author was relatively obscure before the release of her bestselling novel.
Many important discoveries have taken place in seemingly obscure corners of the world.
She was born in an obscure mountain village far from the main roads.
The artist retreated to an obscure community where few outsiders ever visited.

Related Terms

  • antonym: famous - widely known and acclaimed
  • synonym: isolated - (of a place or community) far away from other places, buildings, or people
  • synonym: remote - distant from other places or people
  • synonym: unknown - not known or recognized by many people
  • antonym: well-known - known by many people, familiar
