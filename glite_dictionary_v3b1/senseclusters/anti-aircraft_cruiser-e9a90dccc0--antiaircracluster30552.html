anti-aircraft cruiser

noun
warship equipped for anti-aircraft defense
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.taɪˈɛr.kræft ˈkruː.zɚ / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈeə.krɑːft ˈkruː.zə / (BrE)
a type of warship, particularly a light cruiser, modified or designed to carry multiple high-angle guns for defense against enemy aircraft instead of heavy anti-ship armament

Examples

An anti-aircraft cruiser ship
During World War II, the Royal Navy converted several vessels into anti-aircraft cruisers to enhance air defense at sea.
The anti-aircraft cruiser was deployed to protect the fleet from enemy bombers during the battle.
With extra radar and rapid-firing guns, an anti-aircraft cruiser was vital for convoy protection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cruiser - a large warship faster and more lightly armed than a battleship
