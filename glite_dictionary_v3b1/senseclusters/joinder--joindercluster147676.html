joinder

noun
joining in legal action
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔɪndər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪndə / (BrE)
the act of joining or combining things, especially in a legal or formal context (such as joining parties, claims, or issues in a lawsuit)

Examples

The joinder of parties
The court approved the joinder of several plaintiffs who had similar claims against the company.
A joinder was filed so that both parties could present a united argument before the judge.
Without a proper joinder, the defendant could not participate in the ongoing litigation.
The joinder of the two companies created one of the largest suppliers in the region.
In legal proceedings, a joinder can combine multiple claims into a single case.
The contract specified the joinder of all relevant parties before the agreement could take effect.
The judge approved the joinder of the two lawsuits to save time and resources.
A joinder of parties can occur when multiple people are affected by the same legal issue.
The lawyer filed a motion for joinder to combine related claims into one case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consolidation - the act of combining two or more legal actions into one
  • antonym: separation - the act of moving or being moved apart
  • antonym: severance - the act of separating joined legal actions into individual cases
  • synonym: union - the act of joining together or the state of being joined
