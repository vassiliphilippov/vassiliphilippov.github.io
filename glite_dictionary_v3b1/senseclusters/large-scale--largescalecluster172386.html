large-scale

adjective
involving great size or extent
3.8 13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ërd íÀåske…™l / (AmE), / Ààl…ëÀêd íÀåske…™l / (BrE)
Involving considerable size, scope, or extent; covering a wide area or affecting many people or things.

Examples

Large-scale project
The company faced losses after a large-scale cyberattack affected its core systems.
The government announced a large-scale investigation into environmental violations.
They are planning a large-scale renovation of the entire campus next year.

Synonyms

    4.1 34/m
    extensive covers a wide area or range and often describes work or damage, while large-scale means something of great size or scope.
  • wide-ranging
    4.1 2.37/m
    wide-ranging covers many different subjects or areas, while large-scale focuses on the overall size or reach of something.
  • full-scale
    4.1 2.97/m
    full-scale uses all available resources and shows maximum effort, while large-scale only describes something of big size or scope.
  • far-reaching
    5.0 4.72/m
    far-reaching has strong effects that change many people or systems, while large-scale simply means big in size or scope.
  • expansive
    5.4 3.77/m
    expansive suggests wide, open, or ambitious space or plans, while large-scale says mainly that something is large in scope.
  • vast
    2.1 65/m
    vast describes something extremely large in size or amount, while large-scale highlights wide scope or many people affected.
  • widespread
    3.6 28/m
    widespread happens over a large area or among many people, while large-scale stresses the overall size or scope of an action.
  • sizable
    3.7 5.72/m
    sizable means fairly large in size or amount, while large-scale suggests very big scope or effects across a wide area.
