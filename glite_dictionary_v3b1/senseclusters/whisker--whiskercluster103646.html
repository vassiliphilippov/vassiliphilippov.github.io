whisker

noun
long, stiff hair on a mammal's face
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪskɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪskə / (BrE)
a long, stiff hair that grows from the snout or face of some mammals, such as cats or mice, and is used for touch or sensing surroundings

Examples

Cat whisker
A mouse uses each whisker to navigate through narrow tunnels in the dark.
The cat twitched its whisker as it sensed movement nearby.
The lion's whisker stood out against its golden fur.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vibrissa - a technical term for a stiff facial hair (whisker) in mammals
