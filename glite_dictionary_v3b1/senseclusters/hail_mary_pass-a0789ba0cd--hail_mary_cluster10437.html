Hail Mary pass

noun
a desperate last effort; long, risky attempt
Pronunciation: / ˌheɪl ˈmɛri pæs / (AmE), / ˌheɪl ˈmɛːri pɑːs / (BrE)
an attempt made in desperation, especially a long, last-ditch pass in American football or any final, risky effort to achieve success

Examples

A Hail Mary pass
Her job application was a Hail Mary pass—she didn't expect to be chosen but applied anyway.
Investing all their savings in the startup was their Hail Mary pass before retirement.
With only seconds left on the clock, the quarterback threw a Hail Mary pass to try and win the game.
