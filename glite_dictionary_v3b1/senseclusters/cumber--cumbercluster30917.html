cumber

verb
to hinder or weigh down
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌmbər / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbə / (BrE)
to hinder, obstruct, or make free movement difficult; to burden or weigh down

Examples

To cumber progress
Regulations can sometimes cumber progress rather than promote it.
She chose simple furniture so as not to cumber the small room.
The heavy backpack will cumber you during the long hike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encumber - to restrict or burden in a way that movement is difficult
  • antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easy or easier
  • synonym: impede - to delay or prevent by obstructing
