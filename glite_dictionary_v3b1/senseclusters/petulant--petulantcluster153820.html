petulant

adjective
easily annoyed or irritable
8.7 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛtʃələnt / (AmE), / ˈpɛtjʊlənt / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) showing sudden, impatient irritation, especially over something minor; easily annoyed or irritable.

Examples

A petulant child
Despite his success, he was sometimes petulant with those who disagreed with him.
Her petulant tone made it clear she wasn't happy with the decision.
The child grew petulant when he did not get his way.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irritable - easily annoyed or angered
  • antonym: placid - not easily upset or excited; calm
