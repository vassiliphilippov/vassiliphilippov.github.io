rubber-necking

noun
touring to look at interesting sights
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌbər ˌnɛkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌbə ˌnɛkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of traveling around to look at places of interest, especially as a tourist

Examples

Rubber-necking at sights
He spent the afternoon rubber-necking the historic district with his camera in hand.
Some locals dislike summer because of all the rubber-necking that clogs the roads.
The bus was full of tourists rubber-necking around the city’s famous landmarks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sightseeing - the activity of visiting places of interest as a tourist
