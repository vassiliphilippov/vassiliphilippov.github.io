noun

Pronunciation: /ˈɑːb.dʒekt əv ə pre.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈɒb.dʒekt əv ə pre.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
a noun, pronoun, or noun phrase that follows a preposition and completes its meaning

Examples

The object of a preposition
A pronoun used as the object of a preposition should be in the objective case, like "me" or "him".
In the sentence "She sat beside the window", the phrase "the window" is the object of a preposition.
Understanding the object of a preposition can help you analyze sentence structure more effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: object (grammar) - a noun, pronoun, or noun phrase that receives the action or completes the meaning of a verb or preposition
  • synonym: preposition - a word used before a noun or pronoun to show place, time, direction, etc.
