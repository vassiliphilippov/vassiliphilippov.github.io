Bergsonian

adjective
of Bergson's philosophy
Pronunciation: / bɜːrɡˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / bɜːɡˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas or philosophy of Henri Bergson, especially his views on time, consciousness, and creativity

Examples

Bergsonian philosophy
The novel presents a Bergsonian view of time as fluid and subjective.
Her analysis of memory was deeply influenced by Bergsonian concepts.
Many modern thinkers have drawn on Bergsonian ideas about intuition and creativity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: philosophical - relating to the study of fundamental nature of knowledge and existence
