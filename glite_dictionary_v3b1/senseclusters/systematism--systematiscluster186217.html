systematism

noun
habit of organizing systematically
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstəməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəmətɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or tendency of organizing or classifying things according to a system or method

Examples

Systematism in practice
Despite the project's complexity, her systematism kept everything organized and on track.
His systematism was evident in the way he meticulously categorized every aspect of his life.
The scientist's systematism helped ensure that all experiments were conducted with consistent procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chaos - complete disorder and lack of organization
  • synonym: systematization - the act or process of organizing according to a system
