dash

noun
short sprint race
4.7
Pronunciation: / dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
a short, fast run or race, or a sudden, rapid movement towards something

Examples

A 100-meter dash
Athletes lined up at the start of the 60-yard dash with anticipation.
His specialty is the 200-meter dash, where he excels with explosive speed.
She won the gold medal in the 100-meter dash at the championship.
At the sound of the alarm, everyone started a frantic dash for the exits.
He made a sudden dash across the street to catch the bus.
The children made a dash for the ice cream truck as soon as it arrived.
He made a dash for the train as the doors were closing.
The children raced each other in a dash across the playground.
With a sudden dash, she reached the finish line ahead of everyone else.
