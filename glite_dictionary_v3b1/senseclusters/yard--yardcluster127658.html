yard

noun
area where logs are stored
1.0 39/m
Pronunciation: / j…ërd / (AmE), / j…ëÀêd / (BrE)
an area of land next to or around a building, often enclosed, used for keeping animals, storage, work, or recreation

Examples

A grassy yard
A security guard patrols the yard every night to prevent theft of valuable logs.
After weeks of cutting, the workers brought the timber to the yard.
The logs were piled high in the yard waiting to be transported to the mill.
After the storm, the animals were gathered into the yard until the fences could be repaired.
The chickens roamed freely in the yard behind the farmhouse.
The farmer built a sturdy yard for his cattle to keep them safe at night.
After moving in, they installed a new swing set in their yard.
The children spent the afternoon playing games in the yard.
We planted flowers along the fence that borders our yard.

Synonyms

  • plot of land
    3.0 0.97/m
    plot of land is a small area used for a single purpose like growing plants, while yard sits next to a building and serves many uses.
  • piece of ground
    2.8 0.17/m
    piece of ground is a general area with clear edges for any use, while yard is usually next to a building and often fenced or used for work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: courtyard - an enclosed area surrounded by buildings or walls, often open to the sky
  • synonym: depot - a place where trains or buses are stored or maintained
  • degree higher: field - open land, typically larger and not enclosed, often used for agriculture
  • synonym: garden - a piece of ground for growing flowers, fruits, or vegetables, especially in the UK
  • synonym: timber yard - a place where timber is stored, often for sale or processing
