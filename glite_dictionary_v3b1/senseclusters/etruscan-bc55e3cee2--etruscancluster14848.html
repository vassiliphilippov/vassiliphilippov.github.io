Etruscan

noun
native or inhabitant of ancient Etruria
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtrʌskən / (AmE), / ɪˈtrʌskən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of ancient Etruria, an ancient civilization of Italy that greatly influenced early Roman culture

Examples

Ancient Etruscan tombs
Archaeologists have discovered tombs built by the Etruscans near the ancient city of Tarquinia.
Rome adopted many cultural practices from the neighboring Etruscans before becoming a dominant power.
The Etruscans were known for their skill in metallurgy and art.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Roman - a native or citizen of ancient Rome
