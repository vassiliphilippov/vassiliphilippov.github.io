centralized

adjective
under central control or authority
3.7 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəˌlaɪzd /; / ˈsɛn.tɚ.laɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəlaɪzd /; / ˈsɛn.trə.laɪzd / (BrE)
Controlled, managed, or organized by a single central authority or brought together in one place.

Examples

Centralized authority
Disaster response is often more effective when centralized authority coordinates resources and personnel.
In a centralized government, power is concentrated in a national rather than a local administration.
The company adopted a centralized system to streamline its operations and increase efficiency.
Emergency response in the region became more efficient after being centralised under one authority.
The company adopted a centralised approach to decision-making, streamlining operations across all departments.
The new constitution established a centralised form of government, reducing the autonomy of regional leaders.
