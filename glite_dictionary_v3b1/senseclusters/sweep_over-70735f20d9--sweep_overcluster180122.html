sweep over

verb
to affect suddenly and strongly
4.6 0.8/m
Pronunciation: / swip Àào äv…ö / (AmE), / swiÀêp Àà…ô äv…ô / (BrE)
(of a feeling or sensation) to affect someone suddenly and powerfully; to overwhelm.

Examples

Fear swept over the crowd
A sudden sense of dread swept over the crowd when the alarm sounded.
A wave of relief swept over her as she heard the good news.
Joy swept over him at the sight of his long-lost friend.
