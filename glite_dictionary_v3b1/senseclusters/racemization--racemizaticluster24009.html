racemization

noun
amino acid dating method
9.9
Pronunciation: /reɪ.sə.mə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɪ.sə.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /reɪ.sə.mə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɪ.sə.mɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a method for estimating the age of biological or archaeological samples by measuring the conversion of amino acids to a racemic mixture

Examples

Racemization dating method
Researchers used racemization to determine the age of the ancient shell found at the site.
The process of racemization helps scientists estimate how long ago an organism died.
Advances in racemization techniques have improved the accuracy of archaeological dating.
