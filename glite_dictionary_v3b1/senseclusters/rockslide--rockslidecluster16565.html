rockslide

noun
mass of rocks sliding down a slope
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑkˌslaɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɒkˌslaɪd / (BrE)
a sudden downward movement of a mass of rocks, often along a slope or mountainside

Examples

A rockslide blocks road
A massive rockslide blocked the mountain road for several hours.
Heavy rain triggered a rockslide that damaged several houses in the valley below.
The hikers narrowly escaped when a sudden rockslide crashed down the hillside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: landslide - the downward sliding of a mass of earth or rock from a mountain or cliff
