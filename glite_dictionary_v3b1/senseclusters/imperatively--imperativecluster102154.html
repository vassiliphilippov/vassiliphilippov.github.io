imperatively

adverb
in a commanding or authoritative way
2.5
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛrəˌtɪvli /; / ɪmˈpɛrətɪvli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛrətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that expresses command, authority, necessity, or importance

Examples

Speak imperatively

The officer imperatively ordered the troops to advance.
She imperatively instructed the team to finish the project by Friday.
The doctor spoke imperatively about the need for immediate surgery.
He raised his hand imperatively to silence the noisy crowd.
