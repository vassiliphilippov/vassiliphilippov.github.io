steelman

noun
person who strengthens an opponent's argument
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈstiːl.mən/ /; / /ˈstiːl.mæn/ (AmE), /ˈstiːl.mən/ /; / /ˈstiːl.mæn/ (BrE)
a person who restates another person's argument in the strongest, most persuasive form to test or fairly evaluate it.

Examples

A steelman in debate
The debate team appointed a steelman to restate the opposition's strongest arguments before rebuttal.
She acted as the group's steelman, always ensuring critics faced the fairest version of proposals.
A good editor can be a steelman, improving the argument even before publication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charitable interpretation - the practice of interpreting someone's statements in the most reasonable way
  • antonym: straw man - a weak or misrepresented version of an opponent's argument used to make it easier to attack
