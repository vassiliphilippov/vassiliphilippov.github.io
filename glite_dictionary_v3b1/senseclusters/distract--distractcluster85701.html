distract

verb
to disturb or worry someone
2.7 2.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrækt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrakt /; / dɪˈstrækt / (BrE)
to take someone's attention away from what they are doing or thinking about

Examples

To distract attention
Bad news late at night can easily distract you from sleep.
Her friend's illness distracted her and made it hard to concentrate.
The troubling headlines distract him from focusing on his work.
He distracted the guards so his friend could sneak past.
She tried not to let her phone distract her during the meeting.
The loud noise outside distracted me from my work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - free from agitation or disturbance
  • synonym: divert - to cause someone or something to change course or direction; to distract
  • antonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort; the opposite of distract
  • synonym: perturb - to disturb or make anxious or uneasy
