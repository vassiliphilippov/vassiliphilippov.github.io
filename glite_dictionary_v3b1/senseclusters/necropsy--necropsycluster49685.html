necropsy

noun
post-mortem examination (esp. of an animal)
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛˌkrɑpsi / (AmE), / ˈnɛk.rɒp.si / (BrE)
an examination and dissection of a dead body, especially an animal, to determine cause of death or the effects of disease

Examples

A necropsy report
A detailed necropsy revealed evidence of a rare infectious disease in the animal population.
The veterinarian performed a necropsy to determine why the horse had died unexpectedly.
They decided to order a necropsy after several sudden deaths occurred in the zoo's primate exhibit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autopsy - an examination of a human dead body to find cause of death
