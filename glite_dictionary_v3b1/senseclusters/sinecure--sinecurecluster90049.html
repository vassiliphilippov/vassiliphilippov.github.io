sinecure

noun
position with little or no work
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪnɪˌkjʊr / (AmE), / ˈsɪnɪˌkjʊə /; / ˈsɪnɪkjʊə / (BrE)
a position or job that requires little or no work but gives you money or status

Examples

A sinecure job
Critics accused the politician of appointing friends to lucrative sinecures.
Despite its impressive title, the role turned out to be a sinecure with few actual responsibilities.
He was offered a sinecure at the university, which required him to do almost nothing.
He was appointed to a sinecure that required little actual work but paid a substantial salary.
Historically, many church positions were considered sinecures because they had income but no real duties.
The university post soon became a sinecure for retired politicians who rarely attended meetings.
