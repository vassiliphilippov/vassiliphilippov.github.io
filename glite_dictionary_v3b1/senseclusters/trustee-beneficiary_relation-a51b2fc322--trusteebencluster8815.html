trustee-beneficiary relation

noun
legal relationship of trustee and beneficiary
8.3 8.2
Pronunciation: / trʌsˈtiː bəˌnɛfɪˈʃiːˌɛri rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / trʌsˈtiː bɪˌnɛfɪˈʃɪəri rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the legal relationship in which a trustee is obligated to act in the best interests of the beneficiary

Examples

The trustee-beneficiary relation principle
A key principle of the trustee-beneficiary relation is loyalty to the beneficiary's interests.
Courts will intervene if the trustee-beneficiary relation is breached through self-dealing.
Understanding the trustee-beneficiary relation is essential for anyone managing a trust.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conflict of interest - a situation where personal interests could interfere with acting in another's best interest
  • synonym: fiduciary duty - a legal obligation to act in another party's best interest
