fall

verb
lose one's chastity (archaic, often of a woman)
Pronunciation: / fɔl / (AmE), / fɔːl / (BrE)
to yield to temptation, sin, or moral wrongdoing; to lose innocence or purity

Examples

To fall from grace
In the strict society of the time, to fall could mean permanent disgrace for a young woman.
Literature from the era often alludes to women who fell after being seduced.
She was cruelly shunned by her peers after rumors spread that she had fallen.
According to the story, Adam and Eve fell after eating the forbidden fruit.
Despite her resolve, she eventually fell and broke her promise to avoid dessert.
The minister urged the congregation not to fall into sin.

Related Terms

  • antonym: remain chaste - to keep one's chastity or virginity
  • antonym: resist - to withstand the force or effect of something, especially temptation
  • synonym: succumb - to yield or give in, especially to pressure or temptation
