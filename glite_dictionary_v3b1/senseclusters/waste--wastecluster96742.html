waste

verb
lose strength or flesh, usually from illness or grief
5.0 4.2
Pronunciation: /weɪst/ (AmE), /weɪst/ (BrE)
to become physically weaker or thinner, especially because of illness, neglect, or lack of nourishment

Examples

To waste away
He seemed to waste before our eyes, consumed by sorrow and loneliness.
She began to waste away after her illness, barely eating or leaving her bed.
Without proper care, the patient may slowly waste from both body and spirit.
Starvation can waste a person to the point of emaciation.
The disease began to waste her body, leaving her frail and weak.
Years of illness slowly wasted him away until he could barely stand.
After months of illness, he began to waste and needed constant care.
Many patients waste rapidly if they are not properly nourished.
You could see how he had wasted from his time in the hospital.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - to become weaker or worse
  • synonym: emaciate - to make abnormally thin or weak, usually due to illness or lack of food
  • synonym: pine - to suffer a decline in health or strength due to longing or grief
  • antonym: recover - to return to a normal state of health or strength; opposite of wasting away
  • synonym: weaken - to become or make weaker
  • synonym: wither - to become dry and weak, especially through lack of moisture or vitality
