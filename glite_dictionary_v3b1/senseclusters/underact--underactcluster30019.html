underact

verb
operate with insufficient actuators
9.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈækt/ /; / /ˈʌn.dər.ækt/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈækt/ /; / /ˈʌn.də.ækt/ (BrE)
(in robotics and engineering) to operate or design a mechanical system so it has fewer actuators than needed to control all its movements, causing some motions to be unpowered or passive.

Examples

To underact a robot
To save energy, engineers sometimes underact robotic arms by disabling nonessential motors during transport.
During testing, the team underacted the prototype so some joints moved only passively.
Designers prefer underacting complex systems when weight constraints limit available actuators.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fully actuated - having enough actuators to control all degrees of freedom of a mechanism
  • synonym: underactuated - having fewer actuators than degrees of freedom, so some movements are not directly controlled
