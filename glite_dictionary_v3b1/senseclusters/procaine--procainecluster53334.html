procaine

noun
local anesthetic compound
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌkeɪn / (BrE)
a white crystalline compound used as a local anesthetic in dentistry and medicine.

Examples

Procaine local anesthetic
Before stitching the wound, the doctor administered procaine to minimize pain.
Some patients are allergic to procaine and require an alternative anesthetic.
The dentist injected procaine to numb the area before starting the procedure.
