mineralocorticoid

noun
steroid hormone affecting sodium and potassium
8.7
Pronunciation: / mɪˌnɛrələˈkɔrtɪˌkɔɪd / (AmE), / mɪˌnɪərələʊˈkɔːtɪˌkɔɪd / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex that regulates the metabolism of sodium and potassium in the body

Examples

Aldosterone mineralocorticoid
Aldosterone is the most well-known mineralocorticoid in the human body.
Doctors may prescribe synthetic mineralocorticoid hormones for certain adrenal insufficiency conditions.
Imbalances in mineralocorticoids can lead to disorders of salt and water balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glucocorticoid - a steroid hormone also produced by the adrenal cortex, affecting carbohydrate metabolism
