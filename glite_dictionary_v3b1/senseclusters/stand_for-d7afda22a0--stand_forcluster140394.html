stand for

verb
support or advocate for something
Pronunciation: / stænd fər / (AmE), / stænd fə / (BrE)
to support, advocate, or publicly represent a particular idea, principle, or cause

Examples

Stand for equal rights
Our organization stands for transparency and honesty in government.
She will always stand for justice and equality, even in difficult times.
Throughout history, many leaders have stood for freedom and human rights.
She always stands for what she believes is right, no matter the opposition.
The letters FAQ stand for 'frequently asked questions'.
The new policy stands for equality and justice for all citizens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oppose - to be against or not support something
  • synonym: represent - to be a symbol or embodiment of something
  • synonym: support - to agree with or back up an idea, cause, or person
