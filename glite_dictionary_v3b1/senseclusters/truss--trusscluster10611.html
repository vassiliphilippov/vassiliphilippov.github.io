truss

verb
tie a bird's wings and legs before cooking
9.9
Pronunciation: / trʌs / (AmE), / trʌs / (BrE)
to tie or bind something firmly, especially with rope or string (such as tying a bird for cooking or securing objects)

Examples

To truss a chicken
Before roasting the chicken, the chef carefully trussed it to ensure even cooking.
It is important to truss the bird so the stuffing does not fall out during cooking.
She watched a video to learn how to truss a turkey for Thanksgiving dinner.
Before transporting the boxes, workers had to truss them securely with cord.
Rescuers quickly trussed the injured climber to keep him still on the stretcher.
The chef began to truss the chicken before roasting it in the oven.
