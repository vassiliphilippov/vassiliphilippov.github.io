grass snake

noun
North American green insect-eating snake
Pronunciation: / ɡræs sneɪk / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs sneɪk / (BrE)
a small, harmless, non-venomous green or striped snake found in North or Central America, often in grassy areas

Examples

American grass snake
Children at the nature camp learned how to identify a grass snake by its bright green color.
The grass snake is harmless to humans and feeds mainly on insects and small amphibians.
We spotted a grass snake slithering quietly through the tall weeds behind the barn.
Children were excited to spot a few grass snakes slithering through the tall grass in the field.
Many gardeners welcome the presence of a grass snake because it keeps pests away.
The grass snake basked in the morning sun near the edge of the pond.
