marchese

noun
Italian noble title
Pronunciation: / marˈkeɪzeɪ / (AmE), / mɑːˈkeɪzeɪ / (BrE)
an Italian nobleman ranking below a duke and above a count

Examples

The marchese of Florence
The marchese hosted a grand ball at his villa in Florence.
Many admired the generosity of the local marchese during the festival.
The title of marchese was passed down through generations in his family.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: count - a nobleman ranking below a marquis or marchese
  • degree higher: duke - a nobleman of the highest hereditary rank below the monarch
  • synonym: marquis - a nobleman ranking below a duke and above a count in some European countries
