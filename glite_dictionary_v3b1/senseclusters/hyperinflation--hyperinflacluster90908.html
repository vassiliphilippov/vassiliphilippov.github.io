hyperinflation

noun
excessive increase
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
a rapid and excessive increase in the amount or importance of something, especially used figuratively

Examples

Hyperinflation of choices
There has been a hyperinflation of online courses, making it hard to choose the best ones.
Some argue that the hyperinflation of job titles in tech companies can be confusing.
The hyperinflation of social media platforms has changed how people communicate and share information.
