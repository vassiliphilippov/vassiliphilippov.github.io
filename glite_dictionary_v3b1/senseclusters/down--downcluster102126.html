down

adverb
in cash at time of purchase
Pronunciation: / daʊn / (AmE), / daʊn / (BrE)
(of money) as a cash payment made at the time of purchase, typically as a deposit or partial payment.

Examples

Fifty dollars down
The store let her take the furniture home after paying fifty dollars down.
We offered five hundred dollars down on the new car.
You need to put at least ten percent down as a deposit for most houses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deposit - a sum of money paid as the first installment on something
