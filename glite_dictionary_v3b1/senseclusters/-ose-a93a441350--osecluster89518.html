-ose

x
adjective-forming suffix
8.9
Pronunciation: / oʊs / (AmE), / əʊs / (BrE)
a suffix used to form adjectives meaning 'full of' or 'abounding in', as in 'verbose' or 'grandiose'

Examples

Verbose language
The word 'verbose' uses the suffix -ose to mean 'full of words'.
Adding -ose to a root can create adjectives like 'grandiose' or 'bellicose'.
Writers sometimes use words ending in -ose to sound more elaborate or descriptive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -ous - a suffix forming adjectives meaning 'full of' or 'having the qualities of'
