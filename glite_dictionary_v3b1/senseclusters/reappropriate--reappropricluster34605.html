reappropriate

verb
to allocate again
7.5
Pronunciation: /riː.ə.ˈproʊ.priː.eɪt/ /; / /rɪ.ə.ˈproʊ.priː.eɪt/ (AmE), /riː.ə.ˈprəʊ.priː.eɪt/ /; / /rɪ.ə.ˈprəʊ.priː.eɪt/ (BrE)
to officially allocate or assign money or resources again, often for a different purpose or budget.

Examples

To reappropriate funds
The city council decided to reappropriate funds from the road repair budget to improve local schools.
After the project was canceled, the organization chose to reappropriate the remaining resources to other initiatives.
Government officials may reappropriate money in response to unexpected emergencies or changing priorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocate - to distribute resources or duties for a particular purpose
  • antonym: misappropriate - to use money or resources dishonestly or for the wrong purpose
  • synonym: reassign - to assign something again or to a different person or purpose
