sacred fig

noun
Indian fig tree sacred in Buddhism
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.krɪd fɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd fɪɡ / (BrE)
a large fig tree native to India (Ficus religiosa), regarded as sacred in Buddhism and Hinduism

Examples

The sacred fig tree
Botanists studied the longevity and growth patterns of the sacred fig in India.
Many pilgrims gather under the shade of the sacred fig during religious festivals.
The ancient sacred fig tree is believed to be the spot where Buddha attained enlightenment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banyan - a fig tree with prop roots, often confused with the sacred fig
