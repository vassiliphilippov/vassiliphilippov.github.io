Shafi'i

proper noun
Islamic legal scholar
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃɑːˈfiː.i / (AmE), / ʃæˈfiː.i / (BrE)
Imam Muhammad ibn Idris al-Shafi'i (767–820), an early Islamic scholar and founder of the Shafi'i school of Islamic law.

Examples

Shafi'i the scholar
Many students of Islamic law study the teachings of Shafi'i in their courses.
The Shafi'i school is one of the four main Sunni legal traditions.
Imam Shafi'i is respected for his contributions to Islamic jurisprudence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hanbali - founder of the Hanbali school of Islamic law
  • synonym: Maliki - founder of the Maliki school of Islamic law
