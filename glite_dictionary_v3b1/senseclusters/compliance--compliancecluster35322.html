compliance

noun
act of submitting; yielding to authority
2.9 4.1
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈplaɪ.əns/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈplaɪ.əns/ (BrE)
the act or tendency of obeying rules, laws, requests, or yielding to authority or the wishes of others

Examples

Compliance with rules
She acted in compliance with the director's requests, even when she disagreed.
The king demanded compliance from the rebellious provinces.
Their immediate compliance surprised those who expected resistance.
Compliance with the new tax law is mandatory for all businesses.
All employees are responsible for ensuring compliance with workplace safety rules.
The company's strict compliance with environmental regulations earned it an industry award.
He showed little compliance when asked to change his behavior.
Her compliance made it easy for her coworkers to delegate tasks to her.
The manager was impressed by the team's compliance with his instructions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conformity - behavior that matches socially accepted standards, rules, or laws
  • antonym: defiance - open resistance or bold disobedience
  • synonym: obedience - the act of following orders or rules
