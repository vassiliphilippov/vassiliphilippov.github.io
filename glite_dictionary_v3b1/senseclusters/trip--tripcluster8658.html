trip

verb
cause to stumble or make a mistake
3.1
Pronunciation: / trɪp / (AmE), / trɪp / (BrE)
to stumble or cause someone to stumble or lose balance, either physically or metaphorically

Examples

To trip someone
She set a wire across the door to trip anyone trying to sneak in.
The complex instructions managed to trip up even the most experienced users.
The uneven pavement could easily trip a person not paying attention.
Be careful not to trip over the step.
He tripped on the curb and nearly dropped his coffee.
She tripped while running for the bus but quickly regained her balance.
