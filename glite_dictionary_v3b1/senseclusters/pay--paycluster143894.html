pay

verb
yield a financial return
5.2 58/m
Pronunciation: / peÉª / (AmE), / peÉª / (BrE)
(of an investment or financial instrument) to yield a specified amount of money as profit or interest.

Examples

Bonds pay interest
Government bonds typically pay investors twice a year.
The certificate of deposit paid a higher return than a regular savings account.
This savings account pays 3% interest per year.

Synonyms

  • bear
    8.2 6.91/m
    bear refers to investments that produce interest or dividends over time, while pay names an instrument giving a stated monetary return.
  • yield
    3.6 28/m
    yield means to produce or give a result like profit or product, while pay focuses on a set money return from investments.
  • earn
    1.0 52/m
    earn means to receive money from work or investment through effort, while pay describes an instrument giving a specified monetary return.
  • bring in
    2.8 6.61/m
    bring in means to generate income for a business or person, while pay specifically names an investment's stated interest return.

Related Terms
