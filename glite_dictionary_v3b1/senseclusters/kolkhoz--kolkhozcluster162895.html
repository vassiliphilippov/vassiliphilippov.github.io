kolkhoz

noun
collective Soviet state farm
Pronunciation: / kɑlˈxɔz / (AmE), / kɒlˈxɒz / (BrE)
A collective farm in the former Soviet Union that was owned and run by the state.

Examples

A kolkhoz farm
During the 1930s, millions of peasants were forced to join the kolkhoz system.
Life on a kolkhoz was strictly regulated by Soviet authorities.
Some former members of the kolkhoz still live in the village today.
