underwork

verb
to do too little work
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈwɜrk / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈwɜːk / (BrE)
to work less than is usual, required, or expected

Examples

To underwork on tasks
If you underwork, your performance reviews may suffer.
The manager noticed that several employees had started to underwork after the new policy was introduced.
He was warned not to underwork if he wanted to keep his job.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overwork - to work too much or too hard
  • synonym: slack off - to work less hard than usual or expected
