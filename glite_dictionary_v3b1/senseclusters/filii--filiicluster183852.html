the Latin nominative plural form of 'filius', meaning 'sons', used as the subject in Latin texts and inscriptions

Examples

Filii Romani
In ancient Roman inscriptions, the word filii often appears to indicate the sons of a family.
The historian translated the Latin phrase 'duo filii' as 'two sons'.
Genealogical records sometimes list the filii who inherited family property.
