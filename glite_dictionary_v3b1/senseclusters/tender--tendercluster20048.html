tender

noun
small boat for ship-to-shore transport
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndɚ /; / ˈtɛn.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə /; / ˈten.də / (BrE)
a small boat or ship used to transport people or supplies between a larger vessel and the shore, or to supply other ships

Examples

A tender boat
A small tender was waiting at the dock to transport us to the main vessel.
After anchoring offshore, supplies were delivered to the yacht by tender.
The crew used the tender to bring passengers ashore from the cruise ship.
A small crew remained on the tender to ensure the engines ran smoothly throughout the mission.
During the expedition, the tender carried food and medical supplies to the main ship.
The navy dispatched a tender to refuel the patrol boats at sea.
