Muhammad Ali

proper noun
Ottoman viceroy and founder of modern Egypt
8.6
Pronunciation: / məˌhɑːməd ɑːˈliː / (AmE), / ˌmʊhəməd ɑːˈliː / (BrE)
(1769–1849) Albanian-born soldier and ruler who became the Ottoman viceroy of Egypt, effectively founding modern Egypt by asserting autonomy from the Ottoman Empire and implementing major reforms.

Examples

Muhammad Ali of Egypt
Muhammad Ali introduced sweeping reforms that shaped Egypt's economy and military in the nineteenth century.
Historians regard Muhammad Ali as the founder of modern Egypt due to his extensive modernization efforts.
The dynasty established by Muhammad Ali ruled Egypt until the mid-twentieth century.
