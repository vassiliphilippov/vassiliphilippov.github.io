overpromise

verb
to promise too much
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
to promise more than can realistically be delivered or achieved

Examples

To overpromise results
The company tends to overpromise on delivery times to attract more customers.
Politicians often overpromise during campaigns but struggle to keep their word.
If you overpromise and underdeliver, clients may lose trust in your business.
The marketing team overpromised on the new product's features, leading to customer disappointment.
He tends to overpromise when trying to impress clients, which sometimes causes problems later.
They realized they had overpromised and could not meet all the expectations they set.
Companies sometimes overpromise to attract new customers, but this can lead to disappointment.
He learned not to overpromise after failing to meet several deadlines.
It is risky to overpromise results you cannot guarantee.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underpromise - to promise less than what can actually be delivered
