shiko

noun
sumo leg-stamping exercise
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃiːkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃiːkəʊ / (BrE)
a sumo wrestling exercise in which the wrestler lifts and stamps each leg to the side to build strength and balance

Examples

A shiko exercise
Every morning, the sumo wrestler performed shiko to improve his strength and flexibility.
Coaches emphasize the importance of shiko in developing balance and power for sumo matches.
Spectators watched as the athletes practiced shiko before the tournament began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sumo - a Japanese form of heavyweight wrestling
