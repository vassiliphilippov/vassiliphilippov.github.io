point

noun
a sharp or V-shaped tip
Pronunciation: / pɔɪnt / (AmE), / pɔɪnt / (BrE)
the sharp or narrow end or tip of something (such as a needle, weapon, arrow, mountain, or landform)

Examples

Point of a needle
She noticed that each mountain had a distinct point at its summit.
The point of the knife was extremely sharp.
The points on the star were perfectly symmetrical.
Fishermen often gather at the point where the river meets the sea.
The lighthouse stands on the rocky point overlooking the bay.
We hiked to the point to get a better view of the coastline.
He accidentally pricked his finger on the point of the needle.
Please be careful with the point of the scissors when handling them.
The point of the arrow was incredibly sharp and gleaming in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blunt - having no sharp edge or point
  • synonym: cape - a large headland or promontory of large size extending into a body of water
  • antonym: edge - the outside limit of an object, area, or surface
  • synonym: tip - the pointed or rounded end of something
