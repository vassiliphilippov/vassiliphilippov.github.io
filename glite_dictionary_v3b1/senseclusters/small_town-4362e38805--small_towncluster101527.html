small town

noun
community smaller than a town
Pronunciation: / smɔl taʊn / (AmE), / smɔːl taʊn / (BrE)
a community with a population and infrastructure smaller than a typical town; often rural or less urbanized than a city

Examples

A small town life
After years in the city, he missed the quiet life of a small town.
Many small towns rely heavily on local businesses for employment.
She grew up in a small town where everyone knew each other.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: town - an urban area larger than a small town
  • synonym: village - a small settlement, typically in a rural area
