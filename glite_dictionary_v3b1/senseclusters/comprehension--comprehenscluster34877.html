comprehension

noun
ability to understand
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmprɪˈhɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmprɪˈhɛnʃən / (BrE)
the ability to understand and grasp the meaning or significance of something

Examples

Full comprehension
He read the article with full comprehension and could discuss its main ideas.
Her quick comprehension of the complex problem impressed her teachers.
The lecture was so technical that it was beyond my comprehension.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignorance - lack of knowledge or understanding
  • synonym: understanding - the ability to comprehend or grasp the meaning of something
