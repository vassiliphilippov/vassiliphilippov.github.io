lubber's line

noun
line on compass showing ship's heading
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌb.ərz laɪn / (AmE), / ˈlʌb.əz laɪn / (BrE)
a fixed line marked on a ship's compass to indicate the vessel's forward direction or heading

Examples

Lubber's line on compass
He aligned the lubber's line with the bow to ensure an accurate heading.
Make sure the lubber's line is properly positioned before you set the course.
The captain checked that the lubber's line matched the ship's intended direction.
