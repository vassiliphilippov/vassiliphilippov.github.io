fluid flywheel

noun
type of fluid coupling with a flywheel rotor
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈfluː.ɪd ˈflaɪ.wiːl/ /; / /ˈfluː.əd ˈflaɪ.wiːl/ (AmE), /ˈfluː.ɪd ˈflaɪ.wiːl/ /; / /ˈfluː.əd ˈflaɪ.wiːl/ (BrE)
a type of fluid coupling in which the driving rotor acts as the flywheel, used especially in automotive transmissions to transfer rotational power smoothly.

Examples

A fluid flywheel system
A fluid flywheel uses hydraulic fluid to transfer power from the engine to the transmission.
Engineers replaced the old clutch system with a modern fluid flywheel for better performance.
The fluid flywheel allows for smoother gear changes in automatic transmissions.
