Goth

noun
member of an ancient Germanic people
Pronunciation: / ɡɑθ / (AmE), / ɡɒθ / (BrE)
a member of a Germanic people who invaded the Roman Empire in the 3rd to 5th centuries AD, playing a major role in its downfall

Examples

Ancient Goth tribes
Historians study how the migration of the Goths changed European history.
In the fifth century, the Goth Alaric famously sacked Rome.
The Goths played a significant role in the decline of the Western Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ostrogoth - member of the eastern branch of the Goths
  • synonym: Visigoth - member of the western branch of the Goths
