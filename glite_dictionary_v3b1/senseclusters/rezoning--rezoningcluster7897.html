rezoning

noun
change of zoning
Pronunciation: / riˈzoʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / riːˈzəʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
the official act or process of changing the zoning classification of land or property, usually by a government authority

Examples

The rezoning approval
The city council approved the rezoning of farmland to allow for new housing developments.
Residents attended the meeting to voice their concerns about the proposed rezoning of the neighborhood.
After months of debate, the rezoning finally permitted commercial buildings in the area.
The city council held a meeting to discuss the rezoning of several downtown blocks.
After the rezoning, the land could be used for commercial purposes instead of residential.
Residents voiced concerns about the impact of rezoning on their neighborhood's character.

Related Terms

  • synonym: development - the process of constructing buildings or infrastructure on land
  • synonym: rezone - to change the zoning classification of an area
  • synonym: zoning - the process of dividing land into areas with specific regulations
