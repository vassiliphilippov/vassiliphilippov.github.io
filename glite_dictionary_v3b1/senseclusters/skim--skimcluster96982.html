skim

noun
thin layer on liquid's surface
6.6 1.85/m
Pronunciation: / skÉªm / (AmE), / skÉªm / (BrE)
a thin layer or film that forms on the surface of a liquid, often as the result of fat, scum, or impurities rising to the top

Examples

A skim on tea
A pale skim formed on her tea as it cooled.
He carefully removed the skim from the top of the milk.
There was a thin skim of oil on the surface of the pond.

Synonyms

  • slick
    5.8 0.58/m
    a slick is an oily, shiny patch on water that makes the surface slippery, while a skim is a thin film of fat or scum.
  • scum
    6.6 0.00/m
    scum forms as a dirty layer that builds up on a liquid's surface, while a skim is the thin film that sits on top.
