Mimosa sensitiva

noun
plant with sensitive leaves, tropical America
Pronunciation: / mɪˈmoʊsə sɛnˈsɪtɪvə / (AmE), / mɪˈməʊsə sɛnˈsɪtɪvə / (BrE)
A semi-climbing prickly evergreen shrub native to tropical America, known for its compound leaves that fold or droop when touched or exposed to light.

Examples

Mimosa sensitiva plant
Botanists in South America study the Mimosa sensitiva to understand plant movement in response to stimuli.
The leaves of the Mimosa sensitiva quickly fold up when touched, fascinating both children and scientists.
We grew a Mimosa sensitiva in the greenhouse to observe its unique sensitivity to light and touch.
