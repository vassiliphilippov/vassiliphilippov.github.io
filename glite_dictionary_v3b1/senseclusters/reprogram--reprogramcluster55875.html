reprogram

verb
to change instructions
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
to change the instructions or settings of a computer, device, or system so that it works differently

Examples

To reprogram a computer
The technician will reprogram the security system to accept new access codes.
You may need to reprogram your phone after a software update.
Engineers often reprogram robots to perform different tasks in the factory.
If the alarm keeps going off at the wrong time, you may need to reprogram it.
The technician will reprogram the computer to improve its performance.
After moving to a new city, she had to reprogram her GPS with the updated address.
The engineer had to reprogram the robot to perform the new task.
After the update, you may need to reprogram your remote control for it to work.
She quickly reprogrammed the thermostat to adjust the temperature schedule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: configure - to set up or arrange the settings of a device or system
  • synonym: program - to write or set instructions for a computer or device
  • antonym: reset - to return a device or system to its original state
