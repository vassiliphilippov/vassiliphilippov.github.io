subcontract

noun
contract assigning obligations to a third party
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.kənˌtrækt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.kənˌtrækt / (BrE)
a legal agreement in which a party assigns some of its contractual obligations to another party

Examples

A building subcontract
Before starting the project, we had to review every subcontract for compliance.
She specializes in negotiating complex subcontract agreements for large-scale developments.
The company signed a subcontract with a local firm to complete the electrical work.
