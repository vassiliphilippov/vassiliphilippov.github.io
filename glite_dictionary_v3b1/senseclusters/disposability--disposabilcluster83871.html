disposability

noun
quality of being expendable
Pronunciation: / dɪˌspoʊzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌspəʊzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being easily replaced, regarded as unimportant, or able to be discarded without concern

Examples

Staff disposability
The company treats temporary staff with a sense of disposability that affects morale.
Many worry about the growing disposability of workers in the modern gig economy.
He was frustrated by the feeling of disposability in his new job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expendability - the quality of being able to be used up or sacrificed
  • antonym: indispensability - the quality of being absolutely necessary or essential
