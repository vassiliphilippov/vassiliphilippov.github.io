embarkation

noun
the act of boarding a ship or aircraft
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmˌbɑrˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛmˌbɑːˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of boarding a ship or aircraft, especially by passengers or crew before a journey begins

Examples

The embarkation process
After a quick embarkation, the crew prepared the aircraft for takeoff.
Safety drills are required before embarkation to ensure all travelers know emergency procedures.
The embarkation of passengers began promptly at 10 a.m. at the cruise terminal.
