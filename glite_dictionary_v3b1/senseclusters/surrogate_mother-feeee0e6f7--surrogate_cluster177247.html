surrogate mother

noun
woman who carries a child for others
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.əˌɡeɪt ˈmʌð.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌr.ə.ɡət ˈmʌð.ə / (BrE)
a woman who bears a child for another person or couple, typically when the intended mother is unable to carry a pregnancy herself

Examples

A surrogate mother carries baby
After years of struggling with infertility, they decided to use a surrogate mother to have a child.
Legal agreements are essential before a woman becomes a surrogate mother.
The surrogate mother gave birth and then handed the baby to the intended parents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gestational carrier - a woman who carries a pregnancy created by the egg and sperm of others
