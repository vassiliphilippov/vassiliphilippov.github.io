transpose

verb
change the key of music
Pronunciation: / trænzˈpoʊz / (AmE), / trænzˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to change the key of a piece of music so that it is played, written, or sung higher or lower

Examples

To transpose a song
Could you transpose this song so it's easier for me to play on the guitar?
The choir struggled to transpose the piece into a lower key for the soloist.
The pianist quickly transposed the melody to suit the vocalist's range.
Can you transpose this melody from C major to G major for the concert?
Pianists often need to transpose pieces when accompanying singers in different ranges.
The song was too high for her to sing, so the band decided to transpose it down a few steps.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modulate - to change from one key to another in music
