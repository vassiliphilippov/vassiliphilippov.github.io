reaccumulation

noun
repeat buying phase
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˌkjuːmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəˌkjuːmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a period when investors or institutions buy a security again, leading to price consolidation before a potential upward movement in financial markets

Examples

Stock reaccumulation phase
The stock entered a reaccumulation phase as investors prepared for the next upward trend.
Analysts identified signs of reaccumulation after the initial rally in the market.
During periods of reaccumulation, prices often move sideways before breaking out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accumulation - the process of gradually buying a security over time
  • antonym: distribution - the process of selling off a security, often after a price rise
