orthoboric acid

noun
a weak acid used in industry and as an antiseptic
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθoʊˈbɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊˈbɒrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a white or colorless, weakly acidic solid compound (H3BO3), soluble in water and ethanol, used in various industries and as an antiseptic

Examples

Orthoboric acid compound
Adding orthoboric acid to glass production helps improve its durability.
Antiseptic eye washes often contain diluted orthoboric acid to prevent infection.
The laboratory used orthoboric acid as a reagent for a range of experiments.
