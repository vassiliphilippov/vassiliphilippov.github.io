anaphora

noun
use of a referring word (esp. pronoun) to avoid repetition
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈnæfərə / (AmE), / əˈnafərə / (BrE)
the use of a word, often a pronoun, to refer back to a word or phrase used earlier in a sentence or text, so as to avoid repetition

Examples

Anaphora with pronouns
In the sentence 'Sarah lost her keys and she can't find them,' anaphora is used as 'she' and 'them' refer back to 'Sarah' and 'keys'.
Understanding anaphora is important for analyzing how pronouns function in sentences.
Writers rely on anaphora for cohesion, helping readers link ideas without unnecessary repetition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cataphora - use of a word to refer forward to a later expression
