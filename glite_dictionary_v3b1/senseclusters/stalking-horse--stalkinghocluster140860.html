stalking-horse

noun
decoy candidate or pretext for real intention
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈstɔːk.ɪŋ hɔːrs/ /; / /ˈstɑːk.ɪŋ hɔːrs/ (AmE), /ˈstɔːk.ɪŋ hɔːs/ (BrE)
a person, thing, or action used to hide someone's real intentions or plans

Examples

Political stalking-horse
Rumors about a new CEO proved to be a stalking-horse to test public reaction.
She suspected he was just a stalking-horse for the company's true candidate.
The party used a stalking-horse to distract voters from their real leadership choice.
Her resignation letter was a stalking-horse to distract from deeper problems within the department.
The business merger was just a stalking-horse for the company's real interest in acquiring new technology.
The new proposal acted as a stalking-horse, allowing the negotiators to test reactions without revealing their true intentions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decoy - something used to mislead or draw attention away from the real thing
  • synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation put forward to defend or justify a fault or offense
  • synonym: front - a person or thing used to mask someone else's activities or intentions
  • synonym: pretext - a reason given in justification of a course of action that is not the real reason
  • synonym: ruse - an action intended to deceive someone; a trick
