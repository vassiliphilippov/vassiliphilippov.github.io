anomic

adjective
socially disoriented; lacking social norms
Pronunciation: / əˈnɑmɪk / (AmE), / əˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
experiencing social disorientation due to a breakdown of social norms or values

Examples

An anomic society
A rapid change in cultural norms can create an anomic environment for young people.
After the economic collapse, many people felt anomic and unsure of their place in society.
The novel explores the lives of anomic characters disconnected from traditional community values.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alienated - feeling isolated from a group or society
  • antonym: integrated - feeling connected and involved with society
