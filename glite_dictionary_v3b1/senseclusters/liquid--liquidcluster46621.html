liquid

noun
fluid state of matter
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd / (BrE)
a substance that flows freely, has a definite volume but no fixed shape, and is not a solid or a gas

Examples

The scientist poured a clear liquid into the test tube.

She poured the colored liquid into the test tube for the experiment.
Water is the most common liquid found on Earth.
Mercury is the only metal that is liquid at standard conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluid - substance that can flow, including liquids and gases
  • antonym: gas - state of matter with no fixed shape or volume, easily compressed
  • antonym: solid - state of matter with definite shape and volume, not fluid
