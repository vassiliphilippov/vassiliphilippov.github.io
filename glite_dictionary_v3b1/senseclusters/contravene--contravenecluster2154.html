contravene

verb
to violate a rule or law
8.2 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀêntr…ôÀàviÀên / (AmE), / Àåk…íntr…ôÀàviÀên / (BrE)
to act in violation of a law, rule, or agreement; to go against or oppose officially established regulations

Examples

To contravene the law
Companies that contravene safety regulations may face heavy fines.
He was found guilty of contravening the international treaty.
The proposal contravenes established ethical guidelines.

Synonyms

  • violate
    4.4 36/m
    violate is a common word for breaking a law or rule in speech and news, while contravene sounds more formal.
  • breach
    4.5 1.92/m
    breach refers to breaking a contract, rule, or promise often with clear damage, while contravene focuses on opposing regulations.
  • infringe
    6.8 11/m
    infringe commonly means crossing someone's rights or legal limits, especially patents, while contravene names a general formal violation.
  • transgress
    7.8 1.28/m
    transgress often describes breaking a moral rule or limit and sounds literary, while contravene refers to opposing formal regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comply - to act in accordance with a rule or law
  • synonym: violate - to break or fail to comply with a rule or law
