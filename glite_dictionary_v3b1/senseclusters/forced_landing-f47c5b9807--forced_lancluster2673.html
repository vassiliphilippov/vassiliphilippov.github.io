forced landing

noun
emergency unscheduled aircraft landing
2.2 3.5
Pronunciation: / fɔrst ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːst ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
An unscheduled landing by an aircraft made due to circumstances beyond the pilot's control, such as engine failure or adverse weather.

Examples

A forced landing by plane
All passengers were safe after the forced landing on the deserted road.
Due to heavy fog and low fuel, the aircraft made a forced landing in an open field.
The pilots executed a forced landing after the plane's engines failed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emergency landing - a landing made in response to an emergency situation
