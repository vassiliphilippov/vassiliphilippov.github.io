abelian

adjective
commutative in math
Pronunciation: / əˈbiːliən /; / əˈbiliən / (AmE), / əˈbiːliən / (BrE)
relating to a mathematical group or structure where the order of combining elements does not affect the result (the operation is commutative)

Examples

abelian group
In an abelian group, a plus b equals b plus a for any elements a and b.
The concept of abelian groups is fundamental in abstract algebra.
Matrix multiplication is not abelian because the order of multiplication matters.
In mathematics, an abelian group is one in which the group operation is commutative.
The professor explained that all cyclic groups are abelian by definition.
She studied modules over an abelian ring during the algebra course.
In an abelian group, the result is the same no matter the order of addition.
The mathematician explained that all vector spaces are abelian under addition.
Many important results in algebra involve abelian structures and their properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commutative - where the order of operations does not change the result
  • antonym: non-abelian - not commutative; the order of operations affects the result
