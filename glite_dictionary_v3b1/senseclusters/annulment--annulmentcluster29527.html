Synonyms

  • recision
    9.9 0.04/m
    recision cancels a contract and treats it as if it never existed, while annulment is the general legal act.
  • annulation
    9.8 0.00/m
    annulation is a formal legal voiding of something, while annulment is the common legal term used for the same act.
  • nullification
    8.6 0.22/m
    nullification makes a law or decision have no effect, while annulment often means treating an act as if it never existed.
  • invalidation
    7.3 0.17/m
    invalidation officially declares something legally void, while annulment often focuses on treating it as if it never existed.
  • revocation
    7.3 0.69/m
    revocation cancels a right, permit, or decision that was given, while annulment treats a past act as if it never existed.
  • abrogation
    8.9 0.37/m
    abrogation formally ends a law or agreement, usually by authority, while annulment often cancels individual marriages or contracts.
  • repeal
    5.5 4.31/m
    repeal removes a law or rule officially, often by a government, while annulment usually undoes a single legal act.
  • cancellation
    1.9 3.52/m
    cancellation stops an event, booking, or plan, usually without legal erasure, while annulment treats something as if it never existed.
