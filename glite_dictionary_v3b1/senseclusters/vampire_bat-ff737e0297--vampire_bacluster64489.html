vampire bat

noun
a blood-feeding tropical American bat
Pronunciation: / ˈvæmˌpaɪɚ bæt / (AmE), / ˈvæm.paɪə bæt / (BrE)
a tropical American bat that feeds on the blood of mammals or birds, especially those of the family Desmodontidae

Examples

Vampire bats feed blood
Vampire bats are native to Central and South America and are rarely found elsewhere.
Scientists are studying the saliva of the vampire bat for its potential in medicine.
The vampire bat feeds by making a tiny incision in its victim’s skin and lapping up the blood.
