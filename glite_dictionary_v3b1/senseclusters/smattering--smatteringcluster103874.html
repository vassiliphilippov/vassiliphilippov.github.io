smattering

noun
slight or superficial understanding
Pronunciation: / ˈsmæt̬.ɚ.rɪŋ /; / ˈsmæt̬.ɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsmæt.ər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a small amount of something, especially a slight or superficial knowledge

Examples

A smattering of knowledge
He has only a smattering of Spanish, just enough to ask for directions.
She gained a smattering of knowledge about art history from her weekend classes.
The article provides only a smattering of information about the new technology.
A smattering of stars could be seen through the cloudy night sky.
She had only a smattering of French, just enough to order food in a café.
There was a smattering of applause at the end of the performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundance - a large quantity of something
  • antonym: expertise - deep or thorough knowledge in a subject
  • synonym: glimmer - a faint or slight indication of something, especially knowledge
  • synonym: handful - a small number or amount
