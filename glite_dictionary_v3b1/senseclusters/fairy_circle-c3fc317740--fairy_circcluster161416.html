fairy circle

noun
bare circular patch in arid landscapes
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfeə.riː ˈsɜːr.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈfeə.riː ˈsɜː.kəl/ (BrE)
a roughly circular bare patch of soil or vegetation in arid grasslands or deserts, often caused by termite activity, plant competition, or other ecological processes.

Examples

Desert fairy circle
Researchers mapped a fairy circle in the Namib Desert to study plant patterns and soil moisture.
Locals believed the fairy circle was magical, but scientists linked it to termite activity.
Satellite images revealed dozens of regular fairy circles scattered across the arid plain.
