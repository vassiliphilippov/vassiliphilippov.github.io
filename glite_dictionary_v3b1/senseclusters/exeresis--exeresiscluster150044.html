exeresis

noun
surgical removal
Pronunciation: / ɛgˈzɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / ɛkˈsɪərəsɪs / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a part or organ from the body

Examples

Surgical exeresis of organ
The doctor recommended an exeresis to treat the patient's condition.
After the exeresis, the patient recovered quickly and without complications.
A skilled surgeon is required to perform an exeresis safely and effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excision - the act of cutting out or removing tissue surgically
  • synonym: resection - the surgical removal of part of an organ or structure
