pastoral

noun
literary work idealizing rural life
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpæstərəl / (AmE), / ˈpɑːstərəl / (BrE)
a literary, artistic, or musical work that idealizes or evokes peaceful rural life

Examples

A pastoral poem
Her novel reads almost like a modern pastoral, filled with romanticized descriptions of the countryside.
Many Renaissance writers were inspired to compose pastorals set among shepherds and green meadows.
The poet wrote a pastoral celebrating the simple joys of country life.
Beethoven's Sixth Symphony is often referred to as the 'Pastoral' because of its depiction of countryside scenes.
The gentle flute passages in the pastoral evoked images of sheep grazing in meadows.
The orchestra performed a beautiful pastoral at the summer festival.
