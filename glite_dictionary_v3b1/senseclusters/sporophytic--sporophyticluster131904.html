sporophytic

adjective
of the sporophyte
Pronunciation: / ˌspɔːrəˈfɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌspɒrəˈfɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the sporophyte generation (the diploid stage) in the life cycle of plants and algae, or determined by the sporophyte

Examples

Sporophytic stage
The sporophytic stage is dominant in most vascular plants.
Researchers studied the sporophytic tissue to understand spore development.
In ferns, the sporophytic generation is the leafy plant we commonly see.
In some plants, sporophytic self-incompatibility prevents self-fertilization based on the parent plant's genotype.
The sporophytic phase produces spores that will develop into gametophytes.
Researchers studied the sporophytic control of pollen development in flowering plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gametophytic - relating to the gametophyte generation in plants
