differentiate

verb

to develop or become different or specialized, especially in biology or evolution

Examples

Cells differentiate over time
As the organism grows, its tissues begin to differentiate and perform distinct functions.
During embryonic development, stem cells differentiate into various specialized cell types.
Plant cells differentiate based on environmental cues and genetic instructions.
Over many generations, these birds began to differentiate into two distinct species.
Plant populations will often differentiate when they inhabit separate environments.
The finches on the islands differentiated as they adapted to different food sources.
