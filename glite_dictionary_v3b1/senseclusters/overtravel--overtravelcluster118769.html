overtravel

noun
excess mechanical movement
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌtrævəl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌtrævəl / (BrE)
the extra movement of a mechanical part beyond its intended stopping point

Examples

Machine overtravel
The engineer reduced the overtravel in the machine to improve its accuracy.
Too much overtravel in the switch can cause it to wear out faster.
Adjusting the overtravel on the trigger made the device safer to use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: play - the amount of free movement in a mechanical part
  • antonym: precision - the quality of being exact and accurate in movement
  • synonym: slack - looseness or extra movement in a mechanism
