repolarization

noun
restoring cell charge
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌpoʊlərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which something (such as a cell or material) returns to its normal electrical or polarized state after being activated or disrupted

Examples

Repolarization of cells
After each heartbeat, repolarization prepares the heart cells for the next contraction.
Abnormal repolarization can be detected on an electrocardiogram and may indicate heart problems.
During nerve signaling, repolarization restores the original charge inside the neuron.
The repolarization of the nerve cell membrane is essential for proper nerve signal transmission.
After the electric field was removed, scientists observed the repolarization of the experimental material.
Engineers studied the repolarization process to improve the performance of the new capacitor design.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depolarization - the process by which a cell's electrical charge becomes less negative during activation
