globalist

noun
supporter of global cooperation
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports international cooperation, integration, or global governance

Examples

A globalist leader
The globalist argued that international trade benefits all countries involved.
Many leaders at the summit were described as globalists because of their support for open borders.
Critics of the policy accused the government of listening only to globalist interests.
The globalist believes that countries should work together to solve climate change.
Some politicians criticize globalist policies for undermining national interests.
As a proud globalist, she supports open borders and international trade.
Some critics labeled the candidate a globalist for supporting international trade agreements.
The term globalist is often used in political debates to criticize those who favor global cooperation.
He was called a globalist because of his work with international organizations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internationalist - a person who advocates greater cooperation among nations
  • antonym: nationalist - a person who strongly supports their own nation and its interests
