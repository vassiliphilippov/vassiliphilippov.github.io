soft-pedal the issue

verb
downplay or avoid addressing an issue directly
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔft ˌpɛdəl ði ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ˌpɛdəl ði ˈɪʃuː / (BrE)
to treat a problem or topic as less important than it really is, often to avoid confronting it directly or to minimize its impact

Examples

To soft-pedal the issue
Instead of confronting the problem, she decided to soft-pedal the issue in her report.
Politicians often soft-pedal the issue when it might cause public backlash.
The manager tried to soft-pedal the issue during the meeting to avoid upsetting the team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downplay - to make something seem less important
  • antonym: highlight - to emphasize or make prominent
