xerographic copier

noun
machine for photocopying using xerography
Pronunciation: / zɛɹəˈɡræfɪk ˈkɑːpiɚ / (AmE), / zɛərəˈɡræfɪk ˈkɒpiə / (BrE)
A machine that produces copies of documents or images using xerography, a process involving the action of light on an electrically charged surface to transfer toner.

Examples

Early xerographic copiers
Early xerographic copiers revolutionized the way businesses handled paperwork and records.
He demonstrated how the xerographic copier worked during the staff meeting.
The office purchased a new xerographic copier to improve document reproduction efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photocopier - a machine that makes paper copies of documents and other visual images quickly and cheaply
