pithy

adjective
concise and expressive
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪθi / (AmE), / ˈpɪθi / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) concise and forcefully expressive; terse and full of meaning.

Examples

A pithy remark
Her pithy remarks left a strong impression on everyone at the meeting.
I always appreciate a pithy response over a long-winded explanation.
The article provided a pithy summary of the main issues involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words; brief but comprehensive
  • antonym: verbose - using more words than needed; wordy
