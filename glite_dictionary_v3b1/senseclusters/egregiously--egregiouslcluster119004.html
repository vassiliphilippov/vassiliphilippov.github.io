egregiously

adverb
extremely badly
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˈɡriːdʒəsli / (AmE), / ɪˈɡriːdʒəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is extremely bad, shocking, or noticeable in a negative sense

Examples

Egregiously break the rules
The company egregiously ignored safety regulations, putting its workers at risk.
He was egregiously late to the meeting, arriving an hour after it started.
The report was egregiously inaccurate, containing several major errors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blatantly - in an open and unashamedly obvious way
  • antonym: mildly - in a way that is not extreme or severe
  • synonym: outrageously - in a way that is shockingly bad or excessive
