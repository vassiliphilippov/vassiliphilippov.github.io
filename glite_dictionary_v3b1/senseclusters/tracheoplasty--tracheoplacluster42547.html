tracheoplasty

noun
trachea surgery
Pronunciation: / ˌtreɪkiəˈplæsti / (AmE), / ˌtrækiəˈplæsti / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to repair or reconstruct the trachea

Examples

Tracheoplasty surgery
The surgeon performed a tracheoplasty to widen the patient's airway.
After the accident, a tracheoplasty was necessary to restore normal breathing.
Children with congenital airway problems may require tracheoplasty to improve airflow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laryngoplasty - surgical repair of the larynx
  • synonym: tracheostomy - a surgical opening made in the trachea to help breathing
