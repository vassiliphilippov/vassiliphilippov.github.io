cementation

noun
binding of particles
6.7 0.04/m

the process by which minerals bind soil or rock particles together to form solid material

Examples

Rock cementation
The cementation of sand grains forms solid rock layers over time.
Geologists study cementation to understand how sedimentary rocks are created.
Without cementation, loose sediments would not become hard stone.

Synonyms

  • concretization
    9.1 0.00/m
    concretization describes minerals or sediments hardening into a compact mass, while cementation is minerals binding particles to form solid rock.
  • concretion
    8.9 0.03/m
    concretion is a solid lump formed by compacting or joining materials, while cementation is minerals binding particles into solid rock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consolidation - the process of making something stronger or more solid
  • antonym: erosion - the process by which soil or rock is worn away
