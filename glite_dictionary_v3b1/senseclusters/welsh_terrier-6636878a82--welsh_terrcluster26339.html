Welsh terrier

noun
breed of small, wire-haired terrier
Pronunciation: / wɛlʃ ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / wɛlʃ ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
a breed of small, wire-haired terrier, originally developed in Wales for hunting; resembles a miniature Airedale

Examples

A Welsh terrier dog
After much research, they decided to adopt a Welsh terrier from the local shelter.
His Welsh terrier loves to dig in the garden and chase squirrels.
The Welsh terrier is known for its friendly temperament and lively spirit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Airedale terrier - a large breed of terrier dog, often referenced for resemblance
