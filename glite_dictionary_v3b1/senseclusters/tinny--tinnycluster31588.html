tinny

adjective
of poor quality; flimsy
5.0 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtÉªni / (AmE), / ËˆtÉªni / (BrE)
(of an object or material) of very poor quality or flimsy, often resembling tin in thinness, lightness, or insubstantiality.

Examples

Tinny toy
Her tinny umbrella folded inside out at the slightest gust of wind.
The toy was made of such tinny plastic that it broke after one use.
The walls of the cheap motel room felt tinny and offered little soundproofing.

Synonyms

  • flimsy
    3.8 2.23/m
    flimsy is thin and weak, breaking or bending easily, while tinny suggests a light, not-solid material that feels like thin metal.
  • cheap
    1.0 16/m
    cheap is low in price and often poorly made, while tinny focuses on being thin, light, and not solid like tin.
  • cheesy
    4.3 1.16/m
    cheesy is cheaply made and may seem silly or low quality, while tinny describes a thin, light material feeling like tin.
  • crummy
    6.7 1.60/m
    crummy is general slang for poor or unpleasant quality, while tinny refers to a specific thin, light, and not-solid material.
