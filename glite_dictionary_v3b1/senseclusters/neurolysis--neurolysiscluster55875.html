neurolysis

noun
nerve tissue breakdown
Pronunciation: / nʊˈrɑlɪsɪs / (AmE), / njʊəˈrɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the pathological dissolution, degeneration, or breakdown of nerve tissue due to disease, toxins, or injury

Examples

Toxic neurolysis damage
The patient developed neurolysis after prolonged exposure to toxic chemicals.
Microscopic examination showed clear signs of neurolysis in the affected nerve fibers.
Researchers are studying how certain diseases can trigger neurolysis in the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neuritis - inflammation of a nerve
  • synonym: neurodegeneration - progressive loss of structure or function of neurons
