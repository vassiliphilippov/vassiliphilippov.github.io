Mariotte's law

noun
law about pressure and volume of gases
8.4
Pronunciation: / mæriˈɑts lɔ / (AmE), / maˈrɪɒts lɔː / (BrE)
A scientific law stating that the pressure of an ideal gas at constant temperature is inversely proportional to its volume.

Examples

Mariotte's law of gases
According to Mariotte's law, increasing the volume of a gas at constant temperature decreases its pressure.
Medical ventilators rely on the principles of Mariotte's law when adjusting airflow.
Students learned how Mariotte's law explains the relationship between pressure and volume in their physics class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Boyle's law - the same physical law stating the pressure-volume relationship of a gas at constant temperature
