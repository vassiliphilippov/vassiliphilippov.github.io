dissident

adjective
disagreeing with accepted beliefs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsɪdənt / (BrE)
disagreeing with or rejecting official policy, accepted beliefs, or the majority view

Examples

Dissident voices
She published several dissident articles that questioned government policy.
The dissident views expressed by the scientist challenged the prevailing theories.
The organization welcomes dissident voices and encourages debate among its members.
Several dissident voices challenged the council's decision during the meeting.
The government suppressed dissident journalists who disagreed with its policies.
Within the party, he was known for his dissident stance on key issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformist - supporting or following accepted beliefs or standards
  • synonym: dissent - to disagree or refuse to conform to an official or majority view
