become

verb
come into existence
Pronunciation: / bɪˈkʌm / (AmE), / bɪˈkʌm / (BrE)
to start to be or change into a different state, condition, or role

Examples

To become famous
As night falls, shadows become in the corners of the room.
New traditions often become from small, repeated acts.
Scientists study how new species become over time.
After years of training, she became a skilled dancer.
If you leave the bread out too long, it will become stale.
When the sun set, the sky became a deep shade of purple.
She studied hard to become a doctor.
The traffic light will become green in a moment.
Winter nights become longer and colder each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arise - to come into being or to originate
  • antonym: cease - to come to an end, to stop existing
  • synonym: get - to become or come to be, used informally
  • antonym: remain - to continue to be in the same state; stay unchanged
  • synonym: turn - to become or change into a different state
  • synonym: turn into - to change or transform into something else
