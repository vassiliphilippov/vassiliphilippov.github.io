dip into one's savings

verb
use part of one's saved money
1.3
Pronunciation: / dɪp ˈɪntu ʌnz ˈseɪvɪŋz / (AmE), / dɪp ˈɪntə wʌnz ˈseɪvɪŋz / (BrE)
to take and use part of the money one has put aside as savings, usually for a specific purpose or in an emergency

Examples

To dip into his savings
After losing his job, he had to dip into his savings to pay the rent.
She decided to dip into her savings for a long-awaited vacation.
We try not to dip into our savings unless it's absolutely necessary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: save money - to keep money instead of spending it
  • synonym: withdraw money - to take money out of a bank account
