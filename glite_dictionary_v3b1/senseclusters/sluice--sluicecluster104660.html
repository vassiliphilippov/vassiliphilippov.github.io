sluice

verb
to flow or pour forcefully
7.8 0.2/m
Pronunciation: / sluÀês / (AmE), / sluÀês / (BrE)
(of liquid) to pour or flow out forcefully in a stream, as if from a sluice.

Examples

Rain sluiced down
After the heavy rain, water sluiced down the mountain paths, flooding the valley below.
During the storm, runoff sluiced over the garden, carrying leaves and soil away.
She sluiced the mud from her boots with a powerful jet of water.

Synonyms

  • sluice down
    7.6 0.04/m
    sluice down sends liquid away in a strong, steady stream from a gate or channel, while sluice describes that same powerful flow.
  • gush
    3.2 2/m
    gush bursts out suddenly in large amounts, often with noise and speed, while sluice suggests a stream flowing as from a gate.
  • spout
    3.7 0.8/m
    spout sends liquid or gas in a narrow jet, often from a small hole, while sluice implies a larger, controlled stream.
  • pour out
    2.9 1/m
    pour out flows or causes to flow in quantity, often slowly or gently, while sluice focuses on a fast, forceful stream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pour - (of liquid) to flow rapidly in a steady stream
