underachiever

noun
person who performs below potential
6.6 3.8
Pronunciation: /ʌn.də.rə.ˈtʃiː.vər/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.rə.ˈtʃiː.və/ (BrE)
a person, especially a student, who performs below their potential or expected level, often despite having the ability to do better

Examples

An underachiever student
Although James is very intelligent, he has been labeled an underachiever because of his poor grades.
Her teachers worry she is an underachiever, since her test scores never match her abilities.
The school started a special program to help underachievers reach their full potential.
