red-shafted flicker

noun
western North American woodpecker with red wing and tail shafts
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈʃæftɪd ˈflɪkər / (AmE), / rɛd ˈʃɑːftɪd ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
a large woodpecker of the western United States with a red underside on its wings and tail, regarded as a subspecies of the northern flicker

Examples

The red-shafted flicker bird
Many birdwatchers travel to the west to see the striking red-shafted flicker in its natural habitat.
The bright red wing feathers help identify the red-shafted flicker from other woodpeckers in the area.
We spotted a red-shafted flicker searching for ants on the lawn this morning.
