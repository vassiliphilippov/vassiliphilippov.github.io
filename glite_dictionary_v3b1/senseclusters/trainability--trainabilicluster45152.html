trainability

noun
ease of being trained
Pronunciation: / ˌtreɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌtreɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being easy to teach or train, especially in animals or people

Examples

Dog trainability
The trainability of certain dog breeds makes them popular choices for service work.
When choosing a pet, many people consider the animal's trainability as an important factor.
Some employees show high trainability and quickly adapt to new tasks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stubbornness - the quality of being difficult to change or train
  • synonym: teachability - the quality of being easy to teach
