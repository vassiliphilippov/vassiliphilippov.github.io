daimon

noun
spirit or inner guide
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪmɒn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪmɒn / (BrE)
a spirit or guiding force, especially in ancient Greek philosophy or literature, often seen as an inner voice or influence

Examples

Guiding daimon
In ancient Greece, philosophers believed that a daimon could guide a person's actions and choices.
Socrates claimed that his daimon warned him whenever he was about to make a mistake.
Writers sometimes describe inspiration as a daimon whispering creative ideas into their minds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demon - an evil spirit or supernatural being
  • synonym: genius - an inner guiding spirit or inspiration
