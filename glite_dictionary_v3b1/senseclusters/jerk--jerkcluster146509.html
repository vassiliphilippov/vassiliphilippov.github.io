jerk

noun
rate of change of acceleration (physics)
Pronunciation: / dʒɝk / (AmE), / dʒɜːk / (BrE)
In physics, the rate at which acceleration changes with time.

Examples

A jerk in acceleration
A sudden jerk in acceleration can cause discomfort in vehicles.
Engineers must consider jerk to ensure smooth operation of mechanical systems.
The roller coaster was designed to minimize jerk for a better rider experience.
