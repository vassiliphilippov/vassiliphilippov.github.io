tadpole

noun
larval stage of a frog or toad
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtædˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈtad.pəʊl / (BrE)
the aquatic larval stage of a frog or toad, typically having a rounded body and a long tail

Examples

A tadpole with legs
He watched a tadpole slowly develop legs over several weeks.
The children collected a few tadpoles to observe their transformation into frogs.
The pond is full of tadpoles swimming near the surface.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: froglet - a small frog that has recently metamorphosed from a tadpole
