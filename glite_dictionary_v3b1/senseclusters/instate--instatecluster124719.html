instate

verb
to establish officially
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsteɪt / (BrE)
to establish or introduce a system, rule, law, or practice and put it into effect

Examples

To instate a new rule
The university decided to instate a new code of conduct for all students.
After much debate, the government instated stricter environmental regulations.
The company will instate flexible working hours starting next month.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolish - to formally put an end to a system or practice
  • synonym: implement - to put a plan or system into operation
