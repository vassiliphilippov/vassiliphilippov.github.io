unskillful

adjective
lacking skill; done ineptly
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈskɪlfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈskɪlf(ə)l / (BrE)
Lacking skill or proficiency; done poorly or ineptly.

Examples

An unskillful player
Her unskillful handling of the situation led to confusion among the staff.
It was an unskillful attempt at painting the portrait.
The repairs to the car were unskillful and made things worse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adept - very skilled or proficient
  • synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or action
  • synonym: incompetent - not having or showing the necessary skills
  • antonym: skillful - having or showing skill
