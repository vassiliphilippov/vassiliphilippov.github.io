consort

noun
group of similar musical instruments
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.sɔːrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sɔːt / (BrE)
A group of similar musical instruments played together, especially in Renaissance or Baroque music.

Examples

String consort
A recorder consort consists of recorders of different sizes playing together.
Composers often wrote for the broken consort, mixing strings and winds.
The viol consort performed a suite of 16th-century dances at the concert.
