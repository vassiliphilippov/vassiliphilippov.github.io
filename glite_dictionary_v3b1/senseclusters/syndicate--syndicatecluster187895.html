syndicate

verb
form or join a syndicate
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪndɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to join together or organize as a group or syndicate, especially for a business project or venture

Examples

To syndicate companies
Several media companies decided to syndicate to expand their reach.
Smaller publishers often syndicate to increase their market influence.
The banks syndicated to provide the massive loan needed for the new project.
Several newspapers chose to syndicate the comic strip to increase its readership.
The companies decided to syndicate their resources to bid for the large government contract.
The real estate agents syndicated the project, pooling their funds and expertise.
