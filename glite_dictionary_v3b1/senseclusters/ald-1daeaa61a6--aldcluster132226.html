ALD

noun
atomic layer deposition
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ ˌɛl ˈdi / (AmE), / ˌeɪ ˌɛl ˈdiː / (BrE)
a technique for depositing extremely thin, controlled films on surfaces one atomic layer at a time

Examples

ALD coating process
The engineers used ALD to create a uniform coating on the microchips.
Advances in ALD have enabled the production of thinner and more reliable electronic devices.
Researchers rely on ALD for precise control over film thickness in nanotechnology applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chemical vapor deposition - a process for depositing thin films from a chemical vapor onto a surface
  • synonym: physical vapor deposition - a technique for depositing thin films using physical processes like evaporation
