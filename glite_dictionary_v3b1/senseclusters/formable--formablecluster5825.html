formable

adjective
able to be shaped
4.4 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îrm…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêm…ôb…ôl / (BrE)
able to be shaped, molded, or formed into a particular shape

Examples

Formable metal parts
The metal is formable enough to be used in intricate designs.
Certain plastics remain formable when heated, making them useful in manufacturing.
Artists prefer clay that is highly formable for sculpting detailed figures.

Synonyms

  • shapeable
    3.7 0.00/m
    shapeable is used when something can be formed into a specific model or outline, while formable means able to be shaped generally.
  • moldable
    4.1 0.16/m
    moldable is often used when a soft material is pressed into a mold to make objects, while formable means shapeable broadly.
  • plastic
    3.6 2.72/m
    plastic describes a substance that physical force easily shapes or molds, while formable is a more general ability to be shaped.
  • pliable
    4.2 0.73/m
    pliable means easy to bend or fold and to change shape by hand, while formable stresses making a set shape.
  • pliant
    5.6 0.27/m
    pliant describes materials that bend and give without breaking, often used in writing too, while formable focuses on shaping.
  • deformable
    5.3 0.12/m
    deformable means able to change shape, often under force or damage, while formable suggests being shaped intentionally into a new form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking
  • synonym: malleable - easily shaped or bent
  • antonym: rigid - not able to be bent or changed in shape
