sensorineural hearing loss

noun
hearing loss from inner ear or nerve damage
5.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /sen.sə.ˈnʊr.əl ˈhɪr.ɪŋ lɔːs/ /; / /sen.sə.ˈnjʊr.əl ˈhɪr.ɪŋ lɔːs/ (AmE), /sen.sə.ˈnjʊə.rəl ˈhɪə.rɪŋ lɒs/ /; / /sen.sə.ˈnʊə.rəl ˈhɪə.rɪŋ lɒs/ (BrE)
hearing loss caused by damage to the inner ear or the auditory nerve, rather than problems in the outer or middle ear.

Examples

Sensorineural hearing loss damage
Exposure to loud noise over time can lead to sensorineural hearing loss in adults.
The audiologist diagnosed him with sensorineural hearing loss after a series of tests.
Unlike conductive hearing loss, sensorineural hearing loss is usually permanent.
