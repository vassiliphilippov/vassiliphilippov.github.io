incite

verb
urge or provoke (especially to action)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪt / (BrE)
to encourage or provoke someone to do something, especially something violent, illegal, or harmful

Examples

To incite violence
It is illegal to incite violence or hatred against others.
She was accused of trying to incite unrest within the organization.
The speaker's words were intended to incite the crowd to protest.
She was accused of trying to incite others to join the movement.
Some posts on social media can incite violence and spread disinformation.
The leader's speech was meant to incite the crowd to protest against the new law.
He was arrested for trying to incite a riot during the protest.
Leaders who incite violence are often held responsible for the consequences.
The speaker's words were meant to incite anger among the crowd.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deter - to discourage someone from taking action
  • synonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • synonym: provoke - to stimulate a reaction, usually strong or unwelcome
  • synonym: urge - to strongly encourage someone to do something
