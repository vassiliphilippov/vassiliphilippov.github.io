patricidal

adjective
hostile to father
Pronunciation: / ˌpætrɪˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌpætrɪˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
relating to or showing hostility toward one's father or paternal authority, sometimes used figuratively for opposition to fatherhood or paternal power

Examples

Patricidal feelings
The novel explores the patricidal feelings of a son rebelling against his strict father.
Her patricidal attitude shocked the family, as she openly challenged her father's authority.
The film depicts a patricidal conflict between generations struggling for control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: filial - relating to or befitting a son or daughter
  • antonym: matricidal - relating to the killing of one's mother or hostility toward motherhood
