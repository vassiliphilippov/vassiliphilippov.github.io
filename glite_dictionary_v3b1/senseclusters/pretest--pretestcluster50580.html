pretest

noun
trial before main test
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛst / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtɛst / (BrE)
a test or trial done before the main event to check for issues or measure prior knowledge

Examples

A pretest before exams
The team conducted a pretest to ensure the instructions were clear before the official experiment.
A pretest helped the company identify potential issues with their new product design.
Students took a pretest at the start of the course to measure their initial knowledge.
Before starting the new unit, the teacher gave a pretest to all the students.
The pretest results showed that most participants already understood the basic concepts.
Taking a pretest can help identify which topics need more attention during the course.
Before starting the new unit, the teacher gave a pretest to all the students.
The results of the pretest helped identify which topics needed more review.
Each participant completed a pretest to measure their initial understanding of the material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: final test - the main or concluding test after all preparations
  • synonym: pilot - a small-scale preliminary study to test methods and procedures
  • antonym: posttest - a test given after instruction to measure what has been learned
  • synonym: trial run - a test of something new to see if it works well
