Cervidae

noun
the deer family (mammals)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜːrvɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪdiː / (BrE)
the biological family of mammals that includes all types of deer, such as reindeer, moose, muntjacs, and roe deer

Examples

The Cervidae family
Scientists found a new species within the Cervidae family in the dense forest region.
The Cervidae family includes animals such as moose, reindeer, and deer found across the world.
Unlike antelopes, all members of Cervidae have antlers that are shed and regrown each year.

Related Terms
