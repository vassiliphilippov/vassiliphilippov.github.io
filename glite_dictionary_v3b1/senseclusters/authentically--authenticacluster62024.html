authentically

adverb
in a genuine or authentic way
1.1
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈθɛn.tɪ.kli /; / əˈθɛn.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɛn.tɪ.kli /; / ɔːˈθen.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
in a genuine, real, or true way; showing something is authentic or done with authority

Examples

Speak authentically
She speaks Italian authentically, after living there for many years.
The restaurant serves dishes prepared authentically, following traditional family recipes.
The story was told authentically, capturing the culture and spirit of the time.
She wanted to live authentically, embracing her true self without pretending for others.
The play was authentically performed in the original Shakespearean English, captivating the audience.
The restaurant is known for its authentically Italian dishes prepared by chefs from Rome.

Related Terms

  • antonym: falsely - in a way that is not genuine or true
  • synonym: genuinely - in a real and sincere way
