catheterization

noun
insertion of a catheter for medical purposes
Pronunciation: / kæθətərɪˈzeɪʃən /; / kəˌθɛtərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌθiːtəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the medical procedure of inserting a catheter into the body to allow drainage, deliver fluids, or access internal organs

Examples

Cardiac catheterization
Doctors performed a cardiac catheterization to check for blocked arteries.
Following her operation, catheterization was necessary for several days.
The patient required catheterization during surgery to monitor urine output.
After her surgery, she required catheterisation for a short period.
Cardiac catheterisation is a key diagnostic tool in modern medicine.
The doctor performed catheterisation to help drain the patient's bladder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catheterization - same medical procedure, American English spelling
