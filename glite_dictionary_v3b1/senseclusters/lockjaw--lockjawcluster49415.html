lockjaw

noun
serious infection causing jaw spasms (tetanus)
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑkˌdʒɑ / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌdʒɔː / (BrE)
an acute and serious infection of the nervous system, typically caused by bacteria entering a wound, resulting in muscle stiffness and spasms, especially in the jaw; also known as tetanus

Examples

Lockjaw infection
If not treated promptly, lockjaw can lead to severe complications and even death.
The doctor explained that lockjaw is another name for tetanus, caused by a bacterial toxin.
Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent lockjaw after a deep wound.
