sidereal day

noun
astronomical day based on Earth's rotation relative to stars
Pronunciation: / saɪˈdɪriəl deɪ / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪəriəl deɪ / (BrE)
the time it takes for the Earth to complete one full rotation relative to a distant star, approximately 23 hours, 56 minutes, and 4 seconds, which is about 4 minutes shorter than a mean solar day.

Examples

A sidereal day length
A sidereal day is nearly four minutes shorter than a solar day due to Earth's movement around the sun.
Astronomers use the sidereal day to measure the rotation of Earth relative to distant stars.
Calculating a sidereal day allows scientists to track precise celestial movements.
