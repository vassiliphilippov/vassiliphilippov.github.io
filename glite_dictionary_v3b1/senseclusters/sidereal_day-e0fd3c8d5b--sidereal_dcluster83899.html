Examples

A sidereal day length
A sidereal day is nearly four minutes shorter than a solar day due to Earth's movement around the sun.
Astronomers use the sidereal day to measure the rotation of Earth relative to distant stars.
Calculating a sidereal day allows scientists to track precise celestial movements.
