trumpet arch

noun
conical squinch in architecture
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌmpət ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An architectural feature consisting of a conical squinch, typically used to support a dome over a square room.

Examples

A trumpet arch feature
A beautifully decorated trumpet arch can be seen in the old mosque's main hall.
The builders used a trumpet arch in the corners to support the heavy dome.
The unique shape of the trumpet arch gives the structure an elegant transition from square to dome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pendentive - a curved triangle of vaulting formed by the intersection of a dome with its supporting arches
  • synonym: squinch - an architectural device for transition from a square to a dome
