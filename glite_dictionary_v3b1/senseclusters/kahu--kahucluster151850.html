kahu

noun
Māori ceremonial cloak
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːhuː / (AmE), / ˈkɑːhuː / (BrE)
a traditional Māori cloak, often made from feathers or woven fibres, worn on ceremonial occasions

Examples

Ceremonial kahu cloak
The chief wore a beautiful kahu made from rare bird feathers during the ceremony.
Visitors admired the intricate patterns woven into the kahu displayed at the museum.
She was honored to receive a kahu as a symbol of respect from the Māori elders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: korowai - a type of Māori cloak decorated with tassels or feathers
