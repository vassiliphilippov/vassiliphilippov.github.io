to bring someone or something back to life or active existence after death, extinction, or disappearance

Examples

To resurrect the dead
Many legends describe heroes who are miraculously resurrected from the dead.
The committee hopes to resurrect the festival after a five-year absence.
The scientist claimed he could resurrect extinct species in his laboratory.
Many believe that Jesus was resurrected three days after his death.
Scientists cannot resurrect those who have died, despite advances in medicine.
The ancient myth says a hero will resurrect to save the world in its darkest hour.

Synonyms

  • bring back to life
    4.8 0.33/m
    bring back to life is a plain phrase for causing someone or something to live again, while resurrect is more formal and often religious.
  • raise the dead
    9.9 0.23/m
    raise the dead often suggests a miracle by a person, while resurrect covers returning to life or active existence more broadly.
  • reanimate
    7.2 0.69/m
    reanimate means give life or energy often in science fiction, while resurrect is used more for real death or wider revival.
  • revive
    4.2 8.27/m
    revive brings something back to life, health, or popularity in everyday use, while resurrect implies a full return after death.
  • return from the dead
    7.9 0.15/m
    return from the dead describes someone coming back on their own, while resurrect often names the act of bringing them back.
  • rise
    8.5 5.24/m
    rise (of the dead) sounds biblical and means come back to life, while resurrect is a broader, modern verb for restoring life.
