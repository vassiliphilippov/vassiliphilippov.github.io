turn in one's grave

verb
be posthumously upset or disturbed
1.7 6.9
Pronunciation: /tɜːrn ɪn wʌnz greɪv/ /; / /tɜːrn ən wʌnz greɪv/ /; / /tɜːrn ɪn wʌns greɪv/ (AmE), /tɜːn ɪn wʌnz greɪv/ /; / /tɜːn ən wʌnz greɪv/ /; / /tɜːn ɪn wʌns greɪv/ (BrE)
to be imagined as being very shocked, upset, or disturbed by something that happens after one's death, usually because it goes against what one cared about in life

Examples

To turn in his grave at sight
Historians agree that the original architect would turn in his grave at the sight of these modern alterations.
If our founder could see this policy, he would turn in his grave.
She said her mother would turn in her grave if she saw the mess they've made of the tradition.
