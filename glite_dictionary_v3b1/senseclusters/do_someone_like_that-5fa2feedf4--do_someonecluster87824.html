do someone like that

verb
treat unfairly or badly (informal)
5.8
Pronunciation: / du ˈsʌmwʌn laɪk ðæt / (AmE), / duː ˈsʌmwʌn laɪk ðæt / (BrE)
(informal) to treat someone in a way that is unfair or hurtful, especially unexpectedly.

Examples

To do someone like that unfairly
I can't believe he would do me like that after all we've been through.
Nobody expected the company to do its employees like that during the layoffs.
Why did you do her like that when she trusted you?
