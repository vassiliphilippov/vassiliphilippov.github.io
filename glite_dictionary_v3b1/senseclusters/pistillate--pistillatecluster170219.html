pistillate

adjective
having pistils (female organs) only; female-flowered
8.1
Pronunciation: / pɪˈstɪl.eɪt /; / ˈpɪstəˌleɪt / (AmE), / pɪˈstɪl.eɪt /; / ˈpɪstɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of a flower) having pistils (female reproductive organs) but no stamens (male organs); female-flowered

Examples

Pistillate flower
Botanists examined the pistillate blossoms to study the plant’s reproductive patterns.
Only the pistillate flowers can develop into fruit after pollination.
The squash plant in our garden has both pistillate and staminate flowers.
In some plants, pistillate blossoms grow on separate individuals from the staminate flowers.
Only pistillate plants can produce fruit in this species, while staminate plants contribute pollen.
The pistillate flowers were easily distinguished from the staminate ones by their central ovary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hermaphroditic - having both male and female reproductive organs
  • antonym: staminate - having stamens only; male-flowered (opposite of pistillate)
