alterability

noun
capacity to be altered
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɔltɚəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɔːltərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)

Examples

Genetic alterability
Genetic alterability is a key factor in the evolution of species.
Some legal contracts are intentionally designed to have a low degree of alterability.
The alterability of digital files makes them easy to update or revise.
