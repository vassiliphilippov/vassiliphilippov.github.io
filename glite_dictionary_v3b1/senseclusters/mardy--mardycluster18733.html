mardy

adjective
sulky or moody
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdi / (BrE)
sulky, moody, or easily upset, especially in response to disappointment or criticism

Examples

A mardy child
She became mardy when her friends went to the party without her.
Don't get mardy just because things didn't go your way.
His mardy attitude made it hard for the team to work together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: sulky - bad-tempered or resentful, especially because of disappointment
