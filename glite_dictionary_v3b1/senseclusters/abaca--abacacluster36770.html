abaca

noun
Philippine plant yielding Manila hemp
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːbəˈkɑː / (AmE), / ˌæbəˈkɑː / (BrE)
A Philippine banana plant (Musa textilis) with leafstalks that produce Manila hemp, used for making rope and paper.

Examples

An abaca plant
Farmers in the Philippines grow abaca for use in making high-quality ropes.
The fiber of the abaca plant is prized for its strength and durability.
Traditional paper making in some regions still uses fibers from abaca.
