orthodox

adjective
conforming to accepted beliefs or practices
5.9 5.16/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…îr.Œ∏…ô.d…ëks / (AmE), / Àà…îÀê.Œ∏…ô.d…íks / (BrE)
following or conforming to the traditional or generally accepted beliefs, customs, or practices, especially in religion or philosophy.

Examples

An orthodox belief
Her orthodox approach to education emphasized traditional teaching methods.
His views were considered highly orthodox within his religious community.
Many scientists challenge the orthodox theories to advance new ideas.

Synonyms

  • canonical
    7.9 1.12/m
    canonical is officially accepted by religious authorities, while orthodox describes following traditional beliefs and practices.
  • traditional
    1.5 119/m
    traditional refers to long-established customs or ways of doing things, while orthodox stresses strict following of accepted beliefs.
  • conventional
    2.8 24/m
    conventional follows usual or commonly accepted ways and is often neutral, while orthodox focuses on established religious or philosophical beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conventional - following accepted customs and proprieties
  • antonym: unorthodox - not conforming to established customs or beliefs
