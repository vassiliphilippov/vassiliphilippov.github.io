cohesion

noun
force holding molecules together
2.6
Pronunciation: / koʊˈhiːʒən /; / koʊˈhiʒən / (AmE), / kəʊˈhiːʒən / (BrE)
the force or process that makes things stick together or join as a whole (in science, such as physics or plants)

Examples

Molecular cohesion
Surface tension is a result of molecular cohesion at the surface of a liquid.
The cohesion of the material makes it difficult to break apart.
Water has strong cohesion due to hydrogen bonding between its molecules.
Botanists studied the cohesion of stamens in that species of lily.
Petal cohesion results in a tubular flower structure in certain plants.
Some flowers display cohesion when their petals merge during development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adhesion - the tendency of dissimilar particles or surfaces to cling to one another
