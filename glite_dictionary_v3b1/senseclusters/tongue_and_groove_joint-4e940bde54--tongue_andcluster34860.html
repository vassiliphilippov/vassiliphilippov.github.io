tongue and groove joint

noun
type of wood joint with interlocking edge
6.8
Pronunciation: / tʌŋ ənd ɡruv dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / tʌŋ ənd ɡruːv dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a joint made by fitting a projecting tongue on the edge of one board into a matching groove on another, commonly used in woodwork to connect flat pieces securely

Examples

A tongue and groove joint connection
Cabinet makers often prefer the tongue and groove joint for assembling strong, flat panels.
Installing a ceiling with a tongue and groove joint creates a seamless, attractive surface.
The wooden floorboards were connected using a tongue and groove joint to ensure a snug fit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mortise and tenon joint - a woodworking joint similar in function, using a tenon on one member and a mortise hole in the other
