fiducial

noun
reference marker used to align images or coordinates
Pronunciation: / fɪˈduːʃəl / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃəl / (BrE)
A physical marker or reference point used in medical imaging, computer vision, surveying, or navigation to align coordinate systems or guide image registration.

Examples

A fiducial in surgery
The surgeon placed a fiducial near the tumor to ensure precise alignment during radiation therapy.
Engineers used several fiducial points in the aerial images to align the maps accurately.
The algorithm detects tiny adhesive fiducial markers to register consecutive frames in real-time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiducial marker - a small physical object placed in or on a subject to serve as a reference for alignment
  • synonym: reference point - a fixed location used as a basis for comparison or alignment
