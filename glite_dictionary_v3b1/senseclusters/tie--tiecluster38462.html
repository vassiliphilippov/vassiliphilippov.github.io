tie

noun
horizontal beam in construction
9.4 6.3
Pronunciation: /taɪ/ (AmE), /taɪ/ (BrE)
a horizontal beam in a structure used to prevent parts from spreading apart, or a heavy beam under railroad tracks (sleeper)

Examples

A wooden tie
Builders installed a wooden tie across the rafters to add stability.
The roof was reinforced with a tie to prevent the walls from bowing outward.
Without a tie, the two sections of the frame might eventually separate.
Each wooden tie supports both rails and keeps them evenly spaced apart.
Maintenance crews monitor every tie for signs of rot or decay.
The workers replaced several ties along the busy railway line to improve stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sleeper - British term for a railroad tie; cross brace under railway rails
  • synonym: tie beam - a specific type of tie used in wooden roof construction
