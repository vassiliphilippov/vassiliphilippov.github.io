pound

verb
move or beat with a strong, regular rhythm
2.6 6.99/m
Pronunciation: / paÊŠnd / (AmE), / paÊŠnd / (BrE)
(of a heart or drum) to beat or move with strong, regular, and repeated sounds; to throb rhythmically and forcefully.

Examples

My heart pounds fast
After running up the hill, my chest was pounding loudly.
Her heart began to pound as she walked onto the stage.
The music made the floorboards pound beneath our feet.

Synonyms

  • thump
    3.5 1.09/m
    thump is a heavy, low rhythmic strike you can feel inside the chest, while pound suggests a stronger, more forceful beat.
  • beat
    1.2 64/m
    beat means a regular strike or sound in music or drums, while pound suggests a louder, harder, more forceful throb.
