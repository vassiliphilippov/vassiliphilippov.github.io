retrocession

noun
reinsurance transfer
9.9
Pronunciation: /re.trəʊ.ˈse.ʃən/ /; / /re.trə.ˈse.ʃən/ (AmE), /re.trəʊ.ˈse.ʃən/ /; / /re.trə.ˈse.ʃən/ (BrE)
the transfer of all or part of a risk that a reinsurer has accepted to another reinsurer

Examples

Insurance retrocession
The reinsurer arranged a retrocession to limit its exposure after several large catastrophe claims.
International markets often use retrocession to spread risk among multiple companies.
A retrocession agreement can help stabilize a reinsurer's financial position after major losses.
