(of a structure or object) having repeated square indentations resembling those in a battlement; crenellated.

Examples

Embattled walls
The ancient castle had embattled walls overlooking the valley.
The designer added an embattled cornice to give the house a medieval appearance.

Synonyms

  • crenelated
    9.9 0.01/m
    crenelated shows regular square gaps at a wall top, while embattled is used more broadly to describe that castle-like look.
  • castled
    9.3 0.01/m
    castled has the castle-like notches especially on battlements, while embattled is the standard architectural adjective for that pattern.
  • battlemented
    8.6 0.00/m
    battlemented means having visible battlements on a wall top, while embattled emphasizes the row of square notches themselves.
  • castellated
    9.8 0.00/m
    castellated suggests a building is made to look like a castle, while embattled refers only to its notched battlement detail.
