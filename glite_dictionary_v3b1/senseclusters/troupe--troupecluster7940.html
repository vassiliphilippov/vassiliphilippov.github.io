troupe

noun
group of performers, especially in theatre
4.4 4/m
Pronunciation: / truÀêp / (AmE), / truÀêp / (BrE)
a group of performers, especially actors or dancers, who work together in a theatre, circus, or similar entertainment company

Examples

A theater troupe
A famous French troupe toured across Asia last year, gaining international acclaim.
Our local theater troupe puts on a new show every summer for the community.
The ballet troupe will be performing on Saturday night at the city theater.

Synonyms

  • company
    3.5 12/m
    company includes performers and support staff and often refers to the whole group, while troupe names just the performing members who work together.
  • theater company
    2.7 0.8/m
    theater company creates and stages plays as an organization, while troupe refers more to the group of performers who travel or perform together.
  • ensemble
    5.3 20/m
    ensemble is a tight performing unit often in music or dance that supports a show, while troupe is a broader group of performers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: company - a group of actors, singers, or dancers who perform together
  • synonym: ensemble - a group of musicians, actors, or dancers who perform together
