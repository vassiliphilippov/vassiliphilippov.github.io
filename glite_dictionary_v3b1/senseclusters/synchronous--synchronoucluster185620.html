synchronous

adjective
requiring a common timing signal in communication
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs / (BrE)
(of digital communication) requiring a common timing signal between devices in order to coordinate transmissions.

Examples

Synchronous data transfer
A synchronous transmission allows bits to be sent continuously with precise timing.
Because the network is synchronous, each node relies on a shared clock signal for communication.
The devices must use a synchronous protocol to ensure data is transmitted accurately.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asynchronous - not coordinated by a common timing signal
