drilling fluid

noun
mixture used to lubricate and cool drill bit
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪlɪŋ ˌfluɪd / (AmE), / ˈdrɪlɪŋ ˌfluːɪd / (BrE)
A mixture of water, clays, and chemicals used in drilling operations to lubricate and cool the drill bit, remove cuttings from the hole, and stabilize the wellbore walls.

Examples

The drilling fluid mixture
If the drilling fluid becomes contaminated, it can cause significant problems during the drilling process.
Proper management of drilling fluid is essential for maintaining wellbore stability and safety.
The engineers tested a new drilling fluid to increase the efficiency of the operation.
