enablement

noun
patent law requirement
9.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈneɪ.bəl.mənt/ /; / /ə.ˈneɪ.bəl.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈneɪ.bəl.mənt/ /; / /ə.ˈneɪ.bəl.mənt/ (BrE)
the requirement in patent law that a patent application must describe an invention clearly enough for a skilled person to make and use it

Examples

Patent enablement
The patent was invalidated due to a lack of enablement in the application.
Lawyers debated whether the invention met the enablement standard required by the court.
Clear enablement is essential for a patent to be granted and enforced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disclosure - the act of making information known, especially in a patent application
  • antonym: insufficiency - the state of not being adequate, especially in describing an invention in a patent
