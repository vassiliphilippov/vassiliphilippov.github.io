cling

verb
remain emotionally/intellectually attached
1.4
Pronunciation: / klɪŋ / (AmE), / klɪŋ / (BrE)
to hold on tightly to someone or something, or to stick closely and not let go (physically or emotionally)

Examples

To cling to a friend
Even after many years, he continues to cling to his childhood beliefs.
People often cling to hope in difficult situations.
She clung to the idea of returning home, despite all obstacles.
He clung to the edge of the rock, afraid to let go.
She clings to her beliefs, even when others disagree.
The frightened kitten clung to its owner's sweater during the thunderstorm.
Even after several washes, the smell clings to the fabric.
The wet shirt clung to his back after the rainstorm.
Tiny bits of paper cling to the adhesive tape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adhere - to stick fast to something; to remain attached
  • antonym: detach - to separate or remove (especially emotionally or intellectually)
  • synonym: grip - to hold something tightly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something held
  • synonym: stick - to adhere to a surface or object
