Waldensian

adjective
of the Waldensians
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɔlˈdɛnziən / (AmE), / wɔːlˈdɛnzɪən / (BrE)
relating to the Waldensians, a Christian movement originating in the 12th century, their beliefs, or their historical regions.

Examples

Waldensian church history
The Waldensian church played a significant role in the history of religious reform in Europe.
Many Waldensian communities settled in remote mountain valleys to avoid persecution.
Scholars have translated several Waldensian texts to learn more about their beliefs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Catholic - relating to the Roman Catholic Church
  • synonym: Protestant - relating to Christian churches that separated from the Roman Catholic Church
