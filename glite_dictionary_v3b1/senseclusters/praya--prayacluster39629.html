praya

noun
genus of very long colonial siphonophores
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪə / (AmE), / ˈpraɪə / (BrE)
A genus of colonial siphonophores, marine organisms that can reach lengths of up to 40 meters (about 130 feet).

Examples

A praya colony
Researchers discovered a massive praya during a deep-sea expedition last year.
The praya is among the longest animals in the world, sometimes exceeding the length of a blue whale.
Unlike jellyfish, the praya is a colonial organism made up of many specialized individuals.
