noncircular

adjective
not circular reasoning
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɜːrkjələr / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɜːkjʊlə / (BrE)
not involving circular reasoning or not depending on a claim as its own proof

Examples

Noncircular argument
The professor praised her noncircular argument for its clarity and independence from assumptions.
A noncircular explanation relies on evidence rather than repeating the original claim.
To convince the audience, he presented a noncircular line of reasoning supported by facts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: circular - involving reasoning that repeats itself or depends on its own conclusion
  • synonym: logical - using clear and sound reasoning
