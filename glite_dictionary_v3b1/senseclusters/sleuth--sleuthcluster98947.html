sleuth

verb
to investigate secretly
Pronunciation: / sluːθ / (AmE), / sluːθ / (BrE)
to investigate or search for information about someone or something in a secretive or discreet way

Examples

To sleuth out clues
Reporters tried to sleuth out the source of the leaked documents.
She spent the afternoon sleuthing through old archives at the library.
The detective sleuthed quietly behind the suspect all night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry to discover facts
  • synonym: spy - to observe secretly
