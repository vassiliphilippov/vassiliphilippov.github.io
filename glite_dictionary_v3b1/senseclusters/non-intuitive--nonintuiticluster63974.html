non-intuitive

adjective
not obvious
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn ɪnˈtuːɪtɪv /; / nɑn ɪnˈtuːɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn ɪnˈtjuːɪtɪv /; / nɒn ɪnˈtjuːɪtɪv / (BrE)
not easy to understand, use, or predict because it does not match common sense or expectations

Examples

A non-intuitive interface
The software's non-intuitive interface made it difficult for new users to get started.
Some scientific concepts are so non-intuitive that they challenge our everyday understanding.
He found the controls on the remote to be non-intuitive and hard to remember.
The software's non-intuitive interface made it difficult for new users to find basic functions.
Some scientific theories are non-intuitive and challenge our everyday understanding of the world.
Her explanation was so non-intuitive that most people needed to hear it twice to understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterintuitive - contrary to what one would expect
  • antonym: intuitive - easy to understand or use without special knowledge
  • synonym: unintuitive - not easy to understand or use
