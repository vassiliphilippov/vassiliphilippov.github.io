Party of Democratic Kampuchea

proper noun
communist party that ruled Cambodia (1975–79)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːr.tiː əv də.mə.ˈkræt.ɪk kæm.pə.ˈtʃiː.ə/ (AmE), /ˈpɑː.ti əv də.mə.ˈkræt.ɪk kæm.pə.ˈtʃiː.ə/ (BrE)
a communist political organization that controlled Cambodia from 1975 to 1979, responsible for the deaths of an estimated three million people; commonly known as the Khmer Rouge

Examples

Party of Democratic Kampuchea regime
After being overthrown, the Party of Democratic Kampuchea continued to wage guerrilla warfare from the countryside.
The Party of Democratic Kampuchea seized power in Cambodia in 1975 after years of armed struggle.
Under the rule of the Party of Democratic Kampuchea, millions of people died as a result of executions, starvation, and forced labor.
