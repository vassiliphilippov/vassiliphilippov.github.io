steam line

noun
pipe for carrying steam
Pronunciation: / stiːm laɪn / (AmE), / stiːm laɪn / (BrE)
a pipe or conduit used for carrying steam, typically in industrial or heating systems.

Examples

A broken steam line
A broken steam line can cause dangerous high-pressure releases in the facility.
Maintenance workers replaced an old steam line to improve the heating system's efficiency.
The engineers inspected the steam line for any leaks before starting the turbine.
