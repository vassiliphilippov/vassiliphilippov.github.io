pavilion

noun
large, sumptuous tent
1.3 3.6
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈvɪl.jən/ (AmE), /pə.ˈvɪl.jən/ (BrE)
a large tent or open-sided building, often used outdoors for events, gatherings, or shelter

Examples

A large pavilion
A large pavilion was erected in the field for the guests to gather.
The royal couple dined under a magnificent pavilion draped in silks.
Traders set up their wares inside a colorful pavilion during the festival.

The music festival set up a large pavilion for the main performances.
Families gathered in the park's pavilion to celebrate the holiday together.
We had a picnic under the pavilion in the park.
The music festival was held in a large pavilion near the river.
People gathered in the pavilion to escape the sudden rainstorm.
