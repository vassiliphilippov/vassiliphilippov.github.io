relay

verb
to lay again
Pronunciation: / riːˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈleɪ / (BrE)
to lay something again, such as a road, floor, or track, by putting new material in place of the old

Examples

To relay the road
After the harsh winter, the city decided to relay the damaged road surface.
The workers relaid the old brick path in the garden to make it safer.
It took several days to relay the wooden floor in the historic building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replace - to put something new in the place of something old
