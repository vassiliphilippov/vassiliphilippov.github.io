Citizenship Day

noun
U.S. observance honoring citizenship
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtɪzənʃɪp deɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɪtɪzənʃɪp deɪ / (BrE)
a day observed in the United States to recognize the adoption of the Constitution and to honor all who have become U.S. citizens

Examples

Citizenship Day events
Every year, the local library hosts events honoring Citizenship Day with guest speakers and displays.
On Citizenship Day, new Americans are often sworn in at special naturalization ceremonies.
Schools across the country held assemblies to mark Citizenship Day and educate students about civic duties.
