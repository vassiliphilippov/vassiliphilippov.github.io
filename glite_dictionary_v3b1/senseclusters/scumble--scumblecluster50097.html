scumble

verb
to blend with thin paint
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈskʌm.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈskʌm.bəl/ (BrE)
to apply a thin, light or semi-opaque layer of paint over another color to soften, blend, or change its appearance

Examples

To scumble the colors
The artist scumbled a pale blue over the sky to create a soft, hazy effect.
To achieve a gentle transition between colors, she carefully scumbled the edges of the painted area.
He often scumbles lighter tones over dark backgrounds to add depth to his paintings.
The artist used scumbling to create a misty effect over the landscape.
By carefully scumbling white paint, she softened the harsh shadows in her portrait.
