dental caries

noun
tooth decay; cavity formation
4.1 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…õnt…ôl Ààk…õriz / (AmE), / Ààd…õnt…ôl Ààke…ôriÀêz / (BrE)
decay of a tooth caused by bacterial action, resulting in a soft, damaged area that can progress and destroy the tooth

Examples

Dental caries decay
Children are particularly vulnerable to dental caries if they eat too many sugary snacks.
Poor oral hygiene can increase the risk of developing dental caries.
The dentist found dental caries during the routine examination.

Synonyms

  • tooth decay
    2.2 0.36/m
    tooth decay is everyday English for destruction of a tooth by bacteria, while dental caries is the clinical term used by dentists.
  • caries
    8.1 0.00/m
    caries describes decay of bone or tooth in medical language, while dental caries refers specifically to decay of a tooth.
  • cavity
    3.4 1.12/m
    cavity is the hollow, decayed hole seen on a tooth, while dental caries is the disease process that causes that damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cavity - a hole in a tooth caused by decay
