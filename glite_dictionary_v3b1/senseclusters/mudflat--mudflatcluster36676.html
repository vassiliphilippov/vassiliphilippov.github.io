mudflat

noun
coastal muddy area
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌflæt / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌflæt / (BrE)
a flat area of muddy land, usually near the sea or a river, that is exposed at low tide

Examples

The mudflat at low tide
The children explored the mudflat at low tide, searching for small crabs and shells.
Many migratory birds stop to feed on the mudflat during their long journeys.
At sunset, the mudflat reflected the orange and pink colors of the sky.
Many shorebirds search for food on the mudflat when the tide goes out.
The children explored the mudflat and found small crabs hiding in the wet soil.
At low tide, the wide mudflat stretched far out from the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beach - a sandy or pebbly shore by a body of water
  • synonym: salt marsh - a coastal wetland flooded and drained by salt water
  • synonym: tidal flat - a flat, muddy area that is covered and uncovered by the tide
