ergometrine

noun
postpartum bleeding drug
Pronunciation: / ˌɜrɡoʊˈmiːtriːn / (AmE), / ˌɜːɡəˈmiːtriːn / (BrE)
a drug used in medicine to control bleeding after childbirth by causing the uterus to contract

Examples

ergometrine injection
The nurse prepared an injection of ergometrine to help the patient after delivery.
Doctors often use ergometrine to prevent excessive bleeding following childbirth.
After the birth, ergometrine was given to ensure the uterus contracted properly.
Doctors may administer ergometrine to help stop excessive bleeding after a woman gives birth.
The research team studied the effects of ergometrine on uterine muscle contractions.
Pharmacists must store ergometrine carefully because it is sensitive to light and heat.
