noncustodial

adjective
without legal custody
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkʌˈstoʊdiəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkʌˈstəʊdiəl / (BrE)
not having legal responsibility for the care or custody of someone or something, especially a child or assets

Examples

Noncustodial parent rights
The court granted the noncustodial parent regular visitation rights with the child.
A noncustodial sentence means the offender does not go to prison.
He is the noncustodial guardian and does not have daily care of his son.

Related Terms

  • antonym: custodial - having legal responsibility for custody
