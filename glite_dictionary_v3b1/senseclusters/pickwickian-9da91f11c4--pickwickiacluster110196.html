Pickwickian

adjective
humorous or eccentric
9.9
Pronunciation: / pɪkˈwɪkiən / (AmE), / pɪkˈwɪkiən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the humorous or eccentric style found in Dickens's 'Pickwick Papers', often meaning unusually humorous or odd

Examples

Pickwickian humor style
Her Pickwickian remarks always brought laughter to the classroom.
The play had a Pickwickian charm that delighted the audience.
He told a Pickwickian story full of odd characters and gentle humor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: serious - solemn or not humorous
  • synonym: whimsical - playfully unusual or fanciful
