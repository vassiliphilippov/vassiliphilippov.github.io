mizzenmast

noun
the aft or third mast on a multi-masted ship
9.9 8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪz.ən.mæst/ (AmE), /ˈmɪz.ən.mɑːst/ (BrE)
The third mast from the bow or the aft-most mast on a ship with three or more masts, especially on a yawl, ketch, or dandy.

Examples

The mizzenmast on a ship
On a ketch, the mizzenmast is located aft of the mainmast but ahead of the rudder post.
Sailors scrambled up the mizzenmast to adjust the rigging as the wind shifted.
The mizzenmast was damaged during the storm, making navigation more difficult.
On a ketch, the mizenmast is noticeably shorter than the mainmast.
The captain ordered the crew to lower the mizenmast during the storm.
They repaired a tear in the sail attached to the mizenmast before continuing their journey.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foremast - the mast nearest the bow of a ship
  • degree higher: mainmast - the principal mast of a ship, usually the second from the bow
