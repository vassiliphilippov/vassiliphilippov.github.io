megalodon

noun
extinct giant shark
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡələˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡələdɒn / (BrE)
a giant extinct species of shark that lived millions of years ago and was one of the largest predators in history

Examples

A giant megalodon
The megalodon is believed to have reached lengths of up to 18 meters.
Fossilized teeth of the megalodon have been found on every continent except Antarctica.
Many documentaries explore the mystery of how the megalodon became extinct.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dinosaur - a large extinct reptile from prehistoric times
  • synonym: shark - a large predatory fish with sharp teeth
