sabino

noun
Mexican cypress tree
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / saÀàbino ä / (AmE), / saÀàbiÀên…ô ä / (BrE)
a large cypress tree (Taxodium mucronatum) native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, known for its massive trunk

Examples

Ancient sabino tree
The ancient sabino near the plaza was taller than the nearby church and drew many visitors.
Many people gather under the shade of the old sabino during local festivals.
The sabino is famous for its wide trunk and long lifespan in Mexican folklore.

Synonyms

  • Taxodium mucronatum
    9.9 0.00/m
    Taxodium mucronatum is the formal scientific name used in books and research, while sabino is the common local name.
  • Montezuma cypress
    9.8 0.00/m
    Montezuma cypress is a common English name for the species in Mexico, while sabino is the local name used in parts of the United States.
  • Mexican swamp cypress
    9.6 0.00/m
    Mexican swamp cypress names the tree by its wet riverside habitat, while sabino highlights its massive trunk and broader native range.
