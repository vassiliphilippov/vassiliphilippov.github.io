Salamandridae

noun
family of salamanders and newts
Pronunciation: / səˌlæm.ənˈdraɪ.di /; / sæləˈmændrəˌdi / (AmE), / ˌsæləˈmændrɪdiː /; / sæləˈmændrɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of amphibians that includes true salamanders and newts, with elongated bodies, moist skin, and a lizard-like appearance

Examples

The Salamandridae family
Members of the Salamandridae family are often found in moist woodland habitats.
Scientists study the skin toxins of Salamandridae for their potential medical applications.
The Salamandridae include both newts and true salamanders.
Most members of the Salamandridae family undergo a larval stage before maturing into adults.
The Salamandridae are widely distributed in temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.
Unlike frogs, Salamandridae species retain their tails throughout their lives.
