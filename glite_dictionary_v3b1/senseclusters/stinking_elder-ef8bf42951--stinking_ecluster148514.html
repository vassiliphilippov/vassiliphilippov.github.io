stinking elder

noun
North American shrub or small tree with a bad smell
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A common North American shrub or small tree, known for its unpleasant smell, belonging to the elder family.

Examples

A stinking elder bush
Botanists identified the unusual bush as a stinking elder native to the region.
Some gardeners avoid planting stinking elder because of its distinctive odor.
The grove behind the house was filled with stinking elder and wildflowers.
