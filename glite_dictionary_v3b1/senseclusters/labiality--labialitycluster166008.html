labiality

noun
lip involvement in speech
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪbiˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌleɪbiˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a speech sound that is produced with the involvement of the lips, including lip rounding or labialization

Examples

Labiality in speech sounds
The labiality of the 'p' and 'b' sounds is important in English pronunciation.
Phonetics students learn to identify labiality when analyzing different languages.
Some speech disorders affect the labiality of certain consonants.
The labiality of certain vowels can make them sound quite different in various languages.
Phonetic studies often examine the labiality of consonants to understand accent differences.
In some dialects, increased labiality affects the clarity of spoken words.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dentality - the quality of a speech sound produced with the teeth
  • synonym: labial - a speech sound made with the lips
  • synonym: labialization - the process of making a sound with lip rounding
  • antonym: palatalization - the process of pronouncing a sound with the tongue near the hard palate
