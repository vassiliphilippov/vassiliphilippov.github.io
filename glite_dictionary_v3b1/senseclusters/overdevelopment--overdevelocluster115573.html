overdevelopment

noun
excessive film processing
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
excessive chemical development of photographic film or paper, resulting in images that are too dark or have too much contrast

Examples

Overdevelopment of film
The overdevelopment of the negatives made the photographs appear much darker than intended.
Photographers must avoid overdevelopment to prevent losing details in the highlights and shadows.
A timer malfunction led to overdevelopment, ruining several rolls of film in the darkroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underdevelopment - insufficient chemical development of film or paper, resulting in faint or low-contrast images
