caballero

noun
medieval knight
Pronunciation: / kɑːbəˈjeɪroʊ / (AmE), / kæbəˈjeərəʊ / (BrE)
a medieval knight or gentleman, especially one belonging to a chivalric order sworn to uphold honor

Examples

Medieval caballero
The young caballero rode into the village, his armor shining in the sunlight.
Legends tell of a brave caballero who defended the kingdom against invaders.
As a caballero, he was expected to act with honor and protect the weak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gentleman - a man of good social position, especially one who behaves with honor
  • synonym: knight - a man awarded a rank of honor for military service, especially in medieval times
  • antonym: peasant - a poor farmer of low social status
