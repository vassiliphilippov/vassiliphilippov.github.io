clumsy

adjective
awkward in movement or posture
1.0 1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈklʌm.zi /; / ˈklʌmzi / (AmE), / ˈklʌm.zi /; / ˈklʌmzi / (BrE)
lacking grace, skill, or coordination in movement or action; awkward or inept

Examples

Clumsy dancer
As a child, she was often called clumsy because she tripped over her own feet.

The clumsy waiter spilled soup on the customer's lap.
He made a clumsy attempt to fix the broken lamp, but only made it worse.
Sarah apologized for her clumsy handling of the delicate vase.
The new waiter was clumsy and spilled water on several tables.
