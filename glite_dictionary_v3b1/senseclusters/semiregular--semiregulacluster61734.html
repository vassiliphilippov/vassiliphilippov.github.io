semiregular

adjective
geometry; mixed regular faces
6.9
Pronunciation: /se.mɪ.ˈreɡ.jə.lər/ /; / /siː.mi.ˈreɡ.jə.lər/ (AmE), /se.mɪ.ˈreɡ.jə.lə/ /; / /siː.mi.ˈreɡ.jə.lə/ (BrE)
having regular polygonal faces of more than one type, with each vertex having the same arrangement of faces

Examples

Semiregular polyhedron
A semiregular polyhedron can have both squares and triangles as its faces.
The tiling pattern on the floor was semiregular, combining hexagons and squares at each vertex.
Mathematicians study semiregular tessellations to understand complex geometric structures.
