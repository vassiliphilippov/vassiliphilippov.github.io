castrator

noun
remover of power
Pronunciation: / ˈkæstreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈkæstreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that deprives someone or something of power, effectiveness, or influence, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Castrator of power
The new regulations acted as a castrator of creativity in the workplace.
Many critics viewed the policy as a castrator of local government authority.
Some believe that excessive bureaucracy is the main castrator of innovation in large companies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empowerer - a person or thing that gives power or authority to others
  • synonym: oppressor - a person or group that keeps others powerless or under control
