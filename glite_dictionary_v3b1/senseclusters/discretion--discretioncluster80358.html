discretion

noun
freedom to decide or act
7.5 4.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈskreʃ.ən/ /; / /də.ˈskreʃ.ən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈskreʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the freedom or authority to make decisions or choices according to your own judgment

Examples

Manager's discretion to decide
The final decision will be made at the company's discretion.
The manager has full discretion to approve or reject requests.
Use your own discretion when choosing the appropriate response.
Teachers are allowed some discretion in choosing the materials they use in class.
The awards were given at the committee's discretion.
You may spend the money at your own discretion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders, make decisions, and enforce obedience
  • antonym: compulsion - the action or state of forcing or being forced to do something; constraint
  • antonym: constraint - a limitation or restriction on action or decision-making
  • synonym: judgment - the ability to make considered decisions or come to sensible conclusions
