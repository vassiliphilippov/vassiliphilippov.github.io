misleadingness

noun
the quality of being misleading
5.4
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈliːdɪŋnəs / (AmE), / mɪsˈliːdɪŋnɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being deceptive or tending to create a false or incorrect impression.

Examples

The misleadingness of ads
Researchers studied the misleadingness of political advertisements during the campaign.
The misleadingness of the statement caused confusion among the public.
We must consider the misleadingness of certain statistics before drawing conclusions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceptiveness - the quality of giving a false impression
  • antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being true or honest
