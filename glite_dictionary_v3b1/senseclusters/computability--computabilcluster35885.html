computability

noun
ability to be computed
7.0
Pronunciation: /kəm.pjuː.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ /; / /kəm.puː.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /kəm.pjuː.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /kəm.pjuː.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or property of something being able to be solved or calculated by a computer or algorithm

Examples

The computability of problems
The computability of certain mathematical problems is a central topic in computer science.
Researchers study the computability of algorithms to determine their practical usefulness.
If a task lacks computability, no computer can ever solve it, no matter how powerful.
The concept of computability is fundamental in theoretical computer science.
Researchers debate the computability of certain mathematical problems.
Understanding computability helps define the limits of what computers can achieve.
