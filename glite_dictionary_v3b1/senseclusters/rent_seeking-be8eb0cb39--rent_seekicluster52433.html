rent seeking

noun
seeking profit by manipulating environment, not creating value
6.7 7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛnt ˌsiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛnt ˌsiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
(economics) The practice of attempting to increase one's share of existing wealth without creating new wealth, typically by manipulating the economic or political environment.

Examples

Rent seeking behavior
Many economists argue that extensive lobbying is a form of rent seeking that hinders economic growth.
Reducing rent seeking behaviors can lead to a more efficient and fair market system.
The company engaged in rent seeking by influencing regulations to block new competitors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: profit seeking - trying to make profit, typically through productive means
