superfluid

noun
frictionless liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌfluːɪd / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌfluːɪd / (BrE)
a liquid that can flow without friction, usually at extremely low temperatures

Examples

A superfluid at zero
At extremely low temperatures, helium becomes a superfluid and can flow up the walls of a container.
Scientists study superfluid behavior to understand quantum mechanics and low-temperature physics.
A superfluid can pass through tiny pores that would block ordinary liquids due to its lack of viscosity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquid helium - helium in its liquid state, which can become a superfluid at low temperatures
  • antonym: normal fluid - a liquid that flows with friction and resistance
