budworm

noun
plant-eating caterpillar
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌd.wɜːrm/ (AmE), /ˈbʌd.wɜːm/ (BrE)
a caterpillar (usually a moth larva) that feeds on the buds of plants or trees and can cause damage

Examples

A budworm caterpillar
The spruce trees were infested with budworms that ate most of the new growth.
Farmers worry about budworm outbreaks destroying their crops each spring.
A single budworm can damage several flower buds before it becomes a moth.
A severe budworm infestation killed large areas of spruce and fir across the region.
Foresters monitor budworm populations to prevent widespread tree loss.
The budworm feeds on young needles, weakening the trees over several years.
