Pope Leo IX

proper noun
reforming pope (1049–1054)
9.3
Pronunciation: / poʊp ˈliːoʊ naɪnθ / (AmE), / pəʊp ˈliːəʊ naɪnθ / (BrE)
(1002–1054) The pope from 1049 to 1054, known for initiating major reforms within the Catholic Church in the 11th century.

Examples

Pope Leo IX reforms
Pope Leo IX is remembered for his efforts to combat corruption within the Church.
During the Council of Reims, Pope Leo IX advocated for clerical reform across Europe.
Historians often cite Pope Leo IX as a key figure in the movement for papal authority.
