overcoach

verb
to coach excessively
7.5
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈkoʊtʃ/ /; / /ə.vər.ˈkoʊtʃ/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈkəʊtʃ/ /; / /ə.və.ˈkəʊtʃ/ (BrE)
to coach someone too much, giving excessive instruction that limits natural performance or spontaneity

Examples

To overcoach actors
The director worried that he had overcoached the actors, making their performances feel unnatural.
If you overcoach young athletes, they may lose confidence in their own instincts.
She realized she was overcoaching her students and decided to let them solve problems on their own.
