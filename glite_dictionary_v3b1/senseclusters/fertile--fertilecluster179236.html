(of land or soil) able to produce abundant vegetation or crops; highly productive or fruitful.

Examples

Fertile land
After years of careful irrigation, the once barren land became fertile and lush.
The fertile fields of the valley supply vegetables to the entire region.
This river delta is renowned for its fertile soil and thriving farms.

Synonyms

  • fat
    7.0 0.56/m
    fat describes very rich farm soil full of nutrients for crops, while fertile is the general word for land that produces abundantly.
  • fruitful
    3.9 5.17/m
    fruitful often means producing useful or successful results, while fertile refers especially to soil that grows many plants or crops.
  • productive
    2.7 5.33/m
    productive means making large amounts or results in many settings, while fertile applies mainly to land that grows plants.
  • rich
    1.0 37/m
    rich means having large amounts or strong nutrients, often said of soil, while fertile specifically means land that supports abundant growth.
  • rank
    7.1 0.03/m
    rank describes plants growing profusely and often wild, while fertile describes soil that supports healthy crop growth.
