liquid

noun
linguistics: speech sound like 'l' or 'r'
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd / (BrE)
a speech sound produced with minimal constriction in the vocal tract, especially referring to the consonants 'l' and 'r'; a non-nasal continuant consonant

Examples

An English liquid
In English, the liquid sounds can be found at the start of words like 'lip' and 'run'.
Phonologists classify 'l' and 'r' as types of liquid sounds.
The child had difficulty pronouncing certain liquid consonants correctly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consonant - a basic speech sound in which the breath is at least partly obstructed
