Peace of Westphalia

noun
1648 treaties ending the Thirty Years' War
9.8
Pronunciation: / pis əv wɛstˈfeɪliə / (AmE), / piːs əv wɛstˈfeɪliə / (BrE)
the series of treaties signed in 1648 that ended the Thirty Years' War in Europe, establishing a new political order and principles of national sovereignty

Examples

The Peace of Westphalia treaties
Historians view the Peace of Westphalia as a turning point in European diplomacy.
Many believe that the Peace of Westphalia marked the beginning of the modern international system.
The Peace of Westphalia established the principle of state sovereignty for the first time.
