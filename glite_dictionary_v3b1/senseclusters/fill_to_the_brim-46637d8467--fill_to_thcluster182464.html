fill to the brim

verb
make something completely full
Pronunciation: / fɪl tu ðə brɪm / (AmE), / fɪl tuː ðə brɪm / (BrE)
to make something completely full, so nothing more can be added

Examples

To fill to the brim a glass
Her voice filled to the brim with excitement as she shared the news.
The bartender filled to the brim every glass at the party.
The basket was filled to the brim with fresh fruit from the market.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: half-full - only partly full
  • degree higher: overflowing - so full that the contents spill over
