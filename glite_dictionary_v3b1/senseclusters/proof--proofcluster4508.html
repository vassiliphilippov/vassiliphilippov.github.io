proof

noun
formal logical demonstration
6.0 4.04/m
Pronunciation: / pruÀêf / (AmE), / pruÀêf / (BrE)
a sequence of logical statements demonstrating that a conclusion necessarily follows from premises, especially in mathematics or logic

Examples

A math proof
She spent hours writing out a proof for her geometry assignment.
The mathematician presented a detailed proof of the theorem during the seminar.
Without a proper proof, the statement cannot be accepted as true.

Synonyms

  • mathematical proof
    5.9 0.15/m
    mathematical proof presents a step-by-step logical argument for a theorem, while proof names the general sequence showing a conclusion follows.
  • logical proof
    5.5 0.01/m
    logical proof uses formal logic to show a statement is necessary, while proof is the broader sequence used in math or logic.
  • demonstration
    4.1 0.99/m
    demonstration can mean any clear showing or example in speech or class, while proof is a formal logical argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonstration - a logical argument that shows something is true
  • antonym: disproof - a logical argument showing that something is false
