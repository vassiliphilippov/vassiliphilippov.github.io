proof

noun
formal logical demonstration
4.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /pruːf/ (AmE), /pruːf/ (BrE)
a sequence of logical statements demonstrating that a conclusion necessarily follows from premises, especially in mathematics or logic

Examples

A math proof

The mathematician presented a detailed proof of the theorem during the seminar.
Without a proper proof, the statement cannot be accepted as true.
