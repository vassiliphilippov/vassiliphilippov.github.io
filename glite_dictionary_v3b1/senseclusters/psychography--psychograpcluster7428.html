psychography

noun
psychological profiling
9.9
Pronunciation: /saɪ.ˈkɑː.grə.fiː/ (AmE), /saɪ.ˈkɒ.grə.fiː/ (BrE)
the analysis or classification of people based on psychological attributes such as values, attitudes, and lifestyle, especially in market research

Examples

Psychography in marketing
Modern advertisers rely on psychography to better understand their target audiences.
The report included detailed psychography to identify consumer motivations and preferences.
By using psychography, companies can tailor their products to fit specific lifestyles and attitudes.
