vasodilation

noun
widening of blood vessels
3.4 6.1
Pronunciation: /veɪ.zoʊ.daɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /veɪ.soʊ.daɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /veɪ.zəʊ.daɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /veɪ.səʊ.daɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the widening of blood vessels, especially arteries, resulting from relaxation of muscular walls

Examples

Vasodilation of arteries
Alcohol can promote vasodilation, making your skin feel warm.
During exercise, vasodilation allows more blood to reach the muscles.
Some medications cause vasodilation to lower blood pressure.
During exercise, vasodilatation helps deliver more oxygen to the muscles.
Certain medications can cause vasodilatation to lower blood pressure.
Heat exposure often leads to vasodilatation in the skin to release excess body heat.
