enrobe

verb
to dress or cover with a robe
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈroʊb /; / ɪnˈroʊb / (AmE), / ɪnˈrəʊb / (BrE)
to cover or coat something completely with a layer, like putting a robe on it (often used in cooking or manufacturing)

Examples

To enrobe with chocolate
A layer of chocolate enrobes each delicate truffle.
She enrobed herself in a velvet cloak for the masquerade ball.
The priest was enrobed in ceremonial garments before the procession.
After roasting, the chef decided to enrobe the nuts in caramel for added flavor.
Manufacturers often enrobe cereal pieces with a sugary coating to improve taste.
The chocolatier will enrobe the strawberries in dark chocolate for dessert.

  • synonym: cover - to put something on or over, so as to protect or enclose
