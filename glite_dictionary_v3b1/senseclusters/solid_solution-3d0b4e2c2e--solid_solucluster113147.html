solid solution

noun
homogeneous solid mixture (e.g. in alloys)
4.3 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsɑː.lɪd sə.ˈluː.ʃən/ /; / /ˈsɑː.lɪd sə.ˈljuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈsɒ.lɪd sə.ˈljuː.ʃən/ /; / /ˈsɒ.lɪd sə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (BrE)
a homogeneous solid mixture in which one or more substances are incorporated into the crystal structure of another, forming a single phase, commonly found in alloys and minerals

Examples

A solid solution mixture
Some minerals form as a solid solution series, where chemical composition varies gradually between two end-members.
Stainless steel is a classic example of a solid solution in which carbon atoms are incorporated into iron crystals.
The properties of a solid solution depend on how well the atoms of different elements mix within the crystal lattice.
