directedness

noun
having clear purpose
6.8
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛktɪdnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛktɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a clear direction, purpose, or focus (not random or scattered)

Examples

Clear directedness
Her directedness helped the team stay focused on their goals.
The directedness of his actions impressed his supervisors.
Without directedness, it is easy to lose motivation and become distracted.
The directedness of the laser beam allows it to travel long distances without spreading out.
In psychology, the directedness of attention refers to how focused a person is on a specific task.
The flock's sudden directedness surprised observers, as the birds all turned sharply eastward together.
The directedness of her research helped her achieve impressive results in a short time.
Without clear directedness, the team struggled to make progress on the project.
His directedness inspired others to stay focused and work toward their goals.

  • synonym: purposefulness - the quality of having a definite purpose
  • antonym: randomness - the quality of lacking any definite plan or direction
