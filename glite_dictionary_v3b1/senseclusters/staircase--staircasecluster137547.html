staircase

noun
set of stairs connecting levels
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛrˌkeɪs / (AmE), / ˈstɛə.keɪs / (BrE)
A set of stairs, usually with a supporting structure and often a banister, that allows people to go from one floor to another in a building.

Examples

A staircase with banister
She decorated the staircase with garlands during the holidays.
The old house features a grand wooden staircase in the front hall.
We climbed the staircase to reach the apartment on the third floor.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: escalator - a moving staircase for transporting people between floors
  • degree lower: ladder - a device with two side supports and rungs for climbing up or down
  • synonym: stairway - a set of steps that people use to go from one level to another, especially indoors
