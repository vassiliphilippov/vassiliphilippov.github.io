Synonyms

  • take apart
    3.0 2/m
    take apart means to separate something into its parts, while disassemble means to take something into pieces, often carefully.
  • dismantle
    4.2 4/m
    dismantle means to take a machine or structure apart, while disassemble is a more general idea of taking items into pieces.
  • break apart
    3.0 2/m
    break apart means to split into pieces, often suddenly or by force, while disassemble suggests a planned, careful taking apart.
  • break down
    2.8 9/m
    break down means to separate into parts for study or because it fails, while disassemble focuses on taking things physically apart.
  • break up
    2.7 4/m
    break up means to break into smaller pieces or end a group, while disassemble means to take an object apart carefully.
