leatherleaf saxifrage

noun
a North American plant with leathery leaves and clustered white flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛðɚˌliːf ˈsæksɪˌfrædʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɛðəˌliːf ˈsæksɪˌfrædʒ / (BrE)
A plant of northwestern North America, with tough, leathery elliptic leaves at the base and erect, leafless flower stalks bearing white flowers in dense clusters; found in moist places.

Examples

The leatherleaf saxifrage plant
The leatherleaf saxifrage is easily recognized by its thick leaves and tight clusters of white flowers.
The leatherleaf saxifrage thrives in the moist rocky slopes of the Pacific Northwest.
We photographed several blooming leatherleaf saxifrage during our hike in Oregon.
