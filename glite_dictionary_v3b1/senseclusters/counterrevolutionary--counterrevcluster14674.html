counterrevolutionary

noun
person opposing a previous revolution
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˌrɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˌrɛvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
a person who seeks to overturn or reverse the changes introduced by a previous revolution

Examples

A counterrevolutionary leader
During the aftermath, former officials were branded as counterrevolutionaries and put on trial.
He was accused of being a counterrevolutionary for opposing the new regime.
The government arrested several suspected counterrevolutionaries after the coup attempt.
