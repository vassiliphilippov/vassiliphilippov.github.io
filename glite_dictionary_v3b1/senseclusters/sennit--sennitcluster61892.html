sennit

noun
flat braided cord used on ships
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪt / (BrE)
a flat braided cord or rope, especially one traditionally made from plant fibers and used on ships for various purposes

Examples

A sennit rope
The sailor wove a length of sennit to secure the rigging.
They repaired the damaged line using a strong piece of sennit.
Traditional sennit is often made from palm leaves or other natural fibers.
