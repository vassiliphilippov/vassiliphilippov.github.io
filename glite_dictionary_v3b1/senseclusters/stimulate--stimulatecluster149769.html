stimulate

verb
to provoke rapid occurrence
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to make something or someone more active, alert, or responsive, either physically, mentally, or emotionally

Examples

To stimulate rapid growth
Certain chemicals can stimulate rapid plant growth.
Her arrival stimulated a flurry of activity in the office.
The loud noise stimulated a sudden rush of adrenaline in the group.
Her curiosity was stimulated by the new scientific discovery.
The coach used inspiring stories to stimulate his team to perform better.
The government hopes that lower interest rates will stimulate investment and spending.
Music has the power to stimulate memories and intense feelings.
The inspiring speech stimulated deep emotions in the audience.
The scent of fresh bread can stimulate your appetite even if you aren't hungry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone or something become quiet and relaxed
  • antonym: depress - to make someone feel less active or enthusiastic
  • antonym: discourage - persuade someone not to take action
  • synonym: disturb - to interrupt the peace or order of; to unsettle emotionally
  • synonym: encourage - give support or confidence to do something
  • synonym: excite - to cause strong feelings of enthusiasm or eagerness
  • antonym: inhibit - to hinder or restrain something; to hold back or prevent activity
  • synonym: invigorate - to give strength or energy to someone or something
  • synonym: motivate - provide someone with a reason for doing something
  • synonym: provoke - to cause a reaction or event, especially one that is strong or unwelcome
  • synonym: trigger - to cause an event or situation to happen or exist
  • synonym: unsettle - to cause to feel anxious or uneasy
