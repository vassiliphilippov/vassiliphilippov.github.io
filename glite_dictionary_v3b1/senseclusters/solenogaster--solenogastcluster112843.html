solenogaster

noun
deep-sea wormlike mollusk
9.9
Pronunciation: /soʊ.lə.ˈnɑːg.stər/ /; / /sɑː.lə.ˈnɑːg.stər/ (AmE), /sɒ.lə.ˈnɒg.stə/ /; / /sɒ.lɪ.ˈnɒg.stə/ (BrE)
A small, wormlike, deep-water mollusk lacking calcareous shell plates, with a body covered in fine, slimy spicules on its mantle.

Examples

A solenogaster mollusk
A solenogaster moves slowly along the substrate, searching for tiny prey among the sediment.

Unlike most mollusks, a solenogaster does not have a shell, but features a soft and spiny mantle.
