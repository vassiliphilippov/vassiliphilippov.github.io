Bartonella

proper noun
bacterial genus
9.6
Pronunciation: /bɑːr.tə.ˈnel.ə/ (AmE), /bɑː.tə.ˈnel.ə/ (BrE)
a genus of bacteria that can cause diseases in humans and animals, often transmitted by insects like fleas or ticks

Examples

Bartonella bacteria infection
The veterinarian suspected that the cat was infected with Bartonella after noticing unusual symptoms.
Researchers are studying how Bartonella is transmitted between animals and humans.
Some cases of fever in children have been linked to Bartonella infections.
