Pholiota destruens

noun
a species of large, scaled fungus
Pronunciation: / foʊˈliː.oʊtə dɪˈstruː.ɛnz / (AmE), / fəʊˈlɪətə dɪˈstruː.ɛnz / (BrE)
A large species of fungus with whitish scales on the cap and veil remnants hanging from the cap; it has a thick, hard stalk.

Examples

Pholiota destruens mushroom
Mycologists identified the mushroom as Pholiota destruens due to its thick stalk and whitish scales.
The forest floor was dotted with Pholiota destruens after the heavy autumn rains.
You should avoid picking Pholiota destruens unless you are an expert in mushroom identification.
