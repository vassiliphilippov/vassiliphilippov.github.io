bishop's purple

noun
a moderate reddish-purple color
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪʃəps ˈpɝpl̩ / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəps ˈpɜːpl̩ / (BrE)
A moderate reddish-purple color, slightly bluer and lighter than heliotrope and duller than eupatorium purple.

Examples

She admired the bishop's purple in the artist's palette, finding it both rich and subtle.

The velvet drapes were dyed a deep bishop's purple for the cathedral's restoration.
