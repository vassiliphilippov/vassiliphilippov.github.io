dribble

verb
let liquid fall in drops
5.1 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪb.əl /; / ˈdrɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪb.əl /; / ˈdrɪbəl / (BrE)
to let or cause liquid or saliva to fall or flow slowly in small drops

Examples

To dribble down
Tears dribble down her cheek as she listens to the sad news.
The chef began to dribble olive oil over the roasted vegetables.
You should dribble honey onto the pancakes, not pour it.

Babies sometimes dribble when they are teething.
He tried not to dribble during his nap at the dentist.
