eye

verb
look at closely or with interest
Pronunciation: / aɪ / (AmE), / aɪ / (BrE)
to look at someone or something closely or with interest, often in a way that shows curiosity or desire

Examples

Eyed the prize
As they walked past, the security guard eyed them with suspicion.
He eyed the cake on the counter, wondering if he could have another slice.
The customer eyed the gold necklace carefully before making a decision.

  • degree higher: stare - to look at someone or something for a long time with wide-open eyes
