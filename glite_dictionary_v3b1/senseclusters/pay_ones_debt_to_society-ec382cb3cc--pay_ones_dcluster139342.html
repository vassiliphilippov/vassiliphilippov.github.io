pay one's debt to society

verb
fulfill punishment after a crime
Pronunciation: / peɪ wʌnz dɛt tə səˈsaɪəti / (AmE), / peɪ wʌnz dɛt tə səˈsaɪəti / (BrE)
to serve one's punishment or fulfill the required penalty after committing a crime, especially by serving a prison sentence

Examples

To pay one's debt to society by prison
After ten years in prison, he believed he had paid his debt to society and deserved a second chance.
Once she had completed her community service, she was said to have paid her debt to society.
The headline read that the former convict had finally paid his debt to society and rejoined the community.
