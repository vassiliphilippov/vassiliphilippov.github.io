economism

noun
frugality; thrift
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkɑːnəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmɪzəm / (BrE)
the careful management of money or resources; frugality (archaic use)

Examples

Personal economism
Her strict economism meant she saved every coin to rebuild the family shop.
In the past, economism was considered a virtue in managing household expenses.
The merchant's economism allowed his business to survive difficult times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extravagance - the act of spending too much money or using too many resources
  • synonym: thrift - the quality of using money and resources carefully
