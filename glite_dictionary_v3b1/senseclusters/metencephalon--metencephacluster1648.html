metencephalon

noun
part of the embryonic hindbrain
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtɛnˈsɛfəlɑn / (AmE), / ˌmɛtɛnˈsɛfəlɒn / (BrE)
the part of the embryonic hindbrain that develops into the pons and the cerebellum

Examples

The metencephalon part
Damage to the metencephalon can affect coordination and vital bodily functions.
During early development, the metencephalon forms important structures of the brainstem.
The cerebellum, derived from the metencephalon, is essential for motor control.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cerebellum - part of the brain at the back of the skull, controls muscle coordination and balance
  • degree higher: hindbrain - the posterior part of the brain, including the cerebellum, pons, and medulla oblongata
  • degree lower: pons - part of the brainstem that links the medulla oblongata and the thalamus
