Mandela

proper noun
South African anti-apartheid leader and president
Pronunciation: / mænˈdɛlə / (AmE), / mænˈdɛlə / (BrE)
(1918–2013) South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and politician; the nation's first black president (1994–1999), known for his role in ending apartheid and fostering reconciliation.

Examples

Mandela Nobel Prize
Mandela was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 for his efforts in ending apartheid.
Mandela's legacy continues to inspire people fighting for justice and equality.
Many streets and schools around the world have been named after Mandela.
