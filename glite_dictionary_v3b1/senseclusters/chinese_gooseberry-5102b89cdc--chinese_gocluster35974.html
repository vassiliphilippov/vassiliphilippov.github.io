Chinese gooseberry

noun
vine producing the fruit also called kiwi
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈɡuːzˌbɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ˈɡuːzˌbɛr.i / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to China, cultivated especially in New Zealand for its edible fuzzy fruit with green flesh, now commonly known as the kiwi fruit.

Examples

Chinese gooseberry vine
Before becoming popular worldwide, the Chinese gooseberry was mostly grown in its native China.
Some older cookbooks list Chinese gooseberry as an ingredient instead of kiwi fruit.
The Chinese gooseberry was rebranded as 'kiwi' when it was exported from New Zealand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kiwi - the modern name for the fruit formerly called Chinese gooseberry
