converge

verb
come together to form a single outcome
2.9 4.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɝrdʒ /; / kənˈvɝːdʒ / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to come together from different directions and meet at a place or point

Examples

Crowds converge at the square
All routes converge at the town square, making it a central meeting point.
Economic factors and political interests converged to create an unlikely alliance.
The research teams from different universities converged on a similar solution to the problem.

As the discussion continued, our opinions gradually converged on the same solution.
Thousands of fans converged at the stadium to watch the final match.
