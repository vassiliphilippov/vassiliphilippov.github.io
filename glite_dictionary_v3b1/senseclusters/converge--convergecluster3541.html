converge

verb
come together to form a single outcome
5.5 4.92/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàv…ùrd í /; / k…ônÀàv…ùÀêd í / (AmE), / k…ônÀàv…úÀêd í / (BrE)
to come together from different directions and meet at a place or point

Examples

Crowds converge at the square
All routes converge at the town square, making it a central meeting point.
Economic factors and political interests converged to create an unlikely alliance.
The research teams from different universities converged on a similar solution to the problem.
All the main roads converge at the city center, making it easy to navigate.
The rivers converge just before the falls, creating a dramatic scene.
Their ideas gradually converged during the group discussion, leading to a consensus.
As the discussion continued, our opinions gradually converged on the same solution.
Thousands of fans converged at the stadium to watch the final match.
Three roads converge at the town square, making it a busy intersection.

Synonyms

  • meet
    1.0 18/m
    meet is a general word for joining at a point, while converge suggests moving from different directions to the same spot.
  • come together
    2.1 2.38/m
    come together often describes people or things gathering closely, while converge focuses on paths or lines meeting from different directions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diverge - to move or draw apart; become different or separate
