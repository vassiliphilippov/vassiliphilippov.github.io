excretion

noun
elimination of bodily waste
3.8
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskriːʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈskriːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which living organisms eliminate waste products from the body

Examples

Body excretion process
Regular excretion is important for maintaining good health.
Some diseases can interfere with normal excretion and lead to toxin buildup.
The kidneys play a vital role in the excretion of waste products from the blood.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secretion - the process of producing and releasing substances by cells or glands
