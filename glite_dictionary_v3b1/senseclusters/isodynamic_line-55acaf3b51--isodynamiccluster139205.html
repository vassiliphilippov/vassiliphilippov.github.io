isodynamic line

noun
line joining equal magnetic intensity points
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊdaɪˈnæmɪk laɪn / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk laɪn / (BrE)
A line on a map connecting points that have equal intensity of magnetic force.

Examples

Isodynamic line on map
Scientists use isodynamic lines to identify regions with equal magnetic intensity.
The explorer traced the isodynamic line to study magnetic anomalies in the area.
The map includes several isodynamic lines to illustrate variations in the Earth's magnetic field.
