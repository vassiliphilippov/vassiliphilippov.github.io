complacent

adjective
self-satisfied, unconcerned
4.1 5.2
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpleɪ.sənt/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpleɪ.sənt/ (BrE)
too satisfied with yourself or your achievements, often without awareness of potential dangers or problems

Examples

Be complacent about success
After receiving several awards, she became complacent and stopped striving for improvement.
He was so complacent about his skills that he ignored useful feedback.
If the company grows too complacent, it might lose its competitive edge.
