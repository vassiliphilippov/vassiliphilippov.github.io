squeeze play

noun
tactic using pressure or manipulation
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskwiz pleɪ / (AmE), / ˈskwiːz pleɪ / (BrE)
an aggressive tactic intended to force someone to agree or comply, through pressure or manipulation of power

Examples

A squeeze play tactic
Faced with a squeeze play from the other side, she refused to compromise her principles.
The company used a squeeze play to pressure their competitors into accepting the merger.
Under intense negotiation, the manager attempted a squeeze play hoping the staff would give in to his demands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pressure tactic - a method used to intimidate or coerce
  • antonym: soft sell - a sales approach using subtle persuasion rather than forceful tactics
