jointing

noun
preparing board edges
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making the edge of a board straight or flat to prepare it for joining with another board

Examples

Jointing a board
Accurate jointing is essential for creating strong, seamless furniture joints.
He spent the afternoon jointing the edges of the boards before assembling the tabletop.
Modern woodworking shops often use machines for faster and more precise jointing.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: joining - connecting two pieces of material together
  • synonym: planing - smoothing or shaping wood with a plane
