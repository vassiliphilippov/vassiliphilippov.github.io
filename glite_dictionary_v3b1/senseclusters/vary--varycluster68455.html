vary

verb
make or become more diverse or different
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛri / (AmE), / ˈveəri / (BrE)
to change or make something different; to introduce variety or modify slightly

Examples

To vary the routine
Teachers often vary their lessons to maintain student engagement.
The chef likes to vary the menu each week to keep things interesting.
You can vary the recipe by adding different spices or vegetables.
Her opinions vary from day to day, depending on her mood.
The menu at this restaurant can vary depending on the season and availability of ingredients.
The prices of flights vary greatly over the course of the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: change - to make or become different in general
  • synonym: diversify - to make more varied or diverse
  • antonym: remain - to stay the same or unchanged
  • antonym: standardize - to make things uniform or the same
