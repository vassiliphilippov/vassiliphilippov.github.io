remobilization

noun
making mobile again
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which substances that were previously fixed or settled become mobile and are transported again

Examples

Remobilization of pollutants
Heavy rainfall led to the remobilization of pollutants that had settled in the lake.
Scientists studied the remobilization of nutrients in the soil after the flood.
The remobilization of sediments can impact water quality in rivers and streams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immobilization - the process of making something unable to move
  • synonym: mobilization - the process of making something movable or active
