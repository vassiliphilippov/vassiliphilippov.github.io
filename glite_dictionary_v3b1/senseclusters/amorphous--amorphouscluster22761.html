amorphous

adjective
having no clear structure or organization
5.2 5.8
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmɔːr.fəs/ /; / /eɪ.ˈmɔːr.fəs/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmɔː.fəs/ /; / /eɪ.ˈmɔː.fəs/ (BrE)
having no clear or definite shape or form; lacking a regular structure, especially in physical things like minerals, glass, or fog

Examples

Amorphous glass
Researchers observed an amorphous layer that differentiated the mineral from living tissue.
The substance was completely amorphous, showing no signs of cellular structure.
Unlike most crystals, glass is an amorphous solid with no internal geometric arrangement.
Obsidian is an amorphous volcanic glass, lacking a regular internal structure.
Some materials remain amorphous at room temperature instead of forming crystals.
The amorphous shape of the cloud made it difficult to describe.
A thick, amorphous fog blanketed the entire valley, making it difficult to see ahead.
Certain gels and plastics can appear nearly amorphous before they are molded into a set form.
The artist's new sculpture is amorphous, challenging viewers to define its shape and meaning.
