yellow-blindness

noun
rare color vision deficiency
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ ˈblaɪndnəs / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈblaɪndnəs / (BrE)
A rare form of color blindness in which sensitivity to yellow light is reduced or absent.

Examples

Yellow-blindness condition
People with yellow-blindness struggle to perceive yellow hues accurately in their environment.
Some rare cases of yellow-blindness have been reported in medical literature, though its existence is debated.
The study explored the genetic causes of yellow-blindness and other color vision deficiencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish certain colors; general term for color vision deficiency
  • synonym: dichromacy - condition of having only two functioning types of color receptors in the eyes
