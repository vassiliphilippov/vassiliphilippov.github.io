unconvince

verb
to change belief
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkənˈvɪns / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈvɪns / (BrE)
to cause someone to stop believing or accepting something they previously believed

Examples

To unconvince a person
Despite the evidence, nothing could unconvince him of his original theory.
The documentary managed to unconvince many viewers who once supported the project.
She tried to unconvince her friend, but he remained sure of his decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convince - to make someone believe something is true
  • synonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something
  • antonym: persuade - to cause someone to believe or do something by reasoning
