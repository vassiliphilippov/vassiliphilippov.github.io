OTC market

noun
market for off-exchange securities trading
Pronunciation: / oʊ ti si ˈmɑrkət / (AmE), / əʊ tiː siː ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a financial market where securities are traded directly between parties, typically via telephone or computer, rather than through a centralized exchange

Examples

The OTC market trades
Large companies sometimes trade on the OTC market instead of a traditional exchange.
Some investors prefer the OTC market for its wider variety of securities.
The OTC market allows for more flexible trading of certain stocks and bonds.
