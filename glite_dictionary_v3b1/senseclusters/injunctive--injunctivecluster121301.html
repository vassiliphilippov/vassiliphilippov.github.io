injunctive

adjective
relating to orders
9.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdʒʌŋk.tɪv/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʒʌŋ.tɪv/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdʒʌŋk.tɪv/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʒʌŋ.tɪv/ (BrE)
giving or relating to orders, commands, or rules that tell people what they should do (especially in legal, grammatical, or social contexts)

Examples

An injunctive order
The court issued an injunctive order to prevent the company from polluting the river.
In grammar, the injunctive mood expresses commands or requests.
The lawyer argued that only an injunctive remedy could stop the ongoing harm.
The school implemented injunctive rules to encourage respectful behavior among students.
Researchers studied how injunctive norms influenced people's willingness to conserve water.
An injunctive message told employees what actions were expected in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptive - describing how things are, not how they should be
  • synonym: imperative - giving an authoritative command
  • synonym: prescriptive - giving rules or directions about what should be done
  • antonym: prohibitive - serving to forbid or prevent
