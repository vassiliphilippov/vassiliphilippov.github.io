clingfish

noun
small fish with a sucking disc
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪŋˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈklɪŋ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, flattened marine fish with a sucking disc on its underside, enabling it to cling to rocks and other surfaces

Examples

A clingfish on rock
A marine biologist studied the clingfish to understand how its suction mechanism works.
Several species of clingfish can be found along the coastal waters of Europe and North America.
The clingfish uses its unique suction disc to attach itself to rocky surfaces in tide pools.
