seize

verb
suddenly and strongly affect (emotion, pain, etc.)
4.8 4.82/m
Pronunciation: / siz / (AmE), / siÀêz / (BrE)
(of an emotion, pain, or condition) to suddenly and strongly affect or take hold of someone.

Examples

Fear seizes her
A sudden panic seized her before the performance.
Guilt seized him as he remembered his broken promise.
He was seized with a terrible headache that left him unable to work.

Synonyms

    6.0 3.77/m
    grip clamps onto a person's feelings and can freeze their reactions, while seize means a sudden strong emotion that takes hold.
  • overtake

  • get hold of

  • sweep over
    4.6 0.86/m

  • sweep away
    4.6 0.41/m
    sweep away overwhelms someone emotionally and carries them off, while seize means to affect someone suddenly and strongly.
