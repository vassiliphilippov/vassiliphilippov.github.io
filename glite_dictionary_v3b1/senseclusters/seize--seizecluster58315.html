seize

verb
suddenly and strongly affect (emotion, pain, etc.)
Pronunciation: / siz / (AmE), / siːz / (BrE)
(of an emotion, pain, or condition) to suddenly and strongly affect or take hold of someone.

Examples

Fear seizes her
A sudden panic seized her before the performance.
Guilt seized him as he remembered his broken promise.
He was seized with a terrible headache that left him unable to work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grip - to have a strong, sudden effect on someone (of emotion, pain)
