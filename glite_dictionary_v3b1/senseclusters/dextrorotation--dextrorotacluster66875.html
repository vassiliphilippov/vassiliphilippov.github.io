dextrorotation

noun
rotation to the right (scientific)
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkstɾoʊˌɹoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkstrəʊ.rəʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
rotation to the right; especially, the clockwise rotation of plane-polarized light as observed in chemistry and physics

Examples

dextrorotation of light
In optically active substances, dextrorotation occurs when the light is turned clockwise.
The compound exhibits dextrorotation when dissolved in water.
The direction of dextrorotation or levorotation helps identify certain molecules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: levorotation - rotation to the left (anticlockwise); especially of plane-polarized light
