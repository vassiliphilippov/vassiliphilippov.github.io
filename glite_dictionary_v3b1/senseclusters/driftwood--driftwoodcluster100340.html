driftwood

noun
wood washed ashore or floating in water
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪftˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈdrɪftwʊd / (BrE)
Wood that has been washed onto the shore or floats in water, especially after being carried by currents, tides, or waves.

Examples

A piece of driftwood
A large piece of driftwood lay half-buried in the sand, bleached by the sun.
Artists often use driftwood to create unique sculptures and furniture.
The shore was littered with driftwood after the storm passed through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flotsam - Floating debris, especially from a shipwreck
  • synonym: jetsam - Goods thrown overboard from a ship and washed ashore
