surefooted

adjective
confident, reliable, not prone to error
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʃʊrˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
Confident and reliable in action or judgment; unlikely to make mistakes.

Examples

Surefooted leader
Despite fierce competition, her surefooted decisions led the team to success.
Her surefooted handling of the crisis impressed everyone at the company.
The journalist is known for his surefooted analysis of complex political issues.
