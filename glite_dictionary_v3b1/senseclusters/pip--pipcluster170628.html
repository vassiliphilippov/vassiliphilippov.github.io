pip

noun
disease of young poultry affecting the tongue
8.6
Pronunciation: / pɪp / (AmE), / pɪp / (BrE)
A disease of young poultry characterized by an inflammation and thickening of the membrane on the tongue, often resulting in difficulty in swallowing and loss of appetite.

Examples

Chicken pip disease
Early detection of pip can prevent serious health issues in a flock of chickens.
The farmer noticed that several chicks had developed pip and began administering treatment.
Veterinarians recommend cleaning the coop regularly to reduce the risk of pip among young birds.
