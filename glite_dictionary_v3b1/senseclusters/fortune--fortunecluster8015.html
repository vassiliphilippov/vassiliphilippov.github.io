fortune

noun
chance bringing good outcomes
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrtʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtʃuːn / (BrE)
a power or force, often called luck or chance, that unpredictably brings good or bad outcomes

Examples

By fortune alone
By sheer fortune, the lost wallet was found by an honest person and returned.
Many ancient sailors believed that fortune controlled the winds and waves of the sea.
Sometimes fortune smiles on those who least expect it.
It was pure fortune that we missed the storm by minutes.
No one can control fortune, but we can control our response to it.
Some believe that fortune played a large part in his success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fate - the development of events outside a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • synonym: luck - success or failure apparently brought by chance rather than through one's own actions
  • antonym: misfortune - bad luck; an unfortunate condition or event
