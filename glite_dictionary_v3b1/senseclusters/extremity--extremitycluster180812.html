extremity

noun
extreme condition or state
4.4
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɹɛməti /; / ɪkˈstrɛmɪti / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɹɛmɪti /; / ɪkˈstrɛmɪti / (BrE)
a very severe or extreme condition, or the highest degree of something

Examples

In extremity of danger
In moments of great extremity, people often discover reserves of courage they didn't know they had.
The patient was admitted in a state of extremity, requiring immediate medical intervention.
The village endured the extremity of famine during the long winter.
At the extremity of their patience, they finally spoke out.
She faced problems that tested her to the very extremity of endurance.
The extremity of the cold forced them to seek shelter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain or constraint
  • synonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • synonym: edge - the outside limit of an object, area, or surface
  • synonym: hardship - severe suffering or difficulty
  • synonym: limit - the final, utmost, or furthest boundary or point
