Isabella the Catholic

proper noun
Queen of Castile; unified Spain and sponsored Columbus
8.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˌzæbələ ðə kəˈθɑlɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌzæbɪlə ðə kəˈθalɪk / (BrE)
(1451–1504) Queen of Castile whose marriage to Ferdinand of Aragon unified Spain; known for instituting the Spanish Inquisition and sponsoring Columbus's voyages.

Examples

Isabella the Catholic queen
Isabella the Catholic played a crucial role in establishing the unity of Spain during the late 15th century.
Many historians regard Isabella the Catholic as one of the most influential monarchs in European history.
The voyages of Christopher Columbus were funded by Isabella the Catholic and her husband, Ferdinand.
