dichondra

noun
creeping herb used as grass substitute
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑːndrə / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒndrə / (BrE)
A creeping perennial herb with hairy stems, rounded to kidney-shaped leaves, and small white to greenish flowers, often used as a grass substitute in warm regions.

Examples

Dichondra ground cover
Dichondra thrives in warm climates and spreads quickly to cover bare soil.
The rounded leaves of dichondra give our garden path a soft, green border.
We planted dichondra as a lawn alternative because it requires little mowing.
