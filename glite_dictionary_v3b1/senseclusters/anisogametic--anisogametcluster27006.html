anisogametic

adjective
involving dissimilar gametes in reproduction
6.8 9.6
Pronunciation: /æ.nɪ.sə.ɡə.ˈmet.ɪk/ /; / /æ.nɪ.soʊ.ɡə.ˈmet.ɪk/ (AmE), /æ.nɪ.sə.ɡə.ˈmet.ɪk/ /; / /æ.nɪ.səʊ.ɡə.ˈmet.ɪk/ (BrE)
Relating to either of a pair of dissimilar gametes that combine in sexual reproduction, such as an egg and a sperm.

Examples

Anisogametic sex cells
In many animals, the male is the anisogametic sex, producing sperm cells.
Plants can also exhibit anisogametic reproduction, involving dissimilar male and female gametes.
The anisogametic sex typically produces smaller and more numerous gametes.
