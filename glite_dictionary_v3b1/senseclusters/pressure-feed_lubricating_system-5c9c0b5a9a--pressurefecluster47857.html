pressure-feed lubricating system

noun
lubrication system using pumped oil in engines
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər ˈfid ləˈbrɪkeɪtɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə ˈfiːd ljuːˈbrɪkeɪtɪŋ ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
A mechanical system in which a pump forces oil into the engine bearings of an internal combustion engine to provide lubrication.

Examples

A pressure-feed lubricating system
If the pressure-feed lubricating system fails, the bearings may suffer from inadequate lubrication and rapidly wear out.
In modern cars, a pressure-feed lubricating system is commonly used to improve engine reliability and efficiency.
The pressure-feed lubricating system ensures that critical engine components receive a steady supply of oil under pressure.
