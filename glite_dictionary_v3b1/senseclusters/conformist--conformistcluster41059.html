conformist

adjective
adhering to accepted rules or customs
6.2 4.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈfɔːr.mɪst/ (AmE), /kən.ˈfɔː.mɪst/ (BrE)
following established customs, rules, or standards; not willing to challenge accepted ways

Examples

Conformist attitude
Despite her creative talent, her artwork is surprisingly conformist in style.
His views became more conformist as he grew older and settled into a routine.
The conformist attitude among students discouraged any new ideas from emerging.
His views are decidedly conformist, always aligning with the dominant beliefs in his community.
In a conformist society, challenging religious doctrine can be difficult and even risky.
The school encouraged a conformist attitude, discouraging students from questioning established traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonconformist - someone or something not conforming to established customs or rules
  • synonym: orthodox - conforming to traditional or established beliefs
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who adheres to traditional ideas or practices
