geofence

noun
virtual geographic boundary
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.oʊˌfɛns / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.əʊˌfɛns / (BrE)
A virtual boundary defined for a specific real-world geographic area, used in technology to trigger responses when devices enter or leave the area.

Examples

A geofence area
Delivery companies use a geofence to alert drivers when they are near a customer’s address.
Once the car crossed the geofence, the tracking system sent an automatic update.
The app will notify you whenever your child’s phone enters or leaves the geofence around the school.
