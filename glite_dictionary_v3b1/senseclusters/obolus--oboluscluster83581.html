obolus

noun
ancient Greek coin
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːbələs / (AmE), / ˈɒbələs / (BrE)
an ancient Greek silver coin of low value, used as a small unit of currency

Examples

A silver obolus
The archaeologist discovered an obolus while excavating the ancient ruins.
In ancient Greece, an obolus was often used for small everyday purchases.
The collection included a well-preserved obolus dating back to the fifth century BC.
