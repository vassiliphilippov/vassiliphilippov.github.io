variola vaccinia

noun
infection from cowpox virus vaccination
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛriˈoʊlə ˌvækˈsɪniə / (AmE), / ˌværɪˈəʊlə ˌvakˈsɪnɪə / (BrE)
a local infection in humans caused by inoculation with the cowpox virus to confer immunity to smallpox, typically lasting about three weeks and leaving a pitted scar

Examples

variola vaccinia infection
After receiving variola vaccinia, the patient developed a small pitted scar on her arm.
Doctors monitored the reaction to variola vaccinia to ensure immunity against smallpox.
The introduction of variola vaccinia was instrumental in eradicating smallpox worldwide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smallpox - a serious, contagious viral disease eradicated by vaccination
  • synonym: vaccinia - infection with cowpox virus used for vaccination
