Great Plains

proper noun
vast grassland region of central North America
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt pleɪnz / (AmE), / ɡreɪt pleɪnz / (BrE)
a large, flat, grassy region (prairie) in central North America, stretching from Canada to Texas

Examples

The Great Plains are famous for their wide-open skies and expansive grasslands.
Bison once roamed the Great Plains in vast herds before European settlement.
Many settlers traveled across the Great Plains during the westward expansion of the United States.
The climate of the Great Plains is known for having hot summers and cold winters.
