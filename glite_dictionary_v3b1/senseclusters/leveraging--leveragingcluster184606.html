leveraging

noun
using borrowed funds to increase investment gains
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvɚɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛvərɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of investing with borrowed money to amplify potential gains, often increasing risk as well

Examples

Financial leveraging
Hedge funds often rely on leveraging to boost returns, amplifying both profits and losses.
Many companies engage in leveraging to increase their growth potential, but this strategy carries significant risks.
The bank's aggressive leveraging during the boom years led to its downfall in the recession.
