pre-Romanticism

noun
European cultural movement before Romanticism
7.1 8.2
Pronunciation: /priː.rə.ˈmæn.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ /; / /priː.roʊ.ˈmænt.ɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /priː.rəʊ.ˈmænt.ɪ.sɪ.zəm/ /; / /prɪ.rəʊ.ˈmænt.ɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A cultural movement in Europe from the 1740s onward that anticipated and influenced the Romantic movement in art, literature, and music.

Examples

Pre-Romanticism era
Many painters from the pre-Romanticism era explored themes of nature and imagination.
Scholars often regard pre-Romanticism as paving the way for the emotional emphasis of Romantic art.
The poetry of pre-Romanticism began to focus on individual experience and emotion.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Neoclassicism - European movement preceding pre-Romanticism, emphasizing classical forms and restraint
  • degree higher: Romanticism - artistic, literary, and intellectual movement that originated in Europe in the late 18th century
