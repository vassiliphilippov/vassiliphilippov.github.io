gridding

noun
dividing into grids
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of dividing an area into a grid for organization, analysis, or planning

Examples

City gridding
The city planner used gridding to organize the new residential district efficiently.
In scientific research, gridding helps ensure that data is collected evenly across the study area.
Artists sometimes use gridding to accurately scale and reproduce images on large canvases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mapping - the act of creating a map or plan of an area
  • synonym: partitioning - the act of dividing something into parts
