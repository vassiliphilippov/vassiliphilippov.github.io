psychogalvanic response

noun
change in skin's electrical properties due to stress or anxiety
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊɡælˈvænɪk rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊɡælˈvanɪk rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a measurable change in the skin's electrical properties, such as resistance or current, in response to psychological stress or anxiety

Examples

The psychogalvanic response test
The psychogalvanic response is often measured in experiments on stress.
The device tracks your psychogalvanic response to monitor emotional arousal.
When he was asked a difficult question, his psychogalvanic response indicated increased anxiety.

Related Terms
