redact

verb
formulate or edit in a style or language
9.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdækt / (AmE), / rɪˈdakt /; / rɪˈdækt / (BrE)
to edit, revise, or prepare text by changing its style, content, or form, often for publication or to remove sensitive information

Examples

To redact a document
Can you redact this passage in a more formal tone?

The lawyer carefully redacted the contract to ensure clarity and precision.
Before releasing the report, the agency had to redact sensitive information.
She spent weeks redacting the manuscript to correct errors and clarify unclear sections.
The legal team redacted confidential names from the documents before submitting them to the court.
