verbalism

noun
expression or communication in words
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrbəˌlɪz(ə)m / (AmE), / ˈvəːbəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the act or process of expressing ideas, beliefs, or opinions in words, especially spoken or written words

Examples

Verbalism in speeches
His tendency toward excessive verbalism sometimes made his lectures hard to follow.
Relying solely on verbalism is not enough; actions must support the promises made.
The author's verbalism allowed complex ideas to be conveyed clearly and effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expression - the process of making known one's thoughts or feelings
