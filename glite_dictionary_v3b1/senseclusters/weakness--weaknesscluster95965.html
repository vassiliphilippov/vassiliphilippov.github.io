weakness

noun
financial instability or lack of strength
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːknəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːknɪs / (BrE)
A state in which a currency, market, or financial entity is not strong or stable, often showing lower value or poor performance.

Examples

Market weakness
Investors were concerned about the weakness in the country's banking sector.
Recent weakness in the stock market has prompted many to reconsider their portfolios.
The weakness of the euro led to increased import prices across Europe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strength - the quality or state of being strong, especially financially
