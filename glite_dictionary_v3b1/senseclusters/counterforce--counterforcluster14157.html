counterforce

noun
military targeting strategy
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌfɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌfɔːs / (BrE)
a military action or strategy aimed at targeting an enemy's military forces or installations rather than civilian areas

Examples

Counterforce strategy
The government considered a counterforce approach to limit damage to civilian areas during the conflict.
A counterforce strategy focuses on disabling the enemy's military capabilities rather than attacking cities.
The treaty aimed to reduce the risk of counterforce nuclear strikes escalating into all-out war.

Related Terms

  • antonym: countervalue - a military strategy targeting an enemy's civilian population or infrastructure
  • synonym: preemptive strike - a military attack launched to prevent an anticipated enemy attack
