frequentist

adjective
statistical approach
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːkwəntɪst / (AmE), / ˈfriːkwəntɪst / (BrE)
relating to the school of statistics that interprets probability as the long-run frequency of events and bases inference on sampling distributions

Examples

Frequentist statistics approach
A frequentist method calculates confidence intervals based on repeated sampling from the population.
The professor explained the difference between frequentist and Bayesian interpretations of probability.
Many traditional statistical tests are based on frequentist principles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bayesian - relating to the statistical approach that interprets probability as a degree of belief
