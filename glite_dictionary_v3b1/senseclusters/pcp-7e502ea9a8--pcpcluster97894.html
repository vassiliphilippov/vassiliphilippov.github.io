PCP

noun
hallucinogenic drug, anesthetic
Pronunciation: / pi si pi / (AmE), / piː siː piː / (BrE)
a powerful drug used as an anesthetic in veterinary medicine and illegally as a hallucinogen; also known as phencyclidine

Examples

PCP drug use
The police arrested him for possession of PCP with intent to distribute.
Veterinarians rarely use PCP nowadays due to its high potential for misuse.
When abused, PCP can cause severe hallucinations and dangerous behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ketamine - another anesthetic sometimes used illicitly as a drug
  • synonym: phencyclidine - the chemical name for PCP
