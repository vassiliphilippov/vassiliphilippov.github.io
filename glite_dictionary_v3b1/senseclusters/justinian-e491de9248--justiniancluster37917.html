Justinian

proper noun
Byzantine emperor
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒʌstɪˈniːən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌstɪˈniːən / (BrE)
the Byzantine emperor (reigned 527–565) known for his legal reforms and major building projects, including the Hagia Sophia

Examples

Justinian the emperor
Justinian is remembered for codifying Roman law and shaping the future of European legal systems.
The construction of the Hagia Sophia was completed during the reign of Justinian.
Historians often study Justinian for his ambitious efforts to restore the Roman Empire's former glory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Justinian Code - the collection of laws and legal interpretations developed under Justinian
  • synonym: Theodora - wife and influential empress of Justinian
