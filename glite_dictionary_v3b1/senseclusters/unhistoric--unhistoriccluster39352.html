unhistoric

adjective
not historically accurate
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnhɪˈstɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌnhɪˈstɒrɪk / (BrE)
not based on or consistent with known historical facts; not historically accurate or true.

Examples

Unhistoric account
The novel presents an unhistoric version of the events leading up to the revolution.
Critics argued that the documentary was unhistoric and misrepresented key facts.
His account of the battle is largely unhistoric and contradicts primary sources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ahistorical - not concerned with or related to history or historical development
  • antonym: factual - based on facts or reality
  • antonym: historical - relating to history or past events
