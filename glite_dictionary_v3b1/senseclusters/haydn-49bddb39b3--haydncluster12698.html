Haydn

proper noun
Austrian classical composer (1732–1809)
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdn̩ / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdn / (BrE)
(1732–1809) Austrian composer who greatly influenced the development of classical music, particularly the symphony and string quartet.

Examples

Haydn classical composer
Haydn was a mentor to both Mozart and Beethoven, shaping the future of classical music.
Many symphonies by Haydn are still performed by orchestras worldwide.
The music of Haydn is noted for its clarity, balance, and wit.
