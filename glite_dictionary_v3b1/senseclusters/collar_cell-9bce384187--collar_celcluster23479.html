collar cell

noun
flagellated cell in sponges with a surrounding collar
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːlər sɛl / (AmE), / ˈkɒlə sɛl / (BrE)
a flagellated cell in sponges with a collar of cytoplasm around the flagellum, responsible for maintaining water flow through the body

Examples

collar cell in sponge
Each collar cell uses its flagellum to create a current within the sponge's body.
Scientists observed how the structure of a collar cell enables it to trap nutrients efficiently.
The collar cell filters food particles from the water passing through the sponge.
