winterkill

noun
damage from winter weather
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪntərˌkɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪntəkɪl / (BrE)
death or serious damage to plants or animals caused by severe winter weather conditions

Examples

Severe winterkill in crops
The unusually cold temperatures caused extensive winterkill among the young trees in the orchard.
Farmers worried that the lack of snow cover would increase the risk of winterkill in their crops.
After a harsh winter, the lake's fish population suffered significant winterkill.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drought - a long period of dry weather causing damage to plants and animals
  • synonym: frostbite - injury to body tissues caused by freezing
