deed of trust

noun
legal document securing a loan with property
8.4 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / did …ôv tr åst / (AmE), / diÀêd …ôv tr åst / (BrE)
a legal document in which property is transferred to a trustee to secure the repayment of a loan, such as a mortgage

Examples

Signed deed of trust
Before approving the loan, the bank required a signed deed of trust.
If the borrower defaults, the trustee named in the deed of trust may initiate foreclosure proceedings.
The home was purchased with the help of a deed of trust securing the lender's interest.

Synonyms

  • trust deed
    7.1 0.03/m
    trust deed names the document as a deed held in trust, while deed of trust names the same legal arrangement with different word order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mortgage - a legal agreement by which a bank lends money in exchange for taking the title of the debtor's property
