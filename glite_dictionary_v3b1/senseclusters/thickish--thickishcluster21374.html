thickish

adjective
somewhat thick
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat thick; slightly thicker than normal

Examples

Thickish sauce
The sauce became thickish after simmering for a while on the stove.
She wore a thickish sweater to stay warm during the chilly evening.
The fog was thickish this morning, making it hard to see the road.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thin - having a small distance between opposite sides
