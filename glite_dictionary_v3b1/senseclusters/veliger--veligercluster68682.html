veliger

noun
mollusk larval stage
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛlɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈviːlɪdʒə / (BrE)
the free-swimming larval stage of certain mollusks (such as snails and bivalves), which has a ciliated structure called a velum

Examples

The veliger larva
The veliger swims freely in the water before settling to the ocean floor.
Scientists observed the development of the veliger under a microscope.
After several days, the veliger transforms into a juvenile mollusk.
The marine biologist studied the veliger under a microscope to observe its developing shell.
After hatching, the snail enters the veliger stage before becoming an adult.
Many bivalves release their veliger larvae into the open water as part of their life cycle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - the mature, fully developed stage of an animal
  • synonym: larva - an immature form of an animal that undergoes metamorphosis
