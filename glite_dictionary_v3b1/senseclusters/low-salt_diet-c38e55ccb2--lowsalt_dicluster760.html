low-salt diet

noun
diet limiting salt intake
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /loʊ sɔːlt ˈdaɪ.ət/ /; / /loʊ sɑːlt ˈdaɪ.ət/ (AmE), /ləʊ sɒlt ˈdaɪ.ət/ /; / /ləʊ sɔːlt ˈdaɪ.ət/ (BrE)
a diet that restricts the amount of salt consumed, often prescribed to help manage conditions such as high blood pressure or edema

Examples

A low-salt diet
After her diagnosis, the doctor recommended a low-salt diet to help control her blood pressure.
He found it challenging to stick to a low-salt diet while eating out at restaurants.
Many processed foods are unsuitable for a low-salt diet because they contain hidden sodium.
