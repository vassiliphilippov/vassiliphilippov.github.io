summon

verb
officially order to appear (esp. in court)
5.3 9.88/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒmÉ™n / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒmÉ™n / (BrE)
to officially or urgently call or order someone or something to come, especially for a particular purpose

Examples

To summon a witness
She summoned the witness to testify during the trial.
The lawyer was summoned to appear before the judge on Monday morning.
You may be summoned to court if you ignore the jury notice.
After the accident, he had to summon a doctor immediately.
The board may summon you for an important meeting at short notice.
The principal decided to summon the student to her office for a discussion.
She quickly summoned a taxi when it started to rain heavily.
The principal decided to summon the parents to discuss their child's progress.
We may need to summon extra staff for the busy weekend.

Synonyms

  • call
    2.0 81/m
    call often means asking someone to come by phone or voice, while summon is a formal urgent order to appear.
  • cite
    6.4 1.84/m
    cite formally orders someone to appear in court, while summon is an official urgent call to come for various purposes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: call - to ask someone to come, often less formal
  • antonym: dismiss - to order someone to leave or not to appear
  • synonym: invite - to ask someone to come to an event or place
  • synonym: subpoena - a written order requiring someone to attend court
