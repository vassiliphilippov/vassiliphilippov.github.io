clay

noun
a type of soft, sticky earth
3.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /kleɪ/ (AmE), /kleɪ/ (BrE)
a type of heavy, sticky earth that is soft when wet and hardens when heated, used for making pottery, bricks, and ceramics

Examples

Wet clay for pottery
After the storm, the path was thick with clay and difficult to cross.
He molded the wet clay into the shape of a bowl.
The riverbank was covered in a layer of soft, slippery clay.
After the rain, the clay in the garden became sticky and difficult to walk on.
Bricks are often made by baking clay at high temperatures.
The sculptor molded the clay into a beautiful vase.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mud - soft, wet earth or soil, often mixed with water
  • degree higher: sand - coarser-grained material than clay used in building or construction
  • synonym: soil - upper layer of earth in which plants grow
