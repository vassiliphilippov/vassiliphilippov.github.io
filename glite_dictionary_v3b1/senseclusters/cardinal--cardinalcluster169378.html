cardinal

noun
senior Catholic official assisting the Pope
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdɪnl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl / (BrE)
a senior official of the Roman Catholic Church, appointed by the Pope to serve as a principal advisor and to participate in the election of new Popes.

Examples

Catholic cardinal
A new cardinal was appointed by the Pope last week.
Many cardinals gathered for the important church ceremony.
The cardinal traveled to Rome to attend the papal conclave.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bishop - senior member of the Christian clergy, generally in charge of a diocese
