work stoppage

noun
employee protest by refusing to work
4.2 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / w…ùÀêk Ààst…ëÀêp.…™d í / (AmE), / w…úÀêk Ààst…íp.…™d í / (BrE)
an occasion when a group of employees refuse to work as a form of protest, often about pay or working conditions

Examples

A work stoppage protest
A sudden work stoppage disrupted production across several industries.
After days of negotiation, the work stoppage finally came to an end.
The union called a work stoppage to demand better safety measures at the factory.

Synonyms

  • strike
    3.7 19/m
    strike is an organized stoppage by employees to demand better pay and conditions, while work stoppage is the general term for that action.
  • walkout
    4.8 0.84/m
    walkout is a sudden leaving of a workplace or meeting as protest, while work stoppage refers broadly to employees refusing to work.
