comae

noun
tufts aiding dispersal
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊmiː / (AmE), / ˈkəʊmiː / (BrE)
tufts of long hairs, feathers, or filaments on seeds, fruits, or plants that help with dispersal; plural of coma

Examples

Seed comae
The dandelion's seeds are carried by the wind thanks to their comae.
Botanists studied the structure of the comae on various plant species to understand seed dispersal.
The fluffy comae on milkweed seeds make them easy to spot in the air.

Related Terms
