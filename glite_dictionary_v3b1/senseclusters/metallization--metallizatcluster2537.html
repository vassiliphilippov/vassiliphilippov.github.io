metallization

noun
coating with metal
7.5
Pronunciation: /met.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /met.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of coating or depositing a thin layer of metal onto a surface, often to improve properties or create electrical connections

Examples

Metallization of plastic parts
The metallization of plastic components makes them more resistant to wear and corrosion.
Modern electronics often require metallization to create conductive pathways on circuit boards.
