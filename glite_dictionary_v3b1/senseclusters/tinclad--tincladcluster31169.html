tinclad

adjective
lightly armored
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪnˌklæd / (AmE), / ˈtɪnˌklæd / (BrE)
protected or covered with thin sheets of iron or tin, especially describing warships with light armor

Examples

Tinclad warship
The navy deployed several tinclad vessels to patrol the river during the war.
Unlike the heavily armored ships, the tinclad gunboats were faster but less protected.
Historians studied the role of tinclad warships in nineteenth-century naval battles.
