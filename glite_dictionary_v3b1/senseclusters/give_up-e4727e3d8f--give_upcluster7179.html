give up

verb
resign from a job or position
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /gɪv ʌp/ (AmE), /gɪv ʌp/ (BrE)
to give something you have (such as a right, possession, control, or position) to someone else, often by surrendering, relinquishing, or handing it over

Examples

To give up control
After many years as principal, she decided to give up her position and retire.
He will give up his seat on the board next month.
I never thought she would give up her role so easily.
He was forced to give up ownership of the property due to fraud.
If you break the rules, you may give up your right to appeal.
The company had to give up its license after violating regulations.
After a long struggle, the rebels had to give up their weapons to the authorities.
I'm not ready to give up my rights just because it's inconvenient.
The criminals finally gave up the stolen jewels when faced with evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forfeit - to lose the right to something as a penalty for wrongdoing
  • antonym: keep - retain possession or control
  • synonym: quit - to leave a job, position, or activity; to stop doing something
  • synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim; to give up
  • synonym: resign - to leave a job or office voluntarily
  • antonym: retain - to keep possession of something
  • synonym: spare - to make available or provide something extra
  • synonym: surrender - to stop resisting and submit to another's authority
