Philemon

proper noun
Christian figure addressed by Paul in the New Testament
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈliːmən / (AmE), / fɪˈliːmən / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) a Christian inhabitant of Colossae, known for being the addressee of an epistle from Saint Paul regarding his runaway slave Onesimus.

Examples

Philemon of Colossae
Philemon is considered a significant figure in early Christian history.
The Epistle to Philemon is one of the shortest books in the New Testament.
The apostle Paul wrote a letter to Philemon concerning his escaped slave Onesimus.
