hedge bindweed

noun
climbing wildflower with trumpet-shaped flowers
6.4 6.6
Pronunciation: /hedʒ ˈbaɪnd.wiːd/ (AmE), /hedʒ ˈbaɪnd.wiːd/ (BrE)
a common climbing wildflower of Eurasia and North America, with twining stems and pink or white trumpet-shaped flowers, sometimes classified in the genus Convolvulus

Examples

Hedge bindweed can quickly cover fences and hedges with its lush, twining vines.
Gardeners often struggle to remove hedge bindweed because of its persistent creeping roots.
The pink and white flowers of the hedge bindweed brighten the roadsides in summer.
