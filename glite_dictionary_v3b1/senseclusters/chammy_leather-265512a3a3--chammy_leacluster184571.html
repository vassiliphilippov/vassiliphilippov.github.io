chammy leather

noun
soft leather for cleaning and polishing
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæmi ˈlɛðər / (AmE), / ˈtʃæmi ˈlɛðə / (BrE)
a soft, pliable leather originally made from the skin of the chamois antelope but now usually from sheepskin, used for cleaning and polishing

Examples

A chammy leather cloth
A chammy leather absorbs water quickly, making it ideal for cleaning windows.
He polished the car with a chammy leather to give it a brilliant shine.
Many detailers swear by chammy leather for drying and buffing vehicles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chamois leather - a similar soft, absorbent leather used for cleaning
