fail-safe

adjective
guaranteed not to fail; safe from failure
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪlˌseɪf / (AmE), / ˈfeɪl.seɪf /; / ˈfeɪlˌseɪf / (BrE)
designed to prevent failure or danger; certain to work safely even if something goes wrong

Examples

Fail-safe plan
She always has a fail-safe plan ready in case her first idea doesn't work out.
The engineers installed several fail-safe mechanisms to ensure the system would keep running in an emergency.
This is my fail-safe method for remembering people's names at parties.
All modern elevators are equipped with fail-safe brakes to prevent accidents.
Engineers tested the fail-safe mechanism by simulating a power failure.
The laboratory installed a fail-safe system to shut down equipment in case of overheating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance; able to be trusted
