nonconservation

noun
lack of preservation
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkɑnsərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkɒnsəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the lack of preservation or maintenance of a quantity, property, or resource, especially when it is expected to remain constant

Examples

Nonconservation of energy
The experiment demonstrated the nonconservation of energy in the system.
In some chemical reactions, nonconservation of mass can occur due to gas escaping.
The theory was challenged because it implied nonconservation of electric charge under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservation - the act of preserving or maintaining something, especially a resource or quantity
