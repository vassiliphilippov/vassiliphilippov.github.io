fervid

adjective
intensely passionate or emotional
8.4 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ùÀê.v…™d / (AmE), / Ààf…úÀê.v…™d / (BrE)
having or showing intense passion or feeling; fervent

Examples

Fervid support
His fervid speech inspired the entire audience to volunteer for the cause.
She wrote fervid letters expressing her hopes for a brighter future.
The debate was marked by fervid exchanges between the opposing sides.

Synonyms

  • fervent
    6.1 2.25/m
    fervent shows very strong, sincere feeling in many situations, while fervid suggests a more burning, emotional intensity.
  • perfervid
    9.9 0.04/m
    perfervid is extremely intense and often literary in tone, while fervid is strongly passionate but more common in speech.
  • passionate
    2.8 20/m
    passionate feels strong and personal, often about love or causes, while fervid stresses fiery, intense emotion.
  • ardent
    6.3 3.70/m
    ardent shows eager, steady enthusiasm especially for ideas or causes, while fervid implies hot, intense feeling.
  • impassioned
    5.8 2.10/m
    impassioned describes a speech or plea filled with feeling, while fervid refers to a strong, burning personal passion.
