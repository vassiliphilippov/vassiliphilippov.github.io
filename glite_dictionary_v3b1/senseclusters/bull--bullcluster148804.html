bull

verb
speak insincerely; bluff
Pronunciation: / bʊl / (AmE), / bʊl / (BrE)
to speak insincerely, bluff, or try to deceive someone in conversation, often without concern for facts

Examples

To bull a story
He tried to bull his boss with a story about why he was late.
Stop bulling; just tell me the truth.
You can't bull your way through this meeting—they know the facts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be honest - to speak truthfully and sincerely
  • synonym: bluff - to try to deceive someone by pretending to be confident or knowledgeable
  • synonym: fib - to tell a small or harmless lie
