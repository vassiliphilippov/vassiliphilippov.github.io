Japanese Archipelago

noun
the island group forming Japan
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz ɑrkəˈpɛləˌgoʊ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn.iːz ɑː.kɪˈpel.ə.ɡəʊ / (BrE)
a group of over 3,000 islands off the east coast of Asia, forming Japan and extending from the Sea of Japan to the western Pacific Ocean.

Examples

The Japanese Archipelago
Earthquakes are common throughout the Japanese Archipelago due to its location on tectonic plate boundaries.
The Japanese Archipelago stretches over 1,300 miles, separating the Sea of Japan from the Pacific Ocean.
The climate varies widely across the Japanese Archipelago, from subarctic in the north to subtropical in the south.
