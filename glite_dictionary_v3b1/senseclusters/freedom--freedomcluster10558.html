freedom

noun
immunity from an obligation or duty
1.1 40/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfri.d…ôm / (AmE), / ÀàfriÀê.d…ôm / (BrE)
The state of being exempt from an obligation or duty; immunity from a required responsibility.

Examples

Freedom from duty
He received freedom from jury duty because of his age.
Religious institutions often enjoy freedom from certain taxes.
The new policy granted employees freedom from mandatory overtime hours.

Synonyms

  • exemption
    5.1 9/m
    exemption is a formal permission that frees someone from a specific rule or duty, while freedom is a broader state of being free from obligations.
  • immunity
    4.9 7/m
    immunity is official protection from legal action or penalties granted by authorities, while freedom is being exempt from a required duty or responsibility.

Related Terms
