SATA

noun
computer storage interface
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪtə / (AmE), / ˈsætə / (BrE)
a standard interface used to connect storage devices such as hard drives and SSDs to computers

Examples

SATA cable connection
Most modern computers use SATA ports to connect internal hard drives and solid-state drives.
He upgraded his laptop by installing a new SATA SSD for faster performance.
The technician recommended using a SATA cable to connect the new storage device.

Related Terms

  • antonym: IDE - an older standard interface for connecting storage devices in computers
  • degree higher: NVMe - a newer interface for connecting high-speed storage devices
