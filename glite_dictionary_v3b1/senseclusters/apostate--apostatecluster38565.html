apostate

adjective
having abandoned one's faith or allegiance
7.1
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːsteɪt / (AmE), / əˈpɒsteɪt / (BrE)
not faithful or no longer loyal to a religion, party, or cause; having abandoned former beliefs or allegiance

Examples

Apostate member
He was labeled apostate after openly rejecting the party line.
Several apostate members publicly criticized the church's new direction.
Some saw his apostate views as a betrayal of the movement's ideals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast; keeping faith
