self-satisfied

adjective
excessively pleased with oneself
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsfaɪd /; / ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈsætɪsfaɪd / (BrE)
feeling or showing too much satisfaction with yourself or your achievements, often in a way that ignores faults or annoys others

Examples

A self-satisfied smile
Don't be so self-satisfied; there's always room for improvement.
His self-satisfied attitude irritated his colleagues at the meeting.
She gave a self-satisfied smile after winning the award.
After receiving so much praise, he wore a self-satisfied smile all day.
Her self-satisfied attitude made it hard for others to offer constructive criticism.
The manager's self-satisfied remarks annoyed his hardworking team.

  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest opinion of oneself
  • antonym: modest - having a humble or moderate view of one's own abilities or achievements
  • synonym: smug - having or showing an excessive pride in oneself or one's achievements
