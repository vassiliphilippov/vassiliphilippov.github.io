strawman

noun
misrepresented, easily refutable argument
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɔːˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈstrɔːˌman / (BrE)
An argument or viewpoint that is misrepresented or exaggerated in order to be easily refuted or dismissed.

Examples

Strawman argument
By attacking a strawman version of her opponent's idea, she avoided confronting its real merits.
It's a common fallacy to criticize a strawman rather than engage with someone's actual argument.
The politician set up a strawman to distract from the real issues at hand.

  • synonym: red herring - a misleading or distracting argument
