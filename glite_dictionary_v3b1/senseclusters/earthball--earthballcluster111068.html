earthball

noun
a tough-skinned underground fungus
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrθˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈɜːθˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a type of fungus of the genus Scleroderma, having a tough outer skin and usually growing underground, resembling a truffle

Examples

An earthball fungus
Certain animals dig up earthballs to feed on their spore-filled interiors.
The earthball is often mistaken for a truffle due to its tough skin and round shape.
While hiking in the forest, we spotted a large earthball near the base of an old tree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: puffball - a fungus with a round fruiting body that bursts to release spores
  • degree higher: truffle - an edible subterranean fungus, often highly prized
