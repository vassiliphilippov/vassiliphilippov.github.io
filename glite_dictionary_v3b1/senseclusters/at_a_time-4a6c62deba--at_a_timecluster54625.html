at a time

adverb
simultaneously; in one group or period
Pronunciation: / æt ə taɪm / (AmE), / ət ə taɪm / (BrE)
all at once, together, or during a single period or occasion; simultaneously or in a single group

Examples

Two boxes at a time
He can carry two heavy boxes at a time without any trouble.
Please enter the classroom only five at a time to avoid crowding.
The medication should not be taken more than one pill at a time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: one after another - in succession, not at the same time
  • synonym: simultaneously - at the same time
