Cambrian

noun
Paleozoic geological period
Pronunciation: / ˈkæmbriən / (AmE), / ˈkæmbriən / (BrE)
the first period of the Paleozoic era, lasting from about 541 to 485 million years ago, marked by the appearance of diverse marine invertebrates

Examples

Cambrian period fossils
Fossil evidence from the Cambrian helps scientists understand early life on Earth.
The Cambrian period saw the rapid development of many complex marine animals.
Trilobites were especially abundant during the Cambrian.
