concupiscible

noun
faculty of desire
9.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈkjuː.pɪ.sɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /kən.ˈkjuː.pɪ.sɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
in medieval philosophy, the faculty or part of the soul responsible for desires for pleasure or bodily goods

Examples

The concupiscible part
In medieval philosophy, the concupiscible was thought to govern basic human desires.
The concupiscible and the irascible were seen as distinct faculties of the soul.
Scholars debated how the concupiscible influenced moral decision-making in individuals.
