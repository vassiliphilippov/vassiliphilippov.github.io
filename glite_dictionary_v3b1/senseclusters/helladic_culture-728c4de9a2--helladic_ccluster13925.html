Helladic culture

noun
Bronze Age culture of mainland Greece
Pronunciation: / hɛˈlædɪk ˈkʌltʃɚ / (AmE), / hɛˈlædɪk ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
The Bronze Age culture of mainland Greece, flourishing from approximately 2500 to 1100 BC, noted for its distinctive pottery, architecture, and early advances in society.

Examples

Helladic culture pottery
Archaeologists study pottery to understand the daily life of people during the Helladic culture period.
The discovery of the palace at Mycenae shed new light on Helladic culture.
The transition from Minoan to Helladic culture marked significant changes in ancient Greek society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cycladic culture - The Bronze Age culture of the Cyclades islands, characterized by marble idols
  • synonym: Minoan culture - The Bronze Age culture of Crete, noted for its palaces and art
