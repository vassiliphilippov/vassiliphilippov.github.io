slop

noun
deep, soft mud or slush
3.4
Pronunciation: / slɑp / (AmE), / slɒp / (BrE)
Deep, soft mud mixed with water or slush, making walking difficult.

Examples

Deep slop
Her boots were caked with slop from walking by the riverbank.
The construction site was filled with sticky slop that made movement slow.
We had to cross a field covered in thick slop after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mud - wet, soft earth
  • synonym: slush - partially melted snow or ice mixed with water
