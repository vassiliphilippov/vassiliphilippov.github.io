short-staffed

adjective
not enough staff or workers
4.8 3.9
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt ˈstæft / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈstɑːft / (BrE)
having fewer workers or assistants than are needed for the work to be done effectively

Examples

Short-staffed hospital
Because the hospital was short-staffed, nurses had to work overtime.
The restaurant was short-staffed last night, so service was slower than usual.
We can't handle this many customers if we're short-staffed on weekends.
