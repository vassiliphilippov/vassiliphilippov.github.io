penicillin-resistant

adjective
unaffected by penicillin (medicine)
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnɪˌsɪlɪn rɪˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˌsɪlɪn rɪˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
(of bacteria or other microorganisms) unaffected by penicillin; not killed or inhibited by penicillin.

Examples

Penicillin-resistant bacteria
Doctors are concerned about the spread of penicillin-resistant pathogens in the community.
Some penicillin-resistant strains of bacteria are responsible for hospital-acquired infections.
Testing revealed that the infection was caused by a penicillin-resistant bacterium.

Related Terms

  • antonym: penicillin-sensitive - (of bacteria or microorganisms) susceptible to penicillin; can be killed or inhibited by penicillin
