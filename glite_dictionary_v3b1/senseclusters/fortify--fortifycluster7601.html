fortify

verb
prepare for military confrontation
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrtɪˌfaɪ /; / ˈfɔːrtɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to strengthen a place or group, especially by building defenses or preparing for attack

Examples

To fortify a city
As tensions rose, the general ordered the troops to fortify before dawn.
She decided to fortify herself mentally for the challenges of the upcoming battle.
The country's armed forces began to fortify their position along the border.
During the war, the villagers fortified their homes by building trenches and barriers.
The castle was fortified with high stone walls to withstand invaders.
The town quickly fortified its perimeter after rumors of an impending attack spread.

  • synonym: strengthen - to make stronger
  • antonym: weaken - to make less strong or secure
