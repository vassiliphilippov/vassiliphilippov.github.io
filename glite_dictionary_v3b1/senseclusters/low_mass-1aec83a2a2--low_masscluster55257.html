Low Mass

noun
a Mass without music
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˈmæs / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈmæs / (BrE)
A Roman Catholic Mass recited by a priest with little or no music and fewer ceremonial features than a High Mass.

Examples

A Low Mass service
Historically, Low Mass has been the most common form of worship in rural parishes.
The priest celebrated a Low Mass early in the morning for a handful of parishioners.
Unlike a High Mass, a Low Mass involves little singing and is more subdued.

Related Terms

  • antonym: High Mass - A Roman Catholic Mass with chanting and more ceremonial elements
