Carya aquatica

noun
species of hickory tree, water hickory
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛəriə əˈkwætɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrɪə əˈkwatɪkə / (BrE)
a species of hickory native to the southern United States, characterized by many narrow leaflets and bitter nuts, commonly known as water hickory

Examples

Carya aquatica tree
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Carya aquatica near the riverbanks.
The Carya aquatica is often found in wet bottomland forests across the southeastern United States.
The nuts of Carya aquatica are less palatable than those of other hickories due to their bitterness.
