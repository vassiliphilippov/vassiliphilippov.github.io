echograph

noun
instrument for ultrasound imaging
1.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈek.əʊ.græf/ /; / /ˈek.oʊ.græf/ (AmE), /ˈek.əʊ.grɑːf/ (BrE)
An instrument used to produce images or records by means of ultrasound, especially in medical diagnostics.

Examples

Medical echograph
Modern clinics often rely on an echograph for quick and non-invasive diagnoses.
She scheduled an appointment for an echograph exam on her liver.
The doctor used an echograph to monitor the baby's heartbeat.
