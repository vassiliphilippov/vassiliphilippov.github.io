ruin

verb
to deprive of virginity (archaic)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈruːɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɪn / (BrE)
(archaic) To deprive (a woman) of her virginity, typically with the implication of dishonor or disgrace.

Examples

To ruin a young woman
He was said to have ruined the daughter of a local merchant, leading to scandal.
In many Victorian novels, a young woman's reputation could be destroyed if a man were to ruin her.
The story tells of a lord who tried to ruin the heroine, but she escaped his advances.
