noun

the act of making something legally null, void, or without effect; the action of counteracting or invalidating something

Examples

Court nullification of a law
Some states attempted to assert their power through the nullification of federal legislation.
The court's decision resulted in the nullification of the restrictive law.
The treaty called for the nullification of previous agreements between the countries.

Synonyms

    7.3 0.17/m

  • annulment
    6.5 0.79/m
    annulment is a legal cancellation that treats a marriage or contract as never existing, while nullification makes something legally void.
  • annulation
    9.8 0.00/m
    annulation is the formal act of annulling a document or decision, whereas nullification refers more broadly to making something legally void.
  • abrogation
    8.9 0.37/m
    abrogation is the official repeal of a law or rule, while nullification means making an act or rule have no legal effect.
  • revocation
    7.3 0.69/m
    revocation is the cancellation of a permission, license, or decision that was granted earlier, whereas nullification makes something legally void.
