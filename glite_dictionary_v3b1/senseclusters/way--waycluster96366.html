way

noun
distance to a place
1.0
Pronunciation: / weɪ / (AmE), / weɪ / (BrE)
a route, road, path, or journey from one place to another

Examples

A long way
It's a long way from here to the nearest town.
They walked a great way through the forest to reach the campsite.
We still have a short way to go before reaching the top.
After getting lost, she finally found the way home.
This way leads directly to the city center.
We stopped to ask a passerby for the way to the station.
Please make way for the ambulance so it can pass through quickly.
The hikers struggled to find a clear way through the dense jungle.
There wasn’t enough way for the large truck to enter the narrow alley.

Related Terms

  • antonym: destination - the place to which someone is going or something is being sent
  • synonym: distance - the length of space between two points
  • synonym: path - a track or way made for or by people walking
  • synonym: road - a wide way leading from one place to another, especially one with a specially prepared surface for vehicles
  • synonym: route - a way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination
