elastica

noun
elastic structure
8.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæstɪkə / (AmE), / ɪˈlæstɪkə / (BrE)
a structure or material that can return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed

Examples

Artery elastica
The walls of arteries contain elastica to help them expand and contract with each heartbeat.
Scientists studied the elastica in the tissue to understand its flexibility.
Damage to the elastica can affect the function of blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elastic - a material that returns to its original shape after being stretched
  • antonym: rigid - not able to be bent or changed in shape easily
