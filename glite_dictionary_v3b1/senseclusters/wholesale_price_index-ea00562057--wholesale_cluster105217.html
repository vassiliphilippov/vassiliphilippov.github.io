wholesale price index

noun
economic index of wholesale prices
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊlˌseɪl ˈpraɪs ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl ˈpraɪs ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
an index that measures and tracks the changes in the price of goods sold in bulk by wholesalers over time

Examples

The wholesale price index rises
A steep rise in the wholesale price index can indicate future increases in consumer prices.
Over the past year, the wholesale price index has shown only modest increases.
The government regularly publishes the wholesale price index to monitor inflation trends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consumer price index - an index tracking changes in the price of consumer goods and services
