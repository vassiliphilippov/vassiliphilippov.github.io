Sherlock

noun
informal term for a detective
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɜːrlɑk /; / ˈʃɝːlɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃɜːlɒk / (BrE)
(informal) a person who is good at investigating or figuring things out, especially in a clever or humorous way (from Sherlock Holmes)

Examples

A clever Sherlock
Come on, Sherlock, tell us how you figured out who ate the last cookie.
Nice work, Sherlock—you found the missing keys under the couch.
She thinks she's a real Sherlock after guessing the surprise party.
Don’t try to play Sherlock; we all know who broke the vase.
The security team needs a real Sherlock to crack this case.
With his Sherlock instincts, he pieced the evidence together quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detective - a person, especially a police officer, whose occupation is to investigate and solve crimes
  • synonym: sleuth - an informal or old-fashioned term for a detective
