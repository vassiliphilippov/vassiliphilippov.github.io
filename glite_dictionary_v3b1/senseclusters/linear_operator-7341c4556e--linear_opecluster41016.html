linear operator

noun
mathematical operator preserving addition and scalar multiplication
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪniɚ ˈɑpəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪniə ˈɒpəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a mathematical operator that satisfies the distributive law, meaning it preserves addition and scalar multiplication: A(f + g) = Af + Ag

Examples

A linear operator preserves addition
Any transformation that qualifies as a linear operator must preserve both addition and scalar multiplication.
Fourier transforms are often discussed in terms of their properties as a linear operator.
The differentiation operator is a classic example of a linear operator in calculus.
