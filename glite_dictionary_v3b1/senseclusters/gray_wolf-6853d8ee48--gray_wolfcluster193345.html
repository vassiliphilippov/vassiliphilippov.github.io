gray wolf

noun
a large wolf with grey fur, native to northern regions
2.4 2.6
Pronunciation: /greɪ wʊlf/ (AmE), /greɪ wʊlf/ (BrE)
a large wild wolf native to North America and Eurasia, known for its brindled grey coat and living mainly in forested northern regions; also known as the timber wolf.

Examples

A pack of gray wolves roamed through the snowy forest in search of food.

The gray wolf is one of the most widely distributed land mammals in the world.
Conservation efforts have helped the grey wolf population recover in some areas.
The grey wolf is known for its intelligence and complex social structure.
We spotted a pack of grey wolves moving through the snowy forest.
