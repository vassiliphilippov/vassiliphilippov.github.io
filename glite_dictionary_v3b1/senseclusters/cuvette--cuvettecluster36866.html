cuvette

noun
small water-collecting hollow
Pronunciation: / kuːˈvɛt / (AmE), / kjuːˈvɛt / (BrE)
a small hollow or sunken area on a surface or in the landscape that collects water

Examples

A field cuvette
After the rain, a shallow cuvette formed at the edge of the field.
The hikers stopped to rest beside a cuvette filled with clear water.
Wildlife often gathers around the cuvette during the dry season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basin - a natural or artificial hollow that holds water
  • synonym: depression - a sunken or lower area on a surface
  • antonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land
