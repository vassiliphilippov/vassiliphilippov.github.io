bleb

noun
small fluid-filled blister
8.4
Pronunciation: / blɛb / (AmE), / blɛb / (BrE)
a small blister or bubble on a surface, such as skin or a cell membrane

Examples

A small bleb on skin
A small bleb formed at the injection site, which later disappeared.
Doctors examined the fluid inside the bleb to check for infection.
The patient developed a bleb on his arm after the burn.
The scientist observed several blebs forming on the cell membrane under the microscope.
A small bleb appeared on his skin after he accidentally touched the hot pan.
Doctors noted the presence of blebs in the lung tissue during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blister - A small bubble on the skin filled with serum and caused by friction, burning, or other damage.
  • synonym: vesicle - a small fluid-filled sac in a cell or tissue
