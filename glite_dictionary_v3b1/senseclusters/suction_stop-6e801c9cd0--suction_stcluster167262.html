suction stop

noun
inward-drawn stop consonant
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌk.ʃən stɑp / (AmE), / ˈsʌk.ʃən stɒp / (BrE)
A stop consonant produced by drawing air inward into the mouth, as found in some African languages such as Bantu.

Examples

A suction stop sound
It takes practice to produce a suction stop correctly, especially for speakers of European languages.
Phoneticians study suction stops to understand the diversity of human speech sounds.
The Zulu language features several suction stops that are unusual in English.
