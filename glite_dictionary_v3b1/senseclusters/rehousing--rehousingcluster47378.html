rehousing

noun
providing new homes
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈhaʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhaʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of providing someone with a new home, especially after they have been displaced

Examples

Government rehousing families
After the earthquake, the government prioritized rehousing for families who lost their homes.
The charity focuses on rehousing people who have been living in temporary shelters.
Successful rehousing programs can help communities recover more quickly after disasters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eviction - the act of forcing someone to leave their home
  • synonym: resettlement - the act of moving people to a new place to live
