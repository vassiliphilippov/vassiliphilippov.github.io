twisted

adjective
deliberately altered; misrepresented
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪstɪd / (AmE), / ˈtwɪstɪd / (BrE)
(of words or facts) deliberately altered or misrepresented so as to mislead or fit a particular agenda.

Examples

A twisted story
His remarks were twisted to make him appear dishonest.
She felt her words had been twisted and taken out of context.
The newspaper published a twisted version of the events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distorted - changed from the original meaning or shape
  • antonym: straightforward - presented in an honest and direct way
