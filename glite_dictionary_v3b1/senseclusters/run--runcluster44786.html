run

verb
set animals loose to graze
3.3
Pronunciation: / rʌn / (AmE), / rʌn / (BrE)
to set animals loose to graze or to make animals move quickly

Examples

To run cattle
During the summer, the farmer would run his cattle on the north field.
In the mornings, shepherds run their sheep across the hills to graze.
They ran the horses in the meadow after the fences were checked.
Hunters sometimes run the dogs through the field to prepare them for the season.
She would run her sheepdog daily to keep it fit and alert.
The trainer decided to run the horses early in the morning.
During the hunt, the Duke’s men ran wild boars with skilful precision.
For generations, the villagers would run deer across the open fields.
The hounds were trained to run foxes through the dense woods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drive - to urge animals to move in a particular direction
  • synonym: hunt - to pursue and kill wild animals for sport or food
  • antonym: walk - to move at a slower pace, especially referring to animals
