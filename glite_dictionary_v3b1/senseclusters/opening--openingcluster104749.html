opening

noun
act of becoming open
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənɪŋ / (BrE)
the beginning or first part of something, or an event that marks the start (such as a ceremony, performance, or section)

Examples

Opening ceremony
After the opening of the envelope, she read the letter aloud.
The opening of the flower occurred early in the morning.
The sudden opening of the door startled everyone in the room.
He made a careful opening in the wall to check for hidden pipes.
Her opening of the conference was greeted with enthusiastic applause.
The opening of the envelope revealed a long-lost letter from her grandmother.
Critics eagerly awaited the opening to see how the new cast would perform.
The director gave a short speech before the opening of the musical.
Tickets for the play's opening sold out within hours.
