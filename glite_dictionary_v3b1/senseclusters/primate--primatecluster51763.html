primate

noun
chief bishop or archbishop in some churches
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɪːmət / (BrE)
a senior bishop or archbishop in certain Christian churches, often holding authority over other bishops in a region or country

Examples

Catholic primate of a country
After his appointment as primate, he presided over all the churches in the region.
The primate issued a statement addressing the concerns of the clergy and laity.
The primate of the Church of England is the Archbishop of Canterbury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archbishop - a bishop of the highest rank, in charge of an archdiocese or province
  • degree lower: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy with authority over a diocese
