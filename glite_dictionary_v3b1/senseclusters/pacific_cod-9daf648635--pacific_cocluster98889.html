Pacific cod

noun
a type of cod fish from the North Pacific
Pronunciation: / pəˈsɪfɪk kɑd / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk kɒd / (BrE)
a species of cod (Gadus macrocephalus) found in the North Pacific, closely related to the Atlantic cod and commonly used as food

Examples

Fresh Pacific cod
Pacific cod is often served battered and fried in fish and chips.
Due to overfishing, the population of Pacific cod is closely monitored by marine biologists.
The fisherman hauled up several Pacific cod during his trip in the Bering Sea.
