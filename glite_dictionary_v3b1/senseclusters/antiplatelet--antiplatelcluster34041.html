antiplatelet

adjective
prevents platelet clumping
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈpleɪtlət / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈpleɪtlət / (BrE)
describing a drug or substance that prevents blood platelets from sticking together and forming clots

Examples

Antiplatelet drug therapy
The doctor recommended an antiplatelet drug to help prevent future heart attacks.
Patients with a history of stroke are often prescribed antiplatelet medications.
Taking an antiplatelet agent can reduce the risk of dangerous blood clots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticoagulant - a drug that prevents blood from clotting
  • antonym: procoagulant - a substance that promotes blood clotting
