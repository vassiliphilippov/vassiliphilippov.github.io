overture

noun
a tentative proposal or approach
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətjʊə / (BrE)
a tentative proposal or approach made to someone to elicit their response or begin discussions

Examples

Peace overtures
Despite several overtures for peace, the negotiations failed to produce an agreement.
He made an overture to the new neighbors, hoping to break the ice.
The company's overtures to acquire its rival were swiftly declined.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proposal - a suggestion or offer put forward for consideration
  • antonym: rejection - the act of refusing a suggestion, offer, or overture
