animate

adjective
having animal life; living
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪmət /; / ˈænɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈænɪmət /; / ˈænɪməɪt / (BrE)
having life or the qualities of living things; alive, not inanimate

Examples

Animate beings
Bacteria are animate entities, unlike minerals or water.
Humans and other mammals are considered animate organisms.
In his essay, he explores the difference between animate and inanimate objects.
Children are often fascinated by animate objects and believe their toys have feelings.
Some philosophers debate whether animals are truly animate in the same way humans are.
The scientist distinguished between animate and inanimate matter under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inanimate - not alive, especially not in the manner of animals and humans
  • synonym: living - alive; having life
