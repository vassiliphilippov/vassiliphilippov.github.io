anointing of the sick

noun
Catholic sacrament for the ill or dying
7.7 7.1
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈnɔɪn.tɪŋ əv ðə sɪk/ /; / /ə.ˈnɔɪn.tɪŋ əv ðiː sɪk/ (AmE), /ə.ˈnɔɪn.tɪŋ əv ðə sɪk/ /; / /ə.ˈnɔɪn.tɪŋ əv ðiː sɪk/ (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a sacrament in which a priest applies oil to a seriously ill or dying person and prays for their spiritual healing or salvation.

Examples

The anointing of the sick
Many Catholics receive the anointing of the sick when they are facing serious illness or surgery.
The anointing of the sick is intended to provide comfort and spiritual strength during times of suffering.
The priest performed the anointing of the sick for the elderly man in the hospital.
