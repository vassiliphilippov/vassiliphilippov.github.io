achieve

verb
accomplish with effort
2.3 121/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàt Éiv / (AmE), / …ôÀàt ÉiÀêv / (BrE)
to successfully accomplish something, especially by effort, skill, or courage

Examples

To achieve success
After years of hard work, she finally achieved her dream of becoming a doctor.
It's important to set realistic goals if you want to achieve them.
The team was able to achieve success through determination and collaboration.

Synonyms

  • accomplish
    2.6 64/m
    accomplish means to finish a planned task successfully through effort or skill, while achieve often names reaching an important goal.
  • succeed
    2.0 56/m
    succeed means reach the desired result or win in a situation after trying, while achieve stresses completing a set plan.
  • attain
    3.9 17/m
    attain means reach a level, state, or goal by steady effort and is more formal, while achieve is broader.
  • reach
    1.1 162/m
    reach means arrive at a place or level often physically or slowly, while achieve focuses on finishing something by effort.
  • realize
    2.7 15/m
    realize means make an idea, plan, or hope real in practice, while achieve means successfully complete a task by effort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accomplish - to complete successfully
  • antonym: fail - to not succeed in achieving something
