prakriti

noun
material world in philosophy
Pronunciation: / prəˈkrɪti / (AmE), / prəˈkrɪti / (BrE)
in Indian philosophy, the natural or material world, especially as distinct from consciousness or spirit

Examples

Cosmic prakriti force
In Samkhya philosophy, prakriti is seen as the source of the material universe.
The distinction between prakriti and purusha is fundamental in certain schools of Hindu thought.
Many ancient texts discuss how prakriti interacts with consciousness to create experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nature - the physical world and everything in it not made by people
  • antonym: purusha - in Indian philosophy, the spirit or consciousness, as opposed to matter
