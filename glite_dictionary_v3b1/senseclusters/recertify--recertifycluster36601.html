recertify

verb
to renew certification
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsɜːrtɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɜːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to officially renew or confirm a qualification, certificate, or status, usually by meeting certain requirements

Examples

To recertify a license
After five years, nurses must recertify to continue practicing in the hospital.
He needs to recertify his teaching credentials every three years.
The company requires all technicians to recertify their safety training annually.
Every five years, engineers must recertify to maintain their professional licenses.
The organization requires all volunteers to recertify their training annually.
If you let your certification expire, you will need to recertify before returning to work.
Teachers are required to recertify every five years to maintain their credentials.
After completing the training course, she was able to recertify as a lifeguard.
Many professionals must recertify to keep their licenses active and up to date.
