superior rectus

noun
eye muscle turning eyeball upward and medially
Pronunciation: / suˈpɪriər ˈrɛktəs / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˈrɛktəs / (BrE)
the muscle in the eye that, when contracted, turns the eyeball upward and toward the midline

Examples

Superior rectus muscle
Damage to the superior rectus can affect a person's ability to look straight ahead or up.
The superior rectus muscle helps move the eye upward when you look up.
The doctor checked the function of her superior rectus during the eye examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inferior rectus - the ocular muscle that turns the eyeball downward
