Rhodophyceae

noun
class of red algae
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊdəˈfaɪsiːi /; / ˌroʊdəˈfaɪsiˌiː / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈfaɪsiːiː /; / ˌrəʊdəˈfʌɪsiːiː / (BrE)
a class of red algae (coextensive with Rhodophyta), found mainly in marine environments

Examples

Rhodophyceae red algae
Many species of Rhodophyceae are important sources of agar and carrageenan.
Scientists study the Rhodophyceae for their role in coral reef ecosystems.
The brilliant red color of Rhodophyceae is due to unique pigments in their cells.
Many edible seaweeds, such as nori, are actually members of the Rhodophyceae.
Researchers discovered several new species belonging to the Rhodophyceae in the coastal waters.
The pigments found in Rhodophyceae allow them to photosynthesize efficiently at greater depths.
