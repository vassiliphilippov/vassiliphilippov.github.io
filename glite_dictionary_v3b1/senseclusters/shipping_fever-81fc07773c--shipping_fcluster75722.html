shipping fever

noun
disease in livestock caused by stress and transport
8.5 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪpɪŋ ˌfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpɪŋ ˌfiːvə / (BrE)
A serious disease in cattle and sheep, marked by high fever and pneumonia, often caused by stress and transport.

Examples

Shipping fever in cattle
Proper handling can help prevent shipping fever when moving sheep over long distances.
Several cases of shipping fever were reported after the livestock arrived at the market.
