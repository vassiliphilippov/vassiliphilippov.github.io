hydrothorax

noun
accumulation of fluid in the pleural cavity
5.7 8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
An accumulation of serous fluid in the pleural cavity, especially as a result of disease affecting the heart or kidneys.

Examples

Fluid in hydrothorax
Symptoms of hydrothorax may include shortness of breath and chest discomfort.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hydrothorax after reviewing the chest X-ray.
Treatment of hydrothorax often involves addressing the underlying cause, such as heart or kidney disease.
