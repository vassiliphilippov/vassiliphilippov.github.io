medical officer

noun
doctor with official duties (esp. military or institutional)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdɪkəl ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪkəl ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
a doctor who serves as an official with responsibility for medical services, especially in the armed forces or an institution

Examples

Medical officer in navy
After serving as a medical officer in the navy, she returned to civilian practice.
Every camp had a medical officer to oversee emergencies and provide care.
The medical officer was responsible for inspecting the soldiers' health each morning.
