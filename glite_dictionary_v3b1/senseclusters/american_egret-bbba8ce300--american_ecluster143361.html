American egret

noun
large white heron of the Americas
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈiɡrət / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈiːɡrɪt / (BrE)
a large white heron (Egretta alba or Ardea alba) native to the Americas, distinguished by its long neck and slender body

Examples

A American egret bird
During migration, flocks of American egrets can be seen along the Gulf Coast.
The American egret builds its nest high in the trees near wetlands.
We spotted an American egret standing motionless in the shallow water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: great egret - another common name for Egretta alba, the large white heron
