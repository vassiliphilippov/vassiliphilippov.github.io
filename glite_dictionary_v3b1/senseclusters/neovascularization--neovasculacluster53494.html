neovascularization

noun
growth of new blood vessels
Pronunciation: / ˌniːoʊˌvæskjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˌvæskjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation of new blood vessels, especially as part of normal growth, healing, or response to injury

Examples

Healing neovascularization
The process of neovascularization is essential for wound healing after surgery.
Uncontrolled neovascularization in the eye can lead to vision problems.
Researchers study neovascularization to develop new treatments for heart disease.
Diabetic retinopathy can lead to neovascularization in the eye, which may cause vision problems.
Researchers are studying how neovascularization helps wounds heal more quickly.
Abnormal neovascularization is often seen in certain types of cancerous tumors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angiogenesis - the development of new blood vessels from pre-existing ones
