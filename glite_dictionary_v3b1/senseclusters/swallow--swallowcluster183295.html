swallow

verb
engulf and destroy
4.8 8.38/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsw…ëlo ä /; / Ààsw…ëÀêlo ä / (AmE), / Ààsw…íl…ô ä / (BrE)
to engulf, absorb, or envelop something completely so that it disappears

Examples

To swallow the city
After the revolution, the new regime quickly swallowed all signs of opposition.
Over the centuries, the desert has swallowed abandoned cities, erasing them from the map.
The small company was swallowed by a large multinational and lost its identity.
The city was almost swallowed by the rapidly rising floodwaters.
The darkness seemed to swallow the narrow alley as night fell.
The mist quickly swallowed the mountain peaks, making them disappear from sight.
