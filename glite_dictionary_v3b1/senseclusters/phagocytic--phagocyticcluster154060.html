phagocytic

adjective
capable of ingesting particles or cells
7.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfeɪɡəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɑːɡəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) capable of engulfing and ingesting particles, bacteria, or other cells, as a phagocyte does.

Examples

Phagocytic cells
Macrophages are phagocytic cells that help defend the body against bacteria.
Some white blood cells exhibit strong phagocytic activity in response to infection.
The phagocytic capability of these cells increases during inflammation.
