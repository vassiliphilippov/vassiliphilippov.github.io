ethnocracy

noun
ethnic group rule
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛθˈnɑkrəsi /; / ɛθˈnɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / ɛθˈnɒkrəsi / (BrE)
a system of government or society in which one ethnic group holds most or all political power, often excluding others

Examples

The new constitution aimed to prevent the rise of an ethnocracy by protecting minority rights.
International observers condemned the election process as favoring an ethnocracy over true representation.
Historians debated whether the regime could be classified as an ethnocracy due to its exclusionary policies.
In an ethnocracy, laws and policies often favor one ethnic group over others.
Critics argue that the country is moving toward an ethnocracy rather than a true democracy.
Access to government jobs in an ethnocracy may depend on your ethnic background.
The country was criticized for establishing an ethnocracy that excluded minority groups from government.
Some scholars argue that an ethnocracy can lead to long-term social divisions and unrest.
After the revolution, the new leaders formed an ethnocracy favoring their own ethnic group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: oligarchy - a government in which a small group holds power
