Ursa

proper noun
constellation name
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈɜːr.sə/ (AmE), /ˈɜː.sə/ (BrE)
the Latin word for 'bear', especially used in the names of constellations like Ursa Major and Ursa Minor

Examples

Ursa Major constellation
The constellation Ursa Major is one of the most recognizable patterns in the night sky.
Ancient astronomers named the group of stars Ursa because it resembled a bear.
Both Ursa Major and Ursa Minor are important for navigation in the Northern Hemisphere.
The constellation Ursa Major is named after the great bear in Latin.
In ancient texts, the word Ursa was used to refer to female bears.
Scientists sometimes use Ursa in the classification of bear species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ursa Minor - a constellation in the northern sky, also known as the Little Bear
  • synonym: bear - a large, heavy mammal with thick fur and a short tail
