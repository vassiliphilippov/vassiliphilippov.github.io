falsework

noun
temporary construction support
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔlsˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlsˌwɜːk / (BrE)
temporary structures used to support parts of a building or bridge during construction until the permanent structure is self-supporting

Examples

Temporary falsework structures
The engineers installed falsework to hold the bridge in place while the concrete cured.
After the arch was completed, the construction crew carefully dismantled the falsework.
Without proper falsework, the structure could collapse before the materials set.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formwork - temporary molds into which concrete is poured during construction
  • synonym: scaffolding - temporary framework for supporting workers and materials during construction
