baste

verb
sew with large temporary stitches
8.7 6.0
Pronunciation: / beɪst / (AmE), / beɪst / (BrE)
to sew fabric together temporarily with large, loose stitches, usually to hold it in place before permanent sewing

Examples

To baste fabric pieces
Before sewing the final seam, she decided to baste the pieces of fabric together.
He quickly basted the dress hem to check the fit before making it permanent.
I'm basting the sleeves onto the bodice so they won't shift as I sew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tack - to fasten temporarily with loose stitches
