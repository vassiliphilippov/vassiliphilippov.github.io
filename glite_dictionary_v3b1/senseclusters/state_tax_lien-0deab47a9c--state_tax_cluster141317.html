state tax lien

noun
claim on property for unpaid state taxes
Pronunciation: / steɪt tæks liːn / (AmE), / steɪt tæks liːn / (BrE)
A legal claim by a state government against the property of a taxpayer who defaults on paying state taxes, allowing the state to collect the owed amount through property seizure or sale.

Examples

A state tax lien
A state tax lien can affect your credit rating and ability to sell your home.
She hired a lawyer to help remove the state tax lien from her records.
The IRS filed a state tax lien against his property after years of unpaid taxes.
