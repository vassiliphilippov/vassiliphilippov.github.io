trunk hose

noun
puffed breeches worn over hose (16th-17th c.)
Pronunciation: / trʌŋk hoʊz / (AmE), / trʌŋk həʊz / (BrE)
Puffed, short breeches worn over hose in the 16th and 17th centuries, typically as part of men's fashion.

Examples

Trunk hose fashion
During the Elizabethan period, trunk hose became a fashionable choice among the English gentry.
Shakespeare’s actors would often wear trunk hose to complete their period costumes on stage.
The portrait of the nobleman shows him proudly dressed in trunk hose and a ruffled collar.
