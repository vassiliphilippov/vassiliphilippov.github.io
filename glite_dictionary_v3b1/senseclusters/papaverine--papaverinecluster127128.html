papaverine

noun
alkaloid drug for relaxing muscles
Pronunciation: / pəˈpævəˌriːn / (AmE), / pəˈpævəriːn / (BrE)
an alkaloid drug derived from opium, used to relax smooth muscles in medical treatment, particularly for blood vessel and gastrointestinal spasms

Examples

Papaverine drug
Because papaverine relaxes smooth muscles, it is sometimes used in the treatment of certain gastrointestinal conditions.
The doctor prescribed papaverine to help ease the patient's vascular spasm.
Unlike opiates, papaverine is nonaddictive and does not produce any euphoric effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atropine - alkaloid used to relax smooth muscles and dilate pupils
