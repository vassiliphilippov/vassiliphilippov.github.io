Hemimetabola

noun
insect subclass with incomplete metamorphosis
9.9
Pronunciation: /he.mɪ.mə.ˈtæ.bə.lə/ /; / /he.mɪ.me.ˈtæ.bə.lə/ (AmE), /he.mɪ.mə.ˈtæ.bə.lə/ /; / /he.mɪ.me.ˈtæ.bə.lə/ (BrE)
A subclass of insects characterized by gradual and usually incomplete metamorphosis, with nymphs resembling miniature adults without fully developed wings.

Examples

The Hemimetabola insects
Grasshoppers and cockroaches are members of the Hemimetabola, which do not undergo a pupal stage.
Many true bugs, classified as Hemimetabola, display young nymphs that resemble adults in form.
The concept of Hemimetabola helps entomologists categorize insects based on their development.
