abduce

verb
advance evidence for a conclusion
5.3
Pronunciation: / æbˈdus / (AmE), / æbˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to advance evidence in support of a conclusion or inference

Examples

To abduce evidence
He failed to abduce any convincing support for his theory.
Philosophers often abduce the best explanation for observed phenomena.
The lawyer attempted to abduce several pieces of evidence in support of her argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deduce - to reach a conclusion by reasoning; to infer logically
  • synonym: induce - to lead to a conclusion by generalizing or inferring from specific cases
