clay-colored robin

noun
a Central American robin species
Pronunciation: / ˈkleɪ ˌkʌlɚd ˈrɑbɪn / (AmE), / ˈkleɪ ˌkʌləd ˈrɒbɪn / (BrE)
a species of robin (Turdus grayi) native to Mexico and Central America, recognized by its dull brownish-gray plumage

Examples

The clay-colored robin bird
The clay-colored robin is often seen feeding on fruit in Central American gardens.
Unlike the American robin, the clay-colored robin does not have a red breast.
We spotted a clay-colored robin singing at dawn near the edge of the rainforest.
