face-amount certificate company

noun
company issuing face-amount investment certificates
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪs əˌmaʊnt səˈtɪfɪkət ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈfeɪs əˌmaʊnt səˈtɪfɪkət ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a regulated investment company that issues face-amount certificates and pays a specified sum to certificate holders at a designated maturity date

Examples

A face-amount certificate company
By purchasing from a face-amount certificate company, she ensured a guaranteed return after ten years.
Regulations require each face-amount certificate company to maintain strict reserves for certificate redemption.
The face-amount certificate company promised to pay investors a fixed amount at maturity.
