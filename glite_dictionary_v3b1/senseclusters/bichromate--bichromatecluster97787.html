bichromate

noun
salt containing the dichromate ion
Pronunciation: / baɪˈkroʊ.meɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈkrəʊ.meɪt / (BrE)
A salt containing the dichromate ion, usually derived from dichromic acid and commonly used in chemistry.

Examples

Potassium bichromate
Potassium bichromate is a common laboratory reagent with strong oxidizing properties.
The experiment required a freshly prepared solution of bichromate to test for the presence of alcohol.
Workers should handle bichromate compounds carefully because they can be toxic.
