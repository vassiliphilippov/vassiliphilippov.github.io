wearing

noun
gradual reduction or erosion by friction

The process of being gradually reduced or smoothed by friction, pressure, or repeated action, especially by natural forces such as water or wind; erosion or abrasion.

Examples

Wearing of a surface
The wearing caused by daily use can gradually dull the sharpest blade.
The wearing of rocks by the constant flow of the river forms smooth pebbles over time.
Years of wearing from wind and sand had shaped the mountain into a unique formation.

Synonyms

  • wearing away
    5.1 0.03/m
    wearing away is the action of slowly grinding a surface by wind or water, while wearing is a general term for that process.
  • erosion
    3.7 7.90/m
    erosion is the common scientific word for land or rock carried away by wind or water, while wearing is a broader, everyday term.
  • eating away
    4.9 0.18/m
    eating away suggests slow destruction, often by chemicals or time, while wearing describes physical smoothing by wind, water, or friction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrasion - the process of scraping or wearing something away
  • synonym: erosion - the gradual destruction or diminution of something by natural forces like wind or water
