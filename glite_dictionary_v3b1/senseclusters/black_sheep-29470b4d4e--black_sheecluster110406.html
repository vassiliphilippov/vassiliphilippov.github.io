black sheep

noun
disgraced or outcast family member
Pronunciation: / blæk ʃip / (AmE), / blak ʃiːp /; / blæk ʃiːp / (BrE)
a person who is considered a disgrace or outcast in a family or group

Examples

The black sheep of the family
Every group has its black sheep, but not all deserve the bad reputation.
He became the black sheep of the family after being expelled from college.
She always felt like the black sheep compared to her high-achieving siblings.
Growing up, she felt like the black sheep among her siblings because of her different interests.
He has always been the black sheep of his family, choosing an unconventional career path.
In their small town, being a black sheep meant going against tradition and standing out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outcast - a person who is rejected or excluded
  • antonym: role model - a person looked up to by others as an example to be imitated
  • synonym: scapegoat - a person blamed for the wrongdoing of others
