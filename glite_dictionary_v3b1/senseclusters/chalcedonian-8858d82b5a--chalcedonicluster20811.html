Chalcedonian

adjective
of Council of Chalcedon
Pronunciation: / ˌkælsɪˈdoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌkælsɪˈdəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the Council of Chalcedon or to the doctrines established by it, especially in Christian theology

Examples

Chalcedonian doctrine
The Chalcedonian definition became a central doctrine for many Christian churches.
Some Eastern churches did not accept the Chalcedonian position on the nature of Christ.
The debate between Chalcedonian and non-Chalcedonian Christians shaped church history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nicene - relating to the Nicene Creed or the Council of Nicaea
