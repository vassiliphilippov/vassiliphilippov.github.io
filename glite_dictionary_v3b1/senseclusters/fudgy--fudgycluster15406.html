fudgy

adjective
rich and dense
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌdʒi / (AmE), / ˈfʌdʒi / (BrE)
having a rich, dense, soft, and chewy texture or taste like fudge, especially in desserts

Examples

A fudgy brownie
The brownies were so fudgy that they almost melted in my mouth.
She prefers a fudgy texture in her chocolate cake rather than a light one.
The fudgy center of the dessert made it especially delicious.
The fudgy brownies were a hit at the party.
She prefers her chocolate cake to be extra fudgy rather than light and airy.
The cookies came out fudgy in the center, just the way he likes them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chewy - having a texture that requires a lot of chewing
  • synonym: chocolaty - having the taste or flavor of chocolate
  • synonym: creamy - having a smooth and rich texture
  • antonym: crunchy - firm and making a loud noise when chewed
  • antonym: dry - lacking moisture or richness in texture
  • synonym: gooey - soft and sticky in texture
