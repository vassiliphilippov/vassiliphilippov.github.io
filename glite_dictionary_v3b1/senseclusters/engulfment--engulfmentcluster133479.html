engulfment

noun
being overwhelmed
8.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ /; / /ən.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ /; / /ən.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ (BrE)
a state of being completely overwhelmed or consumed by something, especially emotions or circumstances

Examples

Engulfment of fear
After the accident, he experienced a sudden engulfment of fear that made it hard to think clearly.
The engulfment of stress at work left her feeling exhausted by the end of the week.
During the crisis, many people reported a sense of engulfment by anxiety and uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overwhelm - to affect someone very strongly or completely
  • antonym: relief - a feeling of reassurance and relaxation following release from anxiety or distress
  • synonym: submersion - the act of being completely covered or submerged
