patina

noun
greenish layer on metal from oxidation
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈtinə / (AmE), / ˈpætɪnə / (BrE)
a thin, usually greenish layer that forms on the surface of copper, bronze, and similar metals due to long-term oxidation; often valued for its aesthetic or historical significance

Examples

Copper patina
Collectors highly prize bronze artifacts with a natural patina.
The ancient statue's patina revealed its long history.
The copper roof developed a beautiful green patina over decades.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tarnish - dullness or discoloration of a metal surface caused by exposure to air
