osteolytic

adjective
bone tissue destroying
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɑstioʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing the destruction or dissolution of bone tissue

Examples

Osteolytic bone disease
The doctor explained that the tumor was osteolytic and had weakened the bone.
An osteolytic lesion was detected during the patient's routine scan.
Certain cancers can cause osteolytic changes in the skeleton, leading to fractures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bone-destroying - causing the breakdown of bone tissue
  • antonym: osteogenic - relating to the formation of bone
