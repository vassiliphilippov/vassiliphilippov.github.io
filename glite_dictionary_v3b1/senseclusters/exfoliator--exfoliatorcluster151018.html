exfoliator

noun
removal agent
Pronunciation: / ɛksˈfoʊliˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ɛksˈfəʊliˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a substance or tool that removes surface layers, causing material to peel or flake away, especially from rocks, masonry, or metal surfaces

Examples

Stone exfoliator
The salt acted as a natural exfoliator, causing the stone surface to flake over time.
Engineers applied a chemical exfoliator to remove rust from the metal beams.
Repeated use of an exfoliator can help maintain the appearance of masonry walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrasive - a substance used for grinding, polishing, or cleaning surfaces
  • synonym: corrosive - a substance that causes gradual destruction of materials
