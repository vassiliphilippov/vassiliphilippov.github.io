patent medicine

noun
over-the-counter medicine, often historical
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpætənt ˌmɛdəsən / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtənt ˌmɛdɪsɪn / (BrE)
a medicine protected by a patent and sold without a doctor's prescription, especially one sold in the past that was promoted as a cure-all without scientific evidence

Examples

Patent medicine sales
Before prescription drugs became common, people often relied on patent medicine for minor ailments.
In the 19th century, patent medicine advertisements promised to cure everything from headaches to cancer.
Many patent medicines contained alcohol or opium, leading to calls for better regulation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proprietary medicine - a drug marketed under a protected name or trademark
