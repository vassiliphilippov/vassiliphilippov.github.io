prejudiced

adjective
influenced by personal prejudice
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdʒədɪst / (AmE), / ˈprɛdʒʊdɪst / (BrE)
having or showing an unreasonable dislike or preference for someone or something, based on personal prejudice rather than reason or fairness

Examples

A prejudiced opinion
Her prejudiced remarks revealed a lack of understanding about the issue.
It's difficult to have a fair discussion with someone holding such prejudiced views.
The review was clearly prejudiced and did not consider all the evidence.
He denied being prejudiced, but his comments told a different story.
It's important for a judge to avoid being prejudiced in any case.
The committee made a decision that seemed prejudiced against younger applicants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - having or showing prejudice
  • antonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; unbiased
