intactness

noun
state of being whole or undamaged
4.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtæktnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtæktnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being complete, whole, and undamaged; the condition of being unimpaired.

Examples

Forest intactness
Forest intactness is crucial for preserving biodiversity in the region.
She questioned the intactness of the package after noticing a tear in the wrapping.
The intactness of the ancient vase amazed the archaeologists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: damage - physical harm that impairs value or usefulness
  • synonym: integrity - the quality of being whole or undivided
