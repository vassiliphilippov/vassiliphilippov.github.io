explode

verb
burst violently with energy; detonate
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsploʊd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊd / (BrE)
to burst or cause to burst suddenly and violently, releasing energy, noise, or force (such as fireworks or bombs)

Examples

To explode a bomb
Fireworks exploded in the night sky, delighting the crowd below.
If overheated, the pressure cooker could explode and cause serious injury.
The gas cylinder exploded without warning, shaking the entire building.
Careless handling could explode the fireworks accidentally.
Engineers sometimes explode small charges to test safety protocols.
Scientists used a trigger to explode the device in a remote area.
Everyone watched as the fireworks exploded in the night sky.
The old boiler finally exploded after years of neglect.
The scientist warned that the chemicals could explode if not handled carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blow up - destroy by causing to explode
  • synonym: burst - to break open or apart suddenly and forcefully
  • synonym: detonate - to cause to explode, especially with a sudden and violent energy
  • antonym: implode - to collapse or burst inward violently, as with the opposite of explode
