Clio

proper noun
the Muse of history in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈkliː.oʊ / (AmE), / ˈklaɪ.əʊ / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the Muse of history, one of the nine Muses who inspired the creation of historical and literary works.

Examples

Clio the Muse
An imposing statue of Clio stands in the entrance hall of the history museum.
In poetry, writers sometimes invoke Clio to lend weight to their historical narratives.
The ancient Greeks believed that Clio inspired historians to record the deeds of the past.
