professionalization

noun
process of becoming a profession
3.4 5.9
Pronunciation: /prə.fə.ʃə.nə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /prə.fə.ʃə.nə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which an occupation or activity becomes recognized as a profession, usually involving specialized training and payment for work

Examples

The professionalization of nursing
The professionalization of nursing greatly improved healthcare standards in the 20th century.
There is ongoing debate about the professionalization of teaching and its impact on education quality.
With the professionalization of e-sports, gamers can now compete as full-time athletes.
Many sports have undergone rapid professionalisation in the last century.
The professionalisation of journalism led to the development of ethical standards and training programs.
The professionalisation of teaching has changed how teachers are trained and evaluated.
