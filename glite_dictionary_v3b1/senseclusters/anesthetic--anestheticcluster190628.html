anesthetic

adjective
causing loss of sensation or awareness
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˌænɪsˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
causing or characterized by loss of sensation or awareness, especially of pain

Examples

Anesthetic effect
Some plants contain natural compounds with anesthetic properties.
The dentist used an anesthetic gel before giving the injection.
This medication has a mild anesthetic effect on the skin.
After the injection, her arm felt completely anesthetic, and she could not move her fingers.
During meditation, some people report an almost anesthetic detachment from physical discomfort.
He was so overwhelmed by shock that he entered an anesthetic state, oblivious to pain or fear.
After the accident, his hand felt completely anaesthetic for several minutes.
He stared at the wall with an anaesthetic detachment, oblivious to the chaos around him.
The trauma left her anaesthetic to the pain that followed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analgesic - relieving pain without causing loss of consciousness
  • synonym: anesthetic - American English spelling; causing loss of sensation
  • synonym: insensible - lacking physical sensation or awareness
  • antonym: sensitive - having or displaying sensitivity or sensation
  • antonym: stimulant - increasing physiological or nervous activity in the body
