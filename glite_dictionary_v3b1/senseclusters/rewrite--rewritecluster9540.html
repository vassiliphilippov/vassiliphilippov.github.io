rewrite

noun
complete redevelopment of software
7.2 0.2/m
Pronunciation: / 8riccra6at / (AmE), / 8rid0ccra6at / (BrE)
(in software development) a complete redevelopment or reimplementation of a software system or codebase, typically done from scratch to replace an existing implementation.

Examples

A rewrite of software
After months of problems, the team decided to perform a full rewrite of the backend services.
Management estimated the rewrite would take nine months and a significant increase in budget.
Engineers argued that a careful refactor might suffice rather than an expensive rewrite.

Synonyms

  • recodification
    8.3 0.00/m
    recodification reorganizes or rewrites existing source code to improve structure and performance, while a rewrite rebuilds the whole system from scratch to replace it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebuild - to build or construct something again, often from existing parts or plans
  • degree lower: refactor - to restructure existing code to improve design or readability without changing its external behavior; generally less drastic than a full rewrite
