resituate

verb
to place in new context
7.7
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈsɪtʃ.uː.eɪt/ /; / /rə.ˈsɪtʃ.uː.eɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈsɪtʃ.uː.eɪt/ /; / /rə.ˈsɪtʃ.uː.eɪt/ (BrE)
to place or consider something in a new or different context or framework.

Examples

To resituate an idea
The professor tried to resituate the debate within a broader historical perspective.
Artists often resituate familiar objects to challenge viewers' perceptions.
The report aims to resituate the company's achievements in light of recent industry changes.
