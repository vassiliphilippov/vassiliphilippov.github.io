air-intake

noun
duct or opening for admitting air to an engine
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr ɪnˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈeə ɪnˌteɪk / (BrE)
a duct or opening that allows air to enter a machine, especially to be mixed with fuel in an engine

Examples

The air-intake duct
Before starting the race, he checked the air-intake for dirt and debris.
Engineers redesigned the air-intake to improve airflow and fuel efficiency.
The car's air-intake was clogged with leaves, reducing its performance.
