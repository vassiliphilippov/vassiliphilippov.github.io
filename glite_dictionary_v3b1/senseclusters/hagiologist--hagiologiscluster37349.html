hagiologist

noun
writer of idealizing biographies
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌheɪdʒiˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌhæɡiˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
an author or scholar who writes or studies worshipful or idealizing biographies, especially of saints or revered figures

Examples

A hagiologist studies saints
As a renowned hagiologist, she dedicated her career to chronicling the lives of Christian saints.
Many medieval hagiologists contributed to the development of religious literature in Europe.
The hagiologist's work was criticized for lacking objectivity and historical accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hagiography - a biography that idealizes its subject, especially of a saint
