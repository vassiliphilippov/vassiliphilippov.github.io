long hundred

noun
an old measure of 120
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈhʌndrəd / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈhʌndrəd / (BrE)
an old measure equal to 120; the number obtained by multiplying ten by twelve, used historically in some counting systems

Examples

A long hundred of items
A bale containing a long hundred of cloth would hold 120 pieces.
Historically, a long hundred was used in trade and referred to a count of 120.
Scandinavian merchants sometimes counted in long hundreds, rather than the modern hundred.
