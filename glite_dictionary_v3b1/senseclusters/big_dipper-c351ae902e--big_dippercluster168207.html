Big Dipper

noun
prominent asterism in Ursa Major
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ ˈdɪp.ɚ / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈdɪp.ə / (BrE)
a group of seven bright stars forming a prominent asterism in the constellation Ursa Major, resembling a dipper or ladle

Examples

The Big Dipper stars
Many people use the Big Dipper to help locate the North Star.
On a clear night, you can easily spot the Big Dipper high above the northern horizon.
The distinctive shape of the Big Dipper makes it one of the most recognizable patterns in the night sky.
