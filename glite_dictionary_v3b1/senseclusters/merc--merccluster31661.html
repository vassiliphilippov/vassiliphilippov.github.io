merc

verb
to defeat decisively
Pronunciation: / mɜrk / (AmE), / mɜːk / (BrE)
to kill, seriously injure, or defeat someone decisively, especially in a violent or competitive context (informal, slang)

Examples

To merc an enemy
If you mess with them, they might merc you without hesitation.
The team completely merc'd their rivals in the championship game.
He threatened to merc anyone who betrayed his trust.

  • synonym: destroy - to completely defeat or ruin someone or something
  • antonym: lose - to be defeated in a contest or conflict
