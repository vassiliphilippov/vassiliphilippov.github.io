in theory

adverb
according to principles, not always in practice
2.2 4.9
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈθɪri / (AmE), / ɪn ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
According to what is believed or expected based on principles, even if not true or possible in practice.

Examples

In theory possible
In theory, anyone can learn to play an instrument, but not everyone has the time or motivation.
In theory, the vaccine should prevent the disease, but further studies are needed to confirm this.
The system works perfectly in theory, but there are some problems when it's applied in real life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in practice - as actually done, rather than theoretically
  • synonym: in principle - according to general ideas or rules but not concerning details or execution
