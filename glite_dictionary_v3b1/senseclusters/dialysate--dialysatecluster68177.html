dialysate

noun
dialysis fluid
Pronunciation: / daɪˈælɪˌseɪt / (AmE), / daɪˈælɪseɪt / (BrE)
a fluid used in dialysis to draw waste products and excess substances from the blood

Examples

The dialysate fluid
The nurse checked the dialysate before starting the patient's treatment.
Fresh dialysate is essential for every session to ensure effective removal of toxins.
Technicians monitor the temperature and composition of the dialysate during dialysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialysis - a medical process for removing waste from the blood
