detachment

noun
the act of separating; disconnection
4.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtætʃmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtætʃmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of separating something or someone from a connection; the state of being separated

Examples

Detachment from a group
After years of working there, her detachment from the company was bittersweet.
Proper detachment of the wires is necessary before repairs can be made.
The detachment of the trailer from the truck took only a few minutes.

Improper handling can lead to the detachment of parts from the main device.
The earthquake resulted in the detachment of several rocks from the cliff face.
