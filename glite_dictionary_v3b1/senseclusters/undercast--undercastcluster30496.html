undercast

verb
to cast insufficiently
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈkæst / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈkɑːst / (BrE)
to cast a material with less than the required amount or so that it does not reach the intended level or surface

Examples

To undercast the metal
The part failed because the manufacturer undercast the metal.
If you undercast the concrete, the structure may not be strong enough.
The technician realized he had undercast the mold and needed to redo the process.
