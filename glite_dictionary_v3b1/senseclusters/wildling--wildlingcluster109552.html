wildling

noun
person living in wild
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪldlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪldlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who lives freely in the wild, away from organized society or civilization

Examples

A forest wildling
The villagers told stories about a mysterious wildling who roamed the nearby forests.
As a child, she dreamed of becoming a wildling and living among the animals.
Legends say the wildling survived many winters alone in the mountains.
The wildlings built their homes deep in the forest, far from any town.
Legends tell of a wildling who survived alone in the mountains for years.
Some villagers feared the wildlings, believing they possessed strange knowledge of the woods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: citizen - a person who lives as a member of a society
  • synonym: hermit - a person living in solitude, often away from society
  • synonym: outcast - a person who has been rejected by society
