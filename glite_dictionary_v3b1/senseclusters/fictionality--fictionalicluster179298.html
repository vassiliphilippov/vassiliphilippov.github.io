fictionality

noun
quality of being fictional
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪkʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌfɪkʃəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being fictional or imagined, especially in literature or storytelling

Examples

The fictionality of stories
The fictionality of the story lets the author explore impossible worlds and ideas.
Some readers question the fictionality of the events described in the memoir.
Debates about fictionality are common in literary theory and criticism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiction - literature or stories about imaginary events and people
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
