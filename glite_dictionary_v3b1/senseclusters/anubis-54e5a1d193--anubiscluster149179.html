Anubis

proper noun
ancient Egyptian god of the dead
9.3
Pronunciation: / əˈnuːbɪs / (AmE), / əˈnjuːbɪs / (BrE)
the ancient Egyptian god of tombs and the underworld, usually depicted as a man with the head of a jackal

Examples

Anubis god of tombs
In ancient mythology, Anubis was believed to guide souls into the afterlife.
Many Egyptian amulets feature Anubis as a symbol of protection for the dead.
The statue of Anubis stood at the entrance to the Pharaoh's tomb.
