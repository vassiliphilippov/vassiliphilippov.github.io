ungrip

verb
to release control
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡrɪp / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡrɪp / (BrE)
to free someone or something from a strong influence, control, or emotion; to remove a figurative hold

Examples

To ungrip from fear
Therapy helped her ungrip from the anxiety that had held her back for years.
It took time for him to ungrip from the anger he felt after the argument.
The inspiring speech encouraged the team to ungrip from their doubts and move forward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grip - to hold tightly or have strong control
  • synonym: let go - to stop holding or controlling
  • synonym: release - to set free or let go
