katha

noun
Hindu/Sikh discourse
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.θə/ /; / /ˈkæ.θə/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.θə/ /; / /ˈkæ.θə/ (BrE)
a religious or moral story or storytelling event in Hindu and Sikh traditions, often used to teach sacred texts or lessons; also, rarely, an extract from acacia trees used in tanning and dyeing

Examples

A katha story
The temple organized a katha to explain the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita to the community.
During the festival, families gathered to listen to a katha about ancient heroes and their virtues.
The visiting scholar delivered a katha that inspired many people to reflect on their actions.
The teacher shared a katha with the class to explain the importance of honesty.
During the festival, elders gather to listen to a katha about ancient heroes.
The tannery uses katha extracted from acacia trees in the dyeing process.

  • synonym: sermon - a speech on a religious or moral subject
