Dardanelles campaign

noun
WWI military campaign in the Dardanelles
9.1
Pronunciation: /dɑːr.də.ˈnelz kæm.ˈpeɪn/ /; / /dɑːr.də.ˈnelz kəm.ˈpeɪn/ (AmE), /dɑː.də.ˈnelz kəm.ˈpeɪn/ /; / /dɑː.də.ˈnelz kæm.ˈpeɪn/ (BrE)
a military campaign during World War I (1915) in which British and French forces attempted, unsuccessfully, to secure a sea route to Russia by capturing the Dardanelles Strait from the Ottoman Empire

Examples

The Dardanelles campaign
Churchill's reputation suffered greatly after the Allied defeat in the Dardanelles campaign.
Many historians continue to debate the reasons for the failure of the Dardanelles campaign.
The Dardanelles campaign was intended to break the deadlock on the Western Front and aid Russia.
