macerative

adjective
causing or involving maceration
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmæsəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈmæsərətɪv / (BrE)
characterized by or involving maceration, especially the softening or breaking down of tissue by soaking or enzymatic action

Examples

Macerative skin lesions
Prolonged exposure to moisture can result in macerative skin lesions.
Researchers observed macerative degeneration in the liver during the study of the disease's progression.
The pathologist noted macerative changes in the tissue sample after it had been left in fluid for too long.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macerate - to soften or break down by soaking, especially in a liquid
