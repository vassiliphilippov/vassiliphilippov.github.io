politicize

verb
make political in nature
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪtəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to give a political character or bias to something, especially an issue, action, or discussion

Examples

To politicize an issue
He accused the media of politicizing the issue unnecessarily.
Some commentators tried to politicize the health crisis for their own advantage.
The organization aims to avoid politicizing scientific research.
Activists worry that environmental issues are often politicised rather than addressed objectively.
Some argue that education should not be politicised by government agendas.
The media has politicised the debate over healthcare reform.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depoliticize - to remove a political character from something
  • synonym: politicize - give a political character to (American spelling)
