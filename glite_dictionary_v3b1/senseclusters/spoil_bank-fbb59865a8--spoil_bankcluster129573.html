spoil bank

noun
mining waste pile
Pronunciation: / spɔɪl bæŋk / (AmE), / spɔɪl bæŋk / (BrE)
a pile or ridge of waste earth or rock left over from mining or excavation activities

Examples

A spoil bank pile
The children played near the spoil bank left by the old quarry.
Grass and shrubs began to cover the abandoned spoil bank over the years.
Heavy rains caused part of the spoil bank to collapse into the stream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slag heap - a large pile of waste material from mining or smelting
  • synonym: tailings - residues left after the extraction of minerals from ore
