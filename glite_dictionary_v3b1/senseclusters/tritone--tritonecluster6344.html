tritone

noun
dissonant musical interval
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtraɪtəʊn / (BrE)
a musical interval spanning three whole tones, often producing a dissonant sound

Examples

Tritone interval
The jazz pianist used a tritone to add tension before resolving the chord.
In Western music, the tritone is sometimes called the devil's interval because of its dissonant sound.
Learning to recognize a tritone by ear is important for music students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: augmented fourth - a musical interval equivalent to a tritone
  • synonym: diminished fifth - a musical interval equivalent to a tritone
  • antonym: perfect fifth - a musical interval spanning seven semitones, considered consonant
