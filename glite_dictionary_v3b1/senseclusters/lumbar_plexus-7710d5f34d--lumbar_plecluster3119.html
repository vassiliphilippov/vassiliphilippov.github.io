lumbar plexus

noun
network of nerves in the lower back
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌmbɑr ˈplɛksəs /; / ˈlʌm.bɑr ˈplɛk.səs / (AmE), / ˈlʌmbɑː ˈplɛksəs /; / ˈlʌm.bɑː ˈplɛk.səs / (BrE)
a network of nerves in the lower back, formed by the lumbar spinal nerves, that supplies the lower abdomen, pelvis, and legs

Examples

Damage to the lumbar plexus can result in pain or weakness in the legs.
The lumbar plexus is responsible for innervating much of the lower abdomen and thigh.
