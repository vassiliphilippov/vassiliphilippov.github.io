synaeresis

noun
separation of liquid from a gel
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈnaɪərəsɪs / (BrE)
the separation of liquid from a gel caused by contraction, as in cheese making

Examples

Synaeresis in yogurt
During the production of yogurt, synaeresis can cause whey to pool on the surface.
Excessive synaeresis may indicate a problem with the stability of the gel.
The cheese maker monitored the synaeresis process to achieve the desired texture.
