copay

noun
fixed medical payment
4.1
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ˈpeɪ/ /; / /ˈkoʊ.peɪ/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ˈpeɪ/ /; / /ˈkəʊ.peɪ/ (BrE)
a fixed amount of money you pay for a medical service or prescription, with the rest paid by insurance

Examples

A copay amount
My health insurance requires a copay every time I visit the doctor.
She was surprised by the high copay for her specialist appointment.
You must pay a copay at the pharmacy when picking up your prescription.
Each time you visit the doctor, you must pay a copay at the front desk.
My insurance plan has a $10 copay for generic prescriptions.
Some specialists require a higher copay than regular doctors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coinsurance - a percentage of medical costs you pay after meeting your deductible
  • antonym: deductible - the amount you pay for healthcare before insurance starts to pay
