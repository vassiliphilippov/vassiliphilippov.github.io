clutter

noun
untidy collection of things
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈklʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈklʌtə / (BrE)
A collection of things lying around in an untidy or disorganized state.

Examples

Desk clutter
She spent the weekend clearing out the clutter from her desk.
The garage was filled with so much clutter that I could barely move.
Too much clutter in your workspace can make it hard to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mess - a state of disorder or untidiness
  • antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged in a neat and tidy way
