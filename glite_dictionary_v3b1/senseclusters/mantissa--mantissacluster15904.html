mantissa

noun
fractional part of a logarithm
9.9
Pronunciation: / mænˈtɪsə / (AmE), / mænˈtɪsə / (BrE)
The fractional part of a logarithm, appearing after the decimal point, as in the number 0.808 in log 643 = 2.808.

Examples

The mantissa of logarithm
In the table of logarithms, always look up the mantissa corresponding to the given digits.
The mantissa for log 350 is approximately 0.544, which you add to the integer part.
To find the antilogarithm, you use both the characteristic and the mantissa of the number.

Related Terms

  • antonym: characteristic - the integer part of a common logarithm
