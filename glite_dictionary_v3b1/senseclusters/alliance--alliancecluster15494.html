alliance

noun
state of being allied; formal partnership
Pronunciation: / əˈlaɪəns / (AmE), / əˈlaɪ.əns /; / əˈlaɪəns / (BrE)
a formal agreement, union, or relationship between countries, organizations, groups, or people to work together for a common purpose or benefit

Examples

A alliance of countries
Several companies entered into an alliance to develop the new technology.
The two countries formed an alliance to strengthen their economic ties.
Their political alliance lasted for more than a decade.
NATO is a military alliance of various North American and European countries.
Several non-profit groups entered into an alliance to increase their impact.
The two countries formed an alliance to promote regional security.
Many businesses entered into an alliance to develop new technologies together.
The military alliance was crucial during times of crisis.
The two countries formed an alliance to strengthen their defense capabilities.
