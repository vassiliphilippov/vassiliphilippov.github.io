pronged

adjective
having projecting pointed parts, like a fork
Pronunciation: / prɔŋd / (AmE), / prɒŋd / (BrE)
having one or more thin, pointed parts (prongs or tines) that stick out, like the points of a fork

Examples

Pronged fork tines
A fork has several pronged tines for picking up food.
The electrician used a two-pronged adapter to plug in the device.
The lightning struck the tree with a pronged flash that split into two branches.
He was startled by the sharp, pronged shadow on the wall.
The gardener used a pronged tool to dig up the weeds.
This three-pronged approach should ensure success.
