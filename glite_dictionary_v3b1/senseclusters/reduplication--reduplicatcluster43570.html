reduplication

noun
repetition of words at sentence/line boundaries
Pronunciation: / ˌriːdʊˌplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːdjuːˌplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the rhetorical or poetic repetition of the final words of one sentence or line at the beginning of the next

Examples

Poetic reduplication
In classical poetry, reduplication is often used for dramatic effect.
Teachers sometimes point out reduplication in literature classes to illustrate poetic techniques.
The speech employed reduplication to emphasize the key message to the audience.
