free

adjective
not literal or strictly accurate; loosely adapted
2.6
Pronunciation: / friː / (AmE), / friː / (BrE)
(of a translation or interpretation) not literal or strictly accurate, allowing freedom of expression or adaptation.

Examples

Free translation
He gave a free interpretation of the rules, which led to disagreements among the group.
Her free translation conveyed the poem’s emotion but omitted some details.
The play was staged as a free adaptation rather than following the original script word for word.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literal - Taking words in their usual or most basic sense; not figurative or metaphorical
  • synonym: loose - Not strictly accurate or exact; free in style or manner
