witchweed

noun
parasitic crop plant
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪtʃ.wiːd/ (AmE), /ˈwɪtʃ.wiːd/ (BrE)
a parasitic plant that attaches to the roots of crops, especially in Africa and Asia, causing significant agricultural damage

Examples

Witchweed plant roots
Witchweed can cause serious damage to corn and sorghum fields.
Farmers in some regions struggle to control the spread of witchweed among their crops.
The presence of witchweed often leads to lower harvests and economic losses for local communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dodder - a different parasitic plant that harms crops
  • synonym: striga - a genus of parasitic plants that includes witchweed
