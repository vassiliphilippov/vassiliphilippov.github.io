build

verb
increase or strengthen gradually
1.1 1.2
Pronunciation: /bɪld/ (AmE), /bɪld/ (BrE)
to develop, create, or increase something gradually over time, especially something non-physical like trust, a career, or tension

Examples

To build trust
It takes time to build trust between two people.
Practicing every day helped him build his confidence before the big performance.
The team hopes to build momentum ahead of the championship game.
Tension slowly built between the two colleagues during the project.
The excitement began to build as the team approached the final seconds of the game.
The music builds steadily, creating an intense and dramatic atmosphere.
He worked hard to build a successful career in finance.
She hopes to build lasting partnerships between the two organizations.
The government plans to build a stronger economy over the next decade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accumulate - to gather or collect, often in gradual degrees
  • synonym: construct - to put together or form by combining parts; synonym for 'build' in this sense
  • antonym: destroy - to ruin or put an end to something
  • antonym: diminish - to become less; opposite in meaning to increase or build up
  • synonym: establish - to set up or lay the groundwork for
  • synonym: grow - to become larger or greater over a period of time; similar in meaning
  • synonym: strengthen - to make or become stronger
  • antonym: weaken - to make or become less strong
