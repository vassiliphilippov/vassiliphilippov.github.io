consign

verb
commit irrevocably; hand over permanently
4.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈsaɪn / (AmE), / kənˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to commit or deliver something irrevocably, often permanently or irretrievably, to a particular fate, place, or person

Examples

To consign to oblivion
After the scandal, the politician was consigned to a life outside of public office.
She felt as though her dreams had been consigned to oblivion.
The damaged artwork was consigned to the attic, never to be seen again.
