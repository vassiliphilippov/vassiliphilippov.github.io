squeal

verb
confess to wrongdoing under pressure
6.8 0.75/m
Pronunciation: / skwil / (AmE), / skwiÀêl / (BrE)
to confess to a punishable or immoral act, typically under pressure or to avoid punishment oneself, especially to the authorities

Examples

To squeal to police
He got caught stealing, but he won't squeal unless they threaten him with jail.
The suspect refused to squeal on his accomplices, despite the intense questioning.
Under pressure from the police, she finally squealed and revealed everything.

Synonyms

  • fink
    9.6 0.03/m
    fink is informal and means telling the police about others, while squeal means confessing, often under pressure to avoid punishment.
  • confess
    3.0 21/m
    confess means admitting a wrong or crime, while squeal implies telling authorities, often under pressure to avoid punishment or blame.

Related Terms

  • antonym: keep quiet - to refrain from giving information, especially to authorities
  • synonym: snitch - to secretly inform on someone, especially to the police
