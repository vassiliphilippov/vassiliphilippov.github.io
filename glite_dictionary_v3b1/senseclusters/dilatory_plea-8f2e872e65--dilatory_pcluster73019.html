dilatory plea

noun
legal plea that delays proceedings
8.4 9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪləˌtɔri pli / (AmE), / ˈdɪlətəri pliː / (BrE)
A legal plea intended to delay proceedings without deciding the merits of the case, such as challenging jurisdiction or asserting a procedural disability.

Examples

Dilatory plea in court
Because the dilatory plea did not address the substance of the claim, the trial was postponed.
Lawyers sometimes use a dilatory plea as a strategy to buy more time for the defense.
The defendant entered a dilatory plea to challenge the court's jurisdiction.
