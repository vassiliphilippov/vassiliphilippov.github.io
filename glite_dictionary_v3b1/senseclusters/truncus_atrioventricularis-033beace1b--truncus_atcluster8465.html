truncus atrioventricularis

noun
bundle of heart muscle fibers conducting impulses
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌŋkəs ˌeɪtrioʊvɛnˌtrɪkjʊˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋkəs ˌætrɪəʊvɛnˌtrɪkjuːˈlɛrɪs / (BrE)
a bundle of specialized heart muscle fibers that conducts electrical impulses from the atrioventricular node to the ventricles, causing them to contract

Examples

Damage to the truncus atrioventricularis can lead to serious cardiac rhythm disorders.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully identified the truncus atrioventricularis to avoid damaging it.
The truncus atrioventricularis plays a critical role in coordinating the heartbeat.
