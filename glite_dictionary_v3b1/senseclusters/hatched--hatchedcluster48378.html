hatched

adjective
shaded with fine lines
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhætʃt / (AmE), / ˈhætʃt / (BrE)
(of a drawing or area) shaded with fine parallel or crossed lines to create the effect of shadow or texture.

Examples

Hatched lines in drawings
In technical illustrations, hatched sections often represent cut surfaces.
The architect presented a drawing with hatched areas indicating proposed changes.
The artist carefully hatched the shadow beneath the table for a realistic effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crosshatched - shaded with sets of intersecting parallel lines
  • antonym: stippling - shading using dots instead of lines
