goggle

verb
look with wide eyes, astonished or stupidly
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑɡəl / (AmE), / ˈɡɒɡl / (BrE)
to look with wide-open eyes in astonishment or stupidity

Examples

To goggle in surprise
He goggled in disbelief when he saw the enormous cake on the table.
She was goggling so much at the celebrity that she almost tripped over her own feet.
The children goggled at the magician's disappearing act, unable to believe their eyes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ogle - to look at with greedy or amorous interest
  • synonym: stare - to look fixedly with eyes wide open
