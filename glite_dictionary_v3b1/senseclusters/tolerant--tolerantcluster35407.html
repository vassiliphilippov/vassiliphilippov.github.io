tolerant

adjective
accepting or broad-minded
3.2 5.14/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ëÀêl…ôr…ônt /; / Ààt…ëÀêl…ö…ônt / (AmE), / Ààt…íl…ôr…ônt / (BrE)
willing to accept or allow different opinions, beliefs, behaviors, or people; patient and broad-minded

Examples

Tolerant of differences
A tolerant society values freedom of expression for everyone.
He tried to remain tolerant when listening to opposing viewpoints.
Her parents were surprisingly tolerant of her unconventional lifestyle.
A tolerant society accepts people from different cultures and backgrounds.
He is tolerant of criticism and open to new ideas.
Her parents are very tolerant of her unusual hobbies.
A tolerant attitude is essential when working with people from diverse backgrounds.
Despite the noise and constant interruptions, she remained tolerant throughout the meeting.
He is remarkably tolerant of criticism, never losing his temper or becoming defensive.

Synonyms

  • broad-minded
    3.5 0.19/m
    broad-minded is willing to accept and respect different opinions and ways, while tolerant often means patient and allowing differences.
  • large-minded
    6.0 0.01/m
    large-minded shows a generous, wide view of people and ideas, while tolerant focuses on allowing or being patient with differences.
  • accepting
    3.6 0.00/m
    accepting welcomes new people, ideas, or situations calmly, while tolerant emphasizes patience and allowing beliefs you may not share.
  • open-minded
    3.7 4.16/m
    open-minded is ready to consider new ideas and views, while tolerant stresses acceptance and patience toward people and behaviors.
  • unbigoted
    6.9 0.01/m
    unbigoted simply means not showing hateful bias, whereas tolerant implies actively allowing or putting up with different opinions and people.

Related Terms
