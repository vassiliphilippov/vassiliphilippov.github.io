tolerant

adjective
accepting or broad-minded
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːlərənt /; / ˈtɑːlɚənt / (AmE), / ˈtɒlərənt / (BrE)
willing to accept or allow different opinions, beliefs, behaviors, or people; patient and broad-minded

Examples

Tolerant of differences
A tolerant society values freedom of expression for everyone.
He tried to remain tolerant when listening to opposing viewpoints.
Her parents were surprisingly tolerant of her unconventional lifestyle.
A tolerant society accepts people from different cultures and backgrounds.
He is tolerant of criticism and open to new ideas.
Her parents are very tolerant of her unusual hobbies.
A tolerant attitude is essential when working with people from diverse backgrounds.
Despite the noise and constant interruptions, she remained tolerant throughout the meeting.
He is remarkably tolerant of criticism, never losing his temper or becoming defensive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broad-minded - having tolerant or liberal views
  • antonym: intolerant - unwilling to accept views, beliefs, or behavior that differ from one's own
  • synonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
  • synonym: patient - able to accept or tolerate delays, problems, or suffering without becoming annoyed or anxious
