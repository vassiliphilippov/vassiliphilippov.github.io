globefish

noun
spiny marine fish that can inflate itself
Pronunciation: / ˈɡloʊbˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡləʊbˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A marine fish with an elongated, spiny body that can inflate into a globe-like shape; several species are poisonous.

Examples

The globefish inflates
Scientists study the neurotoxins found in the skin of the globefish.
The chef warned us that globefish must be prepared carefully due to its potent toxin.
While snorkeling, she spotted a globefish inflating to ward off predators.
