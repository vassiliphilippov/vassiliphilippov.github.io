run away from

verb
avoid dealing with something difficult
Pronunciation: / rʌn əˈweɪ frəm / (AmE), / rʌn əˈweɪ frɒm / (BrE)
to avoid confronting or dealing with (an unpleasant situation, problem, or responsibility)

Examples

To run away from problems
Some people run away from change, fearing the uncertainty it brings.
Whenever she feels stressed, she tends to run away from the situation instead of addressing it.
You can’t just run away from your mistakes forever; eventually, you have to take responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoid - to keep away from or not deal with something
  • antonym: face up to - to confront or deal with something directly
