late

adjective
after the expected time; delayed
1.0 96/m
Pronunciation: / leÉªt / (AmE), / leÉªt / (BrE)
arriving, happening, or done after the expected or usual time; delayed

Examples

Late train
If the train is late again, I'll be late for work.
My friends sent me a late birthday card after they forgot the date.
She apologized for being late to the meeting.
She arrived late to the meeting and missed the introduction.
The store stays open late on Thursdays for late-night shoppers.
We decided to have a late lunch after finishing our errands.

Synonyms

  • tardy
    3.9 0.85/m
    tardy is used for people who arrive after the set time, while late is the common general word for that situation.
  • behind time
    3.2 0.02/m
    behind time describes not arriving at the arranged hour, while late is the normal adjective for being after the expected time.
  • unpunctual
    6.2 0.01/m
    unpunctual describes a person who often misses agreed times, while late is the everyday adjective for arriving after the expected time.
  • belated
    4.4 1.31/m
    belated is used for events or gifts that come after their proper time, while late is used more broadly.
  • delayed
    1.3 0.12/m
    delayed means slowed or held up by something, while late simply means arriving or happening after the expected time.
  • behindhand
    8.9 0.02/m
    behindhand means late in doing tasks or payments, often implying backlog, while late covers any arrival or event after schedule.
