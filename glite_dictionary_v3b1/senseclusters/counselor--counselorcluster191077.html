counselor

noun
lawyer; legal adviser or advocate
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊn.səl.ɚ /; / ˈkaʊn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊn.sələ /; / ˈkaʊn.səl.ə / (BrE)
A lawyer who gives legal advice or represents clients in court; an attorney or advocate.

Examples

Counselor in court
Each side in the trial was represented by an experienced counselor.
He worked as a defense counselor in the district court for over ten years.
The counselor argued persuasively on behalf of her client before the judge.
She consulted her counsellor before making any legal decisions.
The defense counsellor presented a compelling argument to the jury.
The judge addressed the counsellor directly during the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in legal matters
  • synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to practice as an advocate, particularly in higher courts
  • synonym: solicitor - a type of lawyer who advises clients and prepares legal documents (esp. in the UK)
