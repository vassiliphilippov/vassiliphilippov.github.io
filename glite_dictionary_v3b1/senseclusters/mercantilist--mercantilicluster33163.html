mercantilist

adjective
about trade theory
8.7
Pronunciation: /mər.ˈkæn.tə.lɪst/ (AmE), /mə.ˈkæn.tə.lɪst/ (BrE)
relating to the economic theory that trade and commercial activity increase a nation's wealth and power

Examples

Mercantilist policy
The government introduced mercantilist policies to encourage exports and limit imports.
Historians often describe the 17th century as a mercantilist era in European economics.
A mercantilist approach prioritizes national wealth through strict control of trade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free trade - an economic policy of minimal restrictions on imports and exports
  • synonym: protectionist - favoring government actions to restrict imports and protect domestic industries
