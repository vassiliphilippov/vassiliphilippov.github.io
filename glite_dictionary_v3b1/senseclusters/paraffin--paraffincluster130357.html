paraffin

noun
(British) kerosine; clear fuel oil
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəfɪn / (AmE), / ˈpærəfɪn / (BrE)
(British) A thin, clear oil used as a fuel or solvent, known as kerosine in American English.

Examples

Paraffin lamp oil
In the UK, paraffin lamps are common during power cuts.
Many old stoves in Britain still use paraffin as a fuel.
We bought a bottle of paraffin to fill the heater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kerosine - a thin, clear oil used as fuel (American equivalent of British 'paraffin')
