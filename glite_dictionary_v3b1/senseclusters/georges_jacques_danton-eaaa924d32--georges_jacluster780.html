Georges Jacques Danton

proper noun
French revolutionary leader (1759–1794)
Pronunciation: / ʒɔːrʒ ʒæks dænˈtoʊn / (AmE), / ʒɔːʒ ʒak dɑ̃tɔ̃ / (BrE)
(1759–1794) A leading figure of the French Revolution who played a key role in overthrowing the monarchy, supported the execution of Louis XVI, and was eventually executed during the Reign of Terror.

Examples

Georges Jacques Danton French leader
Georges Jacques Danton was instrumental in rallying the French people during the early stages of the Revolution.

The execution of Georges Jacques Danton marked a turning point in the French Revolution.
