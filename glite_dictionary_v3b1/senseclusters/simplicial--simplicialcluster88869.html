simplicial

adjective
composed of simple shapes
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈplɪʃəl / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪʃəl / (BrE)
made up of simple geometric shapes, especially triangles or their higher-dimensional equivalents

Examples

Simplicial complex
A simplicial complex is used to study shapes in topology.
The mathematician explained the properties of a simplicial surface built from triangles.
In higher dimensions, simplicial structures help model complex geometric objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simple - not complex; easy or basic in structure
