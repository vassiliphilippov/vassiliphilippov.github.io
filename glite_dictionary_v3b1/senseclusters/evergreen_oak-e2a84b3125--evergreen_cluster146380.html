evergreen oak

noun
oak tree that keeps its leaves year-round
1.0 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɛvərˌɡrin oʊk / (AmE), / ˈɛvəɡriːn əʊk / (BrE)
An oak tree that retains its leaves throughout the year, especially those native to southern Europe, such as the holm oak, which has hard wood and leaves resembling those of holly.

Examples

The evergreen oak tree
Carpenters often prize the wood of the evergreen oak for its strength and durability.
Many Mediterranean forests are dominated by the evergreen oak due to its resilience to drought.
The evergreen oak provides shelter and food for a variety of wildlife throughout the year.
