clear and present danger

noun
legal test for restricting free speech
3.9
Pronunciation: / klɪr ənd ˈprɛzənt ˈdeɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / klɪə ənd ˈprɛzənt ˈdeɪndʒə / (BrE)
a legal principle allowing restriction of free speech if it poses an imminent and obvious threat to public safety or order

Examples

A clear and present danger
He argued that the protest did not constitute a clear and present danger to public order.
The government must prove a clear and present danger before restricting someone's freedom of speech.
This law only applies if there is a clear and present danger to national security.
