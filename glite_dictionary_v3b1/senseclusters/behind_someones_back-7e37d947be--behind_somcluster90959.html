behind someone's back

adverb
secretly; without someone knowing
1.2
Pronunciation: / bɪˈhaɪnd ˌsʌmwʌnz bæk / (AmE), / bɪˈhaɪnd ˌsʌmwənz bæk / (BrE)
without someone’s knowledge, especially in a secret or deceitful way.

Examples

Talk behind someone's back
He criticized his colleague behind her back, never confronting her directly.
She was hurt to learn they had discussed her behind her back.
They made significant changes to the project behind his back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in secret - without anyone knowing; secretly
  • antonym: openly - in a way that is not secretive or hidden
