horsehead

noun
a type of silvery, flat-bodied marine fish
9.8 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrsˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌhɛd / (BrE)
any of several silvery marine fishes with very flat bodies, especially of the genus Eques, distinguished by their slender, compressed forms and prevalent in warm seas.

Examples

A horsehead fish
A local market displayed several freshly caught horseheads among other tropical fish.
Fishermen often catch horsehead near coral reefs due to their abundance in warm seas.
The horsehead's flat body makes it well suited for slipping through narrow marine crevices.
