confessionalism

noun
strict religious adherence
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or doctrine of strictly following official confessions of faith within a religious tradition

Examples

Church confessionalism
The church's commitment to confessionalism shapes its teachings and community life.
Debates about confessionalism often arise when new interpretations of doctrine are proposed.
Some denominations have moved away from strict confessionalism to embrace broader beliefs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ecumenism - promoting unity among different Christian churches
  • synonym: orthodoxy - strict adherence to accepted or traditional beliefs
