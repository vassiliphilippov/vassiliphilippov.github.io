uneven

adjective
not smooth, flat, or uniform
3.1 2.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈniː.vən/ /; / /ʌ.ˈniː.vən/ (AmE), /ʌ.ˈniː.vən/ /; / /ə.ˈniː.vən/ (BrE)
not smooth, flat, regular, or consistent; varying in surface, quality, or pattern

Examples

Uneven surface
His performance was uneven, with some excellent moments and some weak ones.
The table had an uneven surface, making it difficult to write on.
Walk carefully; the trail ahead is quite uneven and rocky.
Attendance at the meetings has been uneven during the past few months.
His performance in the tournament was uneven, with both impressive and poor results.
The movie received uneven reviews from critics, ranging from rave to highly critical.
Her breathing became uneven as she ran up the hill.
The uneven distribution of rainfall caused drought in some areas and floods in others.
There are uneven gaps between the fence posts, making it look poorly built.

Related Terms

  • antonym: even - smooth or regular in surface or structure
  • synonym: inconsistent - not staying the same throughout, lacking consistency
  • synonym: irregular - not even or uniform in pattern or occurrence
  • antonym: reliable - consistently good in quality or performance
  • synonym: variable - liable to change; not consistent
