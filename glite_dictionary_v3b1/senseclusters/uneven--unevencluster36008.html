uneven

adjective
not smooth, flat, or uniform
2.3 5.21/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàiv…ôn / (AmE), /  ånÀàiÀêv…ôn / (BrE)
not smooth, flat, regular, or consistent; varying in surface, quality, or pattern

Examples

Uneven surface
His performance was uneven, with some excellent moments and some weak ones.
The table had an uneven surface, making it difficult to write on.
Walk carefully; the trail ahead is quite uneven and rocky.
Attendance at the meetings has been uneven during the past few months.
His performance in the tournament was uneven, with both impressive and poor results.
The movie received uneven reviews from critics, ranging from rave to highly critical.
Her breathing became uneven as she ran up the hill.
The uneven distribution of rainfall caused drought in some areas and floods in others.
There are uneven gaps between the fence posts, making it look poorly built.

Synonyms

  • unlevel
    3.9 0.05/m
    unlevel describes a surface that slopes or is not flat, while uneven covers both surface and quality differences.
  • rough
    1.0 14/m
    rough feels coarse or harsh to touch and sound, while uneven simply means not smooth or regular.
  • jagged
    3.6 2.54/m
    jagged has sharp, irregular edges or points, while uneven means surfaces or patterns that are not smooth.
  • bumpy
    1.0 2.98/m
    bumpy has many raised parts that cause jolts when moving, while uneven is a more general lack of smoothness.
  • patchy
    3.7 0.85/m
    patchy shows areas of different quality or coverage, while uneven can mean any surface or pattern that is not consistent.
  • spotty
    5.1 1.10/m
    spotty describes poor or mixed performance over time, while uneven refers broadly to surfaces, patterns, or quality that vary.
