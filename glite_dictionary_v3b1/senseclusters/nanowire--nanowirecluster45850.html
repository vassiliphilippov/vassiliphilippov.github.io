nanowire

noun
ultra-thin wire
Pronunciation: / ˈnænoʊˌwaɪr / (AmE), / ˈnænəʊˌwaɪə / (BrE)
a very thin wire with a diameter measured in nanometers, used in electronics, nanotechnology, or produced by microorganisms

Examples

A single nanowire can conduct electricity at the nanoscale for advanced electronic devices.

The new solar cells contain nanowires that improve their efficiency.
