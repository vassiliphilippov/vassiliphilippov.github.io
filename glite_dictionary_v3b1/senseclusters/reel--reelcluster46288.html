reel

verb
spin rapidly around one's axis
4.3 0.49/m
Pronunciation: / ril / (AmE), / riÀêl / (BrE)
to spin or revolve quickly and repeatedly around one's own axis, often in an unsteady or dizzying way

Examples

To reel around
Dancers reeled across the stage, spinning in vibrant costumes.
He tried to stand up, but the room seemed to reel around him after the ride.
The children began to reel in circles on the playground until they fell down laughing.

Synonyms

  • spin around
    2.1 3.16/m
    spin around is a physical phrase for turning quickly in place, while reel often means turning and feeling unsteady or dizzy.
  • spin
    1.0 14/m
    spin means turning fast and can be used for objects or people, while reel often shows unsteady, dizzy movement.
  • whirl
    2.6 2.58/m
    whirl describes quick circular motion with speed and energy, while reel suggests a wobbly, dizzy or uncontrolled turn.
  • gyrate
    6.9 0.80/m
    gyrate means move in circles or spirals, often in dance or machines, while reel implies a shaky, dizzy spin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spin - to turn round and round rapidly
  • synonym: whirl - to move or turn around quickly
