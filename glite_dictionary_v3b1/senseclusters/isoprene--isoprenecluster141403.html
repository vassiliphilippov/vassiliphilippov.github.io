isoprene

noun
plant-emitted compound
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.sə.priːn/ /; / /ˈaɪ.sə.prɪn/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.səʊ.priːn/ /; / /ˈaɪ.sə.priːn/ (BrE)
a volatile organic compound produced by many plants and trees that influences atmospheric chemistry and helps protect leaves from heat stress

Examples

Trees emit isoprene
Many trees emit isoprene on hot days to help protect their leaves from heat damage.
Scientists study isoprene because it plays a role in atmospheric reactions and air quality.
The smell of a forest in summer is partly due to the release of isoprene by plants.
