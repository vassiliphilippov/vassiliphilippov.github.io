geological rise in land elevation

(Geology) A rise of land to a higher elevation, as in the process of mountain building.

Examples

Tectonic uplift
Geologists studied the patterns of uplift that created the plateau over millions of years.
Rapid uplift in the region has resulted in steep cliffs and rugged terrain.
The Himalayan mountains were formed by massive uplift caused by tectonic plate collisions.

Synonyms

  • upthrust
    8.6 0.02/m
    an upthrust is a sudden tectonic push that raises land, while uplift is a general geological rise over time.
  • upheaval
    7.3 0.22/m
    an upheaval is a strong, often abrupt crustal movement that lifts areas, while uplift is a neutral geological rise used broadly.
  • upthrow
    9.9 0.00/m
    an upthrow is a fault-related rise of one side of the ground, while uplift means a general rise of land.
