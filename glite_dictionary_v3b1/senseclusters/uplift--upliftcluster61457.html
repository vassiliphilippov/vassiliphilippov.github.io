uplift

noun
geological rise in land elevation
5.6 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈʌ.plɪft/ (AmE), /ˈʌ.plɪft/ (BrE)
(Geology) A rise of land to a higher elevation, as in the process of mountain building.

Examples

Tectonic uplift
Geologists studied the patterns of uplift that created the plateau over millions of years.
Rapid uplift in the region has resulted in steep cliffs and rugged terrain.
The Himalayan mountains were formed by massive uplift caused by tectonic plate collisions.

  • antonym: subsidence - the sinking or lowering of land
