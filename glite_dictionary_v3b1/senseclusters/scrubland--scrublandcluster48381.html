An area of land covered with low, stunted shrubs and bushes, usually in regions unsuitable for cultivation.

Examples

A scrubland area
After the fire, the area slowly regenerated into a patch of scrubland.
Many animals have adapted to survive in the harsh conditions of the Australian scrubland.
The vast scrubland stretched for miles, dotted with thorny bushes and hardy grasses.

Synonyms

  • shrubland
    4.6 0.13/m
    shrubland is an area with mainly shrubs and low bushes, while scrubland is often wild, uncultivated land with stunted growth.
  • brushland
    4.6 0.04/m
    brushland is land covered in bushes and few trees, whereas scrubland usually refers to uncultivated areas with stunted shrubs.
  • scrub
    6.2 3.48/m
    scrub is vegetation of stunted bushes forming thickets, while scrubland names the actual land area covered by that vegetation.
