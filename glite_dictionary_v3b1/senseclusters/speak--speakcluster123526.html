speak

verb
to talk or converse
1.0 374/m
Pronunciation: / spiÀêk / (AmE), / spiÀêk / (BrE)
to talk or express thoughts, ideas, or feelings using spoken words; to have a conversation

Examples

To speak with friends
Have you spoken to your parents yet about your decision?
The manager wanted to speak with her about the project.
Whenever we speak, we always end up laughing about something.
Please speak clearly so everyone can understand you.
She learned to speak French when she was young.
The children were excited to speak with their grandparents on the phone.
He didn't speak for several minutes, thinking carefully before replying.
Please speak clearly so that everyone can understand you.
She was nervous to speak in front of the large audience.

Synonyms

  • talk
    1.0 988/m
    talk is an everyday, informal way to have a conversation between people, while speak is a general word for expressing ideas aloud.
  • converse
    3.3 2.47/m
    converse is a slightly formal word for taking part in a two-way discussion, while speak is a general term for talking or expressing ideas.
