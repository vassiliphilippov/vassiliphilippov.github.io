armor plate

noun
hardened steel plate for protection
3.4 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrmɚ pleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɑːmə pleɪt / (BrE)
A specially hardened steel or metal plate used to protect vehicles, fortifications, or structures from enemy fire or explosions.

Examples

Battleships rely on thick armor plate to protect vital areas from enemy shells.
Engineers inspected the armor plate after the explosion to assess the damage.
The tank was reinforced with several layers of armor plate to withstand direct hits.
