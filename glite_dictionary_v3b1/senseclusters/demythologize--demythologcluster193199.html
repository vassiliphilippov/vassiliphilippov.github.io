to interpret writings, especially religious texts, by removing or interpreting away mythical or supernatural elements to reveal historical, factual, or rational content.

Examples

To demythologize religious texts
Modern interpreters often demythologize stories to separate fact from fiction.
Some scholars seek to demythologize ancient religious texts to better understand their historical context.
The professor attempted to demythologize the legendary accounts in the epic.
Many scholars attempt to demythologise religious scriptures to better understand their historical context.
Some critics argue that efforts to demythologise classic texts strip them of their intended power.
The professor encouraged students to demythologise ancient legends and seek the truths they conceal.
