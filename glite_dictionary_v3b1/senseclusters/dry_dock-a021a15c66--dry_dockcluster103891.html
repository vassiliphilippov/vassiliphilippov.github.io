dry dock

noun

A large dock that can be drained of water, used for constructing, maintaining, or repairing ships below the waterline.

Examples

A dry dock for ships
After construction, the new cruise liner was floated out of the dry dock for its maiden voyage.
Engineers inspected the hull while the vessel remained in the dry dock all week.
The huge ship was taken into the dry dock for urgent repairs below the waterline.
