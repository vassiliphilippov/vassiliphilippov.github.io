hatchet man

noun
person hired to do unpleasant tasks for a superior
8.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhætʃ.ət mæn/ /; / /ˈhætʃ.ɪt mæn/ (AmE), /ˈhætʃ.ɪt mæn/ /; / /ˈhætʃ.ət mæn/ (BrE)
a person employed to carry out difficult or unpleasant tasks, especially those that may ruin reputations or involve dismissals, on behalf of someone in authority

Examples

Company hatchet man
As the new hatchet man, she was tasked with restructuring the entire department.
He gained a reputation as the company's hatchet man after laying off hundreds of workers.
The politician hired a hatchet man to discredit his opponents during the campaign.
