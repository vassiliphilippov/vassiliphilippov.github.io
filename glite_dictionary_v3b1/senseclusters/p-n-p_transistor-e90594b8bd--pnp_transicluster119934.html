p-n-p transistor

noun
type of junction transistor
7.5
Pronunciation: /piː en piː træn.ˈzɪ.stər/ (AmE), /piː en piː træn.ˈzɪ.stə/ (BrE)
A junction transistor in which a layer of n-type semiconductor is sandwiched between two p-type semiconductors, serving as emitter and collector.

Examples

A p-n-p transistor circuit
A circuit may use either an n-p-n or a p-n-p transistor depending on the required polarity.
She replaced the faulty p-n-p transistor in the amplifier to restore its function.
The p-n-p transistor is widely used in low-power amplification circuits.
