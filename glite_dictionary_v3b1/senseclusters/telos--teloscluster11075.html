telos

noun
final purpose or goal
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːlɒs /; / ˈtɛloʊs / (AmE), / ˈtiːlɒs / (BrE)
the ultimate aim, purpose, or final end of something, especially in philosophy

Examples

The telos of life
The philosopher argued that every living thing has a telos guiding its development.
Reaching the telos of the project required years of dedication and teamwork.
In ancient philosophy, the concept of telos explains why things exist and what they strive for.
In philosophy, every action is believed to have a telos that gives it meaning.
The architect designed the building with a specific telos in mind, focusing on community use.
Understanding the telos of a law can help explain its purpose and application.

  • synonym: goal - the object of a person's ambition or effort
  • antonym: means - a method or way of achieving something
  • synonym: purpose - the reason for which something is done or created
