nerve entrapment

noun
compression of a nerve, usually near joints
Pronunciation: / nɜrv ɪnˈtræpmənt / (AmE), / nɜːv ɪnˈtræpmənt / (BrE)
A condition that occurs when a nerve is compressed or trapped, usually near joints, leading to nerve dysfunction, pain, or numbness.

Examples

Nerve entrapment pain
Early treatment can help prevent permanent damage from nerve entrapment.
Prolonged inflammation around the elbow can result in nerve entrapment and muscle weakness.
The patient was diagnosed with nerve entrapment in her wrist, causing numbness in her fingers.
