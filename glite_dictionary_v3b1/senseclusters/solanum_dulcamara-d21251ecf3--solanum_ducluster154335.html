Solanum dulcamara

A poisonous perennial climbing plant of the nightshade family, native to Europe and Asia but also found in North America, with violet flowers and oval bright red berries; also known as bittersweet nightshade.

Examples

Solanum dulcamara plant
Children should be warned not to eat the berries of Solanum dulcamara, as they are highly toxic.
Gardeners sometimes remove Solanum dulcamara to control its spread as an invasive plant.
In the wetlands, Solanum dulcamara often forms dense tangles of climbing stems and leaves.
