mercerized

adjective
treated cotton thread/fabric with a lustrous finish
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜːrsəˌraɪzd /; / ˈmɝː.sɚ.aɪzd / (AmE), / ˈmɜːsəraɪzd /; / ˈmɜː.sə.raɪzd / (BrE)
(of cotton thread or fabric) treated with sodium hydroxide to shrink it, give it a lustrous appearance, and improve its ability to absorb dye.

Examples

Mercerized cotton fabric
Many luxury towels are woven from mercerized yarns that hold dye better than untreated fibers.
She prefers mercerized thread for embroidery because of its sheen and strength.
The shirt is made from mercerized cotton, making it feel smoother and more lustrous than regular cotton.
For my embroidery project, I prefer using mercerised threads because they are stronger and more lustrous.
The factory only produces mercerised yarn, which holds dye much better than untreated cotton.
This shirt is made from mercerised cotton, giving it a subtle sheen and a smooth texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unmercerised - (of cotton or fabric) not treated with sodium hydroxide for luster or dye affinity
  • antonym: unmercerized - cotton or fabric not subjected to mercerization
