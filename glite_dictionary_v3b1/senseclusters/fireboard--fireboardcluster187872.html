fireboard

noun
friction fire base
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəbɔːd / (BrE)
a flat piece of wood used as the base in traditional friction fire-making, into which a spindle is rotated to create an ember

Examples

Wooden fireboard for fire
The campers prepared a fireboard and spindle to start their fire without matches.
A dry, soft fireboard is essential for making fire using the bow drill method.
He carved a notch in the fireboard to collect the hot ember created by friction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hearth - the floor of a fireplace or the area in front of it
  • synonym: spindle - a stick rotated against the fireboard to produce friction and heat
