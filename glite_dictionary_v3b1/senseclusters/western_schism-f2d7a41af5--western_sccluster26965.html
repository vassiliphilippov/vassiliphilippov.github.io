Western Schism

noun
division in the Catholic Church, 1378–1417
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn ˈskɪzəm, ˈwɛstərn ˈsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈskɪzəm, ˈwɛstən ˈsɪzəm / (BrE)
the division in the Roman Catholic Church from 1378 to 1417, during which rival popes reigned in Rome and Avignon

Examples

The Western Schism period
During the Western Schism, rival popes claimed legitimacy from Rome and Avignon.
Historians still debate the long-term effects of the Western Schism on European society.
The Western Schism caused deep divisions within the Catholic Church in the late Middle Ages.
