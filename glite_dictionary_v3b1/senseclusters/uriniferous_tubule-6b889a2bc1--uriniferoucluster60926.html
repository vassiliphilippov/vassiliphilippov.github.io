uriniferous tubule

noun
small excretory tubule in the kidney
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌjʊrəˈnɪfərəs ˈtjuːbjuːl / (AmE), / ˌjʊərɪˈnɪfərəs ˈtjuːbjuːl / (BrE)
any of the small tubular structures in the vertebrate kidney that function as the basic units of excretion and urine formation

Examples

The uriniferous tubules in kidney
Damage to the uriniferous tubules can impair the ability of the kidneys to produce urine.
Each uriniferous tubule plays a critical role in filtering blood in the kidney.
The scientist studied the structure of the uriniferous tubule to understand kidney diseases.
