bow wood

noun
tree with hard wood used for bows
9.2 8.3
Pronunciation: /boʊ wʊd/ /; / /baʊ wʊd/ (AmE), /bəʊ wʊd/ /; / /baʊ wʊd/ (BrE)
a small deciduous tree (Maclura pomifera) native to south central United States, with spiny branches, glossy dark green leaves, inedible orange-like fruit, and hard orange-colored wood valued for making bows

Examples

A bow wood tree
A hedge of bow wood trees lines the southern edge of the property.
Early settlers prized bow wood for its strength and durability in tool making.
Native Americans traditionally crafted bows from the tough, flexible bow wood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Osage orange - another name for Maclura pomifera, the bow wood tree
  • degree lower: yellowwood - a tree with similar yellow wood, sometimes confused with bow wood
