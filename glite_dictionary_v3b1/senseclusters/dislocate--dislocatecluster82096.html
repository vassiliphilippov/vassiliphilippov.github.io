dislocate

verb
move something out of its normal position
1.0 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪs.loʊ.keɪt/ /; / /ˈdɪs.lə.keɪt/ (AmE), /ˈdɪs.ləʊ.keɪt/ /; / /ˈdɪs.lə.keɪt/ (BrE)
to move or force something, especially a bone in a joint, out of its normal position

Examples

To dislocate a shoulder
He managed to dislocate his shoulder during the football match.
If you dislocate a joint, seek medical attention immediately.
The violent impact caused several bones to dislocate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relocate - to move to a new place
  • antonym: set - to put a dislocated joint back into place (medical context)
