blit

verb
to copy graphics data
9.9
Pronunciation: /blɪt/ (AmE), /blɪt/ (BrE)
to rapidly copy a block of memory or pixel data from one place to another, especially in computer graphics.

Examples

To blit an image
The game engine can blit images onto the screen very efficiently.
Developers often blit sprites to create smooth animations in 2D games.
To update the display quickly, the program will blit the new frame over the old one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - to make a duplicate of data or information
  • synonym: render - to generate and display graphics on a screen
