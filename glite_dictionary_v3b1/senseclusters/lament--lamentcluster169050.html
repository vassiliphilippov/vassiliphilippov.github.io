lament

noun
expression of sorrow or grief
Pronunciation: / ləˈmɛnt / (AmE), / ləˈmɛnt / (BrE)
an expression of sorrow or mourning, especially as a song, poem, or cry

Examples

A sad lament
Every culture has its own lament for the passing of loved ones.
He composed a touching lament following the loss of his friend.
The widow's lament echoed through the silent church.
A traditional Scottish lament was played by a lone piper.
Her poem became a famous lament for those who lost their lives in the war.
The choir sang a moving lament at the memorial service.
During the funeral, a traditional Scottish lament was played on the bagpipes.
Her latest book includes a haunting lament dedicated to lost loved ones.
The poet composed a moving lament after the passing of his dear friend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: celebration - a joyful ceremony or activity, opposite of mourning
  • synonym: dirge - a mournful song or piece of music for the dead
  • synonym: elegy - a poem or song of serious reflection, typically for the dead
  • antonym: rejoicing - great joy; opposite of lament
