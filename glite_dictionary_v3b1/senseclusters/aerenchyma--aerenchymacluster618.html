aerenchyma

noun
plant air-filled tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛˈrɛŋkɪmə / (AmE), / ɪəˈrɛŋkɪmə / (BrE)
a plant tissue containing large air spaces that aids in gas exchange and buoyancy, especially in aquatic plants

Examples

Plant aerenchyma tissue
Many aquatic plants develop aerenchyma to help them survive in waterlogged soils.
The presence of aerenchyma allows rice plants to transport oxygen to their roots.
Scientists studied the formation of aerenchyma in wetland grasses under different flooding conditions.
