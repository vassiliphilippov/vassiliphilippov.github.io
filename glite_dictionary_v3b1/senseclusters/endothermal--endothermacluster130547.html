endothermal

adjective

(of a chemical reaction or compound) occurring or formed with absorption of heat.

Examples

endothermal reaction
An endothermal compound absorbs heat from its surroundings during formation.
Photosynthesis is an endothermal process because it requires sunlight to provide energy.
The researchers studied several endothermal reactions in the laboratory.

Synonyms

  • endothermic
    7.7 0.08/m
    endothermic describes a chemical reaction that absorbs heat from its surroundings, while endothermal refers to one formed by absorbing heat.
  • heat-absorbing
    6.1 0.01/m
    heat-absorbing is plain language for taking in heat in many contexts, while endothermal is a technical term used in chemistry.
