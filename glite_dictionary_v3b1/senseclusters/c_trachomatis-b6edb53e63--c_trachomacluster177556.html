C. trachomatis

noun
bacterium causing chlamydia
Pronunciation: / si ˌtrækəˈmeɪtɪs / (AmE), / si ˌtrækəˈmeɪtɪs / (BrE)
a species of bacteria responsible for the sexually transmitted disease chlamydia and lymphogranuloma venereum

Examples

C. trachomatis infection
C. trachomatis is a common cause of sexually transmitted infections worldwide.
Antibiotics are generally effective in treating infections caused by C. trachomatis.
Doctors recommend regular screening for C. trachomatis in sexually active individuals.
