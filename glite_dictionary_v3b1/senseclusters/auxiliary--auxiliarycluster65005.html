auxiliary

noun
helping verb
Pronunciation: / ɔɡˈzɪljəri / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈzɪliəri / (BrE)
a verb used with a main verb to help form tenses, moods, or voices

Examples

Auxiliary verb
In the question 'Are you coming?', 'are' is an auxiliary.
English sentences often use an auxiliary to form the passive voice.
The teacher explained how to use an auxiliary with the main verb.

Related Terms

  • synonym: helping verb - a verb that assists the main verb in a sentence
  • antonym: main verb - the primary verb in a sentence that shows the main action or state
