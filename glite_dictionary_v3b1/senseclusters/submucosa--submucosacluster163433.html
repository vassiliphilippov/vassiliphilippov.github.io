submucosa

noun
connective tissue beneath a mucous membrane
Pronunciation: / sʌb.mjuˈkoʊ.sə / (AmE), / sʌb.mjuːˈkəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A layer of connective tissue lying beneath a mucous membrane, supporting and nourishing it.

Examples

The submucosa layer
During surgery, careful dissection of the submucosa is important to avoid damaging underlying structures.
Inflammation of the submucosa can contribute to gastrointestinal diseases.
The submucosa contains blood vessels that supply nutrients to the mucous membrane.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: mucosa - the mucous membrane itself
