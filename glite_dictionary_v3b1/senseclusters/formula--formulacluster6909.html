formula

noun
infant liquid food
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmjələ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmjʊlə / (BrE)
a manufactured liquid or powder food designed for feeding infants who are not breastfed

Examples

Baby formula
She carefully measured the powdered formula and mixed it with warm water.
Some parents choose to feed their babies formula instead of breast milk.
The hospital provides new mothers with free samples of infant formula.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: baby food - food specially made for babies other than milk or formula
  • antonym: breast milk - milk produced by a woman's breasts to feed an infant
