peck

noun
large quantity; a lot (esp. 'a peck of')
9.1
Pronunciation: / pɛk / (AmE), / pɛk / (BrE)
(informal) A large quantity or number of something, especially used in the phrase 'a peck of.'

Examples

A peck of problems
After moving last weekend, we still have a peck of boxes to unpack.
I've got a peck of things to do before the guests arrive.
She found herself in a peck of trouble because she missed the deadline.

  • synonym: heap - a large amount or number of something
  • synonym: lot - a large amount or number of something
