distortive

adjective
causing bias or change
7.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstɑːr.tɪv/ /; / /dɪ.ˈstɔːr.tɪv/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstɔː.tɪv/ (BrE)
causing a change or bias that makes something inaccurate, unfair, or no longer true or in its original form

Examples

Distortive policy impact
The new tax policy had a distortive effect on the housing market.
Analysts warned that distortive regulations could harm fair competition.
Subsidies can be distortive if they favor certain industries over others.
The report had a distortive effect on public understanding of the issue.
Some media coverage can be distortive if it omits important facts.
The new tax rules may have distortive impacts on the housing market.

  • antonym: unbiased - not affected by personal feelings or opinions
