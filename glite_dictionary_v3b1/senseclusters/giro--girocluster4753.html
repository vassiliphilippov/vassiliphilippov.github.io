giro

noun
British government benefit cheque
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪərəʊ / (BrE)
A cheque paid by the British government to a person who is unemployed or receiving benefits, which can be cashed at a bank or post office.

Examples

Unemployment giro
After losing his job, he relied on the giro to pay his rent.
He received his giro in the mail every Thursday.
People queued outside the post office to cash their giro payments.
