nitric bacterium

noun
bacterium that converts nitrites to nitrates
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtrɪk bækˈtɪriəm / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtrɪk bækˈtɪəriəm / (BrE)
a bacterium that oxidizes nitrites into nitrates, playing a role in the nitrogen cycle

Examples

Nitric bacterium in soil
Scientists studied the role of nitric bacterium in wastewater treatment plants.
The presence of nitric bacterium in soil helps maintain soil fertility by converting nitrites to nitrates.
Without the activity of nitric bacterium, the nitrogen cycle would be severely disrupted.
