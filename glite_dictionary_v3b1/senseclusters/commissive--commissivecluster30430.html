commissive

adjective
involving action taken
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪsɪv / (AmE), / kəˈmɪsɪv / (BrE)
relating to an act of doing or committing something, especially a deliberate or wrongful action rather than failing to act

Examples

Commissive act
The court distinguished between commissive and omissive acts when determining responsibility.
A commissive offense involves actively breaking the law rather than simply failing to comply.
Ethics discussions often compare commissive wrongdoing with harm caused by inaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: omissive - relating to omission or failure to act
