bulky

adjective
of large size for its weight
2.3 4.50/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÊŒlki / (AmE), / ËˆbÊŒlki / (BrE)
Having a large size or volume relative to its weight; taking up much space.

Examples

A bulky coat
Although the coat was bulky, it weighed almost nothing.
The package looked bulky, but it was surprisingly light to carry.
We had trouble fitting the bulky suitcase into the car's trunk.

Synonyms

  • unwieldy
    6.7 1.75/m
    unwieldy is hard to move or handle because of awkward shape or size, while bulky mainly means it takes up much space.
  • cumbersome
    6.7 2.49/m
    cumbersome is large and heavy and hard to carry or use, while bulky means it takes up a lot of space.
  • awkward
    2.5 5.55/m
    awkward is clumsy to hold or use because of shape or design, while bulky focuses on taking up a lot of space.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compact - closely packed together; taking up little space
  • antonym: heavy - having great weight
