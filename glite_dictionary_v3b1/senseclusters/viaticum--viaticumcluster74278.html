viaticum

noun
Eucharist for the dying
9.9
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈætɪkəm / (AmE), / vaɪˈætɪkəm / (BrE)
the Eucharist given to a person who is dying, especially in Christian tradition as preparation for death

Examples

Final viaticum
The priest brought the viaticum to the elderly woman in her final hours.
Receiving the viaticum is an important tradition for many Christians facing death.
Family members gathered as the patient was given the viaticum in the hospital room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communion - the Christian sacrament of sharing bread and wine
  • synonym: last rites - religious ceremonies performed for someone who is dying
