aluminum

noun
a lightweight silvery metal element
Pronunciation: / əˈluːmɪnəm / (AmE), / ˌaljʊˈmɪniəm / (BrE)
a lightweight silvery-white metal that is ductile, malleable, and resistant to corrosion, widely used in the manufacture of aircraft, packaging, and construction materials

Examples

Lightweight aluminum metal
Because aluminum does not rust easily, it is ideal for outdoor use.
Many beverage cans are manufactured from recycled aluminum.
The frame of the bicycle is made entirely of aluminum to keep it light.
Bauxite is the main ore used to extract aluminium.
Cans made from aluminium are light and easily recycled.
The window frames are constructed from durable aluminium.
