relationism

noun
philosophical theory of relations
Pronunciation: / rɪˈleɪʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈleɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical theory that relationships between things are more fundamental than the things themselves

Examples

Relationism in physics
Many physicists discuss relationism when debating the nature of space and time.
The concept of relationism challenges the idea that objects exist independently of their connections.
In his lecture, the professor explained how relationism differs from other metaphysical theories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: substantivalism - the theory that things themselves are more fundamental than their relationships
