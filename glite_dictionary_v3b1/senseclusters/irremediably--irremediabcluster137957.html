irremediably

adverb
beyond repair
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrəˈmiːdiəbli / (AmE), / ˌɪrəˈmiːdiəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be fixed, corrected, or made better

Examples

Irremediably damaged
The relationship was irremediably damaged after years of mistrust.
The old manuscript was irremediably stained by water and could not be restored.
He felt irremediably lost after moving to a new country alone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irreparably - in a way that cannot be repaired or restored
  • synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever or cannot be changed
  • antonym: temporarily - for a limited time; not permanent
