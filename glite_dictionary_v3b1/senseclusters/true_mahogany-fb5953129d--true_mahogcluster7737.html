true mahogany

noun
West Indies mahogany tree (Swietenia mahagoni)
2.8 6.2
Pronunciation: / tru məˈhɑːɡəni / (AmE), / truː məˈhɒɡəni / (BrE)
A mahogany tree native to the West Indies, specifically Swietenia mahagoni, regarded as the original source of commercial mahogany wood.

Examples

true mahogany tree
Export restrictions were introduced to protect true mahogany forests in the West Indies.
The furniture was crafted from true mahogany, prized for its deep color and durability.
Unlike African mahogany, true mahogany is native to the Caribbean and has a finer grain.
