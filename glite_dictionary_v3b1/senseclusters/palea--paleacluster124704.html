palea

noun
small plant bract
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈpeɪlɪə / (BrE)
a small, thin, scale-like part found in the flowers of grasses or grains, sometimes called chaff or straw

Examples

The palea scale
The palea protects the delicate parts of the grass flower during development.
Under the microscope, the palea appeared as a thin, translucent scale.
In some species, the palea is almost as large as the surrounding glumes.
After threshing, the barn was full of palea that rustled underfoot for days.
The old poem described the golden palea scattered across the field after harvest.
In some grasses, the palea protects the developing seed from the elements.
In many grasses, the palea and lemma together enclose the developing seed.
The botanist carefully examined the palea under a microscope to study its structure.
Removing the palea is an important step in preparing grass seeds for research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaff - the husks of grains separated during threshing
  • synonym: glume - a bract at the base of a grass spikelet
  • synonym: lemma - the lower of the two bracts enclosing a grass floret
  • synonym: straw - the dry stalks of cereal plants after the grain has been removed
