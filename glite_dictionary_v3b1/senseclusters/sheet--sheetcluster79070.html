sheet

noun
broad, thin expanse or surface
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʃit / (AmE), / ʃiːt / (BrE)
a flat, thin piece or layer of material, such as plastic, metal, glass, or other substances, that is much wider and longer than it is thick

Examples

A sheet of metal
A thin sheet of plastic covered the table to protect it from paint.
After the storm, a sheet of water covered the entire road.
In the winter, a sheet of ice forms over the lake.
He slid a sheet of paper across the desk for her signature.
The windows were covered with a sheet of clear plastic during renovations.
They purchased a sheet of metal to repair the roof.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: film - a very thin layer or coating of something
  • synonym: layer - a quantity or thickness of material, typically one of several, covering a surface or body
  • degree higher: slab - a thick, flat piece of stone, concrete, or other material
