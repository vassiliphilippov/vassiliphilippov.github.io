pancytopenia

noun
deficiency of all blood cell types
6.9 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpænsaɪtəˈpiːniə / (AmE), / ˌpansɪtəˈpiːnɪə / (BrE)
an abnormal reduction in the number of all types of blood cells, typically due to bone marrow disease or failure

Examples

pancytopenia after chemotherapy
Certain chemotherapies can cause pancytopenia as a side effect by suppressing bone marrow function.
Doctors closely monitor bone marrow transplant recipients for signs of pancytopenia.
The patient was diagnosed with pancytopenia after a series of blood tests revealed low counts of all blood cells.
