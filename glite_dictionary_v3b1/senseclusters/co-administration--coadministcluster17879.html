co-administration

noun
giving treatments together
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊ ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of giving two or more drugs or treatments at the same time

Examples

Co-administration of medicines
The co-administration of these drugs may reduce the risk of side effects.
Doctors often recommend co-administration of certain vaccines for better protection.
Research shows that co-administration can improve the effectiveness of some cancer treatments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combination therapy - the use of more than one medication or treatment at the same time
  • antonym: monotherapy - treatment using a single drug or therapy
