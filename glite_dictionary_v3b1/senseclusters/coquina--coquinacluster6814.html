coquina

noun
small marine bivalve
Pronunciation: / koʊˈkiːnə / (AmE), / kəʊˈkiːnə / (BrE)
a small marine bivalve mollusk of the genus Donax, found in sandy surf zones and often used as bait or food

Examples

A coquina shell
Children enjoyed collecting colorful coquina shells along the shoreline during their summer vacation.
Fishermen often dig for coquina at low tide to use as bait for catching larger fish.
The sandy beach was dotted with tiny coquina burrowing quickly beneath the surface as the waves receded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clam - a type of edible marine bivalve mollusk
  • synonym: mussel - a small edible bivalve mollusk found in both salt and fresh water
