accrete

verb
(plants/organs) to grow together or fuse
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàkrit / (AmE), / …ôÀàkriÀêt / (BrE)
(of plants and organs) to grow together or fuse during development, so as to form a single structure.

Examples

Petals accrete together
In some flowers, the petals accrete with the stamens as they develop.
Over time, the roots of neighboring trees can accrete into a single tangled mass.
The two wounds may accrete during the healing process, resulting in a scar.

Synonyms

  • grow together
    3.9 0.26/m
    grow together describes plant parts that join by natural growth, while accrete is a formal biological term for that fusion.
  • coalescing
    6.8 0.00/m
    coalescing means parts or fluids coming together into one, while accrete refers to biological tissues fusing during development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: separate - to move or be moved apart; to stop being joined
