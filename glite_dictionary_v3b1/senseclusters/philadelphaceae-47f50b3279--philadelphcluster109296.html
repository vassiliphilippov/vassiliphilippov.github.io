Philadelphaceae

noun
family of flowering plants (plant taxonomy)
Pronunciation: / fɪˌlædəlˈfeɪsiˌiː /; / fɪˌlædəlˌfeɪˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / fɪˌlædəlˈfeɪsiːˌiː /; / fɪˌlædɛlˈfeɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a group of flowering plants that includes the mock orange (Philadelphus), sometimes considered part of the Hydrangeaceae family

Examples

Philadelphaceae plants
Recent botanical studies often place Philadelphaceae within the Hydrangeaceae family.
The Philadelphaceae are sometimes treated as a separate family from Hydrangeaceae in plant classifications.
The genus Philadelphus, known as mock orange, was traditionally assigned to the Philadelphaceae.
Botanists debated whether Philadelphaceae should be a separate family or part of the Hydrangeaceae.
In older classification systems, Philadelphaceae was not always recognized as a distinct group.
The genus Philadelphus is the main representative of the Philadelphaceae subfamily.
