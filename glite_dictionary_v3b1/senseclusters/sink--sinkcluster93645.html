sink

noun
reservoir or process that absorbs energy or substances
Pronunciation: / sɪŋk / (AmE), / sɪŋk / (BrE)
A process, environment, or reservoir that absorbs or removes energy or a substance from a system.

Examples

A carbon sink
In climate models, oceans function as a primary sink for greenhouse gases.
The forests act as a crucial sink for atmospheric carbon dioxide.
Thermal power stations use cooling towers as a heat sink to dissipate excess energy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: source - a place, person, or thing from which something originates or is supplied
