treaty

noun
formal agreement between countries
4.1 30/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtriti / (AmE), / ÀàtriÀêti / (BrE)
a formal written agreement between two or more countries or rulers, especially about peace, trade, or other relations

Examples

A treaty agreement
A nuclear arms reduction treaty was negotiated after years of tension.
The two nations signed a historic treaty to end the conflict.
Under the terms of the treaty, both sides agreed to respect each other's borders.

Synonyms

  • accord
    6.0 6/m
    an accord is a formal written agreement between nations, while a treaty is the official legal document used for international relations.
  • pact
    3.4 9/m
    a pact is often a political or military agreement between states, while a treaty is a formal legal pact used in diplomacy.
  • compact
    6.5 2/m
    a compact is a signed agreement that may be narrower in scope, while a treaty usually covers broad official relations between countries.
  • covenant
    7.2 5/m
    a covenant is a solemn or moral agreement often with long-term duties, while a treaty is a formal political agreement between nations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: war - open armed conflict between countries
