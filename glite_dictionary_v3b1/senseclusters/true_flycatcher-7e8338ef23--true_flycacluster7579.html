true flycatcher

noun
small insect-eating songbird (family Muscicapidae)
8.1 7.2
Pronunciation: /truː ˈflaɪ.kætʃ.ər/ (AmE), /truː ˈflaɪ.kætʃ.ə/ (BrE)
any of various small perching birds of the family Muscicapidae, characterized by feeding on insects caught in flight

Examples

Small true flycatchers
Several species of true flycatchers migrate long distances between Europe and Africa each year.
The true flycatcher is easily recognized by its upright posture and habit of darting out to snap up insects midair.
Unlike other families, true flycatchers rely almost exclusively on flying insects for food.
