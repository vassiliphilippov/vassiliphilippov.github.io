hitch

noun
uneven walking due to injury
Pronunciation: / hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
An uneven way of walking, typically caused by an injured leg.

Examples

A hitch in her walk
Doctors noticed a subtle hitch in her gait during the examination.
He walked with a noticeable hitch after spraining his ankle.
The old dog's hitch in his step worried his owners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limp - walk with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot
