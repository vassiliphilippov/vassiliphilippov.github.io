medicalization

noun
making medical
7.0
Pronunciation: /med.ɪ.kə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mə.dɪ.kə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /med.ɪ.kə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /med.ɪ.kəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of treating or viewing something (such as behaviors or life events) as a medical issue that may require medical treatment

Examples

The medicalization of childbirth
The medicalization of childbirth has led to more hospital births and medical interventions.
Some critics argue that the medicalization of normal aging can lead to unnecessary treatments.
After the disaster, the rapid medicalization of public spaces provided essential care for survivors.
The medicalization of childhood behaviors has led to an increase in diagnoses and treatments.
Some sociologists criticize the medicalization of aging as unnecessary and potentially harmful.
Debates continue about the medicalization of everyday stress and its impact on society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normalization - the process of making something normal or acceptable
  • synonym: pathologization - the process of regarding or treating something as a disease
