prestimulus

adjective
before a stimulus
Pronunciation: / priːˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / priːˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
occurring or existing before a stimulus is presented, especially in an experiment

Examples

Prestimulus brain activity
Researchers analyzed prestimulus brain waves to understand the subject's initial state.
The prestimulus period is important for measuring baseline neural activity.
Changes in prestimulus conditions can affect the results of the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poststimulus - occurring after a stimulus is presented
