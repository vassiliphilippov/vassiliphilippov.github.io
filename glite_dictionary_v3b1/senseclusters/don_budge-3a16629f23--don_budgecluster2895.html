Don Budge

proper noun
American tennis player, first Grand Slam winner
Pronunciation: / dɑn ˈbʌdʒ / (AmE), / dɒn ˈbʌdʒ / (BrE)
(1915–2000) An American tennis player who became the first to win all four major singles championships (Australian, French, Wimbledon, and U.S. Open) in the same year, achieving a Grand Slam in 1938.

Examples

Don Budge tennis player
Don Budge made history by winning all four major tennis titles in 1938.
Tennis historians often regard Don Budge as one of the game's greatest pioneers.
Young players study Don Budge's technique to improve their own game.
