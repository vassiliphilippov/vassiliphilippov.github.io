red-faced

adjective
having a reddened face
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌfeɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌfeɪst / (BrE)
having a face that is red, usually because of embarrassment, anger, or another strong emotion

Examples

Red-faced with embarrassment
He became red-faced after tripping on stage during the ceremony.
She gave a red-faced apology when she realized her mistake.
The red-faced coach yelled instructions from the sidelines.
After tripping on stage, he stood there red-faced but managed to laugh it off.
She was red-faced when she realized she had forgotten his birthday.
The manager became red-faced with anger during the heated meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ashamed - feeling or showing embarrassment or guilt
  • synonym: blushing - having a red face from embarrassment or shyness
  • synonym: flushed - (of a face) red and hot, typically from emotion or exertion
  • antonym: pale - (of a face) lacking color, often from fear or illness
