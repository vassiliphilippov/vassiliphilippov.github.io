Synonyms

  • insufficiency
    6.5 0.36/m
    insufficiency means not having enough of a needed quality or amount, while deficiency is the general state of lacking something necessary.
  • lack
    1.3 122/m
    lack describes simply being without something needed in daily life, while deficiency has a more formal, medical or technical tone.
  • shortage
    2.7 21/m
    shortage describes a situation where supply falls short and people notice it, while deficiency names the general state of not having enough.
  • scarcity
    4.4 4.99/m
    scarcity paints a picture of things being rare and hard to find, while deficiency simply means not having enough.
  • dearth
    8.5 2.23/m
    dearth feels like a serious lack that affects communities and markets, while deficiency simply means a missing or low amount.
  • paucity
    8.6 1.57/m
    paucity suggests a very small, limited amount in formal writing, while deficiency names the ordinary state of not having enough.
  • inadequacy
    5.8 4.27/m
    inadequacy notes a failure to meet standards or needs, while deficiency focuses on the simple lack or shortage of something necessary.
  • shortfall
    5.5 4.03/m
    shortfall names a specific amount missing compared with a target or plan, while deficiency is a broader lack or shortage.
  • deficit
    4.3 42/m
    deficit often refers to money or resources lost or needed, while deficiency is a general state of not having enough.
  • undersupply
    5.1 0.06/m
    undersupply describes markets where demand outstrips supply, while deficiency names any lack of something needed in general contexts.
  • want
    4.3 2.11/m
    want can mean being in need or poverty in older or formal use, while deficiency is the common modern term for lack.
