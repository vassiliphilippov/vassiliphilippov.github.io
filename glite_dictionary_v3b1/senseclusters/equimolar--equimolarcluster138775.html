equimolar

adjective
equal mole amounts
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkwɪˈmoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌiːkwɪˈməʊlə / (BrE)
having equal amounts of substances, measured in moles, in a chemical mixture or solution

Examples

Equimolar amounts
The chemist mixed equimolar amounts of sodium chloride and potassium chloride in the solution.
To ensure a balanced reaction, the experiment used equimolar concentrations of both reactants.
The study compared the effects of equimolar doses of two different drugs on cell growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isomolar - having the same molar concentration
  • antonym: unequal - not equal in amount or value
