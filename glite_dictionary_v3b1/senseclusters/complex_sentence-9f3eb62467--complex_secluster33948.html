complex sentence

noun
sentence with main and subordinate clause(s)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑm.plɛks ˈsɛn.təns / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pleks ˈsɛn.təns / (BrE)
A sentence consisting of at least one main clause and at least one subordinate clause.

Examples

A complex sentence structure
A complex sentence often helps writers express more detailed relationships between ideas.
Although it was raining, Mark walked to the store in a complex sentence structure.
The teacher asked the students to write a complex sentence using a subordinate clause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compound sentence - a sentence with more than one coordinate clause
  • antonym: simple sentence - a sentence consisting of only one clause
