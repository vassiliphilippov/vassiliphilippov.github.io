brake

noun
device for slowing/stopping a vehicle
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
A device for slowing or stopping the motion of a vehicle or machine, typically by applying pressure to the wheels or another moving part.

Examples

Car brake pedal
He pressed the brake hard to avoid hitting the dog.
Sudden use of the brake can cause the car to skid.
The brake pads need to be replaced every few years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accelerator - a device that increases a vehicle's speed
  • synonym: stop - to cease movement or operation
