take a dive

verb
deliberately pretend to lose (especially in sports)
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə daɪv / (AmE), / teɪk ə daɪv / (BrE)
(of a boxer or sports player) to deliberately pretend to be knocked out or lose a contest, often in exchange for money.

Examples

To take a dive in boxing
He denied he would ever take a dive for any reason, insisting his record is clean.
Rumors spread that the team might have taken a dive to fix the result of the game.
Some people believe the boxer took a dive in the fifth round to let his opponent win.

Related Terms

  • synonym: throw the fight - to deliberately lose a competition, especially for a bribe
  • antonym: win fair and square - to win honestly, without cheating or trickery
