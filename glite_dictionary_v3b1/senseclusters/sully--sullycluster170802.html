sully

verb
damage reputation; tarnish
7.6 0.50/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒli / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒli / (BrE)
to damage, spoil, or make something less pure or good, especially someone's reputation or character (often used figuratively)

Examples

To sully a reputation

Several false accusations were made in an effort to sully her reputation.
The scandal threatened to sully the company's image.
Accusations of cheating can easily sully an athlete's reputation.
The scandal threatened to sully the company's good name.
Years of neglect had sullied the once-beautiful monument.
He refused to let jealousy sully his friendship with her.
Publishing false accusations can easily sully someone's reputation.
