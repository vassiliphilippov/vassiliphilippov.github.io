juice

noun
electric current (informal)
Pronunciation: / dʒus / (AmE), / dʒuːs / (BrE)
(informal) Electric current; electrical power supplied to a device or circuit.

Examples

No juice in the wires
The technician checked the cables before turning the juice back on.
We're out of juice; the power went off during the storm.
Without enough juice, the machine simply won't start.
