upsetment

noun
metal thickening process
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈsɛtmənt / (AmE), / ʌpˈsɛtmənt / (BrE)
the process or result of compressing or forging metal to thicken or reshape a part, especially at one end

Examples

Bolt upsetment
The engineer checked the upsetment at the end of the bolt to ensure it met specifications.
Proper upsetment is crucial when forming rivet heads in metalworking.
After heating the rod, the blacksmith created an upsetment to strengthen the joint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compression - the act of pressing or squeezing something together
  • synonym: forging - shaping metal by heating and hammering
