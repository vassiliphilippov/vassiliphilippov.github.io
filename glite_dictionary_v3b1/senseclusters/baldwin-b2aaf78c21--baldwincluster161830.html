Baldwin

noun
variety of American red-skinned apple
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔl.dwɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːl.dwɪn / (BrE)
A variety of American eating apple with red or yellow and red skin, known for its crisp texture and tart flavor.

Examples

The Baldwin apple
Many New England pies traditionally use the Baldwin variety for its tartness.
She packed a Baldwin apple in her lunch for a tasty snack.
The orchard specializes in growing Baldwin apples, which are prized for their crispness and flavor.
