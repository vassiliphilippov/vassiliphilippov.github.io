hydatidosis

noun
disease from echinococcus larvae
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.də.tə.ˈdoʊ.sɪs/ /; / /haɪ.də.tɪ.ˈdoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /haɪ.də.tə.ˈdəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /haɪ.də.tɪ.ˈdəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
an infestation or infection caused by the larval stage of echinococcus tapeworms, resulting in cyst formation in internal organs

Examples

Hydatidosis infection
In regions where livestock are raised, hydatidosis remains a significant public health concern.
The patient was diagnosed with hydatidosis after cysts were found in her liver during imaging scans.
Veterinarians warn that dogs can transmit the parasites responsible for hydatidosis to humans.
