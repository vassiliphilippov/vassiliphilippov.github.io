preshot

adjective
before a shot
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈpriːʃɒt / (BrE)
happening or done immediately before taking a shot, such as a photograph, film take, or sporting attempt

Examples

Preshot checklist
The director called for a preshot meeting to review the scene with the actors.
A quick preshot check ensured the camera settings were correct before the photographer began.
The athlete performed his usual preshot routine before attempting the free throw.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postshot - happening or done immediately after a shot
  • synonym: preliminary - happening before the main event or action
