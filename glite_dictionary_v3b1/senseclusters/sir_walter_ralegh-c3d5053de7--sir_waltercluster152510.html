Sir Walter Ralegh

proper noun
English courtier and explorer (1552-1618)
Pronunciation: / sɚ ˈwɔltɚ ˈreɪli / (AmE), / sə ˈwɔːltə ˈreɪli / (BrE)
(1552–1618) English courtier, explorer, and writer; a favorite of Queen Elizabeth I, known for his attempts to colonize Virginia and introducing potatoes and tobacco to England.

Examples

Sir Walter Ralegh explorer
Sir Walter Ralegh was instrumental in establishing one of the first English colonies in America.
Historians credit Sir Walter Ralegh with popularizing tobacco and potatoes in England.
Many poems and stories have been written about the adventurous life of Sir Walter Ralegh.
