invade

verb
enter by force for conquest
3.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈveɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈveɪd / (BrE)
to enter (a country or region) by force with an armed force in order to conquer or occupy it

Examples

To invade a country
Allied forces began to invade France on D-Day in 1944.
The army plans to invade the neighboring country at dawn.
The general warned that any attempt to invade sovereign territory would not be tolerated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defend - to protect from attack or invasion
  • synonym: occupy - to take control of a place, especially by military force
