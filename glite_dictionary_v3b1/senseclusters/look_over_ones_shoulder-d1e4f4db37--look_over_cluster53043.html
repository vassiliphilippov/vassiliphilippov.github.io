look over one's shoulder

verb
be cautious or feel threatened
2.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /lʊk ˈoʊ.vər wʌnz ˈʃoʊl.dər/ /; / /lʊk ˈəʊ.vər wʌnz ˈʃəʊl.dər/ /; / /lʊk ˈoʊ.vər wʌns ˈʃoʊl.dər/ (AmE), /lʊk ˈəʊ.və wʌnz ˈʃəʊl.də/ /; / /lʊk ˈəʊ.və wʌns ˈʃəʊl.də/ (BrE)
to behave in a cautious or anxious way, often because you fear being followed, watched, or threatened

Examples

To look over her shoulder
After the break-in, Jean couldn't help but look over her shoulder whenever she heard a noise at night.
Ever since he testified in court, he has had to look over his shoulder everywhere he goes.
You don't want to spend your whole life looking over your shoulder in fear of making mistakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be on one's guard - to be watchful and cautious about possible danger
  • antonym: relax - to become less anxious or worried
