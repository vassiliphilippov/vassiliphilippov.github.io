tune

noun
a recognizable melody or musical sequence
1.0 18/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀên / (AmE), / tjuÀên / (BrE)
A sequence of musical notes that are played or sung in a particular order to form a recognizable melody.

Examples

Catchy tune
I can't get that silly tune out of my head.
She kept humming a cheerful tune all morning while she worked.
The children quickly learned the catchy tune of the new song.

Synonyms

  • melody
    1.8 12/m
    melody is the main musical sequence you sing or hum, while tune refers to any recognizable series of notes.
  • melodic line
    4.5 0.15/m
    melodic line describes the main flowing sequence of notes in a piece, while tune names a recognizable melody.
  • air
    6.6 1.36/m
    air often means a short, charming song people remember, while tune is a general word for a melody.
  • line
    7.2 14/m
    line can mean a musical or frequency pattern in technical use, while tune is specifically a recognizable melody.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmony - the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes to produce chords
  • synonym: melody - a sequence of single notes that is musically satisfying
  • synonym: song - a short piece of music with words
