seethe

verb
move or foam as if boiling
6.0 5.1
Pronunciation: /siːð/ (AmE), /siːð/ (BrE)
(of a liquid) to bubble, boil, or move about actively with agitation

Examples

Seething lava
Molten lava could be seen seething inside the volcano's crater.
The cauldron seethed with a thick, bubbling potion.
The water began to seethe in the pot as it reached a rolling boil.
The cauldron seethed and emitted clouds of steam in the dim light.
The pot began to seethe as the soup reached a full boil.
The river was so agitated after the storm that it seemed to seethe with energy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: simmer - to stay just below boiling point while bubbling gently
