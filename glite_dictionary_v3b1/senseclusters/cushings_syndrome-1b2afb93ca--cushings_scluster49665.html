Cushing's syndrome

noun
glandular disorder from excess cortisol
4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkʊʃ.ɪŋz ˈsɪn.droʊm/ (AmE), /ˈkʊʃ.ɪŋz ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ (BrE)
A medical condition caused by excessive production of cortisol by the adrenal glands, characterized by symptoms such as weight gain, high blood pressure, and muscle weakness.

Examples

Cushing's syndrome symptoms
After months of unexplained weight gain and fatigue, the patient was diagnosed with Cushing's syndrome.
One of the main treatments for Cushing's syndrome involves reducing cortisol levels in the body.
Physicians often look for a round face and high blood pressure as possible signs of Cushing's syndrome.
