vulcanize

verb
treat rubber with heat and chemicals
5.7 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈvʌl.kə.naɪz/ (AmE), /ˈvʌl.kə.naɪz/ (BrE)
to treat rubber or similar materials with heat and sulfur (or other agents) to make them stronger and more elastic

Examples

To vulcanize rubber
Charles Goodyear discovered a way to vulcanize natural rubber, revolutionizing the industry.
Engineers must carefully monitor temperature when they vulcanize materials for industrial products.
Modern tire factories routinely vulcanize rubber to ensure durability and safety.
Charles Goodyear was the first to vulcanize rubber, revolutionizing the tire industry.
Modern factories vulcanize rubber to produce durable soles for shoes.
Without being vulcanized, natural rubber remains sticky and weak.
During manufacturing, the rubber is vulcanised to enhance its resilience and strength.
Natural rubber must be vulcanised to make it suitable for most commercial products.
Some industrial processes require polymers to be vulcanised before further use.
