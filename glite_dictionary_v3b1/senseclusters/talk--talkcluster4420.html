talk

noun
public speech or lecture
1.8 37/m
Pronunciation: / t…îk / (AmE), / t…îÀêk / (BrE)
a speech, lecture, or formal presentation given to an audience

Examples

A talk at school
I attended a talk at the library about local history last weekend.
Several experts will be giving talks during the conference.
The professor gave a fascinating talk on climate change to a crowd of students.
After the talk, there was a question-and-answer session with the audience.
She gave a fascinating talk about her travels in South America.
The conference will begin with a talk by Dr. Lee on climate change.

Synonyms

  • speech
    1.8 80/m
    speech is a formal, prepared talk given to an audience, while talk is a more general word for speaking to people.
  • address
    5.3 16/m
    address is a formal speech for an audience on a particular occasion, while talk is used for many kinds of presentations.
  • lecture
    3.1 26/m
    lecture is a lesson-style talk used to teach students or audiences, while talk covers many types of speaking events.
  • presentation
    3.5 42/m
    presentation is a prepared show of information, often with slides, while talk is any spoken event without that focus.
  • oration
    7.6 0.85/m
    oration is a highly formal, often ceremonial speech with grand language, while talk is a simpler, everyday presentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: address - a formal speech delivered to an audience
  • synonym: lecture - an educational speech given to an audience, especially in a university
  • degree lower: seminar - a small group meeting for discussion or training, often academic
