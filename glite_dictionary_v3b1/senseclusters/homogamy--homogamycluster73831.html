homogamy

noun
simultaneous organ maturity
9.8
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒɡəmi / (BrE)
the condition in which the male and female reproductive organs of a flower or plant mature at the same time, allowing self-fertilization

Examples

Flower homogamy
In some plants, homogamy ensures that self-pollination can occur efficiently.
The botanist observed homogamy in the wildflowers, noting that all reproductive organs matured together.
Species with homogamy often have a higher chance of self-fertilization in isolated environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dichogamy - the condition in which male and female reproductive organs mature at different times
