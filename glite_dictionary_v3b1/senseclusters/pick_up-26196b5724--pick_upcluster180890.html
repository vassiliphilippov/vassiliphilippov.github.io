pick up

verb
buy something casually
1.0
Pronunciation: / pɪk ʌp / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to collect, gather, acquire, or buy something or someone, especially by going to a place or casually while doing other things; to give someone a ride

Examples

To pick up groceries
He picked up some bread and milk at the store after work.
I decided to pick up a newspaper on my way to work.
She always picks up souvenirs when she travels.
Could you pick up Sarah from the airport this evening?
The school bus picks up children at this corner every morning.
They stopped to pick up a couple of hitchhikers on their way to the concert.
Could you pick up some groceries on your way home?
The school bus picks up children at 8 a.m. every morning.
We need to pick up our tickets from the box office before the show starts.
