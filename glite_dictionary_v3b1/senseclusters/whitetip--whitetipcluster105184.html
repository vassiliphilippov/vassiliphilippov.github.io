whitetip

noun
shark with white fin tips
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌtɪp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌtɪp / (BrE)
a type of shark known for having distinctive white tips on its fins

Examples

Whitetip shark
The diver was excited to see a whitetip gliding gracefully through the clear water.
Many marine biologists study the behavior of the whitetip in tropical oceans.
A whitetip can often be identified by the bright markings on its fins.

Related Terms

  • antonym: great white shark - a large predatory shark with a white underside
  • synonym: reef shark - a shark species commonly found near coral reefs
