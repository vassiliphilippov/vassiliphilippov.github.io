uninflated

adjective
plain and modest
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (BrE)
not exaggerated, pompous, or made to seem more important; plain and modest in style or presentation

Examples

Uninflated style
Her uninflated account of the event made her testimony seem honest and believable.
The artist's uninflated style appealed to those who preferred simplicity over extravagance.
He gave an uninflated report, avoiding any exaggeration or dramatic language.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exaggerated - made to seem more important or impressive than it is
  • synonym: modest - not showing off or exaggerating importance
  • antonym: pompous - self-important or grand in style
  • synonym: understated - presented in a subtle or restrained way
