sagittal suture

noun
joint connecting skull's parietal bones
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsædʒɪtəl ˈsutʃər / (AmE), / ˈsædʒɪtəl ˈsjuːtʃə / (BrE)
the dense, fibrous joint that connects the two parietal bones of the skull along the midline

Examples

The sagittal suture line
A fracture along the sagittal suture can be seen on the X-ray images.
In infants, the sagittal suture allows for growth of the skull as the brain develops.
The surgeon carefully examined the sagittal suture during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coronal suture - the suture separating the frontal and parietal bones of the skull
  • synonym: lambdoid suture - the suture connecting the parietal bones to the occipital bone
