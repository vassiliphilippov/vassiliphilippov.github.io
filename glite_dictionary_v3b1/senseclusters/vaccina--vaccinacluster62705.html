vaccina

noun
cowpox infection for smallpox immunity
Pronunciation: / vækˈsiːnə / (AmE), / vækˈsiːnə / (BrE)
A localized skin infection in humans caused by intentional inoculation with the cowpox virus, once used to provide immunity to smallpox, typically lasting about three weeks and leaving a scar.

Examples

The vaccina skin infection
In the nineteenth century, vaccina was administered to protect populations from smallpox outbreaks.
Modern smallpox eradication made the use of vaccina unnecessary in most countries.
The pitted scar left by vaccina was once a common mark on the arms of schoolchildren.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vaccine - a substance used to stimulate the production of antibodies and provide immunity against disease
