squalid

adjective
morally degraded or sordid
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˈskwɒlɪd / (BrE)
(of actions or places) morally degraded; showing or involving a lack of moral standards or decency.

Examples

Squalid behavior
He grew up in a squalid environment of crime and corruption.
The squalid details of the scandal shocked the entire community.
The film depicts the squalid underworld of gambling and betrayal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seedy - morally degraded or disreputable
  • synonym: sleazy - morally low or sordid
  • synonym: sordid - involving immoral or dishonest behavior
  • antonym: virtuous - showing high moral standards
