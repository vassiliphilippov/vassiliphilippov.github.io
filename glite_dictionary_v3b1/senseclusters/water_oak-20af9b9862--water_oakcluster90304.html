water oak

noun
oak tree native to southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˌoʊk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌəʊk / (BrE)
A deciduous oak tree (Quercus nigra) native to the southeastern United States, often grown as a shade tree and thriving in wet soils.

Examples

A water oak tree
After the storm, only the sturdy water oak still stood tall among the fallen trees.
Along the riverbank, several water oaks provided shelter for wildlife.
The water oak is commonly planted in parks for its shade and rapid growth.
