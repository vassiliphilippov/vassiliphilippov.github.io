nondemolition

adjective
not destructive
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˈmɪlɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈmɪlɪʃən / (BrE)
not causing damage or destruction, especially referring to methods or inspections that preserve the object or structure being examined

Examples

Nondemolition inspection
The team used nondemolition testing to check the bridge's safety without harming its structure.
Preservationists prefer nondemolition methods when examining historic buildings.
A nondemolition approach allowed the engineers to inspect the wall without removing any original material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: noninvasive - not involving damage or penetration to the object or body
