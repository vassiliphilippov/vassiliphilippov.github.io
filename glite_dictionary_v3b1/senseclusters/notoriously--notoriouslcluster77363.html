notoriously

adverb
in a way that is widely known (often for something bad)
2.1
Pronunciation: / noʊˈtɔːriəsli / (AmE), / nəʊˈtɔːriəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is well known, typically for being bad or unfavorable.

Examples

Is notoriously late
He is notoriously late to meetings, so we usually start without him.
That stretch of road is notoriously dangerous after dark.
The city is notoriously expensive, making it difficult for students to live there.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infamously - in a way that is well known for being bad or disgraceful
