Tashmetum

proper noun
ancient Mesopotamian goddess, consort of Nabu
9.9
Pronunciation: / tæʃˈmeɪtəm / (AmE), / tæʃˈmɛtəm / (BrE)
An ancient Mesopotamian goddess, wife of Nabu, worshipped as a deity of wisdom and listening to prayers.

Examples

Tashmetum goddess of wisdom
In Babylonian mythology, Tashmetum is known as the goddess who listens to prayers.
Priests offered incense to Tashmetum in hopes of gaining favor with Nabu.
The temple dedicated to Tashmetum was a site of pilgrimage for those seeking wisdom.
