wall of silence

noun
refusal to reveal information, especially in groups
Pronunciation: / wɔl əv ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / wɔːl əv ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
an agreement or tendency among a group, especially within organizations like the police, to keep silent and not reveal information, typically to protect members from investigation or criticism

Examples

A wall of silence
After the scandal broke, a wall of silence prevented investigators from getting any answers.
Despite repeated questions from journalists, there remained a wall of silence around the incident.
The corruption was uncovered only when the wall of silence finally crumbled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conspiracy of silence - an agreement to keep silent about something sensitive
  • synonym: omertà - a code of silence, especially among members of a criminal group
