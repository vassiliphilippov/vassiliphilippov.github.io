for

preposition
exchange or substitution
1.0
Pronunciation: / fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
used to indicate exchange, substitution, or something given in return for something else

Examples

Pay for a book
She paid five dollars for the book at the market.
They exchanged their old car for a newer model last week.
He swapped his sandwich for a slice of pizza during lunch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in exchange for - as a substitute or replacement for something
  • antonym: instead of - in place of something
