moiré

noun
wavy-patterned fabric
Pronunciation: / mwɑːˈreɪ / (AmE), / mwɑːˈreɪ / (BrE)
a fabric, especially silk, with a glossy, wavy, or 'watered' surface pattern

Examples

A moire dress
The evening gown was made of shimmering moiré that caught the light beautifully.
He admired the delicate moiré pattern on the antique silk curtains.
Designers often choose moiré for its unique, wavy appearance in luxury garments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damask - a rich, patterned fabric often made of silk or linen
  • synonym: taffeta - a crisp, smooth woven fabric, often used for dresses
