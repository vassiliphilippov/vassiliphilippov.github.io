color

verb
deceive or distort the truth (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlər /; / ˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to influence, distort, or affect something, especially an opinion or the truth

Examples

To color the truth
He tried to color the story to make himself seem innocent.
The politician attempted to color the facts during the interview.
You shouldn't color the truth just to avoid getting in trouble.
Her childhood experiences still color the way she reacts to criticism.
Regret can color your memories of important moments.
The news was colored by the reporter's personal bias.
Her personal experiences color her interpretation of events.
The reporter admitted that his views might color his articles.
You shouldn't let your emotions color your judgment.
