tree branch

noun
limb growing from a tree trunk
Pronunciation: / triː bræntʃ / (AmE), / triː brɑːntʃ / (BrE)
a limb that grows from the trunk or main bough of a tree, typically supporting leaves, flowers, or fruit

Examples

A tree branch with leaves
A bird was perched on the tree branch, singing a cheerful melody.
He climbed up the oak and sat on a sturdy tree branch to enjoy the view.
The wind made the tree branches sway gently against the window.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bough - a main branch of a tree, larger than a tree branch
  • synonym: branch - a part of a tree that grows out from the trunk or from a bough
  • degree lower: twig - a small, thin branch of a tree or bush
