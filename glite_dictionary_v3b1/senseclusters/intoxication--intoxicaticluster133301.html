Examples

Synonyms

    inebriation is a formal word for being drunk in social or legal contexts, while intoxication emphasizes diminished physical and mental control.
  • drunkenness
    4.4 2/m
    drunkenness is an everyday, visible state of heavy drinking and poor control, while intoxication sounds clinical and means reduced physical and mental control.
  • inebriety
    9.9 0.01/m
    inebriety is a rare formal term often found in legal or medical texts, while intoxication describes the temporary loss of control from alcohol.
  • insobriety
    9.9 0.00/m
    insobriety is a less common, somewhat legal-sounding word for lack of sobriety, while intoxication focuses on temporary alcohol-caused loss of control.
