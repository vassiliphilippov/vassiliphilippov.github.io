amphitheater

noun
sloping, tiered gallery for spectators
Pronunciation: / ˈæmfɪˌθiːətər /; / ˈæmfɪˌθiətɚ /; / ˈæm.fəˌθi.ə.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈæmfɪˌθɪətə /; / ˈamfɪˌθɪətə /; / ˈam.fɪˌθɪə.tə /; / ˈam.fɪˌθiː.ə.tə / (BrE)
a large building or area with rows of seats rising around a central open area, used for performances, sports, lectures, demonstrations, or other public events

Examples

A large amphitheater
During the play, the audience filled every seat in the modern amphitheater.
The ancient Roman amphitheater could seat thousands of spectators at once.
The medical students watched the operation from the amphitheater above the surgery room.
The ancient Romans built many impressive amphitheaters for gladiator games and public spectacles.
The new amphitheater in the park hosts theater performances and music festivals throughout the year.
We attended a concert under the stars at the outdoor amphitheater last summer.
From the top tier of the amphitheatre, the audience had a perfect view of the stage.
Several concerts are held each summer in the open-air amphitheatre by the lake.
The ancient Romans built the Colosseum as a grand amphitheatre for public spectacles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arena - a central area for performances or events, usually surrounded by seats
  • synonym: auditorium - the part of a theater, concert hall, or other public building where the audience sits
  • degree lower: theatre - a building where performances are given
