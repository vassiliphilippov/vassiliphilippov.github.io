restartable

adjective
can be resumed
4.0
Pronunciation: / riˈstɑːrtəbəl / (AmE), / riˈstɑːtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be started again after stopping or being interrupted (sometimes from where you left off)

Examples

Restartable software process
The software update process is restartable, so you do not lose progress if your computer shuts down.
A restartable download saves time by continuing from where it left off after a connection drop.
Many modern games are restartable, allowing players to resume from their last checkpoint.
The software is restartable, so you can continue your work after a crash.
A restartable process saves time if the system unexpectedly shuts down.
Not all games are restartable from the last checkpoint.

  • antonym: irreversible - impossible to return to a previous state or start again
  • synonym: resumable - able to be continued after being paused or interrupted
