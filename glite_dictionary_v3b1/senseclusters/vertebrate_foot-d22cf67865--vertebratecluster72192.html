vertebrate foot

noun
end part of a vertebrate limb
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrtʃəˌbreɪt fʊt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪbrət fʊt / (BrE)
the terminal part of a limb in vertebrate animals, used for standing or moving

Examples

The vertebrate foot bones
Researchers studied the bones and muscles in the vertebrate foot to understand evolutionary changes.
The fossilized vertebrate foot provided valuable insight into how ancient animals moved.
The structure of a vertebrate foot varies greatly among species, from hooves to claws to human feet.
