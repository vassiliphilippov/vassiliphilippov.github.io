doddy

adjective
shaggy or woolly
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÉ’di / (AmE), / ËˆdÉ’di / (BrE)
rounded or covered with tufts, especially of wool; having a shaggy or woolly appearance

Examples

A doddy sheep
After a long winter, the sheep appeared doddy and in need of shearing.
The old coat was doddy with tufts of wool sticking out everywhere.
In the countryside, farmers sometimes described their animals as doddy before spring.

Synonyms

  • tufty
    5.5 0.02/m
    tufty has small clumps or tufts sticking out, while doddy means rounded and covered with tufts, often of wool.
  • woolly
    3.0 1.06/m
    woolly looks soft and fluffy like sheep's wool, while doddy means rounded or covered with tufts, especially of wool.
  • shaggy
    3.2 2.30/m
    shaggy has long, untidy strands or fur that look messy, while doddy suggests a rounded, tufted, woolly appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shaggy - having long, thick, or unkempt hair or fur
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough or shaggy
