doddy

adjective
shaggy or woolly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɒdi / (AmE), / ˈdɒdi / (BrE)
rounded or covered with tufts, especially of wool; having a shaggy or woolly appearance

Examples

A doddy sheep
After a long winter, the sheep appeared doddy and in need of shearing.
The old coat was doddy with tufts of wool sticking out everywhere.
In the countryside, farmers sometimes described their animals as doddy before spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shaggy - having long, thick, or unkempt hair or fur
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not rough or shaggy
