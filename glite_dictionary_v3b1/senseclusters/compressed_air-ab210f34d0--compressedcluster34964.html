compressed air

noun
air under higher than normal pressure
2.5
Pronunciation: / kəmˈprɛst ˈɛr / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛst ˈeə / (BrE)
Air that is kept at a pressure higher than normal atmospheric pressure, often used as a power source in machinery or tools.

Examples

Compressed air power
Many paint sprayers work by using compressed air to deliver an even coat.
The factory relies on a compressed air system to power its tools.
The mechanics used compressed air to clean dust from the machinery.
