impoundment

noun
water storage area
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpaʊndmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpaʊndmənt / (BrE)
an area or structure, such as a reservoir, where water is collected and stored, often by a dam

Examples

Water impoundment
The construction of the dam resulted in a massive impoundment that supplies water to the city.
Wildlife has flourished around the new impoundment created for flood control.
Farmers depend on the impoundment to irrigate their crops during dry seasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dam - a barrier built to hold back water and raise its level
  • antonym: release - to let water flow out from a storage area
  • synonym: reservoir - a large natural or artificial lake for storing water
