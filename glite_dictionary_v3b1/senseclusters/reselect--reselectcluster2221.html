reselect

verb
to choose again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːsəˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ˌriːsɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
to choose someone again for a position, office, or candidacy, especially by an official group or party.

Examples

To reselect a leader
After much debate, the committee decided to reselect her as chairperson for another term.
The party will reselect its current candidate for the upcoming election.
If the board is not satisfied, they may choose not to reselect the manager.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dismiss - to remove someone from a position
  • synonym: reappoint - to assign someone to a position again
  • synonym: select - to choose someone or something from a group
