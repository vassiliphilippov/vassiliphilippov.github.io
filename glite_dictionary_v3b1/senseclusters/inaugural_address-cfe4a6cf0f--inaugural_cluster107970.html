inaugural address

noun
formal speech at a ceremony, esp. by a president
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɔɡjərəl əˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ɪˈnɔːɡjərəl əˈdrɛs / (BrE)
a formal speech given at the beginning of an important event or ceremony, especially by a United States president at the start of their term

Examples

The inaugural address
Every new governor prepares carefully for their inaugural address to set the tone for their term.
Many people consider Lincoln's inaugural address one of the greatest speeches in American history.
The president delivered his inaugural address in front of thousands gathered at the Capitol.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inauguration - the formal admission of someone to office
