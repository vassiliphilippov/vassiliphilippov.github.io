sanga

noun
African cattle breed
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːŋɡə / (AmE), / ˈsæŋɡə / (BrE)
a type of African cattle breed, intermediate between humpless taurine and zebu, kept for milk and meat

Examples

A sanga cow
A herd of sanga grazed peacefully on the savanna in the early morning light.
Farmers in several African countries raise sanga for both milk and meat production.
The sanga breed is well adapted to the hot and dry climate of the region.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: taurine cattle - humpless domestic cattle of European origin
  • degree higher: zebu - a humped breed of domestic cattle from South Asia
