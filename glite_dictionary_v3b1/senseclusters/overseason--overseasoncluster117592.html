overseason

verb
to add too much seasoning
6.4
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈsiː.zən/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈsiː.zən/ (BrE)
to add too much seasoning or flavoring to food, making it taste overly strong or unpleasant

Examples

To overseason the soup
If you overseason the stew, it can become too spicy for some people to enjoy.
She accidentally overseasoned the chicken, so it tasted very salty.
It is easy to overseason a dish if you do not taste as you cook.

Related Terms

  • synonym: season - to add herbs, spices, or flavoring to food
  • antonym: underseason - to add too little seasoning to food
