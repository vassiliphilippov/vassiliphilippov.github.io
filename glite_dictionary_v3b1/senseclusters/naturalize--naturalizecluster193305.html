naturalize

verb
adapt plant or land to environment
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃrəˌlaɪz /; / ˈnætʃərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnatʃrəlʌɪz /; / ˈnatʃərəlaɪz /; / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəlʌɪz / (BrE)
to adapt or accustom something (such as a plant, word, or custom) to a new environment or context so that it becomes normal or typical there

Examples

To naturalize plants
Gardeners often naturalize daffodils by allowing them to spread naturally in the wild.
Many settlers tried to naturalize foreign crops to increase food supply in the new land.
Scientists are working to naturalize the area by reintroducing native plants.
Foreign words are often naturalized into a language when they are commonly used.
The French recipe has been naturalized for American tastes over the years.
The ancient myths were naturalized into the culture of the new country.
Botanists hope to naturalise several exotic species in the local forest.
Over time, some non-native plants may naturalise in the new environment and flourish.
With careful management, she was able to naturalise the hillside and restore native flora.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilate - to absorb and integrate into a wider society or culture
  • synonym: cultivate - to prepare and use land for crops or gardening
  • synonym: domesticate - to tame (an animal or plant) for human use
  • synonym: introduce - to bring something into use or operation for the first time in a place
  • synonym: tame - to make a wild animal or plant less wild or easier to control
