civilianization

noun
conversion to civilian use
Pronunciation: / sɪˌvɪliənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˌvɪliənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something civilian instead of military or police, such as equipment, jobs, or control

Examples

The civilianization of bases
The civilianization of former army bases has led to new housing developments and parks.
Many countries encourage the civilianization of military technologies for economic growth.
The civilianization process transformed the airfield into a busy commercial airport.
The civilianization of airport security roles has led to new hiring practices.
Many cities are considering the civilianization of certain administrative police positions.
Debates about civilianization often focus on cost savings and public perception.
The civilianization of the police force aimed to improve community relations and reduce military-style tactics.
After years of military rule, the country began a gradual civilianization of its government institutions.
International observers praised the civilianization process as a step toward greater democracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demilitarization - the removal of military forces or influence from an area or institution
  • antonym: militarization - the process of placing something under military control
