nonconservative

adjective
not cautious or preservative
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈsɜːrvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈsɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
not cautious or preservative in approach; tending to favor more radical, aggressive, or risk-taking actions or policies

Examples

Nonconservative strategy
The committee adopted a nonconservative strategy to address the urgent financial crisis.
Doctors sometimes recommend a nonconservative treatment when the risks of waiting are too high.
Her nonconservative approach to investment led to both big gains and significant losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressive - pursuing goals forcefully or boldly
  • antonym: conservative - favoring caution and preservation; resistant to change
  • synonym: radical - favoring fundamental or extreme changes
