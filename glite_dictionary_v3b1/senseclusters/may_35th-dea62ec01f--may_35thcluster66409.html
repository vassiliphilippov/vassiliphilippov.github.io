May 35th

noun
a fictitious or impossible date
9.9
Pronunciation: /meɪ ˈθɜːr.tiː.fɪfθ/ (AmE), /meɪ ˈθɜː.tiː.fɪfθ/ (BrE)
A humorous or ironic way to refer to a date that does not exist, used to suggest a deadline that will never come or something that will never happen.

Examples

On May 35th
He promised to pay me back on May 35th, which obviously isn't a real date.
If you plan to start exercising on May 35th, it means you don’t plan to start at all.
The project will be finished by May 35th, so in other words, never.
