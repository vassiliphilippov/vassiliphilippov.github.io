tinsel

verb

to give something a superficial or cheaply showy brightness or glamour; to make something appear more attractive than it truly is, especially in a way that lacks genuine value

Examples

To tinsel a story
Critics argued that the director tinselled the story with unnecessary glitz.
Holiday commercials often tinsel everyday products to make them seem exciting.
The politician's speech was tinseled with empty promises and flashy rhetoric.
