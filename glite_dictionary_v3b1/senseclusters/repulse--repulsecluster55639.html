repulse

verb
cause intense distaste or aversion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpʌls / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌls / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel intense distaste or aversion; to be repellent to

Examples

To repulse someone
He was repulsed by the cruelty depicted in the film.
The smell of rotten eggs would repulse anyone with a normal sense of smell.
Their arrogant attitude tends to repulse potential friends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attract - to cause to come near; to draw by appeal
  • synonym: disgust - to cause a strong feeling of dislike or revulsion
