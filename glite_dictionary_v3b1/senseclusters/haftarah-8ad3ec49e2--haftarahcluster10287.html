Haftarah

noun
Jewish reading from the Prophets
Pronunciation: / hɑfˈtɑrə / (AmE), / hɑːfˈtɑːrə / (BrE)
A short reading from the books of the Prophets, recited on Sabbaths and Jewish festivals in synagogue after the Torah reading.

Examples

The Haftarah reading
After the Torah is read, the rabbi chanted the Haftarah passage in Hebrew.
During the service, Sara was honored with reading the Haftarah.
He practiced his Haftarah portion for weeks before his bar mitzvah.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Torah - the central reference of the religious Judaic tradition; the first five books of the Hebrew Bible
