arrange

verb
organize or make plans for
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to organize or make plans for something to happen, such as an event, meeting, or activity

Examples

To arrange a meeting
Could you arrange dinner reservations for tomorrow night?
Let me arrange a taxi for you to the airport.
They managed to arrange the conference in just two weeks.
He is arranging a conference that will bring together experts from around the world.
They arranged a surprise party for her birthday.
We need to arrange a meeting with the marketing team next week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cancel - to decide that something planned will not happen
  • synonym: organize - to arrange or coordinate systematically
