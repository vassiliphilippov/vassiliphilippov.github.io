Boletus

noun
genus of mushrooms with pores
8.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / boʊˈliːtəs / (AmE), / bəʊˈliːtəs / (BrE)
A genus of fleshy, soft mushrooms with pores instead of gills, including both edible and poisonous species.

Examples

The Boletus mushroom
Many gourmet chefs prize the Boletus edulis, known as the porcini mushroom, for its flavor.
Some species within the Boletus genus are toxic and should not be consumed.
The forest floor was dotted with several Boletus mushrooms after the rain.
