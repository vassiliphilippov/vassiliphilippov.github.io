isogeotherm

noun
line of constant subsurface temperature
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊˈdʒioʊˌθɜrm / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈdʒiːəʊˌθɜːm / (BrE)
A line connecting points of equal temperature below the Earth's surface.

Examples

An isogeotherm line
Geologists use isogeotherms to study variations in heat flow beneath the Earth's crust.
The isogeotherm indicates the depth at which the temperature remains constant throughout the region.
The distribution of minerals is often mapped along specific isogeotherm lines.
