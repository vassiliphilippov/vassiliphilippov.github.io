contemporize

verb
arrange events to co-occur in time
8.9 9.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛmpəˌraɪz / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpəraɪz /; / kənˈtɛmpərʌɪz / (BrE)
to arrange or represent events so that they occur at the same time or within the same period

Examples

To contemporize events
Historians often contemporize various events to show how they influenced one another.
Scholars contemporized different text sources to align their timelines for better comparison.
The documentary attempts to contemporize ancient myths by setting them in the same era.
Historians often contemporise events to create a coherent narrative.
It is difficult to contemporise myths from different cultures due to varying timelines.
The scholars attempted to contemporise the major events in ancient Egypt and Greece.
