review

noun
follow-up medical examination
3.0 72/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààvju / (AmE), / r…™ÀàvjuÀê / (BrE)
an examination or assessment of something, especially to check progress, quality, or compliance (medical, legal, official, or general)

Examples

Medical review
After his surgery, he attended a review with his physician every two weeks.
During the review, the nurse checked his blood pressure and overall recovery.
The doctor scheduled a review to assess the effectiveness of her treatment.
After the decision, the lawyers discussed the possibility of seeking review by a higher court.
The Supreme Court granted review to determine whether the trial was fair.
The defendant filed a motion for a judicial review of the case.
A review can provide stakeholders with some reassurance, but not the certainty of an audit.
As part of the merger process, an independent firm conducted a review rather than a full audit.
The company ordered a review of its financial statements before the end of the fiscal year.
