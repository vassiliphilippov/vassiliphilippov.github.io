coarse-grained

adjective
having a rough or large-grained texture
Pronunciation: / kɔrsˈɡreɪnd /; / ˌkɔrsˈɡreɪnd / (AmE), / kɔːsˈɡreɪnd /; / ˌkɔːsˈɡreɪnd / (BrE)
having a rough texture or made up of large, visible grains or particles

Examples

Coarse-grained sandpaper
Some artists prefer coarse-grained canvas for oil painting techniques.
The bread had a distinctive, coarse-grained texture because it contained whole grains.
The countertop was made of coarse-grained granite that felt uneven to the touch.
She prefers coarse-grained sugar for baking certain desserts.
The sandstone in this region is mostly coarse-grained, making it easy to identify.
Under the microscope, the rock appeared distinctly coarse-grained compared to others.
