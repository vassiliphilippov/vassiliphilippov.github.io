prose

verb
to speak dully
Pronunciation: / proʊz / (AmE), / prəʊz / (BrE)
to write or speak in a dull, ordinary, or uninteresting way, often at length

Examples

To prose for hours
He prosed on for hours about the details of his job, and everyone grew bored.
The lecturer prosed through the material without much enthusiasm, losing the students' interest.
She prosed about her daily routine, making the conversation feel endless.

Related Terms

  • antonym: captivate - to attract and hold the attention of someone
  • synonym: drone - to speak in a dull, monotonous tone
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or boring way
