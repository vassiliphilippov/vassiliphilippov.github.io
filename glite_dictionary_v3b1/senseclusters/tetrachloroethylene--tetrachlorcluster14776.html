tetrachloroethylene

noun
chemical anthelmintic and cleaning solvent
9.9
Pronunciation: /te.trə.klɑː.r.oʊ.ˈeθ.ɪ.liːn/ /; / /te.trə.klɔː.r.oʊ.ˈeθ.ɪ.liːn/ (AmE), /te.trə.klɔː.rəʊ.ˈeθ.ɪ.liːn/ (BrE)
A chemical compound used as an anthelmintic agent against hookworm and other nematodes, and also as a solvent in dry cleaning and degreasing.

Examples

Tetrachloroethylene solvent use
Tetrachloroethylene has been employed as a treatment for certain intestinal parasites in humans.
Safety protocols require proper ventilation when handling tetrachloroethylene due to its potentially harmful fumes.
The laboratory used tetrachloroethylene as a solvent for cleaning metal parts.
