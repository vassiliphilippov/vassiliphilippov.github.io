Chilean jasmine

noun
ornamental vine with fragrant white flowers
Pronunciation: / tʃɪˈliən ˈdʒæzmɪn / (AmE), / tʃɪˈliːən ˈdʒæzmɪn / (BrE)
A woody vine native to Argentina, grown as an ornamental plant for its glossy leaves and clusters of large, fragrant, creamy-white, funnel-shaped flowers.

Examples

The Chilean jasmine flower
The Chilean jasmine is prized by gardeners for its lovely scent and attractive foliage.
Unlike true jasmines, Chilean jasmine thrives in cooler climates and still produces beautiful flowers.
We planted a Chilean jasmine by the pergola to enjoy its fragrant blooms all summer long.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jasmine - any of various shrubs and vines of the olive family, producing fragrant flowers
