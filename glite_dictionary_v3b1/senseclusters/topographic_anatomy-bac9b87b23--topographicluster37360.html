topographic anatomy

noun
study of anatomy by body regions
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑːpəˈɡræfɪk əˈnætəmi / (AmE), / ˌtɒpəˈɡræfɪk əˈnætəmi / (BrE)
The study of the body's structure by regions, focusing on the relationships between different anatomical parts within specific areas.

Examples

Topographic anatomy study
In topographic anatomy, the focus is on how muscles, nerves, and vessels relate within a single body region.
Medical students often use topographic anatomy to learn the spatial arrangement of structures before surgery.
The textbook includes detailed illustrations to support the study of topographic anatomy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regional anatomy - study of anatomy by specific body regions
  • antonym: systemic anatomy - study of anatomy by organ systems
