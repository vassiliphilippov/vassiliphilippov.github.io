scruffily

adverb
messily or untidily
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈskrʌfɪli / (AmE), / ˈskrʌfɪli / (BrE)
in a messy, untidy, or shabby way (especially about appearance or dress)

Examples

Scruffily dressed
He walked into the office scruffily dressed, drawing surprised looks from his colleagues.
The children played scruffily in the muddy backyard after the rain.
She was criticized for appearing scruffily at the formal dinner.
He arrived at the interview scruffily dressed, with his shirt untucked and shoes muddy.
The children played outside and returned home scruffily, covered in dirt and grass stains.
She always writes scruffily in her notebook, making it hard for others to read.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neatly - in a tidy and orderly way
  • synonym: shabbily - in a worn or untidy way
