parry

verb
deflect or avoid (a question or issue)
Pronunciation: / ˈpæri / (AmE), / ˈpæri / (BrE)
to avoid answering a question, fulfilling a duty, or dealing with an issue, often by cleverly deflecting it

Examples

To parry a question
She skillfully parried criticism during the interview, refusing to be drawn into specifics.
The spokesperson kept parrying reporters’ questions about the scandal.
When asked about the budget cuts, the minister tried to parry the question with a vague response.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confront - to face up to and deal with a problem or difficult situation
  • synonym: dodge - to avoid (a question, duty, etc.) by being clever or deceitful
  • synonym: evade - to avoid doing or answering something directly
