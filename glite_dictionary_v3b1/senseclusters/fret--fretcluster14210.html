fret

verb
to press or rub tightly; cause discomfort
Pronunciation: / frɛt / (AmE), / frɛt / (BrE)
to wear away, erode, or irritate a surface by rubbing or friction

Examples

Water frets the rock
His boots fretted his ankles after several hours of walking.
If the straps fret your skin, you should adjust the bag for a better fit.
The collar was so tight it began to fret her neck red.
Constant movement caused the collar to fret the dog's fur until it wore thin.
If the edges of the wound fret, healing may be delayed by the irritation.
The rope began to fret the skin on his hands after hours of pulling.
Acidic rain can fret the metal surface of outdoor sculptures.
Over time, the constant dripping of water began to fret the stone walls of the cave.
The fast-moving stream fretted the riverbank, slowly wearing it away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrode - gradually destroy or damage (especially by chemical action)
  • synonym: erode - gradually wear away
