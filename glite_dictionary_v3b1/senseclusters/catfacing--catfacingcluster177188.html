catfacing

noun
fruit deformity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌfeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkætˌfeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of deformity or scarring on fruit, especially tomatoes, often caused by poor pollination or pests

Examples

Catfacing on tomatoes
The farmer noticed catfacing on several tomatoes after a cold spell in the spring.
Gardeners try to prevent catfacing by protecting plants from sudden temperature changes.
Severe catfacing can make fruit less appealing but does not always affect its taste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance of something
  • synonym: blossom end rot - a common disorder in tomatoes causing dark, sunken spots on the fruit
