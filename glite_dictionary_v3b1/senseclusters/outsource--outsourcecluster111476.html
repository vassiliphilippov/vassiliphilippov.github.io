outsource

verb
obtain goods or services externally
5.3 5.11/m
Pronunciation: / Ààa ätÀås…îrs / (AmE), / Ààa ätÀås…îÀês / (BrE)
to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier, especially in place of an internal source; to contract work to another company or individual

Examples

To outsource work
Many small businesses outsource payroll processing to specialized agencies.
The company decided to outsource its customer support to a firm in India.
They are considering outsourcing their IT functions to cut costs.

Synonyms

  • contract out
    4.2 0.29/m
    contract out means arranging for work to be done by an outside company, while outsource focuses on replacing internal staff.
  • subcontract
    5.9 0.58/m
    subcontract is hiring another company to do part of a contract, while outsource is choosing external suppliers instead of doing work internally.
  • farm out
    4.9 0.50/m
    farm out is an informal way to give tasks to others for payment, while outsource is a formal business decision to hire outside suppliers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insource - to use internal resources or personnel instead of contracting externally
