outsource

verb
obtain goods or services externally
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌsɔrs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌsɔːs / (BrE)
to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier, especially in place of an internal source; to contract work to another company or individual

Examples

To outsource work
Many small businesses outsource payroll processing to specialized agencies.
The company decided to outsource its customer support to a firm in India.
They are considering outsourcing their IT functions to cut costs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insource - to use internal resources or personnel instead of contracting externally
