Rejoicing of the Law

noun
Jewish festival marking end of Torah readings
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɔɪ.sɪŋ əv ðə lɔ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɔɪ.sɪŋ əv ðə lɔː / (BrE)
A Jewish holy day that marks the completion of the annual cycle of readings from the Torah, celebrated on the 22nd or 23rd of Tishri.

Examples

Rejoicing of the Law holiday
Children danced with flags during the Rejoicing of the Law celebrations at the synagogue.
Every year, the community gathers for Rejoicing of the Law to mark the Torah's completion.
The Rejoicing of the Law is a joyful occasion in the Jewish calendar.
