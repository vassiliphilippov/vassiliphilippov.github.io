redout

noun
vision loss from acceleration
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdaʊt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary loss or dimming of vision caused by excessive blood flow to the head, typically during high acceleration

Examples

A pilot redout
The pilot experienced a redout during the rapid descent.
High-speed maneuvers can sometimes cause a redout in fighter jet pilots.
After the sharp dive, she reported a brief redout before regaining normal vision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blackout - a temporary loss of vision or consciousness due to reduced blood flow to the brain
