sesquiterpene

noun
plant hydrocarbon
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛskwɪˈtɜːrpiːn / (AmE), / ˌsɛskwɪˈtɜːpiːn / (BrE)
a type of hydrocarbon made of three isoprene units, commonly found in essential oils and plants

Examples

Sesquiterpene compound
Lavender oil contains several sesquiterpene compounds that contribute to its scent.
Scientists study sesquiterpene molecules to understand their effects on human health.
Many plants produce sesquiterpene as part of their natural defense mechanisms.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: diterpene - a terpene made of four isoprene units
  • degree lower: monoterpene - a terpene made of two isoprene units
  • synonym: terpene - a large class of hydrocarbons produced by plants
