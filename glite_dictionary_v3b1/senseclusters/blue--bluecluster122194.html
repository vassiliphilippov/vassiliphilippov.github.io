blue

adjective
of noble or aristocratic descent
8.1 2.24/m
Pronunciation: / bluÀê / (AmE), / bluÀê / (BrE)
(of a person or family) belonging to or characteristic of the nobility; aristocratic.

Examples

Blue blood
Critics accused the government of favoring blue circles in high office.
His blue lineage was a source of pride in the small town.
They belonged to a blue family with a long history of wealth and power.

Synonyms

  • aristocratic
    7.2 3.38/m
    aristocratic describes people or manners of the upper class, while blue means belonging to a noble family by birth.
  • blue-blooded
    8.2 0.10/m
    blue-blooded says someone is born into the nobility and old family lines, while blue means noble or aristocratic.
  • patrician
    8.6 0.68/m
    patrician suggests polished, elite manners and background, while blue focuses on family rank and noble ancestry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocratic - of or relating to the nobility; characteristic of an aristocrat
  • antonym: plebeian - characteristic of commoners; not of the nobility
