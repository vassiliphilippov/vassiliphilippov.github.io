simp

verb
to flatter excessively
Pronunciation: / sɪmp / (AmE), / sɪmp / (BrE)
to act in an overly attentive or submissive way toward someone, often to gain their affection or approval, especially online

Examples

To simp for someone
Some people simp for celebrities by constantly commenting on their social media posts.
He was accused of being a simp after buying expensive gifts for someone he barely knew.
You shouldn't simp just to get someone's attention; be yourself instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fawn - to show exaggerated affection or flattery toward someone
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to someone
