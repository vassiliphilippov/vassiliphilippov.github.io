sure-footed

adjective
not likely to stumble; steady on one's feet
5.6 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʃʊr.fʊt.ɪd/ /; / /ˈʃɔːr.fʊt.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈʃɔː.fʊt.ɪd/ /; / /ˈʃʊə.fʊt.ɪd/ (BrE)
able to walk or move steadily and confidently, especially on difficult ground; not likely to stumble or fall.

Examples

Sure-footed climber
Thanks to her sure-footed steps, she crossed the icy path without slipping.
The goat was sure-footed as it climbed the rocky mountainside.
The horse is famous for being particularly sure-footed on rough trails.

  • synonym: nimble - able to move quickly and easily
