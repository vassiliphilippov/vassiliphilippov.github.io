stoop to

verb
to lower oneself to do something undignified
7.7 0.81/m
Pronunciation: / stuÀêp tu / (AmE), / stuÀêp t ä / (BrE)
to lower oneself to do something considered beneath one's dignity; to be willing to do something morally or socially inferior, especially out of necessity or desperation.

Examples

To stoop to lying
I can't believe you would stoop to lying just to win an argument.
It's sad to see such a talented person stoop to cheating.
She refused to stoop to his level and insult him back.

Synonyms

  • lower oneself
    6.5 0.14/m
    lower oneself means doing something that makes you lose respect, while stoop to often shows you do it out of need or desperation.
  • stoop
    8.1 0.70/m
    stoop is lowering yourself by acting against your standards, while stoop to suggests doing that action because you feel forced or desperate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condescend - to behave as if you are descending to a less dignified level to do something
  • synonym: deign - to do something considered beneath one's dignity
