mineralization

noun
conversion to nutrients
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which microorganisms convert organic compounds in soil into inorganic nutrients that plants can absorb

Examples

Soil mineralization
Soil mineralization is essential for making nutrients available to crops each growing season.
Researchers measure nitrogen mineralization rates to understand soil fertility in different environments.
Proper mineralization helps ensure that plants receive enough nutrients for healthy growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decomposition - the breakdown of organic matter by microorganisms
  • antonym: immobilization - the process where nutrients become unavailable to plants by being incorporated into microbial biomass
