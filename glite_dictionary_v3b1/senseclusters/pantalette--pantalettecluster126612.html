pantalette

noun
19th-century undergarment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpæntəˈlɛt / (AmE), / ˌpæntəˈlɛt / (BrE)
a long, frilled undergarment covering the legs, worn by girls and women in the 19th century

Examples

Long pantalettes with frills
In Victorian times, young girls often wore pantalettes under their dresses for modesty.
The museum displayed a pair of lace-trimmed pantalettes from the 1800s.
She read a novel describing how women carefully ironed their pantalettes each morning.
