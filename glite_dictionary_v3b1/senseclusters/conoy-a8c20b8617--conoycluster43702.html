Conoy

noun
a Native American people of historic Maryland
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɔɪ / (AmE), / kəˈnɔɪ / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian people formerly living in Maryland between Chesapeake Bay and the Potomac River, allied with the Nanticoke people

Examples

The Conoy people
Archaeologists discovered artifacts that belonged to the Conoy people along the Potomac River.
Some historians trace the migration patterns of the Conoy from Maryland into Pennsylvania.
The Conoy were known for their alliances with the Nanticoke in colonial times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nanticoke - a neighboring Algonquian people of Maryland and Delaware
