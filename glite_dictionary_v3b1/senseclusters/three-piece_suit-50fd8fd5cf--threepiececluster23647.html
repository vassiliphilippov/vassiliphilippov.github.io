three-piece suit

noun
business suit with jacket, waistcoat, and trousers
Pronunciation: / ˈθri pis ˌsut / (AmE), / ˈθriː piːs ˌsuːt / (BrE)
A business suit consisting of a matching jacket, waistcoat (vest), and trousers.

Examples

A classic three-piece suit
For the wedding, the groom chose a classic navy three-piece suit.
He looked very professional in his three-piece suit at the conference.
My grandfather's favorite outfit was always a well-pressed three-piece suit.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: two-piece suit - a suit consisting of a jacket and trousers but no waistcoat
