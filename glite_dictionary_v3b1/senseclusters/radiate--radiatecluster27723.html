radiate

verb
spread and diversify into new habitats (biology)
8.3 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.diː.eɪt/ /; / /ˈreɪ.dɪ.ət/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.diː.eɪt/ /; / /ˈreɪ.dɪ.ət/ (BrE)
(of organisms) to spread into new habitats and develop a variety of forms or characteristics, especially as a result of evolutionary diversification.

Examples

Species radiate into habitats
After the mass extinction event, mammals began to radiate into different ecological niches.
Certain bird species have radiated across the archipelago, evolving distinct traits on each island.
Over time, flowering plants radiated to adapt to a wide variety of environments.

  • synonym: diversify - to become more varied or different
