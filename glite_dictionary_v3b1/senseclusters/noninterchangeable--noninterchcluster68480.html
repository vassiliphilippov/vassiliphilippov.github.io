noninterchangeable

adjective
not able to be switched without changing meaning
5.2 6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɪntərˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈtʃeɪndʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
(of things or terms) not able to be switched with each other without affecting the meaning, function, or result.

Examples

The parts are noninterchangeable, so each must be fitted to its specific counterpart.
