crownwork

noun
type of fortification with bastions and flanking walls
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkraʊnwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnwɜːk / (BrE)
a type of fortification consisting of a full bastion with walls on either side ending in half-bastions, from which longer flank walls run back toward the main fortress, used to extend and strengthen defensive lines.

Examples

Stone crownwork walls
During the siege, soldiers took up positions along the crownwork to repel the advancing enemy forces.
The engineers designed a crownwork to protect the vulnerable northern approach to the fortress.
The remains of the old crownwork can still be seen near the entrance of the fortified town.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hornwork - a fortification similar to a crownwork but with demi-bastions instead of a full central bastion
