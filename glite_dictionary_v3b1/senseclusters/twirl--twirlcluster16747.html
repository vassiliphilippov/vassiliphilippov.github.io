twirl

verb
spin or turn with a twist
2.0 4.62/m
Pronunciation: / tw…úrl /; / tw…ùl / (AmE), / tw…úÀêl / (BrE)
to spin or turn (something or yourself) quickly, often in a light or repeated way

Examples

To twirl a ribbon
Autumn leaves twirl through the air before touching the ground.
She loves to twirl her hair around her finger when she's thinking.
The dancers twirled gracefully across the stage.

Synonyms

  • spin around
    2.1 3.16/m
    spin around means revolving quickly and repeatedly on one's axis, while twirl is a lighter, often playful turning motion.
  • spin
    1.0 14/m
    spin means to turn rapidly, often with machines or objects, while twirl is a lighter, playful personal turn.
  • whirl
    2.8 6.28/m
    whirl means moving quickly in circles, often forceful or sudden, while twirl is lighter and more playful.
  • whirl
    2.6 2.58/m
    whirl turns or moves rapidly with a twisting action, while twirl is gentler, repeated turning often done by a person.
  • swirl
    2.7 11/m
    swirl moves in a twisting, circular flow like water or smoke, while twirl is a quick, controlled turn by a person or object.
  • whirl around
    3.7 1.39/m
    whirl around moves rapidly and continuously through the air or space, while twirl is usually a small, controlled spin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spin - to turn around quickly
  • synonym: twist - to turn something, especially repeatedly, so that it forms a spiral shape
  • synonym: whirl - to move or cause to move rapidly around and around
