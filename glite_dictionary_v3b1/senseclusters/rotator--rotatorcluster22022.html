rotator

noun
muscle causing rotation
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊˌteɪtə / (BrE)
a part or muscle that causes or produces rotation around an axis

Examples

Shoulder rotator
The shoulder contains a rotator called the rotator cuff.
In anatomy class, we learned how each rotator helps move the arm in different directions.
An injury to a rotator muscle can make it difficult to lift your arm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexor - a muscle that bends a limb or body part
  • synonym: rotator cuff - a group of muscles and tendons that stabilize the shoulder
