subsoil

verb
to loosen deep soil
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌsɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌsɔɪl / (BrE)
to loosen or break up the soil beneath the surface layer without turning it over, usually to improve drainage or root growth

Examples

To subsoil a field
Farmers often subsoil their fields to help water reach deeper roots.
The agricultural team recommended subsoiling the land to break up compacted layers.
After years of heavy machinery use, the field needed to be subsoiled before planting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plow - to turn over the upper layer of soil with a plow
  • synonym: till - to prepare land for growing crops by turning and breaking up the soil
