consolidator

noun
loan combining service
6.2
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈsɑː.lɪ.deɪ.tər/ (AmE), /kən.ˈsɒ.lɪ.deɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a person, company, or tool that combines several things (such as loans, data, shipments, or services) into one to make them simpler or more efficient

Examples

Loan consolidator
After graduation, she contacted a consolidator to combine her multiple student loans into one payment.
Many people use a consolidator to manage their credit card debts more easily.
The consolidator offered a lower interest rate by merging several small loans into one.
The IT department installed a consolidator to combine reports from all regional offices.
A data consolidator helps businesses see all their information in one place.
Using a consolidator, the analyst merged customer feedback from several platforms.
We booked our flights through a consolidator to save money.
The shipping consolidator combined several small packages into one large shipment.
A data consolidator helps companies manage information from multiple sources efficiently.
