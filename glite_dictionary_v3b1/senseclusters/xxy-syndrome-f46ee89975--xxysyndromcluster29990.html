XXY-syndrome

noun
genetic condition with extra X chromosome in males
8.7
Pronunciation: /eks.eks.ˈwaɪ ˈsɪn.droʊm/ (AmE), /eks.eks.ˈwaɪ ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ (BrE)
a syndrome in males caused by the presence of an extra X chromosome (XXY), characterized by small testes, long legs, enlarged breasts, reduced sperm production, and sometimes developmental or cognitive issues

Examples

XXY-syndrome diagnosis
After genetic testing, he was diagnosed with XXY-syndrome.
Awareness of XXY-syndrome has led to better medical support for those affected.
The doctor explained that XXY-syndrome can lead to infertility in some men.
