overwash

noun
storm-deposited sediment
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.wɑːʃ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.wɔːʃ/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.wɒʃ/ (BrE)
water or material (such as sand, mud, or debris) carried inland by water flowing over a beach, dune, or barrier during storms or high tides

Examples

Storm overwash
The hurricane left a thick layer of overwash covering the coastal highway.
Scientists studied the overwash deposits to understand the impact of the recent storm.
Vegetation was buried under several inches of overwash after the tidal surge.
The hurricane caused significant overwash on the barrier island, damaging the dunes.
After the storm, the road was covered by overwash from the nearby beach.
Engineers studied the effects of overwash to improve coastal protection measures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debris - scattered pieces of waste or remains
  • synonym: flood - an overflow of water that submerges land
  • synonym: sediment - matter that settles to the bottom of a liquid
  • synonym: storm surge - a rise in sea level caused by a storm
