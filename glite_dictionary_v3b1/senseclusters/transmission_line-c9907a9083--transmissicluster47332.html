transmission line

noun
conductor for transmitting signals or power
1.0
Pronunciation: / trænzˈmɪʃən laɪn / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃən laɪn / (BrE)
a conductor or system of conductors designed to transmit electrical or optical signals or electric power from one location to another

Examples

High-voltage transmission line
A high-voltage transmission line runs through the valley, delivering electricity to the nearby city.
Modern telecommunications depend on fiber optic transmission lines for fast data transfer.
The engineer inspected the transmission line to locate the source of the signal loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: power line - a cable carrying electric power over distance
