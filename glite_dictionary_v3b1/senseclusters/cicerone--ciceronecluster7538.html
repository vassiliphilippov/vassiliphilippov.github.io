cicerone

noun
guide for sightseers
8.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪsəˈroʊni / (AmE), / ˌsɪsəˈrəʊni / (BrE)
a person who acts as a guide and shares expert knowledge, especially about history, culture, food, or drink

Examples

A cicerone guide
During their trip to Rome, a knowledgeable cicerone explained the secrets of the ancient city.
The museum cicerone led us through each gallery, sharing fascinating stories about the paintings.
We hired a cicerone to walk us around the historic ruins, making the visit much more informative.
Our cicerone explained the history of each wine we tasted during the vineyard tour.
The museum hired a knowledgeable cicerone to lead visitors through its new exhibition.
As a certified cicerone, she guided us through the city's best craft beer bars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: docent - a person who acts as a guide, especially in a museum or gallery
  • synonym: guide - a person who leads or directs others on a journey or tour
