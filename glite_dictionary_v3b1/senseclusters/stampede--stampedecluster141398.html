stampede

verb
cause to run in panic
Pronunciation: / stæmˈpid / (AmE), / stæmˈpiːd / (BrE)
to (cause to) run or move suddenly and quickly in a group, usually because of fear or excitement

Examples

To stampede the herd
A loud noise may stampede a herd of cattle across the field.
The fire alarm was enough to stampede the shoppers toward the exits.
The sudden movement in the crowd could stampede the children if not controlled.
At the first hint of a sale, shoppers stampede into the store to grab bargains.
Investors began to stampede out of the market as panic spread.
When the concert doors opened, fans stampeded towards the stage.
A clever marketing campaign stampeded fans into buying tickets within minutes.
Fears about shortages stampeded people into stocking up on groceries unnecessarily.
The rumor of a sale stampeded shoppers into crowding the store before it opened.

Related Terms

  • synonym: panic - to suddenly become very afraid and unable to think clearly
  • antonym: retreat - to move away quickly, often in fear or danger
  • synonym: rush - to move quickly and energetically
  • synonym: scatter - to move quickly in different directions
