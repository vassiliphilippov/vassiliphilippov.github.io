mood

noun
grammatical form of verbs expressing attitude
7.5 1.73/m
Pronunciation: / mud / (AmE), / muÀêd / (BrE)
(Grammar) a category of verb forms that expresses the attitude of the speaker toward the action or state, such as whether it is a statement, command, wish, or possibility.

Examples

Conditional mood
Different languages express the conditional mood in various ways.
In the sentence 'If I were rich, I would travel,' the verb 'were' is in the subjunctive mood.
The imperative mood is used to give commands or requests.

Synonyms

  • mode
    7.9 0.33/m
    mode is a technical grammatical term for how an action is viewed, while mood names verb forms showing the speaker's attitude.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tense - time expressed by verb inflection (past, present, future, etc.)
  • synonym: voice - grammatical category expressing the relation between the verb's action and its participants (active, passive, etc.)
