recess

noun
indented space or enclosure
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈriː.sɛs /; / ˈriːˌsɛs, rɪˈsɛs / (AmE), / ˈriː.ses /; / ˈriːsɛs, rɪˈsɛs / (BrE)
a hollow or indented space set back from a surface, such as in a wall, rock, or body of water

Examples

A wall recess
A hidden recess behind the painting served as a secret compartment.
The statue stands in a recess above the fireplace, beautifully lit by spotlights.
There was a small recess in the wall where the bookshelf was placed.
A small recess in the rock sheltered the wildflowers from the wind.
The sculptor placed the statue in a recess in the wall.
We found an ancient relic hidden in the recess of the cave.
At low tide, the sandy recess was exposed, forming a secluded beach.
Seals are often found resting in the recesses of hidden coastal waters.
The boat drifted quietly into a narrow recess between the rocky cliffs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bay - a broad inlet of the sea where the land curves inward
  • synonym: cavity - an empty space within a solid object
  • synonym: inlet - a small arm of the sea, a lake, or a river; entryway of water
  • synonym: niche - a shallow recess, especially in a wall, to display a statue or other ornament
