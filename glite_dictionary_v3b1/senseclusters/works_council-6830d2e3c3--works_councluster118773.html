works council

noun
employee representative committee in a company
3.1 4.9
Pronunciation: /wɜːrks ˈkaʊn.səl/ (AmE), /wɜːks ˈkaʊn.səl/ (BrE)
A group of employees elected to represent workers in discussions and negotiations with management about working conditions, pay, and grievances, especially in the UK and Europe.

Examples

Works council meeting
At the monthly works council meeting, issues about overtime pay were discussed.
German law requires many firms to have a works council representing employees.
The company established a works council to improve communication between staff and management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trade union - an organized association of workers formed to protect their rights and interests
