overbalance

verb
to lose balance
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbæləns / (BrE)
to lose balance and fall or nearly fall, often by leaning or tipping too far

Examples

To overbalance on a ladder
She started to overbalance on the ladder but managed to catch herself in time.
The child overbalanced while reaching for the toy and almost fell.
If you lean too far forward, you might overbalance and tumble off the chair.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - to make or become balanced and not likely to fall
  • synonym: topple - to fall or make something fall over, especially by losing balance
