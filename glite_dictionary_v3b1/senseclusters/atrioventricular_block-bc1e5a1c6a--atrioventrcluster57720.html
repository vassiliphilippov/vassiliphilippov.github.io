atrioventricular block

noun
heart condition; impaired electrical conduction
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.tri.oʊ.vɛnˈtrɪk.jə.lɚ blɒk / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.tri.əʊ.venˈtrɪk.jʊ.lə blɒk / (BrE)
A condition in which the electrical conduction between the atria and ventricles of the heart is impaired, sometimes causing sudden episodes of unconsciousness.

Examples

Atrioventricular block condition
Doctors monitored her heart for signs of atrioventricular block during her hospital stay.
Severe atrioventricular block may require the implantation of a pacemaker.
The patient was diagnosed with an atrioventricular block after experiencing multiple fainting spells.
