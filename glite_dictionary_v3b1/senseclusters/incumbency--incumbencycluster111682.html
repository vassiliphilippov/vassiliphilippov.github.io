incumbency

noun
period of holding office
6.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌmbənsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmbənsi / (BrE)
the period or term during which someone holds a particular office or position

Examples

A long incumbency
A long incumbency can sometimes lead to political stagnation.
During his incumbency as mayor, the city underwent significant changes.
The senator's incumbency lasted for three consecutive terms.
His incumbency as mayor lasted nearly a decade.
Political scandals plagued the senator's incumbency.
The school saw many improvements during her incumbency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retirement - the act of leaving one's job or office after a period of service
  • synonym: tenure - the period or conditions under which an office or position is held
  • synonym: term - a fixed or limited period for which an office or position is held
