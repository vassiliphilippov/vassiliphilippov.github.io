immunogenic

adjective
able to provoke an immune response
4.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.mjuː.nə.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ /; / /ɪ.mjʊ.nə.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (AmE), /ɪ.mjuː.nə.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ /; / /ɪ.mjʊ.nə.ˈdʒen.ɪk/ (BrE)
able to provoke an immune response in the body, especially by stimulating the production of antibodies.

Examples

Immunogenic response
Not all foreign substances are equally immunogenic; some fail to trigger any response at all.
Researchers are working to develop a less immunogenic version of the protein to reduce side effects.
The vaccine was found to be highly immunogenic, producing strong antibody levels in test subjects.
