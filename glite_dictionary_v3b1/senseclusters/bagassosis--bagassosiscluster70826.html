bagassosis

noun
lung inflammation caused by sugarcane dust
9.9
Pronunciation: / bəˌɡæˈsoʊsɪs / (AmE), / bəˌɡæsˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A type of lung inflammation (alveolitis) caused by inhaling dust from bagasse, the residue left after extracting juice from sugarcane.

Examples

Bagassosis lung disease
Doctors diagnosed her persistent cough as a symptom of bagassosis.
Proper ventilation in sugar mills can help prevent bagassosis among employees.
The factory workers were at risk of developing bagassosis due to constant exposure to sugarcane dust.
