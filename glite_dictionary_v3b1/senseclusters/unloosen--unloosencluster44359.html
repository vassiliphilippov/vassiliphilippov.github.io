unloosen

verb
release or set free from restraint
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈluːsən / (AmE), / ʌnˈluːsən / (BrE)
to make something less tight or to release it from restraint or strictness

Examples

To unloosen strict rules
Pressure from the public helped to unloosen the government's strict policies.
She carefully unloosened the knots holding the sail in place.
The guard decided to unloosen the prisoner's chains after the hearing.
Could you unloosen the knot for me?
He stopped to unloosen his tie after a long meeting.
It took a while to unloosen the bolts on the old bike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loosen - to make less tight or restrictive; to set free
  • antonym: tighten - to make or become tight or tighter
