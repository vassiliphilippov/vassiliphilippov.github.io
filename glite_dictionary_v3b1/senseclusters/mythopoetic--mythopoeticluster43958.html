mythopoetic

adjective
evoking mythic themes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪθoʊpoʊˈɛtɪk /; / ˌmɪθəpoʊˈɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɪθəʊpəʊˈɛtɪk / (BrE)
having qualities of myths or relating to the creation or imaginative use of myths

Examples

Mythopoetic style
The novel's mythopoetic style gives ordinary events a sense of ancient mystery.
Her paintings are filled with mythopoetic symbols that suggest universal human experiences.
The film's mythopoetic imagery transforms the landscape into a place of legend.
The poet's mythopoetic style draws on ancient legends to create new worlds.
Many fantasy novels have a strong mythopoetic element that shapes their stories.
Her paintings are celebrated for their mythopoetic imagery and symbolism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: factual - based on facts rather than imagination
  • synonym: legendary - remarkable enough to be famous or like a legend
  • synonym: mythical - relating to or resembling myth
  • synonym: mythological - relating to myths or mythology
  • antonym: realistic - representing things as they are in real life
