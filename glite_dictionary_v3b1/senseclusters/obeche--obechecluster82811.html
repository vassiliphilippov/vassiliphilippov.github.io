obeche

noun
West African tree with soft pale wood
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / o äÀàbiÀê.t Éi / (AmE), / …ôÀàbiÀê.t Éi / (BrE)
A large West African tree (Triplochiton scleroxylon) with broad, lobed leaves, small white flowers, and soft pale wood often used for plywood and veneers.

Examples

An obeche tree
Carpenters often choose obeche because it is easy to work and takes stain well.
The leaves of the obeche tree are large and distinctively lobed, making it easy to identify in the forest.
The lightweight wood of obeche is highly prized in the manufacture of plywood and furniture.
