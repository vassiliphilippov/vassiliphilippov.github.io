electrocoagulation

noun
electric tissue clumping
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊkoʊˌæɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊkəʊˌæɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a process that uses electric current to make particles or tissues clump together, often to stop bleeding or for water treatment

Examples

Electrocoagulation treatment
The surgeon used electrocoagulation to control bleeding during the operation.
Industrial wastewater treatment plants sometimes use electrocoagulation to remove contaminants from water.
Advances in electrocoagulation have improved the safety of many medical procedures.
The hospital uses electrocoagulation to control bleeding during certain surgical procedures.
Many wastewater treatment plants rely on electrocoagulation to remove contaminants from industrial water.
Advances in electrocoagulation have made it a safer option for delicate medical operations.

  • synonym: coagulation - the process of changing from a liquid to a thickened or solid state
