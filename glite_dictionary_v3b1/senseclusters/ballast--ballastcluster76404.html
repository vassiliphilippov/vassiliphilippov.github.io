ballast

noun
device for controlling current in lamps
5.7 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbæl.əst/ (AmE), /ˈbæl.əst/ (BrE)
an electrical device used to regulate current in lighting or other circuits

Examples

Lighting ballast
Electricians often check the ballast when troubleshooting lighting issues.
Modern LED fixtures do not require a ballast like fluorescent lights do.
The fluorescent light stopped working because its ballast failed.
A faulty ballast can cause flickering or overheating in lighting fixtures.
Fluorescent lamps require a ballast to function safely and efficiently.
The technician installed a new ballast to maintain the proper current in the circuit.

  • synonym: resistor - an electrical component that limits or regulates current in a circuit
