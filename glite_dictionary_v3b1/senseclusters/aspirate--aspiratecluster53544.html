aspirate

verb
remove by suction
7.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈæ.spə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈæ.spər.ət/ /; / /ˈæ.spɪr.ət/ /; / /ˈæ.spɪ.reɪt/ (AmE), /ˈæ.spə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈæ.spə.rət/ /; / /ˈæ.spɪr.ət/ (BrE)
to remove fluid or other material from the body or a container by suction, often using a syringe

Examples

To aspirate fluid
Doctors may aspirate pus from an abscess using a syringe.
Technicians routinely aspirate samples in the laboratory to prepare them for analysis.
The nurse had to aspirate fluid from the patient's lungs during the procedure.
The doctor aspirated the excess fluid from the patient's knee to relieve swelling.
During the procedure, a nurse carefully aspirated blood from the vein using a syringe.
If pus accumulates, it may be necessary to aspirate it to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extract - to remove something, especially by effort or force
  • antonym: inject - to force a liquid into something, especially the body
  • synonym: suction - the act or process of removing something by sucking it out
