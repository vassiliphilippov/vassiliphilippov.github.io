vasopressin

noun
neuropeptide affecting social behaviour
Pronunciation: / ccve6aso8ac8pr5bs6an / (AmE), / ccve6as59c8pr5bs6an / (BrE)
(in neuroscience) a neuropeptide produced in the hypothalamus that acts within the brain to influence social behaviour, bonding, aggression, and stress responses.

Examples

Brain vasopressin
Researchers found that elevated vasopressin levels were linked to increased aggression in male voles during mating.
Some clinicians measure vasopressin responses to stress to study connections between hormones and social bonding.
In trials, intranasal vasopressin was tested for its potential to improve emotional recognition in patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antidiuretic hormone - the same peptide often referred to in clinical contexts for its role in reducing urine output
