dismiss

verb
send someone away; allow to leave
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈmɪs / (BrE)
to send someone away or end their participation in a group or activity

Examples

Dismiss the class
After the meeting, the manager dismissed everyone from the room.
Once his testimony was finished, the judge dismissed the witness.
The teacher dismissed her students early due to the snowstorm.
After the scandal, the group chose to dismiss her from their meetings.
Many clubs will dismiss members who consistently break the rules.
When he learned about the betrayal, he decided to dismiss his friend from his life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - allow someone to join a group or company
  • synonym: drop - stop associating with someone
  • synonym: expel - to force someone to leave a place or organization
  • synonym: reject - refuse to accept or associate with someone
  • antonym: retain - to keep someone in a place or position
