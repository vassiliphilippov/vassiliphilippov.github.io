Lanka

proper noun
island in the Ramayana
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːŋkə / (AmE), / ˈlæŋkə / (BrE)
the island kingdom ruled by the demon king Ravana in the Hindu epic the Ramayana

Examples

Lanka in the Ramayana
According to the Ramayana, Ravana ruled Lanka with great power and wealth.
Rama and his army crossed the sea to reach Lanka and rescue Sita.
Many scholars debate the historical location of Lanka described in ancient texts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Ayodhya - the kingdom of Rama in the Ramayana
  • synonym: Sri Lanka - an island country in South Asia, often identified with the Lanka of the Ramayana
