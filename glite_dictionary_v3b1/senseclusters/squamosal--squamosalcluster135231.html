squamosal

noun
skull bone in vertebrates
9.9
Pronunciation: /skwə.ˈmoʊ.səl/ /; / /skweɪ.ˈmoʊ.səl/ (AmE), /skwə.ˈməʊ.səl/ /; / /skweɪ.ˈməʊ.səl/ (BrE)
a bone in the skull of vertebrates that forms part of the side of the head

Examples

Squamosal bone
The paleontologist identified the dinosaur species by examining the squamosal bone.
Damage to the squamosal can affect the shape of the skull in some animals.
In mammals, the squamosal connects with other bones to form the side of the head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parietal - a bone forming the central side and upper back part of the skull
  • synonym: temporal - a bone on the side of the skull near the ear
