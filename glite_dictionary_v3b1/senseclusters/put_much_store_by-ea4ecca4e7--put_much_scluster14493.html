put much store by

verb
consider important; have strong belief in
Pronunciation: / pʊt mʌtʃ stɔr baɪ / (AmE), / pʊt mʌtʃ stɔː baɪ / (BrE)
to consider something as important or valuable; to have a lot of belief or confidence in something.

Examples

To put much store by honesty
He put much store by honesty, always insisting on telling the truth.
Many scientists put much store by data and experimentation when forming conclusions.
She doesn't put much store by expensive gifts, preferring heartfelt gestures instead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discount - to regard something as unimportant or of little value
  • synonym: value - to consider something to be important or beneficial
