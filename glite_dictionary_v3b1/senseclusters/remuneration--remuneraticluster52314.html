remuneration

noun
money paid for work or services

Examples

Fair remuneration
Most employees expect fair remuneration for their contributions to the company.
The package includes not just salary, but also additional remuneration such as bonuses and benefits.
There was disagreement over the amount of remuneration due after the contract was completed.

The company offered a competitive remuneration package to attract the best candidates.

Synonyms

  • compensation
    3.6 20/m

  • emolument
    9.9 0.23/m
    emolument is a formal salary or fee often for an office, while remuneration is a general payment for work or services.
  • recompense
    7.7 0.71/m
    recompense is compensation usually for loss or effort, while remuneration normally means pay for employment or services rendered.
  • earnings
    2.3 33/m
    earnings are money received from work, business, or investment, while remuneration refers specifically to pay for employment or services.
  • payment
    1.0 3.16/m
    payment is the act of giving money in a particular transaction, while remuneration means money given for work or services.
  • payout
    3.4 3.37/m
    payout is a one-time sum paid as a reward, winnings, or return, while remuneration is regular compensation for work or services.

Related Terms
