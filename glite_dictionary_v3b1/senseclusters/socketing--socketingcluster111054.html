socketing

noun
making a socket
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑkətɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɒkɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making or forming a socket, especially by cutting or carving a recess to receive another part

Examples

Careful socketing of wood
The careful socketing of the wooden beams ensured a strong and stable frame.
Stoneworkers spent hours on the socketing required for the new monument's base.
Accurate socketing is essential when fitting metal rods into concrete supports.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carving - the act of shaping something by cutting
  • antonym: insertion - the act of putting one part into another
