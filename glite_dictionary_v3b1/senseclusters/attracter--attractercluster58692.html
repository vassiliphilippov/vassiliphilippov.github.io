attracter

noun
point a system evolves towards (physics)
4.7 8.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtræk.tər/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtræk.tə/ (BrE)
(physics) A point or set in a system's phase space toward which the system tends to evolve, regardless of its starting conditions.

Examples

A attracter point
In chaotic systems, a strange attracter governs the long-term behavior of trajectories.
Physicists study how a pendulum's motion is pulled towards a stable attracter.
Regardless of initial conditions, the weather system eventually settles into its characteristic attracter.
