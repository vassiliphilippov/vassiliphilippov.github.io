neuropathy

noun
nerve disorder or disease
Pronunciation: / nuˈrɑːpəθi / (AmE), / njʊəˈrɒpəθi / (BrE)
A disease or dysfunction of one or more peripheral nerves, typically causing numbness or weakness.

Examples

Peripheral neuropathy
Early treatment of neuropathy can help prevent long-term nerve damage.
Patients with peripheral neuropathy often experience tingling, weakness, or pain in their extremities.
The doctor diagnosed her with diabetic neuropathy after she reported persistent numbness in her feet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neurologic - relating to the nervous system
  • synonym: neuropathy - disease or dysfunction of the nervous system in general
