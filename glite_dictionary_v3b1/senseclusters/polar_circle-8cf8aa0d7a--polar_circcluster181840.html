polar circle

noun
line of latitude near the poles
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊlər ˈsɜrkəl / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlə ˈsɜːkl / (BrE)
Either of the two lines of latitude around the Earth at approximately 66.5° north (the Arctic Circle) or south (the Antarctic Circle) of the equator, marking the boundaries within which, for at least one day each year, the sun does not set or rise.

Examples

The polar circle line
Few plants survive within the harsh climate found inside the polar circle.
North of the polar circle, you can experience the midnight sun in summer.
The expedition crossed the polar circle during its journey toward the Arctic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Antarctic Circle - line of latitude at 66.5° south
  • synonym: Arctic Circle - line of latitude at 66.5° north
