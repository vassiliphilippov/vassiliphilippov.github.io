scarf

verb
join pieces with a scarf joint
9.1
Pronunciation: / skɑrf / (AmE), / skɑːf / (BrE)
to join two pieces of wood or metal together with a scarf joint, especially so as to make them appear as one continuous piece

Examples

To scarf two planks
Shipbuilders often scarf planks to ensure a smoother hull surface.
The carpenter skillfully scarfed the two beams together to form a longer support.
To repair the damaged section, they scarfed new timber onto the existing framework.
