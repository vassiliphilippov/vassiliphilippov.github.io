chivalry

noun
medieval code of knightly conduct
4.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈʃɪ.vəl.riː/ /; / /ˈʃɪ.vəl.ri/ (AmE), /ˈʃɪ.vəl.ri/ /; / /ˈʃɪ.vəl.riː/ (BrE)
The medieval system of values and conduct associated with knighthood, such as honor, bravery, and courteousness, especially toward women.

Examples

Medieval chivalry
Many legends praise the chivalry and bravery of medieval heroes such as King Arthur's knights.
The code of chivalry demanded that knights defend the weak and act honorably in battle.
The young squire aspired to the ideals of chivalry promoted by the knights of the realm.

  • antonym: cowardice - lack of bravery
  • synonym: knighthood - the rank or character of a knight
