switch

noun
railroad track device for changing routes
3.6 5.6
Pronunciation: /swɪtʃ/ (AmE), /swɪtʃ/ (BrE)
a device on a railway track with movable rails used to guide trains from one track to another or to store rolling stock

Examples

Railway switch
If the switch is not set correctly, the train could take the wrong route.
Maintenance crews inspected the switch after reports of a malfunction.
The engineer operated the switch to divert the train onto the side track.

Related Terms

  • synonym: points - movable sections of track for directing trains (chiefly British English)
