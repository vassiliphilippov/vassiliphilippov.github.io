prickly

adjective
covered with sharp points or thorns
3.3 2.15/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆprÉªkli / (AmE), / ËˆprÉªkli / (BrE)
covered with or having sharp points such as thorns, spines, or barbs

Examples

Prickly bush
He avoided walking through the prickly bush to prevent scratching his legs.
Hedgehogs are known for their prickly spines, which protect them from predators.
The cactus has a prickly surface that can be painful to touch.

Synonyms

  • spiny
    3.9 0.32/m
    spiny describes plants or animals with sharp spines used for defense, while prickly describes things with many small sharp points.
  • thorny
    4.2 0.69/m
    thorny describes plants with obvious thorns on stems or branches, while prickly covers many small sharp points across a surface.
  • barbed
    4.4 0.26/m
    barbed has hooked sharp points like wire or hooks, while prickly refers to many simple sharp points such as spines.
  • bristly
    4.6 0.32/m
    bristly means covered with short stiff hairs or bristles that may feel rough, while prickly means actually sharp to touch.
  • bristled
    4.5 0.01/m
    bristled describes something standing with stiff hairs or quills, while prickly emphasizes sharp points that can hurt when touched.
