cataloger

noun
librarian who organizes publications
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəlɔɡər /; / ˈkæt.əˌlɔɡ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkatəlɒɡə /; / ˈkat.əl.ɒɡ.ə / (BrE)
a person, especially a librarian, who classifies and organizes publications or items according to a system of categories or a formal classification system

Examples

Library cataloger
Each cataloger specializes in a different subject area within the library.
She worked as a cataloger, carefully assigning call numbers to every book.
The cataloger updated the library's database to reflect the new acquisitions.
As a junior cataloguer, she learned to assign subject headings accurately.
Every library needs a skilled cataloguer to ensure easy access to resources.
The cataloguer spent hours organizing books into the new digital system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cataloguer - alternate spelling, especially British English, for one who catalogs in a library
  • synonym: librarian - a person in charge of or assisting in a library
