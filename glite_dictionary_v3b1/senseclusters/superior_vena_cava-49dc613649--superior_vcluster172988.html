superior vena cava

noun
large vein returning blood to heart (upper body)
4.5
Pronunciation: / suːˈpɪriər ˌveɪnə ˈkeɪvə / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə ˌveɪnə ˈkɑːvə / (BrE)
a large vein carrying deoxygenated blood from the upper body to the right atrium of the heart

Examples

The superior vena cava vein
A blood clot in the superior vena cava can cause swelling in the face and arms.
Doctors examined the superior vena cava for any signs of blockage.
The superior vena cava empties directly into the right atrium of the heart.
