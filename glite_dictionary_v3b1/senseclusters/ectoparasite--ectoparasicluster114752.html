ectoparasite

noun
external parasitic organism (e.g. flea)
7.8 6.9
Pronunciation: /ek.tə.ˈpæ.rə.saɪt/ /; / /ek.toʊ.ˈpæ.rə.saɪt/ (AmE), /ek.təʊ.ˈpæ.rə.saɪt/ (BrE)
an organism, such as a flea or a tick, that lives on the outside of another living thing (the host) and feeds off it.

Examples

A ectoparasite on animals
The infestation of ectoparasites caused irritation and disease in the cattle herd.
Ticks are a common ectoparasite found on dogs and other animals.
Unlike endoparasites, an ectoparasite lives and feeds on the surface of its host.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endoparasite - parasite that lives inside the body of the host
