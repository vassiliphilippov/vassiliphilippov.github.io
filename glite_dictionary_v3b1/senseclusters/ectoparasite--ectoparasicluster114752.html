an organism, such as a flea or a tick, that lives on the outside of another living thing (the host) and feeds off it.

Examples

A ectoparasite on animals
The infestation of ectoparasites caused irritation and disease in the cattle herd.
Ticks are a common ectoparasite found on dogs and other animals.
Unlike endoparasites, an ectoparasite lives and feeds on the surface of its host.

Synonyms

  • ectozoon
    9.9 0.00/m
    ectozoon is an external parasite that lives on an animal's body, while ectoparasite names organisms living outside and feeding on a host.
  • ectozoan
    9.9 0.00/m
    ectozoan lives on a host's skin and acts as a parasite, while ectoparasite is the general term for such outside feeders.
  • epizoan
    9.9 0.00/m

  • epizoon
    9.9 0.00/m
    epizoon is an external parasitic organism often named with examples like flea or louse, while ectoparasite stresses outside living and feeding.
