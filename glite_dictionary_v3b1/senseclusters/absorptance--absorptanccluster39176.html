absorptance

noun
fraction of absorbed radiant energy
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /ə.b.ˈzɑːr.p.təns/ /; / /ə.b.ˈzɔːr.p.təns/ (AmE), /ə.b.ˈzɔːp.təns/ /; / /æb.ˈzɔːp.təns/ (BrE)
the fraction of incident radiant energy that is absorbed by a material, as opposed to being transmitted or reflected

Examples

By increasing surface roughness, the absorptance of the coating was significantly improved.
Scientists measured the absorptance to assess the effectiveness of the insulation layer.
The absorptance of the material determines how much solar energy is converted into heat.
