inculturation

noun
adoption of culture
9.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kʌl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kəl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.kʌl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kəl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of learning and adopting the cultural traits, values, and behaviors of a group or society

Examples

Cultural inculturation
Through inculturation, children learn the customs and traditions of their community.
Religious leaders often encourage inculturation to help new members feel accepted.
The process of inculturation can take years, especially when moving to a new country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acculturation - the process of adopting the cultural traits of another group
  • synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to another group, especially by adopting its culture
