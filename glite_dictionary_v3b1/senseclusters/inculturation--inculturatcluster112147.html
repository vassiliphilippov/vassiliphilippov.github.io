Examples

Cultural inculturation
Through inculturation, children learn the customs and traditions of their community.
Religious leaders often encourage inculturation to help new members feel accepted.
The process of inculturation can take years, especially when moving to a new country.

Synonyms

  • enculturation
    9.3 0.23/m
    enculturation is a gradual learning of a culture's customs and values, often from childhood, while inculturation is the general process of adopting cultural traits.
  • acculturation
    8.2 2.42/m
    acculturation is adapting to another group's culture through contact and change, while inculturation is learning and adopting a group's cultural traits.

Related Terms
