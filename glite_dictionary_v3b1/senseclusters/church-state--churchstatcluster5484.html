church-state

noun
state ruled by religious authority
5.3
Pronunciation: / tʃɜrtʃ steɪt / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ steɪt / (BrE)
A state governed by religious authorities, where the government is formally linked with the religion.

Examples

A church-state government
In a church-state, laws are typically based on religious texts.
Iran is often cited as a modern example of a church-state.
The early Puritan colonies in America were governed as church-states.

Related Terms

  • synonym: theocracy - a system of government in which priests rule in the name of God or a god
