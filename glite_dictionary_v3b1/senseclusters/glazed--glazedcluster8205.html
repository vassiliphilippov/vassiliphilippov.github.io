glazed

adjective
(of eyes) dull, lacking liveliness
5.5 4.7
Pronunciation: /gleɪzd/ (AmE), /gleɪzd/ (BrE)
(of eyes) showing no emotion or liveliness, often appearing dull or unfocused.

Examples

Glazed eyes
After hours of studying, his expression became glazed and unfocused.
During the dull lecture, many students sat with glazed eyes, barely listening.
Her glazed eyes stared blankly at the wall, lost in thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glassy - (of eyes) having a shiny, fixed appearance, lacking expression
  • antonym: lively - (of eyes) full of life and energy
