phonograph

noun
machine for playing sound records
6.9 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊ.nəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.nə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A machine for reproducing sound from a rotating disc or cylinder record, using a stylus that picks up vibrations and amplifies them acoustically or electronically.

Examples

Old phonograph machine
He placed an old record on the phonograph and listened to the music with nostalgia.
The museum displayed a restored phonograph from the early 1900s.
Thomas Edison is credited with inventing the original phonograph in 1877.
