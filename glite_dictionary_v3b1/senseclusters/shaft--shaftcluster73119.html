shaft

verb
treat unfairly, cheat, or deceive
Pronunciation: / ʃæft / (AmE), / ʃɑːft / (BrE)
to treat someone unfairly or cheat them

Examples

To shaft a friend
He warned his friends not to trust that dealer, who had shafted people before.
I really felt like I'd been shafted when they gave the promotion to someone else.
The company shafted its employees by cutting their benefits without warning.
He felt the company was shafting him by not paying his bonus.
Many customers complained about being shafted by hidden fees in the contract.
She warned her friend not to let anyone shaft her in the business deal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • antonym: reward - to give someone something they deserve for their actions
  • synonym: swindle - to use deception to deprive someone of money or possessions
  • antonym: treat fairly - to act with justice or equity toward someone
