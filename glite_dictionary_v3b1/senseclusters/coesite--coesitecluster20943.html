coesite

noun
dense silica mineral
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a rare, dense mineral form of silica that forms under very high pressure, often found at meteorite impact sites

Examples

Rare coesite mineral
Geologists identified coesite in the rock sample, indicating it had experienced extreme pressure.
The presence of coesite suggests the area was once struck by a meteorite.
Researchers study coesite to learn about the conditions deep within the Earth's crust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quartz - a common crystalline form of silica
  • synonym: stishovite - a very dense, high-pressure form of silica
