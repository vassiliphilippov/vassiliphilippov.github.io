skyward

adjective, adverb
toward the sky
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪwərd /; / ˈskaɪwɚd / (AmE), / ˈskaɪwəd / (BrE)
toward the sky or moving upward (as an adverb or adjective)

Examples

Skyward movement
She pointed her telescope skyward to watch the stars.
Smoke rose skyward from the burning building.
The birds flew skyward as the dog ran toward them.
She tilted her head skyward, watching the clouds drift by.
The birds soared skyward as dawn broke over the forest.
The rocket shot skyward with a brilliant trail of fire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downward - directed toward a lower place or ground
  • antonym: earthward - toward the earth; in the direction of the earth
