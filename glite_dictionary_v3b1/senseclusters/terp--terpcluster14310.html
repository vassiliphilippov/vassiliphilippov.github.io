terp

noun
artificial flood mound
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɜrp / (AmE), / tɜːp / (BrE)
a man-made mound or hill built in low-lying areas to provide safe ground above flood levels

Examples

Village terp
The ancient village was built on a terp to stay safe during floods.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts buried deep within the terp near the coast.
Farmers relied on the terp to protect their homes from rising water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dyke - a wall or embankment built to prevent flooding
  • synonym: levee - a raised bank built to control the flow of water
