fission

verb
to split into parts
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈfɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
to split or divide into two or more parts, especially in a scientific, nuclear, or biological context

Examples

To fission into parts
Under certain conditions, a single cell can fission to produce two identical daughter cells.

Some bacteria fission rapidly, allowing their populations to grow quickly.
In nuclear reactors, uranium atoms fission to release large amounts of energy.
Some single-celled organisms fission to reproduce and increase their population.
