astronomy satellite

noun
satellite for astronomical observation
Pronunciation: / əˈstrɑːnəmi ˈsætəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / əˈstrɒnəmi ˈsætəlaɪt / (BrE)
an artificial satellite equipped with a telescope for observing astronomical phenomena, especially infrared radiation, from space

Examples

Astronomy satellite telescope
Plans for the next astronomy satellite include advanced instruments to study black holes more closely.
Scientists launched an astronomy satellite to gather data on distant galaxies using infrared sensors.
The Hubble Space Telescope is a famous example of an astronomy satellite that transformed our understanding of the universe.
