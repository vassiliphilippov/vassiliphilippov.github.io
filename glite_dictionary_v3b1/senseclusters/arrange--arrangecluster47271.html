arrange

verb
adapt a work for performance
2.3
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to adapt a piece of music or other work so that it can be performed in a different way or by different instruments or voices

Examples

To arrange a song
He will arrange the classic poem as a choral piece.
She was asked to arrange the play for a modern audience.
The composer decided to arrange the folk song for a full orchestra.

  • synonym: adapt - to change something for a new use or purpose
