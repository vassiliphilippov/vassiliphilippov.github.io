anencephalous

adjective
lacking a brain, congenital condition
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ənˈsɛf.ə.ləs / (AmE), / ˌan.ɛnˈsɛf.ə.ləs / (BrE)
Having a partial or complete absence of the brain, often referring to a congenital condition.

Examples

Anencephalous baby
Doctors explained the anencephalous condition to the concerned parents.
Embryonic development can sometimes result in an anencephalous fetus due to neural tube defects.
The anencephalous infant was diagnosed shortly after birth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anencephaly - a congenital condition marked by absence of a major portion of the brain, skull, and scalp
