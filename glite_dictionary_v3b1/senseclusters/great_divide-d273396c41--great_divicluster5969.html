Great Divide

noun
main continental divide in North America
6.8
Pronunciation: /greɪt dɪ.ˈvaɪd/ (AmE), /greɪt dɪ.ˈvaɪd/ (BrE)
the principal, and chiefly Rocky Mountain, section of the continental divide in North America, separating waters flowing to the Pacific Ocean from those flowing to the Atlantic and Arctic oceans

Examples

The Great Divide mountains
Many rivers originate on the Great Divide and flow in opposite directions across the continent.
Standing atop the Great Divide, you can see water draining toward both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
The hikers crossed the Great Divide during their trek through the Rocky Mountains.
