consilient

adjective
agreeing evidence
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɪliənt / (AmE), / kənˈsɪliənt / (BrE)
showing agreement or consistency among different sources of evidence or information

Examples

Consilient results
The research team found consilient results across several independent experiments.
Her argument was strong because it was based on consilient data from multiple disciplines.
When evidence is consilient, it increases our confidence in the theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coherent - logically connected and consistent
  • antonym: contradictory - inconsistent or in conflict with other information
