reframe

verb
to adjust camera view
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfreɪm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfreɪm / (BrE)
to change the visual composition or boundaries of a shot or image, often by moving the camera or adjusting the view.

Examples

To reframe a photo
The photographer decided to reframe the picture to include more of the background scenery.
After the actor moved, the camera operator had to quickly reframe the shot.
Directors often reframe scenes to create a different mood or focus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjust - to change something slightly to improve it or make it fit
  • synonym: crop - to cut or trim the edges of an image
