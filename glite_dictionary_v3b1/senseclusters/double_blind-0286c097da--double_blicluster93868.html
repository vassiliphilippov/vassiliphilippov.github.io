double blind

noun
a bias-reducing experimental procedure
5.1 1/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊŒbÉ™l blaÉªnd / (AmE), / ËˆdÊŒbÉ™l blaÉªnd / (BrE)
A test or experiment in which neither the participants nor the experimenters know who is receiving a particular treatment, used to prevent bias.

Examples

A double blind study
A double blind study reduces the influence of both experimenter and participant expectations.
Researchers used a double blind methodology to test the efficacy of the new drug.
The clinical trial was conducted as a double blind to ensure unbiased results.

Synonyms

  • double-blind experiment
    5.1 0.01/m
    double-blind experiment names a specific research test where neither side knows who gets treatment, while double blind is the general term.
  • double-blind study
    4.2 0.2/m
    double-blind study reports a research project where neither participants nor experimenters know treatments, while double blind names that blinded setup.
  • double-blind procedure
    5.4 0.00/m
    double-blind procedure describes the method used to hide treatment assignments, while double blind is the broader label for that method.
