down

adjective
(baseball) put out in an inning; number of outs
7.6 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / daÊŠn / (AmE), / daÊŠn / (BrE)
(of a player or team, in baseball) having been put out; used to indicate the number of outs recorded in the inning.

Examples

Two down in baseball
After the impressive double play, there were now two down in the top of the inning.
By the time Smith came to bat, there were already three down and the inning was over.
With one down and runners on first and second, the pitcher felt the pressure mounting.
