jump on the bandwagon

verb
join a popular trend or activity
8.8
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp ɑn ðə ˈbændˌwæɡən / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ɒn ðə ˈbændˌwæɡən / (BrE)
to join others in doing something that is currently popular or fashionable, especially in order to gain an advantage

Examples

To jump on the bandwagon quickly
After the product became a bestseller, many companies tried to jump on the bandwagon and release similar items.
It's easy to jump on the bandwagon when a new trend is sweeping through social media.
She resisted the urge to jump on the bandwagon just because all her friends were trying the latest diet.
