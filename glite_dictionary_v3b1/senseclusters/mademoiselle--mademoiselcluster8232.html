mademoiselle

noun
French title for young woman
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmædəmwɑˈzɛl /; / ˌmædəmwɑːˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˌmadəmwɑːˈzɛl / (BrE)
a French word used to address or refer to a young or unmarried woman (similar to 'Miss' in English)

Examples

Bonjour, mademoiselle
The waiter greeted her with a polite 'Bonjour, mademoiselle'.
In the play, the character was always addressed as mademoiselle by the other guests.
He handed the invitation to mademoiselle with a charming smile.
The teacher addressed the new student as mademoiselle during the French lesson.
At the restaurant, the waiter politely called her mademoiselle when taking her order.
In old letters, you might see women referred to as mademoiselle instead of 'Miss'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: madame - a French title for a married woman
  • synonym: miss - a title for an unmarried woman
