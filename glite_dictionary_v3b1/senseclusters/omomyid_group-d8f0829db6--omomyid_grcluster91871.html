omomyid group

noun
extinct group of small primates
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.mə.mɪd gruːp/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.mə.mɪd gruːp/ (BrE)
An extinct group of tiny, nocturnal primates that lived 30 to 50 million years ago, believed to have fed on fruit and insects. They were abundant in North America and Europe and are thought to be related to tarsiers and possibly the ancestors or relatives of anthropoids.

Examples

Many researchers believe the omomyid group gave rise to modern tarsiers.
The omomyid group was abundant in North America during the Eocene epoch.
