sound off

verb
complain or voice opinions loudly
Pronunciation: / saʊnd ɔf / (AmE), / saʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
to express your opinions or complaints loudly and openly, often forcefully

Examples

To sound off angrily
He tends to sound off whenever something doesn't go his way.
People were sounding off on social media about the sudden change in policy.
The workers sounded off about unsafe conditions in the factory meeting.
During the meeting, she didn't hesitate to sound off about the proposed changes.
He tends to sound off whenever someone mentions politics.
Several employees sounded off about their frustrations with the new schedule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • antonym: keep quiet - to not express one's opinion; to remain silent
  • synonym: speak up - to speak more loudly or boldly, especially to express an opinion
