haul

noun
an act of pulling or dragging
2.5 1.30/m
Pronunciation: / h…îl / (AmE), / h…îÀêl / (BrE)
an act of pulling or dragging something, especially with effort or force

Examples

A long haul
He managed the haul of the heavy box without any help.
The haul up the steep mountain took nearly an hour.
Their journey required a difficult haul through thick mud.

Synonyms

  • draw
    2.8 0.67/m
    draw is pulling something, often by steady force or animals, while haul is moving heavier loads over distance with effort.
  • drag
    2.3 1.87/m
    drag is pulling something along a surface with resistance, while haul is moving large or heavy items with steady effort.
  • draft
    6.1 0.49/m
    draft is moving a load using animals or machines, while haul is general pulling done by people or vehicles with effort.
