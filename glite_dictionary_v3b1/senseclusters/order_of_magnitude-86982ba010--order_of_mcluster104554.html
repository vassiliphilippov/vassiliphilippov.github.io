order of magnitude

noun
difference in scale, typically by power of ten
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər əv ˈmæɡnɪˌtud /; / ˈɔrdɚ əv ˈmæɡnɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əv ˈmæɡnɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a level or class used to compare size or quantity, usually measured in steps of ten times bigger or smaller

Examples

An order of magnitude difference
Scientists found energy levels differing by several orders of magnitude.
The accuracy of this measurement is within one order of magnitude of the true value.
The budget for the new project is an order of magnitude higher than last year’s.
Our budget is on the same order of magnitude as last year's, so spending limits remain similar.
Switching to LED bulbs reduced our electricity usage by an order of magnitude.
The energy released by a supernova is several orders of magnitude greater than that of our sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scale - a graduated range of values forming a standard system for measuring or grading something
