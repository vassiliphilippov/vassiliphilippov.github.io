actual damages

noun
compensation for proven losses (law)
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tʃu.əl ˈdæ.mɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈæk.tʃʊ.əl ˈdæ.mɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
compensation awarded for losses that can be clearly proven to have occurred, as opposed to punitive or speculative damages, especially in legal contexts.

Examples

Actual damages awarded
In this lawsuit, the company must pay actual damages for the harm it directly caused.
The court distinguishes between actual damages and punitive damages in its verdict.
The judge awarded actual damages to compensate the plaintiff for medical expenses and lost wages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensatory damages - damages awarded to compensate for loss or injury
  • antonym: punitive damages - damages awarded as a punishment beyond compensatory damages
