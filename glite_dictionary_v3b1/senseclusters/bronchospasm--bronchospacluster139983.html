bronchospasm

noun
spasm of the bronchi causing breathing difficulty
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑŋ.kəˌspæz.əm / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋ.kəˌspaz.əm / (BrE)
a sudden narrowing or contraction of the muscles in the walls of the bronchi, making breathing difficult; commonly occurs in asthma and bronchitis

Examples

Severe bronchospasm attack
Doctors monitored her closely due to recurrent episodes of bronchospasm following surgery.
Inhalers are often used to relieve bronchospasm in people with asthma.
The patient experienced a severe bronchospasm during the allergy attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bronchoconstriction - narrowing of the airways in the lungs due to tightening of surrounding muscles
  • antonym: bronchodilation - widening of the bronchi and bronchioles
