Baconian

noun
believer in Bacon authorship
9.9
Pronunciation: / beɪˈkoʊniən / (AmE), / beɪˈkəʊniən / (BrE)
a person who believes that Francis Bacon was the true author of some or all works attributed to Shakespeare

Examples

Baconian theory
As a dedicated Baconian, he spent years researching evidence to support his theory.
The debate between Baconians and Stratfordians continues among literary scholars.
She identified herself as a Baconian during the discussion about Shakespeare's authorship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Stratfordian - a person who believes William Shakespeare of Stratford-upon-Avon wrote the works attributed to him
