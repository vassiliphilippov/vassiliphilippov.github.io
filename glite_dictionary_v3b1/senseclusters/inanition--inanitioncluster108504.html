inanition

noun
exhaustion from lack of food
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
exhaustion or weakness caused by lack of food, nourishment, or energy; a state of being empty or deprived of vitality

Examples

Severe inanition symptoms
After days lost at sea, the sailors were suffering from inanition and could barely move.
Refugees arriving at the camp showed symptoms of inanition after weeks without adequate food.
The doctor recognized the signs of inanition in the severely malnourished child.
After days without food, his body was weakened by inanition.
Her creative spirit was drained to the point of inanition after months of stress.
The patient showed signs of inanition due to prolonged illness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: starvation - suffering or death caused by lack of food
  • antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and active; energy
