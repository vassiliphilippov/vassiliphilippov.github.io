sleep

noun
device power-saving state
4.7 1.75/m
Pronunciation: / sliÀêp / (AmE), / sliÀêp / (BrE)
a low-power operating state of an electronic device in which most functions are suspended to conserve energy while allowing quick resumption of full operation.

Examples

Device sleep mode
The laptop entered sleep after only five minutes of inactivity to save battery power.
When in sleep, the tablet uses minimal energy but resumes instantly when you press a button.
Adjust your settings so the computer goes into sleep automatically when idle for long.

Synonyms

  • deep sleep
    3.7 0.01/m
    deep sleep is a lower-power mode that shuts most functions down to save energy, while sleep allows a faster return to full operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hibernate - a power-saving state that saves active memory to disk and largely powers down the device
  • antonym: shutdown - to turn a device completely off so it is not running or using power
  • synonym: standby - a low-power state in which a device is ready to resume operation quickly
