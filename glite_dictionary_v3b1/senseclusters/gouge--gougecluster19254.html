gouge

verb
obtain by coercion or intimidation
8.3 0.53/m
Pronunciation: / É¡aÊŠdÊ’ / (AmE), / É¡aÊŠdÊ’ / (BrE)
to obtain money or something else from someone by using force, threats, or unfair pressure

Examples

To gouge money
She was gouged out of thousands of dollars by a fraudulent contractor.
Some loan sharks gouge desperate borrowers by threatening them if they don't pay.
The corrupt official tried to gouge extra money from business owners for 'protection'.

Synonyms

  • extort
    8.1 1.71/m
    extort takes money by threats or force from someone, while gouge often means unfairly taking more money under pressure.
  • rack
    9.4 2.47/m
    rack gets money by threats or intimidation, while gouge usually means taking money through unfair pressure or force.
  • wring
    7.4 2.08/m
    wring forces money or promises from someone by pressure, while gouge often means taking money in an unfair, harsh way.
  • squeeze
    4.8 3.75/m
    squeeze uses pressure or force to make someone give something, while gouge focuses on unfairly taking money or goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extort - to obtain something by force or threats
  • synonym: squeeze - to obtain something by persistent pressure or threats
