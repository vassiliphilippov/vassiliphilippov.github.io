espionage network

noun
cyber infrastructure for spying
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈe.spiː.ə.nɑːdʒ ˈnet.wɜːrk/ /; / /ˈe.spɪ.ə.nɑːʒ ˈnet.wɜːrk/ (AmE), /ˈe.spɪ.ə.nɑːʒ ˈnet.wɜːk/ /; / /ˈe.spiː.ə.nɑːʒ ˈnet.wɜːk/ (BrE)
a coordinated cyber infrastructure — for example, compromised devices, servers, and operator accounts — used to covertly collect intelligence or exfiltrate data.

Examples

A cyber espionage network
Investigators traced the cyber espionage network to a state-backed hacking group.
Security researchers found that the espionage network used thousands of compromised routers to steal data.
The report described an espionage network combining human agents and automated malware-controlled servers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyber espionage - the use of computer systems and networks to conduct covert intelligence collection or attacks
