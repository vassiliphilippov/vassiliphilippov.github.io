hypostatically

adverb
as a concrete entity
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpəˈstætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈstætɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that treats an abstract quality or concept as if it were a real, concrete entity.

Examples

Hypostatically personified ideas
The poet wrote hypostatically, giving emotions like fear and joy their own voices.
In ancient myths, natural forces are often described hypostatically as gods or spirits.
The philosopher argued hypostatically about justice, as if it were a living being.

Related Terms

  • synonym: personify - to represent an abstract quality as a person
  • synonym: reify - to treat something abstract as a real thing
