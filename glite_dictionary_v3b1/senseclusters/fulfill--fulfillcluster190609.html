fulfill

verb
meet requirements or expectations
Pronunciation: / fʊlˈfɪl / (AmE), / fʊlˈfɪl / (BrE)
to do or provide what is required, expected, or promised

Examples

To fulfill a promise
His performance failed to fulfill our high expectations.
She worked hard to fulfill the requirements for her degree.
The company did not fulfill its contractual obligations to the client.
I don't think this proposal will fulfill all the requirements set by the committee.
She hopes her work will fulfill the company's expectations for this project.
The new facility was built to fulfill the growing needs of the community.
After months of planning, he was finally able to fulfill his duty to the team.
She was asked to fulfill the requirements set by the board.
The company failed to fulfill its promise to deliver the product on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accomplish - to successfully complete something
  • antonym: break - to fail to fulfill or satisfy (a promise, condition, etc.)
  • synonym: carry out - to perform or complete a task or order
  • antonym: disappoint - to fail to meet expectations or hopes
  • synonym: execute - to carry out or implement something such as a plan or order
  • synonym: meet - to achieve or fulfill a requirement or condition
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to do something required
  • synonym: satisfy - meet the requirements or needs of
  • antonym: violate - fail to meet or break a requirement or promise
