palindromic

adjective
same forwards and backwards
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpælɪnˈdroʊmɪk / (AmE), / ˌpælɪnˈdrəʊmɪk /; / ˌpælɪnˈdrɒmɪk / (BrE)
reading the same forwards and backwards (used for words, numbers, or sequences such as DNA/RNA)

Examples

Palindromic DNA sequence
A palindromic DNA sequence can be recognized and cut by certain restriction enzymes.

The word 'level' is a classic example of a palindromic word.
Some dates, like 02-02-2020, are considered palindromic because they read the same both ways.
She enjoys finding palindromic sequences in mathematics and language puzzles.
