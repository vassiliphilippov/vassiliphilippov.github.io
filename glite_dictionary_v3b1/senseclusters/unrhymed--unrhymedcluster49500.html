unrhymed

adjective
not having rhyme; not rhymed
5.5
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈraɪmd / (AmE), / ʌnˈraɪmd / (BrE)
(of poetry or verse) not having lines that end with words of similar sounds; not rhymed.

Examples

Unrhymed verse
Her latest poem is written in unrhymed verse, focusing on natural rhythms.
Many Shakespearean plays include passages of unrhymed iambic pentameter known as blank verse.
The teacher asked us to compare unrhymed poetry with traditional rhymed forms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rhymed - (of poetry or verse) having rhyme between line endings
