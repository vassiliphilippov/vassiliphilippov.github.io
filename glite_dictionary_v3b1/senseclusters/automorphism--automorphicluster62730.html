automorphism

noun
structure-preserving mapping
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔtəˈmɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
a mapping of a mathematical object to itself that preserves all its structure

Examples

An automorphism of group
In group theory, an automorphism is a function that maps a group onto itself while preserving its structure.
The symmetry of the object can be described by its automorphisms.
Every identity mapping is an automorphism of the structure it acts on.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: homomorphism - a mapping between algebraic structures that preserves operations
  • synonym: isomorphism - a mapping between two structures that preserves their properties
