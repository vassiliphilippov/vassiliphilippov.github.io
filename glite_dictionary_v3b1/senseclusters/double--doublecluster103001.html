double

adjective
suitable for two people (e.g., bed, room)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl /; / ˈdʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈdʌbəl /; / ˈdʌb(ə)l /; / ˈdʌb.əl / (BrE)
twice as much or as many as usual; involving two people, parts, or aspects

Examples

A double bed
The double bed in the hotel was very comfortable for two people.
They decided to upgrade to a double instead of a single.
We booked a double room for our weekend getaway.
Most trains now use double tracks to allow traffic in both directions.
She was surprised to find an egg with a double yolk in her breakfast.
The house had impressive double doors leading into the entryway.
He made a double remark that could be interpreted in two ways.
Her words had a double meaning, leaving everyone confused about her true intentions.
The politician's double response was meant to please both sides of the debate.
