fair-minded

adjective
impartial and just; not prejudiced
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛrˌmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈfeəˌmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Impartial and just in forming opinions or making judgments; not prejudiced.

Examples

A fair-minded judge
A good judge must remain fair-minded and listen to both sides carefully.
She is known for being fair-minded in all her decisions at work.
Voters appreciated his fair-minded approach to controversial issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust that is derived from prejudice; biased
  • synonym: unbiased - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions; impartial
