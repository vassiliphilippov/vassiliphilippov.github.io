dinar

noun
Algerian currency unit
4.3
Pronunciation: / diːˈnɑr /; / dəˈnɑr /; / ˈdiːnɑːr /; / diˈnɑr /; / ˈdiːnɑr /; / dɪˈnɑr / (AmE), / dɪˈnɑː /; / ˈdiːnɑː / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in several countries, such as Algeria, Libya, Bahrain, Iraq, Tunisia, Kuwait, Jordan, and formerly Yugoslavia

Examples

The dinar currency
Inflation caused the value of the dinar to fluctuate throughout the year.
She exchanged her euros for Algerian dinar at the airport.
The price was listed as 500 dinar for a cup of coffee in Algeria.
He exchanged dollars for Libyan dinar at the airport.
The dinar has declined in value against the euro this year.
The price was listed as 100 dinars per ticket.
A single dinar can be divided into one thousand fils.
He exchanged his dollars for dinar at the airport upon arrival in Bahrain.
The price of the item was listed as fifty dinar in the local market.
