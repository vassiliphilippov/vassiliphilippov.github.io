Archimedes

proper noun
ancient Greek mathematician and inventor
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrkɪˈmiːdiːz / (AmE), / ˌɑːkɪˈmiːdiːz / (BrE)
A Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer, and inventor (c. 287–212 BC), renowned for his discoveries in mathematics, physics, and engineering, especially in hydrostatics and mechanics.

Examples

Archimedes principle discovery
Archimedes famously exclaimed 'Eureka!' upon discovering the principle of buoyancy.
Many consider Archimedes to be one of the greatest mathematicians of all time.
The principles discovered by Archimedes are still taught in physics classes today.
