trade

verb
exchange one thing for another
Pronunciation: / treɪd / (AmE), / treɪd / (BrE)
to exchange something you have for something else, or as payment for something new

Examples

To trade cards
I will trade my sandwich for your apple.
She is always willing to trade ideas with her colleagues.
They traded baseball cards during recess.
She wants to trade her used textbooks for store credit at the campus bookstore.
We decided to trade our old lawn mower for a discount on a new one.
You can trade your smartphone when you upgrade to a newer model.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barter - to exchange goods or services without using money
  • synonym: swap - to exchange one thing for another
  • synonym: trade in - exchange something old as part payment for something new
