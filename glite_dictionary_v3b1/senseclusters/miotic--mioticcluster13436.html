miotic

noun
drug that constricts the pupil
Pronunciation: / maɪˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
a drug that causes constriction of the pupil of the eye

Examples

Miotic eye drops
After receiving the miotic, the patient's pupils became noticeably smaller.
Ophthalmologists may use a miotic in the treatment of glaucoma.
The doctor prescribed a miotic to reduce the patient's intraocular pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mydriatic - a drug that causes dilation of the pupil
