honor

verb
show respect
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːnər / (AmE), / ˈɒnə / (BrE)
to show great respect or admiration for someone or something, or to publicly recognize their achievements

Examples

To honor a promise
It is important to honor your promises if you want people to trust you.
The city plans to honor its veterans with a memorial parade next month.
We must honor those who have dedicated their lives to helping others.
Every year, we honor those who have made a significant contribution to our community.
She was deeply moved when the university chose to honor her for her lifetime achievements.
The city will honor its firefighters at the annual ceremony.
Each year, we honour those who served in the armed forces.
He was honoured for his outstanding contributions to science.
The city decided to honour her achievements with a special ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disgrace - to bring shame or dishonor upon
  • antonym: dishonor - to bring shame or a lack of respect to someone or something
  • antonym: dishonour - to show a lack of respect or bring shame upon
  • antonym: disregard - to ignore or show lack of respect for someone or something
  • synonym: respect - to show admiration for someone or something
  • synonym: reward - to give someone something in recognition of their service, effort, or achievement
