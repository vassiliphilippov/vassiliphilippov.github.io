gabbai

noun
synagogue caretaker
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡɑˈbaɪ /; / ɡɑːˈbaɪ / (AmE), / ɡəˈbaɪ /; / ˈɡæbaɪ / (BrE)
an official or caretaker in a synagogue, responsible for maintenance, organizing services, or managing funds

Examples

The gabbai manages services
The gabbai unlocked the synagogue early every morning to prepare for services.
After the festival, the gabbai organized volunteers to help clean the prayer hall.
When repairs were needed, the gabbai arranged for contractors to fix the roof.
The gabbai made sure the Torah scrolls were ready for the morning service.
During the ceremony, the gabbai called up members of the congregation to participate.
As a gabbai, he helped organize the weekly readings and assisted the rabbi.
The gabbai announced the total amount collected during the holiday fundraiser.
Each year, the gabbai presents a detailed financial report to the congregation.
When the synagogue needed repairs, the gabbai organized a special donation drive.
