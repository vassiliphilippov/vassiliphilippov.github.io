luminous

adjective
softly bright; radiant
4.8 4.77/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêm…™n…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêm…™n…ôs / (BrE)
emitting or reflecting a gentle, soft light; shining with a faint or steady glow

Examples

A luminous glow
Her dress had a luminous quality under the soft lights of the stage.
The luminous stars lit up the night sky.
We admired the luminous glow of the lanterns floating down the river.

Synonyms

  • lambent
    9.6 0.03/m
    lambent light softly flickers with a gentle, moving glow, while luminous light gives a steady, faint shine.
  • aglow
    5.5 0.56/m
    aglow looks brighter and warmly lit in low light, while luminous describes a softer, steady, faint glow on objects.
  • lustrous
    5.3 0.85/m
    lustrous shows a smooth, shiny surface that reflects light, while luminous means something gives or holds a soft glow itself.
  • lucent
    8.8 0.06/m
    lucent lets light pass through and looks clear or translucent, while luminous means something gives or reflects its own steady glow.
