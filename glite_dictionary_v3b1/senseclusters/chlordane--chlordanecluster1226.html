chlordane

noun
banned pesticide
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔːrˌdeɪn / (AmE), / ˈklɔːdeɪn / (BrE)
a toxic, persistent chemical compound formerly used as a pesticide, now banned in many countries due to health risks

Examples

Toxic chlordane pesticide
The government banned chlordane after studies showed it was harmful to humans and wildlife.
Farmers once used chlordane to control insects in crops before its dangers were widely known.
Traces of chlordane can still be found in soil decades after its use was discontinued.

Related Terms

  • synonym: DDT - a banned synthetic insecticide known for its environmental impact
  • synonym: pesticide - a chemical used to kill pests
