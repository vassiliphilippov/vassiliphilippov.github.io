rock fever

noun
infectious bacterial disease
6.5
Pronunciation: / rɑk ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / rɒk ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
An infectious bacterial disease of humans transmitted by contact with infected animals or contaminated meat or milk, characterized by fever and headache.

Examples

Rock fever disease
Consuming unpasteurized milk can increase the risk of getting rock fever.
People who handle livestock are advised to take precautions to avoid contracting rock fever.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with rock fever after he developed a high fever and severe headaches.
