peroneal vein

noun
vein in the leg running alongside peroneal arteries
8.4
Pronunciation: / pɛrəˈniːəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˌpɛr.əˈniː.əl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that accompanies the peroneal arteries, originating in the heel and running up the back of the leg to join the posterior tibial veins of the popliteal vein

Examples

The peroneal vein clot
A clot in the peroneal vein can lead to deep vein thrombosis.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully identified the peroneal vein to avoid injury.
The peroneal vein drains blood from the lateral compartment of the leg.
