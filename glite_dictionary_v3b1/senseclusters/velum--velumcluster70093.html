velum

noun
membranous covering in immature mushrooms
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàviÀêl…ôm / (AmE), / ÀàviÀêl…ôm / (BrE)
a membrane or membranous covering attached to the immature fruiting body of certain mushrooms

Examples

Mushroom velum
As the mushroom grows, the velum breaks, leaving remnants on the stem or cap.
Careful observation of the velum can help identify certain mushroom species.
The velum protects the spore-producing surface of the mushroom until it matures.

Synonyms

  • partial veil
    9.9 0.00/m
    a partial veil is a temporary mushroom membrane from cap edge to stem that breaks, while a velum is a membrane on immature fruiting bodies.
  • veil
    7.7 0.88/m
    a veil is a thin natural membrane that can cover mushrooms or newborns, while a velum is the specific membrane on immature mushroom fruiting bodies.
