taffrail log

noun
ship's speed-measuring device
9.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtæfreɪl lɑg / (AmE), / ˈtaf.reɪl lɒɡ / (BrE)
A device towed from the stern of a ship, equipped with rotary fins, used to measure the ship's speed through the water.

Examples

A taffrail log device
Older ships often relied on a taffrail log to calculate their speed at sea.
The captain checked the taffrail log reading before adjusting their estimated time of arrival.
The sailor dropped the taffrail log overboard to determine how fast the vessel was moving.
