corporation

noun
a legally recognized business entity
4.3 3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔr.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɔː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A large company or group of companies that is legally authorized to act as a single entity and recognized as such in law.

Examples

A big corporation
Many people invest in stocks of a corporation expecting long-term growth.
She works as an accountant for a large corporation based in New York.
The multinational corporation expanded its operations into several new countries this year.
