domino effect

noun
one event triggering a chain of events
2.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.mə.noʊ ə.ˈfekt/ /; / /ˈdɑː.mɪ.noʊ ə.ˈfekt/ (AmE), /ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəʊ ɪ.ˈfekt/ /; / /ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəʊ ə.ˈfekt/ (BrE)
a situation where one event causes a series of similar or related events to happen one after another

Examples

A domino effect happens
After the power outage, there was a domino effect as more and more neighborhoods lost electricity.
One mistake in the system led to a domino effect of failures throughout the network.
The manager warned that a single factory closure could set off a domino effect across the entire industry.
Raising the price of fuel had a domino effect on transportation and the cost of goods.
The announcement triggered a domino effect of changes across the industry.
When one supplier stopped delivering, it set off a domino effect that impacted every department.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chain reaction - a series of events, each caused by the previous one
  • synonym: ripple effect - the continuing and spreading results of an event or action
