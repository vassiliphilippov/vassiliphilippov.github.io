popishly

adverb
in a papal or Roman Catholic manner
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːpɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈpəʊpɪʃli / (BrE)
In a manner characteristic of the Pope or Roman Catholicism, especially in a way suggestive of Roman Catholic practices (often used derogatorily or historically).

Examples

Acting popishly during ceremony
He accused his rival of acting popishly during the ceremony.
The church was decorated popishly, with elaborate ornaments and candles.
They feared the ritual would be performed too popishly for their tastes.
