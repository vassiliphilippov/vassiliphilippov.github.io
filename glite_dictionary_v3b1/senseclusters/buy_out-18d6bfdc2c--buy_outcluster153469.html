buy out

verb
take over a business by purchasing it
3.5 1.75/m
Pronunciation: / baÉª aÊŠt / (AmE), / baÉª aÊŠt / (BrE)
to purchase a company, business, or someone's share in order to gain control or full ownership

Examples

To buy out a company
After months of negotiations, they successfully bought out their main competitor.
He plans to buy out his partner and run the business alone.
The company was bought out by a multinational corporation last year.
After years of negotiations, they finally bought out their competitor.
She managed to buy out her siblings' interest in the family business.

Synonyms

  • take over
    3.8 9.27/m
    take over can mean gaining control by purchase or force from outside owners, while buy out means buying a company's shares to gain full ownership.
  • buy up
    4.1 2.20/m
    buy up means buying most or all of something to control it, while buy out means buying a company or someone's shares to gain control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquire - to come to possess or own, especially a business
  • antonym: sell off - to sell all or part of a business or asset
  • antonym: sell out - to sell all or one's share of ownership, often to another party
