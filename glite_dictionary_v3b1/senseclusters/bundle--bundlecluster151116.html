bundle

verb
to sleep clothed with one's betrothed
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌndəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌnd(ə)l / (BrE)
(historical) To sleep fully clothed in the same bed with one's betrothed, especially as a part of a courtship custom.

Examples

To bundle in bed
In some Puritan communities, it was acceptable for couples to bundle before marriage as part of the courting ritual.
The novel describes a scene where the young couple bundles as their families look on.
Though considered old-fashioned now, the tradition of bundling once signified trust between betrothed partners.
