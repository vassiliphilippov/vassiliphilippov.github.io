noumenon

noun
philosophical term; thing-in-itself
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːməˌnɑn / (AmE), / ˈnjuːmɪnɒn / (BrE)
In philosophy, a thing as it is in itself, independent of perception or sensory experience.

Examples

The noumenon in philosophy
According to Kant, we can never truly know the noumenon; we can only know its phenomena.
Some thinkers debate whether the existence of a noumenon is even necessary in explaining reality.
The philosopher argued that the noumenon cannot be directly apprehended by human senses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phenomenon - A thing as it appears to and is perceived by the senses
