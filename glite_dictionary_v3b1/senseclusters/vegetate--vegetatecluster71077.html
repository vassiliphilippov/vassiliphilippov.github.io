vegetate

verb
be idle or engage in passive relaxation
7.4 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈve.dʒə.teɪt/ /; / /ˈve.dʒɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /ˈve.dʒɪ.teɪt/ /; / /ˈve.dʒə.teɪt/ (BrE)
to spend time in a passive, inactive, or unproductive way; to be idle or do nothing stimulating

Examples

To vegetate on the sofa
After a long week at work, I just want to vegetate on the couch all weekend.
She spent her holiday vegetating at home with a pile of novels and takeout food.
Sometimes it's good to vegetate and binge-watch your favorite shows without any guilt.
After his retirement, he refused to vegetate in front of the television all day.
If you vegetate over the summer, you'll find it hard to get back into a routine.
Without any goals or hobbies, she began to vegetate and felt her days slipping by.
After losing his job, he vegetated for months, watching television and avoiding all responsibilities.
During the long summer break, she vegetated at home instead of traveling or working.
He realized he had vegetated all weekend without accomplishing anything meaningful.

  • synonym: stagnate - to stop developing or progressing
  • antonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful
  • antonym: work - to engage in productive activity
