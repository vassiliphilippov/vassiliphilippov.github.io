regular convex polyhedron

noun
a polyhedron with congruent regular polygonal faces and angles
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr kənˈvɛks pɑˈliːˌhiːdrən / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə kənˈvɛks pɒlɪˈhiːdrən / (BrE)
a polyhedron whose faces are congruent regular polygons and whose angles are all congruent, one of the five Platonic solids

Examples

A regular convex polyhedron solid
A cube is an example of a regular convex polyhedron with six square faces.
Every regular convex polyhedron has faces that are identical regular polygons.
The five regular convex polyhedra have fascinated mathematicians for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Platonic solid - any of the five regular convex polyhedra
