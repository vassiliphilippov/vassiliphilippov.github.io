heliometer

noun
astronomical instrument for measuring star separation
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːliˈɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌhiːliˈɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used in astronomy to measure the angular separation between two stars that are too far apart to be seen together in a regular telescope

Examples

A heliometer instrument
Advances in technology eventually surpassed the accuracy of the traditional heliometer in astronomical observations.
The heliometer played a crucial role in measuring the Earth's distance from the sun in the 19th century.
The astronomer used a heliometer to determine the exact angular distance between the two distant stars.
