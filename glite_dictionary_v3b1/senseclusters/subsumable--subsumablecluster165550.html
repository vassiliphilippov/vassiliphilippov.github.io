subsumable

adjective
able to be included
8.4
Pronunciation: / səbˈsuːməbəl / (AmE), / səbˈsjuːməbəl / (BrE)
able to be included or classified within a larger group or category

Examples

Subsumable under laws
The new regulations are subsumable under existing environmental laws.
Many of these minor issues are subsumable within the broader category of customer complaints.
Her research findings are not easily subsumable into any current scientific theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distinct - clearly different and not included in a larger group
