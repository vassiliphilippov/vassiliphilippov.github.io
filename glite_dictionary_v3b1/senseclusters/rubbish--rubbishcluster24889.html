Synonyms

  • trash
    1.0 20/m
    trash is everyday American word for household waste and discarded items, while rubbish is common British term for the same things.
  • garbage
    1.0 11/m
    garbage often refers to kitchen or spoiled food thrown away, while rubbish covers any unwanted household things.
  • waste material
    2.6 0.4/m
    waste material sounds formal and refers to unusable substances or by-products, while rubbish is everyday talk for unwanted items.
  • waste matter
    1.9 0.02/m
    waste matter is a formal phrase for discarded substances in science or industry, while rubbish is casual household waste talk.
  • waste
    1.0 30/m
    waste is a broad term for unusable or leftover matter in many contexts, while rubbish is specifically thrown-away items.
  • scrap
    2.1 12/m
    scrap is small leftover pieces or metal bits kept or recycled, while rubbish is unwanted items meant to be thrown away.
