in ruins

In a state of complete destruction or severe disrepair, either literally or figuratively.

Examples

In ruins after fire
After the fire, the old library was in ruins and could not be saved.
Many ancient temples still stand in ruins, reminding us of civilizations long gone.
The company was left in ruins following the scandal and mass layoffs.

Synonyms

  • ruined
    3.1 0.02/m
    ruined is completely destroyed or spoiled, while in ruins describes something left standing but in severe disrepair.
  • destroyed
    2.8 0.00/m
    destroyed is totally broken or made unusable, while in ruins often suggests visible wreckage and lasting structural decay.
  • in tatters
    6.4 0.53/m
    in tatters shows things torn, messy, or battered in appearance, while in ruins implies near complete destruction or collapse.
  • wrecked
    4.0 0.00/m
    wrecked is badly damaged often by an accident or crash, while in ruins can result from long decay or violent events.
