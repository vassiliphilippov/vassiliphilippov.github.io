achlorhydric

adjective
lacking hydrochloric acid (esp. in stomach)
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪˌklɔrˈhaɪdrɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌklɔːˈhɪdrɪk / (BrE)
(of a person or organism) lacking hydrochloric acid, especially in the gastric juices of the stomach.

Examples

Achlorhydric stomach condition
Certain medications can cause an achlorhydric condition, leading to digestive problems.
Pernicious anemia is often associated with an achlorhydric stomach environment.
The patient was found to be achlorhydric after laboratory analysis of his gastric secretions.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hyperchlorhydric - producing more than the normal amount of hydrochloric acid
  • degree lower: hypochlorhydric - producing less than the normal amount of hydrochloric acid
