quit

verb
leave or go away
Pronunciation: / kwɪt / (AmE), / kwɪt / (BrE)
to leave a place or to go away, usually permanently

Examples

To quit the house
After the argument, he decided to quit the house and never return.
Many settlers had to quit their homeland in search of a better life.
She quit the noisy party early because she was feeling unwell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depart - to go away from a place
  • antonym: remain - to stay or not leave a place
