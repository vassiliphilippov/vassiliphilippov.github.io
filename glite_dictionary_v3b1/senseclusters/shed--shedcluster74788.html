shed

verb
allow something solid to spill out
Pronunciation: / ʃɛd / (AmE), / ʃɛd / (BrE)
to let something (liquid or solid) fall, flow, or spill out, often unintentionally

Examples

To shed water
Accidentally, he shed flour all over the kitchen floor while baking.
She shed crumbs across the table as she cut the bread.
The machine sheds loose metal shavings after prolonged use.
During the emotional speech, many in the audience shed tears.
She tried not to shed any tears during the sad movie.
The wounded soldier shed much blood on the battlefield.
