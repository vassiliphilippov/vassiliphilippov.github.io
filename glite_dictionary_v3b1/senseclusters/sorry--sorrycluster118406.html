sorry

adjective
feeling regret or sorrow
1.0 227/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ëÀêri / (AmE), / Ààs…íri / (BrE)
feeling or showing regret or sorrow for something that has happened or been done, especially a mistake or wrong

Examples

I am sorry for that
He was sorry for the harsh words he had spoken.
I'm truly sorry that I missed your call last night.
She looked sorry as she apologized for the mistake.

Synonyms

  • regretful
    2.9 0.68/m
    regretful feels deep sorrow about a past action and wishes things had been different, while sorry is more general.
  • apologetic
    3.7 2.95/m
    apologetic offers an apology to another person through words or action, while sorry can be a private feeling.
  • remorseful
    4.6 0.66/m
    remorseful feels deep guilt and pain for wrongdoing and seems very heavy-hearted, while sorry is less intense.
  • contrite
    8.1 0.90/m
    contrite shows deep regret and humble remorse after a wrong, while sorry is more general and common in speech.
  • repentant
    4.8 0.81/m
    repentant shows a wish to change and makes amends after wrongdoing, while sorry may not include a promise to change.
  • rueful
    7.8 0.85/m
    rueful is a mildly amused and sad look about a mistake, while sorry is a plain expression of regret.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regretful - feeling regret or sorrow about something past
  • synonym: remorseful - feeling deep regret or guilt for a wrong committed
