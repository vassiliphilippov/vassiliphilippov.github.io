executive department

noun
U.S. federal government department in the executive branch
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv dɪˈpɑrtmənt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv dɪˈpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
a federal department forming part of the executive branch of the government of the United States, responsible for carrying out laws and administering public affairs

Examples

The executive department manages laws
Congress has the authority to establish new executive departments as needed.
Each executive department is led by a member of the President’s Cabinet.
The Department of State is an important executive department in the United States government.
