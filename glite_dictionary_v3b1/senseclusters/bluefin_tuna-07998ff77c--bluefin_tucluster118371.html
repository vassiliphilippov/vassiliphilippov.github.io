bluefin tuna

noun
a large species of tuna fish
Pronunciation: / ˈbluːˌfɪn ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈbluːfɪn ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
a large species of tuna found in temperate seas, known for its size and value as a food fish

Examples

A giant bluefin tuna
A single bluefin tuna can weigh more than 600 kilograms and fetch a high price at auction.
Overfishing has made bluefin tuna a threatened species in many parts of the world.
The chef prepared sushi using fresh bluefin tuna flown in that morning.
