suppressive fire

noun
gunfire to hinder enemy operations
4.4 6.2
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛsɪv ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / səˈprɛsɪv ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
Sustained gunfire aimed at an enemy position to hinder or prevent its effective operation, especially during combat.

Examples

Heavy suppressive fire
Heavy suppressive fire allowed the assault team to safely cross the open ground.
The soldiers laid down suppressive fire to keep the enemy pinned in their bunker.
Without adequate suppressive fire, the advance stalled under hostile resistance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: covering fire - gunfire aimed to protect advancing or retreating troops
