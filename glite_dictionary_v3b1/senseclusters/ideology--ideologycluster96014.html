ideology

noun
visionary or imaginary theorization
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiˈɒlədʒi /; / ˌʌɪdɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a set of ideas or beliefs, especially those that form the basis of a political, economic, or social system

Examples

Political ideology
Her argument relied on ideology rather than on evidence or facts.
Some critics viewed his utopian plans as a product of pure ideology.
The proposal was dismissed as mere ideology, lacking any practical foundation.
Education can play a vital role in shaping the ideology of young people.
His ideology clashed with that of the organization he worked for.
The political party is driven by a strict ideology that influences all its decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creed - a system of religious belief or faith
  • synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group
  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • antonym: pragmatism - a practical approach to problems and affairs (opposite in some political contexts)
  • antonym: realism - the attitude of accepting facts and dealing with them practically
  • synonym: theory - a set of ideas intended to explain something
