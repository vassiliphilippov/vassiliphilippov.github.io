exclusive

adjective
not shared; limited to one
5.4 26/m

limited to one person, group, or purpose; not shared with others; only allowing certain people to join or participate

Examples

Exclusive club
Membership in this club is exclusive to employees only.
The company granted them exclusive rights to market the new product.
We have exclusive use of the conference room this morning.
His exclusive devotion to science meant he rarely pursued other interests.
She gave her exclusive attention to her daughter's needs during the illness.
The club rewarded him with their exclusive loyalty for years.
She shops at exclusive boutiques that cater to a wealthy clientele.
The country club is very exclusive and only accepts members by invitation.
The gala was so exclusive that only celebrities and dignitaries were invited.

Synonyms

  • private
    1.8 126/m
    private is meant for one person or a small group, while exclusive often implies limited access and higher status.
  • sole
    3.7 18/m
    sole means being the only one and not shared, while exclusive often refers to a group choice or limited membership.
  • closed
    1.3 0.72/m
    closed describes an event or place that is not open to the public, while exclusive stresses selective membership.
  • restricted
    3.9 1.21/m
    restricted means access or amount is controlled, often by rules, while exclusive focuses on being limited to certain people.
  • privileged
    5.4 1.27/m
    privileged is available to a select group because of rank or advantage, while exclusive means limited and often chosen for status.
