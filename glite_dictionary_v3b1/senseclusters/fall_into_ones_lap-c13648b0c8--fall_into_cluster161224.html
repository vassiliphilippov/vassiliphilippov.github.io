fall into one's lap

verb
receive something unexpectedly and easily
1.8
Pronunciation: / fɔl ˈɪntu wʌnz læp / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɪntuː wʌnz læp / (BrE)
to receive or obtain something valuable or desirable without effort or when it is not expected

Examples

Opportunities fall into your lap
Sometimes opportunities fall into your lap when you least expect them.
The answer to her problem fell into her lap during a casual conversation.
When the perfect job fell into his lap, he couldn't believe his luck.
