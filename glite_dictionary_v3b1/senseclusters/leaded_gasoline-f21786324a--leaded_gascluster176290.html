leaded gasoline

noun
gasoline containing a lead additive
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdɪd ˈɡæsəliːn / (AmE), / ˈlɛdɪd ˈɡæsəliːn / (BrE)
gasoline that has been treated with a lead compound, used to reduce engine knocking but now largely phased out due to health and environmental concerns

Examples

Leaded gasoline fuel
Old cars that require leaded gasoline often need special additives today.
The sale of leaded gasoline was banned in many countries due to its harmful effects.
Using leaded gasoline contributed to widespread environmental contamination in the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unleaded gasoline - gasoline that does not contain added lead compounds
