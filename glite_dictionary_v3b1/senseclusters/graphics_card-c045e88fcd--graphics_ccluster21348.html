graphics card

noun
computer hardware for graphics rendering
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræfɪks kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈɡræfɪks kɑːd / (BrE)
a piece of computer hardware that processes and renders images and video, often used to improve visual performance in games and applications.

Examples

A graphics card upgrade
If your screen flickers, your graphics card may need to be replaced.
Many gamers upgrade their graphics card to improve frame rates in the latest games.
My computer runs smoothly because it has a powerful graphics card.

  • synonym: video card - a hardware component that generates and outputs images to a display
