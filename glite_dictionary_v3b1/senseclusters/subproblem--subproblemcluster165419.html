subproblem

noun
smaller part of problem
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌprɑːbləm / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌprɒbləm / (BrE)
a smaller or simpler problem that is part of a larger, more complex problem

Examples

Solve subproblems
To solve the complex equation, we divided it into several subproblems.
Each subproblem was assigned to a different member of the team.
By addressing each subproblem separately, the overall task became much easier.
The project was divided into several subproblems to make it easier to manage.
Solving each subproblem individually can help you find a solution to the whole issue.
Before tackling the main challenge, the team addressed every subproblem in sequence.
