Saint Thomas Aquinas

proper noun
Italian Catholic philosopher and theologian
8.1
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈtɑːməs əˈkwaɪnəs / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈtɒməs əˈkwaɪnəs / (BrE)
(1225–1274) An Italian Catholic philosopher and theologian whose works sought to reconcile faith with reason and who is considered one of the leading figures in medieval scholasticism.

Examples

Saint Thomas Aquinas philosopher
Saint Thomas Aquinas is regarded as one of the Catholic Church’s greatest theologians.
Many universities offer courses on the works of Saint Thomas Aquinas.
The philosophy of Saint Thomas Aquinas greatly influenced Western thought.
