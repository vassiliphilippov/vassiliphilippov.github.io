walkthrough

noun
practice rehearsal
3.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…îkÀåŒ∏ru / (AmE), / Ààw…îÀêkÀåŒ∏ruÀê / (BrE)
a practice or rehearsal in which participants go through the steps of an activity without fully performing it

Examples

Concert walkthrough
Before the concert, the crew did a walkthrough to make sure everything was ready.
The teacher led a walkthrough of the fire drill procedure with the students.
We had a walkthrough of the presentation to check the timing and transitions.

Synonyms

  • run-through
    4.2 0.44/m
    a run-through is an uninterrupted practice that runs the whole event in sequence, while a walkthrough examines each step without full execution.
  • dry run
    4.1 0.27/m
    a dry run is a full practice of an event under realistic conditions, while a walkthrough goes step by step without fully performing it.
  • rehearsal
    3.0 13/m
    a rehearsal is a repeated practice of a play or music to prepare for performance, while a walkthrough goes through steps without full performance.
