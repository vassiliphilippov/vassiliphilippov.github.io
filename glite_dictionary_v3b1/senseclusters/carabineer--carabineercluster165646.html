carabineer

noun
soldier armed with a carbine
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkærəˈbɪnɪr / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈbiːnɪə / (BrE)
a soldier, especially a mounted one, armed with a carbine, historically used in cavalry units

Examples

Carabineers on horseback
During the 18th century, carabineers played a vital role in European armies.
The carabineer rode ahead of the main force, scouting for enemy movement.
The regiment included several carabineers known for their expert marksmanship.
