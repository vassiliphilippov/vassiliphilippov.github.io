impression

noun
a strong mental image or idea

an idea, feeling, or opinion you have about someone or something, especially one formed quickly or without full information

Examples

A good impression
Her description gave me a clear impression of the place.
I still have a vivid impression of my first day at school.
The violent storm left a lasting impression on all who witnessed it.
I was left with the impression that the project would take much longer than expected.
My first impression of the city was that it was very welcoming and lively.
She had the impression that he didn't want to be there, though he never said so.
Despite his nervousness, she had the impression that he was very confident.
He wanted his work clothes to give a professional impression to his clients.
The decor was chosen to create the impression of luxury in the hotel lobby.

Synonyms

  • feeling
    1.0 76/m
    feeling is an inner, often emotional sense about someone, while impression is a quick idea formed from limited information.
  • opinion
    1.0 119/m
    opinion is a considered belief or judgment often based on thought, while impression is a quick view formed with limited information.
  • notion
    3.7 60/m
    notion is a vague or early idea someone holds, while impression is a quick, immediate sense formed from a short encounter.
  • estimation
    3.2 2.01/m
    estimation is a judged value or reputation often based on evidence, while impression is a fast, first idea made without full information.
