indelibility

noun
quality of being difficult to remove or erase
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.dəˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪ.lɪˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being impossible or very difficult to remove, erase, or wipe away.

Examples

The indelibility of memories
Many survivors spoke about the indelibility of childhood memories.
The indelibility of the ink made it perfect for official documents.
The artist was fascinated by the indelibility of emotions captured in a single photograph.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impermanence - the quality or state of being temporary or short-lived
  • synonym: permanence - the state of lasting or remaining unchanged indefinitely
