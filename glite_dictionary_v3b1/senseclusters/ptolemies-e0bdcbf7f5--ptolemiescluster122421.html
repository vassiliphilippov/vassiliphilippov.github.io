Ptolemies

proper noun
Egyptian ruling dynasty
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.lə.miːz/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.lə.miːz/ (BrE)
the Greek-Macedonian royal family (dynasty) that ruled Egypt from 305 BC to 30 BC, including its members

Examples

The Ptolemies dynasty
The Ptolemies blended Greek and Egyptian traditions during their rule over Egypt.
Cleopatra VII was the most famous of the Ptolemies and the last to rule Egypt.
Many ancient temples in Egypt were built or restored by the Ptolemies.
The Ptolemies established their capital in Alexandria, making it a center of learning and culture.
Many of the Ptolemies adopted Egyptian customs to legitimize their rule.
Cleopatra was one of the most famous Ptolemies and the last pharaoh of Egypt.
