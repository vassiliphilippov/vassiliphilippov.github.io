Japanese plum

noun
small Asian tree with large yellow to red plums
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz plʌm / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz plʌm / (BrE)
A small tree native to China and Japan that produces large yellow to red plums, typically less sweet than European plums.

Examples

Tart Japanese plum variety
Compared to European varieties, a Japanese plum often has a tarter taste.
The Japanese plum thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.
We picked several ripe Japanese plums from the tree in the garden.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: European plum - a species of plum native to Europe and western Asia, known for its sweet flavor
  • synonym: plum - a sweet, edible fruit with a smooth skin and central stone
