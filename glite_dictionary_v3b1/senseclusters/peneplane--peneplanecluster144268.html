peneplane

noun
nearly level eroded land surface
4.4 9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpiː.nə.pleɪn/ /; / /ˈpiː.niː.pleɪn/ (AmE), /ˈpiː.nə.pleɪn/ /; / /ˈpiː.niː.pleɪn/ (BrE)
A nearly level land surface formed by prolonged erosion, representing an advanced stage in the evolution of landforms and largely undisturbed by crustal movements.

Examples

A peneplane surface
A true peneplane is rare because tectonic activity often interrupts the erosion process.
Geologists believe the ancient landscape is a peneplane formed by millions of years of erosion.
The coastal region features a vast peneplane stretching for miles with only slight undulations.

  • synonym: plain - a large area of flat land with few trees
