semifluidity

noun
state between solid and liquid
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈfluːɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈfluːɪdɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being semifluid; the property of being between a solid and a liquid in consistency

Examples

The semifluidity of gels
Certain gels are prized for their semifluidity, which allows them to spread easily but hold form.
Cheese at room temperature often achieves a state of semifluidity.
The semifluidity of lava makes it both flow and retain its shape.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: fluidity - the quality of being able to flow easily
  • degree lower: solidity - the quality or state of being solid
