motte

noun
defensible fallback position
Pronunciation: / mɑt / (AmE), / mɒt / (BrE)
a defensible and uncontroversial position that someone retreats to when their more controversial claim is challenged, especially in argument or debate.

Examples

Debate motte
When pressed on the details, he quickly retreated to the motte of his original, safer statement.
Debaters sometimes use a motte to avoid defending their more controversial ideas.
Her argument seemed bold at first, but she soon relied on the motte when challenged.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bailey - a more vulnerable or controversial position contrasted with the motte in argument
  • synonym: retreat - to withdraw to a safer or less controversial position
