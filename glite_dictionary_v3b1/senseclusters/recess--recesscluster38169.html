recess

verb
temporarily suspend proceedings
4.8 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀê.ses / (AmE), / r…™Ààses / (BrE)
to suspend proceedings or activities temporarily, especially during a meeting, court session, or legislative assembly

Examples

To recess a meeting
The committee will recess for an hour before continuing with the agenda.
The judge decided to recess the court for lunch at noon.
They usually recess the meeting when discussions become heated.

Synonyms

  • adjourn
    5.3 2.36/m
    adjourn pauses or ends a formal meeting with plans to resume later, while recess usually means a shorter break during proceedings.
  • suspend
    3.9 6.82/m
    suspend officially stops an activity, often by authority and for varying time, while recess is a short temporary pause in proceedings.
