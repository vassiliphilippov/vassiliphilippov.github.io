labialization

noun
movement toward lips
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪbiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌleɪbɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the movement or positioning of a tooth or anatomical part toward the lips, especially in dentistry or orthodontics

Examples

Dental labialization
The orthodontist monitored the labialization of the canine throughout the treatment period.
Excessive labialization can affect the alignment of the front teeth.
The dentist explained that labialization may be necessary to correct certain bite issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lingualization - movement of a tooth toward the tongue
