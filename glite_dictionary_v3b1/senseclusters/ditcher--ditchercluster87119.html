ditcher

noun
person who skips
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˈdɪtʃə / (BrE)
a person who deliberately avoids or skips an activity, especially school or work

Examples

A school ditcher
The teacher quickly noticed that the same ditcher was missing from class again.
As a known ditcher, he often missed important meetings at work.
Her friends called her a ditcher because she skipped the group project sessions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absentee - a person who is not present at a place where they should be
  • antonym: attendee - a person who is present at an event or activity
  • synonym: truant - a student who stays away from school without permission
