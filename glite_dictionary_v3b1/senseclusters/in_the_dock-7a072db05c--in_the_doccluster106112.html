in the dock

adverb
accused or on trial
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə dɑk / (AmE), / ɪn ðə dɒk / (BrE)
in a situation where one is accused of wrongdoing or is on trial, especially in a court of law

Examples

In the dock for fraud
Several executives were in the dock for fraud charges.
The company's practices have been in the dock in recent weeks.
The politician found himself in the dock after the allegations came to light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: on trial - facing judgment in court
  • synonym: under suspicion - being suspected of wrongdoing
