crook

noun
a bend or curve in something
7.8
Pronunciation: / krʊk / (AmE), / krʊk / (BrE)
a bend or curved part, especially where something turns sharply from a straight line

Examples

A crook in the road
The river formed a sharp crook before flowing south.
There was a crook in the old wooden cane he carried.
They built the fence to follow every crook and turn of the path.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bend - a curve, especially a sharp one in a road or river
  • synonym: curve - a smoothly bending line, without angles
  • antonym: straight - extending in the same direction without curving
