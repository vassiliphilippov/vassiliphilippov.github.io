distancing

noun
increasing a lead
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstənsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɪstənsɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of keeping or increasing physical space between people, things, or competitors

Examples

Physical distancing rules
The cyclist's powerful acceleration resulted in significant distancing from the rest of the pack.
Her strategic moves led to the distancing of her team from their closest rivals.
A late goal caused a clear distancing between the two leading teams in the tournament.
During the pandemic, distancing was required in public places to reduce the spread of disease.
The teacher explained the importance of distancing in crowded classrooms.
Many events were canceled to encourage distancing and protect public health.
