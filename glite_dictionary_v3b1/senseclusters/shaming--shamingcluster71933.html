shaming

noun
public embarrassment
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making someone feel embarrassed or ashamed, especially in public or online

Examples

Online shaming
The celebrity faced intense shaming on social media after her comments went viral.
Public shaming can sometimes lead to long-lasting emotional harm.
Many people believe that online shaming is not an effective way to change behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: humiliation - the act of making someone feel very ashamed or foolish
  • antonym: praise - the act of expressing approval or admiration
