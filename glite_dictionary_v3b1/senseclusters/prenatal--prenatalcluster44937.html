prenatal

adjective
before birth; during pregnancy
3.8 3.66/m
Pronunciation: / priÀàne…™t…ôl / (AmE), / priÀêÀàne…™t…ôl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring before birth, especially to the care of the mother and fetus during pregnancy

Examples

Prenatal care
Doctors recommend vitamins and proper nutrition during the prenatal stage of pregnancy.
She attended regular prenatal checkups to ensure her baby's health.
The prenatal period is crucial for fetal development.

Synonyms

  • antenatal
    6.8 0.14/m
    antenatal is used for routine pregnancy care and checkups, while prenatal is the common term for care before birth.
  • antepartum
    9.9 0.00/m
    antepartum refers to the specific period before labor in medical records, while prenatal covers all care and events before birth.
  • in utero
    7.9 0.62/m
    in utero describes a baby while still inside the womb, whereas prenatal describes care and tests that happen before birth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antenatal - relating to the period before birth (chiefly British)
  • antonym: postnatal - occurring or existing after birth
