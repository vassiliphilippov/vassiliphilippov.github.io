wing it

verb
to improvise
6.1
Pronunciation: / wɪŋ ɪt / (AmE), / wɪŋ ɪt / (BrE)
to do something without planning, preparation, or rehearsal, relying on improvisation

Examples

To wing it in class
I forgot to study for the test, so I had to wing it and hope for the best.
She didn't have time to write a speech, so she decided to wing it in front of the audience.
Sometimes the best moments happen when you just wing it instead of following a strict plan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvise - to perform or do something without preparation
  • antonym: prepare - to make ready or plan in advance
