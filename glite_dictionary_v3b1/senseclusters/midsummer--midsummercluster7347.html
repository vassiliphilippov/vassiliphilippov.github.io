midsummer

noun
the summer solstice (around June 21)
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌsʌmɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsʌmə / (BrE)
The period around June 21, when the sun is at its northernmost point and the day is longest in the northern hemisphere; the summer solstice.

Examples

The midsummer day
Farmers traditionally marked midsummer as an important time in the growing season.
In Scandinavia, midsummer is celebrated with bonfires and dancing.
The longest day of the year falls on midsummer.
