midyear

adjective, adverb
middle of the year
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌjɪr / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌjɪə / (BrE)
happening in or relating to the middle part of the year (around June or July); at or during the middle of the year

Examples

Midyear exam
The school holds a midyear exam to assess students' progress.
We usually review our goals during the midyear meeting in July.
A midyear bonus was given to employees for their hard work.
The company reviews its financial performance midyear to adjust its strategy if needed.
Many students take a break midyear before starting new courses in the fall.
We usually plan our family vacation midyear when the weather is warm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annual - happening once every year
  • synonym: midway - at or near the middle point
  • antonym: year-end - relating to the end of the year
