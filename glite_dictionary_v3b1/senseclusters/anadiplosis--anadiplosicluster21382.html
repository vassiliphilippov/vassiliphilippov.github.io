anadiplosis

noun
repetition of words at sentence or clause boundaries
7.1 9.5
Pronunciation: /æ.nə.ˈdɪ.plə.sɪs/ (AmE), /æ.nə.ˈdɪ.plə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the repetition of the last word or phrase of one clause, sentence, or line at the beginning of the next

Examples

Anadiplosis in poetry
In poetry, anadiplosis can create a powerful rhythm and reinforce key ideas.
The orator used anadiplosis for emphasis, repeating the last word of each clause to begin the next.
The phrase 'Fear leads to anger. Anger leads to hate.' is an example of anadiplosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anaphora - repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses
  • synonym: epistrophe - repetition of the same word or phrase at the end of successive clauses
