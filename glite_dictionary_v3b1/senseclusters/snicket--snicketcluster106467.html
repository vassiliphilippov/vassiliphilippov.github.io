snicket

noun
narrow passageway
Pronunciation: / ˈsnɪkɪt / (BrE)
a narrow passage or alley between buildings, especially in northern England

Examples

A narrow snicket
They slipped quietly through the snicket behind the old bakery.
The children played hide and seek in the narrow snicket between the houses.
Locals often use the snicket as a shortcut to the market square.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alley - a narrow street or passage between buildings
  • synonym: lane - a narrow road or path, especially in a town or village
