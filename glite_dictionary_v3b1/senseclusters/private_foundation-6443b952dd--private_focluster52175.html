private foundation

noun
charity funded mainly by a single source
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvət faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
A non-governmental charitable organization that receives most of its funding from a single source, such as an individual, family, or corporation, rather than from the general public.

Examples

A private foundation fund
A private foundation must adhere to specific tax regulations to maintain its charitable status.
Many research grants are funded by a private foundation rather than by the government.
The local art museum is supported by a private foundation established by a philanthropist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: public foundation - a charitable foundation funded mainly by public donations
