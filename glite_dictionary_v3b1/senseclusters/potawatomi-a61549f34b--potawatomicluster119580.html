Potawatomi

proper noun
Native American people
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑtəˈwɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌpɒtəˈwɒtəmi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from the Great Lakes region of North America

Examples

The Potawatomi tribe
The Potawatomi played an important role in the history of the Great Lakes region.
Many Potawatomi traditions are still celebrated by their descendants today.
A museum exhibit highlighted the art and culture of the Potawatomi people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ojibwe - a Native American people of the northern Midwest and Canada
