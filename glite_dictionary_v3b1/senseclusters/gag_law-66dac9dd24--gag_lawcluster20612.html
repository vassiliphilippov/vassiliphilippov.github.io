gag law

noun
law restricting freedom of the press
Pronunciation: / ɡæɡ lɔ / (AmE), / ɡæɡ lɔː / (BrE)
a law that restricts or limits the freedom of the press or the expression of opinions

Examples

Gag law on newspapers
Many activists protest against any proposed gag law that would threaten press freedom.
The controversial gag law was heavily criticized by journalists and human rights organizations.
Under the new gag law, publishing certain information has become a punishable offense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: censorship - the suppression or prohibition of speech or writing considered harmful or sensitive
  • antonym: free speech - the right to express opinions without censorship or restraint
