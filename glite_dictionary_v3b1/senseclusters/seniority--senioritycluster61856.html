seniority

noun
higher rank due to longer service
1.0 3.7
Pronunciation: /siː.ˈnjɔːr.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /sɪ.ˈnjɔːr.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /siː.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tɪ/ /; / /sɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the state of having a higher rank or status than others, especially because of having worked longer in an organization or profession

Examples

Greater seniority status
Employees with greater seniority are usually the last to be laid off during downsizing.
Her seniority meant she was given priority in choosing vacation days.
Promotion in this company is often based on seniority rather than merit.
