tricyclic

noun
type of antidepressant drug
5.1
Pronunciation: / traɪˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / traɪˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
An antidepressant drug that works by blocking the reuptake of norepinephrine and serotonin, increasing their availability in the brain.

Examples

A tricyclic antidepressant
Newer drugs have replaced the tricyclic in many treatment guidelines.
Some patients experience side effects when taking tricyclic antidepressants.
The doctor prescribed a tricyclic to help manage her depression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antidepressant - a drug used to alleviate symptoms of depression
