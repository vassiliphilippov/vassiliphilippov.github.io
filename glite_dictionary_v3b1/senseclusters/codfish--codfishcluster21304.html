codfish

noun
marine food fish of cold waters
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒdˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A large marine fish found in Arctic and cold-temperate waters, known for its white flesh and valued as food.

Examples

Wild codfish
Fishermen in Norway rely on the annual migration of codfish for their livelihood.
In the 18th century, codfish was a crucial export for New England economies.
The flaky white meat of codfish makes it popular in many traditional dishes.
