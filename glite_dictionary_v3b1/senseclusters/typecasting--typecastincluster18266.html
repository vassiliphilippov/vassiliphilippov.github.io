typecasting

noun
data type conversion
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪpˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of converting a value from one data type to another in computer programming, either automatically or explicitly

Examples

Programming typecasting
In many programming languages, typecasting is necessary when combining integers and floating-point numbers.
The developer used explicit typecasting to avoid errors in the calculation.
Automatic typecasting can sometimes lead to unexpected results if not handled carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: casting - the act of converting a variable from one type to another
