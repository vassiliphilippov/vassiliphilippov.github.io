ironclad

noun
armored 19th-century warship
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪərnˌklæd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌklæd / (BrE)
a 19th-century warship with wooden hulls protected by iron or steel armor plates

Examples

A 19th-century ironclad ship
Many early ironclads were converted from wooden ships to withstand modern artillery.
The ironclad represented a major advance in naval warfare during the Civil War.
The famous battle between the Monitor and the Merrimack demonstrated the strength of ironclad vessels.
