adulterant

noun
substance that reduces purity or effectiveness
Pronunciation: / əˈdʌltɚənt / (AmE), / əˈdʌltərənt / (BrE)
a substance added to another, typically in order to reduce its purity or effectiveness

Examples

An adulterant in medicine
It is illegal to add any adulterant to pharmaceuticals without proper disclosure.
Some unscrupulous vendors include an adulterant to reduce costs, compromising safety.
The laboratory detected an adulterant in the food sample, prompting a recall of the product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contaminant - a substance that makes something impure by contact or mixture
  • antonym: purifier - a substance or agent that removes impurities
