lubber

noun
inexperienced sailor
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌb.ə / (BrE)
an inexperienced or clumsy sailor, especially one on their first voyage

Examples

Green lubber on deck
During his first week at sea, the lubber made several mistakes with the ropes.
The captain could tell at once that the new recruit was a lubber unfamiliar with shipboard life.
The experienced sailors often teased the lubber for getting seasick in calm waters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: landlubber - a person unfamiliar with the sea or sailing
