princeps

proper noun
Latin epithet 'first'
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪnˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˈprɪnˌsɛps / (BrE)
a Latin term used in scientific names to indicate the first or chief among related species.

Examples

Papilio princeps
The butterfly species Papilio princeps was named for being the first of its kind discovered.
In taxonomy, the word princeps often appears to denote a leading or primary species.
Researchers identified a new plant, Rosa princeps, as the chief representative of its group.
