coronoid

noun
jaw bone in reptiles
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənɔɪd / (BrE)
a distinct bone in the lower jaw of some fishes and reptiles, known as the coronoid bone

Examples

Coronoid bone
The paleontologist identified the coronoid among the fossilized jaw bones.
In some reptiles, the coronoid is a separate bone distinct from the rest of the jaw.
Researchers studied the structure of the coronoid to understand the evolution of vertebrate jaws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mandible - the lower jawbone in vertebrates
