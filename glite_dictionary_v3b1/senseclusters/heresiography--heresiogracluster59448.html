heresiography

noun
study of heresies
Pronunciation: / həˌrɛziˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / həˌrɛsiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study or written account of heresies and heretical movements, especially in religious history

Examples

Heresiography of heresies
The professor's heresiography explores the origins of various medieval religious sects.
He devoted years to compiling a comprehensive heresiography of early Christian movements.
Scholars often consult ancient heresiography to understand the development of church doctrine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heresy - a belief or opinion that goes against established religious doctrine
  • synonym: theology - the study of the nature of God and religious beliefs
