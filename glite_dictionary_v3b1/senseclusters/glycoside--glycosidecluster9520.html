glycoside

noun
compound with a sugar bonded to another group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlaɪ.kəˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡlʌɪ.kəˌsʌɪd / (BrE)
any of a group of compounds in which a carbohydrate (usually a sugar) is bound to another functional group via a glycosidic bond

Examples

A glycoside compound
Digitalis contains a cardiac glycoside used to treat certain heart conditions.
Many plant-derived medicines contain a glycoside as the active ingredient.
The glycoside is hydrolyzed in the digestive tract to release the sugar and the aglycone.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monosaccharide - a simple sugar, such as glucose or fructose
