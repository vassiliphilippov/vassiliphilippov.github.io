hydraulic cement

noun
cement that hardens under water
9.9 3.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.ˈdrɔː.lɪk sə.ˈment/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɔː.lɪk sɪ.ˈment/ (AmE), /haɪ.ˈdrɒ.lɪk sə.ˈment/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɒ.lɪk sɪ.ˈment/ (BrE)
a type of cement that hardens when mixed with water, even under water, made by heating limestone and clay and then grinding the result.

Examples

Hydraulic cement hardens underwater
Modern building foundations often use hydraulic cement because it hardens efficiently even when wet.
The engineers selected hydraulic cement to repair the leaking dam wall.
Unlike other types, hydraulic cement can set quickly under water.

  • synonym: Portland cement - a common type of hydraulic cement
