tapper

noun
tavern keeper who taps kegs
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtæpər / (AmE), / ˈtæpə / (BrE)
a person who draws liquor from kegs or casks, especially a tavern keeper who serves drinks

Examples

Bar tapper
He worked as a tapper, drawing ale from the casks to serve thirsty patrons.
In old English villages, the tapper played a central role in the community's nightly gatherings.
The local tapper was known for his generous pours and friendly stories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bartender - a person who serves drinks at a bar
  • synonym: innkeeper - a person who manages or owns an inn
