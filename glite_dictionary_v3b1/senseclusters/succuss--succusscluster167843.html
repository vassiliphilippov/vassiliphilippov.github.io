succuss

verb
shake to detect fluid or air in the body (medical)
5.4
Pronunciation: / səˈkʌs / (AmE), / səˈkʌs / (BrE)
to shake the body or a part of the body, especially to detect the presence of fluid or air inside, as a medical examination method

Examples

To succuss the chest
During the old examination, doctors would succuss the chest to listen for abnormal sounds.
She carefully succussed the patient as part of the diagnostic procedure.
The physician decided to succuss the patient's abdomen to check for fluid accumulation.
