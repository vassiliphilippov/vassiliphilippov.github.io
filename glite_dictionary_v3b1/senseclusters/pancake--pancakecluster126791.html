pancake

verb
to flatten suddenly
Pronunciation: / ˈpænˌkeɪk / (AmE), / ˈpæn.keɪk /; / ˈpænkeɪk / (BrE)
to collapse or flatten suddenly into a thin, flat shape, especially because of force or pressure

Examples

Buildings pancake after earthquakes
The old warehouse began to pancake after the heavy snowstorm weakened its roof.
During the demolition, the building pancaked into a pile of rubble within seconds.
Rescue teams searched for survivors after several floors pancaked during the earthquake.
The old building pancaked after the powerful earthquake shook the city.
Several cars pancaked on top of each other during the massive pileup.
The stack of boxes pancaked when the bottom one gave way.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • synonym: crumple - to become bent or pressed out of shape
  • synonym: flatten - to make or become flat
  • antonym: rise - to move upward or increase in height
