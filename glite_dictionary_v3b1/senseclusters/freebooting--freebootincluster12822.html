Examples

Acts of freebooting
During the 17th century, freebooting was a constant threat to merchant ships in the Caribbean.
The novel tells the story of a crew devoted to freebooting along the African coast.

Synonyms

  • piracy
    6.1 1.58/m
    piracy attacks and seizes ships at sea to steal goods, while freebooting also covers general plundering or armed raiding, often in wartime.
  • buccaneering
    9.9 0.03/m
    buccaneering means sailing and raiding ships for profit in old stories, while freebooting is a broader term for plundering.
  • plundering
    6.0 0.21/m
    plundering is stealing valuable things during war or chaos, while freebooting implies violent raiding often at sea or wartime.
  • pillaging
    7.7 0.40/m
    pillaging is violently taking goods from places during war, while freebooting can mean sea-based piracy or armed raiding more generally.
  • looting
    4.9 3.66/m
    looting is stealing goods during riots or disorder in towns, while freebooting suggests organized seaborne or wartime raiding for profit.
