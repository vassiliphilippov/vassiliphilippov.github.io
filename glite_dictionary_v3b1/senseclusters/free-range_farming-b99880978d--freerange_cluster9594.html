free-range farming

noun
method of farming where animals roam freely
Pronunciation: / ˌfriːˈreɪndʒ ˈfɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌfriːˈreɪndʒ ˈfɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
A method of farming in which animals are allowed to roam freely outdoors, rather than being confined in enclosures.

Examples

Free-range farming method
Debates continue about the environmental impact of free-range farming compared to more intensive systems.
Many consumers prefer meat produced through free-range farming due to concerns about animal welfare.
The government introduced incentives to support free-range farming initiatives across the country.

Related Terms

  • antonym: factory farming - intensive animal farming where animals are kept indoors or confined
