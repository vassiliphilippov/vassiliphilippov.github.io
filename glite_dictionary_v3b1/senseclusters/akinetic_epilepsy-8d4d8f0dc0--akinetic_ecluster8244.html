akinetic epilepsy

noun
epilepsy involving loss of movement
7.1 8.2
Pronunciation: /eɪ.kɪ.ˈne.tɪk ˈe.pɪ.ləp.siː/ (AmE), /ə.kɪ.ˈne.tɪk ˈe.pɪ.ləp.siː/ (BrE)
A form of epilepsy characterized by episodes of akinesia, in which the affected person experiences temporary loss of voluntary movement.

Examples

Akinetic epilepsy episode
During an akinetic epilepsy episode, the patient was unable to move for several seconds.
The doctor diagnosed the child with akinetic epilepsy after witnessing an episode of sudden loss of movement.
Treatment options for akinetic epilepsy differ from those used for other types of seizures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: akinesia - loss or impairment of voluntary movement
  • synonym: epilepsy - a neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent episodes of seizures
