rain shadow

noun
area with low rainfall caused by a mountain barrier
7.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /reɪn ˈʃæ.doʊ/ (AmE), /reɪn ˈʃæ.dəʊ/ (BrE)
an area having little rainfall because it is sheltered from prevailing rain-bearing winds by a range of hills or mountains

Examples

A rain shadow area
Farmers struggle to cultivate crops in the rain shadow region due to the lack of precipitation.
The Andes produce a dramatic rain shadow on their eastern side, influencing the local climate.
The desert lies in a rain shadow created by the surrounding mountains.
