smack

verb
have a suggestion of (often negative)
1.0
Pronunciation: / smæk / (AmE), / smæk / (BrE)
to have or suggest a strong or distinctive taste, flavor, or quality (often undesirable)

Examples

To smack of dishonesty
His apology smacks of insincerity, given his history.
The proposal smacked of favoritism and unfairness.
Their comments smack of prejudice, even if unintentional.
Her explanation smacks of insincerity, much like her previous apologies.
The stew smacked of garlic and fresh herbs.
The wine smacked of oak and a hint of cherry.
