CD-ROM

noun
computer data storage compact disc
4.2
Pronunciation: / siː diː rɑm / (AmE), / siː diː rɒm / (BrE)
A compact disc used with computers to store large amounts of digital data that cannot be changed by the user.

Examples

A CD-ROM disc
I can't save new files to this CD-ROM because it is read-only.
She found an old encyclopedia on CD-ROM in her office drawer.
The installation files are on a CD-ROM included in the box.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: DVD-ROM - a type of optical disc for computer data storage, similar to CD-ROM but with greater capacity
  • degree lower: compact disc - a disc for storing digital data, especially music or data for computers
