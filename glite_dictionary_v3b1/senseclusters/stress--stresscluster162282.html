stress

noun
emphasis on a syllable or note
Pronunciation: / strɛs / (AmE), / strɛs / (BrE)
the emphasis given to a syllable, word, or musical note, especially by means of greater loudness or higher pitch

Examples

Syllable stress
In the word 'alphabet', the stress falls on the first syllable.
Misplacing the stress can completely change the meaning of a word in English.
The teacher explained how to place stress on certain notes in the melody.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accent - emphasis on a particular syllable or word in speech
  • synonym: intonation - the rise and fall of voice pitch in speech
