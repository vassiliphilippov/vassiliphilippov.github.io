pop out

verb
detach UI element into separate window
5.1
Pronunciation: /pɑːp aʊt/ (AmE), /pɒp aʊt/ (BrE)
(computing) to detach a user-interface element (window, panel, video player, chat) so it appears in a separate, floating window outside the main application frame.

Examples

To pop out a window
You can pop out the video into a separate window for easier viewing.
She popped out the chat panel to follow messages while working on her report.
The browser allows users to pop out tools, making multitasking across screens easier.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dock - to attach a floating window or panel back into the main application frame
  • synonym: open in new window - to launch a content item in a separate browser or application window
  • synonym: undock - to release a panel or window from the main application so it can float independently
