stick

verb
become fixed so as to be hard to move
7.8
Pronunciation: / stɪk / (AmE), / stɪk / (BrE)
to become fixed in one position so as to be difficult to move or open

Examples

The door sticks
His bicycle gears would stick every time he tried to change speed.
In the humid weather, the windows often stick shut.
The drawer tends to stick if you close it too hard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jam - to become or cause to become unmovable through wedging or crowding
  • antonym: unstick - to become freed from being stuck or fixed
