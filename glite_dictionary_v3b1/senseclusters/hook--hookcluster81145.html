hook

noun
sharp bend or curved shape
2.9
Pronunciation: / hʊk / (AmE), / hʊk / (BrE)
A sharp bend or curved shape, especially one that resembles the form of a hook.

Examples

A nose hook
His nose had a slight hook at the tip, giving his face character.
The river forms a natural hook as it winds around the mountain.
The road took a sudden hook to the left after the bridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crook - a bend or curve, especially one that resembles a hook
  • synonym: curve - a smoothly flowing, continuous line or shape that bends
  • antonym: straight - having no bends or curves; not curved
