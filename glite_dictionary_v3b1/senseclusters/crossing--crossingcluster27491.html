crossing

(genetics) The act of breeding different species or varieties of animals or plants to produce hybrids.

Examples

Plant crossing
Scientists monitored the crossing of wheat varieties to improve disease resistance.
Selective crossing has led to many modern breeds of cattle.
The experimental crossing of two rose species resulted in a new, hardier hybrid.

Synonyms

  • hybridization
    6.1 0.62/m
    hybridization is the technical process of creating hybrids in breeding or labs, while crossing names the act of making hybrids.
  • cross
    5.9 8.35/m
    a cross is the result or act of breeding two varieties and can mean the offspring, while crossing focuses on the act itself.
  • crossbreeding
    4.4 0.11/m
    crossbreeding is mixing breeds or varieties to get certain traits in offspring, while crossing simply refers to the breeding act.
  • intercrossing
    7.0 0.01/m
    intercrossing is breeding between different groups or lines to mix genes, while crossing is the general act of producing hybrids.
  • interbreeding
    6.2 0.30/m
    interbreeding is mating between members of different populations or species, often natural, while crossing names the deliberate breeding act.
