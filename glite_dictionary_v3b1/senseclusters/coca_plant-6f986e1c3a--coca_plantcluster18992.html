coca plant

noun
South American shrub; source of cocaine
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.kə plænt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.kə plɑːnt / (BrE)
A South American shrub (Erythroxylum coca) whose leaves are traditionally chewed and are a natural source of cocaine.

Examples

Scientists studied the coca plant to better understand the source of cocaine.
The cultivation of the coca plant is an important economic activity in some Andean regions.
The leaves of the coca plant have been chewed by indigenous peoples for centuries.
