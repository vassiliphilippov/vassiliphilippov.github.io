cynancum

noun
a genus of tropical African and Malagasy lianas
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnæŋkəm / (AmE), / sɪˈnan.kəm / (BrE)
A genus of tropical lianas native to Africa and Madagascar, with greenish or purple flowers and long, smooth pods; roots were formerly used as an emetic.

Examples

A cynancum plant
Botanists discovered a new species of cynancum in the forests of Madagascar.
The climbing stems of cynancum make it a notable feature in tropical landscapes.
The roots of cynancum were once used as an emetic in traditional medicine.
