interpretively

adverb
in an explanatory way
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜrprətɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːprətɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that involves personal explanation or interpretation

Examples

Explained interpretively
The teacher explained the poem interpretively, offering her own insights into its meaning.
He answered the question interpretively, rather than sticking strictly to the facts.
During the discussion, she spoke interpretively about the character's motivations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectively - in a way based on facts rather than feelings or opinions
  • synonym: subjectively - in a way based on personal feelings or opinions
