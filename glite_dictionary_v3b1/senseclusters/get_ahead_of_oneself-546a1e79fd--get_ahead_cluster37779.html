get ahead of oneself

verb
act prematurely, assume future success
1.7 6.6
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt əˈhɛd ʌv wʌnˌsɛlf / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈhɛd ɒv wʌnˌsɛlf / (BrE)
to act prematurely or take actions based on assumptions about future success that has not yet occurred

Examples

To get ahead of oneself quickly
He quickly realized he was getting ahead of himself with all the plans for his new company.
It's important not to get ahead of oneself and celebrate before the results are announced.
New entrepreneurs sometimes get ahead of themselves and make big investments too soon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: count one's chickens before they hatch - to assume something will happen before it actually does
  • synonym: jump the gun - to act too soon, especially without thinking carefully
