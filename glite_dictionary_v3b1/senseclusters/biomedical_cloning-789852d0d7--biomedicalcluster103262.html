biomedical cloning

noun
cloning for medical treatment using patient cells
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈmɛd.ɪ.kəl ˈkloʊ.nɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈmed.ɪ.kəl ˈkləʊ.nɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of creating genetically identical cells or organisms, especially by nuclear transplantation of a patient's own cells to produce immune-compatible stem cells for medical use

Examples

Biomedical cloning of cells
Biomedical cloning is being studied as a potential treatment for degenerative diseases such as Parkinson's.
Some ethical debates center on the use of biomedical cloning for creating stem cells.
The research team achieved a breakthrough in biomedical cloning using the patient's own tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reproductive cloning - cloning aimed at creating a whole new organism
  • synonym: therapeutic cloning - cloning of cells for medical treatment and research
