chiaroscuro technique

noun
artistic use of light and shadow
9.9
Pronunciation: / kiˌɑːrəˈskjʊəroʊ tɛkˈniːk / (AmE), / kiˌɑːrəˈskjʊərəʊ tɛkˈniːk / (BrE)
a method in painting, drawing, or other visual arts that uses strong contrasts between light and dark to create a sense of volume and three-dimensionality.

Examples

Chiaroscuro technique in painting
Many contemporary photographers adapt the chiaroscuro technique to achieve mood and drama in portraits.
The artist's chiaroscuro technique dramatically highlights the expressions on her subjects' faces.
Using chiaroscuro technique, Caravaggio gave his work a remarkable sense of depth.
