Chang Kuo

proper noun
mythical Taoist immortal
Pronunciation: / tʃɑŋ ˈɡwoʊ / (AmE), / tʃɑːŋ ˈɡwəʊ / (BrE)
In Chinese mythology, one of the Eight Immortals of Taoism, often depicted as an elderly man riding a donkey backwards.

Examples

Chang Kuo riding donkey
In the traditional painting, Chang Kuo is shown riding his donkey backwards.
Many legends describe Chang Kuo as possessing supernatural wisdom and eccentric habits.
Stories of Chang Kuo are popular during Taoist festivals in China.
