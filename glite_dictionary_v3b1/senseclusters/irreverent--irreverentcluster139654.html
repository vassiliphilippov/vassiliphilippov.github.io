irreverent

adjective
showing lack of respect
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛvərənt / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvərənt / (BrE)
showing a lack of respect for people or things that are usually respected or taken seriously

Examples

Irreverent attitude
Her irreverent attitude towards authority made her popular among her peers.
Some critics found the artist's irreverent treatment of traditional subjects refreshing.
The comedian's irreverent jokes pushed the boundaries of what the audience expected.
His irreverent attitude toward authority sometimes got him into trouble.
Some found the artist's irreverent take on sacred themes offensive, while others praised her creativity.
The comedian's irreverent jokes often poked fun at traditional customs and beliefs.
Her irreverent comments at the formal dinner raised a few eyebrows.
Many teenagers adopt an irreverent attitude toward authority figures.
The comedian’s irreverent humor challenged social norms and made the audience laugh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impudent - not showing due respect, especially in a rude way
  • antonym: respectful - showing respect or deference
  • antonym: reverent - showing deep respect, especially for something sacred
  • synonym: scornful - showing contempt or disdain
