fallow

verb
to leave land unplanted
Pronunciation: / ˈfæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfæləʊ / (BrE)
to leave land unplanted for a period so that its fertility can be restored

Examples

To fallow a field
Farmers sometimes fallow their fields to improve the soil for future crops.
It is common practice to fallow a section of land every few years.
By choosing to fallow the land, the farmer hoped to restore its nutrients.
Farmers often fallow their fields every few years to maintain healthy soil.
The land was fallowed last season to allow nutrients to return to the earth.
Some agricultural practices recommend fallowing a portion of the land each year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cultivate - to prepare and use land for growing crops
  • synonym: rest - to allow something to pause or recover
