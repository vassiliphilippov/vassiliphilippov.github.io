degrader

noun
person who lowers quality or value
6.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡreɪdər / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdə / (BrE)
a person who lowers the quality, character, or value of something

Examples

A degrader of morals
As the degrader of the coinage, he was quickly stripped of his powers.
Some critics saw the artist as a degrader of tradition, while others called him innovative.
The manager refused to be a degrader of standards just to cut costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debaser - a person who reduces the value or quality, especially of money or art
  • antonym: reformer - a person who improves or restores something to a better state
