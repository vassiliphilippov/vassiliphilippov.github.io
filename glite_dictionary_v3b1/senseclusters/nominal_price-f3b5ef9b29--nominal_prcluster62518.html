nominal price

noun
an estimated or stated price (not always market value)
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːmɪnəl ˌpraɪs / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl ˌpraɪs / (BrE)
an estimated or stated price that does not necessarily reflect the asset's actual market value

Examples

A nominal price offer
During negotiation, the buyer offered a nominal price as a starting point.
The artwork was put up for auction with only a nominal price to attract interest.
The company listed a nominal price for the new gadget before setting the final amount.
