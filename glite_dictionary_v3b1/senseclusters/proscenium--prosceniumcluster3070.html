proscenium

noun
front section of a theater stage
9.1 0.31/m
Pronunciation: / pro äÀàsiÀêni…ôm / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàsiÀêni…ôm / (BrE)
the part of a theater stage in front of the curtain, or the arch or wall that separates the stage from the audience

Examples

The proscenium arch
A grand arch framed the proscenium in the old opera house.
Renovations included restoring the ornate details of the proscenium.
The actors emerged onto the proscenium to take their final bow.
During the performance, the lighting on the proscenium was especially dramatic.
She prefers theaters with a traditional proscenium rather than a thrust stage.
The actors stepped forward onto the proscenium to take their final bows.

Synonyms

  • proscenium arch
    8.6 0.05/m
    proscenium arch is a curved frame over the stage opening that viewers see, while proscenium is the stage front or separating structure.
  • proscenium wall
    9.4 0.00/m
    proscenium wall is the solid wall that holds the stage opening and arch, while proscenium is the front that divides audience from stage.
  • forestage
    9.0 0.00/m
    forestage is the part of a stage that projects forward toward the audience, while proscenium refers to the stage front or separating arch.
  • apron
    7.3 1.04/m
    apron is a small area in front of a stage used by actors and crew, while proscenium is the stage front or arch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apron - the part of a theater stage that extends past the proscenium arch toward the audience
