spoil

verb
to plunder and strip of possessions
4.1 6.1
Pronunciation: /spɔɪl/ (AmE), /spɔɪl/ (BrE)
(of soldiers or invaders) to plunder, destroy, and strip (a place) of its possessions, especially during war.

Examples

To spoil a city
For centuries, raiders would spoil villages along the coast, taking valuables and burning homes.
The invading army spoiled the city, leaving nothing but ruins behind.
They feared the conquerors would spoil their homeland, stripping it of everything precious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pillage - to rob a place using violence, especially in wartime
  • synonym: plunder - to steal goods from a place or person, typically using force and in a time of war or civil disorder
  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
