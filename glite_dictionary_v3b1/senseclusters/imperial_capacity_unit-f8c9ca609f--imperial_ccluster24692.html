Imperial capacity unit

noun
volume unit in British Imperial System
4.2 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpɪr.iː.əl kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.tiː ˈjuː.nɪt/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpɪə.riː.əl kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.tiː ˈjuː.nɪt/ (BrE)
a unit of volume measurement defined by the British Imperial System, encompassing both liquid and dry quantities, such as the gallon, pint, or quart

Examples

An Imperial capacity unit gallon
The Imperial capacity unit of a pint differs from the US measurement.
The brewery reports its production in Imperial capacity units such as barrels and gallons.
When buying bulk liquids in the UK, quantities are often given in Imperial capacity units.
