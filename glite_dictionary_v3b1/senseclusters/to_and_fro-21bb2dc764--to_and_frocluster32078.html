to and fro

adverb
back and forth motion
3.4 1.03/m
Pronunciation: / tu …ôn fro ä /; / tu …ônd fro ä / (AmE), / t…ô …ôn fr…ô ä /; / tuÀê …ôn Ààfr…ô ä / (BrE)
moving back and forth repeatedly, or from one place to another and back again

Examples

Move to and fro
Children were running to and fro in the playground during recess.
She paced to and fro across the room, deep in thought.
The clock's pendulum swung to and fro in perfect rhythm.
After hours of pacing to and fro, he finally made his decision.
She watched the dancers glide to and fro across the floor.
The old swing moved to and fro in the gentle breeze.

Synonyms

  • back and forth
    2.0 28/m
    back and forth is the common everyday phrase for repeated movement, used in speech and writing, while to and fro feels older and more literary.
  • backwards and forwards
    3.2 0.13/m
    backwards and forwards is common in British English and can describe debate or movement, while to and fro sounds more poetic and old-fashioned.

Related Terms

  • synonym: back and forth - moving first in one direction and then in the opposite; alternating motion
