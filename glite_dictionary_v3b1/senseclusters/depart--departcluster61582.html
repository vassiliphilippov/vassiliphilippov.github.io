depart

verb
to leave or go away

to leave a place, position, or association, especially to start a journey or move on

Examples

To depart the station
After the meeting, everyone departed in different directions.
She had to depart early to catch her flight.
The train will depart from platform 4 at 8 o'clock.
All guests must depart the building by closing time.
She watched the ship depart slowly from the harbor.
The last train will depart from this platform at midnight.
Many students depart for home at the end of the semester.
The train departed from the station right on schedule.
We will depart for Paris early in the morning to avoid traffic.

Synonyms

  • leave
    1.0 507/m
    leave is to go away from a place, person, or group, while depart often suggests starting a journey or moving on.
  • set out
    2.4 6.66/m
    set out means to begin a journey and leave a place, while depart covers leaving in many situations, formal or informal.
  • set off
    2.4 4.05/m
    set off means to start traveling toward a place, while depart can mean leave generally or begin a longer journey.
  • start out
    2.5 2.44/m
    start out means to begin a journey or career with a first step, while depart focuses on leaving a place or role.
  • set forth
    3.9 0.38/m
    set forth is a formal way to begin a journey or explain ideas, while depart is more common for leaving a place.
  • go away
    1.0 17/m
    go away means to leave a place or person quickly or casually, while depart often sounds more formal or planned.
  • take leave
    3.9 0.20/m
    take leave means to say goodbye or excuse yourself formally before leaving, while depart simply means to go away or move on.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey
  • antonym: join - to become a member or part of an association; opposite action to depart
  • synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • synonym: resign - to leave a job or official position formally
