depart

verb
to leave or go away
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpɑrt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to leave a place, position, or association, especially to start a journey or move on

Examples

To depart the station
After the meeting, everyone departed in different directions.
She had to depart early to catch her flight.
The train will depart from platform 4 at 8 o'clock.
All guests must depart the building by closing time.
She watched the ship depart slowly from the harbor.
The last train will depart from this platform at midnight.
Many students depart for home at the end of the semester.
The train departed from the station right on schedule.
We will depart for Paris early in the morning to avoid traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey
  • antonym: join - to become a member or part of an association; opposite action to depart
  • synonym: leave - to go away from a place or person
  • synonym: resign - to leave a job or official position formally
