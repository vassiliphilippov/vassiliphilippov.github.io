cervical artery

noun
artery supplying the neck muscles
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.vɪ.kəl ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪ.kəl ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the muscles of the neck

Examples

Cervical artery blockage
Blockage of a cervical artery may cause localized muscle weakness.
Injury to the cervical artery can lead to reduced blood flow in the neck muscles.
Surgeons must identify the cervical artery during complex neck procedures.
