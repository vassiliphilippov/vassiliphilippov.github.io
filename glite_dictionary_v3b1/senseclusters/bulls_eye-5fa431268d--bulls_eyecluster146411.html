bull's eye

noun
a perfect hit or decisive success
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlz aɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlz aɪ / (BrE)
a decisive or perfectly accurate success in achieving an aim or goal; the exact target or center point hit

Examples

A bull's eye result
Her innovative solution to the problem was a real bull's eye for the team.
The marketing campaign hit a bull's eye with younger consumers.
You scored a bull's eye with that answer during the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hit the mark - to achieve exactly what was intended
  • antonym: miss the mark - to fail to achieve the intended result
