welted thistle

noun
a European biennial plant with clustered flower heads
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛltɪd ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈwɛltɪd ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A European biennial weed with flower heads in crowded clusters at the ends of branches, introduced in North America.

Examples

The welted thistle plant
Farmers sometimes struggle to control the spread of welted thistle in their fields.
In early summer, the welted thistle produces distinctive clusters of purple flower heads.
The welted thistle can often be found growing along rural roadsides in North America.
