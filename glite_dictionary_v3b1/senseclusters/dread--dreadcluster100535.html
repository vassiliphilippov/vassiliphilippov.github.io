dread

verb
to fear or be deeply afraid of
Pronunciation: / drɛd / (AmE), / drɛd / (BrE)
to feel great fear or apprehension about something

Examples

To dread the dentist
I always dread going to the dentist, even for a simple checkup.
Many students dread taking final exams at the end of the semester.
She dreaded the thought of moving to a new city alone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticipate - to expect or look forward to (normally with a positive attitude, but can be negative)
  • synonym: fear - to be afraid of something or someone
