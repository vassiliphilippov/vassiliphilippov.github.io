untrue

adjective
not true; not factual
2.2 4.56/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàtru / (AmE), /  ånÀàtruÀê / (BrE)
not in accordance with the facts; not true or correct.

Examples

An untrue statement
He admitted that his earlier claim was completely untrue.
She was hurt when she heard the untrue rumors circulating about her.
The report was later found to be untrue by independent investigators.

Synonyms

  • false
    1.0 41/m
    false is factually wrong or misleading in statement or claim, while untrue means not in accordance with the facts.
  • unfactual
    6.1 0.02/m
    unfactual is not based on facts and sounds formal or journalistic, while untrue means not in accordance with the facts.
  • incorrect
    1.4 17/m
    incorrect describes an answer that contains errors or breaks rules, while untrue refers to things not in accordance with facts.
  • wrong
    1.0 64/m
    wrong feels more general and common for mistakes or bad choices, while untrue means not in accordance with the facts.
