John Huss

proper noun
Czech religious reformer, precursor to Reformation
9.5
Pronunciation: /dʒɑːn hʌs/ (AmE), /dʒɒn hʌs/ (BrE)
(c. 1372–1415) A Czech religious reformer who criticized the Catholic Church's authorities and practices, anticipating the Protestant Reformation; he was excommunicated and burned at the stake for heresy.

Examples

John Huss reformer
At university, students often study the works and legacy of John Huss in religious history classes.
Many historians credit John Huss as a key precursor to the Protestant Reformation.
The execution of John Huss sparked outrage and inspired later reformers.
