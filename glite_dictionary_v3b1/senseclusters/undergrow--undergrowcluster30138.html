undergrow

verb
grow beneath something else
9.8 7.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈgroʊ/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈgrəʊ/ (BrE)
(of a plant or vegetation) to grow beneath something else, such as the ground or another object.

Examples

To undergrow beneath plants
Creeping ivy began to undergrow the ancient wall, adding to its mysterious appearance.
In spring, delicate ferns often undergrow the branches of taller trees in the forest.
Wildflowers undergrow the hedges, creating a colorful border along the path.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overgrow - to grow over something, especially excessively
