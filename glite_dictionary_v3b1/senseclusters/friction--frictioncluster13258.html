friction

noun
conflict or tension between people
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən / (BrE)
tension, conflict, or disagreement between people or groups

Examples

Team friction
Despite initial friction, the team eventually learned to work together.
Personal differences can create friction in a workplace environment.
There was a great deal of friction between the two departments over the new policy.
Efforts to resolve the friction in their relationship finally succeeded.
Misunderstandings had caused friction between the two friends.
There was increasing friction in the office as deadlines approached.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • antonym: harmony - agreement; absence of conflict
