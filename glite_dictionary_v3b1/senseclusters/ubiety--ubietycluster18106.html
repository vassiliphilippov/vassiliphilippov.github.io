ubiety

noun
the state of being in a particular place
Pronunciation: / juːˈbaɪəti / (AmE), / juːˈbaɪəti / (BrE)
the condition or quality of being located in a specific place; the state of existing in a particular location

Examples

The ubiety of objects
In metaphysics, ubiety helps to explain how entities occupy space.
Quantum mechanics challenges the classical notion of ubiety by introducing uncertainty about location.
The philosopher argued that material objects possess ubiety, distinguishing them from abstract concepts.
