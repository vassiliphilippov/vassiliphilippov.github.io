vocalize

verb
to express or state clearly
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈvoʊ.kə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈvəʊ.kə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to express something or produce sounds using the voice, especially by speaking or singing

Examples

To vocalize an idea
During the discussion, students were encouraged to vocalize their opinions.
He rarely vocalizes his true feelings to anyone.
She was not afraid to vocalize her concerns during the meeting.
He struggled to vocalize his thoughts after the shocking news.
Many birds vocalize to communicate with each other in the wild.
While reading aloud to the children, she would carefully vocalize each word.
Infants gradually learn to vocalize as they develop language skills.
Some birds are able to vocalize a wide range of complex sounds.
The instructor encouraged the students to vocalize their thoughts clearly during the discussion.
