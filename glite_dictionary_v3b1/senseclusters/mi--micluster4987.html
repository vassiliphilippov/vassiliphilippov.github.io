mi

noun
former British unit of length (nautical mile)
8.1
Pronunciation: / maɪ / (AmE), / maɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for nautical mile, a unit of length used in navigation (about 1,852 meters)

Examples

10 mi at sea
One mi at sea was longer than a land mile.
The old charts are marked in mi rather than kilometers.
The ship covered 200 mi before it reached the coast.
A speed of 15 knots means the boat travels 15 mi per hour.
The ship sailed 200 mi across the open ocean before reaching the coast.
The storm was reported to be 60 mi offshore last night.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: mile - a unit of length equal to 1,609 meters
  • synonym: nautical mile - a unit of length used in navigation, equal to 1,852 meters
  • antonym: statute mile - a unit of length equal to 5,280 feet (1,609.344 meters)
