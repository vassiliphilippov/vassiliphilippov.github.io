cut back

verb
prune or trim plants
3.1 3.2
Pronunciation: /kʌt bæk/ (AmE), /kʌt bæk/ (BrE)
to trim or prune the growth of plants to encourage healthy development or control their size

Examples

To cut back plants
Gardeners often cut back perennials after they finish flowering to promote new growth.
In early spring, it’s a good idea to cut back your rose bushes for healthier blooms.
You should cut back any dead or damaged branches to keep the plant looking tidy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prune - to trim a tree, shrub, or bush by cutting away dead or overgrown branches or stems
  • synonym: trim - to make neat or smaller by cutting away unwanted parts, especially in gardening
