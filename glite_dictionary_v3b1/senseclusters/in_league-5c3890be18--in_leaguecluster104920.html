in league

adjective
acting together, often secretly or for a dishonest purpose
Pronunciation: / ɪn liːɡ / (AmE), / ɪn liːɡ / (BrE)
acting together, especially secretly or for a dishonest purpose; allied or in cooperation (usually with someone)

Examples

Be in league together
Many believe the two companies are in league to fix prices.
She suspected that her coworkers were in league with her competitor.
The politician was accused of being in league with organized crime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: at odds - in conflict or disagreement
  • synonym: in cahoots - colluding or conspiring together, often secretly
