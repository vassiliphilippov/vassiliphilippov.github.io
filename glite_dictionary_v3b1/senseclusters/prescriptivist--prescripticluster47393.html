prescriptivist

noun
supporter of strict rules
8.7
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈskrɪp.tɪ.vɪst/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈskrɪp.tɪ.vɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes in and enforces strict rules or standards, especially in language or grammar

Examples

A prescriptivist teacher
The prescriptivist argued that only standard grammar should be taught in schools.
As a prescriptivist, he believed there was a correct way to behave at formal events.
Some people see the prescriptivist approach as too rigid for modern education.
As a strict prescriptivist, she corrected every grammatical mistake she heard.
The debate between the prescriptivist and the descriptivist was lively and informative.
Some teachers take a prescriptivist approach to teaching English grammar in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptivist - a person who describes and observes practices without enforcing rules
  • synonym: grammarian - an expert in or student of grammar
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports established customs and practices
