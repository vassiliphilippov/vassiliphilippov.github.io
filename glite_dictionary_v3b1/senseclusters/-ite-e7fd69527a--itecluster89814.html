-ite

x
mineral/compound suffix
Pronunciation: / aɪt / (AmE), / aɪt / (BrE)
a suffix used to form the names of minerals or chemical compounds, often indicating a salt or derivative

Examples

Malachite mineral
The mineral malachite ends with the suffix -ite.
Many chemical compounds, such as sulfite, use the suffix -ite to indicate a specific form.
Geologists often identify minerals by their names ending in -ite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -ate - a suffix used in chemistry to indicate a salt or ester of an acid
