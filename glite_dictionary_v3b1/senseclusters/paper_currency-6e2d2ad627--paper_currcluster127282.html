paper currency

noun
money in printed note form
2.5 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpe…™p…ö Ààk…úÀêr…ônsi / (AmE), / Ààpe…™p…ô Ààk år…ônsi / (BrE)
money in the form of printed notes, issued by a government or central bank, and used as a substitute for coins

Examples

Printed paper currency
Many countries have replaced coins with paper currency for higher denominations.
Some collectors value rare paper currency that is no longer in circulation.
The economic crisis led people to lose trust in their paper currency.

Synonyms

  • paper money
    1.6 0.83/m
    paper money is the everyday name people use for printed notes, while paper currency is the formal term used by banks and governments.
  • banknote
    3.0 1.01/m
    banknote is the formal banking name for a printed note used as legal money, while paper currency is the broader everyday term.
  • bill
    1.5 15/m
    a bill is the common word for a single paper note in everyday speech, while paper currency sounds more formal and general.
  • folding money
    3.2 0.03/m
    folding money is an informal phrase for cash you can fold and carry, while paper currency is the neutral official term.
  • bank bill
    3.2 0.01/m
    a bank bill is often a specific banking instrument or older term, while paper currency usually means general printed legal notes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banknote - a piece of paper money issued by a bank
  • antonym: specie - money in the form of coins rather than notes
