unredeemably

adverb
beyond redemption
9.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.rɪ.ˈdiː.mə.blɪ/ (AmE), /ʌn.rɪ.ˈdiː.mə.blɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that cannot be saved, improved, or made acceptable; beyond hope of being made better

Examples

An unredeemably dull film
The film was unredeemably dull, with no interesting characters or plot twists.
He acted unredeemably selfish throughout the entire negotiation, refusing to compromise.
Some mistakes are seen as unredeemably damaging to a person's reputation.
The project was unredeemably behind schedule and could not be completed on time.
His actions were considered unredeemably selfish by everyone involved.
The old building was unredeemably damaged after the fire and had to be demolished.
