ragtag

adjective
disorganized group
5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈræɡ.tæɡ/ (AmE), /ˈræɡ.tæɡ/ (BrE)
disorganized, diverse, and often untidy or poorly dressed (used to describe a group of people or things that do not match or are not orderly)

Examples

A ragtag team
A ragtag team of students managed to win the competition against all odds.
The ragtag collection of old chairs and tables gave the cafe a unique charm.
Despite being a ragtag group, they worked together to finish the project on time.
A ragtag group of musicians played lively tunes on the street corner.
The team looked ragtag but played with incredible determination and spirit.
After the long journey, their clothes were ragtag and their faces tired.
