chip off the old block

noun
person similar to a parent
Pronunciation: / tʃɪp ɑf ði oʊld blɑk / (AmE), / tʃɪp ɒf ði əʊld blɒk / (BrE)
a person who closely resembles one of their parents in appearance, character, or abilities

Examples

A chip off the old block
Everyone says Jake is a chip off the old block because he shares his dad’s sense of humor.
Maria’s athletic skills show she is truly a chip off the old block.
When Greg started his own business at twenty, people called him a chip off the old block.

Related Terms

  • synonym: like father, like son - used to express that a son is similar to his father
