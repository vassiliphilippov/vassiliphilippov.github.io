manifoldness

noun
property of being a manifold
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪˌfoʊldnəs / (AmE), / ˈmænɪfəʊldnəs / (BrE)
the property of a space or object being a manifold, where each point has a neighborhood similar to Euclidean space of a fixed dimension

Examples

Mathematical manifoldness
The manifoldness of the surface allows mathematicians to apply calculus in higher dimensions.
Understanding the manifoldness of the space is crucial for advanced geometry.
Researchers discussed the manifoldness of the new topological model at the conference.

Related Terms
