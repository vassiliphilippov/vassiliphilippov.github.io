sludgy

adjective
thick and muddy
4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈslʌdʒ.iː/ /; / /ˈslʌdʒ.ɪ/ (AmE), /ˈslʌdʒ.iː/ /; / /ˈslʌdʒ.ɪ/ (BrE)
thick, soft, and wet like mud or sludge

Examples

Sludgy ground
After the heavy rain, the field became sludgy and difficult to walk across.
The soup was supposed to be smooth, but it turned out sludgy and unappetizing.
His boots were covered in sludgy dirt after hiking through the wet forest.
After the storm, the path became sludgy and difficult to walk on.
The soup turned sludgy after simmering for too long without stirring.
Her boots were covered in sludgy mud from the construction site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muddy - covered with or full of mud
  • degree higher: thick - having a relatively large distance between opposite sides; not thin or runny
  • degree lower: thin - having little thickness or density; not thick
  • antonym: watery - thin and containing a lot of water
