XOR gate

noun
logic circuit for exclusive OR function
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɛks ɔːr ɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛks ɔː ɡeɪt / (BrE)
A logic circuit that outputs a high signal only when an odd number of its inputs are high, implementing the exclusive OR function.

Examples

An XOR gate circuit
By connecting two inputs to an XOR gate, you can check if they are different.
Many computer circuits rely on the XOR gate to perform logical operations efficiently.
The XOR gate is often used in digital adders to sum binary numbers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: AND gate - a logic circuit that outputs high only when all inputs are high
  • synonym: OR gate - a logic circuit that outputs high if any input is high
  • antonym: XNOR gate - a logic circuit that outputs high only when its inputs are the same
