undistinctive

adjective
not distinguishing
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪˈstɪŋktɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˈstɪŋktɪv / (BrE)
not serving to distinguish between sounds, words, or forms; lacking features that make something different or unique.

Examples

Undistinctive sounds
In this language, the vowel length is undistinctive and does not change the meaning of words.
The artist's style was considered undistinctive compared to her more innovative peers.
Because the sound is undistinctive, listeners often cannot tell the words apart in rapid speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distinctive - serving to distinguish or set apart
  • synonym: non-contrastive - not making a difference in meaning or function
