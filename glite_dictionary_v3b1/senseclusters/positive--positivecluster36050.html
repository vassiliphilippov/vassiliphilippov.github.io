positive

adjective
formally established by authority (esp. law)
1.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.zə.tɪv/ /; / /ˈpɑː.zɪ.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈpɒ.zɪ.tɪv/ /; / /ˈpɒ.zə.tɪv/ (BrE)
(of a law, rule, or institution) formally laid down or imposed; prescribed by human authority rather than derived from natural principles.

Examples

Positive law
Debates about justice often contrast natural law with positive law.
The country’s constitution is a set of positive laws enacted by its government.
Unlike common moral codes, positive laws are created and enforced by official authorities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural - (of law, etc.) arising from nature or reason, not written by human lawmakers
