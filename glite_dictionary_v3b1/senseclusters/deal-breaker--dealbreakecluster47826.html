deal-breaker

adjective
causing rejection
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːlˌbreɪkər / (AmE), / ˈdiːlˌbreɪkə / (BrE)
causing the failure or rejection of an agreement, plan, or relationship, especially when used before a noun

Examples

Deal-breaker condition
The company refused to accept the deal-breaker condition in the contract.
For many buyers, a house without parking is a deal-breaker issue.
The deal-breaker terms made it impossible for the two sides to reach an agreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critical - extremely important or essential
  • synonym: decisive - having the power to decide the outcome
  • antonym: minor - not very important or serious
