reproducibility

noun
ability to be reproduced, especially in experiments
7.2 5.0
Pronunciation: /riː.prə.dʌk.sə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /riː.prə.dʌk.sə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /riː.prə.dʌk.sə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /riː.prə.dʌk.sə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to be repeated or reproduced with the same results, especially in science or computing

Examples

Scientific reproducibility
Ensuring reproducibility is considered essential in scientific research.
Improvements in methodology have increased the reproducibility of clinical trials.
The team questioned the reproducibility of the published results after failing to obtain the same data.
Journals now require reproducibility by sharing code, data, and containerized environments.
The lab improved reproducibility by freezing software versions and using Docker containers for analysis.
Computational reproducibility ensures that the same script and inputs yield identical outputs across machines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repeatability - the ability for an experiment or process to yield the same results when repeated under identical conditions
  • synonym: replicability - the related ability to obtain consistent findings using independent data, methods, or implementations; often contrasted with computational reproducibility
