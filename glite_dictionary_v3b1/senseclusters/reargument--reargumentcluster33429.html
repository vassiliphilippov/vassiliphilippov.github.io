reargument

noun
repeat of arguments
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
a renewed or repeated presentation of arguments, especially in a legal context or court case

Examples

A reargument in court
The court scheduled a reargument after new evidence was submitted by the defense.
Following the initial verdict, the lawyers requested a reargument to clarify key points.
A reargument can sometimes change the outcome of a complex legal case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appeal - a request for a higher court to review a decision
  • degree lower: argument - a reason or set of reasons given to persuade others
