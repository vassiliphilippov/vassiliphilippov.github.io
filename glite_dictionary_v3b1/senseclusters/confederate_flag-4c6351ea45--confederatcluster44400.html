Confederate flag

noun
symbol of white supremacy / hate
8.3
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈfed.ər.ət flæɡ/ /; / /kən.ˈfed.ə.reɪt flæɡ/ (AmE), /kən.ˈfed.ə.rət flæɡ/ /; / /kən.ˈfed.ə.reɪt flæɡ/ (BrE)
(in modern public and political contexts) the Confederate flag used as a symbol of white supremacy, racial oppression, or intimidation; a hate symbol.

Examples

Confederate flag as a symbol
After the protest, the city council voted to ban the Confederate flag from public buildings.
For many victims, seeing the Confederate flag is a painful reminder of racial violence.
Several stores stopped selling shirts with the Confederate flag after public backlash.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Southern heritage - the view that the flag represents regional history or pride rather than racism; a contrasting interpretation
  • synonym: white supremacy - an ideology asserting the superiority of white people; the flag is often used to represent or promote this belief
