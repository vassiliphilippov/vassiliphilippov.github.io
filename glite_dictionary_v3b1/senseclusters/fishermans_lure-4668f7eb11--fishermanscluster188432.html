fisherman's lure

noun
artificial fishing bait with hooks and feathers
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃərmənz lʊr / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃəmənz lʊə / (BrE)
a brightly colored artificial bait, often made of plastic or metal and decorated with feathers, used in fishing to attract fish

Examples

He tied a new fisherman's lure to the end of his line before casting into the river.
Some anglers prefer using a brightly colored fisherman's lure instead of live bait.
The tackle box was filled with every kind of fisherman's lure imaginable.
