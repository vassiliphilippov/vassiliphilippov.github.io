potholing

noun
utility excavation method
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊthoʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɒthəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of digging small, controlled holes to locate or inspect underground pipes, cables, or utilities

Examples

Potholing for cables
The construction team used potholing to safely identify the location of underground cables before digging.
Proper potholing can prevent accidental damage to water or gas lines during roadwork.
Before starting the main excavation, workers performed potholing to check for hidden pipes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excavation - the process of digging or removing earth
  • synonym: trenching - digging a long, narrow ditch for utilities or drainage
