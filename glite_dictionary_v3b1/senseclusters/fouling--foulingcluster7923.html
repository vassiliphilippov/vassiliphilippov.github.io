fouling

noun
unwanted buildup
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
the accumulation of unwanted material or being tangled, which blocks, dirties, or damages a surface, system, or equipment

Examples

Ship hull fouling
The ship's hull suffered from fouling due to algae and barnacles.
Engine fouling can reduce performance and increase maintenance costs.
Regular cleaning helps prevent fouling in water filtration systems.
The ship's propeller stopped working due to fouling by seaweed and debris.
Regular maintenance helps prevent fouling in industrial pipes and machinery.
Divers checked the anchor chain for any fouling before raising it from the seabed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blockage - something that stops movement or flow
  • antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean and free from dirt
  • antonym: clearance - the removal of an obstruction
  • synonym: contamination - the presence of something harmful or unwanted in a place
  • synonym: entanglement - the state of being twisted together or caught in something
