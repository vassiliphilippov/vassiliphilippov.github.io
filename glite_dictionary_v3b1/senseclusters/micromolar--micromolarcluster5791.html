micromolar

noun
concentration unit
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈmoʊlər / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈməʊlə / (BrE)
a unit of concentration equal to one micromole per liter, used to measure the amount of a substance in a solution

Examples

10 micromolar
The scientist prepared a solution with a final concentration of 10 micromolar.
Enzyme activity was measured at various micromolar concentrations of the inhibitor.
The drug was effective at just one micromolar in laboratory tests.
