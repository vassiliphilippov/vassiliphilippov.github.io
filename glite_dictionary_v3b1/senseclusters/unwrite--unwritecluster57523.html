unwrite

verb
to erase writing
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈraɪt / (AmE), / ʌnˈraɪt / (BrE)
to remove, erase, or undo something that has been written or recorded

Examples

To unwrite a message
Once you send an email, you cannot unwrite what you have said.
The author wished she could unwrite the ending of her novel after reading the reviews.
Mistakes in a digital document are easy to unwrite with the delete key.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erase - to remove written or recorded information
  • antonym: write - to form letters, words, or symbols on a surface
