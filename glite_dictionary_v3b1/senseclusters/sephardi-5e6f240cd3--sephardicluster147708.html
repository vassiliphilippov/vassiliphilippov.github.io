Sephardi

noun
Jew of Spanish, Portuguese, or North African descent
9.1
Pronunciation: / səˈfɑrdi / (AmE), / sɪˈfɑːdi / (BrE)
A Jew of Spanish, Portuguese, or North African descent, particularly one whose ancestors were expelled from Spain or Portugal in the late 15th century.

Examples

Sephardi Jewish community
His family identifies as Sephardi, tracing their roots to Morocco.
Many Sephardi traditions differ from those of Ashkenazi communities.
The synagogue was founded by a group of Sephardi Jews fleeing persecution in Spain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Ashkenazi - a Jew of central or eastern European descent
