innest

adjective
most inward
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnɪst / (AmE), / ˈɪnɪst / (BrE)
most inward; located farthest inside or deepest within something (archaic or poetic)

Examples

Innest chamber
The treasure was hidden in the innest chamber of the ancient temple.
She kept her most precious memories in the innest part of her heart.
He placed the photograph in the innest drawer, away from prying eyes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outermost - furthest from the center; most external
