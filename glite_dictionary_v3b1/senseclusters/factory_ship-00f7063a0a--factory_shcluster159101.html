factory ship

noun
ship for processing catches at sea
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tɚ ˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfæk.tə ʃɪp / (BrE)
A large ship equipped with facilities to process and store fish or whales at sea.

Examples

A factory ship at sea
After the ban on commercial whaling, most factory ships were converted for other uses.
Modern fisheries use factory ships to freeze and can fish directly after catching them.
The factory ship stayed at sea for months, processing whales caught by smaller boats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whaling ship - a ship used in the act of hunting whales
