rev up

verb
to speed up or intensify activity
5.7
Pronunciation: / rɛv ʌp / (AmE), / rɛv ʌp / (BrE)
to increase the speed, activity, or intensity of something; to make something go faster or more energetic

Examples

To rev up activity
As the holiday season approached, retailers began to rev up their advertising campaigns.
The coach tried to rev up the team before the big match.
We need to rev up production to meet the deadline.
Marketing efforts were revved up ahead of the product launch.
She tried to rev up the audience with an inspiring speech.
The company plans to rev up production to meet high demand this season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accelerate - to begin to move more quickly; to increase speed
  • antonym: decelerate - to reduce speed; to slow down
  • synonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
