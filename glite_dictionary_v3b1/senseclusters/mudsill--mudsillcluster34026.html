mudsill

noun
lowest social class
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌsɪl / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌsɪl / (BrE)
a social class or group considered the lowest, providing labor or support for higher classes, often used figuratively

Examples

Mudsill class
Some historians argue that the working class was treated as the mudsill of nineteenth-century society.
The politician's speech referred to immigrants as the economic mudsill supporting the nation's prosperity.
Debates about social mobility often mention the struggles faced by those in the mudsill of the class system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elite - the group considered to be the best or most powerful in a society
  • synonym: underclass - the lowest social or economic class in a society
