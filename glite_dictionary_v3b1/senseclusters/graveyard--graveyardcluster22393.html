graveyard

noun
place for burying the dead; cemetery
1.8 5.46/m

An area of land, often beside a church, where people are buried after they die; a cemetery.

Examples

The graveyard behind church
After the funeral, they walked slowly to the family graveyard behind the church.
Many ghost stories are set in a spooky graveyard at midnight.
The old graveyard was shaded by ancient oak trees and covered in wildflowers.

Synonyms

  • cemetery
    2.8 17/m
    cemetery is a planned, often larger burial place used by communities, while graveyard often suggests a churchyard and older burials.
  • burial ground
    3.9 0.80/m
    burial ground is a formal name for land used for burials, while graveyard often implies land beside a church or older site.
  • burying ground
    6.1 0.04/m
    burying ground is an older-sounding phrase for a place where people are buried, while graveyard usually refers to land by a church.
