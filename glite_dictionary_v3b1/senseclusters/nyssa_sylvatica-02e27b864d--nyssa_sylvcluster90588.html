Nyssa sylvatica

noun
North American deciduous tree with colorful fall leaves
Pronunciation: / ˌnɪsə sɪlˈvætɪkə / (AmE), / ˌnɪsə sɪlˈvætɪkə / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its columnar shape, horizontal branches, small late-emerging leaves, and brilliant fall coloration.
