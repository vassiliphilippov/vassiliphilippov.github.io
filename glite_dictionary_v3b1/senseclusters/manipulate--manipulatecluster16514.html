manipulate

verb
change or control dishonestly
3.2 5.0
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈnɪp.jə.leɪt/ /; / /mə.ˈnɪp.jʊ.leɪt/ (AmE), /mə.ˈnɪp.jʊ.leɪt/ /; / /mə.ˈnɪp.jə.leɪt/ (BrE)
to control or influence someone or something, often in a clever or dishonest way

Examples

To manipulate the truth
Investigators found evidence that the company had manipulated sales records to deceive shareholders.

The accountant tried to manipulate the financial statements to hide the losses.
He skillfully manipulated public opinion to achieve his goals.

The company was fined for attempting to manipulate the financial markets.
Traders who manipulate stock prices can face serious legal consequences.

  • synonym: fabricate - to invent or concoct something, often with the intent to deceive
  • synonym: falsify - to alter information or evidence so as to mislead
  • synonym: fix - to dishonestly arrange or influence the outcome of something
  • degree lower: guide - to direct or influence someone's actions, often neutrally
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
