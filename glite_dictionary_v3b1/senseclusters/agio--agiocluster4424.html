agio

noun
fee for exchanging currencies
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒioʊ / (AmE), / ˈadʒiəʊ / (BrE)
A fee or percentage charged for the exchange of one currency for another, especially when currencies differ in value or form.

Examples

An agio fee
Banks may charge an agio when you exchange dollars for euros.
Due to fluctuating rates, the agio can vary from one exchange office to another.
The agio on this transaction made the total cost higher than expected.
