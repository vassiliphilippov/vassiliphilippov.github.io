titled

adjective
having a noble or aristocratic title
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtld / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtld / (BrE)
(of a person) having an official title, especially of nobility or aristocracy.

Examples

A titled noble
Despite being titled, the baroness preferred a simple country life.
His grandmother is a titled member of the British aristocracy.
Several titled guests attended the exclusive ball at the palace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: untitled - not holding a noble or official title
