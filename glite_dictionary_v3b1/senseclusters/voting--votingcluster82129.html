voting

noun
making a choice by casting votes
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of making a choice or decision by expressing a preference among alternatives, often by submitting a ballot or raising a hand, with the outcome determined by counting the expressed preferences

Examples

The voting process
After several hours of voting, the committee finally reached a decision.
Electronic voting has become increasingly common in national elections.
The voting on the proposal will begin at 9 a.m. sharp tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstention - the act of deliberately not voting
  • synonym: election - the process of selecting someone or something by voting
