macroevolutionary

adjective
large-scale evolutionary
8.9
Pronunciation: /mæk.roʊ.iː.və.ˈluː.ʃə.riː/ /; / /mæk.roʊ.e.və.ˈluː.ʃə.riː/ (AmE), /mæk.rəʊ.iː.və.ˈluː.ʃə.riː/ /; / /mæk.rəʊ.e.və.ˈluː.ʃə.riː/ (BrE)
relating to large-scale evolutionary changes that happen over long periods of time, such as the formation of new species or groups

Examples

Macroevolutionary changes
Scientists study macroevolutionary trends to understand how major groups of animals have changed over millions of years.
The extinction of dinosaurs was a significant macroevolutionary event in Earth's history.
Comparing fossils from different eras helps researchers identify macroevolutionary patterns in plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microevolutionary - relating to small-scale evolutionary changes within a species
