saltire

noun
diagonal cross (X-shaped), often in heraldry or flags
Pronunciation: / ˈsæltaɪr / (AmE), / ˈsɔːltaɪə / (BrE)
A cross in the shape of the letter X, with diagonal bars of equal length, especially as used in heraldry and on flags.

Examples

A saltire cross
A red saltire is also known as St. Patrick's Cross.
Many coats of arms include a saltire as a symbol of heritage or allegiance.
The Scottish flag features a prominent saltire in white on a blue background.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cross - a structure or symbol consisting of an upright and a transverse piece
