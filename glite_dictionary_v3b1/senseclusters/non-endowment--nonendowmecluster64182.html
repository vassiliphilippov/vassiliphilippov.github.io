non-endowment

noun
absence of talent
8.8
Pronunciation: / nɑn ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (AmE), / nɒn ɪnˈdaʊmənt / (BrE)
the lack of a natural talent, gift, or innate quality in a person.

Examples

Non-endowment in music
His non-endowment in mathematics made advanced courses especially challenging for him.
The school supports students whose non-endowment in sports affects their confidence.
Despite her non-endowment in art, she enjoyed painting as a hobby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deficiency - a lack or shortage of something necessary
  • antonym: endowment - a natural talent, gift, or innate quality
