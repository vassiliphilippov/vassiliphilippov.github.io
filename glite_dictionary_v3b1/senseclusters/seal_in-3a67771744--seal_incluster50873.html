seal in

verb
confine and prevent escape (e.g. air, flavor)
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːl ɪn / (AmE), / ˈsiːl ɪn / (BrE)
to prevent something (such as air, flavor, or moisture) from escaping by closing tightly or covering completely

Examples

To seal in moisture
Cooking the vegetables quickly helps to seal in their nutrients and flavor.
Make sure to use an airtight container to seal in the freshness of the coffee.
The wax layer seals in moisture, preventing the wood from drying out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lock in - to trap or confine something or someone securely inside
