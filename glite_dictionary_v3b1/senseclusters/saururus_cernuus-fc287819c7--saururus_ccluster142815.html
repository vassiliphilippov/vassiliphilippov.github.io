Examples

Saururus cernuus plant
Saururus cernuus thrives in moist, shady wetlands across the southeastern United States.
Gardeners often plant Saururus cernuus to add texture and interest to water gardens and ponds.
The long, white flower spikes of Saururus cernuus resemble lizards' tails, giving the plant its common name.
