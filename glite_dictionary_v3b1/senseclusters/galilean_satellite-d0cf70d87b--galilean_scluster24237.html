Galilean satellite

noun
one of Jupiter's four largest moons discovered by Galileo
5.6 7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæliˈliən ˈsætəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈliːən ˈsatəlaɪt / (BrE)
any of the four largest moons of Jupiter—Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto—discovered by Galileo Galilei in 1610.

Examples

The Galilean satellite Europa
Each Galilean satellite has unique characteristics, with Europa believed to have a subsurface ocean.
Scientists are studying the Galilean satellites to learn more about the possibility of life beyond Earth.
Through a telescope, Galileo first observed the four large Galilean satellites orbiting Jupiter.
