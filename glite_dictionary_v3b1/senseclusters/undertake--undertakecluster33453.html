undertake

verb
formally agree to do something
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.teɪk/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.teɪk/ (BrE)
to agree to do or take responsibility for a task, project, or responsibility

Examples

To undertake a project
Before we undertake any new commitments, let's review the existing agreements.
She agreed to undertake the role of legal advisor, signing the contract yesterday.
The company will undertake to complete the project by the end of the year.
I undertake to complete the project by the agreed deadline.
She undertook to organize the conference herself.
The company has undertaken to provide free repairs for any faulty products.
Before you undertake such a big project, make sure you have a clear plan.
She decided to undertake the responsibility of organizing the team's annual trip.
The company will undertake a major expansion next year.
