driveler

noun
foolish talker
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪvələr / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvələ / (BrE)
a person who talks nonsense or babbles foolishly

Examples

Old driveler talking
The children laughed as the old driveler told his wild stories in the park.
No one took the driveler seriously because his words made little sense.
She was tired of listening to the office driveler during meetings.
He dismissed the old man as a driveller who never said anything meaningful.
She refused to listen to the driveller any longer and walked away.
The politician was accused of being a driveller during the heated debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - incoherent or foolish talk
  • synonym: babbler - a person who talks rapidly and continuously in a foolish way
  • synonym: chatterbox - a person who talks a lot, especially about trivial matters
  • antonym: orator - a skilled public speaker
