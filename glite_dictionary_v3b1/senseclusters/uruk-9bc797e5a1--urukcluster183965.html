Uruk

proper noun
ancient Mesopotamian period
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈʊ.rʊk/ /; / /ˈuː.rʊk/ (AmE), /ˈʊ.rʊk/ /; / /ˈuː.rʊk/ (BrE)
an archaeological period in southern Mesopotamia (c. 4000–3100 BCE) known for early urbanization and state formation

Examples

Uruk period
The Uruk period saw the rise of some of the world's first cities in Mesopotamia.
Pottery from the Uruk era reveals much about early urban life and trade.
Many scholars consider Uruk a turning point in the development of complex societies.
