bromide

noun
salt of hydrobromic acid; old sedative drug
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊ.maɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.maɪd / (BrE)
any of the salts of hydrobromic acid, especially those once used as sedatives

Examples

Bromide tablets
Medical professionals now use safer alternatives instead of bromide to treat anxiety.
Potassium bromide was once widely prescribed as a sedative in the 19th century.
The laboratory ordered a sample of sodium bromide for chemical analysis.
