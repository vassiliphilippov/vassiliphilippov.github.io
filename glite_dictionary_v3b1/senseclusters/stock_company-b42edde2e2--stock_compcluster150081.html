stock company

noun
theatrical troupe with a set repertoire
3.5
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈkʌmpəni / (AmE), / stɒk ˈkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a theatrical company that regularly performs a set repertoire of plays, often with a permanent group of actors

Examples

Theater stock company
During the 1920s, stock companies were a popular way to bring live drama to small towns.
She joined the local stock company and quickly became one of its leading actresses.
The old theater hired a stock company to perform a variety of classic plays.
