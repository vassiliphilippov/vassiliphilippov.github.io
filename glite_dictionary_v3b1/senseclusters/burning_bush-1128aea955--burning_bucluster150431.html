burning bush

noun
Old Testament bush from which God spoke to Moses
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.nɪŋ bʊʃ/ (AmE), /ˈbɜː.nɪŋ bʊʃ/ (BrE)
a bush described in the Old Testament as being on fire but not consumed by the flames, from which God spoke to Moses

Examples

Moses and the burning bush
According to the Bible, Moses encountered the burning bush on Mount Horeb.
Artists have depicted the burning bush in paintings and stained glass windows for centuries.
The story of the burning bush is a key moment in the Book of Exodus.
