Examples

Disprovable theory
A scientific theory should be disprovable if new evidence contradicts it.
The lawyer argued that the accusation was disprovable with the available facts.
Not every claim is disprovable using current technology or knowledge.

Synonyms

  • refutable
    7.0 0.04/m
    refutable is a claim you can prove wrong with clear evidence or argument, while disprovable is the general word.
  • falsifiable
    8.0 0.28/m
    falsifiable is a scientific idea you can test and show false by observation or experiment, while disprovable is broader.
  • rebuttable
    7.0 0.11/m
    rebuttable is an argument others can challenge or oppose with evidence, while disprovable means it can be shown false.
  • confutable
    9.9 0.00/m
    confutable is a rare formal word for something you can prove wrong, while disprovable is the common everyday term.
