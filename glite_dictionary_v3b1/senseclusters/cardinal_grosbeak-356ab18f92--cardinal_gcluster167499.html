cardinal grosbeak

noun
North American finch with red male plumage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrdɪnəl ˈɡroʊsˌbik / (AmE), / ˈkɑːdɪnəl ˈɡrəʊsbɪk / (BrE)
A crested, thick-billed North American finch, especially the male, which has bright red plumage; also known as the northern cardinal.

Examples

A bright cardinal grosbeak
A brilliant cardinal grosbeak perched on the feeder, flashing its red feathers in the morning sun.
Many birdwatchers hope to spot a male cardinal grosbeak during their spring outings.
The thick beak of the cardinal grosbeak helps it to easily crack open seeds.
