atomically

adverb
indivisibly, as one unit
7.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtɑː.mɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /ə.ˈtɑː.mɪ.kliː/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtɒ.mɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /ə.ˈtɒ.mɪ.kliː/ (BrE)
in a way that is indivisible and cannot be interrupted, especially referring to a single, unbreakable operation in computing or transactions

Examples

Atomically update data
The database updates both records atomically to ensure data consistency.
To prevent errors, the transaction must be completed atomically without interruption.
In programming, certain operations are performed atomically to avoid conflicts between processes.
