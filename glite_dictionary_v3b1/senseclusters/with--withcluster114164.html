with

preposition
introducing circumstance
1.0
Pronunciation: / wɪð / (AmE), / wɪð / (BrE)
used to introduce a circumstance or situation that exists at the same time as another event, often implying 'as' or 'because'.

Examples

With the sun setting
With the sun setting, the hikers hurried to reach the campsite before dark.
With so many people absent, the meeting was postponed until next week.
With her experience, she quickly solved the problem that confused others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: as - used to indicate when something happens or the reason for something
  • synonym: because - used to introduce the reason for something
