hour circle

noun
astronomy: celestial sphere circle through poles
5.2 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈaʊ.ər ˈsɜːr.kəl/ /; / /aʊr ˈsɜːr.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈaʊ.ə ˈsɜː.kəl/ /; / /aʊə ˈsɜː.kəl/ (BrE)
an imaginary great circle on the celestial sphere that passes through both celestial poles and a given celestial object, used to measure right ascension

Examples

The hour circle line
Astronomers use the hour circle along with declination to pinpoint celestial objects.
Each hour circle intersects the celestial equator at a unique right ascension.
The hour circle is essential for locating stars in the sky using right ascension.
