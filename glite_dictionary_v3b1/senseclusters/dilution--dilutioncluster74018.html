dilution

noun
a weakened or diluted solution
5.2 3.6
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈluː.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.ˈluː.ʃən/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈljuː.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.ˈljuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making a liquid weaker or less concentrated by adding water or another solvent, or the liquid that results from this process

Examples

A dilution of liquid
A tenfold dilution of the sample was required for the experiment.
Each dilution must be carefully labeled to ensure accurate results.
The technician prepared a dilution of the chemical for further testing.
After adding more water, the dilution of the orange juice made it less flavorful.
Proper dilution of bleach is important for safe cleaning.
The laboratory technician monitored the dilution of the chemical solution carefully.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrate - a substance made by removing water or diluting agent from a solution
  • antonym: concentration - the process of increasing the strength of a solution by removing solvent
  • synonym: dilute - to make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding another solvent
