firmament

noun
the sky or heavens, especially as a dome
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝː.mə.mənt /; / ˈfɜrməmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.mə.mənt /; / ˈfɜːməmənt / (BrE)
the sky or heavens, especially thought of as a vast dome over the earth

Examples

The firmament above
Ancient cultures believed the firmament was a solid dome that held back the waters above.
He gazed at the clouds drifting lazily across the morning firmament.
The endless firmament sparkled with stars on the clear winter night.
A streak of lightning illuminated the entire firmament for a moment.
Ancient astronomers believed the stars were fixed to the firmament above the earth.
Poets often describe the firmament as a magnificent vault studded with stars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heaven - the sky, especially perceived as the home of divine beings or the afterlife
  • synonym: heavens - the expanse of space that seems to be over the earth like a dome
  • synonym: sky - the region of the atmosphere and outer space seen from the earth
