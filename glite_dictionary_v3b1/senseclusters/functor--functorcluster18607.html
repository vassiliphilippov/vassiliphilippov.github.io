functor

noun
grammatical function word
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌŋktər / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋktə / (BrE)
a word in grammar, such as an article, preposition, or conjunction, that mainly shows relationships between other words rather than carrying its own meaning

Examples

Grammatical functor word
In English, words like 'the' and 'and' are considered functors because they show relationships between other words.
A functor does not carry much meaning by itself but is essential for sentence structure.
Prepositions such as 'in' and 'on' are examples of functor words in grammar.

Related Terms

  • antonym: content word - a word that carries lexical meaning, such as a noun, verb, adjective, or adverb
  • synonym: function word - a word that has little lexical meaning but serves a grammatical purpose
