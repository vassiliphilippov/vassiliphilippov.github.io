family Sepiidae

noun
family of true cuttlefishes (marine mollusks)
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli sɛˈpaɪɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli sɪˈpɪɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine mollusks known as true cuttlefishes, characterized by internal shells and highly developed eyes.

All members of the family Sepiidae possess an internal shell known as a cuttlebone.
Scientists are studying the camouflage abilities of species within the family Sepiidae.
The family Sepiidae contains a wide variety of cuttlefishes found in oceans worldwide.
