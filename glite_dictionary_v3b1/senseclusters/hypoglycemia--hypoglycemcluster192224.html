hypoglycemia

noun
abnormally low blood sugar level
5.1 4.8
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pəʊ.glaɪ.ˈsiː.mi.ə/ /; / /haɪ.poʊ.glaɪ.ˈsiː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /haɪ.pəʊ.glaɪ.ˈsiː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which the level of glucose in the blood is abnormally low, often caused by excessive insulin or inadequate food intake.

Examples

Low hypoglycemia level
Early symptoms of hypoglycemia include shakiness, sweating, and confusion.
People with diabetes must monitor their blood sugar to prevent hypoglycemia.
Skipping meals can increase your risk of experiencing hypoglycemia.
A poor diet can sometimes result in episodes of hypoglycaemia.
People with diabetes must monitor their blood sugar to avoid hypoglycaemia.
Symptoms of hypoglycaemia include sweating, confusion, and shakiness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperglycaemia - an excess of glucose in the bloodstream
  • antonym: hyperglycemia - abnormally high blood sugar level
