impersonalize

verb
to make less personal
7.3
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpɜːr.sən.ə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpɜː.sən.ə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to make something less personal or more objective, often by removing individual characteristics, emotions, or personal connections

Examples

To impersonalize a message
The company impersonalized its emails by removing the customer's name from the greeting.
Some people feel that technology has impersonalized the way we communicate with each other.
The new procedures impersonalize the experience, making it feel more like a routine than a service.
The new policies impersonalize the workplace, making employees feel like just numbers.
Some companies impersonalize their communication to appear more neutral and professional.
Automated responses can impersonalize customer service, leading to less satisfaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depersonalize - to remove personal characteristics or individuality
  • antonym: personalize - to make something more personal or tailored to individuals
