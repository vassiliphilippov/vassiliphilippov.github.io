soil

verb
make dirty or stained
3.7
Pronunciation: / sɔɪl / (AmE), / sɔɪl / (BrE)
to make something dirty or stained, especially by contact with dirt, mud, or another substance

Examples

To soil clothes
Please take off your shoes so you don't soil the carpet.
She accidentally soiled her dress when she dropped her lunch.
The dog's muddy paws soiled the freshly cleaned floor.
