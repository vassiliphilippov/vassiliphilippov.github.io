wind-up

noun
mechanical toy or device
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
a mechanical toy or device that operates when a key or handle is turned to wind a spring inside

Examples

Wind-up toy car
The child laughed as the wind-up car raced across the floor.
He collects vintage wind-up toys from the 1950s.
After turning the key, the wind-up monkey began to play its cymbals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clockwork - a mechanism with a spring and gears, often used in toys
  • antonym: electronic toy - a toy powered by electricity or batteries
