unsifted flour

noun
flour not sifted before use
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈsɪf.tɪd flaʊr/ /; / /ʌn.ˈsɪf.tɪd flaʊr/ (AmE), /ən.ˈsɪf.tɪd ˈflaʊ.ə/ /; / /ʌn.ˈsɪf.tɪd ˈflaʊ.ə/ (BrE)
flour that has not been passed through a sieve to remove clumps or aerate it, often resulting in a denser texture in baked goods.

Examples

Unsifted flour added
He accidentally added unsifted flour to the mixture, making the cookies less light and fluffy.
If you use unsifted flour, your bread may turn out heavier than expected.
The recipe called for unsifted flour, which made a noticeable difference in the cake's texture.
