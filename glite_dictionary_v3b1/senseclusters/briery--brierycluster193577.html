briery

adjective
covered with briars or thorns
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪəri / (AmE), / ˈbraɪəri / (BrE)
having many briars or thorns; covered with prickly plants or spines.

Examples

A briery path
A narrow path led through a briery tangle at the edge of the field.
She wore thick boots to protect her feet from the briery undergrowth.
The hiker avoided the briery patches that lined the forest trail.
His garden was overgrown with briary vines and wild roses.
The briary hedgerow formed a natural barrier between the fields.
We hiked through a briary patch that scratched our legs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prickly - having small sharp points or thorns
  • synonym: thorny - having many thorns or being difficult or troublesome
