over-the-counter market

noun
market for off-exchange securities trading
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚ ði kənˈtaʊnɚ ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvə ði kənˈtaʊnə ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a financial market where securities are bought and sold directly between parties, typically via telephone or computer, rather than through a centralized exchange floor

Examples

The over-the-counter market
Before investing, she researched the risks associated with the over-the-counter market.
Many small companies raise capital by selling shares in the over-the-counter market.
The over-the-counter market allows investors to trade securities that are not listed on formal exchanges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stock exchange - organized market where securities are bought and sold
