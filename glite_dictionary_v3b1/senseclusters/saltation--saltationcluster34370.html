saltation

noun
leaping transport of particles by wind or water (geology)
Pronunciation: / sælˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / sælˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a movement by jumping, leaping, or bouncing, including in dancing or when particles move in jumps (as in geology)

Examples

Saltation of sand grains
During a sandstorm, saltation causes sand grains to bounce along the desert surface.
The riverbank showed evidence of saltation, with pebbles and sand transported in short leaps by the current.
Wind-driven saltation is a key process in shaping dunes and other arid landscapes.
His movement was not a walk or a run, but a peculiar saltation resembling a series of small leaps.
The grasshopper's saltation enabled it to escape the predator swiftly.
The study examined how wind causes the saltation of sand grains across the desert surface.
Her graceful saltation captivated everyone in the ballroom.
The ancient fresco depicted people engaged in lively saltation during a festival.
The tradition involves a unique form of saltation accompanied by drums and singing.
