hide your light under a bushel

verb
conceal your talents or abilities
Pronunciation: / haɪd jʊr laɪt ˈʌndɚ ə ˈbʊʃəl / (AmE), / haɪd jɔː laɪt ˈʌndə ə ˈbʊʃəl / (BrE)
to conceal your talents, abilities, or achievements instead of making them known to others

Examples

To hide your light under a bushel often
It's important not to hide your light under a bushel when applying for a new job.
Many gifted people hide their light under a bushel out of modesty or shyness.
She tends to hide her light under a bushel and rarely talks about her accomplishments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: show off - to display one's talents or abilities in a way that attracts attention
