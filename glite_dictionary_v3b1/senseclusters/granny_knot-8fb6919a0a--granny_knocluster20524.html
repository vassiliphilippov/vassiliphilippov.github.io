granny knot

noun
an insecurely tied knot
4.4 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈgræ.nɪ nɑːt/ /; / /ˈgræ.nɪ nɔːt/ (AmE), /ˈgræ.nɪ nɒt/ (BrE)
An insecure knot made by tying a reef knot (square knot) incorrectly, resulting in a knot that slips easily.

Examples

A granny knot slips
He realized too late that he had made a granny knot, and the package fell apart.
If you tie your shoelaces with a granny knot, they will likely come undone quickly.
Sailors avoid using a granny knot because it is unreliable compared to a proper reef knot.
