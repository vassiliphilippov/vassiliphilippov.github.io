Baltic Republic

noun
Estonia, Latvia, or Lithuania (Baltic countries)
6.1 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbɔːl.tɪk rɪ.ˈpʌb.lɪk/ /; / /ˈbɑːl.tɪk rɪ.ˈpʌb.lɪk/ (AmE), /ˈbɒl.tɪk rɪ.ˈpʌb.lɪk/ /; / /ˈbɔːl.tɪk rɪ.ˈpʌb.lɪk/ (BrE)
any of the three countries of Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania in northern Europe, situated along the eastern coast of the Baltic Sea, formerly part of the Soviet Union.

Examples

The Baltic Republic countries
After regaining independence from the Soviet Union, each Baltic Republic became a member of the European Union.
Estonia is often cited as the most technologically advanced Baltic Republic.
The economies of the Baltic Republics have grown rapidly since the 1990s.
