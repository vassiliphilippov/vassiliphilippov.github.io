majagua

noun
Caribbean tree valued for its timber
Pronunciation: / məˈhɑːɡwə / (AmE), / məˈhɑːɡwə / (BrE)
a tropical tree (especially found in the Caribbean and Central America) valued for its wood or strong fibers

Examples

The majagua tree
Bright orange blossoms covered the majagua lining the narrow Jamaican path.
Carpenters prize the timber of majagua for making durable gunstocks.
The sturdy wood of the majagua is often used for crafting fine furniture in Cuba.
Baskets woven from majagua fibers are both strong and flexible.
In many coastal gardens, the majagua tree is planted for its lush foliage and yellow flowers.
The craftsmen used majagua wood to build sturdy canoe outriggers.
