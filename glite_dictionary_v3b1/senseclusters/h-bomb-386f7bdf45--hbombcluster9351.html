H-bomb

noun
thermonuclear weapon; hydrogen bomb
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ ˌbɑm / (AmE), / ˈeɪ ˌbɒm / (BrE)
a nuclear weapon that derives its explosive power from the fusion of hydrogen nuclei to form helium at extremely high temperatures

Examples

The H-bomb explosion
Scientists debated the moral implications of creating the H-bomb during the Cold War.
The country tested an H-bomb in a remote area, causing international concern.
The detonation of the H-bomb marked a turning point in the history of nuclear weapons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atomic bomb - a nuclear weapon that releases energy by fission of atomic nuclei
  • synonym: thermonuclear bomb - a bomb whose destructive power comes from the fusion of hydrogen nuclei
