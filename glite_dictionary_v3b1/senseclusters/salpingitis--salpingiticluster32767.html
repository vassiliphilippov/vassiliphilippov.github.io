salpingitis

noun
inflammation of a Fallopian or Eustachian tube
Pronunciation: / ˌsælpɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌsælpɪnˈdʒaɪtɪs / (BrE)
Inflammation of a Fallopian tube or a Eustachian tube, usually caused by infection.

Examples

Salpingitis infection
Antibiotics are often used in the treatment of salpingitis caused by bacterial infections.
If left untreated, salpingitis can lead to infertility in women.
The patient was diagnosed with salpingitis after presenting with pelvic pain and fever.
