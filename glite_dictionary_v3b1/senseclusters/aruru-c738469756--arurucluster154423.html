Aruru

proper noun
earth goddess in Mesopotamian mythology
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈruːruː / (AmE), / ɑːˈruːruː / (BrE)
In Mesopotamian mythology, the mother and earth goddess mentioned in the Epic of Gilgamesh, identified with the Sumerian goddesses Ki and Ninkhursag.

Examples

Aruru goddess
According to the Epic of Gilgamesh, Aruru created Enkidu from clay in the wilderness.
Scholars often equate Aruru with other Sumerian mother goddesses like Ki and Ninkhursag.
The legend describes Aruru as the powerful force behind creation and fertility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ki - Sumerian earth goddess
  • synonym: Ninkhursag - Sumerian mother goddess
