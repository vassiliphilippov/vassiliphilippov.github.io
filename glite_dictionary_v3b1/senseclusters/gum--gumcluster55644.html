gum

noun
tree producing gum or resin
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
any of several trees, especially of the genera Eucalyptus, Liquidambar, or Nyssa, that produce gum or resinous sap.

Examples

Gum tree sap
Farmers often tap the gum for its valuable sap, which is used in varnishes and adhesives.
The Australian landscape is famous for its many species of gum trees, particularly the eucalyptus.
The forest was dominated by towering gum trees that provided shade and shelter for wildlife.
