Gentiana clausa

noun
North American gentian species with closed flowers
Pronunciation: / dʒɛnˈʃænə ˈklɔːzə / (AmE), / dʒɛnˈʃiːənə ˈklɑːzə / (BrE)
A North American species of gentian with larger, closed blue or purple flowers, closely related to Gentiana andrewsii.

Examples

Gentiana clausa flower
Gentiana clausa is commonly known as the bottle gentian due to its tightly closed blooms.
In late summer, the Gentiana clausa adds vibrant blue color to the woodland edge.
The flowers of Gentiana clausa remain closed even when fully mature, distinguishing it from other gentians.

Related Terms
