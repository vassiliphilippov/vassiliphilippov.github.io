intangible asset

noun
non-physical asset with value
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtændʒəbəl ˈæsɛt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtændʒɪbəl ˈæsɛt / (BrE)
A non-physical asset, such as a patent, trademark, or goodwill, that has value and can be sold but does not have a material form.

Examples

A intangible asset value
Brand names and patents are considered intangible assets on a company's balance sheet.
Goodwill is an example of an intangible asset that can be acquired during a business merger.
The value of intangible assets can play a major role in a company's total worth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tangible asset - a physical asset with real substance, such as land or machinery
