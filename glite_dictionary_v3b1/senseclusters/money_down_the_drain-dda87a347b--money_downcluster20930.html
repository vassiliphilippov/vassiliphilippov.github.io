money down the drain

noun
money wasted or lost
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌni daʊn ðə dreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌni daʊn ðə dreɪn / (BrE)
money that has been wasted or lost, especially through careless or unwise actions

Examples

Money down the drain after failure
After the failed event, all our investment turned out to be money down the drain.
If you don't read the contract carefully, your deposit could be money down the drain.
Spending so much on untested technology is just money down the drain.
