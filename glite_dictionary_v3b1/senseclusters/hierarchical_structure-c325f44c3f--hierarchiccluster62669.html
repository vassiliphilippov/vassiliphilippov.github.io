hierarchical structure

noun
system organized in levels or ranks
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.əˈrɑːrkɪkəl ˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.əˈrɑːkɪkəl ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
a system or arrangement organized in levels, each subordinate to the one above, often resembling a tree; especially used for data or organizations

Examples

A hierarchical structure system
Biological classification is based on a hierarchical structure from kingdom down to species.
In computer science, a file system often uses a hierarchical structure to organize directories and files.
The company's hierarchical structure makes decision-making very slow.
