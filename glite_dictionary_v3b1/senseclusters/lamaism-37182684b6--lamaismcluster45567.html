Lamaism

noun
Tibetan Buddhist doctrine with local and shamanistic elements
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːməˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlɑːməɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A form of Tibetan Buddhism incorporating traditional Indian Buddhist teachings as well as elements from indigenous shamanism and other local religions.

Examples

Lamaism in Tibet
Critics argue that the term Lamaism oversimplifies the rich variety of Tibetan Buddhist practices.
Many scholars of religion have studied the rituals and texts unique to Lamaism.
The influence of indigenous beliefs makes Lamaism distinct from other forms of Buddhism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Tibetan Buddhism - The branch of Buddhism practiced in Tibet and neighboring areas
