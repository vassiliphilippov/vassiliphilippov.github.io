defeater

noun
argument against justification
8.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfiː.tər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfiː.tə/ (BrE)
a fact, argument, reason, or circumstance that prevents a belief, claim, or plan from being accepted or succeeding

Examples

A logical defeater
The lawyer presented a strong defeater that undermined the prosecution's main argument.
If new evidence emerges, it could serve as a defeater for the current hypothesis.
A single defeater can sometimes overturn years of accepted scientific theory.
The lawyer presented a powerful defeater that challenged the witness's credibility.
If new evidence emerges, it could serve as a defeater for the current theory.
Philosophers often search for a defeater to test the strength of an argument.
A sudden shortage of parts became the defeater of the manufacturing plant's plan to meet orders.
Lack of funding was the main defeater for the community project this year.
The unexpected rainstorm proved to be a defeater for their outdoor wedding plans.
