Nietzscheanism

noun
Nietzsche's philosophical theories and doctrines
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈniː.tʃiː.ə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈniː.tʃɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈniː.tʃɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈniː.tʃiː.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the philosophical system or doctrines of Friedrich Nietzsche, especially those advocating the reevaluation of all values, the will to power, the concept of the superman, and the idea of eternal recurrence

Examples

Nietzscheanism philosophy
Her embrace of Nietzscheanism led her to question traditional religious and moral values.
Many regard Nietzscheanism as a response to the growing sense of nihilism in modern society.
The philosopher’s work is deeply influenced by Nietzscheanism and the concepts of the will to power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: existentialism - a philosophical theory emphasizing individual existence, freedom, and choice
  • antonym: nihilism - the rejection of all religious and moral principles, often in the belief that life is meaningless
