corona

noun
crown-shaped anatomical structure
6.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊ.nə /; / kəˈroʊnə / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊ.nə /; / kəˈrəʊnə / (BrE)
a crown-like or trumpet-shaped structure in the body or in certain flowers

Examples

Flower corona
Certain flowers have a corona—a crown-like appendage surrounding their reproductive structures.
In anatomy, the corona of the glans penis is the rounded base where it meets the shaft.
The dentist examined the corona of the tooth for signs of decay.
In botany class, we learned how the corona differs from the petals and sepals.
The daffodil's bright yellow corona sets it apart from other spring flowers.
The shape and color of a flower's corona can help identify its species.
