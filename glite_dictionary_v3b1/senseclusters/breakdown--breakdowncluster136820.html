breakdown

noun
failure or collapse of a system or order
4.4 6.30/m

the failure or collapse of a system, relationship, or order, so that it can no longer function successfully

Examples

Breakdown in talks
A breakdown in communication between management and staff caused widespread confusion.
Negotiations ended in a breakdown after neither side could reach a compromise.
The financial crisis led to the breakdown of several major banks across the country.

Synonyms

  • collapse
    4.1 28/m
    collapse is a sudden falling down or failure of a system or structure, while breakdown means a system stops working.
  • failure
    3.3 31/m
    failure is a general loss of ability to work or function, while breakdown often implies a sudden collapse.
  • crash
    3.2 5.49/m
    crash is a sudden stop or fault, often in computers or vehicles, while breakdown is a wider system failure.
  • meltdown
    5.8 4.61/m
    meltdown is a sudden, disastrous total failure in a system, while breakdown can be less complete or less extreme.
  • disintegration
    6.7 2.22/m
    disintegration is the process of falling apart and losing cohesion over time, while breakdown often means sudden failure.

  • synonym: failure - lack of success or breakdown in function or structure
