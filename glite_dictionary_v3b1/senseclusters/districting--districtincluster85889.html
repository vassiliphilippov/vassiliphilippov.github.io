districting

noun
dividing into districts
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstrɪktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɪstrɪktɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or result of dividing an area into districts, especially for administrative, electoral, or political purposes

Examples

The districting process
The government began districting the region to improve local administration.
Controversies often arise during districting because the boundaries can affect election results.
The committee reviewed several proposals for districting before making a final decision.
The city council debated the new districting plan for several hours before voting.
Controversy arose when the districting split neighborhoods between different representatives.
Fair districting is important to ensure equal representation for all citizens.
