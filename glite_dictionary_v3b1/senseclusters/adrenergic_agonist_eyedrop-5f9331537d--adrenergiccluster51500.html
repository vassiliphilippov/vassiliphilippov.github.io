adrenergic agonist eyedrop

noun
medicated eye drop for glaucoma
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌædrəˈnɜrdʒɪk ˈæɡənɪst ˈaɪˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˌadrəˈnɜːdʒɪk ˈaɡənɪst ˈaɪdrɒp / (BrE)
A type of medicated eye drop used to treat glaucoma by reducing intraocular pressure, mainly by decreasing aqueous humor production and increasing its outflow.

Examples

Adrenergic agonist eyedrop for glaucoma
Patients should apply the adrenergic agonist eyedrop exactly as directed to lower eye pressure.
The doctor prescribed an adrenergic agonist eyedrop to help control her glaucoma.
Your ophthalmologist may recommend an adrenergic agonist eyedrop if other treatments are not effective.
