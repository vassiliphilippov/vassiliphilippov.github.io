aromatization

noun
making aromatic compounds
Pronunciation: / əˌroʊmətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌrəʊmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making a chemical compound aromatic, especially by forming an aromatic ring in organic chemistry

Examples

Chemical aromatization
The aromatization of hydrocarbons is important in the production of certain fuels.
Catalysts are often used to speed up the aromatization process in industrial chemistry.
Understanding aromatization helps chemists design new drugs with aromatic structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aromatize - to make something aromatic, especially in chemistry
  • antonym: hydrogenation - the process of adding hydrogen to a compound, often the opposite of aromatization
