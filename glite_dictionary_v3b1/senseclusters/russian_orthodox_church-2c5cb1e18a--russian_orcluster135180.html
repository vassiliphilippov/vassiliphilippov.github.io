Russian Orthodox Church

noun
major Eastern Orthodox Christian church in Russia
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌʃən ˈɔrθəˌdɑks tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃən ˈɔːθədɒks tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
The largest autocephalous Eastern Orthodox Church, historically the established church of Russia, led by its own Patriarch.

Examples

The Russian Orthodox Church leader
After the revolution, the Russian Orthodox Church faced years of persecution.
Many cathedrals in Moscow are under the jurisdiction of the Russian Orthodox Church.
The Russian Orthodox Church celebrates Christmas on January 7th, according to the Julian calendar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Eastern Orthodox Church - the whole of the Eastern Orthodox Christian churches
