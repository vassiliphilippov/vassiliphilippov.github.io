rake

noun
angle or slope from horizontal (technical)
3.3
Pronunciation: / reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
the angle or degree of slope away from a horizontal plane, especially used in construction and engineering to describe surfaces such as roofs or the stage in a theater.

Examples

Rake of the roof
Architects designed the building's roof with a gentle rake to aid water drainage.
The theater's stage had a noticeable rake, allowing the audience a better view.
The workers measured the rake of the ramp to ensure it met safety standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pitch - the steepness or degree of slope, especially of a roof or surface
