eternal sleep

noun
euphemism for death
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtɜrnəl sliːp /; / ɪˈtɝːnəl sliːp / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜː.nəl sliːp /; / ɪˈtɜːnəl sliːp / (BrE)
a poetic or gentle way to refer to death, likening it to a never-ending or peaceful sleep

Examples

The eternal sleep of death
Many poets describe death as an eternal sleep, bringing comfort to grieving families.
She believed her grandfather had entered an eternal sleep and would no longer suffer.
The novel ended with the hero finally finding his eternal sleep after a long struggle.
After a long illness, he finally entered eternal sleep surrounded by family.
Many poets have described death as an eternal sleep to provide comfort.
People often refer to a loved one’s passing as their eternal sleep.
