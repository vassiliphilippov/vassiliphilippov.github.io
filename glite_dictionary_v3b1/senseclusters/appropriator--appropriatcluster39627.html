appropriator

noun
person who takes for own use, often without permission
8.9
Pronunciation: / əˈproʊpriˌeɪtɚ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊprieɪtə / (BrE)
a person who takes something for their own use, especially without permission or legal right

Examples

An appropriator of ideas
Copyright law aims to protect original work against any appropriator who copies without permission.
In her critique, the artist accused the museum of being a cultural appropriator.
The corrupt official acted as an appropriator of public funds, using the money for personal gain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: donor - a person who gives something voluntarily
  • synonym: thief - a person who steals
