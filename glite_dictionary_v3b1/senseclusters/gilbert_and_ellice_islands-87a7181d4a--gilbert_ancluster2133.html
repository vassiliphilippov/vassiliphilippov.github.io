Gilbert and Ellice Islands

proper noun
former British possession in the Pacific Ocean
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪlbərt ənd ˈɛlɪs ˈaɪləndz / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlbət ən ˈelɪs ˈaɪləndz / (BrE)
a former British colony in the Pacific Ocean, comprising what are now Kiribati and Tuvalu, split into two independent nations in the 1970s.

Examples

The Gilbert and Ellice Islands colony
During World War II, the Gilbert and Ellice Islands were strategically significant in the Pacific.
Many historic documents from the colonial era reference the Gilbert and Ellice Islands as a British possession.
The Gilbert and Ellice Islands gained independence as Kiribati and Tuvalu in the 1970s.
