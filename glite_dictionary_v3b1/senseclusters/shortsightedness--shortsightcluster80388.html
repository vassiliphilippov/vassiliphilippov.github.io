shortsightedness

noun
lack of foresight or prudence
5.5
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrtˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʃɔːtˈsaɪtɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of not considering the long-term consequences of actions, especially when managing resources; lack of foresight or prudence.

Examples

Business shortsightedness
Environmental shortsightedness often results in the depletion of natural resources.
His shortsightedness was apparent when he ignored the experts' warnings about the risks.
The company's shortsightedness led to financial trouble when they failed to plan for the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foresight - the ability to anticipate future events
  • antonym: prudence - the quality of being cautious and wise
