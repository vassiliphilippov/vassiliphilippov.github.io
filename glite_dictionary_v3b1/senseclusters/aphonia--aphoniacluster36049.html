aphonia

noun
loss of voice
7.5
Pronunciation: / əˈfoʊniə / (AmE), / əˈfəʊnɪə / (BrE)
Loss of the ability to speak due to a disorder of the vocal organs.

Examples

Aphonia from illness
Chronic laryngitis may lead to aphonia if left untreated.
Following her surgery, she experienced aphonia and could not speak for several days.
The singer was devastated when aphonia forced her to cancel her performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dysphonia - difficulty in speaking due to a physical disorder of the mouth, tongue, throat, or vocal cords
  • degree lower: hoarseness - abnormal voice quality, often rough or harsh
