Synonyms

    3.5 30/m
    monitor is used to watch something over time, often with tools or reports, while track means follow or observe movement.
  • keep track
    2.7 8/m
    keep track is a phrase for staying informed about progress regularly, while track focuses on following or observing movement.
  • keep tabs on
    3.9 2/m
    keep tabs on means watch someone closely and informally over time, while track is a general term for following.
  • follow
    4.2 16/m
    follow often means secretly watching someone step by step, while track is broader, observing movement or progress.
  • trace
    3.3 20/m
    trace is about finding an origin or path after events happen, while track is about actively following movement or location.
