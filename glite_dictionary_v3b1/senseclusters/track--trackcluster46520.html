track

verb
observe or follow movement of something
3.1 2.0
Pronunciation: / træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
to follow or observe the progress, movement, or location of someone or something, often to gather information or keep control

Examples

To track a plane
Engineers use radar systems to track incoming aircraft.
She was able to track the package online until it reached its destination.
The scientists tracked the migration patterns of whales across the ocean.
Scientists tracked the migratory patterns of the birds using tiny GPS devices.
The detectives tracked the suspect across several states before finally making an arrest.
The wolf tracked its prey through the snowy forest for hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flee - to run away from a pursuer or danger
  • synonym: follow - to come after in order or time, or to go after someone
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
  • synonym: monitor - to observe and check the progress or quality of something over a period of time
  • synonym: pursue - to follow in order to catch or capture
  • synonym: trace - to find or discover by investigation; to follow the course or trail of something
