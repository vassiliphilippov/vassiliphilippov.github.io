ard

noun
simple plough without mouldboard
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑrd / (AmE), / ɑːd / (BrE)
a simple, light plough made without a mouldboard, used for breaking the soil without turning it over

Examples

A ard plough
Early civilizations relied on the ard for basic tillage before more advanced implements were developed.
The farmer used an ard to prepare the soil for sowing wheat.
Unlike modern ploughs, the ard does not turn over the earth.
