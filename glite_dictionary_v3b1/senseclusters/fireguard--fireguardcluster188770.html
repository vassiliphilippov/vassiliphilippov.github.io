fireguard

noun
strip of land cleared to prevent fire spread
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
A strip of land cleared of vegetation to prevent the spread of a prairie or forest fire.

Examples

Forest fireguard
A well-maintained fireguard can make a critical difference during wildfire season.
Crews cleared a fireguard around the village as a precaution against bushfires.
The rangers built a fireguard to protect the forest from the approaching flames.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firebreak - A barrier of cleared land to stop fire spread
