moorland

noun
open land with heather and peat soil
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʊrlænd / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.lənd / (BrE)
Open, uncultivated land with peaty soil, typically covered with heather, bracken, and moss.

Examples

Open moorland area
After the rain, the moorland was dotted with pools reflecting the cloudy sky.
The Scottish moorland is home to many rare birds and plants.
We hiked across the misty moorland for hours before reaching the village.
