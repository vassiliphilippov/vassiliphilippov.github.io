barnacle

noun
a species of European goose
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrnəkəl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnəkəl / (BrE)
A small European goose (Branta leucopsis) that breeds in the Arctic and migrates south for the winter.

Examples

A barnacle goose
Flocks of barnacle were observed flying south for the winter.
The barnacle can often be seen grazing on salt marshes along the northern coast.
The distinctive black and white markings make the barnacle easy to identify among other geese.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brant - a species of goose similar in size and habitat to the barnacle goose
