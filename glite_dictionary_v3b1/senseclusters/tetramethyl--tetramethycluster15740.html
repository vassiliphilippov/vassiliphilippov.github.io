tetramethyl

noun
four methyl groups
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈmɛθəl / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈmiːθəl / (BrE)
a chemical group or substituent consisting of four methyl (—CH3) groups attached to a central atom or structure

Examples

Tetramethyl group
The bulky tetramethyl group prevented nucleophiles from reaching the reactive center during the experiment.
Adding a tetramethyl substituent can significantly change the properties of a molecule.
Researchers synthesized a compound containing a tetramethyl group to study its chemical stability.

Related Terms
