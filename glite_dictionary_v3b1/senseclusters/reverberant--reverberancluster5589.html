reverberant

adjective
having an echoing quality
8.0 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààv…ùÀêb…ö…ônt / (AmE), / r…™Ààv…úÀêb…ôr…ônt / (BrE)
(of a space or sound) having a quality of echoing or being filled with repeated reflections of sound.

Examples

A reverberant room
Each drumbeat sounded deep and reverberant in the morning mist.
The choir's voices were amplified in the reverberant hall, creating a majestic sound.
The empty warehouse had a strangely reverberant atmosphere when she called out.

Synonyms

  • reverberative
    8.2 0.00/m
    reverberative describes sound that keeps reflecting and ringing, while reverberant describes a space filled with those repeated echoes.
  • echoing
    2.7 0.00/m
    echoing is sound that clearly reflects and repeats in a room, while reverberant means the space holds many overlapping echoes.
  • resonant
    5.6 1.10/m
    resonant is deep, clear sound that keeps ringing in tone, while reverberant focuses on repeated reflections in a space.
  • resounding
    4.9 1.80/m
    resounding is very loud, often emphatic or victorious sounding, while reverberant describes echoing reflections in a space.
