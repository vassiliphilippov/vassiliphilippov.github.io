fieldhand

noun
farm worker employed for field labor
5.9 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfiːld.hænd/ (AmE), /ˈfiːld.hænd/ (BrE)
a person employed to work in the fields on a farm, especially doing manual labor

Examples

A fieldhand worker
During the busy harvest season, the farmer hired a new fieldhand to help with the crops.
Many fieldhands traveled from farm to farm looking for seasonal employment.
The fieldhand worked long hours under the hot sun, planting and watering the rows of corn.
