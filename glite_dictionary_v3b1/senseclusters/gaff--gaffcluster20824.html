gaff

verb
to hook with pole
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɡæf / (AmE), / ɡæf / (BrE)
to hook or catch something, especially a fish, using a pole with a hook

Examples

To gaff a fish
The fisherman gaffed the large tuna and hauled it onto the deck.
After a long struggle, she finally gaffed the fish near the boat.
He quickly gaffs the catch before it can escape back into the water.
The fisherman tried to gaff the large salmon as it neared the boat.
After a long struggle, she managed to gaff the fish and pull it aboard.
He quickly gaffed the catch before it could escape from the net.
