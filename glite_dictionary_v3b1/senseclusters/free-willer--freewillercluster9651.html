free-willer

noun
one who believes in and practices free will
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːˌwɪlər / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌwɪlə / (BrE)
a person who believes in the existence of free will and acts freely and spontaneously

Examples

A free-willer person
Her reputation as a free-willer inspired others to take bold, independent steps.
Known for being a free-willer, he refused to let fate dictate his actions.
Many philosophers debate whether a free-willer truly exists in a deterministic universe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinist - a person who believes that all events are determined by causes external to the will
