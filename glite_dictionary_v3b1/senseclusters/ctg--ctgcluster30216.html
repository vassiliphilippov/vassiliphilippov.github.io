ctg

noun
fetal heart monitoring
8.6
Pronunciation: / si ti ˈdʒi / (AmE), / siː tiː ˈdʒiː / (BrE)
a medical test (cardiotocography) that records a fetus's heart rate and the mother's uterine contractions during pregnancy

Examples

ctg scan in pregnancy
The doctor ordered a ctg to check the baby's well-being during labor.
Nurses regularly monitor the ctg readings to ensure the fetus is not in distress.
A suspicious ctg result may lead to further medical intervention during delivery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cardiotocography - a test that monitors fetal heart rate and uterine contractions
  • antonym: ultrasound - a medical test using sound waves to create images of the inside of the body
