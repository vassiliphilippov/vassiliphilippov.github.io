hector

verb
bully or intimidate someone
8.4 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ›ktÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ›ktÉ™ / (BrE)
to behave in a bossy or bullying way towards someone, often by intimidating or overawing them

Examples

To hector a person
During the meeting, she hectored anyone who disagreed with her plans.
He would often hector his colleagues with unsolicited advice.
The manager was known to hector new employees on their first day.
