lurker

noun
someone who stays hidden, especially to observe
7.7 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ùÀêk…ôr / (AmE), / Ààl…úÀêk…ô / (BrE)
a person who stays hidden, especially in order to observe or wait for someone or something without being noticed.

Examples

A lurker in shadows
In nature documentaries, predators are often described as skilled lurkers while stalking their prey.
Police discovered a lurker hiding near the alley late at night.
The lurker waited silently in the shadows until everyone left the room.

Synonyms

  • slinker
    8.4 0.04/m
    a slinker moves quietly and often avoids responsibility or attention, while a lurker stays hidden to watch or wait.
