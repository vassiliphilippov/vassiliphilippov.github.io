breach of the peace

noun
act disturbing public order
5.9 4.1
Pronunciation: /briːtʃ əv ðə piːs/ (AmE), /briːtʃ əv ðə piːs/ (BrE)
an act that disturbs public order or causes a disturbance or disruption to public tranquility, often regarded as a minor offense in law

Examples

Police warned that any breach of the peace would not be tolerated at the festival.
The man was arrested for committing a breach of the peace during the demonstration.
