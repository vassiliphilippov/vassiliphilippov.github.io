zeke

noun
WWII Japanese fighter
Pronunciation: / ziːk / (AmE), / ziːk / (BrE)
the Allied reporting name for the Japanese Mitsubishi A6M 'Zero' fighter aircraft used during World War II

Examples

Zeke fighter plane
The squadron spotted a zeke flying low over the Pacific waters.
Allied pilots were trained to identify the distinctive shape of a zeke in combat.
During the battle, several zekes engaged the American bombers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spitfire - a British fighter aircraft used during World War II
  • synonym: zero - the Japanese Mitsubishi A6M fighter aircraft of World War II
