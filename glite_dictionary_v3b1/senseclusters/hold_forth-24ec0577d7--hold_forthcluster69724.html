hold forth

verb
speak at length about a topic
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈfɔrθ / (AmE), / həʊld ˈfɔːθ / (BrE)
to speak at length, often formally or authoritatively, about a particular topic

Examples

To hold forth confidently
At family gatherings, my uncle likes to hold forth about his travels around the world.
She was holding forth on the importance of healthy eating to anyone who would listen.
The professor would often hold forth on the history of philosophy during his classes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expound - to explain or present in detail
  • synonym: lecture - to deliver a formal talk or speech
