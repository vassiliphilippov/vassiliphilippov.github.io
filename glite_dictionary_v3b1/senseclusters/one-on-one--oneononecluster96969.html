one-on-one

adjective
direct competition between individuals
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌn ən ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌwʌn ən ˈwʌn / (BrE)
involving direct competition or interaction between one individual and another, especially in a sports context where one defensive player guards one offensive player

Examples

One-on-one defense
She prefers playing one-on-one rather than in a group.
The one-on-one matchup between the two players was exciting to watch.
The coach emphasized a one-on-one defense during practice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: man-to-man - defensive play where each defender guards a specific opponent
