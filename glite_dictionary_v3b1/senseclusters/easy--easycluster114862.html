easy

adjective
readily exploited or deceived
Pronunciation: / ˈiːzi / (AmE), / ˈiːzi / (BrE)
(of a person) readily exploited or tricked; lacking the ability or inclination to defend oneself from being deceived or harmed.

Examples

Easy target
He was an easy mark for scams because he trusted everyone.
The con artist considered her an easy target and tried to steal her purse.
Without much street experience, she proved to be an easy victim for the thieves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something; credulous
  • antonym: hard - difficult to deceive or exploit
  • synonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
