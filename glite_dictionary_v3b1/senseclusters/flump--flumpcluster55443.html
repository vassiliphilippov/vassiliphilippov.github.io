Synonyms

  • plump
    4.1 0.1/m
    plump sets something down suddenly and heavily, often making a soft thud, while flump means falling or dropping with a dull sound.
  • plop
    3.6 1.0/m
    plop drops or is put down with a soft, wet or audible sound, while flump suggests a heavier fall with a dull noise.
  • flop
    2.3 3/m
    flop means to fall or lie down suddenly, often from tiredness and without forceful noise, while flump implies a heavy, dull drop.
  • plunk
    6.0 1/m
    plunk puts or drops something down with a fairly loud, sharp sound, while flump describes a heavy fall with a duller, muffled noise.
  • plonk
    7.8 0.1/m
    plonk drops or puts something down noisily and often carelessly, while flump refers to a heavy, dull-settling fall rather than loud clatter.
