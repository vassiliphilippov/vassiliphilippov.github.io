accusative

adjective
relating to the grammatical object case
8.0 7.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkjuː.zə.tɪv/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkjuː.zə.tɪv/ (BrE)
(in grammar) relating to or denoting a grammatical case used to indicate the direct object of a verb or the object of certain prepositions.

Examples

Accusative pronoun
In Latin, the accusative case is used for direct objects of verbs.
Some languages, like Finnish, have an accusative distinct from other grammatical cases.
The teacher explained how to identify accusative endings in German nouns.
