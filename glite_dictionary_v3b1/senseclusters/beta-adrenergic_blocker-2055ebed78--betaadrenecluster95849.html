A drug that reduces blood pressure and heart rate by blocking beta-adrenergic receptors in the autonomic nervous system, used to treat conditions such as hypertension and arrhythmia.

Examples

Beta-adrenergic blockers lower pressure
Athletes are sometimes prohibited from taking beta-adrenergic blockers because they can affect heart rate.
He experienced fewer arrhythmia episodes after starting a beta-adrenergic blocker.
The doctor prescribed a beta-adrenergic blocker to help control her high blood pressure.
