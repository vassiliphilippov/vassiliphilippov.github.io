apocarpous

adjective
having separate carpels (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈkɑrpəs / (AmE), / ˌapəˈkɑːpəs / (BrE)
(of a flower's ovary) having carpels that are separate from one another, as in buttercups or roses.

Examples

Apocarpous flower ovary
In botany class, we studied flowers with apocarpous and syncarpous ovaries.
Roses produce apocarpous fruits, with each carpel developing independently.
The buttercup is an example of an apocarpous plant, with separate carpels in its ovary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: syncarpous - having carpels that are fused together
