homorganic

adjective
same place of articulation
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊmɔrˈɡænɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊmɔːˈɡænɪk / (BrE)
describing speech sounds that are produced at the same place of articulation in the mouth

Examples

Homorganic speech sounds
In the word 'ten', the sounds 't' and 'n' are homorganic because both use the tongue at the alveolar ridge.
Linguists often study homorganic consonant clusters to understand how speech sounds interact.
The nasal and stop in 'map' are not homorganic since they are produced at different places in the mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coarticulated - produced with two simultaneous places of articulation
  • antonym: heterorganic - produced at different places of articulation
