haste

noun
urgency or speed in action or movement
3.4 4.02/m
Pronunciation: / heÉªst / (AmE), / heÉªst / (BrE)
great speed or urgency of movement or action, often with carelessness or lack of thought

Examples

In haste often
Don't act in haste, or you may regret it later.
In their haste to leave, they left the lights on.
She finished the report in such haste that she forgot to check for errors.
Acting with haste rarely leads to the best decisions.
He packed his bag in great haste and left without saying goodbye.
In her haste to finish the project, she made several mistakes.
In his haste to leave, he forgot his keys on the kitchen table.
Many accidents happen through haste rather than genuine emergencies.
She finished the report in great haste, which led to several mistakes.

Synonyms

  • rushing
    1.0 0.00/m
    rushing is moving quickly, often without care, while haste is the overall speed or urgency that can cause careless actions.
  • rush
    1.8 23/m

  • precipitation
    8.6 0.01/m
