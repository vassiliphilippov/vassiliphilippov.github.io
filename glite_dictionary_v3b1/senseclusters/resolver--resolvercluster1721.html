resolver

noun
problem-solving device
5.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɑːlvər / (AmE), / rɪˈzɒlvə / (BrE)
a person, device, or program that finds solutions to problems or settles disputes

Examples

DNS resolver
The DNS resolver translates website names into IP addresses for your computer.
A skilled resolver can quickly identify and fix technical issues in a network.
Modern operating systems include a built-in resolver to handle domain name queries.
The company hired a resolver to address complex technical issues quickly.
In the software, the resolver automatically corrected the configuration errors.
As a skilled resolver, she was often called upon to mediate disputes between teams.
The company hired a resolver to address ongoing conflicts between team members.
As a skilled resolver, she quickly found a compromise that satisfied both parties.
Whenever there is a disagreement, he acts as the resolver and helps everyone reach an agreement.

  • antonym: troublemaker - a person who causes problems or conflict
