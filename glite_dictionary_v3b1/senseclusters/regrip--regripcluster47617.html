regrip

verb
to adjust grip again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɡrɪp / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɡrɪp / (BrE)
to take hold of something again or adjust one's grip for better control or safety

Examples

To regrip the rock
The climber paused to regrip the rock before continuing up the steep wall.
You should regrip your tennis racket if it starts to slip during a match.
She had to regrip the heavy box to avoid dropping it on the stairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grip - to take hold of something firmly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something held
