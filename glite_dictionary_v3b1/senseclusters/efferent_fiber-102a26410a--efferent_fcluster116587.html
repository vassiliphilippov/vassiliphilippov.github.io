efferent fiber

noun
nerve transmitting signals from CNS to body
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɛfɚənt ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛfərənt ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a nerve fiber that carries impulses away from the central nervous system to muscles or glands

Examples

Efferent fiber to muscles
Damage to an efferent fiber may result in loss of movement or glandular function.
Motor neurons typically use efferent fibers to transmit commands from the brain to the muscles.
The doctor explained how efferent fibers help the body respond to external stimuli.

Related Terms

  • antonym: afferent fiber - a nerve fiber that carries impulses toward the central nervous system
