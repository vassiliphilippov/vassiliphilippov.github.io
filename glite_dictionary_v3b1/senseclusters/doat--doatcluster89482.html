doat

noun
foolish old person
9.9
Pronunciation: / doʊt / (AmE), / dəʊt / (BrE)
an old person who is considered foolish or weak-minded, especially due to age (archaic)

Examples

An old doat
In the village, the children laughed at the doat who told strange stories.
He was dismissed as a harmless doat by those who did not know his past.
Legends warned not to ignore the words of a doat, for wisdom can hide in folly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dotard - an old person, especially one who has become weak or senile
  • antonym: sage - a wise old person
