bluffer

noun
person who tries to deceive or bluff
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌfər / (AmE), / ˈblʌfə / (BrE)
a person who tries to deceive others by pretending to have abilities, knowledge, or intentions that they do not possess

Examples

A bluffer in poker
Don't be fooled by her confidence; she's actually a skilled bluffer.
Everyone knew Tom was a bluffer when it came to poker.
The interview panel quickly realized he was just a bluffer lacking real experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceiver - a person who deceives
  • antonym: honest person - a person who does not bluff or deceive others
