Quercus nigra

noun
deciduous oak tree of southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs ˈnaɪɡrə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˈnɪɡrə / (BrE)
A species of deciduous oak tree native to the southeastern United States, commonly known as the water oak, often grown for shade and thriving in wet soils.

Examples

Quercus nigra tree
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Quercus nigra in swampy areas.
Due to its rapid growth, Quercus nigra is often planted along streets in southern cities.
The park is lined with mature Quercus nigra providing welcome shade in the summer.
