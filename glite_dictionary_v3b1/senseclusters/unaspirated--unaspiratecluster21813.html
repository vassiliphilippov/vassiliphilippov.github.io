unaspirated

adjective
without breath burst
8.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈæspəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈaspɪreɪtɪd / (BrE)
pronounced without a strong burst of breath after the release of a consonant

Examples

Unaspirated consonant sound
The 'k' sound in 'skill' is typically unaspirated in English.
In some languages, the difference between aspirated and unaspirated consonants changes the meaning of words.
Phonetics students learn to distinguish unaspirated sounds by listening carefully to the release of air.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aspirated - pronounced with a strong burst of breath
