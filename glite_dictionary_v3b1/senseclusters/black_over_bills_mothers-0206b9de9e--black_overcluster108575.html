black over Bill's mother's

humorous British phrase about impending bad weather
Pronunciation: / blæk oʊvɚ bɪlz ˈmʌðɚz / (AmE), / blæk əʊvə bɪlz ˈmʌðəz / (BrE)
(British, humorous) Used to indicate that dark clouds are approaching and it looks like rain, or to comment wryly on a situation that isn't as bad as it seems.
