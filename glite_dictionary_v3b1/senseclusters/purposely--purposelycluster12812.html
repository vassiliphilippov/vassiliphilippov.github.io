purposely

adverb
deliberately; with intention
3.5 4.57/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ùÀê.p…ôs.li / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀê.p…ôs.li / (BrE)
in a way that is done deliberately or with a clear intention

Examples

He purposely ignored
He purposely left the message unread to make a point.
She purposely ignored his advice, wanting to make her own decisions.
The teacher purposely gave the students extra time for the test.

Synonyms

  • intentionally
    3.1 11/m
    intentionally is a neutral, common adverb for doing something by choice, while purposely stresses a clear, intended aim.
  • deliberately
    3.7 22/m
    deliberately is often used in careful or formal speech to show planned action, while purposely means done with clear intention.
  • purposefully
    3.9 3.25/m
    purposefully shows determination or a clear aim in action, while purposely focuses simply on the action being intentional.
  • on purpose
    1.6 6.03/m
    on purpose is a common spoken phrase for doing something intentionally, while purposely is a single-word adverb used in writing or speech.
  • knowingly
    3.9 5.03/m
    knowingly means doing something with full awareness of facts or consequences, while purposely means acting with a clear intention.
  • consciously
    3.7 9.90/m
    consciously means doing something with full awareness and attention, while purposely means doing it with a deliberate goal or plan.
  • wittingly
    7.5 0.20/m
    wittingly is a formal word for acting with knowledge, while purposely is more common and broadly means doing something by design.
  • by design
    4.0 2.16/m
    by design means something happens because it was planned, while purposely describes the manner of acting with a clear, deliberate intention.
  • by choice
    3.1 1.64/m
    by choice means doing something because one decides freely, while purposely simply says the action was done deliberately rather than by accident.
