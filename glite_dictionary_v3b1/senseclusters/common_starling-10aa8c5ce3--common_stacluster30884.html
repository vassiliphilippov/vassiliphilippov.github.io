common starling

noun
small, iridescent gregarious bird (Sturnus vulgaris)
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑmən ˈstɑrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkɒmən ˈstɑːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small, gregarious bird (Sturnus vulgaris) with dark, iridescent plumage, often nesting near buildings and widespread worldwide

Examples

Common starlings searching insects
Common starlings are often seen searching for insects on suburban lawns.
A flock of common starlings gathered on the telephone wires as dusk approached.
The common starling is known for its ability to mimic the calls of other birds and even human sounds.
