TDR

noun
transferable development right
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ti di …ër / (AmE), / tiÀê diÀê …ëÀê / (BrE)
a legal right allowing a landowner to transfer the right to develop their property to another location or party

Examples

TDR program
The city introduced TDR to encourage the preservation of historic buildings.
Developers purchased TDR from rural landowners to increase building heights downtown.
By selling their TDR, farmers could protect their land from future development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: easement - a legal right to use another's land for a specific purpose
  • synonym: zoning - laws regulating land use and development
