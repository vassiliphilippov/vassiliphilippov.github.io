all at once

adverb
simultaneously, at the same time
Pronunciation: / ɔl ət ˈwʌns / (AmE), / ɔːl ət ˈwʌns / (BrE)
At the same time; simultaneously.

Examples

Do everything all at once
She tried to carry too many things all at once and dropped her keys.
The crowd cheered all at once as the band took the stage.
The lights in the entire building went out all at once.

Related Terms

  • antonym: one after another - in succession, not at the same time
  • synonym: simultaneously - at the same time
