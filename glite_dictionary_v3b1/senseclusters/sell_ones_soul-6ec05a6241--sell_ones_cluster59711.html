sell one's soul

verb
compromise integrity for gain
Pronunciation: / sɛl wʌnz soʊl / (AmE), / sɛl wʌnz səʊl / (BrE)
to betray your morals or principles for personal gain, such as money, power, or success

Examples

To sell his soul for power
Many believe that he had to sell his soul to reach that level of success.
She would never sell her soul just to become famous.
Some politicians are accused of selling their soul for power.
He accused his rival of selling his soul for power and influence.
Many artists worry about selling their soul to achieve commercial success.
She felt like she was selling her soul by accepting the bribe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compromise - to accept standards that are lower than desirable
  • antonym: keep one's integrity - to maintain one's moral principles
