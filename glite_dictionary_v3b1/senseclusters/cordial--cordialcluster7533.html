cordial

adjective
sincerely and strongly felt
4.1 2.40/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îr.d í…ôl / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀê.di.…ôl / (BrE)
warm, friendly, and sincere (describing feelings, attitudes, or manners)

Examples

A cordial greeting
He offered a cordial welcome to all his guests.
She expressed her cordial thanks for their hospitality.
Their cordial dislike of each other was obvious to everyone.
Her cordial smile put everyone at ease as they entered the room.
The manager gave a cordial welcome to the new employees at the meeting.
They have always maintained cordial relations despite the competition between their companies.
Despite their differences, their conversation remained cordial throughout the evening.
Everyone gave him a cordial welcome when he arrived at the meeting.
She extended a cordial invitation to dinner at her house.

Synonyms

  • warm
    1.0 16/m
    warm is affectionate and shows heartfelt feeling, while cordial is polite and sincere, often used in formal situations.
  • genial
    4.8 1.17/m
    genial is cheerful and pleasantly warm in manner, while cordial sounds more formal and stresses sincere politeness.
  • amiable
    3.4 1.99/m
    amiable is easy to like with a pleasant manner, while cordial is warmer and often more formally sincere.
  • affable
    5.5 1.38/m
    affable is friendly and easy to talk to, while cordial is warmer and may sound more formal or sincere.
  • friendly
    1.0 36/m
    friendly is kind and not hostile in many situations, while cordial is warmer, more sincere, and sometimes more formal.
