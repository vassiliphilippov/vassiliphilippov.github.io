cordial

adjective
sincerely and strongly felt
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.dʒəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.di.əl / (BrE)
warm, friendly, and sincere (describing feelings, attitudes, or manners)

Examples

A cordial greeting
He offered a cordial welcome to all his guests.
She expressed her cordial thanks for their hospitality.
Their cordial dislike of each other was obvious to everyone.
Her cordial smile put everyone at ease as they entered the room.
The manager gave a cordial welcome to the new employees at the meeting.
They have always maintained cordial relations despite the competition between their companies.
Despite their differences, their conversation remained cordial throughout the evening.
Everyone gave him a cordial welcome when he arrived at the meeting.
She extended a cordial invitation to dinner at her house.
