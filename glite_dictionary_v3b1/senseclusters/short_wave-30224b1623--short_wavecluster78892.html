short wave

noun
radio wave (less than 100 m)
3.4 4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrt weɪv / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt weɪv / (BrE)
a radio wave with a wavelength of less than 100 meters, typically having a frequency greater than 3 megahertz, used especially for international broadcasting

Examples

A short wave radio
Because short wave can travel long distances, it remains useful in remote areas.
Many hobbyists still listen to foreign broadcasts via short wave frequencies.
The BBC World Service was once widely heard on short wave radio around the globe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: long wave - a radio wave with a wavelength longer than 1000 meters (a frequency below 300 kilohertz)
  • antonym: medium wave - a radio wave with a wavelength between 100 and 1000 meters (a frequency between 300 kHz and 3 MHz)
