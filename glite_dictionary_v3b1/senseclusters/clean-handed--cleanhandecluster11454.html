clean-handed

adjective
free from guilt or wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / klin ˈhændɪd / (AmE), / kliːn ˈhændɪd / (BrE)
not involved in wrongdoing; free from guilt or moral corruption

Examples

Be clean-handed always
He prided himself on being clean-handed, never accepting bribes or favors.
She was known to be clean-handed throughout her long career in politics.
The investigator assured the public that he remained clean-handed during the entire process.
