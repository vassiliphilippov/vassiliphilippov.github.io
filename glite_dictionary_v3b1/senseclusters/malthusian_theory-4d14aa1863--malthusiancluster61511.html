Malthusian theory

noun
theory that population growth outpaces resources
7.8
Pronunciation: / mælˈθuːziən ˈθɪəri / (AmE), / mælˈθjuːzɪən ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
the theory, proposed by Thomas Malthus, that population growth tends to outpace the means of subsistence, leading to potential shortages and societal issues

Examples

The Malthusian theory of population
Many 19th-century economists debated the implications of the Malthusian theory on food supply and poverty.
Recent studies have revisited the Malthusian theory in light of global population trends and climate change.
The Malthusian theory suggests that unchecked population growth will eventually strain available resources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Malthusianism - the belief system derived from Malthusian theory
  • antonym: demographic transition - theory describing the transition from high birth and death rates to lower rates as a country develops
