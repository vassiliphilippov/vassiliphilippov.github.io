cream

noun
soothing or moisturizing skin preparation
1.0 18/m
Pronunciation: / k…πim / (AmE), / k…πiÀêm / (BrE)
a thick, smooth substance applied to the skin to soothe, moisturize, or protect it

Examples

Skin cream
Apply the moisturizing cream to your hands every night for softer skin.
She bought an anti-aging cream from the pharmacy.
The doctor recommended using a steroid cream for the rash.

Synonyms

  • moisturizer
    3.2 3.74/m
    a moisturizer is a general product for keeping skin soft and hydrated, while cream is a thick, specific form used on skin.
  • emollient
    7.5 0.16/m
    an emollient is a skin-softening liquid or cream used in cosmetics, while cream is the common product you apply to skin.
  • ointment
    3.3 2.22/m
    an ointment is a thicker, oilier, often medicinal paste used to heal skin, while cream is smoother and absorbs more easily.
  • salve
    6.4 0.58/m
    a salve is a semisolid healing mix often for wounds and irritation, while cream is milder and used for general care.
  • balm
    3.7 1.16/m
    a balm is a thick, soothing stick or paste often for lips or small areas, while cream covers larger skin areas.
