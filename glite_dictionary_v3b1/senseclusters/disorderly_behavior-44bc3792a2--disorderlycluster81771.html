disorderly behavior

noun
unruly or disruptive public conduct
3.7 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / d…™sÀà…îrd…ôrli b…™Ààhe…™vj…ö / (AmE), / d…™sÀà…îÀêd…ôli b…™Ààhe…™vj…ô / (BrE)
behavior that disturbs public peace, disrupts order, or causes inconvenience to others, especially in a public place.

Examples

Disorderly behavior in public
Residents complained about disorderly behavior in the park late at night.
The police arrested him for disorderly behavior after he caused a disturbance at the concert.
The university has a strict policy against disorderly behavior on campus.

Synonyms

  • disorderly conduct
    5.0 0.78/m
    disorderly conduct is a legal term for acts that disturb public order, while disorderly behavior describes such disruptive actions more generally.
  • disturbance of the peace
    5.1 0.02/m
    disturbance of the peace refers to acts that interrupt public calm and may be charged by law, while disorderly behavior disturbs others.
  • breach of the peace
    5.0 0.04/m
    breach of the peace is a legal phrase for breaking public calm, while disorderly behavior names the act that causes that disturbance.
  • disturbance
    3.7 2.10/m
    disturbance is a noisy or disorderly event like a commotion, while disorderly behavior focuses on the people's actions causing that event.
  • disorderliness
    6.0 0.01/m
    disorderliness is a general state of being unruly or lacking order, while disorderly behavior shows those actions in public.
  • rowdiness
    4.5 0.07/m
    rowdiness is noisy, rough, and boisterous conduct often in a crowd, while disorderly behavior covers any act that disturbs public order.
  • unruliness
    7.2 0.06/m
    unruliness is the quality of being hard to control or disobedient, while disorderly behavior specifically disturbs public peace.
