antigen

noun
substance that triggers immune response
6.8 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.tɪ.dʒən / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.dʒən / (BrE)
A substance that induces an immune response in the body, especially the production of antibodies.

Examples

The antigen triggers response
Each antigen can trigger a specific response from the immune system.
Researchers identified a new antigen associated with the disease.
The vaccine contains a weakened antigen to stimulate the body's immune system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antibody - a blood protein produced in response to and counteracting a specific antigen
