financial institution

noun
organization dealing with financial services
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃəl ˌɪnstəˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃl ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
an organization that collects funds, such as deposits, and invests them in financial assets, including banks, credit unions, and other entities involved in financial services

Examples

After receiving a loan from the financial institution, she was able to expand her small business.
Credit unions are a type of financial institution that often serve local communities.
The government announced stricter regulations for every financial institution operating in the country.
