Jesuitism

noun
theology or practices of the Jesuits
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛz.juˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛz.ju.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the doctrines, methods, or practices of the Jesuits, sometimes regarded, especially historically, as characterized by casuistry or intrigue

Examples

Jesuitism doctrines
Accusations of Jesuitism were common in the heated religious debates of the era.
Some critics accused the politician of Jesuitism in his evasive answers.
The history essay examined the influence of Jesuitism on 17th-century education.

Related Terms

  • synonym: casuistry - the use of clever but unsound reasoning, especially in relation to moral questions
