centrist

adjective
advocating moderate political views
9.9 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.trɪst/ (AmE), /ˈsen.trɪst/ (BrE)
Supporting or advocating moderate or middle-of-the-road political views, avoiding extreme positions associated with either the left or the right.

Examples

Centrist political views
His centrist stance often puts him at odds with both liberals and conservatives.
She takes a centrist approach, preferring compromise over ideological extremes.
The new party's centrist policies appeal to voters disillusioned with partisanship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; tending to oppose change
  • antonym: liberal - favoring progress or reform in political and social matters
  • synonym: moderate - advocating or holding moderate political views or policies
  • antonym: radical - advocating extreme or far-reaching political ideas
