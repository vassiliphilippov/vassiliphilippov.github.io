deracination

noun
uprooting or removal from native environment
Pronunciation: / diˌræsɪˈneɪʃən /; / dɪˌræsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌræsɪˈneɪʃən /; / dɪˌræsɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of uprooting or removing something or someone from its natural or original environment; displacement

Examples

The deracination of people
His poetry frequently laments the deracination he experienced after moving abroad.
The deracination of indigenous cultures often occurs during periods of colonization.
Urbanization led to the deracination of many rural communities.
Forced deracination can have lasting effects on communities and their identity.
Some critics lament the deracination of traditional customs in modern society.
The deracination of native plants has disrupted the local ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uprooting - the process of being pulled out by the roots; displacement
