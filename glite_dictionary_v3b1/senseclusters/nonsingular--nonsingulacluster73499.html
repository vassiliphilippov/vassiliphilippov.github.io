nonsingular

adjective
smooth or regular
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈsɪŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈsɪŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
smooth or regular; not having points where a mathematical object fails to be well-behaved, such as cusps or self-intersections

Examples

Nonsingular surface
The mathematician proved that the surface was nonsingular throughout its entire domain.
A nonsingular matrix is essential for solving certain systems of equations.
After checking the graph, she confirmed it was nonsingular with no sharp points or breaks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: singular - having points where a mathematical object is not well-behaved
