ooze from

verb
leak or flow out slowly
Pronunciation: / uːz frəm / (AmE), / uːz frɒm / (BrE)
to leak or flow slowly out from a place or object

Examples

To ooze from a cut
A thick sap began to ooze from the cut in the tree trunk.
After the rain, water would ooze from the cracks in the pavement.
Blood could ooze from a shallow wound if not cleaned properly.

Related Terms
