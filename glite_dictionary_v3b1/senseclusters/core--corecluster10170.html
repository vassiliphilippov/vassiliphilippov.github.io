core

noun
magnetic bar in a coil
Pronunciation: / kɔr / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
a bar of magnetic material, such as soft iron, placed inside a coil to increase the coil's inductance

Examples

Iron core in coil
By inserting a core into the solenoid, the inductance is significantly increased.
Modern electromagnets often employ a soft iron core wrapped with wire.
The transformer uses an iron core to improve efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inductor - a coil or device designed to introduce inductance into a circuit
