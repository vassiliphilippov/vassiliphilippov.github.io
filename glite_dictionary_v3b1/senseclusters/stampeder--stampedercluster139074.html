stampeder

noun
one in a stampede
8.6
Pronunciation: / stæmˈpiːdər / (AmE), / stæmˈpiːdə / (BrE)
a person or animal that rushes forward with others in a sudden, uncontrolled movement

Examples

A stampeder runs ahead
The stampeder broke away from the group and ran toward the exit.
During the gold rush, every stampeder hoped to find fortune in the new land.
As the alarm sounded, each stampeder pushed forward in a rush to escape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bystander - a person who is present but does not take part
  • synonym: participant - a person who takes part in an event or activity
