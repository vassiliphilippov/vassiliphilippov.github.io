Pandora

proper noun
first woman in Greek mythology
9.3
Pronunciation: /pæn.ˈdɔː.rə/ (AmE), /pæn.ˈdɔː.rə/ (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the first woman, created by Hephaestus at the command of Zeus, who was given a box containing all the world's evils.

Examples

Myth of Pandora
According to the myth, Pandora was responsible for opening the forbidden box that released evils into the world.
Many scholars analyze the symbolism of Pandora in ancient Greek literature.
The story of Pandora serves as a warning about curiosity and disobedience in mythology.
