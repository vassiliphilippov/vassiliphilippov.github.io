cocoon

verb
retreat into a safe or protected state
8.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈkuːn/ (AmE), /kə.ˈkuːn/ (BrE)
to retreat into a protected, safe, or isolated state, often to avoid discomfort or danger

Examples

To cocoon at home
After a long, stressful week, she likes to cocoon at home with a good book.
During the pandemic, many people chose to cocoon instead of going out.
He cocooned himself in his apartment, avoiding all social contact.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolate - to separate oneself from others or the environment
  • antonym: socialize - to interact with others in a social setting
  • synonym: withdraw - to move back or away, especially into privacy or safety
