setscrew

noun
screw for securing parts together
8.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈset.skruː/ (AmE), /ˈset.skruː/ (BrE)
a screw, often without a head, used to secure or adjust the position of one object within or against another object

Examples

A setscrew holds parts
He lost the tiny setscrew that held the knob in place.
Make sure the setscrew is tightened properly to prevent the gear from slipping.
The mechanic used a setscrew to fasten the pulley securely onto the motor shaft.
If the setscrew is too loose, the tension will not hold properly.
The mechanic tightened the setscrew to secure the gear on the shaft.
You'll need a small hex key to adjust the setscrew on this spring mechanism.

  • synonym: grub screw - screw without a head, used to secure an object in place on a shaft
