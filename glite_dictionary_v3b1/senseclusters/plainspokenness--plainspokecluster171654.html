plainspokenness

noun
direct, honest speech
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪnˌspoʊkənnəs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌspəʊkənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of speaking in a direct, clear, and honest way without trying to hide the truth

Examples

Her plainspokenness style
Her plainspokenness made her a trusted advisor among her colleagues.
People appreciated his plainspokenness during difficult conversations at work.
The politician's plainspokenness set her apart from others who avoided direct answers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: candor - the quality of being honest and straightforward
  • antonym: evasiveness - the quality of avoiding giving clear or direct answers
  • synonym: frankness - the quality of being open, honest, and direct in speech
