civil

adjective
relating to citizens, not military
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪvəl /; / ˈsɪv.əl / (BrE)
relating to ordinary citizens or society, especially as distinct from the military, religion, or natural/astronomical matters; official or legal in nature

Examples

Civil society
Only civil personnel are allowed in this section of the building, not the military.
The dispute was settled by civil courts, rather than by a military tribunal.
The mayor met with all civil authorities to coordinate the response to the emergency.
Jury duty is an important civil responsibility for all citizens.
She fought for civil liberty and equality throughout her life.
The government must protect the civil rights of its people.
A civil day is measured from midnight to midnight, regardless of sunrise or sunset.
The civil calendar differs from the religious calendar in some countries.
The government follows the civil year for all official matters.
