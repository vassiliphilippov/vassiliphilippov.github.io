jabberwocky

noun
nonsense or meaningless language
6.5 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒæb.ər.wɑː.kiː/ /; / /ˈdʒæb.ər.wɔː.kiː/ (AmE), /ˈdʒæb.ə.wɒk.iː/ (BrE)
language or writing that is nonsensical or meaningless, especially in imitation of the style of Lewis Carroll's poem 'Jabberwocky'.

Examples

Nonsense jabberwocky language
Children often delight in inventing their own jabberwocky during playtime.
His explanation was pure jabberwocky—it sounded impressive but meant absolutely nothing.
The essay descended into jabberwocky halfway through, making it impossible to understand.
