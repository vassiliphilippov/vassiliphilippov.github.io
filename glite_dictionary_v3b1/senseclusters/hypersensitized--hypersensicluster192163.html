hypersensitized

adjective
having excessive sensitivity to a stimulus
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈsɛnsəˌtaɪzd /; / ˌhaɪ.pɚˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪzd / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈsɛnsɪˌtʌɪzd /; / ˌhaɪ.pəˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪzd / (BrE)
having an unusually high sensitivity or susceptibility, especially to a specific substance or stimulus; excessively reactive, often due to prior exposure or sensitization.

Examples

Be hypersensitized to chemicals
After repeated exposure to the chemical, she became hypersensitized and developed a rash.
Children with allergies are often hypersensitized to common environmental triggers.
The patient's immune system was hypersensitized following the experimental treatment.
After the vaccine, some patients became hypersensitised to common environmental allergens.
Because of his condition, he was hypersensitised to even the smallest traces of pollen in the air.
Her skin became hypersensitised after repeated exposure to the new detergent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allergic - having an allergy or sensitivity to substances
  • antonym: desensitised - having reduced sensitivity
  • synonym: hypersensitive - extremely sensitive or easily affected by something
