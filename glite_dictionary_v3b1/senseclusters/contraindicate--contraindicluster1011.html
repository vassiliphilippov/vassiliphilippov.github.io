contraindicate

verb
make a treatment inadvisable (medical)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑntrəˈɪndɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈɪndɪkeɪt / (BrE)
(of a condition or circumstance) to make a particular treatment or procedure inadvisable.

Examples

Conditions contraindicate treatment
A history of bleeding disorders can sometimes contraindicate surgery.
The presence of severe liver disease may contraindicate the use of certain medications.
This vaccine is contraindicated in patients with known allergies to its components.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indicate - to suggest as necessary or desirable (medical sense)
