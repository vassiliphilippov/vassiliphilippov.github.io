endocarp

noun
hard inner fruit layer containing the seed
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndoʊˌkɑrp / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌkɑːp / (BrE)
the hard inner layer of the fruit wall (pericarp) in some fruits, such as peaches or cherries, that surrounds and protects the seed

Examples

Hard endocarp layer
In a peach, the seed is enclosed within a hard endocarp that forms the pit.
Olive oil is extracted from the flesh, not from the endocarp containing the pit.
The cherry's endocarp is the tough covering around the seed.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: mesocarp - the fleshy middle layer of the pericarp of a fruit
  • degree higher: pericarp - the part of a fruit formed from the ovary wall after flowering
