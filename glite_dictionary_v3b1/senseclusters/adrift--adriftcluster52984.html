adrift

adjective, adverb
without direction; off course
Pronunciation: / əˈdrɪft / (AmE), / əˈdrɪft / (BrE)
without direction, purpose, or guidance; aimless or off course

Examples

Feeling adrift in life
After the storm, the ship was left adrift in the open sea.
Her mind went adrift during the monotonous lecture, barely registering the words.
Many young people feel adrift after finishing university and facing the challenges of adult life.
After losing his job, Tom felt completely adrift and unsure what to do next.
Many graduates feel adrift during their first year out of university.
The boat floated adrift in the open sea after the engine failed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anchored - held in place; not drifting
  • synonym: lost - unable to find one's way; not knowing one's whereabouts
  • antonym: purposeful - having or showing determination or resolve
