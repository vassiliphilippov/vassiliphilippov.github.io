kanaka

noun
Pacific Island worker
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɑːkə / (AmE), / kəˈnɑːkə / (BrE)
a worker from the Pacific Islands, especially employed on plantations in Australia and other regions during the 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

Plantation kanaka worker
Many kanaka were brought to work on sugar plantations in Queensland during the 1800s.
The history museum displayed artifacts belonging to a kanaka community from the early 20th century.
Some descendants of kanaka workers still live in Australia today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laborer - a person who does unskilled physical work
  • synonym: plantation - a large farm where crops are grown, often using hired workers
