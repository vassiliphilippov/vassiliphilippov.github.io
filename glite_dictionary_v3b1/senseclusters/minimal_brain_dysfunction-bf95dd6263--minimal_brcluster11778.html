minimal brain dysfunction

noun
historical medical term for a behavioral disorder
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnəməl breɪn dɪsˌfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪməl breɪn dɪsˌfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a medical condition, mainly affecting boys, characterized by behavioral and learning disorders, historically used to describe what is now more commonly known as attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)

Examples

Minimal brain dysfunction disorder
In the 1960s, many cases of hyperactivity and poor concentration were attributed to minimal brain dysfunction.
Modern understanding has largely replaced the term minimal brain dysfunction with ADHD in clinical settings.
The doctor explained that the child's symptoms could be related to minimal brain dysfunction rather than a purely emotional problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attention deficit hyperactivity disorder - a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity
