superstardom

noun
extreme fame
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌstɑrdəm / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌstɑːdəm / (BrE)
the state of being extremely famous, successful, and influential, especially as a performer, public figure, or in entertainment or sports

Examples

Rise to superstardom
After her breakthrough role, she quickly rose to superstardom and became a household name.
The pressures of superstardom can be overwhelming for young artists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or not famous
  • degree lower: stardom - the state of being a famous performer
