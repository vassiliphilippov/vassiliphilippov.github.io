casting vote

noun
deciding vote to break a tie
Pronunciation: / ˈkæstɪŋ voʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstɪŋ vəʊt / (BrE)
the vote given by the person presiding over a meeting to resolve a tie between opposing sides

Examples

The casting vote
In the event of a tie, the president shall exercise a casting vote.
She was appointed to the committee and holds the casting vote in case of deadlock.
The chairman used his casting vote to approve the proposal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deciding vote - a vote that determines the outcome in an otherwise even situation
