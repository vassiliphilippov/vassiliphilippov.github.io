confessionalism

noun
personal revelation style
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a style or approach in literature, art, or therapy that emphasizes open personal confession of private feelings or experiences

Examples

Confessionalism in poetry
Modern poetry often features confessionalism, with writers sharing deeply personal stories.
The artist's confessionalism made her paintings feel raw and emotionally honest.
Therapists sometimes encourage confessionalism to help clients process difficult emotions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autobiographical - relating to a person's own life or experiences
  • synonym: introspection - the examination of one's own thoughts and feelings
