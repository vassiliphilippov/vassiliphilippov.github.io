to move quickly back and forth or shake with small, rapid movements (can be physical or used for things like a voice or feelings)

Examples

To vibrate quickly
If you pluck the guitar string, you can see it vibrate from side to side.
The bridge began to vibrate slightly as the heavy truck drove across it.
When the phone is on silent, it will vibrate to alert you of new messages.
Her voice seemed to vibrate with excitement as she shared the news.
The alarm on my phone began to vibrate loudly on the nightstand.
You could feel the walls vibrate during the thunderstorm.

Synonyms

  • quiver
    4.5 5.34/m
    quiver is a slight, quick shaking of the voice or skin, while vibrate means small fast back-and-forth movements.
  • tremble
    2.5 18/m
    tremble is an involuntary shaking from fear, cold, or excitement, while vibrate means small rapid movements not tied to emotion.
  • shake
    1.0 50/m
    shake is a broad, often stronger movement of things or people, while vibrate means small rapid back-and-forth motion.
  • oscillate
    6.5 1.21/m
    oscillate means to move back and forth in a regular rhythm, while vibrate means small, quick shaking that may be less regular.
