Cybele

proper noun
Phrygian mother goddess; nature and fertility deity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪbəˌli / (AmE), / ˈsɪbɪli / (BrE)
an ancient Phrygian mother goddess, often associated with nature, fertility, and mountains, identified with Greek Rhea and Roman Ops

Examples

Cybele mother goddess
In ancient times, Cybele was worshipped as the mother of all gods and protector of cities.
Statues of Cybele have been found throughout Asia Minor, symbolizing her importance in ancient religions.
The cult of Cybele was later incorporated into Roman worship and spread across the empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ops - Roman goddess of fertility, counterpart of Cybele
  • synonym: Rhea - Greek mother of the gods, counterpart of Cybele
