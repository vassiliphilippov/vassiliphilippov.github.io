desocialization

noun
removal of public services
9.8
Pronunciation: /də.soʊ.ʃə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.soʊ.ʃə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /də.səʊ.ʃə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.səʊ.ʃə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of reducing or removing social or public functions, services, or ownership, often by privatization or ending state support

Examples

Desocialization of services
The desocialization of healthcare led to increased costs for many citizens.
Rapid desocialization of public transport resulted in fewer affordable options for commuters.
Critics argue that desocialization can widen the gap between rich and poor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalization - the process of bringing private assets under public or state ownership
  • synonym: privatization - the transfer of ownership from the public sector to private individuals or organizations
