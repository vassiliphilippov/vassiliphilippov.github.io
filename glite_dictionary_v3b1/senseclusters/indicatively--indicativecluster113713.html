indicatively

adverb
approximately, as a guide
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪkətɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪkətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that gives a general idea or rough estimate rather than an exact figure

Examples

Indicatively estimates time
The manager indicatively estimated the project would take about six months to complete.
Prices were listed indicatively and could change depending on demand.
He indicatively pointed to the area on the map where the event might occur.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approximately - in a way that is close to a number or amount but not exact
  • antonym: precisely - in an exact and accurate way
