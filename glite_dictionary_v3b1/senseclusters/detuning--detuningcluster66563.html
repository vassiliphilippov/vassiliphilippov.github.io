detuning

noun
altering standard pitch
7.4
Pronunciation: / diːˈtuːnɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈtjuːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of changing the pitch or frequency of an instrument or device away from its standard tuning

Examples

Detuning a guitar
The band achieved a unique sound by detuning their guitars before recording.
Synthesizer players often use detuning to create richer, more complex tones.
Some genres of music rely heavily on detuning to produce a darker atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retuning - the act of tuning again or to a different standard
  • antonym: tuning - the process of adjusting an instrument to the correct pitch
