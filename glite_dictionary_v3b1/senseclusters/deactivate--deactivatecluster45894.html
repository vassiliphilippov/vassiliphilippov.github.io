deactivate

verb
make inactive; turn off or disable
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈæktɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make something inactive or no longer effective, especially by turning off a device, function, or system

Examples

To deactivate an alarm
Please deactivate your alarm system before entering the house.
She had to deactivate her account due to security concerns.
The technician will deactivate the old access code today.

  • antonym: activate - to make active or operative
