plagioclase

noun
a type of feldspar mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪdʒiəˌkleɪs / (AmE), / ˈpleɪdʒɪəˌkleɪz / (BrE)
any of a group of triclinic feldspar minerals, typically forming rocks such as granite and basalt

Examples

Plagioclase mineral group
Geologists can identify plagioclase by its distinctive twinning patterns under a microscope.
Most basalt rocks are rich in plagioclase, giving them their characteristic appearance.
The granite sample contains abundant plagioclase crystals mixed with quartz and mica.
