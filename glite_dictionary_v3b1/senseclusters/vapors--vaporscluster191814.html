vapors

noun
(archaic) state of depression
8.1 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈveɪ.pərz/ (AmE), /ˈveɪ.pəz/ (BrE)
(archaic) A state of depression or low spirits, especially as a temporary condition.

Examples

In the vapors
He was in the vapors for days after losing his job, barely speaking to anyone.
In old novels, ladies would sometimes fall into vapors after receiving distressing news.
The doctor dismissed her complaints as mere vapors, not realizing she was deeply unhappy.
He was overcome by the vapours that lingered after his loss, barely speaking for days.
In old novels, ladies often suffered from the vapours and withdrew to their chambers.
Medical texts of the eighteenth century sometimes connected the vapours to imbalances in the body's humours.
