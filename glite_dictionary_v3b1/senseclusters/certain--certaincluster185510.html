certain

adjective
confident and sure; having no doubt
1.0 43/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀê.t…ôn / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀê.t…ôn / (BrE)
having or showing no doubt; confident and sure about something

Examples

Certain about the answer
He sounded certain when he explained his decision to the team.
I am certain we'll arrive on time if we leave now.
She felt certain that she had locked the door before leaving the house.

Synonyms

  • sure
    1.0 434/m
    sure is a common, informal way to say you have no doubt, while certain is more neutral or formal.
  • confident
    2.5 39/m
    confident describes self-assurance about ability or outcome, while certain focuses on having no doubt about facts.
  • convinced
    2.8 20/m
    convinced means you feel completely certain after good reasons or proof, while certain is a general lack of doubt.
  • assured
    4.1 0.00/m
    assured sounds more formal and often means guaranteed or calm confidence, while certain simply means having no doubt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confident - feeling sure about one's abilities or qualities
  • antonym: unsure - feeling doubt or lack of confidence
