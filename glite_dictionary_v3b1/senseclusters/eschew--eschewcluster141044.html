eschew

verb
deliberately avoid or abstain from
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪsˈtʃu / (AmE), / ɪsˈtʃuː / (BrE)
to deliberately avoid or abstain from something; to stay clear of

Examples

To eschew bad habits
Many health experts advise people to eschew processed foods for a healthier lifestyle.
She tends to eschew social media during exam season to avoid distractions.
The company decided to eschew traditional advertising and focus on digital marketing instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoid - to keep away from or stop oneself from doing something
  • antonym: embrace - to accept or support willingly or enthusiastically
