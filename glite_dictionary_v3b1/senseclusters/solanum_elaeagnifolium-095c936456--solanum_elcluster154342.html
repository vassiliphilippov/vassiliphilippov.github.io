Solanum elaeagnifolium

noun
species of nightshade with silvery leaves and berries
9.9
Pronunciation: / soʊˈleɪnəm ɪˌliːəɡˌnɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / səˈleɪnəm ɪˌliːəɡˌnɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A species of nightshade native to the central United States to South America, characterized by silvery foliage, bluish to white flowers, and round berries sometimes used traditionally to curdle milk.

Examples

Solanum elaeagnifolium plant species
Solanum elaeagnifolium can be invasive in pastures, outcompeting native grasses.
Farmers sometimes use berries of Solanum elaeagnifolium in cheese-making to curdle milk.
The silvery leaves of Solanum elaeagnifolium make it easy to identify among other weeds.
