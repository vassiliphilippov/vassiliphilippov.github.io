Sol

proper noun
Roman sun god; personification of the sun
9.8
Pronunciation: / soʊl / (AmE), / sɒl / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god who personifies the sun and is associated with light and day; the Roman counterpart of the Greek god Helios.

Examples

Sol the sun god
In ancient Roman religion, Sol was worshipped as the mighty god of the sun.
Statues of Sol often depict him driving a chariot across the sky.
The festival of Sol Invictus was celebrated during the winter solstice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Helios - Greek god who personifies the sun
