mismatch

noun
a bad or unsuitable match
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌmatʃ / (BrE)
A combination of people or things that do not go well together or are not suitable for each other.

Examples

A mismatch of colors
The color scheme was a mismatch that clashed with the overall decor.
Their personalities were enough of a mismatch that they decided not to date again.
There was a clear mismatch between the candidate's skills and the job requirements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incompatibility - the state of being unable to exist or work together in harmony
  • antonym: match - a suitable pair or partnership
