unstop

verb
to become unblocked
4.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstɑp / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɒp / (BrE)
to remove a blockage from something so that it can flow or work again, especially a pipe, drain, or gutter

Examples

To unstop a drain
After hours of rain, the gutter finally unstopped and water drained away.
The pipe unstopped once the plumber removed the buildup inside.
When the leaves were cleared, the fountain unstopped and began to flow again.
The plumber had to unstop the bathroom drain after it became clogged with hair.
She used a plunger to unstop the kitchen sink quickly.
After a heavy rain, workers were called to unstop the blocked street gutters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: block - to prevent movement or flow by obstructing
  • synonym: clear - to remove an obstruction or blockage
