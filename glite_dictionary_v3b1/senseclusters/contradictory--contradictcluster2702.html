contradictory

noun
statement that opposes another in logic
2.6 6.6
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.trə.ˈdɪk.tər.iː/ /; / /kən.trə.ˈdɪk.tər.iː/ (AmE), /kɒn.trə.ˈdɪk.tə.riː/ /; / /kən.trə.ˈdɪk.tə.riː/ (BrE)
(logic) A statement or proposition that is in direct opposition to another, such that both cannot be true or both cannot be false at the same time.

Examples

A pair of contradictories
Debaters often illustrate their points using pairs of contradictories for emphasis.
Every proposition has a unique contradictory that cannot be true at the same time as itself.
The statements 'It is raining' and 'It is not raining' are contradictories in logic.

  • antonym: tautology - a statement that is true in every possible interpretation
