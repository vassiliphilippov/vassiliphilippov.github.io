china clay

noun
fine white clay used in ceramics and industry
5.4 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtʃaɪ.nə kleɪ/ /; / /ˈtʃiː.nɑː kleɪ/ (AmE), /ˈtʃaɪ.nə kleɪ/ /; / /ˈtʃiː.nɑː kleɪ/ (BrE)
a fine, usually white clay formed by the weathering of aluminous minerals, especially feldspar; used chiefly in ceramics, paper, and other industries

Examples

China clay for ceramics
China clay is often added to paper to improve its whiteness and printability.
Large deposits of china clay are found in Cornwall, England.
Potters prefer to use china clay for its purity and smooth texture.
