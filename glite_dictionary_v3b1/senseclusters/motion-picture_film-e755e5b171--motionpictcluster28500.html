motion-picture film

noun
film used for shooting movies
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊ.ʃən ˈpɪk.tʃər fɪlm / (AmE), / ˌməʊ.ʃən ˈpɪk.tʃə fɪlm / (BrE)
Photographic film, typically several hundred feet long and rolled on a spool, used for recording moving images in a movie camera.

Examples

Motion-picture film roll
Archivists carefully store old motion-picture film to preserve classic movies for future generations.
The director insisted on shooting the entire movie on motion-picture film for its unique aesthetic.
Vintage cameras require reels of motion-picture film that are no longer widely available.
