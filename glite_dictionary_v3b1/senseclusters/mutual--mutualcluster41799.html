mutual

adjective
shared by two or more parties
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmjutʃuəl /; / ˈmju.tʃu.əl / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəl /; / ˈmjuː.tʃu.əl / (BrE)
shared or experienced equally by two or more people or groups

Examples

Mutual respect
After years of competition, the firms reached a mutual understanding.
They have a mutual respect that makes their collaboration successful.
Trust is essential for any mutual agreement between companies.
Both companies have mutual interests in pursuing this partnership.
The agreement was based on mutual respect and trust.
They developed a mutual understanding after several discussions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individual - relating to a single person or thing rather than to a group
  • synonym: reciprocal - given, felt, or done in return; mutual
