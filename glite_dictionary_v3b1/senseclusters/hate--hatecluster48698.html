Synonyms

  • detest
    3.5 2.81/m
    detest means to dislike something very strongly and often with anger, while hate is a general strong feeling of dislike.
  • loathe
    5.3 2.71/m
    loathe expresses intense disgust and strong dislike, while hate covers strong aversion, hostility, or unwillingness more broadly.
  • abhor
    7.8 1.63/m
    abhor is a formal word for deep disgust and moral shock, while hate is more general strong dislike or hostility.
  • despise
    4.9 11/m
    despise shows contempt and low respect for someone, while hate means a strong dislike often mixed with hostility or unwillingness.
  • can't stand
    3.2 13/m
    can't stand is informal and says you cannot tolerate someone or something, while hate describes a deeper, stronger, lasting dislike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detest - to dislike intensely, often with a sense of moral disapproval
  • degree lower: dislike - to not like
  • degree higher: loathe - to feel intense disgust or hatred for
  • antonym: love - to feel deep affection or strong positive feeling for someone or something
