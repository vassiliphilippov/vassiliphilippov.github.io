hate

verb
to dislike intensely
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /heɪt/ (AmE), /heɪt/ (BrE)
to dislike someone or something very strongly; to feel or show strong aversion, hostility, or unwillingness

Examples

To hate doing chores
I absolutely hate lying to my friends, even when the truth is difficult.
Many people hate being stuck in traffic for hours every day.
She hates broccoli so much that she won't even touch it.
He hated being the center of attention at social events.
She openly hates her former boss and never speaks kindly of him.
They hate anyone who betrays their trust.
She was hating to admit it, but the facts showed she had been wrong about the plan.
I hate to interrupt, but we need to leave soon if we want to catch the train.
He hated having to say goodbye to his friends at the end of the summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detest - to dislike intensely, often with a sense of moral disapproval
  • degree lower: dislike - to not like
  • degree higher: loathe - to feel intense disgust or hatred for
  • antonym: love - to feel deep affection or strong positive feeling for someone or something
