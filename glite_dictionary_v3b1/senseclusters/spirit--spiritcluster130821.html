spirit

noun
a person's emotional state or mood
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪt /; / ˈspɪr.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪt /; / ˈspɪr.ɪt / (BrE)
a person's mood, energy, or emotional state, especially when lively or positive

Examples

In good spirits
After receiving good news, his spirit lifted instantly.
Despite the setbacks, she managed to keep her spirit high.
Her cheerful spirit made everyone around her feel happier.
Despite facing many setbacks, she maintained a positive spirit throughout her journey.
He is admired for his generous spirit and unwavering determination.
The team's winning spirit inspired everyone to work harder.
His spirit lifted the entire team's performance during the game.
The young dancer performed with such spirit that the audience cheered enthusiastically.
You should approach every challenge with spirit and determination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: body - the physical structure of a person or an organism; opposite of spirit in some contexts
  • antonym: depression - a state of dejection or low mood
  • synonym: energy - the strength and vitality required for sustained activity
  • degree lower: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment or interest; can be an aspect of spirit
  • antonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • synonym: mood - a temporary state of mind or feeling
  • synonym: soul - the spiritual or immaterial part of a human being; synonymous in some contexts
  • synonym: vivacity - the quality of being attractively lively and animated
