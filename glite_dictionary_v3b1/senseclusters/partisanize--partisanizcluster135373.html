partisanize

verb
to recruit as partisans
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːrtɪsəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtɪsənaɪz / (BrE)
to recruit or convert people into partisans or guerrilla fighters, especially during a conflict or occupation

Examples

To partisanize villagers
The resistance leaders hoped to partisanize the villagers to strengthen their fight against the occupiers.
Efforts to partisanize the youth were met with both enthusiasm and fear in the occupied region.
During the war, several groups attempted to partisanize local communities to join their cause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demobilize - to release people from military service or disband a fighting group
  • synonym: mobilize - to organize people for active service or support
