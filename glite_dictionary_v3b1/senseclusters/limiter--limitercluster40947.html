limiter

noun
electronic circuit limiting signal amplitude
7.7 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪm.ɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈlɪm.ə.tər/ (AmE), /ˈlɪm.ɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈlɪm.ə.tə/ (BrE)
a device or mechanism that restricts or controls the maximum level or rate of something (such as sound, speed, or data)

Examples

An audio limiter
A limiter is commonly used in audio equipment to prevent signal distortion.
If the limiter is set too low, the audio may sound unnaturally compressed.
The engineer installed a limiter to protect the speakers from damage.
Many services implement a limiter to cap API calls per minute and prevent abuse.
The network engineer added limiters to control packet bursts and stabilize traffic during peak hours.
You can configure a rate limiter in the gateway to reject excessive requests automatically.
The sound technician installed a limiter to prevent the speakers from being damaged by loud music.
Modern cars often include a speed limiter to help drivers stay within legal limits.
Using a limiter in audio production helps maintain consistent volume levels throughout a recording.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplifier - a device that increases the strength of signals
  • synonym: clipper - an electronic circuit that cuts off part of a signal above or below a certain level
  • synonym: rate limit - a specified maximum number of events allowed in a given time period
  • synonym: regulator - a device that controls or maintains a certain level
  • synonym: throttle - to limit or slow down the rate of data transfer or requests
