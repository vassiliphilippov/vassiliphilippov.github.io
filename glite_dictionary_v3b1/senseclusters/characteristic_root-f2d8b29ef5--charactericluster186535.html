characteristic root

noun
eigenvalue of a matrix
8.2 8.2
Pronunciation: / kəˌræktəˈrɪstɪk ruːt / (AmE), / kəˌræktəˈrɪstɪk ruːt / (BrE)
(Mathematics) A number such that, when it is multiplied by the identity matrix and subtracted from a given square matrix, the resulting matrix has a zero determinant; also known as an eigenvalue.

Examples

A characteristic root of matrix
Every square matrix has at least one characteristic root.
The characteristic roots of the matrix determine its stability in dynamic systems.
To find a characteristic root, solve the matrix's characteristic equation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eigenvalue - a number indicating how much a matrix stretches or shrinks space in a given direction
