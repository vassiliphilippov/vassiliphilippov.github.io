stoke up

verb
add fuel or increase intensity/energy
Pronunciation: / stoʊk ʌp / (AmE), / stəʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to add fuel to a fire or to increase something, especially energy, strength, or intensity.

Examples

To stoke up a fire
A good meal can stoke up your energy for the whole day.
She always stokes up the fireplace before the family gathers in the living room.
The coach tried to stoke up enthusiasm among the players before the big game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boost - to increase or raise something
  • antonym: diminish - to make or become less
  • synonym: fuel - to supply with fuel; to feed or intensify
