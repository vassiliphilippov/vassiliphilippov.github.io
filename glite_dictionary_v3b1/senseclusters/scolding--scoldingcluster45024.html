scolding

noun
harsh verbal reprimand
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈskoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
an angry or harsh rebuke or reprimand, especially spoken to someone for wrongdoing or misbehavior

Examples

A scolding from teacher
After forgetting his homework again, Tom received a scolding from his teacher.
Despite the scolding, he was grateful for the advice.
Her mother gave her a stern scolding for coming home so late.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
