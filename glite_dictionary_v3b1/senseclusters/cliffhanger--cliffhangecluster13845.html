cliffhanger

noun
suspenseful contest or story ending
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪfˌhæŋər /; / ˈklɪfˌhæŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈklɪfˌhæŋə / (BrE)
an event, story, or ending that leaves the audience in suspense or uncertainty about what will happen next

Examples

A cliffhanger ending
Last night's episode ended on a cliffhanger, leaving viewers desperate to know what happens next.
The championship game turned into a true cliffhanger that kept fans on the edge of their seats.
The election proved to be a cliffhanger as votes were counted late into the night.
Every chapter in the novel seemed to have a cliffhanger that kept me turning the pages.
He hates movies that end on a cliffhanger without resolving the main plot.
The season finale ended with a dramatic cliffhanger, leaving viewers eager for the next episode.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nail-biter - something causing intense suspense or anxiety
  • antonym: resolution - the conclusion or solution to a story or problem
  • synonym: suspense - a state of anxious uncertainty about what may happen
