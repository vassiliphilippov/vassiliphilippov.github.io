grieve

verb
feel intense sorrow
3.2
Pronunciation: / ɡriv / (AmE), / ɡriːv / (BrE)
to feel intense sorrow, especially because of a loss

Examples

To grieve a loss
Even years later, he still grieved the loss of his childhood home.
Many people grieve for months after losing a loved one.
She needed time alone to grieve after the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mourn - to feel or show deep sorrow for someone's death
  • antonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
