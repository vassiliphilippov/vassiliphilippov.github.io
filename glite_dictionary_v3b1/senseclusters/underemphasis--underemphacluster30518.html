underemphasis

noun
insufficient attention
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈɛmfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈemfəsɪs / (BrE)
the act or result of giving too little attention or importance to something

Examples

The underemphasis on skills
The underemphasis on practical skills in the curriculum concerned many parents.
Critics pointed out the underemphasis of environmental issues in the government plan.
There was a clear underemphasis on teamwork during the training session.
The report's underemphasis on financial risks led investors to overlook potential problems.
His underemphasis of the team's achievements made their success seem less significant.
Through deliberate underemphasis, the speaker shifted attention away from the controversial topic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emphasis - special importance or attention given to something
  • synonym: neglect - failure to give proper attention or care
  • antonym: overemphasis - too much importance or attention given to something
  • synonym: understatement - the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it is
