A noncancerous growth made up of glandular tissue, often found on the lining of the colon or rectum, that can potentially develop into cancer.

Examples

An adenomatous polyp in colon
During a colonoscopy, the doctor discovered an adenomatous polyp that required removal.
Most cases of colorectal cancer begin as an adenomatous polyp in the inner lining of the colon.
The biopsy revealed that the growth was an adenomatous polyp rather than a malignant tumor.
