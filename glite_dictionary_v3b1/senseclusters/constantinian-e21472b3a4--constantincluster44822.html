Constantinian

adjective
church-state alliance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːnstənˈtɪniən / (AmE), / ˌkɒnstənˈtɪniən / (BrE)
relating to a political or theological view that supports a close alliance between church and state, especially using state power to support a church

Examples

Constantinian model
The Constantinian model shaped the relationship between church and state for centuries in Europe.
Some theologians argue that a Constantinian approach compromises the independence of religious institutions.
Debates about religious freedom often reference the dangers of Constantinian policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
  • synonym: theocratic - relating to a system where religious leaders control the government
