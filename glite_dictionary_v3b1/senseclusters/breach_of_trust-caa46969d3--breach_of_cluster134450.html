breach of trust

noun
failure to uphold a duty of trust
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ əv trʌst / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv trʌst / (BrE)
an act of breaking the trust or responsibility placed in someone, especially by failing in a duty or acting dishonestly

Examples

A serious breach of trust
A serious breach of trust can damage a person's professional reputation permanently.
Investors filed a lawsuit claiming a breach of trust against the fund manager.
The accountant was found guilty of breach of trust after misusing the company's funds.
Customers withdrew their accounts after a breach of trust was revealed at the bank.
The accountant was dismissed for a breach of trust with company funds.
To lie under oath is considered a serious breach of trust in the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betrayal - an act of betraying someone's trust
  • synonym: embezzlement - fraudulent taking of property by someone entrusted with it
  • degree lower: fiduciary duty - a legal duty to act in another's best interest
  • antonym: loyalty - faithfulness or devotion to someone or something
