neutral

adjective
not chemically reactive
5.2 0.42/m
Pronunciation: / ÀànjuÀêtr…ôl / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀêtr…ôl / (BrE)
not chemically reactive; having little or no ability to react with other substances

Examples

Neutral gas in chemistry
Because helium is a neutral gas, it does not easily participate in chemical reactions.
Some metals appear shiny and neutral because they are resistant to corrosion.
The laboratory uses neutral substances to prevent unwanted side reactions.

Synonyms

  • inert
    5.9 1.19/m
    inert describes a substance that does not react with others in normal conditions, while neutral means not chemically reactive.
  • unreactive
    6.3 0.01/m
    unreactive refers to a substance that rarely reacts under given conditions, while neutral means not chemically reactive in itself.
  • inactive
    6.0 0.22/m
    inactive describes a substance not taking part in a reaction at present, while neutral means not chemically reactive.
  • indifferent
    6.7 0.07/m
    indifferent commonly describes a person's lack of interest, while neutral means not chemically reactive when used for substances.
