air-to-surface missile

noun
missile launched from aircraft at ground targets
Pronunciation: / ˌɛr tə ˈsɜrfəs ˈmɪsaɪl / (AmE), / ˌeə tə ˈsɜːfɪs ˈmɪsaɪl / (BrE)
a missile designed to be launched from an aircraft and strike a target on the ground

Examples

Modern air-to-surface missiles
Modern air-to-surface missiles can be guided with remarkable precision.
The fighter jet launched an air-to-surface missile at the enemy position.
The pilot was trained in the use of air-to-surface missiles during combat operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: surface-to-air missile - a missile launched from the ground to destroy aircraft or other missiles
