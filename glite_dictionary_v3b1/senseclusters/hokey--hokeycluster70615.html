hokey

adjective
overly sentimental; insincere
8.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊ.kiː/ /; / /ˈhoʊ.kɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊ.kiː/ /; / /ˈhəʊ.kɪ/ (BrE)
artificial, overly sentimental, or insincere in a way that seems fake or exaggerated

Examples

A hokey apology
His hokey apology convinced no one in the audience.
She thought the commercial was too hokey and turned it off halfway through.
The movie's plot was so hokey that nobody took it seriously.
Her speech sounded hokey and rehearsed rather than heartfelt.
I can't stand those hokey advertisements with fake smiles and over-the-top sincerity.
The movie's emotional ending was so hokey that it made me roll my eyes.
His speech about friendship sounded hokey to the audience.
Some people enjoy musicals, but others find them a bit hokey.
The film was hokey, with predictable dialogue and exaggerated emotions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrived - deliberately created rather than arising naturally
  • synonym: corny - lacking originality or overly sentimental
  • antonym: genuine - authentic or sincere
  • synonym: schmaltzy - excessively sentimental, especially in an insincere way
  • synonym: sentimental - showing or causing exaggerated feelings of tenderness or sadness
  • antonym: sincere - genuine, without pretending or deceit
  • synonym: stilted - stiff, self-conscious, or unnatural
