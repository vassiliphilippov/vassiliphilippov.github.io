potency

noun
capacity to produce strong effects
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊ.tən.si / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.tən.si / (BrE)
the capacity to produce a strong effect, especially physiological or chemical; strength or efficacy

Examples

Potency of medicine
Researchers are testing the potency of the vaccine to ensure its effectiveness.
The potency of the chemical was much higher than initially expected.
The new drug has shown remarkable potency against the virus in clinical trials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: efficacy - the ability to produce a desired or intended result
  • synonym: strength - the degree of being strong or powerful, especially in concentration or effect
  • antonym: weakness - the lack of strength or potency; inability to produce strong effects
