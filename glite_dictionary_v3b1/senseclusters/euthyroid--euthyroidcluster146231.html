euthyroid

noun
person with normal thyroid
Pronunciation: / juːˈθaɪrɔɪd / (AmE), / juːˈθaɪrɔɪd / (BrE)
a person who has normal thyroid function or normal levels of thyroid hormones

Examples

A euthyroid patient
The doctor confirmed that the patient was a euthyroid after reviewing the blood test results.
As a euthyroid, she did not require any thyroid medication.
Most people in the study were classified as euthyroid based on their hormone levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperthyroid - a person with overactive thyroid function
  • antonym: hypothyroid - a person with underactive thyroid function
