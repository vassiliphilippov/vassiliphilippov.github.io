occipital vein

noun
vein draining the occipital region
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɪpɪtəl veɪn / (AmE), / ɒkˈsɪpɪt(ə)l veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the occipital region at the back of the skull

Examples

Occipital vein behind skull
An injury to the occipital vein can lead to swelling and bruising behind the skull.
During surgery, the occipital vein must be handled carefully to prevent excessive bleeding.
The occipital vein plays a crucial role in draining blood from the scalp and back of the head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart
