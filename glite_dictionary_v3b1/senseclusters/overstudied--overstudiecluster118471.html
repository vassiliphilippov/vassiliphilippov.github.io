overstudied

adjective
excessively rehearsed
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstʌdid / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstʌdid / (BrE)
deliberately prepared or rehearsed to the point of appearing artificial or lacking naturalness

Examples

Overstudied performance
The actor's overstudied gestures made the scene feel unnatural to the audience.
Her speech sounded overstudied, as if she had memorized every word without emotion.
Critics noted that the performance was too overstudied and lacked genuine feeling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrived - deliberately created rather than natural or spontaneous
  • antonym: spontaneous - performed or occurring as a result of a sudden impulse or inclination
