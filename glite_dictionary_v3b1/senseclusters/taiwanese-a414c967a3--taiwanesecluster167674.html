Taiwanese

noun
a Chinese language variety spoken in Taiwan and Fujian
Pronunciation: / taɪˈwɑːniːz / (AmE), / taɪˈwɑːniːz / (BrE)
any of the forms of Chinese spoken in Taiwan or originally from Fujian (Fukien) province, especially Minnan (Hokkien, Southern Min).

Examples

Taiwanese language
Taiwanese differs significantly from Mandarin Chinese in tone and vocabulary.
Many people in southern Fujian speak Taiwanese as their first language.
She is studying Taiwanese to communicate with her grandparents.
