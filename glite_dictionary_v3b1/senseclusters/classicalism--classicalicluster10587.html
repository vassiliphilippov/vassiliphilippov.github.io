classicalism

noun
European art and literary movement favoring form and rationality
7.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a movement in literature and art, especially of the 17th and 18th centuries in Europe, characterized by a focus on rationality, restraint, and adherence to strict forms, often inspired by ancient Greek and Roman models

Examples

Classicalism in art
Many neoclassical buildings reflect the ideals of classicalism in their symmetry and proportion.
The spread of classicalism throughout Europe profoundly shaped the aesthetics of painting and sculpture.
Writers influenced by classicalism emphasized clarity, order, and logical structure in their works.
