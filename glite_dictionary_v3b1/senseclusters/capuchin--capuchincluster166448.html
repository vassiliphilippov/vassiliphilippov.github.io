capuchin

noun
a small Central/South American monkey
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.ju.tʃɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæp.ju.tʃɪn / (BrE)
A small monkey of Central and South America, known for its thick hair on the head resembling a monk's cowl.

Examples

Capuchin monkey
At the zoo, we watched a playful capuchin swing from branch to branch.
Many documentaries feature the capuchin because of its expressive face and social behavior.
The capuchin is known for its intelligence and ability to use tools in the wild.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monkey - a primate of the suborder Simian, typically with a long tail
