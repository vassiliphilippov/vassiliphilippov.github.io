anaconda

noun
large South American snake
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈkɑndə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈkɒndə / (BrE)
a very large, non-venomous, semi-aquatic snake native to tropical South America, known for living in rivers and swamps

Examples

The anaconda snake
The anaconda is one of the largest snakes in the world and can be found in the Amazon basin.
The riverbank was muddy, but an anaconda slithered silently among the reeds.
We saw a documentary featuring a giant anaconda hunting for prey in the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boa constrictor - a large non-venomous snake of tropical America
