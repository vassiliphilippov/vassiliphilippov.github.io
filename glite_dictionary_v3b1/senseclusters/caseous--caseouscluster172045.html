caseous

adjective
cheeselike, especially of diseased tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.si.əs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.si.əs / (BrE)
(of tissue) having a cheeselike appearance or consistency, especially as a result of disease or necrosis.

Examples

Caseous tissue
Certain infections can cause tissue to develop a caseous appearance.
The autopsy revealed large caseous masses in the lymph nodes.
The pathologist described the lung lesion as caseous necrosis, characteristic of tuberculosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheesy - resembling or having the qualities of cheese
  • synonym: necrotic - relating to the death of tissue
