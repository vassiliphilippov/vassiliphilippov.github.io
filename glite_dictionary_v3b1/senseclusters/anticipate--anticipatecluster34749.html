anticipate

verb
expect as likely to occur
1.0
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪsɪˌpeɪt /; / ænˈtɪs.ɪ.peɪt / (AmE), / ænˈtɪsɪpeɪt /; / ænˈtɪs.ɪ.peɪt / (BrE)
to prepare for or act in advance of something expected

Examples

To anticipate problems
Many people anticipate a rise in fuel prices over the summer.
She could anticipate the outcome of the meeting from their earlier discussions.
The company did not anticipate such rapid growth this year.
By arriving early, she was able to anticipate and solve issues before the meeting started.
The company tried to anticipate customer needs by updating their software regularly.
We must anticipate any potential problems before launching the product.

Related Terms

  • antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain or lack belief that something will happen
  • synonym: expect - to think that something will happen or is likely
  • antonym: react - respond to something after it happens; antonym in the context of 'beforehand' vs 'after'
