precondition

noun
necessary prior condition or assumption
3.3 4.4
Pronunciation: /priː.kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.kən.ˈdɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a condition or requirement that must be met before something else can happen or be done

Examples

A precondition for success
Adequate funding is a basic precondition for the success of this project.
The agreement set the withdrawal of troops as a precondition for holding further talks.
Trust is often a precondition for effective teamwork in any organization.
Meeting the deadline was a precondition for receiving the grant funding.
One precondition for entry is that you must be over 18 years old.
Trust is often a precondition for successful teamwork in organizations.
A good education is often a precondition for achieving success in many careers.
He set honesty as a precondition for continuing the negotiation.
Registration is a precondition for attending the conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assumption - something accepted as true without proof
  • synonym: condition - a state that must exist for something else to happen
  • antonym: consequence - something that follows as a result
  • synonym: prerequisite - a thing that is required as a prior condition for something else to happen
  • synonym: requirement - something that is needed or demanded
