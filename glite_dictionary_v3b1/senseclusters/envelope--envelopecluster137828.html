envelope

noun
curve tangent to a family of curves
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.vəˌloʊp / (AmE), / ˈɛn.və.ləʊp / (BrE)
A curve that is tangent to each member of a given family of curves, forming their boundary or limit.

Examples

Mathematical envelope curve
In advanced geometry, students study the envelope formed by shifting ellipses.
The concept of an envelope helps in visualizing the outer limits of multiple curves.
The parabola serves as the envelope of this family of straight lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tangent - a straight line that touches a curve at one point without crossing it
