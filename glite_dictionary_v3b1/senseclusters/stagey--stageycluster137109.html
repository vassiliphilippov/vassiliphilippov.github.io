stagey

adjective
artificial or theatrical in manner
8.7 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsteÉªdÊ’i / (AmE), / ËˆsteÉªdÊ’i / (BrE)
(of behavior or style) artificial, exaggerated, or overly theatrical, as if intended for the stage rather than real life.

Examples

A stagey performance
Critics called the production too stagey and lacking in genuine feeling.
Her apology sounded stagey and insincere, as if she were performing for an audience.
The actor's stagey gestures distracted from the emotional depth of the scene.

Synonyms

  • theatrical
    4.8 6.98/m
    theatrical describes acting or stage-like drama used for effect, while stagey feels artificial and clearly meant for show.
  • histrionic
    8.7 0.43/m
    histrionic is strongly showy and seeks attention, while stagey suggests an artificial, staged feeling rather than a real one.
  • melodramatic
    5.6 1.95/m
    melodramatic is overly emotional and sentimental like a soap opera, while stagey stresses a fake, actor-like presentation.
  • overdramatic
    4.9 0.29/m
    overdramatic is more about too much emotion in speech or action, while stagey feels planned and deliberately theatrical.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural - genuine and unaffected, not artificial or staged
  • synonym: theatrical - exaggerated and overly dramatic; suitable for the theatre
