disgraced

adjective
having lost respect due to shame
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɡreɪst / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡreɪst / (BrE)
having lost respect, honor, or approval as a result of a shameful act or scandal.

Examples

Disgraced public figure
Once considered a hero, he returned home disgraced by his actions.
The disgraced athlete struggled to rebuild her reputation after the doping admission.
The disgraced politician resigned after the corruption scandal emerged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disgraceful - bringing or deserving disgrace
  • antonym: honored - having received honor or distinction
