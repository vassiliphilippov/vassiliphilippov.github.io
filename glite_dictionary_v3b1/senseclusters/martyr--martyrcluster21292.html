martyr

verb
to kill for one's beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrtər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtə / (BrE)
to punish, kill, or cause someone to suffer greatly because of their beliefs or principles

Examples

To martyr someone
He was martyred for standing up to oppression in his country.
Many saints were martyred for refusing to renounce their faith.
The regime martyred countless dissidents during the revolution.
She felt as though fate itself sought to martyr her for her convictions.
The novelist described how the hero was martyred by those who could not accept his belief.
The regime continued to martyr its opponents, causing international outrage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: execute - to kill someone as a punishment, especially by law
  • synonym: torment - to cause severe physical or mental suffering
  • synonym: torture - to inflict severe pain as a punishment or to force information
