unsturdy

adjective
not stable or strong
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstɝːdi / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɜːdi / (BrE)
not strong, stable, or well-built; likely to wobble or collapse easily

Examples

An unsturdy ladder
The ladder felt unsturdy as he climbed to the top shelf.
They avoided sitting on the unsturdy bench in the park.
After years of use, the table became unsturdy and needed repairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rickety - poorly made and likely to collapse
  • antonym: sturdy - strong and not easily moved or broken
  • synonym: unstable - not firm or fixed; likely to move or fall
