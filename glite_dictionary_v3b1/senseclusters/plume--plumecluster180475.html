plume

verb
spread or rise like a feather (of smoke, etc.)
6.3 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / plum / (AmE), / pljuÀêm / (BrE)
(of smoke, steam, etc.) to spread or rise into the air in a shape resembling a feather or plume.

Examples

Smoke plumes upward
Dark smoke began to plume from the factory chimneys as the machines started up.
Steam plumed from the kettle as it came to a boil.
The volcano erupted, pluming ash high into the sky.

Synonyms

  • billow
    5.3 3.53/m
    billow is to swell and move out in a rolling mass, while plume means to rise and spread into a thin feather-like shape.
  • arise
    2.7 1.06/m
    arise means to move upward into the air in a simple way, while plume means to rise and shape into a feather-like spread.
