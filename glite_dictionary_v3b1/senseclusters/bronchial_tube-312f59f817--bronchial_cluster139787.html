bronchial tube

noun
main airway branches from the trachea
4.7 2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɑːŋkiəl tuːb / (AmE), / ˈbrɒŋkɪəl tjuːb / (BrE)
either of the two main branches of the trachea that lead into the lungs, allowing the passage of air.

Examples

Narrow bronchial tubes
During an asthma attack, the bronchial tubes narrow and make it hard to breathe.
Inflammation of a bronchial tube can lead to difficulty breathing and persistent coughing.
The doctor explained that the bronchial tubes carry air directly to the lungs.
