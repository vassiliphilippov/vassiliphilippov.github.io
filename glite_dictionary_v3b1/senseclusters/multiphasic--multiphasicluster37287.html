multiphasic

adjective
having multiple phases
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltɪˈfeɪsɪk / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈfeɪsɪk / (BrE)
consisting of or containing two or more distinct physical phases at the same time, such as solid and liquid components in a material

Examples

Multiphasic material sample
The scientist observed a multiphasic reaction where both liquid and solid phases were present.
Certain medications are designed with a multiphasic release to provide effects at different times.
The multiphasic structure of the material gives it unique mechanical properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monophasic - having only one phase or form
