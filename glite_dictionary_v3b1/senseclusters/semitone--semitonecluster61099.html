semitone

noun
musical interval between adjacent keys
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmiˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌtəʊn / (BrE)
the smallest interval commonly used in Western music, equal to the interval between two adjacent keys on a keyboard instrument

Examples

A semitone step
Each step on the piano from one key to the next is a semitone apart.
In Western music, an octave consists of twelve semitones.
The melody ascended by a semitone at the end of each phrase.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: tone - a musical interval of a whole step; two semitones
