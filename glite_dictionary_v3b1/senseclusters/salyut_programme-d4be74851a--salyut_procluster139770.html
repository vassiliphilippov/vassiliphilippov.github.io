Salyut programme

noun
Soviet space station program (1970s)
Pronunciation: / sɐˈlʲut ˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈsaljuːt ˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
the series of Soviet space missions in the 1970s that launched the world's first space stations, called Salyut

Examples

The Salyut programme space stations
Many important experiments were conducted on board the Salyut programme space stations.
The Salyut programme marked the beginning of long-term human habitation in space.
The legacy of the Salyut programme influenced future space station designs, including Mir and the ISS.
