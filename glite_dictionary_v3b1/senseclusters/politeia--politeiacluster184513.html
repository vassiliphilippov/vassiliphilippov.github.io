politeia

proper noun
Plato's dialogue
Pronunciation: / poʊlɪˈteɪə / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈteɪə / (BrE)
the title of Plato's philosophical dialogue, commonly translated as 'The Republic', discussing justice and the ideal state

Examples

Plato's Politeia
Many students read Plato's politeia to understand his ideas about justice and society.
The philosopher referenced the politeia when discussing the concept of the ideal state.
Translations of politeia often differ in how they interpret Plato's original meaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Laws - another philosophical dialogue by Plato about law and governance
  • synonym: The Republic - the English title of Plato's dialogue 'Politeia'
