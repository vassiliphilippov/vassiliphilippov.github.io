Philip II

proper noun
king of ancient Macedonia, father of Alexander the Great
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlɪp ðə ˌsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp ði ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
King of ancient Macedonia (382–336 BC) and father of Alexander the Great, known for unifying Macedonia and laying the foundations for his son's empire.

Examples

Philip II of Macedonia
Philip II transformed Macedonia into a powerful kingdom through military and diplomatic reforms.
Historians credit Philip II with unifying the Greek states under Macedonian rule.
The assassination of Philip II paved the way for Alexander's succession to the throne.
