connectionism

noun
theory of mental networks
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɛkʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈnɛkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory or approach that mental processes can be understood as networks of simple units (like artificial neural networks), used in psychology, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence

Examples

Connectionism theory
Connectionism has influenced the development of artificial intelligence and machine learning models.
Many psychologists debate whether connectionism or symbolic approaches better explain human cognition.
The principles of connectionism are often used to design computer simulations of learning.
Many researchers in artificial intelligence study connectionism to understand how learning can emerge from simple units.
The rise of deep learning has renewed interest in connectionism as a model for human cognition.
Some scientists debate whether connectionism can fully explain complex reasoning and language abilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neural network - a computer system modeled on the human brain's network of neurons
  • antonym: symbolic AI - an approach to artificial intelligence based on explicit rules and symbols
  • antonym: symbolism - a theory that mental processes are based on symbolic representations
