paper tape reader

noun
device for reading punched paper tape
7.1 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpeɪ.pər teɪp ˈriː.dər/ (AmE), /ˈpeɪ.pə teɪp ˈriː.də/ (BrE)
a device used to read information encoded in a series of holes punched in a strip of paper tape, formerly used for data input in computers and machinery

Examples

A paper tape reader device
Early numerical control machines relied on a paper tape reader to load instructions.
The paper tape reader malfunctioned, causing a delay in the assembly line.
The technician fed the program into the computer using a paper tape reader.
