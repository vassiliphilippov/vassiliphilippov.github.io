Mylitta

proper noun
Babylonian goddess of love and war
9.9
Pronunciation: / maɪˈlɪtə / (AmE), / mɪˈlɪtə / (BrE)
a Babylonian and Assyrian goddess of love, fertility, and war, considered the counterpart to the Phoenician goddess Astarte.

Examples

Mylitta goddess of love
Stories about Mylitta emphasize her dual role as a bringer of love and fierce protector in battle.
Temples dedicated to Mylitta once stood in the old cities of Mesopotamia.
The ancient people of Babylon worshipped Mylitta as a powerful goddess of fertility and war.
