subharmonic

adjective
fractional frequency
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌb.hɑːrˈmɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.hɑːˈmɒ.nɪk / (BrE)
relating to a frequency or oscillation that is an exact fraction of a fundamental frequency in a system.

Examples

Subharmonic vibration pattern
The scientist observed a subharmonic response in the vibrating string experiment.
Engineers must account for subharmonic frequencies when designing stable electronic circuits.
A subharmonic signal can indicate resonance at a lower frequency than the main tone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmonic - relating to whole-number multiples of a fundamental frequency
