short-winded

adjective
breathing with difficulty
5.3
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt ˈwɪndɪd / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ˈwɪndɪd / (BrE)
breathing with difficulty or becoming easily out of breath, especially after physical activity

Examples

Be short-winded after exercise
After climbing the steep hill, he was noticeably short-winded and needed to rest.
His short-winded condition worried the doctor, who recommended further tests.
The old dog grew short-winded after running in the park.
After running up the stairs, she became short-winded and needed to rest.
As he got older, he noticed he was becoming more short-winded during exercise.
His explanation was short-winded and failed to address the main issues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: long-winded - talking or writing at length, often tediously so
  • synonym: short of breath - having difficulty breathing; unable to inhale enough air
