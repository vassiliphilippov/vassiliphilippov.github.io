unswaddled

adjective
not wrapped in cloth
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈswɑːdəld / (AmE), / ʌnˈswɒdəld / (BrE)
not wrapped tightly in cloth or a blanket, especially referring to a baby.

Examples

Unswaddled baby
The newborn was unswaddled and able to stretch her tiny legs freely.
After his nap, the baby was unswaddled and smiled at his mother.
Some parents prefer to keep their infants unswaddled during the day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: swaddled - wrapped tightly in cloth or a blanket
  • synonym: unwrapped - not covered or enclosed in wrapping
