subalpine fir

noun
a North American coniferous tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈælpaɪn fɝ / (AmE), / sʌbˈalpʌɪn fə / (BrE)
a medium-tall coniferous tree (Abies lasiocarpa) of western North America, especially the Rocky Mountains, with a narrow, columnar crown

Examples

A subalpine fir tree
A subalpine fir can grow at high elevations where few other trees survive.
Scientists studied the rings of the subalpine fir to track ancient climate changes.
The woods were thick with subalpine fir and spruce, making the trail cool and shady.
