feel like a sucker

verb
to feel foolish or deceived
5.3 6.9
Pronunciation: /fiːl laɪk ə ˈsʌk.ər/ (AmE), /fiːl laɪk ə ˈsʌk.ə/ (BrE)
to feel foolish or deceived, especially after being tricked or taken advantage of

Examples

To feel like a sucker after trick
After realizing he had paid far too much for the painting, he felt like a sucker.
She felt like a sucker when she discovered the sale was a scam.
Whenever I trust people too quickly, I end up feeling like a sucker.

Related Terms

  • antonym: feel smart - to feel clever or shrewd, the opposite of feeling deceived
