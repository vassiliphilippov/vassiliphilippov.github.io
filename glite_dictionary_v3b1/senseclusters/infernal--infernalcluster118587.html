infernal

adjective
relating to Hell; hellish
6.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɜrnəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːnəl / (BrE)
relating to hell or extremely evil, cruel, or tormenting (hellish)

Examples

Infernal cruelty
He described the desert's heat as an infernal furnace.
The infernal noise from the construction site kept us awake all night.
They feared an infernal punishment for their crimes.
She described her pain as an infernal torment that would not let her sleep.
The dictator ruled with an infernal cruelty that terrified the nation.
The movie's villain devised infernal traps for the heroes at every turn.
He shuddered at the thought of the infernal punishments awaiting the wicked.
In medieval literature, demons are often described as infernal creatures.
The ancient myth spoke of infernal realms where lost souls wandered in darkness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diabolical - extremely evil or wicked
  • synonym: fiendish - extremely cruel or wicked
  • antonym: heavenly - relating to heaven; very pleasant or good
  • synonym: hellish - resembling or relating to Hell
