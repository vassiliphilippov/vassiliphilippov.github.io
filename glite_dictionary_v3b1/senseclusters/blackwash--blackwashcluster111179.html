blackwash

verb
apply a black stain or wash
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
to apply a black stain or wash to a surface, especially as a coloring or finishing technique.

Examples

To blackwash a wall
After years of exposure, the stone walls were blackwashed to protect them from further weathering.
He carefully blackwashes each metal sculpture to achieve a consistent, matte finish.
The artist decided to blackwash the wooden frame to give it an antique look.

Related Terms

  • antonym: whitewash - to cover a surface with a white liquid mixture, especially for coloring or concealing
