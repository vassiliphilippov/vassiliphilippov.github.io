Know-Nothing Party

noun
19th-century American nativist political party
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊˈnʌθɪŋ ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˌnəʊˈnʌθɪŋ ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a nativist political party in the United States during the 1850s, known for its anti-immigrant and anti-Catholic stance

Examples

The Know-Nothing Party
Historians often discuss the impact of the Know-Nothing Party on American politics.
Members of the Know-Nothing Party were known for their secretive practices and strong nativist views.
The Know-Nothing Party gained support in the 1850s due to fears about immigration.
