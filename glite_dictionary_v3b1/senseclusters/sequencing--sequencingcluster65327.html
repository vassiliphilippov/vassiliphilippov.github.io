sequencing

noun
determining molecular order
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːkwənsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiːkwənsɪŋ / (BrE)
the laboratory process of determining the exact order of nucleotides in DNA or RNA, or amino acids in a protein

Examples

DNA sequencing
The research team used sequencing to map the entire genome of the bacteria.
Advances in sequencing have made it possible to diagnose genetic diseases more quickly.
Accurate sequencing is essential for understanding how viruses mutate over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analysis - the detailed examination of the elements or structure of something
  • synonym: genotyping - the process of determining differences in the genetic make-up
