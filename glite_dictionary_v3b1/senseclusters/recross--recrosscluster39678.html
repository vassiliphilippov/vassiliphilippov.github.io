recross

verb
to mate again for traits
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to mate an offspring again with one of its parents or with a specific strain to test or reinforce genetic traits

Examples

To recross the offspring
The scientist decided to recross the offspring with the original parent to confirm the inheritance pattern.
To strengthen the desired trait, breeders often recross hybrids with a specific strain.
After the initial experiment, the team planned to recross the plants to observe any genetic changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backcross - to cross a hybrid with one of its parents or an individual genetically similar to its parent
