convulsion

noun
violent disturbance or upheaval
6.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈvʌlʒən /; / kənˈvʌl.ʒən / (AmE), / kənˈvʌlʃən /; / kənˈvʌl.jʊ.ʒən / (BrE)
a sudden, violent disturbance or upheaval, especially in a political, social, or physical context

Examples

Political convulsion
Economic convulsions in the region led to widespread instability.
The country underwent a major convulsion during the revolution.
The earthquake caused a convulsion that shook the entire city.
The country went through a political convulsion that lasted for years.
The global financial crisis caused severe economic convulsions around the world.
There was a sudden convulsion in the city after the news broke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • synonym: upheaval - a violent or sudden change or disruption, especially in society or nature
