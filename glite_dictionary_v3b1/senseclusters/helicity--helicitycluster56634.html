helicity

noun
twisting in fluid flow
9.9
Pronunciation: /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /hɪ.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
a measure in fluid dynamics of how much flow or field lines twist and coil around each other

Examples

Helicity in a tornado
Scientists measured the helicity of the tornado to better understand its destructive power.
High helicity in the atmosphere can indicate a greater chance of severe storms.
The helicity of the ocean current affected how nutrients were distributed in the water.
