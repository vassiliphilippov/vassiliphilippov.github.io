clinch

noun
tight or amorous embrace
4.7
Pronunciation: / klɪntʃ / (AmE), / klɪntʃ / (BrE)
a close or tight hold, especially an embrace or a hold in boxing

Examples

A tight clinch
Caught in a passionate clinch, they did not notice anyone else in the room.
The photograph captured the couple in a joyful clinch after their engagement was announced.
Their clinch on the train platform was watched by several amused commuters.
After several intense rounds, the fighters spent more time in the clinch to catch their breath.
The boxer moved into a clinch to avoid further punches from his opponent.
The referee had to separate the boxers each time a clinch occurred.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embrace - an act of holding someone closely in one's arms
  • synonym: grapple - close hand-to-hand struggle or wrestling hold
  • synonym: hug - an act of holding someone tightly in one's arms to express affection
  • antonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
