speak in circles

verb
talk without making a clear point
2.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /spiːk ɪn ˈsɜːr.kəlz/ (AmE), /spiːk ɪn ˈsɜː.kəlz/ (BrE)
to talk in a way that avoids making a clear or direct point, often by repeating or being vague

Examples

To speak in circles often
During the meeting, she spoke in circles, leaving everyone confused about her true intentions.
Stop speaking in circles and just say what's on your mind.
Whenever asked about his plans, he would speak in circles, never giving a straight answer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat around the bush - to avoid talking about what is important
  • antonym: get to the point - to state directly what one means
