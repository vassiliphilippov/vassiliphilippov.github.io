dissociability

noun
ability to separate
8.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.soʊ.si.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /dɪ.soʊ.siː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /dɪ.səʊ.si.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /dɪ.səʊ.siː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the ability or tendency of a chemical compound to separate into smaller parts, especially ions, when dissolved in a solution

Examples

Dissociability in water
The dissociability of acids in water affects their strength and reactivity.
Scientists study the dissociability of different salts to understand their behavior in biological systems.
High dissociability means the compound easily breaks into ions when dissolved.
