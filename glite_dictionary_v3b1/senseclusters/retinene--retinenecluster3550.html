retinene

noun
pigment from rhodopsin in the eye
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtɪˌniːn / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪniːn / (BrE)
Either of two yellow to red retinal pigments formed from rhodopsin by the action of light.

Examples

Retinene pigment
Retinene is essential for the visual cycle and helps in the conversion of light into nerve signals.
Exposure to bright light causes rhodopsin to break down, producing retinene in the retina.
Researchers study retinene to better understand how the eye adapts to changes in light.

  • synonym: rhodopsin - a light-sensitive pigment in the retina
