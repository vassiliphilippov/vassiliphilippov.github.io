oxidase

noun
enzyme catalyzing oxidation
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the oxidation of a substance, especially by transferring oxygen from the atmosphere to a substrate

Examples

oxidase enzyme
Lactose oxidase is often used in diagnostic tests for glucose levels.
Researchers discovered a new bacterial oxidase that is involved in energy production.
The presence of oxidase in cells facilitates many important metabolic reactions.

Related Terms
