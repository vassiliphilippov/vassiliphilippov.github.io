tympanotomy

noun
eardrum incision surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: /tɪm.pə.ˈnɑː.tə.miː/ (AmE), /tɪm.pə.ˈnɒ.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an incision is made in the eardrum or middle ear to relieve pressure, drain fluid, or repair its structures

Examples

A tympanotomy surgery

A tympanotomy may be necessary when medication alone cannot resolve middle ear problems.
