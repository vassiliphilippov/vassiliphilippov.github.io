fibrosarcoma

noun
cancer of connective tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbroʊsɑrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbrəʊsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a rare type of cancer that develops from fibrous connective tissue in the body

Examples

A fibrosarcoma tumor
The surgeon confirmed that the mass was a fibrosarcoma after reviewing the biopsy results.
Treatment for fibrosarcoma often involves surgery followed by radiation therapy.
Researchers are studying new therapies to improve outcomes for patients with fibrosarcoma.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carcinoma - a cancer that begins in the skin or in tissues that line organs
  • synonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor of connective or other non-epithelial tissue
