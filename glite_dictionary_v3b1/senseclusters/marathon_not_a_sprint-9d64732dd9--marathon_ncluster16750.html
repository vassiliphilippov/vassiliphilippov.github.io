marathon, not a sprint

noun
requiring long-term effort
Pronunciation: / ˈmærəˌθɑn nɑt ə sprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈmɛrəθən nɒt ə sprɪnt / (BrE)
used to indicate that something requires sustained effort over a long period, rather than quick or immediate results

Examples

Marathon, not a sprint effort
Building a successful business is marathon, not a sprint, so patience and persistence are key.
Remember that learning a new language is marathon, not a sprint; steady progress matters more than speed.
Writing a novel requires you to treat it as marathon, not a sprint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marathon - a long-distance running race; used figuratively for a long, difficult task
  • antonym: sprint - a short, fast run; used figuratively for a brief, intense effort
