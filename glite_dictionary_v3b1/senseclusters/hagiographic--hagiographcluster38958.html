hagiographic

adjective
overly idealizing
Pronunciation: / ˌhæɡiəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhæɡiəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
describing someone or something in an overly idealized or saintly way, often ignoring faults; or relating to the writing about the lives of saints

Examples

A hagiographic story
The documentary took a hagiographic approach, focusing only on the leader's achievements.
Some critics found the film too hagiographic and lacking in objectivity.
His hagiographic article ignored any of the artist's controversies.
The author wrote a hagiographic account of the leader, ignoring any of his faults.
Many medieval texts are hagiographic, focusing on the miracles and virtues of saints.
Critics argued that the film's portrayal was too hagiographic and lacked balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biographical - relating to the story of a person's life
  • antonym: critical - expressing adverse or analytical judgments
  • synonym: idolizing - treating someone as a perfect idol
