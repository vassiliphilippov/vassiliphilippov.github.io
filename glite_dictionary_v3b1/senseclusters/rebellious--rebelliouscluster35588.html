rebellious

adjective
resisting authority or government
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbɛliəs / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛliəs / (BrE)
involved in or supporting active resistance or opposition, especially against authority or a constituted government

Examples

Rebellious army
During the civil war, the rebellious provinces declared independence from the central government.
Many songs from that era reflect the spirit of a rebellious youth unwilling to accept authority.
The army was sent to suppress the rebellious uprising in the capital region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defiant - showing resistance or disobedience
  • antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests
