baryonic

adjective
relating to baryons
9.9
Pronunciation: /bæ.rɪ.ˈɑːn.ɪk/ /; / /bə.rɪ.ˈɑːn.ɪk/ (AmE), /bæ.rɪ.ˈɒn.ɪk/ /; / /bə.rɪ.ˈɒn.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to baryons (subatomic particles made of three quarks), or to ordinary matter made of protons and neutrons (as opposed to dark matter)

Examples

Baryonic matter
Scientists study baryonic matter to understand the composition of ordinary atoms in the universe.
The experiment measured the abundance of baryonic particles after the high-energy collision.
Most visible matter in the universe is baryonic, unlike dark matter which is not.
Scientists study baryonic matter to understand the composition of stars and planets.
Most of the visible universe is made up of baryonic particles like protons and neutrons.
Unlike dark matter, baryonic matter interacts with light and can be observed directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baryon - a subatomic particle made of three quarks, such as a proton or neutron
  • antonym: dark - relating to matter that does not emit or interact with electromagnetic radiation
  • antonym: leptonic - relating to leptons, a different class of subatomic particles
