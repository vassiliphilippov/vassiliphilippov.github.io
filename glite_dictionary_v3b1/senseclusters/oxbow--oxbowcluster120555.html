oxbow

noun
U-shaped bend in a river
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒks.bəʊ /; / ˈɒksˌbəʊ / (BrE)
a U-shaped bend in a river or the land inside such a bend

Examples

River oxbow
Flooding can sometimes create a new channel, leaving the old oxbow cut off as a lake.

The river formed a broad oxbow after centuries of slow erosion.
Hiking trails often loop around the oxbow created by the slow-moving river.
The newly formed oxbow provided rich soil for farmers to cultivate crops.
Wildlife flourishes in the oxbow, where water collects after each flood.
