on paper

adverb
in theory, not necessarily in reality
Pronunciation: / ɑn ˈpeɪ.pɚ /; / ɑn ˈpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ɒn ˈpeɪ.pə /; / ɒn ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
according to what is written, planned, or in theory, but not necessarily in practice or reality

Examples

On paper qualifications
On paper, Sarah has all the qualifications needed for the job.
On paper, their team should easily win the championship.
The project looked perfect on paper, but there were many difficulties during implementation.
He qualified on paper, but lacked the necessary experience for the job.
On paper, the company looks very successful, but it has many hidden problems.
The plan seems perfect on paper, but it may not work in real life.
On paper, the new policy should increase efficiency, but real-world results may differ.
The plan looked impressive on paper, yet it failed to deliver the expected benefits.
They seem like the ideal team on paper, but their personalities clash in the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in practice - in actual operation or reality
  • synonym: in theory - as opposed to in practice; theoretically
