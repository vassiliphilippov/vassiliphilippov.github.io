instigation

noun
deliberate initiation or provocation
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstɪˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.stɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnstɪˈɡeɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.stɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of provoking, urging, or starting something, especially trouble or controversial action

Examples

The instigation of trouble
She was accused of the instigation of rumors that led to unrest in the office.
The police investigated the instigation of the protest by outside agitators.
Without his instigation, the disagreement might have died down on its own.
His speech was seen as an instigation to protest against the new law.
She was arrested for her instigation of the workers’ strike.
Without his instigation, the plan might never have been carried out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissuasion - the act of persuading not to do something
  • synonym: incitement - the action of provoking unlawful behavior or urging someone to behave unlawfully
