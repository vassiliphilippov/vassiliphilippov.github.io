gluconeogenesis

noun
making glucose from non-carbs
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…°luÀêko äÀåniÀêo äÀàd í…õn…ôs…™s / (AmE), / Àå…°luÀêk…ô äÀåniÀê…ô äÀàd í…õn…ôs…™s / (BrE)
the metabolic process by which the body produces glucose from non-carbohydrate sources such as amino acids or fats

Examples

Gluconeogenesis process
During prolonged fasting, gluconeogenesis becomes essential for maintaining normal blood sugar levels.
The liver is the main organ responsible for gluconeogenesis in humans.
Certain hormones, like cortisol, can stimulate gluconeogenesis when the body needs more glucose.
