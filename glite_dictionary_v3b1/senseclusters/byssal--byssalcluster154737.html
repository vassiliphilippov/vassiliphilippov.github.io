byssal

adjective
relating to attachment threads
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbɪsəl / (BrE)
relating to the threads produced by certain aquatic animals, such as mussels, to attach themselves to surfaces

Examples

Byssal threads
The byssal threads of mussels are incredibly strong and help them survive in rough waters.
Researchers studied the byssal attachment of marine bivalves to understand their resilience.
Some aquatic snails also produce byssal fibers to anchor themselves to rocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: byssus - the bundle of threads secreted by some mollusks for attachment
