laminate

verb
split wood into thin layers
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlæm.ə.neɪt /; / ˈlæm.əˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈlæm.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to make thin sheets or layers from a material, such as wood or metal

Examples

To laminate thin wood
She learned how to laminate maple wood during her woodworking course.
The carpenter will laminate the oak to create veneer panels for the cabinets.
To produce plywood, manufacturers often laminate large logs into thin, flexible sheets.
Some traditional jewelry techniques involve laminating gold to achieve a unique texture.
The craftsman expertly laminated the copper into thin, flexible sheets.
To produce foil, manufacturers laminate aluminum by hammering or pressing the metal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: veneer - a thin layer of wood glued onto a surface
