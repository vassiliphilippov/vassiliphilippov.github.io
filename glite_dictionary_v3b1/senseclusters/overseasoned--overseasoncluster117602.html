overseasoned

adjective
too much seasoning
4.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈsiː.zənd/ (AmE), /əʊ.vər.ˈsiː.zənd/ (BrE)
containing too much seasoning, such as salt, herbs, or spices, making the flavor too strong

Examples

Overseasoned steak
The chef apologized because the steak was overseasoned and difficult to enjoy.
I found the pasta overseasoned, with too much garlic and pepper overpowering the dish.
If a soup is overseasoned, adding more vegetables can help balance the taste.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bland - lacking strong flavor or seasoning
  • antonym: underseasoned - not having enough seasoning or flavor
