cachet

noun
official seal on a letter or document
Pronunciation: / kæˈʃeɪ / (AmE), / ˈkæʃeɪ / (BrE)
a seal or impression placed on a letter or document as a mark of authentication or distinction

Examples

Cachet seal on letter
Collectors value old envelopes that still bear the original cachet.
The document was given a special cachet by the official seal.
The royal cachet on the envelope indicated its importance.
