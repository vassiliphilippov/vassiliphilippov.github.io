pretexting

noun
deceptive information gathering
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛkstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtɛkstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of pretending to be someone else or inventing a false reason in order to get information or hide your true purpose

Examples

Pretexting in fraud
The company warned employees about the dangers of pretexting in social engineering attacks.
Through pretexting, the scammer convinced the bank to reveal sensitive account details.
Training sessions taught staff how to recognize and prevent pretexting attempts.
The hacker used pretexting to obtain sensitive information from the company.
She was caught pretexting when her real reason for calling was discovered.
Many scams rely on pretexting to trick people into revealing personal details.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deception - the act of making someone believe something that is not true
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and not deceiving
  • synonym: impersonation - pretending to be another person to deceive
  • synonym: phishing - fraudulently obtaining information by pretending to be a trustworthy entity
