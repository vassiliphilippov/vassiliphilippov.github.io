differential limen

noun
smallest detectable difference in stimulus intensity
9.8 8.7
Pronunciation: /dɪf.ə.ˈren.ʃəl ˈlɪ.mən/ (AmE), /dɪf.ə.ˈren.ʃəl ˈlɪ.mən/ (BrE)
the smallest difference in the intensity of a stimulus that a person can detect; also known as just noticeable difference.

Examples

The differential limen threshold
A person's differential limen may vary depending on the sense involved, such as hearing or touch.
The psychologist measured the differential limen for sound intensity in her experiment.
Training can sometimes lower an individual's differential limen, making small differences more perceptible.
