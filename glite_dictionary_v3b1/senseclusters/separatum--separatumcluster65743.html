separatum

noun
separate article copy
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛpəˈreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˌsɛpəˈreɪtəm / (BrE)
a separately printed copy of an article or paper, or any part that has been separated from a larger whole (especially in academic or publishing contexts)

Examples

A separatum copy
The professor handed each student a separatum of his latest published paper.
After her article was accepted, she requested a separatum to share with colleagues.
Libraries often archive a separatum for easier reference to important research.
The scholar kept the separatum in a special folder apart from the main volume.
A rare separatum of the article was found in the university archives.
Collectors often value a separatum more highly than the original publication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appendix - a separate section at the end of a book or document
  • synonym: extract - a part taken from a larger work or whole
  • synonym: offprint - a separately printed excerpt from a larger publication
