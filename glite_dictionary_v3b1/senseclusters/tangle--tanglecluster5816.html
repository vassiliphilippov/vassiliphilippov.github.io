tangle

verb
make complicated or confused
2.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtæŋ.gəl/ (AmE), /ˈtæŋ.gəl/ (BrE)
to make something complicated or confused; to entangle or mix up so that it is difficult to separate or understand

Examples

To tangle ideas
A sudden influx of information can tangle the threads of any simple explanation.
She tried to clarify her point, but only managed to further tangle the discussion.
The plot tangled so much that I couldn't follow who was helping whom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entangle - to twist or become twisted together in a confusing mass
  • antonym: untangle - to remove knots or confusion from; to make clear or straight
