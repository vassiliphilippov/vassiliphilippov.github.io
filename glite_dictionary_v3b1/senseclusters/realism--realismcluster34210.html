realism

noun
belief that objects exist independently of perception
4.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈriː.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that physical objects continue to exist independently of being perceived or thought about.

Examples

Philosophical realism
Many scientists adopt realism as the foundation for their understanding of the physical world.
Philosophers who support realism argue that objects exist even when no one is observing them.
The debate between realism and idealism has shaped much of modern philosophy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealism - the philosophical theory that reality is fundamentally mental or immaterial
