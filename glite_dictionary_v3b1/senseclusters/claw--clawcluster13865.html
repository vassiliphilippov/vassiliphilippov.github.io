claw

verb
move with effort as by dragging or grasping
7.1 4.1
Pronunciation: /klɔː/ /; / /klɑː/ (AmE), /klɔː/ (BrE)
to move, pull, or grasp at something with effort or in a desperate way, as if using claws

Examples

To claw through crowds
She clawed through the crowd to reach the exit during the emergency.
The company clawed back to profitability after years of financial struggle.
The hiker clawed his way up the rocky cliff despite the wind.
He clawed for the edge of the pool as soon as he lost his footing.
She clawed at the doorknob, desperately trying to escape the locked room.
The child clawed at his mother's sleeve when he heard the thunder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clutch - to grasp or hold tightly or firmly
  • synonym: grab - to seize suddenly or roughly
  • synonym: scramble - to move or climb quickly and with difficulty, often using the hands
