isotropy

noun
property of uniformity in all directions
8.1 8.2
Pronunciation: /aɪ.ˈsɑː.trə.piː/ (AmE), /aɪ.ˈsɒ.trə.piː/ (BrE)
(physics) the property of having identical values of a property in all directions; uniformity in all orientations.

Examples

Material isotropy property
Engineers test for isotropy to ensure that a substance behaves consistently regardless of orientation.
In materials science, glass exhibits isotropy because its properties are the same in every direction.
The isotropy of the cosmic microwave background is an important observation in cosmology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anisotropy - the property of being directionally dependent, as opposed to isotropy
