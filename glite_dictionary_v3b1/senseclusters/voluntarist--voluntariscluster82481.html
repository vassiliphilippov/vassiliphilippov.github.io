voluntarist

adjective
focused on willpower
9.7 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ëÀêl…ônÀåt…õr…™st / (AmE), / Ààv…íl…ônÀåt…õr…™st / (BrE)
emphasizing the will, especially human or divine will, as the main factor in action, knowledge, or morality

Examples

Voluntarist theory
The philosopher presented a voluntarist perspective, arguing that free will shapes our moral choices.
Her voluntarist approach to education encourages students to act according to their own decisions.
Some religious traditions have a voluntarist view, stressing the importance of divine will in human affairs.

Synonyms

  • voluntaristic
    9.1 0.02/m
    voluntaristic describes a view that human choice drives events, while voluntarist stresses the will (human or divine) as the main cause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinist - believing that all events are determined by causes external to the will
