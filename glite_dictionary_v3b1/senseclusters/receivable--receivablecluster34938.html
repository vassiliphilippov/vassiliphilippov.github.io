Examples

Synonyms

  • outstanding
    3.9 4.10/m
    outstanding describes money that is still unpaid and owed, while receivable highlights an amount expected to be received by someone.
  • owing
    2.4 0.00/m
    owing marks money that is owed and not yet paid, while receivable focuses on sums expected to be received by a person or business.
  • unpaid
    1.8 3.57/m
    unpaid means not yet paid for an invoice or bill, while receivable refers to money due to be received by the recipient.
  • due
    1.3 26/m
    due means owed and often payable now or appropriate in context, while receivable stresses money expected to be received.
