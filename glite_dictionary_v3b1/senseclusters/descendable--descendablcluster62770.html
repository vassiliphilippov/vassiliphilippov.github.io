descendable

adjective
able to be inherited
7.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsend.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsend.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
capable of being inherited or legally passed down to descendants, especially property, titles, or rights

Examples

Descendable property
The family estate is descendable and will go to the next of kin.
Only descendable titles can be passed to future generations under the law.
The lawyer explained which assets were descendable and which were not.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heritable - able to be inherited from one generation to another
  • antonym: nontransferable - not able to be passed to another person
