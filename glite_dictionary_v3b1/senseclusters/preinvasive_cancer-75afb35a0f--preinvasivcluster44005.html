preinvasive cancer

noun
early-stage malignant tumor not yet invasive
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.ɪnˈveɪ.sɪv ˈkæn.sɚ / (AmE), / ˌpriː.ɪnˈveɪ.sɪv ˈkæn.sə / (BrE)
A cluster of malignant cells that has not yet invaded deeper tissues or spread to other parts of the body.

Examples

A preinvasive cancer cluster
A biopsy revealed the presence of preinvasive cancer cells in the tissue sample.
Doctors often monitor patients with preinvasive cancer closely to prevent it from becoming invasive.
Early detection of preinvasive cancer greatly increases the chances of successful treatment.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: invasive cancer - cancer that has spread into surrounding tissues
