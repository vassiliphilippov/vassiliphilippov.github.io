overexpose

verb
expose too much to something
4.7 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to expose someone or something to too much of something (such as light, attention, or a harmful influence), causing damage or negative effects

Examples

To overexpose a child
Be careful not to overexpose the film to sunlight or your photos will be ruined.
Some psychologists believe that children can become numb to violence if they are overexposed to it at a young age.
The celebrity worried that being in too many commercials would overexpose her and make the public lose interest.
If you overexpose your skin to the sun, you risk getting a serious sunburn.
The celebrity was overexposed in the media, leading to public fatigue.
Photographers must be careful not to overexpose their images when shooting in bright light.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: expose - to subject to light, attention, or influence
  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or exposure
  • antonym: underexpose - expose too little (especially of photographic film)
