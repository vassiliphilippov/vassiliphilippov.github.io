phlebitis

noun
inflammation of a vein (medical)
4.2 7.7
Pronunciation: / fləˈbaɪt̬ɪs / (AmE), / flɪˈbaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of a vein, typically occurring in the legs and often causing pain and swelling

Examples

Phlebitis in legs
Doctors often recommend elevating the affected limb to reduce swelling caused by phlebitis.
The patient was diagnosed with phlebitis in her lower leg after experiencing pain and redness.
Untreated phlebitis can sometimes lead to more serious complications, such as blood clots.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: thrombophlebitis - inflammation of a vein with clot formation
  • synonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart
