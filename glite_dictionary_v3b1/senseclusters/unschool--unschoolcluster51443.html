unschool

noun
self-directed education
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈskuːl / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈskuːl / (BrE)
an educational approach that focuses on self-directed, experience-based learning instead of formal schooling or set curricula

Examples

Unschool philosophy
Many families choose unschool to allow their children to learn at their own pace.
The philosophy of unschool encourages curiosity and real-world experiences over textbooks.
Some parents believe that unschool better prepares children for independent thinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeschooling - education of children at home by parents or tutors
  • antonym: traditional schooling - education based on formal classes and set curricula
