nonfundamental

adjective
not the main frequency
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnfʌndəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnfʌndəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
relating to a frequency, vibration, or component that is not the fundamental or lowest frequency, such as a harmonic or overtone.

Examples

Nonfundamental frequency
The scientist measured the nonfundamental components in the sound wave to analyze its complexity.
Musicians often use nonfundamental tones to add richness to their performances.
The presence of strong nonfundamental frequencies gave the instrument a unique timbre.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fundamental - the lowest or primary frequency of a vibration
  • synonym: harmonic - a frequency that is a whole-number multiple of a fundamental frequency
