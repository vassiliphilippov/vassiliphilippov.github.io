Intourist

proper noun
Russian travel company
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtʊrɪst / (AmE), / ɪnˈtʊərɪst / (BrE)
the official Soviet (and later Russian) travel company and agency that arranged travel and accommodation for foreign visitors

Examples

Intourist travel agency
During the Soviet era, Intourist was the main agency arranging travel for foreign visitors to Russia.
After privatization, Intourist expanded its services to include hotel management and international tours.

During the Soviet era, all foreign tourists had to use Intourist to arrange their visits.
Our hotel reservation in Moscow was handled by Intourist as required by the authorities.
Many travelers remember the strict itineraries organized by Intourist in the 1980s.
