malleable

adjective
easily shaped or influenced
Pronunciation: / ˈmæliəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmalɪəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be easily shaped, bent, or hammered without breaking; easily influenced or changed.

Examples

Malleable metal
Gold is a highly malleable metal, allowing it to be shaped into thin sheets.
Her opinions are still malleable and easily influenced by those around her.
The clay was so malleable that the children could sculpt it into any shape they wanted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ductile - easily drawn out into a thin wire; pliable
  • synonym: pliable - easily bent, flexible
  • antonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; not flexible
