proper fraction

noun
fraction with numerator smaller than denominator
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːpər ˈfrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈprɒpə ˈfrækʃən / (BrE)
a fraction in which the numerator is smaller than the denominator

Examples

A proper fraction
A proper fraction always has a value less than one.
In her math homework, she had to convert each proper fraction into a decimal.
Three-fourths is an example of a proper fraction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: improper fraction - a fraction in which the numerator is equal to or greater than the denominator
