biomaterial

noun
material for tissue repair
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊməˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊməˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a natural or synthetic material used in medicine to replace, repair, or support tissues or organs in the body

Examples

Medical biomaterial
The surgeon implanted a biomaterial to help repair the damaged bone.
Researchers are developing new biomaterials that can support the growth of human tissue.
A biomaterial must be compatible with the body to avoid rejection or infection.
Researchers are developing new biomaterials that can help heal bone fractures more quickly.
The surgeon used a biomaterial to repair the patient's damaged heart valve.
Advances in biomaterial technology have improved the success of joint replacements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implant - a device or material placed inside the body for medical purposes
  • synonym: prosthesis - an artificial device that replaces a missing body part
