tokenism

noun
symbolic effort
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtoʊ.kə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈtəʊ.kə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice of making only a symbolic or superficial effort to include people or address a problem, without real commitment or meaningful change

Examples

Act of tokenism
The company hired one woman to the board as an act of tokenism rather than true diversity.
Many activists criticized the government's response as mere tokenism with no real impact.
Adding a single accessible ramp was seen as tokenism instead of a genuine effort to improve accessibility.
Many organizations are criticized for practicing tokenism instead of making real efforts toward diversity.
Hiring one person from a minority group is often seen as tokenism if no real changes follow.
The committee's attempt at tokenism did not address the deeper issues of inequality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inclusivity - the practice of including people from all backgrounds
  • antonym: substantive change - real and meaningful change or improvement
  • synonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities
  • synonym: window dressing - actions intended to make something appear better than it is
