birth mother

noun
biological mother
3.6
Pronunciation: / bɝːθ ˈmʌðɚ / (AmE), / bɜːθ ˈmʌðə / (BrE)
the woman who gives birth to a child, especially when she is not the legal or social mother (such as in adoption)

Examples

birth mother of a child
After years of searching, he finally met his birth mother for the first time.
The birth mother and adoptive parents agreed to stay in contact after the adoption.
Some children are curious about their birth mother and want to learn more about their origins.
After years of searching, he finally met his birth mother in person.
The agency helped the birth mother and adoptive parents communicate openly.
Some children are curious about their birth mother as they grow older.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adoptive mother - a woman who legally becomes the mother of a child she did not give birth to
  • synonym: biological mother - the woman whose egg and body produced and delivered a child
  • synonym: surrogate mother - a woman who carries and gives birth to a child for another person or couple
