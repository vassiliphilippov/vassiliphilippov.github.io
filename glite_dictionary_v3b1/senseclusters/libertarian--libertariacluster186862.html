libertarian

noun
believer in free will (philosophy)
8.0 6.6
Pronunciation: /lɪ.bər.ˈter.iː.ən/ (AmE), /lɪ.bə.ˈteə.riː.ən/ (BrE)
A person who believes in the doctrine of free will, especially in the philosophical sense that human actions are not determined by external causes.

Examples

Libertarian philosophy debate
As a libertarian, she rejects the notion that fate controls people's actions.
Many debates in metaphysics contrast the libertarian view with determinism.
The philosopher argued from a libertarian perspective that humans are morally responsible for their choices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinist - someone who believes all events, including moral choices, are determined by existing causes
