necrophile

noun
organism feeding on dead matter
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛkrəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈnɛkrəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
an organism, such as a plant, fungus, or animal, that lives on or feeds from dead organic matter

Examples

Necrophile beetle
The carrion beetle is a well-known necrophile in many forest ecosystems.
Certain fungi act as necrophiles, breaking down dead wood and leaves on the forest floor.
Vultures are classic examples of necrophiles in the animal kingdom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decomposer - an organism that breaks down dead organic material
  • synonym: scavenger - an animal that feeds on dead or decaying matter
