man

noun
a human being; member of Hominidae
2.9
Pronunciation: / mæn / (AmE), / man /; / mæn / (BrE)
a human being in general; any member of the human species (sometimes used in scientific or philosophical contexts)

Examples

Early man
Modern man is believed to have originated in Africa.
The discoveries shed light on how early man developed tools and language.
Unlike other primates, man walks upright and has complex language abilities.
In early philosophies, man was believed to be the measure of all things.
The phrase 'no man is an island' is meant to include all people.
The rights of man have been discussed for centuries by thinkers and lawmakers.
