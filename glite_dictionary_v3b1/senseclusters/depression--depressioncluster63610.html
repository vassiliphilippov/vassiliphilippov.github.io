depression

noun
a sunken geological formation
8.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
a sunken area of land, especially a large hollow or dip in the Earth's surface

Examples

A land depression
A shallow depression in the desert collects rainwater during the wet season.
Meteorologists studied the low-pressure depression where the storm developed.
The river cuts through a broad depression in the landscape, forming a natural basin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basin - a broad, bowl-shaped depression in the earth
  • antonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land
