broken

adjective
physically separated into pieces or cracked
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊkən /; / ˈbroʊ.kən / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkən /; / ˈbrəʊ.kən / (BrE)
physically damaged, shattered, or not whole; not working or incomplete

Examples

Broken phone screen
He wore a cast on his arm due to his broken bone.
She found a broken glass on the kitchen floor after the party.
The broken window let in a cold draft all night.
After the storm, several streetlights were found to be broken.
If your phone is broken, you should contact technical support for help.
The office printer is broken and won't print any documents today.
A broken chess set is no fun to play with, since half the pieces are gone.
She donated her broken collection of books, even though some volumes were absent.
We couldn't finish the puzzle because it was a broken set missing several pieces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: busted - informal term for broken, especially out of order or not functioning
  • antonym: complete - having all parts; whole and not lacking anything
  • antonym: fixed - repaired; restored to working order
  • antonym: intact - not damaged or broken; whole
