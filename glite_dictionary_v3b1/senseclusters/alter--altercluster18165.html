alter

verb
to change partly but not entirely
3.9 2.1
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːl.tər/ /; / /ˈɑːl.tər/ (AmE), /ˈɔːl.tə/ /; / /ˈɒl.tə/ (BrE)
to change or modify something, or to become different, usually in a small or significant way but not completely transformed

Examples

To alter a dress
Her mood tends to alter depending on the weather.
His voice alters slightly when he's nervous.
The company's strategy will alter in response to market trends.
Advances in technology can quickly alter the way we communicate.
Her voice was so disguised that it altered her entire appearance.
The architect suggested we alter the design to make better use of space.
She decided to alter her dress to make it fit better.
The building was altered to accommodate more office space.
You can alter the settings to suit your preferences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: change - to make or become different; a more general or complete transformation
  • synonym: modify - to make partial changes to something
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something the same; to maintain in its original state
  • degree higher: transform - to make a thorough or dramatic change
