Battle of Inchon

proper noun
Korean War battle (1950) at Inchon
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ɪnˈtʃʌn / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ɪnˈtʃɒn / (BrE)
a major battle of the Korean War (1950) in which United States forces made an amphibious landing at Inchon, leading to a decisive victory over North Korean forces

Examples

The Battle of Inchon victory
General MacArthur planned the Battle of Inchon as a daring amphibious assault against the North Korean army.
Many historians consider the Battle of Inchon one of the most brilliant military operations of the twentieth century.
The Battle of Inchon marked a turning point in the Korean War, allowing UN forces to recapture Seoul.
