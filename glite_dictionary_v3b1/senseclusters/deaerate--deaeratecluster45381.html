deaerate

verb
remove air or gas from something
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˈɛrˌeɪt / (AmE), / diˈɛəreɪt / (BrE)
to remove air or gas from a substance, especially a liquid

Examples

To deaerate a liquid
Before bottling, manufacturers deaerate the beverage to prevent spoilage.
Engineers must deaerate the water in the pipeline to stop corrosion.
The process will deaerate the liquid, ensuring better taste and preservation.
