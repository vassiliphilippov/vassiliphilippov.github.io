chain fern

noun
fern with chainlike rows of sori
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪn fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn fəːn / (BrE)
A fern of the genus Woodwardia, characterized by chainlike rows of spore cases (sori) on the undersides of its fronds.

Examples

A chain fern plant
Gardeners often plant chain ferns for their attractive foliage and unique texture.
The chain fern thrives in moist woodland environments throughout North America.
The distinct pattern of sori makes the chain fern easy to identify.
