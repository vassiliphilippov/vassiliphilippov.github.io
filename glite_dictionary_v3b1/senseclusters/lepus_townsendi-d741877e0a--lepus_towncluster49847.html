Lepus townsendi

noun
species of North American hare (white-tailed jackrabbit)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈliːpəs ˌtaʊnˈsɛndi / (AmE), / ˈliːpəs ˌtaʊnˈzɛndi / (BrE)
A large species of hare found in the northern plains and western mountains of the United States, known as the white-tailed jackrabbit, with brownish-grey fur in summer and pale grey in winter, and an almost entirely white tail.

Examples

The Lepus townsendi hare
During their winter studies, biologists tracked several Lepus townsendi across the snowy plains.
The Lepus townsendi is commonly known as the white-tailed jackrabbit.
Unlike most hares, the Lepus townsendi has a tail that remains white all year.
