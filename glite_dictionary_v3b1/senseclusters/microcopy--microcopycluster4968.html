microcopy

verb
make reduced-size photographic copy
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààma…™.kro äÀåk…ëÀê.pi / (AmE), / Ààma…™.kr…ô äÀåk…í.pi / (BrE)
to make a photographic copy of printed or graphic material that is reduced in size for storage or reference.

Examples

To microcopy old newspapers
Before digital storage, newspapers were routinely microcopied and stored on microfilm.
Libraries often microcopy rare manuscripts to preserve the content and save space.
The office microcopied all contracts from the last decade for archival purposes.

Synonyms
