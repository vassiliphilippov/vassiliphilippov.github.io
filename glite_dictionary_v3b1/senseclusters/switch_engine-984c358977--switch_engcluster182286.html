switch engine

noun
locomotive for shunting cars in rail yards
7.6
Pronunciation: / swɪtʃ ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / swɪtʃ ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
a locomotive used for moving train cars within a railroad yard, rather than on main lines

Examples

Hiring an experienced crew is essential when operating a switch engine in a busy railroad yard.
The switch engine pushed the freight cars into the proper siding for unloading.
