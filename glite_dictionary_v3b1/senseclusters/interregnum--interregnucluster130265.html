interregnum

noun
period between reigns or governments
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈrɛɡnəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrɛɡnəm / (BrE)
A period between the end of one reign, government, or administration and the beginning of another.

Examples

The interregnum period
During the interregnum, the country was governed by a provisional council.
Scholars studied the effects of the interregnum on political stability.
The brief interregnum led to uncertainty and conflicting claims to the throne.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dynasty - a sequence of rulers from the same family
  • degree lower: reign - the period during which a sovereign rules
