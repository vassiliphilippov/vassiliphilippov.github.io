overspeak

verb
to interrupt by talking
7.2
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈspiːk/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈspiːk/ (BrE)
to speak at the same time as someone else, especially to interrupt or drown them out

Examples

To overspeak a friend
During the heated discussion, he would often overspeak his colleagues, making it hard for others to contribute.
It is considered rude to overspeak someone when they are trying to explain their point.
She realized she had overspoken her friend and quickly apologized for interrupting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking by speaking yourself
  • antonym: listen - to pay attention to what someone else is saying
