move the chains

verb
make steady progress; originally a football term
5.1
Pronunciation: / muːv ðə tʃeɪnz / (AmE), / muːv ðə tʃeɪnz / (BrE)
to make progress towards a goal, especially by achieving necessary steps or advancing in stages; originally used in American football to refer to gaining a first down

Examples

To move the chains forward
If we want to finish this project on time, we really need to move the chains.
The offense struggled to move the chains in the first half of the game.
With every small step, their team continued to move the chains toward success.
