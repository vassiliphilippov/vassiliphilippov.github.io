scramble

verb
make a message or signal unintelligible
Pronunciation: / ˈskræm.bəl / (AmE), / ˈskræm.bəl / (BrE)
to make (a message or signal) unintelligible, especially to prevent it from being understood without special equipment or knowledge

Examples

To scramble a message
The radio operator had to scramble the transmission to secure its contents.
The software can scramble your emails so only the recipient can read them.
They used a device to scramble the television signal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decode - to convert (a coded message) into intelligible language
  • synonym: encrypt - to convert information or data into a code to prevent unauthorized access
