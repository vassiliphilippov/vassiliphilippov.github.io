histoplasmosis

noun
fungal infection
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪstoʊplæzˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɪstəʊplæzˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease caused by infection with the fungus Histoplasma capsulatum, usually affecting the lungs and sometimes spreading to other organs

Examples

Histoplasmosis lung infection
The doctor diagnosed the patient with histoplasmosis after reviewing the results of the lung biopsy.
People with weakened immune systems are at higher risk of developing severe histoplasmosis.
Farmers working in areas with bird droppings may be exposed to histoplasmosis.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with histoplasmosis after reviewing the chest X-ray results.
People who work in caves or clean bird roosts are at higher risk for histoplasmosis.
Early treatment of histoplasmosis can prevent serious lung complications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pneumonia - an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs
  • synonym: tuberculosis - a serious infectious disease that mainly affects the lungs
