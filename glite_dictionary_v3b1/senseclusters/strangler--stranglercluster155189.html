strangler

noun
plant that kills its host tree by enveloping it
6.2 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstræŋɡlər / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡlə / (BrE)
A tropical vine or tree that grows around and eventually kills its host tree by enclosing its trunk, typically by sending down aerial roots; especially a strangler fig.

Examples

Strangler fig tree
Over time, the strangler can completely envelop and kill the tree it grows on.
Strangler figs are among the most well-known types of strangler plants in tropical forests.
The strangler slowly wound its roots around the host tree, cutting off its access to nutrients.
