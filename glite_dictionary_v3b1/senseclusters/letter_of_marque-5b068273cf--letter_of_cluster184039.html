letter of marque

noun
government license to seize enemy ships
9.3 9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtər əv mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə əv mɑːk / (BrE)
an official license granted by a government to a private person, authorizing them to capture enemy ships and cargo during wartime

Examples

A letter of marque
During the war, the navy issued a letter of marque to several merchant captains.
The ship sailed under a letter of marque, making it a legal privateer.
Without a letter of marque, attacking enemy vessels would be considered piracy.
