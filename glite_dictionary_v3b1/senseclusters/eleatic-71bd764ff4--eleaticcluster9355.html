Eleatic

noun
Greek philosophical school
Pronunciation: / ˌɛliˈætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛliˈætɪk / (BrE)
a person from Elea or a member of the ancient Greek Eleatic school of philosophers, known for the belief that reality is unchanging

Examples

An Eleatic thinker
The Eleatic argued that all change is an illusion and reality is one unchanging whole.
As an Eleatic, Zeno developed famous paradoxes to support his philosophical views.
Many students find the ideas of the Eleatic school challenging but fascinating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Heraclitean - relating to Heraclitus, who believed in constant change
  • synonym: Parmenidean - relating to Parmenides, a key Eleatic philosopher
