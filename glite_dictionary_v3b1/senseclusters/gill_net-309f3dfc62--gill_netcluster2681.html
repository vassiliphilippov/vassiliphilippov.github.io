gill net

noun
type of fishing net entangling fish by their gills
Pronunciation: / ɡɪl nɛt / (AmE), / ɡɪl nɛt / (BrE)
a flat net suspended vertically in water designed to catch fish by entangling their gills

Examples

A gill net in water
He repaired the damaged gill net before the next fishing trip.
Many regulations control the mesh size of a gill net to protect smaller fish.
The fishermen set a gill net across the river to catch salmon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trawl net - a large net dragged along the bottom or through the midwater of the sea to catch fish
