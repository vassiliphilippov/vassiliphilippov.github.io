kneecap

verb
to shoot or injure in the knee as punishment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈniˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈniːˌkæp / (BrE)
to shoot or injure someone in the knee, especially as a form of punishment or warning, often associated with paramilitary or terrorist groups

Examples

To kneecap a rival
Several activists were kneecapped by paramilitary groups as a warning to others.
The leader ordered his men to kneecap the informant for betraying their secrets.
The rival gang threatened to kneecap anyone who spoke to the police.
