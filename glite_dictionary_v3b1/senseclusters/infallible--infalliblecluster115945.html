infallible

adjective
incapable of error or failure
9.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfæləbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfaləbl̩ / (BrE)
Never making mistakes or being wrong; incapable of failing or making errors.

Examples

An infallible system
Despite years of research, no system is truly infallible.
She believed her father's advice to be infallible and always followed it.
The team trusted their leader's infallible sense of judgment during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fallible - liable to make mistakes or be erroneous
