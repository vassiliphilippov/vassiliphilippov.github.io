mahagua

noun
tropical tree of Cuba and Jamaica; timber source
9.8
Pronunciation: / məˈhɑːɡwə / (AmE), / məˈhɑːɡwə / (BrE)
a tropical tree (especially in the Caribbean) valued for its wood, fiber, or as an ornamental plant

Examples

Mahagua tree
In some parts of the Caribbean, mahagua trees are valued for their beautiful blossoms.
The cabinetwork in the old house was made from locally sourced mahagua wood.
The craftsman preferred mahagua for its durability when making furniture.
Because of its attractive foliage, mahagua is often planted as an ornamental tree.
Ropes and caulking were traditionally made from the fiber of the mahagua tree.
The villagers used mahagua wood to build sturdy outriggers for their canoes.
