overinterpretation

noun
excessively detailed interpretation
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪnˌtɜrprəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of interpreting something in an excessively elaborate or detailed way, often leading to misrepresentation or distortion of its actual meaning.

Examples

Avoid overinterpretation errors
Diligent reviewers and careful editors can identify mistakes, provide balance, and restrain overinterpretation.
His tendency toward overinterpretation often led to far-fetched conclusions.
The committee warned against overinterpretation of the preliminary results before comprehensive data were available.
