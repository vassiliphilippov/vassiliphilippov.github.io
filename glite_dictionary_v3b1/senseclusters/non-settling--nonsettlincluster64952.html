non-settling

adjective
refusing settlement
6.7
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɛt̬lɪŋ / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɛtlɪŋ / (BrE)
refusing to agree to or accept a settlement in a dispute or negotiation

Examples

Non-settling party
The non-settling party chose to continue the lawsuit instead of accepting the offer.
Her non-settling attitude made negotiations much more difficult for everyone involved.
Because he was non-settling, the case went all the way to trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: settling - agreeing to a settlement in a dispute
  • synonym: uncompromising - not willing to change or make concessions
