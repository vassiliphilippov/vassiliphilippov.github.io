sorosis

noun
fused-flower fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / səˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a type of fruit formed by the fusion of multiple flowers into a single mass, as in a pineapple

Examples

Pineapple sorosis
A pineapple is a well-known example of a sorosis in the plant world.
Botanists classify fruits like the mulberry and pineapple as types of sorosis.
The unique structure of a sorosis results from many flowers merging together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multiple fruit - a fruit formed from the ovaries of several flowers
  • antonym: simple fruit - a fruit formed from a single ovary of one flower
