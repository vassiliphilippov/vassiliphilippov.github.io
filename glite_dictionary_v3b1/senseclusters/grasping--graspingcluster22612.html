grasping

adjective
greedy; acquisitive; eager for wealth
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræspɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːspɪŋ / (BrE)
Always eager to acquire more, especially money; greedy or acquisitive.

Examples

Grasping for money
Her grasping nature made her unpopular among her colleagues.
The grasping landlord raised the rent again despite the tenants' protests.
The novel depicts a grasping aristocrat determined to increase his fortune at any cost.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avaricious - having an extreme greed for wealth or material gain
  • antonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something, especially money, than is strictly necessary or expected
  • synonym: greedy - having or showing an intense and selfish desire for something, especially wealth or power
