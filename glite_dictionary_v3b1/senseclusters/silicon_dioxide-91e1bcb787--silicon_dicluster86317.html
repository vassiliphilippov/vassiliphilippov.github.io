silicon dioxide

noun
a hard compound, SiO2, found in quartz and sand
4.8 4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlɪkən daɪˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪkən dʌɪˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
a white or colorless hard compound (SiO2) found in the earth's crust, occurring naturally as quartz, sand, or other minerals

Examples

Silicon dioxide in sand
Most glass is made by melting silicon dioxide at extremely high temperatures.
Quartz crystals are a pure form of silicon dioxide commonly used in watches and electronics.
Sand is largely composed of tiny grains of silicon dioxide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quartz - a hard mineral form of silicon dioxide
  • synonym: silica - another name for silicon dioxide, especially in its natural form
