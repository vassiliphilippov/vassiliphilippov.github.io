borough

noun
English town represented in parliament
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbʌrə / (BrE)
A town in England that is represented by a member of parliament, or an administrative division with certain self-government rights.

Examples

English borough
Each borough elects its own member of parliament.
She grew up in a small borough just outside of London.
The historic borough still retains many medieval buildings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constituency - an area whose voters elect a representative to a legislative body
