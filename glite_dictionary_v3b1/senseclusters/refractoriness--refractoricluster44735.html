refractoriness

noun
quality of being unmanageable or resistant
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktərinəs / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktərɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being difficult to manage or control; unmanageable or resistant behavior.

Examples

The refractoriness of behavior
Despite repeated warnings, the team's refractoriness persisted throughout the season.
Management struggled to address the refractoriness of some employees who resisted new policies.
The refractoriness of the child made it hard for the teacher to maintain order in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliance - the act of conforming or yielding behavior
  • synonym: obstinacy - stubborn refusal to change one's opinion or behavior
