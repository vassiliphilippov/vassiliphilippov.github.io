prime factor

noun
a prime number that divides another number exactly
3.2
Pronunciation: / praɪm ˈfæktər / (AmE), / praɪm ˈfæktə / (BrE)
a number that is a prime and divides another number exactly, without leaving a remainder

Examples

A prime factor number
Every integer greater than one can be written as a product of its prime factors.
Finding the prime factors of a number is a basic skill in mathematics.
The number 12 has three prime factors: 2, 2, and 3.

Related Terms
