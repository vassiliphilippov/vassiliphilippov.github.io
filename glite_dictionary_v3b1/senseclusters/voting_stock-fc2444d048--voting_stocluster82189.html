voting stock

noun
corporate shares with voting rights
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊtɪŋ stɑk / (AmE), / ˈvəʊtɪŋ stɒk / (BrE)
Shares in a corporation that give the holder the right to vote on corporate matters, such as electing the board of directors.

Examples

Voting stock shares
He converted his preferred shares into voting stock to have a say in corporate governance.
Most founders retain a majority of the voting stock to maintain control over the company.
Shareholders who own voting stock can influence company decisions at the annual meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonvoting stock - shares in a corporation that do not entitle the holder to voting rights
