hereditable

adjective
able to be inherited
7.9
Pronunciation: / həˈrɛdɪtəbəl / (AmE), / həˈrɛdɪtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be inherited, especially referring to property, rights, or titles passed down by law or custom

Examples

Hereditable property rights
Under the old system, only hereditable land could be passed to the eldest son.
The title was not hereditable and ended with the current holder's death.
Certain rights were considered hereditable according to the region's traditional laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heritable - able to be inherited by heirs
  • antonym: nontransferable - not able to be passed on to others
