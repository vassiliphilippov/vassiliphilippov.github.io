abruptly pinnate

adjective
leaf with paired leaflets at apex
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈbrʌpt.liː ˈpɪn.ət/ /; / /ə.ˈbrʌp.liː ˈpɪn.ət/ (AmE), /ə.ˈbrʌpt.liː ˈpɪn.ət/ /; / /ə.ˈbrʌp.liː ˈpɪn.ət/ (BrE)
(of a leaf) having a pinnate arrangement in which the leaflets end with a pair at the apex, rather than a single terminal leaflet.

Examples

Abruptly pinnate leaf
Some ferns display an abruptly pinnate leaf structure with pairs at the tip instead of a single leaflet.
The botanist noted that the compound leaf was abruptly pinnate, distinguishing it from other similar specimens.
The tree is easily identified by its abruptly pinnate leaves, which are unique among local species.
