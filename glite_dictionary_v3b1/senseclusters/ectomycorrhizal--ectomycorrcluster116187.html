ectomycorrhizal

adjective
fungus-root symbiosis
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛktoʊˌmaɪkəˈraɪzəl / (AmE), / ˌɛktəʊˌmaɪkəˈraɪzəl / (BrE)
describing a fungus, plant, or root involved in a symbiotic relationship where the fungus surrounds the root with a sheath (but does not enter the root cells) to help with nutrient exchange

Examples

ectomycorrhizal fungi
Many forest trees depend on ectomycorrhizal fungi to help them absorb nutrients from the soil.
The ectomycorrhizal association benefits both the fungus and the plant by exchanging nutrients.
Researchers studied the ectomycorrhizal relationships in pine and oak forests.
Pine trees are often ectomycorrhizal, relying on fungi to help them absorb nutrients from the soil.
Researchers studied the ectomycorrhizal associations in oak forests to understand their ecological impact.
Some commercial nurseries ensure seedlings are ectomycorrhizal before planting them in the field.
Many trees in temperate forests form ectomycorrhizal relationships with fungi to improve nutrient uptake.
The ectomycorrhizal fungi create a sheath around the roots, aiding in water absorption.
Researchers observed that ectomycorrhizal associations can increase plant resistance to environmental stress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endomycorrhizal - describing a fungus that penetrates plant root cells in symbiosis
  • synonym: mycorrhizal - relating to the symbiotic association between a fungus and plant roots
