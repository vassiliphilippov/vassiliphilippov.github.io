marsupial mouse

noun
small mouse-like marsupial
Pronunciation: / mɑrˈsupiəl maʊs / (AmE), / mɑːˈsuːpɪəl maʊs / (BrE)
any of various small, sharp-nosed insectivorous marsupials of Australia and nearby regions that superficially resemble mice or rats

Examples

The marsupial mouse animal
Although it looks like a rodent, the marsupial mouse is actually a distant relative of kangaroos.
Researchers tracked the populations of several species of marsupial mouse in the national park.
The marsupial mouse is active mainly at night, hunting insects in the undergrowth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antechinus - a genus of marsupial mouse native to Australia
