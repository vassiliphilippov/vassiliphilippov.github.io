magic wand

noun
rod used in magic; simple solution (figurative)
3.5 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmæ.dʒɪk wɑːnd/ /; / /ˈmæ.dʒɪk wɔːnd/ (AmE), /ˈmæ.dʒɪk wɒnd/ (BrE)
An object, typically depicted as a thin rod, believed to have magical powers and used for casting spells or performing magical feats; also used figuratively to refer to a simple solution for complex problems.

Examples

Wishing for a magic wand won’t clean the house—you have to do it yourself.
