magic wand

noun
rod used in magic; simple solution (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ˈmædʒɪk wɑnd / (AmE), / ˈmædʒɪk wɒnd / (BrE)
An object, typically depicted as a thin rod, believed to have magical powers and used for casting spells or performing magical feats; also used figuratively to refer to a simple solution for complex problems.

Examples

A magic wand solution
Many politicians promise a magic wand solution to complex social issues, but reality is never that simple.
The new software update isn’t a magic wand that will fix all our problems overnight.
Wishing for a magic wand won’t clean the house—you have to do it yourself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quick fix - an easy, temporary solution to a problem
