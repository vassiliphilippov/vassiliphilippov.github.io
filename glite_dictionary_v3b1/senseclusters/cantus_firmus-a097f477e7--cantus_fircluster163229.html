cantus firmus

noun
pre-existing melody in polyphonic music
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.təs ˈfɪr.məs / (AmE), / ˈkan.tʊs ˈfɪə.məs / (BrE)
a pre-existing melody used as the structural basis for a polyphonic musical composition, often originally taken from plainchant

Examples

The cantus firmus melody
In the Renaissance, it was common to use a popular tune as the cantus firmus for masses and motets.
Students studied the use of cantus firmus to understand the development of polyphonic music.
The composer built the mass around a cantus firmus borrowed from a Gregorian chant.
