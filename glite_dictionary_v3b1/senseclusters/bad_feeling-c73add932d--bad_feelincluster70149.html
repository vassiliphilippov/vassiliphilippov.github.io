bad feeling

noun
sense of unease or foreboding
Pronunciation: / bæd ˈfiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / bad ˈfiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
A sense of unease, suspicion, or foreboding about something that may happen.

Examples

A bad feeling in air
Despite the good news, there was a bad feeling in the air.
I have a bad feeling about flying in this weather.
She couldn't shake the bad feeling she had before the big exam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreboding - a feeling that something bad will happen
  • synonym: misgiving - a feeling of doubt or apprehension
