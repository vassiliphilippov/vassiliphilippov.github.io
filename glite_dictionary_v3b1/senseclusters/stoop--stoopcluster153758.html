stoop

verb
to dive swiftly, as a bird of prey
5.3 8.9
Pronunciation: / stuːp / (AmE), / stuːp / (BrE)
(of a bird of prey) to descend swiftly in a dive, especially to attack prey.

Examples

A hawk stoops
As the hawk stooped, its prey scattered in all directions.
The falcon began to stoop as soon as it spotted the rabbit below.
The trained eagle stoops at the handler's command during the demonstration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dive - to plunge headfirst and rapidly downward
  • antonym: soar - to fly or rise high in the air
