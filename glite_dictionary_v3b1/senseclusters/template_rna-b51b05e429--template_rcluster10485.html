template RNA

noun
RNA carrying genetic instructions to ribosomes
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmˌplət ɑːr ɛn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɛm.plət ɑːr ɛn ˈeɪ / (BrE)
A form of RNA that carries genetic information from DNA in the nucleus to ribosomes, where protein synthesis occurs.

Examples

Template RNA carries information
Template RNA is essential for conveying genetic instructions required for building proteins in all living cells.
Defects in template RNA can lead to errors in the production of proteins.
Scientists studied the role of template RNA during the translation phase of protein synthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: messenger RNA - RNA molecule that conveys genetic information from DNA to the ribosome
