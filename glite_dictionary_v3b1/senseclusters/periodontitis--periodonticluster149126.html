periodontitis

noun
gum disease causing tissue and bone loss
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪriˌɑːdɒnˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪəʊdɒnˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A serious gum disease that damages the soft tissue and destroys the bone supporting the teeth, potentially leading to tooth loss.

Examples

Periodontitis gum disease
Smoking is a known risk factor for developing periodontitis.
The dentist explained that periodontitis is more serious than gingivitis and requires immediate attention.
Untreated periodontitis can eventually lead to tooth loss and other complications.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gingivitis - mild inflammation of the gums
