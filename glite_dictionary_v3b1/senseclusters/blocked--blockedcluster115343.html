blocked

adjective
obstructed; closed off
1.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / blÉ‘kt / (AmE), / blÉ’kt / (BrE)
completely obstructed or closed off so that movement or flow is prevented (such as a nose, road, or passage)

Examples

Blocked nose
Her nose was so blocked that she could hardly breathe through it.
Several blocked pipes caused flooding in the basement.
The highway was blocked by fallen trees after the storm.
Due to construction, the street remains blocked to all traffic until further notice.
Several blocked intersections caused long delays for commuters this morning.
The main road was blocked by a fallen tree after the storm.

Synonyms

  • obstructed
    4.6 0.00/m
    obstructed means made difficult by an object or barrier, while blocked means completely closed so nothing can pass.
  • plugged
    2.6 0.00/m
    plugged means filled or sealed so nothing can pass, while blocked refers to something completely closed off and impassable.
  • stopped up
    5.1 0.01/m
    stopped up describes a nose or pipe filled with mucus or debris so air cannot pass, while blocked is a general closure.
  • clogged
    2.8 0.3/m
    clogged means filled with build-up that slows or stops flow, while blocked names any complete closure preventing movement.
  • choked
    4.3 0.00/m
    choked means almost closed by pressure or sudden fill, often stopping flow quickly, while blocked is broader and slower.
  • occluded
    8.0 0.00/m
    occluded is a formal medical term for a closed passage, while blocked is common and used in everyday speech.
