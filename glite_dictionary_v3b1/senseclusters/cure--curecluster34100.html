cure

verb
become preserved by drying, salting, etc.
Pronunciation: / kjʊr / (AmE), / kjʊə / (BrE)
to preserve food by drying, salting, smoking, or using chemicals

Examples

To cure meat
In warmer climates, fish are often left out to cure naturally.
It takes several weeks for the ham to properly cure in the smokehouse.
The apricots cure in the sun for several days before packaging.
The farmers cure tobacco leaves before shipping them to factories.
We cure ham by hanging it in a cool, dry place for several months.
You must cure the fish with salt to keep it from spoiling.
