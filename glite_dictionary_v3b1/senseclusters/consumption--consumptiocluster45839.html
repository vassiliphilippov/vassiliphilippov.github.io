consumption

noun
the act of consuming or using up
Pronunciation: / kənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmpʃən /; / kənˈsʌmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act of using, eating, or drinking something, such as food, resources, or goods

Examples

Daily consumption of food
Limiting salt consumption is important for a healthy diet.
The country's energy consumption has increased by 10% over the last year.
The rapid consumption of natural resources has raised concerns among environmentalists.
Alcohol consumption should be limited to maintain good health.
Increasing fiber consumption can improve digestive health.
Overeating leads to excessive consumption of calories.
Many governments are concerned about the high consumption of fossil fuels.
Rising incomes often lead to increased consumption of luxury goods.
The consumption of electricity rises substantially during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expenditure - the act of spending or using up something
  • synonym: ingestion - the process of taking food, drink, or another substance into the body
  • antonym: production - the process of making or manufacturing something
  • synonym: use - the act of employing something for a purpose
