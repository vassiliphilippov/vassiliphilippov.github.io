hypochlorous acid

noun
weak, unstable acid and bleaching agent
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈklɔrəs ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈklɔːrəs ˈasid / (BrE)
a weak, unstable acid (HOCl) known only in solution or in the form of its salts; used mainly as a bleaching and oxidizing agent

Examples

Hypochlorous acid in bleach
Hypochlorous acid is responsible for the bleaching effect of chlorine in laundry detergents.
Exposure to sunlight can rapidly break down hypochlorous acid in solution.
The disinfectant properties of hypochlorous acid are used in water treatment plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chlorine - a greenish-yellow gas used as a disinfectant and bleaching agent
