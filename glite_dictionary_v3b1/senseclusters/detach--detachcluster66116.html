detach

verb
separate a part for a special duty
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtætʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈtatʃ / (BrE)
to separate a part or unit from something larger, especially in order to perform a special task or assignment

Examples

To detach a team
The commander decided to detach a small squad for reconnaissance duty.
They plan to detach a research team from the main group to study the phenomenon.
You can detach certain resources for special assignments as needed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attach - to join or fasten one thing to another
  • synonym: separate - to move or be apart; to set apart
