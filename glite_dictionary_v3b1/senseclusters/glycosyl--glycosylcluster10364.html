glycosyl

noun
sugar-derived group
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈglaɪ.kə.sɪl/ /; / /ˈglaɪ.koʊ.sɪl/ (AmE), /ˈglaɪ.kə.sɪl/ /; / /ˈglaɪ.kəʊ.sɪl/ (BrE)
a group derived from a sugar molecule by removing a hydroxyl group, often involved in forming glycosidic bonds

Examples

Glycosyl group
The enzyme transfers a glycosyl group to the protein during the reaction.
A glycosyl group can attach to various molecules, altering their properties.
Scientists study how glycosyl groups affect the function of biological macromolecules.
