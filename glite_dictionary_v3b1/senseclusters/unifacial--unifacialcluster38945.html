unifacial

adjective
having a single main surface
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈfeɪʃəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈfeɪʃəl / (BrE)
having only one principal or specialized surface, typically used to describe tools, leaves, or organisms worked, formed, or functioning on one side only

Examples

Unifacial stone tool
Many prehistoric tools were unifacial, shaped and sharpened on only one side.
The archaeologist discovered a unifacial stone blade at the ancient site.
This leaf is unifacial, meaning it has differing textures on its upper and lower surfaces.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bifacial - having two principal or specialized surfaces
