textual matter

noun
written content; words in a text
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛkstʃuəl ˈmætər / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstʃuəl ˈmætə / (BrE)
the words or written content in a book, article, or other document, as opposed to illustrations or other elements

Examples

The textual matter of a book
Aside from the illustrations, the report's textual matter contains valuable information for researchers.
Publishers often review the textual matter separately from the diagrams before final approval.
The book's textual matter was carefully edited to improve clarity and accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: text - the main written part of a book or document
