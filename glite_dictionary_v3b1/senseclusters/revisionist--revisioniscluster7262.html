revisionist

noun
Communist who modifies Marxist doctrine
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɪʒənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒənɪst / (BrE)
a person who challenges or changes established beliefs or interpretations, especially in politics, history, or Marxism

Examples

A revisionist historian
Debates erupted within the movement over whether revisionist ideas were corrupting true socialism.
He was labeled a revisionist by party hardliners for advocating reform.
The regime accused its critics of revisionist tendencies that betrayed Marxist principles.
The revisionist argued that the accepted version of events was incomplete and biased.
Many revisionists have published books that question traditional historical narratives.
Some critics accuse the author of being a revisionist for challenging widely held beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: iconoclast - a person who attacks or criticizes traditional beliefs
  • antonym: orthodox - adhering strictly to traditional Marxist doctrine
  • antonym: traditionalist - a person who supports established customs or beliefs
