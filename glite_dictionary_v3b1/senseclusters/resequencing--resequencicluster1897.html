resequencing

noun
DNA analysis process
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsiːkwənsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsiːkwənsɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of determining the order of DNA bases in a sample again, usually to identify genetic differences from a reference genome

Examples

DNA resequencing
The research team used resequencing to compare the patient's DNA with the reference genome.
Advances in technology have made resequencing faster and more affordable for genetic studies.
Doctors recommended resequencing to identify possible mutations related to the rare disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sequencing - the process of determining the order of DNA bases in a sample
