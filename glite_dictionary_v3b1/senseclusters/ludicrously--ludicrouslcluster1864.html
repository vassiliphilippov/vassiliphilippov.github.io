ludicrously

adverb
in a laughably absurd manner
7.0 0.44/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀê.d…™.kr…ôs.li / (AmE), / ÀàluÀê.d…™.kr…ôs.li / (BrE)
in a way that is so absurd, unreasonable, or exaggerated that it arouses laughter or ridicule

Examples

To be ludicrously wrong
He was ludicrously underdressed for such a formal event.
The hat was ludicrously large for her small head.
Their claims were ludicrously exaggerated to make a point.

Synonyms

  • absurdly
    5.0 2.00/m
    absurdly describes actions that are illogical or extreme, while ludicrously points to absurdity that invites laughter or ridicule.
  • preposterously
    8.1 0.27/m

  • laughably
    5.5 0.76/m
    laughably focuses on causing laughter because of weakness or failure, while ludicrously stresses absurdity that invites ridicule.
  • farcically
    7.9 0.01/m

  • risibly
    8.5 0.01/m
    risibly sounds formal or literary and means laughably, while ludicrously is common and shows absurdity that makes people laugh.
