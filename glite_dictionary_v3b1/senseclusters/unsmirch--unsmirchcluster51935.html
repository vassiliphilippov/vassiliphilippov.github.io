unsmirch

verb
to clear from blame
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsmɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsmɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to remove a stain, tarnish, or blame from someone or something; to restore to a clean or untarnished state

Examples

To unsmirch a reputation
The new evidence unsmirched her reputation before the trial concluded.
His honest actions eventually unsmirched his name in the community.
The report unsmirched the company after the investigation found no wrongdoing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exonerate - to officially clear someone from blame or fault
  • antonym: smirch - to stain or damage someone's reputation
  • synonym: vindicate - to clear someone of blame or suspicion
