tepetate

noun
hard volcanic soil
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛpəˈtɑːteɪ / (AmE), / ˌtɛpəˈtɑːteɪ / (BrE)
a hard, compacted layer of volcanic ash or soil found near the surface, especially in Mexico, that hinders plant growth

Examples

Hard tepetate layer
Farmers often struggle to grow crops where tepetate is close to the surface.
The construction project was delayed because workers encountered a thick layer of tepetate.
In some regions of Mexico, tepetate must be broken up before planting can begin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caliche - a hardened natural cement of calcium carbonate found in arid soils
