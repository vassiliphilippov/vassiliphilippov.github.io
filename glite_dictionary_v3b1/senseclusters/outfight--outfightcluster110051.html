outfight

verb
fight better than an opponent
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈfaɪt / (AmE), / aʊtˈfaɪt / (BrE)
to fight better than an opponent; to defeat in combat through superior fighting.

Examples

To outfight an opponent
Despite being outnumbered, the rebels managed to outfight their adversaries.
If you want to succeed in a tough business, you must outfight your competitors.
The underdog team stunned everyone when they outfought the favorites in the final match.

  • antonym: lose - to be defeated in a contest or battle
