refer

verb
to be relevant to; to concern
3.0 14/m
Pronunciation: / r…ôÀàf…ùÀê / (AmE), / r…™Ààf…úÀê / (BrE)
to have a direct connection or relevance to something; to be about or concerning

Examples

Refer to the rules
Does this policy refer to part-time employees as well?
His comments referred directly to the earlier misunderstanding.
The new regulations refer to online privacy rather than traditional mail.

Synonyms

  • concern
    2.1 45/m

  • have to do with
    2.7 29/m
    have to do with is an informal phrase meaning something is connected or related, while refer is more about pointing to or being about something.
  • bear on
    5.1 3.65/m

  • pertain
    4.6 10/m
    pertain is a formal verb used in law or writing to say something relates to a matter, while refer means to be about something.
