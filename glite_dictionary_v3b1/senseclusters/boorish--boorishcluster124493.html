boorish

adjective
rude and ill-mannered
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊrɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʊərɪʃ / (BrE)
Rude, insensitive, and showing a lack of manners or refinement.

Examples

Boorish behavior
Despite his talent, his boorish attitude made him unpopular among colleagues.
His boorish remarks offended everyone at the dinner table.
She was embarrassed by her friend's boorish behavior at the party.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polite - having good manners and showing respect
  • synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners, refinement, or grace
