stichic verse

noun
verse with unbroken matching lines
7.6 9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪkɪk vɝs / (AmE), / ˈstɪkɪk vɜːs / (BrE)
Poetry composed of lines with the same or similar meter and length, not divided into stanzas.

Examples

Stichic verse poetry
Epic poetry in ancient Greece often employed stichic verse rather than grouping lines into stanzas.
Her latest collection contrasts stichic verse with stanzaic forms to highlight differences in structure.
The unbroken flow of stichic verse gives the poem a steady and continuous rhythm.
