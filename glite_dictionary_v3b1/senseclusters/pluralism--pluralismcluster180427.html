pluralism

noun
philosophy: reality made up of many elements
Pronunciation: / ˈplʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈplʊə.rəˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The doctrine that reality is composed of multiple distinct substances or elements, rather than being reducible to a single principle or substance.

Examples

Philosophical pluralism
Debates between pluralism and monism have shaped philosophical discussions for centuries.
In metaphysics, pluralism asserts that the world is constituted by many different substances.
The philosopher defended pluralism against the idea that reality can be explained by a single principle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monism - the philosophical view that reality is ultimately composed of one substance or principle
