disorganized type schizophrenia

noun
subtype of schizophrenia with disorganized symptoms
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsˈɔrɡəˌnaɪzd taɪp skɪtsəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˌdɪsˈɔːɡənaɪzd tʌɪp skɪtsəˈfriːnɪə / (BrE)
A subtype of schizophrenia marked by severe disruption of personality, erratic speech, childish behaviors, and bizarre actions, most often emerging in adolescence.

Examples

Disorganized type schizophrenia symptoms
Doctors noted that disorganized type schizophrenia often manifests during adolescence with extreme emotional disturbance.
The patient was diagnosed with disorganized type schizophrenia after exhibiting disjointed speech and unpredictable behavior.
Treatment for disorganized type schizophrenia requires a comprehensive approach due to its complex and varied symptoms.
