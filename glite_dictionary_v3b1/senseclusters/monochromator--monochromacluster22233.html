monochromator

noun
light wavelength selector
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkroʊmətər / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkrəʊmətə / (BrE)
a device that separates and transmits a single wavelength of light from a mixture of wavelengths

Examples

A monochromator device
The laboratory technician adjusted the monochromator to isolate the green wavelength for the experiment.
A monochromator is essential in spectroscopy to analyze specific colors of light.
By using a monochromator, the researchers could study the effects of individual wavelengths on the sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filter - a device that allows only certain wavelengths of light to pass through
  • synonym: spectrometer - an instrument used to measure properties of light over a specific portion of the spectrum
