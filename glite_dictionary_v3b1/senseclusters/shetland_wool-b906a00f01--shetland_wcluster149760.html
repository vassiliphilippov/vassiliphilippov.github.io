Shetland wool

noun
type of fine wool from Shetland sheep
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛt.lənd wʊl / (AmE), / ˈʃɛt.lənd wʊl / (BrE)
A fine, soft wool obtained from sheep bred on the Shetland Islands, known for its warmth and lightness.

Examples

Shetland wool yarn
Many knitters seek out Shetland wool for its warmth and durability.
This scarf is made from pure Shetland wool and is incredibly soft.
Traditional Fair Isle sweaters are often crafted from Shetland wool.
