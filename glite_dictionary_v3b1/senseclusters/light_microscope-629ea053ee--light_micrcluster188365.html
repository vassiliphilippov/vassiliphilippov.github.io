light microscope

noun
optical microscope using visible light
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌmaɪkrəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌmaɪkrəskəʊp / (BrE)
An optical instrument that uses visible light and a system of lenses to magnify images of small objects, especially in biology and medicine.

Examples

A light microscope lens
She adjusted the focus on the light microscope to get a clearer view of the bacteria.
Students observed onion cells under a light microscope during the biology lab session.
The light microscope revealed detailed structures within the tissue sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electron microscope - microscope using a beam of electrons to create an image
