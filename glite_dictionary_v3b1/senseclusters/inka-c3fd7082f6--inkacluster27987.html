Inka

noun
member of the indigenous people of Peru's Cuzco valley
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈiŋ.kə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋ.kə / (BrE)
A member of the Quechuan people who lived in the Cuzco valley in Peru, known for establishing the Inca Empire before the Spanish conquest.

Examples

An Inka person
Archaeologists discovered artifacts that belonged to the ancient Inka people near Cuzco.
Many descendants of the Inka still live in Peru and preserve their traditions.
The Inka civilization developed advanced agricultural techniques in the Andean highlands.
