self-incrimination

noun
exposing oneself to criminal accusation
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf ɪnˌkrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪnˌkrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of exposing oneself to prosecution by admitting to involvement in a crime or wrongdoing, especially in a legal context

Examples

Avoid self-incrimination
He refused to answer the prosecutor's questions to avoid self-incrimination.
Her statement in court amounted to self-incrimination and helped the prosecution.
The Fifth Amendment protects citizens against self-incrimination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exculpation - the act of clearing someone from alleged fault or guilt
  • synonym: incrimination - the act of making someone appear guilty of a crime
