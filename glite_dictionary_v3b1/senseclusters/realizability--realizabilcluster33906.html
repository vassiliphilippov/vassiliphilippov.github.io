realizability

noun
constructive interpretation
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːələˌzaɪləˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˌlaɪzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the property of a mathematical statement or proof that allows it to be interpreted as having a concrete computational example or witness.

Examples

Realizability in logic
The concept of realizability is central to certain areas of mathematical logic and computer science.
Researchers discussed how realizability can be used to extract algorithms from proofs.
Understanding realizability helps clarify the constructive content of formal mathematical statements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: classical logic - a traditional approach to logic not requiring constructive witnesses
  • synonym: constructivism - a philosophy in mathematics emphasizing the need for explicit constructions
