seismometer

noun
earthquake measuring device
5.3
Pronunciation: /saɪz.ˈmɑː.mə.tər/ (AmE), /saɪz.ˈmɒm.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an instrument used to detect and measure vibrations or movements in the ground, especially from earthquakes

Examples

A seismometer device
The seismometer detected small tremors before the main earthquake occurred.
Scientists installed a new seismometer near the volcano to monitor ground movement.
A network of seismometers helps researchers study the Earth's crust.
The seismometer recorded a strong tremor during the night.
Scientists installed a new seismometer near the volcano to monitor activity.
A network of seismometers helps detect earthquakes around the world.
The seismometer detected strong tremors during the earthquake last night.
Scientists use a seismometer to monitor volcanic activity in the region.
A portable seismometer was set up to record vibrations from nearby construction work.
