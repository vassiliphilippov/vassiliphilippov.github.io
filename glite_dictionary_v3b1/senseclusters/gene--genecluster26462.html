gene

noun
a unit of heredity in DNA
Pronunciation: / dʒin / (AmE), / dʒiːn / (BrE)
a unit of heredity in a living organism, consisting of a segment of DNA that determines a particular characteristic by directing the synthesis of proteins

Examples

A gene for eye color
Eye color is determined by a specific gene passed down from your parents.
Mutations in the BRCA1 gene can significantly raise the risk of breast cancer.
Scientists discovered a new gene linked to increased risk of heart disease.
