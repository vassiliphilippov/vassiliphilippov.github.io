soon

adverb
in the near future
1.0 315/m
Pronunciation: / suÀên / (AmE), / suÀên / (BrE)
in a short time from now; in the near future

Examples

Very soon
She hopes to hear back from the company soon.
The meeting will begin soon, so please take your seats.
They promised to fix the leak soon, but we're still waiting.

Synonyms

  • shortly
    1.0 36/m
    shortly means within a short time from now, while soon is a general word for the near future.
  • momentarily
    3.6 0.90/m
    momentarily means in a very short moment and is more immediate, while soon covers the near future more generally.
  • in just a minute
    1.7 1.42/m
    in just a minute says something will happen almost immediately, while soon can mean any near future time.
  • before long
    1.6 4.21/m
    before long suggests something will happen after a short wait, while soon often implies a nearer, more immediate time.
  • presently
    3.4 0.45/m
    presently is slightly formal and often means soon in a short time, while soon is more common in everyday speech.
