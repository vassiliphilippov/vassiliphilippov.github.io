decussation

noun
crossing of tracts in an X shape
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkəˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛkjuˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
an intersection or crossing of two structures, especially nerve tracts, in the shape of the letter X

Examples

Nerve decussation
Damage to the area of decussation can result in loss of sensory function on the opposite side of the body.
The optic nerves undergo a decussation at the optic chiasm.
The pattern of decussation is critical in understanding how the brain processes visual information.
