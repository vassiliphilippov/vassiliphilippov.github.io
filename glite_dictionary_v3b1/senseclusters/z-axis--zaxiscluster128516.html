z-axis

noun
axis in 3D coordinate system
4.6
Pronunciation: / zi ˈæksɪs / (AmE), / ziː ˈæksɪs / (BrE)
the third axis in a three-dimensional coordinate system, usually representing depth or height perpendicular to the x- and y-axes

Examples

The z-axis depth
In a standard graph, the z-axis is used to represent depth or height in three dimensions.
Rotating the object around the z-axis gives a different perspective of its three-dimensional shape.
The engineer plotted the data points along the z-axis to show changes in altitude.

Related Terms

  • synonym: x-axis - the horizontal axis in a 2D or 3D coordinate system
  • synonym: y-axis - the vertical axis in a 2D or 3D coordinate system
