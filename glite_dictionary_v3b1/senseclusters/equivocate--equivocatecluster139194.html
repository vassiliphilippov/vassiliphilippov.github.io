equivocate

verb
use ambiguous language to mislead
6.8 9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈkwɪ.və.keɪt/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈkwɪ.və.keɪt/ (BrE)
to use ambiguous or unclear language, especially in order to avoid telling the truth or committing oneself

Examples

To equivocate in speech
It's important not to equivocate when honesty is required in sensitive situations.
Rather than giving a straightforward answer, the politician began to equivocate and avoid the issue.
The spokesperson continued to equivocate when asked direct questions about the company's future plans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be frank - to be open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • synonym: lie - to say something that is not true in order to deceive
  • synonym: prevaricate - to avoid telling the truth by not directly answering a question
