mess

noun
state of confusion or disorder
Pronunciation: / mɛs / (AmE), / mɛs / (BrE)
a state of untidiness, confusion, or a situation full of problems

Examples

A big mess
After the party, the living room was a complete mess with cups and plates everywhere.
He tried to clean up the mess in his office before the meeting.
Her finances are in a mess after months of careless spending.
After the storm, the entire project was in a complete mess.
He found himself in a legal mess after signing that contract.
Her finances became a mess when she lost her job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disorder - a lack of order or regular arrangement; confusion
  • synonym: fix - a difficult or awkward situation (informal)
  • synonym: muddle - a messy, confused, or disorganized state or situation
  • antonym: tidiness - the state of being neatly arranged and in order
