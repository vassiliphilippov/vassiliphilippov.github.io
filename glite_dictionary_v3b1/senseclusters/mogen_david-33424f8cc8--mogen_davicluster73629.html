Mogen David

noun
six-pointed star; symbol of Judaism
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊɡən dəˈviːd / (AmE), / ˈməʊɡən dəˈviːd / (BrE)
A six-pointed star formed by two interlaced equilateral triangles, used as a symbol of Judaism; also known as the Star of David.

Examples

The Mogen David star
Historians believe the Mogen David became associated with Judaism in the Middle Ages.
She wore a silver Mogen David around her neck as a symbol of her faith.
The Israeli flag features the Mogen David prominently in its center.
