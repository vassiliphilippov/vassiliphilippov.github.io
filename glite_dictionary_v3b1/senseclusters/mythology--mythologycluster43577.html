mythology

noun
the collection of myths of a culture
Pronunciation: / mɪˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A collection of myths, especially those belonging to a particular cultural, religious, or social tradition.

Examples

Greek mythology
Ancient Greek mythology tells the stories of gods and heroes who shaped the world.
Every culture has its own unique mythology that explains natural phenomena and traditions.
The company's origin has become part of its corporate mythology over the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: folklore - the traditional beliefs and stories of a community
  • antonym: history - the study of past events, especially human affairs
  • synonym: legend - a traditional story popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated
