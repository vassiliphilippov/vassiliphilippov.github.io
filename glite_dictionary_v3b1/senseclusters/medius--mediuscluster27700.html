medius

adjective
middle position
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəs / (BrE)
situated in the middle or between two other parts, especially in anatomical or scientific contexts

Examples

Medius position
The gluteus medius muscle plays a key role in stabilizing the hip.
In anatomy, the term medius often refers to a structure located between two others.
The doctor explained that the medius nerve runs between the other two main nerves in the arm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intermediate - being in the middle between two things
  • antonym: lateral - situated at the side
