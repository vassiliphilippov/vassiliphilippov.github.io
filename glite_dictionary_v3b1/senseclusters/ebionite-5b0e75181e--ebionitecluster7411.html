Ebionite

noun
member of an early Jewish Christian sect
Pronunciation: / ˈiː.bi.əˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈiː.bɪ.ə.naɪt / (BrE)
a member of an early Jewish Christian group who accepted Jesus as the Messiah, adhered to Jewish law, and rejected Pauline epistles, later deemed heretical by the Church.

Examples

An Ebionite follower
Modern scholars have debated the historical influence of the Ebionites on early Christianity.
Several ancient texts describe the beliefs of the Ebionites in detail.
The Ebionites maintained strict observance of Jewish law while accepting Jesus as the Messiah.
