sterncastle

noun
ship's rear structure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstɜrnˌkæsəl / (AmE), / ˈstɜːnˌkɑːsəl / (BrE)
a raised structure at the rear of a ship, used as a cabin or lookout, especially on older sailing vessels

Examples

The sterncastle cabin
The captain gave orders from the sterncastle as the ship approached the harbor.
During storms, the crew would seek shelter beneath the sterncastle.
From the sterncastle, you could see the entire length of the deck.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forecastle - a raised structure at the front of a ship
  • synonym: quarterdeck - the part of a ship's deck near the stern, often reserved for officers
