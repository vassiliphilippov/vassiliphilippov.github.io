polyarteritis nodosa

noun
rare inflammatory disease of arteries
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliɑːrˈtɛrɪtəs noʊˈdoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌpɒliɑːˌtɛrɪtɪs nəʊˈdəʊsə / (BrE)
A rare, progressive inflammatory disease of connective tissue characterized by nodules along medium-sized arteries, which may block blood flow and cause tissue damage.

Examples

Polyarteritis nodosa disease
After several tests, the patient was diagnosed with polyarteritis nodosa due to her persistent vascular symptoms.
The doctor's research focused on rare diseases such as polyarteritis nodosa that affect the arteries.
Treatment of polyarteritis nodosa usually involves immunosuppressive medications to control the inflammation.
