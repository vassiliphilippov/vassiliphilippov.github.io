low-key

adjective
restrained or understated in style
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊˈki / (AmE), / ˌləʊˈkiː / (BrE)
not showy or intense; calm, simple, or understated in style or quality

Examples

Low-key event
He preferred a low-key approach to leadership, avoiding the spotlight.
Her dress was elegant but low-key, without any extravagant details.
The reception was a low-key affair with just close friends and family.
After a stressful week, we opted for a low-key evening at home with movies and pizza.
He enjoys a low-key lifestyle and avoids the spotlight whenever possible.
Their wedding was surprisingly low-key, with just close family and a simple ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extravagant - lacking restraint in spending money or using resources
  • antonym: flashy - showy or ostentatious, attracting attention
  • antonym: showy - attracting attention by being bright or flashy
  • synonym: subdued - quiet and rather reflective or depressed
  • synonym: understated - presented or expressed in a subtle and effective way
