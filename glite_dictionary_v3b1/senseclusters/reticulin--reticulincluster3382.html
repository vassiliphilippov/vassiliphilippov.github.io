reticulin

noun
connective tissue protein
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɪkjəlɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈtɪkjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a protein that forms a fine network of fibers in connective tissue, providing structural support

Examples

Reticulin fibers
The pathologist stained the tissue to highlight the reticulin fibers under the microscope.
Damage to reticulin can affect the structural integrity of organs like the liver and spleen.
Researchers studied how reticulin networks contribute to the strength of connective tissues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collagen - a main structural protein in connective tissues
  • synonym: elastin - a protein forming elastic fibers in connective tissue
