platitude

noun
an overused, unoriginal remark
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈplæt.ɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈplæt.ɪ.tjuːd / (BrE)
A remark or statement, especially one with moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful; a trite or obvious remark.

Examples

Common platitude
Critics dismissed the novel for its reliance on platitudes instead of fresh insights.
He tried to comfort me, but his words sounded like a platitude rather than genuine concern.
Her speech was filled with empty platitudes about teamwork and dedication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banality - the quality of being unoriginal and boring
  • synonym: cliché - an overused expression or idea
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or unique
