grammar school

noun
secondary school focusing on classical languages
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræmər skuːl / (AmE), / ˈɡræmə skuːl / (BrE)
a secondary school, especially formerly, that emphasized Latin and Greek in preparation for university entry

Examples

Classical grammar school
He attended a grammar school where the curriculum focused on Latin and Greek.
In the past, only students who passed an entrance exam could enter a grammar school.
Many famous scholars received their education at a grammar school in their youth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comprehensive school - a British secondary school not based on academic selection
