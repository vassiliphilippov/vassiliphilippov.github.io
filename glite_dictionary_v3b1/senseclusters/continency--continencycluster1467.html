continency

noun
self-control
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːntɪnənsi / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɪnənsi / (BrE)
the ability to control one's actions, feelings, or desires, especially in difficult situations

Examples

Continency under pressure
His continency helped him avoid arguments even when provoked.
Practicing continency can lead to better decision-making in stressful situations.
She admired his continency when he refused to respond angrily to criticism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act without thinking or control
  • synonym: restraint - the act of holding back or limiting oneself
  • synonym: self-control - the ability to control one's emotions and desires
