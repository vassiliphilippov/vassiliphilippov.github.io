long-staple cotton

noun
cotton with long fibers
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈsteɪpəl ˈkɑtn̩ / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈsteɪpl ˈkɒtn / (BrE)
Cotton with relatively long fibers, valued for its superior quality and strength.

Examples

Long-staple cotton fibers
Egypt is famous for producing high-quality long-staple cotton used in luxury textiles.
Farmers often receive higher prices for long-staple cotton compared to other varieties.
The softness and strength of long-staple cotton make it ideal for making premium bed linens.
