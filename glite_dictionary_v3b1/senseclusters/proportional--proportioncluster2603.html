proportional

adjective
having a constant ratio; corresponding in size or amount

having a relationship where two or more things change in size, amount, or degree together in a consistent way (often in mathematics or when comparing amounts)

Examples

Proportional relationship
In mathematics, the sides of similar triangles are proportional.
The speed of the car is proportional to the pressure applied on the accelerator.
Their pay is proportional to the number of hours they work.
The amount of paint you need is proportional to the size of the wall.
The judge decided that the fine should be proportional to the seriousness of the offense.
There is often a proportional relationship between risk and reward in investing.

Synonyms

  • proportionate
    5.1 1.31/m
    proportionate is a way to say something has a suitable size or amount, while proportional describes a strict mathematical linked change.
  • commensurate
    7.5 2.12/m
    commensurate means matching in size or degree in formal contexts, while proportional describes two things that change together in a fixed way.
