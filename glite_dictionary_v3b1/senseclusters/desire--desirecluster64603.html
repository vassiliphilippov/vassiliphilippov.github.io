desire

noun
strong feeling of wanting something
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzaɪɚ /; / dɪˈzaɪər / (AmE), / dɪˈzaɪə / (BrE)
a strong feeling of wanting something or wishing for something to happen

Examples

A desire for success
His desire for knowledge led him to read constantly.
She tried to ignore her desire to eat more chocolate.
The desire to travel can be very powerful.
After years abroad, his greatest desire was to return home.
She has a desire to travel the world and learn new languages.
The company's main desire is to satisfy its customers.
He felt an overwhelming desire to travel the world after graduating.
She tried to suppress her desire for chocolate during the diet.
The artist's desire for perfection drove him to work late into the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aversion - a strong dislike or disinclination
  • synonym: craving - an intense desire for something
  • synonym: longing - a strong feeling of wanting something or someone you cannot have
  • synonym: wish - a hope for something to happen
