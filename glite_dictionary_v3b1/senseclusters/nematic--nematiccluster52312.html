nematic

noun
liquid crystal phase
9.8
Pronunciation: / nɪˈmætɪk / (AmE), / nɪˈmætɪk / (BrE)
a type of liquid crystal material in which the rod-like molecules are aligned parallel but not arranged in layers

Examples

A nematic material
The researchers studied the properties of the nematic under different temperatures.
A nematic is often used in display technologies because of its unique molecular alignment.
When an electric field is applied, the nematic changes its optical properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquid crystal - a substance that flows like a liquid but has some properties of a solid crystal
  • antonym: smectic - a liquid crystal phase with molecules arranged in layers
