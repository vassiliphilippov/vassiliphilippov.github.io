monger

verb
to sell goods from place to place (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
(archaic) To sell or offer for sale (goods), typically by going from place to place.

Examples

To monger goods
He spent his youth mongering fish along the coastal roads.
In the 17th century, merchants would monger their wares at the bustling markets.
Itinerant salesmen once mongered commodities from village to village.
