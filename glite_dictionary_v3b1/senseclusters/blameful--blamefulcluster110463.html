blameful

adjective
deserving blame or censure
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbleɪmfəl / (AmE), / ˈbleɪmf(ə)l / (BrE)
Deserving blame or censure; worthy of disapproval because of wrongdoing or harmfulness.

Examples

A blameful mistake
It was a blameful oversight that led to the loss of important data.
She felt a blameful sense of regret after realizing the consequences of her choice.
The judge considered his actions particularly blameful given the circumstances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blameworthy - deserving blame or criticism
  • synonym: culpable - deserving blame; guilty
  • antonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or offense
