conductus

noun
medieval vocal composition
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌktəs / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktəs / (BrE)
a type of medieval vocal composition with a Latin text, not based on existing chant melodies

Examples

A conductus song
The choir performed a 13th-century conductus at the concert.
Music historians study the conductus to understand medieval polyphony.
A conductus often features newly composed melodies rather than borrowed chants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chant - a simple, monophonic song, often used in religious services
  • synonym: motet - a polyphonic choral composition from the medieval or Renaissance period
