splint bone

noun
small bone beside the cannon bone in horses
Pronunciation: / splɪnt boʊn / (AmE), / splɪnt bəʊn / (BrE)
either of the two slender, rudimentary bones on either side of the main cannon bone in the leg of a horse or related animal

Examples

The splint bone in horse
Foals are sometimes born with an underdeveloped splint bone that fuses as they mature.
Swelling around the splint bone may indicate inflammation or fracture.
The veterinarian examined the horse’s splint bone for signs of injury.
