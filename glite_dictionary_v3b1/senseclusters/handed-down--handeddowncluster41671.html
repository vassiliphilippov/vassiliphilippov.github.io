handed-down

adjective
passed from generation to generation
Pronunciation: / ˌhændɪd ˈdaʊn / (AmE), / ˌhændɪd ˈdaʊn / (BrE)
passed from one generation or person to another, especially within families or communities

Examples

Handed-down customs
Many of the customs in this village are handed-down through centuries of tradition.
She wore a beautiful handed-down wedding dress from her grandmother.
The family recipe was a handed-down secret for generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inherited - received from ancestors through succession
