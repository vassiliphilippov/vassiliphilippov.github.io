support

verb
agree with or approve of an idea or cause
1.0 134/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàp…îrt / (AmE), / s…ôÀàp…îÀêt / (BrE)
to give help, encouragement, or approval to someone or something; to back or stand by a person, idea, or cause

Examples

To support an idea
I cannot support that opinion because I see the situation differently.
Many citizens support the new environmental policy implemented by the government.
She openly supports equal rights for all members of the community.
Even in difficult times, her friends always support her emotionally.
He felt encouraged knowing his family would support him no matter what.
Teachers should support students who struggle with confidence.
At the meeting, she supports the idea of expanding the park.
He strongly supported her during the debate.
Several experts support the proposed changes to the law.

Synonyms

  • back
    1.0 41/m
    back is to give active help or approval to a person or idea, while support covers help, approval, or encouragement broadly.
  • back up
    3.0 12/m
    back up is to support someone with help, facts, or encouragement, while support is a general word for helping or approving.
  • endorse
    4.2 12/m
    endorse is to give public or official approval to a person or plan, while support also includes private or everyday help and encouragement.
  • encourage
    2.3 100/m
    encourage is to give confidence and hope so someone continues, while support can mean practical help, approval, or standing by them.
  • help out
    1.0 11/m
    help out is to give practical or temporary aid in a specific situation, while support covers broader help, approval, or encouragement.
