cognitivist

noun
moral philosophy believer
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːɡnətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnɪtɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that moral statements express beliefs that can be objectively true or false

Examples

A moral cognitivist
The philosopher identified herself as a cognitivist in debates about the nature of moral language.
Many cognitivists argue that ethical statements can be supported by evidence and reason.
A cognitivist would claim that saying 'stealing is wrong' is a statement that can be true or false.

Related Terms

  • antonym: noncognitivist - a person who believes moral statements do not express true or false beliefs
