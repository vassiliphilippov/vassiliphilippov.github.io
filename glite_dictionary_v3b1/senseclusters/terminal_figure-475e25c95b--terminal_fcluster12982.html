terminal figure

noun
architectural statue atop a pillar
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrmɪnəl ˈfɪɡjɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɜːmɪnəl ˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
(in architecture) a statue, usually a bust or animal, carved at the top of a square pillar, originally used as a boundary marker in ancient Rome.

Examples

A terminal figure statue
A terminal figure of Hermes was placed at crossroads in classical Rome.
Museum visitors marveled at the craftsmanship of the restored terminal figure.
The ancient garden was adorned with a terminal figure marking its border.
