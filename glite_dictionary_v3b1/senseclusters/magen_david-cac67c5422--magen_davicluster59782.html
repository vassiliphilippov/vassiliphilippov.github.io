Magen David

noun
six-pointed star, Jewish symbol
Pronunciation: / məˌɡɛn dɑːˈviːd / (AmE), / məˌɡɛn dɑːˈviːd / (BrE)
A six-pointed star formed by two overlapping equilateral triangles, used as a symbol of Judaism.

Examples

The Magen David symbol
He wore a silver Magen David pendant around his neck.
The Israeli flag features the Magen David at its center.
The synagogue's entrance is adorned with a large Magen David.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Star of David - another name for the six-pointed star symbol of Judaism
