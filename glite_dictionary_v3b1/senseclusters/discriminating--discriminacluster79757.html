discriminating

adjective
showing good judgment or taste
2.8 5.0
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈskrɪ.mɪ.neɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskrɪ.mə.neɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈskrɪ.mɪ.neɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈskrɪ.mə.neɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
having or showing good judgment and the ability to notice and appreciate fine differences, especially in matters of taste or quality

Examples

A discriminating reader
Only a discriminating reader would notice the clever references in the novel.
She has a discriminating palate and can easily notice subtle flavors.
The gallery attracts a discriminating clientele who appreciate fine art.
His discriminating taste in art made his collection especially valuable.
Only the most discriminating customers will notice the subtle differences between these wines.
She is a discriminating reader who appreciates high-quality literature.
