lubricity

noun
slipperiness; lubrication efficiency
8.9
Pronunciation: /luː.ˈbrɪ.sɪ.tiː/ /; / /luː.ˈbrɪ.sə.tiː/ (AmE), /luː.ˈbrɪ.sɪ.tiː/ /; / /luː.ˈbrɪ.sə.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a surface or substance that makes movement smooth by reducing friction or wear; effectiveness as a lubricant.

Examples

Lubricity of oil
Engineers assessed the lubricity of the new coating to minimize wear.
Low lubricity in the joint caused increased friction and noisy operation in the machine.
They tested different oils to improve the engine's lubricity under high temperatures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lubrication - the process or result of applying a substance to reduce friction between surfaces
  • synonym: slipperiness - the characteristic of being smooth or causing things to slide easily
