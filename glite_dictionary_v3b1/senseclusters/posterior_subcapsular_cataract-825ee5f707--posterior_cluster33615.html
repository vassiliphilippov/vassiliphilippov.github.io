posterior subcapsular cataract

noun
cataract at the back of the eye's lens capsule
8.4
Pronunciation: / pəˈstɪriɚ ˌsʌbkæpˈsulɚ ˈkætəˌrækt / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪərɪə ˌsʌbkæpˈsjʊlə ˈkætərækt / (BrE)
a type of cataract that forms at the back of the lens capsule in the eye, often affecting vision more in bright light or when reading

Examples

A posterior subcapsular cataract in eye
A posterior subcapsular cataract can cause more difficulty seeing in bright sunlight than other types of cataracts.
Patients with diabetes are at increased risk for developing a posterior subcapsular cataract.
Surgery is often recommended when a posterior subcapsular cataract significantly affects daily activities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cataract - a clouding of the lens in the eye causing vision impairment
