Hipparion

proper noun
extinct three-toed horse
Pronunciation: / hɪˈpɛriən / (AmE), / hɪˈpæriən / (BrE)
an extinct genus of three-toed horses that lived during the Miocene and Pliocene epochs

Examples

Hipparion skeleton
Paleontologists discovered a well-preserved Hipparion skeleton in the ancient riverbed.
The Hipparion roamed across vast grasslands millions of years ago.
Fossil evidence shows that Hipparion lived alongside early ancestors of modern horses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Equus - the genus that includes modern horses, zebras, and donkeys
