dihydroxyacetone

noun
simple ketose sugar
Pronunciation: / daɪˌhaɪdrɒksiˈæsɪtoʊn / (AmE), / daɪˌhɪdrɒksiˈasɪtəʊn / (BrE)
a simple three-carbon sugar used in biochemical research and as an intermediate in metabolic processes

Examples

Dihydroxyacetone in metabolism
Researchers added dihydroxyacetone to the solution to observe its effect on the metabolic reaction.
The presence of dihydroxyacetone is important in the glycolysis pathway of cells.
Many self-tanning products use dihydroxyacetone as the active ingredient for skin coloration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: glucose - a common six-carbon sugar used as energy in living organisms
  • synonym: glyceraldehyde - a simple three-carbon sugar and metabolic intermediate
