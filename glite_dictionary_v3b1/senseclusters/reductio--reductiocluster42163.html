reductio

noun
argument technique exposing an absurdity
9.6 9.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.dʌk.ˈtɪ.oʊ/ /; / /rɪ.dʌk.ˈtiː.oʊ/ (AmE), /rɪ.dʌk.ˈtɪ.əʊ/ /; / /rɪ.dʌk.ˈtiː.əʊ/ (BrE)
A method of argument that disproves a proposition by showing its consequences are absurd, or proves one by showing that its denial leads to a contradiction.

Examples

A reductio argument
A classic reductio argument tests a claim by proposing its logical extreme.
The philosopher employed reductio to demonstrate the weakness in her opponent's position.
Using reductio, the mathematician showed that the assumption must be false.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reductio ad absurdum - an argument that refutes a proposition by showing absurd consequences
