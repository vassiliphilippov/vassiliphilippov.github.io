regolith

noun
loose surface layer
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡəlɪθ / (BrE)
a layer of loose rock, dust, and soil that covers solid bedrock on the surface of a planet, moon, or asteroid

Examples

Loose regolith layer
The rover analyzed the regolith to search for signs of water on Mars.
Scientists study lunar regolith to understand the history of the Moon's surface.
Thick layers of regolith can make construction on asteroids challenging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bedrock - the solid rock layer beneath soil or regolith
  • synonym: soil - the upper layer of earth in which plants grow
