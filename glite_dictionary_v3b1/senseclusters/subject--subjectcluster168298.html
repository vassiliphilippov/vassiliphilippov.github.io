subject

noun
person owing allegiance to a ruler
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.dʒɪkt / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.dʒɪkt / (BrE)
a person who owes allegiance to a sovereign or state, especially one under monarchical rule

Examples

Royal subject
As a subject of the empire, he was required to obey the laws set by the monarch.
During the ceremony, each subject pledged allegiance to the new queen.
The king addressed his loyal subjects in the main square.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citizen - a legally recognized subject or national of a state, either native or naturalized
  • antonym: monarch - a sovereign head of state, especially a king, queen, or emperor
