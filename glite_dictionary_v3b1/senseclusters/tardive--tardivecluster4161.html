tardive

adjective
late-occurring, especially of medical symptoms
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːr.dɪv / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.dɪv / (BrE)
(of symptoms) occurring late in the course of a disease or after treatment has begun, especially used in medical contexts.

Examples

Tardive dyskinesia
Doctors are concerned about the risk of tardive dyskinesia in long-term antipsychotic use.
Research has focused on the prevention of tardive conditions caused by certain drug therapies.
The patient developed tardive symptoms several months after starting the medication.
