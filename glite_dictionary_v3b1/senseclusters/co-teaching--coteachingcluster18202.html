co-teaching

noun
joint classroom teaching
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈtiːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a teaching method in which two teachers work together in the same classroom to instruct students

Examples

Co-teaching in classrooms
The school introduced co-teaching to better support students with diverse learning needs.
Effective co-teaching requires strong communication and planning between both teachers.
Many educators find that co-teaching helps create a more inclusive classroom environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: solo teaching - a method where one teacher instructs a class alone
  • synonym: team teaching - a method where two or more teachers jointly deliver instruction
