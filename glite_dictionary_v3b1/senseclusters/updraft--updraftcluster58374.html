updraft

noun
upward movement of air
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʌp.dræft / (AmE), / ˈʌp.drɑːft / (BrE)
a strong upward movement of air, often caused by warm air rising

Examples

Strong updraft
An updraft kept the glider soaring high above the hills.
Birds often use a rising updraft to gain altitude without flapping their wings.
Thunderstorms are fueled by powerful updrafts that push warm air into the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downdraft - a downward current of air
