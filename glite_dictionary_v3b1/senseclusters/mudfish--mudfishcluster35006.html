mudfish

noun
swamp-dwelling fish
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish that lives in muddy or swampy water and can survive in low-oxygen or dry conditions (such as the New Zealand mudfish or North American bowfin)

Examples

The mudfish swims
The mudfish can survive dry seasons by burrowing into the mud.
Scientists study the mudfish to understand how animals adapt to low-oxygen environments.
Local fishermen often catch mudfish in the shallow, swampy parts of the river.
The rare mudfish can survive in muddy wetlands even when water levels drop.
Scientists are studying how mudfish adapt to changing environmental conditions in New Zealand.
Local communities are working to restore habitats for the endangered mudfish.
The fisherman caught a large mudfish in the shallow, weedy part of the river.
Many anglers consider the mudfish a challenging catch because of its strength.
Children watched as the muddy water revealed a wriggling mudfish near the bank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bowfin - a North American freshwater fish with a long body and rounded tail
  • synonym: catfish - a fish often found in muddy waters with whisker-like barbels
  • synonym: galaxiid - a type of small freshwater fish found in the Southern Hemisphere
  • synonym: lungfish - a fish able to breathe air and survive in mud during dry periods
