henchman

noun
loyal follower, often for wrongdoing
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛntʃ.mən / (AmE), / ˈhɛntʃ.mən / (BrE)
A faithful supporter or subordinate, particularly one who assists a powerful person in criminal or immoral activities.

Examples

A henchman of crime
Detectives arrested the henchman who had been helping to orchestrate the bank heist.
In classic films, the hero often confronts the henchman before facing the main antagonist.
The villain's trusted henchman carried out his orders without question.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime
  • antonym: mastermind - a person who plans and directs an illegal or complex enterprise, often employing henchmen
