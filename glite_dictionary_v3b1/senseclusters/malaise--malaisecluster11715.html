malaise

noun
general feeling of discomfort or illness
Pronunciation: / məˈleɪz / (AmE), / məˈleɪz / (BrE)
A general feeling of physical discomfort, unease, or mild illness, often without a specific cause.

Examples

A malaise feeling
After working nonstop for weeks, she began to feel a persistent malaise that she couldn't shake.
The doctor asked if he had experienced any malaise along with his fever and headache.
There was a general sense of malaise throughout the office after the layoffs were announced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discomfort - a feeling of slight pain or unease
  • antonym: well-being - a state of being comfortable, healthy, or happy
