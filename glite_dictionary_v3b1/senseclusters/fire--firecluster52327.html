fire

noun
element in ancient philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
In ancient and medieval philosophy, one of the four basic elements believed to compose the physical universe, associated with heat and dryness and linked to the bodily humour bile.

Examples

Elemental fire
Empedocles included fire among the basic substances in his theory of the universe.
In ancient philosophy, fire was considered one of the four elements that made up all matter.
The humour bile was thought to be tied to the element fire in medieval medicine.
