purple gallinule

noun
a brightly colored marsh bird with purple-blue plumage
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜr.pəl ˈɡæl.əˌnul / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ˈɡæl.ɪ.njuːl / (BrE)
a brightly colored gallinule bird, especially Porphyrio martinicus, with purple-blue plumage and found in marshes and wetlands of the Americas

Examples

The purple gallinule bird
Birdwatchers often travel to wetlands hoping to catch a glimpse of the elusive purple gallinule.
The purple gallinule is known for its vibrant plumage and striking bright red bill.
We spotted a purple gallinule moving gracefully through the reeds at the edge of the lagoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gallinule - a wading bird of the rail family with long legs and toes
