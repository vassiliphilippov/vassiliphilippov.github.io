pale-faced

adjective
having a pale face
4.1
Pronunciation: / peɪl feɪst / (AmE), / peɪl feɪst / (BrE)
having a face that is unusually light in color, especially as a result of illness, fear, or shock

Examples

Pale-faced person
After weeks indoors, he emerged pale-faced and in need of sunlight.
She looked pale-faced and worried after receiving the bad news.
The pale-faced boy stood silently in the doctor's waiting room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flushed - having a red or pink face, often from exercise, emotion, or illness
  • synonym: pallid - having an unhealthy pale appearance
  • antonym: rosy-cheeked - having pink or red cheeks
