radial-ply tire

noun
tire with radially arranged plies
9.8 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiəl plaɪ ˌtaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəl plaɪ ˌtaɪə / (BrE)
A pneumatic tire in which the reinforcing cords of the carcass ply are arranged at 90 degrees to the direction of travel, improving flexibility and performance.

Examples

New radial-ply tires
He replaced the old bias-ply tires with new radial-ply tires for a smoother ride.
Many modern vehicles are equipped with radial-ply tires for improved comfort and traction.
The advantages of radial-ply tires include longer tread life and better fuel efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bias-ply tire - tire in which plies are diagonally arranged to the direction of travel
