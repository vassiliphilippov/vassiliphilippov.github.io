auxiliary verb

noun
verb used to form tense or mood
5.9 2.5
Pronunciation: /ɑːg.ˈzɪl.jə.riː vɜːr/ /; / /ɔːg.ˈzɪl.jə.riː vɜːr/ (AmE), /ɔːg.ˈzɪl.jə.riː vɜːb/ /; / /ɔːg.ˈzɪl.jɪə.riː vɜːb/ (BrE)
A verb used with another verb to form tenses, moods, voices, or aspects in a verb phrase, such as 'be,' 'have,' or 'do.'

Examples

An auxiliary verb form
English sentences often use an auxiliary verb to form the passive voice.
In the sentence 'She has finished her work,' 'has' is an auxiliary verb.
The word 'will' acts as an auxiliary verb in 'I will go to the store.'

Related Terms

  • synonym: modal verb - a verb that expresses necessity or possibility, like 'can', 'may', 'must'
