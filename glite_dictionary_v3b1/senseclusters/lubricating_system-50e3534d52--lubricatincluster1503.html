lubricating system

noun
system for delivering oil to engine bearings
Pronunciation: / ˈluːbrɪˌkeɪtɪŋ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈluːbrɪˌkeɪtɪŋ ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a mechanical system in an internal combustion engine that forces oil to the engine bearings using a pump to reduce friction and wear

Examples

The lubricating system pump
Failure of the lubricating system can result in serious engine damage due to lack of oil flow.
Modern cars use an advanced lubricating system to ensure optimal performance and longevity.
The lubricating system is essential for keeping engine parts moving smoothly and preventing excessive wear.
