cauldron

noun
large metal pot for boiling
4.8 1.50/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îl.dr…ôn / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀêl.dr…ôn / (BrE)
A large metal pot used for boiling liquids over an open fire.

Examples

A large cauldron
He used a massive cauldron to prepare soup for the entire camp.
In the museum, there was a medieval cauldron on display among the old kitchen tools.
The witches gathered around the cauldron, chanting mysterious spells as the potion bubbled.

Synonyms

  • stockpot
    2.3 0.24/m

  • stewpot
    2.4 0.11/m
    a stewpot cooks stews and soups on a modern stove for family meals, while a cauldron is used over an open fire.
