sinfully

adverb
excessively or guiltily
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnfəli / (AmE), / ˈsɪnfəli / (BrE)
in a way that is extremely pleasurable, excessive, or gives a sense of guilty pleasure (often used for emphasis)

Examples

A sinfully rich cake
He enjoyed a sinfully rich chocolate cake despite being on a diet.
The hotel suite was sinfully luxurious, with every possible comfort provided.
She smiled sinfully as she revealed her surprise plan for the evening.
The chocolate cake was sinfully rich and delicious, making it hard to stop eating.
She spent a sinfully long time relaxing in the hot bath after work.
His new car is sinfully expensive, but he says it is worth every penny.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excessively - to a degree that is more than is necessary or reasonable
  • synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • antonym: moderately - to an average or reasonable degree
  • antonym: virtuously - in a way that shows high moral standards
  • synonym: wickedly - in a way that is morally wrong or mischievous
