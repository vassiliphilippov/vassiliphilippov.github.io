aerate

verb
to supply with air or oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌeɪt /; / ˈɛreɪt /; / ˈɛr.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈeəreɪt / (BrE)
to add or introduce air (or oxygen) into something

Examples

To aerate blood
Doctors sometimes aerate blood outside the body during certain surgical procedures.
Plants aerate the soil with their roots as they grow.
You should aerate your lawn every spring to encourage healthy grass growth.
Continuous aerating of sewage is essential for effective decomposition in many modern facilities.
Engineers aerated the wastewater to promote the activity of beneficial bacteria.
The treatment plant routinely aerates the sewage to accelerate the breakdown of waste.
Gardeners often aerate the soil to help plants grow better.
It is important to aerate your room regularly to keep the air fresh.
You should aerate your old sneakers to get rid of unpleasant odors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxygenate - to supply, treat, or enrich with oxygen
  • synonym: ventilate - to allow fresh air to enter and circulate
