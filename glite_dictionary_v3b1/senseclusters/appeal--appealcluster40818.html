appeal

verb
formally challenge a decision, especially legally
3.5 4.1
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈpiːl/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈpiːl/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪl/ (BrE)
to formally request that a decision, especially a legal or official one, be changed; to challenge a decision in a higher court or authority

Examples

To appeal a decision
If you disagree with the decision, you can appeal within 30 days.
She appealed the verdict, hoping for a different outcome.
The company decided to appeal the ruling in a higher court.
After losing the case, the defense team decided to appeal the verdict to the Supreme Court.
If you believe the court's decision was unfair, you have the right to appeal.
She appealed the ruling, hoping for a more favorable outcome from the higher court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to agree with a decision or outcome, not to challenge
  • synonym: challenge - to formally question a legal decision
  • synonym: contest - to formally challenge or dispute a decision
