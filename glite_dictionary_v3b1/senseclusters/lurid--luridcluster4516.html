lurid

adjective
glowing with an unnatural red or fiery light
Pronunciation: / ˈlʊrɪd, ˈljuːrɪd /; / ˈlʊrɪd / (AmE), / ˈljʊərɪd, ˈlʊərɪd /; / ˈljʊərɪd / (BrE)
shining with a vivid, unnatural, or ghastly light or appearance

Examples

Lurid glow
A lurid light came from the factory, shining through the dense evening smog.
The forest was bathed in a lurid glow as the fire raged through the trees.
We watched the lurid sunset paint the sky with streaks of red and orange.
A lurid light cast strange shadows on the old castle walls.
Her skin took on a lurid hue under the harsh fluorescent lights.
The ghost's face had a lurid glow in the moonlight, making it appear even more frightening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pallid - lacking color or liveliness; pale
  • antonym: vivid - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind
  • synonym: wan - pale and giving the impression of illness or exhaustion
