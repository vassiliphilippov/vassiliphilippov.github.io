transcendentalize

verb
to make spiritual or abstract
Pronunciation: / trænˌsɛndənˈtælaɪz / (AmE), / trænˌsɛndənˈtælaɪz / (BrE)
to make something appear spiritual, abstract, or beyond ordinary experience

Examples

To transcendentalize art
The artist tried to transcendentalize the landscape by using vivid colors and dreamlike shapes.
Philosophers sometimes transcendentalize everyday concepts to explore deeper meanings.
The film director's style tends to transcendentalize ordinary moments, making them feel profound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than reality
  • antonym: materialize - to make something physical or concrete
