saprophytic

adjective
feeding on decaying organic matter
4.6 8.1
Pronunciation: /sæ.prə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ /; / /sə.prə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ (AmE), /sæ.prə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ /; / /sə.prə.ˈfɪt.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of an organism, especially a plant or fungus) getting food by feeding on dead or decaying organic matter

Examples

Saprophytic fungus
Many mushrooms are saprophytic, breaking down fallen leaves and wood in the forest.
Some orchids have lost their chlorophyll and live a saprophytic lifestyle, relying on fungi for nutrients.
The saprophytic bacteria in soil help decompose dead plants and animals.
Many fungi are saprophytic, thriving on decomposing plant material in the soil.
Some plants have partially saprophytic relationships, drawing nourishment from decayed matter.
The saprophytic bacteria helped break down the dead leaves into nutrients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parasitic - living as a parasite; feeding on a living host
