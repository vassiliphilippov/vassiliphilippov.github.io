galvanism

noun
use of electricity for medical therapy
Pronunciation: / ˈɡælvəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡal.və.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the therapeutic use of electricity in the treatment of disease, especially the application of electric currents to stimulate muscles or nerves

Examples

Medical galvanism
Early experiments with galvanism led to important discoveries about how nerves and muscles respond to electricity.
In the nineteenth century, galvanism was considered a promising treatment for paralysis.
Many doctors were fascinated by the apparent healing powers of galvanism in restoring movement to paralyzed limbs.
