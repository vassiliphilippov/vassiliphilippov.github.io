afferent

adjective
carrying sensory signals to the CNS
Pronunciation: / ˈæf.ɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ˈæf.ər.ənt / (BrE)
(of nerves or nerve impulses) carrying sensory information from the sense organs toward the central nervous system.

Examples

Afferent signals
A problem with the afferent pathway can result in loss of sensation.
Damage to afferent fibers hinders the body's ability to respond to external stimuli.
The doctor explained that afferent nerves transmit sensory signals from the skin to the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: efferent - (of nerves or impulses) carrying signals away from the central nervous system to the body
