foot-lambert

noun
unit of luminance (one lumen/sq ft)
Pronunciation: / fʊt ˈlæmbɚt / (AmE), / fʊt ˈlæmbət / (BrE)
a unit of luminance equal to one lumen per square foot, formerly used in photometry.

Examples

One foot-lambert
Older projectors often specified output in foot-lambert rather than candelas per square meter.
The brightness of the cinema screen was measured at 14 foot-lamberts to meet industry standards.
To comply with regulations, the monitor's luminance was set to 16 foot-lamberts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: candela - the SI base unit of luminous intensity
