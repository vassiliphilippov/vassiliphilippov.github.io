creatine phosphoric acid

noun
an energy-storing compound in muscle tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈkriː.əˌtin ˌfɔsˈfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈkriː.əˌtiːn ˌfɒsˈfɒrɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an organic compound found in the muscles of vertebrates that stores energy and releases it during muscular contraction

Examples

Creatine phosphoric acid in muscles
During intense exercise, creatine phosphoric acid donates a phosphate group to help regenerate ATP.
Scientists studied how creatine phosphoric acid levels affect muscle performance in athletes.
The breakdown of creatine phosphoric acid provides rapid energy for short bursts of activity.
