amphibian

noun
cold-blooded vertebrate living on land and water
5.9
Pronunciation: / æmˈfɪbiən / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbiən / (BrE)
a cold-blooded vertebrate animal, such as a frog or salamander, typically living both on land and in water, with aquatic larvae that undergo metamorphosis into the adult form

Examples

A amphibian like a frog
Deforestation threatens the survival of many rare amphibians in the rainforest.
Frogs and salamanders are well-known amphibians found in many parts of the world.
The amphibian spends its early life underwater as a tadpole before moving onto land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frog - a tailless amphibian with long hind legs
  • degree higher: reptile - a cold-blooded vertebrate with scales, such as a snake or lizard
