ruin

verb
destroy or spoil something completely
Pronunciation: / ˈruːɪn /; / ˈruː.ɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːɪn /; / ˈruː.ɪn / (BrE)
to destroy or severely damage something so that it cannot be used, enjoyed, or is no longer valuable (including reputation, finances, or objects)

Examples

To ruin a career
Heavy rainfall ruined the farmer's entire crop.
If you leave your phone in water, you could completely ruin it.
She accidentally ruined her dress with a permanent marker.
One careless investment can ruin a family's finances for generations.
She feared that the lawsuit would ruin her small business.
The scandal completely ruined him financially, leaving him destitute.
If you leave your wet clothes inside the bag, you could ruin everything in it.
One mistake might ruin his entire career.
Too much rain could ruin the outdoor wedding ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bankrupt - to reduce to insolvency by financial loss
  • synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it

  • antonym: restore - to bring back to a former condition; repair or renovate
  • antonym: save - to rescue or protect something from harm or destruction
  • synonym: wreck - to cause extensive or total damage to something
