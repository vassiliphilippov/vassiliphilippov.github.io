sacre

noun
coronation ceremony
9.9 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ëÀêkr…ô / (AmE), / Ààsakr…ô / (BrE)
a formal ceremony of consecration or coronation, especially the ritual crowning of a monarch to make them sacred

Examples

Royal sacre ceremony
The royal sacre was attended by dignitaries from across the continent.
Historians studied the rituals performed during the sacre of medieval kings.
The cathedral was filled with music and incense for the sacre of the new monarch.

Synonyms

  • coronation
    5.3 1.73/m
    coronation is a public crowning that gives a monarch official power and status, while sacre is a sacred crowning ritual making the monarch holy.
  • consecration
    8.7 0.56/m
    consecration is a religious act that makes a person or place holy through a ceremony, while sacre is the ritual crowning to make a monarch sacred.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consecration - the act of making something sacred
  • synonym: coronation - the ceremony of crowning a king or queen
