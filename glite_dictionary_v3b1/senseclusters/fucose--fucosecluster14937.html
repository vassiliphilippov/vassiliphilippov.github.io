fucose

noun
biological sugar
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈfjuːkəʊs / (BrE)
a type of sugar found in plants and animals, important in biological processes such as cell signaling

Examples

Fucose sugar
Scientists discovered that fucose plays a key role in immune system function.
Some seaweeds are rich sources of fucose, which is used in biological research.
The presence of fucose on cell surfaces can affect how cells communicate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galactose - a simple sugar found in milk and some plants
  • synonym: glucose - a simple sugar that is an important energy source
