dystopian

adjective
characterized by great misery or oppression
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtoʊpiən /; / dɪˈstoʊpiən / (AmE), / dɪsˈtəʊpiən /; / dɪsˈtəʊpɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a society or situation marked by great suffering, oppression, or misery, often imagined or in the future

Examples

dystopian society
His paintings often depict dystopian cityscapes full of decay and despair.
Many critics see the novel as a warning about a possible dystopian future.
The dystopian setting of the film left audiences feeling deeply unsettled.
Her latest painting has a distinctly dystopian atmosphere, filled with dark, looming buildings and empty streets.
Many science fiction films explore dystopian societies marked by surveillance and control.
The novel depicts a dystopian future where personal freedoms are severely restricted.

Related Terms

  • antonym: utopian - characterized by ideal perfection or an imagined perfect society
