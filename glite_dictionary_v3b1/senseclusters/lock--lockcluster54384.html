lock

noun
section of canal with gates for raising or lowering boats
5.5 2/m
Pronunciation: / lÉ‘k / (AmE), / lÉ’k / (BrE)
a section of a canal or river that can be closed off with gates to raise or lower the water level and allow boats to pass between stretches of water of different levels

Examples

Canal lock
Construction workers repaired the old lock on the river to ensure smooth passage for boats.
The cargo ship entered the lock to be raised to the next level of the canal.
Tourists watched as the gates of the lock slowly opened and the water rushed in.
