hooded cloak

noun
long cloak with a hood
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhʊdəd kloʊk / (AmE), / ˈhʊdɪd kləʊk / (BrE)
a long outer garment with a hood that can be pulled over the head

Examples

A hooded cloak for travel
In medieval times, travelers often wore a hooded cloak for protection from the elements.
She wrapped herself in a hooded cloak to keep warm on the cold night.
The mysterious figure entered the room, his face hidden by a hooded cloak.
