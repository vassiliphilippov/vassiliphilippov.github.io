gravity-assist

noun
maneuver using gravity of a planetary body
5.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈgræv.ɪ.tiː ə.ˈsɪst/ (AmE), /ˈgræv.ɪ.tiː ə.ˈsɪst/ (BrE)
A method in spaceflight where a spacecraft passes near a planetary body to gain energy and alter its trajectory by using the planet's gravitational field.

Examples

A gravity-assist maneuver
Mission planners designed the trajectory to include a gravity-assist for greater fuel efficiency.
The probe used a gravity-assist at Venus to increase its speed toward the outer planets.
Without the gravity-assist maneuver, the spacecraft would have required much more energy to reach Jupiter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gravitational slingshot - an alternative term for gravity-assist, focusing on the effect of gravity to change speed and direction
