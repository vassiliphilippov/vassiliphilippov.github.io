layperson

noun
non-expert; non-clergy person
4.9 1.22/m
Pronunciation: / Ààle…™Àåp…ùÀês…ôn / (AmE), / Ààle…™Àåp…úÀês…ôn / (BrE)
a person who is not a member of a particular profession or specialized group, especially not a member of the clergy

Examples

A layperson in medicine
As a layperson, she found the medical report difficult to understand.
He spoke about astronomy in a way that was accessible to the average layperson.
The judge explained the law in simple terms so any layperson could follow.

Synonyms

  • layman
    4.9 1.83/m
    layman is an older male term for someone outside a profession or the clergy, while layperson is gender-neutral.
  • nonspecialist
    3.9 0.23/m
    nonspecialist lacks expert training in a subject, while layperson describes someone not part of a profession or clergy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expert - a person with special knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • antonym: professional - a member of a profession or someone with specialized knowledge
