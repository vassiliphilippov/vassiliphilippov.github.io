neo-Lamarckian

adjective
relating to a modern form of Lamarckism
9.0 9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌniː.oʊ.ləˈmɑːrk.i.ən / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊ.ləˈmɑːk.i.ən / (BrE)
relating to or denoting a modern version of Lamarckism, especially theories that propose the inheritance of acquired characteristics

Examples

A neo-Lamarckian theory
Several scientists in the early twentieth century adopted a neo-Lamarckian perspective.
Some textbooks briefly mention neo-Lamarckian ideas alongside other evolutionary theories.
The debate between Darwinian and neo-Lamarckian explanations of evolution was intense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lamarckism - the theory that an organism can pass on characteristics acquired during its lifetime
