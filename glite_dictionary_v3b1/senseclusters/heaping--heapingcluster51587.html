heaping

adjective
very full or piled
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhiːpɪŋ / (BrE)
containing or forming a very large amount, often so much that it forms a pile or heap

Examples

A heaping spoonful
He gave me a heaping spoonful of sugar for my tea.
The chef placed a heaping pile of fries next to the burger.
She carried a heaping basket of laundry up the stairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overflowing - filled beyond the top or limits
  • antonym: scant - barely sufficient or not quite full
