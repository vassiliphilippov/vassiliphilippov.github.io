prolamine

noun
plant protein found in cereal grains
5.7
Pronunciation: / proʊˈlæmɪn / (AmE), / prəʊˈlamɪn / (BrE)
a class of simple plant proteins, mainly found in the seeds of cereal grains, that are soluble in alcohol and have high proline content

Examples

Prolamine in seeds
Researchers studied the structure of prolamine extracted from barley grains.
Some people with celiac disease must avoid foods rich in prolamine proteins.
Wheat contains a type of prolamine that is commonly known as gliadin.

Related Terms

  • antonym: albumin - a water-soluble protein found in egg white, blood, and various plant tissues
  • synonym: gluten - a mixture of proteins found in wheat and related grains
