inbounds

adjective
within playing area
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.baʊndz/ /; / /ɪn.ˈbaʊndz/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.baʊndz/ /; / /ɪn.ˈbaʊndz/ (BrE)
within the boundaries of a playing area and therefore still in play according to the rules of the game

Examples

Inbounds ball
The basketball landed inbounds just before the buzzer sounded.
Players must keep both feet inbounds when catching the ball near the sideline.
The referee confirmed that the pass was completed inbounds during the crucial play.
