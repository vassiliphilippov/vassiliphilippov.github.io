chamber

noun
judge's private room for business
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪmbɚ /; / ˈtʃeɪmbər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
a private or official room, such as a bedroom or a judge's office

Examples

A judge's chamber
Attorneys often meet with the judge in chamber to discuss pretrial matters.
The judge retired to his chamber to deliberate on the case.
The order was signed privately in the judge's chamber.
He retired to his chamber early in the evening.
In old houses, servants would bring breakfast directly to the master’s chamber.
The queen’s chamber was lavishly decorated with tapestries and gold.
