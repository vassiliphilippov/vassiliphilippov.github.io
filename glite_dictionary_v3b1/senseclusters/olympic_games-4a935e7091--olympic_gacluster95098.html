Olympic Games

noun
ancient Greek athletic festival in Olympia
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪmpɪk ˈɡeɪmz / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpɪk ˈɡeɪmz / (BrE)
the ancient Panhellenic festival of athletic and sporting competitions held at Olympia every four years in honor of Zeus, beginning in 776 BC

Examples

Ancient Olympic Games
Historians believe that athletes from all over Greece traveled to compete in the Olympic Games.
The ancient Olympic Games were held to honor the Greek god Zeus every four years.
Women were not allowed to participate in the original Olympic Games of ancient Greece.
