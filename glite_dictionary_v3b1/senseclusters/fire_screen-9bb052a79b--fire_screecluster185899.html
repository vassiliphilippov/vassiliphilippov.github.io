fire screen

noun
protective screen before a fire
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪɚ skriːn / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə skriːn / (BrE)
A protective screen, often of metal, placed in front of an open fire to prevent sparks or embers from escaping into the room.

Examples

A fire screen protector
Always use a fire screen when burning wood in an open hearth.
He placed a fire screen in front of the fireplace to protect the carpet from sparks.
The ornate fire screen matched the Victorian decor of the living room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fireside - the area next to a fireplace
