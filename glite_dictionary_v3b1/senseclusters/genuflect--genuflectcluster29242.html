genuflect

verb
bend knee as sign of respect
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnjʊˌflɛkt /; / ˈdʒɛn.juˌflɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnjʊˌflɛkt /; / ˈdʒɛn.ju.flekt / (BrE)
to bend one or both knees as a sign of respect or worship, especially in a religious setting

Examples

To genuflect before altar
Before entering the chapel, she would always genuflect in front of the altar.
He genuflected hurriedly, hoping not to draw attention to his unfamiliarity with the ritual.
Some politicians are willing to genuflect to powerful interest groups to gain support.
During the ceremony, the congregation was asked to genuflect before the altar.
He paused to genuflect at the entrance of the chapel out of respect.
Many Catholics genuflect upon entering or leaving a pew.
