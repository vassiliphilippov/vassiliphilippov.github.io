economical

adjective
avoiding waste; efficient with resources or time
3.8 3.80/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåiÀêk…ôÀàn…ëÀêm…™k…ôl /; / Àåik…ôÀàn…ëm…™k…ôl / (AmE), / ÀåiÀêk…ôÀàn…ím…™k…ôl / (BrE)
using resources, money, or time carefully and avoiding waste; not spending more than necessary

Examples

Economical use of money
She prefers an economical style of cooking that makes use of every ingredient.
The new car is much more economical than her previous one, saving money on fuel.
Their economical approach to travel allowed them to visit many countries on a tight budget.
Her new car is very economical on fuel and requires little maintenance.
They found it more economical to cook at home rather than eat out every day.
We've adopted several economical measures to reduce waste in our office.

Synonyms

  • frugal
    4.9 2.53/m
    frugal means using little and avoiding luxury in daily life, while economical is a general word for avoiding waste of money, time, or resources.
  • thrifty
    4.4 1.15/m
    thrifty is especially about careful money habits and saving, while economical focuses on avoiding waste in resources or time.
  • economic
    3.7 0.21/m
    economic describes cost-effectiveness or relation to the economy in formal contexts, while economical is used for thriftiness in everyday life.
  • sparing
    3.9 0.05/m
    sparing means using or giving very little to avoid waste, while economical means saving resources but still meeting needs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: efficient - working well without wasting time or resources
  • antonym: extravagant - spending too much money or using too many resources
  • synonym: frugal - careful about spending money; not wasteful
  • synonym: thrifty - using money and resources carefully; not wasteful
  • antonym: wasteful - using resources carelessly or inefficiently
