trumpet

verb
make a trumpet-like sound (e.g., elephant)
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌmpət / (AmE), / ˈtrʌmpɪt / (BrE)
to make a loud, trumpet-like sound or to announce something loudly and confidently

Examples

To trumpet success
She trumpeted her achievements to everyone at the meeting.
The excited elephants trumpeted loudly as they marched through the forest.
The leader trumpeted his plans for the new project with great enthusiasm.
He likes to trumpet his good deeds to everyone he meets.
The company decided to trumpet its latest achievements at the press conference.
Their success was loudly trumpeted in the local newspapers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: announce - to make something known publicly
  • synonym: broadcast - to make widely known; to disseminate information
  • synonym: proclaim - to state something publicly or officially
  • antonym: whisper - to speak or say very softly or secretly
