evapotranspiration

noun
water loss to atmosphere
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvæpoʊˌtrænspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌvapəʊˌtrænspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which water moves from land to the atmosphere through both evaporation from soil and transpiration from plants (including the total amount of water involved)

Examples

Evapotranspiration rate
Farmers track evapotranspiration to determine how much water their crops need.
Climate scientists use evapotranspiration data to study changes in regional water cycles.
High temperatures can increase the rate of evapotranspiration in dry areas.
Scientists measure evapotranspiration to understand how much water is lost from soil and plants.
High temperatures can increase evapotranspiration rates, affecting local water supplies.
Accurate evapotranspiration data helps farmers decide when and how much to irrigate their crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evaporation - the process of liquid water turning into vapor
  • synonym: transpiration - the process by which plants release water vapor
