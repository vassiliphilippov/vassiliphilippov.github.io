breastwork

noun
temporary defensive wall or fortification
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛstˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈbrɛstˌwɜːk / (BrE)
a temporary defensive wall, usually chest-high, used as a fortification in military operations

Examples

A breastwork of earth
A rough breastwork of earth and wood protected the entrance to the camp.
Behind the breastwork, the defenders withstood wave after wave of attacks.
The soldiers quickly constructed a breastwork to defend against the approaching enemy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony or in fortifications
