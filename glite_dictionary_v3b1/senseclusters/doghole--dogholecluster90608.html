doghole

noun
miserable dwelling
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔgˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈdɒgˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small, dirty, or unpleasant place, especially a poor or miserable dwelling

Examples

A dirty doghole
After losing his job, he was forced to live in a tiny doghole behind the factory.
The old doghole at the end of the street was finally demolished by the city.
She refused to stay another night in that filthy doghole with broken windows and no heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hovel - a small, dirty, or poorly built house
  • antonym: palace - a large, impressive, and luxurious house
