bookmarker

noun
object marking a place in a book
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkˌmɑrkər / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌmɑːkə / (BrE)
an object, such as a strip of paper, ribbon, or card, placed between the pages of a book to mark a reader's place

Examples

A bookmarker in a book
I received a handmade bookmarker as a gift from my grandmother.
She slid the bookmarker between the pages before closing the novel for the night.
The library sells colorful bookmarkers near the checkout desk.
