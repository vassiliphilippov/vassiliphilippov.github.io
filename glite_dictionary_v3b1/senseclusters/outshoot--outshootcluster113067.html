outshoot

noun
secondary development
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtʃuːt / (AmE), / ˈaʊtʃuːt / (BrE)
a secondary development or result that grows out from or is produced by something larger or more important

Examples

An outshoot of a project
The new art festival is an outshoot of the city’s cultural initiative.
Her freelance business became a successful outshoot of her previous job in marketing.
The hiking trail is an outshoot from the main path through the forest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - the principal or dominant course, tendency, or trend
  • synonym: offshoot - something that develops from something larger
