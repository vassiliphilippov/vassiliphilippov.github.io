hemiparesis

noun
one-sided weakness
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmiˌpəˈrisɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmiˈpɑːrəsɪs / (BrE)
weakness or partial paralysis affecting one side of the body, usually caused by neurological injury or disease

Examples

Partial hemiparesis weakness
The patient developed hemiparesis after suffering a stroke on the right side of his brain.
Physical therapy can help people with hemiparesis regain strength and mobility.
Doctors diagnosed her with hemiparesis following a traumatic brain injury.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hemiplegia - complete paralysis of one side of the body
  • degree higher: paralysis - loss of the ability to move part or all of the body
  • synonym: paresis - partial or slight paralysis
