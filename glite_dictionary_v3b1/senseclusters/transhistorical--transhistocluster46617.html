transhistorical

adjective
across all history
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzˌhɪˈstɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌtrænzˌhɪsˈtɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
existing or applying across all periods of history, not limited to a specific era or time.

Examples

A transhistorical idea
The idea of love as a universal value is often described as transhistorical.
Some philosophers argue that certain moral principles are transhistorical and apply to all societies.
The exhibition explores transhistorical themes that connect ancient and modern cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: historical - relating to history or past events
  • synonym: timeless - not affected by the passage of time; eternal
