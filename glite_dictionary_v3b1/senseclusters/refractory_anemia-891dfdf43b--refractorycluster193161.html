refractory anemia

noun
anemia unresponsive to treatment
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktəri əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktəri əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
a group of anemic conditions that do not respond to standard treatments and require blood transfusions for management, not caused by another primary disease

Examples

Refractory anemia diagnosis
A diagnosis of refractory anemia was made after several treatments failed to improve his condition.
Doctors are researching new therapies for refractory anemia, as standard treatments are often ineffective.
The patient's refractory anemia made her dependent on regular blood transfusions for survival.
Doctors decided regular transfusions were necessary because her refractory anaemia did not improve with medication.
Research into new drugs aims to offer hope to those suffering from refractory anaemia.
The patient was diagnosed with refractory anaemia after failing to respond to multiple therapies.
