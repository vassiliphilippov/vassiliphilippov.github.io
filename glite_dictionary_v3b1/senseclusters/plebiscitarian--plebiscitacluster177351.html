plebiscitarian

adjective
supporting plebiscites
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌplɛbɪsɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌplɛbɪsɪˈtɛəriən / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the use of plebiscites to make political decisions

Examples

Plebiscitarian democracy
The new constitution was adopted through a plebiscitarian process involving the entire population.
Some critics argue that plebiscitarian democracy can oversimplify complex political issues.
The leader's plebiscitarian style emphasized direct votes on major national decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to authority rather than individual freedom
  • synonym: referendum - a general vote by the electorate on a single political question
