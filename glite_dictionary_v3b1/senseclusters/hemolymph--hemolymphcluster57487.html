hemolymph

noun
insect circulatory fluid
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːməˌlɪmf / (AmE), / ˈhiːməˌlɪmf / (BrE)
the circulatory fluid in insects and some other invertebrates that serves a similar function to blood

Examples

Insect hemolymph
The hemolymph in insects transports nutrients and hormones throughout their bodies.
Unlike mammals, grasshoppers have hemolymph instead of blood to circulate substances.
Scientists studied the hemolymph to understand how the beetle's immune system works.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blood - the red fluid that circulates in the veins and arteries of vertebrates
