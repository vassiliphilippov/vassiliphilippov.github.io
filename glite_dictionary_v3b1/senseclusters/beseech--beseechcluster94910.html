beseech

verb
ask earnestly; implore
8.2 1.62/m
Pronunciation: / b…™Ààsit É / (AmE), / b…™ÀàsiÀêt É / (BrE)
to ask someone earnestly and fervently for something; to implore or beg

Examples

To beseech for help
I beseech you to listen carefully to what I have to say.
She beseeched her friend not to reveal her secret.
The villagers beseeched the king for help during the drought.

Synonyms

  • implore
    5.3 2.17/m
    implore is to ask someone desperately and with strong emotion, while beseech sounds more formal and literary for urgent requests.
  • entreat
    8.1 0.73/m
    entreat is to ask someone urgently and politely, while beseech is more fervent and often a stronger, more urgent plea.
  • plead
    3.9 13/m
    plead is to ask earnestly and sometimes used in legal or emotional appeals, while beseech feels more solemn and urgent.
  • beg
    1.0 10/m
    beg is to ask in a direct and urgent way, often with need or shame, while beseech is more formal and fervent.
  • supplicate
    9.6 0.14/m
    supplicate is to ask humbly, often in prayer or to those in power, while beseech is a more general urgent pleading.
  • pray
    1.6 8.35/m
    pray is to ask in a religious or hopeful way, often to a deity, while beseech is a strong personal plea to someone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beg - to ask someone earnestly or humbly for something
  • antonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully
  • synonym: implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
