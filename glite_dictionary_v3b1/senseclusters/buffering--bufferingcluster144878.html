buffering

noun
resistance to pH change
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbʌfərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or ability of a solution or system to resist changes in acidity or alkalinity (pH)

Examples

Buffering capacity
The buffering capacity of blood helps maintain a stable internal environment.
Scientists measured the buffering effect of the solution to ensure accurate experimental results.
Proper buffering is essential in many chemical and biological processes to prevent harmful pH changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acidification - the process of becoming more acidic
  • synonym: neutralization - the process of making a solution neither acidic nor basic
