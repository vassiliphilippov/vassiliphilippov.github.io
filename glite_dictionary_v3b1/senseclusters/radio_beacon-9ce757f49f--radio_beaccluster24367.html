radio beacon

noun
navigation radio signal transmitter
7.1 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.di.oʊ ˈbiː.kən/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.di.əʊ ˈbiː.kən/ (BrE)
a radio transmitter that sends out a signal to help ships or aircraft find their way

Examples

A radio beacon signal
A radio beacon was set up on the island to guide approaching ships safely to shore.
Pilots rely on the signals from a radio beacon during poor visibility conditions.
The aircraft followed the radio beacon to locate the airport during the storm.
All approaching aircraft use the radio beacon to guide them safely to the runway.
During the storm, the pilot was relieved to pick up the radio beacon signal.
The ship altered its course after detecting a radio beacon from the coastline.
