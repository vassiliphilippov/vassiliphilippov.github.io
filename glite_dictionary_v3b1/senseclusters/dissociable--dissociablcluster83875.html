dissociable

adjective
able to be separated or divided
8.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsoʊʃiəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊsiəbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of being divided, separated, or regarded as distinct from something else

Examples

Dissociable factors
In some research, genetic and environmental influences are considered dissociable factors.
Legal and moral responsibility are sometimes dissociable, depending on the context.
The two theories are not easily dissociable from one another in practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indissociable - not able to be separated
  • synonym: separable - able to be separated
