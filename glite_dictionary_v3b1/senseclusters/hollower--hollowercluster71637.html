hollower

adjective
more empty inside
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑloʊər / (AmE), / ˈhɒləʊə / (BrE)
having more empty space inside or being less solid or substantial than something else.

Examples

Hollower cave walls
After years of erosion, the cave became even hollower than before.
The old tree trunk looked hollower after the woodpeckers made more holes.
His voice sounded hollower in the empty hall than in the crowded room.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hollow - having an empty space inside
  • antonym: solid - firm and without empty space inside
