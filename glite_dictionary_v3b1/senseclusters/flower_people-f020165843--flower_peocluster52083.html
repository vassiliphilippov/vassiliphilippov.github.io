flower people

noun
1960s youth subculture advocating peace and love
7.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈflaʊ.ər ˈpiː.pəl/ /; / /ˈfloʊ.ər ˈpiː.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈflaʊ.ə ˈpiː.pəl/ /; / /ˈfləʊ.ə ˈpiː.pəl/ (BrE)
Members of a youth subculture in the 1960s, especially associated with San Francisco, who promoted peace, love, communal living, and often embraced psychedelic music and alternative lifestyles.

Examples

Flower people of the 1960s
Her parents were flower people who believed in spreading love and rejecting materialism.
Many flower people gathered in San Francisco during the Summer of Love to celebrate peace and music.
The flower people embraced psychedelic art, free expression, and communal living as part of their lifestyle.
