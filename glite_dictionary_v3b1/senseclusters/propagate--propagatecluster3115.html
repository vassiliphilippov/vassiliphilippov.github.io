propagate

verb
to spread or become widespread
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.pəˌɡeɪt /; / ˈprɑpəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒp.əˌɡeɪt /; / ˈprɒpəɡeɪt /; / ˈprɒp.ə.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to spread ideas, beliefs, information, or feelings to many people or places

Examples

To propagate an idea
Excitement about the event propagated across social media within hours.
Rumors tend to propagate quickly in small communities.
The scientist watched as the chemical reaction began to propagate through the solution.
Rumors can propagate quickly through social media platforms these days.
She helped propagate the idea that everyone should have access to clean water.
The organization works hard to propagate its message about environmental protection worldwide.
False rumors can propagate rapidly if not corrected early.
He worked tirelessly to propagate his philosophy among young students.
New scientific theories often propagate quickly through the internet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circulate - to make information pass from person to person
  • synonym: disseminate - to spread information widely
  • synonym: spread - to extend over a large or increasing area, or to become distributed
  • antonym: suppress - to prevent information from becoming known
