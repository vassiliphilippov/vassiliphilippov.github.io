Ch'in

proper noun
Chinese dynasty, 246–206 BC
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʃɪn / (AmE), / tʃɪn / (BrE)
the Chinese dynasty (246 BC–206 BC) that established the first centralized imperial government and constructed much of the Great Wall

Examples

The Ch'in dynasty
Many ancient artifacts from the Ch'in period have been discovered by archaeologists.
The Ch'in dynasty was known for its achievements in unifying China and building the first Great Wall.
Under the rule of the Ch'in emperor, strict legalist policies were implemented throughout the empire.

Related Terms
