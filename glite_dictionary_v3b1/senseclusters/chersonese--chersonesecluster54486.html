chersonese

noun
ancient term for peninsula
Pronunciation: / ˌkɜrsəˈniːz / (AmE), / ˌkɜːsəˈniːz / (BrE)
an old term for a peninsula, especially as used in ancient geography

Examples

Ancient chersonese
The ancient Greeks settled on the Thracian chersonese.
Many explorers wrote about the Golden chersonese in their travel journals.
In old maps, the word chersonese often appears to describe large peninsulas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peninsula - a piece of land almost surrounded by water or projecting out into a body of water
