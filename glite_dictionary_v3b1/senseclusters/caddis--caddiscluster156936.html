caddis

noun
fishing fly imitation
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkædɪs / (AmE), / ˈkædɪs / (BrE)
an artificial fishing fly made to look like a caddisfly, used by anglers to catch freshwater fish

Examples

A caddis fly
The angler selected a caddis to match the insects hatching on the river.
He tied a new caddis before heading out for an evening of fly fishing.
Trout often rise eagerly to a well-presented caddis during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fly - an artificial lure used in fly fishing
  • antonym: nymph - an artificial fly imitating the immature stage of an insect
