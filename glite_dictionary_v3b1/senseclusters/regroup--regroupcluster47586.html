regroup

verb
organize again after a setback
2.6
Pronunciation: / riˈɡruːp / (AmE), / riˈɡruːp / (BrE)
to organize or arrange people or things into new groups, especially after a difficulty, defeat, or separation

Examples

To regroup the team
After the unexpected loss, the team had to regroup and come up with a new strategy.
Once the hikers became separated, they stopped to regroup at a nearby clearing.
The company will regroup its staff following the recent restructuring.
After the failed attempt, the team needed to regroup before trying again.
The birds scattered at first, but eventually they began to regroup nearby.
The company plans to regroup its staff into more efficient working teams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disperse - to break up and move in different directions
  • synonym: reorganize - to arrange something again or differently
  • antonym: scatter - to separate or disperse a group
