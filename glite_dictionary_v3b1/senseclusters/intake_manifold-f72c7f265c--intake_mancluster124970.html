intake manifold

noun
engine pipe for distributing air or fuel mixture
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌteɪk ˈmænɪˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈɪnteɪk ˈmænɪfəʊld / (BrE)
a pipe or chamber in an internal combustion engine that distributes the air or air-fuel mixture to the cylinders

Examples

The intake manifold pipe
A clogged intake manifold can reduce engine performance and fuel efficiency.
Replacing the intake manifold is a complex but sometimes necessary repair in older vehicles.
The mechanic inspected the intake manifold for any signs of leaks or cracks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exhaust manifold - a pipe or chamber that collects exhaust gases from the engine's cylinders
