oil lamp

noun
lamp burning oil for light
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl læmp / (AmE), / ɔɪl læmp / (BrE)
a lamp that burns oil as fuel to produce light, especially before the widespread use of electric lighting

Examples

An oil lamp burns
During the power outage, we used an old oil lamp to light the room.
In many rural areas, oil lamps are still commonly used after dark.
She placed the oil lamp on the table to read after sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kerosene lamp - a lamp specifically burning kerosene oil for light
