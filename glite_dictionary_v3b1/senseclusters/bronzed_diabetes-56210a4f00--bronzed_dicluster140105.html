bronzed diabetes

noun
iron overload disorder causing bronzed skin and diabetes
9.6 8.1
Pronunciation: /brɑːnzd daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz/ /; / /brɑːnzd daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪz/ (AmE), /brɒnzd daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiːz/ /; / /brɒnzd daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs/ (BrE)
A rare condition in which excessive iron accumulation in the body results in bronzed skin, diabetes mellitus, liver enlargement, and organ abnormalities; also known as hemochromatosis.

Examples

Bronzed diabetes symptoms
A classic symptom of bronzed diabetes is a distinctive bronze discoloration of the skin.
Early detection and treatment of bronzed diabetes are essential to prevent damage to vital organs.
The patient's bronzed diabetes was diagnosed after tests revealed high iron levels and liver enlargement.
