oscillograph

noun
instrument for recording electrical waveforms
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ɒˈsɪl.ə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument used to record and display the waveforms of fluctuating electrical voltages or currents

Examples

A oscillograph device
A high-speed oscillograph can accurately capture rapid voltage fluctuations in a circuit.
Early researchers relied on the oscillograph to document electrical surges in power lines.
The engineer connected the oscillograph to observe the signal changes during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oscilloscope - device for viewing oscillations of electrical voltage or current, often visually
