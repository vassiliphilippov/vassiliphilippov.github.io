campaign

verb
run for office or position
5.3
Pronunciation: / kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / kamˈpeɪn /; / kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
to work or compete in an organized way to achieve a goal, especially in politics or social causes

Examples

To campaign for change
He campaigned tirelessly across the state, speaking at rallies and meeting voters.
Several candidates are campaigning hard to win the mayoral seat in the upcoming election.
She decided to campaign for student council president this semester.
Activists continue to campaign tirelessly for climate change action.
She decided to campaign for better access to education in her community.
The senator will campaign across the country ahead of the elections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - to publicly support or recommend a cause or policy
  • antonym: oppose - to act against or work in opposition to something
  • synonym: protest - to express strong objection or disapproval
  • synonym: run - to stand as a candidate in an election
  • synonym: stand - to present oneself as a candidate for election, especially in British English
