hurtle

verb
move or travel with great speed
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɝː.təl /; / ˈhɜːrtəl / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.təl /; / ˈhɜːtəl / (BrE)
to move or travel very fast and forcefully, often with a rushing or violent motion

Examples

To hurtle through space
A meteor hurtled through the sky, leaving a glowing trail behind it.
He hurtled around the corner on his bicycle, narrowly missing the mailbox.
The train hurtled down the track, making everyone in the station take notice.
A meteor seemed to hurtle through the night sky, leaving a bright trail.
She watched the skier hurtle down the mountain at breakneck speed.
The train continued to hurtle down the tracks despite the heavy rain.
