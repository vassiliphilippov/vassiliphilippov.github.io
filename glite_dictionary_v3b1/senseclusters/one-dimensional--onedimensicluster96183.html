Examples

One-dimensional character
Critics called the villain one-dimensional, arguing he lacked real motivation or complexity.
Her analysis was too one-dimensional, ignoring the economic and social factors involved.
The scientist described the model as one-dimensional because it only accounted for length.
Her performance avoided being one-dimensional by showing both strength and vulnerability.

Synonyms

  • unidimensional
    8.4 0.40/m

  • two-dimensional
    5.5 0.32/m
    two-dimensional is a common phrase for flat or shallow characters, while one-dimensional describes things that lack depth or variety.
  • depthless
    7.3 0.08/m
    depthless is rare and often literal about physical lack of depth, while one-dimensional commonly means simple or superficial.
