one-dimensional

adjective
lacking depth or existing in one dimension
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌn dəˈmɛnʃənl̩ /; / ˌwʌn.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / ˌwʌn dɪˈmɛnʃənl̩ /; / ˌwʌn.dɪˈmen.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
lacking depth, complexity, or variety; simple or superficial (used about people, ideas, or things)

Examples

One-dimensional character
Critics called the villain one-dimensional, arguing he lacked real motivation or complexity.
Her analysis was too one-dimensional, ignoring the economic and social factors involved.
The scientist described the model as one-dimensional because it only accounted for length.
Her performance avoided being one-dimensional by showing both strength and vulnerability.
Some critics found the plot a bit one-dimensional compared to the novel's complexity.
The main villain was disappointingly one-dimensional and lacked any real motivation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complex - having many parts or aspects; not simple
  • synonym: superficial - not thorough, deep, or complete; lacking depth
  • degree higher: two-dimensional - existing or considered in two dimensions; having width and height but no depth
