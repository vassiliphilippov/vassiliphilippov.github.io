crowner

noun
archaic term for coroner
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkraʊnər / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnə / (BrE)
an old term for a coroner, an official responsible for investigating deaths, especially those happening under unusual circumstances

Examples

Medieval crowner inquiry
In medieval England, the crowner would determine the cause of unexplained deaths.
The villagers waited anxiously for the crowner to arrive and begin his investigation.
Shakespeare's plays sometimes mention the crowner in scenes involving mysterious deaths.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coroner - an official who investigates deaths, especially those happening suddenly or under suspicious circumstances
