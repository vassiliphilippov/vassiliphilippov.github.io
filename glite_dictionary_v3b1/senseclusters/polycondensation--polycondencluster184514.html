polycondensation

noun
polymer-forming reaction
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˌkɑndənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌkɒndənˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a chemical process in which polymers are formed by joining monomers together with the elimination of small molecules such as water

Examples

Polycondensation reaction
Nylon is produced through a polycondensation reaction involving the elimination of water.
The polycondensation process is essential in manufacturing many synthetic fibers and plastics.
During polycondensation, small molecules like water or methanol are released as byproducts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: addition polymerization - a type of polymerization where monomers add together without the loss of small molecules
  • synonym: polymerization - a process of reacting monomer molecules together to form polymer chains
