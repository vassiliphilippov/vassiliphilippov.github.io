monoid

noun
algebraic structure
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɔɪd / (BrE)
an algebraic structure consisting of a set equipped with an associative operation and an identity element

Examples

A monoid structure
A monoid is a fundamental concept in abstract algebra and computer science.
The set of natural numbers with addition forms a monoid because it has an identity element, zero.
When studying algebraic structures, students often learn about the properties of a monoid.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: group - an algebraic structure with an associative operation, identity, and inverses
  • degree lower: semigroup - an algebraic structure with an associative operation but no identity element
