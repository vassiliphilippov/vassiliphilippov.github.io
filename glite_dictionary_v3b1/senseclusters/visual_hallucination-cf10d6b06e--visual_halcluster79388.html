visual hallucination

noun
AI-generated fabricated image
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪ.ʒuː.əl hə.luː.sən.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈvɪ.ʒ.wəl hə.luː.sən.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈvɪ.ʒuː.əl hə.luː.sɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈvɪ.ʒuː.əl hə.luː.sɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈvɪ.ʒ.əl hə.luː.sɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in computing and AI) an image or visual output produced by a model that appears plausible but is fabricated, incorrect, or not grounded in real data or evidence.

Examples

AI visual hallucination
The art-generation model produced a visual hallucination that looked convincing but wasn't grounded in any training data.
Researchers measure how often a vision system creates visual hallucinations when asked to imagine unfamiliar scenes.
Users complained the new filter kept showing visual hallucinations of objects that never existed in the photo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fabrication - something made up or invented rather than based on fact
  • synonym: hallucination - a false or fabricated perception; in AI, an output not grounded in reality
