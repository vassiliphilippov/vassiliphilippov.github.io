ventilation shaft

noun
air passage for ventilation in buildings

a vertical or horizontal passage in a building or structure designed to allow fresh air to circulate and to remove stale air for ventilation purposes

Examples

A ventilation shaft opening
Smoke filled the ventilation shaft, making it dangerous for the firefighters to enter the building.
The new skyscraper was designed with a central ventilation shaft to improve air quality on every floor.
Workers discovered a bird's nest in the ventilation shaft during routine maintenance.

Synonyms

  • air shaft
    4.7 0.16/m
    an air shaft is usually vertical and found in mines or buildings, while a ventilation shaft can be vertical or horizontal for general airflow.
  • air duct
    2.8 0.48/m
    an air duct is a pipe-like passage that moves air in buildings or vehicles, while a ventilation shaft is usually a larger, often vertical passage.
  • vent
    2.7 7.48/m
    a vent is a small opening that lets air or gas escape, while a ventilation shaft is a larger built passage for circulating fresh air.
