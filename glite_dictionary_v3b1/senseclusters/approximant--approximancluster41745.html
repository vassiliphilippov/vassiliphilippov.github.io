approximant

noun
speech sound type
8.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈprɑːk.sɪ.mənt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈprɒk.sɪ.mənt/ (BrE)
a speech sound made by narrowing the vocal tract without creating friction, such as the English 'w', 'l', or 'r'

Examples

The approximant 'l'
The English 'l' is an example of an approximant in many dialects.
Linguists study how approximants differ from other types of consonants.
In some languages, the sound 'j' is classified as an approximant.
The English 'r' in 'red' is classified as an approximant in phonetics.
Linguists study how children learn to pronounce approximants like 'l' and 'w'.
In some languages, the sound represented by 'y' is an approximant rather than a vowel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consonant - a speech sound made by partially or completely blocking airflow
  • antonym: fricative - a speech sound produced by forcing air through a narrow channel causing friction
  • antonym: stop - a speech sound produced by completely blocking and then releasing airflow
  • degree lower: vowel - a speech sound produced without significant constriction of the vocal tract
