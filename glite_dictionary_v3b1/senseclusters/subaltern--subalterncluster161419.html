subaltern

noun
junior British army officer below captain
6.5
Pronunciation: / səˈbɔl.tɚn / (AmE), / sʌbˈɑːl.tən / (BrE)
a British army officer ranking below captain, especially in the infantry and cavalry

Examples

British Army subaltern
He served as a subaltern in the British Army during the First World War.
Many famous generals began their careers as humble subalterns in the military.
The young subaltern followed his captain's orders with precision and discipline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lieutenant - an army officer immediately junior to a captain
