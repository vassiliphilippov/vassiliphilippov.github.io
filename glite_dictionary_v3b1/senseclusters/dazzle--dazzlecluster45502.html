dazzle

noun
intense brightness that temporarily blinds
Pronunciation: / ˈdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈdæzəl / (BrE)
a brightness so intense that it partially and temporarily blinds or confuses the eyes

Examples

A dazzle of sunlight
After stepping into the sunlight from the theater, the dazzle made me squint.
The dazzle of her diamond necklace caught everyone's eye at the party.
The snow created a white dazzle that was almost blinding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dimness - lack of brightness or light
  • synonym: glare - a strong, harsh light
