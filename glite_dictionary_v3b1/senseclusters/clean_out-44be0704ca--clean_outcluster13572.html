clean out

verb
deprive completely of money or goods
5.1 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / klin a ät / (AmE), / kliÀên a ät / (BrE)
to deprive someone completely of money or possessions, often by theft, spending, or loss

Examples

To clean out his wallet

The new tax bill nearly cleans out my savings every year.
