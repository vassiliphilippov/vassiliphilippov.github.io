flat

noun
deflated tire
Pronunciation: / flæt / (AmE), / flæt / (BrE)
A tire that has lost all or most of its air, making it deflated and unusable until repaired.

Examples

A car flat
After running over a nail, her car had a flat and needed a tow.
He can change a flat in under ten minutes.
We had to pull over because we got a flat on the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: puncture - a small hole in a tire causing air to escape
