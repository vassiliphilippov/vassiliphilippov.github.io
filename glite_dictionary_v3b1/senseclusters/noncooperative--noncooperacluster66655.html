noncooperative

adjective
not working together
6.7
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.koʊ.ə.ˈpɜːr.ə.tɪv/ (AmE), /nɒn.kəʊ.ə.ˈpɜː.rə.tɪv/ (BrE)
describing a situation or model in which parties act independently without making binding agreements or working together

Examples

Noncooperative game theory
In a noncooperative game, each player tries to maximize their own benefit without making agreements.
The negotiations failed because both sides remained noncooperative throughout the process.
Economic models often analyze noncooperative behavior to predict competitive outcomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cooperative - willing to work together with others
  • synonym: uncooperative - not willing to work with others
