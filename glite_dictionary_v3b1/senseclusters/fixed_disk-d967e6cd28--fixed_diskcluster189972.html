fixed disk

noun
permanently installed rigid magnetic disk for computers
Pronunciation: / fɪkst dɪsk / (AmE), / fɪkst dɪsk / (BrE)
A rigid magnetic disk that is permanently installed in a drive unit, used to store computer data; typically refers to a hard disk drive.

Examples

A fixed disk drive
Due to its higher capacity, a fixed disk is preferred for applications requiring large amounts of storage.
Older computers often had a fixed disk that could not be removed or replaced by the user.
The computer uses a fixed disk for storing its operating system and files.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hard disk - a device for storing computer data on a magnetic disk
  • antonym: removable disk - a disk that can be inserted and removed from a computer drive
