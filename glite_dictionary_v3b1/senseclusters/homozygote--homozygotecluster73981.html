homozygote

noun
organism with two identical alleles
2.1 7.2
Pronunciation: /hoʊ.moʊ.ˈzaɪ.goʊ/ /; / /ˈhoʊ.moʊ.zaɪ.goʊ/ (AmE), /həʊ.məʊ.ˈzaɪ.gəʊ/ /; / /ˈhəʊ.məʊ.zaɪ.gəʊ/ (BrE)
(genetics) an organism that has two identical alleles for a particular gene, resulting in uniform expression of a characteristic.

Examples

A homozygote organism
A homozygote will consistently pass down the same genetic trait to its offspring.
Scientists identified the child as a homozygote for the recessive gene.
The frequency of the homozygote in the population was lower than expected.
