Acer palmatum

noun
a small ornamental tree from Japan/Korea
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪsər pælˈmeɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə pælˈmɑːtəm / (BrE)
A species of small ornamental tree native to Japan and Korea, known for its deeply incised leaves and widely cultivated varieties.

Examples

Acer palmatum tree
A mature Acer palmatum can be the focal point of a traditional Japanese garden.
Many gardeners prize the Acer palmatum for its delicate, lacy foliage.
The leaves of the Acer palmatum turn brilliant shades of red and orange in autumn.
