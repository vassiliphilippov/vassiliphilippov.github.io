spy

verb
to gather information secretly
6.5 4.4
Pronunciation: / spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
to secretly watch or collect information about someone or something

Examples

To spy on rivals
Agents were sent to spy on the enemy's military activities.
He was arrested after trying to spy for a foreign government.
She secretly spied on her competitors to learn their business strategies.
He tried to spy on his neighbors through the window but was quickly noticed.
She was caught spying on her sister to find out what birthday present she was getting.
The journalist managed to spy on the meeting and report the secret discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: betray - to be disloyal or reveal secrets inadvertently or deliberately
  • synonym: espionage - the practice of spying or using spies to obtain secret information
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to; disregard
  • synonym: observe - to watch carefully, especially with attention to details
