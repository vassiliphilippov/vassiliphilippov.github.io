electrocardiogram

noun
recording of the heart's electrical activity
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈkɑrdiəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈkɑːdiəɡræm / (BrE)
A record or display of a person's heartbeat produced by an electrocardiograph, used to check for heart abnormalities.

Examples

An electrocardiogram test
He had an electrocardiogram before the surgery to ensure his heart was healthy enough for anesthesia.
The doctor ordered an electrocardiogram to check for any irregularities in her heartbeat.
Your electrocardiogram shows normal cardiac activity and no signs of arrhythmia.
