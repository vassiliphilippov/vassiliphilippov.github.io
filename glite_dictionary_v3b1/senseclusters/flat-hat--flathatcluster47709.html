flat-hat

verb
to fly an aircraft very close to the ground
8.1 8.7
Pronunciation: /flæt hæt/ (AmE), /flæt hæt/ (BrE)
(of a pilot) to fly an aircraft dangerously close to the ground, usually for show or in violation of regulations.

Examples

To flat-hat over runway
During training, he was reprimanded for flat-hatting over the runway.
It's dangerous and against regulations to flat-hat near populated areas.
The stunt pilot loved to flat-hat at airshows to impress the crowds.
