prosopopoeia

noun
representation of ideas as persons
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːsəpəˈpiːə / (AmE), / ˌprɒsəpəˈpiːə / (BrE)
the representation of an abstract quality or idea as if it were a person or creature, especially in literature or rhetoric

Examples

Prosopopoeia in literature
Classical orators frequently employed prosopopoeia to make arguments more persuasive.
In poetry, prosopopoeia allows nature to speak as if it had a human voice.
The author used prosopopoeia to give justice and liberty human form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: personification - attribution of human qualities to something nonhuman
