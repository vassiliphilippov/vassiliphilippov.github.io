lotic

adjective
relating to moving water habitats
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊtɪk / (AmE), / ˈlɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or living in rapidly moving or flowing water, especially streams and rivers.

Examples

A lotic stream
Certain insect species are adapted to lotic environments, such as fast-flowing rivers.
Fish in lotic habitats often have streamlined bodies to cope with strong currents.
The study compared the biodiversity of lotic and lentic freshwater systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lentic - relating to still water habitats, such as lakes or ponds
