inkatha

noun
Zulu unity symbol
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɑːtə / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a ring or coil of grass or woven material used in Zulu culture as an ornament or symbol of unity

Examples

Ceremonial inkatha ring
The chief placed the inkatha at the center of the gathering to represent the community's unity.
During the ceremony, each family contributed grass to weave the inkatha together.
The inkatha is an important cultural object in many traditional Zulu rituals.
