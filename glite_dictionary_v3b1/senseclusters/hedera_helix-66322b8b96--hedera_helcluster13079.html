Hedera helix

noun
a species of evergreen climbing vine (English ivy)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdərə ˈhiːlɪks / (AmE), / ˈhɛdərə ˈhiːlɪks / (BrE)
A species of evergreen climbing or ground-creeping vine, commonly known as English ivy, with lobed leaves and black, berrylike fruits.

Examples

Hedera helix plant
Hedera helix is commonly planted as ground cover in gardens and parks.
Many old buildings are covered by the climbing stems of Hedera helix.
The glossy, dark green leaves of Hedera helix remained vibrant throughout the winter.
