partially

adverb
not completely; to some extent
2.4 20/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêr É…ôli / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀê É…ôli / (BrE)
To some extent; not completely or wholly.

Examples

Is partially damaged
Her vision is partially impaired in her left eye.
The bridge was partially closed after the storm for safety reasons.
The company only partially accepted responsibility for the mistake.

Synonyms

  • partly
    1.0 32/m
    partly means not completely and is common in everyday speech, while partially sounds slightly more formal and appears in writing.
  • in part
    2.2 34/m
    in part means to some extent and is slightly formal in writing, while partially is more neutral and used in everyday speech.
  • somewhat
    2.0 67/m
    somewhat means a small or moderate degree and often sounds softer, while partially means not completely and is more direct.

Related Terms

  • antonym: completely - in every way; totally
  • synonym: partly - to a limited degree or in part, not fully
