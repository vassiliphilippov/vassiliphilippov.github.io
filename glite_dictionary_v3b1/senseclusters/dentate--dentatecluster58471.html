dentate

adjective
having toothlike projections at the margin
9.0 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈden.teɪt/ (AmE), /ˈden.teɪt/ (BrE)
having toothlike or serrated projections along the edge, especially used in biology and botany to describe leaves or anatomical structures.

Examples

Dentate leaf edges
Many mollusk shells are dentate, with ridges resembling teeth on the edge.
The dentate border of the plant's petals distinguishes it from other species.
The leaf is dentate along its outer margin, giving it a spiky appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: entire - having a smooth, unbroken margin
  • synonym: serrated - having a saw-toothed edge
