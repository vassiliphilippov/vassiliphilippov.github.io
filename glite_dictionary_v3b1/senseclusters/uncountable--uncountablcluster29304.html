uncountable

adjective
too large to count
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkaʊntəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkaʊntəbl / (BrE)
in mathematics, too large to be counted; having a cardinality greater than that of the set of natural numbers

Examples

Uncountable set
The set of real numbers is uncountable and cannot be listed in a sequence.
Mathematicians proved that some infinities are uncountable, making them larger than others.
Unlike the set of integers, the set of points on a line is uncountable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: countable - able to be counted; having the same cardinality as the natural numbers
  • synonym: infinite - without limits or end; not finite
