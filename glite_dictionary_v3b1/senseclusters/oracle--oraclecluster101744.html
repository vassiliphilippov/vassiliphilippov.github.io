oracle

noun
person who claims to foretell the future
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.ə.kəl / (AmE), / ˈɒr.ə.kəl / (BrE)
a person with great knowledge or authority who is believed to be able to predict the future or give wise advice, especially in ancient times

Examples

A wise oracle
In ancient Greece, people traveled great distances to consult the oracle at Delphi.
She was seen by her followers as a modern oracle because of her uncanny predictions.
The king relied on the oracle for guidance before making important decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the future
  • synonym: seer - someone who claims to see into the future
  • synonym: soothsayer - a person who predicts the future by magical or intuitive means
