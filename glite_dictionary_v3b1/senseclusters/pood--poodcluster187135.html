pood

noun
old Russian unit of weight (about 36 pounds)
Pronunciation: / pud / (AmE), / puːd / (BrE)
a Russian unit of weight equal to approximately 36 pounds (16.38 kilograms), formerly used in Russia and other countries of the former Russian Empire

Examples

One pood weight
Historical records show taxes were paid in wheat, often quantified by the pood.
She lifted a kettlebell that weighed one pood as part of her workout.
The weight of the sack was measured in poods according to old Russian standards.
