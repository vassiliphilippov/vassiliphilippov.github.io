atypical pneumonia

noun
pneumonia with unusual cause or symptoms
5.3 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / e…™Ààt…™p…™k…ôl nuÀàmo äni…ô / (AmE), / e…™Ààt…™p…™k…ôl njuÀêÀàm…ô äni…ô / (BrE)
A type of pneumonia characterized by high fever and cough, usually caused by organisms such as mycoplasma, and primarily affecting children and young adults.

Examples

Atypical pneumonia in children
Children and young adults are more likely to develop atypical pneumonia than older adults.
The doctor suspected atypical pneumonia after noting the unusual presentation and persistent cough.
Treatment for atypical pneumonia may differ from that of traditional bacterial pneumonia.

Synonyms

  • primary atypical pneumonia
    5.3 0.00/m
    primary atypical pneumonia is the term for the common form linked to mycoplasma in young people, while atypical pneumonia covers related causes.
  • mycoplasmal pneumonia
    4.9 0.00/m
    mycoplasmal pneumonia is an illness caused specifically by mycoplasma bacteria, while atypical pneumonia may be caused by various organisms.

  • synonym: pneumonia - inflammation of lung tissue, commonly due to infection
