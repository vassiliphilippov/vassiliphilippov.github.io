Monothelite

adjective
one-will doctrine
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːθəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / məˈnɒθəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
relating to the doctrine that Christ has only one will, or to its supporters in Christian theology

Examples

Monothelite doctrine
The Monothelite position was declared heretical by the Third Council of Constantinople.
Some early church leaders were accused of holding Monothelite beliefs during the seventh century.
The Monothelite controversy influenced many theological debates in Byzantine history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Dyothelite - relating to the doctrine that Christ has two wills
