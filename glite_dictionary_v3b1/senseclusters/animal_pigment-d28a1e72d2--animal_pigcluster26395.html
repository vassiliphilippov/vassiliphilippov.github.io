animal pigment

noun
pigment occurring in animals
9.4 3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪml ˈpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈænɪml ˈpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
a pigment that is produced by and occurs naturally in animals, responsible for coloration in tissues or skin

Examples

Animal pigment color
Melanin is a common animal pigment that determines the color of skin and hair in many mammals.
Scientists studied the structure and function of the animal pigment responsible for the octopus’s camouflage.
The brilliant colors of butterfly wings are caused by various animal pigments.
