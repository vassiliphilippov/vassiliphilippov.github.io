strike a blow

verb
take significant action (esp. damaging or opposing)
8.5 7.4
Pronunciation: / straɪk ə bloʊ / (AmE), / straɪk ə bləʊ / (BrE)
to have a significant effect, especially in opposition to something; to do something that damages or challenges an idea, system, or group

Examples

To strike a blow at power
Her resignation struck a blow at the leadership, leading to several changes in policy.
The new regulations will strike a blow against illegal dumping in the city.
The scandal struck a blow to the company's reputation that it struggled to recover from.
