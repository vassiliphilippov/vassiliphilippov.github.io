snipe

verb
to grab quickly
7.7
Pronunciation: /snaɪp/ (AmE), /snaɪp/ (BrE)
to take or obtain something quickly or in a sneaky or opportunistic way, often before others can act

Examples

To snipe a deal
He managed to snipe the last slice of pizza before anyone else noticed.
During the online auction, she sniped the rare book in the final seconds.
If you want that deal, you have to snipe it as soon as it appears.
