fragmentarily

adverb
incompletely; in parts
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌfræɡˈmɛntərɪli / (AmE), / ˌfræɡˈmɛntərɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is incomplete or made up of disconnected parts

Examples

Told fragmentarily
The story was told fragmentarily, leaving many details unclear to the audience.
Researchers have only fragmentarily reconstructed the events from the available evidence.
The ruins of the ancient city are fragmentarily preserved across the landscape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: completely - in a way that is whole or finished
  • synonym: partially - to some extent but not completely
