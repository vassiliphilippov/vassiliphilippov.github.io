plunge

verb
begin something suddenly or energetically
4.2 4.69/m
Pronunciation: / plÊŒndÊ’ / (AmE), / plÊŒndÊ’ / (BrE)
to enter, move, or immerse suddenly and energetically into something (such as water, an activity, or a situation)

Examples

To plunge into water
After months of planning, they plunged into the project with great enthusiasm.
She decided to plunge into her new job without any hesitation.
Sometimes it's best to plunge into a challenge rather than overthink your approach.
As soon as the doors opened, the shoppers plunged inside.
She plunged through the crowd toward the exit.
The dog plunged into the river to fetch the stick.
He plunged the vegetables into boiling water for a quick blanch.
Her coach plunged her into intensive training ahead of the competition.
The sudden crisis plunged the company into chaos.

Synonyms

  • dive in
    3.7 0.92/m
    dive in is to start something with eager energy and little hesitation, while plunge focuses on sudden, forceful entry.
  • jump
    1.8 14/m
    jump is to enter eagerly and quickly into an activity or chance, while plunge implies a deeper, more forceful entry.
  • immerse
    4.8 6.14/m
    immerse means become fully absorbed in an activity for a time, while plunge stresses sudden, energetic entry into it.
  • submerge
    3.9 4.19/m
    submerge means go completely under water or liquid, while plunge also covers sudden, forceful entry into activities or situations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charge - to rush forward with force
  • synonym: dip - to put something quickly into a liquid and take it out again
  • synonym: dive - to jump or move quickly downward or forward
  • synonym: engage - to participate or become involved fully
  • antonym: hesitate - to pause before beginning something, often due to uncertainty
  • synonym: immerse - to deeply involve or dip completely into
  • synonym: launch - to start or set in motion with force or enthusiasm
