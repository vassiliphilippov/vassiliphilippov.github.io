overdetermined

adjective
too many constraints
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˈtɜːrmɪnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˈtɜːmɪnd / (BrE)
describing a system, especially in mathematics, with more equations or constraints than unknowns, often making an exact solution impossible

Examples

Overdetermined equations
The system of equations was overdetermined, so the researchers used an approximate solution.
In statistics, an overdetermined model may not have a unique answer for the variables.
When the data set is overdetermined, special methods are needed to find the best fit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inconsistent - not able to be satisfied by any solution
  • antonym: underdetermined - having fewer equations or constraints than unknowns
