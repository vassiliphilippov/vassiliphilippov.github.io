rationalizer

noun
one who justifies actions
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃənəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəˌlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person who tries to explain or justify actions or beliefs with logical reasons, even if these are not true or appropriate

Examples

Excuse-making rationalizer
As a skilled rationalizer, he always found a way to defend his questionable decisions.
The rationalizer in the group tried to make every mistake seem reasonable.
Even when caught, she acted as a rationalizer to avoid taking responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apologist - a person who defends or justifies something, often controversial
  • antonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or finds fault
