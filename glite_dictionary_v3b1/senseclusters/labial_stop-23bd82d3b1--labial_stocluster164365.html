labial stop

noun
stop consonant made with the lips
8.0 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.bi.əl stɑp / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.bɪ.əl stɒp / (BrE)
A stop consonant sound produced by blocking airflow with the lips.

Examples

Labial stop sound
In English, the sounds represented by the letters 'p' and 'b' are both labial stops.
Linguists often study how labial stops differ across various languages.
The child found it difficult to pronounce labial stops clearly at first.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plosive - a consonant produced by stopping airflow and then releasing it
