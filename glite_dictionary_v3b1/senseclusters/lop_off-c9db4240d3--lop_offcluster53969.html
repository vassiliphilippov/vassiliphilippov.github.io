lop off

verb
remove by cutting
4.6 0.52/m
Pronunciation: / lÉ‘p É”f / (AmE), / lÉ’p É’f / (BrE)
to remove something from a larger whole by cutting, especially with a single quick motion

Examples

To lop off a branch
He accidentally lopped off the tip of his finger while slicing vegetables.
The company plans to lop off several departments to reduce costs.
The gardener decided to lop off the low-hanging branches from the tree.

Synonyms

  • chop off
    3.4 1.38/m
    chop off removes something with a strong, single blow or cut, while lop off suggests a quick cut often used on branches.
  • cut off
    4.4 1.01/m
    cut off means to detach something by cutting it away in one action, while lop off implies a quick, single cut.
  • cut away
    3.4 0.47/m
    cut away removes material by cutting around or under something, often leaving a shaped edge, whereas lop off is a quick straight cut.
