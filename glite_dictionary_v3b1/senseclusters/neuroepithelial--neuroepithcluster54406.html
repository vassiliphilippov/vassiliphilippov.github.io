neuroepithelial

adjective
of nervous system cells
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˌɛpəˈθiliəl / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˌɛpɪˈθiːliəl / (BrE)
relating to the specialized epithelial cells that form part of the nervous system

Examples

Neuroepithelial cells
During early development, neuroepithelial cells give rise to many types of brain cells.
Researchers studied the neuroepithelial tissue to understand how the spinal cord forms.
Damage to neuroepithelial cells can affect the growth of the nervous system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epithelial - relating to the cells forming the surface of the body or organs
