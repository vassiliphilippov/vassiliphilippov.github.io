moneyball

noun
data-driven strategy
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌniˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌbɔːl / (BrE)
the use of statistical analysis and data-driven strategies to identify undervalued opportunities, especially in business or other fields

Examples

Moneyball approach
The startup used a moneyball approach to find new markets that competitors had overlooked.
Many companies now rely on moneyball techniques to make smarter hiring decisions.
Her success was credited to a moneyball mindset that focused on data rather than tradition.
