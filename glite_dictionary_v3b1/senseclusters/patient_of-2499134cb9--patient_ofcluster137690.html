patient of

adjective
able to endure (something) without complaint
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪʃənt əv / (AmE), / ˈpeɪʃnt əv / (BrE)
able to tolerate or endure (something) without complaint or loss of patience; accepting or bearing (something) patiently.

Examples

To be patient of criticism
Despite many setbacks, she remained patient of criticism and refused to lose her temper.
He proved himself patient of adversity throughout his difficult career.
The teacher was remarkably patient of repeated interruptions from the class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impatient with - unable or unwilling to endure or tolerate (something)
  • synonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow or endure something, especially opinions or behavior that one does not necessarily agree with
