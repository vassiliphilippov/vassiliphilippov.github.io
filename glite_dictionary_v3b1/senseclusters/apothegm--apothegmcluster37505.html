apothegm

noun
short instructive saying
Pronunciation: / ˈæpəˌθɛm / (AmE), / ˈapəθɛm / (BrE)
A short, pithy, and instructive saying or maxim.

Examples

A apothegm saying
His speech was filled with clever apothegms that made the audience think.
She admired how her grandmother always had an apothegm for every situation.
The teacher shared an ancient apothegm to illustrate the lesson.
