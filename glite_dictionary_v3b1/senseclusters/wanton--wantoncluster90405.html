wanton

verb
to engage in amorous play (archaic)
8.3 9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwɑːn.tən/ /; / /ˈwɔːn.tən/ (AmE), /ˈwɒn.tən/ (BrE)
(archaic/literary) to behave in a playful, flirtatious, or extravagant way, often with sensual pleasure or lack of restraint

He wantoned in luxury, spending far beyond his means.

The children wantoned through the fields, lost in carefree play and laughter.
He spent his youth wantoning about the city, untouched by responsibility.
The nobles would wanton in extravagant feasts lasting through the night.
They had money to spare and chose to wanton along the Riviera each summer.
