autopilot

noun
automatic navigational control device
1.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.təʊ.paɪ.lət/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.təʊ.paɪ.lət/ (BrE)
a device that automatically controls the course of an aircraft, ship, or spacecraft without constant human intervention

Examples

Autopilot keeps the plane steady
During the long flight, the captain relied on the autopilot to maintain altitude and direction.
Sailors activated the autopilot so the ship could hold its course overnight.
The new jet is equipped with an advanced autopilot that can handle takeoff and landing.
