slide

noun
smooth movement along a surface
Pronunciation: / slaɪd / (AmE), / slaɪd / (BrE)
the act of moving smoothly along a surface while maintaining continuous contact with it

Examples

A muddy slide
After the rain, the path was so muddy that every step felt like a slide down the hill.
She took a gentle slide down the icy sidewalk after losing her balance.
The baseball player made a swift slide into second base to avoid being tagged out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glide - an act of moving smoothly, continuously, and effortlessly
  • synonym: skid - an act of sliding, typically sideways, on a slippery surface
