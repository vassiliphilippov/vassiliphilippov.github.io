academic freedom

noun
right to free expression in education
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˌækəˈdɛmɪk ˈfriːdəm / (AmE), / ˌækəˈdɛmɪk ˈfriːdəm / (BrE)
the right of teachers and students in educational institutions to express their ideas and pursue knowledge without undue restriction from authority, religious, political, or institutional pressures

Examples

Academic freedom rights
New legislation threatens to undermine academic freedom at public colleges.
The professor defended his academic freedom when discussing controversial topics in class.
Universities must safeguard academic freedom to promote open inquiry and debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: censorship - suppression or prohibition of speech or writing
