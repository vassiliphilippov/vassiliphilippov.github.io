MRD

noun
measurable residual disease (medical)
6.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / É›m É‘r di / (AmE), / É›m É‘Ër diË / (BrE)
(medical, oncology) measurable residual disease; the small number of cancer cells that remain in the body after treatment and that can be detected with sensitive laboratory tests, used to assess relapse risk and guide further therapy.

Examples

MRD test results
Despite remission, clinicians monitor MRD to evaluate whether additional therapy is needed.
The lab's sensitive test detected low levels of MRD not visible on scans.
Patients with detectable MRD after treatment may be offered maintenance therapy to reduce relapse risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minimal residual disease - an alternative term for small amounts of cancer cells remaining after treatment
