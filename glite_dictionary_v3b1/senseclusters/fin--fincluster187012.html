fin

noun
slat in a door or window for air and rain protection
9.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /fɪn/ (AmE), /fɪn/ (BrE)
a flat or projecting part attached to something (like a ship, radiator, or vent) to improve stability, cooling, or airflow

Examples

Cooling fins
Metal fins are commonly used in modern ventilation systems for both function and design.
The old house had wooden fins on the windows to let in air and block the rain.
They repaired several fins on the louvered door after a storm damaged them.
Engineers inspected the damaged fin before continuing the voyage.
Newer cruise liners are equipped with retractable fins for better stability.
The ship's fin helps minimize excessive rolling in rough seas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: louver - a shutter or blind with horizontal slats that allow air and light to pass while keeping out rain
  • synonym: stabilizer - a device used to maintain stability on ships, aircraft, or vehicles
