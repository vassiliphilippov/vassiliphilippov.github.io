outlast

verb
live or endure longer than another
4.4 1.70/m
Pronunciation: / a ätÀàl√¶st / (AmE), / a ätÀàl…ëÀêst / (BrE)
to live or exist longer than someone or something else; to continue to be active or in existence after something else has ended or disappeared

Examples

To outlast competitors
Few people expected the small business to outlast its corporate competitors, but it did.
The athlete managed to outlast all of his rivals in the grueling marathon.
These ancient trees have outlasted hundreds of years of storms and droughts.
