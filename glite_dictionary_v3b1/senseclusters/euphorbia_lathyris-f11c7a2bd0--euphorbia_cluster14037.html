Euphorbia lathyris

noun
a poisonous spurge plant species
9.9
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈfɔːr.bɪ.ə/ (AmE), /juː.ˈfɔː.bɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a species of spurge native to southern Europe, notable for its poisonous properties and seeds that produce a purgative oil; also known as caper spurge or mole plant

Examples

Euphorbia lathyris plant
Due to its toxicity, Euphorbia lathyris should be handled with care and kept away from children.
Gardeners sometimes plant Euphorbia lathyris to deter moles from their lawns.
The seeds of Euphorbia lathyris are known to yield an oil with strong purgative effects.
