glebe

noun
land belonging to a parish church
6.8 9.3
Pronunciation: /gliːb/ (AmE), /gliːb/ (BrE)
a piece of land belonging to a parish church or its clergy, especially in England, formerly used to support the parish priest

Examples

The glebe land
The boundaries of the glebe were marked with ancient stone walls.
The local church sold a portion of its glebe to fund repairs to the roof.
Traditionally, the income from the glebe helped support the village vicar.
