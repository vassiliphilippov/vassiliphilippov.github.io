drive off

verb
force to go away
4.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /draɪv ɔːf/ /; / /draɪv ɑːf/ (AmE), /draɪv ɒf/ /; / /draɪv ɔːf/ (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave, often by using threats, actions, or deterrents; can refer to both people and things in physical and figurative contexts.

Examples

To drive off worries
She tried to drive off her worries by focusing on her work.
The barking dog managed to drive off the intruders before they could break in.
The loud noise was meant to drive off the birds from the crops.
