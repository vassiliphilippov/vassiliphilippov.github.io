(mathematics) a number that, when added to a given number, results in zero; the opposite of that number under addition.

Examples

The additive inverse number
For every real number, there exists an additive inverse that cancels it out in addition.
The additive inverse of 3 is -3, because 3 + (-3) = 0.
Zero is the only number that is its own additive inverse.
