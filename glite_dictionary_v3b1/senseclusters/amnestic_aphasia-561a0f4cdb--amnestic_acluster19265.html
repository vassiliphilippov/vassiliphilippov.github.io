amnestic aphasia

noun
difficulty naming or recognizing object names
5.2 8.9
Pronunciation: /æm.ˈnes.tɪk ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /æm.ˈnes.tɪk ə.ˈfeɪ.zɪə/ (AmE), /æm.ˈnes.tɪk ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /æm.ˈnes.tɪk ə.ˈfeɪ.zɪə/ (BrE)
A type of aphasia characterized by difficulty in naming objects or recognizing the written or spoken names of objects.

Examples

Amnestic aphasia difficulty naming
Patients with amnestic aphasia often struggle to recall the correct words during conversation.
The neurologist diagnosed her with amnestic aphasia after observing her difficulty in naming everyday objects.
Therapy for amnestic aphasia may involve exercises to improve word retrieval and naming skills.
