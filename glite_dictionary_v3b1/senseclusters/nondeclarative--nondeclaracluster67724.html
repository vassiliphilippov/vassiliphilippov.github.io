nondeclarative

adjective
automatic, unconscious
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˈklɛrətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈklærətɪv / (BrE)
describing knowledge or skills that are performed automatically and do not require conscious recall

Examples

Nondeclarative memory skills
Learning to type quickly is a nondeclarative skill that improves with practice.
Many daily activities, such as walking or driving, rely on nondeclarative memory.
After years of playing piano, her nondeclarative knowledge allowed her fingers to move effortlessly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative - relating to knowledge that can be consciously recalled and described
  • synonym: implicit - understood or learned without being stated or consciously recalled
