charge card

noun
payment card requiring full repayment monthly
3.2 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ërd í k…ërd / (AmE), / t É…ëÀêd í k…ëÀêd / (BrE)
A card that allows the holder to purchase goods or services with the promise that payment will be made by the card issuer, usually requiring the full balance to be paid at the end of each billing period.

Examples

Use a charge card
He used his charge card to pay for the hotel room during his business trip.
Many companies offer a charge card as a convenient way for employees to manage expenses.
Unlike a credit card, a charge card usually requires you to pay the full balance each month.
