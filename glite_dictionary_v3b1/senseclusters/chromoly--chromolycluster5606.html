chromoly

noun
steel alloy for frames
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmɑːli / (AmE), / krəʊˈmɒli / (BrE)
a strong steel alloy containing chromium and molybdenum, commonly used in bicycle frames and machinery

Examples

A chromoly bike
Many high-quality bike frames are made from chromoly because of its strength and durability.
The mechanic recommended a chromoly part for better performance in rough conditions.
Some mountain bikes use chromoly tubing to balance weight and toughness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aluminum - a lightweight, silver-colored metal used in bike frames
  • synonym: steel - a hard, strong metal made from iron and carbon
