philology

noun
study of language and literature
Pronunciation: / fɪˈlɑlədʒi / (AmE), / fɪˈlɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of language and literature, especially their historical development and interrelation

Examples

Study of philology
His research in philology covers the evolution of Latin into the Romance languages.
She decided to major in philology because of her passion for ancient texts.
The journal publishes articles on philology, focusing on both language and literary analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: linguistics - the scientific study of language and its structure
