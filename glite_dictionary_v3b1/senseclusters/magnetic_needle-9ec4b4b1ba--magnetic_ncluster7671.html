magnetic needle

noun
magnetized pointer in a compass
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛdɪk ˈniːdəl / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛdɪk ˈniːdl̩ / (BrE)
a thin, magnetized pointer mounted in a compass that aligns itself with the earth's magnetic field to indicate direction

Examples

The magnetic needle in compass
Because the magnetic needle can move freely, it aligns itself with Earth's magnetic field.
She replaced the old magnetic needle to restore her grandfather's vintage compass.
The magnetic needle in his compass pointed steadily north.
