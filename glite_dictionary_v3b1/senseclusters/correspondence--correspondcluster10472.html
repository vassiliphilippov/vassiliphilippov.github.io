correspondence

noun
agreement; similarity (especially in results)
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːr.əˈspɑːn.dəns /; / ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndəns / (AmE), / ˌkɒr.ɪˈspɒn.dəns /; / ˌkɒrɪˈspɒndəns / (BrE)
a similarity, agreement, or close connection between two or more things (including in science or mathematics)

Examples

Correspondence between results
Scientists look for correspondence between theory and experiment to validate models.
The two sets of results showed a remarkable correspondence in their findings.
There is no correspondence between the predicted outcome and the actual data.
In geometry, a line of correspondence divides a figure into two mirror images.
The artist studied the correspondence of shapes on either side of the sculpture.
The butterfly's wings show perfect correspondence across the body’s midline.
A precise correspondence between supply and demand is rarely achieved in practice.
The architect ensured that there was exact correspondence in the dimensions of the paired columns.
There is a remarkable correspondence between the two ancient texts in style and content.
