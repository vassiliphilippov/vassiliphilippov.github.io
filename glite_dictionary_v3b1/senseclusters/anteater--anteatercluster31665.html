anteater

noun
African nocturnal mammal that eats termites
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈænˌtir / (AmE), / ˈɑːntˌiːtə / (BrE)
A nocturnal burrowing mammal of African grasslands that feeds mainly on termites; the sole extant member of the order Tubulidentata, also known as the aardvark.

Examples

African anteater
Researchers tracked the movements of the anteater across the African grasslands.
The anteater emerged from its burrow at dusk to search for termites.
Unlike other mammals, the anteater has a long snout adapted for eating insects.
