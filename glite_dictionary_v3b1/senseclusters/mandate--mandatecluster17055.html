mandate

verb
assign or authorize under formal mandate
6.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmæn.deɪt/ (AmE), /ˈmæn.deɪt/ (BrE)
to assign or authorize (a territory or institution) to be administered by a specific authority, often under a formal mandate, especially by a government or international body

Examples

To mandate a territory
After World War I, the League of Nations decided to mandate certain territories to be governed by Allied powers.
In 1920, Britain was authorized to mandate the governance of Palestine.
The United Nations chose to mandate administration of the region until stability was restored.

  • synonym: authorize - grant official permission or power to do something
