ontologically

adverb
relating to existence
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːntəˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɒntəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the nature of being or existence

Examples

To be ontologically different
The two theories are ontologically distinct, even if they appear similar on the surface.
Some philosophers argue that time is ontologically fundamental to the universe.
The concept is ontologically complex and requires careful analysis.
