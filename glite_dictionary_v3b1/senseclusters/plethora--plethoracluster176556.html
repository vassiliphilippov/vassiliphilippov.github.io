an amount that is much greater than what is necessary or can be managed; an extreme excess

Examples

A plethora of books
After her birthday, she found herself with a plethora of new books to read.
The city offers a plethora of dining options to choose from.
There is a plethora of information available online, making it hard to know where to start.

Synonyms

  • excess
    3.3 13/m
    excess is too much of something and often used in plain speech, while plethora sounds more formal and literary.
  • superabundance
    7.9 0.16/m
    superabundance is an extremely large amount that overwhelms, while plethora is often a formal word for excess.
  • overabundance
    5.7 0.45/m
    overabundance is a larger than needed quantity often used plainly, while plethora feels more bookish and slightly more dramatic.
  • superfluity
    8.7 0.12/m
    superfluity is an unnecessary extra amount used in formal contexts, while plethora is a general term for extreme excess.
  • profusion
    6.6 1.59/m
    profusion shows a rich, often positive large supply, while plethora points to having too much and feels negative.
  • overplus
    9.8 0.00/m
    overplus is an old or rare word for surplus, while plethora is common in modern writing for extreme excess.
  • embarrassment
    5.6 0.43/m
    embarrassment is an awkward excess that can make choice hard, while plethora names a plain extreme surplus.
