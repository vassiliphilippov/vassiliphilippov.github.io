major affective disorder

noun
mental disorder with major emotional disturbance
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒɚ əˈfɛktɪv dɪsˌɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə əˈfɛktɪv dɪsˌɔːdə / (BrE)
A mental disorder characterized primarily by significant disturbances in mood or emotions, not attributed to identifiable physical causes in the brain.

Examples

Major affective disorder symptoms
Patients suffering from major affective disorder may experience severe mood swings or episodes of depression.
Recent research seeks to better understand the biological basis of major affective disorder.
The diagnosis of major affective disorder often requires careful clinical evaluation by a psychiatrist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anxiety disorder - mental disorder characterized primarily by persistent anxiety
  • synonym: mood disorder - a broader category of mental disorders involving mood disturbance
