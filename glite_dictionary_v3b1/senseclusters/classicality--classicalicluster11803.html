classicality

noun
degree of classical behavior
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌklæsɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌklæsɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the extent to which a system or behavior can be explained by classical physics rather than quantum mechanics

Examples

Classicality in physics
The experiment measured the classicality of the system as it transitioned from quantum to classical behavior.
Physicists debate how to define classicality in systems that show both quantum and classical properties.
Increasing temperature often leads to greater classicality in the motion of particles.
