Social Democratic Party

proper noun
political party for democratic socialism
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊʃəl ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˌsəʊʃəl ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a political party advocating gradual transition from capitalism to democratic socialism, originally rooted in Marxism; notably in Germany, Britain, and other countries

Examples

The Social Democratic Party in Britain
Britain's Social Democratic Party was founded by former Labour members in the 1980s.
Many voters turned to the Social Democratic Party because of its progressive social policies.
The Social Democratic Party played a central role in shaping modern German politics.
