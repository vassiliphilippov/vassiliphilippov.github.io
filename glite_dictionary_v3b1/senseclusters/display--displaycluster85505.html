display

noun
an act of showing something to create an impression
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspleɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈpleɪ /; / dɪˈspleɪ / (BrE)
the act of showing something openly, such as a feeling, quality, or knowledge, to attract attention or communicate

Examples

Display of confidence
Her confident display of knowledge impressed everyone at the meeting.
His sudden display of anger caught everyone by surprise.
They put on a dramatic display of unity for the cameras.
Her display of generosity moved everyone at the event.
The peacock's colorful display attracted the attention of the visitors.
There was an impressive display of fireworks at the festival.
He tried to hide his feelings, but his display of excitement was obvious.
Her sudden display of anger surprised everyone in the room.
The ceremony included a touching display of gratitude toward the volunteers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonstration - an act of expressing a feeling or quality clearly
  • synonym: exhibition - a public display of art or items
  • synonym: show - an act of putting something on view or demonstrating
