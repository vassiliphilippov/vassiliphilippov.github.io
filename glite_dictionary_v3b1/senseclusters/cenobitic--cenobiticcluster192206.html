cenobitic

adjective
of communal religious living
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛnəˈbɪtɪk /; / siːnəˈbɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiːnəˈbɪtɪk /; / siːnəˈbɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the communal life practiced by cenobites, especially monks living together in a religious community.

Examples

cenobitic monastic life
He contrasted the cenobitic communities with the isolated existence of hermits.
Monasteries promoting cenobitic traditions became influential in the history of Christianity.
The cenobitic lifestyle emphasizes shared prayer, meals, and work among its members.
Many early monastic communities followed a coenobitic lifestyle, living and praying together under a shared rule.
The coenobitic traditions emphasized discipline and cooperation among the monks.
Unlike hermits, coenobitic monks share meals and daily routines within the monastery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eremitic - relating to the solitary life of a hermit
