Project Gemini

proper noun
U.S. spaceflight program (1965–66)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːdʒɛkt ˈdʒɛmɪni / (AmE), / ˈprɒdʒɛkt ˈdʒɛmɪni / (BrE)
the NASA program of crewed space flights undertaken by the United States between 1965 and 1966, designed to develop space travel techniques supporting later Apollo missions

Examples

Project Gemini missions
Astronauts learned vital spacewalking techniques during the Project Gemini missions.
Before Apollo, Project Gemini was instrumental in developing docking procedures in space.
The successful completion of Project Gemini paved the way for the Apollo missions to the Moon.
