letterbox

verb
to add black bars
7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈlet.ər.bɑːks/ /; / /ˈlet.ər.bɔːks/ (AmE), /ˈlet.ə.bɒks/ (BrE)
to display or convert a widescreen image for a narrower screen by adding horizontal black bars at the top and bottom

Examples

To letterbox a video
Many classic movies are letterboxed on television to preserve their original aspect ratio.
The streaming service decided to letterbox the film instead of cropping important scenes.
Some viewers prefer not to letterbox content, even if it means losing part of the image.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crop - to remove parts of an image or video
  • antonym: pillarbox - to display a narrow image on a wide screen by adding vertical black bars
