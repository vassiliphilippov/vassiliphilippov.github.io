electron

noun
negatively charged subatomic particle
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trɑːn/ /; / /ɪ.ˈlek.trən/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trɒn/ /; / /ɪ.ˈlek.trən/ (BrE)
A subatomic particle with a negative electric charge, found in all atoms and acting as the primary carrier of electricity in solids.

Examples

Free electron flow
Electric current in metals is carried by the flow of electrons.
The electron orbits the nucleus of an atom in defined energy levels.
When an electron moves between energy levels, it emits or absorbs light.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: neutron - a subatomic particle with no electric charge
  • antonym: proton - a subatomic particle with a positive electric charge
