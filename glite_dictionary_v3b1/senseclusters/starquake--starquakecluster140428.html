starquake

noun
stellar energy release
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑrˌkweɪk / (AmE), / ˈstɑːˌkweɪk / (BrE)
a sudden release of energy on the surface of a star, similar to an earthquake on Earth

Examples

A starquake event
Astronomers detected a powerful starquake on a distant neutron star last night.
The sudden starquake released enormous amounts of energy into space.
Scientists believe that a starquake can change the rotation of a star slightly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthquake - a sudden shaking of the ground caused by movement of the Earth's crust
