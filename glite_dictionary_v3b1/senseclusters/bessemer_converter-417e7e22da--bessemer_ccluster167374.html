Bessemer converter

noun
furnace for converting pig iron to steel
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛsəmɚ kənˈvɝːtɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɛsɪmə kənˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
A large refractory-lined furnace used to convert pig iron into steel by blowing air through the molten metal, as part of the Bessemer process.

Examples

The Bessemer converter furnace
Liquid pig iron is poured into the Bessemer converter to undergo rapid oxidation and removal of impurities.
Many early steel mills featured at least one massive Bessemer converter at the heart of their operation.
The invention of the Bessemer converter revolutionized the steelmaking industry in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bessemer process - the industrial process that uses a converter to turn pig iron into steel
