intermodal

adjective
between transport modes
6.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈmoʊ.dəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈməʊ.dəl/ (BrE)
involving or designed for the use of more than one mode of transport in a single journey or shipment

Examples

Intermodal transport system
The company invested in intermodal shipping to make transporting goods more efficient.
An intermodal terminal connects rail, road, and sea transport in one location.
Using intermodal containers reduces handling costs and saves time during transfers.
The new shipping system uses intermodal containers that can be transferred between ships, trains, and trucks.
The conference featured an intermodal presentation that combined video, music, and live performance.
Researchers studied how intermodal learning helps students understand complex concepts by using both visual and auditory materials.
The company invested in intermodal transport to reduce shipping times and costs.
An intermodal terminal allows goods to be transferred easily between ships and trains.
Many cities are developing intermodal hubs to connect buses, trains, and bicycles for commuters.
