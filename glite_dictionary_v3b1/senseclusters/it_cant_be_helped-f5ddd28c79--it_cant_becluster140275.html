Pronunciation: / ɪt kænt bi hɛlpt / (AmE), / ɪt kɑːnt bi hɛlpt / (BrE)
used to express acceptance of a situation that cannot be changed or avoided, often with a sense of resignation

Examples

The flight was canceled due to snow, and it can't be helped.
We missed the deadline, but it can't be helped now.
