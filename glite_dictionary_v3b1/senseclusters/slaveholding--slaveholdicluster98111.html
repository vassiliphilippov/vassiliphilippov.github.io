slaveholding

adjective
allowing or practicing slavery
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsleɪvˌhoʊldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪvhəʊldɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, institution, or society) allowing or practicing slavery; possessing enslaved people.

Examples

Slaveholding family
He came from a prominent slaveholding family in the nineteenth century.
Many Southern states were considered slaveholding regions before the American Civil War.
The slaveholding plantation owners resisted any changes to the status quo.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolitionist - person who opposes slavery
