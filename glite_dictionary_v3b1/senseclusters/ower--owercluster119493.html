ower

noun
person in debt
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊər / (AmE), / ˈəʊə / (BrE)
a person who owes money or is in debt to someone else, especially in dialectal or archaic usage

Examples

A ower pays debts
The ower promised to settle his debts by the end of the month.
In old records, the ower is listed alongside the amount he owed.
The judge ordered the ower to repay the full sum within thirty days.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creditor - a person or organization to whom money is owed
  • synonym: debtor - a person who owes money
