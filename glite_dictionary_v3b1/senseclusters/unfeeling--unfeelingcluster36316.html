unfeeling

adjective
lacking compassion or sympathy
4.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfiːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈfiːlɪŋ / (BrE)
not showing sympathy, compassion, or emotion; emotionally cold or insensitive

Examples

An unfeeling person
Despite her friend's tears, she remained unfeeling and offered no comfort.
His unfeeling response to the tragedy shocked everyone at the meeting.
Many considered the manager's decision unfeeling, as he ignored the employees' struggles.
Despite her unfeeling words, she actually cared deeply for her friends.
The unfeeling bureaucracy ignored the needs of the community.
The principal's unfeeling response upset many students and parents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compassionate - showing sympathy and concern for others
  • synonym: insensitive - showing a lack of concern for others' feelings
