to suggest that someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing; to accuse or implicate

Examples

To inculpate a suspect
Her testimony did not inculpate anyone directly involved in the case.
It was unfair of the news report to inculpate him before the trial began.
The evidence seemed to inculpate the manager, but later proved to be false.

Synonyms

  • implicate
    7.2 4.03/m
    implicate shows or suggests someone is involved in wrongdoing, while inculpate is a formal term used to blame or charge them.
  • incriminate
    7.4 1.41/m
    incriminate presents evidence or makes someone seem guilty, while inculpate is a broader formal way to suggest their guilt.
  • accuse
    2.7 59/m
    accuse openly says someone did something wrong, often in speech or complaint, while inculpate sounds more formal and legal.

Related Terms
