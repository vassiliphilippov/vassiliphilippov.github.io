inculpate

verb
suggest or accuse someone of guilt
7.7 8.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌl.peɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌl.peɪt / (BrE)
to suggest that someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing; to accuse or implicate

Examples

To inculpate a suspect
Her testimony did not inculpate anyone directly involved in the case.
It was unfair of the news report to inculpate him before the trial began.
The evidence seemed to inculpate the manager, but later proved to be false.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exculpate - to show or declare that someone is not guilty
  • synonym: implicate - to show or suggest someone is involved in a crime
