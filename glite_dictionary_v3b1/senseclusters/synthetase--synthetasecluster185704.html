synthetase

noun
enzyme joining molecules
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈθiːteɪs / (AmE), / sɪnˈθiːteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the joining of two molecules using energy from ATP

Examples

A synthetase enzyme
A synthetase is essential for linking amino acids during protein synthesis.
Researchers discovered a new synthetase involved in DNA repair.
The malfunction of a specific synthetase can lead to metabolic disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrolase - an enzyme that catalyzes the breaking of bonds using water
  • synonym: ligase - an enzyme that joins two molecules together
