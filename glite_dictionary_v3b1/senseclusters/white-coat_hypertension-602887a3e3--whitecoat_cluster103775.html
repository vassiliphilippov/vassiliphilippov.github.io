white-coat hypertension

noun
temporary high blood pressure in medical settings
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt koʊt ˌhaɪpərˈtɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt kəʊt ˌhaɪpəˈtɛnʃən / (BrE)
a temporary increase in blood pressure that occurs when a person is in a medical or clinical setting, especially in the presence of healthcare professionals

Examples

White-coat hypertension in clinic
Her doctor suspected white-coat hypertension after her blood pressure was normal at home but elevated during office visits.
Many patients experience white-coat hypertension, making it difficult to obtain accurate readings in clinical settings.
The nurse explained that white-coat hypertension is common and not always a sign of chronic high blood pressure.
