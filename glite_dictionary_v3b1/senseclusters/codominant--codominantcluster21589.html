codominant

adjective
equally dominant
8.1
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ˈdɑː.mɪ.nənt/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ˈdɒ.mɪ.nənt/ (BrE)
having equal dominance, especially referring to traits or species that are both fully expressed or equally influential

Examples

Codominant genes
In some blood types, both A and B alleles are codominant and are expressed equally.
The two tree species are codominant in this forest, sharing the canopy space.
When traits are codominant, both can be observed in the organism's appearance.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dominant - having more influence or power than others
  • antonym: recessive - having less influence or not expressed when a dominant trait is present
