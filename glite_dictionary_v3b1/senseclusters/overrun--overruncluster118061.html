overrun

verb
to go past a limit or boundary

to go beyond or past the intended limit or boundary (such as a vehicle, process, or liquid)

Examples

To overrun the limit
If you overrun the end of the page, your text will be cut off.
The meeting overran its scheduled time by half an hour.
The plane overran the runway during landing because of the wet conditions.
If you add too much data, the buffer might overrun and cause an error.
The river began to overrun its banks after the heavy rainfall.
The saucepan will overrun if you keep boiling the milk unattended.

Synonyms

  • go beyond
    3.6 14/m
    go beyond is a neutral phrase for passing a limit in many situations, while overrun often suggests being overwhelmed or uncontrolled.
  • exceed
    4.1 29/m
    exceed is a formal verb meaning to go past a set amount or limit, while overrun often implies spreading or overflowing.
  • overstep
    5.9 0.54/m
    overstep means to cross a rule or what is acceptable, while overrun covers passing a physical or planned limit.
  • spill over
    4.2 1.44/m
    spill over describes liquid or crowds moving out of bounds, while overrun also covers processes or vehicles going past limits.
  • overflow
    2.9 3.69/m
    overflow means to flow out because something is too full, while overrun can also mean passing a limit by force or time.
  • run over
    3.2 0.34/m
    run over often means liquid spilling over a container's brim, while overrun can mean passing a limit or overwhelming.
  • overspill
    4.9 0.01/m
    overspill means liquid or things spill beyond an edge in excess, while overrun adds broader senses like vehicles or processes passing limits.
