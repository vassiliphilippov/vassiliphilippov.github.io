ricercare

noun
Renaissance instrumental piece
9.9
Pronunciation: /riː.tʃər.ˈkɑː.reɪ/ (AmE), /riː.tʃə.ˈkɑː.reɪ/ (BrE)
an instrumental composition from the Renaissance or Baroque period, often featuring imitative counterpoint

Examples

A ricercare for harpsichord
The harpsichordist performed a ricercare by Frescobaldi at the concert.
Many composers of the Baroque era wrote at least one ricercare for keyboard instruments.
A ricercare often explores a single musical theme in intricate ways.
