hard

adjective
firm, solid, not easily bent or broken
1.0 59/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërd / (AmE), / h…ëÀêd / (BrE)
firm and solid; not easily bent, broken, or pierced; or (of food) dry and stiff

Examples

Hard surface
She found it difficult to drive nails into the hard wall.
The candy became as hard as a rock after it cooled.
This table is made of hard oak wood and will not scratch easily.
After a week, the cookies became too hard to eat.
She threw away the hard rolls that had been left out overnight.
The hard crust of the bread made it difficult to bite.

Synonyms

  • firm
    1.0 2.79/m
    firm is slightly solid with some give and feels pleasant to press, while hard is more fully solid and unyielding.
  • solid
    1.0 25/m
    solid is dense and keeps its shape like a rock, while hard focuses on surface toughness or resistance to breaking.
  • rigid
    4.0 5.55/m
    rigid does not bend and sounds more formal, while hard covers physical firmness and everyday objects.
  • inflexible
    4.9 0.19/m
    inflexible resists bending and often appears in formal or figurative use, while hard names plain physical firmness.
  • unyielding
    6.7 0.29/m
    unyielding resists pressure and suggests great strength, while hard simply means firm or not easily broken.
  • stiff
    1.7 14/m
    stiff is hard to bend or move, often for clothing or joints, while hard is about solidness or toughness.
  • stony
    3.9 0.10/m
    stony is as hard and cold as rock, often extreme or poetic, while hard is basic everyday firmness.
  • tough
    1.7 2.17/m
    tough resists tearing or chewing and can be flexible, while hard means rigid, solid, or not easily pierced.

Related Terms
