hard

adjective
firm, solid, not easily bent or broken
4.7 1.0
Pronunciation: /hɑːrd/ (AmE), /hɑːd/ (BrE)
firm and solid; not easily bent, broken, or pierced; or (of food) dry and stiff

Examples

Hard surface
She found it difficult to drive nails into the hard wall.
The candy became as hard as a rock after it cooled.
This table is made of hard oak wood and will not scratch easily.
After a week, the cookies became too hard to eat.
She threw away the hard rolls that had been left out overnight.
The hard crust of the bread made it difficult to bite.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft - easily bent or compressed; not hard or firm
  • synonym: stale - no longer fresh and pleasant to eat; hard, musty, or dry, especially of bread and cakes
