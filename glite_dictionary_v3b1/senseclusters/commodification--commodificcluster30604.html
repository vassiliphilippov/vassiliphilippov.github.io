commodification

noun
turning into a commodity
8.4
Pronunciation: / kəˌmɑːdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌmɒdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of turning something, such as an idea, service, or relationship, into a product that can be bought or sold

Examples

The commodification of art
Critics argue that the commodification of art reduces its cultural value.
The commodification of personal data has raised concerns about privacy and consent.
Some believe that the commodification of healthcare leads to inequality in access to services.
Critics argue that the commodification of education undermines its true purpose and value.
The commodification of art has changed how artists create and sell their work.
Some worry that the commodification of personal data threatens privacy and individual rights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercialization - the process of managing or running something mainly for financial gain
  • antonym: decommodification - the process of removing something from the market or making it not for sale
