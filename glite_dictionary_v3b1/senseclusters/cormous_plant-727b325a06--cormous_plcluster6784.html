cormous plant

noun
plant growing from a corm
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrməs plænt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːməs plɑːnt / (BrE)
a plant that grows from a corm, a short, thickened underground stem that stores nutrients

Examples

A cormous plant grows
After flowering, a cormous plant uses its corm to store energy for the next growing season.
Many gardeners choose to plant cormous plants for their colorful and reliable flowers.
The crocus is a well-known example of a cormous plant that blooms in early spring.
