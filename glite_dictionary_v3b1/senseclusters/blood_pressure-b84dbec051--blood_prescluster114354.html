blood pressure

noun
the pressure of blood in the arteries
Pronunciation: / blʌd ˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / blʌd ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
the pressure exerted by circulating blood upon the walls of blood vessels, typically measured to assess health

Examples

High blood pressure
High blood pressure can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.
Regular exercise may help reduce blood pressure over time.
The nurse checked his blood pressure before giving him the medication.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hypertension - abnormally high blood pressure
  • degree lower: hypotension - abnormally low blood pressure
