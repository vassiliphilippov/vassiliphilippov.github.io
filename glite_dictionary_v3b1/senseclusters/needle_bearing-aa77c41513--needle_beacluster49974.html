needle bearing

noun
bearing with long, thin rollers
7.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈniː.dəl ˈber.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈniː.dəl ˈbeə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
A type of bearing that uses long, thin cylindrical rollers to reduce friction in rotating machinery, often in a small space.

Examples

A needle bearing roller
A new needle bearing must be installed whenever the housing is replaced.
Because of their compact size, needle bearings are used in automotive transmissions.
The gearbox contains a needle bearing to support the rotating shaft.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ball bearing - bearing using small spherical balls as the rolling element
  • synonym: roller bearing - bearing that uses rolling elements to maintain separation between moving parts
