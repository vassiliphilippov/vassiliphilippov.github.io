decon

noun
removal of contaminants
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːkɑn / (AmE), / ˈdiːkɒn / (BrE)
the process of removing dangerous substances such as chemicals, biological agents, or radioactive materials from people, equipment, or areas

Examples

Full decon process
After the chemical spill, the team performed a full decon before leaving the site.
All equipment must go through decon procedures to ensure safety after exposure.
The emergency workers waited for decon before entering the clean area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contamination - the presence of harmful substances
  • synonym: decontamination - the process of making something free from dangerous substances
