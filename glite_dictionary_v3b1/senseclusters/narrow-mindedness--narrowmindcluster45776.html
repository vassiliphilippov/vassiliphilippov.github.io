narrow-mindedness

noun
intolerance of differing views or behaviors
3.0 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnæ.r.oʊ.maɪn.dɪd.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈnær.əʊ.maɪn.dɪd.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being unwilling to accept or tolerate opinions, beliefs, or behaviors that differ from one's own

Examples

Narrow-mindedness in opinions
Critics accused the council of narrow-mindedness for rejecting the new art installation.
His narrow-mindedness prevented him from understanding cultures different from his own.
The manager's narrow-mindedness made innovation in the company nearly impossible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas; open to differing opinions
