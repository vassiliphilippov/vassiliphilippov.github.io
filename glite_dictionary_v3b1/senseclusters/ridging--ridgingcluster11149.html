ridging

noun
high-pressure formation
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈrɪdʒ.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈrɪdʒ.əŋ/ (AmE), /ˈrɪdʒ.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈrɪdʒ.əŋ/ (BrE)
the formation or presence of an elongated area of high atmospheric pressure, often resulting in settled and warmer weather

Examples

Atmospheric ridging
Meteorologists observed increased ridging over the western states, leading to a week of dry weather.
The forecast predicted that ridging would keep temperatures above average for several days.
Satellite images revealed strong ridging in the upper atmosphere, blocking incoming storms.
