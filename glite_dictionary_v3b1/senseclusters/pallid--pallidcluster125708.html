pallid

adjective
dim or feeble (of light)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpælɪd / (AmE), / ˈpælɪd / (BrE)
(of light) lacking in intensity or brightness; dim or feeble.

Examples

Pallid light
A pallid moon hung in the sky above the quiet city.
Early morning brought a pallid light through the misty window.
The pallid glow from the lamp barely illuminated the corner of the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting a lot of light
  • synonym: dim - not bright or clear (of light)
