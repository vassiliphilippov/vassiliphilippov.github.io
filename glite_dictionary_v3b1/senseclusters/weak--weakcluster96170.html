weak

adjective
lacking political or governing power
Pronunciation: / wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
lacking authority, political strength, or governing power; not able to exercise effective control or influence

Examples

Weak leadership
During the crisis, the organization appeared weak and unable to enforce its own rules.
Many critics argued that the reforms made the government weak and indecisive.
The country's weak leadership led to widespread instability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: powerless - lacking power or influence
  • antonym: strong - having great power or authority
