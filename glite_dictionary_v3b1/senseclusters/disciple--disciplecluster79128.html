disciple

verb
to train in beliefs
8.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsaɪ.pəl/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsaɪ.pəl/ (BrE)
to teach or train someone to follow a particular belief, doctrine, or way of life, especially in a religious context

Examples

To disciple new believers
The pastor spent years discipling new members of the congregation.
She is passionate about discipling young people in her community.
Many organizations focus on discipling future leaders through mentorship and training.
The elder discipled several young members of the community over many years.
She discipled new students, helping them understand the core values of the group.
Many leaders have discipled others to continue their traditions and teachings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mentor - to guide or train someone, especially over a period of time
  • antonym: mislead - to guide someone in the wrong direction or to a false belief
  • synonym: teach - to give instruction or lessons to someone
