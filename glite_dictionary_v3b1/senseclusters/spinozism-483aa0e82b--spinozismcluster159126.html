Spinozism

noun
doctrine equating God and universe
9.9
Pronunciation: /spɪ.ˈnoʊ.zɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /spɪ.ˈnəʊ.zɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the philosophical system or beliefs developed by Baruch Spinoza, especially the idea that God and the universe are the same and the emphasis on rationalism and unity of substance

Examples

Spinozism philosophy
Many philosophers have debated the implications of Spinozism for modern science and religion.
Her studies in philosophy led her to explore the principles of Spinozism in depth.
Some critics argue that Spinozism challenges traditional views of God and nature.
Many modern philosophers have been influenced by Spinozism in their work.
The core idea of Spinozism is that everything is part of one substance.
He wrote his thesis on the ethical implications of Spinozism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dualism - the belief that mind and body are separate substances
  • synonym: pantheism - the belief that God is identical with the universe
  • antonym: theism - the belief in the existence of a god or gods, especially a creator
