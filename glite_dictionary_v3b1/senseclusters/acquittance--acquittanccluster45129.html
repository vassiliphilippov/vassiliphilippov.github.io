acquittance

noun
legal document for debt discharge
3.7
Pronunciation: / əˈkwɪt.əns / (AmE), / əˈkwɪt.əns / (BrE)
a written legal document that certifies the discharge of a debt or obligation

Examples

A acquittance document
After settling the debt, she received an acquittance from the lender.
He presented an acquittance to prove that his loan had been paid in full.
The court required an official acquittance before closing the file on the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: receipt - an acknowledgment that something has been received, often as payment
