vocalization

noun
production of sound with the vocal folds
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˌvoʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvəʊkələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌvəʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The sound produced when air is expelled through the vocal cords and shaped by the resonance of the vocal tract; the act or process of producing sounds with the voice, especially to communicate.
