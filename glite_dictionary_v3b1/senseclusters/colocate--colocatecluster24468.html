colocate

verb
to place together
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊloʊˈkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊləʊˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to place or arrange things together in the same location, especially for efficiency or convenience

Examples

To colocate servers
Many companies colocate their servers in specialized data centers for better security and maintenance.
The research teams decided to colocate in one building to improve collaboration.
To save costs, several small businesses chose to colocate their offices in a shared workspace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: group - to put things together in a collection
  • antonym: separate - to move or keep things apart
