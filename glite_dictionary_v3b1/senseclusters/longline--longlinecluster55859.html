longline

noun
fishing line with hooks
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a long fishing line with many baited hooks, used to catch fish such as tuna or swordfish

Examples

A longline for tuna
The fishermen set out a longline early in the morning to catch tuna.
Environmental groups are concerned about the impact of longlines on sea turtles and birds.
After hours at sea, the crew began hauling in the longline to check their catch.
The fishermen set a longline overnight to catch swordfish in deep waters.
A single longline can have thousands of hooks spread out over many kilometers.
Environmental groups are concerned about the impact of longline fishing on sea turtles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trawl - a large net dragged along the sea bottom to catch fish
