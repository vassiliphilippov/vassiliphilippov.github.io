uncaught

adjective
not handled (computing)
7.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkɔt / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɔːt / (BrE)
not intercepted, handled, or captured, especially referring to an error or exception in computing that is not managed by code.

Examples

Uncaught error
The application terminated unexpectedly due to an uncaught exception.
If an error is uncaught, the program may crash or stop responding.
Developers should ensure that no uncaught errors reach the end user.

Related Terms

  • antonym: caught - intercepted or handled, especially by error-handling code
  • synonym: unhandled - not dealt with or managed, especially in computing
