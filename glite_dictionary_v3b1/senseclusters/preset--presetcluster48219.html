preset

verb
to set in advance
Pronunciation: / priːˈsɛt / (AmE), / ˈpriːsɛt / (BrE)
to set or adjust something in advance before it is needed or used

Examples

To preset the timer
She was presetting the camera to record the sunrise before dawn.
Before leaving, he preset the thermostat to turn on the heat at 6 a.m.
You can preset your coffee machine to start brewing automatically in the morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjust - to change or set something to a desired state
  • synonym: prearrange - to arrange or organize something beforehand
  • antonym: reset - to set again or differently
