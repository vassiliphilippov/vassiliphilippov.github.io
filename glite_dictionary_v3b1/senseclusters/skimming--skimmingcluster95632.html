skimming

noun
removal of floating material from liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing floating material from the surface of a liquid, often for clarification or cleaning

Examples

Skimming milk
After the milk was boiled, skimming removed the thick layer of cream from its surface.
Regular skimming is necessary in water treatment plants to remove debris from the tanks.
The cook performed skimming to ensure the soup was clear and free of fat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skim - to remove floating matter from a liquid's surface (verb)
