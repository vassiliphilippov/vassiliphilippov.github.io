novellae

noun
later imperial laws
9.9
Pronunciation: / noʊˈvɛlaɪ / (AmE), / nəʊˈvɛlaɪ / (BrE)
collections of new laws or constitutions, especially those issued after a major legal codification, such as Justinian's Novellae in Roman law

Examples

Justinian's novellae
The legal historian analyzed the novellae to understand changes in Byzantine law.
After the main code was published, several novellae were issued to address new legal issues.
Researchers found important reforms in the novellae that were not present in earlier laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: code - a systematic collection of laws or rules
  • synonym: constitution - a formal law or decree, especially in Roman or Byzantine law
