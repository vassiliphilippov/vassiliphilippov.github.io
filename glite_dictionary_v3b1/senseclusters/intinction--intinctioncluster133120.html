intinction

noun
dipping bread in wine
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act of dipping bread into wine during a Christian communion service

Examples

Communion intinction
During the service, the congregation participated in intinction as part of the Eucharist.
Some churches use intinction instead of drinking directly from the chalice.
The bishop explained the tradition of intinction to the new members of the parish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communion - the Christian ceremony of sharing bread and wine
