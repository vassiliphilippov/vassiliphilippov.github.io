William Henry Seward

proper noun
U.S. Secretary of State, arranged Alaska purchase
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəm ˈhɛnri ˈsuːərd / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈhɛnri ˈsjʊəd / (BrE)
(1801–1872) United States politician and Secretary of State who arranged the purchase of Alaska from Russia in 1867, an event referred to as 'Seward's Folly.'

Examples

William Henry Seward purchase Alaska
William Henry Seward played a pivotal role in expanding the territory of the United States by negotiating the purchase of Alaska.
As Secretary of State, William Henry Seward was an influential figure in Abraham Lincoln’s administration.
The purchase of Alaska was widely criticized at the time, and many referred to it as 'Seward's Folly,' named after William Henry Seward.
