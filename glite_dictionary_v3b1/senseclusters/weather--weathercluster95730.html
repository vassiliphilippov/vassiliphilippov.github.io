weather

verb
sail to windward side (nautical)
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
(of a ship or sailor) to sail to the windward side of something, especially to avoid danger or make headway against the wind.

Examples

To weather a headland
The captain ordered the crew to weather the headland despite the strong winds.
The ship managed to weather the rocky shoal and reach safe waters.
They skillfully weathered the cape, avoiding the reefs in the process.
