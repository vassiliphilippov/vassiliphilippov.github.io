greenshank

noun
large sandpiper with greenish legs
8.1 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈgriːn.ʃæŋk/ (AmE), /ˈgriːn.ʃæŋk/ (BrE)
a large wading bird of the sandpiper family, found in Europe and Asia, with greenish legs and a long bill.

Examples

A greenshank bird
A single greenshank was spotted feeding along the bank of the river.
During migration, flocks of greenshanks can be seen on mudflats and estuaries.
The greenshank is known for its distinctive greenish legs and sharp, probing bill.
