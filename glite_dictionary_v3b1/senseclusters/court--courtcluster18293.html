court

verb
try to win someone's favor or support
5.5 3.51/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrt / (AmE), / k…îÀêt / (BrE)
to try to win the favor or support of someone, especially by attention or flattery

Examples

To court support
During the campaign, the candidate courted support from young voters across the country.
He spent months courting the approval of the senior management team.
The company is actively courting investors to expand into new markets.

Synonyms

  • court favor
    6.8 0.01/m
    court favor means trying to gain approval by flattering or acting very polite, while court means trying to win someone's support more generally.
  • curry favor
    7.4 0.59/m
    curry favor is using flattery or extra help to gain advantage, while court means trying to win someone's favor often politely.
  • woo
    5.6 1.33/m
    woo is to try to gain support often by attention or gifts, while court focuses on winning favor sometimes by flattery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to someone or something
  • synonym: woo - to seek someone's support or favor, often with flattery or attention
