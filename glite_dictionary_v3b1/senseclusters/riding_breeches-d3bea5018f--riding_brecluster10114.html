riding breeches

noun
flared trousers for horseback riding
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪdɪŋ ˌbrɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈraɪdɪŋ ˌbriː.tʃɪz / (BrE)
Trousers that are flared at the hips and close-fitting from the knee to the ankle, typically ending at the calves, worn for horseback riding with riding boots.

Examples

Riding breeches with boots
His old-fashioned riding breeches were made of thick, durable fabric.
She put on her riding breeches and boots before heading out to the stables.
The equestrian competition required all riders to wear proper riding breeches and a helmet.
