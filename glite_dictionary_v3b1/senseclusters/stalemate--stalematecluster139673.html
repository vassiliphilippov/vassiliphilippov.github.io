stalemate

noun
draw in chess with no legal moves
6.3 2.41/m

a situation where no progress can be made or no one can win, especially in chess or in an argument or contest

Examples

Chess stalemate
After fifty moves, the game ended in a stalemate, with neither player emerging victorious.
Even though he had more pieces, a clever defense led to a stalemate.
The possibility of stalemate forces chess players to think carefully before making their final moves.
Negotiations reached a stalemate, leaving the two companies unable to move forward.
The match became a stalemate with neither team able to score a decisive goal.
The peace talks ended in a stalemate after both sides refused to compromise.

Synonyms

  • impasse
    8.1 2.56/m
    impasse is a point where talks stop because people cannot agree, while stalemate often means no one can win or make progress.
  • deadlock
    5.8 1.26/m
    deadlock means opposing sides cannot move or agree and work stops, while stalemate often highlights that no side can win.
  • standoff
    6.2 3.20/m
    standoff is a situation where two sides face each other without progress, while stalemate describes a blocked outcome with no clear winner.
  • standstill
    3.5 1.99/m
    standstill is when movement or action stops completely, while stalemate also carries the idea that no one can win or advance.
  • dead end
    3.6 2.66/m
    dead end is a situation with no way forward or development, while stalemate usually means parties are stuck and cannot win.
