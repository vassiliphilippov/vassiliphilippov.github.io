give off

verb
emit or produce (as a by-product)
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ɔf / (AmE), / ɡɪv ɒf / (BrE)
to produce or emit something, such as a smell, light, heat, sound, gas, or even a feeling

Examples

To give off heat
Flowers often give off a pleasant fragrance at night.
The chemical reaction can give off dangerous fumes if performed incorrectly.
The old engine gives off a lot of smoke when started.
That old heater gives off a lot of heat during winter nights.
The flowers give off a sweet fragrance in the evening.
When mixed together, these chemicals give off a harmless vapor.
His speech gave off an air of confidence that impressed the audience.
The flowers gave off a wonderful sweet fragrance in the garden.
The lamp gives off a soft, warm glow at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up (energy, light, or another substance)
  • synonym: emit - to send out (a substance, especially a gas or radiation)
  • synonym: give out - to emit or discharge (something), similar in meaning
  • synonym: release - to allow a substance to escape or be set free
