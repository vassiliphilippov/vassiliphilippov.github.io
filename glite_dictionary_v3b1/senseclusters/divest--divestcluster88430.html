divest

verb
take away possessions, rights, or power
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɛst / (AmE), / daɪˈvɛst /; / dɪˈvɛst / (BrE)
to take away or remove power, rights, or possessions from someone, often officially or by authority

Examples

To divest someone of power
During the reforms, many officials were divested of their positions and privileges.
The law aims to divest certain organizations of their financial assets.
The new government decided to divest the company of its land holdings.
After the scandal, the board decided to divest the CEO of all his executive powers.
She was divested of her presidential role following the investigation.
The new regime divested the aristocrats of their titles and privileges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deprive - to take something away from someone
  • synonym: strip - to remove property, rights, or titles from someone
