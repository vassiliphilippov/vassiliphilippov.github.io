stock

verb
supply with livestock
2.1 9.64/m
Pronunciation: / stÉ‘k / (AmE), / stÉ’k / (BrE)
to provide, supply, or keep something available for use or sale (such as goods, animals, or fish)

Examples

To stock shelves
After purchasing the land, they stocked it with cows and horses.
The farmer stocked the pasture with a new herd of sheep.
To improve productivity, agricultural programs often help families stock their farms with livestock.
Many shops stock up on gifts and decorations before the holiday season begins.
The small store stocks a wide variety of organic produce for its customers.
We should stock enough food to last through the winter in case of emergencies.
Do you stock any batteries for this model?
Our local store does not stock gluten-free bread.
They always stock fresh produce even in winter.

Synonyms

  • carry
    1.0 35/m
    carry means to have items available on hand for use or transport, while stock means to supply and keep goods for sale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carry - to have (something) for sale in a store
  • synonym: hoard - to collect and keep a large amount of something
  • synonym: restock - to provide with a new supply of stock
  • synonym: store - to keep or accumulate something for future use
