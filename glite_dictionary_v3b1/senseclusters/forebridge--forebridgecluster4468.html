forebridge

noun
ship's front platform
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
the raised or forward part of a ship's upper deck, usually at the front, used for navigation, control, or other purposes

Examples

The forebridge deck
The captain gave orders from the forebridge as the ship entered the harbor.
Crew members gathered on the forebridge to watch for obstacles ahead.
From the forebridge, you have a clear view of the sea in front of the vessel.
The captain ordered the crew to gather on the forebridge before setting sail.
During the storm, supplies were secured in the forebridge to prevent them from shifting.
He enjoyed the view from the forebridge as the ship approached the harbor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aftbridge - a platform at the rear of a ship used for control
  • synonym: bridge - the area on a ship from which it is commanded
  • synonym: forecastle - the forward part of a ship below the deck, used as crew quarters
  • antonym: stern - the rear part of a ship
