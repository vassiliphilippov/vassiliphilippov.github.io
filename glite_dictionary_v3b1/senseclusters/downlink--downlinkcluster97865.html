downlink

noun
signal to device
5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.lɪŋk/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.lɪŋk/ (BrE)
a communication channel or signal sent from a satellite or central transmitter to a receiving device or ground station

Examples

Satellite downlink
The satellite's downlink allowed scientists to receive real-time weather data from space.
Engineers tested the downlink to ensure the new device could receive updates from the network.
A strong downlink is essential for streaming high-quality video on your phone.
The engineers monitored the downlink to ensure data was being received from the satellite.
A strong storm can sometimes disrupt the downlink between a spacecraft and mission control.
The new antenna improved the quality of the downlink signal from the weather satellite.
The satellite sent weather images to the ground station via the downlink.
Engineers monitored the downlink to ensure the data was received without interruption.
A strong downlink is essential for real-time communication with spacecraft.
