pernicious anemia

noun
chronic anemia caused by vitamin B12 deficiency
7.4 4.6
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈnɪ.ʃəs ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /pə.ˈnɪ.ʃəs ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
A chronic, progressive type of anemia caused by the body's inability to absorb vitamin B12, usually due to a lack of intrinsic factor secreted by the stomach.

Examples

Pernicious anemia diagnosis
After being diagnosed with pernicious anemia, she required regular vitamin B12 injections.
Early symptoms of pernicious anemia include fatigue, weakness, and pale skin.
Medical tests revealed that his chronic fatigue was due to pernicious anemia.
Because pernicious anaemia affects the absorption of vitamin B12, patients often require lifelong treatment.
Early diagnosis of pernicious anaemia can prevent serious complications such as nerve damage.
The doctor suspected pernicious anaemia after noting the patient's fatigue and neurological symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anemia - a condition in which you lack enough healthy red blood cells
  • antonym: iron deficiency anaemia - anaemia caused by insufficient iron, a different common type of anaemia
  • synonym: megaloblastic anaemia - a type of anaemia characterized by large red blood cells, often caused by vitamin B12 deficiency
