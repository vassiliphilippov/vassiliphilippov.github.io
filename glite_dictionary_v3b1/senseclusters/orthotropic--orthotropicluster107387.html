orthotropic

adjective
upright growth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθəˈtrɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
growing or oriented in a straight, upright, or vertical direction, especially referring to plant stems or shoots aligned with the main axis

Examples

Orthotropic tree growth
The main trunk of the tree is orthotropic, growing straight upward toward the sky.
In some plants, orthotropic shoots develop to form the central stem, while others grow at an angle.
Botanists observed that the orthotropic branches received more sunlight than the horizontal ones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plagiotropic - growing at an angle or horizontally rather than upright
