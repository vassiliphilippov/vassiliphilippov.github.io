hypovolemic

adjective
relating to decreased blood volume
8.4 8.0
Pronunciation: /haɪ.poʊ.və.ˈliː.mɪk/ /; / /haɪ.poʊ.voʊ.ˈliː.mɪk/ (AmE), /haɪ.pəʊ.və.ˈliː.mɪk/ /; / /hɪ.pəʊ.və.ˈliː.mɪk/ (BrE)
Relating to or caused by a decrease in the volume of circulating blood in the body.

Examples

Hypovolemic shock
A hypovolemic state can lead to shock if not treated promptly.
Physicians monitor fluid levels carefully to prevent hypovolemic complications after surgery.
The patient was diagnosed as hypovolemic following severe blood loss after the accident.
Doctors monitored her closely for signs of hypovolaemic complications following the accident.
Rapid fluid replacement is required in cases of hypovolaemic shock.
The patient became hypovolaemic after losing a significant amount of blood during surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypervolaemic - relating to an increased blood volume
  • synonym: hypovolemia - an abnormally low volume of blood in the body
