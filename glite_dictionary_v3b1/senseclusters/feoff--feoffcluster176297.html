feoff

noun
land held under feudal tenure
9.2
Pronunciation: / fɛf / (AmE), / fɛf / (BrE)
A piece of land held by a vassal under the feudal system.

Examples

A feoff of land
Records show that the manor was once a royal feoff granted in the twelfth century.
The knight received a feoff from his lord as a reward for his service.
Under the old laws, a feoff came with duties owed to the lord.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fief - an estate of land held on condition of feudal service
