Cumberland River

proper noun
major river in Kentucky and Tennessee
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌmbɚlænd ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌmbələnd ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southeastern United States that rises in Kentucky, flows through Tennessee, and joins the Ohio River in southwestern Kentucky

Examples

The Cumberland River
Many recreational activities, including fishing and boating, take place on the Cumberland River.
Nashville, Tennessee, is located on the banks of the Cumberland River.
The Cumberland River often floods during heavy spring rains, impacting the surrounding communities.
