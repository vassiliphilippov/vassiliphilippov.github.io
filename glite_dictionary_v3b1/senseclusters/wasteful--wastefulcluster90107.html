wasteful

adjective
causing destruction; laying waste (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪstfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪstfʊl / (BrE)
(archaic) Causing widespread destruction or devastation; laying waste.

Examples

Wasteful destruction
Poetry often speaks of wasteful storms destroying harvests and homes.
The wasteful armies left nothing but ruins in their path.
Years of wasteful raids reduced the ancient city to rubble.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destructive - causing great and irreparable damage
