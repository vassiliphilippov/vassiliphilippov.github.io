mujtahid

noun
Islamic legal scholar (ijtihad)
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / muːdʒˈtɑːhɪd / (AmE), / mʊdʒˈtahɪd / (BrE)
A Muslim scholar qualified to interpret Islamic law by independent effort and reasoning (ijtihad) based on sacred texts.

Examples

A mujtahid scholar
Becoming a mujtahid requires years of dedicated study of Islamic theology and law.
The council consisted of several respected mujtahids from different regions.
The ruling was issued after consultation with a leading mujtahid in the community.
