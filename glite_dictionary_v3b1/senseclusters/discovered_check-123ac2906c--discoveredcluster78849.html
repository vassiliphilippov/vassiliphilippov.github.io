discovered check

noun
chess: check revealed by moving a piece
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskʌvərd tʃɛk / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌvəd tʃɛk / (BrE)
in chess, a check given to the opponent's king when a piece moves away, uncovering an attack by another piece

Examples

A discovered check in chess
A discovered check can be especially dangerous if the attacking piece is a queen or rook.
He won the game with a brilliant discovered check that left his opponent stunned.
She set up a discovered check by moving her bishop out of the way.
