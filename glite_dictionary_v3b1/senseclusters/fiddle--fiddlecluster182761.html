fiddle

verb
avoid or neglect assigned duties
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
(informal) To avoid or neglect one's responsibilities or assigned duties, often by aimlessly occupying oneself with unimportant tasks.

Examples

To fiddle at work
He fiddled at his desk all afternoon instead of starting the new project.
Stop trying to fiddle your way out of helping with the chores and just get them done.
Whenever there's hard work to do, she somehow finds a way to fiddle and avoid it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dodge - evade (a duty or responsibility)
  • antonym: fulfill - carry out a duty, satisfy an obligation
  • synonym: shirk - deliberately avoid doing something one should do
