mute

verb
make a sound quieter, especially by covering
Pronunciation: / mjut / (AmE), / mjuːt /; / mjud0t / (BrE)
to make a sound, message, or notification quieter or silent, either physically or digitally

Examples

To mute the sound
He tried to mute the noise by placing a towel over the alarm.
She quickly muted her phone to avoid disturbing the meeting.
The orchestra muted the brass section for a softer sound.
I mute him on Twitter so his posts no longer appear in my feed.
During meetings she muted notifications from Slack to avoid distractions from channel chatter.
The moderator decided to mute the noisy participant for twenty-four hours in the group chat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: block - to prevent someone from contacting you or seeing your content; a stronger action than muting.
  • synonym: deaden - to make a sound less loud or intense
  • synonym: silence - to stop someone or something from making noise or being seen/heard; used here for hiding posts or notifications.
  • synonym: soften - to make a sound or noise less harsh or loud
