cover

verb
guard an opponent in a game
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvər /; / ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to protect, guard, or shield someone or something, including in sports, military, or insurance contexts

Examples

To cover a player
He struggled to cover the speedy striker in the first half.
Make sure you cover your man at all times during the game.
The defender was assigned to cover the opposing team's best forward.
He always double-checks his work to cover any possible mistakes.
She made a backup of her files to cover herself in case of computer failure.
The lawyer advised him to cover his tracks by keeping thorough records.
Please cover the hallway while I check the offices.
Security guards covered the perimeter to ensure nobody entered without permission.
The officer was assigned to cover the west entrance during the night shift.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defend - to protect from attack or danger, especially in a sporting or military context
  • synonym: guard - to protect or watch over, especially to prevent harm or danger
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone
  • synonym: insure - provide insurance coverage against loss or damage
  • synonym: mark - guard an opponent closely in sports
  • synonym: patrol - to move around an area to monitor or guard it
  • synonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
  • synonym: target - to aim at or direct a weapon toward
