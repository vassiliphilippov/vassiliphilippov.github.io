Prince-of-Wales feather

noun
New Zealand fern with woolly stalks
Pronunciation: / ˌprɪns əv ˈweɪlz ˈfɛðər / (AmE), / ˌprɪns əv ˈweɪlz ˈfɛðə / (BrE)
A New Zealand fern with pinnate fronds and densely woolly stalks; sometimes classified in the genus Todea.

Examples

The Prince-of-Wales feather fern
Botanists disagree about whether to classify the Prince-of-Wales feather in the genus Todea.
She admired the unique shape of the Prince-of-Wales feather in the botanical garden.
The Prince-of-Wales feather thrives in the damp forests of New Zealand.
