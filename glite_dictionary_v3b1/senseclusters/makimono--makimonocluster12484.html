makimono

noun
sushi roll
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːkiˈmoʊnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌmækɪˈməʊnəʊ / (BrE)
a type of sushi roll wrapped in seaweed and rice, usually filled with fish, vegetables, or other ingredients

Examples

Tuna makimono roll
We shared a platter of makimono filled with fresh tuna and avocado at dinner.
The chef demonstrated how to prepare makimono using a bamboo mat and nori sheets.
Vegetarian makimono with cucumber and pickled radish is a popular choice at the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nigiri - a type of sushi with a slice of fish over pressed rice
  • synonym: sushi - a Japanese dish of vinegared rice with various fillings and toppings
