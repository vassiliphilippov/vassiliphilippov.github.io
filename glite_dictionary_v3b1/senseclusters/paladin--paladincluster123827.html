paladin

noun
vigorous champion of a cause
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpæləˌdɪn /; / ˈpælədɪn / (AmE), / ˈpælədɪn / (BrE)
a brave champion or knight, especially someone who fights for a noble cause or strongly supports a movement

Examples

A paladin knight
In literature, the paladin often stands alone against injustice.

In the fantasy novel, the paladin led his companions into battle against the dark forces.
She chose to play a paladin in the game, always defending the weak and upholding justice.
The villagers saw him as a paladin after he saved them from the bandits.
