electrophotography

noun
electrical imaging process
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊfəˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊfəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a printing or imaging process that uses electrical charges to form images on a surface

Examples

Electrophotography printing process
Many modern copiers rely on electrophotography to reproduce documents quickly and efficiently.
The invention of electrophotography revolutionized the printing industry in the twentieth century.
Laser printers use electrophotography to create sharp text and images on paper.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photography - the art or process of producing images by the action of light
  • synonym: xerography - a dry photocopying process using electrostatic charges
