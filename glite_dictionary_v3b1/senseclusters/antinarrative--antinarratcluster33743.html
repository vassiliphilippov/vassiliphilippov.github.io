antinarrative

adjective
rejecting narrative structure
9.6
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈnær.ə.tɪv/ (BrE)
not following or rejecting traditional narrative structure or storytelling conventions, especially in literature, film, or criticism

Examples

Antinarrative film style
The director's antinarrative approach challenged viewers to find meaning without a clear plot.
Some modern novels use antinarrative techniques to break away from traditional storytelling.
Her essays are known for their antinarrative style, avoiding a typical beginning, middle, and end.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - following traditional or established forms
  • synonym: nonlinear - not following a straight or chronological sequence
