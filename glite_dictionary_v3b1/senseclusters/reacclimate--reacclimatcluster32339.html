reacclimate

verb
to adjust again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈækləˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈæklɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to help someone or something become accustomed again to a particular climate, environment, or situation

Examples

To reacclimate a person
After spending months abroad, it took her some time to reacclimate to her hometown's weather.
The rescued animals needed to reacclimate before being released back into the wild.
He struggled to reacclimate to office life after working remotely for so long.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acclimate - to become used to a new climate or environment
  • antonym: disorient - to make someone lose their sense of direction or adaptation
  • synonym: readjust - to adapt again to new or changed conditions
