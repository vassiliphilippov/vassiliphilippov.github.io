dominance

noun
superior development of one body side
5.6 0.42/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…ëÀêm…ôn…ôns / (AmE), / Ààd…ím…™n…ôns / (BrE)
the state in which one side of the body is more developed or more frequently used than the other, often seen in handedness or footedness

Examples

Left-hand dominance
Language skills are sometimes linked to cerebral dominance in the brain.
Physical therapy helped balance out the dominance of her stronger leg.
The dominance of his right hand made tasks with his left feel awkward.

Synonyms

  • laterality
    9.4 0.09/m
    laterality shows a side preference or brain specialization for certain skills, while dominance describes one side being stronger or used more.
  • sidedness
    7.7 0.00/m
    sidedness is a simple tendency to favor one side in movement and tasks, while dominance is a broader measure of side strength or use.
