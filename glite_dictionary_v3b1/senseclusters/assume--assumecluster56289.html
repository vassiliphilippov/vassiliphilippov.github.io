assume

verb
take on a particular form or quality
2.0
Pronunciation: / əˈsum / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to take on or display a particular appearance, form, posture, or to put on clothing

Examples

To assume a shape
In myths, gods often assume the shapes of animals to interact with humans.
She assumed a cheerful tone as she delivered the news to the team.
The actor assumed an angry expression during the dramatic scene.
At the coronation, the monarch assumed the royal robes before entering the hall.
In the play, the actor assumes the costume of a medieval knight.
She quietly assumed her mask and cloak before leaving the palace.
At the ceremony, the president will assume his official seat at the front.
She assumed her place among the panelists before the discussion began.
The dancer gracefully assumed a classic ballet pose as the curtain rose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: don - to put on (an article of clothing)
