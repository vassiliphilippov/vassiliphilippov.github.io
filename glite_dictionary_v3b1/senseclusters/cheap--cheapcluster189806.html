cheap

adjective
low-priced; costing little
1.0 59/m
Pronunciation: / t Éip / (AmE), / t ÉiÀêp / (BrE)
Low in price; costing little compared to others of the same kind.

Examples

Cheap clothes
She prefers to buy cheap clothes so she can change styles often.
The furniture in that store is surprisingly cheap for its quality.
We decided to stay at a cheap hotel to save money during our trip.

Synonyms

  • inexpensive
    1.0 12/m
    inexpensive describes a low price in a neutral way, while cheap often carries a negative idea of poor quality.
  • low-priced
    1.0 1.03/m
    low-priced describes items sold at relatively small amounts, while cheap can mean low quality as well as low price.
  • low-cost
    2.0 5.27/m
    low-cost describes something that needs little money to buy or run, while cheap focuses mainly on low price.
  • affordable
    2.1 21/m
    affordable means within most people's budget and is about ability to pay, while cheap only means low in price and may imply poor quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expensive - high in price; costing a lot of money
  • synonym: inexpensive - low in price; not costing much
