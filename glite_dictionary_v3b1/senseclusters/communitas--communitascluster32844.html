communitas

noun
shared community spirit
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjuːnɪˌtɑs / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪˌtæs / (BrE)
a strong feeling of community, equality, and togetherness experienced by people during shared group events or rituals

Examples

Strong communitas feeling
The festival created a sense of communitas among people from different backgrounds.
During the crisis, the town experienced true communitas as everyone helped each other.
Volunteers often talk about the communitas they feel when working together for a cause.
The festival created a sense of communitas among participants, breaking down social barriers.
Anthropologists study communitas to understand how rituals foster equality and group bonding.
During the pilgrimage, the travelers experienced communitas that made everyone feel like equals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alienation - a feeling of being isolated or separated from a group
  • antonym: hierarchy - a system in which people are ranked one above another
  • synonym: solidarity - unity or agreement among individuals with a common interest
