vibrating reed

noun
strip that vibrates to produce sound
7.1 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈvaɪ.breɪ.tɪŋ riːd/ (AmE), /ˈvaɪ.breɪ.tɪŋ riːd/ (BrE)
a thin, stiff strip of material that vibrates to produce a sound or tone when air passes over it, commonly used in musical instruments such as clarinets or harmonicas.

Examples

A vibrating reed instrument
A metal vibrating reed is often found in scientific measuring instruments.
Clarinets and saxophones use a vibrating reed as a crucial part of their mouthpieces.
The technician replaced the old vibrating reed in the organ to restore its sound quality.
