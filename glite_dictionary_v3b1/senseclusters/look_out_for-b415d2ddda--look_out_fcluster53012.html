look out for

verb
be vigilant or watchful for someone or something
1.0
Pronunciation: / lʊk aʊt fɔr / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt fə / (BrE)
to be alert and attentive to the presence or welfare of someone or something; to watch to protect or support someone or to notice something.

Examples

To look out for danger
During the hike, please look out for any signs warning about dangerous animals.
She always tries to look out for her younger brother at school.
Whenever you're crossing the street, be sure to look out for oncoming traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to someone or something
  • synonym: watch out for - to be vigilant or on guard for something
