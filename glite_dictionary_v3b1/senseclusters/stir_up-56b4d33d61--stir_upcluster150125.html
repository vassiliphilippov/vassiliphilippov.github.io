stir up

verb
deliberately provoke emotions or reactions
5.3 3.9
Pronunciation: /stɜːr ʌp/ (AmE), /stɜː ʌp/ (BrE)
to deliberately cause or provoke strong emotions, reactions, or trouble

Examples

To stir up trouble
Activists hope to stir up debate on environmental issues with their campaign.
Rumors can easily stir up unnecessary panic in a community.
The politician tried to stir up public anger against the new law.
Controversial articles often stir up heated debates online.
He warned that rumors could stir up unnecessary panic.
Her speech was designed to stir up support among the community.
False rumors began to stir up panic in the community.
Social media can sometimes stir up controversy over minor issues.
The politician's speech was meant to stir up anger among the crowd.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm down - to make less agitated or angry
  • synonym: incite - to encourage or stir up, especially unlawful or violent behavior
  • synonym: provoke - to cause a particular reaction or feeling, especially anger
