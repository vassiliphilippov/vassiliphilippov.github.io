hark back to

verb
recall or refer to the past
Pronunciation: / hɑrk bæk tu / (AmE), / hɑːk bæk tʊ / (BrE)
to mention, remember, or be reminiscent of something from an earlier time

Examples

To hark back to history
The architecture of the new library harks back to classical Greek temples.
This tradition harks back to medieval festivals celebrated in the town square.
Whenever politics comes up, he tends to hark back to his days in office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember or deliberately not think about the past
  • synonym: recall - to remember or bring to mind a past fact or event
  • synonym: reminisce - to recall or talk about past experiences with pleasure
