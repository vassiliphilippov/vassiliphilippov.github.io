wheel

verb
move or travel on wheels
2.0 5.60/m
Pronunciation: / wil /; / wiÀêl / (AmE), / wiÀêl / (BrE)
to move, push, or transport something or someone on wheels, or to ride on a wheeled vehicle

Examples

To wheel a patient
Paramedics wheeled the patient down the hallway to the emergency room.
She wheeled her bike through the gate and locked it outside.
The waiter wheeled the dessert trolley to each table with a flourish.
He would wheel down the country lanes every Sunday morning.
She loved to wheel through the city streets on her old bicycle.
The children wheeled around the park together after school.
He wheeled the heavy cart down the long corridor to the storage room.
She wheeled her suitcase behind her as she hurried through the airport.
The nurse expertly wheeled the patient out of surgery and into recovery.

Synonyms

  • roll
    1.0 77/m
    roll moves by turning along a surface, often without help, while wheel moves, pushes, or carries something using wheels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carry - to hold and move (something) from one place to another, often by lifting
  • synonym: drive - to operate and control the movement of a vehicle
  • synonym: push - to exert force to move something away from oneself, often used for wheeled objects
  • synonym: roll - to move along a surface by turning over and over
