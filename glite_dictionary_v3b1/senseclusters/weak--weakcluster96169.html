weak

adjective
lacking skill or ability
Pronunciation: / wiːk /; / wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
lacking skill, ability, or intelligence in a particular area; not good at something or easily influenced mentally

Examples

Weak at math
He is weak in mathematics and struggles to keep up with the class.
Her argument was weak and failed to convince the panel.
Though fluent in conversation, she is weak in English grammar.
He was kind but rather weak intellectually, often failing to grasp complex ideas.
She considered his arguments to be the product of a weak mind.
The students found the exam challenging, but only those with a weak understanding really struggled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dim - not smart or quick to understand
  • antonym: intelligent - having or showing high mental capacity
  • antonym: strong - having great skill or ability in a particular area
  • synonym: stupid - lacking intelligence or common sense
