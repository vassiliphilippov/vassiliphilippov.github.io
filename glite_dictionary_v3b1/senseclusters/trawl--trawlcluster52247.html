trawl

verb
to search thoroughly
4.6
Pronunciation: / trɔl / (AmE), / trɔːl / (BrE)
to search through something thoroughly, especially a large area or collection

Examples

To trawl the internet
She spent hours trawling the internet for information about her family history.
The police are trawling through old records to find clues about the case.
He trawled the library shelves for books on ancient civilizations.
The police trawled through hundreds of files to find the evidence they needed.
Every morning, she trawls the internet for the latest news updates.
Fishermen trawl the deep sea to catch large quantities of fish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scan - to look over quickly or systematically
  • synonym: search - to look for something carefully
