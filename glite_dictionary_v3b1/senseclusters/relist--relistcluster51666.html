relist

verb
to add again to list
3.9
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈlɪst/ /; / /rɪ.ˈlɪst/ /; / /rə.ˈlɪst/ (AmE), /riː.ˈlɪst/ /; / /rɪ.ˈlɪst/ /; / /rə.ˈlɪst/ (BrE)
to put something back on a list or offer it for sale again, especially after it was removed or not sold

Examples

To relist an item
After the item did not sell, the seller decided to relist it on the website.
The company had to relist several products that were previously out of stock.
If your house does not sell, you can always relist it with a new agent.
After the auction ended without a buyer, he decided to relist the painting.
The company will relist the product on their website next month.
If your item does not sell, you can easily relist it with a new price.
After the painting did not sell, the gallery decided to relist it at a lower price.
Sellers often relist items on online platforms if they do not get any offers.
He plans to relist his old laptop next week, hoping for better luck this time.
