inviscid

adjective
having no viscosity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɪsɪd / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɪsɪd / (BrE)
describing a fluid that has no or negligible viscosity and flows without resistance

Examples

An inviscid fluid
The model assumes the liquid is inviscid to simplify the calculations.
In physics, an inviscid fluid is idealized as having no internal friction.
Engineers often use inviscid flow equations to approximate real fluid behavior in certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluid - a substance that flows, such as a liquid or gas
  • antonym: viscous - having a thick, sticky consistency; high in viscosity
