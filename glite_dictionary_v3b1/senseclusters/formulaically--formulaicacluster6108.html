formulaically

adverb
using a formula
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈmjuːləɪkli / (AmE), / fɔːˈmjʊəlɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that uses or follows a formula, especially when calculating or deriving results.

Examples

Formulaically solve problems
The scientist approached the experiment formulaically, relying on established equations to predict the outcome.
Instead of improvising, the teacher graded the assignments formulaically according to a strict rubric.
Some writers structure their stories formulaically, following the same pattern in every novel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intuitively - in a way based on instinct or feeling rather than logic or formula
  • synonym: systematically - in a methodical or organized way
