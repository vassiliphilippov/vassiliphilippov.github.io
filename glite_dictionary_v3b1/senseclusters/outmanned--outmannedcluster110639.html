outmanned

adjective
with fewer people
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈmænd / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈmænd / (BrE)
having fewer people, especially in a competition or conflict, than the opposing side

Examples

To be outmanned in battle
Despite being outmanned, the soldiers held their position until reinforcements arrived.
The basketball team was clearly outmanned but refused to give up.
In the debate, she felt outmanned by the number of opponents on the panel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outnumbered - having fewer people than the other side
  • synonym: overpowered - defeated or controlled by greater force or numbers
  • antonym: superior - having more people or resources
