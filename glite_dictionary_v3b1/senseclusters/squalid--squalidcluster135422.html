squalid

adjective
extremely dirty and unpleasant
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˈskwɒlɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) extremely dirty and unpleasant, especially as a result of poverty or neglect.

Examples

Squalid living conditions
He was appalled by the squalid conditions in the abandoned warehouse.
The investigative report exposed the squalid reality behind the luxurious facade.
The refugees were forced to live in squalid camps on the outskirts of the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities
  • synonym: filthy - extremely dirty
