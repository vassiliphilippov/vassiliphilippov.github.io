graphene

noun
single-layer carbon material
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɡræˈfiːn / (AmE), / ˈɡræfiːn / (BrE)
a very thin, strong material made of a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice

Examples

A graphene sheet
Scientists believe that graphene could revolutionize the electronics industry due to its unique properties.
A single sheet of graphene is almost transparent but incredibly strong.
Engineers are exploring the use of graphene in flexible displays and advanced batteries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbon nanotube - a cylindrical nanostructure made of carbon atoms
  • synonym: graphite - a form of carbon with layers of atoms, used in pencils
