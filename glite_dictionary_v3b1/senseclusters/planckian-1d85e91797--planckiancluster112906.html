Planckian

adjective
relating to Planck scales
Pronunciation: / ˈplæŋkiən / (AmE), / ˈplæŋkiən / (BrE)
relating to the physical constants, scales, or phenomena defined by Max Planck in physics, such as Planck length or Planck time

Examples

Planckian scale
The experiment achieved Planckian temperatures, pushing the boundaries of modern physics.
At Planckian scales, the usual laws of physics may no longer apply.
Scientists study Planckian phenomena to understand the earliest moments of the universe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quantum - relating to the smallest possible unit in physics
