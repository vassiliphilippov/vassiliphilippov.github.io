prearrange

verb
arrange in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.əˈreɪndʒ /; / ˌpriːəˈreɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˌpriː.əˈreɪndʒ /; / ˌpriːəˈreɪndʒ / (BrE)
to arrange or plan something in advance, before it is needed or happens

Examples

All the meetings were prearranged to avoid scheduling conflicts.

We need to prearrange transportation before the conference starts.
They prearranged the meeting last week to ensure everyone could attend.
The flowers were prearranged on the table before the guests arrived.
We decided to prearrange transportation to avoid any last-minute issues.
