lorry

noun
large horse-drawn wagon without sides
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔri / (AmE), / ˈlɒri / (BrE)
a large vehicle for transporting heavy loads, especially a truck (mainly British English); originally a large low wagon without sides, often horse-drawn

Examples

A lorry carrying timber
A lorry carrying timber arrived at the construction site early in the morning.
Farmers once used a lorry like this to move produce to market.
The museum displayed a restored lorry from the nineteenth century in its transport exhibit.
A lorry overturned on the motorway, causing a major traffic jam.
The lorry was loaded with crates of fresh produce for the market.
They hired a lorry to move all their furniture to the new house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: truck - motor vehicle for carrying goods; chiefly American English
  • synonym: wagon - A vehicle with four wheels, usually pulled by horses, used for transporting heavy loads
