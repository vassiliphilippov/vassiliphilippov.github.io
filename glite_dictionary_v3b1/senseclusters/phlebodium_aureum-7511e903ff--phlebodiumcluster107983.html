Phlebodium aureum

noun
a tropical American fern, blue star fern
Pronunciation: / flɪˈboʊdiəm ɔːˈriəm / (AmE), / flɪˈbəʊdiəm ɔːˈrɪəm / (BrE)
A tropical American fern with brown scaly rhizomes and large, deeply lobed bluish-green fronds, often cultivated as a houseplant.

Examples

Phlebodium aureum houseplant
Botanists sometimes debate whether Phlebodium aureum should be classified under the genus Polypodium.
Many gardeners appreciate Phlebodium aureum for its ability to thrive in low-light environments.
The Phlebodium aureum is admired for its striking bluish-green foliage in indoor plant collections.
