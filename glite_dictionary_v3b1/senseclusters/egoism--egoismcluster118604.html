egoism

noun
theory that self-interest underpins morality
Pronunciation: / ˈiːɡoʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
(ethics) The theory that the pursuit of one's own interests or welfare is the foundation of morality.

Examples

Egoism in philosophy
His strong belief in egoism led him to prioritize his own needs above all else.
Many philosophers have debated whether egoism can truly provide a solid basis for ethical behavior.
Some critics argue that egoism ultimately undermines social cooperation and trust.

Related Terms

  • antonym: altruism - the principle or practice of unselfish concern for others
