decree nisi

noun
provisional divorce order (law)
9.9 8.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈkriː ˈniː.saɪ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈkriː ˈniː.si/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈkriː ˈniː.saɪ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈkriː ˈniː.si/ (BrE)
A provisional court order for divorce, which does not take effect until a specified period has passed without objection and then becomes absolute.

Examples

A decree nisi order
A decree nisi is the first formal step in finalizing a divorce in England and Wales.
After the decree nisi was issued, both parties had time to raise any objections.
The court granted a decree nisi, which would become final in six weeks unless challenged.
