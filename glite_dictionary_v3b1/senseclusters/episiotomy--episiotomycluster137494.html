episiotomy

noun
surgical incision during childbirth
6.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˌpiziˈɑtəmi / (AmE), / ɪˌpiːziˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision made in the perineum during childbirth to enlarge the vaginal opening and facilitate delivery

Examples

An episiotomy cut
An episiotomy is not always necessary, and its use has declined in recent years.
Some women recover quickly after an episiotomy, while others may need more time.
The doctor performed an episiotomy to help with the difficult delivery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: caesarean section - surgical procedure to deliver a baby through an incision in the mother's abdomen
  • synonym: perineum - the area between the anus and the genitals
