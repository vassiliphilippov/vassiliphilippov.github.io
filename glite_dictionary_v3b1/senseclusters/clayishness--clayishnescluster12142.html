clayishness

noun
quality of being clay-like or containing clay
Pronunciation: / ˈkleɪɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈkleɪɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being like clay, especially in composition or consistency

Examples

The clayishness of soil
Because of its clayishness, the ground was heavy and sticky underfoot during planting.
The clayishness of the soil made it difficult for water to drain properly after rain.
The region is known for the clayishness of its earth, which affects the crops that can be grown.
After the rain, the trail regained its usual clayishness, making walking tricky.
She disliked the clayishness of the face mask, preferring a lighter texture.
The clayishness of the dough made it difficult to roll out smoothly.
