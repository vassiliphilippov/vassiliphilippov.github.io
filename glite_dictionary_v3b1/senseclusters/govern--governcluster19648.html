govern

verb
require a certain grammatical form
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌvərn / (AmE), / ˈɡʌvən / (BrE)
(of a word or construction) to require that another word be in a particular case, form, or mood in grammatical structure.

Examples

Prepositions govern cases
In Latin, certain prepositions govern the ablative case rather than the accusative.
Some moods in Greek are governed by specific types of subordinate clauses.
The verb 'depend' governs the preposition 'on' in English.

Related Terms

  • synonym: control - have power over; direct the function or course of something
