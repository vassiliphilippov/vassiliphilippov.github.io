Alexandrianism

noun
Alexandrian theology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪɡˈzændriənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌælɪɡˈzændriənɪzəm / (BrE)
a school of Christian theology and biblical interpretation from Alexandria, known for allegorical readings of Scripture and focus on Christ's divine nature

Examples

Alexandrianism in theology
The influence of Alexandrianism can be seen in the writings of early church fathers like Origen.
Debates between Alexandrianism and other theological schools shaped early Christian doctrine.
Many scholars study Alexandrianism to understand the development of allegorical interpretation in Christianity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Antiochene school - a school of Christian theology emphasizing literal interpretation of Scripture
