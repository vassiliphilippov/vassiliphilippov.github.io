sea holm

noun
a spiny-leaved evergreen eryngo plant
Pronunciation: / si hoʊm / (AmE), / si həʊm / (BrE)
A European evergreen eryngo (Eryngium maritimum) with twisted spiny leaves, formerly used medicinally; now naturalized on the U.S. east coast.

Examples

The sea holm plant
The roots of sea holm were once prized for their supposed aphrodisiac properties.
The twisted spiny leaves of sea holm make it easy to spot among other dune plants.
You can find sea holm growing along sandy beaches in certain coastal regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sea holly - another common name for Eryngium maritimum, a spiny coastal plant
