osteoid

noun
unmineralized bone matrix
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɑstiˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪˌɔɪd / (BrE)
the unmineralized, organic part of bone matrix produced by osteoblasts before it becomes hardened bone

Examples

Unmineralized osteoid matrix
The pathologist identified large amounts of osteoid within the patient's fractured bone during the examination.
A deficiency in vitamin D can lead to an accumulation of osteoid in growing bones.
Osteoblasts are responsible for producing osteoid before it mineralizes into mature bone.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bone - the hard, mineralized connective tissue forming the skeleton
  • synonym: bone matrix - the intercellular substance of bone tissue
