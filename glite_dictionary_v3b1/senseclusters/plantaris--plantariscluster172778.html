plantaris

noun
lower leg muscle
Pronunciation: / plænˈtɛrɪs / (AmE), / plænˈtɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a small, thin muscle in the lower leg that runs from the knee to the heel and assists in foot movement

Examples

The plantaris muscle
The plantaris is sometimes absent in people without affecting leg function.
Surgeons may use the plantaris tendon for reconstructive procedures in the hand or foot.
Injuries to the plantaris are rare but can cause pain behind the knee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gastrocnemius - the large calf muscle in the back of the lower leg
  • synonym: soleus - a broad, flat muscle in the lower calf beneath the gastrocnemius
