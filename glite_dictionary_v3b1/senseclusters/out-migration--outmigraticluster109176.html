out-migration

noun
migration from a place to another
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊt.maɪˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaʊt.maɪˈɡreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the movement of people leaving a particular area, especially their native country, to settle elsewhere

Examples

High out-migration rates
Economic decline can often trigger high levels of out-migration in rural areas.
Researchers track out-migration rates to understand population changes.
The town has suffered due to out-migration of young professionals seeking better opportunities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emigration - the act of leaving one's own country to settle permanently in another
  • antonym: immigration - the act of coming to live in a foreign country
