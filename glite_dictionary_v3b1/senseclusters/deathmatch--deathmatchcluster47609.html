deathmatch

noun
elimination contest
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛθˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛθˌmatʃ /; / ˈdɛθˌmætʃ / (BrE)
a game, contest, or match where players or opponents compete to eliminate each other until only one remains (often violent or extreme, as in video games or wrestling)

Examples

Video game deathmatch
The championship was decided by an intense deathmatch between the top two players.
In many video games, the deathmatch mode is popular for its fast-paced action.
After hours of competition, only one team survived the final deathmatch.
The crowd was on the edge of their seats during the intense deathmatch between the two rivals.
Many fans consider a deathmatch the most thrilling type of wrestling event.
He trained for months to prepare for his first deathmatch in the championship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battle royale - a game mode where many players fight until only one survives
  • antonym: exhibition match - a wrestling match without extreme violence or weapons
  • synonym: hardcore match - a wrestling match with fewer rules and more violence
  • synonym: tournament - a series of contests to determine a winner
