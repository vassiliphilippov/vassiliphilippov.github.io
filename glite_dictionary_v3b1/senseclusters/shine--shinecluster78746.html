shine

verb
emit or reflect light; be bright
3.6
Pronunciation: / ʃaɪn / (AmE), / ʃaɪn / (BrE)
to emit or reflect light; to be bright or glossy

Examples

The metal shines brightly
The silverware shines brightly after being polished.
The stars shone in the clear night sky.
The sun was shining through the window, lighting up the whole room.
After the rain, the leaves shone with a fresh, vibrant look.
Polished floors shine as if someone just mopped them.
Tears made her eyes shine in the dim light.
Lights shone through the windows all night.
She polished the silver until it began to shine again.
The stars shine brightly on clear nights in the countryside.
