pregame

verb
to warm up before event
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌɡeɪm / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌɡeɪm / (BrE)
to warm up or practice briefly immediately before a sports game or performance.

Examples

To pregame on the field
The team gathered on the field to pregame before the championship match.
Coaches recommend that athletes pregame to reduce the risk of injury.
She always likes to pregame with some light stretching and jogging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool down - to gradually reduce activity after exercise
  • synonym: warm up - to prepare the body for physical activity
