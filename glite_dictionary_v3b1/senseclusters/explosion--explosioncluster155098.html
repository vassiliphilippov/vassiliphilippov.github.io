explosion

noun
a loud noise made by bursting or breaking violently
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈsploʊ.ʒən/ /; / /ɛk.ˈsploʊ.ʒən/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspləʊ.ʒən/ /; / /ɛk.ˈspləʊ.ʒən/ (BrE)
a sudden and violent burst or release of energy, often causing a loud noise or damage

Examples

A loud explosion
A distant explosion echoed across the valley, startling the hikers.
The explosion woke everyone in the neighborhood in the middle of the night.
We heard a loud explosion and saw smoke rising from the factory.
Scientists are investigating the cause of the explosion at the chemical plant.
The factory was destroyed by a massive explosion caused by leaking gas.
The nuclear explosion produced a shockwave felt miles away.
A loud explosion was heard miles away from the accident site.
The explosion shook nearby buildings and shattered all the windows.
The scientist explained the chemical reaction that led to the sudden explosion in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bang - a sudden loud noise, similar to an explosion
  • synonym: blast - a violent explosion or shockwave
  • synonym: crash - a loud, sudden noise of something breaking
  • synonym: detonation - the act of causing something to explode
  • antonym: implosion - a violent inward collapse, opposite of explosion
