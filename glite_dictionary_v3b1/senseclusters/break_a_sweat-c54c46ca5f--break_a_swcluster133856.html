break a sweat

verb
to start sweating from physical effort
3.2 3.4
Pronunciation: /breɪk ə swet/ (AmE), /breɪk ə swet/ (BrE)
to exert oneself physically or to begin sweating due to effort; often used to suggest something is easy or does not require much effort

Examples

To break a sweat easily
He could carry those heavy boxes all day and never break a sweat.
She finished the 5K run without even breaking a sweat.
You won't break a sweat assembling this simple piece of furniture.
