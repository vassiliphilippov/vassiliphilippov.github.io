subsidiarity

noun
local decision-making principle
Pronunciation: / səbˌsɪdiˈærəti / (AmE), / səbˌsɪdiˈærəti / (BrE)
the principle that decisions should be made by the smallest, lowest, or least centralized competent authority

Examples

The subsidiarity principle
The European Union often applies the principle of subsidiarity when making policy decisions.
Advocates argue that subsidiarity leads to more effective and responsive local governments.
The debate centered on whether subsidiarity should guide the distribution of educational responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralization - the concentration of control under a central authority
  • synonym: decentralization - the transfer of authority from central to local government
