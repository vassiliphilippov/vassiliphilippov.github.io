American leishmaniasis

noun
tropical skin and mucosa disease of the Americas
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˌliːʃ.məˈnaɪ.əsɪs / (AmE), / əˈmer.ɪ.kən ˌliːʃ.məˈnaɪ.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
A form of leishmaniasis found in Mexico, Central America, and South America, characterized by sores limited to the skin and mucous membranes.

Examples

American leishmaniasis skin sores
Health officials launched a campaign to reduce cases of American leishmaniasis in Central America.
Many patients in rural areas are diagnosed with American leishmaniasis after developing persistent skin sores.
Unlike other types, American leishmaniasis often affects the mucous membranes as well as the skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leishmaniasis - a disease caused by protozoan parasites, typically transmitted by sandflies
