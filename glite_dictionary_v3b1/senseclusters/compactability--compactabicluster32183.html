compactability

noun
ability to be compacted
Pronunciation: / kəmˌpæktəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kəmˌpæktəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be compacted or compressed into a smaller volume

Examples

The compactability of soil
The compactability of the soil affects construction stability.
Engineers tested the compactability of different materials before choosing one for the project.
High compactability is important when selecting materials for road foundations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressibility - the ability to be compressed or made smaller
  • synonym: density - the degree of compactness of a substance
