shearling

noun
young shorn sheep
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪəlɪŋ / (BrE)
a young sheep that has been shorn once, usually about one year old.

Examples

A young shearling
The farmer brought a healthy shearling to the county fair for judging.
After its first shearing, the shearling was kept separate from the older sheep.
Wool from a shearling is often softer and highly valued by textile makers.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ewe - an adult female sheep
  • degree lower: lamb - a young sheep, especially one less than a year old
