shrill

adjective
high-pitched, piercing (sound)
4.8 3.63/m
Pronunciation: / ÊƒrÉªl / (AmE), / ÊƒrÉªl / (BrE)
(of a sound or manner) high-pitched, piercing, or unpleasantly loud and forceful

Examples

A shrill whistle
A shrill whistle signaled the end of the game.
Her laughter became almost shrill with excitement.
The teacher's shrill voice easily rose above the noise in the classroom.
His shrill accusations lost him many supporters among the public.
The critics made a shrill call for the company's immediate resignation.
There was a shrill urgency in her voice during the debate.

Synonyms

  • high-pitched
    3.7 3.22/m
    high-pitched describes a sound with a very high tone, while shrill adds that it is unpleasantly loud and forceful.
  • strident
    7.2 2.13/m
    strident sounds are loud and harsh in tone, often forceful or aggressive, while shrill stresses high, piercing pitch.
  • sharp
    3.2 2.16/m
    sharp sounds have a clear, cutting high tone and sudden edge, while shrill feels more unpleasantly loud and piercing.
  • shrieky
    7.2 0.01/m
    shrieky voices are high and sharp like a sudden cry, while shrill is broader and more piercing and unpleasant.
  • screamy
    8.2 0.01/m
    screamy sounds are loud and like a scream, often harsh, while shrill focuses on high, piercing pitch that pains the ear.
  • squawky
    7.5 0.03/m
    squawky noises remind of a bird's harsh cry and feel rough, while shrill is more high, piercing, and sharply loud.
  • squeaky
    1.3 2.46/m
    squeaky sounds are thin, small, and often short like a hinge, while shrill is stronger, piercing, and more unpleasant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mellow - (of a sound) soft, rich, and pleasant
  • synonym: piercing - having a sharp or intense quality, especially a sound
  • synonym: strident - loud and harsh; grating
