swail

verb
to sway gently
9.9
Pronunciation: / sweɪl / (AmE), / sweɪl / (BrE)
to move or sway gently, especially when blown by the wind

Examples

Reeds swail in wind
The tall reeds swail gracefully along the edge of the lake.
In the gentle breeze, the curtains began to swail softly.
She watched the wildflowers swail in the afternoon sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flutter - to move lightly and quickly up and down or from side to side
  • antonym: stand still - to remain motionless without moving
  • synonym: sway - to move slowly or rhythmically back and forth
