beady-eyed

adjective
having small, gleaming, suspicious-looking eyes
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːdi ˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbiːdi ˌaɪd / (BrE)
having small, round, gleaming eyes that often suggest suspicion, alertness, or malice

Examples

A beady-eyed bird
A beady-eyed bird watched us from the fence as we ate our lunch.
She grew suspicious of her beady-eyed coworker, who always seemed to know everyone’s secrets.
The detective fixed him with a beady-eyed stare, searching for any hint of a lie.
