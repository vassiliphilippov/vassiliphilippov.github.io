Pisum arvense

noun
a pea plant grown for forage
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪzəm ɑrˈvɛns / (AmE), / ˈpiːzəm ɑːˈvɛnsɪ / (BrE)
A variety of pea plant native to the Mediterranean region and North Africa, widely grown for use as forage.

Examples

Pisum arvense forage plant
Pisum arvense is often cultivated as a high-protein forage crop for livestock.
In some regions, Pisum arvense is valued for its ability to enrich the soil with nitrogen.
Researchers are studying the drought resistance of Pisum arvense compared to other legumes.
