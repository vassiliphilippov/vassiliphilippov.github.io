central chimpanzee

noun
chimpanzee subspecies from central Africa
1.0 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.trəl tʃɪm.ˈpæn.ziː/ /; / /ˈsen.trəl tʃɪm.ˈpən.ziː/ /; / /ˈsen.trəl tʃɪm.pæn.ˈziː/ (AmE), /ˈsen.trəl tʃɪm.pæn.ˈziː/ /; / /ˈsen.trəl tʃɪm.ˈpæn.ziː/ (BrE)
A subspecies of chimpanzee (Pan troglodytes troglodytes) with a black face, native to central Africa and closely related to eastern chimpanzees.

Examples

Central chimpanzees in Africa
Researchers have discovered new behaviors among central chimpanzees not observed in other subspecies.
The central chimpanzee is known for inhabiting the dense forests of central Africa.
The population of the central chimpanzee is threatened by habitat loss and poaching.
