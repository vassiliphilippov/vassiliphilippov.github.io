wash up

verb
become completely exhausted or worn out
2.7 6.0
Pronunciation: /wɑːʃ ʌp/ /; / /wɔːʃ ʌp/ /; / /wɔːrʃ ʌp/ (AmE), /wɒʃ ʌp/ /; / /wɔːʃ ʌp/ (BrE)
(informal) To become completely exhausted, worn out, or unable to continue effectively, especially due to fatigue or overwork.

Examples

Completely washed up
After hours of hiking, I was completely washed up and could barely stand.
By the end of the long week, she felt mentally washed up and needed a break.
Many athletes retire young because they're physically washed up after years of intense training.
