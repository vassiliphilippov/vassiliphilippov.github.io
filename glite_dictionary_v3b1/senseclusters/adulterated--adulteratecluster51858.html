adulterated

adjective
made impure by adding inferior substances
8.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdʌl.tə.reɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdʌl.tə.reɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
(of a substance) made impure by adding inferior or improper ingredients.

Examples

Adulterated food products
Many consumers prefer organic products to avoid adulterated foods.
The laboratory detected several adulterated samples among the spices imported from overseas.
The milk was found to be adulterated with water, posing a risk to public health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contaminated - having been made impure by exposure to or addition of a poisonous or polluting substance
  • antonym: pure - not mixed with any other substance; clean or unadulterated
