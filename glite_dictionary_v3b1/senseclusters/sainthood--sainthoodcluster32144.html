sainthood

noun
condition of being a saint
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪnthʊd / (AmE), / ˈseɪnthʊd / (BrE)
the status or condition of being recognized as a saint, especially in a religious context

Examples

Awarded sainthood
He was posthumously awarded sainthood for his lifelong dedication to others.
Her acts of compassion led some people to speak of her eventual sainthood.
The process of achieving sainthood can take many years within the Catholic Church.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canonization - the official act of declaring someone a saint
