blacktail jackrabbit

noun
North American hare with black ears and tail
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌteɪl ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (AmE), / ˈblækteɪl ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (BrE)
a large hare of western North America, especially grasslands, with long black-tipped ears and a black streak on its tail

Examples

The blacktail jackrabbit runs
The blacktail jackrabbit can outrun many of its predators in the wild.
The long ears of the blacktail jackrabbit help it detect danger from a distance.
We spotted a blacktail jackrabbit hopping through the tall prairie grass at dawn.
