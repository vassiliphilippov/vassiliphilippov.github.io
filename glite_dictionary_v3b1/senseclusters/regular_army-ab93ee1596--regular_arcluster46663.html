regular army

noun
permanent military land forces
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr ˈɑrmi / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
the permanent, official military land forces of a country, distinct from reserve or irregular troops

Examples

The regular army forces
After completing his training, he joined the regular army and was posted overseas.
The government deployed the regular army to restore order after the unrest.
Unlike the reserve forces, the regular army remains active during peacetime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irregular forces - military forces not part of a regular army, such as guerrillas or partisans
  • antonym: militia - a military force raised from the civil population to supplement a regular army
  • synonym: standing army - a permanent army of professional soldiers maintained even in peacetime
