coarse

adjective
rough in texture; large particles
3.7 4.21/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrs / (AmE), / k…îÀês / (BrE)
having a rough texture or made of large particles; not fine or smooth; of low or inferior quality

Examples

Coarse sand
She sifted out the coarse grains from the flour.
The rug felt coarse under my bare feet.
We used coarse sand to improve drainage in the garden soil.
Only coarse materials were used in the construction of the inexpensive housing.
The craftsman refused to work with coarse wood, preferring more refined materials.
The fabric was of such coarse quality that it wore out after just a few weeks.

Synonyms

  • coarse-grained
    4.8 0.01/m
    coarse-grained describes a surface with large visible grains, while coarse means rough or of low quality more broadly.
  • large-grained
    3.9 0.00/m
    large-grained refers to texture with relatively big, easily seen particles, while coarse means rough and not fine.
  • rough-textured
    3.7 0.04/m
    rough-textured means a surface that feels uneven or bumpy, while coarse also suggests large particles or low quality.
  • grainy
    3.7 2.65/m
    grainy means having many small grain-like particles or a speckled look, while coarse implies larger particles and roughness.
  • gritty
    4.1 1.26/m
    gritty means covered with hard small particles and feels sand-like, while coarse suggests larger, rough particles or low quality.
  • rough
    1.0 14/m
    rough describes anything not smooth, including surfaces or times, while coarse focuses on texture with large particles or low quality.
