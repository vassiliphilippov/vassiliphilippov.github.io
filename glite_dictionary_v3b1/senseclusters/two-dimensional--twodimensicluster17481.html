two-dimensional

adjective
lacking depth; flat
6.4 4.5
Pronunciation: / tuː dɪˈmɛnʃənəl /; / ˌtuː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (AmE), / tuː dɪˈmɛnʃənəl /; / ˌtjuː.dɪˈmɛn.ʃən.əl / (BrE)
lacking depth or complexity; not having substance or detail

Examples

Two-dimensional character
Her description of the city felt two-dimensional, missing any sense of atmosphere or life.
Modern art sometimes intentionally uses two-dimensional forms to challenge the viewer's perception.

He tends to write two-dimensional female characters who lack emotional depth.
The critics found the villain of the story to be too two-dimensional and predictable.
The scenery in the play was very two-dimensional, with simple painted backdrops.
