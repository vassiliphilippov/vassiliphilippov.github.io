territorialization

noun
process of organizing as a territory
Pronunciation: / təˌrɪtɔːriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / təˌrɪtəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of organizing, defining, or demarcating an area as a territory, often for administrative, political, or social purposes.

Examples

Territorialization of land
Debates about territorialization arise when indigenous lands are formalized by national authorities.
The government's territorialization of the region resulted in the creation of new administrative districts.
Urban development often involves the territorialization of previously unregulated spaces.
The territorialisation of land by the government affected the traditional ways of local communities.
The process of territorialisation can result in new borders and regulations within a region.
Urban territorialisation often shapes how neighborhoods develop distinct identities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deterritorialization - the process of undoing territorial organization
  • synonym: territorialization - the American spelling of territorialisation; the act of organising as a territory
