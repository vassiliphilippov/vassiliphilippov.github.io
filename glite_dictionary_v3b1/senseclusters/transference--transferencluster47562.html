transference

noun
psychoanalytic shifting of feelings to another
8.4 0.41/m

(psychoanalysis) the process in which feelings or desires originally associated with one person, especially a parent, are unconsciously shifted onto another person, such as a therapist.

Examples

Emotional transference
Classic cases of transference often involve patients shifting childhood emotions onto their therapists.
During her therapy sessions, she experienced intense transference toward her analyst.
The psychologist carefully addressed the issue of transference to help the patient understand her feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: projection - the unconscious transfer of one's own feelings to another
