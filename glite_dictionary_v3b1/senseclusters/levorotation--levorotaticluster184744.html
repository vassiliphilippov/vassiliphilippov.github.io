levorotation

noun
rotation to the left (esp. of polarized light)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlɛvəroʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌliːvəʊrəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
The rotation of the plane of polarized light to the left (counterclockwise) as viewed towards the light source.

Examples

Levorotation of light
A substance showing levorotation is described as levorotatory in chemical terminology.
Certain organic compounds cause levorotation of polarized light, a key feature in stereochemistry.
The sugar solution exhibited levorotation when tested with a polarimeter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dextrorotation - rotation of polarized light to the right
