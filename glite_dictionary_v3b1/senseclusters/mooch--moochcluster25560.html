mooch

noun
person who tries to get things for free
6.3 7.0
Pronunciation: /muːtʃ/ (AmE), /muːtʃ/ (BrE)
an informal term for a person who habitually tries to obtain things, especially food or money, from others without paying or offering anything in return

Examples

A mooch at lunch
Every group of friends seems to have one mooch who always tags along for free.
He's such a mooch; he never pays for his own meals.
I got tired of being a mooch and decided to buy my own coffee for a change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scrounger - a person who borrows or lives at the expense of others
