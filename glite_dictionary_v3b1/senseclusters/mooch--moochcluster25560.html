mooch

noun
person who tries to get things for free
8.8 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / muÀêt É / (AmE), / muÀêt É / (BrE)
an informal term for a person who habitually tries to obtain things, especially food or money, from others without paying or offering anything in return

Examples

A mooch at lunch
Every group of friends seems to have one mooch who always tags along for free.
He's such a mooch; he never pays for his own meals.
I got tired of being a mooch and decided to buy my own coffee for a change.

Synonyms

  • freeloader
    6.1 1.22/m
    a freeloader takes advantage of others' generosity without returning anything, while a mooch is a more casual, joking term for the same habit.
  • scrounger
    7.6 0.07/m
    a scrounger finds free food or goods by relying on others or chance, while a mooch usually asks people directly for handouts.
  • sponge
    7.2 0.19/m
    a sponge uses others' hospitality and gives little back, while a mooch more often asks directly for free food or money.
  • cadger
    9.9 0.01/m
    a cadger keeps asking politely for small things like food or money, while a mooch is a more general informal taker who freeloads.
  • schnorrer
    9.9 0.02/m
    a schnorrer habitually begs or asks others for money or food and appears in Yiddish-influenced speech, while a mooch is the general informal term.
  • leech
    8.1 0.31/m
    a leech clings to others and takes advantage in a harmful, draining way, while a mooch mainly asks casually for free food or money.
