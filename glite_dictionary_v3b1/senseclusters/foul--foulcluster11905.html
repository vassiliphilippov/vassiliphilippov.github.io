foul

adjective
nautical: entangled, not clear (ropes, anchors, etc.)
8.2 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / faÊŠl / (AmE), / faÊŠl / (BrE)
(nautical) Entangled or not clear; especially of ropes, cables, or anchors that are twisted or caught so they do not function freely.

Examples

Foul anchor
A foul anchor can make it difficult to set sail quickly.
After the storm, several ropes aboard were foul and had to be cleared by the crew.
The fishing net was foul and needed to be carefully untangled.
