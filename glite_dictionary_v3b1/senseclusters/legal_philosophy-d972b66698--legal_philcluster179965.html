legal philosophy

noun
philosophy of law and legal principles
Pronunciation: / ˈliɡəl fəˈlɑsəfi / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
the branch of philosophy that deals with the nature of law and the principles underlying legal systems and court decisions

Examples

Legal philosophy debates
Debates in legal philosophy often revolve around the relationship between law and morality.
He decided to specialize in legal philosophy after taking a course on constitutional law.
The work of John Austin was influential in the development of modern legal philosophy.
