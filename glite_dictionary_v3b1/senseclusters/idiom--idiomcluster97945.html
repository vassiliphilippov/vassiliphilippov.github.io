idiom

noun
expression with non-literal meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəm / (BrE)
a group of words whose meaning is different from the meanings of the individual words (a fixed expression with a special meaning)

Examples

Common idiom in English
Learning new idioms can be challenging for students of English.
She used a common idiom to express her feelings.
The phrase "kick the bucket" is an idiom meaning to die.
Every language has its own set of idiom that can be difficult for learners to master.
He struggled to understand the idiom used by British speakers.
Her writing style is rich in the native idiom of her region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expression - a word or phrase with a particular meaning
  • synonym: phrase - a small group of words standing together as a conceptual unit
