Sabellianism

noun
Christian doctrine of one-person God
9.9
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈbel.i.ə.nɪz.əm/ (AmE), /sə.ˈbel.i.ə.nɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the Christian doctrine that God is a single person who manifests in different forms or modes, rather than as three distinct persons

Examples

Sabellianism doctrine
Early church leaders debated the teachings of Sabellianism and its view of God's nature.
Many theologians wrote extensively to refute Sabellianism in the third century.
Sabellianism is sometimes called modalism because of its explanation of the Trinity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modalism - the belief that God is one person appearing in different modes
  • antonym: trinitarianism - the belief that God exists as three distinct persons
