concord

noun
agreement; harmony (esp. of opinions)
6.7 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀênÀåk…îrd /; / Ààk…ën.k…îrd / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.k…îÀêd / (BrE)
agreement or harmony between people, groups, or elements; a state of being in accord

Examples

Social concord
Debates often threaten concord, but mutual respect can restore it.
The treaty established lasting concord between the two nations.
There was a rare sense of concord among the team members after the meeting.
In his paintings, the artist seeks a concord of form and emotion.
The interior decorator achieved concord with a perfect balance of colors and textures throughout the room.
Their voices blended in such concord that the audience was moved to tears.

Synonyms

  • accord
    4.0 3.90/m
    accord is a formal state of agreement between parties, while concord is a general harmony between groups or elements.
  • agreement
    1.0 26/m
    agreement is the common, everyday word for sharing the same opinion, while concord is a more formal harmony or peaceful accord.
  • concordance
    7.7 1.15/m
    concordance is a formal or technical term for agreement in texts or data, while concord is broader harmony among people or elements.
  • harmony
    2.5 11/m
    harmony means smooth compatibility or pleasant balance of elements, while concord focuses on agreement or peaceful relations between people or groups.
  • consensus
    4.1 26/m
    consensus is a collective decision or general opinion reached by a group, while concord is a broader state of agreement or harmony.
