concord

noun
agreement; harmony (esp. of opinions)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnˌkɔrd /; / ˈkɑn.kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kɔːd / (BrE)
agreement or harmony between people, groups, or elements; a state of being in accord

Examples

Social concord
Debates often threaten concord, but mutual respect can restore it.
The treaty established lasting concord between the two nations.
There was a rare sense of concord among the team members after the meeting.
In his paintings, the artist seeks a concord of form and emotion.
The interior decorator achieved concord with a perfect balance of colors and textures throughout the room.
Their voices blended in such concord that the audience was moved to tears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agreement - harmony or consensus among people
  • antonym: discord - disagreement or lack of harmony
  • synonym: harmony - a pleasing combination or arrangement of parts
