exec

noun
business executive
6.7
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛk / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛk / (BrE)
a person who holds a high-level management or executive position in a company or organization (informal)

Examples

A top exec
The exec approved the new marketing strategy after a brief discussion.
Young professionals often aspire to become a top exec in their field.
Several execs attended the annual conference to network and share ideas.
The exec called a meeting to discuss the company's future plans.
After years of hard work, she finally became an exec at a major tech firm.
Several execs attended the conference to network with industry leaders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: employee - a person employed for wages or salary, especially at non-managerial level
  • synonym: executive - a person with senior managerial responsibility in a business
  • synonym: manager - a person responsible for controlling or administering an organization or group of staff
