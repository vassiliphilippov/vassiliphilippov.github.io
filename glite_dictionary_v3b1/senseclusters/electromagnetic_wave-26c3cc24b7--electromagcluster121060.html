electromagnetic wave

noun
wave of energy from electric and magnetic fields
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊmægˌnɛtɪk weɪv / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmægˌnɛtɪk weɪv / (BrE)
A wave of energy consisting of oscillating electric and magnetic fields, which moves through space at the speed of light.

Examples

An electromagnetic wave travels
An electromagnetic wave can travel through a vacuum, unlike a sound wave.
Light, radio waves, and X-rays are all forms of electromagnetic wave.
The theory of Maxwell explained that an electromagnetic wave travels at the speed of light.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gamma ray - a type of electromagnetic wave with a very short wavelength and high energy
  • synonym: light wave - visible part of the electromagnetic spectrum
  • degree lower: radio wave - a type of electromagnetic wave with a long wavelength
