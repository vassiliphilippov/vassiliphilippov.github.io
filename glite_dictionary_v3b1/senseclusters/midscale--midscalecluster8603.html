midscale

noun
moderate-priced business
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌskeɪl / (BrE)
a business or establishment, especially a hotel or restaurant, that offers moderate prices and services between budget and luxury levels

Examples

A midscale restaurant
They chose a midscale for their vacation to balance comfort and affordability.
Many business travelers prefer a midscale because it offers good value and reliable service.
The city has a wide range of hotels, from budget to midscale to luxury options.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: budget - a business or service offering the lowest prices and basic amenities
  • degree higher: luxury - a business or service offering the highest prices and premium amenities
