subsea

adjective
under the sea
5.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àås åbÀàsiÀê / (AmE), / Àås åbÀàsiÀê / (BrE)
located, happening, or used under the sea, especially relating to underwater engineering or exploration

Examples

Subsea pipeline
The engineers designed a subsea pipeline to transport oil from the offshore platform.
Special equipment is required for subsea exploration at great depths.
The company specializes in subsea cable installation for international communications.

Synonyms

  • undersea
    4.1 1.22/m
    undersea describes things beneath the sea in general texts, while subsea is the technical word engineers use on projects.
  • submarine
    4.9 0.00/m
    submarine often serves as an adjective or a vessel name in speech, while subsea specifically refers to engineering under the sea.
  • underwater
    1.0 8.52/m
    underwater describes anything below any water surface, while subsea refers more narrowly to work or equipment under the sea.
