precept

noun
rule or principle of conduct
8.9 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌsɛpt / (AmE), / ˈpriː.sɛpt /; / ˈpriːsɛpt / (BrE)
a rule or principle that guides behavior or thought, especially in religion, philosophy, or general life

Examples

Central precepts of philosophy
One of the central precepts of his philosophy is kindness toward others.
She tried to live her life according to the Buddhist precepts.
The school is founded on the precept that all students deserve respect.
He lived by the precept that honesty is always the best policy.
Many religions are founded on basic precepts that guide their followers' behavior.
The teacher reminded the students to follow the school's precepts at all times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commandment - an authoritative rule, especially a religious one
  • synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a group
  • degree higher: law - a system of rules created and enforced by authority; a stronger or more formal rule than a precept
  • synonym: principle - a fundamental truth or proposition serving as the foundation for belief or action
  • degree lower: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration; a less forceful rule or guideline compared to a precept
