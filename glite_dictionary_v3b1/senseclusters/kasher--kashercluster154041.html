kasher

verb
to make kosher
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːʃər /; / ˈkɑʃər / (AmE), / ˈkæʃə / (BrE)
to make food or utensils suitable for use according to Jewish dietary law

Examples

To kasher a kitchen
Before Passover, families kasher their kitchens to remove any traces of leavened bread.
The rabbi helped the community kasher the new restaurant's cooking equipment.
It can take several hours to properly kasher all the utensils in a large kitchen.
Before Passover, families often kasher their kitchens to meet religious requirements.
The rabbi explained how to kasher utensils that had been used with non-kosher food.
They hired a professional to kasher the restaurant's equipment before opening.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defile - to make unclean or impure
  • synonym: purify - to make clean or free from impurities
