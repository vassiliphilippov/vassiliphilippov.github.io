minor

adjective
less important or significant
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
less important, serious, or significant; smaller in size, amount, or effect

Examples

Minor problems
Despite some minor disagreements, they quickly reached a consensus.
Fortunately, the accident caused just minor injuries to the passengers.
The committee made only minor changes to the original proposal.
Even as a minor celebrity, she attracted some attention from the press.
His new book covers some minor aspects of the subject that are often overlooked.
They faced only minor challenges during the project, so everything went smoothly.
He played a minor role in the whole affair.
Only a minor portion of the funds was allocated to research.
The differences between the two models are minor and hardly noticeable.
