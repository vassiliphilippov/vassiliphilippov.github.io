Synonyms

    1.7 0.6/m
    lesser is used when comparing size, amount, or rank, while minor describes something lower in importance or degree.
  • slight
    1.2 40/m
    slight describes a small degree or amount, often of change, while minor means less important or serious across contexts.
  • small
    1.0 500/m
    small refers mainly to size or amount and feels physical or measurable, while minor highlights less importance or effect.
  • trivial
    3.9 9/m
    trivial suggests something unimportant and slightly petty, while minor can mean simply less serious or smaller in scale.
  • insignificant
    4.1 7/m
    insignificant implies no real effect or value and often sounds dismissive, while minor can still be somewhat important or relevant.
  • negligible
    5.6 4/m
    negligible means so small it is not worth considering, while minor can be small but sometimes still deserves attention.
