swelling

noun
increase in volume of a substance when heated
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈswɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈswɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
an increase in the volume of a substance, typically when heated, often accompanied by the release of water

Examples

Dough swelling
Bread dough undergoes significant swelling in the oven due to steam release.
During the experiment, the swelling of the polymer was measured after heating.
Metals may exhibit slight swelling when exposed to extremely high temperatures.
