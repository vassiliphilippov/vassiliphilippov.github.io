jabber

noun
rapid, indistinct speech
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæb.ə / (BrE)
rapid and indistinct speech that is difficult to understand

Examples

Loud jabber
He couldn't make sense of the children's jabber in the playroom.
Over coffee, their morning jabber filled the quiet café.
The announcer's excited jabber was hard to follow after the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - speech that is foolish, excited, or incoherent
  • synonym: mumble - speak or say something indistinctly
