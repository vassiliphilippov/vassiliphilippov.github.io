prewire

verb
to prepare in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈwaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈwaɪə / (BrE)
to prepare or condition someone or something in advance to react or behave in a particular way

Examples

To prewire a team
The coach prewired the players to stay calm under pressure during the finals.
Parents often try to prewire their children to respond politely in social situations.
The training program prewires employees to handle customer complaints effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condition - to train or influence someone to behave in a certain way
  • synonym: prime - to prepare someone for a particular situation or reaction
