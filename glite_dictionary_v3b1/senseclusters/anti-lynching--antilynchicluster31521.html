anti-lynching

noun
effort against lynching
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈlɪntʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈlɪntʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a law, bill, campaign, or organized effort aimed at preventing lynching or punishing those responsible for it

Examples

Anti-lynching campaign
The senator introduced an anti-lynching bill to address racial violence in the country.
Activists organized an anti-lynching campaign to raise awareness about the issue.
The museum displayed documents from early anti-lynching movements in American history.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lynching - the illegal killing of someone by a mob, often by hanging
