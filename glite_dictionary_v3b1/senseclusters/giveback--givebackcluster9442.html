giveback

noun
returned payment or benefit
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪvˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈɡɪvˌbæk / (BrE)
a payment, benefit, or rebate returned to customers, investors, or the public, often as a special reward or compensation

Examples

Customer giveback
The company announced a giveback to reward loyal customers with a special discount.
Shareholders were pleased with the unexpected giveback in the form of a cash dividend.
The government offered a tax giveback to stimulate consumer spending during the recession.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deduction - an amount subtracted from a total, often the opposite of a giveback
  • synonym: rebate - a partial refund to someone who has paid too much
  • synonym: refund - money returned to a payer
