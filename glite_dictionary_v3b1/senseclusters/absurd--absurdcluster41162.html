absurd

adjective
wildly unreasonable or ridiculous
Pronunciation: / əbˈsɝd / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːd / (BrE)
wildly unreasonable, illogical, or ridiculous

Examples

An absurd idea
His excuse for missing the meeting was completely absurd and nobody believed him.
It was absurd to suggest that aliens had built the pyramids.
The idea of running a marathon without any training is simply absurd.
Her absurd outfit drew everyone's attention at the party.
It would be absurd to suggest he could finish the project in one day.
The idea that the Earth is flat is simply absurd.

Related Terms

  • antonym: logical - Reasonable or following a clear reasoning process; not absurd.
  • synonym: ludicrous - So foolish, unreasonable, or out of place as to be amusing; ridiculous.
  • antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with logic or reason
  • synonym: ridiculous - Deserving or inviting mockery or derision; absurd.
