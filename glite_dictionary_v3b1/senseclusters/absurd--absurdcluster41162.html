absurd

adjective
wildly unreasonable or ridiculous
4.4 17/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàs…ùd / (AmE), / …ôbÀàs…úÀêd / (BrE)
wildly unreasonable, illogical, or ridiculous

Examples

An absurd idea
His excuse for missing the meeting was completely absurd and nobody believed him.
It was absurd to suggest that aliens had built the pyramids.
The idea of running a marathon without any training is simply absurd.
Her absurd outfit drew everyone's attention at the party.
It would be absurd to suggest he could finish the project in one day.
The idea that the Earth is flat is simply absurd.

Synonyms

  • ridiculous
    1.6 42/m
    ridiculous is extremely silly and invites laughter, while absurd describes something wildly unreasonable or hard to believe.
  • preposterous
    7.3 3.59/m
    preposterous is so contrary to common sense it sounds shocking, while absurd covers many kinds of wild or silly ideas.
  • ludicrous
    6.0 5.46/m
    ludicrous feels so silly and amusing that people laugh, while absurd often stresses being wildly unreasonable or illogical.
  • nonsensical
    5.5 2.86/m
    nonsensical means it makes no sense and may be meaningless, while absurd often adds a sense of strange or laughable unreason.
  • illogical
    4.8 2.92/m
    illogical does not follow clear reasoning and sounds neutral, while absurd adds surprise, oddness, or a sense of being ridiculous.
  • irrational
    4.4 12/m
    irrational shows a lack of reason or sense and suits formal discussion, while absurd reads more like a strong everyday judgment.
  • laughable
    4.1 3.89/m
    laughable makes people want to laugh because it is weak or silly, while absurd stresses being wildly unreasonable or extreme.
  • cockeyed
    8.9 0.19/m
    cockeyed describes a foolish or poorly thought idea in informal speech, while absurd covers both silly and strongly unreasonable things.

Related Terms

  • antonym: logical - Reasonable or following a clear reasoning process; not absurd.

  • antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with logic or reason
  • synonym: ridiculous - Deserving or inviting mockery or derision; absurd.
