benevolent dictator

noun
open-source project leader with final say
7.5
Pronunciation: /bə.ˈnev.əl.ənt dɪk.ˈteɪ.tər/ (AmE), /bə.ˈnev.əl.ənt dɪk.ˈteɪ.tə/ (BrE)
(in software, especially open-source projects) a project leader who has final authority over decisions about the project's direction, often informally for a long time.

Examples

Benevolent dictator leads a project
When disagreements grew, the community deferred to the project's benevolent dictator to make the final call.
Many credited her as the benevolent dictator who guided the project's design decisions for years.
After several governance debates, contributors agreed to move away from a benevolent dictator model toward a steering committee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: BDFL - abbreviation of 'benevolent dictator for life', used for a project leader who has final authority.
  • synonym: project maintainer - a person who manages and makes releases for a software project; may have final technical authority in practice.
