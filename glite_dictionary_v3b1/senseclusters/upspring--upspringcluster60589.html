upspring

verb
to jump upward
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
to jump or spring upward suddenly or quickly

Examples

Deer upspring quickly
The deer would upspring from the grass when startled by a noise.
At the sound of the alarm, he upsprang from his bed in surprise.
Flowers upspring in the meadow as soon as spring arrives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leap - to jump or spring a long way
  • synonym: rise - to move upward or get up
  • antonym: sink - to go down below the surface or to a lower position
