hunch

noun
an intuitive feeling or suspicion
Pronunciation: / hʌntʃ / (AmE), / hʌntʃ / (BrE)
a feeling or guess based on intuition rather than known facts

Examples

A lucky hunch
Even without evidence, the detective followed his hunch and solved the case.
Her hunch about the weather proved to be correct.
I had a hunch that he wasn't telling the whole truth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - something that is known to be true or sure
  • synonym: intuition - the ability to understand something instinctively without conscious reasoning
  • synonym: suspicion - a feeling or belief that something is likely or true
