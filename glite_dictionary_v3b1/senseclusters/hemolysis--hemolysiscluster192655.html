hemolysis

noun
the breakdown of red blood cells
Pronunciation: / hɪˈmɑləsɪs /; / hiːˈmɑːləsɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈmɒləsɪs /; / hiːˈmɒləsɪs / (BrE)
the destruction of red blood cells, releasing hemoglobin into the surrounding fluid

Examples

Hemolysis of red cells
Severe infections can sometimes lead to hemolysis and anemia in patients.
The laboratory tested the blood sample for evidence of hemolysis.
Visible hemolysis in a blood tube may affect the accuracy of some lab tests.
Certain medications increase the risk of haemolysis in sensitive individuals.
Severe infections can cause haemolysis and lead to anemia.
The laboratory test showed signs of haemolysis in the sample.
