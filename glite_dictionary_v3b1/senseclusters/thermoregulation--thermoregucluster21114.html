thermoregulation

noun
body temperature control
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an organism maintains its body temperature within a certain range despite environmental changes

Examples

Animal thermoregulation
Birds use thermoregulation to keep their bodies warm during cold winter nights.
Proper thermoregulation is essential for athletes performing in extreme temperatures.
Some reptiles rely on external heat sources for thermoregulation because they cannot generate their own body heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeostasis - the maintenance of stable internal conditions in an organism
  • antonym: hyperthermia - abnormally high body temperature
  • antonym: hypothermia - dangerously low body temperature
