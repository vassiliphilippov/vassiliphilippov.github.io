time

noun
fourth dimension in physics
1.0
Pronunciation: / taɪm / (AmE), / taɪm / (BrE)
the ongoing, continuous progress of existence and events, or the measurement of this by clocks or in physics

Examples

The passage of time

According to relativity, time is as fundamental as space in describing physical phenomena.
Physicists model the universe using three dimensions of space and one of time.

He didn't realize how quickly time passed until it was too late.
Many philosophers have tried to understand the nature of time.
I lost track of time while reading and missed my train.
She glanced at her watch and saw the time was nearly midnight.
What time does the meeting start?

  • synonym: hour - unit of time equal to 60 minutes
  • degree lower: moment - a very brief period of time
  • synonym: space - the three physical dimensions (length, width, height) that, with time, define physical reality
