Synonyms

  • absorb
    3.0 20/m
    absorb takes in liquids, energy, or ideas steadily and widely, while imbibe often means to drink or receive ideas in a formal way.
  • drink
    1.0 71/m
    drink is the everyday word for swallowing liquids, while imbibe feels more formal or literary and often names drinking alcohol.
  • take in
    3.2 2.50/m
    take in means to understand or learn information, while imbibe often suggests absorbing ideas or drinking in a more formal tone.
  • soak up
    3.9 0.91/m
    soak up means to enjoy and absorb an atmosphere or information fully, while imbibe is more formal and covers both drinking and ideas.
  • drink in
    5.1 0.86/m
    drink in is to look at or enjoy something slowly and deeply, while imbibe also means to drink or absorb ideas.
