imbibe

verb
absorb and retain (ideas, etc.) in the mind
9.9 7.4
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbaɪb / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaɪb /; / ɪmˈbʌɪb / (BrE)
to drink (especially alcohol) or to absorb ideas, knowledge, or principles (literal and figurative use)

Examples

To imbibe knowledge
It is important to imbibe the lessons of history and avoid repeating past mistakes.
She traveled the world to imbibe new cultures and perspectives.
Young students are encouraged to imbibe the values of honesty and respect from an early age.
Guests were invited to imbibe a special punch created for the event.
On a hot day, hikers should imbibe plenty of water to stay hydrated.
The team gathered at the pub to imbibe and celebrate their victory.
During the seminar, the students imbibed knowledge from the experienced speakers.
He imbibes not only fine wine but also new philosophies during his travels.
She eagerly imbibed every detail of the new art movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take in and assimilate (ideas or information)
  • synonym: assimilate - to take in and fully understand (information or ideas)
  • synonym: drink - to take liquid into the mouth and swallow
  • degree higher: guzzle - to drink greedily or rapidly
