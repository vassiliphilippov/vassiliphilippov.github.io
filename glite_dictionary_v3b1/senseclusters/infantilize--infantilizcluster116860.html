infantilize

verb
to treat as a child
8.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfæntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪnˈfæntɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone as if they are a child or less mature than they really are

Examples

To infantilize adults
Some managers unintentionally infantilize their employees by not allowing them to make decisions.
Parents should avoid infantilizing teenagers who are capable of handling responsibility.
The policy was criticized for infantilizing adults with disabilities instead of supporting their independence.
Some teachers unintentionally infantilize their students by not allowing them to make decisions.
The new policy was criticized for infantilizing adults who are capable of managing their own affairs.
Parents should avoid infantilizing their teenagers if they want them to become independent.
Some teachers unintentionally infantilize their students by not allowing them to make decisions.
The policy tends to infantilize adults with disabilities instead of supporting their independence.
Critics argue that the new advertising campaign infantilizes the product's audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empower - to give someone more control or power over their own life
  • synonym: patronize - to treat someone in a way that shows you think you are more intelligent or important
