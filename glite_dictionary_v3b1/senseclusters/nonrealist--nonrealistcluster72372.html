nonrealist

adjective
not realistic
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈriːəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈrɪəlɪst / (BrE)
not representing or concerned with realism, especially in art, literature, or philosophy; rejecting realistic depiction or theory

Examples

Nonrealist art style
The director's nonrealist approach gave the play a dreamlike and symbolic atmosphere.
Many modern artists prefer a nonrealist style that emphasizes emotion over accurate representation.
Her nonrealist philosophy challenges the idea that art must reflect the real world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstract - not representing external reality; existing in thought or as an idea
  • antonym: realist - representing things as they really are; concerned with realism
