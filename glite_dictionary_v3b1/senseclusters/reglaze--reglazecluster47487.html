reglaze

verb
to apply new glaze
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɡleɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɡleɪz / (BrE)
to apply a new layer of glaze to an object, such as pottery or windows, to restore or improve its surface

Examples

To reglaze pottery
The potter decided to reglaze the bowl after noticing some imperfections in the finish.
After years of use, the old windows were reglazed to improve their appearance and insulation.
You can reglaze your bathtub to make it look brand new without replacing it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glaze - to cover with a smooth, shiny coating
  • antonym: strip - to remove a layer from a surface
