put-up job

noun
secretly arranged deception or trick
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpʊt ʌp ˈdʒɑb / (AmE), / ˌpʊt ʌp ˈdʒɒb / (BrE)
a scheme or action that is secretly arranged to deceive or trick people, often making something appear accidental or spontaneous when it is actually planned

Examples

A put-up job scheme
Many believed the evidence was a put-up job to cover up the real story.
She accused the opposition of pulling a put-up job to discredit her reputation.
The investigation revealed that the leak was actually a put-up job orchestrated by insiders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frame-up - a scheme to falsely make someone appear guilty of a crime
  • antonym: genuine - not fake or arranged; the opposite of a deception
  • synonym: setup - an arrangement intended to deceive someone, often to make them appear guilty
