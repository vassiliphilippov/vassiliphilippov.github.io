normalizer

noun
person or device that standardizes
2.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈnɔːr.mə.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈnɔː.mə.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a person or device (including software or hardware components) that brings data, values, or processes to a standard or normal state; in computing, this includes standardizing or scaling input values, features, or channels to a chosen format or numeric range.

Examples

A normalizer device
A normalizer in database systems helps maintain data consistency.
Many audio editors use an automatic normalizer to balance volume levels across tracks.
The software includes a normalizer that adjusts input images for consistent brightness.
As the chief normaliser, she ensured that lab results were consistent across all trials.
He acted as a normaliser, helping both teams agree on common procedures.
In post-conflict zones, a normaliser works to restore stability to society.
The data pipeline uses a normaliser to scale each feature before training the neural network.
Our audio engineer connected the new normaliser to prevent sudden volume spikes during recording sessions.
After ingesting logs, the ETL job applies a normaliser to standardize timestamps and numeric fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: normalize - to bring to a standard or normal condition
  • synonym: normalizer - variant spelling (American English)
  • synonym: scaler - a function or tool that rescales numeric features, especially in machine learning preprocessing.
