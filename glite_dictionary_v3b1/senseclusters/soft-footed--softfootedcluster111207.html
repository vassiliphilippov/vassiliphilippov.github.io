soft-footed

adjective
moving with quiet, gentle steps
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔft ˌfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsɒft ˌfʊtɪd / (BrE)
Moving with quiet and gentle steps; making little or no noise when walking.

Examples

A soft-footed step
He was so soft-footed that he could sneak up on birds without startling them.
The soft-footed cat slipped silently across the room, unnoticed by everyone.
The soft-footed nurse entered the patient's room to avoid waking him.
