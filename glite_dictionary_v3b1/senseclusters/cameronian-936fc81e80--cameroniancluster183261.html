Cameronian

adjective
of Scottish Cameronians
Pronunciation: / ˌkæməˈroʊniən / (AmE), / ˌkæməˈrəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the Cameronians, a Scottish Presbyterian sect founded by Richard Cameron, or to the historic infantry regiment named after them

Examples

Cameronian beliefs
The Cameronian tradition emphasized strict religious discipline and independence from state control.
He wore a Cameronian badge to honor his family's historical roots.
Many Cameronian values influenced the development of Scottish religious life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Presbyterian - relating to a branch of Protestant Christianity governed by elders
