intellectuality

noun
quality of intellect
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˌlɛktʃuˈæləti /; / ˌɪntəlɛk.tʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪntəlɛktʃuˈælɪti /; / ˌɪntəlɛk.tjuˈæləti / (BrE)
the quality, state, or culture of being intellectual or having a highly developed intellect

Examples

The intellectuality of arguments
The debate was notable for the intellectuality of the participants' arguments.
She admired the intellectuality of the university's academic environment.
His writing displays a level of intellectuality that challenges readers to think deeply.
The professor's intellectuality impressed some students but intimidated others.
Critics accused the novel of excessive intellectuality that overshadowed its emotional impact.
Her speeches were marked by a certain intellectuality that appealed to academic audiences.
The university is known for its atmosphere of intellectuality and lively academic debates.
She admired the city's intellectuality, which was evident in its many museums and libraries.
A strong sense of intellectuality attracted scholars from around the world to the conference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anti-intellectualism - opposition to or distrust of intellectuals and intellectual pursuits
  • synonym: intellectualism - the practice of emphasizing reason and knowledge
  • synonym: intelligence - the ability to learn, understand, and think
  • antonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or common sense
