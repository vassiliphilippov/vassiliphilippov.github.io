owl

noun
nocturnal bird of prey with large head and front-facing eyes
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / aʊl / (AmE), / aʊl / (BrE)
A nocturnal bird of prey with a hawk-like beak, sharp claws, and a large head with front-facing eyes, known for its silent flight and distinctive hoot.

Examples

A owl at night
At night, an owl perched on the old barn roof, hooting softly into the darkness.
She was fascinated by the owl’s ability to turn its head almost all the way around.
The wise owl is a popular symbol in many cultures and fairy tales.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eagle - large powerful bird of prey with a massive hooked bill
  • synonym: hawk - bird of prey with broad rounded wings and a long tail
