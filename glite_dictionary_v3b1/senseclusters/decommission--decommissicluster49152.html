decommission

verb
withdraw from active service or use
6.5 1.08/m
Pronunciation: / ÀådiÀê.k…ôÀàm…™ É.…ôn / (AmE), / ÀådiÀê.k…ôÀàm…™ É.…ôn / (BrE)
to officially withdraw something, such as a ship or facility, from active service or use

Examples

To decommission a ship
The navy decided to decommission several of its older ships.
The old power plant was decommissioned after decades of operation.
Workers have begun decommissioning the factory following its closure.

Synonyms

  • inactivate
    7.2 0.02/m
    inactivate officially removes a unit or person from active military lists, while decommission means permanently withdraw a ship or facility from service.
  • deactivate
    4.3 0.61/m
    deactivate removes something from active service, often temporarily or electronically, while decommission officially withdraws ships or facilities permanently.
  • retire
    2.8 1.61/m
    retire takes equipment out of use because it is old or obsolete, while decommission officially withdraws it from service.
  • withdraw
    3.5 10/m
    withdraw means to take something away or stop support in many situations, while decommission is an official removal from service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retire - to take out of use or service, especially after a period of use
