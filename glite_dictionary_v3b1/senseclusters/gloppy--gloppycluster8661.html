gloppy

adjective
thick and messy
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɑpi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒpi / (BrE)
thick, sticky, and messy in texture or appearance

Examples

Gloppy soup
The soup turned out gloppy after simmering for too long on the stove.
She wiped the gloppy paint off her hands with a paper towel.
The mixture was so gloppy that it stuck to the spoon and wouldn't pour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gooey - soft and sticky in texture
  • antonym: runny - more liquid and able to flow easily
