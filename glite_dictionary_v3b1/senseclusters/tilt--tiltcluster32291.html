tilt

noun
emotional state harming play
7.6
Pronunciation: /tɪlt/ (AmE), /tɪlt/ (BrE)
(in poker, video games, and competitive play) a state of emotional upset—usually anger or frustration—that causes a player to make poor, impulsive decisions.

Examples

On tilt after losing
After the bad call, he went on tilt and started making risky, impulsive plays in the tournament.
Coaches teach players to stop and breathe before strong emotions push them into tilt during matches.
The poker pro explained how small losses can slowly build up to a dangerous tilt affecting decision-making.

Related Terms

  • antonym: composure - calmness and self-control in a stressful situation; the opposite of losing control
  • synonym: frustration - a feeling of being upset or annoyed because of inability to change or achieve something
