gley

noun
waterlogged soil
9.9
Pronunciation: /gleɪ/ (AmE), /gleɪ/ (BrE)
a type of soil that is waterlogged, grayish in color, and forms in areas with poor drainage

Examples

Wet gley soil
The farmer struggled to grow crops in the gley because it stayed wet all year.
After heavy rains, the field turned into a patch of sticky gley that was hard to walk on.
Scientists studied the gley to understand how poor drainage affects soil color and texture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clay - a fine-grained natural soil material
  • synonym: peat - soil made of decayed plant material, often found in wetlands
  • antonym: sand - loose granular soil with large particles
