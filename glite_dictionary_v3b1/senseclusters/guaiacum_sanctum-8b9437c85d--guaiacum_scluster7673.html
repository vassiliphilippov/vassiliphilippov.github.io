Guaiacum sanctum

noun
species of evergreen tree, source of lignum vitae
Pronunciation: / ˌɡwaɪ.ə.kəm ˈsæŋktəm / (AmE), / ˌɡwaɪ.ə.kəm ˈsæŋktəm / (BrE)
A small evergreen tree native to the southern United States and West Indies, valued for its hard lignum vitae wood.

Examples

Guaiacum sanctum tree
Conservationists are concerned about overexploitation of Guaiacum sanctum in its native habitats.
The Guaiacum sanctum tree produces vibrant blue flowers and dense, valuable wood.
The heartwood of Guaiacum sanctum has been used for centuries to make durable tools.
