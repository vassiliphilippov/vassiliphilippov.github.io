palsy-walsy

adjective
overly friendly or intimate (informal)
Pronunciation: / ˌpælziˈwælzi / (AmE), / ˌpælziˈwælzi / (BrE)
(informal) Overly friendly or intimate, especially in a way that appears insincere or affected.

Examples

A palsy-walsy attitude
His palsy-walsy manner makes him seem insincere to some colleagues.
She suddenly became all palsy-walsy when she realized he was famous.
They acted palsy-walsy at the meeting, but I'm not sure they even like each other.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aloof - not friendly; distant or detached
  • synonym: friendly - behaving in a kind or pleasant way towards others
