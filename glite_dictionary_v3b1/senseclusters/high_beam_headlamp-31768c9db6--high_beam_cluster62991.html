high beam headlamp

noun
bright car headlamp for open roads
4.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ha…™ bim Ààh…õdÀål√¶mp / (AmE), / ha…™ biÀêm Ààh…õdÀålamp / (BrE)
A car headlamp that produces a bright, far-reaching beam of light without directing it downward or away from other road users, intended for use only when no other vehicles are ahead to avoid dazzling other drivers.

Examples

A high beam headlamp light
Please switch off your high beam headlamp when you see oncoming traffic.
The high beam headlamp is useful for driving on empty country roads at night.
Using the high beam headlamp in built-up areas can be dangerous and inconsiderate.

Synonyms

  • main beam headlamp
    5.0 0.00/m
    main beam headlamp gives bright, focused light used only with no oncoming cars, while high beam headlamp sends a long, strong beam that must not dazzle drivers.
  • full beam headlamp
    5.0 0.00/m
    full beam headlamp gives bright, focused light with little glare control and is used only on clear roads, while high beam headlamp reaches far to light distant objects.
  • driving beam headlamp
    6.8 0.00/m
    driving beam headlamp gives a bright, centre-focused beam that can glare at oncoming drivers, while high beam headlamp lights far distance only when roads are empty.
  • high beam
    3.5 0.21/m
    high beam is the headlight setting that gives bright, far light for clear roads, while high beam headlamp is the actual lamp that makes that beam.
