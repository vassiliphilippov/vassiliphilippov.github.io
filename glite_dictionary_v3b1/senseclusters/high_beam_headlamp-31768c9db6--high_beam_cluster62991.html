high beam headlamp

noun
bright car headlamp for open roads
Pronunciation: / haɪ bim ˈhɛdˌlæmp / (AmE), / haɪ biːm ˈhɛdˌlamp / (BrE)
A car headlamp that produces a bright, far-reaching beam of light without directing it downward or away from other road users, intended for use only when no other vehicles are ahead to avoid dazzling other drivers.

Examples

A high beam headlamp light
Please switch off your high beam headlamp when you see oncoming traffic.
The high beam headlamp is useful for driving on empty country roads at night.
Using the high beam headlamp in built-up areas can be dangerous and inconsiderate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low beam headlamp - a car headlamp with a dipped, downward-directed beam to avoid dazzling other drivers
