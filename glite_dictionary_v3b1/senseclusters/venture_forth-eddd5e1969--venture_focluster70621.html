venture forth

verb
move forward with courage or risk
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛntʃər fɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈventʃə fɔːθ / (BrE)
to go somewhere or take action, especially when it involves risk or uncertainty

Examples

To venture forth bravely
As the sun rose, the hikers decided to venture forth into the dense forest.
Many entrepreneurs venture forth with little certainty of success.
She was nervous, but she knew she had to venture forth and find a new job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proceed - to move forward or continue
  • antonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw from a position or action
