albumin

noun
a type of water-soluble protein
4.3
Pronunciation: / ælˈbjumɪn / (AmE), / ælˈbjuːmɪn / (BrE)
a simple water-soluble protein found in blood plasma, egg white, milk, and other animal tissues and fluids

Examples

Blood albumin
Doctors often measure albumin to assess a patient's nutritional status.
Egg whites are a rich source of albumin, which is used in cooking and baking.
Low levels of albumin in the blood can indicate kidney disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: globulin - another major class of proteins in blood plasma
