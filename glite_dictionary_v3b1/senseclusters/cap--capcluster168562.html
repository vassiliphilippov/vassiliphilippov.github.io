cap

an upper limit; maximum allowed

A maximum limit, especially on an amount of money or number of people allowed.

Examples

Salary cap
Many sports leagues have a salary cap to ensure fair competition.
The government imposed a cap on energy bills to protect consumers from rising costs.
There is a cap on the number of people who can attend the event.

Synonyms

  • ceiling
    3.7 7.17/m
    ceiling is an official upper limit used in law or budgets, while cap is a set maximum on money or people.
  • upper limit
    3.2 1.20/m
    upper limit names the highest allowed amount in rules or figures, while cap is a formal limit often set for money or people.
  • roof
    6.7 4.61/m
    roof is a less common metaphor for a spending limit in reports, while cap usually means a real legal or budget limit.
