cap

noun
an upper limit; maximum allowed
2.9
Pronunciation: / kæp / (AmE), / kap / (BrE)
A maximum limit, especially on an amount of money or number of people allowed.

Examples

Salary cap
Many sports leagues have a salary cap to ensure fair competition.
The government imposed a cap on energy bills to protect consumers from rising costs.
There is a cap on the number of people who can attend the event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceiling - an upper limit, especially on prices, wages, or expenditure
  • antonym: floor - a lower limit, especially on prices or wages
