crash

verb
to be damaged or destroyed by impact
Pronunciation: / kræʃ / (AmE), / kræʃ / (BrE)
to hit, break, or move through something with great force and noise; or to cause something to do so

Examples

To crash a car
Dozens of cars crashed during the icy weather on the highway.
Her drone crashed into a tree and broke apart.
The train crashed into the barrier at high speed.
During the movie, the hero crashed through the window to save the hostages.
Recklessly, the player crashed the puck through the defense to score.
The protestors crashed the security barricade to enter the event.
She crashed the door open when she heard the baby crying inside.
The angry chef crashed the pan onto the stove, startling everyone in the kitchen.
Waves crashed against the rocks with relentless force during the storm.
