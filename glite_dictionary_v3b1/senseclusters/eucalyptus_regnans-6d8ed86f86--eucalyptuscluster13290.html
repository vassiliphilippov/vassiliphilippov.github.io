Eucalyptus regnans

noun
tall Australian eucalyptus tree species
9.2
Pronunciation: /juː.kə.ˈlɪp.təs/ /; / /juː.kəl.ˈɪp.təs/ (AmE), /juː.kə.ˈlɪp.təs/ /; / /juː.kəl.ˈɪp.təs/ (BrE)
A species of tall eucalyptus tree native to southeastern Australia, noted for being the tallest flowering plant and the tallest hardwood in the world.

Examples

Tall Eucalyptus regnans tree
Many conservationists work to protect the remaining old-growth Eucalyptus regnans trees.
The Eucalyptus regnans is renowned for reaching heights over 90 meters in the forests of Victoria.
Timber from the Eucalyptus regnans is highly valued for construction purposes in Australia.
