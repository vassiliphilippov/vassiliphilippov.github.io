amorph

noun
shapeless object
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪmɔrf / (AmE), / ˈeɪmɔːf / (BrE)
a mass, object, or organism that lacks a definite or recognizable shape or form

Examples

A clay amorph
The artist created an amorph from clay that defied any clear interpretation.
Scientists discovered an amorph deep in the ocean, unlike any known organism.
A strange amorph appeared on the shore after the storm, puzzling the locals.

  • antonym: form - the visible shape or configuration of something
