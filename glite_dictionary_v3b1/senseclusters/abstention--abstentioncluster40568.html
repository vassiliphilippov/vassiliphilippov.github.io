abstention

noun
not voting or participating
6.8
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈsten.ʃən/ /; / /æb.ˈsten.ʃən/ (AmE), /əb.ˈsten.ʃən/ /; / /æb.ˈsten.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of deliberately choosing not to vote or take part in something, especially an election or decision-making process

Examples

Abstention in voting
The committee's decision was affected by several members' abstention from the vote.
A high level of abstention can indicate public dissatisfaction with the candidates.
He explained his abstention as a way to remain neutral in the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boycott - the act of refusing to take part in an event or activity as a protest
  • antonym: participation - the act of taking part in something
