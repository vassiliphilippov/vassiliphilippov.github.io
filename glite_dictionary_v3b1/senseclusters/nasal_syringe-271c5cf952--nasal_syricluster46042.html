nasal syringe

noun
bulb device for nasal irrigation or suction
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪzəl səˈrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəl sɪˈrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a medical device with a flexible bulb used to instill or aspirate fluid, especially for irrigating nasal passages or removing nasal congestion

Examples

A nasal syringe device
A nasal syringe is often used to gently clear a baby's stuffy nose.
After the surgery, the doctor recommended using a nasal syringe for daily saline irrigation.
The pediatrician showed the parents how to clean the nasal syringe after each use.
