heterosporous

adjective
producing two distinct types of spores
6.5 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əˈspɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈspɔːrəs / (BrE)
(of plants) producing two different types of spores, typically male and female, in the same organism.

Examples

Heterosporous plants
Many ferns are heterosporous, producing both microspores and megaspores for reproduction.
Selaginella is a well-known example of a heterosporous genus among clubmosses.
The evolution of heterosporous plants marked a significant step in plant diversity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homosporous - producing only one type of spore
