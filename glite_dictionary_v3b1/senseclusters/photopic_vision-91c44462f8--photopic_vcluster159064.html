photopic vision

noun
normal vision in daylight conditions
8.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /fə.ˈtɑː.pɪk ˈvɪʒ.ən/ /; / /foʊ.ˈtɑː.pɪk ˈvɪʒ.ən/ (AmE), /fə.ˈtɒp.ɪk ˈvɪʒ.ən/ /; / /fəʊ.ˈtɒp.ɪk ˈvɪʒ.ən/ (BrE)
Vision under well-lit conditions where the cone cells in the retina are active, enabling perception of color and fine detail.

Examples

Photopic vision in bright light
Photopic vision allows us to read and recognize faces in bright environments.
During the day, humans rely primarily on photopic vision to perceive a wide range of colors.
Unlike scotopic vision, photopic vision provides high visual acuity and color discrimination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: scotopic vision - vision under low light conditions, using rod cells rather than cones
