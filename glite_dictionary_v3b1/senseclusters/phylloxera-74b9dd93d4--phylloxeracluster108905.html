Phylloxera

noun
genus of plant lice affecting vines
7.4
Pronunciation: / fɪˌlɒkˈsɪrə / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒkˈsɪərə / (BrE)
A genus of small sap-sucking insects (plant lice) that include pests of grapevines, notable for causing damage to vineyards.

Examples

Phylloxera insect pest
Phylloxera devastated European vineyards in the late nineteenth century, prompting changes in viticulture practices.
The scientist specialized in the study of Phylloxera and its effects on grapevines.
To prevent Phylloxera infestations, many vineyards plant resistant rootstocks.
