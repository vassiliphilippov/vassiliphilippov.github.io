polish off

verb
kill someone intentionally (informal)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑlɪʃ ɑf / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃ ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) To kill someone intentionally and with premeditation.

Examples

To polish off a rival
In the film, the hitman was hired to polish off a corrupt politician.
Rumors spread that the witness was polished off to prevent him from testifying.
The gang decided to polish off their main rival before he could retaliate.
