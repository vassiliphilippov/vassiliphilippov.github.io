proposition

noun
the act of making a proposal
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑː.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌprɒp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a suggestion, proposal, or offer put forward for consideration, acceptance, or rejection (can be business, private, or romantic)

Examples

A business proposition
During the meeting, she presented a bold proposition to launch a new product line.
He made a business proposition that intrigued several investors.
The board carefully considered her proposition before making a decision.
He was embarrassed when a stranger made a proposition to him at the bar.
She received an unwelcome proposition from her colleague after the meeting.
Some people may be offended by a direct proposition of this kind.
She made a proposition that benefited everyone on the team.
The board considered the proposition and decided to approve the new policy.
Their proposition was rejected after much debate.
