English saddle

noun
a hornless saddle used in English riding
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsæd.əl / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈsæd.əl / (BrE)
a type of horse saddle with a steel cantle and pommel, lacking a horn, used in English-style riding

Examples

An English saddle
She prefers to ride using an English saddle for better contact with the horse.
The English saddle is commonly used in show jumping and dressage events.
Unlike the Western saddle, the English saddle does not have a horn.
