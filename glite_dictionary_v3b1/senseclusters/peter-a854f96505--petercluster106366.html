Peter

proper noun
disciple of Jesus and leader of the Apostles
2.0 108/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpiÀêt…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàpiÀêt…ô / (BrE)
One of the twelve Apostles of Jesus Christ, regarded as the leader of the Apostles and, in Catholic tradition, as the first Pope.

Examples

Peter the Apostle
According to tradition, Peter was crucified upside down in Rome.
Many consider Peter to be the most prominent of the Apostles.
The pope is said to be the successor of Peter in the Catholic Church.

Synonyms

  • St. Peter the Apostle
    6.7 0.00/m
    St. Peter the Apostle is the saintly title stressing his role as apostle, while Peter is the simple personal name.
  • Saint Peter the Apostle
    5.5 0.00/m
    Saint Peter the Apostle is used in formal church writing to show his apostle role, while Peter is the ordinary name.
  • Simon Peter
    5.8 0.05/m
    Simon Peter uses his original given name and sounds more historical, while Peter is the common name people use.
  • Saint Peter
    4.4 0.04/m
    Saint Peter is the reverent form often used in religious text, while Peter is the everyday name people say.
  • St. Peter
    4.1 0.33/m
    St. Peter is the short title seen in signs or captions, while Peter is the plain personal name used in speech.
