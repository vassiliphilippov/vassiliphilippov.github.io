caudate nucleus

noun
tail-shaped brain structure in the basal ganglia
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔː.deɪt ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
a tail-shaped mass of gray matter within the brain, forming part of the basal ganglia and located in the lateral ventricle

Examples

Caudate nucleus in brain
Damage to the caudate nucleus can result in movement disorders such as Huntington's disease.
Researchers observed changes in the caudate nucleus during learning tasks.
The caudate nucleus plays a key role in various motor processes within the brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: globus pallidus - a subcortical structure of the brain involved in the regulation of voluntary movement
  • synonym: putamen - a round structure located at the base of the forebrain, part of the basal ganglia
