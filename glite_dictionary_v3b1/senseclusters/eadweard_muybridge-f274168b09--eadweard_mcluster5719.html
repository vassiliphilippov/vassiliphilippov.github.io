Eadweard Muybridge

proper noun
motion-picture photography pioneer
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdwɚd ˈmaɪˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɛdwəd ˈmaɪbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
(1830–1904) An English-born American photographer and motion-picture pioneer noted for photographic studies of motion, especially of running horses, using a series of still cameras.

Examples

Eadweard Muybridge photographs motion
Eadweard Muybridge is renowned for his groundbreaking photographs capturing the movement of animals and humans.
Many film historians credit Eadweard Muybridge with laying the foundation for modern motion pictures.
The work of Eadweard Muybridge continues to inspire artists and scientists interested in motion analysis.
