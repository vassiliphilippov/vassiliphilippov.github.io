root

verb
become settled or established
5.1 1.35/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀêt / (AmE), / ruÀêt / (BrE)
(of a person) to become established or settled in a place or way of life.

Examples

To root in a city
After years of traveling, he finally decided to root in a small coastal town.
It takes a while for most people to truly root in a new community.
She felt ready to root and build a stable life after moving to the city.
