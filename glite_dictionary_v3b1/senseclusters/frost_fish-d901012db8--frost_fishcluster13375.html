frost fish

noun
long, slender marine fish related to snake mackerel
Pronunciation: / frɔst fɪʃ / (AmE), / frɒst fɪʃ / (BrE)
a long-bodied marine fish with a slender, scaleless, whiplike body and sharp teeth, closely related to the snake mackerel

Examples

The frost fish species
Fishermen occasionally catch frost fish while trawling at great depths.
Researchers studied the migration habits of the elusive frost fish in the Pacific Ocean.
The frost fish is sometimes found washed up on beaches after winter storms.
