middle voice

noun
verb form with subject as both agent and receiver
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl vɔɪs / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl vɔɪs / (BrE)
(grammar) A verb form or voice in which the subject acts upon itself or is both agent and recipient of the action, commonly contrasted with active and passive voices.

Examples

The middle voice form
In the middle voice, the subject is affected by its own action.
Some ancient Greek verbs are commonly used in the middle voice.
The concept of middle voice is difficult for English speakers because English rarely marks it explicitly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active voice - voice where the subject performs the action
  • antonym: passive voice - voice where the subject is acted upon
