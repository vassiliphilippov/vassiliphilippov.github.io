inversion

noun
weather condition with warm air over cooler air
9.0 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈvɜːr.ʒən/ /; / /ɪn.ˈvɜːr.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈvɜː.ʃən/ (BrE)
A weather condition in which a layer of cooler air at the Earth's surface is trapped beneath a layer of warmer air above it.

Examples

Weather inversion
A strong inversion can trap pollutants close to the ground, causing poor air quality in cities.
Because of the inversion, warm air rested above colder surface air, making the morning unusually foggy.
The weather forecast warned of a temperature inversion overnight, increasing the risk of frost in the valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: temperature inversion - a reversal of the normal temperature decrease with altitude
