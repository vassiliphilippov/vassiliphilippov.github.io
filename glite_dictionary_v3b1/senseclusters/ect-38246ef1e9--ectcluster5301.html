ECT

noun
electroconvulsive therapy treatment
Pronunciation: / i si ti / (AmE), / iː siː tiː / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which a strong electric current is passed through the brain to induce convulsions, chiefly used for certain psychiatric disorders

Examples

Modern ECT treatment
Modern ECT is performed under general anesthesia and is closely monitored by medical professionals.
Some patients report feeling much better after undergoing a course of ECT.
The psychiatrist recommended ECT as a treatment for severe depression when other therapies had failed.
