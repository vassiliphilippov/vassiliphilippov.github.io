Shylock

noun
merciless moneylender; villain in Shakespeare
Pronunciation: / ˈʃaɪˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪlɒk / (BrE)
a merciless or greedy moneylender; originally, the Jewish usurer in Shakespeare's play 'The Merchant of Venice'

Examples

A Shylock lender
He warned me not to borrow money from that Shylock in the city.
Some people accused the loan officer of being a Shylock because of the outrageous interest rates.
The play's villain, Shylock, is one of Shakespeare's most complex characters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: usurer - a person who lends money at unreasonably high interest rates
