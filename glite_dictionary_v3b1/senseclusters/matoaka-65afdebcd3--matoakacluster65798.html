Matoaka

proper noun
given name of Pocahontas, Powhatan woman
Pronunciation: / məˈtoʊ.əkə / (AmE), / məˈtəʊ.əkə / (BrE)
the given name of the Powhatan woman better known as Pocahontas, who befriended English settlers at Jamestown and is said to have saved Captain John Smith's life (1595–1617)

Examples

Matoaka Powhatan woman
Matoaka played a crucial role in early relations between Native Americans and English settlers.
Many historians refer to Matoaka by her more famous nickname, Pocahontas.
The life of Matoaka has inspired numerous books, films, and artworks.
