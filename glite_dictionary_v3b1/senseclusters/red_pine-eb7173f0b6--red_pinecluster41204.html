red pine

noun
a North American pine tree with reddish bark
Pronunciation: / rɛd paɪn / (AmE), / rɛd paɪn / (BrE)
A pine tree native to eastern North America, having long needles in pairs and reddish bark, known for its valuable timber.

Examples

Eastern red pine
He recognized the red pine by its distinctive reddish bark and long needles.
Many homes in the area were built using sturdy red pine timber.
The red pine forests are important habitats for many species of wildlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Scots pine - a Eurasian pine tree with reddish bark
