hearty

adjective
full of health and vigor
3.2 0.57/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ëÀêrti / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêti / (BrE)
Strong, healthy, and full of vigor; showing or characterized by good health or robust strength.

Examples

Hearty laugh
After a few weeks of rest, she returned with a hearty glow and renewed energy.
He enjoyed a hearty laugh that filled the whole room with warmth.
The children looked hearty and cheerful after playing outside all afternoon.

Synonyms

  • hale and hearty
    4.6 0.10/m
    hale and hearty describes an older person still healthy and strong, while hearty means general robust health and vigor.
  • lusty
    4.7 1.00/m
    lusty shows strong, lively energy and robust health, while hearty focuses more on steady, wholesome physical strength.
  • full-blooded
    5.2 0.01/m
    full-blooded is used for strong feelings or intense character, while hearty describes a person's healthy physical vigor.
  • red-blooded
    5.8 0.24/m
    red-blooded often suggests passionate, sometimes masculine energy, while hearty mainly means plain, robust physical health and vigor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frail - physically weak; not robust
  • synonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous
