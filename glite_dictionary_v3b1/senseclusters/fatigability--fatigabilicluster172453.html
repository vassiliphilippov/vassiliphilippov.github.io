fatigability

noun
susceptibility to fatigue failure (materials)
9.7
Pronunciation: /fə.tɪ.ɡə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /fə.tiː.ɡə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /fə.tɪ.ɡə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /fə.tiː.ɡə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
(in engineering and materials science) the susceptibility of a material or structural component to fail or develop cracks under repeated cyclic loading; tendency to experience fatigue damage.

Examples

Metal fatigability
The engineers measured the alloy's fatigability through thousands of controlled load cycles in the lab.
A low-temperature heat treatment significantly reduced the fatigability of the aircraft component during testing.
To predict service life, researchers created models linking microstructure to measured fatigability values.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fatigue - progressive and localized structural damage that occurs when a material is subjected to cyclic loading.
  • antonym: fatigue resistance - the ability of a material to resist failure or crack growth under repeated cyclic stresses; opposite of fatigability.
