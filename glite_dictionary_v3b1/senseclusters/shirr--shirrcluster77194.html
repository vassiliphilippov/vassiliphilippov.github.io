shirr

verb
to bake eggs until set
Pronunciation: / ʃɪr /; / ʃɜr / (AmE), / ʃɪə /; / ʃɜː / (BrE)
to cook eggs by baking them in a shallow dish, often with butter or cream

Examples

To shirr eggs
He decided to shirr some eggs for brunch instead of scrambling them.
If you shirr the eggs just right, the yolks will be perfectly runny.
She likes to shirr eggs with a dash of cream for a special breakfast.
She shirred the eggs with a little cream and served them hot for breakfast.
For brunch, he likes to shirr eggs with spinach and cheese in the oven.
The recipe suggests you shirr the eggs until the whites are just set and the yolks remain soft.
