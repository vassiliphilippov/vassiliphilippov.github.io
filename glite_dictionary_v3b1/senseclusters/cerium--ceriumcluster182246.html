cerium

noun
a chemical element, rare-earth metal
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪr.i.əm / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.rɪ.əm / (BrE)
a ductile grey metallic element of the lanthanide series, commonly used in lighter flints and the most abundant of the rare-earth group

Examples

Cerium metal
Cerium is widely used in the manufacture of flints for cigarette lighters.
Scientists studied the properties of cerium to develop new alloys.
When exposed to air, cerium tarnishes and forms an oxide layer.
