univariate

adjective
involving one variable
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈvɛriət / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɛːrɪət / (BrE)
involving or relating to only one variable, especially in mathematics or statistics

Examples

Univariate analysis
The researcher used a univariate analysis to examine the effect of age on test scores.
A univariate function depends on only one independent variable.
In statistics, univariate data sets are often easier to visualize and interpret.
A univariate analysis focuses on a single variable without considering others.
The researcher used univariate methods to examine the distribution of test scores.
In statistics, a univariate dataset contains only one type of measurement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multivariate - involving or depending on more than one variable
