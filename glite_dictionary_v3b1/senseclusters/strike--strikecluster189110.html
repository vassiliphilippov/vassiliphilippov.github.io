strike

noun
(baseball) pitch counted against batter
8.0
Pronunciation: / straɪk / (AmE), / straɪk / (BrE)
(Baseball) A pitch that is counted against a batter when the batter swings and misses, does not swing at a ball within the strike zone, or hits a foul ball (except on the third strike).

Examples

A baseball strike
He swung hard and missed, so it was his second strike.
The pitcher aimed for the corners and consistently threw strikes throughout the game.
The umpire called the pitch a strike, even though the batter disagreed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ball - (baseball) a pitch outside the strike zone not swung at by the batter
