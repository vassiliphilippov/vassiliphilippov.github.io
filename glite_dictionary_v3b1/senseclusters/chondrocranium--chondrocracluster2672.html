chondrocranium

noun
cartilaginous skull base
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːndroʊˈkreɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌkɒndrɒˈkreɪniəm / (BrE)
the cartilaginous part of the skull that forms in embryos and some adult animals, supporting the brain

Examples

The chondrocranium in embryos
In human embryos, the chondrocranium provides the initial structure for the developing skull.
Some fish retain a chondrocranium throughout their lives instead of developing a fully bony skull.
The scientist studied the chondrocranium to understand early vertebrate evolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartilage - a firm, flexible connective tissue
  • antonym: neurocranium - the bony part of the skull that encloses the brain
