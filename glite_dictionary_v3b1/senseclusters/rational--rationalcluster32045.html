rational

noun
number expressible as fraction or integer
8.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.ʃə.nəl/ (AmE), /ˈræ.ʃə.nəl/ (BrE)
a number that can be expressed as an integer or the quotient of two integers

Examples

A rational in math
Every integer is a rational, since it can be written as a fraction with denominator one.
The fraction 3/4 is a rational because both the numerator and denominator are integers.
Zero is considered a rational in mathematics.

Related Terms
