muttering

noun
low, indistinct continuous sound
4.3 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌt.ə.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈmʌt.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a low, continuous, and indistinct sound, often produced by quietly moving the lips without clear or articulate speech

Examples

Soft muttering
His constant muttering during the meeting distracted everyone around him.
In the darkness, the only sound was the muttering of the old man by the fire.
We could hear a low muttering coming from the next room, but couldn't make out the words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct, continuous sound
  • synonym: whisper - to speak very softly using the breath but not the vocal cords
