conditional relation

noun
logical 'if-then' relationship
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃənəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a logical relationship between two propositions where if the first is true, the second must also be true, typically expressed as 'if p, then q'

Examples

A conditional relation in logic
In logic, a conditional relation states that if the hypothesis is true, the conclusion must also be true.
Teachers often use conditional relations to teach students about cause and effect in reasoning.
The statement 'If it rains, the ground gets wet' expresses a conditional relation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biconditional - a logical relation where both 'if p then q' and 'if q then p' hold ('if and only if')
  • synonym: implication - a logical relationship where one statement follows from another ('if p, then q')
