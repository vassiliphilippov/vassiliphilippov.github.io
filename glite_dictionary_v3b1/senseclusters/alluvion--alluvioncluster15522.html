alluvion

noun
material deposited by flowing water
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàluÀêvi…ôn / (AmE), / …ôÀàluÀêvi…ôn / (BrE)
Soil, clay, silt, sand, or gravel carried by flowing water and deposited, especially in a riverbed or on a floodplain.

Examples

Alluvion deposits
Construction was delayed because the site was covered in loose alluvion from previous floods.
During the flood, thick alluvion was deposited onto the fields along the riverbank.
Much of the delta owes its fertility to layers of ancient alluvion.

Synonyms

  • alluvium
    8.9 0.14/m
    alluvium is loose clay, silt, sand, or gravel carried and laid down by running water, while alluvion names that deposited material.
  • alluvial deposit
    5.6 0.03/m
    alluvial deposit is material left by rivers when flow slows, while alluvion refers to soil and sediments carried and deposited.
  • alluvial sediment
    6.0 0.00/m
    alluvial sediment is clay, silt, or gravel dropped by flowing water, while alluvion names the same river or floodplain deposits.
  • alluvial soil
    4.2 0.06/m
    alluvial soil is fine, fertile earth found on floodplains used for farming, while alluvion means deposited sediment like silt or sand.
