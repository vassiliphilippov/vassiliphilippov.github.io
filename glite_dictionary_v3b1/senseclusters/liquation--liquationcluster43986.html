liquation

noun
process of melting
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɪˈkweɪʃən / (AmE), / lɪˈkweɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming liquid or causing something to become liquid, especially by melting

Examples

Liquation of wax
The controlled liquation of the wax allowed the artist to pour smooth casts.
During the refining process, liquation separates metals by melting them at different temperatures.
The scientist studied the liquation of ice under varying pressure conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquefaction - the process of making or becoming liquid
  • antonym: solidification - the process of becoming solid
