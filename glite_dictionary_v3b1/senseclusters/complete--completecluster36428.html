complete

verb
finish or carry out
1.0 107/m
Pronunciation: / k…ômÀàplit / (AmE), / k…ômÀàpliÀêt / (BrE)
to finish doing something or to make something whole or entire by bringing it to an end, often as required or expected

Examples

To complete a task
All students must complete their assignments before the deadline.
She managed to complete the project ahead of schedule.
The athlete failed to complete the marathon due to an injury.
Once you complete the form, please return it to the front desk.
She worked late every night to complete her project before the deadline.
The builders finally completed the new bridge after months of hard work.
A third player will complete our team for the tournament.
She added the final piece to complete the puzzle.
The renovations will complete the transformation of the old building into a modern library.

Synonyms

  • finish
    1.0 172/m
    finish means to bring something to an end in general, while complete often implies making something whole or fully done.
  • finish up
    2.0 4.67/m
    finish up is a casual way to end a task by doing the last steps, while complete sounds more neutral and full.
  • finish out
    4.0 0.33/m
    finish out means to complete remaining parts over time, while complete simply means to bring something to an end.
  • finish off
    2.5 1.19/m
    finish off often means doing the final part, sometimes with force or finality, while complete focuses on making something whole.
  • finalize
    3.8 4.01/m
    finalize focuses on completing the last details to make plans definite, while complete covers finishing something generally.
  • wrap up
    3.0 6.44/m
    wrap up is an informal phrase for finishing a meeting or task quickly, while complete is neutral and broader.
  • cap off
    3.7 0.63/m
    cap off means to finish with a special or notable action at the end, while complete simply means to finish.
  • accomplish
    2.6 64/m
    accomplish means to succeed in doing something after effort, while complete only means to bring something to an end.
  • end
    1.0 299/m
    end means to bring something to a close or stop, while complete often implies making something whole or fully finished.
