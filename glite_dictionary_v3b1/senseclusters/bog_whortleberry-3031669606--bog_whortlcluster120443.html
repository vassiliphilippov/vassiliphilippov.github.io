bog whortleberry

noun
evergreen shrub with edible berries
8.3
Pronunciation: / bɑɡ ˈwɜrtəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / bɒɡ ˈwɜːtəlˌbɛri / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub with leathery leaves, native to bogs and heaths, producing edible blue or black berries

Examples

The bog whortleberry bush
Bears are known to feed on the ripe fruit of the bog whortleberry during summer.
Local foragers collect bog whortleberries to make jams and preserves.
The bog whortleberry thrives in damp, acidic soils found in northern bogs and heaths.

Related Terms

  • synonym: huckleberry - North American shrubs with edible berries; sometimes used synonymously
