Muscovy

proper noun
medieval Russian principality centered on Moscow
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌs.kə.vi / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kə.vi / (BrE)
A Russian principality from the 13th to the 16th centuries, with Moscow as its capital, which later became the core of the Russian state.

Examples

Muscovy principality
During the late Middle Ages, Muscovy expanded its territory and influence across northern Eurasia.
Historians often refer to Muscovy as the cradle of modern Russia.
The rulers of Muscovy played a central role in unifying the Russian lands.
