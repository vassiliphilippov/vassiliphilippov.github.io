rhetorical question

noun
question not needing an answer
5.2 4.6
Pronunciation: / rəˈtɔrɪkəl ˈkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl ˈkwɛstʃən /; / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl ˈkwɛs.tʃən / (BrE)
a question that is asked to make a point or produce an effect, not to get an answer

Examples

Rhetorical question in speech
She often uses rhetorical questions during presentations to engage the audience.
The politician used a rhetorical question to emphasize his point and did not expect anyone to reply.
Why bother asking a rhetorical question if everyone knows the answer already?
His use of a rhetorical question made the argument especially persuasive.
Often, a speaker will end with a rhetorical question to leave the audience thinking.
She posed a rhetorical question to challenge the committee's assumptions.
