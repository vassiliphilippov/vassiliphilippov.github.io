Euphorbia cyathophora

noun
species of poinsettia native to US and Mexico
Pronunciation: / juːˈfɔrbiə saɪˌæθəˈfɔrə / (AmE), / juːˈfɔːbɪə saɪˌaθəˈfɔːrə / (BrE)
A species of poinsettia native to the United States and eastern Mexico, often confused with Euphorbia heterophylla, noted for its showy colored bracts.

Examples

Euphorbia cyathophora plant
Botanists often study Euphorbia cyathophora because of its distinctive red bracts.
The colorful foliage of Euphorbia cyathophora makes it popular in ornamental gardens.
Unlike true poinsettias, Euphorbia cyathophora thrives in various climates across the United States.
