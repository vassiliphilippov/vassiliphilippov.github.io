suffocation

noun
killing by depriving of oxygen
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃn̩ /; / ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃn /; / ˌsʌfəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the state or act of being unable to breathe because of lack of oxygen, which can cause serious harm or death

Examples

Accidental suffocation
Accidental suffocation can occur if a baby sleeps face-down on a soft surface.
He narrowly escaped suffocation when smoke filled the room.
The cause of death was determined to be suffocation due to lack of air.
Prompt treatment prevented suffocation when the child accidentally inhaled a small toy.
Several deaths occurred due to suffocation after the fire filled the room with smoke.
The feeling of suffocation overwhelmed him in the crowded, windowless elevator.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphyxiation - death or unconsciousness caused by lack of oxygen
