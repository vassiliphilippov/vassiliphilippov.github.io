impeachable

adjective
able to be accused
7.7
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpiː.tʃə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpiː.tʃə.bəl/ (BrE)
able to be charged with serious wrongdoing or misconduct, especially in a public office.

Examples

Impeachable offense
The judge considered whether the official's actions were impeachable under the law.
Only certain offenses are considered impeachable in the constitution.
The committee reviewed the evidence to determine if it was impeachable conduct.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unimpeachable - not able to be accused or doubted; beyond reproach
