endangerment

noun
putting at risk
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdeɪndʒərmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdeɪndʒəmənt / (BrE)
the act of putting someone or something at risk of harm or danger

Examples

Child endangerment
The illegal dumping of chemicals resulted in the endangerment of local water supplies.
Neglecting safety rules can lead to the endangerment of workers on construction sites.
Laws are in place to prevent the endangerment of endangered species.
The driver was charged with endangerment after speeding through a school zone.
Leaving chemicals unlocked in a home with children can be considered endangerment.
The new law increases penalties for child endangerment in cases of neglect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jeopardy - danger of loss, harm, or failure
  • antonym: protection - the act of keeping someone or something safe
