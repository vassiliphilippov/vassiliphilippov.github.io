withdrawnness

noun
the state of being withdrawn or distant
8.2
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈdrɔn.nəs / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔːn.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being withdrawn; a tendency to be distant, reserved, or unsympathetic in manner

Examples

Persistent withdrawnness
Her persistent withdrawnness made it difficult for new colleagues to get to know her.
His withdrawnness contrasted sharply with his brother's outgoing nature.
The psychologist noted the child's withdrawnness as a sign of social anxiety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aloofness - the quality of being unfriendly or distant
  • antonym: friendliness - the quality of being friendly and approachable
