sword grass

noun
grass or sedge with sword-shaped, sharp-edged leaves
1.1 6.4
Pronunciation: / sɔrd ˌɡræs / (AmE), / sɔːd ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of several grasses or sedges with long, narrow, sharp-edged leaves resembling the blade of a sword

Examples

Sword grass leaves
In the tropical marshes, sword grass thrives and forms dense thickets along the water's edge.
Some animals avoid areas where sword grass grows because of its razor-like leaves.
The sharp leaves of the sword grass can easily cut through skin if handled carelessly.
