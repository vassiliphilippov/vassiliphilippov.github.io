dopant

noun
substance for conductivity
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊpənt / (AmE), / ˈdəʊpənt / (BrE)
a substance added in small amounts to another material to change its electrical or physical properties (especially in semiconductors)

Examples

A dopant in silicon
A dopant is introduced into silicon to control its electrical conductivity in computer chips.
The choice of dopant affects the performance of the semiconductor device.
Engineers carefully measure the amount of dopant added to ensure the desired material properties.
A small amount of dopant can greatly increase the conductivity of a semiconductor.
Engineers carefully select the type of dopant to achieve the desired material properties.
The effectiveness of a dopant depends on its concentration and how it interacts with the base material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: additive - a substance added to something to improve or change it
  • synonym: impurity - a substance present in small amounts that is not part of the main material
