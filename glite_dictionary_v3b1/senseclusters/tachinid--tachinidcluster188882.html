tachinid

noun
parasitic fly
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtæk.ɪ.nɪd/ /; / /tə.ˈkɪn.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈtæk.ɪ.nɪd/ /; / /tə.ˈkɪn.ɪd/ (BrE)
a fly of the Tachinidae family, whose larvae are parasites or parasitoids of other insects, often used in biological pest control

Examples

A tachinid fly
A tachinid can help control pest populations by parasitizing caterpillars in gardens.
Scientists studied the life cycle of the tachinid to understand its role in ecosystems.
The farmer released tachinids as a natural way to reduce harmful insect numbers.
A tachinid can help control populations of harmful caterpillars in gardens.
Scientists released tachinids to reduce the number of crop-damaging insects.
The larvae of a tachinid develop inside their insect hosts, eventually killing them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fly - an insect of the order Diptera, typically with a single pair of wings
  • antonym: housefly - a common non-parasitic fly found in homes
  • synonym: parasitoid - an organism that lives in or on a host and eventually kills it
