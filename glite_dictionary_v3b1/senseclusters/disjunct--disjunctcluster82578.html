disjunct

adjective
of a melody: moving by large intervals
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪs.dʒʌŋkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.dʒʌŋkt / (BrE)
(of a melody) moving by intervals larger than a major second, rather than by stepwise motion.

Examples

Disjunct melody
Compared to the smooth, stepwise motion, the disjunct passage challenges singers with its wide intervals.
Composers often use disjunct movement in modern music to create a sense of drama or tension.
The piece features a disjunct melody that leaps unpredictably between notes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conjunct - (of a melody) moving mainly by stepwise intervals
