cocoa powder

noun
powder made from cocoa beans, used in baking
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊkoʊ ˌpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəʊ ˌpaʊdə / (BrE)
A fine, dry powder made by grinding roasted cocoa beans and removing most of the fat (cocoa butter), used especially in baking and as a flavoring.

Examples

A spoon of cocoa powder
A dusting of cocoa powder gives tiramisu its characteristic finish.
Many brownie recipes call for unsweetened cocoa powder instead of baking chocolate.
She sprinkled cocoa powder on top of the cake for decoration.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: chocolate - a food made from roasted and ground cacao seeds, sweetened and often flavored
