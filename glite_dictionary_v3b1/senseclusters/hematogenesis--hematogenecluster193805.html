hematogenesis

noun
the formation of blood cells
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmətoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhiːmətəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which blood cells are formed in the body, especially in the bone marrow

Examples

Normal hematogenesis in bone marrow
Certain nutrients are essential for healthy hematogenesis throughout the body.
Diseases such as leukemia disrupt normal hematogenesis in patients.
Recent studies highlight the crucial role of bone marrow in hematogenesis.
Certain drugs can stimulate haematogenesis in patients with low blood cell counts.
Disorders of haematogenesis can lead to anemia or other blood-related conditions.
Research into haematogenesis has helped us understand how blood diseases develop.
