licorice fern

noun
fern with sweet-flavored rootstock
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkərɪʃ fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈlɪkərɪʃ fɜːn / (BrE)
a type of fern (Polypodium glycyrrhiza) whose rootstock has a sweet, licorice-like flavor, found in western North America

Examples

The licorice fern plant
Botanists studied how the licorice fern survives in shaded, moist environments.
Some indigenous peoples traditionally chewed the rootstock of the licorice fern for its sweet taste.
The licorice fern is often found growing on mossy logs in Pacific Northwest forests.
