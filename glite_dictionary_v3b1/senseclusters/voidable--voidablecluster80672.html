voidable

adjective

(of a contract, agreement, etc.) capable of being declared invalid but not automatically void; able to be rescinded at the option of one party.

Examples

Voidable contract
Some agreements are voidable if consent was obtained through fraud or duress.
The contract was found to be voidable due to misrepresentation by one party.
Under certain circumstances, marriage is voidable at the request of one spouse.

Synonyms

  • rescindable
    9.1 0.01/m
    rescindable is able to be cancelled by legal action, while voidable is cancelled at one party's option.
  • defeasible
    9.9 0.07/m
    defeasible is a right that ends if certain conditions occur, while voidable is a contract cancelled at one party's choice.
