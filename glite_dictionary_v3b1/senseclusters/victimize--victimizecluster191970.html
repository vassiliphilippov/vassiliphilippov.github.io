victimize

verb
to treat someone unfairly or punish unjustly
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪktəˌmaɪz /; / ˈvɪktɪˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɪktɪmaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, making them a victim

Examples

To victimize a student
He felt victimized after being punished for something he did not do.
Some children are victimized by bullies at school on a daily basis.
The new law unfairly victimized small business owners who could not afford the extra fees.
Scammers often victimize elderly people by pretending to be officials.
She felt victimized by her coworkers after the mistake was made public.
The new policy unfairly victimizes minority students.
She felt victimised at work after reporting unethical behavior.
Some teenagers reported being victimised by bullies at school.
The new law aims to protect people who have been victimised because of their beliefs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exonerate - to clear someone from blame or fault
  • antonym: favor - to treat someone with preference and fairness, opposite of victimizing
  • synonym: harass - to subject someone to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • synonym: oppress - to keep someone in hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority
  • synonym: persecute - to treat someone cruelly or unfairly, often persistently
  • antonym: protect - to keep someone safe from harm or injury
  • synonym: victimize - treat someone cruelly or unfairly (American spelling)
