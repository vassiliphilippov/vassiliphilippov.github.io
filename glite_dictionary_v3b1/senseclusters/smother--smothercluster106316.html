smother

verb
suppress (a feeling, reaction, sound)
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsmʌð.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈsmʌð.ə / (BrE)
to suppress or restrain a feeling, reaction, or sound so that it is not expressed or noticed

Examples

To smother a laugh
He managed to smother a sigh of disappointment when he heard the news.
I could barely smother my excitement as I opened the letter.
She tried to smother her laughter during the serious meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: express - to show or make known a feeling or thought
  • synonym: repress - to restrain or subdue a feeling or desire
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to an activity, feeling, or action by force
