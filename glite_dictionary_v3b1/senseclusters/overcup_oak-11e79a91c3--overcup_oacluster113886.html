overcup oak

noun
oak tree with deeply cupped acorns
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌkʌp oʊk / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkʌp əʊk / (BrE)
a medium to large deciduous tree of the central and southern United States, characterized by acorns that are deeply enclosed in their cup and mature in the first year

Examples

An overcup oak tree
Planting an overcup oak can help restore native woodland habitats.
The overcup oak thrives in wet bottomlands where its acorns can easily take root.
We identified the tree by its deeply cupped acorns as an overcup oak.
