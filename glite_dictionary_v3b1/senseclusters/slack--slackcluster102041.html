slack

verb
become less active or fast
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
to make or become less active, intense, or strict; to reduce effort, speed, or attention

Examples

To slack speed
The train began to slack its speed as it approached the station.
With fewer customers, business started to slack during the winter months.
You shouldn't slack your efforts just because the project is nearly complete.
He warned that if we slack our efforts now, we could lose our momentum.
The manager decided to slack the pace of work during the slow season.
You might need to slack the rules to encourage more participation.
If demand continues to slack, we may need to reduce our operating hours.
Sales always slack during the off-season in this industry.
The pace of work began to slack as the afternoon wore on.
