drip painting

noun
painting style where paint is dripped onto the canvas
7.7 5.8
Pronunciation: / drɪp ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / drɪp ˈpeɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a style of abstract painting in which paint is dripped or poured onto the canvas, rather than being carefully applied

Examples

Drip painting style
Jackson Pollock is famous for pioneering the drip painting technique in modern art.
She created a drip painting by carefully pouring layers of colored paint on the canvas.
The museum's new exhibit features several striking drip paintings from the 1940s.
