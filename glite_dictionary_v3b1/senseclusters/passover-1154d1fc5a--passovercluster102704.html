Passover

noun
Jewish festival commemorating the Exodus

A major Jewish festival commemorating the exodus of the Israelites from Egypt, traditionally observed for eight days beginning on the 15th of Nissan.

Examples

Passover festival
During Passover, leavened bread is removed from Jewish homes in remembrance of the Exodus.
Families gather each year to celebrate Passover with a special meal called the Seder.
The traditions and stories of Passover have been passed down through generations.

Synonyms

  • Pesach
    9.3 0.07/m
    Pesach is the Hebrew name Jewish people use for the same eight-day festival, while Passover is the common English name.
  • Feast of the Unleavened Bread
    7.4 0.01/m
    Feast of the Unleavened Bread is a formal title referring to the eight-day Exodus observance, while Passover is the everyday name.
  • Pasch
    9.6 0.01/m
    Pasch is an older, less common word for the holiday, while Passover is the modern, commonly used name.
  • Pascha
    9.8 0.01/m
    Pascha is a word sometimes used for both Easter and the Jewish holiday, while Passover refers specifically to the Jewish festival.
