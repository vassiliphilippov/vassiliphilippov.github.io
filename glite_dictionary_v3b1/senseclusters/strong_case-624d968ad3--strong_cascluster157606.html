strong case

noun
well-supported argument or legal position
Pronunciation: / strɔŋ keɪs / (AmE), / strɒŋ keɪs / (BrE)
an argument or legal position that is well supported by evidence and reasoning

Examples

A strong case for change
Although we disagreed, she made a strong case for her proposal.
The prosecution presented a strong case that convinced the jury of the defendant's guilt.
Unless you have a strong case, it will be hard to win in court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: weak case - an argument or legal position with little supporting evidence
