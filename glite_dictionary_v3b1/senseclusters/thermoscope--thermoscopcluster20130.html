thermoscope

noun
early temperature device
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθɜːrmoʊˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈθɜːməskəʊp / (BrE)
an early scientific instrument that shows changes in temperature without giving exact measurements

Examples

An early thermoscope
The inventor used a thermoscope to demonstrate how heat affects air pressure.
Before the thermometer was invented, scientists relied on the thermoscope to observe temperature changes.
Galileo is often credited with developing an early form of the thermoscope in the 17th century.
