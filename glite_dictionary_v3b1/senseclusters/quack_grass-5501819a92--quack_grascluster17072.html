quack grass

noun
invasive weedy European grass
3.3
Pronunciation: / kwæk ɡræs / (AmE), / kwæk ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A European grass (Elymus repens) that spreads rapidly by creeping rhizomes, commonly considered a weed and naturalized in North America.

Examples

Quack grass weed
Quack grass spreads quickly through lawns, making it difficult for other plants to compete.
Farmers often struggle to control quack grass in their fields because of its resilient rhizomes.
The gardener pulled up several clumps of quack grass invading the vegetable patch.
