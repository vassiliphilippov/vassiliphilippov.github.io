photoautotrophic

adjective
using light for food
9.9
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.ɑː.toʊ.ˈtroʊ.fɪk/ /; / /foʊ.toʊ.ɔː.toʊ.ˈtroʊ.fɪk/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.ɔː.təʊ.ˈtrəʊ.fɪk/ (BrE)
describing organisms that produce their own food from inorganic substances using light as an energy source

Examples

Photoautotrophic green plants
Green plants are photoautotrophic because they use sunlight to make their own food.
Algae in the pond are photoautotrophic and play a key role in the ecosystem.
Scientists study photoautotrophic bacteria to understand how life can survive in extreme environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autotrophic - able to produce food from inorganic substances
  • antonym: heterotrophic - requiring organic substances for food
