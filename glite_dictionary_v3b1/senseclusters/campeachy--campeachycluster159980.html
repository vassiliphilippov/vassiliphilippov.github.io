campeachy

noun
spiny Central American tree used for dye
9.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / kæmˈpiːtʃi / (AmE), / kamˈpiːtʃi / (BrE)
A spiny shrub or small tree native to Central America and the West Indies, known for its bipinnate leaves, bright yellow flowers, and hard brown or reddish heartwood used to make black dye.

Examples

The campeachy tree
Botanists studied the campeachy for its unique heartwood and bright yellow flowers.
In colonial times, the campeachy trade flourished thanks to the tree's use in dye production.
The wood of the campeachy was highly valued for making a natural black dye.
