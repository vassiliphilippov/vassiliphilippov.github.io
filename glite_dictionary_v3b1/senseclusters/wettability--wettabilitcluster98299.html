wettability

noun
ability to wet surfaces
7.6
Pronunciation: /we.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ /; / /we.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /we.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ /; / /we.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the ability of a liquid to maintain contact with a solid surface, often affecting how the liquid spreads or adheres

Examples

The wettability of fabric
The wettability of the fabric determines how quickly it absorbs spilled liquids.
Engineers tested the wettability of the new coating to ensure it repels oil effectively.
Improving the wettability of medical implants can help them bond better with body tissues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adhesion - the tendency of different substances to stick together
  • antonym: hydrophobicity - the property of being resistant to wetting by water
