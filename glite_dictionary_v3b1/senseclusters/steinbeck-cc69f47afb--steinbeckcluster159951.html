Steinbeck

proper noun
American novelist (1902–1968)
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪnˌbɛk / (AmE), / ˈstaɪnbɛk / (BrE)
John Steinbeck (1902–1968), a celebrated American author known for his novels about social issues and agricultural workers, such as 'The Grapes of Wrath' and 'Of Mice and Men.'

Examples

Steinbeck novels
Critics praised Steinbeck for his realistic portrayal of migrant workers.
Many students are required to read Steinbeck in their literature classes.
The impact of Steinbeck's works can still be felt in American culture today.
