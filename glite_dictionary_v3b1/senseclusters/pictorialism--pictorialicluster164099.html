pictorialism

noun
emphasis on images
8.4
Pronunciation: /pɪk.ˈtɔːr.ɪ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /pɪk.ˈtɔːr.ɪ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice or quality of representing subjects in a way that emphasizes visual images or picture-like qualities, often focusing on appearance rather than deeper meaning

Examples

Strong pictorialism in art
The artist's pictorialism was evident in the way she turned every scene into a vivid image.
Some critics argue that too much pictorialism in writing can distract from the main message.
Early photography often aimed for pictorialism, making photos look like paintings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstraction - the style of representing subjects in a non-realistic or non-literal way
  • synonym: realism - the style of representing subjects as they appear in everyday life
