undersupply

noun
insufficient supply
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərsəˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəsəˈplaɪ / (BrE)
a situation in which there is not enough of something to meet the demand for it

Examples

An undersupply of workers
There is an undersupply of affordable housing in many cities.
The undersupply of nurses has put extra pressure on the hospital staff.
An undersupply of clean water can lead to serious health problems in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shortage - a lack of something that is needed
  • antonym: surplus - an amount of something that is more than needed
