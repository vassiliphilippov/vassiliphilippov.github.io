Marie Curie

proper noun
French-Polish chemist and physicist; Nobel laureate
4.4
Pronunciation: / məˌriː ˈkjʊri / (AmE), / ˈmɑːri ˈkjʊəri / (BrE)
(1867–1934) A Polish-born French chemist and physicist who was awarded two Nobel Prizes for her pioneering work on radioactivity and her discovery of radium and polonium.

Examples

Marie Curie Nobel Prize
Marie Curie was the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences.
Many young scientists are inspired by the achievements of Marie Curie.
The contributions of Marie Curie to the field of radioactivity changed modern science.
