Prunus

noun
genus of fruit trees in the rose family
Pronunciation: / ˈpruːnəs / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs / (BrE)
a genus of shrubs and trees in the rose family (Rosaceae), widely distributed in temperate regions and including plums, cherries, peaches, apricots, and almonds

Examples

Prunus tree species
Botanists have identified over 400 species within the Prunus group worldwide.
Many species in the Prunus genus are cultivated for their edible fruit.
The ornamental cherry trees belong to the Prunus genus and are famous for their blossoms.
