masque

noun
masked costume party, especially historical
Pronunciation: / mæsk / (AmE), / mɑːsk / (BrE)
a social gathering or entertainment in which participants wear masks and costumes, especially popular in 16th- and 17th-century England

Examples

A royal masque
During the Renaissance, a masque was a favored way to celebrate royal occasions.
Shakespeare often incorporated scenes resembling a masque into his plays to entertain the audience.
The king hosted a splendid masque at the palace, and all the nobility attended in elaborate disguise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: costume party - a party where people dress up in costumes
  • synonym: masked ball - a formal dance where guests wear masks
