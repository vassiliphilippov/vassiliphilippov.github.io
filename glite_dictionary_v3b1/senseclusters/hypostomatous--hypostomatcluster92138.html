hypostomatous

adjective
stomata on underside
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈstoʊmətəs / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈstɒmətəs / (BrE)
having stomata only on the underside of a leaf

Examples

Hypostomatous leaf underside
Many shade plants are hypostomatous to help conserve water in their leaves.
A hypostomatous leaf has all its stomata located on the lower surface.
Botanists often describe certain ferns as hypostomatous due to their unique leaf structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amphistomatous - having stomata on both sides of a leaf
