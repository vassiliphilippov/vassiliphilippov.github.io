nonobjectivity

noun
abstract art quality
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɑbˌdʒɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɒbˌdʒɛkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality in visual art of not representing recognizable objects or subjects, focusing instead on abstract forms or colors

Examples

Nonobjectivity in painting
The artist's commitment to nonobjectivity is evident in her use of bold colors and geometric shapes.
Many modern painters embraced nonobjectivity to express emotions rather than depict real-world scenes.
The exhibition highlighted the rise of nonobjectivity in twentieth-century abstract art.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstraction - art that does not attempt to represent external reality
  • antonym: representation - art that depicts recognizable subjects or objects
