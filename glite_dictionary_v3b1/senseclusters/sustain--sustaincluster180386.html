sustain

verb
uphold as valid (legal)
2.8
Pronunciation: / səˈsteɪn / (AmE), / səˈsteɪn / (BrE)
(of a court, judge, etc.) to accept or uphold as valid, especially a motion or objection in a legal proceeding.

Examples

The judge sustains an objection
After a lengthy debate, the court sustained the plaintiff's motion.
If the evidence is inadmissible, the judge may sustain the objection.
The judge sustained the lawyer's objection during the trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overrule - to reject or disallow (especially a legal motion or objection)
