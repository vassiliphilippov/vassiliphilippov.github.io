compensate

verb
adjust to offset or correct
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːm.pənˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.pənˌseɪt / (BrE)
to adjust or counterbalance something so as to correct an effect or offset a disadvantage

Examples

To compensate for loss
He tried to compensate for his lateness by working extra hours.
Special lighting is used to compensate for the lack of natural sunlight.
The thermostat automatically compensates for temperature fluctuations in the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exacerbate - to make a situation worse, the opposite of correct or offset
  • synonym: offset - to counteract or compensate for something
