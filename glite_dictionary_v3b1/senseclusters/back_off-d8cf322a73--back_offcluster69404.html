back off

verb
move backwards away from something/someone
Pronunciation: / bæk ˈɔf /; / bæk ɔf /; / bæk ɑf / (AmE), / bæk ˈɒf /; / bæk ɒf / (BrE)
to move away from someone or something, or to stop being involved in a situation

Examples

To back off slowly
Police told the crowd to back off for everyone's safety.
She backed off when she realized the argument was getting heated.
When the dog barked, the children had to back off slowly.
He tried to back off from his promise once he realized the commitment required.
I think it’s time you backed off and let the experts handle this responsibility.
When the project became too demanding, she decided to back off and let someone else take over.
After the manager arrived, the reporters finally backed off and stopped asking questions.
If you don't back off now, you might make things worse.
She made it clear that he needed to back off and respect her decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bow out - to withdraw from a commitment or involvement
  • synonym: butt in - to interrupt or interfere in someone else's business (informal)
  • synonym: interfere - to involve oneself in a situation without invitation or necessity
  • synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw, especially from danger or difficulty
  • antonym: step in - to become involved in a situation, especially to help or intervene
