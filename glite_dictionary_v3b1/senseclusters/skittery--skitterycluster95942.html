skittery

adjective
nervous and quick
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪtəri / (AmE), / ˈskɪtəri / (BrE)
moving quickly and lightly in a nervous or easily startled way; easily frightened or jumpy

Examples

A skittery mouse
The skittery mouse darted across the kitchen floor when the lights turned on.
Her skittery movements made it clear she was nervous about the performance.
A skittery breeze sent leaves tumbling quickly down the empty street.
The young colt was skittery around strangers and sudden noises.
She felt skittery before her first big presentation at work.
After the loud crash, the cat became skittery and hid under the bed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing nervousness or quick movement
  • synonym: jumpy - nervous and easily startled
  • synonym: skittish - easily frightened or excited; nervous and jumpy
