phonemic

adjective
relating to language sounds (phonemes)
Pronunciation: / foʊˈniːmɪk / (AmE), / fəˈniːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the phonemes or distinctive sounds of a particular language.

Examples

Phonemic transcription
A phonemic transcription represents only the meaningful sound contrasts of speech.
Linguists often conduct a phonemic analysis to study sound patterns in a language.
The difference between the two words is not just phonemic, but also semantic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phonetic - relating to the actual production and perception of speech sounds
  • synonym: phonological - relating to the sound system of a language
