Disciples of Christ

proper noun
Protestant Christian denomination
6.8
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsaɪpəlz əv kraɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪpəlz əv kraɪst / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination that regards the Bible as the sole source of faith and baptizes by immersion.

Examples

Disciples of Christ church
She was raised in the Disciples of Christ tradition and attended services every week.
The Disciples of Christ emphasize unity among Christians and the importance of baptism by immersion.
The history of the Disciples of Christ dates back to the early nineteenth century.
