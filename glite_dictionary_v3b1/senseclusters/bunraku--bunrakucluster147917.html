bunraku

noun
Japanese puppet theater
9.9
Pronunciation: / buːnˈrɑːkuː / (AmE), / buːnˈrɑːkuː / (BrE)
a traditional Japanese puppet theater featuring large, jointed puppets operated by visible puppeteers, often with live narration

Examples

Traditional bunraku theater
The performance of bunraku amazed the audience with its lifelike puppets and skilled puppeteers.
She studied bunraku to learn more about traditional Japanese performing arts.
A bunraku show often requires three puppeteers to control a single puppet on stage.
We attended a bunraku performance during our trip to Osaka.
The intricate movements of the puppets in bunraku require years of training to master.
Many people are fascinated by the storytelling and artistry of bunraku theater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kabuki - a traditional Japanese form of drama with singing and dancing
  • synonym: puppetry - the art of operating puppets in performances
