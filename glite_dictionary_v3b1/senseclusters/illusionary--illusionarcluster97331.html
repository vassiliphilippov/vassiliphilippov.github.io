illusionary

adjective

Examples

Synonyms

  • illusive
    8.2 0.06/m
    illusive suggests something deceptive and misleading that tricks perception, while illusionary names something that creates an intended sensory illusion.
  • illusory
    7.0 2.57/m
    illusory looks not real or only seeming real, while illusionary describes something made to create an intended deception of the senses.
  • illusional
    9.4 0.04/m
    illusional sounds formal and often describes the presence of an illusion, while illusionary stresses active deception meant to fool the senses.
