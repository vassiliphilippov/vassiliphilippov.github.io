illusionary

adjective
producing or characterized by an illusion
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʒənəri / (BrE)
creating or characterized by an illusion; not real and intended to deceive the senses or mind.

Examples

An illusionary hope
Political promises can create an illusionary sense of hope among voters.
The magician amazed the audience with illusionary tricks that seemed impossible to explain.
The play's illusionary stage design transported viewers to a fantastical world.
