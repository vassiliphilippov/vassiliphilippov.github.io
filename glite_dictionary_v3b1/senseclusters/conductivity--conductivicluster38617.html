conductivity

noun
ability to transmit heat, electricity, or sound
4.9 1.34/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀênd…ôkÀàt…™v…™ti / (AmE), / Àåk…índ åkÀàt…™v…™ti / (BrE)
The ability of a material to allow heat, electricity, or sound to pass through it.

Examples

High conductivity material
Copper is widely used in electrical wiring because of its high conductivity.
Scientists measured the thermal conductivity of the new composite material.
The conductivity of the metal increases when it is heated.
