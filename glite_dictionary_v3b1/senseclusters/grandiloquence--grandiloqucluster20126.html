grandiloquence

noun
pompous or lofty language style
Pronunciation: / ɡrænˈdɪləkwəns / (AmE), / ɡranˈdɪləkwəns / (BrE)
a style of speaking or writing that is lofty, pompous, or overly elaborate; the excessive use of impressive-sounding words or expressions

Examples

Pompous grandiloquence
Critics accused the author of grandiloquence, saying her writing was more style than substance.
His speech was filled with excessive grandiloquence and lacked genuine substance.
The politician's grandiloquence failed to impress an audience seeking honest answers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humility - a modest or low view of one's own importance; opposite of boastful style
  • synonym: pomposity - the quality of being pompous; self-importance
