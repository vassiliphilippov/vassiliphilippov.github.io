finding of law

noun
judicial decision on legal rules in a case
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪndɪŋ əv lɔ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪndɪŋ əv lɔː / (BrE)
a decision by a court or judge regarding the application or interpretation of a rule of law to particular facts in a case

Examples

A finding of law
A finding of law can set important precedents for future legal cases.
On appeal, the court reviewed the finding of law rather than the finding of fact.
The judge made a finding of law that clarified which statute applied to the dispute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finding of fact - a decision by a judge or jury about the facts of a case
