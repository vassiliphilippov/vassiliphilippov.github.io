obey

verb
follow orders or rules
1.0 20/m
Pronunciation: / oÊŠËˆbeÉª / (AmE), / É™ÊŠËˆbeÉª / (BrE)
to do what someone in authority tells you to do, or to follow a rule or law

Examples

To obey the rules
Children are expected to obey their parents and teachers.
The soldiers refused to obey the orders given by their commander.
You must obey the law, even if you disagree with it.

Synonyms

  • comply
    3.8 35/m
    comply is a formal act of doing what rules or orders require in official settings, while obey often means following authority directly.
  • follow
    1.0 56/m
    follow can mean act on advice, rules, or someone's example in many situations, while obey implies submitting to authority orders.
  • abide by
    3.5 4.50/m
    abide by means to accept and act according to rules or decisions calmly, while obey focuses on doing what an authority tells you.
  • observe
    3.4 7.98/m
    observe means to follow or respect a law, custom, or rule publicly, while obey stresses doing what authority commands.
  • adhere
    4.7 9.68/m
    adhere often means stick to a belief or rule steadily and loyally, while obey stresses immediate following of an authority's command.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comply - to act in accordance with a wish or command
  • antonym: disobey - to refuse or fail to obey a command or rule
