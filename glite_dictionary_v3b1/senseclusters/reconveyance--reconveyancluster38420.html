reconveyance

noun
return of property title
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈveɪəns / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈveɪəns / (BrE)
the act or legal document that transfers property back to the original owner after a mortgage or loan has been paid off

Examples

A reconveyance deed
After paying off her mortgage, she received a reconveyance from the lender.
The title company handled the reconveyance to ensure the property was free of liens.
A reconveyance is necessary to prove that the homeowner now fully owns the property.
After the mortgage was paid in full, the bank issued a reconveyance to the homeowner.
The lawyer explained that a reconveyance would return the property title to its original owner.
A reconveyance is necessary to clear the lender's interest from the property records.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deed - a legal document showing ownership of property
  • antonym: foreclosure - the legal process of taking property due to unpaid debt
  • synonym: transfer - the act of moving ownership or rights from one person to another
