badly

adverb
with disapproval; unfavorably
Pronunciation: / ˈbædli / (AmE), / ˈbadli / (BrE)
in a way that causes harm, disadvantage, or is unfavorable to someone; viewed unfavorably

Examples

Spoken of badly
His actions made others view him badly in the community.
Many people spoke badly of the politician after the scandal was revealed.
She was upset when her coworkers thought badly of her for missing the meeting.
After the merger, the smaller company came off badly compared to its rival.
If this deal goes badly for us, we risk losing a lot.
She was treated badly by her teammates during the tournament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ill - with disapproval; unfavorably (archaic or formal)
  • antonym: well - in a favorable or approving manner
