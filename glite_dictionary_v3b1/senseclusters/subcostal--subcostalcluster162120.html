subcostal

noun
insect wing vein
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈkɑstəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈkɒstəl / (BrE)
a vein in an insect's wing that runs just below the leading edge, important in describing wing structure

Examples

Subcostal vein
The entomologist identified the subcostal to help classify the butterfly species.
Damage to the subcostal can affect the strength of an insect's wing.
Researchers compared the subcostal veins of different beetle families in their study.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: costa - the leading edge vein of an insect's wing
  • degree lower: cubitus - a main vein in an insect's wing located further from the leading edge
