gaw

verb
to stare foolishly
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡɔ / (AmE), / ɡɔː / (BrE)
to stare openly and often foolishly, usually with curiosity or amazement; chiefly dialectal

Examples

To gaw at tricks
Children would gaw at the magician as he performed his tricks in the park.
He stopped to gaw at the accident on the side of the road, holding up traffic.
Locals would often gaw at the tourists taking photos in the village square.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gawk - to stare openly and stupidly
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to
  • synonym: ogle - to look at someone with obvious sexual interest
