abasia trepidans

noun
inability to walk due to trembling legs
Pronunciation: / əˈbeɪziə ˌtrɛpɪˈdænz / (AmE), / əˈbeɪzɪə ˌtrɛpɪˈdan(z) / (BrE)
A type of abasia characterized by trembling of the legs, resulting in an inability to walk normally.

Examples

Abasia trepidans symptoms
Neurologists often encounter abasia trepidans in elderly individuals with movement disorders.
Physical therapy may help alleviate the symptoms of abasia trepidans.
The patient was diagnosed with abasia trepidans, which severely affected his mobility.
