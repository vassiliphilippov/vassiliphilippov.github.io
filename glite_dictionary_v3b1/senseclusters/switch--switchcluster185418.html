switch

verb
exchange or give in return
3.5
Pronunciation: / swɪtʃ / (AmE), / swɪtʃ / (BrE)
to change, exchange, or shift from one thing, activity, or person to another

Examples

To switch sides
He switched his old phone for a newer model at the store.
Let's switch seats so you can have a better view.
Would you like to switch your dessert with mine?
After finishing the first task, she decided to switch to something more urgent.
He can switch easily between different programming languages depending on the project.
Please switch the order of the slides for the presentation tomorrow.
After years of following one approach, the company decided to switch to a new strategy.
At the last minute, he switched his vote to support the proposal.
She had planned to study law but switched to medicine after her first year at university.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abandon - to leave something behind, often permanently
  • synonym: alternate - to repeatedly change from one thing to another
  • synonym: change - to make or become different; to alter
  • synonym: exchange - to give something and receive something of the same kind in return
  • synonym: reverse - to change to the opposite direction, order, or position
  • antonym: stay - to remain with the same thing, person, or situation
  • synonym: swap - to give something and receive something in return
