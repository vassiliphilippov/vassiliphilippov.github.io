swaddling bands

noun
cloth strips used to wrap infants
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑd.lɪŋ bændz / (AmE), / ˈswɒd.lɪŋ bændz / (BrE)
long strips of cloth or a garment used to wrap an infant tightly, especially to restrict movement or for warmth

Examples

Swaddling bands for babies
Swaddling bands were often passed down through generations as family heirlooms.
In ancient times, mothers would use swaddling bands to keep babies warm and secure.
The nativity scene depicted the infant Jesus wrapped in swaddling bands.
