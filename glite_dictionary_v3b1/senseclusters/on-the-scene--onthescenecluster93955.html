on-the-scene

adjective
present where something happens
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɑn ðə ˈsiːn / (AmE), / ˌɒn ðə ˈsiːn / (BrE)
Present at the location where an event is happening or has just happened.

Examples

On-the-scene assistance
Firefighters arrived quickly and provided on-the-scene assistance to victims.
Her on-the-scene coverage gave viewers a firsthand look at the protest.
The journalist gave an on-the-scene report during the hurricane.

Related Terms

  • synonym: on-the-spot - present at the exact place where something is happening
