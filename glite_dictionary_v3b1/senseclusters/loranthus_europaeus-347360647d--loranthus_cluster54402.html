Loranthus europaeus

noun
parasitic shrub of central Europe
Pronunciation: / loʊˈrænθəs juˈroʊpiəs / (AmE), / lɒˈranθəs jʊˈrəʊpiəs / (BrE)
A shrub native to central and southeastern Europe, partially parasitic on beeches, chestnuts, and oaks.

Examples

Loranthus europaeus shrub
Loranthus europaeus is sometimes found growing on old chestnut trees in European forests.
Farmers noticed the yellowish flowers of Loranthus europaeus among the branches of local oaks.
The partially parasitic nature of Loranthus europaeus makes it an interesting subject for botanists.
