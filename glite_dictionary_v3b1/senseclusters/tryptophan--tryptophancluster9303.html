tryptophan

noun
an essential amino acid
Pronunciation: / trɪpˈtoʊfæn / (AmE), / trɪpˈtɒfæn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid found in proteins, necessary for growth and normal metabolism, and a precursor of niacin

Examples

Essential tryptophan
A deficiency in tryptophan can affect mood and sleep patterns.
Milk and turkey are known to have high levels of tryptophan.
The body cannot produce tryptophan, so it must be obtained from the diet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amino acid - an organic compound forming the building blocks of proteins
