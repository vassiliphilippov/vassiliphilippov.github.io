LCA

noun
left coronary artery
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl siː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛl siː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
the left coronary artery, which is the main artery supplying blood to the left side of the heart

Examples

Blocked LCA
The cardiologist checked the patient's LCA for any signs of blockage during the procedure.
A narrowing in the LCA can lead to serious heart problems if not treated promptly.
Doctors often use imaging techniques to assess the health of the LCA in cardiac patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: right coronary artery - the main artery supplying blood to the right side of the heart
