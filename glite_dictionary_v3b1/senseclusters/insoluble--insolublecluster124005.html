insoluble

adjective
not able to be dissolved in liquid
6.4 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàs…ëÀêlj…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / …™nÀàs…ílj äb…ôl / (BrE)
(of a substance) not able to be dissolved in a liquid, especially water.

Examples

Insoluble in water
Most plastics are insoluble in water, making them difficult to break down in the environment.
Salt is soluble in water, but sand is insoluble and settles at the bottom.
The chemist explained that the powder was completely insoluble in alcohol.

Synonyms

  • indissoluble
    8.8 0.01/m
    indissoluble describes something that cannot be dissolved by liquids and is often formal or figurative, while insoluble is the common chemical word.
  • water-insoluble
    6.5 0.01/m
    water-insoluble does not dissolve in water and appears in chemical labels and tests, while insoluble can mean not soluble in any liquid.
