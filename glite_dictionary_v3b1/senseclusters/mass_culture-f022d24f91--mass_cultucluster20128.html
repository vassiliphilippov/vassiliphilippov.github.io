mass culture

noun
widely shared culture via mass media
5.6
Pronunciation: / mæs ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / mæs ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the culture that is widely shared and disseminated through mass media such as television, radio, and the internet

Examples

Mass culture today
Critics argue that mass culture can lead to the loss of traditional values and customs.
Social media has greatly accelerated the spread of mass culture across the globe.
Television was a driving force in shaping mass culture during the twentieth century.

  • synonym: popular culture - mainstream ideas, attitudes, and images of a culture
