telescreen

noun
large public display
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛləˌskrin / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskriːn / (BrE)
a large screen or television used to display moving images or broadcasts, often in public places

Examples

Concert telescreen
The crowd watched the concert on a massive telescreen set up in the park.
During the event, highlights were shown on the telescreen for everyone to see.
People gathered in the city square to view the announcement on the telescreen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monitor - a screen used to display images or information
  • synonym: television - an electronic device for receiving and displaying broadcast images and sound
