phase I clinical trial

noun
initial clinical test for safety of a drug or treatment
4.1
Pronunciation: / feɪz wʌn ˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (AmE), / feɪz wʌn ˈklɪnɪkəl ˈtraɪəl / (BrE)
the first stage of a clinical study in which a new drug or treatment is tested on a small group of people to evaluate its safety, determine a safe dosage range, and identify side effects

Examples

A phase I clinical trial
A successful phase I clinical trial is a crucial milestone before broader testing can continue.
Researchers are recruiting volunteers for a phase I clinical trial of the new antiviral medication.
The cancer drug entered a phase I clinical trial to assess its initial safety profile.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: phase II clinical trial - the second stage, assessing effectiveness and side effects in a larger group
