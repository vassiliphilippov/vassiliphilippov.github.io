clear-cutting

noun
removal of all trees
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrˌkʌtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of cutting down all the trees in a particular area at the same time

Examples

Forest clear-cutting
Environmental groups are concerned about the effects of clear-cutting on local wildlife.
The government introduced new regulations to limit clear-cutting in national forests.
After the clear-cutting of the hillside, the area was prone to landslides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deforestation - the removal of a forest or stand of trees from land
  • antonym: selective logging - the practice of cutting down only certain trees in an area
