as sharp as a butter knife

adjective
ineffective or not incisive (idiom)
4.7
Pronunciation: / əz ʃɑrp æz ə ˈbʌtər naɪf / (AmE), / əz ʃɑːp æz ə ˈbʌtə naɪf / (BrE)
used to describe something or someone as ineffective, blunt, or lacking incisiveness or precision, often in a sarcastic manner

Examples

As sharp as a butter knife comment
His comments during the meeting were as sharp as a butter knife, failing to make any real impact.
She tried to make a cutting remark, but it was as sharp as a butter knife.
The comedian's latest joke was as sharp as a butter knife, barely making the audience laugh.

Related Terms

  • antonym: as sharp as a tack - very sharp, intelligent, or incisive (idiom)
