fenster

noun
archaic window
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛnstər / (AmE), / ˈfɛnstə / (BrE)
an opening or window in a wall, especially in older or historical buildings (archaic or borrowed from German)

Examples

Stone fenster
A single narrow fenster allowed sunlight to enter the dim cellar.
The castle's thick walls were pierced by a small fenster overlooking the valley.
He admired the ancient fenster set high above the wooden door.

Related Terms

  • synonym: window - an opening in a wall fitted with glass to let in light or air
