electric energy

noun
energy possessed by electric charges or currents
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
The energy possessed by an electric charge due to its position in an electric field or by an electric current in a magnetic field.

Examples

Electric energy in circuits
During the storm, the electric energy caused the lights to flicker unexpectedly.
Modern homes use electric energy to power appliances and electronic devices.
The battery stores electric energy that can be converted into light or motion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electrical energy - energy derived from electric potential or currents
  • antonym: kinetic energy - energy an object has due to its motion
  • synonym: potential energy - energy stored by an object due to its position
