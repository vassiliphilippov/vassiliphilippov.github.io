biceps

noun
muscle with two origins, esp. upper arm
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.sɛps / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.seps / (BrE)
A muscle having two points of origin, especially the large muscle at the front of the upper arm that bends the elbow.

Examples

Strong biceps muscle
He injured his biceps while lifting heavy weights at the gym.
Her strong biceps were clearly visible as she carried the boxes.
The biceps contracts to bend the arm at the elbow joint.

Related Terms

  • antonym: triceps - a muscle with three origins, especially at the back of the upper arm
