inclined

adjective
having a tendency or preference
3.8 14/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆklaÉªnd / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆklaÉªnd / (BrE)
having a tendency or preference towards something; likely to have a particular attitude or act in a certain way, often used with 'to' or 'towards'

Examples

Inclined to agree
He is inclined to forget people's names.
I'm not inclined to agree with you on this matter.
She felt inclined to accept the offer despite her reservations.

Synonyms

  • disposed
    5.9 0.00/m
    disposed is often formal and used with 'to' for a steady tendency, while inclined means a general tendency or preference.
  • given
    4.3 0.00/m
    given often appears as 'given to' to show a habitual behavior, while inclined names a general tendency or preference.
  • prone
    4.0 12/m
    prone often suggests a likely, usually negative tendency, while inclined is neutral and shows a general preference.
  • minded
    4.9 26/m
    minded often expresses willingness or intent in a situation, while inclined refers to a general tendency or preference.

  • synonym: disposed - having a tendency or willingness
