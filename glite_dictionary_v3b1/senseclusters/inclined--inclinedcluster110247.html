inclined

adjective
having a tendency or preference
5.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈklaɪnd / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪnd / (BrE)
having a tendency or preference towards something; likely to have a particular attitude or act in a certain way, often used with 'to' or 'towards'

Examples

Inclined to agree
He is inclined to forget people's names.
I'm not inclined to agree with you on this matter.
She felt inclined to accept the offer despite her reservations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: averse - having a strong dislike or opposition to something
  • synonym: disposed - having a tendency or willingness
