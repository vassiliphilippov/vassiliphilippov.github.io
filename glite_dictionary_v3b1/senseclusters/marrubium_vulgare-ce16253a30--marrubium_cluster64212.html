Marrubium vulgare

noun
aromatic herb used medicinally and as flavoring
Pronunciation: / məˈruːbiəm vʌlˈgɛər / (AmE), / məˈruːbɪəm vʌlˈɡeə / (BrE)
A European aromatic herb with hairy leaves and white flowers; its leaves yield a bitter extract used medicinally and as flavoring.

Examples

Marrubium vulgare leaves
Marrubium vulgare is commonly used in herbal remedies for coughs and digestive issues.
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of Marrubium vulgare extracts in their clinical trials.
The bitter taste of Marrubium vulgare makes it a distinctive ingredient in traditional candies.
