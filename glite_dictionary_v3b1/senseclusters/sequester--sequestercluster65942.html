sequester

verb
to set apart; to isolate
9.3 6.4
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈkwes.tər/ /; / /sə.ˈkwes.tər/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈkwes.tə/ /; / /sə.ˈkwes.tə/ (BrE)
to isolate or set someone or something apart from others, usually for a specific reason

Examples

To sequester the jury
During the trial, the judge decided to sequester the jury to prevent outside influence.
Researchers sequester specimens in a controlled environment for study.
To stop the spread of infection, doctors may sequester affected patients from others.
During the trial, the jury was sequestered to prevent outside influence.
She sequestered herself in the library for days to finish her thesis.
The scientists sequestered the contaminated samples away from the rest of the lab.

  • synonym: isolate - to separate something or someone from others
  • antonym: release - to allow something or someone to return to the group or public
