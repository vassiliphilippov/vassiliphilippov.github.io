maturity

noun
date when obligation must be repaid
5.5
Pronunciation: / məˈtʃʊrəti, məˈtɜrəti / (AmE), / məˈtjʊərɪti / (BrE)
The date on which a financial obligation, such as a loan or bond, must be repaid in full.

Examples

Maturity date
At maturity, the borrower must pay back the full amount of the loan.
Many investors prefer instruments with a short maturity for greater liquidity.
The bond reaches maturity in ten years, at which time the principal will be repaid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: due date - the date when payment or an obligation is due
