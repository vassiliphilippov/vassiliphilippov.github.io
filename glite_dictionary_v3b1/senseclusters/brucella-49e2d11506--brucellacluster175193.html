Brucella

noun
genus of bacteria causing brucellosis
Pronunciation: / bruˈsɛlə / (AmE), / bruˈsɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of aerobic Gram-negative bacteria that cause brucellosis in humans and animals, sometimes used as a bioweapon

Examples

Brucella bacteria infection
Brucella infections can be contracted through unpasteurized dairy products.
Researchers are developing new tests to quickly identify Brucella in laboratory samples.
The possibility of Brucella being used as a bioweapon is a concern in biosecurity circles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brucellosis - an infectious disease caused by Brucella bacteria
