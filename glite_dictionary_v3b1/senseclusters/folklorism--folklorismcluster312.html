folklorism

noun
adaptation or use of folklore outside original context
3.7 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkˌlɔrˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkˌlɔːˌɪzəm / (BrE)
The adaptation, invention, or use of elements of folklore outside their original cultural context, often for commercial, artistic, or social purposes.

Examples

Folklorism in culture
Her thesis explored the impact of folklorism on national identity in post-war Eastern Europe.
Some critics argue that folklorism can distort the original meaning of traditional customs.
The souvenir industry in many countries is a prime example of folklorism in action.
