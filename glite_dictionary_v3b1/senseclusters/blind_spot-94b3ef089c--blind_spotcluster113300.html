blind spot

noun
area of retina insensitive to light
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪnd spɑt / (AmE), / ˈblaɪnd spɒt / (BrE)
the small area on the retina where the optic nerve enters the eye and where no light-sensitive cells are present, resulting in an absence of vision at that spot

Examples

Eye blind spot
Doctors often explain the blind spot when teaching about the human eye.
Every person has a natural blind spot where the optic nerve exits the retina.
The image fell on my blind spot, so I couldn't see it clearly.
