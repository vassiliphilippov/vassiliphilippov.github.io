whim

noun
a sudden desire or impulse
Pronunciation: / wɪm / (AmE), / wɪm / (BrE)
a sudden and unpredictable desire or idea, often without a clear reason

Examples

A whim decision
At the last minute, they changed their plans just for a whim.
He painted his bedroom purple on a whim.
On a whim, she decided to book a flight to Paris for the weekend.
He bought the bright red shoes on a whim, with no real need for them.
On a whim, she decided to take a weekend trip to the coast, leaving her plans behind.
Sometimes, the best adventures happen when you follow a whim rather than a plan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caprice - a sudden, unpredictable change, as of one's mind or the weather
  • synonym: impulse - a sudden strong urge to act
  • synonym: notion - a belief or idea, especially a vague or fanciful one
  • antonym: plan - an intention or decision about what one is going to do
