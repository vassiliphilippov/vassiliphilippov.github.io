seal

noun
official stamp or emblem for documents
Pronunciation: / siːl / (AmE), / siːl / (BrE)
an official mark, stamp, or device used to show authenticity, approval, or to close something securely (often with wax or an emblem)

Examples

Official seal on a document
A notary public must apply an official seal to the certificate.
She broke the wax seal to read the confidential letter.
The contract was made official with the company's seal at the bottom.
Only certified goods are marked with the official seal.
She received the editor's seal of approval before publishing the article.
The product carries a seal of quality from the industry association.
Before posting letters, she pressed her signet ring into the warm seal to mark them.
He broke the seal on the envelope to read the contents inside.
The official document was closed with a red wax seal bearing the royal insignia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disapproval - an expression or indication of not approving
  • synonym: emblem - a symbol or device representing official approval or authority
  • antonym: signature - a person's written name to authenticate a document
  • synonym: stamp - an instrument for impressing a mark or design
