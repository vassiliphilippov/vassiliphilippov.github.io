indent

verb
make a notch or depression
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɛnt / (BrE)
to make a notch, depression, or jagged cut in the surface or edge of something

Examples

To indent the surface
Please be careful not to indent the metal lid when closing it.
Repeated strikes from the hammer indented the surface of the workbench.
The heavy box indented the wooden floor, leaving a visible mark.
He used a sharp knife to indent the edge of the leather belt.
The artist indented the metal with a series of small cuts.
Years of use had indented the rim of the coin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dent - to make a small hollow in a surface by hitting it
  • synonym: notch - to cut a V-shaped or other shaped groove or indentation
