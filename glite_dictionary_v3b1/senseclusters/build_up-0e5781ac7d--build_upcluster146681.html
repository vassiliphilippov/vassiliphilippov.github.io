build up

verb
accumulate gradually
Pronunciation: / bɪld ʌp / (AmE), / bɪld ʌp / (BrE)
to gradually increase, accumulate, or make something stronger or greater over time

Examples

Build up stress
A lot of stress tends to build up before big exams.
Plaque can build up on your teeth if you don't brush regularly.
Tension began to build up between the two countries after the incident.
Over time, dust tends to build up on unused shelves.
Regular exercise will build up your strength and stamina.
She worked hard to build up her reputation in the industry over many years.
Exercise can help you build up your strength after an illness.
He gradually built up his confidence through practice and encouragement.
The police built up security around the city during the championship.
