darken

verb
make or become darker
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑrkən / (AmE), / ˈdɑːkən / (BrE)
to make or become dark or less bright, either literally (like color or light) or metaphorically (like mood)

Examples

Clouds darken the sky
As the evening progressed, the watercolors in the painting seemed to darken.
Please darken the room by closing the curtains before the presentation.
The clouds began to darken the sky as the storm approached.
Her mood seemed to darken after hearing the bad news.
The room darkened when the lights went out.
The sky began to darken as the storm approached.

  • antonym: lighten - to make or become lighter (in color or brightness)
