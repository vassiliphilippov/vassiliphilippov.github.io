interpretative dance

noun
modern dance expressing emotion or a story
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɝːprəˈteɪtɪv dæns / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːprɪˈteɪtɪv dɑːns / (BrE)
A form of modern dance in which the dancer's movements depict emotions or tell a story rather than follow a set choreography.

Examples

Interpretative dance performance
During the competition, his interpretative dance depicted the struggle between hope and despair.
She performed an interpretative dance that moved the entire audience to tears.
The festival featured an interpretative dance based on the myth of Persephone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modern dance - a style of dance that developed in the early 20th century as a reaction to classical ballet
