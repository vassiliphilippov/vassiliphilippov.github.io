working rule

noun
practical rule for getting work done
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːkɪŋ ruːl / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkɪŋ ruːl / (BrE)
a rule or guideline that is sufficient or practical for work to be carried out, though it may not be perfect or final

Examples

A working rule
Although imperfect, the working rule was effective in ensuring safety at the construction site.
Engineers established a working rule for testing all parts before assembly.
The team followed a simple working rule to stay on schedule despite the project's challenges.
