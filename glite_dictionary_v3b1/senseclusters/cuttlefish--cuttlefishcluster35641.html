cuttlefish

noun
a marine cephalopod with ten arms and a cuttlebone
8.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌt.əl.fɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈkʌt.əl.fɪʃ/ (BrE)
a marine animal of the cephalopod class, with an oval body, ten arms, narrow fins running the length of the body, and a large, internal calcareous shell called a cuttlebone

Examples

Marine biologists studied the camouflage abilities of the cuttlefish.
The cuttlefish uses its ink to escape predators in the ocean.
The aquarium featured a display of living cuttlefish that amazed visitors.
