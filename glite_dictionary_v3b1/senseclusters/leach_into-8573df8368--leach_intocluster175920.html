leach into

verb
gradually seep or flow into something
6.8 6.0
Pronunciation: /liːtʃ ˈɪn.tuː/ /; / /liːtʃ ˈɪn.tə/ (AmE), /liːtʃ ˈɪn.tuː/ /; / /liːtʃ ˈɪn.tə/ (BrE)
(of a substance or quality) to gradually seep or flow into something, often by the action of liquid, or to slowly become part of an area, situation, or person's thoughts.

Examples

Salt can leach into the soil after heavy rainfall, affecting plant growth.
