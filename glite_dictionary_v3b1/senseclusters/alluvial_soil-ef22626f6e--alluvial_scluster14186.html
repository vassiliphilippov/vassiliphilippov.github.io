alluvial soil

noun
fertile soil deposited by water
7.7
Pronunciation: / əˈluvi.əl sɔɪl / (AmE), / əˈluːvɪəl sɔɪl / (BrE)
a fine-grained, fertile soil deposited by running water, typically found on flood plains or in river beds

Examples

Fertile alluvial soil
Much of the fertile land in Egypt consists of alluvial soil brought by the Nile's floods.
Over centuries, alluvial soil has built up in the valley, creating rich farmland.
The farmers found that alluvial soil along the river was ideal for growing crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - sediment deposited by flowing water, especially as a distinct layer
