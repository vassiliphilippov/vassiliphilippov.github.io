straight-laced

adjective
very proper; morally strict
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtˌleɪst / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtˌleɪst / (BrE)
Having very strict or conservative moral standards; extremely proper or prudish.

Examples

A straight-laced person
Despite her straight-laced reputation, she occasionally enjoyed a harmless prank.
He was considered too straight-laced for the relaxed culture of the startup company.
Her parents were so straight-laced that she was not allowed to attend parties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permissive - allowing great or excessive freedom of behavior
  • synonym: prim - very formal and proper in manner or appearance
