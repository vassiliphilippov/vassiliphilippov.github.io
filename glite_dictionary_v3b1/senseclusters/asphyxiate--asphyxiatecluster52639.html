asphyxiate

verb
cause inability to breathe; suffocate
6.0 7.4
Pronunciation: /æs.ˈfɪk.siː.eɪt/ /; / /əs.ˈfɪk.siː.eɪt/ (AmE), /əs.ˈfɪk.siː.eɪt/ (BrE)
to prevent someone or something from breathing, usually by depriving them of oxygen, causing suffocation or even death

Examples

To asphyxiate a person
He tried to asphyxiate the fire by covering it with a blanket.
Some chemicals can asphyxiate fish if released into rivers.
The leaking gas threatened to asphyxiate everyone in the room before anyone could escape.
A plastic bag over his head nearly caused him to asphyxiate before help arrived.
Medical staff acted quickly so the patient did not asphyxiate during the operation.
Without proper ventilation, chemicals can asphyxiate workers in confined spaces.
Failure to remove the obstruction could asphyxiate the patient within minutes.
Some poisonous gases can quickly asphyxiate anyone who inhales them.
The victim began to asphyxiate after being trapped in the smoke-filled room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choke - to prevent someone from breathing by blocking the throat
  • synonym: suffocate - to die or cause to die from lack of air; synonym for asphyxiate
