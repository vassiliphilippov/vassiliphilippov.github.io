consociation

noun
power-sharing system
9.9
Pronunciation: /kən.soʊ.sɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kɑːn.soʊ.sɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒn.səʊ.sɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.səʊ.sɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a political system in which different social, religious, or ethnic groups share power and govern together

Examples

Political consociation

Lebanon is often cited as an example of a country governed by consociation among its major religious groups.
Some scholars argue that consociation can help prevent conflict in deeply divided societies.
