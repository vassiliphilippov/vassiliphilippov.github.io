Zea

noun
genus of grasses including maize/corn
Pronunciation: / ˈziː.ə / (AmE), / ˈziː.ə / (BrE)
A genus of large grasses in the family Poaceae, including maize (corn), cultivated as a cereal crop.

Examples

Zea genus plants
Researchers are studying the genetics of Zea to improve crop yields.
Several wild varieties of Zea can be found in Central America.
The species Zea mays is widely grown for its edible kernels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corn - common cereal plant cultivated for its grain, especially maize (Zea mays)
