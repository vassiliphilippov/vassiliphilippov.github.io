Synonyms

  • dissent
    6.6 6.11/m
    dissent is public or formal disagreement spoken against policies, while objection is a general expression of opposition in many settings.
  • remonstrance
    9.8 0.25/m
    remonstrance is a formal, earnest complaint made to officials, while objection is a common word for any expressed disapproval.
  • expostulation
    9.9 0.09/m
    expostulation is a reasoned, often private protest to persuade someone, while objection can be any spoken or written opposition.
  • protest
    2.5 39/m
    protest is a public, sometimes loud demonstration against actions, while objection is a general expression of disagreement in many contexts.
  • challenge
    3.5 8.60/m
    challenge is formally questioning or disputing a decision or rule, while objection is a spoken or written expression of dislike or opposition.
  • contestation
    8.4 0.77/m
    contestation is a strong public argument or dispute over facts, while objection is an act of saying you disagree.
  • disapproval
    3.0 4.53/m
    disapproval is a feeling or expression of dislike or low opinion, while objection is an active statement of opposition.
  • disagreement
    1.6 18/m
    disagreement is a difference of opinion between people, while objection is a clear act of expressing opposition or complaint.
