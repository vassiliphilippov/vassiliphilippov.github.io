closed primary

noun
primary where only party members can vote
7.1 5.8
Pronunciation: /kloʊzd ˈpraɪ.mə.riː/ (AmE), /kləʊzd ˈpraɪ.mə.riː/ (BrE)
a primary election in which only registered members of a specific political party are allowed to vote

Examples

A closed primary election
In a closed primary, independent voters are not permitted to participate.
Some states hold a closed primary to ensure that only party loyalists decide the nominee.
The closed primary system aims to prevent members of other parties from influencing the selection process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open primary - a primary election where voters are not required to declare party affiliation
