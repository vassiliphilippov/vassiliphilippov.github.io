neocortex

noun
part of brain's cerebral cortex
Pronunciation: / ˌniː.oʊˈkɔːr.teks / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈkɔː.teks / (BrE)
the part of the cerebral cortex concerned with higher-order brain functions such as sensory perception, cognition, and generation of motor commands

Examples

The neocortex area
Damage to the neocortex can severely affect a person's ability to process language and sensory information.
Primates have a larger and more complex neocortex compared to most other mammals.
The study focused on the role of the neocortex in problem-solving and decision-making.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cortex - the outer layer of an organ, especially the brain
