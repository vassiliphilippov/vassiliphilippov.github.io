Examples

Synonyms

  • excessively
    3.0 3.81/m
    excessively means doing or having too much in a general way, while inordinately stresses an unusually large or extreme amount.
  • overly
    3.5 14/m
    overly is used before adjectives to mean too much in a simple way, while inordinately feels more formal and extreme.
  • exorbitantly
    7.3 0.16/m
    exorbitantly describes costs or amounts that are unreasonably high, while inordinately means unusually large in any context.
  • unduly
    6.9 2.35/m
    unduly suggests something is unfairly or unnecessarily beyond reason, while inordinately simply means excessively large or extreme.
