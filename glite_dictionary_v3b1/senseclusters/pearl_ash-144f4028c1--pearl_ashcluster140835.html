pearl ash

noun
impure potassium carbonate
Pronunciation: / pɝl æʃ / (AmE), / pɜːl æʃ / (BrE)
An impure form of potassium carbonate, formerly used especially in baking and soap-making.

Examples

Pure pearl ash
Early bakers commonly used pearl ash as a leavening agent before modern baking powder was developed.
In the 18th century, pearl ash was an important export commodity for American colonists.
The production of pearl ash involves extracting soluble salts from the ashes of burned wood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: potash - another form of potassium carbonate from plant ashes
