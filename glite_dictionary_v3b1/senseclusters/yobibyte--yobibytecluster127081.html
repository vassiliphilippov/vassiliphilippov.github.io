yobibyte

noun
unit of 2^80 bytes in computing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjoʊbɪˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈjɒbɪˌbaɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information storage equal to 1,208,925,819,614,629,174,706,176 bytes (2^80 bytes) or 1024 zebibytes.

Examples

One yobibyte unit
A single yobibyte can store an enormous amount of data, far more than today’s largest hard drives.
Cloud storage capacities have not yet reached the yobibyte scale, but future technologies may demand it.
Scientists discussed petabyte, exabyte, and even yobibyte archives for storing astronomical data.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: kibibyte - a unit of information equal to 1024 bytes or 2^10 bytes
  • synonym: yottabyte - a unit of information equal to 10^24 bytes (uses decimal multiple, not binary)
  • degree lower: zebibyte - a unit of information equal to 1024 exbibytes or 2^70 bytes
