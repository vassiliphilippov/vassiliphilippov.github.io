riverboat

noun
boat for rivers
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvərˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈrɪvəˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a boat designed for travel on rivers, often used for transport, leisure, or entertainment (sometimes historic or with paddle wheels)

Examples

A riverboat tour
They took a historic riverboat tour along the famous river.
The riverboat docked at several small towns during its journey.
We watched the sunset from the deck of a riverboat on the water.
The old riverboat glided slowly past the willow trees along the shore.
They booked a dinner cruise on a historic riverboat for their anniversary.
Cargo was transported down the Mississippi by riverboats in the nineteenth century.
Tourists enjoyed a scenic ride on the historic riverboat as it traveled down the Mississippi.
The old riverboat was once used to transport cotton and other goods along the river.
He dreamed of working as a captain on a riverboat when he was a child.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barge - a flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight on rivers and canals
  • synonym: ferry - a boat that carries people or goods across a river or narrow body of water
  • synonym: steamboat - a boat powered by steam, often used on rivers
