muscle

noun
tissue that contracts to produce movement
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌs.əl /; / ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.l̩ /; / ˈmʌsəl / (BrE)
a tissue in the body that can contract to produce movement or maintain position

Examples

Strong muscle
Athletes often train to build more muscle and improve performance.
Flexing your muscle is a common way to show strength.
He strained a muscle in his leg while running the marathon.
Regular exercise strengthens your muscle and improves overall health.
She strained a muscle while lifting heavy boxes.
The heart is a powerful muscle that pumps blood throughout the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ligament - a band of tissue connecting bones or supporting organs
  • synonym: tendon - a cord of strong tissue connecting muscle to bone
