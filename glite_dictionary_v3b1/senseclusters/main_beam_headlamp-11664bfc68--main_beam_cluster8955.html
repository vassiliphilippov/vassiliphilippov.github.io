main beam headlamp

noun
bright forward-facing headlamp on a car
Pronunciation: / meɪn bim ˈhɛdˌlæmp / (AmE), / meɪn biːm ˈhɛdlæmp / (BrE)
A car headlamp that provides a bright, centre-weighted distribution of light primarily for use when driving without oncoming traffic, as it is liable to dazzle other drivers.

Examples

A main beam headlamp
Make sure to dip your main beam headlamp when another vehicle approaches from the opposite direction.
The car manual explains how to change the bulb in the main beam headlamp safely.
When driving late at night on empty country roads, I always switch on the main beam headlamp for better visibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dipped headlamp - A car headlamp which casts light downward to avoid dazzling other drivers
  • synonym: high-beam headlight - A headlight that provides a bright, unshielded beam for maximum road illumination
