Mark Antony

proper noun
Roman general and lover of Cleopatra
6.9
Pronunciation: /mɑːrk ˈæn.tə.niː/ (AmE), /mɑːk ˈæn.tə.niː/ (BrE)
(c. 83–30 BC) A Roman general and politician, ally of Julius Caesar, and lover of Cleopatra, who was defeated by Octavian at the Battle of Actium.

Examples

Mark Antony the general
After Julius Caesar's death, Mark Antony formed an alliance with Octavian and Lepidus known as the Second Triumvirate.
Historians often debate the political ambitions of Mark Antony during Rome's transition from Republic to Empire.
Shakespeare's play 'Antony and Cleopatra' dramatizes the tragic romance between Cleopatra and Mark Antony.
