Pope Pius VII

proper noun
Italian pope (1800–1823), prisoner of Napoleon
8.6
Pronunciation: /poʊp ˈpaɪ.əs ðə ˈse.vənθ/ (AmE), /pəʊp ˈpaɪ.əs ðə ˈse.vənθ/ (BrE)
(1740–1823) Italian pope from 1800 to 1823, known for being taken prisoner by Napoleon, concluding a concordat with him, and crowning him Emperor of France.

Examples

Pope Pius VII crowned Napoleon
Pope Pius VII was forced into exile by Napoleon but later returned to Rome in 1814.
Napoleon was crowned emperor by Pope Pius VII in a grand ceremony in 1804.
The concordat signed by Pope Pius VII helped reestablish the Catholic Church in France.
