excessively fond of eating or drinking; having an insatiable appetite, especially for food or drink

Examples

A gluttonous eater
After a gluttonous holiday feast, she felt uncomfortably full.
His gluttonous habits led to frequent visits to the doctor.
The gluttonous child ate three desserts before anyone else finished their main course.

Synonyms

  • voracious
    7.5 1.64/m
    voracious is eager for large amounts of food or other things, while gluttonous is mainly about eating too much.
  • edacious
    9.9 0.01/m
    edacious describes a very great appetite for food and appears in formal writing, while gluttonous describes eating too much.
  • wolfish
    7.2 0.01/m
    wolfish looks hungry and fierce, like a wild animal ready to eat, while gluttonous focuses on eating too much food.
  • hoggish
    9.1 0.01/m
    hoggish suggests rude, coarse greed and bad manners at the table, while gluttonous simply means having an insatiable appetite.
