to pull or drag something with effort or force, especially slowly or heavily

Examples

To haul a car
A truck hauled the broken-down car to the nearest garage.
The sailors hauled the heavy nets out of the water early each morning.
Workers haul logs out of the forest using powerful machines.

Synonyms

  • pull along
    3.0 0.5/m
    pull along moves something forward by dragging it with steady effort, while haul stresses heavy, slow pulling over distance.
  • drag
    2.0 30/m
    drag pulls or moves something along the ground with effort, while haul suggests a heavier, slower pull of a large load.
  • lug
    4.4 1/m
    lug means carrying or dragging a heavy awkward object by hand with effort, while haul implies long, heavy pulling.
  • tow
    2.0 3/m
    tow pulls something, often a vehicle, behind another vehicle with a rope or chain, while haul is more general heavy pulling.
  • heave
    3.9 3/m
    heave means give a single strong lift or throw to a heavy object, while haul means pull slowly with steady force.

  • antonym: push - to move something away from oneself by applying force
  • synonym: tug - to pull something suddenly or with force
