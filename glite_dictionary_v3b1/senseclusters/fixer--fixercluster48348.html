fixer

noun
a chemical for making something permanent, especially in photography or dyeing
3.6 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪk.sər/ (AmE), /ˈfɪk.sə/ (BrE)
a chemical compound used to make something permanent, such as fixing dyes to fabrics or stabilizing photographic images so they are no longer sensitive to light

Examples

Photographic fixer
After dyeing the fabric, a fixer is applied to prevent the color from washing out.
Make sure to rinse out the fixer thoroughly during the development process.
The photographic fixer ensures that the image will not fade when exposed to light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fixative - a substance used to fix or preserve something, especially in chemistry and art
