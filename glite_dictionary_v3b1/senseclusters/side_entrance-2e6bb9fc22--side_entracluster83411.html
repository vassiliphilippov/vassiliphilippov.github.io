side entrance

noun
exterior door on a building's side
Pronunciation: / saɪd ˈɛntrəns / (AmE), / saɪd ˈɛntrəns / (BrE)
an exterior door located at one side of a building, typically used as an alternative to the main entrance

Examples

The side entrance door
Deliveries must be made at the side entrance between 8 AM and 5 PM.
She slipped in quietly through the side entrance to avoid being noticed.
Visitors were asked to use the side entrance while repairs were being carried out on the main doors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main entrance - the principal door or way into a building
