reinjure

verb
to hurt emotionally again
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪndʒə / (BrE)
to cause emotional or psychological pain again, especially by bringing up a past hurtful experience

Examples

To reinjure feelings
She was careful not to reinjure his feelings by mentioning the painful event.
Talking about the past can sometimes reinjure old emotional wounds.
He apologized, hoping not to reinjure her by bringing up the subject again.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heal - to make or become healthy or whole again
  • synonym: reopen - to bring up or start again, especially a past issue or wound
