climb

verb
increase in value or amount
Pronunciation: / klaɪm / (AmE), / klaɪm / (BrE)
to increase or rise to a higher level, position, or status (such as prices, confidence, or social rank)

Examples

Climb in prices
House prices continued to climb throughout the summer, making it harder for first-time buyers.
If fuel costs climb any higher, travel will become much more expensive.
The company's stock has climbed steadily over the last quarter, impressing investors.
Ambitious politicians often climb quickly by forging influential connections.
He worked tirelessly to climb the corporate hierarchy.
Many people hope to climb the social ladder through education and networking.
After months of preparation, his confidence finally began to climb.
Profits continued to climb throughout the year, reaching a record high in December.
Temperatures are expected to climb into the mid-80s this weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance - to move forward or make progress, especially in rank or status
  • antonym: descend - to move to a lower social or professional position
  • antonym: fall - to decrease in value or amount
  • synonym: rise - to move upwards or increase to a higher level
