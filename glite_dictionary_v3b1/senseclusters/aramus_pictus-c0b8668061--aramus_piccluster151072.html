Aramus pictus

noun
species of wading bird (limpkin)
Pronunciation: / əˈræməs ˈpɪktəs / (AmE), / əˈraːmʊs ˈpɪktəs / (BrE)
A species of wading bird found in Florida, Cuba, and Jamaica, known for its drooping bill and distinctive wailing call; commonly called the limpkin.

Examples

The Aramus pictus bird
Naturalists are fascinated by the haunting call of the Aramus pictus at dawn.
The Aramus pictus is often spotted in wetland habitats across Florida.
The Aramus pictus uses its specially adapted bill to feed on apple snails.
