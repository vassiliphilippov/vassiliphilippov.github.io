genitive

adjective
expressing possession (grammar)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnɪtɪv / (BrE)
(of a grammatical case) expressing possession or close association, typically marked in English by the apostrophe-s ('s) or by a possessive pronoun.

Examples

Genitive form
In Latin, the genitive case is used to show ownership or relationship.
Most English nouns form the genitive by adding an apostrophe and s, as in 'the girl's book'.
The phrase 'the president's office' uses the genitive construction to show possession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: possessive - indicating possession, especially a grammatical form
