rigidify

verb
become or make rigid
6.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.dɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈrɪdʒ.ə.dɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /ˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.dɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈrɪdʒ.ə.dɪ.faɪ/ (BrE)
to become or make something rigid, stiff, or inflexible (physically or in attitude)

Examples

In fear, her muscles seemed to rigidify and she couldn't move.
The artist's new technique helps colors stand out but may also rigidify the canvas.
Under the intense cold, the metal quickly began to rigidify.
If you rigidify your approach, you may miss out on creative solutions.
The company decided to rigidify its safety protocols after the recent incidents.
The lawyer warned that new regulations could rigidify business practices throughout the industry.
