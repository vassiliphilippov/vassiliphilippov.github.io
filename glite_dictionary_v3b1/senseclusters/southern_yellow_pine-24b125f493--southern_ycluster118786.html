southern yellow pine

noun
a large southeastern US timber pine
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌðərn ˈjɛloʊ paɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈjɛləʊ paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree native to the southeastern United States, known for its long needles, twisted limbs, deeply ridged red-brown bark, and value as a timber tree

Examples

A southern yellow pine tree
A grove of southern yellow pine stretches across the landscape in Georgia.
Many barns in the region were built with sturdy southern yellow pine harvested locally.
The dense wood of the southern yellow pine makes it ideal for heavy construction and flooring.
