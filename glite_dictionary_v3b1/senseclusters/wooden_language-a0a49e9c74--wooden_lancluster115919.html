wooden language

noun
stilted, overly formal language
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwʊd.ən ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
language that is stiff, overly formal, and lacks emotion or naturalness

Examples

Wooden language style
Journalists criticized the politician for relying on wooden language in her address.
The manager's update was full of wooden language, making it hard to connect with the team.
Try to avoid wooden language in your essays and write in a more engaging style.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural language - language that is natural and flowing, not stiff or formal
  • synonym: stilted - (of language) stiff, unnatural, and overly formal
