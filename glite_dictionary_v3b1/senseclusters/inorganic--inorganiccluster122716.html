inorganic

adjective
not from living organisms
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɔːrˈɡænɪk /; / ˌɪnɔrˈɡænɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnɔːˈɡænɪk / (BrE)
not coming from living things; (in chemistry) not containing carbon or not derived from living matter

Examples

Inorganic material
Glass is considered inorganic because it does not come from a living source.
The scientist studied several inorganic compounds found in rocks.
The soil in this region contains both inorganic matter and decaying plant material.
Hydrochloric acid is classified as an inorganic compound in chemistry.
The study of inorganic chemistry focuses on elements and compounds that lack carbon atoms.
Unlike sugars and proteins, minerals are considered inorganic nutrients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: organic - relating to or derived from living organisms
