mandate

noun
official order or command document
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmænˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
an official document giving an order, instruction, or authority to act

Examples

Court mandate
A court mandate was delivered to the company, compelling it to cease operations immediately.
The committee's mandate outlines the specific tasks it is authorized to perform.
The government issued a mandate requiring all citizens to wear masks in public spaces.
