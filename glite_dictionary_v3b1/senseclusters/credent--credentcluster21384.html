credent

noun
believer; accepter
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkriːdənt / (AmE), / ˈkriːdənt / (BrE)
an individual who believes or accepts something as true or trustworthy, especially without demanding proof (archaic)

Examples

A credent follower
In ancient times, a credent might follow a leader without question.
The philosopher warned against being a blind credent in matters of faith.
Few credents remained after the new evidence was revealed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: believer - a person who accepts something as true
  • antonym: skeptic - a person who doubts or questions accepted beliefs
