Edward VII

proper noun
British king, reigned 1901–1910
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdwərd ðə ˈsɛvənθ / (AmE), / ˈɛdwəd ði ˈsɛvənθ / (BrE)
(1841–1910) King of the United Kingdom and the British Dominions from 1901 to 1910, son of Queen Victoria, known for his stylish and sporting lifestyle.

Examples

Edward VII king
As a monarch, Edward VII played a key role in modernising the British royal family.
Portraits of Edward VII can still be found in many historic buildings across Britain.
The Edwardian era, named for Edward VII, was a time of significant social change.
