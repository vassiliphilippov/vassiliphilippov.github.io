carob tree

noun
evergreen Mediterranean tree with edible pods
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrəb triː / (AmE), / ˈkærəb triː / (BrE)
an evergreen Mediterranean tree (Ceratonia siliqua) that produces edible pods, often used as a chocolate substitute

Examples

The carob tree pods
During ancient times, seeds from the carob tree were used as a unit of weight.
The carob tree is widely cultivated in Mediterranean countries for its nutritious pods.
They planted a carob tree in their garden to enjoy its shade and sweet pods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ceratonia siliqua - the scientific name for the carob tree
  • synonym: locust tree - a tree whose pods resemble those of the carob tree
