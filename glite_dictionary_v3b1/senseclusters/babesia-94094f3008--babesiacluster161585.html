Babesia

proper noun
parasitic genus
Pronunciation: / bəˈbiːziə / (AmE), / bəˈbiːziə / (BrE)
a genus of microscopic parasites that infect the red blood cells of mammals, often transmitted by ticks

Examples

Tick-borne Babesia
Ticks can transmit Babesia to humans and animals, causing a disease called babesiosis.
Veterinarians often test for Babesia in dogs showing symptoms of anemia after tick bites.
Researchers study Babesia to better understand how it infects red blood cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Plasmodium - a genus of parasites that cause malaria by infecting red blood cells
