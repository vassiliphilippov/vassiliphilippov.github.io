intuitiveness

noun
ability to sense directly
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtuːɪtɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtjuːɪtɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of understanding or knowing things immediately, without the need for conscious reasoning

Examples

Her intuitiveness at work
Her intuitiveness helped her anticipate customers' questions before they asked them.
The designer's intuitiveness made the new app easy for everyone to use.
He relied on his intuitiveness to make quick decisions during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insight - the ability to gain an accurate understanding of something
  • synonym: perceptiveness - the ability to notice or understand things quickly and easily
  • antonym: rationality - the quality of being based on or in accordance with reason or logic
