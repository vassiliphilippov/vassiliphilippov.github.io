oracularly

adverb
authoritatively, decisively
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɔˈrækjələrli / (AmE), / ɔːˈrækjʊləli /; / ɔˈrækjʊləli / (BrE)
in a way that is wise, important, and possibly mysterious or hard to understand, like an oracle

Examples

To speak oracularly
The professor answered the student's question oracularly, leaving no room for doubt or debate.
He spoke oracularly about the future of technology, as if he knew exactly what would happen.
During the meeting, she oracularly declared the project a guaranteed success.
The professor answered the question oracularly, leaving the students to interpret his meaning.
He spoke oracularly about the future, hinting at changes no one could predict.
During the meeting, she oracularly suggested that big changes were coming.
