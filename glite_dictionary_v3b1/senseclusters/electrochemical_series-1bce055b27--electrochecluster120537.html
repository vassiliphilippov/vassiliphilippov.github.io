electrochemical series

noun
arrangement of elements by electrode potential
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊkəˈmɪkəl ˈsɪəriːz / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊkəˈmɪkəl ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
an ordered list of metals or ions arranged according to their electrode potentials, indicating their tendency to be reduced or oxidized in electrochemical reactions

Examples

The electrochemical series list
Copper is placed below hydrogen in the electrochemical series, making it less likely to be oxidized.
The electrochemical series helps predict which metals will undergo corrosion in a given environment.
Understanding the electrochemical series is essential for designing efficient batteries and electroplating processes.
