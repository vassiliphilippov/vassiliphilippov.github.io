fetus

noun
unborn vertebrate in late development
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːtəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːtəs / (BrE)
An unborn or unhatched vertebrate in the later stages of development, especially in the womb after the basic structure has been established.

Examples

A fetus in womb
Before birth, a human is considered a fetus during the later stages of pregnancy.
Doctors monitored the growth of the fetus throughout the pregnancy.
The ultrasound showed that the fetus was developing normally.
In some cultures, the protection of the foetus is a central ethical issue.
The doctor monitored the health of the foetus during each stage of the pregnancy.
Ultrasound scans can reveal the position and development of the foetus inside the womb.
