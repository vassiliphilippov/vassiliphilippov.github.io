deva

noun
Hindu or Buddhist god
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪvə / (AmE), / ˈdeɪvə / (BrE)
a god or spiritual being in Hinduism and related Indian religions

Examples

Hindu deva
The villagers offered prayers to the local deva for a good harvest.
In many stories, a deva intervenes to help humans in times of trouble.
The ancient temple is believed to be the home of a powerful deva.
In Hindu mythology, each deva is associated with specific powers and responsibilities.
The festival celebrates the victory of a deva over evil forces.
Many temples are dedicated to a particular deva revered by the local community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asura - a class of powerful beings often opposed to the devas in Hindu mythology
  • synonym: deity - a god or goddess; a divine being
  • synonym: god - a supernatural being worshipped as having power over nature or human fortunes
