nonviolence

noun
refusal to use violence for change
4.7 4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈvaɪələns / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈvaɪələns / (BrE)
the policy or practice of rejecting violence in favor of peaceful methods to bring about social or political change, such as through protest, fasting, or refusal to cooperate

Examples

Practice of nonviolence
Gandhi is famous for leading a movement based on nonviolence and civil disobedience.
She believes that true change comes through nonviolence, not through aggression or conflict.
The activists used nonviolence as their primary strategy to protest against unjust laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pacifism - the belief in peaceful opposition to war or violence
  • antonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill
