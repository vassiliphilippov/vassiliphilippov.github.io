nonviolence

noun
refusal to use violence for change

Examples

Practice of nonviolence
Gandhi is famous for leading a movement based on nonviolence and civil disobedience.
She believes that true change comes through nonviolence, not through aggression or conflict.
The activists used nonviolence as their primary strategy to protest against unjust laws.

Synonyms

  • nonviolent resistance
    3.9 0.2/m
    nonviolent resistance uses public tactics like protests, strikes, or civil disobedience to press for change, while nonviolence is a broader rejection of violence.
  • passive resistance
    5.6 0.2/m
    passive resistance refuses to cooperate, often by fasting or quiet boycotts, while nonviolence is a wider policy of avoiding violence.
  • pacifism
    6.2 0.8/m
    pacifism holds that violence is wrong in all cases and opposes war, while nonviolence focuses on peaceful methods to achieve social change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pacifism - the belief in peaceful opposition to war or violence
  • antonym: violence - behavior involving physical force intended to hurt, damage, or kill
