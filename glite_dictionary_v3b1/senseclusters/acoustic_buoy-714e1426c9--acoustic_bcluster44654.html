acoustic buoy

noun
sound-emitting navigation buoy
Pronunciation: / əˈkuːstɪk ˈbuːi / (AmE), / əˈkuːstɪk ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
A buoy equipped with a sound-producing device, allowing it to be heard, especially at night or in poor visibility.

Examples

The acoustic buoy sounds
At night, the sound from the acoustic buoy helped fishermen locate the harbor entrance.
Engineers installed a new acoustic buoy near the rocky coastline for safer navigation.
The ship's crew relied on the acoustic buoy to guide their way through dense fog.
