number agreement

noun
grammatical agreement in number
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌm.bɚ əˌɡriː.mənt / (AmE), / ˈnʌm.bə əˌɡriː.mənt / (BrE)
the grammatical rule that requires words in a phrase or sentence, such as adjectives or verbs, to match the number (singular or plural) of the nouns they are related to

Examples

Number agreement rule
In English, number agreement requires that plural subjects take plural verbs.
Mistakes in number agreement are common for learners of foreign languages.
The teacher explained that number agreement is why we say 'these apples' instead of 'this apples'.
