hold

verb
restrain or control (emotion, impulse)
1.6 1.7
Pronunciation: /hoʊld/ (AmE), /həʊld/ (BrE)
to control, restrain, or confine someone or something; to keep within limits

Examples

To hold your anger
He had to hold his anger during the difficult meeting.
Lisa struggled to hold her laughter when she saw the surprise.
You should hold your tongue if you don't have anything nice to say.
She was held by a verbal agreement to remain with the company for two years.
The contract holds all parties responsible for maintaining confidentiality.
The manager will hold you to your commitment to finish the project on time.
Could you hold the door open until everyone is outside?
Please hold the elevator for me while I grab my bag.
She held the bus by running ahead and signaling to the driver.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confine - to keep someone or something within limits or a space
  • synonym: detain - to keep someone from proceeding; delay
  • antonym: release - to allow something to be free or expressed
  • synonym: restrain - to hold back or keep under control
