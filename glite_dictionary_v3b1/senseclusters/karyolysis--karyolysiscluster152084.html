karyolysis

noun
disintegration of a cell nucleus during death
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrioʊˈlaɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkærɪəˈlaɪsɪs / (BrE)
the dissolution of a cell nucleus during cell death, typically through enzymatic breakdown

Examples

karyolysis of nucleus
After exposure to the toxin, the liver cells showed signs of karyolysis under the microscope.
Karyolysis is often observed during the late stages of necrosis, when the cell's nucleus breaks down and disappears.
The pathologist noted extensive karyolysis in tissues affected by the acute infection.
