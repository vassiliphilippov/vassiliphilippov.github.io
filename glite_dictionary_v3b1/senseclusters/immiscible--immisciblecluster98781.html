immiscible

adjective
incapable of mixing (of liquids)
4.9 7.7
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmɪs.ə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈɪs.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmɪs.ə.bəl/ /; / /ɪm.ˈɪs.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of liquids) incapable of mixing or forming a homogeneous mixture.

Examples

Immiscible liquids
Most organic solvents are immiscible with water.
Oil and water are immiscible and form separate layers when combined.
