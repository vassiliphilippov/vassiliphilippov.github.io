brown power

noun
movement for rights of people of color
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊn ˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn ˌpaʊə / (BrE)
A social and political movement advocating for the rights, empowerment, and cultural pride of people of color, especially those of Hispanic, Latino, or South Asian heritage.

Examples

Brown power movement
Many activists of the 1970s were inspired by brown power and sought better representation in government.
The concept of brown power continues to influence cultural movements today.
The rally was organized to support brown power and demand equal rights for all communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Black Power - movement promoting black rights and empowerment
