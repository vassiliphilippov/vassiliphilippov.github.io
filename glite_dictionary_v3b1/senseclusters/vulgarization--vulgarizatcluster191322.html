vulgarization

noun
making something coarse or less refined
Pronunciation: / ˌvʌlɡərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvʌlɡərʌɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌvʌlɡəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making something coarse, unrefined, or less sophisticated

Examples

Vulgarization of art
Many claim that mass media contributes to the vulgarization of artistic traditions.
She opposed the vulgarization of the language, believing it deserved more respect.
The vulgarization of popular culture worries some critics who cherish classic standards.
Many critics argue that the vulgarisation of classical art diminishes its cultural significance.
Some believe that reality television has led to the vulgarisation of modern entertainment.
The vulgarisation of public discourse has been a subject of concern for educators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coarsening - the process of becoming less refined or more crude
  • antonym: refinement - the process of making something more elegant or cultured
