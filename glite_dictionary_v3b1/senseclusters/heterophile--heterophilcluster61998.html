heterophile

noun
antibody for other species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛtərəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
an antibody that reacts with antigens from a different species, often used in medical diagnostic tests

Examples

Heterophile antibody test
The doctor ordered a heterophile antibody test to help diagnose the illness.
A positive heterophile result can indicate infectious mononucleosis in patients.
Researchers studied how heterophile antibodies react with animal antigens in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system to fight antigens
  • antonym: homophile - an antibody that reacts with antigens from the same species
