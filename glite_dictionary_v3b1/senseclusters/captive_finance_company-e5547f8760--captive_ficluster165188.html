captive finance company

noun
manufacturer-owned finance company
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæptɪv fəˈnæns ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / ˈkæptɪv faɪˈnæns ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a finance company owned by a manufacturer and established to provide loans to dealers or consumers for purchasing the manufacturer's products

Examples

A captive finance company loan
Many electronics brands operate a captive finance company to help customers purchase their products on credit.
The captive finance company offers better loan terms than independent lenders in some cases.
The car dealer arranged my loan through the manufacturer's captive finance company.
