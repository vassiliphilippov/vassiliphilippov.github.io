home plate

noun
baseball: the scoring base in baseball
2.8 3.0
Pronunciation: /hoʊm pleɪt/ (AmE), /həʊm pleɪt/ (BrE)
(in baseball) the base consisting of a flat, five-sided rubber slab over which the batter stands and which a runner must touch to score a run.

Examples

Home plate in baseball
After hitting the home run, she proudly touched home plate to score a run.
The runner slid into home plate just ahead of the tag.
The umpire signaled that the ball was fair as it crossed over home plate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: home base - the fourth and last base in baseball that must be touched to score
