expedient

adjective
convenient but possibly improper or self-serving
6.8
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspiːdiənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspiːdiənt / (BrE)
(of an action) convenient or practical for achieving a goal, even if not ideal or proper

Examples

Expedient solution
She found it expedient to tell a white lie rather than admit her mistake.
The manager made an expedient decision that saved the company money, but it angered many employees.
While it was expedient to ignore the problem for now, it could cause trouble in the future.
It was deemed expedient to delay the meeting until more information was available.

While not ideal, taking a taxi was the most expedient way to reach the airport on time.
