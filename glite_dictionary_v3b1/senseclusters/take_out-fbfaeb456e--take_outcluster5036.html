Synonyms

  • remove
    1.5 100/m
    remove is a more formal, neutral verb meaning to take something away, while take out is more casual and everyday.
  • pull out
    2.7 20/m
    pull out means to physically remove something by effort from a place, while take out can also be used for informal or abstract removals.
  • take away
    2.3 40/m
    take away often implies removing or reducing something permanently or taking food, while take out focuses on general removal.
  • extract
    3.9 8/m
    extract means to remove something by effort or process or get hidden information, while take out is more general in use.
  • withdraw
    3.5 7/m
    withdraw is often formal for removing support, money, or troops from a place, while take out is wider and less formal.
  • draw
    1.0 13/m
    draw can mean pull something out toward you or move it, while take out stresses removing from a place or context.
  • pull
    1.0 160/m
    pull means use force to move something toward you, while take out means remove something completely from a place or group.
