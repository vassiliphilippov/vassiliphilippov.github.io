Gordian knot

noun
a complex or unsolvable problem
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔr.di.ən nɑt /; / ˈɡɔrdiən nɑt / (AmE), / ˈɡɔː.di.ən nɒt /; / ˈɡɔːdiən nɒt / (BrE)
a very difficult or seemingly unsolvable problem (from the legend of King Gordius, whose knot was cut by Alexander the Great)

Examples

The Gordian knot problem
Instead of trying to untie the Gordian knot, she made a decisive move to resolve the issue.
The merger talks turned into a Gordian knot of conflicting interests and regulations.
The political crisis quickly became a Gordian knot that no one could untangle.
Her proposal finally cut through the Gordian knot facing the committee.
The issue of climate change policy is often described as a political Gordian knot.
The merger negotiations turned into a Gordian knot that no one seemed able to unravel.
