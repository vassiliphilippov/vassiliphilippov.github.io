amercement

noun
money penalty imposed by a court
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈmɜrs.mənt / (AmE), / əˈmɜːs.mənt / (BrE)
a monetary penalty imposed by a court, especially one assessed at the discretion of the court rather than by statute

Examples

Court amercement
The judge imposed an amercement on the defendant for failing to appear in court.
The town records show that many villagers paid amercements for minor infractions during that period.
Under medieval law, offenders could be subject to heavy amercements at the discretion of local officials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fine - a sum of money exacted as a penalty by a court of law or other authority
