family Graminaceae

noun
botanical family of true grasses
Pronunciation: / ˈfæmɪli ˌɡræməˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌɡræmɪˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a large botanical family of chiefly herbaceous, occasionally woody plants, including all true grasses such as cereals, bamboos, reeds, and sugar cane

Examples

The family Graminaceae plants
Bamboo, though woody, is classified within the family Graminaceae.
Many staple crops, such as wheat and rice, belong to the family Graminaceae.
The family Graminaceae includes essential forage plants for livestock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Poaceae - the modern botanical family name for the true grasses
  • synonym: grass - herbaceous plants of the family Graminaceae/Poaceae
