force-out

noun
baseball play putting out a forced runner
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrs aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːs aʊt / (BrE)
(in baseball) a play in which a runner is put out because they are required to advance to a base and a fielder, holding the ball, touches that base before the runner arrives.

Examples

A force-out play
The shortstop made a quick throw to second base for the force-out.
There was a close force-out at third, but the umpire called the runner safe.
With two outs and the bases loaded, a simple ground ball could result in a force-out at any base.

Related Terms

  • synonym: putout - an out in baseball; the act of getting a batter or runner out
