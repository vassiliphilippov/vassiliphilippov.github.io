streakiness

noun
having visible streaks
Pronunciation: / ˈstriːkinəs / (AmE), / ˈstriːkinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having visible lines, marks, or streaks, often unevenly distributed

Examples

Streakiness of the glass
The streakiness of the glass made it difficult to see through clearly.
She was unhappy with the streakiness left by the cheap hair dye.
After painting the fence, he noticed some streakiness in the finish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even, unmarked surface
  • synonym: striped - marked with stripes or lines
