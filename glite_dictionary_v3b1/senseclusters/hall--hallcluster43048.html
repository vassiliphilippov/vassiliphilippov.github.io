hall

noun
large entrance or reception area
1.0 1.86/m
Pronunciation: / h…îl / (AmE), / h…îÀêl / (BrE)
a large entrance or reception room or area in a building, typically serving as a passage to other rooms

Examples

Entrance hall
She left her umbrella in the hall by the front door.
The guests gathered in the hall before moving to the dining room.
The museum's main hall was filled with visitors admiring the sculptures.

Synonyms

  • entrance hall
    2.8 0.53/m
    entrance hall is the area just inside a main door used for greeting and entry, while hall is a more general entrance or passage.
  • lobby
    1.3 15/m
    lobby is a spacious reception area at a building's front used by visitors and guests, while hall is a broader entrance or passage.
  • foyer
    3.9 4.08/m
    foyer is often an elegant entrance room in a house, theatre, or hotel used before entering main rooms, while hall is more general.
  • vestibule
    6.0 2.27/m
    vestibule is a small enclosed entry space just inside the outer door that blocks cold or noise, while hall is larger and more open.
  • entryway
    2.6 1.77/m
    entryway is a simple passage or doorway that gives access into a building or room, while hall is a larger entrance area linking rooms.
  • entranceway
    3.5 0.40/m
    entranceway is a path or door giving access to a place, while hall is the interior entrance or reception area inside a building.
  • reception room
    3.2 0.44/m
    reception room is a sitting room where visitors are received and entertained, while hall is mainly an entrance or passage to other rooms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corridor - a long passage in a building from which doors lead into rooms
  • synonym: foyer - entrance hall or lobby, especially in a public building or house
