rolling stock

noun
vehicles owned by a railroad or motor carrier
4.9 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.lɪŋ stɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.lɪŋ stɒk/ (BrE)
the collection of wheeled vehicles, such as locomotives and carriages, owned by a railroad or motor carrier and used on its tracks or roads

Examples

New rolling stock
Due to increased demand, the railway invested heavily in new rolling stock last year.
Most of the rolling stock in the country’s railways is maintained by a national company.
The aging rolling stock was replaced to improve the comfort and safety of train passengers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: locomotive - the powered vehicle in rolling stock that pulls trains
  • synonym: rolling equipment - alternative term for the wheeled vehicles owned by a railway
