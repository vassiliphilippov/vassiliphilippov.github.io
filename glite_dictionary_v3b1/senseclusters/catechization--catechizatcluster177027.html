catechization

noun
religious instruction
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkætɪkəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌkætɪkaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkætɪkaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of teaching or instructing someone in religious beliefs by asking and answering questions, especially in preparation for a religious ceremony

Examples

Religious catechization class
The priest led the weekly catechization of children preparing for their first communion.
Some parents value catechization as a way to pass on their faith to the next generation.
The school included catechization in its curriculum to teach students about religious doctrine.
The priest led the catechization of the children before their confirmation.
Weekly catechization sessions helped the students understand the core beliefs of their faith.
During catechization, participants were encouraged to ask questions about religious teachings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indoctrination - the process of teaching a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • synonym: instruction - the act of teaching or providing knowledge
