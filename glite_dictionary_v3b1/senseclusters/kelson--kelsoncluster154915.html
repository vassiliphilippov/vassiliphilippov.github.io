kelson

noun
source of support
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛlsən / (AmE), / ˈkɛlsən / (BrE)
a person or thing that provides essential support, strength, or stability, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Kelson of support
Her calm advice became the kelson of the committee during difficult negotiations.
In times of crisis, his steady presence was the kelson that held the team together.
The constitution serves as the kelson of the nation's legal system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mainstay - a chief support or main source of strength
  • synonym: pillar - a person or thing regarded as reliably providing essential support
  • antonym: weakness - a lack of strength or support
