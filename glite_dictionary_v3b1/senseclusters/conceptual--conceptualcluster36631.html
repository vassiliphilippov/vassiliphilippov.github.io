conceptual

adjective
relating to concepts or their formation
2.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛptʃuəl / (AmE), / kənˈsɛptjʊəl / (BrE)
relating to or based on mental concepts or the formation of concepts.

Examples

Conceptual thinking
She excels at conceptual thinking and can easily grasp abstract ideas.
The committee engaged in conceptual discussions before setting any policies.
The project required both technical skills and a strong conceptual approach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstract - existing in thought or as an idea but not having physical existence
  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than ideas or theory
  • synonym: theoretical - based on theory rather than practice; concerned with ideas
