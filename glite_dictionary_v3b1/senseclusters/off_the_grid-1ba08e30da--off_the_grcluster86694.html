off the grid

adverb
not relying on public utilities
Pronunciation: / ɑf ðə ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɒf ðə ɡrɪd / (BrE)
Not connected to public utilities such as electricity, water, or gas; living in a way that does not rely on established public systems.

Examples

Living off the grid
Many people choose to live off the grid for environmental or privacy reasons.
They dreamed of moving off the grid and becoming completely self-sufficient.
We spent a week off the grid in a cabin without phone service or electricity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: off-the-grid - Not using or requiring electricity, water, or gas supplied by public utilities; an alternative form
  • synonym: self-sufficient - Able to provide for oneself without help from others
