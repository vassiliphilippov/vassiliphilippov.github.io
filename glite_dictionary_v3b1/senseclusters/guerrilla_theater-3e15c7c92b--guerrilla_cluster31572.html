guerrilla theater

noun
political street performance
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈrɪlə ˈθiːətər / (AmE), / ɡəˈrɪlə ˈθɪətə / (BrE)
a type of spontaneous, politically motivated street performance intended to dramatize social issues, often in public spaces to attract attention and provoke discussion

Examples

Guerrilla theater performance
The group staged a guerrilla theater performance in the city square to raise awareness about climate change.
Through their guerrilla theater acts, the troupe challenged the public to rethink issues of inequality.
We stumbled upon a guerrilla theater piece while walking through the park on Saturday afternoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: street theater - theatrical performances in outdoor public places, often with social or political themes
