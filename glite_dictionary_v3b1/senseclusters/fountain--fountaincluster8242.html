fountain

verb
to spout upward
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊntən / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntɪn / (BrE)
to send liquid up in a stream like a fountain

Examples

Water fountains up
Water began fountaining from the cracked hydrant after the accident.
Oil was fountaining high into the air from the damaged well.
The soda erupted, fountaining over the edge of the bottle and onto the table.
Water fountained from the broken pipe, soaking the entire floor in minutes.
During the celebration, champagne fountained from the bottle after it was opened.
A stream of sparks fountained into the night sky during the fireworks show.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gush - to flow out suddenly and in large amounts
  • synonym: spout - to send out liquid forcefully in a stream
