outfish

verb
to catch more fish
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈfɪʃ / (BrE)
to catch more fish than someone else, especially in a competition (can also mean to perform better than others at fishing)

Examples

To outfish a rival
Despite his experience, he could not outfish his younger brother during the tournament.
She managed to outfish everyone else by using a new bait.
No matter where we go, Tom always seems to outfish the rest of us.
Despite being new to the sport, she managed to outfish all the experienced anglers at the tournament.
He hoped to outfish his brother during their annual fishing trip, but came up short.
In the business world, companies strive to outfish their competitors by offering better products.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to fail to win or do worse than someone else
  • synonym: outperform - to do better than someone else in a particular activity
