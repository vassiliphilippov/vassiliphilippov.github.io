connecting flight

noun
flight with change of aircraft
1.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈnek.tɪŋ flaɪt/ (AmE), /kə.ˈnek.tɪŋ flaɪt/ (BrE)
a flight that involves a stop at an intermediate airport, requiring passengers to change aircraft (and sometimes airlines) to reach their final destination

Examples

A connecting flight delay
My connecting flight from Chicago was delayed due to bad weather.
Passengers must collect their luggage before boarding their connecting flight.
She missed her connecting flight because her first plane arrived late.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct flight - a flight that flies straight to the destination without intermediate stops or changes of aircraft
  • antonym: nonstop flight - a flight that goes directly to the destination without any scheduled stops
