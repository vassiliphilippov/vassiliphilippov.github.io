self-pollinated

adjective
fertilized by its own pollen
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈpɑləˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈpɒlɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a plant, flower, etc.) fertilized by its own pollen, without pollen from another individual.

Examples

Self-pollinated plants
Many self-pollinated plants can produce seeds even when isolated from others.
Some crops have been bred to be self-pollinated for reliable harvests.
The pea plant is commonly self-pollinated, which helps maintain its genetic characteristics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross-pollinated - fertilized by pollen from another individual
