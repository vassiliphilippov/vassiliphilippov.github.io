contemporize

verb
make modern; adapt for today
7.4
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈtem.pə.raɪz/ /; / /kən.ˈtem.praɪz/ (AmE), /kən.ˈtem.pə.raɪz/ /; / /kən.ˈtem.praɪz/ (BrE)
to adapt a work, idea, or event so that it seems contemporary or relevant to present-day audiences; to modernize.

Examples

To contemporize a story
The director chose to contemporize the ancient tale by updating dialogue and setting.
To attract younger readers, the publisher asked the author to contemporize the novel's language.
Conservators are careful not to contemporize historic paintings beyond essential restorative work.
The director decided to contemporise the classic play by setting it in modern London.
Critics argued that the screenplay's attempt to contemporise the novel lost much of its original charm.
When adapting the opera, she chose to contemporise several scenes to connect with younger audiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modernize - to make something more modern in style, design, or function
  • synonym: update - to make something more current or suitable for present needs
