Choloepus hoffmanni

noun
species of two-toed sloth
Pronunciation: / koʊˈliːəpəs hɒfˈmɑːni / (AmE), / kɒˈliːəpəs hɒfˈmaniː / (BrE)
A species of two-toed sloth found in Central and South America, characterized by having two long claws on each forefoot and three on each hindfoot.

Examples

Choloepus hoffmanni sloth
Choloepus hoffmanni is commonly found in rainforests stretching from Honduras to Bolivia.
Researchers observed that Choloepus hoffmanni spends most of its life hanging from tree branches.
The slow movement of Choloepus hoffmanni helps it avoid predators in the dense canopy.

  • synonym: two-toed sloth - a sloth with two claws on each forefoot
