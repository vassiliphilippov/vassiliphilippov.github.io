follow

verb
secretly observe; keep under surveillance
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ / (BrE)
to secretly observe someone or something, especially in order to monitor their actions; keep under surveillance

Examples

To follow someone secretly
It is illegal for private citizens to follow others without their consent.
The detective was assigned to follow the suspect after he left the hotel.
Undercover agents follow criminal networks to gather evidence for investigation.
