Sarda chiliensis

noun
Pacific bonito species with oily flesh
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑrdə kɪliˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɑːdə kɪlɪˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of bonito found along the Pacific coast of the Americas, valued for its dark oily flesh, which is suitable for canning

Examples

Canned Sarda chiliensis
Canned Sarda chiliensis is a staple in some coastal communities.
Fishermen along the coast often catch Sarda chiliensis for both local markets and canneries.
The meat of Sarda chiliensis is prized for its rich, oily flavor.
