urgency

noun
persistent, forceful insistence
4.2 1.29/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…úrd í…ônsi / (AmE), / Àà…úÀêd í…ônsi / (BrE)
persistent and insistent demand or solicitation; the quality of pressing one's request forcefully

Examples

Urgency in his voice
He made his case with such urgency that the committee felt compelled to respond.
Her repeated calls for help carried a sense of urgency that could not be ignored.
The urgency in his requests eventually convinced them to take action.

Synonyms

    3.5 7.62/m
    insistence is the act of demanding something firmly and repeatedly to get a result, while urgency is pressing one's request forcefully.
  • importunity
    9.9 0.05/m
    importunity is repeated, often annoying requests that keep coming, while urgency is persistent, forceful asking to gain attention.
  • demand
    3.1 32/m
    demand is a strong request or claim often made as a right, while urgency is the pressing quality of asking insistently.
  • press
    5.8 1.26/m

  • urging
    3.9 2.01/m
    urging is encouraging someone strongly to act or decide, often with persuasion, while urgency is a more forceful, persistent demand.
