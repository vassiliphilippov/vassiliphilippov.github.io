coproduct

noun
mathematical sum construction
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkoʊ.prə.dʌkt/ /; / /ˈkoʊ.proʊ.dʌkt/ (AmE), /ˈkəʊ.prə.dʌkt/ /; / /ˈkəʊ.prɒ.dʌkt/ (BrE)
a mathematical construction, especially in category theory, representing a 'sum' or disjoint union of objects, dual to a product

Examples

Category theory coproduct
In category theory, the coproduct generalizes the idea of a disjoint union of sets.
The coproduct of two groups can be thought of as their free product in algebra.
Understanding the coproduct helps mathematicians study relationships between different mathematical structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disjoint union - a union of sets with no elements in common
  • antonym: product - a mathematical construction representing a 'product' or combination of objects
  • synonym: sum - the result of adding two or more elements together
