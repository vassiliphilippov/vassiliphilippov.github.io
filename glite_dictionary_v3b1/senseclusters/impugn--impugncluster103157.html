impugn

verb
challenge the truth or validity of something
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpjun / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjuːn / (BrE)
to challenge or call into question the truth, validity, or honesty of a statement or motive

Examples

To impugn a statement
Her motives were unfairly impugned by her political opponents.
It's not wise to impugn someone’s honesty without solid evidence.
The defense lawyer sought to impugn the credibility of the witness during cross-examination.
