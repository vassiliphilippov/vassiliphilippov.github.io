information age

noun
era defined by information technology and digital communication
1.2 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period in modern history, starting in the late 20th century, characterized by the rapid shift from traditional industry to an economy based on information technology, with widespread access to computers and digital communication

Examples

The information age era
Education systems have had to adapt to the challenges of the information age.
Living in the information age means we have instant access to vast amounts of data.
Many historians believe that the information age has transformed how people work and communicate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digital age - era defined by the ubiquity of digital technology
  • antonym: industrial age - the period characterized by industrialization and manufacturing
