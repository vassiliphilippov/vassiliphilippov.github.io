a device that recycles exhaled air so it can be breathed again, usually by removing carbon dioxide; commonly used in diving or hazardous environments

Examples

A rebreather device
The diver used a rebreather to stay underwater for over an hour without surfacing.
Modern rebreathers are designed to be lightweight and efficient for deep-sea exploration.
Firefighters sometimes use a rebreather when working in smoke-filled environments.
The diver used a rebreather to stay underwater for longer periods without producing bubbles.
Modern anaesthetic machines often include a rebreather to conserve expensive gases during surgery.
Training with a rebreather requires learning how to monitor oxygen and carbon dioxide levels carefully.
