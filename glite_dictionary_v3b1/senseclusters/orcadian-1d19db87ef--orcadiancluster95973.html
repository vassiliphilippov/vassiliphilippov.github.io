Orcadian

noun
Orkney dialect
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈkeɪdiən / (AmE), / ɔːˈkeɪdiən / (BrE)
the variety of Scots or English traditionally spoken in Orkney, influenced by Norse language and culture

Examples

Orcadian dialect
The Orcadian spoken by locals contains many words borrowed from Old Norse.
Linguists study the Orcadian dialect to understand the region's unique history.
She enjoyed listening to stories told in Orcadian during her visit to Orkney.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Scots - the Germanic language variety spoken in Lowland Scotland
  • synonym: Shetlandic - the dialect of Scots spoken in Shetland, also influenced by Norse
