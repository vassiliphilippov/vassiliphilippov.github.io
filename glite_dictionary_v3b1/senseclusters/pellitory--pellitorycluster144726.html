pellitory

noun
herbaceous plant with narrow leaves and pink flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛləˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈpɛlɪtəri / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Parietaria or Anacyclus, typically found growing in crevices, with long narrow leaves and small pink, petal-less flowers.

Examples

Wild pellitory plant
Gardeners often find pellitory growing wild in shaded areas near buildings.

The old stone walls were covered in pellitory, thriving in the narrow cracks between the bricks.
