mismark

noun
incorrect asset value
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈmɪsˌmɑːk / (BrE)
an incorrect valuation or reported price of an asset, especially in finance or accounting

Examples

A mismark in accounts
The audit revealed a significant mismark that affected the company's financial statements.
A single mismark can lead to major losses for investors if not corrected quickly.
Regulators are investigating whether the mismark was intentional or simply an error.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mispricing - an incorrect or inaccurate price assigned to something
  • synonym: overvaluation - assigning a value higher than the true worth
  • antonym: undervaluation - assigning a value lower than the true worth
