pharmacokinetic

adjective
drug movement in body
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːrməkoʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməkəʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the way a drug is absorbed, distributed, metabolized, and eliminated by the body

Examples

Pharmacokinetic properties
The pharmacokinetic properties of the drug determine how often it should be taken.
Researchers studied the pharmacokinetic differences between the tablet and liquid forms.
Understanding the pharmacokinetic profile helps doctors adjust medication doses for patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pharmacodynamic - relating to the effects of a drug on the body
