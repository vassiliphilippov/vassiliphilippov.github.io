isotropous

adjective
identical in all directions
9.9 9.2
Pronunciation: /aɪ.sə.ˈtrɑː.pəs/ (AmE), /aɪ.sə.ˈtrɒ.pəs/ (BrE)
Having properties that are the same in all directions; invariant with respect to direction.

Examples

Isotropous material
In an isotropous material, heat is conducted equally in all directions.
Physicists often assume space is isotropous at large scales for their models.
The crystal is isotropous, so its refractive index does not depend on direction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anisotropic - having properties that vary with direction
