paleology

noun
study of ancient antiquities
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˈɑːlədʒi /; / ˌpeɪliˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliˈɒlədʒi /; / ˌpæliˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of ancient artifacts and antiquities, especially from prehistoric periods

Examples

Paleology study ancient artifacts
Advances in paleology have changed our understanding of early human societies.
She decided to specialize in paleology at university, focusing on prehistoric artifacts.
The museum's new exhibit draws heavily on recent findings in paleology.
Advances in technology have greatly aided research in the field of palaeology.
Her passion for palaeology led her to uncover several important prehistoric artifacts.
Many universities offer courses in palaeology as part of their archaeology departments.
