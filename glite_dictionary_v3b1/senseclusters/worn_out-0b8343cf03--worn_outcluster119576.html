worn out

adjective
extremely tired or exhausted
2.9 1.25/m
Pronunciation: / w…îrn a ät / (AmE), / w…îÀên a ät / (BrE)
extremely tired or exhausted; drained of energy or effectiveness

Examples

Completely worn out
After running after the kids all afternoon, she was completely worn out.
By the end of the marathon, every runner appeared worn out but satisfied.
He looked worn out after working overtime all week.

Synonyms

  • fatigued
    4.1 0.86/m
    fatigued is a formal medical-sounding word for feeling very tired, while worn out is more everyday and broad.
  • exhausted
    2.0 3.89/m
    exhausted means completely without energy or strength, while worn out can also mean tired or no longer effective.
  • spent
    3.5 0.00/m
    spent means used up and with no energy left, while worn out focuses on tiredness or reduced effectiveness.
  • drained
    3.4 0.00/m
    drained describes being emptied of energy or resources, while worn out highlights strong tiredness or loss of usefulness.
  • dog-tired
    6.4 0.18/m
    dog-tired is a very informal, vivid way to say someone is exhausted, while worn out is more neutral everyday language.
  • burned-out
    4.4 1.54/m
    burned-out describes long-term, work-related exhaustion from stress, while worn out covers general tiredness or loss of energy.
  • washed-out
    5.1 0.06/m
    washed-out often means pale, tired, or ineffective in appearance, while worn out mainly means exhausted or no longer effective.
