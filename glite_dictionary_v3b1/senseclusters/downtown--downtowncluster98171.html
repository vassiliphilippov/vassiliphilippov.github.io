downtown

adverb
in/toward a city's central business area
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdaʊntaʊn / (BrE)
in or toward the center or main business district of a city or town.

Examples

Go downtown
She works downtown, just a few blocks from the main square.
Traffic is usually heavy when you're driving downtown in the morning.
We decided to go downtown for dinner and a movie.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uptown - in or toward the residential or northern part of a city
