diamond dust

noun
tiny ice crystals in cold air
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəmənd dʌst / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd dʌst / (BrE)
very small ice crystals that form near the ground, often sparkling in sunlight, typically in cold, clear weather

Examples

Winter diamond dust
A faint shimmer of diamond dust floated above the snowy field at sunrise.
On a clear winter morning, diamond dust can make the air look almost magical.
Pilots must be cautious as diamond dust can reduce visibility in the Arctic.
