tuna

noun
large warm-water edible fish
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
a large, fast-swimming sea fish (genus Thunnus), valued as food and often eaten fresh or canned

Examples

Grilled tuna steak
Fresh tuna steaks are a popular option at many seafood restaurants.
She opened a can of tuna to make a quick sandwich for lunch.
The fisherman caught a massive tuna off the coast of Hawaii.
A fresh tuna steak is delicious when grilled with lemon and herbs.
Large fishing boats trawl the sea in search of tuna each year.
Sushi lovers often enjoy slices of raw tuna called maguro.

Related Terms

  • synonym: albacore - a type of tuna with light flesh, also a member of the genus Thunnus
  • synonym: bonito - a related fish to tuna, smaller and also a game fish
  • degree lower: mackerel - a similar fish in the same family as tuna
