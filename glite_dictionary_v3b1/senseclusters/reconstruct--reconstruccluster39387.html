reconstruct

verb
form a mental picture of the past
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt /; / ccrid0k59nc8str8ckt / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt /; / ccrid0k59nc8str8ckt / (BrE)
to form an idea or create a representation of something from available information or data (mentally or using computers)

Examples

To reconstruct an event
After listening to the witnesses, she managed to reconstruct the accident in her mind.
Historians often reconstruct what daily life was like in ancient cultures.
The investigators tried to reconstruct the sequence of events from the available evidence.
Researchers reconstruct a 3D scene from multiple photographs using structure-from-motion algorithms.
The hospital reconstructed the patient's organs in 3D from CT scan data for surgical planning.
By reconstructing sonar returns, scientists created detailed maps of the submerged wreck site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imagine - to form a mental image of something
  • synonym: piece together - to assemble information to form a complete picture
  • synonym: recover - to retrieve lost or inaccessible data or information; related when reconstruction recovers a representation from raw measurements
  • synonym: recreate - to make or produce again; used for producing a representation or model from available information
