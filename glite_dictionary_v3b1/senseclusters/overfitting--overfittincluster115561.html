overfitting

noun
model fits data too closely
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a situation in which a statistical model fits its training data too closely and fails to perform well on new data

Examples

Model overfitting
If your model shows overfitting, it may not work well with real-world data.
Regularization techniques can help prevent overfitting in machine learning models.
The team adjusted their algorithm to reduce overfitting and improve accuracy on new samples.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generalization - the ability of a model to perform well on new, unseen data
  • antonym: underfitting - a model that is too simple to capture the underlying pattern in data
