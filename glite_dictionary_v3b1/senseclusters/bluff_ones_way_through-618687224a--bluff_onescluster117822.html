bluff one's way through

verb
succeed by deceiving others
Pronunciation: / blʌf wʌnz weɪ θruː / (AmE), / blʌf wʌnz weɪ θruː / (BrE)
to succeed in a difficult or challenging situation by deceiving others or pretending to have abilities, knowledge, or confidence that one does not actually possess

Examples

To bluff one's way through a meeting
He tried to bluff his way through the meeting, but the experts quickly saw through his act.
She managed to bluff her way through the job interview even though she lacked experience.
Sometimes, it's possible to bluff your way through a difficult exam if you stay calm and confident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fake it till you make it - to pretend to be confident or capable until it becomes true
