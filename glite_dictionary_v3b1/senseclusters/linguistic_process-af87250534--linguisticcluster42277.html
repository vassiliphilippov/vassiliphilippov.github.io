linguistic process

noun
process in human language
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprɑː.ses /; / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprɑː.sɛs / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprəʊ.ses / (BrE)
a process or mental operation involved in the use, understanding, or production of language

Examples

A linguistic process study
Language acquisition is considered a complex linguistic process studied by researchers.
Memory plays an important role in every linguistic process within the brain.
Reading aloud is a linguistic process that involves both recognition and production of words.
Language acquisition is considered a complex linguistic process involving several stages.
Researchers use brain imaging to study the linguistic processes involved in speech comprehension.
Understanding sarcasm requires a distinct linguistic process from understanding literal statements.
