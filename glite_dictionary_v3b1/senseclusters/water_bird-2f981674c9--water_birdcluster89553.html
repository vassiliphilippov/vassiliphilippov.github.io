water bird

noun
bird living in or near freshwater
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər bɝd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird that lives on or around freshwater bodies such as lakes, rivers, or ponds; especially one that is adapted to living in or near water

Examples

A water bird species
Biologists recorded the behavior of each water bird they observed in the marsh.
Many species of water bird can be found at the edge of the lake during spring migration.
The wetlands provide important nesting grounds for several types of water bird.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waterfowl - a bird that frequents water, especially ducks, geese, and swans
