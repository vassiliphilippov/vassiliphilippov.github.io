grapnel

noun
a small anchor with several flukes
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræpnəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrapnəl /; / ˈɡræpnəl / (BrE)
a small anchor or hook with several prongs, used for anchoring, grasping, or holding things (often attached to a rope)

Examples

A grapnel anchor
He retrieved the lost equipment from the riverbed using a grapnel attached to a sturdy rope.
The fisherman dropped the grapnel overboard to secure the boat near the shore.
We used a small grapnel to anchor our rowboat for a picnic on the lake.
A grapnel was thrown over the wall so the climbers could scale it.
The divers retrieved the lost equipment using a long-handled grapnel.
The sailors used a grapnel to secure the boat to the dock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grappling hook - a device with hooks used for grabbing or anchoring
