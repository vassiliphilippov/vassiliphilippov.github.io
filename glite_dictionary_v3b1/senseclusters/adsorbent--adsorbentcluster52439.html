adsorbent

adjective
capable of adsorbing substances
6.9
Pronunciation: / ædˈzɔrbənt / (AmE), / ədˈsɔːbənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) having the ability to adsorb other substances onto its surface.

Examples

Adsorbent surface
Activated charcoal is a highly adsorbent material used to remove toxins from water.
The laboratory tested several adsorbent surfaces for their effectiveness in trapping pollutants.
Zeolites are naturally adsorbent minerals and play a role in cleaning air and water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorbent - able to soak up or take in fluids or other substances
