take advantage

verb
use unfairly for one's own benefit
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ / (AmE), / teɪk ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ / (BrE)
to use someone or a situation for your own benefit, often in an unfair or unethical way

Examples

To take advantage of people
He always tries to take advantage of people who trust him.
I felt she was taking advantage of my generosity by asking for more favors.
Some companies take advantage of legal loopholes to avoid taxes.
He took advantage of the situation to secure a better deal for himself.
She tried to take advantage of the generous return policy by returning used items.
Unscrupulous companies often take advantage of people who lack financial knowledge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abuse - to use something to bad effect or for a bad purpose
  • synonym: exploit - to use someone or something unfairly for one's own advantage
  • antonym: help - to give assistance, a positive counterpart; acting for someone else's benefit
