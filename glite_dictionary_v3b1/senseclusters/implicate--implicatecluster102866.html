implicate

verb
to involve or imply as necessary
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmplɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪmplɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to involve or imply as a necessary part, accompaniment, or consequence

Examples

Implicate new rules
Any changes to the contract implicate new legal responsibilities for the company.
Increasing production will implicate the need for more resources and staff.
The policy shift could implicate a rise in transportation costs for all clients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entail - to involve something as a necessary or inevitable part or consequence
  • antonym: exclude - to leave out or not include as a necessary part or result
