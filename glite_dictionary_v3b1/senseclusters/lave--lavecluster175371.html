lave

verb
to wash or cleanse with water
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: /leɪv/ (AmE), /leɪv/ (BrE)
to wash or bathe (oneself or something), especially with water, often in a gentle or flowing way

Examples

To lave the face
He laved his face with fresh water to wake himself up.
She laved her hands in the cool stream after the long walk.
The old poet wrote of lovers who lave their bodies in scented baths.
After gardening, he laved his hands thoroughly to remove the dirt.
She laved her face in the cool stream before dinner.
The nurse gently laved the child's scraped knee with warm water.
Cool water laved her tired feet after the long walk.
The morning tide laved the rocks with gentle waves.
The small stream laves the base of the old stone bridge.
