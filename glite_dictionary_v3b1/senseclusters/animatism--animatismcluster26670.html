animatism

noun
belief that natural objects have consciousness
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.ɪˌmæ.tɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈan.ɪˌmæt.ɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the belief that natural objects and phenomena possess consciousness and personality

Examples

Animatism belief
He explained the earthquake through animatism, believing the earth itself was a living being.
Some indigenous cultures practice animatism, attributing life and spirit to rivers and forests.
The study of animatism helps anthropologists understand early human explanations for natural events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animism - belief that objects, places, and creatures all possess a distinct spiritual essence
