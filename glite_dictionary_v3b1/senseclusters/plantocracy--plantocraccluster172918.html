plantocracy

noun
rule by plantation owners
Pronunciation: / plænˈtɑːkrəsi / (AmE), / plænˈtɒkrəsi / (BrE)
a ruling class made up of plantation owners, especially in the Caribbean during colonial times

Examples

The plantocracy ruled
During the colonial era, the plantocracy held most of the political and economic power in the region.
The decline of the plantocracy led to significant changes in the island's social structure.
Historians have studied how the plantocracy influenced laws and daily life in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocracy - a class of people holding exceptional rank and privileges
  • synonym: oligarchy - a small group of people having control of a country or organization
