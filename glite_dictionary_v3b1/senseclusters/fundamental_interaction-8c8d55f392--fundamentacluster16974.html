fundamental interaction

noun
basic force in particle physics
Pronunciation: / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˌɪntərˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˌɪntəˈrækʃən / (BrE)
(Physics) Any of the basic forces that govern the transfer of energy between elementary particles or fields, such as gravity, electromagnetism, the strong nuclear force, and the weak nuclear force.

Examples

The fundamental interaction forces
Gravity is one type of fundamental interaction that affects every object in the universe.
Physicists study each fundamental interaction to understand the nature of matter and energy.
The electromagnetic fundamental interaction is responsible for the behavior of charged particles.

  • synonym: force - a physical influence causing a particle to accelerate
