away

adverb
in a different direction; to another place
Pronunciation: / əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
at a distance from a place, or at a certain time in the future or past

Examples

Look away quickly
Please turn your head away while I change the bandage.
She quickly looked away when their eyes met.
The dog runs away whenever it hears loud noises.
She lives just a few blocks away from the school.
That memory feels like it happened ages away in my childhood.
The concert is still three days away, so we have plenty of time to prepare.
After graduation, she moved away to another city.
Please keep away from the construction site for your safety.
The dog ran away as soon as it saw the stranger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forth - out from a starting point and forward, now archaic in this sense
  • synonym: off - at a distance from a place, either in space or time
  • antonym: toward - in the direction of something
