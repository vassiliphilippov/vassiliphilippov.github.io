woolsorter's pneumonia

noun
inhalational anthrax from wool dust
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊlˌsɔrtɚz pnuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌsɔːtəz njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a form of anthrax infection contracted by inhaling dust containing Bacillus anthracis, formerly common among wool workers, and characterized by severe respiratory symptoms and cardiovascular collapse

Examples

Woolsorter's pneumonia infection
Many workers developed woolsorter's pneumonia after years of exposure to contaminated wool.
Modern safety standards have nearly eliminated cases of woolsorter's pneumonia.
The outbreak of woolsorter's pneumonia alarmed health authorities in the textile town.
