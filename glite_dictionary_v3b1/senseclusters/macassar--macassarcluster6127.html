macassar

noun
furniture protective cloth
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈkæsər / (AmE), / məˈkæsə / (BrE)
a cloth placed on the back or arms of a chair to protect it from hair oil, originally named after a type of hair oil

Examples

Macassar cloth on chair
The old armchair was covered with a white macassar to keep it looking clean.
She carefully washed the macassar that protected the sofa from stains.
A lace macassar decorated the back of every chair in her grandmother's living room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antimacassar - a small cloth placed on furniture to protect it from hair oil
