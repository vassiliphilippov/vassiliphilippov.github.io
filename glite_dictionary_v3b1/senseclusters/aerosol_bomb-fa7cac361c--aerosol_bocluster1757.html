aerosol bomb

noun
bomb using a fuel-air explosive
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr.əˌsɑl bɑm / (AmE), / ˈeə.rəˌsɒl bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb that disperses fuel as an aerosol before ignition, creating a powerful explosion by mixing with air

Examples

A powerful aerosol bomb
A single aerosol bomb can cause massive destruction over a wide area due to its shock wave.
Experts raised concerns about the use of aerosol bombs in civilian areas.
The military tested a new aerosol bomb in the desert to measure its explosive power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermobaric bomb - a bomb that uses a fuel-air explosive for a powerful blast
