responsiveness

noun
readiness to react or respond
5.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɑːnsɪvnəs /; / rɪˈspɑnsɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈspɒnsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of reacting quickly and positively to something, or being emotionally or attentively engaged

Examples

High responsiveness
High responsiveness is essential in customer service roles.
Teachers value students who show responsiveness and enthusiasm during lessons.
The patient's responsiveness to medication was carefully monitored.
Her responsiveness made her a valued manager, always attentive to her team's needs.
The responsiveness of the new software has greatly improved our workflow.
We were impressed by the customer service team's responsiveness to our questions and concerns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: responsivity - the degree to which something is responsive, especially in technical contexts
  • synonym: sensitivity - quality of being sensitive to changes or stimuli
  • antonym: unresponsiveness - lack of reaction or responsiveness
