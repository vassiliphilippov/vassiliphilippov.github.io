radio noise

noun
interference or static in radio signals
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ nɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ nɔɪz / (BrE)
unwanted static or interference present in a radio signal, typically resulting in a hissing or crackling sound

Examples

Background radio noise
Astronomers must account for background radio noise when collecting signals from distant galaxies.
Sometimes, electronic devices cause radio noise that disrupts wireless communication.
The old radio picks up a lot of radio noise during thunderstorms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interference - disturbance that affects a radio signal
  • synonym: static - random noise in a radio or telecommunication signal
