macular degeneration

noun
degenerative eye disease causing vision loss
9.2 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmækjələr ˌdɛdʒənəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmakjʊlə dɪˌdʒɛnəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical condition in which degeneration of the cells of the macula in the eye leads to blurred vision and, in severe cases, blindness

Examples

Macular degeneration disease
His grandmother gradually lost her central vision due to macular degeneration.
Regular eye exams can help detect macular degeneration in its early stages.
She was diagnosed with macular degeneration and now has difficulty reading small print.
