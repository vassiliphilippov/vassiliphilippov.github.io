virulency

noun
capacity to cause harm or disease
6.2 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪr.jə.lən.si / (AmE), / ˈvɪr.jʊ.lən.si / (BrE)
the degree of harmfulness or severity, especially in relation to a microorganism's ability to cause disease

Examples

Virulency of a virus
Doctors monitored the virulency of the infection to determine the appropriate treatment.
Research is ongoing to understand how bacteria develop increased virulency over time.
The virulency of some strains of influenza makes them particularly dangerous during outbreaks.
