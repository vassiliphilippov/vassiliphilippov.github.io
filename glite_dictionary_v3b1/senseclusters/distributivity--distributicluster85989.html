distributivity

noun
math property of distribution
Pronunciation: / dɪˌstrɪbjəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / dɪˌstrɪbjʊˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the property of an operation in mathematics that allows it to be distributed over another operation, such as multiplication over addition

Examples

Distributivity in math
The distributivity of multiplication over addition is a fundamental concept in algebra.
Understanding distributivity helps students solve equations more efficiently.
In mathematics, distributivity explains why a(b + c) equals ab + ac.

Related Terms

  • synonym: associativity - the property of an operation where grouping does not affect the result
  • synonym: commutativity - the property of an operation where order does not affect the result
