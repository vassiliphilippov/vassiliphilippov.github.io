judgment on the pleadings

noun
court decision made before trial based on pleadings
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt ɑn ðə ˈplidɪŋz /; / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt ɑn ðə ˈpliːdɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒmənt ɒn ðə ˈpliːdɪŋz /; / ˈdʒʌdʒm(ə)nt ɒn ðə ˈpliːdɪŋz / (BrE)
a court decision made before a trial, based only on the written pleadings, when there is no dispute over the main facts

Examples

A judgment on the pleadings
After reviewing the written submissions, the judge issued a judgment on the pleadings.
If there are no material factual disputes, a party may request a judgment on the pleadings.
The court granted a judgment on the pleadings because both parties agreed on the key facts.
A judgment on the pleadings is possible when neither side disputes the central facts.
After considering the facts, the court granted judgment on the pleadings in favor of the plaintiff.
The defendant's lawyer filed a motion for judgment on the pleadings to resolve the case quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: summary judgment - a court decision made without a full trial when there are no disputed facts
