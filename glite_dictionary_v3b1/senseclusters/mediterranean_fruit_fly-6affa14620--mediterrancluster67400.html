Mediterranean fruit fly

noun
a fly that infests citrus and fruit crops
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛdəˌteɪrəˈniən ˈfruːt ˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪnɪən ˈfruːt ˌflaɪ / (BrE)
a small black-and-white insect that damages citrus and other fruits by laying eggs inside them, whose larvae feed on the fruit

Examples

The Mediterranean fruit fly infestation
Quarantine measures were enacted after several Mediterranean fruit fly infestations were discovered.
The Mediterranean fruit fly poses a major threat to citrus crops in many countries.
The larvae of the Mediterranean fruit fly can ruin entire shipments of fruit if not controlled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: medfly - common informal name for the Mediterranean fruit fly
