unsense

verb
to make meaningless
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɛns / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛns / (BrE)
to deprive something of sense or meaning; to make senseless or meaningless

Examples

To unsense a message
The teacher's complicated explanation seemed to unsense the simple concept for the students.
Too many technical terms can unsense the main message of your presentation.
If you add unnecessary details, you might unsense what was once a clear idea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarify - to make something easier to understand
  • synonym: obscure - to make unclear or difficult to understand
