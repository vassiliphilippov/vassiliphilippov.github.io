assume

verb
seize control or rights without authority
5.7 7.28/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàsum / (AmE), / …ôÀàsjuÀêm / (BrE)
to take control or seize something such as a right, position, or power, often without proper authority or by force

Examples

To assume power
After the CEO resigned, the vice president quickly assumed control of the company.
He tried to assume authority over the board, even though he wasn't elected.
The colonists assumed the right to govern themselves, defying the king's orders.

Synonyms

  • usurp
    8.6 2.82/m
    usurp means taking power illegally or by force, while assume often means taking a position without proper authority.
  • arrogate
    9.9 0.37/m
    arrogate claims a right or privilege for yourself without authority, while assume can mean taking power or a role.
  • take over
    3.8 9.27/m
    take over gains control of a business or organization, often by purchase or force, while assume focuses on seizing authority.
  • seize
    3.2 15/m
    seize takes hold or captures something quickly and often by force, while assume is used for taking a role or authority.
  • take
    1.0 192/m
    take means to occupy or adopt a position or state in many ways, while assume implies taking control or power.
