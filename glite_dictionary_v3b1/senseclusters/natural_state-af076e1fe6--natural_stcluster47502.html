natural state

noun
primitive, uncultivated condition
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃrəl steɪt / (AmE), / ˈnatʃərəl steɪt / (BrE)
A condition existing in nature, unaltered or untouched by human civilization.

Examples

The natural state of land
For centuries, the island remained in its natural state, with dense forests covering the landscape.
It is increasingly rare to find rivers still flowing in their natural state without dams or pollution.
The reserve aims to preserve the wetlands in their natural state for future generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: civilization - the stage of human social and cultural development and organization
  • synonym: wilderness - an uncultivated, uninhabited, and inhospitable region
