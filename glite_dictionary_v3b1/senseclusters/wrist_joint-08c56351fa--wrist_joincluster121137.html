wrist joint

noun
joint between forearm and hand
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪst ˌdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈrɪst ˌdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
the joint between the lower end of the forearm (radius) and the first row of carpal bones, allowing movement of the hand and wrist

Examples

The wrist joint
She fractured her wrist joint after slipping on the ice.
Swelling around the wrist joint can indicate an injury or inflammation.
The flexibility of the wrist joint is essential for many sports and daily activities.
