photophosphorylation

noun
ATP production by light
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˌfɑsfəˌrɪˈleɪʃən /; / ˌfoʊtoʊˌfɑːsfəˌrɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌfɒsfəˌrɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in plants and some bacteria where light energy is used to produce ATP during photosynthesis

Examples

Photophosphorylation in plants
Photophosphorylation occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells during the light-dependent reactions.
Scientists study photophosphorylation to better understand how plants convert sunlight into chemical energy.
Without photophosphorylation, plants would not be able to produce the ATP needed for growth.
During photosynthesis, photophosphorylation is essential for converting light energy into chemical energy.
Scientists study photophosphorylation to better understand how plants generate ATP in their chloroplasts.
Noncyclic photophosphorylation produces both ATP and NADPH, which are needed for the Calvin cycle.
During photophosphorylation, plants convert light energy into chemical energy stored in ATP.
Scientists study photophosphorylation to better understand how photosynthesis powers plant growth.
Cyclic photophosphorylation helps balance the energy needs of chloroplasts under different light conditions.

  • synonym: photosynthesis - the process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize food
