quantification

noun
logical limitation using quantifiers
6.0 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑːntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkwɒntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or result of applying a quantifier to a variable in logic, thereby limiting its possible values (as with 'some', 'all', or 'no').

Examples

Logical quantification
In mathematics, quantification allows us to state properties that are true for all or some elements in a set.
Proper quantification ensures that the meaning of a statement is clear within a logical system.
The distinction between universal and existential quantification is fundamental in logic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quantifier - a word or phrase expressing quantity in logic or grammar
