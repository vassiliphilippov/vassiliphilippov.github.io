denude

verb
remove covering; make bare
9.5 6.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈnuːd/ /; / /dɪ.ˈnjuːd/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈnjuːd/ /; / /dɪ.ˈnuːd/ (BrE)
to remove the covering, surface, or something that is protecting an object or area, leaving it bare

Examples

To denude the land
After the storm, the floodwaters denuded much of the farmland of its topsoil.
Continuous exposure to harsh chemicals can denude the skin of its natural oils.
Rampant logging can denude entire hillsides and lead to soil erosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bare - to uncover or expose to view
  • antonym: cover - to place something over or upon, as for protection or concealment
  • synonym: strip - to remove all coverings or clothes from something
