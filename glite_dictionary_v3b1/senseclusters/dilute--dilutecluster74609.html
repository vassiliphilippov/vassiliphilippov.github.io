dilute

verb
make impure by adding inferior substances
4.9 2.94/m
Pronunciation: / da…™ÀàluÀêt / (AmE), / da…™ÀàluÀêt / (BrE)
to make something impure or of lower quality by adding a foreign or inferior substance, often by replacing valuable parts with less valuable ones

Examples

To dilute quality
Adding fillers can dilute the purity and quality of the medicine.
Some companies dilute their products with cheaper ingredients to cut costs.
The winemaker was accused of trying to dilute the wine with water.

Synonyms

  • adulterate
    8.2 0.44/m
    adulterate means making food or drink impure by adding poor ingredients, while dilute means lowering quality by mixing in another substance.
  • cut
    6.0 4.40/m
    cut is an informal verb used for watering down liquids like alcohol, while dilute is the neutral term for making things weaker.
  • debase
    6.9 0.57/m
    debase means lowering value or status often in a general way, while dilute means making a substance weaker by adding something.
