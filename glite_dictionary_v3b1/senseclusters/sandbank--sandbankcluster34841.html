sandbank

noun

A large, raised area of sand in a river or near the shore of a sea, which is often submerged but may be exposed at low tide.

Examples

A sandbank near shore
At low tide, the sandbank was clearly visible from the shore.
Birds gathered on the exposed sandbank to search for food.
The ship ran aground on a hidden sandbank just outside the harbor.

Synonyms

  • sandbar
    2.8 1.14/m
    a sandbar is a long narrow strip of sand shaped by currents, while a sandbank is larger and forms broad raised areas.
  • batture
    9.9 0.01/m
    a batture is a riverside strip of sand exposed at low water from river deposits, while a sandbank is a larger raised sand area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandbar - a long, narrow sandbank, especially at the mouth of a river
