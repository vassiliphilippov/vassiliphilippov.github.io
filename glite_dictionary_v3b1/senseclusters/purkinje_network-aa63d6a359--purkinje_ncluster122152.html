Purkinje network

noun
network of fibers in the heart

Pronunciation: /pər.ˈkɪn.dʒiː ˈnet.wɜːrk/ /; / /pɜːr.ˈkɪn.dʒiː ˈnet.wɜːrk/ /; / /pər.ˈkɪn.dʒeɪ ˈnet.wɜːrk/ (AmE), /pə.ˈkɪn.dʒiː ˈnet.wɜːk/ /; / /pɜː.ˈkɪn.dʒiː ˈnet.wɜːk/ /; / /pə.ˈkɪn.dʒeɪ ˈnet.wɜːk/ (BrE)
A network of Purkinje fibers that transmits electrical impulses from the atrioventricular node to the ventricles of the heart, causing them to contract.

Examples

The Purkinje network fibers
A blockage in the Purkinje network may result in incomplete ventricular contraction.
Damage to the Purkinje network can lead to serious heart rhythm disturbances.
The electrical impulse travels through the Purkinje network before causing the ventricles to contract.
