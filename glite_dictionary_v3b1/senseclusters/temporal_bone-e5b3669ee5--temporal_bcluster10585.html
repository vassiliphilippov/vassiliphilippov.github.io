temporal bone

noun
bone at the side of the skull, enclosing the inner ear
2.9 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtem.pər.əl boʊn/ (AmE), /ˈtem.pə.rəl bəʊn/ (BrE)
the thick bone on each side of the human skull, forming part of the cranium and enclosing the structures of the inner ear

Examples

The temporal bone fracture
A fracture of the temporal bone can affect hearing and balance.
The ear canal runs through the temporal bone, protecting the delicate inner ear structures.
The surgeon carefully examined the temporal bone to assess any damage from the injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frontal bone - the bone forming the front part of the skull and the upper part of the eye sockets
  • synonym: occipital bone - the bone that forms the back and base of the skull
  • synonym: parietal bone - the bone forming the central side and upper back part of each side of the skull
