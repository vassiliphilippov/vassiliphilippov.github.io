abrupt

adjective
very steep or sudden (of slopes)
7.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈbrʌpt/ /; / /æ.ˈbrʌpt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈbrʌpt/ /; / /æ.ˈbrʌpt/ (BrE)
(of a slope or drop) very steep or sudden, often so steep as to be potentially dangerous.

Examples

Abrupt slope
An abrupt change in the slope made the hike more challenging.
The river took an abrupt turn and plunged down a series of dramatic falls.
The trail led them to an abrupt cliff overlooking the valley below.
