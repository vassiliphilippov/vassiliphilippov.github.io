abrupt

adjective
very steep or sudden (of slopes)
4.7 0.37/m
Pronunciation: / É™ËˆbrÊŒpt / (AmE), / É™ËˆbrÊŒpt / (BrE)
(of a slope or drop) very steep or sudden, often so steep as to be potentially dangerous.

Examples

Abrupt slope
An abrupt change in the slope made the hike more challenging.
The river took an abrupt turn and plunged down a series of dramatic falls.
The trail led them to an abrupt cliff overlooking the valley below.

Synonyms

  • precipitous
    7.7 0.91/m
    precipitous is extremely steep and often dangerous, while abrupt describes a steep or sudden drop in more general everyday speech.
  • steep
    2.5 15/m
    steep is the common word for a sharply rising or falling slope, while abrupt emphasizes suddenness or danger of the drop.
  • steep-sided
    4.3 0.10/m
    steep-sided describes an object with very steep or near-vertical sides, while abrupt refers to the slope or drop being steep or sudden.
  • sharp
    2.0 9.68/m
    sharp describes something happening very suddenly or intensely and has many uses, while abrupt means a steep or dangerous drop.
