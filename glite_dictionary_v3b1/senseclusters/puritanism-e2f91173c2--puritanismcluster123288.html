Puritanism

noun
religious movement seeking to purify the Church of England
7.4 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpjʊr.ɪ.tən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpjʊr.ə.tən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈpjʊə.rɪ.tən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpjʊə.rə.tən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the beliefs and practices of the Puritans, a Protestant group—mainly Calvinist—that sought to purify the Church of England of Roman Catholic influences, especially during the 16th and 17th centuries

Examples

Early American Puritanism
Many early American settlers practiced Puritanism and sought to create a godly community in the New World.
Some critics view Puritanism as an overly rigid approach to religion and personal behavior.
The strict moral code of Puritanism influenced laws and social norms in colonial New England.

Related Terms
