ice lolly

noun
frozen snack on a stick (British)
Pronunciation: / aɪs ˈlɑli / (AmE), / aɪs ˈlɒli / (BrE)
A piece of ice cream or flavored ice on a small stick, typically eaten as a sweet snack; known as a popsicle in American English.

Examples

A ice lolly stick
After playing in the park, they cooled down with strawberry ice lollies.
She offered me an orange ice lolly from the freezer.
The children enjoyed an ice lolly each on the hot summer afternoon.
