sibilance

noun
hissing sound effect
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪb.ə.ləns/ /; / /ˈsɪb.ɪ.ləns/ (AmE), /ˈsɪb.ə.ləns/ /; / /ˈsɪb.ɪ.ləns/ (BrE)
the repetition of hissing or s-like sounds in speech or writing, often used as a stylistic device

Examples

Poetic sibilance
The poet used sibilance to create a soft, whispering mood in the opening lines.
You can hear clear sibilance in the phrase 'she sells seashells by the seashore.'
In audio recordings, too much sibilance can make speech sound harsh or unpleasant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alliteration - the repetition of initial consonant sounds in nearby words
  • synonym: assonance - the repetition of vowel sounds in nearby words
