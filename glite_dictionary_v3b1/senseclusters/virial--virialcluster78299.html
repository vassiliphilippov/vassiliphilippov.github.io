virial

noun
physics scalar quantity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪəriəl / (BrE)
a scalar quantity in physics, often defined as the sum over particles of the dot product of position and momentum, used in the virial theorem.

Examples

Virial in star clusters
The virial is essential for understanding the stability of star clusters in astrophysics.
Physicists use the virial to relate kinetic and potential energies in a system.
Calculating the virial helps determine whether a galaxy is in equilibrium.

  • synonym: energy - the capacity to do work or cause change
