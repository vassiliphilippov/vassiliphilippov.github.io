in prohibitum

noun
prohibited by law
Pronunciation: / ɪn proʊˈhɪbɪtəm / (AmE), / ɪn prəʊˈhɪbɪtəm / (BrE)
a legal term for something that is forbidden or prohibited by law

Examples

A contract in prohibitum
The judge ruled the agreement void because it was made in prohibitum.
Any contract established in prohibitum cannot be enforced in court.
The lawyer argued that the transaction was clearly in prohibitum under current regulations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permitted - allowed by law or rules
  • synonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law
