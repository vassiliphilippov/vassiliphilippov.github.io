canting

adjective
heraldic visual pun
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæn.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkæn.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
in heraldry, depicting a visual pun on the bearer's name, often by using images that suggest the name's sound or meaning

Examples

Canting arms design
The knight's canting arms featured a lion to represent his surname, Lyon.
Many medieval families used canting symbols to make their coats of arms memorable.
A canting device on the shield cleverly alluded to the family's name through imagery.
