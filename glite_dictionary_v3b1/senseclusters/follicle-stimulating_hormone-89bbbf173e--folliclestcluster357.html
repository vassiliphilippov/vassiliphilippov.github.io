follicle-stimulating hormone

noun
hormone regulating egg and sperm production

Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.lɪ.kəl ˈstɪm.jə.leɪ.tɪŋ ˈhɔːr.moʊn/ /; / /ˈfɑː.lə.kəl ˈstɪm.jə.leɪ.tɪŋ ˈhɔːr.moʊn/ (AmE), /ˈfɒl.ɪ.kəl ˈstɪm.jə.leɪ.tɪŋ ˈhɔː.məʊn/ /; / /ˈfɒl.ə.kəl ˈstɪm.jə.leɪ.tɪŋ ˈhɔː.məʊn/ (BrE)
A hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the development of ovarian follicles in females and the production of sperm in males.

Examples

A rise in follicle-stimulating hormone stimulates ovulation in women.
Low follicle-stimulating hormone can indicate problems with reproductive health.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check her follicle-stimulating hormone levels.
