dullish

adjective
somewhat dull
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdʌlɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat slow in understanding or lacking mental sharpness; slightly dull.

Examples

Dullish mind
After the sleepless night she felt dullish and struggled to follow the professor's argument.
He gave a dullish response, clearly not fully awake yet.
The meeting was so early that everyone seemed a bit dullish at first.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dull - not sharp, quick, or alert in mind
  • antonym: sharp - quick to notice, understand, or react
  • synonym: slow-witted - not quick to understand or think
