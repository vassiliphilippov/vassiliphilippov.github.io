nucleator

noun
initiator of cell structures
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a protein or structure that initiates the formation of filaments or organelles in a cell, such as starting actin or microtubule growth

Examples

Actin nucleator
The cell recruits an actin nucleator to begin forming the new protrusion during migration.
Researchers discovered a new nucleator that helps organize microtubules in plant cells.
Without a proper nucleator, the assembly of cellular filaments would be much less efficient.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a process
  • synonym: initiator - something that begins a process or action
