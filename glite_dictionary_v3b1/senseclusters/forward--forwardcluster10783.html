forward

adverb
toward the front or ahead
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
toward the front or in the direction that one is facing or moving

Examples

Move forward
After a brief pause, the convoy continued forward through the narrow street.
She stepped forward to accept her award.
The troops moved forward despite heavy opposition.
Crew members gathered forward to prepare for docking procedures.
Passengers in the cabin were asked to walk forward for balancing the plane.
The captain moved forward to inspect the equipment near the bow.
He looked forward without glancing to the side.
Please step forward so everyone can see you.
The soldiers marched forward into the fog.
