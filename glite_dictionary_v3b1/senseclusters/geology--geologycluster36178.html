geology

noun
science of Earth's structure and history
1.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /dʒiː.ˈɑː.lə.dʒiː/ /; / /dʒɪ.ˈɑː.lə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /dʒi.ˈɒl.ə.dʒiː/ /; / /dʒɪ.ˈɒl.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
The scientific study of the Earth's physical structure, history, and processes as recorded in rocks and geological formations.

Examples

Study of geology
Advances in geology have helped scientists better understand the age of the Earth.
She decided to major in geology because she was fascinated by volcanoes and earthquakes.
The museum's exhibit on geology features rocks and fossils from around the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biology - science that studies living organisms; contrasting scientific field
  • synonym: geoscience - science dealing with the Earth; another term for earth science
  • synonym: petrology - branch of geology concerned with the origin, structure, and composition of rocks
