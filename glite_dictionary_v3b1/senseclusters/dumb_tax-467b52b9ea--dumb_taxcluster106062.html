dumb tax

noun
cost from making bad decisions
6.7
Pronunciation: / dʌm tæks / (AmE), / dʌm tæks / (BrE)
an informal term for a financial penalty or unnecessary expense incurred as a result of poor decisions or mistakes

Examples

Pay the dumb tax
Buying a new car without negotiating was a classic way to pay the dumb tax.
Forgetting to cancel the free trial meant I had to pay the dumb tax this month.
If you don't read the contract carefully, you might end up paying a dumb tax later.

Related Terms

  • synonym: penalty - a punishment or disadvantage for breaking a rule or making a mistake
