plunger

noun
one who takes big risks for possible gains
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈplʌndʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈplʌndʒə / (BrE)
a person who risks significant losses in the hope of making considerable gains, especially in gambling or speculation

Examples

A plunger in gambling
He earned a reputation as a plunger on the stock market, often investing large sums on a hunch.
In the world of high-stakes gambling, every plunger hopes for that one big win.
Only a true plunger would bet everything on a single hand of poker.

Related Terms

  • synonym: risk-taker - someone who is willing to take risks
