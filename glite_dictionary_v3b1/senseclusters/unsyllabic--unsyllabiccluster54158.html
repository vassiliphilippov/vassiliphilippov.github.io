unsyllabic

adjective
not forming the nucleus of a syllable
6.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /ʌn.sɪ.ˈlæb.ɪk/ /; / /ən.sɪ.ˈlæb.ɪk/ (AmE), /ʌn.sɪ.ˈlæb.ɪk/ /; / /ən.sɪ.ˈlæb.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a sound) not forming or constituting a syllable or the main part (nucleus) of a syllable; not able to stand alone as a syllable.

Examples

Unsyllabic consonant sound
Certain consonants can be either syllabic or unsyllabic depending on their phonetic context.
In the word 'little', the second 'l' is often unsyllabic.
The vowel in the diphthong 'oi' is unsyllabic in English pronunciation.
