dipole

noun
type of antenna with two rods
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.pəʊl / (BrE)
an antenna consisting of two straight rods of equal length connected to a transmission line at their center, typically half a wavelength long

Examples

Radio dipole antenna
A simple dipole can be used for many radio transmission applications.
He installed a dipole antenna to improve his shortwave radio reception.
The dipole is one of the most widely used basic antenna types in broadcasting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monopole - an antenna with a single rod or pole
