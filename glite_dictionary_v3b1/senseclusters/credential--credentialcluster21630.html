credential

noun
digital authentication data
5.6
Pronunciation: /krɪ.ˈden.ʃəl/ /; / /krə.ˈden.ʃəl/ (AmE), /krɪ.ˈden.ʃəl/ (BrE)
(computing) a piece of digital information (for example, a password, token, or certificate) used to verify or authenticate a user's identity or access rights.

Examples

Login credentials
She saved her login credentials in the password manager for quick access.
The server rejects the request when the provided credential does not match any user account.
During the audit they found weak API credentials that allowed unauthorized services to read data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certificate - a digital certificate used to prove the identity of a device or service
  • synonym: password - a secret word or string of characters used to authenticate a user
