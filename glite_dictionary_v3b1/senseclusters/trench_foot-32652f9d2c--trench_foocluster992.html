trench foot

noun
medical condition from cold, wet feet
7.2
Pronunciation: / trɛntʃ fʊt / (AmE), / trɛntʃ fʊt / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by prolonged exposure of the feet to cold, damp, and unsanitary conditions, resulting in pain, swelling, numbness, and sometimes tissue damage.

Examples

Trench foot condition
If you don't keep your feet dry while hiking in wet weather, you might develop trench foot.
Many soldiers suffered from trench foot during the harsh winters of World War I.
Medical staff treated several cases of trench foot after the flood left people stranded in cold water.
