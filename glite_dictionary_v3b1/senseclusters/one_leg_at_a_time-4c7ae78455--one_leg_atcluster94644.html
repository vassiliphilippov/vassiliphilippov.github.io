one leg at a time

x
to emphasize everyone is fundamentally the same
Pronunciation: / wʌn lɛɡ ət ə taɪm / (AmE), / wʌn lɛɡ ət ə taɪm / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize that all people, regardless of their status or abilities, are fundamentally no different from one another, often when performing ordinary tasks.

Examples

Put on one leg at a time
Even the famous actor puts his pants on one leg at a time just like the rest of us.
No matter how successful you are, you still get dressed one leg at a time.
Remember, your new boss is human too—he puts on his trousers one leg at a time.
