jaundice

noun
yellowing of skin and eyes; medical condition
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔn.dɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːn.dɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition marked by yellowing of the skin and the whites of the eyes, caused by an accumulation of bilirubin in the blood, often indicating liver or bile duct disease

Examples

Severe jaundice symptoms
A sudden onset of jaundice can be a sign of a serious liver disorder.
Infants are sometimes born with mild jaundice that usually clears up on its own.
The doctor suspected hepatitis when he noticed the patient's jaundice and other symptoms.
