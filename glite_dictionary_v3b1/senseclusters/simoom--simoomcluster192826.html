simoom

noun
hot desert wind of Arabia/North Africa
Pronunciation: / sɪˈmum /; / sɪˈmun / (AmE), / sɪˈmuːm /; / sɪˈmuːn / (BrE)
a hot, dry, dust-laden wind that blows across the deserts of North Africa and Arabia, often violent and sometimes causing suffocating conditions

Examples

A simoom wind
During their journey across the Sahara, a sudden simoom made travel nearly impossible.
Stories of the deadly simoom have passed down through generations in North African villages.
The explorers took shelter to avoid the suffocating heat of the simoom.
Many legends describe the deadly power of the simoon in the Arabian deserts.
The simoon swept across the desert, making travel impossible for days.
Villagers took shelter from the fierce simoon that carried clouds of sand into their homes.
