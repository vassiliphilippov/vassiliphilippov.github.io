reuptake

noun
absorption of a substance again, especially in biology
Pronunciation: / riˈʌp.teɪk / (AmE), / riːˈʌp.teɪk / (BrE)
the process by which a substance, especially a neurotransmitter, is absorbed again by a cell after being released

Examples

Reuptake of neurotransmitters
Dopamine reuptake is a crucial process in regulating mood and motivation.
Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors are a common class of antidepressant medications.
Some drugs block the reuptake of neurotransmitters, increasing their levels in the brain.
