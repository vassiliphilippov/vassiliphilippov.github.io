commodity

noun
raw material or product for trade
3.1 3.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɑːdədi / (AmE), / kəˈmɒdəti / (BrE)
A raw material or primary agricultural product that can be bought and sold, especially in large quantities and often used in manufacturing or production.

Examples

A commodity like wheat
Farmers rely on stable prices for their commodities like wheat and corn.
Gold is considered a valuable commodity that is traded on international markets.
Oil is a major commodity that plays a crucial role in the global economy.
