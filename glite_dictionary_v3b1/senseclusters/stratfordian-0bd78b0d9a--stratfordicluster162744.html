Stratfordian

noun
Shakespeare authorship believer
9.8
Pronunciation: /stræt.ˈfɔːr.dɪ.ən/ /; / /ˈstræt.fər.dɪ.ən/ (AmE), /stræt.ˈfɔː.di.ən/ /; / /ˈstræt.fə.di.ən/ (BrE)
a person who believes that William Shakespeare of Stratford-upon-Avon wrote the works attributed to him

Examples

A Stratfordian scholar
Many literary scholars identify as Stratfordians in the ongoing debate about Shakespeare's authorship.
The Stratfordian perspective holds that the historical records support Shakespeare of Stratford as the true author.
He became a passionate Stratfordian after reading several biographies of Shakespeare.
