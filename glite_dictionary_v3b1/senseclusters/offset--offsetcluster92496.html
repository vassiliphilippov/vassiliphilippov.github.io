offset

noun
ledge where wall narrows abruptly
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsɛt /; / ˈɑːfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsɛt / (BrE)
a part that is set apart, sticks out, or is not in line with the rest, such as a step in a wall or a shoot from a plant

Examples

A wall offset
At the offset, the thickness of the wall decreases to support the upper floors.
Restoration revealed an original offset buried beneath later additions to the building.
The architect designed an offset to provide visual interest along the exterior wall.
Some houseplants develop offsets at their base, making them easy to multiply.
Strawberries reproduce quickly by sending out offsets that root nearby.
The gardener separated the offsets from the parent plant to propagate new succulents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: runner - a long, slender stem that grows horizontally along the ground and roots to form new plants
