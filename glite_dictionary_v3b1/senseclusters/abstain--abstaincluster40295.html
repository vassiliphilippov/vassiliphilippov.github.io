abstain

verb
choose not to vote
5.9
Pronunciation: / əbˈsteɪn / (AmE), / əbˈsteɪn / (BrE)
to deliberately choose not to cast a vote, usually in a formal decision-making process or election

Examples

To abstain in a vote
Although he attended the meeting, he decided to abstain during the final vote.
Three members chose to abstain from voting on the controversial policy.
You may abstain if you do not wish to take a side in the election.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vote - to express a choice or opinion in a collective decision
