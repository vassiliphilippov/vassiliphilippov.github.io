revolt

verb
to cause disgust or distaste
4.1 4.3
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvoʊlt / (AmE), / rɪˈvəʊlt / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel intense disgust or strong moral aversion

Examples

Revolts at cruelty
It revolts me to see animals being mistreated.
Many people are revolted by graphic images of violence.
The thought of eating snails revolts her.
She was revolted by the blatant corruption in the organization.
The graphic scenes in the film revolted the audience.
The idea of cruelty to animals revolts him.
