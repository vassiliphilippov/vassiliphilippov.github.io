oversalt

verb
to add too much salt
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsɔlt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɒlt / (BrE)
to add too much salt to roads, soil, water, or other non-food surfaces, often causing harm or problems

Examples

To oversalt the road
If you oversalt the driveway, the excess can harm nearby grass and plants.
The city warned workers not to oversalt the roads to avoid polluting local streams.
It is easy to accidentally oversalt the soil when trying to melt ice quickly.
