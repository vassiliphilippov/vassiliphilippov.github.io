nabothian follicle

noun
cyst in the cervix (medical)
Pronunciation: / nəˈboʊθiən ˈfɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / nəˈbəʊθɪən ˈfɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
A cyst that forms in the nabothian glands of the uterine cervix, usually benign and filled with mucus.

Examples

A nabothian follicle cyst
A nabothian follicle is generally harmless and does not require treatment unless symptomatic.
On the ultrasound, the doctor noted the presence of a nabothian follicle in the cervix.
Routine pelvic exams can reveal a nabothian follicle in many women without causing symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nabothian cyst - A cyst occurring in the cervix due to blockage of nabothian gland ducts
