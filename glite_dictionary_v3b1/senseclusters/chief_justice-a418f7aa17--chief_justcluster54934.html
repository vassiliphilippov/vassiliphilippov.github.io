chief justice

noun
presiding judge of a supreme court
Pronunciation: / tʃiːf ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / tʃiːf ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
The judge who presides over a supreme court, especially the highest court in a country or region.

Examples

The chief justice presides
After years of service, she was appointed as the new chief justice of the Supreme Court.
The chief justice delivered the final opinion of the court yesterday.
The chief justice presided over the swearing-in ceremony for the president.
