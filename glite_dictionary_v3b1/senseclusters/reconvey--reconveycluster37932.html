reconvey

verb
to transfer back ownership
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈveɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈveɪ / (BrE)
to transfer property, rights, or title back to a previous owner, usually after a previous transfer or when certain conditions are met

Examples

To reconvey property
After the mortgage was fully paid, the lender agreed to reconvey the property to the homeowner.
The lawyer explained the process to reconvey the title once all obligations were met.
It is important to ensure the bank will reconvey the deed after the loan is settled.
The bank agreed to reconvey the property to the original owner after the loan was repaid.
After the merger, the company needed to reconvey certain assets to its subsidiaries.
A trustee may reconvey the title to the borrower once the debt is settled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convey - to transfer property or rights to another
  • antonym: retain - to keep possession of something
  • synonym: return - to give something back to its original owner
  • synonym: transfer - to move ownership or rights from one person to another
