short-tailed shrew

noun
small North American shrew with short tail
5.3 9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʃɔrt ˈteɪld ʃru / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːt ˈteɪld ʃruː / (BrE)
A small North American shrew characterized by a tail less than half the length of its body.

Examples

A short-tailed shrew
A short-tailed shrew can eat more than its body weight in insects each day.
The short-tailed shrew is commonly found in moist woodland habitats.
Unlike other shrews, the short-tailed shrew has venomous saliva to immobilize its prey.
