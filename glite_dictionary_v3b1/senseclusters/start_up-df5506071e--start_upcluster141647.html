start up

verb
to begin or launch something
Pronunciation: / stɑrt ˈʌp / (AmE), / stɑːt ˈʌp / (BrE)
to begin, launch, or establish a business, project, or organization

Examples

To start up a business
A group of young entrepreneurs plans to start up an eco-friendly travel company next year.
She decided to start up her own bakery after years of dreaming about it.
The computer takes a few minutes to start up when you first turn it on.
After graduating from college, she decided to start up her own bakery.
It takes a lot of planning and resources to start up a nonprofit organization.
The company will start up operations in Asia later this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: begin - to start to happen or exist; to start doing something
  • synonym: launch - to start something, especially a business or campaign
  • antonym: shut down - to stop operating or close something, especially a business or machine
