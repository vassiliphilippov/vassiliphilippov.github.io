reprieve

verb
postpone criminal punishment
7.7 6.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpriv / (AmE), / rɪˈpriːv / (BrE)
to postpone the punishment of a convicted criminal, especially to delay an execution

Examples

To reprieve a prisoner
After new evidence was presented, the court agreed to reprieve the man's punishment.
If the president does not reprieve her, the sentence will be carried out tomorrow.
The governor decided to reprieve the prisoner just hours before the scheduled execution.
