shepherd's crook

noun
long staff with a hooked end used by shepherds
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛpərdz krʊk / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpədz krʊk / (BrE)
a long staff with a curved or hook-shaped end, traditionally used by shepherds to manage and catch sheep

Examples

A shepherd's crook staff
The bishop carried a ceremonial shepherd's crook as a symbol of his pastoral care.
The old painting depicted a shepherd leaning on a shepherd's crook under a tree.
With his shepherd's crook, he gently guided the sheep back into the pen.
