war bride

noun
woman marrying serviceman during wartime
3.4
Pronunciation: / wɔr braɪd / (AmE), / wɔː braɪd / (BrE)
a woman who marries a soldier or serviceman during a war, often from another country and sometimes moving to the soldier's home country

Examples

A war bride woman
Her grandmother was a war bride who met her husband while he was stationed overseas.
Many war brides immigrated to new countries after World War II to join their husbands.
The museum hosted an exhibition on the lives of war brides during the 1940s.
Her grandmother was a war bride who moved to America after World War II.
Many war brides faced loneliness and challenges as they started new lives abroad.
The documentary tells the moving stories of several war brides adjusting to new cultures.
