head rhyme

noun
repetition of initial consonant sounds in poetry
6.8 6.9
Pronunciation: /hed raɪm/ (AmE), /hed raɪm/ (BrE)
the repetition of the same initial consonant sound at the beginning of each stressed syllable in a line of verse

Examples

Head rhyme in poetry
Children love tongue twisters that feature head rhyme, like 'Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers.'
In Old English poetry, head rhyme was more common than end rhyme.
The poet skillfully used head rhyme to create a musical effect in her verse.
