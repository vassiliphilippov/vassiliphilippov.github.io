decipher

verb
make sense of something hard to read
6.3 3.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsaɪ.fər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsaɪ.fə/ (BrE)
to figure out or make sense of something that is difficult to read or understand, such as messy handwriting, faded text, or something written in code

Examples

To decipher a code

She struggled to decipher his messy handwriting on the birthday card.
Historians are still trying to decipher the ancient script found on the ruins.
It took the team several hours to decipher the encrypted message.
She worked hard to decipher his messy handwriting on the letter.
