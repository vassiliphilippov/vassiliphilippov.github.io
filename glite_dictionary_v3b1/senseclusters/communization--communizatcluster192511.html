communization

noun
conversion to state ownership
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmjəˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / kəˌmjunəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌkɑːmjʊnɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌkɑːmjənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmjʊnaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / kəˌmjuːnaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n /; / kəˌmjuːnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring property or business from private ownership to the state or community, or organizing society according to communist principles

Examples

The communization of industries
After the revolution, the new government implemented the communization of major industries.
Debates about communization often focus on the balance between public good and individual rights.
The sudden communization of farmland led to unrest among the local farmers.
Debates about communization were central to the political movements of the twentieth century.
Opponents warned that communization of industry would lead to inefficiency and lack of innovation.
The new government began the communization of farmland soon after taking power.
Many feared that communization would lead to the loss of private property rights.
The history professor gave a lecture on the rapid communization of Eastern Europe after World War II.
The revolutionaries advocated for the immediate communization of all factories and farms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communism - a political theory advocating class war and leading to a society in which all property is publicly owned
  • synonym: nationalization - taking private assets into government ownership
  • antonym: privatization - transferring state-owned assets to private ownership
