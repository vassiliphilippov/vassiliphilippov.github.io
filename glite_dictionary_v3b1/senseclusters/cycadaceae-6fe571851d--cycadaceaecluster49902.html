Cycadaceae

noun
family of ancient, palmlike tropical plants
Pronunciation: / saɪˌkædəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / sɪˌkædəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a family of ancient, palmlike, mostly tropical plants with large compound leaves, closely related to ferns in reproductive method

Examples

The Cycadaceae family
Fossils show that members of the Cycadaceae family existed during the Jurassic period.
Many Cycadaceae species are endangered due to habitat loss and overcollection.
The Cycadaceae are often mistaken for true palms, but they are actually gymnosperms.
