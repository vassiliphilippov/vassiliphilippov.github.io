resaw

noun
wood sawing process
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈriːˌsɔː / (BrE)
the process of sawing a piece of wood again, especially to cut it into thinner boards or veneers

Examples

Resaw process
The carpenter used a resaw to create thinner boards from the thick timber.
After the resaw, the wood was ready to be turned into fine veneers.
Proper technique during the resaw ensures that the resulting boards are even and smooth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: planing - the process of smoothing or shaping wood with a plane
  • synonym: sawing - the act of cutting something with a saw
