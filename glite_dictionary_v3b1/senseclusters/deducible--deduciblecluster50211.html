deducible

adjective
able to be deduced or inferred
6.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdusəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈdjuːsəbəl / (BrE)
Capable of being deduced or inferred by reasoning from available information.

Examples

Deducible from facts
His feelings were deducible from his actions, even if he never spoke about them.
In mathematics, some theorems are not directly stated but are deducible from previously established results.
The answer to the puzzle was deducible from the information given in the text.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inducible - capable of being induced or brought about
  • synonym: inferable - capable of being inferred
