weatherization

noun
energy-saving building process
5.4
Pronunciation: /weð.ə.r.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /weð.ə.r.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making a building more energy efficient by protecting it from the effects of weather

Examples

Home weatherization
The government launched a weatherization program to help families lower their energy bills.
Proper weatherization can make homes more comfortable during both winter and summer.
After weatherization, the old house was much warmer and used less electricity.
The city offered free weatherization services to help residents save on their energy bills.
After weatherization, the old house was much warmer in winter and cooler in summer.
Government grants made weatherization projects affordable for many low-income families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insulation - material or process used to prevent heat loss or gain
  • synonym: retrofitting - adding new technology or features to older buildings
