catchweight

noun
nonstandard fight weight
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃˌweɪt / (AmE), / ˈkætʃˌweɪt / (BrE)
a weight limit for a fight that is agreed upon by both competitors and does not match official weight classes

Examples

Catchweight boxing match
The championship match was held at a catchweight because the fighters were from different divisions.
Negotiations led both teams to agree on a catchweight for the highly anticipated bout.
Fighting at a catchweight allowed both athletes to compete without drastic weight cuts.
