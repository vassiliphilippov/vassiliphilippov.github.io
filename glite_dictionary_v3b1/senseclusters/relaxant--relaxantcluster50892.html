relaxant

adjective
tending to relieve muscular or nervous tension
3.3 5.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlæksənt / (AmE), / rɪˈlæksənt / (BrE)
tending to relieve muscular or nervous tension, especially by relaxing muscles or nerves; often refers to a drug or agent with such an effect.

Examples

Relaxant effect
After the stressful meeting, she took a mild relaxant to calm her nerves.
Many herbal teas are marketed as natural relaxant remedies for anxiety.
The doctor prescribed a relaxant to help ease the patient's back spasms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muscle relaxant - a drug that relieves muscle spasms or stiffness
  • antonym: stimulant - an agent that increases physiological activity
