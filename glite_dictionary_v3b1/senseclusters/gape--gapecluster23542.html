gape

noun
openmouthed look of astonishment
4.6 3.9
Pronunciation: /geɪp/ (AmE), /geɪp/ (BrE)
an act or expression of staring with your mouth open in amazement or wonder

Examples

A surprised gape
Everyone watched in silent gape as the unexpected announcement was made.
He sat there in a gape throughout the entire performance, unable to hide his awe.
The child's face broke into a gape as the magician pulled a rabbit from the hat.
He stood in the museum, his mouth in a wide gape as he admired the ancient sculpture.
Her obvious gape at the magician’s disappearing act made everyone around her chuckle.
The students listened to the astronaut's story, eyes wide and faces frozen in a collective gape.
