nonenforcement

noun
failure to enforce
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnɪnˈfɔrsmənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɪnˈfɔːsmənt / (BrE)
the failure or decision not to apply or carry out rules, laws, or regulations

Examples

The nonenforcement of laws
The nonenforcement of traffic laws led to more accidents in the city.
Critics argue that nonenforcement of environmental regulations harms public health.
Selective nonenforcement can create a sense of unfairness among citizens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enforcement - the act of making sure laws or rules are obeyed
