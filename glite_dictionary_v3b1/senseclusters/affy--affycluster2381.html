affy

noun
British liquor store
Pronunciation: / ˈæfi / (BrE)
a British slang term for an off-licence or liquor store where alcoholic drinks are sold for consumption elsewhere

Examples

The affy shop
He stopped by the affy to pick up some drinks before the party.
On his way home, Tom popped into the affy for a bottle of wine.
Locals often refer to the corner shop as the affy when buying alcohol.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquor store - a shop that sells alcoholic beverages
  • synonym: off-licence - a shop licensed to sell alcoholic drinks for consumption off the premises
