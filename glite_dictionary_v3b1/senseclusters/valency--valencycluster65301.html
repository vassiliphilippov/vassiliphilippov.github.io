valency

noun
capacity to interact in biological processes
9.0 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈveɪ.lən.siː/ /; / /ˈveɪ.lən.sɪ/ (AmE), /ˈveɪ.lən.siː/ /; / /ˈveɪ.lən.sɪ/ (BrE)
the capacity of a biological molecule to react with others, or the number of different types (such as antigens) included in something like a vaccine

Examples

Vaccine valency
A change in valency may alter the biological response to a particular medication.
Researchers studied the valency of viral proteins to assess their interactions in the host.
The valency of an antibody determines how effectively it can bind to antigens.
The tetravalent flu shot's valency means it protects against four influenza strains this season.
Researchers increased the vaccine's valency to provide broader immunity across expected variants.
Public health agencies recommend higher valencies for vaccines when multiple strains circulate in a region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monovalent - containing a single antigen or strain, opposite of multivalent
  • synonym: multivalent - containing multiple different antigens or strains; often used to describe vaccines covering several variants
