moral low ground

noun
unethical stance in an argument
7.4 6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔɹəl loʊ ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈmɒɹəl ləʊ ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
a position or course of action in an argument or debate that is considered unethical or lacking in moral integrity

Examples

Take the moral low ground
Accusing others without evidence is a way of taking the moral low ground in any discussion.
Resorting to bribery put the company firmly on the moral low ground in the eyes of the public.
The politician took the moral low ground by spreading false rumors about his opponent.
