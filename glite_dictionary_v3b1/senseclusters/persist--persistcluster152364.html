(formal) To remain in a place and not leave; to stay for an extended period.

Examples

Symptoms persist
If symptoms persist, consult your doctor before continuing with any activities.
Several protesters chose to persist inside the building even after they were asked to leave.
The fog persisted over the hills for hours, refusing to lift.

Synonyms

  • remain
    1.0 316/m
    remain means to stay in the same place or condition for some time, while persist sounds more formal and long-lasting.
  • stay
    1.0 422/m
    stay means to remain in a place or state without leaving, while persist is more formal and suggests longer duration.
  • stay on
    3.4 6.12/m
    stay on means to continue remaining after others leave or after the expected time, while persist is formal and used in writing.
  • linger
    3.7 18/m
    linger means to stay longer than expected, often because you do not want to leave, while persist sounds formal and neutral.
  • stay in place
    1.5 0.78/m
    stay in place means to remain without moving from a position, while persist is a formal word for staying longer.
  • continue
    1.0 42/m
    continue means to keep going or happen without stopping in any context, while persist is a formal word for staying in place.
