obediential

adjective
enforcing obedience
9.9
Pronunciation: / oʊˌbiːdiˈɛnʃəl / (AmE), / əʊˌbiːdiˈɛnʃəl / (BrE)
intended to produce, enforce, or reinforce obedience, or tending to make people comply with authority.

Examples

Obediential rules
The principal introduced several obediential policies to improve student discipline.
Strict obediential measures were put in place to ensure everyone followed the new safety rules.
Some parents prefer a more obediential approach to raising their children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disciplinary - intended to enforce rules or correct behavior
  • antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great freedom of behavior
