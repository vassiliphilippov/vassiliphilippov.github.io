radiational

adjective
caused by radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the emission or transfer of radiation, especially energy in the form of waves or particles

Examples

Radiational heat loss
The rapid cooling of the ground at night is often due to radiational heat loss.
Meteorologists study radiational processes to understand temperature changes in the atmosphere.
Frost can form on clear nights because of strong radiational cooling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonradiative - not involving the emission of radiation
  • synonym: radiative - relating to the emission or transmission of radiation
