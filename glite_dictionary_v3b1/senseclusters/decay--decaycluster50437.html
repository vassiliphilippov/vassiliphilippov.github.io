decay

verb
decompose; rot
1.4 2.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to decompose or rot through the action of bacteria and fungi.

Examples

To decay in soil
If not preserved properly, food will quickly decay in warm temperatures.
Over time, the fallen leaves decay and enrich the soil.
The abandoned house showed signs of wood beginning to decay after years of neglect.
