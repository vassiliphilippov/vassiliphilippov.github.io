azoic

adjective
before the appearance of life
9.9
Pronunciation: / eɪˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / eɪˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
(of a period or material) existing or formed before the appearance of life; containing no organic remains.

Examples

The azoic era
Early Earth history is divided into the azoic era, predating the emergence of living forms.
The geologists found azoic rocks deep beneath the surface, indicating an ancient time before any life existed.
The region is rich in azoic deposits, which show no trace of fossilized organisms.
