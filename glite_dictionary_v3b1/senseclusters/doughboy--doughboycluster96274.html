doughboy

noun
American infantryman in World War I
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊˌbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊˌbɔɪ / (BrE)
an American infantryman, especially one who served in World War I

Examples

American doughboy soldier
Many young doughboys crossed the Atlantic to fight in the trenches of France.
My great-grandfather was a doughboy and kept a diary from his time overseas.
The memorial honored the sacrifice of every American doughboy who served in World War I.

Related Terms

  • synonym: GI - an enlisted American soldier, especially in World War II
  • antonym: Tommy - a British soldier, especially one from World War I or II
