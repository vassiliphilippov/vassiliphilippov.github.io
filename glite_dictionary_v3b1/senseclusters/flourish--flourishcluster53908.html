flourish

verb
grow or develop vigorously
Pronunciation: / ˈflɜrɪʃ /; / ˈflɝːɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflʌrɪʃ / (BrE)
to grow, develop, or be successful in a healthy or vigorous way

Examples

Plants flourish
Despite the harsh conditions, the young trees managed to flourish.
The small business began to flourish after moving to a larger city.
Wildflowers flourish in this region during the spring months.
Ancient Greek civilization flourished during the 5th century BCE.
The artist's career truly began to flourish after her first major exhibition.
Under her leadership, the small business flourished beyond everyone's expectations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prosper - to succeed, especially in financial or economic terms
  • synonym: thrive - to grow or develop well; prosper
  • antonym: wither - to dry up and shrink; to become weak or feeble
