serac

noun
glacial ice block
Pronunciation: / səˈræk / (AmE), / səˈræk / (BrE)
a large block or column of glacial ice found on crevasses, steep slopes, or icefalls of a glacier

Examples

A large serac
The climbers set up camp near a towering serac that loomed over the glacier.
A sudden collapse of a serac can create dangerous conditions for mountaineers.
Photographers captured the sunlight glinting off the jagged surface of the serac.
The climbers carefully avoided a towering serac that threatened to collapse at any moment.
A massive serac broke off the glacier, sending ice tumbling down the slope.
Photographers captured the sunlight shining through a blue serac on the mountain expedition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crevasse - a deep open crack in a glacier
  • antonym: iceberg - a large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier
  • synonym: icefall - a steep section of a glacier with many crevasses and ice blocks
