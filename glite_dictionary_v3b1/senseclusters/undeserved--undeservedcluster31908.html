undeserved

adjective
not deserved or earned
4.2 0.83/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒÊŒndÉªËˆzÉvd / (AmE), / ËŒÊŒndÉªËˆzÉœËvd / (BrE)
(of a reward, punishment, praise, etc.) not justified or earned by one's actions; not merited.

Examples

An undeserved punishment
His reputation for laziness is completely undeserved and unfair.
She received an undeserved promotion, causing resentment among her colleagues.
The team's defeat felt undeserved after such a strong performance.

Synonyms

  • unmerited
    7.6 0.17/m
    unmerited is used to say praise or blame has no merit and sounds a bit formal, while undeserved is the more general, everyday word.
  • unearned
    5.8 0.24/m
    unearned means received without work or effort and often applies to money or praise, while undeserved focuses on lacking merit.
  • unwarranted
    7.0 2.62/m
    unwarranted describes actions or criticism done without good reason and sounds formal, while undeserved stresses lacking merit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deserved - merited; rightfully earned or warranted
  • synonym: justified - shown or proven to be right or reasonable
