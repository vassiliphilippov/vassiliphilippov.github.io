Arian

noun
follower of Arius
Pronunciation: / ˈɛəriən / (AmE), / ˈɛəriən / (BrE)
a follower of the Christian doctrine taught by Arius, especially in early church history, which denied the full divinity of Jesus Christ

Examples

An Arian believer
The Arians played a significant role in the religious controversies of the fourth century.
Many early church councils were convened to address the beliefs of the Arians.
Historians study the influence of Arians on the development of Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Trinitarian - a believer in the doctrine of the Trinity in Christianity
