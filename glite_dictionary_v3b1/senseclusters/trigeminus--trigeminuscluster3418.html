trigeminus

noun
main sensory nerve of the face
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: /traɪ.ˈdʒem.ɪ.nəs/ /; / /trɪ.ˈdʒem.ɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /traɪ.ˈdʒem.ɪ.nəs/ /; / /trɪ.ˈdʒem.ɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the main sensory nerve of the face and the motor nerve responsible for controlling the muscles involved in chewing (mastication)

Examples

The trigeminus nerve
Damage to the trigeminus can lead to loss of feeling in the face.
Surgeons must be careful not to injure the trigeminus during operations near the jaw.
The dentist explained that the trigeminus is responsible for facial sensation and chewing movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trigeminal nerve - the fifth cranial nerve, responsible for sensation in the face and motor functions such as biting and chewing
