leak

verb
let liquid or gas escape through an opening
1.0 9.44/m
Pronunciation: / lik / (AmE), / liÀêk / (BrE)
to let liquid, gas, or light escape or enter through a hole or crack

Examples

To leak water
If your gas tank leaks, it can be very dangerous.
Rainwater leaked through the hole in the roof after the storm.
The old water bottle leaks if you turn it upside down.
A small amount of gas leaked into the apartment through a crack in the pipe.
The container is leaking oil onto the garage floor.
Water began to leak from the roof during the heavy rain.

Synonyms

  • seep
    2.7 7.15/m
    seep is when liquid moves slowly through tiny holes or pores, while leak means letting liquid, gas, or light escape.
  • ooze
    3.9 4.24/m
    ooze is a slow, often thick flow that comes out visibly from openings, while leak means letting something escape through a hole.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drip - to let liquid fall in drops
  • degree higher: pour - to flow rapidly in a steady stream (degree higher of leaking)
  • synonym: seep - to flow or leak slowly through porous material or small holes
  • synonym: spill - to let liquid flow over the edge of its container
