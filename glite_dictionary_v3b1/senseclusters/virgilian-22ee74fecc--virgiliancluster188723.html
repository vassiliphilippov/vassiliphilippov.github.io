Virgilian

noun
geological time stage
Pronunciation: / vɜːrˈdʒɪliən / (AmE), / vɜːˈdʒɪliən / (BrE)
a stage or series in late Pennsylvanian (Carboniferous) stratigraphy, used in North American geology to classify rock layers

Examples

The Virgilian period
The Virgilian is recognized for its distinctive fossil assemblages in North American rock formations.
Researchers studied coal beds from the Virgilian to understand ancient climate changes.
Several important mineral deposits are found in strata assigned to the Virgilian stage.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Missourian - the stage preceding the Virgilian in Pennsylvanian stratigraphy
  • degree higher: Wolfcampian - the stage following the Virgilian in North American stratigraphy
