canonically

adverb
in a canonical or conforming manner
5.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈnæn.ɪ.kəl.iː/ (AmE), /kə.ˈnɒn.ɪ.kəl.iː/ (BrE)
In a way that conforms to accepted rules or standards, especially those of the church or tradition.

Examples

To be canonically ordained
According to church law, a priest must be canonically ordained before he can administer sacraments.
In mathematics, structures are often defined canonically to provide a standard form.
The document was written canonically to ensure it followed the standards of the tradition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uncanonically - in a way that does not conform to canon or accepted standards
