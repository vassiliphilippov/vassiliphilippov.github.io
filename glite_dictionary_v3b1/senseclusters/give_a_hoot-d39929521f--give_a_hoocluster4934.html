give a hoot

verb
show any concern or interest (usually negative)
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ə huːt /; / ɡɪv ə hut / (AmE), / ɡɪv ə huːt / (BrE)
to care at all or be concerned about something (almost always used in the negative)

Examples

To give a hoot about
He really doesn't give a hoot what people think of him.
Most teenagers don't give a hoot about traditional customs.
Why should I give a hoot if they disagree with me?
I really don't give a hoot about what people say behind my back.
Most politicians don't seem to give a hoot about the average citizen.
She always gives a hoot about animal welfare and helps at the shelter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: give a damn - to care at all; to show any interest (used in the negative)
