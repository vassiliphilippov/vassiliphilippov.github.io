disavow

verb
deny responsibility or association
9.7 6.2
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ə.ˈvaʊ/ (AmE), /dɪs.ə.ˈvaʊ/ (BrE)
to deny responsibility for or connection with something; to refuse to acknowledge or accept.

Examples

To disavow responsibility
After the scandal broke, the politician quickly disavowed any knowledge of the illegal activities.
Despite the rumors, she firmly disavowed any connection to the controversial group.
The company disavowed responsibility for the defective products found in the market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acknowledge - to accept or admit the existence or truth of something
  • synonym: deny - to state that something is not true or that you do not believe something
