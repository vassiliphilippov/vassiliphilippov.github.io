loss leader

noun
item sold at a loss to attract customers
4.3 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / l…ës Ààlid…ôr / (AmE), / l…ís ÀàliÀêd…ô / (BrE)
An item sold at a loss to attract customers to a store or business, hoping they will purchase other items at regular prices.

Examples

A loss leader product
Many electronics retailers use a loss leader strategy with discounted printers to encourage sales of profitable ink cartridges.
Supermarkets often choose bread or eggs as a loss leader to lure customers into their stores.
The store advertised milk as a loss leader to bring in more shoppers over the weekend.

Synonyms

  • leader
    7.9 3.30/m
    leader is a product sold at a loss to attract customers, while loss leader is the full term for this retail strategy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bait-and-switch - A deceptive sales tactic where a low-priced product entices customers, but they are encouraged to buy a higher-priced item instead.
