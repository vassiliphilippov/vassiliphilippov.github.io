extramural

adjective
outside a particular institution or community
7.7 5.8
Pronunciation: /ek.strə.ˈmʊr.əl/ /; / /ek.strə.ˈmjuːr.əl/ (AmE), /ek.strə.ˈmjuː.rəl/ /; / /ek.strə.ˈmʊə.rəl/ (BrE)
(of an activity) taking place outside the boundaries of a particular institution or community, especially used to describe educational or sports activities organized by or for members of an institution but held elsewhere.

Examples

An extramural activity
Her research is funded by an extramural grant from a national agency.
Many students participate in extramural sports leagues that compete with other universities.
The university offers several extramural courses for adult learners in the community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intramural - taking place within the walls or confines of an institution
