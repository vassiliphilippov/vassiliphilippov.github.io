monotonal

adjective
single tone or color
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈtoʊnəl / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈtəʊnəl / (BrE)
having or using only one tone, color, or shade throughout; monochromatic.

Examples

Monotonal painting
The artist created a monotonal landscape using only shades of blue.
Her room was decorated in a monotonal style, with everything in soft gray.
Critics praised the monotonal design for its simplicity and elegance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monochromatic - having only one color or hue
  • antonym: polychromatic - having many colors
