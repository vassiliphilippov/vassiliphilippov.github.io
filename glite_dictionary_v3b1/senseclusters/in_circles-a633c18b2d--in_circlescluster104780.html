in circles

adverb
without making progress; going nowhere
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈsɜr.kəlz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈsɜː.klz / (BrE)
used to describe actions or conversations that are repetitious and do not lead to any progress or conclusion.

Examples

Go in circles
It feels like I'm running in circles with all this paperwork.
The discussion went in circles for hours, without reaching any solution.
They kept talking in circles instead of addressing the main issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: going round in circles - repeatedly doing the same thing without achieving anything
