jeweled

adjective
decorated with jewels or beads
3.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íuÀê…ôld / (AmE), / Ààd íuÀê…ôld / (BrE)
Decorated or covered with jewels, beads, or sequins, often to enhance beauty or value.

Examples

A jeweled brooch
He gave her a jeweled brooch for her birthday.
Her gown was elaborately jeweled and shimmered under the lights.
The princess wore a jeweled crown encrusted with emeralds and rubies.
She wore a stunning jewelled necklace for the ceremony.
The costume designer chose a jewelled mask to add glamour to the scene.
The queen's crown was heavily jewelled and sparkled under the lights.

Synonyms

  • gemmed
    7.0 0.01/m
    gemmed is decorated with jewels or beads for a rich, solid look, while jeweled suggests a more luxurious look and higher value.
  • spangled
    7.1 1.29/m
    spangled is covered with many small shiny pieces like sequins to sparkle, while jeweled implies real gems and higher value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bedazzled - decorated or adorned with sparkling objects
  • synonym: bejeweled - adorned with jewels or precious stones
  • antonym: plain - not decorated or ornamented
