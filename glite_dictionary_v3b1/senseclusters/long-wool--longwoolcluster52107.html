long-wool

adjective
having long wool (of sheep)
4.6
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ wʊl / (AmE), / lɒŋ wʊl / (BrE)
(of sheep) having relatively long wool, typically referring to certain breeds known for their length of fleece.

Examples

Long-wool sheep
Many British breeds are classified as long-wool sheep because of their heavy, lustrous fleeces.
The farm specializes in raising long-wool rams for their high-quality wool.
These long-wool varieties require more frequent shearing than short-wool breeds.
