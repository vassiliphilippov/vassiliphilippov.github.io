give grief

verb
deliberately harass or sabotage players in online games
8.6
Pronunciation: /gɪv griːf/ (AmE), /gɪv griːf/ (BrE)
(in online multiplayer gaming) to deliberately harass, disrupt, or sabotage another player's gameplay, typically by trolling, exploiting glitches, or intentionally ruining their experience.

Examples

To give grief in games
The server banned him after he kept giving grief to new players during matches.
Some kids deliberately give grief by stealing loot and blocking others' progress in raids.
We couldn't finish the mission because one player gave grief by exploiting a game glitch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harass - to disturb or bother someone repeatedly or persistently
  • synonym: troll - to deliberately provoke or upset other users online, often for amusement
