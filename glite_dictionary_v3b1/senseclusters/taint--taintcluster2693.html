taint

verb
contaminate with disease or microorganism
5.9 5.8
Pronunciation: / teɪnt / (AmE), / teɪnt / (BrE)
to make something impure, harmful, or less good by contaminating it or damaging its reputation

Examples

To taint food
Improper handling can taint food with harmful bacteria.
The water supply was tainted by a dangerous microorganism after the flood.
Unsterilized instruments may taint the wound with infection.
Any doubt about her honesty would taint her otherwise impeccable record.
False accusations can taint even the most virtuous person's reputation.
The scandal began to taint the senator's entire career in the eyes of the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sully - to damage the purity or integrity of; to defile or tarnish
