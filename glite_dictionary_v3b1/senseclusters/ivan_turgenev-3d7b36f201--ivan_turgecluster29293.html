Ivan Turgenev

proper noun
Russian novelist and playwright (1818–1883)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvɑn tʊrˈɡɛ.nɛf / (AmE), / ɪˈvæn tʊəˈɡɛ.nɪɛv / (BrE)
(1818–1883) a Russian novelist, short story writer, and playwright known for works such as 'Fathers and Sons' and for his portrayal of Russian society.

Examples

Ivan Turgenev Russian novelist
Ivan Turgenev played a major role in introducing Russian realism to the West.
Many students read Ivan Turgenev’s 'Fathers and Sons' in their literature courses.
The works of Ivan Turgenev often explore themes of generational conflict and social change.
