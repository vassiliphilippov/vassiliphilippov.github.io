bromodeoxyuridine

noun
synthetic DNA analogue
Pronunciation: / ˌbroʊmoʊdiˌɑksiˈjʊrəˌdaɪn /; / ˌbroʊmoʊdiˌɑksiˈjʊrəˌdiːn / (AmE), / ˌbrəʊməʊdiˌɒksɪˈjʊərɪdaɪn /; / ˌbrəʊməʊdiˌɒksiˈjʊərɪdiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic chemical used in research to replace thymidine in DNA and to label or track cell division and DNA synthesis
