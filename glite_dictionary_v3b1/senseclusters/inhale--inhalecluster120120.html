inhale

verb
breathe in air or gas
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈheɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈheɪl / (BrE)
to breathe in air, smoke, gas, or other substances into the lungs

Examples

To inhale smoke
He accidentally inhaled some smoke while cooking.
Please inhale deeply, then exhale slowly for the test.
She closed her eyes and inhaled the scent of blooming flowers.
He laughed so hard that he accidentally inhaled some water.
If you inhale smoke during a fire, it can be very dangerous.
She took a moment to inhale deeply before speaking to the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breathe in - to draw air into the lungs
  • antonym: exhale - to breathe out air from the lungs
  • synonym: inspire - to draw air into the lungs (formal/medical)
