white-shoe

adjective
conservative, elite (esp. US law/business)
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt ˌʃu / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˌʃuː / (BrE)
Of or relating to a company or law firm associated with the wealthy, conservative, and traditionally WASP elite, especially in the US.

Examples

A white-shoe law firm
He landed a job at a white-shoe law firm straight out of Harvard.
The board of directors was dominated by white-shoe businessmen from old families.
The company shed its white-shoe image by hiring a more diverse group of partners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blue-chip - reputable and financially stable (company)
  • synonym: establishment - the dominant group or elite who hold power or authority
