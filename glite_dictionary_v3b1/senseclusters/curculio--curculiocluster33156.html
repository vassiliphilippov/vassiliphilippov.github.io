curculio

noun
fruit-damaging beetle
Pronunciation: / kɜrˈkuːlioʊ / (AmE), / kɜːˈkjuːlɪəʊ / (BrE)
a small beetle (weevil) that damages fruit, nuts, or crops by boring into them

Examples

Apple curculio
The farmer discovered several curculio in the damaged apples after the harvest.
Pecan trees are often threatened by the presence of the curculio each season.
Gardeners use special traps to monitor curculio populations in their orchards.
The farmer discovered several curculios eating the apples in his orchard.
Scientists are studying how curculio infestations affect crop yields each year.
A single curculio can ruin many fruits by laying eggs inside them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beetle - an insect with hard wing cases, belonging to the order Coleoptera
  • synonym: weevil - a small beetle with a long snout, often damaging crops
