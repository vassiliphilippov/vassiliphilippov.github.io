struggle

verb
climb with difficulty or awkwardness
Pronunciation: / ˈstrʌɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstrʌɡl / (BrE)
to move or climb with great difficulty, often in an awkward or unsteady way

Examples

To struggle up a hill
He had to struggle up the steep hillside, grabbing roots and rocks for support.
She struggled through the dense undergrowth, pushing branches out of her way.
The children struggled over the boulders to reach the top of the hill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scramble - to move quickly or awkwardly up or over something, using one's hands and feet
