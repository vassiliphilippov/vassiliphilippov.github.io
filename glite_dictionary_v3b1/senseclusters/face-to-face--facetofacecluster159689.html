face-to-face

adverb
directly facing each other
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
in person; with people physically present and directly facing each other

Examples

Talk face-to-face

Neighbors lived face-to-face in houses across the narrow street.
The two paintings were placed face-to-face on the easels.
After months of video calls, the team finally met face-to-face for a conference.
Many people prefer doing business face-to-face rather than through emails.
Negotiations were easier once the two leaders spoke face-to-face.
