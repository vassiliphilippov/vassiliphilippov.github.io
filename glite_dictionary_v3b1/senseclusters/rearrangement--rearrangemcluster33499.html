rearrangement

noun
change in the arrangement
3.4 0.81/m

the act or process of changing the order or position of something

Examples

A rearrangement of items
A sudden rearrangement of priorities left some projects unfinished.
After the move, a complete rearrangement of the furniture was necessary.
The rearrangement of the schedule allowed everyone to attend the meeting.

  • resequencing
    7.5 0.00/m

  • reshuffling
    4.6 0.25/m

  • reordination
    8.7 0.00/m

  • sequencing
    4.1 0.91/m
    sequencing arranges items into a clear, logical order from start to finish, while rearrangement focuses on changing existing positions.
  • permutation
    7.8 0.31/m
