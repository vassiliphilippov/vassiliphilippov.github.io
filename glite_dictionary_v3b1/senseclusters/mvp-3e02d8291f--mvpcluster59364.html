MVP

noun
minimum viable product (tech)
5.1
Pronunciation: /em.viː.ˈpiː/ /; / /ˈem.viː.piː/ (AmE), /em.viː.ˈpiː/ /; / /ˈem.viː.piː/ (BrE)
(in business and technology) an initial, minimal version of a product that contains only core features, released to early users to test assumptions and gather feedback.

Examples

Launch an MVP app
The startup launched an early MVP to test whether users liked the new scheduling feature.
We built a simple MVP with only the core features to measure customer interest quickly.
After feedback from beta testers, they iterated on the MVP and improved the onboarding flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minimum viable product - the smallest version of a product with enough features to test and learn from users
