wakeman

noun
night watchman
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪkmən / (AmE), / ˈweɪkmən / (BrE)
an old term for a person who kept watch at night in a town to ensure safety

Examples

The wakeman patrols
In medieval towns, the wakeman was responsible for patrolling the streets after dark.
The citizens trusted the wakeman to alert them in case of fire or danger at night.
A bell carried by the wakeman signaled the start of his nightly rounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: watchman - a person who guards or patrols an area, especially at night
