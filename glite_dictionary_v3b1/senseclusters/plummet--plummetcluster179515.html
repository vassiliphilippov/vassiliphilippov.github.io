plummet

noun
metal bob on a plumb line
Pronunciation: / ˈplʌmət / (AmE), / ˈplʌmɪt / (BrE)
A small, heavy metal weight, especially one used at the end of a plumb line to determine vertical alignment.

Examples

A plummet weight
She held the plummet steady until it stopped swaying, confirming the wall's upright position.
Surveyors often use a plummet when establishing true vertical lines on construction sites.
The carpenter attached a plummet to the end of the string to ensure perfect vertical alignment.
