channel

noun
narrow furrow or groove
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.əl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.əl / (BrE)
a long, narrow furrow or groove, especially one cut by natural processes like erosion or made by a tool

Examples

Wooden channel
A tiny channel was carved into the wood to guide the wires neatly.
The needle follows the channel on the surface of the vinyl record as it plays music.
The river cut a deep channel through the soft earth over thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface
  • synonym: trench - a long, narrow ditch
