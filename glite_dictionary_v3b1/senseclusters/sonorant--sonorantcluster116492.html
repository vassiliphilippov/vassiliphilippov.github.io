sonorant

adjective
full, resonant sound
8.4
Pronunciation: / səˈnɔːrənt / (AmE), / səˈnɒrənt / (BrE)
having a full, rich, and resonant sound that is pleasant to hear

Examples

Sonorant voice
The sonorant tones of the cello echoed beautifully in the concert hall.
Her sonorant laughter could be heard across the crowded room.
The actor's sonorant voice made his performance especially memorable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harsh - unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses
  • synonym: mellifluous - pleasantly smooth and musical to hear
  • synonym: resonant - having a deep, clear, and continuing sound
