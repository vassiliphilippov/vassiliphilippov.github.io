impermissibility

noun
state of not being allowed
Pronunciation: / ˌɪm.pɚˌmɪs.əˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˌmɪs.əbɪl.ɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being allowed or permitted; inadmissibility due to being forbidden

Examples

The impermissibility of torture
Many scholars have debated the impermissibility of torture under international law.
The impermissibility of the evidence led to its exclusion from the trial.
The committee discussed the impermissibility of certain business practices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inadmissibility - the quality of being not allowed, especially legally
  • antonym: permissibility - the quality of being permitted or allowed
