herculean

adjective
requiring great strength or effort
Pronunciation: / ˌhɜrˈkjuːliən /; / ˌhɝːkjuˈliːən / (AmE), / hɜːˈkjuːliən /; / ˌhɜːkjuˈliːən / (BrE)
requiring great strength, effort, or courage; extremely difficult or demanding

Examples

A herculean task
Completing the entire project in just one week was a herculean task.
Restoring the old mansion required a herculean amount of effort from the entire family.
She faced the herculean challenge of climbing the mountain alone.
Lifting the massive boulder was a herculean task that took several people.
She tackled her coursework with herculean determination despite the many obstacles.
The team's herculean efforts turned the failing project into a resounding success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arduous - requiring a lot of effort and hard work
  • antonym: easy - not difficult or requiring much effort
  • antonym: ordinary - not unusual or different; normal
  • synonym: superhuman - having or showing exceptional ability or power beyond normal human limits
