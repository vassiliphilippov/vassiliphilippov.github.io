Caucasian race

noun
historical term for light-skinned human group
8.0
Pronunciation: /kɔː.ˈkeɪ.ʒən reɪs/ /; / /kɔː.ˈkeɪ.zɪ.ən reɪs/ (AmE), /kɔː.ˈkeɪ.zɪ.ən reɪs/ /; / /kɔː.ˈkeɪ.ʒən reɪs/ (BrE)
a historical term for a group of people with light skin, originally considered to originate from Europe, North Africa, and parts of Western and South Asia

Examples

The Caucasian race group
Many 19th-century anthropologists classified Europeans as belonging to the Caucasian race.
Modern science questions the validity of the term Caucasian race as a meaningful biological category.
The concept of the Caucasian race has evolved significantly since it was first proposed in the 18th century.
