Solanum wrightii

noun
South American shrub; not a true potato
Pronunciation: / soʊˈlænəm ˈraɪti.aɪ / (AmE), / səˈlaːnəm ˈraɪti.aɪ / (BrE)
A South American shrub or small tree of the nightshade family, widely cultivated in tropical regions; not a true potato.

Examples

Solanum wrightii plant
Solanum wrightii is often grown for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
Botanists have noted that Solanum wrightii can adapt to a variety of soil types.
Unlike the common potato, Solanum wrightii does not produce edible tubers.
