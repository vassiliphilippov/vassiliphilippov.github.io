volumetric analysis

noun
analysis of 3D volumetric data
5.9
Pronunciation: /vɑː.lə.ˈmet.rɪk ə.ˈnæl.ə.sɪs/ /; / /vɑː.lə.ˈmet.rɪk æn.ˈæl.ɪ.sɪs/ /; / /voʊ.lə.ˈmet.rɪk ə.ˈnæl.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /vɒl.jə.ˈmet.rɪk ə.ˈnæl.ə.sɪs/ /; / /vɒl.juː.ˈmet.rɪk ə.ˈnæl.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
Analysis of three-dimensional volumetric (voxel) data — for example MRI or CT scans or simulation outputs — to measure volumes, quantify structures or densities, or support volume rendering and visualization.

Examples

MRI volumetric analysis
Researchers performed volumetric analysis of MRI scans to quantify hippocampal atrophy.
Engineers used volumetric analysis of simulation data to visualize airflow patterns and turbulence zones.
The visualization team applied volumetric analysis for volume rendering of cloud density in the scene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: 3D image segmentation - separating and labelling structures in 3D image data to enable measurement or further analysis
  • synonym: volume rendering - a visualization technique that displays 3D volumetric data by mapping values to color and opacity
