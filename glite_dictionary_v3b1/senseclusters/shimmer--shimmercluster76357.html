shimmer

verb
shine with a soft or wavering light
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪmɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪmə / (BrE)
to shine with a soft, wavering, or flickering light

Examples

To shimmer in sunlight
Heat waves made the distant road shimmer in the afternoon.
Her dress seemed to shimmer as she moved beneath the lights.
The lake began to shimmer under the morning sun.
Moonlight caused the surface of the lake to shimmer gently.
She watched as the heat made the road ahead shimmer in the midday sun.
The silk scarf seemed to shimmer in the evening light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - not shining or reflecting much light
  • degree higher: gleam - to shine with a steady, bright light
  • synonym: glitter - to shine with a bright, shimmering reflected light
