median

adjective
of the middle value in a set
5.3 1.49/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmiËdiÉ™n / (AmE), / ËˆmiËdiÉ™n / (BrE)
relating to or constituting the middle value in a set of ordered values, or the average of the two middle values if there is an even number of values.

Examples

Median value
After arranging the scores in order, the median result was clearly higher than the mean.
The median household income in the city increased last year.
When the data set has an even number of items, the median is the average of the two middle numbers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mode - the value that appears most frequently in a set, often contrasted with median
