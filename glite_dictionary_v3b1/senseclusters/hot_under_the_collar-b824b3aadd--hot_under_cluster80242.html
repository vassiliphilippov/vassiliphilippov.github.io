hot under the collar

adjective
very angry; annoyed
2.8
Pronunciation: / hɑt ˌʌndɚ ðə ˈkɑlər /; / hɑt ˈʌndɚ ðə ˈkɑlɚ / (AmE), / hɒt ˌʌndə ðə ˈkɒlə /; / hɒt ˈʌndə ðə ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
very angry or annoyed, especially because of criticism or provocation

Examples

Hot under the collar with anger
She was hot under the collar when she read the unfair review.
Try not to get hot under the collar over minor mistakes.
Whenever the topic of parking rules comes up, he gets hot under the collar.
He became hot under the collar when the interviewer questioned his honesty.
She was visibly hot under the collar after reading the negative review.
Try not to get hot under the collar if the children make a mess.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angry - feeling or showing strong displeasure or annoyance
  • degree lower: annoyed - slightly angry; irritated
  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling anger or irritation
  • degree higher: furious - extremely angry
