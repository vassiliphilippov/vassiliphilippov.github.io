preponderant

adjective
having greater influence or importance
9.9
Pronunciation: / priˈpɑndərənt / (AmE), / prɪˈpɒndərənt / (BrE)
having greater power, influence, or importance than others; predominant

Examples

The preponderant power
English became the preponderant language in the scientific community by the 20th century.
In the new alliance, one country emerged as the preponderant power.
The preponderant influence of the large corporations shaped government policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominant - having control or prevailing over others
  • antonym: inferior - lower in rank, status, or quality
  • synonym: predominant - having more power or influence than others
