seat

verb
to attach or fix firmly in a base
Pronunciation: / siːt / (AmE), / siːt / (BrE)
to place or attach something firmly in or on a base so that it is supported or fixed

Examples

To seat the lens
Make sure you seat the lens properly on the camera before taking photos.
She carefully seated the glass panel into the metal frame.
The engineer will seat the gasket in its groove to prevent leaks.
