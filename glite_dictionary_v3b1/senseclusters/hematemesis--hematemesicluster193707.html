hematemesis

noun
vomiting of blood
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈtɛməsɪs /; / hiːˌmætəˈmisɪs / (AmE), / ˌhɛməˈtiːməsɪs /; / hiːˌmatɪˈmiːsɪs / (BrE)
The vomiting of blood, often as a symptom of disease or injury, usually resulting from internal bleeding in the stomach or esophagus.

Examples

hematemesis symptom
Chronic liver disease can sometimes lead to hematemesis due to ruptured blood vessels.
Doctors treated her for hematemesis following the severe bleeding.
The patient was rushed to the emergency room after an episode of hematemesis.
Severe peptic ulcers can sometimes cause haematemesis in affected individuals.
The doctor ordered urgent tests due to the presence of haematemesis and abdominal pain.
The patient was admitted to the hospital after experiencing haematemesis during the night.
