Polaroid

noun
plastic film that polarizes light
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊləˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpəʊləˌrɔɪd / (BrE)
a trade-marked plastic film that polarizes light, often used in sunglasses to reduce glare

Examples

Polaroid sunglasses
Many sunglasses use Polaroid lenses to reduce the glare from water surfaces.
Scientists often use Polaroid film in experiments to study polarized light.
The camera filter contains a sheet of Polaroid to help manage reflections.
