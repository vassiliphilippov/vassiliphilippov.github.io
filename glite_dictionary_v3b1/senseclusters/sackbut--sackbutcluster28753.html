sackbut

noun
early form of trombone
Pronunciation: / ˈsækˌbʌt / (AmE), / ˈsak.bʌt / (BrE)
A medieval brass musical instrument similar to the trombone, used especially during the Renaissance and Baroque periods.

Examples

A sackbut instrument
He played the sackbut in the early music ensemble, recreating authentic period sounds.
The museum's collection includes a rare sackbut from the Renaissance era.
Unlike the modern trombone, the sackbut has a narrower bore and less flared bell.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: trombone - a brass instrument with a telescopic slide, derived from the sackbut
