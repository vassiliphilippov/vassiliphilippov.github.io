unit character

noun
genetics: single-gene inherited trait
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈjuː.nɪt ˈkæɹ.ɪk.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪt ˈkær.ɪk.tə / (BrE)
(Genetics) A characteristic inherited on an all-or-none basis, determined by the presence or absence of a single gene.

Examples

A unit character in genetics
Eye color in pea plants often exhibits the properties of a unit character.
In Mendelian genetics, a unit character is typically controlled by a single gene locus.
The presence or absence of dimples is considered a classic example of a unit character in humans.
