gullying

noun
soil erosion by water
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌliɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌliɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which running water erodes soil and creates small channels or gullies

Examples

Gullying after rain
After the storm, extensive gullying was visible across the farmland.
Farmers installed barriers to prevent gullying during heavy rainfall.
Unchecked gullying can lead to significant loss of fertile topsoil.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deposition - the process by which material is laid down or added to a landform
  • synonym: erosion - the gradual wearing away of soil or rock by natural forces
