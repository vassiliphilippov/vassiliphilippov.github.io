distraint

noun
legal seizure of property for debt
8.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstreɪnt/ /; / /də.ˈstreɪnt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstreɪnt/ /; / /də.ˈstreɪnt/ (BrE)
the legal seizure of someone's property in order to obtain payment of money owed, especially rent

Examples

Legal distraint of property
Distraint was used historically by landlords to reclaim unpaid rent from tenants.
The court issued a warrant of distraint on his belongings after he failed to pay the debt.
They feared the threat of distraint if the loan remained unpaid.
