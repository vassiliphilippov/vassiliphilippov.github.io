uncanniness

noun
strange mysterious quality
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkænɪnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈkænɪnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being strange or mysterious, especially in a way that makes people feel uneasy or surprised

Examples

The uncanniness of silence
The uncanniness of the deserted playground made the children hesitate before entering.
She could not explain the uncanniness she felt when she saw the painting move.
There was an uncanniness in his voice that sent chills down my spine.
There was an uncanniness about the abandoned house that made everyone nervous.
The uncanniness of her predictions amazed her friends when they all came true.
He spoke with such uncanniness that it felt as if he could read minds.

  • antonym: normality - the state of being normal or usual
  • synonym: strangeness - the state of being unusual or unfamiliar
