needless

adjective
not necessary; unwarranted
3.0 8.28/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàniÀêdl…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàniÀêdl…ôs / (BrE)
Not necessary; not required or justified.

Examples

A needless argument
Let's avoid any needless arguments and focus on solving the problem.
She suffered a needless injury because the safety rules were ignored.
The manager apologized for the needless delays in processing our application.

Synonyms

  • unnecessary
    1.6 19/m
    unnecessary is not needed and often used in everyday speech, while needless suggests something unjustified or avoidable.
  • unneeded
    2.3 0.78/m
    unneeded is not required and sounds simple and direct, while needless often adds a sense of being unjustified.
  • superfluous
    7.0 2.43/m
    superfluous means extra or more than necessary, while needless focuses on lack of justification or real need.
  • inessential
    5.5 0.02/m
    inessential describes something not essential or important, while needless highlights that it is not required or justified.
  • uncalled-for
    5.1 0.03/m
    uncalled-for feels inappropriate or unwarranted in context, while needless simply says something is not necessary or justified.
  • gratuitous
    7.7 2.09/m
    gratuitous often describes something hurtful or added without reason, while needless is a general label for things not required.
