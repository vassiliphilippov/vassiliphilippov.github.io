popularize

verb
make widely known or accessible
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːpjələˌraɪz /; / ˈpɑːpjəˌlaɪz /; / ˈpɑːpjəˌlɚˌaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊləˌraɪz /; / ˈpɒpjʊləraɪz /; / ˈpɒpjʊləˌaɪz / (BrE)
to make something well known, easily understood, accepted, or liked by many people

Examples

To popularize yoga
Her efforts helped popularize yoga in Western countries.
The documentary series aimed to popularize marine biology among young students.
The scientist worked to popularize complex physics theories for a broader audience.
New technology can sometimes take years to popularize and become part of everyday life.
She worked hard to popularize the sport in rural areas.
The documentary helped popularize the science of astronomy among children.
Many television programs aim to popularise history to engage younger viewers.
She has popularised environmental issues through her books and public talks.
The professor worked hard to popularise complex scientific theories for a wider audience.
