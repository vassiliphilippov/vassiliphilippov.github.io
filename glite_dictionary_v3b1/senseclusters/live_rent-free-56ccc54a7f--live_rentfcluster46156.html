live rent-free

verb
occupy someone's mind effortlessly
Pronunciation: / lɪv rɛnt fri / (AmE), / lɪv rɛnt friː / (BrE)
(of a thought, memory, or person) to occupy someone's mind persistently, especially in an unwelcome or unsolicited way, without any effort or benefit to the person thinking about it.

Examples

To live rent-free in thoughts
Her ex-boyfriend seems to live rent-free in her thoughts, even after the breakup.
That embarrassing mistake continues to live rent-free in my mind years later.
Why do you let their criticism live rent-free in your head?
