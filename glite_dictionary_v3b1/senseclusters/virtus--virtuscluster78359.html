virtus

noun
Roman moral excellence
Pronunciation: / ˈwɜrtuːs / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtʊs / (BrE)
the Roman concept of moral excellence, courage, and virtue, especially as an ideal quality in a person

Examples

Roman virtus ideal
In ancient Rome, virtus was considered essential for leaders and citizens alike.
The historian explained how virtus shaped the values of Roman society.
Many Roman statues were meant to symbolize virtus and inspire the public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vice - immoral or wicked behavior
  • synonym: virtue - moral excellence and righteousness
