outfly

verb
to fly out
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈflaɪ / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈflaɪ / (BrE)
to depart from a place by flying or to fly out from somewhere

Examples

Birds outfly the nest
Every morning, the birds outfly from their nests in search of food.
As the sun rose, hundreds of bats outflew from the cave entrance.
The butterflies have outflown the garden, heading toward the open fields.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrive - to reach a place at the end of a journey
  • synonym: depart - to leave a place
