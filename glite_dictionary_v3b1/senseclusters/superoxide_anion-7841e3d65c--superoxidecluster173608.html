superoxide anion

noun
negatively charged oxygen ion (O2−)
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈɑːksaɪd ˈæniən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərˈɒksaɪd ˈæniən / (BrE)
a negatively charged ion (O2−) formed when an oxygen molecule gains an extra electron; often found in biological systems and involved in oxidative stress

Examples

The superoxide anion molecule
Researchers measured the amount of superoxide anion produced during the chemical reaction.
The enzyme superoxide dismutase helps to neutralize superoxide anion and protect cells from damage.
When cells are stressed, they may generate more superoxide anion as a byproduct.
