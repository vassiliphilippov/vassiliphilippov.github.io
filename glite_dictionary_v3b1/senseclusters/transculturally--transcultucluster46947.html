transculturally

adverb
by cultural exchange
Pronunciation: / trænzˈkʌltʃɚəli / (AmE), / trænzˈkʌltʃərəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves or relates to more than one culture, especially through exchange or mixing

Examples

Evolve transculturally
The music scene evolved transculturally as artists from various backgrounds collaborated.
Many foods we enjoy today have developed transculturally through centuries of trade and migration.
The project was managed transculturally, drawing on ideas from several different countries.
The film was received transculturally, appealing to audiences from many different backgrounds.
Her research examines how ideas spread transculturally in modern societies.
The artist's work is understood transculturally, resonating with people worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interculturally - involving or relating to interaction between cultures
  • antonym: monoculturally - in a way that involves only one culture
