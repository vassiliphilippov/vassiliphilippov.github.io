aneuploidy

noun
abnormal chromosome number
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænjuˈplɔɪdi / (AmE), / ˌanjʊˈplɔɪdi / (BrE)
A condition in which the number of chromosomes in a cell is not an exact multiple of the haploid set, resulting in an abnormal chromosome number.

Examples

Human aneuploidy condition
Down syndrome is a common example of aneuploidy in humans.
Scientists study aneuploidy to understand certain genetic disorders.
The presence of aneuploidy can lead to developmental abnormalities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: euploidy - the state of having a chromosome number that is an exact multiple of the haploid number
  • synonym: polyploidy - the condition of having more than two complete sets of chromosomes
