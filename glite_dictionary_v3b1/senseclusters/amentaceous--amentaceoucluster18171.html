amentaceous

adjective
bearing catkins (botany)
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪmənˈteɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌeɪmənˈteɪʃəs / (BrE)
(of plants) bearing or characterized by aments (catkins).

Examples

amentaceous plants
Many willow trees are amentaceous, producing long, dangling catkins in the spring.
Poplars, birches, and oaks are all commonly known to exhibit amentaceous characteristics.
The botanist identified the shrub as amentaceous due to its distinctive flowering structures.
