Lagrangian

adjective
of Lagrange's methods
Pronunciation: / ləˈɡrændʒiən / (AmE), / ləˈɡrɑːndʒiən / (BrE)
relating to the mathematical methods or functions developed by Lagrange, especially in physics and mechanics.

Examples

Lagrangian method
The Lagrangian approach is often used to analyze the motion of individual particles in a fluid.
In classical mechanics, the Lagrangian formulation provides a powerful alternative to Newton's laws.
Researchers applied a Lagrangian method to optimize the system's performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Eulerian - relating to Euler's methods, especially in fluid dynamics
