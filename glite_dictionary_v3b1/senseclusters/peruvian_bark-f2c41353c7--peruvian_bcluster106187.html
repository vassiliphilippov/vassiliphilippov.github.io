Peruvian bark

noun
bark of cinchona, source of quinine
Pronunciation: / pəˈruːviən bɑrk / (AmE), / pəˈruːviən bɑːk / (BrE)
the dried bark of cinchona trees, used as a source of quinine and quinidine for medicinal purposes

Examples

Peruvian bark medicine
Doctors in the 18th century frequently used Peruvian bark to treat fevers and malaria.
Quinine, extracted from Peruvian bark, became essential in combating malaria.
The healing properties of Peruvian bark were discovered by indigenous peoples in South America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cinchona - genus of trees whose bark yields quinine
  • synonym: quinine - bitter alkaloid used to treat malaria, derived from cinchona bark
