unipolarity

noun
dominance by one power
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪpoʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪpəˈlærəti / (BrE)
a situation in which a single entity, especially a country, holds the majority of power or influence in a system

Examples

Global unipolarity
After the collapse of the Soviet Union, the United States enjoyed a period of unipolarity in global affairs.
Some analysts argue that unipolarity is unstable and leads to new power rivalries.
The rise of new economic powers is challenging the era of unipolarity in international relations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hegemony - leadership or dominance by one state or group
  • antonym: multipolarity - a situation where power is distributed among several entities
