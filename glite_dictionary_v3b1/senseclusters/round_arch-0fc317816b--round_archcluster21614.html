round arch

noun
arch with a continuous curve
Pronunciation: / raʊnd ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / raʊnd ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch with a continuous curved shape, characteristic of Roman architecture.

Examples

A round arch design
Engineers restored the old bridge, preserving its original round arch design.
The round arch is a defining element of Roman architectural style.
The Romanesque cathedral featured a prominent round arch above the main entrance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pointed arch - an arch with a pointed apex, characteristic of Gothic architecture
