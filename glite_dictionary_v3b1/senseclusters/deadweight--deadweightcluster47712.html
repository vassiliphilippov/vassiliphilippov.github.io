deadweight

noun
ship's carrying capacity
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈded.weɪt/ (AmE), /ˈded.weɪt/ (BrE)
the total weight a ship can safely carry, including cargo, fuel, supplies, and crew, often measured as deadweight tonnage

Examples

Deadweight tonnage
The oil tanker has a deadweight of over 100,000 tons when fully loaded.
Shipping companies calculate deadweight to ensure vessels are not overloaded.
The port can handle ships with a maximum deadweight of 80,000 tons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cargo - goods carried by a ship, plane, or vehicle
  • antonym: displacement - the total weight of water a ship displaces when afloat
