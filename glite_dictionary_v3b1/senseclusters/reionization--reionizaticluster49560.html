reionization

noun
cosmic ionization process
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in the early universe when atoms became ionized again after the cosmic dark ages

Examples

Cosmic reionization event
The study of reionization helps scientists understand how the first stars and galaxies formed.
Astronomers use telescopes to observe evidence of reionization in the distant universe.
The period of reionization marked a major transition in the history of the cosmos.
