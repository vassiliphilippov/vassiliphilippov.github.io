stomate

noun
small leaf/stem pore for gas exchange
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈstəʊ.meɪt / (BrE)
a small pore on the surface of a leaf or stem through which gases and water vapor are exchanged

Examples

A stomate on leaf
During hot weather, a plant may close its stomates to reduce water loss.
Plants open their stomate to take in carbon dioxide for photosynthesis.
The microscope revealed several stomates on the underside of the leaf.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stoma - any of the microscopic pores on the surface of leaves or stems, commonly used synonymously with 'stomate'
