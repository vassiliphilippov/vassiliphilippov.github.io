gas-cooled reactor

noun
nuclear reactor using gas coolant
9.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /gæs kuːld riː.ˈæk.tər/ /; / /gæs kuːld rɪ.ˈæk.tər/ (AmE), /gæs kuːld riː.ˈæk.tə/ /; / /gæs kuːld rɪ.ˈæk.tə/ (BrE)
A type of nuclear reactor that uses gas as the coolant to transfer heat from the reactor core.

Examples

A gas-cooled reactor
Britain has been a pioneer in developing gas-cooled reactors for commercial electricity generation.
Engineers chose a gas-cooled reactor for the new plant due to its improved safety characteristics.
The gas-cooled reactor design is known for its ability to operate at higher temperatures than water-cooled alternatives.
