dower

noun
property a woman brings to her marriage
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ.ə / (BrE)
money or property that a woman brings to her husband at the time of marriage, especially as part of an arrangement or contract

Examples

A woman's dower at marriage
Historical novels often mention the importance of a woman's dower in securing a good match.
She was entitled to a dower after her husband's death, as specified in the marriage contract.
The lawyer explained how the dower rights affected the inheritance of the estate.

Related Terms
