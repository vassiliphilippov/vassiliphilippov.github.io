escalator

noun
contract clause for automatic wage or price adjustment
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɛskəleɪtə / (BrE)
a clause in a contract that allows for automatic adjustment of wages, prices, or benefits based on specified conditions such as changes in the cost of living

Examples

Escalator clause
Recent agreements often include an escalator to adjust salaries when the cost of living rises.
The union demanded an escalator in the contract to keep up with inflation.
Without an escalator, employees' real wages could decline during periods of high inflation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: escalator clause - formal term for a contract provision adjusting wages or prices automatically
