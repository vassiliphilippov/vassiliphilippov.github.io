counterplea

noun
plaintiff's reply to a legal plea
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌpli / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌpliː / (BrE)
A plaintiff's reply to a defendant's plea in a legal case.

Examples

A counterplea in court
After receiving the defendant's plea, the court allowed the plaintiff to submit a counterplea.
Her counterplea addressed every argument presented by the opposing counsel.
The lawyer drafted a detailed counterplea in response to the defendant's allegations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plea - a defendant's formal statement of innocence or guilt
