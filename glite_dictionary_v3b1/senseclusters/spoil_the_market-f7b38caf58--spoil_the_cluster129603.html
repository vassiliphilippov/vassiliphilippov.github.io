spoil the market

verb

to harm normal market conditions or lower the credibility or value of a market, often by unfair competition or an oversupply of poor-quality goods

Examples

To spoil the market with goods
Heavy discounting by online retailers can spoil the market for local shops.
If counterfeit products flood the shelves, it may spoil the market for authentic brands.
Price wars among manufacturers often spoil the market and hurt long-term profits.
