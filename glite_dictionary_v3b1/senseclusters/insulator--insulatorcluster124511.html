insulator

noun
material with low electrical or thermal conductivity
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnsəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪnsjʊˌleɪtə / (BrE)
A material that does not easily allow the passage of electricity or heat, such as glass or porcelain.

Examples

A insulator material
Glass is commonly used as an insulator in power lines to prevent electrical shocks.
Rubber is an effective insulator that keeps us safe from live wires.
Thermos flasks use a vacuum as an insulator to keep drinks hot or cold.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conductor - material that allows electricity or heat to pass through easily
