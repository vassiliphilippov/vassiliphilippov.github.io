balcony

noun
upper floor in a theater/auditorium
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbæl.kə.ni /; / ˈbælkəni / (AmE), / ˈbæl.kə.ni /; / ˈbalkəni / (BrE)
a platform that projects from the wall of a building above the ground floor, usually with a railing; also, an upper seating area in a theater or auditorium

Examples

The balcony view
From the balcony, you can see the entire stage and orchestra pit.
She bought tickets for the balcony to get a better view of the performance.
The balcony was filled with excited theatergoers by the time the play began.
He stood on the balcony and waved to the crowd below.
The apartment has a small balcony perfect for growing potted plants.
We enjoyed our morning coffee on the balcony overlooking the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mezzanine - a low-ceilinged story between two main stories of a building, especially the one in a theater above the main floor
  • synonym: terrace - a level paved area next to a building, sometimes similar to a balcony but at ground level
  • synonym: veranda - a roofed platform along the outside of a house, level with the ground floor
