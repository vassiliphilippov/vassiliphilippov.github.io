a group of people forming a legislative, deliberative, administrative, or judicial body

Examples

A legislative chamber
The Supreme Court convened in its chamber to hear the case.
The city council operates as a legislative chamber for local government.
The legislation must pass through both the lower and upper chambers of parliament.

Synonyms

  • house
    7.2 9.49/m
    house is an official assembly in parliament for making laws, while chamber is a broader name for such bodies.
  • legislature
    5.3 30/m
    legislature is the group that makes and changes laws, while chamber is a more general body sometimes used for one part.
  • legislative body
    4.5 0.84/m
    a legislative body is the official authority that makes laws, while a chamber can mean a lawmaking or judicial group.
  • deliberative assembly
    7.8 0.00/m
    a deliberative assembly is a formal meeting where members debate and decide, while a chamber is a standing body with legal authority.
  • congress
    3.2 1.38/m
    congress is often a large formal meeting or national legislature, while chamber usually names one specific governing body within it.
