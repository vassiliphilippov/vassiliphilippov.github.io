prograde

adjective
increasing temperature and pressure
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
in geology, describing changes or processes that occur as rocks are subjected to increasing temperature and pressure during burial

Examples

Prograde metamorphism
The prograde metamorphism of the rock resulted in the formation of new minerals.
Scientists studied the prograde sequence to understand the region's geological history.
During prograde changes, rocks are buried deeper and exposed to higher temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retrograde - describing changes occurring as rocks experience decreasing temperature and pressure
