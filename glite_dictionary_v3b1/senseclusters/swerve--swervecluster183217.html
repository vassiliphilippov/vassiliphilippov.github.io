swerve

verb
change direction suddenly
8.1 2.4
Pronunciation: /swɜːrv/ (AmE), /swɜːv/ (BrE)
to change direction suddenly, especially to avoid hitting something

Examples

To swerve the car
If you swerve suddenly on a wet road, you might lose control of your car.
The cyclist had to swerve to avoid hitting the dog that ran into the road.
The football player managed to swerve past three defenders before scoring a goal.
