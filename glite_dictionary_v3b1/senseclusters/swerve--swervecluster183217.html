swerve

verb
change direction suddenly
2.8 4.17/m
Pronunciation: / sw…ùv / (AmE), / sw…úÀêv / (BrE)
to change direction suddenly, especially to avoid hitting something

Examples

To swerve the car
If you swerve suddenly on a wet road, you might lose control of your car.
The cyclist had to swerve to avoid hitting the dog that ran into the road.
The football player managed to swerve past three defenders before scoring a goal.

Synonyms

  • sheer
    7.8 0.05/m
    sheer is a sudden turn from a course to avoid something, while swerve means a quick change of direction.
  • veer
    4.2 5.27/m
    veer is a sudden or sharp change of direction, while swerve especially suggests moving quickly to avoid a collision.
  • jink
    8.1 0.07/m

  • slew
    7.6 1.18/m
    slew is a sudden sideways slide or loss of control, while swerve implies an intentional quick turn to avoid hitting something.
