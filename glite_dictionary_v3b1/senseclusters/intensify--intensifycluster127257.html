intensify

verb
increase darkness/contrast in a photographic negative
3.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈten.sɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈten.sɪ.faɪ/ (BrE)
(in photography) to make an area of a negative denser or more opaque to produce greater contrast between light and dark.

Examples

To intensify a photo
Some traditional methods can intensify black areas in film to increase contrast.
Special chemicals are used to intensify the negative and produce sharper prints.
The photographer decided to intensify the image to bring out more detail in the shadows.
