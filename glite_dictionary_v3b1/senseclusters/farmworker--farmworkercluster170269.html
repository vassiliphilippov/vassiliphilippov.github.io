farmworker

noun
person working on farm
1.6 1.49/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ërmÀåw…úrk…ôr / (AmE), / Ààf…ëÀêmÀåw…úÀêk…ô / (BrE)
a person who does physical or manual work on a farm, such as planting, harvesting, or caring for animals

Examples

A farmworker plants crops
Many farmworkers start their day before sunrise to tend to the crops.
The rights of farmworkers are often discussed in debates about fair labor practices.
During harvest season, the farm hired extra farmworkers to help with the workload.
The farmworker started planting seeds early in the morning.
Many farmworkers travel from region to region following the harvest seasons.
A farmworker must often work long hours outdoors in all kinds of weather.

Synonyms

  • farmhand
    3.0 0.59/m
    a farmhand does everyday physical work on a farm, like feeding animals or fixing fences, while a farmworker does general manual farm tasks.
  • agricultural laborer
    3.2 0.15/m
    an agricultural laborer works in formal farming jobs, often using tools or machines, while a farmworker refers to any person who does manual farm work.
  • fieldhand
    7.0 0.03/m
    a fieldhand works mainly in the fields planting or harvesting crops, while a farmworker includes people who work with animals or buildings.
  • hired hand
    3.6 0.41/m
    a hired hand is someone paid to do manual labor on a farm or ranch, while a farmworker may be permanent or temporary staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farmhand - a worker employed to do manual labor on a farm
  • synonym: laborer - a person who does physical work, especially outdoors
