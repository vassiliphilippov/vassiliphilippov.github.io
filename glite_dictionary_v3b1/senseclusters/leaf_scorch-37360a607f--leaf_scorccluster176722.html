leaf scorch

noun
disease causing scorched leaves in plants
7.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / lif sk…îrt É / (AmE), / liÀêf sk…îÀêt É / (BrE)
A plant disease characterized by the browning or 'burned' appearance of leaf edges or surfaces, typically caused by environmental stress, pathogens, or poor water conditions.

Examples

Leaf scorch disease
Gardeners recommend deep watering to prevent leaf scorch in newly planted shrubs.
Persistent drought can trigger leaf scorch in sensitive plant species.
The maple trees in our neighborhood are suffering from leaf scorch this summer.

Synonyms

  • scorch
    8.9 0.02/m
    scorch describes general burning damage to plant tissues, while leaf scorch names the specific browning of leaf edges from stress.
