brutalist

adjective
stark and unadorned
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbruː.tə.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈbruː.tə.lɪst/ (BrE)
describing a style (especially in architecture or art) that is stark, raw, and uses bold, unadorned forms, often with exposed concrete

Examples

Brutalist architecture style
The artist's brutalist sculptures were made of raw concrete and steel.
Critics praised the novel's brutalist style for its honesty and lack of embellishment.
Her brutalist approach to design rejected all unnecessary decoration.
The museum's brutalist design features large blocks of exposed concrete.
Many people find brutalist buildings cold, while others appreciate their boldness.
The new library is a striking example of brutalist architecture in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: austere - having a plain and simple style without decoration
  • synonym: modernist - relating to modern architecture with simple forms and minimal ornamentation
  • antonym: ornate - highly decorated or elaborate in style
