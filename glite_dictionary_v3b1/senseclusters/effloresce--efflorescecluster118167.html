effloresce

verb
to blossom or come into flower
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛfləˈrɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛfləˈrɛs / (BrE)
to come into flower; to blossom or develop in a way suggestive of flowering

Examples

Flowers effloresce in spring
After a long winter, the cherry trees finally effloresced in a burst of pink and white.
Her talents truly effloresced once she found a supportive environment.
The city's cultural life effloresces during the annual arts festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blossom - to produce flowers or come into bloom
  • antonym: wither - to decline or shrivel after flowering
