pickerelweed

noun
North American aquatic plant with blue flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪkərəlˌwid / (AmE), / ˈpɪkərə(w)iːd / (BrE)
A North American aquatic plant with spikes of blue flowers, typically found in shallow water of streams and ponds.

Examples

Pickerelweed blue flowers
Ducks often rest among the broad leaves of pickerelweed in freshwater wetlands.
She photographed a stand of pickerelweed beside the quiet pond in June.
The marsh was filled with pickerelweed blooming along the water's edge.
