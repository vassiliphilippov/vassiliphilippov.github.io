cystic vein

noun
vein draining the gallbladder
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪstɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the gallbladder into the portal venous system

Examples

The cystic vein drains
Anatomical variations in the cystic vein may complicate certain gallbladder procedures.
During the surgery, the cystic vein was identified and carefully ligated to prevent bleeding.
The cystic vein plays a crucial role in returning blood from the gallbladder to the portal vein.
