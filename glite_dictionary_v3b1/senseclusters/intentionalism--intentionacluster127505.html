intentionalism

noun
legal interpretation theory
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛnʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the legal theory that laws or constitutions should be interpreted based on the intentions of their original drafters or lawmakers

Examples

Intentionalism in law
The court's decision was influenced by intentionalism, focusing on what the lawmakers meant when writing the statute.
Some legal scholars argue that intentionalism provides a more faithful interpretation of constitutional law.
Debates between intentionalism and textualism often arise in high-profile Supreme Court cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: originalism - the legal theory that interpretation should be based on the original meaning at the time of enactment
  • antonym: textualism - the legal theory that interpretation should focus on the text itself
