congenital afibrinogenemia

noun
rare inherited disorder lacking blood fibrinogen
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl eɪˌfaɪbrɪnoʊdʒəˈniːmiə / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛnɪtəl eɪˌfɪbrɪnəˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
a rare inherited disorder in which the blood lacks fibrinogen, a protein necessary for normal blood clotting

Examples

Congenital afibrinogenemia disorder
Due to congenital afibrinogenemia, clotting factors were absent in his blood tests.
Effective treatment for congenital afibrinogenemia often involves regular infusions of fibrinogen concentrates.
The patient was diagnosed with congenital afibrinogenemia after several episodes of unexplained bleeding.
