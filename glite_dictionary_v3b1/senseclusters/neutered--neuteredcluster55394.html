neutered

adjective
having reproductive organs removed
4.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀànuÀêt…öd / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀêt…ôd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having had its reproductive organs, such as testicles or ovaries, removed so that it cannot reproduce.

Examples

Neutered pet
After the surgery, the cat was officially neutered and could no longer have kittens.
Most veterinarians recommend having your dog neutered for health and behavioral reasons.
The animal shelter only allows adoption of neutered pets to help control the population.

Synonyms

  • altered
    7.2 0.00/m
    altered is the common vet word for animals fixed by surgery to stop breeding, while neutered means having reproductive organs removed.
  • castrated
    9.1 0.00/m
    castrated means the testes or ovaries are surgically removed, while neutered is a general term for animals made unable to reproduce.
  • sterilized
    5.5 0.00/m
    sterilized means made unable to have offspring by medical methods, while neutered usually implies surgical removal of reproductive organs.
