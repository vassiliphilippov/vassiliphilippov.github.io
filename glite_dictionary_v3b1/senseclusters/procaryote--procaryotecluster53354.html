procaryote

noun
unicellular organism without a membrane-bound nucleus
6.7 8.0
Pronunciation: /proʊ.ˈkær.ɪ.oʊt/ /; / /prə.ˈkær.ɪ.oʊt/ (AmE), /prəʊ.ˈkær.ɪ.əʊt/ /; / /prə.ˈkær.ɪ.əʊt/ (BrE)
A single-celled organism whose cells lack a membrane-bound nucleus, such as bacteria and cyanobacteria.

Examples

Single-celled procaryote
Many antibiotics are effective because they target the unique cellular structure of procaryotes.
The scientist studied the genetic material of the procaryote under a microscope.
Unlike a eukaryote, a procaryote does not have a true nucleus.
