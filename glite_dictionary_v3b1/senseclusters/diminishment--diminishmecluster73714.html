diminishment

noun
process of reduction
6.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɪnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of becoming smaller, weaker, or less important

Examples

The diminishment of resources
The diminishment of natural resources is a growing concern for many countries.
He noticed a gradual diminishment in his energy as the week went on.
There was a clear diminishment in the company's profits after the new law was introduced.

Related Terms

  • antonym: increase - the act of becoming larger or greater
  • synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less in amount
