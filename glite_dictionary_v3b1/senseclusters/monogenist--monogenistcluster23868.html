monogenist

noun
believer in single origin
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑdʒənɪst / (AmE), / məˈnɒdʒənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that all humans originate from a single ancestral source or pair

Examples

Human origin monogenist
The monogenist argued that all people are descended from a single pair of ancestors.
In the nineteenth century, debates between monogenists and polygenists were common among scientists.
Her research supports the monogenist view of human origins.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polygenist - a person who believes humans have multiple origins
