Lententide

noun
the period of Lent in the Christian calendar
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛn.tənˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.tən.taɪd / (BrE)
the period of forty weekdays from Ash Wednesday to Holy Saturday, observed by Christians as Lent

Examples

During Lententide period
During Lententide, the church bells were rung in the early mornings.
Many villages once observed Lententide with communal fasting and prayer.
The traditions associated with Lententide have changed greatly over the centuries.
