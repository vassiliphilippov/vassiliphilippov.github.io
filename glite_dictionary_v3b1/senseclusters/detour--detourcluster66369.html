detour

noun
a temporary alternative route
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːˌtʊr / (AmE), / ˈdiː.tʊə / (BrE)
a temporary route taken when the usual road or way is blocked or unavailable, especially due to construction or an accident

Examples

A detour sign
Construction signs indicated a detour, forcing commuters to find an alternate way.
The highway was closed for repairs, so drivers had to take a detour through side streets.
We missed our exit and ended up making a long detour before getting back on track.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bypass - a road passing around a town rather than through it
  • antonym: shortcut - a route shorter than the usual one
