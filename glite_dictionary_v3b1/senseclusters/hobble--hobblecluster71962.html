hobble

noun
an uneven gait caused by injury
5.9 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ‘bÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ’bÉ™l / (BrE)
a manner of walking in which one moves with difficulty, typically due to injury or stiffness in the leg or foot

Examples

A painful hobble
After injuring her ankle during the game, she walked with a painful hobble back to the bench.
His hobble was noticeable after the accident, but he refused to use a cane.
The old dog's hobble grew worse in the cold weather.

Synonyms

  • limp
    3.3 2.32/m
    limp is an uneven, often one-sided walk that favors a sore leg, while hobble is moving slowly and stiffly with general difficulty.
  • hitch
    6.7 0.09/m
    hitch is a small uneven step or short problem in walking, while hobble is moving with lasting difficulty and stiffness.
