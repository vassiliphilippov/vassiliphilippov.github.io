workless

adjective
unemployed; jobless
3.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…úrkl…ôs / (AmE), / Ààw…úÀêkl…ôs / (BrE)
without a job or employment; unemployed

Examples

Workless people
After the factory closed, many residents became workless and struggled to find new jobs.
The government introduced new programs to support workless families in the community.
He felt anxious about being workless for the first time in his adult life.

Synonyms

  • unemployed
    1.7 14/m
    unemployed is the usual formal word for someone without paid work, while workless sounds rare or old-fashioned.
  • jobless
    1.9 1.11/m
    jobless is a short, everyday word for people with no job, while workless is uncommon and sounds awkward.
  • out of work
    1.8 4.85/m
    out of work describes someone who currently has no job in casual talk, while workless is rare or formal.
  • nonworking
    3.4 0.04/m
    nonworking describes someone not engaged in paid work now, while workless refers specifically to being unemployed.
