honeycomb

verb
penetrate or fill every part thoroughly
3.1 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌniˌkoʊm / (AmE), / ˈhʌniˌkəʊm / (BrE)
to fill or make something full of holes or cavities like a honeycomb

Examples

To honeycomb a wall
Corruption has honeycombed the administration at every level.
The revolutionary group honeycombed the army ranks with its secret members.
Worms had honeycombed the old piece of wood, leaving tiny holes everywhere.
The ancient walls were honeycombed with secret passages and tunnels.
The wood was so rotted that it had become completely honeycombed by insects.
Years of mining had honeycombed the hillside, making it unstable.
Over the centuries, erosion honeycombed the cliffs with hundreds of tiny caves.
Rows of tunnels honeycomb the ground beneath the city.
The old wood had been honeycombed by termites, weakening the structure.
