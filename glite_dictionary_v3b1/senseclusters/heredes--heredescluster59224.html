heredes

noun
legal heirs
Pronunciation: / hɛˈriːdiːz / (AmE), / hɛˈriːdiːz / (BrE)
heirs, especially as used in legal or historical contexts to refer to those who inherit property or titles

Examples

The heredes inherit property
The estate was passed on to the heredes after the nobleman's death.
In ancient Roman law, the heredes were responsible for settling the debts of the deceased.
The will clearly named his three children as the sole heredes of his fortune.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heirs - people legally entitled to inherit property or titles
  • antonym: testator - a person who has made a will
