serviceability

noun
ease of maintenance
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜːrvɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌsɜːvɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being easy to maintain, repair, or keep in good working condition

Examples

Easy serviceability
The serviceability of the new printer makes it popular in offices.
Engineers considered the serviceability of the bridge when planning its maintenance schedule.
Good serviceability reduces the long-term costs of owning complex machinery.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: maintainability - the ease with which something can be kept in good condition
