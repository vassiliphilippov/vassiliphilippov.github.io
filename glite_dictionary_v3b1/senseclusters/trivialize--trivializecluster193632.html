trivialize

verb
make seem less important or serious
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪviəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪəlaɪz /; / ˈtrɪviəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something seem less important, serious, or significant than it really is

Examples

To trivialize a problem
His comments seemed to trivialize the impact of climate change on local communities.
The film was criticized for trivializing a very serious topic.
We should not trivialize the challenges faced by students from disadvantaged backgrounds.
It's wrong to trivialise the challenges faced by people with mental illness.
Please don't trivialise my concerns; they are important to me.
Some media reports tend to trivialise serious environmental problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exaggerate - represent something as being larger, greater, or more important than it really is
  • synonym: minimise - reduce something to its smallest possible amount or degree
  • synonym: minimize - reduce the importance or seriousness of something
