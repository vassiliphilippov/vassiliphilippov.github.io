biotite

noun
dark mica mineral in rocks
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ.əˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ.ə.taɪt / (BrE)
a dark brown to black mineral of the mica group, commonly found in igneous and metamorphic rocks

Examples

Sheet of biotite
A thin sheet of biotite split easily when I examined the rock under my microscope.
Geologists identified the black mineral as biotite during their survey of the metamorphic outcrop.
The granite specimen contains visible flakes of biotite interspersed with quartz and feldspar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mica - any group of minerals forming crystalline layers, including biotite
