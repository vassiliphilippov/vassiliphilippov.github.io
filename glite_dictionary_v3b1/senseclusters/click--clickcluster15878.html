click

verb
make a short, sharp sound
Pronunciation: / klɪk / (AmE), / klɪk / (BrE)
to make a short, sharp sound, or to cause something to make this sound (such as with fingers, a pen, teeth, or a latch)

Examples

To click your fingers
The camera will click when the photo is taken.
The old clock in the hallway began to click louder as midnight approached.
You could hear her heels click on the marble floor as she walked through the lobby.
If you listen closely, you'll hear the mother chicken click when she calls her brood.
Some birds click to communicate with each other or warn of danger.
The hen began to click softly as her chicks gathered around her.
Her teeth began to click with cold as the wind picked up.
The nervous tic caused his tongue to click repeatedly against his teeth.
When she was scared, her jaw would involuntarily click a few times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clack - to make a short, sharp noise
  • synonym: snap - to make a sudden, sharp cracking sound
  • synonym: tick - to make a light, regular, repeated sound such as a clock makes
