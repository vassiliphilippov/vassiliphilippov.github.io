sitzkrieg

noun
war with little fighting
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪts.kriːg/ /; / /ˈzɪts.kriːg/ (AmE), /ˈsɪts.kriːg/ /; / /ˈzɪts.kriːg/ (BrE)
a period during a war when there is little or no active fighting between opposing sides

Examples

World War II sitzkrieg
The early months of World War II were known as the sitzkrieg because there was little actual combat.
Historians often refer to the period of waiting and preparation as a sitzkrieg.
During the sitzkrieg, soldiers spent months in their trenches with almost no fighting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blitzkrieg - a fast and intense military attack
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation in which neither side can make progress
