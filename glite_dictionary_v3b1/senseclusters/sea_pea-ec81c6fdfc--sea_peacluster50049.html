sea pea

noun
wild pea plant of seashores
4.6 7.5
Pronunciation: /siː piː/ (AmE), /siː piː/ (BrE)
a wild pea plant (Lathyrus japonicus) found on seashores of the north temperate zone, with tough roots, purple flowers, and used as a sand binder

Examples

The sea pea plant
Purple blooms of the sea pea add color to many northern shorelines during summer.
Researchers praise the sea pea for its ability to prevent soil erosion on sandy beaches.
The sea pea grows in abundance along the windswept dunes of the Atlantic coast.
