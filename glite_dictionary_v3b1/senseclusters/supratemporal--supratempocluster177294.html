supratemporal

noun
skull bone in reptiles
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːprəˈtɛmpərəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːprəˈtɛmpərəl / (BrE)
a bone situated above the temporal region of the skull in certain vertebrates, especially reptiles and early amphibians

Examples

Supratemporal bone
The paleontologist identified the supratemporal bone in the fossilized skull of the ancient reptile.
Damage to the supratemporal can provide clues about the animal's evolutionary history.
In some species, the supratemporal is fused with neighboring bones as the animal matures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parietal - a bone forming the central side and upper back part of the skull
  • antonym: temporal - a bone on the side of the skull near the ear
