high-pressure

adjective
aggressively persuasive (especially in sales)
1.1
Pronunciation: / haɪˈprɛʃər / (AmE), / haɪˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
using aggressive or persistent persuasion, especially in sales or advertising.

Examples

A high-pressure sales pitch
After experiencing a high-pressure pitch, she decided not to buy the product.
He works as a high-pressure recruiter, never taking no for an answer.
The company is known for its high-pressure sales tactics, which many customers find overwhelming.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pushy - excessively assertive or persuasive
  • antonym: soft-sell - a gentle or subtle way of persuasion
