canonical

adjective
conforming to recognized rules or standards
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɑːnɪkəl / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
Accepted as being in accordance with established or orthodox rules or standards.

Examples

Canonical rules
His actions were not canonical and deviated from standard procedure.
The canonical form of the poem is widely accepted by scholars.
This interpretation is considered the most canonical approach in the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orthodox - following traditional or established beliefs or practices
  • antonym: unorthodox - not conforming to established customs or rules
