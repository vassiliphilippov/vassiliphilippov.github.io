mycoplasma

noun
a type of small parasitic bacterium lacking cell walls
7.0 7.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kə.ˈplæz.mə/ (AmE), /maɪ.kə.ˈplæz.mə/ (BrE)
Any of a group of very small parasitic bacteria that lack cell walls and can survive without oxygen, some of which can cause diseases such as pneumonia and urinary tract infection.

Examples

Mycoplasma bacteria
Because mycoplasma lacks a cell wall, it does not respond to penicillin-based antibiotics.
Researchers are developing new antibiotics to target mycoplasma because of its resistance to common treatments.
The doctor confirmed that the infection was caused by mycoplasma rather than a typical bacterium.
