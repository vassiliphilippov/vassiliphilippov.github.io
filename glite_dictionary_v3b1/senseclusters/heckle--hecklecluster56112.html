heckle

verb
interrupt with aggressive comments
5.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhe.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈhe.kəl/ (BrE)
to interrupt or challenge a speaker with aggressive or critical comments, especially from an audience

Examples

To heckle a speaker
Politicians are sometimes heckled by members of the public at rallies.
She was nervous that someone in the crowd might heckle her while she gave her speech.
The comedian tried to ignore the man who continued to heckle him during the performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: applaud - to show approval by clapping or praise
  • synonym: jeer - to make rude and mocking remarks
