case of incarceritis

noun
feeling trapped or bored in a situation
7.5
Pronunciation: / keɪs əv ɪnˌkɑːrsəˈraɪtɪs / (AmE), / keɪs ɒv ɪnˌkɑːsəˈraɪtɪs / (BrE)
a humorous term for the feeling of being stuck, trapped or bored in a situation, such as a long meeting or confinement

Examples

A case of incarceritis
After two hours in traffic, I felt a serious case of incarceritis coming on.
Being stuck at home for days gave him a mild case of incarceritis.
She joked that she had a case of incarceritis during the endless seminar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cabin fever - restlessness or irritability from being confined indoors
