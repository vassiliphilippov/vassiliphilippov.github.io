rub in

verb
apply by massaging or emphasize repeatedly
Pronunciation: / rʌb ɪn / (AmE), / rʌb ɪn / (BrE)
to massage a substance thoroughly into the skin or to repeatedly emphasize something, often to annoy or remind someone

Examples

To rub in lotion
Always rub in lotion after a shower for softer skin.
She didn't need to rub in the fact that she won the award.
You must rub in the ointment until it is fully absorbed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important
  • synonym: emphasize - to give special importance or attention to something
  • synonym: massage - to rub and press someone's body with the hands
