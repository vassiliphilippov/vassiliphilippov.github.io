material

adjective
relating to physical needs or possessions
Pronunciation: / məˈtɪriəl /; / məˈtɪr.i.əl / (AmE), / məˈtɪəriəl /; / məˈtɪə.ri.əl / (BrE)
relating to physical things, matter, or possessions; not spiritual or abstract

Examples

Material goods
People living in poverty often lack basic material resources necessary for survival.
She found little comfort in material possessions after her loss.
The organization aims to support both the spiritual and material development of the community.
His fears seemed very real, almost material to the touch.
It was hard to distinguish between material threats and those that existed only in his mind.
The lawyer argued that the document was material evidence in the case.
Some philosophers believe that only material things truly exist in the universe.
The architect needed material evidence to support her design concepts.
The scientist studied the material properties of the new compound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: physical - relating to the body or material things
  • antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or nonphysical aspect
  • synonym: substantial - having substance, being real or important
