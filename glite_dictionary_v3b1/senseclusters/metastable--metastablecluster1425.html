metastable

adjective
stable but not in the most stable state
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈsteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsteɪbəl / (BrE)
(of a physical system) stable in its current state but liable to change to a more stable state if disturbed.

Examples

A metastable state
A metastable phase can exist under certain conditions until a disturbance triggers a transition.
Supercooled water remains metastable until nucleation causes it to freeze suddenly.
The excited state of the atom is metastable and can persist for a long time before decaying.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established
  • antonym: unstable - prone to change or collapse; not stable
