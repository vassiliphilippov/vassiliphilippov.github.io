valley white oak

noun
a California oak tree species
Pronunciation: / ˈvæli waɪt oʊk / (AmE), / ˈvæli waɪt əʊk / (BrE)
a tall, graceful deciduous oak tree (Quercus lobata) native to California, with leathery leaves and slender, pointed acorns

Examples

The valley white oak tree
Many animals depend on the acorns produced by the valley white oak for food.
Some of the largest valley white oak trees are believed to be hundreds of years old.
The valley white oak is a familiar sight in California's Central Valley woodlands.
