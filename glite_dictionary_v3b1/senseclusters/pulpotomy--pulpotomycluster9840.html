pulpotomy

noun
partial pulp removal
7.9
Pronunciation: /pʌl.ˈpɑː.tə.miː/ /; / /pəl.ˈpɑː.tə.miː/ (AmE), /pʌl.ˈpɒ.tə.miː/ /; / /pəl.ˈpɒ.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a dental procedure in which the upper part of a tooth's pulp is removed to treat infection or decay

Examples

A pulpotomy procedure
The dentist performed a pulpotomy to treat the child's decayed molar.
A pulpotomy can help preserve a tooth that has a deep cavity.
After the pulpotomy, the patient felt relief from tooth pain.
