serious

adjective
requiring effort or concentration; difficult
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəs / (BrE)
requiring considerable thought, effort, or attention; intellectually demanding or substantial

Examples

Serious challenge
Completing the project on time is a serious challenge for the team.
She made a serious effort to solve the complex puzzle.
The committee faced serious questions during the investigation.
His lecture series is intended for students interested in serious academic study.
She prefers watching serious dramas to lighthearted comedies.
The novel is a serious work that explores complex themes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: difficult - not easy to do or understand
  • antonym: light - not serious; entertaining rather than intellectually demanding
  • antonym: trivial - of little value or importance; easy or simple
