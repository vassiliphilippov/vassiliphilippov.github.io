hyphenated

adjective
divided with hyphen
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪfəˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪfənˌeɪtɪd / (BrE)
divided at the end of a line with a hyphen to indicate the word continues on the next line

Examples

Hyphenated at line end
The editor marked the word as hyphenated to fit it neatly at the end of the line.
In printed books, long words are often hyphenated to avoid large gaps in the text.
She noticed that the word was hyphenated between two lines in the article.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broken - divided or separated into parts
  • antonym: unbroken - not divided or separated
