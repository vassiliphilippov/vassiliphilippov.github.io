execute

verb
kill deliberately; official/criminal context
2.1 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈek.sə.kjuːt/ /; / /ˈek.sɪ.kjuːt/ (AmE), /ˈek.sə.kjuːt/ /; / /ˈek.sɪ.kjuːt/ (BrE)
to kill someone as an official punishment, especially by order of law

Examples

To execute a prisoner
After being found guilty of treason, the spy was executed by firing squad.
The mafia leader ordered his men to execute the informer who had betrayed them.
They planned to execute the rival gang member as a warning to others.
A growing number of countries have abolished the right to execute criminals.
The government decided not to execute him due to new evidence.
The prisoner was executed by lethal injection after a lengthy trial.

  • antonym: pardon - to officially forgive and not punish someone
