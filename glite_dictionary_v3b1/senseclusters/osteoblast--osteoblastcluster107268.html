osteoblast

noun
bone-forming cell
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːstiəˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈɒstiəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
a cell that forms new bone by producing the bone matrix

Examples

Osteoblast bone cell
Osteoblasts are responsible for producing the collagen matrix in bone tissue.
A deficiency in osteoblast activity can lead to weakened bones.
During bone growth, osteoblasts work together to form new bone structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: osteoclast - a cell that breaks down bone tissue
