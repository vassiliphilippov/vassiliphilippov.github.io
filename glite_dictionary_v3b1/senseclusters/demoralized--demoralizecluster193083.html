demoralized

adjective
having lost hope or enthusiasm
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɔr.əˌlaɪzd /; / dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒr.ə.laɪzd /; / dɪˈmɒrəlʌɪzd / (BrE)
having lost confidence, hope, or spirit; discouraged or disheartened.

Examples

Feeling demoralized after failure
After weeks of unsuccessful job applications, she felt completely demoralized.
Many workers became demoralized during the long economic downturn.
The team's demoralized attitude was evident after their crushing defeat.
After months of unsuccessful job hunting, she felt demoralised and unsure of what to do next.
Budget cuts have left many teachers feeling demoralised and undervalued.
The demoralised team struggled to find motivation after a series of defeats.
