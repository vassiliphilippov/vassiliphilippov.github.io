miserabilism

noun
French art/literature movement focusing on misery and gloom
5.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪzərəˈbɪlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɪzərəˈbɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
A style or movement in French art and literature, particularly in the mid-20th century, characterized by a focus on themes of misery, gloom, and existential despair.

Examples

miserabilism in art
Some critics argue that miserabilism provided an artistic voice for the existential anxieties of the 1950s.
The novel's tone of unrelenting bleakness aligns it with the principles of miserabilism.
The painter's works were seen as classic examples of miserabilism, reflecting the despair of postwar France.
