surplus

adjective
more than needed; extra; excess
3.4 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀê.pl…ôs / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀê.pl…ôs / (BrE)
more than what is needed, desired, or required; extra or excess

Examples

Surplus goods
After the harvest, there was a surplus of wheat in the market.
She donated the surplus food to a local charity.
The company sold off its surplus inventory at a discount.

Synonyms

  • excess
    4.1 9.96/m
    excess means more than is allowed or needed, while surplus describes extra goods or amounts left over.
  • extra
    1.0 90/m
    extra adds something beyond the usual or expected, while surplus implies an amount left because more was produced or bought.
  • surplus to requirements
    6.1 0.01/m
    surplus to requirements is a phrase meaning no longer needed, often about jobs or items, while surplus means extra in general.
  • spare
    2.1 4.39/m
    spare means kept ready to use as a replacement or extra, while surplus describes unwanted extra amounts.
  • superfluous
    7.0 2.43/m
    superfluous sounds formal and means unneeded or wasteful, while surplus is a neutral word for something extra.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deficit - the amount by which something is too small or less than needed
  • synonym: excess - an amount that is more than necessary, permitted, or desired
