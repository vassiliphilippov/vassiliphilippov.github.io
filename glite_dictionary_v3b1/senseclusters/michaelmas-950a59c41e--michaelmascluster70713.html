Michaelmas

noun
Christian festival and quarter day (Sep 29)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.kəl.məs/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.kəl.məs/ (BrE)
(in England, Wales, and Ireland) A Christian festival observed on September 29th in honor of the archangel Michael; also known as one of the traditional quarter days when rents and other payments fell due.

Examples

Michaelmas festival
During Michaelmas, churches often dedicate special services to the archangel Michael.
Every year, the college holds a Michaelmas feast to mark the beginning of the academic term.
Rents for agricultural land in England were traditionally due at Michaelmas.
