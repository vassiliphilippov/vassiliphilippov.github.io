preterite

noun
past tense in grammar
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛt̬.əˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈpriː.tər.ɪt / (BrE)
(Grammar) A verb tense expressing an action or state that occurred in the past, particularly the simple past tense in some languages.

Examples

The preterite tense
In Spanish, the preterite is used to describe completed actions in the past.
Old English used a distinct form for the preterite tense of verbs.
The preterite differs from the imperfect in how it represents past actions.
