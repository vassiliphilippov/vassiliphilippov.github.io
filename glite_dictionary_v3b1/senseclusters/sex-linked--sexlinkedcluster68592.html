sex-linked

adjective
determined by genes on a sex chromosome
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛks lɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˈseks lɪŋkt / (BrE)
(of characteristics or traits) determined by genes carried on a sex chromosome, especially the X chromosome.

Examples

Sex-linked trait
Color blindness is a sex-linked trait, often passed from mother to son.
Hemophilia is commonly cited as an example of a sex-linked genetic disorder.
Scientists investigate how certain sex-linked conditions affect males and females differently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autosomal - not sex-linked; relating to chromosomes that are not sex chromosomes
