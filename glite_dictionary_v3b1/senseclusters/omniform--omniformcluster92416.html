omniform

adjective
having all forms
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːmnɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈɒmnɪˌfɔːm / (BrE)
having or taking all forms or shapes

Examples

Omniform design
The artist's work is described as omniform due to its varied styles.
The sculpture was praised for its omniform design, blending many shapes into one piece.
Her imagination seemed omniform, able to create stories in every possible genre.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multiform - having many forms or shapes
  • antonym: uniform - always the same; not varying in form or character
