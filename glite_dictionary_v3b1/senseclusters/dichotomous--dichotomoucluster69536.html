dichotomous

adjective
divided into two distinct parts
9.3 1.33/m
Pronunciation: / da…™Ààk…ëÀêt…ômo äs / (AmE), / da…™Ààk…ít…ôm…ôs / (BrE)
Consisting of or involving two sharply defined or mutually exclusive parts or categories.

Examples

Dichotomous choice
Many problems are not as dichotomous as they first appear; real life often involves shades of gray.
The classification system is dichotomous, separating organisms strictly into two groups.
The debate presented a dichotomous view of politics, reducing choices to two starkly opposed options.

Synonyms

  • binary
    5.8 0.56/m
    binary refers to a two-part system or choice, while dichotomous describes things sharply split into two mutually exclusive categories.
  • mutually exclusive
    6.4 2.12/m
    mutually exclusive describes things that cannot happen together, while dichotomous means they form two sharply separate categories.
