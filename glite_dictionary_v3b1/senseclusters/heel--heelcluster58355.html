heel

verb
strike (a golf ball) with the heel of a club
8.7 0.2/m
Pronunciation: / hil / (AmE), / hiÀêl / (BrE)
(in golf) to strike a ball with the heel (the part of the club nearest the shaft) of the club, typically resulting in an off-center shot.

Examples

Heel the ball
During his swing, he managed to heel the club, missing the center of the ball.
If you heel the ball, your shot may lack both power and accuracy.
The amateur golfer accidentally heeled his shot, sending the ball veering right.
