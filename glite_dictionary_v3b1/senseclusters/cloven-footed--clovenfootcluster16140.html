cloven-footed

adjective
having split hooves (two toes)
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkloʊvən ˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌkləʊvən ˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
(of an animal) having hooves that are split into two toes, as in ruminants or swine.

Examples

Cloven-footed animals
Goats are cloven-footed animals commonly seen in mountainous regions.
Pigs, being cloven-footed, leave distinctive tracks in the muddy soil.
The farmer explained that only cloven-footed creatures are allowed to graze in this area.
