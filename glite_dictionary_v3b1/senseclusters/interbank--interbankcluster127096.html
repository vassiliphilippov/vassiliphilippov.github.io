interbank

noun
bank-to-bank market
5.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈbæŋk/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈbæŋk/ (BrE)
the market or system in which banks lend to and borrow money from each other, usually for short-term needs

Examples

Interbank market
The interbank plays a crucial role in maintaining liquidity within the financial system.
Interest rates in the interbank can affect the cost of loans for businesses and consumers.
During the crisis, the interbank became less active as banks grew cautious about lending.

Related Terms

  • synonym: money market - the market for short-term borrowing and lending of funds
  • antonym: retail banking - banking services provided directly to individual customers
