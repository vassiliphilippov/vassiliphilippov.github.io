byword

noun
memorable saying expressing a truth
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌwɜrd / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌwɜːd / (BrE)
A condensed and memorable saying or phrase that embodies an important truth or principle, often accepted widely.

Examples

A famous byword
His name soon became a byword for honesty in the community.
The phrase 'time is money' has become a byword for efficiency in business.
The village is a byword for hospitality among travelers.
