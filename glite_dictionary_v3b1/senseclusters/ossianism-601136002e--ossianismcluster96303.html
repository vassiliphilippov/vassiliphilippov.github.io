Ossianism

noun
imitation of Ossian's epic literary style
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑsiˌænɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪənɪzəm / (BrE)
A literary style or movement influenced by the works attributed to Ossian, characterized by imitation of epic or legendary themes, often presenting recent compositions as if they were ancient.

Examples

Ossianism literary style
During the nineteenth century, Ossianism influenced many writers seeking to evoke a mythical past.
The critics debated whether the novel's embrace of Ossianism was authentic or merely a literary affectation.
The poet's fascination with Ossianism is evident in his numerous ancient-sounding ballads.
