ambagious

adjective
roundabout, wordy (archaic)
8.5 9.9
Pronunciation: /æm.ˈbeɪ.dʒəs/ (AmE), /æm.ˈbeɪ.dʒəs/ (BrE)
using language that is roundabout, indirect, or unnecessarily wordy; circumlocutory.

Examples

An ambagious explanation
Emily's ambagious explanations often left her listeners confused and impatient.
He preferred clear answers to the ambagious responses offered by politicians.
The critic complained that the author's ambagious style distracted from the central point of the essay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circumlocutory - using many words where fewer would do, especially in a deliberate attempt to be vague or evasive
  • antonym: direct - straightforward, without unnecessary elaboration
  • synonym: periphrastic - expressed in a roundabout way
