okra plant

noun
plant cultivated for edible green pods
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊkrə plænt / (AmE), / ˈəʊkrə plɑːnt / (BrE)
A tall, coarse annual plant native to the Old World tropics, widely cultivated for its edible green pods, often used in soups and stews; also known as Abelmoschus esculentus and sometimes placed in the genus Hibiscus.

Examples

Fresh okra plants
She grew several okra plants in her backyard garden to use fresh pods in gumbo.
The okra plant thrives in warm climates with plenty of sunlight and moisture.
The leaves of the okra plant are large and coarse, making it easy to identify.

Related Terms

  • synonym: okra - the edible green seed pod of the okra plant, used as a vegetable
