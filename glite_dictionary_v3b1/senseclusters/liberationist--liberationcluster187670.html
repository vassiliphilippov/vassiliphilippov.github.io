liberationist

noun
supporter of freedom
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or works for the freedom and rights of others, especially from oppression, discrimination, or for a group or country

Examples

A liberationist leader
The liberationist dedicated her life to fighting for civil rights.
Many young people joined the liberationist movement to protest against injustice.
As a proud liberationist, he spoke out against all forms of discrimination.
The liberationist spoke passionately about the need for equal rights in her country.
Many liberationists joined the protest demanding freedom and justice for all citizens.
As a dedicated liberationist, he worked tirelessly to end discrimination in his community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: activist - a person who campaigns for social or political change
  • antonym: oppressor - a person who keeps others in hardship or denies them rights
