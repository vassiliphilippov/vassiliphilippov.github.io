anti-personnel bomb

noun
bomb designed to harm people with fragments
6.9 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.tiː pɜːr.sə.ˈnel bɑːm/ /; / /ˈæn.taɪ pɜːr.sə.ˈnel bɑːm/ /; / /ˈæn.tiː pɜːr.sə.ˈnel bɔːm/ (AmE), /ˈæn.tɪ pɜː.sə.ˈnel bɒm/ /; / /ˈæn.taɪ pɜː.sə.ˈnel bɒm/ (BrE)
A bomb designed to injure or kill people by dispersing small high-velocity fragments, with a small proportion of explosive and most of its weight in casings.

Examples

An anti-personnel bomb explodes
Fragments from the anti-personnel bomb caused injuries over a wide area.
Many international organizations advocate for the ban of the anti-personnel bomb due to its impact on civilians.
The army used an anti-personnel bomb to prevent enemy soldiers from advancing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antipersonnel mine - a mine designed to injure or kill people rather than destroy vehicles
