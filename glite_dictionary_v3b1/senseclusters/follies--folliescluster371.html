follies

noun
a lavish musical revue
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑːliz / (AmE), / ˈfɒliz / (BrE)
a musical revue featuring lavish costumes, elaborate sets, and often chorus lines, popular in the early 20th century

Examples

Broadway follies show
Many early Broadway follies set the standard for later musical revues.
She landed a starring role in one of the city's summer follies.
The Ziegfeld Follies were famous for their spectacular costumes and choreography.
