sociocentric

adjective
group-oriented
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊsiəˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsiəˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
focused on society or social groups rather than the individual; showing strong loyalty to one's own group and putting its interests first

Examples

Sociocentric culture
In a sociocentric culture, people are expected to put the needs of the group before their own.
Her sociocentric perspective made her a strong advocate for community projects.
The school encourages sociocentric values by promoting teamwork and cooperation among students.
The club's sociocentric rules made it difficult for outsiders to join.
A sociocentric culture values the needs of the community above individual desires.
Her sociocentric perspective influenced every decision she made as a leader.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collectivist - emphasizing the group over the individual
  • antonym: egocentric - focused on oneself rather than the group
  • synonym: group-oriented - placing importance on the group over the individual
