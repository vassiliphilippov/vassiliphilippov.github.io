invertebrate

adjective
lacking a backbone or spinal column
6.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɜrtɪbrət / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːtɪbrət / (BrE)
(of an animal) lacking a backbone or spinal column.

Examples

Invertebrate animals
Jellyfish are considered invertebrate species because they have no spinal column.
Many invertebrate animals, such as insects and worms, live in the soil.
The study focused on several invertebrate groups found in marine environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vertebrate - (of an animal) having a backbone or spinal column
