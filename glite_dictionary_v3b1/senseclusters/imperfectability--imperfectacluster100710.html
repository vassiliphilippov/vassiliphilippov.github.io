imperfectability

noun
incapacity for perfection
9.6
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌpɜrfɪktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪmˌpɜːfɪktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being incapable of achieving perfection

Examples

The imperfectability of humans
The imperfectability of human nature is a common theme in philosophy.
Many believe in the imperfectability of political systems, arguing that flaws are inevitable.
He wrote an essay on the imperfectability of scientific knowledge and its limitations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imperfection - the state of being flawed or incomplete
  • antonym: perfectibility - the capability of becoming perfect
