white dwarf

noun
faint, dense star remnant

A faint, extremely dense star that is the remnant of a low- or medium-mass star after it has exhausted most of its nuclear fuel.

Examples

A white dwarf star
A white dwarf no longer produces energy from nuclear fusion but shines with residual heat.
Astronomers discovered a nearby white dwarf using data from the Hubble Space Telescope.
The Sun will eventually become a white dwarf at the end of its life cycle.
