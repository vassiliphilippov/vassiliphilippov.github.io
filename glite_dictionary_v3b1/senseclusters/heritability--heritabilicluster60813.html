heritability

noun
ability to be inherited
7.9
Pronunciation: /hə.rɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /he.rɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /hə.rɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /he.rɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be inherited, especially property, titles, or legal rights

Examples

Heritability of property
The heritability of the estate was confirmed by the court after the owner's death.
Questions about the heritability of noble titles often arise in royal families.
The lawyer discussed the heritability of certain rights with the new property owner.
