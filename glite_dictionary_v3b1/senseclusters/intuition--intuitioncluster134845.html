intuition

noun
instinctive understanding or knowing
1.3 4.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tuː.ˈɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tjuː.ˈɪʃ.ən/ /; / /ɪn.tuː.ˈɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the ability to understand or know something immediately, without the need for conscious reasoning

Examples

Strong intuition
His intuition often leads him to make quick but correct decisions.
She had a strong intuition that something was wrong, even though there were no obvious signs.
Trusting your intuition can sometimes be more effective than analyzing every detail.
Despite all logic, my intuition tells me to take this opportunity.
His sudden intuition warned him that someone was watching.
She trusted her intuition to make the right decision, even without all the facts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instinct - an innate, typically fixed pattern of behavior in animals in response to certain stimuli
  • synonym: premonition - a strong feeling that something is about to happen
  • antonym: reasoning - the action of thinking about something in a logical way
