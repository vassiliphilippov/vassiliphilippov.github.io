straight arch

noun
arch with a straight horizontal top and bottom
4.9
Pronunciation: / streɪt ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / streɪt ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an architectural arch characterized by a straight horizontal top and bottom, formed of mutually supporting wedge-shaped stones or bricks (voussoirs)

Examples

A straight arch design
Restoration of the bridge revealed a rare example of a Roman straight arch.
The ancient builders used a straight arch to span the entrance to the courtyard.
Unlike the curved form, a straight arch requires precise shaping of each stone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arch - a curved structure supporting the weight of material above it
