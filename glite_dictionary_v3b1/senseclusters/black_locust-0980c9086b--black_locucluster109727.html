black locust

noun
thorny tree with white flower clusters
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk ˌloʊkəst / (AmE), / ˈblæk ˌləʊkəst / (BrE)
a large thorny tree (Robinia pseudoacacia) native to eastern and central United States, with compound leaves and hanging clusters of fragrant white flowers; widely grown in temperate regions

Examples

A black locust tree
A row of black locusts lines the road, their white blossoms bright against the green foliage.
Many fence posts in rural areas are made from durable black locust wood.
The black locust is often planted for its attractive, fragrant flowers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Robinia pseudoacacia - the scientific name for black locust
  • antonym: honey locust - a different species of locust tree with less dense wood and thornier branches
