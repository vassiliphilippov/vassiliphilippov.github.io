tabasco pepper

noun
a hot red chili pepper plant used in sauces
4.2 3.9
Pronunciation: /tə.ˈbæs.koʊ ˈpep.ər/ (AmE), /tə.ˈbæs.kəʊ ˈpep.ə/ (BrE)
A variety of chili pepper plant known for its very hot, medium-sized, oblong red fruits, mainly cultivated in the Gulf Coast states for use in hot sauces.

Examples

Tabasco peppers for sauce
Farmers in Louisiana cultivate tabasco peppers for hot sauce production.

The chef used a fresh tabasco pepper to add heat to the salsa.
