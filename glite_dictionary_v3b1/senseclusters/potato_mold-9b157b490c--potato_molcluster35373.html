potato mold

noun
fungal blight affecting potatoes
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪtoʊ moʊld / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ məʊld / (BrE)
A fungal disease causing blight in potato plants, often leading to rot and destruction of the crop.

Examples

Potato mold disease
Experts recommend crop rotation to prevent the spread of potato mold in fields.
Symptoms of potato mold include dark spots and rapid decay of tubers.
The farmer lost a significant portion of his crop to potato mold last season.
