hexamer

noun
six-part molecule
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛksəmər / (AmE), / ˈhɛksəmə / (BrE)
a molecule or structure composed of six subunits or parts, especially in chemistry or biology

Examples

A hexamer molecule
The enzyme assembles into a hexamer to perform its biological function.
Scientists observed that the virus capsid is built from a repeating hexamer structure.
A hexamer can be formed when six identical protein units join together.
