ventilate

verb
express openly or give voice to
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛn.t̬əˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛn.tɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to express or give voice to (an opinion, feeling, or complaint) openly or publicly

Examples

To ventilate an opinion
During the radio show, callers are invited to ventilate their opinions on current events.
He finally ventilated his frustrations after months of silence.
She felt it was important to ventilate her grievances at the meeting.
