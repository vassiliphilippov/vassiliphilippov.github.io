confusion

noun
mixing together with lost distinctions
3.1 1/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàfju í…ôn / (AmE), / k…ônÀàfjuÀê í…ôn / (BrE)
A state or act in which elements are mixed together so that their individual identities are lost and distinctions between them are blurred.

Examples

Confusion of languages
Many languages originated from the confusion of tongues described in biblical stories.
The laboratory experienced a confusion of chemicals, resulting in a new, unidentified substance.
The sudden confusion of ideas made it impossible to tell one concept from another.
