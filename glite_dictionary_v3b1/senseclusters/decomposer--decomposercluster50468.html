decomposer

noun
organism that recycles nutrients
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəmˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəmˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
an organism, such as a bacterium or fungus, that breaks down dead plants and animals and returns nutrients to the environment

Examples

A decomposer mushroom
A decomposer like a mushroom helps break down fallen leaves in the forest.
Without decomposers, dead plants and animals would pile up and nutrients would not return to the soil.
Earthworms are important decomposers in many garden ecosystems.

Bacteria act as decomposers by breaking down organic material in compost heaps.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consumer - an organism that eats other organisms for energy
  • antonym: producer - an organism that makes its own food, usually through photosynthesis
  • synonym: scavenger - an animal that feeds on dead or decaying matter
