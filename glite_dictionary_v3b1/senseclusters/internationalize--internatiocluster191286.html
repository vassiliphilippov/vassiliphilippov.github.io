internationalize

verb
put under international control
4.0 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz /; / ˌɪn.tɚˈnæʃ.ə.nəl.aɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnæʃənaɪz /; / ˌɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.naɪz / (BrE)
to place (something) under the control or regulation of an international body or authority

Examples

To internationalize a region
Attempts to internationalize disputed territories have often met strong resistance from national authorities.
The United Nations sought to internationalize the management of the Suez Canal.
There were proposals to internationalize air traffic regulations after the crisis.
After years of conflict, the city was internationalised to prevent further disputes.
Attempts to internationalise the management of natural resources met with mixed reactions from local authorities.
The government decided to internationalise its nuclear program for greater oversight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalise - to bring (something) under the control of a nation or government
  • antonym: nationalize - to bring under the control of a national government
  • antonym: privatise - to transfer (something) from public or government control to private ownership
