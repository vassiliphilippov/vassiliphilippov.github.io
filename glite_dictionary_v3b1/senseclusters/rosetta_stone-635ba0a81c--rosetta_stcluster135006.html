Rosetta Stone

noun
ancient stone key to deciphering hieroglyphics
7.0
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈze.tə stoʊn/ /; / /roʊ.ˈze.tə stoʊn/ (AmE), /rəʊ.ˈze.tə stəʊn/ /; / /rə.ˈze.tə stəʊn/ (BrE)
an ancient inscribed stone found in 1799, featuring the same text in three scripts (hieroglyphics, Demotic, and Greek), which enabled scholars to decipher Egyptian hieroglyphics

Examples

The ancient Rosetta Stone
Scholars used the Rosetta Stone to unlock the secrets of hieroglyphics.
The British Museum houses the famous Rosetta Stone among its Egyptian artifacts.
The discovery of the Rosetta Stone was a turning point in understanding ancient Egyptian writing.
