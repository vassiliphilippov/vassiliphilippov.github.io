sound effect

noun
artificially created sound for dramatic effect
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊnd ɪˌfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd ɪˌfɛkt / (BrE)
an artificially produced sound used in plays, films, or recordings to imitate real noises and enhance the atmosphere or realism

Examples

To add sound effects
He downloaded several sound effects to add to his podcast.
The movie's sound effect team was nominated for an award.
The play relied on sound effects to create the illusion of a thunderstorm on stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: special effect - an illusion created for films or television by props, camerawork, etc.
