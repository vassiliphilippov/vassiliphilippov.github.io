dibbuk

noun
demon possessing a living body (Jewish folklore)
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdiˌbʊk / (AmE), / ˈdɪbʊk / (BrE)
(in Jewish folklore) a malevolent spirit believed to enter and possess the body of a living person, controlling their actions.

Examples

The dibbuk spirit
In the play, an exorcism is performed to rid the victim of the dibbuk.
She researched cases in which people claimed to be overtaken by a dibbuk.
The legend tells of a dibbuk that possessed the young woman and refused to leave.
