stroma

noun
colorless framework in a chloroplast
7.2 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈstroʊ.mə/ /; / /ˈstrəʊ.mə/ (AmE), /ˈstrəʊ.mə/ /; / /ˈstroʊ.mə/ (BrE)
The colorless, dense, fluid-filled framework inside a chloroplast that supports its structure and contains enzymes and other molecules needed for photosynthesis.

Examples

Chloroplast stroma
During photosynthesis, sugars are produced in the stroma by the Calvin cycle.
Scientists studied how molecules move within the stroma of spinach chloroplasts.
The stroma contains the enzymes essential for the light-independent reactions of photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chloroplast - a plastid containing chlorophyll where photosynthesis takes place
