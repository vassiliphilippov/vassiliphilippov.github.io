overurbanized

adjective
excessively urbanized
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈɜːrbənaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɜːbənaɪzd / (BrE)
having too much urban development or population growth, causing problems like overcrowding, unemployment, or loss of green space

Examples

An overurbanized city
Many developing countries have become overurbanized, resulting in crowded cities and inadequate public services.
The government struggled to address the issues caused by an overurbanized capital city.
Rapid migration from rural areas left the region overurbanized and unable to provide enough jobs.
Many residents feel that their neighborhood has become overurbanized and lacks natural beauty.
The government is trying to address the problems caused by overurbanized areas, such as pollution and congestion.
Some people move to the countryside to escape overurbanized cities and enjoy a quieter life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rural - relating to the countryside rather than the city
  • degree lower: urbanized - having the characteristics of a city or town
