novelistic

adjective
like a novel
6.0
Pronunciation: /nɑː.vəl.ˈɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /nɒv.əl.ˈɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or written in the style of a novel, often including imaginative or fictional elements

Examples

A novelistic style
The director used a novelistic approach to develop the characters in the film.
Her writing style is highly novelistic, with detailed descriptions and complex plots.
The documentary's novelistic structure made it engaging and easy to follow.
The journalist's novelistic approach made the article more engaging but less objective.
Her memoir was praised for its novelistic descriptions and vivid storytelling.
Critics argued that the documentary was too novelistic to be considered true nonfiction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: factual - based on facts rather than imagination
  • synonym: fictional - invented or imagined, not factual
  • synonym: narrative - relating to the telling of stories
