isoelectric

adjective
same electric potential
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (BrE)
having the same electric potential as another point or object; showing no voltage difference

Examples

Isoelectric electrodes
The two electrodes became isoelectric after the circuit was balanced.
In the experiment, the solution reached an isoelectric point where no voltage was detected.
The technician confirmed that both wires were isoelectric before proceeding with the test.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equipotential - having the same electric potential at every point
