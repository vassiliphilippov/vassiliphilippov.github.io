sulfhydryl

noun
–SH chemical group
9.3
Pronunciation: / sʌlˈfaɪdrɪl / (AmE), / sʌlˈfaɪdrɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group consisting of a sulfur atom bonded to a hydrogen atom (–SH), also called a thiol group

Examples

A mutation can alter the sulfhydryl content of a molecule, affecting its function.
The presence of a sulfhydryl group in the molecule affects its chemical reactivity.
Many enzymes rely on sulfhydryl groups for their biological activity.
A strong odor is often a sign that a sulfhydryl compound is present.

Related Terms
