hyperfine

adjective
extremely fine or narrowly split (spectral line)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pɚˈfaɪn / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəˈfaɪn / (BrE)
(of a spectral line or structure) extremely fine or narrowly split into several components, typically due to interactions within atoms or molecules.

Examples

A hyperfine spectral line
Scientists observed a hyperfine splitting in the spectral lines of the element.
The hyperfine structure of hydrogen atoms is important in radio astronomy.
The measurement of hyperfine transitions helps determine precise physical constants.
