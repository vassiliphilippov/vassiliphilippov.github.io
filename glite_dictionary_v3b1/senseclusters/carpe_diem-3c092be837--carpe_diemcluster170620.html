carpe diem

noun
seize the day
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrpeɪ ˈdiːəm / (AmE), / ˌkɑːpeɪ ˈdiːəm / (BrE)
the idea of enjoying the present moment and making the most of today without worrying about the future

Examples

A carpe diem attitude
He decided to carpe diem and booked a spontaneous trip to Italy.
Her philosophy is to carpe diem and not let opportunities pass by.
Whenever he feels hesitant, he reminds himself to carpe diem and take action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: live for the moment - to focus on enjoying the present rather than worrying about the future
  • antonym: procrastinate - to delay or put off doing something
