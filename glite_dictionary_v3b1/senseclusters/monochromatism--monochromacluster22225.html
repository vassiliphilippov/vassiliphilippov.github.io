monochromatism

noun
complete color blindness
6.6 8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəkroʊˈmætɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkrəʊmətɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a condition of complete color blindness in which colors can be distinguished only by their brightness, not by their hue

Examples

Complete monochromatism
Complete monochromatism is rare compared to partial color blindness.
Diagnosing monochromatism requires special tests to assess the ability to distinguish colors.
People with monochromatism perceive the world only in shades of gray.
