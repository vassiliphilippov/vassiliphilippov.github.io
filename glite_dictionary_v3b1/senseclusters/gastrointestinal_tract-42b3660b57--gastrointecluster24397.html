gastrointestinal tract

noun
the digestive tube from mouth to anus
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæstroʊˌɪnˌtɛstəˈnaɪnəl trækt / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˌɪntɛstəˈnaɪnəl trækt / (BrE)
the continuous tube in the body running from the mouth to the anus, through which food is digested and waste is eliminated

Examples

The gastrointestinal tract system
A healthy gastrointestinal tract is crucial for proper digestion and nutrient absorption.
Certain infections can cause inflammation throughout the gastrointestinal tract.
The surgeon explained that the operation would involve part of the gastrointestinal tract.
