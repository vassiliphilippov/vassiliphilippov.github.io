parapet

noun
low protective wall on a roof or balcony
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌpɛt / (AmE), / ˈparəpɪt /; / ˈparəpɛt / (BrE)
a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, balcony, or used as a fortification

Examples

Stone parapet wall
A weathered stone parapet lined the top of the ancient city wall.
The soldiers crouched behind the parapet during the attack.
They leaned over the parapet to admire the view from the balcony.
A low parapet runs along the edge of the bridge for safety.
She admired the view from the stone parapet of the old city wall.
The soldiers crouched behind the parapet as bullets flew overhead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battlement - a parapet at the top of a wall, especially of a fort or castle, with regular gaps for firing arrows or guns
