type AB

noun
blood group with A and B antigens
3.5
Pronunciation: /taɪp ˈeɪ.biː/ (AmE), /taɪp ˈeɪ.biː/ (BrE)
the blood group whose red blood cells have both A and B antigens and whose plasma does not contain anti-A or anti-B antibodies.

Examples

Type AB blood
Her blood was identified as type AB in the emergency room.
People with type AB blood can receive blood from any ABO type.
The doctor explained that type AB is the rarest of the major blood groups.
