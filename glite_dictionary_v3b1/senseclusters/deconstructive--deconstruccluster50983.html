deconstructive

adjective
carefully dismantling
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
involving or relating to the careful dismantling of a building or structure, often to salvage materials rather than demolish

Examples

Deconstructive building work
The team adopted a deconstructive method to preserve valuable materials from the old bridge.
A deconstructive approach can reduce waste by allowing materials to be reused in new projects.
Using deconstructive techniques, workers carefully removed each part of the historic building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demolition - the act of destroying or tearing down a building
  • synonym: dismantling - the process of taking something apart carefully
