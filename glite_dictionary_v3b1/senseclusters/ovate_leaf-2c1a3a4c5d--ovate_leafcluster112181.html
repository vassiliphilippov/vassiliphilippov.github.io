ovate leaf

noun
egg-shaped leaf, broadest at base
7.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.veɪt liːf/ /; / /ˈəʊ.veɪt liːf/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.veɪt liːf/ (BrE)
A leaf that is shaped like an egg, with the broader end at the base.

Examples

An ovate leaf
In botany class, we learned how to distinguish an ovate leaf from a lanceolate one.
Many shrubs in the garden have ovate leaves that help them capture sunlight efficiently.
The magnolia tree is known for its large, leathery, ovate leaves.
