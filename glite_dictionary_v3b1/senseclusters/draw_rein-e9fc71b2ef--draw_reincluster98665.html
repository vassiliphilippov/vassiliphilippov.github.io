draw rein

verb
control or stop (esp. using reins)
7.6
Pronunciation: / drɔ reɪn / (AmE), / drɔː reɪn / (BrE)
to control or direct, especially by using reins; to stop or check (a horse or progress)

Examples

To draw rein slowly
As the carriage approached the village, the coachman drew rein and brought the horses to a halt.
He had to draw rein on his enthusiasm to avoid overwhelming the team.
The riders drew rein at the edge of the forest to rest their horses.
