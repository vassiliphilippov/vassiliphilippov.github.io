reignite

verb
to start again
4.6
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪg.ˈnaɪt/ /; / /rə.ɪg.ˈnaɪt/ (AmE), /riː.ɪg.ˈnaɪt/ /; / /rə.ɪg.ˈnaɪt/ (BrE)
to make something start again after it has stopped, especially a feeling, process, or activity

Examples

To reignite interest
The new policy helped reignite interest in recycling across the city.
After a long break, the band managed to reignite their passion for music.
A spark from the campfire could easily reignite the dry leaves nearby.
The coach's speech helped reignite the team's passion for winning.
After years apart, they managed to reignite their friendship.
The new marketing campaign aims to reignite public interest in the brand.
