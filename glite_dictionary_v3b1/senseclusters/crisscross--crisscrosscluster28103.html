crisscross

verb
to cross back and forth repeatedly
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪsˌkrɔs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪsˌkrɒs / (BrE)
to move or pass over something by crossing back and forth repeatedly, often forming a pattern

Examples

To crisscross the country
During migration, the animals crisscross the plains in search of food.
The river crisscrosses the national park, making it rich in wildlife.
We saw contrails crisscross the sky above the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cross - to go or extend across; pass from one side to the other
