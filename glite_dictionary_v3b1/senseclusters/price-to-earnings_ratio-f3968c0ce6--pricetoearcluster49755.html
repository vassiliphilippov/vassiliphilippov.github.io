price-to-earnings ratio

noun
share price divided by earnings per share
6.6 5.8
Pronunciation: /praɪs tə ˈɜːr.nɪŋz ˈreɪ.ʃoʊ/ (AmE), /praɪs tə ˈɜː.nɪŋz ˈreɪ.ʃəʊ/ (BrE)
A measure of the value of a company's shares, calculated by dividing the current share price by its earnings per share.

Examples

The price-to-earnings ratio
A low price-to-earnings ratio may suggest that a stock is undervalued.
Many investors use the price-to-earnings ratio to compare the value of different companies.
Technology firms tend to have a higher price-to-earnings ratio compared to those in other sectors.
