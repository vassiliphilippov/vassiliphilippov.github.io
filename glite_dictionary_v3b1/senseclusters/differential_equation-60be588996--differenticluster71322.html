differential equation

noun
mathematical equation involving derivatives
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪf.əˈrɛn.ʃəl ɪˈkweɪ.ʒən / (AmE), / ˌdɪf.ərˈɛn.ʃəl ɪˈkweɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
An equation involving one or more derivatives of a function, expressing how the function changes in relation to its variables.

Examples

A differential equation solution
Numerical methods are often used to approximate solutions to complex differential equations.
Solving a differential equation is a fundamental part of many physics problems.
The professor explained how a differential equation models the growth of populations over time.
