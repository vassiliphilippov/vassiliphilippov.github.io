(of a building) having more than one level or floor.

Examples

Multilevel parking garage
Emergency exits are clearly marked on each floor of the multilevel parking garage.
The new shopping center is a multilevel complex with stores on every floor.
They live in a multilevel house with a basement and two upper floors.

Synonyms

  • multileveled
    3.8 0.06/m
    multileveled is an alternative adjective with the same meaning, while multilevel is slightly more usual in modern architectural writing.
  • multitiered
    4.4 0.10/m
    multitiered describes many layers or stacks, while multilevel usually refers to floors or sections of a building.
  • multistory
    3.5 0.11/m
    multistory is the common term in American English for many-floor buildings, while multilevel can mean floors or vertical layers of any structure.
  • multistoried
    3.6 0.07/m
    multistoried is the British-style adjective meaning having many floors, while multilevel is the neutral modern form used generally.
  • tiered
    4.2 0.77/m
    tiered stresses stepped arrangement or order, while multilevel stresses actual separate levels or floors within a structure.
