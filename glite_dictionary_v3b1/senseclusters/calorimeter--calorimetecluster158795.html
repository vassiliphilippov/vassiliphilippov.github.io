calorimeter

noun
instrument for measuring heat
7.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /kæl.ə.ˈrɪ.mɪ.tər/ (AmE), /kæl.ə.ˈrɪ.mɪ.tə/ (BrE)
A scientific instrument used to measure amounts of heat released or absorbed during chemical reactions or physical changes.

Examples

A calorimeter device
A calorimeter can determine the energy content of a sample of food.
Proper calibration of the calorimeter is essential for accurate scientific results.
The researcher used a bomb calorimeter to measure the heat produced in combustion.
