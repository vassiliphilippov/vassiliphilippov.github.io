Thymus vulgaris

noun
common garden herb (thyme)
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪ.məs vʌlˈɡɛr.ɪs / (AmE), / ˈtʌɪ.məs vʌlˈɡɑː.rɪs / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb native to the western Mediterranean, commonly known as thyme, used in cooking and formerly in medicine.

Examples

Thymus vulgaris herb
Drought-tolerant and fragrant, Thymus vulgaris is a popular choice for herb gardens.
Many cooks add fresh Thymus vulgaris to stews and sauces for its distinctive flavor.
Researchers have studied the medicinal properties traditionally attributed to Thymus vulgaris.
