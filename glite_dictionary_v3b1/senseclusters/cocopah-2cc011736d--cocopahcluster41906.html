Cocopah

noun
member of a Native American group near the Colorado River
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊkəˌpɑː / (AmE), / ˈkəʊkəˌpɑː / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people indigenous to the area around the mouth of the Colorado River in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Examples

Cocopah tribe member
He attended a cultural event hosted by the Cocopah tribe.
Several Cocopah elders gathered to share stories about their history.
The Cocopah have maintained many aspects of their traditional culture.
