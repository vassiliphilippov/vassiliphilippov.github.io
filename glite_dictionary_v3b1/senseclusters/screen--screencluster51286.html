screen

verb
test for disease or infection
3.4 6.24/m
Pronunciation: / skrin / (AmE), / skriÀên / (BrE)
to examine, test, or check people or things to find something specific or to decide if they are suitable

Examples

To screen new employees
All new employees are screened for tuberculosis before starting work at the hospital.
Blood donations are routinely screened to prevent the spread of infections.
The doctors will screen the entire community for possible cases of the disease.
All passengers' bags are screened before boarding the airplane.
Health officials will screen travelers for symptoms of infectious diseases at the border.
The company routinely screens job applicants for relevant qualifications.
All candidates are screened before being invited for an interview.
The blood donations must be carefully screened for possible diseases.
We screen the applications to ensure only qualified people are considered.

  • check

  • test

  • examine
    2.5 120/m
