cabin car

noun
crew car on a freight train
Pronunciation: / ˈkæbɪn kɑr / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪn kɑː / (BrE)
a railroad car attached usually at the end of a freight train for the use of the train crew

Examples

The cabin car for crew
After leaving the locomotive, the crew relaxed together in the cabin car until their shift ended.
Old freight trains often had a red cabin car for the crew to rest in during long journeys.
The conductor walked to the cabin car at the rear of the train to check the manifest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caboose - a railroad car on a freight train for crew's use, typically at the rear
