overgrazing

noun
excessive animal grazing
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈɡreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈɡreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
the process in which animals eat too much vegetation in an area, causing damage to the land and environment

Examples

Prolonged overgrazing damage
Prolonged overgrazing can lead to soil erosion and loss of plant life.
Farmers are encouraged to rotate livestock to prevent overgrazing of their pastures.
The effects of overgrazing are visible in the barren patches across the hillside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overstocking - keeping too many animals on a piece of land
  • antonym: underutilization - not using land or resources enough
