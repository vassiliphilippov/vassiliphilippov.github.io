intramedullary

adjective
within bone marrow
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈmɛdʒəˌlɛri / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈmɛdjʊləri / (BrE)
located or occurring within the central part (such as the marrow or medulla) of a bone or organ

Examples

An intramedullary nail
The doctor recommended an intramedullary nail to help the broken bone heal properly.
An intramedullary tumor can affect the function of the spinal cord.
The procedure involved placing an intramedullary rod inside the patient's femur.
The surgeon performed an intramedullary nailing to stabilize the fractured femur.
An intramedullary tumor was discovered during the MRI scan of the patient's spinal cord.
The doctor explained that the lesion was intramedullary, meaning it was inside the central part of the tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extramedullary - located or occurring outside the bone marrow
