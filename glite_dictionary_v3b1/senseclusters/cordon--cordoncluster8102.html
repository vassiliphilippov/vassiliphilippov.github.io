cordon

noun
a line of guards around an area
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrdən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːd(ə)n / (BrE)
a line or circle of police officers, soldiers, or guards placed around an area to prevent people from entering or leaving

Examples

A police cordon
A military cordon was established to prevent anyone from entering the restricted area.
Police set up a cordon around the crime scene to keep onlookers away.
The rescue workers broke through the cordon to reach people trapped inside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrier - a physical structure that blocks or limits movement
  • synonym: perimeter - the outer edge or boundary, especially guarded or protected
