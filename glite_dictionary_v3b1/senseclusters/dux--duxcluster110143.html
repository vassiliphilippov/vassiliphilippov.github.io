dux

noun
ancient leader
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʌks / (AmE), / dʌks / (BrE)
an old term for a leader or commander, especially in ancient Rome

Examples

Roman dux
In ancient Rome, the dux was responsible for leading the army into battle.
The title of dux was given to high-ranking military leaders in the late Roman Empire.
Historians often refer to the dux when discussing Roman military organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commander - a person in charge of a group of soldiers or an organization
  • antonym: follower - a person who supports and is guided by another
