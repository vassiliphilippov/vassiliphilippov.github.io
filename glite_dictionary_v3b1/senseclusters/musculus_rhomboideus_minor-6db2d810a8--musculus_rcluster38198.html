musculus rhomboideus minor

noun
small upper back muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskjələs rɒmˌbɔɪˈdiːəs ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs rɒmˌbɔɪˈdɪəs ˈmɪnə / (BrE)
A small rhomboid-shaped muscle in the upper back that draws the scapula toward the vertebral column and slightly upward.

Examples

The musculus rhomboideus minor muscle
Injury to the musculus rhomboideus minor can cause pain between the shoulder blades.
Physical therapists often target the musculus rhomboideus minor when treating poor posture.
The musculus rhomboideus minor helps stabilize the shoulder blade during arm movements.
