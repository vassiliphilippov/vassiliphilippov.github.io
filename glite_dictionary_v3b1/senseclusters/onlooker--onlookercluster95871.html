Synonyms

    looker-on is a British variant sometimes used in writing, while an onlooker is the common modern form.
  • spectator
    2.6 11/m
    spectator is a person who watches an event as part of an audience, while an onlooker may simply watch without being part of the audience.
  • bystander
    3.4 4/m
    bystander is someone present at an event or incident who does not take part, while an onlooker simply watches without joining.
  • observer
    2.8 40/m
    observer often watches carefully or as an expert in a study, while an onlooker just watches without taking part.
  • watcher
    3.9 6/m
    watcher watches or guards something over time, while an onlooker only watches an event briefly without taking part.
