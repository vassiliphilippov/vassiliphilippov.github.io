transport

verb
move or carry from one place to another
Pronunciation: / trænˈspɔrt /; / trænsˈpɔrt / (AmE), / trænˈspɔːt / (BrE)
to move or carry people or things from one place to another, especially by vehicle or as a business

Examples

To transport goods
He transported the heavy boxes up the stairs by himself.
Special vehicles are used to transport hazardous materials safely across the city.
The company will transport the goods to your doorstep within three days.
Modern technology allows us to transport information instantly across the globe.
The company will transport the goods by truck to the new warehouse.
They transported the injured man to the hospital as quickly as possible.
Ambulances transport patients to the hospital quickly and safely.
The trucks are used to transport goods across the country.
They transported the animals to a wildlife reserve in another state.

  • synonym: deliver - to bring goods to a destination
  • antonym: receive - to be given, presented with, or paid something
  • synonym: ship - to send or transport goods, especially commercially
  • synonym: transfer - to move someone or something from one place to another
  • synonym: transmit - to send something such as a message or signal from one person or place to another
