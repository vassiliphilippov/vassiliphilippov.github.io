infiltrative

adjective
spreading into tissues
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɪltrətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪltrətɪv / (BrE)
tending to spread into or invade surrounding tissues or areas, especially in a medical context

Examples

Infiltrative cancer cells
The doctor noted the infiltrative nature of the cancer during the examination.
Some diseases have an infiltrative pattern, making them harder to treat effectively.
The biopsy revealed infiltrative cells spreading beyond the original tumor site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invasive - tending to spread aggressively into surrounding areas
  • antonym: localized - restricted to a specific area
