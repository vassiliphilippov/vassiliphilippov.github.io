flame-flower

noun
succulent plant with a reddish-orange flower
5.4
Pronunciation: / fleɪm ˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / fleɪm ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A plant native to the southwestern United States, characterized by fleshy roots, upright stems, narrow succulent leaves, and a single reddish-orange flower in each upper leaf axil; its roots were once cooked by Indigenous peoples.

Examples

Desert flame-flower
Its bright flame-flower stands out vividly against the desert landscape.
Native Americans traditionally prepared the roots of the flame-flower as a food source.
The flame-flower blooms in early spring across the arid regions of the Southwest.
