firm

adjective
hard; not easily pressed
5.1
Pronunciation: / fɝːm /; / fɝm / (AmE), / fɜːm / (BrE)
hard or solid to the touch; not soft or easily pressed

Examples

Firm mattress
He prefers a firm handshake, not a limp one.
The mattress felt surprisingly firm even after months of use.
Walk carefully—the snow seems quite firm in the early morning.
She keeps her firm muscles through daily exercise and a balanced diet.
The doctor noted that the patient's abdomen felt firm upon examination.
Years of swimming had given him a firm and healthy physique.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flabby - lacking firmness; loose and soft, especially of muscle or tissue
  • antonym: soft - easy to press or deform; not firm
  • synonym: solid - hard or firm in substance; not hollow or liquid
