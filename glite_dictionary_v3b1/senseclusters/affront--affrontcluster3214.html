affront

verb
openly insult or disrespect
Pronunciation: / əˈfrʌnt / (AmE), / əˈfrʌnt / (BrE)
to insult or treat someone openly with disrespect

Examples

To affront someone openly
He did not mean to affront you with his blunt remarks, but his words sounded harsh.
I was deeply affronted by his careless and dismissive tone during the meeting.
The critic publicly affronted the artist with harsh comments about her work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insult - to speak or act in a way that is rude or offensive to someone
  • synonym: offend - to cause someone to feel hurt, upset, or angry
  • antonym: respect - to show consideration or regard for someone
