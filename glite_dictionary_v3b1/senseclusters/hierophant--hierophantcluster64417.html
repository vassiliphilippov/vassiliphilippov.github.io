hierophant

noun
tarot card of tradition
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪərəˌfænt / (AmE), / ˈhaɪərəˌfænt / (BrE)
a tarot card symbolizing religious authority, tradition, and moral guidance, often representing conformity to established values

Examples

The hierophant tarot card
In her tarot spread, the hierophant appeared, suggesting she should follow traditional advice.
The hierophant card often points to the importance of community and shared beliefs.
When the hierophant is drawn, it can mean seeking guidance from a trusted mentor or institution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high priestess - a tarot card symbolizing intuition and inner wisdom
  • synonym: pope - a tarot card representing spiritual authority and guidance
