front

verb
be oriented towards; face a direction
4.3 1.08/m
Pronunciation: / frÊŒnt / (AmE), / frÊŒnt / (BrE)
(of a building or object) to be oriented towards or face in a particular direction or place.

Examples

Front the street
Several old hotels front the main street in the historic district.
The new office complex fronts onto the city park, making it a desirable location.
Their apartment building fronts the river, providing a stunning view.

Synonyms

  • face
    1.0 43/m
    face means to point or turn toward something for people or buildings, while front describes how an object faces a place.
  • look
    4.4 54/m
    look means that a building or window points or is oriented in a certain direction, while front names the building's facing side.
  • look out on
    3.5 1.03/m
    look out on gives a room a view over a street or garden, while front describes a building's orientation toward a place.
