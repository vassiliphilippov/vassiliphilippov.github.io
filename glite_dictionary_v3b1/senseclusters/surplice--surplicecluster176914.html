surplice

noun
loose white church garment with wide sleeves
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.plɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.plɪs / (BrE)
A loose-fitting, white, knee-length ecclesiastical garment with wide sleeves, worn over other vestments by clergy and choristers.

Examples

A white surplice
After donning his surplice, the priest proceeded to the altar.
The surplice is traditional attire for Anglican clergy during certain ceremonies.
The choirboys all wore a surplice over their robes during the service.
