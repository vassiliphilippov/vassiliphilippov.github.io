aniridia

noun
absence of iris
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.nɪ.ˈrɪ.dɪ.ə/ /; / /æ.nɪ.ˈrɪ.dɪ.ə/ (AmE), /ə.nɪ.ˈrɪ.dɪ.ə/ /; / /æ.nɪ.ˈrɪ.dɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a rare medical condition where the iris (the colored part of the eye) is missing, either from birth or due to injury or disease

Examples

Aniridia eye condition
The doctor diagnosed the patient with aniridia after noticing the absence of an iris in her right eye.
Trauma to the eye can sometimes result in acquired aniridia that requires specialized treatment.
People with aniridia may experience sensitivity to light and vision problems.
Doctors diagnosed the newborn with aniridia after noticing the absence of the colored part of her eyes.
People with aniridia often require special sunglasses to protect their sensitive eyes from bright light.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families affected by aniridia due to its hereditary nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coloboma - a defect in the structure of the eye, often affecting the iris
  • synonym: iridectomy - surgical removal of part of the iris
  • antonym: iris - the colored part of the eye surrounding the pupil
