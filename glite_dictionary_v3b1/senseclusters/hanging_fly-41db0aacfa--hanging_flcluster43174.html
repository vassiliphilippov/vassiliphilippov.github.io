hanging fly

noun
insect of the family Bittacidae
6.1 8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhæŋ.ɪŋ flaɪ/ /; / /ˈhæŋ.gɪŋ flaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhæŋ.ɪŋ flaɪ/ /; / /ˈhæŋ.gɪŋ flaɪ/ (BrE)
Any of several insects of the family Bittacidae, characterized by slender bodies and long legs, often seen hanging from vegetation while waiting for prey.

Examples

A hanging fly insect
In the dusk, a hanging fly dangled motionless beneath a leaf, waiting for prey.
Naturalists observed several hanging flies in the forest, each suspended from a twig by its legs.
The hanging fly uses its long legs to capture and feed on other small insects.
