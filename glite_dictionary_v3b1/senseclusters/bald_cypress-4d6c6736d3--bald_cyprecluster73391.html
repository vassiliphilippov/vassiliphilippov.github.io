bald cypress

noun
durable wood from Taxodium trees
Pronunciation: / bɔld ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / bɔːld ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
The wood of a deciduous coniferous tree of the genus Taxodium, especially native to North America, valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

Examples

Bald cypress wood
Furniture makers often prize bald cypress for its attractive grain.
The dock was constructed from bald cypress, known for its water resistance.
The swamp was filled with ancient bald cypress towering over the water.
