semipolitical

adjective
partly political; somewhat political
3.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /se.mɪ.pə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /se.mɪ.pə.ˈlɪt.ɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
Partly or somewhat political; having political aspects but not entirely political.

Examples

A semipolitical group
Many nonprofit groups maintain a semipolitical stance to avoid direct involvement in partisan debates.
She took on a semipolitical role, influencing policy decisions without holding an official government position.
The organization's activities were considered semipolitical by the media, as they included both advocacy and community events.
