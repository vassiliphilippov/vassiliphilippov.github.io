wind-up

noun
teasing joke
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / ˈwaɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
an action or remark intended to tease, trick, or provoke someone, usually as a joke

Examples

A wind-up joke
He told me I had won the lottery, but it was just a wind-up.
Don't take it seriously; it was only a harmless wind-up among friends.
She didn't realize the story about the talking dog was a wind-up until everyone started laughing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
  • synonym: prank - a playful trick or practical joke
  • synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful way
