harm

noun
damage or injury
1.0 40/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërm / (AmE), / h…ëÀêm / (BrE)
physical or mental injury or damage to a person or thing

Examples

Serious harm
Exposure to chemicals can cause serious harm if proper safety measures are not followed.
He meant no harm by his remark, but she was still offended.
The company took steps to prevent any further harm to the environment.
Doctors were concerned about the harm done by the fall.

You should always use protective equipment to reduce the risk of harm.

Synonyms

  • hurt
    1.0 7.39/m
    hurt is physical pain or emotional suffering often used in everyday speech, while harm is a broader term for injury.
  • injury
    1.4 78/m
    injury is physical damage to a body after an accident or attack, while harm covers physical or mental damage more widely.
  • damage
    1.0 100/m
    damage is loss or harm that makes objects less useful or valuable, while harm also applies to people and feelings.
  • detriment
    6.3 3.18/m
    detriment is a formal word for a negative effect or loss, whereas harm is more direct physical or mental injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs the value, usefulness, or normal function of something
  • synonym: hurt - to cause pain or injury
  • synonym: injury - harm done to a person's body
