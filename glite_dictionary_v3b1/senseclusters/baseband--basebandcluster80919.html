baseband

adjective
original signal frequency
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsbænd / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsbænd / (BrE)
relating to or operating in the original, unmodulated frequency range of a signal before any frequency shifting or modulation

Examples

Baseband signal processing
The audio signal was processed in its baseband form before being transmitted.
A baseband connection is often used for short-distance data transmission in computer networks.
Engineers analyzed the baseband frequencies to identify any unwanted noise in the system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broadband - using a wide range of frequencies for transmitting signals
  • antonym: modulated - having a signal altered in frequency or amplitude
