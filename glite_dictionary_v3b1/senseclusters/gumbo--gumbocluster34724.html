gumbo

noun
Louisiana stew with okra or filé powder
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌmˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
A stew or thick soup originating in Louisiana, made with meat or seafood, vegetables, and thickened with okra or filé powder.

Examples

A bowl of gumbo
Nothing warms me up on a cold day like a bowl of homemade gumbo.
She learned to cook gumbo from her grandmother, who grew up in New Orleans.
The chef prepared a spicy gumbo with shrimp, sausage, and okra for dinner.
