frantically

adverb
in a desperate or uncontrolled way
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfræn.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / ˈfræn.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
in a hurried, desperate, or uncontrolled manner, often due to anxiety or fear

Examples

To call frantically
He called his friend frantically when he realized he was late for the meeting.
She searched frantically for her lost keys before leaving for work.
The doctor worked frantically to save the patient’s life.
