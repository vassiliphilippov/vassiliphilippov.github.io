stump

verb
to puzzle or confound someone
4.7
Pronunciation: / stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
to confuse or puzzle someone so that they are unable to answer or solve something

Examples

A question stumps him
His unexpected response stumped the interviewer for a moment.
Some crossword clues can really stump even experienced solvers.
The complex math question stumped all of the students in the class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baffle - to totally confuse or frustrate someone
  • antonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding
  • synonym: puzzle - to cause confusion in someone's mind
