sine wave

noun
a smooth, repetitive wave (sine curve shape)
Pronunciation: / saɪn weɪv / (AmE), / saɪn weɪv / (BrE)
A smooth, repetitive wave that has the shape of a sine curve, commonly found in mathematics, physics, and engineering.

Examples

A sine wave pattern
In acoustics, pure tones are represented by simple sine waves.
On the oscilloscope, we observed a clean sine wave produced by the generator.
The electrical signal alternates in a perfect sine wave pattern.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cosine wave - a wave with the waveform of a cosine function
