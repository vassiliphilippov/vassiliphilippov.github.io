bollard

noun
short, strong post for security or mooring
7.1 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ël…ôrd / (AmE), / Ààb…íl…ëÀêd / (BrE)
a short, sturdy post used to control or direct road traffic, or to secure mooring lines on a wharf, quay, or ship.

Examples

A bollard post
A strong rope was tied around the bollard to secure the boat at the dock.
Removable bollards allow emergency vehicles to access the pedestrianized street when needed.
The city installed new bollards to separate the bicycle lanes from the car lanes.
