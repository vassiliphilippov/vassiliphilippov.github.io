assassin

noun
member of a secret medieval order of Muslim killers
4.2
Pronunciation: / əˈsæs.ɪn / (AmE), / əˈsas.ɪn / (BrE)
(historical) A member of a secret order of Muslims, founded in the 12th century, who terrorized and killed Christian Crusaders.

Examples

A medieval assassin
During the Crusades, the feared assassins would strike suddenly and disappear without a trace.
Marco Polo wrote accounts of the secretive assassin sect in his travels.
The legend of the assassin order has fascinated historians for centuries.
