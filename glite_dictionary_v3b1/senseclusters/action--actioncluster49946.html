action

noun
firearm mechanism handling cartridges
Pronunciation: / ˈækʃən /; / ˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈakʃən /; / ˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
the mechanism or operating part of a machine, instrument, or firearm that makes it work or move

Examples

Rifle action
Different types of action offer varying rates of fire and reliability in firearms.
He cleaned the action of his rifle thoroughly after the hunting trip.
The revolver's action was smooth and required very little force to cycle.
A faulty action can cause a firearm to misfire or jam.
He prefers the smooth action of his old typewriter over modern keyboards.
The piano technician adjusted the action to make the keys easier to play.
