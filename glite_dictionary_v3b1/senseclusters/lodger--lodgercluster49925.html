lodger

noun
person renting a room in a house
3.4 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêd í…ôr / (AmE), / Ààl…íd í…ô / (BrE)
a person who pays to live in a room in someone else's house, usually sharing living areas with the owner

Examples

A lodger in house
As a lodger, he shared the kitchen and bathroom with the family.
The lodger moved out after finding his own apartment.
They decided to take in a lodger to help with the mortgage.

Synonyms

  • roomer
    6.9 0.09/m
    roomer rents a room often without meals or services, while a lodger pays to live in the owner's house and shares living areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tenant - a person who pays rent for the use of land, a building, or other property
