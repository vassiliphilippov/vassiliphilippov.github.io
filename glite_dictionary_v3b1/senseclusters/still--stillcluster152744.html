still

adverb
nevertheless; in spite of that
Pronunciation: / stɪl / (AmE), / stɪl / (BrE)
nevertheless; in spite of that; used to introduce a statement that contrasts with what has just been said

Examples

Still, he continued
He failed the test. Still, he didn't give up on his studies.
She was tired after work, still she managed to finish all her chores.
The weather was terrible, but still they decided to go for a hike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nevertheless - in spite of that; anyway
  • synonym: yet - used to introduce a contrasting idea
