sloop of war

noun
warship with cannons on one deck
Pronunciation: / sluːp əv wɔr / (AmE), / sluːp əv wɔː / (BrE)
A warship, either sailing or steam-powered, equipped with cannons arranged on a single deck.

Examples

A sloop of war ship
A famous sloop of war participated in the battle, earning several commendations.
During the 18th century, the sloop of war was valued for its speed and maneuverability.
The navy dispatched a sloop of war to patrol the coastline.
