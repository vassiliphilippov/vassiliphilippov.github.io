Brunhild

proper noun
legendary Valkyrie and queen in Norse myth
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbrʊn.hɪld/ /; / /ˈbruːn.hɪld/ (AmE), /ˈbrʊn.hɪld/ /; / /ˈbruːn.hɪld/ (BrE)
In Norse and Germanic legend, a Valkyrie and queen famed for her great strength and tragic love, who plays a central role in the Nibelungenlied and other sagas.

Examples

Brunhild in legend
As a figure of strength and tragedy, Brunhild remains a powerful symbol in Norse mythology.
In the Nibelungenlied, Brunhild is deceived by Siegfried and ultimately takes her own life.
The legend of Brunhild has inspired countless retellings in literature and opera.
