thermosetting resin

noun
synthetic material that hardens permanently when heated
8.5 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊˈsɛtɪŋ ˈrɛzən / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˈsɛtɪŋ ˈrɛzɪn / (BrE)
a synthetic material that irreversibly hardens when heated and cannot be remolded or softened by reheating

Examples

Thermosetting resin hardens permanently
Epoxy is a common type of thermosetting resin used in adhesives and coatings.
Once cured, a thermosetting resin cannot be melted and reshaped.
The durability of the product relies on the properties of the thermosetting resin chosen for the molding process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polymer - a substance composed of large molecules formed from many similar units
  • antonym: thermoplastic - a material that can be molded repeatedly when heated
