esophagostomy

noun
surgical esophagus opening
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.sə.fə.ˈgɑːs.tə.miː/ (AmE), /ɪ.sɒ.fə.ˈgɒs.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical opening made in the esophagus to allow for feeding, drainage, or treatment

Examples

An esophagostomy surgery
After the accident, the doctor performed an esophagostomy to help the patient receive nutrition.
The nurse cleaned the esophagostomy site daily to prevent infection.
A feeding tube was inserted through the esophagostomy to provide liquid meals.
The patient received an esophagostomy after severe injury to the throat made swallowing impossible.
Doctors may recommend an esophagostomy for long-term nutritional support in certain medical conditions.
After the esophagostomy, the medical team carefully monitored the site for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tracheostomy - a surgical opening into the trachea to aid breathing
