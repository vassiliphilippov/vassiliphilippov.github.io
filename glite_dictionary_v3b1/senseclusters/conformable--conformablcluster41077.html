conformable

adjective
willing to comply; compliant
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɔːrməbəl /; / kənˈfɔrməbəl / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːməbəl / (BrE)
willing or inclined to comply with others' wishes, rules, or standards; compliant

Examples

Conformable to orders
In Shakespeare's play, the queen is described as conformable to her husband's wishes.
She was always conformable to the expectations of her superiors.
The new intern proved conformable, quickly adapting to established procedures.
She is quite conformable when it comes to following workplace policies and guidelines.
The team was made up of highly conformable individuals who adapted easily to new procedures.
Unlike his rebellious friends, George was naturally conformable and respected his teachers' requests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amenable - willing to be led or controlled
  • synonym: compliant - willing to obey or conform to rules or others' wishes
  • antonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or actions
  • antonym: recalcitrant - resisting authority or control
