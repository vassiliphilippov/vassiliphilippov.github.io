square

adjective
rigidly conventional or old-fashioned (informal)
Pronunciation: / skwɛr / (AmE), / skweə / (BrE)
(informal) Rigidly conventional, old-fashioned, or unwilling to try new things.

Examples

Square attitude
He used to be a real square, but now he enjoys going to parties and trying new things.
Her square attitude made her uncomfortable with the new trends at work.
They called him square because he always followed the rules and never did anything risky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conventional - conforming to accepted standards (similar meaning)
  • antonym: hip - fashionable or trendy (informal, antonym of this sense)
