textually

adverb
in terms of text
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛkstʃuəli / (AmE), / ˈtɛkstjʊəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the actual words or text of something

Examples

Textually interpret a law
The contract must be interpreted textually to avoid misunderstandings.
She analyzed the poem textually, focusing only on the words themselves.
The translation is textually faithful to the original document.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contextually - in a way that relates to the context
  • synonym: literally - in a literal or exact way
