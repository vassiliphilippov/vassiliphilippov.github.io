upstream

adverb
toward the source of a river or stream
3.5 3.11/m
Pronunciation: / Àå åpÀàstriÀêm / (AmE), / Àå åpÀàstriÀêm / (BrE)
in the direction toward the source of a river or stream, or against the flow of a current

Examples

Swim upstream
Pollution can be traced to factories located upstream from the city.
The canoeists paddled upstream against the strong current.
The salmon swim upstream each year to spawn.

Synonyms

  • upriver
    4.1 1.06/m
    upriver means moving toward a river's source, often used for river travel, while upstream covers rivers and any flowing current.
  • upcurrent
    7.2 0.01/m
    upcurrent describes position toward a stream source relative to a point, while upstream is the general term for that direction.
  • upline
    7.9 0.01/m
    upline often refers to an earlier part of a system or chain, while upstream mainly means toward a river's or flow's source.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downstream - in the direction away from the source of a river or stream; with the current
