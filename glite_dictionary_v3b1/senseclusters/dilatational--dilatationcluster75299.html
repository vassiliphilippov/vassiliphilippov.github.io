dilatational

adjective
of geometric dilation
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪləˈteɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪləˈteɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving geometric dilation, where distances from a fixed center are multiplied by a constant factor.

Examples

Dilatational transformation
The mathematician explained the dilatational properties of the transformation to the students.
A dilatational change in the figure increased all distances from the center by a factor of three.
Engineers studied the dilatational effects on the material when subjected to uniform expansion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contractive - causing or relating to contraction or reduction in size
  • synonym: dilative - causing or relating to expansion or dilation
