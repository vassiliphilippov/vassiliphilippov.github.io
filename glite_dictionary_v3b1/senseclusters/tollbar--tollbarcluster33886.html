tollbar

noun
barrier at a toll road or bridge
5.4 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊlˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈtəʊlˌbɑː / (BrE)
a gate or bar placed across a toll road or bridge, which is lifted once the toll has been paid

Examples

The tollbar lifts
As the tollbar slowly lifted, a line of cars waited to cross the bridge.
In Victorian times, every major road seemed to have a tollbar or tollhouse.
The driver stopped at the tollbar and paid the attendant before proceeding.
