subduct

verb
to move under plate
8.6
Pronunciation: /səb.ˈdʌkt/ /; / /sʌb.ˈdʌkt/ (AmE), /səb.ˈdʌkt/ /; / /sʌb.ˈdʌkt/ (BrE)
to move beneath another tectonic plate, usually causing earthquakes or volcanic activity

Examples

To subduct a plate
When two plates collide, one may subduct beneath the other, creating a deep ocean trench.
Geologists study areas where oceanic plates subduct to understand the causes of earthquakes.
The Pacific Plate is known to subduct under the North American Plate along the coast.
At convergent boundaries, one tectonic plate may subduct beneath another, causing earthquakes.
Scientists study how oceanic plates subduct to understand volcanic activity.
When plates subduct, they can create deep ocean trenches along the boundary.
