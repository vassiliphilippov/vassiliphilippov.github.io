incline

verb
tend or be likely to do something
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈklaɪn/ /; / /ˈɪn.klaɪn/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈklaɪn/ /; / /ˈɪn.klaɪn/ (BrE)
to have or show a tendency or willingness to do something, or to cause someone to feel this way

Examples

Incline toward helping
Even as a child, he would incline toward helping others in need.
Given his background, he naturally inclined to support the proposal.
She inclines to pessimism when facing new challenges.

When faced with conflicting evidence, he inclined to trust his instincts.

His upbringing inclined him toward generosity.
The evidence inclines me to believe that the theory is sound.
