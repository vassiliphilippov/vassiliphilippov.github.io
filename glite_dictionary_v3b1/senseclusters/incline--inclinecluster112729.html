Synonyms

  • tend
    1.9 110/m
    tend means to behave in a regular way or be likely to do something, while incline suggests a personal willingness or leaning.
  • induce
    4.9 6/m
    induce means to persuade or cause someone to act, while incline often shows a milder tendency or personal willingness.
  • lean
    3.7 11/m
    lean means to lean toward an opinion or person, while incline focuses on a tendency or readiness to do something.
  • dispose
    6.6 2/m
    dispose means to make someone ready or willing, while incline also shows a tendency but is more common in everyday speech.
  • run
    5.9 17/m
    run describes a recurring quality or pattern over time, while incline describes a person's willingness or a tendency to act.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disincline - to make or become unwilling or reluctant

  • synonym: lean - to have a tendency toward (a particular opinion, action, etc.)
  • synonym: lean toward - to be likely to prefer or support a particular idea or opinion
  • synonym: persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something by reasoning or urging
  • antonym: resist - to oppose or have a tendency against doing something
  • synonym: tend - to regularly or frequently behave in a particular way; be likely
