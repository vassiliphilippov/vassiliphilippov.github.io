politburo

noun
small, secretive decision-making group
9.1 0.10/m

(informal, figurative) a small, often secretive group of senior people within an organization who make major policy decisions, especially without wider consultation.

Examples

Company politburo
The company's politburo met late at night to decide the controversial restructuring plan.
Journalists accused the NGO's politburo of making secretive funding decisions without consulting staff.
Despite protests, the university's small politburo pushed through changes to the admissions policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cabal - a secret or private group organized to influence decisions
  • synonym: inner circle - a small, influential group of people close to an organization's leadership
