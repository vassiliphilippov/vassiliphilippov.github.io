artillery fire

noun
military gunfire from artillery weapons

the act of firing guns, cannons, or other heavy weapons, especially as part of a military attack

Examples

Heavy artillery fire

The infantry advanced under heavy artillery fire from the enemy positions.
They waited for the artillery fire to subside before crossing the open field.

Synonyms

  • shelling
    8.3 2.32/m
    shelling sends artillery shells over an area to pound it, while artillery fire is the general act of firing heavy guns in attack.
  • cannon fire
    4.9 0.17/m
    cannon fire means shots fired from cannons specifically, while artillery fire covers firing from many heavy weapons in a military attack.
  • barrage
    7.6 1.15/m
    barrage is a heavy, continuous curtain of artillery designed to cover an area, while artillery fire is any use of heavy guns.
  • bombardment
    7.0 3.20/m
    bombardment is a long, intense attack often using bombs or artillery, while artillery fire refers specifically to firing heavy guns.
