mudsill

noun
base support beam
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌsɪl / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌsɪl / (BrE)
a horizontal timber or beam laid at the base of a structure to support the frame and distribute its load

Examples

Wooden mudsill beam
The carpenter replaced the rotting mudsill before building the new wall.
Inspectors found that the mudsill was damaged by moisture and needed immediate repair.
A sturdy mudsill is essential for supporting the weight of the entire structure.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: foundation - the lowest load-bearing part of a building
  • synonym: sill plate - a horizontal piece of wood at the bottom of a wall or building frame
