unmitigated

adjective
absolute; not lessened in severity
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈmɪt̬ɪɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈmɪtɪɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
not lessened or made less severe; absolute or total, often used to emphasize the severity or extent of something unpleasant.

Examples

Unmitigated disaster
Her response was one of unmitigated delight.
I faced unmitigated boredom during the long lecture.
The project was an unmitigated disaster from start to finish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mitigated - lessened in severity
