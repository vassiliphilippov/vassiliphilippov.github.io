briarwood

noun
wood from the root of a briar plant
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪər.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈbraɪə.wʊd / (BrE)
Wood obtained from the root of the Mediterranean briar (Erica arborea), prized for making tobacco pipes due to its hardness and heat resistance.

Examples

Briarwood pipe wood
Many collectors prefer pipes made of briarwood for their durability.
The artisan carved the tobacco pipe from high-quality briarwood.
The shop specializes in handcrafted briarwood products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: briar - a thorny shrub, especially Erica arborea, source of briarwood
