French marigold

noun
bushy garden plant with orange or yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ ˈmærɪˌɡoʊld / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈmærɪɡəʊld / (BrE)
A bushy annual plant with strong-scented orange or yellow flower heads marked with red, native to Mexico and Guatemala and commonly cultivated in gardens.

Examples

French marigold flower
French marigold adds bright color to flower borders throughout the summer.
Gardeners often plant French marigold to help repel pests naturally.
The beds were filled with rows of blooming French marigold and other annuals.
