superimposed

adjective
placed or layered on top of something else
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərɪmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərɪmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
placed or layered on top of something else, so that both things are visible or combined

Examples

Superimposed images
In geology, different layers of rock can be superimposed over millions of years.
Several images were superimposed to create the final photograph.
The architect’s drawing showed new structures superimposed on the existing site plan.
A transparent map was superimposed onto the satellite image for clarity.
Special effects made it appear as if ghostly figures were superimposed over the scene.
The old photograph was superimposed on the modern cityscape to show the changes over time.
