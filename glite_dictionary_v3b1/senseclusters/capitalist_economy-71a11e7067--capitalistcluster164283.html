capitalist economy

noun
economic system with private ownership
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəlɪst ɪˈkɑːnəmi / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəlɪst ɪˈkɒnəmi / (BrE)
an economic system in which industry, trade, and the means of production are privately owned and operated for profit

Examples

A capitalist economy
A capitalist economy encourages competition and innovation among private businesses.
Critics argue that a capitalist economy can lead to greater inequality between rich and poor.
Many countries transitioned to a capitalist economy in the late 20th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: socialist economy - an economic system in which the means of production are owned and controlled collectively or by the government
