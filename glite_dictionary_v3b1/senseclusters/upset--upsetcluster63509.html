upset

verb
make lose composure; disturb emotionally
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈsɛt / (AmE), / ʌpˈsɛt / (BrE)
to make someone feel unhappy, worried, or emotionally disturbed

Examples

To upset a friend
It was never my intention to upset you with my comments.
The bad news really upset him, and he couldn't concentrate.
Try not to upset her by mentioning the accident again.
He was clearly upset by the outcome of the meeting.
I didn't mean to upset you with my remarks.
The news of her dismissal really upset her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful or tranquil; emotionally steady
  • synonym: distress - to cause someone anxiety, sorrow, or pain; upset emotionally
  • synonym: disturb - to interfere with the normal arrangement or function; to make someone anxious
