indiction

noun
15-year Roman chronological cycle
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
a period of fifteen years used as a chronological unit for dating and taxation in ancient Rome, later adopted by some medieval kingdoms

Examples

The indiction period
Ancient Roman tax records were often calculated based on the indiction year.
The indiction cycle was significant for both administrative and legal purposes.
The dating of many medieval documents relies on the system of indiction.
