biocentric

adjective
life-centered view
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈsɛntrɪk /; / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈsɛn.trɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈsɛntrɪk /; / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈsɛn.trɪk / (BrE)
considering all living things as central or most important, not just humans

Examples

A biocentric view
The environmental group promotes a biocentric approach to conservation, protecting all species equally.
Her biocentric philosophy led her to advocate for animal rights and habitat preservation.
A biocentric perspective challenges the idea that humans are separate from or above nature.
The new environmental policy takes a biocentric perspective, valuing all forms of life equally.
A biocentric approach in urban planning can help protect local wildlife and natural habitats.
Some scientists argue that a biocentric view leads to better conservation outcomes than a human-centered one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anthropocentric - considering humans as the most important beings
  • synonym: ecocentric - valuing the ecosystem as a whole as central
