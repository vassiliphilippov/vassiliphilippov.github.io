revegetate

verb
to regrow plants
6.8
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈvedʒ.ə.teɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈvedʒ.ə.teɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈvedʒ.ə.teɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈvedʒ.ə.teɪt/ (BrE)
to make land covered with plants again, either naturally or by planting new vegetation

Examples

To revegetate land
After the wildfire, the hillside began to revegetate over the next few years.
Conservationists hope the area will revegetate naturally without the need for planting.
Heavy rains helped the barren land to revegetate much faster than expected.
After the wildfire, volunteers helped revegetate the damaged hillside with native grasses.
The government launched a project to revegetate areas affected by mining activities.
It can take years to fully revegetate land that has been cleared for construction.
After the wildfire, the community worked together to revegetate the damaged hillside.
Government programs aim to revegetate areas affected by mining and erosion.
It can take years to successfully revegetate land that has been heavily grazed by livestock.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deforest - to clear an area of trees or plants
  • synonym: reforest - to plant trees in an area where the forest has been cut down
  • synonym: regrow - to grow again after being lost or destroyed
