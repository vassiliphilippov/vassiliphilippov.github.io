prescreen

verb
to check in advance
4.1 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpriÀêÀàskriÀên / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀêÀàskriÀên / (BrE)
to check or evaluate someone or something before a main process or event

Examples

To prescreen applicants
All candidates are prescreened before being invited for a final interview.
The volunteers were prescreened to ensure they met the health requirements.
We prescreen movies to make sure they are appropriate for children.

Synonyms

  • precheck
    4.9 0.01/m
    precheck is a general advance review of documents or steps, while prescreen focuses on quickly evaluating people or items before a main selection.
  • pretest
    3.9 0.41/m
    pretest is a small trial to find problems in a system or course, while prescreen checks suitability before the main process.
