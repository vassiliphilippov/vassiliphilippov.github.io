prescreen

verb
to check in advance
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈskriːn / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈskriːn / (BrE)
to check or evaluate someone or something before a main process or event

Examples

To prescreen applicants
All candidates are prescreened before being invited for a final interview.
The volunteers were prescreened to ensure they met the health requirements.
We prescreen movies to make sure they are appropriate for children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: review - to examine or assess something
  • synonym: screen - to examine or test for suitability
