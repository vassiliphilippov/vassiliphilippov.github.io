recommission

verb
to assign again
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
to put something (such as a ship or facility) back into service or use after it has been inactive

Examples

To recommission a ship
The navy decided to recommission the old ship after extensive repairs.
After several years, the museum will recommission the artist for a new installation.
The government plans to recommission the power plant to meet rising energy demands.
After extensive repairs, the navy decided to recommission the historic submarine.
The factory was recommissioned to produce medical supplies during the emergency.
It took several months to recommission the old power plant and ensure it met safety standards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decommission - to officially remove from service or use
  • synonym: reactivate - to make something active again
