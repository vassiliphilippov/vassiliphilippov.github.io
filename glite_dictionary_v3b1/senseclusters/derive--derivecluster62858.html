derive

verb
to come from a source, esp. ancestry
4.5 4.0
Pronunciation: / dəˈraɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪv / (BrE)
to have a specified origin; to come from or be descended from a particular source, often used with reference to ancestry or lineage

Examples

Derive from ancestors
Her family derives from Scottish nobility on her mother's side.
Many modern traditions derive from older religious customs.
The language derives from an ancient Indo-European root.

Related Terms
