to have a specified origin; to come from or be descended from a particular source, often used with reference to ancestry or lineage

Examples

Derive from ancestors
Her family derives from Scottish nobility on her mother's side.
Many modern traditions derive from older religious customs.
The language derives from an ancient Indo-European root.

Synonyms

  • come from
    1.0 171/m
    come from is a simple everyday phrase for origin used in speech and writing, while derive sounds more formal and academic.
  • descend
    4.0 3.96/m
    descend denotes family or blood ties across generations, while derive can mean coming from many types of sources, not only ancestry.
  • originate
    3.8 16/m
    originate describes how something begins or is first created, while derive focuses on coming from an earlier source or origin.
