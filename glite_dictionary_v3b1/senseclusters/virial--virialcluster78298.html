virial

noun
thermodynamic coefficient
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪriəl / (AmE), / ˈvɪəriəl / (BrE)
a coefficient in the virial expansion used in thermodynamics to describe interactions between particles in a gas or liquid

Examples

Second virial coefficient
The second virial coefficient helps scientists understand how gas molecules interact at different temperatures.
Accurate measurement of the virial is essential for predicting the behavior of real gases.
Researchers calculated the virial to improve the accuracy of their thermodynamic models.
