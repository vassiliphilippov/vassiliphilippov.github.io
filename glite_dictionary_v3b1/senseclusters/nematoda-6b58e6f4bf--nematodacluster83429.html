Nematoda

noun
phylum of roundworms (unsegmented worms)
9.4 8.5
Pronunciation: /ne.mə.ˈtoʊ.də/ /; / /nɪ.mə.ˈtoʊ.də/ (AmE), /ne.mə.ˈtəʊ.də/ /; / /nɪ.mə.ˈtəʊ.də/ (BrE)
A large phylum of unsegmented, cylindrical worms, including roundworms, threadworms, and eelworms, many of which are parasitic or free-living in soil or water.

Examples

Soil Nematoda
Many soil-dwelling Nematoda are beneficial for controlling insect pests.
Parasitic Nematoda can cause diseases in plants, animals, and humans.
The biology class examined Nematoda under the microscope to study their distinctive features.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annelid - member of a different phylum of segmented worms
  • synonym: roundworm - any worm of the phylum Nematoda; unsegmented cylindrical worm
