Grison vittatus

noun
weasel-like carnivore of Central and South America
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraɪsən vɪˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈɡrɪsən vɪˈteɪtəs / (BrE)
A carnivorous mammal of Central and South America, resembling a weasel, with a greyish-white back and dark underparts.

Examples

The Grison vittatus animal
Biologists observed a pair of Grison vittatus near the riverbank at dawn.
The Grison vittatus hunts small rodents and birds in its forest habitat.
The Grison vittatus is less well known than the closely related tayra.
