hawsehole

noun
hole for anchor rope in a ship's bow
6.6 9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːs.hoʊl/ /; / /ˈhɔːs.hoʊl/ (AmE), /ˈhɔːs.həʊl/ (BrE)
A hole in the bow of a ship through which an anchor cable or rope passes.

Examples

The hawsehole on ship
Crew members made sure the hawsehole was clear before raising the anchor.
The heavy anchor chain rattled as it was pulled back through the hawsehole.
Years of saltwater had corroded the ship's iron hawsehole.
