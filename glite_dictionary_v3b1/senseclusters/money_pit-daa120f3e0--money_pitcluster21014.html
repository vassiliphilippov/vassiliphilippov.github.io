money pit

noun
property needing excessive repairs or upkeep
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌni pɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌni pɪt / (BrE)
A property or possession that constantly requires large amounts of money to maintain or repair, often more than expected or reasonable.

Examples

A money pit house
Buying an old castle can quickly turn into a money pit if unexpected repairs are needed.
Experts warned that renovating the property might be a money pit for inexperienced buyers.
The boat seemed like a dream at first, but it became a money pit with all the constant fixes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: white elephant - an expensive item that is difficult to maintain and not worth the cost
