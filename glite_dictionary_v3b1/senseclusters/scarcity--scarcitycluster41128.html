Synonyms

  • shortage
    2.7 12/m
    shortage means a situation where people or shops do not have enough of something, while scarcity is a broader, often long-term lack.
  • lack
    1.3 60/m
    lack is the simple state of not having something you need, while scarcity sounds more formal and often refers to limited resources.
  • undersupply
    5.1 0.01/m
    undersupply describes when production or delivery fails to meet demand, while scarcity is the general condition of too few resources.
  • dearth
    8.5 0.7/m
    dearth feels like a formal or poetic lack of something important, while scarcity is a neutral word for low supply.
  • paucity
    8.6 0.3/m
    paucity names a very small amount in formal or academic talk, while scarcity is more general and used across contexts.
  • insufficiency
    6.5 0.07/m
    insufficiency points to not having enough for a specific need, often skills or funds, while scarcity is a broader lack.
  • deficiency
    4.5 5/m
    deficiency means a missing or weak part, often in health or quality, while scarcity describes low amounts available.
