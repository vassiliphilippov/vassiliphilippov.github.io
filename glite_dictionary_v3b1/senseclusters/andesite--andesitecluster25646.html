andesite

noun
dark fine-grained volcanic rock
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.dəˌsaɪt /; / ˈændəˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈan.dɪ.sʌɪt /; / ˈandəsʌɪt / (BrE)
a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock of intermediate composition, usually found in volcanic areas and between basalt and rhyolite

Examples

Dark andesite rock
Geologists often compare andesite to basalt and rhyolite to study volcanic activity.
Scientists discovered a thick layer of andesite beneath the mountain's surface.
The lava flow was composed mainly of dark grey andesite mixed with lighter minerals.
Geologists collected samples of andesite during their fieldwork in the volcanic region.
The presence of andesite suggests previous episodes of volcanic activity in the area.
This mountain range is primarily composed of andesite and basaltic rocks.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock of lower silica content
  • degree higher: rhyolite - a light-colored volcanic rock of higher silica content
