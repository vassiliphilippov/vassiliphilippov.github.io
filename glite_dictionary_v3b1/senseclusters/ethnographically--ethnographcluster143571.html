ethnographically

adverb
in a cultural study way
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθnəˈɡræfɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɛθnəˈɡræfɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the scientific study and description of cultures or peoples

Examples

Studied ethnographically
The village was studied ethnographically to document its unique customs and beliefs.
She approached the festival ethnographically, taking detailed notes on every ritual.
The documentary was filmed ethnographically to accurately represent the community's daily life.

Related Terms
