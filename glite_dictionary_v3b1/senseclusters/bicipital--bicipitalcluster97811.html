bicipital

adjective
having two heads (anatomy)
Pronunciation: / baɪˈsɪpɪtəl / (AmE), / baɪˈsɪpɪtəl / (BrE)
(of a muscle) having two heads or points of origin, as in the biceps muscle.

Examples

Bicipital muscle head
Certain birds have a bicipital crest on their humerus for muscle attachment.
During the examination, the doctor tested the bicipital reflex to assess nerve function.
The bicipital groove of the humerus holds the tendon of the biceps muscle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biceps - a muscle with two heads or points of origin
