nymphalid butterfly

noun
butterfly of the family Nymphalidae
4.1 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈnɪm.fə.lɪd ˈbʌt.ər.flaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈnɪm.fə.lɪd ˈbʌt.ə.flaɪ/ (BrE)
A medium to large butterfly belonging to the family Nymphalidae, typically with brightly colored wings and reduced, nonfunctional forelegs carried folded on the breast.

Examples

Bright nymphalid butterflies
Many nymphalid butterflies have forelegs that are so small they appear to walk on only four legs.
She photographed a brightly patterned nymphalid butterfly resting on a wildflower.
The monarch is a well-known species of nymphalid butterfly found across North America.
