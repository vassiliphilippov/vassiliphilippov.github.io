rabbit on

verb
talk at length, often about unimportant things
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈræb.ɪt ɑn / (AmE), / ˈræb.ɪt ɒn / (BrE)
to talk for a long time in a noisy or excited way, often about trivial matters

Examples

To rabbit on about football
He can rabbit on for hours about football without noticing the time.
I've told him not to rabbit on about work at every dinner party.
She rabbited on during the entire meeting, barely letting anyone else speak.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be quiet - to stop talking or making noise
  • synonym: chatter - to talk quickly and continuously, especially about trivial matters
  • synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
