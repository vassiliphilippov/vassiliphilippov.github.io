cutover

noun
cleared logged land
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an area of land that has been cleared of trees after logging or timber harvesting

Examples

A muddy cutover
The hikers crossed the muddy cutover left by last year's logging operation.
Wildflowers began to grow in the cutover where the forest once stood.
After the timber company finished, the cutover was left bare and open to the sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clearcut - an area where all trees have been cut down
  • antonym: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
