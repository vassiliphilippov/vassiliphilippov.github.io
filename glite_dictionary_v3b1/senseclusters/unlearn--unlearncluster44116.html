unlearn

verb
intentionally discard learned knowledge or habits
3.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɝn /; / ʌnˈlɜrn / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɜːn / (BrE)
to intentionally forget or let go of knowledge, habits, or beliefs that you learned before

Examples

To unlearn bad habits
It can be hard to unlearn old habits built up over many years.
She worked hard to unlearn the biases she grew up with.
Sometimes, you must unlearn what you think you know to make progress.
It can be difficult to unlearn habits that have been with you for years.
She had to unlearn her old way of thinking to adapt to the new job.
Through therapy, he is slowly unlearning negative patterns from his childhood.
