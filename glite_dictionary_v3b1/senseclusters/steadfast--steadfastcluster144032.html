steadfast

adjective
firm and dependable, especially in loyalty
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛdˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈstɛdˌfɑːst / (BrE)
firm, loyal, and unwavering; not easily changed or moved

Examples

A steadfast friend
His steadfast friendship gave me strength during difficult times.
She remained steadfast in her support of the charity despite criticism.
The team was steadfast in pursuing its goals, no matter the obstacles.
Despite repeated setbacks, their steadfast determination led them to success.
His steadfast support was a source of great comfort during difficult times.
She remained steadfast in her beliefs, even when challenged by others.

  • antonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially as regards one's loyalties
  • synonym: loyal - showing firm support or allegiance
  • synonym: resolute - firmly determined or resolute
