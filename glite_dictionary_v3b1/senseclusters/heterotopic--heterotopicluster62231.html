heterotopic

adjective
transplanted in different site
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈtɑpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈtɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to a graft or transplant placed in a different location from the organ's normal position in the body

Examples

Heterotopic heart transplant
The surgeon performed a heterotopic heart transplant, placing the donor heart alongside the patient's own.
A heterotopic liver transplant may be considered when the original liver is not removed.
Doctors discussed the risks and benefits of a heterotopic kidney transplant with the patient.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orthotopic - relating to a graft or transplant placed in the organ's usual position
