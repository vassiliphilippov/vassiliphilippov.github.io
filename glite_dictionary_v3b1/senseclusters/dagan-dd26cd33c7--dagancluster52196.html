Dagan

proper noun
ancient Semitic god of agriculture
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪɡən / (BrE)
An ancient Semitic god of agriculture and the earth, worshipped especially in Mesopotamia; counterpart of the Phoenician Dagon.

Examples

Dagan god worship
Many temples were dedicated to Dagan in ancient Mesopotamian cities.
Some historians believe Dagan was later associated with other fertility gods.
The worship of Dagan spread to various cultures across the ancient Near East.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Dagon - Phoenician god of agriculture, related to Dagan
