nucleoid

noun
prokaryotic DNA region
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈnjuːklɪˌɔɪd / (BrE)
the irregularly-shaped region within a prokaryotic cell where the genetic material (DNA) is located

Examples

The nucleoid region
In bacteria, the nucleoid is not surrounded by a membrane like a nucleus.
Scientists observed the nucleoid under a microscope to study the cell's genetic material.
The nucleoid contains most of the DNA found in a prokaryotic cell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromosome - a structure of DNA and protein carrying genetic information
  • antonym: nucleus - the membrane-bound organelle containing DNA in eukaryotic cells
