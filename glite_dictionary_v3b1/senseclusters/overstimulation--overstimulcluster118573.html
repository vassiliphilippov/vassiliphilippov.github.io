overstimulation

noun
excessive sensory input
6.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.stɪm.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /oʊ.vər.stɪm.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.stɪm.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /əʊ.və.stɪm.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a state in which someone or something receives too much stimulation, causing difficulty focusing, agitation, or negative effects

Examples

Child overstimulation effects
Children can experience overstimulation in environments with loud noises and bright lights.
After hours at the busy festival, he felt a sense of overstimulation and needed a quiet place to rest.
Some people are more sensitive to overstimulation and may need breaks from crowded spaces.
Too much screen time can lead to overstimulation in young children, making it hard for them to sleep.
After hours at the noisy amusement park, she felt overwhelmed by overstimulation and needed a quiet place to rest.
The puppy's overstimulation was obvious as it ran around the house, unable to settle down.
Children can become irritable after prolonged overstimulation from loud noises and bright lights.
The overstimulation of nerve cells may result in headaches or fatigue.
Doctors warn that overstimulation of the heart with certain drugs can be dangerous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of being free from agitation or strong stimulation
  • antonym: calmness - a state of being relaxed and free from excitement
  • synonym: hyperactivity - a state of being unusually or abnormally active
  • synonym: overload - an excessive amount of something, especially sensory input
  • degree lower: stimulation - the act of encouraging activity or increased function
  • antonym: understimulation - insufficient stimulation or activation
