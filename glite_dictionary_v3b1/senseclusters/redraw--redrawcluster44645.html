redraw

verb
to draw again
3.5
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈdrɔː/ /; / /rə.ˈdrɔː/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈdrɔː/ /; / /riː.ˈdrɔː/ (BrE)
to draw something again, usually to improve, correct, or update it

Examples

To redraw a picture
The artist decided to redraw the portrait to capture a better likeness of her subject.
After receiving feedback, the engineer had to redraw the technical diagrams.
Sometimes architects redraw blueprints several times before construction begins.
After noticing the error, the architect had to redraw the entire blueprint.
The teacher asked the student to redraw the diagram for better clarity.
They redrew the boundaries of the district to reflect the new population data.
After receiving feedback, she decided to redraw the illustration for the textbook.
The city council voted to redraw the district boundaries to reflect population changes.
He had to redraw the map because the original was too confusing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: edit - to make changes to text, images, or other content
  • antonym: erase - to remove something that has been drawn or written
  • synonym: revise - to change or improve something, especially a document or plan
  • degree lower: sketch - to make a quick, rough drawing
