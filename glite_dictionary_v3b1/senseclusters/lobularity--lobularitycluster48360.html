lobularity

noun
the state of having lobules
9.9
Pronunciation: / loʊˈbjuːlɚɾi / (AmE), / ləʊˈbjʊːlərɪti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of having lobules, especially in tissues or organs.

Examples

The lobularity of tissues
Certain diseases can increase the lobularity of glandular tissues.
In radiology reports, lobularity is often noted as a distinguishing feature of some lesions.
The lobularity of the liver was evident upon microscopic examination.
