Cathar

noun
modern Cathar follower
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθɑr / (AmE), / ˈkæθɑː / (BrE)
a person in modern times who identifies with or follows beliefs inspired by the medieval Cathars

Examples

Modern Cathar follower
She joined a group of modern Cathar adherents studying the old spiritual texts.
Some people today call themselves Cathars to express their spiritual beliefs.
The Cathar community organizes annual gatherings to discuss their philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gnostic - a person who believes in Gnosticism, an ancient spiritual movement
  • antonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to orthodox religious doctrine
