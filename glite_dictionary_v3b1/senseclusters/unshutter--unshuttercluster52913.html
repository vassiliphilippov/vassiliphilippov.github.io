unshutter

verb
to open shutter
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃʌtər / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃʌtə / (BrE)
to open or release the shutter of a camera or similar device to allow light to enter or the device to operate

Examples

To unshutter a camera
Before taking the photograph, she carefully unshuttered the camera to let in the morning light.
The technician unshuttered the device so it could begin recording the experiment.
He forgot to unshutter the lens, resulting in a completely dark image.

Related Terms

  • antonym: close - to move something so that it is no longer open
  • synonym: open - to move something so that it is no longer closed
