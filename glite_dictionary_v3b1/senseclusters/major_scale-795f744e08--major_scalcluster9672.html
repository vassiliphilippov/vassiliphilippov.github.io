major scale

noun
musical scale with a specific pattern of intervals
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə skeɪl / (BrE)
A diatonic musical scale consisting of seven notes, with intervals of whole steps except between the third and fourth, and seventh and eighth notes, where there are half steps.

Examples

The major scale notes
He practiced playing the major scale on his guitar to improve his technique.
Most popular songs use the major scale for their melodies.
The piece is written in the key of C major scale, which has no sharps or flats.
