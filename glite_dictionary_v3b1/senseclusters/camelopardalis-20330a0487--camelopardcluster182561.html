Camelopardalis

proper noun
northern constellation
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˌmɛləˌpɑːrˈdeɪlɪs / (AmE), / kəˌmɛləˌpɑːˈdɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a faint northern constellation named after the giraffe, located between Ursa Major and Perseus

Examples

Camelopardalis constellation
On a clear night, you can try to find Camelopardalis near the North Star.
Astronomers often use Camelopardalis as a reference point for locating other constellations.
Although Camelopardalis is faint, it covers a large area of the northern sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ursa Major - a large northern constellation also known as the Great Bear
