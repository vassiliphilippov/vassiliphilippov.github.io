unrotted

adjective
not decayed
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈrɑtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɒtɪd / (BrE)
not decayed or decomposed; still fresh or intact

Examples

Fresh unrotted wood
The carpenter selected only unrotted logs for the new cabin.
After the storm, most of the fruit was spoiled, but a few unrotted apples remained.
They found some unrotted wood beneath the old shed that could still be used.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fresh - recently made or obtained; not spoiled or decayed
  • antonym: rotten - decayed or decomposed
