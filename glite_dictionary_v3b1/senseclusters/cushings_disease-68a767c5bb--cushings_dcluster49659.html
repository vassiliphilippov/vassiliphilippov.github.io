Cushing's disease

noun
disease caused by excess ACTH, leading to adrenal overactivity
Pronunciation: / ˈkʊʃɪŋz dɪˌziːz / (AmE), / ˈkʊʃɪŋz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A medical condition caused by excessive production of ACTH by the pituitary gland, leading to overactivity of the adrenal glands and resulting in symptoms such as obesity.

Examples

Cushing's disease symptoms
Obesity, high blood pressure, and muscle weakness are common symptoms of Cushing's disease.
The patient was diagnosed with Cushing's disease after presenting with rapid weight gain and fatigue.
Treatment for Cushing's disease often involves addressing the pituitary tumor causing hormone overproduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cushing's syndrome - condition caused by excess cortisol, including but not limited to pituitary-origin cases
