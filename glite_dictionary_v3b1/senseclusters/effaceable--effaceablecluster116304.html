effaceable

adjective
able to be erased or removed
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfeɪsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪˈfeɪsəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being erased, removed, or made to disappear

Examples

An effaceable mark
Historical records are not always effaceable, even when people wish to forget them.
The emotional wounds she felt were not easily effaceable by an apology.
The old graffiti was effaceable with a special cleaning solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erasable - able to be erased or removed
  • antonym: indelible - impossible to remove, erase, or wash away
