panpsychist

noun
believer in universal consciousness
9.9
Pronunciation: / pænˈsaɪkɪst / (AmE), / pænˈsaɪkɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that all matter, including inanimate objects, has some form of consciousness or mind

Examples

A panpsychist thinker
The panpsychist suggested that even rocks might have a basic form of awareness.
As a panpsychist, she argued that consciousness is a fundamental feature of the universe.
Many philosophers debate the ideas put forward by a panpsychist about the nature of mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animist - a person who believes that natural objects have spirits
  • antonym: materialist - a person who believes only physical matter exists
