clue

noun
a hint or slight indication
1.0 40/m
Pronunciation: / kluÀê /; / klu / (AmE), / kluÀê / (BrE)
a piece of information or evidence that helps to solve a problem or mystery

Examples

A clue to the mystery
A faint smell of perfume was the only clue left at the scene.
The detective found a clue that pointed to the suspect's identity.
There was no clue as to where she had gone.
She found a clue in the old photograph that led her to the answer.
The detective searched the room for any clue that could explain the disappearance.
Without a single clue, solving the puzzle seemed impossible.

Synonyms

    4.8 11/m

  • track
    5.0 17/m

  • hint
    1.5 13/m
    a hint is a small, indirect suggestion meant to guide someone, while a clue is stronger evidence that helps solve a mystery.
