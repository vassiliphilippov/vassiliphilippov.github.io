pruner

noun
tool or program that trims models or data
6.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8pru\u02d0n\u0259r / (AmE), / \u02c8pru\u02d0n\u0259 / (BrE)
(computing, especially machine learning) a program, tool, or person that removes unnecessary parameters, features, or data from a model, dataset, or program to make it smaller, faster, or simpler.

Examples

A model pruner
The ML pipeline uses a pruner to remove redundant parameters during optimization.
During training, the team deployed several pruners that zeroed small weights to shrink the model.
After profiling, the engineers instructed the pruner to discard low-importance features and speed up inference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compress - to reduce the size of a model or dataset, often by removing or encoding information
  • synonym: prune - to remove unwanted parts to improve shape or performance (in gardening and metaphorically in computing)
