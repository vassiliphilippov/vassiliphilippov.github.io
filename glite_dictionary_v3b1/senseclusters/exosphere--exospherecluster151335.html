exosphere

noun
outermost atmospheric layer
Pronunciation: / ˈɛk.soʊˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈɛksəʊˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the outermost layer of a planet's atmosphere, lying above the thermosphere and gradually transitioning into outer space

Examples

The exosphere layer
Because the exosphere contains so few molecules, atmospheric drag is minimal in this region.
Satellites in the exosphere are exposed to extremely low densities of atmospheric particles.
The exosphere marks the boundary where Earth's atmosphere fades into outer space.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: thermosphere - the atmospheric layer directly below the exosphere
