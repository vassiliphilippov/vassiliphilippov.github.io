rootedness

noun
state of having roots
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈruː.tɪd.nəs/ /; / /ˈruː.təd.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈruː.tɪd.nəs/ /; / /ˈruː.təd.nəs/ (BrE)
the state or quality of being firmly fixed in place by roots, especially in plants.

Examples

Rootedness of a tree
The rootedness of the young tree ensured it would survive the strong winds.
Gardeners often check the rootedness of cuttings before transplanting them into larger pots.
Good soil helps promote the rootedness necessary for healthy plant growth.
