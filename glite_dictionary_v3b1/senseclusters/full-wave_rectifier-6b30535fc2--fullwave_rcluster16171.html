full-wave rectifier

noun
device converting AC to DC on both cycles
Pronunciation: / ˌfʊl weɪv rɛkˈtɪfaɪər / (AmE), / ˌfʊl weɪv rɛkˈtɪfaɪə / (BrE)
A device that converts both halves of an alternating current waveform into direct current, producing a positive output for both cycles.

Examples

A full-wave rectifier device
A full-wave rectifier provides a smoother DC output than a half-wave rectifier.
Engineers often choose a full-wave rectifier design for improved power supply performance.
This circuit uses a full-wave rectifier to convert alternating current to direct current efficiently.
