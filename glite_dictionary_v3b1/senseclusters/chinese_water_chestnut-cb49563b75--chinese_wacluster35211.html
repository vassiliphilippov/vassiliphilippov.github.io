Chinese water chestnut

noun
edible tuber from Asian sedge
6.8
Pronunciation: / tʃaɪˈniz ˈwɔtɚ ˈtʃɛsˌnʌt / (AmE), / tʃaɪˈniːz ˈwɔːtə ˈtʃɛsˌnʌt / (BrE)
A type of sedge native to Asia, grown for its edible bulb-shaped tubers, commonly used in Chinese cuisine.

Examples

Fresh Chinese water chestnut
She peeled the Chinese water chestnut before adding them to the salad.
The stir-fry included sliced Chinese water chestnut for added crunch.
You can buy fresh Chinese water chestnut at many Asian grocery stores.
