torsion balance

noun
instrument for measuring small forces via torsion
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔːrʒən ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈtɔːʃən ˈbæləns / (BrE)
a scientific instrument used to measure very small forces by observing the torsion they cause in a thin wire or filament

Examples

A torsion balance instrument
A torsion balance is essential in measuring weak electrostatic or magnetic forces accurately.
Delicate measurements with the torsion balance have contributed to our understanding of physical laws.
The scientist used a torsion balance to detect tiny gravitational changes in the laboratory.
