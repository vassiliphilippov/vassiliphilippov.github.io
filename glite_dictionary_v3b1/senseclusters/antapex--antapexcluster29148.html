antapex

noun
point opposite the solar apex
9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈteɪ.peks/ /; / /æn.ˈtæ.peks/ (AmE), /æn.ˈteɪ.peks/ /; / /æn.ˈtæ.peks/ (BrE)
the point in the sky that is directly opposite the solar apex, representing the direction from which the solar system is moving away.

Examples

The antapex point
Astronomers have located the antapex to better understand the motion of our solar system.
The precise coordinates of the antapex can be calculated using the velocity vector of the solar system.
Unlike the apex, the antapex indicates the direction from which the Sun is receding.
