phase IV clinical trial

noun
clinical trial after drug approval
4.2
Pronunciation: / feɪz fɔr ˈklɪnɪkəl traɪəl / (AmE), / feɪz fɔː ˈklɪnɪkəl traɪəl / (BrE)
a clinical trial conducted after a drug has been approved for general use, to monitor its effectiveness and side effects in diverse populations and gather additional information

Examples

A phase IV clinical trial
After the drug's approval, a phase IV clinical trial was launched to monitor long-term side effects.
Participation in a phase IV clinical trial offers valuable data about real-world drug effectiveness.
Regulators required a phase IV clinical trial to examine the medication's safety in elderly populations.
