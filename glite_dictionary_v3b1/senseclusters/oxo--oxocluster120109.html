oxo

x
chemical prefix for =O
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒksəʊ / (BrE)
used as a prefix in chemical names to indicate the presence of a carbonyl group (=O) replacing a hydrogen atom.

Examples

Oxo prefix in names
The oxo prefix is used in organic chemistry to show a carbonyl group in a molecule.
In acetone, the oxo group is bonded to the central carbon atom.
Chemists often use oxo to describe compounds with a double-bonded oxygen atom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbonyl - a functional group composed of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom
