mealy-mouthed

adjective
insincerely indirect in speech
8.9 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmiÀêli Àåma äŒ∏t / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀêli Àåma äŒ∏t / (BrE)
hesitant or unwilling to state facts or opinions simply and directly, often out of timidity, insincerity, or hypocrisy

Examples

Mealy-mouthed answers
She grew impatient with his mealy-mouthed responses to her direct questions.
The manager's mealy-mouthed explanation failed to reassure the staff.
Voters are tired of mealy-mouthed promises from politicians.

Synonyms

  • evasive
    7.3 1.35/m
    evasive avoids giving direct or clear answers to hide or avoid commitment, while mealy-mouthed speaks weakly and unclearly from timidity or insincerity.
  • closemouthed
    7.5 0.05/m
    closemouthed keeps quiet and shares little information by choice, while mealy-mouthed speaks but avoids saying simple direct facts.
  • tight-lipped
    5.7 0.89/m
    tight-lipped stays silent and refuses to reveal facts, while mealy-mouthed talks around issues in a timid or insincere way.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct - honest and straightforward in speech or manner
  • antonym: forthright - direct and outspoken; straightforward and honest
