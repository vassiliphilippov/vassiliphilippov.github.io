munchkin

noun
small child (affectionate)
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌntʃkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌntʃkɪn / (BrE)
an affectionate term for a small, cute child or person

Examples

Cute munchkin
She called her little brother a munchkin because of his size.
The teacher smiled as the group of munchkins ran into the classroom.
He picked up the sleeping munchkin and carried her to bed.
She picked up her little munchkin and gave him a big hug before school.
The coach called the youngest players his munchkins because of their small size.
After dinner, the munchkin fell asleep on the couch with his teddy bear.

Related Terms

  • antonym: giant - a very large person
  • synonym: tot - a very young child
