snap

verb
break suddenly with a sharp noise
Pronunciation: / snæp / (AmE), / snæp / (BrE)
to break or cause to break suddenly and completely, often with a sharp noise

Examples

To snap a pencil
If you bend the pencil too far, it will snap.
She pulled the stick so hard it snapped in two.
The branch snapped under the weight of the snow.
Be careful not to snap the wire while you're stretching it.
She heard a loud noise as the branch snapped beneath her weight.
The rope was pulled so tightly that it finally snapped.
