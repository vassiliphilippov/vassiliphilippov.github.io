paleographist

noun
a specialist in ancient handwriting (paleography)
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˈɑɡrəfɪst / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪˈɒɡrəfɪst / (BrE)
an archaeologist or scholar skilled in the study of ancient handwriting and scripts (paleography)

Examples

A paleographist studies scripts
As a paleographist, she could decipher Latin texts from the twelfth century with ease.
The paleographist explained how the script style revealed the document's age.
The museum hired a renowned paleographist to authenticate the medieval manuscripts.
