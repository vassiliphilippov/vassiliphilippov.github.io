chaff

noun
thin metal strips used to confuse radar
Pronunciation: / tʃæf / (AmE), / tʃɑːf / (BrE)
Thin strips of metal or foil that are released into the air as a countermeasure to confuse enemy radar.

Examples

Chaff clouds jets
Modern fighter jets are equipped with both chaff and flares for self-defense.
The military exercise included a demonstration of chaff deployment to obscure radar signatures.
The pilot released chaff to evade the incoming missile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radar jammer - device/method to block or deceive radar systems
