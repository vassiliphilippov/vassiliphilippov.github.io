outward

adjective
external; relating to outer appearance
4.7 2.80/m
Pronunciation: / Ààa ätÀåw…ùd / (AmE), / Ààa ät.w…ôd / (BrE)
relating to the external or physical appearance of something, rather than to its inner qualities or thoughts

Examples

Outward appearance matters
Despite his calm outward demeanor, he was quite nervous inside.
She was more interested in outward appearances than genuine emotions.
The outward damage to the car was minor, but the engine had serious problems.

Synonyms

  • external
    4.4 2.12/m
    external is about surface appearance or outside qualities, while outward focuses on how something looks rather than its inner character.
  • outer
    1.0 33/m
    outer sits next to the outside or far from the center, while outward describes a thing's external appearance or look.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inward - relating to the inner thoughts or feelings rather than the external
