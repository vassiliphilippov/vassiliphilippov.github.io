HMG-CoA reductase

noun
enzyme involved in cholesterol production
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛm ˌdʒi si oʊ ˈrɛdʌkteɪs / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛm ˌdʒiː siː əʊ rɛˈdʌktɛɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme in the liver that plays a key role in the production of cholesterol

Examples

HMG-CoA reductase enzyme
Researchers studied the effect of diet on HMG-CoA reductase activity in mice.
Some genetic disorders can lead to an overproduction of HMG-CoA reductase, raising cholesterol levels.
Statins work by inhibiting HMG-CoA reductase, thereby lowering cholesterol levels in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cholesterol - a type of fat made by the liver, linked to heart health
  • synonym: statin - a class of drugs that inhibit HMG-CoA reductase
