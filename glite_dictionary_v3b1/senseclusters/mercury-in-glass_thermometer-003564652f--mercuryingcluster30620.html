mercury-in-glass thermometer

noun
thermometer using mercury in a glass tube
6.3 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmɜːr.kjʊr.i.ɪn.glæs θər.ˈmɑː.mɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈmɜː.kjʊr.i.ɪn.glɑːs θə.ˈmɒm.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a thermometer in which the expansion of mercury in a sealed glass tube indicates temperature, with measurements marked in degrees Celsius or Fahrenheit

Examples

A mercury-in-glass thermometer device
Due to safety concerns, the mercury-in-glass thermometer is being replaced by digital alternatives.
Older households often keep a mercury-in-glass thermometer for emergencies.
The doctor used a mercury-in-glass thermometer to check the patient's temperature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alcohol thermometer - a thermometer using alcohol in place of mercury
  • synonym: clinical thermometer - a thermometer used to measure human body temperature
