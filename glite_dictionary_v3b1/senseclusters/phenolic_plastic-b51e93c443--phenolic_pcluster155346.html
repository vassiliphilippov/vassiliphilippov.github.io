phenolic plastic

noun
plastic made from phenolic resins
4.0
Pronunciation: / fəˈnoʊ.lɪk ˈplæs.tɪk / (AmE), / fɪˈnɒl.ɪk ˈplæs.tɪk / (BrE)
a type of plastic made from or consisting of phenolic resins, known for its heat resistance and insulating properties

Examples

Phenolic plastic material
Bakelite is one of the earliest and most famous phenolic plastics used in industrial applications.
Because of their excellent electrical insulating properties, phenolic plastics are commonly found in electrical devices.
The durability and heat resistance of phenolic plastic make it ideal for use in kitchenware and automotive parts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phenolic resin - a synthetic thermosetting resin obtained by the reaction of phenol with formaldehyde
