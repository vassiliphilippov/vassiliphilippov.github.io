frequentative

noun
verb form expressing repeated action
Pronunciation: / friˈkwɛntətɪv / (AmE), / friˈkwɛntətɪv / (BrE)
(Linguistics) a verb form or a derivative that expresses repeated or habitual action.

Examples

A frequentative verb form
In some languages, a frequentative form of the verb indicates actions that happen repeatedly.
Latin uses special suffixes to create frequentative verbs, which typically mean 'to do often.'
The teacher explained that the frequentative shows habitual actions in certain verb conjugations.
