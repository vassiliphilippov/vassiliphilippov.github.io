honorable mention

noun
an official recognition of notable achievement
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɑn.ɚ.ə.bəl ˈmɛn.ʃən /; / ˈɑːnərəbəl ˈmɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˈɒn.ər.ə.bəl ˈmɛn.ʃən /; / ˈɒnərəbl ˈmenʃən / (BrE)
an official or special recognition given to someone for notable achievement, even though they did not win the main prize

Examples

An honorable mention award
Although her essay did not win, it earned an honorable mention from the committee.
She received an honorable mention for her painting in the art competition.
The judges gave him an honorable mention for his impressive science project.
Even though her novel didn't win, it was awarded an honorable mention by the judges.
He was delighted to receive an honorable mention in the national photography competition.
The science fair judges gave three honorable mentions in addition to the main prizes.
