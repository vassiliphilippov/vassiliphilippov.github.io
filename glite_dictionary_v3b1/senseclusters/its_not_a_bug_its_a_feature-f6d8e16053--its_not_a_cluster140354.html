it's not a bug, it's a feature

x
intentional flaw in software (ironic/humorous)
Pronunciation: / ɪts nɑt ə bʌɡ, ɪts ə ˈfiːtʃər / (AmE), / ɪts nɒt ə bʌɡ, ɪts ə ˈfiːtʃə / (BrE)
used to suggest, often humorously or ironically, that a flaw or unexpected behavior in software is an intentional design element rather than a mistake

Examples

It's not a bug, it's a feature phrase
Every time the clock resets at midnight, they say it's not a bug, it's a feature.
Users complained about the confusing menu, but support replied, 'it's not a bug, it's a feature.'
When the screen turns upside down, the developer insists it's not a bug, it's a feature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bug - an error or flaw in a computer program
  • synonym: feature - an intentional function or characteristic of a software product
