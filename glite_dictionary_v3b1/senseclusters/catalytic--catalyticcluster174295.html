catalytic

adjective
relating to catalysis in chemical reactions
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or causing catalysis, the process of increasing the rate of a chemical reaction by a catalyst.

Examples

Catalytic reaction process
His research focuses on developing catalytic methods to make manufacturing more efficient.
Many industrial processes rely on catalytic converters to reduce pollution.
The catalytic process dramatically sped up the chemical reaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent change
