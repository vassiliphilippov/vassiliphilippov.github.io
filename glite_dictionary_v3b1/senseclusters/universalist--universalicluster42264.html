universalist

noun
believer in unity
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜrsəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that all people, beliefs, or religions are equal or should be united, or that universal rights apply to everyone

Examples

A universalist thinker
As a universalist, he supports the idea that all religions share common values.
The conference brought together universalists from different countries to discuss global unity.
Her universalist beliefs influence her approach to social justice and equality.
As a committed universalist, she argued that human rights should be protected everywhere.
The philosopher was known as a universalist who valued justice for all people.
Many universalists support global cooperation to solve world problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: egalitarian - a person who believes in the equality of all people
  • antonym: particularist - a person who believes in special rights or interests for specific groups
  • antonym: sectarian - a person strongly supporting a particular religious or political group
