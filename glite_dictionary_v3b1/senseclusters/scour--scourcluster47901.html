scour

verb
clean by hard rubbing
8.6 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈskaʊɚ /; / ˈskaʊər / (AmE), / ˈskaʊə / (BrE)
to clean something thoroughly by scrubbing or rubbing hard, often using an abrasive material or liquid

Examples

To scour a pan
After the barbecue, we had to scour the grill grates to get rid of the grease.
He used steel wool to scour the burnt pan until it was spotless.
The cleaning crew scoured every corner of the kitchen before the inspection.
The chef scoured the pan with hot water to get rid of burnt food.
The surgeon will scour the wound with a sterile solution before stitching it up.
You must scour the fuel tank to remove any traces of dirt or debris.
He scoured the countertops every evening as part of his cleaning routine.
If you scour the floor with too much force, you might damage the tiles.
She used a steel brush to scour the burned pan until it was shiny again.
