Lactobacillus acidophilus

noun
probiotic bacterium used in yogurt and supplements
4.1
Pronunciation: /læk.təʊ.bə.ˈɪl.əs æs.ɪ.də.ˈfɪl.əs/ (AmE), /læk.təʊ.bə.ˈɪl.əs æs.ɪ.dɒ.ˈfɪl.əs/ (BrE)
a species of bacterium found in the gut and used in the production of yogurt and probiotic supplements

Examples

Lactobacillus acidophilus bacteria
Consuming yogurt containing Lactobacillus acidophilus can help support a healthy digestive system.
Fermented foods like kefir and certain cheeses may also contain Lactobacillus acidophilus.
Some probiotic supplements list Lactobacillus acidophilus as one of their main active ingredients.
