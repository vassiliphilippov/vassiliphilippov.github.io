secular

adjective
not monastic (of clergy)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkjələr / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊlə / (BrE)
(of clergy) not belonging to a monastic order; relating to clergy who do not take monastic vows.

Examples

Secular priest
The bishop appointed several secular priests to manage the parish.
The church council discussed the roles of secular and regular clergy.
Unlike monks, secular clergy live and work among laypeople in the community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monastic - relating to monks or monasteries
