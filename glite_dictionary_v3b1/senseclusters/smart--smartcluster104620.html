smart

adjective
quick and brisk in movement or action
1.0
Pronunciation: / smɑrt / (AmE), / smɑːt / (BrE)
having or showing intelligence, quick thinking, or mental ability

Examples

A smart student
She made a smart turn at the corner, impressing her driving instructor.
The soldiers gave a smart salute as the officer passed by.
They set off at a smart pace, eager to reach the summit before noon.
His smart decisions saved the company from disaster.
She gave a smart answer to the tricky question.
They need a smart leader to solve these complex problems.
He's always been a smart kid, grasping concepts faster than his peers.
Her daughter is very smart and picks up new languages easily.
The new student proved to be quite smart during math class discussions.
