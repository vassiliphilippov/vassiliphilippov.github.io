leitmotif

noun
recurring theme in music or art
9.1 0.44/m
Pronunciation: / Ààla…™tÀåmo äÀåtif /; / Ààla…™tmo äÀåtif / (AmE), / Ààla…™t.m…ô äÀåtiÀêf /; / Ààla…™tm…ô äÀåtiÀêf / (BrE)
a recurring theme, idea, or musical phrase, especially one associated with a particular person, thing, or situation (often in music or opera)

Examples

A leitmotif theme
In Wagner's works, the leitmotif subtly signals the presence of a particular emotion or character.
The composer assigned a distinct leitmotif to each character in the opera.
The film's main leitmotif recurs whenever the protagonist faces a moral dilemma.
The villain's appearance was always accompanied by a dark leitmotif in the soundtrack.
Throughout the novel, the author uses rain as a leitmotif to symbolize sadness.
In the film, a gentle piano leitmotif plays whenever the main character remembers her childhood.

Synonyms

  • motif
    6.7 0.71/m
    motif is a short musical idea repeated and developed in a piece, while leitmotif is tied to a person or situation.
  • musical theme
    3.4 0.06/m
    musical theme is the main melody or idea often repeated in a piece, while leitmotif links that idea to a character or situation.
  • melodic theme
    5.8 0.01/m
    melodic theme is the principal tune composers develop in music, while leitmotif usually connects that tune to a person, thing, or idea.
