license tax

noun
government fee for obtaining a license
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsəns tæks / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsəns tæks / (BrE)
a fee levied by the government for the privilege of obtaining a license to carry out certain activities, such as selling goods or practicing a profession

Examples

Pay license tax
All liquor retailers are required to pay a license tax before they can legally sell alcohol.
Each business owner must submit the appropriate license tax along with their application forms.
The state increased the license tax for practicing physicians last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: license fee - a charge paid for the granting of a license
  • degree higher: tax - an amount of money paid to the government for public purposes
