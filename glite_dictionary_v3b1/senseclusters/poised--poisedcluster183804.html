poised

adjective
genetically ready to activate
9.6
Pronunciation: /pɔɪzd/ (AmE), /pɔɪzd/ (BrE)
(in genetics and molecular biology) in a prepared state for activation; having molecular features (for example paused RNA polymerase or specific chromatin marks) that enable rapid transcription when signalled.

Examples

Poised gene activation
During differentiation, many developmental promoters remain poised with paused polymerase ready for rapid activation.
Chromatin modifications left the enhancer poised, enabling a quick transcriptional response when signals arrived.
Researchers found genes poised in stem cells that later switched on during early embryonic stages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: primed - prepared or made ready for rapid activation or response
  • antonym: repressed - kept inactive or silenced at the molecular level (opposite of being ready to activate)
