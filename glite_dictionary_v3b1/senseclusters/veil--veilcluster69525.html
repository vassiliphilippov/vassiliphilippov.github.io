veil

noun
priest's vestment or ceremonial silk shawl
Pronunciation: / veɪl / (AmE), / veɪl / (BrE)
a vestment worn by a priest at High Mass in the Roman Catholic Church; a silk shawl draped as part of the ceremony

Examples

Priest's veil
During the procession, the deacon wore a delicate silk veil as part of his vestments.
The priest placed the veil over the chalice during the High Mass ceremony.
The richly embroidered veil was reserved for special liturgical celebrations.
