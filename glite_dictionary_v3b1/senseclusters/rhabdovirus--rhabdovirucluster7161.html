rhabdovirus

noun
virus family including rabies virus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈræb.doʊˌvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈrab.dəʊˌvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
any of a family of viruses (Rhabdoviridae) including those causing rabies, characterized by their rod- or bullet-shaped appearance

Examples

A rhabdovirus infection
Many rhabdoviruses are transmitted by insects such as mosquitoes.
Rabies is caused by a rhabdovirus that attacks the central nervous system.
Scientists are studying the structure of the rhabdovirus to develop better vaccines.
