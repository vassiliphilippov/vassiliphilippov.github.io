neuralgia

noun
acute nerve pain
6.8
Pronunciation: / nʊˈrældʒə / (AmE), / njʊəˈrældʒə / (BrE)
A sharp, severe pain that occurs along the course of a nerve or nerves.

Examples

Severe neuralgia pain
She suffered from neuralgia after her dental surgery, making even light touch unbearable.
The doctor prescribed medication to help manage his chronic neuralgia.
Trigeminal neuralgia can cause severe facial pain on one side of the face.
