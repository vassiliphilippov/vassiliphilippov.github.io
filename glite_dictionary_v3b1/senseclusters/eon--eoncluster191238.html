eon

noun
Gnostic divine being or emanation
Pronunciation: / ˈiː.ɑn /; / ˈiːˌɑn, ˈeɪˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈiː.ɒn /; / ˈiːən, ˈiːɒn / (BrE)
(in Gnosticism) a divine being or spiritual emanation arising from the Supreme Being, often playing a role in the creation or administration of the universe.

Examples

Gnostic eon
According to Gnostic texts, each eon represents a distinct aspect of divine will.
Some scholars believe every eon has a counterpart, linking material and spiritual realms.
The doctrine described the structure of the universe as composed of multiple eons.
According to the text, the last aeon was responsible for shaping the material world.
In Gnostic belief, each aeon represents a stage in the process of universal creation.
Scholars often debate the number and hierarchy of aeons within different Gnostic sects.
