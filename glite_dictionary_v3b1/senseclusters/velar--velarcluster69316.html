velar

noun
consonant made with tongue near soft palate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvilər / (AmE), / ˈviːlə / (BrE)
A consonant sound produced with the back of the tongue touching or approaching the soft palate (velum).

Examples

A velar sound
In phonetics class, we learned to distinguish between alveolar and velar sounds.
Many languages feature both voiced and voiceless velar consonants.
The English sound represented by 'k' in 'cat' is a velar.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: palatal - a consonant sound produced with the tongue near the hard palate
  • degree higher: uvular - a consonant produced with the back of the tongue near the uvula
