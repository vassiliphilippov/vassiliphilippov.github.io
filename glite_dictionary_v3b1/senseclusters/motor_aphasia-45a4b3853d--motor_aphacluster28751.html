motor aphasia

noun
difficulty speaking or writing due to brain injury

A type of aphasia in which the ability to express oneself by speech or writing is severely impaired, though comprehension is relatively preserved.

Examples

Motor aphasia after stroke
After his stroke, he was diagnosed with motor aphasia and struggled to find the words to speak.
The patient's motor aphasia made it challenging for her to write simple sentences.
Therapists worked with him daily to improve communication affected by motor aphasia.

Synonyms

  • Broca's aphasia
    7.5 0.00/m
    Broca's aphasia is the medical name doctors use in clinics, while motor aphasia describes the speech-production problem directly.
  • expressive aphasia
    7.7 0.01/m
    expressive aphasia is the common descriptive term for difficulty speaking or writing, while motor aphasia is more technical and medical.
  • nonfluent aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    nonfluent aphasia is a wider term for speech that is slow or effortful, while motor aphasia focuses on production loss.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sensory aphasia - Aphasia involving impaired comprehension
