sievelike

adjective
full of holes
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːvˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈsiːvˌlaɪk / (BrE)
having many small holes or gaps, resembling a sieve in allowing things to pass through easily

Examples

A sievelike fence
After years of neglect, the fence became so sievelike that animals could easily get through.
The scientist described the rock as sievelike because water passed through it quickly.
His memory was sievelike, and he often forgot important details.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leaky - allowing liquid or gas to escape through holes or cracks
  • synonym: porous - having many small holes that allow liquid or air to pass through
  • antonym: solid - having no holes or gaps; not allowing anything to pass through
