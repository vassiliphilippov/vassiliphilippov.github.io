dilute

adjective
made weaker or less concentrated
4.2 0.33/m
Pronunciation: / daÉªËˆluËt / (AmE), / daÉªËˆluËt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) made weaker or less concentrated by adding another substance, especially a solvent such as water.

Examples

Dilute bleach solution
Even dilute bleach must be handled with care to avoid skin irritation.
The chemist prepared a dilute acid solution for the experiment.
The flavor was very mild because the syrup was too dilute.

Synonyms

  • weak
    1.3 1.35/m
    weak describes a liquid or flavor that tastes thin and insipid, while dilute means made less concentrated by adding a solvent.
  • watered-down
    4.6 1.08/m
    watered-down describes a statement, policy, or product made weaker or less effective, while dilute refers to a liquid made less concentrated.

Related Terms
