dilute

adjective
made weaker or less concentrated
Pronunciation: / daɪˈluːt / (AmE), / daɪˈluːt / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) made weaker or less concentrated by adding another substance, especially a solvent such as water.

Examples

Dilute bleach solution
Even dilute bleach must be handled with care to avoid skin irritation.
The chemist prepared a dilute acid solution for the experiment.
The flavor was very mild because the syrup was too dilute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrated - (of a substance) having a higher strength or concentration
