immature

adjective
not fully developed; not ripe
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˈtʃʊr / (AmE), / ˌɪməˈtjʊə / (BrE)
not fully developed or mature; at an early stage of growth or development (used for people, animals, plants, or things)

Examples

Immature fruit
Because the wood was immature, it burned poorly and produced too much smoke.
The immature apples tasted sour and were not ready for picking.
The scientists studied the immature frogs before they transformed into adults.
Green bananas are still immature and should not be eaten yet.
Her behavior at the meeting was considered immature by her colleagues.
It's important to let children make mistakes; they're still immature and learning.
At this stage, the fruit is still immature and should not be eaten.
Children are naturally more immature than adults.
The immature bird was too young to leave the nest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fledged - (of a bird) having feathers and able to fly
  • antonym: mature - fully developed or ripe
  • synonym: unfledged - (of a bird) not yet able to fly or lacking feathers needed for flight
