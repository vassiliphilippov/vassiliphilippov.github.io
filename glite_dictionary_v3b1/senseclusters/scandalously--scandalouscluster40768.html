scandalously

adverb
in a shocking or shameful manner
7.7 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈskæn.də.ləs.liː/ /; / /ˈskæn.də.ləs.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈskæn.də.ləs.liː/ /; / /ˈskæn.də.ləs.lɪ/ (BrE)
In a way that causes general public outrage because of a perceived offense against morality or law.

Examples

To act scandalously
He was scandalously ignored by the committee despite being highly qualified.
The minister was scandalously overpaid for the little work he did.
They treated their employees scandalously, ignoring all labor protections.
