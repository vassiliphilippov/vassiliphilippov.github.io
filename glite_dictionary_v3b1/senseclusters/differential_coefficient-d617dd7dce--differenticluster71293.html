differential coefficient

noun
mathematical derivative, rate of change
5.0 7.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.fər.ˈen.ʃəl koʊ.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ /; / /dɪ.fər.ˈen.ʃəl koʊ.ɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ (AmE), /dɪ.fə.ˈren.ʃəl kəʊ.ə.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ /; / /dɪ.fə.ˈren.ʃəl kəʊ.ɪ.ˈfɪʃ.ənt/ (BrE)
the result of differentiating a function; the instantaneous rate of change of one quantity with respect to another, especially as expressed by the derivative df(x)/dx.

Examples

The differential coefficient of a function
Calculating the differential coefficient is a fundamental concept in calculus.
He found the differential coefficient of the curve to determine the rate of change at that instant.
The differential coefficient of a function gives the slope of the tangent at any point.
