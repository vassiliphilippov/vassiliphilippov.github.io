Protestant

noun
adherent of a Western Christian church (non-Catholic)
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊt̬.ɪ.stənt / (AmE), / ˈprɒt.ɪ.stənt / (BrE)
A member or follower of any of the Western Christian churches that are separate from the Roman Catholic Church and follow the principles of the Reformation.

Examples

A Protestant believer
Many Protestants celebrate Reformation Day as an important religious holiday.
She was raised as a Protestant in a small town in Germany.
The church service was attended by both Protestant and Catholic members.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Catholic - a member of the Roman Catholic Church
  • synonym: Christian - a follower of Christianity
