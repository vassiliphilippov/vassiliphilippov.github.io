big shagbark

noun
species of hickory tree with large nut
8.6
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ ˈʃæɡˌbɑrk / (AmE), / bɪɡ ˈʃæɡˌbɑːk / (BrE)
A species of hickory tree found in the eastern United States, similar to the shagbark hickory but bearing a much larger nut.

Examples

The big shagbark tree
The forest included several tall big shagbark trees, each producing an abundance of large nuts.
We identified the big shagbark by comparing its nuts to those of the regular shagbark hickory.
Wildlife in the area depend on the nuts from the big shagbark as a food source.

Related Terms
