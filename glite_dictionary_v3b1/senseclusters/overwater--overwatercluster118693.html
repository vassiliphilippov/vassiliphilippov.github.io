overwater

verb
to water excessively
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈwɔtər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to give too much water to a plant or soil, often causing harm

Examples

To overwater plants
If you overwater your houseplants, their roots may rot.
Gardeners should be careful not to overwater their lawns during the rainy season.
She realized she had overwatered the cactus when it started to turn yellow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underwater - to give too little water to a plant or soil
  • degree lower: water - to give water to plants or soil
