foreordain

verb
to decide or determine in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔrɔrˈdeɪn /; / ˌfɔːrɔrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ˌfɔːrɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to decide or determine something in advance, especially by a divine or supernatural power

Examples

To foreordain events
History often makes it appear as though major events were foreordained, though that is rarely the case.
Many believe that their destinies are foreordained by a higher power.
The outcome of the negotiations seemed almost foreordained from the start.
Many believe that their future is foreordained by a higher power.
Some religions teach that all events are foreordained and cannot be changed by human effort.
The outcome seemed foreordained, given the overwhelming odds.
