sulfur

verb
treat with sulfur to preserve
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌl.fɚ /; / ˈsʌlfər / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.fə /; / ˈsʌlfə / (BrE)
to treat or expose (something), especially food, with sulfur or sulfur dioxide, typically in order to preserve it or prevent discoloration

Examples

To sulfur dried fruit
Many wineries sulfur the barrels before use to prevent spoilage.
The packers sulfur the dried apricots to maintain their bright color.
The peaches were carefully sulfured before being stored for the winter.
After you sulphur the fruit, be sure to let it air out before storage.
It is common to sulphur grapes before drying them into raisins.
Many commercial dried apricots are sulphured to maintain their orange color.
