underloaded

adjective
less than full load
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈloʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈləʊdɪd / (BrE)
having less than the usual or required amount of load or weight

Examples

An underloaded truck
The delivery van was underloaded and could have transported more packages.
If the machine is underloaded, it may not operate efficiently.
The ferry left the dock underloaded because fewer passengers boarded than expected.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: loaded - carrying a normal or full amount of load
  • antonym: overloaded - having more than the usual or safe amount of load
