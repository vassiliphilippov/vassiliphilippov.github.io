Yenisei

proper noun
river in Siberia, Russia
Pronunciation: / ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ / (AmE), / ˌjɛnɪˈseɪ / (BrE)
a major river in Siberia, Russia, which flows northward from the Mongolian border to the Kara Sea.

Examples

The Yenisei River
Every spring, the Yenisei floods vast areas, supporting the region’s rich animal life.
The Yenisei is one of the longest river systems in the world, stretching over 3,400 miles.
The Trans-Siberian Railway crosses the Yenisei near the city of Krasnoyarsk.
