rough time of it

noun
to have difficulties or hardship
6.1
Pronunciation: / rʌf taɪm əv ɪt / (AmE), / rʌf taɪm əv ɪt / (BrE)
to experience difficulties or hardship over a period of time

Examples

Have a rough time of it
After the accident, Maria really had a rough time of it getting back to normal.
He’s been having a rough time of it ever since he lost his job.
Students often have a rough time of it adjusting to college life away from home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easy time of it - to experience little or no difficulty
  • synonym: hard time - to experience hardship or trouble
