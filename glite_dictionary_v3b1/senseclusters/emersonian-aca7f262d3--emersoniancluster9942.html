Emersonian

adjective
of Emerson's ideas
Pronunciation: / ˌɛmərˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌɛməsˈəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Ralph Waldo Emerson or his ideas, especially about individualism and nature

Examples

Emersonian philosophy
The poet's Emersonian outlook was clear in his celebration of self-reliance.
Her philosophy class discussed the Emersonian belief in the spiritual connection between humans and nature.
Many writers adopted an Emersonian approach to individual freedom in their essays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transcendentalist - relating to the philosophy of transcendentalism, especially as promoted by Emerson
