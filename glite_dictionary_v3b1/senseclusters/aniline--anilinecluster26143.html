aniline

noun
poisonous liquid used in dyes and chemicals
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.əˌlin / (AmE), / ˈan.ɪliːn / (BrE)
a poisonous, oily liquid amine derived from nitrobenzene, used in making dyes, plastics, and medicines

Examples

Aniline chemical compound
Aniline is a key ingredient in the manufacture of synthetic dyes.
Exposure to aniline must be carefully controlled in chemical plants.
