overpopulation

noun
excessively dense population
1.0 4.3
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.pɑː.pjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /oʊ.vər.pɑː.pjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.pɒ.pjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /əʊ.və.pɒ.pjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The condition of having a population so dense that it causes environmental deterioration, impaired quality of life, or a population faster than the environment's carrying capacity.

Examples

The overpopulation problem
Many environmentalists warn that overpopulation can strain natural resources and lead to ecological crisis.
Policies were introduced to address the problem of overpopulation in certain regions.
Rapid urban overpopulation has resulted in housing shortages in major cities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underpopulation - the condition of having too few people for the available resources
