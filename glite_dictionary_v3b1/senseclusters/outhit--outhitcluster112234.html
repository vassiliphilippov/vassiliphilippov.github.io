outhit

verb
to hit farther
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈhɪt / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈhɪt / (BrE)
to hit a ball or object harder or farther than another person, especially in sports

Examples

To outhit every player
During the tournament, she managed to outhit every other player on the team.
If you want to win, you will have to outhit your opponent in the final round.
Despite his efforts, he could not outhit the champion in the home run contest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outscore - to score more points than another person or team
  • antonym: underperform - to perform less well than someone else
