huscarl

noun
medieval bodyguard
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌsˌkɑrl / (AmE), / ˈhʌsˌkɑːl / (BrE)
an armed member of a king or noble's personal bodyguard or household troops in medieval England or Scandinavia

Examples

A huscarl soldier
The loyal huscarl protected his king during the fierce battle.
Each huscarl was trained to fight with sword and shield.
A huscarl was often rewarded with land for his service.
The king's loyal huscarl stood guard outside the royal chamber all night.
Each nobleman was accompanied by at least one trusted huscarl during the journey.
In medieval times, a huscarl often managed both security and daily affairs in the manor.
