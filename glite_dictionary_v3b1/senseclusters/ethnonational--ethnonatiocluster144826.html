ethnonational

adjective
ethnic and national identity
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθnoʊˈnæʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌɛθnəʊˈnæʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to a specific ethnic group and its national identity, interests, or the idea that a nation should be defined by a single ethnic group

Examples

Ethnonational identity
The new law was criticized for its ethnonational focus, which excluded minority groups.

The party's ethnonational policies appealed to those who valued cultural homogeneity.
Debates about immigration often include ethnonational arguments regarding national identity.
Some historians study the rise of ethnonational movements in twentieth-century Europe.
