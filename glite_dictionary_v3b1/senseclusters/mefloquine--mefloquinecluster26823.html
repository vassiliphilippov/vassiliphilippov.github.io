mefloquine

noun
antimalarial drug
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛfləˌkwin / (AmE), / ˈmɛfləˌkwiːn / (BrE)
an antimalarial drug used to prevent and treat malaria, particularly in cases resistant to chloroquine

Examples

Mefloquine tablets
Because mefloquine can cause side effects, doctors may monitor patients closely during treatment.
Some patients experience vivid dreams or confusion while taking mefloquine for malaria prevention.
Travelers to certain regions are often prescribed mefloquine as a prophylactic medication against malaria.
