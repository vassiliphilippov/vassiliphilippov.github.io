grip

noun
firm hold or understanding
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪp / (AmE), / ɡrɪp / (BrE)
A firm or effective hold or understanding, especially of a subject, concept, or situation.

Examples

Grip on reality
Despite everything going on, he maintained his grip on the situation.
It took me a while to get a firm grip on what the professor was explaining.
She quickly developed a good grip on the basics of chemistry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command - complete mastery or understanding
  • synonym: grasp - firm hold or understanding (literal or figurative)
