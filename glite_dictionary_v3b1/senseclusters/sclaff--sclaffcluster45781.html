sclaff

verb
hit ground in golf shot
Pronunciation: / sklæf / (AmE), / sklæf /; / sklɑːf / (BrE)
(in golf) to hit the ground with the club before the ball, causing a poor shot

Examples

To sclaff the ball
Golfers often lose distance when they accidentally sclaff the ball during their swing.
He managed to sclaff his shot on the fairway, sending the ball only a few yards.
She groaned as she sclaffed the approach, and the ball barely left the ground.
He accidentally sclaffed his approach shot, and the ball barely made it onto the green.
If you sclaff the ball, you'll lose both distance and accuracy.
She has worked hard on her swing to avoid sclaffing during important rounds.
