sanitariness

noun
quality of promoting health or cleanliness
Pronunciation: / səˈnɛt̬.ə.ri.nəs / (AmE), / səˈnɪt.ər.i.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being conducive to health, especially by maintaining absence of dirt, infection, or disease

Examples

Proper sanitariness in hospitals
Proper sanitariness in hospitals is crucial to prevent the spread of infection.
The sanitariness of public restrooms varies greatly from one facility to another.
The sanitariness of the kitchen was inspected by the health department before opening.
