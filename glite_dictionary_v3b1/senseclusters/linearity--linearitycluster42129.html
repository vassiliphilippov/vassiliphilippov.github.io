linearity

noun
direct relationship
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪniˈærəti / (AmE), / ˌlɪniˈærəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a direct, straight-line relationship between two variables or elements

Examples

Linearity of a graph
The linearity of the graph indicates a constant rate of change between the two variables.
Engineers must check the linearity of sensors to ensure accurate measurements.
A lack of linearity in the data suggests a more complex relationship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonlinearity - the state of not having a direct or straight relationship
  • synonym: proportionality - the state of being in direct proportion
