gruff

adjective
deep and harsh-sounding (voice or sound)
5.2 4.6
Pronunciation: / ɡrʌf / (AmE), / ɡrʌf / (BrE)
(of a voice, manner, or person) rough, harsh, or unfriendly, often sounding low or stern

Examples

Gruff voice
After shouting all day, her speech became gruff and raspy.
The dog's gruff barking startled everyone in the house.
The old man greeted us with a gruff voice, making him seem intimidating at first.
Although his manner was gruff, he had a kind heart.
His gruff voice startled the children into silence.
She received a gruff reply when she asked for help.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brusque - abrupt or offhand in speech or manner
  • antonym: gentle - mild and kind in manner or speech
  • synonym: hoarse - rough and harsh in sound, usually due to illness or overuse
  • antonym: soft - gentle and not harsh in sound
