solid state

noun
state of matter with definite shape and volume
4.4 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÉ‘lÉªd steÉªt / (AmE), / ËˆsÉ’lÉªd steÉªt / (BrE)
The physical state of a substance in which it retains a definite shape and volume, resists deformation, and does not flow under moderate stress.

Examples

The solid state of matter
Iron at room temperature is typically found in the solid state.
Many electronic components rely on materials in the solid state for their function.
When water freezes, it changes from the liquid state to the solid state.

Synonyms

  • solid
    1.0 2.19/m
    solid is a common word for a firm substance seen in daily use, while solid state is the technical term for the physical phase that keeps shape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gaseous state - the state of matter without a fixed shape or volume, expands to fill any container
  • antonym: liquid state - the physical state in which a substance flows and takes the shape of its container
