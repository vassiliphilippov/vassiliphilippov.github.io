roll-up

noun
combined summary
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈrəʊl ʌp / (BrE)
a total or summary created by combining several smaller amounts or pieces of data, especially in accounting or reporting

Examples

Roll-up report
The finance team prepared a roll-up of expenses from all departments for the annual report.
Each branch submitted its figures, which were then included in the final roll-up for the company.
The manager reviewed the roll-up to understand the overall sales performance this quarter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggregate - a total amount formed by combining several quantities
  • antonym: breakdown - a detailed list showing separate parts of a total
