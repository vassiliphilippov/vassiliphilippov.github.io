unfrock

verb
remove clerical office or authority
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfrɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈfrɒk / (BrE)
to officially remove a priest or church official from their position or clerical status, usually for misconduct

Examples

To unfrock a priest
A formal decision to unfrock him was announced after a lengthy investigation.
It is rare for the church to unfrock a priest, but exceptions do occur.
The bishop was unfrocked after allegations of misconduct came to light.
After the scandal, the bishop was unfrocked and could no longer perform religious duties.
The church decided to unfrock the minister following a lengthy investigation.
Several priests have been unfrocked in recent years due to serious violations of church rules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defrock - to remove a member of the clergy from their office
  • antonym: ordain - to officially make someone a priest or minister
