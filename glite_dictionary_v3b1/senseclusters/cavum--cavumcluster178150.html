cavum

noun
anatomical hollow or cavity
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.vəm / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.vəm / (BrE)
(Anatomy) A natural hollow or cavity within the body, such as a sinus or chamber.

Examples

Nasal cavum
A detailed examination showed inflammation in the nasal cavum.
Some marine animals possess a specialized cavum for buoyancy regulation.
The MRI revealed an unusual enlargement in the cavum of the septum pellucidum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cavity - a hollow space within a solid object or the body
  • synonym: sinus - a cavity within a bone or other tissue, often in the skull or face
