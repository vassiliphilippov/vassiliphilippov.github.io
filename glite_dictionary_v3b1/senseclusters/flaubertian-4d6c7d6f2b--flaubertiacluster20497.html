Flaubertian

noun
follower of Flaubert
9.9
Pronunciation: / floʊˈbɛrtiən / (AmE), / flɔːˈbɛːtɪən / (BrE)
a person who is influenced by or imitates the literary style or outlook of Gustave Flaubert

Examples

A true Flaubertian
After reading Madame Bovary, she considered herself a true Flaubertian in her writing approach.
The professor described the author as a modern Flaubertian because of his attention to detail.
Many critics labeled the novelist a Flaubertian due to his realistic portrayal of everyday life.
