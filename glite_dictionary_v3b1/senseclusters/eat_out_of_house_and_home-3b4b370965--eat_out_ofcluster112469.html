eat out of house and home

verb
use up all someone's food/resources
Pronunciation: / it aʊt əv haʊs ənd hoʊm / (AmE), / iːt aʊt əv haʊs ənd həʊm / (BrE)
to consume so much of someone's food or resources that almost nothing is left, often unintentionally or through excessive appetite

Examples

To eat out of house and home often
His cousins always eat out of house and home when they come to stay for the summer.
If my roommates keep inviting friends over, they will eat out of house and home before the week is up.
Visitors during the holidays tend to eat out of house and home—I can barely keep the pantry stocked.
