stroud

noun
coarse woolen cloth
Pronunciation: / straʊd / (AmE), / straʊd / (BrE)
a coarse woolen cloth, especially one historically used in trade with Native Americans

Examples

A stroud cloth
The trader exchanged stroud for animal pelts with the local tribe.
Traditional garments were sometimes made from brightly colored stroud cloth.
Museums often display pieces of stroud to illustrate early trade goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blanket - a large piece of soft material used for warmth
  • synonym: broadcloth - a dense, plain-woven woolen cloth
