flaunt

verb
display ostentatiously; show off
3.5
Pronunciation: / flɔnt / (AmE), / flɔːnt / (BrE)
to show or display something in a very noticeable or obvious way, often to attract attention or admiration

Examples

To flaunt wealth
Instead of trying to blend in, he preferred to flaunt his unique style.
She loves to flaunt her designer handbags at every social event.
The actor did not hesitate to flaunt his wealth in front of reporters.
He was accused of flaunting the rules by parking in the reserved space.
She loves to flaunt her designer clothes at every opportunity.
The company flaunted its success with a lavish party.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or impressive
  • synonym: show off - to behave in a way intended to attract attention and admiration
