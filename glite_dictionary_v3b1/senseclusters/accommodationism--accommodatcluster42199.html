accommodationism

noun
policy allowing religion
Pronunciation: / əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or policy that government and public institutions should allow or support religious practices rather than strictly separating religion and state

Examples

Religious accommodationism law
The new law was criticized for promoting accommodationism by allowing religious symbols in public schools.
Debates over accommodationism often arise when governments consider religious holidays in official calendars.
Some argue that accommodationism respects religious freedom, while others see it as a threat to secularism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pluralism - a system that recognizes and affirms diversity, including religious diversity, in society
  • antonym: separationism - the belief that religion and government should be kept strictly separate
