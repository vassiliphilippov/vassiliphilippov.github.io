operational

adjective
working; in use; functioning
Pronunciation: / ˌɑː.pəˈreɪ.ʃən.əl /; / ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃənl / (AmE), / ˌɒp.ərˈeɪ.ʃən.əl /; / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃənəl / (BrE)
in use, working, or ready to be used (especially for machines, systems, or services)

Examples

Operational machines
After the storm, only a few power lines remained operational.
The hospital will not be operational until next week due to construction work.
The team ensured all equipment was operational before the event began.
All equipment must be fully operational before the factory can reopen.
Although the computer is old, it remains operational and useful for basic tasks.
The hospital's backup generator became operational just minutes after the power outage began.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broken - not working; out of order
  • synonym: functional - in working order; able to operate; serving a purpose
  • synonym: functioning - working properly; operating as intended
  • antonym: inactive - not active or not working
  • synonym: usable - able to be used; fit for use
