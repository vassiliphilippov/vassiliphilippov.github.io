pre-election

noun
period before election
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː ɪˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriː ɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
the period of time immediately before an election, often marked by campaigning and political activity

Examples

The pre-election period
The country was tense during the pre-election as candidates made their final appeals to voters.
Many important debates take place in the pre-election period to help people decide whom to support.
Media coverage increases significantly in the pre-election weeks leading up to voting day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: campaign season - the time when candidates campaign before an election
  • antonym: post-election - the period after an election has taken place
