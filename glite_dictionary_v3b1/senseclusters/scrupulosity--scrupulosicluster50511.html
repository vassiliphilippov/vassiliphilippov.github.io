scrupulosity

noun
obsessive moral doubt
9.9
Pronunciation: /skruː.pjə.ˈlɑː.sɪ.tɪ/ /; / /skruː.pjʊ.ˈlɑː.sɪ.tɪ/ /; / /skruː.pjə.ˈlɔː.sɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /skruː.pjʊ.ˈlɒs.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /skruː.pjə.ˈlɒs.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
excessive or obsessive concern with being morally or religiously correct, often causing anxiety or repeated checking

Examples

Religious scrupulosity
Her scrupulosity led her to confess minor mistakes repeatedly, fearing she had done something wrong.
People with scrupulosity may spend hours each day performing rituals to ease their anxiety.
The therapist recognized his scrupulosity as a form of obsessive-compulsive disorder focused on moral issues.
His scrupulosity made him double-check every action to avoid any possible wrongdoing.
People with scrupulosity may struggle with constant guilt over minor mistakes.
The priest recognized her scrupulosity and offered reassurance about her moral concerns.
