edgy

adjective
nervous or tense
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdʒi / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒi / (BrE)
nervous, tense, or easily unsettled

Examples

An edgy crowd
After hours of waiting, the crowd became increasingly edgy.
His edgy mood made everyone around him uncomfortable.
She always gets edgy before giving a big presentation.
She felt edgy before her big presentation at work.
The movie's edgy style attracted a lot of attention from critics.
His jokes are often edgy, pushing the boundaries of what is acceptable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bold - showing a willingness to take risks; confident and courageous
  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other strong emotions
  • synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; anxious
  • antonym: relaxed - free from tension and anxiety
  • synonym: tense - feeling nervous or unable to relax
