quarter

verb
to execute by pulling apart with horses
9.9 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkw…îÀêrd…ôr / (AmE), / Ààkw…îÀêt…ô / (BrE)
(historical) To execute someone by tying each of their limbs to a horse and pulling them apart.

Examples

To be quartered alive
In medieval times, traitors were often quartered as a form of public execution.
Prisoners who rebelled against the king were drawn and quartered for their crimes.
The gruesome practice of quartering has long been abolished.
