offstage

adverb
behind or away from the stage area
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
behind or to the side of a stage and not visible to the audience; away from the area where actors perform

Examples

Moved offstage quickly
Props were quickly moved offstage during the scene change.
She was nervous while standing offstage before her performance began.
The actors waited offstage for their cue to enter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: onstage - on or towards the stage, visible to the audience
