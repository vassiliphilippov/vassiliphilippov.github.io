semistarvation

noun
partial deprivation
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌstɑrˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌstɑːˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of having much less than the necessary amount of something essential, often used metaphorically for deprivation.

Examples

Cultural semistarvation
The community faced cultural semistarvation after the closure of its only library.
Years of budget cuts led to educational semistarvation in the district's schools.
Without new ideas, the organization suffered from creative semistarvation and lost its momentum.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • synonym: deprivation - the lack of something necessary
  • degree higher: starvation - extreme lack of food or essentials
