Milesian

noun
early Greek philosopher
Pronunciation: / maɪˈliːʒən / (AmE), / maɪˈliːzɪən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of the ancient Greek city of Miletus, or a follower of the Milesian school of early Greek philosophy

Examples

A Milesian thinker
Thales is often considered the first Milesian to propose a natural explanation for the origin of the world.
The Milesian philosophers were known for their interest in the fundamental substance of the universe.
Anaximenes, another Milesian, believed that air was the primary element of all things.
The philosopher Thales was a famous Milesian known for his contributions to science.
Artifacts discovered in the ruins revealed much about daily life for a typical Milesian.
During the festival, every Milesian wore a blue cloak as a sign of local pride.
