lance

noun
barbed tool for catching fish
6.4 5.4
Pronunciation: /læns/ /; / /lɑːns/ (AmE), /lɑːns/ /; / /læns/ (BrE)
a long, pointed weapon or tool used for piercing, such as by soldiers, for fishing, or in surgery

Examples

A lance weapon
Ancient cultures often relied on the lance for spearfishing in shallow rivers.
He sharpened the barb of his lance before heading out onto the ice to fish.
The fisherman skillfully threw his lance to catch a large salmon.
A lance is often preferred for making a quick puncture in the skin.
The surgeon sterilized the lance before using it to drain the abscess.
With a swift movement, she used the lance to make a tiny incision.
In museums, you can often see a medieval lance displayed alongside armor.
Modern surgeons sometimes use a lance to open abscesses quickly and cleanly.
The knight lowered his lance and prepared to charge at his opponent.
