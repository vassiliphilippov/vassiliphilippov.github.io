dead duck

noun
something doomed to failure
Pronunciation: / dɛd dʌk / (AmE), / dɛd dʌk / (BrE)
a person or thing that is certain to fail or be unsuccessful

Examples

A dead duck project
After the funding was cut, the project became a dead duck overnight.
Many experts considered the merger proposal a dead duck from the start.
Once public opinion turned, the politician's campaign was a dead duck.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hopeless case - a person or thing with no hope of success
  • synonym: lost cause - a person or thing that can no longer succeed or be changed
  • antonym: success - the accomplishment of an aim or purpose
