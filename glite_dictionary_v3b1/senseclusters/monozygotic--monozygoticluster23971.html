monozygotic

adjective
derived from a single fertilized egg
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəzaɪˈɡɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊzaɪˈɡɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of twins) derived from a single fertilized egg and therefore genetically identical.

Examples

Monozygotic twins
Only monozygotic twins are always of the same sex, sharing identical genetic makeup.
The probability of monozygotic twinning is not influenced by family history.
The researchers compared the health records of several sets of monozygotic twins to study genetic factors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dizygotic - (of twins) derived from two separate fertilized eggs
