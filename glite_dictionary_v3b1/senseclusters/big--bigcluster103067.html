big

adjective
self-important; showing self-importance
Pronunciation: / bɪɡ /; / bɪg / (AmE), / bɪɡ /; / bɪg / (BrE)
behaving in a self-important or boastful way; acting as if one is more important than others

Examples

Act big
He dislikes people who act big just because they have a little authority.
Her big attitude quickly alienated her coworkers.
They were full of big talk but too nervous to follow through.
Don’t get big just because everyone is complimenting your work.
Ever since his promotion, he's acted a bit too big around the office.
She became big after winning the award, but her friends soon noticed the change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance
  • antonym: modest - having a humble view of one's own importance
