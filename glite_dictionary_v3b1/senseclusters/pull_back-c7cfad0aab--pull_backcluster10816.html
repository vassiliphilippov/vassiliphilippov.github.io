pull back

verb
move or draw something backward
1.0
Pronunciation: / pʊl bæk /; / pʊl ˈbæk / (AmE), / pʊl bæk /; / pʊl ˈbæk / (BrE)
to move or withdraw something or oneself backward or away from a position, situation, or commitment

Examples

To pull back quickly
As the ball came toward his face, he instinctively pulled back his head.
He told her to pull back the curtain so the sunlight could enter the room.
The troops received orders to pull back from the front line.
A retractor can be used to pull back the muscle away from the bone.
The surgeon used forceps to pull back the skin and expose the underlying tissue.
We need to pull back the wound edges to gain better access for the procedure.
Investors may pull back from risky markets during uncertain times.
She quickly pulled back her hand from the hot stove.
The army was forced to pull back after heavy losses.
