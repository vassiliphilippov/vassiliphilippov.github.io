oppositive

adjective
expressing opposition or antithesis
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑzətɪv / (AmE), / əˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or construction) expressing opposition, antithesis, or contrast.

Examples

An oppositive relationship
An oppositive relationship in a sentence shows contrast between two ideas.
He used an oppositive structure to highlight the antithesis in his argument.
The conjunction 'but' in the phrase 'poor but happy' is an oppositive word.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adversative - expressing contrast or opposition (especially in grammar)
