interactive

adjective
capable of mutual influence or action
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.t̬ɚˈæk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈræk.tɪv / (BrE)
(of two or more things) capable of acting on or influencing each other.

Examples

Interactive effects
Researchers are studying the interactive effects of genes and environment on health.
The chemicals are interactive and form new compounds when mixed.
Their interactive relationship benefits both companies in the partnership.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independent - not depending on each other; acting or functioning separately
  • synonym: reciprocal - given, felt, or done in return
