cough

verb
expel air noisily from the lungs
Pronunciation: / kɑf / (AmE), / kɒf / (BrE)
to expel air suddenly and noisily from the lungs, usually to clear the throat or as a symptom of illness

Examples

To cough loudly

He tried not to cough during the quiet performance, but couldn't hold it in.
The doctor asked if he had coughed up any phlegm.
