contrived

adjective
deliberately created, artificial
Pronunciation: / kənˈtraɪvd / (AmE), / kənˈtraɪvd / (BrE)
deliberately created and not natural or spontaneous; artificial or forced

Examples

A contrived apology
His apology sounded contrived and insincere.
The movie's happy ending felt a bit contrived to most viewers.
The plot relies on several contrived coincidences to bring the characters together.
His apology seemed so contrived that no one believed he was truly sorry.
She adopted a contrived air of innocence whenever the topic came up.
The dialogue in the play was criticized for being contrived and unnatural.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artificial - made or produced by human beings rather than occurring naturally
  • antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature; not artificial
  • synonym: stilted - stiff, self-conscious, or unnatural (especially in speech or writing)
