Congo copal

noun
fossil resin from Congo, used in varnishes
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːŋɡoʊ koʊˈpæl / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡəʊ kəʊˈpæl / (BrE)
A hard resin obtained chiefly as a fossil from the Congo region, used in making varnishes and jewelry.

Examples

Fossilized Congo copal
Fossilized Congo copal can be polished and used to make beautiful jewelry.
Scientists analyze Congo copal to study ancient plant life from the Congo region.
The craftsman used Congo copal as a key ingredient in his traditional varnish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copal - natural resin, often semi-fossilized and used in varnish
