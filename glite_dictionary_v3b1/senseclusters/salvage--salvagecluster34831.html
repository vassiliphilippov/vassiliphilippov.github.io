salvage

noun
rescue of ship, crew, or cargo from danger
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsæl.vɪdʒ /; / ˈsælvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsæl.vɪdʒ /; / ˈsal.vɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act of saving or recovering something from danger, destruction, or loss (such as a ship, goods, or property)

Examples

Salvage operation
During the storm, the coast guard organized a salvage operation to recover the cargo.
The salvage of the sunken ship’s treasure took several weeks to complete.
They received a reward for their salvage efforts after rescuing the crew from the burning vessel.
Firefighters coordinated the salvage of documents from the burning office building.
The crew managed the salvage of valuable cargo from the sinking ship.
The insurance company discussed the terms of salvage after the flood.
