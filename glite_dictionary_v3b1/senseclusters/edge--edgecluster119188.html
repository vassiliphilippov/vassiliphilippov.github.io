edge

noun
slight competitive advantage
3.5
Pronunciation: / ɛdʒ / (AmE), / ɛdʒ / (BrE)
a slight advantage that makes someone more likely to succeed than others in a competition or situation

Examples

Competitive edge
Innovative technology can provide a crucial edge in a rapidly changing market.
The team had a psychological edge thanks to their last-minute goal.
Years of experience gave her an edge over other candidates for the job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position
  • antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable condition or circumstance that reduces the chances of success
