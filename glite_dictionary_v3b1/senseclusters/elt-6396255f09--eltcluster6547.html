ELT

noun
emergency locator device
7.5
Pronunciation: /iː el tiː/ /; / /elt/ (AmE), /iː el tiː/ /; / /elt/ (BrE)
a device on an aircraft or boat that automatically sends a distress signal to help rescuers locate it in an emergency

Examples

Aircraft ELT
The pilot checked the ELT before takeoff to ensure it was working properly.
After the crash, the ELT automatically sent a distress signal to search and rescue teams.
Every commercial aircraft is required to have a functioning ELT on board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beacon - a device that emits a signal to guide or warn
  • synonym: transponder - an electronic device that transmits a signal in response to receiving one
