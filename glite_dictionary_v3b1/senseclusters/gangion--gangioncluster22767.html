gangion

noun
fishing line with hook
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæn.dʒi.ən / (AmE), / ˈɡæn.dʒi.ən / (BrE)
a short piece of fishing line used to attach a hook to a main fishing line or net

Examples

A gangion line
The fisherman attached a fresh bait to each gangion before lowering the longline into the sea.
If a gangion breaks, it is easy to replace without disturbing the rest of the fishing gear.
Inspect each gangion for damage to ensure the hooks stay secure during fishing.
The fisherman attached a new gangion to each hook before casting his net.
Each gangion must be strong enough to hold a large fish without breaking.
She checked every gangion for wear and tear before heading out to sea.

  • antonym: mainline - the primary fishing line to which gangions are attached
  • synonym: snood - a short line connecting a fishing hook to the main line
