connotatively

adverb
in an implied sense
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːnəˈteɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ˌkɒnəˈteɪtɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the implied or associated meaning of a word or phrase, rather than its literal meaning

Examples

Used connotatively in language
The poet uses the word 'rose' connotatively to represent love and beauty.
When describing the city, she spoke connotatively to evoke feelings of nostalgia.
In literature, words are often chosen connotatively to add deeper meaning to the text.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denotatively - in a way that relates to the literal or primary meaning of a word
