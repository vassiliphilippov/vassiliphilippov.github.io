oversensitivity

noun
excessive sensitivity
6.7
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.sen.sɪ.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.sen.sɪ.ˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the condition of being excessively sensitive, especially in a device or system, causing it to react to very weak signals or produce errors.

Examples

Oversensitivity of the sensor
The oversensitivity of the alarm system caused it to go off even when a leaf fell nearby.
Engineers adjusted the sensor to reduce its oversensitivity to minor temperature changes.
Because of its oversensitivity, the device often gave false readings in noisy environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypersensitivity - an abnormally high degree of sensitivity
  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity or responsiveness
