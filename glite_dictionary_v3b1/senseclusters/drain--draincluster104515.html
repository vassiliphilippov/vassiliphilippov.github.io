drain

verb
gradually use up (resources, energy, etc.)
3.4 9.92/m
Pronunciation: / dreÉªn / (AmE), / dreÉªn / (BrE)
to gradually use up or reduce energy, strength, money, or resources; to make someone tired or weak

Examples

To drain your energy
Long meetings can drain your mental energy if you are not careful.
Parenting can sometimes drain you of both time and patience.
The repair costs drained our savings much faster than we expected.
Caring for a newborn is rewarding but can also drain you of all strength.
Long meetings tend to drain my enthusiasm for the project.
The intense heat of summer can drain your energy completely.
