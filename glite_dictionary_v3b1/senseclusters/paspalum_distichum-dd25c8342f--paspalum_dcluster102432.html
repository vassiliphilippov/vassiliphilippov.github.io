Paspalum distichum

noun
a low-growing weedy grass species
Pronunciation: / ˌpæspələm dɪˈstɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌpaspələm dɪˈstɪkəm / (BrE)
A low-growing weedy grass found in wet areas, with spikelets arranged along the leaf stems; also known as knotgrass.

Examples

Paspalum distichum grass
Flooded rice fields often become overrun with Paspalum distichum.
Gardeners consider Paspalum distichum a troublesome weed in many regions.
The banks of the stream were densely covered in Paspalum distichum.
