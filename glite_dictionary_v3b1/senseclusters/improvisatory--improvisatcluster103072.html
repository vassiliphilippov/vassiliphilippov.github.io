improvisatory

adjective
spontaneous; unplanned
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɑːvəzəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɒvɪzətəri / (BrE)
done or created without prior planning or preparation, often spontaneously

Examples

An improvisatory performance
The actor gave an improvisatory performance that surprised the entire audience.
Her speech had an improvisatory quality, as if she was thinking aloud.
The meeting took an improvisatory turn when the agenda was set aside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvised - created or performed without preparation
  • antonym: planned - done with careful preparation
