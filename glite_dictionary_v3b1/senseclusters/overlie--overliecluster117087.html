to lie upon or on top of something, especially in physical or geological contexts

Examples

Snow overlies the ground
Sheets of snow overlie the mountain peaks throughout the winter.
The newer deposits overlie the fossil-rich limestone beneath.
Thick layers of sediment overlie much older rocks in this region.

Synonyms

  • overlay
    4.2 0.95/m
    overlay covers a surface with another thin or visible layer often placed by people, while overlie describes lying naturally on top.
  • lay over
    3.5 0.59/m

  • overspread
    7.1 0.07/m
    overspread spreads widely across a surface like paint or dust, while overlie refers to lying on top in layers.
