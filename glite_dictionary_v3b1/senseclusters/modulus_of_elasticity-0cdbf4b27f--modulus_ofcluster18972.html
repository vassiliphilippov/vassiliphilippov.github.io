modulus of elasticity

noun
measure of a material's stiffness
5.6 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑd.jəl.əs əv ɪˌlæstɪˈsɪti / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.jʊ.ləs əv ɪˌlæs.tɪˈsɪt.i / (BrE)
A measure of the stiffness of a material, defined as the ratio of the applied stress to the resulting strain within the elastic limit.

Examples

The modulus of elasticity value
A low modulus of elasticity indicates that a material will deform easily under stress.
The modulus of elasticity of steel is much higher than that of rubber.
When designing bridges, engineers must calculate the modulus of elasticity for each material used.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Young's modulus - A specific modulus of elasticity relating to tensile or compressive stress and strain
