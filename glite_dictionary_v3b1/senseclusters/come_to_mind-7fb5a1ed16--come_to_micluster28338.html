Synonyms

  • come
    1.5 64/m
    come is used when an idea enters your mind naturally, while come to mind specifically means you suddenly think of or remember it.
  • come to
    3.1 32/m
    come to often describes a sudden idea or realization arriving, while come to mind focuses on having that idea or memory.
  • occur
    2.4 20/m
    occur means an idea appears in someone's mind suddenly and often formally, while come to mind is more everyday and neutral.
  • spring to mind
    3.4 0.46/m
    spring to mind suggests an idea comes quickly and easily to you, while come to mind is a general sudden thought or memory.
  • pop into
    3.9 0.83/m
    pop into describes a brief sudden thought that appears and passes, while come to mind can mean a thought stays or recurs.
  • come to think of it
    5.3 0.58/m
    come to think of it introduces a speaker's new or recalled idea in conversation, while come to mind simply names a thought appearing.
