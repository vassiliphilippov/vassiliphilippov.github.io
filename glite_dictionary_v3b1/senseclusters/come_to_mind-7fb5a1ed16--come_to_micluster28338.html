come to mind

verb
to be thought of or remembered suddenly
Pronunciation: / kʌm tə maɪnd / (AmE), / kʌm tə maɪnd / (BrE)
if an idea, thought, or memory comes to mind, you suddenly think of it or remember it

Examples

Ideas come to mind
A few possible solutions come to mind if we think this through carefully.
Her name immediately came to mind when I heard about the job opening.
Whenever I smell fresh bread, childhood memories come to mind.
Her kindness is the first thing that comes to mind when I hear her name.
No good reason came to mind when he was asked to explain his absence.
Whenever I think of summer, long days at the beach come to mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to be unable to remember something
  • synonym: occur to - (of an idea or thought) to enter one's mind
