hypomanic

noun
person with mild mania
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈmænɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈmanɪk / (BrE)
a person experiencing a mild episode of mania, marked by increased energy, activity, or euphoria

Examples

A hypomanic talks fast
The psychiatrist observed that the hypomanic was unusually talkative and energetic during the session.
After several days of little sleep, the hypomanic began to make impulsive decisions at work.
Support groups can help a hypomanic manage their elevated mood and maintain stability.
The psychiatrist observed that the patient was clearly hypomanic during the interview.
As a hypomanic, she felt unusually energetic and talked rapidly for hours.
Doctors monitor a hypomanic closely to prevent escalation to full mania.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depressed - a person experiencing low mood and energy
  • degree higher: manic - a person experiencing a full episode of mania
