hapten

noun
small immune-triggering molecule
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhæp.tən/ /; / /ˈhæp.tɪn/ (AmE), /ˈhæp.tən/ /; / /ˈhæp.tɪn/ (BrE)
a small molecule that can cause an immune response only when attached to a larger carrier protein

Examples

A hapten molecule
A hapten by itself cannot trigger an immune response unless it binds to a carrier protein.
Scientists used a hapten to study allergic reactions in laboratory animals.
Some drug allergies occur when a medication acts as a hapten in the body.

  • synonym: antigen - a substance that causes the immune system to produce antibodies
