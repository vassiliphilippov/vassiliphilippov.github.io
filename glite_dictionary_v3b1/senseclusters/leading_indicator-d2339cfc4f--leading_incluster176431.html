leading indicator

noun
economic statistic predicting trends
1.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈɪn.də.keɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈɪn.də.keɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an economic statistic that tends to change before the economy as a whole changes, used to predict future economic trends

Examples

Economic leading indicator
Economists carefully analyze leading indicators such as building permits and new orders to forecast future growth.
Rising consumer confidence can serve as a leading indicator of economic expansion.
The stock market is often considered a leading indicator because it generally reacts before the broader economy does.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coincident indicator - an economic indicator that moves at the same time as the economy
  • antonym: lagging indicator - an economic indicator that changes after the economy has already begun to follow a particular trend
