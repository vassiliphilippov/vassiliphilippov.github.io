glitch

noun
minor fault in a machine or system
4.3
Pronunciation: / ɡlɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɡlɪtʃ / (BrE)
A sudden, usually minor fault or defect in a machine, system, or computer program that causes it to function incorrectly or unexpectedly.

Examples

A computer glitch
Due to a minor glitch in the system, the payment was processed twice.
Engineers worked overnight to resolve the unexpected glitch in the power grid.
The software update fixed the glitch that caused the program to crash.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bug - an error or defect in software or hardware
  • synonym: malfunction - failure to function properly
