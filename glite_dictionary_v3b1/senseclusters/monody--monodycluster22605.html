monody

noun
music for single voice with accompaniment
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.nə.di / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ə.di / (BrE)
A musical composition for a single vocal part, typically with instrumental accompaniment.

Examples

A monody song
Early Baroque composers were pioneers in developing monody as a dramatic musical style.
Modern audiences are often surprised by the expressive nature of Renaissance monody.
The soprano performed a haunting monody accompanied only by a lute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polyphony - music with two or more independent melody lines
