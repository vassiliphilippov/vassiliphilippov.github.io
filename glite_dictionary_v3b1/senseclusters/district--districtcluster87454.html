district

verb
divide into districts for regulation
4.1 4.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈstrɪkt/ /; / /ˈdɪs.trɪkt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈstrɪkt/ /; / /ˈdɪs.trɪkt/ (BrE)
to divide an area into districts, especially for administrative, political, or official purposes

Examples

To district a city
After the new policy, the town was districted to separate residential and commercial zones.
The city council decided to district the suburb to control the types of housing built in each area.
Urban planners are currently districting the city to improve access to public transportation.
The government districted the region to improve local administration.
After the census, the state was districted to reflect population changes.
Officials districted the city to ensure each area had proper representation.
The government is districting the city to ensure fair representation in the council.
After the census, officials began districting the region based on the new population data.
Controversy arose when the committee proposed districting that favored one political party.
