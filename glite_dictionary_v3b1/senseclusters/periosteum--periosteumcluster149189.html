periosteum

noun
membrane covering bones (except joints)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛriˈɑstiəm / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈɒstɪəm / (BrE)
the dense fibrous membrane covering the surface of bones except at the joints, serving as an attachment for tendons and muscles and containing nerves and blood vessels that nourish the bone

Examples

The periosteum membrane
Damage to the periosteum can result in pain and slower healing after a fracture.
During surgery, the periosteum must be carefully lifted to access the underlying bone.
The periosteum plays an essential role in bone growth and repair.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endosteum - a thin vascular membrane lining the inner surface of bones
