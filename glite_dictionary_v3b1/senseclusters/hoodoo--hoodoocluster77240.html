hoodoo

noun
unusually shaped rock column
Pronunciation: / ˈhuːduː / (AmE), / ˈhuːduː / (BrE)
a tall, thin spire of rock that protrudes from the bottom of an arid basin or badland, formed by erosion and weathering

Examples

A rocky hoodoo
After centuries of erosion, only a single slender hoodoo remained standing near the cliff edge.
Geologists studied the layers of rock exposed in the hoodoo formations of Bryce Canyon.
The trail wound past a towering hoodoo that cast a long shadow across the desert floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pillar - a tall, vertical structure of stone; may refer to a natural rock formation or a man-made column
