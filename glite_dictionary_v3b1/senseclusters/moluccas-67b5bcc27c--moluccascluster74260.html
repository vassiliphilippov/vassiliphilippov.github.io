Moluccas

proper noun
island group in eastern Indonesia; Spice Islands
8.3
Pronunciation: / məˈlʌkəz / (AmE), / məˈlʌkəz / (BrE)
a group of islands in eastern Indonesia, historically known as the Spice Islands, important for the spice trade and formerly controlled by the Portuguese and Dutch

Examples

The Moluccas islands
Many spices exported from the Moluccas reached Europe via long trade routes.
Nutmeg and cloves were once found almost exclusively in the Moluccas.
The Portuguese were among the first Europeans to colonize the Moluccas.
