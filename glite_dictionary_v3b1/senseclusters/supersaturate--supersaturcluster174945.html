supersaturate

verb
to overfill a solution
8.2
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.ˈsæ.tʃə.reɪt/ (AmE), /suː.pə.ˈsæ.tʃə.reɪt/ (BrE)
to add so much of a substance to something (especially a liquid) that it holds more than it normally can, often leading to excess or overload

Examples

To supersaturate a solution
Chemists often supersaturate solutions to study crystal formation under controlled conditions.
It is possible to supersaturate sugar in water by heating and then slowly cooling the mixture.
If you supersaturate the solution, even a small disturbance can cause rapid crystallization.
If you supersaturate a solution with sugar, crystals will eventually form as the excess settles out.
Media companies sometimes supersaturate the airwaves with advertisements during major events.
The market became supersaturated with similar products, making it hard for any brand to stand out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flood - to fill or cover something with a large amount of something
  • synonym: overload - to put too much of something into or onto something
  • degree lower: saturate - to cause a solution to hold as much dissolved substance as possible
  • antonym: underwhelm - to fail to impress or excite; to provide less than expected
