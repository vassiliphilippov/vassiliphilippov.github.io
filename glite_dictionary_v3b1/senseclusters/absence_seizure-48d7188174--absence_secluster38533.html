absence seizure

noun
brief seizure with impaired consciousness
7.1 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.səns ˈsi.ʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈæb.səns ˈsiːʒə / (BrE)
a brief, sudden episode of impaired consciousness, often involving subtle movements such as blinking or twitching, common in certain types of epilepsy (especially petit mal)

Examples

An absence seizure episode
During an absence seizure, he often stared blankly into space for a few seconds without responding.
Most absence seizures last less than 20 seconds and may go unnoticed by those around the patient.
The doctor diagnosed her with an absence seizure after she experienced several brief lapses in attention at school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: petit mal - a mild form of epilepsy characterized by brief lapses in consciousness
