logarithmic

adjective
relating to logarithms
Pronunciation: / ˌlɑːɡəˈrɪðmɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɒɡəˈrɪðmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or using logarithms, especially to describe growth, scales, or mathematical functions involving logarithms

Examples

Logarithmic growth
Her research explores logarithmic growth in biological populations.
Radioactive decay is an example of a process that can be described by a logarithmic function.
The chart displayed data on a logarithmic scale to better visualize the differences in magnitude.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exponential - growing at a constant rate per unit time; related to exponents, often contrasted with logarithmic
