A language in which the direct object of a transitive verb and the subject of an intransitive verb are treated the same way, usually marked with the absolutive case, while the subject of a transitive verb is marked differently with the ergative case.

Examples

Ergative-absolutive language example
Basque is a well-known example of an ergative-absolutive language studied by linguists.
In an ergative-absolutive language, the object and intransitive subject share the same grammatical marking.
Many indigenous languages of Australia display patterns consistent with an ergative-absolutive language.
