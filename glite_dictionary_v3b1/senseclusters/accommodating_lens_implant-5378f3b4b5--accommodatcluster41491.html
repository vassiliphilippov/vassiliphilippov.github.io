accommodating lens implant

noun
artificial lens implant allowing near and far vision
6.0
Pronunciation: / əˌkɑməˌdeɪtɪŋ lɛnz ˈɪmplænt / (AmE), / əˌkɒməˌdeɪtɪŋ lɛnz ˈɪmplɑːnt / (BrE)
a type of artificial lens implanted in the eye, designed with a hinge mechanism to allow for both near and far vision, mimicking the function of a natural young lens

Examples

An accommodating lens implant surgery
After surgery with an accommodating lens implant, she was able to read without her glasses again.
Recent advances in accommodating lens implant technology have helped restore natural vision for many cataract patients.
The patient opted for an accommodating lens implant to improve both his near and distance vision.
