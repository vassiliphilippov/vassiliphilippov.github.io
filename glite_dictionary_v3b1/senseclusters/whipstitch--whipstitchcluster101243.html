whipstitch

noun
diagonal sewing stitch over fabric edge

A simple sewing stitch made by passing the needle in and out over the edge of fabric in a diagonal pattern, typically used to join two pieces or to finish raw edges.

Examples

A whipstitch edge
A whipstitch is often used to close the opening on handmade pillows.
For finishing hems, the whipstitch provides both strength and a decorative touch.
She secured the seam with a neat whipstitch all along the edge of the fabric.

Synonyms

  • whipping
    6.9 0.7/m

  • overcast
    9.3 0.00/m
    overcast is a longer edge stitch often sewn continuously to stop unraveling, while whipstitch is a simple diagonal stitch to join fabric edges.
