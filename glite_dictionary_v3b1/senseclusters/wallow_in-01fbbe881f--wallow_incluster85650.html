wallow in

verb
indulge yourself in emotions or memories
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑloʊ ɪn / (AmE), / ˈwɒləʊ ɪn / (BrE)
to indulge oneself in an emotion, situation, or memory, especially when it is unpleasant or self-pitying, without making an effort to change it

Examples

To wallow in self-pity
After the breakup, he spent weeks wallowing in self-pity at home.
Sometimes it's tempting to wallow in your problems instead of facing them head-on.
You can't wallow in regrets forever if you want to move forward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dwell on - to think or talk about something for a long time, often unpleasant
  • antonym: move on - to stop being upset or preoccupied with something and continue with life
