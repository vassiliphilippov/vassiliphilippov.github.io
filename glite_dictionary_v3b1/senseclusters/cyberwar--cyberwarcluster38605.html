cyberwar

noun
influence-focused digital warfare
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.bər.wɔːr/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.bər.wɑːr/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.bə.wɔː/ (BrE)
(in political and security contexts) coordinated use of digital platforms, networks, and tools to influence, deceive, or undermine public opinion, political processes, or social trust, often through disinformation, manipulation, or large-scale influence operations.

Examples

Election cyberwar
Analysts warned that the upcoming election would be targeted by a new cyberwar of false narratives.
Scholars argue that modern cyberwar often centres on manipulating public opinion rather than destroying infrastructure.
The investigators found evidence the campaign was part of a wider cyberwar aimed at undermining voter confidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: information warfare - the strategic use of information, including propaganda and disinformation, to influence or disrupt adversaries
  • antonym: kinetic warfare - traditional physical warfare using weapons and forces; contrasted with non-physical cyber or information operations
