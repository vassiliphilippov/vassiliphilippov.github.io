peel off

verb
come away in thin layers or flakes
3.9 0.63/m
Pronunciation: / pil …ëf /; / pil …îf / (AmE), / piÀêl …íf / (BrE)
for a covering or surface to come away or be removed in strips or flakes, often due to age or damage

Examples

Old paint peels off
After years of neglect, the old wallpaper began to peel off in large strips.
The paint will peel off if you don't apply a primer first.
The sunburn caused the skin on his shoulders to peel off.
After staying in the sun too long, my skin began to peel off in small flakes.
His lips were so dry that they would sometimes peel off in tiny scales.
The old paint has started to peel off the walls.

Synonyms

  • peel
    3.0 4.86/m
    peel describes a surface coming off in thin strips or flakes, while peel off often stresses the action of removing those strips.
  • flake off
    3.9 0.30/m
    flake off means small pieces break away from a surface, while peel off suggests larger strips coming away.
  • flake
    2.6 1.08/m
    flake means to come away in thin flat pieces, while peel off refers to stripping off larger or longer pieces.
  • exfoliate
    6.2 0.98/m
    exfoliate is a formal term for shedding thin layers, while peel off is the everyday way to describe that shedding.
  • come off
    3.5 4.69/m
    come off means to become detached or separated, while peel off implies coming away specifically in strips or flakes.
