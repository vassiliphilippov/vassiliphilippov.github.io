peel off

verb
come away in thin layers or flakes
4.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /piːl ɔːf/ (AmE), /piːl ɒf/ /; / /piːl ɔːf/ (BrE)
for a covering or surface to come away or be removed in strips or flakes, often due to age or damage

Examples

Old paint peels off
After years of neglect, the old wallpaper began to peel off in large strips.
The paint will peel off if you don't apply a primer first.
The sunburn caused the skin on his shoulders to peel off.
After staying in the sun too long, my skin began to peel off in small flakes.
His lips were so dry that they would sometimes peel off in tiny scales.
The old paint has started to peel off the walls.
