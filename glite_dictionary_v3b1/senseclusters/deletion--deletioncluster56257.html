deletion

noun
loss of nucleotides in a chromosome
Pronunciation: / dɪˈliʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈliːʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) The loss or absence of one or more nucleotides from a chromosome, resulting in a genetic change.

Examples

Gene deletion
A deletion in this gene can cause a serious genetic disorder.
Scientists discovered a deletion on chromosome 5 in the sample.
The disease was traced to a single base pair deletion in the DNA sequence.
