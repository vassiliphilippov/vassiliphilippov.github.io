Lake Ladoga

proper noun
the largest lake in Europe, in northwestern Russia
Pronunciation: / leɪk ˈlædəɡə / (AmE), / leɪk ˈlædəɡə / (BrE)
a large lake in northwestern Russia, north of St. Petersburg, and the largest lake in Europe, draining through the Neva River into the Gulf of Finland.

Examples

Lake Ladoga in Russia
During World War II, the Siege of Leningrad relied on supplies transported across Lake Ladoga.
Many rivers and streams flow into Lake Ladoga from the surrounding countryside.
Tourists are often struck by the sheer size and natural beauty of Lake Ladoga.
