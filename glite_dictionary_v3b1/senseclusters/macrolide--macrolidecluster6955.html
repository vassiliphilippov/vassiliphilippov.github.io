macrolide

noun
antibiotic class
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmæk.rəˌlaɪd / (AmE), / ˈmak.rəʊˌlaɪd / (BrE)
a type of antibiotic with a large ring-shaped molecular structure, used to treat bacterial infections

Examples

Macrolide antibiotic
The doctor prescribed a macrolide to treat the infection.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to macrolides, making treatment more difficult.
Erythromycin is a well-known macrolide used for respiratory infections.

Related Terms
