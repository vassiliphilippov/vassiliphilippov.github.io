runner

noun
horizontal plant stem producing new plants
8.1 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌn.ər/ (AmE), /ˈrʌn.ə/ (BrE)
A horizontal stem or branch, usually growing along the surface of the soil, that extends from the base of a plant and produces new plants from buds at its tips.

Examples

Strawberry runner
Gardeners often cut off runners to prevent overcrowding and encourage larger fruit.
Spider plants reproduce by producing long runners with little plantlets at their ends.
The strawberry plant spreads quickly as it sends out runners that root in the soil to form new plants.
