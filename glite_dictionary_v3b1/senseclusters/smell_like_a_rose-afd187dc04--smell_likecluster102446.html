smell like a rose

verb
emerge favorably after trouble
8.6
Pronunciation: / smɛl laɪk ə roʊz / (AmE), / smɛl laɪk ə rəʊz / (BrE)
to appear or emerge in a favorable or unimpaired state, especially after a difficult or problematic situation

Examples

To smell like a rose after trouble
After handling the crisis, the company came out smelling like a rose.
Despite the controversy, he managed to smell like a rose in the end.
No matter the problem at work, she always seems to smell like a rose.
