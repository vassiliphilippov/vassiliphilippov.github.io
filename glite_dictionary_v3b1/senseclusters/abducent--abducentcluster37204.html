abducent

noun
sixth cranial nerve of the eye
9.9
Pronunciation: /æb.ˈduː.sənt/ /; / /æb.ˈdjuː.sənt/ (AmE), /æb.ˈdjuː.sənt/ /; / /æb.ˈduː.sənt/ (BrE)
a cranial nerve (the sixth nerve) that controls the lateral rectus muscle of the eye, responsible for moving the eye outward

Examples

Abducent nerve damage
Damage to the abducent may cause an inability to move the eye laterally.
The abducent is frequently examined in patients with double vision.
The medical student learned that the abducent controls the lateral rectus muscle of the eye.
