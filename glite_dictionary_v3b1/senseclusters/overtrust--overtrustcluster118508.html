overtrust

noun
excessive trust
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtrʌst / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtrʌst / (BrE)
excessive or unwarranted trust in someone or something, often resulting in mistakes or negative consequences

Examples

Blind overtrust
Her overtrust in her business partner led to a costly mistake.
The company's overtrust in outdated data caused them to make poor decisions.
Parents should be careful of overtrust when leaving children unsupervised online.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distrust - lack of trust or confidence in someone or something
  • degree lower: trust - belief in the reliability or truth of someone or something
