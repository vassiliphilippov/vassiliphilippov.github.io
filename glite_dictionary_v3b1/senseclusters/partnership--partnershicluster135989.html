partnership

noun
business contract for shared resources and profit/loss
1.7 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːr.nər.ʃɪp/ /; / /ˈpɑːr.tə.nər.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ˈpɑː.tə.nə.ʃɪp/ /; / /ˈpɑːt.nə.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
a business arrangement or the group of people who run a business together and share profits or losses

Examples

A business partnership
After the dissolution of their partnership, both lawyers started independent practices.
Each partnership must file annual tax returns documenting shared profits and losses.
They entered into a partnership to open a new restaurant downtown.
All six lawyers became part of the partnership after the merger.
Our partnership decided to expand into new markets this year.
The accounting firm is one of the oldest partnerships in the city.

  • antonym: sole proprietor - an individual who owns and runs a business alone
