covalent bond

noun
type of chemical bond involving electron sharing
3.0
Pronunciation: / koʊˈveɪlənt bɑnd / (AmE), / kəʊˈveɪlənt bɒnd / (BrE)
a chemical bond formed by the sharing of one or more pairs of electrons between atoms within a molecule

Examples

A covalent bond shares electrons
A covalent bond differs from an ionic bond in how electrons are distributed.
A water molecule is held together by a covalent bond between hydrogen and oxygen atoms.
In organic chemistry, the covalent bond is fundamental to the structure of most compounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ionic bond - chemical bond formed by the transfer of electrons between atoms
  • synonym: metallic bond - chemical bond involving free electrons shared among a lattice of metal atoms
