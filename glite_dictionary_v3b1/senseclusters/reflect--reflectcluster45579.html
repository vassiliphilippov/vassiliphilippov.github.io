reflect

verb
send back light, sound, etc. from a surface
1.6 2.2
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈflekt/ /; / /rə.ˈflekt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈflekt/ /; / /rə.ˈflekt/ (BrE)
to send back light, heat, sound, or an image from a surface (like a mirror or water)

Examples

Mirrors reflect light
Mirrors reflect light to allow you to see your own image.
The surface of the lake reflected the mountains perfectly on a calm day.
The tall buildings reflected the sound of the city, making it even louder.
Polished metal surfaces reflect a lot of light, making a room brighter.
The calm lake reflects the mountains perfectly in its still water.
The glass windows reflect sunlight during the afternoon, creating a glare.
Her sunglasses reflected his face as he leaned in closer.
The calm lake reflected the mountains perfectly, making the scenery look twice as beautiful.
The shop window reflected the lights of passing cars on the wet street.
