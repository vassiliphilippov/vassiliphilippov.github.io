To make (a substance) volatile; to cause to pass off as vapor.

Examples

To volatilize a liquid
Certain chemicals volatilize easily at room temperature, posing inhalation risks.
Heating the solution will volatilize any remaining solvent.
The laboratory process volatilizes metals for accurate measurement.
Heating the chemical too rapidly may volatilise it.
The process can volatilise hazardous substances, releasing them into the air.
When exposed to sunlight, some pesticides volatilise and become airborne.
