field strength unit

noun
unit measuring magnetic field intensity
Pronunciation: / fild strɛŋθ ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / fiːld strɛŋθ ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
an electromagnetic unit used to measure the intensity of a magnetic field

Examples

Field strength unit measurement
Physicists often convert measurements from one field strength unit to another, such as from gauss to tesla.
The tesla is the SI field strength unit used in scientific research.
Understanding the correct field strength unit is crucial when comparing results from different experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gauss - a unit of magnetic induction in the CGS system
  • synonym: tesla - the SI unit of magnetic flux density
