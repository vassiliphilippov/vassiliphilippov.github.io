lady

noun
woman of the British nobility
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈleɪdi / (BrE)
A woman who holds the rank of peerage in Britain, such as the wife or female equivalent of a lord, or a woman who holds the title in her own right.

Examples

Lady Diana
As a lady of the realm, she was entitled to attend royal ceremonies.
Lady Diana became internationally famous as a lady in the British peerage.
The title of lady was passed to her after her father's death.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lord - a man of the British peerage
