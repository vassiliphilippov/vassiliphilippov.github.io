polished lumber

noun
smooth, finished wood
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlɪʃt ˈlʌmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt ˈlʌmbə / (BrE)
Wood that has been smoothed and finished, typically by sanding or polishing, for use in construction or crafts.

Examples

Polished lumber surface
He prefers working with polished lumber because it requires less preparation before assembly.
Sunlight reflected off the surface of the polished lumber on the deck, highlighting its rich color.
The furniture in the showroom was crafted from polished lumber, giving it a sleek and elegant appearance.
