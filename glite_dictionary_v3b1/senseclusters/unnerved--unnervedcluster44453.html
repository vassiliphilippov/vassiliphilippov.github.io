unnerved

adjective
deprived of courage or confidence
5.8 0.23/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàn…úrvd / (AmE), /  ånÀàn…úÀêvd / (BrE)
deprived of courage or confidence; made to feel nervous or fearful.

Examples

Feeling unnerved by news
After receiving the unexpected news, he looked completely unnerved.
She felt unnerved by the strange noises coming from the empty hallway.
The judge's quiet stare left the witness unnerved and hesitant to speak further.

Synonyms

  • frightened
    1.0 0.06/m
    frightened is clearly afraid or alarmed, while unnerved means losing courage or confidence and feeling shaky in the face of fear.
  • unstrung
    7.6 0.12/m
    unstrung is deeply shaken and unable to think calmly after stress, while unnerved means losing confidence and feeling nervous.
  • disconcerted
    6.9 0.04/m
    disconcerted is unsettled and losing composure because of surprise, while unnerved means losing confidence and feeling nervous or fearful.
  • fazed
    6.5 0.01/m
    fazed is mildly disturbed or unsettled by an event in casual speech, while unnerved implies a stronger loss of confidence or calm.
  • perturbed
    6.4 0.04/m
    perturbed is anxious or worried in a formal or thoughtful way, while unnerved is more about sudden loss of confidence and fear.
  • consternated
    8.4 0.01/m
    consternated is shocked into sudden dismay and confusion by something unexpected, while unnerved means being made nervous and losing confidence.
