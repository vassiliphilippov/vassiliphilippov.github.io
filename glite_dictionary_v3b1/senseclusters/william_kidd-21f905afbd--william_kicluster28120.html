William Kidd

proper noun
historical Scottish sea captain and alleged pirate
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəm kɪd / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm kɪd / (BrE)
(1645–1701) A Scottish sea captain, originally hired to protect British shipping in the Indian Ocean, later accused of piracy and executed.
