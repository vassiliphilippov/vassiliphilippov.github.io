conceit

noun
excessive pride or self-importance
8.0 0.88/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsit / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsiÀêt / (BrE)
excessive pride in yourself or your abilities; thinking you are very important or skilled

Examples

His conceit shows
His constant conceit made it difficult for others to work with him.
She was criticized for her conceit and unwillingness to accept advice.
The artist's conceit was evident in the way he talked about his own work.
Her conceit made her unpopular with her classmates.
His conceit blinded him to his own faults.
There was an air of conceit in the way he spoke about his achievements.

Synonyms

  • pridefulness
    7.4 0.01/m
    pridefulness means having an overly high opinion of oneself, while conceit focuses on seeing your own skills as very important.
  • self-importance
    6.2 0.66/m
    self-importance is an exaggerated sense of one's value, while conceit is pride in your abilities or status.
  • vanity
    5.6 3.78/m
    vanity is pride about your looks or achievements, while conceit covers a general, often skill-based, self-importance.
  • vainness
    6.9 0.00/m
    vainness means being proud of appearance or success, while conceit can mean an overall belief in one's superiority.
  • vainglory
    9.9 0.06/m
    vainglory is boastful pride about achievements, while conceit is a more general, inward feeling of being superior.
  • arrogance
    4.8 8.47/m
    arrogance shows a rude, superior attitude toward others, while conceit is more about silently thinking oneself better.
  • grandiosity
    7.9 0.11/m
    grandiosity is an exaggerated, often unrealistic feeling of greatness, while conceit is everyday overconfidence in your skills.
  • egotism
    6.1 1.19/m
    egotism is talking too much about yourself, while conceit is an inner belief that you are more skilled or important.
  • narcissism
    8.1 2.53/m
    narcissism is strong self-focus and need for admiration, while conceit is simply excessive pride in yourself or your abilities.
  • self-love
    7.0 0.04/m
    self-love is positive care or pride in oneself, while conceit is an excessive, often boastful, belief in one's worth.
