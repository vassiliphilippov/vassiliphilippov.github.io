Tayalic language

noun
language family of indigenous Taiwan
9.9
Pronunciation: / taɪˈælɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / taɪˈælɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A member of a group of Austronesian languages spoken by indigenous peoples of Taiwan, particularly the Atayal people.

Examples

Tayalic language family
Linguists have studied the Tayalic language to learn about Austronesian migration.
The Tayalic language is spoken chiefly in the mountains of northern Taiwan.
The Atayal people use the Tayalic language in traditional ceremonies and daily conversation.
