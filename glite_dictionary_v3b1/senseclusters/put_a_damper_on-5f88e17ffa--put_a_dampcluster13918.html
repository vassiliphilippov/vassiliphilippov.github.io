put a damper on

verb
reduce enthusiasm or enjoyment
4.8
Pronunciation: / pʊt ə ˈdæmpər ɑn / (AmE), / pʊt ə ˈdæmpə ɒn / (BrE)
to make something less enjoyable, successful, or exciting; to reduce enthusiasm or joy about something

Examples

To put a damper on fun
Heavy traffic put a damper on our plans to arrive early at the event.
His harsh criticism put a damper on what should have been a happy occasion.
The unexpected announcement put a damper on the entire conference.
