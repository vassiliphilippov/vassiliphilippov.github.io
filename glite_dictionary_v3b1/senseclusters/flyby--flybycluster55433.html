flyby

noun
ceremonial aircraft pass
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbaɪ / (BrE)
an occasion when an aircraft or spacecraft passes close to a place, often for observation or as a display

Examples

Jet flyby display
The crowd cheered as the jets performed a dramatic flyby during the parade.
The spacecraft completed a successful flyby of the distant planet, sending back valuable images.
At the opening ceremony, a flyby by vintage planes thrilled the spectators.

Related Terms

  • antonym: landing - the act of an aircraft coming down to the ground
  • synonym: overflight - a flight of an aircraft over a particular area
