dogfight

verb
to engage in an aerial battle (aircraft)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɡˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡˌfaɪt / (BrE)
to engage in a close-range aerial battle between fighter aircraft

Examples

To dogfight in planes
Many ace pilots became famous for their ability to dogfight during World War II.
The two squadrons began to dogfight high above the battlefield as the ground troops advanced.
Young aviators train for years before they are allowed to dogfight in real combat missions.
The two pilots began dogfighting as soon as they spotted each other over the desert.
During the training mission, the squadron spent hours dogfighting to improve their skills.
Modern fighter jets are equipped with technology that changes the way pilots dogfight in the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerial combat - fighting between aircraft in the air
  • antonym: retreat - to withdraw from battle or combat
