monosynaptic

adjective
single-synapse reflex
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnoʊsɪˈnæptɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊsɪˈnæptɪk / (BrE)
involving or relating to a reflex or neural pathway with only one synapse between two neurons

Examples

A monosynaptic reflex
The knee-jerk reflex is a classic example of a monosynaptic reflex in humans.
Researchers studied the speed of monosynaptic transmission in the spinal cord.
A monosynaptic pathway allows for a very rapid response to certain stimuli.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polysynaptic - involving more than one synapse in a neural pathway
