weasel-worded

adjective
using evasive or ambiguous language
5.5 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈwiː.zəl ˈwɜːr.dɪd/ /; / /ˈwɪz.əl ˈwɜːr.dɪd/ (AmE), /ˈwiː.zəl ˈwɜːd.ɪd/ /; / /ˈwɪz.əl ˈwɜːd.ɪd/ (BrE)
Using language that is deliberately vague or ambiguous in order to avoid making a direct or clear statement.

Examples

Weasel-worded statement
Critics called the company's statement weasel-worded and lacking in genuine accountability.
Her weasel-worded apology left everyone unsure if she actually regretted her actions.
The politician gave a weasel-worded response that avoided any real commitment to the issue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct - using clear and straightforward language
  • synonym: evasive - deliberate attempt to avoid giving a clear answer
