taste

noun
discrimination in aesthetic matters
2.9 3.3
Pronunciation: /teɪst/ (AmE), /teɪst/ (BrE)
the ability to recognize and appreciate what is beautiful, stylish, or appropriate; a preference or liking for something

Examples

Good taste in clothes
His taste for minimalist décor sets his apartment apart from others.
She has excellent taste in music and always discovers new artists before anyone else.
Wearing bright green to a funeral would be considered in poor taste.
His taste in art is quite sophisticated and modern.
Over the years, my taste for spicy food has become stronger.
She has a taste for classical music and attends concerts regularly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bad taste - lack of aesthetic judgment or appropriateness
  • synonym: discernment - the ability to judge well
  • antonym: dislike - a feeling of distaste or aversion
  • synonym: penchant - a strong or habitual liking for something or tendency to do something
  • synonym: preference - a greater liking for one alternative over others
  • synonym: refinement - cultivated taste or elegance
