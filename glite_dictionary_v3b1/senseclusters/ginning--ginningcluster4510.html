ginning

noun
animal trapping
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɪn.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɪn.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of trapping animals using a gin, which is a type of snare or mechanical trap

Examples

Ginning rabbits
Early settlers spent the winter ginning small game to supply their families with meat.
The practice of ginning was common among trappers in the nineteenth century.
Laws now restrict ginning to protect certain wildlife species from being caught in traps.
