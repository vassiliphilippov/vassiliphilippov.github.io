daylight savings

noun
setting clocks ahead in summer for more evening light
6.4 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdeɪ.laɪt ˈseɪ.vɪŋz/ (AmE), /ˈdeɪ.laɪt ˈseɪ.vɪŋz/ (BrE)
the practice of setting clocks one hour ahead of standard time during the summer months to extend evening daylight

Examples

Daylight savings time
I always forget to change my clocks when daylight savings begins in the spring.
Some countries do not observe daylight savings and keep the same time all year round.
The evenings feel much longer after daylight savings starts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: standard time - the official local time for a region as established by law or custom
