ecumenicity

noun
religious openness
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkjuːməˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌiːkjuːməˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of promoting unity, cooperation, or openness among different religious groups or denominations

Examples

Ecumenicity promotes unity
The conference promoted ecumenicity by inviting leaders from many different faiths.
Her ecumenicity made her a respected figure in interfaith dialogues.
A spirit of ecumenicity can help reduce religious conflict in diverse communities.
The ecumenicity of the conference attracted participants from many different countries and faiths.
Her ideas gained influence because of their ecumenicity and relevance to a wide audience.
The organization's ecumenicity helped bridge gaps between various religious communities.
The ecumenicity of the council was evident in the participation of many denominations.
Leaders praised the ecumenicity shown during the interfaith service.
Her work focused on increasing ecumenicity among local churches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ecumenism - the movement to promote unity among Christian churches
  • antonym: particularity - the quality of being specific or limited in scope
  • antonym: sectarianism - strong support for the beliefs of one's own religious group and intolerance of others
  • synonym: tolerance - acceptance of different opinions or beliefs
  • synonym: universality - the quality of being true or applicable everywhere
