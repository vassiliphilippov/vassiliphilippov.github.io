diuretic drug

noun
drug that increases urine flow
7.1 3.1
Pronunciation: /daɪ.uː.ˈre.tɪk drʌg/ /; / /daɪ.ə.ˈre.tɪk drʌg/ (AmE), /daɪ.jə.ˈre.tɪk drʌg/ /; / /daɪ.ə.ˈre.tɪk drʌg/ (BrE)
A drug or substance that increases the flow of urine, helping the body eliminate excess water.

Examples

A diuretic drug increases urine
Prolonged use of a diuretic drug should always be monitored by a healthcare professional.
Some patients may need to take a diuretic drug to control high blood pressure.
The doctor prescribed a diuretic drug to help reduce swelling in her ankles.
