Synonyms

  • noiseless
    3.5 0.2/m
    noiseless describes something that makes no sound at all, while silent is the general word for being without noise.
  • soundless
    4.0 0.5/m
    soundless describes a complete lack of sound, while silent is the common word for being without noise in many situations.
  • mute
    4.1 2/m
    mute often means unable or chosen not to speak, while silent mainly means without sound and does not focus on speech.
  • hushed
    4.1 2/m
    hushed describes voices or places made very quiet on purpose, while silent means completely without sound in general.
  • still
    1.0 80/m
    still often means not moving and calm, while silent focuses only on the lack of sound rather than lack of movement.
