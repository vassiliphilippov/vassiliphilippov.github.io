ecstatic state

noun
trance-like state from intense devotion
4.3 7.1
Pronunciation: /ek.ˈstæt.ɪk steɪt/ /; / /ɪk.ˈstæt.ɪk steɪt/ (AmE), /ek.ˈstæt.ɪk steɪt/ /; / /ɪk.ˈstæt.ɪk steɪt/ (BrE)
a trance-like condition brought on by intense religious devotion, characterized by overwhelming emotion but not by reduced bodily functions as in other trances

Examples

An ecstatic state of joy
During meditation, she entered an ecstatic state that left her feeling deeply connected to the divine.
Some religious rituals are designed to help participants achieve an ecstatic state through chanting and movement.
The monk described his ecstatic state as a powerful spiritual experience unlike anything else.
