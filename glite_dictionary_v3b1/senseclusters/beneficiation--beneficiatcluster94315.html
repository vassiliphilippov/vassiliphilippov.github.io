beneficiation

noun
ore processing to extract valuable minerals
6.0 8.5
Pronunciation: /bə.nə.fɪ.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ /; / /be.nɪ.fɪ.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /bə.nə.fɪ.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ /; / /be.nɪ.fɪ.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of improving raw materials (such as ore or data) by separating, cleaning, or enriching them to increase their value or usefulness

Examples

Beneficiation of ore
After extraction, the ore undergoes beneficiation to separate valuable metals from impurities.
Modern beneficiation techniques help reduce environmental impact by minimizing waste.
The mining company invested in advanced beneficiation facilities to increase the yield from its ore.
The project emphasizes beneficiation of consumer data to improve targeted marketing.
Our analytics team built a pipeline for beneficiation, combining cleaning, matching, and enrichment steps.
Data beneficiation increased the dataset's value, letting stakeholders run more accurate predictive models.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data cleaning - the process of detecting and correcting (or removing) corrupt or inaccurate records from a dataset
  • synonym: data curation - organizing and maintaining data to ensure its long-term value and usability
  • synonym: data enrichment - the process of enhancing raw data by adding missing or supplementary information
  • synonym: ore-dressing - the process of separating valuable minerals from waste rock
  • degree higher: smelting - extracting metal from its ore by heating and melting
