repulse

verb
drive back or force away
8.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpʌls / (AmE), / rɪˈpʌls / (BrE)
to drive back or force away a person, attack, or feeling

Examples

To repulse an attack
She tried to repulse all negative thoughts during the competition.
The army managed to repulse the invaders at the city gates.
The security team repulsed several attempts to breach the building.
Quick action by the police helped to repulse the would-be robbers.
The castle guards successfully repulsed several waves of invaders during the night.
The soldiers managed to repulse the enemy's attack at dawn.
