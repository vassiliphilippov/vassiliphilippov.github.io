Sassanid

proper noun
ancient Persian dynasty
Pronunciation: / ˈsæsənɪd / (AmE), / ˈsæsənɪd / (BrE)
a member of the ancient Persian dynasty that ruled Iran from 224 to 651 AD

Examples

A Sassanid ruler
The Sassanid Empire was known for its achievements in art and architecture.
Many ancient coins from the Sassanid period have been discovered by archaeologists.
The Sassanid dynasty played a major role in the history of Persia before the rise of Islam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Achaemenid - a member of the earlier Persian dynasty before the Parthians
  • antonym: Parthian - a member of the ancient Iranian dynasty preceding the Sassanids
