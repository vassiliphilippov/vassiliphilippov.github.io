jank

verb
to glitch or stutter
Pronunciation: / dʒæŋk / (AmE), / dʒæŋk / (BrE)
to behave in a jerky, glitchy, or unreliable way, especially referring to software, video, or machinery performance.

Examples

The app janks often
The video player would often jank when trying to play high-resolution files.
If your game starts to jank, try lowering the graphics settings.
During the presentation, the animation janked and made it hard to follow the slides.
