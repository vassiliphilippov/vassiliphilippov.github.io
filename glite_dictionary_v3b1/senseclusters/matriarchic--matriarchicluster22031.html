matriarchic

adjective
female-led or matrilineal (societies/families)
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmeɪ.triˈɑːrk.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌmeɪ.triˈɑː.kɪk / (BrE)
(of societies or families) having a female as the head or having descent traced through the female line.

Examples

Matriarchic society
Anthropologists study matriarchic societies to compare them with patriarchal traditions.
In some matriarchic families, grandmothers hold significant authority over important decisions.
The tribe was known for its matriarchic structure, where property was inherited through the mother’s line.

Related Terms

  • antonym: patriarchic - (of societies or families) having a male as the family head or descent traced through the male line
