expressivism

noun
theory of expressing attitudes
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsprɛsɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪkˈsprɛsɪˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that certain utterances mainly express the speaker's feelings or attitudes rather than state facts

Examples

Linguistic expressivism
According to expressivism, saying 'Boo to theft!' shows disapproval rather than stating a fact.
Some philosophers argue that expressivism better explains how we use moral language in daily life.
In linguistics, expressivism focuses on how utterances reveal speakers' emotions or attitudes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptivism - the view that language primarily describes facts or states of affairs
  • synonym: emotivism - the view that moral statements express emotional attitudes
