visceral leishmaniasis

noun
parasitic disease affecting internal organs

Pronunciation: /ˈvɪs.ər.əl laɪʃ.mə.ˈnaɪ.ə.sɪs/ /; / /ˈvɪs.ər.əl liːʃ.mə.ˈnaɪ.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /ˈvɪs.ər.əl laɪʃ.mə.ˈnaɪ.ə.sɪs/ /; / /ˈvɪs.ər.əl liːʃ.mə.ˈnaɪ.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
A severe infectious disease caused by protozoan parasites, affecting internal organs such as the liver, spleen, and bone marrow.
