visceral leishmaniasis

noun
parasitic disease affecting internal organs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪsəɹəl ˌliːʃmænˈiəsɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɪsərəɫ ˌliːʃmænˈɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A severe infectious disease caused by protozoan parasites, affecting internal organs such as the liver, spleen, and bone marrow.

Examples

Visceral leishmaniasis infection
Doctors in the region are seeing more cases of visceral leishmaniasis due to increased sandfly activity.
Early diagnosis of visceral leishmaniasis is critical to prevent potentially fatal complications.
Treatment for visceral leishmaniasis requires specialized medications and hospital care.

  • synonym: kala-azar - another name for visceral leishmaniasis
