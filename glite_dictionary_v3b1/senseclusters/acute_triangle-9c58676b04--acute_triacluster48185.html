acute triangle

noun
triangle with all angles acute
4.0 3.2
Pronunciation: / əˈkjuːt ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ˈtraɪæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
A triangle in which all three interior angles are less than 90 degrees.

Examples

An acute triangle
Every acute triangle has three angles that measure less than 90 degrees.
If all the angles in a triangle are sharp and less than 90 degrees, it is called an acute triangle.
In geometry class, we learned to identify an acute triangle by looking at its angles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equilateral triangle - a triangle with all sides and all angles equal
  • antonym: obtuse triangle - a triangle with one obtuse angle (greater than 90 degrees)
  • antonym: right triangle - a triangle with one right angle (exactly 90 degrees)
