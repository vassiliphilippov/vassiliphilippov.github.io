dodecanoic acid

noun
saturated fatty acid in natural oils
Pronunciation: / ˌdoʊdɛˈkeɪnoʊɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌdəʊdɛˈkeɪnəʊɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a crystalline saturated fatty acid, occurring in natural fats and oils, especially coconut oil and palm-kernel oil

Examples

Dodecanoic acid in coconut oil
Dodecanoic acid is widely used in the production of soaps and shampoos due to its cleansing properties.
Coconut oil contains a high percentage of dodecanoic acid, making it a valuable raw material for the cosmetic industry.
Researchers measured the amount of dodecanoic acid present in the palm-kernel oil samples.
