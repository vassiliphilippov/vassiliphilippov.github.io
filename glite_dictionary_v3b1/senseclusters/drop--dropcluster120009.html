drop

noun
sudden decrease in quantity or value
1.3
Pronunciation: / drɑp / (AmE), / drɒp / (BrE)
a sudden and significant decrease in the amount, value, or level of something

Examples

A sharp drop
After the announcement, the stock market experienced a sharp drop.
The drop in oil prices had a major impact on the economy.
There was a sudden drop in temperature overnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dip - a slight downward movement or reduction in level or amount
  • synonym: fall - a downward movement or reduction in number, amount, or value
  • antonym: increase - an instance of growing or becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
