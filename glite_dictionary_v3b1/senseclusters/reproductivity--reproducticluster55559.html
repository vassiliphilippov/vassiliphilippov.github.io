reproductivity

noun
ability to be copied
Pronunciation: / ˌriːprəˌdʌkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˌdʌkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality of an image, recording, or design that allows it to be copied or printed accurately and faithfully

Examples

Reproductivity of images
The reproductivity of the photograph was impressive, with every detail appearing sharp in the print.
High reproductivity is essential when creating digital archives of historical documents.
The artist chose materials known for their reproductivity to ensure consistent results in every copy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fidelity - the degree to which something is accurately reproduced
