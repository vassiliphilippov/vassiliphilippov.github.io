agger

noun
Roman embankment
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡər / (AmE), / ˈæɡə / (BrE)
a raised embankment or rampart, especially one built by the ancient Romans as part of a road or fortification

Examples

Roman agger
The ancient agger protected the city from invading armies.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a Roman agger near the riverbank.
The road was built on a high agger to prevent flooding during heavy rains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embankment - a raised structure to hold back water or support a road
  • synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or embankment
