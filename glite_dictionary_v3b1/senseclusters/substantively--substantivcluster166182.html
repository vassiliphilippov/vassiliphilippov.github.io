substantively

adverb
in an essential way
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.stən.tɪv.iː/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.stən.tɪv.iː/ (BrE)
in a way that is important, meaningful, or relates to the essential substance of something

Examples

Substantively different ideas
The two proposals are substantively different, even though they look similar at first glance.
She contributed substantively to the discussion by offering new and important insights.
The law has not changed substantively in the past decade, despite several minor amendments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: essentially - in a way that relates to the most important or basic part
  • synonym: significantly - in a way that is important or noticeable
  • antonym: superficially - in a way that is only on the surface or not deep or important
