low-level

adjective
of low rank or importance
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊˌlɛvəl /; / ˈloʊ ˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlɛvəl /; / ˈləʊ ˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
of low rank, position, or importance within an organization or system

Examples

Low-level job
He started at a low-level position in the company and worked his way up.
It was a low-level meeting involving only junior staff members.
Many low-level employees were affected by the new policy changes.
After graduation, she accepted a low-level position at the company to gain experience.
Most low-level employees rarely interact with senior management directly.
The committee held only low-level discussions before making a decision.
