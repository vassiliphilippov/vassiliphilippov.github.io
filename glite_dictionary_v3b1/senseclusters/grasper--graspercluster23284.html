grasper

noun
animal holding appendage
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræspər / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːspə / (BrE)
an anatomical appendage, such as a pincer or claw, used for holding or seizing objects

Examples

Crab's grasper
The crab used its grasper to pick up pieces of food from the sand.
Some insects have a specialized grasper to hold onto their mates during reproduction.
The lobster's large grasper is powerful enough to crack open shells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claw - a curved, pointed appendage used for gripping or tearing
  • synonym: pincer - a paired appendage used for grasping or holding
