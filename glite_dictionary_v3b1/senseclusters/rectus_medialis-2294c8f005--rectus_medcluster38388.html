rectus medialis

noun
medial eye muscle
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktəs miˈdeɪliəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛktəs miˈdiːliːæs / (BrE)
the eye muscle whose contraction turns the eyeball toward the nose

Examples

rectus medialis muscle
Damage to the rectus medialis can cause difficulty turning the eye inward.
Surgery was performed to correct the alignment of the rectus medialis muscle.
The ophthalmologist examined the function of the rectus medialis to assess the patient's eye movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lateral rectus - the eye muscle that turns the eyeball outward, away from the nose
