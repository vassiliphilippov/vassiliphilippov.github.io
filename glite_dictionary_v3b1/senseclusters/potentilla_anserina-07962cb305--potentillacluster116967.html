Potentilla anserina

noun
species of perennial plant (silverweed)
Pronunciation: / poʊˌtɛn.tɪl.ə ˌæn.səˈriː.nə / (AmE), / pəʊˌtɛntɪlə ˌænsəˈriːnə / (BrE)
A low-growing perennial plant with leaves that are silvery underneath, found in northern parts of the United States, Europe, and Asia; also known as silverweed.

Examples

Potentilla anserina plant
Botanists collected samples of Potentilla anserina to study its silvery foliage and creeping roots.
Gardeners sometimes plant Potentilla anserina as ground cover due to its attractive silver leaves.
The Potentilla anserina thrives in wet meadows and along stream banks in northern climates.
