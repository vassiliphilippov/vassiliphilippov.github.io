bootful

noun
car trunk's worth
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbuːtfʊl / (BrE)
an amount that fills the boot (trunk) of a car, often used humorously or informally

Examples

A bootful of apples
They brought a bootful of groceries home from the market after the big sale.
After the picnic, we packed a bootful of empty bottles and trash to recycle.
She surprised her friends with a bootful of balloons for the party.
