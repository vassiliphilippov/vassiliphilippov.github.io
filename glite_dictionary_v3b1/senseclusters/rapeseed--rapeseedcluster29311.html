rapeseed

noun
seed of rape plant; source of edible oil
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪpˌsid /; / ˈreɪpˌsiːd / (AmE), / ˈreɪpˌsiːd / (BrE)
a plant whose seeds are used to produce oil for cooking, industry, or animal feed

Examples

Rapeseed oil

Farmers in this region grow rapeseed primarily for its valuable oil content.
The price of rapeseed has increased due to rising demand for vegetable oils.
The farmer planted rapeseed in the spring to harvest oil later in the year.
Large fields of blooming rapeseed turn bright yellow in early summer.
Many cooking oils are made from pressed rapeseed.

Related Terms
