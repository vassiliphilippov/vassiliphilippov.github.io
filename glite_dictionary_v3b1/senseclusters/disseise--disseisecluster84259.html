disseise

verb
to dispossess unlawfully
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsiːz / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːz / (BrE)
to dispossess someone of land or property, especially in an unlawful or forceful way

Examples

To disseise a tenant
The court ruled that the landowner had been disseised without proper legal process.
If you disseise someone, you may be required to return the property and pay damages.
After being disseised of their estate, the family sought justice through the courts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dispossess - to deprive someone of land or property
  • synonym: evict - to legally force someone to leave a property
  • antonym: restore - to return something to its original owner
