glassful

noun
the amount a glass holds
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlæsˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈɡlɑːsˌfʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to what a glass can contain.

Examples

A glassful of juice
After the marathon, she drank a whole glassful of orange juice.
He poured himself a glassful of water before sitting down to dinner.
Please measure out one glassful of milk for the recipe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bottleful - the amount that a bottle will hold
  • synonym: cupful - the amount that a cup will hold
