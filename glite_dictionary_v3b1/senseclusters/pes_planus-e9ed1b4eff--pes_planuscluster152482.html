pes planus

noun
medical term for flat foot
7.3
Pronunciation: / peɪs ˈplæn.əs / (AmE), / peɪs ˈplaː.nəs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the arch of the foot is abnormally flattened, causing the sole to come into near-complete or complete contact with the ground; flat foot.

Examples

Pes planus condition
People with pes planus may experience foot pain during prolonged standing or walking.
Special orthotic shoes can help manage the symptoms of pes planus.
The doctor diagnosed him with pes planus after examining his feet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flat foot - foot with little or no arch
