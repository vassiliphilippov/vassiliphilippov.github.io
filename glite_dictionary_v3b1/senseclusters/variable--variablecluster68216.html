variable

adjective
capable of being adjusted (esp. devices)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriəbəl /; / ˈvɛriəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbəl /; / ˈveəriəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to change or be changed; not consistent or fixed

Examples

Variable weather
The camera lens features a variable aperture for different lighting conditions.
This lamp comes with a variable brightness setting so you can change the mood easily.
We installed a variable resistor to control the current in the circuit.
Her mood can be quite variable from day to day, depending on her workload.
The weather in this region is notoriously variable, making it difficult to plan outdoor events.
They face variable costs each month due to fluctuating supply prices.
Test results can be variable depending on the method used.
The artist is known for her variable techniques and unpredictable style.
Weather conditions in the mountains are highly variable throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjustable - able to be adjusted or changed to fit requirements
  • antonym: consistent - always acting or behaving the same way
  • antonym: constant - remaining the same over time; not changing
  • synonym: diverse - showing a great deal of variety; very different
  • antonym: unchanging - not changing, remaining the same
  • synonym: unstable - liable to change rapidly or unpredictably
