clump

verb
gather in a cluster
2.0
Pronunciation: / klʌmp / (AmE), / klʌmp / (BrE)
to come together or gather into a compact group or cluster

Examples

People clump together
After the bell rang, the children began to clump together near the school entrance.
The sheep tend to clump in the shade when the sun gets too hot.
Wildflowers often clump along the riverbank during the spring months.
As the snow fell, it began to clump on the branches of the trees.
The children clumped around the teacher to see the experiment up close.
Wet sand tends to clump together, making it easier to build sandcastles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cluster - to come together in a group or cluster (verb)
  • antonym: scatter - to separate and move apart in different directions
