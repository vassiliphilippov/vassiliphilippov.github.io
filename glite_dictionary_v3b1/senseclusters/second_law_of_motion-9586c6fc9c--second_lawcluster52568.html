second law of motion

noun
Newton's law on force and momentum
4.7 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…õk.…ônd l…î  åv mo äÀà É…ôn / (AmE), / Ààsek.…ônd l…îÀê …ôv m…ô äÀà É…ôn / (BrE)
Newton's law stating that the rate of change of momentum of a body is proportional to the force applied and occurs in the direction of the force.

Examples

Newton's second law of motion
According to the second law of motion, a greater force is required to accelerate a heavier object.
Physics students must understand the second law of motion to solve problems involving acceleration and force.
The rocket's launch can be explained using Newton's second law of motion.

Synonyms

  • Newton's second law of motion
    4.5 0.01/m
    Newton's second law of motion is the formal full name used in textbooks and papers, while second law of motion is the shorter, common term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: first law of motion - Newton's law stating that an object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by force
  • synonym: third law of motion - Newton's law stating that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction
