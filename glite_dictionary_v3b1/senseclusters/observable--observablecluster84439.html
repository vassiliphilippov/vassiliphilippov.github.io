observable

noun
measurable property
4.4
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈzɜːr.və.bəl/ (AmE), /əb.ˈzɜː.və.bəl/ (BrE)
a property or quantity in science that can be measured or observed directly in an experiment

Examples

Physics observable
In quantum mechanics, an observable is a physical quantity that can be measured, such as energy or position.
Scientists record each observable during the experiment to analyze the results accurately.
The temperature of the solution is an important observable in chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: measurement - the process of determining the size, amount, or degree of something
  • synonym: variable - a quantity that can change or be changed in an experiment
