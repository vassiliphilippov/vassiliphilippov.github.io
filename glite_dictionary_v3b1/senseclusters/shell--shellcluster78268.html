shell

noun
hard outer covering of animals
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl / (AmE), / ʃɛl / (BrE)
the hard outer covering or case that protects animals, eggs, nuts, or objects

Examples

A turtle shell
Hermit crabs use empty shells for protection as they grow.
The beaches were scattered with colorful shells from various sea creatures.
The tortoise retracted its head inside its shell when it sensed danger.
Crabs regularly shed their old shell as they grow larger.
The hardness of a beetle’s shell protects it from many predators.
The turtle retreated inside its shell when it felt threatened by the dog.
After the tide went out, we searched the beach for seashells and found a large conch shell.
The shell of the snail was beautifully spiraled and very delicate.
The oyster's shell protected it from predators in the ocean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carapace - hard upper shell of turtles, crustaceans, etc.
  • synonym: case - a container or covering for something; housing
  • antonym: core - the central or inner part of something
  • synonym: exoskeleton - external skeleton supporting and protecting animal's body
  • synonym: husk - the outer covering of some seeds or fruits
  • synonym: shield - a broad piece of metal or other suitable material held by hand as protection against weapons
  • antonym: skin - outer layer of a human or animal's body
  • antonym: yolk - the yellow internal part of a bird's egg, surrounded by the white
