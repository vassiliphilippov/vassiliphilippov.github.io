logginess

noun
dull or sluggish state due to weariness
9.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀê…°i.n…™s / (AmE), / Ààl…í…°.i.n…™s / (BrE)
A state of feeling dull, sluggish, or listless, often as a result of tiredness or lack of energy.

Examples

Feeling logginess after lunch
After lunch, a wave of logginess made it hard to concentrate on my work.
He fought through logginess to finish writing his report before the deadline.
The humid weather added to her sense of logginess all afternoon.

Synonyms

  • listlessness
    6.8 0.19/m
    listlessness is a quiet lack of energy and interest, while logginess feels slow, dull, and heavy from tiredness.
  • grogginess
    4.8 0.12/m
    grogginess is a dazed, partly awake feeling often after sleep or drink, while logginess is general sluggishness and listlessness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alertness - the quality of being awake and attentive
  • synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
