scarer

noun
person who deliberately frightens others
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsker.ər/ (AmE), /ˈskeə.rə/ (BrE)
a person who deliberately frightens other people, especially as a performer in haunted attractions, Halloween events, or similar entertainments.

Examples

Haunted house scarer
The scarer leaped from behind the wall and sent half the visitors screaming.
Two professional scarers practiced timing their jumps to frighten guests at the preview.
Parents warned the children that a friendly scarer might pop out near the maze entrance.
