undirected graph

noun
graph with edges lacking direction
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.dəˈrɛk.tɪd ɡræf / (AmE), / ˌʌn.dɪˈrɛk.tɪd ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A graph in which the edges have no direction; each edge simply connects two vertices without any orientation.
