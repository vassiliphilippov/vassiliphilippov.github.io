regenerate

adjective
spiritually or morally renewed
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛn.ɚ.eɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛn.ə.reɪt / (BrE)
Having undergone spiritual or moral renewal or reform; renewed in character or spirit.

Examples

A regenerate citizen
After years of reckless behavior, he emerged as a regenerate citizen, devoted to helping others.
Her regenerate spirit inspired those around her to seek forgiveness and renewal.
The memoir recounts the journey of a regenerate priest who found faith after despair.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrupt - morally degenerate or wicked
  • synonym: reformed - improved morally or spiritually
