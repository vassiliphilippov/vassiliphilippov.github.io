neurodevelopment

noun
nervous system growth
7.4
Pronunciation: /nʊr.oʊ.dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ /; / /nʊr.ə.dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ (AmE), /njuː.rə.dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ /; / /njʊə.rə.dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ (BrE)
the process by which the nervous system (especially the brain) develops and matures, especially during childhood

Examples

Early neurodevelopment period
Early childhood is a critical period for neurodevelopment as the brain rapidly forms new connections.
Researchers study neurodevelopment to understand how genetic and environmental factors affect brain growth.
Problems in neurodevelopment can lead to disorders such as autism or ADHD.
Proper sleep is essential for healthy neurodevelopment in infants and young children.
Researchers study how environmental factors influence neurodevelopment during early childhood.
Genetic mutations can sometimes disrupt normal neurodevelopment and lead to disorders.
Early nutrition can have a significant impact on a child's neurodevelopment and learning abilities.
Doctors assess neurodevelopment to identify any potential disorders in infants and toddlers.
Research into neurodevelopment helps us understand how the brain matures over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brain development - the growth and maturation of the brain
  • antonym: neurodegeneration - the progressive loss of structure or function of neurons
  • synonym: neurodevelopmental - relating to the development of the nervous system
  • synonym: neurogenesis - the formation of new neurons in the brain
