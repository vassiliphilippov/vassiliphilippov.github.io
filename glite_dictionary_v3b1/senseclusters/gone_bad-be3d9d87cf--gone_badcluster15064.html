gone bad

verb
become spoiled or rotten
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɡɑn bæd / (AmE), / ɡɒn bæd / (BrE)
(of food or other perishable items) to have become spoiled or rotten and no longer safe to use or eat.

Examples

Food gone bad
By the time we opened the jar, the jam had gone bad.
I think the milk has gone bad—it smells sour.
The fish will go bad quickly if not kept cold.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - (of food) not spoiled or rotten
  • synonym: spoiled - (of food) having become unfit for consumption
