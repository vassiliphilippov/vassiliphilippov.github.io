decibel

noun
unit of sound intensity
2.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈde.sɪ.bəl/ /; / /ˈde.sə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈde.sɪ.bəl/ /; / /ˈde.sə.bəl/ (BrE)
a unit for measuring the intensity of sound, calculated on a logarithmic scale and equal to one tenth of a bel

Examples

A decibel level
A whisper is about 30 decibels, while normal conversation is around 60 decibels.
Noise levels above 85 decibels may cause hearing damage if exposure is prolonged.
The concert reached over 100 decibels, making ear protection necessary for the audience.
