popular psychology

noun
psychological concepts for the general public
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑpjələr saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˈpɒpjʊlə saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
psychological theories and concepts that are made easily accessible and understandable for the general public, often through books, media, or self-help materials

Examples

Popular psychology books
Despite its appeal, popular psychology can sometimes misrepresent scientific research.
Many self-help books are based on principles from popular psychology.
Television shows often discuss topics from popular psychology in simple terms.
