Channukah

noun
eight-day Jewish festival
5.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ëÀên…ôk…ô / (AmE), / Ààhan…ôk…ô / (BrE)
An eight-day Jewish festival, also known as the Festival of Lights, commemorating the rededication of the Temple of Jerusalem in 165 BC.

Examples

Channukah festival
Channukah traditions include playing dreidel and eating foods fried in oil.
Every winter, my family lights candles to celebrate Channukah.
The story of Channukah is taught in Jewish communities around the world.

Synonyms

  • Hanukkah
    3.5 0.00/m

  • Festival of Lights
    3.5 0.03/m
    Festival of Lights is a descriptive English name that highlights nightly candle lighting, while Channukah is the festival's traditional Hebrew name.
