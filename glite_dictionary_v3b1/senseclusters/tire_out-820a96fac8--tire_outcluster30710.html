tire out

verb

to exhaust someone or oneself by using up all one's energy, especially through prolonged activity or strain

Examples

To tire out children
Playing with energetic children can quickly tire out even the fittest adults.
She worked so hard that she completely tired out before dinner.
The long hike really tired out the whole group by afternoon.

Synonyms

  • exhaust
    3.2 14/m
    exhaust quickly uses up all physical or mental energy in someone, while tire out often suggests long, repeated activity that causes complete tiredness.
  • wear out
    2.7 0.86/m
    wear out makes a person or thing very tired or useless by overuse, while tire out focuses on tiring someone through activity or strain.
  • tire
    1.0 1.12/m
    tire means become or make someone tired in general, while tire out stresses using up all energy until exhausted.
  • fatigue
    4.6 1.06/m
    fatigue is a formal or medical word for making someone very tired through overwork, while tire out is more everyday and general.
  • weary
    4.4 0.32/m
    weary makes someone feel tired and bored after long effort, while tire out emphasizes complete loss of energy from hard activity.
  • drain
    3.4 9.92/m
    drain slowly uses up a person's energy or strength over time, while tire out often results from active physical effort.
  • wear down
    4.1 1.44/m
    wear down gradually weakens or reduces ability through pressure or stress, while tire out refers to making someone completely exhausted by activity.
