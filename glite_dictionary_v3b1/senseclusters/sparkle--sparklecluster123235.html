sparkle

verb
(of liquid) become bubbly or foamy
3.0 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsp…ërk…ôl / (AmE), / Ààsp…ëÀêk…ôl / (BrE)
(of liquid) to form bubbles or become foamy or effervescent.

Examples

To sparkle with bubbles
As the river rushed over the rocks, it seemed to sparkle with foam.
Champagne is known to sparkle as it is poured into a glass.
The fresh mineral water continued to sparkle even after being left open for a while.

Synonyms

  • effervesce
    8.2 0.03/m
    effervesce describes a liquid producing tiny gas bubbles and getting fizzy, while sparkle means the liquid forms bubbles or becomes foamy.
  • fizz
    3.0 0.23/m
    fizz means a liquid makes bubbles and often a hissing sound, while sparkle focuses on the bubbling and foamy look.
  • bubble
    1.0 2.90/m
    bubble means to form or release individual bubbles in liquid, while sparkle suggests a general bubbly or foamy appearance.
  • foam
    1.7 1.44/m
    foam means to become covered in a thick layer of bubbles, while sparkle describes lighter bubbling or effervescence.
  • froth
    3.0 0.65/m
    froth means to form a light head or mass of bubbles on a liquid, while sparkle means the liquid simply forms bubbles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foam - to produce a mass of small bubbles on the surface of a liquid
  • synonym: froth - to form or produce a mass of bubbles in or on a liquid
