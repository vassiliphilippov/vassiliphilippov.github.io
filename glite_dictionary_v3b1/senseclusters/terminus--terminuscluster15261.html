terminus

noun
statue or bust atop a pillar (arch.); Roman boundary marker
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.mə.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.mɪ.nəs / (BrE)
(architecture) A statue, human bust, or animal figure carved from the top of a square pillar, originally used as a boundary marker in ancient Rome.

Examples

Roman terminus
A terminus depicting a Roman deity was found during the excavation of the site.
In neoclassical landscaping, designers often placed a terminus at the corners to echo Roman traditions.
The ancient villa's gardens were marked by a marble terminus at each entrance.
