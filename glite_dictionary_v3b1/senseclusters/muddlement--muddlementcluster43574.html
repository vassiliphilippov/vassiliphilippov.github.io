muddlement

noun
state of confusion
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdəlmənt / (AmE), / ˈmʌdəlmənt / (BrE)
a state of confusion or disorder, where things are unclear, disorganized, or messy

Examples

Complete muddlement
The instructions were so complicated that they led to complete muddlement among the students.
After the sudden announcement, a sense of muddlement spread through the office.
Her thoughts were in a state of muddlement after the long and tiring day.
After the renovation, the garage was left in a complete muddlement of tools and dust.
Her desk was always in a muddlement, with papers and books scattered everywhere.
The sudden changes left the team in a muddlement, unsure of what to do next.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • synonym: confusion - a lack of understanding or clarity
  • synonym: mess - a situation that is dirty or not organized
  • antonym: order - a state in which everything is arranged neatly
