resampling

noun
statistical sampling method
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɑːmplɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of taking repeated samples from existing data to estimate accuracy or variability, often used in statistics

Examples

Statistical resampling
The researcher used resampling to estimate the confidence intervals for the data.
Modern statistical software makes resampling techniques easy to apply to complex datasets.
By applying resampling, analysts can better understand the variability in their results.
