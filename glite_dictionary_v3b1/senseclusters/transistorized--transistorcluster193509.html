transistorized

adjective
using transistors (in electronics)
6.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /træn.ˈzɪs.tə.raɪzd/ (AmE), /træn.ˈzɪs.tə.raɪzd/ (BrE)
(of an electronic device) equipped with or using transistors rather than vacuum tubes or earlier technology.

Examples

Transistorized electronic device
By the 1960s, most hearing aids had become transistorized, making them much lighter and easier to use.
Our old television set was the first transistorized model in the neighborhood.
The company released a new transistorized radio that was smaller and more reliable than earlier models.
Engineers were proud of their transistorised designs, which replaced bulky vacuum tubes.
The company released a new transistorised radio in the 1960s, revolutionising portable music.
This transistorised amplifier is much more reliable than earlier valve models.
