prickle-weed

noun
North American prairie herb with prickly texture
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪkəl ˌwid / (AmE), / ˈprɪkəl ˌwiːd / (BrE)
A perennial herb of North American prairies with dense heads of small white flowers, known for its prickly texture.

Examples

The prickle-weed plant
Farmers often remove prickle-weed from their fields to prevent it from spreading.
In late summer, prickle-weed produces clusters of tiny white flowers that attract butterflies.
The tall stands of prickle-weed made walking through the prairie difficult without sturdy boots.
