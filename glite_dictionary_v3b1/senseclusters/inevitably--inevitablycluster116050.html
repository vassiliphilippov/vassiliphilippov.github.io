Synonyms

  • unavoidably
    4.1 0.8/m
    unavoidably means something happens and cannot be avoided in normal situations, while inevitably means it is certain to happen.
  • inescapably
    5.8 0.6/m
    inescapably describes something you cannot get away from or avoid in practice, while inevitably says it is bound to happen.
  • unpreventably
    8.2 0.00/m
    unpreventably states that nothing can stop an event from occurring, while inevitably says the event will happen anyway.
  • necessarily
    3.7 70/m
    necessarily often means logically required or as a result, while inevitably focuses on what will certainly happen.
  • of necessity
    4.6 2/m
    of necessity is a formal phrase used to show something must happen, while inevitably is a common adverb for certain events.
  • inexorably
    8.3 2/m
    inexorably suggests a slow, relentless movement that cannot be stopped, while inevitably simply means something will happen.
  • ineluctably
    9.9 0.2/m
    ineluctably is a rare, literary word for something one cannot escape, while inevitably is the everyday word learners use.
