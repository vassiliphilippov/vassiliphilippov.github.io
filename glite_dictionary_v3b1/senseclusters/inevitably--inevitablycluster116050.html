inevitably

adverb
unavoidably, as is certain to happen
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɛvɪtəbli /; / ɪˈnɛvɪtəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɛvɪtəbli /; / ɪˈnɛvɪtəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be avoided; as is certain to happen

Examples

Will inevitably happen
His actions inevitably led to conflict within the team.
If you ignore your health, inevitably problems will arise.
The company will inevitably have to make some tough decisions.
Inevitably, disagreements arose during the long negotiations.
After weeks of rain, the river inevitably flooded the fields nearby.
The truth will inevitably come to light, no matter how long it takes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accidentally - In a way that happens by accident or chance
  • synonym: necessarily - as a necessary result; inevitably
  • synonym: unavoidably - in a way that cannot be avoided
