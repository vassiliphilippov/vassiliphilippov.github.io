noncurrent

adjective
not current or present; often financial term
4.4
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
not current; not belonging to the present time, especially used to describe assets or liabilities not due or realizable within one year.

Examples

A noncurrent asset
Any noncurrent asset is expected to provide economic benefit longer than one year.
The company reported several noncurrent liabilities on its balance sheet this year.
This document contains outdated and noncurrent information that is no longer relevant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: current - belonging to the present time; happening now
