underworld

noun
the world of the dead in religion or myth
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌwɜrld / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌwɜːld / (BrE)
(in religion and mythology) the place where the souls of the dead go after death; the world of the dead.

Examples

Greek underworld
In Greek mythology, souls would journey to the underworld after death.
Legends tell of heroes who ventured into the underworld to rescue lost souls.
Some cultures believe the underworld is ruled by a powerful deity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hades - the Greek underworld or god of the underworld
  • synonym: afterlife - life after death; existence beyond death
