bridge

noun
structure for crossing obstacle
Pronunciation: / brɪdʒ / (AmE), / brɪdʒ / (BrE)
A structure built to span an obstacle, such as a river, road, or railway, and provide a way across for people or vehicles.

Examples

A bridge over water
The old stone bridge across the river has stood for centuries.
They built a new bridge to connect the two towns.
Traffic was delayed due to repairs on the main bridge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tunnel - an underground or underwater passage
  • synonym: viaduct - a long elevated roadway usually supported on a series of arches or columns
