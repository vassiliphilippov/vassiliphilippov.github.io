binocularity

noun
using both eyes
8.1
Pronunciation: /bɪ.nɑː.kjə.ˈlær.ə.tiː/ /; / /bɪ.nɑː.kjuː.ˈlær.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /bɪ.nɒk.jʊ.ˈlær.ə.tiː/ /; / /bɪ.nɒk.juː.ˈlær.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the ability to use both eyes together to perceive depth and form a single image

Examples

Healthy binocularity test
Children are tested for binocularity during routine eye exams to ensure healthy vision development.
Loss of binocularity can make it difficult to judge distances accurately.
Good binocularity is important for activities like driving and playing sports.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monocularity - the use of only one eye for vision
  • synonym: stereopsis - the perception of depth produced by the combined use of both eyes
