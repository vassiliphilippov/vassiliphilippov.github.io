carcinomatous myopathy

noun
muscle weakness disorder in cancer patients
9.9
Pronunciation: /kɑːr.sɪ.nə.ˈmæt.əs maɪ.ˈɑːp.ə.θiː/ (AmE), /kɑː.sɪ.nə.ˈmæt.əs maɪ.ˈɒp.ə.θiː/ (BrE)
A rare disease seen in patients with certain cancers, especially lung cancer, characterized by weakness and fatigue in the hip and thigh muscles and an aching back, caused by antibodies attacking neuromuscular junctions.

Examples

Carcinomatous myopathy symptoms
Effective treatments for carcinomatous myopathy are limited, and management mainly focuses on relieving symptoms.
Patients diagnosed with carcinomatous myopathy often experience difficulty standing up due to weakening thigh muscles.
The doctor suspected carcinomatous myopathy after noticing progressive fatigue and muscle pain in her lung cancer patient.
