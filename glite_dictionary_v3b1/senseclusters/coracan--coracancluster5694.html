coracan

noun
East Indian cereal grass; finger millet
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌkæn / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəkən / (BrE)
An East Indian cereal grass (Eleusine coracana) whose seeds are ground into a somewhat bitter flour, commonly used as a staple food in South Asia and parts of Africa.

Examples

coracan flour
In southern India, coracan flour is often used to prepare nutritious porridge for breakfast.
Researchers are studying coracan for its drought-resistant properties and health benefits.
The villagers harvested coracan after the monsoon season to make traditional flatbreads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: finger millet - another name for coracan, Eleusine coracana
