wrap

verb
cover or enclose completely
2.5 1.0
Pronunciation: /ræp/ (AmE), /ræp/ (BrE)
to cover, enclose, or wind something around something else, especially for protection or as a covering

Examples

To wrap a present
She used a large scarf to wrap the gift box before the party.
The nurse will carefully wrap the wound to keep it clean.
Thick mist began to wrap the village in the early morning hours.
He took time to wrap the birthday gift with colorful paper and a shiny ribbon.
Please wrap the fragile vase before packing it in the box.
She hurried to wrap her scarf tightly around her neck as the wind picked up.
He wrapped the scarf tightly around his neck before going out into the snow.
She gently wrapped her arms around the frightened puppy to comfort it.
The child wrapped the string around the gift several times to keep the box closed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coil - to wind into a series of loops or spirals
  • synonym: cover - to place something over or on top of something else
  • synonym: envelop - to enclose or surround completely
  • antonym: unwrap - to remove a covering from something
