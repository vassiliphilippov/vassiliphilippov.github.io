Heimlich maneuver

noun
emergency procedure for choking
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪmlɪk məˌnuːvɚ /; / ˈhaɪmlɪk məˈnuːvər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪmlɪk məˌnjuːvə /; / ˈhaɪmlɪk məˈnjuːvə / (BrE)
an emergency procedure involving abdominal thrusts to expel an object blocking a person's airway

Examples

Perform the Heimlich maneuver
If someone is choking, you should perform the Heimlich maneuver immediately.
She learned how to do the Heimlich maneuver in her first aid training class.
The waiter used the Heimlich maneuver to save a customer's life.
It's important to learn how to give the Heimlich manoeuvre in case of emergencies.
She saved her friend's life by using the Heimlich manoeuvre at the restaurant.
The waiter performed the Heimlich manoeuvre when he saw the customer choking on a piece of steak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abdominal thrusts - forceful upward compressions of the abdomen to dislodge airway obstructions
  • antonym: choking - the condition of being unable to breathe because the airway is blocked
