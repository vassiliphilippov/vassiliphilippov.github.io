antimetathesis

noun
reversal of word order in clauses for emphasis
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.məˈtæθ.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪ.məˈtæθ.ɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical figure in which the order of corresponding words or phrases is reversed in successive clauses or sentences, often to create contrast or emphasis.

Examples

Antimetathesis in speech
In his speech, the orator relied on antimetathesis to contrast hope and despair in a memorable way.
One famous example of antimetathesis is, 'Ask not what your country can do for you—ask what you can do for your country.'
The poet used antimetathesis to cleverly reverse the structure and deepen the meaning of his lines.

  • synonym: antithesis - a contrast or opposition between two things, especially in literary or rhetorical contexts
