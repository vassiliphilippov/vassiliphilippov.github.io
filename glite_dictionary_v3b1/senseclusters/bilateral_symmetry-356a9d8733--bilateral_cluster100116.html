bilateral symmetry

noun
symmetry with identical left and right sides
Pronunciation: / baɪˈlætərəl ˈsɪmətri / (AmE), / bʌɪˈlat(ə)rəl ˈsɪmɪtri / (BrE)
the quality of having symmetrical arrangement along a central vertical plane, so that the left and right sides are mirror images of each other

Examples

Bilateral symmetry in nature
Butterflies are a classic example of bilateral symmetry in nature.
Most animals, including humans, exhibit bilateral symmetry in their body structure.
The artist designed the sculpture to emphasize bilateral symmetry on either side of its central axis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radial symmetry - symmetry around a central axis
