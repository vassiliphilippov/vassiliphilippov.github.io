catalyzer

noun
agent of change
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈkætəlaɪzə / (BrE)
something or someone that causes or speeds up a process or change

Examples

Catalyzer for change
The new manager acted as a catalyzer for positive changes in the company.
Her innovative ideas became a catalyzer for the project's rapid progress.
Sometimes, a crisis serves as a catalyzer for important reforms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - something that causes or accelerates a change or reaction
  • antonym: inhibitor - something that slows down or prevents a process or reaction
