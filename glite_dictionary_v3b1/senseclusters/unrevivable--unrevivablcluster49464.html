unrevivable

adjective
cannot be restored
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈvaɪvəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈvaɪvəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be brought back to life, restored, or made active again

Examples

An unrevivable object
After the fire, the ancient manuscripts were unrevivable and lost forever.
Some friendships become unrevivable after years of misunderstanding and distance.
The company’s reputation was unrevivable following the major scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irreversible - impossible to change back to a previous state
  • antonym: restorable - able to be brought back or repaired
