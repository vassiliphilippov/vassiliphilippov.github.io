go off half-cocked

verb
act prematurely or without proper preparation
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ɔf hæf ˈkɑkt /; / ɡoʊ ɑf hæf ˈkɑkt / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ɒf hɑːf ˈkɒkt / (BrE)
to act or speak without thinking carefully or being fully prepared, often causing mistakes or problems

Examples

He tends to go off half-cocked and regrets it later.
She realized she had gone off half-cocked after hearing the full story.
