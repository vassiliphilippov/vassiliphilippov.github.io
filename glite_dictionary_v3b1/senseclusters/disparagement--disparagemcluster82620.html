disparagement

noun
belittling or derogatory remarks
7.8 6.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈspær.ɪdʒ.mənt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈspær.ɪdʒ.mənt/ (BrE)
the act of speaking about someone or something in a way that shows strong disapproval or lack of respect; belittling or derogatory remarks

Examples

Constant disparagement of efforts
Her constant disparagement of his efforts made it hard for him to stay motivated.
Public disparagement of the new policy led to heated debate among citizens.
The manager warned that any disparagement of colleagues would not be tolerated in the workplace.
Accusations of disparagement were made after the politician made several critical comments.
His constant disparagement of his colleagues created a toxic work environment.
Teachers should avoid any form of disparagement towards their students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insult - an offensive remark or action
  • antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of someone or something
