chordate

adjective
relating to the phylum Chordata
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
of or relating to animals belonging to the phylum Chordata, which includes vertebrates and some closely related invertebrates.

Examples

Chordate features
All fish, birds, and mammals are considered chordate animals due to shared features.
Some species exhibit chordate characteristics only during early development.
The biologist compared chordate and non-chordate animal groups in her research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: invertebrate - an animal without a backbone
  • synonym: vertebrate - an animal with a backbone
