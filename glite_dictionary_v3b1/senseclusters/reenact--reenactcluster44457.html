reenact

verb
enact again; repeat or perform again
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˈnækt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈnækt / (BrE)
to make (a law or regulation) valid again after it has lapsed, or to perform or repeat the actions of a past event

Examples

To reenact a law
Congress decided to reenact the legislation that had expired last year.
Every year, the town reenacts scenes from its founding for the local festival.
The students reenacted the famous battle for their history project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enact - to make into law
