long paddock

noun
large grazing area for livestock (Australia)
6.3
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈpædək / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈpædək / (BrE)
(Australian English) a large area of land, often alongside a road or route, used for grazing cattle or other livestock, especially during times of drought or when moving stock.

Examples

The long paddock
During the drought, the cattle were moved to the long paddock for better grazing.
Farmers sometimes use the long paddock alongside roads when pasture at home runs short.
He spent his childhood on a farm with a vast long paddock behind the house.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paddock - a small field or enclosure for horses or cattle
