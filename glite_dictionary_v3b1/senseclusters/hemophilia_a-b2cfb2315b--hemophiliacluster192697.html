hemophilia A

noun
hereditary bleeding disorder (factor VIII deficiency)
6.5
Pronunciation: / hiːməˈfɪliə eɪ /; / ˌhiːməˈfɪliə eɪ / (AmE), / hiːməˈfɪliːə eɪ /; / ˌhiːməʊˈfɪliə eɪ / (BrE)
A hereditary disorder characterized by a deficiency of clotting factor VIII, resulting in excessive bleeding; primarily affects males.

Examples

Hemophilia A disorder
Families with a history of hemophilia A often seek genetic counseling before having children.
Gene therapy offers hope for patients suffering from hemophilia A by addressing the underlying factor VIII deficiency.
The young boy was diagnosed with hemophilia A after experiencing frequent nosebleeds and bruising.
Genetic testing confirmed that the child had haemophilia A, requiring lifelong treatment with clotting factor injections.
Most cases of haemophilia A are inherited and identified in early childhood after unusual bruising or bleeding.
The patient's diagnosis of haemophilia A explained his frequent episodes of severe bleeding after minor injuries.
