verse

noun
metrical writing; poetry
3.9 7.13/m
Pronunciation: / v…ùs / (AmE), / v…úÀês / (BrE)
writing arranged with a metrical rhythm, typically having a rhyme; poetry or a part of a poem.

Examples

Simple verse
Children often enjoy learning simple verse because of its rhyme and meter.
Shakespeare wrote much of his work in dramatic verse, which gives it a rhythmic quality.
The first verse of the poem introduces the main theme.

Synonyms

  • poetry
    4.1 32/m
    poetry is the general art of writing in rhythm and image, while verse often names poetry or a single part of a poem.
  • stanza
    4.4 2.27/m
    stanza is a grouped set of lines that forms one unit in a poem, while verse can mean a line or poetry.
  • line of poetry
    3.5 0.19/m
    line of poetry is a single row of words that holds rhythm or meaning in a poem, while verse can mean poetry or part.
  • line of verse
    4.0 0.07/m
    line of verse is one line of a poem that usually carries rhythm, while verse more broadly can mean poetry or a stanza.
  • verse line
    5.4 0.01/m
    verse line is a single metrical line in a poem often used in poetry study, while verse usually means poetry or a simple part.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poetry - literary work in which special intensity is given to the expression of feelings and ideas by the use of distinctive style and rhythm; poems collectively, regarded as a synonym in many contexts
  • antonym: prose - written or spoken language in its ordinary form, without metrical structure
