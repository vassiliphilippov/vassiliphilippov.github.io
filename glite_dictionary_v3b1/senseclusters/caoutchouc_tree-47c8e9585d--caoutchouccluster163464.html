caoutchouc tree

noun
tree yielding commercial rubber
Pronunciation: / kaʊˈtʃuːk triː / (AmE), / kaʊˈtʃuːk triː / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to the Amazon and Orinoco rivers, known for its leathery leaves, fragrant yellow-white flowers, and milky sap, which is the main source of commercial rubber.

Examples

The caoutchouc tree sap
Botanists studied the growth patterns of the caoutchouc tree along the Amazon River.
Latex extracted from the caoutchouc tree is processed to create natural rubber products.
The caoutchouc tree plays a vital role in the economy of many South American countries due to its latex production.
