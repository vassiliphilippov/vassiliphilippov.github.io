delirium tremens

noun
alcohol withdrawal delirium
Pronunciation: / dəˌlɪriəm ˈtrimənz / (AmE), / dɪˌlɪəriəm ˈtriːmens / (BrE)
A severe form of delirium, characterized by tremors, hallucinations, and confusion, caused by withdrawal from alcohol or alcohol poisoning.

Examples

Severe delirium tremens
Doctors recognized the symptoms of delirium tremens and immediately began medical intervention.
If not treated promptly, delirium tremens can be life-threatening.
The patient was admitted to the hospital suffering from delirium tremens after days of heavy drinking.
