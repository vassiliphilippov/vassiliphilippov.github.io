pat

verb
touch gently with the hand
1.0 9.99/m
Pronunciation: / pÃ¦t / (AmE), / pÃ¦t / (BrE)
to touch someone or something gently with your hand, often to show affection, approval, or comfort

Examples

To pat a dog
He patted his friend's back to reassure him.
She patted her dog on the head after he obeyed the command.
The nurse gently patted the child's hand to calm her down.
He gently patted his dog under the chin, making it wag its tail happily.
She patted her niece on the head and then squeezed her cheeks playfully.
The old man smiled and patted his granddaughter's cheek with affection.

Synonyms

  • pet
    1.0 1.92/m
    pet is to stroke or caress gently, often repeatedly on an animal, while pat is a single light touch to show comfort or approval.
  • stroke
    2.5 0.99/m
    stroke is a slow, repeated gentle movement of the hand over something, while pat is a quick single touch for comfort or praise.
  • chuck
    7.4 0.14/m
    chuck is an informal playful pat or light squeeze often under the chin, while pat is a more general, polite light touch.
  • caress
    3.4 4.37/m
    caress is a slow, loving touch that often feels intimate, while pat is shorter and more casual to comfort or praise.
  • dab
    2.8 0.81/m
    dab is to touch or apply something lightly and quickly, often with a cloth, while pat is a brief friendly touch with a hand.
