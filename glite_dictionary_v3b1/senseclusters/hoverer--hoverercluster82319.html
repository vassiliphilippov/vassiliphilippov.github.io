hoverer

noun
overprotective supervisor
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌvərər / (AmE), / ˈhɒvərə / (BrE)
a person who stays very close to someone, often watching or supervising them in an overprotective or intrusive way

Examples

A hoverer parent
The hoverer followed his friend around the party, never letting him out of sight.
As a teacher, she tried not to be a hoverer and gave her students space to work independently.
Some children feel stressed when a hoverer constantly checks on their homework.

Related Terms

  • synonym: helicopter parent - a parent who pays extremely close attention to their child's experiences and problems
  • antonym: neglecter - a person who fails to give proper attention or care
  • synonym: observer - a person who watches or monitors someone or something
