trail

verb
to move slowly or lag behind
4.7
Pronunciation: / treɪl / (AmE), / treɪl / (BrE)
to move slowly or fall behind others; to lag or not keep up

Examples

To trail the leaders
As the race continued, the red car began to trail its competitors.
She trailed behind the rest of the group, exhausted from the long hike.
The little boy trailed after his mother, reluctant to leave the playground.
If you trail behind, you may miss important information in the meeting.
Our sales figures continue to trail those of our competitors this year.
She began to trail the rest of the group as they climbed the hill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lag - to fall behind or move more slowly than others
  • antonym: lead - to go before or be ahead of others
