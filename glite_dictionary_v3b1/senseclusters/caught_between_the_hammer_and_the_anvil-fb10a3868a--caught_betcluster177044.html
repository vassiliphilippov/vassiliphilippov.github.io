caught between the hammer and the anvil

x
in a difficult situation with no good options
7.0
Pronunciation: / kɔt bɪˈtwin ðə ˈhæmɚ ənd ði ˈænvəl / (AmE), / kɔːt bɪˈtwiːn ðə ˈhæmər ənd ði ˈænvɪl / (BrE)
in a situation where you must choose between two equally difficult or unpleasant options; facing pressure from opposing sides

Examples

To be caught between the hammer and the anvil
During the negotiations, the team was caught between the hammer and the anvil and couldn't satisfy either side.
Many small businesses found themselves caught between the hammer and the anvil during the economic downturn.
She felt caught between the hammer and the anvil when both her bosses demanded opposing things.
