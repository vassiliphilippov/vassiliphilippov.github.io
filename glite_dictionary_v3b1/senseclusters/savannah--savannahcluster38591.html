savannah

noun
flat tropical/subtropical grassland
6.0
Pronunciation: / səˈvænə / (AmE), / səˈvanə / (BrE)
a large, flat area of grassland, especially in tropical or subtropical regions, with few trees

Examples

The savannah grassland
During the dry season, the grasses of the savannah turn golden brown.
Lions are often seen roaming the savannah in search of prey.
The African savannah is home to many unique species of wildlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prairie - a large, open area of grassland, especially in North America
  • synonym: steppe - a large area of flat unforested grassland in southeastern Europe or Siberia
