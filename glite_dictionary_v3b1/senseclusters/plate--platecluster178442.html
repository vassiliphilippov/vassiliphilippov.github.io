plate

verb
to score runs (baseball)
7.7
Pronunciation: / pleɪt / (AmE), / pleɪt / (BrE)
to score runs in baseball by bringing batters to home plate, especially as the batter or through a play.

Examples

To plate a run
He plated the winning run with a sharp single to right field.
The team plated five runs in the third inning to take the lead.
With two outs, she plated her teammate from second base with a double.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to let the opposing team score
  • synonym: score - to earn points or runs in a game
