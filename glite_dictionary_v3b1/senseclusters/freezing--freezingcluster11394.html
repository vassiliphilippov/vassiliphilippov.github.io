freezing

adjective
extremely cold
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːzɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely cold, at or below the temperature at which water turns to ice

Examples

Freezing weather
It was freezing outside, so we wore heavy coats.
The water in the pond was freezing after the long winter night.
She shivered in the freezing wind while waiting for the bus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boiling - extremely hot
  • degree lower: chilly - uncomfortably cool or cold
  • degree lower: cold - having a low temperature, especially when compared to the temperature of the human body
