Penates

noun
Roman household gods
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈneɪtiːz / (AmE), / pɪˈneɪtiːz / (BrE)
the household gods worshipped in ancient Roman religion as protectors of the home and family

Examples

Ancient Roman Penates
In ancient Rome, families offered food to the Penates to ensure their household's safety.
The shrine to the Penates was placed in the kitchen of every Roman home.
During festivals, Romans would thank the Penates for their protection and blessings.
