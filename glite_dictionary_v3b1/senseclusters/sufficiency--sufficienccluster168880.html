sufficiency

noun
quality of being enough or adequate
5.9 4.8
Pronunciation: / səˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / səˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
the state or amount of being enough for a particular purpose; adequacy

Examples

A sufficiency of food
A sufficiency of food and water is essential for survival in an emergency.
He doubted the sufficiency of his evidence to convince the jury.
The committee will assess the sufficiency of the funding provided for the project.
Her parents questioned her fiancé's financial sufficiency before consenting to the marriage.
The company's sufficiency allowed it to weather the economic downturn without layoffs.
The foundation works to ensure sufficiency of food and shelter for all families in the region.
The shelter had a sufficiency of food and blankets for all the guests during the storm.
There was no sufficiency of evidence to support the claim.
We must ensure a sufficiency of resources before launching the new project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adequacy - the state of being adequate
  • antonym: excess - an amount more than necessary; surplus
  • antonym: insufficiency - the state of not being enough
