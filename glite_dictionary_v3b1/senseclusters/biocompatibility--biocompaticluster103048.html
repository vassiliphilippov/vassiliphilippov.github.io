biocompatibility

noun
suitability for living tissue
6.3
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.kəm.pəˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.kəm.pəˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the ability of a material or device to be used in or with living tissue or the body without causing harm or rejection

Examples

The biocompatibility of implants
Researchers tested the biocompatibility of the new implant before it was approved for use in patients.
High biocompatibility is essential for materials used in artificial joints and heart valves.
The biocompatibility of the device reduced the risk of inflammation after surgery.
The biocompatibility of the artificial heart valve was confirmed through extensive laboratory testing.
Scientists are developing new polymers with improved biocompatibility for use in medical implants.
Before approval, every implant must be evaluated for biocompatibility to ensure patient safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compatibility - the ability to exist or work together without conflict
  • antonym: toxicity - the quality of being poisonous or harmful to living things
