impressive aphasia

noun
fluent, meaningless speech with poor comprehension (aphasia)

A type of aphasia characterized by fluent but meaningless speech and a severe impairment in understanding spoken or written words.

Examples

Impressive aphasia symptoms
People with impressive aphasia often cannot understand what is being said to them, even if they speak easily themselves.
The neurologist diagnosed the patient with impressive aphasia after noting his fluent but nonsensical speech.
Unlike expressive aphasia, impressive aphasia severely impairs comprehension while speech remains fluent.

Synonyms

  • Wernicke's aphasia
    9.5 0.01/m
    Wernicke's aphasia is the clinical name doctors use for fluent, meaningless speech with poor understanding, while impressive aphasia names the same condition.
  • receptive aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    receptive aphasia focuses on severe difficulty understanding spoken or written language, while impressive aphasia highlights fluent but meaningless speech.
  • sensory aphasia
    9.2 0.01/m
    sensory aphasia is an older name used in past texts for fluent, meaningless speech with poor comprehension, while impressive aphasia is the modern label.
  • fluent aphasia
    8.6 0.01/m
    fluent aphasia describes any aphasia with smooth speech that may be nonsensical, while impressive aphasia specifically involves severe loss of understanding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expressive aphasia - Aphasia characterized by difficulty producing language, also called Broca's aphasia
  • synonym: receptive aphasia - Aphasia in which understanding language is impaired, also known as Wernicke's aphasia
