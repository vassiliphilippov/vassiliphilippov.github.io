Six Nations

noun
Iroquois tribal confederacy
7.4
Pronunciation: /sɪks ˈneɪ.ʃənz/ (AmE), /sɪks ˈneɪ.ʃənz/ (BrE)
a confederacy of Iroquois tribes originally consisting of the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca, later joined by the Tuscarora; also known as the Iroquois Confederacy

Examples

Six Nations council
Delegates from the Six Nations attended the treaty council in Albany.
Descendants of the Six Nations continue to live in communities in Canada and the United States.
The Six Nations played a pivotal role in the politics of colonial North America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Iroquois Confederacy - another name for the Six Nations; historic alliance of Iroquois tribes
