take wing

verb
to begin to fly or to start
Pronunciation: / teɪk wɪŋ / (AmE), / teɪk wɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a bird or something figurative) to begin to fly or to start; to take off or begin in earnest.

Examples

To take wing suddenly
After years of planning, the project finally began to take wing.
At the first sign of danger, the flock would take wing and scatter across the fields.
The butterflies seemed to take wing as soon as the sun appeared.

Related Terms

  • synonym: take off - to leave the ground and begin to fly; to start suddenly
