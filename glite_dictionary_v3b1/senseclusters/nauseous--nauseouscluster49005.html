nauseous

adjective
feeling sick to the stomach
4.4 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…îÀê É…ôs / (AmE), / Ààn…îÀêzi…ôs / (BrE)
feeling as if you are about to vomit; affected by nausea.

Examples

Feel nauseous after eating
Many people feel nauseous after riding a rollercoaster.
She became nauseous during the long, bumpy car ride to the mountains.
The strong smell of paint made me feel nauseous all afternoon.

Synonyms

  • nauseated
    4.5 0.83/m
    nauseated is the direct word for feeling like you will vomit, while nauseous describes being affected by nausea.
  • sick
    1.0 17/m
    sick often means generally ill or vomiting, while nauseous focuses on the specific feeling of wanting to vomit.
  • queasy
    4.1 1.44/m
    queasy feels like a mild uneasy stomach or faint urge to vomit, while nauseous is a stronger sickness feeling.
  • sickish
    6.7 0.06/m
    sickish means somewhat or mildly unwell with slight nausea, while nauseous means clearly feeling like you will vomit.
  • qualmish
    9.9 0.00/m
    qualmish feels uneasy and easily disgusted, often at sights or smells, while nauseous means actual stomach sickness.
