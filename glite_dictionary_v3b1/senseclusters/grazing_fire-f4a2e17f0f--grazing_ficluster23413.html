grazing fire

noun
gunfire nearly parallel to the ground
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪzɪŋ faɪər / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪzɪŋ faɪə / (BrE)
Gunfire directed approximately parallel to the ground so that the center of its trajectory does not rise more than about one meter above ground level.

Examples

Grazing fire line
In military training, soldiers learn how to set up grazing fire for maximum effect.
Proper positioning allows grazing fire to cover the entire open field.
The machine gun was set up to deliver grazing fire along the ridge.
