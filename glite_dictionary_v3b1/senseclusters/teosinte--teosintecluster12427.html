teosinte

noun
wild ancestor of corn
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌteɪ.oʊˈsɪnteɪ / (AmE), / ˌteɪ.əʊˈsɪnteɪ / (BrE)
a wild grass native to Mexico and Central America, regarded as the ancestor of modern corn (maize)

Examples

Wild teosinte grass
Researchers compare teosinte and modern corn to study the process of domestication.
The small, hard seeds of teosinte are very different from those of cultivated maize.
Farmers in Mexico sometimes find wild teosinte growing near their cornfields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corn - a widely grown cereal plant, also called maize
  • synonym: maize - a cereal plant known as corn, domesticated from teosinte
