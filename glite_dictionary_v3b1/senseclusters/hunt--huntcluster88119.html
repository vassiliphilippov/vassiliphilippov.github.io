hunt

verb
to oscillate or yaw back and forth (aviation)
8.8 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / hÊŒnt / (AmE), / hÊŒnt / (BrE)
(of a machine or aircraft) to oscillate or move back and forth around a position instead of staying steady

Examples

The nose hunts mid-flight
Strong crosswinds caused the jet's nose to hunt during the landing approach.
The old propeller plane would sometimes hunt in turbulent air, swaying from side to side.
When not properly trimmed, the helicopter began to hunt around its intended path.
If the feedback loop is not properly tuned, the output may hunt and never stabilize.
The control system began to hunt around the set point, causing instability.
The needle on the gauge would hunt back and forth rather than stay steady.
