adamantine

adjective
unyielding; inflexible
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌædəˈmæntaɪn / (AmE), / ˌadəˈmantɪn / (BrE)
firmly unyielding or unable to be persuaded; completely inflexible to requests or reasoning.

Examples

Adamantine refusal
Despite repeated offers, the manager remained adamantine about the company's policy.
He confronted the criticism with adamantine certainty, unwilling to concede any flaws.
Her adamantine resolve was evident as she refused to reconsider her decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - willing to change or compromise
  • synonym: inexorable - impossible to stop or persuade
  • synonym: intransigent - refusing to change one's views
