agitation

noun
disturbance due to protest or unrest
Pronunciation: / ˌædʒɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌædʒɪˈteɪʃən /; / ˌadʒɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of disturbance or unrest in a group or society, often involving protest or significant change

Examples

Political agitation
Political agitation often results in changes to government policies.
The city was in a state of agitation following the announcement of the new law.
Widespread agitation among workers led to a series of strikes.
Periods of social agitation often lead to significant political changes.
The announcement caused widespread agitation among the workforce, leading to protests.
The country experienced a wave of agitation after the controversial law was passed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of peace or tranquility, especially in a group setting
  • synonym: unrest - a state of dissatisfaction, disturbance, and agitation in a group of people
