batfish

noun
a type of bottom-dwelling Atlantic fish
8.1 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbæt.fɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈbæt.fɪʃ/ (BrE)
a bottom-dwelling fish of warm western Atlantic coastal waters, having a flattened, scaleless body and moving by crawling on fleshy pectoral and pelvic fins

Examples

The batfish on seabed
Because of its flattened shape, the batfish is well adapted to life on the seabed.
Divers spotted a rare batfish crawling along the sandy ocean floor.
The unique movement of the batfish fascinates marine biologists studying locomotion in fish.
