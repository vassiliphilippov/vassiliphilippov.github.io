standard pressure

noun
unit of pressure, reference in science
Pronunciation: / ˌstændɚd ˈprɛʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌstændəd ˈprɛʃə / (BrE)
a unit of pressure equal to the pressure required to support a column of mercury 760 mm high at 0°C at sea level, commonly used as a reference in science

Examples

Standard pressure at sea level
At sea level, water boils at 100°C under standard pressure.
Many laboratory experiments are conducted at standard pressure for consistency.
The definition of standard pressure is crucial for calibrating scientific instruments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atmosphere - unit of pressure equal to standard pressure, used as a scientific reference
