Kniphofia

noun
genus of African flowering plants (torch lilies)
Pronunciation: / nɪˈfoʊfiə / (AmE), / nɪˈfɒfiə / (BrE)
A genus of striking, clump-forming African herbs with grasslike leaves, sometimes known as torch lilies, often placed in the family Asphodelaceae or sometimes Aloeaceae.

Examples

Kniphofia flower spikes
Known as red hot pokers, Kniphofia plants are prized for their bold, upright flower spikes.
Many gardeners grow Kniphofia for their ability to attract hummingbirds and butterflies.
The brightly colored blooms of the Kniphofia made a striking display in the garden.
