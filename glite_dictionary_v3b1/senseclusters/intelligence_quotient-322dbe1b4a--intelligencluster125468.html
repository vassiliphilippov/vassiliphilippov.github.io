intelligence quotient

noun
numerical measure of intelligence (IQ)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns ˈkwoʊʃənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns ˈkwəʊʃənt / (BrE)
a numerical measure of a person's intelligence, calculated from standardized tests and expressed as a ratio of mental age to actual age, multiplied by 100

Examples

An intelligence quotient test
Researchers debated the reliability of using intelligence quotient tests in diverse populations.
She was pleased to discover her intelligence quotient was above average.
The intelligence quotient of the students was measured as part of the study.

Related Terms

  • synonym: IQ - abbreviation for intelligence quotient
  • antonym: emotional intelligence - the ability to recognize and manage one's own emotions and the emotions of others
