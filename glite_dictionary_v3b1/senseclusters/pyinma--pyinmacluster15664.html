pyinma

noun
hard, durable timber from the Queen's crape myrtle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːn.mɑː / (AmE), / ˈpiːn.mə / (BrE)
A relatively hard, durable timber from the Queen's crape myrtle, light reddish-brown, smooth, and lustrous, used especially in fine woodworking.

Examples

Smooth pyinma wood
Artisans prize pyinma for its smooth surface and durability in making decorative boxes.
Because of its lustrous appearance, pyinma is often chosen for specialty veneer applications.
The furniture was crafted from pyinma, giving it a beautiful reddish-brown finish.
