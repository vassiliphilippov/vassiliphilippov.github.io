excuse

verb
regard as justified; forgive a fault
1.0 1.9
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈskjuːz/ /; / /ɪk.ˈskjuːs/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈskjuːz/ /; / /ɪk.ˈskjuːs/ /; / /ek.ˈskjuːz/ /; / /ek.ˈskjuːs/ (BrE)
to forgive, overlook, or make allowances for a fault, mistake, or bad behavior

Examples

Excuse a mistake
His young age does not excuse his reckless actions.
Nothing can excuse such rude behavior in a professional setting.
She tried to excuse her absence by citing a family emergency.
If you excuse the noise, we'll continue with our discussion.
Please excuse my interruption during your meeting.
She asked the teacher to excuse her lateness due to traffic.
She tried to excuse his rude behavior by saying he was tired.
The teacher decided to excuse the students for being late because of the heavy snow.
You can't excuse that kind of dishonesty, no matter what the reason.

  • synonym: condone - to accept or allow morally wrong behavior to continue
  • synonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense
