ballast

noun
coarse gravel base for streets or railways
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbæl.əst /; / ˈbæləst / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əst / (BrE)
heavy material or gravel used to provide stability or support, especially in ships, balloons, or under roads and railways

Examples

Railway ballast
A thick layer of ballast was laid before paving the road to ensure proper drainage.
Maintenance crews routinely check the ballast to keep the railroad in good condition.
The workers spread ballast along the tracks to stabilize the new railway line.
Engineers checked the amount of ballast in the submarine to ensure its stability.
The hot-air balloon pilot released some ballast to help the balloon rise higher.
The ship's hold was filled with ballast to prevent it from rolling in the rough seas.
