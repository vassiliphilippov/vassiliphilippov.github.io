elusively

adverb
in an evasive way
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːsɪvli / (AmE), / ɪˈluːsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that avoids giving clear information or a direct answer, often being difficult to catch or understand

Examples

Answer elusively
The suspect spoke elusively during the interview, never giving a straight answer.
She elusively avoided questions about her future plans at the meeting.
The meaning of the poem remains elusively out of reach for many readers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directly - in a clear and straightforward way
  • synonym: evasively - in a way that avoids giving a direct answer
