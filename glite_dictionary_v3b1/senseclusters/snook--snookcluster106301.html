snook

noun
a tropical American game fish
Pronunciation: / snʊk / (AmE), / snʊk / (BrE)
A large tropical American fish found in coastal and brackish waters, valued as food and game; it resembles a pike.

Examples

A large snook fish
A snook can grow quite large, making it a prized catch for sport fishers.
Anglers travel from afar to catch the elusive snook in Florida waters.
The chef recommended grilled snook as the restaurant's specialty dish.
