dingy

adjective
covered with ingrained dirt or soot
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪndʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɪndʒi / (BrE)
dirty, dull, or lacking brightness; not clean or bright in appearance

Examples

A dingy room
He changed out of his dingy work clothes before coming into the living room.
Shelly scrubbed the dingy tiles, hoping to restore their original shine.
The once-white curtains looked dingy after years of collecting dust and smoke.
Her coat was once white but now looked dingy after months of wear.
The dingy curtains let very little sunlight into the room.
The apartment had dingy yellow walls that made it seem gloomy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - full of light, strong in color
  • synonym: dirty - covered or marked with dirt; not clean
  • synonym: grimy - covered with or characterized by grime; dirty
  • antonym: immaculate - perfectly clean, free from dirt or stains
