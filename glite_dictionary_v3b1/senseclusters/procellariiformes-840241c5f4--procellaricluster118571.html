Procellariiformes

noun
an order of oceanic seabirds (petrels, albatrosses, etc.)
9.9
Pronunciation: /proʊ.sə.lə.ˈrɪ.fɔːr.miːz/ (AmE), /prə.sə.lə.ˈrɪ.fɔː.miːz/ (BrE)
an order of seabirds that includes petrels, albatrosses, shearwaters, and diving petrels, notable for their tubular nostrils and oceanic habits

Examples

The Procellariiformes order
Albatrosses, part of the Procellariiformes, are famed for their impressive wingspans and soaring ability over open water.
Scientists have long studied Procellariiformes to better understand seabird navigation and adaptation.
The Procellariiformes are known for their extraordinary long-distance migrations across the world's oceans.
