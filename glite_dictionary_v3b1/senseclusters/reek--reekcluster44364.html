reek

verb
suggest strongly (of something negative)
7.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /riːk/ (AmE), /riːk/ (BrE)
(of a situation, event, or behavior) to strongly suggest or seem to be full of (something unpleasant or disapproved of).

His letter reeked of insincerity despite the polite language.
The entire situation reeks of corruption and cover-ups from top officials.
