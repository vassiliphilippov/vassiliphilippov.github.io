reek

verb
suggest strongly (of something negative)
6.7 1.25/m
Pronunciation: / rik / (AmE), / riÀêk / (BrE)
(of a situation, event, or behavior) to strongly suggest or seem to be full of (something unpleasant or disapproved of).

Examples

Reek of dishonesty
Her explanation reeked of dishonesty, making everyone suspicious of her motives.
His letter reeked of insincerity despite the polite language.
The entire situation reeks of corruption and cover-ups from top officials.

Synonyms

  • smack
    8.1 2.01/m
    smack often means a clear, usually bad quality that shows in something, while reek implies it is full of unpleasantness.
  • smell
    6.8 7.02/m
    smell can mean something seems suspicious or likely true by a feeling, while reek says it strongly suggests an unpleasant quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smack - have a trace or suggestion of something
  • synonym: stink - be strongly suggestive of something (often something bad)
