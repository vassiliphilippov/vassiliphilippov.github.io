psilocine

noun
psychedelic compound in some mushrooms
5.2
Pronunciation: / sɪˈloʊsɪn / (AmE), / sɪˈlɒsɪn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring psychedelic compound found in certain mushrooms, chemically related to tryptamine and responsible for psychedelic effects

Examples

Psilocine in mushrooms
Most psychedelic mushrooms contain both psilocine and psilocybin, which cause their hallucinogenic effects.
Scientists studied the effects of psilocine on brain function in volunteers.
The legal status of psilocine varies from country to country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: psilocybin - the phosphorylated counterpart of psilocine, also a psychedelic compound in mushrooms
