alibi

verb
to give an excuse
7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.ə.baɪ/ /; / /ˈæl.ɪ.baɪ/ (AmE), /ˈæl.ɪ.baɪ/ /; / /ˈæl.ə.baɪ/ (BrE)
to provide an excuse or evidence showing that one was elsewhere when an event, especially a crime, occurred

Examples

To alibi a friend
He alibied himself by showing receipts from another city.
She alibied her friend by saying they were together all evening.
The suspect alibied by presenting security footage from the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confess - to admit to doing something wrong or illegal
  • synonym: excuse - to give a reason to avoid blame
