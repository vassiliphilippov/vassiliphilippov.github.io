alienation of affection

noun
legal action for interference in marriage
5.7 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪliəˌneɪʃən əv əˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪliəˌneɪʃən əv əˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
A legal action for damages brought by a spouse against a third party accused of deliberately interfering in the marriage by causing one spouse to lose affection for the other.

Examples

Alienation of affection lawsuit
Only a few states still recognize alienation of affection as grounds for a lawsuit.

The court dismissed the alienation of affection lawsuit for lack of sufficient evidence.
