woad

noun
a dye-yielding plant, Isatis tinctoria
9.2
Pronunciation: / woʊd / (AmE), / wəʊd / (BrE)
a yellow-flowered plant of the genus Isatis, especially Isatis tinctoria, formerly cultivated for the blue dye obtained from its leaves

Examples

Woad plant fields
Fields of woad once thrived across medieval Europe for the blue pigment they produced.
Researchers are studying woad as a sustainable source of natural dye.
The ancient Britons used woad to dye their clothes and bodies.
