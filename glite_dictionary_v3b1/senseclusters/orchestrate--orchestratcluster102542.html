Synonyms

  • coordinate
    4.1 15/m
    coordinate arranges people and actions to work together smoothly, while orchestrate implies managing many complex parts and their timing.
  • organize
    1.6 30/m
    organize means to arrange or plan tasks and items in order, while orchestrate involves directing complex parts and people together.
  • direct
    3.7 30/m
    direct means lead or control people or performance closely, while orchestrate includes coordinating many moving parts across a whole project.
  • marshal
    4.6 1.0/m
