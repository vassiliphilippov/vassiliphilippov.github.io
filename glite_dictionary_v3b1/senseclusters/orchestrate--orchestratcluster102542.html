orchestrate

verb
plan and direct a complex activity
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.kəˌstreɪt /; / c854r.k59ccstre6at / (AmE), / ˈɔː.kɪ.streɪt /; / c854d0.k6a.stre6at / (BrE)
to organize, coordinate, or direct the elements of a complex project, event, or system

Examples

To orchestrate a plan
Authorities believe he orchestrated the entire operation from behind the scenes.
She orchestrated every detail of the event to ensure its success.
The manager skillfully orchestrated the merger between the two companies.
The platform uses Kubernetes to orchestrate thousands of microservices across nodes.
She orchestrated the automated deployment pipeline so containers start, scale, and recover without manual steps.
During peak traffic the cloud controller is orchestrating auto-scaling across several regions to keep latency low.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automate - to make a process operate automatically with minimal human intervention
  • synonym: coordinate - to organize different elements to work effectively together
  • antonym: improvise - to create or perform without preparation (opposite of plan carefully)
  • synonym: manage - to be in charge of and control something
