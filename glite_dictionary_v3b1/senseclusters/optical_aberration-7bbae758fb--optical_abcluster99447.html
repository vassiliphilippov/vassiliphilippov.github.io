optical aberration

noun
defect in image formation by a lens or mirror
6.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːp.tɪ.kəl æ.bər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈɒp.tɪ.kəl æ.bə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a defect in a lens or mirror that prevents it from producing a clear or accurate image

Examples

Lens optical aberration
Adjustments to the lens reduced the amount of optical aberration in the microscope.
Engineers strive to minimize optical aberration in camera lenses for sharper photos.
The telescope suffered from optical aberration, making distant stars appear blurred.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: chromatic aberration - type of optical aberration caused by dispersion of light
  • synonym: distortion - a change of an image that misrepresents the object
