self-indulgent

adjective
allowing excessive self-gratification
5.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /self.ɪn.ˈdʌl.dʒənt/ /; / /self ɪn.ˈdʌl.dʒənt/ (AmE), /self.ɪn.ˈdʌl.dʒənt/ /; / /self ɪn.ˈdʌl.dʒənt/ (BrE)
Allowing yourself to have or do whatever you want, especially things that are considered enjoyable but not necessary or good for you.

Examples

Self-indulgent behavior
Critics described the novel as self-indulgent and lacking discipline.
He enjoyed a self-indulgent weekend of movies and takeout food.
Her self-indulgent spending habits quickly got her into debt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indulgent - too generous or lenient with someone
  • antonym: self-disciplined - controlling your own behavior and feelings
