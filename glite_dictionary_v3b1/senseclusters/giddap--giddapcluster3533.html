giddap

interjection
urging an animal on
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡɪˈdæp / (AmE), / ɡɪˈdæp / (BrE)
an exclamation or command used to urge a horse or other animal to start moving or go faster

Examples

Giddap shouted to a horse
The farmer shouted giddap to get the mule moving down the dusty road.
With a quick giddap, she encouraged the horse to start trotting.
He laughed and called out giddap as the old wagon began to roll.
The farmer called out 'giddap!' and the horse began to pull the wagon.
With a quick 'giddap!', the rider urged his horse forward on the dusty trail.
Children playing in the yard shouted 'giddap!' as they pretended to ride horses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: giddy-up - an exclamation to encourage a horse to go faster
  • synonym: giddyup - a command to a horse to go faster
  • antonym: whoa - a command to a horse to stop
