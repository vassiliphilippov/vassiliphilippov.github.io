marching orders

noun
notice of dismissal or discharge (informal)
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrtʃɪŋ ˈɔrdɚz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ ˈɔːdəz / (BrE)
informal notice telling someone to leave a job, place, or situation; dismissal or discharge.

Examples

Get marching orders at work
After the mistake at work, he finally got his marching orders.
She was late too many times and knew she might get her marching orders soon.
The company cutbacks meant several senior managers received their marching orders yesterday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: the boot - informal dismissal from a job
