glue ear

noun
condition with fluid buildup in the middle ear
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡlu ˈɪr / (AmE), / ɡluː ˈɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which thick, sticky fluid builds up behind the eardrum, often leading to hearing problems, especially in children

Examples

Child with glue ear
After her glue ear cleared up, her hearing tests returned to normal.
Persistent glue ear can affect a child's speech and language development if left untreated.
The child suffered from glue ear and required regular checkups with the doctor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: otitis media with effusion - medical name for glue ear: fluid in the middle ear without infection
