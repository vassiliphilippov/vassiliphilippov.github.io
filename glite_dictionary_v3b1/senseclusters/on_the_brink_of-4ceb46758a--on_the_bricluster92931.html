on the brink of

preposition
very close to a major event or change
3.8
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə brɪŋk əv / (AmE), / ɒn ðə brɪŋk ɒv / (BrE)
very close to experiencing or achieving something, usually a significant or decisive event, often involving risk or change

Examples

On the brink of a breakthrough
After years of research, the scientists were on the brink of a breakthrough.
Many feared the country was on the brink of war.
She felt she was on the brink of giving up, but decided to keep going.
