right gastric artery

noun
artery supplying part of the stomach
6.3
Pronunciation: /raɪt ˈgæs.trɪk ˈɑːr.tə.riː/ (AmE), /raɪt ˈgæs.trɪk ˈɑː.tə.riː/ (BrE)
an artery branching from the hepatic artery that supplies blood to the pyloric part of the stomach along the lesser curvature

Examples

The right gastric artery branch
Blockage of the right gastric artery can lead to reduced blood flow to the pyloric region.
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully identified and preserved the right gastric artery.
The right gastric artery arises from the hepatic artery and supplies the lower part of the stomach.
