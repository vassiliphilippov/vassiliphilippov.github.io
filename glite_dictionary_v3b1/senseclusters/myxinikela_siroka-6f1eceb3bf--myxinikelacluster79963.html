Myxinikela siroka

noun
extinct fossil hagfish species
Pronunciation: / mɪkˌsɪnɪˈkeɪlə sɪˈroʊkə / (AmE), / mɪkˌsɪnɪˈkiːlə sɪˈrəʊkə / (BrE)
An extinct species of fossil hagfish from the Pennsylvanian period (c. 300 million years ago) that resembled modern hagfishes.

Examples

Fossil Myxinikela siroka
Fossils of Myxinikela siroka help scientists understand the evolution of jawless fishes.
Researchers discovered new features in Myxinikela siroka that link it to modern hagfish.
The Pennsylvanian rock strata contained a remarkably well-preserved skeleton of Myxinikela siroka.
