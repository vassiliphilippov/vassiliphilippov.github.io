flagellar

adjective
of a flagellum
8.9
Pronunciation: /flə.ˈdʒel.ər/ (AmE), /flə.ˈdʒel.ə/ (BrE)
relating to or having a flagellum, a whip-like structure used for movement in certain cells or organisms

Examples

Flagellar movement
The flagellar structure allows certain bacteria to move efficiently in liquid environments.
Scientists studied the flagellar motion to understand how single-celled organisms swim.
A defect in the flagellar apparatus can prevent the cell from moving properly.
