Talmudic

adjective
overly detailed reasoning
Pronunciation: / tælˈmuːdɪk / (AmE), / tælˈmjuːdɪk / (BrE)
characterized by extremely detailed, subtle, or overly fine argumentation, often focusing on minute points or distinctions

Examples

Talmudic reasoning
The lawyer's Talmudic analysis of the case impressed the judge with its attention to every possible detail.
During the meeting, he raised Talmudic questions about the wording of the policy that no one else had considered.
Her Talmudic approach to the problem meant she examined every possible exception and nuance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pedantic - excessively concerned with minor details or rules
  • antonym: simplistic - treating complex issues as if they are much simpler than they really are
