integrated logistic support

noun
system for pooled support in joint operations
1.0 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntəˌɡreɪtɪd ləˈdʒɪstɪk səˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ˈɪntɪɡreɪtɪd ləˈdʒɪstɪk səˈpɔːt / (BrE)
a system for organizing and pooling specific resources among participating parties to support a joint operation, especially in military or technical contexts

Examples

Coordinated integrated logistic support
By coordinating integrated logistic support, the partners minimized costs and improved efficiency.
Successful peacekeeping operations often require effective integrated logistic support among all allied nations.
The army relied on integrated logistic support to ensure supplies reached all units during the multinational mission.
