disenroll

verb
to remove from program
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈrəʊl / (BrE)
to officially remove or withdraw (oneself or someone) from a course, program, or institution

Examples

To disenroll from a course
If you want to disenroll from the course, you must fill out the official form.
The school will disenroll students who do not meet the attendance requirements.
After moving to another city, he decided to disenroll from his local gym.
If you want to disenroll from the club, you must fill out an official form.
She decided to disenroll from the university after finding a job opportunity.
Students may disenroll before the semester starts without any penalty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enroll - to officially join or register in a program or course
  • synonym: withdraw - to remove oneself from participation in something
