OER

noun
operating expense ratio
8.5
Pronunciation: / oʊ iː ɑːr / (AmE), / əʊ iː ɑː / (BrE)
a financial metric that shows a company's operating expenses as a proportion of its revenue

Examples

OER ratio in finance
The company's OER increased last year, indicating higher operating costs compared to revenue.
Analysts use OER to assess how efficiently a business manages its expenses.
A lower OER generally suggests that a company is controlling its operating costs well.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expense ratio - the ratio of expenses to total revenue or assets
  • antonym: profit margin - the percentage of revenue that remains as profit after expenses
