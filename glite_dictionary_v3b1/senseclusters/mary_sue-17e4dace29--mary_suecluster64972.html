Mary Sue

noun
unrealistically perfect fictional character
9.6 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…õri su / (AmE), / Ààm…õÀêri suÀê / (BrE)
a fictional character, usually in fan fiction, who is portrayed as unrealistically perfect and lacking meaningful flaws, often serving as an author's idealized self-insertion

Examples

A Mary Sue character
He revised his draft to make sure his protagonist wasn't a Mary Sue, adding more realistic setbacks.
Some readers lose interest when a story centers around a classic Mary Sue without real flaws or struggles.
The main character in the novel has been criticized as a Mary Sue because she seems to excel at everything.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gary Stu - a male version of a Mary Sue; an unrealistically perfect male character
  • antonym: antihero - a central character who lacks conventional heroic attributes
