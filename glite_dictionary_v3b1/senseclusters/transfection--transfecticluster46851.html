transfection

noun
introducing DNA to cells
Pronunciation: / trænˈsfɛkʃən / (AmE), / trænˈsfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the process of introducing foreign genetic material (such as DNA or RNA) into cells, usually for research or experimental purposes

Examples

Transfection of cells
The scientist performed a transfection to introduce the new gene into the cultured cells.
Successful transfection is essential for studying how specific genes affect cell behavior.
After the transfection, the team measured the expression levels of the introduced DNA.

Successful transfection is essential for many genetic engineering experiments.
They measured the efficiency of transfection by checking for the presence of the foreign DNA.
The scientist used a chemical method for transfection to introduce new genes into the cells.
Successful transfection is essential for studying gene function in the laboratory.
After transfection, the cells began to express the introduced viral protein.

Related Terms

  • antonym: infection - the invasion of the body by harmful microorganisms
  • synonym: transformation - the genetic alteration of a cell by uptake of foreign DNA
