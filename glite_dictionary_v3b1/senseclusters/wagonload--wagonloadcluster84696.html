wagonload

noun
amount carried by wagon
Pronunciation: / ˈwæɡənˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈwæɡənˌləʊd / (BrE)
the amount or quantity that fills or can be carried by a single wagon

Examples

A wagonload of hay
The farmer brought a wagonload of hay to the market early in the morning.
After the harvest, they collected a wagonload of apples from the orchard.
It took several hours to unload the wagonload of supplies at the construction site.
They delivered a wagonload of hay to the farm early in the morning.
The workers unloaded a wagonload of bricks at the construction site.
After the harvest, a wagonload of apples was taken to the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartload - the amount that fills a cart
  • synonym: truckload - the amount that fills a truck
