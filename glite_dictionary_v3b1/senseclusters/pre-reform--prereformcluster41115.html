pre-reform

noun
time before change
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː rɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌpriː rɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
the period, state, or conditions that existed before a major change or reform was implemented

Examples

The pre-reform period
Researchers compared the pre-reform education system to the one that followed the new policies.
The pre-reform tax laws were much more complicated than those introduced later.
Many traditions from the pre-reform era have disappeared after the changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: post-reform - the period or conditions after a reform has taken place
  • synonym: status quo - the existing state of affairs before change
