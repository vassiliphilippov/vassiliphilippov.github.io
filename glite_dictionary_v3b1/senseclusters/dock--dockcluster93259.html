dock

verb
move a ship into a dock
2.8 3.39/m
Pronunciation: / dÉ‘k / (AmE), / dÉ’k / (BrE)
to bring a ship or boat into a dock and tie it up

Examples

To dock a ship
The captain expertly docked the cruise ship despite the strong currents.
The freight vessel docks at this pier every morning.
We watched the ferry dock at the busy harbor.
After traveling for days, the fishing boat finally docked safely.
The captain skillfully docked the vessel despite the strong winds.
The cruise liner will dock at the port tomorrow morning.

Synonyms

  • berth at the dock
    6.3 0.00/m
    berth at the dock means a ship comes into a port and secures in place, while dock means to bring and tie a ship up.
  • moor
    5.1 1.19/m
    moor is fastening a boat with ropes or anchors, while dock means bringing a ship into a dock and tying it up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: berth - to moor or anchor a ship in a place to stay
  • antonym: depart - to leave, especially from a place such as a port
  • synonym: moor - to secure a vessel with cables or anchors
