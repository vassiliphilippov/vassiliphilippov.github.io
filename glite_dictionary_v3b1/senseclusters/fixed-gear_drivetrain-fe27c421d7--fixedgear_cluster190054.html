fixed-gear drivetrain

noun
bicycle drivetrain without a freewheel
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪkst ɡɪr ˈdraɪvˌtreɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪkst ɡɪə ˈdraɪvˌtreɪn / (BrE)
A drivetrain in which there is no freewheel mechanism, requiring the rider to keep pedalling whenever the bicycle is moving.

Examples

A fixed-gear drivetrain
A fixed-gear drivetrain connects the pedals directly to the rear wheel, so coasting isn't possible.
Many urban cyclists prefer a fixed-gear drivetrain for its simplicity and low maintenance.
With a fixed-gear drivetrain, riding downhill means your feet must keep moving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freewheel - a device that allows a bicycle wheel to rotate freely while the pedals are stationary
