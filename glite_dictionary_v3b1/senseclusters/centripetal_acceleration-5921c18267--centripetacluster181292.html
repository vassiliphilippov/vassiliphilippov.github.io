centripetal acceleration

noun
inward acceleration of an object in circular motion
5.6 5.6
Pronunciation: /sen.ˈtrɪp.ə.təl æk.sə.lər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sen.ˈtrɪp.ə.təl ək.sə.lər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sen.ˈtrɪp.ə.təl æk.sə.lə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sen.ˈtrɪ.piː.təl æk.sə.lə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the acceleration experienced by an object moving along a curved path, directed toward the center of curvature or axis of rotation

Examples

Centripetal acceleration formula
Physicists use the formula v²/r to calculate centripetal acceleration for rotating objects.
The centripetal acceleration keeps the planets in orbit around the sun.
When a car turns a corner, centripetal acceleration acts toward the center of the curve.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centrifugal acceleration - acceleration directed away from the center of rotation
