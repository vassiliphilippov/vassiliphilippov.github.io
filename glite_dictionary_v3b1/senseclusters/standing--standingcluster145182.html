standing

adjective
lasting; permanent (not temporary)
1.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈstæn.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈstæn.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
(of an arrangement, institution, or rule) existing permanently or for a long time; not temporary

Examples

Standing rule
The committee is a standing body that oversees compliance year-round.
The country maintains a standing army to ensure national security.
We have a standing agreement to meet for lunch every Friday.
The company maintains a standing invitation to former employees to return for reunions.
The university established a standing committee to review academic policies.
We have a standing order for weekly deliveries from the supplier.

Related Terms

  • antonym: temporary - lasting for a limited period, not permanent
