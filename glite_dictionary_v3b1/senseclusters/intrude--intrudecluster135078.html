intrude

verb
enter without permission
4.4 5.41/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàtrud /; / …™nÀàtruÀêd / (AmE), / …™nÀàtruÀêd / (BrE)
to go into a place, situation, or conversation where you are not wanted or do not have permission

Examples

To intrude on privacy
If you intrude on private land, you may face a fine or legal action.
She felt uneasy when she noticed someone intruding into her backyard after dark.
The sign warned that anyone who intruded on the property would be prosecuted.
He felt embarrassed to intrude on their conversation without being invited.
I don't mean to intrude, but may I ask what you're discussing?
Reporters continued to intrude into the celebrity's private life despite her requests for privacy.
I'm sorry to intrude, but could I ask you a quick question?
Loud noises would only intrude upon the peaceful atmosphere of the library.
She tried not to intrude on their private conversation.
