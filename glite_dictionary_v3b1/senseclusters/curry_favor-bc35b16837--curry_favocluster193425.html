curry favor

verb
seek approval by flattery
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌr.ɪ ˈfeɪ.vər/ /; / /ˈkɜːr.ɪ ˈfeɪ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈkʌr.ɪ ˈfeɪ.və/ /; / /ˈkɜːr.ɪ ˈfeɪ.və/ (BrE)
to try to gain someone's approval or advantage by flattery or overly helpful behavior

Examples

To curry favor with teachers
He was accused of currying favor with the teachers to get better grades.
Politicians often curry favor with wealthy donors during election campaigns.
She tried to curry favor with her boss by constantly complimenting him on his ideas.
He tried to curry favor by bringing coffee for everyone each morning.
Politicians often curry favor with donors in order to fund their campaigns.
Some employees constantly curry favor with management to secure promotions.
It was obvious he was currying favour with the coach to get more playing time.
Politicians often curry favour with voters by making grand promises during elections.
She tried to curry favour with her boss by complimenting his ideas at every meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butter up - to flatter in order to gain favor
  • synonym: flatter - to praise excessively, especially insincerely
  • synonym: ingratiate - to try to win favor with someone by pleasing them
  • synonym: ingratiate oneself - to try to become popular with someone by praising or pleasing them
  • antonym: offend - to cause displeasure or resentment
