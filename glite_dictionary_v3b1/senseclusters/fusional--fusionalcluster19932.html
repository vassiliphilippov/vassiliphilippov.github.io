fusional

adjective
language with word inflections
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːʒənəl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʒənəl / (BrE)
describing a language in which words change form to express grammatical relationships such as tense, case, or number

Examples

Fusional language type
Russian is a fusional language because its nouns change endings for case and number.
In a fusional language, a single word ending can show several grammatical features at once.
Many European languages are considered fusional due to their complex verb conjugations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agglutinative - describing a language that forms words by stringing together morphemes with distinct meanings
  • synonym: inflectional - relating to the way words change form to express grammatical features
