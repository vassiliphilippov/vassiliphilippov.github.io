hitch

noun
connection between vehicle and what it pulls
Pronunciation: / hɪtʃ / (AmE), / hɪtʃ / (BrE)
a device or connection used to attach a vehicle to the load (such as a trailer) that it pulls

Examples

A trailer hitch
Always make sure your hitch is secure before setting out with a heavy load.
He installed a new hitch on the back of his car for towing.
The truck couldn't pull the trailer without a hitch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coupling - a device for connecting two parts, especially vehicles or rail cars
