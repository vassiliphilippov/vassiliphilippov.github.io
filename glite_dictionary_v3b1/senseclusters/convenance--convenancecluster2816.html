convenance

noun
formal meeting
Pronunciation: / kənˈviːnəns / (AmE), / kənˈviːnəns / (BrE)
an assembly or meeting held for consultation or discussion, especially in earlier times

Examples

Village convenance
The village elders called a convenance to resolve the boundary dispute.
In medieval times, a convenance was often held to discuss important matters of the realm.
The records mention a convenance where local leaders debated new farming laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered together for a common purpose
  • synonym: council - a formal group meeting to discuss or decide matters
  • antonym: solitude - the state of being alone
