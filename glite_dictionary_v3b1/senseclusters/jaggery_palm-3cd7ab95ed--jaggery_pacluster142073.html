jaggery palm

noun
a palm tree used for jaggery and sago
6.1 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæɡəri pɑm / (AmE), / ˈdʒæɡəri pɑːm / (BrE)
a species of fishtail palm (Caryota urens) of South and Southeast Asia, whose sap yields jaggery (brown sugar) and whose pith yields sago

Examples

The jaggery palm tree
In several villages, the jaggery palm provides both food and income for local families.
The pith of the jaggery palm is processed to extract sago starch used in cooking.
The sap of the jaggery palm is traditionally collected to make a sweet brown sugar.
