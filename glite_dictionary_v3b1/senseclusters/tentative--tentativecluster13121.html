tentative

adjective
not certain; hesitant; provisional
7.6 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛn.t̬ə.tɪv /; / ˈtɛn.tə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
not certain, fixed, or fully decided; done with hesitation or as a trial

Examples

A tentative plan
He offered a tentative smile, still uncertain whether he was welcome.
Her reply was tentative, as if she wasn't sure how to answer.
The committee reached only a tentative decision after hours of discussion.
Our travel plans are tentative and might be revised if the weather worsens.
She made a tentative appointment for next week, but it could still change.
The company only announced a tentative agreement with the union so far.

Related Terms

  • antonym: definite - clearly stated or decided; not vague or doubtful
  • antonym: final - not able to be changed; coming at the end
  • synonym: provisional - arranged for the present, possibly to be changed later
  • synonym: uncertain - not completely confident or sure of something
