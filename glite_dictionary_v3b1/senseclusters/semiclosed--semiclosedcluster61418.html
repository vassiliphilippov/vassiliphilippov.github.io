semiclosed

adjective
partly closed
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈse.mɪ.kloʊzd/ (AmE), /ˈse.mɪ.kləʊzd/ (BrE)
partly but not completely closed; allowing limited access, participation, or passage

Examples

Semiclosed door
The door was semiclosed, letting in a narrow beam of light from the hallway.
In phonetics, a semiclosed vowel is produced with the tongue positioned between open and closed.
She left the window semiclosed to allow some fresh air into the room.
The organization adopted a semiclosed membership policy, allowing only certain groups to join.
A semiclosed primary lets both party members and unaffiliated voters participate in the election.
The club is semiclosed, so only invited guests and members can attend the meetings.
