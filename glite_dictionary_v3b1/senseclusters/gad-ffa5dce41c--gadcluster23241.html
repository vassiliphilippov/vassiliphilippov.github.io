GAD

noun
anxiety disorder marked by chronic worry
8.8 0.41/m
Pronunciation: / d íi e…™ di / (AmE), / d íiÀê e…™ diÀê / (BrE)
An anxiety disorder characterized by persistent and excessive worry about a variety of things, with associated symptoms such as tension, sweating, trembling, lightheadedness, or irritability, lasting for more than six months.

Examples

GAD anxiety disorder
Doctors often recommend therapy and medication to manage symptoms of GAD.
Her therapist diagnosed her with GAD after months of persistent anxiety and worry.
People with GAD may experience constant tension or restlessness throughout the day.
