prepack

noun
prepackaged goods
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌpæk / (BrE)
a product or group of products packaged in advance for sale or distribution to stores

Examples

A prepack of fruit
The supermarket ordered a prepack of fresh fruit for the new display.
Each prepack contains a variety of snacks for easy stocking on shelves.
Retailers prefer to buy items in prepack form to save time on preparation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bulk - goods sold in large quantities without packaging
  • synonym: package - a wrapped or boxed item for sale or transport
