double star

noun
two stars orbiting each other
5.5 0.42/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd åb.…ôl st…ër / (AmE), / Ààd åb.…ôl st…ëÀê / (BrE)
a pair of stars that appear close to each other in the sky and are usually bound together by gravity, often orbiting each other

Examples

A double star system
Astronomers discovered that the bright object in the night sky was actually a double star.
Many double stars are gravitationally bound, while others only appear close together from our viewpoint.
Through the telescope, she could clearly distinguish the two components of the double star.

Synonyms

  • binary star
    4.7 0.57/m
    binary star is the standard astronomy term for two stars orbiting a common center, while double star is the everyday visual name used for close pairs.
  • binary
    6.6 1.34/m
    binary is a shorter scientific term for two stars that orbit each other, while double star is a clearer, everyday phrase about close stellar pairs.
  • bistellar system
    8.6 0.00/m
    bistellar system is a formal astronomical phrase for two mutually orbiting stars, while double star is the common, visual expression used by observers.

Related Terms
