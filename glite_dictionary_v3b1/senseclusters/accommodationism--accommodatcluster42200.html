accommodationism

noun
reconciling science and faith
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or policy that religious faith and scientific knowledge can coexist or be reconciled, often encouraging dialogue instead of conflict

Examples

Accommodationism in science and faith
The professor's accommodationism led her to encourage respectful discussions between scientists and religious leaders.
Critics of accommodationism argue that it downplays important differences between science and religion.
Many educators adopt accommodationism to help students understand both scientific and religious perspectives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compatibilism - the view that two seemingly opposing ideas can be compatible
  • antonym: conflict thesis - the idea that science and religion are fundamentally in conflict
