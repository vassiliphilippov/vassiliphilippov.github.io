play tricks

verb
deceive someone playfully or slyly
1.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /pleɪ trɪks/ (AmE), /pleɪ trɪks/ (BrE)
to trick or deceive someone in a playful or mischievous way, often as a joke

Examples

To play tricks on friends
Children sometimes play tricks on their teachers during April Fools' Day.
Don't play tricks on your little brother; it's not nice to deceive him.
She suspected her friends were trying to play tricks on her by hiding her keys.
He tends to play tricks on his coworkers every April Fools' Day.
Someone has been playing tricks with the office supplies again.
The children loved to play tricks on their babysitter when she wasn't looking.

  • synonym: play a joke - to do something to make someone look foolish or to amuse oneself at their expense

  • synonym: trick - to deceive someone or cheat them
