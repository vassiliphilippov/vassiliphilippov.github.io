voluntaristic

adjective
focused on free will
Pronunciation: / ˌvɑːləntəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌvɒləntəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the belief that human will or individual choice is the main driving force in actions or events

Examples

Voluntaristic theory
The philosopher argued for a voluntaristic interpretation of human behavior.
Her voluntaristic attitude emphasized the importance of personal choice in shaping society.
Some sociologists criticize voluntaristic theories for ignoring structural influences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deterministic - believing that events are determined by external causes, not free will
