Japanese black pine

noun
a Japanese ornamental pine tree
2.6
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz blæk paɪn / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz blæk paɪn / (BrE)
a Japanese pine tree (Pinus thunbergii) with long needles in pairs, widely grown as an ornamental and valued for its resistance to salt and pollution

Examples

A Japanese black pine tree
He planted a Japanese black pine in his backyard to withstand coastal winds.
Many bonsai enthusiasts prefer the Japanese black pine for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
The Japanese black pine is often used in landscape gardens for its striking form.
