co-opt

verb
appoint as a member; elect into a group
1.1 7.0
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɑpt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpt / (BrE)
to appoint or add someone to a group, committee, or organization, often without following the usual procedure

Examples

To co-opt a member
She was co-opted onto the advisory committee due to her expertise.
The board decided to co-opt a new member with experience in finance.
The council may co-opt additional members when necessary.
During the crisis, the government attempted to co-opt volunteers into emergency services.
Rebel forces co-opted local resources to support their campaign.
The organization decided to co-opt several experts onto the planning committee without a formal vote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appoint - to assign someone to a position or office
  • synonym: commandeer - to officially take possession or control of something, especially for military purposes
  • synonym: elect - choose someone for a position by vote
  • antonym: exclude - to deny someone membership or participation
