hardened

adjective
made hard or strong by processing
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrdənd / (AmE), / ˈhɑːd(ə)nd /; / ˈhɑːdənd / (BrE)
made hard, strong, or solid, especially by a process like heating, drying, or treatment

Examples

Hardened glass window
Special hardened glass is used in car windows for safety.
The blades are made from hardened steel to ensure durability.
The chef prefers knives with hardened edges because they stay sharp longer.
After several days, the mixture had hardened into a solid block.
The hardened concrete formed the foundation of the new building.
The artist shaped the clay before it hardened and became difficult to work with.
