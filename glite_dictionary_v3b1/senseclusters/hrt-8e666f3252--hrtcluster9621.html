HRT

noun
abbreviation for hormone replacement therapy
3.8
Pronunciation: /eɪtʃ.ɑːr.tiː/ (AmE), /eɪtʃ.ɑː.tiː/ (BrE)
abbreviation for hormone replacement therapy, the use of hormones (such as estrogen and progestin) to treat symptoms of menopause and reduce health risks in postmenopausal women.

Examples

HRT treatment
Before starting HRT, it's important to discuss the benefits and side effects with your physician.
Many doctors recommend HRT to alleviate severe menopausal symptoms.
Some women are concerned about the potential risks associated with HRT.
