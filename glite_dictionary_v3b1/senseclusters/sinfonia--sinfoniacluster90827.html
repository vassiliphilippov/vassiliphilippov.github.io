sinfonia

noun
orchestral introduction
9.4
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈfoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪnˈfəʊniə / (BrE)
a short orchestral piece, often serving as an introduction to a larger musical work such as an opera or cantata

Examples

Opening sinfonia
The orchestra played a beautiful sinfonia to open the evening's concert.
Before the opera began, the audience listened to a dramatic sinfonia.
The composer's new work features a lively sinfonia as its introduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interlude - a short piece of music played between parts of a performance
  • synonym: overture - an orchestral piece introducing a larger musical work
