Nauplius

proper noun
Greek mythological king
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔːpliəs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːplɪəs / (BrE)
a figure in Greek mythology, a seafaring king known as the father of Palamedes and associated with navigation and revenge

Examples

Nauplius in Greek myths
In Greek legends, Nauplius was famous for his skills as a navigator and his quest for revenge.
The story of Nauplius punishing the Greek sailors after the Trojan War is well known in mythology.
Many ancient texts mention Nauplius as the father of Palamedes and a cunning seafarer.
