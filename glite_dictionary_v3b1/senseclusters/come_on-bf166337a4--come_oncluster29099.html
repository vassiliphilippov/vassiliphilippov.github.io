come on

interjection
expression of disbelief or encouragement
5.4 141/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm É‘n / (AmE), / kÊŒm É’n / (BrE)
used to express disbelief, irritation, or to urge someone to do something

Examples

Come on, hurry up
Come on, let's get going before it starts to rain.
Come on, you don't really believe that story, do you?
Oh, come on! You know that's not true.

Synonyms

  • for goodness' sake
    7.6 0.32/m
    for goodness' sake urges someone strongly and often sounds exasperated, while come on is more casual and can show disbelief.
  • for heaven's sake
    7.6 1.68/m
    for heaven's sake feels stronger and sounds urgent when people are annoyed, while come on is softer and more casual.
