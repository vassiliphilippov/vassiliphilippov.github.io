chronicle

noun
record or narrative of past events
5.8 6.30/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr…ëÀên…™k…ôl / (AmE), / Ààkr…ín…™k…ôl / (BrE)
A factual written account of important or historical events in the order they occurred; a record or narrative of past events.

Examples

A chronicle of war
Her memoir acts as a personal chronicle of her experiences during the war.
The ancient chronicle detailed the rise and fall of the forgotten kingdom.
The museum preserves a chronicle of local traditions and festivals.

Synonyms

    5.0 0.03/m
    chronological record lists events in exact time order year by year, while chronicle presents important past events as a narrative.
  • annals
    7.8 0.86/m
    annals list official events year by year for institutions or histories, while chronicle is a single narrative of past events.
  • historical record
    3.3 2.06/m
    historical record provides reliable documentary evidence about past events, while chronicle is a written narrative that arranges events in order.
  • historical account
    3.5 0.67/m
    historical account tells a story of past events with explanation and detail, while chronicle focuses on listing events in their time order.
  • record
    2.5 126/m
    record is a stored document or set of data about past facts, while chronicle presents those facts as an ordered narrative.
  • log
    3.1 10/m
    log is a systematic, often ongoing entry of activities or messages, while chronicle records important past events as a historical narrative.
  • account
    3.0 38/m
    account is a personal or formal report of an event or experience, while chronicle records important past events in time order.
