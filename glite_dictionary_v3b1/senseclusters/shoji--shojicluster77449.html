shoji

noun
Japanese rice-paper screen or door
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ.dʒi / (BrE)
a sliding or folding screen consisting of a wooden frame covered with translucent rice paper, used as a room divider or door, especially in traditional Japanese architecture

Examples

A shoji screen
Many modern homes incorporate a shoji as a decorative yet functional room divider.
She slid the shoji aside to open the way onto the garden.
The sunlight filtered gently through the shoji, filling the room with a soft, diffused glow.
