straight-face test

noun
test of keeping a serious expression
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪt ˌfeɪs tɛst / (AmE), / ˈstreɪt ˌfeɪs tɛst / (BrE)
a situation or test measuring one's ability to maintain a serious expression, especially when faced with something humorous or absurd

Examples

Pass the straight-face test
During the meeting, the ridiculous proposal failed the straight-face test.
He couldn't pass the straight-face test when his friend started making silly faces.
The comedian's absurd story didn't even come close to passing the straight-face test.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keep a straight face - to remain serious and not laugh
