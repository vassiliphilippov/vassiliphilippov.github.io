addition reaction

noun
chemical process combining molecules
4.5
Pronunciation: / əˈdɪʃ.ən riˌæk.ʃən / (AmE), / əˈdɪʃ.ən riˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which two or more molecules combine to form a larger molecule, typically by adding the atoms of one molecule to another without the loss of any atom.

Examples

An addition reaction in chemistry
Catalysts can increase the rate of an addition reaction in organic synthesis.
During the experiment, students observed an addition reaction involving bromine and an alkene.
The addition reaction between hydrogen and ethene produces ethane.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elimination reaction - a chemical reaction in which elements are removed from a molecule to form a simpler molecule
