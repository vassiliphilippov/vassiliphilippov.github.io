flush

noun
sudden rapid flow of liquid
Pronunciation: / flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden flow of liquid, especially water, that happens quickly and in large amounts

Examples

A flush of water
A sudden flush of water swept down the street after the heavy rain.
She heard the flush of the toilet from the next room.
There was a flush of red as blood began to flow from the wound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gush - a sudden rapid flow, especially of liquid
  • synonym: outpouring - an act of flowing out rapidly
