dystopia

noun
an imagined society with extreme suffering or injustice
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtoʊpiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈtəʊpiə / (BrE)
an imagined or fictional society where life is extremely bad, often because of oppression, suffering, or injustice

Examples

A dystopia society
Critics often warn that surveillance technologies could lead society down a dystopia.
Many science fiction films have portrayed a dystopia shaped by environmental disaster or political oppression.
The novel depicts a dystopia where personal freedom has been completely eliminated.
Many novels set in a dystopia use oppressive governments as a central theme.
Some believe our society could become a real-life dystopia if current trends continue.
The film portrays a bleak dystopia where freedom of speech is forbidden.

Related Terms

  • antonym: utopia - an imagined place of ideal perfection
