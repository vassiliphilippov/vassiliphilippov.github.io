poignance

noun
deep emotional sadness or distress
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪ.nəns / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪ.nəns / (BrE)
The quality of evoking deep sadness or distress; emotional intensity.

Examples

Deep poignance of memory
He remembered the day with a poignance that never faded.
The film captured the poignance of losing a loved one.
There was a certain poignance in the way she spoke about her childhood.
