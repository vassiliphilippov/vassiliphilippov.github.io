cover

verb
report on as a journalist
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to report on, discuss, or deal with a subject, event, or area (such as in journalism or writing)

Examples

To cover a story
Dozens of reporters arrived to cover the unfolding story.
He used to cover crime and politics for a major news network.
The New York Times assigned her to cover the presidential election.
Her novel covers a wide range of social issues.
The documentary thoroughly covers the environmental impact of urban development.
The professor will cover all major theories in this semester's course.

Related Terms

  • synonym: address - to deal with or discuss a subject
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not mention or avoid a subject
  • synonym: report - to give a spoken or written account of something (especially as a journalist)
  • synonym: treat - to deal with a subject in speech or writing
