Duralumin

noun
aluminum-based alloy
Pronunciation: / dʊˈræl.jə.mɪn / (AmE), / djʊəˈraljʊmɪn / (BrE)
A lightweight, strong alloy based mainly on aluminum, typically combined with copper, manganese, and magnesium, used especially in aircraft construction.

Examples

Duralumin alloy
Aircraft frames are often built using Duralumin for its balance of strength and lightness.
Early airships were constructed with Duralumin to ensure durability and flexibility.
The engineers selected Duralumin to reduce the overall weight of the structure.
