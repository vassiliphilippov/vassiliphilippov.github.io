rearrest

verb
to have cardiac arrest again
8.6
Pronunciation: /riː.ə.ˈrest/ /; / /rɪ.ə.ˈrest/ (AmE), /rɪə.ˈrest/ /; / /riː.ə.ˈrest/ (BrE)
to experience cardiac arrest again after having been revived or resuscitated from a previous episode

Examples

To rearrest a patient
The patient began breathing again but unfortunately rearrested before reaching the hospital.
Doctors acted quickly when the child rearrested during the night after initial resuscitation.
If a patient rearrests, immediate CPR and advanced care are essential for survival.
