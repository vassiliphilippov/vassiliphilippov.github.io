twire

verb
to flicker or twinkle
Pronunciation: / twaɪr / (AmE), / twaɪə / (BrE)
to twinkle or shine with a flickering or trembling light

Examples

Stars twire at night
In the quiet darkness, the distant lanterns twired along the riverbank.
She watched as the fireflies twire above the tall grass at dusk.
The old poem described how the stars twire in the cold winter sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gleam - to shine with a soft or intermittent light
  • degree lower: glow - to give out a steady light
  • synonym: twinkle - to shine with a flickering or sparkling light
