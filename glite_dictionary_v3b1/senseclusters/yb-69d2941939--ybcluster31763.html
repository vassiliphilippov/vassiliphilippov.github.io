YB

noun
unit of data: 1,024 zebibytes
9.9
Pronunciation: / waɪ bi / (AmE), / waɪ bi / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to 1,024 zebibytes or 2^80 bytes, used in computing to measure data storage

Examples

A YB of memory
A single YB of data can store vast amounts of information about the universe.
Modern databases have not yet reached the YB scale, but technology is steadily advancing.
The YB is rarely used outside theoretical data analysis.
