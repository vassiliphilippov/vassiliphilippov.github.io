paroxetine

noun
antidepressant medication (SSRI)
5.8
Pronunciation: / pæˈrɑksətiːn / (AmE), / pəˈrɒksətiːn / (BrE)
a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor used as an antidepressant medication, commonly known by the trade name Paxil.

Examples

Paroxetine medication
After starting paroxetine, she noticed an improvement in her mood within a few weeks.
Many doctors prescribe paroxetine to treat depression and anxiety disorders.
Some patients may experience side effects when taking paroxetine for the first time.
