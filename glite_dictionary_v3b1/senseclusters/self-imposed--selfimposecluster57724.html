self-imposed

adjective
voluntarily chosen or self-inflicted
4.3
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ɪmˈpoʊzd / (AmE), / sɛlf ɪmˈpəʊzd / (BrE)
(of something unwelcome or unpleasant) voluntarily chosen or imposed by oneself rather than by others.

Examples

Self-imposed exile
After his resignation, he entered a period of self-imposed exile.
Many writers live under self-imposed deadlines to motivate themselves.
She maintained a self-imposed silence during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mandatory - required by law or rules; compulsory
  • synonym: voluntary - done or given because you want to and not because you are forced
