deionization

noun
removal of ions
Pronunciation: / diˌaɪənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which ionized atoms, molecules, or gases regain electrons and become electrically neutral

Examples

Deionization of gases
The deionization of the gas was necessary to restore its original chemical properties.
Scientists observed rapid deionization as the plasma cooled and neutral atoms formed.
Water purification systems often use deionization to remove dissolved salts and minerals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ionization - the process of adding or removing electrons from atoms or molecules to create ions
  • synonym: recombination - the process where ions and electrons combine to form neutral atoms
