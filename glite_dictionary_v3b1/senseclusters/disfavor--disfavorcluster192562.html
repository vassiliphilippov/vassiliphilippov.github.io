disfavor

verb
to put at a disadvantage; hinder
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / dɪsˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to put someone or something at a disadvantage or hinder their progress; to harm

Examples

Rules disfavor newcomers
Some government policies tend to disfavor low-income families.
Such decisions may unintentionally disfavor minority groups.
The new rules disfavor foreign competitors in the local market.
She argued that the proposal would disfavour rural communities.
Strict visa requirements can disfavour international students seeking admission.
The new policy may disfavour smaller companies in the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disadvantage - to put in an unfavorable position; to harm
  • antonym: favor - to support or give preference to
  • antonym: favour - to support or give advantage to
  • synonym: harm - to physically or emotionally injure or damage
  • synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone to do something or for something to happen
