PFD

noun
safety failure probability
9.9
Pronunciation: / pi ɛf di / (AmE), / piː ɛf diː / (BrE)
a reliability metric in safety engineering representing the probability that a safety system will fail to perform its required function when needed

Examples

PFD value
The engineers calculated the PFD to ensure the safety system met the required reliability standard.
A low PFD is essential for critical safety equipment in chemical plants.
Regular maintenance helps reduce the PFD of emergency shutdown systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: failure rate - the frequency with which a system or component fails
  • antonym: reliability - the ability of a system to perform its required functions under stated conditions
