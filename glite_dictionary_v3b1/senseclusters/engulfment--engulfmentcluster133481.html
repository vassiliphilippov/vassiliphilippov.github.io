engulfment

noun
process of taking in
7.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ /; / /ən.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ /; / /ən.ˈgʌlf.mənt/ (BrE)
the process of surrounding and taking in particles, cells, or material, especially by a cell or organism

Examples

Cell engulfment of bacteria
The engulfment of bacteria by white blood cells is a key part of the immune response.
During engulfment, the cell membrane wraps around the particle and draws it inside.
Scientists observed the engulfment of foreign material under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up substances
  • synonym: phagocytosis - the process by which a cell engulfs particles or other cells
  • antonym: release - the act of letting something go or setting it free
