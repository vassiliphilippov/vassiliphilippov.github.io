satin weave

noun
smooth, glossy weaving method
2.6 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsæt.ən wiːv/ /; / /ˈsæt.ɪn wiːv/ (AmE), /ˈsæt.ɪn wiːv/ /; / /ˈsæt.ən wiːv/ (BrE)
a method of weaving in which the threads cross in such a way as to produce a smooth, glossy surface with no visible twill lines

Examples

A satin weave fabric
A satin weave is often used in evening gowns and luxury bedding.
In textile design class, we learned to distinguish plain weave from satin weave.
This fabric has a beautiful sheen because it was made with a satin weave.
