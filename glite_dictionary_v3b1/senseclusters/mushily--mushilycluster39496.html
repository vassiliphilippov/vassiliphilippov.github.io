mushily

adverb
in a soft, pulpy way
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌʃɪli / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃɪli / (BrE)
in a soft, pulpy, or semi-liquid manner, especially describing food or texture

Examples

Mushily cooked vegetables
The vegetables were cooked so long that they turned out mushily and lost their shape.
He mashed the bananas mushily before adding them to the batter.
The bread had been left in the soup too long and dissolved mushily at the bottom of the bowl.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firmly - in a solid or stable manner
  • synonym: softly - in a soft or gentle manner
