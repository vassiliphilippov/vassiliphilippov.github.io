dob

verb
to report someone
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɑːb/ (AmE), /dɒb/ (BrE)
to report someone to an authority, especially for doing something wrong or breaking rules

Examples

To dob a friend in
He dobbed his classmates in to the teacher for cheating on the test.
If you dob someone in, they might not trust you again.
She was afraid her friend would dob her to the principal for skipping class.
She refused to dob her friend in, even though she knew about the cheating.
If you dob on your coworkers, you might lose their trust.
He was worried someone would dob him to the principal for skipping class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something secret or hidden
  • antonym: cover up - to hide the truth about someone's wrongdoing
  • synonym: inform - to tell an authority about someone's wrongdoing
  • synonym: snitch - to secretly tell an authority about someone's misbehavior
  • synonym: tell - to inform an authority about someone's actions
