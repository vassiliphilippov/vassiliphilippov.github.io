tire

verb
to become exhausted or weary
1.4 1.0
Pronunciation: /taɪr/ /; / /ˈtaɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈtaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
to become or make someone physically or mentally exhausted; to lose energy or strength

Examples

To tire yourself out
If you tire yourself out now, you won't have any energy left for tomorrow.
Long hours of studying can tire even the most dedicated students.
Marathon runners often tire during the final miles of the race.
A series of demanding meetings can quickly tire even the most energetic manager.
All the running around will tire you out if you're not careful.
The long hike began to tire the younger children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhaust - to make extremely tired
  • antonym: refresh - to restore energy or strength
