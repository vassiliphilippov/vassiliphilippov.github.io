flotilla

noun
group of small ships or boats
6.1
Pronunciation: / floʊˈtɪlə /; / floʊˈtɪl.ə / (AmE), / fləʊˈtɪlə /; / fləʊˈtɪl.ə / (BrE)
a group of small ships, boats, or similar vehicles (such as drones) operating together as a fleet

Examples

A flotilla of boats
A flotilla of fishing boats sailed out at dawn to catch the day's haul.
During the parade, a flotilla of decorated canoes passed under the bridge.
The navy sent a flotilla to assist with the coastal rescue operation.
A flotilla of small warships participated in the annual maritime exercise.
Reports indicated that a flotilla was patrolling the disputed waters.
The navy deployed a flotilla of patrol boats to secure the coastline.
The city deployed a flotilla of delivery drones to handle last-mile shipments.
At dawn, a flotilla of reconnaissance drones patrolled the harbor perimeter autonomously.
Engineers coordinated three flotillas of agricultural drones to survey the region and spray fertilizer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fleet - a large group of ships sailing together
  • synonym: swarm - a coordinated group of small autonomous units (often drones)
