rig

verb
arrange fraudulently for advantage
2.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /rɪg/ (AmE), /rɪg/ (BrE)
to arrange or influence something dishonestly or unfairly to get the result you want

Examples

To rig an election
Authorities warned companies not to rig prices in the energy market.
He was caught attempting to rig the bidding process for the contract.
There were allegations that officials tried to rig the election results.
The mayor lost credibility when it was revealed he had rigged the election.
The officials conspired to rig the results of the competition.
There were allegations that someone tried to rig the stock market.

  • antonym: legitimize - to make something legitimate or legal
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence by clever or unfair means
