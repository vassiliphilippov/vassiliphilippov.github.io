cist

noun
ancient stone grave
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪst / (AmE), / sɪst / (BrE)
a stone-lined grave or burial chamber used in ancient times, especially for human remains

Examples

Stone cist burial
The archaeologists carefully opened the ancient cist to examine its contents.
A Bronze Age cist was found on the hillside, containing pottery and human bones.
Many prehistoric burials in Europe used a cist made of large flat stones.
