swamp sunflower

noun
sunflower of eastern North American bogs
4.8
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ˈsʌnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A sunflower native to eastern North America, typically found in bogs or wetlands, with narrow leaves.

Examples

The swamp sunflower plant
Gardeners often plant the swamp sunflower to attract bees and butterflies to damp areas.
The swamp sunflower blooms in the late summer, brightening wetland habitats with its yellow petals.
We spotted a field of swamp sunflowers growing along the marshy edge of the lake.
