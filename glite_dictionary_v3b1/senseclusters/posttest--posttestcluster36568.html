posttest

verb
to test after instruction
3.9
Pronunciation: /poʊst.ˈtest/ /; / /ˈpoʊst.test/ (AmE), /pəʊst.ˈtest/ /; / /ˈpəʊst.test/ (BrE)
to give a test to someone after an intervention, treatment, or instruction to measure the results or effects

Examples

To posttest students
The researchers will posttest the students to determine how much they learned from the course.
After the training session, the company plans to posttest all employees to evaluate their progress.
Teachers often posttest their classes to see if the new teaching method was effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assess - to evaluate or measure someone's knowledge or skills
  • antonym: pretest - to test someone before an intervention or instruction
