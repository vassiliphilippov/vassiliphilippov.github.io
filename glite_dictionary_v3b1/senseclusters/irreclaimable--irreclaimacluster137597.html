irreclaimable

adjective
incapable of reform or recovery
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈkleɪməbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈkleɪməbəl / (BrE)
Impossible to reform or recover; incapable of being changed for the better or restored to a former state.

Examples

An irreclaimable person
After years of criminal behavior, he was considered irreclaimable by the authorities.
Some critics view the character as irreclaimable due to her repeated betrayals.
The land was polluted so badly that it became irreclaimable and unsuitable for farming.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irredeemable - impossible to correct or reform
