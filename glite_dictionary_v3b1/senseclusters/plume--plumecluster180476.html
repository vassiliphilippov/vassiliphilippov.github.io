plume

verb
to dress or groom with elaborate care
7.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / pluÀêm / (AmE), / pluÀêm / (BrE)
to dress or groom oneself with great care or pride, often in an elaborate or showy manner

Examples

To plume oneself
He likes to plume himself in expensive suits whenever he goes out.
She spent hours pluming herself before the gala.
The peacock seemed to plume itself before the admiring guests.

Synonyms

    7.0 0.21/m

  • primp

  • prink
    9.9 0.01/m

  • peacock
    8.1 0.02/m
    peacock is acting or dressing very showy to attract attention, while plume is dressing with proud, often elaborate grooming.
