guilder

noun
former Dutch unit of currency
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪldɚ /; / ˈɡɪldər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪldə / (BrE)
the basic unit of money formerly used in the Netherlands and Suriname

Examples

The guilder currency
Before the euro, the Netherlands used the guilder as its national currency.
He exchanged his last few guilders before leaving Amsterdam.
The painting was sold for three million guilders at the auction.
Before adopting the Surinamese dollar, people used the guilder for everyday transactions.
In 2004, the guilder was officially replaced by the Surinamese dollar.
Many old banknotes in Suriname still show values in guilders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dollar - the basic monetary unit used in Suriname after the guilder
