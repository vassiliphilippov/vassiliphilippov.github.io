trolley car

noun
electric streetcar
3.8 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtr…ëli k…ër / (AmE), / Ààtr…íli k…ëÀê / (BrE)
a rail vehicle powered by electricity, typically used to carry passengers on city streets

Examples

Electric trolley car
After the storm, the trolley car service was suspended until crews cleared the tracks.
The trolley car rattled down the street, stopping frequently to pick up passengers.
Tourists enjoyed riding the vintage trolley car through the historic district.

Synonyms

  • streetcar
    3.0 1.60/m
    streetcar runs on rails along public streets and carries city passengers, while trolley car refers to that same electric urban vehicle.
  • tramcar
    3.4 0.03/m
    tramcar is a rail vehicle powered by electricity used in urban passenger service, while trolley car names the same street-running vehicle.
  • trolley
    2.5 1.95/m
    trolley is a rail vehicle used in cities that runs on electric power, while trolley car is the full name for it.
  • tram
    1.3 1.51/m
    tram is a track vehicle that can mean a city streetcar or a mine wagon, while trolley car refers to the urban electric passenger vehicle.

Related Terms
