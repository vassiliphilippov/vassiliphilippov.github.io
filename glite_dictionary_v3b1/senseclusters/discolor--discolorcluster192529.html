discolor

verb
to change color, often undesirably
3.2
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈkʌlər /; / dɪsˈkʌlɚ / (AmE), / dɪsˈkʌlə / (BrE)
to change or lose color, especially in a way that makes something less attractive, faded, or stained

Examples

To discolor fabric
If you wash bright fabrics in hot water, they may discolor.
Some cleaning chemicals tend to discolor bathroom tiles over time.
The sun can discolor artwork left in direct light for too long.
Exposure to sunlight can discolor photographs over time.
Improper cleaning products may discolor the surface of your kitchen counters.
The detergent discolored my shirts after just one wash.
Exposure to sunlight will eventually discolor the fabric of the sofa.
If you leave the apple out, it will begin to discolor and turn brown.
The chemicals can easily discolor your clothes if you're not careful.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bleach - to make white or lighter in color
  • synonym: fade - to lose brightness or color gradually
  • synonym: stain - to mark with discoloration
