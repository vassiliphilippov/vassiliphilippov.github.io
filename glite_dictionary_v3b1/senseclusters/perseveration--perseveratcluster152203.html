perseveration

noun
involuntary repetition of a behavior or response
6.7
Pronunciation: / pərˌsɛvɚˈreɪʃən /; / pərˌsɛv.əˈreɪ.ʃən /; / pərˌsɜrvəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌsɛvəˈreɪʃən /; / pəˌsɜː.vəˈreɪ.ʃən /; / pəˌsɜːvəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the involuntary and persistent repetition of words, ideas, or actions, often seen as a symptom in certain mental or neurological conditions

Examples

His perseveration on the issue prevented progress.
The psychologist noted significant perseveration in the patient's speech patterns.
