rival

verb
compete with or match (verb)
4.7 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.vəl/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.vəl/ (BrE)
to compete with or be as good as someone or something; to match in quality, ability, or achievement

Examples

To rival his skills
No other athlete could rival her speed on the track.
The new artist's paintings rival those of the old masters in beauty.
These two companies rival each other in technological innovation.
Her cooking skills rival those of professional chefs.
No other artist can rival his level of creativity.
This smartphone's battery life rivals that of much more expensive models.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compete - strive to gain or win something by defeating or establishing superiority over others
  • synonym: equal - to be as good as or the same in quality as something else
  • antonym: surpass - to exceed or go beyond in achievement or quality
