promise

verb
give grounds for expectation
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːmɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
to give reason to expect that something will happen or be successful

Examples

Results promise success
Early sales figures promise a successful year for the company.
The new evidence does promise a breakthrough in the investigation.
The weather forecast promises sunshine for the weekend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disappoint - to fail to meet expectations
  • synonym: guarantee - to provide a formal assurance or promise
