boom town

noun
town with rapid economic growth
4.4 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / bum ta än / (AmE), / buÀêm ta än / (BrE)
A town that experiences rapid growth and sudden prosperity, typically as a result of economic expansion or the discovery of a valuable resource.

Examples

A boom town grows fast
During the oil rush, the small settlement transformed into a boom town almost overnight.
Once a sleepy village, it quickly became a boom town after gold was discovered nearby.
The population of the boom town soared as people arrived seeking work and fortune.

Synonyms

  • boomtown
