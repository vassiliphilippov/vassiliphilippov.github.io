hydronium

noun
H3O+ ion
8.4
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdroʊniəm / (AmE), / haɪˈdrəʊniəm / (BrE)
a positively charged ion (H3O+) formed when a hydrogen ion combines with a water molecule

Examples

The hydronium ion
The concentration of hydronium ions determines the acidity of a solution.
When an acid dissolves in water, it increases the number of hydronium ions present.
Chemists often refer to hydronium as the form in which hydrogen ions exist in water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrogen ion - a positively charged hydrogen atom (H+)
  • antonym: hydroxide - a negatively charged ion (OH−) formed when water loses a hydrogen ion
