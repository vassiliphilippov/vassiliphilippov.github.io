rootlessness

noun
absence of roots
Pronunciation: / ˈruːtləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːtləsnəs / (BrE)
the condition of lacking roots, especially in a plant or cutting that has not developed roots

Examples

Rootlessness in cuttings
The rootlessness of the cutting made it impossible for it to absorb nutrients from the soil.
Gardeners often struggle with rootlessness when propagating new plants from stem cuttings.
After several weeks, the plant's continued rootlessness indicated it would not survive transplantation.

  • antonym: rootedness - the state of having roots or being firmly established
