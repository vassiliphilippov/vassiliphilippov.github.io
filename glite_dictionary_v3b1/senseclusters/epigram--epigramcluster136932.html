epigram

noun
witty or clever saying or poem
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpɪˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪɡram / (BrE)
a short, witty, and often pointed saying or poem that expresses an idea in a clever or amusing way

Examples

A witty epigram
Oscar Wilde was famous for his sharp epigrams that critiqued society with wit and humor.
She concluded her speech with a clever epigram that left the audience smiling.
The book is filled with memorable epigrams on the nature of love and friendship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphorism - a concise statement of a general truth or principle
  • synonym: maxim - a short, pithy statement expressing a general truth or rule of conduct
