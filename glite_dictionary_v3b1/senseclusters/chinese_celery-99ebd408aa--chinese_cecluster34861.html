Chinese celery

noun
type of celery with slender stalks
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪ.niz ˈsɛl.ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪ.niːz ˈsɛl.əri / (BrE)
A variety of celery with slender, crisp stalks and light green leaves, commonly used in Asian cooking.

Examples

Chinese celery stalks
She prefers using Chinese celery for its stronger flavor in her noodle soups.
The stir-fry recipe calls for fresh Chinese celery instead of regular celery.
You can find Chinese celery at most Asian grocery stores year-round.
