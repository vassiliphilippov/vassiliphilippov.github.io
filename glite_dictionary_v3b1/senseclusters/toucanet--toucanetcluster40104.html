toucanet

noun
small toucan-like bird
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌtuːkəˈnɛt / (AmE), / ˌtuːkəˈnɛt / (BrE)
A small bird of the toucan family, native to Central and South America, known for its colorful bill.

Examples

A toucanet bird
A toucanet visited the feeder every morning, delighting birdwatchers with its bold colors.
The vibrant plumage of the toucanet stood out among the rainforest foliage.
Unlike their larger relatives, toucanets are smaller but share a similar striking beak.
