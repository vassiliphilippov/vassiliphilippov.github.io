deliquium

noun
sudden loss of consciousness; fainting
8.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈlɪkwiəm / (AmE), / dɪˈlɪkwiəm / (BrE)
A sudden loss of consciousness resulting from insufficient blood flow to the brain; a fainting spell.

Examples

A deliquium spell
Historical accounts describe a sudden deliquium at the news of the king's death.
She recovered quickly after her brief deliquium in the crowded hall.
The patient suffered a deliquium during the stressful proceedings in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fainting - loss of consciousness for a short time because of a temporarily insufficient supply of oxygen to the brain
  • synonym: syncope - temporary loss of consciousness, commonly known as fainting
