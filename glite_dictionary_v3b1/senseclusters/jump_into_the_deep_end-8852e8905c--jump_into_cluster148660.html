jump into the deep end

verb
start something risky or challenging unprepared
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp ˈɪntu ðə dip ɛnd / (AmE), / dʒʌmp ˈɪntuː ðə diːp end / (BrE)
to begin something that is difficult or challenging without any preparation or experience

Examples

To jump into the deep end
Sometimes you have to jump into the deep end to discover what you're capable of.

When he moved out on his own, he jumped into the deep end and learned a lot.
