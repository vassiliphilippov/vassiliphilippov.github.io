equilibrium constant

noun
chemistry: ratio of concentrations at equilibrium
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkwɪlɪˌbriəm ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪlɪˌbriːəm ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
the ratio of concentrations of products to reactants at equilibrium in a reversible chemical reaction, indicating the extent to which a reaction proceeds

Examples

The equilibrium constant value
Temperature changes can affect the equilibrium constant of a chemical reaction.
The value of the equilibrium constant determines the direction in which the reaction will proceed.
To solve the problem, calculate the equilibrium constant using the given concentrations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reaction quotient - the ratio of concentrations for a reaction not necessarily at equilibrium
