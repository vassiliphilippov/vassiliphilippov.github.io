falsum

noun
logic symbol for falsehood
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfæl.səm/ /; / /ˈfɔːl.səm/ (AmE), /ˈfɔːl.səm/ /; / /ˈfæl.səm/ (BrE)
a symbol or constant in formal logic that represents absolute falsehood or a contradiction, always evaluating as false

Examples

Falsum symbol in logic
In classical logic, the falsum symbol is used to indicate a contradiction in a proof.
The proof reached falsum, showing that the original assumption must be false.
When a statement leads to falsum, it is considered logically inconsistent.
