amphistomatic

adjective
stomata on both sides
Pronunciation: / ˌæmfɪstoʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌæmfɪstəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
having stomata on both the upper and lower surfaces of a leaf

Examples

Amphistomatic leaf surface
Some desert plants have amphistomatic leaves to maximize gas exchange in dry conditions.
The researcher noted that the grass species was amphistomatic, unlike most broadleaf plants.
Because it is amphistomatic, this plant can photosynthesize efficiently from both leaf surfaces.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypostomatic - having stomata only on the lower surface of a leaf
