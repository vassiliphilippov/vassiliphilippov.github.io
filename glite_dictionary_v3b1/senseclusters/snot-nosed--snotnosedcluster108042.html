snot-nosed

adjective
arrogant or conceited (esp. young person)
Pronunciation: / ˈsnɑtˌnoʊzd /; / ˈsnɑt ˌnoʊzd / (AmE), / ˈsnɒtˌnəʊzd /; / ˈsnɒt ˌnəʊzd / (BrE)
(informal, disapproving) rude, arrogant, or troublesome (especially of a child)

Examples

Snot-nosed brat
He acted like a snot-nosed brat, refusing to listen to anyone else's opinion.
She couldn't stand the snot-nosed attitude of her little brother's friends.
The manager dismissed him as just another snot-nosed kid fresh out of college.
My neighbor's snot-nosed kids are always making a racket outside.
She refused to listen to the snot-nosed teenager's complaints.
The teacher is tired of dealing with these snot-nosed students who never follow the rules.

  • antonym: polite - showing good manners and respect
  • synonym: snotty - arrogant or conceited
  • synonym: uppity - self-important or arrogant, especially in an upwardly presumptuous way
