mediety

noun
one of two equal parts; a half
5.4
Pronunciation: / mɪˈdaɪəti / (AmE), / mɪˈdiːɪti / (BrE)
one of two (approximately) equal parts; a half.

Examples

Two medieties
In ancient contracts, land ownership was often split between the medieties of two families.
Scholars debated the proper distribution of power between the two medieties of the council.
The estate was divided into two medieties, each going to a different heir.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moiety - each of two parts into which something can be divided; a portion
