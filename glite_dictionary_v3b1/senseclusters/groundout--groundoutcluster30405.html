groundout

noun
baseball out on ground ball
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌaʊt / (BrE)
a play in baseball in which the batter is put out after hitting a ground ball that is fielded and thrown to first base

Examples

A groundout ends the inning
The batter hit a slow roller to third base and was retired on a groundout.
With two outs, the pitcher forced a groundout to end the inning.
He was disappointed after his groundout in the bottom of the ninth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flyout - an out in baseball when a batter's hit is caught before touching the ground
  • antonym: strikeout - an out in baseball when the batter accumulates three strikes
