ambiguously

adverb
in an unclear or ambiguous way
2.1 6.7
Pronunciation: / æmˈbɪɡjuəsli / (AmE), / æmˈbɪɡjuəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is open to more than one interpretation or is unclear in meaning.

Examples

Answer ambiguously
He answered the question ambiguously, leaving everyone confused about his true intentions.
Her statement was phrased ambiguously to avoid committing to either side.
The instructions were written so ambiguously that no one could follow them correctly.
