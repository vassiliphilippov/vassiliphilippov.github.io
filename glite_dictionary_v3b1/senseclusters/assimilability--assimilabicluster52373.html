assimilability

noun
ability to be assimilated (as nourishment)
8.3
Pronunciation: / əˌsɪməˌləbɪlɪti / (AmE), / əˌsɪmɪləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being capable of being absorbed and utilized, especially as nourishment.

Examples

Higher assimilability of food
Higher assimilability can make certain foods more beneficial in malnutrition treatment.
Scientists tested the assimilability of several protein supplements in the laboratory.
The assimilability of different nutrients varies depending on their source and form.
