Ottawa

noun
a member of an Algonquian people of North America
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːtəwɑː / (AmE), / ˈɒtəwɑː / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian Indigenous people originally inhabiting southern Ontario and neighboring regions of Canada and the United States

Examples

An Ottawa leader
Members of the Ottawa played an important role in the fur trade.

The traditions of the Ottawa are celebrated in both Canada and the United States.
