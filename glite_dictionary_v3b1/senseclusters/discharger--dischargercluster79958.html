discharger

noun
device or person releasing
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒər / (AmE), / dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒə / (BrE)
a person or device that releases, expels, or allows something (such as electricity, fluid, gas, or energy) to flow out

Examples

Electric discharger
The discharger released the excess pressure from the tank to prevent an accident.
A battery discharger is used to safely drain stored energy before disposal.
The technician checked the discharger to ensure it was functioning correctly during the test.
The discharger safely released the stored energy from the battery.
A water discharger is used to empty the tank quickly in emergencies.
The technician checked the discharger to ensure it was functioning properly before use.
