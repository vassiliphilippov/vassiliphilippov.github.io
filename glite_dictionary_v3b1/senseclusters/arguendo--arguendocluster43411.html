arguendo

adverb
for argument's sake
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrɡjuˈɛndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌɑːɡjuˈendəʊ / (BrE)
for the sake of argument; used to assume something is true for discussion purposes.

Examples

Assume arguendo
Let us assume, arguendo, that the contract was signed on that date.
The lawyer said, arguendo, that even if the evidence were true, it would not prove guilt.
If we accept, arguendo, the opposing side's facts, the outcome remains unchanged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypothetically - in a way that is based on a suggested idea or theory
