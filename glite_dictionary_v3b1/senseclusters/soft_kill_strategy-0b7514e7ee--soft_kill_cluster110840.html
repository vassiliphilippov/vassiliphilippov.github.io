soft kill strategy

noun
method to incapacitate without killing
Pronunciation: / sɔft kɪl ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / sɒft kɪl ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
a method designed to incapacitate personnel, vehicles, or equipment without causing fatal harm or physical destruction

Examples

A soft kill strategy
Advanced technology allows for a soft kill strategy that targets vehicle electronics instead of physical destruction.
The commander preferred a soft kill strategy to neutralize threats with minimal collateral damage.
The military deployed a soft kill strategy to disable the enemy’s communications without loss of life.

Related Terms
