metaphysic

adjective
about metaphysics
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪk / (BrE)
relating to metaphysics or concerned with the fundamental nature of reality beyond the physical world.

Examples

Metaphysic question
The philosopher presented a metaphysic argument about the nature of existence.
Her research explores metaphysic questions that science cannot easily answer.
The novel is filled with metaphysic themes about reality and perception.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory
  • synonym: philosophical - relating to the study of fundamental nature of knowledge and existence
