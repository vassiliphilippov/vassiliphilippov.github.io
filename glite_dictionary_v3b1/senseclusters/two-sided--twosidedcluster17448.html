two-sided

adjective
having two opposing viewpoints or aspects
Pronunciation: / tuː ˈsaɪdɪd / (AmE), / tuː ˈsaɪdɪd / (BrE)
having two opposing viewpoints or aspects; involving two parties, sides, or perspectives

Examples

Two-sided argument
It's important to consider a two-sided analysis before making a decision about such complex issues.
The discussion on climate policy was truly two-sided, with both advocates and critics presenting their cases.
The negotiation was two-sided, allowing input from both the management and the employees.
