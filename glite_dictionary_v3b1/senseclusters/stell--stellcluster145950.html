stell

noun
deep river pool
9.9
Pronunciation: / stɛl / (BrE)
a deep or still pool in a river or stream where fish rest, especially in Scottish dialect

Examples

Salmon stell
The fisherman waited patiently by the stell where salmon often gathered.
After a long walk, they rested beside a quiet stell shaded by trees.
Local anglers know that the biggest trout hide in the deepest stell of the stream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pool - a small area of still water in a river or stream
  • antonym: riffle - a shallow part of a stream with rapid current and surface agitation
