carve up

verb
divide into parts or portions
5.8 0.61/m
Pronunciation: / k…ërv  åp / (AmE), / k…ëÀêv  åp / (BrE)
to divide something into parts or portions, often with the implication of distributing or apportioning it among several people or groups

Examples

To carve up the market
After the merger, the companies decided to carve up the market between themselves.
In the kitchen, she began to carve up the roast chicken for the family dinner.
The will instructed the executor to carve up the estate fairly among the heirs.
