nucleator

noun
agent that starts nuclei
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliˌeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliˌeɪtə / (BrE)
a substance or agent that initiates the formation of a nucleus, especially in a chemical or physical process such as crystallization

Examples

Crystal nucleator
A nucleator can speed up the crystallization process in a solution.
Scientists added a nucleator to the mixture to help form ice crystals more quickly.
The presence of a nucleator is essential for certain types of chemical reactions to begin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction
  • antonym: inhibitor - a substance that slows down or prevents a chemical reaction
