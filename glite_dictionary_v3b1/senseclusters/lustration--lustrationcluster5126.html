lustration

noun
ritual purification ceremony
9.9
Pronunciation: /lʌs.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /lʌs.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a ceremony or ritual performed to purify or cleanse people, places, or objects, especially in ancient religious practices

Examples

Ancient lustration ceremony
The ancient Romans held a lustration to cleanse the city after a period of misfortune.
A lustration was performed to purify the temple before the sacred festival began.
Historical texts describe the use of lustration in ceremonies to remove spiritual impurity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defilement - the act of making something dirty or impure
  • synonym: purification - the act of making something pure or clean
