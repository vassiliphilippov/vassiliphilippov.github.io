unargumentative

adjective
not inclined to argue
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌɑrɡjəˈmɛntətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌɑːɡjuˈmɛntətɪv / (BrE)
not inclined to argue or engage in disputes; not argumentative.

Examples

An unargumentative person
Despite the heated debate, Maria remained unargumentative throughout the discussion.
His unargumentative nature made him easy to work with in stressful situations.
The teacher appreciated students who were cooperative and unargumentative in class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: argumentative - inclined to argue or dispute
