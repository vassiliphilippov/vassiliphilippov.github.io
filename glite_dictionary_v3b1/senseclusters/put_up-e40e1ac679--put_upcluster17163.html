put up

verb
display or place something to be noticed
1.0
Pronunciation: / pʊt ʌp / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp / (BrE)
to display or place something, especially a sign or notice, so that it can be easily seen

Examples

Put up a sign
The school put up a notice about the holiday schedule for everyone to see.
They asked the caretaker to put up a warning sign near the construction site.
We need permission before we put up posters on that wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: post - put up in a public place, especially notices or signs
  • antonym: take down - remove something that has been put up, especially a notice or sign
