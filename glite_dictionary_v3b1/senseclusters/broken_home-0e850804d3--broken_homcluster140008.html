broken home

noun
family with separated or divorced parents
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊ.kən hoʊm /; / ˈbroʊkən hoʊm / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.kən həʊm /; / ˈbrəʊkən həʊm / (BrE)
a family in which the parents are separated or divorced, often causing problems or difficulties for the children

Examples

A broken home family
Growing up in a broken home affected her outlook on relationships.
He tried to avoid repeating the mistakes of his broken home.
Teachers are often concerned about students from broken homes.
He blamed his difficulties at school on coming from a broken home.
Many social workers pay special attention to children from a broken home.
Support groups can be helpful for teenagers adjusting to life after a broken home.

Related Terms
