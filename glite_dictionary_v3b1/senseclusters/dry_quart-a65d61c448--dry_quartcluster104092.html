dry quart

noun
US unit of dry capacity (2 dry pints)
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdraɪ ˈkwɔrt / (AmE), / ˌdraɪ ˈkwɔːt / (BrE)
a unit of dry capacity in the United States, equal to 2 dry pints or approximately 67.2 cubic inches (about 1.101 liters)

Examples

A dry quart measure
A dry quart of strawberries is often sold at farmer’s markets.
A bushel contains 32 dry quarts in the United States system.
The recipe calls for half a dry quart of flour.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: peck - a US unit of dry capacity, equal to 8 dry quarts
  • degree lower: pint - a unit of capacity, base unit for quarts
