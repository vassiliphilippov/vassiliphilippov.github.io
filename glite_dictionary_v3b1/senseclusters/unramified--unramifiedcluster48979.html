unramified

adjective
without branching points
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈræməˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈræmɪfaɪd / (BrE)
in mathematics, describing a covering map, field extension, or morphism that has no branch points or local multiplicity

Examples

Unramified extension
The mathematician proved that the extension was unramified at all relevant points.
In this case, the covering map is unramified, so each fiber has the same number of elements.
An unramified morphism simplifies the analysis of the structure in algebraic geometry.
