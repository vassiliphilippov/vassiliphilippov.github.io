coenzyme A

noun
important cellular coenzyme for metabolism
4.3
Pronunciation: / koʊˌɛnˌzaɪm ˈeɪ / (AmE), / kəʊˌɛnˌzʌɪm ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a coenzyme found in all living cells that is vital for the metabolism of carbohydrates, fats, and some amino acids

Examples

Coenzyme A in metabolism
Coenzyme A is necessary for the synthesis and oxidation of fatty acids in the body.
A deficiency in coenzyme A can disrupt important metabolic pathways.
Biologists study the role of coenzyme A in cellular energy production.
