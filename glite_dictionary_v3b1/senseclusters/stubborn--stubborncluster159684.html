stubborn

adjective
unwilling to change or yield
6.2 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈstʌb.ərn/ (AmE), /ˈstʌb.ən/ (BrE)
unwilling to change your mind or attitude, even when others try to persuade you

Examples

A stubborn person
A stubborn stain refused to come out of the shirt.
Despite the challenges, his stubborn determination kept him going.
She remained stubborn even after hearing everyone's advice.
Even as a child, he was known for being stubborn and refusing to compromise.
Her stubborn attitude made it difficult to reach an agreement with the team.
No matter what we said, he remained stubborn about his decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - willing to change or compromise
  • synonym: obstinate - refusing to change one's mind; very stubborn
  • synonym: persistent - continuing firmly despite difficulty
  • synonym: rigid - not willing to change or adapt
