synthetism

noun
French painting style with flat colors and symbolism
5.6 9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪn.θə.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /sɪn.ˈθet.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈsɪn.θə.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /sɪn.ˈθet.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a late 19th-century style of French painting characterized by bold, flat areas of color and symbolic representations of abstract ideas, often associated with artists such as Paul Gauguin.

Examples

Synthetism style painting
Art historians often discuss synthetism when analyzing Symbolist movements in European art.
Paul Gauguin was a major proponent of synthetism in his vibrant, symbolic paintings.
The exhibition highlighted works influenced by synthetism from the late 1800s.
