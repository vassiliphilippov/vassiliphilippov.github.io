institutionalize

verb
admit to an institution
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.stəˈtuː.ʃə.nəˌlaɪz /; / ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃə.nə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to send someone to live in an institution, such as a hospital, prison, or care facility, usually for a long period

Examples

To institutionalize a patient
Debates continue about whether it is better to institutionalize people with severe mental illness or to support them in the community.
She was institutionalized for several months following her diagnosis.
The family had no choice but to institutionalize their uncle after his health declined.
After repeated offenses, he was institutionalised by court order.
Her family struggled with the decision to institutionalise her for her own safety.
The doctor decided to institutionalise the patient for ongoing care.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commit - to send someone officially to a prison or hospital
