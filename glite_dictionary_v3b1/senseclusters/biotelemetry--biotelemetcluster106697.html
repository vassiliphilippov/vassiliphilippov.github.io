biotelemetry

noun
remote biological monitoring
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊtəˈlɛmɪtri / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊtəˈlɛmɪtri / (BrE)
the remote measurement and transmission of biological data from living organisms, usually using electronic devices for research or medical monitoring

Examples

Biotelemetry data transmission
Scientists use biotelemetry to monitor the migration patterns of sea turtles in real time.
Advances in biotelemetry have improved patient care by allowing doctors to track vital signs remotely.
The research team relied on biotelemetry to collect heart rate data from animals in the wild.
Researchers used biotelemetry to track the heart rates of wild animals in their natural habitats.
Advances in biotelemetry have improved patient monitoring in hospitals and during space missions.
The study relied on biotelemetry devices to collect real-time data from athletes during training.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biosensor - a device that detects and measures biological information
  • synonym: telemetry - the process of recording and transmitting data from remote sources
