hypoxanthine

noun
cellular chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈzænθiːn / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊˈzanθiːn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring chemical compound found in cells, important in the metabolism of nucleic acids

Examples

The hypoxanthine compound
Scientists detected increased levels of hypoxanthine in the patient's blood sample.
The breakdown of nucleic acids in cells produces hypoxanthine as an intermediate compound.
Researchers studied how hypoxanthine is converted into uric acid during metabolism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uric acid - a product of purine metabolism in the body
  • synonym: xanthine - a purine base found in most human body tissues and fluids
