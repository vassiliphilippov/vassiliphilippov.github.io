reduce

verb
cook until liquid thickens and shrinks
1.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdus / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to cook a liquid, such as a sauce or soup, so that it becomes thicker and less in volume by boiling or simmering

Examples

To reduce a sauce
If you reduce the liquid too much, add a little water to adjust the consistency.
Let the sauce reduce over low heat until it thickens.
Stir the soup continuously as you reduce it to concentrate the flavors.
Simmer the mixture until you reduce it by half, creating a rich, flavorful sauce.
The chef recommended to reduce the wine before adding it to the stew.
To thicken the broth, simply reduce it over low heat for several minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concentrate - to make a liquid stronger by removing water
