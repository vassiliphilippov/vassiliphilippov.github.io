spotted bat

noun
large bat with spots and big ears (U.S.)
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈspɑt̬ɪd bæt / (AmE), / ˈspɒtɪd bæt / (BrE)
a large bat species of the southwestern United States, notable for its white spots and extremely large ears

Examples

The spotted bat species
Researchers tracked several spotted bats using radio transmitters to study their movement patterns.
The spotted bat is easily identified by the white spots on its dark fur.
The spotted bat is rarely seen because it prefers remote canyon habitats.
