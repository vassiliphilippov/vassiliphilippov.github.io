go awry

verb
to go wrong or unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ əˈraɪ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraɪ / (BrE)
to go wrong or not happen as planned or expected

Examples

Plans go awry
His speech went awry after he forgot his lines.
Our holiday plans went awry when the airline lost our luggage.
The experiment went awry because an essential component was missing.
Her carefully organized wedding day went awry when the cake was delivered late.
If anything goes awry during the experiment, inform your supervisor at once.
Their plans for the picnic went awry when it started to rain unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: go wrong - to not proceed as planned or expected
  • antonym: succeed - to achieve a desired result or occur as planned
