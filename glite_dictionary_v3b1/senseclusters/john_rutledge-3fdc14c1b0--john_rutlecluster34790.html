John Rutledge

proper noun
U.S. jurist; second Supreme Court Chief Justice
9.6
Pronunciation: /dʒɑːn ˈrʌt.lɪdʒ/ (AmE), /dʒɒn ˈrʌt.lɪdʒ/ (BrE)
(1739–1800) United States jurist who served briefly as the second Chief Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court after appointment by George Washington, though his appointment was later rejected by the Senate.

Examples

John Rutledge Chief Justice
John Rutledge was appointed as the second Chief Justice of the United States by George Washington.
Historians note that John Rutledge played a significant role in the early years of the Supreme Court.
The U.S. Senate ultimately rejected John Rutledge's appointment as Chief Justice in 1795.
