lap-straked

adjective
a hull with overlapping planks
8.3 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈlæp.streɪkt/ (AmE), /ˈlæp.streɪkt/ (BrE)
(of a boat or hull) having planks that overlap along their edges, especially as a method of construction.

Examples

Lap-straked wooden hull
Many classic wooden rowboats were constructed using a lap-straked hull design.
The fisherman repaired his lap-straked boat before heading back out to sea.
Traditional Norwegian vessels are often lap-straked for added strength and flexibility.
