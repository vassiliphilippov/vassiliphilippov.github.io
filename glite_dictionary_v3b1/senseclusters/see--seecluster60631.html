see

verb
perceive with the eyes
1.0 1384/m
Pronunciation: / siÀê / (AmE), / siÀê / (BrE)
to notice, observe, or watch something with your eyes

Examples

To see the city
From the balcony, you can see the entire city skyline.
I couldn't see anything in the dark room.
She sees her friend waving from across the street.
May I see your identification before you enter the building?
The doctor will see the patient now to check his injuries.
The security guard stopped to see the contents of my bag.
She turned her head to see what had made the noise outside.
The security guard did not see anyone enter the building after midnight.
The surveillance camera can see the entire parking lot at night.

Synonyms

  • notice
    1.0 164/m
    notice is to become aware quickly, often by chance, while see is a general act of using your eyes to observe things.
  • view
    1.0 38/m
    view means to look at something with thought or opinion, while see is the plain act of noticing with your eyes.
  • watch
    1.0 435/m
    watch means to look at something for a period to follow action, while see is a quick or general visual noticing.
  • observe
    2.4 76/m
    observe means to watch carefully, often to learn or record, while see is more general and may be brief or accidental.
  • look at
    1.0 488/m
    look at means to direct your eyes toward something on purpose, while see can happen without trying or attention.
  • regard
    3.2 28/m
    regard means to look at someone with careful attention or respect, while see is the basic act of noticing with eyes.
  • perceive
    3.7 12/m
    perceive means to become aware through the senses and often think about it, while see focuses on simple visual noticing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not pay attention to something

  • synonym: look - to direct one's gaze toward something
  • antonym: miss - to fail to notice or perceive
  • synonym: notice - to become aware of something
  • synonym: observe - to watch carefully, especially for a specific purpose
