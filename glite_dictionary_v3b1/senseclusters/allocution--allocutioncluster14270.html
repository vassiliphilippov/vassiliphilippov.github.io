allocution

noun
formal authoritative speech or address
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈkjuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌaləˈkjuːʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a formal and authoritative speech, especially one that offers advice or exhortation

Examples

Graduation allocution
He prepared an allocution meant to inspire and guide the graduating class as they entered the workforce.
The judge delivered an allocution to the courtroom before announcing the verdict.
The president's televised allocution called for unity and resilience in the face of adversity.
