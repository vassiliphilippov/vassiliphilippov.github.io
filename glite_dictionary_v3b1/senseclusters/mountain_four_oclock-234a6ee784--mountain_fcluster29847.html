mountain four o'clock

noun
leafy wildflower of the US Southwest
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊntən fɔr əˈklɑk / (AmE), / ˈmaʊntɪn fɔːr əˈklɒk / (BrE)
A leafy wildflower with lavender-pink flowers that open in the evening and remain through the cool part of the next day, found in the mountains of southern Colorado to Arizona and into Mexico.

Examples

The mountain four o'clock flower
Hikers in Arizona often come across the vibrant blossoms of the mountain four o'clock in late spring.
She collected seeds from a mountain four o'clock to plant in her wildflower garden.
The mountain four o'clock blooms in the evening, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators.
