let someone down gently

verb
end something kindly; minimize hurt when giving bad news
Pronunciation: / lɛt ˌsʌmwʌn daʊn ˈdʒɛntli / (AmE), / lɛt ˌsʌmwən daʊn ˈdʒɛntli / (BrE)
to end a relationship or deliver bad news in a way that minimizes hurt or embarrassment for the other person

Examples

To let someone down gently
I tried to let him down gently because I didn't want to upset him.
She decided it would be kinder to let her friend down gently rather than be blunt.
When breaking bad news, it's always best to let someone down gently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break up - to end a romantic relationship
  • synonym: spare someone's feelings - to avoid hurting someone emotionally
