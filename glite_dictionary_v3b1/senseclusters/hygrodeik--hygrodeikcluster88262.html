hygrodeik

noun
device for measuring humidity
Pronunciation: / haɪˈɡroʊdiːk / (AmE), / hʌɪˈɡrəʊdiːk / (BrE)
A type of hygrometer that measures humidity using wet and dry bulb thermometers.

Examples

A hygrodeik instrument
The museum conservator checked the display cases using a hygrodeik each morning.
The weather station used a hygrodeik to monitor humidity levels throughout the day.
You can determine the relative humidity by reading a hygrodeik correctly.
