Kamehameha I

proper noun
first king of Hawaii, 1758–1819
Pronunciation: / kəˌmeɪ.həˈmeɪ.hə ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / kəˌmeɪ.həˈmeɪ.hə ði ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
(1758–1819) The first king of Hawaii, famous for uniting the Hawaiian Islands under one rule.

Examples

Kamehameha I united islands
Kamehameha I established the Kingdom of Hawaii, bringing the islands together under his leadership.
Many statues of Kamehameha I can be found throughout Hawaii as a tribute to his legacy.
The unification of Hawaii was largely due to the military strategy and diplomacy of Kamehameha I.
