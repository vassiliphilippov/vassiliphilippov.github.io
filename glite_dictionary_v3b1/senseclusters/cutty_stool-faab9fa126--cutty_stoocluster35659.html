cutty stool

noun
low Scottish stool; church seat for public rebuke
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌti stuːl / (AmE), / ˈkʌti stuːl / (BrE)
A low stool, especially in Scotland; formerly, a seat in a church where someone was publicly rebuked for an offense.

Examples

The cutty stool seat
In old Scottish churches, the cutty stool was a symbol of public shame.
She restored an antique cutty stool she found at a local market.
The minister called the sinner to sit upon the cutty stool during the service.
