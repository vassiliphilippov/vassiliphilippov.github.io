tromp

noun
long tiring walk
7.6
Pronunciation: /trɑːmp/ (AmE), /trɒmp/ (BrE)
a long, tiring walk or the sound made by heavy, steady steps.

Examples

A long tromp
After the long tromp across the moor, everyone collapsed on the grass to rest.
The children returned home muddy and tired from their afternoon tromp through the woods.
We could hear the tromp of boots echoing down the hallway as the workers arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: march - a steady, regular walk, often over a long distance
  • antonym: stroll - a slow, relaxed walk
  • synonym: trudge - a long, slow, and tiring walk
