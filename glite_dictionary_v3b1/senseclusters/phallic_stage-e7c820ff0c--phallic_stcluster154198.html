phallic stage

noun
third psychosexual stage in psychoanalysis
Pronunciation: / ˈfælɪk steɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfælɪk steɪdʒ / (BrE)
In psychoanalytic theory, the third stage of a child's psychosexual development, occurring around ages 3 to 6, when the genitals become the primary focus of pleasure and awareness.

Examples

The phallic stage in development
According to Freud, the phallic stage plays a key role in shaping adult personality.
During the phallic stage, children reportedly become more aware of anatomical differences between sexes.
Some psychologists question the universality of the phallic stage as described in psychoanalytic theory.
