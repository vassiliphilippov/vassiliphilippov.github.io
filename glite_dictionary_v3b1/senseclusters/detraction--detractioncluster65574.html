detraction

noun
petty disparagement; belittling remark
3.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
an unfair or damaging remark or action that reduces the value or reputation of someone or something

Examples

A detraction from reputation
Every achievement was met with some form of detraction from her jealous peers.
He shrugged off the detraction and focused on his work.
The editor's review was fair and free of personal detraction.
Despite criticism and detraction, she remained confident in her abilities.
Her comments were seen as a deliberate detraction from his achievements.
The newspaper's article was full of detraction, damaging his reputation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disparagement - the act of speaking about someone or something in a derogatory or belittling manner
  • antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration
