Alexander the Great

proper noun
king of Macedon; ancient conqueror

(356–323 BC) King of Macedon; a military leader who conquered much of the ancient world, including Greece, Egypt, and Persia, and founded Alexandria.

Examples

The exploits of Alexander the Great have inspired countless legends and stories throughout history.
