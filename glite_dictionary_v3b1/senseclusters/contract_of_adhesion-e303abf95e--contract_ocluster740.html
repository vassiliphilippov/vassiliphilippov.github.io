contract of adhesion

noun
standard contract with unequal bargaining power
7.3 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.trækt ə æd.ˈhiː.ʒən/ /; / /ˈkɑːn.trækt ə ə.ˈhiː.ʒən/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.trækt ə æd.ˈhiː.ʒən/ /; / /ˈkɒn.trækt ə ə.ˈhiː.ʒən/ (BrE)
A contract, usually in a standard form, that gives one party (typically the weaker party) little or no ability to negotiate more favorable terms, often resulting in an imbalance of bargaining power.

Examples

An contract of adhesion form
Insurance policies are often drafted as a contract of adhesion, favoring the company over the policyholder.
Many consumers sign a contract of adhesion when downloading software without reading the terms.
The court ruled that the contract of adhesion was unconscionable due to its one-sided provisions.
