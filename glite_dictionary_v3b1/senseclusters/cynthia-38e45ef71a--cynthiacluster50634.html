Cynthia

proper noun
mythological name for Artemis, Greek goddess of the Moon
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnθiə / (AmE), / ˈsɪnθɪə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a poetic name for Artemis, the virgin goddess of the hunt and the Moon; daughter of Leto and twin sister of Apollo.

Examples

Cynthia in mythology
In mythology, Cynthia was often depicted alongside her twin brother Apollo.
Many ancient poets invoked Cynthia as an epithet for the moon goddess in their works.
The temple dedicated to Cynthia stood proudly atop Mount Cynthus on Delos.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Artemis - Greek goddess of the hunt, wilderness, and the Moon
  • synonym: Diana - Roman counterpart to Artemis, goddess of the Moon and hunt
