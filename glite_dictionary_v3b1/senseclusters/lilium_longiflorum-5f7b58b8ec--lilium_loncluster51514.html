Lilium longiflorum

noun
species of lily with white trumpet-shaped flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪliəm ˌlɔŋɡəˈflɔrəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪliəm ˌlɒŋɡɪˈflɔːrəm / (BrE)
A tall lily species with large, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, commonly known as the Easter lily.

Examples

The Lilium longiflorum flower
Gardeners admire the elegance of the Lilium longiflorum for its striking white blooms.
The Lilium longiflorum is often used to decorate churches during Easter celebrations.
The fragrance of Lilium longiflorum fills the room in early spring.
