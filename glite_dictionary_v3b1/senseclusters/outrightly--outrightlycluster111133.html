outrightly

adverb
completely, directly
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌraɪtli / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌraɪtli / (BrE)
in a direct, complete, or unhesitating way; without any reservation or doubt

Examples

To outrightly deny
He outrightly denied any involvement in the incident during the interview.
The proposal was outrightly dismissed by the committee after a brief discussion.
She outrightly refused to participate in the project, citing personal reasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: completely - in every way; totally
  • synonym: directly - in a straightforward or immediate way
  • antonym: hesitantly - in a way that shows uncertainty or pause
