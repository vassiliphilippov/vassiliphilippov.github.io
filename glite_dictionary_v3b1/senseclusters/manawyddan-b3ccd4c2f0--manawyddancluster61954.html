Manawyddan

proper noun
Celtic sea god in Welsh mythology
Pronunciation: / ˌmænəˈwɪdən / (AmE), / ˌmanəˈwɪðan / (BrE)
A major deity in Welsh mythology, known as a Celtic sea god and the son of Llyr.

Examples

Manawyddan son of Llyr
Manawyddan plays a central role in the Third Branch of the Mabinogi.
In Welsh legend, Manawyddan is celebrated for his wisdom and resourcefulness.
The tales often describe Manawyddan as a powerful figure connected with the sea and magic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Manannán mac Lir - Irish sea god, often considered equivalent or related to Manawyddan
