a shadow of its former self

noun
much diminished from former state
3.1
Pronunciation: / ə ˈʃædoʊ əv ɪts ˈfɔrmər sɛlf / (AmE), / ə ˈʃædəʊ əv ɪts ˈfɔːmə sɛlf / (BrE)
used to say that someone or something is much weaker, less important, or less impressive than they were in the past

Examples

A shadow of its former self after neglect
After years of neglect, the theater became a shadow of its former self and attracted very few visitors.
Since the injury, the athlete has been a shadow of his former self on the field.
The country is now a shadow of its former self, struggling to recover from the conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - a decrease in quality, strength, or value
  • antonym: restore - to bring back to a former or original state
