decoy

noun
person who lures into danger or trap
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdiˌkɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈdiː.kɔɪ / (BrE)
a person who entices someone into danger or a trap, usually as part of a plan or plot

Examples

A decoy person
She suspected that the invitation was just a decoy to get her alone.
The spy used a decoy to distract the guards while he entered the building.
The undercover agent acted as a decoy to draw out the suspects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bait - a person or thing used to lure someone into danger or a trap
  • synonym: lure - something that tempts or attracts with the promise of reward
  • antonym: protector - a person who keeps someone from harm or danger
