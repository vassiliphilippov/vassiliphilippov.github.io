post

verb
assign to a position or place (job/duty)
Pronunciation: / poʊst / (AmE), / pəʊst / (BrE)
to assign someone to a particular position, place, or duty, especially as part of their job or military service

Examples

To post a guard
After finishing his training, the army posted him overseas.
Diplomats are often posted to different countries throughout their careers.
She was posted to the company's London office for two years.
After training, the army decided to post her overseas for her first assignment.
Security guards are posted at the entrance to monitor visitors at all times.
They will post you to a new location when your current contract ends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assign - to allocate someone to a particular task or position
  • synonym: deploy - to move or position people for a particular purpose, especially in a job or military context
  • antonym: recall - to order someone to return from a post or position
  • synonym: station - to assign to a place for duty
