house of ill fame

noun
brothel or disreputable establishment
Pronunciation: / haʊs əv ɪl feɪm / (AmE), / haʊs əv ɪl feɪm / (BrE)
a place, especially a brothel, known for immoral or disreputable activities

Examples

A house of ill fame brothel
In Victorian novels, a house of ill fame often appears as a symbol of moral decline.
In the 19th century, visiting a house of ill fame was grounds for public scandal.
The detective suspected that the criminal operated out of a nearby house of ill fame.
