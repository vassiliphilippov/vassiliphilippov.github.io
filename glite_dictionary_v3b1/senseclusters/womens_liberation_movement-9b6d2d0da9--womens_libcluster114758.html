women's liberation movement

noun
campaigns for women's equality (1960s–70s)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪmɪnz ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈmuːvmənt / (AmE), / ˈwɪmɪnz ˌlɪbəˈreɪʃən ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
a series of campaigns and organized efforts in the 1960s and 1970s advocating for equal rights and opportunities for women

Examples

The women's liberation movement campaigns
Many influential books were published during the women's liberation movement that challenged traditional gender roles.
She became an activist during the women's liberation movement, fighting for fair treatment at work.
The women's liberation movement helped bring about significant changes in laws and attitudes toward gender equality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feminism - the advocacy of women's rights based on equality of the sexes
