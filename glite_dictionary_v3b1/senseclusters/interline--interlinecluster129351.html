interline

noun
note between lines
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or short note written between the lines of a text as an addition or explanation

Examples

An interline note
The editor added an interline to clarify the meaning of the original sentence.
Several interlines in the old book reveal how readers interpreted the text centuries ago.
A brief interline explained the unusual spelling found in the manuscript.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interlineation - the act of writing between the lines of a text
