agalactosis

noun
absence of milk secretion after childbirth
Pronunciation: / ˌæɡəˌlækˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌaɡəlækˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition in which a mother does not secrete milk from her breasts after childbirth.

Examples

Agalactosis after childbirth
Lactation consultants are sometimes called when agalactosis is suspected in new mothers.
Research is ongoing to determine causes and treatment for agalactosis in postpartum women.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with agalactosis after she was unable to breastfeed her newborn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agalactia - absence or failure of milk secretion
