Shasta daisy

noun
hybrid garden daisy with large white flowers
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæstə ˌdeɪzi / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːstə ˌdeɪzi / (BrE)
A hybrid garden plant with large white flower heads, resembling the oxeye daisy, often grown ornamentally.

Examples

White Shasta daisies
She planted several Shasta daisies along the walkway for their cheerful blooms.
The Shasta daisy is a popular choice for brightening up summer gardens.
Unlike wild daisies, the Shasta daisy produces larger and more striking flowers.

Related Terms
