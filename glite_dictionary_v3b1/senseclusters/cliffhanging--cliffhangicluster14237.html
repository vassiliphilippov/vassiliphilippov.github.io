cliffhanging

noun
suspenseful moment
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪfˌhænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈklɪfˌhænɪŋ / (BrE)
a situation or moment in a story that leaves the audience in suspense, not knowing what will happen next

Examples

A cliffhanging in a story
The movie ended with a dramatic cliffhanging that left everyone eager for the sequel.
Fans were frustrated by the show's unexpected cliffhanging at the end of the season.
The author is known for her skillful use of cliffhanging to keep readers turning the pages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cliffhanger - an ending that leaves the audience in suspense
  • antonym: resolution - the conclusion or solution to a story
