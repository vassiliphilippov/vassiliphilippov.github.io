rope ladder

noun
a ladder with rope sides
Pronunciation: / roʊp ˈlædər / (AmE), / rəʊp ˈladə / (BrE)
a ladder with ropes for the sides and rungs made of wood or other material, used especially where a rigid ladder is impractical

Examples

A rope ladder escape
A rope ladder is often used in emergency escape situations where a regular ladder cannot be used.
After the ship docked, the crew lowered a rope ladder for passengers to disembark.
The children climbed up the rope ladder to get into the treehouse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ladder - a structure for climbing up or down, typically with two side pieces and rungs
