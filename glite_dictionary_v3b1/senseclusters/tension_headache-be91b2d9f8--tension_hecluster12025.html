tension headache

noun
headache from tension or stress
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnʃən ˌhɛdˌeɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɛnʃən ˌhɛdeɪk / (BrE)
A mild to moderate headache typically felt at the back of the head or neck, often caused by muscle tension, stress, or fatigue.

Examples

A tension headache pain
After a long, stressful day at work, she developed a tension headache that lasted for hours.
He often experiences a tension headache after staring at his computer screen all day.
Regular stretching and relaxation exercises can help prevent tension headaches.

Related Terms

  • antonym: migraine - a severe recurring headache often accompanied by nausea and sensitivity to light
