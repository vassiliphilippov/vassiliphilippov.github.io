vena sigmoideus

noun
vein draining the sigmoid colon
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.nə sɪg.mɔɪ.ˈdiː.əs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə sɪg.mɔɪ.ˈdɪ.əs/ (AmE), /ˈviː.nə sɪg.mɔɪ.ˈdiː.əs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə sɪg.mɔɪ.ˈdɪ.əs/ (BrE)
A vein that is a tributary of the inferior mesenteric vein, responsible for draining blood from the sigmoid colon.

Examples

Vena sigmoideus vein
Abnormalities in the vena sigmoideus may affect the blood flow to the lower intestine.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully isolated the vena sigmoideus.
The vena sigmoideus is important for draining venous blood from the sigmoid colon.
