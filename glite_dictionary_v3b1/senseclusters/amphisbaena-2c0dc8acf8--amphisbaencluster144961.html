Amphisbaena

noun
genus of worm lizards
Pronunciation: / ˌæm.fɪsˈbiː.nə / (AmE), / ˌam.fɪsˈbiː.nə / (BrE)
A genus of worm lizards, the type genus of the family Amphisbaenidae, known for their elongated, limbless bodies.

Examples

The Amphisbaena genus
Researchers discovered a new species within the Amphisbaena genus in South America.
The Amphisbaena is characterized by a limbless body and is found in tropical regions.
Unlike many reptiles, the Amphisbaena has a distinctive skull structure adapted for burrowing.
