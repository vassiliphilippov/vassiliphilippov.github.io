lose one's mind

verb
become very anxious, confused, or upset
Pronunciation: / luːz wʌnz maɪnd / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz maɪnd / (BrE)
to become extremely anxious, confused, or upset, sometimes to the point of seeming irrational

Examples

To lose her mind quickly
After days without sleep, she thought she might lose her mind at any moment.
Parents sometimes feel like they will lose their minds when caring for newborn twins.
The constant noise from the construction nearly made him lose his mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: go insane - lose one's mental stability or reason
  • antonym: keep one's head - remain calm and sensible
