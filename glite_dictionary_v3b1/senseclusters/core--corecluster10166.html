core

noun
Earth's innermost layer
Pronunciation: / kɔr /; / kɔɹ / (AmE), / kɔː / (BrE)
the central part of a physical object, especially in scientific or technical contexts (such as the Earth or a nuclear reactor)

Examples

Earth's core
Earthquakes can provide information about the structure of the core.
Scientists study the Earth's core to understand how our planet was formed.
The core of the Earth is believed to be made mostly of iron and nickel.
After the meltdown, radiation levels around the core became dangerously high.
Safety protocols require regular inspections of the core in all nuclear power plants.
The fuel rods are inserted directly into the core to initiate the reaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crust - the outermost solid shell of the Earth
  • antonym: mantle - the layer of the Earth between the crust and the core
