monopoly

noun
exclusive control; sole possession
5.1
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːpəli / (AmE), / məˈnɒpəli / (BrE)
the exclusive control or possession of the supply of or trade in a product or service, especially when only one seller exists in a market

Examples

A monopoly on trade
No one group should have a monopoly over public information.
The government introduced new laws to break up the monopoly in the energy sector.
The telecommunications company once held a monopoly on all local phone services.
Anti-trust laws are designed to break up monopoly power and encourage competition.
Because the publisher held a monopoly on the textbook, prices remained very high.
The company maintained a monopoly on electricity in the region for decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym: competition - the act of competing, especially in business, as the opposite of monopoly
  • degree lower: oligopoly - market structure dominated by a small number of firms
