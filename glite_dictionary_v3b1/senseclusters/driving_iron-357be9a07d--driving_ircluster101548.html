driving iron

noun
long golf club with a vertical face
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪvɪŋ ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈdraɪvɪŋ ˈaɪən / (BrE)
a long golf club, typically with a nearly vertical face, used for driving the ball off the tee or from the fairway

Examples

A driving iron club
He reached for his driving iron to make the long shot down the fairway.
Older golfers often used a driving iron instead of a modern hybrid club.
The driving iron is known for its low trajectory and accuracy off the tee.
