Jamaat ul-Fuqra

proper noun
Islamic militant group founded in the 1980s
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒəˌmɑt ʊl fuˈkrɑ / (AmE), / dʒəˌmɑːt ʊl fuːˈkrɑː / (BrE)
an Islamic militant group founded in the 1980s, noted for seeking to purify Islam through violence, with cells in North America and the Caribbean that isolate themselves from Western culture and have targeted other Muslims they regard as heretics

Examples

Jamaat ul-Fuqra militant group
Authorities tracked the activities of Jamaat ul-Fuqra in several North American cities.
Members of Jamaat ul-Fuqra were linked to violent attacks against rival Islamic groups.
The teachings of Jamaat ul-Fuqra emphasize a strict interpretation of Islam, often isolating themselves from the broader community.
