alkylating agent

noun
drug that attacks DNA to treat cancer
4.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /æl.kɪ.ˈleɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ (AmE), /æl.kɪ.ˈleɪ.tɪŋ ˈeɪ.dʒənt/ (BrE)
a chemical substance, especially a drug, that treats certain cancers by attaching alkyl groups to DNA, thereby interfering with cell growth
