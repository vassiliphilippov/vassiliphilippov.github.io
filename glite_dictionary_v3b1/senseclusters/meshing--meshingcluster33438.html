meshing

noun
creating or operating a mesh wireless network
6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmeʃ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈmeʃ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in wireless networking) the process of creating or operating a mesh network in which devices (nodes) relay data for each other so coverage is extended without a single central hub.

Examples

Wi-Fi meshing
The city's public Wi-Fi relies on meshing so nearby routers can share bandwidth and coverage.
Their disaster-response plan uses rapid meshing to restore communications after infrastructure damage.
Many home systems advertise automatic meshing, letting users extend coverage without complex setup.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mesh network - a network topology where nodes forward data for other nodes to extend coverage and resilience
