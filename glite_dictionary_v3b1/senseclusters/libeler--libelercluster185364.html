libeler

noun
person who commits libel
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪbələr / (AmE), / ˈlʌɪbələ / (BrE)
a person who damages the reputation of another by making false and defamatory statements in writing or print

Examples

A libeler in court
A successful libeler can ruin someone’s reputation with nothing more than a pen.
She accused him of being a libeler for spreading malicious rumors in print.
The newspaper was sued after its chief libeler published a false story about the mayor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: libellee - a person against whom a libel action is filed
  • synonym: slanderer - a person who makes false spoken statements damaging to a person's reputation
