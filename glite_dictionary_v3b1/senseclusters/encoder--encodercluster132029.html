encoder

noun
data conversion device
5.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈkoʊ.dər/ /; / /en.ˈkoʊ.dər/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈkəʊ.də/ /; / /en.ˈkəʊ.də/ (BrE)
a device or program that converts information or data into a coded or different format, usually for processing, storage, or transmission

Examples

Audio encoder
The audio encoder compresses sound files to reduce their size for storage.
Modern televisions use a digital encoder to process incoming signals.
A video encoder is essential for live streaming high-quality content online.
The audio encoder compresses music files to reduce their size for streaming.
A video encoder is essential for live broadcasting over the internet.
Modern smartphones include a built-in encoder for processing camera images.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decoder - a device or program that converts encoded data back to its original format
