martensitic

adjective
of martensite steel
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːrtənˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɑːtənˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or having the structure of martensite, a very hard form of steel

Examples

Martensitic steel
The engineer recommended using martensitic steel for its superior hardness in the new tool design.
Many surgical instruments are made from martensitic stainless steel because it can be easily sterilized.
The knife's blade is crafted from martensitic material to ensure durability and strength.

Related Terms

  • antonym: austenitic - relating to austenite, another form of steel structure
