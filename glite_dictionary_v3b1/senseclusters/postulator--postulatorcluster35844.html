postulator

noun
Catholic official proposing sainthood
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstjəˈleɪtər / (AmE), / ˌpɒstjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a person who officially proposes or supports a candidate for beatification or canonization.

Examples

Church postulator
A new postulator was appointed to advance the cause of the saint.
During the process, the postulator interviewed witnesses who attested to the candidate's miracles.
The postulator gathered evidence to support the canonization of the local priest.
