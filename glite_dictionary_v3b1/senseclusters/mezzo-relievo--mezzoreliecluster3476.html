mezzo-relievo

noun
medium-depth sculptural relief
9.0 9.6
Pronunciation: /met.soʊ.rɪ.ˈliː.voʊ/ /; / /med.zoʊ.rɪ.ˈliː.voʊ/ (AmE), /met.səʊ.rɪ.ˈliː.vəʊ/ /; / /med.zəʊ.rɪ.ˈliː.vəʊ/ (BrE)
A sculptural relief in which the figures are raised halfway between bas-relief (low relief) and high relief.

Examples

A mezzo-relievo sculpture
Museum visitors admired the intricate mezzo-relievo panels set into the marble walls.
The ancient temple was adorned with a beautiful mezzo-relievo depicting scenes of daily life.
The artist chose mezzo-relievo for the frieze to achieve a balance between subtlety and depth.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bas-relief - a sculptural relief in which forms project only slightly from the background
  • degree higher: high relief - a sculptural relief in which forms stand out significantly from the background
