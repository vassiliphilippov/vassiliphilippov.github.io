regain

verb
recover something lost
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈɡeɪn / (BrE)
to obtain possession, use, or control of something again after losing it

Examples

To regain lost strength
After months of hard work, he was finally able to regain his strength.
She took a deep breath to regain her composure before speaking again.
The company is trying hard to regain its reputation after the scandal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to cease to have or possess something
  • synonym: recover - to return to a normal state after a setback or loss
