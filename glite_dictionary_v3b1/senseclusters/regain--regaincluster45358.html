Synonyms

  • get back
    1.6 90/m
    get back is a casual phrasal verb for recovering possession, while regain is a neutral verb used in many formal and informal contexts.
  • retake
    4.6 1/m
    retake is to take back control by force or action, while regain is to obtain control again more generally.
  • reclaim
    4.1 4/m
    reclaim is to demand or take back property or rights, while regain is a more general verb for getting something again.
  • take back
    3.0 5/m
    take back is to regain possession of something given or lent, while regain is used broadly for getting control again.
  • recover
    2.5 30/m
    recover means return to a previous state or get back lost things, while regain focuses specifically on obtaining control or possession again.
  • retrieve
    3.7 11/m
    retrieve means go and bring something back, often a physical item, while regain covers getting possession or control again.
  • restore
    3.4 15/m
    restore means give something back or return it to former condition, while regain means to obtain possession or control again.
  • win back
    3.4 2/m
    win back means recover something or someone's trust by effort, while regain is neutral and does not imply that struggle.
  • reacquire
    5.5 0.3/m
    reacquire means get something again, often by buying or formal action, while regain is more general and not usually transactional.
