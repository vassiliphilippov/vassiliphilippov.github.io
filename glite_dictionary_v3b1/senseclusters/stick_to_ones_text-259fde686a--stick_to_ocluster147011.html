stick to one's text

verb
follow a prepared script while speaking
Pronunciation: / stɪk tə wʌnz tɛkst / (AmE), / stɪk tʊ wʌnz tɛkst / (BrE)
to follow a prepared script or written material while speaking, without improvisation or deviation

Examples

To stick to her text
During the press conference, the spokesperson chose to stick to her text and didn't answer any unexpected questions.
He knew he would get nervous, so he made sure to stick to his text during the presentation.
If you stick to your text, you'll avoid making mistakes or saying something you regret.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stick to the script - to follow the written or planned words exactly
