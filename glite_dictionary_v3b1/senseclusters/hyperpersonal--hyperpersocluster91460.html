hyperpersonal

adjective
very intimate communication
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈpɜrsənl / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈpɜːsənl / (BrE)
describing communication that is more intimate, personal, or intense than typical face-to-face interaction

Examples

Hyperpersonal online chats
Some people find that online chats can become hyperpersonal much faster than in-person conversations.
The hyperpersonal nature of their messages surprised both of them after only a few days of talking.
Researchers study how hyperpersonal communication develops in digital relationships.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impersonal - not showing or involving personal feelings
  • synonym: intimate - having a close, personal, or private relationship
