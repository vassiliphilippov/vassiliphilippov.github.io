sealable

adjective
able to be made confidential
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːləbəl / (AmE), / ˈsiːləbəl / (BrE)
able to be officially closed or made confidential, especially by legal order, as with documents or records

Examples

Sealable court file
The court determined that the documents were sealable due to the sensitive information they contained.
Lawyers argued whether the evidence should be considered sealable under the current privacy laws.
Not every record is sealable; some must remain accessible to the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confidential - meant to be kept secret or private
  • antonym: open - not closed or restricted; accessible to all
