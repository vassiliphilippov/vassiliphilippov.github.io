King of Great Britain

noun
male sovereign ruler of Great Britain
4.2
Pronunciation: / kɪŋ əv ɡreɪt ˈbrɪtən / (AmE), / kɪŋ əv ɡreɪt ˈbrɪt.ən / (BrE)
the male sovereign ruler of England, Scotland, and Ireland after the union of the crowns; the reigning king of Great Britain

Examples

The King of Great Britain
After Queen Anne, the first King of Great Britain was George I in 1714.
King George III was the King of Great Britain during the American Revolution.
The coronation of the King of Great Britain was attended by thousands of dignitaries.
