mercantilistic

adjective
profit-focused

Pronunciation: /mɜːr.kən.tɪ.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /mɜːr.kæn.tɪ.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /mɜː.kən.tɪ.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /mɜː.kæn.tɪ.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of mercantilism, especially focusing on trade, profit, or commercial advantage

Examples

A mercantilistic policy
The government adopted a mercantilistic policy to boost exports and protect local industries.
Her mercantilistic attitude sometimes made her seem uninterested in social or environmental issues.
Critics argue that a mercantilistic approach can harm long-term relationships with trading partners.
The government adopted a mercantilistic approach to boost national industry and exports.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of mercantilistic policies in early modern Europe.
Critics argue that mercantilistic strategies can lead to trade wars and economic inefficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercial - concerned with buying and selling or making a profit
  • antonym: laissez-faire - an economic policy of minimal government interference
  • synonym: mercantile - relating to trade or commerce
  • antonym: philanthropic - seeking to promote the welfare of others, often through charitable acts
  • synonym: protectionist - favoring government restrictions on imports
