cathode

noun
positively charged terminal of a cell or battery
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθˌoʊd / (AmE), / ˈkaθəʊd / (BrE)
the positively charged terminal of a voltaic cell or battery from which electric current flows into an external circuit

Examples

Battery cathode
During discharge, the cathode in a lead-acid battery is made of lead dioxide.
Make sure to connect the wire to the cathode to complete the circuit properly.
The cathode of a battery is the terminal from which electrons flow outward in a circuit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anode - the negatively charged terminal of a voltaic cell or battery
