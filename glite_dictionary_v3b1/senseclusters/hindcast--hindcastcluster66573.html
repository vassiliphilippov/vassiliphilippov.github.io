hindcast

verb
to model past events
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhaɪnd.kæst/ (AmE), /ˈhaɪnd.kɑːst/ (BrE)
to use a model to simulate or reproduce past conditions or events, especially to test or validate the model's accuracy

Examples

To hindcast a storm
Researchers hindcast the hurricane's path to check the reliability of their weather model.
The team used computer simulations to hindcast ocean temperatures from the previous decade.
By hindcasting past climate events, scientists can improve their predictions for the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forecast - to predict future events or conditions using a model
  • synonym: simulate - to imitate the conditions or process of something, often using a model
