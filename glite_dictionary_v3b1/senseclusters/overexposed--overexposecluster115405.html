overexposed

adjective
too much exposure
4.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪk.ˈspoʊzd/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪk.ˈspəʊzd/ (BrE)
having received too much light or attention, often resulting in negative or undesirable effects

Examples

An overexposed celebrity
The celebrity became overexposed after appearing on every major talk show in one week.
This photo is overexposed, making the faces look washed out and too bright.
Some artists avoid becoming overexposed by limiting their public appearances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: saturated - filled or supplied to capacity, often with color or attention
  • antonym: underexposed - having received too little light or attention
