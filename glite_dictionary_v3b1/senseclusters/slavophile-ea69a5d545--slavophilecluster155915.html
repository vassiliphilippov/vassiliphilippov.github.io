Slavophile

adjective
admiring Slavic culture
Pronunciation: / ˈslævəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈslævəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
showing strong admiration for Slavic peoples, culture, or the ideas of Slavophilism

Examples

Slavophile attitude
The writer's Slavophile views were evident in his praise of traditional Slavic customs.
Her Slavophile attitude led her to study the languages and history of Eastern Europe.
Many intellectuals in the 19th century held Slavophile beliefs, opposing Western influence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Russophile - showing strong admiration for Russian people or culture
  • synonym: Slavophilism - the belief in the value and superiority of Slavic culture and traditions
  • antonym: Westernizer - favoring the adoption of Western European ideas and culture
