isostatic

adjective
equal pressure applied
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈstætɪk / (BrE)
relating to or produced by equal pressure applied from all directions, especially in industrial or scientific processes.

Examples

Isostatic pressing process
The engineers used an isostatic process to ensure the material was evenly compressed.
In geology, isostatic adjustment explains how the Earth's crust responds to changes in weight.
The laboratory tested the strength of ceramics after isostatic pressing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrostatic - relating to the pressure exerted by a fluid at rest
