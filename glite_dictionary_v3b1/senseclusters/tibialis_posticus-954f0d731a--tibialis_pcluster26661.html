tibialis posticus

noun
deep muscle in lower leg
8.8
Pronunciation: /tɪ.ˈbaɪ.ə.lɪs ˈpɑːs.tɪ.kəs/ (AmE), /tɪ.ˈbaɪ.ə.lɪs ˈpɒs.tɪ.kəs/ (BrE)
a deep muscle located in the back of the lower leg, involved in foot and ankle movement

Examples

The tibialis posticus muscle
Injury to the tibialis posticus can result in flatfoot deformity if left untreated.
Surgeons repaired the torn tibialis posticus tendon successfully after the accident.
The tibialis posticus helps stabilize the ankle and supports the arch of the foot.
