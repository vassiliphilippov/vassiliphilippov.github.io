coagulative

adjective
causing coagulation
8.2
Pronunciation: / koʊˈæɡjələtɪv / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or causing coagulation, especially describing tissue that becomes firm and retains its structure after cell death due to protein denaturation.

Examples

Coagulative necrosis
The pathologist identified coagulative necrosis in the liver sample after the blood supply was cut off.
In myocardial infarction, heart tissue often undergoes coagulative changes due to lack of oxygen.
The coagulative process preserves the basic structure of the affected organ for several days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulate - to change from a liquid to a thickened or solid state
  • antonym: liquefactive - relating to tissue necrosis where tissue becomes liquid
