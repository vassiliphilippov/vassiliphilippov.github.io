olfactory bulb

noun
structure in the brain for processing smell
Pronunciation: / ɑlˈfæktəri bʌlb / (AmE), / ɒlˈfæktəri bʌlb / (BrE)
a rounded, neural structure at the base of the brain involved in processing smells, located just above the nasal cavities

Examples

The olfactory bulb structure
An injury to the olfactory bulb can lead to a loss of the sense of smell.
Scientists study the olfactory bulb to better understand how the brain processes scents.
The olfactory bulb plays a critical role in detecting and interpreting different odors.
