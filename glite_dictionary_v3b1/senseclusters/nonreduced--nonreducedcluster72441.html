nonreduced

adjective
with nilpotent elements
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.rɪˈduːst / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈdjuːst / (BrE)
describing a mathematical object, such as a ring or scheme, that contains nilpotent elements and is not reduced

Examples

Nonreduced scheme
In algebraic geometry, a nonreduced scheme may have extra structure due to nilpotent elements.
The ring was classified as nonreduced because it included elements whose powers became zero.
Understanding nonreduced objects is important for advanced studies in modern mathematics.
