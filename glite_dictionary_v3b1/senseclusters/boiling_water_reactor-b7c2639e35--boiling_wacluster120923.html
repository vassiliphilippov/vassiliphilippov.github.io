a type of nuclear reactor that uses water as both coolant and moderator, allowing the water to boil in the reactor core to produce steam that drives a turbine

Examples

A boiling water reactor design
A boiling water reactor typically requires meticulous safety protocols to manage steam pressure.
The boiling water reactor is widely used in several countries for generating electricity.
Unlike other designs, the boiling water reactor produces steam directly inside the reactor core.
