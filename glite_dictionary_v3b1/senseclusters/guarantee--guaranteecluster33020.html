guarantee

verb
formally assure quality or condition
Pronunciation: / ˌɡærənˈti /; / ˌɡɛrənˈti / (AmE), / ˌɡærənˈtiː / (BrE)
to promise, assure, or make certain that something will happen or that something is of a certain quality

Examples

To guarantee success
He guaranteed that if the item broke, they would replace it for free.
The company cannot guarantee the accuracy of the information provided.
We guarantee all our products against defects for two years.
Regular exercise does not guarantee good health, but it certainly helps.
She worked hard to guarantee her project's success.
The company cannot guarantee delivery by tomorrow, but they will try their best.
If you buy this appliance, I personally guarantee its reliability.
The company guarantees all its products against defects for two years.
We cannot guarantee the outcome, but we will do our best.
