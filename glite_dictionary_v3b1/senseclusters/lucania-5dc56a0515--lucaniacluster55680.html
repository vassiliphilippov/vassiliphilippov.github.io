Lucania

proper noun
ancient region in southern Italy
Pronunciation: / luˈkeɪniə / (AmE), / luˈkeɪnɪə / (BrE)
an ancient region in southern Italy, forming part of the instep of the Italian peninsula, roughly corresponding to modern-day Basilicata.

Examples

Lucania region

The ancient Greeks established colonies in Lucania during the 6th century BCE.
