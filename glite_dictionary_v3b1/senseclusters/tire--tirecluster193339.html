tire

verb
become bored or lose interest
Pronunciation: / taɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪə /; / taɪə / (BrE)
to become bored with something or make someone lose interest

Examples

Tire of waiting
After a few hours, the children began to tire of the same old games.
He quickly tired of the repetitive tasks at work.
She never seems to tire of reading mystery novels.
If you tire of this book, feel free to choose another.
Long meetings can really tire even the most enthusiastic participants.
The lecture on tax law began to tire the audience after an hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bore - to make someone feel weary and uninterested
  • antonym: interest - the feeling of wanting to know or engage with something
  • synonym: weary - to make someone tired or bored
