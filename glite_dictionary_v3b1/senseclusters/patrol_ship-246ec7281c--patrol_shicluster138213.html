patrol ship

noun
vessel assigned to patrol an area
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈtroʊl ʃɪp/ (AmE), /pə.ˈtrəʊl ʃɪp/ (BrE)
A ship assigned to monitor and guard a designated area, especially for military or law enforcement purposes.

Examples

A patrol ship monitors area
A patrol ship conducted regular sweeps of the harbor throughout the night.
International waters are frequently monitored by at least one patrol ship at any given time.
The navy deployed a patrol ship to safeguard the coastline against unauthorized vessels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: merchant ship - a ship used for commercial transport, not patrolling
  • synonym: patrol boat - a smaller vessel assigned to patrol duties
  • synonym: warship - a ship built and equipped for naval warfare
