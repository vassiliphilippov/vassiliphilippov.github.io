Nagasaki

proper noun
city in southern Japan; atomic bombing site
4.0
Pronunciation: / nɑːɡəˈsɑːki / (AmE), / næɡəˈsɑːki / (BrE)
A city in southern Japan on the island of Kyushu, known as an important port and shipbuilding center, and as the second city to be hit by an atomic bomb in 1945.

Examples

Nagasaki city in Japan
Nagasaki was devastated by an atomic bomb during World War II on August 9, 1945.
Many tourists visit Nagasaki each year to learn about its unique history and culture.
The port of Nagasaki has been a crucial point of trade in Japan for centuries.
