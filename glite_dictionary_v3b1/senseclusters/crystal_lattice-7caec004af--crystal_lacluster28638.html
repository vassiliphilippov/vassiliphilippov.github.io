crystal lattice

noun
geometric arrangement in a crystal
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəl ˈlætɪs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəl ˈlætɪs / (BrE)
a three-dimensional geometric arrangement of atoms, molecules, or ions forming the structure of a crystal

Examples

The crystal lattice structure
Impurities can disrupt the crystal lattice, affecting a material's electrical properties.
Salt dissolves in water as the crystal lattice breaks apart into individual ions.
The crystal lattice of diamond gives it remarkable strength and hardness.
