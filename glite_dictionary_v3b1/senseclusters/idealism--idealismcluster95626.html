idealism

noun
belief in or pursuit of ideals over reality
1.0
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdiəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪə.lɪ.zəm /; / aɪˈdɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
The attitude or belief in pursuing noble ideals or perfection, often ignoring practical realities.

Examples

Youthful idealism
Despite his idealism, the harsh realities of politics soon challenged his views.
His idealism led him to believe that every problem could be solved with goodwill.
She admired his idealism, but sometimes wished he were more practical.
Despite his idealism, he was sometimes frustrated by the slow pace of progress.
Her idealism inspired many people to work toward positive change in their community.
The novel is marked by the youthful idealism of its main characters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realism - the attitude of accepting things as they are and dealing with them practically
