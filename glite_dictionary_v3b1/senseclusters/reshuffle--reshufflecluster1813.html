reshuffle

noun
reorganization of positions or roles
5.3
Pronunciation: / riˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / riˈʃʌfəl / (BrE)
an act of reorganizing or changing the order of something, especially people or positions in an organization

Examples

Cabinet reshuffle
After the election, the prime minister announced a reshuffle of the cabinet.
The company underwent a major reshuffle to improve efficiency.
There will be a reshuffle of staff next month to address changing business needs.
A cabinet reshuffle was announced following the prime minister's decision.
After a complaint about fairness, the dealer performed a reshuffle of the deck.
The gambler requested a reshuffle to avoid suspicion of cheating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reorganization - the act of organizing something again
  • synonym: shake-up - a thorough restructuring or reorganization
  • synonym: shuffle - to mix or rearrange, especially cards
