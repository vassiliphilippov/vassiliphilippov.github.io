damar

noun
a hard resin from certain trees, used in varnishes
6.3 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːˌmɑr / (AmE), / ˈdæmɑː / (BrE)
A hard, brittle resin obtained from various trees, especially those of the Dipterocarpaceae family and the genus Agathis, used in varnishes, lacquers, and as an ingredient in certain artistic materials.

Examples

Damar resin
Harvesters tap the trees carefully to collect damar resin without harming the forest.
Many traditional artists use damar varnish to protect their oil paintings.
The collection includes carvings coated with damar to give them a glossy finish.
