potato tuber moth

noun
moth whose larvae damage potatoes
6.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪtoʊ ˈtuːbər mɒθ / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ ˈtjuːbə mɒθ / (BrE)
a greyish-brown moth whose larvae are known as potato tuberworms and are pests of potato crops

Examples

The potato tuber moth larvae
Farmers must monitor for signs of the potato tuber moth to protect their crops from damage.
Proper storage of harvested potatoes helps prevent infestation by the potato tuber moth.
The potato tuber moth is a significant pest in regions where potatoes are cultivated.
