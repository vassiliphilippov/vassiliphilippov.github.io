larboard

noun
the left side of a ship (now archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːr.bɔrd / (AmE), / ˈlɑː.bəd / (BrE)
the left side of a ship or aircraft to someone facing the bow or nose; now chiefly replaced by 'port'

Examples

Larboard side of the ship
Heavy waves battered the larboard during the storm.
The captain issued orders from the larboard side of the deck.
The sailors hauled in the ropes on the larboard as they prepared to dock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: port - the left side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward
  • antonym: starboard - the right side of a ship or aircraft when facing forward
