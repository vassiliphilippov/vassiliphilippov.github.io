Pulicidae

noun
family of common fleas
Pronunciation: / ˌpjuːlɪˈsaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌpjuːlɪˈsiːdiː / (BrE)
A family of fleas that commonly infest humans and domestic animals.

Examples

Pulicidae flea family
Research on Pulicidae has advanced our understanding of flea-borne diseases.
The Pulicidae family includes several species that are notorious pests of dogs and cats.
Veterinarians are trained to recognize infestations caused by Pulicidae in pets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flea - small wingless jumping insect that feeds on the blood of mammals and birds
