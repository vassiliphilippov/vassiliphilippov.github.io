damper

noun
device that reduces oscillations
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈdæmpə / (BrE)
a device that reduces the amplitude of oscillations in electronic, mechanical, acoustical, or aerodynamic systems.

Examples

Shock damper
An acoustic damper helps prevent unwanted vibrations in the piano's soundboard.
Engineers installed a special damper to minimize vibrations in the high-rise building during strong winds.
The car's shock absorber acts as a damper to reduce spring oscillations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dampener - a device that reduces vibration or oscillation
