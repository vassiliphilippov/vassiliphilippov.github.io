Vigna sesquipedalis

noun
bean with very long pods
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪɡnə ˌsɛskwɪpəˈdeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈvɪɡnə ˌsɛskwɪpɪˈdɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A species of bean native to South America, known for its very long, succulent pods, commonly used as a vegetable.

Examples

Vigna sesquipedalis beans
Farmers in the region grow Vigna sesquipedalis for its tender and lengthy green beans.
She identified the plant as Vigna sesquipedalis due to its exceptionally long, edible pods.
The traditional dish required fresh Vigna sesquipedalis pods, sliced and sautéed with spices.
