flare up

verb
erupt or intensify suddenly
Pronunciation: / flɛr ʌp / (AmE), / fleə ʌp / (BrE)
to suddenly become more intense, active, or worse (such as a fire, conflict, emotion, or medical condition)

Examples

An argument flares up
An argument flared up during the meeting over the new policy.
My allergies tend to flare up in the spring when pollen levels rise.
Tensions between the two groups can often flare up without warning.
Everyone panicked when the campfire flared up unexpectedly during the night.
Tempers flared up again despite attempts to calm the group.
The old wound flared up after he started running regularly.

The old injury can flare up if he puts too much strain on it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: die down - to become gradually less intense or active
  • synonym: erupt - to burst forth or break out suddenly
  • synonym: ignite - to catch fire or cause to catch fire
  • antonym: subside - to become less intense, severe, or active
