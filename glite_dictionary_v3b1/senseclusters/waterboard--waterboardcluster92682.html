waterboard

verb
to simulate drowning
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəbɔːd / (BrE)
to torture someone by simulating drowning, typically by pouring water over a cloth covering the face

Examples

To waterboard a prisoner
The authorities were accused of trying to waterboard the suspect during the interrogation.
Human rights organizations strongly condemn the use of waterboarding as an interrogation method.
He testified that he was waterboarded while in captivity.
The suspect was waterboarded in an attempt to extract information during the investigation.
Human rights organizations have condemned the use of waterboarding as a form of torture.
He admitted that he had waterboarded prisoners while serving overseas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: torture - to inflict severe pain as a punishment or to force information
