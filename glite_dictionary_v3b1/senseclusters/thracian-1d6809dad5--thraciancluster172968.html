Thracian

noun
inhabitant of ancient Thrace
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθreɪʃən / (BrE)
A person who lived in or came from ancient Thrace, a historical region in Southeast Europe.

Examples

Ancient Thracian warrior
A skilled Thracian warrior was often recruited into the Roman army.
Historians believe that the ancient Thracians had a distinct language and culture.
The tombs discovered in Bulgaria belonged to a noble Thracian family.
