tarsal

noun
bone of the ankle or hindfoot
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑr.səl / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.səl / (BrE)
any of the bones comprising the tarsus, which forms the ankle and back part of the foot in humans and other vertebrates

Examples

A tarsal bone
An injury to a tarsal can affect your ability to walk comfortably.
Archaeologists discovered well-preserved tarsal bones at the ancient burial site.
The doctor explained that the broken tarsal would require several weeks to heal.
