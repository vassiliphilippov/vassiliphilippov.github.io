conniver

noun
one who enables wrongdoing
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈnaɪvər / (AmE), / kəˈnaɪvə / (BrE)
a person who secretly allows or supports wrongdoing by failing to act or by giving silent approval

Examples

Silent conniver
The corrupt official was exposed as a conniver who let illegal deals happen under his watch.
She refused to be a conniver and reported the cheating to the authorities.
Many saw him as a silent conniver rather than an active participant in the scheme.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime or wrongdoing
  • degree lower: bystander - a person who is present but does not take part in an event
  • antonym: whistleblower - a person who reports wrongdoing or illegal activity
