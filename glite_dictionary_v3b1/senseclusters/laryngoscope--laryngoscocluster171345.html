laryngoscope

noun
instrument for examining the larynx
7.3 7.1
Pronunciation: /lɑːr.ˈɪŋ.gə.skoʊp/ /; / /lɑːr.ˈɪn.dʒə.skoʊp/ (AmE), /lə.ˈrɪŋ.gə.skəʊp/ /; / /lə.ˈrɪn.dʒə.skəʊp/ (BrE)
a medical instrument used to examine the larynx, especially to facilitate observation or medical procedures involving the vocal cords and throat.

Examples

A laryngoscope device
Advances in laryngoscope design have made intubation safer and more efficient.
During the procedure, the anesthesiologist inserted a laryngoscope to visualize the airway.
The doctor used a laryngoscope to examine the patient's vocal cords for any abnormalities.
