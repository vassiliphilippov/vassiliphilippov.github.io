founder

verb
to fill and sink (ship or structure)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndə / (BrE)
(of a ship or other structure) to fill with water and sink below the surface.

Examples

Ship founders at sea
Several vessels have foundered in these treacherous waters over the years.
The ship began to founder after striking the hidden reef.
Water rushed into the boat, causing it to founder rapidly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sink - to go down below the surface of something, especially water
