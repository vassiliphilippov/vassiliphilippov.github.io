free thought

noun
belief in religion based on reason, not revelation
3.6
Pronunciation: / fri θɔt / (AmE), / friː θɔːt / (BrE)
The belief in religious or theological ideas based on reason and evidence, rather than revelation, tradition, or authority.

Examples

Free thought belief
Public debates about free thought encouraged individuals to form their own beliefs rather than accept established doctrine.
She identified with free thought, rejecting dogma and embracing rational inquiry on spiritual matters.
The movement of free thought gained momentum during the Enlightenment, challenging traditional religious authority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freethinker - a person who forms opinions about religion on the basis of reason rather than tradition or authority
