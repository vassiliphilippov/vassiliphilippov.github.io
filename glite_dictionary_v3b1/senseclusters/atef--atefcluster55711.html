atef

noun
Egyptian feathered crown
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːtɛf / (AmE), / ˈɑːtɛf / (BrE)
a white crown with ostrich feathers worn by ancient Egyptian gods and pharaohs, especially Osiris

Examples

The atef crown
The ancient statue of Osiris is shown wearing the distinctive atef on his head.
In Egyptian art, the atef symbolizes divine authority and connection to the gods.
The museum displayed a replica of the atef crown used in religious ceremonies.
