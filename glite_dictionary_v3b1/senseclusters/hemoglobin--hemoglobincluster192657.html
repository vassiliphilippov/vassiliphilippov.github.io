hemoglobin

noun
oxygen-carrying blood protein
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn /; / hiːməˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
a red protein in the red blood cells of vertebrates that carries oxygen from the lungs to tissues throughout the body

Examples

Red hemoglobin protein

Scientists study how hemoglobin releases oxygen under different conditions.

The haemoglobin in red blood cells binds to oxygen for transport around the body.
