phoronid

noun
marine tube-dwelling wormlike animal
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /fə.ˈrɑː.nɪd/ /; / /fə.ˈrɔː.nɪd/ (AmE), /fə.ˈrɒn.ɪd/ (BrE)
A wormlike, hermaphroditic marine animal of the phylum Phoronida, living in tubes in the mud or sand on the sea bottom.

Examples

A phoronid worm
Many phoronids secrete tubes in which they live for protection from predators.
Scientists discovered a colony of phoronids burrowing in the muddy seabed near the coral reef.
The unique feeding structure of a phoronid helps it capture plankton from the surrounding water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemichordate - member of another phylum of similar marine worms, closely related group
