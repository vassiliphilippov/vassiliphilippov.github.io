rod

noun
long, thin piece of material
2.1 14/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ‘d / (AmE), / rÉ’d / (BrE)
a long, thin straight piece of metal, wood, or other material, typically used for a particular purpose

Examples

A metal rod
He used a thin wooden rod to stir the mixture.
The curtain hung from a metal rod above the window.
Workers reinforced the concrete with steel rods.

Synonyms

  • pole
    1.0 25/m
    a pole is a long, usually round stick used to support or hold things, while a rod is a thinner bar used for many tasks.
  • shaft
    4.2 8.40/m
    a shaft is the long central part inside tools or machines, while a rod is a simple straight bar used on its own.

Related Terms
