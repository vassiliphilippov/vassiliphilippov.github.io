lupin

noun
plant with colorful flower spikes (genus Lupinus)
Pronunciation: / ˈluːpən / (AmE), / ˈluːpɪn / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Lupinus, typically having erect spikes of colorful flowers, especially purplish-blue, and compound leaves.

Examples

Purple lupin flowers
Farmers sometimes grow lupin to improve soil fertility and attract pollinators.
She planted several varieties of lupin along the path for their vibrant colors.
The garden was filled with tall lupin blooms swaying gently in the breeze.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lupine - alternative spelling for 'lupin', especially in American English; plant of genus Lupinus
