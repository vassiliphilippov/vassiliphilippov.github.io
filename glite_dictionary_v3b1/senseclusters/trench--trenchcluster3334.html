trench

noun
long, narrow ditch in the ground
2.7
Pronunciation: / trɛntʃ / (AmE), / trɛntʃ / (BrE)
a long, narrow ditch in the ground, especially for drainage, protection, or as a military fortification

Examples

A muddy trench
During World War I, soldiers spent months living in muddy trenches.
The workers dug a trench along the side of the road for the new water pipe.
We planted the seeds in a shallow trench to help them grow.
After days of rain, the trench was filled with water, making movement difficult.
During World War I, life in the trench was notoriously harsh and dangerous.
The soldiers took cover in the trench as artillery fire rained down.
