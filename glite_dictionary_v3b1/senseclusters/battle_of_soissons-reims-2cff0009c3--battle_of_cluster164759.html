Battle of Soissons-Reims

noun
World War I battle in France, May 1918
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbæt.əl əv ˈswɑː.sən riːmz/ /; / /ˈbæt.əl əv ˈswɑː.sən reɪmz/ (AmE), /ˈbæt.əl əv ˈswɒ.sən reɪmz/ /; / /ˈbæt.əl əv ˈswɑː.sən reɪmz/ /; / /ˈbæt.əl əv ˈswɒ.sən riːmz/ (BrE)
a battle during World War I in May 1918, in which German forces attempted an offensive before American troop strength became overwhelming; although initially successful tactically, the attack ultimately failed strategically

Examples

The Battle of Soissons-Reims in 1918
During the Battle of Soissons-Reims, the German forces made tactical gains but ultimately failed strategically.
Historians often analyze the Battle of Soissons-Reims as a turning point in World War I.
The Battle of Soissons-Reims highlighted the growing influence of American troops on the Western Front.
