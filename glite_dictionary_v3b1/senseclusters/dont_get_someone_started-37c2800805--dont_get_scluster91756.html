don't get someone started

x
warn against raising a topic someone talks about too much
5.0
Pronunciation: / doʊnt ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌn ˈstɑːrtəd / (AmE), / dəʊnt ɡɛt ˈsʌmwʌn ˈstɑːtɪd / (BrE)
Used to warn that if a particular topic is brought up, someone will talk about it at length, often with emotion or annoyance.

Examples

Don't get her started
Every time you mention her cat, don't get her started, she has a million stories.
Honestly, don't get me started about the traffic in this city!
Please, don't get him started on politics; we'll be here all night.
