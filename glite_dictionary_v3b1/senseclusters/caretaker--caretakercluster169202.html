caretaker

noun
temporary official; interim office-holder
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrˌteɪkər / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌteɪkə / (BrE)
a person who temporarily performs the duties of an official or office until a permanent replacement is appointed

Examples

Caretaker government
As caretaker, his primary role was to maintain stability during the transition.
She was appointed caretaker after the manager resigned unexpectedly.
The board named him caretaker until a new CEO could be found.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acting - someone temporarily filling a position or office
  • synonym: interim - a temporary or provisional official
  • antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely, not temporary
