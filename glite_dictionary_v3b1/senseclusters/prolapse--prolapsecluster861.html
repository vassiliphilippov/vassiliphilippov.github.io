prolapse

verb
(of a body part) slip out of place
Pronunciation: / proʊˈlæps / (AmE), / prəˈlæps / (BrE)
(of a body part or organ) to slip or fall out of its normal position, especially through the opening it normally occupies.

Examples

To prolapse after birth
After childbirth, some women develop a prolapsed uterus, requiring medical attention.
If the vaginal walls prolapse, they may protrude outside the body.
The surgeon explained how a disc in the spine can prolapse and cause pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dislocate - to disturb the normal position of a body part, often a joint
