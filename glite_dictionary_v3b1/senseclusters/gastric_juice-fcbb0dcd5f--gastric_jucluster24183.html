gastric juice

noun
digestive fluid in the stomach
4.1 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈgæs.trɪk dʒuːs/ (AmE), /ˈgæs.trɪk dʒuːs/ (BrE)
the digestive fluid produced by glands in the stomach, containing hydrochloric acid, enzymes such as pepsin, and mucin

Examples

Gastric juice production
Excess production of gastric juice can lead to stomach ulcers and discomfort.
Scientists studied the composition of gastric juice in different animal species.
The stomach secretes gastric juice to help break down food during digestion.
