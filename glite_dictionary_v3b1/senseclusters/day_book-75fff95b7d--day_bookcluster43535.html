day book

noun
police station's daily event record
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ bʊk / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ bʊk / (BrE)
a daily written record of events and activities kept at a police station, such as details of arrests or incidents

Examples

Police day book
Each morning, the chief inspected the day book to keep track of overnight arrests and activities.
The sergeant made sure every incident was logged in the day book before his shift ended.
When reviewing last week's day book, the officers noticed a pattern in the reported burglaries.
