debit card

noun
bank card for direct payments
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛbɪt kɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdɛbɪt kɑːd / (BrE)
A plastic card that allows the holder to withdraw money or pay for purchases, with payments deducted directly from the holder's bank account.

Examples

Use a debit card
After losing his debit card, he immediately called the bank to block it.
I used my debit card to pay for the groceries at the supermarket.
Many people prefer using a debit card instead of cash for daily transactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: credit card - A card allowing the holder to purchase goods or services on credit
