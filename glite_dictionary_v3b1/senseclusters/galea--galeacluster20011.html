galea

noun
helmet-shaped plant or animal part
9.3 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪliə / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪlɪə / (BrE)
a helmet-shaped organ or structure, especially a vaulted and enlarged petal as seen in certain flowers such as Aconitum

Examples

A galea petal
In many orchids, a petal is modified into a galea to shield the pollen.
The insect’s mouthparts include a galea aiding in feeding on nectar.
The upper sepal of monkshood forms a distinctive galea that protects the flower’s reproductive parts.
