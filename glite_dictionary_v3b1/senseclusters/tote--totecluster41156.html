tote

verb
carry or haul something
Pronunciation: / toʊt / (AmE), / təʊt / (BrE)
to carry or move something, especially if it is heavy or awkward

Examples

To tote heavy boxes
He had to tote three heavy boxes up the stairs by himself.
She was toting her backpack and two shopping bags through the crowded station.
Volunteers toted supplies to the disaster site all afternoon.
She totes her groceries home every week from the market.
The workers toted boxes up the stairs all afternoon.
He totes his backpack everywhere he goes, even on short walks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carry - to hold and move something from one place to another
  • antonym: drop - to let something fall
  • synonym: haul - to pull or drag something with effort
  • synonym: lug - to drag or carry something with great effort
