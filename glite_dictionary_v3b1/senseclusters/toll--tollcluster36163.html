toll

verb
charge a fee for use (road, bridge, etc.)
5.0
Pronunciation: / toʊl / (AmE), / təʊl / (BrE)
to charge a fee for the use of a road, bridge, or similar infrastructure

Examples

To toll a bridge
If they toll the bridge, fewer people might use it during rush hour.
Several cities have started to toll busy roads to reduce traffic congestion.
The government plans to toll the new expressway to cover maintenance costs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charge - to ask for payment as a price; demand payment
  • antonym: waive - to refrain from claiming or enforcing a fee or charge
