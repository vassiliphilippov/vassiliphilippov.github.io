mid

preposition
in the middle of
Pronunciation: / mɪd / (AmE), / mɪd / (BrE)
used before a noun to indicate the middle part or period of something, especially time or position

Examples

Mid-July heat
The flowers bloom in mid-April when the weather starts to warm up.
He woke up in the mid-afternoon after staying up late the night before.
They moved to a new city in mid-2019 to start fresh.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beginning - the start of a period or thing
  • antonym: end - the final part of a period or thing
  • synonym: middle - the central part or point of something
