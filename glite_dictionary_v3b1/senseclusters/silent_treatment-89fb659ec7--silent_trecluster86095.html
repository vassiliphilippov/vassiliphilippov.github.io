silent treatment

noun
deliberate refusal to speak as a punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪlənt ˌtritmənt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt ˌtriːtmənt / (BrE)
the act of deliberately ignoring or refusing to speak to someone as a way of expressing anger or disapproval

Examples

Give silent treatment
After their argument, she gave him the silent treatment for days.
The children knew their mother was angry when she started the silent treatment.
Whenever he's upset, he resorts to the silent treatment instead of talking things through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cold shoulder - disregarding or ignoring someone as a social snub
