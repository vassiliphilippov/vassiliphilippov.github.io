foot

noun
movement by walking
5.2 20/m
Pronunciation: / fÊŠt / (AmE), / fÊŠt / (BrE)
the act of traveling by walking rather than using a vehicle; also, soldiers who fight on foot (infantry)

Examples

On foot journey
After the bus broke down, we continued our journey on foot.
Many commuters choose to travel on foot to avoid traffic jams.
She is swift of foot and often wins the race to school.
During the march, the foot suffered more than the horsemen due to rough terrain.
Historical novels often describe battles where the foot engaged first, followed by the cavalry.
The division included one regiment of cavalry and two of foot.

Synonyms

  • walking
    1.0 23/m
    walking is moving on your feet for travel or exercise, while foot means the act of traveling by walking, not by vehicle.
  • infantry
    5.0 8.85/m
    infantry are soldiers trained to fight on foot in war, while foot also refers to soldiers who fight on foot within an army.
  • foot soldier
    3.8 1.19/m
    foot soldier is a single soldier who fights on foot and can mean an ordinary member, while foot often means the act of walking.
