empiric

adjective
based on experiment and observation
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈpɪrɪk / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪrɪk / (BrE)
Based on experiment and observation rather than theory.

Examples

Empiric evidence
Medical knowledge in ancient times was primarily empiric, relying on observation and experience.
The scientist insisted on using an empiric approach to test the new hypothesis.
Unlike theoretical philosophers, she favored empiric methods in her research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empirical - based on experiment and observation rather than theory
