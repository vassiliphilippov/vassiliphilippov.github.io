antiphon

noun
responsive liturgical chant or verse
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.tɪˌfɑn / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪ.fɒn / (BrE)
A short chant, verse, or song that is sung or recited responsively, especially in Christian liturgical services.

Examples

A antiphon chant
A beautiful antiphon echoed through the cathedral as part of the Easter celebration.
During the evening service, the congregation recited the antiphon in answer to the priest.
The choir sang an antiphon before the start of the main hymn.
