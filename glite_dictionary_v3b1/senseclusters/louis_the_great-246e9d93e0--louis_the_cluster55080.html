Louis the Great

proper noun
King of France, Louis XIV (1638–1715), known for Versailles and influence
Pronunciation: / luːˈiː ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / luːˈiː ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
the epithet of Louis XIV (1638–1715), King of France whose long reign was marked by the expansion of French influence in Europe and the splendor of Versailles.

Examples

Louis the Great of France
French art and culture flourished during the reign of Louis the Great.
Many historians regard Louis the Great as the epitome of absolute monarchy in Europe.
The Palace of Versailles was expanded under Louis the Great to showcase French grandeur.
