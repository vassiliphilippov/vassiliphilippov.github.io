rodent

noun
small gnawing mammal (e.g., mouse, rat, squirrel)
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊdnt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊdnt / (BrE)
a small mammal of the order Rodentia, such as a mouse, rat, or squirrel, characterized by a single pair of continuously growing incisors in each jaw and adapted for gnawing

Examples

A rodent animal
A beaver is a type of rodent known for building dams and lodges.
Some people keep a rodent, such as a hamster or gerbil, as a pet.
The laboratory was infested with rodents that chewed through electrical wires.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mammal - a warm-blooded animal with hair or fur and, in females, milk-producing glands
