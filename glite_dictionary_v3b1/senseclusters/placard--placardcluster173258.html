placard

verb
to display a poster or notice publicly
7.3 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈplæk.ərd/ /; / /ˈplæk.ɑːrd/ (AmE), /ˈplæk.ɑːd/ (BrE)
to display or put up a sign, notice, or poster in a public place

Examples

To placard a wall
Protesters placarded the city hall with signs demanding change.
The shop owner placarded the window with a notice about the upcoming sale.
They placarded warnings about the water shortage throughout the neighborhood.
Activists placarded the city center to raise awareness about climate change.
During the protest, they placarded the walls with demands for reform.
The company placarded the conference hall with its new product advertisements.
