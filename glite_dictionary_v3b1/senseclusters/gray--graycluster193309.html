gray

noun
neutral color between white and black
1.6 1.0
Pronunciation: /greɪ/ (AmE), /greɪ/ (BrE)
A neutral color between white and black (having no hue; often dull or intermediate in shade), including references to clothing or uniforms made from fabric of that color.

Examples

A dull gray
Clouds turned the sky a dull gray before the rain started.
She chose a subtle shade of gray for the living room walls.
The distant mountains appeared as a soft gray on the early morning horizon.
He wore a grey suit to the interview.
Mist hung over the fields, turning everything a pale grey.
The walls were painted a soft grey to create a calming atmosphere.
She prefers to wear grey for formal occasions.
The soldiers marched past, all dressed in grey.
Their school uniform is a simple grey with a white shirt.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: black - the darkest color; the color of coal
  • synonym: gray - a neutral achromatic color midway between white and black (American spelling)
  • synonym: grey - alternative British English spelling for 'gray'
  • degree higher: white - the lightest color; the color of snow or milk
