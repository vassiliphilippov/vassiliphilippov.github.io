undead

noun
reanimated corpse
7.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈdɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈdɛd / (BrE)
a creature that has died but is supernaturally reanimated, such as a zombie or vampire

Examples

A undead zombie
The hero entered the crypt, wary of the undead lurking in the shadows.
Legends say the castle is haunted by an ancient undead king.
In many games, players must defeat powerful undead to complete their quests.
The villagers locked their doors at night, fearing the approach of the undead.
In many horror movies, the undead rise from their graves to haunt the living.
Legends of the undead have inspired countless books and films around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ghost - the spirit of a dead person, often believed to appear to the living
  • antonym: living - being alive; not dead or reanimated
  • synonym: vampire - a mythical being that drinks blood and is undead
  • synonym: zombie - a reanimated corpse, especially in horror fiction
