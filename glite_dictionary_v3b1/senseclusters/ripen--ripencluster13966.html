ripen

verb
become or make ripe
4.1 2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪpən /; / ˈraɪ.pən / (AmE), / ˈraɪpən /; / ˈraɪ.pən / (BrE)
to become or make something fully grown, developed, or ready to eat or use

Examples

To ripen fruit
Farmers wait for their wheat to ripen before harvesting.
It takes several weeks for cheese to ripen fully.
The tomatoes will ripen faster if you leave them in the sun.
Farmers sometimes use chemicals to ripen bananas before sending them to market.
The summer heat helps ripen the tomatoes much earlier than usual.
Time and proper storage conditions ripen cheese, improving its flavor and texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decay - to rot; to undergo decomposition, opposite of ripen
  • synonym: mature - to reach full development or maturity
