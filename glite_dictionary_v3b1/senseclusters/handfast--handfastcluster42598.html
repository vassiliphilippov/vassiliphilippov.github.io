handfast

adjective
steadfast or loyal
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈhændˌfɑːst / (BrE)
firmly fixed, securely fastened, or steadfast and loyal, especially in an archaic or poetic sense

Examples

Handfast loyalty
Her handfast loyalty to the family business kept her working through every crisis.
The knight remained handfast to his oath, never wavering in his duty.
In old poems, lovers are often described as handfast in their devotion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance
  • synonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in purpose or loyalty
