constructional

adjective
about language patterns
7.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌkʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to linguistic constructions or the patterns and structures found in language

Examples

Constructional grammar theory
The linguist developed a constructional analysis to explain how idioms are formed.
Her research focuses on the constructional properties of phrasal verbs in English.
A constructional approach can reveal how new sentence patterns emerge in a language.

Related Terms

  • antonym: semantic - relating to meaning in language
  • synonym: syntactic - relating to the arrangement of words and phrases in sentences
