storyboard

noun
visual layout plan
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstɔːriˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈstɔːriˌbɔːd /; / ˈstɔːribɔːd / (BrE)
a series of drawings or images (on paper or digital) arranged in sequence to plan the scenes or layout of a film, animation, story, or visual project

Examples

A storyboard sequence
The designers created a storyboard to illustrate the user journey through the new app.
Before filming, the director reviewed the storyboard to plan each camera shot.
A digital storyboard helped the team visualize the sequence of screens for the website redesign.
The director used a storyboard to visualize each scene before filming began.
Artists spent several days creating the storyboard for the animated commercial.
Each storyboard panel showed a different camera angle for the action sequence.
Before filming, the team created a detailed storyboard to plan each camera shot.
The animator used the storyboard to visualize how the characters would move in each scene.
We presented the storyboard to the client to get feedback on the commercial's flow.
