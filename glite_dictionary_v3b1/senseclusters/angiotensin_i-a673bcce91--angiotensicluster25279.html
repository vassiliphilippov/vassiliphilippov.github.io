angiotensin I

noun
inactive precursor of angiotensin II peptide
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒi.oʊˈtɛn.sɪn wʌn / (AmE), / ˌandʒi.əʊˈtɛn.sɪn wʌn / (BrE)
a physiologically inactive peptide that is the precursor to angiotensin II, playing a role in the regulation of blood pressure.

Examples

Angiotensin I peptide
Angiotensin I is converted to angiotensin II by the action of angiotensin-converting enzyme in the lungs.
Doctors measured levels of angiotensin I to assess the effectiveness of antihypertensive medication.
The discovery of angiotensin I was a major milestone in understanding blood pressure regulation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: angiotensin II - an active peptide hormone that causes blood vessels to constrict, increasing blood pressure
