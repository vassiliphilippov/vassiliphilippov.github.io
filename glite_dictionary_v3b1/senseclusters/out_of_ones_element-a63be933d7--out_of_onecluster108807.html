out of one's element

adjective
feeling uncomfortable or unfamiliar in a situation
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv wʌnz ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / aʊt ɒv wʌnz ˈɛlɪmənt / (BrE)
in a situation where one feels awkward, uncomfortable, or not suited because it is unfamiliar or outside one's usual experience or expertise

Examples

Out of his element feeling
After moving to a small rural town, Jake felt completely out of his element for several weeks.
He tried to contribute to the cooking class, but it was clear he was out of his element.
Sophie realized she was out of her element during the advanced calculus lecture.
