drip feed

noun
method of slow intravenous fluid administration
Pronunciation: / drɪp fid / (AmE), / drɪp fiːd / (BrE)
a method of administering a liquid, such as blood, saline, or plasma, to a patient intravenously, one drop at a time

Examples

A hospital drip feed
She received nutrients via drip feed when she was unable to eat solid food.
The nurse prepared the drip feed for the child in the intensive care unit.
The patient was kept on a drip feed after surgery to ensure proper hydration.
