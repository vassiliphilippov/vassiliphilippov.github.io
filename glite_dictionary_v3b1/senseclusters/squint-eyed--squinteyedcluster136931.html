squint-eyed

adjective
having eyes or a glance turned to the side
5.4 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈskwɪn.taɪd/ (AmE), /ˈskwɪn.taɪd/ (BrE)
(of a person or look) having the eyes turned partly to one side, often suggesting suspicion, doubt, or envy.

Examples

Squint-eyed glance
He shot a squint-eyed look at his rival, not hiding his envy.
She gave him a squint-eyed glance, clearly doubting his explanation.
The detective watched the suspect with a squint-eyed suspicion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sidelong - directed to one side, often with suspicion or interest
