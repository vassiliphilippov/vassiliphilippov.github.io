aerial torpedo

noun
torpedo launched from an aircraft
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛriəl tɔrˈpidoʊ / (AmE), / ˈeəriəl tɔːˈpiːdəʊ / (BrE)
A torpedo designed to be launched from an aircraft against targets on or under the water.

Examples

An aerial torpedo attack
During World War II, the Allies made extensive use of aerial torpedoes to damage enemy ships.
Technological advances have made the traditional aerial torpedo less common in modern warfare.
The new bomber was specifically designed to carry and deploy aerial torpedoes.
