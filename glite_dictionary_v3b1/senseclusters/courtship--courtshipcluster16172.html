courtship

noun
process of attracting a mate
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtʃɪp / (BrE)
the period or process in which people or animals try to attract a mate, often through specific behaviors or rituals

Examples

Animal courtship display
The peacock's colorful feathers play a key role in its courtship displays.
During their courtship, the couple spent many evenings talking and getting to know each other.
Scientists observed the courtship rituals of the rare bird species in the wild.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flirtation - behavior that suggests a romantic or sexual interest in someone
  • antonym: separation - the state of being apart or not together, especially after a relationship
  • synonym: wooing - the act of seeking someone's affection with the aim of marriage or partnership
