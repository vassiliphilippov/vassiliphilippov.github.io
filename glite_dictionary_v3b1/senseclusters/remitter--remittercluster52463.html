remitter

noun
one who refers a case
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪtə / (BrE)
a person or authority who refers or sends a case or matter to another court, tribunal, or official for action

Examples

Remitter of a case
The judge acted as the remitter and transferred the case to a higher court for review.
As remitter, the committee forwarded the complaint to the appropriate regulatory body.
The remitter must ensure all documents are sent to the correct authority for further action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recipient - a person who receives something
  • synonym: referrer - a person who directs something or someone to another place or authority
