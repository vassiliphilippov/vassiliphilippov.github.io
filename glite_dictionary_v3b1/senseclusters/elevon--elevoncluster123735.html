elevon

noun
aircraft control surface
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛləˌvɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪˌvɒn /; / ˈɛlɪvɒn / (BrE)
a movable control surface on an aircraft that combines the functions of an elevator and an aileron, used to control pitch and roll

Examples

Aircraft elevon control
The pilot moved the elevon to help the aircraft turn smoothly during the maneuver.
Modern fighter jets often use elevons instead of separate elevators and ailerons.

The pilot adjusted the elevon to help the aircraft turn smoothly during the test flight.
Modern flying wing designs often rely on a single elevon for both pitch and roll control.
Engineers inspected the damaged elevon before clearing the plane for takeoff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aileron - a hinged flight control surface on the wing of an aircraft for controlling roll
  • synonym: elevator - a movable control surface on the tail of an aircraft for controlling pitch
