glomerular capsule

noun
membrane surrounding glomerulus in kidney
8.8 6.4
Pronunciation: /glə.ˈme.r.jə.lər ˈkæp.səl/ (AmE), /glə.ˈme.jə.lə ˈkæp.sjuːl/ (BrE)
A thin double-layered membrane that surrounds the glomerulus in the nephron of the kidney, involved in the filtration of blood to form urine.

Examples

The glomerular capsule membrane
Inflammation of the glomerular capsule can impair kidney function.
Renal pathologists examined tissue samples of the glomerular capsule to diagnose the disease.
The glomerular capsule encases the glomerulus and initiates the filtration process in the kidney.
