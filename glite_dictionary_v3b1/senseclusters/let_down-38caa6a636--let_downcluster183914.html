let down

verb
to disappoint; fail to meet expectations
Pronunciation: / lɛt daʊn / (AmE), / lɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to disappoint someone by not doing what is expected or promised

Examples

To let down a friend
He tried hard, but ultimately he let down everyone who was counting on him.
I promised not to let down my team during the big match.
She felt hurt when her friends let down her by not showing up to her birthday.
He promised to help, but in the end he let down everyone.
I was really counting on you—please don't let down the whole team.
She felt let down when her friends forgot her birthday.
I trusted you to help me, but you really let us down this time.
She was afraid her performance would let us down after all the hard work.
The team promised they would not let us down during the final match.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence; the opposite of letting someone down
  • antonym: support - to give assistance or help to
