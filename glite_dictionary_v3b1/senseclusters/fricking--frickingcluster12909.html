fricking

adjective
mild expletive
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
used before a noun as a mild expletive to express anger, annoyance, or emphasis without being strongly offensive

Examples

A fricking mess
I can't believe you lost the fricking keys again.
That fricking alarm clock woke me up at 5 a.m. on a Saturday.
She was so tired of the fricking rain ruining her plans.
