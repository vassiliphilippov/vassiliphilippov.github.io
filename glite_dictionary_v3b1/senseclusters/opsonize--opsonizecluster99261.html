opsonize

verb
make cells more susceptible to phagocytosis
8.3 9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːp.sə.naɪz/ (AmE), /ˈɒp.sə.naɪz/ (BrE)
(of a substance) to make (cells or particles) more susceptible to ingestion and destruction by phagocytes, especially by marking them for an immune response.

Examples

To opsonize bacteria
Antibodies opsonize bacteria to make them easier targets for phagocytes.
Certain proteins in the immune system can opsonize pathogens, aiding in their clearance.
If a cell is opsonized, it will be more rapidly engulfed by macrophages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phagocytize - to ingest or engulf (a particle or cell) by phagocytosis
