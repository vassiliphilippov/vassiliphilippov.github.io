cinnabar

noun
a red-and-black European moth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪn.əˌbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.əˌbɑː / (BrE)
a large red-and-black European moth (Tyria jacobaeae), whose larvae feed on ragwort leaves, and which has been introduced to control ragwort.

Examples

Cinnabar moth
A cinnabar moth larva can be seen feeding on the leaves of a ragwort plant.
Farmers released thousands of cinnabar moths to help control ragwort in the fields.
The cinnabar is easily recognized by its striking red and black wings.
