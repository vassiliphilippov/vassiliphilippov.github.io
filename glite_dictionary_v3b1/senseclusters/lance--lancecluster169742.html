lance

verb
cut open with a sharp instrument
Pronunciation: / læns / (AmE), / lɑːns / (BrE)
to cut or pierce something with a sharp instrument, especially to release fluid or as an attack

Examples

To lance a blister
A specialist should lance the blister instead of draining it at home.
It is important to lance a boil properly to prevent infection.
The doctor decided to lance the abscess to relieve the pain.
In the medieval tournament, they would lance at each other with great force.
She watched as the knight lanced through his rival's shield in the heat of battle.
The knight skillfully lanced his opponent during the joust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incise - to cut into (especially surgically)
  • synonym: pierce - to force a way into or through; penetrate
