left gastric vein

noun
vein draining the upper stomach
6.5
Pronunciation: / lɛft ˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (AmE), / lɛft ˈɡæstrɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the upper part of the stomach and the lower esophagus, running through the lesser omentum to empty into the portal vein

Examples

The left gastric vein
During surgery, the left gastric vein was carefully identified and preserved.
Enlargement of the left gastric vein can indicate portal hypertension in patients with liver disease.
The left gastric vein collects blood from the stomach and transports it to the portal vein.
