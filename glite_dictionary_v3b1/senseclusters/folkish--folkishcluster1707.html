folkish

adjective
ethnic nationalist ideology
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.kɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.kɪʃ/ (BrE)
relating to an ideology that emphasizes the importance or purity of a particular ethnic or national group, often with exclusionary or nationalist connotations

Examples

Folkish ideology
The party's folkish platform focused on preserving traditional customs and ethnic identity.
Critics argued that the group's folkish views encouraged exclusion rather than unity.
Some historians study how folkish ideologies influenced early twentieth-century politics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitan - embracing people from many different countries and cultures
  • synonym: nationalist - supporting the interests of a particular nation, often to the exclusion of others
