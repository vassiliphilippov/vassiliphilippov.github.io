condemner

noun
one who pronounces guilt
7.5
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdem.ər/ /; / /kən.ˈdem.nər/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdem.ə/ /; / /kən.ˈdem.nə/ (BrE)
a person or authority who formally declares someone guilty or deserving of punishment, especially in legal or religious contexts

Examples

Courtroom condemner
The condemner announced the verdict with a stern expression in the crowded courtroom.
In some stories, the priest acts as both the condemner and the guide to redemption.
The role of condemner is often separated from that of the jury in modern legal systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuser - a person who claims someone has done something wrong
  • antonym: defender - a person who supports or protects someone accused
  • synonym: judge - a person who decides cases in a court of law
