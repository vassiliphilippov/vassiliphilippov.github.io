placental

noun
a mammal with a placenta
5.8 6.9
Pronunciation: /plə.ˈsen.təl/ (AmE), /plə.ˈsen.təl/ (BrE)
A mammal of the group Placentalia, characterized by the presence of a placenta, excluding monotremes and marsupials.

Examples

A placental mammal
Most mammals, such as dogs and whales, are classified as placentals.
The discovery of placental fossils helped scientists understand mammalian evolution.
Unlike marsupials, placentals nourish their young through a placenta before birth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: marsupial - a mammal whose young are born incompletely developed and are carried in a pouch
  • antonym: monotreme - egg-laying mammal like the platypus or echidna
