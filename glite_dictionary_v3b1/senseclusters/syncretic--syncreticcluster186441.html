syncretic

adjective
characterized by reduction of inflections
4.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /sɪŋ.ˈkret.ɪk/ /; / /sɪn.ˈkret.ɪk/ (AmE), /sɪŋ.ˈkret.ɪk/ /; / /sɪn.ˈkret.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a language) characterized by the reduction or merging of inflections, often resulting in a simpler grammatical structure.

Examples

Syncretic grammar
A syncretic approach to grammar can be observed in some creole languages.
Latin developed into several syncretic Romance languages over the centuries.
The evolution of English from Old to Modern forms is often described as syncretic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analytic - (of language) characterized by little or no use of inflections; opposite of syncretic
