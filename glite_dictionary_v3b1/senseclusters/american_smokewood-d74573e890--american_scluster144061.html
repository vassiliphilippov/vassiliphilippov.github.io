American smokewood

noun
North American shrub with smoky plumes of flowers
9.0
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈsmoʊkwʊd / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən ˈsməʊkwʊd / (BrE)
A shrubby tree of the southern United States with large plumes of feathery flowers that resemble puffs of smoke.

Examples

The American smokewood tree
Many landscapers in the South recommend planting American smokewood for its attractive appearance and low maintenance.
The American smokewood adds a dramatic effect to gardens with its unique, smoky flower plumes.
The feathery blooms of the American smokewood make it easily distinguishable from other native shrubs.
