backchannel

noun
listener response signal
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌtʃænəl / (BrE)
a short verbal or nonverbal response by a listener, such as 'uh-huh' or a nod, that shows attention or understanding during someone else's speech

Examples

Backchannel feedback signals
During the meeting, she gave frequent backchannel like nodding and saying 'mm-hmm' to show she was listening.
Good listeners often use backchannel to encourage the speaker to continue.
The teacher noticed the students' backchannel and felt reassured that they understood the lesson.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feedback - a response or reaction to what someone says or does
  • antonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking by saying or doing something
