vacuum tube

noun
electronic device with electrodes in a vacuum
5.3 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv√¶k.juÀêm tuÀêb / (AmE), / Ààv√¶k.juÀêm tjuÀêb / (BrE)
An electronic device with electrodes enclosed in a vacuum within glass or metal, used to control electric current.

Examples

Old vacuum tubes
Early radios often used vacuum tubes to amplify audio signals.
The invention of the vacuum tube marked a significant advance in electronics.
With the rise of transistors, vacuum tubes became largely obsolete in modern devices.

Synonyms

  • electron tube
    6.9 0.01/m
    an electron tube is the technical name used in engineering and textbooks, while vacuum tube is the more common everyday term.
  • thermionic vacuum tube
    8.3 0.00/m
    thermionic vacuum tube is the technical term that highlights electron emission by heat, while vacuum tube names the device more generally.
  • thermionic tube
    9.8 0.00/m
    thermionic tube is a shorter technical name stressing hot-electron flow, while vacuum tube is the broader, standard name for the device.
  • thermionic valve
    9.9 0.00/m
    thermionic valve is the older British name often heard in vintage radios and amps, while vacuum tube is the neutral modern term.
  • tube
    8.8 4.65/m
    tube is a short informal name often used by audio people, while vacuum tube is the precise term for the sealed electrode device.
