vacuum tube

noun
electronic device with electrodes in a vacuum
Pronunciation: / ˈvæk.juːm tuːb / (AmE), / ˈvæk.juːm tjuːb / (BrE)
An electronic device with electrodes enclosed in a vacuum within glass or metal, used to control electric current.

Examples

Old vacuum tubes
Early radios often used vacuum tubes to amplify audio signals.
The invention of the vacuum tube marked a significant advance in electronics.
With the rise of transistors, vacuum tubes became largely obsolete in modern devices.
