pterygoid muscle

noun
muscle involved in jaw movement
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛr.ɪˌɡɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ɪˌɡɔɪd ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
Either of two muscles extending from the sphenoid bone to the lower jaw, involved in jaw movement.

Examples

The pterygoid muscle moves jaw
An injury to the pterygoid muscle may affect your ability to open your mouth widely.
Tension in the pterygoid muscle can sometimes lead to jaw pain and headaches.
The dentist explained that the pterygoid muscle helps move the lower jaw during chewing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: masseter - a major muscle of the jaw used for chewing
  • synonym: temporalis - muscle on the side of the head that helps move the jaw
