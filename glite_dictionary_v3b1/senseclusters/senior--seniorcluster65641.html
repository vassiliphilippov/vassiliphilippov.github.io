senior

adjective
older; advanced in years
2.5 8.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêni…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêni…ô / (BrE)
(of a person) advanced in years; older, especially in comparison to others or as designated by an official status.

Examples

Senior citizen
Discounts are available for senior citizens over the age of sixty-five.
My grandmother is a senior member of the local book club.
The hospital provides special care for its senior patients.

Synonyms

  • elderly
    1.0 27/m
    elderly often suggests frailty or health decline in older people, while senior can simply mean older or higher in rank.
  • aged
    1.3 9.76/m
    aged is a more formal, often attributive word for very old people, while senior can mean older or indicate higher status.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aged - having lived to an advanced age
  • synonym: elderly - (of a person) old or aging
  • antonym: junior - younger, especially in comparison to another person
