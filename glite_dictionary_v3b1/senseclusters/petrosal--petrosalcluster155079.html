petrosal

noun
part of temporal bone
9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈtroʊ.səl/ /; / /pɪ.ˈtroʊ.səl/ (AmE), /pɪ.ˈtrəʊ.səl/ /; / /pə.ˈtrəʊ.səl/ (BrE)
a bone or bony part forming the petrous portion of the temporal bone, especially as a distinct bone in some mammals

Examples

A petrosal bone
The paleontologist examined the petrosal to learn more about the animal's hearing abilities.
Damage to the petrosal can affect the inner ear and balance.
In some mammals, the petrosal is a separate bone rather than fused with the temporal bone.
