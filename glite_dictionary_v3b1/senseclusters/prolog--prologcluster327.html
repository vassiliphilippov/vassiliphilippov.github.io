prolog

noun
introduction to a work
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊlɔg / (AmE), / ˈprəʊlɒg / (BrE)
an introduction or preface, especially to a literary or dramatic work

Examples

The prolog introduction
The prolog gave important background information before the main story began.
In some editions, the prolog is written by a different author than the main text.
The audience listened carefully as the actor delivered the prolog on stage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epilogue - a section at the end of a book or play that comments on or concludes the story
  • synonym: preface - an introductory section of a book, typically by the author
  • synonym: prologue - an introduction to a play, book, or other work
