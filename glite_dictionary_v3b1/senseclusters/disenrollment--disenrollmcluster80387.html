disenrollment

noun
removal from membership
7.2
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ən.ˈroʊl.mənt/ /; / /dɪs.ɪn.ˈroʊl.mənt/ (AmE), /dɪs.ən.ˈrəʊl.mənt/ /; / /dɪs.ɪn.ˈrəʊl.mənt/ (BrE)
the act or process of leaving or being removed from membership or enrollment in a group, program, or institution

Examples

University disenrollment
After repeated absences, the university initiated his disenrollment from the program.
Parents were notified about the disenrollment process if their children moved to another school district.
The club's rules allow for disenrollment in cases of serious misconduct.
The new policy led to a significant disenrollment of students from the after-school program.
Healthcare providers are concerned about the impact of disenrollment on patient access to services.
Parents received letters notifying them of their child's disenrollment from the summer camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave an organization or institution
  • synonym: withdrawal - the act of leaving or removing oneself from participation
