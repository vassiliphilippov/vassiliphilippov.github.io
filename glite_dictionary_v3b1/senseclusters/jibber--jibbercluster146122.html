jibber

noun
one who hesitates
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪbər / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪbə / (BrE)
a person or animal, especially a horse, that hesitates or refuses to move forward or proceed

Examples

A stubborn jibber
The old horse was a notorious jibber and often refused to cross streams.
As a jibber, he always hesitated before making important decisions.
Trainers found it difficult to work with a jibber in the racing stables.

Related Terms

  • antonym: go-getter - a person who is energetic and eager to move forward
  • synonym: hesitator - a person who pauses or is reluctant to act
  • synonym: refuser - a person who declines or is unwilling to do something
