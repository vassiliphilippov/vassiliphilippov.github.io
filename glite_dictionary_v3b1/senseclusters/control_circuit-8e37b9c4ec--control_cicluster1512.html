control circuit

noun
electrical circuit that regulates a system
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
An electrical circuit that manages or regulates the operation of a device or system, often using feedback to adjust its input.

Examples

A control circuit board
A faulty control circuit can cause the entire system to malfunction.
Modern appliances rely on a control circuit to ensure safe and efficient operation.
The engineer designed a control circuit to regulate the speed of the motor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feedback circuit - a circuit using feedback to control a system
