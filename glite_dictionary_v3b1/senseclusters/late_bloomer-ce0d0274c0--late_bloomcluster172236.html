late bloomer

noun
person who matures or succeeds later than usual
4.6 3.8
Pronunciation: /leɪt ˈbluː.mər/ (AmE), /leɪt ˈbluː.mə/ (BrE)
a person who develops skills or shows their talents or maturity later than is typical or expected

Examples

A late bloomer in life
Despite being a late bloomer, she eventually became a respected leader in her field.
He was always considered a late bloomer, only finding his passion for music in his thirties.
Many athletes are late bloomers, reaching their peak performance after most have retired.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prodigy - a young person with exceptional abilities or talents
