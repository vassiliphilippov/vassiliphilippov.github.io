Arceuthobium pusillum

noun
parasitic plant of spruce and larch
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌsuːθoʊˈbiːəm pjuːˈsɪləm / (AmE), / ɑːˌsjuːθəˈbiːəm pjuːˈsɪləm / (BrE)
A small parasitic herb with scalelike leaves and berrylike fruits, found on spruce and larch trees.

Examples

Arceuthobium pusillum plant
Arceuthobium pusillum can cause significant damage to spruce forests in North America.
Researchers are studying methods to control the spread of Arceuthobium pusillum on larch trees.
The tiny berries of Arceuthobium pusillum are a key feature used for its identification.
