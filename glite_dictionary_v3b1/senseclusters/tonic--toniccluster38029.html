tonic

adjective
bearing main stress (phonetics)
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of a syllable) bearing the main stress in a word, often accompanied by a pitch change.

Examples

Tonic syllable
In English pronunciation, the tonic syllable often receives a noticeable pitch change.
The tonic syllable in 'about' is the second syllable, not the first.
When teaching intonation, it's important to identify the tonic syllable in each phrase.
