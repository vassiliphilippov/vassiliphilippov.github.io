make sense

verb
to be reasonable or understandable
1.0
Pronunciation: / meɪk sɛns / (AmE), / meɪk sɛns / (BrE)
to be reasonable, logical, or easy to understand

Examples

To make sense to people

After reading the instructions again, everything finally made sense to John.
Can you explain it differently? It doesn’t make sense to me this way.
Her theory doesn’t make sense when you consider the latest evidence.
