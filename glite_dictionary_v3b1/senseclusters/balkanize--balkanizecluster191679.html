balkanize

verb
divide into hostile small units
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːlkəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɔːlkənaɪz / (BrE)
to divide a region, country, or group into smaller, often hostile units or states, especially along ethnic or territorial lines or in a way that leads to conflict or lack of cooperation

Examples

To balkanize a country
Historians warn that continued unrest could balkanize the country, making peaceful resolution difficult.
Some critics argue that social media tends to balkanize public discourse into echo chambers.
The new leadership feared that ethnic disputes might balkanize the entire region.
Politicians warned that sudden autonomy could balkanise the region and lead to conflict.
Some observers fear that the country could balkanise if tensions continue to rise.
The civil war threatened to balkanise the nation into several unstable territories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragment - to break or cause to break into pieces
  • antonym: unify - to make or become united, uniform, or whole
