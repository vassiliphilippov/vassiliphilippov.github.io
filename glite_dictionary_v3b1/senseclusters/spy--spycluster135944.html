spy

verb
catch sight of; see suddenly
Pronunciation: / spaɪ / (AmE), / spaɪ / (BrE)
to see or notice something, especially unexpectedly or after searching

Examples

To spy a ship
From the lookout, she spied a ship on the distant horizon.
He quickly spied his friend in the crowded marketplace.
I spied a rare bird in the trees during my walk yesterday.
As I climbed the hill, I spied a small cottage nestled among the trees.
She quickly spied her friend in the crowded room and waved enthusiastically.
We spied a rare bird perched on the branch near the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: notice - to become aware of something seen or heard
  • antonym: overlook - to fail to notice something
  • synonym: see - to perceive with the eyes
  • synonym: spot - to see or notice someone or something, often suddenly or when looking for it
