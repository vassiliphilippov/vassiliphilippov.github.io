gurgle

verb
drink with a gurgling sound
3.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈgɜːr.ɡəl/ (AmE), /ˈgɜː.ɡəl/ (BrE)
to make a bubbling or irregular liquid sound, like water flowing or someone drinking

Examples

To gurgle water
He gurgled the cold water straight from his flask after the long hike.
She picked up the canteen and gurgled it noisily as her friends laughed.
The traveler gurgled from his bottle, the sound echoing through the quiet camp.
He gurgled a laugh despite the pain, surprising everyone nearby.
The injured man tried to gurgle a plea for help before falling silent.
With water in her throat, she could only gurgle his name.
She could hear a kettle gurgling softly on the stove.
The water in the stream began to gurgle as it flowed over the rocks.
Wine gurgled into the glass with a pleasant, bubbling sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to utter sounds or words incoherently, often as a stream of noise
  • synonym: murmur - to speak in a soft, low, and indistinct voice
  • degree higher: roar - to make a loud deep sound, as of water
  • degree lower: trickle - to flow in a thin stream or drops
