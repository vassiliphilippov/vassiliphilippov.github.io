fundamental analysis

noun
financial evaluation of securities
Pronunciation: / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl əˈnæləsɪs / (BrE)
A method of evaluating securities by examining financial and economic factors such as company earnings, management, and industry conditions.

Examples

Perform fundamental analysis
He performed a thorough fundamental analysis before buying shares of the tech firm.
Investors rely on fundamental analysis to determine the intrinsic value of a company.
Many financial professionals use fundamental analysis to inform their long-term investment decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: technical analysis - security valuation using charts and statistical trends
