A tax calculated as a percentage of the value added to goods or services, typically levied on the difference between the sale price and the cost of production.

Examples

An ad valorem tax rate
Importers are required to pay an ad valorem tax based on the declared value of the goods.
The government imposed an ad valorem tax on luxury vehicles to generate more revenue.
Unlike specific taxes, an ad valorem tax varies with the price of the commodity.
