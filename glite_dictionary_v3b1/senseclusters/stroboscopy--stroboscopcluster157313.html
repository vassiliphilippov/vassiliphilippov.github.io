stroboscopy

noun
motion analysis technique
9.9
Pronunciation: / stroʊˈbɑːskəpi / (AmE), / strəʊˈbɒskəpi / (BrE)
a technique that uses flashing light to observe or analyze rapid motion or vibration by making it appear slower or stationary

Examples

Stroboscopy of vocal cords
Doctors often use stroboscopy to examine the movement of a patient's vocal cords.
The engineer applied stroboscopy to study the rapid vibration of the machine parts.
With stroboscopy, scientists can observe fast-moving objects as if they are moving slowly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endoscopy - a medical procedure using a scope to look inside the body
  • synonym: stroboscope - an instrument that produces regular flashes of light for observing motion
