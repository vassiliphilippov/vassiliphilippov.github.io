purgative

noun
medicine that stimulates bowel evacuation
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrɡətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɜːɡətɪv / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that causes evacuation of the bowels; a laxative.

Examples

A purgative medicine
After taking the purgative, she felt much better.
Some traditional remedies used herbs as natural purgatives.
The doctor prescribed a purgative to relieve his constipation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constipating - causing constipation or inhibiting bowel movements
  • synonym: laxative - a medicine that induces the evacuation of the bowels
