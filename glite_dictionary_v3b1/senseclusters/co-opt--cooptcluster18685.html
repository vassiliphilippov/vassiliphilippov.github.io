co-opt

verb
neutralize by assimilation into a group
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɑpt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpt / (BrE)
to neutralize, win over, or assimilate individuals or groups by making them part of an established group or system, especially to eliminate opposition or dissent

Examples

To co-opt rivals
Our company often co-opts innovative startups rather than competing with them directly.
Several youth activists were co-opted onto the board to help guide the agency’s programs.
The regime tried to co-opt its critics by offering them government posts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take in or assimilate, especially into a group or system
  • antonym: marginalize - to treat a person or group as insignificant or peripheral
