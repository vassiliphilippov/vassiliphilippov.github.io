Calixtus II

proper noun
Pope (1119–1124) notable for church reforms
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈlɪk.stəs ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ˈkæl.ɪk.stəs ðə ˈsek.ənd/ (AmE), /kə.ˈlɪk.stəs ðə ˈsek.ənd/ /; / /ˈkæl.ɪk.stəs ðə ˈsek.ənd/ (BrE)
(died 1124) Pope from 1119 to 1124, notable for compelling Emperor Henry V to sign the Concordat of Worms, affirming the church's right to select its own leaders.

Examples

Calixtus II pope
Calixtus II played a crucial role in resolving the Investiture Controversy between the papacy and the Holy Roman Empire.
As pope, Calixtus II strengthened the church's independence from secular rulers.
The Concordat of Worms was signed under the papacy of Calixtus II in 1122.
