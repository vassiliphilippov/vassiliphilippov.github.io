interior angle

noun
angle inside a polygon
5.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɪriər ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɪəriə ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
An angle formed inside a polygon by two adjacent sides.

Examples

The interior angle of a polygon
In a triangle, the sum of the three interior angles is always 180 degrees.
The students measured each interior angle of the regular hexagon during the geometry lesson.
To find an interior angle of a regular polygon, you can use a specific mathematical formula.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exterior angle - the angle formed outside a polygon by one side and the extension of an adjacent side
