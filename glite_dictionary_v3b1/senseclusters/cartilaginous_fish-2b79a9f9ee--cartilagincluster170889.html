cartilaginous fish

noun
fish with a cartilage-based skeleton
2.1 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːr.tl̩ˈæ.dʒɪ.nəs fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.tɪˈlæ.dʒɪ.nəs fɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish whose skeleton is made primarily of cartilage rather than bone, such as sharks, rays, and skates

Examples

Cartilaginous fish like sharks
Scientists study cartilaginous fish to learn about vertebrate evolution.
Sharks are a type of cartilaginous fish that have skeletons made of flexible cartilage.
Unlike most bony fish, cartilaginous fish such as rays lack true bones.
