endless

adjective
seeming to have no end; very long
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndləs / (AmE), / ˈɛndləs / (BrE)
seeming to have no end; continuing for a very long time or forever

Examples

Endless meetings
After hours of endless meetings, everyone was exhausted and frustrated.
The desert seemed to stretch in an endless expanse of sand and heat.
Their endless arguments about politics made dinner uncomfortable for everyone.
He spoke of an endless universe that has neither beginning nor end.
The ocean's tides move in an endless cycle day after day.
The possibilities of space exploration sometimes seem endless.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brief - of short duration; not long-lasting
  • synonym: eternal - lasting or seeming to last forever; endless in duration
  • antonym: finite - having limits or bounds
  • synonym: infinite - limitless or endless in extent or size
  • synonym: interminable - having or seeming to have no end; tiresomely long
