deconsecration

noun
removal of sacred status
9.4
Pronunciation: /diː.kɑːn.sɪ.ˈkreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.kɑːn.sɪ.ˈkreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.kɒn.sɪ.ˈkreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.kɒn.sɪ.ˈkreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the formal act of removing the sacred or religious status from a place or object

Examples

Church deconsecration ceremony
After the church was closed, a formal deconsecration ceremony was held to mark its new status.
The deconsecration of the old cemetery allowed the land to be used for public development.
Some people opposed the deconsecration of the historic building, believing it should remain a sacred site.
After its deconsecration, the old chapel was turned into a community center.
The deconsecration ceremony marked the end of the building's use as a place of worship.
Many artifacts were removed from the church before its official deconsecration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consecration - the act of making something sacred or holy
  • synonym: desanctification - the act of making something no longer holy
  • synonym: desecration - the act of treating something sacred with disrespect
