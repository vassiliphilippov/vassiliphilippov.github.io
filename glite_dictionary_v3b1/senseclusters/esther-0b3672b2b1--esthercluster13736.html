Esther

proper noun
Jewish queen of Persia in the Bible
Pronunciation: / ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈɛstə / (BrE)
(in the Bible) a Jewish woman who became the queen of Persia and saved her people from a massacre, commemorated by the Jewish festival of Purim.

Examples

Queen Esther
Jews celebrate the courage of Esther as a symbol of faith and bravery.
Many artists have depicted Esther approaching the king in their paintings.
The story of Esther is read aloud during the festival of Purim.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Purim - Jewish festival commemorating Esther's saving of the Jews
