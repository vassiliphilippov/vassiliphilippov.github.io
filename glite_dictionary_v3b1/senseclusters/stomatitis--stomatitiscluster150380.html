stomatitis

noun
inflammation of the mouth lining
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˌstoʊməˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌstəʊməˈtaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the mucous membrane of the mouth, often causing soreness or ulcers

Examples

Stomatitis in mouth
Children with viral infections sometimes develop stomatitis, making it hard for them to eat or drink.
Chronic stomatitis can result from poor dental hygiene or certain medications.
The patient was diagnosed with stomatitis after complaining of painful mouth ulcers.
