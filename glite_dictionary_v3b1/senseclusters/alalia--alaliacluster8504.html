alalia

noun
paralysis of vocal cords; inability to speak
Pronunciation: / əˈleɪliə / (AmE), / əˈleɪlɪə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by paralysis of the vocal cords, resulting in an inability to speak.

Examples

Alalia vocal paralysis
Children with alalia require specialized therapy to help them communicate.
Doctors ruled out other causes for his inability to speak before confirming alalia.
The patient was diagnosed with alalia following a stroke that affected her vocal cords.
