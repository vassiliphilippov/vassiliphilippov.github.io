tremolo

noun
musical trembling effect or rapid note repetition
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛməˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmələʊ / (BrE)
(music) a trembling effect produced by the rapid repetition of a single note or the rapid alternation between two notes.

Examples

Guitar tremolo effect
Guitar players often use a tremolo effect to create a shimmering sound.
The rapid tremolo of the piano gave the melody a sense of urgency.
The violinist used tremolo to add dramatic tension to the piece.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vibrato - (music) a slight regular fluctuation in pitch to enrich musical tone
