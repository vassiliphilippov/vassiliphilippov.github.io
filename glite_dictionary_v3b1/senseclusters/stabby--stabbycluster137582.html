stabby

adjective
aggressive or irritable
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstæbi / (AmE), / ˈstæbi / (BrE)
feeling or appearing aggressive, irritable, or likely to lash out, often in a sharp or dangerous way

Examples

Feeling stabby
After a long, stressful day, she felt a bit stabby and needed some quiet time.
His stabby mood made everyone in the office keep their distance.
The cat gave me a stabby glare when I tried to move her from the chair.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing anger or aggression
  • synonym: hostile - unfriendly or aggressive in attitude
