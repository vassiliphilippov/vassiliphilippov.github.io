flush

verb
glow with warm color or light
Pronunciation: / flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
to become red in the face or skin, or to cause someone to become red, especially from emotion or heat

Examples

To flush with emotion
As dawn broke, the mountaintops flushed with the first light of day.
Her cheeks would flush brightly whenever she spoke in public.
The sunset made the clouds flush with shades of pink and gold.
He always flushes bright red when speaking in public.
Her cheeks flushed after receiving the unexpected compliment.
She tried to hide her face when she began to flush with embarrassment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blush - to become red in the face, often from embarrassment or emotion
