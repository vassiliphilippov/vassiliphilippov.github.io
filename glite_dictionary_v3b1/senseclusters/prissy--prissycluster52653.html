prissy

adjective
excessively fastidious; easily shocked or disgusted
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪsi / (AmE), / ˈprɪsi / (BrE)
excessively fussy or concerned with being proper, neat, or easily shocked by things that are not clean or formal

Examples

A prissy person
He refused to walk in the mud, claiming it was far too dirty for his prissy tastes.
Her prissy attitude made her unpopular among the more laid-back campers.
My roommate can be quite prissy when it comes to sharing a kitchen.
He refused to attend the party because he thought it was too prissy for his taste.
Her prissy attitude made others feel uncomfortable at the informal picnic.
Some critics consider the school's dress code to be unnecessarily prissy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fastidious - very attentive to detail or cleanliness
  • synonym: prim - very formal and proper in behavior or appearance
  • antonym: relaxed - free from tension or strictness, opposite of excessively proper
  • synonym: squeamish - easily made to feel sick or disgusted
  • synonym: straitlaced - having or showing very strict moral attitudes
  • antonym: unfussy - not easily upset or particular
