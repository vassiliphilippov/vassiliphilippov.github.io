intuitionistic

adjective
based on intuition
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnˌtuːɪʃəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪntjuːɪʃəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to a theory or approach based on intuition, especially in mathematics or logic

Examples

Intuitionistic logic
The mathematician preferred intuitionistic logic over classical logic for certain proofs.
Some theories in computer science are developed using intuitionistic principles.
The seminar focused on the differences between intuitionistic and classical approaches to mathematics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: classical - relating to traditional or established theories, especially in logic or mathematics
  • synonym: intuitive - based on what feels to be true without reasoning
