Salian

noun
member of an ancient Frankish tribe
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪliən / (AmE), / ˈseɪliən / (BrE)
a member of the Salian Franks, a subgroup of the Franks who settled in the region now known as the Netherlands in the 4th century AD

Examples

A Salian Frank
Archaeologists discovered artifacts belonging to a Salian settlement near the Rhine River.
As a proud Salian, he traced his ancestry to the early Frankish tribes.
The Salian Franks played a significant role in the decline of Roman authority in Gaul.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Frank - a member of a Germanic people who conquered Gaul in the Roman era
