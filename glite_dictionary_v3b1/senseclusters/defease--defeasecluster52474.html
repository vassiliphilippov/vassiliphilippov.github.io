defease

verb
to frustrate plans
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfiːz / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːz / (BrE)
to defeat, frustrate, or deprive someone or something of power or success

Examples

To defease an enemy
The council hoped to defease the corrupt official's influence over the town.
Their careful planning managed to defease the enemy's strategy before it could succeed.
In ancient times, heroes would often defease the schemes of villains to restore peace.

  • antonym: enable - to make possible or give power to someone or something
