Christiaan Huygens

proper noun
Dutch physicist and astronomer (1629–1695)
9.7
Pronunciation: / krɪˈstiːən ˈhaɪgənz / (AmE), / krɪˈstiːən ˈhɔɪgəns / (BrE)
(1629–1695) A Dutch physicist, mathematician, and astronomer known for formulating the wave theory of light and making significant contributions to optics, mechanics, and astronomy.

Examples

Christiaan Huygens wave theory
Christiaan Huygens proposed the wave theory of light, challenging the particle theory supported by Newton.
Many modern optics principles have roots in the work of Christiaan Huygens.
The Saturnian moon Titan was discovered by Christiaan Huygens in 1655.
