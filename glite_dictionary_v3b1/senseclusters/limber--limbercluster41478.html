limber

adjective
readily adaptable; flexible (figurative)
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbə / (BrE)
(of a person or their mind) able to adapt easily; flexible and open to new ideas.

Examples

Limber mind
A limber imagination helps writers create vivid worlds in their stories.
His limber approach to management made the team more responsive to change.
She has a limber mind that quickly adapts to new challenges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rigid - not willing to change ideas or adapt; inflexible
  • synonym: supple - able to bend or change easily; flexible (both literal and figurative)
