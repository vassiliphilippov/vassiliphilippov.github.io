epimorphic

adjective
having incomplete metamorphosis (biology)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈmɔɹfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially an insect) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis, with the same number of body segments throughout successive developmental stages.

Examples

Epimorphic insects
Many wingless insects are described as epimorphic because their body segments do not change during development.
The life cycle of the cockroach is epimorphic, as it hatches with the full number of body segments.
Unlike holometabolous insects, epimorphic species do not add new segments as they mature.
