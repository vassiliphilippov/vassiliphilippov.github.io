semisentient

adjective
partly conscious
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈsɛnʃənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈsɛnʃənt / (BrE)
having some but not full awareness or consciousness

Examples

Semisentient artificial intelligence
The new artificial intelligence was described as semisentient because it could learn but not truly understand.
Some animals are considered semisentient due to their limited awareness of their environment.
The story featured a semisentient spaceship that could follow instructions but lacked emotions.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: sentient - able to perceive or feel things; fully conscious
  • degree lower: unconscious - not aware or not able to perceive or feel things
