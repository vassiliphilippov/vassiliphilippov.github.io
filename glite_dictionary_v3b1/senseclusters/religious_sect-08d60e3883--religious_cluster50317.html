religious sect

noun
a subgroup within a religion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪdʒəs sɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs sɛkt / (BrE)
a subgroup of a religion with distinct beliefs or practices differing from the main group

Examples

A religious sect community
Some members of the religious sect lived together in a secluded community.
The Puritans were a religious sect that sought to reform the Church of England.
The government closely monitored the activities of the controversial religious sect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sect - a group that has separated from an established religion
