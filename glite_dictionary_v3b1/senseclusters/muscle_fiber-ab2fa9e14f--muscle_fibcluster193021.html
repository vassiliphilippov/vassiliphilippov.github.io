muscle fiber

noun
a cell that forms muscle tissue
1.9 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌs.əl ˈfaɪ.bər/ (AmE), /ˈmʌs.əl ˈfaɪ.bə/ (BrE)
An elongated, contractile cell that makes up the muscles of the body and is responsible for movement.

Examples

Strong muscle fibers
Athletes often train to increase the strength and size of their muscle fibers.
Damage to the muscle fibers can result in weakness or pain.
Each muscle fiber contracts in response to signals from the nervous system.
An injury can cause damage to the muscle fibres in the affected area.
Athletes require a higher proportion of fast-twitch muscle fibres for explosive movements.
Each muscle fibre contracts in response to signals from the nervous system.
