Jakob-Creutzfeldt disease

noun
rare fatal brain disorder with rapid dementia
Pronunciation: / ˌjɑːkoʊb ˈkrɔɪtsfɛlt dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˌjɑːkɒb ˈkrɔɪtsfɛlt dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare, usually fatal brain disorder in middle age caused by a prion infection, characterized by rapid progressive dementia and loss of muscle control.

Examples

Jakob-Creutzfeldt disease symptoms
Jakob-Creutzfeldt disease is often fatal within a year of the onset of symptoms.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with Jakob-Creutzfeldt disease after a rapid decline in mental function.
Researchers believe that Jakob-Creutzfeldt disease is caused by abnormal prion proteins.
