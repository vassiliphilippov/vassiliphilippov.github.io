axonemal

adjective
of the axoneme
Pronunciation: / ˌæksəˈniːməl / (AmE), / ˌæksəˈniːməl / (BrE)
relating to the axoneme, the central structural core of cilia or flagella in cells

Examples

Axonemal structure
The axonemal structure is essential for the movement of cilia in many organisms.
Mutations in axonemal proteins can lead to disorders affecting cell motility.
Scientists observed changes in the axonemal arrangement under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cilia - tiny hair-like structures on cells that help with movement
  • synonym: flagella - long, whip-like structures that help cells move
