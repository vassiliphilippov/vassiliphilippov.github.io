precedented

adjective
having a precedent; supported by precedent
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsɪˌdɛntɪd / (AmE), / ˈpriːsɪˌdentɪd / (BrE)
Having or supported by a precedent; justified by an earlier example or rule.

Examples

A precedented case
It is easier to defend a precedented action than a novel one.
Such an arrangement is precedented in international law and practice.
The court's decision was precedented by similar cases in the past.

Related Terms

  • synonym: precedent - an earlier event or action that is regarded as an example or guide
  • antonym: unprecedented - having no precedent; never done or known before
