overcommit

verb
to allocate beyond limits
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to allocate more of a resource, such as memory or storage, than is actually available, expecting not all will be used at once

Examples

To overcommit memory
The server can overcommit memory to support more virtual machines than its physical RAM allows.
If you overcommit storage, you risk running out of space when all users need their files at once.
Cloud platforms often overcommit CPU resources to maximize efficiency, assuming not all clients are active simultaneously.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overallocate - to assign more resources than are available
  • antonym: undercommit - to allocate fewer resources than are available or needed
