maund

noun
unit of weight in South Asia
Pronunciation: / mɔnd / (AmE), / mɔːnd / (BrE)
a traditional unit of weight used in South Asia, varying by country (for example, about 82.6 pounds in India)

Examples

A maund of rice
During the colonial period, a merchant might sell rice by the maund rather than by kilogram.
Records from the 1800s often mention goods measured in maunds for trade purposes.
The price is ten rupees per maund of wheat at the local market.
