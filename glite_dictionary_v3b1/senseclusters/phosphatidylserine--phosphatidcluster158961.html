phosphatidylserine

noun
brain cell phospholipid
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑsfəˌtaɪdəlˈsɪriːn / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəˌtaɪdɪlˈsɪəriːn / (BrE)
a type of phospholipid found in cell membranes, important for brain function and memory

Examples

Phosphatidylserine brain supplement
Some supplements contain phosphatidylserine to help with memory.
Researchers are studying how phosphatidylserine affects cognitive decline in older adults.
The brain contains high levels of phosphatidylserine in its cell membranes.
