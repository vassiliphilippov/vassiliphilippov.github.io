recoat

verb
to apply another layer
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkoʊt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkəʊt / (BrE)
to apply a new layer of paint or covering to a surface that has already been coated

Examples

To recoat a wall
After the primer dries, you should recoat the surface for a smoother finish.
It is important to recoat the fence every few years to protect it from the weather.
The instructions say to wait two hours before you recoat the wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paint - to cover a surface with paint
  • antonym: strip - to remove a layer of paint or covering
