freeze

noun
temporary suspension or halt in activity
6.2 2.20/m
Pronunciation: / friÀêz / (AmE), / friÀêz / (BrE)
an official action to stop or suspend an activity, process, or the use of money/assets

Examples

A wage freeze
After months of inflation, workers were frustrated by the wage freeze imposed by management.
Negotiators called for a freeze in nuclear weapons development to ease international tensions.
The government announced a freeze on public sector hiring for the next fiscal year.
A freeze on rent increases was introduced to help struggling tenants.
Due to economic uncertainty, the company implemented a hiring freeze across all departments.
The government announced a freeze on public sector salaries to control spending.

Synonyms

  • moratorium
    8.5 2.96/m
    a moratorium is a formal legal pause on activities or payments, while a freeze is an official action that often blocks money or assets.
  • suspension
    4.3 4.20/m
    a suspension is a temporary pause of an activity, while a freeze is an official action that specifically blocks money or assets.
  • halt
    2.3 9.61/m
    a halt is a general stop or pause in movement or activity, while a freeze is an official action to suspend money or assets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • synonym: halt - a temporary stop or pause
  • synonym: moratorium - a temporary prohibition of an activity
  • synonym: suspension - the action of stopping something for a period of time
