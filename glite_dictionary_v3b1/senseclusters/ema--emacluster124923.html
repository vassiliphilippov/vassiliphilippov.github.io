ema

noun
Japanese prayer plaque
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ.mɑ / (AmE), / ˈeɪ.mɑ / (BrE)
a small wooden plaque used in Japan for writing wishes or prayers and hanging at shrines

Examples

Shinto ema plaque
At the Shinto shrine, visitors wrote their wishes on an ema and hung it with the others.
She bought an ema to write a prayer for her family's health during the New Year visit.
Rows of ema with colorful drawings and messages decorated the entrance to the temple.

Related Terms

  • synonym: votive offering - an object offered in fulfillment of a vow or as an act of devotion
