hyperacuity

noun
exceptional sensory precision
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːəti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːɪti / (BrE)
the ability to detect extremely small differences in sensory stimuli beyond the normal range of perception

Examples

Hyperacuity ability
Some birds possess hyperacuity that helps them spot tiny insects from great distances.
Researchers study hyperacuity to understand how the human eye can detect minute changes in patterns.
Her visual hyperacuity allowed her to notice details that others easily missed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acuity - sharpness or keenness of perception
  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of ability to notice or feel small differences
