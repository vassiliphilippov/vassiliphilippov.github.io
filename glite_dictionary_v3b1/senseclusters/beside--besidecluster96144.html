beside

preposition
in comparison with
Pronunciation: / bɪˈsaɪd / (AmE), / bɪˈsaɪd / (BrE)
used to show comparison, meaning that one thing seems less important or impressive when compared to another

Examples

Ordinary beside greatness
Her skills seem ordinary beside those of the world champion.
The small town looks quiet beside the bustling city nearby.
My problems feel minor beside what others are facing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compared to - used to show how two things are similar or different
  • antonym: next to - in a position close to something, but not for comparison
