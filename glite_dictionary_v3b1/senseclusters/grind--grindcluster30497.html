grind

verb
crush or press with a crushing noise
1.0 2.1
Pronunciation: /graɪnd/ /; / /grɪnd/ (AmE), /graɪnd/ /; / /grɪnd/ (BrE)
to crush, break down, or shape something by rubbing, pressing, or using friction, often to make it into powder or a particular shape

Examples

To grind coffee beans
He ground the coffee beans while enjoying the fresh aroma.
She could hear the gears grind as the old elevator moved slowly upwards.
The heavy machinery began to grind the rocks into a fine powder.
He used a special tool to grind the lens to the correct shape.
Skilled workers grind metal parts to precise specifications in the factory.
You must grind the edges carefully to avoid cracks in the glass.
She enjoys grinding floral designs onto small glass bowls.
The artist expertly grinds intricate patterns into crystal glasses.
They ground the family crest onto the silver trophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrade - to wear away by friction
  • synonym: crush - to press something so that it is damaged or broken into pieces
  • synonym: etch - to cut or carve a design into a surface, especially with acid or a sharp tool
  • synonym: mash - to reduce to a pulpy or crushed mass
  • degree higher: polish - to make smooth and shiny by rubbing
  • synonym: powder - to reduce to fine, dry particles
