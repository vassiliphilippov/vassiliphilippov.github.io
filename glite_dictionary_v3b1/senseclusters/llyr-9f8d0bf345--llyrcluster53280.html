Llyr

proper noun
Celtic sea god in Welsh mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɪr / (AmE), / lɪə / (BrE)
A god in Welsh mythology, regarded as the father of Manawydan and often associated with the sea; corresponds to the Irish deity Lir.

Examples

Llyr sea god
In Welsh mythology, Llyr is revered as a powerful sea god.
Scholars often compare the legends of Llyr with those of his Irish counterpart, Lir.
The tales of Llyr and his children are central to medieval Welsh literature.
