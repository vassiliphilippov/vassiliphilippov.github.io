pupation

noun
larva becoming pupa
Pronunciation: / pjuːˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / pjuːˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or stage in an insect's life cycle when a larva becomes a pupa and undergoes transformation

Examples

Pupation stage
The caterpillar enters pupation before emerging as a butterfly.
During pupation, the insect undergoes significant changes inside the protective case.
Scientists observed the timing of pupation in different species of moths.
The caterpillar enters pupation before emerging as a butterfly.
During pupation, the insect's body is completely reorganized inside the chrysalis.
Environmental conditions can affect the length of pupation in many species of moths.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: imago - the final, adult stage of an insect after pupation
  • degree lower: larva - the immature, wingless, feeding stage of an insect before pupation
  • synonym: metamorphosis - the process of transformation from an immature form to an adult form in two or more stages
  • synonym: molting - the process of shedding old skin or covering to allow for growth
