overcall

noun
bridge: bid higher than opponent's
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvɚˌkɔl / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəkɔːl / (BrE)
(bridge) a bid that is made at a higher level than an opponent's bid, especially when your partner has not yet bid and the strength of your hand may not warrant such a bid.

Examples

A bridge overcall
After his opponent bid one spade, John made an overcall of two hearts.
Making an overcall can sometimes mislead the other players about the strength of your hand.
She hesitated before her overcall, knowing her cards were not ideal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bid - to offer a certain number of points or tricks in a game like bridge
