slumbery

adjective
archaic; sleepy, drowsy
Pronunciation: / ˈslʌmbəri / (AmE), / ˈslʌmbəri / (BrE)
archaic: inclined to or marked by drowsiness or sleepiness; sleepy.

Examples

A slumbery gaze
Her slumbery gaze drifted towards the window as the afternoon wore on.
His voice had a soft, slumbery quality that made everyone relax.
The room was filled with a slumbery silence, broken only by the ticking of the clock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drowsy - feeling sleepy or half-asleep
  • synonym: slumberous - sleepy, inducing sleep
