conventionalization

noun
process of making conventional
Pronunciation: / kənˌvɛnʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌvɛnʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or act of making something conform to conventional standards or styles

Examples

Conventionalization of language
Language undergoes conventionalization as new words are adopted and standardized.
Over time, the conventionalization of rituals can alter their original significance.
The conventionalization of art styles often leads to a loss of originality.
Over time, the conventionalisation of language helps communities understand one another more easily.
The conventionalisation of new art movements often leads to a loss of their original radical spirit.
There was criticism about the conventionalisation of once experimental theatre techniques.

  • synonym: conventionalize - to make conform to convention or established practice
  • antonym: innovation - the process of introducing new ideas or methods
