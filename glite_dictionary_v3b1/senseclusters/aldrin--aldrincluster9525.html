aldrin

noun
toxic insecticide
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.drɪn / (AmE), / ˈæl.drɪn / (BrE)
a highly toxic chemical insecticide formerly used to control pests, now banned in many countries

Examples

Toxic aldrin insecticide
The government banned aldrin after studies showed its harmful effects on the environment.
Farmers once used aldrin to protect crops from insects, but it is now considered dangerous.
Exposure to aldrin can cause serious health problems in humans and animals.

  • synonym: pesticide - a substance used to kill pests
