Helicobacter pylori

noun
bacterium linked to gastric ulcers
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛlɪkoʊˈbæktər paɪˈloʊraɪ / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪkəˈbæktə pʌɪˈlɔːraɪ / (BrE)
a species of spiral-shaped bacteria that inhabits the stomach lining and is associated with gastritis, gastric ulcers, and other digestive diseases

Examples

Helicobacter pylori infection
Doctors often prescribe antibiotics to eradicate Helicobacter pylori from the stomach.
If left untreated, an infection with Helicobacter pylori can lead to chronic gastritis.
The discovery of Helicobacter pylori was a breakthrough in understanding the cause of stomach ulcers.
