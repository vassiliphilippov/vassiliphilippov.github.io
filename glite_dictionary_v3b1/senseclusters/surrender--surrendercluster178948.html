surrender

noun
delivery of a person into lawful custody
4.7 3.9
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈren.dər/ (AmE), /sə.ˈren.də/ (BrE)
the act of delivering a person (such as a suspect or defendant) into lawful custody, especially to authorities or the police

Examples

Surrender of a suspect
A formal surrender of the suspect was arranged between the two countries.
His lawyer negotiated the terms of his surrender with the prosecution.
The fugitive agreed to surrender himself to the authorities to resolve the charges.
