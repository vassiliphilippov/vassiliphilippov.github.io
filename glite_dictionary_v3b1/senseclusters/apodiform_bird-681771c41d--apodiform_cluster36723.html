apodiform bird

noun
bird with long wings and weak feet
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑdəˌfɔrm bɝd / (AmE), / əˈpɒdɪfɔːm bɜːd / (BrE)
A nonpasserine bird with long wings and weak feet, spending much of its time in flight, such as swifts and hummingbirds.

Examples

A apodiform bird in flight
An apodiform bird spends much of its life in flight, rarely perching on the ground.
Swifts are a common example of an apodiform bird seen gliding across the evening sky.
The apodiform bird family includes both hummingbirds and swifts, known for their aerial skills.
