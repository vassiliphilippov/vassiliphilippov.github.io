Newton's second law of motion

noun
physics law: force equals mass times acceleration
4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈnjuː.tənz ˈsek.ənd lɔː əv ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈnuː.tənz ˈsek.ənd lɑː əv ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈnjuː.tənz ˈsek.ənd lɔː əv ˈməʊ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the law stating that the rate of change of momentum of an object is proportional to the force applied and occurs in the direction of that force

Examples

Newton's second law of motion force and acceleration
According to Newton's second law of motion, increasing the force on an object increases its acceleration.
Engineers rely on Newton's second law of motion when designing vehicles for maximum efficiency.
Physics students conducted experiments to test Newton's second law of motion using wheeled carts.
