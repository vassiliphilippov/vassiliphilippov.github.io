bolster

verb
to support or strengthen
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊl.stɚ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊl.stə / (BrE)
to support or strengthen something, or to provide encouragement or reinforcement

Examples

To bolster confidence
Her presence served to bolster his determination during difficult times.
The government took action to bolster the economy after the crisis.
The organization's new policies helped bolster employee confidence.
Extra research helped to bolster his argument during the debate.
Government programs are often introduced to bolster the economy during recessions.
She used pillows to bolster his back and help him sit upright.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strengthen - to make or become stronger
  • synonym: support - to bear the weight of, hold up
  • antonym: undermine - to weaken or impair
  • antonym: weaken - to make something less strong
