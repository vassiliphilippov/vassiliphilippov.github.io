gnarly

adjective
having a rough, knotted appearance
8.4 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ërli / (AmE), / Ààn…ëÀêli / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially of old trees or people) having a rough, knotted, or twisted appearance, often with knobs or protuberances.

Examples

Gnarly old tree
Her gnarly hands revealed years of hard work in the fields.
The old oak's gnarly branches twisted high above the ground.
We carefully avoided the tree's gnarly roots that jutted out of the path.

Synonyms

  • gnarled
    7.1 1.72/m
    gnarled describes twisted, knotty wood or hands more exactly, while gnarly is more informal and often used as slang.
  • knotted
    3.9 0.00/m
    knotted shows clear knots and tight twists on wood or skin, while gnarly adds rough bumps and a more rugged look.
  • knotty
    7.0 0.40/m
    knotty has many small knots or tangles in wood or hair, while gnarly means twisted, rough, and often lumpy.
  • knobby
    5.8 0.29/m
    knobby is covered with rounded knobs or bumps, while gnarly is twisted and knotted with a harsher, weathered look.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gnarled - rough and knotted, especially with age
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface
