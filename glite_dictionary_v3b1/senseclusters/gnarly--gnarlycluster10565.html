gnarly

adjective
having a rough, knotted appearance
8.4 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈnɑːr.liː/ /; / /ˈnɑːr.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈnɑː.liː/ /; / /ˈnɑː.lɪ/ (BrE)
(of a surface, especially of old trees or people) having a rough, knotted, or twisted appearance, often with knobs or protuberances.

Examples

Gnarly old tree
Her gnarly hands revealed years of hard work in the fields.
The old oak's gnarly branches twisted high above the ground.
We carefully avoided the tree's gnarly roots that jutted out of the path.

Related Terms
