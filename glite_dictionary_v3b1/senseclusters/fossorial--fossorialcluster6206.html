fossorial

adjective
adapted for digging (biology)
Pronunciation: / fɑˈsɔriəl / (AmE), / fɒˈsɔːriəl / (BrE)
(of limbs or animals) adapted for digging, typically characterized by strong claws or spade-like limbs.

Examples

A fossorial animal
Many rodents develop fossorial behaviors to create burrows for protection.
The anteater’s strong claws are adapted for a fossorial lifestyle.
The mole has fossorial limbs, allowing it to dig efficiently through soil.
