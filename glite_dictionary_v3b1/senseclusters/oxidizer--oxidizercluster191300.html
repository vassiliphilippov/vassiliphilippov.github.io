oxidizer

noun
substance that causes oxidation
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksɪˌdaɪzər /; / ˈɑːksɪˌdaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪzə / (BrE)
a substance that causes or supports oxidation, often by providing oxygen or another oxidizing agent

Examples

A strong oxidizer
A strong oxidizer can react violently with certain fuels.
Liquid oxygen is commonly used as an oxidizer in rocket engines.
The oxidizer must be handled carefully because it can accelerate combustion.
The rocket engine requires an oxidizer to ignite the fuel and produce thrust.
Liquid oxygen is commonly used as an oxidizer in space launch vehicles.
Without an oxidizer, the chemical reaction needed for burning cannot occur in space.
In chemical reactions, an oxidiser can often be a source of oxygen.
Some cleaning products contain a strong oxidiser that removes tough stains.
The rocket uses liquid oxygen as its primary oxidiser to support combustion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxidant - an alternative term for oxidizer, a substance that causes oxidation
  • synonym: oxidizer - substance that oxidizes another; American English spelling
  • antonym: reducer - a substance that donates electrons in a chemical reaction
  • antonym: reductant - a substance that reduces another substance in a redox reaction
