conjugation

noun
pairing of sexes for reproduction
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.dʒəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dʒʊˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of joining together, especially when two organisms or cells join to exchange genetic material (biology)

Examples

Bacterial conjugation
Conjugation in certain bacteria allows genetic material to be exchanged during reproduction.
Spring is a peak time for conjugation in many frog species.
The process of conjugation can ensure greater genetic diversity among offspring.
Bacterial conjugation allows cells to exchange genetic material.
During conjugation, two protozoan cells join briefly to share DNA.
The scientists observed the conjugation of two algae under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copulation - the act of sexual intercourse between a male and a female
  • synonym: mating - the action of coming together to breed
