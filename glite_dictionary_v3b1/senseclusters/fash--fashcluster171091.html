fash

verb
to trouble or annoy
Pronunciation: / fæʃ / (AmE), / fæʃ / (BrE)
to trouble, annoy, or worry someone, especially by causing inconvenience (chiefly Scottish, dialect).

Examples

To fash yourself
Try not to fash yourself over things you cannot change.
He told her not to fash about the late train, as it would arrive soon.
There's no need to fash the children with unnecessary worries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • synonym: bother - to take the trouble to do something or to annoy someone
  • antonym: calm - to make someone peaceful or relaxed
