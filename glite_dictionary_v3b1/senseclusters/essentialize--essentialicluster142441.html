essentialize

verb
to reduce to essentials
9.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈsen.ʃə.laɪz/ /; / /ɪ.ˈsen.ʃəl.aɪz/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈsen.ʃə.laɪz/ /; / /ɪ.ˈsen.ʃəl.aɪz/ (BrE)
to reduce something complex to its most basic or essential features, often ignoring important details or complexity

Examples

To essentialize cultures
It is important not to essentialize entire cultures based on stereotypes.
The article tends to essentialize complex social issues, ignoring important details.
Critics argue that the film essentializes the experiences of its characters, missing their individuality.
It is unhelpful to essentialize people based on stereotypes.
The article tends to essentialize complex cultural differences into simple categories.
Teachers should avoid essentializing students' backgrounds when discussing diversity in class.
The article essentializes the complex debate into a few key points for readers.
It is important not to essentialize cultures by ignoring their diversity.
Her presentation essentialized the research findings for a general audience.
