infrared ray

noun
ray of electromagnetic infrared radiation
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.frəˌrɛd ˈreɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˌrɛd ˈreɪ / (BrE)
A ray of electromagnetic radiation in the infrared spectrum, typically producing a thermal effect and invisible to the human eye.

Examples

An infrared ray heat
Exposure to an infrared ray can increase the temperature of the skin.
Many remote controls transmit signals using infrared rays.
The doctor used an infrared ray to warm the patient's muscles before therapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ultraviolet ray - a ray of electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays
