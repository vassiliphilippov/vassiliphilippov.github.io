predispose

verb
make likely or susceptible
6.5 6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.dɪˈspoʊz / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˈspəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone likely or more susceptible to a particular condition, attitude, or action

Examples

To predispose to illness

Experiencing trauma at a young age can predispose someone to anxiety disorders.
Genetic factors can predispose individuals to certain diseases.
