die

verb
come to an end (feeling, idea, etc)
4.8 38/m
Pronunciation: / daÉª / (AmE), / daÉª / (BrE)
(of a feeling, idea, or quality) to disappear or come to an end.

Examples

Old customs die out
Over time, many old traditions have simply died away.
Some say hope never truly dies, even in the darkest times.
The excitement in the room gradually died as the evening went on.

Synonyms

  • die away
    4.2 0.88/m
    die away means a sound or feeling fades slowly until it is gone, while die means to come to an end.
  • die down
    2.7 2.37/m
    die down means something loud or strong becomes quieter or less intense, while die means to stop or end.
  • disappear
    1.0 92/m
    disappear means to stop being present or seen, while die means to come to an end, often completely.
  • die out
    3.7 2.68/m
    die out describes something like a species or custom fading away completely over time, while die means to come to an end.
