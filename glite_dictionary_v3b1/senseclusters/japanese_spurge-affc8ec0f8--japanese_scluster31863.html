Japanese spurge

noun
Japanese evergreen ground-cover plant
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz spɝːdʒ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz spɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a slow-growing evergreen subshrub from Japan, with terminal spikes of white flowers, often used as a ground cover

Examples

Japanese spurge ground cover
Gardeners often recommend Japanese spurge as a low-maintenance ground cover.
The shady border was filled with Japanese spurge to prevent weeds from growing.
We planted Japanese spurge for its dense green foliage and delicate white flowers.
