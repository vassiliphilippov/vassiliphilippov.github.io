thecodont

noun
extinct reptile group ancestral to dinosaurs and birds
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈθiː.kə.dɑːnt/ (AmE), /ˈθiː.kə.dɒnt/ (BrE)
an extinct group of archosaurian reptiles believed to be ancestral to dinosaurs, crocodiles, and birds; formerly used as a taxonomic classification.

Examples

Fossil thecodonts
Fossils of thecodonts show features that link them to both crocodiles and birds.
Paleontologists once classified many early archosaurs as thecodonts.
The teeth of thecodonts were set in sockets, a trait shared with later dinosaurs.
