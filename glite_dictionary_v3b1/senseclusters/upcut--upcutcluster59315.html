upcut

verb
to cut upward
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkʌt / (BrE)
to make a cut in an upward direction, especially in machining, woodworking, or editing

Examples

To upcut metal
The machinist used a special tool to upcut the groove in the metal plate.
When editing the film, she decided to upcut the scene for a smoother transition.
To fit the joint properly, the woodworker had to upcut along the edge of the board.

  • antonym: undercut - to cut from below or make a cut underneath
