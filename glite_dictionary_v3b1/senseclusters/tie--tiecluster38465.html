tie

verb
to limit or restrict to a situation
Pronunciation: / taɪ / (AmE), / taɪ / (BrE)
to limit or restrict (someone or something) to a particular condition, place, or situation

Examples

To tie to a place
Her responsibilities as a parent tie her to home most evenings.
The new job offer would tie him to the city for several years.
These regulations tie small businesses to strict environmental standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bind - to tie or restrict something closely
  • antonym: free - to release from restriction or attachment
