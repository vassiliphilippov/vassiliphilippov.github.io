cloven-hoofed

adjective
having split hooves (as in cattle, goats)
Pronunciation: / ˌkloʊ.vən ˈhʊft / (AmE), / ˌkləʊ.vən ˈhʊft / (BrE)
(of an animal) having hooves split into two parts, as seen in animals such as cattle, sheep, goats, and pigs.

Examples

Cloven-hoofed animals
Pigs are cloven-hoofed animals commonly found on farms.
She studied the tracks left by cloven-hoofed deer in the muddy ground.
The Bible makes reference to cloven-hoofed creatures in dietary laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ungulate - hoofed mammal
