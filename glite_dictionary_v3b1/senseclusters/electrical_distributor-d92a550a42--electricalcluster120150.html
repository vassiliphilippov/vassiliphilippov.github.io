electrical distributor

noun
device for sending voltage to spark plugs in engines
2.4 5.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trɪ.kəl dɪ.ˈstrɪb.jə.tər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trɪ.kəl dɪ.ˈstrɪb.jə.tə/ (BrE)
A device in a gasoline engine that routes voltage to the spark plugs in the correct firing order.

Examples

An electrical distributor device
An electrical distributor sends high-voltage current to each spark plug in the right sequence.
Older cars often need their electrical distributor caps checked and replaced regularly.
The mechanic replaced the faulty electrical distributor to ensure the engine would start correctly.
