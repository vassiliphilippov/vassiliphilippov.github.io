downforce

noun
aerodynamic downward pressure
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.fɔːrs/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.fɔːs/ (BrE)
the downward pressure created by airflow over a vehicle, increasing its grip on the road at high speeds

Examples

Car downforce at speed
The race car's new design generates more downforce to improve cornering speed.
Engineers adjusted the rear wing to increase downforce during the qualifying laps.
Without enough downforce, the vehicle could lose traction at high speeds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerodynamics - the study of the movement of air and its interaction with solid objects
  • antonym: lift - the upward force created by airflow over a surface
