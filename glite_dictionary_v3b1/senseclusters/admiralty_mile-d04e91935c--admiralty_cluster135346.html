Admiralty mile

noun
former British nautical mile
Pronunciation: / ˌæd.mɪr.əl.ti ˈmaɪl / (AmE), / ˌæd.mɪr.əl.ti ˈmaɪl / (BrE)
a former British unit of length used in marine navigation, equal to 6,080 feet (1,853.184 meters), 800 feet longer than the statute mile

Examples

An Admiralty mile unit
Older naval charts often measure distances in Admiralty miles rather than nautical miles.
Surveyors recorded the coastal length using the Admiralty mile during the 19th century.
The ship logged exactly one Admiralty mile before reaching the buoy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nautical mile - unit of distance used in navigation, approximately 1,852 meters
  • antonym: statute mile - standard mile used on land, equal to 5,280 feet
