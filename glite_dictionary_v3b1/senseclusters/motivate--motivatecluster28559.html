motivate

verb
give someone a reason to act
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊ.t̬ə.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.tɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
to provide someone with a reason or incentive to do something; to stimulate interest or enthusiasm for doing something

Examples

To motivate workers
Money can motivate people, but so can a passion for their work.
She tried to motivate her students by making the lessons more engaging.
The coach worked hard to motivate the team before the championship game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • synonym: inspire - to fill someone with the urge or ability to do or feel something
