seismic activity

noun
earth movements caused by tectonic processes
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪzmɪk ækˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˈsaɪzmɪk ækˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
movements of the Earth's crust caused by tectonic processes, often resulting in earthquakes or tremors

Examples

Increased seismic activity
After the volcano erupted, scientists recorded increased seismic activity in the area.
Seismologists use sensitive instruments to measure even minor seismic activity deep underground.
The region is known for frequent seismic activity, so buildings are designed to withstand earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthquake - sudden shaking of the ground due to seismic activity
