pre-intervention

noun
phase before intervention
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
a phase or assessment conducted before an intervention to record baseline conditions or gather initial data

Examples

A pre-intervention assessment
Researchers collected pre-intervention data to compare with results after the program was implemented.
The pre-intervention survey helped identify the main issues facing the community.
Accurate pre-intervention measurements are essential for evaluating the effectiveness of any treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseline assessment - an initial evaluation before any treatment or intervention
  • antonym: post-intervention - the phase or assessment after an intervention has taken place
