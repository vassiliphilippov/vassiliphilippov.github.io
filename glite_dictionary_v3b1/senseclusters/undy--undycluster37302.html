undy

adjective
wavy-edged (heraldry)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndi / (AmE), / ˈʌndi / (BrE)
having a wavy edge or line, especially in heraldry to describe a border or division on a shield

Examples

Undy border on a shield
The family crest featured an undy line dividing the shield into two sections.
In heraldic art, an undy border often represents water or waves.
The designer chose an undy pattern to give the emblem a sense of movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: straight - having no curves or bends
  • synonym: wavy - having a series of curves or undulations
