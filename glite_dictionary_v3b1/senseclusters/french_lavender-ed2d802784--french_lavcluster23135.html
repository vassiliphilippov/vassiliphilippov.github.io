French lavender

noun
Mediterranean aromatic plant with purple flowers
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ ˈlævəndɚ /; / frɛntʃ ˈlævəndər / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˈlævəndə / (BrE)
a Mediterranean plant (Lavandula stoechas) with fragrant purple flowers, often grown for its scent or as an ornamental plant

Examples

French lavender flowers
A field of French lavender stretched along the hillside, attracting bees and visitors alike.

The gardener planted a row of French lavender along the walkway to add color and fragrance.
Unlike other species, French lavender thrives in warmer, drier climates.
