reduce

verb
make less complex or simpler
2.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdus / (AmE), / rɪˈdjuːs / (BrE)
to make something simpler or less complex by breaking it down, condensing, or summarizing (including in math, arguments, presentations, or data)

Examples

To reduce a problem
Engineers often reduce complex systems into manageable components before designing solutions.
In her report, she tried to reduce the technical details to layman’s terms.
The professor managed to reduce the complicated theory to a few key principles.
In algebra, we often reduce expressions to make calculations easier.
The equation was reduced to a simpler form by collecting like terms.
You can reduce the fraction to its lowest terms by dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor.
All these issues reduce to one core question: fairness.
His argument can ultimately reduce to a call for greater efficiency.
The debate on taxes essentially reduces to how much the government should spend.
