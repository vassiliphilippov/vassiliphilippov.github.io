coltan

noun
a valuable black mineral used in electronics
9.2 9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkoʊl.tæn/ (AmE), /ˈkɒl.tæn/ /; / /ˈkəʊl.tæn/ (BrE)
A valuable black mineral composed chiefly of columbite and tantalite, used as a source of niobium and tantalum in electronic devices.

Examples

Many environmentalists are concerned about the impact of coltan mining on local ecosystems.
Miners working in Africa often extract coltan to supply the global electronics industry.
The demand for coltan has soared with the widespread use of smartphones and laptops.
