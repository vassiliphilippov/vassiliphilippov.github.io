clip-clop

noun

the sound made by the hooves of a horse or similar animal as they strike a hard surface, typically represented as a rhythmic, repeating noise

Examples

The clip-clop sound
He listened to the steady clip-clop as the carriage approached.
The clip-clop of horses echoed down the narrow cobblestone street.
The familiar clip-clop reassured her that the old cart was on its way.

Synonyms

  • clippety-clop
    9.0 0.01/m
    clippety-clop is a longer, playful imitation of horse hooves in stories, while clip-clop is a simpler rhythmic hoof sound.
  • hoofbeat
    4.3 0.38/m
    hoofbeat is the real sound or step of a horse's hoof on the ground, while clip-clop is a word that imitates that rhythmic hoof noise.
  • clop
    7.9 0.30/m
    clop is a single, short loud hit of a hoof on pavement, while clip-clop describes a steady, repeating hoof rhythm.
