prequalification

noun
initial approval process
6.3
Pronunciation: /priː.kwɑː.lə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.kwɑː.lə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.kwɒ.lɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.kwɒ.lɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or result of checking if someone or something meets the basic requirements before being allowed to continue or apply for something

Examples

The prequalification process
The company submitted its prequalification documents before bidding on the government contract.
Mortgage prequalification helps buyers understand how much they can afford before shopping for a home.
Without prequalification, applicants are not allowed to proceed to the next stage of the process.
The bank requires prequalification before you can submit a full mortgage application.
Job seekers often go through prequalification to ensure they meet the basic criteria.
Without prequalification, your application may not be considered by the company.
