steampunk

noun
genre of science fiction with Victorian steam technology
7.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈstiːm.pʌŋk/ (AmE), /ˈstiːm.pʌŋk/ (BrE)
a style, genre, or subculture inspired by 19th-century steam-powered technology and Victorian-era design, often seen in science fiction, fashion, and art

Examples

Steampunk style
He wore a steampunk costume to the convention, complete with goggles and brass gears.
Many films in the steampunk tradition blend Victorian aesthetics with futuristic technology.
The novelist is known for her intricate steampunk worlds filled with airships and clockwork inventions.
She decorated her living room with steampunk lamps, gears, and leather-bound books for an antique feel.
The steampunk community meets monthly to trade handmade goggles, modify clocks, and discuss costume ideas.
Designers showed a steampunk collection combining brass detailing with futuristic silhouettes on the runway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyberpunk - a genre of science fiction featuring advanced technology and dystopian settings
  • synonym: retro-futurism - an artistic and design movement imagining the future from a past perspective, often blending old styles with futuristic ideas
