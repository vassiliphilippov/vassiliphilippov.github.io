unbar

verb
remove a bar from (a door, window, etc.)
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈbɑr / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈbɑː / (BrE)
to remove a bar or bolt from (a door, window, etc.), allowing it to be opened

Examples

To unbar the door
Every morning, he would unbar the shop windows to let in the light.
The guard used a heavy key to unbar the ancient wooden door.
The villagers hurried to unbar the gates before the procession arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bar - to fasten or secure with a bar
