unbar

verb
remove a bar from (a door, window, etc.)
7.4 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Àå ånÀàb…ër / (AmE), / Àå ånÀàb…ëÀê / (BrE)
to remove a bar or bolt from (a door, window, etc.), allowing it to be opened

Examples

To unbar the door
Every morning, he would unbar the shop windows to let in the light.
The guard used a heavy key to unbar the ancient wooden door.
The villagers hurried to unbar the gates before the procession arrived.

Synonyms

  • unbolt
    3.7 0.27/m
    unbolt means undoing a bolt on a door or window, while unbar describes removing a bar or bolt that blocks opening.
  • unlatch
    3.2 0.72/m
    unlatch releases a latch so a door or gate opens easily, while unbar refers to taking away a bar or bolt that keeps it closed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bar - to fasten or secure with a bar
