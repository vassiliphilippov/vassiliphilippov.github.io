sapremia

noun
blood poisoning from putrefied matter
Pronunciation: / sæˈpriːmiə / (AmE), / sæˈpriːmɪə /; / sæˈpriːmiə / (BrE)
A type of blood poisoning caused by putrefactive bacteria, resulting from the absorption of putrefied organic matter.

Examples

In the early 1900s, sapremia was a significant complication in battlefield injuries.
Proper sterilization techniques have greatly reduced the risk of sapremia in modern hospitals.

Early detection of sapraemia is essential to prevent septic shock.
The patient developed sapraemia after consuming spoiled food.
Without prompt treatment, sapraemia can rapidly become life-threatening.
