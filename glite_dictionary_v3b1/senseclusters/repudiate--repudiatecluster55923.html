repudiate

verb
refuse to accept or acknowledge
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpjuːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈpjuːdɪeɪt /; / rɪˈpjuːdieɪt / (BrE)
to refuse to accept, acknowledge, or be associated with something; to reject or deny formally

Examples

To repudiate claims
He chose to repudiate the allegations made against him in the press.
Many historians repudiate the theory as lacking sufficient evidence.
The company may repudiate any agreement that is not legally binding.
After the scandal, the politician was quick to repudiate any involvement.
His family chose to repudiate his actions and cut all ties with him.
The organization decided to repudiate its controversial past.
He tried to repudiate the charges, claiming he owed nothing.
Several nations threatened to repudiate their foreign debt amid the crisis.
The company decided to repudiate its obligation to repay the loan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to consent to receive or undertake something offered
  • antonym: acknowledge - to accept or recognize as valid or true
  • synonym: deny - to declare something is not true
  • synonym: disown - to refuse to acknowledge or accept as one's own
  • antonym: embrace - to accept or support willingly and enthusiastically
  • synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, consider, or submit to something
