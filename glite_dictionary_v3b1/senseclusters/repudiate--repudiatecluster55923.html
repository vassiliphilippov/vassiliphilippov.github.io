repudiate

verb
refuse to accept or acknowledge
8.6 6.5
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈpjuː.dɪ.eɪt/ /; / /rə.ˈpjuː.dɪ.eɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈpjuː.dɪ.eɪt/ /; / /rə.ˈpjuː.dɪ.eɪt/ (BrE)
to refuse to accept, acknowledge, or be associated with something; to reject or deny formally

Examples

To repudiate claims
He chose to repudiate the allegations made against him in the press.
Many historians repudiate the theory as lacking sufficient evidence.
The company may repudiate any agreement that is not legally binding.

He tried to repudiate the charges, claiming he owed nothing.
Several nations threatened to repudiate their foreign debt amid the crisis.
The company decided to repudiate its obligation to repay the loan.
