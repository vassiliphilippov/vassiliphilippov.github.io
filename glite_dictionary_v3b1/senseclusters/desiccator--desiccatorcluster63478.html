desiccator

noun
moisture remover
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdes.ɪ.keɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈdes.ɪ.keɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a sealed container or device used in laboratories to keep substances dry by removing or preventing moisture

Examples

Laboratory desiccator
The laboratory stored sensitive chemicals in a desiccator to keep them dry.
A small desiccator was placed inside the packaging to protect the electronics from moisture.
Silica gel is often used as a desiccator in shipping containers.
The scientist placed the chemical samples in a desiccator to keep them free from moisture.
After washing the glassware, she stored it in a desiccator to ensure it stayed dry.
A desiccator is essential in laboratories where humidity can affect experimental results.
The scientist placed the chemical sample in a desiccator to keep it free from moisture.
After washing the glassware, she stored it in a desiccator overnight.
A desiccator is essential for preserving sensitive materials in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dehumidifier - a device that removes moisture from the air
  • antonym: humidifier - a device that adds moisture to the air
