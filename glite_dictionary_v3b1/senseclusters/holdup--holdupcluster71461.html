holdup

noun
armed robbery; robbery at gunpoint
4.6 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhoÊŠldËŒÊŒp / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ™ÊŠldÊŒp / (BrE)
an act of stealing from someone by using threats or violence, especially a robbery at gunpoint

Examples

Bank holdup
She was traumatized after witnessing a holdup at the convenience store.
The bank holdup lasted only five minutes before the police arrived.
The news reported another armed holdup on Main Street last night.

Synonyms

  • stickup
    7.9 0.24/m
    stickup is a quick, often gunpoint robbery of a person in public, while holdup refers to the same violent theft.
  • armed robbery
    3.2 1.83/m
    armed robbery is the legal term for stealing with a weapon, while holdup is the informal word for such violent theft.
  • robbery
    2.3 17/m
    robbery is the formal crime of taking property by force or threat, while holdup sounds more casual and violent.
  • heist
    6.9 2.38/m
    heist is a planned, often clever theft of money or goods, while holdup usually means a sudden armed robbery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: burglary - the act of entering a building illegally to commit a crime, especially theft
  • synonym: robbery - the act of taking property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
