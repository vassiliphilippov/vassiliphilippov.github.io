(of language or terms) characteristic of or appropriate to ordinary, everyday speech, rather than formal or technical language.

Examples

Vulgar language
Shakespeare often included dialogue in the vulgar tongue to appeal to a wider audience.
The biologist explained the species' scientific and vulgar names to the students.
While the official term is 'canine,' the vulgar form is simply 'dog.'

Synonyms

  • vernacular
    6.5 1.44/m

  • colloquial
    6.5 0.95/m
    colloquial is used in casual spoken conversation and informal writing, while vulgar refers to ordinary, nontechnical speech.
  • common
    1.0 10/m
    common is language or words typical and often used by many people, while vulgar labels ordinary, nonformal speech rather than technical.

Related Terms
