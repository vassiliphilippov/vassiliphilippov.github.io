vulgar

adjective
relating to common or everyday speech
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡə / (BrE)
(of language or terms) characteristic of or appropriate to ordinary, everyday speech, rather than formal or technical language.

Examples

Vulgar language
Shakespeare often included dialogue in the vulgar tongue to appeal to a wider audience.
The biologist explained the species' scientific and vulgar names to the students.
While the official term is 'canine,' the vulgar form is simply 'dog.'

Related Terms

  • synonym: colloquial - used in ordinary or familiar conversation; not formal or literary
  • antonym: formal - marked by strict or official style, as opposed to everyday speech
  • synonym: vernacular - the language spoken by ordinary people in a particular region
