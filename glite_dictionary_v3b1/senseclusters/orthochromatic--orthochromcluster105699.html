orthochromatic

adjective
not sensitive to red light
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrθoʊkrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːθəʊkrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
sensitive to all visible light except red, especially used to describe photographic film or plates

Examples

Orthochromatic film
Early photographers used orthochromatic film, which could not accurately capture red flowers in their images.
Because the film was orthochromatic, the actor's red lipstick appeared much darker in the final photograph.
Switching from orthochromatic to panchromatic film allowed for more realistic color representation in black-and-white photos.

Related Terms

  • antonym: panchromatic - sensitive to all colors of visible light
