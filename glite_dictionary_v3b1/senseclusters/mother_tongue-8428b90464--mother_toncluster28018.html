mother tongue

noun
native language; language learned from childhood
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌðɚ tʌŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌðə tʌŋ / (BrE)
the language that a person has learned from early childhood; one's native language

Examples

Mother tongue language
English is my mother tongue, but I also speak Spanish fluently.
Preserving one's mother tongue is important for maintaining cultural identity.
She returned to her homeland to teach children in their mother tongue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: native language - the language a person learns from birth
  • antonym: second language - a language learned after the mother tongue
