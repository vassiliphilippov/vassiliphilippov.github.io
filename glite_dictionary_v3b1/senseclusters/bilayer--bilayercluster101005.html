bilayer

noun
two-layer structure
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌleɪər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌleɪə / (BrE)
a structure composed of two closely joined layers, especially of molecules or materials

Examples

Lipid bilayer
The cell membrane is made up of a lipid bilayer that controls what enters and leaves the cell.
Scientists studied the properties of the artificial bilayer to better understand biological membranes.
A bilayer can form naturally when certain molecules are placed in water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: membrane - a thin, flexible layer that forms a boundary
  • antonym: monolayer - a single, one-molecule-thick layer
