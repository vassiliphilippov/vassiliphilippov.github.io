overcut

noun
excessive cut area
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.kʌt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.kʌt/ (BrE)
an area, notch, or groove where a cut has gone beyond the intended boundary, especially in machining or carpentry

Examples

A deep overcut
The technician noticed an overcut on the metal piece and marked it for correction.
Careful measurement is needed to avoid an overcut when using power tools.
The carpenter filled the overcut with wood putty before painting the surface.
