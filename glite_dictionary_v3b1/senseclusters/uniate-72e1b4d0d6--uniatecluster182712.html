Uniate

noun
member of an Eastern Catholic Church
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːniˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪeɪt / (BrE)
A member of an Eastern Christian church in communion with the Roman Catholic Church but retaining its own rites and traditions.

Examples

A Uniate church
Her grandparents were Uniates from Eastern Europe.
Many Uniate churches still follow the Byzantine liturgical traditions.
The Uniate community celebrates several unique religious festivals.
