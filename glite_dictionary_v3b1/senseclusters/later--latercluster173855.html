Synonyms

  • subsequent
    4.4 30/m
    subsequent describes something that follows in order in formal contexts, while later is more general and common in speech.
  • subsequently
    4.8 15/m
    subsequently is used in formal writing to show what happens next, while later is a shorter everyday word for the same idea.
  • afterward
    1.1 20/m
    afterward means at a later time in speech or writing, while later is more flexible and often used in casual talk.
  • later on
    1.0 12/m
    later on is a friendly spoken phrase for something happening after now, while later is more neutral and fits many contexts.
  • thereafter
    4.1 11/m
    thereafter means from that time forward over a period, while later refers simply to a time after something, not necessarily continuous.
