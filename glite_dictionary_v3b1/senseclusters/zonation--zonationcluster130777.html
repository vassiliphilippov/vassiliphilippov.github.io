zonation

noun
division into zones
8.5
Pronunciation: / zoʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / zəʊˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the arrangement or division of an area into distinct zones, often based on natural or human factors

Examples

Vegetation zonation
The zonation of vegetation in the mountains changes with altitude and climate.
Urban planners study the zonation of cities to improve transportation and housing.
Coastal zonation helps scientists understand how different species adapt to varying conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniformity - the state of being the same throughout
  • synonym: zoning - the process of dividing land into areas with specific rules or purposes
