unsoftened

adjective
not made softer
4.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɑftənd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɒfənd / (BrE)
not made softer or less hard; still hard or firm (can refer to materials, food, etc.)

Examples

Unsoftened bread
The unsoftened glass was more likely to crack under sudden temperature changes.
Because the metal remained unsoftened, it was difficult to shape into the desired form.
Engineers tested the unsoftened steel to determine its strength and brittleness.
The unsoftened butter was difficult to spread on the bread.
He spoke with an unsoftened tone that made his criticism sound harsh.
The unsoftened fabric felt rough against my skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hard - firm and not easily bent or changed
  • synonym: hardened - made hard or harder, often by heat treatment
  • antonym: softened - made softer or less hard
