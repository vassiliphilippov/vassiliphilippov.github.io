St. Luke

proper noun
Christian apostle and author of a Gospel
Pronunciation: / seɪnt luːk / (AmE), / sənt luːk / (BrE)
a Christian saint and apostle, traditionally regarded as the author of the third Gospel and closely associated with St. Paul

Examples

St. Luke the apostle
Artists often depict St. Luke painting a picture of the Virgin Mary.
Many scholars believe that St. Luke was both a physician and an evangelist.
The Gospel attributed to St. Luke provides a detailed account of the life of Jesus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gospel of Luke - the third Gospel in the New Testament, attributed to St. Luke
  • synonym: St. Matthew - apostle and author of the first Gospel
  • synonym: St. Paul - Christian apostle known for spreading Christianity and writing many New Testament letters
