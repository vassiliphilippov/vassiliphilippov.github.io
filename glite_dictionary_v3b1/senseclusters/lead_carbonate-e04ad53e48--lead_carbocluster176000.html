lead carbonate

noun
poisonous white lead-containing pigment
Pronunciation: / lɛd ˈkɑːrbəˌneɪt / (AmE), / lɛd ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a poisonous white compound containing lead, formerly used as a pigment

Examples

Lead carbonate pigment
Exposure to lead carbonate can be highly toxic, especially to children.
Many artists in the past used lead carbonate as a pigment in their oil paints.
The use of lead carbonate in household paints has been banned in many countries due to its health risks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: white lead - a traditional pigment made from basic lead carbonate
