hidalgo

noun
Spanish minor noble
Pronunciation: / iˈdɑlɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɪˈdælɡəʊ / (BrE)
a member of the lower nobility in Spain or Spanish-speaking countries, especially in historical times

Examples

Spanish hidalgo
The proud hidalgo refused to work with his hands, believing it beneath his status.
In old Spain, a hidalgo might own little land but still command respect in the community.
Many stories feature a wandering hidalgo seeking adventure and honor.
In medieval Spain, a hidalgo often owned land but had little wealth.
The story follows a young hidalgo who dreams of achieving great honor.
Don Quixote is a famous literary hidalgo known for his adventures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nobleman - a man belonging to the nobility
  • antonym: peasant - a poor farmer of low social status
