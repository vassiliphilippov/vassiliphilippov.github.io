subsistent

adjective
barely sufficient
Pronunciation: / səbˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
having or providing only the basic necessities needed to support life or existence at a minimal level

Examples

Subsistent farming
In some remote areas, people rely on subsistent agriculture to feed their families.
The community's subsistent lifestyle means they have little access to modern conveniences.
Many animals in harsh environments lead a subsistent existence, surviving on limited resources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundant - more than enough; plentiful
  • synonym: subsistence - the condition of having just enough to survive
