backcasting

noun
planning from future goal
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌkæstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌkɑːstɪŋ / (BrE)
a planning method that begins with a desired future outcome and works backward to determine the steps needed to achieve it

Examples

Backcasting a future plan
The organization used backcasting to design a roadmap for achieving carbon neutrality by 2050.
In strategic workshops, backcasting helps teams clarify the steps needed to reach ambitious goals.
By applying backcasting, the city identified key actions required for a sustainable future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forecasting - predicting future events based on current trends
  • synonym: planning - the process of making plans for something
