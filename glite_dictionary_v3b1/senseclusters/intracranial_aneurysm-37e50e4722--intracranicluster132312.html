intracranial aneurysm

noun
aneurysm in a brain artery
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈkreɪniəl ˈænjəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈkreɪniəl ˈanjʊərɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A localized dilation or bulging of an artery within the skull, which may lead to serious medical complications if ruptured.

Examples

An intracranial aneurysm bulge
Detecting an intracranial aneurysm early can be lifesaving and prevent a potential brain hemorrhage.
Symptoms of an intracranial aneurysm can include severe headache, vision problems, or loss of consciousness.
The surgeon explained that the patient had an intracranial aneurysm that required urgent treatment.
