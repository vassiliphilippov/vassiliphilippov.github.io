conspiracy theory

noun
belief in secret manipulation of events
Pronunciation: / kənˈspɪrəsi ˌθiəri / (AmE), / kənˈspɪrəsi ˌθɪəri / (BrE)
the belief that certain events or situations are secretly controlled or manipulated by a covert group, often without evidence

Examples

A conspiracy theory claim
He dismissed the story as just another conspiracy theory without any real proof.
She researched the origins of the famous conspiracy theory about the moon landing.
Social media has helped the rapid spread of the conspiracy theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plot - a secret plan to achieve a particular aim
