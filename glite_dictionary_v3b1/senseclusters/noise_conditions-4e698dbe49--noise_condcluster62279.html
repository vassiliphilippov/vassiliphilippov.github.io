noise conditions

noun
state of being noisy, especially in a channel
6.1
Pronunciation: / nɔɪz kənˈdɪʃənz / (AmE), / nɔɪz kənˈdɪʃənz / (BrE)
the state or circumstances in which unwanted, random disturbances affect a system or communication channel, making signals less clear or reliable

Examples

High noise conditions
Certain electronic devices are designed to operate well even in high noise conditions.
Communications in space often suffer from difficult noise conditions due to cosmic radiation.
The experiment was conducted under low noise conditions to ensure accurate readings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interference - disturbance affecting clarity of signals
  • synonym: signal-to-noise ratio - the ratio of desired signal to background noise in a system
