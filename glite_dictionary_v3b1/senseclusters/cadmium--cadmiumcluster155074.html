cadmium

noun
a toxic metallic chemical element (Cd)
Pronunciation: / ˈkæd.mi.əm / (AmE), / ˈkæd.mɪ.əm / (BrE)
a soft, bluish-white, ductile, and malleable toxic metallic element, typically found in association with zinc ores; chemical symbol Cd

Examples

Toxic cadmium metal
Industrial batteries often contain cadmium, which can be hazardous if not disposed of properly.
Researchers detected traces of cadmium in the water samples collected near the mining site.
The presence of cadmium in soil can pose a risk to agricultural crops.
