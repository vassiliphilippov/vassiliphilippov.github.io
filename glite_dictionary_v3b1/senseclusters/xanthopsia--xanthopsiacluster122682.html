xanthopsia

noun
visual defect causing yellow-tinted vision
8.8 9.9
Pronunciation: /zæn.θɑːp.ˈsiː.ə/ /; / /zæn.θɔːp.ˈsiː.ə/ (AmE), /zæn.θɒp.ˈsɪə/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which all objects appear to have a yellowish tint, sometimes occurring in jaundice and certain drug toxicities

Examples

Xanthopsia yellow tint

Some medications can induce xanthopsia as a side effect, making colors appear abnormally yellow.
