amerciable

adjective
liable to discretionary fine (legal)
8.4 9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmɜːr.sɪə.bəl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmɜː.sɪə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a crime or misdemeanor) liable to punishment by a fine set at the discretion of a judge, rather than a fixed statutory penalty.

Examples

An amerciable crime
In medieval England, many minor crimes were deemed amerciable instead of warranting harsher punishment.
Only certain misdemeanors remained amerciable after the legal reforms of the 19th century.
The judge declared that the offense was amerciable and imposed a fine accordingly.
