goujon

noun
large catfish with flat head and projecting jaw
Pronunciation: / ˈɡuːˌʒɑn / (AmE), / ˈɡuːʒɒn / (BrE)
A large catfish of the central United States, characterized by a flattened head and a projecting jaw.

Examples

The goujon fish
Fishermen in the Mississippi often catch the massive goujon during the summer months.
Locals pride themselves on their traditional recipes for cooking goujon caught from regional rivers.
The goujon is known for its distinctive flattened head and strong jaws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catfish - a freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
