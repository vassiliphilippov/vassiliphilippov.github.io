offset

verb
transfer ink to another surface (printing)
4.2 5.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈfset/ (AmE), /ə.ˈfset/ (BrE)
to produce printed material by means of offset printing, or to unintentionally transfer ink onto another surface

Examples

To offset images
Be careful when stacking these brochures, as the images might offset and leave marks.
If wet ink is not allowed to dry properly, it can offset onto the next page.
The printer used special drying techniques to prevent the text from offsetting onto other sheets.
Many scientific journals are still offset rather than printed digitally.
The publisher decided to offset the annual report for higher quality.
These brochures were offset last week at a local print shop.
