coontail

noun
aquatic plant
Pronunciation: / ˈkuːnˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈkuːnˌteɪl / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with leaves arranged in whorls, commonly found in ponds and lakes

Examples

Pond coontail plant
The lake was covered with thick patches of coontail near the shoreline.
Small fish often hide among the leaves of coontail for protection.
Gardeners sometimes add coontail to ponds to help control algae growth.
