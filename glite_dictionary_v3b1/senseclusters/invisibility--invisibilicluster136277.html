invisibility

noun
undetectability to certain electromagnetic waves
9.5 0.4/m

(in physics and materials science) the property or effect of making an object undetectable to a particular range of electromagnetic waves by controlling how those waves interact with the object (for example using metamaterials or cloaking devices).

Examples

Invisibility cloak
Researchers demonstrated invisibility cloaks that guide infrared light around small objects in the lab.
Using metamaterials, engineers achieved partial invisibility at microwave frequencies for a cylindrical test object.
The paper described an algorithm to measure invisibility of surfaces across different electromagnetic wavelengths.
