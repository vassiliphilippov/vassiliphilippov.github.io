transcendental number

noun
irrational, non-algebraic number
6.6
Pronunciation: / trænˌsɛnˈdɛntəl ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / trænˌsɛnˈdɛntəl ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
A real or complex number that is not the root of any nonzero polynomial equation with rational coefficients.

Examples

A transcendental number in math
No algebraic equation with rational coefficients has a transcendental number as its solution.
Proving that e is a transcendental number was a major achievement in mathematics.
The number π is a famous example of a transcendental number.
