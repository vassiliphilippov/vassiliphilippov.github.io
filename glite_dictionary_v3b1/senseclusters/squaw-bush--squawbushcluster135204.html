squaw-bush

noun
California shrub with trifoliate leaves and edible fruit
Pronunciation: / skwɔː bʊʃ / (AmE), / skwɔː bʊʃ / (BrE)
a deciduous shrub native to California, with usually trifoliate, unpleasantly scented leaves and edible fruit

Examples

The squaw-bush plant
She recognized the squaw-bush by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive scent.
The squaw-bush grows in dry, rocky areas of California.
Wildlife in the region feed on the edible fruit of the squaw-bush.
