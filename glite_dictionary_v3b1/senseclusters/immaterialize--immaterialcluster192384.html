immaterialize

verb
make immaterial or incorporeal
1.3 9.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.mə.ˈtɪr.iː.ə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ɪ.mə.ˈtɪə.riː.ə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to render immaterial or incorporeal; to cause to lose physical substance or form

Examples

To immaterialize the body
In some theories, consciousness could immaterialize the mind, separating it from the body.
The villain's body began to immaterialize, vanishing into thin air as we watched.
The wizard chanted a spell to immaterialize the locked door before us.
In some stories, ghosts can immaterialise at will to move between realms.
The character seemed to immaterialise before our eyes, dissolving into thin air.
The wizard whispered a spell to immaterialise his form and pass unnoticed through the wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dematerialize - to lose or cause to lose physical substance; become immaterial
  • antonym: materialize - to become material or take physical form
