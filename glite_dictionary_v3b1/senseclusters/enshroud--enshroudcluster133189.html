enshroud

verb
to cover or conceal completely
5.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈʃraʊd / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃraʊd / (BrE)
to cover or conceal something completely, especially in a way that obscures view or understanding

Examples

To enshroud in fog
A dense fog began to enshroud the harbor, making navigation nearly impossible.
Rumors and secrecy enshroud the details of the ancient manuscript's discovery.
Shame enshrouded his thoughts, preventing him from speaking openly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reveal - to make something known or visible
  • synonym: shroud - to cover or hide something; a covering cloth, especially for a dead body
