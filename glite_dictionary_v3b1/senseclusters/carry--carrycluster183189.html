carry

verb
capture after fighting
8.1 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri /; / ˈkæɹi / (AmE), / ˈkæri /; / ˈkæɹi / (BrE)
to win, secure, or achieve success in a vote, contest, battle, or similar situation

Examples

To carry the vote
After a fierce battle, the army managed to carry the city walls.
The general ordered his men to carry the enemy's stronghold at any cost.
The rebels hoped to carry the fortress before dawn.
If the amendment carries, it will become part of the constitution.
The bill did not carry in the senate last night.
The proposal carried by a wide margin after a lively debate.
Despite a close race, she managed to carry the district by a narrow margin.
No other party has ever carried this region since the reform.
The candidate hopes to carry several key states in the upcoming election.
