paleoclimatic

adjective
ancient climate
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪli.oʊklaɪˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌpeɪli.əʊklaɪˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to the climate of the geological past or ancient times

Examples

Paleoclimatic records
Researchers analyze paleoclimatic records to understand how Earth's climate has changed over millions of years.
The paleoclimatic evidence suggests that the region was once much wetter than it is today.
By studying paleoclimatic data, scientists can predict future climate trends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times
  • synonym: paleoclimatological - relating to the scientific study of ancient climates
