stimulative

adjective
causing increased physiological or psychological activity
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪmjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈstɪmjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
Having the ability to arouse or increase physiological or psychological activity, often by means of a chemical agent.

Examples

Stimulative effect
Caffeine is widely known for its stimulative properties.
Doctors monitored the patient's response to the stimulative medication.
The new drug has a stimulative effect on the central nervous system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depressant - a substance that reduces physiological or nervous activity
  • synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
