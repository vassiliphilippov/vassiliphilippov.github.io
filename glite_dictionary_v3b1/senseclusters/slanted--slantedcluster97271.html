slanted

adjective
showing unfair preference; biased
7.7 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈslæntəd / (AmE), / ˈslɑːntɪd / (BrE)
presented in a way that shows an unfair preference for one person, side, or group over others; biased or partial.

Examples

Slanted news report
Her summary was slanted to make her team look better than the competition.
The newspaper article was clearly slanted in favor of the city's mayor.
Viewers complained that the documentary was slanted and did not show both sides of the issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - showing an unreasonable preference or prejudice
  • antonym: impartial - showing no preference or bias; fair
  • synonym: partial - favoring one side over another
