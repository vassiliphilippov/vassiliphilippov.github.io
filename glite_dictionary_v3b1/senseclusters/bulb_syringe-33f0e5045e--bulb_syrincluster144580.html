bulb syringe

noun
flexible device for suctioning or irrigation, often for nose or ears
Pronunciation: / bʌlb səˈrɪndʒ / (AmE), / bʌlb səˈrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a small device with a flexible rubber bulb, used to suction fluids from or introduce fluids into body cavities, especially the nose and ears

Examples

A bulb syringe device
After washing it thoroughly, she stored the bulb syringe in a clean container.
Parents often use a bulb syringe to clear a baby's stuffy nose.
The nurse gently inserted the bulb syringe into the patient's ear to remove excess wax.
