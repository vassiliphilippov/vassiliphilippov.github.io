drown

verb
die by suffocating under water
1.0 1.8
Pronunciation: / draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
to die or be in danger of dying by being submerged in water or another liquid and unable to breathe

Examples

To drown in a lake
If you cannot swim, there is a real risk you might drown in deep water.
Several swimmers tragically drowned after being caught in the sudden storm.
The rescue team arrived just in time to save the child from drowning.
Lifeguards are trained to respond quickly if someone begins to drown in the pool.
She realized the dog would drown if she didn't intervene immediately.
The storm caused several people to drown when their boat capsized.
The lifeguard arrived too late; the teenager was drowning in the deep end of the pool.
If you are not careful while swimming in the ocean, you could drown in a strong current.
She learned how to swim at a young age to avoid the risk of drowning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rescue - to save someone from a dangerous or distressing situation
  • synonym: sink - to go down below the surface of water
  • synonym: suffocate - to die or have serious difficulty breathing due to a lack of air or obstruction
