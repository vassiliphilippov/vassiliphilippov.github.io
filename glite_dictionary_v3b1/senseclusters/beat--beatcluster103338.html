beat

verb
to stir or mix vigorously, especially in cooking
1.0 14/m
Pronunciation: / bit / (AmE), / biÀêt / (BrE)
to mix or stir (a substance, especially food) vigorously using a rapid, repeated movement

Examples

To beat eggs
He beat the batter vigorously to make sure there were no lumps.
She beat the cream into soft peaks for the dessert.
Use a fork to beat the eggs until they are light and fluffy.

Synonyms

  • whip
    2.4 7.83/m
    whip means mixing cream or eggs very fast to add air and make them fluffy, while beat is a general vigorous stirring action.
  • whisk
    2.8 9.51/m
    whisk means moving a wire tool quickly to combine ingredients with light strokes, while beat means stirring more vigorously to mix.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whisk - to beat or stir a substance rapidly with a whisk or similar utensil
