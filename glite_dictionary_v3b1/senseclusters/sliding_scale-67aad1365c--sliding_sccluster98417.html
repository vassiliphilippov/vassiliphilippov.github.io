sliding scale

noun
variable pricing system based on another factor
4.5 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈslaɪ.dɪŋ skeɪl/ (AmE), /ˈslaɪ.dɪŋ skeɪl/ (BrE)
a system in which wages, fees, or prices vary according to changes in another factor, such as the cost-of-living index

Examples

A sliding scale system
A sliding scale can help accommodate people with different financial means.
Many clinics offer services on a sliding scale based on income.
The union negotiated a sliding scale for wages to keep up with rising prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fixed rate - a rate that does not change over time or with external factors
