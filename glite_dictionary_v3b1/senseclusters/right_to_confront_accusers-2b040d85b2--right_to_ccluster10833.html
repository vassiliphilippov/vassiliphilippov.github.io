right to confront accusers

noun
legal right to face and question accusers in court
Pronunciation: / raɪt tə kənˈfrʌnt əˈkjuːzərz / (AmE), / raɪt tə kənˈfrʌnt əˈkjuːzəz / (BrE)
the legal right of a defendant to cross-examine and challenge witnesses testifying against them in a criminal trial, as guaranteed by the Sixth Amendment to the US Constitution

Examples

The right to confront accusers in court
His lawyer insisted that the right to confront accusers is fundamental to a fair trial.
The defendant's right to confront accusers was upheld during the trial.
Under the Constitution, every citizen has a right to confront accusers in a criminal case.

  • synonym: confrontation clause - the clause in the Sixth Amendment guaranteeing the right to confront accusers
