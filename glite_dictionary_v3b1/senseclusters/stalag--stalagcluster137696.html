stalag

noun
WWII POW camp
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑːlɑːɡ / (AmE), / ˈstælæɡ / (BrE)
a German prisoner-of-war camp for non-commissioned soldiers, especially during World War II

Examples

A stalag camp
He was held in a stalag for two years during the war.
Many soldiers tried to escape from the stalag where they were imprisoned.
The film tells the story of life inside a German stalag during World War II.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentration camp - a camp where large numbers of people are detained, often in harsh conditions
  • synonym: prison camp - a place where prisoners of war are kept
