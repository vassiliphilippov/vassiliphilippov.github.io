untouchability

noun
social exclusion by caste
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌtʌtʃəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌtʌtʃəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the practice or state of being socially excluded or ostracized, especially due to caste or social status

Examples

Abolish untouchability
The government passed laws to abolish untouchability and promote equality among all citizens.
Despite legal reforms, untouchability still affects some communities in rural areas.
Activists continue to fight against untouchability and raise awareness about social justice.
