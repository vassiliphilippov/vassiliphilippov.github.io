drumroll

verb
to play rapid drumbeats
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌmˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmˌrəʊl / (BrE)
to play or perform a rapid series of drumbeats, often to create suspense or emphasis

Examples

To drumroll for effect
The drummer drumrolled before the magician revealed his final trick.
As the tension built, someone drumrolled on the table with their fingers.
The band drumrolled as the winner's name was read aloud.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roll - to play a continuous rapid series of drumbeats
