hail

noun
precipitation of ice pellets
Pronunciation: / heɪl / (AmE), / heɪl / (BrE)
Small balls or lumps of ice that fall from the sky, especially during storms, as a form of precipitation.

Examples

Heavy hail storm
Large pieces of hail covered the ground after the thunderstorm.
The sudden hail damaged cars and roofs in the neighborhood.
We were caught in a storm of hail on our way home from school.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: rain - liquid water drops falling from the sky
  • synonym: sleet - precipitation of ice pellets mixed with rain or snow
  • degree lower: snow - soft white flakes of frozen water vapor falling from the sky
