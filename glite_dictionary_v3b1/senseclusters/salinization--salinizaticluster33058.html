salinization

noun
salt buildup in soil
7.4
Pronunciation: /sə.lɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sæl.ɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sə.lɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sæl.ɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which salt builds up in soil or water, often making it less suitable for plants or farming

Examples

Salinization of soil
Excessive irrigation can lead to salinization of farmland, reducing crop yields.
Scientists are studying ways to prevent salinization in arid regions affected by poor drainage.
The salinization of rivers can harm freshwater fish and other aquatic life.
Improper irrigation methods can cause salinization of agricultural land, making it less productive.
Scientists are studying ways to prevent salinization in areas with high evaporation rates.
The salinization of rivers threatens the survival of many freshwater species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desalinization - the process of removing salt from something
  • synonym: salinity - the amount of salt in soil or water
