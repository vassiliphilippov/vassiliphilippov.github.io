ghrelin

noun
appetite-stimulating hormone
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɛlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡrɛlɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced in the stomach that stimulates appetite and the release of growth hormone from the pituitary gland

Examples

Ghrelin hormone levels
After a meal, ghrelin production decreases, making you feel less hungry.
Researchers discovered that ghrelin increases when people fast, leading to greater feelings of hunger.
Some weight loss medications work by lowering the body's ghrelin levels.
