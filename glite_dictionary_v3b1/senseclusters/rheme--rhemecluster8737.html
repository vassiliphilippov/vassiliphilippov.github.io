rheme

noun
predicate term
Pronunciation: / riːm / (AmE), / riːm / (BrE)
a unit of meaning or term that expresses a quality or possible predicate, rather than a complete proposition, especially in logic or semiotics

Examples

Logical rheme
In Peirce's theory, the rheme represents a possible quality rather than a full statement.
A rheme can be thought of as the part of a sentence that adds new information.
Linguists often analyze the rheme to understand how meaning is constructed in communication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predicate - the part of a sentence or proposition that states something about the subject
  • antonym: subject - the part of a sentence or proposition about which something is stated
