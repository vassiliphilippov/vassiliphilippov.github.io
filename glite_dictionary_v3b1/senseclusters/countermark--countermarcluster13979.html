countermark

noun
mark for validation
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəmɑːk / (BrE)
an official or identifying mark stamped on a coin, document, or goods to show validation, reissue, origin, or ownership

Examples

A coin countermark
The rare coin had a countermark added by the new government after the regime change.
Collectors value coins with a clear countermark because it shows historical changes in authority.
The document was made official by applying a special countermark to its surface.
The jeweler's countermark on the ring proved it was made in Paris.
Collectors look for a rare countermark to verify the authenticity of old coins.
Each piece of pottery bore a unique countermark from the original workshop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erase - to remove or eliminate a mark or sign
  • synonym: hallmark - an official mark stamped on items to certify origin or quality
  • synonym: overstamp - a stamp placed over an existing mark to indicate a change
  • synonym: seal - an official mark or stamp on a document
  • synonym: trademark - a symbol or word legally registered to represent a company or product
