metro cattolico

noun
historical unit of length (seconds pendulum)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈme.troʊ kæt.ˈtɔː.lɪ.koʊ/ (AmE), /ˈme.trəʊ kæt.ˈtɒ.lɪ.kəʊ/ (BrE)
a historical unit of length, defined as the length of a free seconds pendulum and differing from the modern metre by about half a centimetre, introduced by Tito Livio Burattini in 1675.

Examples

The metro cattolico unit
Historians of measurement sometimes refer to the metro cattolico when discussing early attempts at standardization.
In his 1675 proposal, Burattini described the metro cattolico as based on the seconds pendulum.
The metro cattolico was nearly, but not exactly, the same length as the contemporary metre.
