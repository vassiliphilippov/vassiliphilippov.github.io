endoergic reaction

noun
nuclear reaction absorbing energy
9.9
Pronunciation: /en.doʊ.ˈɜːr.dʒɪk riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /en.doʊ.ˈɜːr.dʒɪk rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /en.də.ˈɜː.dʒɪk riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /en.də.ˈɜː.dʒɪk rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
A nuclear reaction in which energy is absorbed from the surroundings.

Examples

An endoergic reaction absorbs energy
An endoergic reaction requires an input of energy to proceed, unlike most spontaneous nuclear changes.
Energy must be supplied for an endoergic reaction to occur within the atomic nucleus.
Scientists observed that the endoergic reaction absorbed more energy than it released.
