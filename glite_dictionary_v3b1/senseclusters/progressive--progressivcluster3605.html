progressive

adjective
favouring reform or progress
3.2
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
favoring or promoting reform, progress, or new, modern ideas, especially in politics, society, or education

Examples

Progressive ideas
Many voters support progressive policies that aim to reduce inequality.
She is known for her progressive views on education and social welfare.
The senator introduced a progressive bill designed to address climate change.
Many voters prefer progressive candidates who advocate for social change.
She teaches at a progressive school that encourages creative thinking.
The university is known for its progressive policies on diversity and inclusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - opposed to rapid change or innovation; holding traditional values
  • synonym: liberal - open to new behaviour or opinions and willing to discard traditional values
