renumber

verb
to assign new numbers
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈnʌmbər /; / riˈnʌmbər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈnʌmbə /; / riˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
to assign new numbers to items, pages, or sections, usually after changes or additions

Examples

To renumber pages
After adding several new entries, the librarian had to renumber the entire catalog.
The teacher asked the students to renumber their answers after rearranging the questions.
When the document was updated, the software automatically renumbered all the pages.
After adding a new section, the editor had to renumber all the pages in the document.
The houses on the street were renumbered to make addresses easier to find.
If you insert a new row, the spreadsheet will automatically renumber the list.
After adding new sections, the author had to renumber all the pages in the document.
The city decided to renumber the houses on the street for better organization.
If you insert a new item, remember to renumber the entire list to keep it in order.

  • synonym: number - to assign numbers to items in a sequence
  • synonym: relabel - to assign new labels or names to something
