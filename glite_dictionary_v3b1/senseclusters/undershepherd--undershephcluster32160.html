undershepherd

noun
assistant church leader
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌʃɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌʃɛpəd / (BrE)
a pastor or church leader who serves under the authority of a higher-ranking leader, such as a senior pastor or bishop

Examples

Church undershepherd
The undershepherd led the congregation in the absence of the senior pastor.
As an undershepherd, she supported the church's main leader and organized community events.
The responsibilities of an undershepherd include caring for members and assisting with services.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, usually in charge of a diocese
  • synonym: pastor - a minister in charge of a Christian church or congregation
