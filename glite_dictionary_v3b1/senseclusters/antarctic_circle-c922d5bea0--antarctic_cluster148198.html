Antarctic Circle

noun
southern polar latitude line
4.6
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈtɑːrk.tɪk ˈsɜːr.kəl/ /; / /æn.ˈtɑːr.tɪk ˈsɜːr.kəl/ (AmE), /æn.ˈtɑːk.tɪk ˈsɜː.kəl/ (BrE)
an imaginary line of latitude at approximately 66°33′ south of the Equator, marking the northern boundary of the Earth's southern polar region.

Examples

The Antarctic Circle line
Explorers often aim to cross the Antarctic Circle on their journeys to the South Pole.
In summer, areas south of the Antarctic Circle experience continuous daylight known as the midnight sun.
The Antarctic Circle passes through several remote regions of Antarctica.
