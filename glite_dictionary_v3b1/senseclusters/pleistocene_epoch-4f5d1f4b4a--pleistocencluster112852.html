Pleistocene epoch

noun
geological epoch with ice ages

The geological epoch from about two million to eleven thousand years ago, marked by extensive glaciations and the evolution of modern humans.

Examples

The Pleistocene epoch ice age
During the Pleistocene epoch, vast ice sheets covered much of the Northern Hemisphere.
Early humans first appeared and evolved during the Pleistocene epoch.
Many large mammals, like mammoths, thrived and went extinct in the Pleistocene epoch.

Synonyms
