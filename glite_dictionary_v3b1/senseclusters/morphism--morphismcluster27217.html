morphism

noun
structure-preserving map
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrˌfɪzəm /; / ˈmɔrˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
a structure-preserving map or function between two mathematical objects, especially in category theory

Examples

A morphism in category theory
In category theory, a morphism connects two objects by preserving their structure.
Every group homomorphism is a type of morphism in abstract algebra.
The professor explained how a morphism can reveal similarities between different mathematical systems.
In category theory, a morphism represents a generalization of the concept of a function.
Every group homomorphism is a type of morphism that preserves group structure.
The diagram commutes if the composition of morphisms along different paths gives the same result.

Related Terms

  • synonym: function - a relation that assigns each input exactly one output
  • synonym: homomorphism - a map between algebraic structures that preserves operations
  • synonym: isomorphism - a morphism that has an inverse, showing two structures are essentially the same
