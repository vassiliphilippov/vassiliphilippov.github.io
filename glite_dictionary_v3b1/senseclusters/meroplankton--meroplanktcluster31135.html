meroplankton

noun
temporary plankton stage
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛrəˈplæŋktən / (AmE), / ˌmɛərəˈplæŋktən / (BrE)
aquatic organisms that live as plankton for only part of their life cycle, usually in their larval stage

Examples

Meroplankton larval stage
Many marine animals begin life as meroplankton before settling on the ocean floor.
The study focused on the seasonal abundance of meroplankton in coastal waters.
Some fish species are classified as meroplankton during their early developmental stages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holoplankton - organisms that spend their entire life cycle as plankton
