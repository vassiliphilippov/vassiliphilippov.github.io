Artiodactyla

noun
order of even-toed hooved mammals
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːr.ti.oʊˈdæk.tɪ.lə / (AmE), / ˌɑː.ti.əʊˈdæk.tɪ.lə / (BrE)
an order of hooved mammals with an even number of functional toes, including pigs, deer, cattle, and camels

Examples

The Artiodactyla order
Biology students studied the skeletal structure unique to the Artiodactyla.
Paleontologists discovered fossils belonging to the Artiodactyla in the region.
The Artiodactyla include animals such as deer, giraffes, and cows.
