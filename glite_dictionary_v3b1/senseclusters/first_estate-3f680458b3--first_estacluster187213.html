first estate

noun
the clergy as a social class in France and Britain
Pronunciation: / ˌfɝst ɪˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ˌfɜːst ɪˈsteɪt / (BrE)
the clergy, considered as the first of the traditional three orders of society in pre-revolutionary France and sometimes Britain

Examples

The first estate clergy
During the Ancien Régime, the first estate held immense political and social power in France.
In Britain, the first estate includes senior members of the established church.
Many conflicts arose between the first estate and the commoners leading up to the French Revolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: second estate - the nobility as the second main social class in pre-revolutionary France
  • synonym: third estate - the commoners as the third main social class in pre-revolutionary France
