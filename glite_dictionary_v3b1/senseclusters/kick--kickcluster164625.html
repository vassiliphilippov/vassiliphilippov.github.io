kick

verb
score a goal by striking with the foot
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /kɪk/ (AmE), /kɪk/ (BrE)
to strike, move, or thrust with the foot or leg (including in sports, exercise, or swimming)

Examples

To kick a ball
He kicked an extra point after his team scored a touchdown.
She confidently kicked the ball into the net during the penalty shootout.
The striker kicked the winning goal in the final minute of the match.
He tried to kick the ball into the net but missed.
She kicked the door in frustration when it wouldn't open.
The horse started kicking wildly after being startled.
He began to kick violently when he was startled by the noise.
She tried to kick her way free from the tangled blanket.
The horse may kick if it feels threatened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: punch - to strike with the fist
  • synonym: punt - to kick a ball dropped from the hands before it touches the ground
  • synonym: score - to gain a point or goal in a game or sport
  • synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
  • antonym: throw - to propel something with force through the air by a movement of the arm and hand
