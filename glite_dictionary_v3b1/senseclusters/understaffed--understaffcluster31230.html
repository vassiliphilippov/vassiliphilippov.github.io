understaffed

adjective
having too few staff
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈstæft / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstɑːft / (BrE)
Not having enough staff or workers to do the work required.

Examples

An understaffed office
Due to budget cuts, the school remained understaffed for the entire year.
Hospitals often become understaffed during flu season when many workers fall ill.
The restaurant was understaffed and service was slower than usual.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overstaffed - having more staff than needed
  • synonym: short-handed - not having enough people to do a job
