understaffed

adjective

Not having enough staff or workers to do the work required.

Examples

An understaffed office
Due to budget cuts, the school remained understaffed for the entire year.
Hospitals often become understaffed during flu season when many workers fall ill.
The restaurant was understaffed and service was slower than usual.

Synonyms

  • short-staffed
    3.9 0.19/m
    short-staffed describes having fewer workers on a team right now, while understaffed is a general term for lacking enough staff.
  • undermanned
    5.6 0.08/m
    undermanned often describes physical teams like crews or police, while understaffed covers any workplace that lacks enough staff.
  • short-handed
    4.4 0.22/m
    short-handed is used when a group has too few helpers for a task, while understaffed is the broader staffing problem.
  • understrength
    5.5 0.04/m
    understrength often refers to units lacking people or resources for full strength, while understaffed focuses specifically on not enough staff.
