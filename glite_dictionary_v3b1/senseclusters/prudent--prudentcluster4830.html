prudent

adjective
careful and sensible; wise in judgment
6.2 7.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpruÀêdnt / (AmE), / ÀàpruÀêdnt / (BrE)
acting with or showing care and thought for the future; wise and sensible in making decisions or taking action

Examples

A prudent investor
A prudent investor diversifies their portfolio to minimize risk.
Her prudent decision-making helped the company avoid financial trouble.
It is always prudent to save some money for unexpected expenses.

Synonyms

    8.0 0.00/m

  • foresightful
    8.1 0.03/m

  • farsighted
    4.6 0.28/m
    farsighted plans long term and sees future trends, while prudent often stresses careful, sensible choices about present actions.
  • sensible
    2.7 14/m
    sensible acts in a practical and reasonable way, while prudent adds a stronger sense of planning and thought for future risks.
  • circumspect
    7.9 1.18/m
    circumspect thinks about risks and avoids hasty acts, while prudent also includes wise planning and sensible long-term decisions.
  • cautious
    2.9 14/m

  • sagacious
    8.6 0.20/m
    sagacious shows deep wisdom and shrewd thought, while prudent is more about careful, sensible decision-making for future safety.
