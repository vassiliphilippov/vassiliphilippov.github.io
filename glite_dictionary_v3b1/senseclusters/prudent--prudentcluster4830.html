prudent

adjective
careful and sensible; wise in judgment
Pronunciation: / ˈpruːdnt / (AmE), / ˈpruːdnt / (BrE)
acting with or showing care and thought for the future; wise and sensible in making decisions or taking action

Examples

A prudent investor
A prudent investor diversifies their portfolio to minimize risk.
Her prudent decision-making helped the company avoid financial trouble.
It is always prudent to save some money for unexpected expenses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment; unwise
  • synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
