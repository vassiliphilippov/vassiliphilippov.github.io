plunk down

verb
set down heavily or noisily
8.6
Pronunciation: / plʌŋk daʊn / (AmE), / plʌŋk daʊn / (BrE)
to set something or oneself down heavily or abruptly, often with a noise

Examples

To plunk down heavily
He plunked down on the couch and sighed in relief after a long day.
She plunked down her suitcase by the door with a loud thud.
Without hesitation, I plunked down the cash to buy the antique mirror.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plonk down - to put something down heavily or suddenly
