ecclesia

noun
ancient Greek assembly
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkliːziə / (AmE), / ɪˈkliːzɪə / (BrE)
the principal assembly of citizens in some ancient Greek city-states, especially Athens, for making decisions and voting on public matters

Examples

Athenian ecclesia
The ecclesia played a crucial role in the democratic process of ancient Athens.
Citizens gathered at the ecclesia to debate and vote on important laws.
Decisions made by the ecclesia affected every aspect of public life in the city-state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assembly - a group of people gathered for a common purpose
  • antonym: senate - a legislative or governing body, especially in ancient Rome
