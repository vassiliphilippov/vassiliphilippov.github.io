inchoately

adverb
in a confused manner
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkoʊətli / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊətli / (BrE)
in a way that is not fully developed, clear, or organized; incomplete or just beginning

Examples

To speak inchoately
The plan was presented inchoately, making it difficult for the team to understand the main points.
He spoke inchoately about his ideas, leaving the audience confused.
Their arguments were inchoately expressed, lacking any clear structure or direction.
The committee's ideas were inchoately expressed and lacked clear direction.
He spoke inchoately about his future plans, unsure of the details.
The movement was inchoately organized, with no clear leadership or structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaotically - in a completely disordered or confused way
  • antonym: coherently - in a logical and organized way
  • antonym: fully - completely or entirely
  • synonym: partially - to some extent but not completely
