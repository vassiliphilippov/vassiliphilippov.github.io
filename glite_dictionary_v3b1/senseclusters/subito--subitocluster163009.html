subito

adverb
suddenly in music
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːbɪtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsuːbɪtəʊ / (BrE)
in music, used as a direction to indicate that something should happen suddenly or immediately

Examples

Subito piano
The conductor signaled for the orchestra to play subito piano, creating a dramatic effect.
In the score, the composer wrote subito forte to surprise the audience with a sudden loud passage.
The violinist executed the subito change in tempo perfectly, catching everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradually - slowly over time, not suddenly
  • synonym: immediately - without delay; at once
