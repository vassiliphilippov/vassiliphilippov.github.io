precative

adjective
expressing entreaty or supplication
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛkətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpriːkətɪv / (BrE)
expressing entreaty or supplication; relating to a request or earnest appeal

Examples

A precative tone
Her tone became precative as she pleaded for understanding.
In liturgical language, a precative mood is used when making prayers or petitions.
The ancient text contains several precative formulas intended to invoke divine favor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: precatory - expressing a wish or request rather than a command
