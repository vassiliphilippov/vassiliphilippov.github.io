infix

verb
to insert or introduce into
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnˈfɪks / (AmE), / ˌɪnˈfɪks / (BrE)
to insert or introduce something into or between other things, especially into the middle of something

Examples

To infix an idea
He tried to infix a sense of hope into the conversation.
In some languages, you can infix particles into root words.
The teacher asked the students to infix the quotation into their essays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embed - to fix or set firmly in a surrounding mass
  • synonym: insert - to place or fit something into another thing
