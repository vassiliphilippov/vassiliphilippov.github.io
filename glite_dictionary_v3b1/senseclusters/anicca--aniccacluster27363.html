anicca

noun
impermanence in Buddhism
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈnɪtʃə / (AmE), / əˈnɪtʃə / (BrE)
the Buddhist concept that all things are impermanent and constantly changing

Examples

The anicca principle
Understanding anicca is central to many Buddhist meditation practices.
The teacher explained that anicca reminds us nothing in life stays the same forever.
Reflecting on anicca can help people cope with loss and change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impermanence - the state of not lasting forever; transience
  • antonym: permanence - the state of lasting or remaining unchanged
