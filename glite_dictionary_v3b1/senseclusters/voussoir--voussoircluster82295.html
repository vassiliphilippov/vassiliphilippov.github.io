voussoir

noun
wedge-shaped block in arches
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / vuˈswɑr / (AmE), / vuˈswwɑː / (BrE)
a wedge-shaped stone or block used to construct an arch or vault

Examples

A voussoir stone
Medieval architects perfected the use of the voussoir in gothic cathedrals.
The mason carefully placed each voussoir to ensure the stability of the archway.
Without a properly cut voussoir, the structure of the stone arch could fail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keystone - the central, topmost stone of an arch, locking the other voussoirs into place
