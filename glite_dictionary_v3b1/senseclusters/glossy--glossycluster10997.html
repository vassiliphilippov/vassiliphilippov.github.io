glossy

adjective
attractive and stylish in a superficial way
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɑːsi / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒsi / (BrE)
superficially attractive or impressive, often suggesting style, wealth, or being deceptive in appearance

Examples

Glossy lifestyle show
She preferred glossy romantic comedies, even though they sometimes felt unrealistic.
The magazine is filled with glossy advertisements featuring glamorous celebrities and luxury brands.
The new restaurant has a glossy interior, but the food was actually rather disappointing.
Beneath its glossy exterior, the campaign lacked substance and real solutions.
She warned us not to be fooled by his glossy charm and practiced smile.
The politician's glossy promises quickly crumbled under closer examination.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gaudy - so showy and flashy as to be tasteless; excessively bright or ornate
  • antonym: genuine - real, sincere, or authentic, not deceptive in appearance
  • synonym: meretricious - superficially attractive but having no real value; insincere or flashy
  • antonym: plain - simple and not elaborate or showy
  • synonym: showy - attracting attention by being bright or extravagant, but often lacking true worth
