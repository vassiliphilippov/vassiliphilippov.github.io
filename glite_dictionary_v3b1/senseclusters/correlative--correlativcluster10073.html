correlative

noun
one of two related or complementary things
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛləˌtɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrɛlətɪv / (BrE)
either of two or more things that are closely related, often in a complementary or corresponding way

Examples

A correlative pair
In the pair 'either...or,' each word is a correlative of the other.
Rights and responsibilities are often regarded as legal correlatives.
The concept of cause has its correlative in the concept of effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complement - something that completes or goes well with another
  • antonym: opposite - something entirely different or contrary
