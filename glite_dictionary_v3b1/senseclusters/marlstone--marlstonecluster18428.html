marlstone

noun
hard sedimentary rock like marl
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrlˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈmɑːlˌstəʊn / (BrE)
A type of sedimentary rock containing a mixture of clay and calcium carbonate, similar in composition to marl but harder.

Examples

A marlstone rock
Geologists took samples of the marlstone to analyze its mineral content.
Layers of marlstone in the valley reveal evidence of ancient lakes.
The cliffs along the coast are made of marlstone, giving them a distinctive pale color.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marl - loose earthy deposit of clay and calcium carbonate
