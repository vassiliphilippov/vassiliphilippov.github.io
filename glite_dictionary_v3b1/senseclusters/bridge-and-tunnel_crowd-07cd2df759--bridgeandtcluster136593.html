bridge-and-tunnel crowd

noun
suburban commuters to urban areas (often pejorative)
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɪdʒ ən ˈtʌnəl kraʊd / (AmE), / ˌbrɪdʒ ənd ˈtʌn.əl kraʊd / (BrE)
People who commute from suburban or outlying areas to an urban center, especially during leisure times, often with an implied characterization of a different or less sophisticated lifestyle.

Examples

The bridge-and-tunnel crowd arrives
After midnight, the bridge-and-tunnel crowd fills the clubs downtown.
She tried to avoid the popular bars frequented by the bridge-and-tunnel crowd.
Some locals complain that the bridge-and-tunnel crowd makes the city more crowded on weekends.
