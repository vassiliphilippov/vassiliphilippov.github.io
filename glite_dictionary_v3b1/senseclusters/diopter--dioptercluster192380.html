diopter

noun
unit of lens optical power
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɑptər /; / daɪˈɒptɚ / (AmE), / daɪˈɒptə / (BrE)
a unit of measurement of the optical power of a lens, equal to the reciprocal of its focal length in meters

Examples

One diopter lens
A lens with a focal length of one meter has an optical power of one diopter.
He adjusted the camera’s viewfinder by a fraction of a diopter to sharpen the image.
The optometrist prescribed glasses with a strength of +2 diopters for reading.
A lens with a focal length of one meter has a refractive power of one dioptre.
My new glasses have lenses with a strength of minus two dioptres each.
Ophthalmologists measure the curvature of the cornea in dioptres.

  • synonym: diopter - alternative spelling (especially American English) for 'dioptre'
