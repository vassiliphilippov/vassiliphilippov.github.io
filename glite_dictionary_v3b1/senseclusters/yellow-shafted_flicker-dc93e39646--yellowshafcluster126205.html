yellow-shafted flicker

noun
North American woodpecker with yellow underwings and tail
7.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌjɛloʊ ˈʃæftɪd ˈflɪkər / (AmE), / ˌjɛləʊ ˈʃɑːftɪd ˈflɪkə / (BrE)
a large woodpecker native to eastern North America, with a red patch on the neck and yellow coloring on the undersides of its wings and tail

Examples

A yellow-shafted flicker bird

The yellow-shafted flicker is often seen feeding on the ground for ants and beetles.
Unlike other woodpeckers, the yellow-shafted flicker frequently forages on lawns and open fields.
