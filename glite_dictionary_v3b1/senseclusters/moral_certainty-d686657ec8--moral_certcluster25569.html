moral certainty

noun
conviction based on moral belief
6.7 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔːr.əl ˈsɜːr.tən.tiː/ /; / /ˈmoʊr.əl ˈsɜːr.tən.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈmɒr.əl ˈsɜː.tən.tiː/ /; / /ˈmɔːr.əl ˈsɜː.tən.tiː/ (BrE)
A conviction or level of certainty that is based on one's moral beliefs or sense, rather than absolute proof.

Examples

A moral certainty feeling
Despite the lack of direct evidence, the jury reached a moral certainty about the defendant's guilt.
In matters of ethics, moral certainty often drives people's actions.
She spoke with such moral certainty that it was hard to question her decision.
