prejudice

noun
unreasonable or unfair bias
5.4 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdʒədɪs / (AmE), / ˈprɛdʒʊdɪs / (BrE)
An unreasonable or unfair opinion or feeling, especially when formed without enough thought or knowledge.

Examples

Prejudice against people
His prejudice against new technology prevented him from embracing innovation at work.
Laws exist to protect people from racial prejudice in the workplace.
She tried to overcome her own prejudice and see the situation more objectively.
