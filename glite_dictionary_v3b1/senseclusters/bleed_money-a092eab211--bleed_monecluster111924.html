bleed money

verb
lose large amounts of money rapidly
Pronunciation: / bliːd ˈmʌni / (AmE), / bliːd ˈmʌni / (BrE)
to lose large amounts of money rapidly, especially over a period of time

Examples

To bleed money fast
During the recession, many restaurants began to bleed money and were forced to close.
If we don't cut unnecessary expenses, the company will bleed money all year.
The project is bleeding money faster than we anticipated, so we need to reconsider our approach.

Related Terms
