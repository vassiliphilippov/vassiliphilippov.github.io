arthrogryposis

noun
congenital joint stiffness
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrθroʊɡrɪˈpoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɑːθrəʊɡrɪˈpəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a rare condition present at birth that causes joint stiffness and limited movement in multiple joints

Examples

Arthrogryposis joint stiffness
Children born with arthrogryposis often require physical therapy to improve their mobility.
The specialist diagnosed the newborn with arthrogryposis after noticing stiffness in several joints.
Families affected by arthrogryposis can benefit from support groups and medical guidance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contracture - a permanent tightening of muscles, tendons, or skin that restricts movement
  • antonym: hypermobility - a condition where joints move beyond the normal range
