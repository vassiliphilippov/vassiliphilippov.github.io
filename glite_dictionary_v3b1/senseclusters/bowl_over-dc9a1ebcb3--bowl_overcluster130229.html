bowl over

verb
to astonish or greatly impress
Pronunciation: / boʊl ˈoʊvɚ /; / boʊl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / bəʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to greatly impress or astonish someone

Examples

To bowl over the crowd
Her incredible singing voice bowls over everyone who hears it.
I was completely bowled over by the generosity of our hosts.
The director's new film really bowled over the critics.
His generosity bowled over everyone at the fundraiser.
I was bowled over by the breathtaking view from the mountain top.
The news of her promotion completely bowled over her colleagues.
His generosity always seems to bowl over those around him.
I was completely bowled over by her performance last night.
The magician's tricks will bowl over any audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disappoint - to fail to fulfill expectations; to let down
  • synonym: stun - to shock or overwhelm someone, especially with surprise
  • antonym: underwhelm - to fail to impress or excite someone
