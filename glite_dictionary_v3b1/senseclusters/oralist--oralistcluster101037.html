oralist

noun
public speaker
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who speaks or performs verbally in public, such as a public speaker or reciter

Examples

Famous oralist speaks
The renowned oralist delivered a moving speech at the graduation ceremony.
As an accomplished oralist, she often performs dramatic readings at literary events.
The competition awarded the best oralist for their clarity and expression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lecturer - a person who gives lectures, especially as a profession
  • antonym: listener - a person who listens to a speaker
  • synonym: orator - a skilled public speaker
