fundamental principle

noun
basic foundational rule or truth
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˌfʌndəˈmɛntəl ˈprɪnsəpəl / (BrE)
A basic rule or truth that serves as the foundation for a system of beliefs or reasoning, from which other concepts or truths can be derived.

Examples

A fundamental principle of honesty
Honesty is considered a fundamental principle in building trust within organizations.
The laws of physics are based on a few fundamental principles that explain natural phenomena.
Understanding supply and demand is a fundamental principle in economics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axiom - a statement or proposition regarded as established or self-evidently true
  • synonym: basic principle - an essential or elementary rule or assumption
