synodic

adjective
relating to celestial cycles
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɑdɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈnɒdɪk / (BrE)
relating to the period between successive similar phases of a celestial body, such as the time between two new moons

Examples

Synodic month period
The synodic month is the time between two new moons.
Astronomers use the synodic period to track the cycles of planets as seen from Earth.
The difference between the synodic and sidereal periods is important in lunar studies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sidereal - relating to the period measured by the stars rather than phases
