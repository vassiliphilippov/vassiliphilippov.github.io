bilevel

adjective
two intensity levels
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
having only two possible values or intensity levels, such as black and white in images or signals

Examples

Bilevel image
The old printer could only produce bilevel images, so everything was either black or white.
A bilevel signal is used in some electronic devices to represent on and off states.
When scanning documents, the bilevel mode helps reduce file size by using just two colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binary - having two possible states or values
  • antonym: multilevel - having more than two possible values or intensity levels
