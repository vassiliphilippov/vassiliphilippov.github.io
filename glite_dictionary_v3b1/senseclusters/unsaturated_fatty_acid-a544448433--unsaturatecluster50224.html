unsaturated fatty acid

noun
a fat molecule with double or triple bonds
Pronunciation: / ʌnˌsætʃəˌreɪtɪd ˈfæti ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˌsætʃəˌreɪtɪd ˈfæti ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a fatty acid whose carbon chain contains one or more double or triple bonds and can absorb more hydrogen atoms

Examples

Plant oil unsaturated fatty acids
Many plant-based oils contain a high proportion of unsaturated fatty acids.
Olive oil is rich in unsaturated fatty acids, which are considered healthier for your heart.
The nutritionist recommended reducing intake of saturated fats and increasing unsaturated fatty acid consumption.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monounsaturated fatty acid - a fatty acid with exactly one double bond
  • degree higher: polyunsaturated fatty acid - a fatty acid with more than one double bond
  • antonym: saturated fatty acid - a fatty acid with no double or triple bonds
