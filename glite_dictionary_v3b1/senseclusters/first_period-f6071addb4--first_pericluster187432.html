first period

noun
initial segment of a game/match
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the initial division of playing time in a game or match that is split into periods, such as in hockey or basketball.

Examples

The first period of game
After a rough first period, the coach decided to make some changes.
He was penalized halfway through the first period for tripping.
The team scored twice in the first period to take an early lead.
