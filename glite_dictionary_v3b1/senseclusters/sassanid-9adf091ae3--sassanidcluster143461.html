Sassanid

proper noun
ancient Persian empire
Pronunciation: / ˈsæsənɪd / (AmE), / ˈsæsənɪd / (BrE)
the dynasty or empire that ruled Persia from 224 to 651 AD, known for its cultural and political achievements

Examples

The Sassanid Empire
The Sassanid Empire was renowned for its advances in art and architecture.
Historians often compare the Sassanid and Roman empires for their influence on world history.
Many ancient texts from the Sassanid period have survived to the present day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Achaemenid - an earlier Persian empire before the Sassanids
  • antonym: Parthian - the dynasty that ruled Persia before the Sassanids
