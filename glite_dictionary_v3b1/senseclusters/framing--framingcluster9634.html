framing

noun
formulating plans or important details
4.0 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfreɪ.mɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈfreɪ.mɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act or process of structuring, presenting, or organizing plans, arguments, or information, especially in a way that influences how people understand or respond to them

Examples

The framing of arguments
Lawyers carefully considered the framing of their arguments before the trial.
The framing of the new policy took several months of careful planning.
The success of the proposal depended largely on the precise framing of its objectives.
The newspaper's framing of the protest emphasized violence rather than peaceful demands, shaping public opinion quickly.
In class we discussed how politicians use framing to focus attention on some facts while ignoring others.
The research showed that different framing of choices produced large changes in people's risk preferences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drafting - writing or preparing a preliminary version
  • synonym: framing effect - a cognitive bias where people's decisions or judgments change depending on how information is presented
  • antonym: objectivity - presentation of information without bias or deliberate influence; opposite of persuasive framing
  • synonym: spin - an interpretation or presentation of information intended to influence opinion, often by emphasizing particular aspects
