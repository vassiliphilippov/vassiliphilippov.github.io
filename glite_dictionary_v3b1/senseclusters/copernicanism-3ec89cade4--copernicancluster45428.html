Copernicanism

noun
heliocentric theory
9.3
Pronunciation: /kɑː.pər.ˈnɪk.ən.ɪz.əm/ /; / /kɑː.pər.ˈnɪk.ə.nɪz.əm/ (AmE), /kɒp.ə.ˈnɪk.ən.ɪz.əm/ /; / /kɒp.ə.ˈnɪk.ə.nɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the astronomical theory that the sun, rather than the earth, is at the center of the solar system

Examples

Copernicanism in astronomy
Copernicanism challenged the long-held belief that the earth was the center of the universe.
The acceptance of Copernicanism marked a turning point in the history of science.
Many early astronomers resisted Copernicanism because it contradicted traditional teachings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: geocentrism - the belief that the earth is at the center of the universe
  • synonym: heliocentrism - the belief that the sun is at the center of the solar system
