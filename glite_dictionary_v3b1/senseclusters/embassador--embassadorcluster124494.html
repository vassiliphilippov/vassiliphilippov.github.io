embassador

noun
archaic spelling of ambassador
2.8
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbæsədər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbæsədə / (BrE)
(archaic) A diplomat of the highest rank, sent as an official representative from one country to another.

Examples

An embassador from a country
In Shakespeare's plays, the term embassador is often used for an envoy or delegate.
Several foreign embassadors attended the royal banquet in the old city.
The king dispatched his trusted embassador to negotiate a peace treaty.
