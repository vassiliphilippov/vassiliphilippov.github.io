hemorrhoidal vein

noun
vein in the anal canal and rectum
Pronunciation: / hɛməˌrɔɪdəl veɪn / (AmE), / hɛm.əˌrɔɪ.dl veɪn / (BrE)
any of several veins that drain blood from the walls of the anal canal and rectum

Examples

Ruptured hemorrhoidal vein
Bleeding from a ruptured hemorrhoidal vein is a common cause of rectal bleeding.
Doctors may examine the hemorrhoidal vein during a colonoscopy to assess for abnormalities.
The hemorrhoidal vein can become enlarged, leading to the development of hemorrhoids.
