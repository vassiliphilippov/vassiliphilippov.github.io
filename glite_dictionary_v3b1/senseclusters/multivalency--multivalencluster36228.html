multivalency

noun
having a valence greater than two (chemistry)
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈveɪlənsi / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈveɪlənsi / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The state or property of having a valence greater than two, especially in atoms or ions able to form more than two chemical bonds.

Examples

Multivalency in chemistry
Due to its multivalency, this element plays a crucial role in organic reactions.
Researchers are studying the multivalency of certain ions to develop new catalysts.
The multivalency of the compound allows it to form several bonds with other molecules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monovalency - the state of having a valence of one
