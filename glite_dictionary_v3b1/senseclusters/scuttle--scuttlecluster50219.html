scuttle

noun
hatchway in a ship or building
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈskʌt.əl / (BrE)
an entrance in a ship or building fitted with a hatch, especially a covered opening in a ship's deck providing access between decks

Examples

Ship scuttle hatch
Emergency drills require quick access to the lifeboats via the nearest scuttle.
Heavy rain forced them to close every scuttle on the lower deck.
The sailor climbed up through the scuttle to reach the deck above.
