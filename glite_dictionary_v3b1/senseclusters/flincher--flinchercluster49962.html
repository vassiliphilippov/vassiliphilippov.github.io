flincher

noun
person who flinches
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪntʃər / (AmE), / ˈflɪntʃə / (BrE)
a person who flinches or draws back from something, especially from fear or discomfort

Examples

A flincher in fear
He was no flincher when it came to facing danger.
Unlike his friends, Mark proved he was not a flincher during the scary movie.
The coach admired players who were never a flincher in tough situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brave - showing courage and not flinching
  • synonym: coward - a person who lacks courage
