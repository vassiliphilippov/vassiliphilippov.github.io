harasser

noun
a person who persistently torments or attacks others
5.6
Pronunciation: / həˈɹæsɚ /; / həˈræsər / (AmE), / həˈræsə /; / həˈrɑːsə / (BrE)
a person who repeatedly annoys, torments, or threatens others, often in a hostile or aggressive way

Examples

A harasser of workers
He was labeled a harasser after repeatedly sending unwanted messages to his colleagues.
Online platforms are taking steps to remove persistent harassers from their communities.
The company implemented new policies to protect employees from a harasser in the workplace.
A workplace harasser can create a hostile environment for everyone involved.
After months of receiving threatening emails, she reported her harasser to the authorities.
Many companies have policies in place to address complaints against a harasser.
