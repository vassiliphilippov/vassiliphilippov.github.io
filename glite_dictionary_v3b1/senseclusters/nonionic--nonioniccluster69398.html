nonionic

adjective
not converted into ions
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.aɪ.ˈoʊ.nɪk/ (AmE), /nɒn.aɪ.ˈəʊ.nɪk/ (BrE)
not forming or dissociating into ions in solution; not ionic

Examples

Nonionic surfactant
A nonionic surfactant does not increase the conductivity of water.
Many laboratory reagents are nonionic to prevent unwanted chemical reactions.
The detergent is nonionic, so it is gentle on sensitive skin.
Many detergents are classified as nonionic because they do not form ions in solution.
Unlike sodium chloride, glucose is a nonionic compound.
Using nonionic surfactants can reduce skin irritation in cosmetic products.
