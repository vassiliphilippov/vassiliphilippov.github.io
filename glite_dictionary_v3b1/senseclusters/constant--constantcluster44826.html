constant

adjective
continuing without pause; unceasing
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
continuing without interruption or pause for a long time; unceasing

Examples

Constant noise
He lived in constant pain after the accident, rarely finding any relief.
The constant noise from the construction site made it hard to concentrate.
Their friendship was a constant source of comfort throughout the years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceaseless - without stopping or ending; unceasing
  • synonym: incessant - (of something regarded as unpleasant) continuing without pause or interruption
  • antonym: intermittent - occurring at irregular intervals; not continuous or steady
