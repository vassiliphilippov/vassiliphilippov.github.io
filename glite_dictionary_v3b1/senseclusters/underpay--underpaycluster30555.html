underpay

verb
to pay less than deserved or due
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈpeɪ/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈpeɪ/ (BrE)
to pay someone less than they deserve or less than the amount due for their work or services

Examples

To underpay workers
If you underpay your taxes, you may face penalties from the government.
Many teachers feel they are underpaid for the important work they do.
They realized they underpay their employees compared to the industry standard.
