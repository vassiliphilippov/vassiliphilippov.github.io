profoundly

adverb
to a great or intense degree; deeply
Pronunciation: / prəˈfaʊndli / (AmE), / prəˈfaʊndli / (BrE)
to a very great or intense degree; deeply, especially in a psychological or emotional sense

Examples

To profoundly affect
His work has profoundly influenced modern philosophy.
She was profoundly grateful for the opportunities she received.
The news profoundly affected everyone in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deeply - to a great depth or extent; intensely
  • antonym: lightly - to a small degree or not deeply; not intensely
