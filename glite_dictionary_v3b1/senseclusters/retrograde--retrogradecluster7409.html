retrograde

adjective
moving in the opposite direction, especially in astronomy
9.9 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈre.trə.greɪd/ /; / /ˈre.troʊ.greɪd/ (AmE), /ˈre.trə.greɪd/ /; / /ˈre.trəʊ.greɡreɪd/ (BrE)
(of motion or orbit) moving in the opposite direction to that considered normal, especially from east to west on the celestial sphere or, for planets, in a direction opposite to Earth's.

Examples

Retrograde motion
During retrograde periods, astrologers often associate the apparent backward movement of planets with delays or confusion.
Mars appears to be in retrograde motion when it seems to move backward across the sky.
Some moons have retrograde orbits around their planets, moving in the opposite direction to the planet's rotation.

Related Terms
