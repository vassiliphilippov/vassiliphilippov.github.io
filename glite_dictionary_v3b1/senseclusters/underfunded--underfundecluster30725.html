underfunded

adjective
lacking sufficient funds
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈfʌndɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈfʌndɪd / (BrE)
not having enough money or financial resources to operate properly or meet needs

Examples

An underfunded school
Many public schools remain underfunded, struggling to provide basic supplies for students.
The research project was underfunded and could not continue after the first year.
Nonprofit organizations are often underfunded despite the important work they do.
The hospital remained underfunded despite the community's efforts to raise money.
Many important research projects are underfunded and cannot continue their work.
Teachers in underfunded schools often buy supplies with their own money.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts owed
  • synonym: unfunded - not provided with financial support
  • antonym: well-funded - having enough or more than enough financial resources
