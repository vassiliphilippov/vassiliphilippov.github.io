the volume or pressure of blood in the heart at the end of filling, stretching the heart muscle before it contracts

Examples

Heart preload
Doctors monitor preload to assess how much blood fills the heart before it contracts.
An increase in preload can lead to symptoms in patients with heart failure.
Medications may be used to reduce preload and ease the workload on the heart.

Related Terms
