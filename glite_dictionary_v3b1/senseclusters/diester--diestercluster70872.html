diester

noun
compound with two esters
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɛstər / (AmE), / daɪˈɛstə / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed when two alcohol molecules react with an acid, producing two ester groups

Examples

A diester compound
A diester is commonly used as a plasticizer to make plastics more flexible.
Scientists analyzed the diester content in the new synthetic lubricant.
The production of biodiesel often involves the formation of a diester from vegetable oil.
