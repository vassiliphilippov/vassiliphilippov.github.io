extraditable

adjective
subject to extradition
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstrəˈdaɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈdaɪtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be legally sent to another country or state for trial or punishment

Examples

An extraditable crime
The court ruled that the offense was extraditable under international law.
He was considered extraditable because the crime was recognized in both countries.
Some political crimes are not extraditable according to the treaty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extradition - the act of sending someone to another country or state for trial
  • antonym: non-extraditable - not able to be sent to another country or state for trial
