fermenting

noun
process of breaking down substances by fermentation
Pronunciation: / fərˈmɛntɪŋ / (AmE), / fəˈmɛntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which an agent, such as yeast or bacteria, causes an organic substance to break down into simpler substances, especially the anaerobic breakdown of sugar into alcohol

Examples

Fermenting sugar
Fermenting is essential in the production of beer, as it transforms sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
During fermenting, microorganisms break down carbohydrates without the presence of oxygen.
The traditional method of fermenting vegetables preserves them while developing complex flavors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fermentation - the chemical breakdown of substances by bacteria, yeasts, or other microorganisms
