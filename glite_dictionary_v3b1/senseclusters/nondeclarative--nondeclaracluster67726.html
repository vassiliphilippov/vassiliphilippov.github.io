nondeclarative

adjective
specifying how to do
8.7
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.dɪ.ˈklær.ə.tɪv/ (AmE), /nɒn.dɪ.ˈklær.ə.tɪv/ (BrE)
relating to a style, especially in computing, that specifies how to perform tasks rather than only what result is desired.

Examples

Nondeclarative programming style
In programming, a nondeclarative approach requires you to write out each step in detail.
The course covered both declarative and nondeclarative methods for solving problems.
Some students find nondeclarative languages more intuitive because they control every operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative - relating to specifying what result is desired rather than how to achieve it
  • synonym: imperative - relating to giving explicit instructions on how to perform tasks
