writerly

adjective
literary, open-ended
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪtərli / (AmE), / ˈraɪtəli / (BrE)
having a literary, creative, or self-aware style that often invites readers to interpret or appreciate the writing

Examples

A writerly style
The novel's writerly style invites readers to interpret its meaning in different ways.
Some critics praise the writerly qualities of experimental fiction for challenging traditional storytelling.
Her poetry is highly writerly, requiring careful attention to language and structure.
The novel's writerly style appeals to those who appreciate creative language and structure.
Her essays are praised for their writerly attention to detail and thoughtful expression.
He admired the writerly qualities in her storytelling, especially her use of vivid imagery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: literary - relating to literature or writing
  • antonym: readerly - describing a text that is straightforward and easy to understand
