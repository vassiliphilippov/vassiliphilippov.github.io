worm

verb
to extract information slyly
8.8
Pronunciation: / wɜrm / (AmE), / wɜːm / (BrE)
to obtain information or something from someone by persistent, often indirect or sly questioning or effort

Examples

To worm secrets
She kept worming the details out of him with casual, friendly questions.
He managed to worm the secret from his friend after hours of conversation.
The journalist was skilled at worming information out of reluctant sources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coax - to gently persuade someone to do or say something
  • synonym: extract - to get something out, especially with effort
