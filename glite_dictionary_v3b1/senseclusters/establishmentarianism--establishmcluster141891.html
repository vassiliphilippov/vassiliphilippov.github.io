establishmentarianism

noun
belief in supporting the established order
Pronunciation: / ɪˌstæblɪʃmənˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˌstæblɪʃməntˈeəriənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The doctrine or belief in supporting the established order, particularly in society, politics, or religion.

Examples

Establishmentarianism belief
Debates about church and state often involve a clash between establishmentarianism and anti-establishment ideas.
Despite the rising call for reform, establishmentarianism remained influential in the party's leadership.
His strong establishmentarianism was evident in his support for traditional institutions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antiestablishmentarianism - opposition to the established order, especially in politics or religion
  • synonym: conservatism - the holding of traditional values and resistance to change
