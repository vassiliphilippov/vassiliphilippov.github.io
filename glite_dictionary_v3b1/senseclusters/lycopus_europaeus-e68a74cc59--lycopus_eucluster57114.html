Lycopus europaeus

noun
a Eurasian herb with white flowers
Pronunciation: / laɪˈkoʊpəs juːˈroʊpi.əs / (AmE), / lɪˈkəʊpəs jʊˈrəʊpiːəs / (BrE)
A hairy Eurasian perennial herb with two-lipped white flowers, commonly found in wet habitats.

Examples

Lycopus europaeus plant
Lycopus europaeus is commonly known as gypsywort or European bugleweed.
Herbalists have traditionally used Lycopus europaeus for a variety of medicinal purposes.
The wetland was filled with Lycopus europaeus, easily recognizable by its white flowers.
