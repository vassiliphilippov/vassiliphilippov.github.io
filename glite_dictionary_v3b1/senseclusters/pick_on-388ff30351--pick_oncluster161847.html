pick on

verb
repeatedly criticize or harass
1.0
Pronunciation: / pɪk ɑn / (AmE), / pɪk ɒn / (BrE)
to repeatedly criticize or treat someone unfairly, especially someone who is weaker or more vulnerable

Examples

To pick on a quiet person
He always complains that his coworkers pick on him for being quiet.
Kids in the playground started picking on the new boy as soon as he arrived.
The teacher told the class not to pick on students who were struggling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bully - to seek to harm or intimidate those who are perceived as vulnerable
