kickdown

noun
forceful kick to break
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɪkˌdaʊn / (BrE)
an act of forcefully kicking something down, especially a door or barrier

Examples

Door kickdown
The police performed a kickdown to enter the locked apartment quickly.
A loud kickdown startled everyone in the hallway during the emergency.
The movie scene showed a dramatic kickdown of the villain's hideout door.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break-in - an act of entering a place by force
  • synonym: smash - to break something violently into pieces
