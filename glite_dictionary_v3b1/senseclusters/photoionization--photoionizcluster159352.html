photoionization

noun
light-induced ionization
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌaɪənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which light causes atoms or molecules to lose electrons and become ions

Examples

Photoionization process
Scientists study photoionization to understand how stars emit energy into space.
The ultraviolet radiation from the sun can cause photoionization in the Earth's upper atmosphere.
In astrophysics, photoionization plays a key role in the formation of ionized gas clouds.
The researchers used photoionization to detect trace gases in the air samples.
Modern mass spectrometers often rely on photoionization for sensitive chemical analysis.
Understanding photoionization is important for studying how light interacts with matter in physics and chemistry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ionization - the process of forming ions by removing or adding electrons
  • antonym: recombination - the process where ions and electrons combine to form neutral atoms
