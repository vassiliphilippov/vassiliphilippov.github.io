cocky

adjective
overly self-confident or arrogant
6.9 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.kiː/ (AmE), /ˈkɒk.ɪ/ (BrE)
having too much confidence in oneself; behaving in an overly self-assured or arrogant way.

Examples

A cocky attitude
Despite his lack of experience, he was extremely cocky during the interview.
He became less cocky after failing his first exam at university.
Some people found her cocky attitude off-putting, while others admired her confidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modest - having a humble opinion of oneself; not arrogant
