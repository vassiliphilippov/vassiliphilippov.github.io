cocky

adjective
overly self-confident or arrogant
4.2 3.22/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkÉ‘ki / (AmE), / ËˆkÉ’ki / (BrE)
having too much confidence in oneself; behaving in an overly self-assured or arrogant way.

Examples

A cocky attitude
Despite his lack of experience, he was extremely cocky during the interview.
He became less cocky after failing his first exam at university.
Some people found her cocky attitude off-putting, while others admired her confidence.

  • conceited
    5.4 0.84/m
    conceited has a proud, often quiet self-view, while cocky shows loud, public bragging and bold behaviour.
  • arrogant
    4.5 12/m
    arrogant acts as if more important and can be overbearing, while cocky is louder and more showy.
  • stuck-up
    7.5 0.60/m
    stuck-up acts snobby and keeps distance from others, while cocky shows bold confidence and brags openly.
  • overconfident
    5.3 0.90/m

  • brash
    6.6 1.84/m
