deacidify

verb
to remove acid
Pronunciation: / diːˈæsɪdɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / diːˈæsɪdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to remove acid from something, especially to prevent damage or deterioration

Examples

To deacidify old paper
Special chemicals are used to deacidify old paper documents in archives.
The process to deacidify wine helps improve its taste and stability.
Technicians regularly deacidify museum artifacts to prevent long-term damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acidify - to make something more acidic
  • synonym: neutralize - to make a substance chemically neutral
