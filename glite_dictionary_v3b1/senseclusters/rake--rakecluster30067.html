rake

verb
gather with a rake
Pronunciation: / reɪk / (AmE), / reɪk / (BrE)
to gather, smooth, or clear leaves, soil, or similar material using a rake

Examples

To rake leaves
Every autumn, we rake leaves in the backyard.
He raked the grass clippings into a neat pile.
She spends Saturday mornings raking around the garden.
After the storm, we had to rake the path to remove all the debris.
Gardeners often rake the soil before planting seeds.
He used a broom to rake the gravel into neat patterns.
