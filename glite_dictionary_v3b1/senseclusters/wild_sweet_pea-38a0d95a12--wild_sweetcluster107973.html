wild sweet pea

noun
North American perennial plant with yellowish-rose flowers
9.3
Pronunciation: / waɪld swit pi / (AmE), / waɪld swiːt piː / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to eastern North America, with downy leaves and yellowish-rose flowers, and known as a source of rotenone.

Examples

The wild sweet pea plant
Farmers once harvested wild sweet pea as a natural source of rotenone.
The wild sweet pea grows vigorously in the meadows of eastern North America.
The delicate flowers of the wild sweet pea add soft color to the field each summer.
