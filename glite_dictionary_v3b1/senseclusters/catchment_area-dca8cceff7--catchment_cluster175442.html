catchment area

noun
land area draining into a river or body of water

an area of land from which all precipitation collects and drains off into a common outlet, such as a river, lake, or reservoir

Examples

The catchment area floods
Flooding often occurs when heavy rainfall overwhelms the river's catchment area.
Strict regulations protect the catchment area of the city's main reservoir.
The Nile's catchment area covers several countries in northeastern Africa.

Synonyms

  • catchment basin
    6.2 0.00/m
    a catchment basin is land where rainfall drains to one outlet, while catchment area names that same drainage area.
  • drainage basin
    4.0 0.11/m
    a drainage basin is the full land area draining into a river or lake, while catchment area is the same general idea.
  • drainage area
    3.7 0.05/m
    a drainage area is the land measured for water flow in reports, while catchment area is the common geographic name.
  • river basin
    3.5 0.31/m
    a river basin is the land drained by a river and its tributaries, while catchment area can refer to any outlet.
  • watershed
    6.1 2.79/m
    a watershed can mean the ridge dividing rivers or the region drained by one river, while catchment area always means drained land.
  • catchment
    6.4 0.97/m
    a catchment can mean either land that collects water or a built tank, while catchment area only means the land that drains.
  • basin
    3.1 9.30/m
    a basin is a general land depression or area where water collects, while catchment area specifically means land draining to one outlet.
