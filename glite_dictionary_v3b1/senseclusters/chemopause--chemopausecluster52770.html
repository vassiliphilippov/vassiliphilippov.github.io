chemopause

noun
atmospheric boundary
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmoʊˌpɔːz / (AmE), / ˈkɛməˌpɔːz / (BrE)
the boundary in a planet's atmosphere where the chemical composition changes sharply

Examples

The chemopause boundary
Scientists study the chemopause to understand how gases are distributed in Earth's upper atmosphere.
The chemopause marks a significant change in the chemical makeup of the air above it.
Satellites collect data near the chemopause to monitor atmospheric changes over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homopause - the boundary in the atmosphere separating the homosphere from the heterosphere
  • synonym: tropopause - the boundary between the troposphere and the stratosphere
