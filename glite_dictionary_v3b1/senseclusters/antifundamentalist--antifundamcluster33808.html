antifundamentalist

noun
opponent of fundamentalism
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˌfʌndəˈmɛntəlɪst / (BrE)
a person or group that opposes fundamentalist beliefs, practices, or movements, especially in religion or politics

Examples

An antifundamentalist group
The antifundamentalist movement gained support among those seeking more progressive reforms.
As an antifundamentalist, she argued for open debate rather than strict doctrinal enforcement.
Many antifundamentalists advocate for tolerance and diversity in religious discussions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critic - a person who expresses disapproval or opposes certain ideas
  • antonym: fundamentalist - a person who strictly follows the basic principles of a religion or ideology
