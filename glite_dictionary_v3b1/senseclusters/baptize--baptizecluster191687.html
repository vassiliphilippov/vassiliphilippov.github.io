baptize

verb
administer baptism; perform a Christian initiation rite
4.3 4.4
Pronunciation: / bæpˈtaɪz /; / ˈbæp.taɪz / (AmE), / bæpˈtaɪz /; / ˈbæp.taɪz / (BrE)
to perform the Christian religious ceremony of baptism on someone by sprinkling or immersing them in water, especially as a religious rite signifying spiritual cleansing and admission to a Christian church

Examples

To baptize a child
Many Christians baptize their children as an important tradition.
She decided to baptize her son despite her family's objections.
The church will baptize the newborn baby on Sunday.
Hundreds of converts were baptised during the annual ceremony at the river.
The priest agreed to baptise their newborn daughter next Sunday.
They watched as the minister baptised each child in turn, blessing them with holy water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: christen - to give a name to at baptism; often used interchangeably with baptize
