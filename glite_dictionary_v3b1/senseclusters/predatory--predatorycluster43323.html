predatory

adjective
ruthlessly exploiting others for gain
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈprɛdəˌtəri / (BrE)
seeking to exploit, control, or take advantage of others for personal gain, often in a ruthless or forceful way

Examples

Predatory behavior
He warned his daughter about the dangers of predatory individuals at parties.
Some companies have been criticized for their predatory lending practices.
The predatory behavior of the scammers left many victims in financial ruin.
For centuries, predatory raids between rival clans devastated the region's economy.
She warned that predatory groups might exploit the chaos after the earthquake.
The pirates launched a predatory attack on the coastal villages, looting and burning as they went.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exploitative - making use of a situation or treating others unfairly in order to gain an advantage or benefit
  • antonym: lawful - acting within or conforming to the law; not criminal
  • synonym: marauding - roaming about in search of things to steal or people to attack
  • synonym: plundering - stealing goods, especially in time of war or civil disorder
