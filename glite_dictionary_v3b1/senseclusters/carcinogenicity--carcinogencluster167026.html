carcinogenicity

noun
ability to cause cancer
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrsɪnəˈdʒɛnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌkɑːsɪnəˈdʒɛnɪsəti / (BrE)
the ability or tendency of a substance to cause cancer

Examples

The carcinogenicity of chemicals
Researchers are investigating the carcinogenicity of new industrial chemicals before they are approved for use.
The carcinogenicity of tobacco smoke has been well documented in scientific studies.
Strict regulations limit the carcinogenicity of additives in processed foods to protect public health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmlessness - the quality of not causing harm
  • synonym: toxicity - the degree to which a substance is poisonous
