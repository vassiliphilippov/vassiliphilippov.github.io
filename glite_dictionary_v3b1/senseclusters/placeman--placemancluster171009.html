placeman

noun
a male appointee, usually by patronage
6.3 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpleɪ.smən/ /; / /ˈpleɪ.smæn/ (AmE), /ˈpleɪ.smən/ /; / /ˈpleɪ.smæn/ (BrE)
a man who is appointed to a position, especially one who is regarded as holding office through patronage rather than merit (often used disparagingly)

Examples

A placeman in office
Many considered him merely a placeman who owed his position to political connections.
She argued that government should stop appointing unqualified placemen to important offices.
The reforms were resisted by entrenched placemen throughout the department.
