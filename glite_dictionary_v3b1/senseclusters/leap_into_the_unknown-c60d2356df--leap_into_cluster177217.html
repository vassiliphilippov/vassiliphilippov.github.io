leap into the unknown

verb
do something risky or uncertain
Pronunciation: / lip ɪnˈtu ði ʌnˈnoʊn / (AmE), / liːp ɪnˈtə ði ʌnˈnəʊn / (BrE)
to do something risky or uncertain, especially without knowing what will happen

Examples

To leap into the unknown boldly
By starting his own business, he made a leap into the unknown despite the risks involved.
Moving to a new country was a real leap into the unknown for her.
Sometimes you have to take a leap into the unknown to discover new opportunities.

Related Terms
