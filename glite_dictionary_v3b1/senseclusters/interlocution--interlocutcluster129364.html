interlocution

noun
dramatic dialogue interlude
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.lə.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.lə.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
a short dramatic dialogue or interlude, often performed between the acts of a play

Examples

Comic interlocution on stage
The play featured a humorous interlocution between two characters during the intermission.
A brief interlocution was performed to entertain the audience while the stage was changed.
The director added an interlocution to provide comic relief between the serious acts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialogue - a conversation between two or more people in a play or book
  • antonym: monologue - a long speech by one actor in a play or movie
