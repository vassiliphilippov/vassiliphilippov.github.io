crowdsource

verb
to seek public input
Pronunciation: / ˈkraʊdˌsɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkraʊdˌsɔːs / (BrE)
to get ideas, services, or content by asking a large group of people, usually online

Examples

To crowdsource ideas
The company decided to crowdsource its new logo design to get fresh ideas from the public.
Many organizations crowdsource solutions to complex problems by inviting online communities to participate.
They chose to crowdsource funding for their project through a popular website.
The museum decided to crowdsource ideas for its next big exhibition.
Many companies crowdsource solutions to technical problems through online platforms.
They plan to crowdsource funding for their new community project.

  • synonym: outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier
