eccentric

adjective
unconventional; markedly unusual
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
conspicuously or grossly unconventional or unusual; not behaving or appearing in a way that is typical or expected.

Examples

An eccentric artist
Her eccentric methods often puzzled her colleagues, but they got results.
That house, shaped like a giant shoe, is truly eccentric in design.
The artist was known for his eccentric taste in clothes and decor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual, especially so as to cause surprise or amusement
  • antonym: conventional - based on or in accordance with what is generally done or believed
  • synonym: unconventional - not based on or conforming to what is generally done or believed
