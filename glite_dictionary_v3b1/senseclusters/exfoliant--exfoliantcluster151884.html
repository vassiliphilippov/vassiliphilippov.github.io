exfoliant

noun
surface-removing agent
Pronunciation: / ɛksˈfoʊliənt / (AmE), / ɛksˈfəʊliənt / (BrE)
a substance or product used to remove dead skin cells or other surface layers

Examples

A gentle exfoliant
The scientist applied an exfoliant to remove the outer layer of the material.
Many skincare products contain an exfoliant to help clear dead skin cells.
A gentle exfoliant can improve the texture of both skin and certain materials.
She applies an exfoliant to her face every Sunday to keep her skin fresh.
A gentle exfoliant can help remove dry patches and reveal smoother skin.
Dermatologists recommend using an exfoliant no more than twice a week for sensitive skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrasive - a material used to smooth or clean a surface by rubbing
  • synonym: cleanser - a substance used to clean the skin
  • antonym: moisturizer - a product used to add moisture to the skin
  • synonym: peeling agent - a substance that causes the outer layer to come off
  • synonym: scrub - a product used to clean or exfoliate the skin
