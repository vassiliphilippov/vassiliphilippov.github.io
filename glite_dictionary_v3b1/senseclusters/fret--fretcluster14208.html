fret

verb
cause worry or annoyance
4.2 3.4
Pronunciation: / frɛt / (AmE), / frɛt / (BrE)
to be or make someone worried, anxious, or irritated, especially about something minor

Examples

To fret about problems
Constant criticism can fret even the most patient person.
Don't let small problems fret you too much.
The loud noise outside began to fret the children.
Parents often fret when their children are late coming home.
She fretted all night over the results of her exam.
Try not to fret about things you cannot change.
She tried not to let the criticism fret her mood, but it lingered.
The memory of the unfair decision continued to fret him for years.
When left unchecked, small disagreements can fret at the unity of a group.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - cause someone to feel irritated or a little angry
  • antonym: calm - make someone peaceful and free from agitation or worry
  • synonym: fuss - worry unnecessarily or excessively
  • synonym: rankle - to cause persistent annoyance or resentment
  • antonym: relax - to become less anxious or tense
  • antonym: soothe - to calm or ease someone's anger or irritation
  • synonym: worry - to feel or cause to feel anxious or troubled
