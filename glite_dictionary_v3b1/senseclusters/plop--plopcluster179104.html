plop

noun
the sound of something dropping into liquid
3.0 0.46/m
Pronunciation: / plÉ‘p / (AmE), / plÉ’p / (BrE)
a short, soft sound made when a rounded object drops into liquid without splashing

Examples

A plop in water
She heard the plop of rainwater dripping from the roof into the bucket.
The stone fell into the pond with a gentle plop.
With a soft plop, the cherry landed in his glass of soda.

Synonyms

  • bloop
    6.7 0.03/m
    bloop is a short bubbling or popping sound in liquid, while plop is a single soft drop sound when an object hits water.
  • plash
    9.9 0.03/m
    plash is a gentle splashing sound of water or small waves, while plop is the single soft sound when something drops in liquid.
