furrow

verb
make creased or wrinkled
5.8 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…úro ä / (AmE), / Ààf…úÀêr…ô ä / (BrE)
to make lines, wrinkles, or creases in something, especially on the skin or face

Examples

To furrow your brow
A deep frown furrowed his forehead when he heard the news.
She furrowed her brow in concentration as she read the instructions.
The old man furrowed his brow, recalling a distant memory.

Synonyms

  • wrinkle
    2.1 4.23/m
    wrinkle means forming folds or lines in skin or fabric, while furrow means making deeper, longer lines often on the face.
  • crinkle
    3.7 1.46/m
    crinkle makes small, close folds or lines on a surface, while furrow makes longer, deeper lines on skin or soil.
  • knit
    4.2 0.33/m
    knit pulls skin together into tight small folds, while furrow makes visible long lines or grooves, often on the face.
  • crease
    2.3 2.43/m
    crease is a sharp fold made by pressing or folding material, while furrow is a natural or formed line often deeper on skin.
