lection

noun
reading passage
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a passage chosen to be read aloud, especially for instruction, recitation, or in a religious service

Examples

A lection for class
The teacher assigned a lection from the textbook for tomorrow's class discussion.
During the ceremony, a special lection was read aloud to the audience.
Each student prepared to present their chosen lection in front of the group.
During the ceremony, the deacon read the lection to the congregation.
Each Sunday, a different member is chosen to deliver the lection from the pulpit.
The lection for today comes from the Book of Psalms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lesson - a passage from scripture read aloud in a church service
  • synonym: reading - a piece of text to be read aloud or studied
  • synonym: recitation - the act of reading something aloud from memory or text
  • antonym: sermon - a talk on a religious or moral subject, often given during a service
