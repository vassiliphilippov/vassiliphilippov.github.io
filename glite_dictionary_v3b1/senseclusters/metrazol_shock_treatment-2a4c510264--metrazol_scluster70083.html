Metrazol shock treatment

noun
convulsive psychiatric treatment using Metrazol
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtɹəzɔl ʃɑk tɹitmənt / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrəzɒl ʃɒk triːtmənt / (BrE)
a former medical procedure in which Metrazol was administered to induce convulsions and coma, used in the treatment of certain psychiatric disorders

Examples

Metrazol shock treatment procedure
Because of its severe side effects, Metrazol shock treatment fell out of favor by the 1950s.
Before electroconvulsive therapy became common, Metrazol shock treatment was widely used in psychiatric hospitals.
The psychiatrist explained the risks associated with Metrazol shock treatment to the patient's family.
