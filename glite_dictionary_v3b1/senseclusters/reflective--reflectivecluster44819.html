reflective

adjective
able to reflect light or sound
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛktɪv / (BrE)
Able to reflect light, sound, or heat; having the property of sending back energy, such as light or sound, from a surface.

Examples

Reflective surface
Her sunglasses had highly reflective lenses that mirrored the sunlight.
Most road signs are made from a reflective material so drivers can see them at night.
The scientist examined the reflective properties of the new coating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mirror-like - having a surface that reflects an image clearly, like a mirror
  • antonym: nonreflective - not capable of reflecting light, sound, or heat
