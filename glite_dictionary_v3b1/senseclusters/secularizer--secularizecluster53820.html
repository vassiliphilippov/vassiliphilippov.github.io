secularizer

noun
one who makes secular
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkjələrˌaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɛkjʊləˌaɪzə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something less religious or more secular

Examples

The secularizer removes religion
The secularizer argued for removing religious symbols from public schools.
As a leading secularizer, she promoted policies that separated religion from government.
Many viewed the new law as the work of a determined secularizer in parliament.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cleric - a religious leader or member of the clergy
  • synonym: reformer - a person who makes changes to improve something
  • synonym: secularist - a person who supports the separation of religion from civic affairs
