Stizolobium deeringiana

noun
woody vine used as green manure and forage
9.9
Pronunciation: / stɪzəˌloʊbiəm diːrɪŋˈjænə / (AmE), / stɪz.əˌləʊ.bi.əm diː.rɪŋˈjænɪə / (BrE)
An annual woody vine of Asian origin, with long clusters of purplish flowers and densely hairy pods, cultivated in the southern United States for green manure and grazing.

Examples

Stizolobium deeringiana plant
Cattle graze on fields of Stizolobium deeringiana, which serves as both forage and green manure.
Farmers in the southern United States often plant Stizolobium deeringiana to enrich the soil between crop cycles.
The dense, hairy pods of Stizolobium deeringiana can be seen hanging from the vine in late summer.
