brutalize

verb
make cruel or inhuman
1.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbruː.tə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ˈbruː.tə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to treat someone with great cruelty and violence, or to make someone cruel, violent, or insensitive (especially by repeated exposure to brutality)

Examples

To brutalize a person
Exposure to constant violence can brutalize even the most compassionate individuals.
The harsh conditions of the prison brutalized many of its inmates.
Years of war had brutalized the soldiers, leaving them numb to suffering.
Media violence can brutalize audiences, making them less sensitive to real suffering.
Some fear that constant exposure to tragedy will brutalize young people emotionally.
Years of war had brutalized the once peaceful population.
The guards were accused of having brutalized the prisoners during their captivity.
Violent video games can sometimes brutalize young people, making them less sensitive to real-life suffering.
Years of war had brutalized the population, leaving deep emotional scars.
