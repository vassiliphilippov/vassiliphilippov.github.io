blunt

adjective
plain, undisguised, or unembellished (statement)
4.5 3.2
Pronunciation: /blʌnt/ (AmE), /blʌnt/ (BrE)
plain and direct in speech or manner, sometimes to the point of rudeness

Examples

Blunt answer
He told her the blunt truth, even though it was hard for her to hear.
Her blunt statement left no room for misunderstanding.
Sometimes you need to face the blunt facts, no matter how uncomfortable they are.
His blunt comments sometimes offend people who expect more diplomacy.
Marta is known for her blunt honesty and direct feedback.
She was refreshingly blunt about the issues facing the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: candid - truthful and straightforward
  • synonym: crude - in a raw or unrefined state; not made more elegant or refined
  • synonym: forthright - direct and outspoken
  • synonym: stark - unpleasantly or sharply clear; impossible to avoid
  • antonym: sugarcoated - made to seem more pleasant or acceptable than it really is
  • antonym: tactful - having or showing sensitivity in dealing with others
