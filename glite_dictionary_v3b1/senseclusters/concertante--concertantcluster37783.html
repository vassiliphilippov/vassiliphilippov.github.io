concertante

noun
orchestral work with solos
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑnsərˈtɑnteɪ / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəˈtænteɪ / (BrE)
a musical composition in which one or more solo instruments are contrasted with an orchestra, often in a lively style

Examples

A concertante for orchestra
The orchestra performed a rarely heard concertante featuring a clarinet and a bassoon as soloists.
She composed a concertante that allowed each section of the orchestra to shine alongside the soloists.
Many 18th-century composers wrote at least one concertante to showcase their virtuoso players.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concerto - a musical work for solo instrument and orchestra
  • antonym: symphony - a long musical composition for orchestra, usually in several movements
