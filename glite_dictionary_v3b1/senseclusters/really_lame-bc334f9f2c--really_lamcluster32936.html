really lame

adjective
very weak or unconvincing
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪli leɪm / (AmE), / ˈrɪəli leɪm / (BrE)
Very weak, unconvincing, or inadequate, especially as an explanation or excuse.

Examples

A really lame excuse
Her apology seemed really lame given what actually happened.
I thought his answer was really lame and didn't make any sense at all.
That was a really lame excuse for missing the meeting.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: lame - weak, unconvincing (informal meaning)
  • antonym: really good - very good, of high quality
