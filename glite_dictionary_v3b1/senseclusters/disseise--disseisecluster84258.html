disseise

noun
unlawful dispossession
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsiːz / (AmE), / dɪˈsiːz / (BrE)
the act of unlawfully depriving someone of possession of land or property

Examples

Unlawful disseise of land
The sudden disseise of the tenant led to a lengthy court battle.
In medieval times, disseise was a common cause of disputes among landowners.
The law protected individuals from unlawful disseise of their property.
