panel

verb
select from a list, esp. for a jury
2.5 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpæ.nəl/ /; / /ˈpæn.əl/ (AmE), /ˈpæ.nəl/ /; / /ˈpæn.əl/ (BrE)
to select people, especially jurors, from a list to form a panel for a particular purpose, especially a legal one.

Examples

To panel a jury
Each year, they panel local residents to serve as jurors in the civil court.
It took several hours to panel the jury from the list of candidates.
The court will panel a group of experts to review the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empanel - to select or enlist individuals, especially jurors, into a panel
