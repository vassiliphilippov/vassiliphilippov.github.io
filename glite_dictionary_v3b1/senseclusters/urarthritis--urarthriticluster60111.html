urarthritis

noun
inflammation of joints from uric acid buildup
8.9
Pronunciation: / jʊrˌɑrˈθraɪtɪs / (AmE), / jʊəˌrɑːˈθraɪtɪs / (BrE)
a painful inflammation of the joints, especially of the big toe and foot, caused by the accumulation of uric acid and its salts due to metabolic defects

Examples

Urarthritis in foot
Certain foods may increase the risk of developing urarthritis in susceptible individuals.
Elevated levels of uric acid in the blood can lead to urarthritis if left untreated.
The patient was diagnosed with urarthritis after experiencing sudden, severe pain in his big toe joint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gout - a form of arthritis characterized by severe pain, redness, and tenderness in joints, caused by uric acid crystals
