Battle of Borodino

noun
Napoleonic battle near Moscow, 1812
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbæt.əl əv bɑːr.ə.ˈdiː.noʊ/ /; / /ˈbæt.əl ə bɑːr.ə.ˈdiː.noʊ/ (AmE), /ˈbæt.əl əv bɒr.ə.ˈdiː.nə/ /; / /ˈbæt.əl ə bɒr.ə.ˈdiː.nə/ (BrE)
a major battle fought in 1812 near the village of Borodino, west of Moscow, between Napoleon's French army and Russian forces, resulting in heavy losses on both sides

Examples

The Battle of Borodino 1812
Many historians regard the Battle of Borodino as one of the bloodiest single-day engagements of the 19th century.
The Battle of Borodino marked a turning point in Napoleon's campaign in Russia.
Tolstoy's 'War and Peace' vividly describes the horrors of the Battle of Borodino.
