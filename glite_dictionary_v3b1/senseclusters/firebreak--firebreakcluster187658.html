firebreak

noun
short lockdown to halt disease spread
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər.breɪk/ /; / /ˈfaɪr.breɪk/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə.breɪk/ /; / /ˈfaɪ.breɪk/ (BrE)
a short, deliberate period of restrictions or lockdown intended to interrupt or slow the spread of an infectious disease.

Examples

COVID-19 firebreak
The government announced a two-week firebreak to reduce COVID-19 transmission before the holidays.
Local leaders debated whether a short firebreak could prevent hospitals becoming overwhelmed this winter.
Businesses urged clear guidance about any planned firebreak so they could prepare and avoid losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circuit breaker - a short, strict set of restrictions intended to break chains of transmission and reduce infection rates
  • synonym: lockdown - a period when people are required to stay at home and many businesses and services are closed to control disease spread
