determined or decided by something else; contingent on certain conditions or circumstances

Examples

Dependent upon conditions
Her promotion is dependent upon the completion of the ongoing project.
The outcome of the case is dependent upon the judge's final decision.
Whether the picnic will happen is dependent upon the weather this weekend.
Approval of the merger is dependent upon regulatory review.
Success in the program is dependent upon your ability to work hard and stay focused.
The university's funding is dependent upon annual government grants.

Synonyms

  • dependent on
    3.2 17/m
    dependent on is the usual modern phrase used in speech and writing to show reliance, while dependent upon sounds slightly more formal.
  • dependent
    2.8 16/m
    dependent often appears alone as an adjective requiring context, while dependent upon explicitly shows the condition or thing it relies on.
  • contingent on
    6.4 1.34/m
    contingent on says something will happen only if certain conditions hold in future, while dependent upon states a direct reliance.
  • contingent upon
    6.4 0.92/m
    contingent upon is more formal and focuses on conditions that must happen first, while dependent upon expresses simple reliance.
  • contingent
    6.8 3.23/m
    contingent often means possible and not certain because of conditions, while dependent upon makes the required link to another factor.
  • subject
    4.7 38/m
    subject is likely to be affected by or under someone's control, while dependent upon emphasizes being decided by other conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contingent on - subject to the condition or happening of something else
  • antonym: independent of - not affected by or reliant on something else
