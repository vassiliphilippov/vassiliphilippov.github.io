Synonyms

  • dependent on
    3.2 8/m
    dependent on is the usual modern phrase used in speech and writing to show reliance, while dependent upon sounds slightly more formal.
  • dependent
    2.8 5/m
    dependent often appears alone as an adjective requiring context, while dependent upon explicitly shows the condition or thing it relies on.
  • contingent on
    6.4 0.6/m
    contingent on says something will happen only if certain conditions hold in future, while dependent upon states a direct reliance.
  • contingent upon
    6.4 0.3/m
    contingent upon is more formal and focuses on conditions that must happen first, while dependent upon expresses simple reliance.
  • contingent
    6.8 0.9/m
    contingent often means possible and not certain because of conditions, while dependent upon makes the required link to another factor.
  • subject
    4.7 14/m
    subject is likely to be affected by or under someone's control, while dependent upon emphasizes being decided by other conditions.
