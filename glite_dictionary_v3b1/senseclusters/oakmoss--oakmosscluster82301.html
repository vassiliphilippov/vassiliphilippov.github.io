oakmoss

noun
lichen for perfumes
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊkˌmɑs / (AmE), / ˈəʊkˌmɒs / (BrE)
a type of lichen that grows on trees, especially oaks, and is used in perfumes for its earthy scent

Examples

Oakmoss in perfume
Many luxury perfumes contain extracts of oakmoss for their earthy aroma.
The forest floor was covered in soft oakmoss clinging to the old oak trees.
Some people are allergic to oakmoss used in scented products.
The perfumer added oakmoss to the fragrance for its earthy scent.
You can often find oakmoss growing on the trunks of old oak trees in the forest.
Collectors carefully harvested oakmoss for use in traditional herbal remedies.
