mangrove snapper

noun
a coastal fish species, Lutjanus griseus
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋɡroʊv ˈsnæpɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡrəʊv ˈsnæpə / (BrE)
a species of snapper (Lutjanus griseus) found in shallow coastal waters of the western Atlantic, especially near mangroves, and common off the coast of Florida

Examples

The mangrove snapper fish
Fishermen often catch mangrove snapper near the piers along the Florida coast.
The chef prepared a delicious dish using fresh mangrove snapper fillets.
We spotted several mangrove snapper hiding among the roots of the mangroves during our boat trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gray snapper - another common name for Lutjanus griseus
