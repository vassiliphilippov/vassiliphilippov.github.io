Alosa chrysocloris

noun
species of shad (skipjack herring)
Pronunciation: / əˈloʊsə ˌkrɪsəˈklɔrɪs / (AmE), / əˈləʊsə ˌkrɪsəˈklɒrɪs / (BrE)
A species of shad, commonly known as the skipjack herring, that spawns in streams within the Mississippi River drainage system.

Examples

Alosa chrysocloris fish
Fishermen value the Alosa chrysocloris for its vigorous fight when caught.
The Alosa chrysocloris is commonly found in streams connected to the Mississippi River.
Unlike some other shad, the Alosa chrysocloris migrates long distances to spawn.
