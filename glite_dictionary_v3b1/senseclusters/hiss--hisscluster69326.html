hiss

noun
sharp sound showing disapproval
Pronunciation: / hɪs / (AmE), / hɪs / (BrE)
a sharp, sibilant sound, like that made by a snake or to show disapproval

Examples

A loud hiss
A sudden hiss from the audience silenced the speaker for a moment.
His attempt at humor was met with a hiss of disapproval from the back row.
The crowd responded to the poor performance with a loud hiss that echoed through the hall.
A sudden hiss from the audience signaled their disapproval of the decision.
She reacted to the speaker's comment with a sharp hiss from the back of the room.
The distinct hiss could be heard over the applause as some people expressed their dissatisfaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boo - an exclamation expressing disapproval or contempt
  • antonym: cheer - a shout of approval, support, or praise
