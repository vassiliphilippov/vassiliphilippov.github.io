expressivity

noun
degree of expressiveness
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌsprɛˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ɪkˌsprɛˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
the degree to which a language, system, or medium can represent or communicate ideas, concepts, or structures

Examples

Language expressivity
The expressivity of the new programming language allows developers to write more concise code.
Artists value the expressivity of different materials when creating their work.
Linguists debate the expressivity of various grammatical structures in human languages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expressiveness - the quality of effectively conveying meaning or feeling
  • antonym: restriction - a limitation or reduction in ability to represent ideas
