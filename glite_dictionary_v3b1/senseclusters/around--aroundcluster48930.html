around

adverb
to someone's place; for a visit
Pronunciation: / əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
used to indicate movement to a person's home or to a particular place, either specified or understood

Examples

Come around later
I'll pop around later to return your book.
They invited us around for dinner on Saturday night.
Why don't you come around for coffee sometime this week?

Related Terms

  • synonym: over - used to indicate going to someone's place, especially in American English
