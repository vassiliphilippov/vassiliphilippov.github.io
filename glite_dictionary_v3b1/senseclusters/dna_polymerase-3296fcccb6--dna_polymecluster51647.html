DNA polymerase

noun
enzyme for DNA synthesis/replication
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːɛnˈeɪ pəˈlɪm.əˌreɪs / (AmE), / ˌdiːɛnˈeɪ pɒˈlɪm.əˌreɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that synthesizes new strands of DNA using an existing strand as a template during DNA replication

Examples

DNA polymerase enzyme
DNA polymerase is essential for copying genetic information during cell division.
Bacteria use a different type of DNA polymerase than humans for replicating their genomes.
Researchers studied how mutations in DNA polymerase can lead to errors in DNA replication.
