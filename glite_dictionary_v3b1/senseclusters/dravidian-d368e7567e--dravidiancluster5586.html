Dravidian

proper noun
South Indian people or language
8.4
Pronunciation: / drəˈvɪdiən / (AmE), / drəˈvɪdiən / (BrE)
a member of a group of peoples native to South India and Sri Lanka, or a language belonging to the Dravidian family

Examples

Dravidian culture
The Dravidian languages include Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam.
Archaeologists have studied ancient Dravidian cultures in southern India.
Many Dravidian people maintain unique traditions and customs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Indo-Aryan - a major language family of northern India, distinct from Dravidian
  • synonym: Tamil - a major Dravidian language spoken in southern India and Sri Lanka
