batoko palm

noun
tropical shrub with red plum-like fruit
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /bə.ˈtoʊ.koʊ pɑːm/ /; / /bə.ˈtoʊ.koʊ pæm/ (AmE), /bə.ˈtəʊ.kə pɑːm/ (BrE)
A small shrubby tree native to Madagascar, cultivated in tropical regions for its deep red, acid fruits that resemble small plums, and often used as a hedge plant.

Examples

The batoko palm fruit
Local markets sell the sour, reddish fruit of the batoko palm during harvest season.
She planted a batoko palm in her backyard for both its beauty and its edible fruit.
The garden was lined with batoko palm to create a natural hedge.
