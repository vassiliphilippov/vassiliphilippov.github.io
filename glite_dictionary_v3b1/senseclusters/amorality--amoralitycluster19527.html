amorality

noun
absence of moral principles
Pronunciation: / ˌæməˈrælɪti / (AmE), / ˌaməˈralɪti / (BrE)
The absence of moral principles or indifference to questions of right and wrong.

Examples

The amorality of actions
Many critics were disturbed by the amorality displayed in the business world during the financial crisis.
Some philosophers argue that true amorality is impossible, as every action can be judged by ethical standards.
The novel explores the amorality of its central character, who acts without concern for right or wrong.
