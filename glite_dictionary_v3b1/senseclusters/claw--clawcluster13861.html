claw

noun
sharp curved structure on animal toe
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /klɔː/ /; / /klɑː/ (AmE), /klɔː/ (BrE)
A sharp, curved, horny structure at the end of the toe of a bird, some mammals, or reptiles, used for grasping or tearing.

Examples

A cat's claw
My cat scratched the sofa with her sharp claws.
The hawk gripped its prey tightly with its powerful claw.
The lizard's claws helped it climb smoothly up the tree.
