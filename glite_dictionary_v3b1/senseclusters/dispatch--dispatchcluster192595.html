dispatch

noun
official report or message sent quickly
9.9 4.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈspætʃ/ /; / /də.ˈspætʃ/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈspætʃ/ (BrE)
an official report or message, especially one sent quickly from one place to another, often in a military or journalistic context

Examples

A military dispatch
An urgent dispatch from headquarters arrived late last night, requesting reinforcements.
She eagerly awaited the daily dispatch from her husband stationed overseas.
The journalist sent a dispatch from the front lines, describing the ongoing conflict.
Her promotion was announced in an official despatch from the ministry.
The general sent a despatch to headquarters reporting the enemy's movement.
War correspondents filed their despatches late into the night.
