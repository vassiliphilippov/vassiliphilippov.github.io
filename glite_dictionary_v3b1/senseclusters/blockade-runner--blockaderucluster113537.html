blockade-runner

noun
ship that slips through a blockade
9.9
Pronunciation: / blɑːˈkeɪd ˌrʌnər / (AmE), / blɒˈkeɪd ˌrʌnə / (BrE)
a ship that manages to pass through or circumvent a naval blockade, especially to bring in supplies or export goods illicitly or covertly during wartime

Examples

A blockade-runner ship
During the Civil War, a famous blockade-runner brought in arms and medicine for the Confederate states.
Modern historians studied artifacts recovered from a sunken blockade-runner off the coast.
The blockade-runner evaded enemy ships to deliver much-needed supplies to the besieged port.
