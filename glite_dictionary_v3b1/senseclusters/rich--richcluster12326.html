Synonyms

  • wealthy
    2.5 32/m
    wealthy describes someone with a large amount of money and assets, while rich is the general everyday word for that.
  • affluent
    5.5 6.54/m
    affluent sounds more formal and describes people or areas with much wealth, while rich is everyday for having money.
  • moneyed
    5.7 0.57/m
    moneyed refers to people who have a lot of money, often in formal or older use, while rich is more common today.
  • prosperous
    4.1 7.64/m
    prosperous describes businesses or people doing well and growing, while rich focuses on having a lot of money.
  • well-off
    3.4 2.33/m
    well-off means having enough money to live comfortably but not extremely wealthy, while rich implies greater wealth or luxury.
  • well-to-do
    4.2 1.89/m
    well-to-do describes a comfortable middle or upper lifestyle, while rich suggests larger wealth and more luxury than well-to-do.
  • well-heeled
    7.2 0.81/m
    well-heeled is an informal phrase for people with money and style, while rich is a plain word for having wealth.
  • lavish
    5.5 4.49/m
    lavish describes gifts, parties, or spending done in large amounts, while rich names a person who simply has much money.
  • opulent
    7.7 1.38/m
    opulent describes very showy, expensive places or things that display wealth, while rich is the simple word for having money.
  • luxurious
    5.6 5.39/m
    luxurious describes very comfortable and expensive things people buy, while rich describes a person's possession of money or wealth.
