expy

noun
fictional counterpart
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkspi / (AmE), / ˈɛkspi / (BrE)
a character, work, or element that is clearly modeled on or stands in for another from a different work

Examples

Expy of a hero
The villain in the new movie is an obvious expy of a classic comic book character.
Fans debated whether the protagonist was just an expy of the author's earlier hero.
Writers sometimes create an expy to pay tribute to their favorite stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or primary version of something
  • synonym: stand-in - a person or thing that takes the place of another
