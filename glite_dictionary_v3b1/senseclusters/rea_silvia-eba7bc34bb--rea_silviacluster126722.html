Rea Silvia

proper noun
mythical mother of Romulus and Remus
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪ.ə ˈsɪl.vi.ə / (AmE), / ˌriː.ə ˈsɪl.vi.ə / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, a vestal virgin who became the mother of the twins Romulus and Remus by Mars.

Examples

Rea Silvia mother
According to legend, Rea Silvia was the mother of Rome's founders, Romulus and Remus.
Many ancient artworks depict Rea Silvia at the moment she encounters the god Mars.
The story of Rea Silvia highlights the importance of myth in Roman cultural identity.
