Neo-Slavism

noun
early 20th-century Slavic unity movement
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊˈslɑːvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈslɑːvɪzəm / (BrE)
a political and cultural movement that arose in Austria-Hungary around 1908, aiming to promote unity and cooperation among Slavic peoples, particularly in Eastern Europe and the Balkans.

Examples

Neo-Slavism movement
Although short-lived, Neo-Slavism had a significant impact on nationalism within the Balkans.
Historians often discuss the role of Neo-Slavism in shaping alliances before World War I.
The rise of Neo-Slavism in early twentieth-century Europe influenced the politics of several Slavic nations.
