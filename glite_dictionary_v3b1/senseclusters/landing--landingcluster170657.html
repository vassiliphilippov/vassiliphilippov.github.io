landing

noun
coming down onto a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈlændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlændɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of coming down onto the ground or another surface, especially from the air or sea

Examples

A smooth landing
Her perfect landing impressed the judges at the gymnastics competition.
The airplane's landing was delayed due to fog.
The astronauts prepared for a safe landing on the lunar surface.
After a long voyage across the Atlantic, their landing was met with cheers from the crowd.
The captain carefully guided the ship to a smooth landing on the island shore.
The plane's landing was delayed due to heavy fog.

  • antonym: departure - the act of leaving a place
  • antonym: takeoff - the act of becoming airborne
