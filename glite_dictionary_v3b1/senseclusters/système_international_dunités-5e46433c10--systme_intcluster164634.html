Système International d'Unités

noun
international metric system of measurement units
4.1
Pronunciation: / siːˌstɛm ɛ̃.tɛʁ.na.sjɔːnal d‿y.niːt / (AmE), / siːˌstɛm ɛ̃.tɛʁ.na.sjɔːnal d‿y.niːt / (BrE)
the internationally accepted metric system of measurement units used in science and engineering, including meter, kilogram, second, ampere, kelvin, mole, and candela as base units.

Examples

The Système International d'Unités system
Researchers must express all results in terms of the Système International d'Unités for publication in major journals.
The Système International d'Unités is the standard for scientific measurements worldwide.
The transition to the Système International d'Unités was gradual but is now nearly universal in science.
