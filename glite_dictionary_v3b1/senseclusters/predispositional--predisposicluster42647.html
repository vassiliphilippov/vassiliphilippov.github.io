predispositional

adjective
tending toward a condition
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːdɪspəˈzɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪspəˈzɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving a tendency or susceptibility to develop a particular condition or quality

Examples

Predispositional factor
Genetic makeup can be a predispositional factor in developing certain allergies.
Researchers are studying the predispositional traits that may lead to anxiety disorders.
Doctors consider both environmental and predispositional influences when assessing patient risk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protective - providing protection; able to prevent harm or risk
  • synonym: susceptible - likely or liable to be influenced or harmed by a particular thing
