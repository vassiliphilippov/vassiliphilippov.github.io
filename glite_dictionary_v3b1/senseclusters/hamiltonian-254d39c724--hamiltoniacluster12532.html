Hamiltonian

adjective
of Alexander Hamilton
Pronunciation: / ˌhæməlˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌhæməlˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to Alexander Hamilton or his political ideas, especially support for a strong central government and national banking system

Examples

Hamiltonian policies
The senator's Hamiltonian approach favored a strong federal government over state autonomy.
Historians often debate the impact of Hamiltonian policies on the development of the U.S. economy.
Her views on banking are distinctly Hamiltonian, supporting a centralized financial system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Jeffersonian - relating to Thomas Jefferson or his political ideas, often favoring states' rights
