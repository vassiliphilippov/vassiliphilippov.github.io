avidin

noun
egg white protein
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæv.ɪ.dɪn/ /; / /ˈæv.ə.dɪn/ /; / /ˈæv.ɪ.dən/ (AmE), /ˈæv.ɪ.dɪn/ /; / /ˈæv.ə.dɪn/ /; / /ˈæv.ɪ.dən/ (BrE)
a protein found in egg whites that binds very strongly to biotin, often used in laboratory tests

Examples

avidin protein
Researchers use avidin in experiments to separate and detect biotin in samples.
Egg whites contain avidin, which can interfere with biotin absorption if eaten raw.
The strong bond between avidin and biotin is useful in many laboratory techniques.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biotin - a B-vitamin that is essential for metabolism and is bound by avidin
  • synonym: protein - a large molecule made of amino acids, essential for living organisms
