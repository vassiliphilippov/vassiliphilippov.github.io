botanize

verb
collect and study plants
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑː.t̬əˌnaɪz /; / ˈbɑːtəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈbɒt.ə.naɪz /; / ˈbɒtənaɪz / (BrE)
To collect and study plants, especially in their natural environment.

Examples

To botanize in nature
Every summer, she loves to botanize in the mountains, seeking new species.
He spent his weekends botanizing in the local forests and meadows.
The group of students botanized along the riverbank, collecting samples for their research.
During her vacation, she loved to botanise in the forests, discovering new plant species.
He has botanised extensively in Europe, contributing valuable data to the botanical society.
The group decided to botanise along the coast, documenting every flower they found.
