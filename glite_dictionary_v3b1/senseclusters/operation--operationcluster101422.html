operation

noun
manner of functioning or working
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən /; / ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the way something works, is used, or is active; the process or act of making something function

Examples

Operation of the machine
Engineers tested the operation of the generator under extreme conditions.
Pilots are trained to monitor the operation of all aircraft systems during flight.
The operation of the new washing machine is much quieter than the old model.
Building a bridge requires several complex operations to be completed in a specific order.
Regular maintenance includes cleaning and other operations necessary for optimal performance.
The new printing machine automates many operations that once required manual labor.
After the merger, many old procedures were no longer in operation.
Safety regulations remain in operation throughout the year.
The new system will soon be put into operation across the entire company.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disuse - the state of not being used or operative
  • synonym: execution - the carrying out or putting into effect of a plan, order, or course of action
  • synonym: function - the action or purpose for which a person or thing is suited or employed
  • antonym: malfunction - failure to function properly
  • synonym: management - the process of dealing with or controlling things or people
  • synonym: performance - the manner in which something functions; accomplishment of a task
  • synonym: procedure - a series of actions conducted in a certain order or manner
