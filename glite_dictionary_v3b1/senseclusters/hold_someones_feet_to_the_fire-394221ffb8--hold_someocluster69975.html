hold someone's feet to the fire

verb
put under intense pressure
6.1
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈsʌmwʌnz fit tə ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / həʊld ˈsʌmwʌnz fiːt tə ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to put someone under intense pressure to act, especially to make them take responsibility or fulfill a commitment

Examples

To hold someone's feet to the fire firmly
As a parent, she believes in holding her children’s feet to the fire when they make promises.
If we don't hold his feet to the fire, he may never complete the project.
The press continued to hold the government’s feet to the fire over the policy failures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: let off the hook - to release someone from responsibility or pressure
  • synonym: pressure - the continuous use of influence or persuasion on someone
