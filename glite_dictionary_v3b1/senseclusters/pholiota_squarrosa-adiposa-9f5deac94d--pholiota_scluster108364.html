Pholiota squarrosa-adiposa

noun
a species of yellow, scaly-capped fungus
Pronunciation: / foʊˈliː.oʊ.tə skwæˈrɔː.sə ˌæd.ɪˈpoʊ.sə / (AmE), / fəˈlɪəʊ.tə skwəˈrɒ.sə ˌæd.ɪˈpəʊ.sə / (BrE)
A species of gilled fungus with yellow, slimy caps and conspicuous tawny scales on both caps and stalks.

Examples

Pholiota squarrosa-adiposa yellow caps
Mycologists identified the yellow-capped mushrooms as Pholiota squarrosa-adiposa due to their distinctive scales.
The forest floor was dotted with clusters of Pholiota squarrosa-adiposa after the autumn rains.
The slimy surface and tawny scales are typical features of Pholiota squarrosa-adiposa.
