catch

verb
discover someone doing something (usually wrong)
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / kætʃ /; / katʃ / (BrE)
to take hold of or seize someone or something that is moving, escaping, or being pursued (including objects, animals, people)

Examples

To catch a ball
I caught my brother sneaking snacks before dinner.
She was caught cheating on the exam by her teacher.
The security guard caught the thief in the act.
After several attempts, he finally caught the escaped parrot.
She ran quickly but couldn't catch her dog before it reached the street.
The police managed to catch the burglar after a long pursuit.
Children tried to catch butterflies in the meadow during summer.
If you want to catch a mouse, you need to use bait in the trap.
The hunter managed to catch a fox using a simple trap.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capture - to take control or possession of, especially by force
  • antonym: fall behind - to not keep up with the pace of others
  • synonym: find - to discover or become aware of something or someone unexpectedly
  • synonym: grab - to quickly take or seize something
  • antonym: miss - to fail to notice or discover someone or something
  • synonym: notice - to become aware of or pay attention to someone or something
  • synonym: overtake - to go past a moving vehicle, person, or animal ahead of you
  • antonym: release - to let go or set free after catching or holding
