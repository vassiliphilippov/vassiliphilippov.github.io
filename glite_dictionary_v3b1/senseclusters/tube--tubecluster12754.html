tube

noun
electronic device with electrodes in a vacuum
8.8 4.65/m
Pronunciation: / tub / (AmE), / tjuÀêb / (BrE)
an electronic device consisting of a system of electrodes arranged in an evacuated glass or metal envelope, used for controlling electric current, amplification, or rectification

Examples

A radio tube
Many old radios still use at least one tube in their circuits.
The audio engineer replaced a faulty tube to restore the amplifier's sound quality.
Vacuum tubes were used in early computers before transistors became standard.

Synonyms

  • vacuum tube
    5.3 0.43/m
    vacuum tube is enclosed and works in a vacuum to control current, while tube is the general name for such devices.
  • electron tube
    6.9 0.01/m
    electron tube uses electron flow through a vacuum to control current, while tube can mean this general electronic device.
  • thermionic vacuum tube
    8.3 0.00/m
    thermionic vacuum tube uses a heated cathode to emit electrons for amplification, while tube is the broader term for similar devices.
  • thermionic tube
    9.8 0.00/m
    thermionic tube relies on heat to free electrons and shape current, while tube is the generic name for such devices.
  • thermionic valve
    9.9 0.00/m
    thermionic valve is the British name for a heated vacuum device used in old radios, while tube is the more general modern term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: transistor - a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals
  • synonym: vacuum tube - an electronic device with electrodes in a sealed, evacuated glass or metal envelope
