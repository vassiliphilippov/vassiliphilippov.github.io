Lactobacillaceae

noun
family of lactic acid bacteria
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlæktəˌbæsɪˈleɪsiaɪ / (AmE), / ˌlaktəʊˌbæsɪˈleɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of bacteria that ferment carbohydrates primarily into lactic acid, including lactic acid bacteria important in food production and some pathogens.

Examples

Lactobacillaceae bacteria family
Researchers identified several species of Lactobacillaceae in the patient’s digestive tract.
Some members of the Lactobacillaceae family are used as probiotics to promote gut health.
Yogurt contains live cultures of Lactobacillaceae, which help with fermentation.
