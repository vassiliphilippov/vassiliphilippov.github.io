asphaltum

noun
bitumen-based varnish
9.9
Pronunciation: / æsˈfæl.təm / (AmE), / æsˈfæl.təm / (BrE)
a dark brown or black varnish or pigment made from bitumen, used by painters and restorers

Examples

Asphaltum pigment
The artist used asphaltum to create rich, dark shadows in the painting.
Restorers often apply asphaltum as a protective layer on old artworks.
A small amount of asphaltum can deepen the color of oil paints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bitumen - a black, sticky substance used in road surfacing and as a pigment
  • synonym: varnish - a liquid applied to surfaces to form a hard, protective coating
