gracilis

noun
thigh muscle
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræsɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡræsɪlɪs / (BrE)
a long, thin muscle on the inner side of the thigh that helps with leg movement, especially bringing the legs together

Examples

Gracilis muscle
The physical therapist explained how the gracilis muscle assists in moving the leg inward.
An injury to the gracilis can make walking and running more difficult.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully repaired the torn gracilis to restore function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adductor - a muscle that moves a limb toward the body's midline
