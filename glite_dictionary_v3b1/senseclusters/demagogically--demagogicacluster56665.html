demagogically

adverb
emotionally manipulative
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛməˈɡɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌdɛməˈɡɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that appeals to emotions and prejudices rather than to reason, often to gain support or power

Examples

To speak demagogically
The leader spoke demagogically to win support from the angry crowd.
Some politicians campaign demagogically instead of presenting logical arguments.
The article criticized the speech for being delivered demagogically rather than thoughtfully.
The politician spoke demagogically to stir up the crowd's emotions during the rally.
Some media outlets report demagogically to attract more viewers and create controversy.
He argued demagogically, using exaggerated claims instead of presenting solid evidence.
The candidate spoke demagogically to stir up anger and fear among the crowd.
Critics accused the leader of acting demagogically during the heated debate.
He addressed the nation demagogically, making promises he knew he could not keep.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honestly - in a truthful and sincere way
  • synonym: manipulatively - in a way that tries to control or influence others unfairly
  • antonym: rationally - in a way that is based on reason or logic
  • synonym: sensationally - in a way that is intended to provoke public interest or excitement
