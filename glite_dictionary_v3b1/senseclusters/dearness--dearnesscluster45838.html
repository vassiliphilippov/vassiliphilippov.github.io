dearness

noun
the state of being expensive or highly valued
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪrnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɪənɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being expensive or costing a lot; the state of having a high price or value.

Examples

The dearness of goods
Due to the dearness of resources, the project was delayed until costs dropped.
She complained about the dearness of housing in the city center.
The dearness of imported goods often makes them unaffordable for many people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheapness - the quality of being low in price
  • synonym: expensiveness - the quality of costing a lot of money
