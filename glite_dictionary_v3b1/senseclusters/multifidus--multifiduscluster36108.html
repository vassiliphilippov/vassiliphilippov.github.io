multifidus

noun
spinal stabilizing muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: /mʌl.tɪ.ˈfɪ.dəs/ /; / /mʌl.ə.ˈfɪ.dəs/ (AmE), /mʌl.tɪ.ˈfɪ.dəs/ /; / /mʌl.ə.ˈfɪ.dəs/ (BrE)
a deep muscle or group of small muscles along the spine that help stabilize the vertebrae

Examples

The multifidus muscle
The multifidus plays a crucial role in supporting the lower back during movement.
Weakness in the multifidus can contribute to chronic back pain.
Physical therapists often recommend exercises to strengthen the multifidus for better spinal stability.
