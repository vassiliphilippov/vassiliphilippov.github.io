plunder

noun
stolen goods; loot
5.5 0.95/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆplÊŒndÉš / (AmE), / ËˆplÊŒndÉ™ / (BrE)
goods or money taken illegally, especially by force during war or robbery.

Examples

War plunder
He was arrested in possession of plunder stolen from the museum.
The pirates divided the plunder among themselves after the raid.
The victors returned home, their carts filled with plunder.

Synonyms

  • pillage
    7.9 0.02/m
    pillage is the violent taking of goods during war, while plunder describes stolen goods or money taken by force.
  • spoil
    7.8 2.14/m
    spoil often appears as 'spoils' meaning things taken after victory, while plunder focuses on goods taken by force or theft.
  • loot
    4.4 1.81/m
    loot is a common word for goods taken in riots or war, while plunder feels more formal and historical.
  • booty
    7.7 4.78/m
    booty names treasure taken in war or by thieves, while plunder sounds more formal and covers many kinds of stolen goods.
  • prize
    9.1 1.88/m
    prize refers to goods seized in naval warfare or legal capture, while plunder is general stolen goods taken by force.

Related Terms

  • synonym: booty - valuables taken by force or theft
  • synonym: loot - stolen goods, especially obtained by force
  • synonym: spoils - goods or benefits taken from others, especially in war
