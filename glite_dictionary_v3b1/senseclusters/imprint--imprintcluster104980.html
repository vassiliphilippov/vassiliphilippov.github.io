imprint

noun
mark made by pressure or printing
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈɪm.prɪnt / (BrE)
a mark or impression made by pressure on a surface, or the device used to make such a mark

Examples

A shoe imprint
A muddy imprint of a shoe was left on the carpet.
He found the imprint of a leaf in the soft clay.
The book's title page bore the imprint of the publisher.
The ancient coin still shows the imprint of the emperor’s face.
The artist used a special tool to create a unique imprint on each piece of clay.
The passport had an official imprint stamped on the final page.
She noticed an imprint of her ring on her finger after removing it.
The fossilized leaf left a clear imprint in the rock.
There was an imprint of a shoe in the soft sand by the riverbank.
