deemphasize

verb
to reduce importance
5.9
Pronunciation: / diˈɛm.fəˌsaɪz /; / diːˈɛmfəˌsaɪz /; / diˈɛmfəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈɛm.fə.saɪz /; / diːˈɛmfəsaɪz / (BrE)
to give less importance, attention, or prominence to something

Examples

To deemphasize a topic
The company tried to deemphasize its role in the controversy during the press conference.
Teachers should not deemphasize the importance of regular practice for language learning.
Some politicians deemphasize environmental issues in their campaigns to focus on the economy.
The manager chose to deemphasize minor errors and focus on the team's achievements.
In her speech, she deemphasized the challenges and highlighted the progress made.
Some schools are starting to deemphasize standardized testing in their admissions process.
The manager tried to deemphasize the company's recent losses during the meeting.
Some teachers deemphasize homework to encourage more creative learning.
The article deemphasizes the risks and focuses on the potential benefits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downplay - to make something seem less important
  • antonym: emphasize - to give special importance or attention to something
  • antonym: highlight - to make something stand out or be more noticeable
