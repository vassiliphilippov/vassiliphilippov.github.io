plantation

noun
historical colony or settlement
2.1
Pronunciation: / plænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / plænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(historical) A newly established colony or settlement, especially in the context of colonization of North America or Ireland.

Examples

Colonial plantation
Convicts were sometimes sent to serve on distant plantations as part of their punishment.
Historical records describe the establishment of the Ulster plantation in Ireland.
In the 17th century, many English settlers sailed to America to join the plantation in Virginia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colony - a country or area under the full or partial political control of another country
