schtick

noun
performer's habitual comic routine or gimmick
Pronunciation: / ʃtɪk / (AmE), / ʃtɪk / (BrE)
a characteristic routine, joke, or act that a performer regularly uses to attract attention or amuse people

Examples

Comedy schtick
After years on television, her schtick has become widely recognized.
His comic schtick always involves pretending to forget people's names.
The magician's latest schtick had the audience in stitches.
He always pulls the same schtick at parties, pretending to be a famous movie star.
Her presentation was so full of schtick that it felt more like a stand-up act than a regular talk.
The comedian's signature schtick never fails to get big laughs from the audience.
He relies on the same shtick in every one of his shows.
Her comedy shtick always gets the audience laughing.
Their new shtick involves juggling and singing at the same time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gag - a joke or an amusing story, especially one forming part of a comedian's act
  • synonym: gimmick - an ingenious or novel device, scheme, or stratagem, especially one designed to attract attention or increase appeal
  • synonym: routine - a regular or repeated performance, often by an entertainer
