drizzle

verb
pour liquid in fine drops over something
1.7 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdrɪ.zəl/ /; / /drɪzl/ (AmE), /ˈdrɪ.zəl/ /; / /drɪzl/ (BrE)
to pour a liquid slowly in a thin stream or in fine drops, especially over food

Examples

To drizzle sauce
After baking, drizzle the cake with a lemon glaze for extra flavor.
She likes to drizzle olive oil over her salad just before serving.
The chef drizzled caramel sauce on top of the dessert.

She likes to drizzle honey on her morning toast.
