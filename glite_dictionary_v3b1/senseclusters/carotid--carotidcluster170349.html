carotid

adjective
relating to main arteries in the neck
6.1 5.5
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈrɑː.tɪd/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrɒ.tɪd/ (BrE)
Relating to either of the two major arteries in the neck that supply blood to the head and brain.

Examples

Carotid artery disease
An ultrasound can detect narrowing of the carotid arteries, which increases stroke risk.
He was admitted for a carotid endarterectomy after his scan revealed significant blockage.
The surgeon carefully examined the carotid vessels before proceeding with the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carotid artery - either of the two main arteries in the neck supplying blood to the head and neck
