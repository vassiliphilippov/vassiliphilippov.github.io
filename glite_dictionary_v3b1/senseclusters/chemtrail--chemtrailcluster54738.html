chemtrail

noun
aircraft chemical trail
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkem.treɪl/ /; / /ˈkəm.treɪl/ (AmE), /ˈkem.treɪl/ /; / /ˈkəm.treɪl/ (BrE)
a visible trail left by an aircraft, believed by some to contain chemicals deliberately released into the atmosphere (conspiracy theory)

Examples

Secret chemtrail theory
Some conspiracy theories claim that chemtrails are used for secret government experiments.
The white lines in the sky were dismissed by scientists as ordinary contrails, not chemtrails.
She took photos of the chemtrails above her house and posted them online.
Some conspiracy theories claim that chemtrails are used to control the weather or population.
Scientists have repeatedly stated that chemtrails are simply water vapor from jet engines.
Photographs of chemtrails often appear on social media alongside discussions about their possible effects.
