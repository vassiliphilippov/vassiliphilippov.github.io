crown

noun
visible part of a tooth above the gum
Pronunciation: / kraʊn / (AmE), / kraʊn / (BrE)
The part of a tooth that is visible above the gum and covered with enamel.

Examples

Crown of a tooth
Damage to the crown can expose sensitive layers of the tooth.
Plaque often accumulates around the crown, leading to gum disease.
The dentist examined the crown of my molar for signs of decay.

Related Terms

  • antonym: root - the part of a tooth below the gum
