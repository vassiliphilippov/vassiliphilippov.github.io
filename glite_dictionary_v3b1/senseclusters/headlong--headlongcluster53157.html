headlong

adjective
done very quickly or rashly
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛd.lɒŋ / (BrE)
Done with great speed, often without thinking about the consequences; excessively quick or rash.

Examples

Headlong decision
He made a headlong dash across the street, ignoring the traffic lights.
The company's headlong expansion led to serious financial trouble.
They rushed headlong into making a decision without considering all the facts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cautious - careful to avoid danger or mistakes
  • synonym: reckless - without thinking about the danger or consequences
