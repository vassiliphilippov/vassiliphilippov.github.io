triboelectric

adjective
electricity from friction
Pronunciation: / ˌtraɪboʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (AmE), / ˌtraɪbəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (BrE)
relating to electric charge produced by friction between different materials

Examples

The triboelectric effect
The triboelectric effect explains why you sometimes get a shock after walking on carpet.
Scientists study triboelectric materials to develop new ways of generating energy.
A triboelectric charge can build up when you rub certain plastics together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electrostatic - relating to stationary electric charges
