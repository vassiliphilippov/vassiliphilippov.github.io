Cheshire cheese

noun
mild, crumbly English cheese
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛʃɚ tʃiz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛʃə tʃiːz / (BrE)
a mild, yellow English cheese with a crumbly texture, originally produced in Cheshire, England

Examples

Cheshire cheese crumbly texture
Cheshire cheese is known for its crumbly texture and mild flavor.
He enjoyed a slice of Cheshire cheese with crackers and a glass of cider.
The shop sells several traditional British cheeses, including Cheshire cheese.
