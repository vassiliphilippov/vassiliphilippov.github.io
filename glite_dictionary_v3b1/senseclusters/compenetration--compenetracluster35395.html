compenetration

noun
mutual indwelling (theology)
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmpɛnɪˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the mutual indwelling or interpenetration of the persons of the Trinity in Christian theology

Examples

Compenetration in the Trinity
The doctrine of compenetration helps explain the unity and diversity within the Trinity.
Some theologians use compenetration to describe the relationship between the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
The concept of compenetration is central to certain interpretations of Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perichoresis - the theological concept of the interrelationship of the persons of the Trinity
