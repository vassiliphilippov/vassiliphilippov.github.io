segmentation

noun
division of fertilized ovum in embryology
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛɡ.mɛnˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(embryology) the repeated division of a fertilized ovum into smaller cells during early development.

Examples

Embryonic segmentation
During early embryonic development, segmentation leads to the formation of multiple identical cells.
In many animals, segmentation begins shortly after fertilization and is essential for proper growth.
The study focused on how errors in segmentation can result in developmental abnormalities.
