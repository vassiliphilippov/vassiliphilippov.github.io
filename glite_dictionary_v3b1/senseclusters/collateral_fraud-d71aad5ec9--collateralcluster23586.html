collateral fraud

noun
legal fraud blocking fair trial
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæt̬.ər.əl frɑːd / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əl frɔːd / (BrE)
fraud that prevents a person from knowing their rights or having a fair opportunity to present their case at trial

The defendant alleged collateral fraud in the contract negotiations, claiming he was misled about his legal options.
