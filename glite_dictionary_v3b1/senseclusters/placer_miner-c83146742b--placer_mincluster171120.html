placer miner

noun
miner extracting minerals from placer deposits
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪsər ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈplɑːsə ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a miner who extracts valuable minerals, especially gold, from placer deposits by washing or dredging.

Examples

placer miner washes gold
During the gold rush, many placer miners traveled west in search of fortune.
Modern placer miner operations often use sophisticated dredging equipment to extract minerals.
The placer miner spent months sifting through riverbeds hoping to find gold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prospector - a person who searches for mineral deposits
