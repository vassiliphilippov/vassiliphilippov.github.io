spermatheca

noun
sperm storage organ
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌspɜːrməˈθiːkə / (AmE), / ˌspɜːməˈθiːkə / (BrE)
a sac or organ in female animals (especially insects and invertebrates) that stores sperm after mating until it is needed for fertilization

Examples

Female spermatheca organ
The spermatheca in female bees allows them to store sperm for months after mating.
Scientists examined the spermatheca to understand how the insect controls fertilization.
In many insects, the spermatheca plays a crucial role in reproduction.
The scientist carefully dissected the insect to study its spermatheca under the microscope.
Female bees use the spermatheca to store sperm for fertilizing eggs over time.
The presence of a well-developed spermatheca is important for the reproductive success of many invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oviduct - a tube through which eggs pass from an ovary
  • synonym: seminal receptacle - an organ in some animals that receives and stores sperm
