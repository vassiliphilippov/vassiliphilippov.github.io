bristle

verb
be full of, teem with activity
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl / (BrE)
to be full of or teeming with, often in a way that suggests lively movement or activity

Examples

The city bristled with life
The conference hall bristled with anticipation before the keynote speaker arrived.
The park bristled with excited schoolchildren during the festival.
The street bristles with new restaurants and cafés every summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abound - to be plentiful or exist in large numbers
  • synonym: overflow - to be so full that the contents go beyond the limits
