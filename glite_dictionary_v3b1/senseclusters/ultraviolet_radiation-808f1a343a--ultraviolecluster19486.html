ultraviolet radiation

noun
electromagnetic radiation beyond visible light
3.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ʌl.trə.ˈvaɪ.ə.lɪt reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ʌl.trə.ˈvaɪ.lɪt reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ʌl.trə.ˈvaɪ.ə.lət reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ʌl.trə.ˈvaɪ.lət reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays, typically responsible for causing sunburns

Examples

Ultraviolet radiation from sun
Ultraviolet radiation from the sun can damage unprotected skin and cause sunburn.
Many insects can see ultraviolet radiation, which is invisible to the human eye.
The laboratory used special lamps emitting ultraviolet radiation to sterilize equipment.
