redeemability

noun
ability to be exchanged
7.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˌdiːməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / rɪˌdiːməˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be exchanged, converted, repaid, or cancelled, especially in financial contexts

Examples

Redeemability of bonds
The redeemability of these bonds makes them attractive to investors seeking flexibility.
Some gift cards have limited redeemability and can only be used at certain stores.
The company improved the redeemability of its loyalty points to encourage more customer engagement.
The redeemability of the certificate made it attractive to cautious investors.
Some bonds have limited redeemability, restricting when they can be paid off early.
Understanding the redeemability of a loan is important before signing any agreement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irredeemability - the state of not being able to be repaid or bought back
  • synonym: liquidity - the ease with which an asset can be converted into cash
  • antonym: nonredeemability - the quality of not being able to be exchanged for something of value
