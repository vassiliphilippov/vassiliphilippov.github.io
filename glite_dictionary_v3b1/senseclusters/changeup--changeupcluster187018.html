changeup

noun
unexpected tactic change
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪndʒˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒˌʌp / (BrE)
an unexpected or deliberate change in tactics, style, or approach intended to surprise or confuse an opponent

Examples

A tactical changeup
The coach called for a changeup in strategy to keep the other team guessing.
Her sudden changeup in presentation style made the audience pay closer attention.
The company's changeup in marketing approach led to a surge in sales.

Related Terms

  • antonym: routine - a regular and unchanging procedure
  • synonym: surprise - an unexpected event or action
  • synonym: switch - a change from one thing to another
