moderately

adverb
to a certain extent; not extremely
2.8 6.68/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ëd…ö…ôtli /; / Ààm…ëÀê.d…ö.…ôt.li / (AmE), / Ààm…íd…ôr…ôtli /; / Ààm…íd.…ôr.…ôt.li / (BrE)
to a moderate degree; not too much or too little; not extremely

Examples

To be moderately impressed
She was moderately impressed with the presentation, though she saw room for improvement.
The hotel is moderately priced, making it affordable for most travelers.
The weather was moderately warm, making it perfect for a picnic.
He tries to eat moderately to stay healthy.
The weather was moderately warm for this time of year.
Their house is moderately priced compared to others in the neighborhood.

Synonyms

  • fairly
    1.0 57/m
    fairly suggests a general or common moderate amount in speech and writing, while moderately is neutral and not extreme.
  • reasonably
    2.2 11/m
    reasonably suggests a sensible or acceptable moderate level in judgment or opinion, while moderately only means not extreme.
  • mildly
    2.7 8.18/m
    mildly describes a gentle or slight degree, often for feelings or pain, while moderately means neither weak nor extreme.
  • somewhat
    2.0 67/m
    somewhat expresses a small or limited degree and feels vague or informal, while moderately denotes a clearer middle degree.
