Synonyms

  • upcoming
    1.4 19/m
    upcoming is used for planned events in the near future, while forthcoming describes something about to happen soon.
  • coming
    1.0 0.00/m
    coming refers to events expected soon in a neutral, everyday way, while forthcoming sounds slightly more formal or planned.
  • approaching
    2.3 0.15/m
    approaching describes time or events moving nearer, while forthcoming simply means they are soon to happen.
  • imminent
    4.8 9.12/m
    imminent means it will happen at any moment and feels urgent, while forthcoming is more neutral and less urgent.
  • impending
    4.0 4.80/m
    impending often suggests an unpleasant or serious event about to occur, while forthcoming is neutral and does not imply harm.
  • in the offing
    5.6 0.81/m
    in the offing suggests something is likely to appear soon, while forthcoming implies it will happen in the near future.
