goitrogenic

adjective
causing goiter
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɡɔɪtrəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌɡɔɪtrəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
causing or tending to cause the development of a goiter, especially by interfering with thyroid function

Examples

Goitrogenic foods
Some foods, such as cabbage and broccoli, are considered goitrogenic when eaten in large amounts.
Doctors may advise patients with thyroid issues to avoid goitrogenic substances in their diet.
Research continues on how goitrogenic chemicals in the environment affect human health.
