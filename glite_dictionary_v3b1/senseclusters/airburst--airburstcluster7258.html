airburst

noun
an explosion occurring in the air
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌbɝst / (AmE), / ˈɛəˌbɜːst / (BrE)
an explosion of a bomb or other projectile that occurs in the air above the ground, rather than on impact

Examples

An airburst explosion
Scientists studied the effects of the airburst caused by the meteor entering the atmosphere.
The nuclear bomb was detonated as an airburst to maximize its destructive radius.
The weapon is designed to function as an airburst, detonating before it hits the ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ground burst - an explosion occurring at or near ground level
