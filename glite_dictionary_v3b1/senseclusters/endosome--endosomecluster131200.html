endosome

noun
cellular transport compartment
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndoʊˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈɛndəsəʊm / (BrE)
a membrane-bound compartment inside a cell that sorts and transports materials taken in from outside the cell

Examples

The endosome inside cell
The endosome helps direct proteins to their correct locations within the cell.
Viruses can enter a cell by being enclosed in an endosome after attachment.
Researchers observed the movement of the endosome under a powerful microscope.
The endosome helps move nutrients and proteins to different parts of the cell.
Scientists study how viruses use the endosome to enter and infect cells.
After endocytosis, materials are delivered to the endosome for sorting and processing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exosome - a small vesicle released from a cell that can transport molecules
  • synonym: lysosome - a cell organelle containing enzymes that break down waste
