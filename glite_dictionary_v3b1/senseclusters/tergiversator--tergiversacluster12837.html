tergiversator

noun
person who evades clear answers
Pronunciation: / tɚˈdʒɪvɚˌseɪtɚ / (AmE), / təˈdʒɪvəseɪtə / (BrE)
a person who avoids giving clear or direct answers; someone who evades straightforward replies or takes ambiguous positions

Examples

A tergiversator avoids answers
During the interview, the candidate's tergiversator tendencies frustrated the panel seeking direct responses.
Rather than addressing the issue head-on, he behaved like a true tergiversator and changed the subject.
The politician was criticized as a tergiversator, never giving a straight answer at the press conference.
