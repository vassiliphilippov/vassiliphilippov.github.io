slump

verb
fall or decline suddenly and markedly
2.2 3.3
Pronunciation: /slʌmp/ (AmE), /slʌmp/ (BrE)
to decrease suddenly and by a large amount, especially in value, quantity, or activity

Examples

Sales slumped sharply
After running the marathon, she slumped onto the bench, exhausted.
Share prices slumped to their lowest level in five years.
The company's profits slumped after a series of poor investments.
Sales of the product slumped following the negative press coverage.
Share prices slumped by nearly 20% in just one day.
The economy slumped into recession after a sharp fall in demand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plummet - to fall or drop straight down at high speed
  • antonym: soar - to rise rapidly or fly high
  • antonym: surge - to rise suddenly and powerfully, especially in price or value
