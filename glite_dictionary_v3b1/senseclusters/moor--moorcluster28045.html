moor

noun
area of open, uncultivated land
Pronunciation: / mʊr / (AmE), / mɔː / (BrE)
an area of open, uncultivated land, often with peaty soil and covered with heather, bracken, and moss, commonly found in highland areas

Examples

A wild moor
Many rare birds nest on the moor, taking advantage of its remote and open habitat.
The vast moor stretched out beneath a grey sky, dotted with patches of purple heather.
They got lost while hiking across the windswept moor in northern England.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heath - an area of open uncultivated land, especially in Europe, with coarse grasses, heather, and gorse
