look on

verb
to consider or regard
3.3 0.69/m
Pronunciation: / lÊŠk É‘n / (AmE), / lÊŠk É’n / (BrE)
to regard or consider (someone or something) in a particular way

Examples

Look on mistakes as lessons
Many people look on failure as a valuable learning experience.
She tends to look on every challenge as an opportunity.
They look on him as the leader of the group.

Synonyms

    3.7 3.75/m

  • regard as
    3.3 21/m

  • view as
    3.1 33/m
    view as is often a formal, visual way to describe opinion, while look on is more general and common in speech.
  • take to be
    3.9 2.16/m

  • think of
    1.0 25/m
    think of can mean have someone in mind or an opinion, while look on specifically means to regard or consider someone.
