chaperonage

noun
supervision of youth
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæpəˌrɑnɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃæpərɒnɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or practice of supervising or accompanying someone, especially to ensure proper behavior or safety

Examples

Chaperonage at events
In the early 20th century, chaperonage was expected at social gatherings for young women.
The school enforced strict chaperonage during the field trip to ensure everyone's safety.
Some parents believe that chaperonage is necessary at teenage parties.
The students traveled under strict chaperonage during their school trip abroad.
Without proper chaperonage, the event quickly became disorganized.
The organization insisted on chaperonage to ensure the safety of all participants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the state of being free from supervision or control
  • antonym: neglect - lack of proper care or attention
  • synonym: oversight - the action of overseeing something
  • synonym: supervision - the act of overseeing or watching over someone
