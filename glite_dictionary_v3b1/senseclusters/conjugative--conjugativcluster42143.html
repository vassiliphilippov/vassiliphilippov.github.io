conjugative

adjective
genetic transfer by conjugation
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑndʒəˌɡeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒʊɡətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or capable of transferring genetic material by conjugation, especially in bacteria

Examples

Conjugative plasmid transfer
A conjugative plasmid can transfer antibiotic resistance genes between bacteria.
Researchers studied the conjugative properties of the new bacterial strain in the laboratory.
The spread of conjugative elements contributes to genetic diversity among microbial populations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonconjugative - not capable of transferring genetic material by conjugation
  • synonym: transmissible - able to be transferred from one organism to another
