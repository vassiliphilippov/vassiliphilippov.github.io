tau-minus particle

noun
a very massive lepton; a fundamental subatomic particle
9.9
Pronunciation: / taʊ ˈmaɪnəs ˈpɑːrtɪkəl / (AmE), / taʊ ˈmʌɪnəs ˈpɑːtɪkəl / (BrE)
a type of lepton with very great mass, also known as a tau lepton, which is a fundamental particle in physics

Examples

The tau-minus particle
A tau-minus particle rapidly decays into lighter particles due to its large mass.
Physicists discovered the tau-minus particle in 1975 during high-energy collision experiments.
The tau-minus particle is much heavier than the electron or the muon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lepton - a fundamental particle that does not undergo strong interactions
  • degree lower: tau neutrino - a neutrino associated with the tau lepton
