American Party

proper noun
former US political party (1850s anti-immigrant, anti-Catholic)
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈpɑːr.ti / (AmE), / əˈmɛr.ɪ.kən ˈpɑː.ti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States during the 1850s that sought to restrict the influence of immigrants and Roman Catholics

Examples

The American Party 1850s
Many historians analyze the rise of the American Party as a reaction to increased immigration.
Several members of Congress were elected under the American Party banner in the 1850s.
The American Party was also known as the Know-Nothing movement in the mid-19th century.
