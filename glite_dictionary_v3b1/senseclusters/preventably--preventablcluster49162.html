preventably

adverb
in an avoidable way
Pronunciation: / prɪˈvɛntəbli / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntəbli / (BrE)
in a manner that can be avoided or stopped from happening

Examples

Can be preventably avoided
Many diseases can be preventably reduced through vaccination and healthy habits.
The fire was preventably started by faulty wiring that should have been fixed.
Traffic jams are often preventably caused by poor planning and lack of public transport.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inevitably - in a way that is certain to happen
  • antonym: unavoidably - in a way that cannot be avoided
