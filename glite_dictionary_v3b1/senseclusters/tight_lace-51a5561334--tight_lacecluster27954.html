tight lace

verb
lace (clothing) very tightly
Pronunciation: / taɪt leɪs / (AmE), / taɪt leɪs / (BrE)
To fasten clothing or a garment very securely or tightly with laces, often to shape or support the body.

Examples

To tight lace a dress
During the fitting, the tailor helped tight lace the dress for a perfect fit.
If you tight lace your boots too much, your feet might get sore.
She had to tight lace her corset to achieve the fashionable silhouette.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loose lace - to lace clothing less tightly so it fits loosely
