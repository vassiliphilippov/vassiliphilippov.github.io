conceptualist

noun
supporter of conceptualism
8.6
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈsep.tʃuː.ə.lɪst/ /; / /kən.ˈsep.tʃə.lɪst/ (AmE), /kən.ˈsep.tʃʊə.lɪst/ /; / /kən.ˈsep.tʃə.lɪst/ (BrE)
a person who supports or believes in conceptualism, the view that universals exist only as mental concepts and not as external realities

Examples

A conceptualist philosopher
The conceptualist argued that abstract ideas have no existence outside the mind.
As a conceptualist, she rejected the notion that universals are real objects.
Many philosophers debated with the conceptualist about the nature of reality and ideas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nominalist - a person who believes universals do not exist at all, only names
  • antonym: realist - a person who believes universals exist independently of the mind
