accord and satisfaction

noun
legal settlement of debt for less than owed
7.2
Pronunciation: / əˈkɔrd ənd ˌsætɪsˈfækʃən / (AmE), / əˈkɔːd ənd ˌsætɪsˈfækʃən / (BrE)
a legal agreement in which a debt is settled by the debtor paying less than what was originally owed, with the creditor accepting this payment as full satisfaction and relinquishing any further claim.

Examples

Accord and satisfaction agreement
By accepting a reduced payment as accord and satisfaction, the creditor gave up the right to sue for the remaining amount.
The contract dispute ended with accord and satisfaction, satisfying both parties without further litigation.
The parties reached an accord and satisfaction to settle the disputed payment out of court.
