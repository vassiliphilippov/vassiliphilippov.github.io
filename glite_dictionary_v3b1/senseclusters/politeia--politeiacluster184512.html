politeia

noun
citizenship rights
Pronunciation: / pəˌlɪˈteɪə / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈteɪə / (BrE)
the rights, duties, and legal status of citizens, especially in ancient Greek society

Examples

Citizen politeia
In ancient Athens, only men of certain birth could claim politeia and participate in government.
The philosopher discussed the concept of politeia in his writings about justice and society.
Gaining politeia allowed individuals to vote and hold public office in the city-state.
