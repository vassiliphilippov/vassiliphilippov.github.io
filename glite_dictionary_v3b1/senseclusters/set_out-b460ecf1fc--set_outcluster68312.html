set out

verb
to begin a journey or activity
Pronunciation: / sɛt aʊt / (AmE), / sɛt aʊt / (BrE)
to start a project, task, or activity; to begin doing something with a particular aim

Examples

Set out to achieve
After months of planning, she finally set out to start her own business.
The team set out to solve the complex problem with a clear strategy.
They set out on their hike before sunrise to avoid the afternoon heat.
She set out to write her first novel with great enthusiasm.
The explorers set out in search of new lands.
We set out at dawn to climb the mountain before noon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: begin - to start doing something
  • synonym: depart - to leave, typically to start a journey
  • synonym: embark - to begin a journey or enterprise
  • antonym: finish - to bring an activity or journey to an end
  • synonym: start - to begin doing or experiencing something
