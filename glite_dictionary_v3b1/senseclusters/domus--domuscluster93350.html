domus

noun
Roman house
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊməs / (AmE), / ˈdəʊməs / (BrE)
an ancient Roman house, especially one owned by a wealthy family

Examples

Roman domus with mosaics
The wealthy merchant's domus was decorated with beautiful mosaics and frescoes.
Archaeologists uncovered the remains of a domus in the center of the ancient city.
Each domus had an open courtyard called an atrium at its heart.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insula - an apartment building or block in ancient Rome
  • synonym: villa - a large and luxurious country house in ancient Rome
