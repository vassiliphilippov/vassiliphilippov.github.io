elevation

noun
visual depth in interface design
6.7 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / cc5bl59c8ve6a8359n / (AmE), / cc5bl6ac8ve6a8359n / (BrE)
(in digital interface design) the perceived vertical position or visual depth of an element, shown by shadows, layering, and z-order to indicate hierarchy and interactivity.

Examples

UI elevation effect
The app uses elevation to create visual depth and indicate which buttons are interactive.
She adjusted the card's elevation to cast a softer shadow and reduce visual clutter.
Material Design defines numeric elevation levels that determine shadow size and z-order in layouts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: visual depth - the apparent distance or layering of elements created by shadows and perspective
  • synonym: z-index - a CSS property controlling stacking order of elements on the z-axis
