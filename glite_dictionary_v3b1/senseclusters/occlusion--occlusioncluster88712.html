occlusion

noun
contact between upper and lower teeth
7.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkluː.ʒən/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkluː.ʒən/ (BrE)
(Dentistry) The alignment and contact between the teeth of the upper and lower jaws when the mouth is closed.

Examples

Dental occlusion
After the braces were removed, her occlusion had greatly improved.
Dentists evaluate a patient's occlusion during routine check-ups to detect potential issues.
Proper occlusion is essential for efficient chewing and clear speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: malocclusion - abnormal alignment of the teeth when the jaws are closed
