dysthymia

noun
mild long-term depression
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈθaɪmiə / (AmE), / dɪsˈθɪmɪə / (BrE)
A mild but long-lasting form of depression, characterized by a chronic low mood and other depressive symptoms.

Examples

Chronic dysthymia symptoms
Her doctor diagnosed her with dysthymia after years of persistent low mood.
People with dysthymia may not realize they have a treatable condition.
Unlike major depression, dysthymia involves symptoms that are less severe but longer lasting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depression - A mental disorder marked by persistent sadness and loss of interest
