long hundredweight

noun
British weight unit, 112 pounds
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈhʌndrədˌweɪt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈhʌndrɪdˌweɪt / (BrE)
a British unit of weight equal to 112 pounds (approximately 50.8 kilograms), used chiefly in non-metric contexts

Examples

A long hundredweight unit
Coal was often sold by the long hundredweight in nineteenth-century Britain.
One long hundredweight is equivalent to 112 pounds or eight stone.
The shipment totalled twenty long hundredweights, as recorded on the invoice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short hundredweight - an American unit of weight equal to 100 pounds
