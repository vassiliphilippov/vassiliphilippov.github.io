sea-puss

noun
seaward undercurrent after waves break
Pronunciation: / ˈsiˌpʊs / (AmE), / ˈsiːˌpʊs / (BrE)
A strong seaward undercurrent created after waves have broken on the shore, capable of pulling swimmers away from the beach.

Examples

The sea-puss current
Be careful swimming here—the sea-puss can pull you out to sea quickly.
Caught in a sudden sea-puss, he struggled to swim back to shore.
Lifeguards often warn about the dangers of the sea-puss near this beach.
