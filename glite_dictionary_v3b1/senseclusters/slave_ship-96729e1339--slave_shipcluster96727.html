slave ship

noun
ship for transporting enslaved people
5.6
Pronunciation: / sleɪv ʃɪp / (AmE), / sleɪv ʃɪp / (BrE)
a ship used in the past to transport enslaved people from their homeland, especially as part of the transatlantic slave trade

Examples

A slave ship voyage
Historians have studied the routes taken by many slave ships during the eighteenth century.
The slave ship carried hundreds of people across the Atlantic in terrible conditions.
The exhibit featured artifacts recovered from a sunken slave ship.
