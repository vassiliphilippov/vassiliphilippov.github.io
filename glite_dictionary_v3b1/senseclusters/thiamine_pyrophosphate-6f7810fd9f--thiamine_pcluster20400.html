thiamine pyrophosphate

noun
coenzyme involved in cellular respiration
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈθaɪ.ə.miːn paɪ.roʊ.ˈfɑːs.feɪt/ /; / /ˈθaɪ.ə.miːn paɪ.rə.ˈfɑːs.feɪt/ (AmE), /ˈθaɪ.ə.miːn paɪ.rəʊ.ˈfɒs.feɪt/ /; / /ˈθaɪ.ə.miːn paɪ.rə.ˈfɒs.feɪt/ (BrE)
a coenzyme derived from thiamine (vitamin B1) that is essential in cellular respiration, particularly in the Krebs cycle

Examples

Thiamine pyrophosphate coenzyme function
Thiamine pyrophosphate is required for the proper functioning of several enzymes in the Krebs cycle.
Deficiency in thiamine pyrophosphate can lead to neurological problems due to impaired cellular respiration.
The enzyme's activity depends on the presence of thiamine pyrophosphate as a cofactor.
