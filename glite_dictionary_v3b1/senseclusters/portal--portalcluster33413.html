portal

noun
a vein carrying blood to the liver
8.1 0.47/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…îrt…ôl / (AmE), / Ààp…îÀêt…ôl / (BrE)
a short vein that carries blood from parts of the digestive system to the liver, especially the hepatic portal vein

Examples

Portal vein
Any blockage in the portal can lead to serious liver complications.
Doctors measured the pressure in the portal to assess the patient's liver function.
The portal carries nutrient-rich blood from the intestines to the liver for processing.

Synonyms

  • hepatic portal vein
    6.8 0.00/m
    hepatic portal vein carries blood from the gut to the liver in medical contexts, while portal is a shorter, general name.
  • portal vein
    4.3 0.04/m
    portal vein brings blood from digestive organs into the liver in everyday medical talk, while portal is the brief, informal form.
  • vena portae
    8.4 0.00/m
    vena portae is the Latin anatomical term used in textbooks, while portal is the common English label used by doctors and patients.
