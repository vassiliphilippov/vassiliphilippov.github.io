portal

noun
a vein carrying blood to the liver
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔrtəl / (AmE), / ˈpɔːtəl / (BrE)
a short vein that carries blood from parts of the digestive system to the liver, especially the hepatic portal vein

Examples

Portal vein
Any blockage in the portal can lead to serious liver complications.
Doctors measured the pressure in the portal to assess the patient's liver function.
The portal carries nutrient-rich blood from the intestines to the liver for processing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hepatic portal vein - the main vein carrying blood from the digestive organs to the liver
