bombastic

adjective
pompous or inflated in style
1.1
Pronunciation: / bɑmˈbæstɪk / (AmE), / bɒmˈbæstɪk / (BrE)
using language in a pompous or inflated way, with little real meaning; high-sounding but with little substance.

Examples

A bombastic speech
His writing style became increasingly bombastic and less engaging.
She accused him of using bombastic language to hide the lack of real ideas.
The politician’s bombastic speech failed to impress the skeptical audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low opinion of one's importance
  • synonym: pompous - affectedly grand, solemn, or self-important
