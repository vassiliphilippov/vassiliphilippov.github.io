underwriting

noun
accepting liability formally
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌraɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌraɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of signing a document to accept responsibility or liability, especially in a formal or historical context

Examples

Contract underwriting
In the past, underwriting was required to make a contract legally binding.
The underwriting of important documents was a common practice in historical business transactions.
Without proper underwriting, the agreement would not be recognized by the authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endorsement - the act of signing a document to show approval or support
  • antonym: rejection - the act of refusing to accept or approve something
