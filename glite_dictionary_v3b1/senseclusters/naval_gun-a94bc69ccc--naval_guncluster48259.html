naval gun

noun
large gun carried on a warship
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪvəl ɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl ɡʌn / (BrE)
a large gun designed to be mounted on a warship, typically used as part of naval artillery

Examples

A naval gun on ship
During the battle, the ship's naval gun delivered sustained fire against enemy vessels.
Recent upgrades have increased the accuracy of the naval gun on this class of warship.
The destroyer was equipped with a modern naval gun capable of firing at both sea and air targets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artillery - large-caliber guns used in warfare, on land or sea
  • synonym: cannon - a large, heavy gun typically mounted on wheels or a fixed base
