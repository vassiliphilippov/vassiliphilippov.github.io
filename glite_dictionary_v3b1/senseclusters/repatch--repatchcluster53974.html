repatch

verb
to patch again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈpætʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpætʃ / (BrE)
to apply a new patch or repair to something that has already been patched before

Examples

To repatch a roof
After the storm damaged the roof again, the workers had to repatch the same area.
The software team needed to repatch the program because the first update did not solve the problem.
He decided to repatch his old jeans when the original patch started to come loose.
