photographic film

noun
material used to capture photographic images
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtəˈɡræfɪk fɪlm / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈɡræfɪk fɪlm / (BrE)
A thin material coated with a light-sensitive emulsion, used in cameras to capture images or make negatives and transparencies.

Examples

A photographic film roll
Many classic movies were originally recorded on photographic film rather than digital media.
She developed her photographic film in a darkroom to produce high-quality black-and-white prints.
The photographer loaded a roll of photographic film into his vintage camera before starting the shoot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: film - A thin layer or coating, especially as used in photography
