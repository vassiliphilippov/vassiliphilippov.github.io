fink

verb
take over a striker's job
8.3
Pronunciation: / fɪŋk / (AmE), / fɪŋk / (BrE)
(of a person) to take over the job of a worker who is on strike, often considered an act of betrayal by other workers.

Examples

To fink during a strike
During the union strike, he was criticized for finking on his coworkers by taking their shifts.
She lost friends after she finked during the factory walkout.
Some workers refused to fink, showing solidarity with those on the picket line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scab - a person who works or replaces striking workers
