sink like a stone

verb
fail or fall rapidly, completely
Pronunciation: / sɪŋk laɪk ə stoʊn / (AmE), / sɪŋk laɪk ə stəʊn / (BrE)
to fail, disappear, or fall very quickly and completely; to literally or figuratively descend rapidly with no resistance

Examples

To sink like a stone quickly
His suggestion at the meeting sank like a stone—no one even responded.
Rumors of the company's bankruptcy caused its stock price to sink like a stone.
The new product sank like a stone after its disappointing launch and poor reviews.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bomb - to fail completely, especially in entertainment
  • synonym: flop - to fail badly
