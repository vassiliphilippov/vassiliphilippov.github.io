Rhynchocephalia

noun
order of reptiles: tuataras and extinct relatives
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪŋkoʊsɪˈfeɪliə / (AmE), / ˌrɪŋkəʊsɪˈfiːlɪə / (BrE)
an order of reptiles comprising the tuataras and various extinct species from the Middle Triassic onward

Examples

The Rhynchocephalia order
During the Mesozoic era, the Rhynchocephalia were much more widespread than they are now.
Fossil evidence helps scientists trace the evolutionary history of the Rhynchocephalia.
The Rhynchocephalia are represented today only by the tuatara of New Zealand.
