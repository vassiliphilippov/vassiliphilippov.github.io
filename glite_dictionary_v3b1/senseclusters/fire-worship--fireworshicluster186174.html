fire-worship

noun
the worship of fire as a deity or sacred force
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪər ˌwɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfaɪə ˌwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
the practice of worshipping fire as a deity or sacred element, especially in certain religions or ancient cultures

Examples

Fire-worship rituals
Scholars have studied the rituals associated with fire-worship in Zoroastrianism.
Some ancient civilizations practiced fire-worship as a central part of their religion.
The tradition of fire-worship continues among some communities today, especially during special festivals.
