overproof

verb
to let dough rise too long
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpruːf / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpruːf / (BrE)
to allow yeast dough or batter to rise for too long, causing it to lose strength or collapse

Examples

To overproof dough
If you overproof the dough, it may collapse and lose its airy texture.
Bakers must be careful not to overproof bread during the final rise.
The rolls were overproofed and turned out flat after baking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overferment - to ferment for too long, resulting in loss of quality
  • antonym: underproof - to allow dough to rise for too short a time
