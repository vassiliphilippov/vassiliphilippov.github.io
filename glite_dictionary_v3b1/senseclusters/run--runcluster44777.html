run

noun
period of continuous operation
1.9
Pronunciation: / rʌn / (AmE), / rʌn / (BrE)
a continuous period or series of events, activities, or successes

Examples

A winning run
The computer finished its overnight run without any errors.
The factory scheduled a run of sixteen hours to meet the tight deadline.
There was a problem during the second run of the printing press last night.
The factory achieved a record run of 5,000 cars before maintenance was needed.
This machine's run today produced enough bricks for the entire project.
We completed a six-hour run of bottling before switching product lines.
Her team was on a winning run until last night’s defeat.
That store enjoyed a brief run of popularity after its grand opening.
The new musical had a remarkable run of two years in London.

  • synonym: streak - a consecutive series of similar events, often referring to wins or successes
  • synonym: stretch - a continuous period or extent of something
