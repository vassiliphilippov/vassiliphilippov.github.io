noun
keystroke biometric identification

(in computing and security) the characteristic pattern of a person's keystrokes (timing, rhythm, pressure) used as a biometric for identifying or verifying them; also the practice of authenticating users by analysing those patterns.

Examples

Typing pattern check
The bank's login system analyses typing rhythms to block impostors who have stolen passwords.
Researchers improved authentication accuracy by combining typing patterns with mouse movement and contextual data.
She opted out after learning the app recorded her typing cadence and used it to identify users.
