series

noun
several sports contests between same teams
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriːz / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriːz / (BrE)
(in sports) several contests played in succession between the same teams, often to determine an overall winner.

Examples

A baseball series
After losing the series, the team analyzed each game's mistakes.
The Yankees won the first two games of the series against the Red Sox.
The basketball series will decide which team advances to the finals.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: match - a single contest between two teams or players
  • degree higher: tournament - a series of contests involving multiple teams or players
