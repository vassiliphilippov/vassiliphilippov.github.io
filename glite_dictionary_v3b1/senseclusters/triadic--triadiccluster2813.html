triadic

adjective
relating to triads
7.3
Pronunciation: / traɪˈædɪk / (AmE), / traɪˈadɪk / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of a triad, especially in music, where chords are made of three notes.

Examples

Triadic chord
The composer used simple triadic harmony throughout the entire piano sonata last year.
Many folk songs are built on triadic chords, making them easy to play on guitar.
Her analysis focused on the triadic structure of the piece, highlighting its three-note chords.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dyadic - relating to or consisting of two parts or elements
  • synonym: triad - a group of three, especially a chord of three notes in music
