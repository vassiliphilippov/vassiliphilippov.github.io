open interval

noun
mathematical interval excluding endpoints
6.6 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈəʊ.pən ˈɪn.tər.vəl/ /; / /ˈoʊ.pən ˈɪn.tər.vəl/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.pən ˈɪn.tə.vəl/ (BrE)
An interval in mathematics that includes all numbers between its endpoints but not the endpoints themselves.

Examples

An open interval in math
A function may be continuous on an open interval but not at the endpoints.
The solution to the inequality is the open interval from 0 to 1.
We considered all values lying within the open interval (3, 5) for our calculations.
