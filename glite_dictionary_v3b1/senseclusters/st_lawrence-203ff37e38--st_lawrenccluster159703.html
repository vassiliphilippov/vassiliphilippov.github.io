St. Lawrence

proper noun
Christian martyr; 3rd-century Roman saint
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈlɔːrəns / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒrəns / (BrE)
A Christian martyr of the 3rd century, famed for his charitable acts and his death by being roasted on a gridiron in Rome.

Examples

St. Lawrence the martyr
According to legend, St. Lawrence was martyred by being roasted alive on a gridiron.
Many churches are named after St. Lawrence in honor of his legacy.
People celebrate the feast of St. Lawrence on August 10th every year.
