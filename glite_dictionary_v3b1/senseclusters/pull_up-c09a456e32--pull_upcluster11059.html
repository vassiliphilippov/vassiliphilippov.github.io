pull up

verb
remove with effort or force
Pronunciation: / pʊl ʌp / (AmE), / pʊl ʌp / (BrE)
to remove or extract something, often with effort; can also mean removing something in an abstract sense

Examples

To pull up weeds
Investigators tried to pull up hidden details from the encrypted files.
She had to pull up all the weeds from the garden before planting new flowers.
The dentist decided to pull up the damaged tooth to stop the pain.
At the end of the race, she pulled up to catch her breath.
He had to pull up his car suddenly when a dog ran into the road.
They plan to pull up the old carpet before installing a new one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accelerate - to move or cause to move faster
  • synonym: extract - to remove something, especially by effort
  • synonym: remove - to take something away or off
  • synonym: stop - to bring something to a halt
