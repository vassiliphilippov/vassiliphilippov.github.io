shoebird

noun
large stork-like bird with a broad bill
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuːbɝːd / (AmE), / ˈʃuːbɜːd / (BrE)
a large stork-like bird (Balaeniceps rex) found in the valley of the White Nile, known for its broad, shoe-shaped bill

Examples

The shoebird bird
Birdwatchers travel long distances hoping to catch a glimpse of the elusive shoebird.
Its massive bill gives the shoebird a striking and somewhat prehistoric appearance.
The shoebird can be found in the marshes of central tropical Africa.
