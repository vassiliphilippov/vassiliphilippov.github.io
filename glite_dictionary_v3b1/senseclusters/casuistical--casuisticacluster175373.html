casuistical

adjective
relating to ethical problem-solving
Pronunciation: / ˌkæʒuˈɪstɪkəl /; / ˌkæzjuˈɪstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌkazjʊˈɪstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the use of subtle or overly fine reasoning to resolve moral or ethical problems, sometimes in a way that seems deceptive or overly complicated

Examples

A casuistical argument
His casuistical reasoning failed to convince those who preferred a more straightforward approach.
The committee relied on casuistical analysis to address the complex ethical dilemma.
The debate turned casuistical as they argued over subtle moral distinctions.
Academic debates can sometimes devolve into casuistical hairsplitting over minor points.
She was accused of casuistical thinking when she justified her actions with convoluted logic.
The lawyer's casuistical arguments left the jury confused rather than persuaded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: casuistic - relating to the use of ethical principles to resolve moral problems
  • synonym: casuistry - the use of subtle reasoning in cases of conscience or moral dilemmas
  • antonym: principled - acting in accordance with moral principles
