coigne

noun
keystone or projecting stone (architecture)
Pronunciation: / kɔɪn / (AmE), / kɔɪn / (BrE)
the keystone or projecting stone at the corner of a building or in an arch, especially in architecture.

Examples

Stone coigne corner
Ancient cathedrals often feature intricately carved coignes at their corners and arches.
The mason placed the coigne at the apex of the stone arch, completing its form.
With careful precision, he shaped the coigne to fit perfectly into the structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keystone - the central stone at the summit of an arch, locking the whole together
