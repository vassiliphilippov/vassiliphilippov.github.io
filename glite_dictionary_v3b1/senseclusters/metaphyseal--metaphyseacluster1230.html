metaphyseal

adjective
of bone metaphysis
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈfaɪsiəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfaɪsiəl / (BrE)
relating to the metaphysis, the region between the shaft and end of a long bone where growth occurs

Examples

Metaphyseal bone region
The doctor explained that a metaphyseal injury can affect bone growth in children.
X-rays revealed a metaphyseal lesion near the knee joint.
A metaphyseal fracture is more common in young, developing bones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diaphyseal - relating to the shaft of a long bone
  • antonym: epiphyseal - relating to the end part of a long bone
