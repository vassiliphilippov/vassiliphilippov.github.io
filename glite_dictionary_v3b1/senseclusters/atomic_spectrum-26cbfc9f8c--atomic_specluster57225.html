atomic spectrum

noun
spectrum of radiation from electron transitions in atoms
4.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtɑː.mɪk ˈspek.trəm/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtɒ.mɪk ˈspek.trəm/ (BrE)
(Physics) A spectrum of radiation emitted or absorbed by an atom, due to electron transitions between energy levels, with lines characteristic of each element.

Examples

The atomic spectrum lines
By analyzing the atomic spectrum produced in the lab, researchers confirmed the sample was pure sodium.
Each element has a unique atomic spectrum, allowing scientists to identify it in distant stars.
The atomic spectrum of hydrogen is well known for its distinct series of lines called the Balmer series.
