thrust of the argument

noun
main point of an argument
Pronunciation: / θrʌst əv ði ˈɑːrɡjəmənt / (AmE), / θrʌst əv ði ˈɑːɡjʊmənt / (BrE)
the central point or main focus of an argument, discussion, or piece of reasoning

Examples

The thrust of the argument is clear
Her analysis captured the thrust of the argument without getting lost in the specifics.
I missed the thrust of the argument, but I caught some interesting details.
The thrust of the argument was that more research is needed before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gist - the essential or main point of a matter
  • synonym: main idea - the most important or central thought of a text or discussion
