angiocarp

noun
tree or plant with fruit in a shell or husk

Pronunciation: /ˈæn.dʒiː.oʊ.kɑːrp/ /; / /ˈæn.dʒiː.ə.kɑːrp/ (AmE), /ˈæn.dʒiː.əʊ.kɑːp/ /; / /ˈæn.dʒiː.ə.kɑːp/ (BrE)
A plant or tree that bears fruit enclosed within a shell, husk, or involucre.

Examples

An angiocarp plant
Many botanists classify fruits like acorns as products of an angiocarp.
The hazel is an example of an angiocarp because its nuts are encased within a hard shell.
Walnut trees are angiocarps by virtue of their fruit being surrounded by a tough husk.
