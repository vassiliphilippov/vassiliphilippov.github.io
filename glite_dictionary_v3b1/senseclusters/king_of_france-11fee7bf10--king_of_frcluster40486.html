King of France

noun
historical French monarch
Pronunciation: / kɪŋ əv fræns / (AmE), / kɪŋ əv frɑːns / (BrE)
the male sovereign who ruled France as its monarch before the abolition of the monarchy

Examples

The King of France
Louis XVI was the last King of France before the French Revolution.
The King of France resided in the magnificent Palace of Versailles.
Throughout history, the King of France wielded great influence over European politics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: king of England - male sovereign ruler of England
  • antonym: queen of France - female consort of the king or reigning queen of France
