red giant

noun
large, old luminous star with low temperature
4.6 7.2
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / rɛd ˈdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
A very large, old star with a low surface temperature and a diameter much greater than that of the sun.

Examples

A red giant star
After exhausting its hydrogen fuel, the star expanded into a red giant.
Betelgeuse is a famous example of a red giant visible in the night sky.
Scientists study red giants to understand the later stages of stellar evolution.
