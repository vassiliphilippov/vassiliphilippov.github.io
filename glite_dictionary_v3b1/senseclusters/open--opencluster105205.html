open

adjective
not requiring union membership (workplace)
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən / (BrE)
(of a workplace) not requiring employees to join a labor union as a condition of employment.

Examples

Open shop policy
Many workers appreciated being able to choose in the open workplace.
The company operates an open shop so employees are not obligated to join the union.
The debate over open versus closed shops has lasted for decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closed - (of a workplace) requiring union membership; opposite of 'open' in labor relations context
