piezoelectric effect

noun
generation of electricity by mechanical pressure on crystals
8.8 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / piËŒÉ›zoÊŠÉªËˆlÉ›ktrÉªk ÉªËˆfÉ›kt / (AmE), / piËŒÉ›zÉ™ÊŠÉªËˆlÉ›ktrÉªk ÉªËˆfÉ›kt / (BrE)
The generation of an electric charge by applying mechanical pressure to certain crystals, or the change in shape that occurs in such crystals when an electric field is applied.

Examples

The piezoelectric effect in crystals
Lighters often use the piezoelectric effect to generate a spark for ignition.
Scientists demonstrated the piezoelectric effect by compressing a crystal and measuring the resulting voltage.
The piezoelectric effect is fundamental to the operation of quartz watches.

Synonyms

  • piezoelectricity
    9.9 0.01/m
    piezoelectricity is the electric output produced when crystals are pressed, while piezoelectric effect describes that physical process and its reverse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: piezoresistance - change in the electrical resistance of a material due to mechanical stress
