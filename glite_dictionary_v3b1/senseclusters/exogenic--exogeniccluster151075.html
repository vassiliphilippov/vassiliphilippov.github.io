exogenic

adjective
originating from outside a system
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.soʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛk.səʊˈdʒɛn.ɪk / (BrE)
originating or produced from outside a system, especially relating to geological processes operating on the Earth's surface.

Examples

Exogenic geological process
Erosion is an exogenic process driven mainly by water, wind, and ice.
Impacts from meteorites are a classic example of exogenic phenomena affecting planetary surfaces.
The scientist studied how exogenic forces shape mountain landscapes over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endogenic - originating or produced from within a system, especially geological processes within the Earth
