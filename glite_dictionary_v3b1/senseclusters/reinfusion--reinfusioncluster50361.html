reinfusion

noun
returning fluid to body
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
the process of putting a fluid, especially blood, back into the body after it has been removed or collected

Examples

Reinfusion of blood
After surgery, the doctor performed a reinfusion of the patient's own blood to aid recovery.
The reinfusion of stem cells is a key step in some cancer treatments.
Modern medical equipment makes the process of reinfusion safer and more efficient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transfusion - the process of transferring blood or blood products into a person's circulation
