lurch

noun
decisive defeat in a game (esp. cribbage)
5.7
Pronunciation: / lɝːtʃ / (AmE), / lɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a decisive defeat in a game, especially one in which the loser fails to score any points, used particularly in cribbage

Examples

A lurch loss
He suffered a lurch in the final round, failing to score a single point.
If you don’t play carefully, you might end up with a lurch against you.
Losing by such a lurch was a rare humiliation in the club's cribbage tournament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defeat - the act of being beaten in a contest or game
  • antonym: victory - win or success in a contest or game
