Heterodera

noun
plant-parasitic nematode
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˈroʊdərə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrəʊdərə / (BrE)
a genus of plant-parasitic nematodes that infest roots and cause crop diseases, especially in potatoes and other vegetables

Examples

Heterodera infestation
Farmers monitor their fields for signs of Heterodera to prevent crop losses.
The presence of Heterodera in the soil can severely damage potato and sugar beet crops.
Researchers are developing new methods to control Heterodera infestations in agriculture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyst nematode - a type of nematode that forms cysts on plant roots
  • antonym: root-knot nematode - a nematode that causes galls on plant roots
