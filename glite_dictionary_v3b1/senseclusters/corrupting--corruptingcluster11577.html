corrupting

adjective
having a harmful or contaminating influence
6.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈrʌp.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrʌp.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
causing someone or something to become morally or ethically worse; having a harmful influence on character or behavior

Examples

Corrupting influence
He warned of the corrupting nature of money in politics.
Some believe that violent video games have a corrupting effect on children.
The corrupting influence of unchecked power can erode even the strongest values.
Many people feared that the corrupting influence of money would ruin the young politician.
Some critics argued that the film had a corrupting effect on impressionable audiences.
There are concerns about the corrupting power of unchecked authority in large organizations.
He avoided bad company, fearing their corrupting influence on his character.
Some parents worry about the corrupting effects of violent video games on children.
The corrupting power of money can change a person's values over time.
