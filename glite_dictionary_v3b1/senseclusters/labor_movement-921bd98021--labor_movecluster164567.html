labor movement

noun
organized efforts by workers for better conditions
3.6 1.92/m
Pronunciation: / Ààle…™b…ö ÀåmuÀêvm…ônt / (AmE), / Ààle…™b…ô ÀåmuÀêvm…ônt / (BrE)
an organized effort by workers, often through unions, to improve working conditions, wages, and rights, or the leaders and organizations involved in such efforts

Examples

The labor movement grows
Historians often study the impact of the labor movement on twentieth-century social reforms.
Leaders of the labor movement organized strikes to demand fair wages and reasonable hours.
The labor movement played a crucial role in securing better working conditions for employees.

Synonyms

  • trade union movement
    2.7 0.18/m
    trade union movement uses formal unions and collective bargaining to win workplace improvements, while labor movement includes wider worker campaigns and political action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: labor union - an organized association of workers formed to protect and further their rights and interests
  • synonym: trade union - an organization formed by workers to protect their interests
