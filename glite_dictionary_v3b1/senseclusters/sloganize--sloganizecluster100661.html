sloganize

verb
to use slogans
Pronunciation: / ˈsloʊɡəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsləʊɡənaɪz / (BrE)
to express, promote, or simplify something by turning it into a slogan, often making it simpler or catchier

Examples

To sloganize a product
The marketing team decided to sloganize the new product to attract more customers.
Politicians often sloganize complex issues to make them easier for the public to understand.
Critics argued that the company tried to sloganize its environmental efforts without real action.
The campaign team tried to sloganize the candidate's message to appeal to more voters.
It is easy to sloganize complicated issues, but real solutions require deeper understanding.
Some media outlets tend to sloganize debates, making them seem simpler than they are.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advertise - to make something known to the public, often for commercial purposes
  • antonym: complicate - to make something more complex or difficult
  • synonym: oversimplify - to make something simpler than it really is
  • synonym: promote - to support or encourage the growth of something
  • synonym: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
