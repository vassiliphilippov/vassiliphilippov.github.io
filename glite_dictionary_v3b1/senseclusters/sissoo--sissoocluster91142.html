sissoo

noun
East Indian timber-yielding tree
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪsuː / (AmE), / ˈsɪsuː / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Dalbergia sissoo) whose leaves are used for fodder and which yields a durable dark brown timber used in shipbuilding and railroad ties.

Examples

Sissoo tree leaves
Farmers often feed the leaves of the sissoo tree to their livestock.
Furniture made from sissoo wood is known for its durability and natural beauty.
The wood of the sissoo is prized for its strength and resistance to decay.
