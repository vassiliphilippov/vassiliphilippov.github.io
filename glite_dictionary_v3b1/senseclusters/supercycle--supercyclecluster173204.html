supercycle

noun
long market trend
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌsaɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəˌsaɪkəl / (BrE)
a long-term period of significant expansion or contraction in a market or industry, often lasting several years or decades

Examples

Market supercycle
Many analysts believe we are entering a new supercycle for renewable energy.
The last commodity supercycle led to a decade of high prices and investment.
Economists debate whether the current growth is part of a true supercycle or just a short-term trend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth
  • antonym: recession - a period of economic decline
