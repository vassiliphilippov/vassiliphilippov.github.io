gas turbine

noun
engine using combusted gas to produce power
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡæs ˈtɝːˌbaɪn / (AmE), / ɡæs ˈtɜːˌbaɪn / (BrE)
A type of engine that produces mechanical power by burning fuel in compressed air and expanding the resulting hot gases through a turbine.

Examples

A gas turbine engine
Engineers are developing more efficient gas turbine technologies for renewable energy sources.
Many modern airplanes are powered by gas turbines that enable high-speed flight.
The power plant installed a new gas turbine to increase its energy output.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jet engine - an engine that generates thrust by expelling a jet of gas
