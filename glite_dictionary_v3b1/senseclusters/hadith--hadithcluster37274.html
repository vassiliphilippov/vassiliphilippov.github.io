hadith

noun
report of Muhammad's sayings or actions
Pronunciation: / həˈdiθ / (AmE), / haˈdiːθ /; / həˈdiːθ / (BrE)
a record or collection of the sayings, actions, and approvals of the Prophet Muhammad, used as a source of Islamic law and guidance

Examples

The hadith collection
Many Muslims refer to the hadith when seeking guidance about ethical issues.
The scholar spent years studying various hadith to understand Islamic jurisprudence.
This hadith emphasizes the importance of honesty in daily life.
Many Muslims look to the hadith for guidance on daily life and religious practice.
Scholars have long debated the authenticity of certain hadith collections.
The hadith provides context for understanding verses in the Quran.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quran - the central religious text of Islam, believed to be the word of God as dictated to Muhammad
  • synonym: sunnah - the traditional social and legal customs and practices of the Islamic community, based on Muhammad's example
