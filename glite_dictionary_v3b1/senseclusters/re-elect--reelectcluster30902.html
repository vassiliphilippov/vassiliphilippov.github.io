re-elect

verb
to elect someone again
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriː ɪˈlɛkt / (AmE), / ˌriː ɪˈlɛkt / (BrE)
To elect (someone) to a position again, especially after their previous term has ended.

Examples

To re-elect a leader
After a successful campaign, he was re-elected by a large majority.
Many hope the board will re-elect her as chairperson next year.
The city voted to re-elect its popular mayor for a third term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elect - to choose someone for a position by voting
