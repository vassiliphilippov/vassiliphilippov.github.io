overqualified

adjective
exceeding job requirements
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkwɑːlɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkwɒlɪfaɪd / (BrE)
having more education, skills, or experience than what is needed for a particular job or position

Examples

Overqualified for job
He was overqualified for the job, so the company worried he might leave soon.
Many recent graduates feel overqualified for entry-level positions in their field.
Despite being overqualified, she accepted the position to gain experience in a new industry.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: qualified - having the necessary skills or experience for a job
  • antonym: underqualified - not having enough skills or experience for a job
