vegetate

verb
to grow vegetation (land, fields)
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛdʒəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈviːdʒɪteɪt /; / ˈvɛdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of land or an area) to produce vegetation or to be planted with plants

Examples

Fields vegetate after rain
After the rainy season, the barren land began to vegetate rapidly.
If properly irrigated, the fields will vegetate more vigorously each spring.
These fertile soils allow crops to vegetate throughout the year.
After construction, the company promised to vegetate all disturbed areas with native grasses and shrubs.
The environmental team worked hard to vegetate the eroded slopes to prevent landslides.
They planned to vegetate the riverbanks to restore the area’s natural habitat.
