Corydalis sempervirens

noun
North American flowering herb of the poppy family
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈraɪ.dəl.ɪs sem.pər.ˈvɪr.ənz/ (AmE), /kə.ˈraɪ.dəl.ɪs sem.pə.ˈvɪr.ənz/ (BrE)
A glaucous herb native to northeastern United States and Canada, with loose clusters (racemes) of yellow-tipped pink flowers; sometimes classified in the genus Fumaria.
