medlar

noun
tree with edible fruit, native to southern Africa
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛd.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛd.lə / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree that bears edible fruit, found in southern Africa or Eurasia

Examples

A medlar tree
The medlar tree produces unusual fruit that are enjoyed in some African cuisines.
The garden features a variety of fruit trees, including a rare medlar.
They harvested ripe medlars from the small grove near the river.
Early botanists often wrote about the distinctive taste of the medlar fruit.
She made a delicious jelly from ripe medlars gathered in October.
The old orchard still had a single medlar tree bearing fruit in autumn.

  • synonym: loquat - evergreen Asian tree with edible yellow fruit, sometimes confused with medlar
