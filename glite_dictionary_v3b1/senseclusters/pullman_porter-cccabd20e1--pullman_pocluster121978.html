Pullman porter

noun
railroad sleeping car attendant
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊlmən ˈpɔrtər / (AmE), / ˈpʊlmən ˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a railroad employee who assists passengers, especially on sleeping cars, often performing services such as making up beds and attending to travelers' needs

Examples

Pullman porter service
A Pullman porter made up the sleeper beds during the long journey.
Many African Americans found employment as Pullman porters in the early twentieth century.
The Pullman porter greeted passengers with a smile as they boarded the overnight train.
