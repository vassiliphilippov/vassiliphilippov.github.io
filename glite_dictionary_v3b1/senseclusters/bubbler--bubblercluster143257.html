bubbler

noun
device for bubbling air or gas through liquid
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌblɚ / (AmE), / ˈbʌblə / (BrE)
a device that forces air or gas through a liquid, causing bubbles, often used in laboratories or aquariums

Examples

Aquarium bubbler
A bubbler can help maintain a healthy environment for fish by increasing air circulation.
Laboratory technicians used a bubbler to mix gases with the solution.
The aquarium contains a bubbler to keep the water oxygenated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerator - a device that introduces air into a substance
