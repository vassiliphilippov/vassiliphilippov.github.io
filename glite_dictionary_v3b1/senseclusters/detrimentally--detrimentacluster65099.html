Examples

Affect detrimentally

Synonyms

  • harmfully
    4.3 0.06/m
    harmfully is used to say something causes direct harm or injury, while detrimentally sounds more formal and refers to harmful effects.
  • damagingly
    4.4 0.01/m
    damagingly describes an action that causes physical or material harm, while detrimentally covers any harmful or damaging effect.
  • deleteriously
    9.0 0.05/m
    deleteriously is a formal word often used in science or medicine to show damage, while detrimentally is more common in general texts.
  • injuriously
    7.6 0.02/m
    injuriously implies causing physical or legal injury, while detrimentally covers harm in wider, less legal or bodily contexts.
  • adversely
    4.9 3.61/m
    adversely often pairs with affect to mean a negative result, while detrimentally highlights harm or damage more strongly.
  • disadvantageously
    7.4 0.02/m
    disadvantageously stresses causing loss or a bad position, while detrimentally focuses more on damage or harmful effects.
  • negatively
    3.3 6.87/m
    negatively simply means in a bad or opposite way, while detrimentally implies real harm or damage to someone or something.
