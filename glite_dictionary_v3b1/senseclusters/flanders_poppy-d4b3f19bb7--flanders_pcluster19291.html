Flanders poppy

noun
annual red poppy of Europe; war remembrance symbol
8.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈflæn.dərz ˈpɑː.piː/ (AmE), /ˈflɑːn.dəz ˈpɒp.iː/ (BrE)
an annual European poppy (Papaver rhoeas) with bright red flowers, commonly found in grain fields and often cultivated, especially as a symbol of remembrance for soldiers who died in war

Examples

A Flanders poppy flower
Every November, people in the UK and Commonwealth wear a Flanders poppy to commemorate fallen soldiers.
She planted Flanders poppies in her garden as a tribute to her grandfather, a World War I veteran.
The fields near Ypres burst into red as the Flanders poppy blooms in summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corn poppy - another name for Papaver rhoeas, the same species
  • synonym: remembrance poppy - artificial poppy worn as a symbol on Remembrance Day
