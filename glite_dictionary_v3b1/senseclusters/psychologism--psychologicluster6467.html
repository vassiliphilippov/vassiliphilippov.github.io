psychologism

noun
explaining by psychology
9.9
Pronunciation: / saɪˈkɑːlədʒɪzəm / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒlədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
the explanation or interpretation of ideas, concepts, or phenomena in terms of psychological factors or processes

Examples

Psychologism in philosophy
Some philosophers argue that psychologism undermines the objectivity of logic.
The theory was dismissed as psychologism because it relied too heavily on mental processes.
Debates about psychologism were central to early twentieth-century philosophy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: logicism - the explanation of mathematics or other fields in terms of logic
  • synonym: reductionism - the practice of explaining complex phenomena in terms of simpler components
