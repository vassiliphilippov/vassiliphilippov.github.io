stipulation

noun
formal legal agreement in court
8.5 5.8
Pronunciation: /stɪp.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /stɪp.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(law) an agreement or arrangement between parties in a legal case, often relating to facts or procedures, that is formally accepted by the court.

Examples

Court stipulation
By signing the stipulation, both parties agreed to the facts presented in the case.
The judge accepted the stipulation and recorded it in the court documents.
The lawyers reached a stipulation regarding the timeline for submitting evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agreement - a negotiated arrangement between parties
  • synonym: condition - something required as part of an agreement
