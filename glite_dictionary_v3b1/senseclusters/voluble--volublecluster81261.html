voluble

adjective
talkative; fluent in speech
9.9 6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑljəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɒljʊbəl / (BrE)
(of a person or speech) speaking or spoken incessantly and fluently; having a ready and continuous flow of words.

Examples

A voluble speaker
Although he was usually voluble, today he was strangely silent during the meeting.
Her voluble explanations made the complicated topic much easier to understand.
The voluble host kept the guests entertained with stories all night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loquacious - tending to talk a great deal; talkative
  • antonym: taciturn - reserved or uncommunicative in speech; saying little
