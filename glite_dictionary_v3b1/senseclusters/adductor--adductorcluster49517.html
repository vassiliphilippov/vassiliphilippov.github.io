adductor

noun
muscle drawing body part inward
9.0 6.3
Pronunciation: / əˈdʌktər / (AmE), / əˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a muscle that brings a body part closer to the body's midline

Examples

The adductor muscle
A strain in the adductor can cause pain when moving the leg inward.
Dancers must stretch their adductor muscles to improve flexibility.
The adductor muscles of the thigh help bring the legs together.
