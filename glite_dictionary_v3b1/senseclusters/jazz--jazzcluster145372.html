jazz

noun
insincere or exaggerated talk
3.9
Pronunciation: / dʒæz / (AmE), / dʒaz / (BrE)
informal talk or statements that are insincere, exaggerated, or meaningless; empty rhetoric.

Examples

Empty jazz
He filled his report with a lot of jazz but no real substance.
Politicians often use jazz to distract from important issues.
Stop giving me all that jazz and tell me the truth.
