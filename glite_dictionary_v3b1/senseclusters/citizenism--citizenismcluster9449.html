citizenism

noun
citizen-focused ideology
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪt.ɪ.zən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsɪt.ə.zən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈsɪt.ɪ.zən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsɪt.ə.zən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a political or social belief that gives importance to the rights, interests, and active participation of citizens in society or government

Examples

Citizenism belief
The party's platform was based on citizenism, focusing on policies that benefit only citizens.
Debates about immigration often highlight the principles of citizenism in public policy.
Critics argue that citizenism can lead to the exclusion of non-citizens from important rights.
The organization was founded on the principles of citizenism, encouraging people to participate in local decision-making.
Advocates of citizenism believe that ordinary people should have a direct say in government policies.
Through citizenism, communities can address their own needs without relying solely on elected officials.

Schools are introducing lessons on citizenism to help students understand their civic responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarianism - the enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • synonym: civic-mindedness - concern for the well-being and duties of the community
  • antonym: cosmopolitanism - the ideology that all human beings belong to a single community
  • synonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: nationalism - a political ideology emphasizing loyalty and devotion to one's nation
  • synonym: populism - support for the concerns and power of ordinary people
