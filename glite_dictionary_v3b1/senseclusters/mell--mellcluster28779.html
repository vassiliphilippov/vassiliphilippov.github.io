mell

verb
to meddle (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: /mel/ (AmE), /mel/ (BrE)
to involve oneself in a matter without invitation; to meddle or interfere (dialectal or archaic).

Examples

To mell in matters
In old stories, villagers would warn children not to mell in the affairs of strangers.
He was told not to mell where he was not wanted, but he could not resist.
Though the word is rare today, to mell once meant to meddle in others' business.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
  • synonym: interfere - to get involved in a situation where you are not wanted
  • synonym: meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
