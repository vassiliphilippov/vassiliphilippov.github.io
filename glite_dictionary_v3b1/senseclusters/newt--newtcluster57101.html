newt

noun
small semiaquatic salamander
Pronunciation: / nut / (AmE), / njuːt / (BrE)
A small, typically brightly colored semiaquatic salamander found in North America, Europe, and northern Asia.

Examples

A bright newt
A newt slipped quietly into the pond as we approached.
Her fascination with wildlife led her to keep a pet newt in a terrarium.
The researcher studied the regeneration abilities of the newt in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: salamander - amphibian with a slender body and tail
