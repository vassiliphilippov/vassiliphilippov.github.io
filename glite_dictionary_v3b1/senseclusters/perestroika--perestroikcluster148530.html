perestroika

noun
radical restructuring or reform
9.1
Pronunciation: /pe.rə.ˈstrɔɪ.kə/ /; / /pe.rɪ.ˈstrɔɪ.kə/ (AmE), /pe.rə.ˈstrɔɪ.kə/ /; / /pe.rɪ.ˈstrɔɪ.kə/ (BrE)
A major, often radical program of restructuring or reform of an organization, system, or institution (used figuratively, especially outside the original Soviet context).

Examples

Corporate perestroika
After the takeover, the CEO promised a complete perestroika of the company's outdated departments.
Policy analysts called for a perestroika of the healthcare system to reduce costs and improve access.
Protesters demanded an economic perestroika rather than minor reforms after years of stagnation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overhaul - a thorough examination and large-scale repair or improvement of something
  • synonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve a system, organization, or policy
  • synonym: restructuring - the act of organizing something (such as a company or system) in a new way
