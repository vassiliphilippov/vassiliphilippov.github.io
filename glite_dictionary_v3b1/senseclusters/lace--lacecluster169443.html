lace

verb
pass a cord through holes
Pronunciation: / leɪs / (AmE), / leɪs / (BrE)
to draw a cord, string, or shoelace through holes or eyelets, especially to fasten something

Examples

To lace shoes
He taught his daughter how to lace her skates before practicing on the ice.
She bent down to lace her new running shoes before leaving the house.
The football coach told the players to lace their boots tightly for extra support.
