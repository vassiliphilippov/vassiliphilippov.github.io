drag one's feet

verb
delay or procrastinate intentionally
5.7 4.4
Pronunciation: /dræɡ wʌnz fiːt/ /; / /dræɡ wʌns fiːt/ (AmE), /dræɡ wʌnz fiːt/ /; / /dræɡ wʌns fiːt/ (BrE)
to delay or be deliberately slow in doing something, especially because you do not want to do it

Examples

To drag their feet
Management accused the team of dragging their feet on the project.
She tends to drag her feet when asked to make difficult decisions.
Whenever it's time to start his homework, he always drags his feet.
He tends to drag his feet when faced with difficult choices.
If you keep dragging your feet, we'll miss the deadline.
The company was accused of dragging its feet on implementing the new policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hasten - to do something quickly or speed up an action
  • synonym: procrastinate - to delay or postpone taking action
