false pretense

noun
crime of deception for gain
Pronunciation: / fɔls prɪˈtɛns / (AmE), / fɔːls prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
(Law) The crime of deliberately misrepresenting a fact with the intention to deceive and thereby obtain property or money.

Examples

false pretense crime
He used false pretense to gain access to confidential company information.
Obtaining goods by false pretense is considered a criminal offense in many jurisdictions.
The suspect was charged with false pretense after convincing the victim to transfer money based on a lie.
He was convicted of false pretence after tricking the victim into signing over her car.
Obtaining money under false pretence is a serious criminal offense in many countries.
The company was accused of selling products on the basis of false pretence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fraud - the crime of deceiving someone to gain money or property
  • synonym: larceny - theft of personal property
