convolve

verb
combine functions by convolution
9.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈvɑːlv/ /; / /kən.ˈvɔːlv/ (AmE), /kən.ˈvɒlv/ (BrE)
(mathematics, signal processing, computing) to combine two functions or signals by the mathematical operation called convolution, producing a third function that expresses how the shape of one is modified by the other.

Examples

To convolve two signals
The neural network layer convolves the input with a small kernel to detect edges in images.
In signal processing, engineers often convolve a signal with a filter to remove unwanted noise.
To compute the system response, the students convolved the input and impulse response by numerical integration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convolution - the mathematical operation that combines two functions to produce a third
