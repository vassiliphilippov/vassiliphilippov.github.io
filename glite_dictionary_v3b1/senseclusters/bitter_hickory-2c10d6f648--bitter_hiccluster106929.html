bitter hickory

noun

5.5 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbɪt.ər ˈhɪk.ə.riː/ /; / /ˈbɪt.ər ˈhɪk.riː/ (AmE), /ˈbɪt.ə ˈhɪk.ə.riː/ /; / /ˈbɪt.ə ˈhɪk.riː/ (BrE)
A species of hickory tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by leaves with 7 or 9 leaflets and thin-shelled, very bitter nuts.

Examples

The bitter hickory tree
Although they look appealing, the nuts of the bitter hickory are not suitable for eating due to their taste.
The wood of the bitter hickory is prized for its strength and durability.
We found a large bitter hickory tree at the edge of the old forest.
