xerostomia

noun
abnormal dryness of the mouth
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌzɪərəˈstoʊmiə / (AmE), / ˌzɪərəˈstəʊmiə / (BrE)
Abnormal dryness of the mouth caused by reduced or absent saliva production.

Examples

Chronic xerostomia
Chronic xerostomia can increase the risk of dental cavities and gum disease.
Doctors often evaluate xerostomia in patients undergoing radiation therapy for head and neck cancers.
The patient complained of xerostomia after starting her new medication.
