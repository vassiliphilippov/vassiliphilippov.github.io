competent

adjective
having the required skill or ability
3.6 9.81/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêmp…ôt…ônt / (AmE), / Ààk…ímp…™t…ônt / (BrE)
having the necessary ability, knowledge, or skill to do something successfully

Examples

Competent worker
Although he was new to the role, Jack proved himself to be competent.
Only competent drivers are allowed to operate the vehicle on this site.
She is a competent engineer, capable of solving complex problems quickly.

Synonyms

  • capable
    1.4 49/m
    capable means having the real ability or potential to do tasks, while competent means meeting the required standard to do them.
  • qualified
    3.0 26/m
    qualified means having the formal skills, experience, or certificates for a job, while competent means having enough practical ability to do it.
  • able
    1.0 428/m
    able means having the power or opportunity to do something, while competent means having the knowledge and skill to do it well.
  • skilled
    3.5 19/m
    skilled means showing special training and good technique in a task, while competent means having enough ability to do it successfully.
  • proficient
    6.0 3.73/m
    proficient means having a high level of skill and speed in a field, while competent means having adequate ability to perform.
