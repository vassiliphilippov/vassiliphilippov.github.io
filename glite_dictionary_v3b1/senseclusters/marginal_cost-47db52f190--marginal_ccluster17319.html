marginal cost

noun
cost of producing one more unit
4.8 1.53/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ërd í…ônl k…îst / (AmE), / Ààm…ëÀêd í…™n…ôl k…íst / (BrE)
the cost of producing one additional unit of a good or service

Examples

The marginal cost of production
If the price exceeds the marginal cost, it is often profitable to produce more.
The company lowered its marginal cost by improving production efficiency.
Understanding marginal cost helps businesses decide whether to increase output.

Synonyms

  • incremental cost
    4.9 0.42/m
    incremental cost shows how total costs change when output increases or decreases by one unit, while marginal cost is the cost of producing one extra unit.
  • differential cost
    6.9 0.01/m
    differential cost records the increase or decrease in total cost from producing one more or one fewer unit, while marginal cost focuses on one extra unit's cost.

Related Terms

  • antonym: average cost - total cost divided by the number of units produced
