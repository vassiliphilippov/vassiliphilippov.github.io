jump

verb
leap suddenly from the ground
Pronunciation: / dʒʌmp / (AmE), / dʒʌmp / (BrE)
to push yourself or something else off the ground and into the air using your legs, or to leap down from a higher place

Examples

To jump over a fence
At the signal, all the students jumped across the finish line together.
She tried to jump the stream but landed on the muddy bank.
The dog loves to jump over the garden fence every morning.
He hesitated before jumping down from the old stone wall.
Many thrill-seekers jump off cliffs into the deep water below.
The firefighter had to jump from the second-floor window to escape the flames.
She managed to jump her horse over the fence on the first try.
The trainer jumped the tiger through the flaming hoop during the performance.
With a loud clap, the magician jumped the rabbit onto the table.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall - to move downward, typically rapidly and freely
  • degree lower: hop - to move by jumping on one foot
  • synonym: leap - to jump a long way or with great force
