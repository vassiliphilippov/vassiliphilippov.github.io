supersymmetry

noun
theory linking fundamental forces in physics
7.0 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.pɚˈsɪm.ə.tri / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˈsɪm.ɪ.tri / (BrE)
A theoretical concept in physics that proposes a relationship between the four fundamental forces by relating each particle to a superpartner with different spin.

Examples

Supersymmetry theory
In theoretical physics, supersymmetry predicts that every fundamental particle should have a corresponding superpartner.
Physicists hope that supersymmetry could help explain the relationship between particles and forces.
Recent experiments at the Large Hadron Collider have not yet found evidence supporting supersymmetry.
