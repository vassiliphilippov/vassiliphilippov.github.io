turbo

noun
engine power booster
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɜːr.boʊ/ (AmE), /ˈtɜː.bəʊ/ (BrE)
a device that increases an engine's power by forcing extra air into the combustion chamber

Examples

Car turbo upgrade
The mechanic installed a turbo to improve the car's acceleration.
Many high-performance vehicles come equipped with a turbo for extra speed.
After adding a turbo, the engine produced significantly more horsepower.
The new model comes with a powerful turbo to improve acceleration.
Many car enthusiasts upgrade their vehicles with a turbo for better performance.
After installing a turbo, the engine produced much more horsepower.
