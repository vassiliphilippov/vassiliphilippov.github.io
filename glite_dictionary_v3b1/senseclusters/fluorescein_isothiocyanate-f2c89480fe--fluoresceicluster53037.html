fluorescein isothiocyanate

noun
fluorescent dye for labeling antibodies
9.6
Pronunciation: / fluˌɔɹəˌsin ˌaɪsoʊˌθaɪoʊsaɪˈəneɪt / (AmE), / flʊˌɒrəsiːn ˌaɪsəʊˌθaɪəʊsaɪˈəneɪt / (BrE)
a yellow-green fluorescent dye used to label antibodies or proteins, especially for use in immunofluorescence microscopy

Examples

fluorescein isothiocyanate dye
A solution containing fluorescein isothiocyanate was prepared for the immunofluorescence assay.
Researchers used fluorescein isothiocyanate to track the movement of the protein within cells.
The antibody was labeled with fluorescein isothiocyanate to enable visualization under the microscope.
