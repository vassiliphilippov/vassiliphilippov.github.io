mensurable

adjective
having notes of fixed rhythmic value (music)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnsjərəbəl / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.sjʊ.rə.bəl / (BrE)
(of music) having notes of fixed rhythmic value; able to be measured in duration.

Examples

Mensurable music
Scholars studied the origins of mensurable rhythms in medieval manuscripts.
The performance featured a mensurable style typical of 14th-century composers.
The transition from chant to mensurable music marked a significant change in notation.
