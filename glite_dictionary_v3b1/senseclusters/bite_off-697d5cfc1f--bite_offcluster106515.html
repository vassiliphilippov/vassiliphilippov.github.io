bite off

verb
remove by biting
1.0
Pronunciation: / baɪt ɑf / (AmE), / baɪt ɒf / (BrE)
to remove a piece from something using the teeth with a sudden or forceful bite

Examples

To bite off chunks
Be careful when you bite off chunks of that toffee, as it can damage your teeth.
She tried to bite off a piece of chocolate but it was too hard.
The parrot managed to bite off a button from his shirt.
