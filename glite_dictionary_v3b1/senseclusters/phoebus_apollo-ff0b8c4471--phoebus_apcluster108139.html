Phoebus Apollo

proper noun
Greek god of light and the arts
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːbəs əˈpɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfiːbəs əˈpɒləʊ / (BrE)
A name for Apollo, the Greek god of light, prophecy, music, poetry, and healing, son of Zeus and Leto, twin brother of Artemis.

Examples

Phoebus Apollo god of light
Artists frequently depicted Phoebus Apollo crowned with laurel and bearing a lyre.
In ancient texts, Phoebus Apollo is often invoked as the patron of poets and musicians.
The temple to Phoebus Apollo at Delphi was a center of prophecy in the Greek world.
