build-out

noun
interior construction process
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪld aʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɪld aʊt / (BrE)
the process of constructing, finishing, or expanding a space, facility, or system to make it ready for use

Examples

The build-out process
The build-out of the new office space took three months to complete.
After the build-out, the restaurant was ready to welcome its first customers.
They hired a contractor to manage the build-out of their retail store.
The company completed the build-out of its new headquarters ahead of schedule.
A major build-out of the city's public transportation system is planned for next year.
The build-out of the data center included installing additional servers and backup power supplies.
The city approved funding for the build-out of new water treatment facilities.
A rapid build-out of charging stations is needed to support electric vehicles.
Delays in the build-out of the broadband network frustrated many rural residents.
