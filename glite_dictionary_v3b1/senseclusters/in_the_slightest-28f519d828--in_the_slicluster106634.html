in the slightest

adverb
not at all; not even a little
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə ˈslaɪtɪst / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈslaɪtɪst / (BrE)
Used to emphasize a complete lack or absence of something; not at all.

Examples

In the slightest degree
He wasn't bothered in the slightest by the criticism.
I don't mind in the slightest if you want to leave early.
She didn't hesitate in the slightest when asked for help.

Related Terms

  • synonym: at all - in any way or to any extent
