Used to emphasize a complete lack or absence of something; not at all.

Examples

In the slightest degree

Synonyms

  • not at all
    1.0 14/m
    not at all gives a clear denial often used alone, while in the slightest stresses there is no amount of something.
  • in the least
    4.4 3.08/m
    in the least is usually used with not to mean not at all, while in the slightest often follows verbs to show no amount.
  • at all
    1.0 233/m
    at all appears in negatives and questions to mean any extent, while in the slightest feels stronger and more emphatic in negative sentences.
  • even a little
    1.3 2.60/m
    even a little means any small amount and can be used in questions or positives, while in the slightest mainly follows negatives to show none.
  • to no degree
    4.7 0.00/m
    to no degree sounds formal and rare, while in the slightest is idiomatic and common in everyday speech to show no amount.
