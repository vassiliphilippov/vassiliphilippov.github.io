feedback circuit

noun
circuit returning output to input
3.4 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfidˌbæk ˈsɝkɪt / (AmE), / ˈfiːdbæk ˈsɜːkɪt / (BrE)
an electrical circuit in which a portion of the output is returned to the input to influence its operation

Engineers adjusted the feedback circuit to stabilize the voltage levels.
