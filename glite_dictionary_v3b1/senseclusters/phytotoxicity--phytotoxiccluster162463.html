phytotoxicity

noun
plant chemical damage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪtoʊtɑkˈsɪɾəti /; / ˌfaɪtoʊtɑkˈsɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌfaɪtəʊtɒkˈsɪsɪti / (BrE)
the harmfulness or toxic effect of a substance on plants

Examples

Symptoms of phytotoxicity include yellowing leaves and reduced plant growth.

High phytotoxicity can cause stunted growth or even death in sensitive plants.
The chemical's phytotoxicity was evaluated before it was approved for agricultural use.

High phytotoxicity can damage crops and reduce agricultural yields.
The chemical's phytotoxicity was evident when the leaves began to turn yellow and wilt.
