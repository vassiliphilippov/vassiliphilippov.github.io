castrated

adjective
deprived of sexual capacity or attributes
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæstreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkæstreɪtɪd / (BrE)
having been deprived of the power to reproduce or sexual attributes, especially by removal of the testes or ovaries.

Examples

Emotionally castrated
He felt emotionally castrated after his demotion at work.
The castrated bull was much calmer and easier to handle than the others.
The play explores the theme of a castrated society with suppressed desires.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fertile - able to produce offspring
  • synonym: sterile - unable to produce offspring
