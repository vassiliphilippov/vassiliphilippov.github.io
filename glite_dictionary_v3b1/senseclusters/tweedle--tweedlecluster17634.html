tweedle

verb
play lightly or negligently on an instrument
9.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtwiÀêd…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàtwiÀêd…ôl / (BrE)
to play a musical instrument lightly, especially in a repetitive or careless manner

Examples

To tweedle a guitar
He tweedled absentmindedly on his guitar while waiting for the band to arrive.
She often tweedles at the piano without really practicing her scales.
The street musician sat on the corner and tweedled a carefree melody on his flute.

Synonyms

  • tootle
    9.3 0.02/m
    tootle means playing a wind instrument lightly and aimlessly, while tweedle means playing an instrument in a repetitive, careless way.
  • noodle around
    8.0 0.04/m
    noodle around means playing casually or improvising without serious purpose, while tweedle means playing an instrument in a repetitive, careless way.
