dissimilate

verb
make or become less similar
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪleɪt / (BrE)
to make or become less similar; to cause things to diverge in form or character

Examples

To dissimilate features
During language evolution, certain speech sounds dissimilate to make words easier to pronounce.
If the twins start dressing differently, they will dissimilate their appearance.
The artist chose to dissimilate the two styles within his new painting.
After their separation, the two companies started to dissimilate in terms of culture and strategy.
Over centuries, the languages spoken in this region began to dissimilate due to isolation.
Some linguists study how consonant sounds may dissimilate within a word over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assimilate - to become similar; to absorb and make alike
  • synonym: differentiate - to recognize or make distinct differences
  • antonym: resemble - to be similar to
