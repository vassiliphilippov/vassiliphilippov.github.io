counterpressure

noun
opposing pressure
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌprɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌprɛʃə / (BrE)
pressure applied in the opposite direction to balance or resist another pressure

Examples

Suit counterpressure
The astronaut's suit provides counterpressure to protect the body in space.
Applying counterpressure to the wound can help stop the bleeding.
The engineer designed a system that uses counterpressure to stabilize the machine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pressure - the continuous physical force exerted on or against an object
  • synonym: resistance - the act of opposing or withstanding force or pressure
