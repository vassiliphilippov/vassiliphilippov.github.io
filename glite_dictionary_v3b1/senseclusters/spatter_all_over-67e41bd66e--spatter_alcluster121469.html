spatter all over

verb
splash or scatter widely in all directions
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈspæt̬ɚ ɔl ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈspætə ɔːl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to splash or scatter (a liquid or substance) in many directions so that it covers a large surface area

Examples

To spatter all over clothes
During the painting lesson, bright colors would spatter all over their aprons and hands.
She accidentally knocked over the glass of juice, causing it to spatter all over the tablecloth.
The rain began to spatter all over the windshield, making it hard to see the road.
