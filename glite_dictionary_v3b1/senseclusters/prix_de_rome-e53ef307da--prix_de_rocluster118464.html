Prix de Rome

noun
French art and music prize for study in Rome
Pronunciation: / pri dɪ ˈroʊm / (AmE), / pri də ˈrəʊm / (BrE)
an annual prize awarded by the French government to artists, architects, and musicians, granting them a period of study in Rome

Examples

The Prix de Rome prize
She won the Prix de Rome in painting, which allowed her to study in Italy for two years.
The Prix de Rome was considered the highest honor for young French artists in the 19th century.
Winning the Prix de Rome launched his career as a composer and opened many professional opportunities.
