crepitate

verb
make a crackling sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛpɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɛpɪteɪt / (BrE)
to make a crackling or popping sound, typically like that of burning or friction

Examples

Leaves crepitate underfoot
Dried leaves crepitate underfoot as you walk through the autumn woods.
During the exam, the doctor noticed the patient's joints would crepitate when moved.
The fire began to crepitate as the new logs were added to the flames.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crackle - to make a series of short, sharp popping sounds
