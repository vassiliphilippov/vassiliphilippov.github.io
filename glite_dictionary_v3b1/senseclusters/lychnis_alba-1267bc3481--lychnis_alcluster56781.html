Lychnis alba

noun
herbaceous plant with evening-blooming white flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪknɪs ˈælbə / (AmE), / ˈlɪknɪs ˈalbə / (BrE)
A bluish-green herbaceous plant with sticky stems and clusters of large white flowers that open in the evening, having a prominently inflated calyx; sometimes classified in the genus Lychnis.

Examples

Lychnis alba plant
Botanists often note the distinctive inflated calyx of Lychnis alba.
Some gardeners plant Lychnis alba for its attractive and fragrant flowers.
The delicate Lychnis alba brightened the garden with its white blooms every evening.
