incense

noun
substance burned for fragrance
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnsɛns /; / ˈɪnˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈɪnsɛns /; / ˈɪn.sens / (BrE)
a substance that is burned to produce a pleasant or fragrant smell, often used in religious ceremonies or for its aroma

Examples

Burn incense sticks
She lit a stick of incense to create a calming atmosphere in her room.
Shops selling herbs and incense lined the narrow street.
The sweet aroma of incense filled the temple during the ceremony.
During the ceremony, the priest lit incense and let its smoke fill the room.
She bought some jasmine incense to freshen the air in her apartment.
The sweet aroma of incense wafted through the temple, creating a peaceful atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragrance - a pleasant, sweet smell
  • antonym: odor - a distinctive smell, especially an unpleasant one
