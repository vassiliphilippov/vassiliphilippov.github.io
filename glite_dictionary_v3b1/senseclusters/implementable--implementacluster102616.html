implementable

adjective
able to be done
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmpləˈmɛntəbl /; / ˈɪmpləməntəbl / (AmE), / ˌɪmplɪˈmɛntəbl /; / ˈɪmplɪmɛntəbl / (BrE)
able to be put into effect or carried out in practice

Examples

An implementable plan
The new policy is only effective if it is actually implementable by local authorities.
We need to ensure our recommendations are realistic and implementable within the given timeframe.
Her proposal was creative but not easily implementable due to budget constraints.
The new security measures are fully implementable with our current technology.
Before approving the plan, the team checked if it was actually implementable within the project timeline.
Some theoretical models are elegant but not implementable in real-world systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feasible - possible to do easily or conveniently
  • antonym: impracticable - not able to be done or carried out
  • antonym: impractical - not sensible or realistic to carry out
  • synonym: practicable - able to be done or put into practice successfully
