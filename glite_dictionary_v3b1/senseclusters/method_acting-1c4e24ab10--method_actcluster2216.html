method acting

noun
acting technique drawing on personal emotions
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛθ.əd ˌækt.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.əd ˌækt.ɪŋ / (BrE)
a style of acting in which the actor draws on personal emotions and experiences to portray a character authentically, based on the techniques of Stanislavsky

Examples

Method acting technique
Many Hollywood stars are known for their commitment to method acting, sometimes staying in character for months.
She immersed herself in method acting to better understand her character's trauma.
The workshop introduced young actors to the basics of method acting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Stanislavski system - the set of acting principles and techniques developed by Konstantin Stanislavsky
