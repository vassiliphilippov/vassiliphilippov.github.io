radial velocity

noun
speed toward or away from observer
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiəl vəˈlɑːsəti / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəl vɪˈlɒsəti / (BrE)
The component of velocity of an object along the line of sight toward or away from the observer.

Examples

Radial velocity change
A sudden change in radial velocity suggested the presence of another unseen object.
Astronomers measured the radial velocity of the star to determine if it was moving away from Earth.
The radial velocity of the planet indicated it was in orbit around a distant sun.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tangential velocity - velocity perpendicular to the line of sight from the observer
