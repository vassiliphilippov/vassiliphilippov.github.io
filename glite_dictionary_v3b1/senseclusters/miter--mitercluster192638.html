miter

noun
bishop's ceremonial headdress
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtə / (BrE)
A tall, pointed headdress with two peaks and two hanging bands, worn by bishops and certain abbots during formal liturgical ceremonies.

Examples

Bishop's miter
During the ceremony, the bishop placed the miter on his head as a symbol of authority.
Several photographs captured the cardinal in full regalia, including his golden miter.
The ornate embroidery on the miter reflected the importance of the occasion.
During the procession, the golden mitre gleamed under the cathedral lights.
She crafted a model mitre for the church display.
The bishop donned his elaborate mitre for the Easter service.
