fruit of the poisonous tree

noun
legal rule excluding evidence derived from illegality
8.6 8.7
Pronunciation: /fruːt əv ðə ˈpɔɪ.zə.nəs triː/ /; / /fruːt əv ðə ˈpɔɪz.nəs triː/ /; / /frʊt əv ðə ˈpɔɪ.zə.nəs triː/ (AmE), /fruːt əv ðə ˈpɔɪ.zə.nəs triː/ /; / /fruːt əv ðə ˈpɔɪz.nəs triː/ /; / /fruːt əv ðə ˈpɔɪ.zə.nəs tʃriː/ (BrE)
a legal rule that excludes evidence in court if it was obtained through illegal or improper means, or is derived from such evidence

Examples

The fruit of the poisonous tree rule
Any evidence found as a result of that illegal search is considered fruit of the poisonous tree.
The defense argued that the confession was fruit of the poisonous tree and should be excluded from the trial.
The judge ruled that the financial records were fruit of the poisonous tree and could not be admitted in court.
Any witness statement obtained after an unlawful search may be considered fruit of the poisonous tree.
The defense attorney argued that all subsequent evidence was fruit of the poisonous tree due to the illegal wiretap.
The judge ruled that the photographs were fruit of the poisonous tree and could not be used in the trial.
