progressive vaccinia

noun
severe vaccinia complication in immunodeficient patients
9.8
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪv vækˈsɪniə / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv vækˈsɪniə / (BrE)
a severe and often fatal complication of vaccinia infection, mainly affecting individuals with weakened immune systems, marked by the progressive enlargement of the original lesion

Examples

Severe progressive vaccinia
Doctors recognized the signs of progressive vaccinia early, which improved the chances for recovery.
Progressive vaccinia is a life-threatening complication that may occur in immunocompromised patients after smallpox vaccination.
The patient developed progressive vaccinia, with the skin lesion growing steadily worse despite treatment attempts.
