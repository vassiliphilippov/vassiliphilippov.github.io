burden of proof

noun
legal duty to prove a claim
6.9 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.dən əv pruːf/ /; / /ˈbɜːr.dən ə pruːf/ (AmE), /ˈbɜː.dən əv pruːf/ /; / /ˈbɜː.dən ə pruːf/ (BrE)
the obligation to present evidence to support one's claim or accusation, especially in a legal dispute

Examples

The burden of proof in court
In civil cases, the burden of proof is often lower than in criminal cases.
The burden of proof rests on the prosecution in a criminal trial.
Whoever makes a claim must bear the burden of proof to support it.
