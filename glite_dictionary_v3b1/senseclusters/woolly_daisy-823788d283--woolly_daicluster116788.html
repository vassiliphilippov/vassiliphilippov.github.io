woolly daisy

noun
small woolly-leaved plant with yellow flowers
7.2 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈwʊl.ɪ ˈdeɪ.ziː/ /; / /ˈwʊl.iː ˈdeɪ.zɪ/ (AmE), /ˈwʊl.ɪ ˈdeɪ.zɪ/ /; / /ˈwʊl.iː ˈdeɪ.ziː/ (BrE)
A small annual plant of the southwestern United States, with grey woolly leaves and small golden-yellow flower heads, sometimes classified in the genus Eriophyllum.

Examples

The woolly daisy plant
Botanists sometimes debate whether to classify the woolly daisy in the genus Eriophyllum.
The woolly daisy thrives in the dry, sandy soils of the American Southwest.
We spotted several patches of woolly daisy along the desert trail.
