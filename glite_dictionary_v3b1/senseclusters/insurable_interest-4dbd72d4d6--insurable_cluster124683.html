insurable interest

noun
interest qualifying one to insure person or property
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈʃʊrəbəl ˈɪntrəst / (AmE), / ɪnˈʃɔːrəbl ˈɪntrəst / (BrE)
a stake in the continued existence of a person or property, recognized by law as justifying the issuance of an insurance policy for protection against loss

Examples

An insurable interest in property
It is essential to prove insurable interest before purchasing a life insurance policy.
The bank had an insurable interest in the house because it held the mortgage.
Without an insurable interest, your claim on the insurance policy will be denied.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beneficiary - a person entitled to benefits from an insurance policy
