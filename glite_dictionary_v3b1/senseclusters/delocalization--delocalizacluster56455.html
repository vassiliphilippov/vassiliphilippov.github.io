delocalization

noun
spreading of electrons
Pronunciation: / diˌloʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which electrons or other particles are spread over several atoms or locations rather than being confined to one

Examples

Electron delocalization
The delocalization of electrons in benzene makes the molecule unusually stable.
In metals, delocalization allows electrons to move freely and conduct electricity.
Chemists study delocalization to understand the properties of aromatic compounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: localization - the confinement of particles or effects to a specific location
