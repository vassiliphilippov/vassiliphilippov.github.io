unclasp

verb
to release or open a clasp
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈklæsp / (AmE), / ʌnˈklɑːsp / (BrE)
to release or open something that is fastened with a clasp, or to let go after grasping tightly

Examples

To unclasp a bracelet
He paused to unclasp her hand before stepping away.
She nervously unclasped her necklace and handed it to the jeweler.
The hiker quickly unclasped his backpack to get some water.
