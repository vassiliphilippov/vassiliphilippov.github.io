push in

verb
enter or cut in line rudely
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ ɪn / (AmE), / pʊʃ ɪn / (BrE)
to enter a place or join a line in a rude or disruptive way, especially by not waiting your turn

Examples

To push in line
It's unfair when someone tries to push in while everyone else is patiently waiting.
Please don't push in; we'll all get served soon enough.
The children complained when a boy attempted to push in at the front of the queue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut in - to move into a queue or conversation before your turn
