push in

verb
enter or cut in line rudely
5.6 0.35/m
Pronunciation: / pÊŠÊƒ Éªn / (AmE), / pÊŠÊƒ Éªn / (BrE)
to enter a place or join a line in a rude or disruptive way, especially by not waiting your turn

Examples

To push in line
It's unfair when someone tries to push in while everyone else is patiently waiting.
Please don't push in; we'll all get served soon enough.
The children complained when a boy attempted to push in at the front of the queue.

Synonyms

  • cut in line
    3.8 0.17/m
    cut in line means moving ahead of people waiting in a queue without permission, while push in emphasizes rude, disruptive joining.
  • jump the queue
    4.8 0.03/m
    jump the queue means unfairly skipping others in a line to get ahead, while push in stresses rude, physical or disruptive joining.
  • shoulder in
    7.2 0.08/m
    shoulder in describes forcing your way in with your body, while push in covers rude joining but not always physical.
  • barge in
    5.5 1.25/m
    barge in means entering suddenly and rudely, often a room or conversation, while push in focuses on joining a line.
