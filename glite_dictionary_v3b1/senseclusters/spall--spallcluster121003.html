spall

noun
fragment broken off stone or ore
9.9
Pronunciation: / spɑl / (AmE), / spɔːl / (BrE)
a fragment, often with at least one thin edge, that breaks off from the edge or surface of stone, ore, or concrete

Examples

A spall of stone
Over time, repeated impacts caused spall to accumulate at the base of the old building.
Safety measures are crucial to protect workers from flying spall during demolition.
The stonecutters swept up the spall left behind after shaping the granite blocks.

  • synonym: splinter - thin, sharp fragment broken off from a larger object
