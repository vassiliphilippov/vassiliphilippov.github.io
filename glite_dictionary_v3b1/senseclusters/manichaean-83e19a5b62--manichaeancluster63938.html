Manichaean

noun
adherent of Manichaeism
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmænɪˈkiːən / (AmE), / ˌmanɪˈkiːən / (BrE)
A follower or adherent of Manichaeism, a major religious movement founded by the prophet Mani in the 3rd century AD.

Examples

A Manichaean thinker
As a Manichaean, he advocated a dualistic view of the world.
Many Manichaeans faced persecution for their religious beliefs in the medieval period.
The writings of a Manichaean priest were discovered among ancient manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Manichaeism - the religious system founded by Mani in the 3rd century AD
