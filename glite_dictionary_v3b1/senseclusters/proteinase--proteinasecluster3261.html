proteinase

noun
enzyme that breaks down proteins
7.2 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpro ätiÀåne…™s / (AmE), / Ààpr…ô ätiÀêÀåne…™z / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down proteins into smaller peptides and amino acids by the process of proteolysis

Examples

A proteinase enzyme
A deficiency in a certain proteinase can lead to metabolic disorders.

Synonyms

  • protease
    7.7 0.97/m
    protease is the common scientific name used in papers and labs, while proteinase is an alternative technical term for the same activity.
  • proteolytic enzyme
    4.4 0.09/m
    proteolytic enzyme describes any enzyme that breaks proteins in a clear phrase used in writing, while proteinase is the single-word technical name.
  • peptidase
    8.8 0.03/m
    peptidase often refers to enzymes that cut short peptides rather than whole proteins, while proteinase focuses on protein breakdown.
