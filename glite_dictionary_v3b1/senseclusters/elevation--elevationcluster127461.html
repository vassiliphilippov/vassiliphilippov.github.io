elevation

noun
angular distance above the horizon
Pronunciation: / ˌɛləˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the angular distance of an object above the horizon, especially used with reference to celestial objects

Examples

Sun's elevation
As the sun rises, its elevation increases rapidly.
The star's elevation was measured at 45 degrees above the horizon.
To determine your position at sea, you need to know the elevation of the sun at noon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altitude - height above a reference point, often used synonymously for celestial position
