superset

adjective
more comprehensive
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəsɛt / (BrE)
more comprehensive or inclusive, containing everything in a smaller category or version.

Examples

Superset rules
The new regulations are a superset of the previous local laws, covering more situations.
This software package is a superset version, offering all the features of the basic one and more.
Their responsibilities form a superset role, including tasks from several departments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • antonym: subset - a smaller group that is part of a larger set
