HDL cholesterol

noun
cholesterol in high-density lipoproteins ('good' cholesterol)
4.7 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ diː ˈɛl kəˌlɛstərɔl / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ diː ˈɛl kəˌlɛstərɒl / (BrE)
the cholesterol carried by high-density lipoproteins in the blood, known as 'good' cholesterol because high levels are thought to reduce the risk of heart disease

Examples

HDL cholesterol levels
A diet rich in healthy fats can help increase your HDL cholesterol levels.
Doctors recommend regular exercise to boost HDL cholesterol for heart protection.
His HDL cholesterol was above average, reducing his risk of heart disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: LDL cholesterol - cholesterol in low-density lipoproteins, 'bad cholesterol'
