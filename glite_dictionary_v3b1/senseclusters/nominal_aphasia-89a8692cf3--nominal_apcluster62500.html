nominal aphasia

noun
inability to name or recognize objects (medical)
9.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ëÀêm…™n…ôl …ôÀàfe…™zi…ô / (AmE), / Ààn…ím…™n…ôl …ôÀàfe…™zi…ô / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the inability to name objects or recognize their names, either spoken or written.

Examples

Nominal aphasia after stroke
After his stroke, the patient developed nominal aphasia and struggled to recall the names of everyday items.
People with nominal aphasia may understand an object's function but cannot remember or say its name.
The neurologist diagnosed her with nominal aphasia after repeated difficulty in naming common objects during the assessment.

Synonyms

  • anomia
    9.9 0.00/m
    anomia is the medical term for trouble finding and saying object names, while nominal aphasia names this specific inability to name objects.
  • amnesic aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    amnesic aphasia causes a person to fail to name or recognize written or spoken names, while nominal aphasia shows the same naming loss.
  • anomic aphasia
    9.6 0.00/m
    anomic aphasia makes word retrieval hard but keeps understanding intact, while nominal aphasia focuses on the specific inability to name objects.
  • amnestic aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    amnestic aphasia gives difficulty naming or recognizing spoken and written names, while nominal aphasia specifically describes the loss of naming.
