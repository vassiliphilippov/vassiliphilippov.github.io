urolith

noun
a stone formed in the urinary tract
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊrəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəˌlɪθ / (BrE)
A stone or calculus formed in the urinary tract.

Examples

A urolith stone
Chronic urinary tract infections can increase the risk of developing a urolith.
The surgeon removed a large urolith from the patient's bladder.
X-rays confirmed the presence of a urolith obstructing the ureter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calculus - a stone formed in the body, especially in the kidney or bladder
