overrefinement

noun
excessive or distorted subtlety
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɚ.rɪˈfaɪn.mənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vər.rɪˈfaɪn.mənt / (BrE)
the act of interpreting or developing something with excessive subtlety or complexity, to the point of distorting its original meaning

Examples

Avoid overrefinement errors
By avoiding overrefinement, we can keep our explanations clear and true to the original idea.
Sometimes overrefinement in interpretation can lead to unnecessary confusion and disagreement among scholars.
The critic argued that the author's theory suffers from overrefinement, twisting the original text beyond its intended meaning.
