cauda

noun
taillike anatomical structure
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔdə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
A taillike structure, especially one found as part of an organism in anatomical or zoological contexts.

Examples

The cauda structure
In certain fish, the cauda is used for steering and balance.
Some protozoa have a cauda that aids in locomotion.
The cauda equina is a bundle of nerve roots at the base of the spinal cord.
