scientific theory

noun
explanation of natural phenomena in science
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ˈθiəri / (AmE), / ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ˈθɪəri / (BrE)
A well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world, based on a body of facts that have been repeatedly confirmed through observation and experiment.

Examples

A scientific theory explains nature
A scientific theory can be revised or replaced if new data contradict it.
A well-established scientific theory must be supported by extensive evidence from many experiments.
The theory of evolution is one of the most thoroughly tested scientific theories in biology.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence
  • degree higher: law - a statement describing consistent, universal relationships in nature
