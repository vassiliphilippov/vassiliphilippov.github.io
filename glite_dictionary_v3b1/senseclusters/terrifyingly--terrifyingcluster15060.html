terrifyingly

adverb
in a frightening way
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛrəˌfaɪɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈtɛrɪˌfaɪɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that is extremely frightening or, by extension, very intense or extreme (often used for emphasis)

Examples

A terrifyingly scary story
The horror movie was terrifyingly realistic and kept everyone on the edge of their seats.
She spoke terrifyingly about the dangers of the storm approaching their town.
The sky turned terrifyingly dark as the tornado drew closer.
The mountain road was terrifyingly narrow, making the drive feel dangerous.
She was terrifyingly calm during the emergency, which surprised everyone.
The results were terrifyingly close, with only a few votes deciding the winner.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmly - in a peaceful or relaxed way
  • synonym: extremely - to a very high degree
  • synonym: frighteningly - in a way that causes fear or alarm
  • synonym: incredibly - to a very great degree; extremely
  • degree lower: slightly - to a small degree; a little
