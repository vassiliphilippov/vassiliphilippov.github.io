unguardedness

noun
lack of caution
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡɑrdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡɑːdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being open, not careful, or unprotected, making someone vulnerable

Examples

Her unguardedness showed
Her unguardedness made her easy to talk to and trust.
He regretted his moment of unguardedness when he revealed too much personal information.
The interviewer's unguardedness encouraged the guest to speak honestly about difficult topics.
His moment of unguardedness during the debate allowed his opponent to make a strong point.
The castle's unguardedness at night made it an easy target for intruders.
She regretted her unguardedness after sharing personal details with a stranger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: caution - care taken to avoid danger or mistakes
  • synonym: exposure - the state of being without protection
  • synonym: openness - the quality of being honest and not hiding information or feelings
  • antonym: security - the state of being protected from danger or harm
  • synonym: vulnerability - the state of being open to harm or attack
