squishiness

noun
lack of firmness
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɪʃinəs / (AmE), / ˈskwɪʃinəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking firmness, decisiveness, or strictness, especially in judgment or policy

Examples

Squishiness in decisions
The squishiness of the new policy left many employees confused about the rules.
Critics pointed out the squishiness in the manager's response to the complaints.
Her squishiness on important issues made it hard for others to trust her decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firmness - the quality of being strong, strict, or decisive
  • synonym: softness - the quality of being gentle or not strict
