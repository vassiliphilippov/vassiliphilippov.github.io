trim

noun
an act of cutting to desired size or shape
Pronunciation: / trɪm / (AmE), / trɪm / (BrE)
the act of cutting something to make it neater or the desired size or shape

Examples

A quick trim
I need a trim before the wedding this weekend.
She asked the stylist for just a little trim, not a full haircut.
The gardener gave the hedge a quick trim to neaten it.
Before the parade, the soldiers made sure their uniforms were in immaculate trim.
Her garden is always in good trim, even during the winter.
The old house was kept in perfect trim throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - the act of making something shorter by removing part of it
  • antonym: disorder - a lack of order or arrangement; untidy state
  • synonym: order - a state of being arranged or organized
