noncausal

adjective
not involving causation
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈkɔzəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈkɔːzəl / (BrE)
not involving or relating to causation; not having a cause-effect relationship.

Examples

Noncausal relationship
A noncausal relationship means that one event does not directly affect another.
The data analysis revealed several noncausal correlations between the variables.
The researcher emphasized that their model was strictly noncausal in nature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: causal - relating to or acting as a cause
