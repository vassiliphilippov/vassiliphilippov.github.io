common dolphin

noun
a species of dolphin found in warm seas
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈdoʊlfɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈdɒlfɪn / (BrE)
a small black-and-white dolphin, known for leaping high out of the water, found in warm and temperate seas

Examples

A group of common dolphins
A group of common dolphins played in the wake of the boat as we sailed across the bay.
Marine biologists often study the behavior of the common dolphin due to its playful nature.
The common dolphin is easily recognized by its distinctive black and white markings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dolphin - a marine mammal known for its intelligence and agility
