sacerdotalism

noun
belief in priestly mediation
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsæsɚˈdoʊtəlˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsasəˈdəʊtəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that priests have the authority to act as mediators between human beings and God

Examples

Some Christian denominations reject sacerdotalism in favor of direct access to God.
The doctrine of sacerdotalism played a major role in medieval church structure.
