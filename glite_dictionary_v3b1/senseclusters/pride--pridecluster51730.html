pride

noun
drive to maintain high personal standards
3.6 1.6
Pronunciation: /praɪd/ (AmE), /praɪd/ (BrE)
a feeling of satisfaction, self-respect, or pleasure in your own or someone else's achievements or qualities

Examples

Personal pride
Despite the setbacks, his pride kept him striving to reach his goals.
He has too much pride to admit when he's wrong, even to himself.
Her pride pushed her to always deliver her best work, no matter the circumstances.
He regained his pride after overcoming many difficulties.
Parents often express pride in their children's achievements.
She felt a great sense of pride when she received the award.
He spoke about his team's success with obvious pride.
Parents often take great pride in their children's achievements.
She felt a surge of pride when she received the award.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: arrogance - exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • antonym: humility - modest view of one's own importance
  • synonym: self-esteem - confidence in one's own worth or abilities
  • synonym: self-respect - regard for one's own character or dignity
  • antonym: shame - a feeling of guilt or disgrace when failing to meet standards
