outpass

verb
to surpass in passing
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈpæs / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈpɑːs / (BrE)
to surpass or go beyond someone or something in passing or progress

Examples

To outpass a rival
During the last stretch of the race, she managed to outpass her main rival.
The new technology will likely outpass all previous innovations in speed and efficiency.
He trained hard so he could outpass his teammates in the next competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall behind - to fail to keep up with others
  • synonym: overtake - to catch up with and pass while traveling in the same direction
  • synonym: surpass - to do or be better than someone or something
