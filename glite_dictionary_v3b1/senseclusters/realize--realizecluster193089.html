realize

verb
make real; give substance to
2.7 15/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀê…ôÀåla…™z / (AmE), / Ààr…™…ôÀåla…™z /; / Ààr…™…ôla…™z / (BrE)
to make something real or concrete; to give reality or substance to an idea, plan, or hope

Examples

Realize a dream
His dream of starting a business was finally realized after years of effort.
The committee worked hard to realize their vision of a community park.
We need innovative strategies to realize our goals in the coming year.
It can take great effort to realise your dreams in the real world.
Our team is determined to realise the project by the end of the year.
The architect worked for years to realise her vision of an eco-friendly city.

Synonyms

  • actualize
    7.1 1.25/m
    actualize is to intentionally make an idea or plan exist in practice, while realize is to give an idea real form or existence.
  • substantiate
    7.4 0.27/m
    substantiate is to provide evidence or shape an idea into a firmer form, while realize is to make that idea actually happen.
  • materialize
    5.8 6.75/m
    materialize is to appear or happen, often without clear effort, while realize means to actively make an idea or plan come true.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actualize - to make actual or real, similar meaning
  • antonym: imagine - to form a mental image of; to not make real
  • synonym: materialize - to become real or actual; to appear or be realized
