Tudor architecture

noun
English architectural style (Tudor era)
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː.dɚ ˌɑːr.kɪˈtɛk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.də ˌɑː.kɪˈtɛk.tʃə / (BrE)
A style of English-Gothic architecture from the Tudor period (1485–1603), characterized by half-timbered houses, steeply pitched roofs, and ornate brickwork.

Examples

Tudor architecture style
Many villages in England still feature Tudor architecture with their iconic black-and-white timber-framed houses.
Scholars often study Tudor architecture to understand the cultural influences of the period.
The museum exhibits a model home built in the distinctive style of Tudor architecture.
