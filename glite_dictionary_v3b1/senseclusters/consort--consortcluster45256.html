consort

verb
associate with (often disapproved)
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːnsɔrt /; / ˈkɑːnˌsɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkɒnsɔːt / (BrE)
to associate or spend time with someone, especially in a way that is disapproved of by others

Examples

To consort with criminals
Politicians should be careful about whom they consort with in public.
Rumors spread after the ambassador was seen consorting with known criminals.
She rarely consorts with people outside her professional circle.
He was criticized for choosing to consort with known criminals.
Rumors spread quickly when she began consorting with politicians.
The princess refused to consort with members of the rival family.

  • antonym: avoid - to keep away from someone or something
