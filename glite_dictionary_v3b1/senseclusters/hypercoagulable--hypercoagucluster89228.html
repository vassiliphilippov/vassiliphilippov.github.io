hypercoagulable

adjective
increased blood clotting
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərkəˈwæɡjələbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəkəʊˈæɡjʊləbl̩ / (BrE)
having an increased tendency for the blood to clot more than normal

Examples

Hypercoagulable blood condition
Patients with certain genetic mutations may be hypercoagulable and at risk for deep vein thrombosis.
Doctors monitor people who are hypercoagulable closely to prevent dangerous blood clots.
Some medications can make a person temporarily hypercoagulable after surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypocoagulable - having a reduced ability for the blood to clot
  • synonym: thrombophilic - having a tendency to develop blood clots
