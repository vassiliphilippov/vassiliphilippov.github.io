theologize

verb
discuss or speculate about theology
4.4 8.1
Pronunciation: /θiː.ˈɑː.lə.dʒaɪz/ (AmE), /θiː.ˈɒl.ə.dʒaɪz/ (BrE)
to discuss, interpret, or think about something in terms of theology or from a theological perspective

Examples

She prefers practical ministry to simply theologizing about spiritual matters.
The professor tends to theologize every topic, linking it back to religious doctrine.
They spent hours theologizing about the nature of sin and redemption.
It annoyed her when he would theologize every moral issue that arose in their discussions.
Scholars gathered at the conference to theologize about the significance of ancient texts.

He tends to theologise every discussion about morality, viewing all actions through a religious lens.
Some writers theologise historical events, interpreting them as part of a divine plan.
The professor cautioned against attempts to theologise political debates in the classroom.
