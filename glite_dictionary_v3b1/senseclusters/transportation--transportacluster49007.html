transportation

noun
sending someone into exile as punishment
1.1 8.0
Pronunciation: /træn.spər.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /træn.spɔːr.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /træn.spɔː.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of sending someone away from their native country as a form of punishment, especially to a penal colony; exile by legal decree

Examples

Transportation to a colony
In 19th-century Britain, transportation to Australia was a common punishment for serious offences.
Many families were separated by forced transportation during that era.
The judge sentenced him to life transportation for his crimes against the crown.
