xenophobia

noun
fear or dislike of foreigners
Pronunciation: / ˌzɛnəˈfoʊbiə / (AmE), / ˌzɛnəˈfəʊbiə / (BrE)
a strong fear or dislike of people from other countries or cultures

Examples

Strong xenophobia
After moving abroad, she was saddened by the xenophobia she occasionally encountered.
Public debates about immigration often reveal underlying xenophobia in some communities.
Widespread xenophobia can pose challenges to building diverse and inclusive societies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: racism - prejudice or discrimination against people of different races
  • antonym: tolerance - acceptance of people from different backgrounds
