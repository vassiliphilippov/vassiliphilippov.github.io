well-mannered

adjective
polite; socially correct in behavior
Pronunciation: / wɛlˈmænɚd /; / ˌwɛlˈmænərd / (AmE), / wɛlˈmænəd /; / ˌwɛlˈmænəd / (BrE)
behaving in a polite and socially acceptable or correct way; showing good manners

Examples

A well-mannered guest
A well-mannered guest knows to thank their host before leaving.
He is always well-mannered and considerate, even in stressful situations.
The children were praised for being well-mannered during the ceremony.
Despite his success, he has remained humble and well-mannered throughout his career.
Her well-mannered response impressed the committee during the interview.
The children at the party were all well-mannered and thanked their host politely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ill-mannered - rude or impolite in behavior
  • synonym: polite - having or showing behavior that is respectful and considerate of other people
