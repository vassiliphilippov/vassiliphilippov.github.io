coercivity

noun
magnetic resistance
Pronunciation: / koʊɚˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / kəʊɜːˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a magnetic material to resist changes in its magnetization, especially when exposed to an external magnetic field

Examples

Magnetic coercivity strength
The coercivity of the alloy makes it suitable for permanent magnets.
Materials with high coercivity are often used in data storage devices.
Scientists measured the coercivity of the sample to determine its magnetic properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remanence - the magnetization left in a material after an external magnetic field is removed
