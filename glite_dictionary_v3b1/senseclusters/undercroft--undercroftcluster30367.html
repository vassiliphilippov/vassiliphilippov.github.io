undercroft

noun
room below building
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌkrɔft / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkɹɒft / (BrE)
a room or space beneath a building, especially one used for storage or as a cellar

Examples

Wine undercroft
The medieval castle's undercroft was cool and dark, perfect for storing wine.
During renovations, workers discovered an old undercroft beneath the city hall.
The museum displays artifacts in the ancient undercroft below the main building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cellar - a room below ground level in a house, often used for storage
  • synonym: crypt - an underground room beneath a church, often used as a burial place
