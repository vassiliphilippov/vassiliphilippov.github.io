undersell

verb
sell for less than competitors
5.7 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.sel/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.sel/ (BrE)
to sell goods or services at a lower price than a competitor

Examples

To undersell a rival
Many small businesses struggle when large chains undersell them.
Online retailers often undersell traditional brick-and-mortar stores.
The new supermarket tried to undersell its rivals by offering deep discounts on groceries.
The company refused to undersell its products just to beat the competition.
She tends to undersell her skills during job interviews, making her seem less qualified.
They undersold the house because they needed to move quickly.
