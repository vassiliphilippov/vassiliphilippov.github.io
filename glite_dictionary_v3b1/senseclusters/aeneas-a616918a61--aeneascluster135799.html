Aeneas

proper noun
Trojan hero of myth, protagonist of the Aeneid
Pronunciation: / əˈniːəs / (AmE), / ɪˈniːəs / (BrE)
In classical mythology, a Trojan hero and leader, son of Aphrodite, who played a central role in the Trojan War and was the protagonist of the Aeneid by Virgil.

Examples

Aeneas in mythology
Historians often debate the historical basis for the character of Aeneas.
Many ancient legends depict Aeneas leading the survivors of Troy to safety.
Virgil's epic poem, the Aeneid, follows the journey of Aeneas from Troy to Italy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Achilles - Greek hero of the Trojan War, foe of Aeneas
  • synonym: Aeneid - Epic poem by Virgil about Aeneas
