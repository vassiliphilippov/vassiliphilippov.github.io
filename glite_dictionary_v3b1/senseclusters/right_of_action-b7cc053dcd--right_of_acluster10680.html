right of action

noun
legal right to sue
Pronunciation: / raɪt əv ˈækʃən / (AmE), / raɪt əv ˈækʃən / (BrE)
The legal right of an individual or entity to bring a lawsuit in a court of law.

Examples

The right of action in court
Legislation was passed to limit the right of action in such cases.
The plaintiff's right of action arose when the contract was breached.
Without a valid right of action, the lawsuit cannot proceed in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cause of action - reason or basis for a lawsuit
