stuck-up

adjective
conceited or arrogant (informal)
2.2 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈstʌk.ʌp/ /; / /ˈstʌk.əp/ (AmE), /ˈstʌk.ʌp/ /; / /ˈstʌk.əp/ (BrE)
Behaving as though you are better than other people; conceited and arrogant.

Examples

A stuck-up person
He came across as really stuck-up at the party, barely talking to anyone.
I can't stand how stuck-up she acts around everyone at work.
The new student seemed stuck-up, but she turned out to be very kind once we got to know her.
