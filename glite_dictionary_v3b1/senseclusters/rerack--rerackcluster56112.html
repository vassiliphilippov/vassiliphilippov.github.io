rerack

verb
to reset on rack
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈræk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈræk / (BrE)
to arrange balls or items back into their starting position on a rack, especially in games like pool or snooker

Examples

To rerack the balls
After each round, the referee will rerack the snooker balls for the next frame.
Please rerack the weights when you finish your workout at the gym.
He forgot to rerack the pool balls, so the next players had to do it themselves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to put things in a neat or required order
  • synonym: reset - to set something back to its original state
  • antonym: scatter - to throw or spread things in different directions
