Tamarindus indica

noun
species of tropical evergreen tree (tamarind)
Pronunciation: / təˌmærɪndəs ɪnˈdaɪkə / (AmE), / təˌmærɪndəs ɪnˈdɪkə / (BrE)
a long-lived tropical evergreen tree with feathery foliage, fragrant flowers, hard yellowish wood, and long pods containing edible, acidic pulp

Examples

Tamarindus indica tree
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Tamarindus indica in tropical climates.
Many traditional medicines incorporate the pulp of Tamarindus indica due to its medicinal properties.
The fruit of Tamarindus indica is widely used in cuisines around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tamarind - the common name for Tamarindus indica, its pod, and its fruit pulp
