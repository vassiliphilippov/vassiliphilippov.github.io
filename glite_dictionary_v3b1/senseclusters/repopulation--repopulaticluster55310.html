repopulation

noun
restoring data entries
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌpɑːpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of restoring or refilling a collection, such as a database or list, with entries or data

Examples

Repopulation of a database
The IT team managed the repopulation of the user list after the accidental data loss.
Automated scripts can speed up the repopulation of forms with default values.
Following the update, the system required a complete repopulation of its records from backup files.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deletion - the act of removing data or entries from a collection
  • synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original state
