Trinitarianism

noun
Christian doctrine of the Trinity
9.7 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Àåtr…™n…™Ààt…õri…ônÀå…™z…ôm / (AmE), / Àåtr…™n…™Ààt…õÀêr…™…ôn…™z(…ô)m / (BrE)
The Christian doctrine that emphasizes belief in the Trinity, the unity of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as three persons in one God.

Examples

Trinitarianism belief
Many denominations embrace Trinitarianism as a central element of their faith.
Some Christian groups reject Trinitarianism in favor of different theological views.
The concept of Trinitarianism was formalized in early church councils.

Synonyms

  • Trinity
    7.5 0.16/m
    Trinity names the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit as one God, while Trinitarianism is the doctrine that teaches belief in them.
  • Holy Trinity
    7.0 0.51/m
    Holy Trinity is the common church name for the three persons as one God, while Trinitarianism is the formal doctrine teaching that belief.
  • Blessed Trinity
    8.9 0.03/m
    Blessed Trinity is a prayerful name used in worship, while Trinitarianism is the doctrine that explains this belief.
  • Sacred Trinity
    9.3 0.00/m
    Sacred Trinity is a reverent, formal term used in theology, while Trinitarianism is the doctrinal teaching about that three-in-one belief.
