cathedral

noun
centralized, top-down development model
8.3
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈθiː.drəl/ (AmE), /kə.ˈθiː.drəl/ (BrE)
A centralized, top-down development or organizational model—especially in software engineering—characterized by careful planning, restricted contributors, and tightly controlled releases.

Examples

Cathedral development model
The company favored a cathedral release process, where a small team approved every feature before launch.
Open-source contributors criticized the project's cathedral approach for slowing innovation and excluding outside feedback.
In his lecture, the architect contrasted the cathedral model with the bazaar model to explain governance differences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bazaar (model) - an open, decentralized development model emphasizing many contributors and rapid iterative change
  • synonym: centralized - organized so that decision-making is concentrated within a small group or location
