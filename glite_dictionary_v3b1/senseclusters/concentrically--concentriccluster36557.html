concentrically

adverb
with a common center
5.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛntrɪkli / (AmE), / kənˈsɛntrɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that has the same center, especially referring to circles or spheres arranged around a common point

Examples

Spread concentrically outward
The ripples spread concentrically from the point where the stone hit the water.
The architect designed the gardens to be arranged concentrically around the central fountain.
The plates were stacked concentrically to save space on the table.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radially - in a way that extends outward from a center
