nepheline

noun
a white silicate mineral used in ceramics
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛfəˌliːn / (AmE), / ˈnɛfɪliːn / (BrE)
A whitish crystalline mineral consisting of sodium or potassium aluminum silicate, used in the manufacture of ceramics and enamels.

Examples

White nepheline mineral
Because of its durability, nepheline is often used in the production of glass and enamels.
Geologists discovered a rich deposit of nepheline in the mountain range.
The ceramic tiles were made with nepheline as a key ingredient.
