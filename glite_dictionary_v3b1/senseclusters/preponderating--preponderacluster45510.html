preponderating

adjective
having superior power or influence
Pronunciation: / priˈpɑndəˌreɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / priˈpɒndəˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
having greater power, influence, or importance than others

Examples

The preponderating role
His preponderating role in the negotiations swayed the final decision.
In the council, a preponderating number of members supported the new environmental policy.
The company's preponderating position in the market made competition difficult for smaller firms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predominant - having more power, influence, or importance than others
  • antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or importance
