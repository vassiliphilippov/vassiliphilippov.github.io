savvy

adjective
practically knowledgeable
Pronunciation: / ˈsævi / (AmE), / ˈsævi / (BrE)
having practical knowledge and good judgment, especially in a particular area

Examples

Savvy shopper
He is the most savvy investor I know, always making smart decisions.
Her savvy approach to social media helped the company grow quickly.
You need to be savvy to succeed in the fast-paced world of technology.
He became much savvy about investing after taking a few courses.
You need to be savvy to avoid scams when shopping online.
Her savvy approach to negotiations impressed everyone at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naive - lacking experience or practical knowledge
  • synonym: shrewd - having sharp judgment and practical intelligence
