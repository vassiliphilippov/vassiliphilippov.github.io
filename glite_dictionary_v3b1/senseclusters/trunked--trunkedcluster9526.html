trunked

adjective
having a trunk
7.7
Pronunciation: / trʌŋkt / (AmE), / trʌŋkt / (BrE)
having a trunk, especially referring to an animal with a long nose or a plant with a main stem

Examples

Trunked elephant
The trunked elephant used its long nose to pick up fruit from the ground.
Some trees are described as trunked when they have a single, thick main stem.
Unlike other animals, the trunked mammal could reach high branches with ease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trunkless - lacking a trunk
