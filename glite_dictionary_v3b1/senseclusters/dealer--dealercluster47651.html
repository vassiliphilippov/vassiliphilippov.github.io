dealer

noun
principal trader on a financial market
4.0 6/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdiÀêl…ö / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀêl…ô / (BrE)
a person or company that buys and sells securities, currencies, or goods as a principal, especially on a stock exchange, trading for their own account rather than on behalf of clients.

Examples

Stock dealer
Every major investment bank employs at least one dealer to manage market risks.
The dealer made significant profits by trading stocks on their own account.
The dealer quoted both buy and sell prices for each security.
