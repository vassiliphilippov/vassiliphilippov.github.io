American wistaria

noun
North American flowering vine species
8.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən wɪ.ˈstɪr.iː.ə/ (AmE), /ə.ˈme.rɪ.kən wɪ.ˈstɪə.riː.ə/ (BrE)
a woody vine of the eastern United States with pale purple-lilac flowers, similar to Japanese wisteria

Examples

American wistaria flowers
Gardeners often choose American wistaria for its hardiness in colder U.S. climates.
The American wistaria blooms in late spring, covering fences with fragrant, lilac-colored flowers.
Unlike its Asian relatives, the American wistaria is less aggressive and easier to control in home gardens.
