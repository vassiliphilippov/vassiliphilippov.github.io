kinematically

adverb
in terms of motion
Pronunciation: / ˌkɪnəˈmætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌkɪnɪˈmætɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the motion or movement of objects, without considering the forces that cause the motion

Examples

To move kinematically
The robot's arm was analyzed kinematically to determine its range of movement.
Engineers studied the system kinematically, focusing only on positions and velocities.
The animation was created kinematically to ensure smooth transitions between poses.
The engineer described how the parts moved kinematically during the test.
The animation was created kinematically to ensure realistic character movement.
Researchers studied the athlete's jump kinematically to improve performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dynamically - in a way that considers forces and causes of motion
  • synonym: mechanically - in a way that relates to mechanics or machinery
