null

verb
to make void
7.6
Pronunciation: / nʌl / (AmE), / nʌl / (BrE)
to make something legally void or invalid; to render null.

Examples

To null a contract
The judge nulled the agreement because it violated state law.
If a contract is signed under duress, it can be nulled by the court.
The election results were nulled after evidence of fraud was discovered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invalidate - to make something not legally acceptable or effective
  • antonym: validate - to make something legally acceptable or effective
  • synonym: void - to declare something invalid or without legal force
