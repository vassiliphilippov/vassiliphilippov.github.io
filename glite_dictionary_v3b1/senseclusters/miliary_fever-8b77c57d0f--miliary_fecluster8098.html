miliary fever

noun
historical epidemic disease marked by sweating
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪliɛri ˈfiːvər / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪəri ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
A historical infectious disease, epidemic in the 15th and 16th centuries, characterized by intense sweating and high mortality; now considered an obsolete medical diagnosis.

Examples

Miliary fever outbreak
During the 16th century, outbreaks of miliary fever devastated many European communities.
Physicians in the Renaissance often struggled to distinguish miliary fever from other epidemic diseases.
Symptoms of miliary fever included profuse sweating and a high risk of death.
