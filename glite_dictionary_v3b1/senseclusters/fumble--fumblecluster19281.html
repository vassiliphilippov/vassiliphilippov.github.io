fumble

verb
move clumsily or blindly
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈfʌmbəl / (BrE)
to handle or search for something in a clumsy or awkward way, often using your hands

Examples

To fumble for keys
He fumbled through the crowded room, bumping into chairs and tables.
In the dark, she fumbled along the wall searching for the light switch.
They fumbled their way down the narrow staircase, unable to see their feet.
Don't fumble the ball during the game or you'll give the other team an advantage.
He began to fumble with the keys, unable to unlock the door in the dark.
She fumbled through her bag, looking for her ringing phone.
He fumbled around in his bag, trying to find his keys.
In the confusion, Mary fumbled through the drawer looking for a pen.
She fumbled for the light switch in the pitch-black room.

  • antonym: manage - to handle or control successfully
