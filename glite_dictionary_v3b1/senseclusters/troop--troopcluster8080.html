troop

noun
group of soldiers
3.2 5.42/m
Pronunciation: / truÀêp / (AmE), / truÀêp / (BrE)
a group of soldiers or a military unit, especially organized for a particular operation

Examples

A troop of soldiers
A troop of soldiers was sent to reinforce the front line.
Local villagers watched as a troop passed through their town at dawn.
The troop marched steadily across the bridge in perfect formation.
Each troop was responsible for covering a different section of the territory.
The commander led his troop into battle with precision and discipline.
They restructured the cavalry, assigning new officers to every troop.
