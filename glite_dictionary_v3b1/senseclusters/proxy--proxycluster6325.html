proxy

verb
to act as intermediary
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈprɑːk.siː/ /; / /ˈprɑːk.sɪ/ (AmE), /ˈprɒk.siː/ /; / /ˈprɒk.sɪ/ (BrE)
to act as a substitute or intermediary for someone else, or to forward something on their behalf

Examples

To proxy a request
The application will proxy your internet traffic to improve security.
Some companies proxy employee requests through a central server for monitoring.
If the website is blocked, you can proxy your connection to access it.
She proxied for her manager at the meeting when he was away.
If you cannot attend the vote, you may have someone proxy for you.
He proxied on behalf of his colleague during the conference call.

  • synonym: stand in - to take the place of someone temporarily
