rush aster

noun
a species of aster plant
9.1
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ ˈæstər / (AmE), / rʌʃ ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
A species of aster (Symphyotrichum boreale) native to North America, with narrow leaves and pale violet or white flowers.

Examples

The rush aster plant
Conservationists are working to protect habitats where rush aster naturally occurs.
The rush aster adds a touch of delicate color to wetlands during late summer.
The narrow leaves of the rush aster distinguish it from other similar wildflowers.
