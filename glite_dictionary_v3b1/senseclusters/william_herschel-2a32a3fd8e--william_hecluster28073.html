William Herschel

proper noun
English astronomer; discovered Uranus and infrared radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəm ˈhɜrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈhɜːʃəl / (BrE)
(1738–1822) English astronomer and composer, born in Germany, known for discovering the planet Uranus, cataloguing stars, and discovering infrared radiation.

Examples

William Herschel discovered Uranus
William Herschel and his sister Caroline made significant contributions to astronomy.
William Herschel was the first to identify the planet Uranus in 1781.
The discovery of infrared radiation is credited to William Herschel.
