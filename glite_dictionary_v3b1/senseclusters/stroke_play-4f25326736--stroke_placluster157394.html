stroke play

noun
golf scoring by total strokes
Pronunciation: / stroʊk pleɪ / (AmE), / strəʊk pleɪ / (BrE)
A method of scoring in golf in which the winner is the player who takes the fewest total strokes over one or more rounds.

Examples

Stroke play golf scoring
He prefers stroke play to match play because it rewards consistent performance.
Many professional golf events use stroke play rather than head-to-head formats.
The tournament was decided using stroke play rules over four rounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: match play - golf scoring by holes won rather than total strokes
