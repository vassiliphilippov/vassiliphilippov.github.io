halloysite

noun
clay mineral
Pronunciation: / həˈlɔɪsaɪt / (AmE), / həˈlɔɪsaɪt / (BrE)
a clay mineral commonly found in volcanic rocks, used in ceramics and as an industrial material

Examples

White halloysite mineral
The white layer in the rock sample was identified as halloysite by the laboratory.
Ceramic manufacturers often use halloysite because of its unique properties.
The presence of halloysite indicates past volcanic activity in the region.
