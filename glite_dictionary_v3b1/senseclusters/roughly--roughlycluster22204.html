roughly

adverb
approximately, not exactly
1.6 37/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÊŒf.li / (AmE), / ËˆrÊŒf.li / (BrE)
approximately; not exactly but close to a particular number, amount, or degree.

Examples

Roughly three hours
The journey took roughly three hours by train.
There were roughly twenty people at the meeting last night.
Tickets for the concert cost roughly fifty dollars each.

Synonyms

  • about
    1.0 509/m
    about is common everyday speech for giving an approximate number or time, while roughly sounds a bit less precise.
  • approximately
    3.2 55/m
    approximately is formal and used in exact contexts like reports or measurements, while roughly is more informal and vague.
  • around
    1.0 28/m
    around is a casual way to say numbers are not exact, while roughly also means approximate but may sound slightly firmer.
  • more or less
    2.1 21/m
    more or less is an idiom that says something is broadly true but vague, while roughly focuses on numeric approximation.
  • or so
    1.0 44/m
    or so attaches after a number to show a small margin up or down, while roughly can be used anywhere to mean approximate.
  • just about
    2.7 39/m
    just about means almost or nearly complete in many situations, while roughly means an approximate amount without implying completion.
  • nearly
    1.0 193/m
    nearly means almost to a high degree or just before something happens, while roughly means an approximate amount or value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approximately - close to but not exactly a particular number or amount
  • antonym: exactly - in a precise and accurate way, with no inaccuracy or approximation
