judge

verb
decide the outcome of a competition
2.3 6.49/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’ÊŒdÊ’ / (AmE), / dÊ’ÊŒdÊ’ / (BrE)
to decide the outcome of a competition or contest, or to act as a judge in such an event

Examples

To judge a contest
He has judged numerous cooking contests across the country.
It can be difficult to judge between two equally talented finalists.
She was selected to judge the singing competition this year.

Synonyms

  • adjudicate
    7.8 1.67/m
    adjudicate means to make an official decision in disputes or competitions, while judge means to decide the outcome or act as a contest judge.
  • jury
    6.5 0.16/m
    jury evaluates entries as a panel member in competitions, while judge refers to deciding a contest's outcome or acting as its official.
  • call
    3.4 21/m
    call is an immediate sports or game decision by an official, while judge means to decide a competition more fully or evaluate entries.
  • decide
    1.0 63/m
    decide brings a contest or disagreement to an end by choosing, while judge means to decide a competition or act as its official evaluator.
  • adjudge
    8.1 0.25/m
    adjudge officially declares a legal decision, often in court, while judge means to decide a competition or act as its event official.

  • synonym: umpire - person who arbitrates sporting events, especially in baseball or cricket
