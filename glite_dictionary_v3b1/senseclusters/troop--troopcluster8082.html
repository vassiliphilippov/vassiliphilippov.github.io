troop

verb
move as a group
3.8 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / truÀêp / (AmE), / truÀêp / (BrE)
to move or walk somewhere as a group, especially in a slow or orderly way

Examples

To troop into class
After the meeting ended, employees trooped out of the conference hall in silence.
Every morning, the children troop into the classroom when the bell rings.
We all trooped down to the beach for an afternoon swim.
After the match, the fans trooped out of the stadium together.
The children trooped past the teachers in orderly lines.
They trooped into the hall for the awards ceremony.

Synonyms

  • march
    3.3 4.23/m
    march is steady and often military, while troop is a general way for a group to move together, often slowly.
  • process
    7.2 0.55/m
    process is formal movement in a line for a ceremony, while troop is a common word for a group moving together in order.
  • file
    3.7 4.42/m
    file means people move one after another in a line, while troop means a group moves together, not single-file.

Related Terms

  • synonym: file - to walk one behind another in a line
  • synonym: march - to walk with regular steps as a group, especially as part of a procession
  • synonym: parade - to walk in a formal procession, typically to be seen by others
  • antonym: straggle - to move along slowly, often behind a group
