respiratory acidosis

noun

A condition in which the acidity of the blood increases due to reduced gas exchange in the lungs, causing excess carbon dioxide to form carbonic acid.

Examples

Respiratory acidosis condition
Chronic lung diseases like emphysema can lead to respiratory acidosis if left untreated.
Doctors monitor blood pH closely in patients at risk of respiratory acidosis.
The patient developed respiratory acidosis after experiencing difficulty breathing for several days.

Synonyms
