father

noun
originator or founder of something
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑðɚ /; / ˈfɑðər / (AmE), / ˈfɑːðə / (BrE)
a man who originates, founds, or is an important figure in a family, institution, idea, or movement

Examples

Father of the nation
George Washington is often called the father of his country.
He became known as the father of the movement that swept the nation.
Marie Curie is regarded as the father of modern radioactivity research in a figurative sense.
He was known as the father of the Smith family, having established their lineage.
Historians often refer to the tribe’s original father when tracing their ancestry.
The values passed down by our family's first father are still cherished today.
Many credit the fathers of modern medicine for pioneering important techniques.
The father of the university was honored at the anniversary celebration.
The fathers of the city met to discuss the new zoning laws.
