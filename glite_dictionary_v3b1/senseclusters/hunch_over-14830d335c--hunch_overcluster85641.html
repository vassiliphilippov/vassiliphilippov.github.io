hunch over

verb
to bend forward with rounded shoulders
5.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /hʌntʃ ˈoʊ.vər/ /; / /hʌnʃ ˈoʊ.vər/ (AmE), /hʌntʃ ˈəʊ.və/ /; / /hʌnʃ ˈəʊ.və/ (BrE)
to bend forward with your shoulders and upper back rounded, usually because of posture, tiredness, or concentration

Examples

To hunch over a desk
After hours at the computer, she would hunch over and rub her back.
Don't hunch over your desk or you'll strain your neck.
The old man tended to hunch over as he walked down the street.
After hours of working, she began to hunch over her laptop, barely noticing her poor posture.
Children often hunch over their books while reading at their desks.
The old man would hunch over his cane as he walked down the street.
