fingerprinting

noun
taking fingerprints for identification
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪŋɡərˌprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of making inked or digital impressions of a person's fingerprints, usually for identification purposes

Examples

Police fingerprinting
All new employees must undergo fingerprinting as part of the security clearance process.
Modern fingerprinting techniques now use digital scanners instead of ink.
The police conducted fingerprinting at the crime scene to identify potential suspects.
