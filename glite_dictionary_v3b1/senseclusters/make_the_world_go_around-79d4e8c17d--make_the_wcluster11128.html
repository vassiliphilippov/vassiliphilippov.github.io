make the world go around

verb
be the essential driving force of life or society
Pronunciation: / meɪk ðə wɝːld ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / meɪk ðə wɜːld ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to be the most important thing that causes everything else to happen or function smoothly, often referring to money, love, or other key motivators in life or society

Examples

Money makes the world go around
Many believe that money is what truly makes the world go around.
She says that kindness, not wealth, is what makes the world go around.
Some people argue that love is what makes the world go around.
