self-determination

noun
right of a people to govern themselves
5.3 4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf dɪˌtɜrməˈneɪʃən /; / ˌsɛlf dɪˌtɝː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf dɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌsɛlf dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the right or ability of a person, group, or nation to make their own decisions and govern themselves without outside control

Examples

Self-determination right
Some countries still struggle to achieve full self-determination for all of their people.
The United Nations Charter recognizes the right of self-determination for all peoples.
The concept of self-determination was a driving force behind many independence movements.
After years of colonial rule, the country finally gained self-determination and established its own government.
Many international conflicts have arisen over the issue of self-determination for ethnic groups.
Personal self-determination is key to developing confidence and a sense of purpose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autonomy - the right or condition of self-government
  • antonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by someone else
  • synonym: independence - the condition of being free from outside control
