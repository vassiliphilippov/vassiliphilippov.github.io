Coombs test

noun
antibody blood test
4.4
Pronunciation: /kuːmz test/ (AmE), /kuːmz test/ (BrE)
a blood test used to detect antibodies (or complement proteins) on red blood cells, often for diagnosing anemia, checking before blood transfusion, or prenatal screening

Examples

Coombs test blood
The doctor ordered a Coombs test to check for antibodies in the patient's blood.
A positive Coombs test can indicate autoimmune hemolytic anemia.
Before the transfusion, the laboratory performed a Coombs test to ensure compatibility.
The doctor ordered a Coombs test to check for antibodies before the blood transfusion.
A positive Coombs test result can indicate an autoimmune reaction against red blood cells.
Pregnant women may receive a Coombs test to screen for potential blood group incompatibility.
The doctor ordered a Coombs test to check for antibodies on the patient's red blood cells.
A positive Coombs test can help diagnose certain types of anemia.
Before the transfusion, the laboratory performed a Coombs test to ensure compatibility.
