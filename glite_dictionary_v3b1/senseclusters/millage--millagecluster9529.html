millage

noun
property tax rate
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
a rate used to calculate property tax, expressed as the amount per thousand dollars of assessed value

Examples

Property millage rate
The city council voted to increase the millage to fund new schools.
Homeowners often check the local millage before buying property in a new area.
A higher millage means residents will pay more in property taxes each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assessment - the process of determining the value of property for tax purposes
  • synonym: tax rate - the percentage at which an individual or corporation is taxed
