ikat

verb
to dye threads for patterns
Pronunciation: / ˈiːkɑt / (AmE), / ˈiːkæt / (BrE)
to dye yarns or threads before weaving, using a resist technique to create patterns in the finished fabric

Examples

To ikat the threads
Artisans carefully ikat the threads before weaving to achieve the traditional geometric designs.
It can take weeks to ikat enough yarn for a single patterned cloth.
She learned how to ikat silk threads from her grandmother, preserving the family tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tie-dye - to dye fabric by tying it to produce patterns
