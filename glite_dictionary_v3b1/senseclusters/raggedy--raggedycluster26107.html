raggedy

adjective
rough or uneven
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈræɡɪdi / (AmE), / ˈræɡɪdi / (BrE)
looking old, worn out, torn, or messy; having a rough or uneven appearance

Examples

A raggedy old blanket
The old blanket looked raggedy after years of use and washing.
He wore a raggedy shirt with holes and frayed sleeves to the party.
The edges of the torn photograph were raggedy and uneven.
The children wore raggedy clothes that were full of holes.
She found an old, raggedy teddy bear in the attic.
After the long hike, his backpack looked completely raggedy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neat - tidy and in good order
  • synonym: ragged - having a rough or uneven edge or surface
  • synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface
  • synonym: tattered - old and torn; in poor condition
