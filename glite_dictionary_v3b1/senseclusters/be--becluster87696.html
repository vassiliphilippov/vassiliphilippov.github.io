be

verb
linking verb indicating state or identity
4.5
Pronunciation: / bi /; / biː / (AmE), / biː / (BrE)
to be the same as, to represent, to have a particular identity, role, or function, or to make up something

Examples

To be a teacher
He is an excellent teacher who inspires his students every day.
I am excited about the upcoming concert.
The soup was too salty for my taste.
His family is his only support in difficult times.
The seven players are the entire team on the field.
These notes constitute her savings for the year.
For tax purposes, five boxes of cereal are one case.
His words were an exact echo of her own thoughts.
In this situation, a negative result is a failure.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: become - to begin to be something; to turn into
  • synonym: compose - to be or make up the parts of; form
  • synonym: constitute - to form or make up; to compose
  • antonym: differ - to be different or not the same
  • synonym: equal - to be the same in value, amount, function, or meaning
  • synonym: represent - to stand for or symbolize; to amount to
  • degree lower: seem - to give the impression of being
  • synonym: serve - to act in a specific role or capacity
