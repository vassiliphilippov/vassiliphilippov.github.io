double

noun
person resembling another, often a famous figure
1.0 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌ.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈdʌ.bəl/ (BrE)
a person or thing that is a copy, substitute, or stand-in for another (such as a lookalike, stunt double, or digital double)

Examples

A double for movies
Many fans were fooled when the singer's double appeared at the airport.
She was mistaken for the actress because she looks so much like her double.
The producer hired a double for the dangerous scenes in the action movie.
After the accident, the studio hired a new double for the leading actor.
She worked as a double in several action films before landing her own roles.
The director praised the double for his skill in the car chase scene.
The studio created a double of the lead actor to film the dangerous rooftop stunts.
For the close-up, the director used a double rendered with CGI to age the character backward.
The VFX team refined the motion data so the double moved exactly like the real actor on screen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digital double - a computer-created replica of an actor used in VFX and stunts
  • synonym: doppelganger - a double or counterpart of a living person
  • synonym: lookalike - a person who closely resembles another, especially a celebrity
  • synonym: stunt double - a person who specifically performs dangerous stunts in place of an actor
