triad

noun
three-note chord (music)
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌæd / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.ad / (BrE)
(Music) A chord consisting of three notes: a root, its third, and its fifth, forming either a major or minor chord.

Examples

Major triad chord
Learning to play a major triad is fundamental for any pianist.
She quickly identified the triad in the melody by listening to the first three notes.
The composer used a triad to build the harmonic structure of the piece.
