suppliant

noun
a person making a humble request
6.2 7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌpliənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪənt / (BrE)
a person who asks for something earnestly and humbly, especially by prayer or petition.

Examples

A suppliant before the king
As a humble suppliant, he pleaded for mercy before the king.
Crowds of suppliants gathered at the temple, each hoping for a favorable answer.
The suppliant knelt before the altar, her hands clasped in silent prayer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beggar - a person who asks others for charity, usually for money or food
  • antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help or money, especially for a good cause
  • synonym: petitioner - a person who presents a formal request to an authority
