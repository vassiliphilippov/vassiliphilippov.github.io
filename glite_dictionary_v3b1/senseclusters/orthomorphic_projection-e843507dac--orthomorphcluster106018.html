orthomorphic projection

noun
map projection preserving local shapes
7.2 9.0
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.θoʊ.ˈmɔːr.fɪk prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɔː.θəʊ.ˈmɔː.fɪk prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (BrE)
a map projection in which small areas are represented in their true shape, preserving local angles and forms

Examples

An orthomorphic projection map
Cartographers often use an orthomorphic projection when accurate local shapes are essential.
The Mercator map is a type of orthomorphic projection used for navigation at sea.
This atlas includes several maps created using an orthomorphic projection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conformal projection - a map projection that preserves local angles and shapes
  • antonym: equal-area projection - a map projection that preserves area, not necessarily shapes
