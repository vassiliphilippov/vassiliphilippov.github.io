viola da braccio

noun
historical string instrument, like a viola
6.9 9.4
Pronunciation: / viˈoʊlə də ˈbrɑːtʃoʊ / (AmE), / viˈəʊlə də ˈbrætʃəʊ / (BrE)
A historical string instrument of the viol family, similar in range to the modern viola and held on the arm while played.

Examples

The viola da braccio instrument
He studied the techniques used to play the viola da braccio in early Italian music.
The viola da braccio was popular in Baroque ensembles before the modern viola emerged.
Unlike the viola da gamba, the viola da braccio is held on the arm when played.

Related Terms

  • synonym: viola - a stringed instrument of the violin family, slightly larger than a violin
  • antonym: viola da gamba - a member of the viol family played between the legs
