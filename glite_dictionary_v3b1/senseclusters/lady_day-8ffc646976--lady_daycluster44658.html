Lady Day

noun
Christian festival on March 25; quarter day in UK and Ireland
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.diː deɪ/ /; / /ˈleɪ.dɪ deɪ/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.diː deɪ/ /; / /ˈleɪ.dɪ deɪ/ (BrE)
A festival in the Christian calendar, observed on March 25, commemorating the Annunciation by the angel Gabriel to the Virgin Mary; also one of the traditional quarter days in England, Wales, and Ireland.

Examples

Lady Day festival
Church services are held on Lady Day to mark the Annunciation.
Many schools in England used to begin their new year on Lady Day.
The tradition of paying rent on Lady Day has gradually faded over time.
