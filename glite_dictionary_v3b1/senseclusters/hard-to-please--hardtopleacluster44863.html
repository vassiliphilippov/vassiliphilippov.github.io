hard-to-please

adjective
difficult to satisfy or please
6.8 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Àåh…ërd t…ô Ààpliz / (AmE), / Àåh…ëÀêd t…ô ÀàpliÀêz / (BrE)
(of a person) demanding and difficult to satisfy; not easily pleased or contented.

Examples

A hard-to-please customer
She is known to be hard-to-please when it comes to choosing a restaurant.
The hard-to-please critic rarely gives a positive review.
Working for a hard-to-please boss pushed him to always do his best.

  • picky
    4.7 2.98/m
    picky focuses on small details and refuses many choices, while hard-to-please describes a person generally demanding and hard to satisfy.
  • particular
    4.4 8.95/m
    particular suggests careful, exact taste or habits and can sound neutral, while hard-to-please stresses being demanding and unhappy with results.
  • finicky
    7.1 0.86/m
    finicky is very fussy about small details or food, while hard-to-please is a broader term for anyone hard to satisfy.
  • choosy
    3.9 0.58/m
    choosy means careful when choosing between options, while hard-to-please means generally demanding and not easily satisfied in many situations.
