desert willow

noun
flowering tree of southwestern North America
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛzərt ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈdezət ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
a small shrubby tree of dry regions in southwestern North America, with long narrow leaves, showy purplish flowers, and elongated seed pods; not a true willow

Examples

A desert willow tree
A mature desert willow can provide shade and attract hummingbirds to your yard.
Gardeners often plant desert willow for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
The desert willow blooms with beautiful purple flowers in late spring.
