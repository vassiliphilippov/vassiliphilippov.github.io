fall

verb
slope downward (of land)
Pronunciation: / fɔl /; / fɑl / (AmE), / fɔːl / (BrE)
to move or drop downward from a higher to a lower position, often suddenly or by gravity

Examples

To fall down stairs
The fields fall gently towards the valley below.
The land falls away to the south, giving a view of the river.
The path fell steeply into a wooded ravine.
After the performance, the curtain fell slowly to end the show.
As the sun set, the temperature began to fall quickly.
Her hair would always fall softly over her shoulders.
Heavy snow began to fall in the early hours of the morning.
The ash from the volcano continued to fall on the nearby village.
When the rain started to fall, everyone rushed inside for shelter.

  • synonym: plunge - to fall or jump suddenly from a high place
  • antonym: rise - to move upward or increase in position or level
  • synonym: set about - to start doing something with energy or determination
  • synonym: shine - (of light) to emit or reflect light on a surface
  • synonym: strike - (of light or sound) to hit or reach a surface
  • synonym: topple - to fall or cause to fall from a position of balance
