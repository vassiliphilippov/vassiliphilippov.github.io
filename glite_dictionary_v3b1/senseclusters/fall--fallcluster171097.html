fall

verb
slope downward (of land)
1.0 164/m
Pronunciation: / f…îl /; / f…ël / (AmE), / f…îÀêl / (BrE)
to move or drop downward from a higher to a lower position, often suddenly or by gravity

Examples

To fall down stairs
The fields fall gently towards the valley below.
The land falls away to the south, giving a view of the river.
The path fell steeply into a wooded ravine.
After the performance, the curtain fell slowly to end the show.
As the sun set, the temperature began to fall quickly.
Her hair would always fall softly over her shoulders.
Heavy snow began to fall in the early hours of the morning.
The ash from the volcano continued to fall on the nearby village.
When the rain started to fall, everyone rushed inside for shelter.

Synonyms

  • drop down
    1.8 2.73/m
    drop down is used for moving from a higher to a lower place, while fall is a general, often sudden downward movement.
  • descend
    2.7 15/m
    descend is a more formal verb for going down slowly or in control, while fall often means dropping suddenly by gravity.
  • come down
    2.8 7.58/m
    come down describes moving or becoming lower in casual talk or prices, while fall stresses a physical drop by gravity.
  • plunge
    3.8 7.00/m
    plunge means to fall or drop very quickly and with force, while fall is a general downward movement.
  • plummet
    5.8 6.38/m
    plummet means to fall steeply and suddenly, often used for prices or numbers, while fall is general downward movement.
  • go down
    1.9 13/m
    go down is used when amounts or levels become lower or when things move down slowly, while fall implies a drop by gravity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often losing structure
  • synonym: drop - to let or cause something to fall
  • synonym: get down to - to begin doing something seriously or with effort
  • synonym: plunge - to fall or jump suddenly from a high place
  • antonym: rise - to move upward or increase in position or level
  • synonym: set about - to start doing something with energy or determination
  • synonym: shine - (of light) to emit or reflect light on a surface
  • synonym: strike - (of light or sound) to hit or reach a surface
  • synonym: topple - to fall or cause to fall from a position of balance
