reviewability

noun
ability to be reviewed
Pronunciation: / rɪˌvjuːəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / rɪˌvjuːəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be reviewed, especially by a higher authority or court

Examples

The reviewability of decisions
The reviewability of administrative decisions is often limited by specific laws.
Lawyers debated the reviewability of the agency's ruling in court.
Questions about the reviewability of the contract terms arose during the negotiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appealability - the quality of being able to be appealed to a higher court
  • antonym: finality - the quality of being final and not subject to review
