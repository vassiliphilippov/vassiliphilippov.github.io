arrogate

verb
claim without right or authority
Pronunciation: / ˈær.əˌɡeɪt /; / ˈærəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈær.əˌɡeɪt /; / ˈærəɡeɪt /; / ˈærəˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to claim or take a right, privilege, or authority for yourself without having the right to do so

Examples

To arrogate power
It is unjust to arrogate the achievements of others as your own.
No government should arrogate the people's fundamental rights.
The new committee tried to arrogate more power than was allocated to them.
It is not for you to arrogate the authority of the committee.
Some leaders arrogate privileges to themselves that rightfully belong to the people.
They attempted to arrogate powers that did not belong to them.
He tends to arrogate authority in meetings, often ignoring the views of others.
Some leaders arrogate the right to speak for everyone, even without broad support.
The committee tried to arrogate powers that did not lawfully belong to it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claim - to demand or assert something as one's right
  • antonym: relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim
  • synonym: usurp - to take a position of power or importance illegally or by force
