color blindness

noun
policy or stance of ignoring race
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌl.ər ˈblaɪnd.nəs/ /; / /ˈkɔːl.ər ˈblaɪnd.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈkʌl.ə ˈblaɪnd.nəs/ (BrE)
The belief or policy of treating people equally by ignoring race or ethnicity, often criticized for masking or allowing systemic racial inequalities to persist.

Examples

Color blindness policy
The company's color blindness policy ignored statistical evidence of hiring disparities last year.
Scholars argue that proclaiming color blindness can conceal structural racism rather than reduce it.
During the debate she criticized the mayor's claim of color blindness in city policy implementation.
Many people argue that colour blindness in hiring ignores structural inequalities and harms minority applicants.
Her school's policy of colour blindness failed to address the persistent achievement gap between students.
Critics say advocating colour blindness reduces complex histories to a single misleading idea in public debates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color blindness - American-English spelling variant used for the same social/political concept.
  • antonym: color-consciousness - awareness of race and its effects, often leading to policies that explicitly address racial disparities
  • synonym: race-blindness - the practice or belief of not considering race in judgments or policies
  • antonym: racial awareness - Active recognition of race and its effects; often presented as the opposite of colour-blind approaches.
