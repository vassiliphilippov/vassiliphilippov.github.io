Camelus dromedarius

noun
one-humped desert camel
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkæm.ə.ləs drɑː.mə.ˈdeɪ.rɪ.əs/ (AmE), /ˈkæm.ə.ləs drɒ.mə.ˈdeə.rɪ.əs/ (BrE)
A species of camel with a single hump, native to the deserts of northern Africa and southwestern Asia; commonly known as the dromedary.

Examples

The Camelus dromedarius camel
Researchers study how the Camelus dromedarius copes with extreme heat and dehydration in its native habitat.
The Camelus dromedarius is well adapted to survive long periods without water in harsh desert environments.
Unlike the Bactrian camel, the Camelus dromedarius has only one hump on its back.

  • synonym: dromedary - Single-humped camel of African and Asian deserts
