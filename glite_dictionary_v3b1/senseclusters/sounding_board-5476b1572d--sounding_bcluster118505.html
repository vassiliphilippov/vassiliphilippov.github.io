sounding board

noun
board in instrument amplifying sound
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndɪŋ ˌbɔːd / (BrE)
A thin board in a musical instrument that vibrates to reinforce and amplify its sound.

Examples

Piano sounding board
Damage to the piano's sounding board can significantly affect its acoustic properties.
Luthiers carefully select wood for the sounding board to enhance the instrument's quality.
The guitar's sounding board is essential for producing a rich, resonant tone.
