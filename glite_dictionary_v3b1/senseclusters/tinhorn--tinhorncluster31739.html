tinhorn

noun
pretender to importance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪnˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈtɪnˌhɔːn / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be important, wealthy, or skilled but actually is not

Examples

A tinhorn bragger
Everyone in the saloon knew the tinhorn was bluffing about his fortune.
The mayor dismissed the critic as just another tinhorn seeking attention.
He acted like a big shot, but people soon realized he was a tinhorn with no real influence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fraud - a person who is not what they claim to be
  • antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
  • synonym: impostor - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive others
