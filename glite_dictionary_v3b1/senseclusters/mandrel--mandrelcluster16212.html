mandrel

noun
shaping rod or bar
9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmæn.drəl/ (AmE), /ˈmæn.drəl/ (BrE)
a rod or bar inserted into material to shape, support, or form it during manufacturing or machining

Examples

Mandrel for pipe bending
The technician inserted a mandrel into the pipe before bending it to prevent collapse.
A mandrel is essential when forming rings to ensure they keep their shape.
During the manufacturing process, the sheet metal was wrapped around a mandrel to create a perfect cylinder.
The technician inserted a mandrel into the pipe before bending it to the desired shape.
To create uniform rings, each one was shaped around a steel mandrel.
The glassblower used a mandrel to support the molten glass as it cooled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: core - a central part or support around which material is shaped
  • synonym: spindle - a rotating rod used to hold or spin material in machines
