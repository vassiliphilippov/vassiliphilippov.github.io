floppy infant syndrome

noun
condition causing poor muscle tone in infants
Pronunciation: / ˈflɑpi ˈɪnfənt ˈsɪndroʊm / (AmE), / ˈflɒpi ˈɪnfənt ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition in infants characterized by reduced muscle tone, resulting in apparent weakness and limpness.

Examples

Floppy infant syndrome diagnosis
Early intervention can improve outcomes for infants diagnosed with floppy infant syndrome.
Parents were educated on the signs of floppy infant syndrome at the neonatal clinic.
The pediatrician was concerned about floppy infant syndrome after noticing the baby's weak muscle tone.
