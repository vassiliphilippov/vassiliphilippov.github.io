biotinylate

verb
to attach biotin
Pronunciation: / baɪˈɑːtənɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / baɪˈəʊtɪnɪˌleɪt / (BrE)
to attach a biotin molecule to another molecule, typically for detection or labeling in scientific experiments

Examples

To biotinylate antibodies
Scientists often biotinylate antibodies to make them easier to detect in laboratory tests.
The protocol requires you to biotinylate the protein before proceeding with the assay.
After they biotinylated the DNA, it could be visualized using a special dye.
