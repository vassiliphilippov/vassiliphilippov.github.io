Bougainvillea

noun
genus of tropical ornamental vines
Pronunciation: / ˌboʊɡənˈvɪliə / (AmE), / ˌbʊɡənˈvɪliə / (BrE)
A genus of ornamental tropical woody vines with brightly colored bracts, commonly grown for decorative purposes in gardens and landscapes.

Examples

A Bougainvillea plant
A healthy Bougainvillea can grow quickly and provide excellent natural shade.
Many gardeners prefer Bougainvillea for its ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
The vibrant Bougainvillea covered the entire garden wall with its magenta blossoms.
