Benedict Arnold

proper noun
famous American traitor in the Revolutionary War
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛnədɪkt ˈɑrnəld / (AmE), / ˌbɛnədɪkt ˈɑːnəld / (BrE)
(1741–1801) An American general during the Revolutionary War who defected to the British, becoming infamous as a traitor.

Examples

Benedict Arnold traitor
After switching sides, he became known as the Benedict Arnold of his generation.
Calling someone a Benedict Arnold is a serious accusation of betrayal.
Many Americans still consider Benedict Arnold the most infamous traitor in their history.
