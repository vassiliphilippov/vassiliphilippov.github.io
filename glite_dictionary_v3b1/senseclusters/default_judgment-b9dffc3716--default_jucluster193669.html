default judgment

noun
judgment for plaintiff when defendant fails to appear
1.6 5.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfɔːlt ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈɑːlt ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfɔːlt ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt/ (BrE)
a judgment entered by a court in favor of the plaintiff when the defendant fails to respond or appear in court

Examples

A default judgment in court
If you do not appear in court, the plaintiff may obtain a default judgment against you.
The court awarded a default judgment after the defendant failed to respond to the lawsuit.
To avoid a default judgment, the company hired a lawyer to file an answer in time.
If you ignore the lawsuit, you could end up with a default judgement against you.
The court issued a default judgement after the defendant failed to show up for the hearing.
The plaintiff won a default judgement when the company did not respond to the complaint.

  • synonym: summary judgement - legal ruling made without a full trial
