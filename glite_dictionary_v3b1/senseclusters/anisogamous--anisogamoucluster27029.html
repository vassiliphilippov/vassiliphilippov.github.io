anisogamous

adjective
having gametes of different sizes or forms
9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.ɪ.ˈsɑː.gə.məs/ /; / /ə.nɪ.ˈsɑː.gə.məs/ (AmE), /æn.ɪ.ˈsɒ.gə.məs/ /; / /ə.nɪ.ˈsɒ.gə.məs/ (BrE)
relating to a form of sexual reproduction in which the gametes (reproductive cells) differ in size or form, typically with one being larger than the other.

Examples

An anisogamous reproduction
An anisogamous reproductive strategy can increase the likelihood of successful fertilization.
In anisogamous organisms, male and female gametes are distinctly different from one another.
Some species of algae are anisogamous, producing sperm and eggs that differ in size.
