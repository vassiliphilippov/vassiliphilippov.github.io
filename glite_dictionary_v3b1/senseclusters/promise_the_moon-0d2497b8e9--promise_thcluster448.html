promise the moon

verb
make extravagant or unrealistic promises
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑmɪs ðə mun / (AmE), / ˈprɒmɪs ðə muːn / (BrE)
to make extravagant or unrealistic promises, often with no intention or means to fulfill them

Examples

To promise the moon
It's easy to promise the moon, but much harder to deliver results.
Salespeople sometimes promise the moon just to close a deal.
The candidate continued to promise the moon to win over undecided voters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overpromise - to promise more than can realistically be delivered
  • synonym: promise the earth - to make extravagant or impossible promises
  • antonym: underpromise - to promise less than what is likely to be delivered
