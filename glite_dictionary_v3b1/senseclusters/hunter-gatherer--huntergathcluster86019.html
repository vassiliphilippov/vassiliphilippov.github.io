hunter-gatherer

noun
person in a society living by hunting and gathering
Pronunciation: / ˌhʌntər ˈɡæðərər /; / ˈhʌn.tɚ ˈɡæð.ɚ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˌhʌntə ˈɡæðərə /; / ˈhʌn.tə ˈɡæð.ər.ə / (BrE)
a member of a society or group that lives by hunting animals and gathering wild plants for food, rather than by farming or raising animals

Examples

A hunter-gatherer society
A hunter-gatherer lifestyle is believed to have shaped much of early human development.
Archaeologists found evidence of hunter-gatherer communities along the riverbanks.
Many hunter-gatherers moved seasonally to follow animal herds and edible plants.
Archaeologists have found tools used by ancient hunter-gatherers near the cave site.
Early humans lived as hunter-gatherers before the development of agriculture.
Some modern groups, such as the Hadza in Tanzania, still maintain a hunter-gatherer lifestyle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: farmer - a person who grows crops or raises animals for food
  • synonym: forager - a person or animal that searches widely for food or provisions
