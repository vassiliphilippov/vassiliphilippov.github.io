exceptional

adjective
deviating from normal ability, esp. in children
1.1
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəl / (BrE)
deviating widely from a norm of physical or mental ability, especially referring to children with abilities significantly different from the average, often used in educational contexts.

Examples

Exceptional children
He was identified as an exceptional student due to his advanced mathematical abilities.
The school provides resources for exceptional children who require additional support.
There are programs designed specifically for exceptional learners with unique educational needs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: special - relating to particular needs or abilities, often used regarding education
