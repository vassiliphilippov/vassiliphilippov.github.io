bulge

verb
cause to swell or protrude outward
1.0
Pronunciation: / bʌldʒ / (AmE), / bʌldʒ / (BrE)
to swell or stick out in a rounded shape; to protrude outward

Examples

To bulge outwards
A hernia can bulge the skin in the affected area.
The heavy books made the backpack bulge at the seams.
Tight packaging may bulge the sides of a box if overfilled.
Her eyes seemed to bulge in surprise at the announcement.
His pockets bulged with coins and receipts.
The bag began to bulge as it filled with groceries.
Her eyes seemed to bulge with surprise at the news.
His jacket began to bulge with all the souvenirs he collected.
The sack started to bulge when it was filled to capacity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protrude - to stick out from a surface
  • antonym: recede - to move back or retreat; to become less prominent
  • antonym: shrink - to become smaller in size, contrast with swelling
  • antonym: sink - to move downward below a surface or inward
  • synonym: swell - to become larger or rounder in size
