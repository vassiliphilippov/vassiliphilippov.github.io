geostrophic

adjective
balance of forces
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːəˈstrɑfɪk / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈstrɒfɪk / (BrE)
describing a state in which the pressure gradient force and the Coriolis force are balanced in a moving fluid, such as air or water

Examples

Geostrophic wind balance
Meteorologists often refer to geostrophic winds when discussing large-scale atmospheric circulation.
In the ocean, geostrophic currents result from the balance between pressure and the Earth's rotation.
The concept of geostrophic flow is fundamental in understanding weather patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrostatic - relating to the equilibrium of fluids at rest
