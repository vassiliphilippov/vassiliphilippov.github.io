unlearned

adjective
not educated or knowledgeable
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɝːnɪd, ˌʌnˈlɜrnɪd /; / ʌnˈlɜrnɪd, ˌʌnˈlɜrnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɜːnɪd, ˌʌnˈlɜːnɪd /; / ʌnˈlɜːnɪd, ˌʌnˈlɜːnd / (BrE)
lacking education or knowledge; not well educated or informed

Examples

An unlearned person
He admitted he was unlearned in matters of modern technology.
Her unlearned opinions were often dismissed by those with more experience.
The panel included both highly educated experts and a few unlearned members of the public.
He was an unlearned man, but he had a great deal of common sense.
In ancient times, most people were unlearned and could not read or write.
The lecture was too complex for the unlearned student to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: educated - having received a good education or instruction
  • synonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
