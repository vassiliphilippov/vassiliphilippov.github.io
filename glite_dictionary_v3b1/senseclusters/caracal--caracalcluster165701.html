caracal

noun
a medium-sized wild cat with tufted ears
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəkæl / (AmE), / ˈkarəkal / (BrE)
a medium-sized wild cat with long tufted ears, native to deserts and dry regions of northern Africa and southern Asia

Examples

A caracal cat
During the safari, we spotted a solitary caracal prowling through the grasslands.
The caracal is known for its remarkable jumping ability, allowing it to catch birds in mid-air.
Unlike other wild cats, the caracal has distinct black tufts on the tips of its ears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lynx - a wild cat with tufted ears and short tail
  • synonym: serval - a medium-sized African wild cat with large ears
