soapy

adjective
excessively ingratiating or smooth in manner
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊpi / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpi / (BrE)
(of a person's manner or speech) unpleasantly smooth, suave, or ingratiating, often in a way that is insincere or excessive.

Examples

Soapy speech
His soapy compliments made everyone at the meeting uncomfortable.
I don't trust politicians who give soapy speeches to gain favor with the public.
The waiter's soapy charm seemed insincere and over the top.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genuine - honest and sincere in manner or speech
  • synonym: smarmy - excessively flattering in an insincere way
  • synonym: unctuous - excessively flattering or ingratiating; oily
