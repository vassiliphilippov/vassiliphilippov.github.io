Orléans

proper noun
city in north central France, site of historic siege
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈliːənz / (AmE), / ɔːˈleɪɒ̃ / (BrE)
a city on the Loire River in north central France, historically known for the siege lifted by Joan of Arc in 1429

Examples

Orleans city France
Orléans is situated on the Loire River, about 130 kilometers southwest of Paris.
Joan of Arc famously helped lift the siege of Orléans during the Hundred Years' War.
The cathedral in Orléans attracts many tourists each year.
