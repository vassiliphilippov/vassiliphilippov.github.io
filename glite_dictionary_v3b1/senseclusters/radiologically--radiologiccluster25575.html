radiologically

adverb
involving radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that involves the use of radiation or medical imaging techniques (such as X-rays or MRI) for detection or treatment

Examples

To be radiologically examined
The tumor was radiologically examined before the doctors decided on a treatment plan.
Patients are radiologically screened to detect any signs of internal damage after exposure.
The area was radiologically assessed to ensure it was safe for the public.
The fracture was radiologically identified before the patient was taken to surgery.
The disease can be radiologically detected even in its early stages.
Doctors monitored the tumor's progress radiologically over several months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biologically - in a way that relates to biology or living organisms
  • antonym: clinically - in a way that relates to the observation and treatment of actual patients
  • synonym: medically - in a way that relates to medicine or medical treatment
