real Shylock

noun
ruthless or merciless moneylender
9.9
Pronunciation: / riːəl ˈʃaɪˌlɑk / (AmE), / rɪəl ˈʃaɪlɒk / (BrE)
A ruthless or merciless moneylender, especially one who charges excessively high interest; used allusively after the character Shylock in Shakespeare's The Merchant of Venice.

Examples

A real Shylock lender
Everyone warned me not to borrow from him, saying he's a real Shylock when it comes to collecting debts.
People avoided the bank after it hired a real Shylock to handle overdue accounts.
The landlord turned out to be a real Shylock, raising the rent at every opportunity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loan shark - a person who lends money at extremely high rates of interest, often illegally
  • synonym: usurer - a person who lends money at unreasonably high interest rates
