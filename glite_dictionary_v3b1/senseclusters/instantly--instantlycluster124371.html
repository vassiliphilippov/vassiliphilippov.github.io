Synonyms

    immediately is the common everyday word for happening at once, while instantly feels slightly sharper and more sudden.
  • instantaneously
    5.6 2/m
    instantaneously sounds formal and technical for a zero time event, while instantly is more natural in speech.
  • at once
    1.0 11/m
    at once tells someone to do something immediately now, while instantly highlights the complete lack of any delay.
  • right away
    1.0 30/m
    right away is a friendly spoken way to act immediately, while instantly gives a stronger, more sudden impression.
  • straightaway
    3.2 0.2/m
    straightaway is chiefly British and used in casual speech for quick action, while instantly is neutral and global.
  • promptly
    2.1 10/m
    promptly suggests acting at the right or expected time with no delay, while instantly stresses immediate, pure immediacy.
  • directly

    directly can mean soon after or without a middle step, while instantly always means at the very same moment with no delay.
