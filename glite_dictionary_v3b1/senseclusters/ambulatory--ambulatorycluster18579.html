ambulatory

noun
covered walkway in church or cloister
Pronunciation: / ˈæmbjəˌleɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈæmbjʊlət(ə)ri / (BrE)
(architecture) a covered passageway for walking, typically found around the apse or cloister of a church or monastery.

Examples

Church ambulatory
Monks used the ambulatory for silent reflection and prayer.
The architect designed an elegant ambulatory around the central garden.
The cathedral's ambulatory allowed visitors to walk behind the high altar.
