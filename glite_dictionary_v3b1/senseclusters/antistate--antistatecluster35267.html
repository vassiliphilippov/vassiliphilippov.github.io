antistate

noun
opponent of the state
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.tɪ.steɪt/ /; / /ˈæn.taɪ.steɪt/ (AmE), /ˈæn.tɪ.steɪt/ /; / /ˈæn.taɪ.steɪt/ (BrE)
a person, group, or movement that opposes the authority or existence of the state, often seeking its abolition

Examples

An antistate group
The activist group identified as antistate and called for the end of centralized government.
Some political philosophers have written extensively about antistate ideologies and their impact on society.
He was labeled an antistate after publicly criticizing all forms of government authority.

Related Terms

  • antonym: statist - a person who supports the authority and power of the state
