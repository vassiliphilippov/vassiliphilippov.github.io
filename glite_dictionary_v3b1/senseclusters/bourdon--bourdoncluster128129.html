bourdon

noun
drone pipe of bagpipes or organ
9.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ùÀêd…ën / (AmE), / Ààb ä…ôd…ín / (BrE)
a pipe of the bagpipe (or organ) that is tuned to produce a single continuous tone, usually at a fixed pitch; a drone pipe

Examples

The bourdon pipe
He adjusted the bourdon on his bagpipes to achieve a more resonant drone.
The low, steady hum of the bourdon is unmistakable in traditional Scottish music.
The organ's largest pipe, called the bourdon, produced a powerful, deep note.

Synonyms

  • drone pipe
    8.8 0.00/m
    a drone pipe is a bagpipe tube that produces a single steady background note, while a bourdon is that pipe tuned to one fixed pitch.
  • bordun
    9.9 0.01/m
    a bordun is a drone pipe or note used as a constant background sound, while a bourdon refers to the tuned drone pipe.
  • drone
    5.2 1.39/m
    a drone is a continuous low sound or instrument part, while a bourdon names the specific drone pipe tuned to one fixed pitch.
