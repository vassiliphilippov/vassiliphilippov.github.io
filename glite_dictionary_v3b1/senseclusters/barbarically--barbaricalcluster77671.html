barbarically

adverb
cruelly or brutally
7.5
Pronunciation: / bɑːrˈbærɪkli / (AmE), / bɑːˈbærɪkli / (BrE)
in a cruel, violent, or extremely uncivilized way

Examples

Act barbarically
The soldiers acted barbarically during the invasion, shocking the world with their cruelty.
Animals in the wild are sometimes hunted barbarically for their fur or tusks.
The dictator's regime punished dissenters barbarically, leaving many in fear.
The invaders acted barbarically, destroying everything in their path without mercy.
Some critics argued that the punishment was barbarically harsh for such a minor offense.
The animals were treated barbarically before new regulations were introduced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brutally - in a harsh or violent way
  • antonym: civilly - in a polite or civilized manner
  • synonym: cruelly - in a way that causes pain or suffering
  • antonym: kindly - in a gentle or caring way
