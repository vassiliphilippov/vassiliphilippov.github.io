antitrade

noun
tropical winds blowing above trade winds
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈtreɪd / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtreɪd / (BrE)
winds in tropical regions that blow from west to east above the trade winds

Examples

Antitrade winds blow
Jet streams at high altitudes often flow as antitrade winds above the equator.
Meteorologists track antitrade currents to study their impact on climate patterns.
The antitrade winds move in the opposite direction to the usual trade winds in the tropics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trade wind - winds that blow steadily from east to west in tropical regions
