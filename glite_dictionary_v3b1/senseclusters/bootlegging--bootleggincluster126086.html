bootlegging

noun
illegal production or sale of alcohol
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of making, distributing, or selling alcoholic liquor illegally

Examples

Bootlegging alcohol
During the 1920s, bootlegging was a common way to circumvent Prohibition laws in the United States.
He was arrested for bootlegging whiskey across state lines.
Police raided a warehouse that was being used for bootlegging operations.
