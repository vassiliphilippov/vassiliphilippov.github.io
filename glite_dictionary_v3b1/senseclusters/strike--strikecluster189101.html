strike

verb
stop work as protest
3.2 7.23/m
Pronunciation: / straÉªk / (AmE), / straÉªk / (BrE)
(of workers) to stop working as a form of protest, usually to demand better conditions or pay.

Examples

Workers strike for pay
The threat to strike forced the company to negotiate with the union.
The workers voted to strike if their safety demands were not met.
Thousands of teachers decided to strike for better salaries and resources.

Synonyms

  • go on strike
    3.2 0.98/m
    go on strike means workers stop working together to demand better conditions, while strike is the general term for that action.
  • down tools
    8.1 0.02/m
    down tools means workers stop working, often by leaving tools or machines, while strike is the formal protest to demand change.
  • walk out
    4.1 0.41/m
    walk out is when employees stop work and leave a place quickly to protest, while strike is a planned stoppage to press demands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boycott - to refuse to take part in something as protest
