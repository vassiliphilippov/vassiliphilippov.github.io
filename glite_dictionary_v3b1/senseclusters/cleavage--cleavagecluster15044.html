cleavage

noun
breaking of a chemical bond
Pronunciation: / ˈkliːvɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkliːvɪdʒ / (BrE)
the splitting of something in a scientific or technical context, such as in chemistry or biology

Examples

Chemical cleavage
Hydrolysis involves the cleavage of a water molecule into hydrogen and oxygen atoms.
The cleavage of DNA by restriction enzymes is a crucial step in genetic engineering.
The enzyme promotes cleavage of the peptide bond in the protein.
After fertilization, the zygote undergoes rapid cleavage to form a multicellular embryo.
Scientists observed the process of cleavage under the microscope to study cell division.
The pattern of cleavage varies among different species during embryonic development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: meiosis - cell division that produces gametes with half the number of chromosomes
  • synonym: mitosis - cell division resulting in two identical cells
  • antonym: synthesis - the process of combining elements to form a compound
