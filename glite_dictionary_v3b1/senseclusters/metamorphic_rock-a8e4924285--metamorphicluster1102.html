metamorphic rock

noun
rock changed by heat and pressure
2.6 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːrfɪk rɑk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfɪk rɒk / (BrE)
Rock that has been changed in form, structure, or composition by intense heat and pressure.

Examples

Metamorphic rock layers
Geologists discovered layers of metamorphic rock deep beneath the mountain range.
Gneiss is a type of metamorphic rock with distinct banding patterns.
When limestone undergoes heating and pressure, it can become the metamorphic rock known as marble.
