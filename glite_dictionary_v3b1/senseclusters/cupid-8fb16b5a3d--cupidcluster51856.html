Cupid

proper noun
Roman god of love in mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuː.pɪd / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.pɪd / (BrE)
the Roman god of love, often depicted as a winged child with a bow and arrows; the counterpart of the Greek god Eros

Examples

Cupid shoots arrows
In Roman mythology, Cupid causes people to fall in love by shooting them with his arrows.
Many artists have depicted Cupid as a mischievous child with a bow and arrow.
The figure of Cupid frequently appears in classical paintings and Valentine's Day cards.
