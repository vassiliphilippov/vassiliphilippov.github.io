fail

verb
to not meet an expectation or duty
1.4 1.0
Pronunciation: /feɪl/ (AmE), /feɪl/ (BrE)
to not do something that is expected, required, or promised; to not provide needed help or support

Examples

To fail a friend
He promised to return the money but failed to do so.
If you fail in your commitments, people may lose trust in you.
She was worried she would fail her teammates during the important match.
He failed to respond to the urgent email from his boss.
She failed to lock the door when she left the house.
The organization fails to provide adequate information to its members.
Her nerves failed her when she stepped onto the stage.
His memory fails him whenever he tries to recall names.
I will never forgive you for how you failed me in my time of need.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fulfill - to carry out or meet an obligation or duty
  • synonym: let down - to disappoint or fail to support someone
  • synonym: neglect - to fail to care for or do something
  • antonym: succeed - to accomplish what is intended or desired
