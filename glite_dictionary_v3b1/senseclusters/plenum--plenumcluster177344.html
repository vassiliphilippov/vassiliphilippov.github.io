plenum

noun
enclosed air space with higher pressure
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈplɛnəm / (AmE), / ˈpliːnəm / (BrE)
an enclosed space or chamber in which the air pressure is higher than that outside; commonly used in ventilation and building systems.

Examples

Plenum chamber
A false ceiling can create a plenum for heating and cooling systems.
Engineers inspected the plenum to ensure adequate air pressure was maintained.
The air in the plenum is filtered before being distributed through the building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vacuum - a space devoid of matter or where pressure is lower than surrounding
