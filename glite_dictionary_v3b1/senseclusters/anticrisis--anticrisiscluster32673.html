anticrisis

noun
measures against crisis
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
a set of measures, policies, or programs designed to prevent or lessen the impact of an economic or financial crisis

Examples

Anticrisis plan
The government announced a new anticrisis plan to stabilize the economy during the downturn.
International organizations often recommend anticrisis measures to countries facing financial instability.
The company implemented several anticrisis strategies to avoid bankruptcy during the recession.

Related Terms

  • antonym: austerity - policies aimed at reducing government deficits, often by cutting spending
  • synonym: stimulus - government action to encourage economic growth
