biocompatible

adjective
safe for living tissue
6.0
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.kəm.ˈpæt.ə.bəl/ /; / /baɪ.oʊ.kəm.ˈpæt.ɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.kəm.ˈpæt.ɪ.bəl/ /; / /baɪ.əʊ.kəm.ˈpæt.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
able to be used with living tissue or cells without causing harm or rejection (especially for medical materials or devices)

Examples

Biocompatible medical material
The new heart valve is made from a biocompatible polymer that reduces the risk of rejection.
Researchers are developing biocompatible materials for use in artificial joints and implants.
It is important that any device implanted in the body is biocompatible to avoid harmful reactions.
The surgeon chose a biocompatible material for the patient's artificial joint to reduce the risk of rejection.
Scientists are testing new biocompatible polymers for use in heart valve replacements.
A biocompatible coating helps medical implants integrate with the body's natural tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incompatible - not able to exist or work with something without causing problems
  • synonym: non-toxic - not harmful or poisonous to living things
