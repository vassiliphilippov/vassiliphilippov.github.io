Nationalist China

noun
Taiwanese government established by Chiang Kai-shek in 1949
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈnæʃ.ə.nəl.ɪst ˈtʃaɪ.nə/ (AmE), /ˈnæʃ.ə.nəl.ɪst ˈtʃaɪ.nə/ /; / /ˈnæʃ.ə.nəl.ɪst ˈtʃiː.ɑː/ (BrE)
the government established on the island of Taiwan in 1949 by Chiang Kai-shek after the Chinese Civil War, also known as the Republic of China

Examples

Nationalist China government
Chiang Kai-shek's leadership played a crucial role in shaping the policies of Nationalist China after 1949.
Many countries initially recognized Nationalist China as the legitimate government of all China.
The United Nations transferred China’s seat from Nationalist China to the People's Republic of China in 1971.
