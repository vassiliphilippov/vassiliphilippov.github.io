volatile

noun
substance that easily vaporizes
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːlətl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
A substance that easily changes from a solid or liquid state into a vapor, often at room temperature.

Examples

A volatile in chemistry
After heating the sample, they analyzed the remaining volatile residues.
Paint often contains volatile components that can evaporate into the air.
The laboratory technician measured the amount of volatile released during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solvent - a substance, usually liquid, that dissolves a solute forming a solution
