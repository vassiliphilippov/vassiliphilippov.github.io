raccoon

noun
nocturnal North American mammal
5.6
Pronunciation: / ræˈkun / (AmE), / rəˈkuːn / (BrE)
A small, mainly nocturnal mammal native to North America and Central America, known for its distinctive black facial markings and omnivorous diet.

Examples

Wild raccoon at night
A raccoon was rummaging through the trash cans behind our house last night.
The raccoon washed its food in the river before eating it.
Zoo visitors enjoyed watching the playful antics of the young raccoons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coati - a related mammal in the same family as the raccoon
  • synonym: opossum - another omnivorous nocturnal mammal native to the Americas
