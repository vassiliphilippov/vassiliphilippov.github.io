vicugna

noun
small Andean mammal valued for its wool
Pronunciation: / vɪˈkuːnjə / (AmE), / vɪˈkuːnjə / (BrE)
A small wild South American mammal of the camel family, native to the Andes, valued for its soft wool.

Examples

Wild vicugna animal
Conservation efforts have helped the population of wild vicugnas recover in parts of the Andes.
The vicugna is prized for the fine quality of its fleece, which is among the softest in the world.
Unlike llamas, the vicugna remains largely undomesticated and lives in high-altitude regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guanaco - a related larger South American mammal
