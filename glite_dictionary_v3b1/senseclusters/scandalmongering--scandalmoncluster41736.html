scandalmongering

adjective
reporting or spreading scandal
Pronunciation: / ˈskæn.dlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæn.dəlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
(of writing or media) involving or characterized by the search for and sensational presentation of scandal.

Examples

Scandalmongering news stories
His scandalmongering articles often stirred up unnecessary controversy among readers.
She grew tired of the scandalmongering coverage in celebrity magazines.
The newspaper was accused of scandalmongering rather than reporting the facts objectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sensationalist - presenting stories in a shocking or exaggerated way
  • synonym: yellow journalism - journalism that is based upon sensationalism and crude exaggeration
