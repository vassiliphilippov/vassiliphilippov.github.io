scrappage

noun
removal for recycling
Pronunciation: / ˈskræpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈskræpɪdʒ / (BrE)
the act or process of removing, dismantling, or destroying old vehicles or equipment, often so they can be recycled or replaced

Examples

Scrappage process
The government introduced a scrappage scheme to encourage people to trade in old cars for newer models.
Many factories schedule regular scrappage of outdated equipment to improve efficiency.
The scrappage of ships is a major industry in some coastal towns.
The government launched a scrappage program to reduce pollution from older cars.
Many people took advantage of the scrappage scheme to trade in their old vehicles for new ones.
The scrappage of outdated buses helped improve air quality in the city.

  • synonym: recycling - the process of converting waste into reusable material
