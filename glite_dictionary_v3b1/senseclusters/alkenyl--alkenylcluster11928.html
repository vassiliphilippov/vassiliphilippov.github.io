alkenyl

noun
alkene-derived group
Pronunciation: / ælˈkiːnɪl / (AmE), / ælˈkiːnɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group or radical derived from an alkene by removing one hydrogen atom

Examples

An alkenyl radical
The chemist added an alkenyl group to the molecule to change its properties.
Certain reactions require the presence of an alkenyl substituent for successful completion.
The structure of the compound includes a long alkenyl chain attached to the ring.
