endow

verb
give a permanent fund or gift
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdaʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaʊ / (BrE)
to give a large sum of money or property to a person, institution, or cause, usually to provide an ongoing income

Examples

To endow a university
Many museums are endowed by private foundations or philanthropists.
She hopes to endow a scholarship for students from her hometown.
The billionaire decided to endow the university with a new science building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disinherit - to deprive of an inheritance or endowment
  • synonym: donate - to give something, especially to a charity or institution
