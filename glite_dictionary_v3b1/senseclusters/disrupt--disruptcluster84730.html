disrupt

verb
interrupt or throw into disorder
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrʌpt / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to interrupt or cause disorder in the normal progress or activity of something

Examples

To disrupt a meeting
Sudden loud noises can easily disrupt my concentration.
The new law could disrupt the entire industry if implemented.
The strike will disrupt train services across the city.
Construction noise can disrupt your concentration during exams.
The power outage disrupted the entire neighborhood for hours.
Unscheduled meetings often disrupt our regular workflow.
Heavy rain could disrupt the outdoor festival this Saturday.
She didn't mean to disrupt the meeting, but her question changed the subject entirely.
The construction work will disrupt traffic downtown for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to go on or persist without interruption
  • synonym: disturb - to interrupt the normal arrangement or functioning of something
  • synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of something for a time
  • antonym: restore - to bring something back to its original or normal state
