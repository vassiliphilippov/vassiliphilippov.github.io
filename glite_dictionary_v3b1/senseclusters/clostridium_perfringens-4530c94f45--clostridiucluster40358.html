Clostridium perfringens

noun
anaerobic bacterium causing food poisoning and gas gangrene
Pronunciation: / kloʊˌstrɪdiəm pərˈfrɪndʒənz / (AmE), / klɒˌstrɪdɪəm pəˈfrɪndʒənz / (BrE)
an anaerobic Gram-positive bacterium that produces toxins and can cause food poisoning and gas gangrene in humans

Examples

Clostridium perfringens bacteria
Clostridium perfringens is a common cause of food poisoning in improperly stored cooked meats.
Doctors found Clostridium perfringens in the contaminated wound, which explained the rapid onset of gas gangrene.
Strict kitchen hygiene helps prevent outbreaks of illness due to Clostridium perfringens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Salmonella - bacterium causing food poisoning
