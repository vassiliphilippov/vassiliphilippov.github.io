bull

verb
push up stock prices by speculative buying
8.3
Pronunciation: /bʊl/ (AmE), /bʊl/ (BrE)
(finance) To attempt to raise the price of stocks or other securities by speculative buying.

Examples

To bull the market
Large investors tried to bull the market in hopes of quick profits.
She was accused of attempting to bull the price of the company's shares.
Whenever rumors start, some traders try to bull the stock to take advantage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bear - try to lower stock prices through speculative selling; opposite in financial jargon
