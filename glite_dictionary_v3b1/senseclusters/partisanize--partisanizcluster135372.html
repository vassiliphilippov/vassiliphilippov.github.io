partisanize

verb
to make partisan
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːr.tɪ.zən.aɪz/ (AmE), /ˈpɑː.tɪ.zən.aɪz/ (BrE)
to make a person, group, or thing strongly support one party, side, or cause, especially in a political context

Examples

To partisanize the media
The media coverage seemed designed to partisanize the debate rather than encourage open discussion.
Efforts to partisanize the committee failed when members insisted on remaining neutral.
Some leaders worry that social media can quickly partisanize public opinion on important issues.
Some commentators warned that new laws could partisanize the education system.
Efforts to partisanize the discussion made it difficult to reach a compromise.
The media should avoid attempts to partisanize public health information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neutralize - to make something neutral or unbiased
  • synonym: polarize - to cause people to divide into opposing groups
  • synonym: politicize - to make something political or influenced by politics
  • antonym: unify - to bring people together as one group
