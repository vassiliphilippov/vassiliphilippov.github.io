reflectivity

noun
measure of how much energy a surface reflects

the ability or quality of a surface or material to reflect light or energy, usually measured as a fraction or percentage

Examples

Mirror reflectivity
A mirror has a high reflectivity, returning almost all the light that strikes its surface.
Scientists measured the reflectivity of the new coating to assess its efficiency.
The reflectivity of snow can make it difficult to see without sunglasses.
Scientists measured the reflectivity of different materials to find the best heat shield.
Snow has a greater reflectivity than grass, which helps keep temperatures lower.
The shiny metal surface has high reflectivity, making it suitable for mirrors.

Synonyms

  • reflectance
    7.9 0.25/m
    reflectance is the proportion of incident light reflected from a surface, while reflectivity refers to the material's general ability to reflect energy.
  • coefficient of reflection
    8.2 0.00/m
    coefficient of reflection is the numeric fraction of incoming light used in calculations, while reflectivity names the property's general reflecting ability.
  • reflection factor
    7.4 0.00/m
    reflection factor is the fraction of incoming radiant energy given in measurements, while reflectivity describes the surface or material's general reflecting ability.
  • reflective power
    6.0 0.00/m
    reflective power is a phrase for how much light a surface sends back as a ratio, while reflectivity names the same general property.
  • albedo
    9.9 1.09/m
    albedo is the proportion of light a planet or moon reflects overall, while reflectivity is the general ability of any surface to reflect energy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorption - the process of taking in energy instead of reflecting it
  • synonym: albedo - proportion of the incident light or radiation that is reflected by a surface
  • synonym: reflection - the process of reflecting light, sound, or heat
