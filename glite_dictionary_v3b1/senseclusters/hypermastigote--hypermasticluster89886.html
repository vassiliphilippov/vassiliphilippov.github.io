hypermastigote

noun
flagellate gut symbiont of termites
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈmæstɪˌɡoʊt / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈmæstɪɡəʊt / (BrE)
a flagellate protozoan that lives as a symbiont in the intestines of certain insects, such as termites

Examples

Hypermastigote protozoan
Hypermastigote protozoa are essential for the digestion of cellulose in termites.
Scientists observed a significant increase in hypermastigote populations within the termite gut.
The loss of hypermastigote symbionts can severely impact termite survival.
