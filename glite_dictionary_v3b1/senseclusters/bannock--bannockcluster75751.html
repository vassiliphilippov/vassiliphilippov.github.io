bannock

noun
flat bread made of oat or barley flour
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbænək / (AmE), / ˈbænək / (BrE)
A flat, round bread made from oat or barley flour, traditionally baked on a griddle or stone, especially in Scotland and parts of North America.

Examples

A bannock bread
She served fresh bannock with butter and jam for breakfast.
The travelers shared a bannock beside the campfire.
Traditional Scottish bannock is often made with oatmeal and cooked on a griddle.
