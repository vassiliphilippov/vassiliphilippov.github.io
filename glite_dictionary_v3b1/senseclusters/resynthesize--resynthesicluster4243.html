resynthesize

verb
to form again
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsɪnθəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɪnθəsaɪz / (BrE)
to produce or form a substance again, especially by biochemical processes within a living organism

Examples

Muscles resynthesize glycogen
After exercise, muscles resynthesize glycogen to restore their energy reserves.
Certain cells can resynthesize proteins that were previously broken down.
The body must resynthesize essential compounds to maintain normal function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decompose - to break down into simpler parts or substances
  • synonym: synthesize - to produce a substance by combining simpler materials
