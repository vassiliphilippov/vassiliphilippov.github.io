unmetaphorical

adjective
literal, not figurative
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnmɛtəˈfɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnmɛtəˈfɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
not using or involving metaphor; literal in meaning or expression

Examples

An unmetaphorical style
Her writing style is very unmetaphorical, focusing on clear and direct statements.
The instructions were unmetaphorical, making them easy for everyone to understand.
He prefers an unmetaphorical approach when explaining complex topics to students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: figurative - using metaphor or non-literal language
  • synonym: literal - using words in their usual or most basic sense
