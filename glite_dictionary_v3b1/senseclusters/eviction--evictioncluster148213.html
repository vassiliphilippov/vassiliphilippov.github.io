eviction

noun
removal of data from a cache

(computing) the act of removing data (such as cache entries or memory pages) from a storage area to free space, typically according to a predefined policy (for example, least recently used).

Examples

Cache eviction
The cache uses an eviction policy to remove least recently used items.
After the memory filled, automatic eviction of old pages prevented the application from crashing.
Profiling showed frequent eviction of cache lines caused a large performance drop under load.

Synonyms

  • removal
    2.0 14/m
    removal is the general act of taking something away, while eviction in computing means automatically removing data to free space under a set policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cache replacement - the policy or algorithm used to decide which items to remove from a cache
  • synonym: purge - to remove data or items (often from a cache or storage) to free space or clear stale entries
