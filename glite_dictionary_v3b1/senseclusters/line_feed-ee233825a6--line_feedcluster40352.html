line feed

noun
move to the next line in text display or printing
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪn ˌfid / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn ˌfiːd / (BrE)
A control character or operation that moves the position in a text to the start of the next line for printing or display.

Examples

A line feed character
Pressing the Enter key typically generates a line feed in many computer systems.
The old printer required a manual line feed to advance the paper to the next line.
When programming, you may need to insert a line feed character to format the output correctly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carriage return - the control character that returns the cursor to the beginning of the line
