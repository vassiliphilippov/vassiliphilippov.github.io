microworld

noun
artificial model environment
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.kroʊ.wɜːrld/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.krəʊ.wɜːld/ (BrE)
a small, self-contained artificial environment or model used for simulation, teaching, or research, especially in computing or education

Examples

A computer microworld
In the computer lab, students programmed robots to interact within a microworld designed to teach basic logic.
Researchers use a microworld to simulate economic systems and test new theories safely.
The educational software provides a virtual microworld where children can experiment with scientific concepts.
