railroad flat

noun
apartment with rooms in a row
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌroʊd flæt / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌrəʊd flæt / (BrE)
An apartment in which all rooms are arranged in a line with doors connecting each room, often without a hallway.

Examples

A railroad flat apartment
Many immigrants lived in railroad flats during the early part of the twentieth century.
She grew up in a narrow railroad flat where you had to pass through one bedroom to reach the kitchen.
The old tenement building was filled with railroad flats that stretched from the front to the back of the floor.
