cervical cap

noun
contraceptive device fitted over the cervix
7.8 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜːrvɪkəl kæp / (AmE), / ˈsɜːvɪkəl kap / (BrE)
a small, thimble-shaped contraceptive device that fits over the cervix to prevent sperm from entering the uterus

Examples

A cervical cap device
Before inserting the cervical cap, she made sure it was properly fitted by her healthcare provider.
Her doctor recommended using a cervical cap as a method of birth control.
Some women prefer the cervical cap because it is hormone-free and reusable.
