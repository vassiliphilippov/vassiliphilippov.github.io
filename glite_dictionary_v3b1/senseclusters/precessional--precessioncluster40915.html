precessional

adjective
of axis movement
9.9
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / prɪˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the slow movement of the axis of a spinning body, such as a planet or a gyroscope

Examples

The precessional cycle
The precessional cycle of the Earth takes about 26,000 years to complete.
Scientists study precessional changes to understand long-term climate patterns.
The gyroscope's precessional motion helps stabilize the spacecraft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rotational - relating to the act of rotating or turning around an axis
