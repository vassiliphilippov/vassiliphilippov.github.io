subprime

adjective
high-risk lending
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈpraɪm / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈpraɪm / (BrE)
relating to loans or borrowers with a poor credit history and higher risk of default

Examples

Subprime loan
The bank stopped offering subprime loans after the financial crisis.
Many subprime borrowers faced difficulties making their mortgage payments.
The collapse of the subprime mortgage market affected the global economy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: high-risk - involving a greater chance of loss or failure
  • antonym: prime - relating to loans given to borrowers with good credit
