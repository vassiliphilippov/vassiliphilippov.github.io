wristlock

verb
to restrain by wrist
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪstˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈrɪstˌlɒk / (BrE)
to twist, hold, or manipulate someone's wrist in order to control or immobilize them, often as a restraint technique

Examples

To wristlock an opponent
The security guard had to wristlock the intruder to stop him from escaping.
During the training session, officers learned how to wristlock a suspect safely.
She quickly wristlocked her opponent in the self-defense class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immobilize - to prevent someone from moving
  • synonym: restrain - to hold back or control someone physically
