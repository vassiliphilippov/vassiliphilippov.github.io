keep

verb
continue a state or activity
Pronunciation: / kip / (AmE), / kiːp / (BrE)
to continue, remain, or maintain something in a particular state, condition, or activity; to retain possession or control

Examples

To keep going
He decided to keep running despite feeling tired.
If you keep studying regularly, you'll improve your grades.
Please keep smiling for the photo!
She managed to keep her job despite the company layoffs.
This fabric will keep its color even after many washes.
We were able to keep the old oak tree standing in the garden.
He managed to keep his balance on the slippery rocks.
She always tries to keep the conversation friendly, even during disagreements.
These exercises will keep your muscles strong and flexible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continue - to persist in an activity or state
  • antonym: lose - to cease having, retaining, or maintaining something
  • synonym: maintain - to continue to have; to keep in existence
  • synonym: retain - to continue to have or keep something; to not lose or give up
  • antonym: stop - to cease continuing an activity or state
