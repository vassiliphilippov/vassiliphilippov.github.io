torture

noun
deliberate infliction of pain for information or punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔr.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.tʃə / (BrE)
The deliberate and systematic infliction of severe physical or mental pain by one person on another, often to obtain information, a confession, or as punishment.

Examples

Prisoner torture
He described the psychological torture he endured while in captivity.
International law prohibits the use of torture under any circumstances.
The prisoners were subjected to torture in order to force a confession.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - a state of physical or mental ease and freedom from pain
  • synonym: torment - severe physical or mental suffering
