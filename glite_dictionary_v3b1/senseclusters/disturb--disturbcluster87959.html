disturb

verb

2.1 6.02/m
Pronunciation: / d…™Ààst…ùb / (AmE), / d…™Ààst…úÀêb / (BrE)
to physically move, interfere with, or damage something, often causing disorder or negative effects

Examples

To disturb the arrangement
Please do not disturb the papers on my desk; I have arranged them carefully.
She disturbed the neatly set table by moving the plates around.
The wind disturbed the sand, leaving patterns across the beach.
He warned the children not to disturb his model train set.
Please don't disturb these documents until the investigation is complete.
The gardener asked us not to disturb the flower beds while they were working.
Heavy equipment operations may disturb the archaeological site.
Movement could disturb the wound and cause further injury.
Please do not disturb the painting while the varnish is drying.

Synonyms

  • upset
    3.0 3.16/m

  • interfere
    3.5 21/m
    interfere means getting involved to stop or hinder an activity, while disturb often means physically moving or damaging things and causing disorder.
