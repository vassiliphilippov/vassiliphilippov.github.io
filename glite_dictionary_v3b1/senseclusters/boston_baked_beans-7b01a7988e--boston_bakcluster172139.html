Boston baked beans

noun
New England dish of sweet baked beans
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑstən beɪkt ˌbinz / (AmE), / ˈbɒstən beɪkt ˌbiːnz / (BrE)
A traditional New England dish made of dried navy beans baked slowly with molasses and salt pork.

Examples

Traditional Boston baked beans
Every Saturday, my grandmother would make Boston baked beans for the whole family.
The secret to delicious Boston baked beans is a long, slow bake with plenty of molasses.
We served Boston baked beans alongside hot dogs at the cookout.

Related Terms
