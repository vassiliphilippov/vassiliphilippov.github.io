disconnect

verb
to detach or remove a connection
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.kəˈnɛkt / (BrE)
to separate or detach something so that it is no longer joined or connected (especially by removing power, gas, water, etc.)

Examples

To disconnect the power
Before fixing the appliance, always disconnect it from the power supply.
Please disconnect the cables from the computer before moving it.
She disconnected the call by mistake when she pressed the wrong button.
Make sure to disconnect your laptop before moving it to another room.
Please disconnect the toaster after breakfast to save energy.
The technician had to disconnect the old washing machine before installing the new one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connect - to join or link together
  • synonym: detach - to remove or separate something from something else
  • synonym: unplug - to remove an electrical plug from its socket
