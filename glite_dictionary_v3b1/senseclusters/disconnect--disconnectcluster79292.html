Examples

To disconnect the power
Before fixing the appliance, always disconnect it from the power supply.

Synonyms

  • detach
    3.9 1.81/m
    detach means to remove something physically, while disconnect focuses on making things no longer joined or connected.
  • separate
    2.9 25/m
    separate means to move things apart or end a relationship, while disconnect often means cutting a real connection or supply.
  • uncouple
    5.3 0.25/m
    uncouple means to release two linked parts, often mechanically, while disconnect covers cutting any kind of connection or service.
  • decouple
    6.7 0.36/m
    decouple means to stop two systems affecting each other, often in technical settings, while disconnect is a general cutting of ties.
  • disengage
    5.3 3.09/m
    disengage means to release from a hold or role so someone can leave, while disconnect means break a physical or service link.
  • disjoin
    7.4 0.07/m
    disjoin means to separate things that are joined, usually in formal language, while disconnect is more common and used in everyday speech.
  • unhook
    2.5 1.55/m
    unhook means to remove something from a hook or fastener, while disconnect means making a broader separation or stopping a supply.
  • unplug
    2.6 1.04/m
    unplug means to pull a plug from a socket for electricity, while disconnect can mean cutting any connection, including non-electrical ones.
  • de-energize
    6.7 0.00/m
    de-energize means to remove electrical power or make equipment inactive, while disconnect is a more general breaking of a link or supply.
