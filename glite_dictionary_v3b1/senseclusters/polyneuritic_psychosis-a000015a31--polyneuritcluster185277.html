polyneuritic psychosis

noun
dementia from chronic alcoholism
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˌnʊˈrɪtɪk saɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌnjuːˈrɪtɪk saɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A form of dementia occurring in the final stages of severe chronic alcoholism, characterized by loss of recent memory while long-term memory remains intact.

Examples

Severe polyneuritic psychosis
Doctors noted that her polyneuritic psychosis was linked to chronic alcoholism.
Symptoms of polyneuritic psychosis include confusion and an inability to remember recent events.
The patient was diagnosed with polyneuritic psychosis after years of heavy drinking.
