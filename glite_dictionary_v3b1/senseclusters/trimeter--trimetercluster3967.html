trimeter

noun
three-foot verse line
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪmətər / (AmE), / ˈtrɪmɪtə / (BrE)
a line of poetry that has three metrical feet

Examples

A trimeter line
The poet used trimeter to create a lively and rhythmic effect in the stanza.
Many classic English ballads are written in alternating lines of trimeter and tetrameter.
Students learned to identify trimeter by counting the metrical feet in each line.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dimeter - a line of poetry with two metrical feet
  • degree higher: tetrameter - a line of poetry with four metrical feet
