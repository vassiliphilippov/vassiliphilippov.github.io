subquestion

noun
supporting question
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌkwɛstʃən / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌkwɛstʃən / (BrE)
a smaller or supporting question that helps address or clarify a main question

Examples

Several subquestions
The teacher divided the assignment into one main question and several subquestions to guide the students.
Each subquestion focused on a different aspect of the research topic.
Before answering the main issue, she addressed every subquestion in detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: follow-up question - a question asked after an initial one to get more information
  • antonym: main question - the primary or central question being addressed
