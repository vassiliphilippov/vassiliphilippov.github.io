angle of refraction

noun
angle of a refracted ray at a surface
4.0 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæŋ.gəl əv rɪ.ˈfræk.ʃən/ /; / /ˈeɪŋ.gəl əv rɪ.ˈfræk.ʃən/ /; / /ˈæŋ.gəl ə rɪ.ˈfræk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈæŋ.gəl əv rɪ.ˈfræk.ʃən/ /; / /ˈæŋ.gəl ə rɪ.ˈfræk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the angle between a refracted ray and the line perpendicular to the surface at the point where the light passes from one medium to another

Examples

The angle of refraction
Snell's law can be used to calculate the angle of refraction when light enters water from air.
The angle of refraction depends on the speed of light in the two media involved.
When light passes from glass to air, the angle of refraction is greater than the angle of incidence.
