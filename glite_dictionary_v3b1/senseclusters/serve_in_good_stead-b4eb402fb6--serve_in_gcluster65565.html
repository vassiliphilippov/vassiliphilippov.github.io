serve in good stead

verb
become useful or beneficial (often later)
Pronunciation: / sɝv ɪn ɡʊd stɛd / (AmE), / sɜːv ɪn ɡʊd stɛd / (BrE)
to prove useful or beneficial to someone, especially in the future

Examples

To serve in good stead for future
Her fluent Spanish will serve in good stead when she travels through South America.
The skills you learn today will serve you in good stead throughout your career.
Years of hard work and dedication served him in good stead during the crisis.
