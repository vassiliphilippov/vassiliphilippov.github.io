underbill

verb
to give less credit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈbɪl / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbɪl / (BrE)
to give someone less prominence in advertising or credits by listing their name lower or in a less noticeable position

Examples

To underbill an actor
The director decided to underbill the new actor, placing her name at the bottom of the credits.
Some musicians feel frustrated when event organizers underbill them despite their popularity.
He was surprised to see his name underbilled on the concert poster, below less experienced performers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
  • antonym: highlight - to give special attention or prominence to something
