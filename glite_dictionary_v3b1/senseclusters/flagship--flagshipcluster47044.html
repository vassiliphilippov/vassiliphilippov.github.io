flagship

noun
commander's ship in a fleet
Pronunciation: / ˈflæɡˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈflæɡʃɪp / (BrE)
the ship in a fleet that carries the commanding officer and flies their distinguishing flag

Examples

Navy flagship
HMS Victory served as Admiral Nelson's flagship at the Battle of Trafalgar.
Most of the important meetings were held on the fleet's flagship.
The admiral transferred to the flagship before the battle began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command ship - a ship from which a commander exercises control
