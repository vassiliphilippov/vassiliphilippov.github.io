Jacob Grimm

proper noun
German philologist and folklorist, elder Grimm brother
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪkəb grɪm / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪkəb grɪm / (BrE)
(1785–1863) The older of the two Grimm brothers, known for collecting German fairy tales and authoring Grimm's law describing consonant changes in Germanic languages.

Examples

Jacob Grimm brother
Jacob Grimm was instrumental in collecting and publishing folk tales that are still read today.
Many credit Jacob Grimm with shaping modern approaches to linguistics and folklore.
The study of Germanic languages often references Jacob Grimm's formulation of sound laws.
