Diderot

proper noun
French Enlightenment philosopher and Encyclopédie editor
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪd.əˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈdiː.də.rəʊ / (BrE)
(1713–1784) A French philosopher and writer, best known as a leading Enlightenment thinker and the principal editor of the Encyclopédie.

Examples

Diderot the philosopher
Diderot played a pivotal role in compiling the famous Encyclopédie during the Enlightenment.
Many philosophers were influenced by the works of Diderot and his contemporaries.
The legacy of Diderot continues to shape modern thought and intellectual history.
