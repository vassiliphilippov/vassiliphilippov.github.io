Ambrosiaceae

noun
plant family including ragweeds
9.9
Pronunciation: /æm.broʊ.zi.ˈeɪ.siː/ /; / /æm.broʊ.zɪ.ˈeɪ.siː/ (AmE), /æm.brəʊ.zɪ.ˈeɪ.siː/ /; / /æm.brəʊ.zi.ˈeɪ.siː/ (BrE)
a family of flowering plants, sometimes considered separate from the larger Compositae (Asteraceae) family, that includes ragweeds and related species

Examples

Ambrosiaceae plant family
Botanists sometimes separate Ambrosiaceae from the broader Compositae family for classification purposes.
Ragweeds are perhaps the most infamous members of the Ambrosiaceae.
The Ambrosiaceae family includes many species known to cause seasonal allergies.
Botanists sometimes classify ragweeds in the Ambrosiaceae rather than the broader Asteraceae family.
Recent studies debate whether Ambrosiaceae should remain a separate family or be merged with Compositae.
The Ambrosiaceae include several species of ragweed known to cause allergies.
