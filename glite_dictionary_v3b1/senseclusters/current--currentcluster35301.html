current

adjective
of the present time
1.0 231/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…úÀêr…ônt / (AmE), / Ààk år…ônt / (BrE)
happening or existing now; belonging to the present time.

Examples

Current news
Keep your address current to receive important mail.
Our current project will be completed by the end of this month.
The newspaper covers all the current events in the city.

Synonyms

  • present
    1.0 91/m
    present describes something existing or happening right now in a place, while current is a more general term for now.
  • contemporary
    4.2 44/m
    contemporary refers to things from the same time or modern life, while current simply means happening now.
  • present-day
    3.2 6.59/m
    present-day describes features or situations of today's era, while current focuses on what is happening at this exact time.
  • modern-day
    3.0 5.73/m
    modern-day is used to talk about things typical of our modern era, while current means happening at this moment.
  • up-to-the-minute
    4.4 0.59/m
    up-to-the-minute gives a sense of the very latest news or updates, while current means simply happening now.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contemporary - existing or happening in the same period; modern
  • antonym: past - belonging to an earlier time
  • degree lower: recent - having happened not long ago
  • degree higher: up-to-date - incorporating the latest developments
