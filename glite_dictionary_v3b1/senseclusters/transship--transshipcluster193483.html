transship

verb
transfer goods from one conveyance to another
5.3
Pronunciation: / trænˈʃɪp / (AmE), / trænˈʃɪp / (BrE)
to transfer goods or cargo from one ship, vehicle, or conveyance to another during transit.

Examples

To transship cargo
After arriving at the hub, the containers were transshipped onto a different vessel.
Goods are often transshipped through intermediate ports to optimize shipping routes.
The company will transship the cargo at the major port before sending it to its final destination.
The containers were transhipped at the main port before being sent inland.
After arriving by sea, the goods were transhipped onto trucks for delivery.
Many international shipments are transhipped at major transportation hubs.
