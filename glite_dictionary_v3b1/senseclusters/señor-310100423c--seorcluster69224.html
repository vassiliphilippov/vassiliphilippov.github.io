señor

noun
Spanish title for a man
Pronunciation: / seɪˈnjɔr / (AmE), / seˈnjɔː / (BrE)
a Spanish title or respectful form of address for a man, equivalent to 'Mr.' or 'sir' in English

Examples

The señor García
In Spain, it's common to address an adult man as 'señor' followed by his surname.
Please ask Señor Martínez to come to the office.
The waiter greeted us with a polite 'señor' as we entered the restaurant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: señora - Spanish title or form of address for a married woman
  • synonym: sir - a respectful form of address for a man in English
