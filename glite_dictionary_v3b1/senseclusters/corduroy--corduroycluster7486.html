corduroy

verb
construct a road from logs placed side by side
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrdəˌrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəˌrɔɪ / (BrE)
to build or reinforce a road by laying logs or similar materials crosswise to make it passable

Examples

To corduroy a path
Early settlers had to corduroy the road through the marshy forest.
Teams spent weeks corduroying new routes for supply convoys during the rainy season.
The construction crew corduroyed the trail to make it passable for wagons.
The workers corduroyed the muddy road so that vehicles could pass after the heavy rain.
To cross the swamp, they quickly corduroyed a path using nearby logs and branches.
In the early days, pioneers often corduroyed trails to make travel possible through forests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pave - to cover a road or path with a hard surface
  • antonym: smooth - to make a surface even and flat
