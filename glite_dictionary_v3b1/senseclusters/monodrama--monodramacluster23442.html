monodrama

noun
solo dramatic performance
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnəˌdrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌdrɑːmə / (BrE)
a dramatic or musical performance in which a single actor or singer performs alone, sometimes portraying multiple characters

Examples

A monodrama performance
The actress captivated the audience with her powerful monodrama about overcoming adversity.
He wrote a monodrama that explores the inner thoughts of a historical figure.
A monodrama can be an intense experience for both the performer and the audience.
The talented actress performed a powerful monodrama that explored themes of loss and hope.
He wrote a monodrama to express his personal journey through adversity.
A monodrama can be especially challenging because the actor must hold the audience's attention alone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dialogue - a conversation between two or more characters in a play or book
  • synonym: monologue - a long speech by one actor in a play or film
  • degree higher: opera - a dramatic work set to music for singers and orchestra
