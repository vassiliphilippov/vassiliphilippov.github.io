put the cart before the horse

verb
to do things in the wrong order
9.9
Pronunciation: / pʊt ðə kɑrt bɪˈfɔr ðə hɔrs / (AmE), / pʊt ðə kɑːt bɪˈfɔː ðə hɔːs / (BrE)
to do things in the wrong or illogical order; to reverse the natural order of things

Examples

To put the cart before the horse
If you try to decorate the house before buying furniture, you're putting the cart before the horse.
Let’s not put the cart before the horse by launching a product no one has tested yet.
You’re putting the cart before the horse by worrying about promotions before you even apply for the job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jump the gun - to do something too soon, before the appropriate time
  • synonym: out of order - not in correct sequence or arrangement
