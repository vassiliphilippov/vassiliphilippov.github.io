mimetism

noun
imitation for protection
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪmətɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪmətɪzəm / (BrE)
the phenomenon of one organism imitating another, especially as a form of protection from predators

Examples

Animal mimetism
Some insects use mimetism to resemble leaves and avoid being eaten.
The snake's mimetism makes it look like a more dangerous species.
Researchers study mimetism to understand how animals survive in the wild.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mimicry - the close external resemblance of an organism to another or to natural objects
