experimental variable

noun
variable manipulated or observed in an experiment
9.9 4.0
Pronunciation: /ɪk.spə.rɪ.ˈmen.təl ˈver.ɪ.ə.bəl/ /; / /ɪk.spə.rɪ.ˈmen.təl ˈvær.ɪ.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪk.spə.rɪ.ˈmen.təl ˈveə.rɪ.ə.bəl/ /; / /ek.spə.rɪ.ˈmen.təl ˈveə.rɪ.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
A variable in an experiment whose values are intentionally changed or observed to determine their effects on other variables.

Examples

The experimental variable changes
Researchers manipulated the experimental variable to observe changes in the participants' responses.
Temperature acted as the experimental variable, affecting the speed of the chemical reaction.
The experimental variable in the study was the amount of sunlight each plant received.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dependent variable - a variable whose value depends on that of another
  • synonym: independent variable - a variable whose variation does not depend on that of another
