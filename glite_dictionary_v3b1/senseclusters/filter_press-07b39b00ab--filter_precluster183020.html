filter press

noun
apparatus for separating solids from liquids
5.1 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪl.tər pres/ (AmE), /ˈfɪl.tə pres/ (BrE)
a machine used to separate solids from liquids in a mixture by passing the liquid through filter cloths stretched over frames, typically arranged in a series

Examples

The filter press machine
After each batch, the operator must open the filter press and remove the collected solids.
Testing revealed that the filter press could effectively separate fine particles from the slurry.
The company installed a new filter press to improve the efficiency of its waste treatment system.
