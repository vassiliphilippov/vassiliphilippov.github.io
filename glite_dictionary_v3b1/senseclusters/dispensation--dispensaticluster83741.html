dispensation

noun
official exemption from a rule or duty
9.2 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪspənˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪspɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
official permission to not follow a particular rule or obligation, especially granted by an authority.

Examples

Special dispensation
A dispensation was required to get married without the usual waiting period.
She received a special dispensation allowing her to work from home.
The church granted him a dispensation from fasting during his illness.
