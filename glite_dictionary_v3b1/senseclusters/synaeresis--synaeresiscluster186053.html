synaeresis

noun
contraction of two vowels into a diphthong
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈne.rə.sɪs/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈne.rə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the process by which two adjacent vowels within a word are pronounced together as a single syllable, often forming a diphthong

Synaeresis in speech
In linguistics, synaeresis often leads to the creation of diphthongs in everyday speech.
