Revolutionary calendar month

noun
month in the French Revolutionary calendar
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˌvɑːluˈʃɛːnəri ˈkælənˌdɚ mʌnθ / (AmE), / rɪˌvɒljʊˈʃɪənəri ˈkælɪndə mʌnθ / (BrE)
Any of the months in the calendar system adopted during the French Revolution in France (1793–1805).

Examples

The Revolutionary calendar month system
Each Revolutionary calendar month had exactly thirty days, making the year easy to subdivide.
Historians often refer to events by their Revolutionary calendar month to keep track of the tumultuous period.
The French abandoned the Revolutionary calendar month system after Napoleon came to power.
