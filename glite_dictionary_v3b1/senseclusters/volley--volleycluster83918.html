volley

verb
strike or send quickly in the air; fire rapidly
3.8 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈvɑː.liː/ /; / /ˈvɑː.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈvɒl.iː/ /; / /ˈvɒl.ɪ/ (BrE)
to hit, kick, or strike a ball before it touches the ground, especially in sports

Examples

To volley the ball
He skillfully volleyed the ball over the net to win the point.
Protesters volleyed questions at the politician during the press conference.
The player managed to volley the ball before it hit the ground.
In soccer, he expertly volleyed the ball into the top corner of the goal.
She moved quickly to volley the ball across the net and win the point.
The player kept volleying until her opponent made a mistake.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lob - to hit or throw a ball high in the air, especially in sports
  • synonym: serve - begin play by hitting the ball in tennis or similar sports
