catechize

verb
give religious instruction by questioning
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkætɪˌkaɪz /; / ˈkætəˌkaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkatɪˌkaɪz /; / ˈkatɪkʌɪz / (BrE)
to question or instruct someone about religious beliefs, especially using a set of questions and answers

Examples

To catechize children
At Sunday school, the priest would catechize the children in preparation for their first communion.
During the class, students were catechized about the importance of the sacraments.
The pastor took time to catechize new converts each week before baptism.
She was catechized about her beliefs by the visiting minister.
Teachers may catechize students to ensure their understanding of the material.
The priest would catechize the children every week before the service.
During Lent, the church elders catechise those preparing for baptism.
Parents are encouraged to catechise their children at home as part of their religious education.
The priest would catechise new members of the parish every Sunday afternoon.
