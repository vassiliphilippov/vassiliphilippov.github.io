misteach

verb
to teach incorrectly
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈtiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈtiːtʃ / (BrE)
to teach something incorrectly or provide wrong information when instructing someone

Examples

To misteach a lesson
The students were mistaught the basic principles of the subject.
If you misteach a concept, it can be difficult for learners to correct their understanding later.
He realized he had mistaught the formula and quickly clarified the correct method.

Related Terms

  • synonym: misinform - to give someone false or incorrect information
  • antonym: teach - to give instruction or knowledge to someone
