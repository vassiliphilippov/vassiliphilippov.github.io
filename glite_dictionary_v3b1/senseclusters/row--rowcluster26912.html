row

noun
angry dispute or argument
5.4
Pronunciation: / raʊ / (AmE), / raʊ / (BrE)
a serious argument or angry dispute, especially a noisy one

Examples

A loud row
A loud row erupted between the neighbors over the fence.
The meeting ended in a row when tempers flared.
They had a bitter row last night about money.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - a situation in which people have the same opinion
  • synonym: argument - an exchange of diverging views, typically heated
  • synonym: quarrel - an angry argument or disagreement
