amitosis

noun
direct cell division of nucleus without chromosomes
7.7 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæmɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌamɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a direct method of cell division in which the nucleus divides without formation of chromosomes

Examples

Amitosis cell division
Amoebae reproduce by amitosis, bypassing the complex steps seen in mitosis.
Some scientists believe amitosis occurs mainly in certain single-celled organisms.
Unlike mitosis, amitosis does not involve the formation of chromosomes during cell division.
