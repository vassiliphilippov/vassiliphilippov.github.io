shortchange

noun
insufficient change
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrtˌtʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtˌtʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
an instance of giving someone less money than they are owed, especially in change after a transaction

Examples

A shortchange at checkout
After counting her money, she realized there was a shortchange of five dollars.
The cashier apologized for the shortchange and quickly corrected the mistake.
Customers should always check their shortchange to avoid losing money.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overpayment - the act of paying more than what is due
  • synonym: underpayment - the act of paying less than what is due
