pursuable

adjective
able to be pursued
Pronunciation: / pərˈsuːəbəl / (AmE), / pəˈsjʊəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be chased, followed, or pursued, especially in a physical sense.

Examples

A pursuable path
The mountain trail was barely pursuable after the heavy snowfall.
The suspect remained pursuable thanks to the clear footprints left in the mud.
Not every opportunity is pursuable if you lack the necessary resources.

  • antonym: unreachable - impossible to reach or catch
