pearlite

noun
steel microstructure of ferrite and cementite
6.3 8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɜːl.aɪt / (BrE)
a microstructure consisting of alternating layers of ferrite and cementite, formed during the cooling of austenite in steel and cast iron

Examples

Steel pearlite layers
The presence of pearlite gives steel its characteristic strength and toughness.
Under the microscope, the metallurgist examined the layers of pearlite in the sample.
When cooled slowly, the alloy developed a distinct pearlite structure.
