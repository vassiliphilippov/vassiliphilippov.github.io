Horace

proper noun
Roman lyric poet (65–8 BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈhɒrəs / (BrE)
A Roman lyric poet (65–8 BC) known for his influential works, especially his Odes, which have shaped Western and English poetry.

Examples

Horace the poet
In his essay, the professor quoted Horace to illustrate the classical ideal of moderation.
Many scholars credit Horace with laying the foundation for much of European lyric poetry.
The works of Horace are studied by students of both Latin and English literature.
