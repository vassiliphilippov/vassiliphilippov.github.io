drastic

adjective
extremely severe or forceful
Pronunciation: / ˈdræstɪk / (AmE), / ˈdræstɪk / (BrE)
(of an action or measure) likely to have a strong or far-reaching effect; extremely severe or rigorous.

Examples

A drastic change
A drastic change in policy was needed to improve the economy.
She decided to make a drastic cut in her spending habits.
The government implemented drastic measures to contain the outbreak.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lenient - not strict or harsh
  • synonym: severe - very great or intense
