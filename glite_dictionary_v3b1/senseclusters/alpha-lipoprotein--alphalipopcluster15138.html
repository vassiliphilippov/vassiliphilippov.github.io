alpha-lipoprotein

noun
type of blood cholesterol-carrying protein
7.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àå√¶l.f…ô Àåla…™.po äÀàpro ä.tiÀên / (AmE), / Àå√¶l.f…ô Àål…™.p…ô äÀàpr…ô ä.tiÀên / (BrE)
A type of lipoprotein with a high proportion of protein and relatively little cholesterol, involved in transporting cholesterol in the blood, and associated with a reduced risk of coronary heart disease.

Examples

High alpha-lipoprotein level
A high level of alpha-lipoprotein in the blood is considered beneficial for cardiovascular health.
Doctors often refer to alpha-lipoprotein as 'good cholesterol' when discussing test results with patients.
Research suggests that exercise can increase alpha-lipoprotein concentrations in the bloodstream.

Synonyms

  • high-density lipoprotein
    4.1 0.12/m
    high-density lipoprotein is the general medical name for protein-rich cholesterol carriers in blood, while alpha-lipoprotein is a specific HDL type linked to lower heart risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: HDL - high-density lipoprotein, another name for alpha-lipoprotein
  • antonym: beta-lipoprotein - a different type of lipoprotein with lower protein and higher cholesterol content
