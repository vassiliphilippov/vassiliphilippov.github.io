Pinus radiata

noun
a California pine tree species
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪnəs rəˈdiːətə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs rəˈdiːətə / (BrE)
a tall pine tree native to California, having long needles in clusters of three, a dense crown, and dark brown deeply fissured bark, widely grown for timber

Examples

Pinus radiata tree
Pinus radiata is commonly planted in Australia and New Zealand for commercial forestry.
The bark of Pinus radiata becomes deeply fissured with age.
The timber industry relies heavily on Pinus radiata plantations for its fast growth.
