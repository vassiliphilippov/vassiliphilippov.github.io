hydrous

adjective
containing chemically combined water
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəs / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪdrəs / (BrE)
containing water, especially as water of crystallization; having water chemically combined within its structure

Examples

Hydrous crystals
Copper(II) sulfate is often purchased in hydrous form as blue crystals.
Many hydrous minerals lose their water content when heated.
The mineral gypsum is a hydrous calcium sulfate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anhydrous - not containing water
