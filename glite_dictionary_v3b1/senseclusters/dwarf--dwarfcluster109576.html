dwarf

verb
make seem small by comparison
9.9 4.0
Pronunciation: /dwɔːrf/ (AmE), /dwɔːf/ (BrE)
to make someone or something seem small or less important by comparison, or to limit growth

Examples

To dwarf a building
Her intelligence dwarfs that of her classmates.
The new skyscraper dwarfs all the surrounding buildings in the city center.
This gigantic oak tree dwarfs everything else in the park.
Her achievements in the field dwarf those of her contemporaries.
Lack of water can dwarf the growth of tomato plants in the garden.
The new skyscrapers dwarf the old church in the city center.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stunt - to prevent from growing or developing normally
