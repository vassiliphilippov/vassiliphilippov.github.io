subshell

noun
atomic shell division
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌʃɛl / (BrE)
a subdivision of an atom's electron shell, made up of orbitals of the same type that can hold a specific number of electrons

Examples

An electron subshell
Each subshell in an atom can hold a different maximum number of electrons.
The 2p subshell is completely filled in a neon atom.
Chemistry students learn how electrons fill each subshell according to specific rules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orbital - a region around an atom's nucleus where electrons are likely to be found
  • degree higher: shell - a main energy level in an atom containing one or more subshells
