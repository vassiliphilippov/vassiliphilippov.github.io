perpetuation

noun
the act of prolonging or continuing
9.9
Pronunciation: / pərˌpɛtʃuˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˌpɛtjuˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of causing something to continue or last for a long time, often indefinitely

Examples

The perpetuation of power
Many leaders strive for the perpetuation of their own power.
Strict policies led to the perpetuation of inequalities within the system.
The perpetuation of outdated traditions can hinder social progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cessation - the act of stopping something
  • synonym: prolongation - the act of making something last longer
