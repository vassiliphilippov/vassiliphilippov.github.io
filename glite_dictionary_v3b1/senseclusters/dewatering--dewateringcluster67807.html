dewatering

noun
removal of water
7.1
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈwɑː.tər.ɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈwɑː.tər.ɪŋ/ /; / /diː.ˈwɔː.tər.ɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈwɔː.tər.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /diː.ˈwɔː.tə.rɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈwɔː.tə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process or system of removing water from a place, material, or construction site, often using pumps or drains

Examples

Dewatering the site
The engineers began dewatering the site to prepare for the foundation work.
Effective dewatering is essential to prevent delays in underground construction projects.
After heavy rain, the team used pumps for dewatering the flooded basement.
The construction team set up dewatering equipment to keep the site dry during the project.
Effective dewatering is essential before laying the foundation in wet soil conditions.
They used a pump for dewatering the flooded basement after the heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drainage - the process of removing excess water from an area
  • antonym: flooding - the covering of an area with water
