panicked

adjective
very frightened or anxious
Pronunciation: / ˈpænɪkt / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkt / (BrE)
Experiencing a sudden, intense feeling of fear or anxiety, making it difficult to think or act calmly.

Examples

A panicked reaction
His panicked reaction made the situation even more chaotic.
She felt panicked when she realized she had missed her flight.
The panicked crowd rushed to the exits after the alarm sounded.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not affected by fear, worry, or excitement
  • synonym: fearful - feeling afraid, showing fear
