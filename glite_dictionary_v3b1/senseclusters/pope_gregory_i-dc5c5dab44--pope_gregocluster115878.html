Pope Gregory I

proper noun
Pope and saint (590–604) famed for church leadership
7.4
Pronunciation: / poʊp ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / pəʊp ˈɡrɛɡəri ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
(c. 540–604) The pope of the Roman Catholic Church from 590 to 604, noted for his leadership and influence on church doctrine and liturgy; also known as Saint Gregory the Great.

Examples

Pope Gregory I leadership
Pope Gregory I is known for initiating the Gregorian Chant, which transformed church music in the Middle Ages.
Many scholars regard Pope Gregory I as one of the most influential popes in Catholic history.
The reforms introduced by Pope Gregory I strengthened the authority of the papacy.
