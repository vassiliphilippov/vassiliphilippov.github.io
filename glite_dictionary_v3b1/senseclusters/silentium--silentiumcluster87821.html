silentium

noun
state of silence
Pronunciation: / saɪˈlɛnʃiəm / (AmE), / saɪˈlɛntiəm / (BrE)
a state of silence or quiet, especially in formal or literary contexts

Examples

Library silentium
The ancient library was filled with an air of silentium as scholars studied.
A deep silentium settled over the audience before the orchestra began to play.
He entered the chapel, appreciating the silentium that allowed for quiet reflection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise
  • synonym: silence - the absence of sound or noise
