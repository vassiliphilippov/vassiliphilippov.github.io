drowning

noun
death by submersion
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdraʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
death or serious harm caused by being unable to breathe under water

Examples

Risk of drowning
The number of drownings increases during the summer months when more people swim in lakes and rivers.
Proper supervision at pools can greatly reduce the risk of drowning among children.
Life jackets are essential for preventing accidental drowning while boating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rescue - saving someone from danger or harm
  • synonym: suffocation - death caused by lack of air or inability to breathe
