pentode

noun
electronic vacuum tube with five electrodes
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈpɛntəʊd / (BrE)
an electronic vacuum tube with five electrodes, typically used to amplify electrical signals

Examples

A pentode tube
Early radio transmitters often used pentodes to achieve higher gain than triodes or tetrodes.
The engineer replaced the faulty pentode in the amplifier to improve sound quality.
Understanding how a pentode works is important for students studying vintage electronics.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hexode - a vacuum tube with six electrodes
  • degree lower: tetrode - a vacuum tube with four electrodes
  • degree lower: triode - a vacuum tube with three electrodes
