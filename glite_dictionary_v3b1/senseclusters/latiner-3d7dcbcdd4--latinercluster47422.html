Latiner

noun
Latin speaker or writer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlætɪnər / (AmE), / ˈlætɪnə / (BrE)
an old term for a person who speaks or writes in Latin

Examples

A Latiner in history
In medieval times, a Latiner was often responsible for translating important documents.
The monastery employed a skilled Latiner to correspond with scholars across Europe.
Few people today would call themselves a Latiner, as Latin is rarely spoken.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Latinist - a person who studies or is skilled in Latin
