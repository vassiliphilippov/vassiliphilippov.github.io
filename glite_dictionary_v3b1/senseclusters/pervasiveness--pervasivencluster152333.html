pervasiveness

noun
quality of spreading throughout
Pronunciation: / pərˈveɪsɪvnəs / (AmE), / pəˈveɪsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being present or spread throughout every part of something

Examples

The pervasiveness of technology
The pervasiveness of technology in our daily lives makes disconnecting almost impossible.
The pervasiveness of the local dialect can be heard in every corner of the city.
There was a pervasiveness of mistrust among the members of the organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rarity - the quality of being rare or uncommon
  • synonym: ubiquity - the state of being everywhere or in many places at once
