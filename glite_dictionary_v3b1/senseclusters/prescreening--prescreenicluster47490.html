prescreening

noun
initial evaluation
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈskriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈskriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of checking or evaluating someone or something before a final decision or approval

Examples

Job prescreening
All candidates must go through a prescreening before the interview.
The hospital requires a prescreening to ensure patients meet the study criteria.
A quick prescreening can help identify the most qualified applicants early in the process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assessment - the act of judging or evaluating something
  • synonym: screening - the process of examining or testing people or things to decide if they are suitable
