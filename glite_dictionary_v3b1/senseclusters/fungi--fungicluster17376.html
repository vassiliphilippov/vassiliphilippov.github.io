fungi

noun
kingdom of yeasts, molds, mushrooms, etc.
3.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfʌn.dʒaɪ/ /; / /ˈfʌn.dʒiː/ (AmE), /ˈfʌn.dʒaɪ/ /; / /ˈfʌn.dʒiː/ /; / /ˈfʌŋ.ɡaɪ/ /; / /ˈfʌŋ.ɡiː/ (BrE)
A taxonomic kingdom of eukaryotic organisms that includes yeasts, molds, and mushrooms, typically distinct from plants and animals.

Examples

Forest fungi
Many fungi play a key role in decomposing organic matter in forests.
Penicillin is derived from a species of fungi known for its antibiotic properties.
Some fungi are edible, but others can be highly toxic to humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mold - type of fungus consisting of a mass of filaments
  • synonym: mushroom - the spore-bearing fruiting body of fungi
  • synonym: yeast - unicellular fungi used in fermentation
