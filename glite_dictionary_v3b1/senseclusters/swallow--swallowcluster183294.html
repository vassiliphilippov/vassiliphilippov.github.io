swallow

verb
to make food or drink go down the throat
1.0 14/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsw…ëÀêlo ä / (AmE), / Ààsw…íl…ô ä / (BrE)
to cause food, drink, or another substance to move from the mouth down the throat into the stomach

Examples

To swallow a pill
He took a deep breath and swallowed nervously before speaking.
It can be dangerous to swallow large pieces of food without chewing properly.
She tried to swallow the bitter medicine without making a face.

Synonyms
