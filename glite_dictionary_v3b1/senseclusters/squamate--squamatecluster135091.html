squamate

adjective
having scales
Pronunciation: / ˈskweɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈskweɪˌmeɪt / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the order Squamata, which includes lizards and snakes, or having scales.

Examples

Squamate skin
The gecko is a squamate reptile, easily identified by its scaly skin.
Scientists study squamate fossils to learn about the evolution of lizards and snakes.
Unlike amphibians, squamate animals have dry, scaly skin that helps prevent water loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scaled - covered with or having scales
  • antonym: smooth-skinned - having skin without scales
