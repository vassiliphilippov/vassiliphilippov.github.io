garrison

verb
station troops in a fort or post
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡær.ə.sən / (AmE), / ˈɡær.ɪ.sən / (BrE)
to station troops in a fort or other military post for its defense

Examples

To garrison a fort
During the occupation, the army garrisoned the captured towns to maintain order.
Plans are underway to garrison the military base with a new regiment next month.
The general decided to garrison additional troops at the border post to reinforce security.
