Valentine's Day

noun
February 14 holiday for expressing affection
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌvælənˈtaɪnz ˌdeɪ /; / ˈvælənˌtaɪnz deɪ / (AmE), / ˌvalənˈtʌɪnz ˌdeɪ /; / ˈvaləntaɪnz deɪ / (BrE)
a holiday celebrated on February 14th when people express love or affection for each other, often by giving cards, gifts, or flowers

Examples

Children often exchange cards with classmates to celebrate Valentine's Day.
He bought her a bouquet of roses for Valentine's Day.
On Valentine's Day, many couples enjoy a romantic dinner together.
Many restaurants offer special menus to celebrate Valentine's Day.
On Valentine's Day, couples often exchange cards and flowers to show their affection.
She received a box of chocolates from her friend on Valentine's Day.
