moraine

noun
glacially deposited debris
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈreɪn / (AmE), / məˈreɪn / (BrE)
An accumulation of earth, stones, and other debris carried and deposited by a glacier.

Examples

A moraine ridge
A thick moraine formed a natural ridge at the edge of the valley.
Scientists studied the composition of the moraine to learn about past glacial activity.
The hiking trail wound along the top of a moraine left by an ancient glacier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glacier - a large slow-moving mass of ice
