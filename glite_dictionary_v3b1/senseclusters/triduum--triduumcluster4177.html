triduum

noun
three-day observance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪd.u.əm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪd.juː.əm / (BrE)
a period of three days, especially for religious observance

Examples

The Easter triduum
The Easter triduum begins on Holy Thursday and ends on Easter Sunday.
Many Christians attend special services during the triduum each year.
The triduum is considered the most sacred time in the liturgical calendar.
The conference was organized as a triduum of lectures, workshops, and networking events.
They planned a triduum of celebrations to mark the anniversary.
A triduum of training sessions helped the new employees get started quickly.
