methanogen

noun
microorganism producing methane in anaerobic conditions
7.0
Pronunciation: / mɛˈθænədʒɛn / (AmE), / mɪˈθanədʒɛn / (BrE)
a microorganism, especially an archaebacterium, that produces methane as a metabolic byproduct in anaerobic conditions

Examples

Methanogen produces methane
Methanogens are crucial for decomposing organic matter in wetlands, where they generate substantial amounts of methane.
Scientists study methanogens to better understand the natural production of methane and its impact on the environment.
The digestive system of cows contains methanogen populations that contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaea - a domain of single-celled microorganisms
  • synonym: bacterium - a single-celled microscopic organism
