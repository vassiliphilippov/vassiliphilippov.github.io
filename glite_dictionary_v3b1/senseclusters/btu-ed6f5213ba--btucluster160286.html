BTU

noun
unit of heat energy
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbiː.tiː.juː/ (AmE), /ˈbiː.tiː.juː/ (BrE)
a unit of heat energy equal to the energy required to raise the temperature of one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit at one atmosphere pressure (equal to about 252 calories)

Examples

BTU per hour
Air conditioners often list their cooling capacity in BTU per hour.
The heater is rated at 5,000 BTUs, making it suitable for small rooms.
To boil this amount of water would require about 1,000 BTUs of energy.
