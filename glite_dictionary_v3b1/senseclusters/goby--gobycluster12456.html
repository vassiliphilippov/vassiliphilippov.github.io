goby

noun
small coastal or brackish water fish
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊ.bi / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.bi / (BrE)
a small, spiny-finned fish found mainly in coastal or brackish waters, with a large head, an elongated body, and ventral fins modified into a sucker

Examples

A small goby fish
Marine biologists discovered a new species of goby near the reef.
Some gobies form symbiotic relationships with burrowing shrimp.
The goby uses its modified fins to cling to rocks in fast-flowing streams.
