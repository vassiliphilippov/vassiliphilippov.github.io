incumbrance

noun
obstruction or burden
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌm.brəns / (BrE)
a burden, obstacle, or something that makes a situation more difficult

Examples

Heavy incumbrance
The fallen tree was an incumbrance that blocked the narrow road for hours.
They viewed the outdated regulations as an unnecessary incumbrance to business growth.
When hiking, it's best to travel without any incumbrance to make fast progress.
After selling the house, she felt free from the incumbrance of property maintenance.
He viewed his financial debt as a serious incumbrance on his plans for the future.
The new law removed many legal incumbrances to starting a small business.

  • antonym: relief - the alleviation or removal of a burden
