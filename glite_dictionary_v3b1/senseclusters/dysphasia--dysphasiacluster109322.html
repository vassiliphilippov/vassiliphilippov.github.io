dysphasia

noun
impairment of speech or language
4.9 8.4
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which a person has difficulty producing or understanding spoken language, often due to brain damage.

Examples

Severe dysphasia symptoms
Her dysphasia made everyday communication challenging, but family support improved her confidence.
Speech therapy is commonly used to help people recover from dysphasia.
The patient developed dysphasia after suffering a stroke that affected the left side of his brain.

  • degree higher: aphasia - complete loss or severe impairment of language ability
