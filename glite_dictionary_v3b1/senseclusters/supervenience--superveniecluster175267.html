supervenience

noun
dependence of properties
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈviːniəns / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈviːniəns / (BrE)
the philosophical idea that one set of properties depends on or is determined by another set of properties

Examples

Supervenience in philosophy
In philosophy of mind, supervenience describes how mental states depend on physical states.
The concept of supervenience is important in discussions about the relationship between ethics and facts.
Some argue that social properties have supervenience on individual behaviors.
The theory of mind-body supervenience suggests that mental states are determined by physical states.
Philosophers debate whether moral values exhibit supervenience on natural facts.
Her research explores the concept of supervenience in the relationship between consciousness and brain activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dependence - the state of relying on or being controlled by something else
  • synonym: emergence - the process by which complex systems arise from simpler interactions
  • antonym: independence - the state of not being influenced or controlled by something else
