tertulia

noun
discussion group
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɛrˈtuːliə / (AmE), / tɛˈtʊliə /; / tɛˈtʊːliə / (BrE)
an informal social gathering or regular meeting where people discuss literature, art, current events, or cultural topics, often held in a café or broadcast

Examples

A tertulia meeting
Every Friday evening, the local writers' tertulia gathers at the café to share ideas.
She was invited to join a tertulia focused on contemporary art and culture.
The tertulia became famous for its lively debates and creative atmosphere.
Every week, the radio hosts a lively tertulia about politics and society.
The university organized a tertulia to discuss recent developments in literature.
Listeners enjoy the cultural tertulia broadcast on Sunday evenings.
Every Thursday, the artists held a tertulia at the local café to share their latest work.
She enjoyed attending the weekly tertulia where friends discussed books and current events.
The writer was invited to a tertulia to talk about his new novel with readers.
