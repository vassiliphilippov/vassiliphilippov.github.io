mollusk

noun
soft-bodied invertebrate, often with a shell
4.5 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.ləsk/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.ləsk/ /; / /ˈmɒ.lʌsk/ (BrE)
Any of a large phylum of invertebrate animals with soft, unsegmented bodies, most of which are usually enclosed in a calcareous or hard shell, such as snails, clams, and octopuses.

Examples

A mollusk animal
A clam is a type of mollusk that lives buried in sand or mud.
Scientists discovered an unusual mollusk deep in the ocean last year.
The garden was full of mollusks such as snails and slugs after the rain.
A shell is often the most recognizable feature of a mollusc such as a snail or clam.
Octopuses, being a type of mollusc, are known for their intelligence and flexibility.
The scientist collected several specimens of mollusc from the tidal pool.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arthropod - Invertebrate with jointed limbs and segments, e.g., insects and spiders (contrasts with mollusk)
  • synonym: cephalopod - class of molluscs including octopuses and squids
  • synonym: gastropod - A class of mollusks including snails and slugs
