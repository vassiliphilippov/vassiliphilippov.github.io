flog a dead horse

verb
waste effort on a hopeless task
Pronunciation: / flɑg ə dɛd hɔrs / (AmE), / flɒɡ ə dɛd hɔːs / (BrE)
to waste effort on something that has no chance of succeeding or is already settled

Examples

To flog a dead horse
Arguing with him about that old decision is just flogging a dead horse.
Despite repeated efforts, she realized she was flogging a dead horse and finally gave up.
There's no point trying to fix what can't be fixed—stop flogging a dead horse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beat a dead horse - to continue pursuing a lost cause
