diehard

noun
person strongly resisting change
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪ.hɑrd /; / ˈdaɪˌhɑrd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.hɑːd /; / ˈdaɪˌhɑːd / (BrE)
a person who is extremely loyal to a belief, cause, or team and strongly resists change

Examples

A diehard fan
Even after the new policy was introduced, the diehard refused to alter his methods.
She was called a diehard for her persistent refusal to embrace new technology.
There are still a few diehards in the party who oppose modernization.
Even after the team lost ten games in a row, the diehard fans kept cheering.
She is a diehard supporter of the old traditions and refuses to accept new ideas.
Only a few diehards stayed until the end of the long meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convert - a person who has changed their beliefs or allegiance
  • synonym: fanatic - a person with extreme enthusiasm for a cause or activity
  • synonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a cause or leader
  • antonym: progressive - a person favoring reform and new ideas
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports tradition and resists change
