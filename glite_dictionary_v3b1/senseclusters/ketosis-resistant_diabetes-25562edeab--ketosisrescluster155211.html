A mild form of diabetes mellitus in adults that usually develops gradually and is not typically accompanied by ketosis; often managed by diet or oral hypoglycemic agents rather than insulin injections.

Examples

Ketosis-resistant diabetes in adults
In cases of ketosis-resistant diabetes, lifestyle modification is often the first line of therapy.
Some patients with ketosis-resistant diabetes can control their condition with dietary changes alone.
The physician explained that ketosis-resistant diabetes does not usually require insulin injections.
