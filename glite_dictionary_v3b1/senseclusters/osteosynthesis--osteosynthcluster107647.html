osteosynthesis

noun
bone repair surgery
Pronunciation: / ˌɑstioʊˈsɪnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊˈsɪnθəsɪs / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which broken bone fragments are joined using mechanical devices such as plates, screws, or rods

Examples

Osteosynthesis with plates
After the accident, the patient underwent osteosynthesis to stabilize the fractured bone.
Modern osteosynthesis techniques often use titanium plates and screws for better healing.
The doctor explained that osteosynthesis would help the bone heal more quickly and correctly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fracture reduction - the process of aligning broken bone ends
  • synonym: internal fixation - surgical stabilization of bones using implants
