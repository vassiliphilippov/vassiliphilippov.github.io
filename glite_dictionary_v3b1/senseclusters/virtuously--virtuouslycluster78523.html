virtuously

adverb
in a morally good manner
5.8 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ùÀê.t Éu.…ôs.li /; / Ààv…ùt Éu…ôsli / (AmE), / Ààv…úÀê.t Éu.…ôs.li /; / Ààv…úÀêt Éu…ôsli / (BrE)
in a way that shows high moral standards or virtue

Examples

To live virtuously
He lived virtuously, never straying from his ethical beliefs.
She always behaved virtuously and was admired by her peers.
The monk virtuously dedicated his life to helping others.
He donated much of his earnings virtuously to charity each year.
She always tries to act virtuously, even when no one is watching.
The knight fought virtuously for justice and the common good.

Synonyms

  • honorably
    4.2 1.15/m
    honorably is used for actions that keep a person's fairness and integrity in public life, while virtuously means showing high personal moral standards.
  • uprightly
    5.9 0.10/m
    uprightly describes honest, straightforward behavior in everyday life, while virtuously refers to acting with high moral excellence and goodness.
  • nobly
    5.3 0.58/m
    nobly shows grand or proud moral actions often with dignity, while virtuously focuses on quiet, steady high moral standards.
  • righteously
    6.5 0.68/m
    righteously describes acting in a way seen as morally right or just, while virtuously emphasizes practicing high personal virtue.
  • morally
    3.5 15/m
    morally talks about right and wrong in general life choices, while virtuously highlights acting with high personal moral excellence.
  • ethically
    4.4 3.12/m
    ethically often refers to rules or professional standards people follow, while virtuously describes inner personal moral goodness in behavior.
  • high-mindedly
    7.9 0.00/m
    high-mindedly sounds lofty and idealistic about morals, while virtuously means behaving with true, humble high moral standards.
