perpendicular

adjective
at right angles; intersecting at 90°
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: /pɜːr.pən.ˈdɪk.jə.lər/ /; / /pɜːr.pən.ˈdɪk.jʊ.lər/ (AmE), /pɜː.pən.ˈdɪk.jə.lə/ /; / /pɜː.pən.ˈdɪk.jʊ.lə/ (BrE)
at a right angle (90°) to a line, surface, or plane; exactly upright or vertical

Examples

Perpendicular line to base
Draw a line perpendicular to the base of the triangle from the top vertex.
The flagpole stands perpendicular to the ground.
The two walls are perpendicular to each other, creating a perfect corner.
The ladder must be placed perpendicular to the ground for safety.
The lines on the graph are perpendicular to each other.
When the flagpole is perpendicular to the street, it looks especially tall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: horizontal - parallel to the ground; at 0 degrees from the horizon
  • antonym: parallel - lines or planes that are always the same distance apart and never meet
  • synonym: vertical - upright; at a right angle to the horizon
