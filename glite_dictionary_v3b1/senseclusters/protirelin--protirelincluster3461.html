protirelin

noun
hypothalamic hormone controlling thyroid-stimulating hormone release
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈtaɪrɪlɪn / (AmE), / prəʊˈtaɪrɪlɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of thyroid-stimulating hormone from the anterior pituitary gland

Examples

Protirelin hormone
A deficiency in protirelin can lead to abnormal thyroid function and related symptoms.
Doctors sometimes use synthetic protirelin to test the pituitary gland's response in clinical studies.
Researchers measured the effect of protirelin injections on patients with suspected thyroid disorders.
