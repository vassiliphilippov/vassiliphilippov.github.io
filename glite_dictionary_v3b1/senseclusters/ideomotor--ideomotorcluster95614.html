ideomotor

noun
unconscious movement
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiəˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiəˈməʊtə / (BrE)
an involuntary physical movement that occurs unconsciously in response to an idea, thought, or suggestion

Examples

An ideomotor action
The psychologist observed a subtle ideomotor response when the subject was asked to imagine moving their hand.
Some experiments use ideomotor actions to study the connection between thoughts and physical movement.
The phenomenon of ideomotor movement is often demonstrated in activities like dowsing or using a Ouija board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflex - an automatic physical response to a stimulus
  • antonym: voluntary movement - a movement made consciously and intentionally
