swail

noun
shallow marshy channel
Pronunciation: / sweɪl / (AmE), / sweɪl / (BrE)
a low, marshy area or shallow channel that temporarily carries water, especially after rain.

Examples

A wet swail
After the heavy rain, water pooled in the swail behind the house.
Wildflowers grew along the edges of the swail in the open field.
Children splashed in the shallow water that filled the swail every spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ditch - a narrow channel dug in the ground for drainage
  • antonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, higher than the surrounding land
  • synonym: swale - a low, moist area or shallow channel that carries water
