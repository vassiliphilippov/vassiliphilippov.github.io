Saint Anselm

proper noun

Pronunciation: /seɪnt ˈæn.səlm/ /; / /sənt ˈæn.səlm/ /; / /seɪnt ˈæn.selm/ (AmE), /seɪnt ˈæn.selm/ /; / /sənt ˈæn.selm/ /; / /seɪnt ˈæn.səlm/ (BrE)
(c. 1033–1109), Italian Benedictine monk, philosopher, and theologian who served as Archbishop of Canterbury and is considered one of the founders of scholasticism, best known for his philosophical arguments for the existence of God.
