Saint Anselm

proper noun
medieval theologian and philosopher (c. 1033–1109)
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈæn.sɛlm / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈæn.sɛlm / (BrE)
(c. 1033–1109), Italian Benedictine monk, philosopher, and theologian who served as Archbishop of Canterbury and is considered one of the founders of scholasticism, best known for his philosophical arguments for the existence of God.

Examples

Saint Anselm philosopher
Saint Anselm is renowned for formulating the ontological argument for the existence of God.
Many philosophy students are required to study the writings of Saint Anselm in their courses.
The abbey where Saint Anselm lived played a central role in the intellectual life of the Middle Ages.
