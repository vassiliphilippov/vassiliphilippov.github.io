decoherence

noun
loss of phase coherence
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkoʊˈhɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəʊˈhɪərəns / (BrE)
the process by which a system, especially a quantum system, loses its coherence and behaves more classically due to interaction with its environment

Examples

Decoherence process
The experiment failed because decoherence disrupted the delicate quantum states.
Engineers work to minimize decoherence in fiber optic communication systems.
Sudden temperature changes can cause decoherence in sensitive acoustic measurements.
Quantum decoherence is a major challenge in building quantum computers.
Researchers are developing new methods to reduce decoherence in quantum systems.
The experiment demonstrated how environmental noise leads to decoherence in the particle's state.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coherence - the quality of waves or signals maintaining a fixed phase relationship
  • synonym: dephasing - loss of phase relationship between quantum states
  • synonym: interference - the effect produced by the combination of two or more waves
