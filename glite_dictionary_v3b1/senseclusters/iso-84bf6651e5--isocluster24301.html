ISO

noun
camera light sensitivity
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ ˌɛs ˈoʊ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ ˌɛs ˈəʊ / (BrE)
a measure of a camera sensor's sensitivity to light, with higher values allowing for better performance in low light

Examples

Camera ISO setting
If you increase the ISO, you can take clearer photos in dimly lit rooms.
Photographers often adjust the ISO setting to balance brightness and image quality.
A high ISO can introduce more noise into your pictures, so use it carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aperture - the opening in a camera lens that controls the amount of light entering
  • synonym: exposure - the amount of light allowed to reach a camera sensor
  • synonym: shutter speed - the length of time a camera's sensor is exposed to light
