venae centrales hepatis

noun
central veins in liver lobules
Pronunciation: / ˈviː.ni sɛnˈtrɑː.lɛs hɛˈpætɪs / (AmE), / ˈviː.ni sɛnˈtrɑː.lɛs hɛˈpætɪs / (BrE)
The terminal branches of the hepatic veins located within the hepatic lobules, receiving blood from liver sinusoids.

Examples

The venae centrales hepatis branches
Damage to the venae centrales hepatis can impair liver function and blood flow.
Microscopic examination revealed blockages in multiple venae centrales hepatis within the sample.
The blood collected in the liver sinusoids ultimately drains into the venae centrales hepatis.
