flee

verb
run away quickly, especially from danger
2.4 16/m
Pronunciation: / fliÀê / (AmE), / fliÀê / (BrE)
to run away from a place or situation of danger quickly

Examples

To flee danger
Hundreds of residents had to flee their homes when the wildfire approached.
Many refugees are fleeing violence and persecution in their home country.
When the alarm sounded, the thieves fled the building without being caught.

Synonyms

  • run away
    1.6 14/m
    run away means leaving suddenly and often secretly, while flee focuses on escaping danger quickly.
  • take to one's heels
    6.1 0.07/m
    take to one's heels means running away quickly in fear, while flee is a general verb for escaping danger.
  • bolt
    2.3 5.21/m
    bolt means to move away suddenly, often without warning, while flee stresses escaping danger quickly.
  • run for one's life
    3.9 0.74/m
    run for one's life means running with extreme urgency to avoid death, while flee covers quick escape from danger.
  • take flight
    3.5 0.84/m
    take flight means to leave quickly, sometimes literally flying, while flee focuses on running away from danger.
  • beat a retreat
    6.0 0.03/m
    beat a retreat means to withdraw quickly in an organized way, while flee describes a person running away from danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: escape - to break free from confinement or control
  • antonym: stay - to remain in a place, not leave
