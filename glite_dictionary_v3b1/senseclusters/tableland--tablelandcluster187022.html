tableland

noun
elevated flat or gently sloping land
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪbəlˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈteɪb(ə)lland / (BrE)
A broad, elevated area of flat or gently sloping land; a plateau.

Examples

A tableland area
Early settlers built their homes on the tableland to avoid flooding in the spring.
Much of the region consists of arid tableland dotted with sparse vegetation.
The ranch was located on a tableland overlooking the surrounding valleys.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plateau - an area of relatively level high ground
