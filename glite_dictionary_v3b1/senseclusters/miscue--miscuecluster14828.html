miscue

verb
to make a mistake
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈkjuː / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkjuː / (BrE)
to make a mistake or error, especially by failing to perform an intended action correctly

Examples

To miscue a serve
The tennis player miscued the serve, sending the ball far out of bounds.
He miscued his lines during the play, but quickly recovered.
She miscued the shot and the ball missed the basket entirely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: err - to make a mistake
  • antonym: succeed - to achieve the desired result
