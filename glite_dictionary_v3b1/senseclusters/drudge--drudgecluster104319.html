drudge

verb
work hard at dull tasks
7.0 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / drÊŒdÊ’ / (AmE), / drÊŒdÊ’ / (BrE)
to work hard, especially at dull or menial tasks for a long period of time

Examples

To drudge all day
For years, she drudged at a factory to help support her family.
He drudges through paperwork every afternoon without complaint.
They spent the summer drudging in the fields from sunrise to sunset.

Synonyms

  • grind
    3.7 3.45/m
    grind means working steadily on dull, repetitive tasks for a long time, while drudge implies doing long, low-paid menial work.
  • toil
    5.3 2.76/m
    toil means working extremely hard, often for a long time, while drudge stresses dull or menial daily tasks.
  • slog
    6.7 0.29/m
    slog is working slowly and hard over a long, tiring job, while drudge focuses on dull, menial work rather than just hard effort.
  • slug away at
    6.3 0.00/m
    slug away at means work persistently on a hard long task, while drudge suggests boring, low-status daily labour.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idle - to do nothing; to avoid work
  • synonym: labor - to work, especially hard physical work
  • synonym: toil - to work extremely hard or incessantly
