mesopelagic

adjective
middle ocean zone
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsoʊpəˈlædʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛsəʊpəˈlædʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to the ocean zone between about 200 and 1000 meters deep, where little sunlight penetrates

Examples

Mesopelagic zone
Many unique fish species live in the mesopelagic zone.
The mesopelagic layer is often called the twilight zone because of its dim light.
Scientists study mesopelagic organisms to understand how life adapts to low-light environments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bathypelagic - relating to the ocean zone below the mesopelagic, from about 1000 to 4000 meters deep
  • degree higher: epipelagic - relating to the uppermost ocean zone, from the surface to about 200 meters deep
