Bromus inermis

noun
species of drought-resistant perennial grass
Pronunciation: / ˌbroʊməs ɪˈnɜrmɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrəʊməs ɪˈnɜːmɪs / (BrE)
A species of drought-resistant perennial grass with creeping rhizomes and short or absent awns, native to Europe and temperate Asia, commonly known as smooth brome.

Examples

Bromus inermis grass
Bromus inermis is widely used for soil conservation and as forage for cattle.
Fields planted with Bromus inermis can withstand long periods of drought.
Researchers are studying the ecological impact of Bromus inermis in North American prairies.
