Diptera

noun
order of insects with one pair of wings
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪptərə / (AmE), / ˈdɪptərə / (BrE)
a large order of insects characterized by a single pair of wings and sucking or piercing mouthparts, including true flies, mosquitoes, gnats, and crane flies

Examples

The Diptera order
Mosquitoes, which belong to the Diptera order, can transmit various diseases to humans.
Scientists collected several species of Diptera for their entomology research project.
The distinctive single pair of wings is a key feature that sets Diptera apart from other insects.
