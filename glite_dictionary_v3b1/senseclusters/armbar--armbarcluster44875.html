armbar

noun
joint lock technique
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrmˌbɑːr / (AmE), / ˈɑːmˌbɑː / (BrE)
a grappling technique in which an opponent's arm is locked and hyperextended at the elbow joint

Examples

A tight armbar
The fighter secured an armbar in the final round to win the match.
She practiced escaping from an armbar during her jiu-jitsu class.
Applying an armbar requires precise technique to avoid injuring your opponent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chokehold - a grappling hold that restricts breathing or blood flow by applying pressure to the neck
  • synonym: joint lock - a grappling hold that manipulates a joint to control or injure an opponent
