Germanic language

noun
branch of Indo-European languages
9.8
Pronunciation: / dʒərˈmænɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒəˈmænɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A branch of the Indo-European language family that includes languages such as English, German, Dutch, and the Scandinavian languages.

Examples

Germanic language family
English is a Germanic language, sharing roots with German and Dutch.
Many common words in English have origins in an ancient Germanic language.
The classification of Icelandic as a Germanic language highlights its ties to Old Norse.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Indo-European - the large language family to which Germanic languages belong
  • degree lower: Scandinavian language - subgroup of North Germanic languages
  • degree lower: West Germanic language - subgroup within the Germanic family, including English and German
