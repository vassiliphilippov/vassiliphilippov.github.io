stiffish

adjective
somewhat formal or rigid
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈstɪfɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat stiff, formal, or lacking in warmth or flexibility in manner or style

Examples

Stiffish greeting
Her greeting was a bit stiffish, as if she was unsure how to act.
The conversation remained stiffish despite their efforts to be friendly.
He gave a stiffish nod and quickly left the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relaxed - friendly and informal in manner
  • synonym: stiff - formal or not friendly in manner
