auxiliary boiler

noun
secondary ship boiler used in port
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɔɡˈzɪljəri ˈbɔɪlər / (AmE), / ɔːɡˈzɪljəri ˈbɔɪlə / (BrE)
A secondary boiler on a ship, typically used to provide steam or hot water when the main boilers are not operating, especially while the ship is in port.

Examples

The auxiliary boiler on ship
During the stay in port, the crew depended on the auxiliary boiler for steam and heating.
Routine maintenance was performed on the auxiliary boiler before departure.
The auxiliary boiler was activated to supply hot water while the ship was docked.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main boiler - the primary steam generator on a ship
