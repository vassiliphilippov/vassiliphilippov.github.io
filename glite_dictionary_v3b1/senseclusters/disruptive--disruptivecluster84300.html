adjective

(of an innovation, technology, product, or business model) causing major change in a market or industry by creating a new market, value network, or way of doing things that displaces established competitors.

Examples

Disruptive technology
Investors hailed the platform as a disruptive technology poised to transform the entire industry.
The startup's disruptive business model attracted venture capital despite its unconventional pricing strategy.
Policy makers debated how disruptive innovations might improve services while unsettling established providers.

Synonyms

  • revolutionary
    5.2 8.25/m
    revolutionary describes a radical, dramatic change in ideas or products, while disruptive reshapes markets and displaces established competitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disruptive innovation - a term in business describing an innovation that creates a new market and eventually displaces established ones
  • antonym: incremental - making small, gradual changes rather than radically changing a market or product
  • synonym: innovative - introducing new ideas, methods, or products
