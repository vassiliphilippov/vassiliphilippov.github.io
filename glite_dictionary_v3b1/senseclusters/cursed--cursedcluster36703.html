cursed

adjective
deserving a curse; used for emphasis
5.3 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…úÀêrst / (AmE), / Ààk…úÀês…™d / (BrE)
Deserving or under a curse; sometimes used for emphasis to express annoyance or strong feeling.

Examples

Cursed with bad luck
He felt cursed with bad luck his entire life.
Not a cursed penny was left when they finished shopping.
The villagers believe the house is cursed and refuse to go near it.

Synonyms

  • accursed
    8.8 0.01/m
    accursed sounds formal and old-fashioned for someone under a real curse, while cursed appears in both literal and strong emotional use.
  • hexed
    8.8 0.01/m
    hexed suggests a spell or witch's curse causing bad luck, while cursed can mean a curse or an annoyed intensifier.
  • jinxed
    7.9 0.05/m
    jinxed signals repeated bad luck or a playful superstition, while cursed usually implies a real or serious curse.
  • damned
    5.6 15/m
    damned is a strong everyday intensifier people use for anger or emphasis, while cursed is less common and can mean under a curse.
