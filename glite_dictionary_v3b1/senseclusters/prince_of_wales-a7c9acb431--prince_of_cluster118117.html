Prince of Wales

noun
male heir to the British throne
Pronunciation: / ˌprɪns əv ˈweɪlz / (AmE), / ˌprɪns əv ˈweɪlz / (BrE)
the male heir apparent to the British throne, traditionally the eldest son of the reigning monarch

Examples

The Prince of Wales title
For centuries, the title Prince of Wales has been given to the monarch's eldest son.
Many people gathered in London to welcome the new Prince of Wales after his investiture.
The Prince of Wales attended the ceremony on behalf of the royal family.
