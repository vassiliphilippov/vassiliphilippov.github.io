graveside

adjective, adverb
at a grave
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈgreɪv.saɪd/ (AmE), /ˈgreɪv.saɪd/ (BrE)
at or relating to the side of a grave, especially during a funeral or ceremony

Examples

Graveside service
A small graveside ceremony was held for close friends and family.
The priest offered prayers during the graveside service on a cold morning.
Many people left flowers at the graveside gathering after the funeral.
Family and friends gathered graveside to pay their last respects.
The priest spoke softly graveside as the ceremony concluded.
She placed flowers graveside every year on the anniversary of his passing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cemetery - a place where dead people are buried
  • synonym: funeral - a ceremony honoring a deceased person
  • synonym: memorial - a ceremony to remember someone who has died
