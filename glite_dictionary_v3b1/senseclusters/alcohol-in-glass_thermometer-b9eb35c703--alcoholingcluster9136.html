alcohol-in-glass thermometer

noun
thermometer with alcohol in a glass tube
Pronunciation: / ˈælkəhɔl ɪn ˈɡlæs θərˈmɑmətər / (AmE), / ˈælkəhɒl ɪn ˈɡlɑːs θəˈmɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a thermometer consisting of a glass tube marked with temperature scales and containing alcohol, which expands and rises with heat and contracts and falls with cold

Examples

An alcohol-in-glass thermometer
The alcohol-in-glass thermometer showed a sharp drop in temperature overnight.
The nurse used an alcohol-in-glass thermometer to check the patient's temperature.
Unlike a mercury device, the alcohol-in-glass thermometer is less toxic and often used in schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mercury thermometer - thermometer using mercury instead of alcohol
