Paleozoic

noun
geological era, 541–252 million years ago
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪ.li.oʊˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpæli.əˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
the era of geological time lasting from about 541 to 252 million years ago, characterized by the development of early life forms

Examples

The Paleozoic era
Fossils from the Paleozoic provide evidence of the evolution of early plants and animals.
Many of the earliest marine animals appeared during the Paleozoic.
Mass extinction events marked the end of the Paleozoic era.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Mesozoic - the era following the Paleozoic, known for dinosaurs
  • degree lower: Precambrian - the era preceding the Paleozoic, before complex life
