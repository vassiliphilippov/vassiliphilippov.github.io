proconsul

noun
powerful colonial or governing official
8.6
Pronunciation: / proʊˈkɑːn.səl / (AmE), / prəʊˈkɒn.səl / (BrE)
an official, especially in a colony or occupied territory, who exercises significant administrative or governing authority, often on behalf of a foreign power or central government

Examples

Colonial proconsul
During the colonial period, a proconsul often made decisions without local input.
Many saw the American administrator as a modern-day proconsul, wielding authority over the occupied country.
The British appointed a proconsul to oversee the administration of the newly acquired territory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consul - an official appointed by a government to live in a foreign city and protect its interests
  • synonym: governor - official who governs a colony or territory
