unblinded

adjective
study with known treatment
6.8
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈblaɪn.dɪd/ /; / /ən.ˈblaɪn.dɪd/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈblaɪn.dɪd/ /; / /ən.ˈblaɪn.dɪd/ (BrE)
describing a study or trial in which participants or researchers know which treatment is being given

Examples

Unblinded clinical trial
In an unblinded trial, both the doctors and patients know which medication is being used.
The results from the unblinded phase were compared to those from the blinded phase.
Researchers decided to make the study unblinded to monitor side effects more closely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blinded - a study in which participants or researchers do not know which treatment is given
  • synonym: open-label - a clinical trial in which both researchers and participants know the treatment being given
