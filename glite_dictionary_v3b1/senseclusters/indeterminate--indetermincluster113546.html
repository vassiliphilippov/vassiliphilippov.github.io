indeterminate

adjective
uncertain or ambiguous in nature
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈtɝːmɪnət /; / ˌɪndɪˈtɜrmɪnət /; / ˌɪndəˈtɜrmənət / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈtɜːmɪnət / (BrE)
not exactly known, defined, or decided; vague or uncertain

Examples

Indeterminate answer
His answer was so indeterminate that no one knew what he really meant.
Surrealist painters often depicted indeterminate forms that defied easy interpretation.
The outcome of the trial remained indeterminate due to a lack of evidence.
The meeting ended with an indeterminate conclusion, leaving many questions unanswered.
The result of the investigation remained indeterminate despite months of research.
Their plans for the summer are still indeterminate at this point.
She gave an indeterminate answer when asked about her future plans.
The effect of the medication is of indeterminate duration.
The package arrived at an indeterminate time yesterday afternoon.
