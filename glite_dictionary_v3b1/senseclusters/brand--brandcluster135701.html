brand

verb
to label as disgraceful or shameful
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /brænd/ (AmE), /brænd/ (BrE)
to describe or label someone or something, especially publicly, in a way that is often negative or damaging to their reputation

Examples

To brand someone a liar
Activists often brand certain policies as unjust or inhumane.
She feared society would brand her as immoral because of her choices.
The media was quick to brand the politician a liar after the scandal emerged.
Public opinion quickly branded the protestors as radicals.
She feared being branded as dishonest because of one small mistake.
The committee unfairly branded him a troublemaker after the incident.
Activists warn against branding dissenters as enemies of the state.
She felt unfairly branded as untrustworthy by her colleagues.
The press quickly branded him a traitor after his comments were published.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denounce - to publicly declare as wrong or evil
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something
  • synonym: stigmatize - to brand or label as socially unacceptable or disgraceful
