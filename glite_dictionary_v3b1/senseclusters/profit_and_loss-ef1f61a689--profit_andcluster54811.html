profit and loss

noun
financial statement listing profit or loss
3.4 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêf…™t …ônd l…îs / (AmE), / Ààpr…íf…™t …ônd l…ís / (BrE)
a financial statement prepared at the end of an accounting period to show a company's revenues, expenses, and the resulting net profit or loss

Examples

The profit and loss statement
Analysts reviewed the profit and loss statement to assess the firm's financial health.
It's important to reconcile the profit and loss account before filing taxes.
The company's profit and loss for the year showed a significant increase in net income.

Synonyms

  • profit-and-loss statement
    4.1 0.09/m
    profit-and-loss statement names the exact period report accountants write, while profit and loss is the common label for that same report.
  • income statement
    3.6 0.45/m
    income statement is the common US business term showing revenues and costs for a period, while profit and loss names the same financial report.
  • profit and loss account
    4.7 0.00/m
    profit and loss account shows gross and net figures in traditional accounting records, while profit and loss is the general end-of-period statement name.
