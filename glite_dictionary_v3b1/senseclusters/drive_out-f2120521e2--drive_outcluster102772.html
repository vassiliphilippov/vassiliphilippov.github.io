drive out

verb
force to leave a place
Pronunciation: / draɪv aʊt / (AmE), / draɪv aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place, situation, or condition

Examples

To drive out residents
Rising rents threatened to drive out longtime residents of the neighborhood.
The new regulations aim to drive out illegal operators from the market.
The villagers managed to drive out the invading wildlife using loud noises.
A cup of tea can help drive out the chill on a cold morning.
She tried to drive out all negative thoughts before her big exam.
The government launched an operation to drive out the rebels from the region.
Strong winds can drive out the clouds and bring clear skies.
The company implemented new measures to drive out inefficiency from its operations.
The protesters hoped their actions would drive out the corrupt officials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - to allow someone or something to enter
  • synonym: evict - to force someone to leave a property, especially through legal process
  • synonym: expel - to force someone to leave a place, especially officially
  • antonym: welcome - to receive or accept someone in a friendly way
