odorant

noun
chemical with smell
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊdərənt / (AmE), / ˈəʊdərənt / (BrE)
a substance that produces or gives off a smell, often used in science, pest control, or to make things detectable by scent

Examples

A odorant substance
The laboratory tested a new odorant to see if it would attract more fruit flies.
Farmers use odorants in traps to control insect populations in their fields.
A strong-smelling odorant was added to the bait to lure pests into the trap.
The researchers introduced a new odorant to test the animal's reaction to unfamiliar smells.
Each odorant was carefully measured to ensure consistent results in the experiment.
Some people are more sensitive to a particular odorant than others.
The laboratory tested each odorant to determine how strongly it could be detected by volunteers.
Natural gas companies add an odorant so that leaks can be easily noticed by smell.
Some flowers produce a powerful odorant to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attractant - a substance that draws animals or insects by smell
  • antonym: deodorant - a substance used to remove or hide body odor
  • synonym: fragrance - a pleasant-smelling substance
  • antonym: odorless - having no smell
  • antonym: repellent - a substance that drives away insects or animals
  • synonym: scent - a distinctive smell, especially a pleasant one
