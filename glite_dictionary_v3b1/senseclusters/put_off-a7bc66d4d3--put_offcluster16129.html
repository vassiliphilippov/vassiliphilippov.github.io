put off

verb
discourage or disgust; cause dislike
Pronunciation: / pʊt ɔf / (AmE), / pʊt ɒf / (BrE)
to discourage someone, make them lose interest or confidence, or cause dislike or discomfort

Examples

Put off new customers
His arrogant attitude tends to put off potential clients.
I was put off by the dirty tables at the restaurant.
The strong smell of garlic can really put off some people from trying the dish.
Don't let their laughter put off your performance.
He was put off by the manager's rude behavior during the interview.
Her critical remarks put off several people in the audience.
Don't let one bad experience put you off trying again.
Her negative comments really put off a lot of new team members.
The smell in the kitchen put me off eating anything at the restaurant.
