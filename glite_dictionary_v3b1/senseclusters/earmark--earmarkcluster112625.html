earmark

noun
funds set aside
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɪr.mɑːrk/ /; / /ˈiːr.mɑːrk/ (AmE), /ˈɪə.mɑːk/ /; / /ˈiːə.mɑːk/ (BrE)
money or resources officially set aside for a specific purpose or project

Examples

A government earmark
The government created an earmark to support renewable energy projects in rural areas.
Each year, the budget includes an earmark for improving public transportation.
The committee debated whether the earmark for arts education should be increased.

Related Terms
