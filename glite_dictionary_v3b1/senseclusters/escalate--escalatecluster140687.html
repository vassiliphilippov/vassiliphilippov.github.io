escalate

verb
increase in extent or intensity
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛskəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛskəleɪt / (BrE)
to become or make something become greater, more serious, or more intense

Examples

To escalate a conflict
Tensions in the region could escalate if diplomatic efforts fail.
The conflict quickly escalated into a full-scale war.
We must address these issues before they escalate any further.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amplify - to make greater or stronger
  • antonym: deescalate - to decrease in extent or intensity
  • synonym: intensify - to become or make more intense
