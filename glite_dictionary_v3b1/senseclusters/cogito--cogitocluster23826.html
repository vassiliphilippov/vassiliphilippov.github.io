cogito

noun
self-aware thought
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.gɪ.toʊ/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.gɪ.təʊ/ (BrE)
the philosophical idea that one's existence is confirmed by the act of thinking; the self as a conscious thinker ('I think, therefore I am')

Examples

The cogito argument
The philosopher described a sudden cogito that made him question his own existence.
During meditation, she experienced a powerful cogito and became aware of her thoughts.
A moment of cogito can lead to deep personal insight and understanding.
The cogito is often cited as the foundation of modern Western philosophy.
Many students learn about Descartes' cogito when studying the nature of existence.
Philosophers have debated the implications of the cogito for centuries.
Descartes' famous cogito forms the foundation of his philosophy of mind.
The concept of the cogito has influenced many debates about consciousness and identity.
Some critics argue that the cogito does not prove the existence of a continuous self.
