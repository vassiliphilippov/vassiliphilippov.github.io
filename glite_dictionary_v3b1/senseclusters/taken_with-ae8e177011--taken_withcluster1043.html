taken with

adjective
strongly attracted or fond of
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkən wɪð / (AmE), / ˈteɪkən wɪð / (BrE)
experiencing a strong, often unreasonable attraction or fondness for someone or something

Examples

To be taken with an idea
He seemed rather taken with the new idea, talking about it all evening.
She was completely taken with his charming personality from the moment they met.
The children were immediately taken with the puppy and wanted to keep it.
