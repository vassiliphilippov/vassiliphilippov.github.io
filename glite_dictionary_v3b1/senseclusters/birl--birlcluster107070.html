birl

verb
rotate a log by treading on it
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɜrl / (AmE), / bɜːl / (BrE)
to spin or rotate something rapidly, especially by turning or twirling it (often used for logs in lumberjack contests)

Examples

To birl a log
Competitors take turns birling logs in the lumberjack contest each summer.
She birled the log so fast that her opponent lost balance and toppled off.
With impressive skill, he birled the log beneath his feet without falling into the water.
She likes to birl her pen between her fingers when she's thinking.
The children laughed as they birled the spinning top faster and faster.
With a flick of his wrist, he made the coin birl across the table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roll - to turn over or rotate on an axis
  • synonym: spin - to turn around rapidly
