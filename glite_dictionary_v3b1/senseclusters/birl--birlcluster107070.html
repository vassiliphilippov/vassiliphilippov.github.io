Examples

To birl a log
Competitors take turns birling logs in the lumberjack contest each summer.

Synonyms

  • spin
    1.0 12/m
    spin is a general verb for turning quickly or causing something to turn, while birl means spinning an object, often a log in contests.
  • whirl
    2.8 6/m
    whirl means moving or spinning rapidly in a circle, often on its own, while birl means spinning something, often a log, by turning it.
  • twirl
    2.0 4/m
    twirl is a light, repeated spin often done by hand or by dancers, while birl is a heavy, forceful spin done to objects like logs.
