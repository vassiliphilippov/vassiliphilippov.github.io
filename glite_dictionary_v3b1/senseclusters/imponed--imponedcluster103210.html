imponed

verb
to pledge as security
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpoʊnd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊnd / (BrE)
to pledge or deposit something, especially as a legal guarantee or security (archaic).

Examples

To imponed property
In medieval courts, property was often imponed as a guarantee for fulfilling obligations.
The merchant imponed a sum of gold to secure the contract under the old law.
Before modern banking, valuables were imponed to ensure trust in business deals.
