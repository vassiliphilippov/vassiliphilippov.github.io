orientalize

verb
make more Asian in character or style
5.5 8.6
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.i.ˈen.tə.laɪz/ /; / /ɔːr.i.ˈen.təl.aɪz/ (AmE), /ɔː.rɪ.ˈen.tə.laɪz/ /; / /ɒr.i.ˈen.tə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to make, depict, or describe something as having characteristics of Asian cultures, often in a stereotyped or exotic way

Examples

To orientalize a garden
She decided to orientalize her garden by planting cherry blossoms and adding a koi pond.
Some artists in the 19th century tried to orientalize their work to appeal to Western fascination with Asia.
The designer worked to orientalize the room by adding bamboo screens and lanterns.
The painter chose to orientalize her landscapes by adding motifs inspired by East Asian art.
Some critics argue that the film tends to orientalize its characters, relying on stereotypes.
The designer's latest collection orientalizes traditional Western garments with Asian patterns and fabrics.
The novel was criticized for orientalizing its characters and misrepresenting their traditions.
Some travel documentaries have been accused of orientalizing Asian cultures for Western audiences.
By orientalizing the artwork, the painter reinforced outdated stereotypes about the region.

  • synonym: stereotype - to oversimplify or generalize about a group or culture
  • antonym: westernize - to make something have characteristics associated with Western cultures
