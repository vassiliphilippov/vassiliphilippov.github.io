Spenserian

noun
imitator of Spenser
9.9
Pronunciation: / spɛnˈsɪəriən / (AmE), / spɛnˈsɪəriən / (BrE)
a poet or writer who imitates the style or language of Edmund Spenser, especially in poetry.

Examples

A modern Spenserian
The literary journal published an essay by a modern Spenserian exploring epic poetry.
Many critics consider him a true Spenserian because of his use of archaic language.
She was called a Spenserian for her elaborate verse and classical references.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Elizabethan - relating to the period of Queen Elizabeth I or its literature
