pulp

noun
soft mass of cellulose fibers (for paper, etc.)
5.3 3.9
Pronunciation: / pʌlp / (AmE), / pʌlp / (BrE)
a soft, wet, shapeless mass of material

Examples

Wood pulp
Many industrial processes rely on pulp as a starting material for production.
Recycled paper is often made from waste pulp collected from old newspapers.
The wood was reduced to pulp before being processed into paper.
After the storm, the ripe tomatoes had turned into a pulp on the ground.
She beat the fruit until it became a soft pulp for the recipe.
The documents were soaked in water and reduced to an unreadable pulp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiber - a threadlike structure forming plant or animal tissue
  • synonym: mash - A soft, pulpy mass made by crushing or mashing something
  • antonym: solid - A firm and stable mass, not soft or mushy
