Taisho

proper noun
Japanese emperor 1912–1926
9.9
Pronunciation: / taɪˈʃoʊ / (AmE), / taɪˈʃəʊ / (BrE)
the emperor of Japan who reigned from 1912 to 1926, known as Emperor Taisho

Examples

Taisho Emperor
During the Taisho era, Japan experienced significant political and cultural changes.
Many historians study the policies of Taisho to understand Japan's modernization.
The reign of Taisho marked a transition between the Meiji and Showa periods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Meiji - the emperor of Japan who reigned before Taisho, from 1867 to 1912
  • antonym: Showa - the emperor of Japan who reigned after Taisho, from 1926 to 1989
