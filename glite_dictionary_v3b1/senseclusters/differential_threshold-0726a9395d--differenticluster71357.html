differential threshold

noun
minimum detectable change in stimulus
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl ˈθrɛʃhoʊld / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl ˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
the smallest change in a stimulus that can be detected by the senses

Examples

The differential threshold in senses
A chef relies on a low differential threshold for taste to notice subtle changes in a recipe.
Researchers measured the differential threshold for sound intensity in a quiet environment.
The differential threshold for detecting light changes varies from person to person.
