pan out

verb
turn out successfully or as planned
Pronunciation: / pæn aʊt / (AmE), / pæn aʊt / (BrE)
to develop or turn out in a particular way, especially to be successful or have a good result

Examples

Plans pan out well
After months of planning, the event finally panned out perfectly.
They're waiting to see how the experiment will pan out before making a decision.
We don't know if the investment will pan out as we hope.
If the weather pans out, we’ll have the picnic in the park.
The new strategy didn’t pan out and had to be revised last quarter.
We made several investments, but only a few actually panned out as we hoped.

Related Terms

  • synonym: work out - to have a good result or solution
