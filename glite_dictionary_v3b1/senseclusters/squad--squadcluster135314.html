squad

noun
small military unit
2.4 21/m
Pronunciation: / skwÉ‘d / (AmE), / skwÉ’d / (BrE)
a small group of people organized for a particular purpose, such as in the military, police, or sports

Examples

A police squad
During the exercise, the squad practiced moving in formation and communicating silently.
Only one squad managed to reach the top of the hill before dawn.
The leader assigned each squad a specific section of the perimeter to guard.
A bomb disposal squad was called to the scene after the suspicious package was found.
The anti-fraud squad investigates financial crimes and scams.
The city formed a special squad to tackle drug-related crimes in the neighborhood.
Our squad practiced every evening to prepare for the tournament.
The coach selected his best squad for the final match.
The new player was excited to join the basketball squad.
