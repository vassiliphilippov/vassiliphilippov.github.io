glial cell

noun
supporting cell in the nervous system
6.9 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°la…™…ôl s…õl / (AmE), / Àà…°liÀê.…ôl s…õl / (BrE)
a type of cell in the nervous system that supports, protects, and maintains the function of neurons

Examples

Glial cells support neurons
Damage to glial cells can lead to various neurological disorders.
Recent studies show that glial cells are involved in learning and memory processes.
Scientists have discovered that glial cells play a key role in supporting neurons in the brain.

Synonyms

  • neuroglial cell
    7.7 0.00/m
    a neuroglial cell helps and protects nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord, while a glial cell is the general supporting cell.
  • neurogliacyte
    9.9 0.00/m
    a neurogliacyte is a specific supporting cell in nervous tissue, while a glial cell is the broader name for such support cells.
  • glia
    9.4 0.08/m
    glia are the group of support cells that make up nervous tissue, while a glial cell is a single cell that does the supporting.
