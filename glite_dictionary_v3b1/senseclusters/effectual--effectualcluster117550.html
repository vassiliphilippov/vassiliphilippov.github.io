effectual

adjective
having legal force or effect
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfɛktʃuəl / (AmE), / ɪˈfɛktʃuəl / (BrE)
having legal force; valid or effective, especially in producing an intended result or upholding a right

Examples

Effectual legal document
An effectual deed is required to transfer ownership of the property legally.
The contract was only effectual after both parties signed it.
The court ruled that the agreement was not effectual due to missing signatures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: valid - having a sound basis in logic or fact; legally acceptable
