dissenting opinion

noun
judicial opinion disagreeing with the majority
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛntɪŋ əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛntɪŋ əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
A written opinion by one or more judges expressing disagreement with the majority decision of a court.

Examples

Dissenting opinion of judge
Her dissenting opinion attracted much attention for its compelling arguments.
Supreme Court justices sometimes write a dissenting opinion to express their disagreement with the ruling.
The judge's dissenting opinion criticized the majority's interpretation of the law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concurring opinion - an opinion by one or more judges agreeing with the majority outcome but for different reasons
  • antonym: majority opinion - the written opinion reflecting the view agreed to by more than half of the judges
