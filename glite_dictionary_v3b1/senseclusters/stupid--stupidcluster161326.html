stupid

adjective
mentally numb or dazed (temporary)
1.6 2.48/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàstuÀê.p…™d, ÀàstjuÀê.p…™d / (AmE), / ÀàstjuÀê.p…™d / (BrE)
(of a person) temporarily unable to think clearly or react normally, especially as a result of shock, fatigue, or a blow; mentally numb.

Examples

Stupid with shock
After hitting his head, he sat on the ground, stupid and motionless for several minutes.
He was so stupid with exhaustion that he could hardly answer their questions.
The news left her stupid with shock, unable to process what had happened.

Synonyms

  • dazed
    4.8 4.17/m
    dazed feels slow and unfocused after a shock or blow, while stupid means temporarily unable to think clearly.
  • stunned
    3.0 1.38/m
    stunned leaves a person frozen by strong shock and unable to react quickly, while stupid means briefly mentally numb.
  • stupefied
    7.0 0.02/m
    stupefied makes someone seem almost motionless with surprise, while stupid means temporarily unable to think or react.
  • muddleheaded
    9.1 0.01/m
    muddleheaded describes someone often confused and slow to think, whereas stupid here means a short, shock-related mental numbness.
