lease

verb
let property for payment
Pronunciation: / lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
to allow someone to use your property or land in exchange for payment (as the owner)

Examples

To lease a house
She leased her apartment to a couple of university students.
The company leases office space in the city center to small businesses.
They decided to lease their house while they worked overseas for a year.
She leased her car instead of buying it outright.
The company leasing the office space must pay rent monthly.
They decided to lease their apartment while working overseas for a year.
