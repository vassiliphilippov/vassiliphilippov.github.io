lease

verb
let property for payment
3.5 4.57/m
Pronunciation: / lis / (AmE), / liÀês / (BrE)
to allow someone to use your property or land in exchange for payment (as the owner)

Examples

To lease a house
She leased her apartment to a couple of university students.
The company leases office space in the city center to small businesses.
They decided to lease their house while they worked overseas for a year.
She leased her car instead of buying it outright.
The company leasing the office space must pay rent monthly.
They decided to lease their apartment while working overseas for a year.

Synonyms

  • rent out
    2.1 1.60/m
    rent out means allowing someone to use your property for payment in everyday speech, while lease is the more formal legal agreement.
  • let
    6.7 42/m
    let allows someone to occupy property under a contract in informal or British use, while lease sounds more formal and legal.
  • rent
    1.8 15/m
    rent often means paying to use something or, less clearly, letting it out in casual speech, while lease is a formal owner agreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: let - to allow someone to use your property in return for payment
  • synonym: rent - to allow someone to use property or land in return for regular payments
