psychophysiological

adjective
mind-body interaction
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊˌfɪziəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˌfɪziəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the connection or interaction between psychological (mental) and physiological (physical) processes in the body

Examples

Psychophysiological responses measured
Researchers measured the psychophysiological responses of participants during the stressful task.
The therapy focused on both psychological and psychophysiological aspects of anxiety.
She wrote her thesis on the psychophysiological effects of meditation on the human body.
The study examined psychophysiological responses to stressful situations in college students.
Researchers measured psychophysiological changes such as heart rate and skin conductance during meditation.
Understanding psychophysiological mechanisms can help explain how emotions affect physical health.
Researchers used psychophysiological measures to examine how stress affects heart rate.
The study explored psychophysiological responses to emotional music in participants.
Advances in psychophysiological techniques have improved our understanding of brain-body interactions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: physiological - relating to the normal functions of living organisms
  • synonym: psychosomatic - relating to the influence of the mind on the body
