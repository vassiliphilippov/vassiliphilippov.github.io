cementation

noun
surface hardening process
8.9
Pronunciation: /sɪ.men.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.men.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sɪ.men.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.men.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a metallurgical process of hardening the surface of iron or steel by introducing carbon into it

Examples

Steel cementation process
The factory used cementation to harden the steel gears' surfaces before assembly.
During cementation, carbon is introduced to the metal to increase its hardness.
Engineers chose cementation to improve the durability of the machine parts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annealing - a process of heating and cooling metal to make it softer
  • synonym: carburizing - a process of adding carbon to the surface of steel
