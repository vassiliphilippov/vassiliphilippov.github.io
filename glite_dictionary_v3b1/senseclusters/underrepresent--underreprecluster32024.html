underrepresent

verb
to downplay importance
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to present, describe, or include something or someone as less important, significant, or numerous than it really is

Examples

To underrepresent a group
The report may underrepresent the true number of people affected by the policy change.
Minority groups are often underrepresented in leadership positions within large organizations.
If you underrepresent the risks, people might not take the necessary precautions.
The survey may underrepresent rural communities due to limited access to the internet.
Women are often underrepresented in leadership positions within the technology industry.
If certain groups are underrepresented in the data, the results may be misleading.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it is
  • degree higher: exclude - to leave out or not include someone or something
  • antonym: overrepresent - to present something as more important or numerous than it is
  • degree higher: represent - to present or include people or things as they actually are
  • synonym: understate - to describe something as less important or serious than it is
