Polish notation

noun
operator-before-operand math notation
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊ.lɪʃ noʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈpɒ.lɪʃ nəʊˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a method of writing mathematical expressions without parentheses, where each operator is placed before its operands

Examples

Polish notation method
In Polish notation, the expression '+ 3 4' represents '3 + 4'.
Learning Polish notation can help computer science students understand parsing algorithms.
Some calculators use Polish notation to simplify the input of complex expressions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reverse Polish notation - notation where operators follow their operands
