preconceived opinion

noun
opinion formed without enough evidence
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd əˈpɪnjən / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈsiːvd əˈpɪnjən / (BrE)
an opinion formed before having enough evidence or knowledge to judge fairly

Examples

A preconceived opinion
Her preconceived opinion made it difficult to accept new information.
Judges are expected to leave any preconceived opinion outside the courtroom.
We must not let a preconceived opinion cloud our judgment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - inclination or prejudice for or against one thing or person
  • antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas without prejudice
  • synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
