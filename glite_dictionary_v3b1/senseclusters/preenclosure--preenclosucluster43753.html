preenclosure

noun
before land enclosure
9.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ɪn.ˈkloʊ.ʒər/ (AmE), /priː.ɪn.ˈkləʊ.ʒə/ (BrE)
the period or condition of land before it was enclosed, especially referring to open or communal landscapes in history

Examples

Preenclosure period
The preenclosure landscape allowed villagers to graze their animals on common land.
Researchers compare preenclosure and post-enclosure maps to study changes in rural England.
Many traditional farming practices disappeared after the preenclosure era ended.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enclosure - the process or state of enclosing land, especially in British history
  • synonym: open field - a large area of land not divided by fences, often used communally
