Prunus cuneata

noun
a species of straggling American cherry tree
Pronunciation: / ˈpruːnəs kjuːˈniːətə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs kjuːˈniːətə / (BrE)
a small, straggling American cherry tree that grows on sandy soil and bears tiny, purplish-black, barely edible fruit

Examples

Prunus cuneata tree
Prunus cuneata thrives best in sandy soils where many other cherry species would not survive.
Few people eat the tiny fruits of Prunus cuneata because they are almost inedible.
The botanist identified the shrubby plant as Prunus cuneata during his field research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cherry - a small, round stone fruit or the tree producing it
