reapply

verb
to focus effort again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈplaɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to direct one's attention or effort to something again, especially after a pause or interruption.

Examples

To reapply effort
After a short break, she decided to reapply herself to her research project.
It can be difficult to reapply your focus after being distracted for a while.
He needed to reapply his efforts to complete the assignment before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to stop doing something completely
  • synonym: persist - to continue firmly in a course of action
  • synonym: resume - to begin again after stopping
