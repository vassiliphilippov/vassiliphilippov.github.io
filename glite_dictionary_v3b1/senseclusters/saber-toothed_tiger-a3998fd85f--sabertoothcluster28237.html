saber-toothed tiger

noun
extinct prehistoric cat with long canine teeth
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪbər tuːθt ˈtaɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈseɪbə tuːθt ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
an extinct large predatory cat known for its long, curved upper canine teeth, which lived from the Oligocene to the Pleistocene epochs

Examples

The saber-toothed tiger fossil
Although the saber-toothed tiger is extinct, it remains a symbol of prehistoric power and ferocity.
Many children are fascinated by the dramatic teeth of the saber-toothed tiger when learning about prehistoric animals.
The fossilized skull of a saber-toothed tiger was discovered in the La Brea Tar Pits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Smilodon - genus of saber-toothed cats, often called saber-toothed tiger
