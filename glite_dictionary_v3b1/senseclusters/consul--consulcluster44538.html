consul

noun
government official in a foreign country
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.səl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.səl / (BrE)
an official appointed by a government to live in a foreign city and protect and promote that government's citizens and interests there

Examples

The consul office
A new consul was appointed to represent the nation's interests in Tokyo.
She contacted the consul when she encountered legal trouble abroad.
The American consul assisted tourists who lost their passports in Paris.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambassador - the highest-ranking diplomatic representative sent by one country to another
