bleach

verb
make whiter or lighter; remove color
3.2 3.73/m
Pronunciation: / bliÀêt É / (AmE), / bliÀêt É / (BrE)
to make something whiter or lighter in color, especially by using chemicals or sunlight

Examples

To bleach clothes
She used the sun to bleach her white shirts naturally.
The hairdresser can bleach your hair to achieve a much lighter shade.
You should bleach the towels to remove those stubborn stains.
Over time, the salty sea air bleached the wooden benches on the porch.
She decided to bleach her stained white shirts before the party.
The sun gradually bleached the color from the old curtains.

Synonyms

  • bleach out
    3.8 0.06/m
    bleach out means making fabric fade by sun or chemicals, while bleach specifically refers to the chemical process to whiten items.
  • whiten
    2.8 0.84/m
    whiten means make something more white using any method, while bleach means using chemicals or sun to whiten more strongly.
  • decolorize
    7.0 0.02/m
    decolorize means remove color often in technical or lab settings, while bleach means using chemicals or sun to make things whiter.
  • lighten
    2.3 1.45/m
    lighten means make a color or room less dark by many methods, while bleach means use chemicals or sun to whiten fabrics or objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: darken - to make or become darker
  • synonym: whiten - to make or become white or whiter
