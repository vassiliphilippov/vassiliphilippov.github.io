bandgap

noun
forbidden energy range
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbænd.gæp/ (AmE), /ˈbænd.gæp/ (BrE)
a range of energies or frequencies that waves (like light or sound) cannot pass through in a material; in physics, especially the energy difference between filled and empty electronic states in a solid (important in semiconductors)

Examples

The bandgap energy
The bandgap of a material determines which wavelengths of light it can absorb or transmit.
Silicon has a bandgap that makes it useful for making solar cells and electronic devices.
By adjusting the structure, scientists can control the bandgap in photonic crystals to block certain frequencies.
The bandgap of a semiconductor determines how it responds to electrical currents.
Materials with a wide bandgap are often used as insulators in electronics.
Scientists are researching new materials with a smaller bandgap for use in solar cells.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: conduction band - the range of electron energies higher than the bandgap where electrons can move freely
  • antonym: conductor - a material with no bandgap, allowing free movement of electrons
  • synonym: energy gap - the difference in energy between two allowed states in a material
  • antonym: insulator - a material with a large bandgap, preventing electron flow
  • degree lower: valence band - the range of electron energies lower than the bandgap where electrons are bound
