nod

verb
move head to show agreement or understanding
Pronunciation: / nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
to move your head up and down slightly and briefly, especially to show agreement, understanding, or greeting

Examples

To nod in agreement
He kept nodding during the conversation to show he agreed.
She gave a quick nod to show she understood the instructions.
The manager nodded in approval as the team presented their ideas.
During the meeting, the manager nodded his approval for the new proposal.
He nodded in response to the question without saying a word.
She gave a quick nod to show she agreed with the plan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shake - move head from side to side to indicate disagreement or refusal
