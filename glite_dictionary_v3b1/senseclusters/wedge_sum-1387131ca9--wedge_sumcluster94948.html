wedge sum

noun
topological union at a point
Pronunciation: / wɛdʒ sʌm / (AmE), / wɛdʒ sʌm / (BrE)
(mathematics) A topological space formed by joining a family of spaces together at a single common point.

Examples

The wedge sum space
In algebraic topology, the wedge sum is frequently used to study spaces built from simpler components.
The wedge sum of two circles looks like a figure-eight.
When considering homotopy groups, the wedge sum can simplify many calculations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bouquet - a topological space formed as a wedge sum of circles or spheres
