fish glue

noun
adhesive made from fish remains
7.6
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ ɡlu / (AmE), / fɪʃ ɡluː / (BrE)
a gelatinous adhesive made by boiling the skins, fins, and bones of fish, used in woodworking and crafts

Examples

Fish glue adhesive
Because fish glue is water-soluble, it is easy to clean up after woodworking projects.
The repairer applied a thin layer of fish glue to restore the old violin's seams.
Traditional bookbinders sometimes use fish glue because it dries clear and remains flexible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animal glue - adhesive made from animal tissues
