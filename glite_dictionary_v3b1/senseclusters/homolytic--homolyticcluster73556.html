homolytic

adjective
bond splitting evenly
9.9
Pronunciation: /hoʊ.mə.ˈlɪt.ɪk/ /; / /hə.mə.ˈlɪt.ɪk/ (AmE), /hɒ.mə.ˈlɪt.ɪk/ /; / /hə.mə.ˈlɪt.ɪk/ (BrE)
describing a chemical bond that breaks so that each atom retains one of the shared electrons, forming two radicals

Examples

Homolytic bond cleavage
A homolytic bond cleavage produces two free radicals in a chemical reaction.
The scientist explained that homolytic fission is common in photochemical processes.
During combustion, certain bonds undergo homolytic breaking to form reactive species.
