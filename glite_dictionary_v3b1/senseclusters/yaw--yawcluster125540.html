yaw

noun
sideways deviation from course
Pronunciation: / jɑ / (AmE), / jɔː / (BrE)
an erratic or side-to-side movement away from the intended course, especially used in navigation and aviation

Examples

A sudden yaw
During the storm, the ship experienced a dangerous yaw to starboard.
The missile's sudden yaw caused it to miss the target.
The pilot corrected the plane's yaw after encountering strong crosswinds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly
