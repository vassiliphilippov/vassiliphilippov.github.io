monocultural

adjective
single crop or culture
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkʌltʃərəl / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkʌltʃərəl / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by the cultivation or dominance of a single crop, species, or culture in a given area.

Examples

Monocultural farming
A monocultural approach to farming can lead to soil depletion and increased pest problems.
The festival celebrated the region's monocultural heritage, focusing on a single traditional craft.
Critics argue that monocultural societies may lack diversity and resilience in the face of change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monoculture - the practice of growing a single crop or species in a given area
  • antonym: polycultural - relating to the cultivation or presence of multiple crops, species, or cultures
