Examples

An overgeneralization error
It is an overgeneralization to say that all teenagers dislike school.
The teacher explained that the student's answer was an overgeneralization and did not fit every situation.
Making an overgeneralization can lead to misunderstandings and incorrect conclusions.
His belief that all dogs are dangerous is an overgeneralization based on a single bad experience.
Teachers often warn students against making an overgeneralization from just one example.
The article was criticized for its overgeneralization about young people's attitudes.
A common overgeneralization in English is adding -ed to form the past tense of all verbs.
The child's use of 'mouses' instead of 'mice' is an example of overgeneralization.
Teachers often address overgeneralization errors when helping students learn irregular verbs.
