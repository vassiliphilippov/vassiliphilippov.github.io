Eysenck Personality Inventory

noun
questionnaire measuring extraversion and neuroticism
9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.zəŋk pɜːr.sə.ˈnæl.ə.ti ˈɪn.vən.tɔːr.i/ /; / /ˈaɪ.zəŋk pɜːr.sə.ˈnæl.ə.ti ɪn.vənˈtɔːr.i/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.zəŋk pə.ˈsɒn.əl.ə.ti ˈɪn.vən.tri/ (BrE)
a self-report questionnaire developed by Hans Eysenck to assess personality traits, primarily extraversion–introversion and neuroticism

Examples

Eysenck Personality Inventory test
Her results on the Eysenck Personality Inventory suggested she was more introverted than extroverted.
Participants were asked to complete the Eysenck Personality Inventory before the study began.
The Eysenck Personality Inventory has been widely used in psychological research since the 1960s.
