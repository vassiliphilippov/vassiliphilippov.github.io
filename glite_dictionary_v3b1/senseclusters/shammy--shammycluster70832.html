shammy

noun
soft absorbent cleaning leather
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæmi / (AmE), / ˈʃæmi / (BrE)
a soft, absorbent type of leather originally made from the skin of the chamois antelope, but now typically from sheepskin, used especially for cleaning and polishing

Examples

A shammy cloth
He used a shammy to dry off his car after washing it.
Keep a shammy in the glove box for cleaning windows on the go.
The old shammy was perfect for polishing his shoes to a high shine.
