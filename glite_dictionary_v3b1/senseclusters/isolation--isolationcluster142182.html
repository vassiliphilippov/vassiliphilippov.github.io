isolation

noun
a country's withdrawal from international politics
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the policy or practice of a country withdrawing from international political or economic relations

Examples

Political isolation
After years of isolation, the country finally opened its borders to foreign diplomats.
His speech criticized the government's policy of isolation in foreign affairs.
Many historians debate the impact of American isolation during the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: engagement - active involvement in international politics
  • synonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting or helping either side in a conflict
  • synonym: nonintervention - the policy of not becoming involved in the affairs of others, especially international conflicts
