crab

noun
rowing error when oar stroke goes wrong
5.2
Pronunciation: / kræb / (AmE), / kræb / (BrE)
(in rowing) a stroke of the oar that either misses the water or digs too deeply, often causing a loss of balance or speed.

Examples

Rowing crab
A sudden crab can throw a rower off balance completely.
During the final sprint, he caught a crab and the boat slowed down.
Her coach explained how to avoid catching a crab during the race.
