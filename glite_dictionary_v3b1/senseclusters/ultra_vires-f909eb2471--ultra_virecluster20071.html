ultra vires

adjective, adverb
beyond one's legal power or authority
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrə ˈvaɪriːz / (AmE), / ˌʌltrə ˈvaɪəriːz / (BrE)
beyond the legal power or authority of a person, official, or body (used as an adjective or adverb, especially in law)

Examples

Ultra vires act
A government agency risks acting ultra vires if it exceeds its statutory authority.
Any decision made ultra vires will not be legally binding.
The contract was declared void because the company had acted ultra vires.
A company cannot act ultra vires its charter and bylaws.
Any contract signed by the secretary without approval is considered ultra vires.
The board's decision was declared ultra vires by the court.

Related Terms
