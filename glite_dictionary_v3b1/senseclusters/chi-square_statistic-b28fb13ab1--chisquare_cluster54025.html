chi-square statistic

noun
statistical measure for comparing observed and expected data
4.6
Pronunciation: / kaɪ skwɛr stəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / kaɪ skweə stəˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
a statistical measure used to compare observed data with data expected according to a specific hypothesis, commonly used in tests of goodness of fit or independence

Examples

The chi-square statistic test
Researchers use the chi-square statistic to determine if there is a significant association between two categorical variables.
The chi-square statistic rises as the difference between observed and expected values increases.
To perform the test, first calculate the chi-square statistic for your dataset.
