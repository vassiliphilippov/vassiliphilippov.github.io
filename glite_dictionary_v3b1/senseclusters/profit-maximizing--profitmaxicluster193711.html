profit-maximizing

adjective
intended to maximize profit
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːfɪt ˌmæksɪˌmaɪzɪŋ /; / ˈprɑːfɪt ˈmæksɪˌmaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɒfɪt ˌmæksɪˌmaɪzɪŋ /; / ˈprɒfɪt ˈmæksɪˌmaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, action, strategy, enterprise, or amount) seeking or intended to make profits as great as possible.

Examples

Profit-maximizing strategy
Economists often debate the profit-maximizing output level for a monopolist.
Setting the profit-maximizing price requires a detailed understanding of supply and demand.
The company adopted a profit-maximizing strategy to boost shareholder returns.
A profit-maximising firm will always adjust production to achieve the highest possible return.
Setting a profit-maximising price can sometimes reduce customer satisfaction in the long run.
The company's new strategy is strictly profit-maximising and focuses on increasing shareholder value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loss-making - operating at a financial loss
  • antonym: loss-minimizing - aimed at making losses as small as possible
  • synonym: profit-oriented - focused on making a profit
  • synonym: profit-seeking - actively trying to gain monetary profit
