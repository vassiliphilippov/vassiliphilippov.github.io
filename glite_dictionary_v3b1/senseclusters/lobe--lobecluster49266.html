lobe

noun
enhanced response region in an antenna's radiation pattern
7.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /loʊb/ (AmE), /ləʊb/ (BrE)
A region in the radiation pattern of an antenna where the signal strength is higher, often represented as a bulge or loop indicating enhanced response in a specific direction.

Examples

Antenna lobe
Engineers analyzed the lobe structure to optimize the antenna's coverage.
Side lobes in the radiation pattern can cause interference with adjacent channels.
The main lobe of the antenna directs most of the energy toward the intended target.
