paper gold

noun
tradable non‑physical gold (ETFs, certificates)
7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpeɪ.pər goʊld/ (AmE), /ˈpeɪ.pə gəʊld/ (BrE)
Tradable financial instruments or electronic claims that give the holder exposure to gold (price or ownership) without receiving physical gold; includes ETFs, certificates, futures positions, and similar products.

Examples

Paper gold ETF investment
Many investors prefer paper gold ETFs because they offer easy trading without storing physical bars.
After reading market reports, she sold her paper gold certificates to reduce counterparty risk.
Traders warned that excessive reliance on paper gold could disconnect prices from the physical bullion market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gold ETF - an exchange-traded fund that holds gold or gold futures to give investors exposure to gold prices without physical possession
  • synonym: gold certificate - a document or electronic record representing ownership of gold held by a custodian, without physical delivery
  • antonym: physical gold - actual gold bars or coins that are physically held and stored; contrasts with non‑physical instruments
