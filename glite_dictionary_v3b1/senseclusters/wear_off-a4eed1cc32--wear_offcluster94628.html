wear off

verb
gradually diminish or disappear
3.5 3.3
Pronunciation: / wɛr ɔf / (AmE), / wɛə ɒf / (BrE)
(of an effect or feeling) to gradually disappear or become less strong over time.

Examples

Pain wears off
Don't worry, the numbness from the dentist will wear off soon.
His enthusiasm for the new job started to wear off as the weeks went by.
The effects of the painkiller began to wear off after a few hours.
