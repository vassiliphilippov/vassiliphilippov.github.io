trefoil

noun
architectural ornament of three arcs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtriːˌfɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈtriːfɔɪl / (BrE)
an architectural ornament consisting of three rounded lobes or arcs arranged in a circle, commonly used in Gothic architecture

Examples

Gothic trefoil design
A carved trefoil can be seen at the top of each column in the cloister.
Medieval architects often used the trefoil as a symbol of the Trinity in their designs.
The church windows were decorated with a trefoil pattern above the arches.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: quatrefoil - an ornamental design of four lobes
