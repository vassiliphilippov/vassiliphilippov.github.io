contagion

noun
spread of financial crisis
7.8
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈteɪ.dʒən/ (AmE), /kən.ˈteɪ.dʒən/ (BrE)
(in finance and economics) the spread of market disturbances, financial distress, or crises from one institution, market, or country to others.

Examples

Financial contagion
During the 2008 crisis, economists warned that sovereign defaults could cause global contagion among banks.
Investors fled emerging markets after the fund collapse, fearing financial contagion would hit neighboring economies.
Regulators introduced swap lines to prevent liquidity shocks from turning into full-blown contagion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resilience - the ability of a financial system, institution, or economy to withstand or recover from shocks
  • synonym: spillover - the transmission of effects (economic, financial, or other) from one market, sector, or country to another
