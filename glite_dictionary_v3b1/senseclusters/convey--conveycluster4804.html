to formally transfer ownership, rights, or something (such as property or an infection) to another person

Examples

To convey property
Her will stated that she wished to convey the land to her children.
The deed was signed last week to officially convey ownership.
The seller agreed to convey the property to the new owner upon completion of the payment.

The teacher warned that a careless handshake could convey germs.

Synonyms

  • transfer
    3.4 16/m
    transfer is an official change of ownership between parties, while convey sounds more formal and can mean passing rights or infections.
  • assign
    6.3 0.90/m
    assign is transferring legal rights or interest to someone, while convey is broader and also covers formal transfer of property or infection.
  • alienate
    7.7 0.26/m
    alienate is a technical law word for giving property to another, while convey is a formal but broader transfer verb.
  • sign over
    4.9 0.42/m
    sign over is transferring ownership by signing documents, while convey is a formal transfer term that can apply more broadly.
  • pass
    6.8 13/m
    pass is a general verb for moving rights or property to someone, while convey is a formal legal term for such transfers.
  • deliver
    3.0 16/m
    deliver means formally hand over goods or people to another, while convey focuses on legally transferring ownership or rights.
  • hand over
    2.8 9.21/m
    hand over is giving possession to someone, often by force or duty, while convey is a formal legal transfer of ownership or rights.
