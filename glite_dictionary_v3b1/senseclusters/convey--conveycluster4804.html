convey

verb
transfer property title or ownership
5.2 4.4
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈveɪ/ (AmE), /kən.ˈveɪ/ (BrE)
to formally transfer ownership, rights, or something (such as property or an infection) to another person

Examples

To convey property
Her will stated that she wished to convey the land to her children.
The deed was signed last week to officially convey ownership.
The seller agreed to convey the property to the new owner upon completion of the payment.
Mosquitoes can convey malaria from one person to another.
The deed will convey ownership of the land to the new buyers.
The teacher warned that a careless handshake could convey germs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transfer - to move ownership from one person or entity to another
  • synonym: transmit - to cause something to pass on from one person or place to another
