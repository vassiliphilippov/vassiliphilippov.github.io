bunchberry

noun
creeping shrub with red berries
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌntʃˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈbʌntʃˌbɛri / (BrE)
A creeping perennial herb of the dogwood family, noted for its whorled leaves and bright red berries, native to northern North America.

Examples

Red bunchberry berries
Clusters of bunchberry cover the forest floor in early summer.
She photographed a patch of bunchberry growing beside the hiking trail.
The bright red berries of bunchberry attract birds and wildlife.
