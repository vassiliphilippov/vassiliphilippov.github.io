cartogram

noun
map scaled by data
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrtəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːtəɡræm / (BrE)
a map in which the size of geographic areas is adjusted to represent a specific variable, such as population or income

Examples

A cartogram map
The cartogram displayed countries resized according to their population rather than their land area.
In the presentation, a cartogram was used to highlight regions with the highest income levels.
A cartogram can make it easier to compare data across different geographic areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choropleth - a map that uses color or shading to show data values in different areas
  • synonym: thematic map - a map designed to show a particular theme or data set
