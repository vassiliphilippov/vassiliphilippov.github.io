trichotomy

noun
division into three parts or categories
Pronunciation: / traɪˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / traɪˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
the division of something into three distinct parts or categories; a system or state of being threefold

Examples

A trichotomy of parts
Some psychologists use a trichotomy to describe three types of learning styles.
The ancient philosophers proposed a trichotomy of mind, body, and soul.
The report presents a trichotomy between urban, suburban, and rural communities.
