uninquiring

adjective
not curious or questioning
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ /; / ˌʌnɪnˈkwaɪ(ə)rɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈkwaɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
not curious or interested in asking questions or finding out information

Examples

An uninquiring mind
She remained uninquiring about her neighbor's unusual habits, preferring not to get involved.
The student's uninquiring attitude meant he rarely participated in class discussions.
Their uninquiring acceptance of the rules led to few changes in the organization.
His uninquiring nature often meant he missed key details others noticed.
She accepted the explanation with an uninquiring mind, never bothering to look deeper.
The audience sat uninquiring, content to listen without asking questions.
