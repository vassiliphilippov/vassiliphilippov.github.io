LCA

noun
military landing boat
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl siː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛl siː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a small, shallow-draft military boat used to transport troops to shore during amphibious operations

Examples

Troops board the LCA
The soldiers boarded the LCA before heading toward the enemy-held beach.
During the reenactment, the museum displayed an original LCA used in World War II landings.
Each LCA could carry about thirty troops and was essential for amphibious assaults.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amphibious vehicle - a vehicle capable of operating on both land and water
  • synonym: landing craft - a boat designed for landing troops and equipment on shore
