pucker in surprise

verb
contract lips/facial muscles in surprise
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌkɚ ɪn səˈpraɪz / (AmE), / ˈpʌkə ɪn səˈprʌɪz / (BrE)
to contract the lips or facial muscles, especially in response to something surprising or unexpected

Examples

To pucker in surprise
He puckered in surprise when the confetti suddenly burst from the box.
She couldn't help but pucker in surprise when she heard the unexpected news.
The children puckered in surprise at the sour taste of the lemonade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: purse your lips - to contract or draw together the lips, typically to express disapproval or thought
