distortive

adjective
causing deformation
7.5
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɔrtɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːtɪv / (BrE)
causing a change in the normal shape, structure, or appearance of something; deforming or warping.

Examples

Distortive mirror effects
The distortive effects of the funhouse mirrors made everyone look taller or shorter than they really were.
Some economic policies can have a distortive impact on market competition.
The artist used a distortive filter to create a surreal effect in the photograph.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deforming - causing something to lose its normal shape
  • antonym: restorative - having the ability to restore or return to original form
  • synonym: warping - causing something to bend or twist out of shape
