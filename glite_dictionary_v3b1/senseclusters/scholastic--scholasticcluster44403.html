scholastic

noun
someone overly focused on academic rules
8.3 6.2
Pronunciation: /skə.ˈlæ.stɪk/ (AmE), /skɒ.ˈlæ.stɪk/ (BrE)
A person who places excessive importance on formal rules and academic learning, often at the expense of practical understanding.

Examples

A strict scholastic
As a true scholastic, he corrected everyone’s grammar constantly, even in casual conversations.
Some critics argue that scholastics are out of touch with the real world.
The university is full of scholastics who value abstract theories above practical solutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pedant - a person excessively concerned with minor academic details or rules
  • antonym: pragmatist - a person oriented toward practical affairs and results
