overinclusive

adjective
too broadly inclusive
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪnˈkluːsɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪnˈkluːsɪv / (BrE)
including more items, people, or cases than necessary or appropriate for a particular purpose

Examples

An overinclusive policy
The new policy was criticized as overinclusive because it applied to many unrelated situations.
Some experts argue that the regulation is overinclusive and restricts more activities than intended.
An overinclusive definition can make it difficult to enforce the rules fairly.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: inclusive - including everything or everyone
  • antonym: underinclusive - not including enough items or people
