goldfields

noun
a yellow-flowered wildflower of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldˌfiːldz / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldˌfiːldz / (BrE)
A small slender annual wildflower with narrow opposite leaves and solitary golden-yellow flower heads, native to southwestern Oregon to Baja California and Arizona; often cultivated for its bright appearance.

Examples

Bright goldfields flowers
In spring, the hills were covered with blooming goldfields and other wildflowers.
She planted goldfields in her garden to add a splash of yellow.
The guide pointed out the goldfields growing alongside the desert trail.
