liner

noun
baseball hit that flies straight out from batter
3.6 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪnə / (BrE)
(Baseball) A ball that is hit hard and travels in a straight, low trajectory through the air, typically parallel to the ground.

Examples

A baseball liner
Fans cheered as the liner sped past the pitcher's mound.
He swung at the pitch and sent a liner into right field for a single.
The shortstop caught the liner just before it hit the ground.
