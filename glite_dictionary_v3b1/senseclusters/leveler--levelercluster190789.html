leveler

noun
advocate of abolishing social distinctions
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvələr / (AmE), / ˈlɛvələ / (BrE)
a person who advocates the elimination of social hierarchies and distinctions and promotes equality; historically, a member of a radical political movement during the English Civil War; more generally, a person, tool, or factor that makes things more equal or reduces differences between people or groups

Examples

A social leveler
Many 17th-century English levelers fought for political and social reforms.
She was called a leveler after voicing her radical views on equality.
The leveler believed society should eliminate all class differences.
During the English Civil War, the Levellers demanded equal rights and political reforms.
She described herself as a leveller, fighting against social inequality.
The activist's leveller philosophy was controversial in conservative circles.
Education is often called a great leveller in society.
Some believe that technology can act as a leveller by providing equal opportunities.
The economic crisis became a harsh leveller for people from all backgrounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divider - something that separates or creates differences
  • synonym: egalitarian - a person who believes in the equality of all people
  • synonym: equalizer - something that makes things equal or fair
