earlywood

noun
light spring wood
8.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…™rÀåliÀêw äd / (AmE), / Àà…™…ôÀåliÀêw äd / (BrE)
the part of a tree's annual growth ring formed at the beginning of the growing season, usually lighter and less dense than latewood

Examples

The earlywood layer
The pale color of the earlywood makes it easy to distinguish from the darker latewood in tree rings.
Scientists study earlywood to learn about the climate conditions during the early growing season.
In some species, the earlywood is much wider than the latewood, indicating rapid spring growth.
