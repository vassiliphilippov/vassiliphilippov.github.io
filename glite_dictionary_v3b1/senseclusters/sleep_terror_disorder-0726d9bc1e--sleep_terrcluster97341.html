sleep terror disorder

noun
sleep disorder causing intense fear during sleep

A sleep disorder characterized by episodes of intense fear, often accompanied by screaming and panic, occurring during non-REM sleep and far more severe than typical nightmares.

Examples

Sleep terror disorder episodes
Children with sleep terror disorder may wake up screaming and be inconsolable for several minutes.
Doctors may recommend counseling or medication to help manage sleep terror disorder in severe cases.
Unlike nightmares, sleep terror disorder episodes happen during non-REM sleep and are rarely remembered.

Synonyms

  • pavor nocturnus

  • night terror
    7.8 0.5/m
    night terror commonly appears in children who wake terrified with no memory, while sleep terror disorder is a more severe clinical diagnosis.
