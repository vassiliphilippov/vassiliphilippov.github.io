Indian cherry

noun
North American shrub with black fruit
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈɪndɪən ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to eastern and central United States, noted for its golden-yellow foliage in autumn and black berrylike fruit.

Examples

The Indian cherry shrub
Gardeners often plant Indian cherry to add seasonal interest to their landscapes.
In autumn, the leaves of the Indian cherry turn a striking golden-yellow color.
The Indian cherry produces small black berries that attract many birds each summer.
