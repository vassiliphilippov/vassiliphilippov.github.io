flap

noun
movable part of an aircraft wing
7.5 4.8
Pronunciation: / flæp / (AmE), / flæp / (BrE)
a movable part on the wing of an aircraft, used to increase lift or drag during takeoff and landing

Examples

Aircraft flap
It is important to retract the flaps after takeoff to reduce drag.
Mechanical failure of the flaps can seriously affect an aircraft's ability to land safely.
The pilot extended the flaps to slow the plane before landing.
Damaged flaps can seriously affect an aircraft's performance.
The flap extended as the plane approached the runway.
The pilot lowered the flap to slow down the airplane before landing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aileron - a hinged flight control surface on the wing of an aircraft, used to control roll
  • synonym: airfoil - a structure designed to provide lift when moving through air
