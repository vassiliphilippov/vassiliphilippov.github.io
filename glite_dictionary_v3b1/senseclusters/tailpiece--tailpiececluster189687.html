tailpiece

noun
string anchor on instrument
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈteɪl.piːs/ (AmE), /ˈteɪl.piːs/ (BrE)
a part at the end of a stringed instrument, such as a guitar or violin, that anchors the strings

Examples

Violin tailpiece
The luthier carefully adjusted the tailpiece to improve the sound of the violin.
After years of use, the tailpiece on her cello needed to be replaced.
He polished the metal tailpiece to match the rest of the guitar's hardware.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bridge - the part of a stringed instrument that supports the strings and transmits their vibrations
