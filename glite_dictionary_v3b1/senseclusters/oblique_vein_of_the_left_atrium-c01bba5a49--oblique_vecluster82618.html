oblique vein of the left atrium

noun
small vein on left atrium's back wall
8.0
Pronunciation: / əˈbliːk veɪn əv ðə lɛft ˈeɪ.tri.əm / (AmE), / əˈbliːk veɪn əv ðə lɛft ˈa.tri.əm / (BrE)
a small vein on the posterior wall of the left atrium that drains into the coronary sinus

Examples

The oblique vein of the left atrium
Damage to the oblique vein of the left atrium can lead to minor bleeding during cardiac procedures.
During surgery, the oblique vein of the left atrium must be carefully preserved to avoid complications.
The oblique vein of the left atrium is also known as Marshall's vein in some anatomical texts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coronary sinus - large vein collecting blood from the heart muscle
