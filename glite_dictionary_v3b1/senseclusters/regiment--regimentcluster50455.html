regiment

verb
control or organize strictly
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdʒəˌmɛnt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʒɪmənt / (BrE)
to organize or control strictly, often by enforced discipline or order

Examples

To regiment daily life
He was criticized for attempting to regiment his team's creativity with too many rules.
Some parents believe it's important to regiment their children's daily routines.
The principal tried to regiment every aspect of student life at the school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: control - to exercise restraint or direction over
  • synonym: discipline - to train or bring under control
  • synonym: order - to arrange methodically or systematically
