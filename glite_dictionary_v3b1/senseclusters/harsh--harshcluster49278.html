harsh

adjective
unpleasantly rough or jarring
3.9 4.64/m
Pronunciation: / hÉ‘rÊƒ / (AmE), / hÉ‘ËÊƒ / (BrE)
Unpleasantly rough, jarring, or severe to the senses.

Examples

Harsh taste
She recoiled at the harsh taste of the medicine.
The harsh glare of the headlights made it hard to see the road.
The harsh sound of metal scraping against metal echoed through the warehouse.

Synonyms

  • raucous

  • bitter
    2.7 3.09/m
    bitter often means a sharp taste or cold sting that hurts, while harsh describes general rough, jarring sensory effects.
