kpc

noun
astronomical distance unit
Pronunciation: / ˌkeɪ piː ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌkeɪ piː ˈsiː / (BrE)
a unit of distance in astronomy equal to one thousand parsecs, commonly used to measure large-scale cosmic distances

Examples

Five kpc distance
The spiral arm of the galaxy extends nearly 5 kpc from its center.
Astronomers measured the distance between the two clusters as approximately 12 kpc.
The Milky Way's disk is about 15 kpc in diameter.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: parsec - an astronomical unit of distance equal to about 3.26 light-years
