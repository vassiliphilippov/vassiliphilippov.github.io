Synonyms

  • protect
    1.0 200/m
    protect is a general everyday verb meaning keep someone safe, while shield often implies using something to block harm directly.
  • safeguard
    3.7 2/m
    safeguard sounds formal and means make someone safe by careful measures, while shield often means block or shelter from danger.
  • cover
    1.9 16/m
    cover often means place something over to protect in many contexts, while shield stresses stopping direct harm or attack.
  • guard
    1.3 4/m
    guard means take precautions or watch to prevent harm, while shield usually means act as a barrier against danger.
  • defend
    1.3 50/m
    defend means fight or act to stop an attack, while shield often means provide a passive barrier or protection from harm.
  • shelter
    2.5 4/m
    shelter means give a safe place or cover from weather or danger, while shield often means stop harm from reaching someone.
  • screen
    3.9 1/m
    screen means hide or block view and risks, while shield usually means protect physically or stop direct danger from reaching someone.
  • ward off
    4.4 2/m
    ward off means keep something unpleasant away before it comes, while shield usually means protect by blocking harm close to you.
  • fend off
    3.9 2/m
    fend off means actively repel an attack or problem by force, while shield usually means provide passive protection or a barrier.
