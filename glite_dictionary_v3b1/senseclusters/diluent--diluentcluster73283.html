diluent

noun
a substance that dilutes another
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪljuənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪljuːənt / (BrE)
A substance added to another substance to dilute it, especially in a chemical or medical context.

Examples

A diluent substance
A saline solution was used as a diluent for the medication.
In medical laboratories, distilled water often serves as a diluent.
The chemist selected an appropriate diluent to reduce the concentration of the acid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves another to form a solution
