hold up to scrutiny

verb
remain valid under careful examination
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ʌp tuː ˈskruːtəni / (AmE), / həʊld ʌp tuː ˈskruːtəni / (BrE)
to remain valid, accurate, or justified when examined carefully or critically

Examples

To hold up to scrutiny in court
Her alibi seemed convincing at first, but it did not hold up to scrutiny in court.
Many politicians make promises that fail to hold up to scrutiny after the election is over.
The scientist's hypothesis did not hold up to scrutiny when more data became available.
