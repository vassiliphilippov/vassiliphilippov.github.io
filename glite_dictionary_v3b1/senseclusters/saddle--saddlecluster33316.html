saddle

noun
low pass or ridge between two peaks
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsædəl / (AmE), / ˈsædəl / (BrE)
A low pass or ridge between two peaks in a mountain range, usually sloping gently and resembling the shape of a saddle.

Examples

Mountain saddle
A narrow saddle connects the two mountain summits.
Rain collected in the saddle between the peaks after the storm.
The hikers crossed the saddle to reach the next valley.
