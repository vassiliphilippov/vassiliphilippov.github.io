confessional

noun
booth for hearing confessions (church)
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛʃənl̩ /; / kənˈfɛʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
a small private booth or room, especially in a church, where a person confesses secrets or sins to a priest or another person

Examples

Church confessional booth
He waited nervously outside the confessional before entering to speak with the priest.
The confessional was decorated with ornate wood carvings.
The church added a new confessional near the altar for parishioners.
The priest waited in the confessional to listen to parishioners seeking forgiveness.
On the reality show, contestants speak honestly about their feelings in the confessional.
She found comfort in the quiet of the confessional as she shared her worries.
