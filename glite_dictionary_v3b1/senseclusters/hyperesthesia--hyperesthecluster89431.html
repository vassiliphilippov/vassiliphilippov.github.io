hyperesthesia

noun
extreme sensitivity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərɛsˈθiːziə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərəˈsθiːziə / (BrE)
a condition in which a person has an unusually high sensitivity to touch or other sensory stimuli

Examples

Painful hyperesthesia
After the surgery, the patient developed hyperesthesia in her left arm.
People with hyperesthesia may find even light touches uncomfortable or painful.
The doctor diagnosed him with hyperesthesia following his complaints of increased skin sensitivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allodynia - pain from stimuli that do not normally cause pain
  • antonym: hypoesthesia - reduced sensitivity to sensory stimuli
