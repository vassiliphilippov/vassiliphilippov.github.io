butchery

noun

Examples

War butchery

The documentary explored the butchery that took place during the civil war.

Synonyms

  • slaughter
    5.7 4.57/m
    slaughter is a strong word for violent mass killing in war or crime, while butchery adds a sense of savage cruelty.
  • massacre
    7.0 10/m
    massacre is often a sudden large-scale killing of civilians, while butchery focuses on very savage, excessive cruelty.
  • carnage
    8.2 3.42/m
    carnage paints a scene of great gore and death across a place, while butchery means brutal, senseless killing.
  • bloodbath
    7.0 1.47/m
    bloodbath describes a place where many are killed in chaos, while butchery stresses savage and excessive violence.
  • mass murder
    6.7 1.51/m
    mass murder is the planned killing of many people by one person or group, while butchery highlights savage brutality.
  • battue
    9.9 0.00/m
    battue names an indiscriminate, often systematic killing and sounds rare or technical, while butchery implies savage, brutal slaughter.
  • bloodshed
    4.6 4.22/m
    bloodshed covers any killing or wounding in a conflict, while butchery refers to especially savage and excessive killing.
  • bloodletting
    9.3 0.47/m
    bloodletting often refers to large-scale political or symbolic killing, while butchery focuses on brutal, savage physical slaughter.
