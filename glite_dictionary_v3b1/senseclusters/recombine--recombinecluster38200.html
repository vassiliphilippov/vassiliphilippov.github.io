recombine

verb
combine again, especially genetic material
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.kəmˈbaɪn /; / ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˌriː.kəmˈbaɪn /; / ˌriːkəmˈbaɪn / (BrE)
to combine genetic material (such as DNA or genes) again, or to undergo genetic recombination

Examples

To recombine DNA
Genetic engineers can recombine fragments of DNA to create new genetic sequences.
It is possible to recombine viruses in the laboratory to study their properties.
Scientists are developing methods to recombine genes to treat certain diseases.
DNA fragments can recombine in the laboratory, producing unique strands.
During meiosis, the chromosomes recombine to create new genetic variations.
Viruses often recombine their genetic material to adapt to new environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combine - to join or merge things together
  • synonym: recombine - to combine again
  • antonym: segregate - to separate genetic material during cell division
