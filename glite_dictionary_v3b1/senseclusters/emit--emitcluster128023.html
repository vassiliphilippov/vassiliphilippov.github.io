emit

verb
produce or release a sound
4.4 8.62/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªËˆmÉªt / (AmE), / ÉªËˆmÉªt / (BrE)
to send out or release something such as light, heat, sound, gas, or radiation

Examples

To emit light
During the speech, some audience members emitted disapproving murmurs.
She couldn't help but emit a small gasp when she saw the accident.
The machine suddenly emits a loud buzzing sound every hour.
Some flowers emit a pleasant fragrance in the evening.
The factory was found to emit dangerous gases into the atmosphere.
When heated, the substance begins to emit smoke and an unpleasant odor.
Many plants emit oxygen during the process of photosynthesis.

Synonyms

  • give off
    2.6 4.19/m
    give off is a common phrasal verb for producing smells, heat, or light, while emit is the more formal verb.
  • radiate
    4.3 2.04/m
    radiate sends energy, heat, or light out from a central source, while emit is a general verb for releasing them.
  • emanate
    6.5 6.21/m
    emanate comes out from a source as a smell, sound, or feeling, while emit covers physical energy or gases.
  • release
    2.7 27/m
    release lets a substance, gas, or energy escape or be discharged, while emit usually describes sending them out.
  • give out
    2.9 0.29/m
    give out produces or sends forth light, heat, or sound, while emit more specifically means to send out energy or gas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up a substance, often used as an antonym to 'emit' for gases or light
  • synonym: release - to allow something to be set free or emitted
  • antonym: suppress - to prevent the expression of a sound or feeling
  • synonym: utter - to express audibly; to say or pronounce
