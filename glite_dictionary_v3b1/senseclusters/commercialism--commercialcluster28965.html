commercialism

noun
emphasis on making profits in commerce
2.3 4.6
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmɜːr.ʃəl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /kə.ˈmɜː.ʃəl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practices and attitudes that emphasize making profits or the importance of commercial activity, often with a focus on sales, purchases, and supplying goods and services

Examples

Holiday commercialism
He was disillusioned by the commercialism that dominated holiday advertising campaigns.
Some critics argue that modern art has been corrupted by commercialism and profit motives.
The festival has faced backlash for its increasing commercialism each year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: altruism - selfless concern for others, opposite of commercial self-interest
  • synonym: materialism - focus on material possessions and wealth
