counterreaction

noun
opposing response
5.9
Pronunciation: /kaʊn.tə.rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /kaʊn.tə.riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /kaʊn.tə.rɪ.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /kaʊn.tə.riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
a response or reaction that happens because of a previous reaction, often to oppose or neutralize it

Examples

A counterreaction occurs
The scientist observed a counterreaction that neutralized the effects of the initial experiment.
A sudden counterreaction in the market caused prices to stabilize after a sharp drop.
The medication produced a counterreaction that helped restore the patient's normal heart rhythm.
The government's decision led to a widespread counterreaction from the public.
Every major reform seems to provoke some kind of counterreaction among those affected.
There was a noticeable counterreaction after the initial excitement faded.
