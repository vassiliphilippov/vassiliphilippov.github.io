choledochotomy

noun
bile duct surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊlɪˌdɑːˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ˌkɒlɪˌdɒkˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the common bile duct, usually to remove an obstruction such as a gallstone

Examples

A choledochotomy surgery
The patient recovered quickly after undergoing a choledochotomy to clear the blocked duct.
A choledochotomy may be necessary if gallstones are lodged in the common bile duct.
During the operation, the surgeon performed a choledochotomy to access the bile duct.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choledocholithotomy - surgical removal of a stone from the common bile duct
