firsthand

adjective, adverb
direct from original source
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝstˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈfɜːstˌhænd / (BrE)
directly from the original source or personal experience; not through someone else

Examples

Firsthand information
I prefer to receive firsthand information rather than rely on rumors.
She gave a firsthand account of the events she witnessed.
Traveling abroad offers the opportunity to gain firsthand experience of different cultures.
He wanted to see the results firsthand before making a decision.
Journalists report news firsthand to ensure accuracy.
She experienced the excitement of the concert firsthand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secondhand - acquired from others rather than directly from the original source
