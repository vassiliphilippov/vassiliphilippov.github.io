deafeningly

adverb
extremely intensely
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛfənɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈdɛfənɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that is extremely intense, obvious, or impossible to ignore, often used figuratively beyond sound.

Examples

Deafeningly clear mistake
The mistake in the presentation was deafeningly clear to everyone in the room.
She was deafeningly silent when asked about the incident, making her discomfort obvious.
The applause was deafeningly loud, showing how much the audience loved the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overwhelmingly - in a way that is very great or intense
  • antonym: subtly - in a way that is not obvious or easily noticed
