laurel-tree

noun
tree with dark red heartwood
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrəl tri / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl tri / (BrE)
A small tree native to the southern United States, known for its dark red heartwood.

Examples

A laurel-tree plant
In her garden, she planted a young laurel-tree to provide shade and fragrant leaves.
Many birds are attracted to the berries of the laurel-tree in the spring.
The rich dark wood of the laurel-tree is highly prized by woodworkers.
