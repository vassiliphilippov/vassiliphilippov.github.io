parol

noun
verbal legal statement
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈroʊl / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊl / (BrE)
a statement or agreement made verbally rather than in writing, especially in legal contexts

Examples

Parol evidence rule
The judge ruled that parol evidence could not be used to alter the written contract.
Lawyers often discuss the importance of parol agreements in contract disputes.
Because the promise was only parol, it was difficult to prove in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oral - spoken rather than written
  • antonym: written - expressed in writing
