reservation

noun
district set aside for a special purpose
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛzərˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛzəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
an area of land set aside by a government for a particular purpose, especially for the use of Indigenous peoples or for wildlife protection

Examples

Wildlife reservation
Many endangered species are protected within the wildlife reservation.
The Navajo Nation is the largest reservation in the United States.
The government established a new reservation for the conservation of native plants.
