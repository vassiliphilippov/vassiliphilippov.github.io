traditional view

noun
long-established belief or opinion
Pronunciation: / trəˈdɪʃənəl vju / (AmE), / trəˈdɪʃənəl vjuː / (BrE)
A belief or opinion that has been held by a group or society for a long time and is considered conventional or customary.

Examples

The traditional view holds
Her research challenges the traditional view of how species evolve.
Many people in rural areas still hold a traditional view of marriage.
The debate centered on whether the traditional view should continue to shape educational policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orthodox - following or conforming to established or traditional beliefs or practices
  • antonym: progressive view - a belief or opinion that favors new ideas and change
