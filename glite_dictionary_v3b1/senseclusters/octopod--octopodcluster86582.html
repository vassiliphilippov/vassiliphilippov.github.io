octopod

noun
eight-armed cephalopod mollusc
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑktəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈɒktəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a cephalopod mollusc with eight arms and no internal shell, such as an octopus

Examples

Deep-sea octopod
Marine biologists discovered a new species of octopod living near the deep-sea vents.
The octopod exhibited remarkable intelligence while navigating the maze in the laboratory.
Unlike cuttlefish, an octopod lacks an internal shell and has eight flexible arms.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: decapod - a crustacean or mollusc with ten limbs
  • synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied, eight-armed mollusc of the order Octopoda
