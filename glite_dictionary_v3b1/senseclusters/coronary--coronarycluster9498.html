coronary

noun
heart blood vessel
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒrənəri / (BrE)
a blood vessel that supplies blood to the heart muscle, especially a coronary artery

Examples

Coronary artery
Doctors monitor the coronary for signs of blockage during heart examinations.
A blocked coronary can lead to a heart attack if not treated quickly.
The surgeon repaired the damaged coronary to restore normal blood flow to the heart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • antonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart
