Synonyms

  • meandering
    4.8 0.00/m
    meandering is slow and follows many gentle curves like a river, while winding simply means having many bends.
  • sinuous
    7.4 0.3/m
    sinuous is smooth and graceful with flowing curves, while winding can be neutral and does not imply elegance.
  • serpentine
    7.6 0.2/m
    serpentine is snake-like and sharply twisting, while winding only describes many bends without the animal image in mind.
  • curving
    2.5 0.00/m
    curving has smooth rounded arcs like a child drawing a curve, while winding suggests many linked bends.
  • twisting
    3.7 0.00/m
    twisting has sharp or tight turns and sudden bends, while winding often means a longer sequence of gentle curves.
  • twisty
    4.7 0.5/m
    twisty is informal and image-heavy, often used in speech or reviews, while winding is neutral and used in maps.
  • wandering
    3.4 0.00/m
    wandering implies a lack of direction and can describe routes that drift, while winding simply notes many bends in the path.
  • rambling
    5.1 0.09/m
    rambling often means spreading out or irregular shape, used for walks or buildings, while winding focuses on curved lines.
  • tortuous
    8.1 0.3/m
    tortuous suggests long, difficult, or complex paths, whereas winding simply describes many physical bends without extra effort to travel.
