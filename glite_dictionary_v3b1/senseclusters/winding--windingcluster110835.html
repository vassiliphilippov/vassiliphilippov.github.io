winding

adjective
having many bends or turns
4.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪn.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈwɪn.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪn.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈwɪn.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
having many bends or curves; not straight (used for roads, paths, rivers, etc.)

Examples

Winding road
The car made its way down the winding road that hugged the side of the mountain.
The river carved a winding course across the valley, glittering in the afternoon sun.
We followed a winding path through the dense forest, unsure of where it would lead.
A winding stream flows behind the old farmhouse.
The winding road through the mountains offered breathtaking views at every turn.
We hiked along a winding path that disappeared into the forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meandering - following a winding and turning course, especially of a river or road
  • antonym: straight - without bends or curves; direct
  • synonym: tortuous - full of twists and turns
  • synonym: twisty - having many twists and turns; not straight
