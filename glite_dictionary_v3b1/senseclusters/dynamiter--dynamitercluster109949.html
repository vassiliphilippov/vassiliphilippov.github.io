dynamiter

noun
person using dynamite, often politically
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈdaɪnəˌmaɪtə / (BrE)
a person who uses dynamite, especially for blasting, demolition, or political purposes

Examples

Dynamiters use explosives
In the late 19th century, some revolutionaries were known as dynamiters for their use of explosives.
Literature from that era often depicted the dynamiter as a shadowy figure plotting destruction.
The police arrested the suspected dynamiter at the scene of the explosion.
The experienced dynamiter ensured all safety protocols were followed before the blast.
A team led by a skilled dynamiter cleared the old bridge in just one day.
During the gold rush, a dynamiter was essential for breaking through tough rock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blaster - a person who sets off explosives for demolition or mining
  • synonym: demolitionist - a person who demolishes buildings or structures, often using explosives
  • synonym: saboteur - person who deliberately destroys or damages things for political or military advantage
