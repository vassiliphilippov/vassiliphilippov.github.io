focus

noun
point of convergence of light or radiation
5.2 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.kəs/ /; / /ˈfoʊ.kɪs/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.kəs/ /; / /ˈfəʊ.kɪs/ (BrE)
the central point where rays of light, energy, or activity come together or start from (such as in optics, geometry, or medicine)

Examples

The focus of a lens
Astronomers adjusted the telescope to find the focus of the distant star.
The lens brings the image into sharp focus on the screen.
The rays meet at a single focus behind the lens.
For an ellipse, the sum of the distances from any point on the curve to each focus is constant.
The parabolic mirror reflects all rays through its focus to form a sharp image.
The planet orbits the sun, which is at one focus of its elliptical path.
Antibiotics are chosen based on the likely focus and type of bacteria involved.
The doctor identified a single focus of infection in the patient's lung.
The removal of the focus is essential to prevent further spread of the disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directrix - a fixed straight line used in the definition of a conic section, together with the focus
  • synonym: focal point - the exact point at which rays converge or diverge in optics
