Carl Friedrich Gauss

proper noun
German mathematician, pioneer in number theory
Pronunciation: / kɑrl ˈfriːdrɪk ˈɡaʊs / (AmE), / kɑːl ˈfriːdrɪk ˈɡaʊs / (BrE)
(1777–1855) A German mathematician who made significant contributions to number theory, as well as to electricity, magnetism, astronomy, and geodesy.

Examples

Carl Friedrich Gauss mathematician
Many concepts in number theory are attributed to Carl Friedrich Gauss and are still taught today.
The contributions of Carl Friedrich Gauss to astronomy revolutionized our understanding of planetary motion.
The mathematician Carl Friedrich Gauss made groundbreaking advances in many fields during the 19th century.
