bridgehead

noun
military defensive position at a bridge
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪdʒˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbrɪdʒˌhɛd / (BrE)
an area in enemy territory, often at the end of a bridge, that is captured and held to allow more troops or equipment to arrive

Examples

Securing the bridgehead was essential to allow reinforcements to cross safely.

The troops quickly established a bridgehead before advancing further into enemy territory.
Defending the bridgehead was crucial to maintaining supply lines.
Paratroopers secured a bridgehead across the river during the early hours of the battle.
The army established a bridgehead to prepare for the next phase of the operation.
