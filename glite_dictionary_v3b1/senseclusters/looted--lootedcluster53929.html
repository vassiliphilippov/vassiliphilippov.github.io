looted

adjective
having been robbed of valuables
5.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêt…™d / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêt…™d / (BrE)
(of a place or object) having been wrongfully stripped of valuables, typically during a robbery, war, or riot.

Examples

Looted stores
After the riot, the looted stores were left empty and damaged.
The museum displayed artifacts recovered from looted gravesites.
Villagers slowly began to return to their looted homes, hoping to rebuild.

Synonyms

  • plundered
    6.1 0.00/m
    plundered is taken by force during disorder, often by soldiers or gangs, while looted is stripped of valuables in similar chaos.
  • pillaged
    7.2 0.00/m
    pillaged is robbed and often badly damaged during war or raids, while looted focuses on valuables taken during riots or theft.
  • ransacked
    6.0 0.00/m
    ransacked is searched and turned upside down to find things, while looted simply means valuables were taken away.
  • sacked
    7.8 0.00/m
    sacked is a place attacked, robbed and often burnt by an army, while looted describes valuables taken during disorder.
  • despoiled
    8.7 0.00/m
    despoiled is a formal word for something stripped or ruined, often used in writing, while looted is a common everyday term.
