gallinaceous

adjective
relating to fowl-like birds (Galliformes)
Pronunciation: / ˌɡælɪˈneɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈneɪʃəs / (BrE)
of or relating to birds of the order Galliformes, such as chickens, turkeys, and pheasants; resembling or characteristic of these birds.

Examples

Gallinaceous birds
Many gallinaceous birds are ground-dwellers and have strong legs for scratching the soil.
The farm is home to several gallinaceous species, including pheasants and quails.
The researchers studied the mating habits of gallinaceous birds in their natural habitat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fowl - a domesticated bird kept for eggs or meat
  • antonym: songbird - a bird noted for singing, usually from the order Passeriformes (contrasting type of bird)
