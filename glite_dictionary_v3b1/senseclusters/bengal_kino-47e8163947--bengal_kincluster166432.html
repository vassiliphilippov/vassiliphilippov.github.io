Bengal kino

noun
reddish resin from dhak tree, astringent
Pronunciation: / bɛnˈɡɔl ˈkiːnoʊ / (AmE), / bɛnˈɡɔːl ˈkiːnəʊ / (BrE)
A reddish dried juice obtained from the dhak tree (Butea monosperma), used as an astringent in traditional medicine and dyeing.

Examples

Bengal kino dye
Fabric was sometimes dyed using Bengal kino to achieve a rich, earthy color.
The reddish resin known as Bengal kino is extracted from the dhak tree in India.
Traditional healers often use Bengal kino for its astringent properties in treating wounds.
