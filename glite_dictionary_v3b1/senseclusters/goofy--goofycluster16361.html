goofy

adjective
silly or foolish
3.5 5.02/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°uÀêfi / (AmE), / Àà…°uÀêfi / (BrE)
silly, ridiculous, or behaving in a foolish way; amusingly absurd.

Examples

A goofy smile
He wore a goofy grin throughout the entire meeting.
Her goofy jokes never fail to make our group laugh.
That was a goofy idea, but somehow it worked out in the end.

Synonyms

  • silly
    1.0 37/m
    silly shows a general lack of sense and often seems childish, while goofy suggests awkward, playful foolishness that looks a bit ridiculous.
  • goony
    8.9 0.06/m
    goony is clumsy and awkward in a silly way, while goofy is more playful and oddly absurd rather than mainly clumsy.
  • daffy
    8.4 0.06/m
    daffy acts slightly crazy and harmlessly silly, while goofy is more gently absurd and often awkward in a funny way.
  • daft
    5.8 0.89/m
    daft means not sensible and a bit foolish in a mild way, while goofy is more playful and visibly ridiculous in action.
  • dippy
    8.2 0.14/m
    dippy seems scatterbrained and a little odd in thinking, while goofy shows silly behaviour that is more physical and awkward.
  • zany
    7.6 0.65/m
    zany looks wild and clownish with strange acts, while goofy is more simple, awkward, and gently silly in a quiet way.
  • wacky
    5.9 3.76/m
    wacky is loudly odd and brightly strange to surprise people, while goofy is more awkward, mild, and gently absurd in a soft way.
