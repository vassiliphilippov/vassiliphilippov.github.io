parasitosis

noun
false belief of infestation
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpærəsaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a psychiatric disorder in which a person falsely believes they are infested with parasites despite no medical evidence

Examples

Delusional parasitosis
The patient insisted she had bugs under her skin, a classic sign of parasitosis.
Doctors ruled out any real infection, but his symptoms pointed to parasitosis.
Treatment for parasitosis often involves both medical and psychological support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delusional infestation - a psychiatric condition involving a false belief of being infested with organisms
  • synonym: paranoia - an irrational and persistent feeling that people are 'out to get you'
