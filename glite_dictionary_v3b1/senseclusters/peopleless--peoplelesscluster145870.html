peopleless

adjective
without people
7.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpiÀêp…ôll…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàpiÀêp…ôll…ôs / (BrE)
having no people present; uninhabited or deserted.

Examples

A peopleless street
The old factory stood peopleless at the edge of town, its windows broken and doors ajar.
After the evacuation, the once-busy streets were eerily peopleless in the early morning light.
They explored a peopleless island, amazed by its untouched beauty and silence.

Synonyms

  • uninhabited
    4.0 1.03/m
    uninhabited means no people live there, while peopleless can mean a place has no people present now.
  • unpeopled
    8.0 0.12/m
    unpeopled sounds literary and means empty of people, while peopleless is more general and used in everyday descriptions.
  • unpopulated
    4.8 0.30/m
    unpopulated means having no population or settled people, while peopleless suggests simply that no people are present now.
  • deserted
    3.0 0.11/m
    deserted looks empty because people left it and often feels abandoned, while peopleless only says no people are present.
  • unoccupied
    3.4 0.53/m
    unoccupied means not being used or filled now and may apply to seats or rooms, while peopleless focuses on having no people present.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crowded - full of people; packed
  • synonym: deserted - left without people; abandoned
  • synonym: empty - containing nothing; not occupied
