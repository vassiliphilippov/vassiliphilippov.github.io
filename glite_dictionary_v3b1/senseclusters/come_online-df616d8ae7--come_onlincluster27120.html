come online

verb
to become operational or available (of a system, etc.)
3.7 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm É‘nËˆlaÉªn / (AmE), / kÊŒm É’nËˆlaÉªn / (BrE)
(of a system, service, or feature) to become operational or available for use, especially after being installed, developed, or prepared.

Examples

To come online soon
The new product will come online by the end of the month.
The website finally came online after weeks of preparation.
We are waiting for the power plant to come online before starting operations.

Synonyms

  • come on
    2.5 14/m
    come on is used for lights or devices starting to work in everyday situations, while come online refers to systems becoming available after setup.
  • come up
    3.5 9.62/m
    come up often describes a computer or server starting to run, while come online emphasizes a service becoming fully available after preparation.
