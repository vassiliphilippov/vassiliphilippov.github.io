ELK

noun
log-management software stack
7.1
Pronunciation: /elk/ (AmE), /elk/ (BrE)
A software stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, and Kibana) used to collect, store, search, and visualize log and event data for monitoring and analysis.

Examples

ELK stack software
The data team deployed the ELK stack to centralize server logs and simplify incident searches.
After the outage, engineers used ELK dashboards to trace the sequence of error events across hosts.
Many startups choose ELK or cloud logging services for real-time monitoring and alerting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Elastic Stack - Alternative name for the ELK stack; includes Elasticsearch, Logstash, and Kibana (and sometimes Beats).
  • synonym: centralized logging - The practice of collecting and storing logs from many systems in one place for search and analysis.
