fight back

verb
defend oneself or resist an attack
Pronunciation: / faɪt bæk /; / faɪt ˈbæk / (AmE), / faɪt bæk /; / faɪt ˈbæk / (BrE)
to resist or retaliate against an attack, criticism, or challenge

Examples

To fight back strongly
Despite being outnumbered, she refused to give up and chose to fight back.

During the debate, she managed to fight back against her critics’ harsh remarks.
He refused to be bullied any longer and finally fought back with confidence.
The villagers decided to fight back against the bandits who threatened their homes.
