membrane

verb
to cover with membrane
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛmˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
to cover, line, or enclose something with a membrane, or to cause something to develop a membrane

Examples

To membrane a surface
The scientists membraned the petri dishes to observe the growth of bacteria.
To protect the sample, the technician carefully membraned the surface with a thin film.
After the experiment, the students noticed that the cells had membraned naturally over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enclose - to surround or close off on all sides
  • antonym: expose - to leave something uncovered or unprotected
