clatter

verb
make a loud rattling noise
Pronunciation: / ˈklæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈklætə / (BrE)
to make a loud, rattling sound, often by striking hard objects together repeatedly

Examples

To clatter down stairs
Footsteps clattered down the empty hallway late at night.
She accidentally clattered the pots and pans while cooking dinner.
The plates began to clatter as the train sped over the tracks.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: crash - make a sudden loud noise as of something breaking
  • synonym: rattle - make a rapid succession of short, sharp sounds
