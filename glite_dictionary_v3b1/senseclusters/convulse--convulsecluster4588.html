convulse

verb
shake violently, esp. with laughter or emotion
2.3 6.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈvʌls / (AmE), / kənˈvʌls / (BrE)
to shake or make something shake violently and uncontrollably, often because of illness, emotion, or force

Examples

To convulse with laughter
His witty remark convulsed everyone at the dinner table.
She convulsed so hard with giggles that she could barely breathe.
The crowd convulsed with laughter at the comedian's jokes.
Crowds watched as the building seemed to convulse during the earthquake.
The child's body would convulse whenever the severe coughing fit occurred.
The patient began to convulse as the seizure struck.
A powerful earthquake convulsed the entire region last night.
She began to convulse with laughter at the comedian's joke.
The sudden explosion made everyone convulse with fear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or cave in; also used for being overcome with laughter
  • antonym: relax - to make or become less tense or rigid
  • synonym: shake - to move with quick, short movements up and down or from side to side
  • synonym: spasm - a sudden involuntary muscular contraction
