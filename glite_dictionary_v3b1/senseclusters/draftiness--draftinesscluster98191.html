draftiness

noun
unfinished quality
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdræftinəs / (AmE), / ˈdrɑːftinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being unfinished, preliminary, or not fully developed, like a draft version

Examples

Draftiness of a report
The draftiness of the report was obvious, with many sections still needing revision.
She apologized for the draftiness of her essay and promised to submit a final version soon.
Despite its draftiness, the proposal contained some promising ideas for the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incompleteness - the state of not being finished or whole
  • antonym: polish - a finished or refined quality
