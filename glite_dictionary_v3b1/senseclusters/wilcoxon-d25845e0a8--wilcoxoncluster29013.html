Wilcoxon

proper noun
statistical test name
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlˌkɑksən / (AmE), / ˈwɪlkəksən / (BrE)
the name of a statistical test used to compare two samples, named after Frank Wilcoxon

Examples

Wilcoxon test
The researcher chose the Wilcoxon test because the data were not normally distributed.
After collecting the results, they applied the Wilcoxon method to compare the two groups.
Many scientists prefer the Wilcoxon test for analyzing small sample sizes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mann-Whitney - a statistical test for comparing two independent samples
  • synonym: t-test - a statistical test used to compare the means of two groups
