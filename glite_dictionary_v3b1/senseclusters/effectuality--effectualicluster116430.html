effectuality

noun
quality of being effective
5.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˌfɛktʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ɪˌfɛktjʊˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being effective or having the power to produce a desired result

Examples

The effectuality of treatment
Questions were raised about the effectuality of the treatment during clinical trials.
Scholars debate the effectuality of economic sanctions in influencing international behavior.
The effectuality of the new policy will be assessed after six months of implementation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ineffectiveness - the lack of ability to produce the desired effect
