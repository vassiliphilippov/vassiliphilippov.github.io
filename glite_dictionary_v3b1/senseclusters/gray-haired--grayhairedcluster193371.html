gray-haired

adjective
having gray or white hair
Pronunciation: / ˌɡreɪˈhɛrd /; / ˈɡreɪˌhɛrd /; / ɡreɪ hɛrd / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪˈheəd /; / ˈɡreɪˌheəd /; / ɡreɪ hɛəd / (BrE)
(of a person) having hair that is gray or white, typically as a sign of aging.

Examples

A grey-haired woman smiled kindly as she boarded the bus.
Despite being grey-haired, she ran faster than many of the younger athletes.
The grey-haired professor captivated the class with his stories of the past.
A crowd of grey-haired supporters gathered at the town hall meeting.
She helped an elderly, grey-haired woman cross the busy street.
The grey-haired professor greeted his students with a warm smile.
