stoniness

noun
rocky condition
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊninəs / (AmE), / ˈstəʊninəs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of land or soil containing many stones, making cultivation or movement difficult

Examples

Stoniness of the soil
The stoniness of the hillside made it difficult to plant crops successfully.
Hikers struggled with the stoniness of the trail after the recent landslide.
The garden's stoniness required extra effort to prepare the soil for flowers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rockiness - the state of being full of rocks or stones
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even or level surface
