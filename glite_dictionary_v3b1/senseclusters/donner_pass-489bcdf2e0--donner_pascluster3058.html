Donner Pass

proper noun
mountain pass in California, site of Donner Party tragedy
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑnɚ pæs / (AmE), / ˈdɒnə pɑːs / (BrE)
a mountain pass in northeastern California near Lake Tahoe, historically known as the site where members of the Donner Party, trapped by snow in 1846-47, resorted to cannibalism to survive

Examples

Donner Pass mountain
Heavy snowfall often closes Donner Pass during winter months, causing major delays on the interstate.
Many hikers visit Donner Pass to see the memorial dedicated to the ill-fated Donner Party.
The tragic events at Donner Pass are a significant part of California's pioneer history.
