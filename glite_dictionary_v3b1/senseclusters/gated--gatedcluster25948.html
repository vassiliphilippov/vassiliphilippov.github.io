gated

adjective
controlled by a gate
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡeɪtɪd / (BrE)
controlled by an electronic or signal-processing gate so that a signal or operation occurs only when permitted by a control input

Examples

Gated signal
The audio signal was gated to eliminate background noise during quiet passages.
A gated circuit only allows current to flow when the control input is active.
Engineers often use gated signals to synchronize different parts of a system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - uninterrupted or occurring without breaks
  • synonym: triggered - activated by a specific signal or event
