glued to

adjective
unable to stop focusing on something
4.5 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / …°luÀêd tu / (AmE), / …°luÀêd t…ô / (BrE)
so interested in or absorbed by something that you cannot stop watching it, listening to it, or paying attention to it

Examples

Be glued to screen
He became glued to the news updates after the storm started.
She was glued to her phone during the entire meeting.
The children sat glued to the television set all afternoon.

Synonyms

  • engrossed
    4.5 0.07/m
    engrossed means being mentally absorbed and giving something your full attention, while glued to shows you cannot stop watching or listening.
  • spellbound
    5.2 0.69/m
    spellbound describes being so fascinated that you give something amazed attention, while glued to is a common phrase for unable to stop watching.
  • captivated
    3.9 0.00/m
    captivated means being charmed and held by interest, while glued to describes being fixed to something and unable to look away.
  • fascinated
    2.1 0.07/m
    fascinated means strongly interested and eager to learn, while glued to implies a physical, stuck attention such as watching without moving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorbed - deeply interested or wholly engaged
  • antonym: detached - not involved or interested; aloof
