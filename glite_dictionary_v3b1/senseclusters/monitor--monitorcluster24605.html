monitor

verb
to observe or check; track by device
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnɪtɚ /; / ˈmɑːnɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
to observe, check, or keep track of something, especially over time or using equipment

Examples

To monitor progress
Government agencies monitor air quality in major cities to ensure public safety.
She monitored the progress of the experiment using special equipment.
The doctor will monitor your heart rate during surgery.
Hospitals monitor patients' vital signs throughout the night.
The company uses sensors to monitor water quality in the river.
The scientist will monitor the experiment closely to ensure accurate results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to; disregard
  • synonym: observe - to watch carefully and attentively
  • synonym: supervise - to oversee someone or something
  • synonym: track - to follow and record the progress or development of something
