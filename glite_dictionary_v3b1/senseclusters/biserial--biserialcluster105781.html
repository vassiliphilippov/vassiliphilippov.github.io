biserial

adjective
in two rows
8.9
Pronunciation: / baɪˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / baɪˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
arranged in two rows or series, especially in scientific or technical contexts

Examples

Biserial arrangement
The crystals in this mineral are biserial, forming two distinct lines.
In some species, the teeth are biserial along the jaw for efficient feeding.
The arrangement of seeds in the pod is biserial, making them easy to count.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: multiserial - arranged in multiple rows or series
  • antonym: uniserial - arranged in a single row or series
