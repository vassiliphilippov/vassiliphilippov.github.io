round up the cattle

verb
gather cattle into a group
8.0
Pronunciation: / raʊnd ʌp ðə ˈkæt̬l̩ / (AmE), / raʊnd ʌp ðə ˈkætl̩ / (BrE)
to gather or collect cattle into a group, especially for the purposes of moving or counting them.

Examples

To round up the cattle quickly
Every spring, the workers round up the cattle for branding and health checks.
The cowboys rounded up the cattle and led them back to the main pasture.
The ranchers had to round up the cattle before the storm arrived.
