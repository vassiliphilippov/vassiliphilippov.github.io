ferocity

noun
wild or savage intensity
6.8 1.79/m
Pronunciation: / f…ôÀàr…ëÀês…ôti / (AmE), / f…ôÀàr…ís…ôti / (BrE)
the quality of being fierce, wild, or savagely intense

Examples

Wild ferocity
He spoke with such ferocity that everyone in the room fell silent.
The ferocity of the lion's attack shocked the onlookers.
The ferocity of the storm caused widespread damage throughout the city.

Synonyms

  • ferociousness
    7.0 0.03/m
    ferociousness shows extreme, often cruel violence in action, while ferocity is wild, fierce intensity without moral judgement.
  • fierceness
    3.5 0.60/m
    fierceness shows strong, energetic force in action, while ferocity suggests wilder, more savage and uncontrolled extreme intensity.
  • savagery
    7.4 1.47/m
    savagery is cruel, untamed violence and harsh acts, while ferocity is fierce, intense behaviour without focus on cruelty.
  • viciousness
    6.3 0.58/m
    viciousness is deliberate cruelty or spite in actions, while ferocity is raw wild intensity that may lack planned malice.
  • brutality
    5.6 8.05/m
    brutality is harsh, often physical cruelty or harm, while ferocity is fierce, untamed intensity rather than planned cruelty.
  • violence
    4.3 2.93/m
    violence means physical force or harm in many settings, while ferocity is fierce, wild intensity often seen in animals or storms.
