served with chips (French fries) with every meal
Pronunciation: / tʃɪps wɪð ˈɛvriˌθɪŋ / (AmE), / tʃɪps wɪð ˈɛvriˌθɪŋ / (BrE)
used to describe the widespread practice, especially in Britain, of serving chips (French fries) with almost any meal regardless of suitability

Examples

Chips with everything meal
At the seaside, the menu offered chips with everything, from fish to curry.
Some tourists are amused by the tradition of serving chips with everything in British restaurants.
When traveling in the UK, you might find chips with everything at many pubs and cafes.
