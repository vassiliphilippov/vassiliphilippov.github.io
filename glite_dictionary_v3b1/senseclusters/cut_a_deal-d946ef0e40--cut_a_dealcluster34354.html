cut a deal

verb
negotiate an agreement
3.5 2.39/m
Pronunciation: / k åt …ô dil / (AmE), / k åt …ô diÀêl / (BrE)
to negotiate and reach an agreement, especially in business or politics

Examples

To cut a deal quickly
After hours of negotiation, both companies finally cut a deal that satisfied everyone involved.
She hoped to cut a deal with her landlord to lower the rent for next year.
The politician promised voters that he would not cut a deal with special interests.

Synonyms

  • strike a deal
    3.4 0.82/m
    strike a deal means reaching an agreement after negotiation, while cut a deal often sounds more informal and practical in business.
  • clinch
    5.1 1.85/m
    clinch means to secure a result decisively, while cut a deal focuses on negotiating and making an agreement.
  • seal the deal
    4.0 0.73/m
    seal the deal shows final confirmation of terms, while cut a deal describes the act of negotiating and reaching that agreement.
  • land a deal
    3.4 0.01/m
    land a deal means successfully secure a contract or business win, while cut a deal is about negotiating the agreement.
