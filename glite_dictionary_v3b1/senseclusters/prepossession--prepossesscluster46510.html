prepossession

noun
an opinion formed ahead of experience
5.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / priˌpoʊˈzɛʃən /; / ˌprɛpəˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / priːpəˈzɛʃən /; / ˌpriːpəˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
a preconceived opinion or feeling about someone or something, often formed before having enough evidence (prejudice or bias)

Examples

A prepossession in favor
Despite her prepossession in favor of the new manager, she tried to remain objective.
His prepossession against modern art made it difficult for him to appreciate the exhibition.
It is important to avoid prepossession when making a fair judgment.
He spoke without prepossession, aiming to be as objective as possible.
Her prepossession in favor of the new proposal was clear during the meeting.
The judge must be free from any prepossession to ensure a fair trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - unfair inclination or preference for or against something
  • antonym: impartiality - fairness, absence of bias or prepossession
  • synonym: prejudice - preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
