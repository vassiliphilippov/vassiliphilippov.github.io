assemblage

noun
group of people gathered together
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛmblɪdʒ /; / əˈsɛm.blɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈsɛmblɪdʒ /; / əˈsɛm.blɪdʒ / (BrE)
a group of people gathered together, or the act of gathering people for a purpose

Examples

Assemblage of people
A remarkable assemblage of scientists attended the international conference on climate change.
An assemblage of protesters gathered outside the courthouse to demand justice.
The curator explained the significance of each assemblage in the exhibition hall.
An assemblage of scientists convened to discuss climate change.
Freedom of assemblage is protected under the constitution in many countries.
The town square filled with an assemblage chanting for reform.
