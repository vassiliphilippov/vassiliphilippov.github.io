tetradrachm

noun
ancient Greek coin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtrəˌdrækm / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəˌdrækm / (BrE)
an ancient Greek silver coin valued at four drachmas, widely used in Greek and Hellenistic times

Examples

An ancient tetradrachm coin
The collector was thrilled to find an Athenian tetradrachm at the auction.
A silver tetradrachm from the Hellenistic period was displayed in the museum's ancient coins section.
Historians study the images on each tetradrachm to learn about ancient Greek culture.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: stater - an ancient Greek gold or silver coin, often of higher value than a tetradrachm
