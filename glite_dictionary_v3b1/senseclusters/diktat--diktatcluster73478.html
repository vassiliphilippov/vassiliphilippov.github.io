diktat

noun
authoritative order
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdɪk.tæt/ /; / /dɪk.ˈtɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈdɪk.tæt/ /; / /dɪk.ˈtɑːt/ (BrE)
an order or command imposed by someone in authority, often without agreement, consent, or discussion

Examples

A diktat from leaders
The workers rejected the management's diktat and demanded a fair negotiation.
Many citizens viewed the new law as a government diktat rather than a democratic decision.
The company issued a diktat requiring all employees to work overtime without prior discussion.
The workers protested against the management's diktat to reduce their lunch break.
Many citizens felt the new law was a diktat rather than a democratic decision.
The company issued a diktat requiring all employees to work overtime during the holidays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decree - an official order issued by a legal authority
  • synonym: edict - an official proclamation or command issued by an authority
  • synonym: mandate - an official command or instruction
  • antonym: suggestion - an idea or plan put forward for consideration
