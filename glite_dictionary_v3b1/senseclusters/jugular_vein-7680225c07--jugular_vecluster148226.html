jugular vein

noun
major vein in the neck
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌɡjələr veɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡjʊlə veɪn / (BrE)
any of several large veins in the neck that return blood from the head to the heart

Examples

The jugular vein in neck
He placed pressure on the jugular vein to stop the bleeding.
Swelling in the jugular vein can be a sign of heart problems.
The surgeon carefully avoided the jugular vein during the operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carotid artery - major artery in the neck supplying blood to the head
