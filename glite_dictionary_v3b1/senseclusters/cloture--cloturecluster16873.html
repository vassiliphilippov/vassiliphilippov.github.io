cloture

noun
procedure to end debate in legislature
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊ.tʃər / (AmE), / ˈkləʊ.tʃə / (BrE)
a procedure by which debate is formally ended or limited in a legislative assembly, enabling a final vote to be taken

Examples

A cloture motion
A motion for cloture requires a specific number of votes to pass.
After hours of debate, cloture was used to bring the discussion to a close.
The Senate invoked cloture to end the filibuster and proceed to a vote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: closure - the act of closing a debate in parliament
