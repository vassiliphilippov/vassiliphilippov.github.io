acute

adjective
extremely serious or urgent
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt / (BrE)
extremely severe or intense (of a feeling, situation, or problem)

Examples

Acute pain
The hospital is facing an acute shortage of medical supplies this month.
The refugee crisis has reached an acute stage, requiring immediate international response.
There is an acute need for affordable housing in many urban areas.
Her acute embarrassment was obvious to everyone in the room.
The patient was suffering from acute pain after the surgery.
They responded quickly to the acute housing crisis in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critical - extremely important or severe
  • synonym: intense - of extreme force or strength
  • antonym: mild - not severe or strong
  • antonym: minor - of lesser or little importance
  • synonym: serious - of great consequence or gravity
