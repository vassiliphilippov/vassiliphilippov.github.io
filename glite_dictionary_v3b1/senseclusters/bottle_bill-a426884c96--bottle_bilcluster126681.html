bottle bill

noun
law requiring recycling of bottles/cans
3.7 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːt.əl bɪl/ (AmE), /ˈbɒt.əl bɪl/ (BrE)
a law that requires retailers or distributors to collect and recycle used beverage containers, usually by offering a refund deposit to the consumer

Examples

A bottle bill law
Many states have enacted a bottle bill to encourage recycling and reduce litter.
Opponents argue that a bottle bill would increase costs for both consumers and businesses.
The proposed bottle bill would require customers to pay a deposit on every beverage container.
