Sikhism

noun
a religion founded by Guru Nanak
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.kɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.kɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A monotheistic religion founded in northern India in the 16th century by Guru Nanak, combining elements of Hinduism and Islam.

Examples

Sikhism religion
Sikhism teaches the belief in one God and the equality of all humans.
Many people in Punjab, India, practice Sikhism as their main religion.
The principles of Sikhism emphasize service to others and devotion to God.
