meningism

noun
symptoms mimicking meningitis, without actual inflammation
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnɪŋˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmiːnɪŋˌɪzəm / (BrE)
A set of symptoms resembling those of meningitis, such as neck stiffness and headache, but occurring without inflammation of the meninges.

Examples

Meningism symptoms
Doctors observed signs of meningism in several cases during the outbreak.
Neck stiffness due to meningism often causes concern for infection, but laboratory results can help clarify.
The patient presented with meningism, but tests ruled out actual meningitis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meningitis - inflammation of the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord
