reformist

noun
person who advocates reform
3.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrmɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates reform, especially in political or social systems

Examples

A leading reformist
As a known reformist, she often clashed with conservative members of the board.
During the election, the reformist candidate promised sweeping changes to the tax system.
The new government appointed a prominent reformist as Minister of Education.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - a person who is averse to change and holds traditional values
  • degree lower: moderate - a person who holds moderate views, especially in politics
  • degree higher: radical - a person who advocates thorough or complete political or social reform
