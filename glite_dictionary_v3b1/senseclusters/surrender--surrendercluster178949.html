surrender

verb
relinquish control or possession
1.9
Pronunciation: / səˈrɛn.dɚ /; / səˈrɛndər / (AmE), / səˈrɛn.də /; / səˈrɛndə / (BrE)
to give up control, possession, or oneself to another person or authority, often because of force or demand

Examples

To surrender control
After a long negotiation, the company agreed to surrender its remaining assets to creditors.
Facing overwhelming evidence, he finally decided to surrender control of the organization.
The army refused to surrender the strategic fortress despite repeated demands.
He refused to surrender his passport to the authorities.
She finally surrendered the documents when confronted by the police.
The army decided to surrender after days of fighting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily cease to keep or claim; give up
  • antonym: resist - to refuse to give in to an opposing force or authority
  • antonym: retain - to keep possession of
  • synonym: yield - to give way to demands or pressure; relinquish
