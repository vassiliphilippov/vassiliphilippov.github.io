mop up

verb
clean or wipe with a mop
2.6 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / mÉ‘p ÊŒp / (AmE), / mÉ’p ÊŒp / (BrE)
to clean or wipe a surface using a mop or something similar, especially to remove liquid or dirt

Examples

To mop up a spill
After the juice spill, he had to mop up the kitchen floor immediately.
She quickly mopped up the water with an old towel before it spread further.
The janitor mops up the hallways every evening after school.

Synonyms

  • mop
    1.0 1.15/m
    mop is a cleaning tool with a long handle used to wash floors, while mop up means using that tool to clean spills.
  • wipe up
    1.8 0.41/m
    wipe up is to rub a spill away with a cloth or towel, while mop up means cleaning liquid with a mop or similar.
  • sop up
    3.3 0.16/m
    sop up soaks or absorbs liquid with a sponge or cloth, while mop up usually removes spills by wiping or mopping.
  • wipe
    1.0 30/m
    wipe means rubbing a surface to clean or dry it, while mop up implies using a mop or similar tool to remove liquid.
