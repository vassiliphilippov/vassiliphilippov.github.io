feeding frenzy

noun
chaotic competition or rush for something
8.7 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈfiː.dɪŋ ˈfren.zɪ/ (AmE), /ˈfiː.dɪŋ ˈfren.zɪ/ (BrE)
a situation in which a group of people compete intensely and chaotically for something, often in a frantic or uncontrollable way

Examples

A feeding frenzy at box office
The announcement of free tickets sparked a feeding frenzy at the box office.
The politician's scandal triggered a media feeding frenzy that lasted for weeks.
Whenever a new product launches, there's often a feeding frenzy among eager buyers.
