sucker

noun
flesh of certain North American food fish
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌkər /; / ˈsʌkɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌkə / (BrE)
a North American freshwater fish with thick lips adapted for suction feeding, or its flesh used as food

Examples

A sucker fish
Fishermen in the Great Lakes region often catch sucker for food.
She prepared a stew using fresh sucker caught that morning.
The restaurant offered fried sucker as a specialty on its menu.
Suckers are commonly found in streams across the northern United States and Canada.
The sucker uses its thick-lipped mouth to feed on algae at the bottom of lakes.
The angler proudly displayed the large sucker he caught in the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carp - a large freshwater fish of the family Cyprinidae
  • synonym: catfish - any of various mostly freshwater fish with whisker-like barbels
