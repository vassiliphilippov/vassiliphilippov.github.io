hornlessness

noun
absence of horns
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrn.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈhɔːn.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
the state or condition of being without horns, especially in animals that typically have them

Examples

Hornlessness in cattle
Selective breeding has increased hornlessness in some cattle breeds.
The hornlessness of the sheep made them easier to handle on the farm.
Scientists studied the genetic basis of hornlessness in goats to improve animal welfare.

Related Terms

  • antonym: horned - having horns
  • synonym: polled - naturally without horns
