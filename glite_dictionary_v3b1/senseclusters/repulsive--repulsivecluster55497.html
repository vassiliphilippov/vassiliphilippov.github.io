repulsive

adjective
having the power to repel
5.7 4.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈpʌl.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈpʌl.sɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈpʌl.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈpʌl.sɪv/ (BrE)
(of a force or object) having the power or tendency to push away or drive apart something else.

Examples

Repulsive force
Electrons carry a negative charge and produce a repulsive electrical field around them.
Scientists studied the repulsive interactions between particles in the experiment.
The north poles of two magnets exert a repulsive force on each other.
