phytopathogen

noun
plant disease organism
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪtoʊˈpæθədʒən / (AmE), / ˌfaɪtəʊˈpæθədʒən / (BrE)
an organism, such as a fungus, bacterium, or virus, that causes disease in plants

Examples

A phytopathogen fungus
The scientist studied a phytopathogen responsible for damaging tomato crops.
Farmers are concerned about the spread of a new phytopathogen in their fields.
Effective control of a phytopathogen can help prevent large-scale crop losses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parasite - an organism that lives on or in a host and causes harm
  • synonym: pathogen - an organism that causes disease
