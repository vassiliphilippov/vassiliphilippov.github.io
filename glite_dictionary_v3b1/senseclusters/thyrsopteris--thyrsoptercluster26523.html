thyrsopteris

noun
genus of South American tree fern
Pronunciation: / θɜːrˈsɑptərɪs / (AmE), / θɜːˈsɒptərɪs / (BrE)
A genus of terrestrial tree ferns native to South America, known for their large fronds.

Examples

A thyrsopteris fern
A mature thyrsopteris can reach impressive heights for a fern.
Botanists studied the unique structure of the thyrsopteris fronds.
The thyrsopteris is often found in the moist forests of southern Chile.
