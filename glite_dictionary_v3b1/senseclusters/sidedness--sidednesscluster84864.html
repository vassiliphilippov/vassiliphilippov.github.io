sidedness

noun
preference for one side
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the tendency to prefer or use one side of the body more than the other, especially in movement or function

Examples

Hand sidedness
The teacher noticed the child's sidedness when he always kicked the ball with his right foot.
Scientists study sidedness to understand why most people are right-handed.
Physical therapists assess sidedness to design better rehabilitation programs for stroke patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ambidexterity - the ability to use both hands equally well
  • synonym: laterality - preference for one side of the body over the other
