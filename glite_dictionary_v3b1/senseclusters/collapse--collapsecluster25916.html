Examples

To collapse suddenly
During the marathon, one of the runners collapsed from heat exhaustion.
He suddenly collapsed at work and was rushed to the hospital.
She collapsed on the floor after days of battling the flu.
After the earthquake, several buildings collapsed within minutes.
Talks between the two countries collapsed after weeks of negotiation.
The bridge was old and began to collapse under the heavy load.

His confidence collapsed after a series of defeats.
The old bridge is expected to collapse if another heavy truck crosses it.

Synonyms

  • give way
    4.2 4.06/m
    give way yields or breaks under strong pressure or force, while collapse means to fall down or fail suddenly.
  • fall in
    4.4 1.20/m
    fall in describes a structure folding or falling inward, while collapse means to fall down or fail suddenly in many situations.
  • fall apart
    2.8 6.48/m
    fall apart breaks into pieces or stops working, often over time, while collapse usually means a sudden fall or failure.
  • crumple
    3.9 3.05/m
    crumple folds or falls down quickly, often from force or weakness, while collapse is a general sudden breaking or failing.
  • crumble
    3.4 15/m
    crumble breaks into small pieces or weakens slowly, while collapse implies a sudden fall or quick failure.
