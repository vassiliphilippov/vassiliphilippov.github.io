collapse

verb
fall down due to illness or exhaustion
2.5 3.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæps / (AmE), / kəˈlæps / (BrE)
to fall down, break apart, or fail suddenly, either physically (like a building or person) or metaphorically (like a business or system)

Examples

To collapse suddenly
During the marathon, one of the runners collapsed from heat exhaustion.
He suddenly collapsed at work and was rushed to the hospital.
She collapsed on the floor after days of battling the flu.
After the earthquake, several buildings collapsed within minutes.
Talks between the two countries collapsed after weeks of negotiation.
The bridge was old and began to collapse under the heavy load.
After the long meeting, she nearly collapsed from exhaustion.
His confidence collapsed after a series of defeats.
The old bridge is expected to collapse if another heavy truck crosses it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break down - to lose control of one's emotions and become unable to function
  • synonym: crash - to fail suddenly and completely
  • synonym: crumble - to fall apart into small fragments, especially gradually
  • synonym: disintegrate - to break into small parts or pieces
  • synonym: fail - to not succeed; to stop functioning or being effective
  • synonym: faint - to suddenly lose consciousness, typically due to a lack of blood to the brain
  • synonym: fall apart - to disintegrate or break into pieces
  • antonym: thrive - to prosper or be successful; to do well
  • antonym: withstand - to remain undamaged or unaffected by; to resist
