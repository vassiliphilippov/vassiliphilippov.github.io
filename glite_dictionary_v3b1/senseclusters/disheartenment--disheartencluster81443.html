disheartenment

noun
loss of confidence or hope
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈhɑrtənmənt / (AmE), / dɪsˈhɑːtənmənt / (BrE)
the feeling or state of losing confidence, hope, or determination; discouragement

Examples

Feeling of disheartenment
Despite initial disheartenment, the team continued to work hard toward their goal.
She tried to hide her disheartenment after the project was rejected.
The news of delayed funding was a source of great disheartenment among the researchers.
After months of job searching without success, a deep disheartenment settled over him.
She fought against disheartenment by focusing on the small wins each day.
The team's repeated failures led to a sense of disheartenment among its members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discouragement - the state of being discouraged or losing motivation
  • antonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support, confidence, or hope
