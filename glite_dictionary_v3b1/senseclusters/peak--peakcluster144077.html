peak

noun
highest point; mountain top
2.1 41/m
Pronunciation: / pik / (AmE), / piÀêk / (BrE)
the highest or pointed top of something, or the greatest level or point that something reaches (such as a mountain, achievement, or amount)

Examples

Mountain peak
From the hotel, you can see the snow-capped peak in the distance.
The flag was placed at the peak to mark the team's achievement.
We finally reached the peak of the mountain after hours of climbing.
At their peak, the city's population was twice what it is today.
She reached the peak of her career when she became CEO of the company.
The demand for electricity usually hits its peak during summer heatwaves.
Electricity usage usually reaches its peak in the evening.
From the peak of the mountain, we could see the entire valley below.
Her career was at its peak when she won the award.
