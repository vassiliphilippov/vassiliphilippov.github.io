arco

adjective
play with the bow (music direction)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɑːkəʊ / (BrE)
(in music) indicating that a stringed instrument (such as violin, viola, cello, or double bass) should be played with the bow rather than by plucking the strings.

Examples

To play arco style
After the pizzicato passage, the musicians switched to arco for a smooth, lyrical melody.
On the score, 'arco' instructed violinists to resume using their bows.
The conductor signaled the cellists to play arco rather than continue plucking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pizzicato - in music, plucking the strings of a violin or other stringed instrument
