Pacific spiny dogfish

noun
small Pacific coast dogfish shark
Pronunciation: / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈspaɪni ˈdɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈspaɪni ˈdɒɡˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small shark of the dogfish family found along the Pacific coast of North America, known for its spiny dorsal fins.

Examples

The Pacific spiny dogfish shark
Fishermen often encounter Pacific spiny dogfish while trawling for other species.
Researchers tracked the migration paths of the Pacific spiny dogfish along the western coastline.
The Pacific spiny dogfish is recognized by the two spines on its back and its slender body.

Related Terms
