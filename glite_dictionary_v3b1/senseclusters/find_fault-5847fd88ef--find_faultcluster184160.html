find fault

verb
criticize or complain about faults
Pronunciation: / faɪnd fɔlt / (AmE), / faɪnd fɔːlt / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something, often consistently or unfairly; to look for and point out mistakes or shortcomings

Examples

To find fault with ideas
Instead of helping, he just found fault with my suggestions during the meeting.
It's exhausting to work with someone who constantly finds fault in every little detail.
She always finds fault with everything I do, no matter how hard I try.
