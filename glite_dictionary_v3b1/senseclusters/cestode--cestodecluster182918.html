cestode

noun
parasitic flatworm (tapeworm)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛsˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈsɛstəʊd / (BrE)
A parasitic flatworm of the class Cestoda, commonly known as a tapeworm, that lives in the intestines of humans and other vertebrates.

Examples

A cestode parasite
After traveling abroad, he was diagnosed with an infection caused by a cestode.
The cestode is a common parasite found in the digestive tracts of mammals.
Veterinarians regularly check animals for signs of cestode infestation.
