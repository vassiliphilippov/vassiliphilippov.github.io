fluidity

noun
ability of a substance to flow easily
3.2
Pronunciation: / fluˈɪdəti / (AmE), / fluˈɪdəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to flow easily, like a liquid; the ability of a substance to flow smoothly

Examples

Fluidity of water
Engineers measured the fluidity of the molten metal before casting the parts.
Rising temperatures increase the fluidity of ice, causing it to melt more quickly.
The fluidity of the new paint allows it to spread smoothly across the canvas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: viscosity - the thickness or resistance to flow of a liquid
