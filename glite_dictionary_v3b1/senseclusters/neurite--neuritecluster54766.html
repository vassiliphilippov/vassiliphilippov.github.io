neurite

noun
nerve cell projection
Pronunciation: / ˈnʊraɪt / (AmE), / ˈnjʊəraɪt / (BrE)
a projection from a nerve cell, such as an axon or dendrite, that conducts nerve impulses

Examples

Neurite growth
Scientists observed the growth of neurites in the developing brain tissue under the microscope.
Damage to neurites can disrupt communication between nerve cells and affect brain function.
Certain proteins help guide neurite extension during the formation of neural networks.
The scientist observed the growth of a neurite under the microscope.
Damage to a neurite can affect how nerve signals are transmitted in the brain.
During development, each neurite extends to connect with other nerve cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axon - a long projection from a neuron that carries impulses away from the cell body
  • synonym: dendrite - a branched projection from a neuron that receives signals from other cells
