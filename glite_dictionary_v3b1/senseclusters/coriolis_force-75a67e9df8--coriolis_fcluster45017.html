Coriolis force

noun
apparent force due to Earth's rotation
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːriˈoʊlɪs fɔːrs / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈəʊlɪs fɔːs / (BrE)
an apparent force that acts on a moving object within a rotating system, arising from the rotation, such as the deflection of winds and currents on the rotating Earth

Examples

The Coriolis force effect
Ocean currents are significantly influenced by the Coriolis force resulting from the Earth's rotation.
Pilots must account for the Coriolis force when planning long-distance flights.
The Coriolis force causes moving air to curve rather than travel in a straight line across the Earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centrifugal force - an apparent force that acts outward on a body moving around a center, arising from the body's inertia
