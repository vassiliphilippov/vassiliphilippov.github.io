plant cell

noun
basic unit of structure in plants
Pronunciation: / plænt sɛl / (AmE), / plɑːnt sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that is a basic structural, functional, and biological unit of a plant, typically distinguished by the presence of a cell wall, chloroplasts, and a large vacuole.

Examples

A plant cell structure
Scientists examined the plant cell under a microscope to study its vacuole size.
The plant cell contains chloroplasts, which enable it to carry out photosynthesis.
Unlike an animal cell, a plant cell has a rigid cell wall for structural support.

Related Terms

  • antonym: animal cell - a cell that is a structural and functional unit of an animal
