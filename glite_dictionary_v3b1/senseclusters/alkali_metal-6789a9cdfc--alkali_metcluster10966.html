alkali metal

noun
a Group I reactive metal element
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.kə.laɪ ˈmɛ.t̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪ ˈmɛ.tl̩ / (BrE)
any of the soft, highly reactive, monovalent metals in Group I of the periodic table, such as lithium, sodium, or potassium.

Examples

An alkali metal element
Lithium is an alkali metal that is used in batteries and mood-stabilizing medications.
Potassium, an alkali metal, is essential for proper cell function in the human body.
When placed in water, an alkali metal reacts vigorously, releasing hydrogen gas.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: alkaline earth metal - any of the divalent metals of Group II of the periodic table, like calcium or magnesium
