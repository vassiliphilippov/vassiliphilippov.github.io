monoline

noun
single-product company
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a company, especially in finance or insurance, that specializes in a single type of product or service

Examples

Monoline insurer
The monoline focused exclusively on insuring municipal bonds.
After the financial crisis, many monoline insurers faced significant losses.
A monoline may be more vulnerable to market changes than a diversified company.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multiline - a company offering multiple types of products or services
  • synonym: specialist - a person or company focusing on a specific area
