heat

noun
the sensation of warmth
1.0 41/m
Pronunciation: / hiÀêt /; / hit / (AmE), / hiÀêt / (BrE)
the state or sensation of being hot or warm; high temperature

Examples

Summer heat
After running, he was overwhelmed by the heat radiating from his body.
She could feel the heat of the fire on her face.
The intense heat from the sun made it hard to stay outside for long.
During the summer, the heat in the city becomes almost unbearable.
He could feel the heat radiating from the campfire.
The intense heat of the afternoon made it hard to work outside.

Synonyms

  • hotness
    1.4 0.03/m
    hotness is the quality of being very hot, while heat names the general state or sensation of being warm or at high temperature.
  • high temperature
    2.8 1.59/m
    high temperature describes a clearly raised degree of warmth, while heat covers the general feeling or presence of hotness.
  • warmth
    1.0 9.07/m
    warmth is a gentle, comfortable heat felt on skin or in a room, while heat can mean stronger high temperature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cold - the sensation caused by low temperature
  • synonym: warmth - the state or sensation of being warm
