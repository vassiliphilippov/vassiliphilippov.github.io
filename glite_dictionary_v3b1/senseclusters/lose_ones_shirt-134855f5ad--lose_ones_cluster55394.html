lose one's shirt

verb
lose all one's money
Pronunciation: / luːz wʌnz ʃɝːt /; / luːz wʌnz ʃɜrt / (AmE), / luːz wʌnz ʃɜːt / (BrE)
to lose all or most of your money, especially because of a bad investment or risky decision

Examples

To lose his shirt
He lost his shirt when his business collapsed.
If you invest recklessly in the stock market, you might lose your shirt.
Many gamblers lose their shirts after a night at the casino.
He invested everything in the company and lost his shirt when it went bankrupt.
Many people lose their shirts at the casino hoping for big wins.
She almost lost her shirt on that risky property deal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: go bankrupt - to become unable to pay debts; insolvency
  • synonym: go broke - to lose all one's money or become bankrupt
  • antonym: make a killing - to make a large amount of money quickly
