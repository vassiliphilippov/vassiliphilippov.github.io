dissolution

noun
process of dissolving into solution
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
The process by which a substance dissolves and forms a solution, especially in a liquid.

Examples

Salt dissolution
Chemical dissolution is an important step in many laboratory experiments.
Proper stirring can speed up the dissolution of tablets in water.
The dissolution of sugar in hot tea happens very quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: precipitation - the process by which a dissolved substance separates from solution as a solid
  • synonym: solution - a homogeneous mixture formed when a substance dissolves in another
