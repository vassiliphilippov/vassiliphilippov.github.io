carbonyl

noun
C=O chemical group
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbəˌnɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbənɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group consisting of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom, found in many organic compounds

Examples

Carbonyl group
The carbonyl group is essential in the structure of many organic molecules.
Chemists often study reactions involving the carbonyl functional group.
Aldehydes and ketones both contain a carbonyl group in their molecular structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aldehyde - an organic compound containing a carbonyl group bonded to at least one hydrogen atom
  • synonym: ketone - an organic compound with a carbonyl group bonded to two carbon atoms
