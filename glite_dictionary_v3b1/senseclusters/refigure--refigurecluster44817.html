refigure

verb
to reinterpret or adapt
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɪɡjər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɪɡjə / (BrE)
to change the form, representation, or interpretation of something, often by adapting it for a new context

Examples

To refigure a painting
The artist decided to refigure the classic painting with modern elements.
Historians often refigure past events to reflect new evidence or perspectives.
The novel refigures traditional myths in a contemporary setting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to change something to suit a new purpose or situation
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original form or state
  • synonym: reinterpret - to explain or present something in a new or different way
