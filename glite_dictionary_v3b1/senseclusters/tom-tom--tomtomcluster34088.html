tom-tom

noun
cylindrical drum without snares
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑm tɑm / (AmE), / ˈtɒm tɒm / (BrE)
A drum with a cylindrical body and no snares, often used in jazz or popular music as part of a drum kit or in traditional ceremonies.

Examples

A tom-tom drum
He replaced the old tom-tom on his drum set with a new one.
The drummer played a solo on the tom-tom during the concert.
Traditional ceremonies often featured rhythms created on a large tom-tom.

  • synonym: drum - A percussion instrument sounded by being struck.
