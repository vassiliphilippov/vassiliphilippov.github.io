kurtosis

noun
statistical peakedness
Pronunciation: / kɜrˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / kɜːˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a statistical measure that describes the sharpness or flatness of the peak and tails of a data distribution

Examples

Data kurtosis value
The kurtosis value helped the analyst understand the distribution's extreme values.
A high kurtosis indicates that the data have heavy tails or outliers.
Researchers compared the kurtosis of several datasets to assess their statistical properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skewness - a statistical measure of the asymmetry of a data distribution
