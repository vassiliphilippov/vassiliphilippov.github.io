high-pass filter

noun
filter passing higher frequencies
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ pæs ˈfɪltər / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ pɑːs ˈfɪltə / (BrE)
An electronic filter that allows signals with a frequency higher than a certain cutoff value to pass through while attenuating lower-frequency signals.

Examples

A high-pass filter setting
Adjusting the high-pass filter settings can clarify vocals by eliminating rumbling sounds.
Many loudspeakers incorporate a high-pass filter to protect them from bass frequencies.
The audio engineer used a high-pass filter to remove unwanted low-frequency noise from the recording.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-pass filter - a filter that passes signals below a certain frequency and attenuates higher frequencies
