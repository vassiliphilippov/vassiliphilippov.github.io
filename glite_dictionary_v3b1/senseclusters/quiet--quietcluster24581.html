quiet

adjective
of the sun: having little surface activity
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪət /; / ˈkwaɪ.ət / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət /; / ˈkwaɪ.ət / (BrE)
not making much noise; calm, still, or peaceful; soft or gentle in sound or manner

Examples

A quiet room
During a quiet phase, the sun displays few or no sunspots visible from Earth.
Scientists monitor changes in solar radiation during both quiet and active periods of the sun.
The last solar minimum was marked by an unusually quiet sun with prolonged inactivity.
After moving to the countryside, they enjoyed a much quiet lifestyle.
During the storm, the usually quiet town became tense and watchful.
The library was unusually quiet despite the number of people inside.
After the storm passed, the lake became quiet and still.
Fishermen prefer to cast their nets early in the morning when the water is quiet.
We anchored in the quiet bay, enjoying the gentle movement of the boat.
