slow

adjective
not active or brisk (business)
Pronunciation: / sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
(of business or economic activity) not active, busy, or brisk; showing less than usual movement or progress.

Examples

Slow sales
Real estate sales have been slow since the economic downturn began last year.
The holiday season was particularly slow for local retailers, with fewer customers than usual.
The restaurant experienced a slow afternoon, serving only a handful of customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brisk - active and energetic (especially in business)
  • synonym: dull - lacking interest or excitement; lacking activity (especially business)
  • synonym: sluggish - moving, reacting, or working more slowly than normal; lacking energy or alertness
