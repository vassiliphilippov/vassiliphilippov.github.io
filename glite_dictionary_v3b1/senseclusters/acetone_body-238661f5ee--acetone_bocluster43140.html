acetone body

noun
ketone compound formed during fat metabolism
6.0 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈæs.ə.toʊn ˈbɑː.dɪ/ /; / /ˈæs.ɪ.toʊn ˈbɑː.dɪ/ (AmE), /ˈæs.ə.təʊn ˈbɒ.dɪ/ /; / /ˈæs.ɪ.təʊn ˈbɒ.dɪ/ (BrE)
any of several ketone compounds (acetoacetic acid, acetone, and beta-hydroxybutyric acid) formed during fat metabolism and found in excess in the blood and urine of people with certain metabolic disorders, such as diabetes mellitus

Examples

The acetone body level
During starvation, the body produces more acetone bodies as it breaks down fat for energy.
Laboratory tests revealed the presence of acetone bodies in the patient's urine sample.
The doctor explained that high levels of acetone bodies might indicate uncontrolled diabetes.
