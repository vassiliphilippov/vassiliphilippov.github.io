utilitarianism

noun
theory that value lies in usefulness or utility
Pronunciation: / juːˌtɪlɪˈtɛriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / juːˌtɪlɪˈteəriəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine that actions are right if they are useful or benefit the majority, especially as the basis for moral or political decisions

Examples

Utilitarianism principle
Debates about animal rights often invoke principles of utilitarianism.
John Stuart Mill was a leading proponent of utilitarianism in the nineteenth century.
The company adopted a philosophy of utilitarianism, focusing on projects that benefited the most people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consequentialism - the ethical theory that the outcomes of actions determine their morality
  • antonym: deontology - the ethical theory that actions are right or wrong in themselves, regardless of consequences
