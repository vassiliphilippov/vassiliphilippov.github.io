enter

verb
register formally as participant or member
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈen.tə /; / ˈɛn.tə / (BrE)
to register for or become a participant in an event, group, or activity; to start being involved in something

Examples

To enter a competition
Hundreds of young athletes enter the tournament each year hoping to win a medal.
She decided to enter university after taking a gap year.
You must enter the competition before the deadline to be considered.
After years of training, she decided to enter the national marathon.
He chose to enter a rehabilitation program to overcome his addiction.
Several companies have agreed to enter negotiations to resolve their differences.
After years in finance, she decided to enter the world of education.
He plans to enter the competition next spring to test his skills.
Many young scientists enter research to make a difference in their field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embark - to begin a new project or activity, especially one that is significant or challenging
  • synonym: enroll - to officially register as a member or participant
  • synonym: join - to become a member or participant in
  • antonym: leave - to depart from a field or activity; to cease participation
  • antonym: withdraw - to leave or cease to participate in something
