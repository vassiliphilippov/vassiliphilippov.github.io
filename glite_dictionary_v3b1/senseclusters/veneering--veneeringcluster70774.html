veneering

noun
thin decorative wooden layer
8.5
Pronunciation: / vəˈnɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / vəˈnɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
a thin layer of high-quality wood glued onto a base of cheaper wood, used as a decorative surface in furniture and paneling

Examples

Wood veneering
Many antique tables show signs of aged veneering on their surfaces.
Modern furniture uses veneering to achieve a luxurious appearance without the high cost.
The cabinet featured a walnut veneering that made it look far more expensive than it was.
