spoil

verb
become unfit for use; go bad
1.6 1/m
Pronunciation: / spÉ”Éªl / (AmE), / spÉ”Éªl / (BrE)
(of food or other perishable items) to become unfit for consumption or use, especially as a result of decomposition or decay.

Examples

Milk spoils fast
Fish spoils faster than most other meats, so it should be cooked the same day.
If not refrigerated, milk will quickly spoil in warm weather.
Leftover food can spoil after a few days without proper storage.

Synonyms

  • go bad
    2.1 0.9/m
    go bad is a casual phrase for food becoming unsafe to eat, while spoil is the general term for perishable items.
  • decay
    4.4 3/m
    decay means materials break down by natural processes over time, while spoil refers specifically to food or perishable goods going bad.
  • rot away
    4.2 0.5/m
    rot away describes slow physical breakdown and loss of structure, while spoil focuses on food becoming unsafe or unusable.
  • deteriorate
    5.9 12/m
    deteriorate means quality or condition fall over time in many contexts, while spoil specifically means food becomes rotten or unsafe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - to decompose, usually as a result of natural processes
  • antonym: preserve - to keep (food) from spoiling
  • synonym: rot - to decay and break down due to bacteria or fungi
