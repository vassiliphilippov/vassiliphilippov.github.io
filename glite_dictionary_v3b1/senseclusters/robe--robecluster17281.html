robe

noun
long formal ceremonial garment
Pronunciation: / roʊb / (AmE), / rəʊb / (BrE)
a long, loose outer garment, often worn on special, official, or ceremonial occasions

Examples

A graduation robe
Graduates proudly put on their caps and robes for the commencement ceremony.
In medieval portraits, kings and queens are often shown in richly decorated robes.
The judge entered the courtroom wearing a black robe and took his seat.
He entered the room dressed in a flowing ceremonial robe.
Judges traditionally wear a black robe in court.
She wore a silk robe when she stepped out of the shower.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloak - a loose outer garment, typically sleeveless, that hangs from the shoulders
  • synonym: gown - a long formal dress or garment, sometimes used for ceremonies
