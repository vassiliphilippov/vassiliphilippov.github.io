discant

noun
musical accompaniment above a melody
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪs.kænt / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.kənt / (BrE)
a decorative musical accompaniment, often improvised, added above a basic melody in music; especially in medieval and Renaissance polyphony.

Examples

The discant melody
In medieval music, the discant part often showcased the composer's creativity.
She improvised a lively discant that soared above the main melody.
The choir added a beautiful discant to the plainchant, enriching the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descant - an independent treble melody sung or played above a basic melody
