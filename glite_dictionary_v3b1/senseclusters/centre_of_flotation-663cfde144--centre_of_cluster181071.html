centre of flotation

noun
center point of buoyancy on a floating object
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntər əv floʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛntə əv fləʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the point at which the buoyant force acts on a floating object; the center of gravity of the displaced water.

Examples

The centre of flotation point
A vessel's balance depends heavily on the location of its centre of flotation.
Engineers calculated the centre of flotation to optimize the ship's stability.
The centre of flotation moves as the ship's displacement changes.
