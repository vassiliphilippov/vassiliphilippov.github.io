acetamide

noun
chemical compound; amide of acetic acid
9.3
Pronunciation: / əˈsitəˌmaɪd, ˌæsɪˈtæmɪd / (AmE), / əˈsiːtəˌmʌɪd, ˌasɪˈtamɪd / (BrE)
A colorless, crystalline solid amide derived from acetic acid, used as a solvent and in the synthesis of organic compounds.
