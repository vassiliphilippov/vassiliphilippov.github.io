agnostic

adjective
relating to agnosticism or an agnostic
8.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /æɡ.ˈnɑː.stɪk/ /; / /əɡ.ˈnɑː.stɪk/ (AmE), /æɡ.ˈnɒs.tɪk/ /; / /əɡ.ˈnɒs.tɪk/ (BrE)
not having a definite belief about whether something (especially God or ultimate reality) exists or can be known; skeptical or noncommittal about claims to knowledge

Examples

Agnostic view on God
His agnostic beliefs prevent him from committing to any particular religion.
She described herself as agnostic on the issue, neither agreeing nor disagreeing.
The discussion took an agnostic approach to the question of life's meaning.
As a scientist, she remained agnostic about the ultimate explanation for the phenomenon.
He took an agnostic position on the debate, refusing to support either side without more evidence.
The committee was agnostic regarding the proposed policy changes until further information became available.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atheist - person who disbelieves or lacks belief in the existence of God or gods
  • antonym: dogmatic - inclined to lay down principles as undeniably true
  • synonym: skeptical - having doubts or reservations; denying certainty
