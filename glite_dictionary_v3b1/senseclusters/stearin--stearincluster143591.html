stearin

noun
a fat used in candles and soaps
Pronunciation: / ˈstiːərɪn / (AmE), / ˈstɪərɪn / (BrE)
A white, odorless, tasteless fat that is the glyceride of stearic acid, used in the manufacture of candles, soaps, and cosmetics.

Examples

High stearin content
A white, waxy appearance is a sign of high stearin content in the product.
Candles made from stearin burn with a steady, bright flame.
The soap recipe called for both stearin and coconut oil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glycerol - a simple polyol compound often used in food and medicine
  • synonym: stearic acid - a saturated fatty acid found in animal and vegetable fats
