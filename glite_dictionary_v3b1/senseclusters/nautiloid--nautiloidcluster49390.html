nautiloid

noun
ancient shelled mollusk
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔtɪˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːtɪlɔɪd / (BrE)
a marine mollusk with a coiled shell, related to the modern nautilus, common in ancient seas

Examples

Fossil nautiloid
The fossilized shell of a nautiloid was discovered in the limestone cliffs.
Scientists study nautiloids to learn about ancient marine environments.
A nautiloid swam gracefully through the prehistoric ocean, its shell providing protection.
