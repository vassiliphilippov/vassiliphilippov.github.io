submandibular salivary gland

noun
salivary gland beneath the jaw
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌb.mænˈdɪb.jələr ˈsæ.ləˌvɛri ɡlænd / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.mænˈdɪb.jʊ.lə ˈsæl.ɪ.vəri ɡlænd / (BrE)
one of a pair of salivary glands situated beneath the lower jaw that produce saliva, particularly at night, and release it under the tongue

Examples

The submandibular salivary gland
During the surgery, the doctor carefully examined the patient's submandibular salivary gland.
Swelling in the submandibular salivary gland can indicate an underlying infection or blockage.
The submandibular salivary gland is responsible for secreting a majority of saliva while you sleep.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parotid gland - largest salivary gland, located near the ear
  • synonym: sublingual gland - salivary gland located under the tongue
