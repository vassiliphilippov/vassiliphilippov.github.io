administratorship

noun
legal estate management
9.5
Pronunciation: / ədˌmɪnəˈstreɪtərʃɪp / (AmE), / ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪtəʃɪp / (BrE)
the legal position or authority of managing a deceased person's estate as a court-appointed administrator

Examples

Court administratorship
After her uncle passed away, she was granted administratorship to handle his estate.
The court reviewed his application for administratorship before making a decision.
Without administratorship, it was difficult to pay the deceased's outstanding debts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: administration - the process of managing a deceased person's estate
  • synonym: executorship - the office or authority of carrying out the terms of a will
