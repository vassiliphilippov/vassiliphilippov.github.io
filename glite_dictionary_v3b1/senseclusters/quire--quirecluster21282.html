quire

noun
set of 24 or 25 sheets of paper
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪr / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪə / (BrE)
A set of 24 or sometimes 25 sheets of paper, traditionally used as a standard measure for paper.

Examples

A quire of paper
In the past, merchants would sell stationery by the quire rather than by the ream.
She found an old quire of parchment tied together with a faded ribbon.
The printer ordered a quire of high-quality paper for the special project.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ream - a quantity of paper, typically 500 sheets
