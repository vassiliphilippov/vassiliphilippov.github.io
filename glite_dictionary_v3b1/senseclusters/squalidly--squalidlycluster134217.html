squalidly

adverb
in a filthy or degraded way
8.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààskw…ëÀêl…™dli / (AmE), / Ààskw…íl…™dli / (BrE)
In a filthy, dirty, or morally degraded manner.

Examples

Behave squalidly
The characters in the novel behave squalidly, showing little regard for morality or cleanliness.
The room was squalidly furnished, with tattered curtains and grimy walls.
They lived squalidly in the overcrowded slum.

Synonyms

  • sordidly
    8.8 0.01/m
    sordidly shows moral shame and ugly actions, while squalidly focuses on very filthy, neglected places or lives.
  • filthily
    5.3 0.01/m
    filthily means in an extremely dirty, physical way, while squalidly often adds poor, neglected living conditions and moral decay.
  • dirtily
    1.8 0.01/m
    dirtily describes a plainly dirty or unclean state, while squalidly also suggests neglect, poverty, or moral wrongness.
  • uncleanly
    7.2 0.00/m
    uncleanly suggests a habit of dirty living or poor hygiene, while squalidly describes filthy places and moral degradation.
