floater

noun
small spot drifting in vision
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊtər / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtə / (BrE)
a small, dark spot or shape that appears to drift across the field of vision, caused by cell fragments in the vitreous humor of the eye

Examples

Eye floater
After staring at the bright sky, she noticed several floaters drifting in her vision.
If you suddenly notice an increase in floaters, you should see an eye doctor.
People who see floaters often describe them as tiny cobwebs or specks floating about.
