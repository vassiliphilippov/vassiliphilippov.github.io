dismutase

noun
enzyme for paired reactions
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈmjuːteɪs / (AmE), / dɪsˈmjuːteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes a reaction in which two identical molecules are converted into two different products

Examples

Superoxide dismutase
Superoxide dismutase protects cells from damage by free radicals.
Researchers studied the activity of dismutase in different tissue samples.
A deficiency in dismutase can lead to increased oxidative stress in the body.
