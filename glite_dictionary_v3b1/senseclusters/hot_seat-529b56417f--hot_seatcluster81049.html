hot seat

noun
position of intense scrutiny or pressure
4.6 0.77/m
Pronunciation: / h…ët sit /; / Ààh…ët siÀêt / (AmE), / h…ít siÀêt /; / Ààh…ít siÀêt / (BrE)
a position where someone is under intense pressure, scrutiny, or criticism, especially because of responsibility

Examples

In the hot seat at work
After the scandal broke, the CEO found himself in the hot seat during every board meeting.
Reporters put the politician in the hot seat over her recent policy changes.
With the team's performance declining, the coach is clearly in the hot seat this season.
After the scandal, the minister found himself in the hot seat at the press conference.
She was surprised when the tough questions put her in the hot seat during the meeting.
Whenever the company posts poor results, the CEO ends up in the hot seat.

Synonyms

  • antonym: easy ride - a situation with little difficulty or criticism
