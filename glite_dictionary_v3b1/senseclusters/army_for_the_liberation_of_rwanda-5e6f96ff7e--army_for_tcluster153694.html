Army for the Liberation of Rwanda

proper noun
militant group opposing Rwandan government
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrmi fər ðə lɪˌbɚˈreɪʃən əv ruˈɑːndə / (AmE), / ˈɑːmi fə ðə lɪbəˈreɪʃən əv ruˈændə / (BrE)
A militant organization seeking to overthrow the Tutsi-led government of Rwanda and restore Hutu control; known for acts of violence and terrorism.

Examples

The Army for the Liberation of Rwanda group
International organizations have condemned the actions of the Army for the Liberation of Rwanda.
The Army for the Liberation of Rwanda was held responsible for several violent attacks in the late 1990s.
The government increased security along the border to prevent incursions by the Army for the Liberation of Rwanda.
