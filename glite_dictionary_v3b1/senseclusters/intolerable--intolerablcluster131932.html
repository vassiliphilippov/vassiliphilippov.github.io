Too bad, unpleasant, or difficult to accept or endure; unable to be tolerated.

Examples

An intolerable situation
His intolerable behavior made it impossible for him to keep any friends.
The heat during the summer became intolerable and forced many residents to seek cooler places.
The noise from the construction site was so intolerable that people complained to the city council.

Synonyms

  • unbearable
    3.9 4.69/m
    unbearable is used for something very painful or extreme that people cannot stand, while intolerable is a general word for what cannot be accepted.
  • insufferable
    7.5 1.23/m
    insufferable describes a person or situation so annoying or harsh that people cannot put up with it, while intolerable is broader.
  • unendurable
    8.0 0.26/m
    unendurable sounds more formal and is used for severe pain or hardship that cannot be endured, while intolerable fits everyday use.
  • unsufferable
    8.4 0.00/m
    unsufferable is a less common variant often seen in old texts or speech, while intolerable is the standard modern choice.
  • insupportable
    7.2 0.06/m
    insupportable is rare and formal, often used in legal or strong complaints, while intolerable is the common everyday term.
