intolerable

adjective
too bad or difficult to endure
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɑːlɚəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒlərəbəl / (BrE)
Too bad, unpleasant, or difficult to accept or endure; unable to be tolerated.

Examples

An intolerable situation
His intolerable behavior made it impossible for him to keep any friends.
The heat during the summer became intolerable and forced many residents to seek cooler places.
The noise from the construction site was so intolerable that people complained to the city council.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tolerable - able to be tolerated or endured
  • synonym: unbearable - too painful or difficult to endure
