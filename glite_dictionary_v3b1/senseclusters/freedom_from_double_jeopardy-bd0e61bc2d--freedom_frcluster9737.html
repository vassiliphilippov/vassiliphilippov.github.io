freedom from double jeopardy

noun
right not to be tried twice for the same crime
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdəm frəm ˈdʌbl̩ ˈdʒɛpərdi / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm frəm ˈdʌbl ˈdʒɛpədi / (BrE)
The legal right not to be tried twice for the same crime, as guaranteed by the Fifth Amendment to the US Constitution.

Examples

Freedom from double jeopardy right
Her lawyer argued that the new trial would violate her freedom from double jeopardy.
Thanks to freedom from double jeopardy, he could not be tried a second time for the same incident.
The Constitution guarantees freedom from double jeopardy to protect individuals from repeated prosecution.
