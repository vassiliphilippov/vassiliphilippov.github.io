run out of steam

verb
lose energy or motivation
4.8 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / r ån Ààa ät …ôv stiÀêm / (AmE), / r ån Ààa ät …ôv stiÀêm / (BrE)
to lose energy, enthusiasm, or motivation and become unable to continue effectively

Examples

To run out of steam quickly
After working nonstop for six hours, I finally ran out of steam and took a break.
She often runs out of steam before finishing her workout routine.
The creative team ran out of steam halfway through the campaign.

Synonyms

  • hit the wall
    7.7 0.45/m
    hit the wall is a sudden stop from exhaustion or a barrier, while run out of steam means a slow loss of energy and motivation.
  • fizzle out
    5.3 0.43/m
    fizzle out is to end gradually and weakly after a strong start, while run out of steam means losing energy or motivation and stopping.

  • synonym: tire - to become physically or mentally fatigued
