transubstantiation

noun
change of bread and wine into Christ's body and blood (Catholic doctrine)
9.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /træn.səb.stæn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /træns.ʌb.stæn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /træn.səb.stæn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /træns.ʌb.stæn.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the Christian belief, especially in the Roman Catholic Church, that during the Eucharist the bread and wine become the body and blood of Christ while still looking like bread and wine

Examples

The transubstantiation of bread
At Mass, believers witness the transubstantiation of bread and wine into the body and blood of Christ.
Many Christians do not interpret transubstantiation literally, preferring a symbolic understanding of the Eucharist.
The doctrine of transubstantiation is central to Roman Catholic theology.
During the Mass, believers affirm their faith in transubstantiation.
Some poets use the term transubstantiation metaphorically to describe spiritual or personal transformation.
