church doctrine

noun
official beliefs of a Christian church
Pronunciation: / tʃɝːtʃ ˈdɑktrɪn / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ ˈdɒktrɪn / (BrE)
the official set of beliefs or teachings held and promoted by a particular Christian church or denomination

Examples

Church doctrine teachings
He studied church doctrine extensively before converting to a new denomination.
Many reformers challenged established church doctrine during the Reformation period.
The council met to discuss changes to church doctrine regarding marriage and family life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true, especially in religion
  • antonym: heresy - belief or opinion that is contrary to the official doctrine of a church
