Elinvar

noun
alloy with constant elasticity, used in watch springs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛlɪnˈvɑr / (AmE), / ˌɛlɪnˈvɑː / (BrE)
A trademarked alloy of steel notable for its constant elasticity over a wide range of temperatures, used especially in watch springs.

Examples

The Elinvar alloy
Swiss manufacturers often choose Elinvar for their precision timepieces.
Thanks to Elinvar, the watch required less frequent adjustments.
The balance wheel of the watch was made from Elinvar to maintain accuracy despite temperature changes.
