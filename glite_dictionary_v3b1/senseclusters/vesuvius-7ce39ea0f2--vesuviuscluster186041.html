Vesuvius

proper noun
volcano in southwestern Italy near Naples
Pronunciation: / vəˈsuːviəs / (AmE), / vɛˈsuːvɪəs / (BrE)
a volcano in southwestern Italy, near Naples, famous for its catastrophic eruption in AD 79 that destroyed Pompeii and Herculaneum

Examples

Vesuvius volcano
Many tourists visit Vesuvius each year to hike to the crater and learn about its history.
Mount Vesuvius erupted in AD 79, burying the cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum under volcanic ash.
The fertile soil around Vesuvius has made the region ideal for growing grapes.
