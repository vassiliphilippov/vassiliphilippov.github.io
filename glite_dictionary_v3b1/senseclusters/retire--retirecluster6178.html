retire

verb
withdraw (bills/shares/bonds) from circulation
3.5 2.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtaɪr/ /; / /riː.ˈtaɪr/ /; / /riː.ˈtaɪ.ər/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtaɪ.ə/ /; / / rə.ˈtaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
to stop using or remove something from service because it is no longer needed or valid

Examples

To retire old notes
The central bank may retire old banknotes as new ones are issued.
The company decided to retire its outstanding shares through a buyback program.
They retired the bonds ahead of schedule to reduce their debt.
After years of use, the chef decided to retire his favorite knife.
She finally retired that old coat, even though it held sentimental value.
The museum will retire several outdated exhibits next month.

  • antonym: keep - to retain or continue to use something
  • synonym: redeem - to exchange a bond, share, or note for payment and remove it from circulation
