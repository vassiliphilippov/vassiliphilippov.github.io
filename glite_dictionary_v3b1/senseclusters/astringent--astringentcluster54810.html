astringent

noun
a substance that contracts body tissues
7.0 6.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈstrɪn.dʒənt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈstrɪn.dʒənt/ (BrE)
a substance or drug that causes the contraction of body tissues and canals, often used to reduce bleeding from minor abrasions.

Examples

Apply an astringent
The doctor applied an astringent to stop the bleeding from the small cut.
The pharmacist recommended an astringent for minor skin irritations.
Witch hazel is commonly used as an astringent in skincare products.
