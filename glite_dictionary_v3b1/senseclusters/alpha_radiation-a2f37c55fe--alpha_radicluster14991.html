alpha radiation

noun
emission of alpha particles in radioactive decay
Pronunciation: / ˈælfə ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈalfə ˌreɪdɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the emission of alpha particles as a form of ionizing radiation, typically during radioactive decay of certain elements

Examples

Alpha radiation emission
Because alpha radiation cannot penetrate paper, it is easily stopped by simple barriers.
Prolonged exposure to alpha radiation can be harmful if radioactive materials are inhaled or ingested.
The scientist measured the level of alpha radiation emitted by the uranium sample.
