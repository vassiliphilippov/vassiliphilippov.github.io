shoot the messenger

verb
blame the bearer of bad news
Pronunciation: / ʃuːt ðə ˈmɛsɪndʒɚ / (AmE), / ʃuːt ðə ˈmɛsɪndʒə / (BrE)
to blame or punish the person who brings bad news, rather than addressing the actual cause of the problem

Examples

To shoot the messenger
After sharing the new company policy, she told her team not to shoot the messenger.
I know it's disappointing, but please don't shoot the messenger—I'm just repeating what the boss said.
Instead of looking for solutions, he tends to shoot the messenger whenever problems arise.
