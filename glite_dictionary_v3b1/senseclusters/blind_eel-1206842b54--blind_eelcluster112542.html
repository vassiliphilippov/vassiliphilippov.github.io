blind eel

noun
an eel-shaped salamander of the southern US
2.7 8.4
Pronunciation: /blaɪnd iːl/ (AmE), /blaɪnd iːl/ (BrE)
A small, aquatic, eel-shaped salamander with very reduced limbs, living in muddy still waters of the southern United States.

Examples

The blind eel swims
Because of its reduced eyesight, the blind eel navigates by touch and smell.
Local fishermen sometimes catch a blind eel while trawling for other species.
The blind eel is rarely seen because it lives in deep, muddy waters.
