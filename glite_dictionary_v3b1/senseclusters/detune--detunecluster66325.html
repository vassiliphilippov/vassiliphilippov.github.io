detune

verb
to reduce performance
6.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈtuːn/ /; / /dɪ.ˈtjuːn/ /; / /diː.ˈtuːn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈtjuːn/ /; / /diː.ˈtjuːn/ (BrE)
to intentionally reduce the power, output, or performance of an engine or system, often for reliability or regulatory reasons

Examples

To detune an engine
To comply with new regulations, the engineers had to detune the car's engine.
Some manufacturers detune their products to increase durability and reduce maintenance costs.
The motorcycle was detuned for export to meet local emission standards.
