Quercus arizonica

noun
species of oak native to Arizona and New Mexico
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɜrkəs ˌærɪˈzoʊnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈkwɜːkəs ˌærɪˈzəʊnɪkə / (BrE)
A semi-evergreen shrub or small tree native to Arizona and New Mexico, known for its acorns with hemispherical cups; commonly called Arizona white oak.
