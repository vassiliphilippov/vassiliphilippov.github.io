Musa basjoo

noun
species of ornamental banana plant
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmjusə ˈbɑːs.dʒuː / (AmE), / ˈmjuːsə ˈbæsdʒuː / (BrE)
A species of banana plant native to Asia, especially cultivated in Japan for its attractive foliage rather than edible fruit.

Examples

Musa basjoo plant
Because Musa basjoo is hardy, it can survive winters in regions cooler than typical banana species.
Gardeners often plant Musa basjoo for its tropical appearance rather than for fruit.
The Musa basjoo thrived in the garden, producing lush green leaves even in cool temperatures.
