Athapaskan language

noun
a group of North American indigenous languages
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈpæskən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌæθəˈpæskən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a group of related indigenous languages spoken in parts of North America, primarily in Alaska, western Canada, and the southwestern United States

Examples

Athapaskan language family
Learning an Athapaskan language can provide insight into indigenous cultures and history.
Linguists have traced the origins of the Athapaskan language family to northwestern Canada.
The Navajo and Apache peoples speak different forms of the Athapaskan language.
