titulus

noun
early Roman parish church
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪtʃʊləs / (AmE), / ˈtɪtjʊləs / (BrE)
an early Christian parish or titular church in Rome, often named after its founder or a saint

Examples

Roman titulus
The ancient titulus was dedicated to Saint Cecilia and became a center for early Christians.
Historians studied the records of each titulus to trace the development of the Roman church.
Pilgrims often visited a titulus to honor the memory of its founding saint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basilica - a large and important church building in Rome
  • synonym: parish - a local church community or its church building
