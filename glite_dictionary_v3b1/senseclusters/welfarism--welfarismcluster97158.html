welfarism

noun
belief in welfare state
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛlˌfɛrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛlˌfɛərɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of a system in which the government provides welfare services to support people's well-being

Examples

Government welfarism policies
Some politicians argue that welfarism encourages dependency on government support.
The country's shift toward welfarism led to expanded healthcare and education services.
Critics claim that excessive welfarism can strain the national budget.

Related Terms

  • antonym: laissez-faire - an economic system with minimal government intervention
  • synonym: welfare state - a system in which the government provides social and economic support to its citizens
