deformative

adjective
harmful influence
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɔrmətɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɔːmətɪv / (BrE)
causing harm or corruption to character, morals, or reputation; having a negative influence.

Examples

Deformative influence
The deformative effects of constant negativity can damage a person's outlook on life.
Some critics argue that violent media has a deformative impact on young viewers.
The deformative influence of peer pressure led him to make poor choices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupting - causing someone or something to become morally bad
  • antonym: uplifting - improving someone's character or mood
