inefficacious

adjective
not effective; lacking efficacy
Pronunciation: / ˌɪˌnɛfɪˈkeɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪfɪˈkeɪʃəs / (BrE)
not effective in producing the intended result or effect; lacking efficacy.

Examples

An inefficacious effort
Attempts at negotiation proved inefficacious, and the conflict continued for months.
Many considered the old policy inefficacious and called for comprehensive reform.
The new drug was found to be inefficacious in treating the disease during clinical trials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: efficacious - having the power to produce a desired effect
  • synonym: ineffective - not producing any or the desired effect
