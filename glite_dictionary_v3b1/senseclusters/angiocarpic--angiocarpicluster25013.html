angiocarpic

adjective
fruit enclosed in a shell or husk
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒioʊˈkɑːrpɪk / (AmE), / ˌandʒɪəʊˈkɑːpɪk / (BrE)
(of a fruit) having the seeds enclosed within a shell, husk, or pericarp.

Examples

Angiocarpic fruit seeds
Botanists often classify nuts as angiocarpic when referring to their enclosed seeds.
Many plants in this family produce angiocarpic fruits, making them distinct from those with exposed seeds.
The walnut is an example of an angiocarpic fruit because its seeds are protected by a hard shell.
