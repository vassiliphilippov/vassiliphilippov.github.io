bitternut hickory

noun
a North American hickory tree with bitter nuts
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪtərˌnʌt ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈbɪtənʌt ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
a species of hickory tree found in eastern North America, recognized by its leaves with 7 or 9 leaflets and thin-shelled, very bitter nuts

Examples

A bitternut hickory tree
A mature bitternut hickory can reach heights of up to 35 meters in the wild.
Although the nuts of the bitternut hickory are edible, their bitter taste makes them unpopular with people.
The wood from the bitternut hickory is valued for making tool handles and furniture.
