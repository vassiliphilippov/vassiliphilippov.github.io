Bacchus

proper noun
Roman god of wine and revelry
Pronunciation: / ˈbæk.əs / (AmE), / ˈbæk.əs / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) the god of wine, revelry, and ecstasy; the Roman equivalent of the Greek god Dionysus.

Examples

Bacchus god of wine
Many lively festivals in ancient Rome were dedicated to Bacchus.
Shakespeare often referenced Bacchus in his plays when portraying scenes of celebration.
The statue in the garden depicts Bacchus holding a goblet of wine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Dionysus - Greek god of wine, revelry, and fertility; equivalent of Bacchus
