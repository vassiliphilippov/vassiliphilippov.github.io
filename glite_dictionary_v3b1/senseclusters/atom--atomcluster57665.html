atom

noun
smallest unit of an element
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈætəm / (AmE), / ˈætəm / (BrE)
the smallest unit of a chemical element that retains the properties of that element, consisting of a nucleus surrounded by electrons

Examples

A hydrogen atom
An atom of hydrogen consists of one proton and one electron.
Chemists study how atoms combine to form molecules.
Every object in the universe is made up of atoms.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: element - a substance made of only one kind of atom
  • synonym: ion - an atom or molecule with an electric charge due to loss or gain of electrons
  • degree higher: molecule - a group of atoms bonded together
