accessary

noun
person aiding a crime
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛsəri / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri / (BrE)
a person who helps someone commit a crime but is not present when the crime happens

Examples

An accessary to crime
In some jurisdictions, being an accessary to a crime carries a severe penalty.
She was charged as an accessary after the fact for helping the suspect escape.
The court found the driver to be an accessary to the robbery, even though he did not enter the bank.
He was found guilty as an accessary after helping plan the burglary.
She was accused of being an accessary to the robbery, though she never entered the bank.
The lawyer argued that his client was merely an accessary and not the main perpetrator.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accessory - person who aids in committing a crime (alternate spelling, more common)
