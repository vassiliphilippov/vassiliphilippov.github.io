roundup

noun
police gathering of suspects
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊn.dʌp / (AmE), / ˈraʊn.dʌp / (BrE)
an act of gathering together people, especially suspects or criminals, by the police in a systematic operation

Examples

Police roundup
A roundup of illegal immigrants led to several arrests in the city.
The police carried out a roundup of suspected gang members early this morning.
There was a massive roundup after the bank robbery, with dozens of people taken in for questioning.
