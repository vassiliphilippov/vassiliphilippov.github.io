inactive

adjective
not engaged in full-time work or duties
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈæktɪv / (BrE)
not participating, not involved, or not currently taking part in an activity, job, or group

Examples

Inactive member
After her retirement, she became an inactive member of several professional organizations.
During his illness, he has remained inactive at work and is not currently involved in any projects.
The army placed him on the inactive reserve list until further notice.
The board has been inactive for months and has not made any decisions.
The chemical remained inactive in the solution, showing no signs of reaction.
Volcanoes can remain inactive for centuries before erupting again.
Many soldiers remain inactive during times of peace.
She served in an inactive unit awaiting deployment orders.
The division was declared inactive after the war ended.
