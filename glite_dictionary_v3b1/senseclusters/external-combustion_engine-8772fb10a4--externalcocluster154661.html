external-combustion engine

noun
engine with external fuel combustion
1.4
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɝːnəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən ˈen.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜː.nəl kəmˈbʌs.tʃən ˈen.dʒɪn / (BrE)
an engine in which fuel is burned outside the engine's cylinder, and the generated heat is used to produce mechanical energy

Examples

An external-combustion engine system
New technologies are exploring ways to improve the efficiency of the external-combustion engine.
The steam engine is a classic example of an external-combustion engine used in early locomotives.
Unlike an internal-combustion engine, an external-combustion engine burns fuel outside the working cylinder.

Related Terms

  • antonym: internal-combustion engine - engine where combustion occurs within the cylinder
