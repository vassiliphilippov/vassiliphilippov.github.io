metric grain

noun
unit of weight for gems (50 mg)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛtrɪk ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛtrɪk ɡreɪn / (BrE)
a unit of weight used for pearls and diamonds, equal to 50 milligrams or 1/4 carat.

Examples

A metric grain unit
A metric grain is equivalent to exactly 50 milligrams, commonly used in gem trades.
Pearls are often measured in metric grain units for greater accuracy.
The diamond was weighed at precisely two metric grains.
