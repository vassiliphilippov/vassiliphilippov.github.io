semi-wild

adjective
partially wild or domesticated
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈwaɪld / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈwaɪld / (BrE)
(of animals, plants, or places) only partly wild; not completely domesticated, cultivated, or tamed.

Examples

Semi-wild animals
Some semi-wild flowers have appeared at the edge of the cultivated field.
The park is home to a herd of semi-wild horses roaming freely across the meadows.
They adopted a semi-wild cat that often hunts but still visits their home for food.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: domesticated - tame; brought under human control or cultivation
  • degree higher: wild - living in a natural state; not domesticated or cultivated
