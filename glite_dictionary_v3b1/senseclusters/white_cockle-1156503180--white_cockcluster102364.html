white cockle

noun
a herbaceous plant with white evening-opening flowers
4.6
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈkɑkəl / (AmE), / waɪt ˈkɒkəl / (BrE)
a bluish-green herb with sticky stems and clusters of large white flowers that open in the evening, often having a much-inflated calyx; sometimes classified in the genus Lychnis

Examples

A white cockle flower
A cluster of white cockle flowers opened just after sunset near the old barn.
Gardeners sometimes struggle to remove white cockle because of its persistent roots.
The meadow was dotted with blooming white cockle in the soft evening light.
