nonconstant

adjective
not steady
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
changing or not staying the same over time; not steady or consistent

Examples

The machine's nonconstant speed caused problems during the production process.

A nonconstant signal can make electronic devices behave unpredictably.
Her mood was nonconstant, changing several times throughout the day.
