plagiarized

adjective
copied and passed off as original
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪdʒəˌraɪzd / (AmE), / ˈpleɪdʒərʌɪzd / (BrE)
copied from someone else's work and presented as if it is your own

Examples

Finding plagiarised content in academic writing can lead to severe consequences for students.
She was accused of submitting a plagiarised essay in her literature class.
The professor rejected the paper because it contained several plagiarised paragraphs from online sources.
