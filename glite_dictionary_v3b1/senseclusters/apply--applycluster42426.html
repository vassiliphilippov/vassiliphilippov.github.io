apply

verb
make use of for a purpose
1.5
Pronunciation: / əˈplaɪ / (AmE), / əˈplaɪ / (BrE)
to use something for a particular purpose or put it into effect (such as a rule, method, or tool)

Examples

To apply a rule
Please apply the brakes gently to avoid skidding.
When you apply this chemical, it cleans the surface instantly.
You should apply the theory we learned in class to solve these problems.
It is important to apply the rules fairly to all students.
Judges must apply the law as it is written.
The new policy will apply to every employee in the company.
He decided to apply his theoretical knowledge to solve real-world problems.
The teacher encouraged the students to apply critical thinking in their daily lives.
You must apply this principle carefully in order to get accurate results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: employ - to make use of something
  • synonym: enforce - to make sure that people obey a law or rule
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to or deliberately disregard rules or laws
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or make use of something
  • synonym: practice - to carry out or perform habitually or regularly
  • synonym: use - to put into service for a purpose
  • synonym: utilize - to make practical use of something
