Crucifixion

noun
the death of Jesus on the cross
Pronunciation: / ˌkruːsəˈfɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌkruːsɪˈfɪkʃən / (BrE)
The execution and death of Jesus Christ by being nailed or bound to a cross, a foundational event in Christian belief.

Examples

The Crucifixion of Jesus
Artists throughout history have depicted the Crucifixion in paintings and sculptures.
Every year, Christians commemorate the Crucifixion on Good Friday.
The story of the Crucifixion is central to Christian theology.
