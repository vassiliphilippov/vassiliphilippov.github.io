prim

noun
very proper person
7.9
Pronunciation: / prɪm / (AmE), / prɪm / (BrE)
a person who behaves in a very proper, formal, or stiff manner

Examples

A prim at the party
The prim in the group refused to join in the playful games.
At the dinner, a prim corrected everyone's table manners.
Some people thought the new teacher was a bit of a prim.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prude - a person who is easily shocked by improper things
  • antonym: rebel - a person who resists authority or convention
  • synonym: stickler - a person who insists on strict rules or correctness
