Western birch

noun
birch tree of western U.S. with brownish bark
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn bɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a species of birch tree of the western United States, similar to the paper birch but with brownish bark

Examples

The Western birch tree
Hikers in the Rockies often encounter groves of Western birch along mountain trails.
The Western birch is easily distinguished from other birches by its unique brownish bark.
The leaves of the Western birch turn a golden yellow in the autumn months.
