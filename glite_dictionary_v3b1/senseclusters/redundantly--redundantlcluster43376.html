redundantly

adverb
with unnecessary duplication
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌndəntli / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌndəntli / (BrE)
in a way that duplicates systems, components, or information to provide backup or increase reliability

Examples

Redundantly stored data
The files are stored redundantly on several hard drives to ensure nothing is lost if one fails.
To improve safety, the system operates redundantly with backup power supplies.
Some instructions were given redundantly, causing confusion among the team members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repetitively - in a way that repeats the same action or process
  • antonym: uniquely - in a way that is one of a kind or without duplication
