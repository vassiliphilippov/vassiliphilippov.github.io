outward-developing

adjective
developing away from a central axis
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtwɚd dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtwəd dɪˈvɛləpɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a structure or pattern, especially in plants) developing away from a central axis, so that the oldest elements are at the center and the youngest at the edge.

Examples

Outward-developing pattern
In the sunflower, the florets exhibit an outward-developing pattern, with new blooms forming at the edges.
Some inflorescences are outward-developing, making the center flowers older than those on the margin.
The outward-developing cycle of the flower cluster helps botanists identify the plant species.
