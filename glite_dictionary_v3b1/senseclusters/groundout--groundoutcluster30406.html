groundout

verb
to hit ground ball out
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌaʊt / (BrE)
to hit a ground ball in baseball that results in the batter or a runner being put out

Examples

To groundout to first base
The batter will likely groundout if he swings at that low pitch.
She managed not to groundout and instead hit a single to left field.
If you groundout to shortstop, the inning could end quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fly out - to be put out by hitting a ball that is caught before touching the ground
  • antonym: strike out - to be put out by failing to hit three pitches in baseball
