stereoscopic photography

noun
photography creating 3D effect with stereoscope
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛriəˈskɑpɪk fəˈtɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəˈskɒpɪk fəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
Photography that produces images with the illusion of depth by taking two slightly different photographs and viewing them together through a stereoscope.

Examples

Stereoscopic photography technique
During the Victorian era, stereoscopic photography allowed people to experience distant landscapes in three dimensions.
He collected antique examples of stereoscopic photography to study early techniques for creating depth.
Modern artists sometimes use stereoscopic photography to create immersive visual experiences.
