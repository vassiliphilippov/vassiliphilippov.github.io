pneumonia

noun
inflammation of the lungs (medical)
4.3 5.91/m
Pronunciation: / nuÀàmo än.j…ô / (AmE), / njuÀêÀàm…ô än.i.…ô / (BrE)
A serious lung infection that causes inflammation of the air sacs in one or both lungs, which may fill with fluid or pus, often due to infection by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.

Examples

Severe pneumonia infection
After spending a week in the hospital with pneumonia, she finally returned home to recover.
Children and the elderly are at higher risk of developing pneumonia during the winter.
The doctor explained that pneumonia could cause high fever and difficulty in breathing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bronchitis - inflammation of the bronchial tubes
