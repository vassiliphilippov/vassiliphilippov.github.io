surround

verb
enclose or envelop completely
1.0 1.5
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /sər.ˈaʊnd/ (AmE), /sə.ˈraʊnd/ (BrE)
to be or go all around something or someone on every side (sometimes for protection or attack)

Examples

Fans surround a star
As soon as he stepped onto the stage, cheering fans surrounded him from every direction.
Tall trees surround the cabin, giving it a feeling of seclusion from the outside world.
The warmth of the blanket surrounded her, making her feel safe and comfortable.
In ancient times, people would surround their settlements with ditches or walls to keep out wild animals.
Medieval cities often surrounded themselves with thick stone walls to prevent invasions.
The general ordered his troops to surround the fortress with barricades for added security.
Beautiful gardens surround the old castle, making it a popular tourist spot.
During the parade, crowds surrounded the marching band, applauding and cheering loudly.
Tall fences surround the entire facility for security reasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besiege - to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force surrender
  • synonym: encircle - to form a circle around; to surround
  • synonym: enclose - to close in on all sides; to surround completely
  • synonym: envelop - to enclose completely and cover on all sides
  • antonym: expose - to leave unprotected or uncovered
  • synonym: fortify - to strengthen a place by building defensive works
  • antonym: open - with nothing surrounding or enclosing
