biomolecule

noun
molecule in living things
3.7
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.ˈmɑː.lə.kjuːl/ /; / /baɪ.oʊ.ˈmɑː.lə.kəl/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.ˈmɒl.ɪ.kjuːl/ /; / /baɪ.əʊ.ˈmɒl.ɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
a molecule produced by living organisms, such as proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, or lipids

Examples

A biomolecule in cells
Proteins are a type of biomolecule essential for life.
Scientists study biomolecules to understand how cells function and communicate.
DNA is a well-known biomolecule that carries genetic information in living organisms.
