bill of health

noun
certificate showing a ship's crew is healthy
9.6 6.4
Pronunciation: /bɪl əv hɛlθ/ (AmE), /bɪl əv hɛlθ/ (BrE)
an official document stating that a ship's crew is free from contagious disease, required to be presented at the next port of arrival

Examples

Ship's bill of health
Because of an outbreak onboard, the ship was denied a clean bill of health.
Every vessel arriving from overseas must present a bill of health at the port authority.
The harbor master refused entry until the bill of health was produced.
