control

verb
check or regulate an experiment
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to regulate, validate, or verify in a scientific or technical context

Examples

To control for errors
It is important to control for confounding variables in clinical trials.
Researchers controlled the experiment by using a placebo group.
You must control the sample conditions to ensure accurate results.
Auditors regularly control inventory records to ensure accuracy.
The accountant will control the account by comparing it with the duplicate register.
You must control the company's financial entries at the end of every month.
It is important to control the temperature during the experiment.
Parents should control what their young children watch on television.
The manager must control every step of the process to maintain quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: audit - an official inspection of an organization's accounts
  • synonym: ensure - to make certain that something will occur or be the case
  • synonym: monitor - to observe and check the progress or quality of something over a period of time
  • synonym: verify - to make sure or demonstrate that something is true or accurate
