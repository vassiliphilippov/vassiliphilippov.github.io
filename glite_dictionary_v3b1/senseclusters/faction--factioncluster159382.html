faction

noun
dissenting subgroup within a larger group
9.1 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfækʃən /; / ˈfæk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfækʃən /; / ˈfæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a small, organized group within a larger one, especially one with different views or seeking power

Examples

A faction of students
During the meeting, a faction of students demanded changes to school rules.
Power struggles between factions often lead to instability in organizations.
The political party was divided by a rival faction that opposed the leader's policies.
Rumors spread quickly about a faction plotting to take control of the company.
The political party was divided into rival factions that often undermined each other.
Within the student council, a faction was secretly organizing resistance against the president's plans.
