unsteady

adjective
not firmly fixed or balanced
3.3 1.64/m
Pronunciation: / ÊŒnËˆstÉ›di / (AmE), / ÊŒnËˆstÉ›di / (BrE)
not firm, stable, or balanced; likely to move, shake, or fall

Examples

An unsteady step
She took an unsteady step as she crossed the icy sidewalk.
Sudden wind gusts made the boat feel unsteady on the water.
The old wooden table was so unsteady that anything placed on it wobbled dangerously.
He spoke in an unsteady voice, barely above a whisper.
Her unsteady walk suggested she might be ill.
The table was unsteady and wobbled every time someone touched it.

Synonyms

  • wobbly
    3.6 2.36/m
    wobbly is likely to shake side to side because of weakness, while unsteady suggests general lack of balance and support.
  • unfirm
    8.0 0.00/m
    unfirm is loose or not solid and often used for objects, while unsteady covers any thing that may move or fall.
  • shaky
    3.3 2.85/m
    shaky can mean physically trembling or unreliable, while unsteady focuses on lack of balance and likely movement.
  • unsturdy
    4.8 0.00/m
    unsturdy describes weak or poorly built things that may collapse, while unsteady is about wobbling or losing balance.
  • unstable
    4.4 6.17/m
    unstable often means likely to change or fail over time, while unsteady is about immediate physical wobble or balance loss.
  • unbalanced
    3.8 1.29/m
    unbalanced means lacking equal weight or fairness, while unsteady suggests movement or shakiness that may cause falling.
