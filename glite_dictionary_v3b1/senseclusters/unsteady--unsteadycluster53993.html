unsteady

adjective
not firmly fixed or balanced
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstɛdi / (AmE), / ʌnˈstɛdi / (BrE)
not firm, stable, or balanced; likely to move, shake, or fall

Examples

An unsteady step
She took an unsteady step as she crossed the icy sidewalk.
Sudden wind gusts made the boat feel unsteady on the water.
The old wooden table was so unsteady that anything placed on it wobbled dangerously.
He spoke in an unsteady voice, barely above a whisper.
Her unsteady walk suggested she might be ill.
The table was unsteady and wobbled every time someone touched it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
  • synonym: unstable - prone to change or fail; not stable
