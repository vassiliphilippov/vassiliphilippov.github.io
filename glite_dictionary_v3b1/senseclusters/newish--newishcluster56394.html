newish

adjective
somewhat new
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈnjuːɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat new; fairly recent but not completely new

Examples

A newish laptop
He bought a newish laptop that still had the latest features.
Their house is newish, built just a few years ago.
She wore a newish jacket that looked almost brand new.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: new - not existing before; made, introduced, or discovered recently
  • antonym: old - having existed for a long time
  • degree lower: oldish - somewhat old; fairly old but not completely old
  • synonym: recent - having happened or started not long ago
