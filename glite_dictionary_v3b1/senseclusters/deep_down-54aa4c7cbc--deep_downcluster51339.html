deep down

adverb
in reality; at heart
3.5 4.09/m
Pronunciation: / diÀêp da än /; / dip da än / (AmE), / diÀêp da än / (BrE)
used to talk about what someone really feels or believes, even if they do not show it or say it

Examples

Deep down inside
Deep down, I knew he was right, even though I disagreed at first.
Deep down, everyone wants to feel accepted and loved.
She seems tough, but deep down she's very sensitive.
Deep down, I think she still misses her hometown despite loving city life.
He acts tough, but deep down he's really quite sensitive.
You might pretend not to care, but deep down it bothers you.
