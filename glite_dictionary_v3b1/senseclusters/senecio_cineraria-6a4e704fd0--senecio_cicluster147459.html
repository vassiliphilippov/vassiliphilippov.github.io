Senecio cineraria

noun
a Mediterranean perennial with white woolly leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˌniːʃioʊ sɪnəˈrɛəriə / (AmE), / sɪˌniːsiəʊ sɪnəˈrɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A Mediterranean perennial plant with much-branched, very white woolly stems and leaves, often grown for its decorative foliage.

Examples

Senecio cineraria plant
Senecio cineraria is often selected for garden borders because of its striking silver foliage.
In Mediterranean climates, Senecio cineraria can thrive with minimal watering.
The leaves of Senecio cineraria are prized for their ability to contrast with brightly colored flowers.
