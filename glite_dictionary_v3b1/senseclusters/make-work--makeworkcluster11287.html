make-work

noun
busywork or pointless tasks
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪkˌwɝk / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkˌwɜːk / (BrE)
work, tasks, or activities done mainly to keep someone busy rather than to produce a useful result

Examples

Paper make-work
Most of the paperwork turned out to be make-work that no one ever read.
Summer interns often find themselves stuck with endless make-work assignments.
The manager handed out some make-work just to keep the staff occupied during a slow afternoon.
