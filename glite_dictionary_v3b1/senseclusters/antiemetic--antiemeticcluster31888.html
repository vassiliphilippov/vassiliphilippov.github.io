antiemetic

noun
drug that prevents nausea or vomiting
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.ɪˈmɛt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ɪˈmɛt.ɪk / (BrE)
A drug or substance that prevents or relieves nausea and vomiting.

Examples

An antiemetic drug
Most people recovering from surgery are given an antiemetic to prevent vomiting.
Some over-the-counter medicines include antiemetic ingredients to treat motion sickness.
The doctor prescribed an antiemetic to help control her nausea during chemotherapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emetic - a substance that causes vomiting
