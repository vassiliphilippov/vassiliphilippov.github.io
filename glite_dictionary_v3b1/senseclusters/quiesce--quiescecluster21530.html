quiesce

verb
become quiet or inactive
Pronunciation: / kwiˈɛs / (AmE), / kwiˈɛs / (BrE)
to become quiet or less active; to settle into a state of rest or inactivity

Examples

To quiesce after noise
As the storm passed, the winds gradually quiesced and the night became calm.
The classroom quiesced when the principal entered and everyone waited for her to speak.
The patient’s symptoms may quiesce after receiving the proper treatment.
