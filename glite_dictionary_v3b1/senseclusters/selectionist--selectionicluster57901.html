selectionist

adjective
favoring selection theory
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the theory that selection, especially natural selection, is the main explanation for a phenomenon.

Examples

Selectionist theory
The selectionist perspective argues that natural selection shapes most traits in a population.
Some scientists debate whether a selectionist or neutralist explanation better fits the observed genetic patterns.
Her research takes a strongly selectionist approach to understanding animal behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptationist - supporting the idea that traits evolve mainly through adaptation by natural selection
  • antonym: neutralist - supporting the idea that most evolutionary changes are not caused by selection
