unknowability

noun
impossibility of knowing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌnoʊəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌnəʊəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being impossible to know or fully understand

Examples

The unknowability of truth
Philosophers often discuss the unknowability of certain truths about existence.
The unknowability of the future makes planning both exciting and challenging.
Some scientists accept the unknowability of what lies beyond the observable universe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: knowability - the quality of being able to be known or understood
  • synonym: mystery - something that cannot be explained or understood
