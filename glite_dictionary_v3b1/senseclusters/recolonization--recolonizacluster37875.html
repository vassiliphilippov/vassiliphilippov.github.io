recolonization

noun
return of species
7.3 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀåk…ëÀêl…ôn…ôÀàze…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀåk…íl…ôn å…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the process by which a species or biological community becomes established again in an area where it was previously lost or removed

Examples

Animal recolonization
The recolonization of the island by native birds took several decades after the invasive species were removed.
Scientists studied the recolonization of coral reefs following the hurricane's destruction.
After pollution levels dropped, the river saw rapid recolonization by fish and aquatic plants.

Synonyms

  • repopulation
    4.5 0.05/m
    repopulation is filling an area again with people, animals, or organisms, while recolonization means species establish a self-sustaining community after local loss.
