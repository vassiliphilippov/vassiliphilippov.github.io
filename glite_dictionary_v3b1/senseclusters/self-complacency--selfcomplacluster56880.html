self-complacency

noun
excessive satisfaction with oneself
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf.kəmˈpleɪ.sən.si / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf.kəmˈpleɪ.sən.si / (BrE)
A feeling of excessive satisfaction with oneself, often without awareness of potential faults or problems.

Examples

His self-complacency attitude
His self-complacency made it difficult for him to accept any criticism.
She warned against self-complacency and encouraged the team to keep striving for improvement.
The company's decline was partly due to the self-complacency of its management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complacency - a feeling of smug satisfaction with one's achievements
  • antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
