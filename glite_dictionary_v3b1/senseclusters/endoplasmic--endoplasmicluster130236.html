endoplasmic

adjective
within a cell
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈplæzmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈplæzmɪk / (BrE)
located or occurring within the inner part of a cell

Examples

Endoplasmic reticulum
The endoplasmic reticulum plays a key role in protein synthesis within the cell.
Scientists observed changes in the endoplasmic structures after the experiment.
Damage to endoplasmic components can affect the overall health of the cell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extracellular - located or occurring outside a cell
  • synonym: intracellular - existing or occurring within a cell
