safety fuse

noun
device that interrupts excess electrical current
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti fjuz / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti fjuːz / (BrE)
a device in an electrical circuit that interrupts the flow of current when it exceeds a safe level, preventing damage or fire

Examples

Electrical safety fuse
If the current exceeds the limit, the safety fuse will break the circuit to prevent damage.
The electrician replaced the blown safety fuse to restore power to the room.
You should always check the safety fuse before troubleshooting other electrical issues.
