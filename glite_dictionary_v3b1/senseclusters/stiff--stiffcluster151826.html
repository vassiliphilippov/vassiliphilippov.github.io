stiff

adjective
not easily bent; rigid
1.7 14/m
Pronunciation: / stÉªf / (AmE), / stÉªf / (BrE)
not easily bent, moved, or flexible; rigid or hard to move

Examples

Stiff legs
After sitting for hours, he stood up slowly, his stiff legs barely moving.
She couldn't turn her head because of a stiff neck.
The cardboard is stiff enough to support the model's structure.
After a long winter, the old door had a stiff hinge that was hard to open.
He found the buttons on the remote control were unusually stiff and unresponsive.
The brakes on my bike are stiff and need some oil.

Synonyms

  • rigid
    4.0 5.55/m
    rigid resists any bending or change of shape, while stiff often means hard to move or slightly bent.
  • inflexible
    4.9 0.19/m
    inflexible cannot be bent or changed in shape, while stiff often also means hard to move or tight.
  • unyielding
    6.7 0.29/m
    unyielding resists pressure or bending and stays firm, while stiff focuses on being hard to bend or move.
  • firm
    1.0 2.79/m
    firm feels solid to the touch and does not give much under pressure, while stiff resists bending or moving.
  • hard
    1.0 59/m
    hard is solid, not soft or easy to pierce, while stiff mainly means difficult to bend or move.
