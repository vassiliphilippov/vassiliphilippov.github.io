untransparent

adjective
not clear or open
Pronunciation: / ˌʌntrænˈspɛrənt / (AmE), / ˌʌntrænˈspærənt / (BrE)
not allowing light to pass through or not easy to understand; not transparent

Examples

Untransparent language
The report was written in such an untransparent way that few people could follow it.
Critics accused the government of making untransparent decisions behind closed doors.
The glass was so untransparent that you could not see anything on the other side.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opaque - not letting light through; hard to understand
  • antonym: transparent - allowing light to pass through; easy to understand
