obviator

noun
one who prevents
9.9
Pronunciation: /əb.vɪ.ˈeɪ.tər/ /; / /ɑːb.vɪ.ˈeɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ɒb.vɪ.ˈeɪ.tə/ /; / /əb.vɪ.ˈeɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a person who prevents or opposes something from happening; an obstructer or adversary (archaic, rare)

Examples

Obviator in council
In ancient councils, an obviator would often challenge new ideas to prevent rash decisions.
The obviator of the plan argued forcefully against any changes to tradition.
Though rarely used today, the term obviator once described those who stood in the way of progress.
