shininess

noun
graphics glossiness parameter

(in computer graphics) a numeric parameter in a shading model that determines how glossy a surface appears by controlling the size and sharpness of specular highlights.

Examples

Shininess setting high
In the material editor, set the shininess to high to create tight highlights.
The artist adjusted the shader's shininess so reflections appeared crisp on the metal surface.
When rendering cars, reducing shininess produced a more realistic, slightly worn paint finish.

Synonyms

  • specularity
    9.4 0.00/m
    specularity is the surface quality that gives mirror-like reflections, while shininess is a numeric shader value controlling specular highlight size and sharpness.
  • glossiness
    4.1 0.05/m
    glossiness is the smooth, shiny feel that shows reflections, while shininess is a numeric shader setting that controls highlight size and sharpness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dullness - the lack of brightness or reflective sheen; opposite of gloss or shininess
  • synonym: glossiness - the degree to which a surface reflects light; similar concept used to describe surface sheen
