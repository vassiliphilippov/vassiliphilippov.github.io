illative

adjective
expressing inference or deduction
Pronunciation: / ˈɪlətɪv / (AmE), / ˈɪlətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word or phrase) expressing or introducing an inference; indicating that something follows from what has been said.

Examples

Illative words
His analysis relied on several illative expressions to explain the conclusions drawn.
In logical arguments, illative indicators help clarify the relationship between statements.
The word therefore is often used as an illative conjunction in formal writing.
