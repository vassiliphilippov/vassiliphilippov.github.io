nephrotic syndrome

noun
kidney disorder with proteinuria and swelling
Pronunciation: / nəˈfrɑːtɪk ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / nɪˈfrɒtɪk ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A kidney disorder marked by swelling (edema), high levels of protein in the urine, and often increased blood cholesterol; commonly associated with glomerulonephritis or various systemic diseases.

Examples

Nephrotic syndrome symptoms
Swelling in the legs and ankles is a common symptom of nephrotic syndrome.
The doctor diagnosed the child with nephrotic syndrome after noticing excessive protein in the urine.
Treatment for nephrotic syndrome usually involves medication and dietary changes.
