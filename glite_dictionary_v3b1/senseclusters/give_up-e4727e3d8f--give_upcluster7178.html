give up

verb
permanently relinquish or surrender
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ʌp / (AmE), / ɡɪv ʌp / (BrE)
to stop doing something, to quit an activity, habit, or effort, or to admit defeat

Examples

To give up smoking
After years of trying, she finally gave up her search for her birth parents.
The company decided to give up control of its overseas operations.
When the hikers realized they were lost, they did not give up hope of being rescued.
After years of debate, they finally decided to give up their opposition to the proposal.
In the negotiations, both companies had to give up some of their demands to reach an agreement.
She refused to give up her claim to the family land, even after much persuasion.
After many attempts, he finally decided to give up smoking for good.
Many people struggle when trying to give up coffee each morning.
She wants to give up sugar to improve her health.
