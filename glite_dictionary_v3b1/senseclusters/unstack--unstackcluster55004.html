unstack

verb
to remove from stack
4.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstæk / (AmE), / ʌnˈstak /; / ʌnˈstæk / (BrE)
to remove items from a stack or pile so they are no longer arranged on top of each other

Examples

To unstack boxes
After the delivery, the workers unstacked the crates and placed them on the shelves.
She carefully unstacked the books to clean the table underneath.
They unstacked the chairs at the end of the meeting to prepare the room for the next event.
After the event, volunteers helped to unstack the chairs and tables in the hall.
Please unstack the boxes before you start unpacking them.
It took us a while to unstack all the books from the tall shelf.
After the delivery, workers began to unstack the crates from the truck.
She carefully unstacked the books to find the one she needed at the bottom.
It took an hour to unstack all the chairs after the event ended.
