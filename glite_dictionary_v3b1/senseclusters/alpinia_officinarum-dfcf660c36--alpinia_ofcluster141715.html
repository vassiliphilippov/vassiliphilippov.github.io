Alpinia officinarum

noun
a species of ginger, lesser galangal
Pronunciation: / ælˈpɪniə ˌɑfɪsɪˈnɛrəm / (AmE), / ælˈpɪniə ˌɒfɪsɪˈneərəm / (BrE)
A species of ginger native to China, with aromatic roots used in medicine and as a spice (commonly known as lesser galangal).

Examples

Alpinia officinarum root spice
Many chefs use Alpinia officinarum to add a unique flavor to Asian dishes.
Researchers are studying the potential health benefits of extracts from Alpinia officinarum.
The dried rhizome of Alpinia officinarum is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galangal - an aromatic rhizome used in Asian cooking, related to ginger
