dimorphism

noun
two distinct forms
8.4
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈmɔːr.fɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the existence of two distinct forms among individuals of the same species, such as male and female differences

Examples

Sexual dimorphism
Sexual dimorphism is common in many animal species, with males and females looking very different.
The bright colors of the male bird are a striking example of dimorphism in this species.
Scientists study dimorphism to understand how different traits evolve within a population.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monomorphism - the existence of only one form in a species
  • degree higher: polymorphism - the occurrence of several different forms in a species
