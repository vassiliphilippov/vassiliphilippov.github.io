survey

verb
formally inspect troops
Pronunciation: / sərˈveɪ /; / sɚˈveɪ / (AmE), / səˈveɪ / (BrE)
to look at, inspect, or review something carefully and thoroughly

Examples

To survey the scene
After the commander surveyed the battalion, the parade officially started.
Dignitaries stood on the platform, surveying the soldiers as they marched past.
The general will survey the troops before the ceremony begins.
He surveyed his desk before starting work to ensure everything was in order.
She took a moment to survey the crowd before stepping onto the stage.
The scientist surveyed the landscape with great interest, making careful notes in her journal.
For months, the detective surveyed the suspect's movements without being noticed.
The agency decided to survey the building after receiving reports of suspicious activity.
They are surveying communications to prevent any potential threats.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to
  • synonym: inspect - to look at something closely to assess its condition or quality
  • synonym: monitor - to observe and check the progress of something over a period of time
  • synonym: observe - to watch carefully, especially with attention to details or circumstances
  • synonym: scan - to look at all parts of something carefully in order to detect some feature
