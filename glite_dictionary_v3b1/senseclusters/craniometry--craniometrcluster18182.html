craniometry

noun
measurement and study of skulls
5.4
Pronunciation: / kræniˈɑːmətri / (AmE), / kræniˈɒmətri / (BrE)
the branch of physical anthropology concerned with the measurement and study of skulls, especially after removal of soft tissues

Examples

Craniometry study
He published a paper on craniometry in early hominids for a leading anthropology journal.
Recent advances in craniometry have improved the accuracy of forensic reconstructions.
The study of ancient populations often relies on craniometry to analyze skeletal remains.
