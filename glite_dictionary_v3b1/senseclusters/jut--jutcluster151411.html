jut

verb
extend out or project
4.8 2.61/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’ÊŒt / (AmE), / dÊ’ÊŒt / (BrE)
(of a part of something) to extend out, project, or stick out beyond the main line or surface.

Examples

To jut out
A pair of branches jutted out from the old tree trunk.
Sharp rocks jut from the edge of the mountain path.
The balcony seemed to jut over the street below.

Synonyms

  • protrude
    5.8 4.35/m
    protrude is a neutral, slightly formal verb meaning to stick out from a surface, while jut is more sudden and sharp.
  • project
    4.3 2.43/m
    project is used to describe something that extends outward smoothly, while jut often means a part that sticks out sharply.
  • stick out
    3.2 4.64/m
    stick out is an informal phrasal verb for extending beyond a surface, while jut suggests a solid, often sharp projection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: project - to extend outward beyond a surface or line
  • antonym: recede - to move back or withdraw
