suckhole

noun
dangerous water current
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌkˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsʌkˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a place in a river, stream, or along a shore where water currents create a strong inward suction, often forming a whirlpool or drain.

Examples

River suckhole
The swimmer was warned to stay away from the suckhole near the rocks.
After heavy rain, a dangerous suckhole formed at the bend in the river.
Kayakers must always be alert for a hidden suckhole that could pull them underwater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eddy - a circular movement of water causing a small whirlpool
  • synonym: whirlpool - a rapidly rotating mass of water forming a vortex
