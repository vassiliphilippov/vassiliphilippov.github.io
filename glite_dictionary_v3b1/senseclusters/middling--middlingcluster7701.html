middling

noun
product of intermediate quality or size
6.8 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.lɪŋ / (BrE)
A product of intermediate quality or size, especially coarse particles of wheat mixed with bran separated during milling.

Examples

Wheat middling
During bread making, middling can be used to add extra fiber to the dough.
Farmers sometimes feed middling to livestock due to its nutritional content.
The flour mill produces middling as a by-product during the refinement process.
