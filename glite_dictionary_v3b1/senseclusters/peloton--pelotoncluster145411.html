peloton

noun
group of cyclists
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛləˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˈpɛlətɒn / (BrE)
the main group of cyclists or participants moving together in a race or event, especially in cycling (can also be used metaphorically)

Examples

Cycling peloton
The peloton surged forward as the race entered its final kilometers.
She managed to stay with the peloton despite the challenging mountain stage.
A few riders broke away from the peloton to try for an early lead.
The cyclists in the peloton worked together to reduce wind resistance during the race.
As the marathon progressed, the peloton began to break apart as runners set their own pace.
In the business world, staying ahead of the peloton can be challenging in a competitive market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breakaway - a small group of cyclists that has separated from the main group
  • antonym: leader - the person or group in front in a race
  • synonym: pack - a group of riders or competitors moving together
