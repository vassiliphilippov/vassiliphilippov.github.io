conduciveness

noun
quality of enabling
7.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈduːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / kənˈdjuːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of making something likely or possible to happen

Examples

The conduciveness of silence
The conduciveness of the quiet library made it easy for her to concentrate on her studies.
Good lighting and comfortable chairs add to the conduciveness of a productive workspace.
The manager praised the conduciveness of the team's positive attitude toward achieving their goals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: favorableness - the quality of being advantageous or helpful
  • antonym: obstacle - something that makes progress or success difficult
