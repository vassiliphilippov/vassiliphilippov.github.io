sandblast

verb
clean or shape using a stream of abrasive material
Pronunciation: / ˈsændˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈsændˌblɑːst / (BrE)
to clean or shape a surface by directing a high-pressure stream of sand or other abrasive material at it

Examples

To sandblast a wall
After years of weathering, the statues needed to be sandblasted to restore their original appearance.
The company was hired to sandblast graffiti from the historic stone walls.
Workers will sandblast the old paint off the metal beams before repainting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrade - to scrape or wear away by friction
  • degree lower: polish - to make a surface smooth and shiny by rubbing
