screencast

verb
to record screen activity
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskriːnkæst / (AmE), / ˈskriːnkɑːst / (BrE)
to record the activity on a computer screen as a video, often including spoken commentary.

Examples

To screencast a lesson
The teacher decided to screencast the lesson so students could review it at home.
You can screencast your presentation to help remote colleagues follow along.
He learned how to screencast software tutorials for his YouTube channel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broadcast - to transmit audio or video to an audience
  • synonym: record - to capture audio or video for later playback
