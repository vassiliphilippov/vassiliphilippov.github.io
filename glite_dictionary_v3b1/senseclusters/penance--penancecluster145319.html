penance

noun
Catholic sacrament of confession and absolution
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnəns / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəns / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a sacrament in which a member confesses sins to a priest and receives absolution and spiritual guidance for repentance and atonement.

Examples

Catholic penance sacrament
After committing the sin, he went to church to perform penance.
She confessed her wrongdoing to the priest and received penance as part of her atonement.
The sacrament of penance offers Catholics a path to forgiveness and spiritual renewal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolution - formal forgiveness of sins, especially by a priest
  • synonym: confession - the act of admitting sins, especially in a religious context
