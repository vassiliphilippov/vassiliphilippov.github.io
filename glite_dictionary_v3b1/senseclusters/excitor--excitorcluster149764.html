excitor

noun
stimulating cell or synapse
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtə / (BrE)
a nerve cell, synapse, or biological structure that increases the activity of another cell by producing excitation

Examples

Spinal excitor cell
The spinal excitor transmitted signals that caused the muscle fibers to contract suddenly.
Researchers studied how each excitor affected the overall activity of the neural network.
Damage to an excitor in the brain can disrupt normal communication between nerve cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibitor - a cell or synapse that decreases the activity of another cell
  • synonym: stimulator - something that increases activity or excitation in a biological system
