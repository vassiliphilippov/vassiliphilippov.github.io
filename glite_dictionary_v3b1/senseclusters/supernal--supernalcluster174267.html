supernal

adjective
celestial; heavenly; divine
9.9 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / suÀàp…úrn…ôl /; / suÀêÀàp…úrn…ôl / (AmE), / suÀàp…úÀên…ôl /; / suÀêÀàp…úÀên…ôl / (BrE)
relating to the heavens, something divine, or celestial; heavenly

Examples

The supernal sky
Poets often describe the night sky in supernal terms, as if it belonged to another realm.
The cathedral was filled with a supernal light that seemed to come from no earthly source.
The choir's voices blended in a supernal harmony that left the audience speechless.
Ancient texts often describe gods as possessing supernal powers beyond human comprehension.
The choir's voices had a supernal quality that moved the entire audience.
The painting was admired for its supernal beauty, evoking a sense of awe and wonder.

Synonyms

  • heavenly
    5.0 5.10/m
    heavenly describes something from or like heaven and is used in everyday speech, while supernal sounds more formal and literary.
  • celestial
    6.9 2.15/m
    celestial often refers to stars, space, or the sky in science and poetry, while supernal focuses on a divine, heavenly quality.
  • empyrean
    9.9 0.03/m
    empyrean names the highest, poetic heaven in old texts, while supernal is a general adjective for something heavenly or divine.
  • ultraterrene
    9.9 0.00/m
    ultraterrene feels rare and formal, describing things not of earth, while supernal is a more familiar literary word meaning heavenly.
  • supramundane
    9.9 0.01/m
    supramundane means beyond the physical and often philosophical, while supernal points more directly to heaven or the divine.
  • ethereal
    8.8 1.68/m
    ethereal suggests light, delicate, or unreal beauty, while supernal emphasizes a heavenly, divine origin rather than mere lightness.
