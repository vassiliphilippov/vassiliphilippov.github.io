alcid

noun
northern seabird family
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.sɪd/ (AmE), /ˈæl.sɪd/ (BrE)
a seabird of the family Alcidae, such as a puffin, auk, or murre, found mainly in northern oceans

Examples

An alcid dives
The alcid dives deep into the ocean to catch small fish and crustaceans.
Many species of alcids nest on rocky cliffs along northern coastlines.
Unlike gulls, an alcid uses its wings to swim underwater in search of food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auk - a diving seabird of the Alcidae family
  • antonym: gull - a common seabird, not in the Alcidae family
  • synonym: puffin - a seabird with a colorful beak, part of the Alcidae family
