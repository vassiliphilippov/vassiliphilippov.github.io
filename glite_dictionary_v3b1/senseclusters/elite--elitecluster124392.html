elite

noun
group with superior status or influence
4.4 3.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlit / (AmE), / ɪˈliːt / (BrE)
a group or class of people who are considered to be the best in a society or organization, often due to their power, intelligence, or wealth

Examples

The elite class
He never felt comfortable mixing with the social elite of his city.
Many believe that only the elite have access to the best education.
The governing elite gathered in the capital to discuss national policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocracy - the highest class in certain societies, especially those holding hereditary titles or offices
  • antonym: masses - the ordinary people in society who do not belong to the elite
