Mohorovicic discontinuity

noun
boundary between Earth's crust and mantle
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊhəˈroʊvɪtʃɪtʃ ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuəti / (AmE), / ˌməʊhɒrəˈvɪtʃɪtʃ ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuːɪti / (BrE)
the boundary layer between the Earth's crust and the underlying mantle, marked by a distinct change in seismic wave velocities

Examples

The Mohorovicic discontinuity boundary
Geologists often refer to the Mohorovicic discontinuity simply as the Moho.
Seismic waves travel faster below the Mohorovicic discontinuity due to the denser mantle material.
The depth of the Mohorovicic discontinuity varies between oceans and continents.
