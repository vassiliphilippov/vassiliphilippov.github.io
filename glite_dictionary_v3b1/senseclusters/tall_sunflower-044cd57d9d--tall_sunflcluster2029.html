tall sunflower

noun
very tall North American sunflower with edible roots
4.1
Pronunciation: / tɔl ˈsʌnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / tɔːl ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A very tall perennial sunflower of central and eastern North America, especially the eastern United States and Canada, with edible tuberous roots.

Examples

The tall sunflower plant
Early settlers used the edible roots of the tall sunflower as a food source.
I planted a few tall sunflowers in the backyard for their impressive height and attractive blooms.
The tall sunflower stood above the other plants in the garden, its yellow petals catching the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Jerusalem artichoke - a type of sunflower with edible tuberous roots, also called sunchoke
