turnkey

noun
prison guard (historical term)
9.9 0.22/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ùnÀåki / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀênÀåkiÀê / (BrE)
an officer in charge of locking and guarding prisoners in a jail; a prison guard (historical).

Examples

Prison turnkey
Dickens often described the harsh manner of a turnkey in his novels.
In Victorian England, the turnkey was responsible for all the cell doors in the old prison.
The turnkey handed the prisoner his food through a small hatch every morning.

Synonyms
