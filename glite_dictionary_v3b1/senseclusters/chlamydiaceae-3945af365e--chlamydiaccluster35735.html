Chlamydiaceae

noun
family of Gram-negative parasitic bacteria
9.9
Pronunciation: / klæmˌɪdiəˈsiːi / (AmE), / klamˌɪdɪəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
A family of Gram-negative bacteria that are obligate parasites in warm-blooded vertebrates, including humans.

Examples

Chlamydiaceae family bacteria
Doctors often test for infections caused by members of the Chlamydiaceae family.
Studies on the Chlamydiaceae have revealed new insights into bacterial parasitism.
The Chlamydiaceae are responsible for several important infectious diseases in humans and animals.
