endogenous

adjective
originating from within
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdɑdʒənəs / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒdʒənəs /; / ɛnˈdɒdʒɪnəs / (BrE)
originating from or produced within an organism, system, or process (not from outside)

Examples

Endogenous factors
Economic models often account for endogenous factors that arise within the system.
Some diseases result from endogenous processes rather than external infections.
The researchers studied endogenous hormones produced by the body.
Certain diseases can be triggered by endogenous factors within the body rather than external agents.
Economic models differ in whether they treat technological change as endogenous or exogenous.
The researchers studied the endogenous rhythms that control animal sleep cycles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exogenous - originating from outside an organism, system, or process
