menhir

noun
tall upright prehistoric stone
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛn.hɪr / (AmE), / ˈmen.hɪə / (BrE)
A tall, upright stone erected in prehistoric times, especially in western Europe.

Examples

A menhir stone
Archaeologists studied the carvings on the surface of the massive menhir.
Many tourists visit Brittany to see its famous rows of Neolithic menhirs.
The ancient menhir stood alone in the grassy field, towering over the landscape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: megalith - a large stone used as a monument or building block in prehistoric structures
