Florida arrowroot

noun
a cycad plant native to Florida with starchy roots
Pronunciation: / flɔːrɪdə ˈæroʊˌruːt / (AmE), / flɒrɪdə ˈærəʊˌruːt / (BrE)
a cycad plant (Zamia integrifolia) native to Florida, the West Indies, and Cuba, whose roots and stems yield a starchy substance called arrowroot

Examples

Florida arrowroot plant
Botanists have studied the Florida arrowroot due to its unique method of storing starch underground.
Overharvesting of Florida arrowroot led to a decline in its population in some regions.
The indigenous peoples of Florida traditionally used the roots of the Florida arrowroot to produce a nutritious flour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrowroot - a starch obtained from the root of certain tropical plants
