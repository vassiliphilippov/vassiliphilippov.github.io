redbrick university

noun
British university founded in late 19th/early 20th century
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd.brɪk juˌnɪˈvɝ.sə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˈrɛd.brɪk juːˌnɪˈvɜː.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
(British informal) a British university founded in the late 19th or early 20th century in a provincial city, especially one characterized by red brick buildings, distinguished from Oxford and Cambridge.

Examples

A redbrick university campus
Manchester is a well-known redbrick university with a strong reputation in science and engineering.
Many students prefer to attend a redbrick university because of their modern approach to teaching.
The architecture of a typical redbrick university campus often reflects the industrial heritage of its city.
