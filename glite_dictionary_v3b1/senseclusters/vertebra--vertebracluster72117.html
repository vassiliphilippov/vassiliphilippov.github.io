vertebra

noun
one of the small bones of the backbone
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrtəbrə / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪbrə / (BrE)
each of the series of small bones forming the backbone, having a hole through which the spinal cord passes

Examples

A vertebra bone
A slipped disc occurs when the soft tissue between two vertebrae in the back is damaged.
Injuries to a vertebra can result in damage to the spinal cord.
The human spine consists of thirty-three vertebrae stacked one on top of another.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disc - the cartilage between vertebrae
  • synonym: spine - the backbone; the series of vertebrae forming the axis of the skeleton
