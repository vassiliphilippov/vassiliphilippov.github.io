interest-bearing

adjective
yielding or paying interest
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.trəst ˌber.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.trəst ˌbeə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a financial instrument or obligation) yielding interest or requiring the payment of interest.

Examples

Interest-bearing account
Sarah chose an interest-bearing savings account to grow her money over time.
The company issued interest-bearing bonds to raise capital from investors.
Unlike cash, an interest-bearing deposit earns you extra income each month.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interest-free - not requiring any payment of interest
