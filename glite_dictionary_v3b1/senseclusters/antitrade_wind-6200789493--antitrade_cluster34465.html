antitrade wind

noun
a west-to-east wind above tropical trade winds
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈtreɪd wɪnd / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtreɪd wɪnd / (BrE)
a wind blowing from west to east above the trade winds in the tropics

Examples

The antitrade wind blows
Above the tropics, antitrade winds can reach impressive speeds during certain seasons.
Meteorologists study antitrade wind patterns to better understand global weather systems.
The airplane climbed high enough to catch the antitrade wind and save fuel on its flight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trade wind - a wind blowing steadily toward the equator from the northeast in the Northern Hemisphere or southeast in the Southern Hemisphere, below the antitrade winds
