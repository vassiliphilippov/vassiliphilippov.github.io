overloaded

adjective
loaded past safe or intended limit
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˈloʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈləʊdɪd / (BrE)
having a load that exceeds the safe or intended capacity; burdened with too much weight, work, or responsibility.

Examples

The electrical circuit became overloaded during the storm.
The truck was overloaded and could not make it up the hill.
