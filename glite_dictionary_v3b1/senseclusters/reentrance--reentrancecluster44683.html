reentrance

noun
inward angle or curve
Pronunciation: / riˈɛntrəns / (AmE), / riˈɛntrəns / (BrE)
an inward angle or curve, especially one that forms a recess or indentation in a structure or shape

Examples

Building reentrance
The architect designed a reentrance to provide a sheltered area at the building's corner.
In geometry, a reentrance is an inward curve that creates a concave shape.
The castle wall included a reentrance that allowed defenders to take cover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: niche - a shallow recess in a wall
  • antonym: salient - an outward projecting angle or part
