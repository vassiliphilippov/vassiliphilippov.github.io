fiddleback

adjective
wavy wood grain
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪdəlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfɪdəlˌbæk / (BrE)
having a wavy or striped grain pattern in wood, especially seen on the backs of violins and similar instruments

Examples

Fiddleback maple wood
The craftsman selected a fiddleback maple for the violin to enhance its beauty.
Collectors often seek guitars with a fiddleback pattern for their unique appearance.
The cabinet's doors were made from fiddleback wood, giving them a striking look.
