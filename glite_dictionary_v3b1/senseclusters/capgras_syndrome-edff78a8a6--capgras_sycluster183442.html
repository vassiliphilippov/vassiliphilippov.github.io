Capgras syndrome

noun
delusion of impostors
9.9
Pronunciation: / kæpˈɡrɑː sɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / kæpˈɡrɑː sɪnˌdrəʊm / (BrE)
a psychological disorder in which a person believes that someone close to them has been replaced by an identical impostor

Examples

Capgras syndrome patient
The psychiatrist diagnosed the patient with Capgras syndrome after she claimed her sister was an impostor.
People with Capgras syndrome may become suspicious of close family members.
Treatment for Capgras syndrome often involves therapy and medication to address the delusional beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delusional disorder - a mental illness involving persistent false beliefs
  • antonym: prosopagnosia - an inability to recognize faces
