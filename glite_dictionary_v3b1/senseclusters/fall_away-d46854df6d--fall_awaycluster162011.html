fall away

verb
decline in standard or quality
1.5
Pronunciation: / fɔl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to decrease or become less in amount, size, quality, or intensity

Examples

Enthusiasm falls away
Her enthusiasm has fallen away over the past few months.
If quality starts to fall away, customers will notice.
Support for the policy began to fall away after the scandal.
As the storm passed, the winds gradually fell away to a gentle breeze.
Attendance at the club meetings began to fall away after the holidays.
Her enthusiasm never seemed to fall away, even in difficult times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deteriorate - to become worse in quality or value
  • synonym: diminish - to become or make less
  • antonym: improve - to become or make better
  • antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size or amount
