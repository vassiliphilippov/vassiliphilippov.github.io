gloom

verb
to become sad or dark
5.4
Pronunciation: / ɡluːm / (AmE), / ɡluːm / (BrE)
to make or become dark, sad, or depressed

Examples

Clouds gloom the sky
The cloudy sky gloomed the entire afternoon, making everyone feel tired.
Bad news can easily gloom a cheerful atmosphere.
Her face gloomed when she heard about the cancellation of the event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brighten - to make or become lighter or happier
  • synonym: darken - to make or become darker
  • synonym: depress - to make someone feel sad or without hope
