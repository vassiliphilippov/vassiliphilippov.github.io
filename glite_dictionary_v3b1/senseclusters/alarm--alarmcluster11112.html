alarm

verb
make someone afraid or concerned
1.9 8.40/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàl…ërm / (AmE), / …ôÀàl…ëÀêm / (BrE)
to make someone feel afraid, worried, or anxious about possible danger or problems

Examples

To alarm people
She didn't want to alarm her family by calling them late at night.
The news of the approaching storm alarmed local residents, prompting evacuations.
The sudden drop in temperature alarmed scientists monitoring climate changes in the area.
News of the sudden policy change alarmed many employees.
Rising infection rates have alarmed public health officials across the country.
The loud crash in the middle of the night alarmed the entire family.

Synonyms

  • worry
    1.0 71/m
    worry makes someone feel anxious about actual or possible problems, while alarm causes sudden fear or urgent anxiety about danger.
  • disquiet
    7.3 0.11/m
    disquiet makes someone feel uneasy and quietly worried, while alarm is sudden fear or urgent anxiety about possible danger.
  • disturb
    2.5 9.66/m
    disturb upsets someone and causes worry or emotional discomfort, while alarm is sudden fear or quick panic about possible danger.
  • frighten
    1.0 25/m
    frighten makes someone feel sudden fear or terror, while alarm is broader and can mean fear plus anxious concern about problems.
  • dismay
    4.4 3.20/m
    dismay makes someone feel shocked, disappointed, and discouraged, while alarm focuses more on sudden fear and anxious worry about danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - to warn someone of danger or put on guard
  • synonym: frighten - to make someone feel afraid or anxious
  • antonym: reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's fears
