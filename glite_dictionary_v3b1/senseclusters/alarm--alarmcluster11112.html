alarm

verb
make someone afraid or concerned
1.0 1.7
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈlɑːrm/ (AmE), /ə.ˈlɑːm/ (BrE)
to make someone feel afraid, worried, or anxious about possible danger or problems

Examples

To alarm people
She didn't want to alarm her family by calling them late at night.
The news of the approaching storm alarmed local residents, prompting evacuations.
The sudden drop in temperature alarmed scientists monitoring climate changes in the area.
News of the sudden policy change alarmed many employees.
Rising infection rates have alarmed public health officials across the country.
The loud crash in the middle of the night alarmed the entire family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - to warn someone of danger or put on guard
  • synonym: frighten - to make someone feel afraid or anxious
  • antonym: reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's fears
