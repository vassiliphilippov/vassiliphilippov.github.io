great adductor muscle

noun
large thigh muscle for adduction and extension
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt əˈdʌktər ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ɡreɪt əˈdʌktə ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a large muscle of the inner thigh responsible for adducting (bringing toward the midline) and extending the thigh

Examples

The great adductor muscle holds shells
In certain shellfish, the great adductor muscle is what holds the shells tightly closed.
Injuries to the great adductor muscle are common among athletes who perform sudden lateral movements.
The great adductor muscle plays an important role in stabilizing the pelvis during walking.
