Johann Gutenberg

proper noun
German inventor of the printing press
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈjoʊ.hɑːn ˈɡuː.tən.bɜːrɡ/ (AmE), /ˈjəʊ.hɑːn ˈɡuː.tən.bɜːɡ/ (BrE)
(c. 1400–1468) A German printer who introduced printing with movable type to Europe and invented the printing press.

Examples

Johann Gutenberg printing press
Johann Gutenberg revolutionized the spread of knowledge by inventing the printing press in the 15th century.
Many historians credit Johann Gutenberg as one of the most influential inventors in human history.
The Bible printed by Johann Gutenberg is considered the first book produced with movable type in Europe.
