Japanese cedar

noun
tall evergreen tree of Japan and China
2.0
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz ˈsiːdɚ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a tall evergreen tree native to Japan and China, valued for its soft wood; also known as Cryptomeria japonica

Examples

Japanese cedar tree
Furniture made from Japanese cedar is prized for its lightweight yet durable wood.
Pollen from the Japanese cedar causes seasonal allergies for many people in East Asia.
The Japanese cedar is often planted in temple gardens throughout Japan for its stately appearance.
