vulgaris

adjective
Latin for common

used in Latin to mean 'common' or 'ordinary', often found in historical texts and scientific names.

Examples

Vulgaris language
In medieval manuscripts, the term vulgaris was used to describe the language of the common people.
The species name Rosa vulgaris indicates that it is a common type of rose.
The charter referred to the citizens as populus vulgaris, meaning the ordinary people of the town.
