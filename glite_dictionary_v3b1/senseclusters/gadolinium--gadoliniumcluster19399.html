gadolinium

noun
a rare earth metallic element
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɡædəˈlɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌɡadəˈlɪnɪəm / (BrE)
a soft, silvery-white, ductile, and ferromagnetic metallic element of the rare earth group, used especially in electronics and as a contrast agent in medical imaging

Examples

Gadolinium metal element
Gadolinium is found in trace amounts in minerals such as monazite and bastnäsite.
Researchers studied the magnetic properties of gadolinium in the laboratory.
The MRI scan required the injection of gadolinium as a contrast agent.
