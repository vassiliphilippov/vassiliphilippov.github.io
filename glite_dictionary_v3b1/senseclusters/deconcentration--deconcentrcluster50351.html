deconcentration

noun
reducing centralization
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkənˌsɛntrəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkɒnsɛntrəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing concentration or centralization in a system, organization, or activity

Examples

Administrative deconcentration
The policy of deconcentration allowed local offices to make more independent decisions.
Many countries have adopted deconcentration to improve the efficiency of public services.
Through deconcentration, resources were distributed more evenly across the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralization - the process of concentrating control or power in a central authority
  • synonym: decentralization - the transfer of authority from a central to a local government or organization
