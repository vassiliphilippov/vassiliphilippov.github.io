babel

noun
confused mixture of voices or sounds
7.8 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
A confused mixture of voices or sounds, making it difficult to understand or distinguish any one; noise and disorder caused by competing sounds.

Examples

A babel of voices
After the concert, the lobby was filled with a babel of excited conversation.
She was overwhelmed by the babel of voices in the crowded hall.
The babel in the marketplace made it hard to hear anything clearly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cacophony - harsh, discordant mixture of sounds
  • synonym: clamor - loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting
  • antonym: silence - complete absence of sound
