specialization

noun
biological adaptation of a body part
Pronunciation: / ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Biology) The process by which a body part, cell, or structure becomes adapted to perform a specific function.

Examples

Cell specialization
During embryonic development, specialization allows stem cells to become muscle or nerve cells.
Nerve cells exhibit a high degree of specialization for transmitting electrical signals.
The specialization of bird beaks enables them to access different food sources.
During embryonic development, specialisation allows cells to take on unique roles in the body.
Nerve cells exhibit extreme specialisation to transmit electrical signals efficiently.
The specialisation of leaves into thorns helps protect certain plants from herbivores.

Related Terms

  • synonym: differentiation - process by which cells or tissues become specialized
