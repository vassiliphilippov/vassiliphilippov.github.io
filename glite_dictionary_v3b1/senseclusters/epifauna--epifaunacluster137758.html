epifauna

noun
surface-dwelling animals
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈfɔnə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈfɔːnə / (BrE)
animals that live on the surface of the seabed or another surface, not buried within it (can refer to one animal or a group)

Examples

Epifauna on seabed
Starfish and sea urchins are common types of epifauna found on rocky ocean floors.
Marine biologists study epifauna to understand the health of coastal ecosystems.
The diversity of epifauna can indicate changes in water quality and habitat conditions.
Scientists studied the epifauna on the coral reef to understand changes in biodiversity.
The storm caused a decline in the epifauna living on the rocky shore.
Many types of epifauna can be found attached to underwater rocks and plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthos - organisms living on or in the seabed
  • antonym: infauna - animals that live within the seabed or substrate
