aposiopetic

adjective
relating to aposiopesis (abrupt speech break)
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˌsaɪoʊˈpɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæpəsɪəʊˈpɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of aposiopesis (a rhetorical device in which a speaker breaks off abruptly).

Examples

Aposiopetic speech
His speech became aposiopetic when he reached the most emotional part of his story.
Literary critics praised the novel's aposiopetic dialogue for its dramatic effect.
The author's aposiopetic style left readers guessing what the character truly intended to say.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aposiopesis - a rhetorical device where a sentence is deliberately broken off and left unfinished
