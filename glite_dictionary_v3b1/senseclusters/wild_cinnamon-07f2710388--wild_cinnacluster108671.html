wild cinnamon

noun
West Indian tree; source of bay rum
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / waɪld ˈsɪnəmɪn /; / waɪld ˈsɪnəmən / (BrE)
an aromatic tree or shrub called wild cinnamon, known for its fragrant leaves or bark (sometimes used to make bay rum or for its scent)

Examples

A wild cinnamon tree
He planted a wild cinnamon tree in his garden for its fragrant leaves.
In the Caribbean, wild cinnamon is prized for its aromatic oils.
The leaves of the wild cinnamon are distilled to produce the famous bay rum fragrance.
Botanists discovered a rare species of wild cinnamon deep in the jungle.
The aromatic bark of wild cinnamon is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
The forest was filled with the scent of wild cinnamon after the rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cinnamon - aromatic spice from the inner bark of several tree species
