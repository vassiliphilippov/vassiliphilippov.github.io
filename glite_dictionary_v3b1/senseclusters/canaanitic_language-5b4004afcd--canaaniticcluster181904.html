Canaanitic language

noun
ancient Semitic language group
Pronunciation: / ˌkænəˈnɪtɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌkanəˈnɪtɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A group of closely related Semitic languages spoken in the ancient Levant, including Phoenician and Hebrew.

Examples

Canaanitic language group
Phoenician, a Canaanitic language, was used along the Mediterranean coast.
Scholars have identified ancient Hebrew as a Canaanitic language spoken in the Levant.
The study of inscriptions helped linguists classify Ugaritic as different from the Canaanitic language group.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Phoenician - an ancient language of the Canaanite group
  • degree higher: Semitic language - a family of languages including Hebrew, Arabic, Aramaic, and others
