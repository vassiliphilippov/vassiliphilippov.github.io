ingrown toenail

noun
toenail growing into the flesh
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌɡroʊn ˈtoʊˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌɡrəʊn ˈtəʊˌneɪl / (BrE)
A toenail whose edge or corner grows into the surrounding flesh, often causing pain, redness, and swelling.

Examples

An ingrown toenail infection
After treatment, her ingrown toenail finally healed and she could walk comfortably again.
He needed to see a doctor because his ingrown toenail was causing severe pain.
Wearing tight shoes can increase the risk of developing an ingrown toenail.
