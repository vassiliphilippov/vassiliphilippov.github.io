regrade

noun
grading again
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈriːˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
the act or process of grading again, such as assigning a new mark or altering the slope or surface of land

Examples

A regrade request
The teacher agreed to a regrade after the student pointed out an error in the marking.
A major regrade of the hillside was needed to prevent landslides after heavy rain.
The city council approved a regrade of several streets to improve traffic flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rescore - to assign a new score or grade
  • synonym: reshape - to change the shape or form of something
