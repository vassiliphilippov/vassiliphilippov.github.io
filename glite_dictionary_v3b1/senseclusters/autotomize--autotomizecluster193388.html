autotomize

verb
cause a body part to be shed (autotomy)
6.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈtɑtəˌmaɪz /; / ɔːˈtɑːtəmaɪz / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒtəˌmaɪz /; / ɔːˈtɒtəmaɪz / (BrE)
(of an animal or body part) to cause a body part to be shed or detached (autotomy), typically deliberately as a defense mechanism or in response to injury.

Examples

To autotomize a leg
Scientists studied how certain insects autotomize legs as a survival mechanism.
Some lizards can autotomize their tails to distract predators and escape.
The crab was observed to autotomize its limb when threatened by a larger animal.
Certain sea slugs are able to autotomise parts of their bodies when threatened.
Some species of lizards can autotomise their tails to escape from predators.
The crab may autotomise a limb if it becomes trapped or severely damaged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autotomy - the spontaneous shedding of a body part by an animal, usually to escape a predator
