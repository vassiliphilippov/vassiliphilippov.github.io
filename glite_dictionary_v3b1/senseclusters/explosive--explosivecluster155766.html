explosive

noun
a substance causing rapid explosion
6.0 4.4
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈsploʊ.sɪv/ /; / /ɛk.ˈsploʊ.sɪv/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspləʊ.sɪv/ /; / /ɛk.ˈspləʊ.sɪv/ (BrE)
A chemical substance that undergoes a rapid reaction, producing gas and heat, especially when heated or struck, often used for blasting or as a weapon.

Examples

A powerful explosive
Dynamite is a well-known commercial explosive used in mining and construction.
Police discovered a small quantity of homemade explosive in the suspect's garage.
The engineers carefully handled the explosive to avoid any accidental ignition.
