drat

verb
to express mild annoyance
8.6
Pronunciation: / dræt / (AmE), / dræt / (BrE)
to express mild annoyance or frustration, often by saying 'drat' as an exclamation

Examples

To drat bad luck
She could not help but drat the broken printer when it jammed again.
He quietly dratted his luck after missing the last bus home.
Whenever things go wrong, my grandfather tends to drat under his breath.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bless - to wish good things or express approval
  • synonym: curse - to use words to express anger or annoyance
