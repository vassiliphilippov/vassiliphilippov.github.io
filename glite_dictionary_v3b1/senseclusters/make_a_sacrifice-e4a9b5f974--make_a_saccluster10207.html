make a sacrifice

verb
give up something for a greater good
Pronunciation: / meɪk ə ˈsækrəˌfaɪs / (AmE), / meɪk ə ˈsækrɪfʌɪs / (BrE)
to give up something valuable or important for the sake of a greater purpose or the benefit of others

Examples

To make a sacrifice for team
He was willing to make a sacrifice for the success of the team.
She made a sacrifice by leaving her dream job to care for her family.
Sometimes you have to make a sacrifice to achieve your long-term goals.
