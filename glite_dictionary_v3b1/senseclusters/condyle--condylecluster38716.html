condyle

noun
rounded bone bump at a joint
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnˌdaɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.daɪl / (BrE)
A rounded protuberance at the end of some bones, forming an articulation with another bone.

Examples

Damage to the mandibular condyle can affect jaw movement.
The femur has a large condyle that connects with the tibia at the knee joint.
The surgeon explained that the broken bone fragment was part of the lateral condyle.
