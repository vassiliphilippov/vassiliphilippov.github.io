back-blast

noun
rearward blast from a recoilless weapon
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈbakˌblɑːst / (BrE)
the forceful backward expulsion of gas or energy from the rear of a recoilless weapon when it is fired

Examples

The back-blast area
Always check behind you to ensure the back-blast area is clear before firing the weapon.
Soldiers are trained to avoid standing in the back-blast zone of an anti-tank launcher.
The missile's back-blast scorched the ground and could injure anyone standing too close.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backfire - unexpected explosion in the opposite direction, often in machinery or weapons
