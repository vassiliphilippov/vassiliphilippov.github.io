sneaking suspicion

noun
a slight, lingering feeling of doubt or mistrust
6.3 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsniː.kɪŋ sə.ˈspɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈsniː.kɪŋ sə.ˈspɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
a slight but persistent feeling that something may be true, especially something negative or worrying, often without definite proof

Examples

A sneaking suspicion
I had a sneaking suspicion that he wasn't telling me the whole story.
She couldn't shake the sneaking suspicion that something was about to go wrong.
There's a sneaking suspicion in the team that the manager will resign soon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - a firm conviction that something is the case
  • synonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
