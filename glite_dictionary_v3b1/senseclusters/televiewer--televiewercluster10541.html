televiewer

noun
borehole imaging device
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtel.ə.vjuː.ər/ /; / /ˈtel.ə.vjuːr/ (AmE), /ˈtel.ə.vjuː.ə/ (BrE)
a specialized instrument used to record continuous images of the inside wall of a borehole or well, often for geological or engineering analysis

Examples

A borehole televiewer
The geologist used a televiewer to examine the structure of the rock layers deep underground.
A televiewer provides detailed images that help engineers assess the stability of a well.
After lowering the televiewer into the borehole, they analyzed the fractures in the rock wall.
