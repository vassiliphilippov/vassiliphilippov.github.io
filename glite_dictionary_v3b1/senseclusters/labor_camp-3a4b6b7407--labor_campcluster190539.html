labor camp

noun
prison where inmates perform forced labor
5.8 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.bər kæmp/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.bə kæmp/ (BrE)
A prison or detention center where prisoners, especially political prisoners, are forced to work.

Examples

A labor camp
Many political dissidents were sent to labor camps during the regime.
Survivors from the labor camp recalled years of grueling work and poor conditions.
The existence of a secret labor camp was later confirmed by international organizations.

  • synonym: gulag - a system of labor camps maintained in the former Soviet Union
  • synonym: prison - a building where people are legally held as punishment for crimes
