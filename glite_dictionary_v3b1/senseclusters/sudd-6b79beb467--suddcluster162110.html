Sudd

proper noun
large South Sudan wetland
Pronunciation: / sʌd / (AmE), / sʌd / (BrE)
a vast swampy region in South Sudan formed by the White Nile, known as one of the world's largest wetlands

Examples

The Sudd wetland
The Sudd is home to a wide variety of birds and aquatic animals.
Seasonal flooding causes the Sudd to expand and cover thousands of square kilometers.
Scientists study the Sudd to understand its impact on the flow of the White Nile.
