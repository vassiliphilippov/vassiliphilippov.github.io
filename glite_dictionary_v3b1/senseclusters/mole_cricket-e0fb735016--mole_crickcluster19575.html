mole cricket

noun
burrowing insect with spade-like forelegs
7.2
Pronunciation: / moʊl ˈkrɪkɪt / (AmE), / məʊl ˈkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
a large, burrowing insect with spade-like forelegs, which digs in moist soil and feeds on plant roots

Examples

The mole cricket digs
Farmers often struggle to control mole cricket infestations that damage their crops.
The mole cricket uses its powerful forelegs to dig tunnels underground.
We noticed that the lawn had several holes made by a mole cricket.
