unannealed

adjective
not annealed; liable to crack or break
8.3 8.2
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ə.ˈniːld/ /; / /ən.ə.ˈniːld/ (AmE), /ʌn.ə.ˈniːld/ /; / /ən.ə.ˈniːld/ (BrE)
(of metal or glass) not having been annealed and therefore more likely to crack or fracture.

Examples

Unannealed steel
Always avoid using unannealed steel in construction, as it can crack easily.
Due to its unannealed state, the metal rod broke under pressure.
The unannealed glass was so brittle that it shattered at the slightest touch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annealed - (of metal or glass) having been subjected to annealing to reduce brittleness
