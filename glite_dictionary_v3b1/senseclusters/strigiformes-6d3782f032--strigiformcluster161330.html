Strigiformes

noun
the scientific order of owls
Pronunciation: / strɪˈdʒɪfɔːrˌmiːz / (AmE), / strɪˈdʒɪfɔːˌmiːz / (BrE)
the order of birds that comprises all owls, characterized by an upright posture, a large head, forward-facing eyes, and silent flight

Examples

The Strigiformes order
All members of the Strigiformes are adapted for nocturnal hunting.
Biologists grouped barn owls and typical owls together in the order Strigiformes.
The fossil record shows that Strigiformes have existed since at least the Paleocene era.
