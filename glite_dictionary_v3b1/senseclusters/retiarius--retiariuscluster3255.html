retiarius

noun
Roman net-fighter
Pronunciation: / ˌriːtiˈɛəriəs / (AmE), / ˌriːtiˈɑːriəs / (BrE)
a type of Roman gladiator who fought using a net and a trident, often against heavily armed opponents

Examples

The retiarius gladiator
The retiarius skillfully cast his net, hoping to trap his armored opponent.
Unlike other gladiators, the retiarius relied on speed and agility in the arena.
Spectators cheered as the retiarius dodged attacks and prepared to strike with his trident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gladiator - a person trained to fight in ancient Roman arenas
  • antonym: secutor - a type of Roman gladiator who often fought against the retiarius
