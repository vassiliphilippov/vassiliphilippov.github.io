patrial

noun
person with ancestral right to British citizenship
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtriəl / (AmE), / ˈpatrɪəl / (BrE)
a person who has the right to be considered a British citizen by virtue of the birth of a parent or grandparent in the United Kingdom or qualifying territory

Examples

A patrial citizen
Because his mother was born in London, he was a patrial under British law.
She applied as a patrial to establish her right of abode in the UK.
The Immigration Act of 1971 first introduced the concept of a patrial.
