blister copper

noun
impure copper with blistered surface
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈblɪs.tər ˈkɑː.pər/ (AmE), /ˈblɪs.tə ˈkɒp.ə/ (BrE)
an impure form of copper obtained during smelting, characterized by a black, blistered surface caused by trapped sulfur dioxide gas

Examples

blister copper surface
During smelting, blister copper forms as gases escape from the molten material.
The characteristic surface of blister copper results from trapped sulfur dioxide bubbles.
The refinery processes blister copper into pure metal for industrial use.
