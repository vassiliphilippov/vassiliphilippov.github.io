winnow

verb
select the best parts from a group
8.6 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.əʊ/ (BrE)
to separate or select the desirable or most important elements from a group, especially after careful examination

Examples

To winnow the choices
After reviewing hundreds of submissions, she finally winnowed them down to the top five.
The committee will winnow the proposals to select the strongest candidates for funding.
We need to winnow the irrelevant data before starting our analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cull - to select or gather the best or necessary items from a larger group
  • antonym: discard - to get rid of something as unwanted or unneeded
  • synonym: sift - to examine something thoroughly and select what is most important
