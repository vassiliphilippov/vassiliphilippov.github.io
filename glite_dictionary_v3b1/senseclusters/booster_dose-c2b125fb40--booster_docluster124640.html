booster dose

noun
an extra dose to prolong effectiveness
3.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbuÀêst…ôr do äs / (AmE), / ÀàbuÀêst…ô d…ô äs / (BrE)
an additional dose of a vaccine or medication given after the initial series to increase or prolong its effectiveness

Examples

A booster dose vaccine
After receiving his initial vaccination, he scheduled a booster dose for six months later.
Some countries now require travelers to show proof of a recent booster dose against certain diseases.
The doctor recommended a booster dose to maintain immunity.

Synonyms

    2.9 0.12/m

  • booster
    3.7 1.25/m
    booster is a short informal name people use for an extra vaccine or medication dose, while booster dose is the clear clinical term.
