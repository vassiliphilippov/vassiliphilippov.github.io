scintillating scotoma

noun
visual disturbance with shimmering border, often before migraine
8.2 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪ.tɪŋ skə.ˈtoʊ.mə/ /; / /ˈsɪn.tə.leɪ.tɪŋ skoʊ.ˈtoʊ.mə/ (AmE), /ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪ.tɪŋ skə.ˈtəʊ.mə/ /; / /ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪ.tɪŋ skɒ.ˈtəʊ.mə/ (BrE)
A localized area of reduced vision bordered by flashing or shimmering lights, typically indicating the onset of a migraine.

Examples

A scintillating scotoma before migraine
A scintillating scotoma often appears as a shimmering zigzag pattern in the vision before a migraine headache.
If you notice a scintillating scotoma, it's best to avoid bright lights and rest in a quiet place.
Many patients report seeing a scintillating scotoma shortly before experiencing any pain.
