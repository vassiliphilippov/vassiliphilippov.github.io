unsell

verb
to discourage purchase
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɛl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛl / (BrE)
to discourage someone from buying something or to make something less appealing to a buyer

Examples

To unsell a car
The salesperson accidentally unsold the car by mentioning its high repair costs.
Negative publicity can quickly unsell a product that was once popular.
If you focus too much on the drawbacks, you might unsell your own idea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something
  • antonym: sell - to persuade someone to buy something
