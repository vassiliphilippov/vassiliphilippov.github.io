aphemia

noun
loss of speech
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈfiː.mi.ə/ /; / /ə.ˈfɪ.mi.ə/ (AmE), /ə.ˈfiː.mi.ə/ /; / /ə.ˈfɪ.mi.ə/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which a person is unable to speak but can still understand language

Examples

Aphemia after stroke
After the stroke, the doctor diagnosed the patient with aphemia due to his inability to speak.
Despite her aphemia, she could still understand everything that was said to her.
The neurologist explained that aphemia affects speech but not language comprehension.
