oddish

adjective
mildly odd, slightly strange
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɒdɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat odd or slightly strange; having a mildly unusual quality

Examples

A oddish feeling
Her choice of decorations was a bit oddish, but it suited her personality.
His behavior at dinner seemed oddish rather than truly bizarre.
The film has an oddish tone that sets it apart from other comedies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal - usual or standard
  • degree higher: odd - strange or unusual
  • synonym: peculiar - unusual or strange
