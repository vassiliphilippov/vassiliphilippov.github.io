cathead

noun
large southern biscuit
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈkætˌhɛd / (BrE)
a large, round biscuit or scone, especially common in the southern United States, named for its size

Examples

A warm cathead biscuit
She warmed a buttered cathead and placed it beside the bowl of collard greens.
At breakfast, he enjoyed a fresh cathead with homemade jam and coffee.
The bakery is famous for its giant cathead biscuits served hot every morning.
