reformulate

verb
develop or express again in a new way
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɔːrmjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɔːmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to develop or express something in a new or improved way, especially a theory, plan, or idea

Examples

To reformulate a plan
After the feedback, she had to reformulate her approach to the project.
Many companies reformulate their products to meet new regulations.
The researchers decided to reformulate their hypothesis based on the new data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formulate - to create or devise methodically (a strategy or proposal)
