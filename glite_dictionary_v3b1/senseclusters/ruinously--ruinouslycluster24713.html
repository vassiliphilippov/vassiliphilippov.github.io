ruinously

adverb
in a way causing great ruin or damage
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈruːɪnəsli / (AmE), / ˈruːɪnəsli / (BrE)
In a way that causes ruin or serious damage; to an extent that can result in destruction or great harm.

Examples

Borrow ruinously high
He borrowed money at ruinously high interest rates, which led to his bankruptcy.
Many people were unable to afford the ruinously high rents in the city.
The restaurant was forced to close after a series of ruinously expensive repairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disastrously - in a disastrous manner; causing great harm or damage
