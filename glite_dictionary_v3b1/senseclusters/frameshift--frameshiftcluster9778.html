frameshift

verb
to alter genetic reading frame
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfreɪmˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈfreɪmˌʃɪft / (BrE)
to cause a change in the reading frame of a DNA or RNA sequence by inserting or deleting nucleotides, altering how the genetic code is read downstream

Examples

To frameshift a gene
A single nucleotide insertion can frameshift the entire gene sequence, leading to a nonfunctional protein.
Scientists discovered that certain chemicals can frameshift DNA during replication.
If you frameshift the sequence, the resulting protein may be completely different from the original.

Related Terms

  • antonym: correct - to restore something to its original or proper state
  • synonym: mutate - to change the genetic material of an organism
