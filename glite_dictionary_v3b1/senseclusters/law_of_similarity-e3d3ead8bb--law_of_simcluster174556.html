law of similarity

noun
Gestalt psychology principle of perceptual grouping
5.3
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv sɪˌmɪləˈrɪɾi / (AmE), / lɔː əv sɪˌmɪləˈrɪti / (BrE)
a principle in Gestalt psychology stating that elements that are similar to each other are perceived as forming a group or pattern

Examples

The law of similarity principle
According to the law of similarity, shapes that look alike will be grouped together by the human brain.
Designers often use the law of similarity to create visual organization in websites and interfaces.
The law of similarity helps explain why people perceive matching uniforms as indicating membership in the same team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: law of closure - Gestalt principle that incomplete figures are perceived as complete
  • synonym: law of proximity - Gestalt principle that elements close together are perceived as a group
