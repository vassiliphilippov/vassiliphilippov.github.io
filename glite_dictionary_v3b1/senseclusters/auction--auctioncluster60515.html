auction

noun
a former variant of bridge (card game)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔkʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːkʃən / (BrE)
A form of the card game bridge, in which tricks made in excess of the contract are scored toward game; now generally replaced by contract bridge.

Examples

Auction bridge game
Before contract bridge became popular, many clubs organized auction tournaments.
Learning auction helps you appreciate the evolution of modern bridge.
My grandfather used to play auction with his friends every weekend.
