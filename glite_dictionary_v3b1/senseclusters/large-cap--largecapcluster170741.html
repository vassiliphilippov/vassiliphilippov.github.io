large-cap

adjective
of stocks from high market capitalization companies
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑrdʒ kæp / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ kæp / (BrE)
(of stocks) belonging to companies with a market capitalization of five billion dollars or more; relating to such companies.

Examples

Large-cap stocks
Many investors prefer large-cap stocks because they are considered more stable and reliable.
She analyzed trends in large-cap equities before making her portfolio decisions.
The fund shifted its focus from large-cap companies to emerging mid-cap firms this year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mid-cap - stocks of companies with a medium market capitalization
  • antonym: small-cap - stocks of companies with a small market capitalization
