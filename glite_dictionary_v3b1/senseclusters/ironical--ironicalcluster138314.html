ironical

adjective
humorously ironic or mocking
9.7
Pronunciation: / aɪˈrɑnɪkəl / (AmE), / aɪˈrɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
using or characterized by irony, especially in a humorous or mocking way.

Examples

Ironical comment
His speech was so ironical that many people failed to realize he was joking.
She gave him an ironical smile when he apologized for being late.
The ironical tone of the article made the criticism less direct but more effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ironic - using irony; characterized by irony
  • synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • antonym: sincere - genuine, not ironic or mocking
