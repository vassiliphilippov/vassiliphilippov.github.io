reinscribe

verb
to revise significance
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to give a person, group, text, or practice a new or revised place or meaning within cultural or historical narratives

Examples

To reinscribe a story
The new documentary seeks to reinscribe indigenous voices into the country's official history.
Scholars often reinscribe classic texts to reflect modern perspectives and values.
The artist's work attempts to reinscribe traditional symbols with contemporary meanings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erase - to remove or eliminate something completely
  • synonym: reinterpret - to explain or understand something in a new or different way
