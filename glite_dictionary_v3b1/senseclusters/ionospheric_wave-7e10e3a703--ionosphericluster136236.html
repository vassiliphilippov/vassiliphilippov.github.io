ionospheric wave

noun
radio wave reflected by the ionosphere
5.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /aɪ.ə.noʊ.ˈsfɪr.ɪk weɪv/ /; / /aɪ.ə.nə.ˈsfɪr.ɪk weɪv/ (AmE), /aɪ.ə.nə.ˈsfɪə.rɪk weɪv/ /; / /aɪ.ə.nəʊ.ˈsfɪə.rɪk weɪv/ (BrE)
A radio wave transmitted into the sky that is reflected back to Earth by the ionosphere.

Examples

Ionospheric waves reflect signals
Radio operators rely on ionospheric waves to transmit signals over long distances.
Scientists study ionospheric waves to better understand atmospheric conditions.
The strength of an ionospheric wave can vary depending on solar activity.
