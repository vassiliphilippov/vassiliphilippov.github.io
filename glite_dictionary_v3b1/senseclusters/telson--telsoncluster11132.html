telson

noun
arthropod tail segment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛlsən / (AmE), / ˈtɛlsən / (BrE)
the last segment or tail part of the body in certain arthropods, such as crustaceans, scorpions, or shrimp

Examples

The telson segment
The scorpion's telson contains its venomous stinger.
In many crustaceans, the telson helps with swimming and balance.
Scientists examined the shape of the telson to identify the species of the fossil.
The shrimp uses its telson to help steer while swimming rapidly away from danger.
Scientists examined the structure of the telson to identify different species of crabs.
The telson and uropods together form the tail fan in many crustaceans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abdomen - the rear part of an arthropod's body
  • synonym: uropod - a paired appendage on the last segment of crustaceans, forming part of the tail fan
