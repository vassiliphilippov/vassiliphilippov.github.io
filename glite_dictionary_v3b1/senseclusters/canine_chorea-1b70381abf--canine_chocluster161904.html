canine chorea

noun
neurological disorder in dogs causing jerky movements
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪnaɪn kɔˈriə / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnaɪn kəˈriːə / (BrE)
A neurological disorder in dogs characterized by involuntary, jerky movements, similar to chorea in humans.

Examples

Canine chorea symptoms
Research into canine chorea has improved treatment options for affected animals.
Symptoms of canine chorea often include rapid, involuntary muscle twitching in the legs.
The veterinarian diagnosed the dog with canine chorea after observing its unusual movements.
