kinkajou

noun
arboreal mammal of Central/South America
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪŋkəˌdʒu / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋkədʒuː / (BrE)
a small, nocturnal mammal from Central and South America with a long prehensile tail, related to raccoons

Examples

A kinkajou animal
A kinkajou was spotted feeding on bananas in the wildlife reserve.
The kinkajou uses its long tail to balance as it climbs through the rainforest canopy.
Unlike many mammals, the kinkajou is most active at night when it searches for fruit.
Many children are fascinated by the playful nature of the kinkajou at the zoo.
She spotted a kinkajou curled up in the canopy during her rainforest trek.
The kinkajou used its long tail to hang from a tree branch while eating fruit.
