townland

noun
Irish land division
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtaʊnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈtaʊnˌlænd / (BrE)
a small rural division of land, especially in Ireland, used as a traditional unit of area

Examples

A townland in Ireland
Her family has lived in the same townland for generations.
Each townland in the region has its own unique name and history.
The farm is located in a quiet townland just outside the village.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: county - a larger administrative division of land
  • synonym: parish - an area served by a church or used as an administrative district
