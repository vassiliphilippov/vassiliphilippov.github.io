hangfire

noun
firearm discharge delay
Pronunciation: / ˈhæŋˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈhæŋˌfaɪə / (BrE)
an unexpected delay between pulling the trigger of a firearm and its discharge

Examples

A gun hangfire
After pulling the trigger, the soldier waited for a possible hangfire before checking the weapon.
A hangfire can be dangerous if the shooter assumes the gun is safe too soon.
Always keep the firearm pointed in a safe direction in case of a hangfire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: misfire - a failure of a firearm to discharge when the trigger is pulled
