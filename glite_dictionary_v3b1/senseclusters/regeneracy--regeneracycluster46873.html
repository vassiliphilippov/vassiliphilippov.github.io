regeneracy

noun
process of renewal
8.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (BrE)
the process or state of being renewed, restored, or regenerated, especially after damage or decline

Examples

City regeneracy
The regeneracy of the city after the earthquake inspired hope among its residents.
Scientists study the regeneracy of certain plants that can recover quickly after being damaged.
Community efforts led to the regeneracy of the neglected park over several years.
The city experienced a period of regeneracy after years of neglect and decline.
Many people hoped for moral regeneracy following the new leadership.
Environmental groups work toward the regeneracy of damaged ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decay - the process of gradual decline or deterioration
  • synonym: regeneration - the act or process of being renewed or restored
