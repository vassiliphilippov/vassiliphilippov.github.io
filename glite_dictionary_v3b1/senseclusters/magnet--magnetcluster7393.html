magnet

noun
object that attracts iron and produces a magnetic field
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡ.nət / (AmE), / ˈmæɡ.nɪt / (BrE)
a piece of iron or another material that attracts iron or steel and produces a magnetic field

Examples

A magnet on fridge
Children love playing with magnets because they stick to the refrigerator door.
Keep your credit cards away from the magnet or their strips might be damaged.
The engineer showed us how an electric current can turn a metal bar into a powerful magnet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demagnetize - to remove magnetic properties from a magnet
  • synonym: electromagnet - a type of magnet in which the magnetic field is produced by an electric current
