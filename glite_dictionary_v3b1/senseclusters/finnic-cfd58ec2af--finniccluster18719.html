Finnic

noun
language group including Finnish and Estonian
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɪnɪk / (BrE)
A branch of the Finno-Ugric language family including Finnish and Estonian, but not Hungarian.

Examples

The Finnic languages
Finnish and Estonian both belong to the Finnic branch of languages.
Scholars study the Finnic languages to understand their historical development.
The Finnic languages are spoken primarily around the Baltic Sea.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Finno-Ugric - a family of languages including Finnish, Estonian, and Hungarian
