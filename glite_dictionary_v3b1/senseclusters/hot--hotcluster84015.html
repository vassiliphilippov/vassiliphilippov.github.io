hot

adjective
newly received or most recent (esp. news)
Pronunciation: / hɑt / (AmE), / hɒt / (BrE)
newly received, fresh, or very recent (especially news or information)

Examples

Hot news
She loves chasing hot news and breaking it before anyone else.
The reporter received some hot information about the scandal just before the press conference.
This story is too hot to ignore—everyone in the office is talking about it.
Reporters rushed to share the hot news story as soon as it broke.
The detective followed a hot lead to the suspect's hideout.
The dogs picked up a hot scent and ran deeper into the forest.
