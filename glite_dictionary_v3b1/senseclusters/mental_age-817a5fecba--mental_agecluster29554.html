mental age

noun
level of intellectual development (as an age)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛn.təl eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.təl eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the level of intellectual development of a person as measured by standardized intelligence tests, expressed as the age at which an average person reaches the same level

Examples

Mental age test score
A person with a mental age of 10 may perform tasks similar to an average 10-year-old.
Tests revealed that her mental age had improved significantly after one year of therapy.
The child's mental age was found to be higher than his chronological age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: IQ - measure of intelligence as a quotient of mental to chronological age
  • antonym: chronological age - actual age of a person counted in years
