uncandid

adjective
not honest or fair
9.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkændɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkændɪd / (BrE)
not honest, open, or straightforward; showing bias or hiding the truth

Examples

An uncandid remark
The manager's uncandid remarks made the team feel unfairly treated.
An uncandid review can damage the reputation of a business.
She was criticized for her uncandid response during the interview.
His uncandid answers made the interviewers suspicious of his motives.
It is difficult to resolve issues when people are uncandid about their feelings.
She regretted her uncandid remarks after realizing they had caused confusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - showing unfair preference or prejudice
  • antonym: candid - honest and impartial; open and fair
  • synonym: dishonest - not truthful or trustworthy
  • antonym: impartial - treating all sides equally; fair and unbiased
  • synonym: partial - favoring one side over another; not impartial
