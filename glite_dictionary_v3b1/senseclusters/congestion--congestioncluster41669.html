congestion

noun
excessive crowding or overfilling
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
A condition of overcrowding, particularly when movement is hindered or slowed, such as with traffic or people.

Examples

Traffic congestion
During the holidays, shopping centers often suffer from severe congestion.
Morning congestion on the main highway caused commuters to be late for work.
The city is taking steps to reduce congestion in the downtown area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearance - the removal or reduction of congestion
  • synonym: jam - a situation where movement is stopped or slowed by crowding
