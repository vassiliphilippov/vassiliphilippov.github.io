ventricular fold

noun
upper fold in the larynx above the vocal cord
Pronunciation: / vɛnˈtrɪkjələr foʊld / (AmE), / vɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə fəʊld / (BrE)
either of the upper two folds of tissue in the larynx, located above the true vocal cords and not usually involved in vocalization

Examples

The ventricular fold tissue
Damage to the ventricular fold can sometimes cause a sensation of throat tightness.
During the examination, the doctor observed swelling in the patient's ventricular fold.
Unlike the true vocal cords, the ventricular folds do not vibrate to produce sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vocal cord - either of two folds of tissue in the larynx involved in sound production
