Luminal

noun
long-acting barbiturate sedative (trade name)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈluːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəl / (BrE)
A long-acting barbiturate drug formerly used as a sedative and anticonvulsant, also known as phenobarbital.

Examples

Luminal drug
After a long history of insomnia, he was given Luminal as a sedative.
The doctor prescribed Luminal to help control the patient's seizures.
The use of Luminal in epilepsy treatment has declined due to newer medications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phenobarbital - a barbiturate used as a sedative and to control seizures
