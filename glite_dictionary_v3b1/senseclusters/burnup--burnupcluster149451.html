burnup

noun
cumulative work-over-time chart (Agile)
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.nʌp/ (AmE), /ˈbɜː.nʌp/ (BrE)
(in project management, especially Agile) a chart that plots cumulative work completed over time, used to track progress toward a sprint or release and to show scope changes.

Examples

Burnup chart
The team studied the burnup chart to evaluate progress toward the sprint goal.
During the meeting, the project manager updated the burnup to reflect completed user stories.
New members learned to read the burnup and compare scope changes across iterations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: burndown chart - a chart showing remaining work over time; contrasts with a burnup chart, which shows completed work
