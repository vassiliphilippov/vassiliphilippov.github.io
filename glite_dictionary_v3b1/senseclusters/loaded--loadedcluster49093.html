loaded

adjective
filled with a great quantity
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊdɪd / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪd / (BrE)
full or filled with as much as possible (such as objects or ammunition)

Examples

Loaded with boxes
After the move, the truck was loaded with boxes from floor to ceiling.
She handed me a loaded shopping bag that was too heavy to lift easily.
The table was loaded with delicious cakes and pastries.
Always assume a loaded gun is dangerous, even if you're sure it's safe.
He checked to make sure the rifle was loaded before going hunting.
The police found a loaded pistol in the suspect's bag.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laden - heavily loaded or weighed down
  • antonym: unloaded - not charged with ammunition (of weapons)
