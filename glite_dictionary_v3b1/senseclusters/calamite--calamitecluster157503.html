calamite

noun
fossil plant stem
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.əˌmaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əˌmaɪt / (BrE)
an extinct, tree-like plant from the Carboniferous period, known mainly from fossils found in coal deposits

Examples

Fossil calamite plant
The paleontologist carefully examined the calamite embedded in the ancient rock layer.
A well-preserved calamite can reveal much about prehistoric plant life.
Many coal mines contain fossils of calamite plants from millions of years ago.
Fossils of calamite are often found in coal deposits.
The museum displayed a large calamite fossil from the Carboniferous period.
Scientists study calamite remains to learn about ancient swamp forests.

  • synonym: lycopod - an ancient group of vascular plants, some of which are now extinct
