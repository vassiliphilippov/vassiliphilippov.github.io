ravelin

noun
triangular fortification
Pronunciation: / ˈrævəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈravəlɪn / (BrE)
a triangular fortification built outside a main fort to protect it from attack

Examples

A ravelin fortification
The soldiers retreated to the ravelin as the enemy advanced toward the main walls.
Engineers designed a new ravelin to strengthen the fortress's outer defenses.
During the siege, the defenders used the ravelin to launch counterattacks against the attackers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bastion - a projecting part of a fortification
  • synonym: redoubt - a small, often temporary, defensive fortification
