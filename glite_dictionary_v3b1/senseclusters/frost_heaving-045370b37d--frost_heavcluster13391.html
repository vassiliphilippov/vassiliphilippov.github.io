frost heaving

noun
upward shift due to soil freezing
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /frɔːst ˈhiː.vɪŋ/ (AmE), /frɒst ˈhiː.vɪŋ/ (BrE)
An upward movement of soil or pavement caused by the expansion of freezing moisture in the ground.

Examples

Severe frost heaving
Severe frost heaving can damage roads and sidewalks during harsh winters.
The garden path was uneven after a season of frost heaving.
To prevent frost heaving, engineers often use gravel or sand under foundations.
