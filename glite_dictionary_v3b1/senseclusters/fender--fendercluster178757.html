fender

noun
cushioning device to prevent damage from impact
7.3 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfen.dər/ (AmE), /ˈfen.də/ (BrE)
a cushion-like device, often made of rubber or rope, placed against a boat, dock, or other structure to absorb shock and prevent damage from impact

Examples

Boat fender
As the yacht approached the pier, the crew prepared the fenders to soften the impact.
The marina requires every boat to have at least one fender on each side.
We tied the fender to the side of the boat to avoid damage while docking.
