washiness

noun
watery quality
8.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ëÀê Éin…ôs / (AmE), / Ààw…í Éin…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being watery, thin, or lacking substance or richness

Examples

Washiness of the paint
The washiness of the paint made the colors appear faded on the canvas.
Critics complained about the washiness of the novel's plot, saying it lacked depth.
He was disappointed by the washiness of the coffee, which tasted weak and watery.

Synonyms

  • wateriness
    5.6 0.00/m
    wateriness feels watery and diluted in food or ideas, while washiness suggests a lack of substance and richness.
  • runniness
    6.6 0.00/m
    runniness means a liquid flows easily and is thin, while washiness emphasizes being weak or lacking flavor or richness.
  • thinness
    3.2 0.01/m
    thinness describes being low in thickness or density, while washiness implies watery thinness plus a lack of substance or richness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: richness - the quality of being full, strong, or concentrated
  • synonym: thinness - the quality of being not thick or dense
