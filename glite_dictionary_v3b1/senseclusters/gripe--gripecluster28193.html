gripe

verb
to complain persistently
7.5
Pronunciation: / ɡraɪp / (AmE), / ɡraɪp / (BrE)
to complain persistently, especially about something minor or trivial

Examples

To gripe all day
Instead of working, he would rather gripe about his assignments all day.
She tends to gripe about the weather whenever it rains.
Stop griping and help us solve the problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
