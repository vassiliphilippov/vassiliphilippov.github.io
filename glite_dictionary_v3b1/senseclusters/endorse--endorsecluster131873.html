endorse

verb
sign a cheque or bill to transfer it
3.8 5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɔrs / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɔːs / (BrE)
to sign (a cheque, bill, or other document) to make it payable to someone other than the stated payee or to evidence legal transfer

Examples

To endorse a check
He forgot to endorse the back of the check, so the bank would not accept it.
The lawyer explained how to properly endorse the negotiable instrument.
You must endorse the cheque before depositing it in your account.

Related Terms
