zoomorphism

noun
attribution of animal traits to a deity or being
Pronunciation: / zuˈɔːmɔrˌfɪzəm /; / zoʊˈmɔːrfɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌzəʊ.əˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm /; / zəʊˈmɔːfɪzəm / (BrE)
the representation of animals or the attribution of animal forms or characteristics to gods, humans, or objects, especially in art or literature

Examples

Zoomorphism in art
Ancient Egyptian religion is well known for its use of zoomorphism in representing gods as animals.
Literature often employs zoomorphism to illustrate deities' powers through animal traits.
The artist's sculptures are striking examples of zoomorphism, blending human and animal features.

In literature, zoomorphism can be seen when characters are described with animal traits.
The artist's use of zoomorphism gives the sculpture a wild and mysterious appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anthropomorphism - attribution of human characteristics to non-human entities
  • synonym: therianthropy - the mythological ability to change from human to animal form
