refilm

verb
to make new film version
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɪlm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɪlm / (BrE)
to make a new film version of an earlier movie or story, often with changes to the cast or script

Examples

To refilm a classic
The director decided to refilm the classic novel with a new cast and updated script.
After negative reviews, the studio chose to refilm several key scenes before the movie's release.
They plan to refilm the story to appeal to a younger audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to change a story so it can be presented in a different form
  • antonym: original - the first or earliest version of a film or story
  • synonym: remake - to make a new version of an existing film or story
