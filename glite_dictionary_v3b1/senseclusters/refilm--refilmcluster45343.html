refilm

verb
to make new film version
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɪlm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɪlm / (BrE)
to make a new film version of an earlier movie or story, often with changes to the cast or script

Examples

To refilm a classic

They plan to refilm the story to appeal to a younger audience.
