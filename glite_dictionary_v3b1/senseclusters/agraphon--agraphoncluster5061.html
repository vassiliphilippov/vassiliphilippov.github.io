agraphon

noun
saying of Jesus outside the canonical gospels
Pronunciation: / əˈɡræfɒn / (AmE), / əˈɡræfɒn / (BrE)
(usually in plural form agrapha) A saying attributed to Jesus that is not recorded in the canonical gospels but is found in other New Testament or early Christian writings.

Examples

An agraphon saying
He cited an agraphon found in an early church father's writings during his lecture.
Scholars often debate whether a particular agraphon is authentic or a later invention.
The collection of agrapha provides a broader perspective on early Christian teachings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: canonical - included in the list of sacred books officially accepted as genuine
  • synonym: logion - a brief saying or quotation, especially of Jesus, often found in non-canonical sources
