Myxobacteriaceae

noun
family of soil-dwelling bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪk.soʊˌbækˌtɪəriˈeɪsi.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌmɪksəʊˌbæktɪəriˈeɪsɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
a family of bacteria, typically found in soils and on dung, known for their complex life cycles and social behavior

Examples

Myxobacteriaceae bacteria family
Myxobacteriaceae play an important role in the decomposition of organic matter in soils.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Myxobacteriaceae family in tropical environments.
Some Myxobacteriaceae can form fruiting bodies when food is scarce.
