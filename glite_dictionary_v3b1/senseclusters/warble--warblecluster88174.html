warble

verb
sing or play with trills or quavers
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑrbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɑːbəl /; / ˈwɔːbəl / (BrE)
to sing or play music with trills, quavering, or rapidly changing notes

Examples

To warble a song
During the concert, the flutist skillfully warbled each note.
She loves to warble her favorite tune with playful trills.
The bird began to warble joyfully as the sun rose over the meadow.
He loves to warble popular songs in the shower, hitting all the playful notes.
She began to warble a joyful tune as she walked through the garden.
The bird warbled high in the branches, delighting everyone nearby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trill - rapid alternation between two adjacent musical notes
  • synonym: whistle - to produce a clear, high-pitched sound by forcing air through the lips
  • synonym: yodel - to sing by rapidly alternating between normal voice and falsetto
