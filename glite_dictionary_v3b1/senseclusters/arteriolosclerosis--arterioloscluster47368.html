arteriolosclerosis

noun
hardening of arterioles
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌtɪriˌoʊloʊsklɪˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriˌəʊləʊsklɪˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A condition in which the walls of the arterioles become thickened and hardened, often leading to reduced blood flow.

Examples

Arteriolosclerosis disease
Chronic hypertension can result in arteriolosclerosis, which impairs blood flow to various organs.
Researchers are studying the risk factors that contribute to arteriolosclerosis in elderly patients.
The biopsy confirmed the presence of arteriolosclerosis in the kidney tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arteriosclerosis - thickening and hardening of the walls of the arteries
