come in

verb
become fashionable; start being in style
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɪn / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn / (BrE)
to become fashionable or popular; to start being in style

Examples

Shorts come in style
Long skirts often come in during the spring fashion season.
That style of music seems to come in and out every decade.
When bright colors come in, the stores sell out quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be in - to be considered fashionable or stylish at a given time
  • antonym: go out - to fall out of fashion; to stop being popular or in style
