respiration

noun
process of energy release in living cells
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /res.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /res.pɪ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /res.pɪ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /res.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The biological process by which organisms obtain energy from organic molecules, producing carbon dioxide and transferring energy within cells.

Examples

Cellular respiration process
During aerobic respiration, cells use oxygen to break down glucose and release energy.
Proper respiration is essential for the survival of all complex organisms.
Scientists measured the rate of respiration in the plant leaves under different light conditions.
