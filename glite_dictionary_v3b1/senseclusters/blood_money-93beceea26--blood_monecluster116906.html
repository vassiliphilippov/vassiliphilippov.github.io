blood money

noun
compensation for a killing
9.0
Pronunciation: / blʌd ˈmʌni / (AmE), / blʌd ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money paid as compensation to the family of someone who has been killed, especially in cultures or legal systems where such payments are customary.

Examples

Blood money compensation
In some legal systems, blood money may be offered as an alternative to criminal prosecution.
The family refused to accept blood money for the loss of their son.
The tradition of paying blood money has existed in various cultures throughout history.
