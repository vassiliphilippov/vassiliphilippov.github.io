pallium

noun
ancient Roman men's cloak
Pronunciation: / ˈpæliəm / (AmE), / ˈpalɪəm / (BrE)
a cloak or mantle worn by men in ancient Rome, typically draped over the shoulders

Examples

Roman pallium
Archaeological finds include fragments of what was once a richly decorated pallium.
In ancient Rome, the pallium was often made of wool and draped elegantly.
The senator adjusted his pallium before stepping onto the rostrum.
