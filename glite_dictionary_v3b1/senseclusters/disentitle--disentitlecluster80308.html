disentitle

verb
to remove a title
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈtaɪtəl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtaɪtəl / (BrE)
to take away someone's right, title, rank, or eligibility for something

Examples

To disentitle a player
The organization decided to disentitle the athlete after the rules violation was confirmed.
If a judge finds misconduct, they may disentitle someone from holding their honorary position.
The board voted unanimously to disentitle the company of its award due to ethical breaches.
A criminal conviction may disentitle a person to vote in some countries.
Failure to pay membership fees could disentitle you to club benefits.
The new policy will disentitle some employees from receiving overtime pay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deprive - to take something away from someone
  • synonym: disqualify - to make someone ineligible for something
  • antonym: entitle - to give a right, title, or honor to someone
  • synonym: strip - to take away a rank, title, or possession
