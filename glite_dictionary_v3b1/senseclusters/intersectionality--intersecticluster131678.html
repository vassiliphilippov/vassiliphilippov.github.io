intersectionality

noun
overlapping social identities
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˌsɛkʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪntəsɛkʃəˈnæləti / (BrE)
the idea or framework that different social identities (such as race, gender, and class) overlap and combine to create unique experiences of discrimination or privilege

Examples

Intersectionality theory
The concept of intersectionality helps explain why some people face multiple forms of discrimination.
Her research uses intersectionality to explore how race and gender affect access to education.
Understanding intersectionality is important for creating fairer social policies.
The concept of intersectionality helps us understand how race and gender can combine to shape people's lives.
Many activists use intersectionality to address the unique challenges faced by people with multiple marginalized identities.
Understanding intersectionality is important for creating fairer policies in workplaces and schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discrimination - unfair treatment based on group membership
  • synonym: identity politics - political positions based on the interests of social groups
