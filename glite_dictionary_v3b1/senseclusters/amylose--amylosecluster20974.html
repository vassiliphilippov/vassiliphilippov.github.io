amylose

noun
plant starch molecule
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæmɪˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈæmɪləʊs / (BrE)
a type of starch molecule made of long, unbranched chains of glucose units found in plants

Examples

amylose starch molecule
Scientists analyzed the amylose content in different types of rice to study their nutritional value.
Potatoes are rich in amylose, which affects how they cook and taste.
The structure of amylose allows it to form helical shapes in solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amylopectin - a branched form of starch molecule found in plants
  • antonym: cellulose - a complex carbohydrate forming the main part of plant cell walls
