Arnold-Chiari deformity

noun
brain protrusion congenital deformity
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.nəld ki.ˈɑːr.iː dɪ.ˈfɔːr.mə.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.nəld ki.ˈɑː.riː dɪ.ˈfɔː.mɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
A congenital condition in which parts of the brain, especially the cerebellum, protrude through an opening in the skull.

Examples

Arnold-Chiari deformity diagnosis
Early diagnosis of Arnold-Chiari deformity can help prevent serious complications.
MRI scans revealed an Arnold-Chiari deformity at the base of the patient's skull.
The neurosurgeon explained that the symptoms were caused by an Arnold-Chiari deformity.
