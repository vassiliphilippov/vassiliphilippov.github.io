multicentric

adjective
from multiple centers
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
involving or originating from several centers or locations

Examples

A multicentric trial
The researchers conducted a multicentric trial to ensure the results were widely applicable.
A multicentric approach allows for data collection from diverse populations.
The disease was found to be multicentric, affecting several organs at once.
The doctor explained that the lymphoma was multicentric, affecting several lymph nodes throughout the body.
A multicentric tumor requires a different treatment approach than a single localized mass.
Researchers studied the spread of multicentric lesions in patients with advanced disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralized - controlled or organized from a single center
  • synonym: multifocal - arising from several foci, often within the same organ
  • synonym: multinodal - having or involving multiple nodes or points
  • antonym: unifocal - originating from a single location or focus
