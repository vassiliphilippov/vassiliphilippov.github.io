find one's tribe

verb
discover a group with shared values or interests
4.0
Pronunciation: / faɪnd wʌnz traɪb / (AmE), / faɪnd wʌnz traɪb / (BrE)
to discover a group of people with whom one feels a strong sense of belonging or shared identity, especially due to similar interests or values

Examples

To find one's tribe quickly
After joining the hiking club, she finally found her tribe and felt at home.
He moved to New York to find his tribe among other aspiring artists.
Many people search for years before they find their tribe in a new city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stand out - be noticeably different from others in a group
