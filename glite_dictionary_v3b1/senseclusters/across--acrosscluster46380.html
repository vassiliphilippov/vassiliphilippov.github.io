across

adverb
to the opposite side
1.2
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
from one side to the other side; to or on the opposite side

Examples

Look across the street
He looked across and waved at his parents in the stands.
She ran across to say hello to her friend on the other side of the street.
The dog swam across to the far bank of the river.
Light streamed across the room from the open window.
She drew a line across the page to separate the sections.
The river was too wide to swim across.

Related Terms

  • synonym: over - from one side to the other side of something higher or covering something
