mutable

adjective
liable to genetic mutation
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
(biology) capable of undergoing genetic mutation; liable to change genetically.

Examples

Mutable genes
Research focuses on the mutable genes of bacteria that adapt to antibiotics.
Some viruses are especially mutable, allowing them to evade immune responses rapidly.
The mutable nature of certain DNA sequences increases the rate of genetic variation.
