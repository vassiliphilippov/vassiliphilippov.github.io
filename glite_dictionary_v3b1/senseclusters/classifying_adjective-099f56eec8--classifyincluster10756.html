classifying adjective

noun
adjective that assigns category or type
Pronunciation: / ˈklæs.əˌfaɪ.ɪŋ ˈædʒ.ɪk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈklɑː.sɪ.faɪ.ɪŋ ˈadʒ.ɪk.tɪv / (BrE)
An adjective that places a noun in a specific category by indicating its type, kind, or class, rather than describing its qualities.

Examples

A classifying adjective type
A classifying adjective indicates the type of the noun, such as 'human' in 'human error'.
In the phrase 'electrical engineer', the word electrical is a classifying adjective.
Terms like 'wooden' in 'wooden spoon' illustrate a classifying adjective rather than a descriptive one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptive adjective - an adjective that describes qualities of a noun, as opposed to classifying it
