ejido

noun
communal village land
Pronunciation: / eˈxiːdoʊ / (AmE), / eˈxiːdəʊ /; / eˈhiːdəʊ / (BrE)
a piece of communal land in Mexico, used by a community for farming or grazing under the ejido system

Examples

Farming ejido land
The villagers gathered on the ejido to celebrate the annual harvest festival.
Farmers in the region rely on the ejido for grazing their cattle during the dry season.
Local children often play soccer on the open ejido after school.
The villagers worked together to plant crops on the ejido every spring.
Many rural communities in Mexico rely on the ejido system for their agricultural needs.
After the reforms, some families gained individual plots from the former ejido land.
The farmers gathered every morning to work on the ejido that supported their village.
After the land reform, many families received plots on the local ejido.
The community decided together what crops to plant on the ejido each season.

  • synonym: commune - a group of people living together and sharing resources
