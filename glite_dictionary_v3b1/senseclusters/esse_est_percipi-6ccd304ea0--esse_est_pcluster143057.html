esse est percipi

verb
to exist by being perceived
Pronunciation: / ˈɛsːeɪ ɛst pɛrˈkɪpi / (AmE), / ˈɛsːeɪ ɛst pəˈkɪpi / (BrE)
to exist is to be perceived by someone, especially in philosophical contexts.

Examples

Esse est percipi principle
The phrase esse est percipi captures Berkeley's view that objects only exist when observed.
In his lecture, the professor explained esse est percipi as a challenge to materialism.
Many debates in philosophy reference esse est percipi when discussing the nature of reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realism - the belief that reality exists independently of perception
  • synonym: solipsism - the philosophical idea that only one's mind is sure to exist
