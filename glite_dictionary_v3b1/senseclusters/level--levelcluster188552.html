level

verb
direct criticism or accusations at someone
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
to aim or direct something (such as criticism, accusations, or a weapon) at someone or something

Examples

To level criticism
Several allegations were leveled against the company during the investigation.
She leveled a series of accusations at her colleague during the meeting.
The manager leveled harsh criticism at the entire team after the mistake.
He leveled the pistol and told everyone to stay where they were.
She leveled her camera straight at the stage to get a good shot.
The guard leveled his rifle at the intruder.

  • antonym: lower - to move something into a lower position, often opposite of raising or aiming upwards
