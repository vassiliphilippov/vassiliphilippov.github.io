inoculum

noun
substance to induce immunity
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɑːkjələm / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒkjʊləm / (BrE)
a substance containing microorganisms (such as bacteria or viruses) or other agents that is introduced into a body or medium, usually to produce immunity or start a culture

Examples

A inoculum sample
A small amount of inoculum was injected into the patient's arm to initiate an immune response.
Researchers carefully measured the inoculum before adding it to the culture medium.
The laboratory prepared an inoculum containing weakened forms of the virus for testing.
The laboratory technician added the inoculum to the petri dish to start the culture.
A fresh inoculum was required to ensure the experiment's accuracy.
Researchers measured the growth rate after introducing the inoculum into the nutrient solution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pathogen - a microorganism that can cause disease
  • synonym: vaccine - a substance used to stimulate the production of antibodies and provide immunity against one or several diseases
