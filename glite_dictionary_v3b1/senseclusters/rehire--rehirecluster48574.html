rehire

verb
to employ again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈhaɪər / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhaɪə / (BrE)
to employ someone again after they have previously left a job or position

Examples

To rehire an employee
After the company expanded, they decided to rehire several experienced employees.
The manager agreed to rehire her after reviewing her previous performance.
Many workers hope the factory will rehire them when production increases.
After a year away, the company decided to rehire her for the new project.
Many businesses choose to rehire former employees who have valuable experience.
He was surprised when his old boss called to rehire him after the merger.
After reviewing her application, the company decided to rehire her for the new project.
Many businesses choose to rehire former employees who left on good terms.
The school board voted to rehire the teacher after a year-long absence.
