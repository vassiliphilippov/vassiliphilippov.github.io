Alexander the Liberator

proper noun
title of Russian czar Alexander II
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛk.sən.dɚ ðə lɪˈbeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / əˈlɛk.səndə ðə lɪˈbeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a title for Alexander II, the Russian czar (1818–1881), known for emancipating the serfs and enacting major societal reforms

Examples

Alexander the Liberator of Russia
As Alexander the Liberator, he sought to modernize Russia and improve the lives of its peasants.
Historians often discuss the legacy of Alexander the Liberator in the context of 19th-century Russian reforms.
The emancipation of the serfs remains one of Alexander the Liberator's most significant achievements.
