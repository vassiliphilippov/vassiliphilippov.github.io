crenel

noun
notch or indentation along an edge
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛnəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɛnəl / (BrE)
a rounded or square indentation or notch along the edge of something, especially one of a series such as those found on a leaf, shell margin, or the surface of certain cells

Examples

Leaf crenel
A lace border often features a repeating crenel pattern for decorative effect.
Each leaf had a delicate crenel at the edge, giving it a scalloped appearance.
The biologist examined the red blood cell's crenel structure under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crenation - the rounded projection between notches, especially along a leaf or cell margin
