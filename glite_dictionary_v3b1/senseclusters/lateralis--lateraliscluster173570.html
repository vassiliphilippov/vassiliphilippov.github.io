anatomical term for side
Pronunciation: / ˌlætəˈrælɪs / (AmE), / ˌlætəˈrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a Latin term meaning 'lateral' or 'to the side', commonly used in anatomical names to indicate a position away from the midline of the body

Examples

Lateralis muscle name
The vastus lateralis is one of the main muscles on the outer side of the thigh.
In anatomy, the term lateralis helps describe the position of structures relative to the body's midline.
The doctor explained that the injury was located on the lateralis aspect of the patient's arm.
