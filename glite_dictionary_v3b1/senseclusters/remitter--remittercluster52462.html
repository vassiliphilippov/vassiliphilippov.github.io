remitter

noun
one who grants remission
8.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪtə / (BrE)
a person or authority who cancels or pardons a debt, penalty, or offense by granting remission

Examples

Remitter of a penalty
The judge acted as the remitter and forgave the defendant's fine after hearing the circumstances.
In some traditions, a bishop may serve as a remitter by granting absolution to those who confess.
The government became the remitter of certain debts during the economic crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pardoner - a person who forgives or excuses an offense
  • antonym: punisher - a person who imposes a penalty or punishment
