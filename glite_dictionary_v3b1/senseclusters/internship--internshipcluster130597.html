internship

noun
medical training position for an intern
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌtɝnˌʃɪp /; / ˈɪntɝːnˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌtɜːnˌʃɪp /; / ˈɪntɜːnʃɪp / (BrE)
a temporary position or period of practical training in a profession, especially for students or recent graduates (including medical training for interns)

Examples

A medical internship
After completing her medical school requirements, she began her internship at a city hospital.
Many doctors remember their first internship as a challenging but rewarding year.
The internship provided valuable hands-on experience in emergency medicine.
She completed a summer internship at a local hospital to gain medical experience.
Many students apply for an internship to improve their job prospects after graduation.
During his internship, he learned valuable skills that helped him in his future career.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apprenticeship - a period of training under a skilled worker
  • antonym: employment - the state of having paid work
  • degree higher: residency - advanced training for medical graduates after internship
