postage stamp

noun
adhesive label showing postal fees paid
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊstɪdʒ stæmp / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstɪdʒ stæmp / (BrE)
A small piece of paper issued by a postal authority, bearing a design and adhesive backing, affixed to mail as proof that postage has been paid.

Examples

A postage stamp collection
Collectors often seek rare and valuable postage stamps from different countries.
She carefully placed a postage stamp on the corner of the envelope before mailing it.
You need to buy a postage stamp to send that letter overseas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stamp - an official mark or label used for various purposes
