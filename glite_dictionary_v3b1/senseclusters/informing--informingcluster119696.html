informing

noun
giving evidence to the police for favors
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of providing incriminating evidence to law enforcement, typically in exchange for some benefit or leniency

Examples

Police informing
Rumors spread that there had been informing among the members to reduce their sentences.
She considered informing as a betrayal, even if it meant going to prison herself.
The gang leader punished him for informing on his associates to the police.

Related Terms

  • synonym: snitching - acting as an informer, especially to the police
