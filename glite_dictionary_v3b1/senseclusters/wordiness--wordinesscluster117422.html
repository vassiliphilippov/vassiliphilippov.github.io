wordiness

noun
use of too many words; verbosity
4.8 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ùÀêdi.n…ôs / (AmE), / Ààw…úÀêdi.n…™s / (BrE)
the quality of using or containing more words than necessary, especially so as to be tedious or verbose

Examples

Avoid wordiness in writing
Good writing avoids unnecessary wordiness to communicate ideas clearly.
His lectures are criticized for their wordiness and lack of focus.
The report's excessive wordiness made it difficult to find the main points.

Synonyms

  • verbosity
    7.3 0.11/m
    verbosity is using more words than needed in speech or writing, while wordiness labels that needless, tiring style.
  • prolixity
    9.9 0.04/m
    prolixity is a formal, often academic way to describe long, boring writing, while wordiness is the general quality of excess words.
  • long-windedness
    7.5 0.02/m
    long-windedness is a casual term for speech that goes on too long and bores listeners, while wordiness names excess wording.
  • verbiage
    8.1 0.76/m
    verbiage is the actual excess words or awkward phrasing someone writes or says, while wordiness is the general habit of using too many words.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point
  • synonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed
