empty suit

noun
incompetent or superficial person in authority
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmpti suːt / (AmE), / ˈɛmpti suːt / (BrE)
a person, especially one in a position of authority, who is considered to lack real ability, substance, or qualifications

Examples

An empty suit leader
Don't be fooled by the fancy office—he's nothing more than an empty suit.
Many employees felt the new manager was an empty suit who offered little value to the team.
The politician was criticized as just another empty suit making empty promises during the campaign.
