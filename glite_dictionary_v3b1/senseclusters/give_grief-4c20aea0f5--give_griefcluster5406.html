give grief

verb
criticize or complain about someone, often persistently
5.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /gɪv griːf/ (AmE), /gɪv griːf/ (BrE)
to cause someone annoyance, distress, or trouble (by criticizing, complaining, or being a source of problems)

Examples

To give grief to friends
If you give grief to your siblings all the time, they'll start avoiding you.
My boss tends to give grief to anyone who makes a small mistake.
Why do you always give grief to your friend for being late?
His constant lateness is really giving me grief at work.
She was upset because her friends kept giving her grief about her decision.
The faulty printer has been giving us grief all week.

  • synonym: give someone a hard time - to criticize, tease, or harass someone persistently
  • antonym: relieve - to ease someone's pain or distress
