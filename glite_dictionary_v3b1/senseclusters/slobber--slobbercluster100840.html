slobber

verb
let saliva drivel from the mouth
4.8 0.88/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsl…ëÀêb…ôr / (AmE), / Ààsl…íb…ô / (BrE)
to let saliva run from the mouth in a messy way, or (figuratively) to show excessive desire or enthusiasm for something

Examples

To slobber on the pillow
She was embarrassed when she realized she was slobbering in her sleep.
The dog began to slobber all over the floor when it saw the treat.
The toddler slobbered on his toy as he chewed it.
He couldn't help but slobber over the display of chocolate desserts.
Please wipe the baby's chin before she starts to slobber everywhere.
The dog began to slobber on my hand when I petted it.

Synonyms

  • drool
    2.1 1.54/m
    drool is saliva that flows from the mouth quietly and often without control, while slobber suggests messier, heavier running.
  • slaver
    9.8 0.18/m
    slaver allows saliva to run or drip from the mouth in a steady way, while slobber feels messier and thicker.
  • slabber
    9.9 0.00/m
    slabber means to let saliva run from the mouth, often in animals or people, while slobber implies a wetter, messier flow.
  • dribble
    2.8 1.32/m
    dribble is to let small drops of liquid fall slowly from the mouth, while slobber describes a larger, messier flow.
  • salivate
    6.1 0.62/m
    salivate often means to feel eager or excited for something, while slobber also means actual messy drooling from the mouth.
  • point-and-drool
    9.9 0.00/m
    point-and-drool is an informal, joking way to show loud admiration, while slobber can mean messy drooling or strong desire.
