slobber

verb
let saliva drivel from the mouth
8.5 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈslɑː.bər/ (AmE), /ˈslɒ.bə/ (BrE)
to let saliva run from the mouth in a messy way, or (figuratively) to show excessive desire or enthusiasm for something

Examples

To slobber on the pillow
She was embarrassed when she realized she was slobbering in her sleep.
The dog began to slobber all over the floor when it saw the treat.
The toddler slobbered on his toy as he chewed it.
He couldn't help but slobber over the display of chocolate desserts.
Please wipe the baby's chin before she starts to slobber everywhere.
The dog began to slobber on my hand when I petted it.
