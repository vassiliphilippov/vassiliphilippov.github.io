taste cell

noun
cell in taste bud responsible for taste
3.9
Pronunciation: / teɪst sɛl / (AmE), / teɪst sɛl / (BrE)
a specialized epithelial cell in a taste bud that detects chemical stimuli and activates sensory nerve fibers involved in taste

Examples

Taste cell in bud
Damage to a taste cell can result in the loss of taste sensation in that area.
Each taste cell in the tongue responds to specific types of chemical molecules found in food.
Scientists are studying how taste cells regenerate after injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gustatory cell - another term for taste cell; cell in a taste bud that detects taste
