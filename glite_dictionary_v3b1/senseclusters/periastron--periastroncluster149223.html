periastron

noun
closest orbital point
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛriˈæstrən / (AmE), / ˌpɛriˈæstrən / (BrE)
the point in the orbit of a star or planet where it is closest to its companion star or object

Examples

The periastron point
The two stars are closest together at periastron, causing their gravitational interaction to increase.
Astronomers scheduled their observations to coincide with the system's periastron passage.
Changes in brightness were detected as the planet reached periastron in its orbit.
The binary stars are at their brightest when they reach periastron in their orbit.
Astronomers observed increased activity as the planet approached periastron with its companion.
The distance between the two stars is shortest at periastron.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apastron - the point in an orbit where a body is farthest from its companion star
  • synonym: perihelion - the point in the orbit of a planet or comet where it is closest to the Sun
