Nile

proper noun
major river in Africa; world's longest river
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪl / (AmE), / ˈnaɪl / (BrE)
the longest river in the world, flowing northward through eastern Africa into the Mediterranean Sea, renowned as the cradle of ancient Egyptian civilization

Examples

The Nile River
Every year, the flooding of the Nile brought fertile soil to the surrounding fields.
Many tourists take cruises along the Nile to view ancient temples and tombs.
The Nile has played a crucial role in the development of Egyptian civilization.
