piezoelectric material

noun
material that becomes electrically charged under stress
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪzoʊɪˈlɛktrɪk məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌpiːzəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
A material that generates an electric charge when subjected to mechanical stress.

Examples

Piezoelectric material generates charge
Researchers are developing new piezoelectric materials for energy harvesting applications.
Some lighters use a piezoelectric material to generate a spark.
The sensor is made from a piezoelectric material that produces a voltage when pressed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ferroelectric material - material with spontaneous electric polarization
