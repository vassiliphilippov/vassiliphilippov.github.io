nonionizing

adjective
not causing ionization
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈaɪəˌnaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈaɪənaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
not having enough energy to remove tightly bound electrons from atoms or molecules

Examples

Nonionizing radiation energy
Microwave ovens use nonionizing radiation to heat food safely.
Visible light is a form of nonionizing radiation that does not damage DNA.
Doctors often explain that cell phones emit nonionizing radiation, which is not strong enough to cause ionization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ionizing - having enough energy to remove electrons from atoms or molecules
