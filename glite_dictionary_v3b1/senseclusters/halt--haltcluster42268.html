halt

verb
stop moving; come to a stop
4.8 2.1
Pronunciation: /hɔːlt/ /; / /hɑːlt/ (AmE), /hɔːlt/ /; / /hɒlt/ (BrE)
to stop or cause something to stop moving, happening, or developing

Examples

To halt progress
All trains will halt at this station for five minutes.
The captain ordered the soldiers to halt immediately.
The driver was forced to halt when the road was blocked by fallen trees.
Heavy rain forced the company to halt work on the new road temporarily.
The government decided to halt the construction project due to safety concerns.
We must act quickly to halt the spread of the disease in the community.
Firefighters managed to halt the spread of oil leaking into the river.
Immediate action is required to halt the flow of toxic chemicals.
Pressure was applied to halt the bleeding from the wound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to keep going or allow to proceed
  • antonym: proceed - to continue moving forward
  • antonym: resume - to begin again after stopping
  • synonym: staunch - to stop the flow of liquid, such as blood
  • synonym: stem - to stop or restrict the flow of something
  • synonym: stop - to cease moving or performing an action
