tranquilising

adjective
having a calming or soothing effect
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtræŋkwəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtræŋkwɪˌlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
having a calming or soothing effect; reducing anxiety or agitation.

Examples

Tranquilising drugs
Doctors sometimes prescribe tranquilising drugs for patients with severe anxiety.
He listened to the tranquilising music to help him fall asleep.
The herbal tea had a tranquilising effect, making her feel much more relaxed after a stressful day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stimulating - causing excitement or activity
