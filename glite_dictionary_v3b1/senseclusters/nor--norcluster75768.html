nor

conjunction
negative conjunction
1.0
Pronunciation: / nɔr / (AmE), / nɔː / (BrE)
used as a conjunction to add another negative idea to a sentence, usually after 'neither' or another negative statement (meaning 'and not')

Examples

Neither this nor that
Neither the rain nor the wind could stop the parade from continuing.
She does not like coffee, nor does she enjoy tea in the morning.
The team has neither the experience nor the resources to complete the project on time.
She does not eat meat nor dairy products.
He didn't call nor did he send a message.
The movie was neither exciting nor funny.
He did not call, nor did he send a message to explain his absence.
The proposal was not accepted, nor was it discussed at the meeting.
She does not eat meat, nor does she drink milk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neither - used before the first of two negative alternatives
  • antonym: or - used to connect alternatives or choices
