Third World

noun
developing nations of Asia, Africa, and Latin America
5.7 7.2
Pronunciation: / θɝd wɝld / (AmE), / θɜːd wɜːld / (BrE)
the group of countries in Asia, Africa, and Latin America with low economic development, often called 'developing countries' (now considered outdated or sometimes offensive)

Examples

Third World countries
Aid organizations work to improve healthcare in Third World regions.
During the Cold War, many nations in the Third World remained non-aligned with the major powers.
The report highlighted poverty and education challenges facing the Third World today.
Concerns about clean water access are still major issues in the Third World.
He has spent years working on education projects in the Third World.
Many international organizations focus their aid efforts on the Third World.

Related Terms

  • antonym: First World - highly developed, industrialized countries
  • synonym: Global South - a modern term for the regions of Latin America, Asia, Africa, and Oceania
  • synonym: developing country - a country with low industrialization and low human development
  • synonym: developing world - countries with less developed industrial bases and lower Human Development Index
