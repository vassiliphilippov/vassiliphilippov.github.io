forgive

verb
stop blaming or pardon someone
Pronunciation: / fərˈɡɪv / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪv / (BrE)
to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone for an offense, mistake, or wrongdoing; to grant pardon to someone.

Examples

To forgive a friend
After a long talk, she decided to forgive her friend for the misunderstanding.
It is not always easy to forgive someone who has hurt you deeply.
Many people find it healing to forgive themselves for past mistakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condemn - to express strong disapproval of or to sentence to punishment
  • synonym: pardon - to officially release someone from punishment or forgive a fault
