quantum jump

noun
sudden change between quantum states
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːntəm dʒʌmp / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm dʒʌmp / (BrE)
a sudden change in the state of an atom, electron, or molecule from one quantum level to another, accompanied by the emission or absorption of energy

Examples

Atomic quantum jump
A quantum jump occurs when an atomic particle transitions instantaneously between energy levels.
Physicists have long studied the mechanics behind each quantum jump in spectroscopic experiments.
The electron made a quantum jump from the ground state to an excited state after absorbing a photon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quantum leap - another term for abrupt change in quantum states, also used figuratively
