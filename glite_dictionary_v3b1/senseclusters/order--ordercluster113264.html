order

noun
set of rules governing an assembly
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər /; / ˈɔːrdər /; / ˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
an authoritative instruction, command, or decision that must be obeyed (including legal orders)

Examples

A court order
Anyone who wished to speak had to adhere to the order of the assembly.
Parliamentary order requires certain motions to be made in a specific sequence.
The committee agreed to follow the order during all formal debates.
During the search, the police acted strictly according to orders from their supervisor.
He refused to follow orders that he believed were unjust.
The captain issued orders for the crew to prepare for departure.
Failure to comply with the court order can result in legal penalties.
She filed a motion to have the restraining order lifted.
The judge issued an order prohibiting the company from selling the disputed assets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command - an authoritative order given to do something
  • synonym: injunction - a court order requiring a person to do or cease doing a specific action
  • synonym: mandate - an official order or authority to do something
  • synonym: procedure - the established or official way of doing something
  • antonym: request - an act of asking politely or formally for something
  • synonym: ruling - an authoritative decision or judgment, especially by a judge
