coverslip

verb
to cover microscope slide
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvərˌslɪp / (AmE), / ˈkʌvəˌslɪp / (BrE)
to place a thin, flat piece of glass over a specimen on a microscope slide to protect it and improve viewing

Examples

To coverslip a slide
The lab technician coverslipped the sample to prevent it from drying out during observation.
After staining the tissue, she coverslipped the slide for examination under the microscope.
Students must coverslip their slides carefully to avoid trapping air bubbles.
