permeability

noun
ability to let liquid or gas pass through
5.5 5.9
Pronunciation: /pɜːr.miː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /pɜːr.mɪ.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /pɜː.miː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /pə.miː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
The quality or state of a material that allows liquids or gases to pass through it.

Examples

Permeability of material
Concrete with low permeability is used in tunnels to prevent water leaks.
Scientists measured the permeability of the membrane to determine how easily nutrients could pass.
The permeability of the soil affects how much water plants can absorb.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impermeability - the quality of not allowing liquids or gases to pass through
  • synonym: porosity - the quality of having tiny holes that allow air or liquid to pass
