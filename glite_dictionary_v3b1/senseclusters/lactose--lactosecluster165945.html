lactose

noun
milk sugar; a sugar found in milk
Pronunciation: / ˈlækˌtoʊs / (AmE), / ˈlæk.təʊs / (BrE)
a sugar found in milk, consisting of one glucose and one galactose molecule

Examples

lactose sugar
Infant formulas are sometimes made without lactose to help babies with sensitive digestion.
Many people who are lactose intolerant avoid drinking cow's milk due to the lactose it contains.
The process of making certain cheeses removes most of the lactose from the final product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galactose - a simple sugar that combines with glucose to form lactose
  • synonym: glucose - a simple sugar that is an important energy source
