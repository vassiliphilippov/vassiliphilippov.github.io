toff accent

noun
upper-class English accent
8.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / tɑf ˈæksənt / (AmE), / tɒf ˈæksənt / (BrE)
An accent or way of speaking characteristic of the English upper class, often perceived as posh or refined.

Examples

Toff accent speech
He put on a toff accent for the school play and pretended to be a lord.
Her toff accent made her stand out among the other students from the countryside.
People assumed he was wealthy because of his toff accent, though his background was quite modest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: posh accent - an accent characteristic of the upper classes, often perceived as refined or affected
