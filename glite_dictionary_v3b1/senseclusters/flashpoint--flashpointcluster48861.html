flashpoint

noun
critical or explosive stage
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃˌpɔɪnt /; / ˈflæʃ.pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈflæʃpɔɪnt /; / ˈflæʃ.pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a situation or place where trouble, violence, or intense activity is likely to start suddenly

Examples

Political flashpoint
Tensions in the region have reached a flashpoint that could lead to conflict at any moment.
The city became a flashpoint for protests after the controversial law was passed.
The manager's harsh words acted as a flashpoint for employee dissatisfaction.
Tensions rose, turning the capital into a political flashpoint overnight.
The border region quickly became a flashpoint after the troops arrived.
The city is considered a major flashpoint in the ongoing conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crisis - a time of intense difficulty or danger
  • synonym: hotspot - a place of significant activity, danger, or unrest, especially politically
  • synonym: tipping point - the critical point at which a significant change occurs
