whir

verb
make a soft, continuous swishing sound
4.6 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / w…úr / (AmE), / w…úÀê / (BrE)
(of an object or machine) to make a continuous, soft, swishing or buzzing sound, especially one that is rapid or low in pitch.

Examples

Machines whir loudly
Helicopter blades whir overhead as they prepare to land.
The old fan began to whir quietly as she turned it on.
You could hear the sewing machine whir from the next room.

Synonyms

  • whirr
    6.7 1.11/m
    whirr is a continuous low humming from fast-moving parts, while whir is a soft, steady swishing or buzzing.
  • birr
    9.9 0.00/m
    birr is a soft, whirring or swishing sound from a machine, while whir describes the same steady buzzing or swish.
  • purr
    3.5 0.49/m
    purr is a soft, vibrating sound often felt as pleasant in machines, while whir is a neutral, steady swishing or buzzing.
  • whiz
    6.2 0.05/m
    whiz is a sharp swish or buzz made by something moving quickly through the air, while whir is a steady machine sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buzz - to make a continuous humming sound
  • synonym: hum - to make a low, steady continuous sound
