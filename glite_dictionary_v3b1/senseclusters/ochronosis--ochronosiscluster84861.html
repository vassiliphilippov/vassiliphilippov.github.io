ochronosis

noun
accumulation of dark pigment in connective tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.krə.ˈnoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /əʊ.krə.ˈnəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
the accumulation of dark pigment in cartilage and other connective tissues, often as a symptom of certain metabolic disorders such as alkaptonuria or phenol poisoning

Examples

Ochronosis in cartilage
A rare complication of alkaptonuria is the development of ochronosis in cartilage and joints.
Early diagnosis of ochronosis can prevent severe connective tissue damage in affected individuals.
Histological examination confirmed the presence of ochronosis in the ear cartilage.
