explosion

noun
release of pressure in stop consonant (phonetics)
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsploʊʒən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊʒən / (BrE)
(Phonetics) The release of built-up pressure in the mouth during the articulation of a stop consonant, resulting in a burst of sound.

Examples

Explosion of a sound
In the word 'pat', the explosion of the 'p' sound is clearly heard at the beginning.
Phoneticians pay close attention to the timing of explosion in stop consonant articulation.
The absence of an audible explosion can distinguish certain dialects in speech analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stop - a consonant produced by blocking the airflow and then releasing it
