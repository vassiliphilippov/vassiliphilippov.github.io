florescence

noun
period of blossoming or flowering
8.4 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / fl…îÀàr…õs…ôns / (AmE), / fl…îÀêÀàr…õs…ôns / (BrE)
the process or period of blossoming; the time during which flowers bloom or a plant produces blossoms

Examples

The florescence period
Botanists studied the florescence of rare alpine plants in the region.
During spring, the garden was a spectacle of colors in full florescence.
The cherry trees reached their peak florescence in early April, attracting crowds to the park.

Synonyms

  • efflorescence
    9.3 0.01/m
    efflorescence is a rarer, technical word for plants' blooming, while florescence names the general period when flowers open and flourish.
  • bloom
    1.0 6.64/m
    bloom refers to the flower itself or the act of flowering, while florescence describes the whole flowering period of a plant.
  • flowering
    2.0 3.24/m
    flowering is the common, everyday word for plants producing flowers, while florescence is a slightly formal term for that period.
  • blossoming
    2.5 0.54/m
    blossoming shows buds opening and the flowers' growth, while florescence names the whole period when those blossoms appear.
  • inflorescence
    8.7 0.01/m
    inflorescence often refers to the flower cluster or its arrangement on a stem, while florescence means the general blooming period.
  • anthesis
    9.9 0.02/m
    anthesis is the technical stage when a single flower opens, while florescence describes the broader period when many flowers bloom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloom - the period or process when a plant produces flowers
  • antonym: wither - to fade, shrivel, or lose freshness (of flowers/plants)
