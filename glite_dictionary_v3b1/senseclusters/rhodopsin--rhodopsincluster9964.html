rhodopsin

noun
light-sensitive protein used in optogenetics
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.dəp.sɪn/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.dɒp.sɪn/ (BrE)
(in molecular biology and optogenetics) a light-sensitive protein, often from microbes or engineered, that is expressed in cells to control or monitor their activity with light.

Examples

Microbial rhodopsin protein
Researchers used rhodopsin to make neurons respond precisely to pulses of blue light.
The study introduced a microbial rhodopsin into brain cells to control activity during experiments.
Clinicians hope that expressing rhodopsin in retinal cells could restore light sensitivity in some patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: channelrhodopsin - a type of microbial rhodopsin that forms a light-gated ion channel used in optogenetics
  • synonym: opsin - a family of light-sensitive proteins that includes rhodopsins
