Battle of the Metaurus

noun
major battle of the Second Punic War (207 BC)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ðə məˈtɔrəs / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ðə mɪˈtɔːrəs / (BrE)
A decisive battle in 207 BC during the Second Punic War, in which Roman forces defeated Hasdrubal, Hannibal's brother, effectively ending Carthaginian hopes of victory in Italy.

Examples

The Battle of the Metaurus in 207 BC
After the Battle of the Metaurus, Carthaginian forces were unable to threaten Rome directly again.
Historians regard the Battle of the Metaurus as a major turning point in the Second Punic War.
Roman victory at the Battle of the Metaurus effectively ended Hannibal's chances of success in Italy.
