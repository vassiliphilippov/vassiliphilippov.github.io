crevassed

verb
split with crevasses
8.2
Pronunciation: / krəˈvæst / (AmE), / krɪˈvæst / (BrE)
split or broken so that deep cracks or crevasses have formed, especially in ice or a glacier.

Examples

Ice crevassed by heat
The glacier was heavily crevassed after the spring thaw, making travel dangerous.
They avoided the most crevassed areas to ensure the safety of the expedition.
The surface became crevassed as the ice shifted and cracked over time.
