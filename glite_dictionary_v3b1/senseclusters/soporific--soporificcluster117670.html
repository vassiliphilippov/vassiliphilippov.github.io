soporific

adjective
inducing sleepiness or mental dullness
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑpəˈrɪfɪk /; / sɑːpəˈrɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɒpəˈrɪfɪk /; / sɒpəˈrɪfɪk / (BrE)
causing sleepiness or making someone feel drowsy

Examples

Soporific effect
His voice was so dull and soporific that I struggled to stay awake.
The professor's soporific lecture sent half the students to sleep.
This medication can have a soporific effect if taken in large doses.
The gentle sound of rain at night is highly soporific for many people.
The professor's soporific lecture caused several students to nod off before noon.
This medication has a soporific effect, so avoid driving after taking it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sedative - having a calming or sleep-inducing effect
  • antonym: stimulant - something that increases mental or physical activity
