(of a person) affectedly or smugly virtuous or self-righteous.

Examples

Goody-goody attitude
He got tired of being called a goody-goody just because he followed the rules.

Synonyms

  • self-righteous
    7.8 2.62/m
    self-righteous is openly sure they are morally right and often strict, while goody-goody sounds childish and smugly virtuous.
  • sanctimonious
    8.5 1.04/m
    sanctimonious makes a show of pious goodness and often hides hypocrisy, while goody-goody is a light, childish way to seem virtuous.
  • holier-than-thou
    8.7 0.22/m
    holier-than-thou shows clear moral superiority and judgment, while goody-goody is a milder, more playful insult about being overly virtuous.
  • pharisaical
    9.9 0.04/m
    pharisaical is a formal, strongly hypocritical label often tied to religion, while goody-goody is an informal, childish jab at virtue.
  • Pharisee-like
    9.9 0.00/m
    Pharisee-like is a biblical, harsh term for hypocritical judgment, whereas goody-goody is a softer, teasing way to call someone overly virtuous.
