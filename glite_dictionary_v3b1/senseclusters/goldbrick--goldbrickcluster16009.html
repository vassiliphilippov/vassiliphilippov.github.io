goldbrick

noun
something supposedly valuable but actually worthless
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldˌbrɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊl(d)ˌbrɪk / (BrE)
something that is presented as valuable or promising but proves to be worthless or disappointing

Examples

A goldbrick offer
Don't let fancy packaging fool you into buying a goldbrick.
Many investors realized too late that the startup was just a goldbrick with no real prospects.
The painting looked authentic, but the appraiser declared it a goldbrick.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fraud - a thing intended to deceive, typically something falsely presented as valuable
  • synonym: sham - something that is not what it purports to be
  • antonym: treasure - something that is genuinely valuable
