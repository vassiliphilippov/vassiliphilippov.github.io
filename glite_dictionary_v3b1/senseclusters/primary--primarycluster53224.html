primary

noun
celestial body orbited by another
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈprʌɪməri / (BrE)
(astronomy) A celestial body, especially a star, around which another object or objects revolve.

Examples

A planet's primary
Each planet is a primary to its natural satellites.
In our solar system, the Sun is the primary for all the planets.
The Earth acts as the primary for the Moon, which orbits around it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secondary - a celestial body orbiting a primary
