breathe

verb
express or display a feeling/quality
Pronunciation: / briːð /; / brið / (AmE), / briːð / (BrE)
to express, show, or fill something with a quality or feeling

Examples

Breathe new life
Her writing breathes compassion for the less fortunate.
The entire house breathed an air of serenity and warmth.
The historic building breathed tradition and elegance at every turn.
Her imaginative ideas breathed energy into the project.
The new coach breathed confidence into the struggling team.
Through his storytelling, he breathed hope into the weary crowd.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exude - to display or emit (a quality or feeling) openly or strongly
  • synonym: revitalize - to give new life or energy to something
