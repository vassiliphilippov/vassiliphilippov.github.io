completeness

noun
state of being complete or whole
1.5
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplit.nəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːt.nɪs / (BrE)
The state of being complete; having all necessary parts or elements.

Examples

Completeness of the work
Many people seek completeness in their work to ensure nothing is left out.
The completeness of his research impressed the entire review committee.
The report has a sense of completeness that makes it easy to understand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incompleteness - the state of being incomplete; lacking parts
  • synonym: wholeness - the quality of being whole or entire
