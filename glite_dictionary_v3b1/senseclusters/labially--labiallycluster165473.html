labially

adverb
toward the lips
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbiəli / (AmE), / ˈleɪbiəli / (BrE)
in a manner relating to or toward the lips, or the lip side of an anatomical structure.

Examples

To move labially
The dentist moved the tooth labially to correct its alignment.
Some sounds in language are produced labially, using the lips.
The crown was positioned labially to match the patient's natural bite.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buccally - toward or relating to the cheek
