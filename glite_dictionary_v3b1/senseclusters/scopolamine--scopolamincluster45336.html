scopolamine

noun
alkaloid drug for nausea and sedation
9.7 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / sko äÀàp…ël…ôÀåmin / (AmE), / sk…ôÀàp…íl…ôÀåmiÀên / (BrE)
an alkaloid drug with anticholinergic properties, used as a sedative, to prevent nausea and motion sickness, and to dilate pupils in eye procedures

Examples

Scopolamine patch
After taking scopolamine, she felt drowsy but no longer nauseous.
Ophthalmologists use scopolamine to dilate patients' pupils for certain eye examinations.
Transdermal scopolamine patches are commonly prescribed to prevent motion sickness during travel.
