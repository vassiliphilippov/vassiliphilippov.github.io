facticity

noun
state of being factual
9.9
Pronunciation: / fækˈtɪsəti / (AmE), / fækˈtɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being a fact or having actual existence

Examples

The facticity of evidence
The facticity of the evidence was never questioned during the trial.
Historians debate the facticity of certain events described in ancient texts.
She emphasized the facticity of her research findings in the presentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actuality - the state of existing in reality
  • antonym: fiction - something that is invented or imagined, not a fact
