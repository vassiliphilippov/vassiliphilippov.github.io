Purkinje's tissue

noun
network of heart fibers conducting impulses
9.0
Pronunciation: / pərˈkɪndʒi ˈtɪʃu / (AmE), / pəˈkɪndʒi ˈtɪsjʉː / (BrE)
a network of specialized heart fibers (Purkinje fibers) that conduct impulses from the atrioventricular node to the ventricles, triggering their contraction

Examples

Purkinje's tissue network
Damage to the Purkinje's tissue can disrupt normal heart rhythms and lead to arrhythmias.
Doctors studied the response of Purkinje's tissue during cardiac stress tests.
The electrical signal travels through the Purkinje's tissue to coordinate the ventricles' contractions.
