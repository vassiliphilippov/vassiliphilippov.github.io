ptyalin

noun
salivary enzyme that breaks down starch
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtaɪ.ə.lən/ /; / /ˈtaɪ.lɪn/ (AmE), /ˈtaɪ.ə.lən/ /; / /ˈtaɪ.lɪn/ (BrE)
an amylase enzyme found in saliva that helps break down starch into sugars during digestion

Examples

Ptyalin enzyme
Ptyalin begins the chemical process of digestion in the mouth by breaking down starch into maltose.
Scientists studied the action of ptyalin on different types of carbohydrate-rich foods.
The secretion of ptyalin decreases when the mouth is dry or when under stress.
