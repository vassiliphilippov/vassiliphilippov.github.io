exceptional

adjective
unusually good or uncommon
2.0
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛpʃənl̩ / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃənl /; / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəl / (BrE)
unusually good, outstanding, or much greater than is usual or expected

Examples

An exceptional performance
Her performance in the play was truly exceptional and won her several awards.
It was an exceptional year for the company, with profits higher than ever before.
The school offers programs for exceptional students who need extra challenges.
His exceptional performance earned him a special award at the ceremony.
She has an exceptional talent for music, stunning everyone who hears her play.
The restaurant is known for offering exceptional service to all its guests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extraordinary - very unusual or remarkable; far beyond the ordinary
  • antonym: ordinary - common or usual; not exceptional
  • synonym: special - better, greater, or different from what is usual
  • antonym: unremarkable - not special or surprising in any way; ordinary
