Spirula peronii

noun
a small tropical cephalopod with a spiral shell
9.9
Pronunciation: / spɪˈruːlə pɛˈroʊni / (AmE), / spɪˈruːlə pɛˈrəʊniː / (BrE)
A small species of tropical cephalopod known for its prominent eyes, short arms, and a many-chambered shell coiled in a flat spiral.

Examples

Spirula peronii shell
Researchers collected several specimens of Spirula peronii off the coast of West Africa.
The rarely seen Spirula peronii is distinguished by its unique internal shell.
Unlike most squids, Spirula peronii remains in deep waters during the day and ascends at night.
