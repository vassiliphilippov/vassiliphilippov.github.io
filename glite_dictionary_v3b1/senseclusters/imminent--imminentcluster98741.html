Synonyms

  • impending
    4.0 5/m
    impending often warns of a near, sometimes threatening event, while imminent simply means likely to happen very soon.
  • impendent
    7.8 0.00/m
    impendent is an older, rare form used in writing, while imminent is the common modern word for something about to happen.
  • close at hand
    4.1 0.1/m
    close at hand means very near in time and often urgent, while imminent describes something likely to happen immediately.
  • at hand
    3.2 3/m
    at hand means easily near in time or place, while imminent stresses that an event is about to occur very soon.
  • approaching
    2.3 0.2/m
    approaching describes a time or event drawing nearer, while imminent stresses that it will happen very soon and without delay.
  • coming
    1.0 0.00/m
    coming refers to an event expected soon or next, while imminent says that event is about to happen immediately.
  • upcoming
    1.4 20/m
    upcoming is used for planned events in the near future, while imminent shows an event is due to happen at once.
  • forthcoming
    4.2 5/m
    forthcoming often appears in formal or written contexts about future plans, while imminent signals that something will happen very soon.
  • in the offing
    5.6 0.8/m
    in the offing suggests something will appear or happen soon in a general way, while imminent means it will occur right away.
