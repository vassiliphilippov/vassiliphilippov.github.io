psychotherapy

noun
treatment of mental disorders by psychological methods
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkoʊˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkəʊˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
the treatment of mental or emotional problems using psychological methods, especially by talking with a therapist

Examples

Long-term psychotherapy
Long-term psychotherapy can be effective for people with chronic emotional problems.
She began psychotherapy to help manage her anxiety and depression.
Their clinic offers both medication and psychotherapy for various mental health conditions.
After months of psychotherapy, she began to feel more in control of her anxiety.
His doctor recommended psychotherapy alongside medication for the best results.
Many people benefit from engaging in psychotherapy for depression or relationship issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counseling - professional advice and guidance, especially by a trained person
