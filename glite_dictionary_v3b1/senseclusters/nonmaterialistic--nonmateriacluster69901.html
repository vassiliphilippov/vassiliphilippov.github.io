nonmaterialistic

adjective
valuing nonphysical realities
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnməˌtɪriəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnməˌtɪəriəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
not caring about material possessions or wealth; valuing mind, spirit, or nonphysical things more than physical things

Examples

Nonmaterialistic attitude
Her nonmaterialistic outlook led her to focus on personal growth rather than wealth.
Many philosophers with nonmaterialistic views argue that consciousness cannot be explained by physical processes alone.
He admired his friend's nonmaterialistic approach to life, valuing experiences over possessions.
He admired her nonmaterialistic approach to life, always valuing relationships over money.
Their nonmaterialistic values led them to live simply and avoid unnecessary purchases.
A nonmaterialistic attitude can help people find happiness beyond material wealth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: materialistic - believing that physical things are the most important
  • synonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or nonphysical aspects of life
