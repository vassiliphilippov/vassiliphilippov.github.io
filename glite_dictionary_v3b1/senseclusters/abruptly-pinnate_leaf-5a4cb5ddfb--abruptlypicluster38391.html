abruptly-pinnate leaf

noun
pinnate leaf ending in a pair of leaflets
9.4
Pronunciation: / əˈbrʌptli pɪˈneɪt lif / (AmE), / əˈbrʌptli pɪˈneɪt liːf / (BrE)
A pinnate leaf that ends with a pair of leaflets at the tip, rather than a single terminal leaflet.

Examples

An abruptly-pinnate leaf tip
Botanists note that an abruptly-pinnate leaf differs from an evenly-pinnate one at the tip.

You can distinguish this species by its unique abruptly-pinnate leaf arrangement.
