mudflow

noun
flowing mass of mud
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmʌd.fləʊ / (BrE)
a rapid flow of water mixed with mud, soil, rocks, and debris down a slope, usually after heavy rain or volcanic activity

Examples

A mudflow after rain
After the heavy rain, a massive mudflow swept through the village, damaging several houses.
Scientists studied the mudflow to understand how volcanic eruptions can trigger such disasters.
The road was blocked for days because a mudflow covered it with thick layers of mud and debris.
The heavy rain triggered a massive mudflow that destroyed several houses in the village.
Scientists studied the path of the mudflow after the volcanic eruption to assess the damage.
Rescue teams worked quickly to clear the road blocked by the sudden mudflow.
After the storm, a massive mudflow blocked the main road to the village.
The sudden mudflow caught the hikers by surprise as they crossed the valley.
Heavy rains increased the risk of a mudflow in the mountainous region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: avalanche - a large mass of snow, ice, or rock falling quickly down a mountain
  • synonym: debris flow - a fast-moving mixture of water and debris down a slope
  • synonym: lahar - a destructive volcanic mudflow on the slopes of a volcano
  • synonym: landslide - a mass of earth or rock moving down a slope
