martyrize

verb
torture or torment like a martyr
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtəraɪz /; / ˈmɑːtɪraɪz / (BrE)
to torture or torment (someone) as if making them a martyr

Examples

To martyrize a person
She felt as though fate had chosen to martyrize her with one hardship after another.
The unjust ruler sought to martyrize anyone who spoke out against his regime.
Writers have described how oppressive regimes martyrize dissidents to instill fear in others.
Do not martyrise yourself for a cause that will not appreciate your sacrifice.
In the novel, the antagonist martyrises the hero, pushing him to the brink of despair.
The regime would martyrise its prisoners to make an example of them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: persecute - to subject someone to hostility and ill-treatment, especially because of race or political or religious beliefs
  • synonym: torture - to inflict severe pain as a punishment or in order to force someone to do or say something
