movable

adjective
able to be moved; not fixed in place
2.5 1.80/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmuÀêv…ôb…ôl /; / Ààmuv…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàmuÀêv…ôb(…ô)l / (BrE)
able to be moved or carried from one place to another

Examples

Movable furniture
According to the contract, only movable items such as furniture and electronics are included in the sale.
All movable assets should be listed separately from real estate in the inventory.
Because the paintings are considered movable property, they can be transported to another gallery.
All the movable furniture was taken out of the room during cleaning.
Emergency shelters are often equipped with movable partitions to create private spaces.
The exhibition displays only movable artworks that can be easily transported.

Synonyms

  • transportable
    3.7 0.32/m
    transportable is meant for items that can be carried between places, while movable simply means it can be moved or carried.
  • relocatable
    4.2 0.01/m
    relocatable describes something that can be moved to a new place or position, while movable just means it can be moved.
  • mobile
    2.7 4.96/m
    mobile can move itself or change location easily, while movable usually means it can be carried by someone or something.
  • shiftable
    4.4 0.02/m
    shiftable often means able to be moved slightly or adjusted in position, while movable covers being moved or carried generally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immovable - not able to be moved; fixed in place (especially in legal property terms)
