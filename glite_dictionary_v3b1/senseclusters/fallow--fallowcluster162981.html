fallow

noun
uncultivated ploughed farmland
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfæləʊ / (BrE)
Land that has been ploughed and left unseeded for one or more growing seasons to restore its fertility.

Examples

A fallow field
Crop rotation often includes a year when the land remains fallow.
The farmer left the field fallow for a season to improve the soil quality.
The lush meadow was once a fallow awaiting next year's planting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arable - land that can be used for growing crops
