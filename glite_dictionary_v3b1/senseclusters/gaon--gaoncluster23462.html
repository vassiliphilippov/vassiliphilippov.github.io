gaon

noun
Jewish scholarly title
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡɑˈoʊn / (AmE), / ɡɑˈəʊn / (BrE)
a title given to a leading Jewish scholar, especially in medieval Babylonia

Examples

The gaon of Vilna
The gaon of Vilna was renowned for his deep knowledge of Jewish law.
Many communities sought guidance from the gaon during times of religious uncertainty.
The writings of a famous gaon are still studied in yeshivas today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rabbi - a Jewish religious leader and teacher
  • synonym: sage - a wise and respected scholar or teacher
