snubber

noun
shock-absorbing device
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsnʌbər / (AmE), / ˈsnʌbə / (BrE)
a device used to reduce or absorb shock, vibration, or surges in machinery, vehicles, or electrical systems

Examples

Engine snubber
The technician replaced the worn snubber to prevent damage from sudden stops.
Modern trains use a snubber to minimize vibration and improve passenger comfort.
A faulty snubber can lead to excessive noise and wear in industrial machines.
The technician added a snubber to the machinery to prevent damage from sudden movements.
A snubber is often used in electrical circuits to absorb voltage spikes and protect sensitive components.
Without a snubber, the repeated shocks could quickly wear out the equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damper - a device that reduces vibration or oscillation
  • synonym: shock absorber - a device that absorbs and dampens shock impulses
  • synonym: surge protector - a device that protects electrical equipment from voltage spikes
