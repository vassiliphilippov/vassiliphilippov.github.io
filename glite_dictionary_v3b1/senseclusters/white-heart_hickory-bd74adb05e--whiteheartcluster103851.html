white-heart hickory

noun
a North American hickory tree species
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌhɑrt ˈhɪkəri / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌhɑːt ˈhɪkəri / (BrE)
a North American hickory tree (Carya tomentosa) with smooth bark, 7 to 9 leaflets, and a hard-shelled edible nut

Examples

The white-heart hickory tree
Each autumn, the white-heart hickory produces hard-shelled nuts that attract wildlife.
The dense wood of the white-heart hickory is prized for tool handles and sporting goods.
We found several white-heart hickory trees along the forest trail, their smooth bark easily identifiable.
