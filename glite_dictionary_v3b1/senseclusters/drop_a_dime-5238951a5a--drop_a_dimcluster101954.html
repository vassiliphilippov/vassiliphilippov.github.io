drop a dime

verb
inform police or authorities; betray by giving information
9.3
Pronunciation: / drɑp ə daɪm / (AmE), / drɒp ə daɪm / (BrE)
to inform the police or other authorities about someone's wrongdoing, often anonymously; to betray someone by providing information to law enforcement

Examples

To drop a dime to police
After witnessing the burglary, she decided to drop a dime and called the police.
Even though he knew about the scam, he refused to drop a dime and kept silent.
Rumors spread that he was planning to drop a dime on his former associates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blow the whistle - to report misconduct or illicit activity to authorities
  • antonym: keep quiet - to not inform or betray; remain silent
  • synonym: snitch - to secretly inform authorities about someone's wrongdoing
