kitchen midden

noun
prehistoric refuse heap found in archaeology
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪtʃən ˈmɪdən / (AmE), / ˈkɪtʃən ˈmɪdən /; / ˈkɪtʃɪn ˈmɪd.ən / (BrE)
an archaeological mound or deposit made up of shells, animal bones, and other domestic waste, marking the site of a prehistoric or ancient human settlement

Examples

A kitchen midden site
Archaeologists uncovered a large kitchen midden near the ancient coastal settlement.
Researchers studied the layers of the kitchen midden to learn about the diet of prehistoric people.
The kitchen midden contained thousands of oyster shells and fragments of pottery.
Archaeologists discovered a kitchen midden containing shellfish remains dating back thousands of years.
By studying a kitchen midden, researchers can learn about ancient diets and daily life.
The presence of a kitchen midden suggests that people inhabited the area during the Neolithic era.
