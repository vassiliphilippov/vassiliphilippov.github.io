line width

noun
thickness of a line
3.0
Pronunciation: / laɪn wɪdθ / (AmE), / laɪn wɪdθ / (BrE)
the thickness or breadth of a drawn, printed, or displayed line

Examples

Drawing line width
You can adjust the line width in the drawing software settings.
The architect specified a line width of 0.5 millimeters for the technical drawings.
Increasing the line width makes the graph easier to read on the screen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thickness - the dimension through an object as opposed to its length or width
  • synonym: width - the measurement or extent of something from side to side
