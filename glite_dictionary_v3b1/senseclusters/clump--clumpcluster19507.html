clump

verb
walk with heavy or clumsy steps
Pronunciation: / klʌmp / (AmE), / klʌmp / (BrE)
to walk or move with heavy, clumsy, or noisy steps

Examples

To clump along
He clumped across the wooden floor, making the boards creak with every step.
The children clumped down the hallway in their winter boots.
They clumped along the muddy trail, their shoes sticking in the thick soil.
She could hear him clumping up the stairs after a long day at work.
The children clumped across the wooden floor in their boots.
The horse clumped along the muddy path, echoing through the valley.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clomp - walk with a heavy tread, producing a dull sound
  • synonym: stomp - to walk with heavy, forceful steps
  • synonym: trudge - to walk slowly with heavy steps, typically because of exhaustion
