isobaric

adjective
same mass number
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈbærɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈbærɪk / (BrE)
relating to nuclides or atoms that have the same mass number but different atomic numbers.

Examples

Isobaric nuclei
Scientists compared isobaric nuclei to understand how different elements can share the same mass number.
The experiment focused on isobaric transitions in radioactive decay processes.
In nuclear physics, isobaric atoms are important for studying atomic structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isotopic - relating to atoms with the same atomic number but different mass numbers
