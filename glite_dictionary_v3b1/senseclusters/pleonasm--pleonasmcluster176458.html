pleonasm

noun
use of redundant words
Pronunciation: / ˈpliənˌæzəm / (AmE), / ˈpliːənaz(ə)m / (BrE)
The use of more words than are necessary to express an idea, often resulting in redundancy.

Examples

A pleonasm example
His speech was filled with pleonasm, which made it unnecessarily long.
The phrase 'free gift' is a classic example of pleonasm in everyday language.
Writers should avoid pleonasm to make their sentences clearer and more concise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redundancy - unnecessary repetition of information
  • synonym: tautology - needless repetition of an idea using different words
