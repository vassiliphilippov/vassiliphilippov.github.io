Ferdinand and Isabella

proper noun
joint monarchs of Spain, late 15th century
Pronunciation: / ˌfɜrdɪnænd ənd ˌɪzəˈbɛlə / (AmE), / ˌfɜːdɪnənd ənd ˌɪzəˈbɛlə / (BrE)
the joint monarchs of Spain, Ferdinand V and Isabella I, who reigned together in the late 15th and early 16th centuries, known for unifying Spain and supporting Christopher Columbus's voyages

Examples

Ferdinand and Isabella monarchs
Ferdinand and Isabella are famous for completing the Reconquista and uniting Spain under Christian rule.
Many historians debate the legacy of Ferdinand and Isabella in shaping modern Europe.
The voyages of Columbus were sponsored by Ferdinand and Isabella of Spain.
