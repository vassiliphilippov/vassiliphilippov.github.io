stereotypy

noun
printing plate process
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstɛriəˌtaɪpi / (AmE), / ˈstɪəriəˌtaɪpi / (BrE)
the historical printing process of creating a solid plate or cast from set type for mass reproduction of pages

Examples

Printing stereotypy process
The invention of stereotypy revolutionized the way books and newspapers were produced in the 19th century.
By using stereotypy, printers could create many identical copies of a page quickly and efficiently.
Before digital printing, stereotypy was an essential technique for mass-producing printed materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: printing - the process of producing text and images on paper
  • synonym: stereotype - a printing plate cast from a mold of set type
