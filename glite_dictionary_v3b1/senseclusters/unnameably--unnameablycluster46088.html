unnameably

adverb
indescribably
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈneɪməbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈneɪməbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be named or described, often because it is too shocking, strange, or hard to identify

Examples

To be unnameably anxious
She was unnameably anxious as she waited for the results.
The painting made him feel unnameably moved, though he could not explain why.
He was unnameably disturbed by the strange silence in the house.
The story contained unnameably disturbing details that left the audience in shock.
She felt an unnameably deep sense of dread as she entered the old house.
There was something unnameably wrong about the abandoned playground at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indescribably - in a way that cannot be described
  • antonym: namably - in a way that can be named or specified
  • synonym: unspeakably - in a way that is too bad or shocking to be expressed in words
