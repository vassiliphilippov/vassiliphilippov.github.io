processive

adjective
continuous action
9.6
Pronunciation: / prəˈsɛsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈsɛsɪv / (BrE)
describing an enzyme or process that continues multiple steps without releasing its substrate after each step

Examples

Processive enzyme action
A processive enzyme can add many nucleotides to DNA without detaching.
The processive nature of this protein makes it efficient in catalyzing long reactions.
Researchers studied how processive enzymes differ from those that act in single steps.
