masticator

noun
chewing muscle
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmæstɪˌkeɪtə / (BrE)
a muscle or group of muscles responsible for chewing, especially those in the jaw

Examples

Masticator muscle
The dentist explained that the masticator plays a key role in breaking down food.
Injury to the masticator can make it difficult to chew or speak properly.
The surgeon examined the patient's masticator for signs of swelling after the accident.
