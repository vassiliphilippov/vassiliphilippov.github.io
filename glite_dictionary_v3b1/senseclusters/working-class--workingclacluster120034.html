working-class

adjective
relating to wage-earning manual or industrial workers
2.4 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwɜːr.kɪŋ klæs/ /; / /ˈwɜːr.kɪŋ.klæs/ (AmE), /ˈwɜː.kɪŋ klɑːs/ /; / /ˈwɜː.kɪŋ.klɑːs/ (BrE)
belonging to or relating to the social class of people who do manual or industrial work for wages

Examples

Working-class people
His parents, both working-class, valued hard work and honesty above all else.
She grew up in a working-class neighborhood in the heart of the city.
The politician promised better opportunities for working-class families struggling to make ends meet.
Her parents have always been proud of their working-class roots.
Many working-class families face challenges with access to higher education.
The novel explores the daily lives of working-class people in industrial towns.

  • synonym: proletarian - relating to the proletariat or working-class people
