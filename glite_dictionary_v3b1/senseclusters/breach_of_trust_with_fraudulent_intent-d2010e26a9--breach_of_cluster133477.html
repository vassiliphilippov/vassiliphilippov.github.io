breach of trust with fraudulent intent

noun
crime of fraudulent misuse of entrusted property
9.8
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ əv trʌst wɪð ˈfrɔːdʒələnt ɪnˈtɛnt / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv trʌst wɪð ˈfrɔːdjʊlənt ɪnˈtɛnt / (BrE)
The crime of illegally misappropriating property entrusted to one's care, where the original taking was lawful but the subsequent conversion is fraudulent.

Examples

Breach of trust with fraudulent intent crime
In court, the prosecutor argued that the manager's actions amounted to breach of trust with fraudulent intent.
She was charged with breach of trust with fraudulent intent for selling company property without permission.
The accountant was convicted of breach of trust with fraudulent intent after transferring company funds into his own account.
