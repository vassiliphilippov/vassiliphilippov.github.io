family Umbelliferae

noun
plant family with umbrella-shaped flower clusters
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌʌmbəˈlɪfəˌri / (AmE), / ˌʌmbɛlɪˈfɪəriː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants characterized by umbrella-shaped clusters of small flowers, including parsley, carrot, anise, caraway, celery, and dill

Examples

family Umbelliferae plants
Celery, dill, and parsley are all part of the family Umbelliferae.
Many plants in the family Umbelliferae are used as spices in cooking.
The carrot belongs to the family Umbelliferae, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Related Terms
