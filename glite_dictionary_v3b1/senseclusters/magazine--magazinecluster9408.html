magazine

noun
film-holding chamber in a camera
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡəˌzin / (AmE), / ˈmæɡəziːn / (BrE)
a container or chamber in a camera that holds film and supplies it for exposure as required

Examples

A film magazine
If you don't attach the magazine securely, the film may jam during exposure.
Technicians checked each magazine to ensure there was enough film for the shoot.
The photographer carefully loaded the fresh magazine into the movie camera before filming began.
