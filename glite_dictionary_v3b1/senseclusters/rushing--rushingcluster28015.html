rushing

noun
running play in American football
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(in American football) an attempt to advance the ball by running with it, especially into the defensive line.

Examples

Rushing yards
Defenders focused on stopping the rushing to force more passing plays.
He broke the school record for most rushing yards in a single season.
The team's rushing was particularly effective in the second half of the game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: passing - throwing the ball to advance play (American football)
