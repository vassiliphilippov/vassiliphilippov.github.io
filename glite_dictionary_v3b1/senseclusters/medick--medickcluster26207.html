medick

noun
plant of the Medicago genus
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdɪk / (AmE), / ˈmiːdɪk / (BrE)
any of several plants of the genus Medicago, native to the Old World, with small flowers and trifoliate compound leaves

Examples

The medick plant
Farmers often use medick as a cover crop to improve soil fertility.
Several species of medick can be found growing in European meadows.
The leaves of the medick are arranged in groups of three, similar to clover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alfalfa - forage plant of the genus Medicago, especially Medicago sativa
