ligature

noun
metal band for securing reed on woodwind mouthpiece
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪɡətʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡətʃə / (BrE)
a metal band that secures the reed to the mouthpiece of a clarinet or saxophone

Examples

Clarinet ligature
If your ligature is too loose, the reed may not vibrate properly.
She bought a new ligature made of gold-plated metal for her saxophone.
The clarinetist tightened the ligature to ensure the reed was in place.
