scan

verb
read data from a digital source
Pronunciation: / skæn / (AmE), / skæn / (BrE)
to use a device to read, capture, or reproduce data or images from a digital source or by moving across an area

Examples

To scan a document
After you insert the disk, the system will scan it for any corrupted files.
He used the software to scan the backup tapes and recover the lost documents.
The program will automatically scan all new files for viruses before opening them.
During the process, the laser scans each line of the photograph to reproduce the image.
Technicians use a special device to scan old pictures for digital archiving.
The machine will scan your document and send it to your email.
Engineers scanned the building's structure to identify potential weaknesses.
She scanned the room, searching for a familiar face in the crowd.
The doctor carefully scanned the patient's chart for any irregularities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - produce a duplicate of an image or document
  • synonym: inspect - to look at something carefully in order to learn more about it, find problems, etc.
  • synonym: photocopy - make a photographic copy of a document or image, typically using a machine
  • synonym: read - to access and interpret data from a computer file or device
  • degree higher: scrutinize - to examine or inspect closely and thoroughly
  • antonym: skim - to read or glance through quickly
  • synonym: sweep - to move quickly and smoothly in a wide curve, often referring to a search or scan
