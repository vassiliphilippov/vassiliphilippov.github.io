Alpine glacier

noun
mountain glacier moving from a high valley
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈælpaɪn ˈɡleɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈalpiːn ˈɡlæsiə / (BrE)
A glacier that forms in a mountainous region and moves down from a high valley.

Examples

An Alpine glacier valley
A typical Alpine glacier moves slowly down its valley, shaping the landscape as it goes.
Scientists monitor the retreat of the Alpine glacier to study climate change.
The Alpine glacier carved deep valleys into the mountain over thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: valley glacier - a glacier confined to a valley and flowing down a mountain
