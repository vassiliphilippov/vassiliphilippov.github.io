relaxation

noun
iterative mathematical method for solving equations
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌlækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌlækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a method for solving simultaneous equations by making an initial guess for the solution and then successively reducing the resulting errors with repeated approximations until the errors become acceptably small

Examples

Relaxation method
By applying relaxation repeatedly, the errors in the calculations were minimized.
Many numerical algorithms rely on relaxation to find accurate solutions to complex systems.
The engineer used the relaxation method to solve the set of nonlinear equations.
