Galilaean

noun
inhabitant of Galilee; epithet for Jesus
Pronunciation: / ˌɡælɪˈliːən / (AmE), / ˌɡalɪˈliːən / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Galilee, especially used as an epithet for Jesus Christ.

Examples

A Galilaean preacher
A proud Galilaean stood before the crowd, proclaiming his origins.
Jesus is sometimes called the Galilaean in historical texts.
The early Christians were often referred to as Galilaeans by their contemporaries.
