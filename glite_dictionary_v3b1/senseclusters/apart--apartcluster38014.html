apart

adjective
separate or distinguished from others
1.7 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàp…ërt / (AmE), / …ôÀàp…ëÀêt / (BrE)
separate, distinct, or isolated from others (describing qualities, people, or things)

Examples

Set apart by talent
Her creative vision set her apart from other artists in her field.
His moral standards kept him apart from the rest of his peers.
The researchers considered themselves a group apart due to their unique methodology.
Her reserved nature kept her apart from her classmates throughout high school.
The monastery stood apart from the bustle of city life, surrounded by thick forests.
The small indigenous tribe remained apart from modern society for centuries.

Synonyms

  • separate
    2.1 61/m
    separate is not joined or connected and describes things standing alone, while apart means being distinct or set away.
  • distinct
    3.5 24/m
    distinct is clearly different and easy to notice, while apart focuses on being separate or not connected.
  • different
    1.0 608/m
    different means not the same in qualities or form, while apart stresses being separate or not together.
  • dissimilar
    3.8 2.13/m
    dissimilar means unlike in appearance or quality, while apart highlights physical or logical separation between items.
  • isolated
    3.8 4.60/m
    isolated is far away or cut off from others and often alone, while apart can mean simply separate or different.
