word-blind

adjective
unable to recognize written words; relating to alexia
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːdˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈwɜːdˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
Relating to or symptomatic of alexia; unable to recognize or read written words due to brain injury or disorder.

Examples

Word-blind patient
After his stroke, he became word-blind and could no longer read even simple texts.
Educational support is crucial for children who are word-blind to help them develop alternative reading strategies.
The neurologist diagnosed her as word-blind following the traumatic brain injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alexic - Relating to or affected by alexia (inability to read)
