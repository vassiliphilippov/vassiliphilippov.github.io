metaplasia

noun
cell type transformation
9.9
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈpleɪ.jə/ /; / /mə.tə.ˈpleɪ.jə/ (AmE), /me.tə.ˈpleɪ.ʒə/ /; / /me.tə.ˈpleɪ.jə/ (BrE)
a process in which one type of mature cell changes into another type, often as a response to stress or injury

Examples

Cell metaplasia
Prolonged acid reflux can lead to metaplasia in the cells lining the esophagus.
Doctors monitor patients for signs of metaplasia to prevent the development of more serious conditions.
Chronic irritation from smoking may cause metaplasia in the respiratory tract.
