unoccupied

adjective
not seized or controlled; not occupied
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɑːkjəˌpaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒkjʊpaɪd / (BrE)
(of a place or area) not seized, controlled, or taken over by a particular group, force, or authority.

Examples

Unoccupied territory
Many families sought shelter in the unoccupied zone during the war.
Several border villages remained unoccupied throughout the conflict.
The territory was declared unoccupied after the enemy retreated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: occupied - seized and controlled by a group or authority
