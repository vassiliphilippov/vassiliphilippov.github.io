bring to a boil

verb
cause to reach boiling point or critical stage
3.8
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ tə ə bɔɪl / (AmE), / brɪŋ tə ə bɔɪl / (BrE)
to heat a liquid until it begins to boil, or to cause a situation or feeling to reach a critical or intense point

Examples

To bring the sauce to a boil
After simmering for ten minutes, slowly bring the sauce to a boil and stir constantly.
Let the water bring to a boil before adding the pasta to the pot.
Rising frustrations among the workers finally brought the issue to a boil during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boil over - to overflow while boiling or to lose control of emotions
