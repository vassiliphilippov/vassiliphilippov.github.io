Gosplan

proper noun
Soviet planning agency
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑsˌplæn / (AmE), / ˈɡɒsˌplæn / (BrE)
the government agency in the former Soviet Union responsible for central economic planning

Examples

The Gosplan committee
The Gosplan played a central role in setting production targets for Soviet industries.
Historians often study the impact of Gosplan on the Soviet Union's economic development.
After the collapse of the USSR, the functions of Gosplan were dissolved or transferred.

Related Terms

  • antonym: market economy - an economic system based on supply and demand with little government control
  • synonym: planning commission - a government body responsible for economic or development planning
