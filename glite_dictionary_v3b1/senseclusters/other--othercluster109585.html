other

verb
to treat as different
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʌðər / (AmE), / ˈʌðə / (BrE)
to treat or regard a person or group as fundamentally different or alien, often leading to exclusion or discrimination

Examples

To other a group
The documentary explores how society often others people with disabilities.
She felt that her classmates were othering her because of her accent.
Policies that other certain groups can lead to social division and misunderstanding.
She felt hurt when her classmates othered her because of her accent.
The documentary explores how certain groups have been othered throughout history.
It is important not to other people just because they have different beliefs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to include or welcome someone as part of a group
  • synonym: alienate - to make someone feel isolated or unwelcome
  • synonym: discriminate - to treat someone unfairly because of their group or identity
  • synonym: exclude - to leave someone out or not include them
