pectoralis

noun
chest muscle (anatomy)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛktəˈreɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɛktəˈreɪlɪs / (BrE)
either of the two large muscles on each side of the upper chest, responsible for movement of the shoulder and upper arm

Examples

The pectoralis muscle
He strained his pectoralis while lifting weights at the gym.
Surgeons sometimes use the pectoralis for reconstructive chest procedures.
The pectoralis muscles help you push objects away from your body.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: pectoralis major - the larger of the two pectoral muscles, covering the upper chest
  • degree lower: pectoralis minor - the smaller, thinner pectoral muscle beneath the pectoralis major
