swound

verb
to faint
9.9
Pronunciation: / swaʊnd / (AmE), / swaʊnd / (BrE)
to faint or lose consciousness, especially in an old-fashioned or literary context.

Examples

To swound from shock
In the old tale, the knight did swound upon hearing the dreadful news.
She seemed about to swound from the heat and excitement of the festival.
The poet wrote of maidens who would swound at the sight of danger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: revive - to regain consciousness
  • synonym: swoon - to faint from extreme emotion
