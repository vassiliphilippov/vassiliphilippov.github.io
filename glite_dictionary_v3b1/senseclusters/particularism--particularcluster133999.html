particularism

noun
focus on individual cases or interests
7.5
Pronunciation: / pərˈtɪkjələrɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈtɪkjʊlərɪzəm / (BrE)
The practice or principle of focusing attention on individual cases, groups, or interests rather than considering all as part of a whole.

Examples

Cultural particularism
His particularism prevented him from supporting broader reforms.
Many critics argue that particularism undermines efforts at social unity.
The country's policy reflects a strong sense of particularism in its regional governance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: universalism - the principle of treating all cases or people in the same way
