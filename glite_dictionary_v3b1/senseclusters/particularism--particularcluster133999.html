particularism

noun
focus on individual cases or interests

Pronunciation: /pə.ˈtɪk.jə.lə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /pə.ˈtɪk.jʊ.lə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /pə.ˈtɪk.jə.lə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /pə.ˈtɪk.jʊə.lə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The practice or principle of focusing attention on individual cases, groups, or interests rather than considering all as part of a whole.

Examples

Cultural particularism

Many critics argue that particularism undermines efforts at social unity.
