bend dexter

noun
heraldic band from upper right to lower left
Pronunciation: / bɛnd ˈdɛkstər / (AmE), / bɛnd ˈdɛkstə / (BrE)
A diagonal band or stripe on a shield running from the upper right (from the bearer's point of view) to the lower left.

Examples

A bend dexter stripe
A bend dexter is often used in heraldry to symbolize defense or protection.
He explained that the bend dexter on the shield indicates the bearer's allegiance.
The family crest features a bend dexter in bright gold across a blue field.
