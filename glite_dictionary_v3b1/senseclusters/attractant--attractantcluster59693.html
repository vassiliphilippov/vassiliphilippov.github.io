attractant

noun
substance that attracts
5.9
Pronunciation: / əˈtræktənt / (AmE), / əˈtræktənt / (BrE)
a substance that attracts animals, insects, or people by appealing to their senses or instincts

Examples

Insect attractant
The gardener used a floral attractant to bring more butterflies into the yard.
Scientists tested a new attractant to monitor the population of fruit flies in the orchard.
A sweet-smelling attractant was placed near the trap to catch the mice.
The researchers placed an attractant near the trap to study the behavior of the insects.
Gardeners often use a sweet-smelling attractant to draw pollinators to their flowers.
A chemical attractant was added to the bait to increase its effectiveness in catching rodents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lure - something used to attract or tempt
  • antonym: repellent - a substance that drives away insects or animals
