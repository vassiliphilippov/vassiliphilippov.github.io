go too far

verb
exceed acceptable limits
1.4
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tu ˈfɑr / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tuː ˈfɑː / (BrE)
to exceed reasonable or acceptable limits in speech, action, or behavior

Examples

To go too far in speech
In his attempt to discipline the team, the coach went too far and was criticized by parents.
It's important to speak up, but don't go too far and become disrespectful.
She tends to go too far with her jokes, sometimes offending people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: go overboard - to do something to an excessive degree
  • antonym: hold back - to restrain oneself from excessive action or words
  • synonym: overstep the mark - to go beyond what is considered acceptable or appropriate
