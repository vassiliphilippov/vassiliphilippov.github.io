impingement

noun
strong impact or influence, often unwanted
4.1 7.0
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpɪndʒ.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpɪndʒ.mənt/ (BrE)
the action or effect of one thing having a strong impact or influence on another, often in a way that is unwanted or intrusive

Examples

Impingement on rights
He objected to the government's impingement on personal privacy rights.
Some artists worry about the impingement of commercial interests on their creative freedom.
The impingement of noise from the nearby construction site made it difficult to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influence - the power to have an effect on someone or something
  • synonym: intrusion - the act of entering or affecting without invitation or permission
