crude

noun
unrefined petroleum oil
5.5 5.42/m
Pronunciation: / kruÀêd / (AmE), / kruÀêd / (BrE)
A dark, unrefined oil consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, extracted from the earth and used to produce fuels and other products.

Examples

Barrel of crude
Much of the world's energy supply is still dependent on crude.
The price of crude rose sharply after the announcement of new sanctions.
Vast tankers transport crude from oil-producing countries to refineries around the world.

Synonyms

  • crude oil
    3.4 4.01/m
    crude oil is the full name for the unrefined dark liquid used to make fuels, while crude is a shorter, less formal label.
  • petroleum
    4.4 9.75/m
    petroleum is a broad, technical term for natural hydrocarbon liquids used in industry, while crude is the unrefined oil taken from the ground.
  • fossil oil
    4.2 0.00/m
    fossil oil is a descriptive term meaning oil formed from ancient plants and animals, while crude refers to the unrefined market oil.
