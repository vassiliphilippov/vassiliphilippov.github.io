process

noun
legal writ compelling appearance in court
8.3 13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀê.s…õs / (AmE), / Ààpr…ô ä.ses / (BrE)
(Law) A writ issued by authority of a court, compelling a defendant's appearance in a civil suit, with failure to appear resulting in a default judgment.

Examples

Court process
Failure to respond to the process can lead to a default judgment.
The court issued a process to ensure the defendant would appear for the hearing.
Without proper process, the lawsuit could not proceed against the absent party.
