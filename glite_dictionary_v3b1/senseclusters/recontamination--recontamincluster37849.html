recontamination

noun
becoming contaminated again
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˌtæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˌtæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of something becoming contaminated again after being cleaned or sterilized

Examples

Equipment recontamination
Improper storage can lead to recontamination of sterilized equipment.
After cleaning the lab, strict procedures are needed to prevent recontamination.
The food was exposed to air, resulting in recontamination before it could be packaged.
Strict hygiene protocols are necessary to prevent recontamination of medical equipment after sterilization.
The water supply faced recontamination due to a broken pipe in the distribution system.
After cleaning the laboratory, any recontamination could compromise the results of the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contamination - the process of making something impure or dirty
  • antonym: decontamination - the process of removing harmful substances
  • antonym: sterilization - the process of making something free from bacteria or other living microorganisms
