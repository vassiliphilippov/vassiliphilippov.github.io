Ananda

proper noun
Buddha's disciple
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈnæn.də/ (AmE), /ə.ˈnæn.də/ (BrE)
the name of a principal disciple and attendant of the Buddha, known for preserving many early Buddhist teachings

Examples

Ananda disciple
Ananda is often mentioned in Buddhist texts as the Buddha's closest attendant.
Many early Buddhist teachings were preserved thanks to Ananda's memory and dedication.
During the First Buddhist Council, Ananda recited many of the Buddha's discourses from memory.
