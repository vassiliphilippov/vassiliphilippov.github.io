common sunflower

noun
a tall plant with yellow flowers and edible seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈsʌnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈsʌnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a tall annual plant with large yellow flowers and edible seeds, widely cultivated for its oil and ornamental value

Examples

A common sunflower plant
A single common sunflower can reach over six feet tall by late summer.
Farmers often grow common sunflower for its oil-rich seeds and animal feed.
The garden was bright with dozens of blooming common sunflower plants.
