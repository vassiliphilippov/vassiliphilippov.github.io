away

adverb
from one's possession or place
Pronunciation: / əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to or at a different place, or to remove something from where it was

Examples

Throw away old things
After cleaning the attic, she threw away many old magazines.
He gave away all his childhood toys to charity.
They handed away the prizes at the end of the contest.
Please put your toys away when you're finished playing.
She swept the crumbs away after dinner.
The old sign was taken away and replaced with a new one.
