having no money; lacking funds

Examples

Completely broke
After losing his job, he was completely broke and struggled to pay rent.
I'm broke until payday, so I can't go out this weekend.
She felt broke after paying all her bills at once.

Synonyms

  • stone-broke
    8.6 0.01/m
    stone-broke means completely without money and unable to pay small bills, while broke means lacking funds in general.
  • skint
    9.9 0.03/m
    skint is a British informal word for having little or no money, while broke is more widely used in English.
  • penniless
    4.3 1.02/m
    penniless means having no money and often suggests long-term poverty, while broke is common for short-term lack of funds.
  • bust
    5.4 0.06/m
    bust often means legally or financially bankrupt, used for companies or people, while broke is general everyday lack of money.
