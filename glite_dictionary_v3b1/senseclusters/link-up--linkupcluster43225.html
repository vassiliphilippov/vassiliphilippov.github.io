link-up

verb
to meet or join
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪŋk ʌp / (AmE), / ˈlɪŋk ʌp / (BrE)
to meet or join with another person or group, especially for a short time or informally

Examples

To link-up at a cafe
Let's link-up at the cafe after work to discuss the project.
They agreed to link-up during the conference for a quick chat.
We can link-up online if meeting in person is not possible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: connect - to join or be joined with someone or something
  • synonym: meet - to come together with someone by arrangement
  • antonym: separate - to move apart or stop being together
