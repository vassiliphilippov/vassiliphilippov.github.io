An abnormal sensation of the skin, such as tingling, prickling, burning, or numbness, typically caused by nerve damage or irritation.

Examples

Chronic paresthesia symptoms
Diabetic neuropathy can lead to chronic paresthesia in the feet and toes.
Many people experience paresthesia or 'pins and needles' after sitting with their legs crossed for a long time.
The patient reported a constant paresthesia in her left hand after the injury.
After sitting with his legs crossed too long, he experienced paraesthesia in his foot.
People with diabetic neuropathy often complain of persistent paraesthesia in their hands and feet.
The patient described a burning paraesthesia that made it difficult to sleep at night.

Synonyms
