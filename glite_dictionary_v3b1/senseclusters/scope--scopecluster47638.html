scope

verb
to define limits
4.9
Pronunciation: / skoʊp / (AmE), / skəʊp / (BrE)
to carefully define or limit the range, extent, or details of something, especially a project or task

Examples

To scope a project
Before development began, the engineers scoped the software requirements in detail.
It is important to scope your research project to avoid taking on too much.
The consultant scoped the client's needs during the initial meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expand - to increase the size, range, or amount of something
  • synonym: outline - to give a general description or plan of something
  • synonym: specify - to state or describe something clearly and exactly
