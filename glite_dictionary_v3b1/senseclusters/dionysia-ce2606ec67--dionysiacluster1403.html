Dionysia

noun
ancient Greek festival honoring Dionysus
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈnɪziə / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈnɪzɪə / (BrE)
An orgiastic festival held in ancient Greece in honor of the god Dionysus, featuring drama, processions, and celebrations.

Examples

The Dionysia festival
During the Dionysia, crowds would gather in Athens to honor Dionysus with days of revelry and drama.
Several tragedies and comedies premiered at the Dionysia, making it a major event in the theatrical calendar.
The ancient Athenians celebrated the Dionysia with theatrical performances and lively processions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bacchanalia - the Roman festivals of Bacchus, similar in character to the Dionysia
