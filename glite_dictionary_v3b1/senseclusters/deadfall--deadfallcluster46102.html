deadfall

noun
fallen forest debris
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌfɔl / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌfɔːl / (BrE)
fallen trees, large branches, or woody debris lying on the ground in a forest, often obstructing movement

Examples

Forest deadfall
After the storm, hikers had to climb over deadfall scattered across the trail.
The forest floor was covered with deadfall, making it difficult to walk through.
Rangers regularly clear deadfall to keep the park's paths safe for visitors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearing - an open space in a forest without trees or debris
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks the way or makes movement difficult
  • synonym: windfall - a tree or branch blown down by the wind
