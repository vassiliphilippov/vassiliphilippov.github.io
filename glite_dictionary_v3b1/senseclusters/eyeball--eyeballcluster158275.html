eyeball

verb
to estimate visually
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.bɔːl/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.bɔːl/ (BrE)
to look at or estimate the size, amount, or distance of something just by using your eyes, without measuring

Examples

To eyeball the amount
He decided to eyeball the distance instead of using a tape measure.
When cooking, she often eyeballs the ingredients rather than following a strict recipe.
Can you eyeball how many people are in the room right now?

Related Terms

  • synonym: guess - to estimate or form an opinion without enough information
  • antonym: measure - to find the exact size, amount, or degree of something using a tool
