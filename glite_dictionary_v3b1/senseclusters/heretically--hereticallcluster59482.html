heretically

adverb
against accepted beliefs
Pronunciation: / həˈrɛtɪkli / (AmE), / həˈrɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that goes against accepted beliefs, doctrines, or established opinions, especially in religion or ideology

Examples

Argue heretically
He argued heretically that some long-held scientific theories should be questioned.
The artist painted heretically, ignoring the traditional styles of her time.
Speaking heretically in that community could lead to social exclusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orthodoxly - in a way that follows traditional or accepted beliefs
  • synonym: unorthodoxly - in a way that does not follow traditional or accepted standards
