pseudopod

noun
temporary projection in microorganisms
5.6 8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːdəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a temporary projection of the cytoplasm in certain single-celled organisms, used for movement or engulfing food

Examples

A pseudopod projection
Some white blood cells use a pseudopod to engulf bacteria.
The amoeba extended a pseudopod to reach its food.
Under the microscope, you can see the pseudopod forming and retracting as the cell moves.
