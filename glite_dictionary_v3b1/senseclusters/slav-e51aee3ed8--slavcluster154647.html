Slav

noun
member of a Slavic-speaking people
5.0
Pronunciation: / slæv / (AmE), / slɑːv / (BrE)
a member of a group of peoples of eastern Europe and Asian Russia who speak a Slavic (Slavonic) language, such as Russian, Polish, Czech, or Serbian

Examples

A Slav nation
Poland, Russia, and the Czech Republic are countries where many Slavs live.
She is a proud Slav, deeply connected to her family's heritage and language.
The migration of the Slavs into Europe had a significant impact on its cultural landscape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Slavic - relating to the Slavs or their languages
