treacherous

adjective
dangerously unstable or unpredictable
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛtʃərəs / (BrE)
(of ground, conditions, etc.) dangerously unstable or unpredictable and likely to cause harm or difficulty.

Examples

Treacherous mountain path
Driving became almost impossible on the treacherous icy roads last night.
The hikers avoided the treacherous path, fearing a sudden landslide.
The river looks calm but its currents can be surprisingly treacherous at certain times of year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dangerous - likely to cause harm or injury
  • antonym: safe - not likely to cause harm or danger
