separate

verb
to move apart or go different ways
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛpəreɪt / (BrE)
to move apart or stop being together; to end an association or relationship

Examples

To separate from friends
After college, the friends decided to separate and pursue different careers.
As soon as the meeting ended, everyone began to separate and head home.
The two groups will separate at the crossroads and meet again in the evening.
After years of disagreements, they finally separated and went their own ways.
At the crossroads, we separated and promised to keep in touch.
The company decided to separate from its European partner last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break up - to end a romantic relationship or close association
  • synonym: part - to leave each other's company or move in different directions
  • synonym: split up - to end a relationship or partnership
  • antonym: unite - to come or bring together to form a whole
