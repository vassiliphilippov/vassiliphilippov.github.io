fibrocartilage

noun
tough cartilage with fibrous bundles
7.3 7.7
Pronunciation: /faɪ.broʊ.ˈkɑːr.tə.lɪdʒ/ /; / /faɪ.brə.ˈkɑːr.tə.lɪdʒ/ (AmE), /faɪ.brəʊ.ˈkɑː.tə.lɪdʒ/ /; / /faɪ.brə.ˈkɑː.tə.lɪdʒ/ (BrE)
A type of cartilage containing abundant fibrous bundles of collagen, making it tough and resilient, found in intervertebral discs and certain joints.

Examples

The fibrocartilage tissue
Injury to the knee often involves damage to the meniscus, a crescent-shaped piece of fibrocartilage.
Some joints, such as the pubic symphysis, are connected by fibrocartilage rather than bone.
The intervertebral discs are made of fibrocartilage to withstand pressure between vertebrae.
