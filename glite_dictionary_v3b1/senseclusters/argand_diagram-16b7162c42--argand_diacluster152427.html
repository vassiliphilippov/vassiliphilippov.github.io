Argand diagram

noun
complex number graph
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrɡænd ˈdaɪəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡænd ˈdaɪəɡræm / (BrE)
a diagram that represents complex numbers as points in a plane, with the horizontal axis for real parts and the vertical axis for imaginary parts

Examples

The Argand diagram plane
The Argand diagram is a useful tool for visualizing complex numbers in mathematics.
Each point on the Argand diagram corresponds to a unique complex number.
Students learned to plot both real and imaginary parts on an Argand diagram during the lesson.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cartesian plane - a plane defined by two perpendicular axes, used for plotting points
  • synonym: complex plane - a plane used to represent complex numbers graphically
