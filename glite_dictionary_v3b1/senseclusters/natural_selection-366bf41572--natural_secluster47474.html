natural selection

noun
process driving evolution by adaptation
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈnætʃ.ər.əl sə.ˈlek.ʃən/ /; / /ˈnætʃ.rəl sə.ˈlek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈnætʃ.ər.əl sə.ˈlek.ʃən/ /; / /ˈnætʃ.rəl sə.ˈlek.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process by which organisms that are better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring, leading to evolutionary change.

Examples

Natural selection process
Charles Darwin developed the theory of natural selection to explain how species evolve over time.
In natural selection, individuals with advantageous traits are more likely to survive and reproduce.
The peppered moth is a classic example of natural selection in action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: artificial selection - the intentional breeding of organisms with desired traits
  • synonym: evolution - the gradual development of organisms over time
