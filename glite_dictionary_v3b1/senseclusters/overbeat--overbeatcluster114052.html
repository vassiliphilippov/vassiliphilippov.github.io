overbeat

verb
to play too forcefully
8.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈbiːt/ /; / /ə.vər.ˈbiːt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈbiːt/ /; / /ə.və.ˈbiːt/ (BrE)
to strike or play a drum or rhythm too forcefully or loudly, resulting in excessive sound

Examples

To overbeat the drums
If you overbeat the drums, the music can become overwhelming and lose its subtlety.
The band leader asked the percussionist not to overbeat during the quiet sections of the song.
She realized she had overbeat the rhythm, making it difficult for the dancers to keep time.
