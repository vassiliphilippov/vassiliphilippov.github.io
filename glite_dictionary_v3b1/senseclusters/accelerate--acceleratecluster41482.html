Examples

Synonyms

  • speed
    1.0 10/m
    speed is a common verb meaning to make something happen faster, while accelerate is a slightly more formal word for increasing speed.
  • speed up
    2.5 8.21/m
    speed up is a phrasal verb used in speech to make something go or happen faster, while accelerate is a neutral, often formal verb.
  • hasten
    4.6 1.70/m
    hasten is to make something happen sooner, often with a sense of urgency, while accelerate is about increasing speed or rate.
  • quicken
    4.1 1.71/m
    quicken means to make something move or happen faster, often for living things or feelings, while accelerate is more general.
  • expedite
    6.5 2.09/m
    expedite means to speed up a process or task by removing obstacles, while accelerate focuses on increasing speed or rate.
