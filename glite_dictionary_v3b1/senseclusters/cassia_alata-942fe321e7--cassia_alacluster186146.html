Cassia alata

noun
tropical shrub with medicinal uses
Pronunciation: / ˈkæsiə əˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˈkæsiə əˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub native to the Americas, bearing yellow flowers and large leaves whose juice is used in traditional medicine to treat ringworm and poisonous bites.

Examples

Cassia alata plant
Many gardeners appreciate Cassia alata for its bright yellow flowers and rapid growth.
The leaves of Cassia alata are commonly used in herbal remedies for skin infections.
Traditional healers in tropical regions still use Cassia alata to treat fungal diseases.
