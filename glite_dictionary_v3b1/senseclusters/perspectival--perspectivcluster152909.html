perspectival

adjective
dependent on perspective
Pronunciation: / ˌpɜrspɛkˈtaɪvəl / (AmE), / ˌpɜːspɛkˈtaɪvəl / (BrE)
describing something whose truth, meaning, or evaluation depends on a particular person's or group's perspective or standards

Examples

Perspectival truth
The meaning of some words is perspectival, changing depending on who is speaking.
Her judgment of the movie was highly perspectival, shaped by her own experiences.
In linguistics, certain adjectives are considered perspectival because their truth depends on the speaker's viewpoint.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objective - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
  • synonym: subjective - based on personal feelings or opinions
