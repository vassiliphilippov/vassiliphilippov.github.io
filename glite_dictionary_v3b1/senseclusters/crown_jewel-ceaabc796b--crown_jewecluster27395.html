crown jewel

noun
the most valuable asset
4.0
Pronunciation: / kraʊn ˈdʒuː.əl / (AmE), / kraʊn ˈdʒuː.əl / (BrE)
the most valuable or prized asset or possession, especially the most important part of a business or organization

Examples

Company's crown jewel
Analysts agreed that the airline's routes in Asia were its true crown jewels.
During the merger, the company's crown jewel was its popular software division.
The new CEO decided to sell off all but the crown jewels of the business.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liability - something that acts as a disadvantage or burden, especially in business
  • synonym: prize asset - a valuable and desirable asset, often targeted in acquisitions
