Anishinaabe

proper noun
Indigenous peoples group
Pronunciation: / əˌniːʃɪˈnɑːbeɪ / (AmE), / əˌniːʃɪˈnɑːbeɪ / (BrE)
the group of related Indigenous peoples of North America, including the Ojibwe, Odawa, and Algonquin, considered collectively

Examples

The Anishinaabe peoples
The Anishinaabe have a rich tradition of storytelling passed down through generations.
Many Anishinaabe communities are located around the Great Lakes region.
The language of the Anishinaabe is still spoken by many elders today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Algonquin - a member of an Indigenous people of eastern Canada
  • synonym: Ojibwe - a member of a Native American people of the northern Midwest and Canada
