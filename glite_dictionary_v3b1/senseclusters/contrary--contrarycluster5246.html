contrary

noun
the opposite; direct opposition
3.5 15/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ën.tr…õr.i /; / Ààk…ëÀênÀåtr…õri / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.tr…ô.ri / (BrE)
the opposite or something that is completely different from what has been stated or expected

Examples

Quite the contrary
Despite the rumors, the evidence proved quite the contrary.
He claimed innocence, but all facts pointed to the contrary.
On the contrary, I believe we are making good progress.
Despite rumors to the contrary, the festival will go ahead as planned.
Many believe the new policy will succeed, but the evidence suggests the contrary.
She accepted his invitation, although all her instincts told her to do the contrary.

Synonyms

  • opposite
    1.0 27/m
    opposite refers to something directly different or the exact reverse, while contrary refers to what is said or expected.
  • reverse
    4.2 5.55/m
    reverse is the exact flip or undoing of a situation, while contrary is an idea that is opposite of what is stated.
  • opposite
    1.0 20/m
    opposite is an adjective meaning completely different in nature or direction, while contrary is used as a noun for the stated or expected reverse.
