contrary

noun
the opposite; direct opposition
1.3 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.trɛr.i /; / ˈkɑːnˌtrɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə.ri / (BrE)
the opposite or something that is completely different from what has been stated or expected

Examples

Quite the contrary
Despite the rumors, the evidence proved quite the contrary.
He claimed innocence, but all facts pointed to the contrary.
On the contrary, I believe we are making good progress.
Despite rumors to the contrary, the festival will go ahead as planned.
Many believe the new policy will succeed, but the evidence suggests the contrary.
She accepted his invitation, although all her instincts told her to do the contrary.
