fluoridation

noun
adding fluoride to water
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌflʊr.əˈdeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌflɔː.rɪˈdeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding a fluoride compound to a water supply in order to reduce tooth decay

Examples

Fluoridation of water
Debates around the safety of fluoridation have continued for decades.
Many cities practice fluoridation to help prevent tooth decay among residents.
The community voted against fluoridation of the public water supply.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defluoridation - removal of fluoride from a water supply
  • synonym: fluorination - the process of adding fluorine or fluoride to something
