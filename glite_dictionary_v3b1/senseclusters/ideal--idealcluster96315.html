ideal

adjective
existing only as a concept or idea
4.9
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdiəl /; / aɪˈdiːəl / (AmE), / aɪˈdɪəl / (BrE)
existing only in the mind or as a concept; relating to ideas or philosophy rather than physical reality

Examples

Ideal world
Her essay contrasted the ideal city described in literature with the flaws of actual urban life.
Some philosophers argue that justice, as discussed in theory, is often only ideal and rarely achieved in practice.
The artist created an ideal landscape that could never exist in the real world.
Her arguments were grounded in an ideal conception of reality, not in physical phenomena.
Some schools of thought believe in an ideal world that is fundamentally mental rather than material.
The philosopher explained an ideal system where only ideas truly exist.
