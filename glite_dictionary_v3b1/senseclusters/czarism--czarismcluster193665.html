czarism

noun
absolute rule by one
9.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààz…ëÀêr…™z…ôm / (AmE), / Ààz…ëÀêr…™z…ôm / (BrE)
A system or period of government ruled by a czar or tsar, especially in Russia before 1917, often characterized by autocratic or oppressive power and sometimes used figuratively for arbitrary rule.

Synonyms

  • czardom
    9.9 0.01/m
    czardom refers to the historical realm and rule under a czar, while czarism emphasizes autocratic government and arbitrary power.
  • czarship
    9.9 0.00/m
    czarship names the office or authority held by a czar, while czarism describes the wider autocratic system or period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dictatorship - government by a dictator with total power
