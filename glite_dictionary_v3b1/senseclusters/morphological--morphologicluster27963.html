morphological

adjective
relating to word structure or formation
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔr.fəˈlɑ.dʒɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌmɔː.fəˈlɒ.dʒɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to the structure, formation, or study of words and their parts in a language, especially how words are formed from morphemes

Examples

Morphological analysis of words
English uses various morphological processes to create new words from existing ones.
Her research focuses on morphological changes in borrowed vocabulary.
The linguist conducted a morphological analysis of the language's verb forms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: syntactic - relating to syntax, the arrangement of words and phrases in sentences
