grain alcohol

noun
pure distilled ethanol from grain
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪn ˈælkəhɒl / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪn ˈælkəhɒl / (BrE)
pure alcohol (ethanol) made by distilling fermented grain, often used as a solvent, in medicinal products, and as a fuel additive

Examples

Grain alcohol solvent
The laboratory uses grain alcohol as a solvent in many experiments.
The new fuel blend contains grain alcohol to reduce carbon emissions.
You can buy grain alcohol at some liquor stores, but it is very strong and should be handled with care.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethanol - the type of alcohol found in alcoholic drinks and as a solvent or fuel
  • antonym: methanol - a different type of alcohol used as a solvent or fuel, poisonous if consumed
