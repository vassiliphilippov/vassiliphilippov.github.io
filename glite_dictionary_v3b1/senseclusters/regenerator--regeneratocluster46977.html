regenerator

noun
signal-restoring device
6.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛnəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛnəreɪtə / (BrE)
a device that receives a weakened electrical or digital signal and retransmits it at full strength to restore signal quality

Examples

Signal regenerator
The technician installed a regenerator to boost the signal along the fiber optic cable.
Without a regenerator, the digital data would be too weak to reach the receiver clearly.
Modern communication networks often rely on a regenerator to maintain high-quality transmissions over long distances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amplifier - a device that increases the strength of an electrical signal
  • synonym: repeater - a device that receives and retransmits a signal to extend its range
