linguistic profiling

noun
identifying social background from speech
3.3
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈproʊfaɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈprəʊfaɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of identifying or making assumptions about a person's race, religion, or social class based on their speech characteristics or dialect

Examples

Linguistic profiling in housing
Policies have been proposed to prevent linguistic profiling in housing and employment decisions.
Researchers study linguistic profiling to understand how language can influence social bias.
The landlord was accused of linguistic profiling after rejecting applicants based on their accents.

  • synonym: racial profiling - identifying or targeting individuals based on race or ethnicity
