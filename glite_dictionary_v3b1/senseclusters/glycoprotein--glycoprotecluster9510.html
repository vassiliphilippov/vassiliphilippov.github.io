glycoprotein

noun
protein with carbohydrate chains
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɡlaɪkəˈproʊtin / (AmE), / ˌɡlaɪkəʊˈprəʊtiːn / (BrE)
a protein molecule that has carbohydrate chains attached to it

Examples

A glycoprotein molecule
Researchers studied the glycoprotein composition of cell membranes to better understand immune responses.
Some hormones are classified as glycoproteins because of the carbohydrate groups attached to their structure.
The viral envelope contains several glycoprotein spikes essential for infection.
