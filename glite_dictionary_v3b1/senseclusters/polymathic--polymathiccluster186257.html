polymathic

adjective
knowledgeable in many fields
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈmæθɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmæθɪk / (BrE)
having or involving knowledge or learning in many different subjects or fields

Examples

Polymathic knowledge
Her polymathic interests ranged from physics to classical music.
The polymathic professor could discuss art, science, and literature with equal expertise.
Leonardo da Vinci is often described as a polymathic genius of the Renaissance.
Her polymathic interests allowed her to connect ideas from literature, science, and art.
The museum's polymathic exhibition combined technology, history, and visual design.
He admired Leonardo da Vinci for his polymathic achievements across so many fields.
