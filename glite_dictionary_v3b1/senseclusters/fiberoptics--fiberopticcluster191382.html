fiberoptics

noun
light transmission through glass or plastic fibers
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbərˈɑptɪks / (AmE), / ˌfaɪbərˈɒptɪks /; / ˌfaɪbəˈɒptɪks / (BrE)
The transmission of light signals through thin glass or plastic fibers, mainly used for high-speed data communication and imaging.

Examples

Fiberoptics data transmission
Advances in fiberoptics have revolutionized medical imaging and telecommunications.

Medical instruments often use fibreoptics to view inside the human body.
Recent advances in fibreoptics make data transmission much faster and more reliable.
