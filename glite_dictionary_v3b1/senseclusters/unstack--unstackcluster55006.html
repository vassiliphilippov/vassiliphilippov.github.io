unstack

verb
to remove from stack
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstæk / (AmE), / ʌnˈstæk / (BrE)
to remove the top item or frame from a stack, especially in computing or data structures

Examples

To unstack a frame
The program will unstack the last command to undo the previous action.
When an error occurs, the system must unstack several frames to recover.
Developers often unstack items from the call stack during debugging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pop - to remove the top item from a stack in computing
  • antonym: push - to add an item to the top of a stack in computing
