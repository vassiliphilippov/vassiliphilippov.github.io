castration anxiety

noun
psychoanalytic anxiety about sexual functions
6.8
Pronunciation: / kæˈstreɪʃən æŋˈzaɪəti / (AmE), / kasˈtreɪʃn æŋˈzaɪəti / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) anxiety arising from real or imagined threats to one's sexual functions, originally thought to affect men but can apply to women.

Examples

Castration anxiety in psychoanalysis
Freud considered castration anxiety to be central to the development of the male psyche.
Some theorists debate whether castration anxiety can also be experienced by women.
The therapist explained how childhood fears could manifest as castration anxiety in adult life.
