adulteration

noun
the act of making something impure
Pronunciation: / əˌdʌl.təˈreɪ.ʃən /; / əˌdʌltəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌdʌl.təˈreɪ.ʃən /; / əˌdʌltəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something impure or poorer in quality by adding another substance

Examples

Food adulteration
Authorities are working to prevent the adulteration of medicines in the market.
The adulteration of foods can pose serious health risks to consumers.
The company was fined for the adulteration of its dairy products with water.
After testing, authorities discovered evidence of adulteration in several imported medicines.
Strict regulations are in place to prevent the adulteration of food products.
The adulteration of milk with water was a widespread problem in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted substances
  • antonym: purification - the act of making something pure
