cheat

verb
defeat by trickery or deceit
Pronunciation: / tʃit / (AmE), / tʃiːt / (BrE)
to act dishonestly or unfairly in order to gain an advantage, especially by deceiving others or breaking rules

Examples

To cheat on a test
He managed to cheat his opponent out of the victory with a clever trick.
If you try to cheat, you’re only cheating yourself in the long run.
The team was furious when they realized the other side had cheated to win the match.
He was caught trying to cheat on the final exam by copying answers.
If you cheat during the game, it's unfair to everyone else playing.
They accused the company of cheating customers out of their money with false promises.
He felt angry after realizing the company had cheated him with extra fees.
The contractor tried to cheat them out of their deposit.
Tourists are often warned not to let taxi drivers cheat them.
