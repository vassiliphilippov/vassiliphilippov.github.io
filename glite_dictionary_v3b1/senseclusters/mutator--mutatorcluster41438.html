mutator

noun
mutation-causing agent
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmjuːteɪtə / (BrE)
an agent, such as a gene or chemical, that causes or increases the rate of mutations

Examples

Mutator gene
A mutator gene can increase the mutation rate in an organism.
Scientists are studying how a mutator affects the evolution of bacteria.
Exposure to certain chemicals acting as a mutator can lead to genetic disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutagen - an agent that causes genetic mutation
