opening

noun
entrance or passageway, often on ships
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈəʊpənɪŋ / (BrE)
a hole, gap, or passage that allows access or movement through something

Examples

A small opening
A narrow opening between the cabins allowed crew members to move quickly during emergencies.
He secured the hatch after passing through the opening in the floor.
The sailors climbed through the opening to reach the lower deck.
The builders created an opening in the wall for the new window.
There was a small opening at the bottom of the door for the cat to come in and out.
We put an opening in the fence so the dogs could run freely.
A blocked nasal opening can cause breathing difficulties.
The small opening between the chambers allows blood to flow through the heart.
The surgeon examined the opening leading to the patient's lung during the operation.
