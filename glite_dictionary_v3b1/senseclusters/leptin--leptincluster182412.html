leptin

noun
appetite-regulating hormone
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛptɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛptɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced by fat cells that helps regulate appetite and body weight

Examples

Leptin hormone level
Scientists discovered that leptin plays a key role in controlling hunger and metabolism.
People with low leptin levels may have difficulty losing weight despite dieting.
The doctor explained how leptin signals the brain when the body has enough fat stored.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ghrelin - a hormone that stimulates appetite
  • synonym: insulin - a hormone that regulates blood sugar levels
