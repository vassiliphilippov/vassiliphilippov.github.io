Riksmal

noun
historical standard form of Norwegian
Pronunciation: / ˈriksˌmɑːl / (AmE), / ˈrɪksmɑːl / (BrE)
a form of the Norwegian language, originally called Riksmål, which was officially renamed Bokmål in 1929 but remains used in certain contexts

Examples

Riksmal language form
Debates over the use of Riksmal versus Bokmal persisted throughout the twentieth century.
Older Norwegian literature is often written in Riksmal, before the official change to Bokmal.
She wrote her thesis in Riksmal rather than Nynorsk, reflecting her family's language tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bokmal - the official written standard of Norwegian that replaced Riksmal as its name in 1929
  • antonym: Nynorsk - the other official written standard of Norwegian
