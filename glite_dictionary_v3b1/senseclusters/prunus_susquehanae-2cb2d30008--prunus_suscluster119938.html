Prunus susquehanae

noun
a small North American cherry plant
Pronunciation: / ˈpruːnəs sʌs.kwəˈhɑː.neɪ / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs sʌs.kwɪˈhɑː.niː / (BrE)
A small, straggling North American cherry that grows on sandy soil and produces minute, scarcely edible purplish-black fruit.

Examples

The Prunus susquehanae tree
Botanists study the Prunus susquehanae for its unique adaptation to poor soils.
The Prunus susquehanae can often be found growing wild in sandy woodlands.
The fruit of the Prunus susquehanae is rarely consumed due to its bitter taste.
