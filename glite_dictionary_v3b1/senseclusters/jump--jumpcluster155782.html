jump

verb
be highly noticeable; stand out
4.1 3.17/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’ÊŒmp / (AmE), / dÊ’ÊŒmp / (BrE)
(of something) to be highly noticeable or standout immediately to attention.

Examples

Jump off the page
Her signature really jumps off the page, making it hard to miss.
The bright colors on the cover jump at you from across the room.
The typo in the headline jumped out to everyone who read it.

Synonyms

  • jump out
    3.4 1.07/m
    jump out is a common phrase that feels sudden and very striking, while jump is the simple verb for the same sudden attention.
  • leap out
    3.9 0.30/m
    leap out feels more vivid and slightly formal, drawing dramatic attention, while jump names a sudden, highly noticeable quality.
  • stand out
    3.2 16/m
    stand out is used for being easily noticed among others over time, while jump means an immediate, sudden catching of attention.
  • stick out
    3.0 1.48/m
    stick out often means to be physically obvious or oddly different in a group, while jump refers to sudden, highly noticeable attention.
