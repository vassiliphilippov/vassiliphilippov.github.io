phylogeography

noun
study of genetic distribution
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪloʊdʒiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌfaɪləʊdʒiˈɒɡrəfi /; / ˌfʌɪləʊdʒɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the study of how genetic lineages are distributed geographically within and among species, often revealing how historical events like migration or isolation have shaped these patterns

Examples

Study of phylogeography
Scientists used phylogeography to understand how glaciers affected the spread of animal populations.
Advances in DNA analysis have greatly improved the field of phylogeography in recent years.
The phylogeography of the plant revealed its migration routes across continents.
Researchers used phylogeography to trace the migration routes of ancient human populations across continents.
The phylogeography of freshwater fish reveals how rivers and lakes shaped their genetic diversity.
Advances in DNA analysis have made phylogeography an important tool in conservation biology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biogeography - the study of the distribution of species and ecosystems in geographic space and through geological time
  • synonym: genetics - the study of heredity and the variation of inherited characteristics
