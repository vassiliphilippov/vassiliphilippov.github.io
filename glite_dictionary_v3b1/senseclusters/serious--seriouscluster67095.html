Important; having significant, often grave, consequences or requiring careful consideration.

Examples

Serious consequences
Climate change poses a serious threat to global health and security.
Deciding where to move is a serious decision that will affect the whole family.
Getting a driver's license is a serious responsibility for teenagers.

Synonyms

  • important
    1.0 540/m
    important is widely used to show big value or effect in many situations, while serious points to possible danger or heavy consequences.
  • grave
    4.2 9.82/m
    grave sounds formal and often shows very bad or sad consequences, while serious is more general and used widely.
  • major
    1.0 250/m
    major means large in size or importance, often in official or formal situations, while serious stresses careful thought or risk.
  • critical
    4.0 93/m
    critical describes a dangerous or deciding moment needing urgent action, while serious covers important or weighty matters more broadly.
