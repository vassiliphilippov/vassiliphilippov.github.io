mousy

adjective
quiet and timid in manner
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊzi / (BrE)
(of a person) quiet, timid, and lacking in confidence or effectiveness; shy or unassuming.

Examples

Mousy quiet girl
Although Sarah was always mousy in meetings, her ideas were excellent when she finally spoke up.
He gave a mousy reply and quickly averted his eyes.
The mousy student rarely participated in class but was a keen observer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confident - sure of oneself; possessing self-assurance
  • synonym: timid - shy, lacking in courage or confidence
