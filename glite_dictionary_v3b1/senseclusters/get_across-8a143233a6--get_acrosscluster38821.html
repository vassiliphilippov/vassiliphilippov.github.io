get across

verb
become understood or clear
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt əˈkrɑs /; / ɡɛt əˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈkrɒs / (BrE)
to communicate an idea or message successfully so that it is understood by others

Examples

To get across an idea
It took several explanations for the importance of safety to get across to the new employees.
Sometimes her enthusiasm doesn't get across in written messages as it does in person.
The seriousness of the situation didn't get across until she saw the look on his face.
Despite the language barrier, he managed to get across what he meant.
Good teachers know how to get across complex ideas in simple terms.
She found it difficult to get across her point during the heated discussion.
