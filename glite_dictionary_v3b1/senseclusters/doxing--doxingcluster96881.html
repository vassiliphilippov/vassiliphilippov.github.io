doxing

noun
publishing private info online to harass
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdɒksɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of publishing private or identifying information about an individual on the internet, typically with the intention of causing harm or harassment.

Examples

Doxing private information
After a disagreement on social media, someone resorted to doxing her by posting her address online.
Law enforcement agencies warn against the dangers of doxing and urge victims to report such incidents.
Victims of doxing often experience severe distress when their personal information is exposed to the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dox - to publish private information about someone online, usually with malicious intent
