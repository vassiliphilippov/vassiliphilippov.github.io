showboat

verb
to attract attention
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊˌbəʊt / (BrE)
to behave in a flashy or showy way to attract attention or admiration

Examples

To showboat for fans
He tends to showboat on the basketball court instead of playing as part of the team.
Some performers showboat to impress the audience with their skills.
Rather than showboating, she focused on helping her teammates succeed.
The player began to showboat after making an impressive dunk in the final seconds.
Some people dislike when athletes showboat instead of focusing on teamwork.
She tried not to showboat during the presentation, even though her project was the best.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride about oneself or achievements
  • synonym: show off - to display oneself or one's abilities to attract attention
  • antonym: understate - to present something as being smaller or less important than it really is
