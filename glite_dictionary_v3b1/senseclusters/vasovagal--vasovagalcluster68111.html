vasovagal

noun
fainting episode
9.9
Pronunciation: /veɪ.soʊ.ˈveɪ.gəl/ (AmE), /veɪ.səʊ.ˈveɪ.gəl/ (BrE)
a brief fainting episode caused by sudden stimulation of the vagus nerve, leading to a drop in heart rate and blood pressure

Examples

A vasovagal episode
He experienced a vasovagal after seeing blood during the medical procedure.
The nurse explained that a vasovagal can happen if you stand up too quickly after an injection.
After the stressful news, she had a brief vasovagal in the waiting room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fainting - sudden loss of consciousness, usually brief
  • synonym: syncope - temporary loss of consciousness due to a drop in blood flow to the brain
