isotonic

adjective
having equal tension (muscles)
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of two or more muscles) having equal tension or tone.

Examples

Isotonic muscles
Maintaining isotonic conditions can be important for balanced muscle development during rehabilitation.
Physical therapists may test whether paired muscles are isotonic to assess coordination.
When both biceps and triceps are isotonic, the arm remains steady during a particular motion.
