latinity

noun
Latin-like style
9.5
Pronunciation: / ləˈtɪnəti / (AmE), / ləˈtɪnɪti / (BrE)
the quality of resembling Latin, especially in language, style, or expression

Examples

Latinity of style
The professor admired the latinity of her student's essay on Roman history.
Writers of the Renaissance often aimed for a high degree of latinity in their works.
His speech was filled with such latinity that only scholars could fully appreciate it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barbarism - a nonstandard word or expression, especially one that is not Latin-like
  • synonym: classicism - the principles or style characteristic of ancient Greek and Roman art and literature
