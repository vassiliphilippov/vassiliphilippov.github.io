closed interval

noun
interval including its endpoints
7.5
Pronunciation: / kloʊzd ˈɪntɚvəl / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈɪntəvəl / (BrE)
an interval on the real line that includes its endpoints

Examples

A closed interval on line
Every closed interval is also a bounded set on the real number line.
In calculus, we often examine functions defined on a closed interval.
The set [2, 5] is a closed interval because it contains both endpoints 2 and 5.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interval - a set of numbers between two endpoints
  • antonym: open interval - an interval that does not include its endpoints
