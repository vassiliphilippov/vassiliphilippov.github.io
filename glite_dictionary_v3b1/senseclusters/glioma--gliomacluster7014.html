glioma

noun
tumor from brain glial cells
Pronunciation: / ɡliˈoʊmə / (AmE), / ɡliˈəʊmə / (BrE)
a tumor in the brain or spinal cord that arises from glial cells

Examples

A glioma tumor
Radiation therapy is often used to treat certain types of glioma.
Recent studies aim to improve survival rates for children with brain gliomas.
The patient was diagnosed with a glioma after experiencing persistent headaches and vision problems.
