limpet

noun
marine mollusk with conical shell
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmpət /; / ˈlɪm.pɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpɪt /; / ˈlɪm.pɪt / (BrE)
a small sea animal (marine mollusk) with a low, cone-shaped shell that attaches tightly to rocks

Examples

A limpet on rocks
A limpet uses its strong foot to attach itself firmly to rocks in the intertidal zone.
At low tide, children searched for crabs and limpets among the tidal pools.
The rocky shoreline was covered with limpets clinging to the stones.
During low tide, children searched for limpets attached to the exposed boulders.
Some birds, like oystercatchers, feed on limpet meat by prising them off rocks.
The rocky shore was covered with limpets and barnacles.
