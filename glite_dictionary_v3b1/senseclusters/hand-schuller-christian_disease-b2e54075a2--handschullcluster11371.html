Hand-Schuller-Christian disease

noun
rare childhood inflammatory disease (cholesterol metabolism)
Pronunciation: / hænd ˈʃuːlər ˈkrɪstʃən dɪˈziz / (AmE), / hænd ˈʃuːlə ˈkrɪstʃən dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A rare inflammatory disease of young children, associated with abnormal cholesterol metabolism, cystic defects of the skull, and often diabetes insipidus.

Examples

Hand-Schuller-Christian disease in children
Despite its rarity, Hand-Schuller-Christian disease should be considered in children with unexplained diabetes insipidus and bone lesions.
Diagnosis of Hand-Schuller-Christian disease requires clinical correlation and specific radiological findings.
The pediatrician suspected Hand-Schuller-Christian disease when the child presented with multiple skull lesions and excessive thirst.
