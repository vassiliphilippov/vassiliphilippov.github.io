pay as you earn

noun
U.S. income‑based student loan plan
4.1
Pronunciation: /peɪ əz juː ɜːrn/ /; / /peɪ əz jə ɜːrn/ /; / /peɪ æz juː ɜːrn/ (AmE), /peɪ əz juː ɜːn/ /; / /peɪ əz jə ɜːn/ /; / /peɪ æz juː ɜːn/ (BrE)
A U.S. federal student‑loan repayment plan that limits borrowers' monthly payments to a percentage of their discretionary income and may forgive any remaining balance after a specified period.

Examples

Pay as you earn plan
Many graduates enrolled in the pay as you earn plan to cap loan payments based on income.
After losing her job, she switched to pay as you earn to lower her monthly student payments.
The counselor explained how pay as you earn can lead to forgiveness of remaining balance after years.
