mass spectrograph

noun
instrument recording mass spectra graphically
Pronunciation: / ˌmæs spɛkˈtroʊˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌmæs spɛkˈtrəʊˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument that separates ions by mass and records the results as a graphical display of the mass spectrum

Examples

A mass spectrograph instrument
The laboratory purchased a new mass spectrograph to analyze chemical compounds more efficiently.
The research paper describes improvements made to the existing mass spectrograph design.
Using a mass spectrograph, scientists identified the isotopic composition of the sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mass spectrometer - an instrument that measures the masses of ions in a sample
