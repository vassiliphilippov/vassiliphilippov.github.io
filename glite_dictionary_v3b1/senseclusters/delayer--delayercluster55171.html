delayer

noun
electronic device that delays signals
7.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈleɪər / (AmE), / dɪˈleɪə / (BrE)
(in electronics) a device or circuit that delays an electrical or digital signal by a fixed or adjustable amount of time, used for timing, synchronization, or signal processing.

Examples

Signal delayer device
The signal passes through the delayer to synchronize the incoming data with the clock.
Engineers installed a short delayer on the board to introduce a precise 10-nanosecond delay.
During testing the technician noticed two delayers overheating and removed them from the prototype.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delay line - a circuit or device that delays a signal by transmitting it through a medium or memory
  • synonym: timing circuit - an electronic circuit designed to generate precise time intervals or synchronize events
