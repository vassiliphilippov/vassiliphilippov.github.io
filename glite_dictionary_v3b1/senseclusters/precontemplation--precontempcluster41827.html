precontemplation

noun
stage before change
8.9
Pronunciation: /priː.kən.təm.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.kən.təm.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prə.kən.təm.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.kən.təm.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.kən.təm.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prə.kən.təm.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the stage in behavior change when a person is not yet considering or intending to change a habit or behavior

Examples

The precontemplation stage
In the precontemplation stage, individuals may not see their smoking as a problem.
Health professionals often encounter patients in precontemplation who are not ready to discuss change.
Understanding precontemplation can help tailor interventions for people resistant to altering their habits.
