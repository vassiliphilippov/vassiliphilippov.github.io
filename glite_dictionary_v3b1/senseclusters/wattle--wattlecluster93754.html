wattle

noun
fleshy skin fold on bird's or lizard's neck
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈwɒt.əl / (BrE)
a fleshy, wrinkled, and often brightly colored fold of skin that hangs from the neck or throat of certain birds (such as chickens and turkeys) or some lizards

Examples

Turkey wattle
Some lizards also display a loose, hanging wattle as part of their throat anatomy.
The rooster's wattle grew more vibrant as mating season approached.
Turkeys have a noticeable red wattle hanging beneath their beaks.
