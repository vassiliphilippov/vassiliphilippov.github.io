sphenoid fontanelle

noun
soft spot on infant's skull at bone junction
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsfiːˌnɔɪd fɒn.təˈnɛl / (AmE), / ˈsfiːnɔɪd fɒn.təˈnɛl / (BrE)
The irregularly shaped soft spot on each side of an infant's skull where the frontal, parietal, temporal, and sphenoid bones meet; it later corresponds to the pterion after ossification.

Examples

The sphenoid fontanelle closes
As the child matured, the sphenoid fontanelle gradually closed and became the pterion in the adult skull.
During the first few months after birth, the sphenoid fontanelle can be felt as a soft area on the infant's skull.
The doctor examined the sphenoid fontanelle to check for proper development of the baby's skull bones.
