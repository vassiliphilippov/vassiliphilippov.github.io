kalends

noun
Roman calendar day
Pronunciation: / ˈkælɛndz / (AmE), / ˈkælɛndz / (BrE)
the first day of the month in the ancient Roman calendar

Examples

Roman kalends
In ancient Rome, debts were often due on the kalends.
The priest announced the festival would begin on the next kalends.
Historical records mention important events dated to the kalends of March.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ides - the middle of the month in the Roman calendar
  • antonym: nones - the ninth day before the ides in the Roman calendar
