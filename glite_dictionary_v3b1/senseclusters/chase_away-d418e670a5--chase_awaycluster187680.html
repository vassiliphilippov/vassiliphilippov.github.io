chase away

verb
force to leave; drive away
2.1 2.2
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪs əˈweɪ / (AmE), / tʃeɪs əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave, often suddenly or by pursuing them, used both literally and figuratively

Examples

To chase away intruders
Security guards chased away the vandals before any damage was done.
She tried to chase away her worries by focusing on her work.
The loud noise quickly chased away the birds from the garden.
