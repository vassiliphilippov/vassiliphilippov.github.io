flow

verb
move or progress freely, smoothly
Pronunciation: / floʊ / (AmE), / fləʊ / (BrE)
to move or progress freely and continuously, especially in a smooth or unbroken manner, like a stream (used for people, things, ideas, or information)

Examples

People flow into the hall
After the speech ended, the crowd began to flow towards the exits.
Ideas seemed to flow effortlessly during the brainstorming session.
Traffic will flow more smoothly once the construction is finished.
At the annual fundraiser, donations flowed in from all over the city.
Champagne flowed all night as the guests celebrated the couple's marriage.
Laughter flowed freely around the dinner table during the holiday meal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ebb - to move backwards or recede, especially with reference to tides
  • synonym: pour - to move in a steady, continuous stream
  • synonym: stream - to move continuously in large numbers, like a stream
