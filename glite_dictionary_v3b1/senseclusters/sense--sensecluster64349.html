sense

noun
a faculty for perceiving the external world
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɛns / (AmE), / sɛns / (BrE)
a faculty by which the body perceives an external stimulus, such as sight, hearing, smell, taste, or touch

Examples

The sense of smell
Humans rely on each sense to interpret and respond to their environment.
Losing the sense of smell can affect a person's ability to enjoy food.
Some animals have a heightened sense of hearing compared to humans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sense organ - an organ that receives sensory input (e.g., eye, ear)
