sense

noun
a faculty for perceiving the external world
1.8 31/m
Pronunciation: / sÉ›ns / (AmE), / sÉ›ns / (BrE)
a faculty by which the body perceives an external stimulus, such as sight, hearing, smell, taste, or touch

Examples

The sense of smell
Humans rely on each sense to interpret and respond to their environment.
Losing the sense of smell can affect a person's ability to enjoy food.
Some animals have a heightened sense of hearing compared to humans.

Synonyms

  • sensory faculty
    5.6 0.00/m
    sensory faculty is a physical ability like sight or touch that senses the world, while sense is the common word for that faculty.
  • sense modality
    5.5 0.03/m
    sense modality is a technical term for one specific system of sensation, like sight, while sense is the general faculty people mean.
