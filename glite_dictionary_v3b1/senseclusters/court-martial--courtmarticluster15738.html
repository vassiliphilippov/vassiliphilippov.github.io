court-martial

noun
military court for service personnel
7.4 1.43/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îrt Àåm…ër É…ôl / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀêt Àåm…ëÀê É…ôl / (BrE)
a military court that tries members of the armed forces accused of breaking military law, or the trial itself conducted by such a court

Examples

A court-martial trial
His actions were judged by a court-martial that convened last month.
If found guilty by a court-martial, he could face severe punishment.
The soldier was brought before a court-martial for disobeying direct orders.
If convicted by court-martial, he could be dishonorably discharged.
The captain faced a court-martial for disobeying direct orders.
Three soldiers were found guilty at their court-martial last week.

Synonyms

  • military court
    4.9 0.54/m
    military court is the formal panel of officers that tries service members for military offences, while court-martial often means the trial by that court.

Related Terms

  • antonym: civilian court - a non-military court dealing with civilian matters
  • synonym: military tribunal - a court with authority in military legal matters
