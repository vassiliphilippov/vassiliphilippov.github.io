sweep

noun
winning all or nearly all tricks in bridge
7.7 0.37/m
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiÀêp / (BrE)
In bridge and similar card games, a win in which one takes all or all but one of the tricks in a hand.

Examples

A sweep in bridge
Achieving a sweep can be challenging against experienced players.
She pulled off a rare sweep in yesterday's bridge tournament.
The team celebrated their sweep after winning every trick in the final hand.

Synonyms

  • slam
    7.4 2.02/m
    slam is a bid or completed contract to take every trick in a hand, while sweep is the actual win.
