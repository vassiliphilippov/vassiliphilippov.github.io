lasiocampid moth

noun
moth of the family Lasiocampidae
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪsi.oʊˈkæmpɪd mɑθ / (AmE), / ˌleɪsi.əʊˈkæmpɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
A medium-sized, stout-bodied moth of the family Lasiocampidae, typically neutral-colored and having comb-like antennae.

Examples

A lasiocampid moth specimen
A naturalist photographed a rare lasiocampid moth resting on a tree trunk during the summer.
Many lasiocampid moths are active at night and attracted to lights in suburban gardens.
The lasiocampid moth is easily recognized by its stout, hairy body and feathery antennae.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lappet moth - a moth of the family Lasiocampidae, another common name
  • degree lower: silkworm moth - a moth from a related family (Bombycidae), often confused with lasiocampid moths
