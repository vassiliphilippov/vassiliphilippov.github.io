optical telegraph

noun
system for sending messages by visual signals
6.8 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːp.tɪ.kəl ˈtel.ə.græf/ /; / /ˈɑːp.tɪ.kəl ˈtel.ɪ.græf/ (AmE), /ˈɒp.tɪ.kəl ˈtel.ə.græf/ /; / /ˈɒp.tɪ.kəl ˈtel.ɪ.græf/ (BrE)
a system for transmitting messages at a distance using visual signals, such as towers with moving arms, lights, or flags

Examples

The optical telegraph system
Before electricity, armies often relied on the optical telegraph for battlefield communication.
Messages could be sent rapidly across the country using a network of optical telegraph towers.
The Chappe brothers invented the first practical optical telegraph in France in the late 18th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electrical telegraph - telegraph that transmits messages using electrical signals
  • synonym: semaphore - system for conveying information by visual signals, especially with flags or mechanical arms
