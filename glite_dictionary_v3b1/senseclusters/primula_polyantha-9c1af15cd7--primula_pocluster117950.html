Primula polyantha

noun
a hybrid group of florists' primroses
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪmjələ ˌpɒliˈænθə / (AmE), / ˈprɪmjʊlə ˌpɒliˈænθə / (BrE)
A group of hybrid primroses cultivated for their colorful flowers, known as florists' primroses, derived from crosses between the oxlip, cowslip, and common primrose.

Examples

Primula polyantha flowers
Hybridization has produced Primula polyantha plants with a dazzling range of colors.
Many varieties of Primula polyantha are cultivated for use in floral arrangements.
The Primula polyantha is prized by gardeners for its vibrant clusters of blooms in early spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: primrose - a plant of the genus Primula with pale yellow flowers
