tracer bullet

noun
bullet with visible flight path
9.2 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈtreɪ.sər ˈbʊl.ət/ /; / /ˈtʃreɪ.sər ˈbʊl.ət/ /; / /ˈtreɪ.sər ˈbʊl.ɪt/ (AmE), /ˈtreɪ.sə ˈbʊl.ɪt/ /; / /ˈtʃreɪ.sə ˈbʊl.ɪt/ (BrE)
A bullet that leaves a visible trail, usually of smoke or light, so its flight path can be observed.

Examples

A tracer bullet at night
During the night exercise, tracer bullets streaked across the sky, marking the trajectory of each shot.
The machine gun's tracer bullets illuminated the battlefield, helping to coordinate the attack.
The soldiers used tracer bullets to see where they were firing in the darkness.
