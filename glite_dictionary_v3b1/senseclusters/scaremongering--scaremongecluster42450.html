scaremongering

verb
to spread fear
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈskeə.r.mʌŋ.gər.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈskɛr.mʌŋ.gər.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈskeə.mʌŋ.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
to deliberately spread fear or alarm, often by exaggerating or presenting misleading information about a situation.

Examples

To scaremonger about risks
The media was accused of scaremongering during the outbreak by focusing on worst-case scenarios.
Some politicians have been scaremongering to influence public opinion before the election.
Experts warned against scaremongering and urged people to rely on verified information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarmism - the practice of exaggerating dangers to cause worry or panic
  • antonym: reassure - to make someone feel less worried or afraid
