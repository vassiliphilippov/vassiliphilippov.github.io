absence

noun
state of being absent; lack of presence
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.səns / (AmE), / ˈæb.səns / (BrE)
the state or fact of not being present or something being missing

Examples

Absence of evidence
During her absence, someone broke into the apartment.
His repeated absence from meetings raised concern among his colleagues.
The teacher noticed the absence of enthusiasm in the students' responses.
An absence of evidence makes the case difficult to solve.
Due to her unexpected absence, the project was delayed.
James's absence from the meeting was noted by everyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: presence - the state of being present or at a place
