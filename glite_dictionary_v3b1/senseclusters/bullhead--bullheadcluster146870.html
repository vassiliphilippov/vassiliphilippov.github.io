bullhead

noun
freshwater fish with a bony, spined head
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish with a broad, flattened head, especially a type of catfish found in North America

Examples

A bullhead fish
Anglers often catch bullheads in shallow streams during the summer.
The bullhead is known for its tough, armor-like skin and distinctive appearance.
The river is home to several species of bullhead that hide among the rocks.
A bullhead is easily identified by its wide, flat head and whisker-like barbels.
Fishermen often use worms as bait to catch bullhead during summer evenings.
We caught several bullhead in the river this afternoon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catfish - a fish with whisker-like barbels around the mouth
