slave

verb
work very hard
7.1
Pronunciation: / sleɪv / (AmE), / sleɪv / (BrE)
to work very hard, especially for a long time and with little rest or reward

Examples

To slave away
He had been slaving at his desk all night to finish the report.
Many people slave away in the kitchen during the holidays to prepare elaborate meals.
She had to slave over the project for months to meet the deadline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax
  • synonym: toil - to work extremely hard or incessantly
