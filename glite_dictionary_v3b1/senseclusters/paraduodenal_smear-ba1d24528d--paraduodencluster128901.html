paraduodenal smear

noun
medical smear from the duodenum
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəduːˈdiːnəl smɪr / (AmE), / ˌparəduːˈdiːnəl smɪə / (BrE)
a medical test involving the microscopic examination of material obtained from the duodenum to check for infection or disease

Examples

A paraduodenal smear test
A paraduodenal smear is often used when symptoms point to duodenal diseases of uncertain origin.
Results from the paraduodenal smear indicated the presence of intestinal parasites.
The doctor ordered a paraduodenal smear to help diagnose the persistent gastrointestinal infection.
