fundamental right

noun
basic legal right guaranteed to individuals
3.6 2.8
Pronunciation: /fʌn.də.ˈmen.təl raɪt/ (AmE), /fʌn.də.ˈmen.təl raɪt/ (BrE)
a basic legal right that is recognized and protected as essential for individuals, often guaranteed by a constitution or legal framework

Examples

A fundamental right
Education is often recognized as a fundamental right in international law.
Freedom of speech is considered a fundamental right in many democracies.
The lawyer argued that her client's fundamental rights had been violated during the investigation.
