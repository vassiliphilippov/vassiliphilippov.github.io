Fahrenheit scale

noun
temperature scale (freezing: 32°F, boiling: 212°F)
4.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfær.ən.haɪt skeɪl/ (AmE), /ˈfær.ən.haɪt skeɪl/ (BrE)
A temperature scale in which the freezing point of water is 32 degrees and the boiling point is 212 degrees.

Examples

The Fahrenheit scale temperature
Many recipes in America list oven temperatures using the Fahrenheit scale.
On the Fahrenheit scale, water boils at 212 degrees and freezes at 32 degrees.
The United States commonly uses the Fahrenheit scale to report weather temperatures.
