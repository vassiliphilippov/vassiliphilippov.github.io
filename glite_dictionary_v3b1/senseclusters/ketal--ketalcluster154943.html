ketal

noun
ketone-alcohol compound
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːtæl / (AmE), / ˈkiːtæl / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed by the reaction of a ketone with an alcohol, typically used as a protecting group in organic synthesis

Examples

A ketal compound
The chemist isolated a ketal during the multi-step synthesis process.
Protecting the carbonyl group as a ketal can prevent unwanted reactions.
After hydrolysis, the ketal was converted back to the original ketone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetal - a compound formed from an aldehyde and an alcohol
