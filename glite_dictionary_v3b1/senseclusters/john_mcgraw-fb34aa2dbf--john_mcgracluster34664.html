John McGraw

proper noun
American baseball player and manager (1873–1934)
6.3
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn məˈɡrɔ / (AmE), / dʒɒn məˈɡrɔː / (BrE)
(1873–1934) A United States baseball player and manager, famous for his long tenure with the New York Giants and his influential role in the sport's history.

Examples

John McGraw baseball manager
John McGraw led the New York Giants to ten National League pennants during his managerial career.
As a player and manager, John McGraw became one of the most respected figures in baseball history.
Baseball historians often credit John McGraw with revolutionizing the game's tactics in the early 20th century.
