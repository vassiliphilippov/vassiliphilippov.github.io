free enterprise

noun
economic system based on market freedom
Pronunciation: / ˌfriː ˈɛn.tɚˌpraɪz / (AmE), / ˌfriː ˈen.təˌpraɪz / (BrE)
an economic system in which private businesses operate in competition and largely free of state control; market forces determine the allocation of resources and prices

Examples

Free enterprise system
Government regulations are sometimes seen as a threat to free enterprise.
Many people believe that free enterprise encourages innovation and economic growth.
The success of small businesses is often used as evidence for the advantages of free enterprise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets
  • antonym: planned economy - an economy where allocation of resources is controlled by the government
