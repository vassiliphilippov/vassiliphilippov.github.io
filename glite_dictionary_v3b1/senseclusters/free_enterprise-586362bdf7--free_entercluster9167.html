free enterprise

noun
economic system based on market freedom
3.8 2.80/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåfriÀê Àà…õn.t…öÀåpra…™z / (AmE), / ÀåfriÀê Ààen.t…ôÀåpra…™z / (BrE)
an economic system in which private businesses operate in competition and largely free of state control; market forces determine the allocation of resources and prices

Examples

Free enterprise system
Government regulations are sometimes seen as a threat to free enterprise.
Many people believe that free enterprise encourages innovation and economic growth.
The success of small businesses is often used as evidence for the advantages of free enterprise.

Synonyms

  • market economy
    3.0 6.79/m
    a market economy uses supply and demand to set prices and allocate goods, while free enterprise highlights private competition and limited state control.
  • laissez-faire economy
    7.2 0.03/m
    a laissez-faire economy relies on minimal government and hands-off policy, while free enterprise describes private businesses competing with limited state control.
  • private enterprise
    3.9 1.71/m
    private enterprise means businesses owned by individuals or firms, while free enterprise refers to a broader system where markets and competition rule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets
  • antonym: planned economy - an economy where allocation of resources is controlled by the government
