deed

noun
legal property document
5.8 6.05/m
Pronunciation: / did / (AmE), / diÀêd / (BrE)
a legal document that is signed and delivered, especially relating to the ownership or transfer of property

Examples

Property deed
Before you can sell the land, you must locate the deed.
She received the deed to the house after the sale was completed.
The lawyer examined the deed to verify the rightful owner.

Synonyms

  • deed of conveyance
    8.6 0.00/m
    a deed of conveyance is the signed paper used to transfer property at a sale, while a deed can be any legal property document.
  • title deed
    6.1 0.02/m
    a title deed is the official paper that shows who owns land or a house, while a deed is the general legal document.
  • conveyance
    8.2 0.15/m
    conveyance is the legal process or document that moves property between people, while a deed is the signed paper used in transfer.
  • title
    4.8 17/m
    title is the legal right to own property or the document that proves it, while a deed is the signed transfer or ownership paper.

Related Terms

  • antonym: act - something that is done or performed (general sense)
  • synonym: title deed - a legal document proving ownership of property
