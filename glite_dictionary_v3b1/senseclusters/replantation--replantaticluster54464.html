replantation

noun
reattachment surgery
Pronunciation: / ˌriːplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːplænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to reattach a body part that has been completely severed from the body

Examples

Thumb replantation surgery
After the accident, the doctors attempted replantation of the patient's severed thumb.
Successful replantation requires careful preservation of the amputated part and skilled microsurgery.
The hospital is known for its expertise in replantation of fingers and hands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amputation - the removal of a limb or body part
  • synonym: reattachment - the act of attaching something again
