radioactive decay

noun
spontaneous breakdown of unstable atomic nuclei
6.6 4.9
Pronunciation: /reɪ.dɪ.oʊ.ˈæk.tɪv dɪ.ˈkeɪ/ (AmE), /reɪ.dɪ.əʊ.ˈæk.tɪv dɪ.ˈkeɪ/ (BrE)
the spontaneous process by which an unstable atomic nucleus loses energy by emitting radiation

Examples

Radioactive decay is the fundamental process that makes nuclear power possible.
