canid

noun
member of the dog family (Canidae)
Pronunciation: / ˈkænɪd / (AmE), / ˈkeɪnɪd / (BrE)
Any member of the family Canidae, which includes dogs, wolves, foxes, jackals, and related mammals.

Examples

A canid animal
Foxes, along with jackals and domestic dogs, are all members of the canid family.
Researchers studied how canids adapt to different habitats across continents.
The gray wolf is one of the largest wild canids in the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dog - domesticated carnivorous mammal (Canis familiaris), a member of the Canidae family
  • antonym: felid - any member of the cat family (Felidae), contrasted with canids
