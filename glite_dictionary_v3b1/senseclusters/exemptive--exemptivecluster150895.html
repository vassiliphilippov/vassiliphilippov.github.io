exemptive

adjective
granting exemption
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛmptɪv / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmptɪv / (BrE)
giving or relating to the act of granting an exemption from a rule or requirement

Examples

Exemptive rule
The government introduced an exemptive clause to help non-profit organizations.
Certain exemptive measures were taken to support small businesses during the crisis.
The policy contains exemptive rules for students with special needs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exempt - free from a rule or requirement
  • antonym: mandatory - required by law or rules
