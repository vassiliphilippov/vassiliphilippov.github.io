anhedonia

noun
inability to experience pleasure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænˌhiˈdoʊniə / (AmE), / ˌan.hɪˈdəʊ.ni.ə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by an inability to experience pleasure from activities usually found enjoyable.

Examples

Anhedonia in depression
People suffering from depression may experience anhedonia, making it difficult to enjoy activities.
Researchers are studying treatments to help alleviate anhedonia in those with mood disorders.
The patient's anhedonia was evident, as he no longer took interest in his hobbies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pleasure - a feeling of happy satisfaction and enjoyment
