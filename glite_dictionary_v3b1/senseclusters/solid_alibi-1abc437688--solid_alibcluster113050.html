solid alibi

noun
convincing excuse for innocence
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑː.lɪd ˈæ.lɪˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈsɒl.ɪd ˈæl.ɪˌbaɪ / (BrE)
A strong or convincing excuse proving that a person was elsewhere when an act, typically a crime, was committed.

Examples

A solid alibi
Her friends confirmed her whereabouts, giving her a solid alibi for the night in question.
The suspect provided a solid alibi that convinced the police of his innocence.
Without a solid alibi, he quickly became the prime suspect in the investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alibi - an excuse or piece of evidence showing someone was elsewhere when a crime took place
