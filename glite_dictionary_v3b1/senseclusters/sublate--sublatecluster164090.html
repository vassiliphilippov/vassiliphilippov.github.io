sublate

verb
to abolish formally
Pronunciation: / səˈbleɪt / (AmE), / sʌˈbleɪt / (BrE)
to take away, remove, or abolish something, especially in a formal or legal context (archaic)

Examples

To sublate a law
The reformers hoped to sublate outdated customs that no longer served the community.
In the old charter, the king could sublate any law he deemed unnecessary.
Historians noted the council's decision to sublate the ancient regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abolish - to formally put an end to something
  • antonym: establish - to set up or create something
  • synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or position
