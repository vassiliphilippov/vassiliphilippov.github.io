subsidence

noun
a lessening in intensity or severity
Pronunciation: / səbˈsaɪdəns / (AmE), / səbˈsɪdəns / (BrE)
a reduction or lessening in intensity, degree, or severity; especially, the diminishing of symptoms in a medical condition

Examples

Subsidence of symptoms
After the storm, there was a gradual subsidence of floodwaters in the town.
Her symptoms showed a clear subsidence after starting the new medication.
The doctor noted a subsidence in the swelling after the treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remission - a period during which the symptoms of a disease are reduced or disappear
