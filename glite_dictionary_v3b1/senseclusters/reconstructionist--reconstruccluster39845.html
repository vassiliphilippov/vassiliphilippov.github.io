reconstructionist

noun
advocate of reform
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices the rebuilding or reforming of beliefs, institutions, or societies.

Examples

Social reconstructionist
The reconstructionist proposed new ideas for updating the education system.
As a reconstructionist, she believed old traditions should be adapted to modern life.
Many reconstructionists work to reform outdated social institutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reformer - a person who works to change and improve something
  • antonym: traditionalist - a person who supports keeping established customs or beliefs
