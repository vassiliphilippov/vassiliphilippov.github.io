crutch

verb
to walk with crutches
5.1
Pronunciation: / krʌtʃ / (AmE), / krʌtʃ / (BrE)
to walk using crutches for support, especially due to injury or disability

Examples

To crutch around
After breaking his leg, he had to crutch around the house for several weeks.
She crutched slowly down the hallway, careful not to slip on the tiles.
He learned how to crutch up and down stairs safely during his recovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limp - to walk with difficulty, typically because of a damaged or stiff leg or foot
  • degree lower: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
