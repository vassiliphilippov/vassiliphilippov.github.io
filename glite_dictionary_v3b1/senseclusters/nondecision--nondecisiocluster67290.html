nondecision

noun
lack of decision
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
a situation where no clear decision is made, often because of avoidance, indecision, or because the issue is not discussed

Examples

Management nondecision
The board's nondecision frustrated employees who were waiting for clear guidance.
After hours of discussion, their nondecision meant the project could not move forward.
A pattern of nondecision can lead to missed opportunities and confusion.
The committee's nondecision left the proposed changes in limbo for another year.
By refusing to address the topic, the board effectively made a nondecision on the matter.
Political leaders sometimes use a nondecision to avoid controversy or public backlash.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decision - a conclusion or resolution reached after consideration
  • synonym: indecision - inability to make a decision
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation in which no progress can be made or no decision can be reached
