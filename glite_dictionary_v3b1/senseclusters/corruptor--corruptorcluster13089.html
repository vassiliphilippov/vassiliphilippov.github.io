corruptor

noun
data-damaging program
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌptər / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a program, device, or process that damages or alters digital data, files, or memory, making them unreadable or incorrect

Examples

A file corruptor
The malware acted as a corruptor, scrambling millions of files on the company's backup server overnight.
A single corruptor can render important documents and photos completely unreadable.
Security experts warn that a new corruptor is spreading quickly through email attachments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protector - something that keeps data safe from harm or damage
  • synonym: virus - a malicious program that can damage or alter computer data
