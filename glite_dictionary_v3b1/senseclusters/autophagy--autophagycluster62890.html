autophagy

noun
cell recycling process
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.tə.fə.dʒiː/ /; / /ˈɔː.tə.fə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.tə.fə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
a process in which cells break down and recycle their own components to maintain cellular health

Examples

Autophagy process
Researchers discovered that autophagy helps protect cells from damage during periods of stress.
A lack of proper autophagy can lead to the buildup of harmful substances in cells.
Certain diets and fasting are thought to stimulate autophagy in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phagocytosis - the process by which a cell engulfs and digests particles
