pass-back

noun
backward pass in sports
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsˌbæk / (BrE)
a pass made backward to a teammate, especially in football or rugby, often to retain possession or reset play

Examples

A pass-back to goalkeeper
The defender made a quick pass-back to the goalkeeper to avoid pressure from the opposing team.
In rugby, a pass-back is often used to keep the ball away from the opposition.
The coach encouraged players to use the pass-back when they could not move forward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backpass - a pass made backward to a teammate, especially to the goalkeeper in football
  • antonym: forward pass - a pass made forward to a teammate
