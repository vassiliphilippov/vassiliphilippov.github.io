acclimation

noun
adaptation to a new climate or environment
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌækləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæklɪˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming accustomed to a new climate, environment, or situation, especially through physiological or behavioral adaptation

Examples

High acclimation
After weeks of acclimation to the high altitude, the team began climbing the mountain.
Farmers monitor the acclimation of crops to changes in temperature each season.
The laboratory subjects underwent acclimation before starting the main experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to new conditions
