tenor

adjective
of or relating to a tenor voice
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnɚ /; / ˈtɛnər / (AmE), / ˈtɛnə / (BrE)
of or relating to the highest natural adult male singing voice, or to a musical instrument or voice of this range

Examples

Tenor saxophone
He auditioned for the choir with his strong tenor voice.
His tenor singing brought a bright energy to the performance.
The new production required several tenor roles to be filled.
He plays the tenor saxophone in the jazz band.
She bought a new tenor recorder for her music class.
The choir features strong tenor voices in the middle range.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: alto - of a musical range just above tenor
  • degree lower: baritone - of or relating to the male singing voice between bass and tenor
  • antonym: bass - of or relating to the lowest adult male singing voice
  • degree higher: countertenor - of or relating to the adult male singing voice higher than tenor
