glassine

noun
semi-transparent paper
Pronunciation: / ɡlæˈsiːn / (AmE), / ˈɡlæs.iːn / (BrE)
a smooth, semi-transparent paper used for packaging, protecting, or storing items such as stamps or photographs

Examples

Glassine envelope
The collector stored her rare stamps in a glassine envelope to protect them from moisture.
Photographers often use glassine sleeves to keep negatives safe and dust-free.
The bakery wrapped each cookie in glassine paper to keep them fresh and presentable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parchment - a stiff, translucent paper used for baking or wrapping
  • synonym: wax paper - paper coated with wax, used for wrapping food
