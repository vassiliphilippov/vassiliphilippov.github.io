post-horse

noun
horse for hire at posting inns

Pronunciation: / Ààpo ästÀåh…îrs / (AmE), / Ààp…ô ästÀåh…îÀês / (BrE)
A horse kept at an inn or post house for hire to travelers or use by mail carriers.

Examples

A post-horse for hire
He relied on a sturdy post-horse to carry his message swiftly across the countryside.
In eighteenth-century England, travelers often changed post-horses at every stage along the mail route.
The tired post-horse was quickly exchanged for a fresh one at the inn.
