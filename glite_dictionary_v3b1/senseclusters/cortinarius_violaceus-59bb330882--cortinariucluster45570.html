Cortinarius violaceus

noun
violet-capped species of fungus
Pronunciation: / kɔːrˈtɪnəriəs vaɪ.oʊˈleɪsiəs / (AmE), / kɔːˈtɪnərɪəs vʌɪ.əʊˈlɑːsɪəs / (BrE)
A species of fungus with a striking violet coloration and a cap covered in small scales, found in forests of temperate regions.

Examples

Cortinarius violaceus fungus
A mycologist documented a rare cluster of Cortinarius violaceus during her fieldwork in Scandinavia.
The deep purple cap of the Cortinarius violaceus makes it easy to spot in the forest.
Unlike many edible mushrooms, Cortinarius violaceus is admired mostly for its vivid color rather than culinary value.
