Sierra lodgepole pine

noun
tall subspecies of lodgepole pine
Pronunciation: / siˈɛrə ˈlɑʤˌpoʊl paɪn / (AmE), / sɪˈeərə ˈlɒʤˌpəʊl paɪn / (BrE)
a tall subspecies of lodgepole pine (Pinus contorta subsp. murrayana) native to the Sierra Nevada and nearby mountains of western North America

Examples

Dense Sierra lodgepole pine forest
Dense forests of Sierra lodgepole pine provide habitat for a variety of wildlife in California.
Researchers are studying the growth patterns of Sierra lodgepole pine in response to climate change.
The Sierra lodgepole pine is commonly found at high elevations in the Sierra Nevada mountains.
