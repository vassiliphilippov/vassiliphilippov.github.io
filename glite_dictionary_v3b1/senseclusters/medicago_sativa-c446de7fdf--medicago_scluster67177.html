Medicago sativa

A European leguminous plant, also known as alfalfa, grown widely as forage for pasture and hay due to its high nutritional value.

Examples

Medicago sativa plant
Medicago sativa is widely cultivated to improve soil fertility and provide nutritious fodder for livestock.
Many farmers plant Medicago sativa to harvest high-quality hay for their animals.
The deep roots of Medicago sativa help it thrive in drought conditions, making it a reliable pasture crop.
