nonreplication

noun
failure to copy data
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌrɛplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the failure or omission to copy data or processes across systems or backup locations, especially in computing

Examples

Nonreplication of files
The audit revealed nonreplication of several important files, putting the project at risk.
Due to nonreplication, the company lost valuable data after the system failure.
IT teams must monitor for nonreplication to ensure all backups are complete and reliable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: replication - the process of copying data or processes across systems
