hemicellulose

noun
plant fiber polysaccharide
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmiˈsɛljəˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmiˈsɛljʊləʊz / (BrE)
a group of plant cell wall polysaccharides that are less complex than cellulose and are a component of dietary fiber, partly fermentable by gut bacteria

Examples

Dietary hemicellulose fiber
Whole grains are a good source of hemicellulose, which helps support digestive health.
Scientists study hemicellulose to understand its role in plant structure and human nutrition.
Some gut bacteria can break down hemicellulose into beneficial short-chain fatty acids.
Researchers analyze hemicellulose to better understand how plants build their cell walls.
The breakdown of hemicellulose is important in the production of biofuels from plant material.
Unlike cellulose, hemicellulose is more easily digested by some microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cellulose - a main structural polysaccharide in plant cell walls
  • synonym: lignin - a complex organic polymer in plant cell walls that adds rigidity
  • synonym: pectin - a soluble fiber found in plant cell walls
