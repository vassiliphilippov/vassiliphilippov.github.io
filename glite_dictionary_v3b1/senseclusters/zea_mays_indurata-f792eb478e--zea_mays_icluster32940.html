A variety of corn with kernels that have a hard outer layer surrounding a soft endosperm, commonly known as flint corn.

Examples

Zea mays indurata corn
Farmers in the region often plant Zea mays indurata because it resists spoilage better than other corn varieties.
Thanks to its hard kernels, Zea mays indurata is often ground to make hominy or cornmeal.
The decorative multicolored ears sold in autumn are usually Zea mays indurata rather than sweet corn.
