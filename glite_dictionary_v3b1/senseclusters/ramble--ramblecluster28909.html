ramble

verb
walk or travel aimlessly
Pronunciation: / ˈræm.bəl /; / ˈræmbəl / (AmE), / ˈræm.bəl /; / ˈræmbəl / (BrE)
to walk or travel aimlessly or without a fixed route, especially for pleasure

Examples

To ramble through fields
Deer sometimes ramble into the outskirts of the city in search of food.
During their vacation, they would often ramble through the countryside for hours.
He likes to ramble around markets, discovering small shops and new foods.
He tends to ramble when telling stories, often including unnecessary details.
On Sunday afternoons, they like to ramble through the countryside without a set route.
During the meeting, she began to ramble and lost track of her main point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort on something specific
  • synonym: meander - to move or talk in a winding or indirect way
  • synonym: roam - move aimlessly or freely over an area
  • antonym: stride - walk with long, decisive steps in a specified direction
  • synonym: wander - move about without a fixed course or aim
