shingle

noun
thin roofing or siding tile
9.4 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈʃɪŋ.ɡəl/ (AmE), /ˈʃɪŋ.ɡəl/ (BrE)
A thin, flat piece of wood, slate, or other material, used in overlapping rows to cover the roofs or sides of buildings.

Examples

A wooden shingle
Cedar shingles are popular for both roofing and siding.
The old cottage had a roof made of wooden shingles.
They replaced the damaged shingles after the storm.
