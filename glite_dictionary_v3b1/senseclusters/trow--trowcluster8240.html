trow

noun
Orkney folklore creature
Pronunciation: / traʊ / (AmE), / traʊ / (BrE)
a supernatural creature, such as a fairy, goblin, or troll, in the folklore of Orkney and Shetland

Examples

A fairy trow
In old stories, a trow would sneak into houses at night to play tricks on people.
Children in Shetland were warned not to wander at dusk for fear of meeting a trow.
The legend says a group of trows danced on the hills under the full moon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fairy - a small, magical being in folklore
  • synonym: goblin - a mischievous, often ugly, supernatural creature in folklore
  • synonym: troll - a mythical creature from Scandinavian folklore, often large and unfriendly
