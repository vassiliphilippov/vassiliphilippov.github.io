guama

noun
tropical tree used for coffee plantation shade
Pronunciation: / ɡwɑːˈmɑː / (AmE), / ɡwɑːˈmɑː / (BrE)
A tropical tree native to Central America, the West Indies, and Puerto Rico, valued for its clusters of white flowers and commonly used as shade for coffee plantations.

Examples

Old guama trees
In Puerto Rico, old guama trees can be found lining the edges of rural plantations.
Many farmers plant guama to provide natural shade for their coffee crops.
The white flowers of the guama attract bees and other pollinators during the rainy season.
