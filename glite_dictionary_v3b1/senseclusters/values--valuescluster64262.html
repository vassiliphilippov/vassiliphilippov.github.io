values

noun
principles or standards of behavior
1.7
Pronunciation: / ˈvæl.juz / (AmE), / ˈvæl.juːz / (BrE)
A person’s or group’s principles or standards of behavior; one’s judgment of what is important in life.

Examples

values and beliefs
Many people develop their values during childhood through family and education.
Society’s values often change over time as new ideas emerge.
The company prides itself on its ethical values and commitment to the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beliefs - ideas accepted as true or real
  • synonym: morals - standards of right or wrong that guide behavior
  • synonym: principles - basic beliefs or rules that influence behavior
