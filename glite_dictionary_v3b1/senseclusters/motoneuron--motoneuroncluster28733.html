motoneuron

noun
neuron transmitting motor signals from CNS
8.4 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààmo ät…ôÀån äÀêr…ën / (AmE), / Ààm…ô ät…ôÀånj ä…ôr…ín / (BrE)
A neuron that carries impulses away from the brain or spinal cord to muscles or glands, resulting in movement.

Examples

Motoneuron controls muscles
A disease that damages motoneurons can result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Researchers are studying how motoneurons develop and function in the spinal cord.
The doctor explained how signals from the brain travel through motoneurons to control movement.

Synonyms

  • motor neuron
    3.9 0.27/m
    motor neuron is the common two-word term seen in textbooks and speech, while motoneuron is the single-word scientific form.
  • efferent neuron
    7.4 0.00/m
    efferent neuron is a technical term for nerve cells that send signals out to muscles or organs, while motoneuron names cells that cause muscle movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: motor neuron - a neuron that conveys impulses from the CNS to a muscle or gland
  • antonym: sensory neuron - a neuron conducting impulses inward to the central nervous system
