barbarism

noun
nonstandard word or usage
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrbəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈbɑːbərɪzəm / (BrE)
a word, expression, or usage that is not considered standard or acceptable in a language

Examples

A language barbarism
Some teachers consider the use of double negatives a barbarism in English.
The editor corrected several barbarisms in the manuscript before publication.
Using outdated slang can sometimes be labeled a barbarism by language purists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solecism - a grammatical mistake in speech or writing
  • antonym: standard - conforming to the accepted form of a language
