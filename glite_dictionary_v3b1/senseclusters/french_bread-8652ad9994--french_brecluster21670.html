French bread

noun
a long, crusty French-style loaf
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ brɛd / (AmE), / frɛntʃ brɛd / (BrE)
a crusty white bread, often sourdough, typically baked in long, slender loaves such as baguettes, originating from France

Examples

A loaf of French bread
He bought a loaf of French bread to go with the cheese and wine.
She prefers French bread for making sandwiches because of its crispy crust.
Warm, crusty French bread was served with the soup at dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baguette - a long, thin loaf of French bread
