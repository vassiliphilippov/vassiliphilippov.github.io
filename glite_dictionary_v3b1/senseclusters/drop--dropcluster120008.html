drop

noun
a small spherical amount of liquid
1.0 18/m
Pronunciation: / drÉ‘p / (AmE), / drÉ’p / (BrE)
a small amount of liquid, or a very small amount of something

Examples

A drop of water
A drop of water fell from the faucet and landed in the sink.
Only a single drop of blood stained his shirt after the accident.
She watched a drop of rain trickle down the windowpane.
I haven't had a drop to drink all day.
The recipe calls for just a drop of vanilla extract.
There wasn't a drop of kindness in his reply.

Synonyms

  • droplet
    3.4 3.17/m
    droplet is a very small, separate sphere of liquid that forms on its own, while drop means any small amount of liquid.
  • globule
    7.2 0.39/m
    globule is a small round mass of liquid that looks like a tiny ball, while drop means a simple small amount of liquid.
  • driblet
    9.3 0.02/m
    driblet is a very small portion or trickle of liquid often in bits, while drop means a single small amount of liquid.
  • bead
    1.0 11/m
    bead is a small round object and also a tiny round spot of liquid on surfaces, while drop means any small amount of liquid.
