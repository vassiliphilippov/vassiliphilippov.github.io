hit bottom

verb
reach the lowest or worst point

to reach the lowest point or worst state, especially after a period of decline or difficulty

Examples

To hit bottom emotionally
After his business failed, Tom felt that he had hit bottom emotionally and financially.
Many economic analysts believe the housing market hit bottom last year and is now improving.
Some addicts only seek help after they hit bottom and see no way out.

Synonyms

  • hit rock bottom
    4.8 0.83/m
    hit rock bottom describes a very deep crisis and turning point, while hit bottom means reaching the lowest state after decline.
  • bottom out
    5.8 0.61/m
    bottom out means reaching the lowest point before starting to improve, while hit bottom only states reaching the worst state.
  • reach a nadir
    8.2 0.01/m
    reach a nadir is a more formal phrase used in writing to mark the lowest point, while hit bottom is everyday and idiomatic.
