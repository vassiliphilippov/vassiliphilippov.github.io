overrun

verb
take control by force; defeat
7.0 0.90/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒoÊŠvÉ™rËˆrÊŒn / (AmE), / ËŒÉ™ÊŠvÉ™ËˆrÊŒn / (BrE)
(especially of armed forces) to seize control of and defeat a place or position by force, typically quickly and completely.

Examples

Troops overrun a city
During the war, enemy troops overran several border towns before being stopped.
Rebels overran the government stronghold after a fierce battle.
The invading army overran the capital in just two days.

Synonyms

  • conquer
    3.7 16/m
    conquer means to take full control of a place, often after long effort, while overrun means to capture it quickly and completely.
  • occupy
    5.9 18/m
    occupy means to enter and hold a place for some time, while overrun means to seize and defeat it quickly and completely.
  • invade
    4.4 14/m
    invade means to enter a country by force to try to take control, while overrun means to seize and defeat it quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capture - to take control of a place or person by force
  • antonym: retreat - to withdraw from enemy forces as a result of defeat
