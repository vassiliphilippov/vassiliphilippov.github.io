Synonyms

  • doubtfulness
    5.5 0.01/m
    doubtfulness means a feeling of uncertainty about a claim, while skepticism is a steady attitude of questioning accepted ideas.
  • dubiousness
    7.8 0.04/m
    dubiousness suggests suspicion or that something seems unreliable, while skepticism is a reasoned habit of doubting claims.
  • incredulity
    8.1 0.9/m
    incredulity is a strong, often surprised refusal to believe, while skepticism is a measured tendency to question ideas.
  • disbelief
    3.0 9/m
    disbelief is an outright lack of belief after considering facts, while skepticism is a cautious habit of doubting claims.
