orchotomy

noun
surgical incision into a testis
Pronunciation: / ɔːrˈkɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ɔːˈkɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into a testis to obtain tissue for analysis, typically used in cases of low sperm count

Examples

An orchotomy procedure
An orchotomy may be performed to collect tissue samples for fertility testing.
Doctors recommended an orchotomy to investigate the cause of his infertility.
The patient underwent an orchotomy after other diagnostic methods were inconclusive.
