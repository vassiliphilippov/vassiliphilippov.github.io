outcrop

verb
become exposed at the earth's surface
7.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆaÊŠtËŒkrÉ‘p / (AmE), / ËˆaÊŠt.krÉ’p / (BrE)
(of rock or other material) to become visible or exposed at the earth's surface.

Examples

Coal beds outcrop
In this region, coal beds outcrop near the foothills and are easily accessible.
Large slabs of granite outcrop along the riverbank, forming natural terraces.
The geologist explained how sedimentary layers outcrop at the surface due to erosion.

Synonyms

  • surface
    3.9 10/m
    surface means to rise up and become visible from below, while outcrop means rock appearing at the earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emerge - to come out or appear from somewhere
  • antonym: subside - to go below the surface or become less visible
