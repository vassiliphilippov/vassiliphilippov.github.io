owner-occupier

noun
a homeowner who lives in their own property
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊnɚ əˈkjuːpaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊnə əˈkjʊpʌɪə / (BrE)
a person who owns and lives in the home they occupy, rather than renting it out to others

Examples

An owner-occupier house
As an owner-occupier, she takes great pride in maintaining her house.
The number of owner-occupiers has increased steadily over the past decade.
There are tax advantages for an owner-occupier as opposed to a landlord.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tenant - a person who rents a property from a landlord
