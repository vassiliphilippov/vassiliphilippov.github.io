kamleika

noun
Aleut waterproof garment
9.9
Pronunciation: /kæm.ˈleɪ.kə/ (AmE), /kæm.ˈleɪ.kə/ (BrE)
a traditional waterproof outer garment made from animal intestines, worn by the Aleut people

Examples

A kamleika garment
The fisherman wore a kamleika to protect himself from the harsh sea spray.
Traditional kamleika were carefully sewn from seal intestines to keep out water.
In the museum, you can see an authentic kamleika used by Aleut hunters.
