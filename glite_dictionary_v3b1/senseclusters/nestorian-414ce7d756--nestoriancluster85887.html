Nestorian

noun
follower of Nestorius' Christian teachings
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɛˈstɔriən / (AmE), / nɛˈstɔːriən / (BrE)
A member of a Christian sect that follows the teachings of Nestorius, especially relating to the distinctness of Christ’s human and divine natures.

Examples

A Nestorian Christian
He studied the disputes between Nestorian Christians and other religious groups in the fifth century.
Many historians debate the influence of Nestorian doctrine on Eastern Christian churches.
The early Nestorian missionaries traveled as far as China spreading their interpretation of Christianity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Monophysite - A member of a Christian sect emphasizing Christ's single divine nature; contrasts with 'Nestorian'.
