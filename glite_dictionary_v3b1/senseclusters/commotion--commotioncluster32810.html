commotion

noun
noisy disturbance or confusion
4.5 3.4
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈməʊ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a state of noisy confusion, disorder, or excitement, often involving many people or activity

Examples

A commotion in hallway
She rushed to see what the commotion in the hallway was about.
The classroom erupted in commotion when the teacher announced a surprise test.
There was quite a commotion outside the building when the fire alarm went off.
She looked out the window to see what had caused the commotion in the street.
The commotion in the lobby drew the attention of the security guards.
There was a sudden commotion as the students rushed toward the exit.
A loud commotion erupted in the hallway just as the principal walked by.
The sudden commotion made everyone in the restaurant turn their heads.
There was quite a commotion outside the store after the power went out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of tranquility or quietness
  • antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or quiet
  • antonym: tranquility - a state of calm and peace
  • synonym: tumult - a loud, confused noise, especially one caused by a large mass of people
  • synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
