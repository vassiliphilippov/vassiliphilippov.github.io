disturbance

noun
unwanted electrical or sound interference
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɝːbəns /; / dɪˈstɝbəns / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbəns / (BrE)
an interruption or interference with the normal state, function, or process of something (including technical, physical, or general situations)

Examples

Technical disturbance
Acoustic disturbance from nearby construction made it difficult to hear the conversation.
Radio disturbance caused the broadcast to be unclear.
The engineers detected a disturbance in the satellite's communication signal.
Any disturbance of the water can affect the fish living in the pond.
Please avoid making a disturbance during the exam.
The loud construction caused a disturbance in the neighborhood early this morning.
Researchers are studying the effects of sleep disturbance on cognitive performance.
The sudden disturbance in the network caused several computers to shut down unexpectedly.
There was a disturbance in the signal that interrupted the broadcast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disruption - an interruption to the usual way something works or is organized
  • synonym: interference - unwanted signal that impedes communication
  • synonym: interruption - the action of stopping something for a period of time
  • synonym: malfunction - failure to work normally or properly
  • synonym: noise - random or unwanted signals affecting transmission
  • antonym: order - a state of peaceful functioning or systematic arrangement
  • antonym: peace - a state of tranquility or quiet
