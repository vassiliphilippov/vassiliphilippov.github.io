generalize

verb
make popular or common; popularize
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnərəˌlaɪz /; / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something popular by adapting it to the tastes or understanding of the general public; to bring into widespread or common use

Examples

To generalize ideas for people
The author aimed to generalize complex scientific ideas for a broader audience.
The organization worked hard to generalize the practice so it would be accepted in daily life.
This approach helped to generalize the once niche cuisine across the entire country.
Her goal was to generalise advanced computer skills for everyday users.
Several authors have tried to generalise the findings so they can benefit a broader audience.
The new textbook generalises complex scientific concepts for young readers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: popularize - to make something popular or well known to the general public
  • synonym: vulgarize - make technical or complex ideas simpler and accessible to ordinary people
