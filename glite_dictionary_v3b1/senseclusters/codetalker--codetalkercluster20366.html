codetalker

noun
Native American military code user in WWII
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊdˌtɔkər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdˌtɔːkə / (BrE)
a member of the group of Native American soldiers, especially Navajos, who used their native language to create an unbreakable code for secure military communication during World War II

Examples

A codetalker soldier
A Navajo codetalker demonstrated how the coded language worked to a classroom of students.
Historians have written many books about the bravery of the codetalkers.
The codetalker played a crucial role in keeping military messages secure during the war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: code talker - a member of a group who transmitted secret military information using native languages during WWII
