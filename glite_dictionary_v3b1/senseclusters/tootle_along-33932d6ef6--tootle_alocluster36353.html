tootle along

verb
move in a leisurely way
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːtəl əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈtuːtəl əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to travel or move in a relaxed, leisurely way, often without particular haste or purpose

Examples

To tootle along slowly
After lunch, she decided to tootle along to the local shop for some bread.
Every Sunday, we tootle along the riverbank and enjoy the scenery.
The old couple would tootle along the country roads in their vintage car.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amble - to walk at a slow, relaxed pace
  • antonym: rush - to move with urgent haste
  • synonym: saunter - to walk in a slow, relaxed manner
