lark

verb
to play boisterously or frolic
Pronunciation: / lɑrk / (AmE), / lɑːk / (BrE)
to play or behave in a lively, carefree, and boisterous way, especially for fun

Examples

To lark around
During the picnic, the cousins larked together on the grassy hill.
The children larked about in the backyard until sunset.
We spent the afternoon larking around by the lake, laughing and splashing water everywhere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frolic - to play or move about cheerfully, excitedly, or energetically
  • synonym: romp - to play or move about in a lively, energetic way
