prairie dock

noun
a tall North American herb with large leaves
7.4 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpreə.riː dɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈpreə.riː dɒk/ (BrE)
a tall perennial herb (Silphium terebinthinaceum) native to eastern North America, with large basal leaves and whitish, daisy-like flowers; traditionally, its leaves were used by Catawba Indians to treat burns

Examples

The prairie dock plant
Each summer, the prairie dock adds bright color to the wildflower meadow with its pale flowers.
In the tallgrass prairie you can spot the distinctive leaves of prairie dock among the grasses.
The Catawba traditionally used prairie dock leaves as a natural remedy for burns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compass plant - a similar North American plant (Silphium laciniatum) with deeply cut leaves
