sinewy

adjective
full of sinews; tough meat

(of meat) containing many sinews; tough and difficult to chew.

Examples

Sinewy meat
After hours of simmering, the once sinewy beef became soft and flavorful.
She pushed aside the sinewy pieces of meat on her plate, preferring the tender parts.
The sinewy steak was difficult to cut and even harder to chew.

Synonyms

  • stringy
    4.4 0.28/m
    stringy meat has long, tough strands that pull apart when chewed, while sinewy meat has visible sinews and is hard to bite.
  • fibrous
    6.0 0.09/m
    fibrous meat contains thick fibers that resist chewing, while sinewy meat has visible sinews and feels firmer and harder to eat.
  • tough
    1.7 2.17/m
    tough meat is generally hard or firm to chew or cut, while sinewy meat has string-like sinews that make chewing difficult.
