moulter

noun
an animal that sheds its outer layer periodically
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊltər / (AmE), / ˈməʊltə / (BrE)
an animal that sheds its outer layer, such as feathers, skin, or hair, periodically

Examples

A moulter animal
During the spring, the duck becomes a moulter, losing its old feathers to make room for new ones.
Many insects are habitual moulters, discarding their exoskeleton several times during development.
The snake is a moulter, regularly shedding its skin as it grows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: molt - the act of shedding feathers, skin, or hair
