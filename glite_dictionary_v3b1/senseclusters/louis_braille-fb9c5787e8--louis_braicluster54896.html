Louis Braille

proper noun
French inventor of the Braille system
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈluː.iː breɪl/ /; / /ˈluː.ɪs breɪl/ (AmE), /ˈluː.iː breɪl/ /; / /ˈluː.ɪs breɪl/ (BrE)
(1809–1852) A French educator who became blind at three and invented the Braille system of writing and printing for blind people.
