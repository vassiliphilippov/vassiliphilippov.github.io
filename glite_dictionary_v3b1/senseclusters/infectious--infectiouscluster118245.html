infectious

adjective
caused by or relating to infection
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃəs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃəs/ (BrE)
relating to a disease or agent that can be passed from one person to another by infection

Examples

Infectious disease
Patients with infectious conditions are treated in separate hospital wards.
Proper handwashing can help prevent the spread of infectious illnesses.
The doctor explained that the flu is an infectious disease caused by a virus.
Hepatitis B is an infectious disease transmitted through blood and bodily fluids.
Some bacteria are not infectious to humans.
The flu is highly infectious and can spread rapidly in schools.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagious - able to be transmitted from one person to another, especially through direct contact
  • antonym: noninfectious - (of a disease or agent) not capable of causing infection
