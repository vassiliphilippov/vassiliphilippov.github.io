Cytisus multiflorus

noun
a low European broom with pale flowers
Pronunciation: / saɪˈtaɪsəs ˌmʌl.tɪˈflɔːr.əs / (AmE), / sɪˈtɪs.əs ˌmʌl.tɪˈflɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A low European shrub with trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers, commonly known as white Spanish broom.

Examples

The Cytisus multiflorus shrub
Bees are attracted to the abundant flowers of Cytisus multiflorus during the early summer months.
Gardeners often plant Cytisus multiflorus for its delicate, ornamental blooms.
The hillside was covered in Cytisus multiflorus, its pale flowers brightening the spring landscape.
