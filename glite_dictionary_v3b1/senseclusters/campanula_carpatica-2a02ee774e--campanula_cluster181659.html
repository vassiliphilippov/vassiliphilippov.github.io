Campanula carpatica

noun
European perennial bellflower species
Pronunciation: / kæmˈpæn.jʊ.lə kɑːrˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / kæmˈpæn.jʊ.lə kɑːˈpatɪkə / (BrE)
A species of European perennial bellflower that grows in clumps with spreading stems and produces blue or white flowers.

Examples

Campanula carpatica blue flowers
Many gardeners choose Campanula carpatica for its vibrant blue blooms in rock gardens.
The clumps of Campanula carpatica add a touch of elegance to the border of the flower bed.
White varieties of Campanula carpatica are less common but equally striking in appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bellflower - plant of the genus Campanula, with bell-shaped flowers
