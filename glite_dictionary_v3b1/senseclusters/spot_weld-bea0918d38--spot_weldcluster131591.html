spot weld

noun
localized weld at a single point
5.1
Pronunciation: / spɑt wɛld / (AmE), / spɒt wɛld / (BrE)
A localized weld made at a single point, typically where two pieces of metal are joined together by applying heat and pressure.

Examples

A spot weld joint
A poorly executed spot weld can compromise the strength of the metal structure.
The assembly line uses automated machines to make hundreds of spot welds per hour.
The technician inspected each spot weld to ensure the car door was securely attached.
