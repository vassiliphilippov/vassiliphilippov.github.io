plunk

verb
make a thudding sound or movement
7.1 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / plÊŒÅ‹k / (AmE), / plÊŒÅ‹k / (BrE)
to move or make a sound with a sharp, dull, or hollow thud, as a horse's hooves striking the ground

Examples

Hooves plunk outside
She could hear the sound of hooves plunking outside her window just before dawn.
The horse plunked along the dirt road, each hoof hitting the ground with a muffled thud.
We watched as the carriage plunked steadily over the cobblestones.

Synonyms

  • clunk
    6.5 0.36/m
    clunk is a dull, heavy sound from a heavy object or hoof strike, while plunk is a single hollow thud.
  • thud
    3.5 0.19/m
    thud is a dull, heavy hit you hear when something falls, while plunk is a sharper or hollow hoof-like thud.
  • clop
    7.9 0.30/m
    clop is a short, clear hoof sound on hard ground, while plunk is a single thud that can sound hollow or dull.
  • clip-clop
    5.1 0.09/m
    clip-clop is a rhythmic series of hoof beats heard while walking fast, while plunk is a single dull or hollow hoof thud.
