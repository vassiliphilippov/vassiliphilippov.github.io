Stanford-Binet test

noun
intelligence test based on Binet-Simon Scale
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈstæn.fərd biː.ˈneɪ test/ (AmE), /ˈstæn.fəd biː.ˈneɪ test/ (BrE)
an individually administered intelligence test that is a revision of the Binet-Simon Scale, widely used to measure cognitive abilities and intelligence quotients (IQ) in children and adults

Examples

The Stanford-Binet test score
After taking the Stanford-Binet test, participants received detailed reports of their intelligence quotients.
Researchers often use the Stanford-Binet test in studies on intellectual development.
The psychologist administered the Stanford-Binet test to assess the child's cognitive strengths and weaknesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: IQ test - a test designed to measure a person's intelligence quotient
