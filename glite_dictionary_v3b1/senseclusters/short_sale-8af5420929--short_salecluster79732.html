short sale

noun
sale of property for less than mortgage
5.6
Pronunciation: /ʃɔːrt seɪl/ (AmE), /ʃɔːt seɪl/ (BrE)
a real-estate transaction in which a homeowner sells a property for less than the amount owed on the mortgage, with the lender's agreement to accept the reduced payoff.

Examples

Home short sale
During the housing crisis, many homeowners pursued a short sale to avoid foreclosure.
The bank approved the short sale after determining it was cheaper than starting foreclosure proceedings.
They negotiated a short sale with the lender to sell the house for less than the mortgage balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distressed sale - a sale forced by financial difficulty, often at a price below market value
  • antonym: foreclosure - the legal process by which a lender seizes property when the borrower fails to keep up mortgage payments; often the alternative to a short sale
