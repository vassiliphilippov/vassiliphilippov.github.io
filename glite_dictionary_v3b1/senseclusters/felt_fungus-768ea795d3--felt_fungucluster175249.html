felt fungus

noun
fungus encircling tree branches, esp. citrus
Pronunciation: / fɛlt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (AmE), / fɛlt ˈfʌŋɡəs / (BrE)
a fungus that often forms a felt-like covering around the twigs and branches of various trees, especially citrus trees, chiefly in the southern United States

Examples

Felt fungus on twigs
Experts identified the white growth around the twigs as felt fungus and recommended immediate treatment.
Gardeners were advised to inspect tree branches for any signs of felt fungus development.
The orange grove suffered damage from a persistent felt fungus infestation.
