arsenous anhydride

noun
a poisonous white powder, arsenic trioxide
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈsiːnəs ænˈhaɪdraɪd / (AmE), / ɑːˈsiːnəs anˈhɪdraɪd / (BrE)
a white, poisonous powder (arsenic trioxide) used in glass manufacturing, pesticides, and weed killers

Examples

White arsenous anhydride powder
Because of its toxicity, handling arsenous anhydride requires protective equipment and strict safety procedures.
Historically, arsenous anhydride was infamously used as a rat poison.
The factory uses arsenous anhydride in its glass-making process.
