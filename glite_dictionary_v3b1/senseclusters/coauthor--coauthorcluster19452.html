coauthor

verb
to write jointly
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɔːθər / (AmE), / kəʊˈɔːθə / (BrE)
to write something, such as a book or article, together with one or more people

Examples

To coauthor an article
They decided to coauthor a research paper on climate change.
The two scientists will coauthor a book about their discoveries.
She enjoys coauthoring articles with colleagues from different countries.
