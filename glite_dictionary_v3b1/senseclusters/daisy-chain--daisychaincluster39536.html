daisy-chain

verb
connect devices in a sequence
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (BrE)
To connect devices together in a sequence, usually by linking each device to the next one in line, especially in computers or electronics.

Examples

To daisy-chain devices
If you daisy-chain too many components, you might experience signal degradation.
Many USB devices can be daisy-chained to simplify cabling on your desk.
The engineers daisy-chained the monitors so all would display the same output.
