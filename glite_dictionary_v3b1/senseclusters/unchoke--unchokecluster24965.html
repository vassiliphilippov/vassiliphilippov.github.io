unchoke

verb
to clear an obstruction
6.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtʃoʊk / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʃəʊk / (BrE)
to remove a restriction or obstruction, especially to allow something to flow or operate freely again

Examples

To unchoke a drain
The plumber had to unchoke the drain so the water could flow again.
After a few attempts, she managed to unchoke the vacuum cleaner's hose.
The mechanic unchoked the carburetor to stop the engine from stalling on cold mornings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: block - to prevent movement or flow by obstructing
  • synonym: clear - to remove something that is blocking or obstructing
