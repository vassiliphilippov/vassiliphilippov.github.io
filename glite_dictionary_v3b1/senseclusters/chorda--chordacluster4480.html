chorda

noun
cordlike anatomical structure
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrdə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdə / (BrE)
a cordlike anatomical structure in the body, such as a nerve or tendon

Examples

Chorda tympani nerve
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the chorda during the procedure.
In anatomy class, we studied the structure of the chorda tympani.
A torn chorda can lead to loss of function in the affected area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nerve - a bundle of fibers transmitting signals in the body
  • synonym: tendon - a strong cord connecting muscle to bone
