Tending to stir up conflict, provoke strong feelings, or incite rebellion.

Examples

Incendiary speech
His incendiary comments ignited widespread protests across the city.
Publishing such an incendiary article was bound to spark controversy.
The leader's incendiary rhetoric inspired many to challenge the authorities.

Synonyms

  • inflammatory
    7.4 2.12/m
    inflammatory is used for speech or writing that arouses anger or violence, while incendiary focuses on stirring rebellion or conflict.
  • incitive
    8.9 0.00/m
    incitive tends to arouse strong feeling or call people to action, while incendiary stresses starting conflict or rebellion.
  • instigative
    8.4 0.00/m
    instigative provokes others to take action or unrest, while incendiary more strongly implies causing open conflict or rebellion.
  • rabble-rousing
    8.6 0.07/m
    rabble-rousing stirs ordinary people to political anger or protest, while incendiary is a broader term for inciting conflict.
  • provocative
    6.7 7.51/m
    provocative causes annoyance or thought and can be playful or sexual, while incendiary aims to inflame feelings or start revolt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: provocative - Causing annoyance, anger, or another strong reaction, especially deliberately
  • antonym: soothing - Having a calming or tranquilizing effect
