subculture

verb
to transfer microorganisms
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
to transfer cells or microorganisms from one growth medium to another to continue their cultivation (scientific use)

Examples

To subculture bacteria
Scientists subculture yeast cells regularly to keep the strains active in the laboratory.
It is important to subculture the bacteria every few days to prevent overgrowth.
After observing the initial results, the technician decided to subculture the cells into fresh medium.
The scientist subcultured the cells to ensure they remained healthy and active.
After three days, the technician subcultured the bacteria into fresh nutrient broth.
It is important to subculture regularly to prevent contamination in the laboratory.

  • synonym: inoculate - to introduce microorganisms into a new environment for growth
