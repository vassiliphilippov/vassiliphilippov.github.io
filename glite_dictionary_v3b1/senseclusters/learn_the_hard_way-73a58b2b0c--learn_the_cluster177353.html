learn the hard way

verb
gain knowledge through difficulty or mistakes
1.0
Pronunciation: / lɜrn ðə hɑrd weɪ / (AmE), / lɜːn ðə hɑːd weɪ / (BrE)
to discover something through difficult or unpleasant experiences, rather than by being taught or warned

Examples

To learn the hard way
After forgetting his umbrella during a storm, Tom learned the hard way why forecasts matter.
Many entrepreneurs learn the hard way that running a business requires more than a good idea.
She learned the hard way that shortcuts often lead to bigger problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: learn one's lesson - to gain knowledge through experience, often negative
