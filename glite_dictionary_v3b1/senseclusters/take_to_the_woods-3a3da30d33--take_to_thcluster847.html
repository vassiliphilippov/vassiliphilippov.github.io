take to the woods

verb
to flee or escape suddenly
4.4
Pronunciation: / teɪk tə ðə wʊdz / (AmE), / teɪk tə ðə wʊdz / (BrE)
to flee or run away, especially suddenly or in a hurry

Examples

To take to the woods quickly
As soon as the rain started pouring, everyone took to the woods for shelter.
The hikers took to the woods once they heard the distant roar of a bear.
When the alarm went off, the thieves took to the woods to avoid being caught.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flee - to run away from danger or trouble quickly
  • synonym: run away - to leave a place or person secretly and suddenly
  • antonym: stand one's ground - to refuse to run away or retreat
