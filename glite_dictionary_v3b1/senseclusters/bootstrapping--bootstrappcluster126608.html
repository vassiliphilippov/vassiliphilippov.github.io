bootstrapping

noun
yield curve method
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbuːt.stræp.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbuːt.stræp.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a financial method for building a zero-coupon yield curve by calculating spot rates step by step from bond prices

Examples

Bond bootstrapping
The analyst applied bootstrapping to derive the spot rates from a series of bond prices.
Understanding bootstrapping is essential for anyone working with fixed income securities.
By using bootstrapping, the team constructed a more accurate yield curve for their financial models.
