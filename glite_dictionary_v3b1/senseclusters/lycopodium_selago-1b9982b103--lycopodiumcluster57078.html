Lycopodium selago

noun
a small clubmoss species
Pronunciation: / laɪkəˈpoʊdiəm ˈsɛləˌgoʊ / (AmE), / laɪkəˈpəʊdiəm ˈsɛləɡəʊ / (BrE)
a species of clubmoss native to northern Europe and America, resembling a small fir tree

Examples

Lycopodium selago plant
Botanists discovered several specimens of Lycopodium selago in the damp, shaded forest.
In some traditional medicines, dried Lycopodium selago has been used for its supposed healing properties.
The leaves of Lycopodium selago resemble those of a tiny fir, making it an easily recognizable clubmoss.
