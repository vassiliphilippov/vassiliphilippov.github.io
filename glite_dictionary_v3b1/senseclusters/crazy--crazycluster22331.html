crazy

adjective
bizarre; fantastic; very strange
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪzi / (AmE), / ˈkreɪzi / (BrE)
very strange, surprising, foolish, or out of the ordinary

Examples

Crazy idea
He wore a crazy costume to the party that made everyone laugh.
Her apartment is decorated in a crazy mix of colors and styles.
We had a crazy idea to drive across the country in two days.
Her friends thought it was a crazy idea to start a business during a recession.
It would be crazy to drive through the blizzard just to buy ice cream.
They invested in a crazy scheme that everyone else said would never work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absurd - wildly unreasonable or illogical
  • synonym: bizarre - very strange or unusual; odd
  • synonym: fantastic - imaginative or fanciful; extraordinary
  • antonym: rational - based on or in accordance with reason or logic
  • antonym: sensible - showing good sense or judgment
