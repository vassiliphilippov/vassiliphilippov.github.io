scramble

noun
a disorderly struggle
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈskræmbəl / (AmE), / ˈskræmbəl / (BrE)
a hurried or disorderly struggle or attempt to get or achieve something, often involving competition

Examples

A scramble for seats
After the announcement, a scramble for seats erupted in the auditorium.
The sudden storm caused a scramble for shelter among the hikers.
There was a frantic scramble for tickets when they went on sale.
In the scramble to finish before the deadline, several errors were overlooked.
The team's victory led to a scramble for tickets to the next game.
There was a scramble for the best seats as soon as the doors opened.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fight - a violent struggle between people
  • antonym: order - a situation where things are arranged neatly or methodically (opposite of a scramble)
  • synonym: rush - a sudden movement towards something, often in competition
  • synonym: scrabble - a hurried, disorganized attempt or search
