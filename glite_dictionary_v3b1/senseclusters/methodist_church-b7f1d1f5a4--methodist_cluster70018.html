Methodist Church

proper noun
Protestant Christian denomination founded by the Wesleys
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛθ.ə.dɪst tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɛθ.əd.ɪst tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination founded on the teachings of John and Charles Wesley, known for its emphasis on personal faith and social justice.

Examples

The Methodist Church community
Many families in the town attend the Methodist Church every Sunday.
She was raised in a Methodist Church community that valued education and charity.
The history of the Methodist Church is closely tied to social reform movements.
