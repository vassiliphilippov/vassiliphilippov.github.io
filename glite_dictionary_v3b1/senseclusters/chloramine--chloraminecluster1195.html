chloramine

noun
chemical compound of chlorine and nitrogen
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔrəˌmin / (AmE), / ˈklɔːrəmiːn / (BrE)
Any of a group of chemical compounds containing chlorine and nitrogen, used as disinfectants or antiseptics.

Examples

Chloramine disinfectant
Doctors sometimes apply chloramine to wounds as an antiseptic.
Exposure to chloramine in swimming pools can irritate the eyes and lungs.
The water treatment plant uses chloramine to disinfect the municipal supply.
