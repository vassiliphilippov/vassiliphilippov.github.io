motion

noun
illusion of movement in rapid images
6.8 3.82/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmoÊŠÊƒÉ™n / (AmE), / ËˆmÉ™ÊŠÊƒÉ™n / (BrE)
an optical illusion in which a sequence of static images, shown rapidly, appear to depict continuous movement

Examples

Motion in movies
Old films used 24 frames per second to produce smooth motion.
Our brains interpret flashing lights as continuous motion, even though each light is actually stationary.
The illusion of motion in animation is created by rapidly displaying a sequence of drawings.

Synonyms

  • apparent motion
    7.2 0.06/m
    apparent motion names the visual illusion of movement from quick still images, while motion is the general term for that visual effect.
  • apparent movement
    7.6 0.01/m
    apparent movement describes stationary objects seeming to move when shown in quick sequence, while motion is the common word for this phenomenon.
  • movement
    6.1 0.39/m
    movement often means physical change of position or a visual illusion from quick images, while motion names the specific illusion in sequences.

Related Terms
