Strix

noun
genus of owls without ear tufts
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪks / (AmE), / ˈstrɪks / (BrE)
A genus of owls that lack ear tufts, including species such as the tawny owl and barred owl.

Examples

Strix owl species
Several species within Strix inhabit forests across Europe and North America.
The tawny owl belongs to the genus Strix, which is known for lacking ear tufts.
Unlike many other owls, members of Strix have rounded heads.
