DNA chip

noun
microchip with DNA probes for analysis
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌɛnˈeɪ tʃɪp / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌenˈeɪ tʃɪp / (BrE)
A microchip containing DNA probes that can detect or analyze DNA sequences in biological samples.

Examples

DNA chip technology
Advances in DNA chip technology have revolutionized medical diagnostics.
Researchers used a DNA chip to identify genetic mutations in the patient's cells.
The DNA chip can test thousands of genes at once, speeding up the analysis process.
