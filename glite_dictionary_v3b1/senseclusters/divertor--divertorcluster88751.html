divertor

noun
fusion reactor device
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɜːrtər / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːtə / (BrE)
a device in a fusion reactor that removes waste particles and impurities from plasma to maintain reactor performance

Examples

Fusion reactor divertor
The divertor plays a crucial role in keeping the plasma clean inside a fusion reactor.
Engineers are developing a new divertor to improve waste removal in next-generation fusion devices.
Without a functioning divertor, the reactor would quickly accumulate harmful impurities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filter - a device that removes unwanted substances from a fluid
