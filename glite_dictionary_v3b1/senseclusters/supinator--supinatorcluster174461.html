supinator

noun
muscle that turns the palm upward
Pronunciation: / suːˈpeɪneɪtər / (AmE), / suːˈpaɪnətə / (BrE)
a muscle, especially in the forearm, that enables supination (turning the palm upward or forward)

Examples

The supinator muscle
An injury to the supinator can make it difficult to turn your palm upward.
Physical therapists often target the supinator during rehabilitation for forearm injuries.
The supinator muscle plays a key role in rotating the forearm so the palm faces upward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pronator - a muscle that turns or holds a part (such as the hand or foot) so that the palm or sole is facing downwards or backwards
  • synonym: supination - the act of turning the palm upward or forward
