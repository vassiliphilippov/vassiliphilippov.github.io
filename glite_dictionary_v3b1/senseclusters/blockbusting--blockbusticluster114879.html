blockbusting

verb
to exploit homeowners' fears
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈblɑkˌbʌstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblɒkˌbʌstɪŋ / (BrE)
to persuade homeowners to sell their property by exploiting fears about neighborhood decline or demographic change, often for profit

Examples

To blockbust a neighborhood
Real estate agents were fined for blockbusting in several urban neighborhoods during the 1960s.
Laws were introduced to prevent blockbusting and protect homeowners from unethical practices.
The community organized to stop blockbusting and preserve the neighborhood's diversity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fair housing - the principle of equal opportunity in housing without discrimination
  • synonym: scare tactics - methods used to frighten people into taking action
