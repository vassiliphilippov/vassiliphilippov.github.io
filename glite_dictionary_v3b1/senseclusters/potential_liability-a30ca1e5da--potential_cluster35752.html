potential liability

noun
possible future risk or disadvantage
7.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛnʃəl laɪəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃəl laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
a person or thing that may cause risk, loss, or disadvantage in the future

Examples

A potential liability risk
A company must assess every potential liability before making a major investment.
Her lack of experience was seen as a potential liability to the project.
If the environment changes, today's advantage could become tomorrow's potential liability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asset - something useful or valuable, opposite of liability
  • synonym: risk - the possibility of loss, injury, or other adverse circumstance
