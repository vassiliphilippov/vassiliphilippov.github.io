sign

verb
enter into a written contract
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / saɪn / (AmE), / saɪn / (BrE)
to write your name on a document, enter into a contract, or apply a digital signature

Examples

To sign a contract
After a long negotiation, the basketball player finally signed with the Los Angeles team.
Several authors signed with the publishing house for their next books.
The singer signed to perform at the music festival next summer.
After months of negotiation, the company finally signed him as their new spokesperson.
She hopes to sign a talented artist to her agency this year.
The club decided to sign several new players before the transfer deadline.
All parties must sign the agreement before it comes into effect.
Have you signed your lease yet, or are you still considering your options?
She refused to sign the contract until her lawyer reviewed it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authenticate - to verify the identity of a sender or the integrity of data, often using cryptographic methods
  • synonym: authorize - to give official permission for something
  • synonym: autograph - to write one's own signature, often as a memento
  • synonym: contract - to formally agree to do something, especially by signing a legal document
  • synonym: digitally sign - to apply a cryptographic signature to a file or message to prove authorship and integrity
  • synonym: endorse - to sign the back of a check or to support officially
  • antonym: fire - to dismiss someone from their job
  • synonym: hire - to employ someone for wages or salary
  • synonym: ratify - to officially approve or confirm, especially a treaty or agreement
