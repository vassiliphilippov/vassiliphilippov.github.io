lost cause

noun
a hopeless or futile case or effort
1.0
Pronunciation: / lɔst kɔz /; / lɑst kɔz / (AmE), / lɒst kɔːz / (BrE)
a person, thing, or effort that has no hope of success or improvement

Restoring the old, damaged painting turned out to be a lost cause.
She refused to give up, even when everyone said it was a lost cause.
