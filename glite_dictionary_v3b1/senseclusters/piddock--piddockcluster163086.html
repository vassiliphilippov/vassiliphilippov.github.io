piddock

noun
marine mollusk that bores into rock or wood
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪdək / (AmE), / ˈpɪdək / (BrE)
a marine bivalve mollusk that bores into rock, clay, or wood using its rough, saw-like shells

Examples

A piddock mollusk
A piddock uses its shells to burrow for protection from predators.
Fishermen often find piddocks embedded in wooden structures near the shore.
The piddock can create tunnels in soft rock along the coastline.
