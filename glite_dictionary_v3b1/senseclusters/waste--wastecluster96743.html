waste

verb
to devastate or destroy completely
2.7
Pronunciation: / weɪst / (AmE), / weɪst / (BrE)
to cause extensive destruction or total ruin; to devastate utterly

Examples

To waste a city
Centuries of war have wasted once-flourishing towns in the valley.
The hurricane threatened to waste the entire coastal region.
The invading army wasted everything in its path, leaving only ruins behind.
