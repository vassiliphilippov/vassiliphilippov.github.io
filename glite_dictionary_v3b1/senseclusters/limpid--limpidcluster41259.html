Synonyms

  • pellucid
    9.9 0.04/m
    pellucid is a very clear, glass-like term for liquids, while limpid describes equally pure, easy-to-see-through liquids or air.
  • crystal clear
    3.1 0.42/m
    crystal clear sounds like everyday speech for perfectly see-through liquids or views, while limpid is a more literary word for the same.
  • transparent
    3.2 6.97/m
    transparent means allowing light through and is common in plain speech, while limpid feels slightly more poetic for liquids or sky.
  • lucid
    4.8 0.14/m
    lucid often describes clear thinking or speech, while limpid mainly describes clear liquids, eyes, or light-filled air.
  • crystalline
    6.3 0.78/m
    crystalline often suggests a sharp, bright clarity like crystal in shape or sound, while limpid stresses pure, see-through liquids.
  • clear
    1.0 43/m
    clear is a broad everyday word for many kinds of openness or no obstruction, while limpid specifically names perfectly transparent liquids.
