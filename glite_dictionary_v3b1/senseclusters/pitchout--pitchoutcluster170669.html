pitchout

verb
to throw outside intentionally
8.5
Pronunciation: /pɪtʃ.ˈaʊt/ (AmE), /pɪtʃ.ˈaʊt/ (BrE)
to throw a pitch deliberately outside the strike zone so the catcher can prevent a baserunner from stealing a base

Examples

To pitchout to catcher
The coach signaled for the pitcher to pitchout when he saw the runner preparing to steal second.
During the tense ninth inning, the team decided to pitchout to stop a possible stolen base.
If you think the runner might go, you can always pitchout to give your catcher a better chance.
