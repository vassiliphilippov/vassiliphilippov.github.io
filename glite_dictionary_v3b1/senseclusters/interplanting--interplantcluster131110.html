interplanting

noun
mixed crop planting
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈplæntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈplɑːntɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of growing different types of plants or crops together in the same area to benefit each other

Examples

Interplanting vegetables and flowers
Gardeners often use interplanting to improve soil health and reduce pests naturally.
Interplanting tomatoes with basil is believed to enhance the flavor of both crops.
The farmer adopted interplanting to make better use of space and resources in his fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: companion planting - growing different plants together for mutual benefit
  • antonym: monoculture - the cultivation of a single crop in a given area
