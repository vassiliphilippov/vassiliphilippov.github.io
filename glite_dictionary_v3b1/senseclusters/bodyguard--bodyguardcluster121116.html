bodyguard

noun
person who protects someone important
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑdiˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈbɒdiˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
a person employed to protect someone important from danger or attack, often by accompanying them in public

Examples

Personal bodyguard
As a bodyguard, he was trained to anticipate security threats.
His bodyguard intervened quickly when the crowd became unruly.
The celebrity hired a bodyguard to keep her safe during public appearances.
