blast

noun
forceful criticism or attack (informal)
Pronunciation: / blæst / (AmE), / blɑːst / (BrE)
an intense and forceful expression of criticism or disapproval, often delivered publicly or strongly

Examples

A blast from critics
Her report received a blast from critics in several major newspapers.
The coach gave the team a blast after their poor performance in the first half.
The politician faced a blast of questions from angry reporters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attack - a strong or aggressive criticism
  • antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration
