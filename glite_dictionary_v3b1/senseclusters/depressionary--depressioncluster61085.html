depressionary

adjective
economic downturn
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛʃənəri / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a period of economic depression

Examples

Depressionary economy
The government introduced new policies to address the depressionary effects on the job market.
Many businesses closed during the depressionary period that followed the stock market crash.
Economists warned of depressionary trends as consumer spending sharply declined.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prosperous - characterized by financial success or good economic conditions
  • synonym: recessionary - relating to or characteristic of a period of economic recession
