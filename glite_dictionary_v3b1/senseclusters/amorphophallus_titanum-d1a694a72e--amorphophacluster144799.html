Amorphophallus titanum

noun
tropical plant with large, smelly bloom
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌmɔr.foʊˈfæl.əs taɪˈteɪ.nəm / (AmE), / əˌmɔː.fəˈfæl.əs tɪˈtɑː.nəm / (BrE)
A large, foul-smelling tropical plant native to Sumatra, known for producing one of the world's largest flowering structures and emitting a strong odor resembling rotting flesh.

Examples

The Amorphophallus titanum flower
Because the Amorphophallus titanum smells like rotting meat, it is often called the 'corpse flower'.
It takes several years for an Amorphophallus titanum to produce its enormous and rare blossom.
The Amorphophallus titanum attracted thousands of visitors when it bloomed at the botanical garden.
