flatland

noun
level open terrain
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈflætˌlænd / (BrE)
a large area of land that is flat and has few or no hills or mountains

Examples

Wide flatland
The flatland made it easy for farmers to plant and harvest their crops.
After driving through mountains, we finally reached the wide flatland of the valley.
Wildflowers covered the flatland as far as the eye could see.
The flatland stretched as far as the eye could see, with no hills in sight.
Farmers prefer to grow crops on flatland because it is easier to cultivate.
Wild horses ran freely across the open flatland under the wide blue sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain
  • synonym: plain - a large area of flat, open land
  • degree higher: plateau - an area of flat land that is higher than the land around it
