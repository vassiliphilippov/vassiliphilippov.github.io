overplant

verb
to plant densely
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈplænt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
to plant more seeds or plants in an area than is needed or suitable, often making it very crowded

Examples

To overplant flower beds
Gardeners often overplant flower beds to create a lush, full appearance.
If you overplant the area, the plants may compete for sunlight and nutrients.
They chose to overplant the hillside with ground cover to prevent soil erosion.
If you overplant your vegetable patch, the crops may compete for sunlight and nutrients.
Gardeners are advised not to overplant so that each flower has enough space to thrive.
He realized he had overplanted the pots when the seedlings started crowding each other.
Farmers sometimes overplant to make sure enough crops survive unpredictable weather.
If you overplant your garden, you may need to thin out the seedlings later.
The company advised growers to overplant in areas with poor soil quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replant - to plant again in the same area
  • synonym: sow - to plant seeds in the ground
  • synonym: underplant - to plant beneath or among existing plants
