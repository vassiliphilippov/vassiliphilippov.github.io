paleoclimate

noun
ancient climate
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliːoʊˈklaɪmət /; / ˌpeɪliˈoʊklaɪmət / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˈklaɪmət /; / ˌpeɪlɪəʊˈklaɪmət / (BrE)
the climate conditions of the Earth or a region during the distant geological past, as determined by scientific evidence

Examples

The paleoclimate record
Researchers analyze ice cores to reconstruct the paleoclimate of Antarctica.
Understanding the paleoclimate helps scientists predict future climate changes.
Fossil records provide valuable clues about the paleoclimate of different regions.
Scientists analyze ice cores to learn about Earth's paleoclimate and how it has changed over time.
The study of paleoclimate helps us understand how current climate patterns might evolve.
Fossilized pollen provides important clues about the paleoclimate of ancient forests.
