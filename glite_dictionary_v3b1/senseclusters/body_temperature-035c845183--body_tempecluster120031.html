body temperature

noun
temperature of a living body
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑdi ˈtɛmpɚˌtʃɝ / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˈtɛmp(ə)rətʃə / (BrE)
The temperature of a living person's or animal's body, typically measured to assess health.

Examples

Body temperature rises
If your body temperature rises above 100°F, you may have a fever.
Normal body temperature for humans is about 37°C.
The nurse checked his body temperature before the doctor arrived.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: fever - elevated body temperature indicating illness
  • degree lower: hypothermia - dangerously low body temperature
