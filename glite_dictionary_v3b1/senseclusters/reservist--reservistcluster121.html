reservist

noun
military reserve member
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɜrvɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːvɪst / (BrE)
a member of a military reserve force who is not in active service but can be called up when needed

Examples

A reservist soldier
After leaving active duty, she became a reservist with the national guard.
As a reservist, he balances civilian life with occasional military duties.
During the emergency, all reservists were called up for training exercises.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regular - a soldier in full-time active service
