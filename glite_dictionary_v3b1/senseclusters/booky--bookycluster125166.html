booky

adjective
literary or scholarly
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊki / (AmE), / ˈbʊki / (BrE)
relating to books, scholarship, or a literary or academic style, often lacking practical examples.

Examples

Booky style
The professor's explanations were too booky for students who wanted hands-on experience.
Her writing style is quite booky, filled with references and academic language.
Some people find the course too booky and wish it included more real-world applications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - focused on real situations or actions rather than theory
  • synonym: scholarly - relating to academic study or learning
