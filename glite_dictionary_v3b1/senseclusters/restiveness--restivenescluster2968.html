restiveness

noun
state of being restless or uneasy
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛstɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈrɛstɪvnəs /; / ˈrɛstɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being restless, uneasy, or difficult to control, often due to nervousness, impatience, or resistance

Examples

Signs of restiveness
Her restiveness was apparent as she tapped her foot impatiently.
The horse's restiveness made it difficult for the rider to maintain control.
There was an air of restiveness in the crowd as the speech dragged on.
A sense of restiveness spread through the crowd as the event was delayed.
His restiveness was obvious as he kept tapping his foot during the long meeting.
The teacher noticed the students' restiveness after hours of sitting still.
After hours of waiting, the children's restiveness became difficult for the teachers to handle.
Political restiveness spread throughout the country as protests increased.
The manager was frustrated by the restiveness of the team during the meeting.

  • antonym: obedience - the act or quality of obeying or being willing to comply
  • synonym: restive - difficult to control; stubborn or restless
  • synonym: restlessness - the state of being unable to rest, relax, or remain still
