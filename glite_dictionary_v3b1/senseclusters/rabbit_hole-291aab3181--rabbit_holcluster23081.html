rabbit hole

noun
hole in ground made by rabbits
Pronunciation: / ˈræbɪt hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈræbɪt həʊl / (BrE)
A hole in the ground used as a nest or burrow by wild rabbits.

Examples

A rabbit hole in the field
Be careful not to trip over a rabbit hole when walking through the field.
The fox chased the rabbit all the way to its rabbit hole.
We spotted several rabbit holes under the hedge in the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by a small animal for shelter
