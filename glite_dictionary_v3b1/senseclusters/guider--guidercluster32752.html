guider

noun
telescope tracking device
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪdər / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪdə / (BrE)
a device that keeps a telescope or camera steadily pointed at a celestial object as it moves across the sky

Examples

Telescope guider
The astronomer attached a guider to the telescope to track the planet all night.
Without a guider, it is difficult to photograph faint stars clearly.
Modern telescopes often include a built-in guider for precise tracking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autoguider - an automated device for keeping a telescope aimed at a moving object
  • synonym: mount - a support or base for a telescope or camera
