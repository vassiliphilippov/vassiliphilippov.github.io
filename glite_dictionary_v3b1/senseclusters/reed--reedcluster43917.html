reed

verb
to add parallel grooves
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / riÀêd / (AmE), / riÀêd / (BrE)
to form or decorate a surface with a series of parallel raised or cut lines or grooves

Examples

To reed a coin
The craftsman carefully reeded the edges of the silver coin for decoration.
Many antique columns are reeded to create a classical architectural effect.
She reeds the glass vases to give them a unique texture.

Synonyms

  • flute
    8.4 0.78/m
    flute makes long, narrow decorative or functional channels across a surface, while reed makes a series of parallel raised or cut lines.
  • groove
    5.1 0.39/m
    groove cuts one long, narrow channel into a surface, while reed decorates with many parallel raised or cut lines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groove - to cut long, narrow channels into a surface
  • antonym: smooth - to make a surface even and without grooves
