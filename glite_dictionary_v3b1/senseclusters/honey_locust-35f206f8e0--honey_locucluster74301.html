honey locust

noun

a tall North American tree (Gleditsia triacanthos), often spiny, with small greenish-white flowers in drooping clusters and long, twisting seed pods; yields hard, durable reddish-brown wood

Examples

The honey locust tree
In autumn, the honey locust drops its distinctive twisted seed pods along the sidewalks.
Many city parks have planted honey locust trees because they provide light, filtered shade.
The wood of the honey locust is prized for its hardness and durability.
