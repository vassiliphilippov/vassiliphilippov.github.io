fluorescein

noun
fluorescent yellow dye used as chemical indicator
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /flʊr.ˈes.iːn/ /; / /fluːr.ˈes.iːn/ (AmE), /flʊə.ˈres.iːn/ /; / /fluːə.ˈres.iːn/ (BrE)
a synthetic yellow or greenish dye that glows brightly under ultraviolet light, used as a tracer or indicator in chemistry and medicine

Examples

A drop of fluorescein
A drop of fluorescein was added to the solution to check for leaks.
The doctor used fluorescein dye to examine the patient's eye injury.
When exposed to UV light, fluorescein emits a strong green fluorescence.
A small amount of fluorescein was sufficient to track the movement of water in the laboratory experiment.
Doctors use fluorescein eye drops to diagnose corneal injuries.
The solution glowed bright green after adding fluorescein and exposing it to UV light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluorescence - the emission of light by a substance that has absorbed light
