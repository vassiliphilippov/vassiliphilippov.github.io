John Wiclif

proper noun
English theologian and church reformer
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn ˈwɪklɪf / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈwɪklɪf / (BrE)
(1328–1384) An English theologian and early dissenter from the Roman Catholic Church, whose teachings anticipated the Protestant Reformation.

Examples

John Wiclif teachings
John Wiclif challenged many teachings of the Catholic Church during the fourteenth century.
Many historians regard John Wiclif as the first major figure in the movement for religious reform in England.
The work of John Wiclif laid the foundation for later reformers such as Martin Luther.
