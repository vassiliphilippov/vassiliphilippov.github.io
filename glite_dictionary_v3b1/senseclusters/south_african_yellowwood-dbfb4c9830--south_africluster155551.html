South African yellowwood

noun
southern African tree; timber source
Pronunciation: / ˌsaʊθ ˈæf.rɪ.kən ˈjɛl.oʊ.wʊd / (AmE), / ˌsaʊθ ˈæf.rɪ.kən ˈjɛl.ə.wʊd / (BrE)
a tree or shrub of southern Africa with ridged dark grey bark and stiff, glossy medium to long leaves, known for its valuable timber

Examples

The South African yellowwood tree
Botanists studied the leaves of the South African yellowwood to learn more about its adaptation to local climates.
Several species of South African yellowwood are protected due to their ecological importance and overharvesting.
The South African yellowwood is highly valued for its durable timber used in furniture making.
