primitive

adjective
simple or naive in style; untrained
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪm.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈprɪm.ɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
(of art, objects, or techniques) produced by people without formal training, marked by simplicity or naivety in style.

Examples

Primitive art
Many collectors are drawn to primitive art for its uniqueness and emotional impact.
She admired the primitive drawings for their honest expression and bold colors.
The design of the cabin was intentionally primitive, reflecting a rustic, handmade aesthetic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: naive - showing a lack of experience or formal training, especially in art
  • antonym: sophisticated - developed to a high degree of complexity or refinement
