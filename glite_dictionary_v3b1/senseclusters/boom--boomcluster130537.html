boom

verb
grow or expand rapidly
3.4 6.49/m
Pronunciation: / buÀêm / (AmE), / buÀêm / (BrE)
to grow or develop rapidly and successfully; to flourish or expand quickly

Examples

To boom in business
After the new policy was introduced, the local tech industry began to boom.
Small businesses have started to boom thanks to increased investment in the region.
Tourism tends to boom in the summer months when the weather is warm.

Synonyms

  • burgeon
    7.6 4.16/m
    burgeon begins rapid growth like new shoots in spring, while boom means quick, wide expansion and success.
  • flourish
    4.1 11/m
    flourish grows strong and healthy like a garden in sun, while boom describes fast expansion often in business.
  • thrive
    3.2 17/m
    thrive does well over time and shows strong success in work or life, while boom focuses on rapid growth.
  • skyrocket
    4.1 3.17/m
    skyrocket rises very fast in number or price in a short time, while boom means broad rapid development and success.
  • surge
    3.7 2.84/m
    surge rises suddenly and strongly like a wave in demand, while boom implies longer, wide growth and success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - to become less or worse
  • synonym: prosper - to succeed financially or grow strong
  • synonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful
