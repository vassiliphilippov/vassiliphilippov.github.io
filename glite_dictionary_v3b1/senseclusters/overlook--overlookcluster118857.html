overlook

verb
fail to notice or include
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈlʊk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlʊk / (BrE)
to fail to notice, include, or consider someone or something; to miss or ignore

Examples

To overlook a mistake
I'm sorry I overlooked your name on the list.
It's easy to overlook small errors when proofreading a long report.
She overlooked several emails that needed a response.
I seem to have overlooked an important detail in your report.
It's easy to overlook punctuation errors when proofreading your own work.
She apologized for overlooking his contribution to the project.
Despite his hard work, the team overlooked him when giving out awards.
It hurts to be overlooked for a promotion you deserved.
She often felt overlooked in group conversations.
