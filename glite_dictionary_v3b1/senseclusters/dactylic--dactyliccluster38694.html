dactylic

adjective
of or relating to dactyls in poetry
9.2
Pronunciation: / dækˈtɪlɪk / (AmE), / dækˈtɪlɪk / (BrE)
(of verse or meter) consisting of or characterized by dactyls, a metrical foot of one stressed syllable followed by two unstressed syllables.

Examples

Dactylic hexameter
Ancient epic poetry often employs dactylic hexameter as its structure.
Students struggled to write their own dactylic lines for the assignment.
The poet chose a dactylic meter to give the poem a rhythmic, galloping feel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anapestic - verse form with anapests, opposite metrical pattern
