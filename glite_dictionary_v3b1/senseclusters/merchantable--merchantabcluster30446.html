merchantable

adjective
suitable for sale
Pronunciation: / ˈmɝː.tʃən.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈmɜː.tʃən.tə.bəl / (BrE)
fit for sale; of a quality suitable to be sold, especially in commercial or legal contexts.

Examples

A merchantable product
In order to be merchantable, food products must meet certain quality standards.
Only merchantable timber is selected by the forest contractors for processing.
The court found that the shipment was not merchantable due to spoilage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marketable - fit to be sold; able to be sold or marketed
