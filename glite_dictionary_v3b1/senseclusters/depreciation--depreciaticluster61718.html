depreciation

noun
decrease in asset value over time
2.8
Pronunciation: / dɪˌpriʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌpriːsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
A reduction in the value of an asset over time, especially due to wear and tear, age, or obsolescence.

Examples

Annual depreciation expense
He calculated the annual depreciation of his company’s vehicles for tax purposes.
Over the years, the house showed little sign of depreciation despite frequent use.
The rapid depreciation of computer equipment means businesses must upgrade frequently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appreciation - an increase in the value of an asset
