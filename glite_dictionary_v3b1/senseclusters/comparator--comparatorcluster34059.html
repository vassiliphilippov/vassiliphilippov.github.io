comparator

noun
device for comparing
6.8
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpæɹəˌtɔɹ / (AmE), / kəmˈpærətə / (BrE)
a device or tool (including electronic circuits) used to compare two things, especially to identify differences in size, quality, or value

Examples

Precision comparator
The technician used a comparator to ensure the two metal rods were exactly the same length.
A digital comparator can quickly highlight even the smallest differences between samples.
In the laboratory, the comparator helped the scientists check the purity of the solutions.
The engineer used a comparator to monitor voltage differences in the circuit.
A comparator outputs a signal when the input exceeds a certain threshold.
Digital systems often rely on a comparator to make quick decisions based on input values.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplifier - an electronic device that increases signal strength
  • synonym: detector - a device that identifies the presence of something
  • synonym: gauge - an instrument for measuring or testing
  • synonym: measuring instrument - a device used to determine the size, amount, or degree of something
