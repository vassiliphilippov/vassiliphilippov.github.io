adjust

verb
arrange to be straight or parallel
2.7 35/m
Pronunciation: / É™ËˆdÊ’ÊŒst / (AmE), / É™ËˆdÊ’ÊŒst / (BrE)
to change, arrange, or regulate something so that it is correct, suitable, or in the right position

Examples

To adjust the settings
He stopped to adjust the chairs so that they were perfectly in line.
Please adjust the blinds to make them parallel with the window frame.
She carefully adjusted the pictures on the wall until they all lined up evenly.
Could you adjust the volume so everyone can hear clearly?
She adjusted her chair to fit under the desk.
The technician needs to adjust the settings to ensure accurate measurements.

Synonyms

  • modify
    3.2 26/m
    modify means to make changes to improve or suit something, while adjust usually means set or regulate it into the correct position.
  • fine-tune
    4.2 2.82/m
    fine-tune means to make small, precise improvements for best performance, while adjust covers larger or routine setting and regulation.
  • tweak
    3.8 5.96/m
    tweak is to make small, often informal changes to improve something, while adjust covers general setting or regulation to fit.
  • tune
    3.2 9.15/m
    tune means adjust a machine or system to work better, while adjust is a broader verb for setting or regulating many things.
  • alter
    2.5 41/m
    alter means change something so it becomes different, sometimes significantly, while adjust stresses setting or making suitable small changes.
  • align
    3.5 9.37/m
    align means arrange items in a straight line or correct relationship, while adjust can mean changing many aspects to make them suitable.
  • correct
    1.0 30/m
    correct means make something right or free from error, while adjust can mean change or set something to be suitable, not only fix mistakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: align - to place or arrange things in a straight line
  • synonym: calibrate - to adjust or mark a measuring device for accuracy
  • antonym: misadjust - to adjust incorrectly
  • synonym: modify - to change or alter something, usually slightly
