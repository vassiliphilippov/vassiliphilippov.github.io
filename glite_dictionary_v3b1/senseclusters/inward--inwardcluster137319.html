inward

adverb
toward the center or interior
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnwɚd /; / ˈɪnwərd / (AmE), / ˈɪnwəd / (BrE)
toward or into the inside or center of something

Examples

Move inward quickly
She drew the curtains inward to let in more light.
The crowd gradually moved inward toward the stage.
When you breathe deeply, your chest moves inward and outward.
As I walked inward, the noise from the street faded away.
She stepped inward to escape the cold wind outside.
The door opened inward, revealing a brightly lit hallway.
