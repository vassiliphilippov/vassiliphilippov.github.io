scoria

noun
porous volcanic rock
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈskɔːriə / (AmE), / ˈskɔːriə / (BrE)
a rough, dark, porous volcanic rock formed from cooled lava with many holes caused by escaping gases

Examples

Volcanic scoria rock
The hillside was covered with black scoria from the ancient eruption.
Builders sometimes use scoria as a lightweight material in construction projects.
After the volcano erupted, a thick layer of scoria blanketed the surrounding fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basalt - a dense, dark volcanic rock formed from cooled lava
  • synonym: pumice - a light, porous volcanic rock formed from rapidly cooling lava
