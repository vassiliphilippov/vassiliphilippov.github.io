bluff

verb
pretend to be stronger to deceive or intimidate
5.8 4.5
Pronunciation: /blʌf/ (AmE), /blʌf/ (BrE)
to try to deceive someone by pretending to have a stronger position or more confidence than you actually have, especially to influence or frighten them

Examples

To bluff your way
He tried to bluff his way through the interview by pretending he had more experience than he did.
She knew he was only bluffing about having powerful connections, but she stayed silent.
They managed to bluff their opponents into folding, even with a weak hand.
He decided to bluff in poker, betting high despite having a weak hand.
She tried to bluff her way out of trouble by pretending she hadn’t seen the message.
Sometimes you need to bluff your opponents to win the game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to cause someone to believe something untrue, often for personal advantage
  • synonym: intimidate - to deliberately make someone feel afraid, especially to influence their behavior
  • antonym: reveal - to make known something that was hidden or unknown, opposite of concealing or bluffing
