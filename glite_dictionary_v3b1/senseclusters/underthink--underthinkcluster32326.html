underthink

verb
to analyze too simply
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈθɪŋk / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈθɪŋk / (BrE)
to not think enough about something or to consider it too simply, missing important details

Examples

To underthink a problem
If you underthink the problem, you might miss important factors that affect the outcome.
She tends to underthink decisions, often choosing the first solution that comes to mind.
It's risky to underthink safety procedures when working with heavy machinery.
If you underthink the instructions, you might make a simple mistake.
She tends to underthink her decisions and act too quickly.
It is risky to underthink important safety procedures at work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neglect - to fail to give proper attention to something
  • antonym: overthink - to think about something too much or for too long
  • synonym: simplify - to make something easier or less complex
