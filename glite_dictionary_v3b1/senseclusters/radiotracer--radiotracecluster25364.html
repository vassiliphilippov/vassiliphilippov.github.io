radiotracer

noun
radioactive imaging agent
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.di.oʊ.treɪ.sər/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.di.əʊ.treɪ.sə/ (BrE)
a radioactive substance used to track or visualize processes inside the body or in scientific studies

Examples

A radiotracer injection
The radiotracer allowed doctors to see how blood moved through the patient's heart.
A small amount of radiotracer was injected before the PET scan began.
Researchers used a radiotracer to study the function of the kidneys in real time.
Scientists injected a radiotracer into the groundwater to monitor how contaminants spread over time.
The team used a radiotracer to follow the flow of oil through the pipeline during the experiment.
By adding a radiotracer to the soil, researchers could observe how nutrients moved through the ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tracer - a substance used to follow the path of a process
