prepositional phrase

noun
group of words starting with a preposition
9.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /pre.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ən freɪz/ (AmE), /pre.pə.ˈzɪʃ.ən freɪz/ (BrE)
A group of words starting with a preposition and usually ending with a noun or pronoun, functioning as a unit in a sentence.

Examples

A prepositional phrase example
A prepositional phrase can show where or when something happens in a sentence.
In the sentence 'She sat on the chair,' the words 'on the chair' are a prepositional phrase.
Teachers often ask students to identify the prepositional phrase in a paragraph.
