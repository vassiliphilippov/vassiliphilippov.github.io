sternly

adverb
in a severe or strict manner
3.9 2.34/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÉnli / (AmE), / ËˆstÉœËnli / (BrE)
In a severe, strict, or serious manner; showing sternness.

Examples

Look sternly
He looked at the students sternly and demanded absolute silence.
She sternly reminded her children to clean their rooms before going outside.
The judge spoke sternly to the defendant, warning against further misbehavior.

Synonyms

  • grimly
    5.5 4.78/m
    grimly is stern and forbidding in mood, while sternly is strict and serious in tone.
  • with asperity
    9.9 0.02/m
    with asperity sounds formal and sharp in tone, while sternly is plain, strict, and used in everyday speech.
  • harshly
    3.5 4.21/m
    harshly feels rough and unkind in action, while sternly is strict but not necessarily cruel.
  • sharply
    2.8 5.94/m
    sharply shows quick, strong criticism or change, while sternly expresses steady strictness and serious control.
  • admonishingly
    7.6 0.02/m
    admonishingly sounds like a warning or scold to correct someone, while sternly shows firm, serious discipline.
