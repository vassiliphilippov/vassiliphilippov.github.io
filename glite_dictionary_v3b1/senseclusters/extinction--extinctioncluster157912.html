extinction

noun
the act of extinguishing a fire or light
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɪŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of putting out or causing something (like a fire, light, or activity) to stop or become inactive

Examples

Extinction of the fire
Following the ceremony, the extinction of the candles marked the end of the event.
The firefighter was praised for his quick extinction of the kitchen blaze.
The sudden extinction of all the lights left the audience in darkness.
After years without eruptions, scientists declared the extinction of the volcano.
Geologists study the extinction of geysers to understand changes in geothermal activity.
The sudden extinction of the fire surprised the adventurers in the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extinguishment - the act of making something no longer active; putting out
