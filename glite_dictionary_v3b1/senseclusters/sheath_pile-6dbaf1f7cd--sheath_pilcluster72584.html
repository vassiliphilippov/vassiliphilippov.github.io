sheath pile

noun
pile used to retain earth or block seepage
2.5 8.0
Pronunciation: /ʃiːθ paɪl/ (AmE), /ʃiːθ paɪl/ (BrE)
a pile installed in a row, side by side with others, to retain earth or prevent the passage of water

Examples

Steel sheath piles
The construction project specified the use of steel sheath piles to reinforce the retaining wall.
The engineers drove a row of sheath piles along the riverbank to prevent soil erosion.
Water seepage was controlled by installing several sheath piles side by side.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sheet pile - a pile with a broad, thin cross-section used for retaining earth or water
