parallelization

noun
connecting in parallel
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˌlɛləˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpærəˌlɛlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of connecting electrical components so that they share the same voltage by linking them in parallel

Examples

Parallelization of circuits
The parallelization of the circuits allowed the devices to operate at the same voltage.
Engineers often use parallelization to increase the total current available in a system.
In the experiment, students learned how parallelization affects the performance of electrical networks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: series connection - connecting components end-to-end so current flows through each in turn
