canso

noun
medieval lyric poem
Pronunciation: / kɑnˈsoʊ / (AmE), / kænˈsəʊ / (BrE)
a type of lyric poem composed by troubadours in medieval Occitan literature, often about love or chivalry

Examples

A medieval canso
The troubadour performed a beautiful canso for the royal court.
Scholars study the structure of the canso to understand medieval poetry.
Her favorite canso tells the story of a knight's unrequited love.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ballad - a narrative poem or song, often with a repeated refrain
  • synonym: sonnet - a poem of 14 lines with a specific rhyme scheme
