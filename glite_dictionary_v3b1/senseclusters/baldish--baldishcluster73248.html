baldish

adjective
somewhat bare
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔldɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːldɪʃ / (BrE)
somewhat bare or lacking covering, especially referring to land, surfaces, or areas with little vegetation or hair.

Examples

Baldish ground
After the drought, the field looked baldish with only a few patches of grass remaining.
The old rug was baldish in the center where people walked the most.
In early spring, the garden appears baldish before the new plants start to grow.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bald - lacking hair or vegetation; completely bare
  • synonym: bare - not covered by anything; exposed
  • antonym: lush - having a lot of vegetation; rich and green
