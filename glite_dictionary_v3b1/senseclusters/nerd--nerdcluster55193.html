nerd

noun
person deeply interested in academic or technical subjects
Pronunciation: / nɝd / (AmE), / nɜːd / (BrE)
a person who is very interested in a particular subject (especially technical or academic), and is often seen as socially awkward or overly studious

Examples

A nerd in science
Despite being labeled a nerd, she proudly embraced her love of physics and computer science.
He used to get teased for being a nerd, but now his expertise makes him valuable at work.
These days, being a nerd is often considered cool, especially in the technology industry.
Being a nerd is no longer seen as a bad thing in many circles.
He was called a nerd in high school because he loved science and rarely went to parties.
Some people embrace their nerd identity and are proud of their interests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool - someone admired or considered socially popular
  • synonym: dork - a socially awkward or inept person
  • synonym: geek - a person who is very interested in and knowledgeable about a particular activity or subject
  • antonym: jock - a person who is very involved in sports and athletics
