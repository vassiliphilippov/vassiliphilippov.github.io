pecky

adjective
full of small holes
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛki / (AmE), / ˈpɛki / (BrE)
having small holes or spots, especially in wood, often caused by insects or fungi

Examples

Pecky pine wood
The old barn was built with pecky pine boards that gave it a unique texture.
Furniture made from pecky cypress is popular for its rustic appearance.
The craftsman selected pecky wood to add character to the new table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: holey - having many holes
  • antonym: smooth - having an even surface without holes or roughness
