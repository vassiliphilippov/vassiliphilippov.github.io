timeshare

verb
to share property use
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtaɪm.ʃer/ (AmE), /ˈtaɪm.ʃeə/ (BrE)
to share the ownership or use of a property so that each person has the right to use it for a specific period each year

Examples

To timeshare a house
They decided to timeshare a mountain cabin with friends to make vacations more affordable.
Many families timeshare holiday apartments in popular tourist destinations.
We plan to timeshare the villa so everyone gets a week each summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: co-own - to own something jointly with others
  • antonym: rent - to pay for the use of property that belongs to someone else
