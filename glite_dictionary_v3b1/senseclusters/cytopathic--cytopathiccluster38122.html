cytopathic

adjective
damaging to cells
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪtoʊˈpæθɪk / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˈpæθɪk / (BrE)
causing damage or changes to living cells, especially as a result of infection by a virus

Examples

Cytopathic effect
The researchers noted a strong cytopathic effect in the cells exposed to the virus.
Certain viral infections can have cytopathic consequences, leading to cell death.
Doctors observed cytopathic changes in the tissue samples under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benign - not causing harm
  • synonym: cytotoxic - toxic to living cells
