predetermination

noun
act of deciding something in advance
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.dɪˌtɝː.məˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriː.dɪˌtɜː.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of deciding or arranging something in advance, especially as if it is inevitable or destined to happen

Examples

Life predetermination
Scientific advances challenge the idea that life events are subject to predetermination.
Some religious doctrines teach predetermination of a person's destiny.
The theory of fate relies heavily on the concept of predetermination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: determinism - the philosophical doctrine that all events are determined by existing causes
  • antonym: free will - the power of acting without the constraint of necessity or fate; the ability to act at one's own discretion
