bubble

verb
form or emit bubbles
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌb.əl /; / ˈbʌbəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl / (BrE)
to form, produce, or release bubbles in a liquid, or to cause bubbles to appear

Examples

To bubble in water
As the chemicals reacted, the solution started to bubble vigorously.
She watched the mud bubble at the bottom of the hot spring.
The pot of water began to bubble as it heated up on the stove.
Please bubble air into the aquarium to keep the fish healthy.
The recipe says to bubble carbon dioxide through the beverage for proper carbonation.
The scientist carefully bubbled nitrogen through the solution during the experiment.
Excitement seemed to bubble just beneath the surface of the crowd.
Lava can bubble up through cracks in the earth’s crust.
The water began to bubble as it reached boiling point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boil - to reach a temperature at which bubbles form and rise to the surface
  • synonym: fizz - make bubbling or hissing sounds and form bubbles
  • synonym: foam - to produce a mass of small bubbles on the surface
