phenylketonuria

noun
genetic disorder affecting phenylalanine metabolism

A rare inherited disorder resulting from the body's inability to break down the amino acid phenylalanine, leading to its accumulation and potentially causing intellectual disability and other health issues if left untreated.

Examples

Phenylketonuria disorder
Early diagnosis and a strict diet can help children with phenylketonuria lead healthy lives.
Newborns in many countries are routinely screened for phenylketonuria to prevent serious developmental problems.
The genetic mutation responsible for phenylketonuria affects the enzyme needed to process phenylalanine.
