retract

verb
hold open wound edges (medical)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrækt /; / rɪˈtrakt / (BrE)
to pull back or draw in something (physically or metaphorically), so it is no longer extended or is away from something

Examples

To retract claws
Medical students learn how to retract organs during complex procedures.
The assistant carefully retracted the tissue to give the doctor a clear view.
The surgeon used a tool to retract the skin around the incision site.
He watched as the tiny robot arm slowly retracted into its compartment.
Modern airplanes retract their wheels after takeoff for better aerodynamics.
The cat instinctively retracted its claws when it was relaxed.
He retracted in fear when the snake lunged at him.
She instinctively retracted her hand from the hot stove.
The cat retracted its claws when it saw the child approach.
