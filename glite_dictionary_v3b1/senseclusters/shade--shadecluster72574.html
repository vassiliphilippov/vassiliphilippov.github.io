shade

verb
insult indirectly, 'throw shade'
8.2
Pronunciation: /ʃeɪd/ (AmE), /ʃeɪd/ (BrE)
informally, to insult, criticize, or show contempt for someone indirectly or subtly, often by making sly or cutting remarks (see also 'throw shade').

Examples

To shade someone online
In online clashes, some players shade rivals with subtle, cutting remarks.
During the roast, he shaded his opponent with a sarcastic comment that made everyone laugh.
Commentators love shading celebrities subtly to create dramatic headlines and attract clicks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insult - to say or do something rude that shows a lack of respect for someone
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration; opposite of insulting
