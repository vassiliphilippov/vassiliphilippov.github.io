suspicion

noun
doubt about someone's honesty or actions
1.0
Pronunciation: / səˈspɪʃən / (AmE), / səˈspɪʃən / (BrE)
A feeling or belief that someone may be guilty of dishonesty or wrongdoing, without definite proof.

Examples

Police suspicion
Despite her alibi, the police still had suspicion about her involvement.
His constant late arrivals raised suspicion among his coworkers.
There was a widespread suspicion that the votes had been tampered with.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distrust - lack of trust or confidence in someone or something
  • antonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability or truth of someone
