homotypic

adjective
same form or type
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊməˈtɪpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməˈtɪpɪk / (BrE)
having the same or similar form, structure, or type, especially in biological contexts

Examples

Homotypic cell lines
The two cell lines are homotypic because they share identical structural characteristics.
Researchers observed homotypic interactions between the proteins in the experiment.
In taxonomy, homotypic species are grouped based on their similar physical forms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterotypic - having a different form or type
  • synonym: homologous - having the same relation, relative position, or structure
