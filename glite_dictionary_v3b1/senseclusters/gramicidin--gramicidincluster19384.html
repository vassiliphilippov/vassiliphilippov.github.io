gramicidin

noun
antibiotic used against Gram-positive bacteria
Pronunciation: / ɡræməˈsaɪdɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡræmɪˈsɪdɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic produced by a soil bacterium, used as an antiseptic for treating local infections caused by Gram-positive bacteria

Examples

Gramicidin antibiotic
Researchers are studying how gramicidin works against various Gram-positive bacteria.
The doctor prescribed gramicidin ointment to treat the patient's skin infection.
You should apply the gramicidin cream only to the affected area as directed.
