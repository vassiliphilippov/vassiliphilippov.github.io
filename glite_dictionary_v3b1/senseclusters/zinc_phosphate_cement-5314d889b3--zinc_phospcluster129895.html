zinc phosphate cement

noun
dental cement for restorations
Pronunciation: / zɪŋk ˈfəʊsfeɪt ˈsɛmɛnt / (AmE), / zɪŋk ˈfəʊsfeɪt ˈsɛmɛnt / (BrE)
A type of dental cement, made from zinc oxide and phosphoric acid, used to lute permanent metal restorations and as a base for dental restorations.

Examples

Zinc phosphate cement for crowns
Zinc phosphate cement is often used to secure crowns and bridges during dental procedures.
After mixing the powder and liquid, the zinc phosphate cement was quickly applied to the dental restoration.
The dentist preferred using zinc phosphate cement because of its proven reliability and strength.
