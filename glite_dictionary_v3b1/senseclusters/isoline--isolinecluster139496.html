isoline

noun
map line of equal value

a line on a map that connects points of equal value, such as temperature, elevation, or pressure

Examples

An isoline on map
The geographer drew an isoline to show areas of equal rainfall on the map.
On the topographic map, each isoline represents a specific elevation above sea level.
Meteorologists use an isoline to indicate regions with the same air pressure.

Synonyms

  • isopleth
    9.9 0.02/m
    isopleth is the common technical term used on weather and climate maps, while isoline is a general name for any equal-value map line.
  • isometric line
    7.2 0.00/m
    isometric line is a literal phrase for lines showing equal measurements on diagrams, while isoline is the standard single-word map term.
  • isarithm
    9.9 0.00/m
    isarithm is a technical or older mapping word for equal-value contours, while isoline is the modern general label on many map types.
  • isogram
    9.9 0.00/m
    isogram names a line of equal value but also means a word with no repeated letters, while isoline only means map contours.
