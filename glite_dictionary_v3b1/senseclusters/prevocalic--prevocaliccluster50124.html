prevocalic

adjective
before a vowel
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːvoʊˈkælɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriːvəʊˈkælɪk / (BrE)
occurring immediately before a vowel sound in a word

Examples

Prevocalic position
The letter 'r' is prevocalic in the word 'red'.
In some accents, the pronunciation of 'l' changes when it is prevocalic.
Teachers often highlight prevocalic sounds when teaching reading skills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postvocalic - occurring immediately after a vowel sound
