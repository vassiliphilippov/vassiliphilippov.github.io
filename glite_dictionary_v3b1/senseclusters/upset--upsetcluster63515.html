upset

noun
an act of overturning or disturbance
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈsɛt / (AmE), / ʌpˈsɛt / (BrE)
an act of overturning, disturbance, or disorder

Examples

A sudden upset
An engine failure caused the plane's upset shortly after takeoff.
Her careful plans suffered an upset when her assistant called in sick.
The sudden upset of the canoe tossed everyone into the river.
Her illness was triggered by an upset of her digestive system.
The merger caused an unexpected upset in the company's operations.
There was an upset in the community after the sudden change in leadership.
