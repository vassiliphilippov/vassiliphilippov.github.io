fibroplasia

noun
formation of fibrous tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbroʊˈpleɪziə / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrəʊˈpleɪzɪə / (BrE)
the formation of fibrous tissue, especially during the healing process after injury

Examples

Wound fibroplasia
During wound healing, fibroplasia helps rebuild the damaged tissue.
The biopsy showed evidence of early fibroplasia in the affected area.
Doctors monitor fibroplasia to ensure proper recovery after surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fibrosis - the thickening and scarring of connective tissue
  • degree lower: inflammation - the body's response to injury or infection, often causing redness and swelling
