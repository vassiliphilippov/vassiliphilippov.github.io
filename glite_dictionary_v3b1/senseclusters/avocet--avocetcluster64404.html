avocet

noun
a black-and-white wading bird with an upturned bill
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈævəsɛt / (AmE), / ˈævəsɛt / (BrE)
a long-legged wading bird with black-and-white plumage and a slender, upward-curving bill, found in shallow waters

Examples

A avocet bird
Large flocks of avocet can be seen feeding in shallow coastal lagoons.
The avocet is easily recognized by its striking black-and-white colors and distinctive upturned bill.
Wildlife photographers often search wetlands in hopes of spotting an avocet.
