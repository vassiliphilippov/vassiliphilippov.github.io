disruptor

noun
agent of major change
6.3
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrʌptər / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes major change or innovation in an industry, field, or system

Examples

Industry disruptor
The new technology company became a disruptor in the financial sector by offering free online services.
As a disruptor, she challenged the traditional ways of doing business in her industry.
Electric cars are seen as a major disruptor to the automobile market.
Many startups aim to be disruptors by introducing new technologies to the market.
The arrival of online streaming services acted as a major disruptor in the film industry.
Electric vehicles are seen as disruptors in the traditional automotive market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: innovator - a person who introduces new ideas or methods
  • synonym: pioneer - a person who is among the first to develop or use a new method or activity
  • antonym: traditionalist - a person who supports established customs and resists change
