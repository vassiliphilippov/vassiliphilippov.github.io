smarts

verb
to feel pain or hurt
6.1
Pronunciation: /smɑːrts/ (AmE), /smɑːts/ (BrE)
to feel a sharp pain or to feel hurt or upset, especially because of criticism or defeat

Examples

Her knee smarts
Her knee still smarts from when she fell on the ice yesterday.
He smarts at the harsh words his friend said during their argument.
The team smarts after losing the championship game by just one point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soothe - to make pain or discomfort less severe
