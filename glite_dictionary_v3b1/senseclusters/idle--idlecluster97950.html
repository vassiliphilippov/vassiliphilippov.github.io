idle

adjective
silly or trivial (of talk or activity)
5.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.dəl/ (BrE)
silly, trivial, pointless, or unfounded; having no real value or basis (of talk, thoughts, fears, etc.)

Examples

Idle talk
He was annoyed by the idle speculation about his personal life.
She dismissed the rumors as idle gossip that should not be taken seriously.
Their conversation was little more than idle small talk to pass the time.
Don't waste your time on idle chatter when there's work to be done.
Her idle remarks caused unnecessary confusion in the meeting.
We dismissed his idle gossip as nothing more than baseless rumors.
His concerns were just idle fears with no real evidence.
Reporters dismissed the story as idle gossip.
We must not make idle threats that we have no intention of acting on.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseless - without basis in fact or reason
  • antonym: meaningful - having a serious, useful, or important purpose
  • synonym: reckless - without care for consequences; showing lack of responsibility
  • synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
  • synonym: unfounded - having no supporting evidence or basis
