regrowth

noun
new growth after loss
Pronunciation: / ˈriˌɡroʊθ / (AmE), / ˈriːɡrəʊθ / (BrE)
the process or result of growing again after being cut, lost, or damaged (such as plants, hair, or tissue)

Examples

Healthy regrowth of tissue
The doctor was pleased to see healthy regrowth of tissue after the surgery.
After the wildfire, the forest showed signs of regrowth within a few months.
Many people use special shampoos to encourage the regrowth of hair.
After the wildfire, the regrowth of trees brought hope to the community.
Hair regrowth can take several weeks after chemotherapy treatment ends.
The gardener was pleased to see rapid regrowth in the damaged flower beds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decay - the process of rotting or breaking down
  • synonym: growth - the process of increasing in size or developing
  • synonym: regeneration - the process of regrowing or renewing something lost or damaged
