fixed cost

noun
business expense not affected by output
Pronunciation: / fɪkst kɔst / (AmE), / fɪkst kɒst / (BrE)
a regular business expense that does not change with the amount of goods or services produced, such as rent or insurance

Examples

A fixed cost expense
Even if production stops, the company must still pay its fixed cost obligations.
Insurance premiums are a typical example of a fixed cost in most businesses.
Rent is considered a fixed cost because it remains the same regardless of output.

Related Terms

  • antonym: variable cost - a business expense that varies with production volume
