prefix notation

noun
operator-first mathematical notation
7.6 8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌfɪks noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpriːfɪks nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a way of writing mathematical expressions in which each operator comes before its operands, without the use of parentheses

Examples

Prefix notation in math
In computer science, prefix notation is also known as Polish notation.
Mathematicians sometimes use prefix notation to avoid parentheses in complex formulas.
The calculator was set to operate using prefix notation rather than the standard infix method.
