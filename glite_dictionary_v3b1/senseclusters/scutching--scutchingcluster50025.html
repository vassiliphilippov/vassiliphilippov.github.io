scutching

noun
fiber cleaning process
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈskʌtʃ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈskʌtʃ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of cleaning and separating plant fibers (such as flax or hemp) by removing unwanted parts before spinning

Examples

The scutching process
The scutching of flax is an important step before the fibers can be spun into linen.
Modern factories use machines for scutching to improve the quality of cotton fibers.
After scutching, the plant fibers are much cleaner and ready for further processing.
After harvesting flax, scutching is performed to remove the woody stems from the fibers.
The quality of linen depends greatly on careful scutching during processing.
Modern machines have replaced manual scutching in most textile factories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carding - a process that disentangles and cleans fibers before spinning
  • synonym: retting - the process of soaking plant stalks to loosen fibers
  • degree higher: spinning - the process of twisting fibers into yarn or thread
