descriptive geometry

noun
geometry of projection and representation
1.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskrɪptɪv dʒiˈɒmətri / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪv dʒiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
the branch of geometry concerned with the graphical representation of three-dimensional objects and properties that remain unchanged under projection

Examples

Descriptive geometry study
Students in engineering often study descriptive geometry to learn how to visualize three-dimensional structures.
The principles of descriptive geometry are used in technical drawing and architectural design.
Understanding descriptive geometry is essential for creating accurate projections of solid objects.
