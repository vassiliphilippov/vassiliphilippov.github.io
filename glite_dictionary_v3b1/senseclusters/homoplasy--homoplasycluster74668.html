homoplasy

noun
independent similarity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊməˌpleɪsi / (AmE), / ˈhɒməˌpləsi / (BrE)
the independent development of similar features or traits in different languages, cultures, or artifacts not due to shared origin

Examples

Linguistic homoplasy
The similar grammatical structures in unrelated languages are an example of homoplasy.
Researchers debated whether the artifact's design was due to homoplasy or cultural exchange.
In evolutionary linguistics, homoplasy complicates the tracing of language families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convergence - the process of becoming similar or coming together
  • antonym: homology - similarity due to shared ancestry
