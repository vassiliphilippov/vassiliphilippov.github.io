overtone

noun
a harmonic frequency above the fundamental
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌtəʊn / (BrE)
a musical tone that is a higher frequency, usually an exact multiple of the fundamental frequency, and that sounds above the basic note

Examples

Musical overtone
By adjusting her breath, the singer was able to isolate a clear overtone above the main note.
Different shapes of musical instruments affect which overtones are more prominent in their sound.
The violin string vibrates to produce both a fundamental and an overtone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harmonic - a component frequency of a sound, an integer multiple of the fundamental
