orphan

verb
deprive of parents by death
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.fən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən / (BrE)
to deprive (a child) of one or both parents through death

Examples

To orphan a child
A sudden accident orphaned her at a young age.
Disease outbreaks continue to orphan families in some regions.
The war threatened to orphan thousands of children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: widow - a woman whose spouse has died
