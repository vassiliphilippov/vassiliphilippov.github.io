handbrake turn

noun
driving maneuver using the handbrake to turn sharply
1.4 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌbreɪk tɝn / (AmE), / ˈhændˌbreɪk tɜːn / (BrE)
A driving maneuver in which the driver pulls the handbrake to make the vehicle pivot sharply, typically causing the rear wheels to slide and the car to turn quickly.

Examples

To do a handbrake turn in a car
He skillfully executed a handbrake turn to avoid the obstacle in the road.
Learning how to do a handbrake turn is a common part of advanced driving courses.
The movie stunt required a precise handbrake turn at high speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drift - A driving maneuver where the car slides sideways, often intentionally
