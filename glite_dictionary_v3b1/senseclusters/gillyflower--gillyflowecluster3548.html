gillyflower

noun
fragrant flowering plant, often a stock or carnation
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪliˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlɪˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a fragrant, colorful Old World plant (such as Matthiola or Dianthus) grown for its flowers

Examples

A gillyflower plant
In old poems, the gillyflower is often praised for its sweet scent.
She picked a gillyflower from the garden and placed it in her hair.
The ancient walled gardens were full of bright gillyflowers in spring.
He admired the vibrant gillyflower blooms in various shades of pink and red.
She planted a row of gillyflowers along the garden path for their color and scent.
The scent of gillyflowers filled the warm summer air by the porch.
