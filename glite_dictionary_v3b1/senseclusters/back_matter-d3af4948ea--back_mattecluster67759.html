back matter

noun
material after main text of a book
Pronunciation: / bæk ˈmætər / (AmE), / bæk ˈmætə / (BrE)
the sections of a book that appear after the main text, such as appendices, indexes, or bibliographies

Examples

The back matter pages
Authors often use the back matter to provide additional information or resources for readers.
Please check the back matter for a list of references and appendices.
The back matter of the book includes a glossary and an index.

Related Terms

  • antonym: front matter - the sections of a book that appear before the main text, such as a preface or introduction
