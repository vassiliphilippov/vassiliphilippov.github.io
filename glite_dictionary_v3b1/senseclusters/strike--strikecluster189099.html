strike

verb
to have a sudden emotional or mental impact
1.8
Pronunciation: / straɪk / (AmE), / straɪk / (BrE)
to suddenly affect, impress, or come into someone's mind (emotion, idea, feeling, disaster, illness, etc.)

Examples

An idea strikes me
His sudden kindness struck me as genuine, not forced.
It struck her that she had forgotten his birthday.
The criticism from her boss struck him deeply, making him rethink his approach.
A sense of déjà vu struck her as she walked through the old neighborhood.
It struck me that she hadn't called in days, and I began to worry.
The possibility of failure suddenly struck him as he prepared for the exam.
Malaria strikes thousands of people in this region every year.
Panic struck the crowd as the fire alarm went off suddenly.
The village was struck by a severe flood last year, causing widespread damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afflict - to cause pain or suffering to
  • synonym: hit - to come suddenly into the mind; affect unexpectedly
  • synonym: impress - to have a strong effect on someone's feelings or thoughts
