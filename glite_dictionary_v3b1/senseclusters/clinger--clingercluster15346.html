clinger

noun
overly attached person
5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈklɪŋ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈklɪŋ.ə/ (BrE)
a person, animal, or thing that stays very close to someone or holds on tightly, often unwilling to let go

Examples

A clinger friend
Ever since they became friends, Mark has been a real clinger and never leaves Tom's side.
Some pets are natural clingers and want to be near their owners all the time.
She worried that being a clinger would push her new friends away.
The puppy was such a clinger that it followed its owner everywhere.
Some children are natural clingers and feel anxious when separated from their parents.
He became known as a clinger in relationships, always needing constant attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independent - not relying on or attached to others
  • synonym: shadow - a person who follows someone everywhere
