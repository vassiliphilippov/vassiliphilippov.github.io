maximize

verb
make as large or effective as possible
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmæk.sɪˌmaɪz /; / ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz /; / ˈmæk.səˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæk.sɪ.maɪz /; / ˈmaks.ɪ.maɪz /; / ˈmak.sɪ.maɪz / (BrE)
to make something as large, great, or effective as possible; to use something to the fullest extent

Examples

To maximize profits
In order to maximize profits, the company invested in new technology.
She always tries to maximize her time by planning each day carefully.
We need to maximize the use of available resources to succeed.
He invested carefully in order to maximize his returns.
Please adjust the settings to maximize the quality of your video.
The company implemented new strategies to maximize efficiency.
Farmers use technology to maximise crop yields during the growing season.
She worked overtime to maximise her earnings before the holidays.
The company introduced new strategies to maximise efficiency across all departments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maximize - to make something as great or large as possible
  • antonym: minimise - to make something as small or unimportant as possible
  • antonym: minimize - to make as small as possible
