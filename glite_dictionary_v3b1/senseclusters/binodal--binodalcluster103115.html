binodal

adjective
having two nodes
9.9
Pronunciation: /baɪ.ˈnoʊ.dəl/ /; / /bɪ.ˈnoʊ.dəl/ (AmE), /baɪ.ˈnəʊ.dəl/ /; / /bɪ.ˈnəʊ.dəl/ (BrE)
having or involving two nodes, points, or peaks; especially used for curves or diagrams in mathematics, statistics, or science (such as a curve marking where two phases coexist)

Examples

A binodal curve
The scientist observed a binodal curve in the experimental data.
A binodal distribution often indicates two dominant groups within the population.
The chart clearly showed a binodal pattern with two separate peaks.
The binodal curve separates the single-phase and two-phase regions in the diagram.
Scientists use the binodal line to determine the conditions for phase separation in mixtures.
Understanding the binodal region is essential for predicting material behavior under different temperatures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bimodal - having two modes or peaks
  • synonym: critical - relating to the point at which a phase transition occurs
  • antonym: mononodal - having a single node or point of transition
  • antonym: unimodal - having a single mode or peak
