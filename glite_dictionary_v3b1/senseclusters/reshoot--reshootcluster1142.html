reshoot

noun
additional filming session
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈʃuːt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃuːt / (BrE)
an additional filming session done after the main shooting of a movie or television show to improve or change scenes

Examples

A movie reshoot
After reviewing the footage, the producers decided a reshoot was necessary for the opening scene.
The actors returned to the set for a quick reshoot of the dialogue sequence.
Budget constraints made the reshoot challenging, but the final result was worth it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retake - an additional attempt to film a scene
  • degree lower: shoot - the act of filming scenes for a movie or show
