direction

noun
the way in which something moves or points
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkʃən /; / dəˈrɛkʃən, daɪˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkʃən /; / dəˈrɛkʃən, daɪˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
the way in which something moves, points, or faces; the course or line along which something moves

Examples

Right direction
After getting lost in the woods, they finally found the right direction to reach the road.
She pointed in the direction of the mountains, saying that was where the village lay.
The compass needle always points in the same direction, no matter where you are.
He used a compass to check the direction of the hiking trail.
Snow drifted in every direction during the storm.
The river flows in a southerly direction toward the sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: course - the path or direction followed by something or someone
  • antonym: destination - the place to which someone is going or something is sent
  • synonym: orientation - the relative physical position or alignment of something
  • synonym: position - the location or area where something is situated, which can relate to direction
  • synonym: route - a way or course taken to get from a starting point to a destination (physical path)
  • synonym: way - a direction or route taken to reach a place
