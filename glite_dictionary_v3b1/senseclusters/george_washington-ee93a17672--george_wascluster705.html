George Washington

proper noun
first President of the United States, 1732–1799
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔrdʒ ˈwɑʃɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːdʒ ˈwɒʃɪŋtən / (BrE)
(1732–1799) The first President of the United States (1789–1797), commander-in-chief of the Continental Army during the American Revolution, and a Founding Father.

Examples

George Washington first president
George Washington led the Continental Army to victory during the American Revolution.
In 1789, George Washington was unanimously elected as the nation's first president.
Many cities and monuments in the United States are named after George Washington.
