directorially

adverb
in a director's manner
Pronunciation: / dəˌrɛkˈtɔːriəli / (AmE), / dɪˌrɛkˈtɔːriəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the actions, decisions, or responsibilities of a director, especially in management or administration

Examples

Directorially managed company
The company was reorganized directorially after the new board members took charge.
He was praised for handling the crisis directorially and making effective decisions.
Changes were implemented directorially to improve the organization's efficiency.
