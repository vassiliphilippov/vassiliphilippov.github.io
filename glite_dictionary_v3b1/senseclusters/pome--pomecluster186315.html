pome

noun
fleshy fruit like apple or pear
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊm / (AmE), / pəʊm / (BrE)
a fleshy fruit, such as an apple or pear, with a central seed chamber surrounded by a thick, edible outer layer

Examples

A pome fruit
Many gardeners can easily identify a pome by its thick, fleshy outer layer and central seed chamber.
The apple is a classic example of a pome found in temperate regions.
Unlike drupes, a pome contains its seeds in a core at the center of the fruit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apple - a common edible fruit; an example of a pome
  • antonym: drupe - a fruit with a single stone or pit, like a cherry or peach
