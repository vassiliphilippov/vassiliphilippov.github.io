delead

verb
to remove lead
Pronunciation: / diːˈlɛd / (AmE), / diːˈlɛd / (BrE)
to remove lead from something, such as paint, pipes, or fuel, especially for safety or environmental reasons

Examples

To delead old paint
The city hired a team to delead the old school building before students returned.
Homeowners must delead their properties to meet new health regulations.
Special equipment is required to safely delead water pipes in older houses.

  • antonym: contaminate - to make something impure or unsafe by adding a harmful substance
