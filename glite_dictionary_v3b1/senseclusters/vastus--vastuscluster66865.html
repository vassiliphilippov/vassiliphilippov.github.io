vastus

noun
thigh muscle
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈvæstəs / (AmE), / ˈvæstəs / (BrE)
a muscle in the front of the thigh that helps extend the leg, part of the quadriceps group

Examples

The vastus muscle
The physical therapist explained how the vastus helps stabilize the knee during movement.
After cycling for an hour, he felt soreness in his vastus muscles.
Injury to the vastus can make it difficult to straighten your leg fully.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hamstring - a group of muscles at the back of the thigh
  • synonym: quadriceps - the large muscle group at the front of the thigh
