challenge the stereotype

verb
question a commonly held belief about a group
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃælɪndʒ ðə ˈstɛriəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒ ðə ˈstɪəriətaɪp / (BrE)
to question or dispute a widely held and oversimplified belief about a particular group or type of person

Examples

To challenge the stereotype
By succeeding in a male-dominated industry, she challenged the stereotype of what women can achieve.
Many educators work hard to challenge the stereotype that boys are better at math than girls.
This film aims to challenge the stereotype of refugees by telling their personal stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reinforce the stereotype - to support or strengthen a widely held but oversimplified idea about a group
