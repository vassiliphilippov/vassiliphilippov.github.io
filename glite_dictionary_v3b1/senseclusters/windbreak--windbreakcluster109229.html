windbreak

noun
hedge or fence to block wind
3.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪnd.breɪk/ (AmE), /ˈwɪnd.breɪk/ (BrE)
A row of trees, bushes, or a fence that is planted or built to protect an area from the wind and reduce erosion.

Examples

A windbreak of trees
A windbreak can reduce soil erosion caused by strong winds.
The farm planted a windbreak of pines along the field to protect the crops.
We enjoyed our picnic behind the windbreak, sheltered from the chilly gusts.
