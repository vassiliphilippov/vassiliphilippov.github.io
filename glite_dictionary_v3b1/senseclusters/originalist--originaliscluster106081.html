originalist

noun
advocate of original meaning
8.6
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪdʒənəlɪst / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪnəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who interprets laws or constitutions according to their original meaning at the time they were written

Examples

A strict originalist
The Supreme Court justice is considered an originalist in her approach to constitutional law.
Many originalists argue that the Constitution should not be interpreted according to modern values.
Debates between originalists and other legal scholars often focus on how to apply old laws today.
The Supreme Court justice is widely regarded as an originalist in her approach to constitutional law.
Many debates about the constitution feature arguments between originalists and those with more modern interpretations.
As an originalist, he believes the law should be applied as it was understood when written.

Related Terms

  • antonym: living constitutionalist - a person who believes a constitution's meaning can evolve over time
  • synonym: textualist - a person who interprets legal texts strictly by their wording
