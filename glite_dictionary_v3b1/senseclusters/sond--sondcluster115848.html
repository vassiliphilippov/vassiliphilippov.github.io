sond

noun
test hole or bore
a small hole or bore made in soil, rock, or other material to examine or sample it, often for testing purposes

Examples

A soil sond
Before starting construction, engineers drilled a sond to check the soil quality.
The geologist examined the sond to determine the layers of rock beneath the surface.
A sond was made to collect samples for environmental testing at the site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: borehole - a deep, narrow hole made in the ground to obtain samples or information
  • synonym: core sample - a cylindrical section taken from soil or rock for analysis
