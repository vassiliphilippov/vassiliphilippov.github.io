annex

noun
an extension or addition to a building
9.9 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈæ.neks/ /; / /ə.ˈneks/ (AmE), /ˈæ.neks/ (BrE)
a building added on to a larger one or situated close to it and serving as an extension or addition

Examples

A school annex
The council built a new annex to accommodate the growing number of students.
The museum's annex houses a collection of contemporary art.
We met in the annex adjacent to the main conference room.
Guests are accommodated in an annexe separate from the main hotel.
The library’s annexe houses rare and archival materials not found in the main building.
The school built a new annexe to hold additional classrooms.
