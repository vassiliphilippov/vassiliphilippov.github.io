Mercian

adjective
of Mercia
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜrʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɜːʃən / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Anglo-Saxon kingdom of Mercia or its people, language, or culture

Examples

Mercian dialect
The Mercian dialect played a significant role in the development of Middle English.
Archaeologists discovered artifacts from a Mercian settlement dating back to the eighth century.
She studied the Mercian influence on early English literature for her thesis.
