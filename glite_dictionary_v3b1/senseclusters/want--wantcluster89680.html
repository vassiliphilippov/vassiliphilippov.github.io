want

verb
to lack; to be without
5.1
Pronunciation: / wɑnt / (AmE), / wɒnt / (BrE)
(formal) to lack or need something; to be without something necessary or desirable

Examples

The plan wants detail
If the plan wants clarity, it will confuse the team.
In times of war, entire communities may want basic necessities.
This soup wants more salt to bring out its flavor.
The garden wants careful attention to flourish in the spring.
This wall wants painting before the visitors arrive.
Your essay wants more detail to fully answer the question.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lack - to be without or deficient in
  • synonym: need - to require something because it is essential or very important
