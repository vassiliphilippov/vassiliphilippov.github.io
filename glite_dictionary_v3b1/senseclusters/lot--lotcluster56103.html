lot

noun
object chosen at random to decide something
2.8 5.9
Pronunciation: /lɑːt/ /; / /lɔːt/ (AmE), /lɒt/ (BrE)
an object (such as a straw or pebble) drawn or chosen at random to decide something, especially to determine a person's share or role

Examples

To cast lots
In ancient times, people often cast lots to settle disputes.
They drew a lot to see who would take the last ticket.
To decide who would speak first, each person picked a lot from the bowl.
