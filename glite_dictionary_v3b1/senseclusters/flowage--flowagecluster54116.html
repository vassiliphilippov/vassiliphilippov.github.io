flowage

noun
artificially created flooded area
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfləʊɪdʒ / (BrE)
an area of land covered by water as a result of deliberate flooding, especially for reservoirs or water management.

Examples

A flowage area
Residents were relocated when the valley was turned into a flowage for flood control.
Several rare bird species nest along the flowage each spring.
The park was established to protect the wetlands surrounding the flowage.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: pond - a small body of still water, often artificial
  • synonym: reservoir - a large natural or artificial lake used to store water
