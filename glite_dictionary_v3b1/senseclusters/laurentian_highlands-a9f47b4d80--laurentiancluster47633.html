Laurentian Highlands

proper noun
plateau region in eastern and central Canada
8.8
Pronunciation: / lɔːˈrɛnʃən ˈhaɪləndz / (AmE), / lɒˈrɛnʃən ˈhaɪləndz / (BrE)
a large plateau region of eastern and central Canada, extending from the Great Lakes northward to the Arctic Ocean and covering more than 40% of Canada's land area

Examples

The Laurentian Highlands region
Much of central Quebec lies within the Laurentian Highlands region.
The Laurentian Highlands are known for their rocky terrain and dense forests.
The geology of the Laurentian Highlands is among the oldest in the world.
