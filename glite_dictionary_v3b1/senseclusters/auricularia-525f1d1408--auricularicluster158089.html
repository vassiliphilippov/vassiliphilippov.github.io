Auricularia

noun
genus of ear-shaped fungi
Pronunciation: / ɔːˌrɪkjəˈlɛriə / (AmE), / ɔːˌrɪkjʊˈlɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of fungi in the family Auriculariaceae, known for species with gelatinous, ear-shaped fruiting bodies.

Examples

Auricularia fungi
Fungi of the genus Auricularia are easily recognized by their gelatinous, ear-shaped fruiting bodies.
Researchers discovered a new species in the Auricularia genus growing on decaying wood.
The jelly ear mushroom, scientifically named Auricularia auricula-judae, is used in many Asian dishes.
