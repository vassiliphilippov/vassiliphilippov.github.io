ring

noun
circular or toroidal shape or arrangement
6.7
Pronunciation: / rɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪŋ / (BrE)
a circular arrangement, shape, or platform (such as a ring of smoke, a boxing ring, or a group of atoms in chemistry)

Examples

Ring of smoke
A ring of smoke drifted slowly toward the ceiling.
The cottage was surrounded by a ring of ancient oaks.
The rescuers formed a ring around the collapsed building.
She watched her brother box from outside the ring.
The fighters stepped into the ring as the crowd cheered.
The referee stopped the fight and cleared everyone from the ring.
Many antibiotics contain a beta-lactam ring in their structure.
The benzene molecule is composed of a six-carbon ring.
The stability of the ring system makes these compounds very useful in industry.
