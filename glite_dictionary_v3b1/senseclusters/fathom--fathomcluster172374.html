fathom

verb
measure the depth of water
Pronunciation: / ˈfæðəm / (AmE), / ˈfaðəm / (BrE)
to measure the depth of (water) using a sounding line

Examples

To fathom the sea
Early sailors would fathom the sea to ensure safe passage for their ships.
The crew fathomed the river before setting anchor near the shore.
Using a weighted line, he fathomed the channel to check its depth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sound - to measure the depth of water, especially with a sounding line
