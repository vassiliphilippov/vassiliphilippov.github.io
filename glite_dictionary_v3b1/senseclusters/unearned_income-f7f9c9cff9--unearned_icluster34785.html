unearned income

noun
income not earned by work
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈɝnd ˈɪnkʌm / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈɜːnd ˈɪnkʌm / (BrE)
income received from sources other than employment or active work, such as dividends, interest, or rent

Examples

Unearned income from rent
If your unearned income exceeds a certain threshold, you may be subject to higher taxes.
Interest payments and rent are typical forms of unearned income for many investors.
She reported significant unearned income from her stock dividends last year.
