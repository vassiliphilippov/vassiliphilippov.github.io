unit trust

noun
investment fund managed by professionals
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːnət ˌtrʌst / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪt ˌtrʌst / (BrE)
a type of investment fund in which investors' money is pooled and managed by professionals, who invest in a portfolio of securities; investors hold units in the trust, entitling them to a share of the fund's profits and assets.

Examples

A unit trust fund
After careful research, Sophie decided a unit trust was the best option for her savings.
Many people choose to invest in a unit trust to diversify their portfolios.
The unit trust offers the investor access to a wide range of assets for a relatively low initial investment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutual fund - an investment program funded by shareholders that trades in diversified holdings and is professionally managed
