katydid

noun
large green insect of the grasshopper family
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪtɪˌdɪd / (AmE), / ˈkeɪtɪˌdɪd / (BrE)
a large green North American insect of the grasshopper family, with long antennae; males produce shrill sounds by rubbing their forewings together

Examples

A green katydid
A bright green katydid landed on the porch screen, blending in with the leaves.
At night, the katydid filled the garden with its rhythmic chirping.
Biologists studied the way a katydid produces its distinctive nighttime call.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grasshopper - a plant-eating insect with long legs for jumping
