special pleading

noun
legal pleading alleging new facts to avoid allegations
4.1 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ˈplidɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈpliːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(Law) A pleading in which a party alleges new facts to avoid or counter the allegations made by the opposing party, rather than directly denying them.

Examples

Court special pleading
His use of special pleading attempted to circumvent the main point of the lawsuit.
The court distinguished between a general denial and a special pleading that introduced new evidence.
The defense attorney submitted a special pleading in response to the plaintiff’s accusation.
