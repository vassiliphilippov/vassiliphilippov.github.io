slime bacteria

noun
bacteria forming slimy colonies
Pronunciation: / slaɪm bækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / slaɪm bækˈtɪə.rɪ.ə / (BrE)
bacteria that form colonies within their own slime, typically found in moist soil, decaying plant material, or animal waste.

Examples

Slime bacteria colonies
Slime bacteria are often found in damp soil where they help decompose organic matter.
Scientists studied colonies of slime bacteria to understand their unique movement patterns.
Some slime bacteria produce fruiting bodies visible to the naked eye under certain conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: myxobacteria - a group of bacteria known for forming slime-covered colonies
