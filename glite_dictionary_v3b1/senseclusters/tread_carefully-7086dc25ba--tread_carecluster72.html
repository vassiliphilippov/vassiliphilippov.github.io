tread carefully

verb
proceed cautiously; act with care
Pronunciation: / trɛd ˈkɛrfəli / (AmE), / trɛd ˈkeəfəli / (BrE)
to act or proceed with great care, especially to avoid mistakes, danger, or offense

Examples

To tread carefully in talks
After the incident, she decided to tread carefully around her colleagues.
During the budget meeting, we had to tread carefully to avoid offending anyone.
You should tread carefully when discussing controversial issues in public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proceed cautiously - to go forward with care and attention
  • antonym: rush in - to act hastily or without caution
