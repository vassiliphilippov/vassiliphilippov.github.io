microcapsule

noun
tiny capsule
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌkæpsjuːl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌkæpsjuːl / (BrE)
a very small capsule that contains a substance and releases it in a controlled way, often used in medicine, technology, or materials

Examples

A microcapsule releases fragrance
The new fabric contains microcapsules that release fragrance when rubbed.
Scientists use microcapsules to deliver drugs directly to specific parts of the body.
Some vitamins are now available in microcapsule form for better absorption.
The new drug uses microcapsules to release the medicine slowly over time.
Scientists developed microcapsules to protect sensitive ingredients in food products.
The paint contains microcapsules that burst and release fragrance when scratched.
