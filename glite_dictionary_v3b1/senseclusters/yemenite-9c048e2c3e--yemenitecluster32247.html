Yemenite

noun
Jew from Yemen
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛmɪˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈjɛmɪnaɪt / (BrE)
a member of the Jewish community originating from Yemen, often called a Yemenite Jew

Examples

A Yemenite Jew
Her grandfather was a Yemenite who immigrated to Israel in the 1950s.
The Yemenite community is known for its unique traditions and music.
At the festival, a group of Yemenites performed traditional dances from their heritage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Ashkenazi - a Jew of Central or Eastern European descent
  • synonym: Sephardi - a Jew of Spanish or Portuguese descent
