emancipationist

noun
advocate for abolition of slavery
6.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmæn.sɪˈpeɪ.ʃə.nɪst / (AmE), / ɪˌmæn.sɪˈpeɪ.ʃən.ɪst / (BrE)

Examples

An emancipationist leader

Many emancipationists faced fierce opposition from those who benefited from the slave trade.
The emancipationist movement gained momentum in the decades leading up to the Civil War.
