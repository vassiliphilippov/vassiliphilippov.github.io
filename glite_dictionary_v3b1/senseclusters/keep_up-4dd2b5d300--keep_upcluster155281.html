keep up

verb
prevent someone from going to bed
Pronunciation: / kip ʌp / (AmE), / kiːp ʌp / (BrE)
to prevent someone from going to bed, or from getting the sleep they need, by causing them to stay awake

Examples

To keep up a child
Her worries about the exam kept up Sarah long past her usual bedtime.
I didn't mean to keep up the children so late with the party.
The loud noise from the street kept up the whole family until midnight.
