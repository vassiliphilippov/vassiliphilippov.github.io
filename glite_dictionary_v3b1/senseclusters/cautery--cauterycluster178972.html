cautery

noun
instrument or substance for tissue destruction (medical)
8.1 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.tər.iː/ /; / /ˈkɔː.tər.iː/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.tə.riː/ (BrE)
an instrument or substance used to destroy tissue, typically by burning, freezing, or using chemicals, for medical purposes such as removing warts or stopping bleeding

Examples

A cautery tool
Early forms of cautery involved heated metal instruments for sealing wounds.
Liquid nitrogen can be applied as a cautery to freeze and remove skin growths.
The doctor used a cautery to stop the bleeding during surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cauterize - to burn tissue with a heated instrument or chemical for medical reasons
