uncover

verb
reveal by removing a cover
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to reveal something by removing a cover or making it visible

Examples

To uncover hidden objects
As the snow melted, it uncovered the fresh green grass below.
She gently uncovered the old painting hidden beneath a sheet.
The investigation aims to uncover the truth behind the incident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cover - to place something over or upon, to hide or protect
  • synonym: reveal - to make visible or known; to disclose
