ceremonious

adjective
marked by formal ceremony or etiquette
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛrəˈmoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈməʊniəs / (BrE)
marked by or showing formal ceremony, etiquette, or excessive formality

Examples

A ceremonious greeting
He greeted each guest in a ceremonious manner, bowing politely as they arrived.
The dinner was a ceremonious affair, complete with speeches and a five-course meal.
Their wedding was less ceremonious than expected, focusing on personal vows rather than traditional rituals.
Despite his friendly words, his ceremonious bow made the meeting feel distant and cold.
Her ceremonious speech impressed the officials, but left the crowd feeling detached.
The ambassador greeted each guest in a ceremonious manner, following every protocol to the letter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formal - done in accordance with rules of convention or etiquette
  • antonym: informal - having a relaxed, unofficial, or casual style
