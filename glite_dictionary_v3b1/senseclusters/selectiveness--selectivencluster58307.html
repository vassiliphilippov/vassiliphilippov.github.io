selectiveness

noun
care in choosing
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛktɪvnəs / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being careful, particular, or limited in choosing people, things, or outcomes

Examples

High selectiveness in friends
Her selectiveness in choosing friends is well known.
The chef's selectiveness about ingredients ensures every dish is of high quality.
Colleges often show great selectiveness when admitting new students.
The scientist praised the selectiveness of the enzyme in targeting only harmful bacteria.
High selectiveness in the reaction led to fewer unwanted byproducts.
Her selectiveness in choosing research topics resulted in a strong academic reputation.
The selectiveness of the private school makes admission very competitive.
Many companies pride themselves on their selectiveness when hiring new employees.
Due to its selectiveness, the club only accepts a handful of new members each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discrimination - the ability to recognize differences and make careful choices
  • synonym: exclusivity - the state of being limited to a select group
  • antonym: indiscrimination - lack of careful choice or distinction
  • antonym: openness - the quality of being open to many people or things
  • synonym: specificity - the quality of being specific to a particular substance or process
