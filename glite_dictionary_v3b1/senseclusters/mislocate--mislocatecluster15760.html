mislocate

verb
to put in wrong place
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsloʊˈkeɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsləʊˈkeɪt / (BrE)
to put or show something in the wrong place or position

Examples

To mislocate an item
If you mislocate your passport, it can cause problems when you travel.
The technician accidentally mislocated the wires during the installation process.
It is easy to mislocate small items when you are in a hurry.
The old map mislocates the river, making it difficult for hikers to find the correct path.
If you mislocate the files in the database, it can cause confusion during data analysis.
Historians discovered that several ancient texts mislocated the capital city by hundreds of miles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: locate - to put something in a particular place
  • synonym: misplace - to put something in the wrong place and lose track of it
