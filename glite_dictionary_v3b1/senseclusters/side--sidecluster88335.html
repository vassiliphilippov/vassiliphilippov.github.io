side

noun
a competing group or team
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: / saɪd / (AmE), / saɪd / (BrE)
one of two or more groups or teams in a competition, conflict, or debate; or an opinion or position in a dispute

Examples

The winning side
Each side presented strong arguments during the heated debate.
The two sides met on the battlefield at dawn.
You have to pick a side before the match begins.
He took his brother's side when their parents had an argument.
She always tries to see both sides of the argument before making a decision.
The judge listened carefully to each side in the dispute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - the state of sharing the same opinion; the opposite of being on different sides
  • antonym: opponent - the other side or group in a competition or conflict
  • synonym: position - a point of view or attitude on an issue
  • synonym: team - a group of people forming one side in a competitive game or sport
  • synonym: viewpoint - a particular attitude or way of considering a matter
