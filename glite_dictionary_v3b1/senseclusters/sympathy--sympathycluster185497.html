sympathy

noun
support for a cause or opinion
5.7 1.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪm.pə.θiː/ (AmE), /ˈsɪm.pə.θiː/ (BrE)
A tendency to support or agree with the opinions or cause of a particular person or group.

Examples

Sympathy with a cause
He made it obvious that his sympathy was with those who demanded reform.
Her sympathy clearly lay with the environmental activists.
The government expressed strong sympathy for the striking workers.
