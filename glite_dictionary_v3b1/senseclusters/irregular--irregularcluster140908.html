irregular

noun
nonstandard word form
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
a word or form that does not follow standard grammatical or spelling rules, especially in language learning.

Examples

An irregular verb
In English, 'children' is an irregular because it does not follow the normal plural rules.
Learners often find it difficult to remember all the irregulars in a new language.
The teacher gave us a list of common irregulars to study for the test.
