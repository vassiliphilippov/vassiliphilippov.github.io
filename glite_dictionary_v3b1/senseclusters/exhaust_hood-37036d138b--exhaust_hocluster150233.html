exhaust hood

noun
vented metal cover to remove smoke or fumes
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɔst hʊd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːst hʊd / (BrE)
A device consisting of a metal covering with a vent, used to remove smoke, fumes, or steam from an area, especially above a stove or in an industrial setting.

Examples

A exhaust hood vent
A modern exhaust hood can greatly improve ventilation in your home.
Inspectors checked the exhaust hood regularly for grease buildup in the restaurant.
The chef turned on the exhaust hood to clear the kitchen of smoke.
