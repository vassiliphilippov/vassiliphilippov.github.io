condensing turbine

noun
steam turbine exhausting to a condenser
4.0
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛnsɪŋ tɝːˈbaɪn / (AmE), / kənˈdɛnsɪŋ ˈtɜːbaɪn / (BrE)
A steam turbine that discharges steam at a pressure lower than atmospheric into a condenser, allowing for greater efficiency in power generation.

Examples

A condensing turbine unit
A condensing turbine is often used in large-scale electricity generation where maximizing output is important.
Operators monitor the pressure closely to ensure the condensing turbine is working optimally.
The power plant installed a new condensing turbine to improve its efficiency.
