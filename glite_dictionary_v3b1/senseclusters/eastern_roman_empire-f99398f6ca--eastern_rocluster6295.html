Eastern Roman Empire

noun
the eastern part of the Roman Empire after 395 AD
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstərn ˈroʊmən ˈɛmpaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈiːstən ˈrəʊmən ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
The eastern part of the Roman Empire, which continued in the Middle East after the division of the empire in 395 AD, also known as the Byzantine Empire.

Examples

The Eastern Roman Empire capital
Constantinople served as the capital of the Eastern Roman Empire.
Greek was the official language of the Eastern Roman Empire in later centuries.
The Eastern Roman Empire survived for nearly a thousand years after the fall of Rome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Byzantine Empire - The empire that succeeded the Eastern Roman Empire, same entity under a later name
  • antonym: Western Roman Empire - The western part of the Roman Empire after its division in 395 AD
