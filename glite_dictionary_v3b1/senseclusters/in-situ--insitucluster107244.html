in-situ

adjective
in the original place; unmoved
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈsɪtu / (AmE), / ɪn ˈsɪtjuː / (BrE)
existing or occurring in its original place; not moved from its usual or natural position.

Examples

In-situ inspection
After the fire, the building was inspected in-situ by engineers to assess the damage.
The artwork was preserved in-situ, maintaining its historical context within the church.
The fossils remained in-situ, allowing scientists to learn about their natural environment.
