geostationary

adjective
having a fixed position above Earth in orbit
7.3 0.1/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd íiÀê.o äÀàste…™. É…ôÀåner.i / (AmE), / Àåd íiÀê.…ô äÀàste…™. É…ôn.…ôri / (BrE)
(of an orbit or satellite) having a geosynchronous orbit such that its position is fixed relative to a specific point on the Earth's surface, remaining above the same spot as the planet rotates.

Examples

A geostationary orbit
A geostationary orbit allows television signals to be beamed directly to antennas without changing position.
Engineers carefully calculated the altitude required for the satellite to stay geostationary above the equator.
The weather satellite remained geostationary, constantly monitoring the same region of the planet.
