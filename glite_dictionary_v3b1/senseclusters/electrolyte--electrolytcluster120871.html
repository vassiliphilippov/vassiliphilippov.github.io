electrolyte

noun
solution that conducts electricity
7.7 4.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trə.laɪt/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trə.laɪt/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trə.laɪt/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trə.laɪt/ (BrE)
a liquid or solution that conducts electricity by containing dissolved ions that allow the movement of electric charge

Examples

Electrolyte sports drink
Athletes often consume sports drinks containing electrolytes to help their bodies recover after intense exercise.
The battery uses a liquid electrolyte to transfer ions between its electrodes.
When dissolved in water, an electrolyte can carry an electric current through the solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conductor - material or substance that permits electricity to flow
  • antonym: insulator - a material that does not conduct electricity
