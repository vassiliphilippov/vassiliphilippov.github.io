mainstay

noun
most important support; central source of strength
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪnˌsteɪ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌsteɪ /; / ˈmeɪnsteɪ / (BrE)
the most important or central support of something or someone; a main source of strength or stability

Examples

A mainstay of society
Education is the mainstay of a strong and prosperous society.
For years, she has been the financial mainstay of her family.
Tourism is the economic mainstay of the island.
As the team's mainstay, she kept everyone motivated during difficult times.
Reliable volunteers like Margaret are the mainstay of our charity organization.
Since his arrival, he has become a mainstay in the local music scene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cornerstone - an important quality or feature on which a particular thing depends or is based
  • synonym: linchpin - a person or thing vital to an organization or system
  • synonym: pillar - a person who is a fundamental support of something
  • antonym: weakling - a person lacking strength or influence
