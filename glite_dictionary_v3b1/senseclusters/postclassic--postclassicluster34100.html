postclassic

noun
period after classical era
7.9
Pronunciation: /poʊst.ˈklæs.ɪk/ (AmE), /pəʊst.ˈklæs.ɪk/ (BrE)
the historical period in a culture that comes after its classical era, often used in archaeology or art history.

Examples

The postclassic period
The postclassic period in Mesoamerican history saw significant changes in political organization.
Archaeologists discovered new types of pottery from the postclassic era.
Many cities declined during the postclassic phase, while others rose to prominence.
