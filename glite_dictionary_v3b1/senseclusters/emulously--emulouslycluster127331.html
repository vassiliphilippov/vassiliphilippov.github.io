emulously

adverb
in a competitive or imitative way
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛm.jə.ləs.li / (AmE), / ˈɛm.jʊ.ləs.li / (BrE)
in a manner showing eagerness to imitate or rival someone, often competitively

Examples

To emulously imitate
He emulously adopted his mentor's style, hoping to achieve similar success.
She watched her colleague emulously recreate the award-winning presentation.
The students emulously competed for the top position in the class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emulous - showing a desire to equal or surpass others
