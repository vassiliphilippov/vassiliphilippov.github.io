John Cabot

proper noun
Italian-born explorer of North America
6.9
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn ˈkæbət / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈkæbət / (BrE)
(c. 1450–1498) An Italian-born explorer who, under the commission of England, led an expedition in 1497 that is credited with the European discovery of the mainland of North America, including areas from Nova Scotia to Newfoundland.

Examples

John Cabot expedition
John Cabot sailed from England in 1497 and made landfall on the coast of North America.
History lessons often mention John Cabot as one of the earliest European explorers to reach Canada.
Many historians believe that John Cabot's voyage paved the way for later English claims in the New World.
