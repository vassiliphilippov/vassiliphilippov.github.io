vote of confidence

noun
expression of approval/support, often formal
2.1 3.3
Pronunciation: / voʊt əv ˈkɑnfədəns / (AmE), / vəʊt əv ˈkɒnfɪdəns / (BrE)
an indication of approval or support, especially by a group for a leader or proposal, often by means of a formal vote

Examples

A vote of confidence
The board gave the CEO a vote of confidence after the successful merger.
The prime minister called for a vote of confidence to strengthen his government's mandate.
Winning the award was a real vote of confidence in her abilities as a writer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vote of no confidence - a formal indication of withdrawal of support from a leader or government
