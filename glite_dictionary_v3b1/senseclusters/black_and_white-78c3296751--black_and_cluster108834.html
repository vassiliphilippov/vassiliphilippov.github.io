black and white

noun
photograph or film without color
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌblæk ən ˈwaɪt / (BrE)
a photograph, film, or television image that is produced in shades of black, white, and gray, without color

Examples

A black and white photo
He prefers to shoot movies in black and white for a more classic feel.
The exhibition features stunning black and white portraits of jazz musicians.
The old family album was filled with black and white photographs from the 1940s.

Related Terms

  • antonym: color photograph - a photograph in full color, as opposed to black and white
  • synonym: monochrome - an image in shades of a single color (typically black and white for photos)
