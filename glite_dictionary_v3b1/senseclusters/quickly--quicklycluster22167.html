Synonyms

  • swiftly
    1.0 8.45/m

  • speedily
    2.4 0.76/m
    speedily is a more formal word for doing something fast, while quickly is more common in everyday speech.
  • fast
    1.0 213/m
    fast can be informal and also an adjective for high speed, while quickly is the common adverb for speed or promptness.
  • in short order
    3.5 1.22/m
    in short order means very soon or quickly in sequence, while quickly is a general adverb for speed or lack of delay.
  • instantly
    1.0 28/m
    instantly means at once with no delay, while quickly often means moving fast and sometimes simply without much delay.
  • immediately
    1.0 121/m
    immediately means right away without any delay, while quickly can mean fast movement or a short time to finish something.
  • right away
    1.0 27/m
    right away means with no delay at the same moment, while quickly can mean moving fast rather than instant timing.
  • straightaway
