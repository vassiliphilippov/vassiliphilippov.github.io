otolith organ

noun
structure in the inner ear detecting gravity/acceleration
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.tə.lɪθ ˈɔːr.gən/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.tə.lɪθ ˈɔː.ɡən/ (BrE)
Either of two structures (the utricle or saccule) in the inner ear of vertebrates, responsible for detecting gravity and linear acceleration.

Examples

The otolith organ in ear
Damage to the otolith organ can result in problems with spatial orientation.
Researchers studied the development of the otolith organ in zebrafish embryos.
The otolith organ helps us maintain balance by detecting changes in head position.
