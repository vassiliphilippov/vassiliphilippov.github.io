defy all odds

verb
succeed despite great difficulties
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfaɪ ɔl ɑdz / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪ ɔːl ɒdz / (BrE)
to achieve success or survive despite being very unlikely to do so because of great difficulties

Examples

To defy all odds
Despite her illness, she managed to defy all odds and finish the marathon.
The tiny startup defied all odds to become a major technology company.
They were told they would never win, but they defied all odds and claimed the championship.
