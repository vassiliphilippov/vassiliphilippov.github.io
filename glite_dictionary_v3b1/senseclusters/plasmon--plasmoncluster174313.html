plasmon

noun
quantum plasma oscillation
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈplæz.mən/ (AmE), /ˈplæz.mɒn/ (BrE)
a quantum of plasma oscillation; a collective oscillation of free electron gas in a material (physics)

Examples

Surface plasmon
Scientists observed a plasmon traveling along the surface of the metal during the experiment.
The energy of a plasmon can be measured using advanced spectroscopy techniques.
Understanding how a plasmon behaves is important for developing new nanotechnology devices.
Scientists observed a strong plasmon resonance in the thin gold film under laser light.
The unique properties of the plasmon make it valuable for developing new optical devices.
By tuning the nanostructure, researchers can control the plasmon frequency for various applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exciton - a bound state of an electron and an electron hole in a material
  • synonym: phonon - a quantum of vibrational energy in a crystal lattice
