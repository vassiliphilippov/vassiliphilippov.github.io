aftershock

noun
earth tremor after main earthquake
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈæf.tɚˌʃɑk / (AmE), / ˈɑːf.təʃɒk / (BrE)
a smaller earth tremor that follows the main shock of an earthquake

Examples

An aftershock tremor
Residents were warned that aftershocks could occur over the next week.
The first aftershock caused some already weakened buildings to collapse.
There was a strong aftershock hours after the main earthquake shook the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tremor - a small or slight earthquake
