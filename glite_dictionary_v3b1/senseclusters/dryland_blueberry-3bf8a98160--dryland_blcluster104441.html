dryland blueberry

noun
shrub with sweet blue berries (U.S. native)
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪˌlænd ˈbluːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈdraɪlənd ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
a low deciduous shrub of the eastern United States that bears dark blue, sweet berries

Examples

The dryland blueberry bush
Birds are often attracted to the sweet fruit of the dryland blueberry.
During our hike, we spotted several bushes covered in ripe dryland blueberries.
The dryland blueberry thrives in sandy, well-drained soils in the eastern United States.
