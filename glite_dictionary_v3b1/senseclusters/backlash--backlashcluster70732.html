backlash

verb
have an unintended negative rebound effect
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈbæk.læʃ / (BrE)
(of an action or statement) to have an unintended negative effect that rebounds on the originator; to backfire.

Examples

Words backlash
If you criticize your boss, your words might backlash and harm your own position.
The company's attempt to cut costs backlashed when employees began to quit en masse.
Their negative campaign backlashed, causing voters to support the opposition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backfire - (of an action) to have the opposite effect to what was intended
