recondition

verb
repair and restore to working order
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to repair or restore something or someone to a good or usable condition

Examples

To recondition old bikes
Before donating the bicycles, we will recondition them so they are safe to ride.
Many companies recondition old electronics for resale at a lower price.
The mechanic was able to recondition the engine and make it run smoothly again.
After the accident, the physiotherapist reconditioned her leg so she could walk normally again.
The mechanic reconditioned the old engine, making it run like new.
Special exercises are used to recondition athletes after a long injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: damage - to harm or spoil something so it is no longer in good condition
  • synonym: refurbish - to renovate and redecorate something, especially a building
  • synonym: rehabilitate - to help someone return to health or normal life through training or therapy
  • synonym: repair - to fix or restore something that is damaged or not working
  • synonym: restore - to bring back to a former condition or position
