maggot

noun
larva of a fly found in decay
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡət / (AmE), / ˈmæɡət / (BrE)
the larva of a fly, especially a housefly or blowfly, typically found in decaying organic matter

Examples

A maggot larva
Fishermen sometimes use maggots as bait for catching certain types of fish.
Rotting food will quickly attract maggots if left uncovered in the kitchen.
The sight of maggots crawling in the garbage can was revolting.
