volley

noun
simultaneous discharge of firearms
6.8 0.96/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…ëÀêli / (AmE), / Ààv…íli / (BrE)
a simultaneous or rapid series of shots, missiles, or other projectiles, especially from firearms

Examples

A volley of bullets
A volley of gunfire echoed across the battlefield as the soldiers advanced.
The enemy unleashed a volley of arrows from behind the walls.
With a sudden volley, the defenders repelled the attackers trying to breach the gate.

Synonyms

  • salvo
    8.6 0.52/m
    salvo is a group of shots fired together, often from artillery, while volley names any quick or simultaneous series of projectiles.
  • fusillade
    9.5 0.32/m
    fusillade is a long, rapid burst of shooting that keeps going, while volley is any quick or simultaneous series of shots.
  • burst
    5.6 2.19/m
    burst is a short, sudden discharge from a weapon, while volley covers any rapid series or many shots together.
  • massed fire
    8.8 0.00/m
    massed fire is coordinated shooting by several weapons at once for a target, while volley may be from one or many weapons.
  • concentrated fire
    7.1 0.02/m
    concentrated fire is aimed shooting by several guns at a single area, while volley can mean a quick series of shots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrage - a concentrated artillery bombardment over a wide area
  • synonym: fusillade - a series of shots fired or missiles thrown all at the same time or in quick succession
