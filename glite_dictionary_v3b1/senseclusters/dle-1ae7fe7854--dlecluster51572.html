DLE

noun
chronic skin disease; red lesions on face
9.9
Pronunciation: / di ɛl i / (AmE), / diː ɛl iː / (BrE)
A chronic skin disease, discoid lupus erythematosus, marked by red lesions, especially over the cheeks and nose, occurring mainly in women aged 20–40.

Examples

DLE skin disease
Proper sun protection is crucial for those diagnosed with DLE to prevent flare-ups.
The dermatologist confirmed that the patient had DLE after examining the facial lesions.
Women with DLE often experience red, scaly patches on their cheeks and nose.
