non-radical

adjective
conservative procedure
Pronunciation: / nɑn ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / nɒn ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
describing a medical procedure that is conservative and does not involve extensive removal of tissue or organs

Examples

Non-radical surgery
The doctor suggested a non-radical approach to minimize the impact on healthy tissue.
A non-radical surgery was chosen to preserve as much function as possible.
Patients often recover faster after a non-radical procedure compared to more extensive operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - not involving drastic or extensive measures
  • antonym: radical - involving extensive or drastic measures, especially in surgery
