lease-lend

noun
policy of supplying allies with goods/services
Pronunciation: / lis lɛnd / (AmE), / liːs lɛnd / (BrE)
the transfer of goods and services to an ally, especially by a government to support a common cause, notably as an official policy during World War II

Examples

The lease-lend policy
The lease-lend policy had a significant impact on the outcome of the war.
The United States implemented lease-lend to support its allies during World War II.
Under the lease-lend agreement, military supplies were sent to nations fighting the Axis powers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lend-lease - the same policy; more common spelling
