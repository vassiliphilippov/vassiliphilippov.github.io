fledged

adjective
of an arrow, having feathers attached
Pronunciation: / flɛdʒd / (AmE), / flɛdʒd / (BrE)
(of an arrow) equipped with feathers for stabilization in flight.

Examples

Fledged arrow
Historically, only fledged arrows were used in competitions for accuracy and range.
The archer selected a fledged arrow from his quiver before taking aim.
Without properly fledged arrows, archers would struggle to maintain a straight flight path.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unfledged - of an arrow, not equipped with feathers
