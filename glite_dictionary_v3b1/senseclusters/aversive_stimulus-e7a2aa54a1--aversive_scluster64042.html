aversive stimulus

noun
unpleasant stimulus to be avoided
Pronunciation: / əˈvɝːsɪv ˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / əˈvɜːsɪv ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
a negative or unpleasant stimulus that causes an organism to learn to avoid it

Examples

An aversive stimulus shock
Electric shocks are often used as an aversive stimulus in animal behavior experiments.
The loud noise served as an aversive stimulus to discourage the rats from entering the dark compartment.
Withdrawal of an aversive stimulus can be a reward in certain learning situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reinforcer - a stimulus that strengthens or increases the likelihood of a behavior
