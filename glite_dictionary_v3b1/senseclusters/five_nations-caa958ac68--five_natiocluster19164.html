Five Nations

proper noun
original Iroquois tribal league in North America
8.2
Pronunciation: /faɪv ˈneɪ.ʃənz/ (AmE), /faɪv ˈneɪ.ʃənz/ (BrE)
a confederation of Iroquois tribes—originally the Mohawk, Oneida, Onondaga, Cayuga, and Seneca—forming a powerful league in northeastern North America before later becoming the Six Nations with the addition of the Tuscarora

Examples

The Five Nations league
Many treaties were signed between the British and the Five Nations in colonial America.
The Five Nations formed a sophisticated political alliance that influenced neighboring peoples.
The unity of the Five Nations was instrumental in their military strength.
