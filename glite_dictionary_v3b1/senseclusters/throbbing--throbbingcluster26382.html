throbbing

noun
strong rhythmic sound or beat
7.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈθrɑː.bɪŋ/ /; / /ˈθrɔː.bɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈθrɒ.bɪŋ/ (BrE)
a strong, rhythmic, repeated beat or vibration, especially as a sound or felt in the body

Examples

The throbbing of music
She was lulled to sleep by the gentle throbbing of the boat's engine.
The throbbing of drums filled the concert hall.
We listened to the distant throbbing of the city late at night.
A persistent throbbing behind his eyes warned him of an approaching migraine.
He woke up with a dull throbbing in his temples after the long night.
The sudden throbbing in her wrist made her realize she had sprained it.
