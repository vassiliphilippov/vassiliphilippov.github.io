all-or-nothing

adjective
complete or not at all; no middle ground
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɔl ər ˈnʌθɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌɔːl ə ˈnʌθɪŋ / (BrE)
involving or allowing no middle ground; happening completely or not at all

Examples

An all-or-nothing choice
Her all-or-nothing attitude made negotiations difficult.
It's an all-or-nothing gamble that could either save or ruin the business.
The company's all-or-nothing approach to risk means no compromise is possible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolute - not qualified or diminished in any way; total
  • antonym: partial - existing only in part; incomplete
