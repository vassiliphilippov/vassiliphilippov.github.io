ambisexual

adjective
having both sexes
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌæmbiˈsɛkʃuəl / (AmE), / ˌæmbɪˈsɛkʃuəl / (BrE)
having both male and female reproductive organs or sexual characteristics, especially in plants or animals

Examples

Ambisexual plants
Some species of fish are ambisexual and can change sex during their lifetime.
The ambisexual nature of the flower allows it to self-pollinate if cross-pollination does not occur.
Scientists studied the ambisexual characteristics of certain snails in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bisexual - having both male and female reproductive organs
  • antonym: unisexual - having only male or only female reproductive organs
