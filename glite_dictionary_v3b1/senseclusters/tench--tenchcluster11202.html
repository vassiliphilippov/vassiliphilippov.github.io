tench

noun
freshwater fish of Europe and Asia
Pronunciation: / tɛntʃ / (AmE), / tɛntʃ / (BrE)
a freshwater fish (Tinca tinca) native to Europe and western Asia, known for its olive-green color and ability to survive in low-oxygen water.

Examples

A tench fish
Anglers often seek out tench for their challenging fight and unique taste.
The lake is home to many species, including the elusive tench.
We caught a large tench while fishing early in the morning.
