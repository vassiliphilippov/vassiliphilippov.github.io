illusionistic

adjective
deceptively realistic
Pronunciation: / ɪˌluːʒəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌluːʒəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
making something look real or three-dimensional, especially in art, but actually based on illusion

Examples

Illusionistic art style
The artist used illusionistic techniques to make the painting look three-dimensional.
Her illusionistic arguments sounded convincing but were not based on facts.
The stage set was so illusionistic that the audience believed it was a real street.
The artist used illusionistic techniques to make the ceiling appear open to the sky.
Her illusionistic painting style tricks viewers into thinking the objects are real.
The museum features several illusionistic murals that create a sense of depth on flat walls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstract - not representing reality or concrete objects
  • synonym: deceptive - giving a false impression or misleading
  • antonym: realistic - representing things as they really are
