conflict

verb
to be in opposition, especially with rules or laws
6.9 3.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈflɪkt / (AmE), / kənˈflɪkt / (BrE)
to be in opposition or not in agreement; to clash or be incompatible

Examples

Conflict with the law
If your beliefs conflict with the law, you may face consequences.
The policy conflicts with existing federal regulations.
Your plan conflicts with the company's code of conduct.
His personal beliefs often conflict with his professional duties.
The dates you suggested conflict with our previously scheduled meeting.
The two witnesses' statements conflict with each other.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to be in harmony or accord
  • synonym: clash - to come into conflict or opposition
  • antonym: comply - to act in accordance with rules or requests
  • synonym: contradict - to assert the opposite or deny the truth of
