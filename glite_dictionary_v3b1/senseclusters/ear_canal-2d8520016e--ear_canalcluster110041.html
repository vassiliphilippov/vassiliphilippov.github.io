ear canal

noun
tube in outer ear leading to eardrum
Pronunciation: / ɪr kəˈnæl / (AmE), / ɪə kəˈnæl / (BrE)
the tube-like passage in the outer ear that leads from the external ear to the eardrum (tympanic membrane)

Examples

The ear canal passage
Earwax buildup can sometimes block the ear canal and affect hearing.
Inserting objects into the ear canal can cause injury or push earwax deeper inside.
The doctor used an otoscope to examine the patient's ear canal for signs of infection.
