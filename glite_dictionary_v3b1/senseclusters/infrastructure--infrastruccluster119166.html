infrastructure

noun
basic facilities and systems of a society
3.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.frə.ˈstrʌk.tʃər/ (AmE), /ɪn.frə.ˈstrʌk.tʃə/ (BrE)
the basic physical and organizational structures and facilities (such as buildings, roads, and power supplies) needed for the operation of a society, business, or system

Examples

Basic infrastructure systems
Modern businesses depend on a reliable infrastructure to operate efficiently.
Much of the country's infrastructure was damaged during the hurricane.
The government is investing in new infrastructure to improve transportation and communication.
After the earthquake, much of the country's infrastructure was damaged, making recovery efforts difficult.
Investing in public infrastructure like roads and bridges is essential for economic growth.
The company upgraded its IT infrastructure to improve data security and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: framework - a supporting structure around which something can be built
  • antonym: superstructure - the part of a building or structure above its foundation
