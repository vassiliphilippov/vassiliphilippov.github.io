pawn

noun
object deposited as collateral
Pronunciation: / pɔn / (AmE), / pɔːn / (BrE)
an object left with a pawnbroker as security for a loan

Examples

A pawn ticket
He left his gold watch as a pawn at the shop when he needed money.
If you fail to repay the loan, the pawn will be sold.
She reclaimed her pawn after paying back the full amount.
He retrieved his watch, which was a pawn for a small loan.
She left her grandmother's ring as a pawn at the shop.
The painting was held as a pawn until he repaid the money.
