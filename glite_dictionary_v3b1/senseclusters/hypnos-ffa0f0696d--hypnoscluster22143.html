Hypnos

proper noun
Greek god of sleep
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪp.nɑs / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.nɒs / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of sleep, son of Nyx (Night).

Examples

Hypnos god of sleep
According to myth, Hypnos could lull even the mightiest gods to sleep.
Artists often depict Hypnos as a gentle youth with wings on his temples.
In many ancient stories, Hypnos would visit mortals to bring them peaceful dreams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Somnus - Roman god of sleep (counterpart of Hypnos)
