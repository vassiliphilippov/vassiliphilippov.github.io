surpass

verb
be greater than; exceed
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: / sərˈpæs / (AmE), / səˈpɑːs / (BrE)
to be better than, do better than, or go beyond someone or something; to exceed a standard or achievement

Examples

To surpass competitors
He hoped his skills would surpass those of his competitors.
Her achievements surpass all expectations.
The new bridge surpasses the old one in both length and beauty.
During the parade, one marching band surpassed another as they moved down the street.
One cyclist managed to surpass the others just before the finish line.
The river surpasses the old bridge and winds through the valley.
He hoped to surpass all previous records with his performance.
Her achievements surpass everyone else's in her class.
The new smartphone surpasses the previous model in speed and battery life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exceed - be greater in number or amount than
  • synonym: excel - to be exceptionally good at something
  • antonym: fall short - fail to reach a desired amount or standard
  • synonym: outdo - to do better than someone else
  • synonym: outstrip - move faster than and overtake
  • synonym: pass - to move past something or someone
