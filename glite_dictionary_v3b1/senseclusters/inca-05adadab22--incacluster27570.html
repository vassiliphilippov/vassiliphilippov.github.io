Inca

proper noun
ancient Peruvian people
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋkə / (AmE), / ˈɪŋkə / (BrE)
a member of the South American people who established a large empire in Peru before the Spanish conquest

Examples

The Inca civilization
The Inca developed an extensive network of roads across the Andes mountains.
Many tourists visit Machu Picchu to learn about the Inca civilization.
The Inca were skilled farmers who built terraces to grow crops at high altitudes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aztec - a member of the Mesoamerican people who built an empire in central Mexico
  • synonym: Maya - a member of the indigenous people of southern Mexico and Central America
