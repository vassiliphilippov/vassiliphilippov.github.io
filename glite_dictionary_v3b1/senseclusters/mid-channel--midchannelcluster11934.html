mid-channel

noun
center of a waterway
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌtʃænəl / (BrE)
the middle part of a river, channel, or waterway, away from the banks or edges

Examples

Mid-channel of the river
The ferry stayed in the mid-channel to avoid shallow water near the banks.
Strong currents are often found in the mid-channel of large rivers.
Swimmers are advised not to cross the mid-channel because of the fast-moving water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shore - the land along the edge of a body of water
