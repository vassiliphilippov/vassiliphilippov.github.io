Agave cantala

noun
Philippine plant yielding strong fiber
9.6
Pronunciation: / əˈɡɑːveɪ ˌkænˈtɑːlə / (AmE), / əˈɡɑːvi ˌkænˈtɑːlə / (BrE)
A species of agave native to the Philippines, known for producing a hard fiber used to make coarse twine and rope.

Examples

Agave cantala fiber
Agave cantala is widely grown in the Philippines for its strong, durable fibers.
In some regions, Agave cantala is cultivated as a source of natural twine.
The coarse rope was made from the fibers of Agave cantala.
