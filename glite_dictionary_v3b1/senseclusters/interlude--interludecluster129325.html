interlude

noun
short performance between acts or scenes
5.8 0.44/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…™n.t…öÀålud / (AmE), / Àà…™n.t…ô.ljuÀêd / (BrE)
A short performance, such as a piece of music or dance, given between the sections of a longer play, concert, or event.

Examples

Musical interlude
A comic interlude provided some relief before the dramatic final scene.
During the interlude, dancers performed a short routine to entertain the audience.
The orchestra played a lively interlude between the first and second acts.

Synonyms

  • intermezzo
    8.5 0.13/m
    an intermezzo is a short musical or dramatic piece played between main parts, while an interlude is any short performance between sections.
  • entr'acte
    9.9 0.03/m
    an entr'acte is a short theatrical break or performance between acts, while an interlude can occur between any event sections.
  • interlocution
    9.9 0.00/m
    an interlocution is a short dramatic dialogue often spoken between acts, while an interlude may be a musical or dance performance.

Related Terms
