posterior cardinal vein

noun
major embryonic vein draining the body
Pronunciation: / pəˈstɪriɚ ˈkɑrdɪnl veɪn / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəriə ˈkɑːdɪnəl veɪn / (BrE)
a major vein in embryonic vertebrates that drains blood from the tail and the caudal part of the body to the heart

Examples

The posterior cardinal vein
In some vertebrates, the posterior cardinal vein regresses as development progresses.

The posterior cardinal vein plays a crucial role in blood circulation during embryonic development.
