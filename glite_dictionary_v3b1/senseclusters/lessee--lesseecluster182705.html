lessee

noun
person renting property under a lease
Pronunciation: / lɛˈsi / (AmE), / lɛˈsiː / (BrE)
a person who holds the lease of a property; a tenant who rents property from a lessor

Examples

The lessee rents property
If the lessee breaks the terms of the lease, they may face eviction.
The lessee is responsible for paying utilities during the rental period.
The contract clearly listed the rights and obligations of both the lessor and the lessee.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lessor - the person who grants a lease; the owner or landlord
  • synonym: tenant - a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord
