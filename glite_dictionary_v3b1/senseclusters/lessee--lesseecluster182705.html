lessee

noun
person renting property under a lease
7.0 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / l…õÀàsi / (AmE), / l…õÀàsiÀê / (BrE)
a person who holds the lease of a property; a tenant who rents property from a lessor

Examples

The lessee rents property
If the lessee breaks the terms of the lease, they may face eviction.
The lessee is responsible for paying utilities during the rental period.
The contract clearly listed the rights and obligations of both the lessor and the lessee.

Synonyms

  • leaseholder
    6.3 0.14/m
    leaseholder holds legal rights to use property under a lease for a set term, while lessee is the formal legal term for that tenant.
  • tenant
    3.0 12/m
    tenant rents or occupies property in everyday speech, while lessee is the formal legal term for the person holding the lease.
  • renter
    1.8 2.20/m
    renter pays to use property or items, often for short terms and in casual speech, while lessee is the formal lease-holder for property.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lessor - the person who grants a lease; the owner or landlord
  • synonym: tenant - a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord
