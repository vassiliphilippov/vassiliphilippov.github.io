flyleaf

noun
blank page at front or back of a book
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌlif / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌliːf / (BrE)
a blank page at the beginning or end of a book, usually forming part of the binding

Examples

The flyleaf page
A pressed flower fell out from between the flyleaf and the cover.
She wrote her name on the flyleaf of the book as soon as she got it.
The dedication was inscribed on the flyleaf in elegant handwriting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endpaper - a sheet of paper at the very beginning or end of a book, pasted to the inside cover
