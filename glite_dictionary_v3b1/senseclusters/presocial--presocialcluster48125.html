presocial

adjective
not forming social groups
Pronunciation: / priːˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / priːˈsəʊʃəl / (BrE)
describing animals, species, or stages that are before or lack social behavior or organization

Examples

Presocial animals
Some wasp species are considered presocial because they do not build communal nests.
Researchers observed that presocial insects rarely interact with others of their kind.
The study focused on the transition from presocial to social behavior in certain animal groups.
Some animals display presocial behaviors before they begin interacting with their group.
Researchers study the presocial phase in infants to understand early development.
The presocial period is important for observing instinctive reactions before social learning occurs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antisocial - not wanting the company of others or harmful to society
  • antonym: eusocial - showing the highest level of social organization in animals
  • antonym: social - relating to society or its organization
  • synonym: solitary - living or existing alone, not in groups
