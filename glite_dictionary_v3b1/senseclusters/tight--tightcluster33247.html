tight

adjective
(of money or credit) scarce or expensive to borrow
1.0
Pronunciation: / taɪt / (AmE), / taɪt / (BrE)
(of money, credit, or resources) scarce or difficult to obtain, especially making borrowing expensive; characterized by financial stringency.

Examples

Tight budget
During a recession, the market often becomes tight as investors hold back.
The central bank acted to ease the tight money supply.
With tight credit conditions, small businesses struggled to secure loans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loose - (of money or credit) plentiful or easy to obtain
