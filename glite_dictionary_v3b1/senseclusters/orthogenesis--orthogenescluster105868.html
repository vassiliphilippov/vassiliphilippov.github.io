orthogenesis

noun
directed evolution theory
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.θoʊ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ /; / /ɔːr.θə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /ɔː.θəʊ.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the theory that evolution proceeds in a predetermined or directed path rather than by natural selection

Examples

Orthogenesis theory
Many early biologists supported orthogenesis before the modern understanding of evolution emerged.
The concept of orthogenesis suggests that evolutionary changes follow a fixed direction.
Most scientists today reject orthogenesis in favor of natural selection as the main driver of evolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolution - the process by which different kinds of living organisms develop over time
  • antonym: natural selection - the process by which organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring
