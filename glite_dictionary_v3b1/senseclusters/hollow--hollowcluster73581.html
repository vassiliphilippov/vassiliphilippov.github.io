hollow

noun
a cavity or empty space in something
3.8 1.86/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ëlo ä /; / Ààh…ëÀêlo ä / (AmE), / Ààh…íl…ô ä / (BrE)
a cavity or empty space within something, especially one that is sunken or depressed relative to its surroundings

Examples

Tree hollow
Rainwater collected in the hollow of the old tree's trunk.
She cupped her hands to scoop water from the hollow between the rocks.
The runner's face showed deep hollows beneath his eyes after the race.
He found shelter in a small hollow beneath the hillside.
Rainwater collected in the hollow left by the fallen rock.
The animal crawled into the hollow at the base of the old tree.

Synonyms

  • cavity
    3.9 2.69/m
    a cavity is an internal space often formed naturally inside a solid, while a hollow is any sunken empty space.
  • chamber
    3.9 15/m
    a chamber is a larger enclosed space like a room within something, while a hollow is a small or sunken empty area.
  • pocket
    5.5 4.85/m
    a pocket is a small enclosed hollow inside material or rock, while a hollow can be larger and more open.
  • hole
    1.0 82/m
    a hole is an opening or gap through something, while a hollow is an internal sunken space that may not go through.
  • recess
    4.3 0.98/m
    a recess is a shallow space set back into a surface, while a hollow is any cavity or sunken area inside something.
  • pit
    5.3 3.96/m
    a pit is a small deep depression in a surface or ground, while a hollow is a general internal sunken space.
  • depression
    5.3 1.55/m
    a depression is a wide sunken area in land or surface, while a hollow is any cavity or smaller internal space.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bulge - a rounded swelling or protuberance that distends something
  • synonym: cavity - an empty space within a solid object
  • synonym: depression - a sunken place or area
  • antonym: mound - a raised area of earth or material, opposite of a hollow
