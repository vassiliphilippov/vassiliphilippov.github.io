fustiness

noun
old-fashioned quality
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌstinəs / (AmE), / ˈfʌstinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being old-fashioned, overly formal, or resistant to change in ideas or behavior

Examples

Fustiness of old rules
The fustiness of the club's rules discouraged younger members from joining.
Critics complained about the fustiness of the museum's outdated exhibits.
He tried to modernize the office and get rid of its fustiness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: modernity - the quality of being modern or up-to-date
  • synonym: staleness - the quality of being no longer fresh or interesting
