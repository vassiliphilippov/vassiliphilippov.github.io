dhole

noun
wild Asian pack-hunting dog
7.8
Pronunciation: / doʊl / (AmE), / dəʊl / (BrE)
a wild dog native to the forests of central and southeast Asia, known for hunting in packs

Examples

A pack of dholes
A pack of dholes was spotted chasing a deer through the dense forest.
The dhole population is threatened by habitat loss and competition with other predators.
Unlike wolves, dholes have a distinctive whistling call to communicate within their pack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wild dog - a nondomesticated dog, especially one living and hunting in the wild
