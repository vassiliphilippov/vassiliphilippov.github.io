Engelmann spruce

noun
a species of spruce native to the Rockies and British Columbia
Pronunciation: / ˈɛŋɡəlˌmæn ˌsprus / (AmE), / ˈɛŋ(ɡ)əlˌman ˌspruːs / (BrE)
a tall spruce tree (Picea engelmannii) native to the Rocky Mountains and British Columbia, with blue-green needles and a sharply conic crown; its wood is used for rough lumber and boxes

Examples

Engelmann spruce wood
Carpenters use Engelmann spruce wood for making boxes and rough lumber.
The Engelmann spruce is commonly found in high-altitude forests of the Rocky Mountains.
We identified a stand of Engelmann spruce by its distinctive blue-green needles.
