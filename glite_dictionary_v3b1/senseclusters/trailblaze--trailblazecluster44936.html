trailblaze

verb
to create a new path
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪlˌbleɪz / (AmE), / ˈtreɪlˌbleɪz / (BrE)
to make or mark a new path through wild or unexplored land, often by clearing obstacles or marking the route

Examples

To trailblaze a path
The explorers set out to trailblaze a route through the thick jungle.
Volunteers worked together to trailblaze a new hiking path in the national park.
It took weeks for the team to trailblaze their way across the rugged terrain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follow - to go after or use a path already made by others
  • synonym: pioneer - to be among the first to explore or develop a new area or idea
