clochan

noun
Irish stone hut
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊxɑn / (AmE), / ˈklɒxən / (BrE)
a small, round, dry-stone hut with a corbelled roof, traditionally found in Ireland

Examples

Ancient clochan hut
The ancient clochan stood alone on the windswept hillside.
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved clochan near the coastal cliffs.
Tourists admired the unique structure of the old clochan during their visit to Ireland.
