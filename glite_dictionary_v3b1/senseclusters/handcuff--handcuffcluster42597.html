handcuff

verb
fasten someone's wrists with handcuffs
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌkʌf / (AmE), / ˈhandkʌf /; / ˈhændˌkʌf / (BrE)
to put handcuffs on someone to restrain them

Examples

To handcuff a suspect
Security guards handcuffed the man after he tried to escape.
She was handcuffed to the chair during the intense interrogation scene.
The police handcuffed the suspect before placing him in the patrol car.
After the arrest, the officer handcuffed the man behind his back.
She watched as they handcuffed the suspect and read him his rights.
The police had to handcuff the suspect to ensure everyone's safety.

  • antonym: unbind - to release from restraints or bonds
