bubble

noun
small pocket of gas in liquid or solid
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌb.əl / (AmE), / ˈbʌb.əl / (BrE)
a small, round pocket of gas, such as air or carbon dioxide, surrounded by liquid or solid

Examples

A soap bubble
A single bubble rose slowly to the surface of the water.
The child giggled as she popped a bubble with her finger.
When you pour soda, you can see bubbles forming along the glass.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: balloon - a large rubber bag filled with gas, especially used as a toy or decoration
  • degree lower: droplet - a small drop of liquid
