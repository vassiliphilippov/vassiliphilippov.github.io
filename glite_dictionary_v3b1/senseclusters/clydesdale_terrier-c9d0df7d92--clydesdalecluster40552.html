Clydesdale terrier

noun
a small Skye terrier breed with silky coat
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪdzˌdeɪl ˈtɛriər / (AmE), / ˈklaɪdzdeɪl ˈtɛriə / (BrE)
a breed of small Skye terrier with erect ears and a long, silky coat, developed through selective breeding

Examples

A Clydesdale terrier dog
Her grandmother owned a Clydesdale terrier with a beautiful, flowing coat.
Many dog enthusiasts appreciate the distinctive look of the Clydesdale terrier.
The Clydesdale terrier was once a popular breed in 19th-century Scotland.
