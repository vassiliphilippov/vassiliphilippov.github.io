muskwood

noun
Australian shrub or tree with musky scent
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈmʌskˌwʊd / (BrE)
A shrub or tree of southern and southeastern Australia, noted for its musky scent and creamy-yellow flower heads.

Examples

The muskwood tree
In the botanical garden, several muskwood trees were in full bloom.
The leaves of the muskwood are glossy and have a distinctive musky fragrance.
The perfumer used muskwood in her fragrance for its natural aroma.
