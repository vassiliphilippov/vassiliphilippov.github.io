saffron

noun
valuable spice from crocus stigmas
3.2 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsæfrən / (AmE), / ˈsæfrən / (BrE)
a spice consisting of the dried stigmas of the saffron crocus, valued for its distinctive flavor, aroma, and yellow color, used especially in cooking and dyeing

Examples

Saffron spice in rice
Genuine saffron is expensive because it must be hand-harvested from thousands of flowers.
She carefully measured out the saffron threads before adding them to the paella.
The recipe calls for a pinch of saffron to enhance both color and flavor.
