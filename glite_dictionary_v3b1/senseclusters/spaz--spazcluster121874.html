spaz

verb
act wildly or lose control
Pronunciation: / spæz / (AmE), / spaz / (BrE)
To act in a wildly nervous, clumsy, or panicked way; to lose control of one's behavior temporarily.

Examples

To spaz out
Don't spaz out if things don't go exactly as planned.
He tends to spaz whenever he's under pressure at work.
She started to spaz when she saw the spider crawling on her bed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freak out - to become very agitated or lose control emotionally
