Socinianism

noun
nontrinitarian theology
9.9
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈsɪn.ɪə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /soʊ.ˈsɪn.ɪə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /sə.ˈsɪn.ɪə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /səʊ.ˈsɪn.ɪə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a Christian theological movement or set of doctrines that rejects the Trinity and original sin, emphasizing reason and a literal interpretation of the Bible

Examples

Socinianism Christian movement
Socinianism spread through parts of Europe during the Reformation, challenging traditional Christian doctrines.
Many scholars have studied the influence of Socinianism on later religious movements.
The rejection of the Trinity is a central belief in Socinianism.
The debate centered on whether Socinianism offered a more rational approach to Christian theology.
Many early modern thinkers were influenced by the principles of Socinianism in their writings.
He wrote a detailed essay outlining the main beliefs of Socinianism for his religious studies class.
