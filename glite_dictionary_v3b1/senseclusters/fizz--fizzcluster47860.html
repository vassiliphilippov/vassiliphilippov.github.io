fizz

verb
to become bubbly or foamy
3.0 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / fÉªz / (AmE), / fÉªz / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to become bubbly or produce bubbles of gas, often making a hissing or spluttering sound.

Examples

Tablets fizz in water
As the tablet dissolved in water, it started to fizz and bubble energetically.
The champagne began to fizz as soon as it was poured into the glass.
When you open a can of soda, it will fizz loudly as the gas escapes.

Synonyms

  • effervesce
    8.2 0.03/m
    effervesce is a formal word for making fine gas bubbles and lively action, while fizz is more casual and noisy.
  • bubble
    2.0 1.80/m
    bubble describes a liquid making rising bubbles and a gurgling sound, while fizz often means a sharper hissing or sputtering noise.
  • froth
    3.0 0.65/m
    froth means forming a loose head or mass of small bubbles on top, while fizz focuses on many small bubbles and hissing.
  • foam
    1.7 1.44/m
    foam means becoming thickly covered with stable bubbles like soap or sea foam, while fizz is lighter and makes a quick hiss.
  • sparkle
    3.0 0.34/m
    sparkle shows bright light or tiny rising bubbles that shine, while fizz emphasizes the noisy, bubbly gas and hissing sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubble - to form bubbles or to rise in bubbles; to effervesce
  • antonym: flat - (of a drink) to have lost its bubbles or effervescence
  • synonym: foam - to produce a mass of small bubbles on the surface of a liquid
