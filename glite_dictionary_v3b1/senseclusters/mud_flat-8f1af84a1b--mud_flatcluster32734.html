mud flat

noun
area of muddy land exposed at low tide
4.9 3.4
Pronunciation: / mʌd flæt / (AmE), / mʌd flæt / (BrE)
a stretch of flat, muddy land near an estuary or coast, covered at high tide and exposed at low tide

Examples

The mud flat near coast
At low tide, the mud flat was covered with feeding shorebirds and exposed shellfish.
Many species of plants and animals are specially adapted to live on the mud flat.
The children explored the mud flat collecting interesting stones and crabs.
