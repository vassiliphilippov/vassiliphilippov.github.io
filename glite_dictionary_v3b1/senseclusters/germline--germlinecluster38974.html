germline

noun
cells producing gametes
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɜːmlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːmlaɪn / (BrE)
the cells or genetic material in reproductive cells (eggs or sperm) that can be passed from parents to their offspring

Examples

The germline cells
Mutations in the germline can be passed from parents to their children.
Scientists are exploring how changes in the germline affect inherited diseases.
The germline is distinct from the body's other cells, which do not contribute to reproduction.
Mutations in the germline can be inherited by future generations.
Scientists study the germline to understand how genetic diseases are passed on.
Changes to the germline affect all descendants of an individual.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gamete - a reproductive cell such as an egg or sperm
  • antonym: somatic - relating to the body cells that are not involved in reproduction
  • antonym: somatic cell - any cell of the body except reproductive cells
