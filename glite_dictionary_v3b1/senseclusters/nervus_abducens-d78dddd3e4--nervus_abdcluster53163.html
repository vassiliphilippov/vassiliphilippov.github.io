nervus abducens

noun
the sixth cranial nerve of the eye
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnɜːr.vəs æb.ˈdjuː.sənz/ /; / /ˈnɜːr.vəs æb.ˈdʒuː.sənz/ /; / /ˈnɜːr.vəs æb.ˈdjuː.snz/ (AmE), /ˈnɜː.vəs æb.ˈdjuː.sənz/ /; / /ˈnɜː.vəs æb.ˈdʒuː.sənz/ /; / /ˈnɜː.vəs æb.ˈdjuː.snz/ (BrE)
the sixth cranial nerve; a small motor nerve that controls the lateral rectus muscle of the eye, enabling movement away from the midline

Examples

Nervus abducens nerve
Damage to the nervus abducens can result in double vision and limited lateral eye movement.
During the neurological examination, the doctor tested the function of the patient's nervus abducens.
The nervus abducens controls the muscle that moves the eye outward, away from the nose.
