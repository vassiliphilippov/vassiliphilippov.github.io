Examples

The stylopod bone
The stylopod gives rise to the humerus in the developing arm of a vertebrate embryo.
Scientists study the stylopod to understand how limbs form and evolve in different species.
In the leg, the stylopod eventually becomes the femur during embryonic development.
The humerus is the bone that makes up the stylopod of the forelimb.
In evolutionary biology, the stylopod is studied to understand limb development.
The stylopod connects the shoulder or hip to the rest of the limb in vertebrates.
