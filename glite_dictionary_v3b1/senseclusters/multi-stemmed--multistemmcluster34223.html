multi-stemmed

adjective
having multiple stems (of a plant)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈstɛmd / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈstɛmd / (BrE)
(of a plant or tree) having two or more stems growing from the base or ground level.

Examples

A multi-stemmed plant
Some shrubs are naturally multi-stemmed, while others can be pruned to encourage this growth habit.
The multi-stemmed rose bush provides a fuller appearance in the garden.
We selected a multi-stemmed tree for the courtyard to create a more natural look.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-stemmed - having only one stem
