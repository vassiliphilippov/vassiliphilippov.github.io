use immunity

noun
legal exemption protecting compelled testimony from self-incrimination
8.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / jus …™ÀàmjuÀên…ôti / (AmE), / juÀês …™ÀàmjuÀên…™ti / (BrE)
A legal exemption whereby a witness's compelled testimony, and any evidence derived from it, cannot be used against them in a criminal case, thus removing the risk of self-incrimination.

Examples

Use immunity protection
Because of use immunity, none of his statements could legally be used to prosecute him.
Prosecutors offered use immunity to ensure the suspect spoke without fear of self-incrimination.
The witness was granted use immunity in exchange for her testimony during the investigation.

Synonyms

  • testimonial immunity
    9.5 0.01/m
    testimonial immunity protects a witness's compelled testimony and any evidence derived from it, while use immunity is the legal term for the same protection.
