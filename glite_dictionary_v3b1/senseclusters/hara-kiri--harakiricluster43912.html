hara-kiri

noun
ritual suicide by samurai in Japan

A ritual suicide by self-disembowelment with a sword, traditionally practiced by samurai in Japan to restore or protect honor.

Examples

Japanese hara-kiri ritual
He referred to his resignation as political hara-kiri, suggesting the end of his career.
Some historians have written extensively about the tradition of hara-kiri in feudal Japan.
The disgraced samurai committed hara-kiri to preserve his family's honor.

Synonyms

  • harakiri
    9.9 0.06/m
    harakiri is the common English spelling for the samurai ritual of self-disembowelment, while hara-kiri is the hyphenated form.
  • harikari
    9.9 0.00/m
    harikari is a less common Romanized form seen in some texts for samurai ritual, while hara-kiri names the same act.
  • seppuku
    9.9 0.15/m
    seppuku is the formal Japanese term used in history books for ritual self-disembowelment, while hara-kiri is the Western name.
