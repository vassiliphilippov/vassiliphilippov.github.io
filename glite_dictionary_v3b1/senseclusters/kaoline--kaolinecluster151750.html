kaoline

noun
fine white clay used in industry
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪəʊˌlaɪn / (BrE)
A fine, usually white clay formed by the weathering of aluminous minerals, used in ceramics, paper, and as an absorbent.

Examples

Fine kaoline clay
Manufacturers often use kaoline as a filler in high-quality paper products.
The absorbent properties of kaoline make it valuable in various industrial processes.
The artist mixed kaoline with water to create a smooth porcelain glaze.
