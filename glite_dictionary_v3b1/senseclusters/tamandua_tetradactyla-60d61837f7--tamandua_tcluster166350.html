Tamandua tetradactyla

noun
species of small anteater from South and Central America
9.9
Pronunciation: / təˌmænduə ˌtɛtrəˈdæktɪlə / (AmE), / təˌmændjʊə ˌtɛtrəˈdaktɪlə / (BrE)
a small, toothless species of anteater with a prehensile tail and four-clawed forelimbs, native to tropical South and Central America

Examples

The Tamandua tetradactyla uses its long tongue to extract ants from nests in the rainforest.
Unlike the giant anteater, the Tamandua tetradactyla is much smaller and more arboreal.
