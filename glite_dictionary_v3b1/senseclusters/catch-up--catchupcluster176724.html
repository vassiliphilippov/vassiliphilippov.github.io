catch-up

noun
making up lost progress
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkætʃ.ʌp/ /; / /ˈkætʃ.əp/ (AmE), /ˈkætʃ.ʌp/ /; / /ˈkætʃ.əp/ (BrE)
a period or activity in which someone tries to reach the same level as others after falling behind

Examples

A catch-up session
After missing several weeks of school, he needed a catch-up session to review the material.
The company scheduled a catch-up meeting to discuss unfinished projects from last month.
Many students attended catch-up classes during the holidays to improve their grades.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delay - a period of being behind schedule or late
  • synonym: recovery - the process of returning to a normal state after a setback
