asterixis

noun
flapping hand tremor
Pronunciation: / ˌæstəˈrɪksɪs / (AmE), / ˌæstəˈrɪksɪs / (BrE)
a sudden, brief loss of muscle control, often seen as a flapping movement of the hands when the arms are extended

Examples

asterixis hand movement
The doctor noticed asterixis when the patient held out his hands.
Liver disease can sometimes cause asterixis in affected individuals.
The presence of asterixis helped the neurologist diagnose the underlying condition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: myoclonus - sudden, brief, involuntary muscle jerks
  • synonym: tremor - an involuntary shaking or trembling movement
