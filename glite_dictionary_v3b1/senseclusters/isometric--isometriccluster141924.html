isometric

adjective
muscle contraction with no length change
4.7 6.2
Pronunciation: /aɪ.sə.ˈme.trɪk/ /; / /aɪ.soʊ.ˈme.trɪk/ (AmE), /aɪ.səʊ.ˈme.trɪk/ /; / /aɪ.sə.ˈme.trɪk/ (BrE)
(of muscular contraction) involving increased tension without change in muscle length.

Examples

Isometric exercise
During an isometric contraction, the muscle does not visibly change length despite increased tension.
Holding a plank position is an example of an isometric exercise.
Physical therapists often include isometric movements to build strength without joint motion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isotonic - muscular contraction in which tension remains the same while length changes
