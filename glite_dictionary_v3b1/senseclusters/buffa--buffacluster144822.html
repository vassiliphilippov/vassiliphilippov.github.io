buffa

adjective
comic opera style
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːfə / (AmE), / ˈbʊfə / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of comic opera, especially in the Italian tradition

Examples

Buffa style opera
The soprano excelled in buffa roles that required both humor and vocal skill.
He preferred the lighthearted atmosphere of buffa operas to more serious performances.
Many 18th-century Italian composers wrote popular buffa works for the stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comic - intended to be funny or amusing
  • antonym: seria - relating to serious opera, especially in the Italian tradition
