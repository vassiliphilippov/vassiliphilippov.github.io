intertie

noun
power system connection
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.tər.taɪ/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.tə.taɪ/ (BrE)
a connection that links two or more power systems or electrical networks to allow the transfer of electricity

Examples

A power intertie
The new intertie improved electricity reliability between the two regions.
Engineers inspected the intertie to ensure stable power flow during peak demand.
A failure in the intertie caused a temporary blackout in both connected cities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isolation - the state of being separated from other systems
