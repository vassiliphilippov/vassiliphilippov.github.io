radical

noun
group of atoms within a molecule (chemistry)
6.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.dɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈræ.dɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
(chemistry) a group of atoms, or an atom with unpaired electrons, that acts as a unit in chemical reactions and is often highly reactive

Examples

A free radical
A hydroxyl radical can react rapidly with other molecules.
Some chemical reactions involve the formation of a free radical.
The methyl radical is commonly found in organic chemistry.
Scientists study how radicals interact with other molecules during cellular metabolism.
The presence of free radicals in the atmosphere can lead to chemical reactions that pollute the air.
Vitamin C helps neutralize radicals before they can damage your cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: free radical - a radical, especially one formed in the body, that can cause cell damage
  • synonym: functional group - A group of atoms responsible for the characteristic reactions of a compound
