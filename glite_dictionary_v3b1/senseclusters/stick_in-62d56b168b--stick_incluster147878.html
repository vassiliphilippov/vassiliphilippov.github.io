stick in

verb
insert or include casually
Pronunciation: / stɪk ɪn / (AmE), / stɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to add or insert something, especially casually or unexpectedly, into speech, writing, or a situation

Examples

To stick in a comment
He tried to stick in a joke during the serious presentation.
If you stick in one more comment, the meeting will run over time.
She managed to stick in a quick thank-you at the end of her remarks.

Related Terms
