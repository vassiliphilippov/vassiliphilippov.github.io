tackle

verb
to address or attempt something challenging
Pronunciation: / ˈtækəl / (AmE), / ˈtæk(ə)l /; / ˈtækəl / (BrE)
to make a determined effort to deal with a difficult task or problem

Examples

To tackle a problem
She decided to tackle the most demanding project first.
The company plans to tackle climate change with a series of new initiatives.
We need to tackle this issue as soon as possible.
After a short break, she was ready to tackle the project with renewed energy.
He felt overwhelmed at first but eventually began to tackle the project confidently.
The team decided to tackle the project in small, manageable steps.

Related Terms

  • synonym: address - to deal with or discuss a problem or issue
  • antonym: avoid - to keep away from or not deal with something
  • synonym: confront - to face up to and deal with (a problem or difficult situation)
  • synonym: take on - to accept a responsibility or challenge
