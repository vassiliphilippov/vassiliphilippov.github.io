pint

noun
US dry measure (0.5 quart; 33.6 in³)
Pronunciation: / paɪnt / (AmE), / paɪnt / (BrE)
A unit of dry measurement in the United States equal to 0.5 quart or approximately 33.6 cubic inches.

Examples

A pint of berries
A pint of fresh berries is needed for this recipe.
Each container holds exactly one US dry pint of rice.
The farmer sold flour in one pint bags at the market.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cup - a US unit of dry or liquid measure equal to 0.5 pint
  • degree higher: quart - a unit of liquid or dry capacity equal to two pints
