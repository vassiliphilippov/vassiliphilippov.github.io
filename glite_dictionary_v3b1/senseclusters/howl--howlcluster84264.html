howl

noun
loud emotional cry or sound
5.8 1.7
Pronunciation: / haʊl / (AmE), / haʊl / (BrE)
a long, loud, emotional cry or sound, especially like that of a dog, wolf, or something similar

Examples

A howl of pain
A sudden howl of pain broke the silence of the night.
The movie audience erupted in howls of laughter at the joke.
Their protests were met with a howl of outrage from the crowd.
A distant howl signaled the arrival of the approaching storm.
The howl of the hound sent chills down my spine.
We heard the howl of a wolf echoing through the night.
A distant howl echoed through the empty streets, sending shivers down her spine.
Suddenly, a howl rose from the wolves in the woods, breaking the silent night.
The howl of the wind kept us awake all night.
