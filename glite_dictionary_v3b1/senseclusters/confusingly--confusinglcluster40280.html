confusingly

adverb
in a way that causes confusion
2.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuːzɪŋli / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzɪŋli / (BrE)
In a manner that causes confusion or is difficult to understand.

Examples

Used confusingly often
Several streets are confusingly similar in name, leading to frequent delivery mistakes.
She confusingly switched topics in the middle of the conversation.
The instructions were confusingly written, making it hard to assemble the furniture.
