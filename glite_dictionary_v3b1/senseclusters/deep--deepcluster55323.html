deep

adjective
clever and secretive (about plans or actions)
Pronunciation: / dip / (AmE), / diːp / (BrE)
(of a plan or person) exhibiting great cunning or secrecy; intricate and often hidden in intention.

Examples

Deep plan
She is known for her deep tactics, always staying several steps ahead of her rivals.
The novel revolves around a deep conspiracy involving several influential families.
There was a deep scheme behind the company's sudden change in direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scheming - having a tendency to make secret plans, often with the intent to deceive or achieve an end
  • antonym: shallow - simple and lacking depth of thought or character; not complex or secretive
