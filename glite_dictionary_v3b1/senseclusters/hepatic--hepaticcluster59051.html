hepatic

noun
a type of nonvascular plant; liverwort
Pronunciation: / hɪˈpætɪk / (AmE), / hɪˈpatɪk / (BrE)
a nonvascular plant of the class Hepaticopsida, commonly known as a liverwort, typically found in moist environments and resembling mosses or seaweeds

Examples

A rare hepatic plant
Botanists studied the reproduction methods of the rare hepatic species.
Some hepatic varieties thrive in shaded woodland environments.
The damp rocks were covered in hepatic and other tiny plants.
