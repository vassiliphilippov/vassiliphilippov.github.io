esse

noun
essence or existence
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛseɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛseɪ / (BrE)
the essential nature or existence of something, especially as discussed in philosophy

Examples

Philosophical esse
In medieval philosophy, the concept of esse was central to discussions about God and creation.
The professor explained that esse refers to the very act of being itself.
Some philosophers debate whether esse can be separated from the qualities of a thing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: being - the state or fact of existing
  • synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • antonym: nonexistence - the state of not existing
