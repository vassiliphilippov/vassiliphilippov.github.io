Aristotelian logic

noun
the traditional logic system of Aristotle
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌærəstəˈtiliən ˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌarɪstəˈtiːlɪən ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
the traditional system of logical reasoning based on the syllogistic principles established by Aristotle and further developed during the Middle Ages

Examples

Aristotelian logic system
He wrote a dissertation on the limitations of Aristotelian logic in scientific reasoning.
Many medieval scholars were well-versed in Aristotelian logic and its applications in philosophy.
The class compared Aristotelian logic to modern symbolic logic.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: formal logic - the branch of logic concerned with the structure of arguments
  • synonym: syllogism - a form of reasoning in Aristotelian logic where a conclusion follows from two premises
