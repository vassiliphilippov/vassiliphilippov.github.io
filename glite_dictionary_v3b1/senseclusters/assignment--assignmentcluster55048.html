assignment

noun
document transferring a right or property
6.6 4.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsaɪn.mənt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsaɪn.mənt/ (BrE)
the legal transfer of a right, interest, or property to another person

Examples

Legal assignment
Any assignment of these rights must be made in writing and signed by both parties.
He received an assignment transferring all rights in the patent to his new company.
The assignment of the leasehold was completed last week by the lawyers.
An assignment of copyright must be made in writing according to the law.
He prepared the assignment to officially transfer the lease to the new tenant.
The property changed hands through an assignment executed by both parties.
