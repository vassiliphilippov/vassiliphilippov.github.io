implosive

adjective
inward movement
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ɪmˈpləʊsɪv / (BrE)
relating to a sound or process involving inward movement, especially in speech sounds produced by drawing air in

Examples

Implosive consonant sound
The linguist explained how an implosive consonant is produced in certain African languages.
The engineer described the implosive force that caused the structure to collapse inward.
Some rare speech sounds are classified as implosive because of the way air is drawn in.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explosive - relating to outward movement or bursting out
