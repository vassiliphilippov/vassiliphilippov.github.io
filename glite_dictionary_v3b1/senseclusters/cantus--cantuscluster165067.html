cantus

noun
fixed melody in polyphony
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.təs / (AmE), / ˈkan.tʊs / (BrE)
the main or fixed melody in a piece of polyphonic music, often from plainchant

Examples

Cantus firmus melody
The medieval composer based his motet on a well-known cantus from the church tradition.
In Renaissance music, the cantus often appears in the highest voice of the composition.
The choir practiced the cantus before adding the other polyphonic parts.
In the choir, the sopranos often carry the cantus while other voices support them.
The composer wrote a beautiful cantus that stands out above the accompaniment.
During rehearsal, the conductor asked the tenors to sing the cantus more clearly.
