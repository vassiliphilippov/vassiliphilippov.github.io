beat

verb
shape by striking repeatedly
7.1 1.98/m
Pronunciation: / bit / (AmE), / biËt / (BrE)
to shape or form (a material) by striking it repeatedly

Examples

To beat metal
Goldsmiths often beat gold into thin sheets called gold leaf.
The artisan beat the metal into a fine bowl.
They beat the iron while it was hot to create the desired shape.

Synonyms

  • hammer
    1.6 11/m
    hammer means shaping material by striking with a tool, while beat means forming a material by repeated blows.
  • pound
    1.3 11/m
    pound means hitting or crushing something hard and loudly, while beat means shaping material by repeated, controlled strikes.
