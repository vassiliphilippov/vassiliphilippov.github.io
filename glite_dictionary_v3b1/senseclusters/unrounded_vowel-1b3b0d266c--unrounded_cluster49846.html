unrounded vowel

noun
vowel sound made without lip rounding
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈraʊndɪd ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈraʊndɪd ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
A vowel sound in which the lips are not rounded, typically remaining slack or drawn back.

Examples

An unrounded vowel sound
In some languages, every vowel has both a rounded and an unrounded vowel counterpart.
Phonetics students must learn to distinguish between a rounded and an unrounded vowel when analyzing speech.
The sound of the English 'ee' as in 'feet' is an example of an unrounded vowel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rounded vowel - a vowel sound in which the lips are rounded
