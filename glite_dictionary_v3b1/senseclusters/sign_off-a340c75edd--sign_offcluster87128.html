sign off

verb
end a broadcast; go off the air
3.8
Pronunciation: / saɪn ˈɔf /; / saɪn ɑf / (AmE), / saɪn ˈɒf /; / saɪn ɒf / (BrE)
to end a broadcast, communication, or work session, often with a closing remark or farewell

Examples

Sign off the show
After delivering the last news report, the anchors signed off for the evening.
The station will sign off at midnight for maintenance.
We'll be signing off now, but join us again tomorrow morning.
The radio host will sign off at midnight after playing the last song.
Before leaving the video call, she remembered to sign off and thank everyone.
After a long day, the team decided to sign off and continue the project tomorrow.
