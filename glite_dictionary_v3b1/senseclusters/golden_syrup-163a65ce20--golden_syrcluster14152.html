golden syrup

noun
pale, sweet syrup used in cooking
4.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈgoʊl.dən ˈsɪ.rəp/ /; / /ˈgoʊl.dən ˈɜːr.əp/ (AmE), /ˈgəʊl.dən ˈsɪ.rəp/ (BrE)
a thick, pale golden-colored syrup made from sugar cane or sugar beet, used especially in baking and desserts

Examples

Golden syrup in baking
British flapjacks are traditionally made with golden syrup instead of honey or molasses.

The recipe calls for two tablespoons of golden syrup to add a rich flavor to the cake.
