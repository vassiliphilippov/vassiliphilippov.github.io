incapacitation

noun
making unable to act
7.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kə.pæs.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kə.pə.sɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.kə.pæs.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kə.pə.sɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of making someone unable to move, act, or think clearly, often temporarily and typically by medical means.

Examples

Medical incapacitation
The incapacitation of the suspect was necessary to ensure the safety of the officers.
Temporary incapacitation can result from certain medications given before surgery.
After the accident, his incapacitation lasted for several weeks while he recovered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disablement - the state of being made unable to function
  • antonym: empowerment - the process of becoming able or enabled
  • synonym: immobilization - the act of preventing movement
