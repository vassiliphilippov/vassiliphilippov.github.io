spreadability

noun
degree of being spread
6.1
Pronunciation: /spred.ə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /spred.ə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the degree to which something, such as a disease, idea, or information, can be spread among people or through a network

Examples

Spreadability of a virus
Scientists are studying the spreadability of the new virus to understand how quickly it can move through a population.
The spreadability of online misinformation makes it difficult to control rumors once they start.
Marketers consider the spreadability of a campaign when planning how to reach a wider audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagiousness - the quality of being able to be transmitted from one person to another
  • antonym: containment - the act of preventing something from spreading
  • synonym: transmissibility - the ability of something to be passed or spread to others
