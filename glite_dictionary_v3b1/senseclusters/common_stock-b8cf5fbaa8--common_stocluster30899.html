common stock

noun
ordinary shares in a corporation
4.0 0.64/m

Shares representing ownership in a corporation, entitling holders to a share of profits and voting rights, as distinct from preferred stock.

Examples

Common stock shares
She invested in common stock to gain voting rights at the annual meeting.
The company issued millions of new common stocks to raise capital.
Unlike preferred shareholders, owners of common stock may receive varying dividends depending on company performance.

Synonyms

  • common shares
    3.8 0.15/m

  • ordinary shares
    5.2 0.02/m
    ordinary shares are used mainly in British companies to mean voting ownership without special dividend rights, while common stock is the US term.
