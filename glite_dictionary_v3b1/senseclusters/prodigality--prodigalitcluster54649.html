prodigality

noun
reckless or wasteful extravagance
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑː.dɪˈɡæl.ə.ti /; / ˌprɑːdɪˈɡæləti / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.dɪˈɡæl.ə.ti /; / ˌprɒdɪˈɡælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or practice of spending money or resources freely and recklessly; wasteful extravagance

Examples

His prodigality wasted money
Despite his early prodigality, he later learned the value of saving.
Her prodigality eventually led to financial ruin.
The old man's prodigality was legendary among his family and friends.
Despite his father's warnings, his prodigality seemed to know no bounds.
Her prodigality soon led to financial difficulties for the entire family.
The novel criticizes the prodigality of the wealthy elite during the period.

  • antonym: frugality - the quality of being economical with money; thriftiness
