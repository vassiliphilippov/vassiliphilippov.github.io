emphasize

verb
give special importance to
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmfəˌsaɪz /; / ˈɛm.fəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈemfəsaɪz /; / ˈɛmfəsaɪz /; / ˈɛm.fə.saɪz / (BrE)
to give special importance or attention to something; to make something stand out or be noticed

Examples

To emphasize a point
In her speech, she emphasized the need for more community involvement.
The teacher emphasized the importance of submitting assignments on time.
We should emphasize safety procedures during our training sessions.
He emphasized his disappointment with the results in his closing remarks.
Teachers often emphasize the importance of reading outside the classroom.
The manager used charts to emphasize his main points during the meeting.
He used bold text to emphasise the key points in his report.
She raised her voice to emphasise that she was serious.
The teacher tried to emphasise the importance of honesty during her lesson.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important
  • synonym: emphasize - American spelling of 'emphasise', meaning to give special importance or attention to something
  • synonym: highlight - to make something stand out as important or noticeable
  • synonym: stress - to give particular importance or significance to something
  • synonym: underline - to mark or highlight something as important, often by drawing a line beneath it
  • antonym: understate - to describe or represent something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
