respectably

adverb
in a decent or reputable manner
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɛktəbli / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛktəbli / (BrE)
in a way that is proper, decent, or worthy of respect

Examples

To live respectably
Although they struggled financially, they managed to live respectably in a modest home.
He conducted himself respectably in court, earning the judge's admiration for his composure.
The children dressed respectably for the school ceremony, wearing clean shirts and polished shoes.
Despite his lack of experience, he performed respectably in the competition.
She managed to recover respectably after the initial setback.
The team finished respectably in the league, taking fifth place overall.

  • antonym: improperly - in a manner not conforming to accepted standards
  • antonym: poorly - to an unsatisfactory or bad degree
