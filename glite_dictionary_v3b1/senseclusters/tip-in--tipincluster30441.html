tip-in

noun
basketball shot tapping in a rebound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪp ɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɪp ɪn / (BrE)
(in basketball) a shot scored by lightly tapping a rebounding ball back into the basket, usually before it touches the ground.

Examples

A tip-in shot
He scored the winning point with a tip-in just before the buzzer.
Her tip-in gave the team a crucial lead in the final minutes.
The center specializes in offensive rebounds and quick tip-ins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: putback - a similar basketball play where a missed shot is immediately scored by a follow-up attempt
