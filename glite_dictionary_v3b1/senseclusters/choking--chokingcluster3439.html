choking

adjective
making breathing difficult (of air or environment)

(of air or an environment) making breathing difficult, especially because of a lack of fresh air or the presence of heat, dust, or smoke.

Examples

Choking smoke
A thick, choking haze filled the valley after the wildfire.
She gasped for breath in the choking atmosphere of the basement.
The choking heat in the crowded subway left everyone drenched in sweat.

Synonyms

  • suffocating
    5.1 0.07/m
    suffocating describes a closed, hot place that makes breathing hard, while choking focuses on air that blocks breathing.
  • smothering
    6.7 0.02/m
    smothering describes heavy, close heat or air that presses on you, while choking highlights lack of fresh air or smoke.
  • stifling
    5.3 0.43/m
    stifling means very hot and oppressive air that makes breathing hard, while choking often implies dust, smoke, or restricted airflow.
  • asphyxiating
    9.6 0.01/m
    asphyxiating refers to causing loss of oxygen and very dangerous suffocation, while choking describes breathing difficulty in an environment.
  • suffocative
    8.6 0.00/m
    suffocative is a rare, more formal word for air that makes breathing hard, while choking is common everyday language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suffocating - making it difficult to breathe due to lack of fresh air
