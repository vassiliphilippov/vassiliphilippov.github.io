choking

adjective
making breathing difficult (of air or environment)
6.0 4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
(of air or an environment) making breathing difficult, especially because of a lack of fresh air or the presence of heat, dust, or smoke.

Examples

Choking smoke
A thick, choking haze filled the valley after the wildfire.
She gasped for breath in the choking atmosphere of the basement.
The choking heat in the crowded subway left everyone drenched in sweat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stifling - oppressively hot and close, making breathing difficult
  • synonym: suffocating - making it difficult to breathe due to lack of fresh air
