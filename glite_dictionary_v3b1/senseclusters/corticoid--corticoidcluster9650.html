corticoid

noun
steroid hormone from adrenal cortex
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtɪˌkɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪkɔɪd / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex or manufactured synthetically, often used as medication to reduce inflammation and suppress immune response

Examples

Topical corticoids
Doctors sometimes recommend topical corticoids for chronic skin conditions.
High-dose corticoid therapy was prescribed to manage his severe asthma.
Prolonged use of corticoids can lead to side effects such as osteoporosis.
