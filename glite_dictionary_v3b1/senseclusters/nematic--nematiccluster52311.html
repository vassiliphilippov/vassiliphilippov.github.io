nematic

adjective
liquid crystal phase
9.9
Pronunciation: /nɪ.ˈmæt.ɪk/ /; / /nə.ˈmæt.ɪk/ (AmE), /nɪ.ˈmæt.ɪk/ /; / /nə.ˈmæt.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to a phase of liquid crystals in which the molecules are aligned parallel but not arranged in layers

Examples

Nematic phase display
The nematic phase is commonly used in liquid crystal displays for its optical properties.
Scientists observed a transition from the nematic to the smectic phase under certain conditions.
A nematic liquid crystal can change its alignment when an electric field is applied.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isotropic - having properties that are the same in all directions; not aligned
  • antonym: smectic - relating to a liquid crystal phase with molecules arranged in layers
