mature

verb
grow old or older
Pronunciation: / məˈtʃʊr /; / məˈtʃʊr, məˈtʃɜr, məˈtjʊr /; / məˈtʃʊr, məˈtʃɚ / (AmE), / məˈtjʊə /; / məˈtjʊə, məˈtʃʊə / (BrE)
to develop or become fully grown, ripe, or perfected (of people, things, food, ideas, etc.)

Examples

To mature over time
As she matured, her hair turned silver and her movements slowed.
As turtles mature, their shells become thicker and more protective.
Many trees mature gradually over several decades.
Experienced winemakers know how to mature a fine wine to its best flavor.
He uses patience and guidance to mature the young athletes' skills.
The greenhouse helps mature the tomatoes before they are shipped to market.
Before announcing the plan, he wanted to mature his thoughts on every aspect.
She took several days to mature her ideas before presenting them to the team.
The proposal had been carefully matured in his mind over months of reflection.
