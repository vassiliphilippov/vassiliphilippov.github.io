potassium hydrogen tartrate

noun
chemical salt used in baking, cream of tartar
Pronunciation: / pəˈtæsiəm ˈhaɪdrədʒən tɑrˌtreɪt / (AmE), / pəˈtæsɪəm ˈhaɪdrɪdʒən ˈtɑːtreɪt / (BrE)
a white, crystalline salt used especially in baking powder and as a food additive, also known as cream of tartar

Examples

Potassium hydrogen tartrate in baking
Baking powder contains potassium hydrogen tartrate as a key ingredient to aid leavening.
The chef suggested adding potassium hydrogen tartrate to the recipe for fluffier pancakes.
When making meringues, a pinch of potassium hydrogen tartrate helps to stabilize the egg whites.
