descender

noun
part of a letter extending below baseline
7.9 6.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsen.dər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsen.də/ (BrE)
the part of a lowercase letter (like 'g', 'j', or 'y') that extends below the line other letters sit on, or a letter with such a part

Examples

Letter descender
In designing a new font, careful attention was paid to the shape of each descender.
Lowercase 'y' and 'p' are examples of letters with descenders in most typefaces.
The letter 'g' has a descender that extends well below the other letters on the line.
In typesetting, attention to descender length is important for readability.
Lowercase 'y' and 'p' both contain a descender.
The letter 'g' is a descender in most fonts.

  • antonym: ascender - the part of a lowercase letter that rises above the main body of the letter
