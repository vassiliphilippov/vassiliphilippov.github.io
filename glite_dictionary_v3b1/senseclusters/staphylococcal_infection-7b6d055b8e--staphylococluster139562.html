staphylococcal infection

noun
infection with staphylococcus bacteria
7.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /stæf.ə.lə.ˈkɑː.kəl ɪn.ˈfek.ʃən/ (AmE), /stæf.ə.lə.ˈkɒk.əl ɪn.ˈfek.ʃən/ (BrE)
An infection caused by staphylococcus bacteria, typically characterized by the formation of abscesses, pus, or other localized symptoms.

Examples

Staphylococcal infection treatment
Antibiotics are commonly used to treat staphylococcal infections, but some strains have become resistant.
Hospital workers must take precautions to avoid spreading staphylococcal infection among vulnerable patients.
The patient was diagnosed with a staphylococcal infection after developing a painful abscess on his arm.
