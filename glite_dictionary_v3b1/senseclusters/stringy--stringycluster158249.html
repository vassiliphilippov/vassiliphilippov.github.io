stringy

adjective
lean and sinewy (body or meat)
9.6 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋi / (BrE)
(of a person's body or meat) lean and sinewy, with prominent muscles or little fat.

Examples

Stringy arms
She was tall and stringy, with wiry muscles from years of working outdoors.
The old man's stringy arms showed the hard life he had lived.
This chicken is too stringy to eat—it feels tough and chewy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: scrawny - unattractively thin and bony
  • synonym: sinewy - lean and muscular, with strong muscles
