bookplate

noun
decorative label showing book owner's name
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkˌpleɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʊkˌpleɪt / (BrE)
a decorative label, usually pasted inside the front cover of a book, indicating its owner's name

Examples

A bookplate label
A handwritten bookplate inside the cover revealed that the novel belonged to Sarah Green.
He collects bookplates from the nineteenth century as a hobby.
The library encourages people to donate by offering a personalized bookplate in each sponsored book.
