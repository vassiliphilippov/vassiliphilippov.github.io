reflex response

noun
instinctive automatic response to stimulus
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌflɛks rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ˈriːflɛks rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
an automatic, instinctive response to a stimulus, not learned or controlled consciously

Examples

A reflex response to danger
Blinking when something gets close to your eyes is a natural reflex response.
Infants have several automatic reflex responses present from birth.
The doctor tested her knee-jerk reflex response with a small hammer.
