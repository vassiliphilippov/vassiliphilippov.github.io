Ordovician

noun
geological period after Cambrian
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.də.ˈvɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ɔː.də.ˈvɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the geological period between the Cambrian and Silurian, approximately 485 to 444 million years ago, marked by diverse marine life

Examples

The Ordovician period
Geologists studied rock layers dating back to the early Ordovician.
Many fossils found in limestone are from the Ordovician period.
The Ordovician was a time of great diversification in marine life.
