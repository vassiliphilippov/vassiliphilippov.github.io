dazzle

verb
amaze or impress greatly
Pronunciation: / ˈdæzəl / (AmE), / ˈdæzəl / (BrE)
to impress or amaze someone greatly, especially by being extremely skillful, beautiful, clever, or brilliant

Examples

To dazzle the audience
Her performance will dazzle even the most experienced critics.
The scientist's brilliance dazzled his peers at the conference.
They hoped to dazzle the judges with their innovative design.
Her innovative designs continue to dazzle the fashion world.
The magician's tricks never fail to dazzle his audience.
The new smartphone features are sure to dazzle customers.
