bark up the wrong tree

verb
make a mistaken or misguided effort
7.7
Pronunciation: / bɑrk ʌp ðə rɔŋ tri / (AmE), / bɑːk ʌp ðə rɒŋ triː / (BrE)
to pursue a wrong or misguided course of action; to make a mistake in the focus or direction of one's efforts or accusations

Examples

To bark up the wrong tree
Before you blame your roommate, consider whether you're barking up the wrong tree.
If you think Tom stole your lunch, you're barking up the wrong tree.
The detective soon realized he was barking up the wrong tree with his main suspect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be on the right track - to pursue the correct course of action
  • synonym: be on the wrong track - to pursue a mistaken line of thought or action
  • synonym: misjudge - to make an incorrect judgment or assessment
