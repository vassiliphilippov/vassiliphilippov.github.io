oyster mushroom

noun
an edible mushroom with greyish cap
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɪstɚ ˌmʌʃˌrum / (AmE), / ˈɔɪstə ˌmʌʃˌruːm / (BrE)
an edible mushroom with a soft, greyish cap that grows in shelf-like clusters on dead wood, commonly used in cooking

Examples

Fresh oyster mushroom
Many vegetarian dishes use oyster mushroom as a meat substitute due to its texture.
The recipe calls for sautéed oyster mushrooms to give the dish a delicate flavor.
Wild oyster mushrooms were growing on the old log in the forest.
