lubricator

noun
device that applies oil
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈluː.brɪ.keɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈlʊ.brɪ.keɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈluː.brɪ.keɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈlʊ.brɪ.keɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a device or person that applies or supplies oil or grease to reduce friction between moving parts

Examples

Automatic lubricator device
The mechanic checked the lubricator to ensure the engine parts moved smoothly.
A faulty lubricator can cause machinery to overheat and break down.
Every few months, the lubricator in the factory equipment needs to be refilled.
The mechanic checked the lubricator to make sure the engine was properly maintained.
Modern machines often have an automatic lubricator to keep moving parts running smoothly.
Without a working lubricator, the gears would quickly wear out due to friction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oiler - a device or person that supplies oil to machinery
