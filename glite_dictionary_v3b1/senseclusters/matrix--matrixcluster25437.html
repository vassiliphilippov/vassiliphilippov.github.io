matrix

noun
fine-grained rock surrounding inclusions (geology)
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.trɪks / (BrE)
(Geology) A mass of fine-grained rock in which fossils, crystals, or gems are embedded.

Examples

Rock matrix
A soft limestone matrix encased the delicate fossils.
In this region, the dinosaur bones are preserved within a sandy matrix.
The gems were painstakingly extracted from the surrounding matrix by geologists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: host rock - the rock surrounding a mineral or fossil deposit
