Bactrian

noun
two-humped camel
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbæktriən / (AmE), / ˈbæktriən / (BrE)
a large camel species with two humps, native to Central Asia

Examples

Bactrian camel
The Bactrian is well adapted to the harsh climates of Central Asian deserts.
Unlike the dromedary, the Bactrian has two humps on its back.
Caravans often relied on the strength of the Bactrian to cross long distances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camel - a large, long-necked mammal adapted to desert life
  • antonym: dromedary - a camel species with a single hump
