unrhetorical

adjective
plain or straightforward, not rhetorical
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.rəˈθɔr.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌʌn.rɪˈθɒr.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
(of language or style) not rhetorical; straightforward and lacking ornate or exaggerated expression.

Examples

A unrhetorical style
He delivered his message in a plain, unrhetorical manner, avoiding grandiose language.
Her speech was remarkably unrhetorical, focusing on facts rather than flourish.
The novel’s unrhetorical prose makes the complex subject easy to understand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rhetorical - relating to or using rhetoric; ornate or persuasive in style
