theory of games

noun
study of strategic competition (game theory)
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪri əv ˈɡeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˈɡeɪmz / (BrE)
a branch of mathematics and economics that studies strategic interactions in which the outcome for each participant depends on the actions of all; also known as game theory.

Examples

Theory of games in economics
John Nash made significant contributions to the theory of games in the field of economics.
The theory of games analyzes how participants choose optimal strategies in competitive situations.
Understanding the theory of games can help businesses make better strategic decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: game theory - the study of mathematical models of strategic interaction among rational decision-makers
