Palaquium

noun
genus of tropical trees with milky sap
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɑːkwiəm / (AmE), / pəˈlakwiəm / (BrE)
A large genus of tropical Southeast Asian trees with milky sap and leathery leaves, often valued for their timber or latex.

Examples

Palaquium tree species
Botanists discovered a rare species of Palaquium in the dense forests of Borneo.
The Palaquium genus includes several trees important to local economies for their high-quality wood.
The latex of the Palaquium tree is used to make gutta-percha, a material valued in dentistry and industry.
