naively

adverb
in a naive, innocent way
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /naɪ.ˈiːv.lɪ/ /; / /naɪ.ˈiːv.liː/ (AmE), /naɪ.ˈiːv.lɪ/ /; / /naɪ.ˈiːv.liː/ (BrE)
In a way that shows a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgement; innocently or unsophisticatedly.

Examples

To naively trust
He naively trusted the salesman, believing his every promise.
She naively assumed that everyone at the meeting would agree with her ideas.
The proposal was naively optimistic, overlooking many potential problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cynically - in a cynical manner; with disbelief in sincerity or goodness
  • synonym: innocently - in an innocent manner; without guile or experience
