bank

noun
long ridge or pile (earth, sand, etc.)
Pronunciation: / bæŋk / (AmE), / bæŋk /; / baŋk / (BrE)
a long raised area or slope of earth, sand, snow, or the sloping ground beside water, a road, or a track

Examples

A bank of a river
A bank of fog drifted across the harbor, obscuring the view.
He built a bank of earth to protect the garden from flooding.
We climbed over a bank of snow at the side of the road.
Children were playing along the grassy bank of the stream, searching for frogs.
Floodwaters rose rapidly and spilled over the bank, covering the nearby fields.
We spread our picnic blanket on the bank of the lake and enjoyed the view.
A sharp bank on the highway helps prevent vehicles from skidding during fast turns.
The cyclists struggled as they climbed the steep bank on the final corner.
The race car handled the bank of the track with ease, maintaining its speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camber - a slight upward curve from the center to the edge of a road or track
  • degree higher: cliff - a steep face of rock and earth, typically by the sea or river
  • synonym: drift - a mass of material, such as snow or sand, heaped up by wind or water
  • degree lower: plain - a large area of flat land without significant slope
  • synonym: shore - the land along the edge of a sea, lake, or river
