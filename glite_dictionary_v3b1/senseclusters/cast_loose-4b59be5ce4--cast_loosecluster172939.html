cast loose

verb
release or detach so it is free
6.0
Pronunciation: / kæst lus / (AmE), / kɑːst luːs / (BrE)
to release or detach something so that it is free, especially by unfastening ties or restraints; to set free from limitations or restrictions

Examples

To cast loose the sail
As the storm approached, they hurried to cast loose the sail and change direction.
Before the ship could leave, the crew cast loose the ropes securing it to the dock.
She decided to cast loose from her family's expectations and pursue her own dreams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secure - to fasten or attach firmly
  • synonym: untie - to undo a knot or binding so something is no longer fastened
