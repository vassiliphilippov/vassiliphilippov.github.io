beat

verb
hit repeatedly
Pronunciation: / bit /; / biːt / (AmE), / biːt / (BrE)
to hit or strike someone or something repeatedly, or to make a regular rhythmic sound by striking

Examples

To beat a drum
He angrily beat his fists on the table during the argument.
She beat the rug with a broom to get the dust out.
The rain beat against the windows all night long.
Children clapped and beat on drums to create a lively parade atmosphere.
She beat the rhythm on the table with her fingers as the song played.
The conductor asked the percussionist to beat a steady rhythm during rehearsal.
Her heart beats faster whenever she hears his name.
The boat beat steadily against the waves as the storm intensified.
The music in the club beat through the walls all night long.

  • synonym: drum - to make a rhythmic sound by striking repeatedly

  • synonym: hammer - strike or hit repeatedly with force (of natural forces)
  • synonym: pound - to hit repeatedly with heavy blows
  • synonym: pulsate - to expand and contract with strong regular movements
  • synonym: pulse - a regular throbbing or rhythmic beating, especially in the body
  • antonym: spare - to refrain from inflicting harm or punishment
  • synonym: strike - to hit forcibly and deliberately
  • synonym: tap - to make light repeated sounds by hitting softly
  • synonym: tick - make a regular, short sound like a clock
