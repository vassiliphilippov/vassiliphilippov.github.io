groundwater

noun
water below ground
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that is located beneath the earth's surface in soil and rocks

Examples

Groundwater supply
Farmers often rely on groundwater to irrigate their crops during dry seasons.
Contamination of groundwater can pose serious health risks to nearby communities.
The city installed new wells to access clean groundwater for its residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aquifer - an underground layer of water-bearing rock or material
  • antonym: surface water - water found on the surface of the earth, such as in rivers and lakes
