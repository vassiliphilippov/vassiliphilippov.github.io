caliginous

adjective
dark and gloomy
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪdʒ.ə.nəs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪdʒ.ɪ.nəs / (BrE)
dark, misty, and gloomy; dim or obscure in appearance or atmosphere, often suggesting mystery or melancholy.

Examples

A caliginous gloom
A caliginous gloom settled over the town as the storm clouds gathered overhead.
Her tale took place in a caliginous forest, where daylight barely penetrated the thick fog.
The caliginous alleyways filled the travelers with unease as they navigated the unfamiliar city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting much light; shining
  • synonym: gloomy - dark or poorly lit, making it hard to see; depressing or melancholy
  • synonym: murky - dark and difficult to see through; obscure or unclear
