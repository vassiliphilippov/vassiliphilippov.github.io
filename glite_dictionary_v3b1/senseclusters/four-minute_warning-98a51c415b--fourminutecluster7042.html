four-minute warning

noun
warning given four minutes before an event ends
1.9 4.4
Pronunciation: /fɔːr ˈmɪn.ɪt ˈwɔːr.nɪŋ/ /; / /fɔːr ˈmɪn.ət ˈwɔːr.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /fɔː ˈmɪn.ɪt ˈwɔː.nɪŋ/ /; / /fɔː ˈmɪn.ət ˈwɔː.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
A brief announcement or alert given four minutes before an important event takes place or a period ends, used especially to warn participants or audiences to prepare for the conclusion.

Examples

The four-minute warning
After the four-minute warning, everyone hurried to submit their answers.
The announcer gave a four-minute warning before the final round began.
The team used the four-minute warning to plan their final strategy.
