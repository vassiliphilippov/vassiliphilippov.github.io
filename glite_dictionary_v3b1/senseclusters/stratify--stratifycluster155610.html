stratify

verb
arrange or form into layers
Pronunciation: / ˈstræt.əˌfaɪ /; / ˈstræt̬.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to arrange or form into layers or strata (such as in rocks, soil, or other materials)

Examples

To stratify rock layers
Geologists often stratify rock samples to study the different layers.
Societies can stratify themselves based on class, occupation, or ethnicity.
The data was stratified by age and income before analysis.
In certain conditions, soil can stratify, creating bands of organic and mineral material.
Over millions of years, sediments stratify into distinct geological layers.
The mixture will gradually stratify if left undisturbed in the container.
