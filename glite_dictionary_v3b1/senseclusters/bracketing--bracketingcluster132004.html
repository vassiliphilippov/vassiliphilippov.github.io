bracketing

noun
photographic exposure technique
Pronunciation: / ˈbrækɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbrækɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the technique of taking several photographs of the same scene at different exposures or settings to ensure the best result

Examples

Bracketing camera shots
The photographer used bracketing to make sure at least one photo had perfect lighting.
In challenging lighting conditions, bracketing can help capture all the details in a scene.
Many modern cameras offer automatic bracketing to assist with difficult exposures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auto exposure - a camera setting that automatically determines the correct exposure
  • synonym: exposure compensation - adjusting the exposure to make a photo lighter or darker
