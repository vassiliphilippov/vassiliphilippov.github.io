appeasement

noun
the act of pacifying by giving in to demands
7.4 1.47/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpizm…ônt / (AmE), / …ôÀàpiÀêzm…ônt / (BrE)
the act of calming or pacifying someone by acceding to their demands, often to avoid conflict

Examples

An appeasement policy
Offering extra privileges was seen as an act of appeasement to calm the protesting students.
Some historians argue that appeasement only encouraged further demands.
The government was criticized for its policy of appeasement toward the aggressor nation.

Synonyms

  • placation
    8.5 0.00/m
    placation soothes anger by yielding to someone's demands, while appeasement often implies giving in to avoid a larger conflict.
  • pacification
    7.7 0.01/m
    pacification often describes calming whole groups or regions, while appeasement means giving in to demands to avoid conflict.
  • mollification
    8.4 0.02/m
    mollification refers to softly calming someone's feelings, while appeasement usually involves yielding to demands to prevent conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conciliation - the action of stopping someone from being angry; placation
  • antonym: provocation - the action of provoking or inciting; causing anger or another reaction
