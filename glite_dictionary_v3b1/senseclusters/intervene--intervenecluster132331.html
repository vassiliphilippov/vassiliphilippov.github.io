intervene

verb
to occur or be situated between
8.9 4.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈviːn/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈviːn/ (BrE)
to happen or exist between events, periods, or objects, often interrupting them

Examples

A pause intervened
A long silence intervened between their conversations.
Many years intervened before the two old friends met again.
Only a few months intervened between the announcement and the launch of the product.
A severe drought intervened between the two rainy seasons.
Several years intervened before the next major outbreak occurred.
The construction of the dam intervened between the floods of 1955 and 1962.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to persist without interruption (opposite sense)
  • synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of an event or process
  • synonym: separate - to set or be between so as to keep apart; to divide
