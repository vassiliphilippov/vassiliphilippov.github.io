Synonyms

  • lay claim
    4.4 1/m
    lay claim is a phrase used to assert ownership of something, while claim often refers to any demand or assertion of a right.
  • assert
    3.9 30/m
    assert is to state something firmly, often to defend a right or opinion, while claim can mean making a formal demand.
  • demand
    2.7 60/m
    demand is to ask forcefully for something, often expecting action, while claim can mean asserting a right without strong pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assert - to state or declare firmly
  • synonym: demand - to ask for forcefully or as a right
  • antonym: forsake - to give up, renounce, or abandon a claim
  • antonym: relinquish - to give up or surrender a claim or right
