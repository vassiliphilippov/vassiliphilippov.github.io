nonchalant

adjective
calm, unconcerned
7.5 1.07/m
Pronunciation: / Àån…ëÀên É…ôÀàl…ënt / (AmE), / Àån…ín É…ôÀàl…ëÀênt / (BrE)
behaving in a calm, relaxed way that shows you are not worried or anxious; showing a lack of concern or enthusiasm

Examples

A nonchalant attitude
Despite the chaos around him, he remained nonchalant and composed.
His nonchalant attitude made it seem as if he didn't care about the outcome at all.
She gave a nonchalant shrug when asked about the results of the test.

Synonyms

  • insouciant
    9.9 0.21/m
    insouciant sounds literary and means carefree in a relaxed way, while nonchalant is common and shows little worry or interest.
  • unconcerned
    3.9 1.62/m
    unconcerned sounds neutral and says one is not worried, whereas nonchalant feels casual and shows little enthusiasm or interest.
  • unfazed
    5.3 0.41/m
    unfazed stays calm after a sudden problem and shows no surprise, while nonchalant suggests a relaxed lack of care or interest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: casual - not showing much care or thought; unconcerned
  • antonym: concerned - worried or upset about something
  • synonym: insouciant - showing a lack of concern or anxiety
