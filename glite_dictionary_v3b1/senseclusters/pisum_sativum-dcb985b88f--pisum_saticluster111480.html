Pisum sativum

noun
plant producing edible green peas
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪzəm səˈtiːvəm / (AmE), / ˈpiːzəm səˈtɪvəm / (BrE)
A climbing annual plant of the legume family, cultivated for its edible green peas, usually eaten fresh rather than dried.

Examples

Pisum sativum plant
Farmers often rotate crops with Pisum sativum to enrich soil nitrogen.
