skimcoat

verb
apply a thin, smoothing layer to a surface
5.1 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈskɪm.koʊt/ (AmE), /ˈskɪm.kəʊt/ (BrE)
to cover a surface with a thin layer of plaster, joint compound, or spackle to create a smooth finish

Examples

To skimcoat a wall
After sanding, she skimcoated the ceiling to hide small imperfections.
Before tiling, the contractor had to skimcoat the uneven concrete floor.
He decided to skimcoat the old walls before painting to achieve a flawless finish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plaster - to cover a wall or ceiling with plaster
  • synonym: spackle - to fill holes or cracks with spackling compound
