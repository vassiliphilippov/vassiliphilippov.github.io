bencher

noun
sideline participant
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˈbɛntʃə / (BrE)
a person who stays on the sidelines and does not actively participate, especially in sports or group activities

Examples

A bencher on the team
During the match, the bencher cheered for his teammates from the sidelines.
Although she was a bencher for most of the season, she stayed positive and supportive.
The coach encouraged every bencher to be ready to play at any moment.
After being a bencher for most games, she finally got a chance to play in the finals.
The coach encouraged every bencher to stay ready in case they were needed on the field.
He was tired of being a bencher and wanted to prove himself as a valuable team member.

Related Terms

  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in an activity
  • synonym: spectator - a person who watches an event without taking part
  • antonym: starter - a player who begins the game on the field or court
  • synonym: substitute - a player who is not in the starting lineup but may replace another
