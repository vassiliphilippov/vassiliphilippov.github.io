blurt

verb
say suddenly and impulsively
4.8 4.62/m
Pronunciation: / bl…ùÀêt / (AmE), / bl…úÀêt / (BrE)
to say something suddenly and without careful thought, often because of strong emotion or nervousness

Examples

To blurt out news
He couldn't help but blurt the news to everyone at the party.
If you blurt out the answer, you might spoil the surprise.
Sometimes she would blurt out comments without thinking.

Synonyms

  • blurt out
    3.9 2.60/m
    blurt out means say something suddenly and without thinking, while blurt often refers to that quick, nervous speech in casual talk.
  • shot from the lip
    9.9 0.00/m
    shot from the lip is an informal phrase for speaking without thinking, while blurt names the sudden act of saying something impulsively.
  • blunder out
    7.4 0.00/m
    blunder out is to say something impulsively and clumsily, while blurt focuses on a sudden, often nervous remark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spill - to reveal (information) unintentionally or too soon
  • antonym: suppress - to keep something from being revealed or expressed
