underdo

verb
to do too little
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈduː / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈduː / (BrE)
to do less than is necessary, required, or expected in a particular activity or task

Examples

To underdo the work
If you underdo your exercise routine, you might not achieve your fitness goals.
She tends to underdo her assignments, often missing important details.
It is better to slightly overcook than to underdo the chicken for safety reasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neglect - to fail to give proper attention or care
  • antonym: overdo - to do more than is necessary or appropriate
