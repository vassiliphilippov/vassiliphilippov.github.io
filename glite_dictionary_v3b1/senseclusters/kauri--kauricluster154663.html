kauri

noun
pale wood from Agathis trees
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊri / (AmE), / ˈkaʊəri / (BrE)
A pale, fine-grained wood from trees of the genus Agathis, especially Agathis australis, native to Australasia and valued for timber and resins.

Examples

Kauri wood furniture
Antique shops in New Zealand often feature items carved from kauri wood.
Early settlers used kauri for building because of its strength and workability.
The table was made from polished kauri, giving it a distinctive sheen.
