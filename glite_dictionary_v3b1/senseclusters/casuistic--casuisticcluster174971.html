casuistic

adjective
relating to casuistry or subtle moral reasoning
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌkæʒuˈɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkæzjuˈɪstɪk /; / ˌkazjʊˈɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the application of general moral or ethical principles to specific cases or problems

Examples

Casuistic reasoning
His arguments were criticized for their casuistic logic and evasiveness.
Some legal scholars warn against casuistic analysis that ignores the broader context.
The philosopher adopted a casuistic approach to resolve ethical dilemmas.
Her approach to the issue was thoroughly casuistic, focusing on applying ethical rules to each unique situation.
Some critics find casuistic reasoning to be overly complex and impractical.
The philosopher presented a casuistic argument to address the moral dilemma.
