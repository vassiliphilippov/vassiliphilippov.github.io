progenitor

noun
direct ancestor; forebear
6.1
Pronunciation: / proʊˈdʒɛnɪtər / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛnɪtə / (BrE)
a person or animal in the direct line of descent; an ancestor, especially one regarded as the originator of a line or family

Examples

The progenitor ancestor
According to family legend, our earliest known progenitor immigrated from Scotland in the 18th century.
Mendel is often referred to as the progenitor of modern genetics for his pioneering experiments with pea plants.
The ancient oak is believed to be the progenitor of all the trees in this grove.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • antonym: descendant - a person who is descended from a particular ancestor
