marbling

noun
pattern of fat in meat
6.6 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrb.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.blɪŋ / (BrE)
the streaks or flecks of fat interspersed within lean meat, especially beef, that contribute to flavor and tenderness

Examples

Beef marbling
High-quality steaks usually have more marbling, making them juicier and more flavorful.
The chef explained that the marbling in the ribeye enhances its taste and tenderness.
When selecting beef, look for even marbling throughout the cut for the best results.
