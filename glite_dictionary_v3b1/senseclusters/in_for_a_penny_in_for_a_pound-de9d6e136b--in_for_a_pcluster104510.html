in for a penny, in for a pound

Pronunciation: /ɪn fɔːr ə ˈpɛ.ni ɪn fɔːr ə paʊnd/ /; / /ɪn fər ə ˈpɛ.ni ɪn fər ə paʊnd/ (AmE), /ɪn fɔː ə ˈpɛ.ni ɪn fɔː ə paʊnd/ /; / /ɪn fə ə ˈpɛ.ni ɪn fə ə paʊnd/ (BrE)
used to express the idea that once you have started doing something, especially something risky or difficult, you should fully commit yourself to it regardless of the cost or consequences.

Examples

Be in for a penny, in for a pound
If she was going to confront her boss, she might as well be in for a penny, in for a pound and address all her concerns.
They had already spent weeks on the plan, so it was in for a penny, in for a pound from then on.
When the meeting went overtime, he thought, 'in for a penny, in for a pound,' and volunteered for the next task too.
