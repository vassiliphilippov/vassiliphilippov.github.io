trembly

adjective
slightly shaking
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛmbli / (AmE), / ˈtrɛmblɪ / (BrE)
shaking slightly, often because of nervousness, fear, or weakness

Examples

Trembly hands
He handed me the letter with trembly fingers, clearly nervous about my reaction.
Her trembly voice made it obvious she was scared to speak in public.
After the long illness, he still felt trembly when he tried to stand up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shaky - not steady; trembling or quivering
  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed, not shaking or moving
