castaway

noun
a shipwrecked and stranded person
Pronunciation: / ˈkæstəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɑːstəweɪ / (BrE)
a person who has been stranded in an isolated place, especially after a shipwreck, or someone who has been rejected or abandoned by society

Examples

A lonely castaway
After the shipwreck, he was left a lonely castaway with only his wits to rely on.
Many novels have explored the psychological struggles faced by a castaway.
The movie tells the story of a castaway surviving on a deserted island.
Once a wealthy merchant, he became a social castaway after his business collapsed.
She felt like a castaway in her own family, unwanted and alone.
The castaway wandered the streets, ignored by all who passed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outcast - a person who has been rejected by society or a social group
  • antonym: rescued - a person who has been saved from danger
  • synonym: survivor - a person who survives a disaster or accident
