indio

noun
native of the Americas
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈindiˌoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiəʊ / (BrE)
a term, originally from Spanish, used to refer to a native person of the Americas, especially during the colonial period

Examples

An indio person
The Spanish explorers encountered many indios when they arrived in the New World.
Artifacts made by the indios reveal a rich and complex culture.
During colonial times, the term indio was commonly used in official documents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colonizer - a person who settles among and establishes control over indigenous people
  • synonym: native - a person born in a particular place, especially an indigenous inhabitant
