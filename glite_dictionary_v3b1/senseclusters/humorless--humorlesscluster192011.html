humorless

adjective
lacking a sense of humor
4.9 1.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàhjuÀêm…öl…ôs /; / ÀàhjuÀêm…ôrl…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàhjuÀêm…ôl…ôs / (BrE)
Lacking a sense of humor; unable or unwilling to see or express the funny side of things.

Examples

A humorless person
Despite his intelligence, Mark remained humorless during the entire meeting.
She delivered the news in a humorless voice, leaving no room for levity.
The film was criticized for its humorless tone and lack of wit.
His humourless expression showed he did not appreciate the prank.
She remained humourless even when everyone around her was laughing at the joke.
The manager's humourless approach made staff meetings tedious and dull.

Synonyms

  • unhumorous
    7.5 0.01/m
    unhumorous does not show wit or amusement and sounds formal, while humorless means someone cannot or will not see the funny side.
  • unfunny
    4.2 0.45/m
    unfunny fails to make people laugh and usually describes jokes or people, while humorless means unable or unwilling to see the funny side.
  • mirthless
    8.0 0.20/m
    mirthless describes a smile or laugh that has no real joy and sounds gloomy, while humorless means someone cannot or will not see jokes.
