nebulization

noun
medicine mist process
7.3
Pronunciation: /ne.bjə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ne.bjʊ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ne.bjʊ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ne.bjə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of turning a liquid (especially medicine) into a fine mist for inhalation or other purposes

Examples

Nebulization treatment
The doctor prescribed nebulization to help relieve the patient's breathing difficulties.
Children with asthma often receive nebulization treatments during flare-ups.
Proper nebulization ensures that the medication reaches deep into the lungs.
The doctor recommended nebulization to help the patient breathe more easily.
In the laboratory, nebulization is used to disperse chemicals into the air for analysis.
The greenhouse used nebulization to raise humidity and keep the tropical plants healthy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerosolization - the process of turning a substance into a fine spray or aerosol
  • synonym: inhalation therapy - treatment involving breathing in medicine as a vapor or mist
