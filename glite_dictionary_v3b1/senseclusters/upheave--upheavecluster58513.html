upheave

verb
lift or raise forcefully from below
8.8 6.8
Pronunciation: /ʌp.ˈhiːv/ (AmE), /ʌp.ˈhiːv/ (BrE)
To lift or raise something forcefully from beneath, often causing disruption or upheaval.

Examples

To upheave the ground
A powerful earthquake can upheave the ground, leaving cracks and ridges behind.
He watched the workers upheave the old stones from the garden path.
Heavy machinery was used to upheave the tree stumps from the muddy soil.
