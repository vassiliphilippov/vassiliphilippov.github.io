RMS

noun
effective value method
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.em.es/ /; / /ˈɑːr.əm.əs/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.em.es/ /; / /ˈɑː.əm.əs/ (BrE)
a mathematical method for calculating the effective value of a varying quantity, especially in physics and engineering

Examples

RMS value calculation
The RMS value of an alternating current is used to determine its power.
Engineers often calculate the RMS voltage to compare different electrical signals.
The RMS method is essential for analyzing sound levels in audio engineering.
