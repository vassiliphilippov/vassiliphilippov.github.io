power shovel

noun
machine for heavy digging or excavating
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊər ˌʃʌvəl / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ˌʃʌvəl / (BrE)
a large machine with a bucket or scoop used for digging and moving earth or other materials, especially in construction or mining.

Examples

A power shovel machine
A power shovel can move tons of earth in just a few minutes on a mining site.
Many modern power shovels are equipped with advanced controls for precise operation.
The construction crew used a power shovel to dig the foundation for the new building.
