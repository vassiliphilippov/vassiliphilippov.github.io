tetrahalide

noun
compound with four halogen atoms
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈhælaɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈhælaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing four halogen atoms in each molecule

Examples

A tetrahalide compound
Scientists studied the reactivity of the new tetrahalide compound in various chemical reactions.
Sulfur tetrafluoride is an example of a tetrahalide commonly used in chemical synthesis.
The properties of a tetrahalide can be significantly different from those of its corresponding trihalide or pentahalide.
