precognitive

adjective
having knowledge of future events
6.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / priˈkɑɡnətɪv / (AmE), / priˈkɒɡnətɪv / (BrE)
relating to or denoting knowledge of an event before it happens; having foresight of the future

Examples

A precognitive impression
She reported a precognitive impression of the accident before it occurred.
Some people claim to have precognitive dreams that reveal upcoming events in their lives.
The experiment aimed to test whether precognitive abilities could be scientifically demonstrated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clairvoyant - having or claiming to have the ability to perceive events in the future
  • antonym: retrocognitive - relating to knowledge of past events not learned through ordinary means
