knight's service

noun
land tenure by serving as a knight
4.8
Pronunciation: / naɪts ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / naɪts ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
a type of land tenure in which land is held by providing military service as a knight to a lord

Examples

Knight's service land
As a form of knight's service, the tenant was obligated to join his lord in battle.
During the Middle Ages, many estates in England were held by knight's service.
The decline of knight's service marked a shift in the English feudal system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: socage - land tenure in medieval England requiring services other than knight's service
