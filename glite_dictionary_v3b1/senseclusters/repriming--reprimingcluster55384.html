repriming

noun
renewal of priming effect
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈpraɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpraɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of restoring or renewing a priming effect, especially to influence later responses in psychology or advertising

Examples

Memory repriming
The researchers studied how repriming could affect participants' memory recall in the second phase of the experiment.
In advertising, repriming is used to reinforce brand recognition after the initial campaign.
The psychologist explained that repriming helps restore the influence of a previously presented stimulus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: priming - the process by which exposure to a stimulus influences later responses
