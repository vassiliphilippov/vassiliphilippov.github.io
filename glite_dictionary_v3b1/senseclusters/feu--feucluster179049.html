feu

noun
Scottish land tenure
9.9
Pronunciation: /fjuː/ (AmE), /fjuː/ (BrE)
a grant of land or the land held under a feudal tenure in Scotland, usually in return for a regular payment called feu-duty

Examples

A Scottish feu
The family acquired a feu in the Scottish Highlands and built a farmhouse there.
Under the old system, tenants paid an annual duty for their feu to the landowner.
Historical records show that the village was divided into several feus during the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fief - an estate of land held under feudal service
  • synonym: lease - a contract granting use of property during a specified time for a specified payment
