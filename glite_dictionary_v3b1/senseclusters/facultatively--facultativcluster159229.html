facultatively

adverb
optionally; not required
9.5
Pronunciation: / fəˈkʌltətɪvli / (AmE), / fəˈkʌltətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is optional or not strictly required, especially referring to organisms that can function under different conditions

Examples

To live facultatively
Some bacteria can live facultatively with or without oxygen, adapting to different environments.
Yeast may grow facultatively in the presence or absence of certain nutrients.
The plant species reproduces facultatively, using either seeds or cuttings depending on conditions.
