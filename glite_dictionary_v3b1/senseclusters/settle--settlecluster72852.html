settle

verb
arrange or put in order
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl / (BrE)
to arrange or place things in the desired order

Examples

To settle things neatly
After unpacking, he settled his belongings in neat rows on the dresser.
She carefully settled the books on the shelf, arranging them by size.
The waiter settled the plates and cutlery on the table before serving the food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to put things in a neat, attractive, or required order
  • antonym: disorder - to make untidy or out of order
