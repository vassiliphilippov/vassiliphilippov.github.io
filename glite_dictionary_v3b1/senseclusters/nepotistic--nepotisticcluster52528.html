nepotistic

adjective
favoring relatives
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛpəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɛpəˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
showing favoritism to relatives, especially by giving them jobs or special advantages

Examples

Nepotistic hiring decisions
The manager was accused of making nepotistic decisions when hiring new staff.
Many believe that nepotistic practices harm the fairness of the workplace.
A nepotistic system can discourage talented outsiders from applying for jobs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - showing unfair preference for one person or group
  • antonym: impartial - treating all people and groups equally
