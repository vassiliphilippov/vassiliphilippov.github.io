casus belli

noun
event justifying war
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑː.səs ˈbɛl.i / (AmE), / ˌkaː.səs ˈbɛl.i / (BrE)
An event or act used to justify or provoke a declaration of war.

Examples

A casus belli event
Historians debate whether the incident truly constituted a casus belli or was merely an excuse.
The invasion was presented as a casus belli to rally public support for military action.
They fabricated a casus belli to legitimize their preemptive strike.
