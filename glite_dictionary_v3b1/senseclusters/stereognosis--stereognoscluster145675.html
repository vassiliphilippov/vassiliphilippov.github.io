stereognosis

noun
recognition by touch
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛriəɡˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriɒɡˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the ability to recognize and identify objects by touch without using sight

Examples

stereognosis test
A neurologist may test stereognosis by asking a patient to identify a key placed in their hand.
Loss of stereognosis can indicate damage to certain areas of the brain.
Children develop stereognosis as they learn to recognize objects without looking at them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agnosia - the inability to recognize objects, people, sounds, or shapes
  • synonym: tactile perception - the sense of touch and ability to perceive objects through touch
