negotiability

noun
transferable status
8.4
Pronunciation: /nə.goʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ /; / /nɪ.goʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /nə.ɡəʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ /; / /nɪ.ɡəʊ.ʃə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (BrE)
the quality of a document or instrument that allows it to be legally transferred to another person who can enforce its rights

Examples

Negotiability of a check
The negotiability of a check allows it to be endorsed and cashed by someone else.
Lawyers discussed the negotiability of the promissory note in court.
Without negotiability, financial instruments cannot be freely transferred between parties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonnegotiability - the quality of not being able to be transferred or assigned
  • synonym: transferability - the ability to be transferred from one person to another
