gesso

verb
to prime for painting
8.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊ’É›soÊŠ / (AmE), / ËˆdÊ’É›sÉ™ÊŠ / (BrE)
to cover a surface, especially canvas or wood, with a white primer to prepare it for painting

Examples

To gesso a canvas
Before starting her oil painting, she carefully gessoed the canvas to create a smooth surface.
Artists often gesso wooden panels to prevent paint from soaking into the wood.
He gessoed several canvases in advance so he could begin new projects at any time.

Synonyms

  • ground
    7.4 0.37/m
    ground is a primer layer applied to a canvas or panel to prepare it for painting, while gesso is specifically a white artist's primer.
  • undercoat
    4.4 0.00/m
    undercoat is a general primer used on many surfaces before painting, while gesso is the white primer artists use on canvas or wood.
