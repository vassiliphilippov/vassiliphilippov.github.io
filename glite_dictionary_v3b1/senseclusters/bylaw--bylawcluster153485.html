bylaw

noun
rule made by an organization
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌlɔ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪlɔː / (BrE)
A rule made by an organization or society to regulate the conduct of its members or the management of its affairs.

Examples

A bylaw rule
A new bylaw prohibits smoking within the premises.
All members must follow the bylaw regarding meeting attendance.
The association revised its bylaws to improve governance.
