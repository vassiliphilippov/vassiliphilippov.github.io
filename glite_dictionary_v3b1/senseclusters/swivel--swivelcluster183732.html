swivel

noun
device that allows rotation at a joint
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈswɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈswɪvəl / (BrE)
a device consisting of two parts joined so that one or both can rotate freely, typically used to connect parts that need to turn independently, such as in a chain or on machinery

Examples

A chain swivel
He attached the chain to a swivel to prevent it from twisting.
The chair's swivel allows the seat to rotate 360 degrees.
The lamp is mounted on a swivel so it can be directed where needed.
