selloff

noun
large-scale sale of assets at low prices
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛlˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈsɛlɒf / (BrE)
a sale of a large quantity of assets, such as stocks or bonds, at low prices, usually to dispose of them quickly rather than as part of normal trading

Examples

A sharp selloff in stocks
A sharp selloff in technology stocks rattled investors on Wall Street.
Following the announcement, there was a massive selloff of government bonds.
The market experienced a selloff after the disappointing earnings report was released.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buyout - the purchase of a large quantity of assets
  • synonym: liquidation - the process of selling off assets to pay debts
