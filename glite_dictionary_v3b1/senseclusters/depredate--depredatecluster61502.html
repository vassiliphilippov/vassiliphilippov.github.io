depredate

verb
to plunder or pillage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛprəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛprɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to plunder, rob, or lay waste to something by force or violence (especially by people)

Examples

To depredate a village
The pirates would often depredate coastal villages, leaving destruction in their wake.
During the war, soldiers depredated the countryside, taking food and valuables from the locals.
Wild animals sometimes depredate farms, causing significant losses to crops and livestock.
The pirates would often depredate coastal towns, leaving destruction in their wake.
Wild animals sometimes depredate farmers' fields, causing significant crop losses.
During the war, soldiers were known to depredate villages for supplies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force
  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or damage
  • synonym: ravage - to cause severe and extensive damage to
