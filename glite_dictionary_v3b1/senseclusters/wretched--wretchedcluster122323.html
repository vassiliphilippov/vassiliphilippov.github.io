wretched

adjective
morally reprehensible; contemptible
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtʃɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtʃɪd / (BrE)
Morally very bad; deserving severe disapproval or contempt.

Examples

Wretched behavior
His wretched crimes shocked the entire community.
Only a truly wretched person could treat others so cruelly.
She refused to forgive his wretched betrayal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admirable - deserving respect and approval
  • synonym: despicable - deserving hatred and contempt
  • synonym: vile - morally very bad or evil
