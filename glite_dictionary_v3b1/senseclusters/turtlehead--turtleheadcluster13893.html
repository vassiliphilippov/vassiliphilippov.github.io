turtlehead

noun
a marshland perennial plant with distinct flowers
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝːtəlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈtɜːtəlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a showy perennial plant of marshlands in eastern and central North America, with waxy, lance-shaped leaves and flowers that have creamy white lower parts and pink to deep purple upper parts

Examples

The turtlehead flower
Because it thrives in moist soil, turtlehead is often found near ponds and marshes.
Clusters of turtlehead brightened the edges of the stream throughout the summer.
The gardener planted pink turtlehead to attract butterflies to the wet area in her yard.
