Taylorism

noun
scientific management system
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪlərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈteɪlərɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of scientific management that analyzes and standardizes workflows to improve industrial efficiency

Examples

Taylorism scientific management
Taylorism introduced time studies and standardized tasks to increase productivity in factories.
Many critics argue that Taylorism treats workers like machines rather than people.
The principles of Taylorism influenced modern management practices around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Fordism - a system of mass production and consumption typical of highly developed economies
  • synonym: scientific management - management theory using scientific methods to improve efficiency
