bushman's poison

noun
toxic South African shrub or tree
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃmənz ˈpɔɪzən / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃmənz ˈpɔɪzn / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub or tree of South Africa, known for its toxic sap traditionally used by indigenous hunters to poison arrows

Examples

bushman's poison plant
Gardeners should handle bushman's poison cautiously due to its poisonous properties.
Many visitors to South Africa are fascinated by the legend of the bushman's poison.
The sap of the bushman's poison is extremely toxic and was used on hunting arrows.
