Greek fret

noun
classical ornamental line pattern
9.4
Pronunciation: / ɡrik frɛt / (AmE), / ɡriːk frɛt / (BrE)
an ornamental design consisting of repeated vertical and horizontal lines forming a continuous pattern, often used in Greek architecture and decorative arts

Examples

Greek fret pattern
Architects often use the Greek fret to add sophistication to columns and moldings.
She admired the intricate Greek fret carved along the marble floor.
The border of the vase featured a classic Greek fret design.
