Selective Service

noun
U.S. military draft registration system
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛktɪv ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛktɪv ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
the system in the United States responsible for registering men and administering compulsory military service when required

Examples

The Selective Service system
All men between the ages of 18 and 25 must register with the Selective Service.
During wartime, the Selective Service may require eligible individuals to join the military.
Failure to register for the Selective Service can lead to legal and financial consequences.
