nonarboreal

adjective
not living in trees
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑːnɑːrˈbɔriəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɑːˈbɔːrɪəl / (BrE)
(of an animal or organism) not inhabiting or frequenting trees; living on the ground or elsewhere rather than in trees.

Examples

Nonarboreal animals
Researchers observed that the nonarboreal birds built their nests on rocky ledges.
The nonarboreal mammals are more likely to be found foraging on the forest floor.
Unlike its arboreal relatives, the nonarboreal species prefers open grasslands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arboreal - living in or frequenting trees
