celestial mechanics

noun
astronomy of motions of celestial bodies
5.0 6.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈles.tʃəl mə.ˈkæn.ɪks/ (AmE), /sə.ˈles.tʃəl mə.ˈkæn.ɪks/ (BrE)
the branch of astronomy that deals with the motions of celestial bodies under the influence of gravitational forces, especially as described by Newton's laws of motion

Examples

Celestial mechanics theory
Scientists use celestial mechanics to predict the trajectories of asteroids and comets.
She pursued advanced studies in celestial mechanics to understand planetary orbits.
The field of celestial mechanics owes much to Isaac Newton's groundbreaking work.
