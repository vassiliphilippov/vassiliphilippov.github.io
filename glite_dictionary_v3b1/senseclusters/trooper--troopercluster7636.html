trooper

noun
soldier in a motorized army unit
Pronunciation: / ˈtruːpər / (AmE), / ˈtruːpə / (BrE)
a soldier who is a member of a cavalry or armored/motorized military unit

Examples

Cavalry trooper
As a trooper in the mechanized brigade, he became skilled at driving tanks.
Each trooper in the unit was specially trained to operate military transports.
The trooper climbed into the armored vehicle before the mission began.
During the charge, each trooper kept close formation with his regiment.
In the old paintings, a trooper is often shown in elaborate cavalry uniform.
The young trooper polished his saddle before every inspection.
