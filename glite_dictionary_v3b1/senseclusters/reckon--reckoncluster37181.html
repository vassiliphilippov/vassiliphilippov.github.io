reckon

verb
to consider or believe
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛk.ən / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.ən / (BrE)
to think, believe, or suppose something; to have an opinion or judgment about something

Examples

I reckon that
I reckon it's going to rain later based on those dark clouds.
Most people reckon him to be the best player on the team.
She doesn't reckon this plan will work without more support.
I reckon it will rain before the afternoon is over.
Many people reckon that the new law will benefit large companies.
She reckoned him to be about forty years old.
Do you reckon she will accept the job offer?
I reckon it will rain later this afternoon, so bring an umbrella just in case.
Most people reckon that house prices will keep rising for the foreseeable future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consider - to think about or regard someone or something in a particular way
  • synonym: deem - to regard or judge in a particular way
  • antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain or not believe something is true
  • synonym: guess - form an opinion without sufficient information
  • synonym: suppose - to assume something to be likely or true
