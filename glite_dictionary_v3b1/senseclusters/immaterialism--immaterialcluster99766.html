immaterialism

noun
belief in non-material reality
Pronunciation: / ɪməˈtɪriəˌlɪzəm /; / ˌɪməˈtɪriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪməˈtɪəriəˌlɪzəm /; / ˌɪməˈtɪəriəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical belief or doctrine that only immaterial things (such as minds or ideas) truly exist and that material things are not fundamental reality

Examples

Philosophical immaterialism
Some philosophers argue that immaterialism better explains the nature of consciousness than materialism.
The debate between immaterialism and materialism has shaped much of Western philosophy.
His belief in immaterialism led him to focus on spiritual rather than physical realities.
George Berkeley is well known for his theory of immaterialism in philosophy.
According to immaterialism, the physical world exists only in our perceptions.
Many debates in philosophy discuss the differences between immaterialism and materialism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idealism - the philosophical theory that reality is fundamentally mental or spiritual
  • antonym: materialism - the belief that only physical matter is real
