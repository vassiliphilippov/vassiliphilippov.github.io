membranophone

noun
percussion instrument with stretched membrane
9.8 9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmem.brə.nə.foʊn/ (AmE), /ˈmem.brə.nə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
a musical instrument that produces sound by means of a vibrating stretched membrane, typically as part of the percussion family

Examples

A membranophone drum
Experts classify drums and tambourines as membranophones because they rely on vibrating membranes to make sound.

The snare drum is a classic example of a membranophone used in orchestras.
