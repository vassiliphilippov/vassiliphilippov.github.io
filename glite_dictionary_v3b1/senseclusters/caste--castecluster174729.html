caste

noun
hereditary social class in Hinduism
6.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /kæst/ (AmE), /kɑːst/ (BrE)
a social class or group that people are born into, often with certain privileges or restrictions, especially in societies like India

Examples

The caste system
He rejected discrimination based on caste and worked toward social equality.
Members of a higher caste were often given privileges denied to others.
The Indian social structure was historically organized around the rigid caste system.
Efforts to eradicate caste discrimination have been ongoing for decades.
In traditional India, a person's caste determined their social status and occupation.
Members of the merchant caste were responsible for trade and commerce.
He was born into the highest caste and enjoyed certain privileges.
In traditional societies, a person's caste often determined their occupation and marital prospects.
Marrying outside one's caste was once considered unacceptable in many communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caste system - a social structure in which classes are determined by heredity
  • synonym: class - a group sharing the same economic or social status
  • synonym: estate - a social class in historical European societies
  • antonym: outcaste - a person who does not belong to any caste
