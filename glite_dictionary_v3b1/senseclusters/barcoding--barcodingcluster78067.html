barcoding

noun
DNA identification method
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːr.koʊ.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈbɑːr.kə.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbɑː.kəʊ.dɪŋ/ /; / /ˈbɑː.kə.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
a laboratory technique that uses short DNA sequences as unique identifiers to identify species or track biological samples

Examples

DNA barcoding
Researchers used barcoding to distinguish between closely related plant species in the rainforest.
The laboratory relies on barcoding to accurately track thousands of biological samples.
Advances in barcoding have made it easier to identify unknown animal species.
