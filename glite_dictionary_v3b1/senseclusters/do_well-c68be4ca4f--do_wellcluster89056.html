do well

verb
act wisely or in one's best interest
1.7
Pronunciation: / duː wɛl / (AmE), / duː wɛl / (BrE)
to act in a way that is wise, prudent, or beneficial for oneself or others; to make a good decision or take appropriate action

Examples

To do well to help
He did well to apologize quickly after the argument.
They will do well to start saving money early for their retirement.
You would do well to listen to her advice before making a decision.
