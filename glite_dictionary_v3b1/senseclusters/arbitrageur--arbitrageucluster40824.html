arbitrageur

noun
person profiting from price differences between markets
6.5 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.bɪ.trɑː.ʒər/ /; / /ɑːr.bɪ.ˈtrɑː.ʒər/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.bɪ.trɑː.ʒə/ /; / /ɑː.bɪ.ˈtrɑː.ʒə/ (BrE)
a person who buys and sells securities, currency, or commodities in different markets to take advantage of differing prices for the same asset

Examples

An arbitrageur trades assets
As an arbitrageur, she constantly monitored stock exchanges around the world for profitable opportunities.
The fund hired an experienced arbitrageur to capitalize on financial market inefficiencies.
The skilled arbitrageur made a fortune by exploiting price differences in various markets.
