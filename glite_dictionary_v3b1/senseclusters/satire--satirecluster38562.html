satire

noun
literary genre using ridicule or irony for criticism
4.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsæ.taɪr/ /; / /ˈsæ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈsæ.taɪ.ə/ (BrE)
the use of humor, irony, or ridicule to criticize or mock people, behaviors, or institutions, often to expose foolishness or bad qualities

Examples

Political satire
His latest play uses satire to highlight the absurdities within the political system.
Many comedians rely on satire to comment on current events and public figures.
The novel is a brilliant satire of the upper classes and their social pretensions.
Her article made clever use of satire to criticize corporate greed.
The novel is rich in satire that targets the absurdities of modern politics.
Through biting satire, the comedian highlighted the flaws of celebrity culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irony - the expression of meaning by using language that normally signifies the opposite
  • antonym: panegyric - a public speech or published text in praise of someone or something
  • synonym: parody - an imitation of the style of a particular genre, artist, or work with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • synonym: sarcasm - use of sharp or cutting remarks to mock or convey contempt
