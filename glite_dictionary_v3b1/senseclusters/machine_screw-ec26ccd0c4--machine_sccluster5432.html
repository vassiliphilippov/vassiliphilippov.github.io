machine screw

noun
screw used with nut or tapped hole
3.7
Pronunciation: / məˈʃin ˌskru / (AmE), / məˈʃiːn ˌskruː / (BrE)
A type of screw with a uniform diameter and a slotted head, designed to be used with a nut or in a tapped (threaded) hole, typically driven by a screwdriver.

Examples

A machine screw fastener
After losing the original fastener, I replaced it with a new machine screw.
The panel was secured tightly with a machine screw and a matching nut.
You need a machine screw of the correct length for this metal bracket.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bolt - A metal fastener with a threaded shaft, usually used with a nut
