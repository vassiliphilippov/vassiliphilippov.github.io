denationalize

verb
transfer from state to private ownership
6.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈnæ.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ /; / /diː.ˈnæ.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ (AmE), /diː.ˈnæ.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈnæ.ʃə.nə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to transfer control or ownership of (a business, industry, or service) from government to private hands

Examples

To denationalize a company
After decades of state management, the electricity sector was denationalized in the late 1990s.
The government decided to denationalize several key industries to encourage competition.
There is a heated debate about whether to denationalize the public transport system.
After the election, the new government decided to denationalise several major industries.
Plans to denationalise public utilities have sparked widespread debate among citizens.
The company flourished after it was denationalised in the late 1990s.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalise - to transfer a private business, industry, or property into government ownership or control
  • antonym: nationalize - to transfer from private to government ownership
