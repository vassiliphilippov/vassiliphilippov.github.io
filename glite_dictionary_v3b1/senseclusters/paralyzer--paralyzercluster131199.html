paralyzer

noun
something that halts action
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpær.ə.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈpær.ə.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
something that causes people or organizations to be unable to act, decide, or make progress, often due to fear or uncertainty

Examples

A fear paralyzer
The threat of a lawsuit acted as a paralyzer for the entire project team.
Uncertainty about the new regulations became a paralyzer for investment in the region.
Fear of making mistakes can be a powerful paralyzer in decision-making processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catalyst - something that causes change or action
  • synonym: obstacle - something that blocks progress or makes it difficult
