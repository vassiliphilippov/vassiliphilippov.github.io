rush

verb
cause to happen quickly
1.1
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ / (AmE), / rʌʃ / (BrE)
to cause or urge someone or something to move or act quickly

Examples

To rush a decision
He didn't want to rush the decision and risk making a mistake.
The new regulations may rush the approval process for certain drugs.
Their announcement rushed the negotiations to an early conclusion.
Please don't rush me—I'll be ready in a moment.
She felt annoyed when he tried to rush her through her meal.
There's no need to rush the children; let them take their time.
Security rushed the celebrity through the crowd to avoid the paparazzi.
She rushed her children out the door so they wouldn't miss the bus.
The coach rushed the players onto the field before the whistle blew.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delay - to make something happen later than planned
  • synonym: hasten - to cause to happen sooner or more quickly
  • synonym: hurry - to urge someone to move or act quickly
