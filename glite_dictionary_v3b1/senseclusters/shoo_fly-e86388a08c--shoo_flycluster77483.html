shoo fly

noun
South American herb with blue-and-white flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈʃuː flaɪ / (BrE)
A coarse South American herb grown for its blue-and-white flowers and distinctive bladder-like fruit containing a dry berry; also known as Nicandra physalodes.

Examples

The shoo fly plant
Despite its beauty, the shoo fly is considered invasive in some regions.
Many gardeners admire the unique fruit of the shoo fly after its flowers fade.
The seeds of the shoo fly are often used as ornamental plants in gardens.
