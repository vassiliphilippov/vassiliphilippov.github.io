autoclaved lightweight concrete

noun
lightweight precast concrete material
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɔː.t̬oʊˌkleɪvd ˈlaɪtˌweɪt ˈkɑn.krit / (AmE), / ˈɔː.təʊˌkleɪvd ˈlaɪtˌweɪt ˈkɒn.kriːt / (BrE)
a lightweight, precast concrete building material that provides structural support, insulation, and resistance to fire and mold, made by curing concrete under heat and pressure in an autoclave

Examples

Autoclaved lightweight concrete blocks
Construction companies are turning to autoclaved lightweight concrete for projects that require both speed and energy efficiency.
The new apartment block was built entirely from autoclaved lightweight concrete to ensure better insulation.
Using autoclaved lightweight concrete reduces the overall weight of the structure without compromising strength.
