go under

verb
to fail (esp. financially) or sink
4.4 1.83/m
Pronunciation: / É¡oÊŠ ËˆÊŒndÉ™r / (AmE), / É¡É™ÊŠ ËˆÊŒndÉ™ / (BrE)
(of a business, person, or other entity) to fail, especially financially, or to sink or become submerged.

Examples

A company goes under
If the lifeboats don't arrive soon, the boat will go under.
Several restaurants in the area had to go under during the pandemic.
The company began to go under after the recession hit.

Synonyms

  • fail
    5.1 20/m
    fail means to stop operating or become bankrupt in business, while go under often suggests sinking or being overwhelmed financially.
  • go belly up
    5.3 0.66/m
    go belly up is an informal phrase meaning a business fails completely or goes bankrupt, while go under is more neutral.
  • collapse
    3.9 31/m
    collapse often describes a sudden falling apart or failure, while go under refers to a business failing, often financially.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often used for financial failure
  • synonym: sink - to go below the surface of water and become submerged
