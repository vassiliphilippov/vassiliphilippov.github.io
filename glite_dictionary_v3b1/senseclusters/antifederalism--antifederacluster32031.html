antifederalism

noun
opposition to central government
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˈfɛdərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈfɛdərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
opposition to a strong central government, especially as a political movement in early U.S. history

Examples

Antifederalism movement
The debates between Federalists and supporters of antifederalism were crucial in shaping the U.S. Constitution.
Many leaders who favored antifederalism worried about losing power to a national government.
Historians study antifederalism to understand early American political conflicts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: federalism - support for a strong central government
