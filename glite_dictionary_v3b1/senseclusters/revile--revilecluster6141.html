revile

verb
to insult or speak abusively
5.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvaɪl / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪl / (BrE)
to criticize or speak about someone or something in a very angry and insulting way

Examples

To revile critics loudly
She refused to revile her critics, instead remaining calm and composed under pressure.
The artist was once reviled as a fool but is now recognized as a visionary.
The politician was reviled by opponents in the media for his controversial remarks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
  • synonym: vilify - to speak or write about in an abusively disparaging manner
