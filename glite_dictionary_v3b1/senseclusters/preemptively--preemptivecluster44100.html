preemptively

adverb
in advance to replace
Pronunciation: / priˈɛmptɪvli / (AmE), / priˈɛmptɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that interrupts, replaces, or takes the place of something before it happens, especially in broadcasting or scheduling

Examples

Preemptively interrupt a show
The news channel preemptively interrupted the movie to cover the breaking story.
The company preemptively announced the product recall before any issues were reported.
To avoid confusion, the organizers preemptively sent out updated schedules to all participants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proactively - in a way that takes action before a problem occurs
  • antonym: reactively - in response to something after it happens
