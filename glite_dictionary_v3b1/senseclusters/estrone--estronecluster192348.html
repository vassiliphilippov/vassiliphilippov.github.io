estrone

noun
a weak estrogenic hormone
Pronunciation: / ˈɛsˌtroʊn /; / ˈɛstroʊn / (AmE), / ˈɛstrəʊn /; / ˈiːstrəʊn / (BrE)
a naturally occurring weak estrogenic hormone produced mainly in the ovaries and used medically to treat estrogen deficiency

Examples

Natural estrone hormone
After menopause, the production of estrone becomes the primary source of estrogen in women.
Doctors may prescribe estrone to treat symptoms caused by low estrogen levels.
Laboratory tests can measure estrone concentrations to help assess hormonal health.
Doctors sometimes prescribe oestrone to treat symptoms of menopause.
Research has shown that oestrone is less potent than other forms of estrogen.
The laboratory measured the patient's oestrone levels to assess hormonal balance.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: estradiol - the most potent naturally occurring estrogen hormone in humans
  • degree lower: estriol - a weak naturally occurring estrogen hormone, less potent than estrone
  • synonym: estrogen - any of a group of steroid hormones that promote the development and maintenance of female characteristics
  • synonym: estrone - American English spelling of 'oestrone', same hormone
