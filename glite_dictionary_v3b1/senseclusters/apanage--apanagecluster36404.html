apanage

noun
customary privilege of rank or office
Pronunciation: / ˈæpənɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈapənɪdʒ / (BrE)
a customary and rightful privilege or perquisite associated with a particular rank or office

Examples

Apanage of nobility
Education was once the apanage of the privileged classes, not accessible to the general public.
For centuries, the title of duke was considered an apanage reserved for members of the royal family.
The use of elaborate robes was seen as an apanage of religious leaders in medieval society.
