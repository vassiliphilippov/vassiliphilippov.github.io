hypohemia

noun
local blood deficiency
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈhiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəʊˈhiːmiə / (BrE)
a local deficiency of blood in a specific organ or tissue, which can impair its function

Examples

Hypohemia in tissue
The doctor diagnosed hypohemia in the patient's left arm after the accident.
Prolonged hypohemia can lead to tissue damage if not treated promptly.
Researchers studied the effects of hypohemia on organ function in laboratory animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ischemia - an inadequate blood supply to an organ or part of the body
