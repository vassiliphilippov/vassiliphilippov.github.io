calcium hydroxide

noun
a white, caustic alkaline compound (Ca(OH)₂)
3.0 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkæl.sɪ.əm haɪ.ˈdrɑːk.saɪd/ /; / /ˈkæl.siː.əm haɪ.ˈdrɑːk.saɪd/ (AmE), /ˈkæl.sɪ.əm haɪ.ˈdrɒk.saɪd/ (BrE)
a white, caustic, alkaline substance, Ca(OH)₂, produced by adding water to quicklime; used in mortars, plasters, and as an antacid

Examples

Calcium hydroxide in mortar
Construction workers mix calcium hydroxide with sand and water to form mortar for bricklaying.
Dental professionals use calcium hydroxide as a lining material during root canal therapy.
To neutralize acidic soil, farmers sometimes add calcium hydroxide to their fields.

  • synonym: slaked lime - an alternative name for calcium hydroxide
