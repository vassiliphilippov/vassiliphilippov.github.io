direct loan

noun
loan made directly by a lender
4.8 2.9
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt loʊn / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ləʊn / (BrE)
A loan made directly by a lender to a borrower without involving an intermediary or third party.

Examples

A direct loan
Banks sometimes prefer providing a direct loan rather than working through brokers.
The government offers a direct loan program to help students pay for college.
With a direct loan, the lender manages all terms and repayment directly with the borrower.
