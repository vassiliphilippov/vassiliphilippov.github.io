move on

verb
proceed to next stage or let go of past
3.1 28/m
Pronunciation: / muÀêv …ën / (AmE), / muÀêv …ín / (BrE)
to progress to the next stage or activity; to stop focusing on the past and proceed with life

Examples

To move on quickly
After the breakup, it took her months to move on and start dating again.
After years in the same job, he decided it was time to move on and seek new opportunities.
Let's move on to the next item on the agenda.

Synonyms

  • move beyond
    4.3 2.66/m
    move beyond is used for getting past a specific issue and aiming for something better, while move on means leaving the past.
  • turn the page
    3.7 0.52/m
    turn the page suggests a clear new start after a past event, while move on means progressing to the next stage.
  • move ahead
    2.7 2.88/m
    move ahead means progressing toward a goal or time, while move on focuses more on leaving the past and starting something new.
  • advance
    3.4 36/m
    advance sounds formal and shows planned forward movement or growth, while move on is more casual and often means leaving the past.
  • progress
    2.5 18/m
    progress describes slow or steady development over time, while move on is an active step to leave the past and begin anew.
