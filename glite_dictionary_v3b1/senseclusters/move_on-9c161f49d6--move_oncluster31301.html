move on

verb
proceed to next stage or let go of past
1.0
Pronunciation: / muːv ɑn / (AmE), / muːv ɒn / (BrE)
to progress to the next stage or activity; to stop focusing on the past and proceed with life

Examples

To move on quickly
After the breakup, it took her months to move on and start dating again.
After years in the same job, he decided it was time to move on and seek new opportunities.
Let's move on to the next item on the agenda.
