incline

verb
lower or bend head/body forward
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈklaɪn / (AmE), / ɪnˈklaɪn / (BrE)
to bend or lower your head or upper body, especially as a gesture

Examples

Incline your head
He inclined forward to listen more closely to the speaker.
She inclined her head respectfully when she entered the room.
The monk inclined his upper body in a gesture of humility.
He inclined his head slightly, signaling that he was listening carefully to the speaker.
She inclined her ear toward her grandmother, eager to catch every word of the story.
The child inclined forward, straining to hear the soft voice at the back of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bow - to bend the upper part of the body forward as a gesture
  • antonym: straighten - to make the head or body upright after bending
