Tetraonidae

noun
family of birds including grouse
Pronunciation: / tɛˌtreɪˈɑːnɪˌdiː / (AmE), / tɛˌtreɪˈɒnɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of birds in the order Galliformes that includes grouse and related species, known for their robust bodies and feathered legs.

Examples

The Tetraonidae family
Among the Tetraonidae, the ruffed grouse is particularly well known in North America.
Scientists studied the habitat preferences of various members of the Tetraonidae.
The Tetraonidae family includes species found in temperate and subarctic regions.
