incuse

verb
to stamp below surface
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàkjuÀêz / (AmE), / …™nÀàkjuÀêz / (BrE)
to press or stamp a design or inscription into a surface so that it is below the surrounding level

Examples

To incuse a design
The artisan carefully incused the family's crest into the bronze plaque using a small chisel.
Ancient coins were often incused with symbols to mark their authenticity.
The jeweler incuses each piece with a unique serial number for identification.

Synonyms

  • imprint
    3.8 0.98/m
    imprint makes a clear mark by pressure on a surface, while incuse presses a design so it lies below the surrounding level.
  • stamp
    2.2 2.88/m
    stamp presses a mark into or onto a surface, often leaving a raised or flat impression, while incuse makes a design sunken below.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emboss - to carve or mold a design so it stands out from the surface
  • synonym: engrave - to cut or carve a design into a hard surface
