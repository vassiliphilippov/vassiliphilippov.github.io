expletive

noun
filler word in sentences
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksplətɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛksplɪtɪv / (BrE)
A word or phrase inserted into a sentence that does not add independent meaning but serves to fill out its structure or rhythm.

Examples

Grammatical expletive
In the sentence 'There are three books on the table,' the word there is considered an expletive.
Sometimes, an expletive can help a sentence flow more naturally, even if it adds no meaning.
The poet used an expletive to maintain the meter of the line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filler - a sound or word used to fill a pause or grammatical slot
