oversampling

noun
high-rate signal sampling
6.8
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈsæm.plɪŋ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.sæm.plɪŋ/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈsæm.plɪŋ/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.sæm.plɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of sampling a signal at a rate significantly higher than the minimum required, often to improve quality or reduce noise in digital conversion

Examples

Audio oversampling
The audio engineer used oversampling to achieve higher sound quality in the final recording.
Modern digital cameras often rely on oversampling to produce clearer images with less noise.
By applying oversampling, the software was able to filter out unwanted background signals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sampling - the process of converting a continuous signal into a set of discrete values
  • antonym: undersampling - sampling a signal at a rate lower than the minimum required
