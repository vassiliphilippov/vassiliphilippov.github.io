marsh plant

noun
plant growing in wet, marshy areas
4.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /mɑːrʃ plænt/ (AmE), /mɑːʃ plɑːnt/ (BrE)
a plant that grows in soft, wet, or marshy areas, typically adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils

Examples

Marsh plants grow
Some marsh plants filter pollutants from the water, improving the habitat for wildlife.
The wetland reserve is home to many marsh plants, including reeds and cattails.
We studied the adaptations of marsh plants for surviving in waterlogged soil.
