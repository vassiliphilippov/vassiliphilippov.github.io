fifth wheel

noun
pivoting bearing for steering wagons
7.8
Pronunciation: / fɪfθ wil / (AmE), / fɪfθ wiːl / (BrE)
a horizontal bearing placed above the front axle of a horse-drawn wagon, allowing it to pivot or rotate for steering

Examples

Wagon fifth wheel
Early carriages used a fifth wheel to connect the front axle to the frame.
The fifth wheel is essential for the horse-drawn wagon's maneuverability.
Without a properly greased fifth wheel, steering the wagon became difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kingpin - the main pivot in steering mechanisms of vehicles
