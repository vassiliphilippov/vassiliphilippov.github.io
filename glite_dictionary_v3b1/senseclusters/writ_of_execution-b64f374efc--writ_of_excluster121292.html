writ of execution

noun
court order to enforce a judgment
5.2 7.9
Pronunciation: /rɪt əv ek.sə.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪt əv ek.sə.ˈkjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
a court order authorizing an enforcement officer, such as a sheriff, to carry out a judgment granted to a plaintiff, typically by seizing or selling property

Examples

A writ of execution order
A writ of execution allows law enforcement to repossess property in accordance with the court's decision.
After winning the lawsuit, the plaintiff applied for a writ of execution to enforce the judgment.
The court issued a writ of execution to seize the defendant's assets.

Related Terms
