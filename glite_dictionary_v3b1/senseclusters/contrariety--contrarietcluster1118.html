contrariety

noun
state of being contrary or opposite
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.trəˈraɪ.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trəˈraɪ.ə.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being contrary; a relationship between things that are opposite or incompatible

Examples

The contrariety of ideas
Philosophers have long debated the contrariety of good and evil.
The contrariety between their opinions was evident in every discussion.
The concept of contrariety is central to many logical arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmony - agreement; absence of opposition or conflict
  • synonym: opposition - the state of opposing or being in conflict
