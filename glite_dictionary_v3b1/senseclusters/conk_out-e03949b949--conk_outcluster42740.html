conk out

verb
stop working suddenly (informal)
Pronunciation: / kɑŋk aʊt / (AmE), / kɒŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to suddenly stop working or to fall asleep, especially because of tiredness or mechanical failure

Examples

To conk out suddenly
After running the marathon, she conked out on the couch from exhaustion.
My old laptop conked out right before I could save my document.
The generator conked out during the storm, leaving us without power.
I was so tired after the hike that I conked out on the couch as soon as I got home.
She usually stays up late, but last night she conked out before nine o'clock.
The old engine finally conked out after years of service, leaving us stranded on the road.
During the boring lecture, he nearly conked out at his desk.
I was so tired after work that I conked out on the couch within minutes.
She conked out before she could finish reading the first chapter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break down - cease to function due to failure
  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, typically from exhaustion
  • synonym: die - (of a machine) to stop working; to break down
  • synonym: faint - to lose consciousness temporarily due to lack of strength
  • synonym: pass out - to faint or lose consciousness unexpectedly
  • antonym: wake up - to stop sleeping or become conscious again
  • antonym: work - to function properly
