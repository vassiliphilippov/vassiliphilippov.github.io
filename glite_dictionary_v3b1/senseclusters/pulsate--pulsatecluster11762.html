pulsate

verb
produce or transmit pulses (esp. waves)
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌlˌseɪt / (AmE), / ˈpʌlseɪt / (BrE)
(of a signal or apparatus) to produce, transmit, or modulate in a series of short bursts or pulses, especially of electromagnetic waves.

Examples

To pulsate energy signals
Engineers designed the system to pulsate bursts of energy rather than emit a continuous signal.
Modern radars pulsate electromagnetic waves to detect objects at long ranges.
The radio transmitter can pulsate signals at regular intervals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pulse - to emit or modulate in pulses
