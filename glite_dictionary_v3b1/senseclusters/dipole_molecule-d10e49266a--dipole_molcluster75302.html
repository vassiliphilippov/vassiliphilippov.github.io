dipole molecule

noun
molecule with a permanent dipole
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪpoʊl ˈmɑlɪˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪpəʊl ˈmɒlɪˌkjuːl / (BrE)
A molecule that has a permanent separation of electric charge, resulting in a positive and a negative end (a permanent dipole).

Examples

A dipole molecule
Ammonia acts as a dipole molecule due to the electronegativity difference between nitrogen and hydrogen.
The behavior of a dipole molecule in an electric field is different from that of a nonpolar molecule.
Water is a classic example of a dipole molecule because it has a significant separation of charge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonpolar molecule - a molecule with no permanent electric dipole moment
  • synonym: polar molecule - a molecule with a permanent electric dipole moment
