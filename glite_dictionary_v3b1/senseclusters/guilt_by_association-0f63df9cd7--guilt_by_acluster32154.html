guilt by association

noun
attribution of guilt due to one's associates
Pronunciation: / ɡɪlt baɪ əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡɪlt baɪ əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the attribution of guilt to someone solely because of their relationship or association with others who are guilty, without direct evidence of their own wrongdoing

Examples

Guilt by association rule
Many people criticize the practice of guilt by association as unfair and unjust.
She suffered from guilt by association simply because her friend was convicted of fraud.
The politician denied any wrongdoing and claimed the accusations were mere guilt by association.
