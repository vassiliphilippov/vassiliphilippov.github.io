washdown

noun
thorough cleaning with water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑʃˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɒʃˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a thorough cleaning of equipment, surfaces, or vehicles using water, often to remove dirt or contaminants

Examples

Complete washdown of tools
After the construction work, a complete washdown of the tools was required.
The restaurant staff performed a nightly washdown of the kitchen floors and counters.
A quick washdown of the truck removed all the mud from the tires.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: rinse - to wash something quickly with clean water
  • degree higher: scrub - to clean something by rubbing it hard, often with a brush
