close

verb
become shut or not open
1.0
Pronunciation: / kloʊz / (AmE), / kləʊz / (BrE)
to shut, block, or make something inaccessible (such as a door, window, gap, place, or computer window)

Examples

To close the door
As the night grew colder, all the windows closed automatically.
Once the store closes, no customers are allowed inside.
The door closed silently behind me as I entered the room.
He forgot to close his umbrella when he got on the bus.
Please close the door quietly behind you.
The surgeon used stitches to close the wound after the operation.
Before painting, make sure to close any holes or cracks in the wall.
The engineers worked to close the leak in the pipe as quickly as possible.
We need to close all gaps around the windows to prevent drafts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exit - to leave or shut down a computer program or application
  • antonym: open - to become unclosed or not shut
  • synonym: seal - to close securely so that nothing can enter or escape
  • synonym: shut - to move something so as to block an opening; to make shut
