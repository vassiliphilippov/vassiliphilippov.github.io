gate

noun
logic circuit with one output, multiple inputs
3.4 6.3
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪt / (AmE), / ɡeɪt / (BrE)
a device or circuit in a computer that performs a logical operation on one or more input signals to produce a single output

Examples

Logic gate
A NAND gate produces an output of 0 when both its inputs are 1.
Each microprocessor contains millions of tiny gates that process information.
The AND gate only outputs a high signal when both of its inputs are high.
