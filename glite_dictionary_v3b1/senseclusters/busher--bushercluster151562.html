busher

noun
person in the wilderness
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃər / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃə / (BrE)
a person who lives or works in the bush or wilderness, especially in remote areas

Examples

Skilled busher
The experienced busher could survive for weeks with only basic supplies.
As a young busher, she learned to track animals and find edible plants.
Many stories were told about the legendary busher who disappeared into the forest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: city dweller - a person who lives in a city
  • synonym: frontiersman - a person living on the frontier, especially in wild or undeveloped regions
