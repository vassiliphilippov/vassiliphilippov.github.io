Dalmatia pyrethrum

noun
white-flowered Balkan plant yielding insecticide
9.9
Pronunciation: /dæl.ˈmeɪ.ʃə paɪ.ˈreθ.rəm/ (AmE), /dæl.ˈmeɪ.ʃə paɪ.ˈreθ.rəm/ (BrE)
a white-flowered plant native to the Balkan area, noted for its pinnate leaves with white, silky hairs underneath, and used as a source of insecticide; sometimes classified in the genus Chrysanthemum

Examples

The Dalmatia pyrethrum plant
Dalmatia pyrethrum has been used for centuries as a natural insecticide in agriculture.
Many gardeners plant Dalmatia pyrethrum near vegetables to help repel insects naturally.
The silky underside of the leaves helps identify Dalmatia pyrethrum in the wild.
