micronucleus

noun
small extra nucleus
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈnuːkliəs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈnjuːkliəs / (BrE)
a small, extra nucleus-like structure in a cell formed when chromosome fragments or whole chromosomes are not incorporated into the main nuclei during cell division

Examples

Cell division micronucleus
The scientist observed a micronucleus in the cell sample after exposure to the chemical.
Detection of a micronucleus can indicate genetic damage in laboratory tests.
Researchers counted the number of micronuclei to assess the effects of radiation on cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nucleus - the central and most important part of a cell, containing genetic material
