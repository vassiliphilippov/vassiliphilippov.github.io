take a tumble

verb
fall suddenly; suffer a sudden drop
5.1 4.4
Pronunciation: /teɪk ə ˈtʌm.bəl/ (AmE), /teɪk ə ˈtʌm.bəl/ (BrE)
to fall or trip suddenly, or to experience a sharp or unexpected decline

Examples

To take a tumble suddenly
Several tech companies took a tumble when the market crashed last week.
She laughed off the fact that she took a tumble during her performance.
Watch your step on the icy sidewalk or you might take a tumble.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rise - to move from a lower to a higher position; to increase
