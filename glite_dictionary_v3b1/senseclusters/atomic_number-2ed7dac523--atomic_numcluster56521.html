atomic number

noun
number of protons in an atom
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑːmɪk ˈnʌmbɚ / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk ˈnʌmbə / (BrE)
the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom, which determines the element’s place in the periodic table

Examples

Atomic number of gold
Gold has an atomic number of 79, so it contains 79 protons in its nucleus.
The atomic number of carbon is 6, meaning each carbon atom has six protons.
When elements are arranged in order of increasing atomic number, their properties show a repeating pattern.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: mass number - the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom
  • synonym: proton - a subatomic particle with a positive charge found in the nucleus of an atom
