predestinarian

noun
one who believes fate is predetermined
7.6 9.7
Pronunciation: /pre.des.tɪ.ˈneɪr.ɪ.ən/ /; / /priː.des.tɪ.ˈneɪr.ɪ.ən/ (AmE), /pre.des.tɪ.ˈneə.rɪ.ən/ /; / /priː.des.tɪ.ˈneə.rɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a person who believes that all events, especially regarding salvation, are determined in advance by a divine power and cannot be changed by human actions

Examples

A strict predestinarian
As a predestinarian, she believed that her life's course was set long before she was born.
He was accused of being a predestinarian because of his strong belief in divine preordination.
The debate between free will and predestinarian thought has divided theologians for centuries.
