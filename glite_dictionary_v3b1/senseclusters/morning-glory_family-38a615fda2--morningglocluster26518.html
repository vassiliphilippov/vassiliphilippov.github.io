morning-glory family

noun
family of climbing plants like morning glory
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrnɪŋ ˌɡlɔri ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ˈmɔːnɪŋ ˌɡlɒri ˈfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Convolvulaceae) with trumpet-shaped flowers and a climbing or twining growth habit, including morning glories, bindweed, and sweet potato

Examples

Morning-glory family plants
Many plants in the morning-glory family are known for their beautiful, trumpet-shaped blooms.
Some species in the morning-glory family can be invasive, rapidly spreading along fences and hedges.
The morning-glory family includes both ornamental flowers and important food crops like the sweet potato.
