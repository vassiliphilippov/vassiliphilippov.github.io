iguanian

noun
lizard of Iguania
Pronunciation: / ɪˈɡwɑːniən / (AmE), / ɪˈɡwɑːniən / (BrE)
a lizard belonging to the clade Iguania, which includes iguanas, chameleons, anoles, and related species

Examples

An iguanian species
The biologist studied an iguanian to learn more about its unique adaptations.
Many iguanian species are found in tropical forests around the world.
Chameleons, a type of iguanian, are famous for their color-changing abilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chameleon - a lizard known for its ability to change color
  • synonym: iguana - a large herbivorous lizard native to Central and South America
