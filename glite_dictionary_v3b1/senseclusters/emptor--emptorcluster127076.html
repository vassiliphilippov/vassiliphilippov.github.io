emptor

noun
a buyer or purchaser
9.8 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õmpt…îr / (AmE), / Àà…õmpt…îÀê / (BrE)
a person who buys something; a buyer, especially in legal or commercial contexts

Examples

The emptor buys goods
In legal documents, the emptor is often contrasted with the vendor.
The emptor must examine goods carefully before purchase, as the principle of caveat emptor applies.
The rights and responsibilities of the emptor are stated in the contract.

Synonyms

  • vendee
    9.5 0.08/m
    vendee is the legal name for the party who buys in contracts, while emptor is a formal or older legal word for a buyer.
  • purchaser
    2.8 3.38/m
    purchaser is a more formal term used for buying goods or property, while emptor is a legal, often older word.
  • buyer
    1.0 28/m
    buyer is the common everyday word for someone who pays for something, while emptor is a formal legal term.
