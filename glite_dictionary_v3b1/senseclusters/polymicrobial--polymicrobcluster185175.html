polymicrobial

adjective
involving multiple microbes
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˌmaɪˈkroʊbiəl / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌmaɪˈkrəʊbiəl / (BrE)
involving or caused by more than one type of microorganism

Examples

Polymicrobial infection
The laboratory confirmed that the wound was polymicrobial and needed a combination of antibiotics.
Doctors often encounter polymicrobial infections in patients with weakened immune systems.
A polymicrobial environment can complicate the treatment of respiratory illnesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monomicrobial - involving or caused by a single type of microorganism
