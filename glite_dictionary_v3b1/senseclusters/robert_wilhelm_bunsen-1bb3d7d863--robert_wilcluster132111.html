Robert Wilhelm Bunsen

proper noun
German chemist, invented the Bunsen burner
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊbɚt vɪlˈhɛlm ˈbʊnsən / (AmE), / ˈrɒbət vɪlˈhɛlm ˈbʊnsən / (BrE)
(1811–1899) A German chemist known for co-inventing the Bunsen burner and pioneering spectrum analysis with Kirchhoff.

Examples

Robert Wilhelm Bunsen chemist
Robert Wilhelm Bunsen and Gustav Kirchhoff introduced spectrum analysis to chemistry in the 19th century.
Thanks to Robert Wilhelm Bunsen, students worldwide still use his famous burner in experiments.
The Bunsen burner, invented by Robert Wilhelm Bunsen, revolutionized laboratory heating methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bunsen burner - a piece of laboratory equipment that produces a single open gas flame, invented by Bunsen
