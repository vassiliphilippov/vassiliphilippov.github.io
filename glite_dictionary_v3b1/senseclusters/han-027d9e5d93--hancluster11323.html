Han

proper noun
Chinese imperial dynasty, 206 BC–AD 220
Pronunciation: / hɑn / (AmE), / hæn / (BrE)
the imperial dynasty that ruled China from 206 BC to AD 220, known for territorial expansion, bureaucratic development, and cultural achievements

Examples

Han dynasty
Many innovations in science and governance originated during the Han period.
Silk production flourished under the rule of the Han emperors.
The Han dynasty is often regarded as a golden age in Chinese history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Han Chinese - the dominant ethnic group in China, named after the Han dynasty
