rotifer

noun
microscopic aquatic animal; plankton constituent
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.t̬ə.fɚ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.tɪ.fə / (BrE)
a microscopic aquatic animal with a ciliated, wheel-like organ used for feeding and locomotion, commonly found in freshwater plankton

Examples

A rotifer animal
Rotifers play an important role in the food chain of freshwater ecosystems.
A drop of pond water revealed dozens of rotifers swimming vigorously among the algae.
The scientist observed a rotifer under the microscope, fascinated by its spinning cilia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plankton - microscopic organisms drifting in water, including rotifers
