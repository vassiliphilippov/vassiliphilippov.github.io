pulley

noun
a wheel with a groove for moving a rope; simple machine
3.4 2.31/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆpÊŠli / (AmE), / ËˆpÊŠli / (BrE)
a simple machine consisting of a wheel with a grooved rim in which a rope or chain can move, used to change the direction of a force or lift objects

Examples

A pulley wheel
A flag is raised and lowered with the help of a pulley on the flagpole.
She demonstrated how a pulley can make lifting a weight much easier.
The workers used a pulley system to lift heavy beams to the upper floors.

Synonyms

  • pulley block
    4.2 0.00/m
    pulley block is a device with a grooved wheel inside a casing used to redirect force, while pulley is simply the wheel guiding the rope.
  • block
    6.8 1.73/m
    block is the grooved wheel unit used inside a casing in rope systems, while pulley is the basic wheel that lifts or redirects force.

Related Terms

  • synonym: winch - a machine for lifting or pulling using a rope or cable wound on a drum
