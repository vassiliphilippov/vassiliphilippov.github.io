glyceric acid

noun
a syrupy organic acid from glycerol oxidation
Pronunciation: / ɡlɪˈsɛrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɛrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
a syrupy organic acid obtained by the oxidation of glycerol or glyceraldehyde, used in biochemical research

Examples

Glyceric acid in metabolism
Glyceric acid plays a role in several metabolic pathways in living organisms.
Researchers measured the concentration of glyceric acid in the fermentation samples.
The oxidation of glycerol produces glyceric acid as one of its primary products.
