Celtic cross

noun
a cross with a ring around the center
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛl.tɪk krɑs / (AmE), / ˈkɛl.tɪk krɒs / (BrE)
A Latin cross with a ring surrounding the intersection, commonly found in Celtic Christian monuments and art.

Examples

A Celtic cross monument
A Celtic cross stood at the top of the hill, overlooking the village below.
She wore a silver necklace with a small Celtic cross pendant.
The ancient graveyard was filled with Celtic crosses carved from weathered stone.
