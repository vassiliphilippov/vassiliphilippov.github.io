outstare

verb
overcome by staring at someone
5.7 6.9
Pronunciation: /aʊt.ˈster/ (AmE), /aʊt.ˈsteə/ (BrE)
to overcome, unsettle, or make someone submit by staring at them for longer or more intensely

Examples

To outstare a rival
Children sometimes attempt to outstare each other in silence until someone blinks.
During the competition, Maria managed to outstare everyone and win the prize.
He tried to outstare the guard, but she remained unfazed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blink - to rapidly close and open the eyes, often losing a staring contest
  • synonym: stare down - to look fixedly at someone until they yield
