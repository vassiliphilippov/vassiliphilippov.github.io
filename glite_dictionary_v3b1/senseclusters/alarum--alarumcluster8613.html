alarum

noun
automatic warning signal, archaic/literary
9.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàl…ëÀêr…ôm / (AmE), / …ôÀàlar…ôm / (BrE)
an automatic signal, especially a sound, that warns of danger; an alarm (chiefly archaic or literary)

Examples

A alarum sounds
In Shakespearean plays, the stage direction 'Enter with alarum' signifies an urgent warning or commotion.
The old castle was equipped with a crude alarum to alert the guards to attack.
The sudden alarum startled everyone in the sleeping quarters.

Synonyms

  • alarm
    1.0 27/m
    alarm is a device or signal that loudly warns people of danger, while alarum is an older, literary word for such a warning.
  • warning signal
    3.2 0.43/m
    warning signal is a sound or visual sign that shows danger automatically, while alarum is an archaic word for such an alarm.
  • alert
    2.8 10/m
    alert is a warning that asks people to pay attention or act, while alarum is an old literary term for this kind of alarm.
