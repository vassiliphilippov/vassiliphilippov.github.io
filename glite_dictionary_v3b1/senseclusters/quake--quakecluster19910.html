quake

noun
earth tremor; earthquake
5.1 6.42/m
Pronunciation: / kweÉªk / (AmE), / kweÉªk / (BrE)
An instance of shaking and vibration at the surface of the earth caused by underground movement such as an earthquake or volcanic activity.

Examples

A strong quake
After the quake, residents gathered in open spaces, fearing aftershocks.
Scientists recorded a small quake near the coast late last night.
The city was struck by a powerful quake that damaged several buildings.

Synonyms

  • earthquake
    2.2 26/m
    an earthquake is a sudden, often damaging shaking of the ground, while a quake is any single instance of earth surface vibration.
  • temblor
    8.6 0.25/m
    a temblor is an informal American word for an earthquake, while a quake is the neutral everyday word for ground shaking.
  • seism
    9.5 0.00/m
    a seism is a technical term for a shaking of the earth's surface, while a quake is the common spoken name for that event.
  • seismic event
    4.6 0.12/m
    a seismic event is a formal, broad term for any sudden release of energy in the crust, while a quake names one shaking.
  • seismic disturbance
    6.1 0.03/m
    a seismic disturbance is a formal phrase for unusual ground movement, while a quake is the familiar short word for shaking.
