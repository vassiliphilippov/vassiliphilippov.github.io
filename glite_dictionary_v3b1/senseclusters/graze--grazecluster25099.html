graze

verb
allow animals to feed on grass
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪz / (AmE), / ɡreɪz / (BrE)
to (let animals) eat grass in a field or pasture

Examples

Cows graze grass
After the rain, the land became lush and ready for the animals to graze.
Farmers often graze their cattle in open pastures during the summer months.
The shepherd moved the flock to a new field to graze.
Cattle were allowed to graze on the open fields during summer.
Every morning, the sheep graze peacefully in the meadow.
Goats often graze on hillsides where the grass is especially lush.
