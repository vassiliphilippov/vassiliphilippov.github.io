hypotyposis

noun
vivid picturesque description (rhetorical device)
6.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.ˈtaɪ.poʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈtaɪ.pəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A vivid and picturesque description that makes a narrative appear immediate, as if unfolding before the eyes.

Examples

A hypotyposis description
Her novel's vividness is achieved through masterly hypotyposis, painting emotions and events in brilliant detail.
In classical rhetoric, hypotyposis is valued for its power to immerse listeners in the moment.
The poet uses hypotyposis to render the battlefield scene so real that readers feel they are present.
