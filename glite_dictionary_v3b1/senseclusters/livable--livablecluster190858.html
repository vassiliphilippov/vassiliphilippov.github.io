livable

adjective
suitable to live in
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪv.ə.bəl /; / ˈlɪvəbəl / (AmE), / ˈlɪv.ə.bəl /; / ˈlɪvəbl / (BrE)
(of a place or conditions) fit or suitable for living in; providing adequate or acceptable living conditions.

Examples

A livable house
After renovation, the once dilapidated house became livable again.
Many cities are working to improve air quality and transportation to become more livable.
The apartment is small but quite livable for a single person.
The apartment was old but still liveable after some repairs.
The climate in this region is barely liveable during the summer months.
They moved to the city because it is known as one of the most liveable places in the country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: habitable - suitable or good enough to live in
  • antonym: uninhabitable - not suitable to live in
  • antonym: unlivable - not suitable for living in or with
