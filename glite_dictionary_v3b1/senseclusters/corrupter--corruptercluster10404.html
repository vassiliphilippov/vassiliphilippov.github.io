corrupter

noun
one who corrupts
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌptər / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes others to become dishonest, immoral, or corrupt

Examples

A corrupter of youth
The judge called the defendant a corrupter of public trust.
Some believe that violent video games are a corrupter of young minds.
He was seen as a corrupter who influenced others to break the law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debaucher - a person who leads others into immoral behavior
  • antonym: reformer - a person who works to improve or correct moral standards
