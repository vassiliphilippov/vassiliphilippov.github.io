rotary press

noun
printing press using a revolving cylinder
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊˌtɛri prɛs / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəri prɛs / (BrE)
A printing press that prints from a revolving cylinder, used for fast, large-scale printing.

Examples

A rotary press machine
Early color printing became possible when the rotary press was adapted to handle multiple ink rollers.
The newspaper was produced overnight using a modern rotary press capable of printing thousands of copies per hour.
With the invention of the rotary press, publishers could print books and magazines much more quickly and efficiently.
