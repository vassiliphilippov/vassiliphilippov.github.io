coagulable

adjective
able to coagulate or become thick
7.7 7.4
Pronunciation: / koʊˈæɡjuˌleɪbəl / (AmE), / kəʊˈæɡjʊləbəl / (BrE)
able to coagulate or become thick and semi-solid, especially by a chemical or physical process.

Examples

Coagulable milk
Milk is coagulable when rennet is added, forming curds and whey.
Some proteins in the blood are coagulable, helping wounds to heal.
The sample remained liquid, indicating it was not coagulable under these conditions.
