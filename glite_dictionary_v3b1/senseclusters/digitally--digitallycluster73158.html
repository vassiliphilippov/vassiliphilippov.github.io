digitally

adverb
using digital technology or integers
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪdʒɪtəli / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪtəli / (BrE)
in a way that uses or relates to digital technology or discrete numerical values, especially integers or binary data.

Examples

Show time digitally
Modern clocks often display the time digitally rather than with hands.
The music was recorded digitally for higher quality and easier editing.
The photograph was enhanced digitally before it was printed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analogue - in a way that uses continuous signals or varying values, not digital
