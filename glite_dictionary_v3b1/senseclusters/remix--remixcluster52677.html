remix

verb
to alter a song
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈmɪks / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmɪks / (BrE)
to create a new version of a song or other creative work by changing, combining, or rearranging its elements

Examples

To remix a song
The producer decided to remix the popular song to give it a fresh sound.
Several artists have remixed the track, each adding their own unique style.
Fans were excited to hear the new remix at the concert last night.
The producer decided to remix the hit single to give it a fresh sound.
Many DJs remix popular songs to create unique versions for their sets.
She spent hours in the studio remixing the track for the upcoming album.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or initial version of something
  • synonym: rework - to make changes to something to improve or update it
