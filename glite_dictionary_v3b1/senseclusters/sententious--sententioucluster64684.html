sententious

adjective
concise and meaningful; sometimes pompous
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈtɛnʃəs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɛnʃəs / (BrE)
using few words in a way that is pompous or moralizing

Examples

His sententious remarks
His sententious remarks packed a great deal of wisdom into just a few words.
She delivered a sententious speech that was brief but full of important ideas.
The author's sententious writing style gave the novel an air of moral authority.
He delivered a sententious toast that seemed more like a sermon than a celebration.
Her speech was so sententious that the audience quickly lost interest in her lofty moralizing.
The professor's lectures often became overly sententious, filled with maxims and moral advice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphoristic - expressed in concise and memorable form
  • synonym: didactic - intended to teach, especially in a moralizing way
  • synonym: pithy - concise and forcefully expressive
  • antonym: terse - sparingly worded; brief and direct in a way that may seem unfriendly
