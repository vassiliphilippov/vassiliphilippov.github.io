inflammatory bowel disease

noun
chronic inflammation of the digestive tract
7.5 2.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌflæməˌtɔri ˈbaʊəl dɪˌzis / (AmE), / ɪnˌflamətəri ˈbaʊəl dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A chronic medical condition involving inflammation of the digestive tract, especially the colon and small intestine, typically referring to Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis.

Examples

Manage inflammatory bowel disease
A healthy diet can sometimes help manage inflammatory bowel disease in some patients.
Many people with inflammatory bowel disease require medication to control their symptoms.
She was diagnosed with inflammatory bowel disease after months of abdominal pain and discomfort.
