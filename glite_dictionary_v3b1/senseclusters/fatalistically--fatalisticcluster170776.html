fatalistically

adverb
with acceptance of fate
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌfeɪtəlɪsˈtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌfeɪtəlɪsˈtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that shows belief that all events are predetermined and cannot be changed by human effort

Examples

React fatalistically to news
She reacted fatalistically to the news, believing nothing could be done to change the outcome.
He fatalistically accepted the loss, making no effort to protest or complain.
Many people respond fatalistically to events they feel are out of their control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: optimistically - in a way that shows hope and confidence about the future
  • synonym: resignedly - in a way that shows acceptance of something unpleasant that cannot be changed
