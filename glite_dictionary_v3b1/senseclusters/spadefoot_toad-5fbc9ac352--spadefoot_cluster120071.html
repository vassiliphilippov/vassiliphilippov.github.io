spadefoot toad

noun
burrowing toad with spade-like hind feet
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈspeɪd.fʊt ˌtoʊd / (AmE), / ˈspeɪd.fʊt ˌtəʊd / (BrE)
A burrowing toad found in the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by a horny, spade-like projection on each hind foot used for digging.

Examples

The spadefoot toad digs
A distinctive feature of the spadefoot toad is the hard, spade-like structure on its hind foot.
Some spadefoot toads emerge after rain to breed in temporary pools.
The spadefoot toad can quickly burrow underground to escape dry conditions.
