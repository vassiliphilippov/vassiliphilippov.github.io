lighting

noun
providing artificial light
4.1 3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the arrangement, use, or effect of artificial light in a space, event, or scene (including the equipment and techniques used)

Examples

Room lighting
Good lighting can completely transform the atmosphere of a room.
Proper lighting is essential for reading and studying efficiently.
The director worked closely with the lighting designer to create dramatic effects on stage.
Professional lighting is essential for shooting high-quality movies.
She adjusted the lighting before the concert began to highlight the main performer.
The lighting in the play created a dramatic and mysterious atmosphere.
She chose soft lighting for a warm and welcoming atmosphere.
The museum's careful lighting highlights the beauty of each painting.
We need better lighting in the kitchen because it is too dim.

Related Terms

  • antonym: darkness - the absence of light
  • synonym: illumination - the act of lighting up a place; lighting
  • synonym: spotlight - a strong beam of light on a small area, especially the stage
