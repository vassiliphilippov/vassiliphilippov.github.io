latent diabetes

noun
mild, symptomless form of diabetes detected by tests
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪtənt daɪəˈbiːtiz / (AmE), / ˈleɪtnt daɪəˈbiːtiːz / (BrE)
A mild form of diabetes mellitus with no obvious symptoms, but abnormal results in diagnostic tests.

Examples

Mild latent diabetes
Doctors may recommend monitoring for those with abnormal test results indicating latent diabetes.
Routine screening can help uncover latent diabetes before it progresses to more serious stages.
Some people live with latent diabetes for years without knowing they have it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diabetes mellitus - chronic condition characterized by high blood sugar
