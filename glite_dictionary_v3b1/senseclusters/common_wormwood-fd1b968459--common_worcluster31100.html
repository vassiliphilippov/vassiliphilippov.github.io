common wormwood

noun
aromatic herb used in absinthe
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈwɜrmˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈwɜːmwʊd / (BrE)
an aromatic herb of temperate Eurasia and North Africa with a bitter taste, used to make the liqueur absinthe

Examples

Common wormwood plant
Common wormwood has been cultivated for centuries for its medicinal properties and its role in making absinthe.
Gardeners often grow common wormwood as an ornamental plant and for its aromatic qualities.
The leaves of common wormwood have a distinctly bitter flavor.

Related Terms
