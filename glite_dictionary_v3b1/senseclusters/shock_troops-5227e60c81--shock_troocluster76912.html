shock troops

noun
specially trained assault soldiers
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk trupz / (AmE), / ʃɒk truːps / (BrE)
soldiers specially trained and equipped to lead assaults, often in the front line of attacks

Examples

Shock troops lead attacks
During the early stages of the battle, shock troops led a fierce attack on the fortress.
Historically, shock troops have often suffered high casualties due to their front-line position.
The general deployed his shock troops to break through the enemy's defenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stormtroopers - soldiers trained for shock assaults, especially in WWI Germany
