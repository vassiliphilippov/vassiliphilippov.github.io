praefectus

noun
administrative official
9.9
Pronunciation: /preɪ.ˈfek.təs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfek.təs/ (AmE), /preɪ.ˈfek.təs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfek.təs/ (BrE)
a civil or administrative official or magistrate, especially in ancient Rome, who governs or manages a region, department, or office

Examples

Roman praefectus of a province
The praefectus of the province was responsible for maintaining order and collecting taxes.
In ancient Rome, a praefectus often held significant authority over military and civil matters.
The newly appointed praefectus introduced reforms to improve local administration.
The praefectus was responsible for maintaining order in the province.
A praefectus could command troops or oversee public works in ancient Rome.
During the crisis, the emperor appointed a new praefectus to restore stability.
