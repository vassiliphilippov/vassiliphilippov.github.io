nephrotoxicity

noun
kidney damage from toxins
Pronunciation: / ˌnɛfroʊtɑkˈsɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌnɛfrəʊtɒkˈsɪsəti / (BrE)
harmful effect on the kidneys caused by a toxic substance or drug

Examples

Nephrotoxicity from drugs
The new medication was withdrawn after several patients developed nephrotoxicity during clinical trials.
Doctors monitor kidney function closely to prevent nephrotoxicity when prescribing certain antibiotics.
Long-term use of some painkillers can increase the risk of nephrotoxicity in vulnerable individuals.
The new medication was discontinued because of its high nephrotoxicity in clinical trials.
Doctors monitor kidney function to detect any signs of nephrotoxicity during treatment.
Reducing the dose can help minimize the risk of nephrotoxicity in vulnerable patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hepatotoxicity - liver damage caused by toxic substances
  • degree higher: renal failure - a condition in which the kidneys stop working properly
