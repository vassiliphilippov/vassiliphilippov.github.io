phage

noun
virus that infects bacteria
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfeɪdʒ / (BrE)
A virus that infects and reproduces inside bacteria, often destroying the bacterial cell in the process.

Examples

A phage virus
Researchers isolated several types of phage from wastewater to target harmful bacteria.
Scientists are studying phage therapy as an alternative to antibiotics for resistant bacterial infections.
The phage attaches to a bacterial cell and injects its genetic material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteriophage - a virus that specifically infects bacteria
