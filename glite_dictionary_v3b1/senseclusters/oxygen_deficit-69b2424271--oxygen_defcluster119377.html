oxygen deficit

noun
temporary shortage of oxygen in cells during exercise
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksɪdʒən ˈdɛfəsɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪdʒən ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (BrE)
a temporary shortage of oxygen in cells that occurs during intense physical activity until the body catches up with the demand

Examples

An oxygen deficit after sprint
After sprinting for several minutes, the athlete experienced oxygen deficit and had to slow down.
During vigorous cycling, your body may temporarily fall into oxygen deficit.
Proper training helps the muscles recover faster from oxygen deficit during intense exercise.
