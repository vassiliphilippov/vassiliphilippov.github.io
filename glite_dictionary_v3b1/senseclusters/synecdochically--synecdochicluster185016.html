synecdochically

adverb
using synecdoche
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˌnɛkˈdɑkɪkli / (AmE), / sɪˌnɛkˈdɒkɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that uses or relates to synecdoche, a figure of speech where a part represents the whole or vice versa

Examples

To speak synecdochically
The poet synecdochically described the nation as 'a sea of faces' in the crowd.
In literature, places are often referred to synecdochically by mentioning a single landmark.
The journalist synecdochically used 'the White House' to mean the entire U.S. administration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metonymically - in a way that uses metonymy, substituting a related term for the thing meant
