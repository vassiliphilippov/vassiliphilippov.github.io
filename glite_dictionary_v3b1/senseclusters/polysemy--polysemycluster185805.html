polysemy

noun
having multiple meanings (word or phrase)
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪsəmi / (AmE), / pɒˈlɪsɪmi / (BrE)
the phenomenon of a single word or phrase having multiple meanings or senses, typically used in different contexts

Examples

The polysemy of words
Many jokes rely on the polysemy of certain words to create double meanings.
The polysemy of the word "bank" makes it an interesting case in semantic studies.
Understanding polysemy is essential for translators working with texts that involve wordplay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguity - uncertainty of meaning or intention
  • antonym: monosemy - the quality of having only one meaning
