privatism

noun
focus on private interests
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvətɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvətɪzəm / (BrE)
a belief or attitude that values private interests or personal privacy over public or community involvement

Examples

Privatism in daily life
Some critics argue that privatism weakens social responsibility and community engagement.
The rise of privatism has influenced how people view their roles in society.
Debates about public policy often highlight the tension between privatism and collective action.
The rise of privatism has led many people to avoid participating in local government.
Some critics argue that privatism weakens the sense of community in modern societies.
After years in the public eye, he turned to privatism and enjoyed a quieter life at home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving priority to the group over the individual
  • antonym: communitarianism - the belief in the importance of community and public life
  • synonym: individualism - the belief in the importance of individual rights and independence
