detest

verb
dislike intensely
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛst / (BrE)
to dislike intensely; to feel strong aversion or antipathy towards someone or something

Examples

To detest bad food
He detested the taste of broccoli as a child, but now he enjoys it.
Many people detest dishonesty in their friends and colleagues.
She absolutely detests being late to meetings and will always arrive early.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abhor - to regard with disgust and hatred
  • synonym: hate - to feel intense or passionate dislike for
  • antonym: like - to find agreeable or enjoyable
  • antonym: love - to feel deep affection or strong liking for someone or something
