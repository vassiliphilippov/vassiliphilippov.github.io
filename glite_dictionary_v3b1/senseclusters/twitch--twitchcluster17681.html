twitch

verb
move or pull with sudden motion
2.1 2.4
Pronunciation: / twɪtʃ / (AmE), / twɪtʃ / (BrE)
to make a sudden, small, and often uncontrolled movement, especially of a body part or muscle

Examples

To twitch an eyelid
His eye began to twitch nervously during the interview.
She twitched the curtain aside to look outside.
The horse twitched its ears at every little sound.
His fingers began to twitch after he drank too much coffee.
She felt her eyelid twitch involuntarily as she grew nervous.
The horse's ear would twitch at the slightest sound.
He felt his right eyelid twitch during the meeting, but tried to ignore it.
His mouth twitched slightly as he tried to hold back a smile.
Whenever she gets nervous, her fingers unconsciously twitch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeze - to become motionless or stop moving suddenly
  • synonym: jerk - to move with a sudden sharp motion
  • synonym: spasm - an involuntary sudden muscular movement
