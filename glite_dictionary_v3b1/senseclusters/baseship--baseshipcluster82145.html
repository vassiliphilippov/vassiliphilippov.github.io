baseship

noun
space command center
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsʃɪp / (BrE)
a large vessel or spacecraft that acts as a main base or command center, supporting smaller craft or operations (especially in science fiction or at sea)

Examples

A baseship command
The enemy fleet retreated as soon as the baseship appeared on their scanners.
Engineers worked around the clock to repair the damaged baseship after the asteroid collision.
In the science fiction series, the baseship served as the main headquarters for the alien invaders.
After a long day at sea, the research team returned to the baseship for debriefing and rest.
The baseship coordinated the movements of several smaller boats during the rescue operation.
Supplies and equipment were delivered to the baseship before the expedition began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depot ship - a ship used as a base for storing supplies and supporting other vessels
  • synonym: mothership - a large ship or spacecraft that carries and supports smaller ones
  • synonym: starship - a spacecraft designed for interstellar travel
