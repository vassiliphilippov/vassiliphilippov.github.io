autotomy

noun
the shedding of a body part as a defense
Pronunciation: / ɔˈtɑtəmi / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒtəmi / (BrE)
the ability of an animal to intentionally shed or cast off a body part, such as a tail or limb, typically as a defensive response when threatened or injured

Examples

Autotomy in lizards
Lizards employ autotomy to escape from predators by detaching their tails.
Some crabs use autotomy to drop a claw and evade capture.
The phenomenon of autotomy in starfish allows them to regrow lost arms.
