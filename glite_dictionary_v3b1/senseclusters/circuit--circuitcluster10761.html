circuit

noun
judicial division under a circuit court of appeals
3.4 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.kət/ /; / /ˈsɜːr.kɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.kɪt/ (BrE)
(law) One of the judicial divisions of a state or of the United States, originally so named because judges traveled (rode circuit) to hold court in different places; specifically, one of the twelve regions of the United States covered by a circuit court of appeals.

Examples

Circuit court
In the Ninth circuit, the court's decisions set precedent for several western U.S. states.
Judges once traveled from town to town to preside over each circuit court session.
The case was appealed to the federal circuit covering their region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: court of appeals - a court that hears appeals from lower courts
