spectrum line

noun
isolated band in a spectrum
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛktrəm laɪn / (AmE), / ˈspɛktrəm laɪn / (BrE)
an isolated narrow band in a spectrum, produced by radiation at a specific frequency or wavelength

Examples

A spectrum line band
Each spectrum line corresponds to a transition between energy levels in atoms or molecules.
Scientists observed a bright spectrum line caused by hydrogen in the emission spectrum.
The astronomer measured the wavelength of the spectrum line to determine the star's composition.
