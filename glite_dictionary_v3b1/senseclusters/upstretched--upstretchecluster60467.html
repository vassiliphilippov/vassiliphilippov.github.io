upstretched

verb
to stretch upward
5.7
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈstrɛtʃt / (AmE), / ʌpˈstrɛtʃt / (BrE)
past tense and past participle of upstretch: to stretch or hold something upward.

Examples

She upstretched her hand
She upstretched her arms to reach the top shelf in the kitchen.
The children upstretched their hands eagerly to answer the teacher's question.
He upstretched his hand to catch the falling letter before it hit the floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position
  • synonym: raise - to lift or move something to a higher position
