hypopituitarism

noun
pituitary hormone deficiency
9.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.poʊ.pɪ.ˈtjuː.ə.tə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /haɪ.pəʊ.pɪ.ˈtjuː.ə.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which the pituitary gland produces insufficient amounts of one or more hormones

Examples

Hypopituitarism diagnosis
The patient was diagnosed with hypopituitarism after showing symptoms of fatigue and weight loss.
Treatment for hypopituitarism often involves hormone replacement therapy.
Doctors monitor people with hypopituitarism regularly to adjust their medication as needed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperpituitarism - excessive hormone production by the pituitary gland
  • synonym: pituitary insufficiency - reduced function of the pituitary gland
