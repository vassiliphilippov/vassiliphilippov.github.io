law of continuation

noun
Gestalt principle of perceiving continuous lines

a principle in Gestalt psychology that refers to the tendency to perceive lines or patterns as continuing in their established direction, rather than as separate or disjointed segments.

Examples

The law of continuation
According to the law of continuation, we tend to see a broken line as a single, unbroken path.
Designers use the law of continuation to create visually pleasing interfaces that guide users' eyes naturally.
In visual perception, the law of continuation explains why railway tracks appear to meet at the horizon.
