razzing

noun
mocking cry or noise of contempt
9.7 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈræzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræzɪŋ / (BrE)
a loud cry or noise, often made by blowing through the lips, used to express ridicule, displeasure, or contempt

Examples

Good-natured razzing
He was used to some good-natured razzing from his friends when he made mistakes.
Her speech was greeted with some playful razzing from the students.
The crowd responded with loud razzing after the team's third error.
