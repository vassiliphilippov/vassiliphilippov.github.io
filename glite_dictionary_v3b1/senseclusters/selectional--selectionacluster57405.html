selectional

adjective
word combination rules
Pronunciation: / səˈlɛkʃənəl / (AmE), / sɪˈlɛkʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the restrictions or rules about which words can combine together in a sentence

Examples

Selectional restrictions
Linguists study selectional restrictions to understand how verbs and nouns can be combined.
The selectional properties of certain adjectives limit which nouns they can describe.
Teachers often explain selectional rules when helping students form correct English sentences.

Related Terms
