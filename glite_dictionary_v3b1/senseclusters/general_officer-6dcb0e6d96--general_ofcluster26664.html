general officer

noun
high-ranking military officer above colonel
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɚl ˈɔfɪsɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an officer in the Army, Air Force, or Marines of a rank above colonel, such as brigadier general, major general, lieutenant general, or general

Examples

A general officer rank
At the ceremony, several retired general officers were honored for their years of service.
Becoming a general officer requires decades of experience and exemplary leadership.
The general officer was responsible for overseeing the strategic operations of the division.
