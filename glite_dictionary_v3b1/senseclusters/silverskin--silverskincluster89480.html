silverskin

noun
thin silvery membrane
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvərˌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvəˌskɪn / (BrE)
a thin, silvery or papery outer layer or membrane found on foods such as meat, garlic, or coffee beans

Examples

The silverskin layer
It is important to remove the silverskin from beef before grilling to ensure tenderness.
The chef carefully peeled away the silverskin from each garlic clove.
Leaving the silverskin on the meat can make it tough after cooking.
The silverskin is removed from coffee beans during the roasting process.
After roasting, the silverskin collects as a light, flaky residue in the roaster.
Baristas sometimes refer to silverskin as chaff when cleaning their equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaff - the dry, scaly covering separated from seeds or grains during processing
  • synonym: membrane - a thin layer of tissue covering a surface
  • synonym: peel - the outer covering of a fruit or vegetable
