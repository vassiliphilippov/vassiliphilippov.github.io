tenuis

adjective
unaspirated sound
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈte.njʊ.ɪs/ /; / /ˈte.nʊ.ɪs/ (AmE), /ˈte.njuː.ɪs/ /; / /ˈte.nuː.ɪs/ (BrE)
describing a sound, especially a consonant, that is unaspirated, voiceless, and produced with little force

Examples

Tenuis consonant sound
In Korean, a tenuis consonant is produced without a burst of air or vocal cord vibration.
Linguists often describe certain stops in Thai as tenuis because they lack aspiration.
The term tenuis is used in phonetics to refer to plain, unaspirated voiceless stops.
