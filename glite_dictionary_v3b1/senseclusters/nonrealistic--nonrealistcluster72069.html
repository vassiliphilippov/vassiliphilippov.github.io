nonrealistic

adjective
not true to life
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnriəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnriəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
not representing things in a way that is true to life or reality

Examples

Nonrealistic art style
The director chose a nonrealistic style for the film to emphasize its dreamlike quality.
Her painting was highly nonrealistic, using bold colors and abstract shapes.
The play's nonrealistic dialogue made it feel more like a poem than a conversation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realistic - showing things as they really are
  • synonym: unrealistic - not showing or accepting things as they really are
