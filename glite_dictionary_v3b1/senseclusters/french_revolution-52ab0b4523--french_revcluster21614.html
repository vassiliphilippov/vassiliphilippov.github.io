French Revolution

noun
revolution in France, 1789–1799
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / frɛntʃ ˌrɛvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the period of radical social and political change in France from 1789 to 1799, marked by the overthrow of the monarchy and the rise of democracy

Examples

The French Revolution period
Many political ideals emerged from the French Revolution and continue to influence modern democracies.
She wrote her thesis on the effects of the French Revolution on European society.
The storming of the Bastille was a pivotal event during the French Revolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Russian Revolution - the revolution in Russia in 1917 that led to the overthrow of the Tsarist government
  • antonym: monarchy - a system of government with a monarch at the head, overthrown in the French Revolution
