concord

verb
to be in agreement; to harmonize
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.kɔrd /; / ˈkɑn.kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kɔːd / (BrE)
(formal) to be in agreement or harmony; to correspond or go together well

Examples

To concord with rules
The new law does not concord with existing regulations.
The two theories concorded surprisingly well during the experiment.
Their interests rarely concord, leading to frequent disagreements.
Both historians concord that the war ended in 1945.
Several studies concord with the new evidence presented by the research team.
Their views concord on most political matters, which makes cooperation easy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agree - to have the same opinion or accept the same point of view
  • antonym: disagree - to not agree; to be in conflict or contrast
  • synonym: harmonize - to be in harmony; to go well together
