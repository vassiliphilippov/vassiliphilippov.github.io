Crohn's disease

noun
chronic inflammatory bowel disease
8.4
Pronunciation: / kroʊnz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / krəʊnz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a chronic inflammatory disease of the gastrointestinal tract, especially the ileum and colon, characterized by periods of abdominal pain, diarrhea, and weight loss.

Examples

Crohn's disease symptoms
New medications have helped many Crohn's disease patients improve their quality of life.
People living with Crohn's disease often face challenges in managing their diet.
The doctor diagnosed her with Crohn's disease after several months of persistent symptoms.
