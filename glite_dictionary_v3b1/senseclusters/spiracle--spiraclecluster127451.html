spiracle

noun
a breathing orifice in some animals
8.0 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈspɪ.rə.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈspɪ.rə.kəl/ (BrE)
A small opening or orifice used for breathing, found in some animals such as insects, fish, and some amphibians.

Examples

A spiracle opening
During development, the tadpole's spiracle plays a key role in respiration.
Some fish have a spiracle located behind each eye for water intake.
The insect breathes through a series of spiracles along its abdomen.
