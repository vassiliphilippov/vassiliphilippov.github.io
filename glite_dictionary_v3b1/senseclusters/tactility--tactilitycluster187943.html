tactility

noun
sense or faculty of touch
7.6
Pronunciation: / tækˈtɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / tækˈtɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the quality or faculty of being able to perceive touch, pressure, heat, or pain through the skin

Examples

The tactility of skin
Her sense of tactility was heightened after losing her sight, helping her navigate the world.
Researchers studied the tactility of various materials to design more comfortable clothing.
The tactility of the sculpture invited visitors to run their hands along its smooth surface.
