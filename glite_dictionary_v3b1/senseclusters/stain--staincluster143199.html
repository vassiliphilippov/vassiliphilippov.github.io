stain

noun
mark or blemish, especially on reputation or cleanliness
6.6
Pronunciation: / steɪn / (AmE), / steɪn / (BrE)
a mark or event that damages someone's reputation, honor, or character; disgrace or shame

Examples

A stain on reputation
The accusation cast a stain on the entire organization.
The scandal left a permanent stain on his reputation.
There was no stain on her honesty or integrity.
Despite many achievements, there was one stain on her otherwise flawless record.
The company's involvement in the fraud became a stain on its reputation.
The scandal left a permanent stain on his political career.
His betrayal was seen as a stain on the honor of the entire family.
The company could not remove the stain of corruption from its history.
The scandal left a lasting stain on the politician's reputation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blemish - a small mark or flaw that spoils the appearance or reputation
  • synonym: blot - a mark or stain; also something that tarnishes reputation
  • synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect as the result of a dishonorable action
  • antonym: honor - high respect or esteem; good reputation
  • synonym: taint - a trace of a bad or undesirable quality or substance
