nonabsolute

adjective
without perfect pitch
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈæbsəˌluːt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈæbsəluːt / (BrE)
describing someone or something that does not have absolute (perfect) pitch and relies on relative pitch instead

Examples

Nonabsolute pitch
The music teacher explained that most people are nonabsolute and use relative pitch to identify notes.
Because he was nonabsolute, he needed a reference note before singing in tune.
Unlike her classmate with perfect pitch, she was nonabsolute and relied on a piano to start.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolute - having perfect pitch; not dependent on comparison
  • synonym: relative - based on comparison or relation to something else
