diastole

noun
phase of heartbeat when heart fills with blood
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæstəˌli / (AmE), / daɪˈæstəli / (BrE)
The phase of the heartbeat when the chambers of the heart widen and fill with blood following contraction.

Examples

Diastole phase
During diastole, the heart chambers relax and fill with blood.
Problems with diastole can affect how well the heart pumps blood throughout the body.
The measurement of blood pressure includes readings taken during both systole and diastole.

Related Terms

  • antonym: systole - the contraction phase of the heartbeat
