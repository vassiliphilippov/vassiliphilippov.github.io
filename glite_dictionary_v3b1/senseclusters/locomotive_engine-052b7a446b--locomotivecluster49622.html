locomotive engine

noun
railway vehicle that pulls trains
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊ.kəˈmoʊ.tɪv ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kəˈməʊ.tɪv ˈɛn.dʒɪn / (BrE)
A self-propelled railway vehicle that supplies the motive power for a train.

Examples

A locomotive engine pulls
A powerful locomotive engine hauled the freight across the country.

The museum restored an old locomotive engine to working condition.
