remobilization

noun
internal nutrient movement
7.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêmo äb…ôla…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêm…ô äb…™la…™Ààze…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the movement of nutrients or stored substances within an organism from one tissue or organ to another, often to support growth or development

Examples

Remobilization of nutrients
Plants often depend on remobilization of nutrients from older leaves to support new growth.
During drought, remobilization helps the plant survive by reallocating stored resources.
The efficiency of remobilization can affect crop yield and quality in agriculture.
