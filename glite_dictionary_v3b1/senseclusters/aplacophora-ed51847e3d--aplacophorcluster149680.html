Aplacophora

noun
an order of shell-less marine mollusks
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪpləˈkɒfərə / (AmE), / ˌæpləˈkɒfərə / (BrE)
an order of small, worm-like, shell-less mollusks distinguished by having a soft, cylindrical body covered with spicules

Examples

The Aplacophora order
Scientists have discovered a new species within the Aplacophora order near the deep-sea vents.
The Aplacophora play an important role in the benthic ecosystem of the ocean floor.
Unlike most mollusks, Aplacophora lack an external shell and have a worm-like appearance.
