trackball

noun
computer input device with a rotatable ball
Pronunciation: / ˈtrækˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈtrækˌbɔːl / (BrE)
an input device consisting of a ball housed in a socket, used to control the movement of a cursor on a computer screen by rotating the ball with the fingers or palm

Examples

A trackball device
A trackball allows the user to control the cursor by moving the ball with their fingers.
Many graphic designers prefer to use a trackball instead of a traditional mouse for precision work.
The old workstation had a trackball attached rather than the usual optical mouse.
