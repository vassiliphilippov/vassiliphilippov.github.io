dirty

adjective
not clean; covered with dirt or grime
1.0 25/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…ùÀêti /; / Ààd…ùti / (AmE), / Ààd…úÀêti / (BrE)
not clean, soiled, or contaminated with dirt, pollution, or germs

Examples

Dirty clothes
After playing soccer in the rain, all the kids came home dirty and wet.
I need to wash these dirty socks before the trip.
The windows were so dirty that we could barely see outside.
Authorities were concerned about the possibility of a dirty bomb being used in a terrorist attack.
Residents near the plant complained that their water supply was dirty and unsafe to drink.
The factory used outdated equipment that made the air dirty with toxic chemicals.
A dirty operating theatre poses a risk of infection to patients.
Antibiotics may be prescribed if the injury is considered a dirty wound.
The surgeon treated several dirty wounds after the accident.

Synonyms

  • soiled
    2.5 0.05/m
    soiled means stained with dirt or marks, while dirty is a general word for not clean or contaminated.
  • unclean
    1.3 0.36/m
    unclean describes things that are not clean and may soil other items, while dirty is general and neutral.
  • filthy
    1.5 6.06/m
    filthy feels extremely dirty and often smells bad, whereas dirty just means not clean or contaminated.
  • foul
    2.7 1.07/m
    foul suggests something is disgustingly dirty or smelly, while dirty simply means not clean or contaminated.
  • grimy
    3.7 2.15/m
    grimy means covered in ingrained dirt or soot, while dirty covers any lack of cleanliness or contamination.
  • grubby
    1.4 0.92/m
    grubby looks slightly dirty or untidy on skin or clothes, while dirty is the general word for unclean.
  • mucky
    3.2 0.44/m
    mucky describes things messy with mud or wet dirt, whereas dirty can mean not clean in many ways.
  • unwashed
    1.0 0.84/m
    unwashed means not washed with water or soap, while dirty can describe any kind of soil or contamination.
