scarlet sumac

noun
North American shrub with red berries
Pronunciation: / ˈskɑːr.lɪt ˈsuː.mæk / (AmE), / ˈskɑː.lɪt ˈsjuː.mæk / (BrE)
a nonpoisonous shrub of eastern North America with waxy compound leaves, green clusters of flowers, and red berries

Examples

Scarlet sumac shrub
Many landscapers choose scarlet sumac for its hardiness and vibrant coloration.
The scarlet sumac brightened the woods with its clusters of red fruit in late summer.
Wildlife often feed on the berries of the scarlet sumac during the winter months.
