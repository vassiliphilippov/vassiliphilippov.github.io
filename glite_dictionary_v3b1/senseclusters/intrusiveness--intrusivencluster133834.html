intrusiveness

noun
quality of being intrusive or unwelcome
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtruːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈtruːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being intrusive, especially by entering or interfering without invitation or welcome

Examples

High intrusiveness
Her constant questioning showed a level of intrusiveness that made her colleagues uncomfortable.
Many people dislike advertisements because of their intrusiveness in personal spaces.
The intrusiveness of security checks at the airport sparked public debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discretion - the quality of behaving or speaking without causing offense or revealing confidential information
  • synonym: intrusive - tending to intrude or encroach; unwelcome interference
