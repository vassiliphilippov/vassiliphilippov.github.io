winker

noun
side eye-cover on a horse's bridle
7.6 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪŋ.kər/ (AmE), /ˈwɪŋ.kə/ (BrE)
A piece of leather or plastic attached to a horse's bridle or halter beside each eye, designed to restrict its peripheral vision and prevent distractions.

Examples

Horse winker straps
Some trainers believe that fitting winkers helps nervous horses focus straight ahead during a race.
The winker on the left side was slightly loose and needed adjustment.
The jockey made sure the horse’s winkers were secure before heading to the starting gate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blinker - a similar device attached to a horse’s bridle to limit side vision
