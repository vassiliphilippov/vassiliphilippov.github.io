kleptomania

noun
compulsive urge to steal without need
Pronunciation: / ˌklɛp.toʊˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (AmE), / ˌklɛp.təˈmeɪ.ni.ə / (BrE)
a mental disorder characterized by a recurrent urge to steal items that are not needed for personal use or monetary value

Examples

kleptomania disorder
After being diagnosed with kleptomania, she started therapy to control her impulses.
People with kleptomania often feel guilt and shame after stealing.
The defendant's repeated shoplifting was attributed to kleptomania rather than financial hardship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impulse control disorder - psychiatric disorder characterized by problems controlling impulses
  • degree higher: theft - the action or crime of stealing
