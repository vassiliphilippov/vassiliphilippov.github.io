underhand

adjective, adverb
with the hand below shoulder level
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəhænd / (BrE)
with the hand swung below shoulder level, especially when throwing, serving, or striking

Examples

Underhand throw
He served the ball underhand to surprise his opponent.
Softballs are often pitched underhand in amateur leagues.
The coach suggested trying an underhand throw for more accuracy.
In badminton, an underhand serve is often used for greater control.
She used an underhand motion to toss the ball to her teammate.
The pitcher delivered an underhand throw to surprise the batter.
