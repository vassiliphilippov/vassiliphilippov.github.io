motley fool

noun
historical court jester
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑtli ful / (AmE), / ˈmɒtli fuːl / (BrE)
a professional clown or jester in the Middle Ages, employed to entertain a king or nobleman

Examples

The motley fool acts
In Shakespeare’s plays, the motley fool is sometimes the wisest character.
The king often laughed at the antics of his motley fool during banquets.
Wearing bells and a colorful suit, the motley fool delighted the entire court.
