sickler

noun
person using a sickle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪklər / (AmE), / ˈsɪklə / (BrE)
a person who harvests grain or other crops using a sickle, especially in traditional or historical contexts

Examples

A skilled sickler
The village once relied on a skilled sickler to bring in the wheat each summer.
In old paintings, a sickler is often shown working in golden fields at dawn.
Before machines, every farm needed at least one experienced sickler during harvest time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harvester - a person or machine that gathers crops
  • synonym: reaper - a person who cuts and gathers crops at harvest
