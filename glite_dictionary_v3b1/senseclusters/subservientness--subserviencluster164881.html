subservientness

noun
quality of being subservient
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôbÀàs…úr.vi.…ônt.n…ôs / (AmE), / s…ôbÀàs…úÀê.vi.…ônt.n…™s / (BrE)
The state or quality of being subservient; willingness to obey others unquestioningly or act in a subordinate manner.

Examples

The subservientness of workers
Critics argued that her subservientness reflected a reluctance to challenge authority.
Her subservientness in meetings made it difficult for her opinions to be heard.
The leader exploited the subservientness of his followers to maintain control.

Synonyms

  • submissiveness
    6.0 0.29/m
    submissiveness is a readiness to accept another's authority calmly, while subservientness shows eager obedience and a lower, subordinate attitude.
  • servility
    8.7 0.24/m
    servility is excessive, often cringing willingness to serve others, while subservientness is a general readiness to obey and be subordinate.
  • obsequiousness
    9.9 0.07/m
    obsequiousness is fawning, flattering behavior to win favor, while subservientness is simple willing obedience without flattery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subservience - the condition of being excessively willing to serve others
