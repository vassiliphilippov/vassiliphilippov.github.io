tighten one's belt

verb
live more frugally; reduce spending
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtən wʌnz bɛlt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪt(ə)n wʌnz bɛlt /; / ˈtaɪtən wʌnz belt / (BrE)
to reduce spending and live more frugally because of financial problems or less income

Examples

To tighten his belt now

When his salary was reduced, he had to tighten his belt and eliminate unnecessary expenses.
With rising prices, many people are being forced to tighten their belts this year.
