dithyrambic

adjective
in the style of a dithyramb; passionate or wild
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪθɪˈræmbɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪθɪˈræmbɪk / (BrE)
written or performed in the style of a dithyramb, often marked by passionate, impetuous, or wild expression.

Examples

Dithyrambic chorus
Ancient Greek festivals often featured dithyrambic choruses celebrating Dionysus.
Some critics found the director's dithyrambic praise of the film excessive and unbalanced.
The poet's dithyrambic verses overflowed with enthusiasm and unrestrained emotion.
