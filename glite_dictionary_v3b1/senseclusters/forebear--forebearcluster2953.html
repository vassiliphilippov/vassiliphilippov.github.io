forebear

noun
ancestor; person you descend from
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌbɛr / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌbɛə / (BrE)
a person, especially one in the past, from whom you are directly descended; an ancestor.

Examples

A forebear in family
His forebear fought in the Civil War, passing down stories through generations.
Many of our forebears immigrated to seek better opportunities for their families.
She felt a deep connection to her Scottish forebears while visiting the old family estate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancestor - a person in your family who lived a long time ago
  • antonym: descendant - a person descended from an ancestor
