pull the football

verb
deceive by withdrawing support repeatedly
Pronunciation: / pʊl ðə ˈfʊtbɔl / (AmE), / pʊl ðə ˈfʊtbɔːl / (BrE)
to repeatedly deceive or disappoint someone by withdrawing promised support or help at the last moment

Examples

To pull the football again
After being promised a raise, I felt my boss would pull the football again and not deliver.
Every year management pulls the football with our bonus, saying it will come but never does.
She warned me not to trust him because he tends to pull the football when it matters most.
