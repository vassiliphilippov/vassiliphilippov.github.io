mutter

verb
speak quietly in a complaining way
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌt̬ɚ /; / ˈmʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtə / (BrE)
to speak in a low, indistinct voice, often when complaining or not wanting to be heard clearly

Examples

To mutter under breath
He began to mutter under his breath after reading the email.
She tends to mutter complaints about her boss when no one is listening.
The students would mutter quietly about the difficult exam as they left the room.
He muttered something I couldn't quite catch before leaving the room.
She began to mutter under her breath as the meeting dragged on.
Stop muttering and speak more clearly if you want me to understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym: grumble - to complain about something in a quiet but slightly angry way
  • synonym: murmur - to say something in a soft, quiet voice; often suggesting continued low sound
  • antonym: shout - to speak loudly and with force
