overhead railway

noun
elevated electric railway system
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˌhɛd ˈreɪlˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəhɛd ˈreɪlweɪ / (BrE)
a railway powered by electricity that runs on a track elevated above street level

Examples

The overhead railway
As we drove below the overhead railway, the train rumbled loudly above us.
The city installed its first overhead railway in the early 20th century to ease traffic congestion.
Tourists enjoy riding the old overhead railway for its unique views of the city skyline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elevated railway - a railway elevated above ground level, usually on a viaduct or framework
