temp

verb
to work temporarily
Pronunciation: / tɛmp / (AmE), / tɛmp / (BrE)
to work in a temporary job, usually for a short period or to fill in for someone else

Examples

To temp at a company
After graduation, he decided to temp at several offices to gain experience.
Many students temp during the holidays to earn extra money.
She was temping at a publishing company when she got a full-time offer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freelance - to work independently for different companies rather than as a permanent employee
  • antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely without change
