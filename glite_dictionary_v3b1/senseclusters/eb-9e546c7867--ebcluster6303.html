EB

noun
unit of digital information (exabyte)
8.4
Pronunciation: /eb/ /; / /iː biː/ (AmE), /iː biː/ /; / /eb/ (BrE)
a very large unit of digital information (exabyte), equal to about one billion gigabytes; sometimes defined as 1,000 petabytes (10^18 bytes) or 1,024 pebibytes (2^60 bytes)

Examples

One EB of data
Global internet traffic is expected to surpass several EBs a year.
Our new data center has a storage capacity of nearly one EB.
Storing the entire library digitally would require less than one EB of space.
An EB is much larger than most organizations will ever need for their data storage.
Scientific data centers may store several EB of raw information for long-term research.
The global volume of internet traffic is expected to reach hundreds of EB in the next few years.
