Hova

noun
Merina commoner class
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊvə / (AmE), / ˈhəʊvə / (BrE)
a historical social class of free commoners in the Merina kingdom of Madagascar, distinct from nobles and slaves

Examples

Hova class in Madagascar
The Hova played a crucial role in the administration of the Merina kingdom.
Merchants and soldiers from the Hova class often supported the monarchy in Madagascar.
Unlike nobles or slaves, the Hova were free commoners with certain rights and responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: noble - a person belonging to a high social class, often with hereditary titles
  • antonym: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and forced to work for them
