bottom

noun
lowest ground under water
1.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑː.təm/ (AmE), /ˈbɒt.əm/ (BrE)
the lowest part or surface of something; the side or area opposite to the top (including the ground under water or at the base of something)

Examples

Bottom of the lake
Divers explored the bottom of the lake for lost artifacts.
Plants grew thickly at the river's bottom, providing shelter for fish.
The ship rested on the sandy bottom after it sank.
He looked under the table, searching near the bottom of the bookshelf.
The water collects at the bottom of the well after heavy rain.
They discovered an old coin buried at the bottom of the garden.
She found her keys at the bottom of her bag.
The book fell to the bottom shelf and was forgotten for years.
There is mud at the bottom of the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bed - the ground surface at the bottom of a body of water
  • antonym: top - the highest part or point of something
