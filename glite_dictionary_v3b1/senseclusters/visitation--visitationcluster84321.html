visitation

noun
legal right to visit (e.g., a child)
5.4
Pronunciation: /vɪ.zɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /vɪ.zɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a court-ordered or legally recognised right or scheduled permission to visit someone in another person's care, especially the right of a non-custodial parent or relative to spend time with a child.

Examples

Court visitation rights
The judge granted him visitation rights so he could see his daughter every other weekend.
Because of safety concerns, the court ordered supervised visitation at the community center on weekdays.
Under the divorce agreement, grandparents were awarded limited visitation to spend afternoons with the grandchildren.

Related Terms

  • synonym: access - in family law, the right to visit or have contact with a child who lives with the other parent
  • antonym: custody - the legal right to care for and make decisions about a child; often contrasted with visitation rights
