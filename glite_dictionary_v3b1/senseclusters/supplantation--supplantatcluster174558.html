supplantation

noun
replacement of something
Pronunciation: / səˌplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌplænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of replacing one thing or person with another, especially by force or strategy

Examples

The supplantation of tradition
The supplantation of traditional books by digital media has changed the way people read.
Rapid supplantation of older employees with younger staff can affect workplace culture.
The supplantation of native plants by invasive species threatens the local ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: displacement - the act of moving something from its usual place
  • synonym: replacement - the act of taking the place of something or someone
  • antonym: retention - the act of keeping something in place
