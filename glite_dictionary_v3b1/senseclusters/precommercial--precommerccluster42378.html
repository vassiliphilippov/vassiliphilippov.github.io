precommercial

adjective
before market release
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkəˈmɜːrʃəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːkəˈmɜːʃəl / (BrE)
happening or existing before something is ready to be sold or become commercially valuable

Examples

Precommercial research
The company invested heavily in precommercial research to ensure the product would succeed.
During the precommercial stage, the team focused on testing and development.
Many drugs fail in the precommercial phase before reaching the market.
The forestry team performed a precommercial thinning to help the young trees grow stronger.
Investors are interested in the precommercial stage of the technology to assess its future potential.
Many precommercial activities in agriculture focus on improving crops before they are sold.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commercial - related to the buying and selling of goods and services
  • synonym: noncommercial - not intended for commercial purposes or profit
  • synonym: prelaunch - before the official launch of a product
