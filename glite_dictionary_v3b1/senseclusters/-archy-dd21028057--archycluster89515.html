-archy

suffix for rule
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɑːrki / (AmE), / ɑːki / (BrE)
a suffix meaning rule, government, or leadership, used to form nouns describing types of governance or authority

Examples

-archy in monarchy
The word 'monarchy' refers to a system of government led by a king or queen.
In 'anarchy', the suffix indicates the absence of government or rule.
Many political terms, such as 'oligarchy', use the suffix to describe who holds power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -cracy - a suffix meaning government or rule, often by a particular group
