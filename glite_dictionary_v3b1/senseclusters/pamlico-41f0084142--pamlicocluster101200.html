Pamlico

noun
extinct Algonquian language
Pronunciation: / ˈpæmlɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpæmlɪkəʊ / (BrE)
the Algonquian language spoken by the Pamlico people, now extinct.

Examples

Pamlico language
Few written records of Pamlico remain after the language became extinct in the 18th century.
Linguists have studied Pamlico to better understand the history of Algonquian languages.
The tribe's oral traditions and their use of Pamlico have greatly influenced regional history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Algonquian - a family of indigenous languages spoken in North America
