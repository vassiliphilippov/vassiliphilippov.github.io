Centaurea solstitialis

noun
European weed; yellow star-thistle
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈtɔriə sɒˌlstɪʃiˈɑlɪs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɔːrɪə sɒlˌstɪʃɪˈɑːlɪs / (BrE)
A European weed, commonly known as yellow star-thistle, with a winged stem and hairy leaves; considered invasive in parts of the United States.

Examples

Centaurea solstitialis weed
Centaurea solstitialis has spread rapidly in California, outcompeting native plants.
Efforts to control Centaurea solstitialis require coordinated management of land and livestock.
The spiny flower heads of Centaurea solstitialis make it difficult for animals to graze.
