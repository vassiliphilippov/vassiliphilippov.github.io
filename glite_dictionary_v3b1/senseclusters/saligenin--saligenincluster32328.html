saligenin

noun
a crystalline phenolic alcohol
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈlɪdʒənɪn / (AmE), / səˈlɪdʒɪnɪn / (BrE)
A crystalline phenolic alcohol (salicylic alcohol), typically obtained by hydrolysis of salicin and used for medicinal and chemical purposes.

Examples

Saligenin from salicin
Researchers found that saligenin can be produced by hydrolyzing salicin extracted from willow bark.
The pharmaceutical study focused on the pain-relieving properties of saligenin.
Unlike salicylic acid, saligenin has both analgesic and mild antiseptic effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: salicin - a glucoside from which saligenin is derived by hydrolysis
