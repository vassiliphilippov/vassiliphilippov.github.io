cave in

verb
collapse or yield under pressure
6.8
Pronunciation: / keɪv ɪn / (AmE), / keɪv ɪn / (BrE)
to collapse suddenly, either physically (as a structure) or metaphorically (as in giving up under pressure)

Examples

The roof caves in
If we don't repair the tunnel soon, it might cave in without warning.
The roof began to cave in after the heavy snowfall.
Under relentless questioning, the suspect finally caved in and confessed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • synonym: give in - to yield under pressure or surrender
