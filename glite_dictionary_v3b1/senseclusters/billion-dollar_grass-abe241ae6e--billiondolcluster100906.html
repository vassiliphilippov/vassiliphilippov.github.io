billion-dollar grass

noun
an annual grass cultivated for forage and seeds
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪljən ˌdɑlɚ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈbɪljən ˌdɒlə ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a coarse annual grass cultivated in Asia for its edible seeds and for forage, and grown in the United States as wildlife food

Examples

billion-dollar grass seeds
Because billion-dollar grass is drought-resistant, it is popular in areas with unpredictable rainfall.
Farmers have started planting billion-dollar grass as an alternative crop for both forage and grain.
Wild turkeys and other birds feed on the seeds of billion-dollar grass throughout the winter.
