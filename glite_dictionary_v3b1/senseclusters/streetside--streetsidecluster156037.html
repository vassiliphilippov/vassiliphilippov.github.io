streetside

noun
edge of a street
3.2 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstriËtsaÉªd / (AmE), / ËˆstriËtsaÉªd / (BrE)
the side or edge of a street, especially the area next to the road used by pedestrians or vendors

Examples

A busy streetside
The food truck parked along the streetside attracted a long line of customers at lunchtime.
She found a lost wallet lying on the streetside while walking home from work.
Artists often display their paintings on the streetside during the city festival.

Synonyms

  • curbside
    2.7 1.25/m
    curbside is the area right next to the curb for pedestrians or quick stops, while streetside means the street edge often used by vendors.
  • roadside
    1.0 5.61/m
    roadside is the strip of land beside a road that can be rural or grassy, while streetside is the pedestrian area next to shops.

Related Terms

  • antonym: roadway - the part of a street used by vehicles
  • synonym: sidewalk - a paved path for pedestrians at the side of a street
