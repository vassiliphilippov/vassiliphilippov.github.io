teres minor

noun
muscle of the shoulder
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛriz ˈmaɪnɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɪəriːz ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
a small, narrow muscle of the shoulder that adducts and laterally rotates the arm

Examples

The teres minor muscle
During the physical exam, the doctor checked the function of the teres minor muscle.
Injury to the teres minor can limit a person's ability to rotate their arm outward.
The teres minor plays an important role in stabilizing the shoulder joint during movement.
