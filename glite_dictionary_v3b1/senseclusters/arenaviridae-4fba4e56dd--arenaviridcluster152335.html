Arenaviridae

noun
a family of viruses, some causing human disease
Pronunciation: / ˌɛrəˌnævəˈraɪəˌdi / (AmE), / ˌɛərəˌnævɪˈraɪədiː / (BrE)
a family of viruses characterized by round or pleomorphic shape, often transmitted by rodents and known to cause various diseases in humans and animals

Examples

Arenaviridae virus family
Lassa fever is caused by a virus belonging to the Arenaviridae family.
Scientists study members of the Arenaviridae to understand their modes of transmission.
Several contagious diseases have been linked to viruses classified under Arenaviridae.
