tethering

noun
restriction of freedom
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of restricting or limiting someone's or something's freedom, movement, or development, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Tethering creativity
The strict policies resulted in the tethering of employees' creativity and innovation.
Many artists feel that too many rules lead to the tethering of their imagination.
He argued that constant supervision was a form of tethering that stifled independence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confinement - the state of being restricted or limited
  • antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free
