sign

noun
objective medical indicator of disease
1.1 3.1
Pronunciation: / saɪn / (AmE), / saɪn / (BrE)
(Medicine) An objective indication of the presence of a disorder or disease, observable by others rather than reported by the patient.

Examples

A sign of illness
Fever and rapid heartbeat can be early signs of illness.
The doctor looked for signs of infection during the examination.
There were no signs of trauma on the patient's body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: symptom - subjective evidence of disease perceived by the patient
