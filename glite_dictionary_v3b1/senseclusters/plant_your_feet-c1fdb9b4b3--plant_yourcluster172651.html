plant your feet

verb
stand firmly or assert oneself
Pronunciation: / plænt jʊr fit / (AmE), / plɑːnt jɔː fiːt / (BrE)
to stand firmly in place, especially in a determined or resolute manner; to assert oneself firmly

Examples

To plant your feet firmly
Coaches often tell athletes to plant their feet before making a big move.
It's not always easy to plant your feet in the face of strong opposition.
When challenged on her ideas, she chose to plant her feet and defend her perspective.
