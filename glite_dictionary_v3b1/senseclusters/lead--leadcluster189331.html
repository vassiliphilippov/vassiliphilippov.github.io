lead

noun
margin by which one is ahead (sports)
1.4
Pronunciation: / lid /; / liːd / (AmE), / liːd / (BrE)
the position or amount by which someone is ahead in a race, competition, or game

Examples

A lead in the race
At halftime, the home team held a narrow lead over their rivals.
She lost her lead in the final lap of the race.
The basketball team built up a ten-point lead early in the game.
After a strong sprint, she gained a substantial lead over her competitors.
He took the lead early in the race and never looked back.
The team held a narrow lead until the final minutes of the game.
Coaches often remind players not to take too much of a lead to avoid being picked off.
The runner took a cautious lead off second base, watching the pitcher closely.
With a big lead, he was able to steal third easily.
