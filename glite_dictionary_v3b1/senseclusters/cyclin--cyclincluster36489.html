cyclin

noun
cell cycle protein
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪklɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪklɪn / (BrE)
a protein that controls the progression of cells through the cell cycle by regulating key processes

Examples

The cyclin protein
The concentration of cyclin rises and falls as the cell prepares to divide.
Researchers study how cyclin interacts with other proteins to control cell growth.
Abnormal levels of cyclin can lead to uncontrolled cell division in cancer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kinase - an enzyme that transfers phosphate groups to other molecules
