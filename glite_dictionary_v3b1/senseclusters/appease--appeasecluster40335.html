appease

verb
to calm or satisfy a feeling or desire
6.0 0.53/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpiz / (AmE), / …ôÀàpiÀêz / (BrE)
to make a feeling or desire less strong, often by giving what is wanted; to calm or satisfy

Examples

To appease hunger
A hot drink will often appease your thirst on a chilly day.
She tried to appease her hunger with a small snack.
The manager gave refunds to appease angry customers.

Synonyms

  • assuage
    7.7 1.80/m
    assuage eases pain or guilt gently and usually calms feelings, while appease often calms by giving someone what they want.
  • allay
    6.8 0.07/m
    allay reduces fears or doubts quickly, while appease often calms by giving someone what they want or ask for.
  • satisfy
    2.0 12/m
    satisfy fulfills needs or desires by giving what is wanted, while appease focuses on calming feelings, sometimes to stop complaints.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravate - to make a problem or feeling worse
  • synonym: quell - to put an end to or suppress (a feeling or disorder)
