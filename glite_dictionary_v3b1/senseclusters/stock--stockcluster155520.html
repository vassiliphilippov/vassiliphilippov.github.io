stock

noun
handle or rear part of a firearm
Pronunciation: / stɑk / (AmE), / stɒk / (BrE)
the handle or supporting part of a tool or weapon, or an ornamental cravat worn around the neck

Examples

Gun stock
He replaced the wooden stock on his shotgun with a lightweight synthetic one.
Soldiers are taught to rest the stock firmly against their shoulder before firing.
The old rifle's stock had visible scratches from years of use.
During Regency-era dances, gentlemen often wore a white stock around the neck.
He adjusted his stock before stepping onto the stage to play an 18th-century nobleman.
The butler wore a perfectly starched stock with his formal suit.
He repaired the worn stock of his favorite pool cue.
She rested the stock of the rifle against her shoulder before aiming.
The archer polished the wooden stock of the crossbow before practice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butt - the thick end of a rifle or shotgun stock
  • synonym: cravat - a neckband or scarf, precursor to the modern tie
