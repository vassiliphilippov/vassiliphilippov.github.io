H. pylori

noun
bacterium causing stomach ulcers
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ paɪˈlɔːraɪ / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ paɪˈlɔːraɪ / (BrE)
a bacterium (Helicobacter pylori) that infects the stomach lining and is linked to gastritis, stomach ulcers, and related digestive diseases

Examples

H. pylori infection
Doctors often test for H. pylori when patients present with persistent stomach pain.
Researchers have found a strong link between H. pylori and the development of gastric ulcers.
Treatment for H. pylori infection usually includes a combination of antibiotics and acid-suppressing drugs.
