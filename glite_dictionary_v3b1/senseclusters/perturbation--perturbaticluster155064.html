perturbation

noun
minor influence causing deviation in a system
9.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /pər.tɜːr.ˈbeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /pə.tɜː.ˈbeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a disturbance or change in a system, process, or situation that causes it to act differently than normal

Examples

Atmospheric perturbation
A minor perturbation in the atmosphere caused unexpected weather changes.
Scientists studied the perturbation of the system caused by the external electromagnetic field.
The orbit of the satellite was affected by a small perturbation from the moon's gravity.
Environmental perturbation can disrupt the delicate balance of an ecosystem.
His unexpected arrival was a perturbation to their peaceful evening.
The sudden perturbation of the schedule caused confusion among the team members.
Any sudden perturbation of the system can lead to unpredictable results.
The medication caused a temporary perturbation of his sleep cycle.
The scientist observed a perturbation in the data, suggesting an unknown influence on the experiment.
