Showing wild, frenzied energy or activity, often as a result of mania or extreme excitement.

Examples

A manic pace
He worked at a manic pace to meet the deadline.
She cleaned the whole house with manic energy after hearing the good news.
The comedian's manic style kept the audience laughing throughout the show.

Synonyms

  • frenzied
    6.5 2.24/m
    frenzied shows wild, fast activity driven by strong emotion, while manic often suggests clinical, prolonged energy from mania.
  • maniacal
    8.6 1.05/m
    maniacal describes wildly dangerous or cruel behavior like a maniac, while manic often means high, clinical excitement.
  • phrenetic
    9.9 0.01/m
    phrenetic is a rare, old-fashioned word for wild excitement or agitation, while manic is the common term often tied to medical mania.
  • crazed
    5.0 0.10/m
    crazed suggests out-of-control, violent or insane behavior and loss of reason, while manic refers to energy from mania.
