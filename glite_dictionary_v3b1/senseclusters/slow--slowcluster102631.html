slow

adjective
at a slow tempo (music)
Pronunciation: / sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
moving, happening, or performed at a low speed; taking more time than usual

Examples

A slow song
She prefers to listen to slow ballads when she wants to relax.
The band played a slow waltz for the first dance.
This piece should be played at a slow tempo, emphasizing each note.
After her injury, her running pace became noticeably slow.
He prefers a slow and steady approach to solving problems.
The traffic was so slow that we arrived an hour late.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fast - performed at a fast tempo (music)
  • synonym: gradual - happening or changing slowly over time
  • synonym: leisurely - unhurried; taking plenty of time
  • synonym: sluggish - lacking energy; slow to move or react
