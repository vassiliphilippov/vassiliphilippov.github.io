lean and mean

adjective
efficient and competitive; using minimal resources
Pronunciation: / lin ænd min / (AmE), / liːn ænd miːn / (BrE)
efficient and competitive, using minimal resources without excess; especially in business or fitness contexts.

Examples

Lean and mean business
After months of training, she was feeling lean and mean and ready for the race.
Startups often strive to be lean and mean to beat larger, slower rivals.
The company became lean and mean after restructuring to cut unnecessary expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: efficient - achieving maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort
  • synonym: streamlined - designed for efficiency or effectiveness with minimal waste
