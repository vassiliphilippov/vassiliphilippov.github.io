overstuff

verb
to stuff too much into something
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstʌf / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstʌf / (BrE)
to fill something with more than it can or should hold, especially so that it becomes bulging or overfilled.

Examples

To overstuff a bag
Don't overstuff the washing machine, or your clothes won't get clean.
She always overstuffs her suitcase, making it hard to close.
The sofa was overstuffed to make it extra soft and comfortable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stuff - to fill a container or space tightly
