gleeman

noun
medieval entertainer
Pronunciation: / ˈɡliːmən / (AmE), / ˈɡliːmən / (BrE)
a traveling singer or musician in medieval England who performed songs and stories

Examples

The gleeman sang songs
The gleeman wandered from village to village, sharing news and music with the people.
At the feast, a gleeman entertained the guests with lively songs and tales.
In old English legends, a gleeman often played an important role in spreading stories.
The gleeman wandered from village to village, sharing tales of distant lands.
At the feast, a gleeman entertained the guests with lively songs and jokes.
In olden days, a gleeman was welcomed for bringing news and music to rural communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minstrel - a medieval singer or musician who performed for nobility
