wellerism

noun
humorous saying with quoted phrase and comment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛl.əˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈwɛl.ər.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
A phrase consisting of a familiar quotation followed by a humorous or ironic comment, often to illustrate an unexpected or literal interpretation.

Examples

A wellerism phrase
A classic wellerism might be: 'Patience is a virtue,' said the dog as he waited for his bone.
Children enjoy creating wellerisms by adding unexpected remarks to famous proverbs.
One wellerism goes, 'Every cloud has a silver lining,' as the man said when he saw the rain wash away his crops.
