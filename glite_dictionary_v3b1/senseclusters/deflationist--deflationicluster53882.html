deflationist

noun
philosophy proponent
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfleɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfleɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes that certain concepts, such as truth or meaning, are not substantial properties but are simply linguistic or logical devices

Examples

A deflationist philosopher
The deflationist argued that the concept of truth does not refer to any deep property.
Many philosophers debate whether being a deflationist about meaning is justified.
As a well-known deflationist, she challenged traditional views on the nature of truth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pragmatist - a person who evaluates theories or beliefs in terms of their practical applications
  • antonym: realist - a person who believes that concepts like truth or meaning have real, substantial existence
