mesenteric plexus

noun
network of nerves in the mesentery
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsənˈtɛrɪk ˈplɛksəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛsənˈtɛrɪk ˈplɛksəs / (BrE)
A network of autonomic nerves located in the mesentery, chiefly involved in the regulation of intestinal functions.

Examples

The mesenteric plexus nerves
A tumor pressing against the mesenteric plexus may cause severe discomfort.
Damage to the mesenteric plexus can result in digestive disturbances and abdominal pain.
Researchers studied the role of the mesenteric plexus in regulating intestinal motility.
