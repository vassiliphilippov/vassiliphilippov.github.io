lipoprotein

noun
a protein-lipid complex in the blood
4.7 4.8
Pronunciation: /lɪp.ə.ˈproʊ.tiːn/ /; / /lɪp.ə.ˈprəʊ.tiːn/ (AmE), /lɪp.ə.ˈprəʊ.tiːn/ (BrE)
A type of protein combined with a lipid, serving as the main vehicle for transporting lipids in the blood.

Examples

Blood lipoproteins
Doctors analyze your blood to measure the levels of different lipoproteins.
High-density lipoprotein helps remove excess cholesterol from the bloodstream.
Low-density lipoprotein is often referred to as 'bad cholesterol.'
