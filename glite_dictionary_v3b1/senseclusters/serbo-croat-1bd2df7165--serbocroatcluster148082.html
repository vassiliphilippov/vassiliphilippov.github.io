Serbo-Croat

noun
South Slavic language of Serbs and Croats
9.9 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.boʊ ˈkroʊ.æt/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.bəʊ ˈkrəʊ.æt/ (BrE)
the South Slavic language formerly spoken by Serbs, Croats, and other groups in the former Yugoslavia, with Serbian generally written in Cyrillic and Croatian in the Roman alphabet; now generally regarded as two or more separate languages (Serbian, Croatian, Bosnian, Montenegrin).

Examples

Serbo-Croat language
Our neighbor spoke both Serbo-Croat and Hungarian fluently.
She studied Serbo-Croat at university to work as a translator.
The novel was originally written in Serbo-Croat before being translated into English.
