Custer's Last Stand

noun
the 1876 battle where Custer was defeated by Sioux and Cheyenne
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌstərz læst stænd / (AmE), / ˈkʌstəz lɑːst stænd / (BrE)
the famous 1876 battle near the Little Bighorn River in which the U.S. cavalry, led by General George Custer, was defeated and killed by Sioux and Cheyenne warriors

Examples

Custer's Last Stand battle
Many historians have written extensively about Custer's Last Stand and its impact on American history.

The phrase Custer's Last Stand is often used to describe a desperate or final effort in any conflict.
