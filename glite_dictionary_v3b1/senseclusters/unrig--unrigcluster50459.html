unrig

verb
to remove manipulation
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈrɪɡ / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪɡ / (BrE)
to remove unfair manipulation or fraudulent arrangements from a contest, system, or process to restore fairness

Examples

To unrig the system
The new laws are designed to unrig the financial system and promote transparency.
Activists are working hard to unrig the election process and ensure every vote counts.
Efforts to unrig the competition have led to fairer outcomes for all participants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve something
  • antonym: rig - to arrange or influence something dishonestly for a desired result
