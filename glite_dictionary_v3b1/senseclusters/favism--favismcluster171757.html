favism

noun
anemia from eating fava beans
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪvɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfeɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
A form of anemia caused by an inherited enzyme deficiency, triggered by eating fava beans.

Examples

Favism anemia
Doctors diagnosed him with favism after he developed symptoms following a meal containing broad beans.
Individuals with favism must avoid eating fava beans to prevent a dangerous drop in red blood cells.
The prevalence of favism is higher in regions where fava beans are a dietary staple.
