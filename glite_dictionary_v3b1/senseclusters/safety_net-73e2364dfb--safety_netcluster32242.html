safety net

noun
guarantee of protection against hardship
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti nɛt / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti nɛt / (BrE)
something (such as a system or arrangement) that provides protection or support in case of difficulty or failure

Examples

Financial safety net
After losing his job, the unemployment benefit acted as a safety net for his family.
Many people rely on social security as a financial safety net in retirement.
Without a safety net, entrepreneurs risk losing everything if their ventures fail.
She relied on her family's safety net after losing her job.
The government established a safety net for those affected by the economic crisis.
Without a financial safety net, many people face serious difficulties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buffer - something that lessens or absorbs the shock of an impact or setback
  • synonym: lifeline - a thing on which someone or something depends or which provides a means of escape from a difficult situation
  • synonym: protection - the action of keeping someone or something safe
  • antonym: risk - exposure to danger, harm, or loss
