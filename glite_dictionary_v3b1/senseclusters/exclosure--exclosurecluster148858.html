exclosure

noun
fenced-off area
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɪksˈkloʊʒər / (AmE), / ɪksˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
an area that is fenced off to prevent animals from entering, usually for research or conservation purposes

Examples

An exclosure area
The scientists built an exclosure to study how plants grow without being eaten by animals.
Inside the exclosure, the grass was much taller than in the surrounding pasture.
Conservationists use an exclosure to protect young trees from grazing livestock.
