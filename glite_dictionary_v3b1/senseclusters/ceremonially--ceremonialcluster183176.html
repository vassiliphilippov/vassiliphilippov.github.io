ceremonially

adverb
in a formal or ritualistic manner
6.8 3.7
Pronunciation: /se.rə.ˈmoʊ.nɪ.ə.liː/ /; / /sə.rə.ˈmoʊ.nɪ.ə.liː/ (AmE), /se.rə.ˈməʊ.nɪ.ə.liː/ /; / /sə.rə.ˈməʊ.nɪ.ə.liː/ (BrE)
in a formal or ritualistic way, as part of a ceremony

Examples

To ceremonially welcome guests
The ambassador was ceremonially welcomed at the palace by traditional dancers.
The athletes marched ceremonially into the stadium during the opening ceremony.
The documents were ceremonially signed by both leaders to mark the historic agreement.
The ambassador was ceremonially welcomed by a crowd waving flags and singing.
The athletes marched ceremonially into the stadium during the opening parade.
The new building was ceremonially inaugurated with speeches and a ribbon-cutting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceremoniously - in a ceremonious or formal manner
  • synonym: formally - in accordance with convention or etiquette
  • antonym: informally - in a casual, not formal manner
