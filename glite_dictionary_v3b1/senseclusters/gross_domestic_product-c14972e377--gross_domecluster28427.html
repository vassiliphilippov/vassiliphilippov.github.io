gross domestic product

noun
total value of goods and services produced in a country
5.6 3.0
Pronunciation: /groʊs də.ˈmes.tɪk ˈprɑː.dəkt/ /; / /groʊs də.ˈmes.tɪk ˈprɑː.dʌkt/ (AmE), /grəʊs də.ˈmes.tɪk ˈprɒ.dʌkt/ /; / /grəʊs də.ˈmes.tɪk ˈprɒ.dəkt/ (BrE)
the total market value of all goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific period, usually one year

Examples

The gross domestic product growth
Economists often use gross domestic product to compare the economic performance of different nations.
The country's gross domestic product grew by 3% last year, indicating a healthy economy.
The government is implementing policies to boost the gross domestic product and create more jobs.
