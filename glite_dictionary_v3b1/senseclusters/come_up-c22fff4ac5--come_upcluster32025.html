come up

verb
rise to the surface
2.1 1.9
Pronunciation: / kʌm ʌp / (AmE), / kʌm ʌp / (BrE)
to rise, appear, or move upward from below or from a lower position (including the sun, fog, or people coming to the surface)

Examples

The sun comes up
After hours underground, the miners finally came up to daylight.
Bubbles began to come up from the bottom of the pond.
The divers had to come up for air every few minutes.
Every morning, Venus comes up in the eastern sky before dawn.
The full moon will come up just after sunset tonight.
We woke up early to watch the sun come up over the mountains.
As the sun warmed the ground, a thick fog began to come up along the river.
Mist will often come up from the grass on cool autumn mornings.
Smoke started to come up from the engine after the sudden stop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descend - to move downward from a higher to a lower place
  • synonym: rise - to move upward or appear above the horizon (sun, moon, etc.)
  • synonym: surface - to rise or come to the surface
