come up

verb

to rise, appear, or move upward from below or from a lower position (including the sun, fog, or people coming to the surface)

Examples

The sun comes up
After hours underground, the miners finally came up to daylight.
Bubbles began to come up from the bottom of the pond.
The divers had to come up for air every few minutes.
Every morning, Venus comes up in the eastern sky before dawn.
The full moon will come up just after sunset tonight.

Synonyms

  • rise
    1.0 70/m
    rise is the general verb for moving upward in many contexts, while come up often means appearing from below.
  • surface
    3.9 11/m
    surface means come to the top or become visible, while come up is more casual and used for many kinds of appearing.
  • rise up
    3.9 2/m
    rise up means move to the surface, especially from liquid, while come up can mean appear or rise in many situations.
  • go up
    1.0 11/m
    go up means move or climb to a higher place, while come up often implies appearing from below or arising.
  • move up
    2.0 2/m
    move up means go to a higher level or position, while come up more often describes something appearing from below.
  • arise
    2.7 0.9/m
    arise describes smoke or mist moving into the air, while come up is more common and also means appearing from below.
  • ascend
    4.0 5/m
    ascend means move upward to a higher place, often in a formal or deliberate way, while come up is more everyday.
  • lift
    1.0 19/m
    lift often means to raise something or for fog to clear, while come up mainly means to rise or appear from below.
