tangor

noun
hybrid citrus fruit; tangerine-orange cross
6.9 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtænˌɡɔr / (AmE), / ˈtanɡɔː / (BrE)
A citrus fruit and tree that is a hybrid between a tangerine and an orange, producing large, sweet, deep orange fruit that is easy to peel.

Examples

A tangor fruit
At the market, I bought a kilo of ripe tangors for making fresh juice.
The farmer grew several tangor trees in his orchard for their juicy fruit.
This variety of tangor is especially popular for its sweet, easy-to-peel segments.
