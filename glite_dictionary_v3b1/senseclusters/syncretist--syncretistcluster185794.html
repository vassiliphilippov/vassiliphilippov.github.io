syncretist

noun
one who blends beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkrətɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkrɪtɪst / (BrE)
a person who combines different beliefs, practices, or schools of thought, especially in religion or philosophy

Examples

A syncretist thinker
As a syncretist, she drew inspiration from both Eastern and Western philosophies.
The new movement was led by a syncretist who valued unity among different faiths.
Many artists are syncretists, blending styles from various cultures in their work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dogmatist - a person who strongly adheres to a single set of beliefs
  • synonym: eclectic - a person who selects from various sources or styles
