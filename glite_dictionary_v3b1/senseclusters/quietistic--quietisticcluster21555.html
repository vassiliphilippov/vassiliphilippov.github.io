quietistic

adjective
relating to Quietism
Pronunciation: / ˌkwaɪəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkwaɪəˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to Quietism, a 17th-century Christian movement emphasizing passive contemplation and withdrawal from outward religious activity

Examples

Quietistic beliefs
Her quietistic approach to spirituality focused on inner peace rather than outward rituals.
The philosopher's quietistic beliefs led him to withdraw from public debates.
Some critics argue that a quietistic attitude discourages active engagement with the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contemplative - involving deep, quiet thought or meditation
