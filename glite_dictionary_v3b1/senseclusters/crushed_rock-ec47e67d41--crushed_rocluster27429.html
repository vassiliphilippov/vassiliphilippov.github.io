crushed rock

noun
rock fragments made by crushing
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌʃt ˈrɑk / (AmE), / ˈkrʌʃt ˈrɒk / (BrE)
small fragments or pebbles of rock produced by mechanically crushing larger stones, often used in construction and landscaping

Examples

Crushed rock surface
The driveway was surfaced with crushed rock to provide better drainage.
We ordered several tons of crushed rock for the new garden path.
Workers spread crushed rock around the construction site to stabilize the ground.
