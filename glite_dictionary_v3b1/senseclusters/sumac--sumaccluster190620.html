sumac

noun
a shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus
9.9 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.mæk/ /; / /ˈsjuː.mæk/ (AmE), /ˈsjuː.mæk/ /; / /ˈsuː.mæk/ (BrE)
A shrub or small tree of the genus Rhus, typically with compound leaves and clusters of red, reddish, or white berries; some species are used as spices or for ornamental purposes; usually refers to non-poisonous varieties.

Examples

Ground sumac spice
Many Middle Eastern dishes use ground sumac as a tangy spice.
The sumac in the garden produces clusters of bright red berries every autumn.
We planted a sumac tree along the edge of the driveway for its vibrant fall colors.
Some species of sumach are popular in landscaping for their ornamental foliage.
The bright red berries of the sumach can be used to make a tangy spice.
They planted a row of sumach trees along the driveway for their striking autumn colors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: poison ivy - a poisonous plant of the genus Toxicodendron, once considered part of Rhus
  • synonym: staghorn sumac - a common species of sumach (Rhus typhina) with distinctive fuzzy branches
