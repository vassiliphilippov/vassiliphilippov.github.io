self-hating Jew

noun
Jew critical or ashamed of own identity
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈheɪtɪŋ dʒu / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈheɪtɪŋ dʒuː / (BrE)
a Jewish person who expresses criticism of, or feels shame about, their own Jewish identity or heritage

Examples

Self-hating Jew identity
He was uncomfortable when fellow students called him a self-hating Jew for questioning certain traditions.
Some critics accused the author of being a self-hating Jew because of his views on Israel.
The label self-hating Jew is often used in heated political arguments within the Jewish community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-hatred - intense dislike or loathing of oneself
