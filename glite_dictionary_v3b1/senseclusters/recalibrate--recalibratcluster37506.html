recalibrate

verb
to adjust plans
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkælɪˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkælɪbreɪt / (BrE)
to change or adjust a plan, attitude, or way of behaving in response to new information or changing conditions

Examples

To recalibrate a plan
After the crisis, the company had to recalibrate its strategy to meet changing customer needs.
You may need to recalibrate your expectations after hearing the latest news.
The team decided to recalibrate their approach in response to the unexpected results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: maintain - to keep something the same without changing it
  • synonym: readjust - to change or correct something again
  • synonym: revise - to change or improve something, especially a plan or document
