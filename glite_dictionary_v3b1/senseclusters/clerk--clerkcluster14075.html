clerk

noun
salesperson in a store
1.1 6.13/m
Pronunciation: / kl…ùk / (AmE), / kl…ëÀêk / (BrE)
a person whose job is to assist customers and handle sales transactions in a shop or store

Examples

Store clerk
I asked the clerk at the counter for help finding my size.
Several new clerks were hired to handle the holiday rush.
The clerk scanned all the items before bagging them.

Synonyms

  • shop clerk
    1.9 0.05/m
    shop clerk is a common everyday name for a store worker who serves customers, while clerk is a shorter, more general term.
  • salesclerk
    2.4 0.26/m
    salesclerk sells goods directly to customers in a store, while clerk includes both selling and handling sales transactions.
  • shop assistant
    1.2 0.06/m
    shop assistant helps customers and gives advice in a shop, while clerk focuses more on sales transactions and payments.
  • salesperson
    2.2 2.21/m
    salesperson sells products or services for a business, while clerk usually works in a shop helping customers and handling sales.
  • counterperson
    4.6 0.01/m
    counterperson serves customers from behind a counter in shops or diners, while clerk handles sales and assists customers across a store.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cashier - person who handles payments and receipts in a store
  • synonym: salesperson - person whose job is selling goods in a store
