flite

verb
to scold sharply
Pronunciation: / flaɪt / (AmE), / flaɪt / (BrE)
to scold or rebuke someone sharply, especially in an angry or nagging manner (archaic or dialect)

Examples

To flite a child
In old tales, mothers would flite their children for staying out after dark.
The neighbors could hear her fliting her husband over his careless ways.
He was often flited by elders for his mischievous behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: scold - to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong
