highline

noun
elevated cable system
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhaɪ.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈhaɪ.laɪn/ (BrE)
a cable or rope stretched high above the ground, used for moving people or goods, or for walking or balancing

Examples

A highline over water
The rescue team set up a highline to safely move supplies across the river.
Workers used a highline to transport heavy logs over the steep hillside.
During the construction project, a highline was essential for moving equipment between buildings.
The athlete carefully stepped onto the highline suspended between two tall buildings.
Walking a highline requires intense focus and excellent balance skills.
She conquered her fear of heights by practicing on a highline in the mountains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ropeway - a system for transporting goods or people using suspended cables
  • synonym: slackline - a length of flat webbing used for balancing close to the ground
  • synonym: tightrope - a rope or wire stretched tight for walking or balancing, usually in circuses
  • synonym: zipline - a suspended cable for sliding from one end to the other
