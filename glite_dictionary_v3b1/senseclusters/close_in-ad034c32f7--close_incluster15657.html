close in

verb
surround or enclose completely
1.6 3.7
Pronunciation: /kloʊz ɪn/ /; / /kloʊs ɪn/ (AmE), /kləʊz ɪn/ /; / /kləʊs ɪn/ (BrE)
to move closer from all sides in order to surround or enclose someone or something

Examples

Mist closes in
As the mist began to close in, visibility dropped sharply across the fields.

They decided to close in the patio to protect it from the wind.
As the storm clouds began to close in, we quickly packed up our picnic.
Enemy troops started to close in from every direction, leaving the defenders with little room to maneuver.
The police were about to close in on the suspect when he slipped away.
