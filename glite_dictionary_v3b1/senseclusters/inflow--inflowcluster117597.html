inflow

noun
movement or process of flowing in
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪn.fləʊ / (BrE)
the movement or process of something flowing in, especially into a place, system, or container.

Examples

The inflow of water
Improper drainage can affect the inflow of nutrients to the plants.
The dam regulates the inflow of water into the reservoir, especially during rainy seasons.
There has been a steady inflow of foreign investment in the region over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influx - a sudden or large arrival or flow of people or things
  • antonym: outflow - the process of flowing out
