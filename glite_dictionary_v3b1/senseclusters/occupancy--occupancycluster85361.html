occupancy

noun
the act of occupying a place
4.6 1.19/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêkj…ôp…ônsi / (AmE), / Àà…íkj…ôp…ônsi / (BrE)
the act or state of using, living in, or possessing a building, room, or property

Examples

Occupancy of a house
Fire codes limit the maximum occupancy of this auditorium to 300 people.
The occupancy of the new office will begin next month after renovations are complete.
The hotel reported full occupancy during the holiday season.
Hotel occupancy rates tend to increase during the holiday season.
Regulations limit the occupancy of this conference room to fifty people.
The landlord inspected the apartment before the start of the tenant's occupancy.

Synonyms

  • occupation
    4.6 0.32/m
    occupation often means taking control or temporary use of a building, while occupancy describes who uses or lives in it.
  • residence
    3.3 8.55/m
    residence is the place where someone lives long-term, while occupancy covers any use, living, or possession of a building.
  • habitation
    4.2 0.51/m
    habitation refers to living in a place as a home, whereas occupancy also covers using or possessing property without living there.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occupation - the action of living in or using a building or other place
  • synonym: tenancy - the possession or occupancy of land or premises by lease
  • antonym: vacancy - the state of being unoccupied
