fuguing

noun
fugue-style singing
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfjuː.ɡɪŋ/ /; / /ˈfuː.ɡɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈfjuː.ɡɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act or style of composing or singing music in the manner of a fugue, often with overlapping voices or parts

Examples

Fuguing section in choir
The choir included a fuguing section that showcased each vocal part entering in turn.
Early American hymns often feature fuguing passages to create a rich, layered sound.
The composer's use of fuguing brought complexity and energy to the choral piece.
