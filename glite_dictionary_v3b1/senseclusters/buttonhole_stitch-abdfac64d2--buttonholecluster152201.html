buttonhole stitch

noun
looped sewing stitch for edges or buttonholes
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌtənˌhoʊl stɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌtənhəʊl stɪtʃ / (BrE)
a decorative and reinforcing looped stitch used for finishing the edges of fabric, especially around buttonholes

Examples

Buttonhole stitch edge
He admired the precise buttonhole stitch around the coat's buttons.
Learning to sew a neat buttonhole stitch is essential for garment making.
The seamstress used a buttonhole stitch to reinforce the edge of the fabric.
