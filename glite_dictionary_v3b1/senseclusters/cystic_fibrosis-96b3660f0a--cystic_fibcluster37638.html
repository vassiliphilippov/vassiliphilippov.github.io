cystic fibrosis

noun
hereditary disease causing thick mucus in organs
5.0 0.44/m

A serious hereditary disease in which the lungs, pancreas, and other organs become clogged with thick, sticky mucus, leading to respiratory and digestive problems. It is caused by a defect in a single gene and has no known cure.

Examples

Cystic fibrosis disease
New genetic treatments offer hope to children born with cystic fibrosis.
Regular exercise can help manage symptoms for people living with cystic fibrosis.
The doctor explained that cystic fibrosis requires lifelong medical care and frequent respiratory therapy.
