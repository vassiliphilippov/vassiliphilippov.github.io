stratify

verb
to layer seeds in earth or sand for preservation or germination
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstræt̬.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
(in horticulture) To store or treat (seeds) by placing them between layers of earth or sand, usually to preserve them or promote germination.

Examples

To stratify seeds
Gardeners often stratify seeds to improve their chances of germination in the spring.
Some rare plants require you to stratify the seeds for several weeks before planting.
To break seed dormancy, horticulturists may stratify kernels in moist sand over winter.
