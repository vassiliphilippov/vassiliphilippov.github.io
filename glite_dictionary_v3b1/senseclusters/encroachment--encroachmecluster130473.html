encroachment

noun
gradual, unwanted influence or intrusion
5.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkroʊtmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrəʊt.mənt / (BrE)
the act of gradually and often undesirably influencing or affecting something, especially by spreading into its area

Examples

Encroachment of culture
He spoke out against the encroachment of Western culture on traditional values.
Many worry about the encroachment of digital technology into every aspect of daily life.
The encroachment of commercial interests on public lands has sparked protests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intrusion - the act of entering or affecting without permission
  • antonym: withdrawal - the act of removing or pulling back influence
