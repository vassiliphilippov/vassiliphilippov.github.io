geodesic line

noun
shortest path on a curved surface
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪk laɪn / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəˈdɛsɪk laɪn / (BrE)
(mathematics) the shortest path between two points on a mathematically defined surface, such as a straight line on a plane or an arc of a great circle on a sphere.

Examples

A geodesic line on a sphere
General relativity predicts that light follows a geodesic line in curved spacetime.
On a sphere, the shortest route between two cities is a geodesic line, not a straight line on a map.
The crew plotted their course using a geodesic line to minimize travel distance over the Earth's surface.
