overshadow

verb
cast a shadow over; obscure
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
(of a thing) to cast a shadow over something else, making it less bright or less noticeable.

Examples

Tall buildings overshadow houses
A new shopping center overshadowed the smaller shops on the street, both literally and figuratively.
The old cathedral overshadows the surrounding market square during the afternoon.
Towering skyscrapers now overshadow much of the city’s historic district.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obscure - make something less visible or clear
