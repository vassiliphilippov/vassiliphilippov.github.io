lacewing

noun
insect with delicate, net-like wings
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪsˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈleɪs.wɪŋ / (BrE)
a delicate insect of the families Chrysopidae or Hemerobiidae, characterized by net-like, transparent wings; its larvae feed on insect pests such as aphids.

Examples

A lacewing insect
A lacewing can often be found near garden plants, where it hunts for aphids.
Gardeners value the lacewing as a natural predator of pest insects.
The delicate wings of a lacewing resemble fine lace when examined up close.
