prescribe

verb
order medicine or treatment
1.0
Pronunciation: / prɪˈskraɪb / (AmE), / prɪˈskraɪb / (BrE)
(of a doctor) to officially tell someone to take a particular medicine or have a particular treatment, usually in writing.

Examples

To prescribe medicine
If your symptoms worsen, please let me know before I prescribe any new medications.
Physicians often prescribe physical therapy after surgery.
The doctor will prescribe antibiotics to treat your infection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proscribe - to officially forbid something, especially by law
