Zea mays indentata

noun
type of corn with indented kernels
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌziː.ə ˈmeɪz ˌɪn.dɛnˈteɪ.tə / (AmE), / ˌziː.ə ˈmeɪz ˌɪn.dɛnˈteɪ.tə / (BrE)
A variety of corn whose kernels contain both hard and soft starch, resulting in a characteristic indentation at maturity; also known as dent corn.

Examples

Zea mays indentata kernels
Farmers often choose Zea mays indentata over other varieties due to its high yield and versatility.
In the Midwest, Zea mays indentata is the leading crop used for animal feed and ethanol production.
The distinctive kernels of Zea mays indentata are easily identified by their central indentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dent corn - corn with kernels that have an indentation, commonly used for animal feed and industrial products
