Newcastle disease

noun
contagious viral disease in birds
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːˌkæsəl dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːˌkɑːsəl dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a highly contagious viral disease affecting domestic fowl and other birds, often causing respiratory and neurological symptoms

Examples

Newcastle disease in chickens
Symptoms of Newcastle disease in chickens include coughing, sneezing, and paralysis.
The poultry farm was quarantined due to an outbreak of Newcastle disease.
Veterinarians recommend regular vaccinations to protect flocks against Newcastle disease.
