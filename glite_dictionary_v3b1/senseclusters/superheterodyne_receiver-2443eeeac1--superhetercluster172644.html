superheterodyne receiver

noun
type of radio receiver using intermediate frequency
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuː.pɚˌhɛ.t̬ɚ.doʊˈdaɪn rɪˈsiː.vɚ / (AmE), / ˌsuː.pəˌhɛ.tɪ.rəʊˈdaɪn rɪˈsiː.və / (BrE)
a radio receiver in which incoming signals are combined with a locally generated frequency to produce a fixed intermediate frequency for easier amplification and demodulation

Examples

Superheterodyne receiver design
Engineers often prefer a superheterodyne receiver because of its superior selectivity and sensitivity.
Most modern radios still use some form of the superheterodyne receiver principle in their design.
The superheterodyne receiver was a key advancement in radio technology during the 20th century.
