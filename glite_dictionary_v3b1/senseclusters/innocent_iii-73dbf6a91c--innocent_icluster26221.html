Innocent III

proper noun
pope who reigned from 1198 to 1216
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnəsənt ðə ˈθɜrd / (AmE), / ˈɪnəsənt ðə ˈθɜːd / (BrE)
(c. 1160–1216) Pope from 1198 to 1216, known for launching the Fourth Crusade and greatly increasing papal influence in European politics.

Examples

Innocent III pope
Innocent III played a crucial role in organizing the Fourth Crusade.
During the reign of Innocent III, the power of the papacy reached unprecedented heights.
Several medieval reforms were enacted under Innocent III's leadership.
