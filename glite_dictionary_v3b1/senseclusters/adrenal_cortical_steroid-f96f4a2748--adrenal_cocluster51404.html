adrenal cortical steroid

noun
steroid hormone from the adrenal cortex
Pronunciation: / əˌdriː.nəl ˈkɔr.tɪ.kəl ˈstɛr.ɔɪd / (AmE), / əˌdriː.nəl ˈkɔː.tɪ.kəl ˈstɛr.ɔɪd / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex, or a synthetic analog, that can reduce inflammation and suppress the immune response

Examples

Adrenal cortical steroids reduce inflammation
Some athletes have abused adrenal cortical steroids despite their potential side effects.
The doctor prescribed adrenal cortical steroids to help control the patient's severe allergic reaction.
Treatment with an adrenal cortical steroid can be necessary for autoimmune diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corticosteroid - a class of steroid hormones produced by the adrenal cortex
