pseudoscalar

adjective
parity-changing property
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːdoʊˌskeɪlər / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəʊˌskeɪlə / (BrE)
describing a quantity or field that changes sign under spatial reflection (parity), unlike a true scalar

Examples

Pseudoscalar field property
A pseudoscalar field reverses its sign when the spatial coordinates are reflected.
Physicists study pseudoscalar particles to understand violations of parity symmetry.
The interaction involves a pseudoscalar quantity that behaves differently from a normal scalar.

Related Terms

  • antonym: scalar - a quantity with only magnitude, unchanged by spatial reflection
  • antonym: vector - a quantity with both magnitude and direction
