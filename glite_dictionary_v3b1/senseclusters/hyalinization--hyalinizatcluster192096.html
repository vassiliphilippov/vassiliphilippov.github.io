hyalinization

noun
process where tissue becomes glassy or hyaline
Pronunciation: / haɪˌælɪnəˈzeɪʃən /; / haɪəlɪˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / haɪˌælɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / haɪəˌlɪnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process or state of tissue becoming hyaline, typically due to degeneration, resulting in a glassy, translucent appearance under a microscope.

Examples

Hyalinization of tissue
Microscopic examination revealed extensive hyalinization of the connective tissue.
Researchers noted hyalinization in the glomeruli of the affected kidney.
The degree of hyalinization can indicate the severity of vascular damage.
Excessive hyalinisation can lead to stiffening of the blood vessels.
Pathologists noted hyalinisation of the glomeruli under the microscope.
The biopsy confirmed the presence of hyalinisation in the lung tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hyaline - glassy, translucent substance or appearance in tissue
  • synonym: hyalinization - the same process, US spelling
