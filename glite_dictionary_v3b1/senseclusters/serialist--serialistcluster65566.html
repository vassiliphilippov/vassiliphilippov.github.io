serialist

noun
composer using serialism
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪriəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɪəriəlɪst / (BrE)
a composer or musician who creates music using serial techniques, especially twelve-tone methods

Examples

Modern serialist composer
The famous serialist challenged traditional harmony with his innovative compositions.
Many twentieth-century composers were influenced by the techniques of the serialist movement.
As a dedicated serialist, she preferred composing with strict twelve-tone rows.
The orchestra performed a new piece by a renowned serialist last night.
Many twentieth-century serialists experimented with innovative musical structures.
She became interested in the works of serialist composers during her studies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atonalist - a composer who writes atonal music
  • antonym: tonalist - a composer who uses tonal music
