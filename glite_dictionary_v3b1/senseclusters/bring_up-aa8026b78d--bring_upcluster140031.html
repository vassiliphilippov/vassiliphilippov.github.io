bring up

verb
mention or introduce for discussion
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /brɪŋ ʌp/ /; / /brɪŋ əp/ (AmE), /brɪŋ ʌp/ /; / /brɪŋ əp/ (BrE)
to mention or introduce a subject or topic for discussion

Examples

To bring up a topic
I didn't want to bring up the subject of money at dinner.
Let's not bring up old arguments again.
She brought up an important question during the meeting.
He tactfully avoided bringing up the subject of money.
I didn't want to bring up the argument at dinner last night.
She occasionally brings up old memories when we talk.
Please don't bring up work at the dinner table tonight.
She brought up an important issue during the debate.
Whenever someone brings up politics, the conversation gets heated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bring down - to reduce or cause to fall, as in 'bring down the topic', opposite in context to 'bring up' a subject
  • antonym: bring up - to raise a child (different sense of the same phrasal verb)
  • synonym: broach - to raise (a sensitive or difficult subject) for discussion
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not mention or refer to
  • synonym: mention - to refer to or speak about something briefly
  • synonym: raise - to mention a subject so that it can be discussed
