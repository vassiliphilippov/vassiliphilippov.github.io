deus

noun
a god; divine being
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ.əs / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.əs / (BrE)
a god or divine being, especially in literary, religious, or historical contexts

Examples

A deus in stories
The ancient text refers to a deus who created the world.
In Roman mythology, each deus had specific powers and responsibilities.
The philosopher wrote about the nature of a supreme deus governing the universe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mortal - a human being, especially as subject to death
