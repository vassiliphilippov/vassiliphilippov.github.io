meshy

adjective
having many connections
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛʃi / (AmE), / ˈmɛʃi / (BrE)
having the qualities or appearance of a mesh, with many small holes or interconnected parts

Examples

Meshy structure
The new software created a meshy structure, allowing every device to communicate with each other.
Her explanation was so meshy that it was hard to follow the many connections between ideas.
Modern cities often have meshy transportation networks, making it easy to travel between different areas.
The curtains were made of a meshy material that let in plenty of light.
She wore a meshy top over her swimsuit at the beach.
The insect netting was so meshy that even tiny bugs could not get through.
