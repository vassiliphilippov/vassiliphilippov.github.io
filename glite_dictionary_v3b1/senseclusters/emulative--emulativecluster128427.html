emulative

adjective
imitating to compete
Pronunciation: / ˈɛm.jəˌleɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈɛm.jʊ.lə.tɪv / (BrE)
trying to equal or surpass someone, especially by imitating their actions or achievements

Examples

Emulative behavior
His emulative spirit drove him to match his older brother's achievements.
The team's emulative approach led them to adopt the strategies of their rivals.
She developed an emulative attitude after seeing her friend's success in the competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: competitive - wanting to win or be more successful than others
  • synonym: imitative - copying or following someone else's actions
