sieve

verb
strain or separate through a sieve
7.5 4.7
Pronunciation: /sɪv/ (AmE), /sɪv/ (BrE)
to separate or strain a substance by passing it through a sieve to remove larger particles or lumps

Examples

To sieve flour
After gathering the soil sample, he sieved it to separate the fine grains from the rocks.
Carefully sieve the flour into the bowl to remove any lumps.
She sieved the soup before serving to make it perfectly smooth.
