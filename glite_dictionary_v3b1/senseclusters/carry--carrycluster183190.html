carry

verb
extend effectively over a distance
1.5 1.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.riː/ /; / /ˈkæ.rɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.rɪ/ (BrE)
to transmit, convey, or communicate something such as sound, information, or disease from one place or person to another

Examples

To carry a message
Her voice carried across the crowded hall, reaching those at the back.
The wireless signal doesn't carry very far in this building.
This old cannon can carry a projectile over a mile.
Metals often carry heat and electricity efficiently.
Radio waves carry the broadcast across long distances.
The voice of the singer carried clearly to the back of the auditorium.
Her message was carried far beyond the borders of her own country.
Rumors of the closure were carried through the entire community within hours.
The local newspaper will carry the details of the new policy tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broadcast - to transmit (a program or some information) by radio or television
  • synonym: conduct - allow heat, electricity, sound, etc. to pass through
  • synonym: convey - to make known; communicate
  • synonym: infect - to cause someone or something to have a disease
  • antonym: receive - to get or accept something that is sent or given
  • antonym: resist - to not be affected by or to fight off a disease or infection
  • synonym: transmit - send or convey something from one person or place to another
  • synonym: travel - to move or be transmitted from one place to another (esp. sound, influence)
