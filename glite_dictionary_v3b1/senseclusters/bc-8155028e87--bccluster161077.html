BC

adverb
indicates years before the birth of Christ
2.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / bi si / (AmE), / biÀê siÀê / (BrE)
Used after a year to indicate a date before the birth of Christ, according to the traditional Western calendar.

Examples

500 BC
Many famous Greek philosophers lived during the 5th century BC.
The city of Rome was founded in 753 BC according to legend.
The wall was built around 200 BC to protect the settlement.

Synonyms

  • before Christ
    4.8 0.27/m
    before Christ is the full phrase written after a year, while BC is its common abbreviation seen on dates.
  • BCE
    4.4 0.05/m
    BCE is a religiously neutral label written after a year, while BC uses the traditional Christian naming.

Related Terms

  • antonym: AD - used for years after the birth of Christ ('Anno Domini')
