mantle

noun
protective tissue layer in mollusks
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmæntəl / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tl̩ / (BrE)
(Zoology) The soft, protective layer of tissue in mollusks and brachiopods that secretes the substance forming the shell.

Examples

Snail's mantle
A snail withdraws its body under the protection of its mantle when threatened.
Scientists studied the structure of the mantle in various species of mollusks.
The clam's mantle secretes calcium carbonate to build its shell.
