punctuative

adjective
intermittent interruptions
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌŋktʃuˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈpʌŋktjʊətɪv / (BrE)
happening at intervals, interrupting a flow, or marking/emphasizing a change in action, sound, or meaning

Examples

Punctuative pauses
The silence in the library was punctuative, broken only by the occasional turning of a page.
Her speech was punctuative, with frequent pauses for emphasis.
Rain fell in a punctuative pattern, starting and stopping throughout the afternoon.
Her single nod was punctuative, sharply emphasizing the end of his long explanation.
The sudden silence acted as a punctuative pause in the heated discussion.
He used a punctuative gesture to signal that the meeting was over.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - happening without interruption or pause
  • synonym: emphatic - showing or giving emphasis
  • synonym: intermittent - happening at irregular intervals; not continuous
  • antonym: neutral - not showing emphasis or change
