inalienably

adverb
in a way that cannot be taken away
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈeɪliənəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈeɪliənəbli / (BrE)
In a manner that cannot be taken away, transferred, or surrendered.

Examples

Belong inalienably to rights
Certain fundamental rights belong inalienably to every citizen.
She believed that freedom of speech was inalienably linked to democracy.
The constitution stated that power resided inalienably with the people.

Related Terms
