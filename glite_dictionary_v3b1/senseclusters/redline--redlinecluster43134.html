redline

noun
non-negotiable limit
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a limit or boundary that must not be crossed, especially in negotiations or technical situations

Examples

A redline in talks
The negotiators agreed that crossing the redline would end the talks immediately.
Each party established a redline that could not be compromised during the discussions.
The engineer warned that exceeding the redline could damage the equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boundary - a dividing line or limit
  • antonym: compromise - an agreement reached by each side making concessions
  • synonym: threshold - a point or level at which something begins or changes
