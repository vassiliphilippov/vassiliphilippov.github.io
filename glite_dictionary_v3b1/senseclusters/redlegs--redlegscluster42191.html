redlegs

noun
poor whites in Caribbean
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌlɛɡz / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌlɛɡz / (BrE)
a historical term for poor white people living in the Caribbean, especially in Barbados and nearby islands

Examples

Poor redlegs in Barbados
The plantation owners often looked down on the redlegs who worked in the fields.
Many redlegs struggled with poverty and harsh living conditions in colonial Barbados.
Historians have studied the origins and lives of the redlegs in the Caribbean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indentured servant - a person bound by contract to work for another for a set period
