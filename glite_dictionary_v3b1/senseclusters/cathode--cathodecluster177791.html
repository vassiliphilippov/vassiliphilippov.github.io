cathode

noun
negatively charged electrode
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθoʊd / (AmE), / ˈkæθəʊd / (BrE)
the electrode in an electrical device where electrons enter or leave; its charge depends on the type of device

Examples

Cathode emits electrons
Electrons travel from the cathode to the anode in a typical galvanic cell.
Replacing the worn-out cathode improved the device's performance significantly.
The television's old picture tube contains a heated cathode that emits electrons.
In a battery, electrons flow toward the cathode during discharge.
The cathode in a fluorescent lamp emits electrons that help produce light.
Scientists measured the voltage between the anode and the cathode in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anode - the positively charged electrode in an electrical device
  • synonym: electrode - a conductor through which electricity enters or leaves a device
