auscultatory

adjective
relating to auscultation in medicine
9.9 8.8
Pronunciation: /ɔːs.ˈkʌl.tə.tɔː.riː/ (AmE), /ɔːs.ˈkʌl.tə.tɔː.riː/ (BrE)
relating to or involving auscultation, the act of listening to sounds from the body, typically with a stethoscope, as part of a medical examination

Examples

Auscultatory examination
Auscultatory evidence revealed abnormal lung sounds during the examination.
A skilled clinician pays close attention to auscultatory changes when diagnosing respiratory conditions.
The doctor noted an auscultatory finding consistent with a heart murmur.
