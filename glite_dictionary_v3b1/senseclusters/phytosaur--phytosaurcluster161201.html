phytosaur

noun
extinct Triassic reptile
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪtoʊˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtəsɔː / (BrE)
a large, extinct, crocodile-like reptile that lived during the Triassic period

Examples

A phytosaur skeleton
The museum displayed a complete phytosaur skeleton found in ancient riverbeds.
Scientists believe the phytosaur hunted fish and other small animals in prehistoric swamps.
Unlike true crocodiles, the phytosaur had its nostrils near the top of its head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crocodile - a large, predatory, semi-aquatic reptile
  • synonym: dinosaur - a group of extinct reptiles from the Mesozoic era
