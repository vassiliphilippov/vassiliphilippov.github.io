moorwort

noun
evergreen shrub of wet, northern regions
Pronunciation: / ˈmʊrˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈmʊəˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A wiry evergreen shrub found in wet acidic areas of the Arctic, Canada, and the northeastern United States, typically bearing pendent clusters of white or pink flowers.

Examples

The moorwort shrub
Botanists documented a new population of moorwort along the edge of the acidic marsh.
Clusters of pink moorwort flowers add color to the wet Arctic landscape in summer.
The moorwort thrives in the boggy soils of Newfoundland and Labrador.
