sustain

verb
hold up physically; bear weight
1.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈsteɪn/ (AmE), /sə.ˈsteɪn/ (BrE)
to physically support or bear the weight of something; to keep something from falling or collapsing

Examples

Sustain the weight
That cable is not strong enough to sustain the bridge's load during heavy traffic.
The old bookshelf could no longer sustain the growing pile of books and started to sag.
The pillars sustain the immense weight of the ancient temple's roof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: support - to bear all or part of the weight of; hold up
  • synonym: uphold - to support or keep upright
