occlusal

adjective
tooth contact surfaces
9.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkluː.zəl/ /; / /ə.ˈklʌ.zəl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkluː.zəl/ (BrE)
relating to the surfaces of the teeth that touch each other when the jaws close or during chewing

Examples

Occlusal tooth surface
The dentist checked the occlusal surfaces for cavities during the examination.
Proper occlusal alignment is important for comfortable chewing and overall dental health.
A filling was placed on the occlusal surface of her molar to repair the damage.
The dentist checked the occlusal surfaces of each tooth for signs of wear.
Proper occlusal alignment is important for comfortable chewing and speaking.
An occlusal adjustment may be needed after getting a new dental crown.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bite - the way the upper and lower teeth fit together
  • antonym: buccal - relating to the cheek or the side of the mouth
  • antonym: lingual - relating to the tongue side of the teeth
  • antonym: malocclusion - improper alignment of the teeth when the jaws are closed
