lobotomy

noun
surgical severing of brain nerve tracts
6.7
Pronunciation: / loʊˈbɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ləʊˈbɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation in which nerve pathways in the frontal lobe of the brain are cut, formerly used to treat certain mental illnesses, often resulting in significant changes in personality or cognitive ability

Examples

A lobotomy operation
Doctors once performed a lobotomy to manage severe mental disorders, but it is now rarely used.
The controversial practice of lobotomy faded as psychiatric medicines became more advanced.
The patient experienced dramatic personality changes after undergoing a lobotomy.
