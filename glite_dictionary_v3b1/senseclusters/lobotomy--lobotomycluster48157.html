lobotomy

noun
surgical severing of brain nerve tracts
9.9 0.65/m
Pronunciation: / lo äÀàb…ëÀêt…ômi / (AmE), / l…ô äÀàb…ít…ômi / (BrE)
a surgical operation in which nerve pathways in the frontal lobe of the brain are cut, formerly used to treat certain mental illnesses, often resulting in significant changes in personality or cognitive ability

Examples

A lobotomy operation
Doctors once performed a lobotomy to manage severe mental disorders, but it is now rarely used.
The controversial practice of lobotomy faded as psychiatric medicines became more advanced.
The patient experienced dramatic personality changes after undergoing a lobotomy.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.03/m
    frontal lobotomy is a surgical cutting of frontal connections with major personality effects, while lobotomy is the broad historical operation.
  • prefrontal lobotomy
    9.9 0.01/m

  • prefrontal leukotomy
    9.9 0.00/m
    prefrontal leukotomy is a medical term for cutting prefrontal nerve pathways in old psychiatric care, while lobotomy is the common historical name.
  • leukotomy
    9.9 0.00/m
    leukotomy refers to severing white-matter fibers in the frontal brain used in clinical texts, while lobotomy is the more familiar general term.
