uprise

verb
move or rise upward
7.0 7.5
Pronunciation: / əˈpraɪz /; / ʌpˈraɪz / (AmE), / ʌpˈraɪz / (BrE)
to move or rise upward; to ascend or go up (such as mist or sound)

Examples

Mist uprises from ground
As the sun warmed the earth, a delicate mist began to uprise from the valley.
Clouds seemed to uprise along the distant horizon before the storm.

As the sun set, a gentle hum uprose from the distant meadow.
From the valley, a joyful chorus uprose and reached the listening crowd.
The organ's deep notes uprise and echo through the cathedral's arches.
