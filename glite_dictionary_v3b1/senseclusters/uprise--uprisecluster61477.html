Synonyms

  • rise
    1.0 70/m
    rise means to move upward or go higher in many situations, while uprise is a rarer word for the same action.
  • arise
    2.7 0.9/m
    arise describes smoke or mist moving up into the air, while uprise also means upward movement but sounds old-fashioned.
  • ascend
    4.0 5/m
    ascend means to go up to a higher place, often in formal or technical contexts, while uprise is more general.
  • go up
    1.0 11/m
    go up is the everyday phrase for moving or increasing upward, while uprise is less common and sounds formal.
  • come up
    2.2 20/m
    come up describes things rising or appearing from below, while uprise focuses on upward movement and feels less natural in speech.
  • move up
    2.0 2/m
    move up is a neutral phrasal verb for going to a higher level, while uprise is a single formal word.
  • lift
    1.0 19/m
    lift often means being raised by an outside force or wind, while uprise means to rise on its own or ascend.
