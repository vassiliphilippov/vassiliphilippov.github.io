infaunal

adjective
living in sediments
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔnəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːnəl / (BrE)
living within the sediments of the sea floor or a lake bed

Examples

Infaunal sea animals
Many clams are infaunal organisms that burrow into the sand.
Researchers studied the infaunal communities found deep within the muddy lake bed.
The infaunal lifestyle helps some marine animals avoid predators on the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthic - relating to the bottom of a sea or lake
  • antonym: epifaunal - living on the surface of the sea floor or a lake bed
