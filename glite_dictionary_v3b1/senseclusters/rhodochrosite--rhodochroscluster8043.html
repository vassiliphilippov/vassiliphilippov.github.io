rhodochrosite

noun
manganese carbonate mineral
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊdəˈkroʊsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌrəʊdəˈkrəʊsaɪt / (BrE)
a pink to red mineral consisting of manganese carbonate, used as a source of manganese and sometimes as a gemstone

Examples

Pink rhodochrosite mineral
Rhodochrosite is valued both as a source of manganese and as a collector's mineral.
Miners discovered several large deposits of rhodochrosite in the mountain region.
The museum has a stunning rhodochrosite crystal on display in the gemstone section.
