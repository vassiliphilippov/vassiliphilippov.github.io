symbiogenesis

noun
origin by merging species
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmbaɪoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbiəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the formation of a new organism or structure through the merging of two different species in symbiosis

Examples

Symbiogenesis theory
The theory of symbiogenesis suggests that mitochondria originated from ancient bacteria.
Many scientists now accept symbiogenesis as a key process in the evolution of complex cells.
Lynn Margulis was a major proponent of symbiogenesis in explaining the origin of eukaryotic cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolution - the process by which different kinds of living organisms develop and change over time
  • synonym: symbiosis - a close and long-term biological interaction between two different species
