Cox-2

noun
enzyme involved in inflammation
Pronunciation: / kɑks tuː / (AmE), / kɒks tuː / (BrE)
an enzyme responsible for the formation of prostaglandins, which promote inflammation, pain, and fever

Examples

Blocking Cox-2 enzyme
Blocking Cox-2 can help alleviate symptoms of pain and fever.
Drugs that inhibit Cox-2 are often prescribed for arthritis to reduce inflammation.
Researchers are developing new Cox-2 inhibitors with fewer side effects.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Cox-1 - an enzyme similar to Cox-2, but with different physiological roles
  • synonym: cyclooxygenase - any enzyme that catalyzes the formation of prostaglandins
