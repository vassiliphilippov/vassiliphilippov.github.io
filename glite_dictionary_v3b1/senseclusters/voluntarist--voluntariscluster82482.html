voluntarist

noun
theological believer in will
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːlənˌtɛrɪst / (AmE), / ˈvɒlənˌtærɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that will (human or divine) is the main driving force in reality, morality, or human affairs

Examples

A leading voluntarist
The philosopher was known as a leading voluntarist in debates about the nature of morality.
Some medieval thinkers were voluntarists, arguing that divine will is above human reason.
A voluntarist might claim that moral laws depend entirely on God's choices.
As a voluntarist, she argued that personal choice shapes society more than any other factor.
The debate between the voluntarist and the determinist highlighted their opposing worldviews.
Many philosophers consider William James to be a prominent voluntarist in the history of psychology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinist - a person who believes all events are determined by causes external to will
  • synonym: voluntarism - the doctrine that will is the fundamental principle of reality or ethics
