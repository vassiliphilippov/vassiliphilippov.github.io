toot your own horn

verb
boast about oneself
7.5 0.14/m
Pronunciation: / tuÀêt j är o än h…îrn / (AmE), / tuÀêt j…îÀêr …ô än h…îÀên / (BrE)
to boast or brag about your own achievements or abilities

Examples

To toot your own horn loudly
If you never toot your own horn, people might not notice your strengths.
It's okay to toot your own horn when you've worked hard on a project.
She doesn't like to toot her own horn, but her accomplishments speak for themselves.

Synonyms

  • blow one's own trumpet
    7.8 0.03/m
    blow one's own trumpet is a British idiom that means boasting loudly, while toot your own horn is its American counterpart.
  • brag
    2.7 6.65/m
    brag means speaking with excessive pride about yourself, while toot your own horn suggests showing off publicly and informally.
  • trumpet one's achievements
    7.0 0.00/m
    trumpet one's achievements means publicly announce successes, while toot your own horn is a more casual way to boast.
  • crow about
    7.9 0.43/m
    crow about means brag loudly in a way that annoys others, while toot your own horn is more neutral about boasting.
  • talk big
    7.8 0.19/m
    talk big is to boast and make exaggerated claims, while toot your own horn is a general informal way to show pride.
  • puff
    7.0 0.36/m
    puff means to speak in a self-important or boastful way, while toot your own horn is specifically public bragging.
  • pat oneself on the back
    6.9 1.93/m
    pat oneself on the back means praise oneself for a job well done, while toot your own horn means boast loudly to others.
