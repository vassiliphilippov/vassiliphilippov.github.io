Pteridospermopsida

noun
extinct class of seed ferns
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɛrɪdoʊspɜrˈmɑːpsɪdə /; / ˌtɛrɪˌdoʊspərˈmɑpsɪdə / (AmE), / tɛrɪdəʊspɜːˈmɒpsɪdə /; / ˌtɛrɪˌdəʊspɜːˈmɒpsɪdə / (BrE)
an extinct class of gymnosperm plants, known as seed ferns, that lived from the Carboniferous to Jurassic periods

Examples

Pteridospermopsida seed ferns
Pteridospermopsida species are often referred to as seed ferns due to their fern-like leaves and seed-bearing structures.
Fossils of Pteridospermopsida have been key to understanding plant evolution in the Paleozoic era.
The extinction of Pteridospermopsida marked a significant transition in prehistoric plant life.
Fossils of Pteridospermopsida provide important clues about the evolution of seed plants.
Researchers study Pteridospermopsida to better understand the transition from spore-bearing to seed-bearing plants.
The Pteridospermopsida dominated many forest ecosystems during the Carboniferous period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cycadophyta - a division of ancient and modern cycads, also seed plants
  • antonym: Pteridophyta - division of true ferns, non-seed plants
  • synonym: seed fern - any of a group of extinct plants with fern-like foliage but reproducing by seeds
