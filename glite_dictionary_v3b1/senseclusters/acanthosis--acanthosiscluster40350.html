acanthosis

noun
abnormal skin thickening
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.ænˈθoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌak.ænˈθəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
An abnormal but non-cancerous thickening of the prickle-cell layer of the skin, often seen in certain skin diseases such as psoriasis.

Examples

acanthosis skin thickening
Biopsy results confirmed acanthosis rather than a malignant growth.
Certain cases of obesity in children are associated with acanthosis of the skin.
The dermatologist diagnosed the dark, velvety patch as acanthosis.
