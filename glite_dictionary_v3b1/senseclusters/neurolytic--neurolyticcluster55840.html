neurolytic

noun
nerve-destroying agent
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
a substance or agent used to destroy nerve tissue, usually to relieve severe pain by blocking nerve signals

Examples

A neurolytic injection
The doctor administered a neurolytic to help manage the patient's chronic pain.
A neurolytic can be used in procedures to block nerve signals in cancer patients.
Researchers are studying the long-term effects of neurolytics on nerve regeneration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analgesic - a drug used to relieve pain
  • synonym: neurolysin - an enzyme that breaks down certain proteins in nerve tissue
