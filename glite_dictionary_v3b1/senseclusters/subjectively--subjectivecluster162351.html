subjectively

adverb
in a personal or biased way
3.0 4.9
Pronunciation: / səbˈdʒɛktɪvli / (AmE), / səbˈdʒɛktɪvli / (BrE)
In a way that is based on personal feelings, tastes, or opinions rather than external facts.

Examples

To think subjectively
Because we experienced the event subjectively, our memories of it are quite different.
It's important not to judge the situation subjectively without considering all the facts.
She viewed the results subjectively, allowing her emotions to influence her interpretation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectively - in a way that is based on observable facts, not personal feelings
