snake-haired

adjective
having snakes instead of hair
Pronunciation: / sneɪk hɛrd / (AmE), / sneɪk hɛəd / (BrE)
(of a creature or monster) having snakes instead of hair on the head.

Examples

Snake-haired monster
In Greek mythology, only one snake-haired Gorgon survived Perseus's quest.
The artist's painting featured a terrifying snake-haired figure glaring from the canvas.
The legend describes Medusa as a snake-haired monster who could turn men to stone.
