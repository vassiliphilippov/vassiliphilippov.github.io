fragmentariness

noun
state of being incomplete
Pronunciation: / ˈfræɡməntərinəs / (AmE), / ˈfræɡməntərinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of consisting of disconnected, incomplete, or broken parts

Examples

The fragmentariness of ruins
The fragmentariness of the ancient ruins made it hard to imagine their original form.
Critics noted the fragmentariness of the story, which jumped between unrelated events.
Because of the fragmentariness of the evidence, the case remained unsolved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incompleteness - the state of not being whole or finished
  • antonym: wholeness - the state of being complete and undivided
