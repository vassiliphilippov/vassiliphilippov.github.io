regenerate

verb
amplify a signal by feedback in electronics
3.8 5.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛnəreɪt / (BrE)
(of an electronic circuit) to amplify an electronic signal by feeding part of the output back into the input circuit.

Examples

To regenerate a signal
Certain radio circuits can regenerate weak incoming broadcasts, making them clearer.
If too much feedback is used, the amplifier may regenerate excessively and cause distortion.
The engineer designed the radio to regenerate the signal for stronger reception.
