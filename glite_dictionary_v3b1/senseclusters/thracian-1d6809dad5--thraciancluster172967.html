Thracian

noun
extinct language of ancient Thrace
Pronunciation: / ˈθreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθreɪʃən / (BrE)
the extinct Indo-European language once spoken by the ancient people of Thrace until the early Middle Ages

Examples

Thracian language
Scholars have studied ancient inscriptions to learn more about the Thracian language.
The Thracian language is thought to have some similarities with Phrygian and other ancient Indo-European tongues.
Very few written records of Thracian have survived to the present day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Phrygian - an ancient Indo-European language spoken in Phrygia, closely related to Thracian
