magister

noun
master's degree holder
Pronunciation: / məˈɡɪstər / (AmE), / məˈɡɪstə / (BrE)
an academic degree equivalent to a master's degree, or a person who holds that degree, used in some countries

Examples

Magister degree
After years of study, she finally became a magister in biology.
In some European countries, the title magister is highly respected in academic circles.
He introduced himself as a magister during the university conference.
