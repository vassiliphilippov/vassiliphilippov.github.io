Examples

Words of comfort
He was accused of giving comfort to the criminal by hiding him from the police.
Providing comfort to the enemy is considered a serious offense during wartime.
The law prohibits any act that would offer comfort or aid to those committing treason.
After her loss, the kindness of friends brought her great comfort.
He offered words of comfort during a difficult time.
Sometimes just being present can be a source of comfort to someone in distress.
He found comfort in the thought that he had done his best.
Her kind words brought comfort to everyone after the bad news.
The presence of family gave her comfort during difficult times.

Synonyms

  • solace
    5.1 3.98/m
    solace offers quiet emotional support in sad times, while comfort is general words or actions that ease someone's distress.
  • consolation
    3.8 4.97/m
    consolation is support given after loss or disappointment, while comfort covers words or actions for many kinds of distress.
  • relief
    1.0 34/m
    relief is the feeling when pain or worry decreases, while comfort is the active support people give to ease suffering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consolation - the act of comforting or being comforted, especially after a loss or disappointment
  • antonym: distress - extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain, which comfort seeks to relieve
  • synonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort, also emotional relaxation
