convertibility

noun
ability to be exchanged or converted
1.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /kən.vɜːr.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /kən.vɜː.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to be converted into another form, or of being exchangeable, especially the ability to exchange a currency freely for gold or other currencies.

Examples

Convertibility of currency
Capital convertibility varies from one country to another, affecting ease of financial transactions.
During the gold standard era, currency convertibility was a major concern for governments.
Many investors value the convertibility of their assets in international markets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inconvertibility - the quality of not being able to be converted or exchanged
