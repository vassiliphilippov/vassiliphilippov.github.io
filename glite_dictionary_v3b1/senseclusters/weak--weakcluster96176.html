weak

adjective
declining in price or value (finance)
Pronunciation: / wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
(of prices or markets) tending to decrease or decline in value or strength.

Examples

Weak market
A weak real estate market led to lower home prices last year.
After the announcement, shares opened in a weak position and continued to fall.
The weak dollar has caused concerns among international investors.
