energetics

noun
study of energy flow
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnərˈdʒɛtɪks / (AmE), / ˌɛnəˈdʒɛtɪks / (BrE)
the scientific study of how energy moves, changes, and is related in physical, chemical, or biological systems

Examples

Chemical energetics
The energetics of chemical reactions help scientists understand how energy is released or absorbed.
Researchers in biology often analyze energetics to understand animal movement.
Advances in energetics have improved the efficiency of renewable energy systems.
The energetics of the reaction help scientists understand how much energy is needed for it to occur.
In biology, energetics explains how organisms obtain and use energy from food.
Engineers analyze the energetics of machines to improve their efficiency and performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioenergetics - the study of energy flow in living organisms
  • antonym: kinetics - the study of the rates of chemical processes
  • synonym: thermodynamics - the branch of physics dealing with heat and energy transfer
