heart valve

noun
artificial replacement for natural heart valve
3.5 3.2
Pronunciation: / hɑrt vælv / (AmE), / hɑːt vælv / (BrE)
an artificial device implanted to replace a damaged or diseased natural valve in the heart

Examples

Artificial heart valve
A mechanical heart valve can last for decades if cared for appropriately.
After surgery, the patient's new heart valve was functioning properly.
Doctors discussed the risks and benefits of heart valve replacement with the family.

Related Terms

  • antonym: natural heart valve - the original valve in the heart, not replaced by an implant
  • synonym: prosthetic valve - an artificial substitute for a damaged valve, especially in the heart
