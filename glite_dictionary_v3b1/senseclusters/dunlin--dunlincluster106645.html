dunlin

noun
a small migratory sandpiper

a small wading bird (Calidris alpina) with a distinctive black belly patch in breeding plumage, found in northern or Arctic regions and migrating south in winter.

Examples

A dunlin bird
Flocks of dunlin migrate along the coast each spring, stopping to feed on tidal mudflats.
In winter, you may spot a dunlin among other waders on southern shorelines.
The dunlin can be identified by its long, slightly curved bill and dark belly patch.
