sorghum molasses

noun
sweet syrup from sweet sorghum
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrɡəm məˈlæsɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːɡəm məˈlæsɪs / (BrE)
a thick, sweet syrup made from the juice of sweet sorghum, used as a sweetener or spread

Examples

Sweet sorghum molasses
Farmers harvested sweet sorghum to produce homemade sorghum molasses every autumn.
She poured sorghum molasses over her pancakes for a distinctive, rich flavor.
Traditional recipes in the South often call for sorghum molasses as a primary sweetener.
