lay

adjective
non-professional, not expert
4.9 2.88/m
Pronunciation: / leÉª / (AmE), / leÉª / (BrE)
not belonging to a particular profession or the clergy; relating to ordinary people

Examples

Lay people
From a lay perspective, the scientific explanation seemed very complex.
He offered a lay opinion on the architectural plans, not being an expert in the field.
It's important to translate medical information into lay terms for patients to understand.
Many lay people volunteer in church activities without being ordained.
The church offers a training program for lay ministers.
The decision was made by a lay committee rather than by priests.

Synonyms

  • laic
    9.9 0.00/m
    laic is a formal word for non-clergy people, while lay refers to ordinary, non-professional people in everyday language.
  • secular
    4.7 17/m
    secular refers to non-religious or worldly matters, while lay focuses on ordinary people or those outside a profession or clergy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clerical - relating to the clergy
  • antonym: professional - belonging to a profession or having specialist knowledge
