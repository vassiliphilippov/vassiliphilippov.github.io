wild clary

noun
a Eurasian sage with blue flowers
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈklæri / (AmE), / waɪld ˈklɑːri / (BrE)
A Eurasian sage (Salvia verbenaca) with blue flowers and foliage resembling verbena, often found naturalized in the United States.

Examples

wild clary plant
Gardeners prize wild clary for its attractive blue flowers and ability to thrive in poor soil.
Many herbs, such as wild clary, are used in traditional remedies.
The wild clary was blooming all along the country path.
