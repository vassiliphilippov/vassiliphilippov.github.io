swamp sparrow

noun
North American marsh songbird
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ˈspɛr.oʊ / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈspɑː.rəʊ / (BrE)
a small North American songbird found in marshy areas, known for its rust-colored wings and melodic song

Examples

Swamp sparrows in wetlands
During migration, swamp sparrows can be found in wetlands across the eastern United States.
The distinctive trill of the swamp sparrow echoed through the marshlands.
We spotted a swamp sparrow singing in the reeds by the lake.
