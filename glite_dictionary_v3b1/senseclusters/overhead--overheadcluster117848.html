overhead

noun
regular operating costs (business/property)
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəhɛd / (BrE)
the regular and necessary costs of operating a business or property, such as rent, utilities, and insurance, but not including depreciation, financing, or income taxes

Examples

Overhead costs
High overhead expenses can make a business less profitable.
The company reduced its overhead by moving to a smaller office.
You need to budget for all overhead when running a rental property.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cost - the amount of money needed to buy, do, or make something
  • synonym: expense - an amount of money spent to buy or do something
