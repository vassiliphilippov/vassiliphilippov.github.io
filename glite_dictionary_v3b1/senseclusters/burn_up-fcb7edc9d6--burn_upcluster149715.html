burn up

verb
be destroyed by fire
3.7 0.97/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùn  åp / (AmE), / b…úÀên  åp / (BrE)
to be completely destroyed or consumed by fire

Examples

The house burns up
If you don't watch the stove, the food might burn up and ruin the pot.
The documents burned up before anyone could save them from the flames.
The old barn burned up in the lightning storm last night.

Synonyms

  • burn down
    3.0 4.14/m
    burn down means to be destroyed completely by fire, while burn up means to be completely consumed by fire.
  • go up
    4.4 1.49/m
    go up means to burn completely, often of buildings, while burn up emphasises being consumed entirely by fire.
  • incinerate
    7.4 0.23/m
    incinerate means to reduce something to ashes, often in a controlled process, while burn up implies total destruction by fire.
  • reduced to ashes
    6.4 0.15/m
    reduced to ashes describes something left only as ash after burning, while burn up focuses on being destroyed by fire.
  • up in flames
    6.9 0.66/m
    up in flames often suggests sudden, dramatic loss or failure, while burn up simply means to be entirely destroyed by fire.
