skip distance

noun
minimum distance for radio signals to be received after ionospheric reflection
9.4
Pronunciation: / skɪp ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / skɪp ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
the minimum distance from a transmitter that allows radio signals of a given frequency, reflected by the ionosphere, to be received

Examples

Calculate skip distance
Radio operators must calculate the skip distance to ensure their signals reach the intended location.
The skip distance varies depending on the frequency of the radio waves and ionospheric conditions.
With higher frequencies, the skip distance increases, making closer reception impossible.
