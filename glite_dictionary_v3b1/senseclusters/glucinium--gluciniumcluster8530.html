glucinium

noun
former name for beryllium (chemical element)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡluˈsɪniəm / (AmE), / ɡluˈsɪniəm / (BrE)
An old name for the chemical element beryllium, a light, strong, brittle, grey, toxic, bivalent metallic element.

Examples

Old name glucinium
In early chemistry texts, beryllium was often called glucinium.
The discovery of glucinium was significant due to its unique properties among metals.
The mineral beryl contains glucinium, now more commonly known as beryllium.
