planetesimal

noun
small early solar system body
Pronunciation: / plænəˈtɛsɪməl / (AmE), / plænɪˈtɛsɪməl / (BrE)
a small solid celestial body which is believed to have existed at an early stage of the solar system's formation, and from which planets originated

Examples

Small planetesimal body
Astronomers study asteroids to learn about the composition of ancient planetesimals.
Many scientists believe that planetesimals collided and merged to form the planets we observe today.
The Kuiper Belt is thought to contain countless planetesimals left over from the early solar system.
