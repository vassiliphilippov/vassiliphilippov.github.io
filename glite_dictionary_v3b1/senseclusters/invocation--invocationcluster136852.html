invocation

noun
calling upon a spirit or supernatural power
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.voʊˈkeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɪnvəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.vəʊˈkeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɪnvəˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of calling upon a spirit, devil, or supernatural force, especially through a ritual, prayer, or incantation

Examples

Ritual invocation
During the ceremony, an invocation was used to call forth the demon.
Medieval grimoires often contain instructions for the invocation of supernatural beings.
The priest began the invocation to summon ancient spirits from beyond.
He recited an ancient invocation hoping to raise the dark spirit.
The ritual required a precise invocation to successfully conjure the entity.
The sorceress began her invocation to summon the demon from the underworld.

Related Terms

  • antonym: banishment - a ritual or spell to send away a spirit
  • synonym: incantation - a series of words said as a magic spell
