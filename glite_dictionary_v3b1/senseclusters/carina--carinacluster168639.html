carina

noun
keel-shaped anatomical or botanical ridge
Pronunciation: / kəˈriːnə / (AmE), / kəˈriːnə / (BrE)
a keel-shaped structure or ridge, especially on the breastbone of a bird or formed by fused petals in some flowers

Examples

The carina ridge
In legumes, the carina is formed by the fusion of two petals.
Researchers studied the prominence of the carina in different species of birds.
The bird's carina is highly developed, allowing for powerful flight muscles.
