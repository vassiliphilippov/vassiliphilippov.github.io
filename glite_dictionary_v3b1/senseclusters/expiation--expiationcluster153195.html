expiation

noun
making amends for a wrong
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkspiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkspiˈeɪʃən /; / ˌɛkspɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of making amends or reparation for wrongdoing or guilt

Examples

Her donation to charity was seen as a form of expiation for her earlier mistakes.
The ceremony was performed as an act of expiation for past misdeeds.

The ritual served as a symbolic expiation for the community's past transgressions.
Through charity and prayer, she hoped to find expiation for her sins.
