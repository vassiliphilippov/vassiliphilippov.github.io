to originate or develop

(of an idea, feeling, or movement) to begin to exist or develop; to originate or arise gradually.

Examples

Grow a friendship
Over time, a deep respect grew between the two colleagues.
Sometimes, friendships grow into something more meaningful.
The idea for the festival grew out of a community project.

Synonyms

  • emerge
    4.0 76/m
    emerge is to come into view or become known slowly, while grow is to begin and increase over time.
  • arise
    3.3 38/m
    arise means to appear or start, often suddenly, while grow suggests a slower beginning and steady development.
  • rise
    2.6 4.55/m
    rise means to come into view or begin to form, often of numbers or levels, while grow implies gradual development.
  • originate
    3.8 16/m
    originate means to have a clear source or starting point, while grow describes gradual coming into being.
  • develop
    1.6 96/m
    develop means to change and become larger or better over time, while grow focuses on first arising or starting.
  • evolve
    4.0 46/m
    evolve means to change slowly into a more complex form, while grow can mean simply to begin or increase.
