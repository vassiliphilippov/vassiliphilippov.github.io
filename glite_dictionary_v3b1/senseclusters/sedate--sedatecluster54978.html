sedate

verb
to calm or make sleepy, especially with a drug
4.2 5.5
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈdeɪt/ /; / /sə.ˈdeɪt/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈdeɪt/ /; / /sə.ˈdeɪt/ (BrE)
to make someone calm or sleepy by giving them a drug; to calm or quiet using a sedative

Examples

To sedate a patient
After the accident, the paramedics chose to sedate the injured passenger to ease his pain.
She was so anxious that the doctor decided to sedate her for the dental surgery.
The veterinarian had to sedate the dog before performing the procedure.
