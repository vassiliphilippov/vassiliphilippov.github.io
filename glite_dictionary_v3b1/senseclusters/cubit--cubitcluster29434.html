cubit

noun
ancient unit of length (elbow to fingertip)
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuː.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bɪt / (BrE)
an ancient unit of length, roughly equal to the distance from the elbow to the tip of the middle finger, used in various civilizations and usually considered to be about 18 inches (45.7 cm)

Examples

An ancient cubit measure
Archaeologists discovered a measuring rod marked in cubits at the site.
Construction guidelines in the Bible often specify dimensions in cubits.
The ancient Egyptians measured the pyramids using the cubit as their standard unit.
