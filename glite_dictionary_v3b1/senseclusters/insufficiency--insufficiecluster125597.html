(medical) Inability of a bodily organ or part to function normally or adequately.

Examples

Heart insufficiency
Cardiac insufficiency can lead to decreased blood flow throughout the body.
Renal insufficiency often requires close monitoring and medical treatment.
The patient was diagnosed with adrenal insufficiency after showing symptoms of fatigue and weight loss.

Synonyms

  • incompetence
    6.4 0.1/m
    incompetence means an organ cannot perform its usual work properly, while insufficiency means it fails to work adequately in the body.
  • hypofunction
    9.3 0.05/m
    hypofunction describes below-normal activity of an organ, while insufficiency says the organ really cannot work adequately overall.
  • dysfunction
    6.1 6/m
    dysfunction means organ activity is abnormal or impaired, while insufficiency means the organ cannot perform its needed tasks well.
  • failure
    3.3 30/m
    failure is a total or sudden loss of function in an organ, while insufficiency often refers to reduced or inadequate function.
  • impairment
    6.1 16/m
    impairment is a general loss or reduction in ability from damage, while insufficiency refers to the organ's failure to work properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deficiency - Lack or shortage of something necessary in the body
  • synonym: failure - Collapse or cessation of normal function, especially in organs
