chromic-sulfuric acid

noun
cleaning mixture of chromic and sulfuric acids
Pronunciation: / ˌkroʊ.mɪk ˌsʌlˈfjʊr.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌkrəʊ.mɪk ˌsʌlˈfjʊə.rɪk ˈæs.ɪd / (BrE)
a mixture of chromic acid and sulfuric acid, used as a powerful cleaning agent for laboratory glassware

Examples

Chromic-sulfuric acid cleaning solution
After an experiment, the glassware was soaked in chromic-sulfuric acid to remove stubborn residues.
Always wear gloves and goggles when handling chromic-sulfuric acid due to its corrosive nature.
Laboratories have begun to replace chromic-sulfuric acid with less hazardous cleaning agents.
