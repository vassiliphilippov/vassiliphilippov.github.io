freeze

verb
prohibit the use or movement of assets
5.3 7.39/m
Pronunciation: / friÀêz / (AmE), / friÀêz / (BrE)
to officially stop or suspend an activity, process, or the use of money/assets; or the official suspension itself

Examples

To freeze assets
A court order can freeze a person's funds during a legal dispute.
The government decided to freeze all accounts linked to the suspect organization.
When sanctions were imposed, the central bank had to freeze foreign assets immediately.
Due to budget cuts, the company will freeze hiring for the next six months.
The bank can freeze your account if suspicious activity is detected.
The government decided to freeze all foreign investments until further notice.

Synonyms

  • suspend
    3.9 6.82/m
    suspend means to officially stop something for a time, while freeze often implies legally blocking funds or activities.
  • moratorium
    8.5 2.96/m
    moratorium is an official legal pause on activities or payments, while freeze can be used more broadly by authorities.
  • suspension
    4.3 4.20/m
    suspension is the act or period of stopping something temporarily, while freeze suggests an official block, often for assets.
  • immobilize
    7.6 0.02/m
    immobilize makes assets or capital unavailable in finance, while freeze is an official stop or hold on those funds.
  • on hold
    2.5 5.57/m
    on hold means temporarily paused by a company or service, while freeze implies an official or legal suspension.
  • on ice
    4.9 0.40/m
    on ice means temporarily postponed in a casual or informal way, while freeze suggests an official or legal stoppage.
  • put on ice
    5.8 0.17/m
    put on ice means to postpone a plan or project for now, while freeze refers to formally stopping or blocking activity.
  • halt
    2.4 14/m
    halt means to stop movement or progress, often immediately or completely, while freeze usually means an official temporary suspension.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - to make inaccessible for use
  • synonym: suspend - to temporarily prevent something from continuing or being in force or effect
  • antonym: unfreeze - to release from legal or official prohibition (funds, assets)
