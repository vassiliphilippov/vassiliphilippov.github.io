maple-leaf begonia

noun
South African begonia with maple-like leaves
4.3 7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmeɪ.pəl.liːf bə.ˈɡoʊ.njə/ /; / /ˈmeɪ.pəl.liːf bə.ˈɡoʊ.ni.ə/ (AmE), /ˈmeɪ.pəl.liːf bə.ˈɡəʊ.niː.ə/ /; / /ˈmeɪ.pəl.liːf bə.ˈɡəʊ.njə/ (BrE)
A tuberous or semi-tuberous begonia native to South Africa, notable for its shallowly lobed, ovate leaves resembling those of a maple and small white flowers.

Examples

The maple-leaf begonia plant
Gardeners often choose the maple-leaf begonia for its attractive leaves and delicate white flowers.

The maple-leaf begonia thrives in shaded gardens with well-drained soil.
