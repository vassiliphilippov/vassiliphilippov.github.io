interdict

verb
to impede or destroy by firepower (military)
5.0 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.tər.dɪkt/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.tə.dɪkt/ (BrE)
(of military forces) to destroy, block, or impede (an enemy's movements or communications) by use of firepower or bombardment.

Examples

To interdict enemy supplies
Allied bombers interdicted the railway bridges, hindering enemy troop movements.
The air force was ordered to interdict enemy supply routes throughout the valley.
They successfully interdicted hostile convoys, cutting off reinforcements to the front lines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permit - to allow to happen
