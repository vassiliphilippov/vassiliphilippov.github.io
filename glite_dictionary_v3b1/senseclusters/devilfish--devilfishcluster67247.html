devilfish

noun
large plankton-eating tropical ray
5.9 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛvəlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a very large tropical ray, especially the manta ray, known for its broad, wing-like fins and plankton-feeding habits; generally harmless but can be dangerous due to its size when threatened

Examples

Giant devilfish swims
Fishermen once feared the devilfish because of its impressive size.
The devilfish feeds on plankton and small fish as it swims near the ocean surface.
The diver was thrilled to spot a massive devilfish gliding gracefully through the water.
