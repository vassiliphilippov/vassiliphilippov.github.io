electric guitar

noun
guitar using electronic amplification
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktrɪk ɡɪˈtɑr / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrɪk ɡɪˈtɑː / (BrE)
A guitar that produces sound electronically and must be connected to an amplifier.

Examples

An electric guitar sound
He prefers the sound of an electric guitar to that of an acoustic.
She learned to play the electric guitar when she was sixteen.
The band's lead guitarist performed a solo on his vintage electric guitar.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acoustic guitar - a guitar that produces sound acoustically, without electronic amplification
