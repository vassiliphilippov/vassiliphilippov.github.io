rough justice

noun
harsh, unofficial punishment
Pronunciation: / rʌf ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / rʌf ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
punishment that is harsh and not administered through legal or official channels

Examples

Rough justice by community
Many communities turn to rough justice when they lose trust in the legal system.
Some see public shaming as a kind of rough justice in the digital age.
The gang delivered their own form of rough justice to the thief before the police arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym: due process - fair treatment through the legal system
  • synonym: vigilante - a person who takes the law into their own hands
