Oxfordian

noun
supporter of Oxford theory
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑksˈfɔːrdiən / (AmE), / ɒksˈfɔːdiən / (BrE)
a person who supports the theory that Edward de Vere, 17th Earl of Oxford, was the true author of Shakespeare's works

Examples

Oxfordian supporter
The Oxfordian presented new evidence supporting Edward de Vere's authorship at the literary conference.
Many Oxfordians debate with Stratfordians about the true identity of Shakespeare.
As an Oxfordian, she has written several articles challenging the traditional view of Shakespeare's works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Stratfordian - a person who believes William Shakespeare of Stratford-upon-Avon wrote the works attributed to him
