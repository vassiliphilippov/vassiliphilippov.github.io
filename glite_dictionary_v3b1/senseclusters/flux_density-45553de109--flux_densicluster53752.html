flux density

noun
rate of flow per unit area (physics)
4.8 6.1
Pronunciation: / flʌks ˈdɛn.sə.ti / (AmE), / flʌks ˈdɛn.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Physics) The amount of energy or particles passing through a given surface per unit area, typically measured per second.

Examples

Neutron flux density
Scientists measured the neutron flux density inside the reactor core.
Solar panels must withstand high levels of solar flux density in outer space.
The magnetic flux density in the coil increased as more current was applied.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flux - continuous flow of a substance, especially energy or particles
