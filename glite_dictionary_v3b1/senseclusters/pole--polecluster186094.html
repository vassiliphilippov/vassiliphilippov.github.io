pole

noun
long rod of wood, metal, or plastic
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
a long, thin, usually round rod or stick used for support, as a tool, or in sports

Examples

A wooden pole
A metal pole supports the new streetlight.
He used a pole to vault over the fence.
They replaced the broken flag pole in front of the school.
She selected a new pole for the upcoming pole vault competition.
The athlete gripped the pole tightly before his jump.
These fiberglass poles are designed to be both flexible and strong.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pole vault - an athletic event in which an athlete uses a pole to jump over a high bar
  • synonym: rod - a straight, slender stick or bar, typically of wood or metal
  • synonym: shaft - a long, narrow part or section of an object
