organized crime

noun
large-scale criminal activities by groups
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.ɡə.naɪzd kraɪm / (AmE), / ˈɔː.ɡə.naɪzd kraɪm / (BrE)
Criminal activities carried out by structured groups, typically involving illegal operations on a large scale.

Examples

Organized crime networks
International cooperation is vital in tackling the spread of organized crime networks.
Movies about organized crime often depict powerful criminal syndicates and their leaders.
The police launched a new initiative to combat organized crime in the city.
