swinishness

noun
the quality of being like a swine
Pronunciation: / ˈswaɪnɪʃnəs / (AmE), / ˈswʌɪnɪʃnɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being like a swine; gluttonous, coarse, or debased behavior or character.

Examples

His swinishness behavior
His swinishness at the dinner table disgusted everyone present.
She resented the swinishness of his remarks during the meeting.
The novel depicts the swinishness of a society obsessed with greed and excess.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brutishness - the quality of being brutal, coarse, or uncivilized
  • antonym: refinement - cultivated, elegant, or sophisticated behavior (opposite quality)
