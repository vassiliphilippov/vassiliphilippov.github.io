sheen

verb
to shine softly
4.0 0.07/m
Pronunciation: /  ÉiÀên / (AmE), /  ÉiÀên / (BrE)
to shine with a soft, smooth, or bright light

Examples

To sheen in sunlight
The polished wood sheened in the afternoon sunlight.
Her hair sheened under the bright lights of the stage.
After the rain, the leaves sheened with tiny droplets of water.

Synonyms

  • glisten
    4.1 6.59/m
    glisten means a soft reflected shine on wet or polished surfaces, while sheen suggests a smooth surface brightness.
  • gleam
    1.9 9.49/m
    gleam is a brief soft bright or reflected light seen from edges, while sheen suggests an even, smooth shine.
  • shine
    1.0 24/m
    shine means to emit or reflect light in many ways, while sheen refers to a soft, smooth surface brightness.
  • shimmer
    4.3 6.31/m
    shimmer is a soft, wavering or flickering light on surfaces, while sheen is a steady, smooth form of shine.
  • glow
    1.2 15/m
    glow is a steady, warm light that seems to come from inside, while sheen is a smooth surface shine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - to lack brightness or shine
