sheen

verb
to shine softly
Pronunciation: / ʃiːn / (AmE), / ʃiːn / (BrE)
to shine with a soft, smooth, or bright light

Examples

To sheen in sunlight
The polished wood sheened in the afternoon sunlight.
Her hair sheened under the bright lights of the stage.
After the rain, the leaves sheened with tiny droplets of water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - to lack brightness or shine
  • synonym: gleam - to shine with a steady light
  • synonym: glow - to give off a steady light without flames
