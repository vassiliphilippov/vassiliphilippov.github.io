chemoprophylactic

noun
disease-preventing drug
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˌproʊfəˈlæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˌprɒfɪˈlæktɪk / (BrE)
a drug or chemical agent used to prevent the development of a disease or infection

Examples

A chemoprophylactic medicine
The doctor recommended a chemoprophylactic before traveling to the malaria-endemic area.
Taking a chemoprophylactic can help prevent certain infections during outbreaks.
Some travelers forget to bring their chemoprophylactic medications and risk getting sick.
