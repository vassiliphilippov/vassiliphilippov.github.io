multiplet

noun
grouped musical notes
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪplət / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪplɪt / (BrE)
a group of musical notes played in the time usually given to a different number of notes, such as a tuplet

Examples

Rhythmic multiplet
The pianist played a fast multiplet that added excitement to the melody.
Learning to count a multiplet can be challenging for beginner musicians.
The score included a rare five-note multiplet in the second measure.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: triplet - three notes played in the time of two
  • synonym: tuplet - a group of notes played in the time of a different number of notes
