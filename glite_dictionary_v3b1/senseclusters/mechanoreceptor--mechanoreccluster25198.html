mechanoreceptor

noun
pressure-sensing cell
Pronunciation: / məˌkænəˈrɛsɛptər / (AmE), / mɪˌkænəʊˈrɛsɛptə / (BrE)
a sensory cell or organ that detects mechanical pressure, touch, or distortion in the body

Examples

A mechanoreceptor in skin
A mechanoreceptor in your skin helps you sense touch and pressure.
The scientist studied how each mechanoreceptor responds to different types of vibration.
Damage to a mechanoreceptor can reduce your ability to feel textures or gentle contact.
