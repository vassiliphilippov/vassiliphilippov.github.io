adaptation

noun
physiological adjustment of a sense organ
3.3 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌæd.əpˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæd.æpˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(Physiology) The process by which a sense organ, such as the eye, adjusts to different conditions, such as changes in light.

Examples

Adaptation of the eye
Adaptation of the eye to darkness takes several minutes.
Rapid adaptation to loud noise can affect hearing sensitivity.
Retinal adaptation helps us see clearly when moving from bright sunlight to a dim room.
