immunogen

noun
substance causing immune response
8.0 0.22/m

a substance or organism that stimulates an immune response, producing immunity, when introduced into the body

Examples

A immunogen in vaccine
A vaccine contains an immunogen that helps the body recognize and fight specific pathogens.
Researchers tested the immunogen in mice to measure the immune system's response.
The effectiveness of an immunogen depends on its ability to elicit a strong and lasting immunity.

Synonyms

  • immunizing agent
    4.4 0.00/m
    an immunizing agent is a substance given to create immunity in the body, while an immunogen is any substance that triggers an immune response.
  • inoculant
    8.7 0.16/m
    an inoculant is a substance deliberately introduced to build or boost immunity, while an immunogen broadly describes any agent that stimulates immunity.
  • antigen
    5.0 2.56/m
