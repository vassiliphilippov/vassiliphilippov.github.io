photoproduction

noun
production by light
8.1
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ /; / /foʊ.tə.prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ /; / /fəʊ.tə.prə.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the production of a substance or effect as a result of the action of light

Examples

Photoproduction of oxygen
The photoproduction of oxygen is a key step in the process of photosynthesis.
Scientists study photoproduction to understand how light can create chemical changes in materials.
In some bacteria, photoproduction of hydrogen gas occurs when they are exposed to sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: photodecomposition - the breakdown of substances by the action of light
  • synonym: photosynthesis - the process by which green plants use sunlight to make food
