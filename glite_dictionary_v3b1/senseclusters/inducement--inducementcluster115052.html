inducement

noun
something that motivates or encourages
6.3 1.02/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàdusm…ônt / (AmE), / …™nÀàdjuÀêsm…ônt / (BrE)
something that persuades or encourages someone to do something; a motive or incentive.

Examples

An inducement to invest
Tax breaks often serve as an inducement to attract investors to a region.
The company offered a generous bonus as an inducement for employees to meet their targets.
Without some kind of inducement, people may not be willing to participate in the study.

Synonyms

  • incentive
    3.9 37/m
    incentive offers a clear reward or benefit to motivate action, while inducement names a reason that persuades someone to act.
  • enticement
    6.2 0.49/m
    enticement tempts by offering pleasure or a reward, while inducement is a more general motive to persuade action.
  • urging
    3.9 2.01/m
    urging means strong, direct encouragement or pressure to do something, while inducement names any motive or incentive to act.
  • goad
    7.4 0.10/m
    goad pushes someone sharply, often to provoke a quick response, while inducement suggests a softer reason to persuade.
  • prompting
    3.8 0.03/m
    prompting is a gentle suggestion or reminder that helps someone decide, while inducement is a broader motive or incentive to act.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incentive - something that encourages a person to do something
