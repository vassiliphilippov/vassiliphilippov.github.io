Forth River

proper noun
river in southern Scotland
4.5
Pronunciation: / fɔrθ ˈrɪvɚ / (AmE), / fɔːθ ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A river in southern Scotland that flows eastward into the Firth of Forth, notable for passing through Stirling and Edinburgh.

Examples

The Forth River in Scotland
Many bridges have been built across the Forth River, including the famous Forth Bridge.
The Forth River flows past the historic city of Stirling before reaching the North Sea.
The scenery along the banks of the Forth River attracts thousands of tourists every year.
