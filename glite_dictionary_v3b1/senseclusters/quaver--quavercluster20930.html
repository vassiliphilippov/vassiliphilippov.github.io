quaver

verb
sing or play with a trembling or trilling sound
Pronunciation: / ˈkweɪvər / (AmE), / ˈkweɪvə / (BrE)
to produce a trembling or wavering sound, especially in singing or speaking

Examples

To quaver nervously
He tried to quaver the last note, but his voice was too weak.
The soprano began to quaver on the high note, giving the melody a haunting quality.
With skilled fingers, the violinist could quaver between notes, adding expressive depth to the music.
Her voice began to quaver with emotion as she spoke about her childhood.
The note quavered in the cold night air, barely audible above the wind.
The old man's words quavered as he struggled to remember the details of the story.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
  • synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, often used for sound or voice
  • synonym: vibrate - to move or sound with slight rapid motion or variation
  • synonym: wobble - to move or sound unsteadily from side to side
