joint author

noun
co-author; collaborative writer
3.3
Pronunciation: / dʒɔɪnt ˈɔθɚ / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt ˈɔːθə / (BrE)
a person who collaborates with one or more others to write a book, article, or other work, sharing authorship responsibility

Examples

A joint author credit
As a joint author, she shared both the credit and the responsibilities of publication.
The novel was written by two joint authors who combined their skills to create the story.
Under copyright law, each joint author has equal rights to the work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: co-author - a person who writes something together with one or more others
  • antonym: sole author - a person who is the only writer of a work
