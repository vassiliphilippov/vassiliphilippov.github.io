hypothetical

noun
a supposed situation or possibility
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈθɛtɪkəl / (BrE)
a supposed situation, circumstance, or proposition that is assumed for the sake of argument or investigation

Examples

A hypothetical example
In philosophical debates, one often examines a hypothetical to see if the theory holds.
Let’s use a hypothetical to clarify how the policy might work in extreme cases.
The researcher asked us to respond to a hypothetical where two laws conflicted.
