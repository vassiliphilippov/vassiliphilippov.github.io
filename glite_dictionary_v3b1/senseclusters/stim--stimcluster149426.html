stim

noun
self-soothing movement
9.9
Pronunciation: / stɪm / (AmE), / stɪm / (BrE)
a repetitive movement or action, often used by autistic people, that helps self-regulation or provides comfort

Examples

A hand-flapping stim
Many autistic people use stims like rocking or hand-flapping to help manage stress.
She found that tapping her fingers was a helpful stim during overwhelming situations.
Some teachers allow students to use stims in class to help them focus.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: calm - a state of being relaxed and free from agitation
  • synonym: fidget - to make small movements, often unconsciously, usually due to nervousness or boredom
