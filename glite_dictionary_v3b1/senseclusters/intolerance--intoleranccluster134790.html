intolerance

noun
impatience with annoyances or minor disruptions
2.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɑːlərəns /; / ɪnˈtɑːlɚəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɒlərəns /; / ɪnˈtɒl(ə)rəns / (BrE)
unwillingness or inability to accept differences, annoyances, or people with different opinions or beliefs

Examples

Intolerance of others
His intolerance of even the slightest interruption made meetings difficult.
Increasing intolerance towards noise has made city life stressful for him.
She showed intolerance for minor mistakes, which made her colleagues nervous.
His intolerance for opposing viewpoints made discussions with him very difficult.
Social media can sometimes fuel intolerance instead of promoting understanding.
The organization was founded in response to religious intolerance in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bigotry - intolerance toward those who hold different opinions
  • degree lower: patience - the ability to accept or tolerate delays or problems
  • antonym: tolerance - willingness to accept annoyances or difficulties
