signal/noise ratio

noun
ratio of signal strength to noise
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪɡnəl ˈnoɪz ˌreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / ˈsɪɡnəl ˈnɔɪz ˌreɪʃiəʊ / (BrE)
The ratio of the strength of a desired signal to the strength of background noise, often expressed in decibels.

Examples

High signal/noise ratio
A low signal/noise ratio makes it difficult to distinguish the actual data from background interference.
Engineers aim to maximize the signal/noise ratio in data transmission systems for better performance.
The signal/noise ratio of this audio recording is quite high, resulting in clear sound quality.
