mathematical group

noun
set with group operation in mathematics
Pronunciation: / ˌmæθəməˈtɪkəl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmatɪkəl ɡruːp / (BrE)
A set of elements with a binary operation that is closed, associative, has an identity element, and every element has an inverse; a basic structure in abstract algebra.

Examples

A mathematical group structure
Learning about the properties of a mathematical group helps in understanding advanced topics in mathematics.
Symmetry operations in a crystal can often be described using a mathematical group.
The concept of a mathematical group is fundamental in abstract algebra.
