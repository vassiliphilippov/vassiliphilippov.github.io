call someone's bluff

verb
challenge someone to prove a claim
9.1
Pronunciation: / kɔl sʌmwʌnz blʌf /; / kɔl ˈsʌmwʌnz blʌf / (AmE), / kɔːl sʌmwʌnz blʌf /; / kɔːl ˈsʌmwʌnz blʌf / (BrE)
to challenge someone to prove that what they are saying or threatening is true, because you think they are not being honest or are exaggerating

Examples

To call someone's bluff in negotiation
During the negotiation, the agent tried to call their bluff by insisting on seeing the competing offer.
She threatened to quit, but her boss called her bluff and said she was free to leave if she wanted.
The manager decided to call his bluff and asked him to present his findings to the board.
He likes to boast, but if you call his bluff, he usually backs down.
She called their bluff by demanding proof of their accusations.
When I threatened to quit, my boss decided to call my bluff and didn't try to stop me.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluff - pretend to have a stronger position or more resources than is actually the case
  • synonym: challenge - to invite someone to prove or justify something
