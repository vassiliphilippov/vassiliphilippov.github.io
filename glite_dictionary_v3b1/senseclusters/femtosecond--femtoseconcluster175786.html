femtosecond

noun
one quadrillionth of a second
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛm.toʊˌsɛk.ənd / (AmE), / ˈfɛm.təʊˌsɛk.ənd / (BrE)
A unit of time equal to one quadrillionth (10⁻¹⁵) of a second; one thousandth of a picosecond.

Examples

A femtosecond unit
Laser pulses of just a femtosecond can be used to study molecular motion.
The reaction was measured on a femtosecond timescale for greater accuracy.
Ultrafast lasers can emit bursts of light lasting only a few femtoseconds.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: picosecond - a unit of time equal to one trillionth of a second (10⁻¹² second)
