noncommitment

noun
lack of commitment
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
the state or quality of not making a definite decision, promise, or showing clear support for something

Examples

Her noncommitment to project
Her noncommitment to the project made it difficult for the team to move forward.
The politician's noncommitment on the issue disappointed many of his supporters.
Because of his noncommitment, the group struggled to make important decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commitment - the state of being dedicated to a cause or activity
  • synonym: indecision - the inability to make a decision quickly
