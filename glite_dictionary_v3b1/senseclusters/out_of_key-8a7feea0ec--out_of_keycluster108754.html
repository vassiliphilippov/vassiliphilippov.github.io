out of key

adjective
not in correct musical pitch
5.8 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / a ät …ôv ki / (AmE), / a ät …ôv kiÀê / (BrE)
not in the correct musical pitch; out of tune.

Examples

Out of key singing
If the piano is out of key, the whole band sounds off.
The audience noticed that the opening chords were badly out of key.
The soloist was clearly out of key during the difficult section of the song.

Synonyms

  • out of tune
    3.3 0.61/m
    out of tune means not having the correct musical pitch or harmony, while out of key names the same lack of correct pitch.
  • off-key
    4.5 0.59/m
    off-key describes a voice or note that sounds wrong in melody, while out of key means not matching the correct musical pitch.
  • untuned
    5.3 0.02/m
    untuned refers to an instrument not adjusted to the right pitch, while out of key describes something sounding wrong in pitch.
  • unattuned
    7.5 0.00/m
    unattuned often means not in harmony or not adjusted to surroundings, while out of key refers to wrong musical pitch.
