syntactically

adverb
in a way related to syntax
6.2
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈtæk.tɪ.kli / (AmE), / sɪnˈtæk.tɪ.kli / (BrE)
In a way that relates to or is in accordance with syntax (the arrangement of words and phrases to create sentences).

Examples

To syntactically arrange words
Children often learn to produce syntactically complex sentences as they grow older.
That paragraph is syntactically awkward and should be rewritten for clarity.
The sentence is syntactically correct but confusing in meaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: syntactic - relating to syntax; grammatical structure
