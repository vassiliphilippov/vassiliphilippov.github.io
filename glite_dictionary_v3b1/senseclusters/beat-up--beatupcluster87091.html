beat-up

adjective
damaged or worn from use
5.2 2.19/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbit Àå åp / (AmE), / ÀàbiÀêt Àå åp / (BrE)
(of an object) damaged or worn out from rough treatment or long use.

Examples

An old beat-up truck
He drove an old beat-up truck across the country.
The book's cover was completely beat-up after years of use.
We sat on a beat-up sofa that had seen better days.

Synonyms

  • battered
    3.7 0.00/m
    battered shows heavy damage and a shabby surface from rough use, while beat-up simply means damaged or worn from rough treatment.
  • worn
    2.1 0.52/m
    worn shows signs of long use like faded fabric or thin soles, while beat-up suggests more rough treatment or dirt.
  • damaged
    1.1 0.04/m
    damaged means harmed or spoiled in any way, while beat-up implies physical wear or rough treatment to an object.
  • worn-out
    3.1 1.46/m
    worn-out is too old or broken and often unusable, while beat-up describes an object that is damaged but may still work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battered - damaged by repeated blows or hard use
  • antonym: pristine - in perfect condition; not damaged or worn
