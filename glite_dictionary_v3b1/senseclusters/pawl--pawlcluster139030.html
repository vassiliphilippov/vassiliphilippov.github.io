pawl

noun
pivoted catch in a ratchet mechanism
9.0 8.3
Pronunciation: /pɑːl/ /; / /pɔːl/ (AmE), /pɔːl/ (BrE)
A pivoted lever or catch that fits into the notches of a ratchet wheel to prevent backward motion or to allow forward movement.

Examples

A pawl lever
A broken pawl can cause the gear to slip and may lead to mechanical failure.
Every time the winch handle turns, the pawl engages another notch on the ratchet.
The bicycle's freewheel mechanism includes a pawl to prevent the wheel from spinning backward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ratchet - a mechanism consisting of a wheel with notches and a pawl
