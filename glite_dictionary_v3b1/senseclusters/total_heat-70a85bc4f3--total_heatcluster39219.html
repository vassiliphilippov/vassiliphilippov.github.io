total heat

noun
thermodynamic quantity (enthalpy)
7.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààto ät…ôl hit / (AmE), / Ààt…ô ät…ôl hiÀêt / (BrE)
(Thermodynamics) The sum of the internal energy of a system and the product of its pressure and volume, also known as enthalpy.

Examples

Total heat energy
In thermodynamics, total heat is an essential concept when analyzing energy transfer processes.
The engineer calculated the total heat needed to raise the temperature of the water.
Understanding total heat helps in designing efficient heating and cooling systems.
