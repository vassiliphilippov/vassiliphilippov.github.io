copyleft

noun
open licensing principle
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑpiˌlɛft / (AmE), / ˈkɒpiˌlɛft / (BrE)
a licensing practice or type of license that allows creative works to be freely used, modified, and shared, as long as the same rights are preserved in copies and derivatives

Examples

A copyleft license
The software was released under a copyleft license, allowing anyone to modify and share it freely.
Many collaborative projects use copyleft to protect the freedom of their content.
By choosing copyleft, the creators ensured their work would always remain open to the public.
The developer chose a copyleft license to ensure everyone could use and improve the software.
Many collaborative projects rely on copyleft to protect users' freedom to share and modify content.
By releasing his artwork under copyleft, he encouraged others to build upon his ideas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: copyright - the legal right to control the use and distribution of creative works
  • synonym: open source - software with source code that anyone can inspect, modify, and enhance
  • degree higher: public domain - works not protected by copyright and free for anyone to use
