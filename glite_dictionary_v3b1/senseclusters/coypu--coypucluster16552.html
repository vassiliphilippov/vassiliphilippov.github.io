coypu

noun
South American aquatic rodent
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.jpu / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪ.pjuː / (BrE)
a large semiaquatic rodent native to South America, resembling a beaver, often bred for its fur

Examples

A coypu rodent
A coypu can be mistaken for a beaver, but its tail is round and less flat.
Farmers are concerned because escaped coypus can harm local wetland habitats.
The coypu is sometimes seen swimming in rivers and lakes across southern South America.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: beaver - a large, semiaquatic rodent known for building dams
  • synonym: nutria - another name for the coypu, especially in fur trade
