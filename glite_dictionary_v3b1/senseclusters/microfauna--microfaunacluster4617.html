microfauna

noun
microscopic animals
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈfɔnə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈfɔːnə / (BrE)
very small animals, especially those only visible under a microscope

Examples

Soil microfauna
Scientists study microfauna in soil to understand their role in breaking down organic matter.
Aquatic microfauna are essential for maintaining healthy freshwater ecosystems.
The presence of diverse microfauna can indicate good soil quality in agricultural fields.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macrofauna - animals large enough to be seen with the naked eye
  • synonym: microorganism - a microscopic living organism, such as bacteria or protozoa
