Danzig

proper noun
port city in northern Poland (historical name for Gdańsk)
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdæn.zɪg/ (AmE), /ˈdæn.zɪg/ (BrE)
a port city in northern Poland near the mouth of the Vistula River on the Baltic Sea, known as Gdańsk in Polish; historically an important member of the Hanseatic League

Examples

Danzig port city
Historically, Danzig was a major trading center of the Hanseatic League.
In the 20th century, Danzig became a focal point of tension between Germany and Poland.
The Treaty of Versailles declared Danzig a free city after World War I.
