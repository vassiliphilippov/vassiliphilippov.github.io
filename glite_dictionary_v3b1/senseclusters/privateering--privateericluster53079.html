privateering

noun
authorized ship attacks
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪvətˈɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpraɪvətˈɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of attacking and capturing enemy ships with government authorization during wartime

Examples

Age of privateering
During the 18th century, privateering was a common way to disrupt enemy trade.
Many sailors turned to privateering as a way to profit during times of war.
The government issued letters of marque to legalize privateering against enemy nations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corsair - a privateer, especially in the Mediterranean
  • antonym: piracy - the act of attacking ships at sea without government permission
