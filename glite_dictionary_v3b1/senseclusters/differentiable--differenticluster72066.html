differentiable

adjective
having a derivative (mathematics)
4.4 7.8
Pronunciation: /dɪf.ə.ˈren.ʃə.bəl/ /; / /dɪf.ə.ˈren.tɪ.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /dɪf.ə.ˈren.ʃə.bəl/ /; / /dɪf.ə.ˈren.tɪ.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a function) having a derivative at every point in its domain or at a particular point.

Examples

Differentiable function
Every continuous and differentiable function on a closed interval is integrable.
If a function is not differentiable at a point, it may still be continuous there.
The graph of a differentiable function is always smooth, with no sharp corners.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: continuous - (mathematics) a function with no breaks or jumps in its domain
  • antonym: non-differentiable - not having a derivative at a point or on a domain
