artifact

noun
unintended digital distortion or anomaly
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.tə.fækt/ /; / /ˈɑːr.tɪ.fækt/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.tɪ.fækt/ /; / /ˈɑː.tə.fækt/ (BrE)
An unintended distortion, anomaly, or visible/heard error in a digital image, video, audio recording, or dataset, produced by processing, compression, transmission, or measurement.

Examples

Image artifact
The photo shows a clear artefact caused by heavy JPEG compression.
Video editors removed a distracting artefact that appeared after color grading the clip.
Signal interference produced an unusual artefact in the audio recording of the interview.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artifact - Alternative (American) spelling of artefact; also used for the same digital meaning.
  • synonym: glitch - A sudden, usually minor malfunction or irregularity, often producing visible or audible errors similar to artefacts.
