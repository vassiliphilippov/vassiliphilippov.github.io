race realism

noun
belief in biological differences among races
9.9
Pronunciation: / reɪs ˈriː.ə.lɪz.əm / (AmE), / reɪs ˈrɪə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief that there are inherent biological differences among human races, often associated with controversial and disputed claims about intelligence, behavior, or ability

Examples

Race realism belief
Many scholars criticize race realism as a misuse of science to justify prejudice.
Some online forums promote race realism, drawing condemnation from civil rights organizations.
The professor's comments on race realism sparked heated debate among students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarianism - the belief in human equality, especially with respect to social, political, and economic rights and privileges
  • synonym: scientific racism - the pseudoscientific belief that empirical evidence exists to support or justify racism
