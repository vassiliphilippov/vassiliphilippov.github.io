intumesce

verb
swell or bubble up (esp. from heat)
7.1 9.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tuː.ˈmes/ /; / /ɪn.tʊ.ˈmes/ (AmE), /ɪn.tjuː.ˈmes/ /; / /ɪn.tjʊ.ˈmes/ (BrE)
to swell up or expand, especially as a result of heat or absorbing fluid; sometimes used metaphorically

Examples

To intumesce with heat
New ideas seemed to intumesce in his mind as the discussion progressed.
The plastic coating will intumesce and form an insulating layer when exposed to high temperatures.
When heated, the substance began to intumesce, releasing small bubbles of gas.
After absorbing water, the tablet started to intumesce noticeably.
Some minerals intumesce when subjected to high temperatures, forming a protective barrier.
The tissue began to intumesce after prolonged exposure to heat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflate - reduce or collapse in volume, especially after swelling
  • synonym: swell - expand in size or volume, often gradually
