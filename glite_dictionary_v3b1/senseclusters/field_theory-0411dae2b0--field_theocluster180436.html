field theory

noun
theory explaining phenomena via fields in physics
3.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /fiːld ˈθiː.ə.riː/ /; / /fiːld ˈθɪə.riː/ (AmE), /fiːld ˈθɪə.riː/ /; / /fiːld ˈθiː.ə.riː/ (BrE)
A theory in physics that explains physical phenomena by describing how physical quantities are distributed in space and time as fields, and how these fields interact with matter or other fields.

Examples

Field theory in physics
Einstein's general relativity can be viewed as a field theory of gravity.
In physics class, we studied the basics of field theory to understand electromagnetic waves.
Quantum electrodynamics is a field theory that describes how light and matter interact.
