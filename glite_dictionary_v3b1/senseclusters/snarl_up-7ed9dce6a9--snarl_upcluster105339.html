snarl up

verb
to tangle or complicate
8.6
Pronunciation: / snɑːrʌp / (AmE), / snɑːlʌp / (BrE)
to cause something to become tangled, complicated, or confused, especially so that progress is hindered

Examples

To snarl up traffic
A minor accident on the highway can snarl up traffic for hours.
If too many people try to use the system at once, it could snarl up the entire process.
Negotiations were snarled up by disagreements over minor details.
