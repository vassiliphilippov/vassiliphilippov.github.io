whirl around

verb
move/spin rapidly in circles
3.7 1.39/m
Pronunciation: / w…úrl …ôÀàra änd / (AmE), / w…úÀêl …ôÀàra änd / (BrE)
to move rapidly and continuously in a circular or spinning motion, often through the air

Examples

To whirl around quickly
Dust and debris whirled around the construction site during the storm.
The dancers whirled around the stage in a blur of color and motion.
The leaves began to whirl around as the autumn wind picked up.

Synonyms

  • whirl
    2.6 2.58/m
    whirl means to move quickly in a circular or twisting way, while whirl around describes rapid continuous spinning, often through the air.
  • spin around
    2.1 3.16/m
    spin around is to revolve quickly around one's axis, while whirl around often implies continuous, faster spinning and movement through the air.
  • spin
    1.0 14/m
    spin is to turn quickly on or around an axis, while whirl around often suggests continuous circular motion through the air.
  • gyrate
    6.9 0.80/m
    gyrate means to move in circles or spirals, often in formal descriptions, while whirl around suggests rapid, vivid spinning through the air.
  • twirl
    2.7 0.54/m
    twirl is a quick, controlled spin often done by a person or object, while whirl around implies wilder, continuous motion through the air.
  • swirl
    2.7 11/m
    swirl is a smooth twisting motion seen in wind or water, while whirl around is faster, more forceful spinning through the air.
