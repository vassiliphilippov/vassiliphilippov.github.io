irrelevance

noun
lack of relevance; unrelatedness
4.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛlɪvəns / (AmE), / ɪˈrelɪvəns / (BrE)
The state or quality of not being related or pertinent to the matter at hand.

Examples

The irrelevance of facts
His comment was dismissed due to its obvious irrelevance to the discussion.
Spending too much time on details of irrelevance can distract from the main issue.
The judge warned the lawyer against introducing evidence of irrelevance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pertinence - the quality of being relevant or applicable to a particular matter
  • antonym: relevance - the quality of being closely connected or appropriate to the matter at hand
