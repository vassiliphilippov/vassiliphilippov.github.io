right atrioventricular valve

noun
heart valve between right atrium and ventricle
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt ˌætrioʊˌvɛnˈtrɪkjələr vælv / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌætrɪəʊvɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə vɑːlv / (BrE)
the valve with three cusps located between the right atrium and right ventricle of the heart, allowing blood flow from the atrium to the ventricle and preventing backflow during ventricular contraction

Examples

The right atrioventricular valve function
Damage to the right atrioventricular valve can lead to problems with blood circulation in the heart.
During heart surgery, the surgeon carefully examined the patient's right atrioventricular valve.
The right atrioventricular valve prevents blood from flowing back into the right atrium during ventricular contraction.
