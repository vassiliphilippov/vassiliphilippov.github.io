hindcast

noun
past weather simulation
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪndˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈhaɪndˌkɑːst / (BrE)
a calculation or simulation of past weather or ocean conditions using present-day models or data

Examples

A hindcast study
Researchers performed a hindcast to better understand the causes of the 2010 flood.
A detailed hindcast can reveal how ocean currents behaved during past storms.
The team used a hindcast to compare historical weather data with modern climate models.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forecast - a prediction of future weather conditions
  • synonym: simulation - an imitation of a process or system over time
