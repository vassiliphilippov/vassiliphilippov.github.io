signal

verb
communicate non-verbally with gestures or signs
3.4 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪg.nəl/ (AmE), /ˈsɪg.nəl/ (BrE)
to communicate information or intentions silently, using gestures, signs, or other non-verbal cues

Examples

To signal with a wave
He signals his agreement with a nod rather than speaking out loud.
She signaled for help by waving her hand high above her head.
The teacher signaled the class to be quiet with a finger to her lips.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gesture - a movement of part of the body to express an idea or meaning
  • synonym: indicate - to show or make known, often indirectly
