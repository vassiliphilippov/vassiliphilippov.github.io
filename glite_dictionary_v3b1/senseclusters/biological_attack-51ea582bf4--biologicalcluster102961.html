biological attack

noun
an attack using biological agents
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl əˈtæk / (AmE), / ˌbʌɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl əˈtak / (BrE)
an attack that uses bacteria, viruses, or toxins as weapons to harm or kill people, animals, or crops

Examples

A biological attack event

Experts warned that a biological attack could pose a major threat to urban populations.
Military forces are now trained to respond quickly to any potential biological attack.

  • antonym: chemical attack - an attack using chemical agents as weapons
