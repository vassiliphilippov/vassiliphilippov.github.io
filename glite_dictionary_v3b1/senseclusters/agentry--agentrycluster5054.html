agentry

noun
capacity to act independently
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒəntri / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒəntri / (BrE)
the capacity or power of individuals or groups to act independently and make their own choices

Examples

Agentry in decision-making
The new policy increased the agentry of local organizations in decision-making processes.
Teachers can foster student agentry by encouraging independent thinking and responsibility.
Social movements often arise when people feel their agentry is being limited or ignored.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agency - the ability to act and make decisions independently
  • antonym: powerlessness - the lack of ability to act or influence events
