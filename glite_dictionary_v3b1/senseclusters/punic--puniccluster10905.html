punic

adjective
treacherous or deceitful (historical)
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpjuÀê.n…™k / (AmE), / ÀàpjuÀê.n…™k / (BrE)
(of behaviour or character) treacherous or faithless, as stereotypically attributed to the Carthaginians by the Romans; perfidious.

Examples

Punic behaviour
Historians often refer to the Romans' view of Carthaginians as punic and untrustworthy.
The general accused the diplomat of making a punic promise that was soon broken.
Their punic conduct during the negotiations became a cautionary tale for generations.
