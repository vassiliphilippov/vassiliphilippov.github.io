deworming

noun
removal of worms
3.3
Pronunciation: / diˈwɜrmɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈwɜːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or campaign of removing parasitic worms from the body of people or animals, usually by giving medicine

Examples

Child deworming program
Regular deworming is important for children's health in some regions.
The veterinarian recommended deworming the puppy every three months to keep it healthy.
Many schools organize deworming programs to prevent the spread of intestinal parasites among students.
The government launched a deworming program to improve children's health in rural areas.
Regular deworming of pets is important to prevent the spread of parasites.
After the annual deworming campaign, school attendance rates increased noticeably.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthelmintic - a drug used to expel or destroy parasitic worms
  • antonym: infection - the invasion of the body by harmful organisms such as bacteria or parasites
  • antonym: infestation - the state of being invaded by parasites or pests
