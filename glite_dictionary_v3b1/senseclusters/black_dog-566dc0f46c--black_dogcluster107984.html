black dog

noun
mythical dog; metaphor for depression
7.3
Pronunciation: / blæk dɔɡ / (AmE), / blæk dɒɡ / (BrE)
A mythical creature in folklore, often seen as a large, spectral dog; also used metaphorically to refer to a state of depression.

Examples

A black dog spirit
For centuries, stories of a spectral black dog have circulated in English folklore.
He described his struggle with the black dog after losing his job.
The legend told of a terrifying black dog that haunted lonely travelers at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depression - a mental state of severe despondency and dejection
  • synonym: melancholy - a deep, persistent sadness
