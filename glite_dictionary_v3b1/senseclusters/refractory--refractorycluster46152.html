refractory

adjective
stubbornly resistant to authority or control
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræk.tɚ.i / (AmE), / rɪˈfræk.tər.i / (BrE)
resisting authority or control; stubbornly uncooperative or difficult to manage

Examples

Refractory child
Doctors had trouble treating the patient's refractory infection, which did not respond to standard medications.
His refractory attitude made teamwork difficult for the rest of the group.
The refractory student refused to follow classroom rules despite repeated warnings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests; submissive to authority
  • synonym: unruly - difficult to control; disorderly or disobedient
