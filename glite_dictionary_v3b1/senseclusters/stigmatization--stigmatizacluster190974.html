stigmatization

noun
the act of marking as disgraceful
Pronunciation: / stɪɡmətəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌstɪɡməˌtaɪzəˈʃeɪʃən / (AmE), / stɪɡmətʌɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌstɪɡmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of treating someone or something as disgraceful or socially unacceptable.

Examples

Social stigmatization
Media coverage contributed to the stigmatization of certain groups during the outbreak.
Public stigmatization can make it difficult for former prisoners to reintegrate into society.
The stigmatization of people with mental illness often prevents them from seeking help.
Campaigns to reduce the stigmatisation of addiction are vital for public health.
Social stigmatisation can lead to exclusion and discrimination in the workplace.
The stigmatisation of mental illness often prevents people from seeking the help they need.

  • synonym: stigma - a mark of shame or social disapproval
  • synonym: stigmatize - to mark someone as disgraceful, to shame
