uncommissioned

adjective
not officially appointed
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkəˈmɪʃənd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkəˈmɪʃənd / (BrE)
not officially appointed, authorized, or given a formal commission for a particular role or task

Examples

Uncommissioned officer
He served as an uncommissioned officer during the crisis, taking charge without formal authority.
The uncommissioned artwork was never displayed in the official gallery.
As an uncommissioned volunteer, she helped organize the event without any official title.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commissioned - officially appointed or given authority for a role
  • synonym: unofficial - not officially recognized or approved
