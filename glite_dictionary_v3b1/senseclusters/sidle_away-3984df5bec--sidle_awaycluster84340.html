sidle away

verb
leave quietly or sneakily
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdəl əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdəl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move away from a place or person quietly and unobtrusively, often in a furtive or cautious manner

Examples

To sidle away quietly
As soon as the argument started, I saw him quietly sidling away towards the exit.
She felt embarrassed and wanted to sidle away from the crowded room unnoticed.
When the teacher turned her back, Tom tried to sidle away from the group discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slip away - to leave quietly and unnoticed
  • synonym: sneak away - to leave stealthily or furtively
