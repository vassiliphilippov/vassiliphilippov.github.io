polarimeter

noun
instrument measuring rotation of polarized light
5.5 7.6
Pronunciation: /poʊ.lə.ˈrɪm.ɪ.tər/ (AmE), /pəʊ.lə.ˈrɪm.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an optical instrument used to measure the rotation of the plane of polarized light, especially in chemical analysis

Examples

A polarimeter instrument
A polarimeter measures the degree to which a substance rotates polarized light.
The chemist used a polarimeter to determine the concentration of sugar in the solution.
This laboratory is equipped with a digital polarimeter for analyzing optical activity.
