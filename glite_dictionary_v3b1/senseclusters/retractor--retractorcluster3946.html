retractor

noun
surgical instrument for holding back tissue
Pronunciation: / ɹɪˈtræktər / (AmE), / ɹɪˈtraktə / (BrE)
A surgical instrument used to hold back the edges of an incision or wound during an operation.

Examples

Surgical retractor tool
After making the incision, the nurse carefully positioned the retractor to hold the tissue aside.
Different types of retractors are used depending on the area being operated on.
The surgeon asked for a retractor to improve visibility during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forceps - a surgical instrument for grasping or holding objects
