monolithically

adverb
as a single unit
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈlɪθɪkli /; / ˌmɑnəˈlɪθɪkli / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈlɪθɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is large, unified, and not divided into parts; acting or organized as a single whole

Examples

To work monolithically
The software was designed monolithically, making updates difficult and time-consuming.
Because the company operated monolithically, it struggled to adapt to rapid market changes.
In the past, many systems were built monolithically instead of using flexible, modular approaches.
The company was organized monolithically, with all decisions made by a single leader.
The ancient temple was constructed monolithically from a single massive stone.
Some critics argue that the culture is viewed too monolithically, ignoring its diversity.
The organization was managed monolithically, leaving little space for individual ideas.
Policies were enforced monolithically across all departments, regardless of their unique needs.
The system operated monolithically, making it difficult to introduce any changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexibly - in a way that is adaptable or able to change
  • antonym: fragmentedly - in a way that is broken into pieces or parts
  • antonym: modularly - in a way that is divided into separate, independent parts
  • synonym: rigidly - in a strict or inflexible way
  • synonym: uniformly - in a way that is the same throughout
