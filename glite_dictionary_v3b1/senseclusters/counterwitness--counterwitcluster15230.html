counterwitness

noun
opposing witness
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər ˈwɪt.nəs/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.tər ˈwɪt.nɪs/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə ˈwɪt.nəs/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.tə ˈwɪt.nɪs/ (BrE)
a person or statement that provides evidence or testimony opposing or contradicting another person's account or claim

Examples

A counterwitness in court
The defense attorney called a counterwitness to dispute the main witness's version of events.
A strong counterwitness can sometimes change the outcome of a trial.
The judge listened carefully to the counterwitness before making a decision.
The activist became a counterwitness by sharing evidence that challenged the official report.
During the debate, a counterwitness presented facts that contradicted the main speaker's story.
Historians often act as counterwitnesses when they uncover new information about past events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or challenges another
  • antonym: supporter - a person who agrees with or defends another's claim
  • synonym: testimony - a formal written or spoken statement given in a court of law
  • antonym: witness - a person who gives evidence in a court of law
