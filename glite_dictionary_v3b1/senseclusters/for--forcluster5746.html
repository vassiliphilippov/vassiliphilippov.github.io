for

preposition
comparison context
1.0
Pronunciation: / fɔr / (AmE), / fɔː / (BrE)
used to indicate a comparison that makes something seem surprising or appropriate in a particular situation.

Examples

Tall for his age
He is very tall for his age, which surprises his teachers.
The meal was quite good for the price we paid at the restaurant.
She did an excellent job for someone with so little experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: considering - taking into account a particular fact or situation
