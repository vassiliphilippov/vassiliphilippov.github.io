root

noun
basic form from which words are derived
Pronunciation: / rut / (AmE), / ruːt / (BrE)
the base form of a word from which other words are formed (in linguistics)

Examples

Word root
Linguists often trace a family of words back to a single root.
Many Indo-European languages share a common root for basic concepts like 'mother' or 'water'.
The root of the word 'generation' is the Latin 'gen-'.
In the word 'unhappiness', the root is 'happy'.
Linguists often analyze the root to understand the origin of a word.
Many English words share the same root, giving them similar meanings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stem - the main part of a word, to which affixes may be added
