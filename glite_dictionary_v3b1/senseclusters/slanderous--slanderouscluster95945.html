slanderous

adjective
false and damaging to reputation
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈslændɚəs / (AmE), / ˈslɑːndərəs / (BrE)
(of a statement) false and damaging to a person's reputation; intending to discredit or malign.

Examples

Slanderous rumors
Spreading slanderous rumors about your colleagues can lead to serious consequences.
The company denied all the slanderous allegations made by its competitors.
The politician filed a lawsuit over the slanderous remarks published in the newspaper.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complimentary - expressing praise or admiration
  • synonym: defamatory - intended to harm someone's reputation
  • synonym: libelous - (of a statement) containing or constituting a written defamation
