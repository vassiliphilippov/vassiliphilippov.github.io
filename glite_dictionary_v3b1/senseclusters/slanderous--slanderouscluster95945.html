Synonyms

  • calumniatory
    9.9 0.00/m
    calumniatory is a formal word for false statements meant to harm someone's reputation, while slanderous is more common and direct.
  • calumnious
    9.9 0.02/m
    calumnious describes false, hurtful accusations in formal or legal writing, while slanderous is used more often in everyday speech.
  • defamatory
    7.9 0.76/m
    defamatory describes writing or speech that harms someone's reputation and often appears in law, while slanderous stresses spoken or direct attacks.
  • smeary
    8.6 0.00/m
    smeary suggests unfair, dirty allegations or a smear campaign in public, while slanderous simply means false statements that harm reputation.
