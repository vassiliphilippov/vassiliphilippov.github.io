equal

verb
make the same in amount or degree
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈiːkwəl / (AmE), / ˈiːkwəl / (BrE)
to be or make the same in value, amount, quality, or ability as something else

Examples

To equal the record
The new law aims to equal salaries for men and women.
The school tried to equal the number of students in each class.
They will equal last year's record if they win this game.
His salary does not equal the amount he used to earn at his previous job.
No one has ever equaled her record in the long jump.
Two plus two equals four.
Even in her rookie season, her speed equaled that of the team’s best runner.
Few authors have ever equaled Shakespeare's mastery of language.
The new model just doesn't equal last year's product in performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: differ - to be unlike or not equal to something
  • synonym: equalize - to make equal or uniform
  • synonym: match - to make equal or cause to correspond
  • antonym: surpass - to be better than; to exceed in quality or ability
