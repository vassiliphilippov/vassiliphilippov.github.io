job action

noun
temporary workers' protest action
6.9
Pronunciation: / dʒɑb ˈækʃən / (AmE), / dʒɒb ˈækʃən / (BrE)
a temporary act by workers to protest against management or to demand better conditions, sometimes short of a full strike

Examples

A job action protest
Because of the job action, several public services were temporarily disrupted.
Teachers announced a job action that will include a work slowdown next week.
The union is planning a job action if negotiations with management fail.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: strike - a refusal to work organized by employees as a form of protest
