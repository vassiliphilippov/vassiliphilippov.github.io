encephalogram

noun
X-ray of the brain using contrast gas
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /en.sef.ə.ˈlɑː.ɡræm/ /; / /en.sef.ə.ˈloʊ.ɡræm/ (AmE), /en.sef.ə.ˈlɒ.ɡræm/ /; / /en.sef.ə.ˈləʊ.ɡræm/ (BrE)
an X-ray image of the brain made by replacing spinal fluid with a gas to enhance contrast

Examples

X-ray encephalogram
Advancements in brain imaging have largely replaced the traditional encephalogram technique.
An encephalogram was performed after the patient experienced repeated seizures.
The neurologist studied the patient's encephalogram to detect signs of abnormal brain activity.

Related Terms
