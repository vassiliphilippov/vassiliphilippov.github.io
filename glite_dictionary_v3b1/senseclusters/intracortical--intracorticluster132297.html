intracortical

adjective
within the cortex
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈkɔrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈkɔːtɪkəl / (BrE)
located or occurring within the cortex of an organ, especially the brain

Examples

Intracortical brain activity
The scientists measured intracortical activity to understand how neurons communicate within the brain.
Damage to intracortical pathways can affect sensory processing in patients.
Advanced imaging techniques reveal intracortical changes after a stroke.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subcortical - located below the cortex of an organ
