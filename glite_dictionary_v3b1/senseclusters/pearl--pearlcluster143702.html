pearl

noun
a pale, slightly lustrous shade of white
1.0 1.59/m
Pronunciation: / p…ùl / (AmE), / p…úÀêl / (BrE)
a pale shade of white resembling the color of a pearl or bleached bones

Examples

Pearl white color
She wore a dress in pearl that complimented her skin tone beautifully.
The car's exterior gleamed in a pearl finish under the afternoon sun.
The decorator chose pearl for the living room walls to give them a soft, elegant look.

Synonyms

  • bone
    3.8 4.89/m
    bone is a dull, bleached white often used for paint and fabric, while pearl is paler and has a soft, warm sheen.
  • ivory
    3.5 1.26/m
    ivory is a creamy, slightly yellow white seen on tusks and paper, while pearl is cooler and often has a faint shine.
  • alabaster
    7.6 0.26/m
    alabaster is an almost pure, translucent white used for marble and skin, while pearl is a softer, warmer pale white.
  • pearly
    3.2 1.41/m
    pearly describes a shiny, iridescent white surface, while pearl names the pale color itself and the object giving the sheen.
