comparativist

noun
student of comparisons
9.5
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpærətɪvɪst / (AmE), / kəmˈpærətɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or practices the comparison of different subjects, especially in fields like literature or linguistics

Examples

A comparativist scholar
The comparativist analyzed similarities between ancient Greek and Indian epics.
As a comparativist, she often travels to study different cultures' literature.
Many universities offer courses for aspiring comparativists interested in cross-cultural studies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: specialist - a person who focuses on a particular subject or area
