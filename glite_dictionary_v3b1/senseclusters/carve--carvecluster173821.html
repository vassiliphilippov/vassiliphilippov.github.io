carve

verb
form by cutting into shape
2.1 19/m
Pronunciation: / k…ërv / (AmE), / k…ëÀêv / (BrE)
to shape, form, or engrave something by cutting into a material, such as wood, stone, or other surfaces

Examples

To carve a pumpkin
Each student tried to carve a pumpkin with a different face for the festival.
He spent hours trying to carve a statue that resembled an ancient warrior.
The artist will carve a delicate bird from a single block of wood.
He decided to carve their initials into the wet cement.
Some people like to carve their names on tree trunks to leave a mark.
The artist skillfully carved a lion's head from the block of marble.

Synonyms

  • engrave
    5.2 3.16/m
    engrave means cutting fine letters or designs into a hard surface, while carve means shaping or cutting material more generally.
  • grave
    7.7 0.04/m
    grave means cutting or etching letters and patterns into hard surfaces in older or formal writing, while carve is more general shaping.
  • chisel
    5.1 1.56/m
    chisel means cutting or shaping wood or stone with a chisel tool, while carve can mean shaping by hand or many tools.
  • sculpt
    4.3 4.87/m
    sculpt means making a work of art by shaping or carving materials, while carve often describes the act of cutting into material.
  • etch
    5.5 1.75/m
    etch means cutting or using acid to make fine lines or patterns on a surface, while carve is broader and may form shapes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engrave - to cut or etch a design onto a hard surface
  • synonym: sculpt - to shape or form something by carving or molding material
