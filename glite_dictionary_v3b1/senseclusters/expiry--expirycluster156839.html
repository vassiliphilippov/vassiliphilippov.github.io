expiry

noun
end of validity period
3.6 2.5
Pronunciation: /eks.ˈpaɪ.riː/ /; / /ɪks.ˈpaɪ.riː/ (AmE), /ɪks.ˈpaɪ.ə.riː/ /; / /eks.ˈpaɪ.ə.riː/ (BrE)
the end of a period after which something (such as a document, contract, or product) is no longer valid, effective, or safe to use

Examples

The expiry date
Check the expiry date on your passport before booking your trip.
His insurance policy is up for expiry next month.
The expiry of the lease means we must find a new office soon.
Always check the expiry date on your medicine before taking it.
The contract reached its expiry and needed to be renegotiated.
After the expiry of her visa, she had to leave the country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expiration - the ending of the period of validity of something
  • antonym: renewal - the act of making something valid again after it has expired
