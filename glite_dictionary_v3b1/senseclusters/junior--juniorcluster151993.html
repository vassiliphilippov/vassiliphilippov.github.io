junior

noun
address for a younger or annoying male
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːniər / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːniə / (BrE)
a younger or less experienced person, or used as a mildly patronizing term of address for such a person

Examples

Listen, junior
Calm down, junior, and let the adults handle this.
I wouldn't try that again if I were you, junior.
Listen, junior, you need to watch your tone when you speak to me.
Although he is only my junior by three months, he looks much younger.
At family gatherings, Anna often feels overlooked as the junior to her older brother.
She took on a mentoring role, guiding her junior at work through the project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: senior - the elder of two people for comparison of age
