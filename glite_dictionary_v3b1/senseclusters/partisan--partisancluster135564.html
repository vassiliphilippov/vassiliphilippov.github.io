partisan

adjective
strong supporter of a cause or party
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːr.tə.zən / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.zæn / (BrE)
strongly supporting a particular cause, party, or group, often without considering other views or being fair

Examples

Partisan politics
It is important for judges to remain impartial and not let partisan opinions influence their decisions.
She wrote a partisan article in support of her political party.
The debate became heated and partisan as both sides refused to compromise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - unfairly prejudiced for or against something
  • antonym: neutral - not supporting any side in a conflict or disagreement
