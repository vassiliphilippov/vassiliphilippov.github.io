occupy

verb
to live in (a place)
3.0 8.26/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêkj…ôÀåpa…™ / (AmE), / Àà…íkj äÀåpa…™ / (BrE)
to live in or have one's home in a particular place

Examples

To occupy a house
After the renovation, the family will occupy the newly expanded apartment.
Several graduate students occupy the upper floor of the old house.
She was delighted to occupy a small cottage by the sea for the summer.

Synonyms

  • live
    1.0 331/m
    live is the everyday verb for having a home in a place, while occupy sounds more formal and can mean taking space.
  • reside
    3.0 11/m
    reside is a more formal word used in legal or official talk, while occupy is neutral and common in ordinary contexts.
  • inhabit
    3.5 7.86/m
    inhabit often describes animals or people living in an area long term, while occupy focuses on having or using a place.
  • dwell
    3.2 2.78/m
    dwell sounds old-fashioned and appears in books, while occupy is modern and commonly used in speech or reports.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inhabit - to live in a place
  • antonym: vacate - to leave a place that was previously occupied
