cyanide group

noun
the monovalent group -CN in chemistry
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪəˌnaɪd ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪənaɪd ɡruːp / (BrE)
the monovalent chemical group -CN, consisting of a carbon atom triple-bonded to a nitrogen atom, found in various compounds

Examples

A cyanide group compound
During synthesis, a cyanide group can be introduced to alter the molecule's properties.
The cyanide group is often found in organic compounds called nitriles.
The presence of a cyanide group is responsible for the compound's toxicity.
