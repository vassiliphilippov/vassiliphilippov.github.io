glut

verb
supply with an excess of
6.0 5.6
Pronunciation: /glʌt/ (AmE), /glʌt/ (BrE)
to supply or fill something with an excessive amount, especially so that it becomes undesirable or worthless

Examples

To glut the market
A sudden drop in demand can glut the market with unsold inventory.
Supermarkets often glut their shelves with seasonal products after the holidays.
They warned that more production could glut the city with low-quality apartments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flood - to overwhelm or fill with an excessive amount (as in flood the market)
