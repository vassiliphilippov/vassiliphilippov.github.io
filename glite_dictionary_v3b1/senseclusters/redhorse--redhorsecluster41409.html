redhorse

noun
North American fish with reddish fins
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌhɔrs / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌhɔːs / (BrE)
A North American freshwater fish of the sucker family, distinguished by its reddish fins.

Examples

The redhorse fish
Anglers prize the redhorse for its impressive size and spirited fight.
Researchers are studying the habitat needs of the redhorse to help conserve the species.
The redhorse is often found in clean streams and rivers throughout the Midwest.
