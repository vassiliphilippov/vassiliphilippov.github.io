bone-chilling

adjective
extremely cold or frightening
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊnˌtʃɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊnˌtʃɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
Extremely cold or causing intense fear or dread.

Examples

A bone-chilling wind
A bone-chilling wind swept across the lake, making it hard to stay outside.
His bone-chilling scream echoed through the empty house at midnight.
The movie's bone-chilling ending left the audience in stunned silence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chilling - causing fear or coldness
  • antonym: cozy - warm and comfortable, opposite of cold
  • synonym: terrifying - causing extreme fear
