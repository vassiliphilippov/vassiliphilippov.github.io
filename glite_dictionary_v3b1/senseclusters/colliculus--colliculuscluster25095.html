colliculus

noun
anatomical prominence
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪkjələs / (AmE), / kɒˈlɪkjʊləs / (BrE)
a small rounded elevation or prominence on the surface of an organ or tissue, especially in anatomy.

Examples

Urethral colliculus
The surgeon identified a colliculus during the examination of the patient's urethra.
A prominent colliculus can be seen on the dorsal surface of the midbrain in anatomical diagrams.
Damage to the superior colliculus may affect visual processing in the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depression - a sunken or hollow area on a surface
  • synonym: eminence - a raised area or projection on a body part
