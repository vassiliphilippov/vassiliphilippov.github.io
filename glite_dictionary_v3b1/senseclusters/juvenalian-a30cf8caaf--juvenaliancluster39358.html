Juvenalian

adjective
bitter satire style
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒuːvəˈneɪliən / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːvəˈneɪliən / (BrE)
relating to a bitter, scornful, and harsh style of satire, especially as used by the Roman satirist Juvenal

Examples

Juvenalian satire
The comedian's Juvenalian humor targeted political leaders with sharp criticism.
Her novel is known for its Juvenalian tone, exposing the darker sides of society.
Critics described the film's Juvenalian approach as both powerful and unsettling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Horatian - relating to a gentle, playful style of satire
  • synonym: satirical - using humor, irony, or ridicule to criticize
