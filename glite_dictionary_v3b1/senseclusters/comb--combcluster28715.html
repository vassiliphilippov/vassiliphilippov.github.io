comb

noun
cilia-based swimming plate in ctenophores
Pronunciation: / koʊm / (AmE), / kəʊm / (BrE)
A row of cilia forming a plate-like structure used for locomotion in ctenophores (marine invertebrates).

Examples

Ctenophore comb
Ctenophores move through the water using eight rows of combs that beat in coordinated waves.
Scientists examined the structure of each comb under a microscope to learn more about ctenophore locomotion.
The iridescent appearance of a ctenophore is often caused by light reflecting off its moving comb rows.
