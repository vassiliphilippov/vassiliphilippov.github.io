Cottrell precipitator

noun
device for dust removal by electrostatic precipitation
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈtrɛl prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪtər / (AmE), / kəˈtrɛl prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪtə / (BrE)
an apparatus that removes dust particles from gases using electrostatic precipitation, especially in industrial processes

Examples

A Cottrell precipitator device
A Cottrell precipitator relies on electrostatic forces to collect fine particulate matter from industrial exhaust.
Many power plants use a Cottrell precipitator to control air pollution from burning coal.
The installation of a Cottrell precipitator has significantly improved the factory's emission standards.
