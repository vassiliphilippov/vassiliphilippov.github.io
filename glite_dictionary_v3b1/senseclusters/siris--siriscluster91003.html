siris

noun
a large Old World tree with notable seed pods
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɪərɪs / (BrE)
A large, spreading tree native to the Old World, with large leaves, round clusters of greenish-yellow flowers, and long seed pods that rattle in the wind.

Examples

A siris tree
Many birds nest in the siris because of its broad canopy and protective foliage.
The siris tree is valued for its shade and ornamental flowers in tropical gardens.
We could hear the siris pods rattling in the afternoon breeze as we walked by.
