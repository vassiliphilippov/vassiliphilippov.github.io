sickling

noun
cutting with a sickle
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪklɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪŋ / (BrE)
the action or process of cutting plants, especially grain, with a sickle during harvest

Examples

Sickling the wheat
The workers spent the morning sickling the tall wheat in the golden fields.
Traditional sickling requires skill and careful handling of the sharp blade.
After the rain stopped, the villagers resumed sickling to finish the harvest before sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harvesting - the process of gathering mature crops from the fields
  • synonym: reaping - the act of cutting and gathering crops at harvest
