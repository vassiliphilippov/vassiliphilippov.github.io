sold-out

adjective

(of tickets, shows, events, etc.) having all available tickets or items sold; with no more available for sale.

Examples

Sold-out concert
Every copy of the new book was sold-out on the first day of release.
The concert was completely sold-out within hours of ticket sales opening.
We couldn't get seats because the theater was sold-out for the entire weekend.

Synonyms

  • fully booked
    2.4 0.11/m
    fully booked means all places or tickets are already reserved in advance, while sold-out describes events or shows with no tickets left.
  • out of stock
    1.8 0.43/m
    out of stock is used for shop items when none remain to sell, while sold-out mainly describes tickets or event seats.
  • sold
    1.0 0.00/m
    sold means an item has been bought by someone, while sold-out shows that all tickets or stock are gone.
