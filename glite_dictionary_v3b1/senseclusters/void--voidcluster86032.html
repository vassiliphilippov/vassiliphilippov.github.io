void

verb
declare invalid or null
Pronunciation: / vɔɪd / (AmE), / vɔɪd / (BrE)
to declare something, especially a legal document or agreement, as invalid or without legal force

Examples

To void a check
If you sign under duress, the agreement may be voided later in court.
The judge decided to void the contract based on new evidence.
The law allows the board to void any vote that does not comply with regulations.
If the signature is missing, the agreement may be voided by the court.
The company decided to void the contract after discovering a breach of terms.
You should check if any actions could void your warranty before making changes to the product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annul - to declare or make legally invalid
  • synonym: invalidate - to make something not valid
  • antonym: uphold - to support or confirm the validity of something
  • antonym: validate - make something legally valid
