miscasting

noun
unsuitable role assignment
6.5
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈkæs.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈkɑː.stɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of choosing someone unsuitable for a particular role, job, or position, especially in acting or performance

Examples

Lead role miscasting
The play was criticized for the miscasting of the lead actor, who did not fit the role.
Many fans agreed that the miscasting ruined what could have been a great movie.
Directors try to avoid miscasting by carefully considering each actor's strengths.

Related Terms

  • antonym: casting - the process of choosing actors for roles
  • synonym: misplacement - putting someone or something in the wrong place or position
