reorientate

verb
orient or adjust again
7.2 5.1
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈɔːr.ɪ.ən.teɪt/ /; / /riː.ə.ˈrɪn.teɪt/ (AmE), /riː.ˈɔːr.ɪ.ən.teɪt/ /; / /riː.ə.ˈrɪn.teɪt/ (BrE)
to adjust or align (something or oneself) again, especially after becoming disoriented; to change direction or focus.

Examples

To reorientate yourself
After moving to a new country, it took her some time to reorientate herself to the unfamiliar surroundings.
He paused to reorientate his map and make sure they were heading in the right direction.
The company had to reorientate its business strategy in response to market changes.
