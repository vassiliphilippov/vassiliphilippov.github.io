reorientate

verb
orient or adjust again
6.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / riÀà…îri…ônÀåte…™t / (AmE), / riÀà…îÀêri…ônte…™t / (BrE)
to adjust or align (something or oneself) again, especially after becoming disoriented; to change direction or focus.

Examples

To reorientate yourself
After moving to a new country, it took her some time to reorientate herself to the unfamiliar surroundings.
He paused to reorientate his map and make sure they were heading in the right direction.
The company had to reorientate its business strategy in response to market changes.

Synonyms

  • reorient
    5.6 1.21/m
    reorient means change direction or focus in a clear, simple way, while reorientate often appears as a longer, slightly more formal variant.
  • realign
    4.9 1.43/m
    realign moves parts into a correct physical position, while reorientate can mean changing direction or the focus of a person or object.
  • refocus
    3.9 1.31/m
    refocus means move attention or effort back to something, while reorientate means changing direction or adjusting attention and position more broadly.
  • shift focus
    3.8 1.67/m
    shift focus means turn attention from one thing to another, while reorientate means change direction or reposition oneself or plans.
