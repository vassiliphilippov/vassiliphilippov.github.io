cassiterite

noun
main ore of tin
Pronunciation: / kəˈsɪtəˌraɪt / (AmE), / kəˈsɪtərʌɪt / (BrE)
A hard, heavy, dark mineral that is the main ore of tin.

Examples

Dark cassiterite mineral
Scientists analyzed the cassiterite samples under a microscope.
The extraction of tin begins with the mining of cassiterite.
The mine was rich in cassiterite, providing a steady supply of tin.
