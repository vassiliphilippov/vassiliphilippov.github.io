Asclepian

noun
ancient Greek healer
Pronunciation: / əˈskliːpiən / (AmE), / əˈskliːpiən / (BrE)
a priest, attendant, or follower of Asclepius, especially one involved in healing rituals or medical practice in ancient Greece

Examples

An Asclepian healer
The villagers brought their sick to the Asclepian for healing rituals at the temple.
An Asclepian would often oversee ceremonies dedicated to Asclepius in ancient Greece.
Many believed that an Asclepian could cure illnesses through sacred rites and herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: healer - a person who cures or treats illness
  • synonym: physician - a medical doctor
