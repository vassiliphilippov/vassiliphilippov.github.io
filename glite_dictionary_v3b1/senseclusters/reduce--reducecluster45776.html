reduce

verb
to weaken a speech sound by destressing
1.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈduːs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈdjuːs/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈdjuːs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈdʒuːs/ (BrE)
(in phonetics) To pronounce a vowel or syllable with less stress, making the sound weaker or shorter, often resulting in a schwa or similar sound.

Examples

To reduce a vowel
Many unstressed vowels are reduced in rapid speech, making them harder to distinguish.
The vowel in the second syllable of 'sofa' is typically reduced to a schwa sound in English.
When pronouncing 'photograph', the second 'o' is often reduced and sounds less clear.

  • antonym: strengthen - to pronounce with more force or stress
