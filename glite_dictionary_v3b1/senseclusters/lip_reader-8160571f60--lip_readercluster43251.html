lip reader

noun
software/device that reads lips
4.3
Pronunciation: /lɪp ˈriː.dər/ (AmE), /lɪp ˈriː.də/ (BrE)
(computing) a software program or device that analyses a speaker's lip movements in video and converts them into text or synthesized speech.

Examples

AI lip reader
Researchers developed a lip reader that transcribes silent CCTV footage into searchable text.
The courtroom used a real-time lip reader to help transcribe videos for the jury.
She trained a neural network to become an accurate lip reader for silent interview clips.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automatic lipreading - computer-based techniques for decoding speech from silent video
  • synonym: visual speech recognition - automatic recognition of speech from visual cues such as lip movements
