curtaining

noun
streaks in coatings
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜːrtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːtənɪŋ / (BrE)
a pattern of streaks or bands appearing in thin films or coatings, usually caused by uneven deposition or preparation

Examples

Curtaining in coatings
The technician noticed curtaining on the surface of the thin film under the microscope.
Improper coating techniques can result in visible curtaining that affects the material's performance.
To avoid curtaining, the sample must be prepared with consistent thickness and care.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banding - the appearance of bands or stripes in a material
  • antonym: uniformity - the quality of being even or consistent throughout
