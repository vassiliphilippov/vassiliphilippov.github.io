migrant shrike

noun
a migratory North American shrike
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪɡrənt ʃraɪk / (AmE), / ˈmaɪɡrənt ʃraɪk / (BrE)
a shrike of central North America that migrates south in winter, especially to Texas and the southern Mississippi valley

Examples

A migrant shrike bird
A birdwatcher spotted a migrant shrike near the banks of the Mississippi River.
Every autumn, the migrant shrike leaves central North America for warmer climates.
The migrant shrike is often seen in grasslands during its spring migration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shrike - any bird of the family Laniidae, known for impaling prey on thorns
