ribonucleotide

noun
RNA building block
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌraɪboʊˈnuːkliəˌtaɪd / (AmE), / ˌraɪbəʊˈnjuːkliətaɪd / (BrE)
a molecule made of ribose sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base; the basic unit of RNA and sometimes involved in cellular energy transfer

Examples

A ribonucleotide molecule
A single ribonucleotide contains a ribose sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base.
The sequence of ribonucleotides in RNA determines the genetic code for protein synthesis.
Scientists study how each ribonucleotide contributes to the structure of RNA molecules.
ATP is a common ribonucleotide that provides energy for many cellular processes.
The synthesis of RNA requires each ribonucleotide to be added in a specific sequence.
Scientists measured the concentration of ribonucleotides in the cell to study metabolic activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deoxyribonucleotide - a nucleotide containing deoxyribose, found in DNA
  • synonym: nucleotide - a basic unit of nucleic acids, consisting of a sugar, phosphate, and base
