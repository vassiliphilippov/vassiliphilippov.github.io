flatland

noun
two-dimensional world
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈflætˌlænd / (BrE)
a two-dimensional world or plane, especially as described in literature or mathematics

Examples

Mathematical flatland
The concept of flatland helps students understand dimensions in mathematics.
In flatland, creatures can only move forward, backward, left, or right, but not up or down.
The novel 'Flatland' uses flatland to explore ideas about perception and reality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plane - a flat, two-dimensional surface in geometry
  • antonym: space - a three-dimensional area or expanse
