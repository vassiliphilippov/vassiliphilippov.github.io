glucosamine

noun
amino sugar; component of polysaccharides
7.6
Pronunciation: / ɡluˈkoʊsəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ɡluˈkəʊsəmiːn / (BrE)
an amino sugar derived from glucose, found as a component of many polysaccharides and used in dietary supplements to support joint health

Examples

Glucosamine dietary supplement
Cartilage in the body contains glucosamine as a key structural component.
Many people take glucosamine supplements to help alleviate joint pain.
Research on the effectiveness of glucosamine for osteoarthritis is ongoing.
