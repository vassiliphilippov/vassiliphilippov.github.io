spiny-finned fish

noun
fish with sharp, rigid fin rays
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪni fɪnd fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈspaɪni fɪnd fɪʃ / (BrE)
A teleost fish whose fins are supported by sharp, inflexible spines rather than soft rays; members of the group Acanthopterygii.

Examples

A spiny-finned fish
Biologists study the adaptability of spiny-finned fish in different marine environments.
Many aquarium enthusiasts prefer spiny-finned fish for their unique appearance.
The lionfish is an example of a spiny-finned fish found in tropical waters.

Related Terms
