p.m.

adverb
between noon and midnight
1.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpiÀê Àà…õm / (AmE), / ÀåpiÀê Ààem / (BrE)
used to indicate the time between noon and midnight in the 12-hour clock system.

Examples

Arrives at 8:00 p.m.
Our flight departs at 5:30 p.m. sharp.
She usually starts her evening shift at 6 p.m..
The store closes at 10 p.m. every night.

Synonyms

  • pm
    1.0 0.00/m
    pm is the same short form without dots often used in casual writing and clocks, while p.m. keeps dots and suits formal text.
  • post meridiem
    5.3 0.01/m
    post meridiem is the full Latin phrase used in formal time notation, while p.m. is the common abbreviated form.

Related Terms

  • antonym: a.m. - used to indicate the time between midnight and noon
