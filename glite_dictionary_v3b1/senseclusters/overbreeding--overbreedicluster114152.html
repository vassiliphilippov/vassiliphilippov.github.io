overbreeding

noun
excessive animal breeding
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of breeding animals too much or too often, often causing health problems or other negative effects

Examples

Overbreeding animals
The health issues in some dog breeds are a direct result of overbreeding for appearance.
Animal welfare groups warn against overbreeding because it can lead to genetic disorders.
The documentary highlighted the negative effects of overbreeding in popular pet species.
The irresponsible overbreeding of dogs has led to crowded animal shelters in many cities.
Animal welfare groups warn against overbreeding because it can cause serious health problems for the animals.
Strict regulations were introduced to prevent overbreeding on commercial farms.
The overbreeding of rabbits in the area has caused serious damage to local crops.
Animal shelters struggle to cope with the overbreeding of unwanted pets.
Environmentalists warn that overbreeding can upset the balance of natural ecosystems.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: breeding - the process of producing offspring in animals
  • synonym: inbreeding - breeding between closely related animals, often causing genetic problems
  • synonym: overpopulation - the condition of having too many animals in a given area
  • synonym: selective breeding - the intentional breeding of animals to produce desired traits
