afraid

adjective
filled with fear or worry
1.0
Pronunciation: / əˈfreɪd / (AmE), / əˈfreɪd / (BrE)
feeling fear, worry, or anxiety about something; being reluctant or concerned

Examples

Afraid of the dark
He was too afraid to speak up during the meeting.
Many children are afraid of the dark and need a night light to sleep.
She felt afraid when she heard the strange noise outside the window.
He was afraid to ask for help, even though he needed it.
Many people are afraid of trying new things at first.
She felt afraid to speak her mind in the meeting.
I'm afraid I can't come to your party this weekend.
Many children are afraid of speaking in public.
She looked afraid when she heard the thunder outside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brave - showing no fear; courageous
  • antonym: confident - feeling certain and unafraid
  • antonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something
  • synonym: reluctant - unwilling or hesitant to do something
  • synonym: scared - feeling frightened or worried
