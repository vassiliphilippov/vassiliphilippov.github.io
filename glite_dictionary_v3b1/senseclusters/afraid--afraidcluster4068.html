Synonyms

  • frightened
    1.0 0.06/m
    frightened is a sudden strong feeling of alarm, while afraid is a general feeling of fear or worry.
  • scared
    1.0 58/m
    scared feels sudden and personal, often from danger, while afraid is broader and can mean worry or reluctance.
  • fearful
    1.3 10/m
    fearful describes someone showing clear fear or timidity, while afraid is more common and used in everyday speech.
  • apprehensive
    5.0 2.58/m
    apprehensive means uneasy about a likely bad event, while afraid can mean fear now or general worry.
  • anxious
    2.7 13/m
    anxious shows nervous worry about an uncertain outcome, while afraid can mean simple fear or being reluctant.
  • worried
    1.0 63/m
    worried is a steady concern about problems or future events, while afraid may be sudden fear or general unease.
  • nervous
    1.3 49/m
    nervous means physically tense and easily upset, while afraid focuses more on feeling fear or being reluctant.
