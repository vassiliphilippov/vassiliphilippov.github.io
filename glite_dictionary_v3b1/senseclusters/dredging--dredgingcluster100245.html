dredging

noun
removal of sediment
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɛdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrɛdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of removing mud, sand, or other material from the bottom of a river, lake, or harbor

Examples

River dredging
The city began dredging to deepen the harbor and allow larger ships to enter.
Regular dredging of the canal prevents it from becoming blocked by silt.
Environmental groups monitor the effects of dredging on local wildlife habitats.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deposit - to put or leave material in a place, especially by a natural process
  • synonym: excavation - the process of digging out and removing earth or material
