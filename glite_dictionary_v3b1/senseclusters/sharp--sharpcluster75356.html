sharp

adjective
high-pitched or piercing (of a sound)
3.2 2.16/m
Pronunciation: /  É…ërp / (AmE), /  É…ëÀêp / (BrE)
(of a sound) having or emitting a high-pitched, piercing, or shrill tone.

Examples

Sharp whistle
A sharp whistle pierced the morning silence.
Her voice turned sharp when she shouted across the playground.
The sharp cry of the baby startled everyone in the room.

Synonyms

  • high-pitched
    3.7 3.22/m
    high-pitched is a sound with a high frequency and clear top notes, while sharp means a piercing, thin tone.
  • shrill
    4.8 3.63/m
    shrill is high and often loud in an unpleasant way, while sharp is more neutral but still high and piercing.
  • shrieky
    7.2 0.01/m
    shrieky is loud and like a quick shriek, while sharp is a high, piercing sound that can be less noisy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mellow - (of a sound) pleasantly smooth or soft; not harsh or sharp
  • synonym: piercing - (of a sound) very high and loud, making you feel uncomfortable
  • synonym: shrill - high-pitched and piercing in sound
