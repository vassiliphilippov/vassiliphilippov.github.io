The state of having power, influence, or control over others; dominance or superiority.

Examples

Complete ascendency over rivals
During that era, the empire enjoyed complete ascendency over its rivals.
He slowly established his ascendency within the organization through hard work and persistence.
The political party's ascendency was challenged by growing public dissatisfaction.

Synonyms

  • ascendancy
    8.2 1.79/m
    ascendancy is the usual spelling for having power or influence over others, while ascendency is an alternate form used in the same way.
  • dominance
    5.1 11/m
    dominance is a general state of power and control over people or groups, while ascendency emphasizes influence or rising superiority.
  • domination
    4.7 8.62/m
    domination is the active exercise of power over others, while ascendency describes a state of influence or superiority.
  • supremacy
    7.1 5.27/m
    supremacy is being superior to all others in power or status, while ascendency can mean influence without total superiority.
  • hegemony
    9.5 5.28/m
    hegemony is long-term leadership of one group or nation over others, while ascendency is a general state of influence or control.
  • control
    2.8 200/m
    control is the power to direct or manage people, things, or situations, while ascendency focuses on wider influence or dominance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominance - the state of being dominant or having power over others
  • antonym: subordination - the state of being lower in rank or under the control of another
