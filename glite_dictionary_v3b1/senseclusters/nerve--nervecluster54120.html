nerve

noun
bundle of fibers transmitting impulses in the body
3.5 17/m
Pronunciation: / n…ùv / (AmE), / n…úÀêv / (BrE)
A bundle of fibers in the body that transmits impulses of sensation to the brain or spinal cord, and impulses from these to the muscles and organs.

Examples

A damaged nerve
Doctors were able to repair the damaged nerve during surgery.
Injury to a nerve can lead to loss of sensation in the affected area.
The optic nerve carries visual information from the eye to the brain.
