inscriptible

adjective
able to be inscribed
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈskrɪptəbl / (AmE), / ɪnˈskrɪptəbl / (BrE)
able to be inscribed within another figure, especially in geometry, such as fitting a circle inside a polygon so it touches all sides or vertices.

Examples

Inscriptible hexagon
A regular hexagon is inscriptible because a circle can touch all its sides.
The mathematician explained which polygons are inscriptible in a given circle.
Not every quadrilateral is inscriptible, as some cannot fit a circle touching all sides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: circumscribable - able to be circumscribed around another figure
  • synonym: tangential - touching at a single point, especially in geometry
