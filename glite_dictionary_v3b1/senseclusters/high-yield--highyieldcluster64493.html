high-yield

adjective
producing a large amount; productive
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈjiːld / (AmE), / haɪ ˈjiːld / (BrE)
Producing a large amount of agricultural, industrial, or financial output compared to others of its kind.

Examples

High-yield crops
Farmers chose high-yield crops to maximize their harvests.
Researchers are developing high-yield strains of wheat to address food shortages.
The company invested in high-yield manufacturing processes to increase its profits.

  • antonym: low-yield - producing a small output compared to others
