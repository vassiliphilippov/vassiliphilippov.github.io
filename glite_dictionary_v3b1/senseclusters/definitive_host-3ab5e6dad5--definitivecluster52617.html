definitive host

noun
organism where a parasite reproduces sexually
1.0 6.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfɪn.ə.tɪv hoʊst/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfɪn.ɪ.tɪv hoʊst/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfɪn.ɪ.tɪv həʊst/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfɪn.ə.tɪv həʊst/ (BrE)
An organism in which a parasite undergoes sexual reproduction as part of its life cycle.

Examples

The definitive host organism
Humans are the definitive host for certain tapeworms, completing the parasite's life cycle.
In the case of toxoplasmosis, the cat serves as the definitive host.
The mosquito acts as the definitive host for the malaria parasite, where sexual reproduction occurs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intermediate host - organism where a parasite develops but does not reproduce sexually
