bagel

noun
ring-shaped bread roll
3.1 1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈbeɪɡl̩ /; / ˈbaɪɡəl / (BrE)
a dense, ring-shaped (doughnut-shaped) yeast-raised bread roll, typically boiled and then baked, with a chewy interior and a crisp, shiny or hard crust; sometimes glazed; traditionally made in Jewish communities.

Examples

A bagel with cream cheese
I bought a freshly baked bagel with cream cheese for breakfast.
She sliced the bagel in half before toasting it.
The bakery offers bagels in a variety of flavors, including sesame and poppy seed.
I bought a fresh beigel from the old bakery on Brick Lane.
She prefers her beigel with smoked salmon and cream cheese.
The beigel was chewy inside and perfectly crispy on the outside.
