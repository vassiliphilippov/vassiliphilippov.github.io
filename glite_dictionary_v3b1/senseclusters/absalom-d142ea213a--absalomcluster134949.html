Absalom

noun
rebellious son
Pronunciation: / ˈæbsəˌlɑm / (AmE), / ˈæbsələm / (BrE)
a person, especially a son, who rebels against or betrays a parent or authority figure

Examples

An Absalom figure
After his public argument with his father, some called him an Absalom in the community.
The story of Absalom is often used as a warning against betraying one's family.
He feared his actions would make him seem like an Absalom to those who trusted him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to a leader or cause
  • synonym: traitor - a person who betrays someone or something
