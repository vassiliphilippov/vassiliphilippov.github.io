propagate

verb
cause a plant to reproduce
5.9 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈprɑː.pə.geɪt/ (AmE), /ˈprɒ.pə.geɪt/ (BrE)
to cause plants or animals to reproduce or to pass on traits to the next generation

Examples

To propagate new plants
Gardeners often propagate plants by taking cuttings from healthy specimens.
Many fruit trees are propagated by grafting rather than from seed.
You can propagate roses successfully using the layering technique in spring.
Many gardeners propagate roses by taking cuttings from mature plants.
She learned how to propagate orchids through tissue culture.
Some bacteria propagate rapidly by simple cell division.
Many plants propagate their traits through both seeds and cuttings.
Scientists study how genetic mutations propagate within animal populations.
Traditions are often propagated from parents to children over generations.
