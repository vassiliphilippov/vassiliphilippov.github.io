catchline

noun
brief heading
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkætʃˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a brief heading or title at the top of a page, section, or article summarizing its content

Examples

Report catchline
The catchline at the top of the report made the topic clear to readers.
Editors often write a catchline to quickly inform readers about the article's subject.
Each chapter began with a short catchline summarizing its main idea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: headline - the main title of a newspaper article or story
  • synonym: subtitle - a secondary title giving additional information
