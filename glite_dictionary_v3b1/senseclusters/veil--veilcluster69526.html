veil

noun
membranous covering on young mushrooms
9.9
Pronunciation: / veɪl / (AmE), / veɪl / (BrE)
a thin natural membrane covering something, such as in mushrooms or newborns

Examples

Mushroom veil
As the mushroom grew, its veil broke apart, leaving remnants on the stem.
In some species, the veil creates a ring around the stalk after it ruptures.
The veil was still attached to the edge of the young mushroom's cap.
Babies born with a veil are sometimes said to possess a special destiny in folklore.
Doctors carefully removed the veil to clear the infant's airways shortly after delivery.
The newborn emerged with a veil over her face, a phenomenon sometimes considered lucky.
