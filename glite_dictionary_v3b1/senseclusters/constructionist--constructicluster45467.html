constructionist

noun
knowledge theory believer
8.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that knowledge is constructed through experience and social interaction rather than being innate

Examples

Constructionist learning theory
As a constructionist, she encouraged students to learn through hands-on activities and collaboration.
The constructionist approach to education values personal experience and group discussion.
Many modern teachers identify as constructionists because they believe students build knowledge actively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constructivist - a person who supports the theory that knowledge is actively built by learners
  • synonym: empiricist - a person who believes knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
  • antonym: nativist - a person who believes knowledge is innate rather than acquired
