totalitarian regime

noun
government with absolute, centralized control
Pronunciation: / toʊˌtæləˈtɛriən rɪˈʤim / (AmE), / təʊˌtælɪˈteəriən rɪˈʒiːm / (BrE)
a government that seeks to control all aspects of public and private life, subordinating individuals to the state through coercive measures

Examples

A totalitarian regime controls
Dissent was brutally suppressed under the totalitarian regime that ruled the country for decades.
Many historians have studied how a totalitarian regime uses propaganda to maintain control.
The totalitarian regime banned free speech and monitored all citizens' activities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarian regime - government favoring strict obedience at the expense of personal freedom
  • antonym: democracy - a form of government in which power is held by the people
