bow-legged

adjective
legs curving outward at the knees
7.7 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbaʊ.legd/ /; / /ˈboʊ.legd/ (AmE), /ˈbaʊ.legd/ /; / /ˈbəʊ.legd/ (BrE)
Having legs that curve outward at the knees, creating a gap between them when standing with feet together.

Examples

Bow-legged person
After years of riding horses, he became noticeably bow-legged.
Her bow-legged gait made her self-conscious throughout her teenage years.
The doctor explained that mild bow-legged posture in toddlers usually corrects itself over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: knock-kneed - having knees that touch and ankles that are apart when standing
