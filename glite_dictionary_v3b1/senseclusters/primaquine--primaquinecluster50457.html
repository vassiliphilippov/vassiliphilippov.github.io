primaquine

noun
synthetic antimalarial drug
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːməˌkwiːn / (AmE), / ˈprɪməkwiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used to treat and prevent malaria, especially to eliminate dormant liver forms of the parasite

Examples

Primaquine drug
Researchers studied the effectiveness of primaquine in treating different types of malaria.
The doctor prescribed primaquine to prevent a recurrence of malaria.
Without primaquine, some forms of the disease can return months after the initial infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chloroquine - another drug used for the treatment of malaria
