injury

noun
physical harm or damage
1.0 1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndʒəri / (AmE), / ˈɪndʒəri / (BrE)
physical harm or damage to a person's body, usually caused by an accident, attack, or similar event

Examples

Sports injury
He suffered a serious injury during the soccer match and had to leave the field.
She returned to work after recovering from her injury.
The cyclist wore a helmet to reduce the risk of injury.
Repeated strain can lead to injury over time, even in seemingly safe activities.
The cyclist suffered a serious injury when he fell off his bike.
The doctor examined the patient's injury and recommended immediate treatment.
Combat injuries often require immediate medical attention on the battlefield.
She received a commendation for treating injury victims under fire.
The soldier suffered a serious injury during the battle but survived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damage - physical harm that impairs the value, usefulness, or normal function of something
  • antonym: death - the end of life, especially as a result of combat or injury
  • antonym: healing - process of becoming healthy after injury
  • synonym: hurt - physical pain or injury
  • synonym: trauma - physical injury or severe emotional shock
  • synonym: wound - an injury in which the skin or flesh is cut, torn, or broken
