liquefiable

adjective
able to be made into a liquid
Pronunciation: / lɪˈkwifiəbl / (AmE), / lɪˈkwɪfiəbl / (BrE)
capable of being made into a liquid; able to become liquid through a process such as heating or compression.

Examples

A liquefiable metal
Certain metals become liquefiable when heated to extreme temperatures.
Not all solids are liquefiable with common laboratory methods.
The gas is liquefiable under high pressure and low temperature.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: liquid - a substance that flows freely but is of constant volume
