Indian rhubarb

noun
Asian medicinal herb of the Himalayas
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ruːˌbɑːrb / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈruːbɑːb / (BrE)
An Asian herb native to the Himalayas, known for its medicinal roots and resemblance to common rhubarb.

Examples

Medicinal Indian rhubarb
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Indian rhubarb in its native habitat.
She purchased dried Indian rhubarb at the herbal market for its purported health benefits.
The roots of Indian rhubarb are used in traditional medicine across the Himalayas.
