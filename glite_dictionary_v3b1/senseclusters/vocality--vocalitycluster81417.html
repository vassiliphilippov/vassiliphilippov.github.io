vocality

noun
voiced sound quality
7.7
Pronunciation: /voʊ.ˈkæl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /və.ˈkæl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /vəʊ.ˈkæl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /və.ˈkæl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality of a speech sound being produced with vocal fold vibration or having a vowel-like character

Examples

Vocality of a sound
Linguists study the vocality of different sounds to understand how languages use vowels and consonants.
The vocality of a syllable can affect where the stress falls in a word.
Some consonants have higher vocality because they are produced with more vocal fold vibration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sonority - the loudness or resonance of a speech sound
  • synonym: voicing - the vibration of the vocal folds during speech
