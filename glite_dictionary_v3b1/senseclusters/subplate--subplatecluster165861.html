subplate

noun
smaller tectonic plate
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.pleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.pleɪt/ (BrE)
a smaller or subsidiary tectonic plate or fragment that is part of or associated with a larger tectonic plate and can affect local geological activity

Examples

Tectonic subplate
Scientists discovered a subplate beneath the main Pacific Plate that influences earthquake patterns.
The movement of the subplate caused minor tremors in the coastal region last year.
Geologists are studying how the subplate interacts with larger plates to better understand seismic risks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microplate - a very small tectonic plate
  • synonym: tectonic plate - a large, rigid piece of Earth's lithosphere that moves and interacts with others
