bimetallic strip

noun
strip of two bonded metals that bends with heat
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪməˈtælɪk strɪp / (AmE), / ˌbaɪmɪˈtælɪk strɪp / (BrE)
A thin strip made of two different metals bonded together, which bends when heated due to differing expansion rates.

Examples

A bimetallic strip bends
As the bimetallic strip warms up, it curves due to the different expansion rates of the two metals.
Old electric irons often used a bimetallic strip to regulate their temperature automatically.
The bimetallic strip in the thermostat bends and triggers the heating system when the temperature drops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thermostat - device that uses bimetallic strips to regulate temperature
