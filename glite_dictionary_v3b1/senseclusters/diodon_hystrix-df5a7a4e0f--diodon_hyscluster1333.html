Diodon hystrix

noun
species of porcupinefish
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.oʊˈdɑn ˈhɪstrɪks / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əˈdɒn ˈhɪstrɪks / (BrE)
A species of porcupinefish found worldwide in warm waters, known for erecting its spines when its body is inflated.

Examples

The Diodon hystrix fish
Many divers have spotted the Diodon hystrix while exploring coral reefs in tropical oceans.
The Diodon hystrix can inflate its body and stand its spines to deter predators.
When threatened, a Diodon hystrix puffs up dramatically, making itself appear much larger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: porcupinefish - a fish with spines that inflate for defense
