tamas

noun
darkness or inertia
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.məz/ /; / /ˈtɑː.məs/ (AmE), /ˈtɑː.məs/ /; / /ˈtɑː.məz/ (BrE)
in Indian philosophy, the quality of darkness, inertia, or ignorance that impedes clarity and action

Examples

Tamas in philosophy
According to the Bhagavad Gita, tamas leads to confusion and lack of progress.
Meditation is said to reduce tamas and increase clarity of mind.
A person dominated by tamas may feel unmotivated and lethargic.
