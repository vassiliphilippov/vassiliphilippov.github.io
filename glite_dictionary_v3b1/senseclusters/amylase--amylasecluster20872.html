amylase

noun
starch-digesting enzyme
5.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈæm.ɪ.leɪs/ /; / /ˈæm.ə.leɪs/ (AmE), /ˈæm.ɪ.leɪs/ /; / /ˈæm.ə.leɪs/ (BrE)
an enzyme present in saliva and pancreatic juice that helps break down starch into sugar

Examples

Amylase enzyme
Doctors sometimes check blood amylase levels to help diagnose pancreatitis.
Potatoes contain amylase that helps convert their starch into sugars during sprouting.
Salivary amylase begins the process of digesting carbohydrates in the mouth.
