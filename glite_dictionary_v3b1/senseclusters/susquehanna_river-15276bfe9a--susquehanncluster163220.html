Susquehanna River

proper noun
major river in the northeastern United States
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌskwəˈhænə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˌsʌskwɪˈhænə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the northeastern United States that flows from New York through Pennsylvania and Maryland to Chesapeake Bay

Examples

The Susquehanna River in Pennsylvania
Last summer, we went kayaking on the Susquehanna River in Pennsylvania.
Many towns along the Susquehanna River rely on it for drinking water and recreation.
The Susquehanna River is one of the oldest rivers in the world, dating back over 300 million years.
