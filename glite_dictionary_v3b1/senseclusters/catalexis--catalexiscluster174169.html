catalexis

noun
omission of final syllable in verse
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈlɛksɪs / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈlɛksɪs / (BrE)
The omission of a syllable from the final foot of a line of verse, especially in metrical poetry.

In classical poetry, catalexis can affect the overall rhythm and musicality of a verse.
