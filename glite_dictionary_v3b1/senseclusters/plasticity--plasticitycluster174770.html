plasticity

noun
ability to adapt
6.3
Pronunciation: / plæˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / plæˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
the ability to change or adapt easily, especially in response to new conditions or environments

Examples

Brain plasticity
The plasticity of the human brain allows people to recover skills after injury.
Plants show remarkable plasticity in adapting to different climates.
Artists admire the plasticity of clay, which can be shaped into many forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibility - the quality of being able to change or adapt easily
  • antonym: rigidity - the quality of being stiff and not able to change
