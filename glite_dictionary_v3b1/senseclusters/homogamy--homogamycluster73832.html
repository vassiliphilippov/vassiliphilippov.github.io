homogamy

noun
marriage to similar person
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑːɡəmi / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒɡəmi / (BrE)
the practice of marrying or forming relationships with people who have similar social, cultural, or personal characteristics

Examples

Marriage homogamy
Researchers found that homogamy is common among couples with similar educational backgrounds.
The concept of homogamy helps explain why people often marry within their own social class.
Sociologists analyze homogamy to understand patterns in modern relationships.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endogamy - marriage within a specific social group or community
  • antonym: heterogamy - marriage or pairing with someone different in background or characteristics
