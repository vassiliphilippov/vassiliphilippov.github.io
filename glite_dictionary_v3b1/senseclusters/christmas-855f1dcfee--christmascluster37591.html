Christmas

noun
Christian holiday period, Dec. 24–Jan. 6
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪs·məs / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs / (BrE)
The period in the Christian calendar extending from December 24 (Christmas Eve) to January 6 (Epiphany), traditionally celebrated as the season of Christmas.

Examples

Christmas season
In some cultures, Christmas lasts until Epiphany on January 6.
Many families exchange gifts throughout the entire Christmas period, not just on December 25th.
The church holds special services every day during Christmas.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Advent - the period of preparation before Christmas
  • synonym: Christmastide - the season of Christmas extending from December 24 to January 6
  • degree higher: Epiphany - the celebration ending the Christmas season on January 6
