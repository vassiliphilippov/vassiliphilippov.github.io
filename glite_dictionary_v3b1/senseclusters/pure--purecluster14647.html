pure

adjective
free from mixture or discordant qualities
Pronunciation: / pjʊr / (AmE), / pjʊə / (BrE)
not mixed with anything else; free from other substances, elements, or qualities

Examples

Pure water
His intentions were pure, free from any hint of selfishness or ulterior motive.
She admired the pure blue of the sky, untouched by clouds or haze.
The choir's voices joined in a pure and harmonious melody that filled the cathedral.
It's important to drink pure water when you're hiking in the wilderness.
Scientists used pure oxygen for the experiment to avoid contamination.
The jeweler confirmed that the ring was made of pure gold.
Artists often seek pure colors before blending them to create new shades.
The pure blue of the sky seemed almost unnatural.
This pigment gives a pure red without any trace of orange or pink.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clean - free from dirt or impurities; not contaminated
  • antonym: diluted - (of color) weakened in intensity, especially by mixing with white, grey, or black
  • antonym: impure - mixed with something else; not pure
  • synonym: saturated - (of color) intense, rich, not dull or faded
  • synonym: unmixed - not mixed with anything else; pure
