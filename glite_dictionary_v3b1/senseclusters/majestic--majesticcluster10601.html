majestic

adjective
having impressive beauty or dignity
1.9 3.5
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈdʒes.tɪk/ (AmE), /mə.ˈdʒes.tɪk/ (BrE)
having or showing impressive beauty, dignity, or grandeur, often in a way that is like a king or queen

Examples

A majestic mountain
Her majestic presence commanded attention in every room she entered.
The mountains looked especially majestic in the glow of the sunrise.
The orchestra performed a majestic symphony that filled the audience with awe.
A majestic eagle soared gracefully over the valley, commanding everyone's attention.
The majestic mountains rose above the clouds, their peaks dusted with snow.
The orchestra played in a majestic hall filled with golden chandeliers and velvet seats.
She entered the ballroom in a majestic velvet gown that caught everyone's attention.
The lion moved across the savanna with a majestic dignity, commanding respect from all who saw it.
The palace stood atop the hill, giving it a truly majestic presence over the land.
