regression toward the mean

noun
statistical tendency for values to move toward the average
9.2 6.8
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈgreʃ.ən tə.ˈwɔːrd ðə miːn/ /; / /rə.ˈgreʃ.ən tɔːrd ðə miːn/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈgreʃ.ən tə.ˈwɔːd ðə miːn/ /; / /rə.ˈgreʃ.ən tə.ˈwɔːd ðə miːn/ (BrE)
the tendency for extreme or unusual values in a data set to be followed by values closer to the average on subsequent measurement

Examples

Regression toward the mean effect
Regression toward the mean can explain why extreme sports performances are rarely repeated in subsequent competitions.
The phenomenon of regression toward the mean is important to consider when interpreting outlying data points.
When students perform exceptionally well on a test, regression toward the mean suggests their next scores may be closer to average.
