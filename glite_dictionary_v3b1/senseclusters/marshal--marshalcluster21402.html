marshal

noun
military officer of highest rank
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
(in some countries) a military officer of the highest rank, often commanding an army or air force.

Examples

Army marshal
During the parade, the marshal inspected the troops with great formality.
He was promoted to the rank of marshal after decades of distinguished service.
The title of marshal exists only in some national armies today.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: general - a high-ranking military officer, but usually lower than marshal
