micromanipulation

noun
excessive control of details
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˌmænɪpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˌmænɪpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of controlling or directing every small detail of a process, task, or person's behavior, often excessively

Examples

Manager's micromanipulation of tasks
The manager's micromanipulation of every project detail frustrated her team.
Employees felt stifled by the constant micromanipulation of their daily tasks.
Too much micromanipulation can reduce creativity and independence in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delegation - the act of giving responsibility or tasks to others
  • synonym: micromanagement - the practice of closely controlling every part of a task or project
