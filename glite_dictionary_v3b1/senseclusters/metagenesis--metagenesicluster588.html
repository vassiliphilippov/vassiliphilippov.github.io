metagenesis

noun
alternation of sexual and asexual generations
9.2 9.9
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈdʒe.nə.sɪs/ (AmE), /me.tə.ˈdʒe.nə.sɪs/ (BrE)
a form of reproduction in which an organism alternates between sexual and asexual generations

Examples

Metagenesis in animals
Jellyfish are known for their metagenesis, switching between polyp and medusa forms.
Many parasitic worms undergo metagenesis to exploit different hosts in their life cycles.
The study of metagenesis in plants has revealed important evolutionary adaptations.
