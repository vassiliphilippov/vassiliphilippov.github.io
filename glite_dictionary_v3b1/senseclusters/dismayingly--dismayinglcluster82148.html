dismayingly

adverb
causing dismay
7.5
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈmeɪ.ɪŋ.liː/ /; / /dɪs.ˈmeɪŋ.liː/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈmeɪ.ɪŋ.liː/ /; / /dɪs.ˈmeɪŋ.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that causes or shows dismay, distress, disappointment, or shock

Examples

Dismayingly inconclusive results
The results of the experiment were dismayingly inconclusive, leaving the researchers frustrated.
She found the lack of progress dismayingly slow despite everyone's hard work.
The weather turned dismayingly cold just before the outdoor event was about to start.
The test scores were dismayingly low across the entire class.
She found the lack of progress dismayingly obvious after months of hard work.
The weather was dismayingly cold for a spring morning.

  • synonym: disappointingly - in a way that causes disappointment
  • synonym: distressingly - in a way that causes distress or anxiety
  • antonym: reassuringly - in a way that makes someone feel less worried
