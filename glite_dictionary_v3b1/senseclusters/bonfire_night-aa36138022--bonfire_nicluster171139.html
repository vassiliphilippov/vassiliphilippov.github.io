Bonfire Night

noun
UK festival on November 5th with bonfires and fireworks
3.3 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑːn.faɪr naɪt/ (AmE), /ˈbɒn.faɪə naɪt/ (BrE)
In the UK, an annual celebration on November 5th marked by fireworks and the burning of bonfires, traditionally commemorating the failure of the Gunpowder Plot of 1605.

Examples

Bonfire Night fireworks
Children made a large effigy of Guy Fawkes to burn on Bonfire Night.
Every year, we celebrate Bonfire Night by watching the town's impressive fireworks display.
The history teacher explained the origins of Bonfire Night and the Gunpowder Plot.
