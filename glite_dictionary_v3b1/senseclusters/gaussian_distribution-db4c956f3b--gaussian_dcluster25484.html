Gaussian distribution

noun
a symmetric probability distribution; normal distribution
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaʊsiən dɪstrɪˈbjuʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊzɪən dɪstrɪˈbjuːʃən / (BrE)
a theoretical probability distribution that is symmetric about the mean, showing that data near the mean are more frequent in occurrence than data far from the mean; also known as a normal distribution.

Examples

A Gaussian distribution curve
Many natural phenomena, such as heights and test scores, approximate a Gaussian distribution.
Statisticians use the Gaussian distribution to model random variables with unknown distributions.
The errors in measurement followed a Gaussian distribution centered around zero.

Related Terms

  • synonym: normal distribution - the commonly used statistical distribution equivalent to the Gaussian distribution
