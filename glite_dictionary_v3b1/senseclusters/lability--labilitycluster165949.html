lability

noun
tendency to change
9.4
Pronunciation: / ləˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being unstable or likely to change, especially in mood, condition, or mental state

Examples

Emotional lability
The patient's emotional lability made it difficult for doctors to assess her mental state.
Financial lability in the market can cause investors to act cautiously.
Rapid weather lability in the region makes it hard to plan outdoor events.
The lability of emotional responses can make therapy more challenging for some patients.
Researchers study the lability of memories to understand how they can be modified after recall.
Hormonal changes may increase the lability of mood in adolescents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instability - the state of being unstable or likely to change
  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and not likely to change
  • synonym: volatility - the tendency to change rapidly and unpredictably
