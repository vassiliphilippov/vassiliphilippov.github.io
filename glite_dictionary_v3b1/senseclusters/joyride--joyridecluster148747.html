joyride

verb
drive or ride for pleasure without purpose
4.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɔɪ.raɪd/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɔɪ.raɪd/ (BrE)
to drive or ride in a vehicle for pleasure or excitement, often without permission and sometimes illegally or recklessly

Examples

To joyride a car
He was caught joyriding in his parents' car without their permission.
On Saturday night, they decided to joyride around the city, laughing and playing music.
Teenagers sometimes joyride through town just to pass the time.
The teenagers were caught joyriding in a convertible late at night.
He spent the afternoon joyriding through the countryside with his friends.
Some people think joyriding is harmless fun, but it can be very dangerous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commute - to travel regularly between home and work
  • synonym: cruise - to travel at a moderate speed for pleasure, especially in a car
  • synonym: race - to drive or move at high speed, often competitively
