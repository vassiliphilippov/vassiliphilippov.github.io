Avogadro's hypothesis

noun
principle: equal volumes of gases have equal numbers of molecules
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌævəˈɡɑːdroʊz haɪˈpɑːθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌavəˈɡɑːdrəʊz hʌɪˈpɒθɪsɪs / (BrE)
the scientific principle that equal volumes of all gases, under identical conditions of temperature and pressure, contain the same number of molecules

Examples

Avogadro's hypothesis in gases
According to Avogadro's hypothesis, two liters of hydrogen gas and two liters of oxygen gas at the same temperature and pressure contain the same number of molecules.
Many introductory chemistry courses teach Avogadro's hypothesis as a fundamental concept in gas behavior.
The acceptance of Avogadro's hypothesis was crucial in understanding molecular formulas in chemistry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Avogadro's law - gas law stating that the volume of a gas is proportional to the number of molecules (at constant temperature and pressure)
