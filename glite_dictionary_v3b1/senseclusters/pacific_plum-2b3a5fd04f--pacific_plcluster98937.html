Pacific plum

noun
shrub bearing small, bland red fruit
Pronunciation: / pəˈsɪfɪk plʌm / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk plʌm / (BrE)
A shrub native to the Pacific coast of the United States, producing small, red, and bland-tasting fruit.

Examples

The Pacific plum fruit
Birds often feed on the fruit of the Pacific plum during summer.
The Pacific plum is known for its small, red fruit that isn't very sweet.
We planted a Pacific plum in our backyard for its ornamental value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plum - a sweet, round stone fruit; the general fruit type
