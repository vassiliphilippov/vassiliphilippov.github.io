indistinctive

adjective
not clearly recognizable
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈstɪŋktɪv / (BrE)
not having features that make something different, unique, or easily recognized; lacking distinguishing characteristics

Examples

An indistinctive feature
The painting's colors were so muted that its subject appeared indistinctive to most viewers.
His voice was indistinctive over the phone, making it hard to tell who was speaking.
The new logo is too indistinctive to stand out among competitors.
The new logo was so indistinctive that customers barely noticed the change.
His style of writing is rather indistinctive compared to other authors in the genre.
Many modern office buildings look indistinctive and lack character.
In this language, vowel length is indistinctive and does not change the meaning of words.
The two paintings are so similar that their styles are almost indistinctive.
In some dialects, certain sounds are indistinctive and do not help to tell words apart.
