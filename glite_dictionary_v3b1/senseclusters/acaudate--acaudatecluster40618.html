acaudate

adjective
lacking a tail
6.4
Pronunciation: / əˈkɔːˌdeɪt / (AmE), / əˈkɔː.deɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal or organism) lacking a tail or taillike appendage.

Examples

Acaudate animal
In the fossil record, several acaudate mammals have been identified by their skeletal structure.
Some species of frogs are acaudate as adults, having lost their tails during development.
The tadpole transitions from having a long tail to becoming an acaudate adult frog.
