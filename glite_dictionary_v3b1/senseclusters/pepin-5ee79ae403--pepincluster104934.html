Pepin

proper noun
Frankish king, father of Charlemagne
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpe.pɪn/ (AmE), /ˈpe.pɪn/ (BrE)
A king of the Franks (ruled 751–768), father of Charlemagne, who defended papal interests and founded the Carolingian dynasty.

Examples

Pepin the king
Pepin was crowned King of the Franks in 751, marking the beginning of the Carolingian dynasty.
Historians credit Pepin with strengthening the alliance between the Frankish kingdom and the papacy.
The reforms introduced by Pepin influenced the political landscape of medieval Europe.
