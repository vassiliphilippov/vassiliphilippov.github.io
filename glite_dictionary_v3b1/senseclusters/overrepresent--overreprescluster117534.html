overrepresent

verb
to appear too often
6.5
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.re.prɪ.ˈzent/ /; / /oʊ.vər.rə.prɪ.ˈzent/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.re.prɪ.ˈzent/ /; / /əʊ.və.rə.prɪ.ˈzent/ (BrE)
to be present or given attention in a group or situation in greater numbers or influence than is fair or expected

Examples

To overrepresent minority groups
Minority groups can sometimes be overrepresented in certain types of research studies.
The survey results may be biased if one age group is overrepresented in the sample.
Critics argue that wealthy neighborhoods are overrepresented in city planning decisions.
The survey may overrepresent the opinions of younger people due to its online format.
Certain groups are often overrepresented in media coverage, leading to a biased perspective.
Critics argue that the committee overrepresents business interests compared to those of the general public.
