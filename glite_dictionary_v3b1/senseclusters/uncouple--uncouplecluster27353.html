uncouple

verb
disconnect or separate things joined
5.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkʌpəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈkʌpəl / (BrE)
to disconnect or separate two things that are joined together, especially physically or mechanically

Examples

To uncouple two parts
She decided to uncouple her finances from her business partner's.
Technicians must uncouple the machinery before performing maintenance.
The two train cars were uncoupled before reaching the station.

Related Terms

  • antonym: couple - to join or connect together
  • synonym: disconnect - to detach or break the connection of something
  • synonym: separate - to set or keep apart
