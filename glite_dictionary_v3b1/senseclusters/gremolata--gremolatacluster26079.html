gremolata

noun
Italian herb garnish
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrɛməˈlɑːtə / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛmɒˈlɑːtə / (BrE)
a garnish made from chopped parsley, garlic, and lemon zest, often used to flavor Italian dishes

A sprinkle of gremolata brightened the flavor of the roasted vegetables.
She prepared fresh gremolata with parsley, garlic, and lemon zest for the meal.
