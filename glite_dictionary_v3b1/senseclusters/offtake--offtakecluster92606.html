offtake

noun
amount withdrawn
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌteɪk / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌteɪk / (BrE)
the amount or rate at which a commodity is taken or withdrawn from a supply or production source

Examples

High offtake of gas
The factory's offtake of electricity increased significantly during the winter months.
Water offtake from the river is carefully monitored to prevent shortages downstream.
Negotiations determined the annual offtake of natural gas for the power plant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consumption - the act of using up a resource
  • antonym: supply - the amount of a resource available for use
