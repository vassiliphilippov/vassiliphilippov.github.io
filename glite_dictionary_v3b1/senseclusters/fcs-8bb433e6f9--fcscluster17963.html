FCS

noun
U.S. farm credit banking system
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɛf si ɛs / (AmE), / ɛf si ɛs / (BrE)
a cooperative nationwide system of banks and associations in the United States that provides credit to farmers and related businesses; originally federally capitalized, now owned by members and borrowers

Examples

FCS farm loans
Farmers joined the FCS to access competitive loan rates and cooperative ownership.
Many agricultural businesses rely on the FCS for flexible credit options and financial services.
The FCS played a crucial role in supporting rural communities during difficult economic times.
