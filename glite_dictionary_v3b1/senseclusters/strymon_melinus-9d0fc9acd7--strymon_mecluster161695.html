Strymon melinus

noun
a North American butterfly species
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪmɒn məˈlaɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈstraɪmən mɪˈlaɪnəs / (BrE)
A North American butterfly species, also known as the gray hairstreak, whose larvae are pests of various economic plants.

Examples

Strymon melinus butterfly
Farmers are often concerned about infestations of Strymon melinus in their fields.
Many gardeners recognize Strymon melinus by its gray wings with orange spots.
The larvae of Strymon melinus can cause damage to cotton, beans, and other crops.
