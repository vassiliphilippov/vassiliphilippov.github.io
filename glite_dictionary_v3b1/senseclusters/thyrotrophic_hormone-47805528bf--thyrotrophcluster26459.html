thyrotrophic hormone

noun
hormone stimulating thyroid function
8.6
Pronunciation: /θaɪ.rə.ˈtroʊ.fɪk ˈhɔːr.moʊn/ (AmE), /θaɪ.rə.ˈtrɒf.ɪk ˈhɔː.məʊn/ (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the thyroid gland to function

Examples

Thyrotrophic hormone levels
Levels of thyrotrophic hormone can indicate how well the pituitary gland is functioning.
Some disorders of the thyroid are linked to abnormal secretion of thyrotrophic hormone.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check for elevated thyrotrophic hormone levels.
