Seneca

noun
the Iroquoian language of the Seneca people
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsɛnɪkə / (BrE)
the Iroquoian language traditionally spoken by the Seneca people, part of the Iroquois Confederacy in North America.

Examples

Seneca language speaker
He is fluent in both English and Seneca, thanks to his grandparents.
Linguists have worked to document the Seneca language before it disappears.
Several schools are now offering courses in Seneca to revitalize the language.
