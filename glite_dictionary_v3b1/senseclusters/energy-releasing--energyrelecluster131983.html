energy-releasing

adjective
releasing energy (esp. in biology/chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnɚdʒi rɪˈlisɪŋ /; / ˈɛnərdʒi rɪˌlisɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi rɪˈliːsɪŋ /; / ˈɛnədʒi rɪˌliːsɪŋ / (BrE)
describing a process or reaction that releases energy

Examples

Energy-releasing process
Cellular respiration is an energy-releasing process that provides fuel for cellular functions.
Foods undergo energy-releasing reactions during digestion in the body.
Photosynthesis differs from energy-releasing reactions because it stores rather than releases energy.
Nuclear fission is a classic example of an energy-releasing process.
The explosion was triggered by a sudden energy-releasing event.
The scientist explained how an energy-releasing reaction powers the sun.
