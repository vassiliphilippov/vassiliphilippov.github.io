homosporous

adjective
producing only one type of spore
6.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /hoʊ.mə.ˈspɔːr.əs/ (AmE), /həʊ.mə.ˈspɔː.rəs/ (BrE)
(of a plant or organism) producing only one kind of spore, as opposed to heterosporous.

Examples

Homosporous plants
Many ferns are homosporous, producing a single type of spore for reproduction.
The homosporous nature of these plants means they do not differentiate between male and female spores.
Unlike seed plants, mosses are typically homosporous throughout their life cycle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterosporous - producing two different kinds of spores
