Genevan

noun
follower of Calvinist doctrine
8.1
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnivən / (AmE), / dʒɪˈniːvən / (BrE)
An adherent of the theological doctrines of John Calvin, especially as practiced in Geneva during the Reformation.

Examples

Genevan beliefs
As a devoted Genevan, he embraced Calvin's teachings with great enthusiasm.
The Genevan approach to church governance was influential throughout Protestant Europe.
The council was dominated by Genevans seeking religious reform in the city.
