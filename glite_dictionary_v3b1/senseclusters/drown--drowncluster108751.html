drown

verb
cover or overwhelm completely
Pronunciation: / draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
to cover or overwhelm completely, making something imperceptible or inaudible

Examples

Music drowns the noise
Her apology was drowned out by the angry crowd's shouting.
I was drowned in paperwork after my vacation.
The aroma of the flowers was drowned by the smell of fresh paint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overwhelm - to overpower completely
  • synonym: submerge - to cover completely with liquid
  • antonym: uncover - to make known or exposed by removing a cover
