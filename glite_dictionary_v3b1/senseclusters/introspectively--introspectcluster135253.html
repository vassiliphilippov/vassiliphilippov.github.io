introspectively

adverb
inwardly examining
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈspɛktɪvli / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈspɛktɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that involves examining or analyzing the internal state, structure, or properties of something, especially in technical or computing contexts

Examples

Software runs introspectively
The software examined its own processes introspectively to detect any unusual behavior.
During the audit, the system was analyzed introspectively to ensure all components were functioning correctly.
Developers often look introspectively at their code to find hidden bugs or inefficiencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analytically - in a way that involves careful examination or analysis
  • antonym: externally - in a way that focuses on the outside rather than the inside
