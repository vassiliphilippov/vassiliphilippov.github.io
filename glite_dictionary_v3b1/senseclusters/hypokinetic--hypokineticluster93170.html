hypokinetic

adjective
reduced movement (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊkɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
showing abnormally reduced movement or contraction, especially of a part of the heart as seen in medical imaging

Examples

Hypokinetic heart muscle
The doctor noted a hypokinetic region in the patient's left ventricle during the echocardiogram.
A hypokinetic segment of the heart muscle may indicate previous damage from a heart attack.
Imaging revealed that the patient's right ventricular wall was hypokinetic compared to normal tissue.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: akinetic - showing no movement or contraction
  • degree higher: hyperkinetic - showing abnormally increased movement or activity
