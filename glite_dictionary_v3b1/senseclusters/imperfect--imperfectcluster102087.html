imperfect

noun
past tense showing ongoing/repeated action
3.2 6.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɝːfɪkt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːfɪkt / (BrE)
(in grammar) a verb tense used to describe an action or state that was ongoing or repeated in the past.

Examples

Imperfect tense in Spanish
In Spanish, the imperfect is used to talk about habitual actions in the past.

The imperfect tense often expresses an action without a definite endpoint.
