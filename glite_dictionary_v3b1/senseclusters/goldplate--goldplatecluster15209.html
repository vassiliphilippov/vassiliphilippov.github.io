goldplate

verb
add unnecessary, extravagant features
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈgoʊld.pleɪt/ /; / /goʊld.ˈpleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈgəʊld.pleɪt/ /; / /gəʊld.ˈpleɪt/ (BrE)
(in business, engineering, and software) to add unnecessary, overly elaborate, or cosmetic features to a product or project (often to impress stakeholders), typically increasing cost or delaying delivery without improving core value.

Examples

To goldplate a project
The product team refused to goldplate the prototype and focused on essential features instead.
During the meeting, he warned that adding animations would goldplate the app and delay delivery.
They avoided goldplating the device, knowing extra features would increase cost without benefit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feature creep - the tendency for product requirements to increase during development, adding unnecessary features
  • synonym: over-engineer - to design something to be more complex or capable than necessary
  • antonym: simplify - to make something less complex or complicated; opposite of adding unnecessary features
