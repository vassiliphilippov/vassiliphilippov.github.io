boar thistle

noun
a European thistle weed with prickly leaves
Pronunciation: / bɔr ˈθɪsəl / (AmE), / bɔː ˈθɪsəl / (BrE)
A European thistle with large flower heads and prickly leaves, widely naturalized as a weed in the United States.

Examples

The boar thistle plant
Gardeners often struggle to remove boar thistle due to its prickly leaves and deep roots.
The boar thistle has become a common sight along rural roadsides in the United States.
The fields were overrun with boar thistle, making it difficult for the livestock to graze.
