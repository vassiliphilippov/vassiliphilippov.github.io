trade unionism

noun
the system and principles of labor unions
Pronunciation: / treɪd ˈjuːniˌɪzəm / (AmE), / treɪd ˈjuːnjənɪzəm / (BrE)
The system, principles, or theory relating to the formation and activities of labor unions.

Examples

Trade unionism principles
Opponents of trade unionism argue that it can hinder economic flexibility.
She wrote a thesis on the history of trade unionism in Britain.
The rise of trade unionism contributed significantly to improved working conditions in the early twentieth century.
