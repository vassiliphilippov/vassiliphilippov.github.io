countermining

noun
defensive tunneling
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌmaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌmaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of digging tunnels to intercept, destroy, or neutralize enemy mines or tunnels, especially during military operations

Examples

Countermining operations
The army used countermining to stop the enemy from blowing up the city walls.
Engineers specialized in countermining worked tirelessly beneath the battlefield.
Successful countermining operations saved the fortress from destruction.
The engineers began countermining to prevent the enemy from breaching the fortress walls.
Successful countermining operations saved the city from a surprise underground attack.
During the siege, countermining was essential to detect and stop enemy tunnels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mining - the act of digging tunnels or placing explosive mines, especially in warfare
  • synonym: sapping - the act of digging trenches or tunnels to approach or undermine enemy positions
