John Tyler

proper noun
10th President of the United States (1790–1862)
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn ˈtaɪlɚ / (AmE), / dʒɒn ˈtaɪlə / (BrE)
(1790–1862) The 10th President of the United States, who succeeded to the presidency upon the death of William Henry Harrison.

Examples

John Tyler presidency
Many historians debate the legacy of John Tyler as the 10th U.S. president.
President John Tyler succeeded Harrison after his sudden death in 1841.
The town held a parade in honor of John Tyler on Presidents' Day.
