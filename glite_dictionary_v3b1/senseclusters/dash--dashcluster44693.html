dash

noun
long Morse code signal
1.0
Pronunciation: / dæʃ / (AmE), / dæʃ / (BrE)
the longer of the two signals used in Morse code, represented by a horizontal line or a long sound

Examples

A dash in Morse
Each dash in Morse code is three times as long as a dot.
In Morse code, the letter 'O' is represented by three dashes.
She memorized the sequence of dots and dashes to send her message.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dot - the shorter of the two telegraphic signals used in Morse code
