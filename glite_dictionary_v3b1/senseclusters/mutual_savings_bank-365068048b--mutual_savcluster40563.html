mutual savings bank

noun
bank owned by depositors, state-chartered
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtʃuəl ˈseɪvɪŋz bæŋk / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃuəl ˈseɪvɪŋz baŋk / (BrE)
a type of savings bank owned by its depositors, managed by a board of trustees, and typically chartered by a state

Examples

A mutual savings bank owner
As a member of a mutual savings bank, you are technically one of its owners.
Many people prefer to save with a mutual savings bank because of its community-oriented approach.
The local mutual savings bank offers higher interest rates on deposits than many commercial banks.
