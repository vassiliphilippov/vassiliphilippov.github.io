focus on

verb
concentrate attention or effort on
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəs ɑn / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəs ɒn / (BrE)
to give most of your attention or effort to one particular person, subject, or thing

Examples

To focus on your work
She needs to focus on finishing her project before the deadline.
The government should focus on improving public transportation systems.
The speaker asked the audience to focus on the key issues of the presentation.
During meetings, it's important to focus on the key issues.
I need to focus on my studies to pass the upcoming exams.
She tried to focus on her work despite the noise around her.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concentrate on - to direct your attention or effort completely to something
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
