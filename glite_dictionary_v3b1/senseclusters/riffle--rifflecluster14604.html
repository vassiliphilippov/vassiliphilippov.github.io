riffle

verb
to create ripples by stirring water
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪfəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪfəl / (BrE)
to stir up water or cause a slight movement so as to form ripples on the surface

Examples

To riffle the stream
A duck paddled by, its feet riffling the clear stream.
Children tossed pebbles, watching them riffle the calm surface of the pond.
The breeze began to riffle the water of the quiet lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ripple - to form or cause to form small waves on the surface
