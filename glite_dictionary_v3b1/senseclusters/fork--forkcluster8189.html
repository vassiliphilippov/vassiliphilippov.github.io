fork

noun
the act of branching or dividing
8.8
Pronunciation: / fɔrk / (AmE), / fɔːk / (BrE)
the point or place where something, such as a road, river, or tree, divides into two or more branches or parts; or the act of splitting into branches

Examples

A road fork
A river forms a wide fork as it splits around the island.
The discussion took a fork when new evidence was introduced.
There is a fork in the road just past the old church.
A squirrel darted up the fork of the tree and disappeared among the leaves.
The river splits at the northern fork just past the old mill.
We stopped at the fork in the road to decide which direction to go.

Related Terms

  • synonym: branch - a division or subdivision of the stem or axis of a tree, shrub, or other plant
  • synonym: crotch - the angle formed by the junction of two branches or limbs
