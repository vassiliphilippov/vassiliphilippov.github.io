overdevelopment

noun
excessive growth
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˈvɛləpmənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˈvɛləpmənt / (BrE)
excessive growth or development of a body tissue, organ, or area, often leading to abnormal size or function

Examples

Muscle overdevelopment
The doctor explained that overdevelopment of the muscle could cause pain and limited movement.
Urban overdevelopment has led to crowded neighborhoods and a lack of green spaces.
Scientists are studying the causes of overdevelopment in certain glands during puberty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypertrophy - the enlargement of an organ or tissue from the increase in size of its cells
  • antonym: underdevelopment - insufficient or incomplete growth or development
