monohybrid

noun
hybrid differing at one gene locus
8.8 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.noʊ.haɪ.brɪd/ /; / /ˈmɑː.nə.haɪ.brɪd/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.nə.haɪ.brɪd/ /; / /ˈməʊ.nə.haɪ.brɪd/ (BrE)
A hybrid organism produced by crossing parents that differ at only one gene locus with two alleles, as in Mendelian genetics.

Examples

A monohybrid cross
A monohybrid shows a single trait difference inherited from each parent.
In Mendel's classic experiments, the monohybrid cross revealed the principle of dominance.
The genetic outcomes of a monohybrid cross are often illustrated using a Punnett square.
