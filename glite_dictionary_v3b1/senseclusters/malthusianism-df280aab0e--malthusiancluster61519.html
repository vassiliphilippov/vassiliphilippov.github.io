Malthusianism

noun
theory on population outpacing resources
Pronunciation: / mælˈθuːziəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / mælˈθjuːziəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
The theory proposed by Thomas Malthus that population growth will eventually outpace the growth of food production and other resources, leading to widespread poverty or famine.

Examples

Malthusianism population theory
Critics of Malthusianism argue that technological advances can overcome population pressures.
Many demographers continue to debate the relevance of Malthusianism in the modern world.
Some people fear that unchecked population growth will validate the predictions of Malthusianism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Malthusian - relating to Malthus or his theory of population
