atomic pile

noun
early type of nuclear reactor
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑmɪk paɪl / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk paɪl / (BrE)
a nuclear reactor that uses controlled nuclear fission to generate energy, especially an early type developed in the mid-20th century

Examples

Early atomic piles
Early atomic piles played a crucial role in the development of nuclear energy.
Scientists monitored the atomic pile carefully to prevent uncontrolled reactions.
The world's first atomic pile was built under the stands of the University of Chicago's football stadium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nuclear reactor - a device for initiating and controlling a nuclear chain reaction
