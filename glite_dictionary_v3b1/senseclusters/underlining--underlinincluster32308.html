underlining

noun
emphasizing with a line
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌlaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌlaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or result of drawing a line beneath text to emphasize or highlight it; the act of emphasizing something

Examples

Underlining important words
The teacher's underlining in the textbook helped students focus on key concepts.
She used underlining to make important dates stand out in her notes.
Many readers use underlining as a way to remember significant passages in a book.
The teacher's underlining of key terms helped students focus on important concepts.
In her speech, there was a clear underlining of the need for urgent action.
The article's underlining of safety concerns led to new regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emphasis - special importance or attention given to something
  • synonym: highlighting - marking text to make it stand out
  • antonym: minimizing - the act of reducing the importance or attention given to something
  • antonym: strikethrough - a line drawn through text to indicate deletion
