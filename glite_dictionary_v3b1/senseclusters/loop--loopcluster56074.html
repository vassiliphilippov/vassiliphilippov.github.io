loop

verb
fasten or join with a loop
2.5 3/m
Pronunciation: / luÀêp / (AmE), / luÀêp / (BrE)
to make, form, or move in a loop or loops; to wind or fasten something in a curved or circular shape

Examples

To loop the rope
He looped the rope around the post to secure it.
I looped the ribbon into a neat bow before tying the package.
She looped her scarf through the handles of her bag for extra flair.
She looped her scarf around her neck twice to keep warm.
The horse looped around the open field before coming to a stop.
The river looped gently through the narrow valley.
He carefully looped the wire around the post to secure it.
She looped the scarf around her neck before heading outside.
The snake looped itself around the branch with surprising speed.

Synonyms

  • coil
    3.8 0.8/m
    coil means winding into a spiral or many connected loops, while loop means making or fastening one round or circular shape.
  • wind
    3.2 14/m
    wind wraps or twists something around an object or into a spiral, while loop makes or fastens a closed circular form.
