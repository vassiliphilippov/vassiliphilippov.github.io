cheddaring

noun
cheese-making process
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛdərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛdərɪŋ / (BrE)
a process in cheese-making where curds are cut, stacked, and turned to remove whey and develop texture

Examples

The cheddaring process
The cheddaring process is essential for creating the firm texture of traditional cheddar cheese.
During cheddaring, the curds are repeatedly stacked and turned to remove excess whey.
Modern cheese factories use machines to automate the cheddaring step in production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheddar - a type of firm, yellow cheese originating from England
  • synonym: pressing - a cheese-making step where curds are compressed to remove whey
