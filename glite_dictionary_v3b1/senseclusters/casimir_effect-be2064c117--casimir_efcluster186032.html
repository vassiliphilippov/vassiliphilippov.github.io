Casimir effect

noun
quantum force between objects
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæz.ɪ.mɪr ɪ.ˈfekt/ /; / /ˈkæz.ɪ.mɪr ə.ˈfekt/ (AmE), /ˈkæz.ɪ.mɪə ɪ.ˈfekt/ /; / /ˈkæz.ɪ.mɪə ə.ˈfekt/ (BrE)
a physical force that appears between very close objects due to quantum fluctuations in empty space

Examples

The Casimir effect force
Physicists observed the Casimir effect when two metal plates were placed extremely close together in a vacuum.
The Casimir effect demonstrates how quantum mechanics can produce measurable forces in the laboratory.
Some nanotechnology devices must account for the Casimir effect when designing tiny moving parts.
