turbinate bone

noun
scroll-shaped bone in nasal passages
9.5 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.bɪ.neɪt boʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.bɪ.nət bəʊn / (BrE)
Any of the thin, scroll-shaped bones in the nasal passages of humans and other vertebrates that help filter and humidify inhaled air.

Examples

The turbinate bone in nose
In mammals, the turbinate bone helps trap dust and moisten the air before it reaches the lungs.
Inflammation of the turbinate bone can cause nasal congestion and discomfort.
The surgeon carefully repositioned the turbinate bone to improve the patient's breathing.
