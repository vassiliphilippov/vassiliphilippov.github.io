remineralize

verb
to restore minerals
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈmɪnərəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmɪnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to restore minerals to something (especially teeth, bones, or soil) after they have been lost

Examples

To remineralize tooth enamel
Dentists recommend products that help remineralize tooth enamel after acid exposure.
Farmers sometimes remineralize soil to improve crop growth and restore nutrients.
Using fluoride mouthwash can assist in the process to remineralize weakened teeth.
Dentists recommend fluoride toothpaste to help remineralize tooth enamel and prevent cavities.
After a period of poor nutrition, the doctor explained how the body can remineralize bones with proper diet.
Special treatments are used to remineralize water in areas where it is naturally soft.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demineralize - to remove mineral content from something
  • antonym: deplete - to reduce the amount of something, especially minerals or nutrients
  • synonym: fortify - to strengthen something by adding nutrients or minerals
  • synonym: restore - to bring back to a former condition
