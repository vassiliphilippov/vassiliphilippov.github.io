anticlimax

noun
disappointing shift from excitement
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈklaɪ.mæks / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈklaɪ.maks /; / ˌæn.tiˈklaɪ.maks / (BrE)
a disappointing or less exciting ending after something has been built up as important or exciting

Examples

An anticlimax ending
The ending of the movie was an anticlimax after so much built-up tension.
The party promised excitement, but the rain made it a disappointing anticlimax.
When the surprise guest turned out to be the janitor, it was a real anticlimax.
After hours of buildup, the final scene felt like a bit of an anticlimax.
His retirement was considered an anticlimax after his illustrious career.
The end of the novel was an unexpected anticlimax after so much suspense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: climax - the most intense or exciting point in something
  • synonym: letdown - a disappointing or frustrating experience
