chorditis

noun
inflammation of the vocal cords
Pronunciation: / kɔrˈdaɪtɪs / (AmE), / kɔːˈdaɪtɪs / (BrE)
inflammation of the vocal cords (or rarely, the spermatic cord)

Examples

Chorditis of vocal cords
Long-term exposure to irritants can result in chorditis and damage to the voice.
The singer was diagnosed with chorditis after experiencing persistent hoarseness.
Treatment of chorditis typically involves vocal rest and anti-inflammatory medication.
If left untreated, chorditis can result in chronic throat discomfort and voice changes.
Singers are sometimes affected by chorditis due to excessive use of their vocal cords.
The otolaryngologist diagnosed him with chorditis after he complained of persistent hoarseness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laryngitis - inflammation of the larynx
  • synonym: pharyngitis - inflammation of the pharynx, the membrane-lined cavity behind the nose and mouth
