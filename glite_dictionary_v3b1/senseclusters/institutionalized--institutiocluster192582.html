institutionalized

adjective
incorporated into a structured system
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.stəˈtuː.ʃə.nəˌlaɪzd /; / ɪnˌstɪtuˈʃənəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃə.nə.laɪzd /; / ɪnˌstɪtjuˈʃənaɪzd / (BrE)
Given the character of an institution or incorporated into an established and usually structured system.

Examples

Institutionalized racism
Corruption in the organization became so widespread that it was practically institutionalized.
She criticized the institutionalized discrimination present in hiring practices.
Their country has an institutionalized system of social welfare.
He campaigned against institutionalised racism in schools and workplaces.
Many forms of discrimination have become institutionalised over time, making change difficult.
The country struggled with institutionalised corruption that affected every level of government.

Related Terms

  • synonym: institutionalized - US spelling; given the character of an institution
  • synonym: systematic - done according to a fixed plan or system
