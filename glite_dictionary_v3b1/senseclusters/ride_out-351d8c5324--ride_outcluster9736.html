ride out

verb
endure and survive a difficult situation
Pronunciation: / raɪd aʊt / (AmE), / raɪd aʊt / (BrE)
to endure and survive a difficult or dangerous situation without being defeated or harmed

Examples

To ride out a storm
She stayed calm and tried to ride out the storm of criticism.
The company managed to ride out the economic crisis without major layoffs.
We just have to ride out these tough times until things get better.

Related Terms

  • antonym: succumb - to fail to resist a difficult situation
  • synonym: weather - to come safely through a difficult period
