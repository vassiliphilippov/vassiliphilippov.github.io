freeze-dry

verb
to preserve by freezing and vacuum drying
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːzˌdraɪ / (AmE), / ˈfriːzˌdraɪ / (BrE)
to preserve food or other materials by rapidly freezing them and then removing moisture in a vacuum

Examples

To freeze-dry food
Many astronauts' meals are freeze-dried to make them lightweight and easy to transport.
The laboratory will freeze-dry the new vaccine samples for long-term storage.
We decided to freeze-dry the blueberries to keep them fresh for months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dehydrate - to remove water from something, especially food, usually for preservation
