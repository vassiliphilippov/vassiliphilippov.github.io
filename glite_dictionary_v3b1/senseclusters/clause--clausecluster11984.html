clause

noun
section of a legal document
2.9
Pronunciation: / klɔz / (AmE), / klɑːz / (BrE)
a distinct section or provision in a legal document, such as a contract, statute, or will

Examples

A contract clause
Each clause in the agreement must be carefully reviewed for accuracy and completeness.
If either party fails to meet the conditions in this clause, the contract will be considered void.
The contract contains a confidentiality clause that prohibits sharing sensitive information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: article - a numbered section of a legal document or contract
  • synonym: provision - a specific requirement or stipulation in a legal document
