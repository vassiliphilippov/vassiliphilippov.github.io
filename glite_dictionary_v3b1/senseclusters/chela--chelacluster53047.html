chela

noun
pincer-like structure on an arthropod's limb
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːlə / (AmE), / ˈkiːlə / (BrE)
a pincer-like grasping structure found at the end of a limb in certain crustaceans and arthropods, such as crabs and lobsters

Examples

Crab chela
A lobster’s large chela is used both for defense and for hunting prey.
Some scorpions have developed specialized chelae for capturing their food.
The crab used its chela to grasp and break open the shell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claw - a sharp, curved nail on the foot of an animal; also refers to similar structures
