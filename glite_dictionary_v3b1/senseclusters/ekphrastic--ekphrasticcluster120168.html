ekphrastic

adjective
describing art in writing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈfræstɪk / (AmE), / ɛkˈfræstɪk / (BrE)
describing or relating to a vivid written description of a work of art, especially in literature

Examples

Ekphrastic poetry
The poet's ekphrastic verses brought the painting to life for the audience.
In her essay, she used an ekphrastic approach to analyze the sculpture.
Many writers enjoy creating ekphrastic works inspired by famous pieces of art.
The poet wrote an ekphrastic poem inspired by a famous painting.
Her ekphrastic essay explored the emotions evoked by the sculpture.
Many ekphrastic works use vivid language to bring visual art to life.
The poet's ekphrastic lines brought the ancient statue to life in the reader's imagination.
Her ekphrastic writing style made every scene in the novel feel vivid and real.
During the workshop, we practiced creating ekphrastic descriptions of everyday objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descriptive - giving details about how something looks, feels, or works
  • antonym: plain - simple and not detailed in description
