gand flower

noun
European flowering plant, linked to lactation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡænd ˈflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ɡænd ˈflaʊə / (BrE)
A small European perennial plant with many branches and racemes of blue, pink, or white flowers; formerly believed to promote lactation.

Examples

The gand flower plant
Despite its beautiful appearance, the medicinal value of the gand flower is now considered largely folkloric.
Many gardens in Europe feature the gand flower for its attractive blue and pink racemes.
The gand flower was once commonly used in herbal remedies to support breastfeeding mothers.
