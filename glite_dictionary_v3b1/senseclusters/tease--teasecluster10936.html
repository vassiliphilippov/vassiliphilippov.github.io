tease

verb
arouse desire or curiosity without satisfying
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: / tiz / (AmE), / tiːz / (BrE)
to arouse someone's hope, desire, or curiosity without intending to satisfy it

Examples

To tease with secrets
He liked to tease his friends about a surprise without telling them what it was.
Movie trailers often tease exciting scenes to build anticipation before a film's release.
The magazine cover teases readers with hints about the new product but reveals no details.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tempt - to entice or try to attract someone, often by offering something appealing
