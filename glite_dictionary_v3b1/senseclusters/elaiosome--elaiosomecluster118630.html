elaiosome

noun
seed structure for ants
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlaɪ.əˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ɪˈleɪ.əˌsəʊm / (BrE)
a fleshy structure attached to some seeds that attracts ants, aiding in seed dispersal

Examples

The elaiosome on seed
The elaiosome on the seed attracts ants, which carry it back to their nest.
Many woodland plants rely on their elaiosome to encourage ants to disperse their seeds.
Researchers studied how the size of the elaiosome affects the distance seeds are moved by ants.
