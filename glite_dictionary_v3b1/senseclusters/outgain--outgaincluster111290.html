outgain

verb
to surpass in gain
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈɡeɪn / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈɡeɪn / (BrE)
to gain or achieve more than someone else, especially in a competition or comparison

Examples

The runner managed to outgain all his competitors in the final lap of the race.
Our team hopes to outgain their rivals in total points this season.
Despite a slow start, she outgained everyone else by the end of the competition.
The new company managed to outgain its competitors in the first year.
If we work together, we can outgain our rivals in the market.
Despite their efforts, the team could not outgain the leading group.
The team managed to outgain its rivals in total yards during the game.
If we continue at this pace, we will outgain all our competitors by the end of the year.
Despite a slow start, she was able to outgain everyone else in the final round.
