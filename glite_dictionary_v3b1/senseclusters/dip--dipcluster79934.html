dip

noun
angle of a magnetic needle with the horizon
8.3
Pronunciation: /dɪp/ (AmE), /dɪp/ (BrE)
(Physics) The angle formed between a magnetic needle and the horizontal plane at a specific location, indicating the inclination of the Earth's magnetic field.

Examples

Magnetic dip angle
A compass designed to measure dip is known as a dip circle.
Scientists measured the dip to determine the local intensity of the Earth's magnetic field.
The dip of the magnetic needle varies depending on your position on Earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inclination - the angle between the Earth's magnetic field and the horizontal
