dip

noun
angle of a magnetic needle with the horizon
8.3 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / dÉªp / (AmE), / dÉªp / (BrE)
(Physics) The angle formed between a magnetic needle and the horizontal plane at a specific location, indicating the inclination of the Earth's magnetic field.

Examples

Magnetic dip angle
A compass designed to measure dip is known as a dip circle.
Scientists measured the dip to determine the local intensity of the Earth's magnetic field.
The dip of the magnetic needle varies depending on your position on Earth.

Synonyms

  • magnetic dip
    7.9 0.00/m
    magnetic dip is the technical name for the Earth's field angle to the horizon, while dip is the short term.
  • magnetic inclination
    7.1 0.00/m
    magnetic inclination describes the field's tilt relative to the horizon, while dip names the same magnetic angle in texts.
  • angle of dip
    9.1 0.00/m
    angle of dip refers to the measured tilt of a magnetic needle from the horizon, while dip is the common short label.
