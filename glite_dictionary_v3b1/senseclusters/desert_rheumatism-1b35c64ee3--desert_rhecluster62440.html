desert rheumatism

noun
fungal lung disease (coccidioidomycosis)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛzɚt ˌruːməˈtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdezət ˌruːməˈtɪzəm / (BrE)
A fungal infection of the lungs and sometimes the skin, especially prevalent in arid regions, characterized by coughing, excessive sputum, and nodules; also known as coccidioidomycosis.

Examples

Desert rheumatism infection
In regions with dry, dusty climates, desert rheumatism is a common health concern.
People with weakened immune systems are especially at risk for contracting desert rheumatism.
The doctor diagnosed her with desert rheumatism after she returned from the Southwest with respiratory symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coccidioidomycosis - A fungal disease affecting the lungs, also called Valley Fever or desert rheumatism
