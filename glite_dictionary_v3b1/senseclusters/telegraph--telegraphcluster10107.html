telegraph

verb
to reveal in advance
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf /; / ˈtɛlɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
to make your thoughts, feelings, or intentions clear to others before you say anything, often without meaning to

Examples

To telegraph your feelings
His body language telegraphed his true feelings before he even spoke.
The coach warned the player not to telegraph his next move to the opponent.
She telegraphed her excitement with a big smile as soon as she saw the results.
She tried not to telegraph her excitement before the surprise announcement.
His body language would often telegraph his true feelings, even when he said nothing.
During the negotiation, he accidentally telegraphed his willingness to compromise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • synonym: signal - to indicate or show something, often unintentionally
