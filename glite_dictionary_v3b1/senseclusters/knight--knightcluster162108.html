knight

verb
to confer knighthood upon
Pronunciation: / naɪt / (AmE), / naɪt / (BrE)
to make someone a knight, especially in a formal ceremony by a monarch or other important figure

Examples

To knight someone
Each year, a few individuals are knighted in a ceremony at Buckingham Palace.
It was a great honor when she watched her father knighted by the Queen.
Sir Paul McCartney was knighted for his contributions to music.

Related Terms
