mothership

noun
main supporting vessel
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌðərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈmʌðəʃɪp / (BrE)
a large ship or vehicle that carries, supports, or controls smaller ones, especially in space or at sea

Examples

Space mothership
The astronauts returned to the mothership after exploring the surface of the planet.
Several submarines received supplies from the mothership during the long mission.
In the science fiction movie, the alien mothership hovered above the city, releasing smaller crafts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flagship - the most important ship in a fleet, often carrying the commander
  • synonym: support ship - a vessel that provides assistance to other ships
