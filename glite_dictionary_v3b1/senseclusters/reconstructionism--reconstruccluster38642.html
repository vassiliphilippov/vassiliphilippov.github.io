reconstructionism

noun
method of rebuilding the past
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the scholarly method or practical process of rebuilding or inferring past languages, cultures, buildings, or events from incomplete evidence

Examples

Reconstructionism in history
The museum's approach to ancient artifacts is based on reconstructionism to help visitors imagine the original sites.
Through reconstructionism, linguists attempt to piece together lost languages from surviving records.
Modern reconstructionism often combines technology and traditional research to recreate historical environments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something in its existing state
  • synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original condition
