Hibiscus mutabilis

noun
Chinese shrub with color-changing flowers
Pronunciation: / haɪˈbɪskəs mjuːˈteɪbəlɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈbɪskəs mjuːˈteɪbɪlɪs / (BrE)
a Chinese shrub or small tree with large white or pink flowers that turn deep red at night, widely cultivated and naturalized in the southeastern United States

Examples

Hibiscus mutabilis flowers
Gardeners are drawn to Hibiscus mutabilis for its striking transformation from pale pink to deep red blossoms.
In the southeastern United States, Hibiscus mutabilis has become a popular ornamental plant.
The Hibiscus mutabilis in her garden dazzled visitors with its flowers that changed color throughout the day.
