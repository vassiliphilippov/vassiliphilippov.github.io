Marburg hemorrhagic fever

noun
severe viral disease caused by Marburg virus
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑr.bɚg ˌhɛm.ɚˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfi.vɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɑː.bɜːɡ ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
a rare and severe viral disease in humans and primates, caused by the Marburg virus, that leads to high fever, bleeding, and often fatal complications

Examples

Marburg hemorrhagic fever outbreak
Doctors wore protective clothing while treating patients with Marburg hemorrhagic fever.
Outbreaks of Marburg hemorrhagic fever have been reported in several African countries.
The mortality rate of Marburg hemorrhagic fever can be extremely high without prompt medical care.
