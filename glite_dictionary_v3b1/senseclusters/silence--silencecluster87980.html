silence

verb
prevent from expressing opinion
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪ.ləns /; / ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.ləns /; / ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
to make someone or something quiet or stop them from speaking or making noise

Examples

To silence critics
Journalists who reported on corruption were quickly silenced.
She refused to be silenced by intimidation and continued to speak out.
The government tried to silence its critics by threatening arrest.
He managed to silence his critics with a convincing argument.
She quickly tried to silence her phone before the meeting began.
The referee motioned to silence the noisy crowd.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone, especially to speak out
  • synonym: muzzle - to prevent someone from expressing themselves freely
  • synonym: quiet - to make less noisy
  • synonym: shush - to tell or make someone to be quiet, often abruptly
  • synonym: suppress - to forcibly put an end to something, especially speech or expression
