deep-sea diver

noun
person who dives in deep sea
Pronunciation: / ˌdip si ˈdaɪvər / (AmE), / ˌdiːp siː ˈdaɪvə / (BrE)
a person who dives and works in the deeper parts of the sea, especially using special equipment to withstand the pressure

Examples

A deep-sea diver suit
As a deep-sea diver, she must be trained to handle extremely high-pressure environments.
The deep-sea diver explored the wreck of an old ship nearly 200 meters below the surface.
The equipment used by a deep-sea diver is designed to protect against the dangers of the deep ocean.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diver - a person who dives, especially as a sport or occupation
