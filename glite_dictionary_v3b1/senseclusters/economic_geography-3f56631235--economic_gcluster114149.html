economic geography

noun
study of economic activity and spatial location
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkɑnəmɪk dʒiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / iˌkɒnəmɪk dʒiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the branch of geography that studies the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services in relation to spatial locations

Examples

Economic geography studies locations
An understanding of economic geography can help businesses make informed decisions about location.
She is pursuing a PhD in economic geography to study how trade patterns influence urban development.
The conference focused on recent advances in economic geography and regional economic disparities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: human geography - the branch of geography dealing with how human activity affects or is influenced by the earth's surface
