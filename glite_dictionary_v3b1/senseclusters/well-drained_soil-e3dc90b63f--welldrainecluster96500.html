well-drained soil

noun
soil that drains excess water easily
7.6 2.5
Pronunciation: /wel.ˈdreɪnd sɔɪl/ /; / /wəl.ˈdreɪnd sɔɪl/ (AmE), /wel.ˈdreɪnd sɔɪl/ /; / /wəl.ˈdreɪnd sɔɪl/ (BrE)
Soil that allows excess water to drain away easily, preventing waterlogging and promoting healthy plant growth.

Examples

Well-drained soil for plants
Roses generally prefer well-drained soil and a location with plenty of sunlight.
To avoid root rot, always make sure your plants are in well-drained soil.
Vegetables will grow best if you amend your garden beds with compost to improve well-drained soil.

Related Terms

  • antonym: waterlogged soil - soil where water cannot drain, leading to saturation
