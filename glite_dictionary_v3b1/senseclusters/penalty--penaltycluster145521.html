penalty

noun
disadvantage or painful consequence
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnəlti / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti / (BrE)
a punishment or negative consequence for breaking a law, rule, or making a mistake

Examples

Face a penalty
Every choice we make can carry a hidden penalty if we are not careful.
He suffered the penalty for arriving late to the important meeting.
Neglecting your studies now will eventually bring its own penalty.
Breaking the rules can result in a severe penalty.
If you fail to return the books on time, you will have to pay a penalty.
The company faced a financial penalty for violating the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punishment - a penalty imposed for doing something wrong, especially legally or morally
  • antonym: reward - something given in recognition of service, effort, or achievement; the opposite of penalty
