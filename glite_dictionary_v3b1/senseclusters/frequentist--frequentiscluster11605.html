frequentist

noun
probability theorist
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfriː.kwən.tɪst/ /; / /ˈfriː.kwən.təst/ (AmE), /ˈfriː.kwən.tɪst/ /; / /ˈfriː.kwən.təst/ (BrE)
a person who interprets probability as the long-run frequency of events and uses this approach in statistical analysis

Examples

A frequentist researcher
As a frequentist, she relies on repeated experiments to estimate probabilities.
The debate between a frequentist and a Bayesian can be quite intense in statistics classes.
Many traditional statistical methods were developed by frequentists in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Bayesian - a person who interprets probability as a degree of belief, not just frequency
  • synonym: statistician - a person who works with or is an expert in statistics
