grow out of

verb
cease to do or use as one matures
4.4
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊ aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / ɡrəʊ aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
to stop doing, using, or being affected by something as a result of maturing or developing

Examples

To grow out of habits
As he entered college, he began to grow out of his interest in comic books.
Most children eventually grow out of their childhood fears.
She managed to grow out of her habit of biting her nails.
