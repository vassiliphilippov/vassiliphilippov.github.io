enlightening

adjective
providing insight or knowledge
4.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈlaɪtnɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈlaɪtənɪŋ / (BrE)
giving new knowledge, understanding, or insight about a subject or situation

Examples

An enlightening speech
Her speech was truly enlightening and changed the way I thought about the issue.
I always find his lectures to be both entertaining and enlightening.
The museum tour was enlightening, revealing aspects of history I had never imagined.
Her trip to Asia was truly enlightening and changed her perspective on life.
Reading about ancient civilizations proved to be an enlightening experience for the students.
The professor gave an enlightening explanation of quantum mechanics that everyone understood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: edifying - providing moral or intellectual instruction
  • synonym: illuminating - helping to clarify or explain; giving new insight
  • synonym: instructive - useful and informative
  • antonym: misleading - giving the wrong idea or impression
