pyrrole

noun
nitrogen ring compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪroʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɪrɒl / (BrE)
a five-membered aromatic ring compound containing one nitrogen atom, found in many natural substances such as heme and chlorophyll

Examples

Pyrrole ring structure
The structure of hemoglobin contains several pyrrole rings linked together.
Chlorophyll, essential for photosynthesis, also includes a pyrrole unit in its core.
Researchers synthesized a new drug based on the pyrrole structure to treat certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indole - an aromatic compound with a fused benzene and pyrrole ring
  • synonym: porphyrin - a group of organic compounds containing four pyrrole rings
