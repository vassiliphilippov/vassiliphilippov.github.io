denaturized

adjective
changed in nature, esp. chemically
5.6 7.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈneɪ.tʃə.raɪzd/ /; / /diː.ˈneɪ.tʃə.raɪzd/ /; / /də.ˈneɪ.tʃə.raɪzd/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈneɪ.tʃə.raɪzd/ /; / /diː.ˈneɪ.tʃə.raɪzd/ /; / /də.ˈneɪ.tʃə.raɪzd/ (BrE)
(of a substance) changed in nature or natural quality, especially by chemical process, so that its natural qualities or composition are altered; often to make it unsuitable for consumption or its original use; a variant form of 'denatured'.

Examples

Denaturized food products
Some food products are denaturized during processing to alter their properties.
The laboratory used denaturized alcohol for cleaning the equipment.
The scientist explained that the protein had been denaturized by heat.
Because it is denaturised, this alcohol is not safe for drinking.
Many products contain denaturised ingredients to prevent misuse or accidental ingestion.
The laboratory uses denaturised alcohol as a solvent for chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denatured - (of a substance) changed so as to lose its natural qualities
