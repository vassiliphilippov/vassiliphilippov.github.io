Priam

proper noun
last king of Troy in Greek mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpraɪ.əm/ /; / /ˈpraɪ.æm/ (AmE), /ˈpraɪ.əm/ (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the last king of Troy, father of Hector, Paris, and Cassandra, known for his role in the Trojan War.

Examples

Priam king of Troy
According to myth, Priam had fifty sons and many daughters, including the famous Cassandra.
In Homer's Iliad, Priam pleads with Achilles for the return of his son Hector's body.
The fall of Troy marked the tragic end of Priam's long reign.
