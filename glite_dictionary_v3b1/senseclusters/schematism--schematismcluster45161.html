schematism

noun
Kantian linking process
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskiːməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈskiːmətɪzəm / (BrE)
in Kantian philosophy, the mental process that connects pure concepts to sensory experience through a schema

Examples

Kantian schematism
Kant's theory of schematism explains how abstract concepts can be applied to real-world experiences.
The philosopher discussed the importance of schematism in bridging the gap between thought and perception.
Without schematism, pure concepts would remain disconnected from our sensory experiences.
