prairie cordgrass

noun
a tall North American grass of prairies and wetlands
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛri ˈkɔrdˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈkɔːdˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A tall, coarse grass native to North America, with leaves having dry membranous margins and glumes with long awns, commonly found in prairies and wetlands.

Examples

Prairie cordgrass plant
Prairie cordgrass is often used for erosion control along streams and riverbanks.
Many birds and small animals make their homes in stands of prairie cordgrass.
Researchers are studying the potential of prairie cordgrass as a bioenergy crop.
