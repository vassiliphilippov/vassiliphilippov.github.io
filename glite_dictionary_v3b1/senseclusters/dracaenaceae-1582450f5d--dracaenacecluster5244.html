Dracaenaceae

noun
plant family (now mostly included in Asparagaceae or Agavaceae)
9.9
Pronunciation: / drəˌsiːnəˈsiː.iː /; / drəˌsiːnəˈsiː.i / (AmE), / drasɪˌiːnəˈsiː.iː /; / drəˌsiːnəˈsiː.i / (BrE)
a group of tropical plants (including dragon trees and related species), formerly considered a separate family or subfamily, now usually included in the Agavaceae or Asparagaceae families

Examples

The Dracaenaceae family
Botanists debated whether Dracaenaceae should be treated as a separate family or a subfamily within Agavaceae.
Many species formerly classified in the Dracaenaceae are now placed under the family Asparagaceae.
The dragon tree, a well-known ornamental plant, once belonged to the Dracaenaceae family.
Research on the Dracaenaceae provides insight into the evolution of monocots.
Some botanists classify the genus Dracaena in the Dracaenaceae rather than Agavaceae.
The Dracaenaceae are widely distributed throughout tropical and subtropical regions.
