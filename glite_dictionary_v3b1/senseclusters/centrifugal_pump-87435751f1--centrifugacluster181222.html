centrifugal pump

noun
a pump using centrifugal force
2.3 4.3
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈtrɪfjəɡəl pʌmp / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɪfjʊɡəl pʌmp / (BrE)
A pump that uses centrifugal force to move fluid through a rotating impeller and discharge it into a pipe.

Examples

A centrifugal pump
A centrifugal pump operates efficiently when transferring large volumes of liquid at low pressure.
Before maintenance, always disconnect the power supply to the centrifugal pump for safety reasons.
The engineer recommended installing a centrifugal pump to improve the water circulation in the system.
