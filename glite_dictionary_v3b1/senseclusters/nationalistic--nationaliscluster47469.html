nationalistic

adjective
strongly devoted to one's nation
4.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /næ.ʃə.nə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /næʃ.nə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /næ.ʃə.nə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /næʃ.nə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
having or showing strong devotion or support for one's nation, often with preference for it above others

Examples

Nationalistic feelings rise
History has shown that nationalistic movements can both unite and divide nations.
Some people criticized the policy as too nationalistic and exclusionary toward outsiders.
The politician's nationalistic rhetoric appealed to voters concerned about foreign influence.
Critics warned that the rising nationalistic sentiment might lead to isolationism.
He gave a nationalistic speech, urging citizens to support only local businesses.
The new government's nationalistic policies have caused tension with neighboring countries.
