macroeconomy

noun
national economic system
5.7
Pronunciation: /mæ.kroʊ.ɪ.ˈkɑː.nə.mɪ/ /; / /mæk.roʊ.ɪ.ˈkɑː.nə.mɪ/ (AmE), /mæ.krəʊ.ɪ.ˈkɒn.ə.mɪ/ /; / /mæk.rəʊ.ɪ.ˈkɒn.ə.mɪ/ (BrE)
the overall economic system of a country or region, including factors like growth, employment, inflation, and national income

Examples

The macroeconomy of a country
Government policies can have a major impact on the macroeconomy of a country.
Understanding the macroeconomy helps economists predict trends like unemployment and inflation.
A strong macroeconomy often leads to higher living standards for the population.
Government policies can have a significant impact on the macroeconomy of a nation.
Understanding the macroeconomy helps explain trends in unemployment and inflation.
The health of the macroeconomy affects businesses and individuals across the country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macroeconomics - the study of the overall economy and large-scale economic factors
  • antonym: microeconomy - the economic system at the level of individuals or small groups
