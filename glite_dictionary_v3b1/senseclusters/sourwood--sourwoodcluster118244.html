sourwood

noun
deciduous North American tree with sour-tasting leaves
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊɚˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈsaʊəˌwʊd / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to eastern North America, known for its deeply fissured bark, sprays of small white fragrant flowers, and sour-tasting leaves.

Examples

The sourwood tree
Bees produce a prized honey from the nectar of sourwood blossoms.
The sourwood tree is admired for its delicate white flowers in the summer months.
The leaves of sourwood have a pleasantly tart taste when chewed.
