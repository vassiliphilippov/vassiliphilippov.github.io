sudation

noun
the secretion of sweat by the glands
Pronunciation: / suːˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / sjuːˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which the sweat glands of the skin secrete a salty fluid, resulting in sweating

Examples

Sudation of sweat
Certain medications can cause excessive sudation as a side effect.
Doctors sometimes monitor sudation levels in patients with thyroid disorders.
Intense physical exertion increases sudation to help regulate body temperature.
