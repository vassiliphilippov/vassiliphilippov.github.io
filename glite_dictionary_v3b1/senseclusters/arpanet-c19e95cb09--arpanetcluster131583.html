ARPANET

noun
early computer network
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrpəˌnɛt / (AmE), / ˈɑːpəˌnɛt / (BrE)
the first large-scale computer network, developed in the late 1960s, that became the foundation of the modern internet

Examples

The ARPANET network
The ARPANET was created to connect researchers at different universities across the United States.
Email was first developed and used on the ARPANET in the early 1970s.
Many of the protocols used on the internet today were originally tested on the ARPANET.
