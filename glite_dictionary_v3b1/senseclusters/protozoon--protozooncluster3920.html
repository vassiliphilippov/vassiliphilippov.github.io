protozoon

noun
unicellular organism (protozoa)
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtəˈzəʊən / (BrE)
A single unicellular organism belonging to the group of protozoa, typically nonphotosynthetic and often microscopic.

Examples

A protozoon cell
A protozoon can only be seen with a microscope due to its minute size.
Malaria is caused by a protozoon of the genus Plasmodium.
Scientists observed the movement of a protozoon in a drop of pond water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protozoa - the plural form referring to protozoan organisms
