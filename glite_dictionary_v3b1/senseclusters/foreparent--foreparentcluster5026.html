foreparent

noun
ancestor or predecessor
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌpɛrənt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌpɛərənt / (BrE)
a person or original form from whom others are descended; an ancestor (including in a biological or evolutionary sense)

Examples

Our foreparents in history
Our foreparents faced many challenges when they first settled in this region.
The traditions of our foreparents are still celebrated in our family today.
Many people honor their foreparents by learning about their family history.
Scientists believe that birds share a common foreparent with certain types of dinosaurs.
The foreparent of modern horses was much smaller and lived in forests.
Researchers study fossils to learn more about the foreparent of present-day mammals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ancestor - a person from whom one is descended
  • antonym: descendant - a person who is descended from a particular ancestor
