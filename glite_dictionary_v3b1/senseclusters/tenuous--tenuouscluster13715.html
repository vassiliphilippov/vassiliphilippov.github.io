tenuous

adjective
having a thin or weak consistency
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnjuəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnjuːəs /; / ˈtɛnjʊəs / (BrE)
physically thin, weak, or not dense; slender or fragile in form or substance

Examples

Tenuous thread
A tenuous mist hovered above the cool morning grass.
The atmosphere on Mars is so tenuous that humans cannot breathe it without help.
The liquid was so tenuous that it barely left a mark on the surface.
A single tenuous thread held the delicate spider's web together.
Sunlight filtered through the tenuous curtains, softly illuminating the room.
The tenuous wires easily broke under the slightest pressure.

  • antonym: thick - having a large distance between opposite sides
  • synonym: thin - having little thickness or depth
