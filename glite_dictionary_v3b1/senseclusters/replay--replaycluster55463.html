replay

verb
play a game again between same opponents
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈpleɪ /; / riˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to play a game or match again, usually because the first result was inconclusive or invalid

Examples

To replay the match
After the tie, the teams agreed to replay the match next week.
If the score remains even, the referee may order the teams to replay the entire match.
The cup final was replayed after heavy rain caused the original game to be abandoned.
After reviewing the footage, the referee decided to replay the last minute of the match.
She often replays her favorite songs when she's feeling nostalgic.
The team agreed to replay the game due to a technical error.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rematch - a second match between the same opponents
  • synonym: repeat - to do something again
