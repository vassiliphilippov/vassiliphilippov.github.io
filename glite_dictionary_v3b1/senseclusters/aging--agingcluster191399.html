aging

noun
process of improving quality over time
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which something acquires desirable qualities through being left undisturbed for a period of time, as with cheese or wine

Examples

Wine aging
Oak barrels are used in the aging of fine wines.
Proper aging of meat produces a more tender and flavorful product.
The aging of cheese intensifies its flavor and texture.
Many winemakers believe that careful ageing enhances wine's complexity.
The ageing of cheese is crucial to its flavor development.
The process of ageing beef results in more tender and flavorful meat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maturation - the process of becoming mature or fully developed, especially in food or drink
