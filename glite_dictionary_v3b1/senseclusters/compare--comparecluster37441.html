compare

verb
consider as similar or equal
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɛr / (AmE), / kəmˈpeə /; / kəmˈpɛə / (BrE)
to examine or describe similarities and differences between two or more things

Examples

To compare two things
In her essay, she compared the two novels to highlight their similarities.
It's not fair to compare the two candidates, as their backgrounds are very different.
Many people compare modern technology to the inventions of the Industrial Revolution.
It's useful to compare different insurance plans before making a decision.
She compared her notes with her classmates after the lecture.
When you compare these two paintings, you can see the influence of the same artist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contrast - to compare in order to show differences
  • synonym: liken - to point out similarities between things
