Aspergillales

noun
order of fungi with scattered asci
Pronunciation: / ˌæspərˌdʒɪˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌaspəˌdʒɪˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
An order of fungi characterized by a closed ascocarp (cleistothecium) with scattered asci rather than a centralized hymenium.

Examples

Aspergillales fungi order
Aspergillales includes many fungi that are important in food spoilage and fermentation processes.
Researchers have identified several new species within Aspergillales that are significant in biotechnology.
The genus Aspergillus belongs to the order Aspergillales within the Ascomycota division.
