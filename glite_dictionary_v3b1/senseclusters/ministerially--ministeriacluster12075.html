ministerially

adverb
in the manner of a minister or clergyman
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪnɪˈstɛriəli / (AmE), / ˌmɪnɪˈstɪəli / (BrE)
in a way that is characteristic of or appropriate to a minister or clergy; in a manner befitting a religious minister

Examples

Act ministerially at service
He was called upon to act ministerially during the memorial service.
She visited the sick ministerially, offering comfort and spiritual guidance.
The bishop intervened ministerially when the congregation faced difficult times.

Related Terms
