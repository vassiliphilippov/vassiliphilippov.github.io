tunicated

adjective
having a sheath
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːnɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈtjuːnɪˌkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
having a covering or sheath, especially referring to plant bulbs or certain animals with protective layers

Examples

Tunicated onion bulb
The tunicated bulb of the onion protects it from drying out.
Some sea creatures are tunicated, having a tough outer layer for defense.
Gardeners prefer tunicated bulbs because their coverings help retain moisture in the soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sheathed - covered with a protective layer or sheath
