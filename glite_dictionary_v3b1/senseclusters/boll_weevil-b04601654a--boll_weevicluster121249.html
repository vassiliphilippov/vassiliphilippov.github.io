boll weevil

noun
pest beetle damaging cotton crops
9.9 0.47/m
Pronunciation: / bo äl ÀàwiÀêv…ôl / (AmE), / b…ô äl ÀàwiÀêv…ôl / (BrE)
A small, grayish beetle that lays its eggs in cotton bolls and destroys cotton crops.

Examples

The boll weevil beetle
Farmers lost their entire crop to the boll weevil invasion.
Scientists are developing new methods to control boll weevil populations.
The boll weevil has been a threat to southern U.S. cotton fields for over a century.

Synonyms

  • Anthonomus grandis
    9.9 0.00/m
    Anthonomus grandis is the scientific name used in research and reports, while boll weevil is the common everyday name farmers use.
