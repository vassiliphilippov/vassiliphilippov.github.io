tendrilled

verb
to grow with tendrils
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndrɪld / (AmE), / ˈtɛndrɪld / (BrE)
to climb, attach, or grow by producing or using tendrils, especially of a plant.

Examples

Ivy tendrilled up
The ivy tendrilled quickly, winding itself tightly around the old stone wall.
Morning glories tendrilled up the trellis, covering it with green leaves and purple flowers.
The cucumber plant tendrilled along the fence, securing itself as it grew taller.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cling - to hold on tightly or attach to something
  • antonym: detach - to separate or remove from something
