oversell

verb
to sell beyond supply
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɛl / (BrE)
to sell more of something than is available or can be supplied, often resulting in an inability to fulfill all orders

Examples

To oversell tickets
The concert organizers accidentally oversold tickets, leaving some fans unable to enter the venue.
If you oversell a product, you risk disappointing customers who cannot receive what they ordered.
The airline apologized after it oversold seats on the popular holiday flight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overbook - to accept more reservations than there is space for
  • antonym: undersell - to sell less than the available supply
