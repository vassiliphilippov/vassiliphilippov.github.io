repolarize

verb
to restore polarity
7.9 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàpo äl…ôÀåra…™z / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàp…ô äl…ôra…™z / (BrE)
to restore the electrical polarity or charge of a cell, component, or material after it has been disrupted

Examples

To repolarize a cell
After the power surge, the technician had to repolarize the capacitor before the circuit worked again.
In nerve cells, special channels help repolarize the membrane after an impulse passes.
The engineer explained how to repolarize the device to ensure proper function.
Scientists needed to repolarize the nerve cell membrane after it had been depolarized during the experiment.
The technician applied a voltage to repolarize the magnetic material in the device.
After each signal, the heart cells must repolarize before they can contract again.
After an action potential, the neuron must repolarize before it can fire again.
Certain medications can affect how quickly heart cells repolarize after each beat.
If a muscle cell fails to repolarize properly, it may not function as expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depolarize - to reduce or remove the electrical polarity of something
  • synonym: polarize - to cause something to have electric or magnetic polarity
  • synonym: reset - to set something back to its original state
