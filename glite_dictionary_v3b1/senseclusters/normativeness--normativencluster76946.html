normativeness

noun
binding authority
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrmətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈnɔːmətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of having binding authority or requiring compliance, as with a rule or principle

Examples

Legal normativeness
The normativeness of international law is often discussed in legal theory classes.
Some critics questioned the normativeness of the new company policies.
Cultural differences can affect the normativeness of social rules in various societies.

  • antonym: permissiveness - the quality of allowing or permitting rather than requiring
