nonvirtuous

adjective
lacking virtue
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈvɜrtʃuəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈvɜːtjuəs / (BrE)
not having or showing moral goodness or virtue; lacking moral principles.

Examples

The nonvirtuous actions
The nonvirtuous actions of the politician led to a loss of public trust.
In many stories, nonvirtuous characters eventually face the consequences of their choices.
She refused to follow the nonvirtuous path suggested by her peers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immoral - not conforming to accepted standards of morality
  • antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
