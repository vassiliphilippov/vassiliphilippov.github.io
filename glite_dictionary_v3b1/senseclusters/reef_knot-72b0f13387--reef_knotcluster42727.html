reef knot

noun
a knot for joining two rope ends
3.8
Pronunciation: / ɹif nɑt / (AmE), / ɹiːf nɒt / (BrE)
a knot made by tying two ends of a rope together with a left-over-right and right-over-left sequence, used especially in sailing and first aid

Examples

A reef knot tie
A reef knot is commonly used in first aid to tie bandages securely.
During sailing lessons, we learned how to tie a reef knot for reefing the sails.
He secured the package with a reef knot to keep the ends from slipping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: square knot - a knot with the same form as a reef knot; commonly used synonym
