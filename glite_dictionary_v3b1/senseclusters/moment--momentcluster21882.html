moment

noun
a particular point in time
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈməʊmənt / (BrE)
a very short period of time or a particular point in time (including the present time, now)

Examples

At that moment
At that moment, everything changed for him.
Please wait a moment while I find your file.
There was a pause, and then, in a single moment, the decision was made.
At this moment, all we can do is wait.
I'm afraid he's not available at the moment.
She is very busy at the moment, so please call back later.
I'll be ready in just a moment if you can wait.
She paused for a moment to gather her thoughts before speaking.
That moment passed so quickly, I almost missed it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eternity - an infinite or unending length of time
  • synonym: instant - a particular point in time
  • synonym: now - the present moment; at the current time
  • degree lower: second - a short and precise time unit
  • degree higher: while - a period of time, longer than a moment
