debilitation

noun
serious weakening and loss of energy
7.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A serious weakening and loss of strength or energy.

Examples

Serious debilitation
After her surgery, she suffered temporary debilitation but regained her strength through rehabilitation.
Long periods of malnutrition can cause debilitation and make recovery difficult.
The disease led to a gradual debilitation of his muscles over several years.
