water cabbage

noun
floating aquatic plant; river/lake weed
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər ˌkæbɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌkæbɪdʒ / (BrE)
a pantropical aquatic plant with floating rosettes of soft, wedge-shaped leaves, commonly found as a weed in rivers and lakes

Examples

Water cabbage plant
In some regions, water cabbage is considered an invasive species that clogs waterways.
The pond was covered with water cabbage, making it difficult for boats to pass through.
We could see fish swimming beneath the floating water cabbage in the shallow lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pistia - genus of aquatic plants known as water cabbage
