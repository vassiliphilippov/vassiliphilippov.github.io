fee-tail

verb
restrict inheritance to certain heirs (legal)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / fiËˆteÉªl / (AmE), / fiËËˆteÉªl / (BrE)
(law) To restrict the inheritance of property so that it may be passed only to a specified class of heirs.

Examples

To fee-tail property
By fee-tailing his lands, the lord prevented their division among distant relatives.
The estate was fee-tailed so only direct male descendants could inherit it.
The family decided to fee-tail the property to ensure it stayed within their bloodline.

Synonyms
