succade

noun
fruit preserved in sugar and coated with crystals
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / s åÀàke…™d / (AmE), / s äÀàk…ëÀêd / (BrE)
Pieces of fruit that have been cooked in sugar syrup and then coated with sugar crystals, used as a sweetmeat or for decoration in confectionery.

Examples

Pieces of succade
Boxes of succade were displayed in the bakery window.
He garnished the dessert with a piece of orange succade.
The cake was studded with colorful succade and nuts.
