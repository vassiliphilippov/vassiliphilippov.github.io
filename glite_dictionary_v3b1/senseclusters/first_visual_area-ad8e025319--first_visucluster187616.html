first visual area

noun
primary visual region of the brain
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrst ˈvɪʒuəl ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈvɪʒuəl ˈeəriə / (BrE)
the region of the occipital cortex that receives fibers from the optic radiation and is the primary receptive area for vision

Examples

The first visual area
Damage to the first visual area can result in permanent loss of vision in certain parts of the visual field.
Scientists mapped activity in the first visual area while subjects looked at different images.
The first visual area is crucial for processing the basic features of what we see.

Related Terms

  • synonym: primary visual cortex - the region of the brain responsible for initial processing of visual information
