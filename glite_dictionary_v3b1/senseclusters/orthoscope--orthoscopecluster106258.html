orthoscope

noun
ophthalmoscope with water layer
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrθəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəskəʊp / (BrE)
A type of ophthalmoscope that uses a layer of water to neutralize the refraction of the cornea during eye examinations.

Examples

The orthoscope device
Advances in technology have led to modern versions of the orthoscope used in ophthalmology clinics.
An orthoscope reduces corneal refraction, allowing for more accurate measurements during an eye exam.
The doctor examined the patient's eye using an orthoscope to obtain a clearer image of the retina.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ophthalmoscope - instrument for inspecting the retina and other parts of the eye
