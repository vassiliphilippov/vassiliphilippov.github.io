current

noun
prevailing movement of ideas or events
3.2 13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ùÀê.…ônt / (AmE), / Ààk år.…ônt / (BrE)
a steady flow or movement, especially of water, air, or ideas

Examples

Ocean current
Academic theories often change with the current of new discoveries and perspectives.
He tried to resist the current of revolutionary thought sweeping through his country.
Many people followed the current of popular opinion during the election.
A current of cold water ran beneath the surface of the lake.
The swimmer fought against the strong current to reach the shore.
Warm air currents swept across the plain, bringing a sudden change in temperature.

Synonyms

  • flow
    2.3 33/m
    flow is the steady, continuous movement or supply of people, things, or ideas, while current often names movement in water or air.
  • stream
    1.0 16/m
    stream is a narrow moving flow of water or ideas seen as a line, while current is a wider steady movement.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: eddy - a circular movement of water, counter to a main current
  • synonym: stream - a small, narrow river; also, a continuous flow of something
  • synonym: trend - a general direction in which something is developing or changing
