sleep

verb
put a device into low-power mode
4.8 1.30/m
Pronunciation: / sliÀêp / (AmE), / sliÀêp / (BrE)
to put (or enter) an electronic device into a low-power sleep state to reduce energy use while allowing quick restart.

Examples

To sleep a computer
Most smartphones will sleep after a short period of inactivity to save power.
The IT team sleeps lab computers overnight to reduce electricity costs.
He accidentally slept the printer, interrupting the long print job.

Synonyms

  • put to sleep
    3.4 0.03/m
    put to sleep is used for causing a device to enter low-power standby, while sleep refers to that low-power state.
  • go to sleep
    3.1 2.44/m
    go to sleep means a device enters low-power mode automatically after inactivity, while sleep can mean entering that mode manually or automatically.
  • fall asleep
    3.0 0.01/m
    fall asleep describes a device entering a low-power state slowly after idleness, while sleep can also mean manually setting that state.
  • suspend
    3.9 6.82/m
    suspend officially stops or pauses activity for a time, while sleep specifically refers to a low-power device state for quick restart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hibernate - to place a device into a deeper power-saving state that preserves memory to disk
  • antonym: shutdown - to turn a device off completely (opposite of putting it to sleep)
  • synonym: standby - to place or remain in a low-power ready state
