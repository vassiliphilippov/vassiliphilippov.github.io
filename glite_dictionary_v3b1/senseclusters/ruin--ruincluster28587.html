ruin

verb
fall into decay or destruction
3.5 3.45/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀê.…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàruÀê.…™n / (BrE)
to fall into a state of decay or destruction; to become ruined or dilapidated

Examples

Old buildings ruin
After decades of neglect, the castle began to ruin amidst the surrounding forest.
Many old mansions ruin when there is no one left to care for them.
The factory will ruin quickly if repairs are not made soon.

Synonyms

  • decay
    4.4 2.75/m
    decay is a slow breakdown or decomposition of materials, while ruin means to fall into long-term destruction.
  • dilapidate
    8.4 0.03/m
    dilapidate means to become run-down from neglect and use, while ruin means to fall into decay or collapse.
  • rot away
    4.2 0.55/m
    rot away describes slow decay, often of organic material, while ruin means general destruction or loss of structure.
  • go to seed
    7.3 0.29/m
    go to seed means to become shabby, overgrown, or low quality, while ruin means to fall into decay or destruction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • synonym: decay - to rot or decompose over time
