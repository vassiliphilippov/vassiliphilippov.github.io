brokenly

adverb
in a damaged way
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊkənli / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊkənli / (BrE)
in a way that is not smooth or continuous, often because of emotion or interruption (especially when speaking)

Examples

To speak brokenly
He spoke brokenly, struggling to find the right words after the accident.
The lights flickered brokenly during the storm, making it hard to see.
Music played brokenly from the old speaker, cutting in and out unexpectedly.
She spoke brokenly, her voice trembling with emotion after hearing the news.
The old radio played music brokenly, with static interrupting the melody.
After the accident, the pieces of the vase lay brokenly across the floor.
He spoke brokenly after hearing the sad news, struggling to find the right words.
The child answered brokenly, pausing often as she tried to explain what happened.
She laughed brokenly, her voice catching with emotion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fluently - in a smooth and effortless way
  • synonym: haltingly - in a slow and hesitant manner
  • antonym: smoothly - in a continuous and even way
