worlder

noun
mortal inhabitant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɜrldər / (AmE), / ˈwɜːldə / (BrE)
a person who lives in the world, especially as a mortal being; used in archaic or literary contexts

Examples

A mortal worlder
In ancient legends, a worlder could never escape the fate of all living things.
The poet described himself as a humble worlder wandering beneath the stars.
Even the wisest worlder cannot know what lies beyond this life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immortal - a being not subject to death
  • synonym: mortal - a human being subject to death
