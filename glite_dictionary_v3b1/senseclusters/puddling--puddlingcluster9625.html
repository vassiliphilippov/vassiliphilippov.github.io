puddling

noun
insect feeding behavior
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpʌd.lɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpʌd.lɪŋ/ (BrE)
a behavior in which butterflies and some insects gather on wet soil or mud to obtain salts and nutrients

Examples

Butterflies puddling on mud
After the rain, dozens of butterflies were puddling on the damp riverbank.
Scientists observed puddling as butterflies gathered to absorb minerals from the mud.
The guide explained that puddling helps butterflies get essential nutrients not found in nectar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nectaring - feeding on flower nectar, especially by butterflies
