double replacement reaction

noun
type of chemical reaction involving ion exchange
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌbəl rɪˈpleɪsmənt riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl rɪˈpleɪs.mənt riˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which the ions of two compounds exchange places to form two new compounds

Examples

A double replacement reaction forms compounds
A double replacement reaction often results in the formation of a precipitate or gas.
In a double replacement reaction, silver nitrate reacts with sodium chloride to form silver chloride and sodium nitrate.
Teachers use clear demonstrations to show how a double replacement reaction occurs between two salt solutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: precipitation reaction - a chemical reaction resulting in the formation of a solid from solution
  • antonym: single replacement reaction - a reaction where one element replaces another in a compound
