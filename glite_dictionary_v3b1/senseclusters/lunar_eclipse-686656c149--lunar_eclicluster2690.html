lunar eclipse

noun
when the moon passes through Earth's shadow
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈluː.nər ɪ.ˈklɪps/ /; / /ˈluː.nər iː.ˈklɪps/ (AmE), /ˈljuː.nə ɪ.ˈklɪps/ /; / /ˈljuː.nə iː.ˈklɪps/ (BrE)
an occasion when the moon passes into the Earth's shadow, causing the moon to appear darkened or reddish in color

Examples

A lunar eclipse event
A lunar eclipse occurs when the Earth moves between the sun and the moon.
Last night’s lunar eclipse lasted several hours and was visible across multiple continents.
Many cultures viewed a lunar eclipse as a significant event in the night sky.
