cathartically

adverb
by purging physically
9.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑrtɪkli / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that causes physical purging or evacuation of the bowels, especially by a laxative.

Examples

Act cathartically on digestion
The medicine acted cathartically, providing relief from constipation within a few hours.
After taking the herbal remedy, his system responded cathartically as expected.
Doctors sometimes prescribe treatments that work cathartically to cleanse the digestive tract before procedures.
