interventionist

noun
supporter of involvement
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or takes active steps to become involved in the affairs of others, especially in politics or international relations

Examples

A political interventionist
The interventionist believed that government action was necessary to solve the economic crisis.
As an interventionist, she advocated for international involvement in humanitarian issues.
Critics argued that the interventionist approach could lead to unwanted consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: activist - a person who takes action to bring about change
  • antonym: noninterventionist - a person who opposes involvement in others' affairs
