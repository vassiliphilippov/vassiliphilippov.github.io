descriptivism

noun
doctrine describing actual language use
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskrɪptɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪptɪvɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine or practice in linguistics that aims to objectively describe how language is actually used, rather than prescribing how it should be used.

Examples

Linguistic descriptivism
His teaching was grounded in descriptivism, reflecting actual language usage rather than idealized forms.
Modern linguistics largely embraces descriptivism rather than trying to enforce strict language rules.
The debate between descriptivism and prescriptivism is common among grammar enthusiasts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prescriptivism - the doctrine of prescribing how language should be used
