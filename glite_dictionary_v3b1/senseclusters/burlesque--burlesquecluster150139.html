burlesque

verb
parody or mock through exaggerated imitation
8.3 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùÀêÀàl…õsk / (AmE), / b…úÀêÀàl…õsk / (BrE)
to imitate or represent someone or something in a way that exaggerates or mocks them, often for comic effect

Examples

To burlesque a play
In the play, several characters were cleverly burlesqued for comic relief.
The comedian burlesqued famous politicians in his new stand-up routine.
Their skit burlesqued the overly dramatic scenes in classic soap operas.

Synonyms

  • parody
    5.3 1.60/m
    parody copies a work's style and exaggerates its traits to make fun, while burlesque often mocks and exaggerates people or actions in performance.
  • spoof
    6.3 0.30/m
    spoof makes a playful imitation of a film or story to get laughs, while burlesque often focuses on mocking people with exaggeration.
  • take off
    4.2 0.58/m
    take off imitates someone's voice or manner in a funny, casual way, while burlesque is often more theatrical and sharply mocking.
  • mock
    3.4 9.98/m
    mock makes fun of someone with ridicule or contempt, while burlesque usually aims for comic exaggeration rather than pure insult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parody - to imitate the style of someone or something for comic effect
