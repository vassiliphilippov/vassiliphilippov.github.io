occupied

adjective
having tenants or inhabitants
2.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêkj…ôÀåpa…™d / (AmE), / Àà…íkj äÀåpa…™d / (BrE)
(of a room, building, or land) being lived in, used, or leased by someone; having tenants or inhabitants.

Examples

Occupied apartment
During the festival, every hotel room in town was occupied.
Most of the occupied apartments in the building were well maintained.
The house has remained occupied since it was built a century ago.

Synonyms

  • inhabited
    2.9 0.00/m
    inhabited means people or animals live in a place, while occupied often refers to a room or land that is used or leased.
  • tenanted
    5.5 0.00/m
    tenanted means a property is lived in by tenants who pay rent, while occupied is a more general word for being used or lived in.
  • populated
    2.5 0.00/m
    populated means an area is filled with people or animals, often noting how many live there, while occupied focuses on use or tenancy.
