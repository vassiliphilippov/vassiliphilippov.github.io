operant

noun
behavior causing effect
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːpərənt / (AmE), / ˈɒpərənt / (BrE)
an organism or behavior that produces an effect on the environment, especially as studied in psychology

Examples

A trained operant
In the experiment, the operant was trained to press a button to receive a reward.
Psychologists study how an operant changes its behavior in response to consequences.
The rat became the operant by learning to navigate the maze for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actor - a person or thing that takes action
  • antonym: respondent - an organism or behavior that reacts to a stimulus
