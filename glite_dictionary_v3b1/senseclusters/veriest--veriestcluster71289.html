veriest

adjective
most absolute (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriɪst / (AmE), / ˈvɛriɪst / (BrE)
being the most complete or absolute, used to emphasize an extreme degree, now archaic.

Examples

The veriest coward
He was the veriest coward, running from even the smallest challenge.
In the veriest darkness of night, not a single star could be seen.
She told the veriest lie, hoping no one would discover the truth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: least - the smallest in amount or degree
  • synonym: uttermost - the greatest or most extreme degree
