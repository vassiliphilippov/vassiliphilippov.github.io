mushroom coral

noun
a flattened, disk-shaped stony coral
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌʃˌruːm ˈkɔr.əl / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃ.rʊm ˈkɒː.rəl / (BrE)
a type of stony coral with a flattened, disk-shaped body, usually solitary and unattached to other surfaces

Examples

A mushroom coral colony
A single mushroom coral can reach impressive sizes despite being unattached to rocks.
Divers marveled at the vibrant colors of the mushroom coral growing on the reef.
Marine biologists study how mushroom coral populations respond to changing ocean temperatures.
