unprotesting

adjective, adverb
accepting without protest
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnprəˈtɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnprəˈtɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
not showing objection or resistance; accepting something without protest (as an adjective or adverb)

Examples

Unprotesting acceptance
He sat unprotesting as the teacher assigned extra homework to the class.
The child remained unprotesting while the nurse gave her a vaccination.
Despite the unfair decision, she was unprotesting and quietly accepted the outcome.
She listened unprotesting as her manager explained the new policy.
The children sat unprotesting during the long ceremony, even though they were bored.
He followed the instructions unprotesting, not wanting to cause any trouble.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compliant - willing to do what others want without resistance
  • antonym: defiant - openly resisting or refusing to obey
  • synonym: obediently - in a way that follows orders or rules without resistance
  • synonym: passively - in a way that accepts or allows what happens without active response
  • antonym: resistingly - in a way that shows opposition or resistance
  • synonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others
