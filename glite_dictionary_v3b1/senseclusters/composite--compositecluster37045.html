composite

noun
combined material
4.9
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɑːzɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
a material made by combining two or more different substances to create a product with improved properties

Examples

Composite material
Modern bicycles often use a composite for the frame to make them lighter and stronger.
Engineers selected a special composite to withstand high temperatures in the spacecraft.
The new bridge was built using a durable composite instead of traditional concrete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alloy - a mixture of two or more metals
  • antonym: element - a pure chemical substance that cannot be broken down
  • synonym: mixture - a combination of different substances
