casus

noun
unexpected event
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsəs / (AmE), / ˈkeɪsəs / (BrE)
an accident, misfortune, or unexpected event, especially one with significant consequences (archaic)

Examples

A tragic casus
The villagers spoke in hushed tones about the strange casus that ruined the harvest.
A sudden casus forced the travelers to change their route unexpectedly.
In old legal texts, a casus often referred to an unforeseen circumstance affecting the outcome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accident - an unexpected event, often causing harm or damage
  • synonym: fortune - luck, especially when resulting in an unexpected event
  • antonym: plan - a deliberate arrangement or intention
