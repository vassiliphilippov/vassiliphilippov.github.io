smoke

noun
cloud of particles from burning
1.0
Pronunciation: / smoʊk / (AmE), / sməʊk / (BrE)
a visible cloud of fine particles and gases produced by burning something

Examples

Thick smoke rises
As the grill heated up, a plume of smoke rose from the coals.
Cigarette smoke filled the small room, making it hard to breathe.
The thick smoke from the fire could be seen for miles.
After a few minutes, white smoke emerged from the chimney, signaling the fire was lit.
Black smoke billowed from the burning building and drifted into the sky.
The campers gathered around the fire, coughing as the smoke blew in their direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fume - gas or vapor that smells strongly or is dangerous to inhale
  • degree lower: mist - fine spray or light fog suspended in the air
