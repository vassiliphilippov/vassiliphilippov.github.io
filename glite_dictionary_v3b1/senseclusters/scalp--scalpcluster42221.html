scalp

verb
sell tickets or goods illegally at high price
Pronunciation: / skælp / (AmE), / skælp / (BrE)
to sell tickets or goods, especially illegally, at a price higher than the official one

Examples

To scalp tickets
He tried to scalp concert tickets outside the stadium.
It is illegal to scalp tickets on the street without a license.
Two people were arrested for attempting to scalp seats to the sold-out show.
He was caught scalping tickets outside the stadium before the concert began.
Some people make money by scalping limited-edition sneakers online.
Authorities are cracking down on those scalping event passes for inflated prices.

  • synonym: resell - to sell something again after buying it
