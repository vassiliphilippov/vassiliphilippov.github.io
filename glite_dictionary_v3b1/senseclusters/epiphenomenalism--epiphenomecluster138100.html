epiphenomenalism

noun
mind as by-product
9.9
Pronunciation: /e.pɪ.fə.ˈnɑː.mə.nəl.ɪz/ (AmE), /e.pɪ.fə.ˈnɒm.ə.nəl.ɪz/ (BrE)
the theory that mental events are caused by physical processes in the brain but do not themselves influence physical events

Examples

Epiphenomenalism theory
Some philosophers argue that epiphenomenalism cannot explain how thoughts influence actions.
The debate over epiphenomenalism continues in discussions about the mind-body problem.
Critics of epiphenomenalism claim it ignores the apparent effects of mental states on behavior.
Many philosophers have discussed epiphenomenalism as a possible explanation for the relationship between mind and body.
Critics argue that epiphenomenalism cannot account for the apparent influence of thoughts on actions.
The debate over epiphenomenalism continues in both philosophy and neuroscience today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dualism - the belief that mind and body are distinct and separable
  • antonym: materialism - the doctrine that only physical matter exists
