take turns

verb
alternate doing something
1.0 1.6
Pronunciation: /teɪk tɜːrnz/ (AmE), /teɪk tɜːnz/ (BrE)
(of two or more people) to do something one after another, usually by alternating or sharing the activity

Examples

To take turns speaking
Please take turns answering the questions so everyone has a chance to speak.
The children take turns playing with the dog in the backyard.
We took turns driving so neither of us got too tired during the long trip.
After dinner, the children take turns washing the dishes and sweeping the floor.
During the meeting, everyone took turns sharing their ideas for the new project.
We should take turns driving on the long road trip so no one gets too tired.
