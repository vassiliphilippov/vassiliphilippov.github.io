inchoative aspect

noun
grammatical aspect marking beginning of action
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkoʊətɪv ˈæspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊətɪv ˈæspekt / (BrE)
(in linguistics) an aspect of a verb that expresses the beginning or initiation of an action or state.

Examples

The inchoative aspect marker
Scholars debated whether a separate marker for the inchoative aspect exists in the old texts.
Some Slavic languages explicitly mark the inchoative aspect to distinguish it from ongoing actions.
The verb form in this language indicates the inchoative aspect, meaning the action is just starting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: perfective aspect - aspect expressing a completed action
  • antonym: progressive aspect - aspect indicating ongoing action
