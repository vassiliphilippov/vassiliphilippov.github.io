John B. Watson

proper noun
founder of behavioristic psychology
Pronunciation: / dʒɑn bi ˈwɑtsən / (AmE), / dʒɒn bi ˈwɒtsən / (BrE)
(1878–1958) United States psychologist known as the founder of behavioristic psychology.

Examples

John B. Watson psychologist
John B. Watson proposed that psychology should focus on observable behavior rather than internal mental states.
Many university courses in psychology discuss the contributions of John B. Watson to behaviorism.
The theories of John B. Watson had a significant influence on 20th-century psychological research.
