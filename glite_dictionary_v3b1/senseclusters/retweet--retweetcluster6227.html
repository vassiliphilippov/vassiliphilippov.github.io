retweet

verb
to share a post
Pronunciation: / riˈtwiːt /; / ˌriːˈtwiːt / (AmE), / riˈtwiːt /; / ˌriːˈtwiːt / (BrE)
to share or repost someone else's tweet or post on Twitter (or a similar social media platform) so your followers can see it

Examples

To retweet a message
She retweeted the news article so her friends could read it too.
Many users retweet funny memes to spread them quickly across the platform.
He accidentally retweeted an old post from last year.
If you find an interesting article, you can retweet it so your followers see it too.
He often retweets news updates from official sources to keep his audience informed.
Many people retweet funny memes to share them with their friends.
She decided to retweet the announcement so more people would see it.
Many users retweet funny memes to share them with their followers.
If you agree with the message, feel free to retweet it.
