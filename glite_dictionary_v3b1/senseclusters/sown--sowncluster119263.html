sown

adjective
land with seeds scattered or planted
Pronunciation: / soʊn / (AmE), / səʊn / (BrE)
(of land) having had seeds scattered or planted on it.

Examples

Land sown with seeds
The gardener checked that every section of the garden had been properly sown.
The newly sown field needs regular watering to help the seeds germinate.
Wildflowers sprouted all over the sown meadow by summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seeded - with seeds sown or scattered
  • antonym: unsown - not having been sown with seed
