Sophora secundiflora

noun
an evergreen shrub/tree with fragrant blue flowers and red seeds
9.9
Pronunciation: / soʊˈfɔrə sɪˌkʌndɪˈflɔrə / (AmE), / səˈfɔːrə sɪˌkʌndɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, with evergreen pinnate leaves, fragrant blue or violet flowers in dense clusters, and bright red poisonous seeds.

Examples

Sophora secundiflora shrub
Children should avoid the bright red seeds of Sophora secundiflora because they are highly poisonous.
Every spring, the Sophora secundiflora fills the air with a scent similar to grape bubblegum.
The Sophora secundiflora is commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel in gardening and horticultural circles.
