noninformative

adjective
lacking useful information
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɪnfərˈmeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪnfəˈmeɪtɪv / (BrE)
not providing useful, helpful, or relevant information

Examples

A noninformative message
The instructions were so noninformative that I could not assemble the furniture.
Her answer was noninformative and left everyone confused about the next steps.
We found the website noninformative because it lacked details about the event.
The analyst explained that a noninformative prior would not influence the results of the Bayesian model.
Because the survey responses were mostly blank, the data set was considered noninformative.
Using a noninformative approach allows the evidence to come entirely from the observed data.

Related Terms

  • antonym: informative - providing useful or helpful information
  • synonym: uninformative - not giving useful or helpful information
