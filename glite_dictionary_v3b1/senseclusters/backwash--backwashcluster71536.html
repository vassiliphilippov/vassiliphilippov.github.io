backwash

noun
wave or disturbed water behind a boat
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌwɑʃ /; / ˈbækwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌwɒʃ /; / ˈbækwɒʃ / (BrE)
the flow of water or air moving backward behind something, such as a boat, aircraft, or wave

Examples

Boat backwash
Swimmers are warned to stay clear of the ferry's backwash when it docks.
The ducks glided smoothly through the backwash left by the passing canoe.
The large yacht's backwash rocked the small fishing boat gently.
Make sure to stand clear of the backwash when the aircraft is preparing for takeoff.
Strong backwash from the engines can create hazardous conditions on the tarmac.
The pilot felt the backwash as he walked behind the spinning propeller.
The backwash pulled the sand away from my feet as the wave receded.
Children laughed as the backwash swirled around their ankles on the shore.
After the wave crashed, the backwash carried small shells back into the sea.

Related Terms

  • antonym: swash - the movement of water up the beach after a wave breaks
  • synonym: undertow - a current of water below the surface moving away from shore
  • synonym: wake - the trail of disturbed water left by a boat or ship
