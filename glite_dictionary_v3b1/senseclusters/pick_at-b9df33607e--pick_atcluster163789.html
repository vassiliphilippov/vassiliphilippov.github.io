pick at

verb
eat reluctantly; do something reluctantly
Pronunciation: / pɪk æt / (AmE), / pɪk æt / (BrE)
to eat only a small amount of food or to do something in a slow or reluctant way, often without enthusiasm

Examples

To pick at work
He kept picking at his project instead of finishing it quickly.
Stop picking at your food and eat something substantial.
Whenever she feels upset, she only picks at her meals without really eating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nibble - to take small bites of food, usually in a hesitant or uninterested way
