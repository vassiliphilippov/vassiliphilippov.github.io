embryo

noun
undeveloped plant inside a seed (botany)
1.4 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈem.briː.oʊ/ /; / /ˈem.brɪ.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈem.brɪ.əʊ/ /; / /ˈem.briː.əʊ/ (BrE)
(Botany) A minute undeveloped plant contained within a seed or an archegonium, capable of growing into a mature plant.

Examples

Seed embryo
A pine seed contains both stored food and an embryo ready to develop into a sapling.
The embryo inside the seed begins to grow when optimal moisture and temperature conditions are reached.
Under a microscope, scientists examined the early stages of the plant embryo.
