novation

noun
replacement of contract or obligation in law
Pronunciation: / noʊˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / nəʊˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) The substitution of a new contract, obligation, or party for an existing one, with the agreement of all relevant parties.

Examples

A novation agreement
A novation can simplify complex business arrangements by replacing old agreements.
The merger required a novation to officially transfer the contractual rights.
The parties agreed to a novation that introduced a new supplier to the contract.
