X chromosome

noun
sex chromosome found in both males and females
Pronunciation: / ɛks ˈkɹoʊmoʊˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ɛks ˈkrəʊməsəʊm / (BrE)
the sex chromosome present in both males and females, singly in males and doubly in females, that determines sex-linked traits

Examples

The X chromosome gene
Abnormalities in the X chromosome can lead to various genetic disorders.
Females typically have two X chromosomes in each cell, while males have only one.
The X chromosome carries many genes unrelated to determining sex.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Y chromosome - the other human sex chromosome, present only in males
