semisedentary

adjective
partly settled
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmisaɪˈdɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌsɛmisaɪˈdɛntəri / (BrE)
partly settled in one place but also moving periodically, not fully sedentary or fully nomadic

Examples

A semisedentary community
The semisedentary community farmed during the summer but traveled to hunt in the winter.
Many ancient peoples were semisedentary, combining settled agriculture with seasonal migration.
Researchers studied the semisedentary lifestyle of groups who moved only part of the year.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: nomadic - moving from place to place without a permanent home
  • degree higher: sedentary - living in one place and not moving around
