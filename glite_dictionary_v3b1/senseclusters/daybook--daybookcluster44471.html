daybook

noun
accounting journal for daily transactions
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ.bʊk / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.bʊk / (BrE)
a book used to record daily financial transactions or business activities before they are entered into the main ledger

Examples

The daybook record
After correcting an error, he updated the daybook to reflect the new figures.
She glanced at the old daybook to check last month's entries.
The accountant made sure every sale was written in the daybook before the day ended.
Before the ledger is updated, all transactions are first noted in the daybook.
She reviewed the previous week's entries in the daybook to ensure accuracy.
The accountant entered each sale into the daybook at the close of business.
