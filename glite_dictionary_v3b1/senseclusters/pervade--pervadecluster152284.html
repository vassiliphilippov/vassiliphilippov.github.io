pervade

verb
spread through every part of something
8.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈveɪd/ /; / /pə.ˈveɪd/ (AmE), /pə.ˈveɪd/ (BrE)
(of a feeling, influence, or quality) to spread through and be perceived in every part of a place or thing.

Examples

To pervade the room
A sense of excitement seemed to pervade the entire stadium before the big game.
Questions about the future pervade every discussion these days.
The smell of fresh paint pervaded the newly renovated office.

Related Terms
