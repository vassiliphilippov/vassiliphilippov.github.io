immoderately

adverb
excessively; beyond reasonable limits
7.0 7.6
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɑdɚətli / (AmE), / ɪˈmɒdərətli / (BrE)
in a way that is excessive or goes beyond what is reasonable or moderate

Examples

Spend immoderately on things
He spent immoderately on luxury items, far beyond his means.
The dessert was immoderately sweet, making it difficult to enjoy more than a few bites.
The politician spoke immoderately, ignoring the time limits set for the debate.
Eating immoderately can lead to various health issues over time.
He spent immoderately on luxury items, soon exhausting his savings.
The critics argued that the film relied immoderately on special effects, overshadowing the story.
