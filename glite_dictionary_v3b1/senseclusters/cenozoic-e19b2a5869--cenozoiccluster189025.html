Cenozoic

noun
the current geological era
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛn.əˈzoʊ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌsiː.nəˈzəʊ.ɪk / (BrE)
the current geological era, beginning about 66 million years ago, characterized by the development of mammals and birds

Examples

The Cenozoic era
Geological evidence from the Cenozoic provides insight into how Earth's climate has changed.
Many modern mammals evolved during the Cenozoic period.
The extinction of the dinosaurs marked the beginning of the Cenozoic era.
