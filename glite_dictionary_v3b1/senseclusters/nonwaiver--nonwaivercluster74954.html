nonwaiver

adjective
preserving legal rights
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈweɪvər / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈweɪvə / (BrE)
not intended to give up or relinquish a legal right or claim; preserving a party's legal rights.

Examples

Nonwaiver clause
The contract included a nonwaiver clause to ensure the company retained its rights.
A nonwaiver agreement was signed so that neither party would lose any legal claims.
The lawyer explained that the nonwaiver provision protected their interests during negotiations.
