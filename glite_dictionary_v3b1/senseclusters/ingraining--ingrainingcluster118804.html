ingraining

noun
impressing something firmly by repetition
7.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈgreɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ɪŋ.ˈgreɪ.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈgreɪ.nɪŋ/ /; / /ɪŋ.ˈgreɪ.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
The process of teaching or impressing something firmly upon the mind by frequent instruction or repetition.

Examples

The ingraining of habits
Cultural values are often passed down by the ingraining of traditions over generations.
The ingraining of good habits in children requires patience and consistency.
Through persistent ingraining of safety procedures, accidents were reduced in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implanting - the process of firmly establishing something (idea, habit, etc.) in the mind or being
  • synonym: instilling - gradually establishing an idea or attitude in a person's mind
