southern red oak

noun
a large North American deciduous tree with deeply lobed leaves
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌðɚn rɛd oʊk / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən rɛd əʊk / (BrE)
a large deciduous tree of eastern and southeastern North America, with spreading branches and narrow, deeply lobed leaves, known for its round-topped crown and wood similar to that of northern red oak

Examples

A large southern red oak
A massive southern red oak shaded the front yard of the old farmhouse.
The leaves of the southern red oak are narrower and more deeply lobed than those of some other oaks.
Wood from the southern red oak is valued for furniture and flooring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: northern red oak - a similar species of red oak found in the northern United States
