suboptimization

noun
partial system optimization
9.6
Pronunciation: /səb.ɑːp.tɪ.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /səb.ɔːp.tɪ.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /səb.ɒp.tɪ.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sʌb.ɒp.tɪ.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of optimizing a part of a system in a way that reduces the effectiveness of the whole system

Examples

System suboptimization
The manager's focus on one team resulted in suboptimization of the entire project.
When departments compete for resources, suboptimization can harm the company's overall goals.
Effective leaders avoid suboptimization by considering the needs of the whole organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: local optimization - improving a specific part of a system without considering the whole
  • antonym: optimization - the process of making something as effective or functional as possible
