cytokinesis

noun
division of a cell's cytoplasm
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪtəʊkɪˈniːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪtəʊkɪˈniːsɪs / (BrE)
the division of the cytoplasm of a cell following nuclear division, resulting in two daughter cells

Examples

Cell cytokinesis process
Defects in cytokinesis can result in abnormal cell growth or cancer.
During cytokinesis, the cell's cytoplasm splits to form two separate cells.
Plant cells undergo cytokinesis differently from animal cells, often forming a cell plate.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: karyokinesis - division of a cell's nucleus during cell division
  • synonym: mitosis - cell division resulting in two identical daughter cells
