molecular pump

noun
protein or device that transports molecules across membranes
5.1
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈlek.jə.lər pʌmp/ /; / /mə.ˈlek.jʊ.lər pʌmp/ (AmE), /mə.ˈlek.jʊ.lə pʌmp/ /; / /mə.ˈlek.jə.lə pʌmp/ (BrE)
(in biology and nanotechnology) a molecule, protein complex, or synthetic nano‑machine that uses energy to move ions or other molecules across a cellular or artificial membrane.

Examples

Cell molecular pump
Researchers designed a synthetic molecular pump that transports ions across an artificial lipid bilayer.
The sodium–potassium molecular pump helps maintain the cell's electrochemical gradient and resting potential.
During the lecture, she explained how ATP powers each molecular pump in the cell membrane to move molecules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ion pump - a protein that moves ions across a membrane, often using energy like ATP
  • synonym: membrane transporter - a protein that moves substances across biological membranes, passive or active
