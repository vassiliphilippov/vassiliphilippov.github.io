dare

verb
to have courage to do something
Pronunciation: / dɛr / (AmE), / dɛə / (BrE)
to have enough courage to do something risky, difficult, or considered improper

Examples

To dare to speak
He didn't dare speak up in the meeting, even though he disagreed.
She dares to question traditional ways in her art.
Would you dare to jump off the high diving board?
Do you dare interrupt the meeting again without permission?
How dare you question my honesty in front of everyone?
She didn't dare defy the manager's instructions, even though she disagreed.

  • synonym: challenge - to invite someone to take part in a contest; a closely related verb
  • antonym: fear - to be afraid or worried
  • synonym: presume - to act without permission or take liberties
