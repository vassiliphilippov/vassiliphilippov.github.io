temperate zone

noun
region between tropics and polar circles; mild climate
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpərət zoʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərət zəʊn / (BrE)
the region of the Earth's surface lying between the tropics and the polar circles, characterized by mild or moderate climate

Examples

The temperate zone region
Britain is located within the temperate zone and experiences mild summers and winters.
Crops that require cooler weather often thrive in the temperate zone.
Many of the world's major cities are found in the temperate zone due to its favorable climate.
