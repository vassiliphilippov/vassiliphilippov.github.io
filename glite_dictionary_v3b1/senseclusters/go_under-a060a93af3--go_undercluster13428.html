to sink below the surface, especially of water, and often be submerged completely

Examples

To go under water
After the storm, several small dinghies went under near the dock.
If you don't keep swimming, you might go under in the deep lake.
The old fishing boat began to go under as water filled the hull.

Synonyms

  • go down
    3.8 2.49/m
    go down is often used for ships that sink slowly near the surface, while go under means becoming fully submerged.
  • sink
    1.0 15/m
    sink is the common verb for going below a liquid's surface, while go under often means being fully submerged.
  • submerge
    3.9 4.19/m
    submerge is used to mean putting or going completely under water, while go under sounds more everyday and simple.
