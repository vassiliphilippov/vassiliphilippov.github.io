ecclesiastical

adjective
relating to the Christian Church or clergy
9.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˌklisiˈæstɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːsiˈæstɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the Christian Church or its clergy; associated with organized church activities, institutions, or beliefs.

Examples

Ecclesiastical ceremonies
Ancient manuscripts revealed details about the ecclesiastical ceremonies of the early church.
He devoted his entire career to ecclesiastical law and advising church leaders.
The cathedral is renowned for its ecclesiastical architecture and ornate stained glass windows.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
