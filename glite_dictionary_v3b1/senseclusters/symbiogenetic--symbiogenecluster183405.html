symbiogenetic

adjective
from merging organisms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmbaɪoʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbɪəʊdʒəˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resulting from symbiogenesis, the merging of two different organisms to form a new organism

Examples

symbiogenetic origin
The symbiogenetic origin of chloroplasts is widely accepted in biology.
Researchers study symbiogenetic processes to understand how complex cells evolved.
The theory suggests that mitochondria are the result of a symbiogenetic event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independent - not relying on or resulting from another organism
  • synonym: symbiotic - involving interaction between two different organisms living together
