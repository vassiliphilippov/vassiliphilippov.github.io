ramie

noun
fiber-yielding Asian plant
Pronunciation: / ˈræmi, ˈreɪmi / (AmE), / ˈrami, ˈreɪmi / (BrE)
a tall perennial plant native to tropical Asia, cultivated for its strong, silky fibers obtained from its woody stems

Examples

The ramie plant
Farmers in China have cultivated ramie for centuries due to its valuable fiber.
She prefers summer shirts made of ramie because they are cool and breathable.
The durable fabric was woven from ramie harvested in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flax - plant grown for its textile fiber, similar in use to ramie
