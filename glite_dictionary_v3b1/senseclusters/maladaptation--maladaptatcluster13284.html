maladaptation

noun
harmful adjustment
Pronunciation: / ˌmælˌædæpˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmælˌædæpˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a way of behaving, responding, or adjusting that is harmful or not suitable for a situation or environment

Examples

A maladaptation in behaviour
The therapist noted that the client's avoidance represented a clear maladaptation in social situations.
Chronic stress can lead to maladaptation and negatively affect both mental and physical health.
His maladaptation to the new environment made it difficult for him to form friendships.
The animal's maladaptation to the changing environment made survival difficult.
Psychologists study how maladaptation can affect a person's mental health.
Rapid urbanization sometimes leads to maladaptation in local wildlife populations.
Prolonged maladaptation to a new school environment can lead to academic and social difficulties.
The species' maladaptation to climate change has put it at risk of extinction.
His maladaptation after moving to a different country affected his well-being for months.
