Geiger-Muller tube

noun
ionization tube in Geiger counter
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪɡər ˈmʊlər tuːb / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪɡə ˈmʌlə tjuːb / (BrE)
A type of ionization tube used in a Geiger counter to detect ionizing radiation.

Examples

A Geiger-Muller tube detector
A faulty Geiger-Muller tube can cause inaccurate readings when measuring radioactivity levels.
Technicians replaced the damaged Geiger-Muller tube to restore the laboratory's radiation detector to working order.
The Geiger-Muller tube is the essential component that makes Geiger counters sensitive to radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ionization chamber - device that detects ionizing particles
