immunity

noun
being unaffected by something
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
The condition or quality of being unaffected or not influenced by something.

Examples

Immunity to criticism
His strong sense of self gave him a surprising immunity to criticism from his peers.
She seemed to have developed an emotional immunity to negative comments over the years.
The politician displayed a remarkable immunity to public opinion, refusing to change his stance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invulnerability - being immune to harm or attack
  • antonym: susceptibility - the quality of being easily affected or influenced
