parakeratosis

noun
skin cell abnormality
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌparəkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a skin condition in which cells in the outer layer of the skin retain their nuclei instead of losing them

Examples

Skin parakeratosis
The dermatologist explained that parakeratosis can be seen in certain types of eczema.
A skin biopsy revealed parakeratosis along with other signs of inflammation.
Treatment was adjusted after the doctor identified parakeratosis in the patient's skin sample.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal keratinization - the usual process where skin cells lose their nuclei as they move outward
