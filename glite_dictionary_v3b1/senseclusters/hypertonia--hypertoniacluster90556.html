hypertonia

noun
abnormally increased muscle tone
7.6 7.7
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.ˈtoʊ.niː.ə/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈtəʊ.niː.ə/ (BrE)
A condition in which there is an abnormal increase in muscle tone, resulting in greater than normal tension or resistance to movement in the muscles.

Examples

Muscle hypertonia condition
Children with cerebral palsy often experience hypertonia in their limbs.
Physical therapy can help manage symptoms of hypertonia in recovering stroke patients.
The patient's muscles were stiff due to hypertonia, making movement difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypotonia - decreased or abnormally low muscle tone
  • synonym: spasticity - a condition with increased muscle tone and reflexes
