pseudomorphosis

noun
mineral shape retention
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˈmɔːrfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈmɔːfəsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which a mineral changes its chemical composition but keeps the external shape of the original mineral

Examples

Mineral pseudomorphosis
The geologist explained how pseudomorphosis allowed the mineral to keep its original crystal shape.
Through pseudomorphosis, the rock's appearance remained unchanged despite its new chemical makeup.
Museum specimens often display pseudomorphosis where one mineral has replaced another over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replacement - the act of substituting one thing for another
  • synonym: transformation - a complete change in form or appearance
