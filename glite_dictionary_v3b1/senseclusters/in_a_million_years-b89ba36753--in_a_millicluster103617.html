in a million years

adverb
to emphasize extreme improbability
2.4 3.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn ə ˈmɪl.jən jɪrz/ (AmE), /ɪn ə ˈmɪl.jən jɪəz/ (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is extremely unlikely or impossible to happen, either now or at any time in the future

Examples

Not in a million years
He said he wouldn't betray her in a million years.
I would never in a million years expect you to lie to me.
She couldn't imagine moving away from her family in a million years.
