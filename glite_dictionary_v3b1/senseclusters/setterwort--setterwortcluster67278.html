setterwort

noun
a type of hellebore plant with an unpleasant smell
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛtərˌwɔrt / (AmE), / ˈsɛtəˌwɔːt / (BrE)
A hellebore plant with digitate leaves, an unpleasant smell, and irritating qualities if ingested.

Examples

The setterwort plant
If ingested, setterwort can cause severe irritation and should be handled with care.
The roots of setterwort are known for their strong medicinal effects, though toxicity is a risk.
Victorian gardeners sometimes planted setterwort for its unusual foliage despite its foul smell.
