recast

verb
to assign a new role to an actor
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈkæst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkɑːst / (BrE)
to assign (an actor) a different role, or to select a new actor for a role in a play, film, or television production

Examples

To recast the role
After the lead actor quit, the director decided to recast the part entirely.
Due to creative differences, the studio had to recast several supporting roles.
The producer chose to recast her as the film's antagonist for the sequel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cast - to select actors for roles in a play or film
