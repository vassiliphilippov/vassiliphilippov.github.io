bogbean

noun
a marsh plant with white or purplish flowers
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔɡˌbin / (AmE), / ˈbɒɡˌbiːn / (BrE)
a perennial marsh plant of Europe and North America, with racemes of white or purplish flowers and bitter trifoliate leaves, often growing at water margins.

Examples

Bogbean plant leaves
Gardeners admire the floating leaves of bogbean in ponds and marshy areas.
Many herbalists once used bogbean leaves for making bitter remedies.
The wetland was covered in bogbean with delicate white blossoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Menyanthes trifoliata - the scientific name for bogbean
