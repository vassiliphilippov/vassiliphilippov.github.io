detainer

noun
legal custody order
Pronunciation: / dɪˈteɪnər / (AmE), / dɪˈteɪnə / (BrE)
a legal order that authorizes keeping someone in custody or preventing their release from detention

Examples

Prison detainer order
The prison received a detainer from another state, requiring them to hold the inmate for extradition.
After the trial, a new detainer was filed to prevent the suspect's immediate release.
Immigration authorities placed a detainer on the individual pending further investigation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from custody
  • synonym: warrant - an official document authorizing an action, such as arrest or search
