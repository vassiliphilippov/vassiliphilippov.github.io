lodicule

noun
grass flower scale
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑdɪˌkjul /; / ˈloʊdɪˌkjul / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪˌkjuːl /; / ˈlɒdɪˌkjuːl / (BrE)
a small, scale-like structure at the base of a grass flower that swells to help the flower open during blooming

Examples

The lodicule swells
The lodicule swells with water to help the grass flower open during blooming.
Scientists studied the function of the lodicule in different species of wheat.
Without a healthy lodicule, the grass flower may not open properly.
The lodicule swells with water to help the grass flower open during blooming.
