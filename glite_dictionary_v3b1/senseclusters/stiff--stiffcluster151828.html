stiff

adjective
(of a drink) strong or potent
3.7
Pronunciation: / stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
strong or forceful, especially of wind, drinks, or similar things

Examples

Stiff wind
After a long day, he poured himself a stiff drink to relax.
She prefers her cocktails stiff rather than sweet.
The tea was so stiff that it kept me awake all night.
A stiff wind kept the boats in the harbor rocking all night.
After the rain passed, a stiff current pushed the debris downstream.
The hikers battled a stiff breeze as they reached the hilltop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentle - moderate in force or strength (of wind or current)
  • synonym: potent - having a strong effect; powerful or effective
  • synonym: strong - having great force or power
