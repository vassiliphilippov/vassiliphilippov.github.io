dislodgeable

adjective
able to be removed
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈlɑːdʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɒdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be removed from a place or position, especially with some effort

Examples

Easily dislodgeable object
The old paint was barely dislodgeable from the wooden surface after so many years.
Make sure the shelves are not easily dislodgeable before placing heavy items on them.
Some rocks in the riverbed are more dislodgeable than others after heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fixed - firmly attached and not able to be moved
  • synonym: removable - able to be taken away or separated from a place
