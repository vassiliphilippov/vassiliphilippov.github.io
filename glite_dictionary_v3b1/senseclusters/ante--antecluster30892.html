ante

verb
put up a stake in betting
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.ti /; / ˈænti / (AmE), / ˈæn.ti /; / ˈænti / (BrE)
to put up a stake or bet at the start of a card game

Examples

To ante five dollars
Each player must ante five dollars before the cards are dealt.
He forgot to ante and was reminded by the dealer.
If you want to stay in the game, you have to ante up.
Before the cards were dealt, each player anted five dollars into the pot.
He anted up quickly, eager to stay in the game.
If you want to play, you have to ante the minimum amount first.
Before the first hand, each player anteed a small amount to start the pot.
He anteed five dollars, hoping his cards would be strong enough to win.
They anteed quickly so the poker game could begin without delay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bet - to risk money on the outcome of an event or game
  • antonym: fold - to withdraw from a round of betting in a card game
  • synonym: raise - to increase the amount of a bet in gambling
