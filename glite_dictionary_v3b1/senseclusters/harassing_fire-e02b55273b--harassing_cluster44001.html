harassing fire

noun
military: fire to disturb and demoralize enemy
6.0 9.3
Pronunciation: / həˈræsɪŋ ˈfaɪər / (AmE), / həˈræsɪŋ ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
gunfire or artillery fire intended to disturb and fatigue enemy troops, restrict their movements, and reduce morale, rather than to inflict heavy casualties

Examples

Harassing fire attack
Harassing fire was used to disrupt enemy supply lines and prevent repairs to their defenses.
The artillery unit laid down harassing fire throughout the night to keep the opposing troops awake.
The commander ordered a pattern of harassing fire to unsettle the advancing enemy forces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suppressive fire - gunfire intended to hinder enemy action by keeping them under threat
