unship

verb
to unload cargo
8.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃɪp / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɪp / (BrE)
to remove or unload goods, cargo, or equipment from a ship or to take something off its mountings or out of service

Examples

To unship cargo
The workers began to unship the containers as soon as the vessel docked.
It took several hours to unship all the equipment from the truck after the long journey.
They carefully unshipped the fragile items to avoid any damage during unloading.
The crew unshipped the cargo as soon as the vessel reached the port.
Before repairs could begin, the engineers unshipped the damaged propeller from its mount.
All lifeboats were unshipped and checked for safety before the voyage.
The crew will unship the lifeboats for maintenance before the next voyage.
After reaching the harbor, they began to unship the heavy machinery from the deck.
It took several hours to unship all the supplies from the vessel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disembark - to leave a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle
  • antonym: embark - to put or take something onto a ship or vehicle
  • antonym: load - to put goods or cargo onto a vehicle or container
  • synonym: unload - to remove goods from a vehicle or container
