hyperviscosity

noun
abnormally thick blood
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərvɪsˈkɑsəti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəvɪsˈkɒsəti / (BrE)
the condition of having abnormally thick or sticky blood or other body fluid

Examples

Blood hyperviscosity
The doctor explained that hyperviscosity can cause problems with blood flow in the body.
Patients with hyperviscosity may experience headaches, vision changes, or bleeding.
Treatment for hyperviscosity often involves reducing the thickness of the blood.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypoviscosity - the condition of having abnormally thin or less sticky blood or fluid
  • synonym: viscosity - the thickness or stickiness of a liquid
