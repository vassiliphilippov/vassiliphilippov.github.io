stomp

verb
walk with heavy, noisy steps
Pronunciation: / stɑmp / (AmE), / stɒmp / (BrE)
to walk heavily and noisily, usually showing anger or determination

Examples

To stomp upstairs
He was so upset that he stomped up the stairs without saying a word.
She stomped out of the room, slamming the door behind her.
The children stomped through the puddles in their boots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stamp - to bring down one's foot heavily; to walk with heavy steps
