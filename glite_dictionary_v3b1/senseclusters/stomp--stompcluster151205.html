stomp

verb
walk with heavy, noisy steps
2.9 6.65/m
Pronunciation: / stÉ‘mp / (AmE), / stÉ’mp / (BrE)
to walk heavily and noisily, usually showing anger or determination

Examples

To stomp upstairs
He was so upset that he stomped up the stairs without saying a word.
She stomped out of the room, slamming the door behind her.
The children stomped through the puddles in their boots.

Synonyms

  • tromp
    6.3 0.65/m
    tromp means to walk heavily and crush things underfoot, while stomp is heavy noisy walking often used to show anger.
  • pound
    3.1 0.55/m

  • stump
    5.9 0.18/m
    stump is walking with heavy noisy steps that suggest effort or clumsiness, while stomp often shows anger or firm purpose.
  • clomp
    7.5 0.37/m
    clomp is loud, clumsy walking often caused by heavy shoes, while stomp adds a sense of anger or strong intent.
