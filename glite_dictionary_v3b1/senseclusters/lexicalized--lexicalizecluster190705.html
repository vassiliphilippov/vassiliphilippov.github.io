lexicalized

adjective
expressed as or by a word
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛksɪkəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈlɛksɪkəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a concept or expression) represented by a single word rather than by a phrase; incorporated into the vocabulary as a distinct word.

Examples

A lexicalized word
Some idioms are not lexicalized and must be explained by a phrase.
Technical terms may be lexicalized over time as they become common usage.
The concept of 'internet' has become fully lexicalized in modern languages.
In linguistics, we study how concepts are lexicalised in different languages.
Some idioms become so common that they are eventually lexicalised into single words.
The word 'edit' is a lexicalised form of the longer phrase 'to make editorial changes'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phrasal - expressed as a phrase; not lexicalized
