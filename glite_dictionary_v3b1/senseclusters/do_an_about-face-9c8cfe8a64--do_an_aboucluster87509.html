do an about-face

verb
to reverse one's opinion or policy
9.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /də ən ə.ˈbaʊt.feɪs/ /; / /duː ən ə.ˈbaʊt.feɪs/ (AmE), /də ən ə.ˈbaʊt.feɪs/ /; / /duː ən ə.ˈbaʊt.feɪs/ (BrE)
to suddenly change one's opinion, policy, or course of action to its opposite

Examples

To do an about-face quickly
After initially rejecting the idea, the board did an about-face and approved the proposal.
He did an about-face after hearing all the evidence presented in court.
The government surprised everyone when it did an about-face on the new policy.
