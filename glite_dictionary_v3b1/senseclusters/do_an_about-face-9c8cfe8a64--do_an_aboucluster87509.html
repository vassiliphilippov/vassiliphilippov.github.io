do an about-face

verb
to reverse one's opinion or policy
6.6 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / du …ôn …ôÀàba ät Àåfe…™s / (AmE), / duÀê …ôn …ôÀàba ät Àåfe…™s / (BrE)
to suddenly change one's opinion, policy, or course of action to its opposite

Examples

To do an about-face quickly
After initially rejecting the idea, the board did an about-face and approved the proposal.
He did an about-face after hearing all the evidence presented in court.
The government surprised everyone when it did an about-face on the new policy.

Synonyms

  • about-face
    6.7 0.16/m
    about-face is a sudden change of opinion or position, while do an about-face means making a direct, complete reversal.
  • do a 180
    6.8 0.12/m
    do a 180 is an informal way to say you completely reverse a decision, while do an about-face is the same sudden reversal.
  • volte-face
    9.6 0.05/m
    volte-face is a formal, literary term for a sudden full change of view, while do an about-face is common spoken phrasing.
  • execute a U-turn
    5.3 0.00/m
    execute a U-turn is a figurative phrase often used for policy reversals, while do an about-face focuses on a sudden change.
  • reverse course
    5.5 0.60/m
    reverse course means change direction or plan, sometimes gradually, while do an about-face stresses a sudden, complete reversal.
