overstory

noun
top forest layer
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌstɔːri / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌstɔːri / (BrE)
the uppermost layer of leaves and branches in a forest, formed by the crowns of the tallest trees

Examples

Forest overstory
The dense overstory provides shade and shelter for many forest animals.
Sunlight rarely reaches the ground because the overstory is so thick in this rainforest.
After the storm, several trees in the overstory were damaged, changing the forest's appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canopy - the upper layer of a forest formed by tree crowns
  • antonym: understory - the layer of vegetation beneath the main canopy of a forest
