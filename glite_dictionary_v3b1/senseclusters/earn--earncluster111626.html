earn

verb
receive money for work or business
1.0 52/m
Pronunciation: / …ùn / (AmE), / …úÀên / (BrE)
to receive money as payment for work, services, or as profit from an investment or business

Examples

Earn money
He began to earn a steady income after getting his new job.
She hopes to earn enough this year to take a long vacation.
Their bakery started to earn more money after they launched online sales.

Synonyms

  • pull in
    4.1 0.73/m
    pull in often describes how much money someone earns in a period, while earn is the general word for receiving pay.
  • make money
    1.2 21/m
    make money means earning profit or income in general, while earn focuses on receiving payment for work or investment.
  • take in
    4.9 1.48/m
    take in is informal and means getting income or profit, while earn is the neutral verb for receiving payment.
  • clear
    6.0 4.44/m
    clear means to have net profit or to settle debts after costs, while earn is the general act of receiving payment.
  • gain
    1.4 83/m
    gain means getting something often nonfinancial like advantage or knowledge, while earn specifically means receiving money for work or investment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lose - to fail to keep or earn money, especially in business or investments
  • synonym: make - to receive money as payment, especially as wages or profit
