neurotoxicity

noun
damage to nerve tissue
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊtɑkˈsɪɾəti / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊtɒkˈsɪsɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being poisonous or harmful to nerve tissue

Examples

The neurotoxicity of chemicals
The neurotoxicity of certain chemicals can lead to long-term brain damage.
Doctors monitor patients for signs of neurotoxicity when administering high doses of chemotherapy.
Researchers are developing drugs to reduce neurotoxicity in neurological diseases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neuroprotection - the process of preventing nerve cell damage
  • synonym: neurotoxin - a substance that is poisonous to nerve tissue
