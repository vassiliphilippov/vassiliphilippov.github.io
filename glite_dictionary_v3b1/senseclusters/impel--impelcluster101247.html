impel

verb
to drive or urge to action
6.1
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛl / (BrE)
to urge or force someone to take action, especially because of pressure or necessity

Examples

To impel to act
A sense of duty impelled her to volunteer for the difficult assignment.
His curiosity impelled him to ask more questions about the mysterious discovery.
The urgent message impelled the team to take immediate action.
Her conscience impelled her to confess the truth.
Strong currents impel the boat down the river.
The desire for justice impelled many people to join the protest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compel - to force someone to do something
  • synonym: propel - to drive or push something forwards
  • antonym: restrain - to hold back or keep under control
