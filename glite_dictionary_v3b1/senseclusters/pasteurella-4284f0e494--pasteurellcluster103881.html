Pasteurella

noun
bacteria genus
Pronunciation: / ˌpæstʃəˈrɛlə / (AmE), / ˌpæstjʊˈrɛlə / (BrE)
a genus of bacteria that can cause infections in humans and animals, often following animal bites or scratches

Examples

Pasteurella infection
The doctor identified Pasteurella as the cause of the infection after the cat bite.
Veterinarians often test for Pasteurella in animals showing signs of respiratory illness.
Prompt treatment is important if a wound is infected with Pasteurella bacteria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteria - microscopic single-celled organisms
  • synonym: infection - the invasion of the body by harmful microorganisms
