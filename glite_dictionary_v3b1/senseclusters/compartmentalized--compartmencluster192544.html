compartmentalized

adjective
divided into distinct sections or categories
7.9
Pronunciation: / kəmˌpɑːrtˈmɛntəˌlaɪzd /; / kəmˌpɑrtˈmɛntəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / kəmˌpɑːtmɛntaˈlaɪzd /; / kəmˌpɑːtˈmɛntəlaɪzd / (BrE)
divided into separate sections or categories, often limiting interaction or integration between them

Examples

A compartmentalized life
Her life was so compartmentalized that her friends rarely met her coworkers.
In traditional companies, departments are often compartmentalized and do not share information easily.
Many researchers feel that academic disciplines have become overly compartmentalized.
Her approach to life was highly compartmentalised, separating work, family, and hobbies into distinct spheres.
In many large companies, departments remain compartmentalised, rarely collaborating with one another.
The museum's compartmentalised displays make it difficult to see connections between different historical periods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrated - combined into a whole; not divided into sections
