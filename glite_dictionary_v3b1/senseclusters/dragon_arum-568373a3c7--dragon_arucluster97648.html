dragon arum

noun
a European arum plant with dark purple flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡən ˈɛrəm / (AmE), / ˈdræɡən ˈarəm / (BrE)
a European plant (Dracunculus vulgaris) with large, dark purple flowers and a distinctive, unpleasant odor, resembling the cuckoopint

Examples

The dragon arum flower
Gardeners are often surprised by the strong smell produced by the dragon arum when it blooms.
The leaves of the dragon arum are large and sharply divided, adding to its dramatic look.
The striking appearance of the dragon arum makes it a popular plant for botanical gardens.
