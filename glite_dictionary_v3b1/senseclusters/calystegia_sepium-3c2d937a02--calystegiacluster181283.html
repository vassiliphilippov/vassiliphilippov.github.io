Calystegia sepium

noun
a species of wild climbing plant (hedge bindweed)
Pronunciation: / kəˌlɪstɪˈdʒaɪə ˈsiːpiəm / (AmE), / kəˌlɪstɪˈdʒiːə ˈsiːpiəm / (BrE)
A species of bindweed, a climbing plant commonly found in Eurasia and America, with pink or white trumpet-shaped flowers; often called hedge bindweed.

Examples

Calystegia sepium plant
Gardeners often struggle to remove Calystegia sepium due to its extensive root system.
In late spring, the pink and white flowers of Calystegia sepium bloom along country roadsides.
The rampant growth of Calystegia sepium can quickly cover garden fences and hedges.
