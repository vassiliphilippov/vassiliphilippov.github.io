push

noun
act of applying force to move something
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ / (AmE), / pʊʃ / (BrE)
the act or force of moving something away from yourself or in a particular direction

Examples

A strong push
After a big push, the heavy door finally opened.
He gave his friend a gentle push to encourage him forward.
With a single strong push, the box slid across the floor.
Engineers measured the push of the water flowing through the pipes.
She felt the push of the wind at her back as she walked along the pier.
The sudden push against the door startled everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pull - the act of applying force to draw something toward oneself
  • synonym: thrust - a strong push; the driving force or pressure behind something
