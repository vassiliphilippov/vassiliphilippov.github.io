false alarm

noun
unfounded warning or alert
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: / fɔls əˈlɑrm / (AmE), / fɔːls əˈlɑːm / (BrE)
an alert or warning that turns out to be unfounded or about an event that does not happen

Examples

A false alarm
The news report about the storm was a false alarm, so school was not cancelled.
We rushed to evacuate, but thankfully it was just a false alarm.
When the smoke detector went off, it turned out to be a false alarm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: real alarm - an actual occurrence requiring a warning or alert
  • synonym: scare - a sudden alarm or fright, sometimes unfounded
