risorius

noun
facial muscle for smiling
Pronunciation: / raɪˈsɔːriəs / (AmE), / rɪˈsɔːriəs / (BrE)
a facial muscle that pulls the corner of the mouth sideways, especially during smiling

Examples

The risorius muscle
The risorius is activated when you pull your lips sideways to form a wide smile.
Damage to the risorius can affect a person's ability to express emotions through facial movements.
During the anatomy lesson, the professor pointed out the location of the risorius on the model.
