elm family

noun
family of flowering plants including elms
Pronunciation: / ɛlm ˈfæməli / (AmE), / ɛlm ˈfamɪli / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants (Ulmaceae) that includes elm trees and related genera such as Ulmus, Celtis, Planera, and Trema

Examples

The elm family trees
Celtis, a member of the elm family, is commonly known as the hackberry tree.
Many species in the elm family are valued for their tough wood and attractive foliage.
The elm family includes several important shade trees found throughout temperate regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ulmaceae - the botanical family that elm trees belong to
