sarus

noun
ancient river name
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːrəs / (AmE), / ˈsɑːrəs / (BrE)
the ancient name of a river in Cilicia, now known as the Seyhan River in southern Turkey

Examples

The Sarus river
Ancient texts describe the sarus as a vital waterway for trade in Cilicia.
Archaeologists have traced settlements along the banks of the sarus dating back thousands of years.
The sarus played an important role in the history of southern Anatolia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Seyhan River - a major river in southern Turkey, formerly called the Sarus
