purplish pink

noun
a bright pink dye with purple tint
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːplɪʃ pɪŋk / (AmE), / ˈpɜːplɪʃ pɪŋk / (BrE)
A bright pink dye with a slight purple tinge, originally discovered in 1859 and named for the Battle of Solferino.

Examples

Bright purplish pink dye
Artists often choose purplish pink to add a touch of warmth and energy to their paintings.
During the 19th century, fabrics in purplish pink became especially fashionable after the discovery of the new dye.
The dress was dyed a vivid purplish pink that caught everyone's eye.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solferino - a magenta-pink dye discovered in 1859, similar color
