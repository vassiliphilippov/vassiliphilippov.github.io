hereditarian

noun
believer in heredity
9.9
Pronunciation: /hə.rɪ.ˈdɪ.tər.iː.ən/ /; / /he.rɪ.ˈdɪ.tər.iː.ən/ (AmE), /hə.rɪ.ˈdɪ.tə.riː.ən/ /; / /he.rɪ.ˈdɪ.tə.riː.ən/ (BrE)
a person who believes that heredity is the main factor in determining people's characteristics, abilities, or behavior

Examples

A hereditarian scientist
The hereditarian argued that intelligence is mostly inherited rather than learned.
As a hereditarian, he often debated with those who emphasized the role of environment.
Some researchers are labeled hereditarians because of their focus on genetic influences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: environmentalist - a person who believes environment shapes people more than heredity
  • synonym: geneticist - a scientist who studies genes and heredity
