complementation

noun
linguistic distribution of forms in exclusive contexts
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmpləˌmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪmenˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The occurrence of related speech sounds or forms so that each appears only in different contexts and never in exactly the same environment.

Examples

Phonetic complementation
In English, nasal and oral vowels show complementation in some dialects.
Phonologists study complementation to understand how languages organize similar sounds.
The phenomenon of complementation explains why certain allophones never occur in the same phonetic environment.
