overregistration

noun
excessive image alignment
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌrɛdʒɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
the problem in printing where images or colors are misaligned, causing visual artifacts or blurring

Examples

Overregistration in printing
The technician noticed overregistration in the printed images, which caused unwanted color fringes.
Careful calibration is needed to prevent overregistration when combining satellite photos.
Artifacts such as ghosting can result from overregistration during digital image processing.
The magazine cover showed signs of overregistration, with the colors appearing slightly out of place.
Careful calibration is needed to prevent overregistration in high-quality printing jobs.
If overregistration occurs, the final product may look blurry or have ghosted images.

  • antonym: registration - the process of aligning two or more images
