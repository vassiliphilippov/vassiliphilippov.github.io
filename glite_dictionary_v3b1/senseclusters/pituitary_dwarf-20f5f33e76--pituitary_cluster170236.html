pituitary dwarf

noun
person with short stature due to pituitary hormone deficiency
8.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / p…™ÀàtuÀê…™Àåt…õri dw…îrf / (AmE), / p…™ÀàtjuÀê…™t…ôri dw…îÀêf / (BrE)
a person of unusually short stature caused by a deficiency of growth hormone from the pituitary gland, rather than by genetic factors affecting bone growth

Examples

pituitary dwarf with hormone deficiency
Advances in medicine allow a pituitary dwarf to achieve near-normal height with early treatment.
The pituitary dwarf requires regular medical attention and hormone therapy.
Unlike an achondroplastic dwarf, a pituitary dwarf has normal body proportions.

Synonyms

  • hypophysial dwarf
    9.9 0.00/m
    hypophysial dwarf is a formal medical term describing short stature from pituitary growth hormone deficiency, while pituitary dwarf is the more common phrase.
  • Levi-Lorrain dwarf
    9.9 0.00/m
    Levi-Lorrain dwarf names short stature from a lack of growth hormone without naming the pituitary, while pituitary dwarf points to a pituitary cause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: achondroplastic dwarf - a person with short stature due to achondroplasia, a genetic bone growth disorder
