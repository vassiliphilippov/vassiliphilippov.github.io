radar dome

noun
protective enclosure for radar antenna
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪˌdɑr doʊm / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɑː dəʊm / (BrE)
A rounded structure enclosing a radar antenna, designed to protect it while being transparent to radio waves.

Examples

The radar dome on ship
On the ship’s mast, the white radar dome is clearly visible against the sky.
Technicians inspected the radar dome for any signs of damage after the storm.
The plane’s nose houses the radar dome that protects the navigation equipment.
