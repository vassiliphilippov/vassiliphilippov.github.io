white silk-cotton tree

noun
large tropical tree, source of kapok fiber
7.8
Pronunciation: / waɪt sɪlk ˈkɑtən tri / (AmE), / waɪt sɪlk ˈkɒtən triː / (BrE)
A massive tropical tree with deeply ridged trunk and large pods containing seeds covered with silky floss; source of kapok fiber.

Examples

A white silk-cotton tree trunk
During their hike in Southeast Asia, they saw a massive white silk-cotton tree with its distinctive ridged bark.
Farmers harvest the silky fibers from the white silk-cotton tree to use as stuffing for mattresses and pillows.
The white silk-cotton tree stood majestically at the edge of the rainforest, its trunk towering above the other plants.
