military commission

noun
military tribunal
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪl.ɪ.trɪ kəˈmɪʃ.ən/ /; / /ˈmɪl.ə.trɪ kəˈmɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈmɪl.ɪ.trɪ kəˈmɪʃ.ən/ /; / /ˈmɪl.ə.trɪ kəˈmɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
A tribunal or panel authorized to try offences under military law, often convened by the military or government to hear cases such as war crimes or terrorism charges.

Examples

Military commission trial
The international human rights group criticized the military commission for its secrecy and lack of due process.
After the attacks, lawmakers debated whether to use a military commission or civilian court for terrorism suspects.
Defense attorneys argued that evidence obtained under torture should be excluded from the military commission.

Related Terms

  • synonym: court-martial - a military court for trying members of the armed forces; similar in function but distinct in procedure from some military commissions
  • synonym: tribunal - an official body established to judge or decide disputes and legal cases
