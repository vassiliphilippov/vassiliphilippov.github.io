sensory

adjective
relating to the physical senses
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.sə.ri / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sər.i /; / ˈsɛn.səri / (BrE)
relating to sensation or the physical senses; transmitted or perceived by the senses

Examples

Sensory experience
Our sensory nerves help us detect heat, cold, pain, and other stimuli.
The chef created a sensory menu designed to stimulate all five senses.
The painting provides a rich sensory experience, engaging sight, touch, and even smell.
Bright colors and loud sounds can sometimes overwhelm a child's sensory system.
Many animals have specialized sensory organs for detecting heat, light, or movement.
The sensory cortex plays a crucial role in processing touch and other bodily sensations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: motor - relating to movement (opposite of sensory, in physiology)
  • synonym: perceptual - relating to the ability to perceive or the process of perception
  • synonym: sensual - relating to physical, especially sexual, pleasure
