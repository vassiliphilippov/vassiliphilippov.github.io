weak-stemmed

adjective
having a weak stem (botany)
6.7
Pronunciation: / wiːk ˈstɛm(d) / (AmE), / wiːk ˈstɛm(d) / (BrE)
(of a plant) having a stem that is not strong and may be unable to support itself upright.

Examples

Weak-stemmed plant
Because it is weak-stemmed, this species tends to sprawl across the ground.
Many garden flowers are weak-stemmed and can benefit from stakes or trellises.
The weak-stemmed plant needed a support to grow upright.

  • antonym: strong-stemmed - having a strong stem
