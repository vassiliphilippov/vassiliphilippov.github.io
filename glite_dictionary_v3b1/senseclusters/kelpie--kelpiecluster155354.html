kelpie

noun
water spirit from Scottish folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛl.pi / (AmE), / ˈkɛl.pi / (BrE)
In Scottish folklore, a supernatural water spirit, usually appearing as a horse, said to lure and drown travelers.

Examples

Mythical kelpie horse
According to legend, the kelpie would appear by the loch to lure unwary travelers onto its back.
Stories warn children never to ride a strange horse by the water for fear it might be a kelpie.
The sculpture in the park depicts a mythical kelpie rising gracefully from the river.
