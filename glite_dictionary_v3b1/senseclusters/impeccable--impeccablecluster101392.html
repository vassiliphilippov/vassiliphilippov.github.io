impeccable

adjective
incapable of sin (theological)
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛkəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛkəbl̩ / (BrE)
(Theology) Not capable of sin; incapable of doing wrong or committing sin.

Examples

Impeccable purity
He believed in the impeccable purity of the divine, untouched by sin or temptation.
Some theologians argue that Christ was impeccable, unable to commit sin by his very nature.
The doctrine held that angels, unlike humans, were impeccable beings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fallible - capable of making mistakes or being wrong
  • synonym: sinless - without sin; morally pure
