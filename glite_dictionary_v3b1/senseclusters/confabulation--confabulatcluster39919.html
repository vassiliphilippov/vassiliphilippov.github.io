confabulation

noun
fabricated memory to fill memory gaps
Pronunciation: / kənˌfæbjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfabjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry) A plausible but imagined memory that a person unconsciously creates to fill gaps in their actual memory.

Examples

Memory confabulation
Doctors recognized that his vivid recollections were actually confabulation caused by his brain injury.
Her confabulation convinced even her family that she had lived through events that never occurred.
The patient’s confabulation made it difficult to distinguish between real and imagined childhood events.
