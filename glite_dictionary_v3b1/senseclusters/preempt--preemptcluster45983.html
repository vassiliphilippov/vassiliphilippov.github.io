preempt

verb
make a preemptive bid in bridge
8.2 7.2
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈempt/ /; / /prɪ.ˈempt/ (AmE), /priː.ˈempt/ /; / /prɪ.ˈempt/ (BrE)
(in bridge) to make a preemptive bid, typically at a higher level than necessary, to interfere with the opponents' bidding sequence.

Examples

To preempt at three
During last night's game, he preempted at the three-level to keep his rivals out of the auction.
It can be risky to preempt on a weak hand, but sometimes it pays off.
She decided to preempt with a three-diamond bid, hoping to disrupt the opponents' communication.
