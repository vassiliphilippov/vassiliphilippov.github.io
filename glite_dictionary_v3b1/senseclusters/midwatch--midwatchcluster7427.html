midwatch

noun
nautical night watch (midnight–4 a.m.)
8.0 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪd.wɑːtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈmɪd.wɒtʃ/ (BrE)
a shift of duty or period of watch at night, usually from midnight to 4 a.m., especially on ships

Examples

The midwatch shift
During his midwatch, he often enjoyed the solitude and the sound of the waves.
She was assigned the midwatch and had to stay alert while most of the crew slept.
The ship's routine changed each day, but the midwatch remained the quietest time.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dogwatch - a shorter watch period on a ship, usually in the evening
  • synonym: graveyard shift - a night shift, especially in non-nautical contexts
