endosteum

noun
membrane lining bone cavity
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdɑstiəm / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒstɪəm / (BrE)
the thin vascular membrane of connective tissue that lines the inner surface of the bone cavity, especially in long bones

Examples

The endosteum membrane
An infection may spread along the endosteum, affecting the entire bone structure.
Osteoblasts are often found lining the endosteum within the medullary cavity.
The endosteum plays a crucial role in bone growth and repair.

Related Terms

  • antonym: periosteum - membrane covering the outer surface of bones
