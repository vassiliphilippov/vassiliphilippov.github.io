stone curlew

noun
a type of nocturnal shorebird
Pronunciation: / stoʊn ˈkɜr.luː / (AmE), / stəʊn ˈkɜː.ljuː / (BrE)
a large-headed, large-eyed nocturnal or crepuscular shorebird of the Old World and tropical America, known for its thickened knee joint

Examples

The stone curlew bird
Because of its large yellow eyes, the stone curlew stands out among other wading birds.
Birdwatchers travel to special reserves in Europe to catch a glimpse of the elusive stone curlew.
The stone curlew is rarely seen during the day because it is mostly active at dusk and night.
