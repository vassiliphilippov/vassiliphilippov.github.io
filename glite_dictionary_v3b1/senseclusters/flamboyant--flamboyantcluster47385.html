Synonyms

  • showy
    3.2 0.67/m
    showy is bright and designed to attract attention in appearance, while flamboyant is more bold, elaborate, and attention-grabbing in style.
  • ostentatious
    7.7 1.07/m
    ostentatious is showy to impress others and often feels pretentious, while flamboyant is bold and elaborate but not always meant to impress.
  • florid
    8.6 0.31/m
    florid is highly ornate and richly decorated, often in language or design, while flamboyant focuses on bold, showy appearance or style.
  • glitzy
    4.8 0.82/m
    glitzy is bright and glamorous in a superficial way, often for places or events, while flamboyant is more elaborate personal style.
  • flashy
    4.1 3.72/m
    flashy is loud and often seen as tasteless or vulgar in style, while flamboyant is showy and more artistically elaborate.
  • splashy
    4.9 0.39/m
    splashy is designed to grab quick public attention and may seem lacking in taste, while flamboyant is boldly elaborate and stylistic.
  • flaunty
    8.9 0.00/m
    flaunty openly shows off possessions or looks in a boastful way, while flamboyant describes a striking, elaborate style that may not boast.
