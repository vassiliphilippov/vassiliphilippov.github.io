disqualification

noun
act of declaring ineligible
2.7
Pronunciation: / dɪsˌkwɑləfəˈkeɪʃən /; / dɪsˌkwɑlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪsˌkwɒlɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of being made ineligible to participate, usually because of not meeting rules or requirements

Examples

Immediate disqualification
Failure to provide the required documents resulted in immediate disqualification.
His aggressive behavior brought about his disqualification from the tournament.
The athlete's false start led to her disqualification from the race.
After reviewing the evidence, the judges confirmed her disqualification.
Any sign of cheating will result in disqualification.
His actions led to immediate disqualification from the competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: qualification - the act of qualifying or the state of being qualified
