reforestation

noun
restoration of forests by planting trees
5.2 3.2
Pronunciation: /riː.fɔː.rə.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /riː.fə.rə.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.fɒ.rə.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /riː.fə.rə.ˈsteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of planting trees to restore a forest that has been reduced or destroyed, especially by fire or logging

Examples

Reforestation efforts
After the wildfire, reforestation efforts focused on restoring native species to the area.
Many environmental groups support reforestation to combat climate change.
The government launched a major reforestation project to revive the depleted woodland.

Related Terms

  • antonym: afforestation - the process of planting trees to create a new forest where none existed before
  • antonym: deforestation - the removal of trees from a forested area
