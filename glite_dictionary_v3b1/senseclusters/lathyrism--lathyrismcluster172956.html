lathyrism

noun
legume-induced paralysis
Pronunciation: / ˈlæθəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlæθɪˌrɪzəm / (BrE)
a disease caused by eating certain legumes, especially Lathyrus species, resulting in paralysis or muscle weakness

Examples

Lathyrism disease
Cases of lathyrism have been reported in regions where certain wild peas are a dietary staple.
Doctors identified lathyrism in several patients who had eaten large amounts of grass pea seeds.
Efforts to prevent lathyrism include educating communities about the risks of consuming toxic legumes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neurolathyrism - a form of lathyrism affecting the nervous system
  • synonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move part or most of the body
