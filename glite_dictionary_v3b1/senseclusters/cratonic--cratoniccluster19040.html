cratonic

adjective
of a stable landmass
Pronunciation: / krəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / krəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a craton, a stable and ancient part of the Earth's continental crust

Examples

Cratonic region
Geologists study cratonic regions to understand the early history of the Earth.
The shield area is composed mainly of cratonic rocks that have remained unchanged for billions of years.
Many mineral deposits are found in cratonic zones due to their geological stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: craton - a stable, ancient part of the continental lithosphere
