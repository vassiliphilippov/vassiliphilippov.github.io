tromp

verb
to trample heavily
6.3
Pronunciation: /trɑːmp/ (AmE), /trɒmp/ (BrE)
to walk with heavy, noisy steps, often making a loud sound or crushing things underfoot

Examples

To tromp through mud
The children tromped through the muddy field, leaving deep footprints behind them.
Please do not tromp on the new grass while it is still growing.

The children tromped through the forest, laughing as they crushed the fallen leaves underfoot.
After the rain, workers tromped across the muddy construction site, leaving deep tracks behind.
She tromped up the stairs in her heavy boots, making the whole house shake.
The children tromped through the muddy field after the rain.
He tromped up the stairs, leaving muddy footprints behind him.
We could hear someone tromping around in the hallway late at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crush - to press or squeeze something with force so that it is damaged
  • synonym: stomp - to walk with heavy, forceful steps
  • antonym: tiptoe - to walk quietly and carefully on the toes
  • synonym: tramp - to walk with heavy, firm steps
  • synonym: trample - to step heavily on something and crush it
  • synonym: trudge - to walk slowly and with heavy steps, often because of exhaustion
