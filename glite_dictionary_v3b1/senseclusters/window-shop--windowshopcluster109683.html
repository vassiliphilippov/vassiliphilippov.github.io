window-shop

verb
browse shop windows without buying
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndoʊ ʃɑp / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊ ʃɒp / (BrE)
to look at goods displayed in shop windows without intending to buy them

Examples

To window-shop in stores
He prefers to window-shop rather than buy things he doesn't need.
On Saturdays, she loves to window-shop in the city center, admiring the new collections.
We spent the afternoon window-shopping before deciding on a café for lunch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: browse - to look at goods for sale in a casual way
