sensitivity

noun
ability to detect or respond to stimuli
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛn.səˈtɪv.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the ability to detect or respond to physical stimuli, or to register small differences in amount or intensity

Examples

Instrument sensitivity
Technicians measured the sensitivity of the instrument before beginning their experiment.
The new sensor boasts exceptional sensitivity to changes in temperature.
The patient's sensitivity to light made the procedure challenging for doctors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of ability to detect or respond to stimuli
  • synonym: sensitiveness - capacity to sense or detect minor physical stimuli
