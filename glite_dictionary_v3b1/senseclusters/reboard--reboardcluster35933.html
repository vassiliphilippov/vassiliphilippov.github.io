reboard

verb
to board up again
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbɔrd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbɔːd / (BrE)
to cover windows, doors, or a building with boards again, usually for protection or security

Examples

To reboard the windows

The shop owner decided to reboard the entrance after another break-in attempt.
Volunteers helped reboard the abandoned house before the storm arrived.
