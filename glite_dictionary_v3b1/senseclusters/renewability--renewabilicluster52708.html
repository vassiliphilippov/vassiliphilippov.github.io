renewability

noun
ability to be renewed
5.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˌnuːəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / rɪˌnjuːəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be renewed, replaced, or restored naturally or through human action

Examples

Renewability of resources
The renewability of wind energy is a key reason for its growing popularity worldwide.
Many companies are investing in materials with high renewability to reduce their environmental impact.
Concerns about the renewability of certain resources have led to stricter conservation policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exhaustibility - the quality of being able to be used up or depleted
  • synonym: sustainability - the ability to be maintained or continued over time
