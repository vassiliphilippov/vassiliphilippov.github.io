factor I

noun
a blood protein involved in clotting
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfæk.tər wʌn/ (AmE), /ˈfæk.tə wʌn/ (BrE)
a protein present in blood plasma that is converted to fibrin when blood clots form

Examples

Factor I protein
A deficiency of factor I can lead to bleeding disorders.
Doctors measured her factor I levels to assess her clotting ability.
When blood clots, factor I is transformed into fibrin to help stop bleeding.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: factor II - another blood clotting factor (prothrombin)
  • synonym: fibrinogen - the soluble blood protein; precursor to fibrin in clotting
