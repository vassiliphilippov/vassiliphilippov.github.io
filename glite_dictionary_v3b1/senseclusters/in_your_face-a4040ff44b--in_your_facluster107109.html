in your face

adjective
provocatively bold or confrontational
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɪn jɔɹ feɪs / (AmE), / ɪn jɔː feɪs / (BrE)
provocative, aggressive, or confrontational in manner or style, often intended to attract attention or challenge others

Examples

An in your face style
He celebrated the victory with an in your face gesture toward the crowd.
Her in your face advertising style certainly gets noticed.
The team's in your face attitude intimidated their opponents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brazen - bold and without shame
  • antonym: subtle - not obvious or overt
