discharge

noun
official document releasing from duty or obligation
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌtʃɑrdʒ /; / ˈdɪs.tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ /; / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the act of officially releasing someone from a duty, service, or obligation; or the official document stating this

Examples

A hospital discharge
Before he left the hospital, he signed the official discharge papers.
She was given a full discharge from her duties at the end of her contract.
The soldier received an honorable discharge from the army after ten years of service.
After the discharge, she quickly found a new job.
His discharge from the company came as a shock to everyone.
The soldier received an honorable discharge after twenty years of service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dismissal - the act of removing someone from employment
  • synonym: release - the action of setting someone free from an obligation or duty
  • antonym: resignation - the act of an employee voluntarily leaving a job
