leadoff

noun
first batter
Pronunciation: / ˈliːdˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈliːdˌɒf / (BrE)
the first batter in a team's batting order, especially the player who bats at the start of an inning in baseball

Examples

Leadoff batter
The team's leadoff set the tone for the game with a powerful double.
As the leadoff, she was known for her ability to get on base early.
The coach chose a fast runner for the leadoff position this season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cleanup - the fourth batter in a baseball team's batting order
  • synonym: leadoff hitter - the player who bats first in the lineup
