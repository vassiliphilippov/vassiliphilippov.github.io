autolytic

adjective
relating to self-digestion by own enzymes
Pronunciation: / ˌɔtəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or caused by the destruction of a cell or tissue by its own enzymes (self-digestion).

Examples

Autolytic tissue breakdown
Certain ulcers may exhibit autolytic tissue breakdown, requiring special medical attention.
Researchers studied autolytic changes in tissue samples left at room temperature.
The autolytic process begins when the cell's own enzymes start breaking down cellular components.
