sustain

verb
to keep something going for longer
4.3 3.3
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈsteɪn/ (AmE), /sə.ˈsteɪn/ (BrE)
to make something continue or last for an extended period of time; to maintain or lengthen the duration of something

Examples

Sustain an effort
It is important to sustain efforts to improve public health over time.
She tried to sustain the note for as long as possible during her vocal performance.
The company was able to sustain its growth for several years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cease - to bring or come to an end
  • synonym: continue - to persist or keep going
  • synonym: maintain - to keep something at the same level or condition
  • synonym: prolong - to make something last longer
