twist

verb
to injure by turning suddenly
2.2 9.05/m
Pronunciation: / twÉªst / (AmE), / twÉªst / (BrE)
to turn or strain a part of the body, especially a joint, so that it is injured or sprained

Examples

To twist an ankle
He twisted his knee on the uneven pavement and had to limp home.
If you twist your wrist during a fall, it may swell up quickly.
She twisted her ankle while jogging in the park.

Synonyms

  • turn
    3.7 2.32/m
    turn means to move a joint suddenly and cause a sprain, while twist is a general action that can injure a body part.
  • sprain
    3.2 1.74/m
    sprain injures a joint by sudden twisting and causes swelling, while twist is the action of turning or straining a body part.
  • wrench
    5.3 1.69/m
    wrench means to twist a limb violently and cause sharp pain, while twist can be less forceful and may not injure.
  • crick
    7.9 0.04/m
    crick bends the neck into a stiff, painful position, while twist refers to turning or straining a joint that may cause injury.
