bellwether

noun
person or thing indicating future trends
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛlˌwɛðər / (AmE), / ˈbɛlˌwɛðə / (BrE)
a person or thing that assumes a leadership role or indicates future trends

Examples

Market bellwether
Analysts look to these companies as potential bellwethers of future market trends.
Her early adoption of new technology made her a bellwether in the industry.
The state has long been considered a bellwether for national election results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leader - a person who leads others or commands a group
