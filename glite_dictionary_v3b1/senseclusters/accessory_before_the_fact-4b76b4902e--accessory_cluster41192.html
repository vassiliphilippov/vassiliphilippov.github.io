accessory before the fact

noun
someone who helps arrange a crime, not present at the scene
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛsəri bɪˈfɔr ðə fækt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəri bɪˈfɔː ðə fækt / (BrE)
a person who aids, abets, or encourages the commission of a crime but is not present when the crime is actually committed

Examples

An accessory before the fact to crime
Even though she was not at the scene, she was considered an accessory before the fact.
He was charged as an accessory before the fact for planning the robbery.
The court found him guilty of being an accessory before the fact to murder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accomplice - a person who helps another commit a crime, present or not
  • antonym: principal - the main perpetrator of a crime, as opposed to an accessory
