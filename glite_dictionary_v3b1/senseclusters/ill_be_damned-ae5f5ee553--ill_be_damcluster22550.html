I'll be damned

interjection
expression of surprise or strong refusal
Pronunciation: / aɪl bi dæmd / (AmE), / aɪl bi dæmd / (BrE)
used to express surprise, disbelief, or strong refusal; sometimes indicates determination not to do something.

Examples

I'll be damned if I
I'll be damned if I let that cat scratch up my couch again.
I'll be damned if I'm going to apologize when I did nothing wrong.
I'll be damned, I never thought they'd actually finish the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: I'll be blowed - British informal equivalent of 'I'll be damned', expressing surprise
