placatory

adjective
intending to pacify or appease
7.1 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪ.kəˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / pləˈkeɪ.tər.i / (BrE)
Intended to pacify or appease someone, especially by acceding to demands or making concessions.

Examples

A placatory letter
He sent a placatory letter in hopes of repairing their friendship.
Her placatory remarks helped to calm the angry audience.
The manager adopted a placatory tone to resolve the dispute.
