RTW

noun
return-to-work program
Pronunciation: / ɑr ti ˈdʌbəl.ju / (AmE), / ɑː tiː ˈdʌbəl.juː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'return-to-work', referring to a program or process that helps employees resume work after illness, injury, or absence

Examples

RTW program
After her surgery, she participated in the company's RTW program to ease her transition back to work.
The manager explained the benefits of the RTW initiative to employees returning from long-term leave.
Many organizations now offer RTW support to help staff recover and reintegrate after illness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leave of absence - a period when an employee is away from work
  • synonym: rehabilitation - the process of helping someone return to health or normal life
