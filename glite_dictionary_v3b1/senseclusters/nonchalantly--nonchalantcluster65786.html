nonchalantly

adverb
in an unconcerned or casual manner
4.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ʃə.ˈlɑːnt.liː/ /; / /nɑːn.ʃə.ˈlænt.liː/ (AmE), /nɒn.ʃə.ˈlɑːnt.liː/ /; / /nɒn.ʃə.ˈlænt.liː/ (BrE)
in a casual, calm, or unconcerned way; not showing worry or excitement

Examples

To act nonchalantly
He strolled nonchalantly into the meeting, not worried about being late.
She nonchalantly tossed her coat onto the chair and began talking as if nothing had happened.
The student nonchalantly admitted he had forgotten his homework, showing no sign of embarrassment.
He walked into the meeting nonchalantly, as if nothing important was happening.
Jake answered the difficult question nonchalantly, impressing everyone with his composure.
She shrugged nonchalantly when asked about the mistake, showing little concern.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anxiously - in a manner showing worry or concern
  • synonym: casually - in a relaxed and informal way
