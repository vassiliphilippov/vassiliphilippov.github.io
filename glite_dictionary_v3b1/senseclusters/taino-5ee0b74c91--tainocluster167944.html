Taino

proper noun
Caribbean indigenous people
9.9
Pronunciation: / taɪˈiːnoʊ / (AmE), / taɪˈiːnəʊ / (BrE)
a member of the indigenous people of the Caribbean, especially the Greater Antilles, encountered by Columbus in the late 15th century

Examples

A Taino farmer
The Taino developed advanced agricultural techniques long before European contact.
Artifacts from the Taino culture can be found in museums throughout the Caribbean.
Many words in Caribbean Spanish come from the Taino language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arawak - a group of indigenous peoples of South America and the Caribbean
