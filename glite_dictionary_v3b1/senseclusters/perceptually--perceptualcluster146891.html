perceptually

adverb
in a way relating to perception
Pronunciation: / pərˈsɛptʃuəli / (AmE), / pəˈsɛptjʊəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to perception or the ability to perceive things

Examples

To perceptually compare colors
Some colors are perceptually closer together than others on the visible spectrum.
The design was altered to be more perceptually balanced and appealing.
The illusion is perceptually striking, making it hard to distinguish the shapes.
