time unit

noun
unit of time measurement
3.1 1.3
Pronunciation: / taɪm ˈjuː.nɪt / (AmE), / taɪm ˈjuː.nɪt / (BrE)
a unit of measurement used to express a quantity of time, such as a second, minute, or hour

Examples

A time unit like hour
Payroll is calculated based on the number of hours, which is a standard time unit.
The time unit commonly used in scientific experiments is the second.
When programming, you often have to specify a time unit for delays, such as milliseconds or seconds.
