final injunction

noun
permanent court order after a trial
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪnəl ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a court order issued at the conclusion of a trial, permanently prohibiting or requiring certain actions by a party

Examples

A final injunction order
After months of litigation, a final injunction was issued to stop further construction on the site.
The court refused to issue a final injunction because the plaintiff failed to prove ongoing harm.
The judge granted a final injunction preventing the company from using the disputed trademark.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: injunction - a court order requiring or forbidding certain actions
  • degree lower: interim injunction - a temporary court order to maintain the status quo until a final decision is made
