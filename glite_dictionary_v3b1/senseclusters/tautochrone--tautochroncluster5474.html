tautochrone

noun
curve with equal descent time
Pronunciation: / tɔːˈtɑːkroʊn / (AmE), / tɔːˈtɒkrəʊn / (BrE)
a curve on which an object slides to the lowest point in the same amount of time, regardless of its starting position

Examples

The tautochrone curve
In physics, the tautochrone problem explores how objects move along certain curves.
The cycloid is a well-known example of a tautochrone studied in mathematics.
Engineers sometimes refer to the tautochrone when designing efficient paths for moving objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cycloid - a curve traced by a point on a circle as it rolls along a straight line
