Oceanid

noun
sea nymph in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊʃiənɪd / (AmE), / əʊˈsiːənɪd / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a sea nymph who is a daughter of Oceanus and Tethys.

Examples

The Oceanid nymph
In the ancient tales, each Oceanid had her own stream, fountain, or cloud to guard.
Many poets have described the grace and beauty of the mythical Oceanids.
The painting depicted an Oceanid rising from the water, surrounded by shells and seaweed.
