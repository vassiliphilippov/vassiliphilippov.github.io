American persimmon

noun
North American tree with edible fruit
4.0 6.8
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən pər.ˈsɪ.mən/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kən pɜː.ˈsɪ.mən/ (BrE)
A medium-sized tree native to southern and eastern United States, bearing yellow or orange astringent fruit that becomes edible when fully ripe.

Examples

An American persimmon tree
She planted an American persimmon in her backyard for its ornamental value and delicious harvest.
The American persimmon produces fruit that must be fully ripe before it can be eaten.
Wildlife such as deer and raccoons often feed on the fallen American persimmon fruit.
