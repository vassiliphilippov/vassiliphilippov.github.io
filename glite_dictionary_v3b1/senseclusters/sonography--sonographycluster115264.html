sonography

noun
imaging using sound waves (medical)
6.2 4.6
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈnɑː.grə.fiː/ (AmE), /sə.ˈnɒ.grə.fiː/ (BrE)
the use of high-frequency sound waves to create images of internal body structures, often used to observe fetal development or examine organs

Examples

Sonography of organs
A technician performed a sonography to get clear images of the patient's heart.
Advances in sonography have improved the diagnosis of many abdominal conditions.
The doctor recommended sonography to monitor the baby's growth during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: MRI - a technique that uses magnetic fields and radio waves to generate images of organs in the body
  • synonym: ultrasound - sound or other vibrations having an ultrasonic frequency, particularly as used in medical imaging
