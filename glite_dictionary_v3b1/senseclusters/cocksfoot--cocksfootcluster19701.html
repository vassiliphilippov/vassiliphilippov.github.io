cocksfoot

noun
a type of pasture grass
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑksˌfʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɒksfʊt / (BrE)
A tall, tufted grass of the Old World, widely cultivated for hay and pasture, especially Dactylis glomerata.

Examples

Tall cocksfoot grass
Cocksfoot is resistant to drought and can survive in poor soil conditions.
Dairy cattle thrive on hay made from cocksfoot because of its high nutritional value.
The farmer planted cocksfoot to improve the quality of the pasture for his sheep.
