subsumption

noun

Pronunciation: / səbˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / səbˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
(Logic) The minor premise in a syllogism that contains the subject of the conclusion.

Examples

Logical subsumption step
In traditional logic, the subsumption is the premise that pairs the subject with a general class.
