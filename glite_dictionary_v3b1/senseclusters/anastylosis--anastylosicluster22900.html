anastylosis

noun
rebuilding monuments from original fragments
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ə.stɪˈloʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌan.ə.stɪˈləʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
the archaeological process of reconstructing ruined monuments using original fragments, adding new materials only when necessary

Examples

Anastylosis of ruins
Preserving historical authenticity, the archaeologists applied anastylosis techniques to the fallen columns of the ruins.
The restoration team used anastylosis to rebuild the ancient temple with its original stones.
Through anastylosis, the monument was reassembled, minimizing the use of new materials whenever possible.
