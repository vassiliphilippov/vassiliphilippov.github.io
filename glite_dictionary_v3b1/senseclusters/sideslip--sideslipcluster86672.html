sideslip

verb
to move sideways
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.slɪp/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.slɪp/ (BrE)
to move or slide sideways, either by accident or as a controlled maneuver (especially in vehicles or aircraft)

Examples

To sideslip a plane
The glider began to sideslip as the pilot adjusted the controls for landing.
If you sideslip on icy roads, you should steer gently to regain control.
The instructor demonstrated how to sideslip the airplane to lose altitude quickly.
The car sideslipped on the icy road, narrowly avoiding the guardrail.
During the landing, the airplane sideslipped to adjust its position on the runway.
He lost control of his bicycle and sideslipped on the wet pavement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drift - to move slowly or gradually, especially sideways
  • synonym: skid - to slide sideways, often out of control
  • synonym: swerve - to turn aside suddenly from a straight course
