common European ash

noun
a tall European ash tree species
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑm.ən ˌjʊr.əˈpi.ən ˈæʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.ən ˌjʊə.rəˈpiː.ən ˈæʃ / (BrE)
a tall tree native to Europe and parts of Asia, with dark-green, shiny leaves above and pale, downy leaves beneath; commonly known as the European ash (Fraxinus excelsior)

Examples

A common European ash tree
A row of common European ash trees lined the old country road.
Botanists studied the leaves of the common European ash to understand its adaptation to the environment.
The wood of the common European ash is highly valued for its strength and elasticity.
