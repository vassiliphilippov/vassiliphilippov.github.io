backbite

verb
speak maliciously about someone not present
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌbaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbækˌbaɪt / (BrE)
to speak spitefully or maliciously about someone who is not present

Examples

To backbite about coworkers
He has backbitten nearly everyone in the department at some point.
She regretted backbiting her friends when she realized the harm it caused.
They would often backbite their absent colleagues in the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation or reports about others, usually involving details not confirmed as true
  • synonym: slander - make false and damaging statements about someone
