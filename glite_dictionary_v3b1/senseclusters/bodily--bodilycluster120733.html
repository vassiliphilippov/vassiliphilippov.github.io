bodily

adjective
relating to the physical body
4.0 3.61/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ëÀêd…™li /; / Ààb…ëÀêd…ôli / (AmE), / Ààb…íd…™li /; / Ààb…íd…ôli / (BrE)
relating to or having a physical, material body; pertaining to the body rather than the mind or spirit

Examples

Bodily health
Regular exercise is important for maintaining bodily health.
She complained of bodily discomfort after the long flight.
The philosopher discussed the nature of bodily existence versus spiritual existence.
Her illness caused both mental and bodily suffering.
Regular exercise helps maintain bodily health throughout life.
The doctor assessed his bodily functions during the examination.
Many religions teach that the soul is separate from the bodily form.

Synonyms

  • corporeal
    8.4 1.18/m
    corporeal is a formal word for a physical, material body often used in writing, while bodily is common everyday adjective.
  • physical
    2.1 98/m
    physical refers to the body or things you can touch in everyday life, while bodily focuses on the body's parts or harm.
  • somatic
    7.9 1.93/m
    somatic is a medical term used for body changes or symptoms in science, while bodily is more general daily usage.
  • corporal
    6.8 1.78/m
    corporal describes physical punishment or the body in legal texts, while bodily is neutral and used in everyday speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spiritual - relating to the spirit or soul rather than to physical things
