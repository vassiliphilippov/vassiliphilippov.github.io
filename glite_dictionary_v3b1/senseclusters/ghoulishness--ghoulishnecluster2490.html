ghoulishness

noun
eerie or grotesque quality
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈguː.lɪʃ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈguː.lɪʃ.nəs/ (BrE)
a grotesque, eerie, or frightening quality that resembles or suggests a ghoul

Examples

Ghoulishness of the house
The ghoulishness of the haunted house made visitors shiver with fear.
There was a certain ghoulishness to the old photographs found in the attic.
The film's ghoulishness was heightened by its eerie soundtrack and shadowy lighting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerfulness - a quality of being happy and lively
  • synonym: creepiness - a quality that causes unease or fear
  • synonym: macabreness - a quality that is grim, gruesome, or related to death
