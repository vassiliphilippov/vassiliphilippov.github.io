lacrimal artery

noun
artery supplying the lacrimal gland and nearby tissues
Pronunciation: / ˈlækrəməl ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ˈlækrɪməl ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the ophthalmic artery and supplies blood to the lacrimal gland, rectus eye muscles, upper eyelid, and forehead

Examples

The lacrimal artery supplies blood
Damage to the lacrimal artery may affect the upper eyelid's function.
Surgeons must carefully avoid the lacrimal artery during orbital procedures.
The lacrimal artery plays a crucial role in providing blood supply to the tear gland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ophthalmic artery - main artery supplying the eye and its structures
