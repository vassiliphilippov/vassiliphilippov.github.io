facit

verb
Latin: he/she/it does
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.kɪt/ /; / /ˈfæ.sɪt/ (AmE), /ˈfɑː.kɪt/ /; / /ˈfæ.sɪt/ (BrE)
he, she, or it makes or does; used in Latin inscriptions or mottos to indicate action by a person or entity.

Examples

Motto facit
The city’s motto includes the word facit to show its commitment to action.
On the ancient coin, facit appears beneath the image of a craftsman at work.
The inscription reads 'Labor omnia facit', meaning 'Work does everything.'
