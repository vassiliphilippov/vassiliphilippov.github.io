resensitization

noun
restoring drug response
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌsɛnsɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of restoring the responsiveness of cells, organisms, or pathogens to a drug or treatment after resistance has developed

Examples

Resensitization to drugs
Researchers are studying resensitization to improve the effectiveness of antibiotics against resistant bacteria.
The patient underwent a period of resensitization to respond again to allergy treatments.
Doctors hope that resensitization will allow cancer therapies to work after initial resistance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desensitization - the process of making less responsive to a stimulus or drug
  • synonym: sensitization - the process of making sensitive or responsive to a stimulus or drug
