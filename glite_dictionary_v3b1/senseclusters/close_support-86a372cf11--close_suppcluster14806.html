close support

noun
direct combat assistance by nearby unit

Military assistance given by one unit firing at or attacking an enemy in close proximity to a friendly unit engaged with that enemy.

Examples

To provide close support
Air units provided close support to the ground troops during the battle.
The infantry advanced under close support from the artillery, which kept enemy positions suppressed.
The tank squadron moved in for close support as the soldiers encountered heavy resistance.
