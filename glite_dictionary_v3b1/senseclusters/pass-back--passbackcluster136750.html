pass-back

noun
return to previous handler
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpæs.bæk/ /; / /pæs bæk/ (AmE), /ˈpɑːs.bæk/ /; / /pɑːs bæk/ (BrE)
an act of returning something, such as a document or task, to the person or group who previously handled it for further action or review

Examples

A pass-back of documents
The manager requested a pass-back of the report to the finance team for further clarification.
After the initial review, the document underwent a pass-back to address the missing details.
A quick pass-back can help ensure all necessary corrections are made before final approval.
