coyote bush

noun
evergreen shrub of southwestern US
Pronunciation: / kaɪˈoʊti bʊʃ / (AmE), / kaɪˈəʊti bʊʃ / (BrE)
A widely spreading evergreen shrub of the southwestern United States, known for its small flower heads arranged in leafy clusters.

Examples

Coyote bush flowers
Coyote bush blooms in the fall, attracting many pollinators to its small flowers.
Gardeners often use coyote bush to create drought-tolerant landscapes in California.
The hillsides were covered with dense coyote bush where wildlife took shelter.
