saturnism

noun
lead poisoning (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈsætɚˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsatəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A toxic condition caused by the absorption of excessive amounts of lead into the body.

Examples

High saturnism level
A high level of saturnism was reported in children living near the smelting plant.
Early symptoms of saturnism may include fatigue, abdominal pain, and irritability.
The doctor diagnosed the factory worker with saturnism after prolonged exposure to lead fumes.
