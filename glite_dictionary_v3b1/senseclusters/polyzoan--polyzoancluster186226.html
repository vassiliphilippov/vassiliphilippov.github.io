polyzoan

noun
sessile aquatic animal forming mossy colonies
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈzəʊən / (BrE)
A sessile aquatic animal that forms colonies of small polyps, each with a ridge bearing tentacles; commonly found attached to stones or seaweed and reproduces by budding.

Examples

Polyzoans form colonies
Polyzoans reproduce by budding, resulting in extensive colonies on submerged surfaces.
Many polyzoans filter microscopic particles from the water using their tentacle-bearing ridges.
The scientist examined a cluster of polyzoans attached to the rocky sea floor.

Related Terms
