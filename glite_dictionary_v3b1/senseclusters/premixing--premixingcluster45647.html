premixing

noun
prepared mixture
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌmɪksɪŋ /; / ˌpriːˈmɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌmɪksɪŋ /; / ˌpriːˈmɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of mixing ingredients together before a main step, or a mixture that has been prepared in advance

Examples

The premixing process
The bakery relies on premixing to speed up the bread-making process each morning.
Using premixing ensures that all the ingredients are evenly distributed before baking.
For large events, caterers often use premixing to prepare sauces and batters ahead of time.
The premixing of dry ingredients ensures an even distribution of flavors in the cake.
Proper premixing can help prevent lumps when adding liquids to the batter.
Some recipes require premixing to activate certain ingredients before cooking.
