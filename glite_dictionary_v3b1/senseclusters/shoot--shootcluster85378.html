shoot

verb
kill by firing a missile
1.8 148/m
Pronunciation: /  Éut / (AmE), /  ÉuÀêt / (BrE)
to fire a gun, weapon, or projectile at someone or something, often to hit or kill

Examples

To shoot a gun
During the robbery, the criminals threatened to shoot anyone who resisted.
Hunters are not allowed to shoot deer out of season.
The poacher tried to shoot the endangered elephant, but the rangers stopped him just in time.
Police warned not to shoot unless absolutely necessary.
She was accidentally shot in the leg during the robbery.
The hunter tried to shoot the deer but missed.
During the robbery, the thief threatened to shoot if anyone moved.
She practiced at the firing range, learning how to shoot safely and accurately.
The police ordered him to drop the gun, but he refused and shot.

Synonyms

  • fire
    1.0 49/m
    fire means to shoot or discharge a gun or weapon, while shoot often means to fire aiming to hit or kill.
  • gun down
    7.4 1.93/m
    gun down means to shoot someone usually causing death or serious harm, while shoot can mean firing a weapon more generally.
  • gun
    3.2 2.49/m
    gun means to shoot with a firearm, often at someone, while shoot covers firing many kinds of weapons or projectiles.
  • blast
    3.5 2.12/m
    blast means to hit or propel something with great force, often loudly, while shoot focuses on firing a weapon at a target.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blast - to shoot rapidly with a firearm
  • synonym: fire - to discharge a gun or other weapon
  • synonym: gun down - to shoot and kill someone, often suddenly or deliberately
  • antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or injuring
