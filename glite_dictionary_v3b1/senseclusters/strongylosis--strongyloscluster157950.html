strongylosis

noun
parasitic animal disease
Pronunciation: / ˌstrɒndʒɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌstrɒndʒɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
a disease in animals caused by infection with strongyle worms, especially affecting horses and livestock

Examples

Strongylosis in horses
The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with strongylosis after noticing weight loss and poor coat condition.
Farmers vaccinate their livestock regularly to prevent strongylosis and other parasitic diseases.
Symptoms of strongylosis can include diarrhea, anemia, and reduced growth in young animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colic - abdominal pain in animals, often caused by parasites
  • synonym: helminthiasis - disease caused by parasitic worms
