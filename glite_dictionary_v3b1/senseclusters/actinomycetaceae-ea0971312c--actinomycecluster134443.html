Actinomycetaceae

noun
family of filamentous anaerobic bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.tɪˌnoʊ.maɪˌsi.təˈsiː.i / (AmE), / ˌæk.tɪ.nəˌmaɪ.siː.təˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A family of filamentous, anaerobic bacteria found mainly in soil and known for their role in decomposition and antibiotic production.

Examples

Actinomycetaceae bacteria family
Infections caused by Actinomycetaceae can be difficult to treat due to their resistance to common drugs.
Researchers are studying Actinomycetaceae for their potential to produce new antibiotics.
The soil sample contained several members of the Actinomycetaceae family, which are important in organic matter decomposition.
