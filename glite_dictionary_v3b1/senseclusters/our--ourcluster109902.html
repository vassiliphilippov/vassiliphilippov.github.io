our

determiner
group possessive determiner
Pronunciation: / aʊɚ / (AmE), / aʊə / (BrE)
used before a noun to show that something belongs to or is connected with the speaker and at least one other person

Examples

Our house
In our culture, family gatherings are very important.
Our generation has seen many technological changes.
During our time, people valued handwritten letters more than emails.
Our team won the championship after a long and difficult season.
We invited all of our friends to the housewarming party last weekend.
Our school is organizing a charity event to help the local community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: my - belonging to the speaker
  • antonym: their - belonging to or associated with a group of people or things previously mentioned
  • antonym: your - used to show something belongs to the person being spoken to
