arrowleaf

noun
arrow-shaped leaf
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæroʊˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈærəʊˌliːf / (BrE)
a leaf that is shaped like the head of an arrow, with a pointed tip and two spreading lobes at the base

Examples

An arrowleaf
The plant is easily recognized by its large arrowleaf that stands out among the other foliage.
Botanists often use the term arrowleaf to describe leaves with a pointed tip and two basal lobes.
In the wetland, many aquatic plants display the distinctive arrowleaf shape.

  • synonym: sagittate - shaped like an arrowhead
