illegalize

verb
make illegal; outlaw
Pronunciation: / ɪˈliɡəˌlaɪz /; / ɪˈliːɡəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈliːɡəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something illegal by law or authoritative action; to outlaw

Examples

To illegalize an action
After years of debate, the city council decided to illegalize short-term rentals.
Some activists want to illegalize wild animal performances in circuses.
The government plans to illegalize the sale of certain pesticides.
Activists are campaigning to illegalise single-use plastics across the country.
If a substance is illegalised, strict penalties are put in place for possession or sale.
The government decided to illegalise the sale of certain pesticides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criminalise - to make something a criminal offense by law
  • antonym: legalise - to make something legal by law
  • antonym: legalize - to make something legal; to permit by law
