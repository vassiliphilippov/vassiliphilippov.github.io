Synonyms

  • neo-Gothic architecture
    8.0 0.00/m
    neo-Gothic architecture names the building style inspired by medieval Gothic, while neo-Gothic describes things that belong to that style.
  • Gothic revival architecture
    9.1 0.00/m
    Gothic revival architecture describes 18th and 19th century buildings using pointed arches, while neo-Gothic names the adapted style or its features.
  • Gothic revival
    8.1 0.05/m
    Gothic revival refers to the wider movement of returning to medieval Gothic designs, while neo-Gothic describes a later architectural style or decorative form.
