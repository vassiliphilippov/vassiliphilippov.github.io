Synonyms

  • rent
    1.8 16/m
    rent means allowing someone to use property under a formal paid agreement, while rent out is the phrasal, owner-focused verb.
  • let
    6.7 70/m
    let means allowing someone to occupy property under a rental contract, while rent out is a broader, more neutral phrasal verb.
  • lease
    3.5 3/m
    lease means giving long-term use of land or property under a formal contract, while rent out can also cover shorter, simpler rentals.
  • hire out
    3.9 0.2/m
    hire out means offering things or services for temporary pay, while rent out usually refers to letting property to tenants.
