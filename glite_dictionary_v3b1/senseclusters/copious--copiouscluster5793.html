Synonyms

  • abundant
    3.0 12/m
    abundant describes plenty available in nature or supply, while copious stresses things produced or written in very large amounts.
  • plentiful
    2.5 4.90/m
    plentiful means common and easy to find, while copious focuses on things being produced or recorded in large amounts.
  • ample
    3.7 10/m
    ample means more than enough in size or space, while copious refers to very large quantities produced or written.
  • profuse
    6.5 0.38/m
    profuse describes something given or produced in excess and often freely, while copious points to large but neutral quantities.
  • bountiful
    4.9 1.25/m
    bountiful describes a generous or fruitful supply like a harvest, while copious simply means present in very large amounts.
  • voluminous
    7.0 1.89/m
    voluminous means very large in size or length, often for books or fabric, while copious refers to many items or details.
