TRF

noun
hormone controlling thyroid-stimulating hormone release
Pronunciation: / ti ɑr ɛf / (AmE), / ti ɑː ɛf / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the hypothalamus that stimulates the release of thyroid-stimulating hormone from the anterior pituitary gland

Examples

TRF hormone level
A deficiency in TRF can lead to symptoms of hypothyroidism.
Researchers measured the levels of TRF in patients with thyroid disorders.
The hypothalamus secretes TRF to regulate hormone balance.
