pin

verb
chess: immobilize a piece by threat
4.3 6.5
Pronunciation: / pɪn / (AmE), / pɪn / (BrE)
(in chess) to make a piece unable to move without exposing a more valuable piece, such as the king or queen, to capture.

Examples

To pin a piece (chess)
He pinned his opponent's bishop, forcing it to stay in place.
In the game, she managed to pin his knight to the king with her bishop.
It's a common tactic to pin a rook against the queen along an open file.
