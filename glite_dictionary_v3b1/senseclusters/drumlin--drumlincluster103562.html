drumlin

noun
elongated glacial hill
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌmlɪn / (AmE), / ˈdrʌmlɪn / (BrE)
An elongated hill formed by glacial ice, composed of glacial drift and typically having a smooth, streamlined shape.

Examples

A drumlin hill
A series of drumlins could be seen stretching across the glacial landscape.
Many farms in the area are built on top of ancient drumlins.
The geology field trip focused on identifying the shape and composition of a nearby drumlin.
