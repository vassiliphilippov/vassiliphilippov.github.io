sit around

verb
be idle or do nothing in a place
1.0 1.1
Pronunciation: /sɪt ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /sɪt ə.ˈraʊn/ (AmE), /sɪt ə.ˈraʊnd/ /; / /sɪt ə.ˈraʊn/ (BrE)
to spend time sitting and doing little or nothing, often without any particular purpose

Examples

To sit around all day
The kids were sitting around watching TV all afternoon instead of going outside.
We didn't do much yesterday; we just sat around talking and drinking coffee.
You can't sit around waiting for opportunities to come to you—you have to take action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hang around - to remain or stay idly in a place
  • synonym: laze about - to relax or be lazy without doing much
  • antonym: work - to engage in physical or mental activity; to do something productive
