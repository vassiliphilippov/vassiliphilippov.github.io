potbound

adjective
roots tangled from a small pot
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑtˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a potted plant) having grown too large for its container, causing the roots to become matted or tangled.

Examples

Potbound plant roots
The fern became potbound and stopped growing, so I repotted it into a larger container.
When a plant is potbound, its roots often circle around the inside of the pot.
You should check if your plant is potbound before adding fertilizer, as it might need more space instead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rootbound - (of a plant) with roots restricted by the pot and forming a dense mass
