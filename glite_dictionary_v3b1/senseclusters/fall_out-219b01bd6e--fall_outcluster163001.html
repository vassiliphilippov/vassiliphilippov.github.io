fall out

verb
(of soldiers) leave a formation or barracks
4.5
Pronunciation: / fɔl aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
(of soldiers) to leave a place in a barracks or a military formation, usually at an order.

Examples

Soldiers fall out
After inspection, the soldiers were told to fall out and relax.
At the sergeant's command, the troops fell out and assembled in the yard.
You may fall out now, but stay nearby for further orders.
