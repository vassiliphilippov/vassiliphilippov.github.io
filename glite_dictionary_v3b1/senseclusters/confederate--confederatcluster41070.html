confederate

verb
join together in a confederation
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛdərˌeɪt /; / kənˈfɛdɚˌeɪt / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərəɪt /; / kənˈfɛdərət / (BrE)
to join together in a confederation, league, or alliance for a common purpose

Examples

To confederate nations
If these regions confederate, they could form a powerful economic bloc.
Several small states decided to confederate for mutual defense against larger neighbors.
The provinces had confederated under a single federal government by the end of the decade.
Labor unions sometimes confederate in order to negotiate better terms.
Several political parties decided to confederate to increase their influence.
The tribes chose to confederate against a common enemy.

  • antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to not unite
