grandfathering

noun
exemption from new rules
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrændˌfɑðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːnˌfɑːðərɪŋ /; / ˈɡrɑːnfɑːðərɪŋ / (BrE)
a rule or practice that allows existing people or situations to continue under old rules when new rules are introduced

Examples

Grandfathering old rules
The new zoning laws do not apply to older homes because of grandfathering.
Many employees kept their benefits through grandfathering when the company changed its policy.
Without grandfathering, all businesses would have to meet the new safety standards immediately.
The new building codes do not apply to older homes because of grandfathering.

Without grandfathering, all businesses would have to follow the updated regulations immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exemption - the act of freeing someone from an obligation or rule
  • antonym: restriction - a rule or law that limits what people can do
