falsum

noun
false statement or document
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑlsəm / (AmE), / ˈfɔːlsʊm / (BrE)

Examples

A legal falsum
The lawyer argued that the contract was a falsum and should not be accepted as evidence.
In philosophy, a falsum is used to represent a statement that is logically false.
The court dismissed the evidence as a clear falsum.
