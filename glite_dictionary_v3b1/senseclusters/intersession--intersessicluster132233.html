intersession

noun
meeting between sessions
7.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈseʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈseʃ.ən/ (BrE)
a short period or session between regular sessions, often used for special purposes (such as meetings, classes, or legislative breaks)

Examples

An intersession meeting
The board called an intersession to discuss the urgent changes in policy.
During the intersession, members reviewed proposals that could not wait until the next regular meeting.
An intersession was necessary to address the unexpected budget shortfall.
Many students enroll in special courses during the intersession to earn extra credits.
The university offers language workshops in the intersession between the fall and spring terms.
She traveled abroad for a short program during the January intersession.
During the intersession, lawmakers returned to their home districts to meet with constituents.
The court will not hear any new cases until the current intersession ends.
Many committees use the intersession to draft reports and prepare for the next meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adjournment - a break or pause between sessions
  • synonym: recess - a break or pause between sessions of a formal body
  • synonym: semester break - a break between two academic semesters
  • antonym: session - a formal meeting or period when a legislature or court is in operation
  • synonym: special session - a meeting called outside the regular schedule to deal with specific issues
  • antonym: term - a fixed period during which classes are held at a school or university
