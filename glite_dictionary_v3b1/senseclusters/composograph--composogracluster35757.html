composograph

noun
fake photograph
9.9
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈpoʊ.zə.græf/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈpəʊ.zə.grɑːf/ /; / /ˈkɒm.pə.zə.grɑːf/ (BrE)
a fake or manipulated photograph made by combining several images, often used to deceive or create sensational news stories

Examples

A composograph photo
The magazine published a sensational composograph to attract more readers.
Experts quickly identified the image as a composograph rather than a real photograph.
He was accused of using a composograph to support his false claims.
The tabloid's front page featured a dramatic composograph of the celebrity at the scene.
Many readers were misled by the composograph that appeared to show the event as real.
In the early 20th century, newspapers sometimes used a composograph to illustrate sensational stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authentic photograph - a genuine, unaltered photograph
  • synonym: deepfake - a synthetic media in which a person in an existing image or video is replaced with someone else's likeness
  • synonym: fake - something that is not genuine or real
  • synonym: photomontage - an image made by combining several photographs
