row

noun
continuous sequence without interruption
Pronunciation: / ɹoʊ / (AmE), / rəʊ / (BrE)
a continuous sequence of events or actions happening one after another without interruption

Examples

Three times in a row
He saw the same movie three times in a row.
She has received an award for five years in a row.
The team lost six games in a row before finally winning.
