lipolysis

noun
breakdown of fats
Pronunciation: / laɪˈpɑləsɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈpɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process in which fats are broken down in the body to release energy

Examples

Lipolysis process
During exercise, the body increases lipolysis to provide energy from stored fat.
Researchers studied the effects of hormones on lipolysis in human fat cells.
Certain medications can stimulate lipolysis to help reduce body fat.
During intense exercise, lipolysis increases to provide energy for the muscles.
A diet low in carbohydrates can stimulate lipolysis in the body.
Researchers study lipolysis to better understand how the body manages fat stores.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fat metabolism - the chemical processes that break down or use fats in the body
  • antonym: lipogenesis - the process of forming fat from other substances in the body
