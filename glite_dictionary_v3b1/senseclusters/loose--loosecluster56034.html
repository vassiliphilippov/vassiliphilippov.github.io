loose

adjective
not tight or closely fitted
Pronunciation: / lus /; / luːs / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
not tight, not firmly fixed, or not securely attached; not fitting closely

Examples

A loose shirt
Make sure your dog's collar isn't too loose or it might slip off.
She prefers to wear loose dresses during hot weather for extra comfort.
The screw was so loose that the shelf nearly fell down.
He wanted loose change for the vending machine.
I bought a bag of loose tea instead of tea bags.
There are loose screws scattered in the drawer.
His shirt hung loose around his thin frame.
Make sure the rope is not loose before you start climbing.
She tied her hair back, but a few strands were still loose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detached - separated or disconnected, especially not attached
  • antonym: fixed - securely fastened or attached
  • antonym: packed - secured within a package or container
  • synonym: slack - not tight or taut; loose or limp
  • antonym: tight - closely fitted or firmly fixed
  • synonym: unbound - not fastened or tied
