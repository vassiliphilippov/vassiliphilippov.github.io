think

verb
to consider or reflect
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /θɪŋk/ (AmE), /θɪŋk/ /; / /fɪŋk/ (BrE)
to use your mind to consider, reason, or reflect on something

Examples

To think deeply
She likes to think about possible outcomes before acting.
Sometimes I need to think things over before making a decision.
You should think carefully before you answer such a question.
I couldn't think what to say in that situation.
She needs to think carefully about her career options.
Take some time to think before you answer the question.
Infants gradually learn to think as their brains develop.
Some animals appear to think and solve problems in remarkable ways.
Unlike computers, only humans truly think, reflecting upon their own existence.

  • degree higher: decide - to make a choice after thinking

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
