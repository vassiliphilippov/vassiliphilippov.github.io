law of areas

noun
Kepler's law of planetary motion (equal areas in equal times)
8.4 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / l…î …ôv Àà…õri…ôz / (AmE), / l…îÀê …ôv Ààe…ôri…ôz / (BrE)
the scientific principle stating that a line joining a planet and the sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time, meaning a planet moves faster when closer to the sun and slower when farther away; Kepler's second law of planetary motion.

Examples

The law of areas
According to the law of areas, planets travel more quickly when they are closer to the sun.
Kepler's law of areas explains why the speed of a planet changes as it orbits.
Physics students studied the law of areas to understand planetary motion.
