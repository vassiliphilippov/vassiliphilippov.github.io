coquille

noun
dish shaped like a scallop shell
9.6
Pronunciation: / koʊˈkiːl / (AmE), / kɒˈkiːj / (BrE)
A dish, often made of china or metal, shaped like a scallop shell and used especially for serving certain seafood dishes.

Examples

Porcelain coquille dish
She bought a set of porcelain coquilles for her dinner parties.
The classic dish 'Coquilles Saint-Jacques' is traditionally baked in a coquille.
The seafood was served in a beautiful coquille that resembled a real shell.
