blue-green

adjective
relating to a two-environment deployment strategy
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbluː.griːn/ (AmE), /ˈbluː.griːn/ (BrE)
(in computing) relating to a deployment strategy that uses two identical production environments named 'blue' and 'green' so traffic can be switched between them for safe releases and quick rollback.

Examples

Blue-green deployment
The ops team performed a blue-green deployment to avoid downtime during the software upgrade.
We switched traffic to the blue-green environment after verifying the new release passed all tests.
Implementing blue-green allowed instantaneous rollback when the monitoring alerted us to regressions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blue-green deployment - a deployment approach using two identical environments to switch user traffic between versions for release and rollback
