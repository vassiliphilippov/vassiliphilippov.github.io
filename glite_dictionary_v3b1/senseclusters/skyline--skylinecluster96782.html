skyline

verb
to be silhouetted
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to be outlined or silhouetted against the sky, especially when standing on a ridge or hilltop

Examples

To skyline a figure
The mountain climbers were skylined against the evening sky as they reached the summit.
At dawn, the lone tree was skylined on the top of the hill.
The photographer waited until the deer were perfectly skylined before taking the shot.
