flatfooted

adjective
with feet flat on the ground
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˌflatˈfʊtɪd /; / ˌflætˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
having flat feet or standing/moving with the feet flat on the ground (not on the toes)

Examples

Flatfooted landing
He landed flatfooted after jumping off the step, hurting his ankles.
She remained flatfooted, refusing to move until the music started.
The children stood flatfooted at the edge of the pool, waiting for instructions.
Children who are flatfooted may need special shoes for support.
During the examination, the doctor noticed the patient was flatfooted.
His flatfooted stance made it difficult for him to run quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flatfoot - a condition in which the arch of the foot is flattened
