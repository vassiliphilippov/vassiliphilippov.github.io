pneumonectomy

noun
surgical removal of a lung
6.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /nuː.mə.ˈnek.tə.miː/ /; / /njuː.mə.ˈnek.tə.miː/ (AmE), /njuː.mə.ˈnek.tə.miː/ /; / /nuː.mə.ˈnek.tə.miː/ (BrE)
A surgical operation to remove a lung, usually performed to treat disease such as lung cancer.

Examples

A pneumonectomy operation
After her diagnosis of lung cancer, the doctors recommended a pneumonectomy to remove the affected lung.
Recovery from a pneumonectomy can be lengthy and requires careful post-operative care.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of a pneumonectomy to the patient and her family.
