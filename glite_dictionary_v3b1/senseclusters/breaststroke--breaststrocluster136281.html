breaststroke

noun
a type of swimming stroke
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛstˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbrɛst.strəʊk / (BrE)
a swimming stroke in which the arms are extended forward and swept outward while the legs perform a frog kick

Examples

A breaststroke race
His favorite way to swim across the lake is using the breaststroke.
She won a gold medal in the 100-meter breaststroke at the national championships.
The breaststroke is often the first stroke swimmers learn after the basics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backstroke - a swimming stroke performed on the back with arm and leg movements
  • antonym: butterfly - a swimming stroke performed on the chest with both arms moving symmetrically
  • antonym: freestyle - a swimming stroke where the swimmer can use any style, usually the front crawl
