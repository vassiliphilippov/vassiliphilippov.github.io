Notonecta undulata

noun
species of aquatic predatory insect
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊtəˈnɛktə ˌʌndjuˈleɪtə / (AmE), / ˌnɒtəˈnɛktə ˌʌndjʊˈleɪtə / (BrE)
A species of predaceous aquatic insect, commonly known as the backswimmer, which swims on its back and can deliver painful bites.

Examples

Notonecta undulata insect
Caution is advised, as the Notonecta undulata can inflict a rather painful bite if handled.
Researchers observed a Notonecta undulata preying on other aquatic insects in the pond.
The Notonecta undulata is well known for its ability to swim upside down beneath the water's surface.
