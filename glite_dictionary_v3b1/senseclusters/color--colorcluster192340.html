color

noun
interest, variety, or vividness (figurative sense)
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɚ /; / ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
liveliness or distinctiveness; the quality of adding interest, variety, or vividness to something, especially in writing, speech, or personality.

Examples

Color in stories
Her stories always have plenty of color, making the characters seem real and engaging.
The speech added color to the otherwise dull proceedings.
There was a lack of color in his writing, making it difficult to stay interested.
His speech lacked colour, making it difficult for the audience to stay interested.
She brought a lot of colour to the office with her creativity and humor.
The novel was full of colour and kept readers engaged from start to finish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dullness - the quality of lacking interest or excitement
  • synonym: vividness - the quality of being clear, graphic, or lifelike
