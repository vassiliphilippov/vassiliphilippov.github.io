new

adjective
recently created, acquired, or discovered
Pronunciation: / nu /; / nuː / (AmE), / njuː / (BrE)
recently created, discovered, or existing for the first time; original or different from anything before

Examples

A new discovery
Astronomers have identified a new planet outside our solar system.
She moved into a new apartment right after graduation.
The company is selling a new line of smartphones this year.
She started her new job last week after leaving her old position.
The company has announced a new policy that differs from the previous one.
We need to find a new approach to solve this problem.
The discovery of penicillin was a new achievement in medical history.
They faced challenges in a new situation unlike any before.
This type of art represents a new approach without prior example.

Related Terms

  • synonym: innovative - featuring new methods; advanced and original
  • synonym: modern - related or belonging to the present time; up to date
  • synonym: novel - new or unusual in an interesting way
  • antonym: old - having existed for a long time; not new
  • synonym: recent - having happened or started only a short time ago
  • synonym: unfamiliar - not known or recognized; not experienced before
  • synonym: unprecedented - never having happened or existed before
