meridionality

noun
north–south flow
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˌrɪdiəˈnæləti / (AmE), / məˌrɪdiəˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which atmospheric flow is oriented north–south rather than east–west, especially in meteorology

Examples

Meridionality of the jet stream
Meteorologists observed increased meridionality in the jet stream, leading to unusual weather patterns across the continent.
Periods of high meridionality can result in more extreme temperature swings between regions.
The forecast discussed how meridionality might bring colder air further south than usual this winter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: zonal flow - atmospheric flow oriented mainly east–west
