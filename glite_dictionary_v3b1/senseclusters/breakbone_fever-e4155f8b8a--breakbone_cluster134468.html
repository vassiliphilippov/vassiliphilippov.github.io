breakbone fever

noun
tropical disease, also called dengue fever
6.2 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbreɪk.boʊn ˈfiː.vər/ (AmE), /ˈbreɪk.bəʊn ˈfiː.və/ (BrE)
an infectious tropical disease transmitted by mosquitoes, characterized by severe muscle and joint pain, rash, and headache; also known as dengue fever.

Examples

Breakbone fever symptoms
Doctors recognized the symptoms of breakbone fever immediately due to the high fever and rash.
Some travelers returned from the Caribbean suffering from breakbone fever after being bitten by mosquitoes.
The severe joint pain caused by breakbone fever can last for weeks and is often debilitating.
