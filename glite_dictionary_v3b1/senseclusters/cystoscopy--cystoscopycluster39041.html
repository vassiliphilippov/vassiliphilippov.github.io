cystoscopy

noun
bladder examination procedure
Pronunciation: / sɪˈstɑːskəpi / (AmE), / sɪˈstɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a thin tube is inserted into the bladder (usually through the urethra) to examine or treat problems inside the bladder

Examples

A cystoscopy procedure
The urologist scheduled a cystoscopy to check for any abnormalities in the bladder.
After experiencing frequent infections, she underwent a cystoscopy for further diagnosis.
A cystoscopy can help doctors identify the cause of blood in the urine.
The doctor recommended a cystoscopy to investigate the cause of her frequent urinary tract infections.
During the cystoscopy, the urologist found and removed a small bladder stone.
A cystoscopy can help diagnose problems such as bleeding or tumors in the bladder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biopsy - the removal of tissue for examination
  • synonym: colonoscopy - a procedure to examine the inside of the colon
  • synonym: endoscopy - a procedure using a scope to examine the inside of the body
