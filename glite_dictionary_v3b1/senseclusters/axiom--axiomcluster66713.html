axiom

noun
self-evident statement or principle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæksiəm / (AmE), / ˈæksiəm /; / ˈæksɪəm / (BrE)
a statement or principle that is accepted as true and used as a starting point for reasoning or argument

Examples

A axiom of truth
His argument was founded on the axiom that all men are created equal.
Many mathematical systems begin with a set of axioms from which other truths are derived.
The basic axiom of geometry is that through any two points, there is exactly one straight line.
It is an axiom of economics that resources are limited.
One old axiom holds that actions speak louder than words.
The scientist began her argument with the axiom that all living things require water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adage - a traditional saying expressing a common experience or observation
  • synonym: maxim - a well-known phrase expressing a general truth or rule of conduct
  • synonym: postulate - statement assumed true without proof, especially for argument or reasoning
  • antonym: theorem - statement proved on the basis of previously accepted statements
