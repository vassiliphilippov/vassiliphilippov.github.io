ironclad

adjective
protected by iron plates
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪərnˌklæd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌklæd / (BrE)
(of a ship, vehicle, or structure) protected by iron plates for defense.

Examples

Ironclad vehicle
In the late 19th century, navies replaced wooden ships with ironclad ones for improved protection.
That museum displays an ironclad train car used during the civil war.
The warship was an ironclad vessel, nearly impervious to enemy fire.
