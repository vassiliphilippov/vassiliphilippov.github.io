sympathy

noun
mutual understanding or harmony
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəθi / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəθi / (BrE)
A relationship of mutual understanding or harmony between people, where whatever affects one correspondingly affects the other.

Examples

Sympathy between friends
She felt a deep sympathy with her colleague, sharing both his ambitions and frustrations.
Their ideas were in close sympathy, leading to a harmonious partnership.
There was an immediate sympathy between the two teammates, making their collaboration effortless.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affinity - a natural liking or attraction between people
  • antonym: antipathy - a feeling of strong aversion or opposition
