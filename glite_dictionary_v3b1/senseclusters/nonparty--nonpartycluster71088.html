nonparty

adjective
not involved legally
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈpɑːti / (BrE)
not directly involved in or named as a party to a lawsuit, contract, or legal agreement

Examples

Nonparty witness
The judge determined that the nonparty witness was not subject to the court's order.
A nonparty organization submitted evidence to support the case.
The agreement does not impose any obligations on a nonparty individual.

Related Terms

  • antonym: party - a person or group directly involved in a legal agreement or dispute
  • synonym: third party - a person or group not directly involved in a legal agreement or dispute
