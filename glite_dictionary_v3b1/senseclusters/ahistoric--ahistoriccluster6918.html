ahistoric

adjective
outside historical context
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪhɪˈstɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪhɪˈstɒrɪk / (BrE)
existing or appearing to exist outside of history; not influenced by, based on, or concerned with historical facts or context

Examples

An ahistoric style
The building's ahistoric style makes it difficult to place in any particular era.
Critics described the novel as ahistoric, lacking any reference to real historical events.
Her approach to fashion is deliberately ahistoric, ignoring trends from any specific decade.
The novel takes an ahistoric approach, ignoring the real timeline of events.
Critics argued that the documentary was too ahistoric to be considered reliable.
His ahistoric interpretation of the past led to several factual errors.
The film's ahistoric setting ignores the real events of the era it depicts.
Critics argued that the policy was ahistoric and failed to consider past experiences.
Her analysis was criticized for being ahistoric and lacking historical context.
