phase transition

noun
change of state (solid, liquid, gas) in matter
2.9 4.5
Pronunciation: /feɪz træn.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /feɪz træn.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /feɪz trɑːn.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a physical process in which a substance changes from one state (such as solid, liquid, or gas) to another without altering its chemical composition

Examples

A phase transition process
Scientists study the phase transition between liquid and gas to understand boiling points.
Sudden shifts in temperature can cause a rapid phase transition in some materials.
The melting of ice into water is a classic example of a phase transition.
