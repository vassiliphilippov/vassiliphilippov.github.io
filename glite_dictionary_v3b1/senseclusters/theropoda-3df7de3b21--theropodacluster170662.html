Theropoda

noun
group of carnivorous saurischian dinosaurs
9.9
Pronunciation: / θəˈrɑːpədə / (AmE), / θɪˈrɒpədə / (BrE)
a group of carnivorous saurischian dinosaurs with short forelimbs, known from the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods

Examples

Theropoda dinosaurs
Fossils of Theropoda have been found on every continent, including Antarctica.
Many iconic dinosaurs, such as Tyrannosaurus rex, belong to the Theropoda.
Scientists believe that birds evolved from members of the Theropoda.
