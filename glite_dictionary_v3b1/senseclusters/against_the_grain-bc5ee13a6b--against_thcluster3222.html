against the grain

adverb
contrary to what is usual or expected
7.9
Pronunciation: / əˈɡɛnst ðə ɡreɪn / (AmE), / əˈɡenst ðə ɡreɪn / (BrE)
in a way that is different from what is normal, usual, or expected, especially by challenging established customs or values

Examples

Against the grain style
Her unconventional outfit was against the grain at such a formal event.
Sometimes doing what you believe is right means going against the grain.
Speaking out in that meeting really went against the grain for him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conform - to behave in a way that is expected or accepted by most people
