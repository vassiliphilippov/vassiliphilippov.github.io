scholarship

noun
financial aid for students based on merit
4.4 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈskɑː.lər.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ˈskɒ.lə.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
an amount of money given to a student to help pay for their education, usually awarded on the basis of academic achievement or merit

Examples

A scholarship award
Applying for a scholarship can help reduce the financial burden of higher education.
His excellent grades helped him win a scholarship for the upcoming academic year.
She received a full scholarship to study engineering at the university.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bursary - a scholarship to attend a college or university, usually based on financial need
  • synonym: grant - an amount of money given for a purpose, especially study or research
