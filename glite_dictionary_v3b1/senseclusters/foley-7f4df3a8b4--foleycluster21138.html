Foley

noun
sound effects creation
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊli / (AmE), / ˈfəʊli / (BrE)
the creation or reproduction of everyday sound effects for use in film, television, or other media

Examples

Foley sound
The Foley team added the sound of rustling leaves to make the forest scene more realistic.
Without good Foley, movies would feel strangely silent and unnatural.
She studied Foley techniques to improve the audio quality of her short films.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dialogue - spoken words in a film or play
  • synonym: sound design - the process of creating audio elements for media
