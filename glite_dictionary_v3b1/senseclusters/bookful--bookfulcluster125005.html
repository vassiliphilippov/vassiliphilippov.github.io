bookful

adjective
full of books
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkfəl / (AmE), / ˈbʊkfʊl / (BrE)
containing or filled with many books; having a large number of books present.

Examples

Bookful shelves
The old library was especially bookful, with every shelf packed tightly.
His office appeared bookful, making it a paradise for any avid reader.
She dreamed of a bookful study where she could read in peace.
