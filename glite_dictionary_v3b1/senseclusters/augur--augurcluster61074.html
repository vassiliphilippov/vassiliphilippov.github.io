augur

verb
to predict or be a sign of the future
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɡər / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡə / (BrE)
to be a sign of what will happen in the future; to predict or foretell, especially based on omens

Examples

Dark clouds augur trouble
Heavy clouds augur a storm is on the way.
Such kindness may augur better relations between the two countries.
The early success did not augur well for the rest of the project.
Some believe that dreams augur important events in one's life.
Strong sales augur well for the company's future.
The dark clouds augur a coming storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bode - to be an omen of a particular outcome
  • synonym: foretell - to predict something before it happens
  • synonym: portend - to be a sign or warning of something to come
  • synonym: predict - to say what will happen in the future
