substance

noun
essential or most important part
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbstəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌbstəns / (BrE)
the most important or essential meaning or main idea of something spoken, written, or argued

Examples

The substance of the speech
Let's focus on the substance of the proposal instead of minor details.
She summarized the substance of the meeting for those who arrived late.
The substance of his speech was that more funding is needed for education.
Please focus on the substance of the proposal rather than its presentation.
The substance of her argument was lost in the lengthy introduction.
While his speech was entertaining, it lacked real substance.
His speech was entertaining, but it lacked real substance.
I don't think the article had much substance beyond a few vague ideas.
It's important to focus on the substance of her argument rather than her tone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: core - the central or most important part of something
  • synonym: essence - the intrinsic nature or indispensable quality of something
  • antonym: form - the outward appearance or structure, as distinct from meaning or substance
  • synonym: gist - the most essential part or main point
  • degree lower: idea - thought or suggestion as to a possible course of action
  • antonym: surface - the superficial or outward appearance of something, often lacking depth
