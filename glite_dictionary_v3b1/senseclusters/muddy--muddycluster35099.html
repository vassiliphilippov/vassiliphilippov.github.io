muddy

verb
to make unclear
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
to make something less clear or more confusing

Examples

To muddy the issue
The technical jargon only served to muddy the explanation for most listeners.
Bringing up unrelated issues will just muddy the discussion and distract from the main topic.
He tried not to muddy the facts with his personal opinions during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarify - to make something easier to understand
  • synonym: obscure - to make something difficult to understand or see
