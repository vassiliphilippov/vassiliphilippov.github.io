procellariiform seabird

noun
long-winged seabird with tubular nostrils
8.8
Pronunciation: / proʊˌsɛləˌraɪ.əˌfɔrm ˈsiːˌbɝd / (AmE), / prəˌsɛləˌraɪ.əˌfɔːm ˈsiːˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A large, long-winged seabird with tubular nostrils and a hooked bill, belonging to the order Procellariiformes, known for wandering the open seas.

Examples

A procellariiform seabird species
Many species of procellariiform seabird nest on remote islands far from human habitation.
Researchers tracked a procellariiform seabird for thousands of miles during its annual migration.
The procellariiform seabird glided effortlessly over the stormy southern ocean.
