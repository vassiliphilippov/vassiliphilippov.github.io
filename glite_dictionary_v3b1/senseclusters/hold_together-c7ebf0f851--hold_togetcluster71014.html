hold together

verb
keep parts joined; stay united
Pronunciation: / hoʊld təˈɡɛðər / (AmE), / həʊld təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
to keep parts or people united and functioning as a whole; to remain whole or not fall apart

Examples

To hold together tightly
If the parts don't hold together, the structure will collapse.
Strong leadership helped the group hold together during tough times.
The old book was so fragile it could barely hold together without extra care.
Her leadership held together the team during the crisis.
The cake held together surprisingly well, even without eggs.
The old book barely held together after so many years of use.

  • synonym: stick together - to remain united or attached
