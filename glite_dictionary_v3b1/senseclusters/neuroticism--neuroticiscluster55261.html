neuroticism

noun
tendency toward negative emotional states
8.7 0.65/m
Pronunciation: / n äÀàr…ëÀê.t…™Àås…™z.…ôm / (AmE), / nj ä…ôÀàr…ít.…™.s…™.z…ôm / (BrE)
A personality trait characterized by frequent experience of negative emotions such as anxiety, worry, fear, anger, and depression, not caused by any known neurological or organic disorder.

Examples

High neuroticism levels
Her high levels of neuroticism made even minor setbacks feel overwhelming.
People with low neuroticism tend to remain calm under pressure.
Psychologists often assess neuroticism as part of the Big Five personality traits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emotional instability - lack of emotional stability; easily upset or affected by stress
  • antonym: emotional stability - the trait of being calm and resistant to stress or anxiety
