bottomland

noun
low-lying, fertile land near a river
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑtəmˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəmˌlænd / (BrE)
low-lying land, especially along a river, that is often rich in alluvial soil and subject to flooding

Examples

The bottomland near river
After the river flooded, the bottomland was soaked but remained incredibly productive.
Farmers rely on the rich soil of the bottomland to grow their crops each spring.
The Mississippi Delta contains vast stretches of fertile bottomland ideal for agriculture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: floodplain - flat area of land next to a river prone to flooding
