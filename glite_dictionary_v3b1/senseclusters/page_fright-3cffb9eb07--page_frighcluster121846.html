page fright

noun
anxiety before public performance or speaking
7.6
Pronunciation: / peɪdʒ fraɪt / (AmE), / peɪdʒ fraɪt / (BrE)
Nervousness or anxiety experienced before performing or speaking in front of an audience, especially when presenting written material.

Examples

Page fright before speech
He struggled to overcome his page fright before giving the keynote speech.
Her page fright faded as soon as she started reading the poem aloud.
Many authors admit to feeling page fright at literary readings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stage fright - nervousness before performing on stage
