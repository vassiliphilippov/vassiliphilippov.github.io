the Irish Famine

noun
major famine in Ireland, 1845–1852
6.1
Pronunciation: / ði ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈfæmɪn / (AmE), / ði ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈfæmɪn / (BrE)
the famine that occurred in Ireland between 1845 and 1852, caused by a potato blight, resulting in widespread starvation and mass emigration

Examples

The Irish Famine disaster
Historians believe the Irish Famine fundamentally changed Ireland's population and society.
Many families were forced to emigrate to the United States during the Irish Famine.
The devastating effects of the Irish Famine are still remembered in Irish culture and history.
