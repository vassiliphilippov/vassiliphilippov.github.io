really

adverb
in actual fact; truly
Pronunciation: / ˈriːli /; / ˈrɪli / (AmE), / ˈriːəli /; / ˈrɪəli /; / ˈriːli / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is true, genuine, or actually the case

Examples

Really true
Do you really think it will snow tonight, or are you just hoping?
No one really saw the shark despite the rumors.
She really is coming to the party, even though she rarely goes out.
Do you really believe that story, or are you joking?
I really appreciate all the help you've given me this week.
It's really important to follow the instructions carefully.
Do you really believe those rumors about him?
I'm really not interested in going to that concert.
She was surprised to find the city really beautiful in the spring.
