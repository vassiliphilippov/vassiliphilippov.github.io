ostensible

adjective
apparent, but possibly not genuine
9.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /ɑː.ˈsten.sə.bəl/ /; / /ə.ˈsten.sə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɒ.ˈsten.sɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
appearing or stated to be true, but possibly not genuine or real

Examples

The ostensible reason
His ostensible concern for our safety seemed insincere.
She was the ostensible leader, though others held the real power.
The ostensible reason for the meeting was to discuss the budget, but other issues were addressed.
Although his ostensible concern was the budget, he really wanted more influence in the project.
She accepted his ostensible invitation to work late, suspecting there was another reason involved.
The company’s ostensible reason for the merger was efficiency, but market control was the real motivation.
His ostensible reason for leaving the job was stress, but he had already accepted another offer.
She gave an ostensible explanation for her absence, but few people believed it.
The ostensible purpose of the meeting was to discuss finances, though other matters were addressed.
