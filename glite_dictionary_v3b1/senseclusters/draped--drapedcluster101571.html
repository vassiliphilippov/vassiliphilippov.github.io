draped

adjective
covered with cloth in folds
3.9 2.8
Pronunciation: /dreɪpt/ (AmE), /dreɪpt/ (BrE)
covered or decorated with cloth or material that hangs in loose folds

Examples

Draped silk gown
The princess entered the hall with a draped silk gown that shimmered softly in the candlelight.
The statue stood draped in a white sheet, waiting to be unveiled.
They passed through heavy, draped curtains into the private lounge.
She entered the room, her shoulders draped with a delicate shawl.
The statue was draped in a velvet cloth for the ceremony.
Vines draped over the old fence, giving it a wild, natural beauty.
