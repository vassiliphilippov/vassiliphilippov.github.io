redwing

noun
small European thrush with reddish flanks
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
a small European or Asian thrush with reddish flanks and underwings (Turdus iliacus)

Examples

European redwing
During migration, large flocks of redwings can be seen across northern Europe.
The distinctive call of the redwing is often heard at dusk in rural areas.
We spotted a redwing feeding among the autumn leaves.
We spotted a redwing perched on the fence during our morning walk.
The redwing is easy to identify by the bright red patches on its wings.
In winter, flocks of redwing often visit gardens in search of berries.
