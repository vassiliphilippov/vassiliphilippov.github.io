fetalization

noun
retaining juvenile traits
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfiːtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfiːtələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an organism retains juvenile or fetal characteristics into adulthood

Examples

Fetalization in adults
Researchers observed fetalization in certain dog breeds that maintain puppy-like features as adults.
The concept of fetalization helps explain why some mammals have large eyes and rounded faces.
Evolutionary biologists debate the role of fetalization in the development of human traits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neoteny - the retention of juvenile features in the adult animal
