determinacy

noun
fixed sentencing system
8.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɜːrmɪnəsi / (AmE), / dɪˈtɜːmɪnəsi / (BrE)
the practice or quality of having fixed, definite prison sentences or penalties rather than indeterminate terms.

Examples

Determinacy in sentencing
The new law emphasized determinacy in sentencing to ensure fairness and consistency.
Advocates argue that determinacy helps reduce uncertainty for both victims and offenders.
The shift toward determinacy meant judges had less discretion in setting prison terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: definiteness - the quality of being clearly defined or determined
  • antonym: indeterminacy - the quality of having uncertain or variable prison sentences
