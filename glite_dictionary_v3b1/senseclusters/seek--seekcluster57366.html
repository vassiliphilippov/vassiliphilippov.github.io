seek

verb
inquire; ask for (information/advice)
1.0 80/m
Pronunciation: / siÀêk / (AmE), / siÀêk / (BrE)
to ask for or try to obtain information, advice, or help from someone

Examples

To seek advice
If you get lost, you should seek directions from someone nearby.
The organization encourages everyone to seek help if they are struggling.
Tourists often seek advice from hotel staff when exploring a new city.

Synonyms

  • ask
    1.0 939/m
    ask is a direct, everyday way to put a question to someone, while seek is a broader, sometimes formal attempt to obtain information or help.
  • inquire
    3.0 9.46/m
    inquire is a more formal way to ask for facts or details, while seek covers asking, trying, or looking for advice and help.
  • consult
    3.5 21/m
    consult is to get advice from an expert or source before acting, while seek covers asking or trying to obtain information or help.

Related Terms

  • synonym: request - to ask for something politely or formally
