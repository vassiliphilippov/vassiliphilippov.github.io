brevet

noun
temporary military commission
1.0
Pronunciation: / brəˈvɛt / (AmE), / ˈbrɛvɪt / (BrE)
an official certificate or document, especially one giving a military officer a temporary higher rank without extra pay, or a certificate for completing a long-distance cycling event

Examples

A brevet promotion
During the war, many officers were granted a brevet to fill urgent leadership roles.
Her brevet allowed her to command a larger unit, though her pay remained unchanged.
The general received a brevet raising him to major for his outstanding service.
After years of service, he was awarded a brevet that recognized his leadership in the field.
Cyclists who finish the 300-kilometer ride receive a special brevet as proof of their achievement.
The officer's brevet allowed him to command a larger unit, though his pay remained the same.
