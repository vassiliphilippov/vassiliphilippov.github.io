uprush

noun
sudden surge
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌrʌʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, strong upward surge or rise, either of emotion, activity, or a physical substance like water or air

Examples

An uprush of emotion
An uprush of excitement filled the stadium as the team scored the winning goal.
He was surprised by an uprush of anger during the heated discussion.
The news triggered an uprush of memories she thought she had forgotten.
The uprush of water after the storm surprised the swimmers on the shore.
A sudden uprush of air lifted the leaves high into the sky.
The volcano's uprush of ash could be seen from miles away.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ebb - a gradual decline or lessening of emotion or activity
  • synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement
  • synonym: wave - a sudden strong feeling or occurrence
