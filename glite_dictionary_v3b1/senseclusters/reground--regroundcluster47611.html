reground

verb
to reconnect to ground
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to connect an electrical device or circuit again to earth or a grounding conductor for safety or stability

Examples

To reground a wire
The technician needed to reground the faulty outlet to prevent electrical hazards.
After moving the equipment, the engineer made sure to reground all the connections.
It is important to reground the system after any major repairs to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disconnect - to remove a connection, especially for safety
  • synonym: ground - to connect an electrical device to earth for safety
