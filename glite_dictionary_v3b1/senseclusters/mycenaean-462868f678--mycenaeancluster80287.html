Mycenaean

noun
person from ancient Mycenae
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪsɪˈniːən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪsɪˈniːən / (BrE)
a native or inhabitant of ancient Mycenae, an important center of Greek civilization in the late Bronze Age.

Examples

A Mycenaean king
Archaeologists discovered the tomb of a wealthy Mycenaean dating back to the 14th century BC.
Many treasures were found in the graves of Mycenaeans near the ancient citadel.
The Mycenaean was known for his skill in warfare and his elaborate palatial culture.
