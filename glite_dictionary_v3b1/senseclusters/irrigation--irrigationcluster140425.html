irrigation

noun
artificial watering of land or crops
3.9 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪr.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪr.ɪˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The artificial supply of water to land or crops to help growth, typically by means of channels, ditches, or pipes.

Examples

Irrigation system for crops
Modern irrigation systems have made it possible to grow crops in the desert.
Proper irrigation is essential for rice cultivation in many countries.
The new dam has transformed farming in the region through improved irrigation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drainage - removal of excess water from land
