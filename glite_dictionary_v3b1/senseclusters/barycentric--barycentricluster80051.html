barycentric

adjective
center of mass
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛriˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌbærɪˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
relating to or measured from the center of mass of two or more bodies

Examples

Barycentric motion of planets
Astronomers use barycentric coordinates to describe the motion of planets in a solar system.
The barycentric position of the system helps scientists predict orbital changes.
Calculations based on a barycentric frame are essential in celestial mechanics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centric - relating to or situated at the center
