juxtapositional

adjective
side-by-side placement
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to the placement of elements side by side, especially without a connecting word or conjunction.

Examples

Juxtapositional sentence structure
The sentence uses a juxtapositional structure, placing two nouns together without a linking word.
In poetry, juxtapositional phrases can create striking contrasts between images.
Some languages prefer juxtapositional constructions instead of using conjunctions to connect words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjacent - next to or adjoining something else
  • antonym: conjunctive - joined together, especially by a conjunction
