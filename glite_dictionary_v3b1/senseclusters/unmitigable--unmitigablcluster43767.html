unmitigable

adjective
unable to be lessened or softened
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmɪt̬ɪɡəbl̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɪtɪɡəbl̩ / (BrE)
unable to be lessened, softened, or reduced in severity or intensity.

Examples

An unmitigable crime
Despite their pleas, the judge saw the crime as unmitigable and imposed the maximum sentence.
His anger was so profound and unmitigable that nothing could calm him down.
The hurricane caused unmitigable damage across the coastal region, leaving widespread devastation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irreducible - impossible to make smaller or simpler
  • antonym: mitigable - capable of being lessened or moderated
