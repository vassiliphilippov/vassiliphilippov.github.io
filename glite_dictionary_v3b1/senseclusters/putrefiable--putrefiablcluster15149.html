putrefiable

adjective
liable to decay or become putrid
Pronunciation: / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfaɪəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌpjuːtrɪˈfaɪəbl̩ / (BrE)
liable to decay or spoil or become putrid; capable of undergoing putrefaction.

Examples

Putrefiable food items
Meat and dairy products are among the most putrefiable food items in your refrigerator.
Organic waste is highly putrefiable and attracts bacteria if not disposed of properly.
The scientist noted that the sample was putrefiable and required immediate analysis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonperishable - not subject to rapid spoilage or decay
  • synonym: perishable - liable to spoil or decay quickly
