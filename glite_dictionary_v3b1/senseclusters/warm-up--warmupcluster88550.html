warm-up

noun
preparatory exercise session
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrm ˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːm ˌʌp /; / ˈwɔːm ʌp / (BrE)
light exercise or activity done to prepare for more strenuous activity or performance

Examples

A warm-up routine
Her warm-up routine includes stretching and light jogging.
It is important to do a proper warm-up before running to prevent injury.
The team gathered on the field for their pre-game warm-up.
Be sure to do a warm-up before lifting heavy weights to avoid injury.
Her warm-up routine takes about fifteen minutes before every swim practice.
The choir began with a short warm-up to relax their vocal cords.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool-down - period of light activity to help the body recover after strenuous exercise
