whip

noun
flexibility of a golf club shaft
8.2
Pronunciation: / wɪp / (AmE), / wɪp / (BrE)
The flexibility of the shaft of a golf club, affecting the way it bends during a swing.

Examples

Club whip
Some professional golfers prefer less whip in their clubs for more control.
The whip of the shaft allowed for greater distance on his swing.
This driver has a lot of whip, making it easier to launch the ball higher.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexibility - the quality of bending easily without breaking
