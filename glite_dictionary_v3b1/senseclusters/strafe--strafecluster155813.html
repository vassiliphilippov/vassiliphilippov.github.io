strafe

verb
attack from a low-flying aircraft
Pronunciation: / streɪf / (AmE), / streɪf / (BrE)
to attack a target with machine-gun or cannon fire (or bombs) from a low-flying aircraft

Examples

To strafe enemy troops
Helicopters would sometimes strafe the jungle to flush out hidden combatants.
Several villages were strafed during the surprise attack, causing widespread panic.
The fighter jets began to strafe the enemy positions along the riverbank.
Enemy fighters began to strafe the convoy as it crossed the bridge.
Helicopters were seen strafing the hillside in support of ground troops.
The air force strafed enemy positions at dawn, causing chaos among their ranks.
The fighter jets strafed the enemy convoy as it moved along the road.
In the video game, you can strafe left and right to avoid incoming fire.
The helicopter began to strafe the area, forcing the soldiers to take cover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombard - to attack continuously with bombs, shells, or missiles
  • antonym: defend - to protect from attack
  • synonym: dive-bomb - to attack from a steep diving flight in an aircraft
