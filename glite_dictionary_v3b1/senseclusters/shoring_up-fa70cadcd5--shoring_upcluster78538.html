shoring up

noun
propping up a structure with supports
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrɪŋ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːrɪŋ ʌp / (BrE)
the act of supporting or propping up a structure, wall, or building with shores or supports to prevent collapse or provide stability

Examples

Shoring up a wall
Before renovation could start, extensive shoring up was needed to stabilize the historic facade.
Emergency crews focused on the shoring up of the collapsed building’s remains.
The engineers began the shoring up of the old bridge to make it safe for traffic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collapse - the sudden failure of a structure
  • synonym: reinforcement - the action of strengthening or supporting something
