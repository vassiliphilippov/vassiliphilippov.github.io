lay

verb
impose a duty, burden, or punishment
Pronunciation: / leɪ / (AmE), / leɪ / (BrE)
to impose (a duty, burden, or punishment) on someone or something

Examples

To lay a tax
New regulations lay strict requirements on businesses in the area.
She did not want to lay extra responsibilities on her team during the busy season.
The court laid a heavy fine on the company for its negligence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impose - to force something, such as a duty or burden, to be accepted or obeyed
