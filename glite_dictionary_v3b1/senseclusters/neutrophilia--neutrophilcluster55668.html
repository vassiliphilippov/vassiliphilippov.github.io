neutrophilia

noun
high neutrophil count
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːtrəˈfɪliə / (AmE), / ˌnjuːtrəˈfɪliə / (BrE)
an increase in the number of neutrophils in the blood, usually as a response to infection or inflammation

Examples

High neutrophilia count
The doctor explained that neutrophilia often indicates the body is fighting an infection.
After surgery, the patient developed neutrophilia as part of the healing process.
A blood test revealed neutrophilia, prompting further investigation for possible inflammation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leukocytosis - an increase in the total number of white blood cells
  • antonym: neutropenia - a decrease in the number of neutrophils in the blood
