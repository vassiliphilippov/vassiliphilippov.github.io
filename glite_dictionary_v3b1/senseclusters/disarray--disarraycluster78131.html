Examples

To disarray the room

Synonyms

  • disorganize
    4.5 0.24/m
    disorganize means causing a system or plan to lose order and structure, while disarray describes the messy, confused state that follows.
  • disarrange
    6.4 0.03/m
    disarrange means moving or upsetting items so they are out of place, while disarray is the general messy state that results.
  • disorder
    5.5 1.34/m
    disorder disturbs an existing order or arrangement, while disarray is the visible untidiness and confusion that appears afterward.
  • jumble
    3.8 1.54/m
    jumble mixes things together in a confused way, often making a pile of items, while disarray means disorder or untidiness.
  • mix up
    2.7 2.06/m
    mix up shuffles or confuses items or ideas so they are unclear, while disarray focuses on physical untidiness and disorder.
  • mess up
    3.0 1.13/m
    mess up makes something untidy or broken in casual speech, while disarray is a more neutral word for disorder.
  • confuse
    1.9 2.57/m
    confuse makes ideas or people unclear in mind or meaning, while disarray refers to things being messy or out of order.
