transmigration

noun
passing of a soul into another body after death
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzmaɪˈɡreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzmʌɪˈɡreɪʃən / (BrE)
the passage of a soul into another body after death, especially as believed in some religions and philosophies

Examples

The transmigration of souls
Many ancient cultures held strong beliefs in the transmigration of souls.
She wrote an essay exploring transmigration and its impact on spiritual traditions.
The doctrine of transmigration is central to certain schools of Hindu philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metempsychosis - the supposed transmigration at death of the soul of a human being or animal into a new body
  • synonym: reincarnation - the rebirth of a soul in a new body
