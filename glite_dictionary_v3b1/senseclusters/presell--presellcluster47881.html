presell

verb
to sell in advance
4.8
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈsel/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsel/ (AmE), /priː.ˈsel/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsel/ (BrE)
to sell a product or service before it is available or officially released

Examples

To presell a product
The publisher decided to presell the book to gauge reader interest before printing.
Many companies presell their products to secure funding for production.
Customers lined up online to presell the highly anticipated video game.
The company decided to presell tickets for the concert to gauge interest.
Many video games are presold months before their official release date.
They managed to presell all the apartments in the new building before construction began.

  • synonym: preorder - to order a product before it is available
  • degree lower: sell - to exchange something for money
