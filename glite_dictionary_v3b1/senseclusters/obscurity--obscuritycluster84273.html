obscurity

noun
state of being unclear or difficult to understand
5.2
Pronunciation: / əbˈskjʊrəti / (AmE), / əbˈskjʊərɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being unclear, difficult to understand, or not easily seen or recognized

Examples

Intellectual obscurity
Her explanation was shrouded in obscurity, leaving the audience feeling confused.
Legal documents are often criticized for their unnecessary obscurity.
The obscurity of his writing made it difficult for readers to grasp his main argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguity - the quality of being open to more than one interpretation
  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
