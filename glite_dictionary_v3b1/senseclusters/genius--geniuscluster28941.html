genius

noun
exceptional natural talent
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːniəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːniəs / (BrE)
exceptional natural talent or ability, especially in a particular area

Examples

Genius for music

Her genius for languages became apparent at an early age.

Her genius for mathematics was evident from a young age.
Many consider Beethoven's genius to be unmatched in classical music.
The invention displayed remarkable genius and creativity.
Her genius for painting was apparent from an early age.

  • antonym: mediocrity - lack of exceptional ability or quality
  • degree higher: prodigy - a person, especially a young one, endowed with extraordinary ability
  • synonym: talent - a natural aptitude or skill
