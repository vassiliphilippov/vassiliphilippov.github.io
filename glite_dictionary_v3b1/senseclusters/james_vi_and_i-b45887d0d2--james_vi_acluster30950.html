James VI and I

proper noun
king of Scotland, England, and Ireland (1567–1625)
9.1
Pronunciation: /dʒeɪmz ðə sɪksθ ənd ðə fɜːrst/ /; / /dʒeɪmz ðə sɪksθ ænd ðə fɜːrst/ (AmE), /dʒeɪmz ðə sɪksθ ənd ðə fɜːst/ /; / /dʒeɪmz ðə sɪksθ ænd ðə fɜːst/ (BrE)
the king of Scotland as James VI from 1567 to 1625 and of England and Ireland as James I from 1603 to 1625, known for uniting the crowns of England and Scotland and advocating the divine right of kings

Historians often debate the legacy of James VI and I regarding his views on the monarchy.
