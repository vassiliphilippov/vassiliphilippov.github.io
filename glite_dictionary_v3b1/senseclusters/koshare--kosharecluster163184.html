koshare

noun
Pueblo ceremonial clown
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈʃɑːreɪ / (AmE), / kəʊˈʃɑːreɪ / (BrE)
a sacred clown or trickster figure who performs in Pueblo Native American ceremonies to entertain and teach lessons

Examples

The koshare clown
During the festival, the koshare made the audience laugh while sharing important cultural lessons.
The children watched as the koshare danced and played tricks in the ceremonial plaza.
A koshare often uses humor to challenge social rules and encourage reflection among the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clown - a performer who entertains with jokes and antics
  • synonym: trickster - a figure in folklore who uses humor or mischief to teach or challenge norms
