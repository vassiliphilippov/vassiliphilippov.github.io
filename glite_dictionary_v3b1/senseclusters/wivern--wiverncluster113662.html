wivern

noun
heraldic two-legged dragon
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪvərn / (AmE), / ˈwɪvən / (BrE)
A mythical fire-breathing dragon with two legs, wings, and a serpentine tail, often depicted in medieval heraldry.

Examples

A wivern dragon
In medieval stories, a wivern was said to guard a hidden treasure.
Many noble families used the image of a wivern in their coats of arms to symbolize strength.
The knight's shield featured a fearsome wivern with outstretched wings and a flaming tongue.
