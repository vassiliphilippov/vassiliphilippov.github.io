enharmonic

noun
musical equivalent note
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛnhɑːrˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛnhɑːˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
a note, interval, or chord that sounds the same as another but is written differently in musical notation

Examples

An enharmonic note
In music theory, F sharp and G flat are considered enharmonic notes.
The composer used an enharmonic to smoothly change keys in the piece.
Understanding enharmonic equivalents is important for reading complex sheet music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equivalent - having the same value, function, or meaning
