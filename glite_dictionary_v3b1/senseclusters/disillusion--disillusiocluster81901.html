disillusion

noun
freedom from false beliefs or illusions
4.3 5.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.sɪ.ˈluː.ʒən/ /; / /dɪ.sə.ˈluː.ʒən/ (AmE), /dɪ.sɪ.ˈluː.ʒən/ /; / /dɪ.sə.ˈluː.ʒən/ (BrE)
the process or state of being freed from false beliefs or illusions, especially resulting in disappointment

Examples

A harsh disillusion
After years of optimism, the harsh facts brought about a painful disillusion.
His disillusion with politics began when he realized promises were rarely kept.
Traveling abroad was a source of disillusion as her idealized views were challenged by reality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enlightenment - the act of gaining knowledge and truth, often bringing freedom from illusion
  • antonym: illusion - a false idea or belief
