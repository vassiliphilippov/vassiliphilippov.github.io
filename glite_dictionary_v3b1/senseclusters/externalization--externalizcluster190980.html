externalization

noun
expressing something internally in outward form
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɜrnələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜːnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of expressing or embodying something internally experienced in an outward or visible form.

Examples

Externalization of feelings
His artwork was an externalization of his private thoughts and emotions.
The play served as a powerful externalization of social issues facing the community.
Therapy allowed him to achieve the externalization of his anxieties through dialogue.
Many artists view their work as an externalisation of their inner thoughts and feelings.
The externalisation of his emotions made it easier for others to understand him.
Therapists often encourage the externalisation of problems to help clients gain perspective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embodiment - the act of giving a concrete form to something abstract
  • synonym: externalization - the act of expressing something internally in an outward form (American spelling)
  • antonym: internalisation - the process of integrating something into one's internal understanding or behaviour
