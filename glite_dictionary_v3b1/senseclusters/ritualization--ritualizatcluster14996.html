ritualization

noun
making into a ritual
8.1
Pronunciation: /rɪt.ʃuː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪt.juː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪt.ʃuː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪt.juː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of making actions or events into rituals, or carrying them out in a formal, repeated way

Examples

Cultural ritualization
The ritualization of the graduation ceremony gave it a sense of tradition and importance.
Some animals show ritualization in their mating behaviors, repeating specific actions each season.
Daily meetings became a form of ritualization that helped the team stay organized and focused.
The ritualization of morning coffee helps many people start their day with a sense of order.
In some animal species, the ritualization of courtship behaviors increases the chance of successful mating.
Cultural traditions often arise from the ritualization of everyday activities over generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceremonial - relating to or used in a ceremony or ritual
  • synonym: habitualization - the process of making something a habit
  • synonym: ritual - a set of fixed actions performed regularly, often with symbolic meaning
  • antonym: routine - a regular way of doing things, often without special meaning
