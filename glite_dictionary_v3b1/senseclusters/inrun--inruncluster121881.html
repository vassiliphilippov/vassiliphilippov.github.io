inrun

noun
ski jump approach
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌrʌn / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌrʌn / (BrE)
the slope or track that a skier uses to gain speed before taking off in ski jumping or freestyle skiing

Examples

The inrun slope
The athlete accelerated down the inrun before launching into the air.
Maintenance crews prepared the inrun to ensure a smooth surface for the competition.
A longer inrun allows skiers to reach higher speeds before the jump.
