borshch

noun
Eastern European beetroot soup
Pronunciation: / bɔrʃtʃ / (AmE), / bɔːʃtʃ / (BrE)
A traditional Eastern European soup, especially Russian or Polish, made with beetroot as its main ingredient, often served hot or cold.

Examples

Traditional borshch soup
My grandmother makes borshch every winter using a traditional family recipe.
She ordered a bowl of borshch with sour cream at the Ukrainian restaurant.
The vibrant red color of borshch comes from its main ingredient, beetroot.
