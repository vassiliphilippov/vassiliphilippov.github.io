Cassegrain

proper noun
French inventor surname
Pronunciation: / ˌkæsəˈɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˌkæsəˈɡreɪn / (BrE)
a French surname, especially referring to Laurent Cassegrain, a 17th-century priest and inventor of the Cassegrain reflector

Examples

Laurent Cassegrain
The Cassegrain design revolutionized the construction of reflecting telescopes.
Laurent Cassegrain is credited with inventing a new type of telescope in the 17th century.
Many modern observatories use the Cassegrain configuration for their main telescopes.
