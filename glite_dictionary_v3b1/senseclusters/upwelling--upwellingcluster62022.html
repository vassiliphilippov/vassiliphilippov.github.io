upwelling

noun
upward movement of fluid
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈʌp.wel.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʌp.wəl.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʌp.wel.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʌp.wəl.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the upward movement of a liquid, gas, or other material toward the surface, especially from below the ground or sea.

Examples

Upwelling of water
The upwelling of groundwater created a small spring in the middle of the field.
Scientists studied the upwelling of volcanic gases from cracks in the earth's surface.
A sudden upwelling of warm air lifted the hot air balloon higher into the sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eruption - a sudden release or movement of material from below the surface
  • antonym: subsidence - the downward movement of a surface or material
