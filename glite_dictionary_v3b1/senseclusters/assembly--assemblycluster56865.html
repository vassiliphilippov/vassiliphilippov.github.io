assembly

noun
unit of fitted-together components
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛm.bli /; / əˈsɛmbli / (AmE), / əˈsɛm.bli /; / əˈsɛmbli / (BrE)
a unit made by putting parts or components together, especially in machinery or construction

Examples

Engine assembly
Before shipping, the factory tests each assembly for defects and correct operation.
Each car is made from hundreds of individual assemblies that are put together on the production line.
The technician checked the assembly to ensure all the screws were tightened properly.
Before shipping, the manufacturer inspects every gear assembly for quality and performance.
Each printer contains a fuser assembly that heats and bonds the toner to the paper.
The engine assembly was carefully lowered into the car's chassis by the technicians.
