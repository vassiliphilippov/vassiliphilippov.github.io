common stinkhorn

noun
a widespread foul-smelling fungus
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈstɪŋkˌhɔrn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈstɪŋkˌhɔːn / (BrE)
A widespread species of fungus (Phallus impudicus) known for its foul odor, sometimes formerly used in folk remedies for rheumatism.

Examples

The common stinkhorn fungus
Gardeners were surprised to discover a common stinkhorn growing near the compost heap.
The common stinkhorn can often be found in damp woodland during late summer and autumn.
The foul smell of the common stinkhorn attracts flies, which help spread its spores.
