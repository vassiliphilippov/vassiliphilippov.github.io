centrifugal

adjective
tending away from centralization
9.3
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈtrɪfjəɡəl /; / sɛnˈtrɪfjəgəl / (AmE), / sɛnˈtrɪfjʊɡəl /; / sɛnˈtrɪfjʊgəl / (BrE)
moving or tending to move away from a center or central point, either physically or in a figurative sense (such as power or authority)

Examples

Centrifugal forces
As regional governments gained more power, centrifugal forces became more apparent.
Reforms were enacted to counter centrifugal pressures within the organization.
The federation had several centrifugal tendencies that threatened its unity.
A centrifugal pump moves fluids by converting rotational energy to hydrodynamic energy.
In amusement park rides, riders experience centrifugal forces as the ride spins rapidly.
The spinning drum creates a centrifugal force that pushes the clothes against the washer walls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralizing - seeking to bring under central control, the antonym of 'centrifugal' in this context
  • antonym: centripetal - moving or tending to move toward a center
