light-of-love

noun
fickle or promiscuous woman (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪt əv ˈlʌv / (AmE), / ˌlaɪt əv ˈlʌv / (BrE)
an inconstant or fickle woman in love; historically, a term for a woman who is considered promiscuous or unfaithful

Examples

A light-of-love woman
In Shakespeare's plays, a light-of-love is often the subject of both admiration and scorn.
She was labeled a light-of-love by the village gossips for changing suitors so frequently.
The nobleman abandoned his wife for a light-of-love whose affections proved short-lived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coquette - a woman who flirts flirtatiously; a flirt
  • antonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast in love
