ankyloglossia

noun
restricted tongue movement from short tissue under tongue
6.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /æŋ.kɪ.lə.ˈgɑː.sɪə/ /; / /æŋ.kɪ.lə.ˈgɑː.siː.ə/ (AmE), /æŋ.kɪ.lə.ˈgɒ.sɪə/ /; / /æŋ.kɪ.lə.ˈgɒ.siː.ə/ (BrE)
A congenital condition in which the tissue connecting the underside of the tongue to the floor of the mouth is abnormally short, restricting tongue movement.

Examples

Congenital ankyloglossia condition
Babies born with ankyloglossia may have difficulty breastfeeding due to limited tongue movement.
Surgical intervention can correct ankyloglossia and improve speech development in some children.
The pediatrician diagnosed the infant with ankyloglossia during a routine check-up.
