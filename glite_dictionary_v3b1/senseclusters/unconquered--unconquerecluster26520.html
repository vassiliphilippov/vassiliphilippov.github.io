unconquered

adjective
not conquered or defeated
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkɑŋkərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɒŋkəd / (BrE)
(of a place, people, or spirit) not taken control of or defeated by force.

Examples

An unconquered spirit
Her unconquered spirit inspired everyone around her to keep fighting.
The mountain remained unconquered despite several attempts by experienced climbers.
This land has stood unconquered for thousands of years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conquered - having been overcome or taken control of by force
