harrowing

adjective
extremely distressing or painful
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhæroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhærəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
extremely distressing or painful; causing great anxiety or sorrow.

Examples

A harrowing experience
It was a harrowing experience to see the aftermath of the disaster.
She found it harrowing to relive those memories during therapy.
The film provides a harrowing account of life during wartime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comforting - alleviating distress, bringing comfort
  • synonym: distressing - causing anxiety, sorrow or pain
