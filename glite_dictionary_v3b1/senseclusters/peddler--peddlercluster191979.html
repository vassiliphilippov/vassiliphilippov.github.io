peddler

noun
itinerant street vendor
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛdlɚ /; / ˈpɛdələr / (AmE), / ˈpɛdlə / (BrE)
a person who travels from place to place selling goods, typically in the streets or at markets

Examples

Street peddler
A peddler knocked on our door yesterday, hoping to sell cleaning supplies.
Street peddlers often gather at the festival, selling food, toys, and souvenirs.
The peddler wandered through the village, offering colorful scarves and trinkets to passersby.
A pedlar stood at the corner, offering fresh fruit from his cart.
Many people in the town recognized the local pedlar by his distinctive bell.
The pedlar moved from village to village, selling trinkets and fabric.

Related Terms

  • antonym: customer - a person who buys goods or services
  • synonym: hawker - a person who sells goods in the street or door-to-door
  • synonym: peddler - someone who travels about selling their wares; chiefly American spelling
  • synonym: vendor - a person or company offering something for sale
