electromotive force

noun
source voltage causing electric current
5.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛk.trəˈmoʊ.tɪv fɔrs / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛk.trəˈməʊ.tɪv fɔːs / (BrE)
The energy supplied by a source per unit electric charge that causes current to flow in a circuit, measured in volts.

Examples

The electromotive force voltage
A higher electromotive force results in a greater flow of electricity in the wire.
Scientists measured the electromotive force generated by the chemical reaction inside the cell.
The battery provides the electromotive force that drives current through the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: potential difference - difference in electric potential between two points
  • synonym: voltage - electrical potential difference in a circuit
