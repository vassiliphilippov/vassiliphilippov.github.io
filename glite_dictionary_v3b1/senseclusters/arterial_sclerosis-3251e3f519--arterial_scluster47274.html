arterial sclerosis

noun
hardening of artery walls
5.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / …ëÀêrÀàt…™ri…ôl skl…öÀào äs…™s / (AmE), / …ëÀêÀàt…™…ôri…ôl skl…™…ôÀàr…ô äs…™s / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the walls of the arteries become thickened and hardened, often restricting blood flow.

Examples

Arterial sclerosis disease
After years of high cholesterol, he was diagnosed with arterial sclerosis in his early sixties.
Studies have shown that diet and exercise can help prevent arterial sclerosis.
The doctor explained that arterial sclerosis could increase the risk of heart attacks.

Synonyms

  • arteriosclerosis
    7.4 0.20/m
    arteriosclerosis is the standard medical name for thick, stiff artery walls, while arterial sclerosis is a less common variant term.
  • hardening of the arteries
    3.6 0.14/m
    hardening of the arteries is a plain descriptive phrase used in patient explanations, while arterial sclerosis is the medical term.
  • induration of the arteries
    8.9 0.00/m
    induration of the arteries is a formal pathology phrase for hardened artery tissue, while arterial sclerosis is the usual clinical diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arteriosclerosis - the thickening and hardening of the walls of the arteries
  • synonym: atherosclerosis - a type of arteriosclerosis involving the buildup of fats in artery walls
