honor guard

noun
ceremonial escort or guard of honor
2.6 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.nər gɑːrd/ (AmE), /ˈɒ.nə gɑːd/ (BrE)
A group of people, usually military, who act as an escort or perform ceremonial duties for a distinguished guest or at a funeral.

Examples

A honor guard ceremony
A military honor guard welcomed the visiting dignitary at the airport.
The honor guard stood at attention as the casket was carried into the church.
The presidential honor guard performed a precise drill during the state ceremony.
