incompatibility

noun
state of things that cannot coexist or be true together
4.6 5.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kəm.pə.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ɪn.kəm.pæt.ɪ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ɪn.kəm.pə.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ɪn.kəm.pæt.ɪ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the state of being such that two or more things, especially statements or propositions, cannot all be true or valid at the same time

Examples

Logical incompatibility
Logical incompatibility makes it impossible for both claims to be correct simultaneously.
The incompatibility of these theories was the subject of much debate among philosophers.
There is a clear incompatibility between the witness’s statement and the evidence presented.
