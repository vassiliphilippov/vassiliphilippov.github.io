amebic dysentery

noun
dysentery caused by ameba infection
7.6 6.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmiː.bɪk ˈdɪs.ən.tər.iː/ /; / /ə.ˈmiː.bɪk ˈdɪs.ən.triː/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmiː.bɪk ˈdɪs.ən.triː/ /; / /ə.ˈmiː.bɪk ˈdɪs.ən.tə.riː/ (BrE)
A severe intestinal infection caused by the parasite Entamoeba histolytica, typically spread through contaminated food or water, and characterized by severe diarrhea, abdominal pain, and often blood in the stool.

Examples

Severe amebic dysentery infection
He was hospitalized after symptoms of amebic dysentery appeared, including persistent diarrhea and fever.
Many travelers to tropical countries are advised on how to avoid contracting amebic dysentery.
Untreated amebic dysentery can lead to severe dehydration and other complications.
Drinking untreated water in the area increased the risk of contracting amoebic dysentery.
Severe abdominal cramps can be a symptom of amoebic dysentery in infected individuals.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with amoebic dysentery after a series of laboratory tests.
