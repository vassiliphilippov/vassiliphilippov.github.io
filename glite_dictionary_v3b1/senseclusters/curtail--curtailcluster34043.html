curtail

verb
reduce or cut short
Pronunciation: / kɚˈteɪl / (AmE), / kɜːˈteɪl /; / kəˈteɪl / (BrE)
to reduce, limit, or cut something short; to stop something before it is finished or at its full extent

Examples

To curtail spending
Due to budget cuts, the school had to curtail extracurricular activities.
His trip was curtailed by an unexpected family emergency.
The new policy seeks to curtail excessive spending without affecting essential services.
New laws were enacted to curtail smoking in public places.
The company had to curtail its spending due to budget cuts.
They decided to curtail their vacation because of the weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expand - to increase in extent, number, volume, or scope
  • antonym: extend - to increase in length or duration
  • synonym: reduce - make smaller in size, amount, or number
  • synonym: shorten - make or become shorter
