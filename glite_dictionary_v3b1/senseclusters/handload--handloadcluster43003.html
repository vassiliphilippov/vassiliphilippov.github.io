handload

verb
to load by hand
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈhændˌləʊd / (BrE)
to load something by hand instead of using machines or automated equipment

Examples

To handload boxes
After the conveyor belt broke, workers had to handload every box onto the truck.
It took much longer to handload the equipment than to use the usual machinery.
They decided to handload the fragile items to prevent any damage during transport.

Related Terms

  • antonym: automate - to use machines or technology to perform a task
  • antonym: unload - to remove goods from a vehicle or container
