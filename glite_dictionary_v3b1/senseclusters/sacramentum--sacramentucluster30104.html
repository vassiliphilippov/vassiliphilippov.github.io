sacramentum

noun
Christian religious rite
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / ˌsakrəˈmɛntəm / (BrE)
a Christian religious rite, such as baptism or the Eucharist, regarded as a means of receiving divine grace

Examples

Christian sacramentum
In early Christianity, the sacramentum was seen as a sacred promise between believers and God.
The priest described the importance of each sacramentum in the spiritual life of the church.
Baptism is considered a fundamental sacramentum in many Christian traditions.
