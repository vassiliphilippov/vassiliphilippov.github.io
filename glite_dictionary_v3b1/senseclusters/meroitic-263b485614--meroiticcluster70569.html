Meroitic

noun
ancient Nubian language
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛroʊˈɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛrəʊˈɪtɪk / (BrE)
the language of the ancient kingdom of Meroë, known from inscriptions and short texts dating from about 300 BCE to 400 CE

Examples

Meroitic language texts
Scholars are still working to fully understand the Meroitic script found on ancient monuments.
The Meroitic language was used in the kingdom of Meroë for several centuries.
Few people today can read Meroitic, as much of it remains undeciphered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nubian - the group of languages spoken in Nubia, including ancient and modern forms
