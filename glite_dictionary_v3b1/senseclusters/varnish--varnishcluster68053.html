varnish

verb
to conceal faults
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
to make something seem more attractive or acceptable by hiding its faults or problems, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

To varnish the truth
He kept varnishing his CV, exaggerating responsibilities to make a better impression.
The company tried varnishing its poor safety record in the annual report.
Instead of varnishing the truth, she decided to be completely honest about her mistakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover up - to hide the truth about something
  • synonym: embellish - to make a story or statement more interesting by adding details, often untrue
  • antonym: reveal - to make something known or visible
