aftermarket

noun
market after IPO
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæf.tər.mɑːr.kɪt/ (AmE), /ˈæf.tə.mɑː.kɪt/ /; / /ˈɑːf.tə.mɑː.kɪt/ (BrE)
the market for buying and selling securities after they have been issued in an initial public offering or similar event

Examples

Aftermarket stock trading
Investors paid close attention to the aftermarket to see how the new stock would perform.
The company's shares rose sharply in the aftermarket following a successful IPO.
Many traders prefer to buy in the aftermarket rather than during the initial offering.

Related Terms

  • antonym: primary market - a market where new securities are issued and sold for the first time
  • synonym: secondary market - a market where previously issued securities are traded among investors
