undersign

verb
sign at the bottom of a document
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈsaɪn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to sign one's name at the bottom of a document, especially as a means of authentication or agreement

Examples

To undersign a document
All partners must undersign the agreement before it becomes effective.
Each applicant is required to undersign the form in the presence of a notary.
The document was undersigned by the company's directors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sign - to write one's name on a document to show agreement or approval
