consonant system

noun
set of consonant sounds in a language
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.sə.nənt ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
the complete set of consonant sounds used in the phonology of a particular language

Examples

The consonant system of a language
Children learning Spanish may find its consonant system simpler than that of Polish.
Linguists compared the consonant systems of several indigenous languages in the region.
The English consonant system includes both voiced and voiceless sounds like /b/ and /p/.
