pass

verb
be inherited or transferred to someone
Pronunciation: / pæs / (AmE), / pɑːs / (BrE)
to transfer property, rights, or titles to another person

Examples

Rights pass to heirs
Ownership of the land passed to several distant relatives after the owner's will was read.
The title to the house will pass to his daughter upon his death.
Under the new law, property passes automatically to the surviving spouse.
All rights to the invention pass to the university upon graduation.
Ownership of the company passed into foreign hands after the merger.
When her father died, the house passed to her eldest brother.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devolve - (of property or rights) to be passed on to someone else, especially by law
  • antonym: retain - to keep possession or control of something
  • synonym: transfer - to move something from one person or place to another
