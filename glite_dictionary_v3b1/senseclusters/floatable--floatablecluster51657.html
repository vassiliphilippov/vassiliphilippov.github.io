floatable

adjective
able to stay suspended
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtəbəl / (BrE)
able to float or stay on the surface of a liquid or suspended in a gas

Examples

Floatable plastic toy
The lightweight plastic is highly floatable and will not sink in water.
Engineers designed the device to be floatable in case it falls into a river.
Fine dust particles are floatable in the still air and slowly settle after hours.
The new life jacket is highly floatable and keeps swimmers safe in the water.
Engineers tested whether the material was floatable before using it in the boat's design.
She bought a floatable toy for her child to play with in the bathtub.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buoyant - able to float or rise in a fluid
  • antonym: sinkable - able to sink in a fluid
