overblown

adjective
excessively pompous or exaggerated
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbloʊn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbləʊn / (BrE)
exaggerated, pompous, or pretentious; inflated beyond what is reasonable or justified, especially in speech or manner

Examples

Overblown claims
Her overblown claims did not impress the skeptical audience.
Some critics dismissed the film’s style as overblown and self-indulgent.
The senator gave an overblown speech that failed to address any real issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grandiloquent - using lofty or extravagant language
  • antonym: modest - not boastful or exaggerated
  • synonym: pompous - affectedly grand, solemn, or self-important
