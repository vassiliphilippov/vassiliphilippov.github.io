heart

noun
courage to continue despite difficulties
1.0 1.6
Pronunciation: /hɑːrt/ (AmE), /hɑːt/ (BrE)
the center of emotions, especially love, courage, or compassion; feelings or emotional strength

Examples

A kind heart
After his failure, he didn't have the heart to try again immediately.
Even when the team was losing, she never lost heart and kept pushing until the end.
You have to have a lot of heart to make it through such tough times.
Deep in her heart, she knew she had made the right decision.
He finally spoke from the heart, admitting his true feelings.
The film touched everyone’s heart with its powerful story.
After much debate, she finally had a change of heart about the job offer.
His heart was no longer in the project, and it showed in his work.
Sometimes, following your heart is better than listening to logic.
