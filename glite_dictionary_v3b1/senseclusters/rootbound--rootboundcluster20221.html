rootbound

adjective
plant with roots tangled from outgrowing its pot
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈrutˌbaʊnd /; / ˈruːtˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈruːtˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a plant) having roots that are densely tangled or matted because the plant has outgrown its container

Examples

Rootbound plant
Gardeners often check if a plant is rootbound before transplanting.
If your plant looks unhealthy and is rootbound, consider repotting it soon.
The fern became rootbound and had to be moved to a larger pot.
Rootbound flowers often stop growing properly until they are transplanted to a larger container.
A rootbound tree needs to have its roots loosened before it can thrive in a new location.
The plant became rootbound after staying in the same pot for several years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pot-bound - (of a plant) having roots confined by the pot, similar to rootbound
