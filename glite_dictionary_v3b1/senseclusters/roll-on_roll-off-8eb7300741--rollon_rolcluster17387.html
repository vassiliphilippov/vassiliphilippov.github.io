roll-on roll-off

noun
vehicle transport method allowing self-driving on/off
2.6 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊl.ɑːn ˈroʊl.ɔːf/ /; / /ˈroʊl.ən ˈroʊl.ɔːf/ /; / /ˈroʊl.ɑːn ˈroʊl.ɑːf/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊl.ɒn ˈrəʊl.ɒf/ /; / /ˈrəʊl.ɒn ˈrəʊl.ɔːf/ (BrE)
A method of transporting vehicles by allowing them to be driven on and off the carrier, such as a ferry or ship, under their own power.

Examples

A roll-on roll-off ferry
A roll-on roll-off ferry can load dozens of vehicles at once, making it efficient for transportation.
The port specializes in roll-on roll-off services for cars and trucks.
They shipped the construction equipment overseas using a roll-on roll-off vessel.
