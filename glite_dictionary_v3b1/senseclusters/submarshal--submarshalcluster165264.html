submarshal

noun
junior military officer
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌmɑrʃəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌmɑːʃəl / (BrE)
a person who assists a marshal, especially in organizing people or events (such as in military, historical, or ceremonial contexts)

Examples

The submarshal was tasked with ensuring the troops received their supplies on time.

As a submarshal, she ensured all participants followed the event schedule.
Each submarshal reported directly to the chief marshal before the ceremony began.
