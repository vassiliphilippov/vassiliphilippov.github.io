straw

noun
plant stalks used for bedding or weaving
Pronunciation: / strɔ / (AmE), / strɔː / (BrE)
the dried stalks of cereal plants, used as bedding for animals or for making things like baskets and hats

Examples

Straw for animals
A woven basket made of straw sat on the kitchen table.
After the harvest, fields are often left with scattered straw on the ground.
The farmer piled the straw in the barn to use as bedding for the animals.
She wore a handmade straw hat to protect herself from the sun.
The farmer used straw to make a comfortable bed for the animals.
They filled the basket with fresh fruit and lined it with straw for extra cushioning.
