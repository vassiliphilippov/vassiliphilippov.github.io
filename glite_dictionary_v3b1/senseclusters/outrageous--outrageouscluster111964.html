Synonyms

    excessive means more than needed or reasonable in amount, while outrageous adds a sense of shock or strong social disapproval.
  • immoderate
    8.0 0.2/m
    immoderate means extreme and going beyond limits, while outrageous sounds more emotional and publicly shocking.
  • obscene
    6.1 1/m
    obscene describes something shockingly extreme or offensive, while outrageous is broad shock at what is unacceptable or excessive.
  • over-the-top
    5.2 3/m
    over-the-top is informal and wildly exaggerated, while outrageous feels stronger and often implies real moral or social shock.
  • extravagant
    6.0 2/m
    extravagant means lavish or unrestrained in spending or expression, while outrageous stresses being shockingly beyond what people accept.
  • unconscionable
    8.9 2/m
    unconscionable is morally wrong and unfair to most people, while outrageous focuses on shock and being beyond what is reasonable.
  • exorbitant
    7.9 2/m
    exorbitant describes prices or demands much higher than expected, while outrageous covers many shocking excesses beyond reason.
  • extortionate
    7.9 0.1/m
    extortionate often means unfairly high, like a price forced on someone, while outrageous is broader shock at extreme excess.
  • ridiculous
    1.6 40/m
    ridiculous feels silly and laughable, while outrageous is serious and shocks people by going beyond reasonable or acceptable limits.
  • absurd
    4.4 16/m
