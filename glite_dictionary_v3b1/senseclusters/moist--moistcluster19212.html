moist

adjective
slightly wet
Pronunciation: / mɔɪst / (AmE), / mɔɪst / (BrE)
Slightly wet, especially in a way that is pleasant or suitable.

Examples

Slightly moist ground
After the rain, the ground was still moist under our feet.
She dabbed her moist eyes with a tissue during the movie.
The cake should be moist and soft in the middle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damp - slightly wet, often unpleasantly
  • antonym: dry - free from moisture or liquid; not wet
  • degree higher: wet - covered or saturated with water or another liquid
