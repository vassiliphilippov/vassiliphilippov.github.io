deracialize

verb
to remove racial aspects
Pronunciation: / diˈreɪʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈreɪʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to remove racial characteristics, associations, influences, or considerations from something or someone

Examples

To deracialize hiring practices
The new policy aims to deracialize hiring practices in the company.
Over time, the festival began to deracialize and attract a more diverse crowd.
Efforts to deracialize the curriculum have made the school more inclusive.
The new policy was designed to deracialize the admissions process at the university.
Efforts to deracialize the curriculum have led to more inclusive educational materials.
Activists called on the city to deracialize its approach to law enforcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desegregate - to end the separation of people by race
  • synonym: integrate - to bring people of different races together equally
  • antonym: racialize - to give a racial character to something
