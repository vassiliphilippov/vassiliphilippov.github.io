ecocentric

adjective
nature-focused
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkoʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːkəʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
giving equal or greater importance to the needs of nature and all living things, not just humans

Examples

An ecocentric view
The new policy takes an ecocentric approach, prioritizing the health of ecosystems over economic growth.
Her ecocentric values influence every decision, from what she eats to how she travels.
Many conservationists argue for a more ecocentric perspective in managing natural resources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anthropocentric - focused on human needs and interests above all else
  • synonym: environmental - concerned with the natural world and its protection
