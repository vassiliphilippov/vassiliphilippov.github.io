family Rosaceae

noun
plant family including roses and apples
Pronunciation: / ˈræzəˌsiːiː / (AmE), / ˌrɒzəˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants in the order Rosales, including roses, apples, cherries, and many others, typically having alternate leaves and five-petaled flowers with numerous stamens

Examples

family Rosaceae plants
Gardeners often choose ornamental plants from the family Rosaceae because of their attractive flowers.
Many popular fruits such as apples, cherries, and strawberries belong to the family Rosaceae.
The family Rosaceae is known for species that produce five-petaled blossoms.
