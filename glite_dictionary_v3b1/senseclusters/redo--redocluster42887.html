redo

verb
do again or make new
2.1 1.62/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀàdu / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàduÀê / (BrE)
to do something again or make it new, often in order to improve or correct it

Examples

To redo the work
I need to redo this assignment because I made too many mistakes the first time.
If you don't pass the test, you'll have to redo the entire course.
She decided to redo her kitchen to give it a more modern look.

Synonyms

  • remake
    3.5 3.38/m
    remake means making something again in a new form to improve it, while redo means doing it again to correct or improve.
  • rework
    4.0 2.65/m
    rework means changing or improving parts by working on them again, while redo means doing the whole thing again to fix it.
  • make over
    4.1 0.32/m
    make over means changing appearance or function, often in a noticeable way, while redo means doing something again to fix or improve.
  • refashion
    4.9 0.51/m
    refashion means remake something into a new style or shape, often clothing or design, while redo means doing it again to correct or improve.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remake - to make something again or in a different way
  • antonym: undo - to reverse the effect of something done
