botulinus toxin

noun
bacterial toxin causing botulism
Pronunciation: / ˌboʊ.tʃəˈlaɪ.nəs ˈtɑk.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌbɒ.tjʊˈlaɪ.nəs ˈtɒk.sɪn / (BrE)
a potent bacterial toxin produced by Clostridium botulinum that causes botulism; used medically in small doses and potentially as a bioweapon

Examples

Botulinus toxin poisoning
Botulinus toxin is considered one of the most poisonous biological substances known.
Doctors use purified botulinus toxin in small doses for certain medical and cosmetic procedures.
Exposure to botulinus toxin can cause paralysis and is potentially fatal without prompt treatment.
