assibilate

verb
change into a sibilant sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪbəˌleɪt / (AmE), / əˈsɪbɪleɪt / (BrE)
to change a sound (especially a consonant) into a sibilant sound, or to add a sibilant sound to another sound (in linguistics)

Linguists note that the /d/ can assibilate to /dz/ depending on the phonetic environment.

Linguists study how certain phonemes assibilate in different language families.
Some dialects assibilate the 't' sound at the end of words.
The process will assibilate the original consonant.
