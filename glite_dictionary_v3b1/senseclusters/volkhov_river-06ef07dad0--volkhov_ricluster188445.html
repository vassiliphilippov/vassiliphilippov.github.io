Volkhov River

proper noun
river in northwestern Russia
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊlk.hɔv ˈrɪv.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.kɒf ˈrɪv.ə / (BrE)
a river in northwestern Russia that flows north into Lake Ladoga

Examples

The Volkhov River floods
Every spring, the Volkhov River floods the nearby fields.
Many trade routes in medieval Russia passed along the Volkhov River.
The ancient city of Novgorod lies on the banks of the Volkhov River.
