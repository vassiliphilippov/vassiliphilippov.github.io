net profit

noun
final profit after all deductions
5.3 2.7
Pronunciation: /net ˈprɑː.fɪt/ /; / /net ˈprɑː.fət/ (AmE), /net ˈprɒ.fɪt/ /; / /net ˈprɒ.fət/ (BrE)
the profit that remains after all expenses, taxes, and deductions have been subtracted from total revenue

Examples

The net profit margin
After accounting for all expenses, the net profit was reported at $2 million.
Rising costs have caused their net profit margin to shrink this quarter.
The company's net profit increased significantly compared to last year.
After calculating all expenses and taxes, the company's net profit was significantly higher than last year.
Investors care most about net profit because it reflects a company's true financial performance.
She focused on reducing costs to maximize net profit for her small business.

Related Terms

  • antonym: net loss - when total expenses exceed total revenues over a period
