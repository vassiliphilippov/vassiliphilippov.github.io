sinker

noun
baseball pitch with sharp downward movement
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkər / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkə / (BrE)
A type of baseball pitch that drops sharply downward as it approaches home plate.

Examples

Throws a sinker
His sinker is known for its late, sharp drop near the plate.
She struck out the last batter with a perfect sinker.
The pitcher threw a sinker that fooled the batter completely.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: curveball - a pitch that curves while approaching the batter
  • synonym: fastball - a pitch thrown at high speed with little curve
