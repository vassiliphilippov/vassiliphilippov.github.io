azurite

noun
a blue mineral, copper carbonate
Pronunciation: / ˈæʒəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈazjʊˌrʌɪt / (BrE)
a deep blue mineral consisting of a basic copper carbonate, used as an ore of copper and as a pigment

Examples

Deep blue azurite
Artists once ground azurite to create rich blue pigments for historic paintings.
Copper is often extracted from minerals such as azurite and malachite.
The museum's collection includes a stunning specimen of azurite crystals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malachite - a green copper carbonate mineral, associated with azurite
