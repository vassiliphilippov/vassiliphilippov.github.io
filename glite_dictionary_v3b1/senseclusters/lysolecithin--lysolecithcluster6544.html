lysolecithin

noun
compound for demyelination
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪsoʊˈlɛsɪθɪn / (AmE), / ˌlaɪsəʊˈlɛsɪθɪn / (BrE)
a compound used in laboratory research to disrupt myelin and create localized demyelination in nerve tissue

Examples

Lysolecithin for nerve injury
Scientists use lysolecithin to induce demyelination in animal models of nerve injury.
The injection of lysolecithin allowed researchers to study the process of myelin repair.
By applying lysolecithin to nerve tissue, the team observed changes in cell behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lecithin - a fatty substance found in animal and plant tissues
