put on ice

verb
postpone or delay something
Pronunciation: / pʊt ɑn aɪs / (AmE), / pʊt ɒn aɪs / (BrE)
to postpone or temporarily stop something, especially a plan, project, or activity

Examples

To put on ice a project
After the budget cuts, management had no choice but to put on ice several ongoing projects.
Let's put on ice any further discussions until next week.
The company decided to put on ice the expansion plans due to economic uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: postpone - to delay or arrange for something to take place at a later time
  • synonym: shelve - to decide not to continue with a plan, idea, etc., for a period of time
