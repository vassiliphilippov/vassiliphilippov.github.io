rococo

adjective
elaborately ornate and asymmetrical style
Pronunciation: / roʊˈkoʊkoʊ / (AmE), / rəˈkəʊkəʊ / (BrE)
(of art, architecture, or decoration) characterized by elaborate, asymmetrical ornamentation, often with intricate detail and curves.

Examples

Rococo decoration details
She bought a rococo clock with intricate designs and delicate curves for her mantelpiece.
The ceiling of the palace was covered in rococo plasterwork and golden reliefs.
The museum features a room filled with rococo furniture and art from the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: baroque - an artistic style preceding rococo, known for grandeur and opulence
  • synonym: ornate - elaborately or highly decorated
  • antonym: simple - free from ornamentation or complexity
