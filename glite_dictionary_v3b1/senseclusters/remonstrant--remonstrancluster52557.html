remonstrant

noun
Dutch Protestant group member
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɑnstrənt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɒnstrənt / (BrE)
a member of the Remonstrant Brotherhood, a Dutch Protestant movement that began in the early 17th century

Examples

Remonstrant church member
The remonstrant defended his beliefs during the heated religious debates in 17th-century Holland.
Many remonstrants advocated for greater religious tolerance in the Dutch Republic.
The history class discussed how the remonstrant movement influenced Protestant theology in Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arminian - a follower of the theological ideas of Jacobus Arminius
  • antonym: Calvinist - a follower of the theological teachings of John Calvin
