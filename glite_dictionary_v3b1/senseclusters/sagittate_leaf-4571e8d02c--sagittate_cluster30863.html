sagittate leaf

noun
arrowhead-shaped leaf
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsædʒəˌteɪt liːf / (AmE), / ˈsædʒɪteɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf shaped like an arrowhead, with two basal lobes pointing downward.

Examples

A sagittate leaf shape
Some aquatic plants develop a distinct sagittate leaf as they mature.
The sagittate leaf of the arrowhead plant makes it easy to identify in the wild.
The botanist noted the sagittate leaf shape in her field observations.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cordate leaf - a heart-shaped leaf
  • degree higher: hastate leaf - a leaf shaped like a spearhead, but with the basal lobes spreading outward
