plunder

verb
steal by force, destroy and strip of possessions
5.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈplʌn.dər/ (AmE), /ˈplʌn.də/ (BrE)
to steal goods, especially by force and during war, disaster, or disorder

Examples

To plunder a city
After the invasion, the soldiers began to plunder the city, taking everything of value.
The pirates plundered the merchant ship before disappearing into the fog.
Vikings would often plunder coastal villages, leaving destruction behind them.
During the chaos, looters began to plunder abandoned shops.
Invaders would often plunder conquered cities, taking everything of value.
The army proceeded to plunder the town after breaking through the gates.
Archaeologists discovered that the ancient tomb had been plundered centuries ago.
Opportunistic thieves attempted to plunder abandoned shops after the hurricane struck.
The invaders began to plunder villages as they advanced through the region.

  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
