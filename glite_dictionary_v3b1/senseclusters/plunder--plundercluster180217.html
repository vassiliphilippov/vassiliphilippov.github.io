Synonyms

  • loot
    4.2 2.32/m

  • pillage
    8.1 1.33/m
    pillage means using violence to seize treasures in war, while plunder is a general word for stealing by force.
  • despoil
    9.3 0.44/m
    despoil means violently taking and damaging valuable things, while plunder focuses on stealing goods in war or disorder.
  • spoil
    7.6 0.16/m
    spoil describes soldiers stripping and destroying a place to take goods, while plunder stresses stealing by force.
  • sack
    8.6 0.81/m
    sack means attacking and destroying a town after capture to take goods, while plunder is stealing goods by force in disorder.
  • ransack
    6.3 1.51/m
    ransack means searching a place roughly and making a mess, while plunder emphasizes stealing goods by force during disorder.
