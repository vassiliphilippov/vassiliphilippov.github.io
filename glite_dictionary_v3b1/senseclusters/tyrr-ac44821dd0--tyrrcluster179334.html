Tyrr

proper noun
Norse god of war and strife
Pronunciation: / tɪr / (AmE), / tɪə / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the god of war and strife, son of Odin, and identified with the Anglo-Saxon Tiu.

Examples

Tyrr god of war
According to legend, Tyrr sacrificed his hand to bind the wolf Fenrir.
Many ancient runestones bear inscriptions dedicated to Tyrr.
The day Tuesday is named after Tyrr, the Norse god of war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Mars - Roman god of war (mythological counterpart)
  • synonym: Tyr - Alternative spelling; Norse god of war and sky
  • antonym: peace - Opposite state to war and strife
