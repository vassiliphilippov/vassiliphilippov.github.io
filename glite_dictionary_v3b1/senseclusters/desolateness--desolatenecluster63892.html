desolateness

noun
deep loneliness
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛsələtnəs / (AmE), / ˈdɛsələtnəs / (BrE)
a state or quality of being empty, lonely, or sad, often because of loss or because a place is deserted

Examples

The desolateness of place
He stared out the window, overwhelmed by a sense of desolateness after his friends moved away.
The abandoned house reflected the desolateness she felt inside.
During the long winter, a feeling of desolateness settled over the small village.
The desolateness of the empty beach made her feel even more alone.
After the factory closed, the town's desolateness became impossible to ignore.
He was struck by the desolateness of the winter landscape stretching for miles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrenness - the state of being empty or unproductive
  • synonym: bleakness - the quality of being cold, empty, or without hope
  • synonym: desolation - a feeling of emptiness and sadness
  • antonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness
  • antonym: liveliness - the quality of being full of life and activity
  • synonym: loneliness - the state of being alone and unhappy about it
