Hadrosauridae

noun
family of duck-billed dinosaurs
Pronunciation: / hædrəˈsɔːrɪdiː /; / ˌhædrəˌsɔrɪˈdiː / (AmE), / hædrəˈsɔːrɪdiː /; / ˌhædrəˌsɔːrɪˈdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, herbivorous dinosaurs with duck-billed snouts that lived during the Upper Cretaceous period

Examples

Hadrosauridae family dinosaurs
Fossils of Hadrosauridae have been discovered on several continents, shedding light on their wide distribution.
Scientists believe that Hadrosauridae traveled in herds and were among the most common herbivores of their time.
Some members of Hadrosauridae had elaborate crests that may have been used for communication.
Many museums display reconstructed skeletons of Hadrosauridae species such as Parasaurolophus and Edmontosaurus.
Scientists believe that members of the Hadrosauridae family were highly adapted herbivores.
The discovery of Hadrosauridae fossils has greatly improved our understanding of dinosaur evolution.
