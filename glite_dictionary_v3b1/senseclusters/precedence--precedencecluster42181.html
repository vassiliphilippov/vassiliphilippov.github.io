precedence

noun
order of evaluation (computing)
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpre.sɪ.dəns/ (AmE), /ˈpre.sɪ.dəns/ (BrE)
(computing, mathematics) the rule or set of rules that determines the order in which operators in an expression are evaluated.

Examples

Operator precedence
The language defines operator precedence to determine the order of evaluation.
In the expression 3 + 4 * 5, multiplication takes precedence over addition by convention.
Students must learn operator precedence rules to avoid mistakes on programming and math tests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: order of operations - the conventional rules that determine the sequence in which arithmetic operations are performed
  • synonym: priority - the condition of being regarded or treated as more important or to come before others in order
