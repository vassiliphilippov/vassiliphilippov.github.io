casuistically

adverb
with deceptive reasoning
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæʒuˈɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌkæzjuˈɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that uses overly subtle, deceptive, or specious reasoning to justify actions or avoid clear judgment

Examples

Casuistically justify actions
He argued casuistically to justify his questionable business decisions.
The politician responded casuistically, avoiding a direct answer to the reporter's question.
Some people defend their actions casuistically rather than admitting their mistakes.
