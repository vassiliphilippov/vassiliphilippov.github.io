hippy

noun
person who rejects conventional social values
8.0 0.86/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉªpi / (AmE), / ËˆhÉªpi / (BrE)
A person, especially of the 1960s and 1970s, who rejects conventional social values and advocates a liberal, often nonconformist, approach to politics and lifestyle.

Examples

A hippy lifestyle
He dressed like a hippy at the music festival, with long hair and tie-dye shirts.
My parents used to be hippies who traveled the country in a van.
The hippy lifestyle promotes peace, love, and communal living.

Synonyms

  • hippie
    6.3 6.52/m
    hippie is the common spelling seen in books and media, while hippy is a less common variant spelling.
  • flower child
    6.8 0.22/m
    flower child is a 1960s young person focused on peace, love, and simple communal life, while hippy rejects mainstream social values more broadly.
