enterococcus

noun
intestinal bacteria
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛntəroʊˈkɑːkəs / (AmE), / ˌɛntərəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a type of bacteria commonly found in the intestines of humans and animals, which can sometimes cause infections or be used as an indicator of water contamination

Examples

Researchers are studying how enterococcus bacteria develop resistance to antibiotics.

Public health officials monitor enterococcus to ensure the safety of recreational waters.
Swimming was temporarily banned because enterococcus counts exceeded safe limits.
