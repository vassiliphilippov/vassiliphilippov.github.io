yielding

adjective
inclined to give in or surrender
Pronunciation: / ˈjiːldɪŋ /; / ˈjildɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
willing to give in, submit, or be easily influenced; not resistant

Examples

Yielding personality
Although generally yielding, he finally stood his ground on the important issue.
His yielding nature made him easy to persuade in group decisions.
She was so yielding that she seldom voiced her own opinions at meetings.
Ella was known to be a yielding colleague who rarely argued with team decisions.
His yielding nature made him popular, but sometimes others took advantage of him.
Though gentle and yielding at first, he eventually stood up for himself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compliant - willing to agree or obey
  • antonym: resistant - unwilling to yield or submit to influence
  • antonym: uncompromising - not willing to change or give way
