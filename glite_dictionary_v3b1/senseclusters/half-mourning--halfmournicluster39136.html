half-mourning

noun
a transitional state or attire during mourning
2.2 8.5
Pronunciation: /hæf.ˈmɔːr.nɪŋ/ /; / /hæf.ˈmʊr.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /hɑːf.ˈmɔːn.ɪŋ/ /; / /hɑːf.ˈmʊə.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
a period of mourning, usually following full mourning, during which the bereaved wear less somber clothing and observe less strict customs

Examples

In half-mourning clothes
At the tea party, several widows were dressed in half-mourning attire.
During the Victorian era, half-mourning was marked by wearing gray or lavender instead of black.
She entered a period of half-mourning a few months after her husband's funeral.
