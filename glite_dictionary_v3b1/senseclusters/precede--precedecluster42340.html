precede

verb
come before in time or order
7.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈsiːd/ /; / /prə.ˈsiːd/ /; / /priː.ˈsiːd/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈsiːd/ /; / /prə.ˈsiːd/ /; / /priː.ˈsiːd/ (BrE)
to come before something or someone in time, order, or position

Examples

To precede an event
A sudden silence preceded the announcement from the president.
Stone age tools precede bronze tools in human history.
Thunder usually precedes the arrival of heavy rain in summer storms.
A brief introduction precedes each chapter in the book.
A formal dinner preceded the main event.
Lightning usually precedes thunder during a storm.
A sudden silence preceded the announcement of the winner.
In the company, Sarah preceded Tom as the department manager.
The invention of the telephone preceded the development of the radio.
