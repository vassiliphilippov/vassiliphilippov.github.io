overoptimism

noun
unrealistic positivity
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈɑptɪˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərˈɒptɪmɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to expect only positive outcomes, often ignoring possible risks or negative results

Examples

Her overoptimism about success
Her overoptimism made her believe the project would succeed without any problems.
Investors should be careful not to let overoptimism cloud their judgment about potential risks.
The team's overoptimism led them to ignore important warning signs during the process.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: optimism - the belief that good things will happen
  • antonym: pessimism - the tendency to expect bad outcomes
