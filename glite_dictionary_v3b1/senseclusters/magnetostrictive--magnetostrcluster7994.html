magnetostrictive

adjective
changes shape with magnetism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmæɡnɪtoʊˈstrɪktɪv / (AmE), / ˌmæɡnɪtəʊˈstrɪktɪv / (BrE)
relating to the change in shape or size of a material when exposed to a magnetic field

Examples

Magnetostrictive material change
The magnetostrictive properties of the alloy make it useful in precision sensors.
A magnetostrictive actuator can convert magnetic energy into mechanical movement.
Researchers studied the magnetostrictive response of the material under different magnetic fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: piezoelectric - relating to materials that generate electricity when pressure is applied
