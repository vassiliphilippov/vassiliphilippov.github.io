fault

noun
equipment or circuit defect causing failure
Pronunciation: / fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
a defect, imperfection, or malfunction in a machine, object, system, or material that causes it not to work properly

Examples

A fault in the system
A sudden fault caused the computer to shut down unexpectedly.
Engineers tested the system to detect any possible fault before launch.
The technician quickly isolated the fault in the circuit board.
If your washing machine develops a fault, you should contact customer service for repairs.
The engineer found a fault in the wiring that caused the device to stop working.
There was a small fault in the ceramic vase that made it less valuable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defect - a fault or imperfection in something
  • synonym: flaw - a mark, fault, or other imperfection that mars a substance or object
  • synonym: malfunction - failure of a machine or part to function properly
