overexplain

verb
to explain too much
6.0
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪk.ˈspleɪn/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪk.ˈspleɪn/ (BrE)
to give more explanation than is necessary or helpful, often making things confusing or tedious

Examples

To overexplain a topic
Sometimes teachers overexplain simple topics, which can make students lose interest.
If you overexplain your point, people might think you do not trust their understanding.
She tends to overexplain her decisions, even when a brief answer would be enough.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elaborate - to explain or describe something in more detail
  • antonym: underexplain - to provide too little explanation
