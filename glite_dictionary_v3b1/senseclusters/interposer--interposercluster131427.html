interposer

noun
connecting component
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌpoʊzər / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌpəʊzə / (BrE)
a device or component placed between two parts to connect, separate, or enable communication between them

Examples

A circuit interposer
The technician inserted an interposer to connect the two circuit boards.
An interposer can help improve signal quality between electronic components.
Modern computer chips often use an interposer to manage complex connections.
The engineer installed an interposer to connect the processor to the memory module.
A special interposer was used to ensure compatibility between the two electronic components.
Without an interposer, the chips could not communicate directly with the main board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapter - a device used to connect different parts or systems
  • synonym: separator - a device that keeps two parts apart
