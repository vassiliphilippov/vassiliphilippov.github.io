still

adjective, adverb
not moving; motionless
1.0 83/m
Pronunciation: / stÉªl / (AmE), / stÉªl / (BrE)
not moving or making a sound; calm, motionless, or silent (used to describe people, things, water, air, or moments)

Examples

Sit still
He sat perfectly still as the artist painted his portrait.
Keep still while I take your picture.
The forest was eerily still after the storm passed through.
Remain still during the test so we get an accurate result.
She waited still in the dark, hoping not to be noticed.
The children stood still while the photographer took their picture.
A still atmosphere made the forest seem quiet and peaceful.
Early in the morning, the river was completely still before the wind picked up.
The still surface of the lake perfectly reflected the mountains.
