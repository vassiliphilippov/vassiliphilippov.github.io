raised hand

noun
gesture to signal participation or response
3.8 2.1
Pronunciation: /reɪzd hænd/ (AmE), /reɪzd hænd/ (BrE)
an act of lifting one's hand to signal a desire to speak, respond, or indicate agreement or disagreement

Examples

A raised hand signal
During the vote, several students showed their support with a raised hand.
If you have a question, please signal with a raised hand rather than speaking out.
The teacher looked around and called on the student with the raised hand.
