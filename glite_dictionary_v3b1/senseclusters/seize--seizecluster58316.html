seize

verb
capture someone’s attention or imagination
5.4
Pronunciation: / siz / (AmE), / siːz / (BrE)
to capture and hold someone's attention, interest, or imagination strongly and suddenly

Examples

Seize the imagination
Her performance instantly seized the imagination of both critics and audiences.
The dramatic news seized the public’s interest for weeks.
The gripping novel seized my attention from the very first page.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engage - to occupy or attract the attention or interest of someone
  • synonym: grab - to capture and hold attention quickly; to take hold of something suddenly
  • antonym: lose - to fail to keep someone's attention or interest
