hypothecation

noun
pledging property as security
9.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.ˈθek.eɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈθek.eɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a legal arrangement in which property or assets are pledged as security for a loan, but the owner keeps possession and ownership

Examples

Hypothecation of property
The bank required hypothecation of the company's inventory to secure the business loan.
Through hypothecation, the borrower kept possession of the car while using it as collateral.
Legal documents outlining hypothecation must clearly state the rights of both lender and borrower.
The bank required hypothecation of the car as collateral for the loan.
Through hypothecation, she was able to secure a business loan without selling her equipment.
In some countries, hypothecation is a common practice for obtaining short-term credit.
The bank required hypothecation of the company's inventory before approving the loan.
Through hypothecation, the borrower kept possession of the car while using it as loan security.
The contract specified the hypothecation of certain assets to protect the lender's interests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collateral - property or assets pledged as security for a loan
  • antonym: foreclosure - the process of taking possession of a mortgaged property when the borrower fails to keep up payments
  • synonym: mortgage - a legal agreement by which a bank lends money in exchange for taking title of the debtor's property
  • synonym: pledge - something given as security for the fulfillment of a contract
