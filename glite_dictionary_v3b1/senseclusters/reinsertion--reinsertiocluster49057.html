reinsertion

noun
surgical reattachment
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈsɜːrʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈsɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of putting something back into its original place or position

Examples

Reinsertion of a joint
After the accident, the patient required reinsertion of the dislocated joint.
The surgeon performed a reinsertion of the dental implant to improve stability.
Successful reinsertion of the tendon allowed the athlete to return to training.
The reinsertion of the battery fixed the device and made it work again.
After editing, the reinsertion of the deleted paragraph improved the clarity of the report.
Successful reinsertion of the bone was confirmed by the surgeon after the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reattachment - the act of attaching something again
  • antonym: removal - the act of taking something away
  • synonym: replacement - the act of putting something new in the place of something else
