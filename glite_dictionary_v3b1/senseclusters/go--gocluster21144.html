go

verb
to be sounded or worded (of a song, poem, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ / (BrE)
to make or imitate a particular sound; to have a particular wording or phrasing (songs, sayings, noises)

Examples

A song goes
Do you remember how the national anthem goes?
How does this song go again?
The poem goes like this: 'Roses are red, violets are blue.'
Suddenly, the dog went 'woof' and chased the ball.
The bell goes ding-dong every hour.
The children went 'shhh' to quiet their friend during the movie.
As the proverb goes, 'Actions speak louder than words.'
The rumor went that he was moving abroad.
The story goes that the castle is haunted by a ghost.
