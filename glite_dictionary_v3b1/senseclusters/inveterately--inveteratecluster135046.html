inveterately

adverb
habitually, firmly
8.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈve.tər.ət.liː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈve.tə.rə.t.liː/ (BrE)
in a manner that is firmly established and unlikely to change, often over a long period

Examples

To inveterately arrive late
She inveterately arrives late to every meeting, no matter how important it is.
He inveterately avoids discussing his personal life with colleagues.
The town's residents inveterately support the local football team year after year.
