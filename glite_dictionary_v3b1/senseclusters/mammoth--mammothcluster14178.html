mammoth

noun
extinct large, hairy elephant
Pronunciation: / ˈmæməθ / (AmE), / ˈmaməθ / (BrE)
an extinct type of large, hairy elephant with long, curved tusks, widespread during the Pleistocene epoch

Examples

Mammoth skeleton
Museums often display mammoth skeletons to illustrate prehistoric life.
Scientists recently discovered a nearly intact mammoth frozen in Siberian permafrost.
The mammoth had long, curved tusks that could reach over four meters in length.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elephant - a large, living, thick-skinned mammal with a trunk and tusks
