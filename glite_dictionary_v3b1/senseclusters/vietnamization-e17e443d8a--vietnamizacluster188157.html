Vietnamization

noun
making more Vietnamese
8.4
Pronunciation: /viː.et.nə.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /vɪ.et.nə.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /vɪ.et.nə.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /viː.et.nə.maɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making something more Vietnamese in character, especially by introducing Vietnamese people, culture, language, or institutions

Examples

Vietnamization of culture
The Vietnamization of the neighborhood was evident in the new shops and street signs.
Cultural festivals played a key role in the Vietnamization of the local community.
Some historians study the Vietnamization of cuisine in cities with large Vietnamese populations.
