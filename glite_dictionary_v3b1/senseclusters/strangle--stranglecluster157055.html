strangle

verb
prevent progress or free movement
5.2 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈstræŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈstræŋɡl /; / ˈstraŋɡl / (BrE)
to severely restrict, suppress, or prevent the progress, growth, or expression of something (such as a feeling or idea)

Examples

To strangle progress
Bureaucracy often strangles creativity and innovation in large organizations.
Strict regulations can strangle economic growth in developing countries.
The new policy threatens to strangle independent media outlets.
He managed to strangle his anger before responding to the criticism.
Julia had to strangle a yawn as the lecture dragged on.
She tried to strangle a laugh during the serious meeting.

  • synonym: repress - to hold back or prevent the expression of a feeling or idea
