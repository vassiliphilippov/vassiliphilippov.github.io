supercell

noun
enlarged unit cell
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəsɛl / (BrE)
an enlarged periodic cell composed of several unit cells, used in solid-state physics and materials modeling

Examples

Crystal supercell
Researchers built a supercell to simulate the effects of atomic defects in the crystal structure.
The properties of the material were analyzed using a computational model based on a supercell approach.
By expanding the lattice into a supercell, scientists could observe interactions between multiple impurities.
