language requirement

noun
rule requiring knowledge of certain languages
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ rɪ.ˈkwaɪr.mənt/ /; / /ˈlæŋ.ɡwədʒ rɪ.ˈkwaɪr.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ rɪ.ˈkwaɪ.ə.mənt/ /; / /ˈlæŋ.ɡwədʒ rɪ.ˈkwaɪ.ə.mənt/ (BrE)
an official rule or condition in an educational or professional context that requires proficiency in one or more languages, often as part of degree or job requirements.

Examples

A language requirement for graduation
Many universities have a language requirement for graduation to ensure students have foreign language skills.
She was able to waive the language requirement by passing an advanced Spanish exam.
The college's language requirement can be met by studying French, German, or Latin.
