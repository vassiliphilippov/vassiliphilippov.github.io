minister

noun
head of a government department
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnɪstɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstə / (BrE)
a senior government official in charge of a department or area of policy

Examples

Prime minister
Each minister is responsible for developing policies within their department.
She was appointed as the minister of education last year.
The Prime Minister announced new reforms during the press conference.
After the election, he was appointed as minister of foreign affairs.
Each minister is responsible for overseeing their department's budget and policies.
The new minister for health outlined her plans in parliament today.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: deputy - person acting as a second in command or substitute
  • degree higher: prime minister - the head of government in parliamentary systems
  • synonym: secretary - head of a government department (especially in the US)
