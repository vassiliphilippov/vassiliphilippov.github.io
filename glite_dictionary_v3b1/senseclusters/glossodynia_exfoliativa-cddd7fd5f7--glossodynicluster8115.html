glossodynia exfoliativa

noun
glossitis type with tongue patches
9.9
Pronunciation: /glɑːs.ə.ˈdaɪ.niː.ə eks.fə.lɪ.ə.ˈteɪ.və/ (AmE), /glɒs.ə.ˈdaɪ.niː.ə eks.fə.lɪ.ə.ˈteɪ.və/ (BrE)
A superficial form of glossitis characterized by irregular red patches on the tongue and increased sensitivity to hot or spicy foods.

Examples

Red patches on glossodynia exfoliativa
A dental examination revealed that her tongue irregularities were caused by glossodynia exfoliativa.
Doctors often advise people with glossodynia exfoliativa to avoid spicy foods that can aggravate the condition.
The patient was diagnosed with glossodynia exfoliativa after complaining of burning sensations and red patches on their tongue.
