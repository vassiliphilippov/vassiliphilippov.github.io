Barabbas

proper noun
biblical figure
Pronunciation: / bəˈræbəs / (AmE), / bəˈræbəs / (BrE)
the man in the New Testament who was released by Pontius Pilate instead of Jesus before the crucifixion

Examples

Barabbas freed by Pilate
According to the Gospels, the crowd demanded that Barabbas be released instead of Jesus.
Many artists have depicted the moment when Barabbas was set free by Pilate.
The story of Barabbas raises questions about justice and mercy in biblical history.
