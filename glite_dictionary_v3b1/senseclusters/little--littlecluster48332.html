little

adjective
young or immature (children, animals)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.əl / (BrE)
(of children or animals) young or not fully grown; immature.

Examples

A little puppy
He was a little boy with an infectious smile and endless energy.
Our cat was just a little kitten when we adopted her.
The little girl laughed as she played with her stuffed animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: big - of a size that is larger than normal or usual
  • synonym: small - of a size that is less than normal or usual
