unwholesome

adjective
harmful to health or morals
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhoʊlsəm / (AmE), / ʌnˈhəʊlsəm / (BrE)
harmful to health or morals; likely to cause physical or moral harm

Examples

An unwholesome effect
She warned the students about the unwholesome effects of eating too much junk food.
Some television programs are criticized for promoting unwholesome values among children.
The air in the old factory became unwholesome after years of neglect and pollution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: wholesome - conducive to health or moral well-being
