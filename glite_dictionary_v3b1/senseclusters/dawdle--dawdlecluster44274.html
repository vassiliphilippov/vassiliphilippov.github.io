dawdle

verb
to waste time or move slowly
8.3 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdɔː.dəl/ /; / /ˈdɑː.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈdɔː.dəl/ /; / /ˈdɒ.dəl/ (BrE)
to move or act slowly and waste time, often causing delay

Examples

To dawdle in the morning
If you dawdle in the morning, you might miss your bus.
Please don't dawdle; we need to leave soon.

He tends to dawdle over breakfast, enjoying his coffee and the morning news.
