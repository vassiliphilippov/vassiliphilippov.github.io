drum up

verb
to try to generate or gather support or interest
4.1 1.25/m
Pronunciation: / drÊŒm ÊŒp / (AmE), / drÊŒm ÊŒp / (BrE)
to try to increase or generate interest, support, or business by making an effort to attract attention or persuade people

Examples

To drum up support
She handed out flyers downtown to drum up interest in the upcoming concert.
The company is looking for ways to drum up more business during the slow season.
The team worked hard to drum up support for their new project.
She traveled across the country to drum up support for her project.
Social media can be a useful way to drum up excitement about an upcoming event.
The marketing team worked hard to drum up interest in the new campaign.

Synonyms

  • beat the drum for
    6.3 0.05/m
    beat the drum for means speaking loudly and often in favor of someone, while drum up means actively trying to gather interest or support.
  • talk up
    4.1 0.79/m
    talk up means speaking positively to make something seem better, while drum up means making an effort to attract people or business.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dampen - to make something less lively or intense; to reduce (opposite of generating excitement)
  • synonym: generate - to cause something to exist; to create
  • synonym: rally - to bring people together in order to support a cause
  • synonym: solicit - to ask for or try to obtain something from someone
  • synonym: spark - to cause the start of something, especially excitement or interest
