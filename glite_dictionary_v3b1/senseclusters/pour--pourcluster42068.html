pour

verb
cause liquid to flow in a stream
Pronunciation: / pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
to make a liquid or rain flow in a steady stream, or to flow in this way, especially in large amounts

Examples

To pour water
It's easy to pour too much detergent if you're not careful.
She poured the coffee into two mugs and handed one to her guest.
Water poured over the edge of the sink onto the floor.
As we drove through the countryside, it began to pour with rain.
It's supposed to pour all day tomorrow, so bring an umbrella.
We got soaked because it started to pour just as we left the building.
Rain began to pour down on the city, drenching everyone in minutes.
She accidentally tilted the jug and juice poured onto the table.
Tears poured from his eyes as he listened to the sad news.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: drizzle - to rain lightly in very small drops
  • antonym: spill - cause or allow liquid to flow over the edge and be lost
  • degree lower: trickle - (of a liquid) to flow slowly in a thin stream
