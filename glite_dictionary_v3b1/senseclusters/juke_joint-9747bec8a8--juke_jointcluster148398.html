juke joint

noun
southern US bar with food, drink, and dance
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːk ˌdʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːk ˌdʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
A small, informal establishment in the southeastern United States, typically beside a road, where people eat, drink, and dance to music played on a jukebox.

Examples

A juke joint dance
Many famous musicians got their start performing in humble juke joints across the Mississippi Delta.
On Friday nights, the old juke joint at the end of town is crowded with locals dancing to blues music.
The movie captures the lively atmosphere and cultural history of the southern juke joint scene.
