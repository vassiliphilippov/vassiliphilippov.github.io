reconditioning

noun
behavioral retraining
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of changing or relearning conditioned responses or behaviors through training or therapy

Examples

Behavior reconditioning
The psychologist recommended reconditioning to help the patient overcome their fear of dogs.
Through systematic reconditioning, the child learned to respond calmly to loud noises.
Modern therapy often uses reconditioning techniques to address phobias and anxiety disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conditioning - the process of learning or training a response to a stimulus
  • synonym: rehabilitation - the process of restoring skills or abilities through training or therapy
