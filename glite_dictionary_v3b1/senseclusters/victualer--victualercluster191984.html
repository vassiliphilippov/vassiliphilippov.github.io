victualer

noun
supplier of food and provisions
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪtələr / (AmE), / ˈvɪtələ / (BrE)
a person or company that supplies food and provisions, especially to an army or ship.

Examples

Army victualer supplies
During the campaign, the victualer ensured a steady supply of food for the soldiers.
In old naval records, a victualer is often listed alongside ship officers and crew.
The victualer arrived with fresh bread and salted meat for the encampment.
Centuries ago, being a victualler was a vital trade in port towns.
During the campaign, the army depended on a victualler for fresh bread and meat.
The victualler delivered barrels of salted pork to the navy ship every week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: provisioner - a person who supplies provisions, especially food
  • synonym: purveyor - a person or company that supplies goods, especially food
