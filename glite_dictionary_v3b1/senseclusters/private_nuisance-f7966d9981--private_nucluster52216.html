private nuisance

noun
legal interference with private land use
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvət ˈnuːsəns / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət ˈnjuːsəns / (BrE)
A legal term for an unlawful interference with a person's use or enjoyment of their land.

Examples

A private nuisance claim
Building a high fence that blocks your neighbor's sunlight could be considered a private nuisance.
The court found that the loud parties constituted a private nuisance to the property owner next door.
The factory was sued for creating a private nuisance by polluting the local residents' wells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: public nuisance - an act affecting the community or public at large
