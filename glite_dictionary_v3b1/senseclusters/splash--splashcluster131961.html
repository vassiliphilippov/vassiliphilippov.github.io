splash

verb
to soil or stain with a splashed liquid
Pronunciation: / splæʃ / (AmE), / splæʃ / (BrE)
to cause liquid to move, scatter, or make noise by hitting, throwing, or moving through it

Examples

To splash water
A passing car splashed muddy water onto my shoes.
She accidentally splashed her dress with coffee as she ran to the meeting.
The children splashed the kitchen floor with water during their water fight.
She splashed through the flooded field, her boots quickly covered in mud.
The children loved to splash through the puddles after the rain.
We had to splash across the muddy path to get home.
She loves to splash through puddles after it rains.
The children laughed as they splash in the shallow part of the river.
The dog began to splash around excitedly when it saw the water.
