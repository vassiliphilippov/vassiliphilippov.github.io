ablative

adjective
designed to be removed at high temperatures
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæbləˌtɪv / (AmE), / ˈæblətɪv / (BrE)
(of a material) designed to be removed or vaporized at very high temperatures, especially to protect something from heat.

Examples

Ablative heat shield
An ablative coating burns away gradually, carrying heat away from the underlying surface.
Engineers selected an ablative shield for the spacecraft to ensure safe passage through the atmosphere.
The rocket's nose cone was coated with an ablative material to withstand reentry heat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-ablative - not designed to be removed or vaporized at high temperatures
