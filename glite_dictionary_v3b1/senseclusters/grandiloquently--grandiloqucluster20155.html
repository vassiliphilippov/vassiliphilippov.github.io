grandiloquently

adverb
in a pompous or lofty manner
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɡrænˈdɪl.ə.kwənt.li / (AmE), / ɡrænˈdɪl.ə.kwənt.li / (BrE)
In a grandiloquent manner; using lofty, pompous, or elaborate language.

Examples

To grandiloquently speak
During the ceremony, he grandiloquently praised his team for their hard work and innovation.
She wrote grandiloquently in her essay, though some readers found it overly ornate.
The politician addressed the crowd grandiloquently, using elaborate metaphors to inspire his supporters.
