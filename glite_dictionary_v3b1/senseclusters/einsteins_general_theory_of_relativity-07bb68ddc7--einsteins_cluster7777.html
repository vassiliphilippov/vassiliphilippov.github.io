Einstein's general theory of relativity

noun
theory of gravity and spacetime by Einstein
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪnstaɪnz ˈdʒɛnɚəl ˈθɪəri əv rɪˌlætɪˈvɪti / (AmE), / ˈaɪnstaɪnz ˈdʒɛnərəl ˈθɪəri ɒv rɪˌlætɪˈvɪti / (BrE)
the scientific theory developed by Albert Einstein in 1915, extending special relativity to include gravity and describing the gravitational force as a curvature of spacetime

Examples

Einstein's general theory of relativity explains gravity
Many predictions of Einstein's general theory of relativity have been confirmed by experimental evidence.
The bending of light by gravity was one of the first tests of Einstein's general theory of relativity.
Understanding black holes requires knowledge of Einstein's general theory of relativity.
