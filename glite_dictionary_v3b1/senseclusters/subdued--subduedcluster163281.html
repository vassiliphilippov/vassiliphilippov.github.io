subdued

adjective
quiet and under control; restrained
5.5 1.12/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôbÀàduÀêd / (AmE), / s…ôbÀàdjuÀêd / (BrE)
(of a person or their behavior) quiet and controlled, not noisy, excited, or forceful; restrained or softened in manner or tone.

Examples

Subdued voice
After the argument, the children were suddenly subdued and unusually quiet.
Her subdued voice hinted that something was wrong.
The once lively celebration turned subdued after the disappointing news.

Synonyms

    3.7 0.00/m

  • low-keyed

  • composed

  • mild
