all together

adverb
at the same time; collectively
1.4
Pronunciation: / ɔl təˈɡɛðər /; / ɔl təˈɡɛðɚ / (AmE), / ɔːl təˈɡɛðə / (BrE)
with everyone or everything in a group, at the same time or in the same place

Examples

All together now
Please speak all together so I can hear everyone's answer at once.
The children shouted 'Surprise!' all together when the guest entered the room.
They stood all together and applauded as the winner was announced.
After the meeting, we gathered all together to discuss our next steps.
During the power outage, the family sat all together in the living room.
The children were all together in the garden, laughing and playing.
