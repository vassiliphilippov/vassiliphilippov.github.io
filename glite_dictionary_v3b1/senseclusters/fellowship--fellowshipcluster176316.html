fellowship

verb
to associate socially
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛloʊˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊˌʃɪp / (BrE)
to spend time together in friendly association, especially for religious or social purposes.

Examples

To fellowship together
After the ceremony, the members fellowshipped in the hall, enjoying food and conversation.
Every Sunday, the group fellowships together to strengthen their community bonds.
They fellowshipped late into the evening, sharing stories and laughter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isolate - to separate oneself from others
  • synonym: socialize - to spend time with others in a friendly way
