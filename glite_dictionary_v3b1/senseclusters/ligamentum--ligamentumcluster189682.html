ligamentum

noun
archaic binding strip
Pronunciation: / lɪˈɡæməntəm / (AmE), / lɪˈɡamɛntəm / (BrE)
an archaic or historical term for a strip or band of material used for tying or binding things together

Examples

Leather ligamentum
The old manuscript was held together by a leather ligamentum wrapped around its spine.
In medieval times, a ligamentum was often used to secure scrolls and documents.
She replaced the worn ligamentum on the book with a new strip of cloth.

  • synonym: cord - a long, thin, flexible string or rope
