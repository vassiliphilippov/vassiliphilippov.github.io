sheet-fed

adjective
using single sheets
Pronunciation: / ˈʃiːt fɛd /; / ˈʃiːtˌfɛd / (AmE), / ˈʃiːt fɛd /; / ˈʃiːtˌfɛd / (BrE)
describing a machine or process (especially in printing) that uses individual sheets of paper instead of a continuous roll

Examples

Sheet-fed printer
The company invested in a sheet-fed printer to handle specialty paper sizes.
A sheet-fed press is ideal for printing small batches of high-quality brochures.
Unlike roll-fed machines, sheet-fed devices can process different types of paper more easily.
The new sheet-fed printer produces sharper images than the older model.
Many commercial printing companies use sheet-fed presses for high-quality brochures.
A sheet-fed machine is ideal for short print runs and specialty papers.
