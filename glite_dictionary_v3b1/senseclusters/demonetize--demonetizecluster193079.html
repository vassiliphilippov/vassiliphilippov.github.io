demonetize

verb
remove online monetization
7.6
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈmɑː.nə.taɪz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈmɑː.nə.taɪz/ (AmE), /diː.ˈmɒn.ə.taɪz/ /; / /diː.ˈmʌn.ə.taɪz/ (BrE)
to remove or disable the ability of online content, a channel, or a creator to earn money from advertising or other monetization features

Examples

To demonetize a video
After the controversy, the streaming service demonetized the creator's channel until the investigation was complete.
Small channels risk losing income when platforms demonetize videos for vague or broad guideline violations.
They warned that repeated copyright strikes could lead to demonetizing and removal of monetization features.
The government decided to demonetize the old currency notes to fight corruption.
Many video creators were upset when their channels were suddenly demonetized by the platform.
After the announcement to demonetize certain coins, people rushed to exchange them at banks.
After the policy change, the site demonetised several videos for violating the advertising rules.
Many creators fear the platform will demonetise their channels without giving a clear explanation.
When advertisers pulled out, the streamer was quickly demonetised and lost most of their income.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deplatform - to remove a person or their content from a website or online service, often restricting access or visibility
  • synonym: invalidate - to make something no longer valid or acceptable
  • antonym: legalize - to make something legal or officially allowed
  • antonym: monetise - to enable something (for example content or a channel) to earn money
  • antonym: monetize - to earn money from content, services, or products; opposite of removing monetization
