accountability

noun
responsibility to justify actions
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /ə.k.ˈaʊn.tə.bɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.kə.ˈaʊn.tə.bɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ə.k.ˈaʊn.tə.bɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.kə.ˈaʊn.tə.bɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the state of being responsible for your actions and having to explain or justify them to others

Examples

Effective accountability
Effective accountability helps prevent corruption in government institutions.
Public officials must demonstrate accountability to the people they serve.
The new policy increases accountability for managers in the organization.
Regulators increasingly demand accountability for automated hiring systems that screen job applicants.
The company introduced new logs and reports to improve accountability after the biased algorithm scandal.
Civil rights groups argue that algorithmic accountability requires clear explanations and easy access to remedies.
