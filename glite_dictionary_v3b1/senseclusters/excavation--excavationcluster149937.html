excavation

noun
hole or cavity made by digging
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən /; / ˌɛk.səˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskəˈveɪʃən /; / ˌɛk.səˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a hole, cavity, or site in the ground created by digging, especially for archaeology or construction

Examples

A deep excavation
After the excavation was completed, they reinforced the sides to prevent collapse.
Archaeologists discovered ancient artifacts in the excavation site.
The construction workers dug a deep excavation for the building's foundation.
Security measures were taken to protect the excavation from looters overnight.
The researchers spent weeks carefully sifting through the excavation for ancient pottery shards.
Tours of the excavation are available for visitors interested in seeing archeological work firsthand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dig - an informal term for an archaeological site where excavation occurs
  • antonym: mound - a raised area of earth, opposite of a hole
  • synonym: pit - a large hole in the ground, often for mining or construction
