flux

noun
continuous change; instability
Pronunciation: / flʌks / (AmE), / flʌks / (BrE)
a state of continuous change, movement, or instability

Examples

Life in flux
Her career was in flux as she considered different job opportunities.
Technology is always in a state of flux, making it hard to keep up.
The political situation in the country remained in flux for months after the election.
After the merger, the company was in a period of flux as new leadership was established.
Politics in the region remain in flux after the recent election results.
The whole industry is in flux due to rapid technological changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instability - the quality of being likely to change or not firmly established
  • antonym: stability - the state of being stable, not changing
