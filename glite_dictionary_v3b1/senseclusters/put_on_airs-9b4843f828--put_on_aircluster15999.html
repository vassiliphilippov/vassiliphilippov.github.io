put on airs

verb
act pretentiously; behave superiorly
8.3 6.7
Pronunciation: /pʊt ən eərz/ /; / /pʊt ɑːn eərz/ (AmE), /pʊt ən eəz/ /; / /pʊt ɒn eəz/ (BrE)
to behave in a way that shows you think you are better or more important than other people; to act pretentiously

Examples

To put on airs
After receiving the promotion, he started to put on airs around his colleagues.
Even though he came from a humble background, he was often accused of putting on airs at formal events.
She dislikes people who put on airs and pretend to be something they're not.
He never puts on airs despite his great success.
She started to put on airs after she was promoted at work.
There's no need to put on airs when we're all friends here.
