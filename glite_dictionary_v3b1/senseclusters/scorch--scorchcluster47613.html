scorch

verb
become superficially burned
Pronunciation: / skɔrtʃ / (AmE), / skɔːtʃ / (BrE)
to burn or singe the surface of something slightly, causing browning or discoloration but not destroying it

Examples

To scorch a shirt
If you leave the shirt too close to the iron, it might scorch and leave a brown mark.
The grass began to scorch under the intense midday sun.
When the bread stayed in the toaster too long, its edges started to scorch.
If you leave the pan on too long, the food will scorch.
The fabric will scorch if you iron it at too high a temperature.
The grass began to scorch after weeks without rain.
A wave of hot air scorched his face as he opened the oven.
She forgot the vegetables on the stove, and the heat scorched the bottom of the pan.
The relentless sun scorched the earth, leaving the fields dry and cracked.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: burn - to be damaged or destroyed by fire or heat
  • degree higher: char - to burn the surface of something so that it becomes black
  • degree higher: scorching - very hot or burning; causing to scorch
  • synonym: sear - burn or scorch the surface of with sudden, intense heat
  • synonym: singe - to burn something lightly so that only the surface is affected
  • degree lower: wilt - to become limp through heat, loss of water, or disease
