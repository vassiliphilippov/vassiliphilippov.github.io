overripen

verb
to become too ripe
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈraɪpən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈraɪpən / (BrE)
to become too ripe, often resulting in loss of freshness or quality

Examples

Fruit overripens quickly
If you leave the fruit in the sun too long, it will overripen and become mushy.
The cheese began to overripen, developing a strong smell and unusual texture.
Sometimes an idea can overripen and lose its original impact or freshness.
If you leave peaches in the sun, they will overripen and become mushy.
Grapes can overripen on the vine if not harvested at the right time.
The fruit began to overripen, attracting fruit flies to the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ripen - to become fully mature or ready to eat
  • degree higher: rot - to decay or decompose due to natural processes
  • synonym: spoil - to become unfit for use or consumption
