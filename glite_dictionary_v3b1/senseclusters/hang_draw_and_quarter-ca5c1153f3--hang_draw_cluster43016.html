hang, draw and quarter

verb
severely punish or criticize; historical execution method
Pronunciation: / hæŋ drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːrtər / (AmE), / hæŋ drɔː ənd ˈkwɔːtə / (BrE)
to execute someone by hanging, disemboweling, and dividing the body into four parts; used figuratively to mean subjecting someone to severe punishment or criticism.

Examples

To hang, draw and quarter a traitor
His teammates threatened to hang, draw and quarter him after he missed the winning shot.
In medieval England, traitors would often be hang, draw and quarter for their crimes.
She said if I lost the documents, my boss would probably hang, draw and quarter me.

Related Terms

  • synonym: execute - to put someone to death, especially as a legal penalty
  • synonym: punish - to subject someone to a penalty for an offense
