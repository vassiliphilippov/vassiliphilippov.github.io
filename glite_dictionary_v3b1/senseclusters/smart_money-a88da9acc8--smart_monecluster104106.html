smart money

noun
punitive damages awarded as legal compensation
8.6 8.4
Pronunciation: / smɑrt ˈmʌni / (AmE), / smɑːt ˈmʌni / (BrE)
money awarded as compensation in addition to actual damages, intended to punish the wrongdoer in cases of malicious or willful misconduct (punitive damages).

Examples

Court awards smart money
In addition to actual losses, the jury decided on a significant amount of smart money.
The court awarded smart money to the plaintiff to punish the company's intentional misconduct.
The judge explained that smart money serves as a warning to others who might act maliciously.
