bog rosemary

noun
evergreen shrub found in boggy Arctic and northern habitats
5.1 8.3
Pronunciation: /bɑːg ˈroʊz.mər.iː/ /; / /bɔːg ˈroʊz.mər.iː/ (AmE), /bɒg ˈrəʊz.mə.riː/ (BrE)
a wiry evergreen shrub with clusters of white or pink, pendulous flowers, found in wet, acidic habitats in Arctic regions, Canada, and northeastern United States

Examples

Bog rosemary shrub
Bog rosemary prefers sphagnum bogs and thrives in acidic conditions.
He photographed the delicate pink flowers of bog rosemary during his trip to northern Canada.
The wetland was dotted with clusters of bog rosemary and other rare plants.
