say it all

verb
express everything necessary (often nonverbally)
Pronunciation: / seɪ ɪt ɔl / (AmE), / seɪ ɪt ɔːl / (BrE)
to express everything necessary, often without using words

Examples

Say it all without words
Her silence said it all—she didn't approve of the plan.
The look on his face said it all as he saw the surprise.
When she slammed the door, her actions said it all.
Her expression said it all when she walked into the room.
His silence after hearing the news said it all.
Sometimes, just a glance between friends can say it all.
