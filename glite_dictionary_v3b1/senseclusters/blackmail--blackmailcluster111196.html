blackmail

verb
obtain something by threats or coercion
2.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /blæk.ˈmeɪl/ (AmE), /blæk.ˈmeɪl/ (BrE)
to force someone to give you money, an advantage, or do something by threatening to reveal secrets or cause harm

Examples

To blackmail someone
He tried to blackmail the politician by threatening to release confidential photos.
The company was blackmailed into paying a large sum of money to keep its secrets safe.
They are blackmailing her to force her into signing the documents.
Never allow anyone to blackmail you into doing something unethical.
She attempted to blackmail her boss by threatening to expose his secret.
The politician claimed that his opponents tried to blackmail him into stepping down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coerce - to persuade someone to do something by force or threat
  • synonym: extort - to obtain something by force or threats
  • antonym: persuade - to make someone do or believe something by giving them a good reason
