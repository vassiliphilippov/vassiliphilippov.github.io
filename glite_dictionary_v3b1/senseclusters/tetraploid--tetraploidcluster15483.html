tetraploid

adjective
four chromosome sets
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtrəˌplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrəplɔɪd / (BrE)
having four complete sets of chromosomes in each cell instead of the usual two

Examples

Tetraploid cells
The scientist discovered that the new species was tetraploid after analyzing its genetic material.
Many cultivated plants are tetraploid, which can make them larger or more robust.
A tetraploid cell contains twice as many chromosomes as a diploid cell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diploid - having two complete sets of chromosomes
  • synonym: polyploid - having more than two sets of chromosomes
