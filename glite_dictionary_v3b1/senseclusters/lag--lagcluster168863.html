lag

noun
a delay between events or processes
5.8 3.4
Pronunciation: /læg/ (AmE), /læg/ (BrE)
a period of delay or the act of falling behind or moving more slowly than others

Examples

A time lag
Technological advancements often have a lag before they are adopted by society.
The effects of policy changes can show a significant lag before becoming apparent.
There is a noticeable lag between submitting an application and receiving a response.
During the video call, there was a noticeable lag in the audio.
Improvements are needed to reduce the lag in processing customer requests.
We noticed a significant lag between sending the command and getting a response from the device.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delay - a period of time by which something is late or postponed
  • synonym: interval - a period of time between two events
  • antonym: lead - a position ahead of others; the act of being in front
