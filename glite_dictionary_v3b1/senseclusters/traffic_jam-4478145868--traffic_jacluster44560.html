traffic jam

noun
congestion of vehicles on a road
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtræfɪk dʒæm /; / ˈtræfɪk ˌdʒæm / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪk dʒam /; / ˈtræfɪk ˌdʒæm / (BrE)
a situation where many vehicles are stuck or move very slowly because the road is too crowded

Examples

A traffic jam on road
She listens to podcasts to pass the time when caught in a traffic jam.
The accident caused a massive traffic jam on the highway.
We were stuck in a traffic jam for nearly an hour during the morning commute.
A minor accident during rush hour quickly led to a massive traffic jam.
She always leaves early to avoid getting caught in a traffic jam on her way to work.
We were stuck in a traffic jam for more than an hour on the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congestion - the state of being overcrowded, especially with traffic
  • antonym: free-flowing traffic - traffic moving easily without delays
  • synonym: gridlock - a situation where nothing can move in a traffic jam
