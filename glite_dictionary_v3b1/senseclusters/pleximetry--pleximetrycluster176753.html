pleximetry

noun
diagnostic tapping of the body
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / plɛkˈsɪmətri / (AmE), / plɛkˈsɪmɪtri / (BrE)
the medical practice of tapping a part of the body to assess the condition of underlying structures, especially as a diagnostic aid

Examples

Pleximetry in medicine
Pleximetry is commonly used by doctors to examine the lungs and detect fluid or air.
Proper technique in pleximetry can help distinguish between healthy and diseased tissues.
The physician performed pleximetry on the patient's chest to check for abnormalities.
