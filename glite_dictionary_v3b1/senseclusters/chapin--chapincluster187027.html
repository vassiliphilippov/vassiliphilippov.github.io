chapin

noun
elevated platform shoe
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʃəˈpiːn / (AmE), / tʃəˈpiːn / (BrE)
an elevated or high-soled shoe or wooden platform shoe, especially worn in the past or in certain regions

Examples

A chapin shoe
The museum displayed a rare chapin from the 17th century in its footwear collection.
Nobles once wore chapins to keep their dresses above the muddy streets.
She studied the intricate carvings on the wooden chapin with fascination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chopine - a type of high platform shoe worn in 16th-17th century Europe
  • degree lower: sandal - an open type of footwear with a sole held to the foot by straps
