decidability

noun
ability to be decided
8.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.saɪ.də.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ /; / /də.saɪ.də.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /dɪ.saɪ.də.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ /; / /də.saɪ.də.ˈbɪ.lɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a problem or question being able to be decided, especially by a definite method or procedure

Examples

The decidability of problems
The decidability of certain mathematical problems is a key topic in computer science.
Researchers debated the decidability of the new logical system for several years.
Understanding the decidability of an algorithm helps determine if a solution can always be found.
