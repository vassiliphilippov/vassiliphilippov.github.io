insistent

adjective
persistently demanding or repetitive
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɪstənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪstənt / (BrE)
continuing to demand attention or action in a firm and persistent way, not allowing refusal or delay

Examples

Insistent demand
Despite my refusal, the salesperson remained insistent until I finally agreed to listen.
Her insistent questions made it impossible to avoid the topic any longer.
The insistent sound of the alarm woke everyone up in the middle of the night.
Her insistent knocking finally made me open the door.
The child grew ever more insistent in her demands for a snack.
There was an insistent buzzing that distracted everyone in the room.
Despite her insistent requests, they refused to change their minds.
The insistent beeping of the alarm clock finally woke him up.
The insistent knocking on the door made it impossible to ignore the visitor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: persistent - continuing firmly or obstinately in a course of action
  • synonym: relentless - oppressively constant; determined and unyielding
  • antonym: unobtrusive - not attracting attention; discreet
  • synonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention
  • antonym: yielding - giving way under pressure; not firm or insistent
