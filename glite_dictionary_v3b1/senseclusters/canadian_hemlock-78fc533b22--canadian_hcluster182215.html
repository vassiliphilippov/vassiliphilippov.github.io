Canadian hemlock

noun
North American coniferous tree, Tsuga canadensis
5.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈneɪ.di.ən ˈhɛm.lɑk / (AmE), / kəˈneɪ.di.ən ˈhem.lɒk / (BrE)
a coniferous tree (Tsuga canadensis) native to eastern North America, valued for its wood and often used in pulpwood production

Examples

A Canadian hemlock tree
Large forests of Canadian hemlock cover parts of the northeastern United States.
Pulp from the Canadian hemlock is used in the production of various types of paper.
The dense shade of the Canadian hemlock makes it a popular choice for landscaping.
