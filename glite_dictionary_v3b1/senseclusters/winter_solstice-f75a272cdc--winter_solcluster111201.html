winter solstice

noun
the shortest day of the year
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪntər ˈsɑl.stɪs / (AmE), / ˈwɪntə ˈsɒl.stɪs / (BrE)
The time in December when the sun is at its southernmost point, marking the shortest day and longest night of the year in the northern hemisphere.

Examples

The winter solstice day
Many cultures hold celebrations or festivals around the winter solstice.
Stonehenge is famously aligned with the sunrise on the winter solstice.
The days gradually begin to lengthen after the winter solstice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: summer solstice - the longest day of the year, typically around June 21
