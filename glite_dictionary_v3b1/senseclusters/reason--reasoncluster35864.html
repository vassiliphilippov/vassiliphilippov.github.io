reason

verb
to present reasons or argue logically
4.1 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈriːzən /; / ˈrizən / (AmE), / ˈriːzən / (BrE)
to think, argue, or discuss logically in order to reach a conclusion

Examples

To reason carefully
After much debate, they reasoned out a solution to the problem.
He tried to reason with the angry crowd to calm them down.
The lawyer reasoned that her client was not present at the scene of the crime.
After examining all the evidence, the committee reasoned that a new policy was necessary.
She reasoned carefully before making her final decision on the project.
They reasoned that the meeting should be postponed due to the weather forecast.
She tried to reason through the problem before making a decision.
The judge must reason carefully when interpreting the law.
Young children gradually learn to reason as they grow older.

Related Terms

  • synonym: argue - to give reasons for or against something; to contend in conversation
  • antonym: assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • synonym: conclude - to reach a decision or opinion by reasoning
  • synonym: deduce - to reach a conclusion by reasoning; synonym for 'reason' in logic
  • antonym: guess - to form an opinion without sufficient information or reasoning
