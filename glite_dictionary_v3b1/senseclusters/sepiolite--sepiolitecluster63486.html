sepiolite

noun
white clay-like mineral
9.6 9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈse.pi.ə.laɪt/ (AmE), /ˈse.pi.ə.laɪt/ (BrE)
a white, clay-like mineral composed mainly of magnesium silicate, used in making lightweight materials and absorbents.

Examples

White sepiolite mineral
Because of its absorbent properties, sepiolite is used to clean up oil spills.
Deposits of sepiolite are often found in arid regions and are mined for industrial use.
The artisan carved a beautiful pipe from sepiolite because of its softness and durability.
