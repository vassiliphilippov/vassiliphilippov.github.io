block out

verb
prevent from entering (light, sound, etc.)
Pronunciation: / blɑk aʊt / (AmE), / blɒk aʊt / (BrE)
to prevent light, sound, or air from entering or passing through; to physically block

Examples

To block out light
Heavy curtains can block out unwanted streetlights at night.
She wore sunglasses to block out the glare from the snow.
The dense foliage blocks out most of the sunlight in the forest.
Heavy curtains can block out most of the sunlight in the room.
She wore an eye mask to block out the early morning light.
The thick foliage blocked out the afternoon sun, casting the path in shade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: let in - to allow something such as light or air to enter
  • synonym: screen - to protect or conceal by a screen; to shield from view or light
  • synonym: shut out - to prevent from entering or coming in
