four-hitter

noun
baseball game with only four hits allowed by pitcher
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr ˌhɪtər / (AmE), / ˈfɔː ˌhɪtə / (BrE)
(in baseball) a game in which a pitcher allows the opposing team only four hits.

Examples

A four-hitter game
He celebrated his first career four-hitter with his teammates after the game.
The rookie pitcher threw a brilliant four-hitter against the league's top team.
Yesterday's four-hitter secured his spot in the starting rotation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: five-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows five hits
  • degree lower: no-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows no hits
  • degree lower: one-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows one hit
  • degree lower: three-hitter - a game in which a pitcher allows three hits
