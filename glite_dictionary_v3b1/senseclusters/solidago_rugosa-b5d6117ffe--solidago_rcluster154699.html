Solidago rugosa

noun
a species of goldenrod with yellow flowers used in dyeing
Pronunciation: / səˈlɪdəɡoʊ ruˈɡoʊsə / (AmE), / sɒˈlɪdəɡəʊ ruˈɡəʊsə / (BrE)
An eastern North American herbaceous plant of the goldenrod family, known for its wrinkled leaves and clusters of yellow flowers, historically used in dyeing.

Examples

Solidago rugosa plant
Solidago rugosa blooms in late summer, brightening fields and roadsides with its clusters of yellow flowers.
Botanists identified the specimen as Solidago rugosa, noting its wrinkled leaves and robust flowering stems.
Historically, the flowers of Solidago rugosa were harvested and used to produce a yellow dye for fabrics.
