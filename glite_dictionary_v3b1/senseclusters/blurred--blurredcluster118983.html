blurred

adjective
unclear; indistinct; hard to perceive
3.4 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / bl…ùd / (AmE), / bl…úÀêd / (BrE)
not clear or distinct in form, outline, or meaning; indistinct or difficult to perceive

Examples

A blurred image
Her memory of that day has become blurred over time.
The edges of the photograph were blurred, making it hard to see the details.
The line between friendship and romance can sometimes be blurred.
Her memories of childhood are only blurred images now.
The artist deliberately painted blurred figures to create a sense of mystery.
Through the rain-soaked window, the city lights appeared blurred and distant.

  • hazy
    3.9 3.45/m
    hazy often means hard to see or remember due to mist or doubt, while blurred focuses on unclear visual shape.
  • foggy
    1.4 0.31/m

  • muzzy

  • unsharp
