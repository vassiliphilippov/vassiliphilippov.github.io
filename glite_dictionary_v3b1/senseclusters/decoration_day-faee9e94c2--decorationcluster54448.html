Decoration Day

noun
former name for Memorial Day (U.S.)
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən deɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɛkəˈreɪʃən deɪ / (BrE)
a former name for Memorial Day, a United States holiday observed on the last Monday in May to honor members of the armed forces who were killed in war

Examples

Decoration Day holiday
In the nineteenth century, Decoration Day was observed throughout the United States.
Many communities held parades on Decoration Day to honor fallen soldiers.
She placed flowers on the graves of veterans every Decoration Day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Memorial Day - the current name of the U.S. holiday honoring fallen military personnel
