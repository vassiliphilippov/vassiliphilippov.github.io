chipboard

noun
pressed wood chips bonded with resin
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪpˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪpˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a type of stiff, inexpensive material made from wood chips that are pressed and bonded together with resin, often used in furniture and building

Examples

A chipboard panel
Moisture can easily damage chipboard if it is not properly sealed.
Some companies use chipboard as a backing material for cheaper furniture.
The bookshelves were made of chipboard, which is lightweight and affordable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: particleboard - a similar material made from wood particles rather than chips
  • degree higher: plywood - a material made from thin layers of wood glued together
