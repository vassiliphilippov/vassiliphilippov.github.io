tobacco budworm

noun
destructive crop pest larva
Pronunciation: / təˈbækoʊ ˈbʌdˌwɜrm / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ ˈbʌdwɜːm / (BrE)
the larva of a noctuid moth (Heliothis virescens), highly destructive to crops such as cotton, corn, and tomatoes

Examples

The tobacco budworm larva
Crops like tomatoes and corn can be devastated by a tobacco budworm infestation.
Farmers are concerned about the damage caused by the tobacco budworm to their cotton fields.
The tobacco budworm has developed resistance to several types of insecticides.
