allegiance

noun
loyalty or commitment
5.6 9.44/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàlid í…ôns /; / …ôÀàliÀêd í…ôns / (AmE), / …ôÀàliÀêd í…ôns / (BrE)
loyalty or commitment to a group, cause, person, or authority

Examples

Allegiance to country
Her allegiance to the team remained strong even after several losses.
The new law required citizens to declare their allegiance openly.
The soldier swore allegiance to his country before departing for service.
He changed his allegiance from one political party to another.
Questions about allegiance often arise during times of war.
The soldiers swore their allegiance to the new government.

Synonyms

  • loyalty
    1.3 23/m
    loyalty shows steady faith and support for people or groups, while allegiance names formal duty to authority or cause.
  • faithfulness
    2.0 1.50/m
    faithfulness means staying true and reliable in relationships or tasks, while allegiance points to loyalty to a group or ruler.
  • commitment
    3.3 54/m
    commitment is a promise to stay involved over time, while allegiance implies loyalty specifically to a group or authority.
  • devotion
    3.9 4.11/m
    devotion is a deep emotional dedication often shown by strong love, while allegiance often means political or based on duty loyalty.
  • dedication
    3.9 7.28/m
    dedication is strong care and hard work for a task or goal, while allegiance names loyalty to a person, group, or authority.

Related Terms

  • antonym: treason - the crime of betraying one's country
