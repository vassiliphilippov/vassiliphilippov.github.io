allegiance

noun
loyalty or commitment
3.1 4.6
Pronunciation: / əˈlidʒəns /; / əˈliːdʒəns / (AmE), / əˈliːdʒəns / (BrE)
loyalty or commitment to a group, cause, person, or authority

Examples

Allegiance to country
Her allegiance to the team remained strong even after several losses.
The new law required citizens to declare their allegiance openly.
The soldier swore allegiance to his country before departing for service.
He changed his allegiance from one political party to another.
Questions about allegiance often arise during times of war.
The soldiers swore their allegiance to the new government.
